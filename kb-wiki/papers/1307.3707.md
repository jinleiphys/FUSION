# Coupling constant for $Λ(1405)\bar{K}N$

> The value of the $Λ(1405){\bar K}N$ coupling constant $g_{Λ(1405)\bar{K}N}$ is obtained by fitting it to the experimental data on the total cross sections of the $K^-p \to π^0Σ^0$ reaction. On the basis of an effective Lagrangian approach and isobar model, we show that the value $|g_{Λ(1405)\bar{K}N}| = 1.51 \pm 0.10$ could be extracted from the available experimental data by assuming that the $s-$channel $Λ(1405)$ resonance plays a dominant role, whereas, the background contributions from the $s-$channel $Λ(1115)$, $t-$channel $K^*$ and $u-$channel nucleon pole processes are small and can be neglected. However, the $u-$channel nucleon pole diagram may also give an important contribution in the present calculations. After the background contributions are taken into account, the above value of $g_{Λ(1405)\bar{K}N}$ is reduced to $|g_{Λ(1405)\bar{K}N}| = 0.77 \pm 0.07$, which is not supported by the previous calculations and the recent CLAS measurements. The theoretical calculations on differential cross sections are also presented, which can be checked by future experiments.

## Key claim

The coupling constant \(g_{\Lambda(1405)\bar{K}N}\) is extracted from \(K^-p \to \pi^0\Sigma^0\) total cross-section data, but the extracted value depends critically on whether background contributions (especially the \(u\)-channel nucleon pole) are included: the value is \(|g| = 1.51 \pm 0.10\) when only the \(s\)-channel \(\Lambda(1405)\) is considered, but drops to \(|g| = 0.77 \pm 0.07\) when all background diagrams are included.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach and isobar model at tree level, considering Feynman diagrams for the \(s\)-channel \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\Lambda(1115)\), \(t\)-channel \(K^*\) exchange, and \(u\)-channel proton exchange. They fit the product \(g_{\Lambda^*\bar{K}N} \, g_{\Lambda^*\pi\Sigma}\) to 10 experimental total cross-section data points near the \(K^-p\) threshold using the MINUIT fitting program, with a monopole form factor cutoff \(\Lambda\) constrained between 0.6 and 1.2 GeV. Two fits are performed: Fit I includes all four diagrams, while Fit II includes only the \(s\)-channel \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance.

## Key numbers

*   \(|g_{\Lambda(1405)\bar{K}N}| = 0.77 \pm 0.07\) from Fit I (all diagrams included), with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.9\) and cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.6\) GeV for all channels.
*   \(|g_{\Lambda(1405)\bar{K}N}| = 1.51 \pm 0.10\) from Fit II (only \(s\)-channel \(\Lambda(1405)\)), with \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.7\) and fitted cutoff \(\Lambda_s^{\Lambda(1405)} = 3.00 \pm 2.62\) GeV.
*   \(|g_{\Lambda(1405)\pi\Sigma}| = 0.90 \pm 0.02\) obtained from the \(\Lambda(1405) \to \pi\Sigma\) partial decay width using PDG values (mass \(1405.1^{+1.3}_{-1.0}\) MeV, total width \(50 \pm 2\) MeV).
*   Ratio \(R = |g_{\Lambda^*\bar{K}N}/g_{\Lambda^*\pi\Sigma}| = 1.68 \pm 0.12\) from Fit II values; if the two fit results are taken as lower and upper limits, \(R = 1.27 \pm 0.41\).

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of the \(\Lambda(1405)\bar{K}N\) coupling, which cannot be obtained from partial decay widths because the \(\Lambda(1405)\) mass (1405 MeV) lies below the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold (1434.6 MeV). The value \(|g| = 1.51 \pm 0.10\) is comparable to the value 1.84 from a separable potential model by Xie (2010) and to results from an algebra-of-currents approach by Weil (R = 2.19) and a coupled-channel analysis by J. K. Kim (R = 2.61 \(\pm\) 1.34). The smaller value 0.77 is not supported by those prior calculations or by recent CLAS measurements of the \(\Sigma\pi\) mass distribution, which show a sharp drop at the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold that requires a larger coupling. The differential cross sections predicted here can be tested by future experiments to clarify the role of the \(u\)-channel nucleon pole.
