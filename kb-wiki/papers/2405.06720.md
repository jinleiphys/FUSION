# One-photon annihilation of the electron-positron pair at heavy atomic nuclei

> We investigate one-photon annihilation of the electron-positron pair in the field of a central, very heavy and positively charged atomic nucleus. The explicit formula for the annihilation rate of this process $Γ^{(b)}_{1 γ}$ is derived. Our formula for this rate can directly be used to describe the actual one-photon annihilation in the ground (bound) states of all positronium hydrides HPs, quasi-stable triplet states of the positron-helium atoms $e^{+}[$ He($2^{3}S_e$)] ions and other systems.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact QED formula for the one-photon annihilation rate of an electron-positron pair in the Coulomb field of an infinitely heavy, positively charged atomic nucleus, correcting approximations made by Heitler (1954) and the author's own earlier work on positronium hydride (Frolov, 2020). The derivation explicitly neglects nuclear recoil, so the results apply only to systems with infinitely heavy nuclei.

## Method

The author applies standard Feynman rules of Quantum Electrodynamics to compute the S-matrix element for one-photon annihilation of an electron-positron pair in the static Coulomb potential of a heavy nucleus with charge Qe. The unpolarized differential cross section is obtained by averaging over electron and positron spins, summing over photon polarizations, and evaluating the traces of products of Dirac gamma matrices using standard identities. The final expression is written in manifestly covariant form and then transformed into a three-dimensional angular distribution formula analogous to the Bethe-Heitler formula for bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic cross section for one-photon annihilation is derived as: sigma_nr = (8 pi^2 Q^2 alpha^3) / (3 m_0^5) * (v_+^2 + v_-^2) / (|v_+| |v_-|), where v_+ and v_- are positron and electron velocities relative to the nucleus, m_0 is the electron mass, and alpha is the fine-structure constant.
- The low-energy limit uses the replacements: E_+ = E_- = m_0, omega = 2 m_0, q = m_0, with omega much greater than |p_+| and |p_-|.
- The differential cross section in the non-relativistic limit is: d sigma_nr = (2 pi Q^2 alpha^3 / m_0^5) * (p_+^2 sin^2 theta_+ + p_-^2 sin^2 theta_-) / (|p_+| |p_-|) d omega d n_f, where theta_+ and theta_- are the polar angles of the positron and electron momenta relative to the photon direction.

## Context

This work corrects the approximate treatment by Heitler (1954), who reduced one-photon annihilation to a photodetachment calculation with a replacement of the final electron by an incoming positron, and the author's earlier approximate evaluation for positronium hydride HPs (Frolov, 2020) which used the formula for the three-body positronium ion Ps-. The derived formula can be applied to describe one-photon annihilation in ground states of positronium hydrides, quasi-stable triplet states of positron-helium atoms e+[He(2^3S_e)] ions, and other positron-containing atomic systems, with applications in astrophysics and solid state physics.
