# Screening masses of mesons in 2+1 flavour QCD

> We present results for screening masses of light and strange mesons in 2+1 flavour QCD using improved (p4fat3) staggered fermions on 6x24^3 lattices. We have studied the screening masses of scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector and axial-vector mesons along the line of constant physics, determined by a pion mass ~220 MeV and a kaon mass ~500 MeV. In order to investigate the cut-off and volume dependencies we have also performed studies of the meson screening correlators in the non-interacting theory using the p4 and the standard staggered discretizations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice results for meson screening masses in 2+1 flavour QCD with realistic quark masses, but the results are obtained on a single lattice size (6×24³) and the authors note that screening masses show strong volume dependence in the free theory, meaning the interacting results may have significant finite-volume effects that have not yet been quantified.

## Method

The authors used gauge configurations generated by the RBC-Bielefeld collaboration with the improved p4fat3 staggered fermion action on 6×24³ lattices along a line of constant physics defined by a pion mass of approximately 220 MeV and a kaon mass of approximately 500 MeV. They computed spatial (screening) correlators for all eight local staggered meson operators, extracting screening masses for scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels in the \(\bar ud\), \(\bar us\), and \(\bar ss\) quark sectors. To assess cut-off and volume dependencies, they also performed semi-analytic calculations of meson screening correlators in the non-interacting theory using both the p4 and standard staggered discretizations.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass ≈ 220 MeV and kaon mass ≈ 500 MeV define the line of constant physics
- Lattice size: 6×24³ (Nτ×Ns³)
- Transition temperature Tc ≈ 194 ± 5 MeV
- Pseudo-scalar screening masses start deviating from zero-temperature masses for T ≳ 145 MeV; in the range 145 MeV ≤ T < Tc, they differ by only 5-10% depending on quark mass
- At T > 400 MeV, both PS and SC screening masses differ from the free continuum value of 2πT by ∼10%
- PS and SC screening masses become degenerate only at T > 250 MeV for the \(\bar ud\) and \(\bar us\) sectors
- Vector and axial-vector screening masses are compatible with 2πT for all T > Tc, but differ by ∼20% from their free lattice values
- Three highest temperatures studied: T = 321, 363, and 413 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of meson screening masses in QCD, which had been limited to fewer flavours or unphysical quark masses, as reviewed by Karsch (2002) and more recent lattice results cited in the paper. The results enable investigation of chiral and UA(1) axial symmetry restoration in QCD at finite temperature, suggesting that effective UA(1) restoration likely does not occur at the transition temperature but at significantly higher temperatures (T > 250 MeV for light quarks). The strong volume dependence observed in the free theory indicates that future studies on larger lattices are needed to control finite-volume effects.
