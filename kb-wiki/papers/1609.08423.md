# Particle production and chemical freezeout from the hybrid UrQMD approach at NICA energies

> The energy dependence of various particle ratios is calculated within the Ultra-Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics approach and compared with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and measurements from various experiments, including RHIC-BES, SPS and AGS. It is found that the UrQMD particle ratios agree well with the experimental results at the RHIC-BES energies. Thus, we have utilized UrQMD in simulating particle ratios at other beam energies down to 3 GeV, which will be accessed at NICA and FAIR future facilities. We observe that the particle ratios for crossover and first-order phase transition, implemented in the hybrid UrQMD v3.4, are nearly indistinguishable, especially at low energies (at large baryon chemical potentials or high density).

## Key claim

The hybrid UrQMD v3.4 model, implementing either crossover or first-order phase transitions, produces particle ratios that are nearly indistinguishable from each other, especially at low beam energies (large baryon chemical potentials or high density), suggesting that the chemical freezeout stage is not strongly influenced by the order of the quark-hadron phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid UrQMD v3.4 event generator to simulate Au-Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 3 to 19.6 GeV, with both crossover and first-order phase transition equations of state. They extracted ten particle ratios from ensembles of 10,000 to 150,000 events per energy and performed statistical fits of the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model to these simulated ratios to determine the freezeout temperature and baryon chemical potential at each energy.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV with crossover transition: T_ch = 132 MeV, mu_b = 436 MeV, chi^2/dof = 15.5/10
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV with crossover transition: T_ch = 138.1 MeV, mu_b = 391 MeV, chi^2/dof = 8.95/10
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV with crossover transition: T_ch = 145 MeV, mu_b = 200 MeV, chi^2/dof = 8.58/10
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV with crossover transition: T_ch = 101 MeV, mu_b = 636 MeV, chi^2/dof = 6.3/6
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV with first-order transition: T_ch = 99 MeV, mu_b = 630 MeV, chi^2/dof = 6.4/6
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 19 GeV with first-order transition: T_ch = 142 MeV, mu_b = 250 MeV, chi^2/dof = 8.43/10
- From HRG fit to STAR BES measurements at 7.7 GeV: T_ch = 141 MeV, mu_b = 412 MeV, chi^2/dof = 11.7/9
- From HRG fit to STAR BES measurements at 11.5 GeV: T_ch = 150 MeV, mu_b = 312 MeV, chi^2/dof = 6.9/9
- From HRG fit to STAR BES measurements at 19.6 GeV: T_ch = 153 MeV, mu_b = 149 MeV, chi^2/dof = 7.6/9

## Context

This work extends the application of the hybrid UrQMD model to the low-energy range (down to 3 GeV) that will be accessed by the NICA and FAIR facilities, building on prior comparisons between UrQMD and experimental data from RHIC-BES, SPS, and AGS. The results are compared with freezeout parameters from Cleymans et al., Tawfik et al., HADES, FOPI, and from higher-order moments of net-proton multiplicity analyzed with the SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model and HRG. The authors note that alternative approaches such as Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) and Three-Fluid Hydrodynamics (3FH) may perform better than UrQMD at low energies, and they plan future comparisons between all these approaches at NICA energies.
