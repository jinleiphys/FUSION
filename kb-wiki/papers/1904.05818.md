# Three-Body Hypernuclei in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> We calculate the structure of three-body hypernuclei with $S=-1$ using pionless effective field theory at leading order in the isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$ sectors. In both sectors, three-body hypernuclei arise naturally from the Efimov effect and a three-body parameter is required at leading order. We apply our theory to the hypertriton and the hypothetical $Λnn$ bound state and calculate the corresponding scaling factors. Moreover, we discuss constraints on the existence of the $Λnn$ bound state. In particular, we elucidate universal correlations between different observables and provide explicit calculations of wave functions and matter radii.

## Key claim

Three-body hypernuclei in both the isospin I=0 (hypertriton) and I=1 (Λnn) channels arise naturally from the Efimov effect and require a three-body parameter at leading order in pionless effective field theory, but the three-body parameter for each isospin channel is not related at the resolution level of the theory.

## Method

The authors constructed integral equations for the hypertriton (I=0) and Λnn system (I=1) using pionless effective field theory at leading order with contact interactions, employing the dibaryon formalism for two-body subsystems. They performed an asymptotic analysis of the three-body equations to determine the scaling exponent s0 and the need for a three-body force, then solved the integral equations numerically using a cutoff regularization with the three-body force tuned to reproduce the binding energy as input.

## Key numbers

- Scaling exponent s0 = 1.00624 for equal masses (y=0), corrected from the earlier value of 1.35322 found in Hammer (2001)
- Scaling exponent s0 = 1.00760 for the physical mass ratio M/Μ = 0.84 (y=0.086), applicable to both the hypertriton and Λnn channels
- Three-body parameter for the hypertriton: Λ*^(I=0) = (6.372 ± 0.008) MeV, fixed using B3^Λ = 2.35 MeV
- Three-body parameter for the Λnn system: Λ*^(I=1) = (13.95 ± 0.02) MeV, fixed using B_Λnn = 1.1 MeV
- Predicted Λd scattering length for physical Λ mass: a_Λd^(y=0.086) = 15.4^(+4.3)_(-2.3) fm
- Predicted Λd scattering length for equal masses: a_Λd^(y=0) = 16.25^(+4.45)_(-2.40) fm
- Deuteron binding momentum γd = 45.68 MeV used as input for the spin-triplet NN interaction
- Neutron-neutron scattering length a_nn = -18.63 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.44 (syst.) ± 0.30 (theo.) fm used for the spin-singlet NN interaction
- ΛN spin-singlet scattering length a_s^Λp = -2.91 fm and spin-triplet scattering length a_t^Λp = -1.61 fm from chiral EFT at NLO by Haidenbauer (2013)
- Scaling factor exp(π/s0) ≈ 22.7 for equal masses, giving the periodicity of the limit cycle

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT treatment of the hypertriton by Hammer (2001) to include both I=0 and I=1 three-body hypernuclei, correcting the earlier scaling exponent and integral equation factors. It addresses the ongoing debate about the existence of a bound Λnn system following the HypHI collaboration evidence from 2013, providing a model-independent framework that complements earlier studies by Gal (2014), Garcilazo (2014), Richard (2014), and Hiyama (2014) which mostly rejected such a bound state. The universal correlations and scaling factors derived here enable future experimental searches for the Λnn state at facilities such as ALICE, and provide predictions for Λd scattering observables that can be tested experimentally.
