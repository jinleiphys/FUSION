# Collective Path Connecting the Oblate and Prolate Local Minima in 68Se

> By means of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method and the pairing plus quadrupole interaction, we have obtained the selfconsistent collective path connecting the oblate and prolate local minima in 68Se for the first time. Along the collective path, the triaxial deformation parameter (gamma) changes between 0 and 60 (degree) keeping the axially symmetric deformation parameter (beta) approximately constant, indicating the importance of triaxial deformation dynamics in the oblate-prolate shape coexistence phenomena.

## Key claim

Using the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method with a pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) interaction model, the authors obtained the first self-consistent collective path connecting the oblate and prolate local minima in 68Se, with the path running through a valley in the gamma direction rather than through the spherical point.

## Method

The authors solved the ASCC equations for a realistic P+Q Hamiltonian, using spherical single-particle energies from the modified oscillator model and force parameters chosen to reproduce the constrained HFB potential energy surface from a Skyrme-HFB calculation by Yamagami et al. (2001). Starting from one HFB local minimum, they iteratively solved the moving frame RPA equations and the HFB equation in the moving frame to generate the collective path step by step, verifying the path by starting from the other minimum and proceeding in reverse.

## Key numbers

- Pairing force parameter G = 0.320 MeV (for both protons and neutrons) and quadrupole force parameter chi' = 0.248 MeV were used to reproduce the constrained HFB potential energy surface.
- The triaxial deformation parameter gamma changes between 0 degrees and 60 degrees along the collective path, while the axially symmetric deformation parameter beta remains approximately constant.
- The frequency of the gamma mode becomes imaginary in the region 12 degrees less than gamma less than 45 degrees.
- The collective mass M(q) was defined such that M(q) = 1 MeV^{-1} at the scale of the collective coordinate q.

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method, previously tested on the exactly solvable multi-O(4) model by Kobayasi et al. (2003), to a realistic P+Q interaction model for the first time. It contrasts with the generator coordinate method used by Duguet et al. (2003) and the microscopic calculations of Petrovici et al. (2002), which ignored triaxial shape vibrational degrees of freedom; the result strongly indicates the necessity of including the gamma degree of freedom for describing oblate-prolate shape coexistence in 68Se. The authors note that a similar approach was pursued by Almehed and Walet (2004), though they encountered difficulties in obtaining self-consistent collective paths for different nuclei.
