# Chiral phase transition of a dense, magnetized and rotating quark matter

> We investigate the chiral symmetry restoration/breaking of a dense, magnetized and rotating quark matter within the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model including $N_f=2$ and $N_c=3$ numbers of flavors and colors, respectively. Imposing the spectral boundary conditions, as well as the positiveness of energy levels, lead to a correlation between the magnetic and rotation fields such that strongly magnetized plasma can not rotate anymore. We solve the gap equation at zero and finite temperature. At finite temperature and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, we sketch the phase diagrams $T_c(μ_B)$ and $T_c(RΩ)$ in different cases. As a result, we always observe inverse-rotational catalysis mean to decrease $T_c$ by increasing $RΩ$. But the magnetic field has a more complex structure in the phase diagram. For slowly rotating plasma, we find that $T_c$ decreases by increasing $eB$, while in the fast rotating plasma we see that $T_c$ increases by increasing $eB$. Also, we locate exactly the position of Critical End Point by solving the equations of first and second derivatives of effective action with respect to the order parameters, simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a dense, magnetized, and rotating quark matter described by the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with spectral boundary conditions, the chiral critical temperature \(T_c\) always decreases with increasing rotation \(R\Omega\) (inverse-rotational catalysis), while the magnetic field \(eB\) produces a more complex effect: for slowly rotating plasma \(T_c\) decreases with increasing \(eB\) (inverse-magnetic catalysis), but for fast rotating plasma \(T_c\) increases with increasing \(eB\) (magnetic catalysis). This behavior is conditional on a correlation between magnetic field strength and rotation imposed by the boundary condition and the positiveness of energy levels, which forbids strong magnetic fields from coexisting with fast rotation.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor (\(N_f=2\)), three-color (\(N_c=3\)) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in curved space, employing the Ritus method to solve the Dirac equation for quarks in a constant magnetic field and uniform rotation. They impose spectral boundary conditions on a cylindrical volume of radius \(R\) and require positive energy eigenvalues, which yields a correlation between the dimensionless magnetic parameter \(\alpha = |eB|R^2/2\) and the rotation parameter \(R\Omega\). The gap equation for the chiral order parameter \(\sigma\) is solved numerically at zero and finite temperature, and the phase diagrams \(T_c(\mu_B)\) and \(T_c(R\Omega)\) are constructed for various fixed values of \(\alpha\) and \(R\Omega\). The location of the critical end point (CEP) is determined by simultaneously solving the first and second derivatives of the effective action with respect to \(\sigma\).

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength is parameterized by the dimensionless quantity \(\alpha \equiv |eB|R^2/2\); for \(\alpha \leq \alpha_c = 7\), the allowed rotation range is \(0 \leq R\Omega \leq 1\), while for \(\alpha \geq \alpha_c\) the maximum rotation \(R\Omega_\text{Max}\) decreases with increasing \(\alpha\), such that for \(\alpha \sim 30\) and beyond, \(R\Omega_\text{Max} \sim 0\).
* The threshold coupling for non-zero dynamical mass at small magnetic fields is \(G_c\Lambda^2 \sim 2\pi^2/(N_c N_f)\), consistent with the normal NJL model; this threshold coupling decreases with increasing \(\alpha\), indicating magnetic catalysis.
* At finite temperature, the critical temperature \(T_c\) always decreases with increasing \(R\Omega\) (inverse-rotational catalysis) for fixed \(\alpha\) and \(\mu_B\).
* For slowly rotating plasma, \(T_c\) decreases with increasing \(eB\) (inverse-magnetic catalysis); for fast rotating plasma, \(T_c\) increases with increasing \(eB\) (magnetic catalysis).
* The slope \(dT_\text{CEP}/d(R\Omega)\) at fixed \(\mu_B\) is larger for smaller \(\alpha\) and approaches zero for very large \(\alpha\).

## Context

This work extends studies of chiral phase transitions in rotating quark matter within the NJL model, such as those by Chernodub (2016, 2017) for uncharged fermions and Wang (2018) for charged quarks under rotation, and by Sadooghi (2021) for uncharged fermions in combined magnetic and rotation fields. It also relates to lattice QCD results on inverse magnetic catalysis by Bali (2011), Endrodi (2015), and Bruckmann (2013). The novel interplay between magnetic and rotation fields, producing both inverse-magnetic catalysis and magnetic catalysis depending on rotation speed, has not been reported previously. The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in heavy-ion collisions where both strong magnetic fields and large angular momenta are present.
