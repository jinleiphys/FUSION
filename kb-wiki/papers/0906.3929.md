# Exploring the gluoN_c plasma

> We report lattice computations in SU(N_c) pure gauge theory, where N_c is increased beyond the physical value of 3. We demonstrate two-loop scaling of T_c, thus obtaining the variation of T_c/Lambda_MSbar with N_c, and fixing the temperature scale. We study the equation of state of the gluoN_c plasma, the conformal anomaly, and the approach to the weak coupling theory. We find that the weak-coupling prediction is always closer to the lattice data than the conformal field theory is.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for SU(\(N_c\)) pure gauge theory with \(N_c = 4\) and 6, the lattice equation of state is always closer to the weak-coupling prediction than to a conformal field theory (where the interaction measure \(\Delta = 0\)), across all temperatures studied, with no temperature window where a non-trivial conformal theory provides a better description.

## Method

The authors performed lattice Monte Carlo simulations of SU(4) and SU(6) pure gauge theory using the Wilson action on \(N_t \times N_s^3\) lattices with \(N_t = 4, 6, 8\) and \(N_s \ge 2N_t\). The temperature scale was set by measuring the zero-temperature plaquette, inverting its weak-coupling expansion to define the renormalized coupling in the V-scheme, and then solving the two-loop renormalization group equation to obtain \(T/T_c\). The equation of state (pressure and interaction measure) was computed using the integral method with the two-loop beta function.

## Key numbers

- For SU(4) pure gauge theory, two-loop scaling of the temperature scale is adequate for \(N_t = 6\) or more.
- The interaction measure \(\Delta = E - 3P\) scales as \(T^2\) in the high-temperature phase for SU(4).
- The continuum limit for the pressure in SU(4) is reached at \(N_t = 6\).
- The ratio \(T_c / \Lambda_{\overline{MS}}\) is determined for each \(N_c\) and scales to a finite value in the \(N_c \to \infty\) limit with \(1/N_c\) corrections.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of the deconfinement transition and equation of state in SU(\(N_c\)) gauge theories beyond the physical \(N_c = 3\), building on prior simulations for \(N_c = 3\) (nc3), \(N_c = 4\) (nc4), and higher \(N_c\) (largen). The results enable quantitative comparison of the high-temperature phase of large-\(N_c\) gauge theories with both weak-coupling resummed perturbation theory (Mikko) and conformal field theory predictions, showing that weak-coupling theory is always a better description. The phase diagram arguments connect to universality class analyses by Pisarski and Wilczek (Piswil) and the Banks-Casher relation (Banks).
