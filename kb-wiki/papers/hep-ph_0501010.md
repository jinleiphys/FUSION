# Mixing and decays of the antidecuplet in the context of approximate SU(3) symmetry

> We consider mixing of the antidecuplet with three J^P=1/2^+ octets (the ground-state octet, the octet containing N(1440), Λ(1600), Σ(1660) and Ξ(1690) and the octet containing N(1710), Λ(1800), Σ(1880) and Ξ(1950)) in the framework of approximate flavor SU(3) symmetry. We give general expressions for the partial decay widths of all members of the antidecuplet as functions of the two mixing angles. Identifying N_{anti-10} with the N(1670) observed by the GRAAL experiment, we show that the considered mixing scenario can accommodate all present experimental and phenomenological information on the Θ^+ and N_{anti-10} decays: Θ^+ could be as narrow as 1 MeV; the N_{anti-10} -> N + ηdecay is sizable, while the N_{anti-10} ->N + πdecay is suppressed and the N_{anti-10} ->Λ+ K decay is possibly suppressed. Constraining the mixing angles by the N_{anti-10} decays, we make definite predictions for the Σ_{anti-10} decays. We point out that Σ_{anti-10} with mass near 1770 MeV could be searched for in the available data on K_S p invariant mass spectrum, which already revealed the Θ^+ peak. It is important to experimentally verify the decay properties of Σ(1770) because its mass and J^P=1/2^+ make it an attractive candidate for Σ_{anti-10}.

## Key claim

The authors show that mixing the antidecuplet with three known \(J^P=1/2^+\) octets (the ground-state octet, the octet containing N(1440), and the octet containing N(1710)) can accommodate all present experimental and phenomenological information on the \(\Theta^+\) and \(N_{\overline{10}}\) decays, provided the mixing angles are small and the \(G_{\overline{10}}\) coupling constant is positive; the \(\Theta^+\) could be as narrow as 1 MeV, the \(N_{\overline{10}} \to N\eta\) decay is sizable, while the \(N_{\overline{10}} \to N\pi\) decay is suppressed and the \(N_{\overline{10}} \to \Lambda K\) decay is possibly suppressed.

## Method

The authors derived general SU(3) expressions for the partial decay widths of all antidecuplet members into a ground-state baryon and a pseudoscalar meson as functions of two mixing angles (\(\theta_2\) and \(\theta_3\)), using the chiral quark soliton model for the coupling constants and the mixing angle with the ground-state octet (\(\theta_1\)). They determined the octet-octet and octet-decuplet transition coupling constants for the two excited octets from a \(\chi^2\) fit to experimental partial decay widths, then constrained the mixing angles by requiring \(\Gamma_{N_{\overline{10}} \to N\pi} \leq 1\) MeV to match the partial-wave analysis of Arndt (2004).

## Key numbers

* The \(\Theta^+\) mass is taken as 1540 MeV; the \(\Xi_{\overline{10}}\) mass is taken as 1862 ± 2 MeV from the NA49 experiment.
* The \(N_{\overline{10}}\) mass is identified with the peak at 1670 MeV seen by the GRAAL experiment; the \(\Sigma_{\overline{10}}\) mass is assumed to be 1765 MeV (equally spaced between \(N_{\overline{10}}\) and \(\Xi_{\overline{10}}\)).
* The total width of the \(\Theta^+\) is varied between 1 and 5 MeV; the pion-nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_{\pi N}\) is varied between 45 and 75 MeV.
* The universal octet coupling constant is \(G_8 \approx 18\); the antidecuplet coupling constant satisfies \(|G_{\overline{10}}| \ll G_8\).
* The NA49 experiment gives an upper limit \(\Gamma_{\Xi_{\overline{10}}} < 18\) MeV.
* The partial-wave analysis of Arndt (2004) indicates \(\Gamma_{N_{\overline{10}} \to N\pi} \leq 0.5\) MeV for a candidate state near 1680 MeV.
* The STAR collaboration observes a narrow peak at 1734 ± 0.5 ± 5 MeV and a weak indication of a peak at 1693 ± 0.5 MeV in the \(\Lambda K_S\) invariant mass.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark soliton model predictions of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and the mixing analysis of Arndt et al. (2004) by including simultaneous mixing of the antidecuplet with three octets rather than only the ground-state octet. The authors show that the popular scenario of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003), which assumes nearly ideal mixing of N(1440) with N(1710), is inconsistent with experimental data on N(1440) and N(1710) decays and the \(\Theta^+\) width constraint. The results enable predictions for the yet-unmeasured decay modes of the \(\Sigma_{\overline{10}}\) and suggest that the \(\Sigma_{\overline{10}}\) with mass near 1770 MeV could be searched for in existing \(K_S p\) invariant mass data that already revealed the \(\Theta^+\) peak.
