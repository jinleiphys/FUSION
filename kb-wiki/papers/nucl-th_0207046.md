# Charge symmetry breaking in pion production

> Chiral effective field theory predicts a charge symmetry violating (CSB) amplitude for pion-nucleon scattering. This mechanism provides a very large contribution also to the CSB forward-backward asymmetry in the angular distribution of the reaction pn --> d pi0. This contribution was so large that it had a potential to cause a large effect also in CSB elastic NN scattering and to disturb its present understanding. However, it can be seen that, contrary to pion production, in this case the ud-quark mass difference and the electromagnetic contribution to the np-mass difference tend to cancel causing the total effect in the effective range parameters Delta a = a_pp - a_nn and Delta r_0 = r_0,pp - r_0,nn to be relatively small. In the lowest order and within the static approximation for the nucleons CSB pion-nucleon rescattering does not influence np scattering.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory predicts a charge symmetry violating (CSB) amplitude for pion-nucleon scattering that gives a very large contribution to the forward-backward asymmetry in \(pn \to d\pi^0\), but the same mechanism produces only small effects in elastic \(NN\) scattering because the \(ud\)-quark mass difference and the electromagnetic contribution to the \(np\)-mass difference tend to cancel.

## Method

The author calculates the CSB forward-backward asymmetry \(A_{fb}\) in \(pn \to d\pi^0\) at 279.5 MeV using a chiral effective Lagrangian that includes the \(u\) and \(d\) quark mass difference and the electromagnetic "hard photon" exchange contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference. The same Lagrangian is then applied to compute the class III two-pion exchange interaction in elastic \(NN\) scattering in lowest order and within the static approximation for nucleons, including contributions from intermediate \(\Delta(1232)\) isobars.

## Key numbers

- The integrated forward-backward asymmetry in \(pn \to d\pi^0\) at 279.5 MeV is given by \(A_{fb} \simeq \left(-28 + \frac{24}{\text{MeV}} (\delta m_N - \frac{\bar\delta m_N}{2})\right) \times 10^{-4}\), where the first term comes from conventional meson contributions (dominated by \(\eta\pi\) mixing with \(G_\eta^2/4\pi = 3.68\) and \(\langle\pi |H|\eta\rangle = -5900\) MeV\(^2\)).
- The total \(A_{fb}\) varies in the range 0.3–0.6% given the ranges \(\delta m_N = 2\)–3 MeV and \(\bar\delta m_N = (-0.5)\)–\((-1.5)\) MeV.
- The factor \(\delta m_N + 2\bar\delta m_N\) in elastic \(NN\) scattering ranges from +0.8 to –0.2 MeV, while \(\delta m_N - \bar\delta m_N/2\) ranges from 2.2 to 3.4 MeV.
- Contributions to the scattering length difference \(\Delta a = a_{pp} - a_{nn}\) vary between 0.17 fm and –0.05 fm, and to the effective range difference \(\Delta r_0 = r_{0,pp} - r_{0,nn}\) between 0.003 and –0.001 fm.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to CSB in pion-nucleon scattering from Ref. knm (2001) and applies it to both pion production and elastic \(NN\) scattering. It contrasts with earlier calculations that treated \(\eta\pi\) mixing as the dominant CSB mechanism in pion production at threshold (Ref. few, 1990s) and \(\rho\omega\) meson mixing as dominant for \(pp\) vs. \(nn\) differences (Ref. physrep, 1990s). The results enable future experimental tests at TRIUMF (Ref. e704, ongoing) with an anticipated resolution of 0.12% to constrain the quark-mass-difference parameters \(\delta m_N\) and \(\bar\delta m_N\), provided the uncertainties in \(\eta NN\) coupling can be resolved.
