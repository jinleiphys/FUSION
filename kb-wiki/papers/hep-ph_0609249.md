# Thermodynamics of the PNJL model with nonzero baryon and isospin chemical potentials

> We have extended the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for two degenerate flavours to include the isospin chemical potential ($μ_I$). All the diagonal and mixed derivatives of pressure with respect to the quark number (proportional to baryon number) chemical potential ($μ_0$) and isospin chemical potential upto sixth order have been extracted at $μ_0 = μ_I = 0$. These derivatives give the generalized susceptibilities with respect to quark and isospin numbers. Similar estimates for the flavour diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities are also presented. Comparison to Lattice QCD (LQCD) data of some of these susceptibilities for which LQCD data are available, show similar temperature dependence, though there are some quantitative deviations above the crossover temperature. We have also looked at the effects of instanton induced flavour-mixing coming from the $U_A(1)$ chiral symmetry breaking 't Hooft determinant like term in the NJL part of the model. The diagonal quark number and isospin susceptibilities are completely unaffected. The off-diagonal susceptibilities show significant dependence near the crossover. Finally we present the chemical potential dependence of specific heat and speed of sound within the limits of chemical potentials where neither diquarks nor pions can condense.

## Key claim

The PNJL model extended to include isospin chemical potential reproduces the qualitative temperature dependence of lattice QCD susceptibilities, but shows quantitative deviations above the crossover temperature, particularly for the isospin number susceptibility and the second-order flavour off-diagonal susceptibility, indicating that the model's Polyakov-loop dynamics overestimates the splitting between the Polyakov loop and its conjugate at high temperatures.

## Method

The authors extended the two-flavour Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model to include an isospin chemical potential μ_I alongside the quark number chemical potential μ_0. They computed all diagonal and mixed derivatives of pressure with respect to μ_0 and μ_I up to sixth order at μ_0 = μ_I = 0 by fitting the pressure as a function of both chemical potentials to a sixth-order polynomial, using data in the range 0 < μ_0 < 50 MeV and 0 < μ_I < 50 MeV with 0.1 MeV spacing. They also varied the instanton-induced flavour-mixing strength by tuning the parameter α in the NJL coupling parameterization G_1 = (1-α)G_0 and G_2 = α G_0, examining the extreme cases α = 0 (no mixing) and α = 1 (maximal mixing).

## Key numbers

* The crossover temperature at μ_0 = μ_I = 0 is T_c = 227 MeV, extracted from the peak in dΦ/dT.
* The Polyakov-loop potential parameter T_0 is set to 270 MeV, fitted from pure gauge lattice QCD data.
* The analysis is restricted to μ_I < 70 MeV and μ_0 < 200 MeV to avoid pion condensation and diquark formation.
* The least-squares error of all polynomial fits to the pressure was 10^{-14} or less.
* The second-order quark number susceptibility c_2 compares well with lattice QCD data, while the isospin number susceptibility c^I_2 reaches its ideal gas value around 2 T_c, whereas lattice QCD values remain lower and match c_2.
* The fourth-order coefficient c^I_4 is close to lattice QCD data for the full temperature range up to 2 T_c, while c_4 matches lattice data only up to T ~ 1.05 T_c and deviates significantly thereafter.
* The sixth-order coefficients c_6 and c^I_6 are both consistent with lattice QCD results.
* The second-order flavour off-diagonal susceptibility c^{ud}_2 remains significantly nonzero in the PNJL model up to 2 T_c, while lattice QCD data shows it is nearly zero away from T_c.
* All diagonal coefficients, including c^I_2 and c_4, are independent of the flavour-mixing parameter α.

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model studies of Mukherjee et al. (2006) and Ratti et al. (2006) by incorporating isospin chemical potential, enabling comparison with lattice QCD susceptibilities from Gavai and Gupta (2006). The results show that the PNJL model captures the qualitative behaviour of lattice data but has systematic discrepancies, particularly for the isospin susceptibility above T_c and the flavour off-diagonal susceptibility, suggesting that the Polyakov-loop dynamics may need modification or that additional couplings (such as isoscalar-vector and isovector-vector terms as studied by Redlich et al.) are required. The flavour-mixing analysis addresses the debate from Buballa (2005) and Toublan (2005) about whether instanton effects can suppress the splitting of u and d quark condensates at nonzero isospin chemical potential. The specific heat and speed of sound calculations provide input for hydrodynamic models of heavy-ion collisions and for locating the critical end point in the QCD phase diagram.
