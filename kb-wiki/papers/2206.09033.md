# Magnetic quadrupole transitions in the relativistic energy density functional theory

> Background: Magnetic quadrupole (M2) excitation represents a fundamental feature in atomic nucleus associated to nuclear magnetism induced by spin and orbital transition operator. So far it has only been investigated within the non-relativistic theoretical approaches, and available experimental data are rather limited. Purpose: We aim to investigate the properties of M2 transitions in closed and open-shell nuclei using the framework of relativistic nuclear energy density functional. The calculated M2 transition strengths could be used to constrain the quenching of the spin gyromagnetic factors. Methods: The M2 excitations are described using the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) with the residual interaction extended with the isovector-pseudovector term. Results: The M2 transition strength distributions are described and analyzed for closed and open shell nuclei. The results are compared with available experimental data and the strength missing from the experiment is discussed. The evolution of M2 transition properties has been investigated within the $^{36-64} \rm Ca$ isotope chain. Conclusion: The main M2 transitions have rather rich underlying structure and their collectivity increases with the mass number due to larger number of contributing particle-hole configurations. Pairing correlations in open shell nuclei have strong effect, causing the M2 strength reduction and shifting of the centroid energies to higher values. The analysis of M2 transition strengths indicate that considerable amount of experimental strength may be missing, mainly due to limitations to rather restricted energy ranges. The calculated M2 strengths for Ca isotopes, together with the future experimental data will allow constraining the quenching of the $g$ factors in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

This work provides the first relativistic-microscopic analysis of nuclear M2 transitions using the energy density functional framework, finding that the calculated M2 strengths for Ca isotopes could be used to constrain the quenching of spin gyromagnetic factors, but noting that considerable experimental strength may be missing due to limited energy ranges in measurements.

## Method

The nuclear ground state was calculated with the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model using point-coupling interactions (DD-PC1 and DD-PCX parameterizations), while M2 excitations were described using the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) with the residual interaction extended by an isovector-pseudovector (IV-PV) term. For open-shell nuclei, pairing correlations were described using the phenomenological Gogny interaction (D1S set). The IV-PV coupling constant was constrained by minimizing the relative error between experimental and theoretical M1 centroid energies for magic nuclei, yielding values of 104.41 MeV fm^3 for DD-PC1 and 124.11 MeV fm^3 for DD-PCX.

## Key numbers

- For O-16: total M2 strength sum up to 50 MeV is 1534.58 mu_N^2 fm^2 (full), with spin component 1492.2 mu_N^2 fm^2 and orbital component 44.3 mu_N^2 fm^2; centroid energy is 16.55 MeV
- For Ca-48: total M2 strength sum up to 50 MeV is 4915.5 mu_N^2 fm^2 (full); centroid energy is 15.48 MeV; energy-weighted moment m1 = 76.1 x 10^3 MeV mu_N^2 fm^2, compared to experimental value of 15.7 x 10^3 MeV mu_N^2 fm^2 up to 15 MeV
- For Pb-208: total M2 strength sum up to 50 MeV is 35648 mu_N^2 fm^2 (full); centroid energy is 11.87 MeV; summed strength up to 9.88 MeV is 11785 mu_N^2 fm^2, compared to experimental sum of 8500 +/- 750 mu_N^2 fm^2 between 6-8 MeV
- The IV-PV coupling constant for DD-PC1 interaction is alpha_IV-PV = 0.53 x 197 = 104.41 MeV fm^3; for DD-PCX interaction it is 0.63 x 197 = 124.11 MeV fm^3
- The Weisskopf single-particle M2 estimate is B_SP-M2 = 1.45 A^(2/3) mu_N^2 fm^2, giving 9.21 mu_N^2 fm^2 for O-16, 19.2 mu_N^2 fm^2 for Ca-48, and 50.9 mu_N^2 fm^2 for Pb-208

## Context

This work extends the relativistic energy density functional framework, previously applied to M1 transitions by Kruzic et al. (2021, 2022), to the study of M2 transitions for the first time. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between experimental M2 strengths and theoretical predictions from non-relativistic approaches such as shell model and RPA calculations by Cosel et al. (1995), and the quasiparticle phonon model study by Vdovin (1985). The results enable future experimental measurements of M2 strengths in Ca isotopes to constrain the quenching of spin gyromagnetic factors in nuclear medium, and suggest that beyond-RPA effects may be more essential for M2 than for M1 transitions.
