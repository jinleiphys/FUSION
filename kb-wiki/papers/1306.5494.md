# The study of Anisotropic Flows at LHC with purturbative simulation

> We study the harmonic flows, for example, the directed, elliptic, third and fourth flow of the system of partons formed just after relativistic heavy ion collisions. We calculate the minijets produced during the primary collisions using standard parton distributions for the incomming projectile and target nucleus. We solve the Boltzmann equations of motion for the system of minijets by Monte Carlo method within only the perturbative sector. Based on the flow data calculated, we conclude the simulation can not explain the experimental results at RHIC and LHC so that the nonperturbative sector plays much more important roles even from the earliest stage of heavy ion collisions.

# The study of Anisotropic Flows at LHC with perturbative simulation

## Key claim

The perturbative Monte Carlo simulation of minijet evolution, even with artificially enhanced cross sections (K-factor up to 20), cannot reproduce the measured directed, elliptic, third, or fourth harmonic flows at either RHIC or LHC energies, implying that nonperturbative dynamics dominate the system from the earliest stage of heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate primary minijet production from nucleon-nucleon collisions using standard parton distribution functions (CTEQ4 or GRV98) with nuclear modifications (EKS98 parametrization) and leading-order QCD scattering cross sections. They then solve the semi-classical Boltzmann equations via a Monte Carlo parton cascade simulation (PCC) that includes gluon radiation (gg → ggg) and other 2→2 and 2→3 perturbative processes, evolving the system up to 10 fm/c and extracting harmonic flow coefficients from the parton momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energy √s = 2.76 TeV, with Q₀ = 2.4 GeV and K = 1.5, the number of primary minijets is about 9,700 partons for b = 0, mostly gluons (using CTEQ4)
- At RHIC energy √s = 200 GeV, with Q₀ = 1.6 GeV and K = 2, the number of primary minijets is about 3,800 partons
- The minimum momentum transfer for small-angle scatterings is set at p_min = 0.3 GeV/c
- The QCD coupling constant is fixed at α_s = 0.3 throughout the simulation
- The total cross section for gg → gg with momentum cutoff is about 10/GeV², approximately 4 mb
- Perturbative collisions occur within 2 fm/c for both RHIC and LHC energies at b = 7 fm
- The impact parameter b = 7 fm corresponds to 20-30% centrality

## Context

This work extends the perturbative parton cascade approach of Shin (2002, 2003) and Geiger (1992) to the study of anisotropic flows at LHC energies. The null results for v₁, v₂, and v₄ contradict experimental measurements from ALICE at LHC and from BRAHMS, PHOBOS, STAR, and PHENIX at RHIC, which report significant elliptic flow. The authors note that 3+1D hydrodynamics (Schenke) incorporating viscosity successfully explains QGP evolution, while hadron transport formalisms like uRQMD describe hadronic gas evolution, but neither addresses the formation or hadronization questions. This work motivates inclusion of parton shower (1→2 branching) and color electromagnetic forces (Mrowczynski) as necessary improvements to the perturbative sector.
