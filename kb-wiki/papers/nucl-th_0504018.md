# Valence pairing, core deformation and the development of two-neutron halos

> We explore the evolution of the structure of the ground state of a nucleus with two valence nucleons as the system approaches the two particle threshold. We use a three-body model of $core+n+n$ where the core is deformed and allowed to excite. We find that both NN correlations and correlations due to deformation/excitation of the core inhibit the formation of halos. Our results suggest that it is unlikely to find halo nuclei on the dripline of deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that both neutron-neutron correlations (especially the tensor part of the NN interaction) and correlations due to core deformation/excitation inhibit the formation of two-neutron halos, and that it is unlikely to find halo nuclei on the dripline of deformed nuclei. The claim is hedged by the condition that the conclusions are based on a three-body model with core excitation, which is appropriate for qualitative features but not for quantitative predictions.

## Method

The author uses a three-body core+n+n model where the core is treated as a rotor with a quadrupole deformation parameter β₂, allowing the core to exist in its ground state and first 2⁺ excited state. The system is forced artificially toward the two-neutron threshold by scaling the strength of the core-n interaction (Woods-Saxon plus spin-orbit) with a factor λ, and the ground-state wavefunction is expanded in hyperspherical coordinates. The probability of the K=0 hyper-momentum component is used as the criterion for halo formation, with K=0 > 50% considered necessary for a two-neutron halo.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹²Be-based system with both NN interaction and core deformation (β₂=0.67), the K=0 probability saturates at lim_{S₂n→0} P(K=0) ≈ 0.3 (30%), well below the 50% halo threshold.
- With core deformation only (β₂=0.67, no NN interaction), the K=0 probability saturates at lim_{S₂n→0} P(K=0) ≈ 0.4 (40%).
- With NN interaction only (β₂=0), the K=0 probability saturates at lim_{S₂n→0} P(K=0) ≈ 0.6 (60%).
- With neither NN interaction nor deformation, the K=0 probability saturates at lim_{S₂n→0} P(K=0) ≈ 0.8 (80%).
- For the A=100 core with β₂=0.3, including both NN interaction and deformation gives lim_{S₂n→0} P(K=0) ≈ 0.5 (50%), at the border line for halo formation.
- The hyper-radius scale for the ¹²Be example is ρ²₀ = 53 fm², and the halo condition is ⟨ρ²⟩/ρ²₀ > 2.
- The GPT NN interaction enhances the l_{y2}=0 (dineutron) component by ≈ 3% in the ¹²Be-based system.

## Context

This work extends the three-body model with core excitation developed by Nunes and collaborators for ¹¹Be, ¹²Be, and ¹⁴Be (Nunes, 2004; Nunes, 2005) to systematically study the effect of pairing and deformation on halo formation. It contradicts earlier single-particle Nilsson model results by Misu (1997) and by another group (2001, 2002) which suggested that deformation does not hinder one-neutron halo formation. The paper also engages with Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov studies of pairing and halos by other authors (2000, 2001, 2002), noting that the tensor part of the NN interaction (which reduces halo probability) goes beyond the S-wave pairing typically included in mean-field approaches. The results enable future fully self-consistent mean-field calculations that include both pairing and deformation, as planned with the work of Duguet (2004).
