# The decay $J/ψ\toγϕϕ$: spin dependence of amplitude and angular distributions of photons with linear polarizations

> Based on the effective invariant amplitudes of the transitions $J/ψ\toγX(J^P)$ and $X(J^P)\toϕϕ$, the spin dependence of the $J/ψ\toγX(J^P)\toγϕϕ$ decay amplitude is obtained in the case of the intermediate resonances $X$ with $J^P=0^\pm,1^\pm,2^\pm$. Angular distributions of photons with the definite linear polarizations relative to the plane spanned by the momentum of initial electron and the total momentum of the $ϕϕ$ pair in the reaction $e^+e^-\to J/ψ\toγX(J^P)\toγϕϕ$ are calculated. It is shown that the sign of the asymmetry of the distributions of the photons polarized in the above plane and orthogonal to it correlates with the signature $P_X(-1)^{J_X}$ of the $X$ resonance with given spin-parity $J_X^{P_X}$ so it may help to establish this quantum number in a way which does not depend on the specific model of the $X(J^P)\toϕϕ$ amplitude.

## Key claim

The sign of the asymmetry between angular distributions of photons linearly polarized in the plane spanned by the initial electron momentum and the total momentum of the \(\phi\phi\) pair, versus orthogonal to that plane, correlates with the signature \(P_X(-1)^{J_X}\) of the intermediate resonance \(X\) in the decay chain \(e^+e^-\to J/\psi\to\gamma X(J^P)\to\gamma\phi\phi\), providing a model-independent way to determine the spin-parity of the \(X\) resonance.

## Method

The author constructed effective invariant amplitudes for the transitions \(J/\psi\to\gamma X(J^P)\) and \(X(J^P)\to\phi\phi\) for intermediate resonances with \(J^P=0^\pm,1^\pm,2^\pm\). Using these amplitudes, he calculated the angular distributions of linearly polarized photons in the reaction \(e^+e^-\to J/\psi\to\gamma X(J^P)\to\gamma\phi\phi\), expressed in terms of independent helicity amplitudes of the radiative decay and amplitudes of the \(X(J^P)\to\phi\phi\) decay with given spin and orbital angular momentum. The asymmetry between the two linear polarization states was then evaluated, and numerical estimates were obtained using resonance parameters extracted from fits to BESIII data.

## Key numbers

* The asymmetry for a pure \(0^+\) resonance is \(A^{(0^+)}(\theta_\gamma)=\sin^2\theta_\gamma/(1+\cos^2\theta_\gamma)>0\); for a pure \(0^-\) resonance it is \(A^{(0^-)}(\theta_\gamma)=-\sin^2\theta_\gamma/(1+\cos^2\theta_\gamma)<0\).
* The combined spin-zero asymmetry, using relative production rates \(N^{(0^+)}=63\) and \(N^{(0^-)}=708\) from fits to BESIII data, is \(A^{(0^++0^-)}(\theta_\gamma)=-0.84\sin^2\theta_\gamma/(1+\cos^2\theta_\gamma)\).
* The maximum asymmetry for a pure \(2^+\) resonance is \(A^{(2^+)}_{\text{max}}=0.10\pm0.02\).
* The integrated asymmetry for a pure \(2^+\) resonance is \(A^{(2^+)}_{\text{int}}=0.07\pm0.01\), compared to \(A^{(0^\pm)}_{\text{int}}=\pm1/2\) for spin-zero resonances.

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals by Chang (1979), Chang (1978), and Trueman (1978) to use the \(\phi\phi\) decay mode for parity determination of resonances, specifically addressing the difficulty that the \(K^+K^-\) decay planes from \(\phi\to K^+K^-\) cannot be attributed to a specific \(\phi\) meson due to interference. The author provides a theoretical framework for using linearly polarized photons in the reaction \(e^+e^-\to J/\psi\to\gamma\phi\phi\) as an alternative to the standard partial wave analysis, enabling a model-independent determination of the resonance signature. The numerical estimates use resonance parameters extracted from fits to BESIII data (2016) by Kozhevnikov (2019).
