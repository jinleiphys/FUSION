# Plutonium-241 as a possible isotope for neutrino mass measurement and capture

> Tritium has been the isotope of choice for measurements of the neutrino mass and planned detection of the relic neutrino background. The low mass of $^{3}$H leads to large recoil energy of the nucleus. This has emerged as a limiting factor for both measurements. We investigate $^{241}$Pu as an alternative. The recoil is 80x smaller and it has similar decay energy and lifetime as $^{3}$H. We evaluate for the first time its soft-neutrino capture cross-section and find $(σv)_ν = 1.52 \times 10^{-45}$. This is 40% of the capture cross-section for tritium and makes $^{241}$Pu an interesting alternative for $^{3}$H.

## Key claim

Plutonium-241 is a promising alternative to tritium for neutrino mass measurement and cosmic neutrino background detection, provided the background from its daughter isotope uranium-237 can be removed or vetoed; the energy uncertainty from zero-point motion is 25 times smaller than for tritium, potentially allowing CNB observation for neutrino masses above 30 meV.

## Method

The neutrino capture cross-section for Pu-241 was extracted from a precisely measured beta decay spectrum (Loidl 2010) using the analytical extrapolation method of Mikulenko (2021), which relates the differential beta decay rate at low neutrino momentum to the capture cross-section. As a cross-check, a spectrum was generated using the BetaShape program (Mougeot 2014) with 0.06 eV energy bins, and the same procedure was validated against known results for Sm-151, Tm-171, and Ni-63.

## Key numbers

- Pu-241 neutrino capture cross-section: (σv)ν = 1.52 × 10⁻⁴⁵ cm², which is 40% of the tritium capture cross-section
- Pu-241 recoil energy: 47 meV, 80 times smaller than tritium's 3.4 eV recoil
- Pu-241 decay energy: 20.78 keV, half-life: 14.4 years
- Pu-241 γ factor (zero-point motion figure of merit): 0.039 relative to tritium (γ/γ_³H), 25 times smaller than tritium
- Pu-241 α decay branching ratio to U-237: 2.4 × 10⁻⁵, with U-237 half-life of 6.7 days and beta decay energy up to 459 keV
- BetaShape calculation gave (σv)ν = 1.34 × 10⁻⁴⁵ cm², within 10% of the experimental result
- For comparison: tritium (σv)ν = 39.2 × 10⁻⁴⁶ cm², Tm-171 (σv)ν = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴⁶ cm², Sm-151 (σv)ν = 0.048 × 10⁻⁴⁶ cm²

## Context

This work extends the candidate isotope search for neutrino mass measurement and CNB detection beyond tritium, which has been the standard choice used by KATRIN (2022) and planned for Project8 and PTOLEMY. The study directly addresses the zero-point motion energy spread problem identified by Cheipesh (2021), who advocated for nuclei with A>100 and proposed Tm-171 and Sm-151 as alternatives. The neutrino capture cross-section calculation follows the method of Mikulenko (2021) and is compared to earlier calculations by Cocco (2008) for tritium and Ni-63. The result enables experimental designs using Pu-241 as a replacement for tritium, with the caveat that the U-237 background must be mitigated through recoil removal or gamma veto.
