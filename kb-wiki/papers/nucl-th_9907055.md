# Fractal Structure of Random Matrices

> A multifractal analysis is performed on the universality classes of random matrices and the transition ones.Our results indicate that the eigenvector probability distribution is a linear sum of two chi-squared distribution throughout the transition between the universality ensembles of random matrix theory and Poisson .

## Key claim

The eigenvector probability distribution throughout the transition between random matrix theory (RMT) ensembles and Poisson is a linear sum of two chi-squared distributions, not a single chi-squared distribution as previously proposed by Alhassid and Levine (1986).

## Method

The authors performed a multifractal analysis on an interpolating random matrix ensemble generated by the Maximum Entropy Principle, where a parameter beta controls the transition from the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) to Poisson. For N=2 matrices, they derived the component probability distribution analytically and computed the dimension spectrum D_q and f(alpha)-spectrum. For larger matrices (N=10 and N=100), they performed numerical simulations, extrapolating the dimension function to the limit of vanishing cell size l using results from two small l values.

## Key numbers

- For N=2 matrices in the GOE limit (beta=0), the dimension function D_q equals 1 for q<2 and q/[2(q-1)] for q>2, with f(alpha) spectrum points at alpha=1 with f(alpha)=1 and alpha=1/2 with f(alpha)=0
- For N=2 matrices with large beta (approaching Poisson), D_q equals 1 for q<1 and 0 for q>1, with f(alpha) spectrum points at alpha=1 with f(alpha)=1 and alpha=0 with f(alpha)=0
- For GOE with general N, the dimension function shows three regimes: D_q = (N-1)/2 * q/(q-1) for q<-2/(N-3), D_q = 1 for -2/(N-3)<q<2, and D_q = 1/2 * q/(q-1) for q>2
- Numerical simulations were performed for matrices of dimension N=100 and N=10

## Context

This work extends the multifractal analysis, previously applied to the metal-insulator transition in condensed matter physics by Castellani and Peliti (1986) and Janssen (1994), to the abstract space of random matrices. The interpolating ensemble between RMT and Poisson, generated by the Maximum Entropy Principle, was introduced by Hussein and Pato (1993, 1998). The results provide theoretical support for the empirical observation by Pato, Nunes, Lima, Hussein, and Alhassid (1994) that a combination of two chi-squared distributions is needed to describe the transition, contradicting the single chi-squared distribution proposal of Alhassid and Levine (1986). The analysis enables a thermodynamic interpretation of the chaos-order transition as a first-order phase transition in the chaoticity parameter.
