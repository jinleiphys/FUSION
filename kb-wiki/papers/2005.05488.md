# Electrical neutrality and $β$-equilibrium conditions in dense quark matter: generation of charged pion condensation by chiral imbalance

> The phase diagram of dense quark matter with chiral imbalance is considered with the conditions of electric neutrality and $β$-equilibrium. It has been shown recently that chiral imbalance can generate charged pion condensation in dense quark matter, so it was interesting to verify that this phenomenon takes place in realistic physical scenarios such as electrically neutral matter in $β$-equilibrium, because a window of pion condensation at dense quark matter phase diagram (without chiral imbalance) predicted earlier was closed by the consideration of these conditions at the physical current quark mass. In this paper it has been shown that the charged pion condensation is generated by chiral imbalance in the dense electric neutral quark/baryonic matter in $β$-equilibrium, i. e. matter in neutron stars. It has been also demonstrated that pion condensation is inevitable phenomenon in dense quark matter with chiral imbalance if there is non-zero chiral imbalance in two forms, chiral and chiral isospin one. It seems that in this case pion condensation phase can be hardly avoided by any physical constraint on isopin imbalance and that this conclusion can be probably generalized from neutron star matter to the matter produced in heavy ion collisions or in neutron star mergers. The chiral limit and the physical piont (physical pion mass) has been considered and it was shown that the appearance of pion condensation is not much affected by the consideration of non-zero current quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that charged pion condensation, which is absent in electrically neutral dense quark matter under β-equilibrium at the physical pion mass, can be generated by chiral imbalance (nonzero chiral isospin chemical potential μI5 and/or chiral chemical potential μ5) and that this condensation is "inevitable" when both forms of chiral imbalance are present, even under realistic neutron star conditions.

## Method

The authors use a (3+1)-dimensional Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors (u and d) and electrons, incorporating baryon, isospin, chiral, and chiral isospin chemical potentials, under the constraints of electric charge neutrality and β-equilibrium (which forces μI = 0). They compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation at zero temperature, studying the phase structure both in the chiral limit (m0 = 0) and at the physical current quark mass (m0 = 5.5 MeV), and determine the ground state by minimizing the thermodynamic potential with respect to the σ and π condensates.

## Key numbers

- The physical current quark mass used is m0 = 5.5 MeV.
- At nonzero chiral isospin chemical potential μI5 and chiral chemical potential μ5, charged pion condensation occurs for values of these potentials larger than the pion mass (approximately 140 MeV), and the nonzero m0 does not significantly affect the results.
- The paper states that without chiral imbalance, charged pion condensation is absent in electrically neutral dense quark matter under β-equilibrium at the physical point (m0 = 5.5 MeV), consistent with prior work by Abuki (2005).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Khunjua, Klimenko, and Zhokhov (2018–2019) that showed chiral imbalance can generate charged pion condensation in dense quark matter, but those studies did not include electric neutrality or β-equilibrium constraints. It also addresses the earlier finding by Abuki (2005) that charged pion condensation in neutral dense matter disappears at the physical current quark mass. The results suggest that charged pion condensation is robust in neutron star matter and may also occur in heavy ion collisions and neutron star mergers, provided chiral imbalance exists.
