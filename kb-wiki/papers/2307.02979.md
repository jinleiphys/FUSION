# Relativistic mean field model for ultra-compact low mass neutron star of HESS J1731-347

> The recent observation of the object HESS J1731-347 suggests the existence of a very light and very compact neutron star being a challenge for commonly used equation of state for dense matter. In this work we present a relativistic mean field model enriched with meson crossing terms among isovector and isoscalar mesons. Such interactions particularly dominate the behavior of the symmetry energy and accounts for small size of compact star radius. The proposed model fulfill the recent constraints concerning the symmetry energy slope and state-of-the-art compact stars constraints derived from the NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 pulsars as well as from the GW170817 event and its associated electromagnetic counterparts AT2017gfo/GRB170817A.

## Key claim

The authors present a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with meson crossing terms that can simultaneously reproduce the very small radius of HESS J1731-347 (a low-mass, ultra-compact neutron star candidate) while satisfying constraints from NICER pulsar measurements, GW170817, and nuclear experiments, provided the symmetry energy slope parameter L is around 40 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a hadronic RMF model (named CCT, Cracow Crossing Terms) that includes standard σ, ω, ρ, and δ mesons plus additional quadratic coupling terms between σ-δ and ω-δ mesons. They fit the isoscalar coupling constants to symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties (n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, K₀ = 230 MeV) and treat Cσ as a free parameter (12–14 fm²) to control stiffness, while the isovector constants are adjusted to achieve specific values of the symmetry energy slope L (40, 60, or 80 MeV). The model is then used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, with the Sly4 model for the crust and Maxwell construction for phase transitions.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy slope L = 40 MeV is required to reproduce the ultra-compact nature of HESS J1731-347, with values of 60 and 80 MeV also tested
* Saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and compressibility K₀ = 230 MeV used to fit isoscalar constants
* Cσ coupling constant varied between 12 and 14 fm² to ensure maximum neutron star mass above 2.1 M⊙
* The crossing term constants are fixed at Λσδ = 0.032 and Λωδ = 0.02
* Symmetry energy at saturation fixed at E_sym(n₀) = 30 MeV
* The model with Cσ = 14 fm² and L = 40 MeV best satisfies the radius constraint from the 1.4 M⊙ star and the tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 and GW190814

## Context

This work addresses the challenge posed by the HESS J1731-347 observation (Doroshenko 2022), which suggests a very light and compact neutron star that most standard equations of state cannot reproduce. The authors extend their earlier RMF model with σ-δ crossing terms (Zabari 2019) by adding ω-δ coupling, following similar work by Miyatsu (2022). The model is compared against APR (Akmal 1998), DD2, DD2F (Typel 2014), BSK20 (Chamel 2011), FSUGarnet, IUFSU, and BigApple (Fattoyev 2020) equations of state. The results enable future studies of cooling properties (Sagun 2023) and rotational configurations of compact stars within this framework.
