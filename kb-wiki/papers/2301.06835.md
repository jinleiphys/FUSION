# Measurement of the $^{236}$U fission cross section and angular distributions of fragments from fission of $^{235}$U and $^{236}$U in the neutron energy range of 0.3-500 MeV

> The $^{236}$U fission cross section and the angular distributions of fragments from fission of $^{235}$U and $^{236}$U were measured for incident neutron energies from 0.3 MeV to 500 MeV on the time-of-flight spectrometer of the neutron complex GNEIS at the NRC "Kurchatov Institute" -- PNPI. Fission fragments were registered using position-sensitive low-pressure multiwire counters. In the neutron energy range above 20 MeV, the angular distributions of $^{236}$U fission fragments were measured for the first time. The fission cross section of $^{236}$U$(n,f)$ was measured relative to the fission cross section of $^{235}$U$(n,f)$, which is an accepted international standard. The obtained data are compared with the results of other experimental works. Theoretical calculations of the fission cross section and the anisotropy of angular distribution of fission fragments for the $^{236}$U$(n,f)$ reaction performed within the framework of our approach are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurements of $^{236}$U fission fragment angular distributions for neutron energies above 20 MeV, up to 500 MeV, while also measuring the $^{236}$U fission cross section relative to the $^{235}$U standard cross section in the same experiment. The authors hedge that the accuracy of existing theoretical approaches does not meet the needs of new nuclear technologies, and that their measurements aim to reduce total uncertainty of estimated data.

## Method

Fission fragments from $^{235}$U and $^{236}$U targets were registered simultaneously using an assembly of two position-sensitive low-pressure multiwire proportional counters (MWPCs) on the time-of-flight spectrometer of the GNEIS neutron complex at the NRC "Kurchatov Institute" -- PNPI. The $^{236}$U fission cross section was measured relative to the $^{235}$U$(n,f)$ international standard cross section, with the experiment performed for two orientations of the setup relative to the neutron beam to account for momentum transfer effects. Neutron energy was determined by the time-of-flight method over a 36.50(5) m flight path, and fission events were selected using waveform digitizer data with corrections for detector efficiency calculated via Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key numbers

- Neutron energy range covered: 0.3 MeV to 500 MeV
- Flight path length: 36.50(5) m
- $^{235}$U target mass in the measured circular area: 3.480(48) mg (relative statistical uncertainty 0.9%)
- $^{236}$U target mass in the measured circular area: 5.796(44) mg (relative statistical uncertainty 0.2%)
- Average substance thickness in selected circles: 192(3) $\mu$g/cm$^2$ for $^{235}$U and 320(2) $\mu$g/cm$^2$ for $^{236}$U
- Maximum registration angle: $\theta_{max} \simeq 71^{\circ}$ ($\cos\theta > 0.325$)
- Spatial resolution of MWPCs: 2 mm in x and y coordinates
- Total detector efficiency: approximately 45%
- Working gas pressure: 8 mbar isobutane C$_4$H$_{10}$
- Neutron beam diameter: 90 mm
- Integral neutron flux for beam No. 5: $4.2 \times 10^5$ n/(cm$^2$ s) for neutrons with energies between 1-1000 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of $^{236}$U fission cross sections, which were mostly limited to neutron energies below 40 MeV (Meadows 1978, Fursov 1985, Goverdovskii 1985, Kanda 1986, Meadows 1988, Diakaki 2020) with only two studies reaching 200-400 MeV (Lisowski 1992, Tovesson 2014). The angular distribution data for $^{236}$U$(n,f)$ were previously only available for neutrons from 0.4 to 15 MeV (Simmons 1960, Leachman 1965, Huizenga 1969, Shpak 1991). The new measurements provide data needed for accelerator-driven systems (ADS) design and for improving theoretical models of fission at intermediate and high neutron energies.
