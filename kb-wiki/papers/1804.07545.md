# Diffusion monte carlo calculations of three-body systems

> Application of diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm in three-body systems is studied. We develop a program and use it to calculate the property of various three-body systems. Regular Coulomb systems such as atoms, molecules and ions are investigated. Calculation is then extended to exotic systems where electrons are replaced by muons. Some nuclei with neutron halos are also calculated as three-body systems consisting of a core and two external nucleons. Our results agree well with experiments and others' work.

## Key claim

The diffusion Monte Carlo method, when applied to three-body systems without the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, yields ground-state energies that agree with experiments and high-precision variational calculations to within 0.05% for most Coulomb systems, though the hydrogen molecular ion shows a larger error of about 0.6% due to the simplicity of the trial wave function.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrödinger equation using a diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm with importance sampling, where walkers evolve according to a short-time Green's function that accounts for particles of different masses. Trial wave functions are optimized by variational Monte Carlo, then used to guide the diffusion and branching of 2000 initial walkers over many iterations, with results extrapolated to zero time step to eliminate time-step error. Calculations are performed for Coulomb systems (atoms, molecules, ions), muonic systems (where electrons are replaced by muons), and Yukawa three-body systems (halo nuclei treated as a core plus two external nucleons), all without the Born-Oppenheimer approximation.

## Key numbers

- Helium atom (⁴He²⁺e⁻e⁻): DMC ground-state energy = -2.902 a.u., matching the experimental value of -2.902 a.u. (error 0.05%)
- Hydrogen molecular ion (p⁺p⁺e⁻): DMC ground-state energy = -0.5938 a.u., compared to experimental value -0.5974 a.u. (error 0.6%)
- Lithium ion (⁷Li³⁺e⁻e⁻): DMC ground-state energy = -7.275 a.u., compared to Ancarani's result -7.279 a.u.
- Muonic system p⁺μ⁻μ⁻: DMC ground-state energy = -96.97 a.u., compared to Frolov's result -97.57 a.u.
- Muonic system p⁺p⁺μ⁻: DMC ground-state energy = -101.8 a.u., compared to Bailey's result -96.86 a.u. (DMC result is lower)
- ¹¹Li halo nucleus: DMC ground-state energy = -0.59 MeV, matter RMS radius = 2.83 fm; experimental values are -0.35(±0.05) MeV and 3.10(±0.17) fm
- ¹⁴Be halo nucleus: DMC ground-state energy = -1.18 MeV, matter RMS radius = 2.78 fm; experimental values are -1.12(±0.20) MeV and 3.10(±0.30) fm
- ¹⁷B halo nucleus: DMC ground-state energy = -1.09 MeV, matter RMS radius = 2.73 fm; experimental values are -1.49(±0.20) MeV and 3.00(±0.40) fm

## Context

This work extends the diffusion Monte Carlo method, previously applied to positronium-atom complexes and light nuclei, to a unified treatment of Coulomb three-body systems (atoms, molecules, ions), muonic systems, and halo nuclei without the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The results for halo nuclei (¹¹Li, ¹⁴Be, ¹⁷B) improve upon the equivalent two-body variational methods of Ren (2004) and agree well with the Faddeev equation results of Chu (2007), while offering the advantage that diffusion Monte Carlo can be extended to many-body systems where Faddeev equations become intractable.
