# Comparison between LQCD and PNJL model at finite chemical potentials

> Lattice QCD has the sign problem at real quark chemical potential. There are some regions with no sign problem; one is the imaginary quark chemical potential region and the others are the real and imaginary isospin chemical potential regions. We show that the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can reproduce LQCD data in the regions. We also determine the model parameters from the data and predict the QCD phase diagram in the real quark chemical potential region.

# Comparison between LQCD and PNJL model at finite chemical potentials

## Key claim

The Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can reproduce lattice QCD data in the sign-problem-free regions (imaginary quark chemical potential, real and imaginary isospin chemical potential), but only when the model includes a specific Polyakov-loop potential (RRW-type) and additional four-quark and eight-quark interactions; the model then predicts the QCD phase diagram at real quark chemical potential, though the prediction remains untested by direct lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors compare the two-flavor PNJL model in the mean-field approximation with lattice QCD data in three regions free of the sign problem: imaginary quark chemical potential (mu_q = i theta_q T), imaginary isospin chemical potential, and real isospin chemical potential. They test two forms of the Polyakov-loop potential (RRW-type and F-type) and determine model parameters by fitting to lattice QCD data at imaginary mu_q, then use those parameters to predict the phase diagram at real mu_q. For the imaginary isospin region, they also compare with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model below T_c.

## Key numbers

- The RW transition temperature T_RW = 190 MeV, above which the RW phase transition appears at theta_q = pi/3 mod 2pi/3
- Lattice QCD data at T = 175 MeV and T = 250 MeV are used for comparison of Omega/T^4, n_q/T^3, and n_I/T^3 as functions of theta_q and theta_I
- The PNJL model with RRW-type U_Phi reproduces lattice QCD data at finite theta_q, while the model with F-type U_Phi does not
- The RW endpoint is first order for RRW-type U_Phi, consistent with lattice QCD data for small quark mass, but second order for F-type U_Phi

## Context

This work extends the comparison between effective models and lattice QCD beyond the mu_q = 0 line, building on the Roberge-Weiss periodicity (Roberge and Weiss, 1986) and earlier PNJL model studies (Fukushima, 2004; Rossner et al., 2007). The authors show that the PNJL model with RRW-type Polyakov potential and scalar-type eight-quark interactions reproduces lattice QCD data from multiple lattice groups (LQCD-1, LQCD-2, LQCD-3, LQCD-4) across all sign-problem-free regions. This enables prediction of the QCD phase diagram at real quark chemical potential, including the location of the chiral critical endpoint (CEP) and the tricritical point (TCP) for pion superfluidity, which are relevant for compact star physics and heavy-ion collisions.
