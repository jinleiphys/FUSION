# MARTINI for heavy quarks: initialization, parton evolution, and hadronization with event generation

> We present additions to the MARTINI event generator for examining heavy quarks and quarkonia in heavy-ion collisions. All stages of a heavy-ion collision affect the observables associated with heavy quarks: the initial phase space of the heavy quarks are sampled with PYTHIA8.1, the heavy quarks are evolved using Langevin dynamics and a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamical description of the heavy-ion collision, and are fragmented and hadronized using a modified version of the color evaporation model that takes into account non-trivial evolution in position space, as well as the possibility of recombinant quarkonium production in heavy-ion collisions. We use this to re-examine the production of quarkonium at RHIC, and anticipating vertex detection we predict yields of B_c mesons at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents additions to the MARTINI event generator that enable full event-by-event simulation of heavy quarks and quarkonia in heavy-ion collisions, including recombinant quarkonium production, which the authors find to be larger than previous semi-analytic estimates because event generation correctly accounts for fluctuations in charm quark number and spatial correlations.

## Method

Heavy quark pairs are initialized using PYTHIA8.1 with EKS98 nuclear parton distribution functions and Glauber model spatial sampling, then evolved through a 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamical medium using relativistic Langevin dynamics with a temperature-dependent potential derived from lattice QCD internal energy. Hadronization uses a modified color evaporation model that checks invariant mass thresholds for direct quarkonium formation, then considers recombinant pairing of heavy quarks from different hard processes weighted by Boltzmann factors, and finally applies Peterson fragmentation for open heavy flavor mesons.

## Key numbers

- Charm production cross sections: 0.6 μb for proton-proton collisions at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV) and 3.0 μb for the LHC (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV)
- Spatial diffusion coefficient: 2πT D_HQ = 3.0 used in all simulations
- Kinetic freezeout temperatures examined: T_fo = 190 MeV and T_fo = 135 MeV for open charm and quarkonium
- In a typical central gold-gold collision at RHIC approximately 20 heavy quark pairs are produced; at the LHC approximately 100 pairs
- Pseudorapidity cuts: |η| < 0.35 for PHENIX detector at RHIC; CMS detector cuts at LHC
- B_c meson yields predicted at both RHIC and LHC energies as a function of centrality, with the authors stating heavy-ion experiments can be the main means for studying these rare states

## Context

This work extends the MARTINI event generator, previously used for jets and photons (Schenke 2009, Young 2011, Dion 2011), to heavy quarks and quarkonia. It builds on prior calculations of J/ψ suppression using heavy quark diffusion with 2+1-dimensional hydrodynamics (Young 2008, Young 2009) and on the color evaporation model (Fritzsch 1978, Amundson 1996). The results contradict the expectation that sequential quarkonium dissociation would be clearly observable at the LHC, instead finding that recombinant production of excited charmonium leads to an increase in R_AA with N_part. The paper enables future work eliminating the diffusive approximation for heavy quarks and including radiative energy loss effects at LHC momenta.
