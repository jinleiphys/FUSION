# Weak binding effects on the structure of $^{40}$Mg

> While the phenomenon of one- and two-neutron ground-state halo nuclei is well established, the effects of weak binding on nuclear excitation properties remain largely unexplored. Motivated by this question and by recent data in $^{40}$Mg we investigate the coupling of weakly bound (halo) valence neutrons to a core using the known properties of $^{40}$Mg to explore and illustrate possible particle-core coupling schemes and their impact on the low-lying excitation spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that weak binding in a potential two-neutron halo nucleus like \(^{40}\)Mg can qualitatively alter the low-lying excitation spectrum by reducing the coupling between the valence neutrons and the deformed core, leading to a regime of weak particle-surface coupling where the core and halo degrees of freedom mix strongly only when their characteristic energy scales become comparable.

## Method

The authors use a phenomenological Weak Coupling Model and the Particle-Rotor Model to describe the coupling of two weakly bound \(p_{3/2}\) valence neutrons to a deformed \(^{38}\)Mg core. They diagonalize a 2×2 matrix for the \(2^+\) states as a function of the two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}\), and fit the mixing matrix element and binding energy to reproduce the observed \(\gamma\)-ray energies. They also compute relative reaction cross sections from a one-proton knockout reaction and predict ratios of \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The two observed \(\gamma\)-ray transitions in \(^{40}\)Mg are 500(14) keV and 670(16) keV, assigned as \(2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+\) and \(2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+\), respectively.
- The mass table evaluation gives an expected \(S_{2n}\) for \(^{40}\)Mg of \(667 \pm 706\) keV.
- The fitted mixing matrix element between core and two-neutron states is \(V_{nn-core} = 69\) keV, with a best-fit \(S_{2n} = 877\) keV.
- The wavefunction amplitudes are \(\alpha = 0.89\) (core component) and \(\beta = 0.45\) (two-neutron component) for the \(2^+_1\) state.
- The predicted gyromagnetic factor for the \(2_1^+\) state is \(g(2^+_1) \approx -0.14~\mu_N\).
- In the Particle-Rotor Model, the pairing gap in \(^{40}\)Mg is reduced to \(2\Delta \approx 0.75\) MeV, half of that in \(^{38}\)Mg.

## Context

This work extends the study of halo phenomena beyond ground-state properties (established by Tanihata (1985) and Hansen and Jonson (1987)) into the domain of nuclear excitation spectra, motivated by recent data on \(^{40}\)Mg from Crawford (2019). It builds on the particle-plus-core formalism of Fossez (2016) and the Weak Coupling Model of Arima (1971), and contrasts with other theoretical approaches to \(^{40}\)Mg such as those of Tsunoda and Suzuki (2021). The results enable future experimental tests via mass measurements, interaction cross sections, Coulomb excitation, and magnetic moment measurements to confirm the halo nature and weak-coupling regime.
