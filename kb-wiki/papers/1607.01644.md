# Dynamical scheme for hadronization with first-order phase transition

> We present a dynamical scheme for hadronization with first-order confinement phase transition. The thermodynamical conditions of phase equilibrium, the fluid velocity profile, and the dissipative effect determine the macroscopic changes of the parton volume and the corresponding hadron volume during the phase transition. The macroscopic volume changes are the basis for building up a dynamical scheme by considering microscopic transition processes from partons to hadrons and backwards. The established scheme is proved by comparing the numerical results with the analytical solutions in the case of a one-dimensional expansion of a dissipative fluid with Bjorken boost invariance. The comparisons show almost perfect agreements, which demonstrate the applicability of the introduced scheme.

## Key claim

The authors present a dynamical scheme for first-order confinement phase transition from gluons to pions, but explicitly assume this first-order transition despite acknowledging that the QCD transition at zero baryon chemical potential is actually a crossover; the scheme is validated only for the simplified case of one-dimensional Bjorken expansion with constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Method

The scheme derives macroscopic volume changes of gluon and pion phases from Gibbs phase equilibrium conditions combined with hydrodynamic equations including shear viscosity, then uses these macroscopic changes to determine microscopic transition probabilities for processes \(g+g \to \pi+\pi+\pi\), \(g+g \to \pi+\pi\), and their back reactions. The transition probabilities and an energy amplification factor \(x\) (accounting for latent heat from the bag constant) are solved from coupled equations for particle number and energy balance, with one free parameter set to zero. The scheme is implemented stochastically in the BAMPS parton cascade framework and tested against analytical solutions for a one-dimensional Bjorken-expanding dissipative fluid.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant: \(B^{1/4} = 0.23\ \text{GeV}\)
- Gluon degeneracy factor: \(d_g = 16\); pion degeneracy factor: \(d_\pi = 3\)
- Transition temperature \(T_c\) is given by Eq. (tc): \(T_c = \left\{ e^{-\mu_g(\tau_0)/T_g(\tau_0)} [T_g(\tau_0)]^{1/(r_g-1)-3} \frac{\pi^2 B}{d_g - d_\pi} \right\}^{1 - 1/r_g}\), where \(r_g = 4/3 - 8/[15 n_g(\tau_0) \tau_0 \sigma_g]\)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio at \(\tau_c\): \(\eta_g(\tau_c)/s_g(\tau_c) = \frac{6(d_g-d_\pi)}{5d_g} \frac{T_c^2}{\sigma_g B} \frac{1}{4 - \mu_g(\tau_0)/T_g(\tau_0) - \frac{4-3r_g}{r_g-1} \ln[T_c/T_g(\tau_0)]}\)
- Pion cross section related to gluon cross section: \(\sigma_\pi = (d_g/d_\pi) \sigma_g = (16/3) \sigma_g\)

## Context

This work extends the BAMPS parton cascade (Xu, 2004) by adding a dynamical hadronization interface, differing from hadronization procedures in AMPT (Lin, 2004) and PHSD (Cassing, 2008). The scheme is intended as a first step toward a full description of first-order phase transitions from quarks and gluons to mesons and baryons at finite baryonic chemical potential, and will enable future examination of the Cooper-Frye prescription (Cooper, 1974) used in viscous hydrodynamic calculations.
