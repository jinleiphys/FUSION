# Effect of pairing on transfer and fusion reactions

> In the present contribution, the effect of pairing on nuclear transfer and fusion reactions close to the Coulomb barrier is discussed. A Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock + BCS (TDHF+BCS) microscopic theory has been developed to incorporate pairing. One- and two-particle transfer probabilities can be obtained showing the importance of pairing. The calculated transfer probabilities are compared to the recent experimental results obtained for the $^{96}$Zr+$^{40}$Ca. Reactions involving the $^{18}$O with lead isotopes are also presented, that are also of current experimental interest. Finally, a study of the fusion barrier height predicted with the TDHF+BCS theory is compared to the experimental values for the $^{40,44,48}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca reactions.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations enhance two-particle transfer probabilities in sub-barrier nuclear reactions, but this enhancement is primarily due to the fragmentation of single-particle states (reducing Pauli blocking) rather than the anomalous density components; however, including pairing alone is insufficient to fully reproduce experimental two-particle transfer data, indicating additional correlations beyond the quasiparticle picture are needed.

## Method

The authors developed a Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock + BCS (TDHF+BCS) microscopic theory incorporating pairing correlations through a Skyrme functional (SLy4d) with a mixed contact-type pairing interaction. They simulated central collisions for several systems (96Zr+40Ca, 18O+206,208Pb, and 40,44,48Ca+40Ca) by initializing nuclei in their BCS ground states, evolving them on a lattice, and extracting transfer probabilities using a double projection technique that projects onto neutron/proton numbers in the outgoing fragments.

## Key numbers

- For the 96Zr+40Ca reaction at 90 MeV center-of-mass energy, the TDHF+BCS two-neutron transfer probability is below experimental data by a factor of 4; at 93 MeV, only 15% of the probability is missed.
- The experimental data for 96Zr+40Ca shows a strong enhancement with P2 ≈ 3P1^2, compared to the uncorrelated expectation of P2 ≈ P1^2.
- For 18O+206Pb, the fusion threshold is found at 73.85 MeV; for 18O+208Pb, the fusion threshold is 73.35 MeV.
- In the equal-filling approximation (no pairing), the two-neutron transfer probability for 18O+206Pb is systematically larger by a factor of 2 than for 18O+208Pb.

## Context

This work extends the TDHF+BCS approach previously developed by the same authors (Scamps 2013, 2014) to nuclear collisions near the Coulomb barrier, comparing directly to experimental transfer data from Corradi (2011) for 96Zr+40Ca and to shell-model calculations from the same reference. The results show consistency between the unified TDHF+BCS approach and the separate structure-plus-reaction treatment of Corradi (2011), while suggesting that theories beyond quasiparticle approaches, such as time-dependent density matrix theory (Assie 2009) or stochastic mean-field theory (Yilmaz 2011), may be needed to fully capture two-particle transfer. The fusion barrier calculations for calcium isotopes agree reasonably with experimental data from Siwek-Wilczynska (2004) and show that pairing has almost no effect on fusion barrier properties.
