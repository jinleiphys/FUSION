# Superfluid-Normal Quantum Phase Transitions in an Imbalanced Fermi Gas

> We investigate the superfluid-to-normal zero temperature quantum phase transitions of asymmetric two-component Fermi gases as a function of the chemical potential imbalance $h$. The calculations are performed for homogeneous and trapped imbalanced systems. We concentrate at unitarity, characterized by a divergent interaction parameter $k_F a$, where most of the current experiments are realized. For homogeneous systems, we determine the critical chemical potential imbalance $h_c$ at which possible phase transitions occur. In the case of trapped gases, we show how $h_c$ can be consistently determined from experimental observations.

# Superfluid-Normal Quantum Phase Transitions in an Imbalanced Fermi Gas

## Key claim

The superfluid-to-normal quantum phase transition in imbalanced Fermi gases at unitarity occurs at a critical chemical potential imbalance that depends sensitively on whether the normal phase is treated as a fully polarized gas or a partially polarized (mixed) normal phase, and on whether interactions in the normal phase are included. The correct critical imbalance for the transition from superfluid to a partially polarized normal phase is approximately $h_c/\mu = 0.88$ for homogeneous systems and $h_c/\mu \simeq 0.79$ for trapped systems, with the latter derived from experimental cloud radii measurements.

## Method

The author calculates the zero-temperature phase diagram by equating the pressures of the superfluid and normal phases at equilibrium, using the universal parameter $\xi$ (Bertsch parameter) for the superfluid pressure at unitarity and a polaron model for the interacting normal phase pressure. For homogeneous systems, the equilibrium condition is solved analytically for the critical chemical potential ratio $\eta = \mu_\downarrow/\mu_\uparrow$, yielding $\eta_{c1} \sim 0.061$ for the superfluid-to-normal-mixed transition. For trapped systems, the local density approximation is applied with a harmonic trapping potential, and the critical imbalance is extracted from experimental ratios of cloud radii $R_c/R_\uparrow = 0.43$ and $R_\downarrow/R_\uparrow = 0.728$ from Shin et al. (2008), using a derived $r$-dependent expression for $h/\mu$.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential ratio for homogeneous systems: $\eta_{c1} \sim 0.061$, corresponding to $(h/\mu)_c = 0.88$
- Critical imbalance for the superfluid-to-normal-mixed transition in trapped systems: $(h/\mu)_c \simeq 0.79$, derived from $R_c/R_\uparrow = 0.43$, $\eta(R_c/R_\uparrow) = 0.03$, $\xi = 0.42$, $n_s/n_0 = 1.72$, and $R_\uparrow/R_{TF} \approx 0.95$
- Saturation field for the normal-mixed to fully polarized transition in trapped systems: $(h/\mu)_s \simeq 1.059$, from $R_\downarrow/R_\uparrow = 0.728$ and $\eta_\downarrow \approx -0.69$
- Maximum field for the fully polarized to vacuum transition in trapped systems: $(h/\mu)_m \simeq 1.41$
- For homogeneous systems, the normal-mixed to fully polarized transition occurs at $\eta_{c2} = -0.615$, giving $(h/\mu)_s = 4.19$
- The weak-coupling Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit gives $h_{cc}/\Delta_0 = 1/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707$

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of quantum phase transitions in imbalanced Fermi gases at unitarity, building on the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit (Chandrasekhar, 1962; Clogston, 1962) and the universal parameter approach of Bedaque (2003) and Caldas (2004). The results are compared with several theoretical approaches: the 1/N expansion (Sachdev), functional renormalization group (Boettcher), quantum Monte Carlo (Lobo; Sanjay), Luttinger-Ward formalism (Frank), and experiments (Shin, 2008; Exp3). The work enables a consistent determination of the critical imbalance from experimental observables in trapped systems and identifies the regime $h_c < h < h_s$ as an open question for future investigation of possible exotic phases such as the Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov state.
