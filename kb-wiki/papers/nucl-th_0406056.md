# Partial energies fluctuations and negative heat capacities

> We proceed to a critical examination of the method used in nuclear fragmentation to exhibit signals of negative heat capacity. We show that this method leads to unsatisfactory results when applied to a simple and well controlled model. Discrepancies are due to incomplete evaluation of potential energies.

## Key claim

The method used by D'Agostino et al. (2002) to infer negative heat capacities from nuclear fragmentation data, which replaces the true kinetic energy fluctuation with a quantity \(K'\) that neglects intra-fragment deformation energies and inter-fragment nuclear interactions, yields spurious negative heat capacities when tested on a simple Lennard-Jones model where the true heat capacity remains positive.

## Method

The authors performed microcanonical molecular dynamics simulations of a Lennard-Jones fluid with \(N=64\) particles confined in a container, with no Coulomb interactions. They computed the true heat capacity \(c_v\) both from kinetic energy fluctuations and from the derivative of the caloric curve, and compared it to the approximate quantity \(c'_v\) calculated from the fluctuation of \(K' = E - \sum_i B_i\) (total energy minus sum of fragment binding energies), following the same procedure used in experimental nuclear fragmentation analyses by D'Agostino et al. (2002). Fragments were identified using the energetic Hill criterion, and the binding energies of Lennard-Jones clusters were taken from a mass table.

## Key numbers

- For the Lennard-Jones fluid with \(N=64\) particles, the true heat capacity \(c_v\) is always positive except in a small zone at very low density (less than \(1/10\) of normal liquid density) and at low temperatures around the liquid-solid transition.
- The approximate quantity \(c'_v\) becomes negative when \(\sigma_{K'}^2 / T^2 C_1 > 1\), which occurs in a large zone of the density-temperature phase diagram, including the super-critical region where heat capacities cannot be negative.
- For an isochore path at density \(\rho = 0.35\) and excitation energy per particle \(E^*/N = 4.12\) (in units of the Lennard-Jones potential \(\epsilon\)), the fluctuations of \(K'\) largely exceed those of \(K\) and are not in phase with them.
- The intra-fragment potential energy term \(\sum_i (V_i - B_i)\) remains important at all densities, indicating fragments are not compact spheres.

## Context

This work critically examines the method of D'Agostino et al. (2002) that claimed observation of negative heat capacities in nuclear fragmentation, showing that the approximation of neglecting intra-fragment deformation energies and inter-fragment nuclear interactions leads to incorrect results even in a simple Lennard-Jones model. The paper extends earlier theoretical work by Gross (1997), Chomaz and Gulminelli (1999), and Pleimling and Hüller (2001) on negative heat capacities in small systems, and contradicts the experimental conclusions of D'Agostino et al. (2002). It suggests that future analyses must account for the neglected potential energy terms, and notes that Ison and Dorso (2004) found that adding Coulomb interactions to a Lennard-Jones potential produces no qualitative changes in heat capacities.
