# Constraining relativistic viscous hydrodynamical evolution

> We show that by requiring positivity of the longitudinal pressure it is possible to constrain the initial conditions one can use in 2nd-order viscous hydrodynamical simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate this explicitly for 0+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics and discuss how the constraint extends to higher dimensions. Additionally, we present an analytic approximation to the solution of 0+1 dimensional 2nd-order viscous hydrodynamical evolution equations appropriate to describe the evolution of matter in an ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

By requiring positivity of the longitudinal pressure during the hydrodynamic evolution, it is possible to constrain the allowed initial conditions (initial time τ₀ and initial shear tensor Π₀) for 2nd-order viscous hydrodynamical simulations of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the constraint depending non-trivially on the transport coefficients and the coupling regime (weakly-coupled QCD or strongly-coupled N=4 SYM).

## Method

The authors studied 0+1 dimensional (boost-invariant, transversely uniform) second-order conformal viscous hydrodynamics, solving the coupled differential equations for energy density ε and shear tensor component Π as functions of proper time τ. They used both numerical integration and an approximate analytic solution (involving confluent hypergeometric and Meijer G functions) to compute the time evolution of the momentum-space anisotropy parameter Δ = p_T/p_L − 1, for both strongly-coupled (N=4 SYM) and weakly-coupled QCD transport coefficients, varying the initial time τ₀ and initial shear Π₀ while holding the initial temperature fixed at T₀ = 350 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For strongly-coupled transport coefficients (η/s = 1/(4π), τ_π = (2−log2)/(2πT), λ₁ = 2 η² s/T) with T₀ = 350 MeV and Δ₀ = 0, the anisotropy parameter Δ peaks in the range 0.2 ≲ Δ ≲ 1 for τ₀ = {0.4, 1, 2} fm/c.
- For weakly-coupled transport coefficients (η/s = 10/(4π), τ_π = 6η/T, λ₁ = (9/2) η² s/T) with T₀ = 350 MeV and Δ₀ = 0, the anisotropy parameter Δ peaks in the range 1 ≲ Δ ≲ 9 for τ₀ = {0.4, 1, 2} fm/c.
- For the weakly-coupled case with τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, the longitudinal pressure becomes negative (Δ diverges) during the simulation, indicating a breakdown of the hydrodynamic description.
- The approximate analytic solution deviates from exact numerical integration by at most 1% for the energy density in the strongly-coupled case and at most 8% in the weakly-coupled case over 10 fm/c of simulation time.

## Context

This work extends the study of viscous hydrodynamic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, addressing the systematic uncertainty in initial conditions (Glauber-type or Color-Glass-Condensate-based) noted by Luzum and Romatschke (2008) and others. The constraint derived from positivity of longitudinal pressure is complementary to the kinetic-theory-based applicability criterion of Molnar and Huovinen (2008), as it uses only the hydrodynamic equations themselves. The results enable more reliable extraction of transport coefficients like η/s from experimental data by restricting the range of viable initial simulation times and shear tensor values.
