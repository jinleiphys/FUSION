# Beam-Normal Single-Spin Asymmetry in $^{208}$Pb at low energy: discrepancy resolved or new kinematic puzzle?

> A longstanding discrepancy between measured and predicted beam-normal single-spin asymmetries $A_{n}$ in elastic electron scattering off $^{208}$Pb has challenged our understanding of two-photon exchange (TPE) in heavy nuclei. We report a new measurement at 570 MeV and $Q^2$=0.04 $GeV^2$/$c^2$, yielding$A_n = (-9.1 \pm 2.1~\text{(stat)} \pm 0.7~\text{(syst)})~\mathrm{ppm}$. This nonzero value contrasts with previous results at higher energies and suggests a kinematic dependence of TPE effects not captured by current theory, prompting a reevaluation of earlier interpretations.

# Beam-Normal Single-Spin Asymmetry in $^{208}$Pb at low energy: discrepancy resolved or new kinematic puzzle?

## Key claim

The new measurement of the beam-normal single-spin asymmetry $A_n$ in elastic electron scattering off $^{208}$Pb at 570 MeV and $Q^2 = 0.04$ GeV$^2$/c$^2$ yields a nonzero value of $(-9.1 \pm 2.1_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.7_{\text{syst}})$ ppm, which contrasts with the vanishing asymmetry reported by PREX at 1.06 GeV; this suggests a kinematic dependence of two-photon exchange effects not captured by current theory, but the abstract's framing as a "discrepancy resolved or new kinematic puzzle" remains open because no single theoretical model simultaneously describes both the low-energy MAMI data and the high-energy PREX result.

## Method

A vertically polarized continuous-wave electron beam at 570 MeV was scattered from a 0.5 mm thick $^{208}$Pb foil, and elastically scattered electrons were detected in two high-resolution magnetic spectrometers (A and B) positioned symmetrically at forward angles, defining $Q^2 = 0.04$ GeV$^2$/c$^2$. A newly developed FPGA-based data acquisition system counted individual PMT pulses from fused-silica Cerenkov detectors, and the experimental asymmetry was corrected via multi-parameter linear regression for helicity-correlated differences in beam current, position, angle, and energy, then normalized by the vertical beam polarization measured with a Moller polarimeter cross-calibrated against a Mott system. Two independent analysis chains processed 232.7 hours of data collected across two campaigns (November 2023 and June 2024), with systematic uncertainties evaluated by varying key analysis parameters and quantifying their effect on the extracted asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- $A_n = (-9.1 \pm 2.1_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.7_{\text{syst}})$ ppm for $^{208}$Pb at beam energy 570 MeV and $Q^2 = 0.04$ GeV$^2$/c$^2$
- Spectrometer A: $A_n = 8.954$ ppm with statistical error 2.416 ppm and total systematic error 0.192 ppm at scattering angle 20.14$^\circ$ and $Q^2 = 0.040$ GeV$^2$/c$^2$
- Spectrometer B: $A_n = 9.568$ ppm with statistical error 4.331 ppm and total systematic error 0.628 ppm at scattering angle 20.56$^\circ$ and $Q^2 = 0.041$ GeV$^2$/c$^2$
- Only 2.8% of events were rejected during manual data selection
- Target thickness: 0.5 mm (567 mg/cm$^2$), corresponding to about one-tenth of a radiation length
- Beam current during physics runs: 20 $\mu$A; calibration runs used 50 nA for tracking detectors

## Context

This measurement extends the MAMI program studying $A_n$ across nuclear targets (Esser 2018, Esser 2020), which found results for $^{12}$C, $^{28}$Si, and $^{90}$Zr consistent with dispersion-based predictions, and directly addresses the longstanding anomaly from PREX (Abrahamyan 2012) and PREX-II/CREX (Adhikari 2022), which reported a vanishing $A_n$ in $^{208}$Pb at 1.063 GeV. The nonzero result at 570 MeV reveals a kinematic dependence that updated calculations using new Compton form factors (PhysRevC.103.064316) fail to reproduce for either the low-energy MAMI data or the high-energy PREX result, motivating a planned measurement campaign at MAMI and future opportunities at the MESA accelerator to constrain two-photon exchange in heavy nuclei, which is essential for the planned Mainz Radius EXperiment (MREX) aiming to determine the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb at 155 MeV using parity-violating electron scattering.
