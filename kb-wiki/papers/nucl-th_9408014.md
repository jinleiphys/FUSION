# Fermion to boson mappings revisited

> We briefly review various mappings of fermion pairs to bosons, including those based on mapping operators, such as Belyaev-Zelevinskii, and those on mapping states, such as Marumori; in particular we consider the work of Otsuka-Arima-Iachello, aimed at deriving the Interacting Boson Model. We then give a rigorous and unified description of state-mapping procedures which allows one to systematically go beyond Otsuka-Arima-Iachello and related approaches, along with several exact results.

## Key claim

The paper provides a rigorous and unified description of state-mapping procedures for fermion-to-boson mappings, showing how to systematically go beyond the Otsuka-Arima-Iachello (OAI) approach, but only under specific conditions on the pair structure and interactions can one guarantee a Hermitian boson image that is finite and has at most two-body terms.

## Method

The authors construct boson representations of fermion operators by mapping fermion-pair states directly onto boson states, then deriving boson operators that preserve matrix elements. They introduce a norm operator that enforces the Pauli principle and show that in the full boson Fock space the Hamiltonian representation factorizes into a boson image times the norm operator, yielding the same result as the Belyaev-Zelevinskii mapping. For truncated spaces, they derive conditions under which a finite, Hermitian Dyson image exists, and they give explicit formulas for the boson images of one-body and two-body fermion operators in terms of traces over the pair amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The norm operator in the full space is expressed as a normal-ordered exponential of Casimir operators: $\hat{\cal N}_B = \, \colon \exp \left ( - { 1\over 2} \sum_{k=2}^\infty { (-1)^{k} \over k}\hat{C}_k \ \right ) \colon$, where $\hat{C}_k = 2^k \,{\rm tr \,} \left( {\bf P } \right)^k$ and ${\bf P }= \sum_{\sigma \tau} {b}^\dagger_\sigma {b}_\tau {\bf A}_\sigma {\bf A}^\dagger_\tau$.
* For the pairing interaction in a truncated space with factorization, the Dyson image becomes $ G \left \lbrace \hat{{\rm N}}_0 [ 1 - \frac{2}{\Omega} \hat{{\rm N}} + \frac{1}{\Omega} + \frac{\hat{{\rm N}}_0}{\Omega}] + \ldots \right \rbrace$, where $\Omega = 2 \Omega_a \Omega_c$ and $\hat{{\rm N}}$ is the total boson number.
* In the SO(8) and Sp(6) models, the renormalization factor $f_{\Omega_a} = 4\Omega_a^2/N_a$ for the two-boson part of the Hermitian Dyson image ranges from unity (full space) to 2 for a very small subspace, where $N_a=\Omega_a(2\Omega_a+(-1)^p)$.

## Context

This work extends the state-mapping approach of Marumori (1960s) and the OAI mapping of Otsuka, Arima, and Iachello (1970s-1980s), which aimed to derive the Interacting Boson Model from the shell model but had limited success for well-deformed nuclei. The paper provides a rigorous framework that unifies these earlier approaches and shows how to systematically include higher-order corrections beyond the OAI prescription, which only exactly mapped the $n_d = 0,1,2$ space. The results enable microscopic calculations of IBM parameters from shell-model interactions and can be applied to models such as SO(8) and Sp(6) that have closed subalgebras.
