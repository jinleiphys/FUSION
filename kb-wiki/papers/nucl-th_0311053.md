# Charge-dependent calculations of single-particle energies in nuclei around ^{16}O with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions

> The binding energies of the ground states and several excited states related to single-particle and -hole states in nuclei around ^{16}O are calculated taking charge dependence into account. Effective interactions on the particle basis are constructed from modern charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions and the Coulomb force within the framework of the unitary-model-operator approach. Single-particle (-hole) energies are obtained from the energy differences of the binding energies between a particle (hole) state in ^{17}O or ^{17}F (^{15}N or ^{15}O) and the ground state of ^{16}O. The resultant spin-orbit splittings are small for the hole state and large for the particle state in comparison with the experimental values though the differences between the experimental and calculated values are not very large. The charge dependence of the calculated single-particle energies for the ground states are in good agreement with the experimental values. Furthermore, the Thomas-Ehrman shift due to the Coulomb force for the 1s_{1/2} states in ^{17}O and ^{17}F can be observed.

# Charge-dependent calculations of single-particle energies in nuclei around 16O with modern nucleon-nucleon interactions

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that single-particle energies in nuclei around 16O can be calculated with reasonable accuracy using modern charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon interactions within the unitary-model-operator approach on the particle basis, though the calculated spin-orbit splittings are systematically smaller for hole states and larger for particle states compared to experimental values.

## Method
The authors constructed effective interactions from four high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen 93, Nijm I, CD Bonn, and N3LO chiral potential) plus the Coulomb force using the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) on the particle basis rather than the isospin basis. A two-step procedure was employed: first, effective interactions were derived in a large harmonic-oscillator model space using relative and center-of-mass coordinates; second, these interactions were unitarily transformed again to eliminate two-particle two-hole excitation vertices. Single-particle and single-hole energies were obtained as energy differences between binding energies of 17O or 17F (15N or 15O) and the 16O ground state, with excitations up to two-particle one-hole (one-particle two-hole) included.

## Key numbers
- The unperturbed ground-state energy of 16O converged after more than 4 iterations in the self-consistent calculation using the CD-Bonn potential with rho_1 = 12 and hbar-Omega = 14 MeV.
- The boundary number rho_1 for the first-step model space was taken as large as possible to ensure results independent of this value, with rho_X = 20 for the Pauli principle treatment.
- The N3LO potential reproduces nucleon-nucleon data below E_lab = 290 MeV with high accuracy.

## Context
This work extends the unitary-model-operator approach (Suzuki 1994, Suzuki 1987) from the isospin basis to the particle basis, enabling charge-dependent calculations that treat the Coulomb force accurately for proton-proton channels. The approach contrasts with G-matrix methods (Kuo 1990, Hjorth-Jensen 1995) and the low-momentum potential V_low-k approach (Bogner 2003, Coraggio 2003) for calculating ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei. The results enable future application of the particle-basis UMOA to neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei near drip lines, where charge-dependent effects become important.
