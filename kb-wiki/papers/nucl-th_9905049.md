# Eta and eta-prime meson production in the reaction pn -> dM near threshold

> The two-step model with pi-, rho- and omega- exchanges taken into account is applied to investigate the reactions pn -> d eta and pn -> d eta-prime. The existing experimental data on the reaction pn -> d eta are analysed and predictions for the cross section of the reaction pn -> d eta-prime are presented. It is found that pi- as well as rho exchange yield significant contributions in both reactions. The effect of the final state interaction is also studied.

## Key claim

The two-step model (TSM) with pi, rho, and omega exchanges can reproduce the measured cross section of pn -> d eta near threshold only after including an overall normalization constant (0.68) to account for initial-state interaction, and the analysis favors eta-N scattering lengths with real part <= 0.3 fm, which is below the value needed for an eta-d bound state.

## Method

The authors applied the two-step model (TSM) to the reactions pn -> d eta and pn -> d eta-prime, where the initial nucleons first produce the meson via virtual pi, rho, or omega exchange, and then the proton and neutron form a deuteron. The S-wave amplitudes for the elementary pi N -> M N reactions were taken from experimental cross sections, while the rho and omega exchange contributions were estimated using the vector-dominance model from gamma N -> M N data. The final-state interaction between the eta meson and the deuteron was treated using both an optical-potential method and a multiple-scattering (Foldy-Brueckner) approach.

## Key numbers

- The S-wave cross section for pi- p -> eta n is (21.2 +/- 1.8) * p_cm^M microbarns (p_cm^M in MeV/c)
- The S-wave cross section for pi- p -> eta-prime n is (0.35 +/- 0.03) * p_cm^M microbarns (p_cm^M in MeV/c)
- The rho-exchange amplitude squared |f|^2 = 720 microbarns/sr for rho^0 p -> eta p, compared to 360 microbarns/sr for pi^0 p -> eta p
- The rho-exchange amplitude squared |f|^2 = 60-90 microbarns/sr for rho^0 p -> eta-prime p, compared to 9.8 microbarns/sr for pi^0 p -> eta-prime p
- The initial-state interaction reduction factor from phase-shift analysis is lambda_ISI = 0.46 at T_lab ~ 1250 MeV (eta threshold) and 0.83 at T_lab ~ 2400 MeV (eta-prime threshold)
- The overall normalization constant N = 0.68 was needed to match the pn -> d eta data for Q > 10 MeV
- The predicted cross section for pn -> d eta-prime at Q ~ 30 MeV is about 1 microbarn or more
- The eta-N scattering length a_etaN = 0.291 + i 0.360 fm (from eta photoproduction data) describes the near-threshold enhancement well
- The eta-d scattering length for the favored eta-N values is A_eta d ~ 0.4 + i 1.2 fm

## Context

This work re-analyzes the reaction pn -> d eta using new data from CELSIUS (Calen et al., 1998-1999), which ruled out older estimates by Plouin and Laget that predicted much larger cross sections. The two-step model was previously applied to Pontecorvo reactions (Kondratyuk et al., 1990s). The authors find that rho exchange dominates over pi exchange, supporting an argument by Wilkin (1998) about the large cross section ratio sigma(pn -> pn eta)/sigma(pp -> pp eta). The analysis constrains the eta-N scattering length to Re a_etaN <= 0.3 fm, which is below the value of about 0.7 fm needed for an eta-d bound state (Green et al., 1990s), and provides predictions for pn -> d eta-prime that should be measurable at COSY.
