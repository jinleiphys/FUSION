# Van der Waals Equation of State with Fermi Statistics for Nuclear Matter

> The van der Waals (VDW) equation of state is a simple and popular model to describe the pressure function in equilibrium systems of particles with both repulsive and attractive interactions. This equation predicts an existence of a first-order liquid-gas phase transition and contains a critical point. Two steps to extend the VDW equation and make it appropriate for new physical applications are carried out in this paper: 1) the grand canonical ensemble formulation; 2) an inclusion of the quantum statistics. The VDW equation with Fermi statistics is then applied to a description of the system of interacting nucleons. The VDW parameters $a$ and $b$ are fixed to reproduce the properties of nuclear matter at saturation density $n_0=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ and zero temperature. The model predicts a location of the critical point for the symmetric nuclear matter at temperature $T_c\cong 19.7$ MeV and nucleon number density $n_c \cong 0.07$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The paper extends the classical van der Waals (VDW) equation of state to include Fermi statistics, enabling a description of symmetric nuclear matter that satisfies the third law of thermodynamics (entropy goes to zero at zero temperature), which the Boltzmann-statistics VDW equation fails to do.

## Method

The authors formulate the VDW equation in the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) and replace the ideal Boltzmann gas pressure and density with their ideal Fermi gas counterparts, while keeping the same functional form for the excluded-volume and mean-field terms. The VDW parameters \(a\) and \(b\) are fixed by requiring that at zero temperature the model reproduces the saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(-16\) MeV, and zero pressure for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) at zero temperature.
* Binding energy per nucleon: \(-16\) MeV at saturation.
* VDW parameter \(a \cong 329\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\).
* VDW parameter \(b \cong 3.42\) fm\(^3\) (corresponding to a hard-core nucleon radius \(r \cong 0.59\) fm).
* Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: \(T_c \cong 19.7\) MeV.
* Critical nucleon number density: \(n_c \cong 0.07\) fm\(^{-3}\) (approximately \(0.4\,n_0\)).
* Classical VDW (Boltzmann) critical temperature with the same \(a\) and \(b\): \(T_c = 8a/27b \cong 28.5\) MeV.
* Classical VDW critical density: \(n_c = 1/3b \cong 0.10\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* Maximum nucleon density in the model: \(n_{\rm max} = 1/b \cong 0.29\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the GCE formulation of the VDW equation for Boltzmann statistics developed by the same authors (Vovchenko et al., 2014) to include quantum statistics, which is necessary for a correct description at low temperatures. The model is applied to symmetric nuclear matter, a system studied extensively since the 1980s by authors such as Jaqaman et al. (1983), Goodman et al. (1984), and Csernai and Kapusta (1986), and it predicts a liquid-gas phase transition with a critical point whose temperature is close to experimental estimates from Natowitz et al. (2002) and Karnaukhov et al. (2003). The framework enables future extensions to include nucleon clusters, baryonic resonances, or comparisons with more sophisticated mean-field models.
