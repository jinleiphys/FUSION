# Heavy quarkonium production and polarization

> We present a perturbative QCD factorization formalism for the production of heavy quarkonia of large transverse momentum $p_T$ at collider energies, which includes both the leading power (LP) and next-to-leading power (NLP) contributions to the cross section in the $m_Q^2/p_T^2$ expansion for heavy quark mass $m_Q$. We estimate fragmentation functions in the non-relativistic QCD formalism, and reproduce the bulk of the large enhancement found in explicit NLO calculations in the color singlet model. Heavy quarkonia produced from NLP channels prefer longitudinal polarization.

## Key claim

The paper presents a perturbative QCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production at large transverse momentum that includes both leading power (LP) and next-to-leading power (NLP) contributions in the \(m_Q^2/p_T^2\) expansion, and shows that the NLP channels, despite being suppressed by two powers of \(p_T\), can be phenomenologically important because the probability for a heavy quark pair to evolve into a quarkonium is naturally enhanced compared to that of a single parton.

## Method

The authors develop a factorization formula that separates the cross section into short-distance coefficient functions (calculable in perturbative QCD) convoluted with universal long-distance fragmentation functions. For the NLP contribution, they define heavy quark pair fragmentation functions for various color and spin states of the pair, and estimate these functions using the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) formalism to reduce them to local NRQCD matrix elements. They then compute the NLP cross section for \(J/\psi\) production at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) from a color singlet \(^3\mathrm{S}_1\) pair and compare it to full next-to-leading order (NLO) color singlet model (CSM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The NLP contribution is suppressed by \(p_T^{-2}\) relative to the LP contribution, which scales as \(p_T^{-4}\) in \(d\sigma/dp_T^2\).
- The calculated NLP cross section for \(J/\psi\) production reproduces as much as eighty percent of the full NLO CSM result.
- The calculation uses \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV and \(\langle O^{J/\psi}_{1(^3\mathrm{S}_1)}\rangle = 1.32\) GeV\(^3\).
- The polarization parameter \(\alpha = (\sigma^T - \sigma^L)/(\sigma^T + \sigma^L)\) is computed and shown to be consistent with results from Gong (2008) and Lansberg (2008).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD factorization approach for heavy quarkonium production beyond the leading power (gluon fragmentation) formalism of Nayak (2005) and Nayak (2007), and addresses the surprising large NLO and NNLO corrections found in color singlet model calculations by Campbell (2007). The formalism enables systematic analysis of the competition between LP and NLP channels, and the finding that NLP channels produce longitudinally polarized quarkonia offers a path to resolving the long-standing polarization puzzle in quarkonium production, as summarized in the review by Brambilla (2010).
