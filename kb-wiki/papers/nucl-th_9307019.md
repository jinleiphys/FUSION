# Study of the Three-Nucleon (e,e') Longitudinal Response Function with a New Approach

> A new method for studying the many-body response functions is elaborated and first applied to the $^3$He longitudinal response. An integral transform of the response function is calculated from the bound-state-type equations for several versions of the N-N force. The equations are solved with the help of the hyperspherical expansion. The final-state interaction is completely taken into account. The results are compared with the integral transform of the experimental response function for 250 MeV/c$\leq q \leq 500$ MeV/c. The difference amounts to 20-25\% with the experimental response substantially exceeding the theoretical one in the low-energy region.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for calculating the three-nucleon longitudinal response function that avoids computing the full set of final-state continuum wave functions, but the theoretical results still differ from experiment by 20-25% in the low-energy region, with the experimental response substantially exceeding the theoretical one.

## Method

The authors calculate an integral transform of the response function, Phi(q,sigma), by solving bound-state-type inhomogeneous equations for the localized solution, rather than computing the response directly from the definition requiring final-state continuum wave functions. The equations are solved using a hyperspherical expansion with complete final-state interaction, employing effective central N-N forces acting in the s-wave (S2, S3, MT(I+III), and EH potentials) supplemented with a realistic singlet p-wave N-N force. The theoretical transform is compared with the integral transform obtained from experimental data by fitting the experimental spectra with analytical expressions and extrapolating the high-energy tails.

## Key numbers

- At q=250 MeV/c and lowest sigma values, the relative deviation between theoretical and experimental transforms is -25% for the S2 potential and -21% for the MT(I+III) potential
- At q=500 MeV/c and lowest sigma values, the deviation is -(22-23)% for both S2 and MT(I+III) potentials
- For the S3 potential, the deviation is -17% at q=250 MeV/c and -24% at q=500 MeV/c
- For the EH potential, the deviation is -8% at q=250 MeV/c and -20% at q=500 MeV/c
- At high sigma values, differences between experimental and theoretical transforms do not exceed 6.5% in absolute value for all potentials
- The p-wave N-N force contributes 4% at the lowest q values and is negligible at the highest q values
- Coulomb interaction components change results by at most 3% at the lowest q values considered
- Calculations were performed at q=250, 400, and 500 MeV/c with L_max=10 and K_max=20 for L=0 and K_max=10 for other L values

## Context

This work extends the integral transform method developed by Efros (1985, 1993) and Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1993) to the three-nucleon system, providing a microscopic analysis of the He-3 longitudinal response with full final-state interaction. The results are compared with experimental data from Marchand et al. (1985), and the discrepancies suggest either deficiencies in the conventional description of nuclear states or in the conventional nuclear charge density operator. The method enables future investigations of exclusive reactions including 2 to N reactions induced by strong interaction, as noted by Efros (1985).
