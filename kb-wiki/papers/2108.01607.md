# Nonlinear diffusion of gluons

> It is proposed to consider the fast thermalization of gluons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a diffusion process in momentum space. Closed-form analytical solutions of a nonlinear boson diffusion equation (NBDE) with constant drift and diffusion coefficients v, D and boundary conditions at the singularity are derived. The time evolution towards local central temperatures T< 600 MeV through inelastic gluon scatterings in heavy-ion collisions is calculated for under- and overoccupied systems in the full momentum range. The results are consistent with QCD-based numerical calculations for gluon thermalization via inelastic gluon collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that fast thermalization of gluons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be modeled as a nonlinear diffusion process in momentum space, with closed-form analytical solutions of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation (NBDE) that respect boundary conditions at the singularity \(p=\mu=0\), thereby achieving the Bose-Einstein limit over the full energy range as \(t\rightarrow\infty\), a feature not captured by free Green's function solutions.

## Method

The author derives closed-form analytical solutions of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation (NBDE) with constant drift and diffusion coefficients \(v, D\) for massless gluons, using a nonlinear transformation that relates the occupation-number distribution to the logarithmic derivative of a time-dependent partition function. A bounded Green's function that vanishes at \(\epsilon=0\) is introduced to enforce boundary conditions at the singularity, restricting the energy range to \(\epsilon \ge 0\) and ensuring that the stationary solution becomes the Bose-Einstein distribution with \(\mu=0\) for inelastic collisions. The time evolution is calculated for both under- and overoccupied systems using schematic initial conditions, with transport coefficients determined from the equilibrium temperature \(T\) and local equilibration time \(\tau_\mathrm{eq}\) via the relations \(D = 4T^2/(9\tau_\mathrm{eq})\) and \(v = -4T/(9\tau_\mathrm{eq})\).

## Key numbers

* Local central temperatures \(T \lesssim 600\) MeV are reached through inelastic gluon scatterings in heavy-ion collisions.
* The local equilibration time is chosen as \(\tau_\mathrm{eq} \simeq 0.14\) fm/\(c\), shorter than the upper limit of 0.25 fm/\(c\) from Ref. [fmr18].
* The equilibration time at the gluon saturation momentum \(p = Q_\mathrm{s}\) is given by \(\tau_\mathrm{eq} = 4D/(9v^2)\).
* The thermalization time for soft modes is \(\tau_\mathrm{eq} \lesssim 0.1\) fm/\(c\), compared to a collision duration of about 8–10 fm/\(c\) in central Pb–Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear diffusion model for quarks and gluons proposed by Wolschin (2018) to include boundary conditions at the singularity, enabling closed-form solutions for gluon thermalization via inelastic collisions. It is consistent with QCD-based numerical calculations for gluon thermalization via inelastic collisions, such as those by Kurkela and Zhu (2015) and Blaizot et al. (2012, 2013), and supports the finding of Blaizot et al. (2017) that gluon condensate formation in overoccupied systems is suppressed because equilibration through inelastic scatterings occurs faster than elastic condensate formation. The analytical solutions provide a transparent, tractable model for aspects of kinetic evolution previously studied numerically in more involved QCD-based approaches.
