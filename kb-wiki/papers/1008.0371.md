# A covariant formalism for the N* electroproduction at high momentum transfer

> A constituent quark model based on the spectator formalism is applied to the gamma N -> N* transition for the three cases, where N* is the nucleon, the Delta and the Roper resonance. The model is covariant, and therefore can be used for the predictions at higher four-momentum transfer squared, Q2. The baryons are described as an off-mass-shell quark and a spectator on-mass-shell diquark systems. The quark electromagnetic current is described by quark form factors, which have a form inspired by the vector meson dominance. The valence quark contributions of the model are calibrated by lattice QCD simulations and experimental data. Contributions of the meson cloud to the inelastic processes are explicitly included.

## Key claim
The covariant spectator quark model, calibrated by lattice QCD simulations and experimental data, can describe the valence quark contributions to nucleon, Delta, and Roper electroproduction transitions, but the meson cloud contributions must be explicitly added to match experimental data for inelastic processes, particularly for the Delta transition where the pure valence quark core contribution at Q2=0 is limited to an upper value of 2.07 versus the experimental 3.02.

## Method
The baryons are described as an off-mass-shell quark interacting with an on-mass-shell spectator diquark system, with the quark electromagnetic current parametrized by a vector meson dominance motivated form involving two vector meson poles (a light meson mass m_v = m_rho and an effective heavy meson mass M_h = 2 M_N). The valence quark contributions are calibrated using lattice QCD data in the regime where pion cloud effects are suppressed, then extrapolated to the physical region, and meson cloud contributions are added separately using phenomenological forms or large-Nc limit results.

## Key numbers
- The upper bound for the bare magnetic dipole form factor G_M^b(0) from the spectator quark model is 2.07, compared to the experimental value of 3.02 for the gamma N -> Delta transition.
- The effective heavy vector meson mass M_h is set to 2 M_N (twice the nucleon mass) in the quark form factor parametrization.
- The model is applied in the Q2 region from 0 to 10 GeV^2, where QCD cannot be solved exactly.
- For the gamma N -> Roper transition, the model results are consistent with CLAS data for Q2 > 2 GeV^2.
- The pion cloud contribution to G_M^* is parametrized as G_M^pi = lambda_pi * (lambda_pi^2 / (Lambda_pi^2 + Q^2))^2 * (3 G_D), where G_D is the nucleon dipole factor.

## Context
This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously developed for the nucleon (Ramalho, 2008) and the Delta transition (Ramalho, 2008) to include the Roper resonance, and it contrasts with dynamical coupled channel models (Diaz, 2007; Kamalov, 2001) and chiral effective field theories that are limited to low Q2. The results enable predictions for higher Q2 electroproduction experiments at Jefferson Lab and provide a framework for separating valence quark and meson cloud contributions to nucleon resonance transitions, with further applications to Delta and decuplet baryon electromagnetic form factors.
