# Quark-model Baryon-Baryon Interaction Applied to the Neutron-Deuteron Scattering. I Noyes-Kowalski Approach to the AGS Equations

> We solve the neutron-deuteron (nd) scattering in the Faddeev formalism, employing the NN sector of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2. The energy-dependence of the NN interaction, inherent to the resonating-group formulation of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction, is eliminated by the standard off-shell transformation utilizing the normalization kernel of two-cluster systems. An extra nonlocality originating from this procedure is very important to reproduce all the nd scattering observables below E_n < 65 MeV. A new algorithm to solve the Alt-Glassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations is presented in the Noyes-Kowalski method. The treatment of the moving singularity from the three-body Green function is shown in detail, which is based on the spline function method originally developed by the Bochum-Krakow group. The predicted elastic differnetial cross sections reproduce the observed deep cross section minima at E_n=35-65 MeV and $θ_{c.m}=130^{\circ}-135^{\circ}$, which is consitent with the nearly correct triton binding energy predicted by fss2 without the three-body force.

## Key claim

The quark-model NN interaction fss2, after removing its inherent energy dependence via an off-shell transformation that introduces an extra nonlocality, reproduces the deep minima in neutron-deuteron elastic differential cross sections at incident energies 35–65 MeV and center-of-mass angles 130°–135°, consistent with the nearly correct triton binding energy predicted by fss2 without any three-body force.

## Method

The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum representation for neutron-deuteron scattering, using the NN sector of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2 formulated in the resonating-group method (RGM). The energy dependence of the RGM kernel is eliminated by a standard off-shell transformation using the square root of the normalization kernel, yielding an energy-independent renormalized RGM kernel with an extra nonlocality. The deuteron pole singularity in the NN t-matrix is handled by the Noyes-Kowalski method, and the moving singularity of the three-body free Green function is treated via a spline interpolation technique originally developed by the Bochum-Krakow group.

## Key numbers

- The calculation includes NN partial waves up to I_max = 4, with a maximum neutron incident energy of about E_n = 65 MeV in the laboratory system.
- The predicted elastic differential cross sections reproduce observed deep minima at E_n = 35–65 MeV and θ_cm = 130°–135°.
- The deficiency of the triton binding energy predicted by fss2 without three-body force is 350 keV, which is much smaller than the standard values of 0.5–1 MeV given by modern meson-exchange NN potentials.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the three-nucleon bound state using the quark-model NN interaction fss2 (Fujiwara et al., 2004) to the neutron-deuteron scattering continuum. It contrasts with modern meson-exchange NN potentials (e.g., those reviewed by Nogga et al., 2000) which typically require three-body forces to achieve correct triton binding and scattering observables. The results enable systematic investigation of nonlocality effects from quark-model interactions in three-nucleon scattering, with subsequent papers planned for polarization observables and deuteron breakup cross sections.
