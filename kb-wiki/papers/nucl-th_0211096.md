# Energy loss of partons traversing a QGP fluid

> We analyse the azimuthal correlation function for high p_T charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at the RHIC energy. By using a dynamical model in which hydrodynamics is combined with explicit propagation of high p_T partons, we study the effect of the intrinsic transverse momentum of initial partons on the azimuthal back-to-back correlations.

## Key claim

The authors find that parton energy loss alone, even when combined with large intrinsic transverse momentum of initial partons (up to \(\langle k_{\mathrm{T}}^2 \rangle = 4\) GeV\(^2\)/\(c^2\)), is insufficient to fully suppress the away-side peaks in azimuthal correlations of high-\(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) hadrons at RHIC; an additional mechanism beyond these effects is required to explain the observed disappearance of back-to-back correlations in central Au+Au collisions.

## Method

The authors use a "hydro+jet" model that couples a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation of the QGP fluid with explicit propagation of high-\(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) partons generated via PYTHIA 6.2 with CTEQ5 leading-order parton distribution functions and a Gaussian intrinsic \(k_{\mathrm{T}}\) distribution. Partons with initial transverse momentum above 2 GeV/\(c\) are propagated on straight-line trajectories through the dynamically evolving fluid, losing energy according to a formula \(\Delta E = C \int_{\tau_0}^{\tau_f} d\tau (\tau-\tau_0) \rho(\tau,\Vec{x}(\tau)) \ln(1+2E/\mu^2 R_{\mathrm{Au}})\) with screening mass \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV and coefficient \(C = 0.25\) tuned to reproduce the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\). The azimuthal correlation function for charged hadrons is computed for trigger particles with \(4 < p_{\mathrm{T, trigger}} < 6\) GeV/\(c\) and associated particles with \(2 < p_{\mathrm{T,associate}} < p_{\mathrm{T, trigger}}\) GeV/\(c\) in midrapidity (\(|\eta| < 0.7\)) for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic transverse momentum width is varied as \(\langle k_{\mathrm{T}}^2 \rangle = 1\), 2, and 4 GeV\(^2\)/\(c^2\) to study its effect on the azimuthal correlation function.
- The coefficient \(C = 0.25\) in the energy loss formula is chosen so that the suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) at \(p_{\mathrm{T}} = 3\) GeV/\(c\) matches that of a previous incoherent energy loss model (Hirano and Nara, 2002).
- The screening mass is taken as \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV.
- The minimum transverse momentum for hard partons is 2 GeV/\(c\).
- The initial time for hydrodynamic simulation is \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the hydro+jet model previously developed by Hirano and Nara (2002) to study the dynamical effect of hot QCD matter on parton energy loss, building on earlier hydrodynamic calculations by Hirano and Tsuda (2002) that reproduced low-\(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) spectra but deviated from data above 1.5-2.0 GeV/\(c\). The results contradict the simple expectation that parton energy loss plus intrinsic \(k_{\mathrm{T}}\) broadening can fully explain the disappearance of away-side peaks observed by the STAR collaboration (2002); the authors conclude that additional mechanisms, such as \(p_\perp\) broadening of jets in the medium (Baier et al., 2002), are needed to account for the experimental data.
