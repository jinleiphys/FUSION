# $Ξ$-nucleus potential and $(K^-,K^+)$ inclusive spectrum at $Ξ^-$ production threshold region

> The $(K^-,K^+)$ $Ξ^-$ production inclusive spectrum is reinvestigated in view of the very weak $Ξ$-nucleus potential predicted by microscopic calculations with the SU$_6$ quark-model baryon-baryon interaction. The inclusive spectrum is evaluated by the semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) method. The explicit comparison of the strength function with that of the Green-function method demonstrates the quantitative reliability of the SCDW approximation. It is presumed that the presently available data at the $Ξ$ production threshold region does not necessarily imply the attractive strength of about 15 MeV for the $Ξ$-nucleus potential in a conventional Woods-Saxon form. Instead, an almost zero potential is preferable.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the available $(K^-,K^+)$ $\Xi^-$ production data at the threshold region do not necessarily require an attractive $\Xi$-nucleus potential of about 15 MeV in a conventional Woods-Saxon form; instead, an almost zero potential is preferable, contradicting earlier DWIA analyses that claimed a depth of about 14 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) method to calculate the $(K^-,K^+)$ $\Xi^-$ inclusive production spectrum on $^{12}$C at an incident $K^-$ momentum of 1.8 GeV/c. The SCDW method treats the energy and angle dependences of the elementary $K^- + p \rightarrow K^+ + \Xi^-$ cross section explicitly and incorporates nuclear Fermi motion via the Wigner transformation of target single-particle wave functions, with kaon distorted waves described by the Klein-Gordon equation. The reliability of the SCDW approximation was verified by comparing its strength function with that of the Green-function method for three choices of the $\Xi$-nucleus potential strength.

## Key numbers

- The SCDW strength function agrees with the Green-function strength function to within at most 10% for the three tested $\Xi$-nucleus potential depths ($U_\Xi^0 = -20$, $-5$, and $+10$ MeV) on $^{12}$C at $p_{K^-}=1.8$ GeV/c.
- The $\Xi$-nucleus potential derived from the SU$_6$ quark-model interaction fss2 is parameterized as a sum of two Woods-Saxon forms with parameters: $U_1^0 = -4.139$ MeV, $r_{0,1}=3.569$ fm, $a_1=0.5291$ fm; $U_2^0 = +10.53$ MeV, $r_{0,2}=2.130$ fm, $a_2=0.3032$ fm.
- The experimental resolution of $\Delta E = 6.1$ MeV (Khaustov et al., 2000) was folded into the calculated spectrum using a Gaussian function.
- A half width $\Gamma/2 = 2$ MeV was used for the Lorentz-type smearing to account for inelastic channels, consistent with $\Xi^- p$ cross section measurements by Ahn et al. (2006).
- To match the SCDW cross sections at the threshold region using the Green-function method, a factor $\beta (d\sigma/d\Omega)_{av} = 0.33 \times 35$ $\mu$b/sr was needed, compared to $\beta = 0.73$ used in the earlier analysis by Khaustov et al. (2000) for $p_{K^-}=1.65$ GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic calculations of the $\Xi$-nucleus potential by Kohno and Fujiwara (2009) using the SU$_6$ quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2, which predicted a very weak potential fluctuating around zero inside the nucleus. It directly contradicts the earlier DWIA analyses by Fukuda et al. (1998) and Khaustov et al. (2000), which inferred a $\Xi$-nucleus potential depth of about 14–16 MeV from the same threshold data. The results enable future experiments at J-PARC to infer the $\Xi$-nucleus potential strength from the shape of the inclusive spectrum, even if no $\Xi$ bound state is observed.
