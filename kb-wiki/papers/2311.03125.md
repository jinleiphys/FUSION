# Charge and energy deposition in the McDIPPER framework

> In this short note we present aspects of the energy and charge deposition within the McDIPPER, a novel 3D resolved model for the initial state of ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion collisions based on the $k_\perp$-factorized Color Glass Condensate hybrid approach. This framework is a initial-state Monte Carlo event generator which deposits the relevant conserved charges (energy, charge and baryon densities) both in the midrapidity and forward/backward regions of the collision. The event-by-event generator computes the gluon and (anti-) quark phase-space densities using the IP-Sat model, from where the conserved charges can be extracted directly. In this work we present the centrality and collision energy dependence for the deposited conserved quantities at midrapidity and the full event, the so-called $4π$ solid angle range.

## Key claim

The McDIPPER framework deposits energy, electric charge, and baryon charge in both midrapidity and forward/backward regions of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the gluon energy contribution becoming more important at higher collision energies and the total integrated charges being virtually independent of collision energy due to strict conservation constraints from parton PDFs.

## Method

The framework computes inclusive gluon and net-quark single-particle distributions as functions of momentum rapidity, transverse momentum, and transverse position using leading-order cross-sections with transverse-momentum-dependent dipole gluon distributions from the IP-Sat model and collinear parton distributions from the LHAPDF library. Energy deposition is obtained by integrating the transverse momentum-weighted single-particle distributions, with a free parameter K_g=1.85 fixed by fitting to minimum-bias pp and pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. Electric and baryon charge densities are computed from the net-quark number densities using the respective quark electric and baryon charges.

## Key numbers

- K_g = 1.85 for the IP-Sat model, fixed by fitting to deposited energy of pp and pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- Quark contribution comprises a minimum of 20% of the energy deposition at the collision energies studied
- Total integrated electric and baryon charges are virtually constant with respect to sqrt(s_NN), depending only on geometrical constraints from sampling nucleons from a Woods-Saxon profile
- Midrapidity charge deposition decreases monotonically with increasing sqrt(s_NN), with nuclei becoming more transparent at midrapidity

## Context

This work extends the McDIPPER framework presented by Garcia-Montero et al. (2023) by providing a detailed study of initial charge and energy deposition as a function of centrality and collision energy. The framework is motivated by experimental observations of longitudinal structure in long-range correlations from CMS (2015) and theoretical work on 3D initial conditions by Petersen et al. (2011) and Pang et al. (2018). The results enable future quantitative studies using kinetic theory of the early-stage evolution of the thermalizing plasma, particularly for observables sensitive to chemical equilibration such as photon and dilepton production.
