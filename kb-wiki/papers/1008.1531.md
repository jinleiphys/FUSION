# Two-pion interferometry for viscous hydrodynamic sources

> The space-time evolution of the (1+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics with an initial quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is studied numerically. The particle-emitting sources undergo a crossover transition from the QGP to hadronic gas. We take into account a usual shear viscosity for the strongly coupled QGP as well as the bulk viscosity which increases significantly in the crossover region. The two-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry for the viscous hydrodynamic sources is performed. The HBT analyses indicate that the viscosity effect on the two-pion HBT results is small if only the shear viscosity is taken into consideration in the calculations. The bulk viscosity leads to a larger transverse freeze-out configuration of the pion-emitting sources, and thus increases the transverse HBT radii. The results of the longitudinal HBT radius for the source with Bjorken longitudinal scaling are consistent with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shear viscosity alone has a negligible effect on two-pion HBT radii, but the inclusion of bulk viscosity (which increases significantly in the crossover region) leads to larger transverse freeze-out configurations and thus increases the transverse HBT radii, with the effect being larger for lower freeze-out temperatures.

## Method

The authors numerically solved (1+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations based on the Israel-Stewart second-order formalism for a Bjorken-cylinder system with zero net baryon density, using the relativistic Harten-Lax-Leer-Einfeldt (RHLLE) algorithm. They incorporated both shear viscosity (with ratio to entropy density \(\widetilde{\eta}/s = 2C_s(1/4\pi)\) for \(C_s=1,2\)) and bulk viscosity (with \(\widetilde{\zeta}/\widetilde{\eta} \simeq -\kappa (v_s^2 -1/3)\), \(\kappa=4.75\)), using a parametric equation of state combining hadron resonance gas at low temperatures with lattice QCD at high temperatures, and then performed simulated two-pion Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry by generating pion momenta on the freeze-out hypersurface via the Cooper-Frye formula with viscous corrections.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c; initial central energy density \(\varepsilon_0 = 30\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (with additional calculations at 20 and 40 GeV/fm\(^3\)); Gaussian transverse width \(\sigma_0 = 3.3\) fm.
- Transition temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV; freeze-out temperatures studied: \(0.7 T_c\) and \(0.8 T_c\).
- For \(k_T = 0.24\) GeV/c and \(T_f = 0.8 T_c\): ideal fluid \(R_{\rm out} = 4.69 \pm 0.04\) fm, \(R_{\rm side} = 5.01 \pm 0.04\) fm, \(R_{\rm long} = 5.89 \pm 0.06\) fm; viscous fluid with both shear and bulk viscosities (\(C_s=2\)): \(R_{\rm out} = 4.89 \pm 0.04\) fm, \(R_{\rm side} = 5.02 \pm 0.05\) fm, \(R_{\rm long} = 6.16 \pm 0.06\) fm.
- For \(k_T = 0.84\) GeV/c and \(T_f = 0.8 T_c\): ideal fluid \(R_{\rm out} = 4.23 \pm 0.12\) fm, \(R_{\rm side} = 4.42 \pm 0.13\) fm, \(R_{\rm long} = 3.37 \pm 0.10\) fm; viscous fluid with both shear and bulk viscosities (\(C_s=2\)): \(R_{\rm out} = 4.77 \pm 0.14\) fm, \(R_{\rm side} = 4.85 \pm 0.15\) fm, \(R_{\rm long} = 3.67 \pm 0.12\) fm.
- Shear viscosity ratio \(\widetilde{\eta}/s = 2C_s(1/4\pi)\) with \(C_s=1,2\); bulk viscosity coefficient \(\kappa = 4.75\); relaxation times \(\tau_\pi = 6\widetilde{\eta}/(sT)\) and \(\tau_\Pi = \tau_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dissipative fluid dynamics in heavy ion collisions, particularly those investigating shear viscosity effects on transverse momentum distributions and elliptic flow (e.g., Romatschke (2007), Song and Heinz (2008), Luzum and Romatschke (2008)), and previous HBT studies for viscous sources (Romatschke (2007), Bozek (2009), Li et al. (2009)). By including both shear and bulk viscosities in a (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken-cylinder geometry with a crossover transition, the authors show that bulk viscosity significantly affects transverse HBT radii while shear viscosity alone does not, and that the longitudinal HBT radius results are consistent with experimental data from PHENIX (2004) and STAR (2005). This enables future work to use HBT interferometry as a probe of bulk viscosity in the quark-gluon plasma.
