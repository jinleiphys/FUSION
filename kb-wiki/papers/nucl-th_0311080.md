# Green's Function Approach to Inclusive Electron Scattering

> A Green's function approach to the inclusive quasielastic ($e,e'$) scattering is presented. The components of the nuclear response are written in terms of the single-particle optical model Green's function. The explicit calculation of the Green's function can be avoided by its spectral representation, which is based on a biorthogonal expansion in terms of the eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and of its Hermitian conjugate. This allows one to treat final state interactions consistently in the inclusive ($e,e'$) and in the exclusive ($e,e'N$) reactions. Numerical results for the longitudinal and transverse response functions obtained in a nonrelativistic and in a relativistic framework are presented and discussed also in comparison with data.

## Key claim

The Green's function approach, using a spectral representation based on a biorthogonal expansion of the non-Hermitian optical potential, allows a consistent treatment of final state interactions in both inclusive (e,e') and exclusive (e,e'N) reactions, but the authors explicitly state they do not aim at a unified and consistent description of longitudinal and transverse response data, as the model is a single-particle approach that retains only the one-body part of the nuclear current.

## Method

The nuclear response functions for inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering are expressed in terms of the single-particle optical model Green's function, avoiding explicit calculation through a spectral representation based on a biorthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. The hadron tensor is reduced to a single-particle expression using projection operators, with the interference between different channels included via a factor involving the energy derivative of the optical potential. The mean field is replaced by the empirical phenomenological optical potential, and the resulting matrix elements are calculated using the same relativistic and nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) treatments previously applied to exclusive (e,e'p) data.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal and transverse response functions calculated for 12C at q = 400 MeV/c and compared with Saclay data; the longitudinal response shows satisfactory agreement while the transverse response is underestimated.
- Longitudinal and transverse response functions calculated for 12C at q = 500 MeV/c and compared with Saclay data; good agreement is obtained for R_L while R_T is underestimated.
- Response functions for 40Ca at q = 450 MeV/c are compared with MIT-Bates and Saclay data; calculated results are of the same order of magnitude as MIT-Bates data, while for Saclay data R_L is overestimated and R_T underestimated.
- The contribution from the integral term in the hadron tensor expression is important and essential to reproduce the experimental longitudinal response in the relativistic frame, while in the nonrelativistic frame this contribution is very small and can be neglected.

## Context

This work extends the Green's function approach originally developed in a nonrelativistic frame (Capuzzi et al., earlier work) to a relativistic frame, building on the Feshbach projection operator formalism and multiple scattering theory (Horiuchi, Chinn et al., Bouchard, Capuzzi et al.). The approach enables consistent treatment of final state interactions in both inclusive and exclusive reactions, using the same phenomenological ingredients for bound state wave functions and optical potentials as in previous DWIA treatments of exclusive (e,e'p) data (Meucci et al., DWEEPY code). The results confirm the longstanding problem identified in the 1980s and 1990s (Oxford data, Sick) that the longitudinal response appears quenched while the transverse response shows excess strength, suggesting that two-body meson exchange currents and tensor correlations (Leidemann, Fabrocini) must be added to the present single-particle approach.
