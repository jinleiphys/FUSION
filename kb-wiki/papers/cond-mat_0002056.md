# Fermi systems with long scattering lengths

> Ground state energies and superfluid gaps are calculated for degenerate Fermi systems interacting via long attractive scattering lengths such as cold atomic gases, neutron and nuclear matter. In the intermediate region of densities, where the interparticle spacing $(\sim 1/k_F)$ is longer than the range of the interaction but shorter than the scattering length, the superfluid gaps and the energy per particle are found to be proportional to the Fermi energy and thus differs from the dilute and high density limits. The attractive potential increase linearly with the spin-isospin or hyperspin statistical factor such that, e.g., symmetric nuclear matter undergoes spinodal decomposition and collapses whereas neutron matter and Fermionic atomic gases with two hyperspin states are mechanically stable in the intermediate density region. The regions of spinodal instabilities in the resulting phase diagram are reduced and do not prevent a superfluid transition.

# Fermi systems with long scattering lengths

## Key claim

Ground state energies and superfluid gaps in degenerate Fermi systems with long attractive scattering lengths become proportional to the Fermi energy in the intermediate density region (where the interparticle spacing is longer than the range of the interaction but shorter than the scattering length), but this scaling holds only for systems with sufficiently few spin-isospin or hyperspin degrees of freedom; symmetric nuclear matter with four spin-isospin states undergoes spinodal decomposition and collapses in this regime, whereas neutron matter and two-hyperspin atomic gases remain mechanically stable.

## Method

The author solves Galitskii's integral equations for the in-medium scattering amplitude via ladder resummation, treating the two-body interaction as a contact potential with scattering length a and range R. The ground state energy is obtained from the on-shell effective scattering amplitude by summing over momenta of interacting particles, and the BCS superfluid gap is extracted from the Cooper pairing pole of the in-medium amplitude. The calculation is extended to finite temperatures using a high-temperature classical approximation for thermal pressure to construct the phase diagram, and the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation is employed as an independent check at intermediate densities.

## Key numbers

- For triplet 6Li atoms, the scattering length is a = -2160 Bohr radii; for neutron-neutron interactions, a ≈ -18.8 fm.
- The critical statistical factor for mechanical stability is ν_c ≈ 2.5, derived from the intermediate-density energy per particle expression E/N = E_F [3/5 - (ν-1)c_1] with c_1 = 35/9(11-2ln2) ≈ 0.40.
- In the dilute limit (k_F|a| ≪ 1), the BCS gap is Δ = E_F (8/e^2) exp(π/2k_F a); for ν spin states including Gorkov corrections, Δ = E_F (8/e^2) (4e)^{ν/3-1} exp(π/2ak_F).
- The dilute-limit energy per particle expansion is E/N = E_F [3/5 + (ν-1)(2/3π)k_F a + (ν-1)(4(11-2ln2)/35π^2)(k_F a)^2 + O((k_F a)^3)].
- The critical particle number for collapse under an artificial gravitational force is N_c ≈ (G m^3 a)^{-3/2}.

## Context

This work extends the study of dilute Fermi gases beyond the low-density regime where the scattering-length expansion (Lenz, 1950s; Huang and Yang, 1957) applies, into the intermediate density region where previous extrapolations fail. The results are supported by variational Monte Carlo calculations for neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter (Vijay, 1999) and by Pade approximants to R-matrix calculations (Baker, 1999). The paper enables predictions for ongoing cold-atom experiments with trapped fermionic gases (JILA, 1999) and provides a framework for understanding stability and superfluidity in systems ranging from neutron star matter to atomic gases with tunable interactions via Feshbach resonances.
