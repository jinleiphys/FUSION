# Exclusive production of a pair of high transverse momentum photons in pion-nucleon collisions for extracting generalized parton distributions

> We show that exclusive production of a pair of high transverse momentum photons in pion-nucleon collisions can be systematically studied in QCD factorization approach if the photon's transverse momentum $q_T$ with respect to the colliding pion is much greater than $Λ_{\rm QCD}$. We demonstrate that the leading power non-perturbative contributions to the scattering amplitudes of this exclusive process are process-independent and can be systematically factorized into universal pion's distribution amplitudes (DAs) and nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs), which are convoluted with corresponding infrared safe and perturbatively calculable short-distance hard parts. The correction to this factorized expression is suppressed by powers of $1/q_T$. We demonstrate quantitatively that this new type of exclusive processes is not only complementary to existing processes for extracting GPDs, but also capable of providing an enhanced sensitivity to the dependence of both DAs and GPDs on the active parton's momentum fraction $x$. We also introduce additional, but the same type of exclusive observables to enhance our capability to explore GPDs, in particular, their $x$-dependence.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that exclusive production of a high-transverse-momentum photon pair in pion-nucleon collisions can be systematically factorized in QCD when the photon transverse momentum \(q_T\) is much larger than \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), with power-suppressed corrections of order \(1/q_T\); however, the factorization proof requires separate treatment for two kinematic regions (ERBL and DGLAP regions of GPDs) due to differences in soft gluon pinch contributions in the Glauber region.

## Method

The authors develop a QCD collinear factorization formalism for the exclusive \(2\to 3\) process \(\pi(p_\pi)+N(p)\to\gamma(q_1)+\gamma(q_2)+N'(p')\), with the hard scale provided by the photon transverse momentum \(q_T\) and the soft scale by \(t=(p-p')^2\). They first demonstrate factorization for the simpler \(\pi^+\pi^-\to\gamma\gamma\) process, showing that loop momentum integrals are pinched to force active partons near their mass shells, then extend the argument to the pion-nucleon case by analyzing the Glauber region and the distinct ERBL and DGLAP kinematic regimes of the nucleon GPDs. The factorized amplitude is expressed as a convolution of a pion distribution amplitude (DA), a nucleon generalized parton distribution (GPD), and a perturbatively calculable short-distance hard coefficient.

## Key numbers

* The factorization condition requires \(q_T \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), where \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) is the QCD scale of order a few hundred MeV.
* The power-suppressed correction to the factorized expression is of order \(1/q_T\) (or equivalently \(|t|/q_T^2\)).
* The kinematic variable \(\kappa = 4q_T^2/s \leq 1\) relates the photon transverse momentum to the center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}\) of the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) collision.
* The typical hadronic scale is \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}) \sim 1/R\) with hadron radius \(R \sim 1\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the QCD factorization approach previously proved for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) in lepton-nucleon scattering by Collins et al. (1997, 1998), and for exclusive Drell-Yan processes in pion-nucleon collisions by Berger et al. (2001). It addresses the challenge that existing GPD extraction from DVCS suffers from a deconvolution problem, as shown by Bertone et al. (2021), where different \(x\)-dependent GPDs can fit the same Compton form factors. The proposed observable provides enhanced sensitivity to the \(x\)-dependence of both pion DAs and nucleon GPDs, and can be measured at facilities such as J-PARC.
