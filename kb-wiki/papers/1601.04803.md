# Systematic study of the isotopic dependence of fusion dynamics for neutron- and proton-rich nuclei using a proximity formalism

> The behaviors of barrier characteristics and fusion cross sections are analyzed by changing neutron over wide range of colliding systems. For this purpose, we have extended our previous study (Eur. Phys. J. A \textbf{48}, 21 (2012), it is devoted to the colliding systems with neutron-rich nuclei) to 125 isotopic systems with condition of $0.5\leq N/Z \leq 1.6$ for their compound nuclei. The AW 95, Bass 80, Denisov DP and Prox. 2010 potentials are used to calculate the nuclear part of interacting potential. The obtained results show that the trend of barrier heights $V_B$ and positions $R_B$ as well as nuclear $V_N$ and Coulomb $V_C$ potentials (at $R=R_B$) as a function of ($N/Z-1$) quantity are non-linear (second-order) whereas the fusion cross sections follow a linear-dependence.

# Systematic study of the isotopic dependence of fusion dynamics for neutron- and proton-rich nuclei using a proximity formalism

## Key claim

The barrier heights and positions, as well as nuclear and Coulomb potentials at the barrier radius, follow a non-linear (second-order) dependence on (N/Z-1) across the full range 0.5 ≤ N/Z ≤ 1.6, but the fusion cross sections exhibit a linear dependence on the same quantity. The linear behavior previously reported for neutron-rich systems (N/Z ≥ 1) also holds for proton-rich systems (N/Z ≤ 1) when considered separately, but the full range requires a quadratic parameterization.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear interaction potentials using four proximity formalisms (AW 95, Bass 80, Denisov DP, and Prox. 2010) for 125 fusion reactions involving C, O, Mg, Si, S, Ca, Ar, Ti, and Ni isotopes as colliding pairs, with compound nucleus N/Z ratios spanning 0.5 to 1.6. Barrier heights and positions were extracted from the total interaction potentials, and fusion cross sections were computed using the Wong model (one-dimensional penetration model) with the sharp cutoff formula at above-barrier energies. The percentage differences of barrier characteristics, nuclear and Coulomb potentials, and fusion cross sections were analyzed relative to the symmetric (N=Z) reaction for each isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- 125 isotopic systems were studied, with compound nucleus N/Z ratios in the range 0.5 ≤ N/Z ≤ 1.6
- The lightest proton-rich nucleus used was ¹⁰C (half-life 19.30 s) and the heaviest was ⁵⁴Ni (half-life 104.00 ms)
- The product of atomic numbers for the colliding pairs ranged from Z₁Z₂ = 84 to Z₁Z₂ = 784
- Fusion cross sections were computed at center-of-mass energies E_cm = 1.125 V_B⁰ and E_cm = 1.375 V_B⁰ (where V_B⁰ is the barrier height for the symmetric reaction)
- The selected proximity models reproduce experimental fusion barrier heights within ±10% on average
- The linear coefficient γ for the fusion cross section dependence Δσ_fus(%) = γ(N/Z-1) was tabulated for each potential and energy (Table 4 in the paper)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ghodsi et al., 2012) which analyzed 50 fusion reactions with 1 ≤ N/Z ≤ 1.6, and generalizes earlier investigations by other groups that examined Ca and Ni isotopes only (Refs. [1-3] in the paper, covering N/Z ranges of 1-2, 0.6-2, and 0.5-2). The present study is the first systematic analysis using the AW 95, Bass 80, Denisov DP, and Prox. 2010 potentials for proton-rich systems (N/Z ≤ 1), and it enables predictions of fusion properties for superheavy element synthesis where experimental data are unavailable. The results are consistent with the non-linear trends reported by earlier studies for Ca and Ni isotopes (Refs. [2,3]) but provide parameterized coefficients for a broader set of colliding systems.
