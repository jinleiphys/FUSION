# Initial fluctuations and correlation of finite distributions of secondary particles in interaction of heavy ions with photoemulsion nuclei

> The study of the peculiarities of the distribution of secondary particles depending on the degree centrality and the degree of asymmetry of the interacting nuclei, is performed. The number of multicharged fragments of the projectile nucleus $N_f$ in interactions sharply asymmetric nuclei depends on the centrality degree of interaction. At that, the events with $N_f$ =1 are separated clearly in the distribution of the total charge of the fragments of a projectile nucleus $Q$ depending on the nature of the correlation of the number of fragments of the target nucleus and the multiplicity of secondary particles.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the number of multicharged fragments of the projectile nucleus ($N_f$) in sharply asymmetric heavy-ion collisions depends on the centrality of the interaction, and that events with $N_f = 1$ can be separated in the distribution of the total charge of projectile fragments ($Q$) when correlated with the number of target fragments and secondary-particle multiplicity. The hedge is that this separation is only clearly observed for sharply asymmetric systems (gold on light emulsion nuclei) and that the interpretation relies on visual separation of peaks in the distributions.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental data from interactions of 10.7 AGeV $^{197}$Au ions with nuclear photoemulsion (containing both light CNO and heavy AgBr target nuclei). They studied the distribution of the total charge $Q$ of projectile fragments for events with different numbers of multicharged fragments $N_f$, and examined the correlation between the number of target fragments $N_h$ and the multiplicity of secondary particles $n_s$ to assess centrality and separate interactions with light and heavy emulsion nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 10.7 AGeV for $^{197}$Au ions.
- Events with $N_f = 1$ and $Q > 70$ are visually separated on a low background of other events, characterizing peripheral interactions.
- In $Au+AgBr$ interactions, the average $N_h$ shows steady growth up to $n_s = 110$, then decreases and plateaus for $n_s > 250$.
- In $Au+CNO$ interactions, the average $N_h$ shows similar behavior but at lower multiplicities: $n_s = 40$ and $n_s > 100$, respectively.
- Selection of interactions with light nuclei requires the maximum number of target fragments $N_h$ not to exceed 8 (corresponding to the charge of oxygen).

## Context
This work extends studies of initial-state fluctuations and centrality dependence in heavy-ion collisions, as previously discussed by authors cited in references [1]-[11] (unnamed in the text). It uses the specific dataset of 10.7 AGeV gold interactions with photoemulsion nuclei from reference [12]. The results enable separation of peripheral and central events in asymmetric collisions based on the correlation of target fragments and secondary-particle multiplicity, and provide a method to distinguish interactions with light (CNO) and heavy (AgBr) emulsion nuclei.
