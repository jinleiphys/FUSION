# Chiral and U(1) axial symmetry restoration in linear sigma models with two quark flavors

> We study the restoration of chiral symmetry in linear sigma models with two quark flavors. The models taken into consideration have a U(2)_L x U(2)_R and an O(4) internal symmetry. The physical mesons of these models are sigma, pion, eta and a_0 where the latter two are not present in the O(4) model. Including two-loop contributions through sunset graphs we calculate the temperature behavior of the order parameter and the masses for explicit chiral symmetry breaking and in the chiral limit. Decay threshold effects introduced by the sunset graphs alter the temperature dependence of the condensate and consequently that of the masses as well. This correctly reproduces a second-order phase transition for the O(4) model and for the U(2)_L x U(2)_R model with an axial U(1) anomaly as expected from universality class arguments. Chiral symmetry tends to be restored at higher temperatures in the two-loop approximation than in the Hartree-Fock approximation. To model a restoration of the axial U(1) symmetry we imply a temperature-dependent anomaly parameter that sharply drops at about 175 MeV. This triggers the restoration of chiral symmetry before the full symmetry is restored and lowers the transition temperatures significantly below 200 MeV.

## Key claim

Including two-loop sunset graphs in the linear sigma model with two quark flavors correctly reproduces a second-order phase transition for both the O(4) model and the U(2)_L x U(2)_R model with an axial U(1) anomaly, as expected from universality class arguments, but the transition temperatures are significantly higher than those suggested by lattice QCD calculations unless a temperature-dependent anomaly parameter that drops sharply at about 175 MeV is introduced.

## Method

The author computed the effective potential using the two-particle point-irreducible (2PPI) effective action formalism including all graphs up to two loops, with the two-loop contributions consisting of sunset graphs that incorporate decay threshold effects. For the U(2)_L x U(2)_R model, the mass gap equations were solved in the Hartree-Fock approximation while the condensate equation included the full two-loop sunset contributions, a simplification necessitated by numerical instability near decay thresholds. The parameters were fixed at tree-level using experimental meson masses from the Particle Physics Booklet, with the sigma meson mass set to 600 MeV and the condensate fixed to the pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- In the O(4) model chiral limit, the two-loop approximation yields a second-order phase transition at T_c approximately 210 MeV, compared to a first-order transition at T_c approximately 181 MeV in the Hartree-Fock approximation
- In the U(2)_L x U(2)_R model with axial anomaly in the chiral limit, the Hartree-Fock approximation gives a first-order phase transition at T_c approximately 178 MeV, while the two-loop approximation gives a second-order transition at T_c approximately 272 MeV
- The temperature-dependent anomaly parameter is modeled to sharply drop at about 175 MeV, which lowers transition temperatures significantly below 200 MeV
- The condensate phi_0 is fixed to 92.4 MeV for explicit symmetry breaking and 90 MeV in the chiral limit
- The sigma meson mass is set to 600.0 MeV, pion mass to 139.6 MeV, a_0 mass to 984.7 MeV, and eta mass to 547.3 MeV for explicit symmetry breaking with anomaly

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of linear sigma models using the Hartree-Fock approximation by Roder (2003) and Lenaghan (2000), and the O(N) model analyses by Baacke (2002, 2003) using the 2PPI formalism, by including two-loop sunset contributions that capture decay threshold effects. The results contradict the first-order phase transitions found in Hartree-Fock approximations for both models and instead confirm the universality class predictions of Pisarski and Wilczek (1983) for second-order transitions. The temperature-dependent anomaly parameter provides a phenomenological model for axial U(1) symmetry restoration, enabling future studies of how instanton effects might lower the chiral transition temperature toward the lattice QCD values of approximately 170 MeV for two flavors found by Karsch (2001) and Laermann (2003).
