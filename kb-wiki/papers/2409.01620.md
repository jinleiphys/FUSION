# Entropy in Toy Regge models

> The probabilistic interpretation of the standard Regge-Gribov model with triple pomeron interactions is discussed. It is stated that introduction of probabilities within this model is not unique and depends on what is meant under the relevant substructures, The traditional interpretation in terms of partons (quarks and gluons) is shown to be external to the model, imported from the QCD, and actually referring to the single pomeron exchange without interactions. So this interpretation actually forgets the model as such. Alternative probabilities based on the pomerons as basic quantities within the model are discussed. Two different approaches are considered, based either on the pomerons in Fock's expansion of the wave function or on pomeron propagators in Feynman diagrams. These pomeron probabilities and entropy turn out to be very different from the mentioned standard ones in the purelyp obabilistic treatment. The entropy, in particular, either rises with the rapidity and saturates at a certain fixed value or first rises, reaches some maximum and goes down to zero afterwards. Possible observable manifestations of these probabilities and entropy are to be seen in the distributions of the cross-section in powers of the coupling constants to the participants. 23

## Key claim

The probabilistic interpretation of the standard Regge-Gribov model with triple pomeron interactions is not unique; the traditional parton-based probabilities (imported from QCD) are external to the model and effectively ignore the pomeron interactions, while alternative pomeron-based probabilities defined within the model yield radically different entropy behaviors.

## Method

The author considers the zero-dimensional (toy) Regge-Gribov model with a non-Hermitian Hamiltonian containing a triple pomeron vertex. Two alternative definitions of pomeron-number probabilities are introduced: one based on rescaling the coefficients of the Fock expansion of the wave function, and another based on counting pomeron propagators in tree-level Feynman diagrams. These are applied to the quasi-classical fan diagram approximation and to multiple-fan (eikonal) and nucleus-nucleus scattering cases, with numerical evolution of the coefficients and iterative solution of the equations of motion.

## Key numbers

- For the fan diagram case with the rescaling method, the average number of pomerons saturates at \(\bar{n}_\infty = 1 + 1/\rho\) where \(\rho = \mu/\lambda\); for \(\mu=0.1\) and \(\lambda=0.01\) (\(\rho=10\)), the limiting entropy is \(S_\infty = 0.3551\).
- For the fan diagram case with the diagrammatic method, the entropy starts at zero, reaches a maximum at approximately \(\mu y = 0.7\) for \(\rho=1\), and then falls to zero at large rapidity.
- For the eikonal (multiple fan) case with the reaction-diffusion approach, the entropy at \(y=0\) is \(S(0)=2.561\) for \(\mu=0.1\), \(\lambda=0.01\), and \(g_1=1\).
- For the eikonal case with the rescaling method, the entropy at \(y=0\) is \(S(0)=0.8121\) for the same parameters.
- For the nucleus-nucleus scattering case with \(\mu=0.1\) and \(\lambda=0.01\), the action values at \(y=10,20,30,40,50\) are 2.052, 3.569, 5.406, 7.400, and 9.413 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the probabilistic interpretation of toy Regge-Gribov models previously studied in the reaction-diffusion approach by Braun (2024) and in earlier works by Amati et al. (1970s) and by Schwimmer (1980s). The parton-based probabilities and entropy, which grow linearly with rapidity and were related to hadron multiplicities by Kharzeev (2000s) and by Hentschinski et al. (2020s), are shown to be incompatible with the internal structure of the Regge-Gribov model itself. The alternative pomeron-based definitions enable a direct connection to the distribution of the scattering amplitude in powers of the coupling constants to the participants, which could be tested experimentally by varying the transverse distribution of the nuclear target.
