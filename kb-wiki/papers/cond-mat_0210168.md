# Effective mass in quasi two-dimensional systems

> The effective mass of the quasiparticle excitations in quasi two-dimensional systems is calculated analytically. It is shown that the effective mass increases sharply when the density approaches the critical one of metal-insulator transition. This suggests a Mott type of transition rather than an Anderson like transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the sharp increase of the effective mass in quasi two-dimensional systems as density approaches the metal-insulator transition can be explained quantitatively by Coulomb correlations between electrons and charged impurities, supporting a Mott-type transition rather than an Anderson-type transition. The hedge is that the calculation assumes a large mass difference between transport electrons and scattering impurity ions, and the nature of the insulating state itself is not clarified.

## Method

The author calculates the quasiparticle effective mass analytically using the dynamically screened approximation (GW) for the selfenergy, expressed via real-time nonequilibrium Green's functions. The model considers electrons scattering with heavy impurity ions in a quasi two-dimensional electron gas, where the large mass difference suppresses vertex corrections and allows neglect of quantum fluctuations of the impurities. The effective mass is derived from the on-shell selfenergy at the Fermi surface, and the result is compared with experimental data without any adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- For typical densities of \(7 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\), the inverse screening length parameter is \(\kappa_a = 284.9 / \sqrt{n_a / (7 \times 10^{10} \, \text{cm}^{-2})} \cdot (m^* / m)\).
- The on-shell selfenergy at the Fermi surface is \(\Sigma_a(p_f, p_f^2 / 2 m_a) / \epsilon_f = \pi^2 (T / \epsilon_f) \kappa_a\).
- The effective mass ratio is given by \(m / m^* = (1 - \pi^2 (T / \epsilon_f) \kappa_a + 4 (n_i / n_a) (m g_{ab} r_0 / \hbar^2)^2 (\ln \kappa_i - 1))\) for small corrections.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the metal-insulator transition in quasi two-dimensional systems, following experimental measurements by Shashkin et al. (2001) that showed a sharp increase in effective mass near the critical density. The calculation reproduces these experimental values without additional parameters, supporting a Mott transition picture over an Anderson transition scenario. The approach enables further investigation of the temperature dependence of conductivity and the role of neutral impurities near the transition, with alternative mechanisms such as three-particle clusters (Nakhmedov and Morawetz, 2001) remaining possible in high magnetic fields.
