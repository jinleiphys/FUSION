# A new renormalization procedure of the quasiparticle random phase approximation

> The ground state of a many body Hamiltonian considered in the quasiparticle representation is redefined by accounting for the quasiparticle quadrupole pairing interaction. The residual interaction of the newly defined quasiparticles is treated by the QRPA. Solutions of the resulting equations exhibit specific features. In particular, there is no interaction strength where the first root is vanishing. A comparison with other renormalization methods is presented.

# A new renormalization procedure of the quasiparticle random phase approximation

## Key claim

The proposed renormalization procedure prevents the QRPA collective excitation energy from vanishing at a critical interaction strength; instead, the first root reaches a minimum value and then increases with further increase of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength, as if the effective interaction changes from attractive to repulsive.

## Method

The ground state of a many-body Hamiltonian in the quasiparticle representation is redefined by including quasiparticle quadrupole pairing correlations via a unitary transformation of the BCS vacuum, producing "deformed quasiparticles" with magnetic-quantum-number-dependent energies. The residual interaction of these new quasiparticles is then treated by the standard QRPA formalism, yielding a dispersion equation for the phonon energies. The method is applied numerically to a single $j = i_{13/2}$ shell with 10 nucleons.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical QRPA (first-order BCS) with $G = 0.4$ MeV, the quasiparticle energy is $E_q = 1.4$ MeV, and the collective mode energy vanishes at $X \approx 1.82 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV fm$^{-4}$.
- The deformed quasiparticle energies $e_m$ are computed for $q_0 = 20$ fm$^2$ and shown as functions of $X$ for the $j = i_{13/2}$ multiplet.
- The first QRPA root $\omega(1)$ reaches a minimum at the same $X$ value where the spherical QRPA energy vanishes, and then increases for larger $X$.
- The average number of quasiparticles in the second-order BCS state, $\langle \widetilde{BCS} | \hat{N}_q | \widetilde{BCS} \rangle = \sum_m v_m^2$, increases with increasing $X$.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach of Hara (1964), which corrected the quasi-boson approximation to make the collective root vanish only asymptotically, and the proton-neutron extension by Toivanen and Suhonen. It contrasts with standard QRPA, which collapses at a critical interaction strength (Kumar and Baranger, 1968). The new method enables a unified description of spherical and deformed systems using a spherical single-particle basis, and is compatible with subsequent angular momentum projection to define $K=0$ rotational bands.
