# Energy Loss versus Shadowing in the Drell-Yan Reaction on Nuclei

> We present a new analysis of the E772 and E866 experiments on the nuclear dependence of Drell-Yan (DY) lepton pair production resulting from the bombardment of $^2H$, Be, C, Ca, Fe, and W targets by 800 GeV/c protons at Fermilab. We employ a light-cone formulation of the DY reaction in the rest frame of the nucleus, where the dimuons detected at small values of Bjorken x_2 << 1 may be considered to originate from the decay of a heavy photon radiated from an incident quark in a bremsstrahlung process. We infer the energy loss of the quark by examining the suppression of the nuclear-dependent DY ratios seen as a function of projectile momentum fraction x_1 and dimuon mass M. Shadowing, which also leads to nuclear suppression of dimuons, is calculated within the same approach employing the results of phenomenological fits to deep inelastic scattering data from HERA. The analysis yields -dE/dz =2.73 +/- 0.37 +/- 0.5 GeV/fm for the rate of quark energy loss per unit path length, a value consistent with theoretical expectations including the effects of the inelastic interaction of the incident proton at the surface of the nucleus. This is the first observation of a nonzero energy loss effect in such experiments.

## Key claim

The analysis claims the first observation of a nonzero quark energy loss effect in Drell-Yan experiments, extracting a value of \(-dE/dz = 2.73 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.5\) GeV/fm, but this result depends on correctly separating shadowing contributions using a light-cone formulation in the target rest frame, unlike previous analyses that overestimated shadowing and found energy loss consistent with zero.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the E772 and E866 experimental data on Drell-Yan dimuon production from 800 GeV/c protons incident on \(^2\)H, Be, C, Ca, Fe, and W targets. They employed a light-cone formulation in the nuclear rest frame where the Drell-Yan process at small Bjorken \(x_2\) is treated as bremsstrahlung of a heavy photon from an incident quark, with the color-dipole cross section fitted to HERA deep inelastic scattering data used to calculate nuclear shadowing. The quark energy loss was inferred from the suppression of nuclear-dependent Drell-Yan cross section ratios as a function of projectile momentum fraction \(x_1\) and dimuon mass \(M\), with the path length distribution calculated for each nucleus rather than assuming an \(A^{1/3}\) dependence.

## Key numbers

- Quark energy loss rate: \(-dE/dz = 2.73 \pm 0.37 \pm 0.5\) GeV/fm, extracted from the combined E772/E866 data
- String model prediction for the constant energy loss rate: \(\kappa_s \approx 1\) GeV/fm, derived from the mesonic Regge trajectory slope parameter \(\alpha_R'\) via \(\kappa_s = 1/(2\pi\alpha_R')\)
- Effective quark mass used to regularize the light-cone wave function: \(m_q \approx 0.2\) GeV, taken from previous DIS analyses
- Nuclear density used in path length calculations: \(\rho_A \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Quark-nucleon cross section in the additive quark model: \(\sigma^{qN} = \sigma^{NN}_{in}/3 \approx 10\) mb

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of the E772 data by Gavai and Moss (ignoring shadowing) and the E866 collaboration analysis by Vasiliev et al. (which included shadowing from a phenomenological fit and found energy loss consistent with zero). The authors argue that the Vasiliev et al. analysis overestimated shadowing by using a phenomenological extraction that had already attributed all suppression to shadowing, whereas the present work calculates shadowing from first principles using the universal color-dipole cross section fitted to HERA DIS data. This enables a clean separation of energy loss and shadowing effects, and the result supports theoretical expectations from string models and perturbative QCD that predict a total energy loss rate of about 2 GeV/fm from the combination of constant string tension and induced energy loss from multiple interactions.
