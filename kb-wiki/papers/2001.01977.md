# A proton imagining via double parton scattering

> In this contribution we discuss the main outcomes of our studies on the so called double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), accessible quantities in high energy proton-proton and proton nucleus collisions, in double parton scattering processes (DPS). These new distributions are almost unknown, nevertheless they encode information on how partons inside a proton are correlated among each other. Double PDFs represent a new tool to explore the three dimensional partonic structure of hadrons. Here, we show results obtained from calculations of dPFDs. In particular we focus our attention on the impact of double correlations in experimental observables by showing how the latter could be studied in the next LHC run. We also discuss how the present knowledge on a peculiar experimental observable could unveil new information on the transverse proton structure

# A proton imaging via double parton scattering

## Key claim
Double parton correlations (DPCs) in double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) cannot be easily neglected even under experimental kinematic conditions at the LHC, and their effects on the observable σ_eff could be measured in the next LHC run with a luminosity of 1000 fb⁻¹ at 68% confidence level, provided the departure of σ_eff from a constant value is observed.

## Method
The author used constituent quark model (CQM) calculations to evaluate double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) at the energy scale of LHC experiments, including non-perturbative double parton correlations. These dPDFs were then employed to compute the DPS cross section and the effective cross section σ_eff for same-sign W pair production, a golden channel for DPS observation, and the results were compared to experimental data and to calculations neglecting correlations.

## Key numbers
- The total same-sign W pair DPS cross section (sum of W⁺W⁺ and W⁻W⁻ decays into same-sign muons) is found to be σ⁺⁺ + σ⁻⁻ ∼ 0.69 fb.
- The mean value of the effective cross section for this process is ⟨σ̃_eff⟩ ∼ 21.04 mb.
- The experimental constant value of σ_eff is approximately 15 mb, compatible with data within errors when double parton correlations are neglected.
- A luminosity of L ∼ 1000 fb⁻¹ is estimated to be sufficient to measure the departure of σ_eff from a constant value at 68% confidence level in the next LHC run.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of double parton distribution functions using constituent quark models (Rinaldi et al., 2015-2019) and addresses the open question of whether double parton correlations can be neglected in experimental analyses, as is commonly done. The results enable future experimental measurements at the LHC to extract new information on the transverse proton structure, specifically the mean transverse distance ⟨z_⊥²⟩ between two partons, which can be bounded by the inequality σ_eff/(3π) ≤ ⟨z_⊥²⟩ ≤ σ_eff/π, a relation verified across all available dPDF models including for the pion target.
