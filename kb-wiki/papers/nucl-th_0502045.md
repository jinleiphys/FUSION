# On the Interpretation of the Processes 3He(e,e'p)2H and 3He(e,e'p)(pn) at High Missing Momenta

> It is shown that the effects of the Final State Interaction (FSI) in the exclusive processes 3He(e,e'p)2H and 3He(e,e'p)(pn), can be successfully treated in terms of a Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA), based upon the direct calculation of the Feynman diagrams describing the rescattering of the struck nucleon. As a matter of fact, using realistic three-body wave functions corresponding to the AV18 interaction, the results of our parameter free calculation exhibits a very satisfactory agreement with recent experimental data from Jlab covering the region of missing momentum and energy with p_m < 1.0 GeV/c and E_m < 100 MeV. It is demonstrated that the missing momentum dependence of the experimental cross section of the process 3He(e,e'p)2H is mainly governed at p_m < 0.2 GeV/c by the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA), at 0.2 < p_m < 0.6 GeV/c by the single rescattering FSI, and at 0.6 GeV/c < p_m by the double rescattering FSI.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the missing momentum dependence of the cross section for 3He(e,e'p)2H can be interpreted as a signature of different orders of final state rescattering, with the Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA) providing a parameter-free description that agrees with JLab data up to p_m = 1.0 GeV/c, though the authors note that a systematic underestimation of the 3bbu channel data is observed at p_m = 820 MeV/c and E_rel <= 110 MeV.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross sections for 2-body breakup (2bbu) and 3-body breakup (3bbu) channels using realistic three-body wave functions from the AV18 interaction, treating final state interactions via a Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA) based on direct evaluation of Feynman diagrams for single and double rescattering of the struck nucleon. The GEA extends the standard Glauber approximation by partially removing the frozen approximation through inclusion of longitudinal momentum transfer corrections (Delta_z terms). The electron-nucleon cross section used was sigma_cc1 from Forest (1996), and all NN scattering parameters (total cross section, ratio of real to imaginary parts, slope) were taken from energy-dependent values of Said (2004).

## Key numbers

- Missing momentum range covered: p_m < 1.0 GeV/c (experimental data) and calculations extended to p_m <= 1.2 GeV/c
- Missing energy range: E_m <= 100 MeV
- Four-momentum transfer for JLab data: Q^2 = 1.55 (GeV/c)^2, x = 1
- The PWIA dominates at p_m < 0.2 GeV/c, single rescattering FSI dominates at 0.2 < p_m < 0.6 GeV/c, and double rescattering FSI dominates at p_m > 0.6 GeV/c
- Effects of the Delta corrections in the GEA were found to be of order few percent for the kinematics considered

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Ciofi degli Atti and Kaptari, 2004, referred to as I) which had been compared with preliminary JLab data up to p_m <= 0.6 GeV/c, now covering the full published JLab data range up to p_m <= 1.0 GeV/c from JLab1 and JLab2 (2005). The results are contrasted with a recent calculation by Laget (2005) which achieves agreement at high missing momentum through a three-body mechanism involving meson emission and reabsorption, and the authors note that clarifying the differences between the two approaches requires further experimental data at higher p_m for 3He(e,e'p)X and for the process 4He(e,e'p)3H.
