# Electric dipole moments of superheavy elements - A case study on copernicium

> The multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method was employed to calculate atomic electric dipole moments (EDM) of the superheavy element copernicium (Cn, $Z=112$). The EDM enhancement factors of Cn, here calculated for the first time, are about one order of magnitude larger than those of Hg. The exponential dependence of enhancement factors on atomic number $Z$ along group 12 of the periodic table was derived from the EDMs of the entire homolog series, $^{69}_{30}$Zn, $^{111}_{\phantom{1}48}$Cd, $^{199}_{\phantom{1}80}$Hg, $^{285}_{112}$Cn, and $^{482}_{162}$Uhb. These results show that superheavy elements with sufficiently large half-lives are good candidates for EDM searches.

## Key claim

The multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) calculations show that the atomic electric dipole moment (EDM) enhancement factors of copernicium (Cn, Z=112) are about one order of magnitude larger than those of mercury (Hg), but this result is conditional on the specific isotope chosen (285Cn) and the accuracy of the computed energy denominators, which the authors estimate to be better than 20% for the lowest excited levels of mercury.

## Method

The authors employed the multiconfiguration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method, implemented in the GRASP2K package, to generate numerical wave functions for the ground states of five group-12 diamagnetic atoms: 69Zn, 111Cd, 199Hg, 285Cn, and 482Uhb. They evaluated atomic EDM contributions from four mechanisms (tensor-pseudotensor, pseudoscalar-scalar, nuclear Schiff moment, and electron EDM interaction with the nuclear magnetic field) by summing over bound excited states using equation (4) from their prior work, with a Riemann zeta tail correction applied for 199Hg and 285Cn to account for higher-lying bound states.

## Key numbers

* The TPT interaction contribution to the EDM of 285Cn at the VOS.5 level is -57.51 (Th) in units of 10^-20 C_T <sigma_A> |e| cm, compared to -4.84 for 199Hg at the same level.
* The PSS interaction contribution to the EDM of 285Cn at VOS.5 is -236.88 (Th) in units of 10^-23 C_P <sigma_A> |e| cm, compared to -17.02 for 199Hg.
* The Schiff moment contribution to the atomic EDM of 285Cn at VOS.5 is -17.62 (Th) in units of 10^-17 [S/(|e| fm^3)] |e| cm, compared to -2.22 for 199Hg.
* The electron EDM contribution to the atomic EDM of 285Cn at VOS.5 is 299.67 (Th) in units of d_e x 10^-4, compared to 10.00 for 199Hg.
* The Riemann zeta tail correction is about 1.5% for 199Hg and below 2% for 285Cn.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous MCDHF calculations on mercury and ytterbium (Radziute et al., 2014) to the superheavy element copernicium, providing the first EDM enhancement factors for Cn. The results show an exponential dependence of enhancement factors on atomic number Z along group 12, which the authors derived from the full homologous series including Zn, Cd, Hg, Cn, and the predicted element Uhb (Z=162). This enables future experimental EDM searches using superheavy elements with sufficiently long half-lives, such as the predicted 290-294 isotopes of copernicium which may have half-lives counted in years (Zagrebaev, 2013).
