# One- and two- neutron halo at the dripline. From 11Be to 11Li and back: 10Li and parity inversion

> The nuclei 11Be and 11Li provide paradigmatic examples of one-and two- neutron halo systems. Because the reaction 1H(11Li,9Li)3H is dominated by successive transfer, one can use the quantitative picture emerging from a nu- clear field theory description of the structure and reaction mechanism of the above Cooper pair transfer process and of the 2H(10Be,11Be)1H and 1H(11Be,10Be)2H reactions, to shed light on the structure of 10Li. This analysis provides important support for a parity inverted scenario with a 1/2+ virtual state at about 0.2 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parity-inverted scenario in \(^{10}\)Li, with a \(1/2^+\) virtual state at about 0.2 MeV, is strongly supported by a nuclear field theory description of the structure and reaction mechanisms of the one- and two-neutron halo nuclei \(^{11}\)Be and \(^{11}\)Li, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the reaction \(^1\)H(\(^{11}\)Li,\(^9\)Li)\(^3\)H is dominated by successive transfer rather than direct pair transfer.

## Method

The authors apply nuclear field theory (NFT) to calculate the renormalization of single-particle states in \(^{11}\)Be, \(^{10}\)Li, and \(^{11}\)Li by coupling them to the quadrupole vibration of the core (\(^{10}\)Be or \(^9\)Li). For \(^{11}\)Li, they also compute the soft dipole pygmy resonance (DPR) using a QRPA approach with a separable dipole-dipole interaction, and estimate the induced pairing interaction between halo neutrons from the exchange of this DPR mode. The structure results are then used to interpret one- and two-neutron transfer reaction cross sections.

## Key numbers

- \(^{11}\)Be: dressed single-particle energies \(\tilde{\epsilon}_{s_{1/2}} = -0.5\) MeV, \(\tilde{\epsilon}_{p_{1/2}} = -0.18\) MeV, \(\tilde{\epsilon}_{d_{5/2}} = 1.45\) MeV (Table 1).
- \(^{11}\)Be: \(B(E1; 1/2^- \to 1/2^+) = 0.11\, e^2\text{ fm}^2\) (theory), compared to experimental \(0.102 \pm 0.002\, e^2\text{ fm}^2\), carrying about 10% of the TRK sum rule.
- \(^{11}\)Li: effective radius \(R_{eff} \approx 4.8\) fm, leading to \(\langle r^2\rangle^{1/2} \approx 3.7\) fm, compared to experimental \(3.55 \pm 0.1\) fm.
- \(^{11}\)Li: DPR centroid energy \(\hbar\omega_{DPR} \approx 1\) MeV, compared to experimental \(1.03 \pm 0.03\) MeV from Kanungo (2015).
- \(^{11}\)Li: correlation energy of the halo Cooper pair \(E_{corr} \approx -0.4\) MeV, compared to experimental \(-380\) keV.
- \(^{10}\)Li: renormalized energies \(\tilde{\epsilon}_{s_{1/2}} = 0.15\) MeV (virtual state) and \(\tilde{\epsilon}_{p_{1/2}} = 0.60\) MeV (resonant state) (Table 2).
- \(^{10}\)Li: single-particle content (Z-factor) of both \(\widetilde{1/2}^+\) and \(\widetilde{1/2}^-\) states is 0.83.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear field theory approach previously applied to \(^{11}\)Be by Barranco (2017) to the two-neutron halo system \(^{11}\)Li and the unbound system \(^{10}\)Li. The analysis supports a parity-inverted scenario for \(^{10}\)Li with a low-lying \(s_{1/2}\) virtual state, contradicting the interpretation of Cavallaro (2017) which found little \(s_{1/2}\) strength; the authors argue this discrepancy arises from the limited angular range of those measurements. The results enable a unified description of one- and two-neutron halo structure and transfer reactions, and suggest that the \(0^{+*}\) state of \(^{12}\)Be at \(E_x \approx 2.25\) MeV and the 2.71 MeV \(1^-\) state on top of it can be understood as excitations built on the halo pair addition mode of \(^{11}\)Li.
