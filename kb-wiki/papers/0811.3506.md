# Production of fragments with and without strangeness within a combined BUU+SMM approach

> The formation of hypernuclei in hadron-induced reactions and in heavy-ion collisions within a combination of a covariant transport model and a statistical fragmentation approach is investigated. We study the applicability and limitations of such a hybrid approach by comparing fragmentation data in heavy-ion collisions and proton induced reactions. It turns out that the combined approach describes fairly well multiplicities and momentum spectra of fragments. We thus extend the model by including strangeness degrees of freedom in the fragmentation process, modeled by a phase-space coalescence method. We provide theoretical predictions on spectra and on inclusive cross sections of light hypernuclei for the future experiments on hypernuclear physics at the new GSI facility.

## Key claim

The combined GiBUU+SMM hybrid approach reproduces fragment multiplicities and momentum spectra fairly well in both proton-induced reactions and spectator fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions, but the extension to hypernuclei relies on a phase-space coalescence method whose parameters are adjusted to match earlier predictions by Wakai et al. (2005), rather than being independently determined from first principles.

## Method

The initial non-equilibrium stage of hadron-induced or heavy-ion reactions is modeled by the covariant relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport equation, implemented as the Giessen-BUU (GiBUU) code, which evolves the one-body phase-space distribution function for nucleons, resonances, pions, kaons, and other mesons. When the system reaches an equilibrated excited configuration (identified by an anisotropy ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressure approaching unity, typically at t ~ 75 fm/c), the subsequent fragmentation is treated statistically using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM). For hypernuclei, strangeness degrees of freedom are added via a phase-space coalescence algorithm in coordinate and momentum space, with coalescence parameters fixed to results from Wakai et al. (2005).

## Key numbers

- In p+Pb at 0.8 GeV, double differential cross sections for emitted neutrons are reproduced by the combined model, matching experimental data from the spectraEXP collaboration.
- In p+Au at 0.8 GeV, mass distributions for isotopes in the spallation region and for fission fragments are reproduced reasonably by the combined model, compared with data from yieldsEXP.
- In Au+Au at 0.6 AGeV, the centrality dependence of average mass number and excitation energy per nucleon of spectator matter quantitatively fits data from the ALADIN collaboration.
- For 12C+12C at 2 AGeV, hyperfragment production is about 5-6 orders of magnitude less than normal fragment production.
- Total production cross sections for light hypernuclei in spectator fragmentation are: 2.2 microbarn for 4ΛH, 4 microbarn for 4ΛHe, and 1.4 microbarn for 5ΛH.
- The contribution from secondary pion-nucleon scattering inside spectators is moderate: 0.3 microbarn for 4ΛH, 0.2 microbarn for 4ΛHe, and 0.03 microbarn for 5ΛH.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical studies by Wakai et al. (2005) on hypernucleus production and by Kerman and Weiss (1973) on the original proposal for light hypernuclei formation, while also providing predictions for the HypHI and PANDA collaborations at the new FAIR facility at GSI. The combined GiBUU+SMM approach enables future comparisons with experimental data from the upcoming FAIR experiments, and provides a basis for investigating double-strange hypernuclei and exotic multi-strange bound objects such as A XΛΛ and A XΩ in antiproton-induced reactions.
