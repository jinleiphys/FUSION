# Impurity effect of Lambda particle on the structure of 18F and 19F_Lambda

> We perform three-body model calculations for a $sd$-shell hypernucleus $^{19}_Λ$F ($^{17}_Λ{\rm O}+p+n$) and its core nucleus $^{18}$F ($^{16}{\rm O}+p+n$), employing a density-dependent contact interaction between the valence proton and neutron. We find that the $B(E2)$ value from the first excited state (with spin and parity of $I^π=3^+$) to the ground state ($I^π=1^+$) is slightly decreased by the addition of a $Λ$ particle, which exhibits the so called shrinkage effect of $Λ$ particle. We also show that the excitation energy of the $3^+$ state is reduced in $^{19}_Λ$F compared to $^{18}$F, as is observed in a $p$-shell nucleus $^{6}$Li. We discuss the mechanism of this reduction of the excitation energy, pointing out that it is caused by a different mechanism from that in $^{7}_Λ$Li.

## Key claim

The addition of a Λ particle to ¹⁸F reduces both the B(E2) value and the excitation energy of the 3⁺ state in the resulting hypernucleus ¹⁹_ΛF, but the mechanism for the excitation energy reduction is different from that in the lighter system ⁷_ΛLi, where the core-deuteron spin-orbit interaction plays a crucial role.

## Method

The authors performed three-body model calculations treating ¹⁸F as ¹⁶O + p + n and ¹⁹_ΛF as ¹⁷_ΛO + p + n, using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential for the core-nucleon interaction and a density-dependent contact interaction for the proton-neutron pairing. The Λ particle was assumed to occupy the 1s₁/₂ orbital, and its effect on the valence nucleons was included via a folded N-Λ potential derived from a Gaussian interaction. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a valence two-particle subspace with a cutoff energy of 10 MeV, and the pairing interaction parameters were fitted to reproduce the ground state (−9.75 MeV) and first excited state (−8.81 MeV) energies of ¹⁸F.

## Key numbers

- B(E2, 3⁺ → 1⁺) for ¹⁸F: 15.8 e²fm⁴ (calculated); measured value is 16 ± 0.6 e²fm⁴
- B(E2, 3⁺ → 1⁺) for ¹⁹_ΛF: 15.2 e²fm⁴ (sum over hypernuclear transitions)
- Ratio of B(E2) values (¹⁹_ΛF / ¹⁸F): approximately 0.96
- Excitation energy of 3⁺ state in ¹⁸F: 0.94 MeV
- Excitation energy of 3⁺⊗Λ_{s₁/₂} state in ¹⁹_ΛF: 0.66 MeV
- Core-pn rms distance for ¹⁸F ground state: 2.72 fm; for ¹⁹_ΛF ground state: 2.70 fm
- p-n rms distance for ¹⁸F ground state: 5.38 fm; for ¹⁹_ΛF ground state: 5.33 fm
- Polarization charges used: e^(p)_pol = 0.098e, e^(n)_pol = 0.40e

## Context

This work extends the study of the Λ impurity effect from p-shell hypernuclei, where the shrinkage effect is well documented in systems like ⁷_ΛLi (Tanida 2001, Hiyama 1999), to the sd-shell nucleus ¹⁸F. The authors build on the semi-microscopic cluster model of Hagino and Koike (2011) for ⁶Li and ⁷_ΛLi, which explained the relation between shrinkage and rotational spectra. The results enable future comparison with planned γ-ray spectroscopy experiments for ¹⁹_ΛF at J-PARC, and suggest that the wave function of a core nucleus can be probed through hypernuclear spectroscopy.
