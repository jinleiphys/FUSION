# Two-photon exchange: myth and history

> After recalling the arguments for possible excess of two-photon contribution over $α$-counting, model independent statements about the consequences on the observables will be given. The relevant experimental data are discussed: (polarized and unpolarized) electron and positron elastic scattering on the proton, as well as annihilation data. A reanalysis of unpolarized electron-proton elastic scattering data is presented in terms of the electric to magnetic form factor squared ratio. This observable is in principle more robust against experimental correlations and global normalizations. The present analysis shows indeed that it is a useful quantity that contains reliable and coherent information. The comparison with the ratio extracted from the measurement of the longitudinal to transverse polarization of the recoil proton in polarized electron-proton scattering shows that the results are compatible. These results bring a decisive piece of information in the controversy on the deviation of the proton form factors from the dipole dependence.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the claimed discrepancy between proton form factor ratios extracted from unpolarized (Rosenbluth) and polarized (Akhiezer-Rekalo) experiments does not require a large two-photon exchange contribution; instead, the discrepancy can be resolved by a reanalysis of the unpolarized data that accounts for an arbitrary renormalization applied to low-energy spectrometer data in the key experiment of Andivahis (1994).

## Method

The authors reanalyze unpolarized electron-proton elastic scattering data from Andivahis (1994) by fitting the reduced cross section in terms of the squared form factor ratio \(R^2 = (G_E/G_M)^2\) rather than extracting the individual form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) separately. They compare three alternative analyses: (II) using the published cross sections without the original renormalization of the low-energy spectrometer data; (III) omitting the low-energy points entirely; and (IV) deriving a \(Q^2\)-dependent normalization factor from the condition that low-energy points lie on the line fitted to high-energy points. The resulting ratios are compared to those from polarization experiments (Puckett 2011, 2017).

## Key numbers

- The original renormalization factor applied to the low-energy (LE) setting data was a constant \(C = 0.956\), fixed from the lowest \(Q^2\) points at 1.75 and 2.50 GeV\(^2\).
- The reanalysis shows the needed correction factor increases with \(Q^2\), from \(0.951144 \pm 0.0156952\) at \(Q^2 = 1.75\) GeV\(^2\) to \(0.982138 \pm 0.0142443\) at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), approaching unity at higher \(Q^2\).
- The VEPP-3, CLAS, and OLYMPUS experiments found the radiatively corrected positron-to-electron cross section ratio reaches 6-7% at \(Q^2 \le 3\) GeV\(^2\), but most of this asymmetry comes from initial/final photon emission, not two-photon exchange.
- Polarization data from the GEp collaboration extend to \(Q^2 \simeq 9\) GeV\(^2\) and show the ratio \(\mu G_E/G_M\) deviates from unity.
- The dipole-normalized cross section deviates by 20-30% at large \(Q^2\), attributed mainly to the magnetic form factor.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pacetti (2016) and Tomasi-Gustafsson (2009), who questioned the evidence for two-photon exchange in elastic electron-proton scattering. It contradicts the proposal of Guichon (2003) that two-photon exchange resolves the form factor discrepancy, noting that the assumed real and \(\epsilon\)-linear two-photon amplitude contradicts the complex, two-variable nature of such contributions. The paper enables a reinterpretation of existing Rosenbluth data without invoking large two-photon effects, but notes that new measurements at higher \(Q^2\) (beyond 3 GeV\(^2\)) are needed to settle the question definitively.
