# Radii of proton emitters

> Nuclear radius is a fundamental structural observable that informs many properties of atomic nuclei and nuclear matter. Experimental studies of radii in drip line nuclei are in the forefront of research with radioactive ion beams. Of particular interest are charge radii of proton-unbound nuclei that will soon be approached in laser spectroscopy. In this Letter, using the complex-energy approach and direct time propagation, we investigate the radius of the proton resonance whose size is ill defined in the standard stationary quantum-mechanical description. An early-time plateau is identified during which the radius of the Gamow resonance coincides with the real-energy radius accessible experimentally. We demonstrate a nonmonotonic dependence of the complex radius on decay energy and a local increase of the charge radius across the threshold (a halolike enhancement).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a well-defined complex root-mean-square (rms) radius can be assigned to proton-unbound resonant states using complex-energy methods, and that this complex radius coincides with the experimentally accessible real-energy radius during an early-time plateau in the decay process, provided the measurement is performed on timescales much shorter than the half-life.

## Method

The authors study single-proton emitters using a core-plus-valence-proton model with a Woods-Saxon potential including spin-orbit, Coulomb, and centrifugal terms. Resonant (Gamow) states are solved in the Berggren basis with outgoing boundary conditions, and the complex rms radius is evaluated using exterior complex scaling to regularize the divergent integral. A complementary time-dependent approach solves the Schrodinger equation for an initially localized wave packet prepared via the two-potential approach, tracking the evolution of the standard rms radius during the decay.

## Key numbers

- For the d5/2 resonant state in 15F at Qp = 2.06 MeV, the complex scaling uses R0 = 20 fm and rotation angle theta = pi/4, with numerical uncertainty below 1 percent.
- The Berggren-HO difference in the real part of the complex radius becomes appreciable above the proton threshold, starting from Qp approximately -1 MeV, indicating a halolike enhancement.
- For the d5/2 state in 15F at Qp = 0.69 MeV, the outer turning point r0 lies between 6 and 23 fm.
- The early-time plateau persists for t less than approximately 10^{-3} T1/2, where the standard rms radius remains constant and coincides with the complex rms radius.
- 105Sb has T1/2 = 1.12 s and 147Tm has T1/2 = 0.58 s, with lifetimes exceeding the nuclear Hamiltonian timescale (approximately 10^{-22} s) by over 20 orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the complex-energy framework previously applied to halo nuclei by Myo (2010), Papadimitriou (2011), Kruppa (2014), and others, now specifically to proton emitters near the drip line. It addresses the theoretical challenge raised by upcoming laser spectroscopy measurements of charge radii in proton-unbound nuclei planned by Cheal (2022) and Lynch (2024). The results enable interpretation of future charge-radius measurements in long-lived proton emitters such as 105Sb and 147Tm, and establish a practical connection between the complex-radius formalism and experimentally accessible observables.
