# Induced Pseudoscalar Coupling Constant

> The recent TRIUMF experiment for $μ^- p \to n ν_μ γ$ gave a surprising result that the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant $g_P$ was larger than the value obtained from $μ^- p \to n ν_μ$ experiment as much as 44 %. Reexamining contribution of the axial vector current, we found an additional term to the matrix element of Beder and Fearing which was used to extract the $g_P$ value from the measured photon energy spectrum. This additional term plays a key role to restore the reliability of $g_P (- 0.88 m_μ^2) = 6.77 g_A (0)$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a previously missing "seagull" term in the axial current matrix element used to analyze the TRIUMF radiative muon capture (RMC) experiment resolves the 44% discrepancy between the extracted induced pseudoscalar coupling constant \(g_P\) and the value predicted by PCAC and confirmed by ordinary muon capture (OMC). When this term is included, the PCAC value \(g_P(-0.88 m_\mu^2) = 6.77 g_A(0)\) becomes consistent with the measured RMC photon energy spectrum.

## Method

The authors derived the axial current in the presence of an electromagnetic field using a linear sigma-model Lagrangian with chiral symmetry, applying a standard gauge transformation and the extended Gell-Mann-Levy equations to obtain the PCAC relation. From this derivation, they identified an additional term in the axial current matrix element (the fourth term in their equation 22) that was absent in the earlier Beder and Fearing (1980, 1987) formulation. They then incorporated this \(\Delta M_e\) term into the relativistic amplitude for radiative muon capture on a proton, decomposed the statistical spin mixture into hyperfine states of the \(\mu p\) atomic system, and calculated the photon energy spectrum for comparison with the TRIUMF data.

## Key numbers

- The PCAC prediction for the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant is \(g_P(-0.88 m_\mu^2) = 6.77 g_A(0)\).
- The TRIUMF RMC experiment extracted \({\hat g}_P \equiv g_P(-0.88 m_\mu^2)/g_A(0) = 9.8 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.3\), which is 44% larger than the PCAC value.
- The number of RMC photons observed for photon energy \(k \ge 60\) MeV was \(279 \pm 26\); the solid curve (without \(\Delta M_e\) term, \({\hat g}_P = 9.8\)) gave 299 photons, while the authors' result including the \(\Delta M_e\) term (with \({\hat g}_P = 6.77\)) gave 273 photons.
- The \(\pi NN\) form factor with \(\Lambda^2 = m_\rho^2 + m_\pi^2\) contributes only \(4-5\%\) because the process occurs at low momentum transfer \(q^2 = -0.88 m_\mu^2\).

## Context

This work addresses the puzzle created by the TRIUMF RMC experiment (Jonkmans et al., 1996), which extracted a \(g_P\) value 44% larger than the PCAC prediction confirmed by ordinary muon capture (Bardin et al., 1981; Gorringe et al., 1994). The authors extend the earlier theoretical framework of Beder and Fearing (1980, 1987) by identifying a missing seagull term in the axial current, analogous to terms in pion photoproduction (Berends et al., 1967). The result restores consistency between RMC and OMC determinations of \(g_P\) and suggests that chiral perturbation theory calculations of RMC (Meissner et al., 1997; Ando and Min, 1997) should be reexamined to include this term.
