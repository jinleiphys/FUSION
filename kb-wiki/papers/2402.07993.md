# Neutrinoless double beta decay rates in the presence of light sterile neutrinos

> We investigate neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0νββ$) in minimal extensions of the Standard Model of particle physics where gauge-singlet right-handed neutrinos give rise to Dirac and Majorana neutrino mass terms. We argue that the standard treatment of these scenarios, based on mass-dependent nuclear matrix elements, is missing important contributions to the $0νββ$ amplitude. First, new effects arise from the exchange of neutrinos with very small (ultrasoft) momenta, for which we compute the associated nuclear matrix elements for the decays of ${}^{76}$Ge and ${}^{136}$Xe. These contributions can dominate the $0νββ$ rate in cases with light sterile neutrinos. The ultrasoft terms are also relevant in the more standard scenario of just three light Majorana neutrinos where they lead to a $10\%$ reduction of the total $0νββ$ amplitude. Secondly, we highlight the importance of short-range terms associated with medium-heavy sterile neutrinos and provide explicit formulae that can be used in phenomenological analyses. As examples we discuss impact of these new effects in several explicit scenarios, including a realistic $3+2$ model with two right-handed gauge-singlet neutrinos.

## Key claim

The standard treatment of neutrinoless double-beta decay in models with sterile neutrinos, which uses mass-dependent nuclear matrix elements based on a modified neutrino propagator, misses important contributions from ultrasoft neutrino exchange and short-range terms; these new effects can dominate the decay rate for light sterile neutrinos and also cause a 10% reduction of the total amplitude in the standard three-light-neutrino scenario.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory (EFT) techniques to dissect the neutrino-momentum integral in the 0νββ amplitude into hard, soft, potential, and ultrasoft momentum regions, then matched quark-level interactions onto chiral EFT to compute hadronic and nuclear matrix elements. They performed nuclear shell model calculations of the required nuclear matrix elements for the decays of ⁷⁶Ge and ¹³⁶Xe, incorporating the ultrasoft contributions for the first time, and provided explicit formulae for short-range terms associated with medium-heavy sterile neutrinos.

## Key numbers

* For ¹³⁶Xe, the fit to shell-model results gives ⟨p²⟩ ≈ (175 MeV)² for the effective momentum scale in the standard interpolation formula.
* For ⁷⁶Ge, the fit to shell-model results gives ⟨p²⟩ ≈ (160.5 MeV)² for the effective momentum scale.
* The ultrasoft terms lead to a 10% reduction of the total 0νββ amplitude in the standard scenario of three light Majorana neutrinos.
* The phase-space factor G₀₁ used is 1.5 × 10⁻¹⁴ yr⁻¹ for ¹³⁶Xe and 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹ for ⁷⁶Ge, taken from Kotila (2012) and Horoi (2017).
* The axial-vector coupling constant used is g_A = 1.2754 ± 0.0013, from Workman (2022).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of sterile neutrino effects on 0νββ rates by Blennow (2010), Mitra (2011), Faessler (2014), Barea (2015), Giunti (2015), and Asaka (2005-2016), which only considered a subset of leading contributions by replacing the massless neutrino propagator with a massive one. The new EFT treatment reveals that hard-neutrino exchange contributions (previously identified by Cirigliano (2018, 2019) for the standard mechanism) and ultrasoft contributions become important for sterile neutrinos, and the authors provide the first shell-model calculations of ultrasoft nuclear matrix elements for ⁷⁶Ge and ¹³⁶Xe. This enables more complete phenomenological analyses of 0νββ in minimal extensions of the Standard Model, including realistic 3+2 models with two right-handed gauge-singlet neutrinos.
