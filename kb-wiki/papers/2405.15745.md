# Magnetic effects in the Hadron Resonance Gas

> We discuss the modeling of the hadronic phase of QCD at finite magnetic field in the framework of hadron resonance gas (HRG). We focus on the statistical description of particle yields that include contribution from resonance decays. We demonstrate that the swift increase in the number of protons with magnetic field predicted in the HRG is due to the ill-defined description of higher-spin states. We discuss fluctuations of conserved charges and show that at present the qualitative comparison of the model predictions with the Lattice QCD data should be treated with care. We also discuss the principle of detailed balance which allows to study the magnetic field dependence of neutral resonances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the rapid increase in proton yields with magnetic field predicted by the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model is an artifact of treating higher-spin resonances as structureless point particles, and that qualitative comparisons of HRG predictions with Lattice QCD data on fluctuations of conserved charges should be treated with care at present.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase of QCD using the HRG framework, where hadrons and their resonances are treated as non-interacting particles with Landau quantization in a constant external magnetic field. They compute particle densities, yields after resonance decays, and second-order fluctuations of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) as functions of magnetic field strength at fixed temperatures. They also implement a detailed balance condition for neutral resonances by assigning effective chemical potentials derived from their charged decay products.

## Key numbers

- At T = 0.155 GeV, proton yields increase substantially with magnetic field due to decaying Delta resonances, while pi+ and K+ yields change only a few percent at eB less than or equal to 0.2 GeV^2
- The HRG model systematically underestimates Lattice QCD data on net-baryon number susceptibility for eB less than or equal to 0.16 GeV^2 at both T = 0.145 and 0.155 GeV
- At eB = 0.3 GeV^2, the calculated susceptibility drastically overshoots Lattice QCD data, with baryons of spin greater than or equal to 3/2 contributing 95% of the result
- The contribution of baryons with spin greater than or equal to 3/2 ranges from 75% at vanishing eB to 95% at eB = 0.3 GeV^2
- Under detailed balance assumptions, a 30% increase in the proton-to-pion ratio occurs at eB = 0.13 GeV^2 (approximately 6.6 m_pi^2) and eB = 0.20 GeV^2 (approximately 10.1 m_pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the HRG model with magnetic field effects originally developed by Endrodi (2013) and applied to QCD equation of state by Bali (2014) and fluctuations by Bhattacharyya (2015) and Ding (2023). The authors identify that the problematic behavior of higher-spin states in magnetic fields, previously noted by Endrodi (2013), leads to unreliable predictions for particle yields and fluctuations. The paper enables future work on S-matrix formulations of statistical mechanics extended to finite magnetic fields, and highlights the need for non-perturbative approaches to describe bound states in external magnetic fields.
