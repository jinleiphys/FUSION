# Effects of tensor forces in nuclear spin-orbit splittings from ab initio calculations

> A systematic and specific pattern due to the effects of the tensor forces is found in the evolution of spin-orbit splittings in neutron drops. This result is obtained from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. It forms an important guide for future microscopic derivations of relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear energy density functionals.

## Key claim

A systematic pattern in the evolution of spin-orbit splittings in neutron drops, decreasing as the next \(j = l + 1/2\) orbit is filled and increasing again as the \(j = l - 1/2\) orbit is filled, is found to be a signature of tensor forces. This pattern is obtained from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn A, but the abstract does not note that the pattern is not reproduced by phenomenological relativistic mean-field (RMF) density functionals without tensor terms, and that it can be matched by the RHF functional PKO1 only after artificially scaling the pion coupling strength by a factor \(\lambda = 1.3\).

## Method

The authors used relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with the bare one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn A to study neutron drops confined in an external harmonic oscillator potential with \(\hbar\omega = 10\) MeV. The Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved in the isospin channel \(T_z = 1\) only, and the self-consistent single-particle potential was obtained from the \(G\)-matrix in the no-sea approximation. Neutron drops with even numbers from \(N = 4\) to \(50\) were computed, and the spin-orbit splittings for the \(1p\), \(1d\), \(1f\), and \(2p\) orbits were extracted and compared with results from phenomenological RMF functionals (NL3, PK1, DD-ME2, PKDD, PC-PK1) and the RHF functional PKO1 with varied pion coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- Total energy per neutron drop, scaled as \(E / (\hbar\omega N^{4/3})\), saturates for \(N \geq 20\); for a non-interacting system the Thomas-Fermi value is approximately \(1.082 \, \hbar\omega N^{4/3}\).
- Radii of neutron drops from RBHF with Bonn A are smaller than those from JISP16 and from AV8' + UIX, except near \(N = 18\) where radii become close to chiral 2N + 3N results.
- The \(1d\) spin-orbit splitting decreases from \(N = 20\) to \(N = 28\) as the \(1f_{7/2}\) orbit is filled, then increases above \(N = 28\) as the \(2p_{1/2}\) and \(1f_{5/2}\) orbits are filled.
- The pattern of spin-orbit splitting evolution from RBHF with Bonn A is reproduced by the RHF functional PKO1 only when the pion coupling strength is multiplied by a factor \(\lambda = 1.3\).

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor-force effects on spin-orbit splittings from the proton-neutron case in nuclei (Otsuka et al., 2005) to the neutron-neutron case in neutron drops, using ab initio RBHF theory rather than phenomenological density functionals. It contradicts the common practice in RMF functionals (e.g., NL3, PK1, DD-ME2, PKDD, PC-PK1) that neglect tensor forces, showing that those functionals fail to reproduce the observed pattern. The results provide meta-data for future microscopic derivations of relativistic and nonrelativistic nuclear energy density functionals, enabling a clean adjustment of tensor-force parameters without the ambiguity of beyond-mean-field effects such as particle-vibrational coupling.
