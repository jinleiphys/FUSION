# Thermalization of Bottomonium in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the approach to equilibrium of bottomonium in the quark-gluon plasma within the open quantum system framework. We perform large-scale simulations of the long-time behavior in three dimensions using the quantum trajectory method to observe the emergence of steady states and determine the timescale of thermalization in position-, angular-momentum-, and color-space. We find that the thermalization timescale increases with decreasing temperature and decreasing coupling to the medium, which is given by transport coefficients of the medium. Additionally, we observe that the steady states exhibit small corrections to the Gibbs state due to medium interactions and show that these corrections diminish for weaker medium coupling and higher temperature. At a temperature of $450\,$MeV, quarkonium relaxes to a state that is approximately thermal, with the most significant correction being a smaller overlap of the $1S$ state with respect to the Gibbs state. We compare these findings with the master equation obtained at leading order in the expansion of the binding energy over the temperature, which we find to have a trivial steady state.

# Thermalization of Bottomonium in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that bottomonium in a quark-gluon plasma relaxes to a unique steady state that is approximately thermal, but with small corrections to the Gibbs state that diminish for weaker medium coupling and higher temperature; at 450 MeV, the most significant correction is a smaller overlap of the 1S state compared to the Gibbs prediction, and the thermalization timescale increases with decreasing temperature and decreasing coupling to the medium.

## Method

The authors perform large-scale three-dimensional simulations of the pNRQCD Lindblad equation at next-to-leading order in E/(πT) using the quantum trajectory method (QTraj code) with a box size of 20 GeV⁻¹, 1024 lattice points (dr = 0.004 fm), and transport coefficients κ = 4 T³ and γ = 0. They impose angular momentum cutoffs l_max = 3, 6, 9 with time steps δt = 0.00025 fm (l_max = 3) and δt = 0.0001 fm (l_max = 6, 9), sampling approximately 3 × 10⁵ trajectories per simulation to observe steady-state emergence.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: T = 450 MeV (constant temperature simulation)
- Box size: L = 20 GeV⁻¹; lattice spacing: dr = 0.004 fm
- Transport coefficient: κ = 4 T³; strong coupling: α_s = 0.468
- Steady state emerges around 20 fm for all angular momentum cutoffs
- Equilibration in color space takes approximately 10 fm
- Time step convergence demonstrated: δt = 0.00025 fm for l_max = 3; δt = 0.0001 fm for l_max = 6 and l_max = 9
- For phenomenological Bjorken evolution from 425 MeV to 190 MeV, δt = 0.001 fm is sufficient (results agree with δt = 0.0001 fm within statistical uncertainties)

## Context

This work extends previous one-dimensional studies of quarkonium thermalization, such as Miura (2022) who observed equilibration to Boltzmann distribution within 150 fm, and Delorme (2024) who found an offset from Boltzmann for charmonium. It contrasts with Angelides (2025) who showed stronger dissipation slows equilibration in the open Schwinger model, indicating qualitative differences between models. The results enable future studies of Wigner distributions and entanglement in quarkonium evolution, and clarify the need for genuine quantum simulations of quarkonium suppression.
