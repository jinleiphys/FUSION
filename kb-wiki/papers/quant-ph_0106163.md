# On the exact solutions of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model

> We present the many-particle Hamiltonian model of Lipkin, Meshkov and Glick in the context of deformed polynomial algebras and show that its exact solutions can be easily and naturally obtained within this formalism. The Hamiltonian matrix of each $j$ multiplet can be split into two submatrices associated to two distinct irreps of the deformed algebra. Their invariant subspaces correspond to even and odd numbers of particle-hole excitations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model's Hamiltonian matrix for each angular momentum multiplet can be split into two submatrices corresponding to distinct irreducible representations of a deformed polynomial algebra, with the splitting naturally distinguishing between even and odd numbers of particle-hole excitations. The authors claim this provides a "new symmetry" not previously known, but the splitting is entirely consistent with the existing property that the interaction only excites or de-excites two particle-hole pairs.

## Method

The authors reformulate the LMG Hamiltonian in terms of deformed polynomial algebra generators, showing that the standard su(2) generators can be rescaled to satisfy a polynomial deformation of sl(2,R). They then construct two types of representations (q=1 and q=2) of this deformed algebra, and demonstrate that the q=2 representation decomposes into a direct sum of two q=1 representations, which correspond to invariant subspaces with even and odd numbers of particle-hole excitations. The secular equation for each subspace is solved analytically for N=7 and N=8 particles, and the eigenvalues are obtained as functions of the interaction strength parameter delta.

## Key numbers

* For N=7 particles, the largest Hamiltonian matrix corresponds to j=7/2 and splits into two equal matrices both with J=(N-1)/4 = 3/2
* For N=7, the secular equation for j=5/2 is a cubic: E^3 - 6c E^2 - (13/4 + 4/7 delta^2) E + 15/2 c + (120/49) c delta^2 = 0, with c = +/- 1/4
* For N=7, the secular equation for j=7/2 is a quartic: E^4 - 8c E^3 - (17/2 + 18/7 delta^2) E^2 + (38 + 888/49 delta^2) c E + 105/16 + 75/14 delta^2 + 135/343 delta^4 = 0, with c = +/- 1/4
* For N=8 particles, the largest Hamiltonian matrix corresponds to j=4 and splits into two submatrices with J=2 and J=3/2
* For N=8, the eigenvalue for j=1, J=1/2 is +/- sqrt(1 + 1/64 delta^2)
* For N=8, the eigenvalue for j=2, J=1/2 is +/- sqrt(1 + 9/64 delta^2)
* For N=8, the eigenvalue for j=4, J=3/2 is +/- sqrt(5 + 113/64 delta^2 +/- sqrt(16 + 19/2 delta^2 + 275/128 delta^4))

## Context

This work extends the original LMG model solutions from Lipkin, Meshkov and Glick (1965), who provided analytic solutions for up to 8 particles and numerical solutions for larger N only for the maximum angular momentum j=N/2. The deformed algebra approach, following the polynomial deformation method of Debergh (2000), allows treatment of the entire spectrum including all j multiplets, not just the largest one. The method enables systematic reduction of the diagonalization problem: finding eigenstates for N particles reduces to diagonalizing the largest matrix once the states for N-2 particles are known, and the splitting into two submatrices reduces the largest matrix dimension from N+1 to either N/2+1 and N/2 (for N even) or two matrices of (N+1)/2 (for N odd).
