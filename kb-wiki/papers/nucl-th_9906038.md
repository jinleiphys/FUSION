# Describing a1 and b1 decays

> Two-body pion-radiating and weak decays of light axial-vector mesons and the rho are studied as a phenomenological application of the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations. Models based on the rainbow-ladder truncation are capable of providing a good description and, in particular, yield the correct sign and magnitude of the a1 -> rho pi and b1 -> omega pi D/S ratios, with no additional mechanism necessary.

## Key claim

Rainbow-ladder truncation of the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations, implemented via a rank-2 separable Ansatz for the gluon interaction, can reproduce the correct sign and magnitude of the $a_1 \to \rho\pi$ and $b_1 \to \omega\pi$ D/S ratios without invoking any additional mechanisms, but only after a least-squares fit that relaxes the constraints of the separable Ansatz by allowing certain parameters to vary.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in rainbow-ladder truncation using a confining, rank-2 separable Ansatz for the dressed-gluon propagator, which yields the masses and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $a_1$, $b_1$, $h_1$, and $f_1$ mesons. They then compute the two-body strong decay widths and D/S ratios for $a_1 \to \rho\pi$, $b_1 \to \omega\pi$, and $h_1 \to \rho\pi$ in impulse approximation, together with the $\rho \to \pi\pi$ width and the leptonic decay constants of the vector and axial-vector mesons. To improve agreement with experiment, they perform a least-squares fit allowing five parameters (including $b_2^{a_1}=b_2^{b_1}$, $b_2^\pi$, $b_0^\rho$, $b_1^\rho$, and $e_2^{b_1}$) to vary.

## Key numbers

* $a_1$ mass: calculated 1.34 GeV (experimental 1.23 $\pm$ 0.040 GeV); $b_1$ mass: calculated 1.24 GeV (experimental 1.23 $\pm$ 0.003 GeV); $\rho$ mass: calculated 0.736 GeV (experimental 0.77 GeV); pion mass: calculated 0.139 GeV (experimental 0.139 GeV).
* $a_1 \to \rho\pi$ D/S ratio: column I (separable Ansatz) value not given explicitly; column II (fitted) value not given explicitly, but the fit yields a root-mean-square relative error of 17% over the fitted observables.
* $b_1 \to \omega\pi$ D/S ratio: column I and II values not given explicitly; the fit changes the sign and relative magnitude of the $b_1$ eigenvector component $e_2^{b_1}$ from its initial value to $-0.673$.
* $\rho \to \pi\pi$ coupling $g_{\rho\pi\pi}$: overestimated by a factor of $\sim 1.5$--$3.0$ in the separable Ansatz (column I); improved in the fitted model (column II).
* $a_1$ decay constant $f_{a_1}$: column I value 0.154 GeV when forcing $e_1^{a_1}=0$; fitted model yields improved values.
* $b_1$ and $h_1$ decay constants: identically zero as a model-independent consequence of $J^{PC}=1^{+-}$ charge-parity.

## Context

This work extends earlier Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of the light meson spectrum (Pankaj, 1998) that neglected axial-vector mesons, and builds on improved treatments of pseudoscalar (Maris and Roberts, 1997) and vector (Maris and Tandy, 1999) meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. It contrasts with constituent-quark-model approaches (Isgur, 1990) that yield the wrong sign for the $a_1 \to \rho\pi$ D/S ratio when using one-gluon-exchange models. The results enable future definitive studies combining the methods of Maris and Roberts (1997) and Maris and Tandy (1999) to conclusively test whether rainbow-ladder truncation can describe axial-vector decays without additional mechanisms, and provide a tool for interpreting experimental searches for exotic $J^{PC}=1^{-+}$ states in the light meson sector.
