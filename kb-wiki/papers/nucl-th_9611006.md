# Modification of The Transition Rate in The Hydrogen Atom placed in Finite Space

> When a hydrogen atom trapped in finite space formed by two parallel perfectly conducting plates, the transition rates can be modified. Life-time of the hydrogen $2P_{1/2}$-state is estimated to be shorter as much as $56.3ps $ with a separation distance of $b = 1.2 μm$ between two plates. Although the modification is dependent on position of the atom, the life-time can be shorten or lengthen by adjusting the separation distance even for the case that the atom is placed at the center between two plates.

# Modification of The Transition Rate in The Hydrogen Atom placed in Finite Space

## Key claim

When a hydrogen atom is placed between two parallel perfectly conducting plates, the transition rate of the 2P1/2 state is modified by an amount that depends on both the plate separation distance and the atom's position between the plates, with the modification showing oscillatory behavior as a function of separation distance. The abstract's claim of a 56.3 ps lifetime shortening applies specifically to the case where the atom is at the center (Z=0) with a plate separation of b = 1.2 μm, but the sign and magnitude of the shift can change dramatically with both parameters.

## Method

The author calculated the spontaneous transition rate for the hydrogen 2P1/2 → 1S1/2 transition by quantizing the electromagnetic field in the finite space between two parallel perfectly conducting plates, imposing boundary conditions that discretize the photon momentum component perpendicular to the plates. Starting from the standard time-dependent perturbation theory expression for the transition rate in free space, the author replaced the continuous integral over kz with a discrete summation over modes kz = πn/b and used Poisson's summation formula to evaluate the resulting series. The calculation explicitly separated the transition rate into a position-dependent but plate-separation-independent term W1 and a separation-dependent term W2(b), with the final expression given in terms of spherical Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- Free-space lifetime of the hydrogen 2P1/2 state: τ0 = 1.597 × 10⁻⁹ s (1.597 ns)
- Free-space transition rate for 2P1/2 → 1S1/2: W0 = 0.626288 × 10⁹ Hz
- Transition frequency for 2P1/2 → 1S1/2: ν = 2466060337.7 MHz
- For b = 1.2 μm and atom at center (Z=0): lifetime shift Δτ = -56.33 ps (lifetime shortened), transition rate shift ΔW = +22.90 MHz
- For b = 0.7 μm and Z=0: ΔW = +38.15 MHz, Δτ = -91.69 ps
- For b = 0.8 μm and Z=0: ΔW = -25.93 MHz (rate decreased), Δτ = +68.99 ps (lifetime lengthened)
- For b = 1.2 μm and Z = b/10: ΔW = +22.80 MHz, Δτ = -56.10 ps
- For b = 1.2 μm and Z = b/14: ΔW = -0.532 MHz, Δτ = +1.36 ps (near-zero shift)
- For a lower frequency ω0 = 1.2 × 10¹¹ Hz, the b-dependent term Δ(b) reaches 33% for b = 1 cm

## Context

This work extends the Casimir effect framework (Casimir, 1948) to atomic transition rates, building on earlier calculations of atomic energy level shifts between parallel plates by Cheon (1988), Barton (1987), and Plunien, Muller and Greiner (1986). The author explicitly derives the b-dependent term Δ(b) that was not evaluated in previous calculations by Kreuzer and Svozil (1986) and Barton (1988). The predicted shifts of 22.9 MHz for b = 1.2 μm are claimed to be measurable given the experimental precision of 4.8 MHz reported by Hildum et al. (1986). The strong sensitivity to atomic position identified in the calculation suggests that precise positioning of the atom is critical for experimental verification, but the author argues that observation of the shifts is definitely possible.
