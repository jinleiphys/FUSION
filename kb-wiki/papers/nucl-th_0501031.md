# Dilepton and vector meson production in elementary and in heavy ion reactions

> We present a unified description of the vector meson and dilepton production in elementary and in heavy ion reactions. The production of vector mesons is described via the excitation of nucleon resonances. Medium effects in heavy ion reactions are discussed.

# Dilepton and vector meson production in elementary and in heavy ion reactions

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified description of vector meson and dilepton production in both elementary and heavy ion reactions, but the model's success in reproducing elementary reaction data does not automatically resolve the DLS "puzzle" in heavy ion collisions without introducing additional in-medium effects such as collisional broadening and decoherence.

## Method

The authors develop an extended vector meson dominance (eVMD) model that describes vector meson production via excitation of nucleon resonances, with fully relativistic and kinematically complete treatment of resonance decays to nucleon plus vector meson or dilepton pairs. The resonance model is applied within a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport framework to simulate heavy ion collisions, where medium effects such as collisional broadening of vector meson widths and decoherence of quantum interference are incorporated.

## Key numbers

- Collisional width of the ρ meson in medium: Γ_ρ^coll = 150 MeV, extracted from DLS data at average nuclear density of about 1.5 ρ₀
- Collisional width of the ω meson in medium: Γ_ω^coll = 100–300 MeV, extracted from DLS data at average nuclear density of about 1.5 ρ₀
- ω-meson angular distribution data from COSY-TOF taken at √s = 2.83 GeV, with excess energy ε = 173 MeV
- Dominant resonances for ω production at this energy: N*(1720) 3/2⁺ and N*(1900) 3/2⁺

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic models based on dispersion analyses of forward scattering amplitudes (Klingl, Kaiser, Weise; Rapp and collaborators) and addresses the DLS "puzzle" observed at BEVALAC energies around 1 A.GeV, where transport calculations by Ernst and collaborators, and by Bratkovskaya, Cassing, et al. (1998) underestimate dilepton yields. The model enables future comparisons with higher-precision data from the HADES experiment, which should constrain the in-medium widths more accurately.
