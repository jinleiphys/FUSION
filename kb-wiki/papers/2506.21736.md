# Four types of phase transitions in interacting boson (meson) matter at high temperatures

> The thermodynamics of the interacting system of relativistic particles and antiparticles in the presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate was investigated within the framework of the mean-field model. It is assumed that the total isospin (charge) density is conserved. We show that, depending on the strength of the interaction, a bosonic particle-antiparticle system exhibits four types of phase transitions to the condensate phase. Three types correspond to the second-order phase transition, while one is a first-order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an interacting bosonic particle-antiparticle system with conserved isospin density exhibits four distinct types of phase transitions to the condensate phase, depending on the strength of the attractive interaction parameter \(\kappa\). The abstract glosses over the condition that the classification depends on the ratio of attractive to repulsive mean-field parameters, with the fourth type occurring only for supercritical attraction (\(\kappa > 1\)).

## Method

The authors use a thermodynamically consistent mean-field model with both attractive and repulsive terms to study a relativistic pion-antipion gas at high temperatures, with the total isospin (charge) density held constant. They solve self-consistent gap equations for the particle and antiparticle number densities as functions of temperature, varying the dimensionless attraction parameter \(\kappa\) (the ratio of attraction to repulsion). The phase transitions are identified by analyzing the temperature dependence of the number densities, energy density, heat capacity, and the order parameter (condensate density).

## Key numbers

*   For an ideal single-component bosonic gas at constant particle density \(n = 0.1 \,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the critical temperature is \(T_{\rm c} = 123\) MeV.
*   For the interacting pion gas with isospin density \(n_I = 0.1 \,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and attraction parameter \(\kappa = 0.5\), the system exhibits a second-order phase transition of the first type.
*   For \(\kappa = 0.96\) and \(n_I = 0.1 \,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), multiple second-order phase transitions (second type) occur, with temperatures \(T_{\rm c}^{(-)}\), \(T_{\rm c1}^{(-)}\), and \(T_{\rm c2}^{(-)}\).
*   For \(\kappa = 1\) and \(n_I = 0\), a meta phase transition of the second order occurs at temperature \(T_{*}\), with no condensate formation.
*   For \(\kappa = 1.1\) and \(n_I = 0\), a first-order phase transition occurs at \(T = T_{\rm cd}\), followed by a second-order phase transition at \(T = T_{\rm c}\) (fourth type).
*   For \(\kappa = 1.1\) and \(n_I = 0.1 \,\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the critical temperature splits into two: \(T_{\rm c}^{(-)}\) and \(T_{\rm c}^{(+)}\), corresponding to separate exit from the condensate phase for \(\pi^-\) and \(\pi^+\) mesons.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Bose-Einstein condensation in relativistic bosonic systems, which were first discussed by Haber and Weldon (1981) and Kapusta (1981). It generalizes the thermodynamic mean-field model developed by Anchishkin and Vovchenko, which was previously applied to systems with zero isospin density (Anchishkin, 2019) and finite isospin density (Anchishkin, 2022; Universe, 2023). The paper enables a classification of phase transitions in hot, dense meson matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and high-energy proton collisions, and it shows that simultaneous condensation of particles and antiparticles is only possible for supercritical attraction (\(\kappa > 1\)).
