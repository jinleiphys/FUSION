# Transverse force distributions in the proton from lattice QCD

> Single-spin asymmetries observed in polarised deep-inelastic scattering are important probes of hadron structure. The Sivers asymmetry has been the focus of much attention in QCD phenomenology and is yet to be understood at the quark level. In this Letter, we present a lattice QCD calculation of the spatial distribution of a colour-Lorentz force acting on the struck quark in a proton. We determine a spin-independent confining force, as well as spin-dependent force distributions with local forces on the order of 3 GeV/fm. These distributions offer a complementary picture of the Sivers asymmetry in transversely polarised deep-inelastic scattering.

# Transverse force distributions in the proton from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the spatial distribution of the colour-Lorentz force acting on a struck quark in a proton, claiming that these distributions offer a complementary picture of the Sivers asymmetry observed in transversely polarised deep-inelastic scattering. The hedge is that the results are obtained at an unphysical pion mass of approximately 450 MeV and require further extrapolation to physical quark masses for direct comparison with experiment.

## Method
The authors compute off-forward matrix elements of twist-three operators on three lattice ensembles with different lattice spacings at the SU(3)-flavour symmetric point, using a stout-smeared non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson action and tree-level Symanzik improved gluon action. They extract three new form factors (Φ₁, Φ₂, Φ₃) from ratios of three-point and two-point correlation functions, apply momentum-subtraction renormalisation in the RI′-MOM scheme at a scale of 2 GeV, and perform two-dimensional Fourier transforms to obtain force distributions in transverse impact-parameter space.

## Key numbers
- Local colour-Lorentz forces on the order of 3 GeV/fm are estimated close to the origin, which is 3 times larger than the average force scale in QCD (the static quark potential predicts approximately 1 GeV/fm at large separations).
- The proton d₂ value is determined as d₂⁽ᵖ⁾(mπ ≈ 450 MeV, a = 0) = 0.046(7)ₛₜₐₜ(16)ₛᵧₛ and the neutron d₂ value as d₂⁽ⁿ⁾(mπ ≈ 450 MeV, a = 0) = 0.023(5)ₛₜₐₜ(8)ₛᵧₛ.
- The Φ₁ form factor dipole fit yields χ²/d.o.f of 1.31 for up quarks and 0.40 for down quarks; the Φ₂ dipole fit for up quarks gives χ²/d.o.f of 0.81; the Φ₃ dipole fit for up quarks gives χ²/d.o.f of 1.32.
- The magnitude of the up quark Φ₁ form factor is a little more than double that of the down quark, consistent with quark counting expectations.

## Context
This work extends the chromodynamic lensing framework of Burkardt (2013, 2019) by computing the off-forward twist-three matrix elements that encode colour-Lorentz force distributions directly from lattice QCD, rather than relying on phenomenological models. The results provide a complementary view to the Sivers function (Sivers, 1991) in transverse-momentum space, as impact-parameter space is not the Fourier conjugate of quark transverse momentum (Diehl, 2015). The force distributions are consistent with experimental single-spin asymmetry observations from HERMES (2007, 2008) and COMPASS (2006), and the work enables future studies connecting these lattice calculations to phenomenology at physical pion masses and with extended momentum ranges.
