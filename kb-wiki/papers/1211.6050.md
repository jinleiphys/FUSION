# Reanalysis of nuclear spin matrix elements for dark matter spin-dependent scattering

> We show how to include in the existing calculations for nuclei other than $^{129}$Xe, $^{131}$Xe, the corrections to the isovector coupling arising in chiral effective field theory recently found in Ref. \cite{Menendez1}. The dominant, momentum independent, 2-body currents effect can be taken into account by formally redefining the static spin matrix elements $< \mathbf{S}_{p,n} >$. By further using the normalized form factor at $q\neq 0$ built with the 1-body level structure functions, we show that the WIMP-nucleus cross section and the upper limits on the WIMP-nucleon cross sections coincide with the ones derived using the exact functions at the 2-body level. We explicitly show it in the case of XENON100 limits on the WIMP-neutron cross section and we recalculate the limits on the WIMP-proton spin dependent cross section set by COUPP. We also give practical formulas to obtain $< \mathbf{S}_{p,n} >$ given the structure functions in the various formalisms/notations existing in literature. We argue that the standard treatment of the spin-dependent cross section in terms of three independent isospin functions, $S_{00}(q)$, $S_{11}(q)$, $S_{01}(q)$, is redundant in the sense that the interference function $S_{01}(q)$ is the double product $|S_{01}(q)|=2\sqrt{S_{00}(q)}\sqrt{S_{11}(q)}$ even when including the new effective field theory corrections.

# Reanalysis of nuclear spin matrix elements for dark matter spin-dependent scattering

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dominant momentum-independent two-body current corrections from chiral effective field theory can be incorporated into existing spin-dependent scattering calculations for any nucleus by simply redefining the static spin matrix elements ⟨Sp,n⟩, without requiring full recalculation of the structure functions. The standard treatment using three independent isospin functions S00(q), S11(q), and S01(q) is redundant because the interference function S01(q) is always determined by the double product |S01(q)| = 2√S00(q)√S11(q), even after including the new effective field theory corrections.

## Method

The author shows that the two-body current correction δa1 from chiral effective field theory renormalizes the isovector coupling a1 at zero momentum transfer, and this effect can be absorbed into redefined spin matrix elements ⟨Sp⟩2b and ⟨Sn⟩2b using Eq. (SpSn2b). By using the normalized form factor built from the standard one-body level structure functions together with these corrected static spin values, the resulting WIMP-nucleus cross section and upper limits coincide with those derived using the exact two-body level functions. The method is validated by recalculating XENON100 limits on the WIMP-neutron cross section and COUPP limits on the WIMP-proton cross section.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory corrections to the isovector coupling first calculated by Menendez et al. (2012) for 129Xe and 131Xe, providing a general method applicable to all nuclei used in dark matter direct detection experiments. The paper contradicts the longstanding assumption that three independent isospin structure functions S00, S11, and S01 are needed, showing instead that S01 is determined by the other two. The results enable experimental collaborations such as XENON100, COUPP, SIMPLE, and PICASSO to incorporate the two-body current corrections into their spin-dependent limits without requiring new nuclear structure calculations, using the existing structure functions from shell-model calculations by Divari et al. (2000), Dimitrov et al. (1995), Ressel and Dean (1997), and Vergados et al. (2007).
