# Compact object of HESS J1731-347 and its implication on neutron star matter

> In this work, we investigate the impact of the possibility of a small, subsolar mass compact star, such as the recently reported central compact object of HESS J1731-347, on the equation of state (EOS) of neutron stars. We have used a hybrid approach to the nuclear EOS developed recently where the matter around nuclear saturation density is described by a parametric expansion in terms of nuclear empirical parameters and represented in an agnostic way at higher density using piecewise polytropes. We have incorporated the inputs provided by the latest neutron skin measurement experiments from PREX-II and CREX, simultaneous mass-radius measurements of pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425. The main results of the study show the effect of HESS J1731-347 on the nuclear parameters and neutron star observables. Our analysis yields the slope of symmetry energy $L=45.71^{+38.18}_{-22.11}$ MeV, the radius of a $1.4 M_\odot$ star, $R_{1.4}=12.18^{+0.71}_{-0.88}$ km, and the maximum mass of a static star, $M_{\rm max}= 2.14^{+0.26}_{-0.17} M_\odot$ within $90\%$ confidence interval, respectively.

## Key claim
If the central compact object of HESS J1731-347 is a neutron star with the reported mass and radius, then the slope of symmetry energy and neutron star radii shift to lower values than inferred from PREX-II alone, but the maximum mass remains largely unchanged. The authors caution that the HESS J1731-347 mass-radius estimate remains controversial due to assumptions about atmospheric composition and distance.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid equation of state (EOS) model combining a Taylor expansion around nuclear saturation density in terms of empirical nuclear parameters (incompressibility, symmetry energy, slope parameter, isovector incompressibility) with an agnostic three-segment piecewise polytrope at higher densities. They performed Bayesian inference using nested sampling, incorporating likelihoods from gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425, NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, neutron skin thickness measurements from PREX-II and CREX, and the mass-radius posterior of HESS J1731-347.

## Key numbers
- Slope of symmetry energy: L = 45.71^{+38.18}_{-22.11} MeV (90% confidence interval) after including all constraints including HESS J1731-347.
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_{1.4} = 12.18^{+0.71}_{-0.88} km (90% confidence interval) with all constraints.
- Maximum mass of a static neutron star: M_max = 2.14^{+0.26}_{-0.17} solar masses (90% confidence interval) with all constraints.
- Inferred mass of HESS J1731-347: 0.83^{+0.26}_{-0.19} solar masses (90% confidence interval).
- Inferred radius of HESS J1731-347: 11.78^{+0.81}_{-0.74} km (90% confidence interval).
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass star: approximately 447 after including HESS J1731-347.
- Central pressure of HESS J1731-347: 25.10^{+18.11}_{-9.09} MeV fm^{-3} (90% confidence interval).

## Context
This work extends the hybrid EOS framework developed by Biswas (2020) and used in previous multimessenger analyses of neutron star properties. It directly addresses the controversial mass-radius measurement of HESS J1731-347 reported by Doroshenko (2022), which has been questioned by Alford (2023) due to assumptions about carbon atmosphere and distance. The results show that if confirmed, this subsolar mass object would push nucleonic EOS models to their limits, potentially requiring exotic compositions such as strange quark matter or dark matter admixture as explored by Horvath (2023) and Sagun (2023).
