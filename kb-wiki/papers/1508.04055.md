# Superdeformed band in the $N = Z+4$ nucleus $^{40}$Ar: A projected shell model analysis

> It has been debated whether the experimentally-identified superdeformed rotational band in $^{40}$Ar [E. Ideguchi, et al., Phys. Lett. B 686 (2010) 18] has an axially or triaxially deformed shape. Projected shell model calculations with angular-momentum-projection using an axially-deformed basis are performed up to high spins. Our calculated energy levels indicate a perfect collective-rotor behavior for the superdeformed yrast band. However, detailed analysis of the wave functions reveals that the high-spin structure is dominated by mixed 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle configurations. The calculated electric quadrupole transition probabilities reproduce well the known experimental data and suggest a reduced, but still significant, collectivity in the high spin region. The deduced triaxial deformation parameters are small throughout the entire band, suggesting that triaxiality is not very important for this superdeformed band.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) calculations reproduce the experimental data for the superdeformed band in \(^{40}\)Ar and show that the band has a nearly axially-symmetric shape, with triaxial deformation parameters \(\gamma\) averaging only a few degrees, contradicting the triaxial superdeformation interpretation proposed by Taniguchi et al. (2014) but supporting the axial-deformation conclusion of Ideguchi et al. (2010).

## Method

The authors performed projected shell model (PSM) calculations using an axially-deformed Nilsson single-particle basis with a quadrupole deformation parameter \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.48\), incorporating pairing correlations via BCS and including up to 4-quasiparticle configurations (0-qp, 2-qp neutron, 2-qp proton, and 4-qp neutron-proton states). Angular momentum projection was applied to transform basis states from the intrinsic to the laboratory frame, followed by diagonalization of a quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian (including monopole and quadrupole pairing terms) in the projected space. The PSM wavefunctions were then used to compute energy levels, \(g\)-factors, electric quadrupole transition probabilities \(B(E2)\), and spectroscopic quadrupole moments \(Q(I)\), from which triaxial deformation parameters \(\gamma\) were extracted.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole deformation parameter used in the basis is \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.48\), consistent with the experimental deformation \(\beta_2 \sim 0.5\) from Ideguchi et al. (2010).
- The monopole pairing strength is taken as \(G_M = 18.65/A\) and the quadrupole pairing strength as \(G_Q = 0.2 G_M\).
- The measured transition quadrupole moment is \(Q_t = 1.4^{+0.49}_{-0.31}\) eb (Ideguchi et al. 2010), averaged over the spin interval \(I = 4-12\).
- The calculated \(B(E2)\) at \(I = 18\) is only about 2/3 of that at \(I = 6\), indicating decreasing collectivity with spin.
- The extracted triaxial deformation parameters \(\gamma\) range from 4.4 degrees at \(I=4\) to 2.4 degrees at \(I=18\), with a maximum of 5.9 degrees at \(I=8\).
- The calculated neutron pairing gap at \(I=0\) is \(\Delta_n = 0.55\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PSM study of the superdeformed band in \(^{36}\)Ar (Long et al. 2001) to the \(N = Z+4\) nucleus \(^{40}\)Ar, addressing a debate between the axial-deformation interpretation of Ideguchi et al. (2010) based on cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations and the triaxial-deformation interpretation of Taniguchi et al. (2014) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics plus generator coordinate method (AMD+GCM). The PSM results support the axial-deformation conclusion and provide detailed predictions for \(g\)-factors and spin-dependent \(B(E2)\) values that can be tested by future experiments, including potential applications to neutrino detection via the ICARUS liquid argon time projection chamber and to hypernuclear studies.
