# Comment on ``Proton Polarization Shifts in Electronic and Muonic Hydrogen''

> We demonstrate that criticisms concerning our work nucl-th/9704043, made in the recent preprint hep-ph/9903352, are unfounded. The improvements over our results claimed in hep-ph/9903352 are in fact spurious, being based mainly on misunderstandings and on dubious theoretical models.

## Key claim

The criticisms of Khriplovich and Sen'kov's earlier work (nucl-th/9704043) made in hep-ph/9903352 are unfounded, and the claimed improvements are spurious, based on misunderstandings and dubious theoretical models. The authors maintain that their logarithmic approximation (with an accuracy of about 15%) is the only theoretical approximation in their original calculation, and that the shift of the mean excitation energy from 300 MeV to 410 MeV changes the result by only 5%, which is within that uncertainty.

## Method

The authors derived the correction to the hydrogen Lamb shift from proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities by starting with the electron-proton forward scattering amplitude, using the gauge A0=0 for virtual photons. They performed the integration with logarithmic accuracy, neglecting terms of order unity compared to ln(mean excitation energy / electron mass), where the mean excitation energy is about 300 MeV and the electron mass is 0.511 MeV, making the logarithm about 6-7. The resulting effective operator in coordinate representation is V = -alpha * m_e * [5*alpha(0) - beta(0)] * ln(mean E / m_e) * delta(r), where alpha and beta are the nuclear electric and magnetic polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- The sum of proton polarizabilities from newer experimental data: alpha_p(0) + beta_p(0) = (13.69 +/- 0.14) x 10^(-4) fm^3 (from Babusci et al.)
- The sum of proton polarizabilities used in the original work: alpha_p(0) + beta_p(0) = (14.2 +/- 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- The difference of proton polarizabilities from backward Compton scattering: alpha_p(0) - beta_p(0) = (10.0 +/- 1.8) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- The final correction to the hydrogen 1S state Lamb shift: -70 Hz (updated central value) with an uncertainty of +/- 7 Hz
- The original central value was -71 Hz, well within the +/- 7 Hz uncertainty

## Context

This comment responds to a preprint (hep-ph/9903352) that criticized the authors' earlier work (nucl-th/9704043) on proton polarization shifts in electronic and muonic hydrogen. The authors defend their logarithmic approximation approach against claims that higher multipoles and transverse excitations were neglected, and argue that the simple harmonic oscillator quark model used by the critics cannot improve the 15% theoretical accuracy estimate. The work enables continued use of the logarithmic approximation for calculating Lamb shift corrections, with the main uncertainty remaining from experimental data on backward Compton scattering rather than from the sum of polarizabilities.
