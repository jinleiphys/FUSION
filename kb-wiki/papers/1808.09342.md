# Binding-energy independence of reduced spectroscopic strengths derived from (p, 2p) and (p, pn) reactions with nitrogen and oxygen isotopes

> A campaign of intermediate energy (300-450 MeV/u) proton-induced nucleon knockout measurements in inverse kinematics has been recently undertaken at the R 3 B/LAND setup at GSI. We present a systematic theoretical analysis of these data with the aim of studying the quenching of the single-particle strengths and its binding-energy dependence. For that, the measured semi-inclusive (p, 2p) and (p, pn) cross sections are compared with theoretical predictions based on single-particle cross sections derived from a novel coupled-channels formalism and shell-model spectroscopic factors. A systematic reduction of about 20-30% is found, with a very limited dependence on proton-neutron asymmetry.

## Key claim
The reduction (quenching) of single-particle strengths extracted from intermediate-energy (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions on nitrogen and oxygen isotopes shows a systematic factor of about 0.7-0.8 with very limited dependence on proton-neutron asymmetry, contrary to the strong asymmetry dependence found in heavy-ion knockout reactions but consistent with transfer reaction results.

## Method
The authors employed the transfer-to-the-continuum (TC) reaction formalism, a coupled-channels method that describes the (p,2p) and (p,pn) process as a transfer reaction to unbound states, using the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon interaction and shell-model spectroscopic factors from the WBT interaction. Semi-inclusive cross sections (summed over bound states of the residual nucleus) were calculated for 13 reactions on oxygen and nitrogen isotopes at incident energies of 300-450 MeV/u, using two different sets of distorting potentials: phenomenological Dirac (EDAD2) and microscopic Paris-Hamburg folding potentials.

## Key numbers
- Reduction factors Rs range from 0.53(8) for 21N(p,2p) to 0.89(17) for 23O(p,2p) depending on the optical potential used
- Linear fit for Dirac potentials gives Rs = 0.694(17) - 2.5(12) x 10^-3 ΔS, with reduced chi-squared of 1.15
- Linear fit for Paris-Hamburg potentials gives Rs = 0.766(18) + 0.4(13) x 10^-3 ΔS, with reduced chi-squared of 0.76
- For 16O(p,2p), the extracted Rs values are 0.74(5) (Dirac) and 0.78(6) (Paris-Hamburg)
- For 22O(p,pn), Rs values are 0.74(4) (Dirac) and 0.79(5) (Paris-Hamburg)
- Single-particle cross sections range from 2.844 mb (23O(p,2p), PH) to 10.813 mb (13O(p,2p), Dirac)

## Context
This work provides a unified theoretical analysis of the experimental data from Atar et al. (2018) and Diaz Fernandez et al. (2018), which had previously yielded inconsistent quenching factors due to different reaction formalisms. The results confirm the weak asymmetry dependence found by Atar et al. (2018) using eikonal DWIA and extend it to a larger set of isotopes, while disagreeing with the stronger asymmetry and smaller Rs values reported by Diaz Fernandez et al. (2018) using Faddeev/AGS calculations. The weak ΔS dependence is consistent with low-energy transfer reaction studies (Flaherty et al., 2013) and exclusive (p,2p) measurements (Kawase et al., 2018), but contrasts sharply with the steep asymmetry found in heavy-ion knockout reactions (Gade et al., 2008). The analysis enables more reliable extraction of spectroscopic strengths from (p,pN) reactions and suggests that the strong asymmetry in heavy-ion knockout may reflect reaction mechanism issues rather than nuclear structure effects.
