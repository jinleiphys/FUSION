# Microscopic calculation of proton capture reactions in mass 60-80 region and its astrophysical implications

> Microscopic optical potentials obtained by folding the DDM3Y interaction with the densities from Relativistic Mean Field approach have been utilized to evaluate S-factors of low-energy $(p,γ)$ reactions in mass 60-80 region and to compare with experiments. The Lagrangian density FSU Gold has been employed. Astrophysical rates for important proton capture reactions have been calculated to study the behaviour of rapid proton nucleosynthesis for waiting point nuclei with mass less than A=80.

## Key claim

Microscopic optical potentials built from RMF densities and the DDM3Y interaction, with the real part normalized by a factor of 0.7 and the imaginary part by 0.1, can reproduce experimental S-factors for low-energy (p,γ) reactions in the mass 60–80 region, but the agreement depends on the choice of level density and gamma strength model (HFB performs best).

## Method

Nuclear densities were calculated in a Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach using the FSU Gold Lagrangian density with BCS pairing. A microscopic optical potential was constructed by folding the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction with these densities in the local density approximation, adding a Scheerbaum spin-orbit term. Cross sections and astrophysical rates were computed in the Hauser-Feshbach formalism using the TALYS1.2 code, with level densities and E1 gamma strength functions taken from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations. A small reaction network was designed to study two-proton capture bridging of waiting point nuclei (⁶⁴Ge, ⁶⁸Se, ⁷²Kr, ⁷⁶Sr) at a density of 10⁶ g/cm³ and proton fraction 0.7.

## Key numbers

- Real part of folded DDM3Y potential normalized by factor 0.7; imaginary part normalized by factor 0.1.
- Zero-range pseudopotential J₀₀(E) = −276(1 − 0.005 E/A) MeV fm³.
- Density-dependent factor constants: C = 2.07, b = 1.624 fm².
- Spin-orbit depth parameters: λ_vso = 130 exp(−0.013E) + 40, λ_wso = −0.2(E − 20), with E in MeV.
- Effective half-life of ⁶⁴Ge reduced to substantially less than 10 seconds at 1–2 GK, but uncertainty in Q-values prevents firm conclusion.
- Effective half-lives of ⁶⁸Se and ⁷²Kr not significantly affected by rapid proton capture; ⁷²Kr requires density > 10⁷ g/cm³ to be bridged.
- Effective half-life of ⁷⁶Sr decreases by more than a factor of two at 1–2 GK.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic optical model approach previously applied in the mass-60 region by Lahiri and Gangopadhyay (2010) to a broader set of nuclei up to A=80, using the same RMF+DDM3Y framework but with a revised normalization that avoids nucleus-dependent gamma-strength tuning. It contrasts with the global Hauser-Feshbach rates of Rauscher et al. (2000), which use phenomenological density prescriptions that may be unreliable far from stability. The calculated rates enable a more reliable assessment of whether two-proton capture can bridge waiting point nuclei in rp-process nucleosynthesis during X-ray bursts, with the key finding that ⁷²Kr is unlikely to be bridged, in agreement with the qualitative conclusion of Rauscher et al. but with a slightly different temperature dependence.
