# Diffractive Dissociation of Alpha Particles as a Test of Isophobic Short-Range Correlations inside Nuclei

> The CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Laboratory has compared nuclear parton distributions for a range of nuclear targets and found that the EMC effect measured in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering has a strongly "isophobic" nature. This surprising observation suggests short-range correlations between neighboring $n$ and $p$ nucleons in nuclear wavefunctions that are much stronger compared to $p-p$ or $n-n$ correlations. In this paper we propose a definitive experimental test of the nucleon-nucleon explanation of the isophobic nature of the EMC effect: the diffractive dissociation on a nuclear target $A$ of high energy $\rm ^4He$ nuclei to pairs of nucleons $n$ and $p$ with high relative transverse momentum, $α+ A \to n + p + A' + X $. The comparison of $n-p$ events with $p-p$ and $n-n$ events directly tests the postulated breaking of isospin symmetry. The experiment also tests alternative QCD-level explanations for the isophobic EMC effect. In particular it will test a proposal for hidden-color degrees of freedom in nuclear wavefunctions based on isospin-zero $[ud]$ diquarks.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that diffractive dissociation of high-energy alpha particles on a nuclear target can provide a definitive experimental test of whether the isophobic nature of the EMC effect (where proton-neutron short-range correlations are strongly favored over proton-proton or neutron-neutron pairs) originates from nucleon-level short-range correlations or from QCD-level hidden-color degrees of freedom such as hexa-diquark configurations.

## Method

The authors propose measuring the diffractive dissociation of relativistic ^4He nuclei on a nuclear target A, where the alpha particle breaks into pairs of nucleons with high relative transverse momentum via the reaction α + A → n + p + A' + X. The experiment would compare the relative transverse momenta of n-p events against p-p and n-n events, where isophobic short-range correlations predict that n-p pairs will have maximized transverse momentum differences by a factor of 20 to 1 compared to same-nucleon pairs. The experiment requires verifying Feynman scaling by measuring the cross section at two different beam energies (e.g., 4 GeV and 3.5 GeV) to confirm the cross section is energy independent, then measuring the relative transverse momenta of dissociated products.

## Key numbers

- CLAS collaboration found that proton-neutron pairs in short-range correlations are favored at a factor of 20 times higher than proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs (from CLAS data, 2018)
- The deuteron has a weak binding energy of 2.2 MeV, which the hexa-diquark model may help explain
- For verifying Feynman scaling, the authors suggest a 4 GeV ^4He beam with a second measurement at 3.5 GeV yielding the same cross section results
- The EMC effect is observed in the Bjorken scaling parameter range of 0.3 < x_B < 0.7

## Context

This work extends the diffractive dissociation method developed by Ashery (1999) for probing pion structure via π + A → q + q̄ + A' at Fermilab, applying it to nuclear systems to test the isophobic short-range correlation interpretation of CLAS data (2018). The paper contrasts two models for the EMC effect: a nucleon-based model where short-range correlated n-p pairs dominate, and a QCD-level model proposed in an accompanying paper by West (2020) involving hexa-diquark [ud][ud][ud][ud][ud][ud] configurations and hidden-color degrees of freedom originally discussed by Brodsky (1983). The proposed experiments could discriminate between these models and also test predictions from light-front holographic QCD.
