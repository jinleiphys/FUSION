# Effective meson masses, effective meson-nucleon couplings and neutron star radii

> Using the generalized mean field theory, we have studied the relation among the effective meson masses, the effective meson-nucleon couplings and the equation of state (EOS) in asymmetric nuclear matter. If the effective omega-meson mass becomes smaller at high density, the EOS becomes stiffer. However, if we require that the omega-meson mean field is proportional to the baryon density, the effective omega-nucleon coupling automatically becomes smaller at the same time as the effective omega-meson mass becomes smaller. Consequently, the EOS becomes softer. A similar relation is found for the effective rho-meson mass and the effective rho-nucleon coupling. We have also studied the relation among the effective meson masses, the effective meson-nucleon couplings and a radius R of a neutron star. The R depends somewhat on the value of the effective omega-meson mass and the effective omega-nucleon coupling.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while a reduction of the effective omega-meson mass alone would stiffen the equation of state (EOS), if one simultaneously requires that the omega-meson mean field remains proportional to the baryon density (a condition motivated by quark physics), the effective omega-nucleon coupling automatically decreases along with the mass, and this coupling reduction overcomes the mass effect, making the EOS softer instead.

## Method

The authors use a generalized mean field theory based on a sigma-omega-rho effective Lagrangian that includes arbitrary meson self-interactions and meson-nucleon couplings. They derive analytical relations connecting the effective meson masses, effective meson-nucleon couplings, incompressibility, and symmetry energy. They then perform numerical calculations using the NL3 parameter set as a baseline, adding specific higher-order interactions (omega-meson self-interaction and omega-omega-omega-nucleon interaction) and re-determining parameters to reproduce the five basic nuclear matter properties of NL3.

## Key numbers

- Normal baryon density: rho_B0 = 0.148 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: a_1 = 16.299 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: m*_0 = 0.6 m (with m = 939 MeV)
- Incompressibility: K = 271.76 MeV
- Symmetry energy: a_4 = 37.4 MeV
- In parameter set B_1, c_{omega4} is chosen to yield m*_omega^2 / m_omega^2 = 0.9 at rho_B = rho_B0 and rho_3 = 0
- In parameter set D_1, g_{omega3} is chosen to yield m*_omega^2 / m_omega^2 = 0.9 at rho_B = rho_B0 and rho_3 = 0
- The ambiguity in neutron star radius R is a few hundred meters if |m*_{omega0}^2 - m_omega^2| ~ 0.1 m_omega^2 at normal density

## Context

This work extends the earlier analytical study by Tuchitani et al. (2004) and the findings of Kouno et al. on symmetric nuclear matter to the case of asymmetric nuclear matter. It contrasts with results from relativistic Brueckner Hartree Fock calculations (Brockmann, de Jong) and models with omega-meson self-interaction (Bodmer, Sugahara) that were previously used to describe the EOS. The paper enables more precise predictions of neutron star radii by clarifying how competing medium effects on the omega-meson mass and coupling jointly determine the stiffness of the EOS, which is important for distinguishing normal hadronic matter from exotic matter such as pion condensation (Nakano) or quark matter (Alford).
