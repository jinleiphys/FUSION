# Phase structure of spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases

> We investigate the phase structure of spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases beyond mean-field theory by means of the Functional Renormalization Group. In this approach, quantum and thermal fluctuations are resolved in a systematic manner. The discretization of the effective potential on a grid allows us to accurately account for both first- and second-order phase transitions that are present on the mean-field level. We compute the full phase diagram in the plane of temperature and spin-imbalance and discuss the existence of other conjectured phases such as the Sarma phase and a precondensation region. In addition, we explain on a qualitative level how we expect that in-situ density images are affected by our findings and which experimental signatures may potentially be used to probe the phase structure.

## Key claim

The authors use the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) to compute the phase diagram of spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases beyond mean-field theory, finding that bosonic fluctuations reduce the critical temperature significantly but, contrary to naive expectation, can stabilize the superfluid order at low temperatures and large spin imbalance, raising the critical imbalance above the mean-field value.

## Method

The authors start from a two-channel model of ultracold fermions near a broad Feshbach resonance, integrate out the fermionic fields to obtain an effective description in terms of the bosonic pairing field, and then solve the FRG flow equation for the effective average potential using a grid discretization with at least 100 points to resolve first- and second-order phase transitions. The truncation includes a scale-dependent derivative expansion for the boson propagator while keeping the fermion propagator in its microscopic form, and the flow is computed for two different fermionic regulator choices to check consistency.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature of the balanced system: \(T_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.40\), in good agreement with measurements and Quantum Monte Carlo calculations.
* Tricritical point located at \((\delta\mu_{\rm CP}/\mu,\, T_{\rm CP}/\mu) = (0.76,\, 0.20)\).
* Critical spin-imbalance at zero temperature: \(\delta\mu_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.83\) from extrapolation of the first-order transition line computed for \(T \geq 0.01\), compared to the mean-field value of \(\delta\mu_{\rm c}^{\rm MFA}/\mu = 0.807\).
* Mean-field gap at the zero-temperature first-order transition: \(\Delta_0/\mu = 1.162\) at \(\delta\mu_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.807\).
* Mean-field critical temperature for the balanced case: \(T_{\rm c}/\mu = 0.665\).

## Context

This work extends mean-field studies of spin-imbalanced unitary Fermi gases (e.g., PhysRevLett.96.060401, Gubbels (2006), 2007NatPh...3..124P) by including bosonic fluctuations via the FRG, which resolves the failure of mean-field theory to predict the correct order of phase transitions in some cases. The results enable quantitative comparison with experiments on ultracold Fermi gases (e.g., Ku (2012), PhysRevA.88.063614) and provide guidance for future Monte-Carlo simulations that suffer from the sign problem in imbalanced systems, as well as for studies using new techniques developed by Braun (2012) and Roscher (2013).
