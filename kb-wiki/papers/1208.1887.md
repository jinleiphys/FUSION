# Event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics for Cu-Au collisions at 200GeV

> Event-by-event hydrodynamics is applied to Cu-Au collisions at 200GeV. Predictions for charged particle distributions in pseudorapidity, transverse momentum spectra, femtoscopy radii are given. The triangular and elliptic flow coefficients are calculated. The directed flow at central rapidity in the reaction plane in asymmetric collisions is nonzero, fluctuations of the initial profile lead to a further increase of the directed flow when measured in the event plane.

## Key claim

Event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics predicts a nonzero directed flow at central rapidity in Cu-Au collisions due to the intrinsic asymmetry of the fireball in the reaction plane, with fluctuations of the initial density profile further increasing this directed flow when measured in the event plane.

## Method

The authors solved 3+1-dimensional second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations with shear viscosity to entropy density ratios of η/s = 0.08 or 0.16 and a bulk viscosity of ζ/s = 0.04 in the hadronic phase. Initial entropy density profiles were generated from a Glauber Monte Carlo model with a Gaussian wounding profile, and particle emission at freeze-out (T = 150 MeV) was performed using a Monte Carlo procedure. For each centrality class, 100 hydrodynamic events were generated, with 1000-1500 statistical emission events per freeze-out configuration.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity at central rapidity: dNch/dηPS = 320 for 0-5% centrality and 140 for 20-30% centrality
- Average transverse momenta in central collisions: pions 405 MeV, kaons 587 MeV, protons 775 MeV
- Integrated elliptic flow (v2) for charged particles with pT 150-2000 MeV: 0.022 (η/s=0.08) and 0.020 (η/s=0.16) for central events; 0.048 (η/s=0.08) and 0.043 (η/s=0.16) for peripheral events
- Integrated triangular flow (v3) for charged particles: 0.013 (η/s=0.08) and 0.011 (η/s=0.16) for central events; 0.016 (η/s=0.08) and 0.012 (η/s=0.16) for peripheral events
- Baryon chemical potential at freeze-out: μB = 22 MeV at T = 150 MeV
- Directed flow changes sign at pT ≃ 850 MeV for charged particles with |ηPS| < 1

## Context

This work extends event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations, previously applied to symmetric systems like Au-Au collisions (Bozek 2012), to asymmetric Cu-Au collisions at 200 GeV. The predictions provide a baseline for experimental measurements at RHIC, particularly for the novel directed flow signal arising from the nuclear asymmetry. The results enable tests of initial-state models that assume approximate boost-invariance (Gavin 2008, Gelis 2009, Dusling 2012) and can constrain baryon stopping mechanisms through comparisons of proton and antiproton directed flow (Vance 1998, Weber 2002, Mehtar-Tani 2008).
