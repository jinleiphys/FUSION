# On the nature of the mass-gap object in the GW190814 event

> In this work, we make a very extensive study on the conditions that allow the mass-gap object in the GW190814 event to be faced as a degenerate star instead of a black hole. We begin revisiting some parametrizations of the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and then study under which conditions the hyperons are present in such a massive star. Afterward, using a vector MIT based model, we study if self-bound quark stars, satisfying the Bodmer-Witten conjecture fulfill all the observational constraints. Finally, we study hybrid stars within a Maxwell construction and check for what values of the bag, as well as the vector interaction, a quark core star with only nucleons, and with nucleons admixed with hyperons can reach at least 2.50 M$_\odot$. We conclude that, depending on the choice of parameters, none of the possibilities can be completely ruled out, i.e., the mass-gap object can be a hadronic (either nucleonic or hyperonic), a quark, or a hybrid star. However, some cases are more probable than others.

## Key claim

The mass-gap object in GW190814 (2.5–2.67 M☉) can be a hadronic star (nucleonic or hyperonic), a self-bound quark star, or a hybrid star depending on the choice of model parameters, although some possibilities are more probable than others; no single scenario can be completely ruled out.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model with a modified NL3* parametrization including ω-ρ coupling to compute equations of state for pure nucleonic and hyperonic matter, with hyperon-meson couplings varied via a SU(3) flavor symmetry parameter α. They then employed a vector MIT bag model with two coupling prescriptions (universal and symmetry-group-based) to study self-bound quark stars, and used a Maxwell construction to model hybrid stars with quark cores. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations, and tidal deformabilities were computed from the Love number formalism.

## Key numbers

- Pure nucleonic neutron star maximum mass: 2.75 M☉ with radius 12.87 km
- Hyperonic star with α = 0.50 reaches 2.57 M☉; with α = 0.75 reaches 2.46 M☉ (below the 2.50 M☉ threshold)
- Canonical 1.4 M☉ star radius: 13.38 km for all hadronic models
- Dimensionless tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ = 644 for all hadronic models
- Central speed of sound squared must exceed 0.62 to produce at least a 2.50 M☉ hyperonic star
- Strangeness fraction at core must be below 18% for a 2.50 M☉ hyperonic star
- Λ⁰ hyperon onset occurs at density around 0.33 fm⁻³ for all α values
- Direct Urca process in pure nucleonic stars occurs only above 2.56 M☉

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the GW190814 mass-gap object, including pure nucleonic studies (ref. nuc1, nuc2), hybrid star analyses (ref. rot1, rot2, Han), and quark star investigations (ref. ss1, ss2, Odilon). It contradicts the conclusion of ref. nopt that rules out quark-hadron phase transitions in non-rotating stars, and ref. rot2 that found hyperons unlikely even at Kepler frequencies. The results enable future work to refine which parameter combinations best match the full set of observational constraints from GW190814, NICER, and PREX2.
