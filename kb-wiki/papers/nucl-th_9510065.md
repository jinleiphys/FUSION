# Statistical properties of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for non-nucleon-emission and nucleon-emission processes

> Statistical properties of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) are classical in the case of nucleon emission processes, while they are quantum mechanical for the processes without nucleon emission. We first clarify that there coexist mutually opposite two statistics in the AMD framework: One is the classical statistics of the motion of wave packet centroids and the other is the quantum statistics of the motion of wave packets which is described by the AMD wave function. We prove the classical statistics of wave packet centroids by using the framework of the microcanonical ensemble of the nuclear system. We show that the quantum statistics of wave packets emerges from the classical statistics of wave packet centroids. It is emphasized that the temperature of the classical statistics of wave packet centroids is different from the temperature of the quantum statistics of wave packets. We then explain that the statistical properties of AMD for nucleon emission processes are classical because nucleon emission processes in AMD are described by the motion of wave packet centroids. When we improve the description of the nucleon emission process so as to take into account the momentum fluctuation due to the wave packet spread, the AMD statistical properties for nucleon emission processes change drastically into quantum statistics. Our study of nucleon emission processes can be conversely regarded as giving another kind of proof of the fact that the statistics of wave packets is quantum mechanical while that of wave packet centroids is classical.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework contains two coexisting but opposite statistics: the motion of wave packet centroids obeys classical statistics, while the motion of wave packets described by the AMD wave function obeys quantum statistics. The key qualifier is that these two statistics have different temperatures, and the statistical character relevant for observables depends on whether the process involves nucleon emission (classical) or is confined inside the nucleus (quantum).

## Method

The authors prove the classical statistics of wave packet centroids by applying the microcanonical ensemble technique to a realistic many-nucleon system with an effective two-nucleon force (Volkov No.1 force), using the AMD equation of motion to compute the relation between temperature and energy for 12C. They prove the quantum statistics of wave packets by calculating the occupation probability of harmonic oscillator single-particle levels for a one-dimensional 4-fermion system, assuming classical canonical statistics for the centroids, and comparing the result to the quantum mechanical canonical ensemble average. An analytical proof is also given for distinguishable particles under a harmonic oscillator mean field.

## Key numbers

- For the 12C system, the calculated caloric curve follows \(E^* / 11 = 3 T_c\), where \(E^*\) is the excitation energy and \(T_c\) is the temperature of wave packet centroids, consistent with classical statistics for a system with \(3(A-1)\) degrees of freedom (A=12).
- For the one-dimensional harmonic oscillator system with 4 fermions, the relation \(\langle {\cal H}(Z) \rangle_{T_c} = A T_c + \hbar \omega \sum_{n=0}^{A-1} (n + 1/2)\) holds with very high accuracy, confirming classical statistics of centroids.
- For distinguishable particles, the analytical relation between centroid temperature \(T_c\) and quantum temperature \(T\) is \(T_c = \hbar \omega / (\exp(\hbar \omega / T) - 1)\).
- The occupation probability for distinguishable particles satisfies \(\langle {\cal P}_n (Z) \rangle_{T_c} = \langle \langle P_n \rangle \rangle_T \propto \exp(-n \hbar \omega / T)\).

## Context

This work resolves a controversy between Ohnishi and Randrup (who argued AMD statistics is classical) and Schnack and Feldmeier (who argued it is quantum mechanical), by showing both are correct for different aspects of the AMD framework. The paper extends the analysis beyond the harmonic oscillator model used by both groups to realistic nuclear systems with effective interactions, and provides a microcanonical proof of classical statistics for wave packet centroids. The findings enable improved treatment of nucleon-emission processes in AMD by incorporating wave packet spread effects, which converts the statistics from classical to quantum mechanical for emission processes.
