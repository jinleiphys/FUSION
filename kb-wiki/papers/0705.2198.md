# Charmonium at high temperature in two-flavor QCD

> We compute charmonium spectral functions in 2-flavor QCD on anisotropic lattices using the maximum entropy method. Our results suggest that the S-waves (J/psi and eta_c) survive up to temperatures close to 2Tc, while the P-waves (chi_c0 and chi_c1) melt away below 1.2Tc.

## Key claim

The authors compute charmonium spectral functions in 2-flavor QCD on anisotropic lattices using the maximum entropy method, and their results suggest that S-wave states (J/psi and eta_c) survive up to temperatures close to 2Tc, while P-wave states (chi_c0 and chi_c1) melt away below 1.2Tc. The hedge is that the survival of S-waves is suggested rather than proven, and the melting temperatures for P-waves may be underestimated because the charm quark masses used are somewhat smaller than the physical charm quark mass.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2 dynamical light quark flavors using anisotropic lattices with a spatial lattice spacing of approximately 0.165 fm and a renormalized anisotropy of approximately 6. They computed charmonium correlators in the pseudoscalar (eta_c), vector (J/psi), scalar (chi_c0), and axial-vector (chi_c1) channels using local operators and all-to-all propagators, then extracted spectral functions using the Maximum Entropy Method with Bryan's algorithm and a modified kernel. The pseudocritical temperature Tc was determined by measuring the Polyakov loop on 12^3 x N_tau lattices, finding a jump between N_tau=34 and 33, corresponding to approximately 205-210 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature Tc determined to be approximately 205-210 MeV from Polyakov loop measurements on 12^3 x N_tau lattices
- Spatial lattice spacing a_s approximately 0.165 fm with renormalized anisotropy xi = a_s/a_tau approximately 6
- Sea quark mass corresponds to m_pi/m_rho approximately 0.54
- Zero-temperature ground state masses for Run 7: pseudoscalar 3.145 GeV, vector 3.174 GeV, axial-vector 3.637 GeV, scalar 3.615 GeV
- Pseudoscalar (eta_c) peak persists up to at least T = 392 MeV (N_tau=18), with no peak surviving at T = 440-450 MeV (N_tau=16)
- Vector (J/psi) ground state peak appears to melt around 350 MeV (T/T_c approximately 1.7, N_tau=20)
- Axial-vector (chi_c1) ground state peak survives up to T = 243 MeV (T/T_c=1.16, N_tau=29) but shows no sign at T = 252 MeV (T/T_c=1.2, N_tau=28)
- Scalar (chi_c0) shows no sign of surviving bound state at T = 235 MeV (T/T_c=1.12, N_tau=30)
- In the pseudoscalar channel at T=221-226 MeV, reconstructed correlator differs from actual correlator by no more than 1.5% at large tau when using zero-temperature reference
- In the vector channel at highest temperatures (441-451 MeV), reconstructed correlator differs by less than 10% from actual correlator
- In P-wave channels at T=252 MeV (T/T_c=1.2), reconstructed correlator differs from actual correlator by up to 50%

## Context

This work extends previous quenched lattice QCD studies of charmonium spectral functions (Umeda 2002, Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003, Jakovac 2006) to include dynamical 2-flavor QCD, addressing the question of whether charmonium states survive as bound states in the quark-gluon plasma. The results support the sequential suppression scenario proposed by Karsch (2005), where excited states (P-waves) melt shortly above Tc while the ground state (S-waves) survive to higher temperatures, and provide lattice input for understanding J/psi suppression data from SPS and RHIC experiments. The study enables future work with finer lattices and higher statistics to resolve remaining uncertainties about the exact melting temperatures and to investigate transport contributions in the vector channel related to charm diffusion.
