# On the current correlators in QCD at finite temperature

> Current correlators in QCD at a finite temperature $T$ are considered from the viewpoint of operator product expansion. It is stressed that at low $T$ the heat bath must be represented by hadronic, and not quark-gluon states. A possibility to express the results in terms of $T$-dependent resonance masses is discussed. It is demonstrated that in order $T^2$ the masses do not move and the only phenomenon which occurs is a parity and isospin mixing.

## Key claim

At finite temperature, to order \(T^2\), hadronic resonance masses do not shift; the only phenomenon is parity and isospin mixing in current correlators, contrary to earlier claims of temperature-dependent masses in the QCD sum rule literature.

## Method

The authors analyze thermal current correlators in QCD using the operator product expansion on the light cone, with the heat bath represented by hadronic states (not quark-gluon states) for temperatures below the phase transition. They restrict to the leading order in \(T^2\) using PCAC and current algebra, summing only over one-pion states in the thermal average, and derive mixing relations between vector and axial correlators.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter for low temperatures is \(T^2/f_{\pi}^2\), where \(f_{\pi}=1333\ \text{MeV}\) is the pion decay constant.
- In the chiral limit, the mixing parameter \(\epsilon = (1/3)(T^2/f_{\pi}^2)\) governs the parity mixing between vector and axial correlators.
- The confinement scale is \(R_c^{-1} \sim 0.5\ \text{GeV}\), used to define the condition \(|q^2| \gg R_c^{-2}\) for the OPE.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature QCD sum rule approach of Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (1986) and corrects earlier misinterpretations by Hatsuda, Kunihiro, and Lee (1992) who claimed temperature-dependent masses. The result that masses do not move in order \(T^2\) agrees with chiral perturbation theory calculations for the nucleon by Leutwyler and Smilga (1990). The paper clarifies that the standard QCD sum rule method at \(T=0\) cannot be directly applied at finite temperature due to additional singularities in the \(s\)-channel, and calls for lattice calculations of correlators at intermediate \(q^2\) to determine temperature-dependent condensates.
