# Improved effective-range expansions for small and large values of scattering length

> The textbook effective-range expansion of scattering theory is useful in the analysis of low-energy scattering phenomenology when the scattering length $|a|$ is much larger than the range $R$ of the scattering potential: $|a|\gg R$. Nevertheless, the same has been used for systems where the scattering length is much smaller than the range of the potential, which could be the case in many scattering problems. We suggest and numerically study improved two-parameter effective-range expansions for the cases $|a| > R$ and $|a| < R$. The improved effective-range expansion for $|a| > R$ reduces to the textbook expansion for $|a|/R \gg 1$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes improved two-parameter effective-range expansions for low-energy scattering that work in regimes where the textbook expansion fails: when the scattering length is much smaller than the range of the potential (|a|/R ≪ 1) and when |a|/R < 1 or |a|/R > 1, with the improved expansions reducing to the textbook form only in the limit |a|/R ≫ 1.

## Method

The author derives analytic low-energy Taylor series expansions for k cot δ and k⁻¹ tan δ from the exact S-wave scattering solution for an attractive square-well potential of range R and depth β². By imposing the conditions |a|/R ≪ 1 and |a|/R ≫ 1 on these expansions and keeping next-order terms, the author obtains improved two-parameter expansions (involving scattering length and effective range) for the regimes |a|/R < 1 and |a|/R > 1. The performance of these expansions is tested numerically by comparing them to the exact square-well results for various scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

- For the square-well potential, the first nontrivial zero of scattering length a occurs at βR ≈ 4.4934, where there is a single bound state.
- Tested values of βR near this zero: β = 4.4, 4.45, 4.4934, and 4.515, giving scattering lengths a/R = 0.2963, 0.1633, 0, and -0.21, respectively, all satisfying |a|/R ≪ 1.
- For the large-scattering-length regime, the paper tests a/R = -3.14, 0, and 2.54.
- For the intermediate regime |a| ∼ R, the paper tests a/R = -1 and a/R = 1.

## Context

This work extends the textbook effective-range expansion (Bethe 1949) which is valid only for |a|/R ≫ 1, as used in the triplet and singlet neutron-proton scattering channels. The paper addresses the breakdown of the standard expansion when a → 0, as noted in neutron-deuteron and pion-pion scattering (Adhikari 1998) and in cold-atom scattering near Feshbach resonances (Ketterle 2002). The improved expansions enable reliable low-energy scattering phenomenology for systems where the scattering length is comparable to or smaller than the interaction range, and can be tested against experimental data or more sophisticated potential models.
