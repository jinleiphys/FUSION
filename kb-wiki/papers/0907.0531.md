# One-neutron removal strength of 7He into 6He using the complex scaling method

> We study the one-neutron removal strength of the 7He ground state, which provides us with the 6He-n component in 7He. The He isotopes are described on the basis of the 4He+Xn cluster model (X=1,2,3). The complex scaling method is applied to describe not only the Gamow resonances but also the nonresonant continuum states of valence neutrons, with the correct boundary condition of particle decays. The one-neutron removal strength of 7He into the unbound states of 6He is calculated using the complex-scaled Green's function, in which a complex-scaled complete set of 4He+n+n states is adopted. Using this framework, we investigate resonant and nonresonant contributions of the strength, which individually produce specific structures in the distributions. In addition, we propose a method to obtain the real-value strength using the complex values of spectroscopic factors of Gamow states. As a result, the 6He(2+) resonance is found to give the largest contribution.

## Key claim

The one-neutron removal strength of the 7He ground state into unbound states of 6He is calculated using the complex scaling method, with the 6He(2+) resonance found to give the largest contribution; however, the calculated 7He ground state energy of 0.184 MeV above the 6He+n threshold is slightly overbound compared to experimental values of 0.44(2) MeV and 0.36(5) MeV, and only when the observed energy is fitted by reducing the three-cluster interaction strength does the decay width of 0.14 MeV agree with experiments.

## Method

The He isotopes are described using the 4He+Xn cluster orbital shell model (COSM) with X=1,2,3, employing the modified KKNN potential for 4He-n interactions and the Minnesota potential for n-n interactions. The complex scaling method (CSM) is applied to transform the Hamiltonian, allowing description of Gamow resonances and nonresonant continuum states of valence neutrons with correct boundary conditions for particle decays. The one-neutron removal strength of 7He into 6He is calculated using the complex-scaled Green's function, which incorporates a complete set of 4He+n+n states including bound, resonant, and continuum contributions, and the strength is decomposed into contributions from 6He resonances, 5He+n continuum, and 4He+n+n continuum.

## Key numbers

- 7He ground state resonance energy: 0.184 MeV above the 6He+n threshold (calculated), compared to experimental values of 0.44(2) MeV (Ko99) and 0.36(5) MeV (Sk06)
- 7He ground state decay width: smaller than experimental value of approximately 0.16 MeV (Ko99, Sk06); when fitted to observed energy of 0.44 MeV, width becomes 0.14 MeV
- 6He(0+1) ground state energy: -0.974 MeV from the 4He+2n threshold, matching the experimental value of -0.975 MeV
- 6He(0+2) resonance: energy 3.90 MeV, width 8.77 MeV
- Sum rule value of the one-neutron removal strength integrated over energy: 3 (number of valence neutrons in 7He)
- Gaussian basis functions used: at most 17 with maximum range of 40 fm; radial part of operator a_alpha expanded with 40 Gaussian basis functions with maximum range of 80 fm
- Scaling angle theta: 30 degrees

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Myo 2007) on 7He spectroscopy using the complex scaling method, which only considered resonant components (S factors) of final states, by now including nonresonant continuum contributions to obtain the full observable strength function. The method is analogous to successful analyses of Coulomb breakup strengths of halo nuclei using CSM (Myo 2001, Aoyama 2006). The approach enables unambiguous classification of contributions from 6He resonances, 5He+n continuum, and 4He+n+n continuum, and provides a way to obtain real-valued observable strengths from complex spectroscopic factors of Gamow states, addressing a problem noted in prior work (Myo 2007).
