# Manifestation of the Berry phase in the atomic nucleus $^{213}$Pb

> The neutron-rich $^{213}$Pb isotope was produced in the fragmentation of a primary 1 GeV $A$ $^{238}$U beam, separated in FRS in mass and atomic number, and then implanted for isomer decay $γ$-ray spectroscopy with the RISING setup at GSI. A newly observed isomer and its measured decay properties indicate that states in $^{213}$Pb are characterized by the seniority quantum number that counts the nucleons not in pairs coupled to angular momentum $J=0$. The conservation of seniority is a consequence of the Berry phase associated with particle-hole conjugation, which becomes gauge invariant and therefore observable in semi-magic nuclei where nucleons half-fill the valence shell. The $γ$-ray spectroscopic observables in $^{213}$Pb are thus found to be driven by two mechanisms, particle-hole conjugation and seniority conservation, which are intertwined through the Berry phase.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed decay properties of a newly discovered isomer in \(^{213}\)Pb are driven by the Berry phase associated with particle-hole conjugation, which becomes gauge invariant and therefore observable in semi-magic nuclei where nucleons half-fill the valence shell. The authors hedge that this mechanism explains *partial* conservation of seniority for \(j=9/2\) (rather than total conservation), and that the argument depends on the two-body interaction being invariant under the particle-hole transformation, which would not hold for three-body interactions.

## Method

The neutron-rich isotope \(^{213}\)Pb was produced via fragmentation of a 1 GeV/A \(^{238}\)U beam on a Be target at GSI, separated by the FRS spectrometer, and implanted for isomer decay \(\gamma\)-ray spectroscopy using the RISING germanium detector array. Gamma-gamma coincidence and time-decay analysis were used to construct the level scheme and extract transition probabilities. The experimental results were compared with two shell-model calculations: a simple \((1g_{9/2})^5\) model with matrix elements interpolated between \(^{210}\)Pb and \(^{216}\)Pb, and a large-scale shell-model calculation using the Kuo-Herling interaction in the full neutron valence space beyond \(N=126\).

## Key numbers

- Half-life of the \(21/2^+\) isomeric state in \(^{213}\)Pb: \(t_{1/2} = 0.26(2)\ \mu\)s.
- Gamma-ray energies assigned to the isomer decay: 772, 488, 369, 311, 190, and 176 keV, with uncertainties of \(\pm1\) keV (except \(\pm2\) keV for the 176 keV transition).
- Reduced \(E2\) transition probability from the \(21/2^+\) isomer to the first \(17/2^+\) state: \(B(E2;21/2^+\rightarrow17/2^+_1) = 1.1(4)\ e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- Reduced \(E2\) transition probability from the \(21/2^+\) isomer to the second \(17/2^+\) state: \(B(E2;21/2^+\rightarrow17/2^+_2) = 32(5)\ e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- Large-scale shell-model calculation with Kuo-Herling interaction predicts \(B(E2;21/2^+\rightarrow17/2^+_1) = 0.2\ e^2\text{fm}^4\) and \(B(E2;21/2^+\rightarrow17/2^+_2) = 65\ e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- Overlap of large-scale shell-model wave functions with \((1g_{9/2})^5\) configurations: 0.84 for \(21/2^+\), 0.83 for \(17/2^+_1\) (both \(\upsilon=3\)), and 0.93 for \(17/2^+_2\) (\(\upsilon=5\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of seniority conservation in semi-magic nuclei, previously investigated in \(^{210}\)Hg by the same collaboration, and provides the first clear experimental signature of the Berry phase in nuclear structure. The results confirm the partial dynamical symmetry predicted by Escuderos et al. (2006) and Van Isacker et al. (2008) for \(j=9/2\) mid-shell nuclei, where seniority mixing is restricted to \(\Delta\upsilon = \pm4\). The findings enable future studies of seniority selection rules in one-nucleon transfer reactions and motivate investigations of Berry-phase consequences in nuclei where both neutrons and protons are at mid-shell.
