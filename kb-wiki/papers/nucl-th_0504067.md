# Renormalization of the Deuteron with One Pion Exchange

> We analyze the deuteron bound state through the One Pion Exchange Potential. We pay attention to the short distance peculiar singularity structure of the bound state wave functions in coordinate space and the elimination of short distance ambiguities by selecting the regular solution at the origin. We determine the so far elusive amplitude of the converging exponential solutions at the origin. All bound state deuteron properties can then be uniquely deduced from the deuteron binding energy, the pion-nucleon coupling constant and pion mass. This generates correlations among deuteron properties. Scattering phase shifts and low energy parameters in the 3S1-3D1 channel are constructed by requiring orthogonality of the positive energy states to the deuteron bound state, yielding an energy independent combination of boundary conditions. We also analyze from the viewpoint of short distance boundary conditions the weak binding regime on the light of long distance perturbation theory and discuss the approach to the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The authors show that the One Pion Exchange (OPE) potential, taken seriously down to arbitrarily short distances without any physical cutoff, yields unique and cutoff-independent predictions for deuteron properties and scattering observables once the deuteron binding energy is treated as an input parameter, contradicting earlier claims that an additional condition (such as setting the converging exponential coefficient to zero) would predict the binding energy itself.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrödinger equations for the \(^3S_1-{}^3D_1\) system using the OPE potential in coordinate space, integrating the asymptotic solutions from infinity inward and imposing regularity at the origin by requiring the coefficient of the diverging exponential in the repulsive channel to vanish. They use six different short-distance boundary conditions (BC1-BC6) as auxiliary regulators to numerically extract the asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta\) and other deuteron observables, checking stability as the cutoff radius is removed. Scattering phase shifts and low-energy parameters are constructed by enforcing orthogonality of scattering states to the deuteron bound state.

## Key numbers

* Asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta_{\rm OPE} = 0.02633\) for \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\) and \(m=138.03\ {\rm MeV}\), compared to the experimental value \(\eta_d = 0.0256(4)\)
* S-wave normalization \(A_S = 0.8681(1)\ {\rm fm}^{-1/2}\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* Matter radius \(r_m = 1.9351(5)\ {\rm fm}\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* Quadrupole moment \(Q_d = 0.2762(1)\ {\rm fm}^2\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* D-state probability \(P_D = 7.88(1)\%\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* Inverse radius \(\langle r^{-1} \rangle = 0.476(3)\ {\rm fm}^{-1}\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* Scattering length \(\alpha_0 = 5.335(1)\ {\rm fm}\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* Effective range \(r_0 = 1.638(1)\ {\rm fm}\) for the OPE calculation with \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\)
* The OPE length scale \(R = 1.07764\ {\rm fm}\) for \(g_{\pi NN}=13.1083\), and \(R = 1.1108\ {\rm fm}\) for \(g_{\pi NN}=13.3158\)

## Context

This work extends the early correlation analysis of Glendenning and Kramer (1962), who recognized that truncating the OPE potential at a finite radius generates correlations among deuteron observables, and the rigorous inequality approach of Klarsfeld, Martorell, and Sprung (1980, 1984). It contradicts the claim of Martorell et al. (1994) that the converging exponential coefficient \(C_{2R}\) must vanish, a condition implicitly used by Beane et al. (2001) and supported by large \(N_c\) arguments from Beane et al. (2002). The results enable a systematic investigation of two-pion exchange contributions and other effects in a cutoff-independent manner, and provide a framework for studying the chiral limit and pion mass dependence of deuteron properties.
