# Transition From Ideal To Viscous Mach Cones In A Kinetic Transport Approach

> Using a microscopic transport model we investigate the evolution of conical structures originating from the supersonic projectile moving through the hot matter of ultrarelativistic particles. Using different scenarios for the interaction between projectile and matter, and different transport properties of the matter, we study the formation and structure of Mach cones. Especially, a dependence of the Mach cone angle on the details and rate of the energy deposition from projectile to the matter is investigated. Furthermore, the two-particle correlations extracted from the numerical calculations are compared to an analytical approximation. We find that the propagation of a high energetic particle through the matter does not lead to the appearance of a double peak structure as observed in the ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. The reason is the strongly forward-peaked energy and momentum deposition in the head shock region. In addition, by adjusting the cross section we investigate the influence of the viscosity to the structure of Mach cones. A clear and unavoidable smearing of the profile depending on a finite ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is clearly visible.

## Key claim

Using a microscopic transport model (BAMPS) with a jet-like source (JET scenario) that deposits both energy and forward-peaked momentum, a double-peak structure in two-particle correlations does not appear even in the nearly ideal fluid limit, because the head shock region dominates the angular distribution; a double peak only appears in the unphysical pure energy deposition (PED) scenario with a sufficiently high energy deposition rate.

## Method

The Boltzmann equation for massless on-shell particles was solved using the BAMPS parton cascade in a static box with a uniform medium at T_med = 400 MeV, considering only binary isotropic collisions. Two source scenarios were implemented: a pure energy deposition (PED) source emitting particles isotropically in its local rest frame, and a JET source representing a highly energetic massless particle depositing energy and momentum via collisions while its momentum is reset after each collision. The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s was varied from 0.005 to 0.5 by adjusting the mean free path, and the energy deposition rate dE/dx was varied from 1 to 200 GeV/fm.

## Key numbers

- Medium temperature: T_med = 400 MeV, corresponding to LRF energy density e_med = 16.28 GeV/fm^3.
- Energy deposition rates studied: dE/dx = 1, 10, and 200 GeV/fm.
- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratios studied: η/s = 0.005, 0.05, 0.2, and 0.5.
- For the analytic model with dE/dx = 10 GeV/fm: e_cone = 22.15 GeV/fm^3 and v_coll = 0.137; for dE/dx = 200 GeV/fm: e_cone = 62.55 GeV/fm^3 and v_coll = 0.537.
- Jet energies used in the JET scenario: E_jet = 20, 200, and 20000 GeV.
- Snapshot time for all Mach cone visualizations: t = 2.5 fm/c.
- Ideal weak-perturbation Mach angle: α_w = arccos(1/√3) ≈ 54.73°.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of Mach cones in ideal hydrodynamics (Betz 2008) and AdS/CFT (Noronha 2008) by using a kinetic transport approach (BAMPS) that can handle arbitrary gradients and any viscosity, unlike viscous hydrodynamics which becomes unreliable at large η/s. The results contradict the earlier assumption that Mach cones from jets produce the double-peak structure observed in two-particle correlations at RHIC (Wang 2004), and instead support the alternative explanation from triangular flow due to initial fluctuations (Ma 2010). The scaling relation derived for the energy density profiles connects this work to the one-dimensional Riemann problem studied in Bouras (2009).
