# On the Role of Energy Conservation in High-Energy Nuclear Scattering

> We argue that most commonly used models for nuclear scattering at ultra-relativistic energies do not treat energy conservation in a consistent fashion. Demanding theoretical consistency as a minimal requirement for a realistic model, we provide a solution for the above-mentioned problem, the so-called ``Parton-Based Gribov-Regge Theory''. In order to keep a clean picture, we do not consider secondary interactions. We provide a very transparent extrapolation of the physics of more elementary interactions towards nucleus-nucleus scattering, without considering any nuclear effects due to final state interactions. In this sense we consider our model a realistic and consistent approach to describe the initial stage of nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that most commonly used models for ultra-relativistic nuclear scattering (including Gribov-Regge theory and the parton model) treat energy conservation inconsistently between cross section calculations and particle production calculations; they present the "Parton-Based Gribov-Regge Theory" as a solution that enforces energy conservation in both cases, but only for the initial stage of nuclear collisions, without considering secondary interactions or final state effects.

## Method

The authors construct a multiple scattering theory based on a Fock state expansion of hadron eigenstates, where hadron-hadron and nucleus-nucleus scattering amplitudes are expressed as convolutions of elementary parton-parton scattering amplitudes (soft, hard, and semi-hard contributions) with momentum distributions of participating parton constituents. The elementary interactions are treated as Pomeron exchanges, with the hard and semi-hard contributions incorporating perturbative QCD parton ladders, and energy conservation is enforced by sharing the total incoming energy among all parallel elementary interactions through the momentum fractions of the constituent partons.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff for perturbative QCD applicability is set at Q0^2 ~ 1 GeV^2
- The soft Pomeron amplitude is parameterized as T(s,t) ~ i s^(alpha_0 + alpha' t), with the intercept alpha_soft(0) and slope alpha'_soft as parameters
- The hard scattering slope R_hard^2 is negligible compared to the soft Pomeron slope, approximated as R_hard^2 ~ 0
- The factorization scale for hard processes is taken as M_F^2 = p_perp^2/4
- A K-factor of K ~ 1.5 is used to account for higher order QCD corrections
- The characteristic hadronic mass scale is s0 ~ 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends Gribov-Regge theory (Gribov 1968, 1969) by enforcing consistent energy conservation in both cross section and particle production calculations, a problem first discussed by Abramovsky (1992) and Braun (1990). It contrasts with semi-classical treatments and classical Yang-Mills equations approaches (McLerran 1994) that ignore quantum mechanical interference, and with the parton model approach (Sjostrand 1987) where eikonalization does not provide a clear path to nucleus-nucleus collisions. The model enables a consistent description of the initial stage of nuclear collisions for RHIC and LHC, with the next planned step being the inclusion of screening corrections from triple-Pomeron interactions to restore unitarity at very high energies.
