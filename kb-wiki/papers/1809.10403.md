# Manifestation of important role of nuclear forces in emission of photons in scattering of pions off nuclei

> Bremsstrahlung of photons emitted during the scattering of $π^{+}$-mesons off nuclei is studied for the first time. Role of interactions between $π^{+}$-mesons and nuclei in the formation of the bremsstrahlung emission is analyzed in details. We discover essential contribution of emitted photons from nuclear part of Johnson-Satchler potential to the full spectrum, in contrast to the optical Woods-Saxon potential. We observe unusual essential influence of the nuclear part of both potentials on the spectrum at high photon energies. This phenomenon opens a new experimental way to study and check non-Coulomb and nuclear interactions between pions and nuclei via measurements of the emitted photons. We provide predictions of the bremsstrahlung spectra for pion scattering off $^{44}{\rm Ca}$.

## Key claim
The authors claim that bremsstrahlung photons emitted during pion-nucleus scattering are sufficiently sensitive to the shape of the nuclear potential to serve as a new experimental probe of non-Coulomb nuclear interactions, but this sensitivity is conditional on the high-energy region of the photon spectrum and depends on which potential model (Johnson-Satchler vs. Woods-Saxon) is used.

## Method
The authors generalize a previously developed bremsstrahlung formalism for proton-nucleus scattering to the pion-nucleus system, starting from the Dirac equation and reducing it to a Pauli-type equation for the many-body system. They compute the leading matrix element for photon emission based on relative motion between the pion and the nucleus, using two different local potential models for the nuclear interaction: the Johnson-Satchler potential (derived from a nonlocal Kisslinger-type form via the Krell-Ericson transformation) and the optical Woods-Saxon potential. The calculations are performed for elastic scattering of positive pions off calcium-44, neglecting the imaginary part of the potentials and internal nuclear structure effects.

## Key numbers
- The bremsstrahlung spectra are predicted for pion scattering off calcium-44 (no specific cross-section numbers are given in the truncated text).
- The incident pion energy range considered in the broader context of the field is from 30 MeV up to 860 MeV (cited from prior experimental work).
- The nucleus used for predictions is calcium-44, which has 20 protons and 24 neutrons.

## Context
This work extends the study of bremsstrahlung from nuclear reactions, previously applied to proton-nucleus scattering by Maydanyuk (2011, 2012, 2015) and alpha decay by Maydanyuk (2003, 2006, 2008, 2009, 2016), to the unexplored case of real pion-nucleus scattering. It contrasts the Johnson-Satchler potential (Johnson and Satchler, 1996) with the Woods-Saxon optical model potential (Akhter, 2001) and finds that the nuclear part of both potentials has an unexpectedly large influence on the high-energy photon spectrum. The work enables future experimental measurements of bremsstrahlung photons as a new tool to study non-Coulomb pion-nucleus interactions, which previously could only be probed through elastic and inelastic scattering cross-section measurements.
