# Photon Self-Energy in a Color Superconductor

> In a color superconductor the diquark condensates break spontaneously both the color and ordinary electromagnetism, leaving a remanining rotated U(1) symmetry unbroken. The gauge interactions associated to this rotated symmetry may be considered as the in-medium electromagnetism. We compute the in-medium photon self-energy in the presence of diquark condensates at high baryonic density and weak coupling. This is done to one-loop order for the cases of two and three quark flavors. For vanishing temperature, a detailed discussion is given of the low momentum behavior of the photon polarization tensor. A simple physical picture for the propagation of light in color superconducting media is obtained. The main new effect is due to the diquark condensates, which lead to a strong dielectric constant of the medium. The magnetic permeability remains unchanged, because the primary condensates have vanishing spin and angular momentum. In the two flavor case, an additional contribution arises due to gapless quarks and electrons, which is responsible for Debye screening effects. We also discuss the low energy effective theory for the three flavor case in the presence of electromagnetic interactions.

# Photon Self-Energy in a Color Superconductor

## Key claim

The diquark condensates in a color superconductor produce a strong dielectric constant for the in-medium (rotated) photon, while the magnetic permeability remains unchanged because the condensates have vanishing spin and angular momentum; this result holds at one-loop order for both two-flavor (2SC) and three-flavor (CFL) phases at high baryonic density and weak coupling, but only at vanishing temperature and for low momenta compared to the gap.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop photon self-energy in Euclidean space-time using the imaginary time formalism, working in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism with color-flavor basis transformations that diagonalize the gap matrix. They evaluate the polarization tensor for both the CFL and 2SC phases by summing over Matsubara frequencies and performing the color-flavor traces, then analytically continue to Minkowski space to study the low-momentum behavior. The charged pion and kaon contributions are computed separately and shown to be negligible in the infrared limit.

## Key numbers

- The dielectric constant of the CFL medium is given by $\widetilde\epsilon = 1 + \widetilde\kappa = 1 + \frac{2}{9\pi^2}\frac{\widetilde e^2\mu^2}{\Delta^2}$, where $\widetilde e = e\cos\theta_{\rm CFL}$ is the rotated photon coupling, $\mu$ is the chemical potential, and $\Delta$ is the octet gap.
- The coefficient $\widetilde\kappa = \frac{c}{18\pi^2}\frac{\widetilde e^2\mu^2}{\Delta^2}$ with $c=4$ for the CFL phase, counting the squared charges of all gapped quarks in units of $\widetilde e$.
- The Debye mass in the hard dense loop limit for the CFL phase is $\widetilde M^2 = 4\frac{\widetilde e^2\mu^2}{\pi^2}$.
- The rotation angle for the in-medium photon in the CFL phase satisfies $\cos\theta_{\rm CFL} = \frac{\sqrt{3}g}{\sqrt{3g^2+4e^2}}$ and $\sin\theta_{\rm CFL} = \frac{2e}{\sqrt{3g^2+4e^2}}$, where $g$ is the strong coupling and $e$ the electromagnetic coupling.
- The longitudinal and transverse parts of the polarization tensor at low momentum are $\Pi_L(p_0,{\bf p}) = -\widetilde\kappa p^2$ and $\Pi_T(p_0,{\bf p}) = -\widetilde\kappa p_0^2$.

## Context

This work extends the study of gauge field propagation in color superconductors, building on the symmetry breaking patterns identified by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) for the CFL phase and by Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (1998) for the 2SC phase. The computation parallels the analysis of the unbroken $SU(2)_c$ gauge field polarization tensor in the 2SC phase by Rischke (2000), and the results enable calculation of electromagnetic masses for charged pions and kaons in the CFL phase (previously estimated by Manuel and Tytgat, 2001) as well as transport properties such as viscosities and conductivities of superconducting quark matter relevant for neutron star cores.
