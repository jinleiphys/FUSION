# Partially conserved axial current constraints on pion production/absorption within nonrelativistic dynamics

> We show the necessity of two-nucleon axial currents and associated pion emission/ absorption operators for the partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) nuclear matrix elements with arbitrary nuclear dynamics described by a nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation. As examples we construct such nonrelativistic axial two-body currents in the linear- and heterotic (g_A = 1.26) sigma models, with an optional isoscalar vector (omega) meson exchange. The nuclear matrix elements obey PCAC only if the nuclear wave functions used in the calculation are solutions to the Schroedinger equation with the static one-meson-exchange potential constructed in the respective (sigma) model. The same holds true for the nucler pion production amplitude, since it is proportional to the divergence of the axial current matrix element, by virtue of PCAC. Thus we found a new consistency condition between the pion creation/absorption operator and the nuclear Hamiltonian. We present examples drawn from our models and discuss implications for one-pion-two-nucleon processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-nucleon axial currents and associated pion emission/absorption operators are always necessary for partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) in nonrelativistic nuclear dynamics, and that the nuclear wave functions used must be solutions to the Schroedinger equation with the same one-meson-exchange potential from which the axial currents are derived; this consistency condition between the pion creation/absorption operator and the nuclear Hamiltonian is a new requirement not previously recognized.

## Method

The authors prove generally that for any nonrelativistic two-nucleon system described by a Schroedinger equation with a nontrivial potential, the commutator of the potential with the one-body axial charge density never vanishes, necessitating two-body axial meson-exchange currents (MECs) to satisfy PCAC. They then construct explicit nonrelativistic axial two-body currents by taking the nonrelativistic limit of covariant Feynman amplitudes from the linear sigma model and the heterotic sigma model (with g_A = 1.26), including optional isoscalar vector (omega) meson exchange, and verify that the resulting currents satisfy the PCAC relation when used with wave functions from the corresponding one-boson-exchange potentials.

## Key numbers

- The heterotic sigma model uses g_A = 1.26 for the spatial part of the axial current, while the linear sigma model uses g_A = 1
- The one-body axial charge density is of order 1/M, where M is the nucleon mass
- The sigma-exchange potential is of order 1/M^0 = 1, while the pion-exchange potential is of order 1/M^2
- The sigma-exchange axial MECs are constructed to order 1/M in the nonrelativistic expansion
- The Goldberger-Treiman relation in the linear sigma model gives M = g_0 f_pi, while in the heterotic sigma model it gives g_A M = g_piNN f_pi

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Bethe-Salpeter approach to axial current conservation (Dmitrasinovic and Sato, 1996) into the nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation framework, showing that the same consistency constraints between wave functions and currents apply. It contradicts earlier approaches by Blin-Stoyle and Tint (1967) and Kroll and Ruderman (1966) that treated pion production operators without reference to the nuclear wave functions, and provides a systematic method for constructing consistent pion production/absorption operators for use in reactions such as pp -> pi0 pp and pp -> pi+ d, building on models by Horowitz (1993) and Riska (1993).
