# Mean-field description of ground-state properties of drip-line nuclei. (I) Shell-correction method

> A shell-correction method is applied to nuclei far from the beta stability line and its suitability to describe effects of the particle continuum is discussed. The sensitivity of predicted locations of one- and two-particle drip lines to details of the macroscopic-microscopic model is analyzed.

## Key claim

The shell-correction method, when applied to nuclei far from stability, yields unstable shell corrections that depend strongly on the size of the harmonic-oscillator basis used to discretize the continuum, meaning that total masses and separation energies for drip-line nuclei cannot be reliably estimated using the standard averaging method with quasibound states.

## Method

The authors applied the macroscopic-microscopic (shell-correction) method using a deformed Woods-Saxon average potential with parameters from Dudek (1981), expanding eigenstates in an axially deformed harmonic-oscillator basis. They compared two approaches for computing the smooth single-particle energy: the standard Strutinsky averaging method (SAM) using quasibound states, and the semiclassical Wigner-Kirkwood (WK) expansion, which treats the continuum properly through phase-shift integrals. The BCS and Lipkin-Nogami pairing treatments were also examined, with pairing strengths determined by both the average gap method and a fitted isospin-dependent formula.

## Key numbers

- For the neutron-rich system ¹²²Zr, the shell correction from SAM varied linearly with the oscillator basis size N_osc (tested from 10 to 50), showing no convergence; for ¹⁰⁰Zr the shell correction slowly increased with N_osc, while for ¹²²Zr it decreased.
- The average deviation between E_shell(SAM) and E_shell(WK) ranged from approximately 0.4 MeV in ¹⁰⁰Zr and ¹⁶⁶Os to approximately 4.6 MeV in ¹²⁴Zr and approximately 7.6 MeV in ²²⁶Os.
- For the spherical shell correction of ¹⁰⁰Zr with N_osc=12, a local plateau in E_shell was found at approximately 6.7 MeV for p=6,8 and γ/ħω₀=1.0–1.2; for ¹²²Zr no unambiguous value could be extracted.
- The smoothing range γ was set to ħω₀B_s (with B_s=1 at spherical shape), curvature-correction order p=8, and basis size N_osc=12 in the SAM calculations, following the prescription of Möller et al. (1993).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lin (1970) and Ross and Bhaduri (1972) on continuum effects in the Strutinsky method, and directly addresses the instability of shell corrections for drip-line nuclei noted by Bunatian et al. (1972) and Strutinsky and Ivanyuk (1979). The authors demonstrate that the semiclassical Wigner-Kirkwood expansion, shown by Jennings (1973, 1975) to be equivalent to the full Strutinsky averaging with proper resonance treatment, provides a practical correction for large-scale mass calculations such as those of Möller et al. (1993). The results motivate the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov treatment of pairing in drip-line nuclei presented in Part II of this work (Dobaczewski 1994).
