# Unraveling collisional energy loss of a heavy quark in quark-gluon plasma

> At leading order in QCD coupling constant, we compute the energy loss per traveling distance of a heavy quark $dE/dz$ from elastic scattering off thermal quarks and gluons at a temperature $T$, including the thermal perturbative description of soft scatterings ($-t<-t^{\ast}$) and a perturbative QCD-based calculation for hard collisions ($-t>-t^{\ast}$). Within this soft-hard factorization model, we find that the full results of $dE/dz$ behaves a mild sensitivity to the intermediate cutoff $t^{\ast}$, supporting the validity of the soft-hard approach within the temperature region of interest. We re-derive the analytic formula for $dE/dz$ in the high-energy approximation, $E_{1}\gg m^{2}_{1}/T$, where $E_{1}$ is the injected heavy quark energy and $m_{1}$ is its mass. It is realized that the soft logarithmic contribution, $dE/dz\propto ln(-t^{\ast}/m^{2}_{D})$, arises from the $t$-channel scattering off thermal partons, while the hard logarithmic term, $dE/dz\propto ln[E_{1}T/(-t^{\ast})]$, stems from the $t$-channel scattering off thermal partons, and the one $dE/dz\propto ln(E_{1}T/m^{2}_{1})$ comes from the $s$- and $u$-channel scattering off gluons. The sum of these contributions cancels the $t^{\ast}$-dependence as observed in the full result. The mass hierarchy is observed $dE/dz(charm)>dE/dz(bottom)$. Our full results are crucial for a better description of heavy quark transport in QCD medium, in particular at low and moderate energy. We also calculate the energy loss by imposing the Einstein's relationship. The related results appear to be systematically larger than that without imposing the Einstein's relationship.

## Key claim

The paper computes the collisional energy loss per distance \(dE/dz\) of a heavy quark in quark-gluon plasma using a soft-hard factorization model at leading order in QCD coupling, finding that the full result has only mild sensitivity to the intermediate cutoff \(t^{\ast}\) and that the mass hierarchy \(dE/dz(\text{charm}) > dE/dz(\text{bottom})\) holds, but the results are derived in the weak-coupling or high-temperature limit where the coupling is very small, which may not strictly apply to realistic RHIC and LHC conditions.

## Method

The authors decompose heavy quark elastic scattering off thermal quarks and gluons into soft collisions (\(-t < -t^{\ast}\)) treated with hard-thermal-loop (HTL) propagators to account for Debye screening, and hard collisions (\(-t > -t^{\ast}\)) treated with tree-level perturbative QCD matrix elements. The total energy loss is the sum of soft \(t\)-channel, hard \(t\)-channel, and \(s\)- and \(u\)-channel contributions, with the intermediate cutoff \(t^{\ast}\) chosen to satisfy \(m_D^2 \ll -t^{\ast} \ll T^2\). The energy loss is also calculated via the Einstein fluctuation-dissipation relation by relating it to the drag coefficient derived from momentum diffusion coefficients.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark energy loss \(dE/dz\) at initial energy \(E = 100\ \text{GeV}\) is shown as a function of temperature for \(-t^{\ast} = 4 m_D^2\) and \(\mu = \pi T\); the soft contribution dominates over the hard \(t\)-channel and \(s+u\) contributions across the temperature range.
* The Debye screening mass squared is \(m_D^2 = (N_c/3 + N_f/6) g^2 T^2\) with \(N_c = 3\) and \(N_f = 3\).
* The two-loop running coupling uses \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 261\ \text{MeV}\) and scale \(\mu = \pi T\).
* In the high-energy approximation (\(E_1 \gg m_1^2/T\)), the total energy loss is \(dE/dz = (4/3)\pi \alpha_s^2 T^2 \left[ (1 + N_f/6) \ln(E_1 T / m_D^2) + (2/9) \ln(E_1 T / m_1^2) + d(N_f) \right]\) with \(d(N_f) \approx 0.145901 N_f + 0.050213\).
* The soft logarithmic contribution scales as \(dE/dz \propto \ln(-t^{\ast}/m_D^2)\), the hard \(t\)-channel term as \(\ln[E_1 T/(-t^{\ast})]\), and the \(s\)- and \(u\)-channel term as \(\ln(E_1 T / m_1^2)\); the sum cancels the \(t^{\ast}\)-dependence.

## Context

This work extends the original Bjorken (1982) perturbative calculation of collisional energy loss for massless partons by providing a complete leading-order calculation for heavy quarks (charm and bottom) at low and moderate energies, improving upon treatments that used simple infrared cutoffs or Debye mass regulators. The soft-hard factorization approach follows earlier work by Braaten and Thoma (1991) and Romatschke and Strickland (2003-2004), and the authors previously applied it to momentum diffusion coefficients (Li et al., 2021). The results enable more accurate modeling of heavy quark transport in QGP, particularly for low- and moderate-energy heavy quarks in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
