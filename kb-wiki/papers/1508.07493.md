# Universal noise and Efimov physics

> Probability distributions for correlation functions of particles interacting via random-valued fields are discussed as a novel tool for determining the spectrum of a theory. In particular, this method is used to determine the energies of universal N-body clusters tied to Efimov trimers, for even N, by investigating the distribution of a correlation function of two particles at unitarity. Using numerical evidence that this distribution is log-normal, an analytical prediction for the N-dependence of the N-body binding energies is made.

## Key claim

The probability distribution of the two-body correlation function at unitarity is numerically consistent with a log-normal distribution, and this observation leads to an analytical prediction for the N-dependence of universal N-body binding energies tied to Efimov trimers, specifically for even N. The hedge is that the log-normal distribution is not exact: the lattice data deviates from log-normal by approximately 2%, and the prediction is not expected to hold for arbitrarily large N due to non-universal cutoff effects.

## Method

The author uses a lattice formulation of non-relativistic particles interacting via a Hubbard-Stratonovich auxiliary field, with open temporal boundary conditions and the two-particle coupling tuned to the unitary fixed point. The probability distribution of the two-body correlation function is sampled by calculating the observable on each field configuration at large Euclidean time, and the resulting histogram is compared to a log-normal distribution. The moments of the distribution are identified with 2N-body correlation functions, whose long-time behavior is governed by the ground-state energies of N-body bound states at unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The lattice data shows a deviation of approximately 2% from an exact log-normal distribution, as quantified by the cumulant expansion for the logarithm of the two-body correlation function.
- The energy cutoff imposed by the lattice spacing is a factor of approximately 335 times larger than the energy of the lowest Efimov trimer, providing a large separation of scales for universal behavior.
- The predicted energy ratio for even N is given by E_0^(2N)/E_0^(4) = (1/2) N(N-1).
- For the energies from vonStecher (2009), the discrepancy from log-normal (given by the cumulant expansion at third order) varies between approximately 17% and 30%.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal N-body clusters tied to Efimov trimers, previously investigated numerically by vonStecher (2009), Blume (2011), and Yan (2015), as well as analytically by Gattobigio (2013) and Kievsky (2014). The log-normal distribution provides a new analytical tool for predicting the N-dependence of binding energies, and the approach may enable an effective field theory expansion around the unitary fixed point, similar to the KSW expansion of Kaplan (1998), to calculate non-universal effects at large N.
