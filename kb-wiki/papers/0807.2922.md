# Electromagnetic form factors of the Delta in a S-wave approach

> Without any further adjusting of parameters, a relativistic constituent quark model, successful in the description of the data for the nucleon elastic form factors and of the dominant contribution for the nucleon to Delta electromagnetic transition, is used here to predict the dominant electromagnetic form factors of the Delta baryon. The model is based on a simple Delta wave function corresponding to a quark-diquark system in an S-state. The results for E0 and M1 are consistent both with experimental results and lattice calculations. The remaining form factors E2 and M3 vanish, given the symmetric structure taken for the Delta.

## Key claim

Without any additional parameter fitting, a relativistic constituent quark model previously calibrated to nucleon elastic form factors and the dominant NΔ transition predicts the Δ baryon's dominant electromagnetic form factors (E0 and M1) that are consistent with experimental results and lattice calculations, but only because the model restricts the Δ wave function to an S-wave state, causing the E2 and M3 form factors to vanish identically.

## Method

The authors employed a covariant spectator quark model where the Δ is described as a quark-diquark system in an S-wave state (zero orbital angular momentum). The scalar wave function for the quark-diquark momentum distribution was taken from a prior fit to NΔ transition data (model II of Ramalho and Pena), and the quark current was parametrized using vector meson dominance with parameters fixed by nucleon elastic form factor data. The electromagnetic current was evaluated in the relativistic impulse approximation, and the resulting form factors were computed from the current decomposition into multipole components.

## Key numbers

- For Δ⁺⁺, the model predicts G_M1(0) = 6.71 (in units where μ_Δ = (M_Δ/M) G_M1(0) in nuclear magnetons), compared to the experimental value 7.34 ± 2.49 from the Particle Data Group
- For Δ⁺, the model predicts G_M1(0) = 3.29, compared to the experimental value 3.54^{+4.59}_{-4.72} from Mainz (Kotulla 2002)
- For Δ⁰, the model predicts G_M1(0) = -0.12, and for Δ⁻, G_M1(0) = -3.54
- The model gives a charge radius r_E0 = 0.57 fm for Δ⁺, compared to the lattice dipole fit value of 0.691 ± 0.006 fm from Alexandrou (2007)
- For Δ⁺, the model estimates r_M1 = 0.61 fm, consistent with the lattice result r_M1 = 0.642 ± 0.038 fm from Alexandrou (2007)
- The D-state admixture correction to the charge form factor is less than 7% and to the magnetic dipole form factor is less than 14% for Q² < 1.0 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model of Ramalho and Pena (2008) from nucleon elastic form factors and NΔ transition form factors to the elastic Δ electromagnetic form factors, without any new parameter adjustment. The results are compared to quenched lattice QCD extrapolations from Alexandrou (2007) and Cloet (2003), and to various theoretical predictions including SU(6) quark models, relativistic quark models (Schlumpf 1993), QCD sum rules (Lee 1998, Aliev 2000), chiral quark-soliton models (Kim), and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Butler 1994). The model enables predictions for Δ form factors at finite Q² where experimental data are essentially absent, and provides a baseline for assessing the importance of pion cloud effects and D-state components in future unquenched lattice QCD studies.
