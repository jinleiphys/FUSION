# Meson retardation in deuteron photodisintegration above pi-threshold

> Photodisintegration of the deuteron above pi-threshold is studied in a coupled channel approach including N Delta- and pi d-channels with pion retardation in potentials and exchange currents. A much improved description of total and differential cross sections in the energy region between pi-threshold and 400-450 MeV is achieved.

# Meson retardation in deuteron photodisintegration above pi-threshold

## Key claim

Including full pion retardation (not just the leading-order p/M expansion) in both the hadronic potentials and the exchange currents within a coupled channel approach with NΔ and πd channels removes the 20-30% underestimation of the total cross section and the incorrect shape of differential cross sections that plagued earlier parameter-free coupled channel and three-body models, achieving agreement with experiment up to 400-450 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled channel model in a Hilbert space of NN, NΔ, and πd channels, using projection operator techniques to eliminate the Q-space (containing mesons). They employed energy-dependent retarded π-exchange potentials (based on the Bonn OBEPT model) for NN, NΔ, and ΔΔ interactions, and derived corresponding retarded two-body exchange currents including recoil contributions that vanish in static treatments. The γNΔ coupling was fixed by fitting the M1+(3/2) multipole of pion photoproduction on the nucleon using the model of Tanabe and Ohta (1985), and the πd channel was included via a separable ansatz for the driving term.

## Key numbers

- The static calculation (without πd-channel and ρπγ/ωπγ MECs) considerably underestimates the experimental total cross section, though the discrepancy is less dramatic than in Wilhelm et al. (1993) because the γNΔ coupling from Tanabe and Ohta (1985) is somewhat larger.
- Inclusion of the πd-channel enhances the total cross section by about 8% at 260 MeV.
- Inclusion of ρπγ/ωπγ MECs enhances the total cross section by about 3% at 260 MeV.
- The complete calculation agrees well with experimental data over the energy range from π-threshold to 400-450 MeV.
- All multipoles and scattering waves up to L=4 and j=4 were included; retardation in meson-, vertex-, contact-, and ρπγ/ωπγ currents was included only up to L=2.

## Context

This work extends the coupled channel approach of Wilhelm et al. (1993) and the unitary three-body model of Tanabe and Ohta (1989), both of which underestimated the total cross section by 20-30% and produced differential cross sections that were too flat with a pronounced dip around 90 degrees. The key improvement is the inclusion of full pion retardation rather than the static approximation, which recovers the cancellation between wave function renormalization and recoil current that is lost above π-threshold. The success enables future application of the same retarded framework to related electromagnetic processes on the deuteron such as π-photoproduction and electrodisintegration.
