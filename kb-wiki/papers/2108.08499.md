# Local correlation among the chiral condensate, monopoles, and color magnetic fields in Abelian projected QCD

> Using the lattice gauge field theory, we study the relation among the local chiral condensate, monopoles, and color magnetic fields in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). First, we investigate idealized Abelian gauge systems of 1) a static monopole-antimonopole pair and 2) a magnetic flux without monopoles, on a four-dimensional Euclidean lattice. In these systems, we calculate the local chiral condensate on quasi-massless fermions coupled to the Abelian gauge field, and find that the chiral condensate is localized in the vicinity of the magnetic field. Second, using SU(3) lattice QCD Monte Carlo calculations, we investigate Abelian projected QCD in the maximally Abelian gauge, and find clear correlation of distribution similarity among the local chiral condensate, monopoles, and color magnetic fields in the Abelianized gauge configuration. As a statistical indicator, we measure the correlation coefficient $r$, and find a strong positive correlation of $r \simeq 0.8$ between the local chiral condensate and an Euclidean color-magnetic quantity ${\cal F}$ in Abelian projected QCD. The correlation is also investigated for the deconfined phase in thermal QCD. As an interesting conjecture, like magnetic catalysis, the chiral condensate is locally enhanced by the strong color-magnetic field around the monopoles in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the local chiral condensate is primarily correlated with the color magnetic field (quantified by the Euclidean magnetic quantity ℱ) rather than with monopoles themselves, based on both idealized Abelian systems and SU(3) lattice QCD in the maximally Abelian gauge; the authors conjecture that the chiral condensate is locally enhanced by strong color-magnetic fields around monopoles, similar to magnetic catalysis.

## Method

The authors first constructed idealized U(1) lattice gauge systems with a static monopole-antimonopole pair and a static magnetic flux without monopoles, then computed the local chiral condensate using Kogut-Susskind fermions coupled to the Abelian gauge field. They then performed SU(3) lattice QCD Monte Carlo calculations in the maximally Abelian gauge, extracted Abelian link variables, and computed the local chiral condensate, monopole density, and the Euclidean color-magnetic quantity ℱ = (1/3)∑ᵢ (1/4)Fᵢ^{μν}Fᵢ^{μν} on the same lattice sites. The correlation between these quantities was quantified using the correlation coefficient r.

## Key numbers

- Correlation coefficient r ≃ 0.8 between the local chiral condensate and the Euclidean color-magnetic quantity ℱ in Abelian projected QCD in the maximally Abelian gauge.
- In the idealized static monopole-antimonopole system with distance l = 4 (lattice units), the local chiral condensate takes significant values in the vicinity of the magnetic field, with quark mass m = 0.01 in lattice units.
- In the idealized static magnetic flux system without monopoles (l = L_z), the local chiral condensate again takes significant values near the magnetic field, with quark mass m = 0.01 in lattice units.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(3) lattice QCD study (Ohata 2020) that found a clear correlation between monopoles and the chiral condensate in the maximally Abelian gauge, by now quantitatively investigating the role of color magnetic fields. The results support the dual superconductor picture proposed by Nambu (1974), 't Hooft (1975), and Mandelstam (1974), and are consistent with magnetic catalysis phenomena (Suganuma 1990, Suganuma 1991, Klevansky 1992, Gusynin 1995) where strong magnetic fields induce chiral symmetry breaking. The findings enable future studies of the microscopic mechanism linking color magnetic fields to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.
