# Strongly damped nuclear collisions: zero or first sound ?

> The relaxation of the collective quadrupole motion in the initial stage of a central heavy ion collision at beam energies $E_{lab}=5\div20$ AMeV is studied within a microscopic kinetic transport model. The damping rate is shown to be a non-monotonic function of E_{lab} for a given pair of colliding nuclei. This fact is interpreted as a manifestation of the zero-to-first sound transition in a finite nuclear system.

## Key claim

The damping rate of collective quadrupole motion in central heavy ion collisions is a non-monotonic function of beam energy, with a maximum at approximately 10 AMeV, which the authors interpret as evidence for a zero-to-first sound transition in a finite nuclear system, though the transition is more smeared-out than predicted by analytical models.

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport model to simulate central collisions of ⁶⁴Ni + ²³⁸U at beam energies from 6.53 to 20.53 AMeV. They extracted the reduced friction coefficient β from the time evolution of the quadrupole moment Q_zz of the dinuclear system (DNS) by fitting the oscillatory part to a damped sinusoidal function. The temperature of the DNS was determined from an energy balance equation using the potential energy drop during the initial contact phase, with level density parameter a = A/8 MeV⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- The reduced friction coefficient β reaches a maximum at beam energy E_lab ≈ 10 AMeV, corresponding to a DNS temperature T ≈ 4.6 MeV
- For the ⁶⁴Ni + ²³⁸U collision at 6.53 AMeV, the extracted temperature is T = 3.1 MeV
- The oscillation period of the quadrupole moment is t_osc ≈ 100 fm/c, giving an estimated β ≈ 70 × 10²¹ s⁻¹
- The two-body relaxation time parameter κ ≈ 1900 MeV² fm/c for an isotropic isospin-averaged nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_NN = 40 mb
- The giant quadrupole resonance frequency used in analytical calculations is ħω_R = 64.7 A⁻¹/³ MeV
- The Landau parameters used are F_0 = 0.2, F_1 = 0
- The one-body relaxation time uses a numerical factor ξ = 1.85 for the isoscalar quadrupole mode

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of zero-to-first sound transitions in Fermi liquids, previously studied in liquid ³He by Khalatnikov and Abrikosov (1960s) and Abe (1966), to finite nuclear systems. It builds on earlier analytical calculations by Kolomietz et al. (1996) who predicted a transition temperature of 4-5 MeV for isoscalar giant resonances, and contrasts with experimental analyses by Wilczyński et al. (1996) and Donangelo et al. (1999) who extracted reduced friction coefficients from prescission neutron multiplicities in fast fission reactions, finding very large β values (10-100 × 10²¹ s⁻¹) at temperatures T = 2-3 MeV. The present work enables future experimental searches for enhanced quadrupole vibrations at late times (t > 200 fm/c) for beam energies above 10 AMeV, which would accelerate fast fission and should be observable as increased fast-fission cross sections.
