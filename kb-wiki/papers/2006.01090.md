# Fluctuations of topological charge and chiral density in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions

> We study systematically the topological charge density and the chiral density correlations in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions: the intial condition is given by the McLerran-Venugopalan model and the evolution of the gluon fields is studied via the Classical Yang-Mills equations up to proper time $τ\approx 1$ fm/c for an $SU(2)$ evolving Glasma. Topological charge is related to the gauge invariant $\bm E \cdot \bm B$ where $\bm E$ and $\bm B$ denote the color-electric and color-magnetic fields, while the chiral density is produced via the chiral anomaly of Quantum Chromodynamics. We study how the correlation lengths are related to the collision energy, and how the correlated domains grow up with proper time in the transverse plane for a boost invariant longitudinal expansion. We estimate the correlation lengths of both quantities, that after a short transient results of the order of the typical energy scale of the model, namely the inverse of the saturation scale. We estimate the proper time for the formation of a steady state in which the production of the chiral density in the transverse plane per unit rapidity slows down, as well as the amount of chiral density that would be present at the switch time between the Classical Yang-Mills evolution and the relativistic transport or hydro for the quark-gluon plasma phase.

# Fluctuations of topological charge and chiral density in the early stage of high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The correlation lengths of topological charge density and chiral density in the evolving Glasma are of order the inverse of the saturation scale, but this result holds only for an SU(2) gauge group with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and neglects longitudinal fluctuations that would break boost invariance.

## Method

The authors initialize gluon fields using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model with random color charge densities, then solve the Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) equations numerically on a 4 fm x 4 fm transverse lattice with spacing 0.04 fm, using fourth-order Runge-Kutta integration up to proper time tau approximately 1 fm/c. They compute the topological charge density from the gauge-invariant quantity Tr(E dot B) and obtain the chiral density per unit rapidity by integrating the chiral anomaly equation over proper time. Ensemble averages are performed over 200 events with different random initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Correlation length of topological charge density lambda_T lies in the range (0.1 fm, 0.2 fm) for g^2 mu between 1.0 GeV and 3.4 GeV
- Dimensionless correlation length g^2 mu lambda_T lies within about 0.5 to 2.5 over the range of g^2 mu analyzed
- At g^2 mu = 1.0 GeV, sqrt(⟨N_5^2⟩/V^2) = 1.79 x 10^{-1} fm^{-3} in the steady state
- At g^2 mu = 1.7 GeV, sqrt(⟨N_5^2⟩/V^2) = 8.85 x 10^{-1} fm^{-3} in the steady state
- At g^2 mu = 3.4 GeV, sqrt(⟨N_5^2⟩/V^2) = 6.48 fm^{-3} in the steady state
- Steady state formation time tau_s is approximately 1/g^2 mu, corresponding to (0.1 fm/c, 0.2 fm/c) for the range studied
- Lattice spacing a = 0.04 fm, giving g^2 mu a = 0.2 for the reference case g^2 mu = 1 GeV
- Transverse box size is 4 fm x 4 fm
- The ratio Q_s/(g^2 mu) is approximately 0.57, following Lappi (2008)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of gauge-invariant correlators in the evolving Glasma by Dumitru (2014) and others, and of topological charge domains in the initial condition by Kharzeev (2002). It provides the first systematic calculation of how correlation lengths of topological charge density and chiral density depend on the collision energy scale g^2 mu, using the full CYM evolution rather than only the initial Glasma configuration. The results enable future studies of anomalous particle production via the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions, and can inform the initial conditions for relativistic hydrodynamics or transport simulations at the switch time from CYM evolution to the quark-gluon plasma phase.
