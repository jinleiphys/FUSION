# Holographic Collisions in Non-conformal Theories

> We numerically simulate gravitational shock wave collisions in a holographic model dual to a non-conformal four-dimensional gauge theory. We find two novel effects associated to the non-zero bulk viscosity of the resulting plasma. First, the hydrodynamization time increases. Second, if the bulk viscosity is large enough then the plasma becomes well described by hydrodynamics before the energy density and the average pressure begin to obey the equilibrium equation of state. We discuss implications for the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in non-conformal holographic plasmas, hydrodynamization (the onset of hydrodynamic description) can occur before "EoSization" (the time when the average pressure obeys the equilibrium equation of state), provided the bulk viscosity is sufficiently large. This ordering is the non-conformal analogue of the earlier finding that hydrodynamization can precede isotropization in conformal theories.

## Method

The authors numerically simulate gravitational shock wave collisions in a five-dimensional Einstein-scalar model with a non-trivial potential that drives a renormalization group flow between two AdS fixed points. They extract the boundary stress tensor at mid-rapidity as a function of time for collisions of "1/2-shocks" and "1/4-shocks" (defined by the dimensionless thickness parameter μω = 0.30 and 0.12, respectively) at several values of the initial energy density in units of the conformal-breaking scale Λ. Hydrodynamization time is defined by a 10% agreement between the longitudinal and transverse pressures and their first-order viscous hydrodynamic predictions; EoSization time is defined by a 10% agreement between the average pressure and the equilibrium pressure from the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The maximum hydrodynamization time for 1/2-shock collisions is 2.5 times larger than the conformal result (the conformal value is thy * Thy = 0.56).
- For a collision of 1/4-shocks with μ/Λ = 0.94 and hydrodynamization temperature Thy/Λ = 0.24, hydrodynamization occurs at thy * Λ = 4.2 and EoSization at teq * Λ = 9.6, giving teq/thy = 2.4.
- The maximum ratio teq/thy for 1/2-collisions is 2.56.
- The bulk viscosity-to-entropy ratio at the temperatures where hydrodynamization precedes EoSization is ζ/s = 0.025 (at Thy/Λ = 0.19) and ζ/s = 0.017 (at Thy/Λ = 0.38).
- The bulk viscosity attains its maximum at T = 0.22 Λ, with ζ/η = 4π ζ/s ≈ 0.35 at that temperature.
- At Thy/Λ = 4.8, the hydrodynamization time differs from the conformal value by only 0.5%.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of far-from-equilibrium dynamics, which were all performed in conformal field theories (Chesler 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2013), to non-conformal theories with a non-zero bulk viscosity. It builds on near-equilibrium studies of non-conformal plasmas (Buchel 2015, Janik 2015, Gursoy 2015, Attems 2016) and on far-from-equilibrium studies of homogeneous non-conformal plasmas without hydrodynamic modes (Craps 2015, Ali-Akbari 2016, Gursoy 2016). The results suggest that comparing the early-time dynamics of heavy ion collisions at different energies and system sizes (p-Pb, d-Au, He-Au, p-p) could experimentally probe the two different relaxation paths (hydrodynamization before or after EoSization) depending on the value of the bulk viscosity near the critical temperature.
