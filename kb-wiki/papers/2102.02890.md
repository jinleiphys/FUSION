# On the interpretation of $Λ$ spin polarization measurements

> The physics interpretation of the recent measurements of the spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is discussed. We suggest that the polarization measured in the $Λ$ rest frame should be projected along the direction of the total angular momentum that is first transformed to the same frame, and only then averaged over $Λ$'s with different momenta in the center-of-mass frame. While this procedure does not affect the current measurements done in a broad transverse-momentum range, it may become important (represent a correction of about 10\%) for the most energetic hyperons under study (with transverse momenta reaching 4-5 GeV/c). The proposed treatment is generally more appropriate for relativistic $Λ$'s. Throughout the paper, we deliver explicit expressions for various boosts, rotations, and transformations of angular distributions, which may help to compare model predictions with the experimental results.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the standard experimental procedure for measuring $\Lambda$ spin polarization projects the polarization vector onto the $y$-axis of the $\Lambda$ rest frame, but this $y$ direction differs from the $y$ direction in the center-of-mass frame (COM) due to Lorentz boosts. They suggest that the polarization should instead be projected along the direction of the total angular momentum vector after it has been transformed into the $\Lambda$ rest frame, and only then averaged over $\Lambda$'s with different momenta in COM; this correction may reach about 10% for the most energetic hyperons with transverse momenta of 4-5 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors define the COM frame for heavy-ion and proton-proton collisions and introduce the canonical boost from COM to the $\Lambda$ rest frame $S'(\mathbf{p}_\Lambda)$. They derive the transformation law for the total angular momentum vector $\mathbf{L}$ from COM to the $\Lambda$ rest frame, obtaining $\mathbf{L}' = \gamma_\Lambda \mathbf{L} - [\gamma_\Lambda^2/(\gamma_\Lambda+1)] \, \mathbf{v}_\Lambda (\mathbf{v}_\Lambda \cdot \mathbf{L})$, and compute the unit vector $\hat{\mathbf{L}}'$ in terms of the $\Lambda$ three-momentum. They then present explicit expressions for the weak decay angular distribution of protons from $\Lambda \to p + \pi^-$ and show how the polarization vector components are extracted from averaged proton momentum components in the $\Lambda$ rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The correction to the polarization projection becomes important (about 10%) for hyperons with transverse momenta reaching 4-5 GeV/c.
- The $\Lambda$ decay constant used in the weak decay law is $\alpha_\Lambda = 0.732$.
- The Lande $g$-factor for $\Lambda$ hyperons is $g_\Lambda = 0.613$.
- The mean $\Lambda$ lifetime in its rest frame is $2.63 \times 10^{-10}$ s.
- The spin precession angle due to a magnetic field is $\Delta \Phi_{\text{prec}} = 0.0077 \, x \, \gamma_\Lambda$, where $x$ is the magnetic field in Tesla in COM; for $x=0.5$ and $\gamma_\Lambda \sim 2$, this gives 0.022.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for interpreting $\Lambda$ spin polarization measurements in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, following the experimental observations by STAR (2017, 2018) and ALICE (2019) that confirmed non-zero global polarization. The authors address a subtlety neglected in previous experimental analyses, which assumed the $y$ direction in the $\Lambda$ rest frame is the same as in COM; they note that an alternative method using laboratory-frame quantities was proposed by Siddique (2017) but does not discuss the boost effects on the angular momentum direction. The proposed procedure enables more accurate comparisons between model predictions and experimental results for relativistic $\Lambda$'s, particularly for high-transverse-momentum hyperons.
