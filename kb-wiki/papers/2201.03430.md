# Steady asymptotic equilibria in conformal relativistic fluids

> When one considers a shock wave in the frame where the shock is at rest, on either side one has a steady flow which converges to equilibrium away from the shock. However, hydrodynamics is unable to describe this flow if the asymptotic velocity is higher than the characteristic speed of the theory. We obtain an exact solution for the decay rate to equilibrium for a conformal fluid in kinetic theory under the relaxation time approximation, and compare it to two hydrodynamic schemes, one accounting for the second moments of the distribution function and thus equivalent, in the small deviations from equilibrium limit, to an Israel-Stewart framework, and another accounting for both second and third moments. While still having a finite characteristic speed, the second model is a significant improvement on the first.

## Key claim

Kinetic theory under the relaxation time approximation yields a smooth approach to equilibrium for any asymptotic velocity below the speed of light, whereas any finite-order hydrodynamic scheme has a finite characteristic speed that sets an upper bound on the asymptotic velocity for which a regular solution exists; the paper demonstrates that including third moments of the distribution function significantly raises this bound compared to the Israel-Stewart (second-moment) scheme, though both still break down at a finite velocity.

## Method

The author solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation with the Anderson-Witting (relaxation time) collision term for a steady, one-dimensional flow converging to equilibrium far from a shock, obtaining an exact parametric expression for the spatial decay rate. He then derives the decay rate in two divergence-type theories (DTTs): one matching the second moments of the distribution function (equivalent to Israel-Stewart in the linear regime) and one matching both second and third moments. The decay rates are compared as functions of the asymptotic fluid velocity on the supersonic side of the shock.

## Key numbers

- For the Israel-Stewart model with the Grad constitutive relation (eta_0 = 4 tau_0 / 5), the theory breaks down when v_L^2 >= 3/5 (v_L approximately 0.775).
- For the DTT including third moments, the theory breaks down when v_L^2 is approximately 0.74 (v_L approximately 0.86).
- The exact kinetic-theory decay rate diverges as gamma = (1 - v_L^2)^(-1/2) when v_L approaches 1, a stronger divergence than the gamma^(1/2) behavior from holography (KKM 2010).
- For the Landau-Lifshitz theory, the decay rate is lambda_LL = 3 T_L (1 - v_L^2)^(1/2) (v_L^2 - 1/3) / (eta_0 v_L).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of shock structure in relativistic fluids from ideal and Landau-Lifshitz hydrodynamics (KKM 2010) and Israel-Stewart theory (KKM 2010) to a hierarchy of divergence-type theories. It provides a concrete realization of the scenario discussed by Olson and Hiscock (1990) and by Jaiswal and Pal (1991), where the finite characteristic speed of any hydrodynamic theory limits its ability to describe strong shocks. The results enable quantitative comparison between kinetic theory and hydrodynamic truncations, and support the use of the third-moment DTT (which also reproduces the energy-momentum propagators from kinetic theory as shown by Perna and Calzetta 2021) as a more accurate framework for applications such as heavy-ion collisions or early-universe cosmology.
