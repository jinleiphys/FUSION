# Remarks on the extraction of freeze-out parameters

> I review the extraction of kinetic and chemical freeze-out parameters from experimental data, with particular emphasis on the underlying assumptions and the validity of the conclusions.

## Key claim
Exponential transverse momentum spectra alone are not sufficient evidence for thermal equilibrium; the exponential shape can arise purely from multiparticle phase space in a 2→N process, and the slope parameter extracted from such spectra cannot distinguish between a thermal system and one that is not in thermal equilibrium.

## Method
The paper derives the single-inclusive invariant cross section for a 2→N process, showing that the momentum-space volume factor yields an exponential in transverse momentum for large N, independent of any thermal assumption. It then compares this to the Cooper-Frye formula for a thermally equilibrated system, noting the slope difference arises from the factor 1/E in invariant momentum space versus thermodynamic momentum space. For kinetic freeze-out, the paper reviews how slope parameters that increase linearly with particle mass in AA collisions indicate collective flow, and how two-parameter fits (T_f.o. and ⟨v_T⟩) are correlated. For chemical freeze-out, it reviews χ² fits of particle ratios to extract T_ch. and μ_B, ch. under assumptions of common freeze-out hypersurface, kinetic equilibrium, and chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- For massless particles in a 2→N process, the inverse slope parameter is T_slope = E/2N, while for a thermal system it is T = E/3N; the ratio is 3/2.
- For PbPb collisions at E_Lab = 158 A GeV, extracted kinetic freeze-out temperatures range from 100 MeV to 140 MeV, and average transverse flow velocities ⟨v_T⟩ range from 0.43 to 0.60, depending on the analysis.
- At CERN-SPS energies and above, chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch. ∼ 160 MeV, which is higher than kinetic freeze-out temperatures.
- All (T, μ_B) combinations from particle ratio fits fall on a line of constant energy per particle ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ ≃ 1 GeV.
- In a chiral model analysis for PbPb at SPS energies, using in-medium masses gives T_ch. ≃ 144 MeV, approximately equal to the kinetic freeze-out temperature.

## Context
This review extends the work of Hagedorn (1965) on phase-space dominance in multiparticle production and the work of Cleymans and Redlich (1999) on chemical freeze-out systematics. It contrasts the interpretation of exponential spectra as thermal (Cooper and Frye, 1974) with the purely phase-space explanation. The paper enables critical assessment of freeze-out parameter extraction methods and highlights the need for more pp and pA data at comparable energies to distinguish between thermodynamic equilibrium and "born into equilibrium" scenarios (Stock, 1986). It also points to the work of Zschiesche et al. on in-medium mass modifications as a way to reconcile chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures.
