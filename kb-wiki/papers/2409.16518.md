# Disoriented isospin condensates in heavy-ion collisions

> Anomalous neutral to charged kaon correlations measured by the ALICE collaboration have defied usual explanations. We propose that the large fluctuations could arise because of a disoriented isospin condensate where there is an imbalance between up and down condensates at the time kaons hadronize. This could happen in heavy-ion collisions when the quark condensate re-forms as the system cools and the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD is broken. Within the linear sigma model, we show that the energy cost of forming a disoriented isospin condensate is small making it very plausible.

# Disoriented isospin condensates in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The anomalous neutral-to-charged kaon correlations measured by ALICE in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV could arise from disoriented isospin condensates (DIC), where the up and down quark condensates are imbalanced at the time kaons hadronize. Within the linear sigma model, the energy cost of forming such condensates is small enough to make them plausible, but only for temperatures near the hadronization temperature; at low temperatures the cost becomes prohibitively large.

## Method

The authors model the quark condensate fluctuations using a 2+1 flavor linear sigma model, relating the scalar fields to the condensates as σ_u = -⟨ūu⟩/√2c' and σ_d = -⟨dd̄⟩/√2c', with an angle θ ranging from 0 to π/2 that quantifies the isospin imbalance. They compute the energy cost ΔU(θ,T) for deviations from the equilibrium value θ = π/4 using lattice QCD temperature-dependent condensates, then calculate the relative probability P(θ) = exp(-ΔU(θ,T)V/T) for a domain volume V = 100 fm³. The resulting neutral kaon fraction distributions are folded with thermal kaons and multiple domains to fit the ALICE ν_dyn data using a two-parameter model.

## Key numbers

- ALICE measured ν_dyn(K±,K_S) in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV; the anomaly extended over more than one unit in rapidity
- The scaling factor α ≈ 6/N_K^tot, where N_K^tot is the total number of kaons proportional to total multiplicity
- For a model with N_d domains, ν_dyn = 4β_K(β_K/(3N_d) - 1/N_K^tot), where β_K is the fraction of kaons from domains
- The relative probability calculation used a domain volume V = 100 fm³
- Only the five most central data points were fit, as the number of domains falls below two for the last two data points

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kapusta (2022) and proposes an explanation for the ALICE anomaly (ALICE collaboration, 2021) that conventional heavy-ion simulators like AMPT, HIJING, and EPOS-LHC (Nayak, 2019) could not reproduce. The DIC mechanism builds on the linear sigma model framework of Schaffner-Bielich (1998) and uses lattice QCD temperature-dependent condensates from Bazavov (2011). Recent work by Chabowski (2024) showed that DICs equilibrate on time scales much shorter than those relevant for heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that a full integration of DIC with hydrodynamic models could constrain the physics of chiral symmetry restoration and be informed by lattice QCD calculations.
