# Inconsistency of QED in the Presence of Dirac Monopoles

> A precise formulation of $U(1)$ local gauge invariance in QED is presented, which clearly shows that the gauge coupling associated with the unphysical longitudinal photon field is non-observable and actually has an arbitrary value. We then re-examine the Dirac quantization condition and find that its derivation involves solely the unphysical longitudinal coupling. Hence an inconsistency inevitably arises in the presence of Dirac monopoles and this can be considered as a theoretical evidence against their existence. An alternative, independent proof of this conclusion is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Dirac quantization condition (DQC) inevitably involves only the unphysical gauge coupling associated with the longitudinal photon field, not the physical transverse coupling, so that enforcing any nonzero magnetic charge forces a non-observable parameter to become physically meaningful, an inconsistency that constitutes theoretical evidence against the existence of Dirac monopoles.

## Method

The authors first formulate QED by orthogonally decomposing the photon field into a transverse physical part and a longitudinal pure-gauge part, assigning them independent gauge couplings \(e\) and \(\tilde{e}\). They then re-derive the Dirac quantization condition using three standard approaches (single-valuedness of wavefunctions, the Aharonov-Bohm phase argument, and angular-momentum quantization) and show that in each derivation only the unphysical coupling \(\tilde{e}\) appears. An alternative proof enlarges the gauge symmetry to \(U(1)_{em} \times U(1)_{\Theta}\) by introducing an additional pure gauge field \(\Theta\) with its own arbitrary coupling \(\tilde{e}\), and derives two independent DQCs whose only consistent simultaneous solution is zero magnetic charge.

## Key numbers

* The Dirac quantization condition derived is \(\tilde{e} g = n/2\) for integer \(n\), where \(\tilde{e}\) is the unphysical gauge coupling and \(g\) is the magnetic charge.
* The angular-momentum approach yields \(J_z = -i\partial/\partial\varphi - \tilde{e}g\), requiring half-integer eigenvalues and reproducing the same condition \(\tilde{e} g = n/2\).
* The renormalization analysis shows the unphysical coupling \(\tilde{e}\) has a vanishing \(\beta\)-function and is renormalization-scale-independent, while the physical coupling \(e_R\) runs; at loop level \(e_R \neq \tilde{e}_R\) even if set equal at tree level.
* The unique physically consistent solution to the DQC is \(g = 0\) (corresponding to \(n = 0\)).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing critique of Dirac monopoles (Dirac 1931) by showing that the standard DQC derivation conflates physical and unphysical gauge couplings, a subtlety overlooked in prior treatments. It contrasts with 't Hooft-Polyakov monopoles (1974), which arise as finite-energy solitons in non-Abelian theories and have no string singularities, so their DQC involves only the physical transverse coupling. The paper enables a re-examination of whether any consistent quantum field theory of point monopoles can be constructed, and it suggests that experimental non-observation of monopoles may have a deeper theoretical basis than previously recognized.
