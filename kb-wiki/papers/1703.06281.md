# $β$-decay properties of waiting point nuclei for astrophysical applications

> We report microscopic calculation of key $β$-decay properties for some of the crucial waiting point species having neutron closed magic shells 50 and 82. Our calculation bear astrophysical significance vis-á-vis speeding of the $r$-process. The $β$-decay properties include half-lives, energy rates of $β$-delayed neutrons and their emission probabilities, both under terrestrial and stellar conditions. We perform a pn-QRPA calculation with a separable multi-shell interaction and include both allowed and unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions in our calculation. We compare our results with previous calculations and measured data. Our calculation is in good agreement with the experimental data. For certain cases, we noted a significant decrease in the half-life calculation with the contribution of U1F transitions. This is in contradiction to the shell model study where only for $N$ = 126 waiting-point nuclei, the forbidden transitions were reported to significantly reduce the calculated half-lives. Our model fulfills the Ikeda sum rule for even-even cases. For odd-A cases the rule is violated by up to 15$\%$ for $^{125}$Tc.

# β-decay properties of waiting point nuclei for astrophysical applications

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA model with separable multi-shell interaction, including both allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions, produces β-decay half-lives for N=50 and N=82 r-process waiting point nuclei that agree well with experimental data, but contrary to shell model studies (which found significant forbidden-transition effects only for N=126), the authors report that U1F contributions substantially reduce calculated half-lives for N=82 cases and some N=50 cases as well.

## Method
The authors performed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) calculations using a separable multi-shell interaction in a large model space (up to 7 major shells), including both allowed Gamow-Teller and unique first-forbidden transitions. They computed terrestrial β-decay half-lives, stellar β-decay and positron capture rates, energy rates of β-delayed neutrons, and neutron emission probabilities for ten waiting point nuclei (six with N=50 and four with N=82), quenching calculated strengths by a factor of (0.55)². The interaction strength parameters were fitted to reproduce measured half-lives within 1σ deviation, yielding mass-dependent formulas: χ_GT = 64.6/A MeV, χ_U1F = 64.6/A MeV fm⁻², κ_GT = 5.6/A MeV, κ_U1F = 11.7/A MeV fm⁻².

## Key numbers
- For ⁷⁸Ni: calculated half-life with GT+U1F = 0.152 s, compared to experimental value 0.140 s; inclusion of U1F reduced half-life from 0.210 s (GT only)
- For ¹²⁸Pd: calculated half-life with GT+U1F = 0.025 s, compared to experimental value 0.020 s; U1F contribution caused more than 95% reduction from GT-only value of 2.431 s
- For ¹²⁶Ru: calculated half-life with GT+U1F = 0.019 s, compared to experimental value 0.017 s; U1F contribution caused more than 95% reduction from GT-only value of 0.709 s
- For ¹²⁵Tc: Ikeda sum rule violated by up to 15% (calculated sum 99.2 vs theoretical 117)
- For ¹²⁷Rh: Ikeda sum rule compliance 88% (calculated sum 97.9 vs theoretical 111)
- For even-even cases (⁷⁶Fe, ⁷⁸Ni, ⁸⁰Zn, ¹²⁶Ru, ¹²⁸Pd): Ikeda sum rule fulfilled exactly (e.g., ⁷⁶Fe: calculated 72.0 vs theoretical 72)
- For ⁸¹Ga at T₉ = 30: positron capture rates become an order of magnitude bigger than β-decay rates
- Phase space for U1F transitions at low stellar temperatures (T₉ = 0.01) is up to an order of magnitude larger than allowed phase space for most N=50 and N=82 nuclei

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA approach previously applied by Klapdor-Kleingrothaus et al. (1984), Staudt et al. (1989, 1990), and Hirsh et al. (1993) to predict β-decay half-lives for exotic nuclei, and follows the inclusion of first-forbidden contributions first implemented in pn-QRPA by Homma et al. (1996). The results contradict the large-scale shell model study by Zhi et al. (2013), which reported that forbidden transitions significantly reduce half-lives only for N=126 waiting point nuclei, whereas the present work finds substantial U1F effects for N=82 and some N=50 cases. The calculated stellar weak rates and β-delayed neutron probabilities are intended for use in r-process nucleosynthesis simulations, particularly for understanding matter flow at the N=50 and N=82 waiting points that produce the observed r-process abundance peaks.
