# Linear Boltzmann transport for jet propagation in the quark-gluon plasma: Inelastic processes and jet modification

> A Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) Monte Carlo model has been developed to describe jet propagation and interaction with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A complete set of elastic scattering processes and medium-induced gluon emissions based on the higher-twist formalism are incorporated for both jet shower and medium recoil partons. It has been employed to describe experimental data on large transverse momentum hadron and jet spectra, correlation and jet substructures in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We document in detail the structure of the model and validation of the Monte Carlo implementations of the physics processes in LBT, in particular, the inelastic process of medium-induced gluon radiation. We carry out a comprehensive examination of the jet-medium interaction as implemented in LBT through energy loss and momentum broadening of a single hard parton, the energy and transverse momentum transfer from leading partons to medium-induced gluons and jet-induced medium excitation, and medium modification of reconstructed jets in a static and uniform medium. With realistic and event-by-event hydrodynamic medium in heavy-ion collisions, we compute and compare to experimental data on the jet cone-size dependence of the single inclusive jet suppression at both Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), the dijet asymmetry at LHC and $γ$-jet correlation at RHIC. Effects of medium-induced gluon emissions and jet-induced medium excitation on jet observables are systematically examined. Rescatterings of the radiated gluons and recoil partons with the QGP are found essential to account for the enhancement of soft particle yield toward the edge of the jet cone.

## Key claim

The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model provides a satisfactory description of jet modification in the quark-gluon plasma when both elastic scatterings and medium-induced gluon emissions (based on the higher-twist formalism) are incorporated, but the rescattering of radiated gluons and recoil partons with the QGP is found essential to account for the enhancement of soft particle yield toward the edge of the jet cone.

## Method

The LBT Monte Carlo model simulates jet propagation through a QGP medium by solving the Boltzmann equation with collision integrals for both 2→2 elastic scatterings and 2→2+n inelastic processes. Elastic scattering rates are computed from leading-order pQCD matrix elements with Debye screening regulation, while inelastic rates are derived from the higher-twist formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation including LPM interference effects. The model treats jet shower partons, radiated gluons, recoil partons (scattered out of the medium), and "negative" partons (back-reaction holes) on equal footing, with all partons continuing to propagate and interact until the local temperature falls below the QCD phase transition temperature.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening mass is given by μ_D² = (N_c + N_f/2)g²T²/3, with N_c=3 and N_f=3
- For heavy quarks, bare masses are m_c=1.27 GeV for charm and m_b=4.19 GeV for beauty quarks
- The jet transport coefficient for a gluon is a factor C_A/C_F=9/4 larger than for a quark
- The inelastic scattering rate for large (t-t_i)E is Γ^a_inel = (t-t_i)α_s C_A q̂_a/(2μ_D)[1 + (μ_D/E)ln(μ_D/E) + O(μ_D/E)]
- For (t-t_i)E < 4, the integrated inelastic rate is Γ^a_inel ≈ (t-t_i)² α_s C_A q̂_a/(4π) ln(E/μ_D)
- The jet transport coefficient in small angle approximation is q̂_a = C_2(a)(42ζ(3)/π)α_s²T³ ln(c_aET/μ_D²), where ζ(3)≈1.202, C_2(q)=4/3, C_2(g)=3, c_q=5.8 and c_g=5.6
- The strong coupling constant α_s is set to 0.3 in the brick test calculations

## Context

This work extends the LBT model previously developed by He et al. (2015) and Cao et al. (2017) by providing detailed documentation of the Monte Carlo implementations, particularly for inelastic processes. It complements other jet quenching models such as the virtuality-ordered medium-modified splittings (Armesto et al., 2009; Zapp et al., 2011; Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2014; Cao et al., 2017) and the JETSCAPE framework (2017, 2022). The results enable systematic comparison with experimental data on jet suppression at both RHIC and LHC, and provide a foundation for extracting transport properties of the QGP with precision.
