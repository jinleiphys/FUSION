# Generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon studied in the linear sigma model (II)

> In a previous paper virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon has been investigated in the one-loop approximation of the linear sigma model in order to determine the 3 scalar generalized polarizabilities. We have now extended this work and calculated the 7 vector polarizabilities showing up in the spin-dependent amplitude of virtual Compton scattering. The results fulfill 3 model-independent constraints recently derived. Compared to the constituent quark model there exist enormous differences for some of the vector polarizabilities. At vanishing three-momentum of the virtual photon, the analytical results of the sigma model and of chiral perturbation theory can be related. The influence of the $π^{0}$ exchange in the $t$ channel has been discussed in some detail. Besides, the vector polarizabilities determine 2 linear combinations of the third order spin-polarizabilities appearing in real Compton scattering.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model (LSM) calculation of the 7 vector generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon, performed in the one-loop approximation with infinite sigma mass, fulfills three model-independent constraints derived from charge conjugation and nucleon crossing; however, the results show enormous differences from the constituent quark model (CQM) for some polarizabilities, with the LSM predicting substantially larger values for 5 of the 7, indicating that nonresonant pion-nucleon background dominates over resonance contributions for most vector polarizabilities.

## Method

The authors extended their previous LSM calculation of the 3 scalar generalized polarizabilities to compute the 7 vector polarizabilities that appear in the spin-dependent amplitude of virtual Compton scattering (VCS). They evaluated the one-loop diagrams of the LSM in the limit of infinite sigma mass, including the contribution of pi0 exchange in the t-channel (anomaly diagrams), and performed a low-energy expansion of the residual (non-Born) VCS amplitude in the final photon energy omega-prime. The results were expanded in powers of the pion mass and compared with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and the constituent quark model.

## Key numbers

- At Q0^2 = 0.33 GeV^2, the ratio of scalar to vector polarizabilities for the proton is P_p^{(01,01)0} / P_p^{(01,01)1} ≈ -9.4 and P_p^{(11,11)0} / P_p^{(11,11)1} ≈ -7.8.
- For the polarizability P^{(11,00)1}, the LSM result is larger than the CQM prediction by three orders of magnitude.
- The leading term of the chiral expansion for the non-vanishing vector polarizabilities at Q0^2 = 0 is proportional to m_pi^{-2}, while scalar polarizabilities diverge like m_pi^{-1} in the chiral limit.
- The anomaly contribution to the polarizabilities at Q0^2 = 0 is given by I(0) = g_{pi N}^2 / (6 pi^2 m_pi^2).
- The first derivatives of the polarizabilities P^{(01,01)1} and P^{(11,11)1} at Q0^2 = 0 diverge like m_pi^{-1} in the chiral limit, while the derivatives of P^{(11,02)1}, P^{(01,12)1}, and hat{P}^{(01,1)1} diverge like m_pi^{-3}.

## Context

This work completes the authors' earlier LSM calculation of scalar polarizabilities (Metz 1996) and extends the theoretical framework for VCS established by Guichon et al. (1995), who defined the 10 generalized polarizabilities. The results are compared with the CQM calculation of Liu et al. (1996) and with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Hemmert et al. (1997) and Bernard et al. (1995). The LSM results agree with ChPT at leading order in the chiral expansion for all vector polarizabilities except hat{P}^{(11,2)1}, which vanishes in ChPT at order O(p^3). The work enables experimental programs at MAMI (Q^2 = 0.33 GeV^2), MIT-Bates (lower Q^2), and Jefferson Lab (higher Q^2) to test these predictions through double polarization observables such as p(e,e'p)gamma.
