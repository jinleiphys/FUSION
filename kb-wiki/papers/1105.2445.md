# Region of hadron-quark mixed phase in hybrid stars

> Hadron--quark mixed phase is expected in a wide region of the inner structure of hybrid stars. However, we show that the hadron--quark mixed phase should be restricted to a narrower region to because of the charge screening effect. The narrow region of the mixed phase seems to explain physical phenomena of neutron stars such as the strong magnetic field and glitch phenomena, and it would give a new cooling curve for the neutron star.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark mixed phase in hybrid stars, which is often expected to occupy a wide region of the inner core, should be restricted to a narrower region when the charge screening effect is fully accounted for without linear approximation, and this narrow region may help explain neutron star phenomena such as strong magnetic fields and glitches.

## Method

The author applied an equation of state (EOS) for the mixed phase previously derived using density functional theory under the local density approximation, fully solving the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential without linear approximation. The geometrically structured mixed phase (SMP) was modeled with Wigner-Seitz cells containing either droplets, rods, slabs, tubes, or bubbles of one phase embedded in the other, with a sharp boundary described by the bag model with a surface tension parameter of 40 MeV/fm². The EOS was then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation to compute hybrid star structures, comparing cases with and without charge screening.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses: 2.51 M_sun for pure hadron matter, 1.73 M_sun for the screened mixed phase, and 1.68 M_sun for the not-screened mixed phase.
- The difference in maximum mass between the screening and no-screening cases is about 0.05 M_sun.
- Without screening, the mixed phase region extends over a radius of about 6 km in a star with radius 12.6 km.
- With screening, the mixed phase region is reduced to only about 3 km in a star with radius 12.9 km.
- The bag constant used is B = 120 MeV/fm³; the surface tension used is 40 MeV/fm², with the mixed phase disfavored at about 90 MeV/fm².
- The s-quark mass is taken as m_s = 150 MeV; nuclear saturation properties used: binding energy -15.6 MeV, saturation density 0.16 fm⁻³, symmetry energy 18 MeV, compression modulus 285 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous EOS (Endo, 2011) that included charge screening without approximation, and contrasts with earlier studies by Heiselberg et al. (1993) and Glendenning and Pei (1995) who predicted wide mixed-phase regions using the Gibbs condition with moderate surface tension. The results are similar to those of Voskresensky et al. (2003) who found mechanical instability from charge screening but used a linear approximation to the Poisson equation, and the narrow mixed-phase region is consistent with estimates by Bejger et al. (2005) for glitch phenomena. The work enables new cooling curves for neutron stars and provides constraints for models of strong magnetic fields from spin-polarized quark matter, as proposed by Tatsumi (2000).
