# Deuteron Compton scattering and electromagnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon

> Differential cross section of deuteron Compton scattering has been calculated using the Bonn NN-potential, a consistent set of meson-exchange currents and seagulls, and lowest- and higher-order electromagnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon. Estimates of the polarizabilities of the neutron are obtained from recent experimental data.

# Deuteron Compton scattering and electromagnetic polarizabilities of the nucleon

## Key claim
The differential cross section of deuteron Compton scattering can be calculated using a potential-model approach with the Bonn NN-potential and consistent meson-exchange currents, and from comparison with experimental data the isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities can be extracted. However, the resulting neutron magnetic polarizability deviates strongly from both the proton value and dispersion-theory predictions, indicating that further theoretical and experimental work is needed before firm conclusions can be drawn.

## Method
The authors used the nonrelativistic Bonn one-boson-exchange potential with meson-exchange currents and seagulls derived via photon coupling to intermediate mesons and meson-baryon vertices, implemented in a gauge-invariant manner with meson-baryon form factors. Additional contributions from Delta-isobar excitation and retardation effects in the pion propagator were included, along with leading-order relativistic corrections (spin-orbit term and a spin-independent seagull of order 1/M^3) and higher-order polarizabilities (spin, quadrupole, dispersion) estimated from dispersion relations. The theoretical accuracy was checked against the dispersion relation at forward angle, finding agreement better than 3% in the amplitude between 20 and 100 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities extracted from comparison with experimental data: alpha_N + beta_N = (17.1 +/- 1.6) x 10^-4 fm^3, alpha_N - beta_N = (4.0 +/- 1.5) x 10^-4 fm^3 (theoretical-model errors not included).
- Resulting neutron polarizabilities: alpha_n = (9 +/- 3) x 10^-4 fm^3, beta_n = (11 +/- 3) x 10^-4 fm^3.
- Higher-order polarizabilities gave a positive shift in the extracted electric polarizability of the neutron of about +5 x 10^-4 fm^3.
- The long-range pion exchange almost saturates the meson-exchange currents and seagulls, while short-range contributions from heavy mesons (rho, omega, a_0, sigma) and form factors almost cancel each other.

## Context
This work extends the potential-model approach to deuteron Compton scattering previously used by Wilbois (1995), Levchuk (1995), Karakowski and Miller (1999), and Levchuk (2000), and contrasts with effective field theory calculations by Chen (1998) and Beane (1999). The authors argue that the potential approach remains more precise than EFT because higher-order terms in the EFT expansion are not negligible and introduce numerous unknown low-energy constants. The extracted neutron polarizabilities disagree with dispersion-theory expectations (L'vov 1999) which predict beta_n approximately equal to beta_p, and the authors call for evaluations of all 1/M^2 relativistic corrections, calculations with modern high-quality NN potentials, and more precise measurements of the gamma-d differential cross section below pion photoproduction threshold.
