# Identity method to study chemical fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations of the chemical composition of the hadronic final state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions carry valuable information on the properties of strongly interacting matter produced in the collisions. However, in experiments incomplete particle identification distorts the observed fluctuation signals. The effect is quantitatively studied and a new technique for measuring chemical fluctuations, the identity method, is proposed. The method fully eliminates the effect of incomplete particle identification. The application of the identity method to experimental data is explained.

## Key claim

The paper proposes the "identity method" to fully eliminate the effect of incomplete particle identification on measurements of event-by-event chemical fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the method is restricted to two-component systems (e.g., kaons and pions) and cannot handle misidentification among more than two particle types simultaneously.

## Method

The authors define a fluctuation measure Ψ, a strongly intensive modification of the Φ measure, and introduce a continuous "kaon identity" variable w_K(m) = ρ_K(m)/ρ(m) based on the measured mass (or energy-loss) distributions of kaons and pions. They show analytically that the raw Ψ measured with imperfect identification can be corrected to Ψ_CI (the value for complete identification) by dividing by a factor (1 − V_I/V_R)^2, where V_I and V_R are integrals over the mass distribution that quantify the quality of the particle identification procedure. The correction factor is determined entirely from experimental data without any model assumptions.

## Key numbers

* The Monte Carlo simulation uses a model where pions have a mean multiplicity of 100 and kaons are strictly correlated as the integer closest to N_π/5 (≤ N_π/5).
* The dE/dx distributions of pions and kaons are modeled as Gaussians centered at 1.4 and 1.2 (arbitrary units), respectively, with equal width σ varied from 0 to 0.08.
* The identity method correction factor is (1 − V_I/V_R)^2, where V_R = ⟨N_K⟩(⟨N⟩ − ⟨N_K⟩)/⟨N⟩ and V_I = ∫ dm ρ(m) w_K(m)(1 − w_K(m)).

## Context

This work extends earlier fluctuation measures used by the NA49 Collaboration (σ_dyn) and the STAR Collaboration (ν_dyn), which either included misidentification effects in mixed events or assumed unique particle identification. The identity method enables the use of the strongly intensive measure Φ (Gazdzicki 1992) for chemical fluctuations, which was previously impossible due to the lack of a correction for misidentification. The method is currently being applied to NA49 data (Mackowiak 2011) and opens the way for systematic experimental studies of chemical fluctuations as signals of deconfinement (Gorenstein 2003) or the critical point (Koch 2005).
