# Low and medium energy deuteron-ind ced reactions on $^{63,65}$Cu nuclei

> The activation cross sections of (d,p), (d,2n), (d,3n), and (d,2p) reactions on 63,65Cu were measured in the energy range from 4 to 20 MeV using the stacked-foils technique. Then, following the available elastic-scattering data analysis that provided the optical potential for reaction cross sections calculations, an increased effort has been devoted to the breakup mechanism, the direct reaction stripping, and the pre-equilibrium and compound-nucleus cross section calculations, corrected for the breakup and stripping decrease of the total reaction cross section. The overall agreement between the measured and calculated deuteron activation cross sections proves the correctness of the nuclear mechanisms account, next to the simultaneous analysis of the elastic-scattering and reaction data.

# Low and medium energy deuteron-induced reactions on $^{63,65}$Cu nuclei

## Key claim
The overall agreement between measured and calculated deuteron activation cross sections proves the correctness of the nuclear mechanisms account, but only when the breakup and stripping decrease of the total reaction cross section is explicitly corrected for, and when the deuteron optical potential is adjusted from the Daehnick et al. global potential rather than using default TALYS or global potentials.

## Method
Activation cross sections for (d,p), (d,2n), (d,3n), and (d,2p) reactions on $^{63,65}$Cu were measured from 4 to 20 MeV using the stacked-foils technique with natural copper and aluminum foils at the NPI cyclotron U-120M. The measured data were compared with calculations using the TALYS-1.2 code, where the default deuteron optical model potential was replaced by an adjusted parameter set based on Daehnick et al., and the breakup mechanism was treated via a phenomenological parametrization combined with Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations using the FRESCO code.

## Key numbers
- Incident deuteron energy range: 4 to 20 MeV, with energy determination accuracy of 1.0% and FWHM spread up to 1.8%
- $^{65}$Cu(d,2p)$^{65}$Ni cross section at 11.36 MeV: 0.101 mb (with statistical uncertainty 29 in the last digit)
- $^{65}$Cu(d,2p)$^{65}$Ni cross section at 19.88 MeV: 1.47 mb (with statistical uncertainty 21 in the last digit)
- $^{63}$Cu(d,3n)$^{62}$Zn cross section at 16.44 MeV: 0.018 mb (with statistical uncertainty 4 in the last digit)
- $^{63}$Cu(d,3n)$^{62}$Zn cross section at 19.88 MeV: 3.72 mb (with statistical uncertainty 38 in the last digit)
- $^{65}$Cu(d,2n)$^{65}$Zn cross section maximum: 949 mb at 15.17 MeV (with statistical uncertainty 95 in the last digit)
- $^{65}$Cu(d,p)$^{66}$Cu cross section maximum: 322 mb at 7.13 MeV (with statistical uncertainty 36 in the last digit)
- $^{63}$Cu(d,2n)$^{63}$Zn cross section maximum: 382 mb at 17.89 MeV (with statistical uncertainty 54 in the last digit)
- Deuteron binding energy: B_d = 2.224 MeV
- Foil thickness: 25 micrometers for Cu, 50 micrometers for Al, with 2% uncertainty from weighing
- Beam current uncertainty: 10%; detector efficiency uncertainty: 2% (long-lived) and 5% (short-lived)

## Context
This work extends the simultaneous analysis of deuteron elastic scattering and activation data previously applied to $^{27}$Al (Bem 2009, Ma 2009), $^{54,56,58,nat}$Fe (Ma 2008), and $^{59}$Co and $^{93}$Nb (Ma 2010c), now targeting $^{63,65}$Cu for the International Fusion Material Irradiation Facility (IFMIF) engineering design. The measured $^{65}$Cu(d,2p)$^{65}$Ni cross sections are the first experimental values except for one data point around 19 MeV. The results enable improved evaluated nuclear data libraries for deuteron-induced reactions up to 40 MeV needed for IFMIF radiation hazard assessment, and demonstrate that the CDCC method provides guidance for extrapolating elastic-breakup systematics beyond the 15-30 MeV range where Kleinfeller (1981) systematics were established.
