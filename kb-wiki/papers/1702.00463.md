# Subthreshold resonances and resonances in the $R$-matrix method for binary reactions and in the Trojan Horse method

> In this paper we discuss the $R$-matrix approach to treat the subthreshold resonances for the single-level and one channel, and for the single-level and two channel cases. In particular, the expression relating the ANC with the observable reduced width, when the subthreshold bound state is the only channel or coupled with an open channel, which is a resonance, is formulated. Since the ANC plays a very important role in nuclear astrophysics, these relations significantly enhance the power of the derived equations. We present the relationship between the resonance width and the ANC for the general case and consider two limiting cases: wide and narrow resonances. Different equations for the astrophysical S-factors in the $R$-matrix approach are presented. After that we discuss the Trojan Horse Method (THM) formalism. The developed equations are obtained using the surface-integral formalism and the generalized $R$-matrix approach for the three-body resonant reactions. It is shown how the Trojan Horse (TH) double differential cross section can be expressed in terms of the on-the-energy-shell astrophysical S-factor for the binary sub-reaction. Finally, we demonstrate how the THM can be used to calculate the astrophysical S-factor for the neutron generator $^{13}{\rm C}(α,\,n)^{16}{\rm O}$ in low-mass AGB stars. At astrophysically relevant energies this astrophysical S-factor is controlled by the threshold level $1/2^{+}, E_{x}= 6356$ keV. Here, we reanalyzed recent TH data taking into account more accurately the three-body effects and using both assumptions that the threshold level is a subthreshold bound state or it is a resonance state.

## Key claim

The paper derives new \(R\)-matrix equations linking the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) to the observable reduced width for subthreshold resonances, both when the subthreshold bound state is the only channel and when it is coupled to an open resonance channel. The authors show that the Trojan Horse Method (THM) double differential cross section can be expressed in terms of the on-the-energy-shell astrophysical \(S\)-factor for the binary sub-reaction, with the penetrability factor in the entrance channel removed, but caution that two additional factors appear: a term containing logarithmic derivatives and a radial integral requiring regularization, which was not accounted for in previous THM publications.

## Method

The authors use the surface-integral formalism and the generalized \(R\)-matrix approach to derive equations for subthreshold resonances in single-channel and two-channel cases, relating the ANC to observable reduced widths and resonance widths. They then extend this formalism to the Trojan Horse Method for three-body resonant reactions, expressing the TH reaction amplitude and differential cross sections in terms of the on-the-energy-shell \(R\)-matrix amplitude of the binary sub-reaction. The theory is applied to reanalyze recent TH data for the reaction \(^{13}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,n)^{16}\mathrm{O}\), treating the threshold level at \(E_x = 6356\) keV either as a subthreshold bound state or as a resonance state.

## Key numbers

- The threshold level in \(^{17}\mathrm{O}\) controlling the astrophysical \(S\)-factor for \(^{13}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,n)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) is the \(1/2^{+}\) state at excitation energy \(E_x = 6356\) keV.
- The subthreshold bound state in \(^{17}\mathrm{O} = (\alpha\,^{13}\mathrm{C})\) lies at \(-3\) keV relative to the \(\alpha + ^{13}\mathrm{C}\) threshold.
- The nucleon Compton wavelength used in the \(S\)-factor formula is \(\nu_N = 0.2118\) fm.
- The atomic unit mass used in the \(S\)-factor formula is \(E_N = 931.5\) MeV.
- The observable partial resonance width of a subthreshold resonance is given by \(\tilde\Gamma_i^{(s)}(E_i) = 2 P_i (\tilde\gamma_i^{(s)})^2\), where \(P_i\) is the penetrability factor and \(\tilde\gamma_i^{(s)}\) is the observable reduced width.

## Context

This work extends the classical \(R\)-matrix formalism of Lane and Thomas (1958) to treat subthreshold resonances coupled with open channels, and generalizes earlier ANC-reduced width relations from Mukhamedzhanov (1999) to the two-channel case. The derived THM equations provide a theoretical foundation for extracting astrophysical \(S\)-factors from indirect measurements, specifically enabling analysis of the neutron source reaction \(^{13}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,n)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) for \(s\)-process nucleosynthesis in low-mass AGB stars. The paper addresses the need for a consistent treatment of three-body effects in THM that were not fully accounted for in prior analyses.
