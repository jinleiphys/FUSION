# A Study of the $πB \to Y K $ reactions for Kaon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Parametrizations of total cross sections sufficient for all channels of the $πB \rightarrow Y K$ reactions are completed using a resonance model. As well as discussing the $πN \rightarrow ΛK$ reactions, which were not presented in our previous publications, we present the differential cross section for $πN \rightarrow ΛK$. This report also aims at presenting supplementary discussions to our previous work.

# A Study of the πB → YK reactions for Kaon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The authors complete parametrizations of total cross sections for all channels of the πB → YK reactions using a resonance model, but the differential cross sections for πN → ΛK are reproduced only in general trends, not in detail. The parametrizations for πΔ → YK reactions are obtained without inclusion of interference terms, limiting their accuracy.

## Method
The authors employ a resonance model including the N(1650), N(1710), and N(1720) resonances plus K*(892) exchange to compute amplitudes for πN → ΛK reactions, using interaction Lagrangians with form factors and a cut-off parameter ΛC = 0.8 GeV. For πN → ΣK reactions, they treat the Δ(1920) resonance as an effective resonance simulating other Δ resonances around 1.9 GeV, scaling coupling constants by a factor of 1.861. Total cross section parametrizations are fitted to resonance model calculations for all πB → YK channels.

## Key numbers
- Cut-off parameter ΛC = 0.8 GeV for all meson-baryon-(baryon resonance) vertices
- Coupling constant g_{K*(892)ΛN} = 0.45
- Form factor parameters for K*(892)-K-π vertex: C = 2.72 fm, β = 8.88 × 10^{-3} fm^2
- The Δ(1920) resonance contributes 10.4 out of 37.37 total Δ resonance contributions to πN → ΣK, giving a ratio of 0.278
- Scaling factor for each coupling constant g_{KΣΔ(1920)} and g_{πNΔ(1920)} is 1.861, consistent with the ratio sqrt(1.11×0.417 / 3.83×1.44) = 0.289
- Differential cross sections for π^- p → ΛK^0 are shown at pion beam momenta 0.980 GeV/c, 1.13 GeV/c, and 1.455 GeV/c
- Parametrizations for total cross sections are given in units of mb as functions of √s (in GeV) with threshold energies of 1.613 GeV (ΛK) and 1.688 GeV (ΣK)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous resonance model studies (Tsushima et al., 1995) by completing parametrizations for all πB → YK channels and presenting πN → ΛK results not given earlier. The parametrizations are intended for use in microscopic transport models of heavy ion collisions, replacing earlier parametrizations by Cugnon and Lombard (which assumed isospin averaging and N=Z symmetry) and by Randrup and Ko for baryon-baryon collisions. The next planned step is to investigate B1B2 → B3YK reactions using the same resonance model with parameters already fixed by this work.
