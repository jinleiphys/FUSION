# Dependence of Ridge Formation on Trigger Azimuth: Correlated Emission Model

> Ridge formation in near-side correlation in heavy-ion collisions is studied in the framework of a phenomenological model, called Correlated Emission Model (CEM). Successive soft emissions due to jet-medium interaction lead to the enhancement of thermal partons which follow the local flow directions. The correlation between the flow direction and the semihard parton direction is the major factor that causes the ridge formation to depend on the trigger direction relative to the reaction plane. With the use of a few parameters we have been able to reproduce the data on the ridge yields as functions of the trigger azimuthal angle for different centralities. An inside-outside asymmetry function is proposed to further probe the characteristics of the azimuthal correlation function. Insights are provided for the understanding of some detailed aspects of the centrality dependence.

## Key claim

The ridge yield in near-side dihadron correlations depends on the trigger azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane because of a mismatch between the semihard parton direction and the local collective flow direction; this dependence is reproduced by a phenomenological model with only a few parameters, but the model is restricted to the transverse plane with \(|\eta|<1\) and does not address the long-range \(\Delta\eta\) ridge.

## Method

The Correlated Emission Model (CEM) treats semihard partons created at points in the almond-shaped overlap region, traveling along straight-line trajectories at angle \(\phi_s\) to the reaction plane. The probability of a parton surviving to trigger is given by an exponential survival probability \(S(t)=\exp[-t/\tau(x_0,y_0)]\) with \(\tau(x_0,y_0)=t_0/d(x_0,y_0)\), where \(d\) is the relative density from a Glauber model. Along the trajectory, successive soft emissions enhance thermal partons; the ridge-particle distribution is built from a product of the survival factor, the local density \(D(x_\xi,y_\xi)\), a Gaussian correlation function \(C(x,y,\phi_s)=\exp[-(\phi_s-\psi(x,y))^2/(2\lambda)]\) between the parton direction and the local flow direction \(\psi\), and a Gaussian fluctuation factor \(\Gamma(x,y,\phi)=\exp[-(\phi-\psi(x,y))^2/(2\gamma t')]\). The ridge yield per trigger is obtained by integrating over all creation points and normalizing by the probability of the parton emerging.

## Key numbers

* For mid-central (20-60%) Au-Au collisions, the ridge yield decreases rapidly with increasing \(\phi_s\) from \(0^\circ\) to \(90^\circ\), while for top 5% centrality the dependence is mild.
* Best-fit parameters: \(t_0 = 0.2\) (in units of \(R_A=7\) fm, so \(\sim 1.4\) fm) and \(\lambda = 0.11\) (corresponding to a correlation width \(\sigma_c = \sqrt{\lambda} \approx 0.34\) rad \(\approx 20^\circ\)).
* The fluctuation parameter \(\gamma = 1\) is determined from the \(\Delta\phi\) distribution for \(15^\circ < \phi_s < 30^\circ\) at 20-60% centrality.
* Trigger momentum range: \(3 < p_T^{\rm trig} < 4\) GeV/c; associated particle momentum range: \(1.5 < p_T^{\rm assoc} < 2.0\) GeV/c.
* The nuclear radius is taken as \(R_A = 7\) fm; the Woods-Saxon parameters are \(r_0 = 6.45\) fm and \(\xi = 0.55\) fm; the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is 40 mb.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological understanding of ridge formation in heavy-ion collisions, following experimental observations by Feng (2008) of the \(\phi_s\) dependence of the ridge yield at RHIC. It builds on prior studies of dihadron correlations (e.g., by Adams et al. and Adler et al.) and on the recombination model for hadronization (e.g., by Fries et al., Greco et al., and Hwa and Yang). The CEM reproduces the centrality-dependent \(\phi_s\) dependence and predicts an inside-outside asymmetry function that can be tested against existing data; the model does not address the long-range \(\Delta\eta\) ridge observed by PHOBOS, which involves different physics.
