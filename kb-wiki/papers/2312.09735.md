# Collective Dynamics -- theoretical overview

> I overview the recent progress of phenomenological studies exploring collective dynamics in relativistic nuclear collisions to understand various QCD properties. Originally, collectivity was interpreted as a manifestation of the hydrodynamic behaviour of the QGP as a response to the initial collision geometry. Over the past decade, however, particularly following the experimental observation of collectivity in small colliding systems, pioneering studies have demonstrated the possibility of other interpretations. In this talk, I highlight recent studies aimed at understanding various QCD properties at different collision stages through the lens of collectivity and emphasize the importance of establishing Monte Carlo event generators for relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Collectivity in relativistic nuclear collisions can arise from multiple physical mechanisms beyond the traditional hydrodynamic interpretation, and distinguishing these contributions requires quantitative model-to-data comparisons using Monte Carlo event generators, though the relative importance of initial-state versus final-state effects remains an open question.

## Method

The paper reviews phenomenological studies of collective dynamics in relativistic nuclear collisions, examining evidence from ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) at ATLAS, comparing color glass condensate (CGC) and hydrodynamic calculations, analyzing nuclear structure effects through flow observables in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, and evaluating Bayesian parameter estimation frameworks including the dynamical core-corona initialization framework (DCCI2) and EPOS4 event generator.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon width extracted from Bayesian analysis was \(w\sim 0.8-1.0\) fm before including total hadronic nucleus-nucleus cross-section \(\sigma_{AA}\); after inclusion, the estimated \(w\) becomes below 0.7 fm
- The onset of QGP dominance appears at \(\langle dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/d\eta_s \rangle \sim 10\)-20, consistent between DCCI2 and EPOS4
- The fraction of hadrons produced from the core component \(R_{\mathrm{core}}\) shows clear scaling with multiplicity for p-p collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV and 13 TeV, and Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV
- Four-particle cumulants \(c_2\{4\}\) in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV show non-negligible non-equilibrium correction from corona components, especially in mid-central collisions

## Context

This review extends the discussion following the experimental observation of collectivity in small systems (p-p and p-A collisions) by ATLAS (2021), which challenged the traditional interpretation that collectivity arises solely from hydrodynamic expansion against initial geometrical anisotropy (Ollitrault 1992). The work connects to prior studies using CGC (Shi 2020), hydrodynamics (Zhao 2022), and the (3+1)D IP-Glasma framework (Schenke 2022) for understanding collectivity origins. It enables future full 3D Bayesian parameter estimation with Monte Carlo event generators for quantitative extraction of QGP properties, and extends the applicability range to lower collision energies for investigating the full QCD phase diagram.
