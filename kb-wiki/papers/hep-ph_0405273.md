# Finite temperature pion vector form factors in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We discuss the thermal behaviour of the pion vector form factors and calculate them in one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory. The perturbative result is used to analyze the $T$-dependent electromagnetic pion charge radius, obtaining a rough estimate of the deconfinement critical temperature. Imposing thermal unitarity, we generate the $ρ$ resonance pole for the form factor in the center of mass frame. The $ρ$ peak height in the modulus of the form factor decreases for increasing temperature, while its width increases and the peak position is slightly shifted downwards for $T\simeq$ 150 MeV. These results point in the direction suggested by many analysis of dilepton production data in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Finite temperature pion vector form factors in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
The thermal behavior of the pion vector form factor is calculated in one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory, showing that the ρ resonance peak height decreases with temperature while its width increases, with a slight downward shift of the peak position at T ≃ 150 MeV; however, these results are qualitative estimates that rely on imposing exact thermal unitarity and are limited to the center-of-mass frame, with the deconfinement temperature estimate being merely qualitative and based on a rough criterion.

## Method
The authors calculate the time-ordered product of two pion fields and one electromagnetic current in the imaginary time formalism of thermal field theory, using the SU(2) chiral Lagrangian at next-to-leading order (one-loop). They analytically continue the Matsubara sums to continuous external pion energies and apply the LSZ reduction formula to obtain the retarded real-time form factors. For the nonperturbative treatment, they impose exact thermal unitarity by constructing a unitarized form factor proportional to the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) amplitude, which reproduces the ρ resonance pole without introducing explicit resonance fields.

## Key numbers
- The pion decay constant is taken as fπ ≃ 92.4 MeV and the pion mass as mπ ≃ 139.6 MeV.
- The low-energy constant l̅6 is determined to be 16.5 ± 1.1 from the one-loop charge radius, with a recent two-loop evaluation giving 16.0 ± 0.5 ± 0.7.
- The experimental vacuum charge radius is ⟨r²⟩₀ = 0.45 ± 0.01 fm².
- The deconfinement temperature estimate using the thermal charge radius gives Tc ≃ 200 MeV, with uncertainties in l̅6 amounting to ±4 MeV; using only the vacuum radius gives Tc ≃ 265 MeV.
- The ρ resonance peak position is at Mρ ≃ 770 MeV at T = 0.
- The form factor is evaluated at temperatures T = 0, 100, and 150 MeV, showing the peak height decrease and width increase at the highest temperature.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations of the ππ thermal scattering amplitude (Gomez Nicola et al., 2002) and its unitarization (Dobado et al., 2002) to the pion vector form factor, which is directly relevant to dilepton production in heavy ion collisions. The results are consistent with earlier model-dependent analyses using Vector Meson Dominance (Song, 1996) and QCD sum rules (Dominguez, 1994), and qualitatively support the interpretation of CERES and DLS collaboration data showing a broadening of the ρ resonance in medium. The approach enables future work including three-momentum dependence and space-time evolution of the plasma for more realistic comparisons with experimental dilepton spectra.
