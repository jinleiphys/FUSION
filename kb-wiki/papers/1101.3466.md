# Azimuthal angle and rapidity dependence of di-hadron correlations in QCD

> I discuss novel QCD phenomena recently observed in p+p, p+A and A+A collisions, that result from the non-linear dynamics of small-x gluons. I focus on di-hadron correlation measurements, as opposed to single-hadron observables often too inclusive to distinguish possible new effects from established mechanisms. Specifically, I discuss angular correlations of forward di-hadrons in d+Au collisions and long-range rapidity correlations in high-multiplicity p+p and Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that novel QCD phenomena observed in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions, specifically the azimuthal decorrelation of forward di-hadrons in d+Au collisions and long-range rapidity correlations in high-multiplicity p+p and Au+Au collisions, can be consistently interpreted as manifestations of parton saturation and non-linear gluon dynamics described by the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, though the author notes that alternative explanations exist for some observations and that the CGC formalism is not fully realized at current energies.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical calculations within the CGC framework, which resums coherent multiple scatterings and non-linear parton evolution at small momentum fraction x. For forward di-hadrons in d+Au collisions, the double-inclusive hadron production cross section is computed using the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, with parameters constrained by single-inclusive forward hadron production data. For long-range rapidity correlations in high-multiplicity p+p and Au+Au collisions, the Glasma field description is used, where the double-inclusive particle production cross section is expressed in terms of single-gluon distributions under a Gaussian approximation for the CGC wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale for d+Au collisions: \( Q_s^2(x_0=0.02) = 0.6 \) GeV\(^2\)
- Saturation scale for p+p collisions: \( Q_s^2(x_0=0.01) = 0.2 \) GeV\(^2\) (noted as uncomfortably close to \(\Lambda^2_{QCD}\))
- For forward di-hadrons in central d+Au collisions, the away-side peak (around \(\Delta\phi=\pi\)) is absent, while it is prominent in p+p collisions, as shown in preliminary STAR data
- In central-forward di-hadron production at mid-rapidity, the integrated coincidence probability ratio \(I_{dAu}\) in central d+Au collisions is about half that in p+p collisions, reflecting depletion of the away-side peak
- The ridge in Au+Au collisions extends several units in rapidity (\(\Delta\eta\)), confirming early-time origin of correlations
- In high-multiplicity p+p collisions at the LHC, the near-side ridge is observed only when hadron transverse momenta satisfy \(1 < p_T < 3\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based interpretation of small-x QCD phenomena previously applied to single-inclusive forward hadron production (Albacete, 2010) and azimuthal correlations (Albacete, 2010) in d+Au collisions. It contrasts the CGC approach with the Glauber-eikonal formalism (Qiu, 2004) for mid-rapidity di-hadrons, noting that both describe the data but neither underlying assumption is fully satisfied in that kinematic regime. The paper enables further quantitative tests of saturation physics through more differential measurements of the coincidence probability as a function of transverse momentum, rapidity, and centrality, while also identifying the need for improved theoretical treatment of multi-gluon distributions beyond the Gaussian approximation.
