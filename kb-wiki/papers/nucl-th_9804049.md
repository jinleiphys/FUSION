# Photoexcitation of the Nucleon in the Constituent Quark Model

> Photoexcitation of the nucleon is studied in the framework of the constituent quark model with nonrelativistic quark-quark interactions. Utilizing the dominance of the pairwise structure of the relevant three-quark potential, the bound and excited state wave functions have been constructed using the Faddeev-type Integrodifferential Equation Approach. The method is based on the Hyperspherical Harmonics expansion and takes two-body correlations exactly into account and therefore reliable wave functions, required in photo processes, can be obtained. The integrated photoabsorption cross sections for the E1-and M1-transitions are calculated and compared with experimental values as well as with those of other theoretical calculations. Our results, in overall, are in good to very good agreement with the experimental values.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic constituent quark model, using the Integrodifferential Equation Approach (IDEA) based on Hyperspherical Harmonics, can reproduce experimental integrated photoabsorption cross sections for E1 and M1 transitions in nucleons, but the agreement is conditional on the specific quark-quark potential used and is generally better for E1 transitions than for M1 transitions.

## Method

The authors solved the three-quark bound state problem using the Faddeev-type Integrodifferential Equation Approach (IDEA), which expands the wave function in Hyperspherical Harmonics and treats two-body correlations exactly. They calculated the transition matrix elements for electric dipole (E1) and magnetic dipole (M1) photoexcitation of the nucleon, using the Siegert form for the E1 Hamiltonian and the quark magnetic moment operator for the M1 Hamiltonian. The integrated photoabsorption cross sections were computed for transitions from the nucleon ground state to the N(1520) resonance (E1) and from the proton to the Δ⁺(1232) resonance (M1), using several different quark-quark potentials including Martin, Cornell, Lichtenberg, Ono-Schöberl, and the AP1 and AP2 potentials of Silvestre-Brac.

## Key numbers

- For the E1 transition to N(1520) using spin-independent potentials with IDEA: Σ₁ = 0.80 μb·GeV (Martin), 0.74 μb·GeV (Cornell), 0.72 μb·GeV (Lichtenberg); experimental value is 0.70 μb·GeV
- For the E1 transition to N(1520) using spin-dependent AP1 potential with IDEA: Σ₁ = 0.72 μb·GeV; experimental value is 0.70 μb·GeV
- For the E1 transition to N(1520) using spin-dependent AP2 potential with IDEA: Σ₁ = 0.71 μb·GeV; experimental value is 0.70 μb·GeV
- For the M1 transition to Δ⁺(1232) using Ono-Schöberl potential with IDEA: Σ_M1 = 1.10 μb·GeV; experimental value is 0.95 μb·GeV
- Quark masses used: m_u = m_d = 0.277 GeV (AP1), 0.280 GeV (AP2)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleon photoexcitation using the nonrelativistic constituent quark model, particularly addressing the unsatisfactory agreement with experiment found by Brizzolara and Giannini (1990) using the Isgur-Karl model and bag model approaches. The authors show that the IDEA method, which treats two-body correlations exactly, provides more reliable wave functions than the simpler Hypercentral Approximation (HCA) used by earlier authors such as Fabre de la Ripelle (1980) and McTavish (1983), though for the soft quark-quark potentials the two methods give similar results. The work enables more reliable calculations of electromagnetic transition amplitudes in baryons and suggests that remaining discrepancies, especially for M1 transitions, may require inclusion of meson coupling or qq̅ pair contributions.
