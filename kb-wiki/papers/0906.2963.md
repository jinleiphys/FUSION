# Study of the $J/ψ\to ϕ(ω) f_2(1270)$, $J/ψ\to ϕ(ω) f'_2(1525)$ and $J/ψ\to K^{*0}(892) \bar{K}^{* 0}_2(1430)$ decays

> We present an approach to study the decay modes of the $J/ψ$ into a vector meson and a tensor meson, taking into account the nature of the $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, $\bar{K}^{* 0}_2(1430)$ resonances as dynamically generated states from the vector meson-vector meson interaction. We evaluate four ratios of partial decay widths in terms of a flavor dependent OZI breaking parameter and the results obtained compare favorably with experiment. The fit to the data is possible due to the particular strength and sign of the couplings of the resonances to pairs of vector mesons given by the theory, thus providing a nontrivial test for the idea of these tensor states as dynamically generated from the vector-vector interaction.

# Study of the $J/\psi \to \phi(\omega) f_2(1270)$, $J/\psi \to \phi(\omega) f'_2(1525)$ and $J/\psi \to K^{*0}(892) \bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)$ decays

## Key claim

The four independent ratios of partial decay widths for these $J/\psi$ decays into a vector meson and a tensor meson can be described with a single flavor-dependent OZI breaking parameter, providing a nontrivial test of the idea that the tensor states $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $\bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)$ are dynamically generated from vector-vector interactions; however, the experimental uncertainties remain large, and the agreement is only reasonable rather than definitive.

## Method

The authors assume the tensor resonances $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $\bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)$ are dynamically generated states from the vector meson-vector meson interaction, using the hidden gauge formalism and unitarization in coupled channels via the Bethe-Salpeter equation as developed in prior work (Geng et al., 2008). They compute the primary weights for $J/\psi$ decay into a vector meson ($\phi$, $\omega$, or $K^{*0}(892)$) plus a pair of vector building blocks by treating the $J/\psi$ as an SU(3) singlet with ideal mixing for $\phi$ and $\omega$, introducing a single parameter $\nu = T^{(1,1)}/T^{(8,8)}$ to account for OZI violation. The decay amplitudes are then constructed by summing over the vector-vector channels weighted by the production weights, the loop functions $G_i$, and the couplings $g_i$ of each resonance to the vector-vector pairs, and the parameter $\nu$ is fitted to the experimental branching ratio data.

## Key numbers

*   Best-fit value of the OZI breaking parameter: $\nu = 1.45$.
*   Ratio $R_1 = \Gamma(J/\psi \to \phi f_2(1270)) / \Gamma(J/\psi \to \phi f'_2(1525))$: theory gives $0.13 - 0.61$ (central $0.28^{+0.33}_{-0.15}$), experiment gives $0.22 - 0.47$ (central $0.33^{+0.14}_{-0.11}$).
*   Ratio $R_2 = \Gamma(J/\psi \to \omega f_2(1270)) / \Gamma(J/\psi \to \omega f'_2(1525))$: theory gives $2.92 - 13.58$ (central $5.88^{+7.70}_{-2.96}$), experiment gives $12.33 - 49.00$ (central $21.50^{+27.50}_{-9.17}$).
*   Ratio $R_3 = \Gamma(J/\psi \to \omega f_2(1270)) / \Gamma(J/\psi \to \phi f_2(1270))$: theory gives $6.18 - 19.15$ (central $10.63^{+8.52}_{-4.45}$), experiment gives $11.21 - 23.08$ (central $15.85^{+7.23}_{-4.65}$).
*   Ratio $R_4 = \Gamma(J/\psi \to K^{*0} \bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)) / \Gamma(J/\psi \to \omega f_2(1270))$: theory gives $0.83 - 2.10$ (central $1.33^{+0.77}_{-0.50}$), experiment gives $0.55 - 0.89$ (central $0.70^{+0.19}_{-0.15}$).
*   Derived OZI parameter $\lambda_\phi = 0.18$, consistent with the range $0.13 - 0.20$ from earlier work on scalar mesons (Ulf-G. Meissner and J. A. Oller, 2001; L. S. Geng and E. Oset, 2008).
*   Experimental branching ratios used: $BR(J/\psi \to \omega f_2(1270)) = (4.3 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-3}$ (PDG), $BR(J/\psi \to \phi f_2(1270)) = (0.27 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-3}$ (BES), $BR(J/\psi \to \phi f'_2(1525)) = (0.82 \pm 0.12) \times 10^{-3}$ (BES), $BR(J/\psi \to K^{*0} \bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)) = (3.0 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-3}$ (PDG), and $BR(J/\psi \to \omega f'_2(1525)) < 0.2 \times 10^{-3}$ (PDG).

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to $J/\psi$ decays into a vector meson and a scalar meson (Ulf-G. Meissner and J. A. Oller, 2001; L. S. Geng and E. Oset, 2008) to the tensor meson sector, replacing the dynamically generated scalar states $\sigma(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ with the tensor states $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $\bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)$. The tensor resonances were shown to arise as bound states of vector meson pairs in prior work (R. Molina et al., 2008; L. S. Geng et al., 2008) using the hidden gauge formalism. The success of the fit depends sensitively on the relative signs of the couplings of the resonances to different vector-vector channels, particularly the opposite relative sign of the $\rho\rho$ and $K^*\bar{K}^*$ couplings for $f_2(1270)$ versus $f'_2(1525)$, which provides a nontrivial consistency check for the dynamical generation picture. Further refinements of the experimental data would enable a more stringent test of the theoretical approach.
