# Nuclear electron capture rate in stellar interiors and the case of 7Be

> Nuclear electron capture rate from continuum in an astrophysical plasma environment (like solar core) is calculated using a modified Debye-Huckel screening potential and the related non-Gaussian q-distribution of electron momenta. For q=1 the well-known Debye-Huckel results are recovered. The value of q can be derived from the fluctuation of number of particles and temperature inside the Debye sphere. For 7Be continuum electron capture in solar core, we find an increase of 7 -- 10 percent over the rate calculated with standard Debye-Huckel potential. The consequence of this results is a reduction of the same percentage of the SSM 8B solar neutrino flux, leaving unchanged the SSM 7Be flux.

## Key claim

For continuum electron capture by 7Be in the solar core, using a modified Debye-Huckel screening potential and a non-Gaussian q-distribution of electron momenta, the capture rate increases by 7–10 percent over the standard Debye-Huckel rate, but only for q values between 0.84 and 0.88; the result depends on the fluctuation of particle number inside the Debye sphere.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear electron capture rate from continuum electrons using a modified Debye-Huckel (MDH) screening potential V_q(r) derived from a super-statistics approach, where the inverse Debye radius fluctuates around its average value. The electron density at the nucleus (Fermi factor) is obtained by numerically solving the Schrodinger equation with the MDH potential, and the rate is averaged over a non-Gaussian q-distribution of electron momenta f_q(v) instead of the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution. For the specific case of 7Be in the solar core, the parameter q is estimated from the fluctuation of the number of particles inside the Debye sphere, yielding a range of 0.84 to 0.88.

## Key numbers

- At solar core temperature kT = 1.27 keV and average Debye radius xi = 0.45 a_0, the 7Be continuum electron capture rate R_q(T) increases by 7–10 percent over the standard Debye-Huckel rate.
- The number of particles inside the Debye sphere in the solar core is about 4, derived from an average electron density n_e = 9.1 a_0^{-3}.
- For q = 0.85 at xi = 0.45 a_0 and kT = 1.27 keV, the relative variation of the rate over the Coulomb rate is -9.03 percent (meaning R_q is 9.03 percent larger than the Coulomb rate).
- For q = 0.95 at the same conditions, the relative variation is -2.65 percent; for q = 0.75, it is -15.06 percent.
- The standard Debye-Huckel rate (q = 1) at xi = 0.45 a_0 and kT = 1.27 keV is 0.69 percent smaller than the Coulomb rate.

## Context

This work extends the Debye-Huckel treatment of plasma screening in stellar interiors, which was previously found by Johnson et al. to have violated assumptions in stellar cores due to too few particles per Debye sphere. It builds on earlier calculations by Bahcall and Moeller and by Gruzinov and Bahcall, who found that plasma screening and fluctuations changed continuum electron capture rates by only 1–2 percent; the present work finds a larger effect (7–10 percent) by using a non-Gaussian electron momentum distribution. The result implies a reduction of the same percentage in the standard solar model 8B solar neutrino flux, while leaving the 7Be neutrino flux unchanged, which the authors note is consistent with experimental findings discussed by Haxton.
