# Gamow-Teller Strength in the Region of $^{100}$Sn

> New calculations are presented for Gamow-Teller beta decay of nuclei near $^{100}$Sn. Essentially all of the $^{100}$Sn Gamow-Teller decay strength is predicted to go to a single state at an excitation energy of 1.8 MeV in $^{100}$In. The first calculations are presented for the decays of neighboring odd-even and odd-odd nuclei which show, in contrast to $^{100}$Sn, surprisingly complex and broad Gamow-Teller strength distributions. The results are compared to existing experimental data and the resulting hindrance factors are discussed.

# Gamow-Teller Strength in the Region of $^{100}$Sn

## Key claim

Essentially all of the Gamow-Teller decay strength of $^{100}$Sn is predicted to go to a single state at 1.8 MeV excitation in $^{100}$In, but this result depends on a renormalization of the proton-neutron G-matrix elements by a factor of 0.7 to bring the calculated neutron single-particle energies and GT strength distributions into agreement with experiment. In contrast, the decays of neighboring odd-even and odd-odd nuclei show surprisingly complex and broad GT strength distributions.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in two model spaces (SNA and SNB) starting from a closed-shell configuration for $^{100}$Sn, with proton holes occupying the 0f$_{5/2}$, 1p$_{3/2}$, 1p$_{1/2}$, and 0g$_{9/2}$ orbitals and neutron particles occupying the 0g$_{7/2}$, 1d$_{5/2}$, 1d$_{3/2}$, 2s$_{1/2}$, and 0h$_{11/2}$ orbitals. The proton-proton interaction used the Ji-Wildenthal fit to N=50 isotones, the neutron-neutron interaction was scaled from a fit to N=82 isotones with a $^{132}$Sn core, and the proton-neutron interaction was calculated from the bare G matrix based on the Paris potential, then renormalized by a factor of 0.7. For $^{100}$Sn specifically, a 2p2h model space was used to include core-polarization corrections.

## Key numbers

- The lowest 1$^{+}$ state in $^{100}$In is predicted at an excitation energy of 1.8 MeV, carrying 80% of the GT strength calculated in the SNB model space.
- Total GT strength extracted from $^{98}$Cd decay experiment is 3.5$^{+0.8}_{-0.7}$, compared to a calculated total of 13.4 within the sensitivity limit, giving an experimental hindrance factor h$_{exp}$ = 3.8$^{+0.7}_{-0.6}$.
- The hindrance factor for $^{100}$Sn from the 2p2h calculation is h$_{0g}$ = 1.25, compared to the range h$_{0g}$ = 1.29-1.71 from Towner (1985).
- Using h = 2.09 and Q$_{ec}$ = 7 MeV, the calculated half-life for $^{100}$Sn is T$_{1/2}$ = 0.53 s.
- Using h = 4, the calculated half-life for $^{101}$Sn is T$_{1/2}$ = 1.4 s, close to the experimental limit of T$_{1/2}$ $\geq$ 1 s.
- Using h = 4, the calculated half-life for $^{100}$In is T$_{1/2}$ = 6.8 s, close to the experimental result of T$_{1/2}$ = 5 $\pm$ 1 s.
- The beta-delayed proton branching ratio for $^{101}$Sn is predicted above 40%, with the proton separation energy S$_{p}$ $\approx$ 1.3 MeV in $^{101}$In.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model calculations for N=50 isotones by Skouras and Manakos (1993), Plochocki et al. (1992), and Johnstone (1991), and provides the first calculations for GT decays of odd-even and odd-odd nuclei near $^{100}$Sn beyond $^{93}$Tc and $^{95}$Rh. The predicted simple decay of $^{100}$Sn to a single state contrasts with the complex strength distributions in neighboring nuclei, and the hindrance factors are compared to those from the 0d1s shell (Brown and Wildenthal, 1985) and to perturbation theory results from Towner (1985) and Johnstone (1991). The results enable experimental tests using beta-delayed gamma and proton spectroscopy, and the TAGS method is identified as essential for measuring the total decay energy in complex decays such as $^{101}$Sn and $^{100}$In.
