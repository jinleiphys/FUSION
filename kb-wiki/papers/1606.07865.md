# Azimuthal instabilities of the Gribov-Levin-Ryskin equation

> We introduce the phenomenology of elliptic flow in nuclear collisions, and argue that its scaling across energies, rapidities and system sizes could be suggestive of a QCD-based rather than a hydrodynamical explanation. As a hypothesis for such an explanation, we show that the GLR equation develops unstable modes when the parton distribution function is generalized to depend on azimuthal angle. This generally means the structure function aquires an azimuthal dependence. We argue that this process is a plausible alternative explanation for the origin of elliptic flow, one that naturally respects the scaling experimentally observed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that azimuthal instabilities in the Gribov-Levin-Ryskin (GLR) equation, when the parton distribution function is generalized to depend on azimuthal angle, could provide a QCD-based alternative explanation for elliptic flow in nuclear collisions that naturally respects the observed scaling across energies, rapidities, and system sizes, rather than requiring a hydrodynamic interpretation.

## Method

The authors introduce an azimuthal-angle dependence into the GLR equation for gluon evolution at low Bjorken x, then perturb the azimuthally symmetric saturation solution with a Fourier expansion in the angle. They analyze two ansatzes for the Fourier coefficients: a polynomial ansatz and a Bessel function ansatz, solving the linearized integro-differential equation in the high-Q^2 Mueller-Qiu limit to study the growth of azimuthal instabilities as x decreases toward mid-rapidity.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale is given by Q_s(x) ~ x^(2λ+1), with λ a parameter from the GLR equation.
- The Bessel function solution has index ν = (4α_s N_c / [π(2λ+1)])^(1/2), where α_s is the strong coupling constant and N_c = 3 is the number of colors.
- The polynomial ansatz coefficients include B = π^2 / (C_F S_⊥ α_s^3), C = 2λ+1, D = 2α_s N_c / [(2λ+1)π], and ε = (2R-b)/(2R+b) where R is the nuclear radius and b is the impact parameter.
- The Bessel function solution yields u_2/ε ~ 10^(-2) at moderate x, growing rapidly as x decreases; the authors impose a saturation cutoff at u_2^(max) = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the GLR equation (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin, 1983) and the Mueller-Qiu formulation to include azimuthal dependence, contrasting with earlier approaches that assumed azimuthal symmetry in saturation physics (Color Glass Condensate, JIMWLK, BK equations). The paper argues that the observed scaling of elliptic flow across system sizes (from pp to pA to AA collisions, as reported by CMS, PHENIX, and PHOBOS collaborations) and its near-independence of energy and rapidity (STAR, BRAHMS) are difficult to explain hydrodynamically but could arise naturally from QCD evolution. The mechanism differs from the Sivers effect and from CGC field-based models (Nara, 2011; Raju, 2014) because the anisotropy is generated dynamically by the evolution equation itself rather than imposed via boundary conditions.
