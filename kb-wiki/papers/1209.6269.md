# Effective interactions in the delta-shells potential

> We determine two-body Skyrme force parameters from a Nucleon-Nucleon interaction as a function of the maximal momentum fitting NN scattering data. We find general agreement with Vlowk interactions based on high quality potentials.

## Key claim

The authors determine two-body Skyrme force parameters directly from NN scattering data below pion production threshold, rather than from fits to nuclear binding energies, and find general agreement with the \(V_{\rm low k}\) approach applied to high-quality potentials, but only when the tensor-force coupling is treated in an "uncoupled" approximation that neglects the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel mixing.

## Method

The authors fit a delta-shells local potential (short-range delta-shells plus one-pion-exchange and electromagnetic tail beyond 3 fm) to neutron-proton phase shifts up to a maximum center-of-mass momentum \(\Lambda\), using a fit with \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f}=1.06\) below pion production threshold (362 MeV). From this potential, they compute the Skyrme parameters \(t_0, x_0, t_1, x_1, t_2, x_2, W_0, t_T, t_U\) via integrals over S- and P-wave and tensor-mixing partial-wave potentials, and compare the resulting scale dependence with the AV18-\(V_{\rm low k}\) interaction at \(\Lambda_{\rm low k}=2.1\ {\rm fm}^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- The delta-shells fit to np phase shifts achieves \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f}=1.06\) for data below pion production threshold \(p \le \sqrt{M_N m_\pi} = 362\ {\rm MeV}\).
- The coarse-graining scale implied by the pion mass is \(\Delta r \sim 1/\sqrt{M_N m_\pi}=0.54\ {\rm fm}\).
- Skyrme parameters from the delta-shells fit (up to \(E_{\rm LAB}=350\ {\rm MeV}\)): \(t_0 = -555.3\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^3\), \(x_0 = -0.36\), \(t_1 = 1711.8\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^3\), \(x_1 = -0.05\), \(t_2 = 2746.8\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^3\), \(x_2 = -0.845\), \(W_0 = 108.7\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^5\), \(t_U = 1476.2\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^5\), \(t_T = -4576.4\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^5\).
- Corresponding AV18-\(V_{\rm low k}\) values (at \(\Lambda_{\rm low k}=2.1\ {\rm fm}^{-1}\)): \(t_0 = -999.6\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^3\), \(x_0 = 0.002\), \(t_1 = 1854.2\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^3\), \(x_1 = -0.02\), \(t_2 = 2198.3\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^3\), \(x_2 = -0.91\), \(W_0 = 84.1\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^5\), \(t_U = 1235.2\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^5\), \(t_T = -3864.0\ {\rm MeV\,fm}^5\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Arriola (2010), who determined Skyrme parameters from NN threshold properties (scattering lengths, effective ranges, volumes) without explicit finite-range effects. The present method instead fits phase shifts up to a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\), analogous to the \(V_{\rm low k}\) renormalization-group procedure of Bogner (2003, 2009). The results enable a systematic connection between realistic NN potentials and Skyrme-type mean-field calculations for double-closed shell nuclei, as outlined in Navarro Perez (2011).
