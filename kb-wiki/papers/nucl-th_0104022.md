# Broad Band Equilibration of Strangeness

> We develop the "broad band equilibration" scenario for kaon productions at GSI energies with in-medium effects.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the ratio of hyperons to antikaons (and thus the ratio of positive to negative kaons) is roughly independent of density in heavy-ion collisions at GSI energies, a scenario they term "broad band equilibration." This independence arises because the in-medium antikaon mass shift and the baryon chemical potential combine to keep the relevant Boltzmann factor nearly constant across a range of densities, contrary to the low-density freezeout required by free-space hadron masses.

## Method
The authors use a thermal model with in-medium kaon masses derived from chiral symmetry breaking, incorporating a kaon-nucleon sigma term (Σ_KN ≈ 400 MeV) and a vector potential that saturates above nuclear saturation density ρ₀. They compute the ratio R = Λ/K⁻ as a function of density, using the effective antikaon mass m*_K⁻ and the sum μ_B + ω*_K⁻ (where ω*_K⁻ is the antikaon self-energy), and compare with experimental data from GSI for Ni-Ni collisions at 1.8–1.93 A GeV and with transport calculations by Bratkovskaya and Cassing.

## Key numbers
- The measured ratio K⁺/K⁻ = 32 in central Ni+Ni collisions at E = 1.93 A GeV (Menzel), implying Λ/K⁻ ≈ 31.
- The chemical freezeout parameters from Cleymans, Oeschler and Redlich (1998) are T = 70 MeV and μ_B = 750 MeV, corresponding to a density of ρ₀/4.
- The effective kaon-nucleon sigma term is Σ_KN ≈ 400 MeV, with a range correction factor (1 − 0.37 ω*_K⁻²/m_K²).
- As μ_B varies from 860 MeV to 905 MeV, the density goes from 1.2ρ₀ to 2.1ρ₀, and μ_B + ω*_K⁻ varies only from 1230 to 1227 MeV at fixed T = 70 MeV; at ρ₀/4, μ_B + ω*_K⁻ = 1245 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the statistical thermal model of Cleymans, Oeschler and Redlich (1998), which successfully fit particle ratios at GSI but required an unrealistically low freezeout density (ρ₀/4) when using free-space hadron masses. The authors instead incorporate in-medium kaon masses from chiral symmetry breaking (following Brown, Kubodera, Rho 1987) and vector decoupling at high density, showing that the Λ/K⁻ ratio becomes density-independent. This "broad band equilibration" scenario enables a natural explanation of the centrality-independence of K⁺/K⁻ observed at GSI (Menzel) and AGS (Dunlop), and connects to kaon condensation in neutron stars, setting a maximum neutron star mass of 1.5 M_⊙ (following Lee, Brown, Bethe 1995).
