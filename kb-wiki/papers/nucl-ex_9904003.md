# Anisotropic Flow at STAR

> We first review previous work on anisotropic flow at the AGS and SPS. Then the physics related to flow is discussed as well as the interaction of flow with other non-flow measurements. From 40k RQMD and 100k HIJING events predictions for anisotropic flow at RHIC are presented. Using the STAR detector acceptance, estimates for the resolution obtainable with STAR are shown. We conclude that it should be possible to obtain good measurements for elliptic flow with either the STAR main TPC or forward TPCs. Anisotropic flow should be easily one of the first results from STAR.

## Key claim

Using 40k RQMD and 100k HIJING events, the authors predict that elliptic flow (v₂) can be reliably measured with the STAR detector at RHIC, but only if the RQMD predictions for multiplicity and v₂ magnitude are correct; the measurement of directed flow (v₁) in the forward TPCs is found to be infeasible due to negligible resolution at all impact parameters.

## Method

The authors generated 40,000 Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV using the RQMD v2.4 model and 100,000 Au+Au collisions at the same energy using HIJING v1.35, both in the impact parameter range 5 ≤ b ≤ 10 fm. They calculated the Fourier coefficients v₁ and v₂ in momentum space and r₁ and r₂ in coordinate space for nucleons and charged pions as functions of rapidity and transverse momentum. Using the RQMD predictions for multiplicity and v₂, they estimated the event-plane resolution for the STAR main TPC (|y| ≤ 1.5) and forward TPCs (2.5 ≤ |y| ≤ 4.0) by comparing subevent resolutions to the true reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- RQMD v2.4 predictions: v₂ for charged pions integrated over the TPC rapidity region (-1.5 ≤ y ≤ 1.5) is shown as a function of impact parameter b; the corresponding multiplicity and resolution are also shown.
- For the FTPCs (2.5 ≤ |y| ≤ 4.0), v₂ for π⁺, π⁻, and protons combined is shown versus b; the resolution for v₂ using subevents is shown in the figure.
- For v₁ in the FTPC region at large b, the magnitude becomes approximately 1%, but the resolution is negligible at all values of b.
- The authors estimate that 1,000,000 minimum bias events would be needed to obtain 10 centrality bins for studying the dependence of v₂ on b.
- In HIJING, v₂ is slightly negative over the whole rapidity range for both charged pions and nucleons; for particles with p_t ≥ 1.5 GeV, v₂ becomes more negative especially at forward and backward rapidity.

## Context

This paper extends the Fourier expansion method for anisotropic flow developed by Ollitrault (1992) and applied by the E877 Collaboration at the AGS and NA49 at the SPS, providing the first detailed predictions for the STAR detector at RHIC. The work enables future measurements of elliptic flow as a probe of the equation of state and possible QGP formation, as suggested by Hung and Shuryak and by Rischke, and allows cross-checks with hydrodynamic and cascade model predictions such as those from Sorge and from parton cascade studies by Zhang et al. (1999).
