# Thermalization of mini-jets in a quark-gluon plasma

> We complete the physical picture for the evolution of a high-energy jet propagating through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma by investigating the thermalization of the soft components of the jet. We argue that the following scenario should hold: the leading particle emits a significant number of mini-jets which promptly evolve via quasi-democratic branchings and thus degrade into a myriad of soft gluons, with energies of the order of the medium temperature T. Via elastic collisions with the medium constituents, these soft gluons relax to local thermal equilibrium with the plasma over a time scale which is considerably shorter than the typical lifetime of the mini-jet. The thermalized gluons form a tail which lags behind the hard components of the jet. We support this scenario, first, via parametric arguments and, next, by studying a simplified kinetic equation, which describes the jet dynamics in longitudinal phase-space. We solve the kinetic equation using both (semi-)analytical and numerical methods. In particular, we obtain the first exact, analytic, solutions to the ultrarelativistic Fokker-Planck equation in one-dimensional phase-space. Our results confirm the physical picture aforementioned and demonstrate the quenching of the jet via multiple branching followed by the thermalization of the soft gluons in the cascades.

## Key claim

The paper argues that soft gluons produced by democratic branchings within a jet propagating through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma thermalize via elastic collisions with medium constituents over a time scale considerably shorter than the mini-jet lifetime, but this thermalization is not instantaneous and the medium does not act as a perfect sink, leading to energy loss somewhat smaller than the ideal turbulent flow prediction.

## Method

The authors study a simplified kinetic equation describing jet dynamics in longitudinal phase-space, which includes two collision terms: an inelastic term for multiple BDMPSZ-type branchings and an elastic term for 2-to-2 collisions with medium constituents in the Fokker-Planck approximation. They solve this equation using both semi-analytical methods (combining exact analytic solutions to the relativistic Fokker-Planck equation with known analytic solutions for the ideal branching process) and numerical methods, inserting an infrared cutoff at the thermal scale p_* = T in the branching integral to mimic the termination of the cascade.

## Key numbers

- The branching time for a gluon with energy ω is t_br(ω) = (1/α_bar) sqrt(ω / q_hat), where α_bar = α_s N_c / π and q_hat is the jet quenching parameter.
- The characteristic energy scale for democratic branchings over a distance L is ω_br(L) = α_bar^2 q_hat L^2, estimated as approximately 12 GeV for q_hat = 1 GeV^2/fm and L = 5 fm.
- The relaxation time for soft gluons via elastic collisions is t_rel ~ T^2 / q_hat.
- The energy loss by the jet under the perfect sink assumption is ΔE_flow = (υ/2) ω_br(L) = (υ/2) α_bar^2 q_hat L^2, with υ ≈ 4.96.
- The transverse phase-space volume occupied by a gluon is Δp_perp^2 Δx_perp^2 ~ 1/α_bar^4, independent of energy.
- Numerical simulations use a leading particle with initial energy E = 90 T, with T the medium temperature.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for medium-induced gluon cascades developed by Blaizot et al. (2013, 2014, 2015) and Fister et al. (2014), which established the existence of wave turbulence and scaling spectra in jet branching but did not address thermalization of soft components. The paper clarifies the role of elastic collisions as the physical mechanism terminating the cascade at the thermal scale, resolving the unphysical prediction of energy accumulating at arbitrarily low energies. It enables quantitative predictions for the energy deposited by jets into the quark-gluon plasma and provides the first exact analytic solutions to the ultrarelativistic Fokker-Planck equation in one-dimensional phase-space.
