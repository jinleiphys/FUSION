# A Lattice Calculation of Thermal Dilepton Rates

> Using clover improved Wilson fermions we calculate thermal vector meson correlation functions above the deconfinement phase transition of quenched QCD. At temperatures 1.5 Tc and 3Tc they are found to differ by less than 15% from that of a freely propagating quark anti-quark pair. This puts severe constraints on the dilepton production rate and in particular rules out a strong divergence of the dilepton rate at low energies. The vector spectral function, which has been reconstructed using the Maximum Entropy Method, yields an enhancement of the dilepton rate over the Born rate of at most a factor two in the energy interval 4 < E/T < 8 and suggests that the spectrum is cut-off at low energies by a thermal mass threshold of about (2-3)T.

## Key claim

The thermal vector meson correlation function in quenched QCD above the deconfinement transition deviates by less than 15% from the free quark-antiquark result at 1.5 Tc and 3 Tc, which rules out a strong divergence of the dilepton rate at low energies but does not exclude sharp resonances such as van Hove singularities present in HTL-resummed calculations.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations in the quenched approximation using non-perturbatively improved clover Wilson fermions on lattices of size 48^3 x 12 and 64^3 x 16, with fixed aspect ratio N_sigma / N_tau = 4. They computed Euclidean vector current correlation functions at zero momentum for temperatures T = 1.5 Tc and T = 3 Tc, and reconstructed the vector spectral function using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) with a lattice-adapted kernel that accounts for the finite temporal extent.

## Key numbers

- At T = 1.5 Tc, G_V(1/2T, T) / T^3 = 2.23 ± 0.05; at T = 3 Tc, G_V(1/2T, T) / T^3 = 2.21 ± 0.05, both about 10% larger than the free infinite-volume value of 2.
- The correlation functions at 1.5 Tc and 3 Tc differ from the free correlator by less than 15% at all Euclidean times.
- The reconstructed spectral function yields an enhancement of the dilepton rate over the Born rate of at most a factor two in the energy interval 4 < omega/T < 8.
- The spectral function shows a sharp cut-off at low energies, with the spectrum vanishing below omega/T approximately 3, corresponding to a thermal mass threshold of about (2-3)T.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of thermal vector correlation functions above Tc using improved fermions, providing the first MEM reconstruction of the vector spectral function at finite temperature. The results contradict simple quasi-particle models with only massive poles (Kapusta, Mustafa, Kar01) that predict a reduction of the correlator, and instead support aspects of HTL-resummed perturbation theory (Aurenche, HTL) while ruling out the divergent low-energy behavior predicted by those same perturbative approaches. The findings enable quantitative constraints on thermal dilepton production models for heavy ion collisions and suggest a threshold that may be observable at RHIC and LHC energies.
