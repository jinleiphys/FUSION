# Phase transitions of hadronic to quark matter at finite T and μ_B

> The phase transition of hadronic to quark matter and the boundaries of the mixed hadron-quark coexistence phase are studied within the two Equation of State (EoS) model. The relativistic effective mean field approach with constant and density dependent meson-nucleon couplings is used to describe hadronic matter, and the MIT Bag model is adopted to describe quark matter. The boundaries of the mixed phase for different Bag constants are obtained solving the Gibbs equations. We notice that the dependence on the Bag parameter of the critical temperatures (at zero chemical potential) can be well reproduced by a fermion ultrarelativistic quark gas model, without contribution from the hadron part. At variance the critical chemical potentials (at zero temperature) are very sensitive to the EoS of the hadron sector. Hence the study of the hadronic EoS is much more relevant for the determination of the transition to the quark-gluon-plasma at finite baryon density and low-T. Moreover in the low temperature and finite chemical potential region no solutions of the Gibbs conditions are existing for small Bag constant values, B < (135 MeV)^4. Isospin effects in asymmetric matter appear relevant in the high chemical potential regions at lower temperatures, of interest for the inner core properties of neutron stars and for heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The critical temperature at zero chemical potential depends only on the Bag constant and can be reproduced by a naive ultrarelativistic quark gas model without any hadron contribution, while the critical chemical potential at zero temperature is very sensitive to the equation of state of the hadronic sector and cannot be described by the simple quark gas model.

## Method

The authors used a two-equation-of-state approach with Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium between hadronic and quark matter. Hadronic matter was described using relativistic mean field models with both constant and density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (NL-RMF and DDRH-RMF), while quark matter was described using the MIT Bag model with massless u and d quarks. The boundaries of the mixed hadron-quark coexistence phase were obtained by solving the Gibbs equations for different Bag constants, temperatures, baryon chemical potentials, and isospin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential follows the relation T_c = 0.815 B^(1/4) MeV, with the fit shown for B^(1/4) values in the 160-220 MeV interval
- Critical chemical potential at zero temperature follows the linear fit μ_c = a + b B^(1/4), with a = 680 MeV and b = 5.7, for B^(1/4) in the 160-220 MeV interval
- No solutions of the Gibbs conditions exist at low temperature and finite chemical potential for Bag constant values B < (135 MeV)^4
- The pure ultrarelativistic quark gas model predicts μ_c^B = 6.33 B^(1/4) MeV at zero temperature, which disagrees with the full Gibbs calculation
- Both NL-RMF and DDRH-RMF models give the same critical temperature T_c = 130 MeV for B^(1/4) = 160 MeV
- The squeezing-reopening effect of the binodal surface first disappears for a Bag constant B^(1/4) ≈ 155 MeV

## Context

This work extends the two-equation-of-state approach for hadron-quark phase transitions previously applied to neutron star interiors and heavy ion collisions, building on studies by Muller and collaborators, Di Toro and collaborators (2006-2010), Pagliara (2010), and Cavagnoli (2010). The authors show that the hadronic equation of state is much more relevant for determining the transition at finite baryon density and low temperature, which has implications for neutron star inner core properties and intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions with neutron-rich nuclei. The results also provide a connection to lattice QCD calculations with physical quark masses, suggesting an effective Bag constant around B ≈ (180-190 MeV)^4 at low chemical potential.
