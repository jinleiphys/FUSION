# High-Precision Mass Measurements of Proton-Rich Rh, Pd, Cd isotopes in the vicinity of 100Sn and Impact on X-Ray Burst and Supernova Nucleosynthesis

> Using the ZeroDegree multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrograph of the CRISMASS project at RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory, we performed high-precision mass measurements of proton-rich nuclei near the doubly magic nucleus 100Sn, achieving uncertainties on the order of 10 keV. The masses of 91Rh, 92Pd, and 96Cd were determined for the first time with high precision, and the accuracy of several additional masses was substantially improved. Incorporating the new data into X-ray burst simulations significantly reduces the abundance uncertainties in the $A$ = 90-100 region, shifting the reaction flow toward $A$ = 90 production and suppressing the synthesis of heavier nuclei. Further investigation of the $νp$-process indicates that 99Rh plays a significant role in the reaction flow within the mass region studied. These high-precision mass measurements refine the mass surface near 100Sn and provide critical constraints on models of proton-rich nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The masses of 91Rh, 92Pd, and 96Cd were determined for the first time with uncertainties on the order of 10 keV, and incorporating these new data into X-ray burst simulations significantly reduces abundance uncertainties in the A = 90-100 region; however, the abstract's claim that the reaction flow shifts toward A = 90 production and suppresses heavier nuclei is conditional on the specific one-zone X-ray burst model used, and the impact on the nu p-process is limited to 99Rh playing a significant role only under certain astrophysical conditions (e.g., proto-neutron star masses near 2.0 solar masses).

## Method

The experiment used the ZeroDegree multi-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrograph (ZD-MRTOF-MS) of the CRISMASS project at the RIKEN Radioactive Isotope Beam Factory. A 345 MeV/nucleon 124Xe primary beam at 140 pnA impinged on a 3 mm 9Be target, and projectile-fragmentation products were separated in flight by the BigRIPS separator, transported through the ZeroDegree spectrometer, and injected into the ZD-MRTOF-MS after stopping in a radio-frequency carpet gas catcher and cooling in an ion trap system. Masses were determined using a single-reference method comparing time-of-flight ratios between analyte ions (charge state 2+) and isobaric reference ions (charge state 1+), with a mass resolving power of approximately 6.0 x 10^5.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 91Rh: -58456(8) keV, first precise determination (AME2020 extrapolation was -58570(298) keV)
- Mass excess of 92Pd: -54645(25) keV, first precise determination (AME2020 extrapolation was -54779(345) keV)
- Mass excess of 96Cd: -55540(17) keV, first precise determination (AME2020 extrapolation was -55572(410) keV)
- Mass excess of 96Ag: -64641(7) keV, consistent within 3 sigma of the JYFLTRAP value of -64656.69(95) keV (Ge 2024)
- Mass excess of 99In: -61418(9) keV, consistent with the ISOLTRAP value of -61429(77) keV (Mougeot 2021) with reduced uncertainty
- Mass excess of 93Pd: -59083(8) keV, consistent with the FRS group value of -59127(35) keV (Kripko 2025)
- The 90Ru(p,gamma)91Rh reaction Q value from the new mass is 0.86 MeV, compared to 0.98 MeV from AME2020
- The 99Rh(n,gamma)100Rh reaction rate increased by 2.7% due to the new mass measurement

## Context

This work extends the experimental mass surface near the doubly magic nucleus 100Sn, where previous AME2020 values for many N approximately Z nuclei were based on theoretical estimates or extrapolations from mirror nuclei. The results directly address the sensitivity study by Schatz (2017), which identified mass uncertainties of nuclei such as 91Rh, 93Pd, 94Ag, and 96Ag as significantly affecting X-ray burst light curves and final abundances. The new masses enable more reliable predictions for rp-process nucleosynthesis in Type I X-ray bursts and nu p-process in core-collapse supernovae, and provide benchmarks for investigating shell evolution near 100Sn, with a detailed nuclear-structure discussion to follow in a forthcoming publication.
