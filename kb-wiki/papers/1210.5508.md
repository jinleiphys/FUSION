# Imprinting Quantum Fluctuations on Hydrodynamic Initial Conditions

> Starting from the two-point covariance function derived in [1], we have developed a toy model to investigate the effect of gluonic fluctuations on the transverse energy profile predicted by MC-KLN Color-Glass Condensate initial conditions. We find that the effect of these fluctuations on the eccentricity harmonics varies strongly with the correlation length and consequently the value of the fixed saturation momentum Qs used in [1]. Using approximate values for the minimum and maximum saturation momenta probed at RHIC in Au-Au collisions, we estimate the increase in the eccentricity coefficients resulting from gluonic fluctuations is as large as 10 to 20 percent in central collisions.

# Imprinting Quantum Fluctuations on Hydrodynamic Initial Conditions

## Key claim
Gluonic sub-nucleon fluctuations increase the eccentricity harmonics in central Au-Au collisions by 10 to 20 percent, but this enhancement depends strongly on the gluon correlation length and the assumed saturation momentum; the paper provides both a lower bound (using a constant correlation length of 0.28 fm) and an upper bound (using 0.54 fm) rather than a single definitive value.

## Method
The authors developed a toy model that imprints sub-nucleonic fluctuations onto MC-KLN transverse energy density profiles using a Gaussian random field simulator with a Cauchy covariance function fitted to the Müller-Schäfer two-point covariance. They then mapped the Gaussian field onto a positive-definite negative binomial distribution to avoid unphysical negative energy densities, and multiplied the resulting fluctuation field onto 20,000 MC-KLN Au-Au events at 200 A GeV. The harmonic eccentricity coefficients were computed for both textured and untextured events as functions of participant number.

## Key numbers
- The Cauchy covariance fit parameters are C = 0.4679, a = 0.2878 fm, and b = 1.7732.
- The squared Gaussian width at zero distance is (Δε(0)/ε₀)² = (0.684)².
- For the nominal correlation length a = 0.28 fm, the eccentricity enhancement reaches 5-10% in central collisions.
- For the inflated correlation length a = 0.54 fm (nucleon size), the enhancement reaches 20-25% in central collisions.
- The NBD mapping uses n̄ = 100 and adjusts k such that the variance 1/k + 1/n̄ matches the Gaussian width.
- The Gaussian random field was generated on a 4000 × 4000 lattice with grid spacing Δx = 0.1 fm.
- There is a 7.2% chance of unphysical negative energy densities in the raw Gaussian field.
- The MC-KLN code used Gaussian nucleons of width σ = 0.54 fm.

## Context
This work extends the earlier calculation by Müller and Schäfer (2012) of the two-point covariance function for gluon field fluctuations in central Au-Au collisions, and builds on the MC-KLN Color-Glass Condensate initial condition model. The study qualitatively confirms earlier findings by Tribedy and Venugopalan (2010) and Schenke, Tribedy, and Venugopalan (2012) that sub-nucleonic fluctuations increase eccentricity harmonics, but finds smaller enhancements than reported in some prior work when using realistic correlation lengths. The toy model enables isolating the effect of sub-nucleonic fluctuations from nucleon position fluctuations, which dominate in peripheral collisions, and provides a simpler framework compared to the more realistic IP-Glasma model.
