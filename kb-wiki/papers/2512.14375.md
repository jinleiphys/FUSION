# Signatures of local acceleration of quark-gluon plasma in the dilepton production

> Dilepton production is one of the key probes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) that encodes the imaginary part of the electromagnetic current-current correlator. We investigate the effect of local acceleration on the dilepton production by treating acceleration as a small perturbation. Using the thermal Dirac propagator in an accelerated frame within the imaginary-time formalism, we compute the photon polarization tensor and extract its imaginary part. Comparison with the zero-acceleration case isolates the distinct contributions of acceleration to dilepton yields.

# Signatures of local acceleration of quark-gluon plasma in the dilepton production

## Key claim

Treating acceleration as a small perturbation (α = a/T ≪ 1), the authors show that a weakly accelerated quark-gluon plasma produces fewer low-mass dileptons but more intermediate-mass dileptons compared to the non-accelerated case, with the crossover mass scale depending on temperature but not on the acceleration value itself.

## Method

The authors compute the photon polarization tensor in an accelerated frame using the thermal Dirac propagator within the imaginary-time formalism, expanding perturbatively to first order in the dimensionless acceleration parameter α = a/T. They perform a Wigner transform to handle the loss of translational invariance in the accelerated medium, then extract the imaginary part of the electromagnetic current-current correlator to obtain the dilepton production rate ratio between accelerated and non-accelerated media.

## Key numbers

- For fixed α = 0.03, results are shown at temperatures T = 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6 GeV, corresponding to acceleration values a = 12, 15, and 18 MeV respectively.
- For fixed temperature T = 0.4 GeV, results are shown at α = 0.01, 0.03, and 0.05, corresponding to acceleration values a = 4, 12, and 20 MeV respectively.
- The maximum accelerations considered (20 MeV) are consistent with those explored in recent Lattice QCD studies by Chernodub (2024).
- The calculation uses N_f = 2 quark flavors and N_c colors.

## Context

This work extends the study of extreme conditions in heavy-ion collisions, where acceleration has been argued to be significant in the early stages until the central rapidity plateau forms, following earlier work by Castorina (2007) and Prokhorov (2025). The authors note a parallel behavior in the low-mass region to that seen in a weakly magnetised medium when the Schwinger proper-time propagator is expanded perturbatively in powers of eB by Bandyopadhyay (2017), suggesting the suppression at low masses is generic to weak-field expansions. The results provide input for space-time evolution models of the QGP and enable future extensions to arbitrary acceleration values, polarization effects of the outgoing lepton pair, and spacetime-dependent acceleration profiles.
