# Lepton pair production from a hot and dense QCD medium in the presence of an arbitrary magnetic field

> In this article, we have explored the very important quantity of lepton pair production from a hot and dense QCD medium in presence of an arbitrary external magnetic field for simultaneous nonzero values of both the parallel (along the direction of the external field) and perpendicular (lying on the transverse plane to the external field) components of the dilepton momentum. As opposed to the zero magnetic field case (the so-called Born rate) or the lowest Landau level approximated rate, where only the annihilation process contributes, here we observe contributions also arising out of the quark and antiquark decay processes. We found the encouraging result of considerable enhancement of lepton pair production in presence of an arbitrary magnetic field. We decompose the total rate into different physical processes and discuss their behaviors for both zero and nonzero baryon density. The whole analysis is then subjected to an effective model treatment, where we have incorporated the magnetic field induced novel effects of magnetic catalysis (MC) and inverse MC (IMC) through a medium dependent scalar coupling, which leads to some further interesting observations.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the lepton pair production rate from a hot and dense QCD medium in the presence of an arbitrary magnetic field, extending previous work by allowing both the parallel and perpendicular components of the dilepton momentum to be nonzero simultaneously, and find that the total rate is considerably enhanced compared to the zero-field Born rate, with contributions from quark and antiquark decay processes that are absent in the lowest Landau level approximation.

## Method

The photon self-energy was computed at one-loop order using the Schwinger proper-time propagator for quarks in an arbitrary magnetic field within the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory, with the lepton pairs treated as massless and unaffected by the magnetic field. The total dilepton production rate was decomposed into three physical processes: quark-antiquark annihilation, quark decay, and antiquark decay, and the perpendicular momentum integrals were performed analytically using Laguerre polynomials. The analysis was then repeated within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective model using a medium-dependent scalar coupling \(G_S(eB,T)\) to incorporate both magnetic catalysis (MC) and inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effects.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the dilepton production rate in a magnetic field that were restricted to the strong-field limit (Bandyopadhyay 2016, Bandyopadhyay 2017) or to the case of zero perpendicular momentum \(p_{\perp}=0\) (Ghosh 2018). It also generalizes the Ritus eigenfunction method calculation of Sadooghi (2016) by using the Schwinger proper-time approach and including nonzero \(p_{\perp}\). The inclusion of IMC effects via a medium-dependent coupling in the NJL model (Farias 2016) allows the study of the rate near the chiral crossover temperature, where the effective quark mass shows a mass gap that widens and shifts leftward as \(p_{\perp}\) increases.
