# Nuclear Level Density within Extended Superfluid Model with Collective State Enhancement

> For nuclear level densities, a modification of an enhanced generalized superfluid model with different collective state enhancement factors is studied. An effect of collective states on forming the temperature is taken into account. The ready-to-use tables for the asymptotic value of $a$-parameter of level density as well as for addition shift to excitation energy are prepared using the chi-square fit of the theoretical values of neutron resonance spacing and cumulative number of low-energy levels to experimental values. The systematics of these parameters as a function of mass number and neutron excess are obtained. The collective state effect on gamma-ray spectra and excitation functions of neutron-induced nuclear reactions is investigated by the use of EMPIRE 3.1 code with modified enhanced generalized superfluid model for nuclear level density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modification of the Enhanced Generalized Superfluid Model (EGSM) for nuclear level densities that incorporates different vibrational enhancement factors, with the key hedge being that the BAN (boson partition function with averaged occupation numbers) approach is identified as the most appropriate among the tested methods, but the choice of vibrational enhancement factor significantly affects calculated gamma-ray spectra and excitation functions.

## Method

The authors used the EGSM framework from the EMPIRE 3.1 code to calculate nuclear level densities as a product of intrinsic-rotational level density and a vibrational enhancement factor \(K_{vibr}\). They tested four different vibrational enhancement factor approaches (EM, DN, BAN, and BANT) and performed chi-square fits of theoretical neutron resonance spacings and cumulative numbers of low-lying levels to experimental data for 291 nuclei, extracting the asymptotic \(a\)-parameter \(\tilde a\) and the additional excitation energy shift \(\tilde\delta_{shift}\). Systematics of these parameters as functions of mass number \(A\) and neutron excess \(I=(N-Z)/A\) were derived, and the collective state effects on gamma-ray spectra and excitation functions of neutron-induced reactions on natural iron were calculated.

## Key numbers

* For the systematics of \(\tilde a\): \(\alpha_V = 0.5276\), \(\alpha_{VI} = 4.506\), \(\alpha_S = -1.172\), \(\alpha_{SI} = 13.32\), \(\alpha_C = -0.0447\) (units: MeV\(^{-1}\) for \(\tilde a\) coefficients).
* For the systematics of \(\tilde\delta_{shift}\): \(\delta_1 = 1.372\) MeV, \(\delta_{1I} = -11.75\), \(\delta_2 = 0.000000031\) MeV, \(\delta_{2I} = 76503\), \(\delta_3 = -0.6979\) (dimensionless for coefficients with \(I^2\) terms).
* The average chi-square ratio \(\sum \chi_i^2(K_{vibr}) / \sum \chi_i^2(K_{vibr}=1)\) across three experimental datasets was 1.1 for BAN and 1.0 for BANT, compared to 2.2 for EM and 2.7 for DN.
* Within the BAN approach, \(K_{vibr}(S_n) \sim 2\) to \(5\) for nuclei with mass number \(A \sim 100\).

## Context

This work extends the Enhanced Generalized Superfluid Model (EGSM) of Herman et al. (2007) by testing multiple phenomenological vibrational enhancement factors, including the damped occupation number approach of Ignatyuk et al. (1979, 1993, 2009) and the response function method of Plujko et al. (2005, 2007). The results enable improved nuclear level density calculations for neutron-induced reaction modeling, with the BAN approach showing better agreement with experimental data from Oslo (Agvaanluvsan et al., 2004), Dubna (Sukhovitskii et al., 2007), and Obninsk (Zhuravlev et al., 2009) compared to other vibrational enhancement models.
