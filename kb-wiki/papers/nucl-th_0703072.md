# Isospin symmetry breaking in an algebraic pairing Sp(4) model

> An exactly solvable sp(4) algebraic approach extends beyond the traditional isospin conserving nuclear interaction to bring forward effects of isospin symmetry breaking and isospin mixing resulting from a two-body nuclear interaction that includes proton-neutron (pn) and like-particle isovector pairing correlations plus significant isoscalar pn interactions. The model yields an estimate for the extent to which isobaric analog 0+ states in light and medium mass nuclei may mix with one another and reveals possible, but still extremely weak, non-analog beta-decay transitions.

## Key claim

The paper uses an exactly solvable Sp(4) algebraic model to show that isospin mixing between isobaric analog 0+ states due to a charge-dependent pure nuclear pairing interaction is extremely small (less than 0.17% for single-j shells), but that this mixing may produce non-analog beta-decay transitions that are "possible, but still extremely weak" and not expected to dominate isospin-symmetry breaking corrections in superallowed Fermi decays.

## Method

The authors employ an Sp(4) algebraic model (isomorphic to SO(5)) that extends the isospin-conserving nuclear Hamiltonian to include charge-dependent terms: an isotensor isospin-mixing interaction (parameter F) and an isospin-splitting interaction (parameter D). The model Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis of fully-paired 0+ states for nuclei with valence protons and neutrons occupying the same shell, using Coulomb-corrected experimental energies from a recursion formula based on Pape and Antony (1988). The interaction strength parameters are fixed by fitting to 149 nuclei across three model spaces (1d3/2, 1f7/2, and 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 shells) in the mass range 32 ≤ A ≤ 100.

## Key numbers

- The charge dependence of the pure nuclear interaction in the 1f7/2 level is estimated to be on average ~2.5% when comparing the T0=0 two-body model interaction relative to the T0=1 interaction in T=1 multiplets.
- The isospin mixing between isobaric analog 0+ states in single-j shells is less than 0.17%, with specific overlaps for 48Cr(0) showing 99.83534% T=0 and 0.16465% T=2 admixture.
- The non-analog beta-decay mixing correction δIAS for the strongest decay in the 1f7/2 level (48Mn(2) → 48Cr(0)) is 0.14328%.
- The δIAS correction for the 44V(2) → 44Ti(0) decay is 0.098%, which is around two-thirds of the maximum value.
- The δIAS correction for the 46Mn(3) → 46Cr(1) decay is 0.0169%, which is around one-eighth of the maximum value.
- The smallest δIAS value reported is 4.5 × 10−7% for the 48Mn(4) → 48Cr(0) decay.
- The isospin breaking parameter |D/(E/2Ω)| is 0.090 for the 1d3/2 level, 0.133 for the 1f7/2 level, and 0.628 for the 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 shell.
- The F/Ω parameter is 0.007 for the 1d3/2 level, 0.072 for the 1f7/2 level, and 0.056 for the 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 shell.

## Context

This work extends the Sp(4) algebraic approach developed by Sviratcheva, Georgieva, and Draayer (2003, 2004) for describing isobaric analog 0+ states, and builds on the finding by Zuker, Lenzi, and Martinez-Pinedo (2002) that the isotensor nature of the nuclear interaction is dominated by a J=0 pairing term. The model provides a microscopic estimate for isospin mixing due to charge-dependent pairing correlations that complements phenomenological studies of isobaric multiplet mass equation coefficients and superallowed Fermi beta-decay corrections reviewed by Towner and Hardy (2002, 2003). The results enable identification of specific non-analog beta-decay transitions that may be detectable in future experiments, particularly for N=Z even-even nuclei near the middle of the shell.
