# Investigating the muon catalyzed fusion in muonic lithium hydride

> In this work, we consider the muonic LiH as a new stage of the $μ$ catalyzed fusion. We calculate the bound states of the muonic LiH and LiH$^+$ and their wave functions based on the three and four body approximation. In order to solve the Schr$\ddot{\rm o}$dinger equation, we apply the Gaussian expansion method which gives us both the eigen energies and the wave functions. The existences of the bound states in muonic LiH and LiH$^+$ are confirmed. We find the dynamical behaviors between the $^7$Li and proton in muonic LiH and LiH$^+$ are similar as the ones in dt$μ_2$ and dt$μ$, respectively. The $α$ sticking probability in the muonic LiH$^+$ after the nuclear fusion is studied. The calculated $α$ sticking probability of being captured to the ground state of He$μ$ is 5 times smaller than the one in dt$μ$.

## Key claim

The authors propose muonic lithium hydride (LiH) as an alternative stage for muon-catalyzed fusion, claiming that the alpha sticking probability in muonic LiH⁺ is approximately five times smaller than in the conventional dtμ system. However, they acknowledge that the total improvement to the muon-catalyzed fusion cycle may be marginal because the reaction requires four or three muons and produces two alpha particles, and that formation of muonic LiH or LiH⁺ is a major practical impediment.

## Method

The authors treat muonic LiH as a four-body Liμ₂-p-μ-μ system and muonic LiH⁺ as a three-body Liμ₂-p-μ system, approximating the Liμ₂ complex as a single entity. They solve the Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with Jacobian coordinates and Rayleigh-Ritz variational approach to obtain bound-state energies and wave functions. The alpha sticking probability after fusion is calculated by solving a four-body Aμ₃ system (where A represents the combined Li+p nucleus) and evaluating the overlap integral between the muon wave function and the final-state wave function of the He-μ ground state.

- Binding energy of dtμ₂: 536.8 eV with respect to the tμ-dμ threshold

## Context

This work extends the study of muon-catalyzed fusion beyond the conventional dtμ system investigated by Jackson (1957), Zeldovich (1960), and Kamimura (1988, 1989, 2022), proposing LiH as a new fusion stage to address the alpha sticking problem that limits the number of fusions per muon. The authors compare their results to the dtμ₂ and dtμ systems calculated by Kamimura (2001) and find similar dynamical behaviors between the Li-p and d-t systems. The work enables future investigations into fusion rates in muonic LiH, the competing ⁷Li+p→⁷Be+n reaction channel, and muon transfer reactions between Heμ and Liμ, though the authors note that practical formation of muonic LiH or LiH⁺ remains a significant challenge.
