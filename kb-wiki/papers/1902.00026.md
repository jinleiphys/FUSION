# Spectrum of light- and heavy-baryons

> A symmetry-preserving truncation of the strong-interaction bound-state equations is used to calculate the spectrum of ground-state $J=1/2^+$, $3/2^+$ $(qq^\prime q^{\prime\prime})$-baryons, where $q, q^\prime, q^{\prime\prime} \in \{u,d,s,c,b\}$, their first positive-parity excitations and parity partners. Using two parameters, a description of the known spectrum of 39 such states is obtained, with a mean-absolute-relative-difference between calculation and experiment of 3.6(2.7)%. From this foundation, the framework is subsequently used to predict the masses of 90 states not yet seen empirically.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder truncation of the three-body Faddeev equation with only two parameters, the authors obtain a description of the known spectrum of 39 ground-state and excited baryons with a mean-absolute-relative-difference of 3.6(2.7)% compared to experiment, but the framework produces degenerate masses for the Lambda and Sigma baryons in pure rainbow-ladder truncation, requiring an equal-spacing rule to separate them.

## Method

The authors solve a Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation in rainbow-ladder truncation for three-valence-quark bound states, using a quark-quark scattering kernel defined by an interaction that combines an infrared Gaussian term with a perturbative logarithmic term. Dressed-quark propagators are obtained from the corresponding rainbow-truncated gap equation, with current-quark masses fitted to reproduce the empirical pion and kaon masses for light quarks and to known heavy-quark masses for charm and bottom. For baryons with mixed quark flavours, masses are estimated using an equal-spacing rule based on spectrum-constituent quark masses extracted from the directly computed nucleon and Delta systems with all quarks of a single flavour.

## Key numbers

- Mean-absolute-relative-difference between calculation and experiment for 39 known baryon states: 3.6(2.7)%
- Nucleon mass computed directly: m_N = 0.948 GeV, with sensitivity to omega variation of less than 1%
- Number of baryon states predicted not yet seen empirically: 90
- Light current-quark masses at renormalization scale zeta_19: m_l = 3.3 MeV, m_s = 74.6 MeV
- Charm current-quark mass at zeta_19: m_c = 0.83 GeV
- Bottom current-quark mass at zeta_19: m_b = 3.66 GeV
- Computed pion decay constant: f_pi = 0.094 GeV (experiment: 0.092 GeV)
- Computed rho meson mass: m_rho = 0.75 GeV (experiment: 0.78 GeV)
- Computed Delta baryon mass: m_Delta = 1.210 GeV
- Computed Omega baryon mass: m_Omega = 1.670 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation approach previously applied to nucleon and Delta baryons (Eichmann 2009, Sanchis-Alepuz 2014) and to J^P=3/2^+ baryons with heavy quarks (Qin 2018) to include all ground-state J^P=1/2^+ and 3/2^+ baryons, their first positive-parity excitations, and negative-parity partners. The framework enables predictions for 90 unobserved baryon states and provides a foundation for future studies of baryon structure and transition form factors, complementing lattice QCD results (Durr 2008) and experimental efforts to chart the excited nucleon spectrum using electromagnetic probes.
