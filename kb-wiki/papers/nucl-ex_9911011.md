# Entropy in hot ^{161,162}Dy and ^{171,172}Yb nuclei

> The density of accessible levels at low spin in the (^3He,αγ) reaction has been extracted for the ^{161,162}Dy and ^{171,172}Yb nuclei. The entropy of the even-odd and even-even nuclei has been deduced as a function of excitation energy, and found to reach a maximum of 15 k_B before neutron evaporation. The entropy of one quasi-particle outside an even-even core is found to be 1.70(15) k_B. This quasi-particle picture of hot nuclei is well accounted for within a simple pairing model. The onset of two, four and six quasi-particle excitations in the ^{162}Dy and ^{172}Yb nuclei is discussed and compared to theory. The number of quasi-particles excited per excitation energy is a measure for the ratio of the level energy spacing and the pairing strength.

## Key claim

The entropy of hot nuclei in the rare-earth region reaches a maximum of approximately 15 k_B just before neutron evaporation, and the entropy excess of an even-odd nucleus relative to its even-even neighbor is about 1.7 k_B, interpreted as the entropy of a single quasi-particle. This quasi-particle picture holds within a simple pairing model, but the experimental onset of two, four, and six quasi-particle excitations is smeared out in excitation energy and does not show clear step-like behavior.

## Method

The authors used the (³He,αγ) reaction with 45 MeV ³He projectiles on self-supporting targets of ¹⁶²,¹⁶³Dy and ¹⁷²,¹⁷³Yb, detecting α-particles with Si telescopes and γ-rays with the CACTUS NaI array. From the coincident data, they constructed a matrix of excitation energy versus γ-ray energy, unfolded the NaI spectra, and extracted the first-generation γ-ray matrix using a subtraction technique. The level density ρ(E) was then obtained by factorizing the primary γ-ray matrix according to the Brink-Axel hypothesis, with the normalization fixed by known discrete levels at low energy and neutron-resonance spacings at the neutron binding energy. The entropy was computed in the microcanonical ensemble as S(E) = k_B ln[ρ(E)/ρ₀], and a simple pairing Hamiltonian with equidistant single-particle levels was solved exactly for N=11 and N=12 particles to compare with experiment.

## Key numbers

- Maximum entropy before neutron evaporation: S ≈ 15 k_B (extrapolated to the neutron binding energy B_n for ¹⁶¹,¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁷¹,¹⁷²Yb).
- Entropy of one quasi-particle outside an even-even core: 1.70(15) k_B.
- Entropy excess ΔS in the 1.5–5.5 MeV excitation region: ~1.8(1) k_B for dysprosium isotopes and ~1.6(1) k_B for ytterbium isotopes.
- Critical temperature for pair correlations: k_B T_c ≈ 0.5 MeV.
- Excitation energy per broken quasi-particle pair: 1.6 MeV (experimental average over 0.5–5 MeV), compared to 1.7 MeV from the theoretical model with δ = 0.5.
- Maximum number of excited quasi-particles: n ≈ 6 at an excitation energy of 5.5 MeV in ¹⁶²Dy and ¹⁷²Yb.
- Spin distribution of the reaction: ⟨J⟩ ≈ 4.4 ħ with standard deviation σ_J ≈ 2.4 ħ.

## Context

This work extends the Oslo group's method for extracting nuclear level densities from γ-ray spectra (Melby et al., 1999; Schiller et al., 1999) to deduce entropy and quasi-particle number in hot rare-earth nuclei. The results are compared to a simple pairing model solved exactly for small particle numbers, and to earlier theoretical work by Døssing et al. (1995) using BCS-based approaches. The finding that pairing correlations become negligible above k_B T ≈ 0.5 MeV agrees with the critical temperature reported by Schiller et al. (1999). The quasi-particle picture, successful for rotational bands in cold nuclei, is shown to describe thermodynamic properties of hot nuclei, enabling future modeling of nuclei up to high intrinsic energy with multiple quasi-particle excitations.
