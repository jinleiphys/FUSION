# Is the Chiral Magnetic Effect fast enough?

> It depends: While we find within holography that the lifetime of the magnetic field for collider energies like the ones achieved at RHIC is long enough to build up the chiral magnetic current, the lifetime of the magnetic field at LHC seems to be too short. We study the real time evolution of the chiral magnetic effect out-of-equilibrium in strongly coupled holographic gauge theories. We consider the backreaction of the magnetic field onto the geometry and monitor pressure and chiral magnetic current. Our findings show that generically at small magnetic field the pressure builds up faster than the chiral magnetic current whereas at strong magnetic field the opposite is true. At large charge we also find that equilibration is delayed significantly due to long lived oscillations. We also match the parameters of our model to QCD parameters and draw lessons of possible relevance to the realization of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions. In particular, we find an equilibration time of about $\sim0.35$ fm/c in presence of the chiral anomaly for plasma temperatures of order $T\sim300-400$ MeV.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) can build up fast enough to be observable at RHIC energies, but the magnetic field lifetime at LHC energies appears too short for the CME current to fully develop, based on holographic modeling of strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma with full backreaction of the magnetic field on the geometry.

## Method

The authors study the real-time evolution of the CME out of equilibrium using a holographic bottom-up model with a $U(1)_A \times U(1)_V$ symmetry, including a Chern-Simons term to implement the mixed $VAA$ anomaly. They solve the Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations in a five-dimensional asymptotically AdS spacetime with infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, starting from an initial state with vanishing CME current and vanishing dynamical pressure anisotropy, then monitoring the time evolution of the vector current and pressure anisotropy for varying magnetic field strength, Chern-Simons coupling, and axial charge density.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time of about $\sim 0.35$ fm/c in the presence of the chiral anomaly for plasma temperatures of order $T \sim 300-400$ MeV
- Chern-Simons coupling matched to three-flavor QCD gives $\alpha = 6/19 \approx 0.316$
- Holographic Newton constant matched to QCD entropy gives $\kappa^2 = (24\pi^2)/19 \approx 12.5$
- For RHIC parameters: temperature $T = 300$ MeV, magnetic field $B = m_\pi^2$, axial chemical potential $\mu_5 = 10$ MeV
- For LHC parameters: temperature $T = 1000$ MeV, magnetic field $B = 15 m_\pi^2$, axial chemical potential $\mu_5 = 10$ MeV
- At small magnetic field the pressure builds up faster than the chiral magnetic current; at strong magnetic field the opposite is true
- At large axial charge density ($q_5 = 1.5$), equilibration is delayed significantly due to long-lived oscillations

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of out-of-equilibrium chiral transport by Lin (2013), Ammon (2016), Grieninger (2017), Haack (2018), and Cartwright (2020), and specifically builds on the Vaidya-type metric approach of Landsteiner (2017), Fernandez-Pendas (2019), and Morales-Tejera (2020) by including the full backreaction of the magnetic field onto the geometry. The pressure anisotropy evolution without anomaly effects was previously studied by Fuini (2015). The results enable quantitative estimates of whether the CME can be observed in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, and provide a framework for matching holographic parameters to QCD observables.
