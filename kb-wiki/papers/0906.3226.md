# Treatment of the proton-proton Coulomb force in proton-deuteron breakup Faddeev calculations

> We extend our approach to incorporate the proton-proton (pp) Coulomb force into the three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev calculations from elastic proton-deuteron (pd) scattering to the breakup process. The main new ingredient is a 3-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix obtained by a numerical solution of the 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation. We demonstrate numerically that the proton-deuteron breakup observables can be determined from the resulting on-shell 3N amplitudes increasing the screening radius. However, contrary to the pd elastic scattering, the screening limit exists only after renormalisation of the pp t-matrices.

# Treatment of the proton-proton Coulomb force in proton-deuteron breakup Faddeev calculations

## Key claim

The authors extend their existing approach for incorporating the screened proton-proton (pp) Coulomb force into three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev calculations from elastic proton-deuteron (pd) scattering to the breakup process, demonstrating numerically that breakup observables can be obtained by increasing the screening radius. However, contrary to elastic pd scattering where no renormalisation is needed, the screening limit for breakup exists only after renormalisation of the pp t-matrices, specifically the half-shell t-matrices acquire an infinitely oscillating phase factor that must be removed.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equation in momentum space using a partial wave basis, distinguishing between states with total two-nucleon angular momentum j below some j_max (where both nuclear and screened Coulomb interactions act) and states with j above j_max (where only the screened Coulomb force acts). The screened pp Coulomb t-matrix is treated in 3 dimensions without partial wave decomposition, obtained by numerical solution of the 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The coupled set of integral equations is solved by iteration and Pade summation for off-shell Jacobi momenta, then on-shell Faddeev amplitudes are obtained through an additional equation that requires half-shell t-matrix elements, which must be renormalised by removing the oscillating phase factor e^{-i Phi_R(p)}.

## Key numbers

- Screening radii used: R = 20, 40, 60, 80, 100, 120, and 140 fm for generating partial-wave decomposed t-matrices
- For exponential screening with n=4, screening radius R=20 fm requires all l <= l_max=10 partial wave states to reproduce the 3-dimensional pp screened Coulomb t-matrix at E_p^{lab}=13 MeV; increasing to R=120 fm requires l_max approximately 50
- Convergence in screening radius for QFS and SST configurations is achieved at R=60 fm
- Total angular momenta of the p-p-n system up to J <= 15/2 and both parities were included
- The off-shell screened Coulomb pp t-matrix reaches the pure Coulomb screening limit without renormalisation for screening radii R > 20 fm
- The partial wave decomposed half-shell t-matrices require renormalisation and converge for screening radii R > 40 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for elastic pd scattering (Witala et al., 2008) to the breakup reaction, addressing limitations of earlier calculations by Deltuva et al. (2005) that used partial wave decomposition for the screened Coulomb force. Those earlier calculations found negligible Coulomb effects for the pd space-star (SST) configuration and only slight improvement for pp quasi-free-scattering (QFS) data, motivating the present 3-dimensional treatment. The approach enables future studies of pd breakup with modern nuclear forces including three-nucleon forces, where the perturbative approximation for the 3-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix can be avoided and higher partial wave components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction can be included.
