# Relativistic Quantum Transport Theory for Electrodynamics

> We investigate the relationship between the covariant and the three-dimensional (equal-time) formulations of quantum kinetic theory. We show that the three-dimensional approach can be obtained as the energy average of the covariant formulation. We illustrate this statement in scalar and spinor QED. For scalar QED we derive Lorentz covariant transport and constraint equations directly from the Klein-Gordon equation rather than through the previously used Feshbach-Villars representation. We then consider pair production in a spatially homogeneous but time-dependent electric field and show that the pair density is derived much more easily via the energy averaging method than in the equal-time representation. Proceeding to spinor QED, we derive the covariant version of the equal-time equation derived by Bialynicki-Birula et al. We show that it must be supplemented by another self-adjoint equation to obtain a complete description of the covariant spinor Wigner operator. After spinor decomposition and energy average we study the classical limit of the resulting three-dimensional kinetic equations. There are only two independent spinor components in this limit, the mass density and the spin density, and we derive also their covariant equations of motion. We then show that the equal-time kinetic equation provides a complete description only for constant external electromagnetic fields, but is in general incomplete. It must be supplemented by additional constraints which we derive explicitly from the covariant formulation.

## Key claim

The three-dimensional (equal-time) formulation of quantum kinetic theory can be obtained as the energy average of the covariant formulation, and this energy averaging method provides a complete description that reveals the equal-time kinetic equation is incomplete for general time-dependent external electromagnetic fields, requiring additional constraints derived from the covariant formulation.

## Method

The authors derive Lorentz covariant transport and constraint equations for scalar QED directly from the Klein-Gordon equation, then perform a semiclassical expansion in ℏ to obtain Vlasov-type transport equations. For spinor QED, they derive the covariant version of the equal-time equation from Bialynicki-Birula et al. (1991) and show it must be supplemented by another self-adjoint equation for completeness. They then perform spinor decomposition and energy averaging to obtain three-dimensional kinetic equations and study their classical limit.

## Key numbers

- The scalar Wigner function is expanded in powers of ℏ as W₄ = W₄⁽⁰⁾ + ℏW₄⁽¹⁾ + ℏ²W₄⁽²⁾ + ...
- The zeroth-order constraint equation gives the classical mass-shell condition (p² - m²)W₄⁽⁰⁾(x,p) = 0
- Quantum corrections to the scalar Wigner function appear only at second order in ℏ, while in spinor QED they arise at first order in ℏ due to spin interactions
- The asymptotic pair density in phase-space for scalar particles in a time-dependent electric field is n_pair(t=∞) = r/(1-r), where r = |C₂|²/|C₁|² is the transmission coefficient from the oscillator problem
- The total particle density at t=∞ is n(t=∞) = (1+r)/(1-r) = 1 + 2r/(1-r), where the first term is a vacuum contribution requiring renormalization

## Context

This work extends the covariant quantum transport theory developed by Vasak, Gyulassy and Elze (1987) for spinor electrodynamics and the equal-time transport equation proposed by Bialynicki-Birula, Gornicki and Rafelski (1991). It also builds on the extension of the equal-time method to scalar electrodynamics in the Feshbach-Villars representation by Best and Eisenberg. The paper shows that the equal-time kinetic equation provides a complete description only for constant external electromagnetic fields, and the energy averaging method enables a simpler derivation of pair production results compared to the Feshbach-Villars approach, while also providing a systematic framework for studying quantum corrections to transport equations.
