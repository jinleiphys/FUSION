# Cross Sections for pi- and rho-induced Dissociation of J/psi and psi'

> We evaluate the cross sections for the dissociation of $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ by $π$ and $ρ$ at low collision energies, using the quark-interchange model of Barnes and Swanson. The dissociation cross section for $J/ψ$ by $π$ is found to be relatively small with a maximum of about 1 mb and a kinetic energy threshold of 0.65 GeV. The pion-induced $ψ'$ dissociation cross section is found to be much larger, with a maximum of about 5 mb and a lower threshold. Dissociation cross sections for $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ by $ρ$ mesons are also evaluated and are found to be large near threshold.

# Cross Sections for π- and ρ-induced Dissociation of J/ψ and ψ'

## Key claim
The quark-interchange model yields dissociation cross sections for J/ψ and ψ' by π and ρ mesons that differ significantly from prior calculations, with π+J/ψ dissociation being small (about 1 mb maximum) while ρ+J/ψ and ρ+ψ' cross sections are large and diverge at threshold. The results are subject to systematic uncertainties from the post-prior ambiguity and parameter variations, indicated as bands in the figures.

## Method
The authors employed the Barnes-Swanson quark-interchange model, using a nonrelativistic quark potential Hamiltonian with parameters fixed by fits to the meson spectrum. Scattering amplitudes were calculated at Born order as overlap integrals of quark model wavefunctions, summing four quark line diagrams (transfer and capture diagrams) for each channel. The mean of the post and prior forms of the scattering amplitude was taken as the theoretical cross section, with the spread between them and parameter variations providing error estimates.

## Key numbers
- π+J/ψ dissociation: maximum cross section of about 1 mb, with a kinetic energy threshold of 0.65 GeV
- π+ψ' dissociation: maximum cross section of about 6.2(0.8) mb at kinetic energy of about 0.1 GeV, with a secondary maximum of 4.6(1.8) mb at about 0.22 GeV
- ρ+J/ψ dissociation: total cross section of about 11(3) mb at kinetic energy of 0.1 GeV, decreasing to 6(2) mb at 0.2 GeV
- ρ+ψ' dissociation: total cross section decreases from 15(2) mb to 6(2) mb as kinetic energy increases from 0.1 to 0.2 GeV
- Model parameters: α_s = 0.58, b = 0.18 GeV², σ = 0.897 GeV, m_u = m_d = 0.345 GeV, m_c = 1.931 GeV, V_con = -0.612 GeV (primary set); alternative set: α_s = 0.594, b = 0.162 GeV², m_u = m_d = 0.335 GeV, m_c = 1.6 GeV

## Context
This work extends the quark-interchange model previously applied to I=2 ππ scattering (Barnes and Swanson 1992), I=3/2 Kπ, and KN scattering to charmonium dissociation processes relevant for interpreting J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions. The results contradict the much larger π+J/ψ cross section (about 7 mb) found by Martins, Blaschke, and Quack (1995) using a similar approach but with a simplified Gaussian confining potential and neglecting important destructive interference between transfer and capture diagrams. The calculations also differ from the parton model results of Kharzeev and Satz (1994, 1996) which gave very small cross sections, and from the meson exchange models of Matinyan and Muller (1998) and Haglin (1999) which gave results sensitive to form factor choices. The large ρ-induced cross sections imply that ρ mesons in the medium could rapidly dissociate charmonium, which should be incorporated in Monte Carlo simulations of Pb+Pb collisions before drawing conclusions about quark-gluon plasma signatures.
