# Exclusive $K^+$ production in proton-nucleus collisions

> The exclusive $K^+$ meson production in a proton-nucleus collision, leading to two body final states, is investigated in a fully covariant two-nucleon model based on the effective Lagrangian picture. The explicit kaon production vertex is described via creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710) and $N^*$(1720) intermediate baryonic states in the initial collision of the projectile nucleon with one of its target counterparts which is modeled by the one-pion exchange process. The calculated cross sections show strong sensitivity to the medium effects on pion propagator and to the final hypernuclear state excited in the reaction.

## Key claim

The exclusive $(p,K^+)$ reaction on a $^{40}$Ca target is described within a fully covariant two-nucleon model, but the calculated cross sections are exploratory because plane waves are used for the incoming proton and outgoing kaon, and distortion effects are omitted.

## Method

The authors model kaon production via one-pion exchange between the projectile proton and a bound target nucleon, exciting intermediate $N^*$(1650), $N^*$(1710), and $N^*$(1720) baryonic resonances that decay into a kaon and a $\Lambda$ hyperon. Bound-state baryon spinors are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with scalar and vector Woods-Saxon potentials, with depths adjusted to reproduce binding energies from density-dependent relativistic hadron field theory. The pion propagator includes medium effects via a complex self-energy with particle-hole and delta-hole excitations renormalized by a Landau-Migdal parameter $g^\prime$.

## Key numbers

- Peak differential cross section near $10^\circ$ is about 0.1 nb/sr for excitation of the $^{41}_\Lambda$Ca($0d_{3/2}$) hypernuclear state at a beam energy of 2.0 GeV.
- The $N^*$(1710) resonance dominates the total cross section at 2.0 GeV beam energy.
- The momentum density of the $\Lambda$ hyperon in the $0p_{3/2}$ state of $^{41}_\Lambda$Ca is of order $10^0$ at momentum 0.35 GeV/c, compared to $10^{-8}$ to $10^{-9}$ at 1.0 GeV/c.
- The pion self-energy is calculated at zero energy for a Fermi momentum of 230 MeV/c, with magnitudes decreasing as $g^\prime$ increases from 0 to 0.7.

## Context

This work extends the covariant two-nucleon model previously applied to elementary $pp \to pK^+\Lambda$ reactions by Shyam (1999) to the nuclear $(p,K^+)$ reaction on $^{40}$Ca, which had only been treated in non-relativistic frameworks by Shyam (1986), Komarov (1995), and Krimer (1995). The results show strong sensitivity to medium effects on the pion propagator, suggesting that $(p,K^+)$ reactions could probe pion propagation in nuclei, though definitive conclusions require including distortion effects and heavy meson exchange diagrams.
