# Partial conservation of seniority in nuclei

> A review is given of the relation between pairing, quasi-spin algebras and seniority. The former two concepts are closely connected, the relation being that the quasi-spin formalism allows an efficient solution of the pairing problem. Seniority is of much wider applicability than either pairing or quasi-spin and applications can even be extended to encompass the notion of partial symmetry. A recent application of partial conservation of seniority and its importance for nuclear isomers is discussed.

## Key claim

Seniority is conserved exactly for two specific states (a \(J=4\) and a \(J=6\) state) in a system of four identical fermions in a \(j=9/2\) shell for *any* arbitrary two-body interaction, not just for a pairing interaction; this partial conservation is crucial for explaining the existence of seniority isomers in semi-magic nuclei.

## Method

The paper reviews the group-theoretical connection between pairing, quasi-spin algebras (Sp(2) for identical nucleons, Sp(4) for neutrons and protons), and seniority, deriving eigenvalue expressions for the pairing interaction. It then presents the necessary and sufficient conditions for a general two-body interaction to conserve seniority (Eq. 4), and identifies a non-trivial case of partial seniority conservation in the \((9/2)^4\) system by constructing Gram-Schmidt orthonormalized wavefunctions that are pre-determined and have exact seniority \(v=4\) for \(J=4\) and \(J=6\). The paper applies this result to shell-model calculations of \(N=50\) isotones and nickel isotopes, comparing calculated spectra to observed levels.

## Key numbers

- For four identical fermions with \(j=9/2\), the \(J=4\) state with exact seniority \(v=4\) has eigenenergy \(E = \frac{68}{33}\nu_2 + \nu_4 + \frac{13}{15}\nu_6 + \frac{114}{55}\nu_8\) (Eq. 7).
- For four identical fermions with \(j=9/2\), the \(J=6\) state with exact seniority \(v=4\) has eigenenergy \(E = \frac{19}{11}\nu_2 + \frac{12}{13}\nu_4 + \nu_6 + \frac{336}{143}\nu_8\) (Eq. 7).
- In \(^{94}\)Ru (\(Z=44\)), the observed \(4^+_2\) and \(6^+_2\) levels (candidates for exact \(v=4\) states) lie at 2503 keV and 2818 keV, respectively; the calculation gives 2422 keV and 2828 keV.
- The \(J^\pi=8^+\) levels in \(^{94}\)Ru and \(^{96}\)Pd have half-lives of 71 \(\mu\)s and 2.2 \(\mu\)s, respectively.

## Context

This review extends the classic work on seniority by Racah (1943, 1949) and the quasi-spin formulation by Kerman (1961) and Helmers (1961), and builds on the discovery by Escuderos and Zamick (2006) of a non-trivial partial seniority conservation in the \(j=9/2\) shell. The paper shows that this partial symmetry explains the existence of seniority isomers in semi-magic nuclei such as the \(N=50\) isotones (reviewed by Grawe et al., 1997), and clarifies why similar isomers are not observed in neutron-rich nickel isotopes like \(^{72}\)Ni and \(^{74}\)Ni (Sawicka et al., 2003). The results enable a simple analytic condition for isomer existence, governed primarily by the quadrupole pairing matrix element \(\nu_2\).
