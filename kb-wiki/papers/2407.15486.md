# Average speed of sound in neutron stars

> The structure of the dense-matter equation of state is essential for the phenomenology of neutron stars. In this work, I relate the thermodynamic properties to the average speed of sound in the interior of a star. I study the consequences of the vanishing of the trace anomaly. In particular, I show that if the trace anomaly vanishes in the centers of maximally massive neutron stars, the speed of sound likely exceeds its conformal value and exhibits nonmonotonic behavior. I also find that the additional assumption of positive definiteness of the trace anomaly naturally induces a local peak of the speed of sound at densities realized in the cores of neutron stars. I also reanalyze the stability condition for hybrid neutron stars. Possible implications for the dense matter equation of state and the phenomenology of neutron stars are also discussed.

## Key claim
If the trace anomaly vanishes in the centers of maximally massive neutron stars, the speed of sound likely exceeds its conformal value of 1/3 and exhibits nonmonotonic behavior; the additional assumption of positive definiteness of the trace anomaly naturally induces a local peak of the speed of sound at densities realized in neutron star cores.

## Method
The author relates the speed of sound and the trace anomaly to the average speed of sound (defined as the ratio of pressure to energy density) using thermodynamic identities. Low-density constraints from chiral effective field theory at N3LO order are combined with recent estimates of the central energy density of maximally massive neutron stars to derive bounds on the average speed of sound between the low-density regime and the density where the trace anomaly vanishes. The stability condition for hybrid neutron stars after a first-order phase transition is reanalyzed in terms of the average speed of sound.

## Key numbers
- The maximal central energy density of neutron stars at the TOV limit is eps_TOV = 1.073 (+0.267, -0.202) GeV/fm^3 at 95% confidence level (Marczenko 2023).
- The trace anomaly at the TOV limit is Delta_TOV = -0.02 +/- 0.03 (0.1) at 68% (95%) confidence level (Marczenko 2023).
- Using the low-density constraint at eps_l = 0.3 GeV/fm^3 (2.0 eps_sat) with average speed of sound csavg_l = 0.047-0.077, the average speed of sound between eps_l and eps_c is alpha = 0.41-0.49 when eps_c matches the TOV central density.
- The minimal value of csavg_c for which alpha >= 1/3 is csavg_c >= 0.26 at 95% confidence level, corresponding to Delta_c <= 0.07.
- The lowest energy density at which the trace anomaly can vanish under causality is eps_c ~ 0.42 GeV/fm^3.
- For unstable hybrid neutron stars after a first-order phase transition, the condition csavg_0 < (2/3) * csavg_t / (1 + csavg_t) applies; this region corresponds to roughly 41% of all thermodynamically allowed combinations of csavg_t and csavg_0.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Fujimoto (2022) connecting the speed of sound to the trace anomaly, and builds on estimates of central energy densities and trace anomaly values from Marczenko (2023). The analysis uses low-density constraints from chiral effective field theory calculations by Drischler (2020) and the concept of average speed of sound developed by Saes (2021, 2024). The results confirm that large speed of sound is a consequence of conformal symmetry restoration in heavy neutron star cores, and the stability condition for hybrid stars reanalyzes the criterion from Alford (2013). The work enables further constraints on the dense matter equation of state from the interplay between trace anomaly positivity and neutron star phenomenology.
