# Inhomogeneous Phases in the Chiral Gross-Neveu Model on the Lattice

> We discuss possible existence of inhomogeneous phase in low temperature and high density region in the 1+1 dimensional chiral Gross-Neveu ( $χ$GN$_2$ ) model on the lattice. First we investigate the phase structure of the $χ$GN$_2$ model at vanishing chemical potential, changing temperature. From behavior of $Δ^2 = Σ^2 + Π^2$ as a function of Monte Carlo time, a candidate of an order parameter for chiral symmetry in the $χ$GN$_2$ model is $Δ$. At vanishing chemical potential, we observe restoration of chiral symmetry at high $T$. In low temperature and high chemical potential region, we find existence of inhomogeneous phase in spatial correlation functions of $σ$ and $π$. The signal of inhomogeneous phase in correlation function of $Δ$ is more clearly than that of $σ$ or $π$.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice evidence for an inhomogeneous phase in the 1+1 dimensional chiral Gross-Neveu model at low temperature and high chemical potential, but the authors caution that the current lattice size (spatial extent \(N_s = 32\)) may be too small to fully resolve the spatial structure, and further investigation with smaller lattice spacing and larger lattice size is needed for conclusive results.

## Method

The authors perform Monte Carlo simulations of the \(\chi\)GN\(_2\) model on a lattice with naive fermions, using auxiliary scalar fields \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\) introduced via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. They generate ensembles of field configurations at fixed spatial extent \(N_s = 32\) and varying temporal extent \(N_t = 4, 6, \ldots, 32\) (corresponding to varying temperature), with three different coupling constants \(g^2 = 1.7132, 1.8132, 1.9332\) and chemical potentials \(\mu/\Delta_0 = 0.0\) and \(0.8886\). They analyze spatial correlation functions \(C_{\sigma\sigma}(x)\), \(C_{\pi\pi}(x)\), and \(C_{\Delta^*\Delta}(x)\) to search for oscillatory behavior indicative of inhomogeneous phases.

## Key numbers

- At vanishing chemical potential, chiral symmetry restoration begins at \(T/\Delta_0 \sim 0.09\) for \(N_f = 8\) with \(g^2 = 1.7132\), \(1.8132\), and \(1.9332\).
- The lattice spacing parameter \(a\Delta_0\) takes values \(0.4153(3)\), \(0.3791(2)\), and \(0.3436(2)\) for \(g^2 = 1.9332\), \(1.8132\), and \(1.7132\), respectively.
- Inhomogeneous phase signals are observed at \((T/\Delta_0, \mu/\Delta_0) = (0.1204, 0.8886)\) and \((0.1003, 0.8886)\).
- The amplitude of the correlation function of \(\Delta\) is approximately twice as large as those of \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\) at the same parameters.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of the Gross-Neveu model by Lenz et al. (2020) to the chiral Gross-Neveu model, which includes both scalar and pseudoscalar four-fermion interactions. The authors compare their results to the large \(N_f\) limit calculations by Schon and Thies (2000) and Basar and Dunne (2009), finding that even at \(N_f = 8\) the behavior is consistent with the large \(N_f\) limit. The study enables future lattice investigations of the QCD phase diagram at high density, particularly for inhomogeneous chiral condensation, and suggests that the \(\Delta\) field provides a clearer signal than \(\sigma\) or \(\pi\) individually.
