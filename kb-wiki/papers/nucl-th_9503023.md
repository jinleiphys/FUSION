# The three-baryon $ΛNN$ potential

> Using a three-baryon $ΛNN$ potential having a spin-independent dispersive and a two-pion exchange components, realistic variational Monte Carlo calculations have been performed for the $Λ$-seperation energy for $^{5}_Λ$He. A relationship between strengths associated with both pieces of $ΛNN$ force giving exact experimental $Λ$-seperation energy has been obtained which then combined with our previous study on $^{17}_{\,\,Λ}$O hypernucleus determines a unique and acceptable set of strength parameters for $ΛNN$ potential. The $ΛNN$ force is found important for the core polarization.

## Key claim

Using variational Monte Carlo calculations for the Lambda separation energy in 5-Lambda-He, the author finds that a unique set of strength parameters for the three-baryon Lambda-NN potential can be determined by combining constraints from both 5-Lambda-He and 17-Lambda-O hypernuclei, but only when the dispersive and two-pion exchange components satisfy a specific relationship that yields the exact experimental separation energy.

## Method

The author performed variational Monte Carlo calculations for 5-Lambda-He using a three-baryon Lambda-NN potential composed of a spin-independent dispersive component and a two-pion exchange component, with the Cluster Monte Carlo technique extended from previous work on 17-Lambda-O. The variational wavefunction included two-body NN, three-body NNN, Lambda-N, and Lambda-NN correlations under the independent triplet approximation, using the Argonne v14 NN potential and Urbana model VII NNN potential. The author varied the strength parameters W0 (dispersive) and Cp (two-pion exchange) across multiple combinations, computed the resulting Lambda separation energies, and fit these to a quadratic functional form to obtain a relationship between W0 and Cp that reproduces the experimental separation energy.

## Key numbers

- Experimental Lambda separation energy for 5-Lambda-He: 3.12 +/- 0.02 MeV (from Juric et al.)
- Calculated Lambda separation energy with the newly determined potential: 3.1 +/- 0.1 MeV
- Determined strength parameters: W0 = 0.015 MeV, Cp = 0.67 MeV
- Core energy of 4-He with Lambda-NN potential: -23.4 +/- 0.2 MeV; without Lambda-NN potential: -21.0 +/- 0.2 MeV (distortion of 2.4 MeV)
- 4-He energy calculated with first six operators of Argonne v14 potential: 31.1 +/- 0.1 MeV
- Two-pion exchange contribution with non-central correlations: -2.65 +/- 0.05 MeV; without non-central correlations: 0.19 +/- 0.03 MeV
- For W0 = Cp = 0, B_Lambda(5-Lambda-He) = 4.8 +/- 0.1 MeV; for W0 = 0.016, Cp = 0.7, B_Lambda = 2.9 +/- 0.1 MeV
- Fitting constants for B_Lambda functional form: a = 4.8 MeV, b = 2.45, c = 2.5 MeV^-1, d = -291

## Context

This work extends the Cluster Monte Carlo technique previously applied to 17-Lambda-O by Usmani, Pieper, and Usmani (in press) to the lighter hypernucleus 5-Lambda-He, enabling a systematic study of the A-dependence of the Lambda-NN potential expectation value. The results contradict earlier determinations of the Lambda-NN potential using central correlations by Bodmer and Usmani (1988), showing that operatorial correlations completely change the previously determined potential parameters. The newly determined potential enables future calculations of Lambda spin-orbit splittings in excited states of hypernuclei such as 16-Lambda-O, where the Lambda occupies a p-shell with J=3/2 and 1/2 states, and ongoing studies of 6-Lambda-Li, 6-Lambda-He, and 16-Lambda-O are in progress.
