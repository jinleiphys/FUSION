# Mass structure and pressure forces inside the nucleon

> We summarize recent works on the question of the nucleon mass decomposition and the 2D relativistic distribution of pressure forces on the light front. All these mechanical properties are encoded in the energy-momentum tensor of the system which can be constrained using various types of high-energy lepton-nucleon scatterings. Some further developments for targets with spin $>1/2$ are also reported.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the common interpretation of the QCD trace anomaly as indicating gluons dominate the nucleon mass is incorrect; the large gluon contribution actually arises from a large negative gluon pressure-volume work, reflecting the relativistic nature of the nucleon, and a proper mass decomposition must separate energy contributions from pressure-volume work.

## Method

The author analyzes the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) of the nucleon using Poincaré symmetry and generalized parton distributions (GPDs), which give access to gravitational form factors (GFFs). For spin-1/2 targets, the matrix element of the partial (quark or gluon) EMT is expressed in terms of GFFs \(A_{q,G}(t)\) and \(\bar C_{q,G}(t)\), with the \(\bar C\) term accounting for non-conservation due to pressure-volume work. Transverse 2D spatial distributions of pressure forces are defined in the symmetric Drell-Yan frame using light-front formalism, avoiding relativistic corrections that plague 3D Breit-frame distributions.

## Key numbers

- Quark energy contribution: \(U_q \approx 0.44 M\) (where \(M\) is the nucleon mass).
- Quark pressure-volume work: \(W_q \approx 0.11 M\).
- Gluon trace anomaly contribution: \(\langle P|\frac{\beta(g)}{2g} G^2|P\rangle / 2M = U_G - 3W_G \approx 0.89 M\).
- The sum of quark and gluon pressure-volume works vanishes: \(W_q + W_G = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nucleon mass decomposition by Ji (1994) and Lorcé (2017), correcting misinterpretations of the QCD trace anomaly. It builds on the framework of GPDs and GFFs developed by Diehl (2003), Belitsky (2005), and Kumerički (2016), and on the 3D pressure distribution formalism of Polyakov (2002). The results enable a tomographic understanding of the origin of mass, spin, and pressure forces inside the nucleon, and provide new constraints for confinement mechanisms. Future work includes further constraints from exclusive high-energy experiments and Lattice QCD, as well as extensions to higher-spin targets like the spin-1 case studied by Cosyn (2019).
