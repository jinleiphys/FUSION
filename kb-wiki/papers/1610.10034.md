# Holographic QCD predictions for production and decay of pseudoscalar glueballs

> The top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model for low-energy QCD, augmented by finite quark masses, has recently been found to be able to reproduce the decay pattern of the scalar glueball candidate f0(1710) on a quantitative level. In this Letter we show that this model predicts a narrow pseudoscalar glueball heavier than the scalar glueball and with a very restricted decay pattern involving eta or eta' mesons. Production should be either in pairs or in association with eta(') mesons. We discuss the prospect of discovery in high-energy hadron collider experiments through central exclusive production by comparing with eta' pair production.

## Key claim

The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) holographic model predicts a narrow pseudoscalar glueball heavier than the scalar glueball, with a very restricted decay pattern involving only eta or eta' mesons, provided that mixing with quarkonia remains small; this contrasts with many phenomenological models that predict dominant decay into K Kbar pi.

## Method

The authors derived an effective four-dimensional Lagrangian for pseudoscalar glueball interactions from the top-down holographic WSS model by expanding the Ramond-Ramond 1-form field C_1 in pseudoscalar glueball modes and coupling them to the flavor singlet pseudoscalar eta_0 and scalar glueball fields. They computed the coupling constants numerically from the mode expansions and used them to calculate partial decay widths for the process pseudoscalar glueball to scalar glueball plus eta('), including subsequent decays of the scalar glueball into pairs of pseudoscalar mesons. Production rates for pseudoscalar glueball pairs and pseudoscalar glueball plus eta' pairs relative to eta' pair production via virtual scalar glueball exchange were also calculated.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the lowest pseudoscalar glueball mode is M_P approximately 1.885 M_KK, where M_KK = 949 MeV from matching the rho meson mass, giving M_P approximately 1789 MeV.
- The scalar glueball mass is M_D approximately 1.567 M_KK, giving approximately 1487 MeV.
- The Witten-Veneziano mass for eta_0 is m_0 = 730 to 967 MeV for 't Hooft coupling lambda between 12.55 and 16.63.
- The coupling constant for the dominant decay vertex (pseudoscalar glueball to scalar glueball and eta_0) is td_0 = 2.5833 lambda^{1/2} N_f^{1/2} N_c^{-3/2} M_KK.
- For a pseudoscalar glueball mass of 2.6 GeV, the ratio of produced pairs N(tilde-G tilde-G)/N(eta' eta') is independent of the 't Hooft coupling and shown as a function of center-of-mass energy; the ratio N(tilde-G eta')/N(eta' eta') ranges between values corresponding to lambda = 12.55 and 16.63.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the WSS model to scalar glueball decays (Brunner 2015, 2016), where the decay pattern of f_0(1710) was reproduced quantitatively. It contradicts earlier phenomenological approaches such as Rosenzweig (1981, 1982) and the extended linear sigma model of Eshraim (2012), which predicted dominant K Kbar pi decay modes for the pseudoscalar glueball, and instead agrees with the large-N_c chiral Lagrangian arguments of Gounaris (1988) that the pseudoscalar glueball should not couple directly to the chiral anomaly. The predictions enable searches for the pseudoscalar glueball in central exclusive production experiments at the LHC and in proton-antiproton collisions at the PANDA experiment at FAIR, with production rates comparable to those of eta' pair production.
