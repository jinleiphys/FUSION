# Symmetry energy dependence of long timescale isospin transport

> Isospin transport occurring within dinuclear projectile-like fragments (PLFs) produced in heavy- ion collisions is explored as a probe of the nuclear symmetry energy. Within the framework of the Constrained Molecular Dynamics model (CoMD), the existence of the long-lived dinuclear PLFs, for up to 800 fm/c, is observed. It is demonstrated that changes in the <N/Z> of the two fragments resulting from the breakup of the dinuclear PLF is due to isospin transport. The rate of the transport between the two fragments is shown to be dependent on the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density. Comparison of the CoMD calculations with experimental data establish that the evolution of <N/Z> could be used to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The rate and magnitude of isospin transport within long-lived dinuclear projectile-like fragments (PLFs) is shown to depend on the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density, but the agreement between CoMD calculations and experimental data is qualitative rather than quantitative, with the model underpredicting the neutron-richness of the light fragment and suggesting a significantly faster rotational frequency than originally estimated.

## Method

The Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model was used to simulate the 45 MeV/A 64Zn + 64Zn reaction for 1000 fm/c, with event selection matching the experimental angular coverage (4.5° < θlab < 27°) and fragment charge requirements (ZH > 11, ZL > 3). Three different forms of the symmetry energy were implemented, all with Esym(ρ0) = 30 MeV but with varying slopes L = 51, 78, and 105 MeV, and approximately 350,000 events were simulated per parameterization over an impact parameter range of 0 to 12 fm. The binary decay events were analyzed by tracking the time evolution of the dinuclear PLF and identifying the resulting ZH and ZL fragments at each time step.

## Key numbers

- Dinuclear PLFs survive for up to 800 fm/c in the CoMD simulation, with the most probable breakup time being considerably shorter.
- Extracted average short lifetimes ⟨τshort⟩: 38 fm/c (L=51 MeV), 52 fm/c (L=78 MeV), 57 fm/c (L=105 MeV).
- Extracted average long lifetimes ⟨τlong⟩: 429 fm/c (L=51 MeV), 593 fm/c (L=78 MeV), 711 fm/c (L=105 MeV).
- The ZL fragment loses on average 0.39 ± 0.02 neutrons per cos(α) while the ZH fragment gains on average 0.41 ± 0.03 neutrons per cos(α), confirming isospin transport dominates the evolution.
- Average angular momentum from CoMD: J = 8.6 ħ, compared to experimental extraction of J = 6 ± 1 ħ.
- Experimental results reported dinuclear PLFs surviving for 600-900 fm/c based on rotational angular momentum estimates.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Brown (2013) and Hudan (2012, 2014), who first proposed binary breakup of excited PLFs as a probe of isospin transport over timescales of 600-900 fm/c, approximately six times longer than previously reported by Steiner (2005) and Tsang (2004). The CoMD calculations confirm the physical picture and demonstrate that the evolution of ⟨N/Z⟩ with breakup time is sensitive to the slope of the symmetry energy, providing a novel experimental probe of the nuclear Equation of State. The results also connect to earlier work by the CHIMERA collaboration (Amo09) examining incomplete fusion in low-energy reactions, where a similar trend of decreased average lifetime with decreased L was observed.
