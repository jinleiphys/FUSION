# Pairing phase transition in the odd-A nuclei: identification and classification

> The investigation into the pairing phase transition in the odd-A nucleus 161Dy utilizes a sophisticated blend of covariant density functional theory and the shell-model-like approach. It is discerned that variations in thermodynamic quantities at the critical temperature do not exclusively align with pairing phase transitions. The presence of an S-shaped heat capacity curve, often interpreted as an indication of such transitions, does not offer a definitive confirmation. Additional factors, including the blocking effect, can modify the heat capacity curve and impede the transition process. The pairing phase transition in 161Dy, occurring around 0.7 to 1.0 MeV, is unequivocally characterized as a first-order transition. Furthermore, an exploration into the impact of varying strengths of pairing correlations on these transitions reveals a nonlinear relationship, adding complexity to the transition dynamics.

## Key claim

The S-shaped heat capacity curve, often taken as a hallmark of the pairing phase transition, does not exclusively indicate such a transition; the pairing phase transition in 161Dy is unequivocally first-order, but the blocking effect and the strength of pairing correlations modify the heat capacity curve and can produce an S-shape even without a true pairing phase transition.

## Method

The authors combined covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the shell-model-like approach (SLAP), which diagonalizes the Hamiltonian in a multi-particle configuration space while conserving particle number and treating the blocking effect for odd-A nuclei. Thermodynamic quantities were computed in the canonical ensemble, and the order of the phase transition was determined by analyzing the distribution of zeros (DOZ) of the canonical partition function in the complex temperature plane, using the exponent alpha derived from the first three zeros closest to the real axis. Ideal toy models with controllable pairing strength were also constructed to isolate the effect of pairing correlations from other nuclear structure factors.

## Key numbers

* The pairing phase transition in 161Dy occurs around 0.7 to 1.0 MeV (critical temperature range).
* The critical temperature for neutrons in 161Dy is 0.77 MeV; for protons it is 0.95 MeV (from zero-point analysis).
* The experimental critical temperature for 161Dy is 0.52 MeV (from PhysRevC.63.021306).
* The alpha values for neutrons and protons in 161Dy are -2.04 and -5.66, respectively, indicating a first-order phase transition.
* For the ideal even system, the phase transition order changes with pairing strength G: first-order for G less than or equal to 0.7 MeV, higher-order for G between 0.8 and 0.9 MeV, and second-order for G = 1.0 MeV (alpha = 0.94).
* For the ideal odd system, the phase transition is first-order for G less than or equal to 0.7 MeV and higher-order for G between 0.8 and 1.0 MeV.
* The neutron pairing strength used for 161Dy is 0.29 MeV; the proton pairing strength is 0.32 MeV.
* The model space for 161Dy spans 7.56 MeV in single-particle energy.

## Context

This work extends the classification of pairing phase transitions in even-even nuclei, such as the study of 162Dy by Gao (2023) using the DOZ method, to the odd-A nucleus 161Dy. It directly addresses the ambiguity of the S-shaped heat capacity curve as a signature of pairing phase transitions, a topic previously debated in the literature (e.g., PhysRevC.63.021306, PhysRevLett.87.022501). The results enable future studies to correctly identify and classify pairing phase transitions in odd-A nuclei and to understand the nonlinear role of pairing strength, with implications for experimental spectroscopic properties.
