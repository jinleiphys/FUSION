# Centrality dependence of kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity in high energy nuclear collisions

> Centrality-dependent double-differential transverse momentum spectra of charged pions, kaons, and (anti)protons produced in mid-pseudorapidity interval in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV gold-gold and deuteron-gold collisions with different centralities are analyzed by the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics. Meanwhile, the mentioned spectra in mid-rapidity interval in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV lead-lead and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV proton-lead collisions with different centralities are analyzed by the same model. The model results are approximately in agreement with the experimental data in special transverse momentum ranges. It is shown that with the increase of event centrality and energy, the kinetic freeze-out temperature of the emission source and the transverse flow velocity of the produced particles slightly increase in some cases but they do not give an obvious change in other cases. Meanwhile, the kinetic freeze-out temperature (transverse flow velocity) increases (decreases) with the increase of particle mass. The average transverse momentum and initial temperature increase with the increase of event centrality, collision energy, and particle mass. This work also confirms the maximum size dependent effect, which states that the main parameters such as the kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity are mainly determined by the heaviest nucleus from proton-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity extracted from the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics show a slight increase with event centrality and collision energy in some cases but no obvious change in other cases, with the specific transverse momentum range where the model applies varying from 0 to 2-3 GeV/c in peripheral collisions to 0 to 4.5 GeV/c or more in central collisions.

## Method

The authors fitted double-differential transverse momentum spectra of charged pions, kaons, and (anti)protons using the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (Eq. 5), which describes the soft excitation process in the low transverse momentum region. The model was applied separately to each particle species and centrality class for Au-Au and d-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV from PHENIX, and for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV and p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV from ALICE, using least-squares fitting with a restricted chi-squared range (chi^2_min <= chi^2 <= 1.05 chi^2_min).

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au at 200 GeV, the most central (0-5%) pi- yield T_0 = 0.140 +/- 0.004 GeV and beta_T = 0.368 +/- 0.004 c, decreasing to T_0 = 0.120 +/- 0.003 GeV and beta_T = 0.367 +/- 0.005 c for the most peripheral (80-92%) collisions
- For Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, the most central (0-5%) pi- yield T_0 = 0.135 +/- 0.003 GeV and beta_T = 0.430 +/- 0.003 c, while the most peripheral (80-90%) yield T_0 = 0.108 +/- 0.004 GeV and beta_T = 0.428 +/- 0.006 c
- For p-Pb at 5.02 TeV, the most central (0-5%) pi+ + pi- yield T_0 = 0.119 +/- 0.006 GeV and beta_T = 0.469 +/- 0.005 c, decreasing to T_0 = 0.100 +/- 0.001 GeV and beta_T = 0.463 +/- 0.001 c for the most peripheral (80-100%) collisions
- For Au-Au at 200 GeV, antiprotons in the most central (0-5%) collisions show T_0 = 0.208 +/- 0.006 GeV and beta_T = 0.333 +/- 0.005 c, compared to pi- with T_0 = 0.140 +/- 0.004 GeV and beta_T = 0.368 +/- 0.004 c, demonstrating that kinetic freeze-out temperature increases with particle mass while transverse flow velocity decreases

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics to centrality-dependent spectra across multiple collision systems at RHIC and LHC energies, following earlier applications by the same group (Waqas and Liu, 2018) and others using similar thermal models. The analysis confirms the maximum size dependent effect proposed in prior work (Liu et al., 2013), which states that freeze-out parameters are mainly determined by the heaviest nucleus in collisions from proton-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus systems. The results enable future comparisons with hydrodynamical models (such as those by Heinz and collaborators) that describe the full evolution of the collision system, and provide systematic parameter sets for tuning event generators.
