# Recent progress towards a chiral effective field theory for the NN system

> Since Weinberg's proposal two decades ago, chiral effective field theory in the NN sector has been developed and applied up to order $O((Q/M_{hi})^4)$. In principle it could provide a model-independent description of nuclear force from QCD. However, in spite of its huge success, some open issues such as the renormalization group invariance and power counting, still remain to be solved. In this talk we refine the chiral effective field theory approach to the NN system based on a renormalization group analysis. Our results show that a truly model-independent description of NN system can be obtained by a new power counting which treats the subleading order corrections perturbatively.

# Recent progress towards a chiral effective field theory for the NN system

## Key claim
A truly model-independent description of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) system can be obtained by a new power counting that treats subleading order corrections perturbatively, based on a renormalization group (RG) analysis. However, the authors note that this RG-correct scheme could converge too slowly to be useful, and the superiority of either this scheme or the conventional Weinberg power counting cannot be determined before full implementation in few- and many-body calculations.

## Method
The authors performed a renormalization group analysis of chiral effective field theory (χEFT) for the NN system, examining the cutoff dependence of amplitudes when the ultraviolet cutoff Λ is iterated in the Lippmann-Schwinger or Schrödinger equation. They found that the conventional Weinberg power counting (WPC) fails RG requirements for Λ > 1 GeV, and propose instead a new scheme where the leading-order (LO) potential is iterated to all order while all subleading chiral potentials are included perturbatively via Distorted-Wave-Born-Approximation, with contact terms determined by RG-analysis to guarantee correct RG behavior.

## Key numbers
- Conventional WPC has been carried out to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N³LO), defined based on the pion-exchange part of the potential.
- RG-analysis indicates that conventional WPC fails to fulfill RG requirements once the ultraviolet cutoff Λ > 1 GeV.
- The new scheme requires staying in the range 500 < Λ < 1000 MeV if one insists on building a NN potential based on χEFT and using it in a conventional way.
- Phase shifts from the new scheme are evaluated up to NNLO, with agreement comparable to those from WPC at the same order.

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to NN interactions originally proposed by Weinberg (1990), which has been the standard for many conventional calculations (Entem and Machleidt, 2003; Epelbaum, 2005; Epelbaum, 2012). The paper addresses open issues of renormalization group invariance and power counting that have been debated in the literature, with some authors (Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck, 2005; Yang, 2009; Zeoli and Entem, 2012) showing that WPC fails RG requirements at high cutoff, while others (Birse, 2009; Pavon Valderrama and Phillips, 2009) have developed perturbative treatments of subleading potentials. The new scheme enables full RG- and power counting analysis, making theoretical error estimation easier, and future tasks include refining power counting with Lepage plot techniques, including Delta (1232) contribution, and deciding power counting for high partial-waves (l ≥ 2).
