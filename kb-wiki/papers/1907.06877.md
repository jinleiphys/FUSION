# Microscopic calculations of weak decays in superheavy nuclei

> Half-lives of beta+ decay and electron capture are studied in some selected superheavy nuclei produced in hot fusion reactions, namely, 290Fl, 293Mc, 294Lv, and 295Ts. The nuclear structure is described microscopically from deformed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations that include pairing correlations. The sensitivity of the half-lives to deformation and to the QEC energies, which are still not determined experimentally, are studied. The results are compared with phenomenological alpha-decay half-lives, showing that the latter decay mode is dominant in this mass region.

## Key claim

The paper calculates beta-plus decay and electron capture half-lives for four superheavy nuclei produced in hot-fusion reactions, finding that these weak decay modes are generally much slower than alpha decay, but shape-isomeric states with superdeformed configurations could have half-lives short enough to compete with alpha decay, depending on the still-unknown QEC energies.

## Method

The author performed deformed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations with SLy4 interaction and BCS pairing correlations to obtain nuclear structure, then computed Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the equilibrium shapes of each nucleus. Half-lives were calculated by summing allowed GT transition strengths weighted with phase-space factors, using QEC energies from several mass formulas (FRDM, FRDM-12, DZ, SkP, SLy4) and from extrapolated experimental systematics, since experimental masses are not available for these superheavy nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the superdeformed shape isomers (beta2 approximately 0.5) using extrapolated experimental QEC energies, half-lives in the range of 100-1000 seconds were obtained for 290Fl, 293Mc, and 294Lv, and around 100 seconds for 295Ts
- The ground state of 290Fl and 294Lv is oblate with quadrupole deformation beta2 approximately -0.1, with two additional prolate minima at beta2 approximately 0.1 and beta2 approximately 0.5 at excitation energies of about 1 MeV and 4 MeV, respectively
- QEC energies for the superheavy nuclei are distributed within about 1 MeV across the different mass formulas, with extrapolated experimental values lying inside this range
- For the test case of 252Fm, the ground state is prolate with beta2 approximately 0.3, with oblate (beta2 approximately -0.3) and prolate (beta2 approximately 0.7) minima at excitation energies around 8 MeV and 4 MeV, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological studies of beta-plus/EC decay in superheavy nuclei by Karpov et al. (2012) and Moller et al. (1997), which used constant log(ft) values or folded-Yukawa single-particle Hamiltonians, by providing a fully microscopic description of the nuclear structure from an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results enable assessment of whether beta-plus/EC decay branches could open alternative decay chains in superheavy nuclei produced via proton-evaporation and alpha-evaporation channels of hot-fusion reactions, as proposed by Zagrebaev et al. (2012) and Karpov et al. (2012), potentially allowing access to new superheavy nuclei not belonging to the original alpha-decay chains.
