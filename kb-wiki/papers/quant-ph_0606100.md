# Semi-spectral Chebyshev method in Quantum Mechanics

> Traditionally, finite differences and finite element methods have been by many regarded as the basic tools for obtaining numerical solutions in a variety of quantum mechanical problems emerging in atomic, nuclear and particle physics, astrophysics, quantum chemistry, etc. In recent years, however, an alternative technique based on the semi-spectral methods has focused considerable attention. The purpose of this work is first to provide the necessary tools and subsequently examine the efficiency of this method in quantum mechanical applications. Restricting our interest to time independent two-body problems, we obtained the continuous and discrete spectrum solutions of the underlying Schroedinger or Lippmann-Schwinger equations in both, the coordinate and momentum space. In all of the numerically studied examples we had no difficulty in achieving the machine accuracy and the semi-spectral method showed exponential convergence combined with excellent numerical stability.

## Key claim

The semi-spectral Chebyshev method provides exponential convergence and machine accuracy for solving time-independent two-body quantum mechanical problems (Schrödinger and Lippmann-Schwinger equations) in both coordinate and momentum space, but the paper restricts its demonstration to short-ranged potentials with three specific analytic models (exponential, Morse, and Hulthén) for which exact solutions exist to benchmark the method.

## Method

The method discretizes the problem by introducing a Chebyshev grid of N points (the zeros of the Chebyshev polynomial T_N(t) on [-1,1]), then solves the differential or integral equation exactly at all grid points, and finally uses Chebyshev cardinal functions for interpolation to obtain the solution at arbitrary points. The approach converts differentiation and integration into matrix multiplications using the Chebyshev spectral representation, with the Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature providing exact integration for polynomials up to order 2N+1. For singular integrals, the method provides analytic weight functions for Cauchy principal value and logarithmic singularities.

## Key numbers

- The Chebyshev interpolation error bound is given by |f(t)-P_N(t)| ≤ (1/(N+1)!) f^{(N+1)}(ξ) ∏(t-t_i), where the product term is minimized by choosing grid points as Chebyshev zeros, giving max |Q_N(t)| ≥ 1/2^{N-1} for any monic polynomial Q_N(t) of degree N on [-1,1]
- The Gauss-Chebyshev quadrature for the weighting function 1/√(1-t^2) gives weights w_j = π/N for all j, with N grid points
- The definite integral ∫_{-1}^{1} f(t) dt is approximated by ∑ w_j f(t_j) with weights w_j = -(4/N) ∑'_{i=0}^{[(N-1)/2]} T_{2i}(t_j)/(4i^2-1), and the sum rule ∑ w_i = 2
- The Cauchy principal value integral weights satisfy the sum rule ∑ ω_j(z) = log[(1-z)/(1+z)]

## Context

This work extends the semi-spectral Chebyshev approach to quantum mechanical two-body problems, building on the mathematical foundations of spectral methods described by Fornberg and Boyd. The method is demonstrated on three potentials (exponential, Morse, and Hulthén) that have been used in nuclear physics phenomenology by Darewych and Green (Morse potential for nucleon-nucleon interaction) and by Durand, van Dijk, and others (Hulthén potential). The paper provides the necessary integration, differentiation, and singular integral tools for applying the method to both the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space, enabling future work on more complex potentials and scattering problems.
