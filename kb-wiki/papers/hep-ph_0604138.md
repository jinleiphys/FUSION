# Shear Viscosity to Entropy Density Ratio of QCD below the Deconfinement Temperature

> Using chiral perturbation theory we investigate the QCD shear viscosity to entropy density ratio below the deconfinement temperature (~170 MeV) with zero baryon number density. It is found that the viscosity to entropy density ratio of QCD is monotonically decreasing in temperature (T) and reaches 0.6 with estimated ~50% uncertainty at T=120 MeV. A naive extrapolation of the leading order result shows that the ratio reaches the 1/(4 pi) minimum bound proposed by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets using string theory methods at T~200 MeV. This suggests a phase transition or cross over might occur at T less than 200 MeV in order for the bound to remain valid. Also, it is natural for the ratio to stay close to the minimum bound around the phase transition temperature as was recently found in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of QCD below the deconfinement temperature is monotonically decreasing in temperature and reaches 0.6 with an estimated 50% uncertainty at T=120 MeV; a naive extrapolation of the leading order result suggests the ratio reaches the 1/(4π) minimum bound at T~200 MeV, implying a phase transition or crossover must occur below 200 MeV for the bound to remain valid.

## Method

The authors used chiral perturbation theory (χPT) at leading order to compute ππ scattering amplitudes, then solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for a pion gas at zero baryon number density to obtain the shear viscosity η. The entropy density s was computed from a free pion gas partition function, as interaction contributions are higher order in the leading order calculation. The solution for the shear viscosity was obtained by expanding the deviation function B(p) in orthogonal polynomials and solving the resulting linear equations.

## Key numbers

- η/s = 0.6 at T = 120 MeV, with an estimated 50% uncertainty
- The conjectured minimum bound from string theory is 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08
- The deconfinement temperature is ~170 MeV at zero baryon density
- The pion mass mπ = 139 MeV; the kaon mass is 495 MeV
- The averaged center of mass momentum at T = 120 MeV is ~460 MeV, and at T = 140 MeV is ~530 MeV, both below 4πfπ ~ 1 GeV
- The isoscalar ππ scattering length increases by ~40% at next-to-leading order, increasing the cross section by ~100% and reducing η by ~50% near threshold
- The pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV (87 MeV in the chiral limit)

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to compute meson matter viscosity using the Boltzmann equation and phenomenological phase shifts by Davesne and Dobado et al. (early 2000s), and complements state-of-the-art perturbative QCD calculations of η in the deconfinement phase by Arnold et al. (2003). The results enable comparison with the conjectured η/s minimum bound proposed by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) using string theory methods, and suggest that the η/s ratio staying close to the minimum bound around the phase transition temperature is natural, as was recently found in heavy ion collisions at RHIC.
