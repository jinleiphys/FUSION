# Resonance and continuum states in weakly bound systems

> Linear response theories in the continuum capable of describing continuum spectra and dynamical correlations are presented. Our formulation is essentially the same as the continuum random-phase approximation (RPA) but suitable for uniform grid representation in the three-dimensional (3D) Cartesian coordinate assuming no spatial symmetry. Effects of the continuum are taken into account either by solving equations iteratively with a retarded Green's function or by an absorbing boundary condition. The methods are applied to giant resonances in a deformed nucleus 12C.

## Key claim
The paper presents two complementary methods for incorporating the outgoing boundary condition into linear response calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid without spatial symmetry, enabling continuum RPA for deformed nuclei. The abstract glosses over the fact that the absorbing boundary condition (ABC) method fails for low-energy outgoing particles below about 10 MeV and cannot handle long-range Coulomb potentials properly.

## Method
The authors implement continuum random-phase approximation (RPA) on a uniform 3D grid using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock ground state. They treat the continuum either by iteratively solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a retarded Green's function constructed from a spherical reference Hamiltonian (OBC method), or by adding an absorbing complex potential at the grid boundary to simulate outgoing waves (ABC method). Both methods are applied to isoscalar giant monopole and quadrupole resonances in the deformed nucleus 12C using the BKN Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- The highest occupied single-particle energy in 12C is -13.9 MeV.
- The ABC method shows small discrepancies with OBC below E = 19 MeV, corresponding to outgoing particle energies below 6 MeV.
- The ABC method satisfies the absorber condition for outgoing particle energies above 10 MeV (E - 13.9 MeV > 10 MeV).
- The monopole strength distribution shows two peaks: one at E ≈ 20 MeV with FWHM ≈ 5 MeV, and one at E ≈ 30 MeV with FWHM ≈ 15 MeV.
- The K=2 quadrupole strength is localized around 16 MeV.
- The K=0 quadrupole strength shows a single peak at E ≈ 21 MeV in the OBC calculation, while the BBC calculation splits this into three peaks.
- The box boundary condition calculation used a box radius of R = 18 fm; the OBC and ABC calculations used a model space of R = 8 fm.
- The ABC calculation used an absorbing region of width Δr = 10 fm.
- The mesh spacing was Δx = Δy = Δz = 1 fm.
- A smoothing parameter Γ = 1 MeV was used for all response curves.

## Context
This work extends the continuum RPA method, previously developed for spherical nuclei by Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) and applied with Skyrme HF by Giai and Sagawa (1981) and Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1996), to deformed nuclei by solving the boundary condition problem in 3D Cartesian coordinates. The authors had previously applied essentially the same techniques to photoabsorption in small molecules (Nakatsukasa and Yabana, 2001). The methods enable future calculations of response and reactions in drip-line nuclei, where continuum effects are essential.
