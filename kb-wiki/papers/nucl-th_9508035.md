# Isospin-Violating Meson-Nucleon Vertices as an Alternate Mechanism of Charge-Symmetry Breaking

> We compute isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants and their consequent charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon-nucleon potentials. The couplings result from evaluating matrix elements of quark currents between nucleon states in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model; the isospin violations arise from the difference in the up and down constituent quark masses. We find, in particular, that isospin violation in the omega-meson--nucleon vertex dominates the class IV CSB potential obtained from these considerations. We evaluate the resulting spin-singlet--triplet mixing angles, the quantities germane to the difference of neutron and proton analyzing powers measured in elastic $\vec{n}-\vec{p}$ scattering, and find them commensurate to those computed originally using the on-shell value of the $ρ$-$ω$ mixing amplitude. The use of the on-shell $ρ$-$ω$ mixing amplitude at $q^2=0$ has been called into question; rather, the amplitude is zero in a wide class of models. Our model possesses no contribution from $ρ$-$ω$ mixing at $q^2=0$, and we find that omega-meson exchange suffices to explain the measured $n-p$ analyzing power difference~at~183 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors propose that isospin-violating meson-nucleon vertices, computed in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with up-down quark mass difference, can replace the conventional on-shell rho-omega mixing mechanism as the dominant source of charge-symmetry breaking in nucleon-nucleon interactions. They find that omega-meson exchange alone suffices to explain the measured neutron-proton analyzing power difference at 183 MeV, without requiring any contribution from rho-omega mixing at zero momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors compute isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants by evaluating matrix elements of quark currents between nucleon states in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model, with isospin violations arising from the difference in up and down constituent quark masses. They then construct one-boson exchange charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon-nucleon potentials from these couplings and evaluate the resulting spin-singlet-triplet mixing angles relevant to the difference of neutron and proton analyzing powers in elastic n-p scattering. The momentum dependence of the couplings is modeled using Bonn B potential form factors.

## Key numbers

- The omega-meson exchange CSB potential at q²=0 is approximately 2.49 GeV⁻², compared to 2.07 GeV⁻² from on-shell rho-omega mixing
- The rho-meson exchange CSB potential at q²=0 is approximately 0.18 GeV⁻², representing only a 7% correction to the omega contribution
- The rho-omega mixing amplitude at q²=0 is zero in this model due to vector mesons coupling to conserved currents
- The neutral pion exchange CSB potential at q²=0 is approximately 36.83 GeV⁻², comparable to the on-shell pi-eta mixing value of 52.01 GeV⁻²
- The quark mass difference used is Δm = 4.1 MeV
- The ratio of isospin breaking in the quark model versus nucleon model for neutral pion exchange is approximately 6, favoring the quark model

## Context

This work extends the search for new sources of isospin violation following the suggestion by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1992) that rho-omega mixing contributions to charge-symmetry-breaking observables are suppressed at low momentum transfer. The suppression has been confirmed in many subsequent calculations (Piekarewicz and Williams 1993; Hatsuda et al. 1993; Krein et al. 1993; Mitchell et al. 1994; O'Connell et al. 1994; Maltman 1995), showing the mixing amplitude is zero at q²=0 in models with vector mesons coupled to conserved currents. The present work provides an alternate mechanism that restores agreement with the experimental analyzing power difference measured by Knutson et al. (1990) at 183 MeV, which previously relied on the on-shell rho-omega mixing contribution. The results enable future calculations of charge-symmetry-breaking observables without invoking the problematic rho-omega mixing amplitude at zero momentum transfer.
