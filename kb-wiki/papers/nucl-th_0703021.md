# Initial-State Coulomb Interaction in the dd -> alpha pi^0 Reaction

> The effects of initial-state Coulomb interactions in the charge-symmetry-breaking reaction dd -> alpha pi^0 are investigated within a previously published formalism. This is a leading order effect in which the Coulomb interaction between the two initial state protons leads to the breakup of the two deuterons into a continuum state that is well connected to the final alpha pi^0 state by the strong emission of a pion. As a first step, we use a simplified set of d and alpha wave functions and a plane-wave approximation for the initial dd state. This Coulomb mechanism, by itself, yields cross sections that are much larger than the experimental ones, and which are comparable in size to the contributions from other mechanisms. Inclusion of this mechanism is therefore necessary in a realistic calculation.

## Key claim

The initial-state Coulomb interaction between the two protons in the \(dd\to\alpha\pi^0\) reaction, treated as a leading-order effect within a plane-wave approximation and simplified wave functions, yields cross sections much larger than the experimental values and comparable in size to other known mechanisms; therefore this Coulomb mechanism must be included in any realistic calculation, but the present simplified treatment is only a first step and does not yet reproduce the measured cross section.

## Method

The authors compute the \(dd\to\alpha\pi^0\) amplitude using a previously published formalism in which the Coulomb interaction between the two initial-state protons breaks the two deuterons into a four-nucleon continuum state, followed by strong pion emission via the one-body pseudovector operator. They employ Gaussian wave functions for both the deuteron and the alpha particle, and also test a Hulthén deuteron wave function, while treating the initial \(dd\) state as a plane wave and neglecting strong initial-state interactions. The amplitude is reduced to a two-dimensional integral (Gaussian case) or a four-dimensional integral (Hulthén case) over auxiliary parameters, which is evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers

- The experimental cross sections are \(\sigma = 12.7 \pm 2.2\) pb at \(T_d = 228.5\) MeV and \(15.1 \pm 3.1\) pb at \(T_d = 231.8\) MeV (IUCF Cooler ring data).
- The threshold deuteron kinetic energy is \(T_d = 225.6\) MeV.
- The Gaussian wave function parameters are \(\alpha = 2.77\) fm (alpha particle) and \(\beta = 3.189\) fm (deuteron), fixed using measured rms point radii \(\langle r_\alpha^2\rangle^{1/2} = 1.47\) fm and \(\langle r_d^2\rangle^{1/2} = 1.953\) fm.
- The Hulthén deuteron wave function parameters are \(a = 0.23161\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(b = 1.3802\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The pion mass used is \(\mu = 134.974\) MeV.
- The \(^4\)He binding energy is \(\epsilon_B \simeq 28.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier chiral EFT calculations of the \(dd\to\alpha\pi^0\) reaction by Lähde and Miller (2006, 2007), which used plane-wave approximations and Gaussian wave functions (first paper) and later included strong initial-state interactions and realistic wave functions (second paper), yielding cross sections of several hundred pb or more. The present paper addresses a previously neglected contribution, the initial-state Coulomb interaction, and finds it is comparable in size to other mechanisms, so it must be included in future realistic calculations that also incorporate strong initial-state interactions and loop diagrams as in Lensky (2007). The results enable a more complete treatment of charge-symmetry-breaking effects in this reaction, which is a unique probe of the light quark mass difference.
