# Bulk viscosity, chemical equilibration and flow at RHIC

> We study the effects of bulk viscosity on p_T spectra and elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. We argue that direct effect of the bulk viscosity on the evolution of the velocity field is small, but corrections to the freezeout distributions can be significant. These effects are dominated by chemical non-equilibration in the hadronic phase. We show that a non-zero bulk viscosity in the range $ζ/s \lsim 0.05$ improves the description of spectra and flow at RHIC.

## Key claim
A non-zero bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio in the range ζ/s ≲ 0.05 improves the description of p_T spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC, but the direct effect of bulk viscosity on the evolution of the velocity field is small; the significant corrections come from freezeout distributions and are dominated by chemical non-equilibration in the hadronic phase.

## Method
The authors performed hydrodynamic calculations incorporating a single freezeout temperature of 140 MeV with no hadronic afterburner, but including feed-down from hadronic resonances. They solved second-order hydrodynamic equations and modeled the non-equilibrium distribution function by an off-equilibrium chemical potential for total pion number, extended to a hadronic resonance gas where relative chemical potentials for different species are determined by fast reactions such as ρ ↔ ππ and p + p̅ ↔ nπ. The temperature dependence of the bulk viscosity followed the model described in Dusling (2011).

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio at freezeout: ζ/s ≲ 0.05 improves description of spectra and flow.
- Extracted value of bulk viscosity from data: ζ/s ≃ 0.01.
- Freezeout temperature: T_fr ≃ 140 MeV.
- Pion chemical potential extracted: μ_π ≃ 10-20 MeV, depending on local expansion rate.
- Bulk viscosity of pure gauge theory in perturbative QCD: ζ = 0.44 α_s^2 T^3 / log(α_s^{-1}), implying ζ ≃ 48 (c_s^2 - 1/3)^2 η.
- Bulk viscosity of a pion gas scales as ζ ∼ (f_π^8 / m_π^5) exp(-2m_π / T).

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions at RHIC by incorporating bulk viscosity effects, which had previously been neglected in purely hydrodynamic models. The authors show that effects previously attributed to hadronic non-equilibrium effects in kinetic afterburners can be described efficiently in terms of bulk viscosity. The results enable a more precise determination of transport coefficients from experimental data and provide a framework for including difficult hadronic reactions (such as p p̅ annihilation into several pions) that are challenging to implement in kinetic models.
