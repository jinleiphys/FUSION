# Critical exponents and transport properties near the QCD critical endpoint from the statistical bootstrap model

> We present an estimate of the behaviour of the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients when the QCD critical point is approached from the hadronic side, describing hadronic matter within the statistical bootstrap model (SBM) of strong interactions. The boostrap model shows critical behavior near the quark-hadron transition temperature if the parameter characterizing the degeneracy of Hagedorn states is properly chosen. We calculate the critical exponents and amplitudes of relevant thermodynamic quantities near the QCD critical point and combine them with an Ansatz for the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients to derive the behavior of these coefficients near the critical point. The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is found to decrease when the temperature is increased, and to approach the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound 1/(4π) faster near the critical point, while the bulk viscosity coefficient is found to rise very rapidly.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio decreases with increasing temperature and approaches the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound \(1/(4\pi)\) faster near the QCD critical point, while the bulk viscosity coefficient rises very rapidly, provided the parameter \(a\) in the Hagedorn density of states is chosen as \(a = -4\) to ensure finite energy and entropy densities at the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors describe hadronic matter using the statistical bootstrap model (SBM), where the hadron mass spectrum includes a discrete part (all known hadrons up to 2.25 GeV) and a continuous Hagedorn density of states \(\rho(m) = A m^a e^{m/T_H}\). They derive the critical exponents \(\hat{\alpha}\), \(\hat{\beta}\), \(\hat{\gamma}\), and \(\hat{\nu}\) for thermodynamic quantities near the critical temperature by taking appropriate derivatives of the partition function in the limit \(T \to T_c\). These exponents are then combined with an Ansatz for the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients (expressed in terms of thermodynamic quantities and the correlation length) to obtain the singular behavior of \(\eta/s\) and \(\zeta/s\) as the critical point is approached from the hadronic side.

## Key numbers

* For the specific choice \(a = -4\) in the Hagedorn density of states, the energy density remains finite as \(T \to T_c\) (the Hagedorn temperature), implying a phase transition.
* The critical exponent for the specific heat is \(\hat{\alpha} = a + 9/2\); for \(a = -4\), this gives \(\hat{\alpha} = 0.5\).
* The critical exponent for the isothermal compressibility is \(\hat{\gamma} = -(a + 5/2)\); for \(a = -4\), this gives \(\hat{\gamma} = 1.5\).
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is found to approach the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound \(1/(4\pi)\) near the critical point.
* The bulk viscosity coefficient is found to rise very rapidly near the critical point, scaling as \(\zeta \sim \xi^3\) where \(\xi\) is the correlation length.

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model (SBM) analysis of critical exponents by Satz (1978) to the dynamical properties of the QCD critical endpoint. It provides an estimate of transport coefficients near the critical point from the hadronic side, complementing lattice QCD studies which are limited by the sign problem at finite baryon density and by the difficulty of extracting real-time quantities from Euclidean data. The results enable phenomenological modeling of the hydrodynamic evolution of matter created in heavy-ion collisions near the QCD critical point, as explored in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and SPS.
