# Directed flow at RHIC from Lee-Yang zeroes

> Directed flow in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed using the reaction plane from elliptic flow, which reduces the bias from nonflow effects. We combine this method with the determination of elliptic flow from Lee-Yang zeroes. The resulting method is more consistent and somewhat easier to implement than the previously used method based on three-particle cumulants, and is also less biased by nonflow correlations. Error terms from residual nonflow correlations are carefully estimated, as well as statistical errors. We discuss the application of the method at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method for analyzing directed flow ($v_1$) in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions that combines the reaction plane from elliptic flow with the determination of elliptic flow from Lee-Yang zeroes, claiming it is more consistent and less biased by nonflow correlations than the previously used three-particle cumulant method, provided the parameter $\epsilon$ is chosen small enough to satisfy $\epsilon \ll V_2/V_1$.

## Method

The method uses a generating function $g_\epsilon^{\theta_1,\theta_2}(z)$ defined as a product over all detected particles, where $\theta_1$ and $\theta_2$ are angles, $\epsilon$ is a small parameter, and $z$ is a complex variable. Integrated elliptic flow $V_2$ is first determined by finding the first minimum $r_0^{\theta_2}$ of $|\langle g_0^{0,\theta_2}(ir)\rangle_{\rm evts}|$ on the imaginary axis and using $|V_2| = \langle j_{01}/r_0^{\theta_2}\rangle_{\theta_2}$ where $j_{01}\simeq 2.40483$. Integrated directed flow $V_1$ is then estimated from the generating function evaluated at $z=ir_0^{\theta_2}$ with small $\epsilon$, averaging over $\theta_1$ and $\theta_2$ to eliminate statistical fluctuations. Differential directed flow $v'_1$ is obtained from the derivative of the generating function with respect to the proton weight.

## Key numbers

- $j_{01} \simeq 2.40483$: the first root of the Bessel function $J_0(x)$, used to relate the position of the first minimum $r_0^{\theta_2}$ to $|V_2|$ via $|V_2| = \langle j_{01}/r_0^{\theta_2}\rangle_{\theta_2}$
- The systematic error from nonflow effects scales as $(\delta v_1)_{\rm nonflow}/v_1 = \mathcal{O}(v_2/(M v_1^2)) + \mathcal{O}(1/(M v_2))$, where $M$ is the event multiplicity
- Compared to the standard event-plane method where $(\delta v_1)_{\rm nonflow}/v_1 = \mathcal{O}(1/(M v_1^2))$, the present method reduces nonflow error by a factor of $1/v_2$, typically 15 to 20 at RHIC
- For large resolution parameters $\chi_1$ and $\chi_2$, the statistical error on integrated directed flow is $\delta V_1 = \sigma_1/\sqrt{2 N_{\rm evts}}$, where $\sigma_1^2 \simeq \langle \sum_j w_j^2 \rangle_{\rm evts}$
- The statistical error on differential directed flow satisfies $(\delta v'_1)^2 = (1+\chi_1^2)/\chi_1^2 (\delta v'_2)^2$, where $\delta v'_2$ is the statistical error on differential elliptic flow from Lee-Yang zeroes

## Context

This work extends the three-particle cumulant method for directed flow analysis introduced by Borghini and Ollitrault (2002) and the Lee-Yang zeroes method for elliptic flow analysis developed by Bhalerao, Borghini, and Ollitrault (2003, 2004). The method was first implemented at the CERN SPS by the NA49 Collaboration and recently led to the discovery of directed flow at RHIC by the STAR Collaboration (2003). The approach enables simultaneous analysis of elliptic and directed flows with reduced nonflow bias, and the authors discuss its application at RHIC and LHC energies.
