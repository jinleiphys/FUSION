# Proton Structure Functions from Holographic Einstein-Dilaton Models

> We study the proton structure functions $F_1$ and $F_2$ in the context of holography. We develop a general framework that extends previous holographic calculations of $F_1$ and $F_2$ to the case where the bulk geometry stems from bottom-up Einstein-Dilaton models, which are commonly used in the literature to describe some properties of QCD in the strong coupling regime. We focus on a choice of the dilaton potential that leads to a holographic model able to reproduce known lattice QCD results for the glueball masses at zero temperature and pure Yang-Mills thermodynamics above deconfinement. Once the parameters of the background holographic model are fixed, we introduce probe fermionic and gauge fields in the bulk {\it a la} Polchinski and Strassler to determine the corresponding structure functions. This particular realization of the model can successfully describe the proton mass and provide results for $F_2$ at large $x$ in very good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present a general holographic framework for computing proton structure functions \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) using bottom-up Einstein-Dilaton models where the bulk geometry is dynamically determined from the equations of motion, rather than chosen ad hoc; for a specific dilaton potential that reproduces lattice QCD glueball masses and pure Yang-Mills thermodynamics, the model successfully describes the proton mass and yields results for \(F_2\) at large Bjorken \(x\) in very good agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Einstein and scalar field equations for a five-dimensional Einstein-Dilaton action with a specific dilaton potential (model B1 from Finazzo (2014)) to obtain the metric functions \(A(\phi)\) and \(B(\phi)\) in the Gubser-Nellore ansatz. They then introduce probe fermionic fields (representing baryonic states) and gauge fields (representing the virtual photon) in the bulk following the Polchinski and Strassler (2002) approach, solve their equations of motion numerically on this dynamically determined background, and compute the hadronic tensor from the interaction action to extract the structure function \(F_2\).

## Key numbers

- The dilaton potential parameters are: \(a = 1\), \(\beta = \sqrt{2/3}\), \(b_2 = 5.5\), \(b_4 = 0.3957\), \(b_6 = 0.0135\) (in units where the AdS radius \(R = 1\)).
- The scalar field mass squared is \(m^2 R^2 = -3\), satisfying the Breitenlohner-Freedman bound and giving conformal dimension \(\Delta = 3\).
- The photon field profiles are computed for \(q^2\) values of \(0.0\), \(4.7\), \(11\), and \(18\) GeV\(^2\).
- The linear coefficients extracted from the numerical metric functions are \(a_1 \approx 3/8\) and \(b_1 \approx 5/9\) for the UV expansions \(A(\phi) \approx -\log\phi - a_1\phi\) and \(B(\phi) \approx -\log\phi - b_1\phi\).

## Context

This work extends previous holographic calculations of structure functions (Polchinski and Strassler (2002), and many subsequent works using fixed AdS/QCD geometries) to the case where the bulk geometry is obtained dynamically from an Einstein-Dilaton model, providing greater systematic control over the geometry and its regime of validity. The specific dilaton potential used was previously shown by Finazzo (2014) to describe the tensor glueball spectrum and thermodynamic properties of large \(N\) pure Yang-Mills theory; this work applies that zero-temperature confining background to deep inelastic scattering for the first time, enabling predictions for proton structure at large \(x\) that can be tested against experimental data from SLAC and HERA.
