# Evaluation of cluster expansions and correlated one-body properties of nuclei

> Three different cluster expansions for the evaluation of correlated one-body properties of s-p and s-d shell nuclei are compared. Harmonic oscillator wave functions and Jastrow type correlations are used, while analytical expressions are obtained for the charge form factor, density distribution, and momentum distribution by truncating the expansions and using a standard Jastrow correlation function f. The harmonic oscillator parameter b and the correlation parameter βhave been determined by a least-squares fit to the experimental charge form factors in each case. The information entropy of nuclei in position-space (S_r) and momentum-space (S_k) according to the three methods are also calculated. It is found that the larger the entropy sum S=S_r+S_k (the information content of the system) the smaller the values of χ^2. This indicates that S is a criterion of the quality of a given nuclear model, according to the maximum entropy principle. Only two exceptions to this rule, out of many cases examined, were found. Finally an analytic expression for the so-called "healing" or "wound" integrals is derived with the function f considered, for any state of the relative two-nucleon motion and their values in certain cases are computed and compared.

## Key claim

The paper compares three cluster expansions (FIY, FAHT, LOA) for calculating correlated one-body properties of s-p and s-d shell nuclei, finding that the information entropy sum S = S_r + S_k can serve as a criterion for model quality, with larger S generally corresponding to smaller chi-squared values, though two exceptions to this rule were observed out of many cases examined.

## Method

The authors used harmonic oscillator wave functions with a Jastrow-type correlation function f(r) = 1 - exp[-beta (r_i - r_j)^2] to calculate the charge form factor, density distribution, and momentum distribution for s-p and s-d shell nuclei. Three different cluster expansions (FIY, FAHT, LOA) were truncated at the two-body level (with LOA including a partial three-body term), and the harmonic oscillator parameter b and correlation parameter beta were determined by least-squares fitting to experimental charge form factors for each nucleus. The information entropy in position-space (S_r) and momentum-space (S_k) was calculated for each expansion, and healing integrals w_nl^2 were derived analytically for any state of relative two-nucleon motion.

## Key numbers

- For FIY and FAHT expansions, chi-squared values differed by less than 2% in each nucleus
- In the two exceptions to the entropy criterion (C-12 for FIY vs FAHT, and Si-28 for FIY* vs FAHT*), the difference in chi-squared values was less than 1%
- The correlation parameter beta showed larger values in open shell nuclei (C-12, Mg-24, Si-28, S-32) than in closed shell nuclei (He-4, O-16, Ca-40)
- The high-momentum component of n(k) was increased considerably by short-range correlations, with similar behavior up to 2 fm^-1 for all three expansions, and slight differences in the region 2 fm^-1 < k < 5 fm^-1
- Healing integrals w_nl^2 depend on the dimensionless product y = 2 beta b^2, with values smaller for FIY expansion and larger for LOA expansion

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of cluster expansions for He-4 (Ciofi degli Atti, 1969) to a broader range of s-p and s-d shell nuclei, and generalizes previous calculations using FIY (Massen, 1999; Moustakidis, 2000) and LOA (Stoitsov, 1993) expansions. The information-theoretic criterion for model quality builds on earlier findings (Lalazissis, 1998) that larger entropy S correlates with better nuclear models, and the results enable systematic comparison of different correlation treatments for open-shell nuclei where occupation probabilities can be treated as free parameters.
