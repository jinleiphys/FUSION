# Dyson-Schwinger calculations of meson form factors

> The ladder-rainbow truncation of the set of Dyson-Schwinger equations is used to study the pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors and the $γ^\star π^0 γ$ transition form factor in impulse approximation. With model parameters previously fixed by the pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants, the obtained form factors are in good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations, with model parameters fixed solely by pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants, produces meson form factors in good agreement with experimental data without any parameter readjustment, though numerical limitations prevent precise determination of asymptotic constants.

## Method

The dressed-quark propagator from its Dyson-Schwinger equation, the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the quark-photon vertex from the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation were combined in impulse approximation to compute pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors and the gamma-star-pi-zero-gamma transition form factor. A ladder truncation with an effective quark-antiquark interaction that reduces to the perturbative running coupling at large momenta was used, preserving both the vector Ward-Takahashi identity and the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Context

This work extends the successful application of rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger equations from masses and decay constants of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons (Maris and Roberts, 1997; Maris and Tandy, 1999) to form factor calculations. The results agree with experimental data from Bebek et al. (1978) for pion form factors, Amendolia et al. (1986) for charge radii, and CLEO/CELLO collaborations for the gamma-star-pi-gamma transition form factor. The approach enables systematic calculation of meson electromagnetic properties within a framework that preserves chiral symmetry and current conservation, with the gamma-star-pi-gamma form factor lying between the Brodsky-Lepage asymptotic limit (8 pi^2 f_pi^2) and the DSE limit (16/3 pi^2 f_pi^2).
