# Pion production by neutrinos in the delta resonance region and possible application to CP searches

> We present the results of extensive calculations for charged and neutral current reactions of neutrinos and antineutrinos in the small $Q^2\lsim\unit[0.20]{GeV^2}$ region. The results include single $\frac{\d σ}{\d Q^2}$ and double $\frac{\d σ^{(A)}}{\d Q^2 \d E_π}$ differential cross sections at energies relevant for oscillation experiments. We include nuclear corrections in the Adler-Nussinov-Paschos model and point out that on isoscalar nuclear targets there are charge symmetry relations that hold in extended kinematic regions. We discuss how the results can be used in long baseline experiments in order to study oscillation parameters and search for CP asymmetries.

## Key claim

The authors present extensive calculations for charged and neutral current neutrino and antineutrino reactions in the small momentum transfer region ($Q^2 \lesssim 0.20$ GeV$^2$), claiming that for antineutrino reactions on neutrons the axial current dominates because the vector and interference terms approximately cancel, but this result is specifically derived under the assumption that the $I=3/2$ amplitude dominates and that a small nonresonant background (approximately 10%) is neglected.

## Method

The authors compute single differential cross sections $d\sigma/dQ^2$ and double differential cross sections $d\sigma^{(A)}/dQ^2 dE_\pi$ for neutrino and antineutrino reactions on proton and neutron targets in the delta resonance region, using the Adler-Nussinov-Paschos (ANP) model for nuclear corrections. The pion energy and angular spectra are obtained by computing the $\pi p \rightarrow \Delta \rightarrow \pi p$ cross section in the resonance rest frame and transforming it to the laboratory frame via Lorentz transformations. Nuclear corrections for isoscalar targets are incorporated using a charge-exchange transport matrix originally calculated for carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic region studied is $Q^2 \lesssim 0.20$ GeV$^2$, with the maximum of the differential cross section occurring at $Q^2 \approx 0.02$ GeV$^2$ after integration over pion energy.
- For the benchmark reaction $\nu_\mu p \rightarrow \mu^- \Delta^{++}$, the cross section for $\nu_\mu n \rightarrow \mu^- \Delta^+$ is reduced by a Clebsch-Gordan coefficient factor of $1/3$ relative to the benchmark.
- The nonresonant background for $W \leq 1.6$ GeV is approximately 10% of the delta resonance contribution.
- For nuclear corrections on isoscalar targets, the absorption factor $A$ ranges from 0.791 for carbon to 0.631 for iron.
- The charged current yields on isoscalar targets after nuclear corrections for $\nu_\mu$ are: $\pi^+$ yield = 0.953, $\pi^0$ yield = 0.318, $\pi^-$ yield = 0.075, all multiplied by $A \times \sigma(\nu_\mu p \rightarrow \mu^- \Delta^{++})$.
- For $\overline{\nu}_\mu$ on isoscalar targets, the yields are: $\pi^+$ = 0.075, $\pi^0$ = 0.318, $\pi^-$ = 0.953, multiplied by $A \times \sigma(\overline{\nu}_\mu n \rightarrow \mu^+ \Delta^-)$.
- For neutral currents on isoscalar targets, the yields for both $\nu_\mu$ and $\overline{\nu}_\mu$ are: $\pi^+$ = 0.473, $\pi^0$ = 1.06, $\pi^-$ = 0.473, multiplied by the appropriate standard cross section.

## Context

This work extends the PCAC-based framework for resonance production developed by Paschos (2011) and the coherent scattering calculations by Paschos (2005, 2009) and Berger (2008), providing explicit pion energy spectra and nuclear corrections for the delta resonance region at neutrino energies of 1 to 2 GeV relevant for oscillation experiments. The results enable long-baseline experiments to use charge-symmetric reactions on isoscalar targets to determine neutrino and antineutrino fluxes in near detectors and predict yields in far detectors for studying oscillation parameters and searching for CP asymmetries, with the method extendable to higher resonances as pursued by Kamano (2012) using a dynamical hadron model.
