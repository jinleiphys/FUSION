# Relativistic and Dynamical Love

> Gravitational waves emitted in the late inspiral of binary neutron stars are affected by their tidal deformation. We study the tidal dynamics in full general relativity through matched-asymptotic expansions and prove that the dynamical tidal response can be expanded in a complete set of modes. We further prove that the mode amplitudes satisfy an effective, forced harmonic oscillator equation, which generalizes the overlap-integral formulation of Newtonian gravity. Our relativistic treatment of dynamical tides will avoid systematic biases in future gravitational-wave parameter estimation.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the linearized Einstein-Euler system for a non-rotating neutron star in a tidal environment becomes self-adjoint when the strong-field interior solution is matched asymptotically to a post-Newtonian solution in a buffer zone, enabling a mode-sum decomposition of the dynamical tidal response in full general relativity.

## Method

The authors use matched asymptotic expansions to separate the spacetime into inner body, outer body, buffer, and post-Newtonian zones. They work in harmonic gauge and show that the linearized perturbation equations, when combined with post-Newtonian boundary conditions in the buffer zone, yield a symmetric operator equation whose eigenfunctions form a complete set. They then analytically continue the tidal field inside the star by imposing tidal Hamiltonian and momentum constraints, which allows them to rewrite the perturbed Einstein-Euler system as a forced harmonic oscillator equation for the mode amplitudes, with the driving force expressed as relativistic overlap integrals.

## Context

This work extends the Newtonian mode-expansion formalism for dynamical tides (Press and Teukolsky, 1970s; Lai, 1993) to full general relativity, overcoming the problems that quasi-normal modes of isolated neutron stars cannot form a complete basis and that the tidal field cannot be cleanly separated from the star's self-field in GR. Prior relativistic treatments (Poisson, 2020; Pitre and collaborators, 2023; Hegade K. R. and collaborators, 2024) obtained purely numerical dynamical tidal responses through direct integration of perturbation equations. The present formalism provides a physical mode-sum framework that generalizes the overlap-integral formulation of Newtonian gravity and can be extended to slowly-rotating neutron stars, nonlinear tidal excitations, and tidal interactions in the presence of elastic, electromagnetic, or dark matter fields.
