# Structure and reaction study of Z=120 isotopes using non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field formalism

> An extensive study is carried out for the island of stability in the superheavy nuclei of Z=120 and N=164-220 within the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) and the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approaches. The relativistic G3 and IOPB-I and non-relativistic SLy4 and SkMP parameter sets are used for the investigations. Surface properties such as symmetry energy, neutron pressure and the curvature coefficient of symmetry energy are discussed within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) using the Skyrme and the Brückner energy density functionals. The volume and surface contributions of symmetry energy are evaluated using Danielewicz's liquid drop approximation within the CDFM. The total nuclear reaction and elastic differential cross-sections are also obtained for both SHF and E-RMF within the Glauber model. The peaks in the symmetry energy at N = 182 for SHF and N=184 for E-RMF are seen, which are absent in the Brückner functional. The shifting of peak in the symmetry energy with Brückner functional can be correlated to the Coester-band problem. The enhanced total reaction cross-section for relativistic density of $^{304}120$ suggests the extra stability of this nucleus. This further confirms the shell/sub-shell closure of N = 184 in E-RMF force. The differential cross-section shows its force independent nature and significant increase with the scattering angle.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the enhanced total reaction cross-section for the relativistic density of $^{304}120$ suggests extra stability for this nucleus, confirming a shell/sub-shell closure at neutron number N=184 within the relativistic mean-field formalism, but this enhanced cross-section is not significant for the non-relativistic case for $^{302}120$, indicating the result is force-dependent.

## Method

The authors used the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) formalism with G3 and IOPB-I parameter sets and the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with SLy4 and SkMP parameter sets to calculate ground state properties of Z=120 isotopes with neutron numbers N=164-220. Surface properties including symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and curvature coefficient were computed within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) using both Skyrme and Brückner energy density functionals, with volume and surface contributions evaluated via Danielewicz's liquid drop approximation. Total nuclear reaction cross-sections and elastic differential cross-sections were calculated using the Glauber model with spherical-equivalent densities fitted to two-Gaussian functions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on superheavy nuclei with Z=120, including predictions by Kruppa et al. (2012) for shell gaps at Z=124 and 126 with N=184, Bhuyan et al. (2012) suggesting Z=120 with N=172, 182/184, 208, 258 as neutron magic numbers, and Sil et al. (2004) identifying Z=120 with N=172 and 258 as spherical doubly magic nuclei. The study connects structural properties to reaction dynamics using the Glauber model, enabling experimental guidance for searching the next magic nucleus after $^{208}Pb$ in the superheavy region, and introduces newly derived Skyrme energy density functionals for SLy4 and SkMP forces applied for the first time within the CDFM framework.
