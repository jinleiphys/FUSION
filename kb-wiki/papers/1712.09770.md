# High-energy $μ^+μ^-$ electroproduction

> The cross sections of high-energy $μ^+μ^-$ pair production and paradimuonium production by a relativistic electron in the atomic field are discussed. The calculation is performed exactly in the parameters of the atomic field. Though the Coulomb corrections to the cross sections, related to the multiple Coulomb exchange of $μ^+,\,μ^-$ and an atom, are negligible, the Coulomb corrections to the differential cross sections, related to the electron interaction with an atom, are large. However, the latter Coulomb corrections to the cross sections integrated over the final electron momentum are small. Apparently, this effect can easily be observed experimentally. Furthermore, it is shown that the asymmetry of the cross section with respect to the permutation of the momenta of $μ^-$ and $μ^+$ is large.

# High-energy μ⁺μ⁻ electroproduction

## Key claim

The Coulomb corrections to the differential cross sections of high-energy μ⁺μ⁻ pair production and paradimuonium production by a relativistic electron in an atomic field, those related to the electron's interaction with the atomic field, are large (up to 20–30% deviation from the Born result) for cross sections differential over the final electron transverse momentum, even though the Coulomb corrections to the cross sections integrated over that momentum are negligible.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for μ⁺μ⁻ pair production by a high-energy electron in an atomic field using a quasiclassical approximation that accounts for the electron's interaction with the atomic field exactly in the parameter η = Zα. They separate the amplitude into a term T⁽⁰⁾ (pair non-interacting with the atomic field) and T⁽¹⁾ (electron interaction with the atomic field), using a model potential V(r) = −η/√(r²+R²) for the atomic field. The cross sections are computed for both unbound μ⁺μ⁻ pairs and bound paradimuonium (spin-zero dimuonium), with integration over the transverse momenta of the produced muons.

## Key numbers

- For unbound μ⁺μ⁻ production at ε₁ = 50 m_μ, ω = ε₁/2, ε₃ = ε₄ = ω/2, Z = 79 (gold): the exact cross section differential over p_{2⊥} is 20–30% smaller than the Born result in the region p_{2⊥} ∼ m_e, and about 10% larger than the Born result at p_{2⊥} ≫ m_e.
- For paradimuonium production at ε₁ = 50 m_μ, ω = ε₁/2, Z = 79: the exact result in the peak region is about 30% less than the Born result; in the wide region m_e ≪ p_{2⊥} ≲ m_μ the exact result is about 10% larger than the Born result.
- The asymmetry A of the unbound cross section with respect to permutation of μ⁻ and μ⁺ momenta can reach tens of percent at ω ∼ ε₁ for ε₁ = 50 m_μ, ε₃ = ε₄ = ω/2, p_{4⊥} = m_μ, Z = 79.
- Screening becomes important only for energies ε₁ ≳ (m_μ²)/(α Z^{1/3} m_e) ∼ 1 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of e⁺e⁻ electroproduction by heavy charged particles (Krachkov and Milstein, 2017) and by ultrarelativistic electrons (Krachkov and Milstein, 2016, 2017), where similar large Coulomb corrections to differential cross sections were found. It contradicts the commonly accepted view that Coulomb corrections to μ⁺μ⁻ electroproduction are negligible, as previously assumed in work by Hencken, Kuraev, and Serbo (2007) and others. The results show that using an electron beam as a source of equivalent photons for observing charge asymmetry in μ⁺μ⁻ photoproduction, an asymmetry studied by Di Piazza, Lee, Milstein, and Reiss (2014) arising from next-to-leading quasiclassical corrections, is problematic because the interference between T⁽⁰⁾ and T⁽¹⁾ in electroproduction produces a large asymmetry that obscures the photoproduction effect.
