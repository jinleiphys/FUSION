# QED nuclear medium effects at EIC energies

> We present the first calculation of quantum electrodynamics (QED) nuclear medium effects under the experimental conditions of future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) experiments. Our work offers numerical estimates, particularly in the context of inclusive deep inelastic scattering on a $^{208}_{82}\mathrm{Pb}$ nucleus. While prior studies have predominantly focused on elastic scattering, our investigation extends to the more complex scenarios of inelastic processes within a nuclear medium. Our findings suggest that the cross-section corrections due to QED nuclear medium effects could be substantial, reaching or exceeding the level of experimental precision. This work further compares the effects of single re-scattering events with those of multiple re-scatterings, as particles travel the nuclear volume. We estimate the dominant source of the uncertainties associated with our formalism by varying the scale of the atomic physics where the screening of the electric field of the nucleus happens. This calculation not only contributes to the understanding of QED nuclear medium effects, but also offers a path to a more precise extraction of the process-independent non-perturbative structure of nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first calculation of QED nuclear medium effects for inelastic processes (not just elastic scattering) under the experimental conditions of the future Electron-Ion Collider, finding that cross-section corrections can reach or exceed the level of experimental precision, but only in specific kinematic regimes (low momentum transfer, low beam energy, or edges of phase space).

## Method
The authors evaluate QED nuclear medium corrections to elastic and neutral-current inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross sections on nucleons inside a lead-208 nucleus, using the soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber photon exchanges (SCET_G). They compute corrections at first order in the opacity expansion (single re-scattering) and resummed over multiple interactions (Moliere multiple scattering), averaging over the Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution and varying the atomic screening scale to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Relative cross-section correction at first order in opacity for elastic scattering on lead-208 reaches the percent level when squared momentum transfer approaches 0.2 GeV^2 and lower values, and is energy-independent within the range sqrt(s) = 15, 80, and 140 GeV.
- Resummed elastic scattering corrections reach 1-3% level at the lowest EIC energy (sqrt(s) = 15 GeV) for scattering at small angles.
- For inclusive DIS at first order in opacity, corrections can reach 10% at lowest energies (sqrt(s) = 15 GeV) near kinematic boundaries of the Bjorken variable x.
- Resummed inclusive DIS corrections reach a percent level for x = 10^{-2} and can be enhanced by an additional 10-50% going to lower x values.
- The atomic screening scale is taken as zeta = m_e Z^{1/3} / 192, with uncertainty estimated by varying to zeta = 36 m_e Z^{1/3} / 192 (corresponding to principal quantum number n = 6 for lead-208).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of QED nuclear medium effects in elastic scattering (Tomalak 2022, 2023, 2024) to the more complex case of inelastic processes within a nuclear medium, providing the first numerical estimates for the kinematic regime of the future Electron-Ion Collider and the Electron-Ion Collider in China. The results enable more precise extraction of non-perturbative nuclear structure from EIC data by showing when QED medium corrections must be accounted for, and can be extended to polarized inclusive DIS, semi-inclusive DIS, and exclusive reactions at future colliders.
