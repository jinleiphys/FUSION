# Generalized Cluster Correlation Expansion theory for STIRAP processes in the presence of a spin bath

> The Stimulated Raman Adiabatic Passage (STIRAP) is applied to a system coupled to a bath made of fully-interacting two-level systems, whose dynamics is studied exploiting the generalized Cluster Correlation Expansion (gCCE) theory. We specialize our analysis to a negatively charged silicon vacancy (SiV-1) in non-purified 4H-SiC to assess the possibility of transferring population between two states of the ground manifold, also taking into account the interaction with a spherical nuclear spin bath formed by nuclei of 29Si and 13C. For this system, it is demonstrated that the presence of a small/medium sized bath has no effect on the protocol, finding in particular a set of parameter values for an efficient STIRAP process.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for a negatively charged silicon vacancy (SiV⁻¹) in non-purified 4H-SiC, the presence of a small to medium sized nuclear spin bath (formed by ²⁹Si and ¹³C nuclei) has no effect on the STIRAP protocol, provided a specific set of parameter values is used to achieve efficient population transfer within a time shorter than the system's decoherence time.

## Method

The authors model the SiV⁻¹ defect as an effective five-level system coupled to a spherical bath of fully-interacting spin-1/2 nuclear spins, with system-bath and intra-bath interactions described by dipolar hyperfine and dipolar tensors. They apply the generalized Cluster Correlation Expansion (gCCE) theory to compute the time-dependent populations of the electron spin states, using a product expansion over clusters of increasing size to account for many-body correlations within the bath. The STIRAP protocol is implemented with Gaussian pump and Stokes pulses, with parameters chosen according to the perfect STIRAP framework of Dogra et al. (2022) to achieve population transfer within approximately 1 microsecond.

## Key numbers

- The bath contains approximately 100 nuclear spins for a spherical radius of R = 2 nm, and approximately 1500 nuclear spins for a radius of 5 nm.
- The common laser pulse amplitude is Ω = 35.6 MHz, determined from the adiabaticity condition σΩ ≫ √π/4.
- The external magnetic field is B = 10 mT, and the detuning values studied are Δ = 50 MHz and Δ = 60 MHz.
- The Zero-Field Splitting longitudinal component for SiV⁻¹ in 4H-SiC is D = 2.5 MHz.
- The optical A₂ transition energy is 1.44 eV.
- The decoherence time of the SiV⁻¹ system is approximately 10 μs, while the STIRAP protocol achieves population transfer in T ∼ 1 μs.
- The natural abundance of ²⁹Si is 4.7% and of ¹³C is 1.1% in non-purified samples.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on STIRAP in the presence of spin baths by Militello (2023, 2024), which considered homogeneous models and weak coupling regimes without intra-bath interactions. The present study incorporates fully-interacting bath spins and applies the gCCE method, previously used by Yang et al. (2008, 2020) for decoherence calculations and by Onizhuk (2021) for clock transitions in divacancy systems. The results enable the use of STIRAP as a coherent initialization method for SiV⁻¹ defects in non-purified 4H-SiC, with potential applications in quantum technology platforms where nuclear spin baths are unavoidable.
