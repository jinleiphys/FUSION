# First measurement of the circular beam asymmetry in the gamma p --> pi0 eta p reaction

> The circular photon asymmetry for pi0 eta photoproduction on the proton was measured for the first time at the tagged photon facility of the MAMI C accelerator using the Crystal Ball/TAPS photon spectrometer. The experimental results are interpreted within a phenomenological isobar model that confirms the dominant role of the Delta(1700)D33 resonance. The measured asymmetry allows us to identify small contributions from positive-parity resonances via interference terms with the dominant D33 amplitude.

## Key claim
The circular beam asymmetry in the reaction gamma p -> pi0 eta p was measured for the first time, and the data confirm the dominant role of the Delta(1700)D33 resonance while also revealing small contributions from positive-parity resonances via interference with the D33 amplitude. However, the full isobar model of Fix et al. (2008) shows systematic deviations at higher photon energies (above 1.2 GeV), indicating that further theoretical improvements are needed.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the MAMI C accelerator using the Crystal Ball/TAPS photon spectrometer with a tagged photon beam and a liquid hydrogen target. The circular photon asymmetry W^c(phi) was extracted from the helicity-dependent differential cross sections, corrected for detector acceptance and photon flux, and then fitted with a Fourier expansion over sin(n*phi) terms. The measured asymmetries were compared to predictions from a phenomenological isobar model that includes the dominant D33 amplitude plus contributions from positive-parity resonances (P31, P33, F35) via interference terms.

## Key numbers
- Electron beam polarization: P_e = (80.9 +/- 2.5)%
- Degree of circular photon polarization: varies between 84% and 99% over the photon energy range
- The coefficient A_1 (sin phi term) is the most important; A_2 and A_3 are significantly suppressed
- At photon energies E_gamma > 1.2 GeV, the single D33 model provides a slightly better description of the asymmetry than the full isobar model
- At E_gamma > 1.3 GeV, positive parity resonances cause a strong increase of the sin(2*phi) term in the Fourier expansion

## Context
This work extends previous measurements of total and differential cross sections and linear beam asymmetries for gamma p -> pi0 eta p by Horn et al. (2008), Weinheimer et al. (2000), and others. The circular asymmetry provides a new spin observable that is particularly sensitive to interference between the dominant D33 partial wave and smaller positive-parity resonances, complementing the linear beam asymmetry measurements of Gutz et al. (2010). The results enable further refinement of phenomenological isobar models for pi0 eta photoproduction, which are needed to reliably extract resonance parameters for states such as the P33(1600), P31(1750), F35(1905), and P33(1920).
