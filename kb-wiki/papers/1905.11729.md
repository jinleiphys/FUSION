# Strong isospin symmetry breaking in light scalar meson production

> Isospin symmetry breaking is discussed as a tool for studying the nature and production mechanisms of light scalar mesons. We are concerned with isospin breaking effects with an amplitude $\sim\sqrt{m_d-m_u}$ (instead of the usual $\sim m_d-m_u$), where $m_u$ and $m_d$ are the $u$ and $d$ quark masses, whose magnitude and phase vary with energy in a resonance-like way characteristic of the $K\bar K$ threshold region. We consider a variety of reactions that can experimentally reveal (or have revealed) the mixing of $a^0_0(980)$ and $f_0(980) $ resonances that breaks the isotopic invariance due to the mass difference between $K^+$ and $K^0$ mesons. Experimental results on the search for $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing in $f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)ﾏ^0\toﾏ^+ﾏ^-ﾏ^0$ and $ﾎｷ(14 05)\to f_0(980)ﾏ^0\toﾏ^+ﾏ^-ﾏ^0$ decays suggest a broader perspective on the isotopic symmetry breaking effects due to the $K^+$ and $K^0$ mass difference. It has become clear that not only the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing but also any mechanism producing $K\bar K$ pairs with a definite isospin in an S wave gives rise to such effects, thus suggesting a new tool for studying the nature and production mechanisms of light scalars. ## Key claim

Isospin symmetry breaking in light scalar meson production can exhibit amplitudes proportional to \(\sqrt{m_d-m_u}\) rather than the usual \(\sim m_d-m_u\), with the magnitude and phase varying in a resonance-like manner characteristic of the \(K\bar K\) threshold region, specifically between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds separated by 8 MeV.

## Method

The authors review the \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing mechanism driven by \(K\bar K\) loop diagrams, where the amplitude is calculated from the unitarity condition using the coupling constants of the resonances to the \(K\bar K\) channel. They analyze experimental data from BESIII, VES, and other collaborations on decays such as \(f_1(1285)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\) and \(\eta(1405)\to f_0(980)\pi^0\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\), and discuss the role of anomalous Landau thresholds (logarithmic triangle singularities) in the \(\eta(1405)\) decay chain involving \(K^*\bar K\) intermediate states.

## Key numbers

- The \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds are separated by 8 MeV.
- The estimated mixing amplitude \(|\Pi_{a^0_0f_0}(m\approx 2m_K)| \approx 0.03\) GeV\(^2\).
- For comparison, the \(\rho^0-\omega\) and \(\pi^0-\eta\) mixing amplitudes are approximately 0.003 GeV\(^2\).
- Predicted branching ratio for \(f_0(980)\to K\bar K\to a^0_0(980)\to\eta\pi^0\) is about 0.3\%.
- Predicted branching ratio for \(a^0_0(980)\to K\bar K\to f_0(980)\to\pi^+\pi^-\) is about 0.14\%.
- Estimated average cross section for \(\pi^+\pi^-\to\eta\pi^0\) between the \(K^+K^-\) and \(K^0\bar K^0\) thresholds is approximately 0.39 mb, about 8\% of the unitary limit for the allowed \(S\)-wave process \(\pi^+\pi^-\to\pi^0\pi^0\) (approximately 4.8 mb at \(m\approx 2m_K\)).
- For the reaction \(\pi^-p\to\eta\pi^0n\) at \(P^{\pi^-}_{lab}=18.3\) GeV/\(c\), the one-pion exchange total cross section is approximately 11 nb, about 5.5\% of the total cross section for \(\pi^-p\to a^0_0(980)n\to\eta\pi^0n\) (approximately 200 nb).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov (1979) on \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing as a threshold phenomenon, contrasting it with the well-studied \(\rho^0-\omega\) mixing. It incorporates recent experimental results from BESIII on \(J/\psi\) and \(\chi_{c1}\) decays and from VES at Protvino, showing that not only direct resonance mixing but any mechanism producing \(K\bar K\) pairs with definite isospin in an \(S\)-wave can generate such isospin-breaking effects. The work enables future searches for isospin violation in charmed meson decays (\(D^+_s\), \(D^0\)), bottomonium decays (\(\Upsilon(10860)\to\Upsilon(1S)f_0(980)\)), and central production in \(pp\) collisions, and suggests photoproduction at Jefferson Laboratory as promising for discovering a four-quark tensor state with \(I=2\).
