# Partial dynamical symmetry in Bose-Fermi systems

> We generalize the notion of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) to a system of interacting bosons and fermions. In a PDS, selected states of the Hamiltonian are solvable and preserve the symmetry exactly, while other states are mixed. As a first example of such novel symmetry construction, spectral features of the odd-mass nucleus $^{195}$Pt are analyzed.

# Partial dynamical symmetry in Bose-Fermi systems

## Key claim

The authors generalize the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) from purely bosonic or fermionic systems to mixed Bose-Fermi systems, and demonstrate that selected states of the Hamiltonian remain exactly solvable and preserve the symmetry while other states are mixed. The claim is hedged by the fact that this is presented as a first example of such a construction, applied specifically to the odd-mass nucleus $^{195}$Pt, and the authors note that the results are of a generic nature but are demonstrated only for this one case.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian in the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) framework, starting from the SO$^{\rm BF}$(6) dynamical symmetry limit for a system of $N$ bosons (with $\ell=0$ and $\ell=2$) coupled to a single fermion (with $j=1/2, 3/2, 5/2$). They identify two-particle tensor operators that annihilate specific classes of states (those with U$^{\rm BF}$(6) labels $[N+1]$ and $[N,1]$), and add these operators as interactions to the exactly solvable dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian. The resulting PDS Hamiltonian preserves exact solvability for the ground-band states ($[7,0]\langle7,0\rangle$) while mixing the excited-band states ($[6,1]\langle6,1\rangle$ and $[6,1]\langle5,0\rangle$). The model is applied to $^{195}$Pt, with parameters fitted to reproduce the experimental energy spectrum and 25 measured $B$(E2) transition rates.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square (rms) deviation for the $[7,0]\langle7,0\rangle$ levels in $^{195}$Pt is 12 keV using the DS Hamiltonian.
- The rms deviation for both $[7,0]\langle7,0\rangle$ and $[6,1]\langle6,1\rangle$ levels in the PDS calculation is 20 keV.
- The rms deviation for all levels shown (including $[6,1]\langle5,0\rangle$ states) in the PDS calculation is 23 keV.
- DS Hamiltonian parameters (in keV): $a=45.3$, $b=-41.5$, $c=49.1$, $d=1.7$, $d'=5.6$.
- PDS interaction parameters (in keV): $a'_1=10$, $a_2=-97$, $a_3=112$, $a_0=306$, with $a$ readjusted to $37.7$ keV.
- The $B$(E2) value for the $3/2 \rightarrow 1/2$ transition at 212 keV (solvable to solvable) is $190(10) \times 10^{-3}~e^2{\rm b}^2$ experimentally, compared to 179 in both DS and PDS.
- The $B$(E2) value for the $3/2 \rightarrow 1/2$ transition at 99 keV (mixed to solvable) is $38(6) \times 10^{-3}~e^2{\rm b}^2$ experimentally, compared to 35 in DS and 34 in PDS.
- Effective boson and fermion charges used: $e_{\rm b}=-e_{\rm f}=0.151$ $e$b.

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry, previously developed for bosonic systems (Leviatan 1996, Isacker 1999, Leviatan 2002, Leviatan 2011) and fermionic systems (Escher 2000, Rowe 2001, Isacker 2008), to mixed Bose-Fermi systems for the first time. The specific application to $^{195}$Pt builds on the SO(6) limit of the interacting boson model for even-even platinum isotopes (Arima 1979, Cizewski 1978) and the interacting boson-fermion model description of odd-mass platinum isotopes (Isacker 1984, Bijker 1985). The results enable future exploration of additional PDSs in odd-mass nuclei using operators with deformation parameter $\beta \neq 1$, and open the possibility of studying partial supersymmetry by embedding the Bose-Fermi algebras in graded Lie algebras.
