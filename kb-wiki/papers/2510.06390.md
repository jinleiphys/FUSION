# Proper time expansions and glasma dynamics

> The earliest phase of an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision can be described as a highly populated system of gluons called glasma. The system's dynamics is governed by the classical Yang-Mills equation. Solutions can be found at early times using a proper time expansion. Since the expansion parameter is the time, this method is necessarily limited to the study of early time dynamics. In addition compute time and memory limitations restrict practical calculations to no more than eighth order in the expansion. The result is that the method produces reliable results only for very early times. In this paper we explore several different methods to increase the maximum time that can be reached. We find that, depending slightly on the quantity being calculated, the latest time for which reliable results are obtained can be extended approximately 1.5 times (from $\sim0.05$~fm/$c$ using previous methods to about $0.08$~fm/$c$).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the maximum proper time for which reliable glasma dynamics results can be obtained from a proper time expansion can be extended approximately 1.5 times, from about 0.05 fm/c using previous eighth-order methods to about 0.08 fm/c, depending slightly on the quantity being calculated.

## Method

The authors use three distinct methods to extend the validity of proper time expansions of the classical Yang-Mills equations for glasma: (1) the Li-Kapusta approximation, which assumes two widely separated ultraviolet scales (Λ ≫ Q_s) to extend the expansion to 20th order; (2) Padé approximants constructed from eighth-order results with physically motivated constraints on coefficients; and (3) symbolic regression via the PySR package to learn higher-order coefficients of the transverse-longitudinal pressure anisotropy from data generated using known lower-order coefficients with added random noise.

## Key numbers

- The radius of convergence of the eighth-order proper time expansion is about 0.05–0.06 fm/c, depending on the quantity calculated.
- Using the Li-Kapusta approximation, results become reliable to Λτ ≈ 3.2 with Λ = 8 GeV, giving an upper bound τ ≈ 0.08 fm.
- Padé approximants for the energy density extend reliability to Q_s τ ≈ 1.5 or τ ≈ 0.15 fm (with Q_s = 2.0 GeV).
- Padé approximants for the pressure anisotropy A_TL extend reliability to Q_s τ ≈ 0.8 or τ ≈ 0.08 fm (with Q_s = 2.0 GeV).
- Machine learning yields the 10th-order numerator coefficient n_10 = −2394 ± 47 and 12th-order coefficient n_12 = 2606 ± 76 for A_TL.
- The radial flow P at τ = 0.06 fm for (Q_s, Λ) = (2.0, 8.0) GeV is 0.759 GeV/fm³ in the full calculation and 0.757 GeV/fm³ in the Li-Kapusta approximation, a difference of −0.259%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proper time expansion calculations (Carrington 2023, 2020, 2021, 2024) which were limited to eighth order and very early times (∼0.05 fm/c). The Li-Kapusta method (Li and Kapusta 2016) is adapted to exploit scale separation between the ultraviolet regulator Λ and the saturation scale Q_s. The Padé and machine learning approaches provide complementary techniques to push beyond the convergence radius. The results enable better connection between glasma dynamics and the subsequent hydrodynamic evolution of the system, and relate to studies of hydrodynamic attractors (Heller 2015, Almaalol 2020, Jankowski 2023).
