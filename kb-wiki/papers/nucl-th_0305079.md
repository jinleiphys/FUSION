# Hadronization in heavy ion collisions: recombination or fragmentation?

> We show that hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at transverse momenta larger than 2 GeV/c can be explained by the competition of two different hadronization mechanisms. Above 5 GeV/c hadron production can be described by fragmentation of partons that are created perturbatively. Below 5 GeV/c recombination of partons from the dense and hot fireball dominates. This can explain some of the surprising features of RHIC data like the constant baryon-to-meson ratio of about one and the small nuclear suppression for baryons between 2 to 4 GeV/c.

# Hadronization in heavy ion collisions: recombination or fragmentation?

## Key claim
Hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at transverse momenta above 2 GeV/c can be explained by the competition between two mechanisms: recombination of partons from the dense fireball dominates below 5 GeV/c, while fragmentation of perturbatively created partons dominates above 5 GeV/c. The crossover point is not sharp but varies by hadron species, occurring earlier for pions (around 4 GeV/c) than for protons (around 6 GeV/c).

## Method
The authors developed a covariant recombination formalism using light-cone wave functions and phase-space densities of partons on a hadronization hypersurface, with the narrow-width approximation (distributing hadron momentum equally among valence quarks) shown to deviate by less than 20% from full wave-function calculations. For fragmentation, they used leading-order perturbative QCD calculations of parton production cross sections together with leading-order KKP fragmentation functions, incorporating parton energy loss via a momentum-dependent shift Δp_T = √(λ p_T). The parameters λ (average energy loss) and T (effective temperature of the thermal parton spectrum) were determined by fitting to the inclusive charged hadron spectrum from PHENIX, yielding λ ≈ 1 GeV and T ≈ 350 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Crossover between recombination-dominated and fragmentation-dominated regimes occurs around 5 GeV/c, with pions crossing at approximately 4 GeV/c and protons at approximately 6 GeV/c
- The proton-to-pion ratio shows a plateau of about 1 between 1.5 GeV/c and 4 GeV/c, then decreases steeply to about 0.1 in the fragmentation-dominated regime
- Parton energy loss parameter λ ≈ 1 GeV, determined from fit to PHENIX charged hadron data
- Effective temperature T ≈ 350 MeV for the thermal parton spectrum, containing blue-shift from radial flow (physical temperature expected around 175 MeV)
- Hadronization hypersurface fixed at τ_f = 5 fm/c
- Nuclear suppression factor R_AA for protons nearly reaches a value of 1 below 4 GeV/c, while pions show only moderate growth at low P_T
- Jet quenching suppression can be as large as a factor of 6 above P_T = 5 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends earlier recombination ideas applied to hadron collisions (Das and Hwa, 1977) and heavy ion collisions (Gupta, 1983), and was stimulated by surprising RHIC results including the constant baryon-to-meson ratio and differential nuclear suppression. The formalism builds on covariant treatments developed for baryon coalescence into light nuclei (Dover et al., 1991; Scheibl and Heinz, 1999). The paper enables a unified description of hadron production across the 2-10 GeV/c transverse momentum range at RHIC, and predicts a steep decrease in the proton/pion ratio above 4-6 GeV/c that had not yet been observed at the time of publication.
