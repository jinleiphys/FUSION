# Excited Hadrons and Quark-Hadron Duality

> We review how Quark-Hadron Duality (QHD) for (u,d) flavors at high energies and in the scaling regime suggests a radial and angular behaviour of mesonic and baryonic resonance masses of the Regge form $M^2_{nJ} = μ^2 n + β^2 J + M_0^2 $. The radial mass dependence is asymptotically consistent with a common two-body dynamics for mesons and baryons in terms of the quark-antiquark ($q \bar q)$ and quark-diquark ($qD$) degrees of freedom, respectively. This formula is validated phenomenologically within an uncertainty determined by half the width of the resonances, $ΔM_{nJ}^2 \sim Γ_{nJ} M_{nJ}$. With this error prescription we find from the non-strange PDG hadrons different radial slopes $μ^2_{\bar qq}=1.34(4) {\rm GeV}^2$ and $μ^2_{qD}=0.75(3) {\rm GeV}^2$, but similar angular slopes $β_{\bar q q}^2 \sim β_{ q D}^2 \sim 1.15 {\rm GeV}^2$.

# Excited Hadrons and Quark-Hadron Duality

## Key claim
Quark-Hadron Duality (QHD) for (u,d) flavors at high energies and in the scaling regime implies a Regge-type mass formula M²_nJ = μ² n + β² J + M₀² for both mesons and baryons, but the radial slopes differ between the two systems while the angular slopes are similar. The validation of this formula depends on assigning an uncertainty equal to half the width of each resonance, ΔM²_nJ ~ Γ_nJ M_nJ.

## Method
The authors analyzed non-strange hadrons from the Particle Data Group (PDG) and performed Regge fits to the mass-squared spectra using a chi-squared function where the uncertainty for each resonance is taken as the product of its width and its mass (Γ_exp M_exp). For mesons, the underlying two-body dynamics is quark-antiquark (q\bar q), while for baryons it is quark-diquark (qD), both treated via a Salpeter equation with a linear confining potential in the semiclassical limit.

## Key numbers
- Radial slope for mesons (q\bar q): μ²_q\bar q = 1.34(4) GeV²
- Radial slope for baryons (qD): μ²_qD = 0.75(3) GeV²
- Angular slope for mesons: β²_q\bar q ~ 1.15 GeV²
- Angular slope for baryons: β²_qD ~ 1.15 GeV²
- Quark-diquark string tension from a relativistic model (De Sanctis 2014): σ_qD = 1.57 fm⁻², corresponding to μ²_qD = 0.76 GeV²
- Average width-to-mass ratio for both mesons and baryons: Γ/M = 0.12(8)

## Context
This work extends the concept of Quark-Hadron Duality, reviewed by Melnitchouk (2005), to constrain the asymptotic density of hadronic states. The radial mass dependence derived from QHD is consistent with a linear confining potential in the Salpeter equation, as previously studied by Arriola (2006) for mesons. The quark-diquark picture for baryons follows the relativistic model of De Sanctis (2014), and the finding that μ²_q\bar q is approximately twice μ²_qD supports the interpretation that the PDG hadron spectrum can be organized into q\bar q and qD sectors. The results enable a systematic classification of excited hadrons and may help address the problem of missing baryon resonances.
