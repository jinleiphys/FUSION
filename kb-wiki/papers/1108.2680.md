# Chiral three-nucleon forces and bound excited states in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes

> We study the spectra of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes based on chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. First, we benchmark our many-body approach by comparing ground-state energies to coupled-cluster results for the same two-nucleon interaction, with overall good agreement. We then calculate bound excited states in 21,22,23O, focusing on the role of three-nucleon forces, in the standard sd shell and an extended sdf7/2p3/2 valence space. Chiral three-nucleon forces provide important one- and two-body contributions between valence neutrons. We find that both these contributions and an extended valence space are necessary to reproduce key signatures of novel shell evolution, such as the N = 14 magic number and the low-lying states in 21O and 23O, which are too compressed with two-nucleon interactions only. For the extended space calculations, this presents first work based on nuclear forces without adjustments. Future work is needed and open questions are discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral three-nucleon forces, included consistently to third order in many-body perturbation theory, are necessary but not sufficient to reproduce bound excited states in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes; an extended valence space beyond the standard sd shell is also required to capture key signatures of shell evolution such as the N=14 magic number and the low-lying states in 21O and 23O.

## Method

The authors used many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) to third order, starting from a low-momentum N3LO two-nucleon potential evolved to a resolution scale of 2.0 fm^-1, combined with chiral three-nucleon forces at N2LO. They calculated valence-shell interactions and single-particle energies (SPEs) for valence neutrons in both the standard sd shell and an extended sdf7/2p3/2 valence space, then performed shell-model diagonalizations using the Antoine code. Ground-state energies were benchmarked against coupled-cluster calculations using the same two-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies of oxygen isotopes relative to 16O were calculated; with NN forces only, all isotopes out to 28O remain bound, while inclusion of 3N forces in the extended sdfp space gives very good agreement with experiment.
- In 22O, the first 2+ state energy with NN+3N forces in the extended sdfp space is approximately twice the 2+ energies of 18O and 20O, reproducing the N=14 magic number signature.
- For 23O, the 5/2+ excitation energy with NN+3N forces in the extended sdfp space is brought very close to the experimental value; 3N forces increase this excitation energy by approximately 2 MeV compared to the NN-only result.
- Calculated SPEs in the sd shell: d5/2 = -3.78 MeV, s1/2 = -2.42 MeV, d3/2 = 1.45 MeV; in the sdfp shell: d5/2 = -3.46 MeV, s1/2 = -2.20 MeV, d3/2 = 1.92 MeV, f7/2 = 3.71 MeV, p3/2 = 7.72 MeV.
- In 18O and 20O with NN+3N extended-space calculation, first excited 2+ (4+) energies are 2.25 MeV (3.90 MeV) and 2.14 MeV (4.99 MeV), compared with experimental values 1.98 MeV (3.55 MeV) and 1.67 MeV (3.57 MeV).
- 3N contributions to SPEs range between 1.5-3.0 MeV, approximately an order of magnitude larger than typical normal-ordered two-body matrix elements.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of oxygen isotopes that found two-nucleon forces alone predict too much binding and bound isotopes out to 28O (Oxygen, 2009), and that three-nucleon forces are decisive for explaining why 24O is the heaviest bound oxygen isotope (Oxygen, 2009). It builds on coupled-cluster calculations that showed the dripline location depends on resolution scale with NN forces only (CCoxygen, 2009), and on phenomenological shell-model interactions such as USDb and SDPF-M that were adjusted to correct deficiencies later traced to neglected 3N forces (Zuker, 2003). The results enable future work on theoretical uncertainties from chiral EFT truncation, continuum coupling for unbound states, and nonperturbative approaches to valence-shell interactions such as the in-medium similarity renormalization group.
