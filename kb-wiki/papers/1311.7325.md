# Fluctuation induced equality of multi-particle eccentricities for four or more particles

> We discuss eccentricities (ellipticity and triangularity) generated in nucleus-nucleus and proton-nucleus collisions. We define multi-particle eccentricities $ε_n\{m\}$ which are associated with the $n'th$ angular multipole moment for $m$ particles. We show that in the limit of fluctuation dominance all of the $ε_n\{m\}$'s are approximately equal for $m \ge 4$. For dynamics linearly responding to these eccentricities such as hydrodynamics or proposed in this paper weakly interacting field theory, these relations among eccentricities are translated into relations among flow moments $v_n\{m\}$. We explicitly demonstrate it with hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

In the limit where fluctuations dominate over any mean-field component, the multi-particle eccentricities for four or more particles, $\epsilon_n\{m\}$ with $m \ge 4$, are approximately equal for both elliptic ($n=2$) and triangular ($n=3$) deformations, while the two-particle eccentricity $\epsilon_n\{2\}$ is always larger. This equality holds for both nucleus-nucleus (A+A) and proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions, and under linear response it translates into analogous relations for the flow harmonics $v_n\{m\}$.

## Method

The authors define multi-particle eccentricities $\epsilon_n\{m\}$ from cumulants of the event-by-event distribution of the initial spatial eccentricity $\epsilon_n$, in precise analogy to the standard flow cumulants $v_n\{m\}$. They compute these eccentricities numerically for a simple random-point model (points sampled uniformly in a circle) and for Glauber model simulations of p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions, with additional Gamma-distributed fluctuations in the wounded-nucleon sources. They also demonstrate linear response by performing event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations for p+Pb collisions at 0-10% centrality using Glauber+negative binomial initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- In the random-point model, for $N=10$ points, $\epsilon_2\{4\} \approx \epsilon_2\{6\} \approx \epsilon_2\{8\}$ while $\epsilon_2\{2\}$ is distinctly larger; the same pattern holds for $n=3$.
- In the Glauber model for p+Pb collisions with additional Gamma-distributed fluctuations, $\epsilon_2\{4\}/\epsilon_2\{2\} \simeq 0.7$.
- For very large $N$, $\epsilon_2\{4\}$ decreases as $1/N^{3/4}$ while $\epsilon_2\{2\}$ behaves as $1/N^{1/2}$.
- From event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations for p+Pb at 0-10% centrality (Glauber+NB initial conditions): $v_2\{2\} = 0.082 \pm 0.002$, $v_2\{4\} = 0.055 \pm 0.004$, $v_2\{6\} = 0.052 \pm 0.005$.
- For p+A systems, $\epsilon_n\{m\} \simeq (0.6-0.7) \epsilon_n\{2\}$ for $m \ge 4$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of eccentricity cumulants by Bhalerao (2011) and Voloshin (2007), who noted that subleading effects give nonzero $\epsilon_3\{4\}$ even in fluctuation-dominated systems. It also connects to experimental observations by the ALICE and CMS collaborations (Bilandzic 2012, Chatrchyan 2013) that $v_2\{4\} = v_2\{6\} = v_2\{8\}$ in heavy-ion collisions, and to similar patterns seen in p+A collisions by ATLAS (2013) and CMS (2013). The results enable a direct test of whether initial-geometry-driven explanations (hydrodynamics or weakly interacting field theory) apply in p+A collisions, by comparing the predicted equality of higher-order $v_n\{m\}$ with experimental data. The paper also notes that two independent works by Yan (2013) and Bzdak (2013) appeared shortly after submission, providing analytical calculations of the same effect.
