# Configuration Mixing Effects in Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonance

> Based on an explicit verification of the coupling matrix elements between the 1p1h and 2p2h states we propose a new method of selecting the most important 2p2h states responsible for fragmentation effects. In this way the dimensionality of the problem is reduced, such that the computation becomes feasible and the spreading of the strength is realistic, as verified by some tests of convergence. Calculations in $^{208}$Pb show that due to sizable mixing effects only about 50% of the total isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) $3\hbarω$ strength is located in the energy region between 20 and 25 MeV. This is the energy region which currently is available in experiment. Even above 30 MeV we find about 10% of the total strength. This indicates that the current experimantal evaluations of the ISGDR centroid energy may significantly underestimate its value.

## Key claim

The authors propose that due to configuration mixing between 1p1h and 2p2h states, only about 50% of the total isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) 3ℏω strength in 208Pb lies in the experimentally accessible energy region between 20 and 25 MeV, with about 10% above 30 MeV, suggesting that current experimental evaluations of the ISGDR centroid energy may significantly underestimate its value.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the nuclear Hamiltonian in a combined space of 1p1h and 2p2h states, using a local Woods-Saxon potential including Coulomb interaction for the mean field and a density-dependent zero-range residual interaction. They propose a new selection method that retains only those 2p2h states whose coupling matrix elements with the 1p1h states exceed a threshold value Hth, reducing the dimensionality from approximately 10^6 2p2h states to a computationally feasible number. Calculations are performed for the Jπ=1- sector of 208Pb using six mean field shells (three above and three below the Fermi surface), with the isoscalar dipole operator f=r3Y1 - ηrY1 where η=5⟨r2⟩/3 removes the spurious center of mass motion.

## Key numbers

- Only about 50% of the total ISGDR 3ℏω strength in 208Pb is located in the energy region between 20 and 25 MeV
- About 10% of the total strength is found above 30 MeV
- The number of relevant 2p2h states is of the order of 10^6
- Three threshold values were tested: Hth=0.4 MeV (349 2p2h states included), Hth=0.3 MeV (1125 states), and Hth=0.2 MeV (4374 states)
- The paper suggests the empirical estimation of nuclear incompressibility for 208Pb (KA=126±6 MeV) from Davis et al. (1997) may be too low

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by Drozdz, Nishizaki, Speth, and Wambach (1990, 1994) on configuration mixing effects in nuclear giant resonances, and addresses the specific challenge of the ISGDR which is typically located above 20 MeV. The ISGDR centroid energy can be directly related to the nuclear compression modulus, as shown by Stringari (1982) and de Haro, Krewald, and Speth (1982). The results suggest that experimental evaluations of the ISGDR centroid energy, such as those by Davis et al. (1997), may significantly underestimate its value because a large fraction of the strength lies above 25 MeV where it is dominated by other multipoles and escapes experimental detection.
