# Full heavy dibaryons

> The existence of full heavy dibaryons $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{bbb}$, $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$ with $J=0,~1,~2,~3$ and $P=\pm1$ are investigated in the framework of a constituent quark model. The dibaryon composed of six $c$ or $b$ quarks with $J^{P}=0^{+}$ is possible to be bound, while the dibaryon with the $cccbbb$ configuration is difficult to form any bound state. We also study the interaction between two full heavy baryons, and the effective potentials, suggesting the existence of dibaryons $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$, and the absence of binding for $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{bbb}$ system. Besides, the calculation of the low-energy scattering phase shifts of the $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$, as well as the scattering length also confirm the existence of stable full heavy dibaryons $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ and $Ω_{bbb}Ω_{bbb}$.

# Full heavy dibaryons

## Key claim

The dibaryons composed of six charm quarks (ΩcccΩccc) or six bottom quarks (ΩbbbΩbbb) with quantum numbers J^P = 0^+ are possible bound states, while the mixed system ΩcccΩbbb with three charm and three bottom quarks does not form any bound state. The binding arises specifically from the antisymmetrization requirement between identical baryon clusters, which introduces an attractive interaction absent in the mixed system.

## Method

The authors employed a constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian including confinement potential and one-gluon-exchange (OGE) interaction, neglecting spin-orbit and tensor forces for ground-state calculations. They used the resonating group method (RGM) and generating coordinates method to perform dynamical calculations of the six-quark systems, solving a generalized eigenvalue problem for the energy and wavefunctions. Low-energy scattering phase shifts were computed using the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method, and scattering lengths and effective ranges were extracted from the phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Ωccc baryon mass: 5068.8 MeV; Ωbbb baryon mass: 15111.6 MeV
- ΩcccΩccc threshold: 10137.6 MeV; ΩbbbΩbbb threshold: 30223.2 MeV; ΩcccΩbbb threshold: 20180.4 MeV
- ΩcccΩccc with J^P = 0^+: energy 10136.3 MeV, binding energy -1.3 MeV below threshold
- ΩbbbΩbbb with J^P = 0^+: energy 30222.9 MeV, binding energy -0.3 MeV below threshold
- ΩcccΩccc scattering length a0 = 3.1572 fm; effective range r0 = 1.1729 fm
- ΩbbbΩbbb scattering length a0 = 3.7003 fm; effective range r0 = 1.1644 fm
- All other quantum number combinations (J=1,2,3 with appropriate parity) for ΩcccΩccc and ΩbbbΩbbb lie above threshold; all ΩcccΩbbb states lie at 20181.4 MeV, above the 20180.4 MeV threshold

## Context

This work extends previous constituent quark model studies of strange dibaryons NΩ and ΩΩ by the same group (Huang et al., prior work) to the full heavy sector with only charm and bottom quarks. The results contradict the potential model study of Richard (prior work), which found no evidence for stable states in very heavy six-quark systems with cccbbb configurations. The authors suggest that lattice QCD calculations, which have studied deuteron-like heavy dibaryons such as ΩcΩcc and ΩbΩbb (Junnarkar and Mathur, prior work), should check whether the full heavy dibaryons ΩcccΩccc and ΩbbbΩbbb with J^P = 0^+ exist. The work is motivated by the recent LHCb observation of the full-charm tetraquark X(6900) and the application of heavy-antiquark-diquark symmetry.
