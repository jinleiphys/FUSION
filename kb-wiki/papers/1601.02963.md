# Nuclear collisions at the Future Circular Collider

> The Future Circular Collider is a new proposed collider at CERN with centre-of-mass energies around 100 TeV in the pp mode. Ongoing studies aim at assessing its physics potential and technical feasibility. Here we focus on updates in physics opportunities accessible in pA and AA collisions not covered in previous Quark Matter contributions, including Quark-Gluon Plasma and gluon saturation studies, novel hard probes of QCD matter, and photon-induced collisions.

## Key claim

The Future Circular Collider (FCC) would enable qualitatively new studies of the Quark-Gluon Plasma and gluon saturation in pA and AA collisions at centre-of-mass energies around 100 TeV in pp mode, but the physics opportunities described are contingent on the technical feasibility and detector design studies that are still ongoing, with target operation start around 2035-2040.

## Method

The authors review extrapolations of soft observables (multiplicity, volume, lifetime, energy density, temperature) from LHC to FCC energies using parametric models and viscous hydrodynamics calculations. They estimate cross-section increases for hard probes (top, Z, bottom, jets) using next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD, and evaluate the potential for secondary charm production using rate equations within 2+1 hydrodynamical evolution. The study also examines the kinematic reach of ultraperipheral collisions for constraining nuclear parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- PbPb collisions at FCC would have centre-of-mass energy per nucleon pair sqrt(s_NN) ~ 39 TeV, compared to ~ 5.5 TeV at the LHC
- pPb collisions at FCC would have sqrt(s_NN) ~ 63 TeV
- Peak luminosity for PbPb: 13 x 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1; integrated luminosity: 8 nb^-1 per month
- Peak luminosity for pPb: 3.5 x 10^30 cm^-2 s^-1; integrated luminosity: 1800 nb^-1 per month
- Charged particle multiplicity expected to increase by about a factor of two from top LHC to FCC energy
- Energy density at FCC reaches 35 GeV/fm^3 at 1 fm/c, about a factor of two higher than at LHC
- Initial temperature could be as large as T0 ~ 800 MeV if formation time is significantly lower than 0.1 fm/c
- Cross-section increases from LHC to FCC: top production by factor 80, Z+1 jet (pT > 50 GeV/c) by factor 20, bottom by factor 8, Z by factor 7
- Expected yields per year: tens of thousands of t tbar -> b bbar l lbar nu nubar events in PbPb and pPb; thousands of t W -> b l lbar nu nubar events

## Context

This paper extends the physics case for nuclear collisions at the FCC presented in earlier Quark Matter contributions (Armesto 2014) and complements studies for a future hadron accelerator in China (Chang 2015). The work builds on extrapolations from LHC heavy-ion data and lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi 2014) to assess new opportunities in QGP studies, hard probes including top quarks, and photon-induced collisions. The results enable quantitative comparisons with future electron-ion colliders such as the LHeC and FCC-he, and motivate detector design studies for a heavy-ion programme with general-purpose detectors at the FCC.
