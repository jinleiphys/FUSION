# An Effective Field Theory Calculation of the Parity Violating Asymmetry in n+p -> d+gamma

> Weak interactions are expected to induce a parity violating pion-nucleon coupling, h_{πNN}^{(1)}. This coupling should be measurable in a proposed experiment to study the parity violating asymmetry A_γin the process \vec n + p \to d+γ. We compute the leading dependence of A_γon the coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)} using recently developed effective field theory techniques and find an asymmetry of A_γ= +0.17 h_{πNN}^{(1)} at leading order. This asymmetry has the opposite sign to that given by Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein.

# An Effective Field Theory Calculation of the Parity Violating Asymmetry in n+p -> d+gamma

## Key claim

The parity violating asymmetry A_γ in polarized cold neutron capture on protons is computed to leading order in an effective field theory expansion, yielding A_γ = +0.17 h_{πNN}^{(1)}. This result has the opposite sign to the prediction of Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1980), and the calculation is performed at leading order in a momentum expansion where the relevant scale Q ~ 45.7 MeV is set by the deuteron binding energy.

## Method

The authors use the effective field theory (EFT) approach of Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) for two-nucleon systems, which treats the large scattering lengths in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels nonperturbatively. The parity violating asymmetry arises from interference between the parity-conserving amplitude (computed from diagrams with nucleon magnetic moment couplings) and the parity-violating amplitude (computed from diagrams with an insertion of the ΔI=1 weak pion-nucleon coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)}). The leading-order calculation includes one-loop and two-loop diagrams where the neutron and proton rescatter through a contact interaction, with the photon coupling to the exchanged pion.

## Key numbers

- A_γ = +0.17 h_{πNN}^{(1)} at leading order in the effective field theory expansion for the process n + p → d + γ with cold neutrons
- The deuteron binding energy is B = 2.224 MeV, giving the momentum scale Q = √(M_N B) = 45.70 MeV
- The isovector nucleon magnetic moment is κ_1 = 2.35294 in units of nuclear magnetons
- The 1S0 NN scattering length is a_0 = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm
- The pion mass is m_π ≈ 140 MeV and the pion decay constant is f_π = 132 MeV
- The axial coupling constant is g_A = +1.25
- The current experimental limit on A_γ is -(1.5 ± 4.8) × 10^{-8}, and the proposed experiment expects precision of ±5 × 10^{-9}
- A naive dimensional analysis estimate gives |h_{πNN}^{(1)}| ~ 5 × 10^{-7}, implying |A_γ| ~ 0.8 × 10^{-7}

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory techniques developed by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) for two-nucleon electromagnetic processes to include parity violation. The calculation contradicts the sign of the asymmetry found by Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1980) and Desplanques and Missimer (1978), who obtained A_γ ~ -0.11 h_{πNN}^{(1)}. The result enables a relatively clean determination of the weak pion-nucleon coupling h_{πNN}^{(1)} from the proposed cold neutron capture experiment, provided this coupling is not anomalously small as suggested by circular polarization experiments in 18F.
