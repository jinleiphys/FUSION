# Non-Gaussian eccentricity fluctuations

> We study the fluctuations of the anisotropy of the energy density profile created in a high-energy collision at the LHC. We show that the anisotropy in harmonic $n$ has generic non-Gaussian fluctuations. We argue that these non-Gaussianities have a universal character for small systems such as p+Pb collisions, but not for large systems such as Pb+Pb collisions where they depend on the underlying non-Gaussian statistics of the initial density profile. We generalize expressions for the eccentricity cumulants $\varepsilon_2\{4\}$ and $\varepsilon_3\{4\}$ previously obtained within the independent-source model to a general fluctuating initial density profile.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-Gaussian eccentricity fluctuations in high-energy collisions are generic, but their character differs between small systems (p+Pb) where they are universal, and large systems (Pb+Pb) where they depend on the specific non-Gaussian statistics of the initial density profile. The authors generalize previous expressions for eccentricity cumulants beyond the independent-source model to arbitrary fluctuating initial density profiles.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative expansion of eccentricity fluctuations in terms of the underlying density fluctuations, assuming fluctuations are small and uncorrelated over short distances (locality hypothesis). They express the fourth-order cumulant of eccentricity \(\varepsilon_n\{4\}^4\) in terms of the second, third, and fourth cumulants of the energy density field (\(\kappa_2\), \(\kappa_3\), \(\kappa_4\)), and validate their perturbative results against Monte Carlo simulations using both identical sources and weighted sources (negative binomial fluctuations).

## Key numbers

- For a Gaussian average density profile with identical sources, \(\varepsilon_2\{2\}^2 = 2/N\) and \(\varepsilon_2\{4\}^4 = 16/N^3\) in the large-\(N\) limit (Eq. 5, from exact result Eq. 4).
- For a uniform average density profile in a disk, \(\varepsilon_2\{2\}^2 = 4/(3N)\) and \(\varepsilon_2\{4\}^4 = 368/(135N^3)\) (Eq. 7).
- The perturbative result for identical sources with Poisson fluctuations gives \(\varepsilon_n\{4\}^4 = 8/\langle N\rangle^3\) for a Gaussian profile (Eq. 13), a factor of 2 smaller than the fixed-\(N\) case.
- The effective number of sources is defined as \(N_{\rm eff} \equiv (\int_z \kappa_1(z))^2 / \int_z \kappa_2(z)\), which for weighted sources becomes \(N_{\rm eff} = \langle w\rangle^2 \langle N\rangle / \langle w^2\rangle\) (Eq. 14).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative analysis of eccentricity fluctuations initiated in Blaizot (2014) and generalizes earlier results from the independent-source model by Alver (2008) for \(\varepsilon_2\) and Bhalerao (2011) for \(\varepsilon_3\). The paper shows that the observation of positive \(v_n\{4\}\) in experiments (ALICE 2011, Chatrchyan 2013) directly indicates that the density field has positive skew, and that the universal relation between \(\varepsilon_2\{4\}\) and \(\varepsilon_2\{2\}\) proposed by Yan (2013) does not hold for all density profiles. The results enable using eccentricity cumulants to extract information about the non-Gaussian statistics of the initial energy density profile in heavy-ion collisions.
