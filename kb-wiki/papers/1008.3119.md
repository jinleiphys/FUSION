# Single Transverse-Spin Asymmetry in Hard-Exclusive Meson Electroproduction in the Backward Region

> We discuss the relevance of studying single transverse-spin asymmetry in hard-exclusive meson electroproduction in the backward region. Such an asymmetry could help us discriminate between contributions from a soft baryon exchange in the u-channel and a hard parton-induced scattering.

# Single Transverse-Spin Asymmetry in Hard-Exclusive Meson Electroproduction in the Backward Region

## Key claim

The study of target single transverse-spin asymmetry (SSA) in hard-exclusive meson electroproduction in the backward region can discriminate between contributions from a soft baryon exchange in the u-channel and a hard parton-induced scattering, provided that the asymmetry is leading-twist and of the same order as the unpolarised cross section for the hard-parton case, whereas it would decrease for increasing W² and Q² in the soft-exchange case.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order-in-αₛ amplitude for γ*P → P'π⁰ in the backward kinematics (small u = (p_π − p₁)²) using a factorised framework where a perturbatively calculable hard-scattering amplitude is convoluted with proton Distribution Amplitudes and baryon-to-meson Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs). They then evaluate the spin-dependent cross-section difference σ^(s₁) − σ^(−s₁) by summing over photon polarisations and final-proton spins, retaining only the leading-twist contributions, and express the resulting SSA in terms of the imaginary part of the product of two TDA-dependent integrals I and I'.

## Key numbers

- The hard-scattering amplitude scales as 1/Q⁴, with the overall factor |C|²/Q⁸ appearing in the spin-summed squared amplitude.
- The spin asymmetry σ^(s₁) − σ^(−s₁) is proportional to (|C|²/Q⁶) × (|Δ_T|/M) × ((1+ξ)/ξ) × sin(φ − φ_S) × Im(I' I*), where Q² is the virtual-photon squared momentum, Δ_T is the transverse momentum transfer, M is the proton mass, and ξ is the skewness parameter.
- The asymmetry is leading-twist as soon as Δ_T ≠ 0 and the integrals I or I' are not purely real or purely imaginary, which occurs when DGLAP-region contributions (one x_i < 0) are included.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier framework for backward pion electroproduction using TDAs (Lansberg, Pire, Szymanowski, 2007) and the concept of Generalised Parton Distributions (GPDs) pioneered by Frankfurt et al. (1999). It contrasts with the meson-cloud model study of TDAs by Pasquini et al. (2009) and with spectral-representation work by Pire et al. (2010). The proposed SSA measurement enables model-independent tests of whether hard parton-induced scattering or soft baryon exchange dominates in the backward regime, and motivates experimental searches at large Q² and W².
