# Probing the isovector transition strength of the low-lying nuclear excitations induced by inverse kinematics proton scattering

> A compact approach based on the folding model is suggested for the determination of the isoscalar and isovector transition strengths of the low-lying ($ΔS=ΔT=0$) excitations induced by inelastic proton scattering measured with exotic beams. Our analysis of the recently measured inelastic $^{18,20}$O+p scattering data at $E_{\rm lab}=30$ and 43 MeV/nucleon has given for the first time an accurate estimate of the isoscalar $β_0$ and isovector $β_1$ deformation parameters (which cannot be determined from the (p,p') data alone by standard methods) for 2$^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ excited states in $^{18,20}$O. Quite strong isovector mixing was found in the 2$^+_1$ inelastic $^{20}$O+p scattering channel, where the strength of the isovector form factor $F_1$ (prototype of the Lane potential) corresponds to a $β_1$ value almost 3 times larger than $β_0$ and a ratio of nuclear transition matrix elements $M_n/M_p\simeq 4.2$.

## Key claim
The paper presents a folding-model approach that can determine both isoscalar and isovector deformation parameters from inelastic proton scattering data alone, without requiring separate (p,p') and (n,n') measurements. The method reveals that the isovector mixing in the 2⁺₁ excitation of ²⁰O is unexpectedly strong, with the isovector deformation parameter β₁ nearly three times larger than the isoscalar β₀.

## Method
The author constructs proton and neutron transition densities using the Bohr-Mottelson prescription separately for each species, then folds them with a density- and isospin-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (CDM3Y6) to obtain the inelastic proton-nucleus form factor in terms of isoscalar and isovector components. The proton deformation length is fixed to reproduce the measured B(Eλ↑) value from gamma-decay data, leaving only the neutron deformation length as a free parameter to be determined from a DWBA fit to the inelastic scattering data. This analysis is applied to elastic and inelastic ¹⁸,²⁰O+p scattering data at 30 and 43 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- For the 2⁺₁ state in ¹⁸O: β₀ = 0.401 ± 0.016, β₁ = 0.861 ± 0.034, Mₙ/Mₚ = 1.80 ± 0.13, M₁/M₀ = 0.286 ± 0.023
- For the 3⁻₁ state in ¹⁸O: β₀ = 0.456 ± 0.014, β₁ = 0.432 ± 0.016, Mₙ/Mₚ = 1.35 ± 0.08, M₁/M₀ = 0.149 ± 0.010
- For the 2⁺₁ state in ²⁰O (43 MeV data): β₀ = 0.500 ± 0.020, β₁ = 1.295 ± 0.052, Mₙ/Mₚ = 4.25 ± 0.28, M₁/M₀ = 0.619 ± 0.050
- For the 2⁺₁ state in ²⁰O (30 MeV data): β₀ = 0.489 ± 0.019, β₁ = 1.248 ± 0.050, Mₙ/Mₚ = 4.13 ± 0.27, M₁/M₀ = 0.611 ± 0.050
- For the 3⁻₁ state in ²⁰O: β₀ = 0.401 ± 0.012, β₁ = 0.308 ± 0.009, Mₙ/Mₚ = 1.55 ± 0.09, M₁/M₀ = 0.216 ± 0.013
- The ratio of volume integrals of the isovector to isoscalar parts of the real folded optical potential is J₁/J₀ ≈ -0.37 for both ¹⁸O and ²⁰O
- The adopted average Mₙ/Mₚ for the 2⁺₁ state in ²⁰O is 4.2 ± 0.3

## Context
This work extends the folding-model approach previously developed for elastic scattering (Khoa, 1996, 2002) to inelastic channels, providing a method to extract isovector transition strengths that previously required simultaneous (p,p') and (n,n') measurements (Finlay, 1979; Grawert, 1980). The results for ¹⁸O agree with earlier determinations from (p,p') and (n,n') data at 24 MeV (Grawert, 1980) and with the weighted average of Mₙ/Mₚ ≈ 2 from multiple studies (Jensen, 1999). For ²⁰O, the extracted Mₙ/Mₚ ≈ 4.2 is significantly larger than the values of 3.25 ± 0.80 obtained by Khan (2000) using QRPA densities or 2.9 ± 0.4 from a simple collective model analysis (Jensen, 1999). The approach enables determination of isospin distributions in low-lying excited states of exotic nuclei using only inverse-kinematics proton scattering data, which is important for unstable beam experiments where simultaneous (n,n') measurements are not feasible.
