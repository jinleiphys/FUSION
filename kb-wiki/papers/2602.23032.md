# Nuclear binding, correlations, and the $A$-dependence of the EMC effect

> The measurements of inclusive electron scattering from nuclear targets carried out at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility in the mid 2000s have provided valuable novel information on the $A$-dependence of the modifications of nuclear structure functions known as EMC effect. We argue that these data are best described in terms of the scaling variable $\widetilde{y}$, designed to take into account dynamical effects in interacting many-particle systems, and analyse the $A$-dependence of the slope of the inclusive cross section ratios, $R_A = (σ_A/A)/(σ_2/2)$, providing a measure of the size of the EMC effect in the region where nuclear binding plays a leading role. The results of our study clearly hint at a linear correlation between $dR_A(\widetilde{y})/d\widetilde{y}$ and the average nucleon removal energy $\langle E_A \rangle$. The role of correlation effects in the determination of $\langle E_A \rangle$ is highlighted.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the EMC effect, the modification of nuclear structure functions per nucleon relative to deuterium, is best described using the scaling variable \(\widetilde{y}\) rather than the Bjorken variable \(x\), and that the slope of the cross-section ratio \(R_A\) as a function of \(\widetilde{y}\) exhibits a linear correlation with the average nucleon removal energy \(\langle E_A \rangle\), with short-range correlations (SRC) contributing significantly to \(\langle E_A \rangle\) but the linear relationship being largely independent of the microscopic nuclear dynamics model.

## Method

The authors analyzed inclusive electron scattering cross sections from Jefferson Lab (E03-103 and E12-10-008 experiments) and SLAC (E139 experiment) for nuclei including \({}^{2}\)H, \({}^{3}\)He, \({}^{4}\)He, \({}^{9}\)Be, \({}^{10}\)B, \({}^{11}\)B, \({}^{12}\)C, and \({}^{40}\)Ca, as well as symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) from an \(A \to \infty\) extrapolation. They computed the slopes \(dR_A/d\widetilde{y}\) of the cross-section ratios \(R_A = (\sigma_A/A)/(\sigma_2/2)\) in the region \(0.35 < x < 0.7\) and correlated these slopes with average nucleon removal energies \(\langle E_A \rangle\) obtained from the Galitskii–Migdal–Koltun sum rule, using ground-state expectation values from Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations for \(A \leq 12\) and cluster variational Monte Carlo (CVMC) for \({}^{40}\)Ca, with the Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon and Illinois-7 or Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

* The average nucleon removal energy \(\langle E_A \rangle\) for \({}^{12}\)C is 51.589 MeV, obtained from GFMC calculations using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) and Illinois-7 potentials.
* The average nucleon removal energy \(\langle E_A \rangle\) for \({}^{4}\)He is 34.427 MeV, obtained from GFMC calculations using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) and Illinois-7 potentials.
* The average nucleon removal energy \(\langle E_A \rangle\) for \({}^{9}\)Be is 42.261 MeV, obtained from GFMC calculations using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) and Illinois-7 potentials.
* The average nucleon removal energy \(\langle E_A \rangle\) for \({}^{40}\)Ca, corrected using the experimental ground-state energy, is 47.446 MeV, obtained from CVMC calculations using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) and Urbana IX potentials.
* For symmetric nuclear matter at equilibrium density, the average removal energy computed from the spectral function of Benhar (1994) is within less than 3% of the value obtained from the Koltun sum rule using the results of Akmal and Pandharipande (1997).
* In symmetric nuclear matter, the strength located at momenta larger than the Fermi momentum \(p_F \approx 260\) MeV contributes 37% of the average removal energy.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering study of Benhar (2012) by using the scaling variable \(\widetilde{y}\), originally proposed by Benhar (2000), which has a clear physical interpretation in the target rest frame and allows unambiguous identification of nuclear binding effects. The results support the linear correlation between the EMC slope and \(\langle E_A \rangle\) first noted by Arrington (2012), but provide a more thorough treatment of short-range correlations in the determination of \(\langle E_A \rangle\) using state-of-the-art quantum Monte Carlo methods. The analysis enables a largely model-independent explanation of the origin and \(A\)-dependence of the EMC effect in the kinematic region where nuclear binding plays a leading role, and clarifies the role of SRC by showing they contribute 37% of the average removal energy in nuclear matter.
