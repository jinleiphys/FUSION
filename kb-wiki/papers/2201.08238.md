# Strangeness ${S=-3}$ and ${-4}$ baryon-baryon interactions in chiral effective field theory

> Studies of the baryon-baryon interaction involving hyperons within chiral effective field theory, so far performed up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the chiral expansion, have shown that for the strangeness $S=-1$ ($ΛN$, $ΣN$) and $S=-2$ ($ΛΛ$, $ΞN$) sectors a consistent and satisfactory description of the available scattering data and experimental constraints can be achieved based on the assumption of broken SU(3) flavor symmetry. We explore the possible extension of this approach at the NLO level to strangeness $S=-3$ and $S=-4$ baryon-baryon systems where empirical information is rather scarce. Specifically we address the question how measurements of two-body correlation functions in heavy-ion collisions and/or in high-energetic proton-proton collisions can help to constrain the interaction in channels like $ΞΛ$ or $ΞΞ$.

## Key claim
The authors extend chiral effective field theory (χEFT) at next-to-leading order (NLO) to strangeness \(S=-3\) and \(S=-4\) baryon-baryon systems, but they cannot fix the additional SU(3) symmetry-breaking low-energy constants (LECs) without experimental data; instead, they show that two-particle momentum correlation functions measured in heavy-ion or proton-proton collisions can provide the needed constraints.

## Method
The baryon-baryon interaction is constructed within SU(3) χEFT up to NLO, including pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and contact terms with LECs. For \(S=-3\) and \(S=-4\), the LECs are taken from fits to the \(S=-2\) sector (specifically the \(\Xi N\) and \(\Lambda\Lambda\) systems) using the NLO16 and NLO19 potentials. The two-particle momentum correlation functions \(C(k)\) are computed using the Koonin-Pratt formalism with a Gaussian source function, including coupled-channel effects and Coulomb interaction where relevant.

## Key numbers
- For \(\Xi^-p\) correlation functions, the NLO19 potential with source radii \(R = 1.43\) fm (for \(p\)-Pb at 5.02 TeV) and \(R = 1.18\) fm (for \(pp\) at 13 TeV) agrees with ALICE data.
- For the NLO16 potential, source radii \(R = 1.27\) fm (5.02 TeV) and \(R = 1.05\) fm (13 TeV) from Kamiya et al. (2021) are used.
- For \(\Xi^-\Lambda\) with LECs from NLO16, the scattering lengths are \(a_s = -0.99\) to \(-0.89\) fm (\({}^{1}S_0\)) and \(a_t = 0.026\) to \(-0.12\) fm (\({}^{3}S_1\)).
- For \(\Xi^-\Lambda\) with LECs from NLO19, the \({}^{3}S_1\) scattering lengths are \(a_t = -0.42\) to \(-1.66\) fm.
- For \(\Xi^-\Xi^-\), predictions are shown for source radii \(R = 1.0\) fm and \(R = 2.5\) fm, with three scenarios for the SU(3) symmetry-breaking LEC \(C^\chi_2\).

## Context
This work extends the Juelich-Bonn-Munich Collaboration's χEFT studies from \(S=-1\) (Haidenbauer et al., 2013, 2019) and \(S=-2\) (Haidenbauer et al., 2015, 2019) to \(S=-3\) and \(S=-4\). Prior LO extensions (Haidenbauer and Meissner, 2010; Liu et al., 2020) predicted strongly attractive interactions that are ruled out by preliminary ALICE data on \(\Xi^-\Lambda\) and STAR data on \(\Xi^-\Xi^-\). The results enable future fits of SU(3) symmetry-breaking LECs once final correlation-function data become available.
