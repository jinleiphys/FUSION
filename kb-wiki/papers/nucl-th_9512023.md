# Chiral Perturbation Approach to the pp -> pp pi0 Reaction Near Threshold

> The usual theoretical treatments of the near-threshold $pp \rightarrow ppπ^0$ reaction are based on various phenomenological Lagrangians. In this work we examine the relationship between these approaches and a systematic chiral perturbation method. Our chiral perturbation calculation indicates that the pion rescattering term should be significantly enhanced as compared with the traditional phenomenological treatment, and that this term should have substantial energy and momentum dependence. An important consequence of this energy-momentum dependence is that, for a representative threshold kinematics and within the framework of our semiquantitative calculation, the rescattering term interferes destructively with the Born-term in sharp contrast to the constructive interference obtained in the conventional treatment. This destructive interference makes theoretical cross sections for $pp \rightarrow ppπ^0$ much smaller than the experimental values, a feature that suggests the importance of the heavy-meson exchange contributions to explain the experimental data.

## Key claim

The chiral perturbation calculation indicates that the pion rescattering term should be significantly enhanced compared to traditional phenomenological treatments, and that for representative threshold kinematics this rescattering term interferes destructively with the Born term (rather than constructively as in conventional treatments), making theoretical cross sections much smaller than experimental values and suggesting the importance of heavy-meson exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors applied heavy-fermion formalism chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to the near-threshold pp → ppπ⁰ reaction, calculating tree diagrams up to chiral order ν=1 (Born term and rescattering term) and including loop corrections to the Born term that are of the same chiral order as the leading rescattering diagram. They compared the resulting effective ππNN vertex strength κ(k,q) with the phenomenological Hamiltonian parameters λ₁ and λ₂, using empirical values for the low-energy constants c₁, c₂, c₃ determined from πN scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell χPT value for 4πλ₁/mπ is (0.87 ± 0.20) GeV⁻¹, about twice the conventional value of (0.43 ± 0.20) GeV⁻¹ (λ₁ = 0.005 ± 0.002)
- For typical threshold kinematics (q ∼ (mπ, 0), k ∼ (½mπ, √(mπmN))), the rescattering vertex strength κth gives 4πλ₁/mπ ∼ -1.5 GeV⁻¹, opposite in sign to the on-shell case
- The low-energy constants used are: c₁ = -0.87 ± 0.11 GeV⁻¹, c₂ = 3.34 ± 0.27 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -5.25 ± 0.22 GeV⁻¹, based on σπN(0) = 45 ± 8 MeV, αA = 2.28 ± 0.10 mπ⁻³, and a⁺ = (-0.83 ± 0.38)·10⁻² mπ⁻¹
- The exchanged pion in the rescattering process probes distances ∼ 0.5 fm, corresponding to an effective exchanged mass √(mπmN) = 370 MeV

## Context

This work extends the nuclear chiral perturbation theory approach of Weinberg (1990) and Park, Min and Rho (1993) to the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction, which had previously been treated with phenomenological Lagrangians by Koltun and Reitan (1966) and others. The calculations confirm the importance of off-shell effects in the rescattering amplitude as emphasized by Hernández and Oset (1995), but find the opposite sign for the off-shell coupling strength, leading to destructive rather than constructive interference with the Born term. This result supports the earlier suggestion by Lee and Riska (1993) that heavy-meson exchange contributions (scalar and vector exchanges) are needed to explain the experimental cross sections, which are a factor of ∼5 larger than early theoretical predictions.
