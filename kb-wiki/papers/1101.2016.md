# Observation of quark-hadron duality in gamma*-p helicity cross sections

> Combining data on unpolarized and polarized inclusive proton structure functions, we perform the first detailed study of quark-hadron duality in individual helicity-1/2 and 3/2 virtual photoproduction cross sections. We find that duality is realized more clearly in the helicity-1/2 channel, with duality violating corrections < 10% over the entire nucleon resonance region, while larger, < 20% corrections are found in the helicity-3/2 sector. The results are in general agreement with quark model expectations, and suggest that data above the Delta resonance region may be used to constrain both spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distributions.

# Observation of quark-hadron duality in gamma*-p helicity cross sections

## Key claim

Quark-hadron duality holds more clearly in the helicity-1/2 virtual photoproduction cross section than in the helicity-3/2 channel, with duality violating corrections less than 10% for H1/2 and less than 20% for H3/2 over the entire nucleon resonance region at Q2 ≤ 4 GeV2; this is considerably smaller than the duality violation found in the spin-dependent g1 structure function alone.

## Method

The authors combined Jefferson Lab EG1b measurements of the g1/F1 ratio with the Christy-Bosted global fit for the unpolarized F1 structure function, using a phenomenological parametrization for the g2 correction, to construct experimental helicity structure functions H1/2 and H3/2. They compared these resonance-region data (W < 2 GeV) with theoretical helicity structure functions derived from the Blumlein-Bottcher global fit for spin-dependent structure functions combined with the Alekhin et al. F2 fit and the R1998 parametrization of the longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratio, which represent deep inelastic scattering at higher W. Duality violation was quantified by computing integrals over three individual resonance regions (1st: 1.3 ≤ W2 ≤ 1.9 GeV2, 2nd: 1.9 ≤ W2 ≤ 2.5 GeV2, 3rd: 2.5 ≤ W2 ≤ 3.1 GeV2) and the entire region W2 ≤ 4 GeV2, and taking ratios of data to the global fit.

## Key numbers

- Duality violating corrections for H1/2 are less than 10% over the entire resonance region (W < 2 GeV) at Q2 ≤ 4 GeV2.
- Duality violating corrections for H3/2 are less than 20% over the entire resonance region (W < 2 GeV) at Q2 ≤ 4 GeV2.
- The analysis covers Q2 values ranging from 1.7 GeV2 to 5 GeV2.
- The Christy-Bosted global fit covers Q2 < 8 GeV2 and W between 1.1 and 3.1 GeV.
- The CLAS EG1b data cover x > 0.15 and Q2 from 0.05 to 5 GeV2.
- The three resonance regions are defined on W2 intervals: 1.3-1.9 GeV2 (1st), 1.9-2.5 GeV2 (2nd), and 2.5-3.1 GeV2 (3rd).
- The quark model predicts H3/2/H1/2 = 2/7 when summed over all channels.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-hadron duality in unpolarized structure functions F2 and FL by Ioana et al., Liang et al., and Malace et al., and in polarized structure functions by the SPIN-p and SPIN-n collaborations, to the individual helicity cross sections for the first time. The results support recent efforts by the CTEQX and Alekhin et al. groups to include resonance-region data in global fits of unpolarized parton distribution functions, and suggest that data above the Delta resonance region could similarly constrain spin-dependent PDFs. The patterns of duality violation are in general agreement with quark model predictions based on spin-flavor symmetry by Close and Isgur and by Close and Melnitchouk.
