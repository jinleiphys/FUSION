# Space-time structure of 3+1D color fields in high energy nuclear collisions

> We perform an analytic calculation of the color fields in heavy-ion collisions by considering the collision of longitudinally extended nuclei in the dilute limit of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory of high-energy QCD. Based on general analytic expressions for the color fields in the future light cone, we evaluate the rapidity profile of the transverse pressure within a simple specific model of the nuclear collision geometry and compare our results to 3+1D classical Yang-Mills simulations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first analytical calculation of the initial energy deposition in heavy-ion collisions beyond the boost-invariant high-energy limit, by solving the 3+1D classical Yang-Mills equations within the dilute limit of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. The analytic results are valid only in the weak-field (dilute) approximation, which is a perturbative expansion in the color charge densities of the projectile and target nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge by splitting the gauge field into background fields from single nuclei and perturbative fields describing the Glasma created in the collision. They obtain analytic expressions for the perturbative gauge fields in the future light cone using retarded propagators and a partial Fourier transform over transverse coordinates. Using a McLerran-Venugopalan type model with Gaussian longitudinal profiles and correlations, they evaluate the rapidity profile of the transverse pressure and compare their semi-analytic results to non-perturbative 3+1D classical Yang-Mills simulations.

## Key numbers

* The color charge density correlation function uses widths R and xi identified as the Lorentz contracted size of the nucleus and longitudinal correlation length respectively, with the correlation function given by Eq. (13): <ρ^a_{A/B}(x±, x⊥) ρ^b_{A/B}(x'±, x'⊥)> = g^2 μ^2_{A/B} δ^{ab} T_R((x± + x'±)/2) U_ξ(x± - x'±) G(x⊥ - x'⊥)
* The perturbative energy-momentum tensor is computed up to quartic order O(ρ_A^2 ρ_B^2) in the color charge densities
* The transverse pressure p_T = T^{ii}/2 = ε_{E,L} + ε_{B,L} is expressed in terms of longitudinal electric field contributions ε_{E,L} = <tr f^{+-} f^{+-}> and longitudinal magnetic field contributions ε_{B,L} = (1/2) <tr f^{ij} f_{ij}>

## Context

This work extends previous analytic calculations of color fields in the boost-invariant high-energy limit by Kovner (1995), Dumitru (2001), and McLerran (2016) to include finite longitudinal extent of colliding nuclei. It complements numerical 3+1D classical Yang-Mills simulations developed by the same authors using colored particle-in-cell methods (Ipp 2018, Ipp 2020) and dynamically updated color currents (Schlichting 2020). The results enable analytic understanding of rapidity-dependent observables and longitudinal dynamics beyond mid-rapidity, which is needed to interpret experimental studies of factorization breakdown by PHOBOS (2006), CMS (2015), and ATLAS (2017).
