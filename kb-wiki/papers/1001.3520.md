# Realistic shell-model calculations: current status and open problems

> The main steps involved in realistic shell-model calculations employing two-body low-momentum interactions are briefly reviewed. The practical value of this approach is exemplified by the results of recent calculations and some remaining open questions and directions for future research are discussed.

# Realistic shell-model calculations: current status and open problems

## Key claim
Realistic shell-model calculations using two-body effective interactions derived from free nucleon-nucleon potentials via the low-momentum (V_low-k) approach can accurately describe nuclear structure properties, but the approach remains limited by open questions about the role of three-body forces and the convergence of the effective interaction expansion, particularly for nuclei with more than two valence particles.

## Method
The authors derive shell-model effective interactions by first constructing a smooth low-momentum potential (V_low-k) from a free nucleon-nucleon potential (typically CD-Bonn) using the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation with a cutoff momentum around 2 fm^-1. They then compute the effective interaction within a chosen model space using the Q-box folded-diagram expansion, including diagrams up to second order in most calculations, with single-particle energies taken from experiment. The resulting two-body effective interaction is applied to calculate spectroscopic properties of nuclei near doubly magic cores such as 100Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb.

## Key numbers
- The cutoff momentum for V_low-k is chosen in the vicinity of 2 fm^-1, preserving phase shifts up to E_Lab approximately 350 MeV (the inelastic threshold).
- For calculations around doubly magic 100Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb, the rms deviation between calculated and experimental spectra does not exceed 100 keV.
- The nucleus 136Sb has an N/Z ratio of 1.67, making it the most exotic open-shell nucleus beyond 132Sn with known excited states.
- The V_low-k cutoff range where different NN potentials become very similar is approximately 2.1 fm^-1.
- Moderate variations of the cutoff around 2 fm^-1 (approximately 0.3 fm^-1) produce essentially the same shell-model results.

## Context
This paper reviews the realistic shell-model approach developed by the authors and collaborators (Coraggio, Itaco, Kuo) over the preceding decade, extending earlier work by Barrett (1970), Schucan (1972), and Suzuki (1980) on effective interaction theory. The V_low-k method, introduced by Bogner (2002, 2003), is presented as an advantageous alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix approach. The paper contrasts this method with ab initio approaches such as the Green's function Monte Carlo method (Pieper, 2008), no-core shell model (Navratil, 2009), and coupled cluster method (Hagen, 2007), which are limited to light nuclei. The open problems identified, particularly the overbinding of nuclei with many valence particles and the role of three-body forces, point toward needed extensions of the method, including consistent inclusion of three-nucleon forces as recently explored by Jurgenson (2009) for A ≤ 4 systems.
