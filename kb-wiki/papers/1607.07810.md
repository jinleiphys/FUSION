# General intrinsic theory of general large $N_{c}$ QCD, SU(3) QCD, SU(2) hadron-dynamics and U(1) QED gauge field theories in general field theory and progress towards solving the nucleon spin crisis

> This paper gives general intrinsic theory of general large $N_{c}$ QCD, SU(3) QCD, SU(2) hadron-dynamics and U(1) QED gauge field theories in general field theory and progress towards solving the nucleon spin crisis, i.e., presents general large $N_{c}$ QCD's inner structures, gauge invariant angular momenta and new corresponding Coulomb theorem in quark-gluon field interaction systems based on general field theory, and naturally deduces the gauge invariant spin and orbital angular momentum operators of quark and gauge fields with $SU(N_{c})$ gauge symmetry by Noether theorem in general field theory. In the general large $N_{c}$ QCD, we discover not only the general covariant transverse and parallel conditions ( namely, non-Abelian divergence and curl ), but also that this general system has good intrinsic symmetry characteristics. Specially, this paper's generally decomposing gauge potential theory presents a new technique, it should play a votal role in future physics research. Therefore, this paper breakthroughs the some huge difficulties in the nucleon spin crisis and opens a door of researching on lots of strong interacting systems with different symmetric properties, which is popularly interesting, and keeps both the gauge invariance and their angular momentum commutation relations so that their theories are consistent. Especially, the achieved results here can be utilized to calculate the general QCD strong interactions and to give the precise predictions that can be exactly measured by current particle physics experiments due to their gauge invariant properties etc.

## Key claim

This paper claims to provide a general intrinsic theory of large \(N_c\) QCD, SU(3) QCD, SU(2) hadron-dynamics, and U(1) QED gauge field theories within a unified field-theoretic framework, and to make progress toward solving the nucleon spin crisis by constructing gauge-invariant spin and orbital angular momentum operators. The hedge is that the theory achieves gauge invariance and angular momentum commutation relations simultaneously "for the first time" in a general field theory, but the resolution of the nucleon spin crisis is presented as a "new viewpoint" and "essential steps" rather than a complete solution.

## Method

The authors generalize the Abelian decomposition of gauge potentials into transverse and longitudinal components (using projection operators \(T_k^j\) and \(L_k^j\)) to non-Abelian SU(\(N_c\)) gauge fields. They derive six consistent conditions, non-Abelian generalizations of the Abelian divergence, curl, and orthogonality relations, that ensure gauge covariance and consistency of the decomposed theory. Using Noether's theorem on the resulting action, they obtain gauge-invariant expressions for the total angular momentum of the quark-gluon system, decomposed into quark spin, quark orbital, gluon spin, and gluon orbital contributions.

## Key numbers

- \(N_c\) is taken as a general large integer; the paper treats the cases \(N_c = 3\) (QCD), \(N_c = 2\) (SU(2) hadron-dynamics), and \(N_c = 1\) (U(1) QED) explicitly.
- For \(N_c = 3\), the contraction of structure constants is given as \(f^{ace}f^{bde} = \frac{2}{3}(\delta^{ab}\delta^{cd} - \delta^{ad}\delta^{bc}) + d^{abe}d^{cde} - d^{ade}d^{bce}\) (Eq. 5.7).
- For \(N_c = 2\), the contraction reduces to \(f^{ace}f^{bde} = \delta^{ab}\delta^{cd} - \delta^{ad}\delta^{bc}\) (Eq. 5.8).
- The gauge group has \(N_c^2 - 1\) generators, with indices \(a,b,c = 1,2,\dots,N_c^2-1\).

## Context

This work extends the Abelian decomposition methods of Chen et al. (2008) and the SU(2) isospin gauge theory results of Huang et al. (2013) to general SU(\(N_c\)) gauge groups, including QCD with \(N_c = 3\). It also generalizes the QED decomposition of the gauge potential by Wang et al. (2014). The paper claims to unify previous approaches and to provide gauge-invariant angular momentum operators that can be used for lattice QCD calculations and experimental measurements, addressing the nucleon spin crisis where quark spins account for only about one-third of the nucleon spin.
