# Elastic pbar-d scattering and total pbar-d cross sections reexamined

> We update our recent analysis of pbar-d scattering, performed within the Glauber theory including the single- and double pbar-N scattering mechanisms. Specifically, now we consider also Nbar-N amplitudes from a new partial-wave analysis of pbar-p scattering data. Predictions for differential cross sections and the spin observables A_y^d, A_y^pbar, A_xx, A_yy are presented for antiproton beam energies between 50 and 300 MeV. Total polarized cross sections are calculated utilizing the optical theorem. The efficiency of the polarization buildup for antiprotons in a storage ring is investigated.

# Elastic pbar-d scattering and total pbar-d cross sections reexamined

## Key claim

The Glauber theory predictions for pbar-d scattering observables, including spin-dependent cross sections and polarization buildup efficiency, depend significantly on which set of Nbar-N amplitudes is used; the new partial-wave analysis by Zhou and Timmermans yields larger vector analyzing powers and different signs for some spin-dependent cross sections compared to the Jülich potential models, but the overall polarization degree for antiprotons from a deuterium target remains in the order of 5-10%, which is smaller than for a hydrogen target.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber theory calculations for elastic pbar-d scattering including single- and double pbar-N scattering mechanisms, with full spin dependence of the elementary pbar-N amplitudes and both S- and D-wave components of the deuteron. They reconstructed Nbar-N amplitudes from the phase shifts and inelasticity parameters of the Zhou and Timmermans partial-wave analysis (2012) and compared results to those obtained using amplitudes from the Jülich potential models A and D. Total polarized cross sections were calculated via the optical theorem, and the efficiency of polarization buildup for antiprotons in a storage ring was investigated using the spin-filtering formalism.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section at 179 MeV: first diffractive peak described well; first minimum located at q² ≈ 0.12–0.13 (GeV/c)² (θ_cm ≈ 55°); disagreement with data at q² > 0.15 (GeV/c)² (θ_cm > 60°)
- Total unpolarized pbar-d cross section from ZT amplitudes is somewhat smaller than from Jülich models and below the bulk of experimental data from HERA, Carroll, and Hamilton
- The isospin I=1 amplitude from Jülich models gives σ_pbar-n about 10–15% larger than from the ZT amplitudes
- Polarization degree for antiprotons from deuterium target: 5–10% in the energy region considered (50–300 MeV), for acceptance angle of 20 mrad
- Polarization degree for hydrogen target found to be around 20% in Ref. Timmermans3 for the same acceptance angle
- Large values of around 30% reported in Ref. salnikov using the old Nijmegen partial-wave analysis

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Glauber theory analysis of pbar-d scattering (Uzikov and Haidenbauer, 2012) by incorporating Nbar-N amplitudes from the updated Nijmegen partial-wave analysis by Zhou and Timmermans (2012), which provides the best available representation of the spin dependence of the Nbar-N interaction. The results are relevant for the PAX Collaboration's plans to produce polarized antiproton beams via spin-filtering at the FAIR facility; the calculations show that a deuterium target yields significantly lower polarization buildup efficiency than a hydrogen target, and that the old Nijmegen analysis (Timmermans, 1994) used by Salnikov overestimates the polarization degree.
