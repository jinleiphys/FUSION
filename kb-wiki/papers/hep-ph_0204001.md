# Absence of two-flavor color-superconductivity in compact stars

> The simplest pattern of color superconductivity involves BCS pairing between up and down quarks. We argue that this ``2SC'' phase will not arise within a compact star. A macroscopic volume of quark matter must be electrically neutral and must be a color singlet. Satisfying these requirements imposes a significant free energy cost on the 2SC phase, but not on color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter, in which up, down and strange quarks all pair. As a function of increasing density, therefore, one may see a single phase transition from hadronic matter directly to CFL quark matter. Alternatively, there may be an intervening phase in which the different flavors self-pair, or pair with each other in a non-BCS pattern, such as in a crystalline color superconductor.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase, in which only up and down quarks pair, will not occur inside compact stars because the requirements of electric and color neutrality impose a significant free energy cost on the 2SC phase, unlike the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase where all three quark flavors pair. The hedge is that this conclusion applies specifically to bulk quark matter in compact stars older than a few minutes, where weak equilibrium has been reached and temperature is negligible.

## Method

The authors computed the free energy of unpaired quark matter, CFL quark matter, and 2SC quark matter to order $(M_s/\mu)^4$ and $(\Delta/\mu)^2$, imposing electric neutrality (via a chemical potential $\mu_e$) and color neutrality (via color chemical potentials $\mu_3$ and $\mu_8$) by requiring that the corresponding charge densities vanish. They compared the free energies of these phases under the constraints of neutrality and weak equilibrium, treating the strange quark mass $M_s$ and the pairing gap $\Delta$ as parameters.

## Key numbers

- The electron chemical potential required for electric neutrality in unpaired quark matter is $\mu_e = M_s^2/(4\mu)$ to lowest order in $M_s$.
- The free energy of neutral unpaired quark matter is $\Omega_{\rm unpaired}^\neut = -3\mu^4/(4\pi^2) + 3M_s^2\mu^2/(4\pi^2) - (7-12\log(M_s/2\mu))M_s^4/(32\pi^2)$.
- The free energy of neutral CFL quark matter is $\Omega_{\rm CFL}^\neut = \Omega_{\rm unpaired}^\neut + (3M_s^4 - 48\Delta^2\mu^2)/(16\pi^2)$.
- CFL quark matter is favored over unpaired quark matter when $\Delta > M_s^2/(4\mu)$.
- The strange quark mass $M_s$ is estimated to lie between 120 MeV and 500 MeV, while the quark chemical potential $\mu$ is around 500 MeV in compact star cores, and the gap $\Delta$ is of order 10 to 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of color superconductivity by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) on the CFL phase, and by Rapp et al. (1998) on the 2SC phase, by incorporating the previously neglected constraints of electric and color neutrality. The paper contradicts the assumption in earlier work that a stable bulk 2SC phase would exist at intermediate densities, and instead suggests that compact stars may undergo a direct transition from hadronic matter to CFL quark matter, or possibly to an intervening non-BCS phase such as crystalline color superconductivity. This enables future work to focus on the CFL phase and other exotic pairing patterns as the relevant forms of quark matter in neutron star interiors.
