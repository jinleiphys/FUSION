# Universal relations between the quasinormal modes of neutron stars and magnetic tidal deformability

> Tidal deformabilities are one of the observable quantities characterizing neutron stars, which are strongly associated with the stellar compactness, the ratio of the stellar mass to the radius. In addition to the tidal deformability, the quasinormal modes excited in a neutron star are also an important property for extracting information about the neutron star interior, adopting gravitational wave asteroseismology. In this study, we especially focus on the magnetic tidal deformability, which acts on the gravitational waveform from a neutron star binary merger as a higher-order effect than the electric tidal deformability, and derive the universal relations expressing the quasinormal modes, such as the fundamental ($f$-), 1st pressure ($p_1$-), and 1st spacetime ($w_1$-) modes, as a function of the magnetic tidal deformability. The universal relations derived in this study exhibit accuracy more or less comparable to those of the electric tidal deformability.

## Key claim

The paper derives universal relations between the magnetic tidal deformability (a higher-order tidal effect) and the frequencies and damping rates of neutron star quasinormal modes (f-, p1-, and w1-modes), finding that these relations have accuracy comparable to those using the electric tidal deformability, but the paper notes that the damping rate for extremely low-mass neutron stars significantly deviates from the fitting formula due to avoided crossing between the f- and p1-modes.

## Method

The author solves the linearized Einstein equations for axial-type metric perturbations on spherically symmetric neutron star backgrounds constructed with eight different equations of state (DD2, Miyatsu, Shen, FPS, SKa, SLy4, SLy9, Togashi). The perturbation equation for the gravitomagnetic tidal deformability is integrated from the stellar center to the surface to obtain the ℓ=2 gravitomagnetic Love number j2 and the dimensionless magnetic tidal deformability Σ2. The quasinormal mode frequencies and damping rates are determined by solving eigenvalue problems with the coupled matter and metric perturbation equations, and fitting formulae are constructed as functions of log10(-Σ2).

## Key numbers

- The fitting formula for the mass-scaled f-mode frequency as a function of x = log10(-Σ2) is: f_f M_1.4 (kHz) = 2.7464 - 1.7864x + 0.2781x^2 + 0.096669x^3 - 0.03856x^4 + 0.0036256x^5
- The fitting formula for the mass-scaled f-mode damping rate is: log10(M_1.4/τ_f (1/sec)) = 0.9650 - 0.3548x - 0.3673x^2 + 0.1472x^3 - 0.031854x^4 + 0.0026456x^5
- The f-mode frequency and damping rate can be estimated within a few percent accuracy for canonical neutron stars where -Σ2 is less than about 10
- The mass-scaled p1-mode frequencies can be estimated from the magnetic tidal deformability within about 10% accuracy for a canonical neutron star model
- The radius-scaled w1-mode frequencies and damping rate can be estimated with an accuracy of at least 5% using the fitting formulae
- The relation between Σ2 and the electric tidal deformability Λ2 is given by: log10(-Σ2) = -0.8843 + 0.4808χ + 0.028172χ^2 + 0.0014241χ^3 - 0.0002369χ^4, where χ = log10(Λ2), and this relation gives Σ2 within a few percent for canonical neutron stars with Λ2 less than 1000

## Context

This work extends the known universal relations between neutron star properties, such as those between the f-mode frequencies and electric tidal deformability found by Chan (2014), Sotani and Kumar (2021), and Pradhan, Chatterjee, and Villa (2023). It also builds on the universal relations between moment of inertia, tidal Love numbers, and spin-induced quadrupole moment established by Maselli (2013), Yagi (2014), and others. The derived relations enable estimation of quasinormal mode frequencies from the magnetic tidal deformability, which may become observable in future gravitational wave detections of binary neutron star mergers, particularly through the higher-order post-Newtonian correction to the waveform phase given by Ψ2 = Σ (5/224) Σ2^i (X_i^4/η)(X_i - 1037X_j) x^(7/2).
