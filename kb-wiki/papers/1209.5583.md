# Shell evolution in neutron-rich carbon isotopes: Unexpected enhanced role of neutron-neutron correlation

> Full shell-model diagonalization has been performed to study the structure of neutron-rich nuclei around $^{20}$C. We investigate in detail the roles played by the different monopole components of the effective interaction in the evolution of the N=14 shell in C, N and O isotopes. It is found that the relevant neutron-neutron monopole terms, $V^{nn}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}$ and $V^{nn}_{s_{1/2}s_{1/2}}$, contribute significantly to the reduction of the N=14 shell gap in C and N isotopes in comparison with that in O isotopes. The origin of this unexpectedly large effect, which is comparable with (sometimes even larger than) that caused by the proton-neutron interaction, is related to the enhanced configuration mixing in those nuclei due to many-body correlations. Such a scheme is also supported by the large B(E2) value in the nucleus $^{20}$C which has been measured recently.

# Shell evolution in neutron-rich carbon isotopes: Unexpected enhanced role of neutron-neutron correlation

## Key claim

The neutron-neutron monopole terms $V^{nn}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}$ and $V^{nn}_{s_{1/2}s_{1/2}}$ contribute significantly to the reduction of the N=14 shell gap in carbon and nitrogen isotopes, with an effect comparable to or sometimes larger than that caused by the proton-neutron interaction, but this enhancement is conditional on the configuration mixing induced by many-body correlations in those nuclei.

## Method

Full shell-model diagonalization was performed in $0\hbar\omega$ and $2\hbar\omega$ model spaces (the latter allowing up to two nucleons excited from the p to sd shell) using the WBT, WBT*, WBTM, and MK effective Hamiltonians. The effective single-particle energies (ESPEs) were calculated using shell-model occupancies from diagonalization to include correlation effects, and the contribution of each monopole interaction term to the N=14 shell gap difference between $^{22}$O and $^{20}$C (and $^{21}$N) was decomposed via the monopole interaction formula.

## Key numbers

- The $E(2^{+}_{1})$ in $^{20}$C is much larger than in $^{16,18}$C; experimental $B(E2)$ for $^{20}$C is $37.5^{+15.0+5.0}_{-8.5-2.0}$ $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$ (Petri 2011), compared to $13.0\pm1.0\pm3.5$ $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$ (Ong 2008) and $20.8\pm3.7$ $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$ (Wiedeking 2008) for $^{16}$C.
- The $B(E2)$ calculated with WBT* at $0\hbar\omega$ for $^{20}$C is 31.95 $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$, with proton matrix element $A_{p}=2.96$ fm and neutron matrix element $A_{n}=11.30$ fm.
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment ratio $|Q_{0}(s)/Q_{0}(t)|$ for $^{20}$C is 0.97 (WBT* $0\hbar\omega$) and 1.00 (WBT $0\hbar\omega$), indicating near-rotor behavior, while for $^{18}$C the ratio is 0.58 (WBT* $0\hbar\omega$).
- The $^{20}$C intrinsic quadrupole moment from experiment is $|Q_{0}(t)| = 19.41^{+5.18}_{-2.72}$ fm$^{2}$ (Petri 2011), consistent with an oblate deformed structure.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of N=14 shell evolution from oxygen isotopes (where the shell gap is clear) to carbon and nitrogen isotopes, where the gap erodes. Prior work by Stanoiu (2008) and Sohler (2008) had shown the need to reduce the neutron-neutron monopole interaction in the sd shell (by factors 0.75 for C and 0.875 for N) to reproduce experimental $E(2^{+}_{1})$ values. The present study quantifies that the neutron-neutron terms $V^{nn}_{d_{5/2}d_{5/2}}$ and $V^{nn}_{s_{1/2}s_{1/2}}$ contribute comparably to the proton-neutron interaction (discussed by Stanoiu 2008) in reducing the N=14 gap, and that this effect arises from enhanced configuration mixing. The large $B(E2)$ in $^{20}$C, measured by Petri (2011), is interpreted as evidence for oblate deformation, consistent with AMD calculations (Enyo 2005) and deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (Sagawa 2004). The authors suggest similar analysis of $V^{nn}_{jj}$ contributions should be applied to other shell evolution cases, such as N=16 from $^{24}$O to $^{30}$Si (Otsuka 2001).
