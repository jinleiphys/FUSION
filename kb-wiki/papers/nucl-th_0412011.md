# ^{1}S_{0} proton superfluidity in neutron star matter: Impact of bulk properties

> We study the ^{1}S_{0} proton pairing gap in neutron star matter putting emphasis on influence of the Dirac effective mass and the proton fraction on the gap within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. The gap equation is solved using the Bonn-B potential as a particle-particle channel interaction. It is found that the maximal pairing gap Δ_{max} is 1-2 MeV, which has a strong correlation with the Dirac effective mass. Hence we suggest that it serves as a guide to narrow down parameter sets of the relativistic effective field theory. Furthermore, the more slowly protons increase with density in the core region of neutron stars, the wider the superfluid range and the slightly lower the peak of the gap become.

## Key claim

The maximal $^{1}S_{0}$ proton pairing gap in neutron star matter is 1-2 MeV and has a strong correlation with the Dirac effective mass, suggesting that this correlation can serve as a guide to narrow down parameter sets of the relativistic effective field theory. The more slowly protons increase with density in the core region, the wider the superfluid range and the slightly lower the peak of the gap become.

## Method

The authors solved the gap equation for $^{1}S_{0}$ proton pairing at zero temperature within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model, using the Bonn-B potential as the particle-particle channel interaction. They employed four distinct equations of state from two RMF parameter sets (NL3-hp and Z271) with and without an isovector nonlinear coupling $\Lambda_{\omega}$, plus the TM1 set for comparison, to study the effects of the Dirac effective mass and the proton fraction on the pairing gap.

## Key numbers

- Maximal pairing gap $\Delta_{max}$ is 1-2 MeV, dependent on the parameter set used.
- The NL3-hp set gives a Dirac effective mass of $0.59M$ at saturation density $\rho_0 \simeq 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$; the Z271 set gives $0.80M$.
- For the NL3-hp set with finite $\Lambda_{\omega}$, the direct URCA process starts at $\rho_B = 0.442$ fm$^{-3}$ with $Y_p = 0.136$; for the Z271 set with finite $\Lambda_{\omega}$, it starts at $\rho_B = 1.028$ fm$^{-3}$ with $Y_p = 0.141$.
- The critical temperature $T_c$ is obtained by $k_B T_c = 0.57 \Delta(k_F^{(p)}; T=0)$, with typical neutron star interior temperatures around $10^8$ K.
- The neutrino emissivity of the nucleon direct URCA process in normal fluid is $Q_0 = 7.55 \times 10^{30} \mu_e T_9^6 (M_n^{*2} M_p^{*2} / M^2) \theta(p_{Fe} + p_{Fp} - p_{Fn})$ in erg s$^{-1}$ cm$^{-3}$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $^{1}S_{0}$ proton superfluidity in neutron star matter, which had been performed using nonrelativistic approaches and the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach giving maximal gaps $\lesssim 1.0$ MeV and closing densities $\rho_c \sim 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$ (Takatsuka 1997, Elgaroy 1996). The authors use the RHB model to treat bulk properties and superfluidity on the same footing, building on the RMF framework of Bodmer (1991) and the EFT-inspired Lagrangian of Horowitz (2001) that introduces a nonlinear $\omega$-$\rho$ coupling to control the symmetry energy and proton fraction. The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling scenarios, particularly the suppression of the direct URCA process by proton superfluidity, and provide a guide for selecting RMF parameter sets.
