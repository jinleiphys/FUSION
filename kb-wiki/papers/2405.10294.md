# Corrections to adiabatic behavior for long paths

> The cost and the error of the adiabatic theorem for preparing the final eigenstate are discussed in terms of path length. Previous studies in terms of the norm of the Hamiltonian and its derivatives with the spectral gap are limited in their ability to describe the cost of adiabatic state preparation for certain physically large systems. We argue that total time is not a good measure for determining the computational difficulty of adiabatic quantum computation by developing a no-go theorem. From the result of time-periodic Hamiltonian cases, we suggest that there are proxies for computational cost which typically grow as path length increases when the error is kept fixed and small and consider possible conjectures on how general the behavior is.

## Key claim

The paper argues that total time is not a good measure for determining the computational difficulty of adiabatic quantum computation, because there exists a no-go theorem: for any proposed bound on asymptotic scaling of time as a function of path length at fixed error, one can always construct a time-dependent Hamiltonian that violates that bound.

## Method

The authors define a reparameterization-invariant path length L for the ground state along a Hamiltonian trajectory, parameterized by λ, and introduce a velocity v(λ) = dλ/dt. They analyze the asymptotic series for adiabatic error, which depends only on endpoint properties, and examine the special case of time-periodic Hamiltonians where errors accumulate over multiple cycles. They then prove a no-go theorem by constructing pairs of Hamiltonians related by H_B(λ) = H_A(λ) v_B(λ)/v_A(λ), which yield identical states at each λ but different traversal times, allowing arbitrary scaling of time with path length.

- For the 3-level counterexample (Eq. 3-level), the error reaches a maximum value ε_max = 1/√(1 + Δ²τ²) and the time to reach path length L scales as t(L) → √(2Lτ²) as L → ∞, which is slower than linear in L.
- For time-periodic Hamiltonians, the total error after N cycles is ε = N ε_sc = (L/L_sc) exp(-f(1/v̄)), where f grows faster than logarithmically, implying T = L f⁻¹(log(L/(L_sc ε))) which scales faster than linearly in L.
- The spectral gap in the 3-level model is Δ for all time, constant throughout the evolution.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of adiabatic state preparation costs initiated by Ref. (PhysRevA.81.032308), which claimed that minimum time must be proportional to the ratio of eigenstate path length to the gap. The authors provide a counterexample showing this claim does not hold generically, and relate their analysis to the reflection-improved-quantum-Zeno (RIQZ) approach of Cohen (2023) which achieves linear scaling of computational resources with path length. The paper suggests that a dimensionless proxy for computational cost is needed instead of time, and identifies conditions such a proxy should satisfy, including scaling faster than linearly with L for time-periodic Hamiltonians.
