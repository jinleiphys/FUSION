# Elasticity of nuclear medium as a principal macrodynamical promoter of electric dipole pygmy resonance

> Motivated by arguments of the nuclear core-layer model formulated in [S.I. Bastrukov, J.A. Maruhn, Z.Phys. A 335 (1990) 139], the macroscopic excitation mechanism of electric pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) is considered as owing its origin to perturbation-induced effective decomposition of nucleus into two spherical domains - undisturbed inner region treated as static core and dynamical layer undergoing elastic shear vibrations. The focus is placed on the imprinted in the core-layer model mechanism of emergence of the low-energy dipole electric resonant excitation as Goldstone soft mode of translation layer-against-core oscillations. To accentuate this attitude we regain the obtained in the above paper spectral equation for the frequency of spheroidal elastic vibrations trapped in the finite-depth layer, but by working from canonical equation of elastic dynamics of continuous medium. The obtained analytic equations for the frequency of dipole vibrational state in question and its excitation strength exhibit fundamental character of this soft dipole mode of nuclear resonant response.

# Elasticity of nuclear medium as a principal macrodynamical promoter of electric dipole pygmy resonance

## Key claim

The electric pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) can be understood as a Goldstone soft mode of elastic shear oscillations confined to a finite-depth peripheral nuclear layer, existing only when the nucleus effectively decomposes into a static core and a dynamical layer under perturbation. The authors show that this dipole mode disappears (frequency goes to zero) when the core radius tends to zero, establishing that the mode requires a finite-depth layer rather than whole-volume oscillations.

## Method

The authors apply the core-layer model of Bastrukov and Maruhn (1990), treating the nucleus as an elastic continuous medium with uniform density and shear modulus. They solve the vector Laplace equation for irrotational (poloidal) material displacement fields in a spherical geometry, imposing boundary conditions of zero radial displacement at the core surface and compatibility with surface distortions at the nuclear radius. The frequency and excitation strength of the dipole mode are computed using Rayleigh's energy variational method, with the inertia and stiffness integrals evaluated analytically for the layer geometry.

## Key numbers

- PDR energy centroid: $E_{PDR}(E1) = [31 \pm 1] \, A^{-1/3}$ MeV throughout the nuclear chart
- Total excitation probability: $B_{PDR}(E1) = [1.85 \pm 0.05] \times 10^{-3} \, Z^2 \, A^{-2/3}$ $e^2$ fm$^2$
- Best-fit core-to-nucleus radius ratio: $x = R_c/R = 0.33$
- Fractional charge density of peripheral layer: $\gamma = 6.6 \times 10^{-2}$
- Shear modulus of nuclear matter extracted from quadrupole resonance data: $\mu \simeq 4 \times 10^{33}$ dyn cm$^{-2}$
- Shear viscosity of nuclear matter from giant resonance widths: $\eta \simeq 3 \times 10^{11}$ dyn sec cm$^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the core-layer model of Bastrukov and Maruhn (1990), which previously treated quadrupole and octupole overtones of shear elastic oscillations, by focusing on the dipole overtone as a Goldstone soft mode. The authors contrast their isoscalar elastodynamic mechanism with the isovector hydrodynamic model of Myers, Swiatecki, and others (1971, 1990), which treats PDR as a low-frequency counterpart of the giant dipole resonance driven by symmetry energy gradients. The results enable a unified elastodynamic interpretation of electric PDR and magnetic dipole resonance (MDR) as soft modes of differentially translational and differentially rotational layer-against-core oscillations, respectively, and connect to asteroseismology of neutron stars where similar elastic vibrations are observed.
