# Physics of neutrino flavor transformation through matter-neutrino resonances

> In astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron star-neutron star or neutron star-black hole mergers where dense neutrino media are present, matter-neutrino resonances (MNRs) can occur when the neutrino propagation potentials due to neutrino-electron and neutrino-neutrino forward scattering nearly cancel each other. We show that neutrino flavor transformation through MNRs can be explained by multiple adiabatic solutions similar to the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein mechanism. We find that for the normal neutrino mass hierarchy, neutrino flavor evolution through MNRs can be sensitive to the shape of neutrino spectra and the adiabaticity of the system, but such sensitivity is absent for the inverted hierarchy.

# Physics of neutrino flavor transformation through matter-neutrino resonances

## Key claim

Neutrino flavor transformation through matter-neutrino resonances (MNRs) can be explained by multiple adiabatic solutions analogous to the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) mechanism, but only for the normal neutrino mass hierarchy does the flavor evolution show sensitivity to the shape of neutrino spectra and the adiabaticity of the system; for the inverted hierarchy such sensitivity is absent.

## Method

The authors modeled a homogeneous, isotropic neutrino gas initially consisting of mono-energetic or continuous-spectrum electron neutrinos and antineutrinos, solving the neutrino flavor-isospin (NFIS) equations of motion numerically. They compared these numerical solutions to adiabatic MSW-like solutions derived from a self-consistent equation for the total NFIS, and to the simple MNR criterion (MFM) proposed by Malkus et al. (2014). The analysis was performed for both normal and inverted neutrino mass hierarchies, using box-shaped and pinched energy spectra with varying expansion timescales.

## Key numbers

- For a mono-energetic gas with antineutrino-neutrino ratio α = 4/3, constant matter potential λ = 10 ω₀, and initial neutrino potential μ₀ = 100 ω₀ decreasing as μ(t) = 100 ω₀ e^{-ω₀ t/20}, the adiabatic solution showed good agreement with numerical results for the normal hierarchy.
- For a mono-energetic gas with λ = 100 ω₀ and μ₀ = 1000 ω₀, the MSW-like resonance condition ω cos2θᵥ - (λ + 2μ S₃) = 0 was satisfied at μ/ω₀ ≈ 120 for neutrinos in the inverted hierarchy, and at μ/ω₀ ≈ 280, 110, and 45 for neutrinos and μ/ω₀ ≈ 290 for antineutrinos in the normal hierarchy.
- For a box spectrum with width Δω = 0.4 ω₀, the transition from monochromatic-like to super-adiabatic behavior occurred at τ ∼ 270 ω₀⁻¹.
- For a pinched spectrum with β = 3 and ⟨E⟩ = 12 MeV, the transition between monochromatic-like and super-adiabatic behavior occurred at τ ∼ 35 ω₀⁻¹.
- The neutrino mass-squared difference used for pinched spectrum calculations was δm² = 2.44 × 10⁻³ eV².

## Context

This work extends the discovery of matter-neutrino resonances by Malkus et al. (2012, 2014, 2015), which identified MNRs in black-hole accretion disk environments where antineutrino fluxes exceed neutrino fluxes. The authors build on the generalized adiabatic MSW framework first discussed by Qian (1994) and formulated more explicitly by Duan (2007), showing that MNRs can be understood through multiple adiabatic solutions with different alignment configurations. The results enable more accurate predictions of neutrino flavor transformation in core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, which is essential for understanding neutrino-driven supernova explosions and neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis, though the authors note that the homogeneity and isotropy assumptions may be broken by collective neutrino oscillations as shown by Raffelt (2013), Mirizzi (2013), Duan (2013), and others.
