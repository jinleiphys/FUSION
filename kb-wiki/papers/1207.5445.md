# Recent results on heavy quark quenching in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> In this contribution, we present some predictions for the production of D and B mesons in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies and confront them with experimental results obtained so far by the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE and CMS collaborations. We next discuss some preliminary results obtained with an improved description of the medium based on EPOS initial conditions, and its possible implications on the nuclear modification factor and on the elliptic flow of heavy quarks.

# Recent results on heavy quark quenching in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The authors show that both collisional-only and collisional-plus-radiative energy loss models can describe RHIC non-photonic single-electron data, but only after applying a global rescaling factor (K=2 and K=0.65 respectively), revealing a model "fragility" that stems from the stiff initial pT-distribution of heavy quarks. At LHC energies, their predictions for D meson quenching exceed the ALICE data, especially when radiative energy loss is included, indicating the need for further study.

## Method
The authors use the MC@sHQ (Monte Carlo at Heavy Quarks) generator to simulate heavy quark propagation through a fluid-dynamic background medium, employing either collisional energy loss alone or collisional plus radiative energy loss with coherence effects (LPM). The background medium is modeled using two approaches: the Kolb-Heinz (KH) fluid dynamics with a first-order phase transition equation of state, and an improved description using EPOS initial conditions with a lattice QCD-based crossover equation of state in 3+1D fluid dynamics.

## Key numbers
- Spatial diffusion coefficient extracted from RHIC data: 2πT Ds ≈ 1.9 ± 0.5 at T = 200 MeV and 2πT Ds ≈ 2.6 ± 0.65 at T = 300 MeV, for both c and b quarks
- For LHC Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, the predicted RAA for non-prompt J/ψ (proxy for B mesons) is approximately 0.35 for 6 GeV/c < pT(J/ψ) < 30 GeV/c, in agreement with CMS data
- The EPOS initial conditions use τ0 = 0.35 fm for fluid dynamic expansion, compared to τ0 = 0.6 fm for the KH medium
- The crossover transition in the EPOS equation of state occurs between T = 145-165 MeV, while the KH equation of state has a first-order phase transition at a constant T = 165 MeV
- The radial velocity in the EPOS medium reaches up to approximately 0.9c at the edges of the fluid, exceeding the maximal values from the KH medium

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of heavy quark energy loss (Gossiaux 2011) by comparing collisional and radiative mechanisms against RHIC data from STAR and PHENIX, and providing predictions for LHC experiments (ALICE and CMS). The improved medium description using EPOS initial conditions (Werner 2010) represents a significant advance over the Kolb-Heinz fluid dynamics (Kolb and Heinz) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions, a lattice QCD-based crossover equation of state (Borsanyi 2010), and initial radial velocities. The results enable discrimination between energy loss mechanisms using future high-pT data (10-30 GeV/c) and demonstrate the sensitivity of heavy quark observables to the details of the medium evolution, particularly the radial flow profile.
