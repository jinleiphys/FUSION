# Comprehensive Study of Observables in Compton Scattering on the Nucleon

> We present an analysis of $13$ observables in Compton scattering on the proton. Cross sections, asymmetries with polarised beam and/or targets, and polarisation-transfer observables are investigated for energies up to the $螖(1232)$ resonance to determine their sensitivity to the proton's dipole scalar and spin polarisabilities. The Chiral Effective Field Theory Compton amplitude we use is complete at N$^4$LO, $\mathcal{O(}e^2未^4)$, for photon energies $蠅\sim m_蟺$, and so has an accuracy of a few per cent there. At photon energies in the resonance region it is complete at NLO, $\mathcal{O}(e^2未^0)$, and so its accuracy there is about $20$\%. We find that for energies from pion-production threshold to about $250\;\mathrm{MeV}$, multiple asymmetries have significant sensitivity to presently ill-determined combinations of proton spin polarisabilities. We also argue that the broad outcomes of this analysis will be replicated in complementary theoretical approaches, e.g., dispersion relations. Finally, we show that below the pion-production threshold, $6$ observables suffice to reconstruct the Compton amplitude, and above it $11$ are required. Although not necessary for polarisability extractions, this opens the possibility to perform "complete" Compton-scattering experiments. An interactive Mathematica notebook, including results for the neutron, is available from judith.mcgovern@manchester.ac.uk .

## Key claim

The paper systematically maps the sensitivity of all 13 Compton-scattering observables on the proton to the six dipole polarisabilities, finding that multiple asymmetries between pion-production threshold and about 250 MeV have significant sensitivity to presently ill-determined combinations of proton spin polarisabilities, but the analysis relies on the assumption that the Chiral Effective Field Theory energy dependence is correct up to that energy while only the polarisability values are varied.

## Method

The authors used a Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) Compton amplitude that includes a dynamical Delta(1232) resonance, complete at N^4LO (O(e^2 delta^4)) for photon energies around the pion mass and at NLO (O(e^2 delta^0)) in the resonance region. They computed cross sections, beam-target asymmetries, and polarisation-transfer observables for the proton at energies up to the Delta(1232) resonance, then varied the six dipole polarisabilities (alpha_E1, beta_M1, and four spin polarisabilities gamma_i) around their ChiEFT reference values to assess each observable's sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The ChiEFT amplitude has an accuracy of "a few per cent" at photon energies omega ~ m_pi (regime I, omega less than about 140 MeV)
- The ChiEFT amplitude has an accuracy of about 20% at photon energies in the Delta(1232) resonance region (regime II, omega ~ 300 MeV)
- Below the pion-production threshold, 6 observables suffice to reconstruct the Compton amplitude; above it, 11 are required
- The expansion parameter delta is approximately 0.4, with a breakdown scale Lambda_chi of about 650 MeV
- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting is approximately 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the seminal analysis of Babusci et al. (1998) on the relationship between Compton amplitudes and observables, and builds on earlier ChiEFT studies of Compton scattering by Hildebrandt et al. (2003, 2005) and Pasquini et al. (2007). The study was motivated by recent polarised Compton experiments at MAMI (Martel et al., 2014; Sokhoyan et al., 2016) and provides a roadmap for extracting the poorly-known proton spin polarisabilities, whose current uncertainties vary from 10% to over 100%. The interactive Mathematica notebook provided with the paper enables future experimental analyses and also includes results for the free neutron target.
