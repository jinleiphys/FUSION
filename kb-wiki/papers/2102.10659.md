# Magneto-rotational dissociation of heavy hadrons in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> A heavy hadron traversing Quark-Gluon Plasma and dragged along in rotational motion is subject to the Lorentz and centrifugal forces. The Lorentz force, sourced by the valence quarks of heavy-ions, possesses the electric and magnetic components in the hadron comoving frame. The electric component renders the hadron unstable by empowering one of its quarks to tunnel through the potential barrier. Assuming that the magnetic field is parallel to the plasma vorticity, the hadron dissociation probability is computed using the Imaginary Time Method and is found to strongly depend on the sign of the quark electric charge. The dissociation probability monotonically increases as a function of vorticity for negative electric charges, whereas for positive charges it exhibits a minimum at a finite value of vorticity. The dissociation probability for the negative charges is larger than for the positive ones at the same magnetic field and vorticity. In relativistic heavy-ion collisions this implies lower abundance of the negatively charged hadrons as compared to the positively charges ones. This effect is significant at moderate collision energies where the plasma vorticity is comparable or larger than the synchrotron frequency.

## Key claim

The dissociation probability of a heavy hadron in a rotating Quark-Gluon Plasma depends strongly on the sign of the light quark's electric charge, with negatively charged hadrons dissociating more readily than positively charged ones, provided the magnetic field and plasma vorticity are parallel. This effect is significant only at moderate collision energies where the plasma vorticity is comparable to or larger than the synchrotron frequency.

## Method

The author derives the non-relativistic equations of motion for a light quark in a hadron comoving frame that rotates with uniform angular velocity parallel to a uniform magnetic field, with a perpendicular electric field arising from the hadron's translational motion through the plasma. The dissociation probability is computed using the Imaginary Time Method, which evaluates the quasi-classical tunneling of the quark through the binding potential barrier by finding the extremal sub-barrier trajectory in Euclidean time and calculating the imaginary part of the restricted action.

## Key numbers

- The dissociation probability is expressed via the parameter γ = √(2ε_b/m) × (B/E), where ε_b is the hadron binding energy, m is the quark mass, B is the magnetic field, and E is the electric field in the hadron comoving frame.
- In the strong electric field limit (γ ≪ 1), the difference in the imaginary part of the action between positive and negative charges is given by: 2 Im W(e>0) - 2 Im W(e<0) = (8 m³ Ω B)/(45 e² E³) × (2ε_b/m)^(5/2).
- The ratio κ = Ω/ω_B (vorticity to synchrotron frequency) is of order unity at collision energies √s ∼ 50-130 GeV for a u quark with mass m = gT/√3 at impact parameter b = 10 fm immediately after the collision.
- The dissociation probability is computed for binding energy parameter √(2ε_b/m) = 1 and hadron velocities V = 2/3, 1/3, and 1/6 relative to the plasma.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of magnetic dissociation of heavy hadrons by Marasinghe and Tuchin (2011) to include the effect of plasma rotation, which has been observed in heavy-ion collisions (Csernai 2013, Becattini 2015, Deng 2016). The paper provides a qualitative benchmark for future phenomenological studies of magneto-rotational effects on heavy meson production, particularly for D-mesons where the light quark charge determines the sign dependence. The effect is most pronounced at lower collision energies where vorticity is large, and could explain a relative suppression of negatively charged heavy mesons compared to positively charged ones.
