# Bulk viscosity and $n$-component fluids

> Understanding the hydrodynamics of out-of-equilibrium dense viscous fluids is of key importance to accurate descriptions of physical systems such as compact stars, particularly their mergers. We consider a near-equilibrium relativistic fluid with $n$ independent and small chemical potentials restoring the system back towards equilibrium. By diagonalising the evolution equation for the out-of-equilibrium chemical potentials, we construct an explicit evolution equation for the bulk scalar of the system in second-order hydrodynamics in terms of equilibrium quantities. We find expressions for the $2n$ transport quantities and show that in the rest frame of the fluid, the system admits a Green's function corresponding to an $n$-component fluid.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general framework for relativistic bulk viscous fluids with $n$ independent chemical potentials, showing that the system's evolution equation factorises into $n$ separate components each obeying an Israel–Stewart-type equation, but only under the assumptions that the matrix $\Tmati$ is invertible (i.e., all reaction rates are nonvanishing and the pressure is nondegenerate) and that the convective derivative commutes with $\Tmati$ (which holds because $\Tmati$ contains only equilibrium quantities).

## Method

The author starts from a near-equilibrium relativistic fluid with $k$ particle species and $n$ independent reaction chemical potentials, writing the linearised evolution equations for the chemical potentials in terms of equilibrium susceptibilities and reaction rates. By applying the Cayley–Hamilton theorem to the matrix $\Tmati$ (which encodes the reaction rates and susceptibilities), the system of $n$ coupled first-order differential equations is diagonalised into a single $n$th-order differential equation for the bulk scalar $\Pi$. The transport coefficients (relaxation times and bulk viscosities) are then expressed in terms of equilibrium quantities by matching the Green's function of the resulting equation to a Prony series.

## Key numbers

* The system yields $2n$ transport quantities: $n$ relaxation times $\tau_\alpha$ and $n$ bulk viscous coefficients $\zeta_\alpha$, where $n$ is the number of independent chemical potentials.
* The relaxation times $\tau_\alpha$ are the eigenvalues of the matrix $\Tmati$, which has units of $[\text{time}]^{-1}$.
* For the $n=2$ example of unpaired quark matter (without neutrinos), the bulk scalar evolution equation reads $\tau_+ \tau_- D_u^2 \Pi + (\tau_+ + \tau_-) D_u \Pi + \Pi = -\langle \boldsymbol{\Pi},\bvec\rangle \vartheta - \tau_+ \tau_- \langle \boldsymbol{\Pi},\Tmati \bvec\rangle$, with the Green's function $G(t) = \theta(t)(\zeta_1/\tau_1)e^{-t/\tau_1} + \theta(t)(\zeta_2/\tau_2)e^{-t/\tau_2}$.
* The partial bulk viscosities for $n=2$ are $\zeta_1 = \tau_1 (\langle \boldsymbol{\Pi},\boldsymbol{z}\rangle - \tau_2 \langle \boldsymbol{\Pi},\Tmati\boldsymbol{z}\rangle)/(\tau_1 - \tau_2)$ and $\zeta_2 = \tau_2 (\langle \boldsymbol{\Pi},\boldsymbol{z}\rangle - \tau_1 \langle \boldsymbol{\Pi},\Tmati\boldsymbol{z}\rangle)/(\tau_2 - \tau_1)$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cruz Rojas et al. (2024) and Hernandez et al. (2025) for two-component fluids to a general $n$-component framework, providing a systematic diagonalisation method that avoids the unwieldy direct treatment of many-species systems. The formalism is motivated by neutron star merger hydrodynamics, where bulk viscosity from chemical reactions affects the gravitational wave signal (Alford et al., 2017; Most et al., 2022; Chabanov et al., 2023). The result enables numerical simulations of dense relativistic fluids with many particle species (e.g., hadronic matter with numerous composite particles) by reducing the system to $n$ independent Israel–Stewart-type equations, and connects the fluid description to the generalised Maxwell model (Prony series) known from viscoelasticity.
