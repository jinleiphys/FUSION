# Heavy quark jet quenching with collisional plus radiative energy loss and path length fluctuations

> With the QGP opacity computed perturbatively and with the global entropy constraints imposed by the observed $dN_{ch}/dy\approx1000$, radiative energy loss alone cannot account for the observed suppression of single non-photonic electrons. We show that collisional energy loss, which previously has been neglected, is comparable to radiative loss for both light and heavy jets and may in fact be the dominant mechanism for bottom quarks. Predictions taking into account both radiative and collisional losses significantly reduce the discrepancy with data. In addition to elastic energy loss, it is critical to include jet path length fluctuations to account for the observed pion suppression.

## Key claim
Radiative energy loss alone, computed with perturbative QGP opacity and global entropy constraints from observed \(dN_{ch}/dy \approx 1000\), cannot account for the observed suppression of single non-photonic electrons; collisional energy loss, previously neglected, is comparable to radiative loss for both light and heavy jets and may be the dominant mechanism for bottom quarks.

## Method
The authors computed jet suppression using a model that generalizes the energy loss probability \(P(E_i \rightarrow E_f)\) to include both elastic and inelastic energy loss and their fluctuations, and also incorporated geometric path length fluctuations. They assumed initial \(dN_g/dy = 1000\) and a fixed coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), and compared predictions for non-photonic single electrons and pions in central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Radiative energy loss alone predicted an upper band for electron \(R_{AA}\) that significantly overestimates the data; the new prediction including collisional loss and path length fluctuations produces a lower band that reduces the discrepancy.
- Elastic energy loss for light and charm quarks is smaller but of the same order of magnitude as inelastic loss above \(E > 10\) GeV; for bottom quarks, elastic loss can be greater than inelastic up to \(\sim 15\) GeV.
- Elastic energy loss was previously estimated as \(dE^{el}/dx \sim 0.3-0.5\) GeV/fm, which was considered small compared to radiative loss of several GeV/fm.
- Varying the fixed coupling from \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) to 0.4 improves the fit to electron data but overpredicts pion quenching.

## Context
This work extends the jet tomography framework of Djordjevic (2005) by adding collisional energy loss and path length fluctuations, which were not considered in that earlier calculation. It addresses the discrepancy between radiative-only predictions and non-photonic electron data from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC. The results motivate further theoretical work on deconvoluting coherence and finite time effects and implementing a running coupling, as well as experimental measurement of \(D\) meson spectra to separate charm and bottom contributions.
