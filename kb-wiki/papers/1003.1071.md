# Change of shell structure and magnetic moments of odd-N deformed nuclei towards neutron drip line

> Examples of the change of neutron shell-structure in both weakly-bound and resonant neutron one-particle levels in nuclei towards the neutron drip line are exhibited. It is shown that the shell-structure change due to the weak binding may lead to the deformation of those nuclei with the neutron numbers $N \approx$ 8, 20, 28 and 40, which are known to be magic numbers in stable nuclei. Nuclei in the "island of inversion" are most easily and in a simple manner understood in terms of deformation. As an example of spectroscopic properties other than single-particle energies, magnetic moments of some weakly-bound possibly deformed odd-N nuclei with neutron numbers close to those traditional magic numbers are given, which are calculated using the wave function of the last odd particle in deformed Woods-Saxon potentials.

# Change of shell structure and magnetic moments of odd-N deformed nuclei towards neutron drip line

## Key claim

The change in neutron shell structure for weakly-bound and resonant one-particle levels, driven by the strong ℓ-dependence of the centrifugal barrier, can lead to deformation in nuclei with neutron numbers N ≈ 8, 20, 28, and 40 (traditional magic numbers in stable nuclei), and this deformation provides a simple explanation for the "island of inversion" nuclei. The calculated magnetic moments of deformed odd-N nuclei in these regions differ significantly from spherical expectations and agree with available measurements without adjusting g_s or g_R factors to individual nuclei.

## Method

The coupled Schrödinger equations for neutrons in a deformed Woods-Saxon potential were solved in coordinate space with correct asymptotic boundary conditions for both bound and resonant one-particle levels, using the eigenphase formalism to identify resonances. The potential depth was adjusted so that the last occupied one-neutron level matched the measured neutron separation energy for each odd-N nucleus, while standard parameters for diffuseness, spin-orbit strength, and radius were taken from Bohr and Mottelson (1969). Magnetic dipole moments were calculated using the deformed one-particle wave functions with the formulas from Bohr and Mottelson (1975), employing g_s^eff = g_s^free for the N ≈ 8 region and g_s^eff = 0.7 × g_s^free for heavier regions.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁷C₁₁ (N=11): measured magnetic moment of ground state (I^π = 3/2^+) agrees with calculated value based on the [211 3/2] Nilsson orbit; the near degeneracy of 1d₅/₂ and 2s₁/₂ levels at β=0 is cited as the Jahn-Teller origin of deformation.
- For ¹¹Be₇: measured magnetic moment |μ| = 1.720 ± 0.009 μ_N (I=1/2^+) is nearly equal to that of ¹⁵C₉ (spherical), explained by the [220 1/2] wave function consisting exclusively of 1d₅/₂ and 2s₁/₂ components with zero non-diagonal magnetic dipole matrix element.
- For ³¹Mg₁₉: measured magnetic moment of ground state (I^π = 1/2^+) is −0.88355(15) μ_N (Geithner et al., 2005).
- For ³³Mg₂₁: measured magnetic moment is −0.7456(5) μ_N with spin 3/2 (D. Yordanov et al., 2007); the negative value is interpreted as requiring negative parity for the ground state.
- For ⁴²Si₂₈: E(2₁^+) = 770(19) keV (Bastin et al., 2007); for ⁴⁴S₂₈: E(2₁^+) = 1297(18) keV (T. Glasmacher et al., 1997).
- For ⁴¹Si₂₇: reported neutron separation energy S_n = 1.34 MeV with large ambiguity (B. Jurado et al., 2007); at β=0 the 2p₃/₂ level lies only 1.2 MeV above the 1f₇/₂ level when the former is bound by 0.28 MeV.
- For ⁴³S₂₇: the 320 keV isomeric state has I^π = 7/2^- and g = −0.317(4) (L. Gaudefroy et al., 2009).
- For ⁶⁴Cr₄₀: cited as a possible center of deformation with first excited 2^+ state energy ≈ 500 keV (P. Adrich et al., 2008).
- For ⁶⁷Fe₄₁: a 387 keV isomeric state is reported (M. Sawicka et al., 2003), possibly from [301 1/2] or [303 5/2] intrinsic configuration if deformed.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Hamamoto, 2004, 2005, 2006, 2007a) on weakly-bound and resonant neutron levels in deformed potentials, applying the framework to four mass regions where traditional magic numbers (N=8, 20, 28, 40) may break down. The shell-structure change described is distinct from and independent of the neutron-proton tensor force mechanism (T. Otsuka et al., 2005), which is evaluated using harmonic oscillator wave functions and does not include weak-binding effects. The calculated magnetic moments for ¹¹Be, ¹⁷C, ³¹Mg, and ³³Mg agree with measurements, and the results are applicable not only to ground states of odd-N nuclei but also to excited or isomeric states in neighboring nuclei, particularly in the N≈28 and N≈40 regions where shape coexistence is expected.
