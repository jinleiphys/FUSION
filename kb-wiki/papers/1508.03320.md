# A New Dynamical Picture for the Production and Decay of the $XY \! Z$ Mesons

> I introduce an entirely new dynamical description for exotic charmoniumlike hadrons, based upon the competing effects of the strong attraction between quarks in a diquark, and the inability of the diquark to hadronize on its own due to being a color nonsinglet. This mechanism naturally explains, for example, the strong preference of the $Z(4475)$ to decay to $ψ(2S)$ rather than the $J/ψ$, the existence of a state $X(4630)$ that decays to $Λ_c$ baryon pairs, and why some but not all exotics lie near hadronic thresholds. Owing to high-energy constituent counting rules, the four-quark nature of the states produces major changes to both the high-$s$ scaling of cross sections for producing such states and to the potency of the cusp effect of attracting resonances to pair-production thresholds. The recently observed $P_c^+$ pentaquark candidates are seen to fit naturally into this scheme.

# A New Dynamical Picture for the Production and Decay of the XY Z Mesons

## Key claim
The paper proposes that exotic charmoniumlike hadrons such as the Z(4475), X(3872), and P_c^+ pentaquarks are not meson molecules or hadrocharmonium states, but rather bound states of compact color-triplet diquarks and antidiquarks (or antitriquarks) that separate to large distances due to the energy release of their production process, remaining bound only through color confinement and decaying via the large-r tails of meson or baryon wave functions.

## Method
The author applies the Cornell linear-plus-Coulomb potential (with parameters α_s = 0.5461, b = 0.1425 GeV^2, σ = 1.0946 GeV) to estimate the separation distance between diquark and antidiquark in a tetraquark state, using the kinetic energy imparted by the weak decay that produces them. The resulting spatial extent is compared to charmonium wave function sizes to explain decay branching ratios. High-energy constituent counting rules are used to derive scaling laws for cross sections and form factors of multiquark states, and the cusp effect from diquark thresholds is compared to that from meson thresholds.

## Key numbers
- The X(3872) mass minus the D*0 plus D0 threshold is -0.11 ± 0.21 MeV; minus the J/ψ plus ρ^0_peak threshold is -0.49 ± 0.30 MeV; minus the J/ψ plus ω_peak threshold is -7.88 ± 0.21 MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy of 2.22 MeV is about twenty times larger than the central value of the first mass difference above.
- The Z(4475) width is 180 ± 31 MeV, compared to the ρ(770) width of 150 MeV.
- The X(3872) width is less than 1.2 MeV.
- The estimated diquark-antidiquark separation for Z(4475) is 1.16 fm; for X(3872) it is 0.56 fm.
- The Cornell potential applied to charmonium gives ⟨r_{J/ψ}⟩ = 0.39 fm and ⟨r_{ψ(2S)}⟩ = 0.80 fm.
- The Z(4475) decays to ψ(2S) at least 10 times more frequently than to J/ψ.
- The Λ_c^+ Λ_c^- threshold is 4573 MeV (2m_{Λ_c}); the X(4630) lies above this threshold.
- The electromagnetic form factor of a tetraquark state scales as 1/s^3 at large s; if diquarks act as fundamental units, it scales as 1/s^1.
- The ratio σ(e^+ e^- → Z_c^+ π^-)/σ(e^+ e^- → μ^+ μ^-) scales as 1/s^4 for a four-quark state or 1/s^2 if diquarks are tightly bound.
- The ratio σ(e^+ e^- → Z_c^+ π^-)/σ(e^+ e^- → Λ_c Λ_c) scales as 1/s^0.
- The Λ_b root-mean-square matter radius is estimated at 0.22 fm.

## Context
This work extends the diquark picture for tetraquarks first studied by Maiani (2004) and improved by Maiani (2014), but introduces a dynamical mechanism where diquarks separate rather than orbit one another. It addresses limitations of the meson molecule picture (which struggles with prompt production of X(3872) at LHC as shown by Esposito (2013) and Guerrieri (2014)), the hadrocharmonium picture of Dubynskiy (2008), and the cusp effect of Bugg (2008). The paper enables experimental tests using constituent counting rules at facilities like JLab, and provides a natural explanation for the P_c^+ pentaquark candidates observed by LHCb (2015) through a diquark-triquark mechanism.
