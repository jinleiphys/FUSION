# Assisted fusion

> A model of nuclear fusion consisting of a wave packet impinging into a well located between square one dimensional barriers is treated analytically. The wave function inside the well is calculated exactly for the assisted tunneling induced by a perturbation mimicking a constant electric field with arbitrary time dependence. Conditions are found for the enhancement of fusion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a time-dependent external perturbation (modeled as a constant electric field with arbitrary time dependence) can enhance nuclear fusion by increasing the penetration rate of a wave packet into a well between barriers, provided the condition \(\mu Y(t)/|q_0| > t\) is satisfied; this condition depends on the coupling strength, the time-integrated perturbation, and the well depth, and is independent of the pole structure of the unperturbed system.

## Method

The author solves the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation for a wave packet incident on a square well flanked by square barriers, with a time-dependent spatially linear perturbation \(V(x,t) = \mu x G(t)\). The wave function is expanded in the complete orthonormal set of even and odd stationary states of the unperturbed system, and the time evolution of the expansion coefficients is derived analytically using a superposition integral. The long-time behavior of the wave function inside the well is obtained via contour integration in the complex energy plane, exploiting the poles of the normalization factors near their minima.

## Key numbers

- For an \(^{16}\text{O}\) nucleus model: mass \(m = 15000\ \text{MeV}\), well half-width \(x_0 = 7\ \text{fm}\), barrier outer edge \(d = 12\ \text{fm}\), barrier strength \(\gamma = 10\ \text{MeV}\), well depth \(V_0 = 40\ \text{MeV}\).
- The condition for assisted fusion requires \(\mu > 600\ \text{MeV/cm}\), corresponding to an electric field amplitude \(|E_0| \approx 8 \times 10^7\ \text{Volt/cm}\).
- The required perturbation frequency is \(\Omega \approx 17\ \text{GHz}\), with pulse durations on the order of nanoseconds.
- For a low-density oxygen gas at \(100\ \text{K}\) and \(0.1\ \text{Pa}\), the average collision time is \(\delta t \approx 6 \times 10^{-10}\ \text{sec}\), and the condition \(\Omega \delta t = 10\) is used.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analytical studies of assisted tunneling in one-dimensional metastable states (Kälbermann, 2008, 2009) to the case of fusion, modeled as a wave packet penetrating a well between barriers. It contrasts with \(\alpha\) decay, where assisted tunneling accelerates decay, by identifying a different parameter space for enhancing penetration into the well. The results suggest that external time-dependent fields (e.g., microwave pulses) could enable experimental investigation of fusion enhancement, though the author notes that actual nuclear fusion involves more complex nuclear structure than this simplified model.
