# A perturbative QCD analysis of charged-particle distributions in hadronic and nuclear collisions

> We compute the distributions of charged particles at large transverse momenta in $p\bar p(p)$, $pA$ and $AA$ collisions in the framework of perturbative QCD, by using collinear factorization and the modern PDFs and fragmentation functions. At the highest cms-energies the shape of the spectra measured in $p\bar p(p)$ collisions at large $q_T$ can be well explained. The difference between the data and the lowest-order computation is quantified in terms of a constant $K$-factor for each energy. The $K$-factor is found to systematically decrease with growing $\sqrt s$. Also a lower limit for the partonic transverse momentum, $p_0$, is extracted for each $\sqrt s$ based on the comparison with the measurements. A systematic increase of $p_0$ as a function of $\sqrt s$ is found. Nuclear effects in the charged-particle spectra in $pA$ and $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC are studied in the framework of collinear factorization by applying the EKS98 nuclear corrections to the parton distributions. The nuclear effects are shown to mostly enhance the computed spectra. A comparison with the recent PHENIX data from central and peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC is done.

## Key claim

The authors show that a strictly leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD calculation, supplemented only with a constant K-factor and no other phenomenological factors (such as intrinsic kT effects), can reproduce the shape of measured charged-particle transverse momentum spectra at large qT in hadronic collisions, with the K-factor systematically decreasing as center-of-mass energy increases.

## Method

The inclusive charged-hadron production cross sections were computed in LO pQCD using collinear factorization, CTEQ5 parton distribution functions, and KKP fragmentation functions, with scales set to Q = pT, μF = qT, and the 1-loop running coupling. For each center-of-mass energy, the K-factor (defined as K ≡ dσ_exp / dσ_LO) was determined by a χ² fit to the highest-qT data points, requiring χ²/N ≤ 1, and a lower limit for the partonic transverse momentum cutoff p0 was extracted by requiring the computation not to overshoot the data at qT ≥ p0. Nuclear effects in pA and AA collisions were studied by applying EKS98 nuclear modifications to the parton distributions within the same collinearly factorized framework.

## Key numbers

- K = 6.19 ± 0.58 for √s = 63 GeV (AFS data, p+p → h+X, |y| < 0.6, qTN = 4.65 GeV, N = 2)
- K = 3.42 ± 0.40 for √s = 200 GeV (UA1 data, p+anti-p → h+X, |η| < 2.5, qTN = 3.45 GeV, N = 8)
- K = 1.94 ± 0.20 for √s = 500 GeV (UA1 data, qTN = 3.35 GeV, N = 9)
- K = 2.19 ± 0.11 for √s = 630 GeV (UA1 MIMI data, |η| < 3.0, qTN = 7.988 GeV, N = 15)
- K = 1.09 ± 0.10 for √s = 900 GeV (UA1 data, qTN = 4.9 GeV, N = 10)
- K = 1.28 ± 0.18 for √s = 1800 GeV (CDF data, |y| < 1.0, qTN = 6.0 GeV, N = 5)
- Lower limit p0 = 1.9 GeV for √s = 630 GeV (UA1 MIMI data)
- Lower limit p0 = 2.2 GeV for √s = 1800 GeV (CDF data)
- Nuclear effects enhance pQCD spectra in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC at qT less than about 10 GeV by at most 15%

## Context

This work extends the LO pQCD approach of X.-N. Wang (1991) and Lévai (1995) for charged-particle production to higher energies, and contrasts with NLO calculations by Borzumati (1998) and Aurenche (2000) which used NLO partonic cross sections and NLO fragmentation functions. The systematic decrease of the K-factor with √s and the extracted p0 values provide constraints for minijet-based models of particle production (such as those by Sjöstrand and by Eskola, Kajantie, and Lindfors), and the nuclear effect predictions using EKS98 nuclear PDFs enable comparison with RHIC data from PHENIX to test whether collinear factorization alone can describe nuclear collisions.
