# Electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions: progress and open questions

> I present an overview of photon and dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, highlighting recent progress and ongoing challenges, with focus on hard initial scatterings, pre-equilibrium electromagnetic emission, as well as thermal and hadronic production. The potential of photons and dileptons to probe low-energy collisions is discussed briefly.

# Electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions: progress and open questions

## Key claim
Photons and dileptons can provide stringent tests of our understanding of nuclear collisions across all stages, but experimental uncertainties remain considerable compared to hadronic measurements, and increasing the accuracy of electromagnetic measurements will provide valuable insights into heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The paper reviews photon and dilepton production mechanisms across different stages of heavy-ion collisions: hard initial scatterings (prompt photons and Drell-Yan dileptons), pre-equilibrium emission, thermal emission from the hydrodynamic regime, and hadronic interactions and decays. It contrasts microscopic approaches (e.g., partonic cross-sections, hadronic transport codes like SMASH, UrQMD, JAM, PHSD) with macroscopic approaches using equilibrium and non-equilibrium emission rates combined with hydrodynamic simulations, and discusses how viscous corrections to the energy-momentum tensor modify electromagnetic emission rates.

## Context
This review extends the overviews of photon and dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions by David (2019) and Geurts (2022), and discusses recent progress presented at HardProbes2023. It connects electromagnetic probes to ongoing challenges in understanding the early stage of collisions, the transition to and from the hydrodynamic regime, and the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of transport coefficients. The paper enables future work by identifying open questions, particularly the need for better understanding of low-p_T photon and low-invariant-mass dilepton production in proton-proton collisions, and the challenge of modeling jet-medium photons and fragmentation photons in heavy-ion collisions.
