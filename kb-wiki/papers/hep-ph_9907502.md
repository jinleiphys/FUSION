# Low-Mass Dileptons at the CERN-SpS: Evidence for Chiral Restoration?

> Using a rather complete description of the in-medium $ρ$ spectral function - being constrained by various independent experimental information - we calculate pertinent dilepton production rates from hot and dense hadronic matter. The strong broadening of the $ρ$ resonance entails a reminiscence to perturbative $q\bar q$ annihilation rates in the vicinity of the phase boundary. The application to dilepton observables in Pb(158AGeV)+Au collisions - incorporating recent information on the hadro-chemical composition at CERN-SpS energies - essentially supports the broadening scenario. Possible implications for the nature of chiral symmetry restoration are outlined.

# Low-Mass Dileptons at the CERN-SpS: Evidence for Chiral Restoration?

## Key claim
The strong broadening of the in-medium rho meson spectral function in hot and dense hadronic matter produces dilepton rates that approach perturbative quark-antiquark annihilation rates near the phase boundary, which may indicate a lowering of the quark-hadron duality threshold rather than a direct signature of chiral restoration. The authors caution that an unambiguous identification of chiral restoration requires simultaneous treatment of the axial correlator, which has not been performed here.

## Method
The authors calculated the in-medium rho meson spectral function using a many-body Dyson equation approach, incorporating meson gas effects through interactions with pions, kaons, and rho mesons saturated by s-channel resonances up to 1.3 GeV, and baryonic effects through rho-nucleon interactions and pion cloud modifications. They applied these rates to a thermal fireball expansion model for 30% central Pb(158 AGeV)+Au collisions, with a space-time trajectory constrained by hadro-chemical freezeout analyses at (T, mu_B)_ch = (175, 270) MeV and entropy per baryon s/rho_B = 26, including finite pion and kaon chemical potentials arising from pion-number conservation between chemical and thermal freezeout.

## Key numbers
- At T = 150 MeV and mu_B = 436 MeV, where about 2/3 of baryons are in excited states, the additional broadening from baryonic resonance excitations on excited baryons amounts to about 40% compared to excitations on nucleons alone.
- The fireball trajectory passes through (T, mu_N) = (175, 250) MeV, compatible with the experimentally deduced chemical freezeout point, with a small mu_pi of about 20 MeV.
- At thermal freezeout (T, mu_N)_fo approximately (110-120, 415-450) MeV, mu_pi and mu_K increase to around 80 and 110 MeV, respectively.
- Initial conditions (T, rho_B)_ini = (190 MeV, 2.55 rho_0) with freezeout at (T, rho_B)_fo = (115 MeV, 0.33 rho_0) yield a fireball lifetime of about t_fo = 11 fm/c.
- Reducing initial conditions to (180 MeV, 1.92 rho_0) reduces the dilepton signal from the fireball by about 15%.
- The overall dilepton yield exceeds hydrodynamical calculations with mu_pi = 0 by about a factor of 2.5.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier many-body calculations of the in-medium rho spectral function (Rapp, Chanfray, Wambach, 1997) and applies them to CERES/NA45 data from Pb(158 AGeV)+Au collisions. It contrasts with the "dropping rho mass" scenario of Brown and Rho (1991) and with hydrodynamical simulations that do not include finite pion chemical potentials, such as those by Heinz and collaborators (1999). The inclusion of finite pion chemical potentials between chemical and thermal freezeout, following the work of Bebie and collaborators, resolves a large part of the discrepancy in total dilepton yield between transport calculations and chemical equilibrium-based hydrodynamic simulations. The results enable further tests with upcoming high-resolution measurements and the more baryon-dominated 40 GeV run at the CERN-SpS.
