# Microscopic Structure of Rotational Damping

> The damping of collective rotational motion is studied microscopically, making use of shell model calculations based on the cranked Nilsson deformed mean-field and on residual two-body interactions, and focusing on the shape of the gamma-gamma correlation spectra and on its systematic behavior. It is shown that the spectral shape is directly related to the damping width of collective rotation, \Gammarot, and to the spreading width of many-particle many-hole configurations, \Gammamu. The rotational damping width is affected by the shell structure, and is very sensitive to the position of the Fermi surface, besides mass number, spin and deformation. This produces a rich variety of features in the rotational damping phenomena.

## Key claim

The spectral shape of gamma-gamma correlation spectra is directly related to both the rotational damping width Γrot and the spreading width Γμ of many-particle many-hole configurations, and the rotational damping width is very sensitive to the position of the Fermi surface due to shell structure, not just to mass number, spin, and deformation as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors performed shell model diagonalizations using the cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky deformed mean-field with residual two-body interactions (surface delta interaction, volume delta force, and random two-body interaction) to describe off-yrast energy levels and rotational E2 transitions. They included all many-particle many-hole (np-nh) configurations in the relevant energy region (up to a few MeV, corresponding to 10^2-10^3 levels per spin and parity) for six nuclei: 114Te, 168Yb, 234U, 143Eu, 152Dy, and 192Hg. The gamma-gamma correlation spectra for two consecutive E2 gamma rays were calculated and decomposed into two Gaussian components to extract the narrow component width and the rotational damping width.

## Key numbers

- Rotational damping width Γrot for 168Yb: 224 keV (spin interval I=36-45, states 51st to 100th)
- Rotational damping width Γrot for 114Te: 387 keV (same spin and level interval)
- Rotational damping width Γrot for 234U: 33 keV (same spin and level interval)
- Rotational damping width Γrot for superdeformed 143Eu: 143 keV (spin interval I=46-55, states 51st to 100th)
- Rotational damping width Γrot for superdeformed 152Dy: 67 keV (spin interval I=46-55, states 51st to 100th)
- Rotational damping width Γrot for superdeformed 192Hg: 23 keV (spin interval I=46-55, states 51st to 100th)
- Spreading width Γμ for 168Yb: 45 keV (same interval)
- Spreading width Γμ for 114Te: 98 keV (same interval)
- Spreading width Γμ for 234U: 46 keV (same interval)
- Spreading width Γμ for superdeformed 143Eu: 43 keV (same interval)
- Spreading width Γμ for superdeformed 152Dy: 59 keV (same interval)
- Spreading width Γμ for superdeformed 192Hg: 28 keV (same interval)
- Number of rotational bands Nband near yrast for superdeformed 192Hg: 150

## Context

This work extends the microscopic description of rotational damping developed by Lauritzen et al. (1986) and earlier calculations by Matsuo et al. (1993, 1997), providing a systematic comparison across six nuclei spanning normal-deformed (114Te, 168Yb, 234U) and superdeformed (143Eu, 152Dy, 192Hg) regions. The results enable quantitative predictions for quasicontinuum gamma-ray spectra observed in experiments on superdeformed nuclei (Eucont, Schiffer, Lauritsen 1992, Hgcont) and identify 192Hg as being near the borderline for the emergence of "ergodic rotational bands" as proposed by Mottelson and by Aberg (1996), where Γrot becomes comparable to the level spacing.
