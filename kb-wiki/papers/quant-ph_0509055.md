# The Trigonometric Rosen-Morse Potential in the Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics and its Exact Solutions

> The analytic solutions of the one-dimensional Schroedinger equation for the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential reported in the literature rely upon the Jacobi polynomials with complex indices and complex arguments. We first draw attention to the fact that the complex Jacobi polynomials have non-trivial orthogonality properties which make them uncomfortable for physics applications. Instead we here solve above equation in terms of real orthogonal polynomials. The new solutions are used in the construction of the quantum-mechanic superpotential.

# The Trigonometric Rosen-Morse Potential in the Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics and its Exact Solutions

## Key claim
The trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential can be solved exactly using real orthogonal polynomials rather than the complex Jacobi polynomials with complex indices and arguments that were previously reported in the literature. The authors claim that the complex Jacobi polynomials have non-trivial orthogonality properties that make them uncomfortable for physics applications, and that their new real solutions avoid these difficulties.

## Method
The authors transform the one-dimensional Schroedinger equation for the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential into a Sturm-Liouville form through successive variable changes (first to an exponential factor, then to x = cot z, then a factorization ansatz). They then apply the Rodrigues formula to generate a family of real orthogonal polynomials C_n^{(a,b)}(x) that satisfy the resulting differential equation, with the weight function w_n(x) = (1+x^2)^{-(n+a)} exp(-alpha_n arccot x) and s(x) = 1+x^2. The superpotential is constructed from the exact ground state wave function using the standard supersymmetric quantum mechanics relation U(z) = -(hbar/sqrt(2m)) d/dz ln psi_gst(z).

## Key numbers
- Energy spectrum for the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential: epsilon_n = (n+a)^2 - b^2/(n+a)^2, with n >= 1
- For the special case a=0: epsilon_n = n^2 - b^2/n^2
- Normalization constant for a=0 case: K_n = [(n!)^2 n^3 (1 - exp(-2 pi b/n)) / (4 b (b^2 + n^4))]^(1/2)
- Ground state wave function for a != 0: R_1(z) proportional to exp(-b z/(a+1)) sin^(a+1) z
- Superpotential: U(z) = -b/(a+1) + (a+1) cot z
- The supersymmetric companion Hamiltonian: tilde H(z) = -d^2/dz^2 - 2b cot z + (a+1)(a+2) csc^2 z

## Context
This work extends the known exactly soluble potentials in supersymmetric quantum mechanics, specifically the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential previously treated by Khare (no year given) using complex Jacobi polynomials derived from the Eckart potential (Eckart, no year given). The authors note that the complex Jacobi polynomials have orthogonality properties studied by Jacobi-c and Jacobi-otros (no years given) that depend on indices and integration contours. The new real solutions enable straightforward construction of the superpotential and hierarchy of Hamiltonians following Sukumar (no year given), and the authors suggest applications to baryon spectroscopy based on a mass formula from RVM-KMS and RVM-K (no years given) that matches the tRMP spectrum pattern for nucleon and Delta resonances.
