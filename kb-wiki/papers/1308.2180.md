# Amplified Fermion Production from Overpopulated Bose Fields

> We study the real-time dynamics of fermions coupled to scalar fields in a linear sigma model, which is often employed in the context of preheating after inflation or as a low-energy effective model for quantum chromodynamics. We find a dramatic amplification of fermion production in the presence of highly occupied bosonic quanta for weak as well as strong couplings. For this we consider the range of validity of different methods: lattice simulations with male/female fermions, the mode functions approach and the quantum 2PI effective action with its associated kinetic theory. For strongly coupled fermions we find a rapid approach to a Fermi-Dirac distribution with time-dependent temperature and chemical potential parameters, while the bosons are still far from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The authors confirm a dramatic amplification of fermion production in the presence of highly occupied bosonic quanta, extending previous results from weak to strong couplings, and find that for strongly coupled fermions a Fermi-Dirac distribution emerges rapidly while the bosonic sector remains far from equilibrium.

## Method

The authors simulate the real-time dynamics of Dirac fermions coupled to scalar fields in a linear sigma model using three complementary methods: lattice simulations with male/female fermions (a stochastic approach), the exact mode functions expansion, and the quantum 2PI effective action with its associated kinetic theory. They employ a pseudoscalar Wilson term for improved lattice discretization to resolve high-momentum behavior, and initialize the system with a homogeneous scalar field amplitude to study parametric resonance.

## Key numbers

- The initial scalar field amplitude is set to \(\phi(t=0) = \sigma_0 \sqrt{6N/\lambda}\), where \(\sigma_0\) sets the overall scale for simulations.
- The simulated spatial volume is \(V = (N a_s)^3\) with lattice spacing \(a_s\) and number of lattice points \(N\) in each direction.
- The temporal lattice spacing \(a_t\) is chosen much smaller than the spatial lattice spacing \(a_t \ll a_s\) to avoid temporal doublers.
- The fermion dispersion relation with the pseudoscalar Wilson term is \(\omega({\bf p}) = \sqrt{m_\psi^2 + \bar p_i \bar p^i + a_s^2 {\bf p}_{\rm lat}^4 / 4}\), eliminating the \(O(a_s)\) contribution.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Berges, Gelfand, and Pruschke (2013) on amplified fermion production from overpopulated Bose fields, confirming the effect for both weak and strong couplings. The study compares lattice simulations with male/female fermions (Borsanyi and Hindmarsh, 2008), the mode functions approach (Aarts and Smit, 1999), and the quantum 2PI effective action (Berges, 2002). The results enable future studies of fermion production in preheating after inflation and in low-energy effective models for quantum chromodynamics, particularly in regimes where bosonic fields are highly occupied and far from equilibrium.
