# Effects of final state interactions on charge separation in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Charge separation is an important consequence of the Chiral Magnetic Effect. Within the framework of a multi-phase transport model, the effects of final state interactions on initial charge separation are studied. We demonstrate that charge separation can be significantly reduced by the evolution of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Hadronization and resonance decay can also affect charge separation. Moreover, our results show that the Chiral Magnetic Effect leads to the modification of the relation between the charge azimuthal correlation and the elliptic flow that is expected from transverse momentum conservation only. The transverse momentum and pseudorapidity dependences of, and the effects of background on the charge azimuthal correlation are also discussed.

## Key claim

Final state interactions (parton cascade, hadronization, and resonance decay) significantly reduce initial charge separation from 10% to 1-2% in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, meaning that the Chiral Magnetic Effect signal must survive these strong final state effects to be observable; the same-charge azimuthal correlation data cannot be explained by transverse momentum conservation alone and require an initial charge separation of about 10%.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with the string melting option to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV with a 10 mb parton cross section. They artificially introduced initial charge separation by swapping the transverse momentum components of up and anti-up quarks (and down and anti-down quarks) moving in opposite directions perpendicular to the reaction plane, then tracked the evolution of the charge azimuthal correlation observable through parton cascade, coalescence hadronization, and resonance decay stages.

## Key numbers

- Initial charge separation of 10% yields final charged pion correlations matching STAR data for the 60-70% centrality bin; 5% initial separation produces results nearly identical to zero separation, indicating a detection threshold
- Initial charge correlations have magnitude about 5.5 x 10^{-3}; after parton cascade, correlations are significantly reduced, especially for central collisions
- From 10% initial charge separation, only 1-2% remains at the end, with more peripheral collisions retaining larger percentages
- For the same-charge correlation without initial separation, the power-law exponent n in the transverse momentum dependence is 2.24 ± 0.27, consistent with transverse momentum conservation; with 10% initial separation, n decreases to 1.54 ± 0.18
- The same-charge correlation without initial separation follows the relation <cos(phi_alpha+phi_beta)> = -v2/N from transverse momentum conservation; with 10% initial separation, the linear relation is preserved but with a coefficient much larger than 1

## Context

This work extends studies of the Chiral Magnetic Effect by Kharzeev (1998, 2004, 2007) and Fukushima (2008, 2010) by including for the first time the dynamical effects of final state interactions (parton cascade and resonance decay) on charge separation observables. It directly addresses the alternative explanations of Bzdak (2010) who attributed charge correlations to transverse momentum conservation, Wang (2009) who argued for cluster correlations, and Schlichting and Pratt (2010) who emphasized local charge conservation with elliptic flow. The results enable future work to incorporate magnetic field evolution, fluctuating domain sizes, and charge balancing mechanisms as suggested by Schlichting and Pratt (2010) to achieve a complete description of experimental data.
