# Addendum to Strangeness Production and Color Deconfinement

> Recent extensive data from the beam energy scan of the STAR collaboration at BNL-RHIC provide the basis for a detailed update for the universal behavior of the strangeness suppression factor gamma_s as function of the initial entropy density, as proposed in our recent paper [1]. [1] P. Castorina, S. Plumari and H. Satz, Int. J. Mod. Phys. E26 (2017) 1750081 (arXiv:1709.02706)

## Key claim

The strangeness suppression factor γ_s, when expressed as a function of the initial entropy density (s₀τ₀), forms a universal curve for pp, pA, and AA collisions that rises from about 0.5 to unity precisely in the color deconfinement transition region; the new STAR beam energy scan data provide more detailed support for this claim, though the transverse area estimates for non-central Au-Au collisions are acknowledged as rough and will be refined by a future Glauber analysis.

## Method

The authors took values of γ_s(√s, N_part) from STAR’s beam energy scan analysis of Au-Au collisions at cms energies 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV, for various centralities measured by N_part. They converted these into γ_s(s₀τ₀) using the one-dimensional hydrodynamic formula for initial entropy density, specifying transverse areas: R_pp = 0.8 fm, R_pPb = 1.3 fm, and R_AuAu = 1.25 (N_part/2)^(1/3) for centrality-dependent Au-Au collisions. The resulting γ_s values were plotted against s₀τ₀ together with earlier pp and pPb data from their previous work.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement temperature from lattice studies is T_c = 154 ± 9 MeV, corresponding to an entropy density range s₀ = 2.5 ± 0.3 fm⁻³.
- The guiding curve for γ_s as a function of s₀τ₀ is γ_s = 1 − 0.997 exp{−0.575 s₀τ₀}, with s₀τ₀ in units fm⁻².
- For the most central Au-Au collisions at all considered beam energies (7.7 to 39 GeV), γ_s values lie in the range 0.9 – 1.0.
- The transverse area for pp collisions is R_pp = 0.8 fm; for pPb collisions, R_pPb = 1.3 fm.

## Context

This addendum extends the authors’ earlier work (Castorina, Plumari, Satz, 2017) which proposed that γ_s becomes a universal function of initial entropy density or temperature. The new STAR beam energy scan data (Xu, 2018) provide more detailed confirmation across a range of energies and centralities, showing that peripheral AA collisions are necessary to see the full transition from suppression to equilibration, whereas earlier data restricted to central collisions had concluded γ_s ≈ 1. The results corroborate the coincidence of deconfinement and strangeness equilibration, and the authors plan a future study using a Glauber analysis for more precise transverse area determination.
