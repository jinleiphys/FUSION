# High temperature Bose-Einstein condensation

> The indications of a possible pion condensation at the LHC are summarized. The condensation is predicted by the non-equilibrium hadronization model for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. The model solves the proton/pion puzzle and reproduces the low $p_T$ enhancement of the pion spectra, as well as the spectra of protons and antiprotons, charged kaons, $K^0_S$, $K^*(892)^0$ and $ϕ(1020)$. The obtained parameters allow to estimate the amount of pion condensate on the level of 5\% from the total number of pions at the LHC. The condensate is located at $p_T<250$ MeV.

## Key claim

The non-equilibrium hadronization model predicts that approximately 5% of the total number of pions produced in 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC may be in a Bose-Einstein condensate, located at transverse momentum below 250 MeV, contingent on the explicit treatment of the hadronic ground state.

## Method

The author fits mean multiplicities of pions, kaons, protons, and strange particles using a non-equilibrium hadron-resonance gas (NEQ) model with parameters temperature, volume, and non-equilibrium factors γ_q and γ_s. The particle spectra are then calculated using the THERMINATOR Monte-Carlo event generator with the Cracow freeze-out hypersurface, which assumes Hubble-like flow. A modification is introduced to treat the ground state explicitly by replacing the momentum integral with a discrete sum over low-momentum quantum levels when the chemical potential approaches the pion mass, allowing for a condensate component.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the BEC temperature in a pion gas to that in atomic gases is approximately 10^12, derived from the mass and size ratios: T_C(π)/T_C(A) ≃ (m_A/m_π)(r_A/r_π)^2 ≃ 10^12.
- The condensate is located at p_T < 250 MeV, with the maximal transverse momentum from the freeze-out hypersurface movement given by p_T < 250 MeV.
- The condensate constitutes approximately 5% of the total number of pions at the LHC, based on fits of mean multiplicities and spectra.
- The non-equilibrium chemical potential for pions is μ_π = 2T ln γ_q ≃ 134 MeV, close to the π^0 mass of 134.98 MeV.
- The σ meson implemented as a Breit-Wigner pole with M_σ = 484 MeV and Γ_σ/2 = 255 MeV produces up to 5% of pions, but the true contribution from ππ phase shifts is -0.3%.

## Context

This work extends the non-equilibrium hadron-resonance gas approach of Rafelski and collaborators (2015) and the SHARE model of Torrieri et al. (2004), applied previously to lower-energy collisions. It addresses the proton/pion puzzle at the LHC, where equilibrium hadron-resonance gas fits over-predict protons by nearly three standard deviations (Stachel, 2013) and show a 50% deficit of low-p_T pions compared to hydrodynamic models (Abelev, 2013). The inclusion of the ground state resolves discrepancies between equilibrium and non-equilibrium models, and the updated σ meson treatment from Broniowski (2015) shows that the σ decay contribution is negligible, strengthening the case for pion condensation. The results enable further experimental searches for high-temperature BEC in heavy-ion collisions.
