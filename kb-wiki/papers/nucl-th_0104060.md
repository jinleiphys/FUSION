# Dipole resonances in oxygen isotopes in time-dependent density-matrix theory

> The strength functions of isovector dipole resonances in the even oxygen isotopes $^{18\sim24}$O are calculated by using an extended version of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory known as the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM). The results are compared with recent experimental data and also with a shell-model calculation. It is found that the observed isotope dependence of low-lying dipole strength is reproduced in TDDM when the strength of the residual interaction is properly chosen. It is also found that there is a difference between the TDDM prediction and the shell-model calculation for $^{24}$O.

## Key claim

The observed isotope dependence of low-lying isovector dipole strength in oxygen isotopes is reproduced by time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) only when the strength of the residual interaction is appropriately chosen; the TDDM prediction for $^{24}$O differs from a shell-model calculation, originating in the difference in the spreading width of the giant dipole resonance between the two models.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) and TDDM calculations for the even oxygen isotopes $^{18-24}$O using the Skyrme III effective interaction with spin-orbit force. A correlated ground state was obtained by adiabatically evolving the HF ground state with a time-dependent residual interaction of delta-function form, and the isovector dipole mode was excited by boosting the single-particle wavefunctions with a dipole velocity field. The strength function was extracted from the Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment, with a damping factor of $\Gamma = 1$ MeV applied.

## Key numbers

- Residual interaction strength $v_0 = -230$ MeV fm$^3$ was determined to approximately reproduce the observed occupation probability of the proton $1d_{5/2}$ state in $^{16}$O; a stronger value $v_0 = -330$ MeV fm$^3$ was also used.
- The time constant $\tau$ for the adiabatic treatment of the residual interaction was 150 fm/c; the time step size was 0.75 fm/c.
- TDDM calculations were stopped at $t = 1200$ fm/c; the upper limit of time integration for the strength function was 450 fm/c, making the strength below about 3 MeV not well determined.
- The contribution of the momentum-dependent part of the Skyrme interaction to the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) was about 28% of the total EWSR value; the term from ground-state correlations was less than 1% of the total sum rule value.
- The fractions of EWSR values depleted over the energy range 0-40 MeV were about 85% in TDDM and about 90% in TDHF.
- Calculated occupation probabilities of the neutron $2s_{1/2}$ and $1d_{3/2}$ states were 2-3% in the oxygen isotopes considered.

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory TDDM calculations for $^{22}$O by Tohyama (1994) that neglected spin-orbit force and overestimated ground-state correlations. The results are compared with recent experimental data from Leistenschneider et al. (2001) at GSI and with shell-model calculations by Sagawa and Suzuki (1999). The paper enables further investigation of the magicity of $N=16$ in neutron-drip-line nuclei, as suggested by Ozawa et al. (2000) and Utsuno et al. (1999), though the authors note that quantitative discussion of this magicity is difficult with the present TDDM calculations due to the weak $N$-dependence of the neutron $2s_{1/2}$-$1d_{3/2}$ energy gap.
