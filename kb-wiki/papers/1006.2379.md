# Finite-size effects on the radiative energy loss of a fast parton in hot and dense strongly interacting matter

> We consider finite-size effects on the radiative energy loss of a fast parton moving in a finite temperature strongly interacting medium, using the light cone path integral formalism put forward by Zakharov. We present a convenient reformulation of the problem which makes possible its exact numerical analysis. This is done by introducing the concept of a radiation rate in the presence of finite-size effects. This effectively extends the finite-temperature approach of AMY (Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe) to include interference between vacuum and medium radiation. We compare results with those obtained in the regime considered by AMY, with those obtained at leading order in an opacity expansion, and with those obtained deep in the LPM (Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal) regime.

## Key claim

This paper extends the finite-temperature AMY (Arnold-Moore-Yaffe) formalism for radiative energy loss to include finite-size effects and interference between vacuum and medium radiation, by reformulating the BDMPS-Z (Baier-Dokshifter-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff) light-cone path integral approach into a numerically tractable radiation rate that depends on a single compact time integration.

## Method

The authors re-express Zakharov's light-cone path integral formula for the probability of bremsstrahlung emission from a fast parton, which contains two non-compact time integrations, by introducing a time derivative to define a radiation rate with a single compact time integration. They Fourier-transform the problem to transverse momentum space, integrate by parts to remove the infinite time integrals, and identify the emission time as the time of the last collision influencing the emission. The resulting rate equation is solved numerically for a "QCD brick" (a slab of thermal partonic matter at temperature T), and compared with the AMY infinite-medium limit, the leading-order opacity expansion (GLV), and the deep LPM harmonic oscillator approximation.

## Key numbers

- The paper defines the QCD Casimirs as C_A=3 for gluons and C_F=4/3 for quarks, with N_F T_F = 3/2.
- The Debye screening mass in QCD is given as m_D^2 = (3/2) g_s^2 T^2.
- The collision kernel used is C(q) = g_s^2 m_D^2 T / [q^2 (q^2 + m_D^2)], obtained at leading order in thermal field theory.
- The quark momentum broadening coefficient is defined as hat{q}_q = (4/3) integral over q^2 C(q) up to a cutoff q_max.
- The paper states that formation times scale as t ~ k/T^2 in the Bethe-Heitler regime and t ~ k^{1/2}/T^{3/2} in the LPM regime.

## Context

This work bridges the finite-temperature AMY formalism (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2001-2003) with the BDMPS-Z approach (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1997; Zakharov, 1996) by showing that the AMY rate is recovered in the large-time limit of the new formulation. It extends the opacity expansion approach of Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (GLV, 2000-2001) by providing an exact numerical implementation of the BDMPS-Z formula that includes all orders in the number of collisions. The paper enables future phenomenological studies of jet quenching at RHIC and LHC by separating finite-size effects from medium evolution dynamics, using the idealized "QCD brick" as a testbed for comparing different theoretical approaches.
