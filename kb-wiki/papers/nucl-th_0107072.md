# Two-body relaxation times in heated nuclei

> The retardation and temperature effects in two-body collisions are studied. The collision integral with retardation effects is obtained on the base of the Kadanoff- Baym equations for Green functions in a form with allowance for reaching the local equilibrium system. The collisional relaxation times of collective vibrations are calculated using both the transport approach and doorway state mechanism with hierarchy of particle-hole configurations in heated nuclei. The relaxation times of the kinetic method are rather slowly dependent on multipolarity of the Fermi surface distortion and mode of the collective motion. The dependence of the relaxation times on temperature as well as on frequency of collective vibrations is considered and compared. It is shown that variations of the in-medium two-body cross-sections with energy lead to non-quadratic dependence of the collisional relaxation time both on temperature and on collective motion frequency.

## Key claim

The paper derives a modified collision integral for two-body collisions in heated nuclei that includes retardation (memory) effects while ensuring the system relaxes toward local equilibrium, correcting the Born approximation which previously did not satisfy this property. The authors show that variations of in-medium two-body cross-sections with energy lead to a non-quadratic dependence of the collisional relaxation time on both temperature and collective motion frequency.

## Method

The authors start from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green functions to obtain a collision integral with retardation effects, then modify the Born-approximation expression to ensure the system tends toward local equilibrium. They calculate partial collective relaxation times using both a semiclassical kinetic equation approach (with the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov procedure for momentum and energy integrations) and a doorway state mechanism based on the hierarchy of particle-hole configurations in heated nuclei. The relaxation times are evaluated for isoscalar and isovector modes of collective vibrations as functions of temperature, frequency, and multipolarity of the Fermi surface distortion.

## Key numbers

- The parameter defining relaxation times in the isotropic scattering approximation is \(\widetilde{\alpha}^{(\pm)}_{\ell} = 4.18 / \left[ c_{\ell} + 1.3 d^{(\pm)}_{\ell}\right]\) MeV, with free-space cross sections \(\sigma^{(free)}_{av} = 3.75\) fm² and \(\sigma^{(free)}_{np} = 5\) fm² adopted near Fermi energy.
- At \(\ell \to \infty\), the parameter \(\widetilde{\alpha}^{(\pm)}_{\ell=\to \infty} \equiv \widetilde{\alpha} = 4.18\) MeV.
- The ratio \(\widetilde{\alpha} / \widetilde{\alpha}^{(-)}_{\ell=1} \simeq 0.9\) and \(\widetilde{\alpha}^{(-)}_{\ell=2} / \widetilde{\alpha}^{(-)}_{\ell=1} \simeq 1.1\) for isovector modes.
- The ratio \(\widetilde{\alpha} / \widetilde{\alpha}^{(+)}_{\ell=2} \simeq 0.8\) and \(\widetilde{\alpha}^{(+)}_{\ell=3} / \widetilde{\alpha}^{(+)}_{\ell=2} \simeq 1.4\) for isoscalar modes.
- The relaxation time formula in the isotropic scattering approximation is \(\hbar / \tau^{(\pm)}_{\ell} = (1 / \alpha^{(\pm)}_{\ell}) \left[(\hbar \omega/2 \pi)^2 + T^2\right]\).
- The energy-integrated factor from the kinetic approach is \({\cal R}(\omega, T) = (2/3 \pi)(m / \hbar^2) \{ (\hbar \omega)^2 + (2\pi T)^2 \}\) in the low-temperature, low-frequency limit with \(m^{*} \simeq m\).

## Context

This work extends the treatment of collisional damping in Fermi liquids by incorporating retardation effects into the collision integral, building on the Kadanoff-Baym formalism used by KMP (1992) and KP (1994). It provides a unified framework connecting the kinetic approach of Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1958) with the doorway state mechanism for giant resonances, enabling calculations of damping widths for isoscalar and isovector collective vibrations in heated nuclei. The results enable comparison with the Landau prescription for zero sound attenuation (Landau, 1957) and with earlier work by Bertsch (1978) on viscosity relaxation times.
