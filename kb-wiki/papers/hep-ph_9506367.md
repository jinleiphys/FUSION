# Parton Gas Model for the Nucleon Structure Functions

> A phenomenological model for the nucleon structure functions is presented. Visualising the nucleon as a cavity filled with parton gas in thermal equilibrium and parametrizing the effects due to the finiteness of the nucleon volume, we obtain a good fit to the data on the unpolarized nucleon structure functions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple statistical model treating the nucleon as a gas of noninteracting partons in thermal equilibrium, when augmented with a phenomenological parametrization of finite-volume corrections (involving two free parameters), can reproduce the observed small-x rise of the nucleon structure functions and the violation of the Gottfried sum rule, despite the fact that the bare gas model predicts F2(x) ~ x^2 at small x in violent conflict with data.

## Method

The nucleon is modeled as a spherical cavity of radius R = 1.11 fm containing massless quarks, antiquarks, and gluons in thermal equilibrium at temperature T with chemical potentials μu and μd. The parton density of states is multiplied by a phenomenological finite-size correction factor Φ(ER) containing either one or two free parameters (B, δ or a, b). The three constraints of net quark numbers (nu - nū = 2, nd - nđ = 1) and total longitudinal momentum fraction unity are solved for T, μu, and μd. The resulting parton distributions at input scale Q0² = M² are evolved using leading-order Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi equations with ΛQCD = 0.3 GeV, and the free parameters are fitted to deep inelastic scattering data on F2p(x) at Q² = 15 GeV².

## Key numbers

- Gottfried sum SG = 0.22 predicted at Q² = 4 GeV², consistent with the experimental value 0.235 ± 0.026
- For the Φ1 parametrization: fitted parameters B = 0.269 and δ = 2.14, with T = 63 MeV, μu = 124 MeV, μd = 64 MeV
- For the Φ2 parametrization: fitted parameters a = -1.88 and b = 2.24, with T = 72 MeV, μu = 162 MeV, μd = 81 MeV
- At x = 10⁻³ and Q² = 15 GeV²: gas model alone gives F2p ~ 0.02, QCD-evolved gas model gives F2p ~ 0.23, full model with both finite-size corrections and QCD gives F2p ~ 1.1
- Power-law fit to F2p(x)/x data at Q² = 15 GeV² gives c = 0.229 ± 0.005 and m = 1.22 ± 0.01 for 0.0004 ≤ x ≤ 0.2
- Proton charge rms radius ρ = 0.862 fm, yielding R = √(5/3) ρ = 1.11 fm

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach to nucleon structure functions previously attempted by Mac and Ugaz (1989), Cleymans et al. (1994), and Bourrely et al. (1994), which had limited success at small x. The key innovation is the phenomenological finite-size correction factor that produces the observed small-x rise, which earlier statistical models could not reproduce. The model enables a simple physical interpretation of the Gottfried sum violation and provides predictions for the W charge asymmetry in pp collisions and the ratio d(x)/u(x) that can be tested against data from the NA51 and CDF collaborations.
