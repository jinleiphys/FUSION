# Electric and Magnetic Moments and Transition Probabilities in $^{208}$Pb $\pm 1$ Nuclei

> We present moments and transition probabilities in the neighboring odd-mass nuclei of $^{208}$Pb calculated fully self-consistently from the s.p. properties of $^{208}$Pb with polarization corrections from its excitations, both given from previous Skyrme-Hartree-Fock and RPA calculations. The electric results agree nicely with the data with two very interesting exceptions. In the magnetic case we obtain similar results. We discuss also polarization contributions to the $l$-forbidden $M1$ transitions, which are, however, much too small compared to the data. With a modified external field operator which accounts effectively for mesonic and many-body effects the description of the data can be substantially improved.

## Key claim

Electric moments and transition probabilities in odd-mass neighbors of 208Pb are reproduced well by self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus RPA calculations, except for two specific cases where resonance effects between single-particle energies and core excitations cause significant discrepancies; magnetic results are similar but require a modified external field operator with an adjustable parameter (xi_t = 1.5) to account for mesonic and many-body effects, particularly for l-forbidden M1 transitions where the bare polarization contribution is much too small.

## Method

The authors calculate single-particle moments and transition probabilities for odd-mass nuclei (207Tl, 207Pb, 209Pb, 209Bi) using the mean-field of the 208Pb core with polarization corrections computed from the RPA response of the core to the extra nucleon or hole. Three Skyrme energy density functionals (SLy4, SV-bas, SV-bas_m) are employed fully self-consistently, with the effective operator including a tensor term [Y2 x sigma_1]_1 parameterized by xi_t to simulate Landau renormalization and virtual meson exchange effects. The single-particle basis is computed on a spherical coordinate-space grid with box radius 18 fm and maximum single-particle energy 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent Skyrme-DFT approach to single-particle moments and transition strengths in odd nuclei near 208Pb, a topic previously addressed mainly with non-self-consistent methods such as the Landau-Migdal approach (Speth 1977, Ring 1973a, Bauer 1973). The calculations use the same strategy as empirical models but with fully self-consistent mean-field and RPA from Skyrme functionals, enabling extrapolation away from stability. The results enable improved description of magnetic properties through the inclusion of mesonic and many-body effects via the tensor term, and highlight the need for better treatment of resonance effects in E3 and E5 transitions where the theoretical excitation energies of core states can accidentally match single-particle spacings.
