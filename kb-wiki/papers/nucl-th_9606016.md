# Quasifree pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the $Δ$ region

> Photo production of pions on the deuteron is studied in the spectator nucleon model. The Born terms of the elementary production amplitude are determined in pseudovector $π$N coupling and supplied with a form factor. The $Δ$ resonance is considered both in the $s$ and the $u$ channel. The parameters of the $Δ$ resonance and the cutoff of the form factors are fixed on the leading photoproduction multipoles. Results for total and differential cross sections are compared with experimental data. Particular attention is paid to the role of Pauli correlations of the final state nucleons in the quasifree case. The results are compared with those for pion photoproduction on the nucleon.

# Quasifree pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the Δ region

## Key claim

The spectator nucleon model, using a unified elementary production operator with the same Δ parametrization for neutral and charged pion photoproduction, reproduces total and differential cross sections for π⁻ production on the deuteron reasonably well up to about 400 MeV photon energy, but systematically overestimates the data in the resonance peak; reducing the πN coupling constant from f²/4π = 0.0735 to 0.069 improves agreement at the peak but leads to significant underestimation below 280 MeV.

## Method

The elementary photoproduction amplitude was constructed from Born terms in pseudovector πN coupling with a monopole form factor (cutoff Λ = 800 MeV) plus Δ(1232) resonance contributions in both s and u channels, with parameters fixed to the leading multipoles (E₀₊^(1/2), E₀₊^(3/2), M₁₊^(3/2)). This amplitude was then embedded into the two-nucleon space of the deuteron using the spectator nucleon model with plane waves for the final NN state and the Bonn potential deuteron wave function, including full antisymmetrization of the final two-nucleon state.

## Key numbers

- Δ resonance parameters: M_Δ = 1232 MeV, f²_ΔNπ/4π = 0.31, cutoff Λ_Δ = 315 MeV
- πN coupling constant for nonresonant background: f²_πN/4π = 0.0735 (reduced to 0.069 in alternative calculation)
- Nonresonant background form factor cutoff: Λ = 800 MeV
- The G^(M1)_ΔN coupling was increased by a factor of 1.15 relative to Wilhelm and Arenhövel (1995) to fit the M₁₊^(3/2) multipole
- Good agreement with experimental multipoles up to photon energy of about 400 MeV
- For quasifree kinematics, Pauli correlations reduce the differential cross section by up to about 10% for small pion emission angles
- For backward pion emission, the simple spectator relation (Equation g19) is verified within about 1%

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic approach of Laget (1977, 1981) and Blomqvist and Laget (1977) by using a unified Δ parametrization for both neutral and charged pion channels, whereas those earlier works used different parametrizations. The study also improves upon the description of the leading multipoles by including a form factor in the nonresonant background, which was essential for reproducing the real part of M₁₊^(3/2). The results serve as a baseline for future investigations that will include πNN final state dynamics and pion rescattering, which are neglected here. The finding that Pauli correlations modify quasifree cross sections by up to 10% is important for the extraction of neutron data from deuteron experiments.
