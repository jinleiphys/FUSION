# From bound states to the continuum

> This white paper reports on the discussions of the 2018 Facility for Rare Isotope Beams Theory Alliance (FRIB-TA) topical program "From bound states to the continuum: Connecting bound state calculations with scattering and reaction theory". One of the biggest and most important frontiers in nuclear theory today is to construct better and stronger bridges between bound state calculations and calculations in the continuum, especially scattering and reaction theory, as well as teasing out the influence of the continuum on states near threshold. This is particularly challenging as many-body structure calculations typically use a bound state basis, while reaction calculations more commonly utilize few-body continuum approaches. The many-body bound state and few-body continuum methods use different language and emphasize different properties. To build better foundations for these bridges, we present an overview of several bound state and continuum methods and, where possible, point to current and possible future connections.

## Key claim
This white paper argues that constructing better bridges between many-body bound-state calculations and continuum/scattering methods is a critical frontier in nuclear theory, but acknowledges that the many-body bound-state and few-body continuum communities use different languages and emphasize different properties, making integration challenging.

## Method
The paper reports on discussions from the 2018 Facility for Rare Isotope Beams Theory Alliance (FRIB-TA) topical program, reviewing several bound-state and continuum methods and identifying current and possible future connections between them. It covers few-body methods that emphasize continuum degrees of freedom, many-body methods built from bound single-particle states, and approaches to connect bound-state methods with continuum degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- Only about 50-60% of the Gamow-Teller sum-rule strength is observed when including strengths up to excitation energies that include the giant-resonance region.
- Charge-exchange reactions at intermediate energies (E greater than or equal to 100 MeV/u) are important for studies of the isovector response of nuclei.
- The paper states that theory with controlled approximations is needed, with uncertainties at least about 10% for analyzing and interpreting data.

## Context
This white paper extends discussions from the 2018 FRIB-TA topical program and reviews the accomplishments and limitations of various methods for connecting bound-state calculations with scattering and reaction theory. It builds on prior work including the Ikeda conjecture (Ikeda et al.) that alpha-cluster states appear near alpha-particle decay thresholds, and references the dispersive optical model work of Mahzoon (2017) for extracting neutron skin thickness. The paper enables future development of tools to calculate reaction observables for rare isotope beam facilities such as FRIB, GSI, TRIUMF, RIKEN, GANIL, CERN, and RAON.
