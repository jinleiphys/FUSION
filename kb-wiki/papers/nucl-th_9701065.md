# Origin of subthreshold K^+ production in heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the origin of subthreshold $K^+$ production in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. In particular we study the influence of the pion induced $K^+$ creation processes. We find that this channel shows a strong dependence on the size of the system, i.e., the number of participating nucleons as well as on the incident energy of the reaction. In an energy region between 1--2 GeV/nucleon the pion induced processes essentially contribute to the total yield and can even become dominant in reactions with a large number of participating nucleons. Thus we are able to reproduce recent measurements of the KaoS Collaboration for 1 GeV/nucleon Au on Au reactions adopting a realistic momentum dependent nuclear mean field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pion-induced kaon production processes (πB → YK⁺) are not negligible but instead become dominant in heavy systems with more than about 150 participating nucleons, resolving an apparent contradiction where previous calculations required unrealistically weak nuclear repulsion to match kaon data.

## Method

The authors performed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) simulations of central heavy-ion collisions for Ne+Ne, Ca+Ca, Nb+Nb, and Au+Au systems at 1 GeV/nucleon, using a soft Skyrme force (K=200 MeV) with momentum-dependent interactions (SMD). Kaons were treated perturbatively, and the elementary cross sections for pion-induced channels were taken from the resonance model of Tsushima et al., which includes N* and Δ intermediate states and distinguishes isospin channels explicitly.

## Key numbers

- Total kaon yield scales as M_K ∼ A_part^τ with τ = 1.61 for the total yield, τ^BB = 1.40 for baryon-induced processes, and τ^πB = 1.69 for pion-induced processes (compared to experimental τ^exp = 1.75 ± 0.15)
- Pion-induced channels become dominant for participant numbers greater than A_part ∼ 150
- In central Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon, the total kaon multiplicity (×1000) is 41.8 with the SMD force, of which 18.6 comes from baryon-induced processes
- The ratio R = M_K^πB / M_K^BB peaks around E_lab ∼ 1.2 GeV/nucleon, reaching nearly 50% for Ca+Ca and 55-60% for Au+Au in the 1-2 GeV/nucleon range
- Threshold differences: πΔ channel requires ΔE_thres ≈ 237 MeV, NΔ channel requires ΔE_thres ≈ 377 MeV, πN channel requires ΔE_thres ≈ 530 MeV, NN channel requires ΔE_thres ≈ 670 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Hartnack (1994) and Huang (1993) that considered only baryon-induced kaon production and required a weakly repulsive nuclear mean field to match experimental data. The paper contradicts the claim by Xiong (1990) that pion-induced channels contribute less than 25% to the total kaon yield, showing that this conclusion was system-dependent (based only on Ca+Ca at 0.8 GeV/nucleon). The results enable future measurements of K⁺/π⁺ correlations in the 1-2 GeV/nucleon energy range with maximal participant numbers to further probe the nuclear equation of state.
