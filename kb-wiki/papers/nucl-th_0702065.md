# Evolution of nuclear shells with the Skyrme density dependent interaction

> We present the evolution of the shell structure of nuclei in Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme's density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The role of the tensor part of the Skyrme interaction to the Hartree-Fock spin-orbit splitting in spherical spin unsaturated nuclei is reanalyzed. The contribution of a finite range tensor force to the spin-orbit splitting in closed shell nuclei is calculated. It is found that the exact matrix elements of a Gaussian and of a one-pion exchange tensor potential could be written as a product Skyrme's short range expression times a suppression factor which is almost constant for closed shell nuclei with mass number $A\geq 48$. The suppression factor is $\sim 0.15$ for the one-pion exchange potential.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the contribution of a finite-range tensor force (such as the one-pion exchange potential) to the spin-orbit splitting in closed-shell nuclei can be accurately represented by the short-range Skyrme tensor parametrization multiplied by a nearly constant suppression factor, provided one considers states with the maximum orbital angular momentum in each shell.

## Method

The authors derived the contribution of a tensor force to the Skyrme energy density functional using the density matrix expansion method of Negele and Vautherin. They then calculated the exact matrix elements of Gaussian and Yukawa (one-pion exchange) tensor potentials for spherical closed-shell nuclei and compared them to the short-range approximation, extracting a suppression factor as a function of mass number. Finally, they performed Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme SIII interaction plus a tensor term, fitting the tensor parameters to reproduce single-particle energy differences in Sn isotopes, N=82 isotones, and Ca isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The suppression factor for the one-pion exchange tensor potential is approximately 0.147 for nuclei with mass number A ≥ 48, and about 0.16 for nuclei near A = 28.
- For a Gaussian tensor potential with range a = 1.2 fm, the total suppression factor S^G ranges from 0.459 (A=28) to 0.452 (A=208).
- The tensor parameters used in the Hartree-Fock calculations are α = -118.75 MeV fm^5 and β = 120 MeV fm^5, corresponding to α_T = -180 MeV fm^5 and β_T = 120 MeV fm^5.
- The central exchange part of the Skyrme SIII interaction gives α_c = 61.25 MeV fm^5 and β_c = 0.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Brink and Stancu (1977) on the tensor contribution to spin-orbit splitting in magic nuclei, now applying the analysis to exotic nuclei far from stability. The paper directly addresses the concern raised by Otsuka et al. (2005) that the Skyrme energy density functional might not be justified for finite-range tensor forces, demonstrating that the short-range Skyrme parametrization remains valid with a constant suppression factor. The results enable systematic Hartree-Fock studies of shell evolution in neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei using the Skyrme interaction with tensor terms, as demonstrated for Sn isotopes, N=82 isotones, and Ca isotopes.
