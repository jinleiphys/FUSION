# Strangeness-driven phase transition in (proto)- neutron star matter

> The phase diagram of a system constituted of neutrons, protons, $Λ$-hyperons and electrons is evaluated in the mean-field approximation in the complete three-dimensional space given by the baryon, lepton and strange charge. It is shown that the phase diagram at sub-saturation densities is strongly affected by the electromagnetic interaction, while it is almost independent of the electric charge at supra-saturation density. As a consequence, stellar matter under the condition of strangeness equilibrium is expected to experience a first as well as a second-order strangeness-driven phase transition at high density, while the liquid-gas phase transition is expected to be quenched. An RPA calculation indicates that the presence of this critical point might have sizable implications for the neutrino propagation in core-collapse supernovae.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strangeness-driven phase transition (first- and second-order) can occur in (proto-)neutron star matter at supra-saturation densities, and that this transition survives the screening effect of electrons and persists over a large temperature domain; however, the existence of this transition depends on the assumed strengths of nucleon-Λ and Λ-Λ interactions.

## Method

The authors model a four-component system of neutrons, protons, Λ-hyperons, and electrons using a non-relativistic mean-field approximation with the Balberg-Gal potential energy density functional (parameter set BG I). They evaluate the phase diagram in the three-dimensional space of baryon, strange, and lepton charge densities, using a hybrid ensemble with a one-dimensional Maxwell construction to identify phase-coexistence domains. The neutrino mean free path near the critical point is calculated in the linear response (RPA) approximation using the dynamical response function in the vector channel.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass for a n,p,Λ+e system with the BG I parameter set is 2.04 M⊙, in agreement with the mass of PSR J 1614-2230 (almost two solar masses).
- The strangeness-driven phase transition domain lies at supra-saturation densities nB ≳ 2 n0, where n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density.
- The critical temperature for the strangeness-driven phase transition at μS=0 is shown as a function of baryon density and electron fraction Ye, with values typically reached within the cooling proto-neutron star (specific critical temperature values are given in Fig. 2bis but not as explicit numbers in the text).
- The neutrino mean free path is calculated at T=20 MeV along a constant-Ye=0.2981 trajectory, showing a dramatic reduction near the critical point (the ratio of mean-field+RPA over mean-field mean free path is shown in Fig. 4b, with values approaching ~0.2 near the critical density).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exploratory two-component (n,Λ) model (Gulminelli et al., 2012) by including protons and electrons, and builds on studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in dilute nuclear matter (Ducoin, 2006; Barranco, 1990; Shlomo, 1990; Haensel, 1989; Rios, 2008). The possibility of a first-order hyperon phase transition in neutron stars was previously evoked by Schaffner-Bielich (2002) and Schaffner-Bielich (2000) using relativistic mean-field models, and by Wang (2004), Yang (2003), and Yang (2005) for effective RMF models. The present work enables future studies of the impact of this phase transition on core-collapse supernova simulations and proto-neutron star cooling, with a first application to core-collapse supernovae reported by Peres (2012).
