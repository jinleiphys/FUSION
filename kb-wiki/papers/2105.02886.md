# Impact of PREX-II and combined Radio/NICER/XMM-Newton's mass-radius measurement of PSRJ0740+6620 on the dense matter equation of state

> In this paper, we discuss the impact of the following laboratory experiments and astrophysical observation of neutron stars (NSs) on its equation of state (EoS): (a) The new measurement of neutron skin thickness of $\rm ^{208} \! Pb$, $R_{\rm skin}^{208} = 0.29 \pm 0.07$ fm by the PREX-II experiment. (b) The mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 has been slightly revised down by including additional $\sim 1.5$ years of pulsar timing data. As well as the radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 by joint NICER/XMM-Newton collaboration which has a similar size to PSR J0030+0451. We combine these information using Bayesian statistics along with the previous LIGO/Virgo and NICER observations of NS using a hybrid nuclear+piecewise polytrope (PP) EoS parameterization. Our findings are as follows: (a). Adding PREX-II result yields the value of empirical parameter $L = 69^{+21}_{-19}$ MeV, $R_{\rm skin}^{208} = 0.20_{-0.05}^{+0.05}$ fm, and radius of a $1.4 M_{\odot}$ ($R_{1.4}) = 12.75_{-0.54}^{+ 0.42}$ km at $1 σ$ confidence interval (CI). We find these inferred values are mostly dominated by the combined astrophysical observations as the measurement uncertainty in $R_{\rm skin}^{208}$ by PREX-II is much broader. Also, a better measurement of $R_{\rm skin}^{208}$ might have a little effect on the radius of low mass NSs, but for the high masses there will be almost no effect. (b) After adding The revised mass and radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620, we find the inferred radius of NSs is slightly pushed towards the larger values and the uncertainty on the radius of a $2.08 M_{\odot}$ NS is moderately improved.

## Key claim
Adding the PREX-II neutron skin measurement of lead-208 to existing astrophysical constraints (gravitational waves, NICER mass-radius) yields a symmetry energy slope parameter L = 69^{+21}_{-19} MeV, but this result is dominated by the astrophysical data because the PREX-II uncertainty is still much broader than the astrophysical constraints.

## Method
The author uses a hybrid nuclear+piecewise polytrope (PP) equation of state parameterization, connecting a nuclear empirical model near saturation density (with free parameters K0, e_sym, L, K_sym) to a three-segment polytrope at higher densities. Bayesian inference via nested sampling (Pymultinest) combines likelihoods from: (a) PREX-II neutron skin thickness (Gaussian with 0.29 ± 0.07 fm), (b) GW170817 and GW190425 tidal deformability, (c) mass-radius of PSR J0030+0451 from NICER, (d) revised mass and joint NICER/XMM-Newton radius of PSR J0740+6620 from two independent analyses (Riley 2021 and Miller 2021). The PREX-II likelihood uses a universal relation R_skin^208 [fm] = 0.101 + 0.00147 × L [MeV] from Vinas (2013).

## Key numbers
- Inferred symmetry energy slope L = 69^{+21}_{-19} MeV at 1σ CI after adding PREX-II to astrophysical data (prior was Gaussian with mean 58.7 MeV, σ = 28.1 MeV)
- Inferred neutron skin thickness of lead-208: R_skin^208 = 0.20^{+0.05}_{-0.05} fm (compared to PREX-II measured 0.29 ± 0.07 fm)
- Radius of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star: R_1.4 = 12.75^{+0.42}_{-0.54} km at 1σ CI after adding PREX-II and both PSR J0740+6620 analyses
- Radius of a 2.08 M⊙ neutron star: R_2.08 = 12.92^{+0.97}_{-1.11} km (90% CI) when using Miller+ data for PSR J0740+6620; 12.70^{+1.01}_{-1.25} km when using Riley+ data
- Maximum mass: M_max = 2.16^{+0.30}_{-0.19} M⊙ (90% CI) with Miller+ data; 2.17^{+0.24}_{-0.15} M⊙ with Riley+ data
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 M⊙: Λ_1.4 = 575^{+262}_{-232} (90% CI) with Miller+ data

## Context
This work extends the author's previous Bayesian analysis (Biswas 2020) combining gravitational wave and NICER observations, now incorporating the PREX-II result (Adhikari 2021) and the revised mass and joint NICER/XMM-Newton radius of PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021; Miller 2021). The results are compared with Reed (2021), who found a much larger L (106 ± 37 MeV) and tension with GW170817 using a correlation-based approach with fewer EoSs; the author argues their nested sampling method is more robust. The inferred R_1.4 is slightly higher (by ~0.3 km) than results from nonparametric Gaussian process analyses (Legred 2021; Miller 2021) due to additional heavy-ion collision constraints informing the nuclear empirical parameterization, but lower than the chiral-effective-theory-informed analysis of Pang (2021) which uses discrete EoS sampling.
