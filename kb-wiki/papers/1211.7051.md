# Non-perturbative description of self-interacting charged scalar field at finite temperature and in the presence of an external magnetic field

> It is studied the symmetry restoration in a theory of a self-interacting charged scalar field at finite temperature and in the presence of an external magnetic field. The effective potential is evaluated nonperturbatively through the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) nonperturbative method. In addition, we present an efficient numeric way to deal with the sum over the Landau levels in the case of working with weak magnetic fields

# Non-perturbative description of self-interacting charged scalar field at finite temperature and in the presence of an external magnetic field

## Key claim
The Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) method predicts a second-order phase transition for a self-interacting charged scalar field in an external magnetic field at finite temperature, in disagreement with results from other resummation methods such as those by Ayala (year not specified in text). The external magnetic field strengthens the symmetry-broken phase and delays the phase transition.

## Method
The authors apply the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) to a self-interacting quartic complex scalar field model with U(1) global symmetry and spontaneous symmetry breaking at tree level. The effective potential is evaluated nonperturbatively at O(delta^1) using an interpolation procedure with an arbitrary mass parameter eta, which is then fixed by the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS). To handle the sum over Landau levels in the weak magnetic field limit, the authors employ the Euler-Maclaurin (EM) formula, which provides an identity relating sums to integrals and their derivatives.

## Key numbers
- For parameters m/M = 20 and lambda = 0.00375, the temperature dependence of the vacuum expectation value (VEV) is shown for B = 0.
- For parameters m/M = 1, lambda = 0.1, T/M = 15, the critical magnetic field is eB_c/M^2 approximately 53.93.
- The magnetic field dependence of the VEV is computed for fixed T/M = 15 above T_c, with parameters m/M = 1 and lambda = 0.1.
- The phase diagram on the (B, T) plane is presented for parameters m/M = 1 and lambda = 0.1.

## Context
This work extends nonperturbative studies of phase transitions in thermal field theories under extreme conditions, specifically combining finite temperature and external magnetic fields. The results disagree with those obtained using a different resummation method by Ayala (year not specified in text), but the variation of critical temperature with magnetic field agrees with theoretical models by Andersen (year not specified in text), despite disagreement with lattice results. The Euler-Maclaurin approach for summing Landau levels, previously developed by the authors in an earlier paper (Duarte, Farias, and Ramos, year not specified in text), provides an efficient numerical method for weak magnetic fields where many Landau levels must be included.
