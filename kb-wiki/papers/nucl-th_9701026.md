# Polarized deuteron structure functions at small x

> We investigate shadowing corrections to the polarized deuteron structure functions g_1^d and b_1. In the kinematic domain of current fixed target experiments we observe that shadowing effects in g_1^d are approximately twice as large as for the unpolarized structure function F_2^d. Furthermore, we find that b_1 is surprisingly large at x < 0.1 and receives dominant contributions from coherent double scattering.

## Key claim

Shadowing corrections to the polarized deuteron structure function \(g_1^d\) are approximately twice as large as those for the unpolarized structure function \(F_2^d\), but in the kinematic domain of current fixed target experiments these corrections amount to less than 5% of the experimental error on the extracted neutron spin structure function \(g_1^n\).

## Method

The authors calculate shadowing corrections to the deuteron structure functions \(g_1^d\) and \(b_1\) at small Bjorken \(x < 0.1\) using the optical theorem, expressing the structure functions in terms of forward virtual Compton scattering helicity amplitudes on the deuteron. The coherent double scattering contribution is evaluated using a non-relativistic deuteron wave function and helicity-dependent diffractive photon-nucleon forward amplitudes, with the result expressed in terms of helicity-dependent longitudinal deuteron form factors. A "one-state" approximation is employed to relate the shadowing corrections to measured unpolarized shadowing data from the E665 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Shadowing correction ratio: \(\delta g_1 / g_1^N = \mathcal{R}_{g_1} \, \delta F_2 / F_2^N\) with \(\mathcal{R}_{g_1} = 2.7\) (Paris potential) and \(\mathcal{R}_{g_1} = 2.4\) (Bonn one-boson exchange potential) at \(x \lesssim 0.01\).
- Ratio for \(b_1\): \(b_1 / F_1^N = \mathcal{R}_{b_1} \, \delta F_2 / F_2^N\) with \(\mathcal{R}_{b_1} = -1.03\) (Paris potential) and \(\mathcal{R}_{b_1} = -0.58\) (Bonn one-boson exchange potential).
- The shadowing correction to \(g_1^d\) amounts to less than 5% of the experimental error on \(g_1^n\) for the SMC analysis, given \(|g_1^N| < 0.5\) in the kinematic domain of recent experiments.
- The deuteron size is \(\langle r^2 \rangle_d^{1/2} \approx 4\) fm, used as the threshold for coherent double scattering when the propagation length \(\lambda = 2\nu/(Q^2 + M_X^2)\) exceeds this value.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear shadowing, previously established in unpolarized lepton-nucleus scattering by experiments such as NMC at CERN and E665 at FNAL, to the polarized case. The results are directly relevant to the extraction of the neutron spin structure function \(g_1^n\) from deuteron data, as performed by the SMC collaboration, and to planned measurements of the deuteron structure function \(b_1\) by the HERMES collaboration. The finding that \(b_1\) receives dominant contributions from coherent double scattering involving the deuteron D-state confirms a similar conclusion reached independently by Nikolaev and Schäfer (1996).
