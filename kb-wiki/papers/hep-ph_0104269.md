# Analysis of the first RHIC results in the String Fusion Model

> First results from RHIC on charged multiplicities, evolution of multiplicities with centrality, particle ratios and transverse momentum distributions in central and minimum bias collisions, are analyzed in a string model which includes hard collisions, collectivity in the initial state considered as string fusion, and rescattering of the produced secondaries. Multiplicities and their evolution with centrality are successfully reproduced. Transverse momentum distributions in the model show a larger $p_T$-tail than experimental data, disagreement which grows with increasing centrality. Discrepancies with particle ratios appear and are examined comparing with previous features of the model at SPS.

## Key claim

The String Fusion Model (SFM) successfully reproduces charged multiplicities and their evolution with centrality at RHIC, but it produces a larger high-\(p_T\) tail than experimental data (a disagreement that grows with centrality) and overestimates antibaryon production, similar to problems already seen at SPS energies.

## Method

The model treats elementary inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions as parton collisions in the transverse plane; hard collisions are simulated via PYTHIA with GRV 94 LO parton densities and EKS98 nuclear modifications, while soft collisions produce strings in pairs. Strings are allowed to fuse if their parent partons are close in impact parameter, and the produced particles undergo a simplistic rescattering model. The model includes only fusion of two soft strings (hard strings are not fused), and the rescattering model has no proper space-time evolution.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of charged multiplicities at 200 GeV and 130 GeV per nucleon in central AuAu collisions: model gives 1.08, slightly smaller than the experimental value of 1.14 ± 0.05 measured by PHOBOS.
- At RHIC, hard elementary collisions amount to only 1 % of the total number of elementary inelastic collisions; at LHC this fraction is predicted to be 32 %.
- Model results for particle ratios at \(\eta \sim 0\) in central AuAu collisions at 130 GeV per nucleon (with string fusion and rescattering): \(\bar \Lambda/\Lambda = 0.87\), \(\bar \Xi^+/\Xi^- = 0.88\), \(K^+/K^- = 1.04\), \(\bar {\rm p}/\pi^- = 0.04\), \(K^-/\pi^- = 0.16\).
- The model gives a p–\(\bar{\rm p}\) yield at midpseudorapidity of \(\sim 2\) for 6 % centrality, lower than the preliminary BRAHMS value of 8–10.

## Context

This work extends the String Fusion Model, previously applied to SPS data (Armesto et al., 2000), to the first RHIC results from PHOBOS, STAR, PHENIX, and BRAHMS. The model’s success in reproducing multiplicities suggests that string fusion (or similar shadowing/saturation mechanisms) is important at RHIC, but the failure to describe the \(p_T\) spectrum points to the need for jet quenching (as proposed by Gyulassy and Wang, 1994) or other non-conventional physics. The authors note that the model’s simplistic rescattering prevents quantitative conclusions on particle ratios, and that for LHC energies fusion of more than two strings and of hard strings must be introduced.
