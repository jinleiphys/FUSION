# Beyond relativistic mean-field approach for nuclear octupole excitations

> We report the first beyond-mean-field study of low-lying parity-doublet states in 224Ra by extending the multireference relativistic energy density functional method to include dynamical correlations related to symmetry restoration and quadrupole-octupole shape fluctuation with a generator coordinate method combined with parity, particle-number, and angular-momentum projections. We clarify full microscopically that the origin of spin-dependent parity splitting in low-spin states is related to the octupole shape stabilization of positive-parity states, the dominated shapes of which drift gradually to that of negative-parity ones.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first beyond-mean-field study of low-lying parity-doublet states in 224Ra, demonstrating that the spin-dependent parity splitting in low-spin states originates from the octupole shape stabilization of positive-parity states, whose dominant shapes drift gradually toward those of negative-parity states. The authors clarify that 224Ra has dynamical octupole shapes in low-spin positive-parity states and acquires a stable octupole shape close to that of negative-parity states after rotation with spin up to approximately 8 hbar.

## Method

The authors extended the multireference relativistic energy density functional method to include dynamical correlations from symmetry restoration and quadrupole-octupole shape fluctuation. They used a generator coordinate method combined with parity, particle-number, and angular-momentum projections, superposing 39 reference states in the (beta2, beta3 >= 0) deformation plane from deformation-constrained selfconsistent mean-field calculations based on the PC-PK1 relativistic energy functional. The Dirac equation was solved in a three-dimensional isotropic harmonic-oscillator basis with fourteen major shells, and BCS pairing correlations were included with zero-range pairing forces.

## Key numbers

- The global energy minimum of 224Ra is located at deformations beta2 = 0.179, beta3 = 0.125, beta4 = 0.146, slightly larger than the values beta2 = 0.154, beta3 = 0.097, beta4 = 0.15 from Gaffney (2013).
- The average octupole deformation of negative-parity states is almost constant around 0.144 with increasing spin J, while for positive-parity states it increases smoothly from <beta3> = 0.101 (J = 0) to <beta3> = 0.141 (J = 10 hbar).
- The average quadrupole deformation <beta2> varies between 0.180 and 0.191 across spin states.
- The numbers of mesh points in rotation Euler angle and gauge angle were chosen as 16 and 9, respectively, for angular momentum and particle number projections.

## Context

This work extends previous beyond-mean-field methods for triaxial nuclei (Yao 2010) by including the octupole shape degree of freedom, providing the most advanced beyond-mean-field calculation for nuclear octupole excitations. It contradicts the picture of stable octupole deformation at low spin suggested by Gaffney (2013) and instead supports the rotation-induced phase transition picture proposed phenomenologically by Jolos (1994). The results enable future coupled-channel calculations for nuclear fusion and provide a starting point to examine dynamical correlation effects on nuclear Schiff moments relevant for measuring atomic electric-dipole moments.
