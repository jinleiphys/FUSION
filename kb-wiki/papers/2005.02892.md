# The global geometrical property of jet events in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We present the first theoretical study of medium modifications of the global geometrical pattern, i.e., transverse sphericity ($S_{\perp}$) distribution of jet events with parton energy loss in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In our investigation, POWHEG+PYTHIA is employed to make an accurate description of transverse sphericity in the p+p baseline, which combines the next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD calculations with the matched parton shower (PS). The Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model of the parton energy loss is implemented to simulate the in-medium evolution of jets. We calculate the event normalized transverse sphericity distribution in central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, and give its medium modifications. An enhancement of transverse sphericity distribution at small $S_{\perp}$ region but a suppression at large $S_{\perp}$ region are observed in A+A collisions as compared to their p+p references, which indicates that in overall the geometry of jet events in Pb+Pb becomes more pencil-like. We demonstrate that for events with 2 jets in the final-state of heavy-ion collisions, the jet quenching makes the geometry more sphere-like with medium-induced gluon radiation. However, for events with $\ge 3$~jets, parton energy loss in the QCD medium leads to the events more pencil-like due to jet number reduction, where less energetic jets may lose their energies and then fall off the jet selection kinematic cut. These two effects offset each other and in the end result in more jetty events in heavy-ion collisions relative to that in p+p.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first theoretical study showing that the overall global geometry of jet events in Pb+Pb collisions becomes more pencil-like (smaller transverse sphericity) compared to p+p collisions, even though for events with exactly two jets the geometry becomes more sphere-like. The net pencil-like shift arises because the jet number reduction effect (where less energetic jets fall below the kinematic threshold after energy loss) dominates over the sphere-like modification of two-jet events.

## Method

The authors generate p+p baseline events using POWHEG+PYTHIA, which combines next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD matrix elements with parton shower matching. The in-medium evolution of jets in Pb+Pb collisions is simulated using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic scattering and inelastic gluon radiation via the higher-twist formalism. The bulk medium evolution is described by the 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamic model with initial conditions from the AMPT model. Jets are reconstructed with the anti-kT algorithm (R=0.4 for Pb+Pb, R=0.6 for p+p comparisons) and events are required to have at least two jets with the leading jet pT > 110 GeV and all jets pT > 30 GeV within |η| < 2.5.

## Key numbers

- The cross-over point between enhancement and suppression of the normalized S⊥ distribution in Pb+Pb relative to p+p occurs at ln(S⊥) = -3.25, corresponding to S⊥ = 0.03788
- The fraction of two-jet events increases from 57% in p+p collisions to 70% in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- For two-jet events, the nuclear modification factor R^{n_jet=2}_{AA} ≈ 0.85 in the back-to-back dominated region
- For events with ≥3 jets, R^{n_jet≥3}_{AA} ≈ 0.87 in the multi-jet dominated region
- The jet number reduction factor for two-jet events K^{n_jet=2}_{AA}/K^{n_jet=2}_{pp} ≈ 1.228, while for ≥3 jet events (1-K^{n_jet=2}_{AA})/(1-K^{n_jet=2}_{pp}) ≈ 0.7
- In two-jet events, the azimuthal angle correlation shows suppression by ~10% in the back-to-back region (Δφ12 ~ π) and enhancement by ~300% in the Δφ12 ~ 2.14-2.9 region

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching from individual jet observables (such as inclusive jet R_AA, dijet momentum imbalance A_J, and tagged jet momentum imbalance z_J) to the global geometrical properties of entire events using transverse sphericity. The authors build on previous LBT model studies that successfully described light and heavy flavor hadron suppression, single inclusive jet suppression, and boson-jet correlations (He 2015, Cao 2016, Luo 2018, Zhang 2018). The jet number reduction effect was previously noted in studies of dijet momentum imbalance in Pb+Pb collisions (Apolinario 2012). This work enables future experimental measurements of event shape observables in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC to provide additional constraints on jet quenching models.
