# Nuclear de-excitations in low-energy charged-current $ν_e$ scattering on $^{40}$Ar

> Background: Large argon-based neutrino detectors, such as those planned for the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment, have the potential to provide unique sensitivity to low-energy (few to tens of MeV) electron neutrinos produced by core-collapse supernovae. Despite their importance for neutrino energy reconstruction, nuclear de-excitations following charged-current $ν_e$ absorption on $^{40}$Ar have never been studied in detail at supernova energies. Purpose: I develop a model of nuclear de-excitations that occur following the $^{40}\mathrm{Ar}(ν_e,e^{-})^{40}\mathrm{K}^*$ reaction. This model is applied to the calculation of exclusive cross sections. Methods: A simple expression for the inclusive differential cross section is derived under the allowed approximation. Nuclear de-excitations are described using a combination of measured $γ$-ray decay schemes and the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model. All calculations are carried out using a novel Monte Carlo event generator called MARLEY (Model of Argon Reaction Low Energy Yields). Results: Various total and differential cross sections are presented. Two de-excitation modes, one involving only $γ$-rays and the other including single neutron emission, are found to be dominant at few tens-of-MeV energies. Conclusions: Nuclear de-excitations have a strong impact on the achievable energy resolution for supernova $ν_e$ detection in liquid argon. Tagging events involving neutron emission, though difficult, could substantially improve energy reconstruction. Given a suitable calculation of the inclusive cross section, the MARLEY nuclear de-excitation model may readily be applied to other scattering processes.

## Key claim

The paper develops the first detailed model of nuclear de-excitations following charged-current νe absorption on ⁴⁰Ar at supernova energies (few to tens of MeV), but the model relies on the compound nucleus assumption and the allowed approximation, which work best at low momentum transfers and require further investigation to confirm their adequacy for low-energy neutrino-nucleus reactions.

## Method

The inclusive differential cross section for ⁴⁰Ar(νe,e⁻)⁴⁰K* is derived under the allowed approximation, which combines the long-wavelength (q→0) limit and the slow-nucleon limit. Nuclear de-excitations are modeled using a combination of measured γ-ray decay schemes from the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF) and the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model for unbound states. All calculations are implemented in a new Monte Carlo event generator called MARLEY (Model of Argon Reaction Low Energy Yields), and existing measurements from ⁴⁰Ti beta decay and ⁴⁰Ar(p,n)⁴⁰K scattering are re-evaluated using updated ⁴⁰K level data from 2017.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section under the allowed approximation is σ = (G_F² |V_ud|² / π) × CoulombFactor × (E_i E_f / s) × E_ℓ |p_ℓ| × [B(F) + B(GT)], where B(F) and B(GT) are the reduced Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements.
- For the isobaric analog state at 4383.7(7) keV in ⁴⁰K (0⁺), the combined matrix element B(F)+B(GT) is measured as 4.01(31) from Liu et al. (1998) and 3.84(17) from Bhattacharya et al. (1998).
- For the 1⁺ level at 2289.868(11) keV in ⁴⁰K, the combined matrix element B(F)+B(GT) is 0.83(8) from Liu et al. (1998) and 0.90(4) from Bhattacharya et al. (1998), while the weak GT matrix element from Bhattacharya et al. (2009) is 1.64(16) after scaling by g_A² = 1.26².
- The Coulomb correction factor is defined as the smaller of the Fermi function and the modified effective momentum approximation (MEMA), with the nuclear radius estimated as R ≈ 1.2 × A^(1/3) fm / (ħc).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical calculations of the ⁴⁰Ar(νe,e⁻)⁴⁰K* cross section, which were all fully inclusive and did not model de-excitation channels, by providing the first exclusive cross sections at tens-of-MeV energies. It builds on compound nucleus de-excitation modeling used for other nuclei by Kolbe (1992), Langanke (1996), and others, and re-evaluates matrix element measurements from Liu et al. (1998), Bhattacharya et al. (1998), and Bhattacharya et al. (2009) using updated ENSDF level data from Chen (2017). The MARLEY event generator enables practical calculations that have already informed DUNE sensitivity studies by Abi et al. (2020), and the de-excitation model can be applied to other scattering processes given a suitable inclusive cross section calculation.
