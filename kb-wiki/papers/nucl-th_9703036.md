# Parity violation in quasifree electron scattering off the deuteron

> For deuteron electroweak disintegration, parity violating effects are investigated which arise from the interference of $γ$ and $Z$ exchange as well as from the hadronic sector via a small parity violating component in the deuteron. The general formalism for the differential cross section and polarization observables of electromagnetic deuteron disintegration is extended to incorporate parity violating contributions. Formal expressions for the additional structure functions are derived. Results are presented for these parity violating structure functions for quasifree kinematics neglecting final state interaction and two-body effects. Both types of parity violating contributions to the asymmetry of the inclusive reaction with respect to longitudinally polarized electrons are evaluated. The one from parity violating deuteron components is negligible over the whole range of momentum transfers considered.

## Key claim

Parity-violating effects in deuteron electroweak disintegration arise from two sources: electroweak interference (γ and Z exchange) and a small parity-violating component in the deuteron wave function; the latter contribution to the asymmetry of the inclusive reaction with longitudinally polarized electrons is negligible over the whole range of momentum transfers considered, while the electroweak interference contribution dominates.

## Method

The general formalism for the differential cross section and polarization observables of electromagnetic deuteron disintegration was extended to incorporate parity-violating contributions from both γ-Z interference and hadronic parity violation. The parity-violating deuteron components were calculated using first-order perturbation theory with the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) one-boson-exchange model for the weak NN potential, using the Bonn OBEPQ model for the unperturbed deuteron wave function. The parity-violating structure functions and the longitudinal asymmetry were evaluated for quasifree kinematics, neglecting final-state interaction and two-body effects, using the one-body nucleon current in nonrelativistic reduction to order O(1/M).

## Key numbers

- The total P-state probability from parity-violating admixture in the deuteron is \(P_P = 2.7 \times 10^{-14}\), corresponding to an admixture amplitude of order \(10^{-6}\) as expected from McKellar (1969).
- The unperturbed deuteron binding energy used is \(E_B = -2.2246\) MeV.
- The weak mixing angle used is \(\sin^2 \theta_W = 0.232\).
- The quasifree ridge is characterized by \(E_{np}/\text{MeV} \approx 10\,\vec{q}^{\,2}/\text{fm}^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of parity violation in inelastic electron-deuteron scattering by Hwang et al. (1980, 1981) in the low energy and momentum transfer domain, and by Hadjimichael et al. (1992) for a larger kinematical range including quasifree kinematics at high momentum transfers. It also complements recent calculations by Poulis (1996) and Mosconi and Ricci (1997), which neglected parity-violating nuclear components. The results are relevant for experiments such as SAMPLE (Beise et al., 1996) that aim to measure the strange magnetic form factor \(G_M^{(s)}\) at low momentum transfer, by demonstrating that hadronic parity violation in the deuteron can be safely neglected in such measurements.
