# Investigating the temperature dependence of the specific shear viscosity of QCD matter with dilepton radiation

> This work reports on investigations of the effects on the evolution of viscous hydrodynamics and on the flow coefficients of thermal dileptons, originating from a temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity $η/s (T)$ at temperatures beyond 180 MeV formed at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). We show that the elliptic flow of thermal dileptons can resolve the magnitude of $η/s$ at the high temperatures, where partonic degrees of freedom become relevant, whereas discriminating between different specific functional forms will likely not be possible at RHIC using this observable.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of thermal dileptons can resolve the magnitude of the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) at temperatures above 180 MeV where partonic degrees of freedom become relevant, but discriminating between different specific functional forms (linear versus quadratic temperature dependence) will likely not be possible at RHIC using this observable.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, focusing on the 20-40% centrality class. They modeled \(\eta/s(T)\) with two linear and two quadratic parametrizations that rise above a threshold temperature of 180 MeV, while keeping \(\eta/s=1/(4\pi)\) below that threshold, and computed thermal dilepton emission rates including viscous corrections beyond the Israel-Stewart approximation by solving the Boltzmann equation in the constant cross-section approximation.

## Key numbers

- The threshold temperature for the rise of \(\eta/s(T)\) is set to \(T_{tr}=0.18\) GeV (180 MeV).
- For the linear parametrization, slope \(m=0.5516\) gives \(\eta/s=0.755\) at \(T=0.4\) GeV; slope \(m=0.2427\) gives \(\eta/s=0.3775\) at \(T=0.4\) GeV.
- For the quadratic parametrization, coefficient \(a=0.4513\) gives \(\eta/s=0.755\) at \(T=0.4\) GeV; coefficient \(a=0.1986\) gives \(\eta/s=0.3775\) at \(T=0.4\) GeV.
- The hydrodynamic evolution begins at \(\tau_0=0.4\) fm/\(c\) with initial shear stress \(\pi^{\mu\nu}(\tau_0)\equiv 0\).
- The freeze-out temperature is \(T_{FO}=145\) MeV.
- Two hundred MC Glauber events were generated for each of the four \(\eta/s(T)\) parametrizations, plus 200 events with constant \(\eta/s=1/(4\pi)\).
- The dilepton yield increases by 5% in the hadronic region and 10% in the QGP region due to \(\eta/s(T)\) relative to constant \(\eta/s=1/(4\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that used hadronic observables to probe the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) (Niemi 2011, Denicol 2015) by instead using thermal dileptons as a complementary electromagnetic probe. The authors note that charged hadron elliptic flow at RHIC is insensitive to \(\eta/s(T)\) in the QGP phase (Niemi 2011), and show that thermal dileptons can break this degeneracy. The study enables future experimental measurements of dilepton elliptic flow at RHIC to constrain the magnitude of \(\eta/s\) at high temperatures, though not its detailed functional form.
