# Manifestations of SU(3) symmetry in heavy deformed nuclei

> The rapid increase of computational power over the last several years has allowed detailed microscopic investigations of the structure of many nuclei in terms of Relativistic Mean Field theories as well as in the framework of the no-core Shell Model. In heavy deformed nuclei, in which microscopic calculations remain a challenge, algebraic models based on the SU(3) symmetry offer specific predictions, parameter-independent in several cases, directly comparable to experimental data. Two different approximate models for heavy deformed nuclei based on the SU(3) symmetry, the pseudo-SU(3) and the proxy-SU(3) schemes will be discussed and the compatibility between their predictions for the nuclear deformation parameters will be shown. In particular, the dominance of prolate over oblate shapes in the ground states of even-even nuclei and the prolate to oblate shape phase transition occurring in heavy rare earths will be considered.

## Key claim

The paper shows that within the pseudo-SU(3) scheme, using the highest weight irreducible representation (hw irrep) beyond the middle of a harmonic oscillator shell, rather than the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second-order Casimir operator, correctly predicts the prolate-to-oblate shape transition in heavy rare earths around neutron number N=116, whereas the Casimir-maximizing choice incorrectly places the transition at the neutron midshell.

## Method

The authors used the pseudo-SU(3) algebraic model to classify valence protons and neutrons in the rare-earth region (Z=50-82, N=82-126) into normal and abnormal parity orbitals based on Nilsson diagrams and deformations from the Gogny D1S interaction. For each nucleus, they determined the SU(3) irreducible representation (irrep) for normal-parity protons (U(10) symmetry) and neutrons (U(15) symmetry) using two selection criteria: the highest weight irrep and the irrep with the highest eigenvalue of the second-order Casimir operator C₂(λ,μ). They then computed the collective γ deformation parameter from the total irrep using a parameter-independent formula.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁵⁴Sm₉₂, the expected deformation from Gogny D1S is β=0.342, close to the experimental value of 0.340 reported in Pritychenko.
- For ¹⁸⁰Hf₁₀₈, the expected deformation from Gogny D1S is β=0.299, close to the experimental value of 0.273 reported in Pritychenko.
- The prolate-to-oblate transition occurs around N=116 for the W, Os, and Pt isotope series, with ¹⁹²Os₁₁₆ and ¹⁹⁰W₁₁₆ suggested as lying at the prolate-oblate border, and ¹⁹⁴Os₁₁₈ and ¹⁹⁸Os₁₂₂ having oblate character.
- In the hw irrep case, the γ values jump at N=114 and complete the jump at N=116, with only Hf, W, Os, and Pt series entering the oblate region (γ > 30°).
- In the highest C₂ eigenvalue case, all isotope series from Xe to Pt jump into the oblate region at the neutron midshell.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) scheme, originally developed by Draayer and collaborators, to the second half of harmonic oscillator shells, where previous applications were limited to the first half. The results are compatible with predictions from the proxy-SU(3) scheme (Bonatsos et al., 2017) and address the long-standing question of prolate-over-oblate dominance in even-even nuclei raised by Hamamoto. The findings enable parameter-free predictions of deformation parameters β and γ for heavy deformed nuclei, which can be compared to Gogny D1S interaction results and Relativistic Mean Field theory calculations.
