# Transverse Expansion and High $p_T$ Azimuthal Asymmetry at RHIC

> Rapid 3+1D transverse plus Bjorken collective expansion in $A+A$ collisions at ultra-relativistic energies is shown to reduce substantially the azimuthal asymmetry resulting from jet quenching. While the azimuthal asymmetry in non-central collisions, $v_2(p_T>2 \rm{GeV/c})\sim 0.15$ reported by STAR at RHIC, can be accounted for by spatially anisotropic jet energy loss through a 1+1D expanding gluon plasma with $dN^g/dy\sim 1000$, we show that if rapid transverse collective expansion of the plasma is assumed, then the asymmetry due to jet quenching may be reduced below the observed level. Possible implications of this effect are discussed.

## Key claim

If rapid transverse collective expansion of the quark-gluon plasma (with flow velocities up to v_T ~ 0.6) is assumed, the azimuthal asymmetry v_2 from jet quenching may be reduced below the observed level of ~0.10-0.15 at high p_T, even though the overall magnitude of jet energy loss is approximately independent of transverse expansion.

## Method

The authors computed the first-order GLV radiative energy loss for hard gluon jets propagating through an expanding elliptic plasma, using analytic line integrals over time-dependent density profiles that incorporate both longitudinal Bjorken expansion and transverse flow. They considered several transverse density profiles (sharp elliptic, Gaussian, exponential, and Vlasov free-streaming) and compared analytic results with full 3+1D hydrodynamic calculations for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, averaging over jet production points and impact parameter b=7 fm.

## Key numbers

- STAR reported v_2(p_T > 2 GeV/c) ~ 0.10-0.15 for non-central collisions
- Initial gluon rapidity density dN^g/dy(b=0 fm) ~ 1000 was estimated from earlier work
- For b=7 fm, dN^g/dy = 325 was used with alpha_s = 0.2
- Spatial anisotropy parameters: R_x = 2.5 fm, R_y = 4.7 fm for b=7 fm
- Transverse flow velocities from boosted thermal models: v_T ~ 0.5-0.6
- Velocity asymmetry parameter nu_a ~ 0.05-0.10 from hydrodynamic results
- Mean energy loss for 10 GeV gluon jet was approximately constant within +/-5% for both central and semi-central collisions
- The second harmonic delta_2(E) of energy loss was reduced by a factor of 2-4 for realistic flow velocities
- For Vlasov expansion, the equivalent escape factor stayed within +/-10% of R^eff for flow velocities up to v_T = 0.8

## Context

This work extends the earlier jet quenching analysis of Gyulassy, Vitev, and Wang (2001) and Wang (2001), which used a 1+1D Bjorken expansion approximation and found that anisotropic jet energy loss could explain the STAR v_2 data. The present paper shows that including 3+1D transverse expansion (as suggested by hydrodynamic calculations of Kolb, Heinz, Huovinen, Eskola, and Tuominen (2001) and boosted thermal model fits by Xu and Kaneta (2001) and Broniowski and Florkowski (2001)) significantly reduces the predicted azimuthal asymmetry. This opens the question of whether alternative mechanisms such as baryon junction dynamics (Vitev and Gyulassy, 2001) or dissipative hydrodynamics are needed to explain the observed v_2, and motivates flavor-dependent measurements of elliptic flow for pions and protons at RHIC.
