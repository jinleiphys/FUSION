# Comparative study for non-statistical fluctuation of net- proton,baryon, and charge multiplicities

> We calculate the real and non-statistical higher moment excitation functions ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 11.5 to 200 GeV) for the net-proton, net-baryon, and the net-charge number event distributions in the relativistic Au+Au collisions with the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE. It turned out that because of the statistical fluctuation dominance it is very hard to see signature of the CP singularity in the real higher moment excitation functions. It is found that the property of higher moment excitation functions are significantly dependent on the window size, and hence the CP signatures may show only in a definite window for a given conserved observable non-statistical higher moments. But for a given widow size, the CP singularity may show only in the non-statistical higher moment excitation functions of a definite conserved observable.

## Key claim

The paper argues that signatures of the QCD critical point (CP) are extremely difficult to see in real higher moment excitation functions due to dominance of statistical fluctuations, but may appear in non-statistical higher moments (cumulants) only for a specific conserved observable and only within a definite acceptance window size.

## Method

The authors used the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE to generate real event distributions of net-proton, net-baryon, and net-charge multiplicities in 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 11.5 to 200 GeV. Mixed events were constructed by randomly combining particles from different real events while preserving the event multiplicity distribution, and non-statistical moments were obtained as the difference between real moments and mixed moments. The real and non-statistical higher moment excitation functions (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis, and products Sσ and κσ²) were calculated for several transverse momentum and pseudorapidity windows.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: √s_NN = 11.5 to 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality: 0-5% most central collisions
- Acceptance windows studied: (0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c, |y| < 0.5); (0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c, |y| < 1); (0.2 < p_T < 1.6 GeV/c, |y| < 1)
- Net-proton kurtosis was always positive in all windows studied, unlike the negative values reported by Bleicher (2009) using UrQMD
- Net-baryon kurtosis decreased to a small negative value at low √s_NN, but not to the large negative value reported by Bleicher (2009)
- Net-charge kurtosis remained approximately zero across all energies, consistent with Bleicher (2009)
- The net-proton non-statistical skewness and Sσ showed a sizable negative dip within a small energy region that may serve as a CP signature, becoming more pronounced with increasing window size
- The net-baryon non-statistical kurtosis and κσ² showed a possible CP signature only in the smallest window (0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c, |y| < 0.5)
- No CP signature was found in any net-charge non-statistical excitation functions

## Context

This work extends the earlier UrQMD study by Bleicher (2009) which found no CP singularity in kurtosis excitation functions for net-proton, net-baryon, and net-charge, but reported different behaviors among the three observables. The paper also builds on the concept of non-statistical moments introduced by Zhou (2012) to overcome the dominance of statistical fluctuations. The results highlight the strong dependence of higher moment excitation functions on acceptance window size, consistent with the concerns raised by Koch (2012) about finite acceptance effects on cumulants of conserved observables. The findings suggest that future experimental searches for the QCD critical point at RHIC Beam Energy Scan must carefully select both the conserved observable and the acceptance window to isolate non-statistical fluctuations.
