# Critical point in the phase diagram of primordial quark-gluon matter from black hole physics

> Strongly interacting matter undergoes a crossover phase transition at high temperatures $T\sim 10^{12}$ K and zero net-baryon density. A fundamental question in the theory of strong interactions, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), is whether a hot and dense system of quarks and gluons displays critical phenomena when doped with more quarks than antiquarks, where net-baryon number fluctuations diverge. Recent lattice QCD work indicates that such a critical point can only occur in the baryon dense regime of the theory, which defies a description from first principles calculations. Here we use the holographic gauge/gravity correspondence to map the fluctuations of baryon charge in the dense quark-gluon liquid onto a numerically tractable gravitational problem involving the charge fluctuations of holographic black holes. This approach quantitatively reproduces ab initio results for the lowest order moments of the baryon fluctuations and makes predictions for the higher order baryon susceptibilities and also for the location of the critical point, which is found to be within the reach of heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

Using holographic black hole engineering, the authors predict a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram at a temperature of 89 MeV and baryon chemical potential of 724 MeV, but note that the temperature may change by at most 13% and the chemical potential by at most 5% due to uncertainties in the lattice QCD results used to fix the model parameters.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional holographic gravity model with a real scalar field to break conformal invariance and a U(1) gauge field to represent baryon charge. They numerically solve the equations of motion to generate over two million charged black hole solutions, each corresponding to a point in the temperature-chemical potential plane. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing lattice QCD results for the entropy density and second-order baryon susceptibility at zero baryon chemical potential, after which higher-order baryon number susceptibilities are computed via numerical differentiation using thin-plate spline interpolation and Savitzky-Golay filtering.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint located at T_CEP = 89 MeV and mu_B^CEP = 724 MeV
- T_CEP may change by at most 13% and mu_B^CEP by at most 5% due to systematic uncertainties
- The critical point lies along the line mu_B/T approximately 8.1 in the phase diagram
- The critical point could be probed using heavy ion experiments with center-of-mass energy in the range sqrt(s) = 2.5 to 4.1 GeV
- Higher-order susceptibilities chi_6(T) and chi_8(T) at mu_B=0 are computed and shown in Figure 1
- The Taylor series expansion in mu_B/T is applicable up to mu_B/T approximately 2.5 when including chi_8(T)

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach of DeWolfe (2010), which found a critical point at (143, 783) MeV using older lattice results with a transition temperature near 190 MeV, whereas the current work uses the more precise modern value of approximately 155 MeV. The results are consistent with lattice QCD constraints from Bazavov (2017) that exclude a critical point in certain regions of the phase diagram, and with finite-size scaling analysis from Fraga (2011). The predicted critical point lies within the reach of the HADES experiment, the planned Fixed Target program at RHIC, and the future Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR, but below the minimum collision energy of 7.7 GeV for the second phase of the RHIC beam energy scan in collider mode.
