# The effective force NL3 revisited

> Covariant density functional theory based on the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian with the parameter set NL3 has been used in the last ten years with great success. Now we propose a modification of this parameter set, which improves the description of the ground state properties of many nuclei and simultaneously provides an excellent description of excited states with collective character in spherical as well as in deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a modified parameter set, NL3*, for the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian that improves the description of ground state properties of many nuclei while simultaneously providing an excellent description of collective excited states in both spherical and deformed nuclei, though the authors note that the incompressibility and symmetry energy remain somewhat high compared to other parameter sets with density dependence in the isovector channel.

## Method

The authors performed a new global fit of ground state properties of twelve spherical nuclei and infinite nuclear matter, keeping the functional form of the Lagrangian unchanged from the original NL3 parameter set. The fit included binding energies, charge radii, and neutron skin data, with pairing correlations treated in the BCS approximation using empirical pairing gaps. The resulting parameterization was then tested in relativistic RPA/QRPA calculations for giant resonances and in cranked relativistic mean field calculations for rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.150 fm$^{-3}$ (NL3 gave 0.148 fm$^{-3}$)
- Nuclear matter binding energy at saturation: 16.31 MeV (NL3 gave 16.30 MeV)
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K: 258.28 MeV (NL3 gave 271.76 MeV)
- Symmetry energy J: 38.6 MeV (NL3 gave 37.4 MeV)
- Effective mass m*/m: 0.594 (NL3 gave 0.60)
- Calculated ISGMR energy in $^{208}$Pb: 13.9 MeV (experimental: 14.1 $\pm$ 0.3 MeV)
- Calculated IVGDR energy in $^{208}$Pb: 12.95 MeV (experimental: 13.3 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV)
- Calculated GDR centroid energy in $^{100}$Mo differs from experiment by less than 0.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the highly successful NL3 parameter set (Lalazissis et al., 1997) by incorporating modern experimental data on nuclear masses, charge radii, and neutron skins that have become available over the subsequent decade. The new NL3* parameterization addresses specific difficulties NL3 encountered in describing light Hg and Pb isotopes (Niksic et al., 2002) while maintaining the excellent description of giant resonances that made NL3 unique among non-linear RMF forces. The parameter set enables consistent studies of both ground state and collective excited state properties within covariant density functional theory, and the authors note that future improvements could come from including non-linear couplings in the rho-channel, as in the FSUGold parameter set (Piekarewicz, 2005), or from density dependent coupling constants as in the DD-ME1 and DD-ME2 parameter sets.
