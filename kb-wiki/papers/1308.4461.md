# Quark deconfinement in protoneutron star cores: effect of color superconductivity within the MIT bag model

> We analyze the effect of color superconductivity in the transition from hot hadron matter to quark matter in the presence of a gas of trapped electron neutrinos. To describe strongly interacting matter we adopt a two-phase picture in which the hadronic phase is described by means of a non-linear Walecka model and just deconfined matter through the MIT bag model including color superconductivity. We impose flavor conservation during the transition in such a way that just deconfined quark matter is transitorily out of equilibrium with respect to weak interactions. Our results show that color superconductivity facilitates the transition for temperatures below $T_c$. This effect may be strong if the superconducting gap is large enough. As in previous work we find that trapped neutrinos increase the critical density for deconfinement; however, if the just deconfined phase is color superconducting this effect is weaker than if deconfined matter is unpaired. We also explore the effect of different parametrizations of the hadronic equation of state (GM1 and NL3) and the effect of hyperons in the hadronic phase. We compare our results with those previously obtained employing the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in the description of just deconfined matter and show that they are in excellent agreement if the bag constant $B$ is properly chosen.

## Key claim

Color superconductivity facilitates the deconfinement transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in protoneutron star cores for temperatures below the critical temperature \(T_c\), but this effect is conditional on the superconducting gap being large enough; trapped neutrinos increase the critical density for deconfinement, but this effect is weaker when the just deconfined phase is color superconducting than when it is unpaired.

## Method

The authors adopt a two-phase picture: the hadronic phase is described by a non-linear Walecka model (with GM1 and NL3 parametrizations, with and without hyperons), and the just deconfined quark phase is described by the MIT bag model including color superconductivity with a 2SC pairing pattern. They impose flavor conservation during the transition, so that just deconfined quark matter is transiently out of equilibrium with respect to weak interactions, and apply the Gibbs criterion (equality of pressure, temperature, and Gibbs free energy per baryon) together with color neutrality conditions to determine the deconfinement density.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Lugones (2005) and Lugones and Carmo (2013) that used the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for just deconfined quark matter, showing that the MIT bag model with a properly chosen bag constant \(B\) yields results in excellent agreement with the NJL model. It also builds on earlier findings by Lugones (1998) that trapped neutrinos increase the critical density for deconfinement, but demonstrates that color superconductivity compensates this effect, resulting in a transition density that varies only slightly with neutrino chemical potential. The results enable more realistic modeling of the deconfinement transition in protoneutron stars, particularly during the deleptonization stage when neutrino chemical potentials evolve from \(\sim 150-200\) MeV to essentially zero.
