# Equation of state at finite net-baryon density using Taylor coefficients up to sixth order

> We employ the lattice QCD data on Taylor expansion coefficients up to sixth order to construct an equation of state at finite net-baryon density. When we take into account how hadron masses depend on lattice spacing and quark mass, the coefficients evaluated using the p4 action are equal to those of hadron resonance gas at low temperature. Thus the parametrised equation of state can be smoothly connected to the hadron resonance gas equation of state. We see that the equation of state using Taylor coefficients up to second order is realistic only at low densities, and that at densities corresponding to s/n_B > 40, the expansion converges by the sixth order term.

## Key claim

The Taylor expansion of the QCD equation of state in baryon chemical potential converges by the sixth order term only when the entropy per baryon satisfies \(s/n_B \gtrsim 40\); at lower densities the second-order expansion is sufficient, but at higher densities the fourth and sixth order terms become necessary.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD data for Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure up to sixth order, computed with the p4 action, and corrected for discretisation effects by shifting the p4 data by 30 MeV in temperature to match the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model at low temperature. They parametrised the second, fourth, and sixth order coefficients with inverse polynomials, matched to HRG values at switching temperatures of 160 MeV (second order) and 155 MeV (fourth and sixth order), and combined these with a parametrisation of the zero-density trace anomaly from continuum-extrapolated stout data.

## Key numbers

* The second-order Taylor expansion is realistic only for \(s/n_B \gtrsim 100\), corresponding to collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \gtrapprox 64\) GeV.
* The expansion converges by the sixth order term for densities with \(s/n_B \gtrsim 40\), which corresponds to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 17\) GeV at midrapidity.
* The p4 lattice data below 206 MeV were shifted by 30 MeV to account for discretisation effects; the data point at 209 MeV was shifted by 15 MeV.
* The switching temperature for matching second order coefficients to HRG is \(T_\mathrm{SW}=160\) MeV; for fourth and sixth order coefficients it is \(T_\mathrm{SW}=155\) MeV.
* The zero-density trace anomaly was matched to HRG at \(T_\mathrm{SW}=167\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state at zero baryon density by the Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2011) and the hotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2012), who published continuum-extrapolated second order Taylor coefficients. It also builds on previous studies by the authors (Huovinen et al., 2009, 2011, 2012; Petreczky et al., 2013) that showed discretisation errors in higher-order coefficients could be accounted for by a temperature shift. The resulting parametrised equation of state enables realistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, particularly for collisions with \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) down to 17 GeV.
