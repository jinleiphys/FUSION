# Particle configurations in the $NN\bar K$ system

> Three-body $AAB$ model for the $NN{\bar K}(s_{NN}=0)$ kaonic cluster is considered based on the configuration space Faddeev equations. Within a single-channel approach, the difference between masses of nucleons and kaons and the charge independence breaking of nucleon-nucleon interaction are taken into consideration. We definite the particle configurations in the system according to the particle masses and pair potentials. There are two sets of the particle configurations, $ ppK^-$, $np \bar{K^0}$ and $nn {\bar K}^0$, $npK^-$, charged and neutral. The three-body calculations are performed by applying $NN$ and $N\bar K$ phenomenological isospin-dependent potentials. The mass and energy spectra related to the particle configurations are presented. We evaluate the mass and energy uncertainties for the $NN\bar K$ model. An analogy to $NNN$ model for the $^3$H and $^3$He nuclei is proposed.

## Key claim
The four particle configurations of the $NN\bar K(s_{NN}=0)$ system ($ppK^-$, $np\bar K^0$, $npK^-$, $nn\bar K^0$) produce mass and energy splittings that are small enough to be considered a fine-structure effect, validating the isospin $NN\bar K$ model as an acceptable approximation despite the charge independence breaking of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and particle mass differences.

## Method
The authors solved configuration-space Faddeev equations for the three-body $AAB$ system using an $s$-wave single-channel approach with phenomenological isospin-dependent potentials. The $NN$ interaction was described by a modified Malfliet-Tjon MT I-III potential with scaling factors (0.9745 for $pp$, 1 for $np$, 0.982 for $nn$) to account for charge symmetry breaking, while the $N\bar K$ interaction used the Akaishi-Yamazaki effective potential with Gaussian form and different strengths for isospin singlet ($V_0^s=-595.0$ MeV) and triplet ($V_0^t=-175.0$ MeV) channels. The Coulomb interaction was ignored to isolate the effects of mass differences and potential variations.

- Two-body binding energy $E_2$ of the $N\bar K(I=0)$ subsystem: -30.259 MeV for $ppK^-$, -30.867 MeV for $np\bar K^0$, -30.285 MeV for $npK^-$, -30.893 MeV for $nn\bar K^0$ (Table 1).
- Three-body binding energy $E_3$ of the $NN\bar K$ system: -45.7 MeV for $ppK^-$, -46.7 MeV for $np\bar K^0$, -46.1 MeV for $npK^-$, -46.5 MeV for $nn\bar K^0$ (Table 1).
- Mass $M$ of each configuration: 2324.5 MeV ($ppK^-$), 2328.7 MeV ($np\bar K^0$), 2325.4 MeV ($npK^-$), 2330.2 MeV ($nn\bar K^0$) (Table 1).
- Maximum energy difference between a particle configuration and the averaged isospin model $E-E_{NN\bar K}$: about 0.8 MeV for $ppK^-$, corresponding to a relative energy error of about 1.7%.
- Mass differences $M-M_{NN\bar K}$ are less than 3.5 MeV, corresponding to a deviation less than 0.15% of the $NN\bar K$ mass.

## Context
This work extends the isospin formalism for the $NN\bar K$ system previously developed by the same group (Filikhin and Vlahovic, 2020) and addresses the ongoing debate about the binding energy of the "$ppK^-$" kaonic cluster, where experimental values from J-PARC E15 (2019, 2020) report -43 to -47 MeV, close to predictions by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2007). The particle-configuration approach is analogous to the $NNN$ model for $^3$H and $^3$He nuclei (Filikhin et al., 2019), and the results show that configuration splitting is comparable to the charge symmetry breaking effect in those nuclear systems. The work enables more precise calculations that could incorporate Coulomb corrections (estimated at 0.5 MeV by Oset et al., 2017) and provides a framework for distinguishing between charged and neutral kaonic cluster configurations in future experiments.
