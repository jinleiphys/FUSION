# First, second, third and fourth flow harmonics of deuterons and protons in Au+Au reactions at 1.23 A GeV

> We explore the directed, elliptic, triangular and quadrangular flow of deuterons in Au+Au reactions at a beam energy of 1.23 AGeV within the UrQMD approach. These investigations are of direct relevance for the HADES experiment at GSI that has recently presented first data on the flow of light clusters in Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV. To address the deuteron flow, UrQMD has been extended to include deuteron formation by coalescence. We find that this ansatz provides a very good description of the measured deuteron flow data, if a hard equation of state is used for the simulation. In addition we show that light cluster formation has a sizable impact on the proton flow and has to be taken into account to obtain reliable results in the forward/backward region. Based on the observed scaling of the flow, which is a natural result of coalescence, we conclude that deuteron production at GSI energies is a final state recombination effect. Finally, we also discuss the scaling relations of the higher order flow components up to $v_4$. We show that $v_3 \sim v_1v_2$ and $v_4 \sim v_2^2$ as function of transverse momentum and that the integrated $v_2^2 \sim v_4$ over the investigated energy range from $E_{lab}$=0.1 AGeV to 40 AGeV.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model with a hard equation of state and a coalescence mechanism for deuteron formation provides a very good description of the measured deuteron flow data from the HADES experiment, but only if a hard equation of state is used; a soft momentum-independent equation of state cannot achieve a good description.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model with a hadronic Skyrme potential using parameters for a hard equation of state (alpha = -124 MeV, beta = 71 MeV, gamma = 2.00). Deuteron formation was implemented via a coalescence approach applied to proton-neutron pairs after their individual kinetic freeze-out, with coalescence parameters of relative coordinate distance Delta r < 3.575 fm and relative momentum distance Delta p < 0.285 GeV. The simulations were performed for Au+Au collisions at a fixed-target beam energy of 1.23 AGeV with impact parameter b = 6-9 fm (20-30% centrality), and the HADES detector acceptance was implemented by using only hadrons with 18 degrees < theta_lab < 85 degrees.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 1.23 AGeV for Au+Au fixed-target collisions
- Impact parameter range: b = 6-9 fm, corresponding to 20-30% centrality
- Coalescence parameters: Delta r_max = 3.575 fm, Delta p_max = 0.285 GeV
- Hard EoS parameters: alpha = -124 MeV, beta = 71 MeV, gamma = 2.00
- The ratio v4/v2^2 = 1/2 for both protons and deuterons at midrapidity for transverse momenta up to pT = 1.5 GeV
- The integrated v4 = v2^2 for protons at midrapidity over the beam energy range from Elab = 0.1 AGeV to 40 AGeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton flow at HADES energies by Hillmann (2018) and provides the first transport model study of the first four flow harmonics of deuterons for Au+Au collisions at 1.23 AGeV. The results are directly relevant for the HADES experiment at GSI, which recently published first data on light cluster flow by Kardan (2018). The finding that v3 is correlated with the reaction plane at these low energies (in contrast to high energies where v3 arises from initial state fluctuations) was first predicted by Hillmann (2018) and is confirmed here. The observed constituent-number scaling supports deuteron formation by coalescence rather than direct thermal emission, and the v4/v2^2 = 1/2 result suggests ideal fluid expansion behavior similar to that discussed by Borghini (2005) for high-energy collisions.
