# Axial Transition Form Factors and Pion Decay of Baryon Resonances

> The pion decay constants of the lowest orbitally excited states of the nucleon and the $Δ(1232)$ along with the corresponding axial transition form factors are calculated with Poincaré covariant constituent-quark models with instant, point and front forms of relativistic kinematics. The model wave functions are chosen such that the calculated electromagnetic and axial form factors of the nucleon represent the empirical values in all three forms of kinematics, when calculated with single-constituent currents. The pion decay widths calculated with the three forms of kinematics are smaller than the empirical values. Front and instant form kinematics provide a similar description, with a slight preference for front form, while the point form values are significantly smaller in the case of the lowest positive parity resonances.

## Key claim

The pion decay widths of baryon resonances calculated with Poincare covariant constituent-quark models are systematically smaller than empirical values regardless of the form of relativistic kinematics used, with front and instant forms providing a similar description but point form yielding significantly smaller values for the lowest positive parity resonances.

## Method

The authors calculated axial transition form factors and pion decay widths for the lowest orbitally excited states of the nucleon and the Delta(1232) using Poincare covariant constituent-quark models with three forms of relativistic kinematics: instant, point, and front forms. The model wave functions were parameterized to reproduce the empirical electromagnetic and axial form factors of the nucleon with single-constituent currents, and the axial current operators were generated dynamically from single-quark current matrices using Wigner or Melosh rotations appropriate to each kinematic form.

## Key numbers

- Axial vector coupling constant of the nucleon G_A(0): 1.1 with instant and point form kinematics, 1.2 with front form kinematics (empirical value is 1.267)
- G_A^Delta(-m_pi^2) for Delta(1232) to N transition: 1.74 (instant), 1.70 (point), 1.79 (front); empirical value is 2.67
- Pion decay width of Delta(1232): 42 MeV (instant), 41 MeV (point), 45 MeV (front); empirical value is 100 MeV
- Pion decay width of N(1440): 10 MeV (instant), 4 MeV (point), 8 MeV (front); empirical range is 126-147 MeV
- Pion decay width of N(1535): 84 MeV (instant), 60 MeV (point), 147 MeV (front); empirical range is 59-93 MeV
- Pion decay width of Delta(1600): 12 MeV (instant), 0 MeV (point), 19 MeV (front); empirical range is 30-75 MeV
- G_A^N(1440)(-m_pi^2): 0.11 (instant), 0.07 (point), 0.10 (front); empirical range is 0.38-0.41
- G_A^N(1535)(-m_pi^2): 0.24 (instant), 0.20 (point), 0.31 (front); empirical range is 0.20-0.25
- G_A^Delta(1600)(-m_pi^2): 0.30 (instant), 0.06 (point), 0.38 (front); empirical range is 0.48-0.75

## Context

This work extends the investigation of Julia-Diaz, Riska, and Coester (2002) on elastic baryon form factors to axial transition form factors and pion decay widths of baryon resonances. The results contradict the static quark model prediction of 5/3 for G_A(0) and the semirelativistic quark model value of 2.12 for G_A^Delta(-m_pi^2) from Hemmert (1999). The failure of point form kinematics for positive parity resonances confirms findings reported by Melde (2003). The systematic underprediction of decay widths suggests that realistic descriptions require inclusion of quark-antiquark configurations beyond the three-quark Hilbert space, as previously argued by Lee (1999) and supported by the observation that experimental pionic decay ratios of Delta to N pi, Sigma* to Sigma pi, and Xi* to Xi pi follow 12:4:1 rather than the simple 3-quark expectation of 9:4:1.
