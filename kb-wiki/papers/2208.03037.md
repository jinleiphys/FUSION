# Electroweak nuclear radii constrain the isospin breaking correction to $V_{ud}$

> We lay out a novel formalism to connect the isospin-symmetry breaking correction to the rates of superallowed nuclear beta decays, $δ_\text{C}$, to the isospin-breaking sensitive combinations of electroweak nuclear radii that can be accessed experimentally. We individuate transitions in the superallowed decay chart where a measurement of the neutron skin of a stable daughter even at a moderate precision could already help discriminating between models used to compute $δ_\text{C}$. We review the existing experimental situation and make connection to the existing and future experimental programs.

# Electroweak nuclear radii constrain the isospin breaking correction to $V_{ud}$

## Key claim

The isospin-symmetry breaking correction $\delta_\text{C}$ to superallowed nuclear beta decay rates can be constrained experimentally through measurements of electroweak nuclear radii, specifically combinations of charge radii and weak radii across isotriplets, provided the isospin-breaking perturbation is predominantly isovector in character (with isotensor contributions neglected at the 10-15% level).

## Method

The authors develop a perturbative formalism connecting $\delta_\text{C}$ to two experimentally accessible combinations: $\Delta M_A^{(1)}$, which combines the charged weak radius of the daughter nucleus with its neutral weak radius (neutron skin), and $\Delta M_B^{(1)}$, which combines proton charge radii across the $T_z = -1, 0, +1$ members of the isotriplet. They evaluate these quantities using five different nuclear models (Woods-Saxon shell model, density functional theory, Hartree-Fock, random phase approximation, and a microscopic model) for seven superallowed transitions with $26 \leq A \leq 54$ where the daughter nucleus is stable. Under the assumption of isovector monopole dominance, they derive a direct proportionality between $\delta_\text{C}$ and $\Delta M_{A,B}^{(1)}$ that eliminates the dependence on unknown reduced matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- $\delta_\text{C}$ ranges from approximately 0.1% for $^{10}$C decay to approximately 1.5% for $^{74}$Rb decay across the superallowed decay chart
- For the $^{38m}\text{K} \rightarrow ^{38}\text{Ar}$ transition, model predictions for $\Delta M_A^{(1)}$ span an order of magnitude, from -1.3 fm$^2$ (microscopic model) to -14.6 fm$^2$ (DFT)
- The ratio $|\Delta M_A^{(1)}/(AR^2/4)|$ for $^{38m}\text{K} \rightarrow ^{38}\text{Ar}$ ranges from 1.0% (microscopic) to 11.2% (DFT), indicating the precision needed in neutron and proton radius measurements
- For $^{38m}\text{K} \rightarrow ^{38}\text{Ar}$, the experimental charge radii are $R_\text{Ch}(^{38}\text{Ar}) = 3.4028(19)$ fm, $R_\text{Ch}(^{38}\text{Ca}) = 3.467(1)$ fm, and $R_\text{Ch}(^{38m}\text{K}) = 3.437(4)$ fm
- The extracted $\Delta M_B^{(1)}(A=38) = -0.00(12)(52)(14)$ fm$^2$ from existing charge radius data
- The $Q$-value for $^{38m}\text{K} \rightarrow ^{38}\text{Ar}$ is $Q_\text{EC} \approx 6.04$ MeV, giving a recoil effect $Q_\text{EC}^2 R_\text{CW}^2/6 \approx 0.25\%$
- The PREX-2 experiment measured $R_\text{NW}$ of $^{208}$Pb to 1.4% precision; CREX measured $^{48}$Ca to 0.95% precision

## Context

This work extends the perturbative formalism of Miller and Schwenk (2008, 2009) and the isovector monopole dominance approach of Auerbach (2008) by connecting both to experimentally measurable electroweak radii. It directly addresses the model dependence of $\delta_\text{C}$ calculations that currently rely solely on nuclear models such as the Woods-Saxon shell model calculations of Hardy and Towner (2020), density functional theory of Satula (2016), Hartree-Fock of Ormand (1995), and RPA of Liang (2009). The proposed measurements would provide the first direct experimental constraints on $\delta_\text{C}$, which is critical for testing CKM unitarity given the apparent violation from recent improvements in single-nucleon radiative corrections by Seng (2018, 2020) and Czarnecki (2019). The work also connects experimental programs in rare isotope physics (BECOLA at FRIB), parity-violating electron scattering (PREX, CREX, P2), and neutrino experiments (T2K, DUNE).
