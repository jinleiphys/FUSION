# Quark mass function from a one-gluon-exchange-type interaction in Minkowski space

> We present first results for the quark mass function in Minkowski space in both the spacelike and timelike regions calculated from the same quark-antiquark interaction kernel used in the latest meson calculations using the Gross equation. This kernel consists of a Lorentz vector effective one-gluon-exchange-type interaction, a vector constant, and a mixed scalar-pseudoscalar covariant linear confining interaction that does not contribute to the mass function. We analyze the gauge dependence of our results, prove the gauge independence of the constituent quark mass and mass gap equation, and identify the Yennie gauge as the appropriate gauge to be used in CST calculations. We compare our results in the spacelike region to lattice QCD data and find good agreement.

## Key claim

The authors present first results for the quark mass function in Minkowski space from a kernel that includes a one-gluon-exchange-type interaction exactly, but only under the condition that the gluon propagator is modified by a fixed mass and a prescription that replaces $q^2$ with $-|q^2|$ to avoid overlapping pole singularities, and only for the case $\lambda=0$ where the linear confining part does not contribute to the self-energy.

## Method

The quark self-energy was calculated using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) formalism, where the Dyson equation is reduced to three-dimensional integrals by keeping only the residues of quark propagator poles. The interaction kernel consisted of a Lorentz vector effective one-gluon-exchange (OGE) interaction with a gluon mass $M_{\rm g}$, a vector constant term, and a mixed scalar-pseudoscalar linear confining interaction that was set to give zero contribution to the mass function. The self-energy integrals were evaluated in general linear covariant gauge, with the gauge parameter $\xi$, and the results were analyzed in both the spacelike ($p^2<0$) and timelike ($p^2>0$) regions.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark mass $m$ is determined by the gap equation $M(m^2)=m$, which is proven to be completely independent of the gauge parameter $\xi$.
- In the spacelike region, the mass function $M(p^2)$ from the complete kernel shows good agreement with lattice QCD data.
- The Yennie gauge is identified as the appropriate gauge for CST calculations, with the gauge parameter $\xi=3$ giving specific simplifications in the self-energy expressions.
- The gluon mass parameter $M_{\rm g}$ is introduced with the condition that for $0\le M_{\rm g}^2\le 4m^2$, the gluon propagator has no singularities at the on-shell point $p_0^2=m^2$.

## Context

This work extends the CST approach previously applied to meson spectra calculations (Leitao 2017, 2014) by including the quark self-energy from the same interaction kernel, moving toward a self-consistent quark model. It addresses problems with overlapping quark and gluon poles that were encountered in earlier CST self-energy work by Gross and Milana (1991, 1994) and Gross and Savkli (2001), and introduces a prescription (Prescription C) previously used in nucleon-nucleon scattering (Gross 2008) to avoid these singularities. The results enable future calculations of light meson properties, particularly the pion, by incorporating dynamical chiral symmetry breaking consistently with the meson spectrum.
