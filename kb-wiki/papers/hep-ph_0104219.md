# Markov-Yukawa Transversality On Covariant Null-Plane: Baryon Form Factor And Magnetic Moments

> The baryon-$qqq$ vertex function governed by the Markov-Yukawa Transversality Principle ($MYTP$), is formulated via the Covariant Null-Plane Ansatz ($CNPA$) as a 3-body generalization of the corresponding $q{\bar q}$ problem, and employed to calculate the proton e.m. form factor and baryon octet magnetic moments.The e.m. coupling scheme is specified by letting the e.m. field interact by turn with the `spectator' while the two interacting quarks fold back into the baryon. The $S_3$ symmetry of the matrix element is preserved in all d.o.f.'s together. The $CNPA$ formulation ensures, as in the $q{\bar q}$ case, that the loop integral is free from the Lorentz mismatch disease of covariant instantaneity ($CIA$), while the simple trick of `Lorentz completion'ensures a Lorentz invariant structure. The $k^{-4}$ scaling behaviour at large $k^2$ is reproduced. And with the infrared structure of the gluonic propagator attuned to spectroscopy, the charge radius of the proton comes out at $0.96 fm$. The magnetic moments of the baryon octet, also in good accord with data, are expressible as $(a+bλ)/(2+λ)$, where $a,b$ are purely geometrical numbers and $λ$ a dynamics-dependent quantity. ## Key claim

The Markov-Yukawa Transversality Principle (MYTP) formulated via the Covariant Null-Plane Ansatz (CNPA) is extended from the q-qbar meson problem to the qqq baryon system, yielding a baryon vertex function that reproduces the expected k^{-4} scaling behavior for the proton form factor and gives magnetic moments for the baryon octet in fair accord with experiment, provided the infrared structure of the gluonic propagator is attuned to spectroscopy.

## Method

The baryon-qqq vertex function under MYTP is derived via a 3D-4D interlinkage using Green's functions, generalizing the covariant instantaneity (CIA) formulation to the covariant null-plane ansatz (CNPA). The electromagnetic coupling is specified by letting the photon interact with the spectator quark while the two interacting quarks fold back into the baryon, preserving S_3 symmetry. The 3D wave function is taken as a gaussian solution of the fermionic counterpart of the 3D Bethe-Salpeter equation, with transverse and longitudinal scale parameters determined from baryon spectroscopy.

## Key numbers

* Proton charge radius: 0.96 fm (from expansion of form factor, giving <R^2> = 23.14 GeV^{-2})
* Proton magnetic moment: +2.710 baryon magnetons (experiment: +2.793)
* Neutron magnetic moment: -1.570 baryon magnetons (experiment: -1.913)
* Lambda magnetic moment: -0.741 baryon magnetons (experiment: -0.614)
* Sigma+ magnetic moment: +2.407 baryon magnetons (experiment: +2.33)
* Sigma- magnetic moment: -1.456 baryon magnetons (experiment: -0.89)
* Xi0 magnetic moment: -1.438 baryon magnetons (experiment: -1.236)
* Xi- magnetic moment: -0.876 baryon magnetons (experiment: +0.75)
* Lambda-Sigma transition moment: +1.278 baryon magnetons (experiment: 1.61)
* Transverse scale parameters beta_t^2 for baryon octet: N (0.068 GeV^2), Sigma (0.080 GeV^2), Lambda (0.076 GeV^2), Xi (0.079 GeV^2)
* Longitudinal scale parameters beta_l^2 for baryon octet: N (0.054 GeV^2), Sigma (0.062 GeV^2), Lambda (0.061 GeV^2), Xi (0.063 GeV^2)

## Context

This work extends the MYTP-CNPA formalism previously applied to the pion form factor by Mitra and Sodermark (2000) to the three-body baryon system, building on the CIA formulation of the baryon-qqq vertex by Mitra and collaborators (1990s). The approach reproduces the scaling law for baryon form factors and provides magnetic moments comparable to the Schwinger model of electromagnetic substitution (1960s) and vector meson dominance (VMD). The results serve as calibration of the MYTP principle and enable future studies of gluonic radius, higher-order electromagnetic effects on the proton radius, and strangeness effects in baryons.
