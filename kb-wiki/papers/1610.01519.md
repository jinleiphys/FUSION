# Critical behavior of non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes in a strongly coupled plasma

> We study the behavior of quasinormal modes in a top-down holographic dual corresponding to a strongly coupled $\mathcal{N} = 4$ super Yang-Mills plasma charged under a $U(1)$ subgroup of the global $SU(4)$ R-symmetry. In particular, we analyze the spectra of quasinormal modes in the external scalar and vector diffusion channels near the critical point and obtain the behavior of the characteristic equilibration times of the plasma as the system evolves towards the critical point of its phase diagram. Except close to the critical point, we observe that by increasing the chemical potential one generally increases the damping rate of the quasinormal modes, which leads to a reduction of the characteristic equilibration times in the dual strongly coupled plasma. However, as one approaches the critical point the typical equilibration time (as estimated from the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode frequency) increases, although remaining finite, while its derivative with respect to the chemical potential diverges with exponent -1/2. We also find a purely imaginary non-hydrodynamical mode in the vector diffusion channel at nonzero chemical potential which dictates the equilibration time in this channel near the critical point.

## Key claim

The characteristic equilibration times of the strongly coupled plasma, estimated from the lowest non-hydrodynamic quasinormal mode frequency, remain finite at the critical point but their derivative with respect to the chemical potential diverges with exponent -1/2, indicating a novel type of dynamical critical behavior not captured by the standard analysis of hydrodynamic modes.

## Method

The authors computed quasinormal mode spectra for external scalar and vector diffusion channels in the 1-R charge black hole model, a top-down holographic dual of N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma with finite U(1) R-charge chemical potential. They solved the linearized equations of motion for fluctuations on the black brane background using Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates and a pseudospectral method, obtaining the quasinormal eigenfrequencies as functions of the dimensionless ratio mu/T and momentum k/T. The equilibration times were extracted from the imaginary parts of the lowest non-hydrodynamic modes at zero wavenumber.

## Key numbers

- The critical point of the phase diagram occurs at mu/T = pi/sqrt(2), corresponding to Q/r_H = sqrt(2).
- The derivative of the equilibration time with respect to mu/T diverges at the critical point with exponent theta = 1/2, as determined from numerical fits to the scaling relation d(T*tau_eq)/d(mu/T) ~ (pi/sqrt(2) - mu/T)^(-theta).
- The dimensionless ratio mu/T ranges over the interval [0, pi/sqrt(2)] for the stable branch of solutions.
- The heat capacity C_mu and higher-order R-charge susceptibilities chi_n (n >= 2) diverge at the critical point.
- The static critical exponent delta = 2 for the R-charge density near the critical point.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasinormal modes in holographic models, such as the bottom-up Einstein-dilaton constructions of Alanen (2011), Janik (2015, 2016), and the top-down N=2* plasma study of Buchel (2015), which were limited to zero chemical potential. It also builds on the finite-density bottom-up analysis of Rougemont (2015) by using the analytically tractable 1RCBH model to precisely determine critical exponents. The finding that non-hydrodynamic modes display critical behavior with exponent 1/2 opens a new direction for investigating dynamical critical phenomena in strongly coupled plasmas, beyond the hydrodynamic classification scheme of Hohenberg and Halperin (1977), and could be further explored in more realistic QCD-like holographic models relevant to the beam energy scan program at RHIC.
