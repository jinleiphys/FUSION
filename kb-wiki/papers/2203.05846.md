# Signatures of gluon saturation from structure-function measurements

> We study experimentally observable signals for nonlinear QCD dynamics in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small Bjorken variable $x$ and moderate virtuality $Q^2$, by quantifying differences between the linear Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) evolution and nonlinear evolution with the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation. To remove the effect of the parametrization freedom in the initial conditions of both equations, we first match the predictions for the DIS structure functions $F_2$ and $F_{\rm L}$ from both frameworks in a region in $x,Q^2$ where both frameworks should provide an accurate description of the relevant physics. The differences in the dynamics are then quantified by the deviations when one moves away from this matching region. For free protons we find that the differences in $F_2$ remain at a few-percent level, while in $F_{\rm L}$ the deviations are larger, up to $10\,\%$ at the EIC and $40\,\%$ at the LHeC kinematics. With a heavy nucleus the differences are up to $10\,\%$ in $F_2$, and can reach $20\,\%$ and $60\,\%$ in $F_{\rm L}$ for the EIC and the LHeC, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the genuine differences between linear DGLAP and nonlinear BK evolution dynamics for DIS structure functions, by first forcing both frameworks to agree on a matching line in the $x$-$Q^2$ plane where both should be valid, then measuring deviations away from that line, rather than comparing predictions that include different parametrization freedoms or experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute proton and nuclear ($^{197}\mathrm{Au}$) structure functions $F_2$ and $F_{\mathrm{L}}$ using the BK equation with running-coupling corrections and an MV$^e$ initial condition fitted to HERA data. They then use Bayesian reweighting of the NNPDF3.1 (proton) and nNNPDF2.0 (nucleus) PDF replica sets to force the DGLAP-evolved structure functions to match the BK results on a line $10 Q_s^2(x) < Q^2 < 11 Q_s^2(x)$, where $Q_s^2(x)$ is the proton saturation scale. The relative differences between the two frameworks are then mapped across the $x$-$Q^2$ plane for EIC and LHeC kinematics.

## Key numbers

- For free protons, differences in $F_2$ remain at a few-percent level across most of the studied kinematic range.
- For free protons, differences in $F_{\mathrm{L}}$ reach up to $10\,\%$ at EIC kinematics and up to $40\,\%$ at LHeC kinematics.
- For a heavy nucleus ($^{197}\mathrm{Au}$), differences in $F_2$ are up to $10\,\%$.
- For a heavy nucleus ($^{197}\mathrm{Au}$), differences in $F_{\mathrm{L}}$ reach up to $20\,\%$ at EIC kinematics and up to $60\,\%$ at LHeC kinematics.
- The matching was performed for $x < 5.6\times10^{-3}$ for the proton and $x < 1\times10^{-2}$ for gold, above a lower limit $x > 1.03\times10^{-5}$.
- The effective number of replicas after reweighting was set to $N_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 10$, with $\delta_{\mathrm{BK}} = 4.5$ (proton $F_2$), $11.5$ (proton $F_{\mathrm{L}}$), $39.5$ (nuclear $F_2$), and $46$ (nuclear $F_{\mathrm{L}}$).

## Context

This work extends previous studies that attempted to distinguish DGLAP from saturation dynamics by including pseudodata from nonlinear models in DGLAP global fits (Rojo et al. 2009, LHeC Study Group 2012, Marquet et al. 2017). Unlike those approaches, which mix the effects of evolution dynamics with existing data constraints and parametrization choices, this paper isolates the pure dynamical difference by matching both frameworks on a common line before comparing predictions. The results enable experimental searches for gluon saturation at the EIC and LHeC by identifying the kinematic regions where deviations between linear and nonlinear evolution are largest, particularly in $F_{\mathrm{L}}$ for heavy nuclei.
