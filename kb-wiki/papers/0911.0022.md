# On the Dynamics of Unstable Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Since the quark-gluon plasma, which is unstable due to anisotropic momentum distribution, evolves fast in time, plasma's characteristics have to be studied as initial value problems. The chromodynamic fluctuations and the momentum broadening of a fast parton traversing the plasma are discussed here. The two quantities are shown to exponentially grow in time.

# On the Dynamics of Unstable Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The chromodynamic field fluctuations and the momentum broadening of a fast parton traversing an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma both grow exponentially in time due to unstable modes, but the explicit demonstration is performed only for a simplified two-stream system rather than for the full momentum distribution relevant to heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author linearizes the transport equations for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons together with the Yang-Mills equations around a stationary, homogeneous, colorless state, then solves the initial value problem via one-sided Fourier transformation. The correlation functions of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields are computed from the initial correlations of the distribution functions, which are identified with those of a free classical system. For the momentum broadening of a fast parton, the Wong equations are solved in a gauge where the potential vanishes along the parton trajectory, and the ensemble-averaged transverse momentum squared is expressed through the field correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ extracted from experimental data on relativistic heavy-ion collisions varies in the range $0.5-15\;\mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$ depending on the model.
- The instability growth rate $\gamma$ is of order $gT$, where $T$ is the characteristic parton momentum corresponding to the temperature of equilibrium plasma.
- For the two-stream system, the effective parton density in a single stream is $n$, the stream velocity is $\mathbf{u} \equiv \mathbf{q}/|\mathbf{q}|$, and the parameter $\mu^2 \equiv g^2 n / 2|\mathbf{q}|$ defines the instability condition $k^2 (\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{u})^2 < 2\mu^2 (k^2 - (\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{u})^2)$.
- The equilibrium formula for $\hat{q}$ holds for inverse wave vectors satisfying $|\mathbf{k}| \ll T$, where $T$ is the plasma temperature.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier review of plasma instabilities in anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (Mrowczynski, 2005) and is based on two recent publications (Mrowczynski, 2008; Majumder, 2009). It contrasts with previous calculations of $\hat{q}$ in anisotropic plasma by Romatschke (2006) and Baier (2008), which treated the plasma as a stationary medium and missed the exponential growth from unstable modes. Numerical simulations by Dumitru (2007) and Schenke (2008) had indicated that $\hat{q}$ receives a sizable contribution from unstable growing modes, which this analytic approach confirms. The work enables future study of transport coefficients and other plasma characteristics in the early-stage quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though quantitative predictions require analysis beyond the two-stream toy model.
