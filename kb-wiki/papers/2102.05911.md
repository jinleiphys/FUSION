# Pairing and the spin susceptibility of the polarized unitary Fermi gas in the normal phase

> We theoretically study the pairing behavior of the unitary Fermi gas in the normal phase. Our analysis is based on the static spin susceptibility, which characterizes the response to an external magnetic field. We obtain this quantity by means of the complex Langevin approach and compare our calculations to available literature data in the spin-balanced case. Furthermore, we present results for polarized systems, where we complement and expand our analysis at high temperature with high-order virial expansion results. The implications of our findings for the phase diagram of the spin-polarized unitary Fermi gas are discussed, in the context of the state of the art.

## Key claim
The unitary Fermi gas does not show an anomalously suppressed spin susceptibility for imbalances $\beta h \gtrsim 1.2$ in the sense of the attractive 2D Hubbard model; for $\beta h \lesssim 1.2$, a pseudogap region may exist but its precise limits cannot be conclusively studied with thermodynamic probes such as the spin susceptibility.

## Method
The authors used the complex Langevin approach on a $(3+1)$-dimensional spacetime lattice with $N_x = 11$ spatial sites and inverse temperature $\beta = 8.0$ to compute the static spin susceptibility of the unitary Fermi gas in the normal phase. They compared their results to high-order virial expansion calculations up to fifth order using Pade resummation, and to existing literature data from Luttinger-Ward, T-matrix, Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink, and auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo studies.

## Key numbers
- Spin susceptibility $\chi/\chi_{\mathrm{P}}$ for the balanced gas shows a very broad maximum centered at the spin-gap temperature $T_s > T_c$, with values $\chi/\chi_{\mathrm{P}} \gtrsim 0.6$ in the region around $T_s$
- For polarized systems with $|\beta h| \le 2.0$, the spin susceptibility shifts toward larger values with increasing asymmetry, but even at $\beta h = 2.0$ the interacting system still clearly differs from the noninteracting limit
- The critical temperature in the balanced limit is $(T/\mu)_c \approx 0.4$, consistent with experimental determinations by Nascimbene (2010) and Ku (2012)
- Statistical uncertainty of the complex Langevin results is on the order of 1-2 percent based on approximately 4000 decorrelated samples

## Context
This work extends previous non-perturbative ab initio investigations by Jensen (2020) and Richie-Halford (2020) that calculated the susceptibility for the unpolarized case, now presenting results for polarized systems at several temperatures and polarizations. The findings are compared to studies using the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink formalism by Pantel (2014), T-matrix approaches by Palestini (2012) and Tajima (2014), Luttinger-Ward formalism by Enss (2012), and auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo studies by Wlazlowski (2013) and Jensen (2019, 2020). The results enable improved characterization of the phase diagram of the spin-polarized unitary Fermi gas, particularly regarding the spin-gap temperature and the critical temperature as a function of polarization.
