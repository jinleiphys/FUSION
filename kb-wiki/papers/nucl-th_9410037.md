# Quadrupole Collective States in a Large Single-J Shell

> We discuss the ability of the generator coordinate method (GCM) to select collective states in microscopic calculations. The model studied is a single-$j$ shell with hamiltonian containing the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. Quadrupole collective excitations are constructed by means of the quadrupole single-particle operator. Lowest collective bands for $j$=31/2 and particle numbers $N$=4,6,8,10,12, and $14$ are found. For lower values of $j$, exact solutions are obtained and compared with the GCM results.

# Quadrupole Collective States in a Large Single-J Shell

## Key claim

The generator coordinate method (GCM) can successfully select collective quadrupole states from the complete shell-model spectrum in a single-j shell, provided that exact particle-number and angular-momentum projections are performed; however, the method's ability to reproduce exact results depends on the number of mesh points used for the intrinsic deformation coordinates, and for j=31/2 the exact solutions are inaccessible for most particle numbers.

## Method

The authors studied a single-j shell with even numbers of identical particles interacting via a pure quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian (with no pairing term, G=0 and chi=1 MeV). They constructed GCM generating states using the single-particle coherent excitation model (SCEM), where quadrupole excitations are built by acting with the quadrupole fermion operator on a monopole-pair condensate, and then performed exact particle-number projection via the Fomenko discretization method and exact angular-momentum projection using Euler angles as generator coordinates. The Hill-Wheeler equation was solved in the intrinsic frame on a mesh of 10 or 19 points in the beta-gamma deformation plane, with beta_max=6, and results were compared to exact diagonalizations for j=15/2 where the full space of 526 states is accessible.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 particles with pure quadrupole force, moments of inertia J = 3 hbar^2 / E(2+) range from 51 hbar^2/MeV for j=15/2 to 333 hbar^2/MeV for j=31/2.
- For N=4 particles, E(4+)/E(2+) ratios vary between 3.23 and 3.32 across j=15/2 to j=31/2.
- For N=8 particles in j=15/2 with half-filled shell, E(4+)/E(2+)=1.92.
- The maximum dimension of the many-fermion space for j=15/2 is 526 in the M representation and 35 in the J representation; for j=31/2 these dimensions are 8,908,546 and 200,691 respectively.
- The average overlap between neighboring mesh points was 0.73 for N=10 particles in j=31/2 and 0.80 for N=8 in j=15/2.
- The GCM calculations used beta_max=6, which allows description of spins up to I_max=18.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the single-j shell model with quadrupole-quadrupole force by Mulhall and Sips (1964), Baranger and Kumar (1965, 1968), Arima (1968), and Friedman and Kelson (1970), who found rotational structures and collective effects in such systems. The present paper applies the generator coordinate method as a filter to select collective states from the complete spectrum, building on the single-particle coherent excitation model (SCEM) of Dobaczewski and Rohozinski (1990, 1991). The results enable future application of GCM to more realistic nuclear structure calculations where exact diagonalization is impossible, and demonstrate that for j=31/2 the collective bands can be interpreted in terms of gamma vibrations coupled to an axial rotor with K as an approximately good quantum number.
