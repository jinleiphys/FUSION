# The size of the proton - closing in on the radius puzzle

> We analyze the recent electron-proton scattering data from Mainz using a dispersive framework that respects the constraints from analyticity and unitarity on the nucleon structure. We also perform a continued fraction analysis of these data. We find a small electric proton charge radius, r_E^p = 0.84_{-0.01}^{+0.01} fm, consistent with the recent determination from muonic hydrogen measurements and earlier dispersive analyses. We also extract the proton magnetic radius, r_M^p = 0.86_{-0.03}^{+0.02} fm, consistent with earlier determinations based on dispersion relations.

## Key claim
The proton electric charge radius extracted from the Mainz electron-proton scattering data using a dispersive framework that respects analyticity and unitarity is small (0.84 fm), consistent with the muonic hydrogen value, but this result depends critically on properly including the two-pion continuum contribution which had been overlooked in previous direct fits of the same data.

## Method
The authors re-analyzed the Mainz electron-proton scattering data using unsubtracted dispersion relations for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, parameterizing the spectral functions as a sum of continua (two-pion, K-anti-K, rho-pi) and effective vector meson poles. They fit simultaneously to the Mainz cross section data and world neutron form factor data, imposing superconvergence relations and constraining the light vector meson couplings. As a cross-check, they also performed a continued fraction analysis of the same data, studying the dependence of the extracted radius on the maximum momentum transfer included.

## Key numbers
- Proton electric charge radius: r_E^p = 0.84_{-0.01}^{+0.01} fm from the dispersive analysis
- Proton magnetic radius: r_M^p = 0.86_{-0.03}^{+0.02} fm from the dispersive analysis
- Neutron magnetic radius: r_M^n = 0.88 ± 0.05 fm from the dispersive analysis
- Neutron electric radius squared: (r_E^n)^2 = -0.127 fm^2
- Reduced chi-squared of the dispersive fit: chi^2_red = 2.2
- Continued fraction analysis for Q^2_max between 0.1 GeV^2 and 0.98 GeV^2 gave r_E^p = 0.85 ± 0.01 fm (for n=5, 8, and 12 terms)
- The two-pion continuum was varied by 5% below and 20% above the first minimum of the pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes; K-anti-K and rho-pi continua were varied by 20%

## Context
This work extends the dispersive analyses of the nucleon form factors pioneered by Hoehler (1974) and continued by Belushkin (2006), which consistently favored smaller proton radii. It directly contradicts the larger radius r_E^p = 0.876(8) fm obtained by Bernauer (2010, 2011) from the same Mainz data using polynomial and spline fits that did not incorporate analyticity constraints. The result resolves the "proton radius puzzle" by showing that the muonic hydrogen value from Pohl (2010) is consistent with electron scattering data when proper theoretical constraints are applied, and it enables future refinements using Roy-Steiner equations for the pion-nucleon phase shifts as being developed by Ditsche (2012).
