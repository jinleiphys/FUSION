# How to reveal the exotic nature of the P_c(4450)

> The LHCb Collaboration announced two pentaquark-like structures in the $J/ψp$ invariant mass distribution. We show that the current information on the narrow structure at 4.45 GeV is compatible with kinematical effects of the rescattering from $χ_{c1} p$ to $J/ψp$: First, it is located exactly at the $χ_{c1} p$ threshold. Second, the mass of the four-star well-established $Λ(1890)$ is such that a leading Landau singularity from a triangle diagram can coincidentally appear at the $χ_{c1} p$ threshold, and third, there is a narrow structure at the $χ_{c1}\,p$ threshold but not at the $χ_{c0} p$ and $χ_{c2} p$ thresholds. In order to check whether that structure corresponds to a real exotic resonance, one has to measure the process $Λ_b^0\to K^-χ_{c1} p$. If the $P_c(4450)$ structure exists in the $χ_{c1} p$ invariant mass distribution as well, then the structure cannot be just a kinematical effect but is a real resonance, otherwise, one cannot conclude the $P_c(4450)$ to be another exotic hadron. In addition, it is also worthwhile to measure the decay $Υ(1S)\to J/ψp \bar p$: a narrow structure at 4.45 GeV but not at the $χ_{c0} p$ and $χ_{c2} p$ thresholds would exclude the possibility of a pure kinematical effect.

## Key claim

The narrow structure at 4.45 GeV observed by LHCb, denoted \(P_c(4450)\), is compatible with kinematical effects from \(\chi_{c1}\,p\) rescattering into \(J/\psi\,p\) rather than necessarily being a genuine exotic pentaquark resonance; the claim is conditional on the absence of a corresponding narrow peak in the elastic \(\chi_{c1}\,p\) channel.

## Method

The authors analyze the two-point loop and triangle-diagram rescattering mechanisms for \(\Lambda_b^0 \to K^- J/\psi\,p\) decays, where \(\chi_{c1}\,p\) rescatters into \(J/\psi\,p\). They fit the two-point loop amplitude (with a Gaussian form factor) to the Argand plot of the \(P_c(4450)\) amplitude from LHCb, and evaluate the triangle singularity condition using the Landau equation for an intermediate \(\Lambda^*\) baryon exchanged in the \(t\)-channel. They also examine heavy quark spin symmetry relations to compare production rates of \(\chi_{c0}\), \(\chi_{c1}\), and \(\chi_{c2}\) in weak \(b\)-quark decays.

## Key numbers

* The \(P_c(4450)\) mass is \((4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5)\) MeV and its width is \((39 \pm 5 \pm 19)\) MeV (LHCb values).
* The \(\chi_{c1}\,p\) threshold is \((4448.93 \pm 0.07)\) MeV; the difference \(M_{P_c(4450)} - M_{\chi_{c1}} - M_p = (0.9 \pm 3.1)\) MeV.
* The best fit to the Argand plot using the two-point loop amplitude yields \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.}=1.75\) with parameters \(N=3144\), \(b=-2.9\times10^{-4}\) GeV\(^4\), and \(\Lambda=0.16\) GeV.
* The triangle singularity lies at the \(\chi_{c1}\,p\) threshold when the exchanged \(\Lambda^*\) mass is 1.89 GeV; the \(\Lambda(1890)\) baryon (four-star, \(3/2^+\)) has a width of 60–100 MeV.
* The branching fractions for \(B^+ \to J/\psi K^+\) and \(B^+ \to \chi_{c1} K^+\) are \((10.27 \pm 0.31)\times10^{-4}\) and \((4.79 \pm 0.23)\times10^{-4}\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of kinematical effects (threshold cusps and triangle singularities) previously applied to \(Z_c\) and \(Z_b\) states (e.g., Szczepaniak (2015), Guo (2014)) to the pentaquark candidate \(P_c(4450)\) observed by LHCb (Aaij 2015). It contradicts a naive interpretation of the peak as a genuine resonance and proposes that measuring \(\Lambda_b^0 \to K^- \chi_{c1} p\) can distinguish a real exotic state from a kinematical artifact. The analysis also suggests that \(\Upsilon(1S) \to J/\psi\,p\,\bar{p}\) decays could provide a cross-check.
