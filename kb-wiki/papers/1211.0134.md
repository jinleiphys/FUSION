# Nucleon momentum distributions, their spin-isospin dependence and short-range correlations

> The nucleon momentum distribution $n_A(k)$ for $A=$2, 3, 4, 16, and 40 nuclei is systematically analyzed in terms of wave functions resulting from advanced solutions of the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation, obtained within different many-body approaches. Particular attention is paid to the separation of the momentum distributions into the mean-field and short-range correlations (SRC) contributions. It is shown that at high values of the momentum $k$ the high-momentum components ($k\gtrsim 1.5-2$ fm$^{-1}$) of all nuclei considered are very similar, exhibiting the well-known scaling behavior with the mass number $A$, independently of the used many-body approach and the details of the bare $NN$ interaction. The number of $NN$ pairs in a given ($ST$) state, viz., ($ST$)=(10), (00), (01), and (11), and the contribution of these states to the nucleon momentum distributions are calculated. It is shown that, apart from the (00) state, which has very small effects, all other spin-isospin states contribute to the momentum distribution in a wide range of momenta. It is shown that that for all nuclei considered the momentum distributions in the states T=0 and T=1 exhibit at $k\gtrsim 1.5-2$ fm$^{-1}$ very similar behaviors, which represents strong evidence of the A-independent character of SRCs. The ratio $n_A(k)/n_D(k)$ is analyzed in detail stressing that in the SRC region it always increases with the momentum and the origin of such an increase is discussed and elucidated. The relationships between the one- and two-body momentum distributions, considered in a previous paper, are discussed and clarified, pointing out the relevant role played by the center-of-mass motion of a correlated pair in the (10) state. The relationship of the present approach with the many-body methods based upon low-momentum effective interactions is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The high-momentum components (k ≳ 1.5–2 fm⁻¹) of the nucleon momentum distributions for A = 2, 3, 4, 16, and 40 nuclei exhibit a universal scaling behavior with mass number A, independent of the many-body approach and the details of the bare NN interaction, though different approaches lead to some quantitative differences that do not hinder this general conclusion.

## Method

The authors solved the nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation with realistic bare NN interactions (including central short-range repulsion and tensor forces) using advanced many-body approaches to obtain ground-state wave functions for A = 2, 3, 4, 16, and 40. They then computed spin-isospin dependent one- and two-body density matrices and momentum distributions, separating mean-field and short-range correlation (SRC) contributions, and analyzed the number of NN pairs in each (ST) state: (10), (00), (01), and (11).

## Key numbers

- The high-momentum scaling region is identified for k ≳ 1.5–2 fm⁻¹, where the momentum distributions of all nuclei considered become very similar.
- The number of NN pairs in each (ST) state was calculated for A = 2, 3, 4, 16, and 40, with the (00) state having very small effects.
- The ratio n_A(k)/n_D(k) in the SRC region always increases with momentum.
- The per nucleon probability a_2 of deuteronlike configurations in nuclei was calculated (explicit numerical values not provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-nucleon momentum distributions by Alvioli et al. (2007, 2012) to the one-nucleon momentum distribution, systematically analyzing the spin-isospin structure across a range of nuclei. The results confirm and quantify the A-independent character of SRCs predicted by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) and observed in experiments such as those by Tang et al. (2002) and Shneor et al. (2007). The findings enable more precise extraction of SRC properties from inclusive and exclusive electron scattering experiments and clarify the relationship between nuclear and deuteron momentum distributions, which had been assumed in earlier Monte Carlo calculations by Schiavilla and others.
