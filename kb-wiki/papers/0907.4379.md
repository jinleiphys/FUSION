# Dynamical Effects on Jet Energy Loss in a QCD Medium

> Computation of radiative energy loss in a finite size dynamically screened QCD medium is a key ingredient for obtaining reliable predictions for jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We develop a theory which allows calculating, to first order in the number of scattering centers, the energy loss of a heavy quark traveling through a finite size dynamical QCD medium. We show that the result for a dynamical medium is significantly larger compared to a medium consisting of randomly distributed static scattering centers. Therefore, a quantitative description of jet suppression at RHIC and LHC experiments must correctly account for the dynamics of the medium's constituents. Furthermore, qualitative predictions that come from this energy loss formalism are also presented.

# Dynamical Effects on Jet Energy Loss in a QCD Medium

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss of a heavy quark in a finite-size dynamical QCD medium is significantly larger than in a static medium for all experimentally realistic jet energies at RHIC and LHC, but at asymptotically large jet energies the static approximation becomes valid up to a multiplicative constant that can be renormalized.

## Method

The author computed the medium-induced radiative energy loss for a heavy quark to first order in the number of scattering centers (lowest order in opacity), considering one gluon radiation induced by one collisional interaction with dynamical medium partons. The calculation used a two Hard-Thermal Loop approach within a finite-size QCD medium modeled following Zakharov (1996), assuming the medium has size L and that the collisional interaction must occur inside the medium. The resulting expression for fractional radiative energy loss was compared to the corresponding static medium result from Djordjevic and Gyulassy (2004).

## Key numbers

- The difference in effective mean free paths between dynamical and static media increases the energy loss in the dynamical medium by approximately 20%.
- The change in effective differential cross sections contributes an additional approximately 40% increase in the energy loss in the dynamical medium.
- At asymptotically large jet energies, the ratio of dynamical to static energy loss approaches the ratio of static to dynamical mean free paths: $\lim_{E \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\Delta E_{\mathrm{dyn}}}{\Delta E_{\mathrm{stat}}} = \frac{\lambda_\mathrm{stat}}{\lambda_\mathrm{dyn}}$.
- For LHC conditions (T = 400 MeV, L = 5 fm), light and charm quarks lose all of their energy in the range up to 70 GeV, and above 70 GeV they lose the same amount of energy.
- For a 10 GeV bottom quark jet, the energy loss thickness dependence overlaps with the incoherent Bertsch-Gunion limit; for a 1 TeV bottom quark jet, it overlaps with the LPM limit.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior calculations of radiative energy loss in infinite dynamical QCD media (Djordjevic and Heinz, 2008) to the case of a finite-size optically thin medium, which is necessary for realistic RHIC and LHC conditions. It contradicts the common assumption in earlier studies that the QCD medium can be treated as randomly distributed static scattering centers, as used in the static medium formalism of Djordjevic and Gyulassy (2004). The formalism enables reliable quantitative predictions for jet suppression at RHIC and LHC experiments and identifies bottom quark suppression as an excellent probe for studying finite mass effects on energy loss, while showing that charm suppression is not a good probe for the dead-cone effect.
