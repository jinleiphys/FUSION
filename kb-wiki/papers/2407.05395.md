# Quantifying angular distributions in multinucleon transfer reactions with a semi-classical method

> The multinucleon transfer (MNT) process in low-energy heavy ion collisions can be utilized to produce unknown nuclei far beyond the stability line. However, the reaction products exhibit broad angular and energy distributions, which could lower the experimental detection efficiency. We present a classical approach that employs a parameterized angular distribution to describe the complex issue. By analyzing limited experimental data on angular distribution, we proposed a three-parameter formula to calculate the angular distribution and identified the dependencies of the parameters. We also discuss the sensitivity of these parameters within this method. A comprehensive comparison with microscopic models and experimental data across a wide range of conditions is conducted. The proposed formula offers an efficient and straightforward way to determine the angular distribution of MNT products.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-parameter semi-classical formula for calculating angular distributions in multinucleon transfer reactions, but the authors acknowledge that the parameterization is based on only 17 sets of experimental data and that minor discrepancies remain between the fitted parameter functions and the data points, particularly in forward-angle regions of light projectile-target combinations where a significant nuclear rainbow effect exists.

## Method

The authors construct a deflection function using two parameters (β and δ) that describe the mean scattering angle as a function of entrance-channel angular momentum, based on Wolschin's (1978) formalism. They then apply a Gaussian expansion around this mean deflection trajectory, introducing a third parameter (Dθ) that controls the width of the angular distribution via a diffusion coefficient related to interaction time through the Fokker-Planck equation. The three parameters are fitted to experimental angular distributions from 17 reaction systems, and their dependence on the modified Sommerfeld parameter η′ is parameterized analytically.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 136Xe + 209Bi at Ec.m. = 569 MeV, the optimal parameters were β = 11, δ = 0.09, and Dθ = 12
- The parameter β(η′) = 4.7 + 81.2·exp[−(η′ − 160.8)/126.5]
- The parameter δ(η′) = 0.029 + 0.127·exp[−(η′ − 176.3)/364]
- The parameter Dθ(η′) = 21 − 0.0178η′

## Context

This work extends the two-parameter empirical deflection function approach of Wolschin (1978) and Li (1983), who noted that earlier fits produced angular distributions that were too narrow compared to experimental data. The method provides an efficient alternative to microscopic models such as time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory and the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, avoiding uncertainties from interaction potential parameters and frictional form factors. The parameterized formula enables optimization of detector placement in future MNT experiments and can be implemented in extended dynamics calculations such as the dinuclear system model.
