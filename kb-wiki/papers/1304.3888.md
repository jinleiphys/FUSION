# Minimal Ward-Takahashi Vertices and Pion Light Cone Distribution Amplitudes from Gauge Invariant, Nonlocal, Dynamical Quark Model

> The gauge-invariant, nonlocal, dynamical quark model is proved to generate the minimal vertices which satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identities. In the chiral limit, the momentum-dependent quark self-energy results in a flat-like form with some end point delta-funtions for the light-cone pion distribution amplitudes, similarly found in the Nambu Jona-Lasino model with constant constituent mass. The leading order nonzero pion and current quark masses corrections lead concave type asymptotic-like form modifications to twist-2 pion distribution amplitude with end point pillars and twist-3 tensor pion distribution amplitude above the flat-like form backgrounds. A by-product of our investigation shows that the variable u appearing in pion light-cone distribution amplitudes is just the standard Feynman parameter in the Feynman parameter integrals; also chiral perturbation works well for these amplitudes.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant, nonlocal, dynamical quark (GND) model is shown to generate the minimal vertices that satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identities, and in the chiral limit the momentum-dependent quark self-energy yields flat-like pion distribution amplitudes with end-point delta-function pillars, contrary to the expectation that momentum dependence would force deviations from flat forms.

## Method

The authors start from the GND model action, integrate out rotated light-quark fields, and compute the three-point Green's functions to extract the scalar, pseudo-scalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor vertices in the chiral limit. They then compute the light-cone pion distribution amplitudes (PDAs) by relating the operator product to the GND model's quark propagator and vertices, using Feynman parameterization to identify the PDA variable \(u\) with the standard Feynman parameter. Chiral perturbation theory is employed to expand the momentum integrations in powers of the pion mass squared \(m_\pi^2\) and the current quark mass \(m\), enabling numerical evaluation of the PDA coefficients from the Schwinger-Dyson equation solution for the quark self-energy.

## Key numbers

* \(\phi_{\delta0}/m_\pi^2 = 1.85 \times 10^{-2}\): height of the symmetric end-point pillars for the twist-2 PDA \(\phi(u)\) in the chiral limit.
* \(\phi_0/m_\pi^2 = 10.13 \times 10^{-2}\): flat-like background height for \(\phi(u)\) in the chiral limit.
* \(\phi_{11}/m_\pi^2 = -2.75 \times 10^{-2}\): coefficient of the asymptotic-like concave correction \(u(1-u)\) to \(\phi(u)\) at first order in chiral perturbation.
* \(\phi_{p,\delta0}/m_\pi^3 = 82.22 \times 10^{-2}\): height of the non-symmetric end-point pillar at \(u=0\) for the twist-3 pseudo-scalar PDA \(\phi_p(u)\) in the chiral limit.
* \(\phi_{\sigma,0}/m_\pi^2 = 8.13 \times 10^{-2}\): flat-like background height for the twist-3 tensor PDA \(\phi_\sigma(u)\) in the chiral limit.
* \(\phi_{\sigma,11}/m_\pi^2 = -0.17 \times 10^{-2}\): coefficient of the asymptotic-like concave correction \(u(1-u)\) to \(\phi_\sigma(u)\) at first order.
* Numerical inputs: \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} = 440\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_\pi = 139.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), current quark mass \(m = 12.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), Euclidean cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the GND model developed by Wang and collaborators (2002, 2010) by explicitly proving that it satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identities and generates minimal vertices of the form assumed by Holdom (1989). The result that PDAs in the chiral limit are flat-like with end-point pillars agrees with earlier local NJL model calculations (flat, 1998) but contradicts the expectation from other nonlocal chiral quark models (nonlocalPDAs1) that momentum-dependent quark self-energies would produce non-flat PDAs. The chiral perturbation expansion enables quantitative estimates of PDA corrections from nonzero pion and quark masses, providing a starting point for QCD Gegenbauer evolution to compare with phenomenological extractions from experiments.
