# Observation of nuclear suppression in coherent $Υ$(1S) photoproduction off heavy nuclei at the LHC

> The first measurement of coherent $Υ$(1S) meson photoproduction off heavy nuclei is performed using ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions collected by the CMS experiment at a nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV. The nuclear gluonic structure is probed at a nucleon momentum fraction of order $x$ $\sim$ 10$^{-3}$, determined by the kinematics of the process. Owing to the large $Υ$(1S) mass, the measurement reaches the highest scale accessible so far through coherent vector-meson photoproduction, $μ^2$ = 22.4 GeV$^2$, where nonlinear quantum chromodynamics effects are expected to be minimal. In the $Υ$(1S) rapidity range $\lvert y\rvert$ $\lt$ 1, the ratio of the measured photoproduction cross section to a baseline model prediction that neglects nuclear effects is $S_{Υ\text{(1S)}}$ = 0.25 $\pm$ 0.06 (stat) $\pm$ 0.02 (syst), thereby demonstrating nuclear suppression in this process. Expressed in terms of a nuclear gluon suppression factor, the result yields $R_\text{g}^\text{Pb}$($x$ $\approx$ 10$^{-3}$, $μ^2$ = 22.4 GeV$^2$) = 0.55 $\pm$ 0.12 (stat) $\pm$ 0.02 (syst). The measured $R_\text{g}^\text{Pb}$ is only slightly larger than the values previously reported for coherent $ϕ$ photoproduction, despite the probed $μ^2$ differing by approximately two orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The CMS Collaboration reports the first measurement of coherent Upsilon(1S) meson photoproduction off heavy nuclei, finding a nuclear suppression factor S_Upsilon(1S) = 0.25 +/- 0.06 (stat) +/- 0.02 (syst) relative to a baseline model that neglects nuclear effects, with the caveat that this measurement probes the highest scale accessible via coherent vector-meson photoproduction (mu^2 = 22.4 GeV^2) where nonlinear quantum chromodynamics effects are expected to be minimal.

## Method

The measurement uses ultraperipheral lead-lead collisions at a nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV recorded by the CMS experiment, selecting events where the Upsilon(1S) meson is produced coherently off the entire lead nucleus. The coherent photoproduction cross section is extracted in the Upsilon(1S) rapidity range |y| < 1, and the nuclear suppression factor S_Upsilon(1S) is obtained by taking the ratio of the measured cross section to a baseline model prediction that neglects nuclear effects. The result is also expressed as a nuclear gluon suppression factor R_g^Pb at the probed kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear suppression factor S_Upsilon(1S) = 0.25 +/- 0.06 (stat) +/- 0.02 (syst) for coherent Upsilon(1S) photoproduction in lead-lead collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, rapidity |y| < 1.
- Nuclear gluon suppression factor R_g^Pb(x approx 10^-3, mu^2 = 22.4 GeV^2) = 0.55 +/- 0.12 (stat) +/- 0.02 (syst).
- The measurement probes a nucleon momentum fraction x ~ 10^-3 and a scale mu^2 = 22.4 GeV^2, the highest scale accessible through coherent vector-meson photoproduction.

## Context

This measurement extends the study of nuclear gluon suppression via coherent vector-meson photoproduction to the Upsilon(1S) mass scale, following previous measurements of coherent phi photoproduction at lower scales. The measured R_g^Pb is only slightly larger than values previously reported for coherent phi photoproduction, despite the probed mu^2 differing by approximately two orders of magnitude, providing a new constraint on the scale dependence of nuclear gluon modifications. The result enables tests of quantum chromodynamics predictions for nuclear shadowing at high scales where nonlinear effects are expected to be minimal.
