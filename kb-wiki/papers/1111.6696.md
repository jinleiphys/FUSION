# Production of large transverse momentum dileptons and photons in $pp$, $dA$ and $AA$ collisions by photoproduction processes

> The production of large $P_{T}$ dileptons and photons originating from photoproduction processes in $pp$, $dA$ and $AA$ collisions is calculated. We find that the contribution of dileptons and photons produced by photoproduction processes is not prominent at RHIC energies. However, the numerical results indicate that the modification of photoproduction processes becomes evident in the large $P_{T}$ region for $pp$, $dA$ and $AA$ collisions at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The contribution of dileptons and photons produced by photoproduction processes is not prominent at RHIC energies, but becomes evident in the large transverse momentum region for pp, dA and AA collisions at LHC energies, with the specific threshold depending on the collision system and particle type.

## Method

The authors calculate the invariant cross sections for large transverse momentum dileptons and photons from direct and resolved photoproduction processes in pp, dA and AA collisions, using the photon spectrum from charged partons and parton distribution functions with nuclear modification factors. They compare these photoproduction contributions to the standard sources: direct and fragmentation dileptons/photons from initial parton collisions, thermal dileptons/photons from the quark-gluon plasma, and jet-dilepton/photon conversion in the medium, using Bjorken 1+1 D expansion and initial temperatures of 370 MeV for Au+Au at RHIC and 710-845 MeV for Pb+Pb at LHC.

## Key numbers

- For dileptons in pp collisions at LHC (sqrt(s)=7 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 1 GeV
- For dileptons in d+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=6.2 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 1 GeV
- For dileptons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 2 GeV
- For dileptons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 4 GeV
- For real photons in pp collisions at LHC (sqrt(s)=7 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 4 GeV
- For real photons in d+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=6.2 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 4 GeV
- For real photons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 5 GeV
- For real photons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), photoproduction contributions become evident for P_T > 7 GeV
- Initial temperatures used: T_0=370 MeV for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, T_0=710 MeV for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, T_0=845 MeV for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV
- Initial times: tau_0=0.26 fm/c for RHIC, tau_0=0.09 fm/c for LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV), tau_0=0.088 fm/c for LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV)
- Dilepton mass range used: 100 MeV <= M <= 300 MeV
- Jet energy loss before conversion into photons or virtual photons is about 20%

## Context

This work extends the photoproduction mechanism, previously applied to ep deep inelastic scattering at HERA, to dilepton and photon production in hadronic collisions. It complements prior calculations of direct and fragmentation dileptons from initial parton collisions (as in the work of Owens and others), thermal production from the QGP (as in the work of Kapusta and others), and jet-photon conversion in the thermal medium (as in the work of Fries and others). The results enable experimental searches at LHC to distinguish photoproduction contributions from other sources in the large transverse momentum region, particularly for Pb+Pb collisions where the effect becomes visible above 2-7 GeV depending on the particle type and collision energy.
