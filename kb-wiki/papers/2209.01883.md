# Possible origin of HADES data on proton number fluctuations in Au+Au collisions

> Recent data of the HADES Collaboration in Au+Au central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV indicate large proton number fluctuations inside one unit of rapidity around midrapidity. This can be a signature of critical phenomena due to the strong attractive interactions between baryons. We study an alternative hypothesis that these large fluctuations are caused by the event-by-event fluctuations of the number of bare protons, and no interactions between these protons are assumed. The proton number fluctuations in five symmetric rapidity intervals $Δy$ inside the region $ΔY=1$ are calculated using the binomial acceptance procedure. This procedure assumes the independent (uncorrelated) emission of protons, and it appears to be in agreement with the HADES data. To check this simple picture we suggest to calculate the correlation between proton multiplicities in non-overlapping rapidity intervals $Δy_1$ and $Δy_2$ placed inside $ΔY=1$.

## Key claim

The large proton number fluctuations observed by HADES in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4\) GeV can be explained by event-by-event fluctuations of the number of bare protons with no interactions between them, rather than by critical phenomena; the binomial acceptance procedure assuming independent emission of protons agrees with the data, but the authors note that this simple picture should be checked by calculating correlations between proton multiplicities in non-overlapping rapidity intervals.

## Method

The authors applied the binomial acceptance procedure, which assumes each proton is accepted into a given rapidity interval \(\Delta y\) with a fixed probability \(\alpha\) independent of event properties, to compute the scaled variance, skewness, and kurtosis of proton number fluctuations in five symmetric rapidity intervals within \(\Delta Y = 1\). They used the HADES proton rapidity distribution fitted by a Gaussian to determine \(\alpha\) for each \(\Delta y\), and took the measured cumulants in the full \(\Delta Y = 1\) interval as input parameters to predict the fluctuation measures in smaller intervals via Eqs. (w-alpha), (s-alpha), and (k-alpha).

## Key numbers

- Scaled variance \(\omega\) for protons in the symmetric rapidity interval \(\Delta Y = 1\) exceeds 2, increasing monotonously from unity at \(\Delta y \ll 1\).
- The HADES data cover six symmetric rapidity intervals: \(\Delta y = 0.1, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8\), and \(\Delta Y = 1\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.42\) GeV.
- A transverse momentum cut of \(0.4 < p_T < 1.6\) GeV/\(c\) was applied to the HADES data.
- The Gaussian fit to the proton rapidity distribution yields parameters \(C = 90/\sqrt{2\pi a^2}\) and \(a = 0.62\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of proton number fluctuations at low collision energies, where the HADES data (HADES Collaboration, 2020) show increasing scaled variance with rapidity interval, in contrast to the monotonous decrease observed at RHIC and LHC energies. The authors note that effects of baryon conservation and excluded volume repulsion, which describe high-energy data (Vovchenko, 2022), fail to explain the HADES results even qualitatively. The proposed binomial acceptance explanation enables a testable prediction: universal positive correlations between proton multiplicities in non-overlapping rapidity intervals, which can be checked against existing HADES data.
