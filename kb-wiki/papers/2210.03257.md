# Confronting anomalous kaon correlations measured in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV

> Measurements of the dynamical correlations between neutral and charged kaons in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV by the ALICE Collaboration display anomalous behavior relative to conventional heavy-ion collision simulators such as AMPT, EPOS, and HIJING. We consider other conventional statistical models, none of which can reproduce the magnitude and centrality dependence of the correlations. The data can be reproduced by coherent emission from domains which grow in number and volume with increasing centrality. We show that the energy released by condensation of strange quarks may be sufficient to explain the anomaly.

## Key claim

The anomalous dynamical correlations between neutral and charged kaons measured by ALICE in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV cannot be reproduced by conventional statistical models or heavy-ion simulators, but can be explained by coherent emission from domains of strange quark condensation, provided the energy released by such condensation is sufficient.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological model where kaons are emitted from independent domains, with a fraction of kaons coming from coherent (condensate) sources and the remainder from random sources. They fit this two-parameter model to the five most central ALICE data bins, using hydrodynamic simulations (MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions) to estimate the proper time duration of the deconfined phase. They also calculated the correlation from a full hadron gas model using canonical ensemble partition functions with multiple hadron species, and from simple analytic models of distinguishable and indistinguishable kaon systems with and without charge conservation.

## Key numbers

- The ALICE measurement of $\nu_{\rm dyn}/\alpha$ for the 0-5% centrality bin is $0.213 \pm 0.021$, with $1/\alpha = 31.20$, $N_{K^0_S} = 46.80$, and $N_{K^\pm} = 93.60$
- The two-parameter fit to the five highest multiplicity bins yields $b = 0.1044 \pm 0.0380$ and $b^2/a = 0.2187 \pm 0.0458$, assuming chemical freeze-out at $T = 160$ MeV
- Using a reference condensation energy density $\epsilon_{\zeta} = 25$ MeV/fm$^3$, the fit gives $v_0 = 2.07$ fm$^3$ and yields for the 0-5% bin: $N_d = 9.32$ domains, total domain volume $V_d = 1120$ fm$^3$, and fraction of kaons from condensates $\beta_K = 0.302$
- The proper time duration from MUSIC hydrodynamics for the 0-5% centrality bin ranges from 11.56 fm/c at $T=160$ MeV to 14.93 fm/c at $T=140$ MeV
- The full hadron gas model gives $\nu_{\rm dyn}/\alpha \approx 0.04$ for large volumes, with over 90% from $\phi$ meson decays, and after acceptance corrections the background is estimated at $\lesssim 0.035$
- For a system of degenerate kaons with charge conservation, $\nu_{\rm dyn}/\alpha = (2/9)(n_K+1)$ where $n_K$ is the number of kaons in the domain

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between ALICE measurements of kaon correlations and predictions from standard heavy-ion simulators such as AMPT, EPOS, and HIJING, as well as conventional statistical models. The authors extend the disordered chiral condensate (DCC) picture proposed by Bjorken (1991) and Rajagopal (1992), showing that an ordinary strange condensate gives similar results. The analysis builds on the formalism of Gavin and Kapusta for isospin fluctuations, and uses the canonical ensemble approach of Pratt (2020). The results suggest that if confirmed, the anomalous correlations would provide evidence for strange quark condensation in heavy-ion collisions, motivating further experimental and theoretical study of domain formation and the linear sigma model for strange quarks.
