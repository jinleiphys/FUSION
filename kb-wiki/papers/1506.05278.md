# Leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics for central collisions

> We use leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics to study an azimuthally-symmetric boost-invariant quark-gluon plasma. We impose a realistic lattice-based equation of state and perform self-consistent anisotropic freeze-out to hadronic degrees of freedom. We then compare our results for the full spatiotemporal evolution of the quark-gluon plasma and its subsequent freeze-out to results obtained using 1+1d Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics. We find that for small shear viscosities, 4 pi eta/s ~ 1, the two methods agree well for nucleus-nucleus collisions, however, for large shear viscosity to entropy density ratios or proton-nucleus collisions we find important corrections to the Israel-Stewart results for the final particle spectra and the total number of charged particles. Finally, we demonstrate that the total number of charged particles produced is a monotonically increasing function of 4 pi eta/s in Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics whereas in anisotropic hydrodynamics it has a maximum at 4 pi eta/s ~ 10. For all 4 pi eta/s > 0, we find that for Pb-Pb collisions Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics predicts more dissipative particle production than anisotropic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

For small shear viscosities (4π η/s ~ 1), leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics and Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics agree well for nucleus-nucleus collisions, but for large shear viscosity to entropy density ratios or proton-nucleus collisions, anisotropic hydrodynamics predicts important corrections to the Israel-Stewart results for final particle spectra and total charged particle number.

## Method

The authors implemented 1+1d leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics for an azimuthally-symmetric, boost-invariant quark-gluon plasma using an ellipsoidal form for the local-rest-frame one-particle distribution function, with dynamical equations obtained from moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation. They imposed a realistic lattice-based equation of state parameterized from Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration data and performed self-consistent anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out to hadronic degrees of freedom, comparing results to a 1+1d Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics code with identical initial conditions, freeze-out hypersurface algorithm, and lattice-based equation of state.

## Key numbers

- For 4π η/s = 1, the two methods agree well for nucleus-nucleus collisions (qualitative statement from abstract and text)
- The total number of charged particles in anisotropic hydrodynamics has a maximum at 4π η/s ~ 10, whereas in Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics it is monotonically increasing with 4π η/s
- For all 4π η/s > 0, Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics predicts more dissipative particle production than anisotropic hydrodynamics for Pb-Pb collisions
- The factorization of thermodynamic variables in the non-conformal case is accurate to ≤ 5% for 0.1 ≲ P_L/P_T ≲ 10 and masses m/T ≲ 1, with largest corrections for strongly prolate plasma (P_L ≫ P_T)

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Martinez (2012), Tinti (2013), and Nopoush (2014) by adding two fundamental ingredients: a realistic lattice-based equation of state from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2010) and self-consistent anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out. The results contradict the monotonic behavior of charged particle production with shear viscosity predicted by Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics (Denicol 2012), showing instead a peak at 4π η/s ~ 10. This enables future development of 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics codes for phenomenological modeling of heavy-ion collisions, with planned inclusion of bulk viscous effects and generalization to "viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics" (Bazow 2013, 2015).
