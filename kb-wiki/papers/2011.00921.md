# Two-Meson Form Factors in Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We present a comprehensive analysis of form factors for two light pseudoscalar mesons induced by scalar, vector, and tensor quark operators. The theoretical framework is based on a combination of unitarized chiral perturbation theory and dispersion relations. The low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory are fixed by a global fit to the available data of the two-meson scattering phase shifts. Each form factor derived from unitarized chiral perturbation theory is improved by iteratively applying a dispersion relation. This study updates the existing results in the literature and explores those that have not been systematically studied previously, in particular the two-meson tensor form factors within unitarized chiral perturbation theory. We also discuss the applications of these form factors as mandatory inputs for low-energy phenomena, such as the semi-leptonic decays $B_s\to π^+π^-\ell^+\ell^-$ and the $τ$ lepton decay $τ\rightarrowπ^{-}π^{0}ν_τ$, in searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim
The paper presents a comprehensive analysis of scalar, vector, and tensor two-meson form factors using unitarized chiral perturbation theory (uChPT) combined with dispersion relations, but the results are limited to energies up to approximately 1.2 GeV and rely on a modified coupled-channel inverse amplitude method (IAM) that removes imaginary parts of t- and u-channel loops to restore exact unitarity, which introduces some scheme-dependence.

## Method
The authors first performed a global fit of the low-energy constants (LECs) in next-to-leading-order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory to available two-meson scattering phase shift data (ππ, Kπ, ππ→K̅K, πη) using a modified coupled-channel IAM that removes imaginary parts of troublesome t- and u-channel loops to restore exact unitarity. They then calculated scalar, vector, and tensor form factors from uChPT and improved each form factor by iteratively applying a dispersion relation to eliminate unphysical sub-threshold singularities related to Adler zeros.

## Key numbers
- The LECs were fitted with χ²/dof = 7.76 for Fit 1 (original data errors) and χ²/dof = 1.21 for Fit 2 (errors increased by 5% of central values)
- The unitarity violation in the I=1, J=1 channel was as much as 20% around the ρ-peak (√s ≈ 0.77 GeV) before modification
- The improved form factors behave well in the energy range 0 to 1.2 GeV
- The pion decay constant Fπ ≈ 92.1 MeV is used as a reference scale
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ ∼ 4πFπ

## Context
This work extends previous studies of two-meson form factors in uChPT by Meißner (2000) and Lähde (2006) for scalar form factors, and by Gómez Nicola (2001) for the IAM method, while also providing the first systematic study of tensor form factors within uChPT. The results enable improved calculations of low-energy phenomena such as B_s → π⁺π⁻ℓ⁺ℓ⁻ semileptonic decays and τ → π⁻π⁰ν_τ decays for searches of physics beyond the Standard Model, and can be compared with dispersive analyses by Moussallam (1999) and Hoferichter (2012) for scalar form factors.
