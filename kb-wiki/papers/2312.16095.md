# Excluded volume effects in the quark-mass density-dependent model: implications for the equation of state and compact star structure

> We present a significant extension of the quark mass density-dependent model (QMDDM), initially revised in our prior study (Lugones and Grunfeld, Phys. Rev. D 107, 043025 (2023)), where thermodynamic inconsistencies were addressed. Our current work enriches the QMDDM by incorporating excluded volume effects, as a step towards a more realistic representation of the quark matter equation of state (EOS) at zero temperature. We introduce the concept of ``available volume'' in the Helmholtz free energy formulation, accounting for the space excluded by each quasiparticle due to its finite size or repulsive interactions. We present a methodology to modify the EOS for point-like particles, allowing for a simple and direct incorporation of excluded volume effects. This is first addressed in a simple one-flavor model and then extended to a more realistic three-flavor system, incorporating both mass and volume dependencies on the baryon number density. We examine various ansatzes for the excluded volume, ultimately adopting one that aligns with the asymptotic freedom behavior of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). The EOS for electrically neutral systems in chemical equilibrium is computed, focusing on self-bound and hybrid matter scenarios. We show that the incorporation of excluded volume effects renders the EOS stiffer and that excluded volume effects are essential to align the mass-radius relation of self-bound and hybrid stars with modern astrophysical constraints.

## Key claim

The paper extends the quark-mass density-dependent model (QMDDM) by incorporating excluded volume effects, showing that this modification makes the equation of state (EOS) stiffer and is essential for aligning the mass-radius relation of self-bound and hybrid stars with modern astrophysical constraints, but only when the excluded volume ansatz is chosen to vanish at high densities in accordance with asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The authors replace the system volume \(V\) with an "available volume" \(\tilde{V} = V - b(n)N\) in the Helmholtz free energy of point-like particles, where \(b(n)\) is a density-dependent excluded volume per particle. They derive modified expressions for energy density, pressure, and chemical potential in terms of the point-like EOS evaluated at an adjusted density \(n/q\) with \(q = 1 - n b(n)\), and apply this formalism to one-flavor and three-flavor QMDDM systems at zero temperature. The EOS for electrically neutral matter in chemical equilibrium is computed for both self-bound and hybrid star scenarios using an ansatz \(b = \kappa n^{-\ell}\) that respects asymptotic freedom.

## Key numbers

* For the constant excluded volume ansatz with \(b = 0.1~\mathrm{fm}^3\), the EOS becomes invalid above a particle number density of approximately \(62 n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is nuclear saturation density); for \(b = 0.3~\mathrm{fm}^3\), the threshold is approximately \(20 n_0\).
* For the density-dependent ansatz \(b = \kappa n^{-1}\), the maximum allowed value of \(\kappa\) is 1 from the condition that available volume cannot exceed total volume, and a more stringent upper limit of \(\kappa \approx 0.64\) is derived from the constraint that excluded volume at \(n = 0.16~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) should not exceed approximately \(4~\mathrm{fm}^3\).
* The speed of sound exceeds the speed of light at densities above a certain threshold for the constant \(b\) ansatz with \(b \neq 0\), and \(c_s\) does not approach the conformal limit of \(1/\sqrt{3}\) at asymptotically high densities for that ansatz.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior reformulation of the QMDDM (Lugones and Grunfeld, 2023) which addressed thermodynamic consistency issues in the canonical ensemble. The excluded volume approach is analogous to the Van der Waals equation of state as applied in earlier works by Rischke (1991), Yen (1997), and Steinheimer (2010). The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in compact stars, particularly to satisfy constraints from observations of very high-mass compact stars such as those reported by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013).
