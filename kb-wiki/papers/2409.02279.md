# Structure of odd-mass Ne, Na, and Mg nuclei

> The island of inversion is a region of neutron-rich nuclei that are deformed in their ground states. In this region, less is known about the energy levels of odd-mass nuclei, how they evolve with increasing neutron numbers, and how they can be organized into rotational bands. We perform {\it ab initio} coupled-cluster calculations of spectra in odd-mass Ne, Na, and Mg nuclei based on an interaction of chiral effective field theory. Our results confirm some tentative spin and parity assignments, predict the structure of nuclei near the neutron dripline, and inform us about rotational bands in this region of the nuclear table.

## Key claim

The paper claims that *ab initio* coupled-cluster calculations, guided by the Nilsson model and using angular-momentum projection, can accurately describe rotational bands in odd-mass Ne, Na, and Mg nuclei within the island of inversion, but only for states that are sufficiently well-bound and below the neutron separation energy, as continuum effects are not included.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) calculations starting from axially symmetric, symmetry-breaking mean-field reference states constructed by placing the odd nucleon into a Nilsson orbital with definite spin/parity K^pi. They used a spherical Hartree-Fock procedure with fractional filling to normal-order the Hamiltonian at the two-body level, then employed a deformed density matrix reflecting the expected Nilsson occupations. Angular-momentum projection was applied after the CCSD computation using a disentangled method that integrates a differential equation to combine the Thouless theorem with the coupled-cluster wavefunction, yielding energies for states of total angular momentum J within rotational bands built on each K^pi band head.

## Key numbers

- Model spaces used N_max = 8 and hbar-omega = 14 MeV for all Ne, Na, and Mg nuclei.
- Computed moments of inertia in even-even nuclei have an uncertainty of about 20 percent; energy differences between band heads have theoretical uncertainties of about 1 MeV, estimated from N_max = 8 versus 12 comparisons in Sun (2024).
- For the benchmark nucleus ^9Be, the K^pi = 3/2^- ground-state band was accurately reproduced compared to experiment and no-core shell model results from Caprio (2015).
- For ^21Ne, the CCSD binding energy was about 159 MeV compared to the experimental 161 MeV, while the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) from Lin (2024) gave about 119 MeV.
- For ^21Ne, the CCSD calculation yielded a J^pi = 7/2+ state in the K^pi = 1/2+ band at 6.6 MeV, while the PGCM gave 10.1 MeV; data did not place a 7/2+ level into this band.
- For ^25Na, Hartree-Fock energies for different configurations ranged from -138.5 MeV to -139.7 MeV, with the normal filling giving E_{3/2+} = -138.5 MeV and E_{5/2+} = -137.9 MeV, while a larger deformation gave E_{3/2+} = -139.7 MeV and E_{5/2+} = -139.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* coupled-cluster approach for deformed nuclei, previously applied to even-even nuclei up to mass A approximately 80 by Hu (2024) and Sun (2024), into the odd-mass regime within the island of inversion. It builds on the Nilsson model as a guide for deformed odd-mass nuclei, analogous to how the spherical shell model guided earlier coupled-cluster computations for doubly-magic nuclei by Hagen (2014, 2016) and Morris (2018). The results enable future studies of rotational band structure near the neutron dripline and highlight the need for multi-reference or continuum-including approaches for nuclei such as ^25,27,29Na where single-reference calculations fail to reproduce the near degeneracy of 5/2+ and 3/2+ states.
