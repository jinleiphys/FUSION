# Summary of Working Group 3 of the Neutrino Factory '02 Workshop

> The parallel sessions of the working group 3 were devoted to the discussions of short-baseline neutrino physics program at neutrino factories. First, possible studies of parton distribution functions (PDFs), in particular nuclear and polarized PDFs, are discussed in this summary. Second, the extractions of alpha_s from sum rules and structure functions of deep-inelastic neutrino-nucleon scattering, higher-order perturbative QCD corrections and estimates of high-twist effects are summarized. Third, the situation of the observed NuTeV sin^2 theta_W anomaly is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes discussions from Working Group 3 of the Neutrino Factory '02 Workshop, arguing that future neutrino factories could significantly improve determinations of parton distribution functions (especially nuclear and polarized PDFs), the QCD coupling constant α_s, and the understanding of the NuTeV sin²θ_W anomaly, but notes that many of these improvements depend on resolving correlations between perturbative and non-perturbative QCD effects that become problematic at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Method

The working group held parallel sessions discussing short-baseline neutrino physics at proposed neutrino factories. Presentations covered global χ² analyses of nuclear PDFs using F₂ and Drell-Yan data, global χ² analyses of polarized PDFs using spin asymmetry A₁ or g₁ data from electron/muon scattering, and extractions of α_s from sum rules (Gross-Llewellyn Smith, Bjorken unpolarized) and structure functions of deep-inelastic neutrino-nucleon scattering, including higher-order perturbative QCD corrections and estimates of high-twist effects. The NuTeV anomaly was examined through comparisons of neutral-current to charged-current cross-section ratios.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV collaboration measured R^ν = 0.3916 ± 0.0007 and R^ν̄ = 0.4050 ± 0.0016 from their data
- NuTeV extracted sin²θ_W^{on-shell} = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.009 (syst), with a 3σ deviation from the LEPEWWG value of 0.2227 ± 0.0037
- From the Borel-resummed perturbative expression for the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule I_{F₃}, α_s(M_Z) = 0.1167^{+0.0128}_{-0.0118} (exp) ± 0.0008 (th)
- From the truncated NNLO perturbative series for I_{F₃}, α_s(M_Z) = 0.114^{+0.005}_{-0.006} (stat)^{+0.007}_{-0.009} (syst) ± 0.005 (th) (CCFR/NuTeV collaboration)
- From NLO fits to CCFR xF₃ data: α_s(M_Z) = 0.120 ± 0.002 (stat) ± 0.005 (syst) ± 0.002 (thresh)^{+0.010}_{-0.006} (scale)
- From the same NLO fits: IRR model parameter A₂' = -0.125 ± 0.053 GeV²
- From NNLO fits to CCFR'97 xF₃ data: A₂' = -0.013 ± 0.051 GeV²
- From NNLO fits to F₂ data: A₂' = -0.0065 ± 0.0059 GeV²
- From normalizing I_{F₁} to Q²=4 GeV²: Δ^{HT}α_s(M_Z)=0.012; to Q²=10 GeV²: Δ^{HT}α_s(M_Z)=0.007

## Context

This workshop summary extends the line of work on neutrino-nucleon deep-inelastic scattering as a tool for QCD and electroweak parameter extraction, following earlier studies by Mangano et al. (2001) and Albright et al. on structure functions and PDFs at neutrino factories. It discusses the first serious nuclear PDF parameterization by Eskola, Kolhinen, Ruuskanen, and Salgado (EKRS) and the χ² analysis approach of Kumano et al. (2001). The NuTeV anomaly (Zeller, 2001) is presented as an unresolved 3σ deviation from the LEPEWWG result, with no convincing explanation found in the talk by Davidson (2001). The results enable future planning for neutrino factory experiments that could measure the Bjorken unpolarized sum rule for the first time and improve determinations of polarized PDFs, particularly the spin content ΔΣ and the polarized gluon distribution.
