# Medium effects in the pion pole mechanism (photon photon --> pion-zero --> neutrino-R antineutrino-L (neutrino-L antineutrino-R)) of neutron star cooling

> Nuclear medium effects in the neutrino cooling of neutron stars through the exotic reaction channel γγ--> π^0--> ν_R \bar{ν_L} (ν_L \bar{ν_R}) are incorporated. Throughout the paper we discuss different possibilities of right-handed neutrinos, massive left-handed neutrinos and standard massless left-handed neutrinos (reaction is then allowed only with medium modified vertices). It is demonstrated that multi-particle effects suppress the rate of this reaction channel by 6-7 orders of magnitude that does not allow to decrease existing experimental upper limit on the corresponding π^0ν\barν coupling. Other possibilities of the manifestation of the given reaction channel in differente physical situations, e.g. in the quark color superconducting cores of some neutron stars, are also discussed. We demonstrate that in the color-flavor-locked superconducting phase for temperatures T < (0.1-10) MeV (depending on the effective pion mass and the decay width) the process is feasibly the most efficient neutrino cooling process, although the absolute value of the reaction is rather small.

## Key claim

Nuclear medium effects suppress the rate of the neutrino cooling reaction channel γγ → π⁰ → ν_R ν̄_L (ν_L ν̄_R) in dense hadron matter by 6-7 orders of magnitude, preventing any improvement of the existing experimental upper limit on the π⁰νν̄ coupling; however, in the color-flavor-locked superconducting phase of quark matter cores for temperatures T ≲ (0.1-10) MeV, the process may become the most efficient neutrino cooling mechanism, though the absolute reaction rate remains rather small.

## Method

The authors computed the energy loss rate (emissivity) for the pion-pole mechanism γγ → π⁰ → νν̄ in dense nuclear matter by replacing the free pion propagator in the vacuum cross section with the in-medium retarded pion propagator, which incorporates the full retarded polarization operator Π^R_π⁰(ω,k,ρ,Y,T). The polarization operator includes the Δ-particle–nucleon hole contribution and a regular part constrained by pionic atom data, with the imaginary part accounting for strong-interaction pion absorption. The emissivity integral was evaluated numerically for baryon densities ρ = 0.5ρ₀, ρ₀, and 2ρ₀ over temperatures from 10 to 60 MeV, and the results were compared to the vacuum calculation. For the color-flavor-locked superconducting phase, the same formalism was applied using a modified pion propagator with a simplified dispersion relation ω² = k²/3 + m*²_π⁰ and a width dominated by the π⁰ → γγ decay.

## Key numbers

* The ratio Q^med/Q^vac (medium-suppressed to vacuum emissivity) decreases by 6-7 orders of magnitude depending on temperature and density, with the strongest suppression at ρ = 2ρ₀.
* For ρ = 0.5ρ₀, the dimensionless integral I(τ) takes values: 468277 at T=10 MeV, 605757 at T=20 MeV, 157430 at T=30 MeV, 41569 at T=40 MeV, 12907 at T=50 MeV, and 4713 at T=60 MeV.
* For ρ = ρ₀, I(τ) takes values: 115894 at T=10 MeV, 131020 at T=20 MeV, 38552 at T=30 MeV, 11663 at T=40 MeV, 4149 at T=50 MeV, and 1722 at T=60 MeV.
* For ρ = 2ρ₀, I(τ) takes values: 25594 at T=10 MeV, 22432 at T=20 MeV, 8165 at T=30 MeV, 3044 at T=40 MeV, 1293 at T=50 MeV, and 621 at T=60 MeV.
* The vacuum emissivity at low temperatures (τ ≲ 0.01) is Q^vac ≃ 0.6 × 10²⁰ (m_π⁻¹ Γ(π⁰ → νν̄)) T_9¹¹ erg/(cm³·s), where T_9 = T/10⁹ K.
* At high temperatures (τ ≫ (m_π/(10Γ_π))^{1/4}), both vacuum and medium emissivities approach Q^vac ≃ Q^med ≃ 2.6 × 10²⁹ (m_π⁻¹ Γ(π⁰ → νν̄)) T_9⁷ erg/(cm³·s).
* The modified Urca process emissivity is Q^MU ∼ 10²¹ T_9⁸ erg/(cm³·s) for comparison.
* In the color-flavor-locked phase, the emissivity Q^SC ∝ T¹¹ (m_π*)⁻⁴ at very low temperatures and ∝ T⁷ at high temperatures, with the interpolation formula given by Eq. (29) in the paper.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Fischbach et al. (1976) that the γγ → π⁰ → νν̄ reaction could be significant for stellar cooling, and addresses the criticism by Raffelt and Seckel (1988) that the pion width in dense matter (dominated by nucleon absorption rather than free decay) would strongly suppress the process. The authors use the in-medium pion polarization operator formalism developed by Migdal (1978), Migdal et al. (1990), and Voskresensky et al. (1995) to quantitatively confirm that suppression. The results show that no astrophysical limit on the neutrino mass better than existing experimental bounds can be obtained from this channel in hadronic matter. The discussion of the color-flavor-locked superconducting phase extends the work of Schafer and Wilczek (1999) and others on low-lying mesonic excitations in dense quark matter, suggesting a possible role for the pion-pole mechanism in cooling neutron stars with quark cores, though the absolute rate remains small.
