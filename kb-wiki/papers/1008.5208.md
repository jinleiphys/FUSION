# Poincaré Invariant Quantum Mechanics based on Euclidean Green functions

> We investigate a formulation of Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics where the dynamical input is Euclidean invariant Green functions or their generating functional. We argue that within this framework it is possible to calculate scattering observables, binding energies, and perform finite Poincaré transformations without using any analytic continuation. We demonstrate, using a toy model, how matrix elements of $e^{-βH}$ in normalizable states can be used to compute transition matrix elements for energies up to 2 GeV. We discuss some open problems.

## Key claim
The paper argues that Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics can be formulated using Euclidean Green functions or their generating functional as the dynamical input, enabling calculation of scattering observables, binding energies, and finite Poincaré transformations without any analytic continuation. The authors qualify that this is demonstrated only for a toy model and that constructing suitable model Green functions remains an open problem.

## Method
The authors construct a physical Hilbert space from Euclidean generating functionals using reflection positivity, then extract Poincaré generators (H, P, J, K) as self-adjoint operators by differentiating Euclidean transformations with respect to group parameters. For scattering calculations, they employ a two-Hilbert-space formulation of Haag-Ruelle scattering theory and use a Kato-Birman trick to replace the unitary time evolution operator \(e^{iHt}\) with powers of \(e^{-\beta H}\), which can be computed directly from the generating functional without analytic continuation. The method is tested on a separable potential model with a form factor \(\langle \mathbf{k} | g \rangle = 1/(m_\pi^2 + \mathbf{k}^2)\).

## Key numbers
- Transition matrix elements were computed for energies up to 2 GeV in the toy model.
- Sharp T-matrix elements were calculated with an error of approximately 1% using wave packets with momentum widths about 1/10 of the center-of-mass momentum.
- Values of \(n\) between 200 and 300 were adequate for convergence of the Kato-Birman approximation in the toy model.
- Polynomial approximations of degree approximately 300 agreed with \(e^{2ine^{-\beta H}}\) uniformly to better than 13 significant figures.

## Context
This work extends the reconstruction theorem of Osterwalder and Schrader (1973, 1974) and is strongly motivated by Fröhlich's (1974) solution of the reconstruction problem using generating functionals. The key observation from Osterwalder and Schrader that locality is not needed to construct the quantum theory is exploited here. The approach enables a direct relation to Lagrangian-based field theory models and provides a computational framework for relativistic few-body models, though constructing model Green functions satisfying reflection positivity remains an open problem requiring further study.
