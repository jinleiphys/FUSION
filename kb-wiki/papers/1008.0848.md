# New Nuclear Physics for Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We discuss nuclear reactions which could play a role in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN). Most of these reactions involve lithium and beryllium isotopes and the rates for some of these have not previously been included in BBN calculations. Few of these reactions are well studied in the laboratory. We also discuss novel effects in these reactions, including thermal population of nuclear target states, resonant enhancement, and non-thermal neutron reaction products. We perform sensitivity studies which show that even given considerable nuclear physics uncertainties, most of these nuclear reactions have minimal leverage on the standard BBN abundance yields of 6Li and 7Li. Although a few have the potential to alter the yields significantly, we argue that this is unlikely.

## Key claim

The paper argues that most nuclear reactions involving lithium and beryllium isotopes, even when considered with large rate uncertainties, have minimal leverage on standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) abundance yields of ⁶Li and ⁷Li; although a few reactions have the potential to alter yields significantly, the authors argue this is unlikely given realistic nuclear physics constraints.

## Method

The authors added 25 new or previously unconstrained nuclear reactions to a standard BBN reaction network code, focusing on reactions involving ⁷Li, ⁷Be, ³H, and ³He. They calculated thermonuclear reaction rates from experimental S-factors where available, or estimated rates from analogous reactions and resonance parameters, then performed sensitivity studies by multiplying uncertain rates by factors of 10 and 1000 to assess their maximum possible impact on final abundances. They also modeled non-thermal neutron effects from the ³H(d,n)⁴He reaction using a power-law parameterization of the n-p scattering cross section and considered thermal population of the first excited state of ⁷Be (⁷Be*) at 0.429 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ⁷Be first excited state (⁷Be*) has excitation energy 0.4292 MeV; its thermal population factor at T₉ ≈ 1 is P* ≈ 3×10⁻³
- The S-factor for ⁷Li(³He,⁴He)⁶Li to ground state is 17.2 MeV-barn; to the second excited state it is 12.3 MeV-barn, giving a total of 29.5 MeV-barn
- The S-factor for ⁷Li(³He,p)⁹Be(ground state) is 5.3 MeV-barn at energies below 100 keV
- The S-factor for ⁷Li(³H,⁴He)⁶He(ground state) is 0.84 MeV-barn; to the first excited state it is 13.9 MeV-barn
- The ³He(³H,γ)⁶Li reaction rate is given as N_A⟨σv⟩ = 1.02×10⁷ T₉⁻²/³ e⁻⁷·⁷²⁹ T₉⁻¹/³ cm³ s⁻¹ mole⁻¹
- Increasing the ⁷Be(d,p)⁸Be rate by a factor of 100 reduced the ⁷Be yield by approximately 30%, insufficient to achieve the required reduction factor of roughly 3
- The n-p total cross section parameterization uses σ₀ = 0.685 barns at E₀ = 14.1 MeV with exponent α = −0.834, reproducing data within 10% for 3 < E < 20 MeV
- The ⁶He ground state beta-decays with a half-life of 807 ms to ⁶Li; its laboratory beta decay rate is approximately 0.9 s⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive nuclear reaction study of Nollett et al. (1996) and the exploration of nuclear solutions to the ⁷Li problem by Cyburt and Pospelov (2009), who suggested resonant enhancement of ⁷Be(d,p)2α could reduce ⁷Li yield by a factor of 3. The paper adds new reactions not previously included in BBN codes, particularly those involving radioactive targets like ⁷Be and ³H, and examines non-thermal neutron effects not fully treated in earlier work by Voronchev (2008). The results enable future BBN calculations to incorporate these rates and sensitivity limits, and they clarify that standard nuclear physics cannot resolve the ⁶Li and ⁷Li discrepancies, leaving non-standard particle physics scenarios as the primary avenue for explanation.
