# Coupled-channels Faddeev AGS calculation of $K^{-}ppn$ and $K^{-}ppp$ quasi-bound states

> Using separable $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ potentials in the Faddeev equations, we calculated the binding energies and widths of the $K^{-}pp$, $K^{-}ppn$ and $K^{-}ppp$ quasi-bound states on the basis of three- and four-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations in the momentum representation. One- and two-pole version of $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interaction are considered and the dependence of the resulting few-body energy on the two-body $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ potential was investigated. The $s$-wave [3+1] and [2+2] sub-amplitudes are obtained by using the Hilbert-Schmidt expansion procedure for the integral kernels. As a result, we found a four-body resonance of the $K^{-}ppn$ and $K^{-}ppp$ quasi-bound states with a binding energy in the range $B_{K^{-}ppn}\sim{55-70}$ and $B_{K^{-}ppp}\sim{90-100}$ MeV, respectively. The calculations yielded full width of $Γ_{K^{-}ppn}\sim{16-20}$ and $Γ_{K^{-}ppp}\sim{7-20}$ MeV.

## Key claim
Using coupled-channel Faddeev Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations with separable \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) potentials, the authors find four-body quasi-bound states for \(K^{-}ppn\) and \(K^{-}ppp\), but the binding energies and widths depend on whether a one-pole or two-pole version of the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\) interaction is used, and the results are obtained under the assumption that \(\Sigma N-\Lambda N\) and \(\pi N\) interactions can be neglected.

## Method
The authors solved three- and four-body AGS equations in momentum representation using separable \(s\)-wave potentials for the \(\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma\), \(NN\), and \(\Sigma N\) subsystems. The Hilbert-Schmidt expansion (HSE) method was used to obtain separable representations of the [3+1] and [2+2] sub-amplitudes, reducing the integral equations to single-variable form. The pole positions of the quasi-bound states were found by searching for complex energies where the first eigenvalue of the kernel matrix equals one.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of \(K^{-}pp\): \(B_{K^{-}pp} \sim 45-55\) MeV (range from one-pole and two-pole potentials, Table IV).
- Binding energy of \(K^{-}ppn\): \(B_{K^{-}ppn} \sim 55-70\) MeV (range from one-pole and two-pole potentials, Table IV).
- Binding energy of \(K^{-}ppp\): \(B_{K^{-}ppp} \sim 90-100\) MeV (range from one-pole and two-pole potentials, Table IV).
- Width of \(K^{-}ppn\): \(\Gamma_{K^{-}ppn} \sim 16-20\) MeV (range from one-pole and two-pole potentials, Table IV).
- Width of \(K^{-}ppp\): \(\Gamma_{K^{-}ppp} \sim 7-20\) MeV (range from one-pole and two-pole potentials, Table IV).
- For the one-pole potential \(V^{SIDD}_{One-pole}\), the \(K^{-}pp\) pole is at \(-48.7-i34.3\) MeV, \(K^{-}ppn\) at \(-68.8-i11.0\) MeV, and \(K^{-}ppp\) at \(-99.6-i10.5\) MeV (Table IV).
- For the two-pole potential \(V^{SIDD}_{Two-pole}\), the \(K^{-}pp\) pole is at \(-45.4-i24.4\) MeV, \(K^{-}ppn\) at \(-55.9-i8.8\) MeV, and \(K^{-}ppp\) at \(-87.8-i3.5\) MeV (Table IV).
- The \(^{3}\mathrm{He}\) binding energy calculated with the PEST \(NN\) potential is 9.7 MeV (experimental value 8.54 MeV).

## Context
This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of the three-body \(K^{-}pp\) system (Shevchenko 2007, Ikeda 2008) to the four-body \(K^{-}ppn\) and \(K^{-}ppp\) systems using the AGS formalism. It contrasts with variational calculations by Gal (2014) and Roman (2015) that found shallower binding (\(\sim 30\) MeV) for \(K^{-}ppn\), and with Faddeev results by Maeda (2013) using a one-channel real potential that found binding near 69 MeV. The results enable future calculations of formation cross sections for kaon-induced reactions, such as the \(^{3}\mathrm{He}(K^{-},N)\) reaction at J-PARC.
