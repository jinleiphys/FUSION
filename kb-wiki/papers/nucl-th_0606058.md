# Neutral current neutrino-nucleus interactions at intermediate energies

> We have extended our model for charged current neutrino-nucleus interactions to neutral current reactions. For the elementary neutrino-nucleon interaction, we take into account quasielastic scattering, Delta excitation and the excitation of the resonances in the second resonance region. Our model for the neutrino-nucleus collisions includes in-medium effects such as Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, nuclear binding, and final-state interactions. They are implemented by means of the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) coupled-channel transport model. This allows us to study exclusive channels, namely pion production and nucleon knockout. We find that final-state interactions modify considerably the distributions through rescattering, charge-exchange and absorption. Side-feeding induced by charge-exchange scattering is important in both cases. In the case of pions, there is a strong absorption associated with the in-medium pionless decay modes of the Delta, while nucleon knockout exhibits a considerable enhancement of low energy nucleons due to rescattering. At neutrino energies above 1 GeV, we also obtain that the contribution to nucleon knockout from Delta excitation is comparable to that from quasielastic scattering.

## Key claim

The authors extend their charged-current neutrino-nucleus model to neutral-current reactions, finding that final-state interactions (rescattering, charge-exchange, and absorption) significantly modify pion and nucleon distributions, with the caveat that the strange axial form factor remains poorly constrained and the results depend on the assumed value of Δs.

## Method

The elementary neutrino-nucleon interactions (quasielastic scattering, Δ(1232) excitation, and resonances P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535)) are modeled using relativistic form factors and the Rein-Sehgal model. Nuclear effects (Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, binding, and final-state interactions) are implemented via the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) coupled-channel transport model, which propagates particles in a mean-field potential and includes collision terms for rescattering, absorption, and charge exchange.

## Key numbers

- For the axial dipole mass in quasielastic scattering, the authors use MA = 1.0 GeV.
- The strange axial form factor is parameterized as FA^s(Q^2) = Δs / (1 + Q^2/MA^2)^2, with representative values Δs = -0.15 and Δs = 0.
- The weak-mixing angle is taken as sin^2 θW = 0.2228.
- For higher resonances, the dipole masses are MA = 1.032 GeV and MV = 0.84 GeV.
- The branching ratios for the second-resonance-region decays to πN are b1 = 0.69 (P11), b2 = 0.59 (D13), and b3 = 0.51 (S11).
- At neutrino energies above 1 GeV, the contribution to nucleon knockout from Δ excitation is comparable to that from quasielastic scattering.
- The Δ potential in the nucleus is set to approximately -30 MeV, versus the nucleon potential of approximately -45 MeV at normal nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' charged-current model (Leitner, Alvarez-Ruso, Mosel, Phys. Rev. C 73, 065502, 2006) to neutral currents, incorporating a coupled-channel transport approach that goes beyond simpler Glauber or distorted-wave impulse-approximation treatments. It confirms the importance of side-feeding effects from charge-exchange scattering, as previously noted by Nieves et al. (2005), and provides a framework for interpreting neutrino oscillation experiments (K2K, MiniBooNE, MINERνA) and for extracting the strange axial form factor, which is needed for future experiments like FINeSSE.
