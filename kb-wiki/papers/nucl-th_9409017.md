# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations and Six-Quark Cluster Effects in Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Lepton Scattering off Few-Nucleon Systems

> Semi-inclusive deep inelastic lepton scattering off few-nucleon systems is investigated assuming that virtual boson absorption occurs on a hadronic cluster which can be either a two-nucleon correlated pair or a six-quark bag. In both cases the relevance of nuclear effects on forward and backward nucleon emissions is illustrated and the differences expected in the energy distribution of the emitted nucleons are analyzed both at x>1 and x<1.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations and Six-Quark Cluster Effects in Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Lepton Scattering off Few-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off few-nucleon systems can distinguish between two competing short-range hadronic structures, two-nucleon correlated pairs versus six-quark bags, but the differences in the emitted nucleon energy distribution are not sharp for kinetic energies between 50 and 250 MeV; only at higher momenta (p2 > 1 GeV/c) do six-quark cluster effects become clearly distinguishable from the spectator mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculate the semi-inclusive nuclear structure function for the process A(e,e'N)X using two models: (1) the two-nucleon correlation (2NC) model, where the virtual photon is absorbed by a quark in a nucleon belonging to a correlated NN pair, and the recoiling nucleon is detected (spectator mechanism); and (2) a six-quark bag model, where the photon is absorbed by a quark in a colorless 6q cluster, which then fragments into a detected nucleon and a penta-quark residuum. For each model, both a simple version (hadronic cluster at rest relative to the residual A-2 system) and an extended version (including center-of-mass motion of the cluster) are evaluated, using the free nucleon structure function of Gluck, Hoffmann, and Reya (1982) and relative/c.m. momentum distributions from Morita (1991). Calculations are performed for 4He targets at x<1 and x>1.

## Key numbers

- The cross section for backward nucleon emission at x<1 and forward emission at x>1 drops by orders of magnitude as the detected nucleon kinetic energy T2 increases above 250 MeV (p2 > 0.7 GeV/c).
- The mean light-cone momentum <z1> of the struck nucleon predicted by the extended 2NC model is larger than the 2NC model value by up to approximately 0.25, corresponding to a c.m. momentum k_c.m. ~ sqrt(<k^2_c.m.>_soft) ~ k_F ~ 0.25 GeV/c.
- At x>1, backward nucleon emission is kinematically forbidden in the 2NC model (k_c.m. = 0) and strongly suppressed in the extended 2NC model; forward emission at x>1 requires x > 1 + k_F/M ~ 1.3 for the semi-inclusive DIS cross section to strongly depend on nuclear effects.
- Six-quark cluster effects become clearly distinguishable from the spectator mechanism only when the detected nucleon momentum p2 > 1 GeV/c.
- The break-up probability of the 5q system into mesons or baryons is assumed to be 50%, corresponding to gamma_(5q) = 2.

## Context

This work extends the spectator-mechanism analysis of Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1993) from complex nuclei to few-nucleon systems (deuteron, 3He, 4He), and generalizes it to include six-quark bag configurations following the approach of Carlson and collaborators (1991) for the deuteron. The paper addresses the discrepancy between the predictions of the original 2NC model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1981) and experimental data on single-proton backward production in neutrino-neon scattering from the WA59 collaboration (Matsinos et al., 1989), showing that the c.m. motion of the correlated pair resolves much of the disagreement without invoking intranuclear cascade or few-nucleon correlations. The results enable future experiments at higher momentum transfer to probe the short-range structure of hadronic matter and the origin of the EMC effect, as discussed by Dounas (1991).
