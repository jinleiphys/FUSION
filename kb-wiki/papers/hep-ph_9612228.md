# Chiral dynamics of rare eta decays and virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon

> Two different topics relevant for DA$Φ$NE and CEBAF, respectively, are covered. First we analyze the rare radiative eta decay mode $η\toππγγ$ within the framework of chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$. We then discuss virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon at low energies. Predictions for the two spin-independent generalized polarizabilities are shown.

# Chiral dynamics of rare eta decays and virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$ provides a lowest-order prediction for the rare decay $\eta\to\pi\pi\gamma\gamma$, but the decay rates are dominated by pole contributions (the $\pi^0$ pole for the neutral mode and the bremsstrahlung singularity for the charged mode), and ${\cal O}(p^6)$ counter terms or loop contributions may be important for a realistic description. For virtual Compton scattering, the two spin-independent generalized polarizabilities can be expressed in terms of known quantities up to second order in momenta, with chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^3)$ giving predictions that agree well with experimental real Compton scattering polarizabilities.

## Method
The rare decay $\eta\to\pi\pi\gamma\gamma$ was analyzed using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian ${\cal L}_2$ plus the Wess-Zumino-Witten action, without including ${\cal L}_4$ of Gasser and Leutwyler since it contributes only at ${\cal O}(p^6)$ for this odd-number-of-Goldstone-boson electromagnetic process. For virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon, model-independent predictions for the amplitude functions $A_i$ were obtained based on Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, and discrete symmetries, and the spin-independent generalized polarizabilities $\alpha(|\vec{q}|)$ and $\beta(|\vec{q}|)$ were calculated in chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^3)$.

## Key numbers
- $\eta$ total width: $\Gamma = (1.18 \pm 0.11)$ keV
- Branching fractions for $\eta$ decays: $2\gamma$ ($39.25 \pm 0.31$%), $3\pi^0$ ($32.1 \pm 0.4$%), $\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ ($23.2 \pm 0.5$%), $\pi^+\pi^-\gamma$ ($4.78 \pm 0.12$%)
- Upper limit for $\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\gamma$: $\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\gamma)/\Gamma < 2.1 \times 10^{-3}$
- Anticipated $\eta$ production at DA$\Phi$NE: $\sim 3.2 \times 10^8$ etas per year
- Pion decay constant used: $F_\pi = 93$ MeV; $F_8 = 1.25 F_\pi$; $F_1 = 1.06 F_\pi$
- $\eta-\eta'$ mixing angle: $\theta = -20^\circ$
- ChPT ${\cal O}(p^3)$ predictions for real Compton scattering polarizabilities: $\alpha_0 = 12.8 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, $\beta_0 = 1.3 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
- Experimental values: $\alpha_0 = (12.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, $\beta_0 = (2.1 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$
- ChPT ${\cal O}(p^3)$ expansion for generalized polarizabilities: $\alpha(|\vec{q}|) = \alpha_0 [1 - (7/50)(|\vec{q}|^2/m_\pi^2) + O(|\vec{q}|^4/m_\pi^4)]$, $\beta(|\vec{q}|) = \beta_0 [1 + (1/5)(|\vec{q}|^2/m_\pi^2) + O(|\vec{q}|^4/m_\pi^4)]$

## Context
This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory to rare $\eta$ decays relevant for the DA$\Phi$NE facility, following the framework established by Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for the meson sector, and the heavy-baryon formulation for the nucleon sector. The $\eta\to\pi\pi\gamma\gamma$ analysis builds on earlier work by Knöchlein, Scherer, and Drechsel (1996) and notes that chiral pion loops at ${\cal O}(p^6)$ (Bellucci and Isidori, 1996) lead to notable enhancement. For virtual Compton scattering, the work provides model-independent constraints on generalized polarizabilities, reducing the number of independent spin-independent polarizabilities from three to two based on charge conjugation invariance and nucleon crossing (Drechsel, Knöchlein, Metz, and Scherer, 1996), and gives ChPT predictions that can be tested at CEBAF.
