# Strange Antibaryons from QGP

> We study as function of the collision energy and stopping the thermal conditions reached in a quark-gluon plasma fireball formed in a relativistic heavy ion collision. We explore strange particle yields for the current round of Pb-Pb and Au-Au experiments.

# Strange Antibaryons from QGP

## Key claim

The thermal conditions reached in a quark-gluon plasma fireball formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be determined by balancing the thermal pressure of the deconfined quarks and gluons against the dynamical compression exerted by in-flowing nuclear matter, provided that the stopping fractions for energy, baryon number, and momentum are approximately equal and that the fireball hadronizes directly without full re-equilibration.

## Method

The authors develop a first-principle model in which the initial fireball conditions are set by solving the pressure balance equation between the QGP thermal pressure (computed using perturbative QCD with thermal masses) and the dynamical compression pressure from in-flowing matter plus a vacuum pressure term (bag constant B ≈ 0.1 GeV/fm³). The energy per baryon in the fireball is taken equal to the kinematic energy available in the collision, assuming equal stopping fractions for energy and baryon number. The fireball is evolved at constant pressure and energy per baryon, with chemical equilibration rates for light quarks and gluons set equal, while strange quarks equilibrate more slowly (γ_s → 0.15 initially, then relaxed to near equilibrium in the final stage). Strange particle ratios are computed assuming direct hadronization of the QGP.

## Key numbers

- For Au–Au at AGS: E/B = 2.6 GeV; for S–W/Pb at CERN: E/B = 8.8 GeV; for Pb–Pb at CERN: E/B = 8.6 GeV.
- Vacuum pressure (bag constant) used: B ≈ 0.1 GeV/fm³.
- Strange quark chemical relaxation constant is about 7 times smaller than that for light quarks.
- At AGS energies, the absolute yield of strange antibaryons is typically a factor 100 smaller compared to SPS energies.
- The three strange antibaryon ratios (anti-Lambda/anti-proton, anti-Xi-/anti-Lambda, anti-Omega/anti-Xi-) all increase as collision energy is reduced, and at fixed m⊥ ≥ 1.7 GeV all ratios are greater than unity.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Letessier, Rafelski, and Tounsi (1994) that pressure balance between thermal QGP and dynamical compression determines freeze-out conditions. It provides a systematic dynamical model to describe strange particle yields measured by the WA85/94 and NA35 collaborations in 200 GeV A sulphur-nucleus collisions, and makes predictions for the then-upcoming Pb–Pb and Au–Au experiments. The approach contrasts with scenarios where full re-equilibration during hadronization would erase QGP memory; here the rapid disintegration of the central fireball preserves information about the primordial QGP state. The model enables quantitative comparison of QGP-fireball predictions with experimental results across the AGS and SPS energy ranges.
