# Heavy baryons in the Skyrme model: the role of highly anharmonic collective motion

> In the heavy quark and large Nc limits, ordinary (exotic) heavy baryons can be considered as bound states of heavy mesons (anti-mesons)and chiral solitons. In these limits, the heavy mesons (or anti-mesons) and the chiral solitons are extremely heavy and are presumed to fall to the bottom of the effective potential. Previous studies have approximated the effective potential as harmonic about the minimum. However, when realistic masses for the heavy meson and chiral soliton are considered, longer range parts of the effective potential can become relevant. In this paper, we show that these longer-ranged effects yield effective wave functions which are qualitatively very different from those expected from the combined large Nc and heavy quark limits. These potentials can support bound heavy pentaquarks under some conditions. The consequence of these new energy states and wave functions on the Isgur-Wise function for the semileptonic decay of heavy baryons is also considered.

# Heavy baryons in the Skyrme model: the role of highly anharmonic collective motion

## Key claim

When realistic masses for the heavy meson and chiral soliton are considered instead of the idealized heavy quark and large Nc limits, the longer-range parts of the effective potential become relevant and yield wave functions that are qualitatively different from those expected in the combined limits; these potentials can support bound heavy pentaquarks under some conditions, but the simplest Skyrme-type interactions do not create bound states with realistic binding energies for ordinary heavy baryons.

## Method

The authors derived the complete effective potential for the collective degree of freedom between a heavy meson and a chiral soliton in the Skyrme model, using heavy quark effective theory (HQET) for the heavy meson sector and the standard Skyrme Lagrangian for the soliton sector. They solved the Schrodinger equation with the full radial potential (rather than the harmonic approximation used in prior work) for the isospin-0 light quark spin-0 and spin-1 channels, using a profile function constructed by minimizing the soliton mass while keeping the nucleon-pion coupling gA and the pion decay constant fpi fixed. The Skyrme parameter was determined to be e = 4.10 and the soliton mass M = 949 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Heavy meson-soliton coupling g = 0.59, measured from D* meson decays into D mesons and pion emissions (CLEO collaboration)
- For the charm case (D meson mass 1864 MeV): binding energy of 155 MeV for the ground state Lambda_c, with a weakly bound radial excited state at 6.18 MeV and an l=1 excited state at 42.0 MeV
- For the bottom case (B meson mass 5279 MeV): binding energy of 177 MeV for the ground state Lambda_b, with a weakly bound radial excited state at 19.32 MeV and an l=1 excited state at 68.0 MeV
- No bound l=2 states were found for either charm or bottom systems
- No bound states were found in the light quark spin-1 channel for either charm or bottom

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model treatment of heavy baryons by Jenkins, Manohar, and Wise (1993) and others, who approximated the effective potential as harmonic about its minimum. The authors show that this harmonic approximation breaks down for realistic masses, producing wave functions that extend well beyond the harmonic region. The results also connect to the model-independent argument by Hohler (2006) that bound heavy pentaquarks must exist in the extreme limits, but show that for physical masses the existence of such states is sensitive to short-distance details. The findings enable future calculations of the Isgur-Wise function for semileptonic decays of heavy baryons that incorporate the non-universal, interaction-dependent form arising from the full potential.
