# Quantification of nuclear uncertainties in nucleosynthesis of elements beyond Iron

> Nucleosynthesis beyond Fe poses additional challenges not encountered when studying astrophysical processes involving light nuclei. Generally higher temperatures and nuclear level densities lead to stronger contributions of transitions on excited target states. This may prevent cross section measurements to determine stellar reaction rates and theory contributions remain important. Furthermore, measurements often are not feasible in the astrophysically relevant energy range. Sensitivity analysis allows not only to determine the contributing nuclear properties but also is a handy tool for experimentalists to interpret the impact of their data on predicted cross sections and rates. It can also speed up future input variation studies of nucleosynthesis by simplifying an intermediate step in the full calculation sequence. Large-scale predictions of sensitivities and ground-state contributions to the stellar rates are presented, allowing an estimate of how well rates can be directly constrained by experiment. The reactions 185W(n,gamma) and 186W(gamma,n) are discussed as application examples. Studies of uncertainties in abundances predicted in nucleosynthesis simulations rely on the knowledge of reaction rate errors. An improved treatment of uncertainty analysis is presented as well as a recipe for combining experimental data and theory to arrive at a new reaction rate and its uncertainty. As an example, it is applied to neutron capture rates for the s-process, leading to larger uncertainties than previously assumed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that experimental uncertainties cannot be directly adopted as stellar reaction rate uncertainties because excited-state contributions and theoretical model errors must be accounted for, leading to larger uncertainties than previously assumed for s-process neutron capture rates.

## Method
The author defines sensitivity factors s for nuclear reaction model input quantities and ground-state contributions X_0 to stellar rates, then uses these to derive a formalism for combining experimental data with theoretical predictions. This formalism is applied to neutron capture rates for the s-process, using the reactions 185W(n,γ) and 186W(γ,n) as worked examples.

## Key numbers
- For 185W(n,γ): ground-state contribution X_0 = 0.98 at kT = 8 keV and X_0 = 0.75 at kT = 30 keV, two important s-process energies.
- For 186W(γ,n): ground-state contribution X_0 = 7 × 10^{-3} at T_9 = 0.1 and X_0 = 5 × 10^{-3} at T_9 = 0.4, showing negligible contribution to the stellar rate.
- The new uncertainty factors for stellar (n,γ) rates at kT = 30 keV are considerably larger than experimental uncertainties which are of the order of 5% or lower.

## Context
This work extends the sensitivity analysis framework introduced by Rauscher (2012) and provides large-scale tabulations of sensitivities and ground-state contributions for compound reactions between Ne and Bi. It contradicts the common assumption that high-precision measurements directly constrain stellar rates, showing that for s-process neutron capture rates the true uncertainties are larger. The results enable improved Monte Carlo variation studies of nucleosynthesis and were used to explain observed Eu isotope ratios in stars and presolar grains (Avila, year not given; Eu proceedings, year not given).
