# Does the fireball found in AuAu collisions at RHIC resemble plasma?

> General properties of the hot and dense hadron matter (fireball) discovered in AuAu collisions with pSNN = 130 and 200 GeV at RHIC are compared with these for ideal electromagnetic plasma. None of them was found to be in contrary to those of the plasma typical signs distinguishing its from other aggregate states of the matter. The author notes that modern experimental data about the fireball properties are limited to make their comprehensive comparison with plasma signatures. The author also points out the directions needed to be studied to answer question whether the hadron matter observed in the RHIC experiments is a color analogue of the electromagnetic plasma or not.

# Does the fireball found in AuAu collisions at RHIC resemble plasma?

## Key claim

The hot and dense hadron matter (fireball) produced in AuAu collisions at RHIC exhibits properties that are not in contradiction with those of ideal electromagnetic plasma, but the existing experimental data are insufficient to make a comprehensive comparison. The author concludes that the fireball medium resembles ordinary electron plasma more closely than the image of QGP derived from simple quark descriptions, yet definitive identification requires further experiments on wave excitations, color charge screening, and direct temperature measurements.

## Method

The author qualitatively compares the general properties of the fireball observed in AuAu collisions at √sNN = 130 and 200 GeV at RHIC against five characteristic signatures of ideal electromagnetic plasma: quasineutrality, charge dissociation (deconfinement), collective particle interactions, wave motions (quasi-particle degrees of freedom), and charge screening. The analysis draws on experimental results from elliptic flow measurements, charmonium suppression data at SPS and RHIC, and parton energy loss studies, evaluating whether each plasma property has a corresponding analogue in the fireball medium.

## Key numbers

- AuAu collisions at √sNN = 130 and 200 GeV at RHIC are the systems analyzed.
- The characteristic energy density required for color charge separation is at the order of several GeV/fm³.
- The fireball constituents interact with hadrons approximately 50 times stronger than ordinary hadron matter, motivating the term "strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP)."
- SPS charmonium suppression experiments were performed at √sNN = 17 GeV.
- The Debye screening radius rD is identified as the characteristic scale for charge screening in plasma.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of whether the matter created at RHIC constitutes a quark-gluon plasma by comparing it directly to classical electromagnetic plasma signatures, a perspective the author notes has not been previously explored in this configuration. The analysis builds on hydrodynamic studies of elliptic flow (Heinz and Kolb, 2004) and parton energy loss models (Zakharov, 2001) that first indicated collective behavior and quasi-particle excitations in the fireball medium. The paper points to needed future measurements of thermal photon spectra for direct temperature determination and improved precision in charmonium suppression studies at RHIC and LHC to resolve whether the fireball is a color analogue of electromagnetic plasma.
