# Effect of isovector scalar meson on equation of state of dense matter within relativistic mean field model

> The effects of the isovector-scalar $δ$-meson field on the properties of finite nuclei, infinite nuclear matter and neutron stars are investigated within the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model which includes non-linear couplings. Several parameter sets (SRV's) are generated to asses the influence of $δ$-meson on the properties of neutron star. These parametrizations correspond to different values of coupling constant of $δ$-meson to the nucleons with remaining ones calibrated to yield finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter properties consistent with the available experimental data. It is observed that to fit the properties of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter, a stronger coupling between isovector-vector $ρ$ meson and nucleons is required in the presence of $δ$ field. Furthermore, the $δ$-meson is found to affect the radius of canonical neutron star significantly. The value of dimensionless tidal deformability, $Λ$ for the canonical neutron star also satisfies the constraints from the waveform models analysis of GW170817 binary neutron star merger event. A covariance analysis is performed to estimate the statistical uncertainties of the model parameters as well as correlations among the model parameters and different observables of interest.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the isovector-scalar delta-meson field in a relativistic mean field model significantly affects the radius of a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star, but this conclusion is conditional on fixing the delta-meson coupling constant to discrete values (g_delta = 0.0, 1.0, 2.0, 3.0, 4.0) while optimizing all other parameters, rather than allowing g_delta itself to be a free parameter in the fit.

## Method

The authors generated five new relativistic mean field parameter sets (SRV00 through SRV04) by fixing the delta-meson coupling g_delta to values from 0.0 to 4.0 and optimizing the remaining coupling constants to reproduce experimental binding energies and charge radii of spherical nuclei from oxygen to lead, along with the neutron skin thickness of Pb-208. The equation of state for neutron star matter was then computed in beta-equilibrium, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities. A covariance analysis was performed to estimate statistical uncertainties and correlations among model parameters and observables.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.149 fm^{-3} for all five SRV parametrizations, with uncertainties of 0.002-0.008 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon E/A ranges from -16.09 to -16.12 MeV across the five parametrizations
- Incompressibility K ranges from 221.11 to 223.94 MeV across the five parametrizations
- Symmetry energy J ranges from 33.31 to 33.75 MeV across the five parametrizations
- Slope of symmetry energy L decreases from 65.23 MeV (SRV00, g_delta=0.0) to 55.31 MeV (SRV04, g_delta=4.0)
- Maximum gravitational mass M_G ranges from 2.04 to 2.13 solar masses across the five parametrizations
- Radius of 1.4 solar mass neutron star R_1.4 ranges from 12.92 km (SRV00) to 13.86 km (SRV04), an increase of 7.27 percent
- Dimensionless tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 ranges from 484 (SRV00) to 784 (SRV04)
- Neutron skin thickness of Pb-208 varies only slightly from 0.222 fm (SRV00) to 0.214 fm (SRV04)

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean field studies of the delta-meson effect on neutron star properties, particularly following the approach of Li et al. (2022) who also fixed the delta coupling while optimizing other parameters. The study is motivated by recent observational constraints from the GW170817 binary neutron star merger event, the NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0740+6620, and the parity-violating electron scattering measurement of the Pb-208 neutron skin thickness by the PREX collaboration (Adhikari 2021). The results enable future full optimizations where the delta-meson coupling itself is treated as a free parameter, and suggest that higher-order mixed scalar interactions of the delta meson could provide additional flexibility in the high-density equation of state.
