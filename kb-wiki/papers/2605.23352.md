# Intertwined quantum phase transitions in the even-even $^{90-100}$Sr isotopes

> The even-even $^{90-100}$Sr isotopes are identified as a region of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs). In this scenario, a quantum phase transition involving the crossing of normal and intruder configurations is accompanied by a shape evolution within the intruder configuration. Using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), we show that the strontium chain exhibits coexisting Type I and Type II QPTs, where the intruder configuration evolves from a near-spherical structure in $^{90-96}$Sr to a deformed one in $^{98,100}$Sr, while the normal and intruder configurations cross between $^{96}$Sr and $^{98}$Sr. As a result, the ground state changes abruptly from a weakly collective normal configuration to a deformed intruder configuration. Evidence for this scenario is provided by a detailed comparison with experimental excitation energies, spectroscopic quadrupole moments, isotope shifts, and monopole $E0$ transition strengths, together with the configuration and $n_d$ decompositions of the calculated wave functions. The results place the strontium isotopes alongside the neighboring zirconium chain as another realization of IQPTs in the intricate $A\approx100$ region.

# Intertwined quantum phase transitions in the even-even $^{90-100}$Sr isotopes

## Key claim

The even-even $^{90-100}$Sr isotopes exhibit intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), where a Type II QPT (configuration crossing between normal and intruder configurations) occurs simultaneously with a Type I QPT (shape evolution within the intruder configuration itself), with the critical point for both lying between $^{96}$Sr and $^{98}$Sr. The mixing between configurations remains weak throughout, allowing the two types of QPTs to be distinguished.

## Method

The interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) was employed with two configurations: a normal configuration ($0p$-$2h$ proton excitations relative to the $Z=40$ subshell) and an intruder configuration ($2p$-$4h$ proton excitations). The Hamiltonian parameters were kept constant or followed simple trends across the chain, with the normal configuration parameters held constant and the intruder configuration governed by a stronger quadrupole interaction ($\kappa^{(A)} \approx \frac{1}{6} \kappa^{(B)}$). The calculated results were compared with experimental excitation energies, spectroscopic quadrupole moments, isotope shifts, and monopole $E0$ transition strengths, alongside configuration and $n_d$ decompositions of the wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The normal configuration content $v^2_{(N_A,0^+_1)}$ exceeds 99% for $^{90-96}$Sr, then inverts abruptly at $^{98}$Sr where the ground state is about 89% intruder
- The $2^+_1$ energy drops from 0.815 MeV at neutron number 58 to 0.145 MeV at neutron number 60, marking the transition to a collective-deformed intruder ground state
- The energy offset $\Delta_p$ decreases gradually from 3.0 MeV at neutron number 52 to 1.8 MeV at 58, then drops suddenly to 0.8 MeV at neutron numbers 60-62 due to the Type II QPT
- The $E2$ transition $2^+_1 \to 0^+_1$ in $^{98}$Sr is 96(3) W.u.
- The $0^+_2 \to 0^+_1$ monopole transition strength in $^{98}$Sr is 53(5)
- The $E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)$ ratio in $^{98}$Sr is approximately 3.01, indicating a strongly deformed rotational sequence
- The mixing parameter $\omega = 0.017$ MeV remains constant across the chain
- The isotope shift parameter $\eta = 0.053$ fm$^2$ is about half the zirconium value (0.12 fm$^2$)

## Context

This work extends the IQPT framework previously established for even-even zirconium isotopes (Gavrielov 2019, 2020, 2022) and subsequently demonstrated in odd-mass niobium (Gavrielov 2022c, 2023a) and zirconium (Gavrielov 2025) isotopes to the strontium chain, placing them alongside zirconium in a unified picture of the $A \approx 100$ region. The results reinforce the necessity of incorporating multiple configurations and analyzing their individual shape evolution to understand nuclear structure in this region, and open the way for analogous studies of odd-mass yttrium ($Z=39$) and strontium isotopes by coupling a proton or neutron to the strontium boson core.
