# Exact solution of Boltzmann equation in a longitudinal expanding system

> Analytical solutions to the microscopic Boltzmann equation are useful in testing the applicability and accuracy of macroscopic hydrodynamic theory. In this work, we present exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, based on a new family of exact solutions of the relativistic ideal hydrodynamic equations [Phys. Rev. C 105, L021902].. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first exact solution that allows longitudinal expansion with broken boost invariance.

# Exact solution of Boltzmann equation in a longitudinal expanding system

## Key claim

The paper presents exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation for a longitudinally expanding system, claiming this is the first exact solution that allows longitudinal expansion with broken boost invariance. The solutions are derived by finding the co-moving frame of a newly discovered family of exact solutions to the ideal relativistic hydrodynamic equations from Shi (2022), and the claim is hedged by the restriction to conformal (massless) particles and homogeneity in the transverse plane.

## Method

The authors start from a family of exact solutions to the ideal relativistic hydrodynamic equations in Milne coordinates, characterized by a parameter a that controls asymmetry between forward and backward rapidity and a time constant t0 that translates Minkowski time. They construct a new coordinate system (the co-moving frame) in which the ideal hydrodynamic solution becomes static, then write the Boltzmann equation with relaxation time approximation in this frame. For the symmetric case (a=1), the Boltzmann equation reduces to a form solvable by the formal solution method of Baym (1984) and Florkowski (2013), yielding integral equations for the effective temperature and shear viscous stress tensor that are solved numerically and analyzed asymptotically.

## Key numbers

* The speed of sound for the conformal system is c_s = 1/√3, with energy density ε = 3T^4/π^2 and pressure P = T^4/π^2
* For the symmetric case (a=1) at central rapidity, the ideal temperature decays as T(τ) = T_i (τ_i/(τ+t0))^(1/3), which approaches the Bjorken limit when τ ≫ t0
* The long-time asymptotic behavior of the scaled temperature is T_eff/T_ideal = e_η̄ - (2η̄/3)(τ_i/τ)^(2/3) + O(τ^(-4/3)), where e_η̄ is a viscosity-dependent constant greater than unity
* The long-time asymptotic behavior of the scaled shear viscous stress tensor is (π^xx τ)/s = 2η̄/3 + (40η̄^2)/(63 e_η̄ T_i τ_i)(τ_i/τ)^(2/3) + O(τ^(-4/3))
* The overlap time parameter is set to t0 = 0.01 fm/c following Shi (2022)
* The longitudinal to transverse pressure ratio approaches P_L/P_T = 1 - (3η̄)/(2 e_η̄ T_i τ_i)(τ_i/τ)^(2/3) + O(τ^(-4/3))

## Context

This work extends the family of exact solutions to the ideal hydrodynamic equations found by Shi (2022) to the microscopic Boltzmann equation level, enabling tests of how macroscopic hydrodynamic theory emerges from kinetic theory in systems with broken boost invariance. Prior exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation existed only for boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow, as in the work of Baym (1984), Florkowski (2013), and Chattopadhyay (2021). The solutions enable systematic comparison between full kinetic theory results and various hydrodynamic approximations including the Navier-Stokes limit, and can be extended to study anisotropic hydrodynamics by taking initial distributions that are anisotropic in momentum space.
