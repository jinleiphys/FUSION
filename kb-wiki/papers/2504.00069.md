# Transverse Energy--Energy Correlators at Small $x$ for Photon--Hadron Production

> We study the transverse energy--energy correlator (TEEC) observable in photon--hadron and photon--jet production in p+p and p+A collisions at small $x$. We derive the relevant expressions in the high-energy limit of the scattering where the dipole picture is applicable and show how the dependence on the fragmentation function of the hadron cancels due to the momentum-sum rule. The nonperturbative scattering with the target nucleus is expressed in terms of the dipole amplitude, which also describes nonlinear gluon saturation effects. The TEEC observable is computed in the RHIC and LHC kinematics, and we show that it can be sensitive to the dipole amplitude, making it a potentially good observable for studying saturation effects.

# Transverse Energy–Energy Correlators at Small $x$ for Photon–Hadron Production

## Key claim

The transverse energy–energy correlator (TEEC) in photon–hadron and photon–jet production at small $x$ is sensitive to the dipole amplitude describing nonlinear gluon saturation, and the dependence on the hadron fragmentation function cancels exactly due to the momentum-sum rule, making the observable a potentially clean probe of saturation effects in p+p and p+A collisions at RHIC and LHC kinematics.

## Method

The authors derive the TEEC expression for photon–hadron and photon–jet production in the high-energy (small-$x$) limit using the dipole picture, where the partonic cross section is expressed in terms of the Fourier-transformed dipole amplitude $\mathcal{F}_x(\mathbf{k})$. The dipole $S$-matrix is evolved using the running-coupling Balitsky–Kovchegov (rcBK) equation with an initial condition from a 4-parameter fit to the MV model, and the nuclear dipole amplitude is obtained by scaling the saturation scale as $Q_{s0,A}^2 = c A^{1/3} Q_{s0}^2$ with $c \in [0.5, 1.0]$. The TEEC and its asymmetry (ATEEC) are computed numerically for representative RHIC and LHC kinematics using the CT18NLO proton PDF.

## Key numbers

- RHIC kinematics: $p_T^\gamma = 5$ GeV, $y_\gamma = 2$, $p_T^{\rm jet} = 5$ GeV, $y_{\rm jet} = 2$, $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV
- LHC kinematics: $p_T^\gamma = 10$ GeV, $y_\gamma = 4$, $p_T^{\rm jet} = 10$ GeV, $y_{\rm jet} = 4$, $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV
- Nuclear saturation scale scaling: $Q_{s0,A}^2 = c A^{1/3} Q_{s0}^2$ with $c$ varied between $0.5$ and $1.0$
- rcBK parameters: $N_f = 3$, $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.241$ GeV
- The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for photon–jet TEEC at RHIC is above 1 throughout $\tau \in [0.1, 0.9]$, while at LHC it is below 1 in the same $\tau$ range
- The ratio of dipole amplitudes for Au/Pb to proton shows a Cronin peak around $k = 1$–$3$ GeV at RHIC energies, with peak positions scaling as $Q_{s0}(c=1.0)/Q_{s0}(c=0.5) = \sqrt{2}$

## Context

This work extends the study of transverse energy–energy correlators to photon–hadron and photon–jet production in the small-$x$ regime, building on prior TEEC studies in deep inelastic scattering by Kang et al. (2023) and on earlier work on photon–hadron production at small $x$ by Jalilian-Marian et al. (2005) and Dominguez et al. (2011). The cancellation of fragmentation function dependence via the momentum-sum rule is a key advantage over standard azimuthal correlations. The results enable future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to constrain the small-$x$ gluon structure and saturation dynamics, and the formalism can be extended to dihadron and dijet TEEC observables.
