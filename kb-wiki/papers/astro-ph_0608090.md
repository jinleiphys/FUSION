# Upper Bound on the Dark Matter Total Annihilation Cross Section

> We consider dark matter annihilation into Standard Model particles and show that the least detectable final states, namely neutrinos, define an upper bound on the total cross section. Calculating the cosmic diffuse neutrino signal, and comparing it to the measured terrestrial atmospheric neutrino background, we derive a strong and general bound. This can be evaded if the annihilation products are dominantly new and truly invisible particles. Our bound is much stronger than the unitarity bound at the most interesting masses, shows that dark matter halos cannot be significantly modified by annihilations, and can be improved by a factor of 10--100 with existing neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The total dark matter annihilation cross section in halos today can be bounded by requiring that the cosmic diffuse neutrino signal from annihilation to the least detectable Standard Model final states (neutrinos) does not exceed the measured atmospheric neutrino background, assuming a 100% branching ratio to neutrinos as the most conservative case.

## Method

The authors calculate the cosmic diffuse neutrino flux from dark matter annihilations in all halos across the universe, using the formalism of Ullio et al. (2002) with a Navarro-Frenk-White halo profile and a clustering factor Delta^2 = 2 x 10^5. They compare this signal to the measured atmospheric neutrino background from the Frejus and AMANDA detectors, requiring that the signal integrated over a bin of width Delta log10(E_nu) = 0.5 just below E_nu = m_chi be no more than 100% of the angle-averaged atmospheric muon neutrino background.

## Key numbers

* For a thermal relic, the required cross section is <sigma_A v> ~ 3 x 10^-26 cm^3 s^-1.
* The unitarity bound for s-wave dominated annihilation is <sigma_A v> <= 1.5 x 10^-13 cm^3/s x (GeV/m_chi)^2 x (300 km/s / v_rms).
* The Kaplinghat, Knox, and Turner (KKT) model requires <sigma_A v>_KKT ~ 3 x 10^-19 cm^3/s x (m_chi/GeV).
* Unitarity restricts the KKT model to m_chi less than about 80 GeV for v_rms = 300 km/s.
* The clustering factor for NFW halos is Delta^2 = 2 x 10^5; for cuspier Moore profiles it could be about 10 times larger, for flatter Kravtsov profiles about 2 times smaller.
* The bound from relativistic energy density gives <sigma_A v> < 10^-17 x (m_chi/GeV) cm^3/s.
* The bound implies that for all m_chi greater than 0.1 GeV, the annihilation-flattened core density is rho_A greater than 5 x 10^3 GeV/cm^3, which occurs only at radii less than about 1 pc in the Milky Way for an NFW profile.
* Existing neutrino experiments could improve the bound by a factor of 10 to 100 over the mass range 0.1 to 10^5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the unitarity bound of Griest and Kamionkowski (1990) and Hui (2001) by providing a model-independent bound based on the appearance of annihilation products rather than just the disappearance rate. It directly rules out the proposal of Kaplinghat, Knox, and Turner (2000) to modify dark matter halos through annihilation, which required cross sections about 10^7 times larger than the thermal relic scale. The bound can be evaded if annihilation products are dominantly new and truly invisible non-Standard Model particles, and future analyses by Super-Kamiokande and AMANDA could improve sensitivity to approach the natural scale for thermal relics.
