# Can Induced Theta Vacua be Created in Heavy Ion Collisions?

> The development of the early Universe is a remarkable laboratory for the study of most nontrivial properties of particle physics. What is more remarkable is the fact that these phenomena at the QCD scale can be, in principle, experimentally tested in heavy ion collisions. We expect that, in general, an arbitrary theta-state would be created in the heavy ion collisions, similar to the creation of the disoriented chiral condensate with an arbitrary isospin direction. It should be a large domain with a wrong $θ\neq 0$ orientation. We test this idea numerically in a simple model where we study the evolution of the phases of the chiral condensates in QCD with two quark flavors with non-zero theta-parameter. We see the formation of a non-zero theta-vacuum with the formation time of the order of $10^{-23}$ seconds. This result will have important implications for a possible axion search experiment at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a non-zero induced theta-vacuum state can be created in heavy ion collisions, analogous to disoriented chiral condensates, but only when the combination of chiral phases is close to its vacuum value so that the large energy cost from the U(1)_A anomaly is avoided and the energy difference scales with the small quark mass parameter.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the zero-temperature equations of motion for two coupled nonlinear partial differential equations describing the phases of the chiral condensate in QCD with two quark flavors and a non-zero theta parameter. They start from random initial values of the chiral fields on a 3D grid of 16^3 points with periodic boundary conditions, evolve the system using a predictor-corrector method with damping gamma ~ 200 MeV, and analyze the Fourier modes to track the formation of a condensate.

## Key numbers

- The formation time of the non-zero theta-vacuum is approximately 0.064 MeV^{-1} ≈ 4 × 10^{-23} seconds for a total volume of (10 fm)^3 and theta = 2π/16.
- The parameter E = ⟨b α_s/(32π) G^2⟩ ∼ 10^{-2} GeV^4 is much larger than M_i = -m_q ⟨Ψ̄Ψ⟩ ∼ 10^{-3} GeV^4, with m_q ∼ 5 MeV and ⟨Ψ̄Ψ⟩ ≃ -(240 MeV)^3.
- The predicted decay width Γ(η → ππ) ∼ 0.5 MeV (sin(θ/2))^2 and Γ(η' → ππ) ∼ 2 MeV (sin(θ/2))^2, the latter being almost an order of magnitude larger than the full width of the η' meson in the theta=0 world.
- The grid spacing was determined by varying the spatial grid side length, with time steps fixed at about 10^{-5} MeV^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the concept of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) studied by Rajagopal (1995) and others to include the U(1)_A phase, showing that non-trivial theta-vacua can form similarly to DCC domains. It contradicts earlier expectations that the large energy cost from the U(1)_A anomaly would prevent theta-vacuum formation, as argued by HZ (1998). The results suggest that RHIC experiments could search for CP-odd signatures through η(η') → ππ decays, which are strongly forbidden in the theta=0 world, following proposals by Pisarski (1994) and others.
