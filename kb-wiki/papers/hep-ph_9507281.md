# Nuclear Neutron Haloes as Seen by Antiprotons

> Nuclear interactions of antiprotons in atomic states are discussed. The total as well as partial widths for single nucleon capture events are calculated. These are compared to the X-ray and recent single nucleon capture data. The rates of the neutron or proton captures test nuclear density distributions at the extreme nuclear surface. Recently found cases of neutron and proton haloes are analysed.

# Nuclear Neutron Haloes as Seen by Antiprotons

## Key claim

The ratio of neutron to proton capture rates in antiprotonic atoms tests the extreme nuclear surface, but the interpretation in terms of "neutron halo" or "neutron skin" depends critically on the unknown initial atomic state from which capture occurs and on the range of the nucleon-antinucleon force, which together introduce significant uncertainty into the deduced density moments.

## Method

The authors calculate total and partial widths for single-nucleon capture of antiprotons from atomic states using an optical potential linear in nuclear density, with parameters fitted to X-ray data from oxygen isotopes. They then compute the fraction of captures that leave the residual nucleus cold (below neutron separation threshold) by modeling final-state pion interactions via eikonal damping factors and Monte Carlo averaging over pion multiplicities and phase space. Nuclear densities are obtained from an asymptotic density (AD) model following Bethe-Siemens and from self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations with the Skyrme SkP interaction on a spatial mesh.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit complex scattering length extracted from oxygen isotope X-ray data is \( t_{\NNB} \approx -1.5 - i2.5 \) fm.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential at central densities is 200 MeV strong, giving a mean free path well below 1 fm.
- Cold captures amount to 10–20 percent of the total capture rate, nearly independent of the nucleus.
- For \( ^{58} \)Ni, the experimental cold-to-total capture ratio is 0.098(8) and the neutron-to-proton capture ratio is 0.9(1); the HF calculation gives 0.110 and 0.785 respectively.
- For \( ^{238} \)U, the experimental cold-to-total ratio is 0.114(9) and the neutron-to-proton ratio is 6.0(8); the HF calculation gives 0.092 and 4.09.
- For \( ^{144} \)Sm, the experimental neutron-to-proton capture ratio is ≤ 0.4, indicating a proton halo.
- For \( ^{176} \)Yb, the experimental neutron-to-proton capture ratio is 8.1(7), the largest measured.
- The ratio of antiproton-neutron to antiproton-proton absorptive amplitudes \( R_{np} \) is taken as 0.63 from mesonic studies in \( ^{12} \)C or 0.82 from deuteron data.

## Context

This work extends earlier antiprotonic atom studies by Batty (1989) and kaonic atom studies by Friedman (1990) to the analysis of single-nucleon capture data obtained via radiochemical methods at CERN. The authors compare two nuclear density models: an asymptotic density model following Bethe and Siemens (1957) and self-consistent HF/HFB calculations with the Skyrme SkP interaction of Dobaczewski et al. (1984). The results show that the AD model overestimates neutron haloes and that shell effects from angular momentum are essential, while HFB-type pairing correlations have small effect. The method enables future experiments to determine where capture occurs and to give precise meaning to the terms "neutron halo" and "neutron skin," though anomalous cases in Yb, Te, and Sm require further study.
