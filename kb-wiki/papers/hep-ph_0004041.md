# B_c Meson Production in Nuclear Collisions at RHIC

> We study quantitatively the formation and evolution of B_c bound states in a space-time domain of deconfined quarks and gluons (quark-gluon plasma, QGP). At the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) one expects for the first time that typical central collisions will result in multiple pairs of heavy (in this case charmed) quarks. This provides a new mechanism for the formation of heavy quarkonia which depends on the properties of the deconfined region. We find typical enhancements of about 500 fold for the B_c production yields over expectations from the elementary coherent hadronic B_c-meson production scenario. The final population of bound states may serve as a probe of the plasma phase parameters.

# B_c Meson Production in Nuclear Collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

The formation of B_c mesons in a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at RHIC via a new mechanism, recombination of mobile heavy quarks in a deconfined region, can enhance production yields by approximately 500-fold compared to expectations from elementary coherent hadronic production, but this enhancement depends critically on the existence of multiple charm quark pairs per central event and on the properties of the deconfined phase.

## Method

The authors model the chemical kinetics of B_c bound states in an expanding QGP using master equations that include formation (b + anti-c -> B_c + g) and dissociation (B_c + g -> b + anti-c) reactions, with cross sections derived from a color-dipole operator product expansion and related via detailed balance. They assume an isentropic ellipsoidal expansion of the QGP with longitudinal and transverse growth, and solve the rate equations numerically over the QGP lifetime from thermalization at initial temperature T_0 to hadronization at T_f = 0.15 GeV, using initial charm quark multiplicity N_{c0} = 10 per central Au-Au collision at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor of about 500-fold for B_c production yields over expectations from the elementary coherent hadronic B_c-meson production scenario.
- Approximately 10 c anti-c pairs per central event in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, and about 1 in 20 central events produces a b anti-b pair.
- B_c dissociation cross section reaches a maximum of about 1.5 mb when gluon momentum k = (10/7) k_0.
- B_c binding energy of 0.84 GeV, derived from hadronic open flavor B and D meson masses; B_c mass of 6.3 GeV used in calculations.
- Quark masses used: m_b = 5.8 GeV, m_c = 1.3 GeV.
- For the nominal benchmark assumptions, the fractional B_c yield per initial b anti-b pair is at 5%.
- Dissociation time scales for B_c in the range of initial temperatures expected at RHIC (roughly 300 to 500 MeV) are of order 1–10 fm/c.
- QGP freeze-out temperature T_f = 0.15 GeV; initial temperatures explored range from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV.
- Initial longitudinal sizes explored: l_i = 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 fm; initial transverse radius r_i = 5 fm for Au-Au collisions.
- Speed of sound in ideal relativistic gas taken as v_r = 0.58 c; also tested v_r = 0.79 c and v_r = c.
- Ratio B_c(2S)/B_c(1S) varies by at least a factor of two within the expected initial temperature range for RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the authors' preliminary studies (FTR99, TSR99) and introduces a new production mechanism for heavy quarkonia that depends on the existence of a deconfined state, in contrast to the standard scenario of suppression relative to expected yields as a signature for deconfinement. The mechanism becomes relevant at RHIC because typical central collisions produce multiple charm quark pairs, unlike at lower energies. The paper enables using B_c final populations as a probe of QGP phase parameters, with the B_c(2S)/B_c(1S) yield ratio potentially serving as a thermometer of the plasma phase, analogous to the charmonium system at SPS (Heinz 1997). The cross section formalism generalizes earlier work on J/psi breakup in QGP (Kha94, Kha96) to any heavy quarkonium 1S state.
