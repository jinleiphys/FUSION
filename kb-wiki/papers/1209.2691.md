# Status of reaction theory for studying rare isotopes

> Reactions are an important tool to study nuclear structure and for extracting reactions relevant for astrophysics. In this paper we focus on deuteron induced reactions which can provide information on neutron shell evolution as well as neutron capture cross sections. We review recent work on the systematic comparison of the continuum discretized coupled channel method, the adiabatic wave approximation and the Faddeev momentum-space approach. We also explore other aspects of the reaction mechanism and discuss in detail difficulties encountered in the calculations.

# Status of reaction theory for studying rare isotopes

## Key claim
Deuteron-induced reactions, particularly (d,p) transfer, can provide information on neutron shell evolution and neutron capture cross sections for rare isotopes, but the accuracy of reaction theory predictions depends critically on the validity of approximations used in the three-body models. The continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method and the adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) are good approximations to the exact Faddeev solution for reactions around 10 MeV/u, but agreement deteriorates at higher beam energies, and for breakup observables at low energies CDCC fails to reproduce Faddeev results.

## Method
The authors systematically compared three theoretical approaches for (d,p) reactions: the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method, the adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA), and the exact Faddeev momentum-space integral formalism. They performed calculations using the Fresco code for CDCC and compared results for elastic scattering, transfer, and breakup observables on targets including 12C and 48Ca at various deuteron beam energies, using identical Hamiltonians across methods to isolate differences due to approximations rather than input interactions.

## Key numbers
- For 12C(d,p)13C at Ed = 12 MeV, the percentage difference at the peak of the transfer angular distribution between CH89 and KD optical potentials is approximately 10%.
- For 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at Ed = 56 MeV, breakup effects change the shape of the transfer angular distribution completely compared to the 1-step approximation.
- For 48Ca breakup at Ed = 56 MeV, using l_max = 6 was sufficient to obtain convergence in the np partial wave expansion.
- For 12C breakup at Ed = 12 MeV, calculations with l_max = 6 were very far from Faddeev results, and l_max = 8 became numerically unfeasible with the numerical methods used in Fresco.

## Context
This work extends systematic benchmarking studies of (d,p) reaction models performed within the TORUS collaboration, comparing CDCC and ADWA against Faddeev calculations as reported in Upadhyay (2012) and Nunes (2011). The paper identifies that no benchmarks currently exist for heavier systems because Faddeev calculations cannot handle the Coulomb interaction for heavy nuclei, motivating the development of a new formalism by Akram (2012) that can handle beam energies from 5-50 MeV/u across light and heavy systems. The results enable better understanding of when approximate methods like DWBA (distorted wave Born approximation) are inadequate, particularly for cases where breakup effects significantly modify transfer cross sections.
