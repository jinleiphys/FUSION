# REAL-TIME DYNAMICS OF PARTON-HADRON CONVERSION

> We propose a new and universal approach to the hadronization problem that incorporates both perturbative QCD and effective field theory in their respective domains of validity and that models the transition between them in analogy to the finite temperature QCD phase transition. Using techniques of quantum kinetic theory, we formulate a real-time description in momentum and position space. The approach is applied to the evolution of fragmenting $q\bar q$ and $gg$ jets as the system evolves from the initial 2-jet, via parton multiplication and cluster formation, to the final yield of hadrons. We investigate time scale of the transition, energy dependence, cluster size and mass distributions, and compare our results for particle production and Bose-Einstein correlations with experimental data for $e^+e^-\rightarrow hadrons$. An interesting possibility to extract the space-time evolution of the system from Bose enhancement measurements is suggested.

# REAL-TIME DYNAMICS OF PARTON-HADRON CONVERSION

## Key claim

The authors propose a universal approach to hadronization that combines perturbative QCD with an effective field theory of hadrons, modeling the transition between them in analogy to the finite-temperature QCD phase transition. The approach is applied to fragmenting quark-antiquark and gluon-gluon jets, but the authors hedge that the particular functional forms chosen for the scale-dependent coupling and quark mass are not crucial, as long as they satisfy the boundary conditions of confinement.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian that interpolates between perturbative QCD at short distances (using elementary gluon and quark fields) and a chiral effective field theory at long distances (using collective scalar and pseudoscalar meson fields), linked by scale-dependent functions κ_L and μ_L that control the color-dielectric property of the vacuum. From this Lagrangian, they derive quantum kinetic equations for Wigner-transformed Green functions, yielding phase-space distribution functions F_α(r,p) for partons and hadrons. They then simulate the space-time evolution of e+e- annihilation events at Q = 10, 34, 91, and 100 GeV in discrete time steps of 0.01 fm, using a stochastic cascade where partons branch, coalesce into pre-hadronic clusters via a bubble nucleation mechanism, and clusters decay into final hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Characteristic confinement length L_c = 0.6 fm for bag constant B^{1/4} = 230 MeV; L_c = 0.8 fm for B^{1/4} = 180 MeV
- Parton and hadron pressures cross over at time t ≈ 0.7 fm, nearly independent of the choice of B (and hence L_c), for qqbar-initiated showers at Q = 100 GeV
- Surface tension σ^{1/3} = 40 MeV for B^{1/4} = 230 MeV; σ^{1/3} = 48 MeV for B^{1/4} = 180 MeV
- Critical cluster mass M_crit = 4 GeV, above which clusters are forced to continue partonic shower development (affects less than 5% of clusters)
- Time step used in simulation: Δt = 0.01 fm
- Charged particle multiplicity n_ch grows with energy in consistency with experimental data at Q = 34 GeV and Q = 91 GeV
- Bose-Einstein enhancement factors are distinguishable between the two parameter choices (B^{1/4}=230 MeV, L_c=0.6 fm versus B^{1/4}=180 MeV, L_c=0.8 fm) and are essentially independent of Q

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous work (Ellis and Geiger, 1995, Ref. [ms36]) and builds on finite-temperature QCD phase transition phenomenology (Campbell, Ellis, and Olive, 1990) to model the parton-hadron conversion as a dynamical, non-equilibrium process. The approach contrasts with standard parton shower Monte Carlos that describe hadronization phenomenologically, and with lattice QCD which cannot yet perform dynamical calculations of parton-hadron conversion. The results enable systematic extraction of the space-time evolution of hadron emission regions from Bose-Einstein correlation measurements, and suggest applicability to deep inelastic scattering, hadron-hadron collisions, and nucleus-nucleus collisions.
