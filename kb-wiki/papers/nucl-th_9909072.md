# Algebraic mean field theory

> Mean field theory has an unexpected group theoretic mathematical foundation. Instead of representation theory which applies to most group theoretic quantum models, Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov have been formulated in terms of coadjoint orbits for the groups U(n) and O(2n). The general theory of mean fields is formulated for any arbitrary Lie algebra {\textbf g} of fermion operators. The moment map provides the correspondence between the Hilbert space of microscopic wave functions and the dual space {\textbf g}$^\ast$ of densities. The coadjoint orbits of the group in the dual space are phase spaces on which time-dependent mean field theory is equivalent to a classical Hamiltonian dynamical system. Indeed it forms a finite-dimensional Lax system. The SU(3) mean field theory is constructed explicitly in the coadjoint orbit framework.

## Key claim

Mean field theory for any Lie algebra of fermion operators can be formulated in terms of coadjoint orbits, with the moment map providing the correspondence between microscopic wave functions and densities; this generalizes the known coadjoint-orbit formulations of Hartree-Fock (for U(n)) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (for O(2n)) to arbitrary physically relevant algebras such as SU(3).

## Method

The authors construct a general algebraic mean field theory by defining a moment map from the Hilbert space of A-fermion wave functions to the dual space g* of a Lie algebra g of hermitian fermion operators. The mean field approximation restricts densities to a single coadjoint orbit of the corresponding Lie group G, which is diffeomorphic to a coset space G/G_ρ and carries a nondegenerate symplectic structure. The ground state density is found by minimizing an energy functional on the coadjoint orbit, and the mean field Hamiltonian h is derived from the derivative of the energy functional via the symplectic form, leading to time-dependent dynamics governed by the Lax equation ρ̇ = [h(ρ), ρ]. The formalism is then applied explicitly to the Elliott SU(3) algebra, where the coadjoint orbits are classified by the irreducible representation labels (λ, μ) and the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction yields the mean field Hamiltonian h = 2ρ^t.

## Key numbers

- For Hartree-Fock with U(n), the dimension of the coadjoint orbit of idempotent densities is 2A(n-A), where A is the number of fermions and n is the dimension of the single-particle space.
- For SU(3), the coadjoint orbit dimension is 0 for (λ, μ) = (0, 0), 4 for (λ ≠ 0, μ = 0) or (λ = 0, μ ≠ 0), and 6 for (λ ≠ 0, μ ≠ 0).
- The quadratic Lax invariant for the SU(3) highest weight orbit is I₂ = (λ² + λμ + μ²)/3, evaluated at the highest weight density.
- At an arbitrary orbit point ρ = A + iB, the quadratic Lax invariant is I₂ = 1/4 L² + 1/12 Q·Q.

## Context

This work extends the coadjoint orbit formulation of mean field theory, previously developed for Hartree-Fock with U(n) by Rosensteel and Rowe (1981) and for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with O(2n) by Rowe (1981), to arbitrary Lie algebras of fermion operators. It provides a unified mathematical framework that connects the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958) to mean field theory via coadjoint orbits, enabling the treatment of collective degrees of freedom beyond the single-particle picture. The formalism opens the way to constructing mean field approximations for other physically relevant algebras, with the Lax equation structure providing conserved quantities and integrable dynamics.
