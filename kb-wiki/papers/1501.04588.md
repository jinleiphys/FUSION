# Correlation Trends in the Hyperfine Structures of $^{210,212}$Fr

> We demonstrate the importance of electron correlation effects in the hyperfine structure constants of many low-lying states in $^{210}$Fr and $^{212}$Fr. This is achieved by calculating the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole hyperfine structure constants using the Dirac-Fock approximation, second order many-body perturbation theory and the coupled-cluster method in the singles and doubles approximation in the relativistic framework. By combining our recommended theoretical results with the corresponding experimental values, improved nuclear magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments of the above isotopes are determined. In the present work, it is observed that there are large discrepancies between the hyperfine structure constants of the $7D_{5/2}$ state obtained from the experimental and theoretical studies whereas good agreements are found for the other $D_{5/2}$ states. Our estimated hyperfine constants for the $8P$, $6D$, $10S$ and $11S$ states could be very useful as benchmarks for the measurement of these quantities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that electron correlation effects are crucial for accurate hyperfine structure calculations in francium, with the non-linear terms in the coupled-cluster method (representing triple and quadruple excitations) being essential for obtaining reliable results, particularly for states with higher angular momentum. The authors note a large discrepancy between experimental and theoretical hyperfine structure constants for the \(7D_{5/2}\) state, while good agreement is found for other \(D_{5/2}\) states.

## Method

The authors calculated magnetic dipole (\(A_{hf}\)) and electric quadrupole (\(B_{hf}\)) hyperfine structure constants for 17 low-lying states in \(^{210}\)Fr and \(^{212}\)Fr using the Dirac-Fock (DF) approximation, second-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT(2)), and the coupled-cluster method with singles and doubles (CCSD), including a perturbative treatment of triple excitations (CCSD\(_{\text{t3}}\)). They used a relativistic framework with a \(V^{N-1}\) potential and a basis of analytic exponential functions with quadratic exponents to accurately describe wave functions in the nuclear region.

## Key numbers

- The \(A_{hf}/g_I\) value for the \(7s\ ^2S_{1/2}\) state in \(^{210}\)Fr is 9885.24 MHz at the CCSD\(_{\text{t3}}\) level, compared to 6531.06 MHz at the DF level.
- The \(A_{hf}/g_I\) value for the \(7p\ ^2P_{1/2}\) state in \(^{210}\)Fr is 1279.56 MHz at the CCSD\(_{\text{t3}}\) level, compared to 707.70 MHz at the DF level.
- The \(A_{hf}/g_I\) value for the \(6d\ ^2D_{5/2}\) state in \(^{210}\)Fr is \(-58.82\) MHz at the CCSD\(_{\text{t3}}\) level, showing a sign change from the DF value of 14.28 MHz.
- The \(B_{hf}/Q\) value for the \(7p\ ^2P_{3/2}\) state in \(^{210}\)Fr is 259.73 MHz at the CCSD\(_{\text{t3}}\) level, compared to 118.03 MHz at the DF level.
- The \(A_{hf}/g_I\) values for the \(s\)-states in \(^{212}\)Fr are reduced by \(-1.141\) MHz (7S), \(-0.242\) MHz (8S), \(-0.095\) MHz (9S), \(-0.047\) MHz (10S), and \(-0.028\) MHz (11S) relative to \(^{210}\)Fr.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperfine structure in francium, such as those by Owusu et al. (using relativistic MBPT for \(S\)-states), Gomez et al. and Safronova et al. (using the SDpT method), and Dzuba et al. (using a restricted Hartree-Fock method with MBPT). The results provide benchmark theoretical hyperfine constants for the \(8P\), \(6D\), \(10S\), and \(11S\) states, which can guide future measurements. The demonstrated importance of non-linear correlation effects in the CCSD method is relevant for improving wave function accuracy in parity nonconservation and electric dipole moment studies in francium.
