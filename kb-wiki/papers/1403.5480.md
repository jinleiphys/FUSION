# Dynamics of strongly interacting parton-hadron matter

> In this study we investigate the dynamics of strongly interacting parton-hadron matter by calculating the centrality dependence of direct photons produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. As sources for 'direct' photons, we incorporate the interactions of quarks and gluons as well as hadronic interactions ($π+π\toρ+γ$, $ρ+π\toπ+γ$, meson-meson bremsstrahlung $m+m\to m+m+γ$, meson-baryon bremsstrahlung $m+B\to m+B+γ$), the decays of $ϕ$ and $a_1$ mesons and the photons produced in the initial hard collisions ('pQCD'). Our calculations suggest that the channel decomposition of the observed spectrum changes with centrality with an increasing (dominant) contribution of hadronic sources for more peripheral reactions. Furthermore, the 'thermal' photon yield is found to scale roughly with the number of participant nucleons as $N_{part}^α$ with $α\approx$ 1.5, whereas the partonic contribution scales with an exponent $α_p \approx1.75$. Additionally, we provide predictions for the centrality dependence of the direct photon elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$. The direct photon $v_2$ is seen to be larger in peripheral collisions compared to the most central ones since the photons from the hot deconfined matter in the early stages of the collision carry a much smaller elliptic flow than those from the final hadronic interactions.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of direct photon production in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV is calculated within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, showing that the channel decomposition changes with centrality: hadronic sources become dominant for more peripheral reactions, contrary to the expectation that direct photons primarily originate from the early hot deconfined phase.

## Method

The PHSD transport approach was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV across different centralities, incorporating photon production from partonic interactions (quark-gluon plasma channels and initial hard pQCD collisions), hadronic interactions (π+π→ρ+γ, ρ+π→π+γ, meson-meson bremsstrahlung, and meson-baryon bremsstrahlung), and decays of φ and a₁ mesons. The centrality dependence was analyzed by binning events according to the number of participant nucleons Npart calculated in a Monte-Carlo Glauber model, and the elliptic flow v₂(pT) of direct photons was computed for each centrality bin.

## Key numbers

* Thermal photon yield scales with number of participant nucleons as Npart^α with α ≈ 1.5 for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, integrated over pT in the interval 0.4 ≤ pT ≤ 5 GeV/c
* Partonic contribution to thermal photons scales with exponent αp ≈ 1.75, while hadronic bremsstrahlung channels scale with exponent ≈ 1.5
* QGP photon contribution reaches slightly more than 30% of total direct photons for the most central event bin at √sNN = 200 GeV
* QGP photon contribution falls below 10% in the most peripheral centrality bin
* Photons from partonic channels in the initial hot deconfined phase have a very small elliptic flow of the order of 2%

## Context

This work extends previous PHSD calculations for minimum bias collisions (Linnyk 2013) to study centrality dependence, addressing the "photon v₂ puzzle" where PHENIX Collaboration measurements showed direct photon elliptic flow comparable to pions, contrary to theoretical expectations from hydrodynamical calculations (Chatterjee 2005, Chatterjee 2013, Shen 2013). The results suggest that hadronic sources, which carry large elliptic flow, dominate in peripheral collisions and can explain the observed v₂, while alternative explanations involving the conformal anomaly (Basar 2012, Bzdak 2012) may also need to be considered. Future experimental measurements of photon spectra and elliptic flow as a function of centrality will be needed to clarify the puzzle.
