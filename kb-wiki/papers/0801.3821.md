# On the radiative decays of light vector and axial-vector mesons

> We study the light vector and axial-vector mesons. According to the hadrogenesis conjecture the nature of the two types of states is distinct. The axial-vector mesons are generated dynamically by coupled-channel interactions based on the chiral Lagrangian written down in terms of the Goldstone bosons and the light vector mesons. We propose a novel counting scheme that arises if the chiral Lagrangian is supplemented by constraints from large-N_c QCD in the context of the hadrogenesis conjecture. The counting scheme is successfully tested by a systematic study of the properties of vector mesons. The spectrum of light axial-vector mesons is derived relying on the leading order interaction of the Goldstone bosons with the vector mesons supplemented by a phenomenology for correction terms. The f_1(1282), b_1(1230), h_1(1386), a_1(1230) and K_1(1272) mesons are recovered as molecular states. Based on those results the one-loop contributions to the electromagnetic decay amplitudes of axial-vector molecules into pseudo-scalar or vector mesons are evaluated systematically. In order to arrive at gauge invariant results in a transparent manner we choose to represent the vector particles by anti-symmetric tensor fields. It is emphasized that there are no tree-level contributions to a radiative decay amplitude of a given state if that state is generated by coupled-channel dynamics. The inclusion of the latter would be double counting. At present we restrict ourselves to loops where a vector and a pseudo-scalar meson couple to the axial-vector molecule. We argue that final and predictive results require further computations involving intermediate states with two vector mesons. The relevance of the latter is predicted by our counting rules.

## Key claim

The axial-vector mesons $f_1(1282)$, $b_1(1230)$, $h_1(1386)$, $a_1(1230)$, and $K_1(1272)$ are molecular states generated dynamically by coupled-channel interactions, but the radiative decay calculations presented are partial and not yet predictive because contributions from intermediate states with two vector mesons are omitted, as required by the authors' novel counting rules.

## Method

The authors use a chiral Lagrangian written in terms of Goldstone bosons and vector mesons, representing vector particles by antisymmetric tensor fields to ensure gauge invariance in the non-perturbative coupled-channel framework. Axial-vector mesons are generated dynamically via the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction and chiral correction terms parameterized by $g_D$, $g_F$, and $b_D$, with the latter fixed from vector meson mass splittings. One-loop contributions to electromagnetic decay amplitudes of axial-vector molecules into a pseudo-scalar or vector meson are evaluated, restricted to loops where a vector and a pseudo-scalar meson couple to the axial-vector molecule.

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant is taken as $f = 90$ MeV throughout the work.
- The parameter $b_D = 0.92 \pm 0.05$ is extracted from the mass splitting of vector mesons, using $m^2_{K^*} - m^2_\rho$ and $m^2_\rho - m^2_\phi$.
- The axial-vector mesons recovered as molecular states are $f_1(1282)$, $b_1(1230)$, $h_1(1386)$, $a_1(1230)$, and $K_1(1272)$.
- At leading order, typical discrepancies between computed and empirical resonance masses are 50–100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadrogenesis conjecture, first demonstrated for axial-vector mesons by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2004), by computing radiative decays of the dynamically generated states. It contradicts the approach of Roca, Hosaka, and Oset (2007), which the authors argue suffers from conceptual inconsistencies regarding gauge invariance and double counting of tree-level contributions. The study enables future predictive calculations once contributions from two-vector-meson intermediate states are included, as required by the novel counting rules combining chiral and large-$N_c$ QCD constraints.
