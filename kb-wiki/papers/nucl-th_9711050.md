# A Study of the Coulomb Dissociation of 8B and the 7Be(p, gamma)8B Reaction

> We study the breakup reactions of 8B projectiles in high energy (50 and 250 MeV/u) collisions with heavy nuclear targets (208Pb). The intrinsic nuclear wave functions are calculated using a simple model, as well as a simple optical potential. We demonstrate that nuclear effects are negligible and evaluate the contributions of various (E1, E2 and M1) multipolarities. A good agreement with measured data is obtained with insignificant M1 contribution (at 50 MeV/u) and very small E2 contribution.

# A Study of the Coulomb Dissociation of 8B and the 7Be(p, gamma)8B Reaction

## Key claim
Nuclear excitations, E2, and M1 electromagnetic excitations are negligible for the Coulomb dissociation of 8B on 208Pb targets at 50 and 250 MeV/u, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific kinematic conditions of the RIKEN (50 MeV/u) and GSI (250 MeV/u) experiments and on the use of a simple nuclear model with a spectroscopic factor of unity.

## Method
The authors constructed a single-particle model for 8B assuming a p3/2 proton coupled to a 3/2- 7Be core, solving the Schrodinger equation with spin-orbit term and matching to asymptotic Coulomb waves. They built an optical potential using an M3Y-type effective interaction modified to reproduce the energy dependence of total reaction cross sections, and calculated nuclear and Coulomb excitation cross sections using the eikonal approximation with distorted waves. The cross sections were computed for E1, E2, and M1 multipolarities and compared to measured angular distributions from the RIKEN experiment at 50 MeV/u.

## Key numbers
- At 50 MeV/u and a relative energy of 0.2 MeV, the peak E1 cross section at theta ~ 0.4 degrees is more than one order of magnitude larger than E2 and more than two orders of magnitude larger than M1 excitation.
- The nuclear contribution to the total excitation cross section is about 3 orders of magnitude smaller than the Coulomb excitation cross sections for the whole spectrum at both 50 and 250 MeV/u.
- The 1+ resonance is located at Ex = 0.63 MeV with a width Gamma approximately 50 keV from nuclear broadening; the 3+ resonance is at Ex = 2.17 MeV.
- The optical potential at 250 MeV/nucleon is about two times smaller than at 50 MeV/nucleon.
- The Tassie model parameters for the ground-state density of 8B are alpha = 0.631 fm and a = 1.77 fm.

## Context
This work extends the Coulomb dissociation method of Baur (1986) and Bertulani (1988) for extracting radiative capture cross sections relevant to nuclear astrophysics, specifically the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction which is the most uncertain nuclear input to standard solar models. The authors address lingering questions from the RIKEN experiment of Motobayashi (1994) and the GSI experiment of Suemmerer (1996) regarding the importance of nuclear excitations and the relative contributions of E1, E2, and M1 multipolarities. The results enable more reliable extraction of the astrophysical S-factor for 7Be(p,gamma)8B from Coulomb dissociation data, and invalidate calculations based on the Tassie model for transition densities in halo nuclei, as previously noted by Bertulani and Sagawa (1995).
