# Impact of nonextensivity on the transport coefficients of a magnetized hot and dense QCD matter

> We have studied the impact of the nonextensivity on the transport coefficients related to charge and heat in thermal QCD. For this purpose, the electrical ($σ_{\rm el}$), Hall ($σ_{\rm H}$), thermal ($κ$) and Hall-type thermal ($κ_{\rm H}$) conductivities are determined using the kinetic theory approach in association with the nonextensive Tsallis statistical mechanism. The effect of nonextensivity is encoded in the nonextensive Tsallis distribution function, where the deviation of the parameter $q$ from 1 signifies the degree of nonextensivity in the concerned system. The thermal and electrical conductivities are found to increase with the introduction of nonextensivity, which means that the deviation of the medium from thermal equilibrium enhances both charge and heat transports. With the magnetic field, the deviations of $σ_{\rm el}$, $σ_{\rm H}$, $κ$ and $κ_{\rm H}$ from their respective equilibrated values increase, whereas these deviations decrease with the chemical potential. We have also studied how the extent of the nonextensivity modulates the longevity of magnetic field. Present work is further extended to the study of some observables associated with the aforesaid transport phenomena, such as the Knudsen number and the elliptic flow within the nonextensive Tsallis framework.

# Impact of nonextensivity on the transport coefficients of a magnetized hot and dense QCD matter

## Key claim

The electrical, Hall, thermal, and Hall-type thermal conductivities of a weakly magnetized QCD medium increase when the system deviates from thermal equilibrium, as quantified by the Tsallis nonextensive parameter q deviating from unity; however, this enhancement is conditional on the magnetic field and chemical potential, with deviations from equilibrium values increasing with magnetic field strength but decreasing with chemical potential.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation, incorporating the nonextensive Tsallis distribution function to describe deviations from thermal equilibrium. They calculated the electrical conductivity, Hall conductivity, thermal conductivity, and Hall-type thermal conductivity for a hot and dense QCD medium with three quark flavors (u, d, s) in the weak magnetic field limit (T² ≫ |q_f B|), using quasiparticle thermal masses for partons. The study also examined how the nonextensive parameter q modulates the lifetime of the magnetic field produced in noncentral heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: eB = m_π² (≈ 10¹⁸ Gauss) at RHIC and eB = 15 m_π² at LHC
- The nonextensive parameter q for hadronic matter can deviate up to 1.08–1.2 based on fits to RHIC and LHC spectra; for quark matter, q deviates up to 1.22
- Initial temperature set at 390 MeV and initial time at 0.2 fm for magnetic field lifetime calculations
- Impact parameter b = 4 fm used in magnetic field decay calculations
- The relaxation time for quarks and antiquarks is given by τ_f = 1/[5.1 T α_s² log(1/α_s) (1 + 0.12(2N_f + 1))]
- The quasiparticle mass of quarks is given by m_fT² = (g²T²/6)(1 + μ_f²/(π²T²))

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in thermal QCD matter (Rath 2019, Rath 2020, Thakur 2019, Kurian 2019) by incorporating nonextensive Tsallis statistics, which has been shown to provide good fits to transverse momentum distributions from STAR (Abelev 2007), PHENIX (Adare 2011), ALICE (Aamodt 2011), and CMS (Khachatryan 2011) collaborations. The study enables future investigations of how nonextensivity affects observables such as the Knudsen number and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions, and extends prior work on magnetic field lifetime in conducting media (Tuchin 2013, McLerran 2014) to the nonextensive framework.
