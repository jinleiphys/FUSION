# Machine learning approach to analyze heavy quark diffusion coefficient in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The diffusion coefficient of heavy quarks in the deconfined medium is examined in this research using a deep convolutional neural network (CNN) trained with data from relativistic heavy ion collisions involving heavy flavor hadrons. The CNN is trained using observables such as the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2$ of non-prompt $J/ψ$ from B-hadron decay in different centralities, where B meson evolutions are calculated using the Langevin equation and the Instantaneous Coalescence Model. The CNN outputs the parameters characterizing the temperature and momentum dependence of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. By inputting the experimental data of non-prompt $J/ψ$ $(R_{AA}, v_2)$ from various collision centralities into multiple channels of the well-trained network, we derive the values of the diffusion coefficient parameters. Additionally, We evaluate the uncertainty in determining the diffusion coefficient by taking into account the uncertainties present in the experimental data $(R_{AA}, v_2)$, which serve as inputs to the deep neural network.

## Key claim

The paper uses a deep convolutional neural network (CNN) trained on theoretical calculations of non-prompt J/ψ observables to extract the temperature and momentum dependence of the heavy quark spatial diffusion coefficient, finding that the extracted value of the diffusion coefficient at the critical temperature (α) is larger than recent lattice QCD results but consistent with previous model calculations, with a strong temperature dependence (β) and a weak momentum dependence (γ).

## Method

The authors generate training data using the Langevin equation plus Instantaneous Coalescence Model (LICM) to simulate bottom quark evolution in the quark-gluon plasma and B-meson hadronization in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, varying the shadowing factor S and three parameters (α, β, γ) that characterize the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s 2πT = [α + β(T/T_c - 1)] × (m_Q/E_Q)^γ. A CNN with four input channels (R_AA in three centralities and v_2) and four output parameters (S, α, β, γ) is trained on 20,000 parameter combinations. Experimental data points for non-prompt J/ψ R_AA and v_2 from CMS are sampled within their error bars and fed into the trained network to extract parameter distributions.

## Key numbers

- The mean value of α (D_s 2πT at critical temperature and zero momentum) is ⟨α⟩ = 4.87 with standard deviation 0.90
- The mean value of β (temperature dependence parameter) is ⟨β⟩ = 4.16 with standard deviation 2.32
- The mean value of γ (momentum dependence parameter) is ⟨γ⟩ = 0.058 with standard deviation 0.12
- The mean shadowing factor is ⟨S⟩ = 0.82 with standard deviation 0.050
- Training data parameters were sampled from ranges: α ∈ [2.0, 6.0], β ∈ [0.0, 8.0], γ ∈ [0.0, 1.0], S ∈ [0.6, 1.0]
- The bottom quark mass is taken as m_b = 4.75 GeV
- The critical temperature for coalescence is T_c = 150 MeV, and kinetic freeze-out temperature is T_fo = 120 MeV
- The root-mean-square radius of the B meson is √⟨r²⟩_B = 0.43 fm

## Context

This work extends Bayesian statistical analysis approaches previously applied to extract the heavy quark diffusion coefficient from charmed hadron data (Xu 2017) by using a CNN instead of Bayesian methods and focusing on B mesons rather than D mesons. The results are compared with recent lattice QCD calculations (Altenkort 2023) that found a smaller diffusion coefficient than previous quenched lattice QCD (Altenkort 2020, Brambilla 2020) and phenomenological estimates (Liu 2016, Scardina 2017). The extracted parameters enable data-driven determination of the temperature and momentum dependence of heavy quark diffusion, which is needed because a simple constant diffusion coefficient cannot simultaneously explain both R_AA and v_2 measurements.
