# Possible interpretation of the complex expectation values associated with resonances

> We propose a possible scheme to interpret the complex expectation values associated with resonances having the complex eigenenergies. Using the Green's function for resonances, the expectation value is basically described by the Breit-Wigner distribution as a function of the real excitation energy. In the expression of the complex expectation values for resonances, the real part brings the integral value of the distribution, while the imaginary part produces the deviation from the Breit-Wigner distribution,which explains a shift of the peak in the strength from the resonance energy. We apply the present scheme to the several nuclear resonances of $^{12}$C including the Hoyle state, and neutron/proton-rich nuclei of $^6$He, $^6$Be, $^8$He, and $^8$C. In these nuclei, many-body resonances are obtained as the complex-energy eigenstates under the correct boundary condition using the complex scaling method, and their nuclear radii are uniquely evaluated. We discuss the peculiar energy dependence of the strength function of the square radius for the resonances in these nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the complex expectation values of Hermitian operators for resonances can be interpreted through the Breit-Wigner distribution of the strength function, where the real part gives the integrated strength and the imaginary part produces a deviation that shifts the peak energy from the resonance energy; this interpretation is conditional on using the complex-scaled Green's function and the extended completeness relation.

## Method

The authors formulate the strength function for an arbitrary Hermitian operator using the complex-scaled Green's function and the extended completeness relation, separating resonance contributions from non-resonant continuum states. They apply the complex scaling method to obtain many-body resonance wave functions with complex eigenenergies for five nuclei described by alpha-cluster models: a 3-alpha model for 12C and an alpha-plus-valence-nucleon model (cluster orbital shell model) for 6He, 6Be, 8He, and 8C. The complex expectation values of the square radius operator are then used to construct the resonance component of the strength function, whose energy dependence is analyzed using the derived formulas.

## Context

This work extends the Morimatsu-Yazaki method (1994) for calculating energy spectra of hadron resonances to a general framework for interpreting complex expectation values of any Hermitian operator in many-body systems. It addresses the long-standing problem of interpreting complex expectation values for resonances, previously discussed by Berggren (1978, 1996), Burgers (1996), and Michel (2022), among others. The scheme enables the unique evaluation of nuclear radii for resonances using the complex scaling method and provides a basis for utilizing complex expectation values of various operators in resonance studies across nuclear, molecular, and hadron physics.
