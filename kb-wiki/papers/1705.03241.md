# Modeling pion production in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Pion production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at intermediate energies was modeled in the framework of the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model. The effects of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations in initialization and mean-field potential, isospin-dependent in-medium baryon-baryon elastic and inelastic cross sections and pion in-medium effect are all considered in this model. It is found that the ratio and yields of $π^{-}$and $π^{+}$ in Au+Au reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon reproduce the FOPI/GSI data very well especially with a soft symmetry energy in the present transport model. Predictions on the single and double $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio are made for the isotope reaction systems $^{132}\rm {Sn}+^{124}\rm {Sn}$ and $^{108}\rm {Sn}+^{112}\rm {Sn}$ at 300 MeV/nucleon since related experiments are being carried out at RIKEN/Japan.

## Key claim

The IBUU transport model, when including nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations, in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections, and pion in-medium effects, reproduces FOPI/GSI data on pion yields and ratios in Au+Au at 400 MeV/nucleon only when a **soft symmetry energy** (x = 1, slope L = 37 MeV) is used; stiffer symmetry energies (x = 0, -1) produce smaller pi- numbers than the data.

## Method

The author used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model, which solves the time evolution of the single-particle phase-space distribution function including mean-field potentials, elastic and inelastic two-body collisions. The model incorporates Skyrme-Hartree-Fock initial density distributions with a 20% high-momentum tail (proportional to 1/k^4 up to 2k_F) in nucleon momentum distributions, isospin-dependent in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections reduced by the square of the ratio of effective to free reduced masses, and a density- and momentum-dependent pion optical potential with isoscalar and isovector contributions. Pion production proceeds via Delta resonance formation and decay, with the Delta mass sampled from a modified Breit-Wigner distribution.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon: the model with soft symmetry energy (x = 1) reproduces FOPI data for both pi- and pi+ yields; with stiffer symmetry energies (x = 0, -1) the model gives smaller pi- numbers than experimental data.
- The symmetry energy slopes for the three x parameters: x = 1 gives L = 37 MeV, x = 0 gives L = 87 MeV, x = -1 gives L = 138 MeV.
- Saturation properties used: density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy E_0 = -16 MeV, incompressibility K_0 = 230 MeV, symmetry energy Esym(rho_0) = 30 MeV.
- Maximum cross sections for pion-nucleon resonance formation: sigma_max(pi+ p -> Delta++) = sigma_max(pi- n -> Delta-) = 200 mb; sigma_max(pi- p -> Delta0) = sigma_max(pi+ n -> Delta+) = 66.67 mb; sigma_max(pi0 p -> Delta+) = sigma_max(pi0 n -> Delta0) = 133.33 mb.
- Predictions are made for isotope reaction systems 132Sn+124Sn and 108Sn+112Sn at 300 MeV/nucleon incident beam energy.

## Context

This work extends previous IBUU04 transport model calculations (lyz05) by adding nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations, updated in-medium cross sections, and pion mean-field potentials. It addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, which has shown inconsistent results on pion production when comparing simulations to FOPI data (Reisdorf07, cozma17). The predictions for single and double pi-/pi+ ratios in Sn isotope reactions at 300 MeV/nucleon are designed for comparison with ongoing experiments at RIBF-RIKEN in Japan, and the approach can be applied at future facilities including FRIB (US), FAIR (Germany), HIRFL-CSR (China), and KoRIA (Korea).
