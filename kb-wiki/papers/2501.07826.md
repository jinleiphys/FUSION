# Covariant analysis of electromagnetic current on the light cone: exposition with scalar Yukawa theory

> We present the first systematic investigation of the Lorentz covariance of the charge form factor for a strongly coupled scalar theory in (3+1)-dimensions. Our results are based on the first non-perturbative solution of the scalar Yukawa theory with a Fock sector expansion including up to thee-particles (one mock nucleon plus two mock pions or two mock nucleons plus one mock anti-nucleon). The light-front Hamiltonian is constructed and renormalized using a Fock sector dependent scheme. The derived eigenvalue equation is then solved non-perturbatively to obtain the wave functions, which are then used to compute the current matrix element. We perform a covariant analysis of the current matrix element taking into account possible violation of the Poincaré symmetry due to the Fock sector truncation. The physical form factor depends on two boost invariants $ζ, Δ^2_\perp$, instead of the single Lorentz invariant $Q^2$. Instead of adopting the conventional Drell-Yan frame $ζ= 0$, we evaluate the form factor in general frames, and use the frame dependence to quantitatively gauge the loss of the Lorentz covariance. Our numerical result shows that as more Fock sectors are included, the frame dependence reduces dramatically. In particular, the anti-nucleon degree of freedom plays an important role in the reduction of the frame dependence, even though it only takes a small portion within the state vector. We also find that there is no zero-mode contribution to the current for the scalar Yukawa theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Lorentz covariance of the charge form factor in scalar Yukawa theory, which is violated by Fock sector truncation, can be systematically restored by including higher Fock sectors, particularly the anti-nucleon degree of freedom, even though the anti-nucleon constitutes only a small portion of the state vector.

## Method

The authors solved the scalar Yukawa theory non-perturbatively using a Fock sector expansion truncated at three particles, including the anti-nucleon degree of freedom (unquenched solution: |N⟩ + |πN⟩ + |ππN⟩ + |NN̄N⟩). They constructed and renormalized the light-front Hamiltonian using a Fock sector dependent scheme, solved the eigenvalue equation to obtain wave functions, and computed the current matrix element. The form factor was evaluated in general frames with q⁺ ≠ 0 rather than the conventional Drell-Yan frame (ζ = 0), and the frame dependence was used to quantitatively gauge the loss of Lorentz covariance.

## Key numbers

- The mock nucleon mass was set to m = 0.94 GeV and the mock pion mass to μ = 0.14 GeV
- The dimensionless coupling constant was defined as α = g²/(16πm²), corresponding to the tree-level Yukawa potential V(r) = -αe^{-μr}/r
- The Pauli-Villars regularization scheme was employed with physical masses m₀ ≡ m and μ₀ ≡ μ, and PV masses satisfying 0 < μ < m ≪ μ_PV < m_PV
- The Fock sector expansion included up to three particles: one mock nucleon plus two mock pions, or two mock nucleons plus one mock anti-nucleon

## Context

This work extends previous non-perturbative solutions of scalar Yukawa theory with three-body truncation (Karmanov 2008, 2016) and four-body truncation (Li 2014, 2015), which were solved with the quenched approximation excluding antiparticle degrees of freedom. The authors note that Karmanov (2012) showed that Poincaré symmetry could be partially restored by incorporating the anti-particle degree of freedom. The paper also builds on the covariant analysis framework for light-front hadronic matrix elements developed by Cao (2024), and addresses the long-standing discrepancy identified by Isgur and Llewellyn-Smith (1988) regarding frame dependence of form factors evaluated with longitudinal momentum transfer.
