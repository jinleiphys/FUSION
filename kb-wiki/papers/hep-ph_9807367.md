# Mixing and decay constants of pseudoscalar mesons: Octet-singlet vs. quark flavor basis

> Although eta-eta' mixing is qualitatively well understood as a consequence of the U(1)_A anomaly in QCD together with a broken SU(3) flavor symmetry, until recently the values of decay and mixing parameters of the eta and eta' were only approximately known, e.g. values for the octet-singlet mixing angle between -20 degrees and -10 degrees could be found in the literature. New experimental data, especially for the reactions gamma gamma* -> eta, eta' and B -> eta' K, together with new theoretical results from higher order corrections in chiral perturbation theory stimulated a phenomenological re-analysis of this subject, which led to a coherent qualitative and quantitative picture of eta-eta' mixing and even of eta-eta'-eta_c mixing.

# Mixing and Decay Constants of Pseudoscalar Mesons: Octet-Singlet vs. Quark Flavor Basis

## Key claim

The paper argues that a proper treatment of eta-eta' mixing requires distinguishing between matrix elements of local currents (decay constants) and overall state mixing, with the quark flavor basis providing a simpler description than the octet-singlet basis when SU(3) flavor symmetry is broken. The analysis claims to produce a coherent quantitative picture of eta-eta' mixing and even eta-eta'-eta_c mixing, but the results depend on the specific choice of basis and the phenomenological input from processes like gamma gamma* -> eta, eta' and B -> eta' K.

## Method

The author defines two mixing schemes: the quark flavor basis (with mixing angle phi) and the octet-singlet basis (with mixing angle theta = phi - arctan sqrt(2)), then derives the relationship between decay constants and state mixing in each basis. Using the divergence of axial-vector currents including the U(1)_A anomaly, the mass matrix in the quark flavor basis is constructed and solved for the mixing parameters, which are then compared to phenomenological determinations from decay widths of eta, eta' -> gamma gamma, J/psi -> P gamma, and gamma* gamma -> P form factors.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle in quark flavor basis: phi = 39.3 degrees +/- 1.0 degrees (phenomenological)
- Octet-singlet mixing angle: theta = -15.4 degrees +/- 1.0 degrees (phenomenological)
- Decay constant ratio: f_q/f_pi = 1.07 +/- 0.02 (phenomenological)
- Decay constant ratio: f_s/f_pi = 1.34 +/- 0.06 (phenomenological)
- Theoretical (SU(3) flavor symmetry) values: f_q/f_pi = 1.00, f_s/f_pi = 1.41, phi = 42.4 degrees
- J/psi -> eta' gamma / J/psi -> eta gamma ratio yields phi = 39.0 degrees +/- 1.6 degrees (or theta = -15.7 degrees +/- 1.6 degrees)
- Intrinsic charm decay constants: f_eta^c = (-2.4 +/- 0.2) MeV, f_eta'^c = (-6.3 +/- 0.6) MeV
- Charm mixing angle: theta_c = -1.0 degrees
- Parameter y = f_q/f_s = 0.81 +/- 0.03 (phenomenological)
- Anomaly parameter a^2 = 0.265 GeV^2 (phenomenological)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological re-analysis of eta-eta' mixing stimulated by new experimental data from CLEO (1997) on gamma gamma* -> eta, eta' and B -> eta' K, together with higher-order chiral perturbation theory results from Leutwyler (1997). The author's approach with collaborators (Feldmann, Kroll, Stech, 1998) provides a coherent framework that resolves discrepancies between earlier mixing angle determinations that ranged from -20 degrees to -10 degrees. The analysis enables improved predictions for J/psi radiative decays and clarifies that intrinsic charm in the eta' cannot be the dominant contribution to B -> K eta' decays, contrary to assumptions by some authors (Chao, 1997; Hai-Yang Cheng, 1997). The formalism also generalizes to eta-eta'-eta_c mixing, with results consistent with independent bounds from transition form factors (Feldmann, Kroll, 1997).
