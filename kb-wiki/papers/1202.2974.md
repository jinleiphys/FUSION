# Pair-transfer probability in open- and closed-shell Sn isotopes

> Approximations made to estimate two-nucleon transfer probabilities in ground-state to ground-state transitions and physical interpretation of these probabilities are discussed. Probabilities are often calculated by approximating both ground states, of the initial nucleus A and of the final nucleus A\pm 2 by the same quasiparticle vacuum. We analyze two improvements of this approach. First, the effect of using two different ground states with average numbers of particles A and A\pm2 is quantified. Second, by using projection techniques, the role of particle number restoration is analyzed. Our analysis shows that the improved treatment plays a role close to magicity, leading to an enhancement of the pair-transfer probability. In mid-shell regions, part of the error made by approximating the initial and final ground states by a single vacuum is compensated by projecting onto good particle number. Surface effects are analyzed by using pairing interactions with a different volume-to-surface mixing. Finally, a simple expression of the pair-transfer probability is given in terms of occupation probabilities in the canonical basis. We show that, in the canonical basis formulation, surface effects which are visible in the transfer probability are related to the fragmentation of single-particle occupancies close to the Fermi energy. This provides a complementary interpretation with respect to the standard quasiparticle representation where surface effects are generated by the integrated radial profiles of the contributing wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using two different ground states (for nuclei A and A±2) instead of approximating both by the same quasiparticle vacuum enhances the pair-transfer probability near shell closures, while in mid-shell regions the error from the single-vacuum approximation is partially compensated by particle-number projection.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for the chain of Sn isotopes using a zero-range density-dependent pairing interaction with two different surface/volume mixing parameters (pure surface with η=1 and mixed with η=0.65). They derived pair-transfer probabilities in both the quasiparticle representation and the canonical basis representation, comparing the standard approximation (using only the wave functions of nucleus A) with an improved treatment that uses wave functions of both A and A±2, and further applied particle-number projection techniques.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength parameter V0 = -670 MeV fm⁻¹ for pure surface interaction (η=1)
- Pairing strength parameter V0 = -390 MeV fm⁻¹ for mixed interaction (η=0.65)
- Box radius of 20 fm used in coordinate-space HFB calculations
- Cutoff of 60 MeV in quasiparticle energies and maximum j value of 15/2
- ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ and α = 1 in the density-dependent pairing interaction
- Fomenko discretization with 199 points used for gauge-angle integration in particle-number projection

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Shimoyama and Matsuo (2011) on pair-transfer probabilities in Sn isotopes, who used only the single-vacuum approximation. It also connects to the original formulation of Broglia and collaborators (1970s) where wave functions of both initial and final nuclei appear. The results enable improved interpretation of two-neutron transfer reactions as experimental probes of pairing interaction properties, particularly the surface/volume mixing character, and provide a framework for treating both closed-shell and open-shell nuclei within a unified HFB-based approach.
