# Charge-Exchange and multi-scattering effects in (e,e'n) knockout

> Final-state interactions in (e,e'n) knockout reactions in the quasi-free region are studied by considering the multistep direct scattering of the ejectile nucleon. Primary and multiple particle emission are included within the same model and are found to become important with increasing excitation energy. Charge-exchange effects taken into account through the two-step (e,e'p)(p,n) and three-step (e,e'p)(p,N)(N,n) processes are also found to increase with energy. A comparison with the results obtained with an isospin-dependent optical potential at small excitation energies is presented.

## Key claim

Charge-exchange and multi-scattering effects in (e,e'n) knockout reactions on ⁴⁰Ca are significant and increase with excitation energy, contrary to earlier estimates using isospin-dependent optical potentials which found these effects to be negligible (≈1-2%); the present multistep direct scattering (MSD) approach finds contributions up to ≈30% even at the lowest excitation energies.

## Method

The authors applied the quantum-mechanical multistep direct (MSD) theory of Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin (1980) to describe final-state interactions in (e,e'n) reactions on ⁴⁰Ca in the quasi-free region. They calculated direct neutron knockout, primary multistep emission (including two-step (e,e'p)(p,n) and three-step (e,e'p)(p,N)(N,n) charge-exchange processes), and multiple (secondary) particle emission using the formalism of Chadwick (1994) for nucleon-induced reactions, adapted for electron scattering. The calculations used an incident electron energy of 497 MeV, electron scattering angle 52.9°, and scattered electron energy fixed at 350 MeV, with distorted waves from the optical potential of Giannini et al. (1976) and bound-state wavefunctions from a Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

- At the lowest missing energy, charge-exchange contributions from multistep processes amount to ≈30% of the total cross section, compared to ≈1-2% from an isospin-dependent optical potential approach (Giannini et al., 1989).
- The direct knockout (e,e'n) process is dominant only at lower excitation energies and forward angles where missing momentum p_m ≤ 200 MeV/c.
- Calculations were performed for ⁴⁰Ca with incident electron energy E_k = 497 MeV, electron scattering angle θ = 52.9°, and scattered electron energy E_k' = 350 MeV.
- At a residual nucleus excitation energy U = 22 MeV, the charge-exchange effect from an optical potential is ≈1-2%, while the MSD approach gives ≈30% at the same energy.

## Context

This work extends the MSD theory of Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin (1980) and the multiple emission formalism of Chadwick (1994) to electron-induced nucleon knockout reactions, specifically (e,e'n). It contradicts earlier results from Giannini et al. (1989) and Ryckebusch et al. (1989), who used isospin-dependent optical potentials and found charge-exchange effects to be negligible (≈1-2%) at low excitation energies. The findings suggest that charge-exchange in multistep processes may also be important in two-nucleon knockout reactions such as (e,e'pp) and (e,e'pn), where previous work by Giannini et al. (1994) using optical potentials found such effects negligible.
