# Elliptic flow of Lambda, Xi and Omega in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> The elliptic flow v_2 for strange and multi-strange bayons in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions is investigated with VISHNU hybrid model that connects 2+1-d viscous hydrodynamics with a hadron cascade model. It is found that VISHNU nicely describes v_2(p_T) data for Λ, Ξand Ωat various centralities. Comparing with the ALICE data, it roughly reproduces the mass-ordering of v_2 among π, K, p, Ξand Ωwithin current statistics, but gives an inverse v_2 mass-ordering between p and Λ.

## Key claim
The VISHNU hybrid model roughly reproduces the mass-ordering of elliptic flow among π, K, p, Ξ and Ω within current statistics, but gives an inverse v₂ mass-ordering between p and Λ for the selected centralities.

## Method
The authors used the VISHNU hybrid model, which connects 2+1-d viscous hydrodynamics for the QGP fluid expansion to a hadron cascade model (UrQMD) for the kinetic evolution of hadronic matter. They employed MC-KLN initial conditions with a switching temperature of 165 MeV between hydrodynamics and the hadron cascade, and set the QGP specific shear viscosity (η/s)QGP to 0.16. The elliptic flow v₂(pT) for Λ, Ξ and Ω was calculated in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions at various centralities and compared to ALICE data.

## Key numbers
- QGP specific shear viscosity (η/s)QGP set to 0.16, with starting time τ₀ = 0.9 fm/c obtained from fitting the slope of pT spectra for all charged hadrons below 2 GeV.
- Switching temperature between hydrodynamics and hadron cascade set at 165 MeV.
- For 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions, the extracted (η/s)QGP range was 1/(4π) < (η/s)QGP < 2.5/(4π) (0.08 < (η/s)QGP < 0.20), with uncertainties dominated by initial conditions from MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models.
- For LHC calculations with MC-KLN initial conditions, (η/s)QGP = 0.20–0.24 described all charged hadron data, while identified hadron v₂(pT) preferred (η/s)QGP = 0.16.

## Context
This work extends earlier VISHNU hybrid model calculations that extracted QGP viscosity from elliptic flow at RHIC (Song 2010, Song 2011) and extrapolated to LHC energies (Song 2011). It directly compares with ALICE measurements of strange and multi-strange hadron elliptic flow (Zhou 2013). The results contrast with earlier pure hydrodynamic calculations using VISH2+1 (Shen 2011), which predicted the mass ordering among π, K, p, Ξ, Ω and Λ but over-predicted elliptic flow for proton and Λ at 10-20% centrality. The VISHNU hybrid model improves descriptions of proton and Λ elliptic flow through microscopic hadronic rescatterings, though the inverse mass ordering between p and Λ indicates that hadronic cross sections in UrQMD need improvement. Higher statistics for Ξ and Ω are needed in both experiment and theory for further understanding.
