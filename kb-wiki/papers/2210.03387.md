# Ab initio calculation of charge symmetry breaking in $A=7$ and $8$ $Λ$-hypernuclei

> The separation energies of the isospin triplet $^7_Λ\mathrm{He}$, $^7_Λ\mathrm{Li^{*}}$, $^7_Λ$Be, and the $T=1/2$ doublet $^8_Λ$Li, $^8_Λ$Be are investigated within the no-core shell model. Calculations are performed based on a hyperon-nucleon potential derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order. The potential includes the leading charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction in the $Λ$N channel, whose strength has been fixed to the experimentally known difference of the $Λ$ separation energies of the mirror hypernuclei $^4_Λ\mathrm{He}$ and $^4_Λ\mathrm{H}$. It turns out that the CSB predicted for the $A=7$ systems is small and agrees with the splittings deduced from the empirical binding energies within the experimental uncertainty. In case of the $A=8$ doublet, the computed CSB is somewhat larger than the available experimental value. Using other experimental input for $A=4$ can change this prediction moving it closer to experiment.

## Key claim

The charge symmetry breaking (CSB) splittings predicted for the A=7 isospin triplet are small and agree with empirical values within experimental uncertainty, while for the A=8 doublet the computed CSB is somewhat larger than the available experimental value; using different experimental input from the A=4 systems can change this prediction, moving it closer to experiment.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for A=7 and A=8 Lambda hypernuclei using a hyperon-nucleon (YN) potential derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading order (NLO13 and NLO19), including leading charge-symmetry breaking (CSB) interactions whose low-energy constants were fixed to the experimentally known separation-energy differences of the mirror hypernuclei 4_Lambda_He and 4_Lambda_H. The nucleon-nucleon interaction used was the semi-local momentum-space-regularized (SMS) N4LO+ potential with cutoff 450 MeV, and chiral N2LO three-nucleon forces were included; all potentials were similarity-renormalization-group (SRG) evolved to a flow parameter of 1.88 fm^-1, and SRG-induced YNN forces were included. The CSB splittings were computed perturbatively from expectation values of kinetic, NN, and YN operators using wave functions from the largest accessible model spaces (N_max=10 for A=7, N_max=9 for A=8) at omega_opt=16 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the A=4 systems, the CSB splittings used as input are Delta B_Lambda(0+) = 233 +/- 92 keV and Delta B_Lambda(1+) = -83 +/- 94 keV (from Achenbach 2016 and Gazda 2016)
- For the NLO19-CSB potential, the predicted difference in Lambda p and Lambda n scattering lengths is Delta a^CSB(1S0) = 0.62 +/- 0.08 fm and Delta a^CSB(3S1) = -0.10 +/- 0.02 fm
- For the alternative STAR-based scenario (CSB*), the scattering length differences become Delta a^CSB(1S0) = 0.71 fm and Delta a^CSB(3S1) = -0.05 fm
- The estimated numerical uncertainties from model space truncation are 10 keV for A=4, 30 keV for A=7, and 50 keV for A=8 isospin multiplets
- For the A=4 systems, the NCSM results for NLO19-CSB give Delta B_Lambda(0+) = 238 keV and Delta B_Lambda(1+) = -85 keV, in good agreement with Faddeev-Yakubovsky results of 238 keV and -85 keV respectively

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Hiyama et al. (2009, 2012, 2013) who used cluster models with phenomenological Lambda N CSB forces tuned to the A=4 splittings but overestimated CSB in heavier systems or predicted shifts in the wrong direction. The present study benefits from refined experimental data from J-PARC (Yamamoto 2015) and Mainz (Esser 2015, Schulz 2016) that established significantly smaller CSB splittings in A=4 than previously accepted, and from the development of consistent chiral EFT descriptions of both charge-symmetry-preserving and CSB components of the Lambda N interaction (Haidenbauer 2021). The results enable future inclusion of chiral Lambda NN and Sigma NN three-body forces, which are needed to resolve discrepancies in the 5_Lambda_He separation energy and may further improve agreement with experiment for heavier hypernuclei.
