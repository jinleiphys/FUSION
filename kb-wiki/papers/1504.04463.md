# Determination of $U(1)_{\rm A}$ restoration from pion and $a_0$-meson screening masses: Toward the chiral regime

> We incorporate the effective restoration of $U(1)_{\rm A}$ symmetry in the 2+1 flavor entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model by introducing a temperature-dependent strength $K(T)$ to the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction. $T$ dependence of $K(T)$ is well determined from pion and $a_0$-meson screening masses obtained by lattice QCD (LQCD) simulations with improved p4 staggered fermions. The strength is strongly suppressed in the vicinity of the pseudocritical temperature of chiral transition. The EPNJL model with the $K(T)$ well reproduces meson susceptibilities calculated by LQCD with domain-wall fermions. The model shows that the chiral transition is second order at the "light-quark chiral-limit" point where the light quark mass is zero and the strange quark mass is fixed at the physical value. This indicates that there exists a tricritical point. Hence the location is estimated.

## Key claim

The effective restoration of $U(1)_{\rm A}$ symmetry is incorporated into the 2+1 flavor entanglement Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model by introducing a temperature-dependent strength $K(T)$ for the Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) determinant interaction, with the $T$ dependence determined from lattice QCD data on pion and $a_0$-meson screening masses; the model then indicates that the chiral transition is second order at the light-quark chiral-limit point, implying the existence of a tricritical point whose location is estimated.

## Method

The authors extended the 2+1 flavor EPNJL model by introducing a phenomenological temperature-dependent coupling strength $K(T)$ for the KMT determinant interaction, assuming a Gaussian suppression form $K(T)=K(0)$ for $T<T_1$ and $K(T)=K(0)e^{-(T-T_1)^2/b^2}$ for $T\ge T_1$. They determined the three adjustable parameters ($\alpha_1$ in the entanglement coupling, and $T_1$ and $b$ in $K(T)$) by fitting the model's predictions for pion and $a_0$-meson screening masses to lattice QCD data from simulations with improved p4 staggered fermions. The meson screening masses were calculated from the pole of the mesonic correlation function in the complex $\tilde{q}$ plane using the Pauli-Villars regularization scheme extended from the previous 2-flavor prescription to 2+1 flavors.

## Key numbers

* The pseudocritical temperature of chiral transition is $T_c = 154 \pm 9$ MeV from 2+1 flavor lattice QCD simulations (Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2011, Kanaya).
* The determined parameters for $K(T)$ are $T_1 = 0.79 T_c = 121$ MeV and $b = 0.23 T_c = 36$ MeV.
* The entanglement coupling parameter is determined as $\alpha_1 = 1.0$.
* The EPNJL model parameters reproduce vacuum observables: pion decay constant $f_\pi = 92$ MeV, kaon decay constant $f_K = 105$ MeV, pion mass $M_\pi = 141$ MeV, kaon mass $M_K = 512$ MeV, and $\eta'$-meson mass $M_{\eta'} = 920$ MeV.
* To match the lattice QCD vacuum pion mass of 175 MeV, the light quark mass is set to $m_l = 9.9$ MeV, yielding vacuum pole masses $M_{a_0}(0) = 711$ MeV, $M_\eta(0) = 481$ MeV, and $M_\sigma(0) = 537$ MeV.
* The deconfinement pseudocritical temperature in the EPNJL model is $T_c^{\rm deconf} = 165$ MeV, compared to the lattice QCD value of $170 \pm 7$ MeV (Aoki 2009).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $U(1)_{\rm A}$ restoration using the PNJL and EPNJL models (Costa 2005, Costa 2009, Ruivo 2012a, Ruivo 2012b) by incorporating a temperature-dependent KMT coupling determined from state-of-the-art lattice QCD screening mass data (Cheng 2010). The analysis builds on the authors' prior 2-flavor formulation for meson screening masses (Ishii 2013) and the Pisarski-Yaffe (1980) instanton suppression mechanism. The results enable estimation of the tricritical point location in the Columbia plot for 2+1 flavor QCD, addressing the open question of the chiral transition order and universality class in the light-quark chiral limit, which has been controversial in lattice QCD studies (Fukugita 1990, Karsch 1994, Iwasaki 1997, Aoki 1998, Bernard 2000, D'Elia 2005, Ejiri 2009, Bonati 2014).
