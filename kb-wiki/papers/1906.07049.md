# Weak production of strange $Ξ$ baryons off the nucleon

> The charged current Cabibbo-supressed associated $K Ξ$ production off the nucleon induced by anti-neutrinos is studied at low and intermediate energies. The non-resonant terms are obtained using a microscopical model based on the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian. The basic parameters of the model are $f_π$, the pion decay constant, Cabibbo's angle, the proton and neutron magnetic moments and the axial vector coupling constants for the baryons octet, D and F, that are obtained from the analysis of the semileptonic decays of neutron and hyperons. In addition, we also consider $Σ^\ast(1385)$ resonance, which can decay in $K Ξ$ final state when this channel is open. The studied mechanism is the prime source of $Ξ$ production at anti-neutrino energies around $2$ GeV and the calculated cross sections at these energies can be measured at the current and future neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The charged-current Cabibbo-suppressed associated $K\Xi$ production off the nucleon induced by anti-neutrinos is the prime source of $\Xi$ production at anti-neutrino energies around 2 GeV, but the calculated cross sections are only about 3-6 percent of the corresponding $\Delta S=0$ kaon production processes, consistent with Cabibbo suppression.

## Method

The non-resonant background terms were obtained from a microscopical model based on the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian expansion, using the pion decay constant $f_\pi$, Cabibbo's angle, proton and neutron magnetic moments, and axial vector coupling constants D and F from semileptonic decays of neutron and hyperons. The $\Sigma^\ast(1385)$ resonance, which can decay into the $K\Xi$ final state, was also included using SU(3) symmetry to relate its transition form factors to those of the well-known $N\rightarrow\Delta(1232)$ transition. Transition form factors for the non-resonant background were related to the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors via SU(3) symmetry, with the vector form factors protected by the Ademollo-Gatto theorem and the axial form factors given a dipole $q^2$-dependence with axial dipole mass $M_A=1.0$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The Cabibbo angle is $\theta_c\sim 13^\circ$, giving the $\sin^2\theta_c$ suppression factor for $|\Delta S|=1$ processes
- The axial vector coupling constants used are ${\cal F}^{A}(0)=0.463$ and ${\cal D}^{A}(0)=0.804$, obtained from semileptonic hyperon decays
- The axial dipole mass is taken as $M_A=1.0$ GeV
- At $E_\nu=2.2$ GeV, applying an invariant mass cut of $W_{cut}=2$ GeV reduces the cross section by about 40 percent for $\bar\nu_\mu n\rightarrow\mu^+ K^0 \Xi^-$ and $\bar\nu_\mu p\rightarrow\mu^+ K^0 \Xi^0$, and 20 percent for $\bar\nu_\mu p\rightarrow\mu^+ K^+ \Xi^-$
- The cross sections for $K^0$ and $K^+$ production are about 3 and 6 percent, respectively, of the corresponding $\Delta S=0$ processes
- SU(3) symmetry breaking effects are about 15 percent for the $\bar\nu_\mu n\rightarrow\mu^+ K^0 \Xi^-$ and $\bar\nu_\mu p\rightarrow\mu^+ K^+ \Xi^-$ channels, and negligible for $\bar\nu_\mu p\rightarrow\mu^+ K^0 \Xi^0$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model of $|\Delta S|=1$ single kaon/antikaon production (Alam 2011, Rafi Alam 2010) to associated $K\Xi$ production, and is the first theoretical examination of higher strange particle production via charged current weak interactions. The mechanism is well suited for semi-exclusive measurements at current and future experiments such as MINER$\nu$A, MicroBooNE, NO$\nu$A, and ArgoNeut, and the results can be implemented in event generators like GENIE, NEUT, NuWro, and GiBUU. The $\Sigma^\ast(1385)$ resonance plays a much more important role here than in single antikaon production (Alam 2011), though higher resonances like $\Lambda(1520)$ (Shyam 2011) may also contribute at higher energies and remain to be included in future work.
