# Left-right symmetry and electric dipole moments. A global analysis

> We perform a global fit using results of searches for electric dipole moments (EDM) of diamagnetic systems within the context of the minimal left-right symmetric model. In this way, we disentangle the new "left-right" electroweak and $\barθ$ contributions that cannot be separated using a single EDM system. Although the fit is done for a specific model, the approach can be applied to any particle physics model. Finally, we revisit the constraint on the $D$ coefficient in $β$-decay and find that current EDM bounds do not preclude observation of this T-violating effect in a possible next generation $β$-decay experiment.

## Key claim
The authors perform a global fit of electric dipole moment (EDM) results from multiple diamagnetic systems within the minimal left-right symmetric model (mLRSM) to disentangle the otherwise inseparable contributions from the QCD theta parameter (θ̄) and the new "left-right" electroweak CP-violating operator. The key hedge is that while the fit is done for a specific model, the approach can be applied to any particle physics model, and the bounds on θ̄ and the left-right coupling κLR are significantly relaxed (by roughly one order of magnitude) compared to analyses that assume only one source of CP violation.

## Method
The authors performed a χ² fit (with 2 degrees of freedom) using experimental EDM results for the neutron, mercury (Hg), xenon (Xe), radium (Ra), and thallium fluoride (TlF) systems, incorporating theoretical uncertainties in hadronic, nuclear, and atomic parameters via a range fit. They varied the hadronic parameters αn, βn, λ0, λ1, and γ1 within updated ranges from chiral effective field theory, and the nuclear Schiff moment parameters a0, a1, and κS within ranges from the literature, selecting the parameter combinations that give the most conservative (weakest) bounds on θ̄ and κLR.

## Key numbers
- The 95% confidence level upper bound on |θ̄| is approximately 2.4×10⁻⁹, relaxed from the typical single-system bound of about 1.5×10⁻¹⁰.
- The 95% confidence level upper bound on κLR is approximately 3×10⁻⁶, relaxed from the sole-source bound of about 3×10⁻⁶ (the sole-source bound is also approximately 3×10⁻⁶).
- The best-fit point lies near θ̄ ≈ 4×10⁻¹⁰ and κLR ≈ 3×10⁻⁶.
- The updated range for the hadronic parameter γ1^{φud} is (254–552)×10⁻⁷, based on results from Cirigliano (2016).
- The updated range for the hadronic parameter λ0 is 0.013–0.018, based on results from deVries (2015).
- The experimental limit on the neutron D coefficient in β-decay is D_n = (−1.0 ± 2.1)×10⁻⁴, leaving a window for new physics observation up to D_n ∼ 10⁻⁷.

## Context
This work extends the global analysis approach of Chupp (2014, 2017) by applying it specifically to the mLRSM, using updated hadronic parameters from Cirigliano (2016) and deVries (2015). The analysis shows that when both θ̄ and the left-right operator contribute, EDM bounds are significantly weaker than in sole-source analyses, and that current EDM constraints do not preclude observation of T-violation in neutron β-decay at the level of D_n ∼ 10⁻⁷, which is larger than the uncertainty from final-state interactions computed by Ando (2009). This enables future β-decay experiments to potentially observe CP violation even if EDM searches remain null.
