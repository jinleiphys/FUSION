# Density dependent speed of sound and its consequences in neutron stars

> We introduce a parametrized density-dependent speed of sound and construct an ensemble of equations of state for neutron stars which are found to closely resemble the realistic equations of state calculated using relativistic mean field theory. We show that each of these parameters display an unique feature relevant to the properties of the compact stars. The emergence of special points in the Mass-Radius plot is a significant outcome for neutron stars which is more commonly seen in case of hybrid stars. We have also shown that the curvature term in the speed of sound changes its sign for these hadronic equations of state without the matter reaching the conformal limit or undergoing any phase transition. It is related to the 1st derivative of the energy per nucleon reaching a maximum. We have also examined the detailed behavior of the trace anomaly and polytropic index for RMF models, as well as for a density-dependent parametrized speed of sound. Our analysis demonstrates that the sign of the trace anomaly at high densities is sensitive to the stiffness or softness of the EOS. Different observational constraints from mass-radius and tidal deformability can restrict the range of parameters in the proposed speed of sound model.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a three-parameter density-dependent speed of sound parametrization that can closely reproduce realistic relativistic mean field (RMF) equations of state, and demonstrates that the sign change in the curvature term \(\beta\) of the speed of sound can occur in purely hadronic matter without the system reaching the conformal limit or undergoing a phase transition, contrary to previous interpretations.

## Method

The authors parametrize the speed of sound as \(C_s^2 = a[1 - \exp(-b (\varepsilon/\varepsilon_0)^c)]\) with saturation energy density \(\varepsilon_0 = 140\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), then integrate to obtain the equation of state. They fit this parametrization to six RMF models (NL3, GM1, IUFSU, FSU2R, DD2, DDME2) and compare thermodynamic quantities including the slope term \(\alpha\), curvature term \(\beta\), polytropic index \(\gamma\), and trace anomaly \(\Delta\). The TOV equations are solved to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformability for the parametrized equations of state.

## Key numbers

* The saturation energy density is taken as \(\varepsilon_0 = 140\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\).
* The parameter \(a\) is varied from 0.3 to 0.9, \(b\) from 0.10 to 0.20, and \(c\) from 1.4 to 2.0.
* For the IUFSU RMF model, the curvature term \(\beta\) changes sign at a density of \(\rho = 0.7\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The polytropic index \(\gamma\) for the hadronic models ranges from 1.15 to 3.5.
* The combined quantity \(d_c\) spans a range of \([0, 0.45]\).
* The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 is \(70 \le \Lambda_{1.4} \le 580\).

## Context

This work extends the constant speed of sound parametrization used for quark matter by Alford (2013) and Alford (2017) to a density-dependent form for hadronic matter. It directly contradicts the interpretation of Marczenko (2023) that a sign change in the curvature term \(\beta\) signals the onset of strongly coupled conformal matter or a phase transition, showing instead that this behavior occurs in pure hadronic RMF models. The results enable future studies to use the parametrization for rapid exploration of equation of state space under observational constraints from pulsar mass-radius measurements (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451) and gravitational wave events (GW170817).
