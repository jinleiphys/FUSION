# Coulomb effects on growth of instabilities in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We study the effects of the Coulomb interaction on the growth of unstable modes in asymmetric nuclear matter. In order to compare with previous calculations we use a semiclassical approach based on the linearized Vlasov equation. Moreover, a quantum calculation is performed within the R.P.A.. The Coulomb effects are a slowing down of the growth and the occurrence of a minimal wave vector for the onset of the instabilities. The quantum corrections cause a further decrease of the growth rates.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction suppresses the growth rate of unstable modes in asymmetric nuclear matter and introduces a minimum wave vector for the onset of instabilities; quantum corrections further reduce the growth rates, with the combined suppression factor being approximately 0.7 across the range of temperatures and asymmetries studied.

## Method

The authors used the linearized Vlasov equation (semiclassical approximation) and a full quantum RPA calculation to study the growth rates of unstable density oscillations in asymmetric nuclear matter. They employed a Skyrme-like effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with parameters fitted to saturation properties, and included the Coulomb interaction via the Hartree-Fock approximation with the exchange term evaluated in the local density approximation. The dispersion relation for unstable modes was obtained by solving the coupled RPA equations for proton and neutron density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Incompressibility modulus of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: 201 MeV
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation: 15.75 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: 28 MeV (in the Bethe-Weizsacker mass formula)
- For density 0.4 times saturation density, the minimum wave vector for instability onset is about 1/5 of the Fermi momentum of symmetric nuclear matter (alpha=0)
- The wavelength corresponding to the minimum wave vector is approximately 32 fm for density 0.4 times saturation density
- Quantum corrections lower the maxima of the growth rate curves by a factor of approximately 0.85 for all temperatures and asymmetries shown
- The combined suppression factor from Coulomb effects and quantum corrections is approximately 0.7
- The wave vector of the most unstable mode for alpha=0 is approximately 0.6 times the Fermi momentum

## Context

This work extends previous studies of unstable modes in asymmetric nuclear matter by Fabbri et al. (1998) and Baran et al. (1997), which used Skyrme forces without Coulomb effects, and is motivated by experimental investigations of neutron-rich nuclei and nuclear collisions. The results are relevant for understanding fragment formation in heavy-ion collisions and the dynamics of neutron star matter, where chemical instabilities in asymmetric matter play a crucial role. The authors note that for symmetric nuclear matter, their quantum corrections agree qualitatively with the results of Ayik et al. (1995), who used a different nuclear interaction without Coulomb effects.
