# Theoretical predictions for the magnetic dipole moment of $^{229m}$Th

> A recent laser spectroscopy experiment [J. Thielking et al., Nature (London) 556, 321 (2018)] has determined for the first time the magnetic dipole moment of the 7.8 eV isomeric state $^{229m}$Th. The measured value differs by a factor of approximately 5 from previous nuclear theory predictions based on the Nilsson model, raising questions about our understanding of the underlying nuclear structure. Here, we present a new theoretical prediction based on a nuclear model with coupled collective quadrupole-octupole and single-particle motions. Our calculations yield an isomer magnetic dipole moment of $μ_{ IS}= -0.35μ_N$ in surprisingly good agreement with the experimentally determined value of $-0.37(6)μ_N$, while overestimating the ground state dipole moment by a factor 1.4. The model provides further information on the states' parity mixing, the role and strength of the Coriolis mixing and the most probable value of the gyromagnetic ratio $g_R$ and its consequences for the transition probability $B(M1)$.

## Key claim

The authors' model predicts an isomer magnetic dipole moment of μ_IS = -0.35 μ_N, in good agreement with the experimentally determined value of -0.37(6) μ_N, but only when the collective gyromagnetic ratio g_R is quenched by a factor of 0.6 relative to the liquid-drop value; the same model overestimates the ground state dipole moment by a factor of approximately 1.4.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier coherent quadrupole-octupole motion (CQOM) model coupled to a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with BCS pairing and Coriolis mixing (CQOM-DSM-BCS) to calculate magnetic dipole moments. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing the low-lying energy spectrum of 229Th, independent of MDM experimental data, and the collective gyromagnetic ratio g_R is treated as a variable parameter with quenching factors q_R = 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, and 1.0 relative to the liquid-drop value Z/(Z+N) = 0.393.

## Key numbers

- Isomer MDM μ_IS = -0.347 μ_N for q_R = 0.6, compared to experimental value -0.37(6) μ_N from Thielking et al. (2018)
- Isomer MDM μ_IS = -0.323 μ_N for q_R = 0.7, also within the experimental error bar
- Ground state MDM μ_GS = 0.528 μ_N for q_R = 0.6, overestimating the experimental value 0.360(7) μ_N from Safronova et al. (2013) by a factor of 1.47
- Ground state MDM μ_GS = 0.654 μ_N for q_R = 1.0 (no quenching)
- Predicted spectroscopic electric quadrupole moment Q_GS = 2.79 eb, close to experimentally determined 3.11(16) eb from Campbell et al. (2011)
- Isomer-decay B(M1) value ranges from 0.0081 W.u. (q_R = 1.0) to 0.0056 W.u. (q_R = 0.6)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier CQOM-DSM-BCS model (Minkov and Palffy, 2017) which predicted B(M1) values for the isomer decay, and directly addresses the discrepancy between the first experimental measurement of the 229mTh magnetic dipole moment (Thielking et al., 2018) and the previous Nilsson model prediction of μ_IS = -0.076 μ_N (Dykhne and Tkalya, 1998). The results enable refined predictions for the isomer decay B(M1) transition probability and support the need for further tuning of model parameters including hexadecapole deformation and spin-gyromagnetic quenching to bring the ground state MDM into better agreement with experiment.
