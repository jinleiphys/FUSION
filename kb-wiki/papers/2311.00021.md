# Anomalies in global SMEFT analyses: a case study of first-row CKM unitarity

> Recent developments in the Standard Model analysis of semileptonic charged-current processes involving light quarks have revealed $\sim 3σ$ tensions in Cabibbo universality tests involving meson, neutron, and nuclear beta decays. In this paper, we explore beyond the Standard Model explanations of this so-called Cabibbo Angle Anomaly in the framework of the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT), including not only low-energy charged current processes (`L'), but also electroweak precision observables (`EW') and Drell-Yan collider processes (`C') that probe the same underlying physics across a broad range of energy scales. The resulting `CLEW' framework not only allows one to test explanations of the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly, but is set up to provide near model-independent analyses with minimal assumptions on the flavor structure of the SMEFT operators. Besides the global analysis, we consider a large number of simpler scenarios, each with a subset of SMEFT operators, and investigate how much they improve upon the Standard Model fit. We find that the most favored scenarios, as judged by the Akaike Information Criterion, are those that involve right-handed charged currents. Additional interactions, namely oblique operators, terms modifying the Fermi constant, and operators involving right-handed neutral currents, play a role if the CDF determination of the $W$ mass is included in the analysis.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the most favored beyond-Standard-Model explanations of the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly (CAA), as judged by the Akaike Information Criterion, involve right-handed charged currents; however, this conclusion depends on the inclusion of electroweak precision observables and LHC Drell-Yan data, and the CDF 2022 W-mass measurement further influences which additional operators (oblique operators, Fermi-constant modifiers, right-handed neutral currents) become relevant.

## Method

The authors perform a global Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) analysis at dimension six, combining low-energy charged-current observables (neutron, nuclear, meson, and kaon decays), electroweak precision observables (Z-pole measurements, W mass), and LHC Drell-Yan processes (pp to dilepton and monolepton). They consider up to 37 independent Wilson coefficients, first imposing a U(3)^5 flavor symmetry as a warm-up, then moving to a flavor-assumption-independent analysis that decouples charged-current observables from flavor-changing neutral currents. The Akaike Information Criterion is used to compare fits with different subsets of SMEFT operators against the Standard Model.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit value of the CKM unitarity deficit is Delta_CKM = -1.48 (53) x 10^{-3}, deviating from zero at the 3-sigma level.
* The fractional uncertainties in V_ud and V_us extractions are 0.03% and 0.22% respectively, leading to an uncertainty of about 5 x 10^{-4} in |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2.
* The theoretical uncertainty in nuclear-structure-dependent corrections is taken as Delta_delta_NS,A = 3.3 x 10^{-4}, and Delta_delta^i_NS,E = 0.8 x 10^{-4} Q/MeV for transition energy Q.
* The superallowed Ft values are proportional to 1/Gamma_i, with the Fermi matrix element M_F^2 = 2 for 0+ to 0+ transitions.

## Context

This work extends previous EFT analyses of the CAA that were limited to low-energy charged-current data (Gonzalez-Alonso 2016, Falkowski 2017) or to subsets of SMEFT operators (Grossman 2019, Crivellin 2020, Kirk 2020). It also connects to analyses of the W-mass anomaly (CDF 2022) that showed explanations focusing only on electroweak precision observables can aggravate the CAA (Cirigliano 2022, Bagnaschi 2022). The CLEW framework enables near-model-independent tests of CAA explanations and can be extended to include low-energy neutral-current phenomena and semileptonic tau decays.
