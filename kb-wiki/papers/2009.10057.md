# Comment on "A sensitivity study of the primary correlators used to characterize chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation'' by Magdy, Nie, Ma, and Lacey

> This note points out an apparent error in the publication Phys.~Lett.~B {\bf 809} (2020) 135771 by Magdy, Nie, Ma, and Lacey.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the statistical uncertainties reported in Magdy, Nie, Ma, and Lacey (2020) for the quantities \(f_1\) and \(f_{\text{CME}}\) are incorrectly propagated, leading to errors that are "many" times smaller than they should be; the authors further conclude that the AMPT data points in that publication are "statistically unnatural."

## Method

The authors used a digital ruler to read off the data points of \(\Delta\gamma(\Psi_{\text{SP}})\), \(\Delta\gamma(\Psi_{\text{PP}})\), \(f_1\), \(f_2\), and \(f_{\text{CME}}\) from Figs. 2 and 3 of the target paper (MNML). They then computed \(r_1 = \Delta\gamma(\Psi_{\text{SP}}) / \Delta\gamma(\Psi_{\text{PP}})\), \(r_2 = 1/\sqrt{f_2+1}\), \(f_1 = r_1/r_2 - 1\), and \(f_{\text{CME}} = f_1/f_2\) with standard error propagation, and compared these to the values reported in MNML. They also performed quadratic fits to the computed quantities to assess the statistical consistency of the data.

## Key numbers

- The relative error on \(f_1\) computed by the authors is 33% at \(a_1=0\%\), 64% at \(a_1=1\%\), 900% at \(a_1=2\%\), 240% at \(a_1=2.35\%\), 75% at \(a_1=3\%\), and 21% at \(a_1=4\%\), compared to the MNML reported relative errors of 12%, 9.9%, unreadable, unreadable, 9.4%, and 6.5% respectively.
- The relative error on \(f_{\text{CME}}\) computed by the authors is 33% at \(a_1=0\%\), 64% at \(a_1=1\%\), 900% at \(a_1=2\%\), 240% at \(a_1=2.35\%\), 75% at \(a_1=3\%\), and 21% at \(a_1=4\%\), compared to the MNML reported relative errors of 11%, 11%, unreadable, 14%, 8.3%, and 6.3% respectively.
- A quadratic fit to the authors' computed \(f_1\) yields \(\chi^2/\text{NDF} = 0.074/3\) with a p-value of 0.995, indicating the data points are too smooth relative to the error bars.
- A quadratic fit to the authors' computed \(r_1\) yields \(\chi^2/\text{NDF} = 0.109/3\) with a p-value of 0.991.
- Quadratic fits to the individual \(\Delta\gamma(\Psi_{\text{SP}})\) and \(\Delta\gamma(\Psi_{\text{PP}})\) give \(\chi^2/\text{NDF}\) (p-value) of 5.22/3 (0.157) and 0.90/3 (0.825), respectively.

## Context

This comment identifies an error in the statistical uncertainty propagation in Magdy, Nie, Ma, and Lacey (2020), which used the method proposed by Xu (2018) to extract the chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal fraction from charge-dependent azimuthal correlators. The authors note that the issue was raised at an internal STAR meeting when the preprint (arXiv:2002.07934v1) appeared, and again when a later preprint (arXiv:2006.04132v2) used the same data, but the error was not corrected. The comment also points out that the convex \(R_{\Psi_2}\) distribution from AMPT with no CME signal (\(a_1=0\)) in MNML contradicts background studies using hydrodynamics (Bozek, 2018), toy model resonance simulations (Feng, 2018), and multiple versions of AMPT. The corrected errors make the claimed turn-on threshold effect in \(f_{\text{CME}}\) moot, and the convexity of the \(a_1=0\) result undermines the claim of little background contamination in \(R_{\Psi_2}\).
