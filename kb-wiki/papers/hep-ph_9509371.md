# The Spin-Flavor Dependence of Nuclear Forces from Large-N QCD

> We show that nuclear interactions are SU(4) symmetric at leading order in chiral perturbation theory in the large-N limit of QCD. The nucleons and delta resonances form a 20-dimensional representation of SU(4) and we show how Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry SU(4)_{sm}, under which the nucleons transform as a 4-dimensional representation, follows as an accidental low energy symmetry. Exploiting SU(4) symmetry allows one to express the 18 independent leading nucleon, delta interaction operators invariant under spin and isospin symmetries in terms of only two couplings. The three flavor analogue allows one to express the 28 leading octet, decuplet interactions also in terms of only two couplings, which has implications for hypernuclei and strangeness in ``neutron'' stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear interactions are approximately SU(4) symmetric (for two flavors) or SU(6) symmetric (for three flavors) at leading order in chiral perturbation theory, but only in the large-N limit of QCD; the symmetry is not exact in real QCD (N=3), with corrections of order 1/N^2 for SU(4) and order 1/N for SU(6), plus additional SU(3) breaking from the strange quark mass.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin-flavor dependence of baryon-baryon interactions by classifying connected Feynman diagrams in the 1/N expansion of QCD, where baryons contain N quarks. They show that leading connected diagrams scale as N and that the dominant operators involve symmetrized products of the spin-flavor generator G_ia, leading to an interaction proportional to the sum of SU(4) generators squared. They then match this large-N analysis onto the dimension-six operators of chiral perturbation theory for nucleons, deltas, octet baryons, and decuplet baryons.

## Key numbers

- The 18 independent leading N, Delta interaction operators invariant under SU(2)_I x SU(2)_J are reduced to only 2 couplings (a and b) under SU(4) symmetry.
- The 28 leading octet, decuplet interactions are reduced to only 2 couplings under SU(6) symmetry.
- For two flavors, SU(4) predicts C_T = 0 and C_S = 2(a - b/27)/f_pi^2, where f_pi = 132 MeV.
- From a square-well model of the NN potential, C_T/C_S is approximately 0.126/2.24 = 5.6%, consistent with a 1/N^2 effect.
- The spin-triplet square well has depth V_0t = 38.5 MeV and range R_t = 1.93 fm; the spin-singlet has V_0s = 20.3 MeV and R_s = 2.50 fm.
- The spin-triplet scattering length is a_1 = 5.423 fm with deuteron binding energy E_B = 2.225 MeV; the spin-singlet scattering length is a_0 approximately -20 fm.
- Gamow-Teller transitions in A=18 nuclei show SU(4)_sm symmetry at the approximately 20% level, with shell-model computations indicating 87% probability that the relevant states belong to the same SU(4)_sm multiplet.

## Context

This work extends Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry SU(4)_sm (Wigner, 1937) by deriving it from the large-N limit of QCD, and generalizes it to include Delta resonances and three-flavor octet and decuplet baryons. The SU(4) symmetry reduces the 18 independent N-Delta operators found in the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1990-1992) and van Kolck (1992-1994) to just two couplings, and similarly reduces the 28 octet-decuplet operators from the work of Savage and Wise (1995) to two couplings. The results enable systematic effective field theory analyses of hypernuclei and strangeness in neutron stars, and provide a theoretical foundation for the observed approximate SU(4) symmetry in light nuclei.
