# $K^-p$ Correlation Function from High-Energy Nuclear Collisions and Chiral SU(3) Dynamics

> The two-particle momentum correlation function of a K^-p pair from high-energy nuclear collisions is evaluated in the KbarN-piSigma-piLambda coupled-channels framework. The effects of all coupled channels together with the Coulomb potential and the threshold energy difference between K^-p and Kbar0 n are treated completely for the first time. Realistic potentials based on the chiral SU(3) dynamics are used which fit the available scattering data. The recently measured correlation function is found to be well reproduced by allowing variations of the source size and the relative weight of the source function of piSigma with respect to that of KbarN. The predicted K^-p correlation function from larger systems, which is less affected by the piSigma source function, indicates that the investigation of its source size dependence is useful in providing further constraints in the study of the KbarN interaction.

## Key claim

The measured $K^-p$ correlation function from high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions is well reproduced by the chiral SU(3) coupled-channel framework, but only when the source size $R$ and the relative weight of the $\pi\Sigma$ source function $\omega_{\pi\Sigma}$ are allowed to vary as free parameters; the fit is not unique, with acceptable solutions spanning $R \approx 0.6$–$1.1$ fm and $\omega_{\pi\Sigma} \approx 0$–$5$.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation in the charge basis of six channels ($K^-p$, $\bar{K}^0n$, $\pi^-\Sigma^+$, $\pi^0\Sigma^0$, $\pi^+\Sigma^-$, $\pi^0\Lambda$) using a realistic local potential derived from next-to-leading order chiral SU(3) dynamics that fits existing $K^-p$ scattering data and the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift. The correlation function was computed from the $s$-wave part of the coupled-channel wave functions with outgoing boundary conditions in the $K^-p$ channel, including the Coulomb potential and threshold energy differences among channels. The source was modeled as a common Gaussian with radius $R$ and channel-dependent weights $\omega_j$, and the theoretical correlation was fitted to ALICE data below $q < 120$ MeV/$c$ using a normalization constant $\mathcal{N}$ and a pair purity parameter $\lambda$.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{K}^0n$ threshold cusp appears at relative momentum $q \simeq 58$ MeV/$c$.
- Best-fit parameters for $R = 0.9$ fm give $\omega_{\pi\Sigma} = 2.95$, $\mathcal{N} = 1.13$, $\lambda = 0.58$, with $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 0.58$.
- Acceptable fits ($\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} \lesssim 1$) span the region from $(R, \omega_{\pi\Sigma}) = (0.6\ \mathrm{fm}, 0)$ to $(1.1\ \mathrm{fm}, 5.0)$.
- The statistical model estimate for the $\pi\Sigma$ source weight is $\omega_{\pi\Sigma}^\mathrm{(stat)} \simeq 2.0$ using $T_c = 154$ MeV.
- The $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance extracted from the data has $E_R = 1520.9$ MeV and $\Gamma = 9.7$ MeV, consistent with the PDG mass $1517 \pm 4$ MeV and width $15^{+10}_{-8}$ MeV.
- The ALICE collaboration fixed $R = 1.18$ fm from $K^+p$ femtoscopy; using that value requires $\omega_{\pi\Sigma} \gtrsim 8$ for a good fit.

## Context

This work extends prior coupled-channel calculations of the $K^-p$ correlation function by Ohnishi (2016), Cho (2017), and Haidenbauer (2018) by including for the first time all six coupled channels ($\bar{K}N$, $\pi\Sigma$, $\pi\Lambda$) together with the Coulomb potential and threshold energy differences. It uses the realistic potential of Miyahara (2018) based on chiral SU(3) dynamics from Ikeda, Hyodo, and Weise (2011, 2012). The successful reproduction of ALICE data (2019) demonstrates that femtoscopic correlation functions can constrain the $\bar{K}N$ interaction in low-energy regions inaccessible to scattering experiments. The prediction for larger systems ($R = 1.6$ fm) shows reduced sensitivity to $\omega_{\pi\Sigma}$, suggesting that $pA$ or $AA$ collisions could provide complementary constraints.
