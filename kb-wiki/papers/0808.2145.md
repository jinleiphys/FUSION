# Harmonic oscillator eigenfunction expansions, quantum dots, and effective interactions

> We give a thorough analysis of the convergence properties of the configuration-interaction method as applied to parabolic quantum dots among other systems, including \emph{a priori} error estimates. The method converges slowly in general, and in order to overcome this, we propose to use an effective two-body interaction well-known from nuclear physics. Through numerical experiments we demonstrate a significant increase in accuracy of the configuration interaction method.

## Key claim

The configuration-interaction method using harmonic oscillator basis functions converges slowly for parabolic quantum dots due to the Coulomb cusp singularity in the wave function, but using an effective two-body interaction adapted from nuclear physics can increase convergence rates by at least one order of magnitude without altering the computational complexity of the method.

## Method

The author analyzes the convergence properties of Hermite function expansions in one and multiple dimensions, establishing that the decay rate of expansion coefficients depends on the weak differentiability order of the wave function. For the two-electron quantum dot, the exact ground state wave function is shown to have only one weak derivative due to the Coulomb cusp, leading to slow coefficient decay. The author then applies an effective two-body interaction routinely used in nuclear physics to the parabolic quantum dot system, performing configuration-interaction calculations for up to N=5 electrons in two dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For the two-electron quantum dot ground state at coupling strength lambda=1, the exact wave function has the form Psi_0 = D(r + a)exp(-(r^2+R^2)/2) with a cusp at r=0, giving only k=1 weak derivative
- The shell-weight decay follows p(r) = o(r^{-(k+1)}) where k is the number of weak derivatives, so for k=1 the coefficients decay as |c_n| = o(n^{-(1+1+epsilon)/2}) = o(n^{-(1+epsilon)})
- The effective interaction method achieves convergence rates at least one order of magnitude higher than the original configuration-interaction method
- Typical values for GaAs semiconductors have coupling constant lambda close to 2

## Context

This work generalizes and refines findings from a previous study of one-dimensional systems (Kvaal, 2007) and extends the effective interaction approach from nuclear physics (Hjorth-Jensen, 1995; Navratil, 2000) to electronic quantum dot systems, where such effective interactions had not been previously applied. The analysis builds on smoothness results for many-electron wave functions near coalescence points established by Hoffmann-Ostenhof and collaborators (1992, 1994). The results enable practical a priori error estimates for configuration-interaction calculations of quantum dots and suggest that similar effective interaction techniques could improve convergence for other systems where harmonic oscillator basis expansions are used, including nuclear structure calculations and quantum chemistry.
