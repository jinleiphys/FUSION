# Microscopic bell-shape nonlocality: the case of proton scattering off $^{40}$Ca at 200 MeV

> This work is part of an ongoing effort to build microscopically-driven nonlocal optical potentials easily tracktable in scattering codes. Based on the separable `$JvH$' structure proposed recently \cite{arellano_22}, where the potential can be cast as the product of a radial and nonlocality form factors, we investigate its angular dependence in momentum space. We find that scattering observables have a weak angular dependence between the momentum transfer {$\bm q=\bm k-\bm k'$}, and {$\bm K=(\bm k+\bm k')/2$}. The study is focussed on proton elastic scattering off $^{40}$Ca at 200 MeV, where the $JvH$ structure is found to be inadequate. We conclude that any improvement of the $JvH$ structure of the potential can be made to the lowest order in multipole expansions in $Kq-$representation of the potential.

# Microscopic bell-shape nonlocality: the case of proton scattering off \(^{40}\)Ca at 200 MeV

## Key claim

The \(JvH\) separable form of the optical potential, which works well at incident energies up to 40 MeV, is inadequate at 200 MeV; however, the \(Kq\)-representation of the potential retains the main physics at its zero-th multipolar order, and any improvement to the \(JvH\) structure can be made to the lowest order in multipole expansions in the \(Kq\)-representation.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic optical potential based on a density-dependent infinite nuclear-matter model using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock \(g\)-matrix with the AV18 bare interaction, folded with proton and neutron densities from Gogny D1S Hartree-Fock calculations. They expanded the potential in two alternative momentum-space representations: the \(kk'\)-expansion in orbital angular momentum partial waves, and the \(Kq\)-expansion using mean momentum \(\mathbf{K}\) and momentum transfer \(\mathbf{q}\). They truncated the \(Kq\)-expansion at orders \(N=0\) and \(N=2\), transformed back to the \(kk'\)-representation, and solved the scattering equations using the SWANLOP code to compute elastic observables for proton scattering off \(^{40}\)Ca at 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Reaction cross section for the full potential: 526.5 mb for p+\(^{40}\)Ca at 200 MeV.
* Reaction cross section for the \(N=0\) truncation in \(Kq\)-representation: 528.4 mb, a difference of about 0.4% from the full potential.
* Reaction cross section for the \(N=2\) truncation in \(Kq\)-representation: 526.5 mb, agreeing with the full potential to four significant figures.
* The Perey-Buck nonlocality parameter used for comparison: \(\beta = 0.85\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic optical model approach of Arellano (2022), which validated the separable \(JvH\) form for incident energies up to 40 MeV, to the higher energy of 200 MeV where that form fails. The \(JvH\) structure relates directly to the phenomenological nonlocal optical potential of Perey and Buck (1962), which factorizes into a Gaussian nonlocal form factor and a Woods-Saxon radial shape. The results show that the \(Kq\)-representation truncated at zero-th order captures the leading physics at 200 MeV, and that the failure of the \(JvH\) form at this energy arises from neglecting the \(K\)-dependence in the radial form factors \(v^{(c)}(q)\) and \(v^{(so)}(q)\). This work enables future phenomenological constructions of optical potentials guided by microscopic approaches, with corrections to the \(JvH\) factorization now identified as requiring \(K\)-dependent terms.
