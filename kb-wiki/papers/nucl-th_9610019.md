# Coulomb Effect on phi->K+K- Invariant Mass

> We demonstrate that coulomb interaction between a static charge source and K+ and K- from phi-meson decay could introduce systematic shift in the phi invariant mass reconstructed from the K+K- pair.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction between a positively charged fireball and the K+ and K- from phi-meson decay can introduce a systematic shift in the reconstructed phi invariant mass, on the order of the shift observed in E859 Si+Au data; however, the analysis is semi-quantitative due to many unphysical assumptions (static, spherical, uniform charge fireball) and classical treatment.

## Method

The author performed a Monte Carlo simulation generating phi mesons with exponential transverse mass and Gaussian rapidity distributions, decaying them into K+K- pairs, and applying the Coulomb potential of a static spherical uniform charge fireball to shift the kaon momenta. The E859 spectrometer acceptance (5 deg and 14 deg, magnetic field polarities 4A and 4B) was applied to the resulting kaons, along with momentum cuts (0.4–3.5 GeV/c) and kaon decay probabilities. The phi invariant mass shift was computed from the Coulomb-modified kaon four-momenta as a function of phi transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- For central Si+Au collision: fireball net charge Z = 43, radius R = 5.2 fm.
- For central Au+Au collision: fireball net charge Z = 158, radius R = 8.2 fm.
- Out of 20 million generated phi mesons (rapidity 0.5–2.5, pT 0.2–2.2 GeV/c), approximately 55,000, 46,000, 10,000, and 7,500 phi mesons were accepted by the spectrometer for the 5 deg 2 KG, 5 deg 4 KG, 14 deg 2 KG, and 14 deg 4 KG settings, respectively.
- The phi mass shift in central Au+Au collision is about 2 times that in central Si+Au collision for the same spectrometer settings.
- Using a cylindrical fireball model instead of spherical increases the mass shift by a factor of 1.5–2.

## Context

This work extends the experimental observation by the E859 collaboration (1990s) of a systematic decrease in the phi invariant mass with increasing centrality in Si+Au collisions at 14.6 A·GeV/c, for which no experimental systematics had been identified. The paper provides a Coulomb-interaction explanation for that shift, and predicts a larger shift for Au+Au collisions at 11.1 A·GeV/c. The analysis is limited by its classical treatment and static fireball assumptions; the author suggests a future quantum relativistic analysis following the approach of Gyulassy (year not given in text) for single-particle Coulomb effects.
