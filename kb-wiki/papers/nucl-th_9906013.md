# Nucleonic resonance effects in the $ϕ$ meson photoproduction

> The process $γp \to ϕp$ close to threshold is investigated focusing on the role played by the {\it s}- and {\it u}-channel nucleonic resonances. For this purpose, a recent quark model approach, based on the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry with an effective Lagrangian, is extended to the $ϕ$ meson photoproduction. Another non-diffractive process, the {\it t}-channel $π^0$ exchange, is also included. The diffractive contribution is produced by the {\it t}-channel Pomeron exchange. Contributions from non-diffractive {\it s}- and {\it u}-channel process are found small in the case of cross sections and polarization observables at forward angles. However, backward angle polarization asymmetries show high sensitivity to this non-diffractive process. Different prescriptions to keep gauge invariance for the Pomeron exchange amplitudes are investigated. Possible deviations from the exact $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ symmetry, due to the configuration mixing, are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that non-diffractive s- and u-channel nucleonic resonance contributions to phi meson photoproduction are small for cross sections and forward-angle polarization observables, but backward-angle polarization asymmetries show high sensitivity to these non-diffractive processes. This sensitivity is conditional on the specific signs and magnitudes of the quark-vector-meson coupling parameters a and b', which carry significant uncertainty.

## Method

The authors extended a quark model approach based on SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry with an effective Lagrangian for quark-vector-meson coupling to phi meson photoproduction near threshold, including s- and u-channel nucleonic resonances with masses up to 2.0 GeV. They added t-channel Pomeron exchange (treated as a C=+1 isoscalar photon) as the dominant diffractive contribution and t-channel pi0 exchange as another non-diffractive process, then fitted the two free parameters (a and b') to differential cross section data at forward angles while investigating different gauge invariance prescriptions for the Pomeron amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy threshold for phi production: E_gamma ~ 1.57 GeV, corresponding to total center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) ~ 1.96 GeV
- Constituent quark mass used: m_q = 330 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator strength: alpha = 410 MeV
- Fitted parameter values: a = -0.035 ± 0.166, b' = -0.338 ± 0.075 (with adopted absolute values |a| = 0.15, |b'| = 0.3 for further calculations)
- Pomeron parameters: epsilon = 0.08, alpha' = 0.25 GeV^-2, mu_0 = 1.2 GeV, s-quark-Pomeron coupling beta_s = 1.27 GeV^-1
- Pi0 exchange couplings: g^2_piNN/4pi = 14, g^2_phipigamma = 0.143, alpha_pi = 300 MeV
- From phi to e+e- decay: f^2_phi/M_phi = 26.6 MeV, giving parameter a = 0.16 from the Pomeron analogy
- Eight nucleonic resonances included explicitly in the s-channel with masses from 1440 MeV (P11(1440)) to 2000 MeV (F15(2000))
- Resonance contributions become negligible above photon energy ~ 2.8 GeV
- Pi0 exchange contribution at E_gamma = 2.0 GeV is negligible compared to both Pomeron exchange at small |t| and resonance contributions at large |t|
- Differential cross section at E_gamma = 2.0 GeV shows s- and u-channel contributions become dominant roughly above |t| = 1.0 (GeV/c)^2
- Beam polarization asymmetry at E_gamma = 2.0 GeV increases by about a factor of 3 around 110 degrees and shows a sign change above 150 degrees when s- and u-channel contributions are included

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach of Zhao et al. (1998) for vector meson photoproduction, which previously omitted the dominant diffractive Pomeron exchange and could not constrain parameters or investigate polarization observables. It builds on the Pomeron exchange model of Donnachie and Landshoff for high-energy elastic scattering and the treatment of the Pomeron as a C=+1 isoscalar photon. The study enables systematic investigation of nucleonic resonance contributions near threshold, which is relevant for understanding OZI rule violation mechanisms reported in antiproton-nucleon annihilation experiments and for searching for strangeness components in nucleons via phi meson photoproduction.
