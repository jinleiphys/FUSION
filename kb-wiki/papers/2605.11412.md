# Multiple shape coexistence near Sn118: First 03+ lifetime measurement

> The intruder bands in Sn isotopes, built on the 2p-2h excitation across the $Z = 50$ proton shell gap, are well-known examples of shape coexistence near the neutron mid-shell region. Spectroscopic signatures for shape coexistence include enhanced $E0$ transitions between the $0^+$ band heads. However, the underlying shape coexistence and mixing has been unclear because lifetime information for the excited $0^+$ states was incomplete in $^{118}$Sn. We thus present here the first measurement of the $0^+_3$ lifetime in $^{118}$Sn using the fast-timing technique following thermal-neutron capture. The observed enhancement in $ρ^2(E0; 0^+_3 \rightarrow 0^+_2)$ of 150(30) milliunits provides compelling indications for multiple shape coexistence in $^{118}$Sn. Additionally, three distinct shapes in $^{116,118,120}$Sn naturally emerged in theoretical calculations based on the quantum-number-projected generator coordinate method employing a relativistic energy density functional.

## Key claim
The first measurement of the 0+3 lifetime in 118Sn yields a ρ2(E0; 0+3 → 0+2) value of 150(30) milliunits, which the authors interpret as "compelling indications" for multiple shape coexistence in 118Sn, but the claim is hedged by noting that the MR-CDFT calculations overestimate excitation energies and that further experiments such as Coulomb excitation are needed to firmly establish the scenario.

## Method
The 0+3 state in 118Sn was populated via thermal-neutron capture on an isotopically enriched 117Sn target at the Institut Laue-Langevin, using the FIPPS spectrometer with 8 HPGe clover detectors and 15 LaBr3 fast-timing detectors. The lifetime was extracted using the generalized centroid difference method from triple-γ coincidence data, with the total cascade energy technique to suppress background, and the weighted average from three independent decay cascades gave the final value. Theoretical calculations employed the multireference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) using the quantum-number-projected generator coordinate method with a relativistic energy density functional.

## Key numbers
- 0+3 lifetime in 118Sn: τ = 74(13) ps (weighted average from three cascades)
- ρ2(E0; 0+3 → 0+2) = 150(30) × 10−3 (milliunits) in 118Sn
- ρ2(E0; 0+3 → 0+1) = 2.0(5) × 10−3 in 118Sn
- B(E2; 0+3 → 2+1) = 0.80(14) W.u. in 118Sn
- Minimum quadrupole shape difference between 0+2 and 0+3 states in 118Sn: Δβ2 > 0.24
- For comparison, ρ2(E0; 0+3 → 0+2) = 100(20) milliunits in 116Sn (Bäcklin et al., 1981) and 120(50) milliunits in 120Sn (Wu et al., 2025)

## Context
This work extends the systematic study of E0 transition strengths in even-even Sn isotopes, following measurements in 116Sn by Bäcklin et al. (1981) and in 120Sn by Wu et al. (2025). The large ρ2(E0) values across 116,118,120Sn, combined with MR-CDFT calculations, suggest three distinct shapes (spherical, prolate, oblate) coexist in these semimagic nuclei, paralleling multiple shape coexistence observed in Ni isotopes (Rodríguez et al., 2016; Leoni et al., 2024) and Pb isotopes (Andreyev et al., 2000). The results enable future Coulomb excitation studies to extract quadrupole invariants and fusion-evaporation experiments to search for rotational bands built on the 0+3 states.
