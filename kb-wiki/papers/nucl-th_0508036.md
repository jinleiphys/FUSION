# Quarks and Antiquarks in Nuclei

> The Chiral Quark-Soliton model provides the quark and antiquark substructure of the nucleon, which is embedded in nuclear matter. This provides a new way to asses the effects of the nuclear medium on the nucleon. We calculate nuclear binding and saturation, describe the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect consistently with Drell-Yan experiments, and predict modifications to the polarized parton distributions. We also calculate the electromagnetic form factors of a bound proton, and find significant modifications of the electromagnetic form factors in the nuclear environment. In every case, the properties of the sea of quark-antiquark pairs in the nucleon are very important, and serve to mitigate the valence quark effect. The changes in the sea quarks when the nucleon is immersed in the nuclear medium are the primary mode by which consistency is maintained with experimental constraints (Drell-Yan data, magnetic moment), while still maintaining a significant effect needed to explain the deep inelastic scattering and polarization transfer data.

## Key claim

The Chiral Quark-Soliton model can describe the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect consistently with Drell-Yan experimental constraints, but only when the modifications to the sea of quark-antiquark pairs in the nucleon, rather than valence quark effects alone, are treated as the primary mechanism for maintaining consistency with experimental data.

## Method

The author embeds the Chiral Quark-Soliton model nucleon in symmetric infinite nuclear matter, solving the Dirac equation with a hedgehog pion field and a Pauli-Villars regulator in a finite Kahana-Ripka basis. Quark distributions are computed from light-cone correlation functions evaluated in the nucleon rest frame, with the nuclear medium represented by a Fermi momentum \(k_F\). The calculation includes both the valence level and the filled negative-energy continuum, with vacuum subtraction to obtain physical observables.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark mass is fixed at \(M = 420\text{ MeV}\), with the Pauli-Villars regulator mass \(M_{PV} \simeq 580\text{ MeV}\) determined from the pion decay constant \(f_\pi \simeq 93\text{ MeV}\).
- The instanton packing fraction is \(\pi^2 \bar{\rho}^4 / \bar{R}^4 \simeq 1/8\), with instanton size \(\bar{\rho} \sim 0.3\text{ fm}\) and separation \(\bar{R} \sim 1\text{ fm}\).
- The dynamical constituent quark mass from the instanton vacuum is \(M(0) \simeq 350\text{ MeV}\), falling to zero for momenta \(p \gg 1/\bar{\rho} \simeq 650\text{ MeV}\).
- Unpolarized quark distributions are calculated at Fermi momenta \(k_F = 0\) and \(k_F = 1.38\text{ fm}^{-1}\).
- The largest Kahana-Ripka basis used has cutoff \(\Lambda = 5000\text{ MeV}\) and box size \(L = 6\text{ fm}\), yielding \(\sim 1.7 \times 10^6\) basis functions reduced by symmetry to \(\sim 20,000\).

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Quark-Soliton model calculations of free nucleon properties by Christov (1995) and Diakonov (1997) to the nuclear medium, addressing the EMC effect first observed by Aubert (1983). It provides a mechanism that reconciles deep inelastic scattering data with Drell-Yan constraints from Alde (1990), which previous binding-effect explanations (Bickerstaff, 1984; Ericson, 1984) could not satisfy. The approach enables predictions for polarized parton distributions in nuclei and for medium-modified electromagnetic form factors, which can be tested at facilities such as Jefferson Lab.
