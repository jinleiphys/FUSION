# Antiproton-nucleus quasi-bound states within the 2009 version of the Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential

> We studied the ${\bar p}$ interactions with the nuclear medium within the 2009 version of the Paris ${\bar N}N$ potential model. We constructed the $\bar{p}$--nucleus optical potential using the Paris $S$- and $P$-wave ${\bar p}N$ scattering amplitudes and treated their strong energy and density dependence self-consistently. We considered a phenomenological $P$-wave term as well. We calculated $\bar{p}$ binding energies and widths of the $\bar{p}$ bound in various nuclei. The $P$-wave potential has very small effect on the calculated ${\bar p}$ binding energies, however, it reduces the corresponding widths noticeably. Moreover, the $S$-wave potential based on the Paris amplitudes supplemented by a phenomenological $P$-wave term yields the ${\bar p}$ binding energies and widths in very good agreement with those obtained within the RMF model consistent with ${\bar p}$-atom data.

# Antiproton-nucleus quasi-bound states within the 2009 version of the Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential

## Key claim
The Paris $S$-wave potential supplemented by a phenomenological $P$-wave term yields $\bar{p}$ binding energies and widths in very good agreement with those obtained within the RMF model consistent with $\bar{p}$-atom data, but only when the $P$-wave term is phenomenological rather than derived from the Paris amplitudes themselves, because the Paris $P$-wave amplitudes fail to reproduce $\bar{p}$ atom data without scaling.

## Method
The authors constructed a $\bar{p}$-nucleus optical potential using the Paris 2009 $\bar{N}N$ potential's $S$- and $P$-wave $\bar{p}N$ scattering amplitudes, treated the strong energy and density dependence self-consistently by solving the Dirac equation with the optical potential, and incorporated medium modifications of the $S$-wave amplitudes via the multiple scattering approach of Wass et al. (WRW). They supplemented the $S$-wave potential with either the Paris $P$-wave amplitudes or a phenomenological $P$-wave term with constant $f^P_{\bar{p}N}=2.9+i1.8$ fm$^3$, and performed both static calculations (where the core nucleus is unaffected) and dynamical calculations (where nuclear core polarization is included).

## Key numbers
- The phenomenological $P$-wave term used a constant amplitude $f^P_{\bar{p}N}=2.9+i1.8$ fm$^3$ to fit $\bar{p}$ atom data.
- The saturation density used for medium modifications was $\rho_0=0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The average binding energy per nucleon $B_{Nav}=8.5$ MeV was used in the self-consistent energy argument $\sqrt{s}$.
- The energy shift $\delta\sqrt{s}$ reached approximately $-200$ MeV in static calculations and approximately $-255$ MeV in dynamical calculations.
- The difference in $\bar{p}$ binding energies and widths within $1p$ and $1d$ spin doublets in the RMF approach was up to $\sim 1$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic Paris $\bar{N}N$ potential model (2009 version) to calculations of $\bar{p}$-nuclear quasi-bound states for the first time, following earlier phenomenological RMF approaches by Mishustin and by Hrtánková and Mareš (2016) that used G-parity motivated coupling constants. The study was motivated by the analysis of Friedman et al. (2015) which showed that the Paris $S$-wave potential supplemented by a phenomenological $P$-wave term fits $\bar{p}$ atom data and low-energy scattering, but that the Paris $P$-wave amplitudes alone fail to reproduce those data. The results enable direct comparison between microscopic and phenomenological approaches for predicting $\bar{p}$-nuclear states, and provide two limiting scenarios (static vs. dynamical) for the role of nuclear core polarization.
