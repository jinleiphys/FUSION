# Strange matter in compact stars

> We discuss possible scenarios for the existence of strange matter in compact stars. The appearance of hyperons leads to a hyperon puzzle in ab-initio approaches based on effective baryon-baryon potentials but is not a severe problem in relativistic mean field models. In general, the puzzle can be resolved in a natural way if hadronic matter gets stiffened at supersaturation densities, an effect based on the quark Pauli quenching between hadrons. We explain the conflict between the necessity to implement dynamical chiral symmetry breaking into a model description and the conditions for the appearance of absolutely stable strange quark matter that require both, approximately masslessness of quarks and a mechanism of confinement. The role of strangeness in compact stars (hadronic or quark matter realizations) remains unsettled. It is not excluded that strangeness plays no role in compact stars at all. To answer the question whether the case of absolutely stable strange quark matter can be excluded on theoretical grounds requires an understanding of dense matter that we have not yet reached.

## Key claim

The role of strangeness in compact stars (whether in hadronic or quark matter realizations) remains unsettled, and it is not excluded that strangeness plays no role in compact stars at all; the question of whether absolutely stable strange quark matter can be excluded on theoretical grounds requires an understanding of dense matter that has not yet been reached.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical scenarios for strange matter in compact stars, comparing ab-initio approaches based on effective baryon-baryon potentials (which produce a "hyperon puzzle") with relativistic mean field models (which do not). It analyzes the conflict between implementing dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in quark matter models and the conditions required for absolutely stable strange quark matter, and discusses constraints from pulsar mass measurements and the recent GW170817 gravitational wave event.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density is n_S ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}; densities in the center of the most massive neutron stars can reach four or more times this value.
- PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of 1.928 ± 0.017 M_⊙ (from Shapiro delay and orbital data).
- PSR J0348+0432 has a mass of 2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙ (from orbital data and white dwarf modeling).
- Neutron star radii are typically between 8 and 16 km.
- The energy per baryon of iron is 931 MeV; the paper shows that in none of the vBag model cases considered is strange matter more stable than iron in a density domain where chiral symmetry is restored.

## Context

This review extends discussions of the "hyperon puzzle" introduced by Zdunik (2012) and the problem of reconciling massive neutron stars with hyperonic degrees of freedom. It contrasts the thermodynamic bag model approach (Bodmer 1971, Witten 1984, Farhi 1984) with effective models that incorporate chiral symmetry breaking, such as Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models (Buballa 2003) and Dyson-Schwinger approaches (Cloet 2013). The paper enables future work on whether mass twin neutron star configurations (Blaschke 2013, Alvarez-Castillo 2017) could serve as indicators of a first-order QCD-like phase transition to strange matter cores.
