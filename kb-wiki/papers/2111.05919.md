# Prediction of the excitation energies of the 2$^+_1$ states for superheavy nuclei based on the microscopically derived Grodzins relation

> As the result of synthesis of nuclei with large proton numbers a new region of investigations of the structure of nuclei has been discovered. Due to the recent significant increase in the yield of superheavy nuclei their gamma-spectroscopic studies became possible. The purpose of paper is to predict the excitation energies of the $2^+_1$ states of nuclei with Z$\ge 100$ using the microscopic variant of the Grodzins relation derived based on the geometrical collective model. The excitation energies of the $2^+_1$ states of the even-even nuclei from $^{256}$Fm to $^{296}_{120}$X which differ from each other in the number of $α$-particles are predicted. It is shown that at the beginning of the chain of the studied nuclei the excitation energies of the $2^+_1$ states don't exceed 100 keV. Then $E(2^+_1)$ sharply increases with $A$ and reaches maximum value of $400-500$ keV in $^{284}$Fl or $^{292}$Og.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the excitation energies of the first 2+ states for a chain of even-even superheavy nuclei (Z=100 to 120) using a microscopically derived Grodzins relation, but the authors note that for nuclei with small deformation their predictions likely underestimate the true values, so the reported maximum energies of 400-500 keV should be considered a lower boundary.

## Method

The authors derived a microscopic variant of the Grodzins relation from the collective Bohr Hamiltonian, expressing the excitation energy E(2+_1) in terms of the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ and inertia coefficients for rotational, β-, and γ-vibrational motion. These inertia coefficients were calculated using the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM) with a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and monopole pairing forces, employing the cranking model expressions for B_rot, B_β, and B_γ. The calculations were performed for four different sets of quadrupole deformation parameters: two variants ([A] and [B]) obtained from the Strutinsky method with different spin-orbit parameters, and two sets taken from Kowal (2010) and Möller (2010).

## Key numbers

- For ²⁵⁶Fm, the predicted E(2+_1) ranges from 23 keV (Möller deformation) to 58 keV (Kowal deformation), with variant [A] giving 44 keV and variant [B] giving 49 keV.
- For ²⁸⁴Fl, the predicted E(2+_1) ranges from 140 keV (Kowal deformation) to 473 keV (Möller deformation), with variant [A] giving 217 keV and variant [B] giving 141 keV.
- For ²⁹²Og, the predicted E(2+_1) ranges from 196 keV (Möller deformation) to 532 keV (variant [A]), with variant [B] giving 523 keV and Kowal deformation giving 433 keV.
- For ²⁸⁰Cn, the predicted E(2+_1) ranges from 83 keV (Kowal deformation) to 247 keV (Möller deformation), with variants [A] and [B] giving 86 keV and 87 keV respectively.
- For ²⁷²Hs, the predicted E(2+_1) ranges from 40 keV (Möller deformation) to 75 keV (variant [A]), with variant [B] giving 72 keV and Kowal deformation giving 73 keV.

## Context

This work extends the Grodzins relation, originally formulated in 1962 and later refined by Raman et al. (2001), to the superheavy region where both E(2+_1) and B(E2) values are experimentally unknown. The authors build on their previous derivation of the Grodzins relation from the collective Bohr Hamiltonian (Jolos, 2015) and apply it to predict excitation energies for nuclei from ²⁵⁶Fm to ²⁹⁶120X, which are now becoming accessible to gamma-spectroscopic studies at the Factory of superheavy elements in Dubna. The predictions provide guidance for planned experiments and can be tested against future measurements, particularly for the transition from deformed to spherical shapes near Z=114-120.
