# Proton polarisability contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen at fourth order in chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the amplitude T_1 for forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, to fourth order in the chiral expansion and with the leading contribution of the gammaNDelta form factor. This provides a model-independent expression for the amplitude in the low-momentum region, which is the dominant one for its contribution to the Lamb shift. It allows us to significantly reduce the theoretical uncertainty in the proton polarisability contributions to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen. We also stress the importance of consistency between the definitions of the Born and structure parts of the amplitude. Our result leaves no room for any effect large enough to explain the discrepancy between proton charge radii as determined from muonic and normal hydrogen.

# Proton polarisability contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen at fourth order in chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The authors calculate the forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering amplitude T₁(0,Q²) to fourth order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding that the resulting proton polarisability contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen is too small to explain the discrepancy between proton charge radii determined from muonic and normal hydrogen, provided the amplitude is matched smoothly onto expected high-Q² behaviour from the operator-product expansion.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-averaged forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering amplitude T₁(0,Q²) using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to fourth order in the chiral expansion, including the leading contribution of the γNΔ form factor treated within the δ-counting of Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003). They separate the full amplitude into a Born piece (calculated from the Dirac equation with on-shell Dirac and Pauli form factors) and a residual structure piece, then subtract the Born contribution expanded to the appropriate order. The resulting structure amplitude is expressed as a form factor whose slope at Q²=0 defines a mass parameter M_β, which is then matched onto a dipole form with the expected 1/Q² asymptotic behaviour to evaluate the Lamb shift integral.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic polarisability β = (3.1 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, determined from real Compton scattering within the same chiral framework by Grießhammer et al. (2012)
- Mass parameter in the form factor for T₁(0,Q²): M_β = 460 ± 100 ± 40 MeV, where the first error is from the order-Q⁴ term and the second from β
- For the Lamb shift extrapolation: M̄_β = 485 ± 100 ± 40 ± 25 MeV, where the third error accounts for the choice of matching point
- Subtraction term contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen: ΔE_sub = 4.2 ± 1.0 μeV
- Total two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift: ΔE_{2γ} = -33 ± 2 μeV

## Context

This work extends the third-order HBChPT calculation of Nevado and Pineda (2008), who found a small polarisability contribution, by including fourth-order contributions known to be important for proton polarisabilities from Bernard et al. (1993) and the Δ resonance effects emphasised by Pineda (2005). It addresses questions raised by Miller et al. (2011) about off-shell form factors and by Hill and Paz (2011) about the use of on-shell form factors, while providing a model-independent constraint that supersedes the ad hoc use of the magnetic form factor in Pachucki (1999). The result enables a more reliable determination of the two-photon exchange contribution to the Lamb shift, confirming that conventional physics cannot explain the proton radius puzzle.
