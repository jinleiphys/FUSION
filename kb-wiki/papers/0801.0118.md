# Spin observables of the reaction pd-3He eta and quasi-bound 3He-eta pole

> A formalism for spin observables of the reaction $pd\to ~^3Heη$ is derived in a model independent way. The general case with a full set of six independent spin amplitudes is studied. Furthermore, approximations by five and four spin amplitudes are investigated in the near threshold region. This region is of great interest to search for a quasi-bound $^3He-η$ state, in particular, by measurement of energy dependence of relative phases of s- and p-wave amplitudes. Complete polarization experiments, allowing determination of spin amplitudes, are analyzed. It is shown that measurement of only analyzing powers and spin correlation coefficients hardly allows one to separate the s- and p-wave amplitudes, but additional measurement of polarization transfer coefficients simplifies this problem. Specific observables, given by products of one s- and one p-wave amplitudes, are found. Measurement of these observables will provide new independent information on the $^3He-η$ pole position.

# Spin observables of the reaction pd-3He eta and quasi-bound 3He-eta pole

## Key claim

The paper derives a model-independent formalism for spin observables in the reaction $pd\to ^3He\eta$ and shows that measurement of only analyzing powers and spin correlation coefficients is insufficient to separate s- and p-wave amplitudes, but additional measurement of polarization transfer coefficients simplifies this problem. The formalism is developed for the general case of six independent spin amplitudes, with specific approximations for four and five amplitudes in the near-threshold region where a quasi-bound $^3He-\eta$ state may exist.

## Method

The transition matrix element for $pd\to ^3He\eta$ is expressed in terms of six independent spin amplitudes (A, B, C, D, E, F) using a non-orthogonal basis with Pauli matrices and the deuteron polarization vector, respecting P-parity and angular momentum conservation. All spin observables (analyzing powers, spin correlation coefficients, and polarization transfer coefficients) are derived in Cartesian form for the general six-amplitude case, then reduced to the five-amplitude case (F=0) and four-amplitude case (E=F=0) by neglecting specific terms. Complete polarization experiments are analyzed for each case, and specific observables that isolate products of s-wave and p-wave amplitudes are identified for determining the quasi-bound pole position.

## Key numbers

- The pole position from prior unpolarized data (Mersmann et al.) is $Q_0=[(-0.30\pm0.15_{stat}\pm0.04_{syst})\pm i(0.21\pm0.29_{stat}\pm0.06_{syst})]$ MeV, very close to threshold
- For the four-amplitude case, ten observables (including spin transfer coefficients) are needed to completely determine all amplitudes, with three of them measurable only roughly
- For the five-amplitude case, 13 accurate measurements are required to determine all amplitudes
- In collinear kinematics, only two spin amplitudes ($M_2^{coll}$ and $M_5^{coll}$) describe the reaction completely, requiring four measurements
- The unpolarized cross section formula contains terms proportional to $\sin^2{\theta_\eta}$ that are negligible at $Q<11$ MeV based on existing data from Mersmann et al. and Smyrski et al.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Wilkin (2007) for the $pd\to ^3He\eta$ reaction, which used only four spin amplitudes, by including the full set of six independent amplitudes and investigating the five-amplitude case. The reaction has been studied experimentally at SATURNE (Berger et al.) and COSY (Mersmann et al., Smyrski et al.), where near-threshold cross section enhancement was interpreted as evidence for a quasi-bound $^3He-\eta$ state (Wilkin, 1993). The paper enables experimental determination of whether the $^3He-\eta$ system is quasi-bound ($Re p_0>0$) or anti-bound ($Re p_0<0$) through measurement of specific spin observables that isolate products of s-wave and p-wave amplitudes, complementing earlier theoretical work by Rakitiansky et al. (four-body calculations supporting quasi-bound state) and Fix et al. (supporting anti-bound state).
