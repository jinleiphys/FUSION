# Systematics from NICER Pulse Profiles Drive Uncertainty in Multi-Messenger Inference of the Neutron Star Equation of State

> We present new constraints on the neutron star equation of state (EOS) and mass distribution using a unified Bayesian inference framework that incorporates latest NICER measurements, including PSR J0614$-$3329, alongside gravitational wave data, radio pulsar masses, and nuclear theory. By systematically comparing four inference scenarios--varying in the inclusion of PSR J0614$-$3329 and in the pulse profile model used for PSR J0030+0451--we quantify the impact of observational and modeling choices on dense matter inference. We find that pulse profile systematics dominate EOS uncertainties: the choice of hot spot geometry for PSR J0030+0451 leads to significant shifts in the inferred stiffness of the EOS and maximum neutron star mass. In contrast, PSR J0614$-$3329 mildly softens the EOS at low densities, reducing the radius at \(1.4\,M_\odot\) by \(\sim 100\)~m. A Bayesian model comparison yields a Bayes factor of $\log_{10} \mathrm{BF} \approx 1.58$ in favor of the ST+PDT model over PDT-U, providing strong evidence that multi-messenger EOS inference can statistically discriminate between competing NICER pulse profile models. These results highlight the critical role of NICER systematics in dense matter inference and the power of joint analyses in breaking modeling degeneracies.

# Systematics from NICER Pulse Profiles Drive Uncertainty in Multi-Messenger Inference of the Neutron Star Equation of State

## Key claim
Pulse profile modeling systematics for PSR J0030+0451 dominate equation of state uncertainties, with the choice between a standard two-spot (ST+PDT) and a more complex unconstrained multi-hotspot (PDT-U) geometry producing significant shifts in inferred EOS stiffness and maximum neutron star mass. A Bayesian model comparison yields a Bayes factor of log10 BF ≈ 1.58 in favor of the ST+PDT model over PDT-U, providing strong evidence that multi-messenger EOS inference can statistically discriminate between competing NICER pulse profile models.

## Method
The authors employed a hierarchical Bayesian inference framework integrating NICER mass-radius posteriors for PSRs J0030+0451, J0740+6620, J0437-4715, and J0614-3329; tidal deformability posteriors from GW170817 and GW190425; 70 radio pulsar mass measurements; chiral effective field theory and perturbative QCD priors; and PREX-II/CREX neutron skin constraints. The EOS was parametrized using a hybrid approach combining a low-density empirical nuclear parameterization (with slope L and curvature K_sym of the symmetry energy) matched to a three-segment piecewise polytrope at higher densities, with posterior sampling performed via nested sampling using PyMultiNest. Four inference scenarios were systematically compared, varying the inclusion of PSR J0614-3329 and the pulse profile model used for PSR J0030+0451.

## Key numbers
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: approximately 12.2-12.3 km for ST+PDT scenarios, approximately 13.0-13.1 km for PDT-U scenarios
- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: approximately 405 for ST+PDT scenarios, approximately 669 for PDT-U scenarios
- Maximum non-rotating neutron star mass: approximately 2.2 solar masses for ST+PDT scenarios, approximately 2.4 solar masses for PDT-U scenarios
- Inclusion of PSR J0614-3329 reduces the radius at 1.4 solar masses by approximately 100 meters
- Bayes factor: log10 BF ≈ 1.58 (Δ log Z = 3.63 ± 0.019) in favor of ST+PDT over PDT-U
- PSR J0614-3329 inferred radius: 10.29^{+1.01}_{-0.86} km at mass 1.44^{+0.06}_{-0.07} solar masses
- PSR J0030+0451 ST+PDT solution: mass 1.40^{+0.13}_{-0.12} solar masses, radius 11.71^{+0.88}_{-0.83} km
- PSR J0030+0451 PDT-U solution: mass 1.70^{+0.18}_{-0.19} solar masses, radius 14.44^{+0.88}_{-1.05} km
- Primary mass distribution component: mean μ1 ≈ 1.33 solar masses, standard deviation σ1 ≈ 0.08 solar masses
- Secondary mass distribution component: mean μ2 ≈ 1.66 solar masses, standard deviation σ2 ≈ 0.21 solar masses

## Context
This work extends the hierarchical Bayesian framework of Biswas (2024) by incorporating the recent NICER mass-radius measurement of PSR J0614-3329 from Mauviard (2025) and evaluating alternative pulse profile models for PSR J0030+0451 from Vinciguerra (2023). The results contrast with Rutherford (2024), who found smaller differences between ST+PDT and PDT-U scenarios using piecewise polytrope and speed of sound parametrizations, likely due to differences in EOS modeling and inclusion of additional constraints. The analysis enables future work to use multi-messenger EOS inference as a diagnostic tool for assessing consistency across different NICER modeling assumptions, and demonstrates that joint frameworks can statistically discriminate between competing geometric interpretations of X-ray pulse profiles.
