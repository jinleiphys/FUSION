# Hot and dense matter in quark-hadron models

> We present a general approach to incorporate hadronic as well as quark degrees of freedom in a unified approach. This approach implements the correct degrees of freedom at high as well as low temperatures and densities. An effective Polyakov loop field serves as the order parameter for deconfinement. We employ a well-tested hadronic flavor-SU(3) model based on a chirally symmetric formulation that reproduces properties of ground state nuclear matter and yields good descriptions of nuclei and hypernuclei. Excluded volume effects simulating the finite size of the hadrons drive the transition to quarks at high temperatures and densities. We study the phase structure of the model and the transition to the quark gluon plasma and compare results to lattice gauge calculations.

# Hot and dense matter in quark-hadron models

## Key claim
The Hadron-Quark-Model (HQM) produces a smooth cross-over transition between hadronic and quark degrees of freedom for all values of temperature and chemical potential, not a first-order phase transition, when excluded volume effects drive the hadron-to-quark transition.

## Method
The authors couple a chirally symmetric hadronic flavor-SU(3) model in mean-field approximation to a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for quarks, using an effective Polyakov loop field as the deconfinement order parameter. Excluded volume effects are included in a thermodynamically consistent manner to suppress hadrons at high densities and temperatures. The grand canonical potential is minimized for given temperature and baryochemical potential to obtain the phase structure.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature (defined as maximum of the derivative of the scalar field) at vanishing chemical potential: T_c = 183 MeV.
- Liquid-gas phase transition critical endpoint temperature: T_cep ≈ 16 MeV.
- Quark repulsive interaction strength used in one calculation: g_{qω} = 3.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Papazoglou (1998) for the hadronic SU(3) model and the Polyakov loop potential of Ratti (2005) fitted to quenched lattice results. The HQM model produces a smooth cross-over transition consistent with lattice simulations by Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2009). The resulting equation of state has been applied to heavy-ion collision simulations, particularly for dilepton production, and further calculations are in progress.
