# New solution to the hyperon puzzle of neutron stars: Quantum many-body effects

> The hyperon puzzle refers to the challenge of reconciling the existence of hyperons in neutron star cores and the observed high masses of neutron stars. The recent discovery of PSR J0952-0607 ($2.35\pm0.17 M_{\odot}$) has intensified this challenge. Existing solutions fail to achieve such a high mass, and often predict unrealistically fast cooling that is at odds with observations. Here, we propose a novel solution to the hyperon puzzle. Using the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, we incorporate the quantum many-body effects caused by strong baryon-meson interactions into the equation of state for cold baryonic matter and find it stiff enough to support a maximum hyperon-star mass of $M_{\mathrm{max}} \approx 2.59 M_{\odot}$, which can explain all the observed high neutron-star masses. The resulting proton and hyperon fractions are remarkably low, thus the nucleonic and hyperonic direct Urca processes are significantly suppressed. As a result, fast cooling typically does not occur in ordinary neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors claim that incorporating quantum many-body effects via the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach into the equation of state for cold baryonic matter can simultaneously resolve both the mass and cooling aspects of the hyperon puzzle, producing a maximum hyperon-star mass of approximately 2.59 solar masses that can explain all observed high neutron-star masses, while also suppressing fast cooling.

## Method

The authors employ a quantum hadrodynamics model with baryons (full octet) coupled to sigma, omega, and rho mesons, and solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the renormalized baryon propagator to obtain three energy-averaged renormalization functions (Landau damping, velocity renormalization, and mass renormalization). These functions are then incorporated into a relativistic mean-field framework to compute the equation of state, energy density, and pressure for cold baryonic matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality. The hyperon-meson couplings are fixed using SU(6) symmetry relations and empirical hypernuclear potentials, while the symmetry energy slope is varied by tuning a density-dependent isovector coupling parameter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hyperon-star mass: \(M_{\mathrm{max}} \approx 2.59 M_{\odot}\)
- Observed mass of PSR J0952-0607: \(2.35 \pm 0.17 M_{\odot}\)
- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_{\mathrm{B0}} = 0.1597~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- Binding energy at saturation: \(E_{\mathrm{b}} = -16.4255~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Incompressibility at saturation: \(K = 220.5497~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: \(E_{\mathrm{s}} = 28.9727~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Three symmetry energy slope values used: \(L_{\mathrm{s}} = 60, 80, 87.56~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: \(m^{\ast}_{\mathrm{N}}/m_{\mathrm{N}} = 0.6462\)
- Hyperon potentials: \(V_{\Lambda} = -28~\mathrm{MeV}\), \(V_{\Sigma} = +30~\mathrm{MeV}\), \(V_{\Xi} = -18~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Hyperon-sigma coupling ratios: \(x_{\sigma\Lambda} = 0.5969\), \(x_{\sigma\Sigma} = 0.4223\), \(x_{\sigma\Xi} = 0.3105\)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously developed by Zhu (2024) to include the full baryon octet, addressing the hyperon puzzle that has persisted since the discovery of massive neutron stars like PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010), PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013), and PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019). Prior solutions within relativistic mean-field theory, such as those invoking hybrid hadron-quark phases or extra repulsive interactions, have failed to reach masses above about 2.2-2.3 solar masses while also predicting unrealistically fast cooling via direct Urca processes. The results enable future studies of neutron-star cooling with superfluid and superconducting effects, and provide a new framework for investigating the internal structure of neutron stars that goes beyond mean-field approximations.
