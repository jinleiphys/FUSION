# Three-body dynamics of the $a_1(1260)$ resonance from lattice QCD

> Resonant hadronic systems often exhibit a complicated decay pattern in which three-body dynamics play a relevant or even dominant role. In this work we focus on the $a_1(1260)$ resonance. For the first time, the pole position and branching ratios of a three-body resonance are calculated from lattice QCD using one-, two-, and three-meson interpolators and a three-body finite-volume formalism extended to spin and coupled channels. This marks a new milestone for ab-initio studies of ordinary resonances along with hybrid and exotic hadrons involving three-body dynamics.

## Key claim

For the first time, the pole position and branching ratios of a three-body resonance (the $a_1(1260)$) are calculated from lattice QCD using a three-body finite-volume formalism extended to spin and coupled channels, though the determination is limited by having only two three-body levels available at the simulated pion mass of 224 MeV.

## Method

The authors extract the finite-volume spectrum in the $a_1(1260)$ sector using an ensemble with $N_f=2$ dynamical fermions at a pion mass of 224 MeV, employing one-, two-, and three-meson interpolators in a variational method. They generalize the Finite Volume Unitarity (FVU) formalism to include two-particle subsystems with spin (the $\rho$ meson) and coupled S/D-wave channels, deriving a quantization condition that maps the lattice spectrum to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes. The resulting three-body scattering amplitude is analytically continued to the complex plane to determine the resonance pole position and residues.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 224 MeV; lattice spacing: $a = 0.1215$ fm
- Lattice volume such that $m_\pi L \approx 3.3$
- Two three-body levels were extracted below the inelastic scattering threshold
- The $\tilde K_2^{-1}$ parameters fixed from two-pion spectrum: $a_0 = -0.1577\,m_\pi^2$, $a_1 = 0.0133$
- Cutoff for spectator momentum: $|\bm{p}| \leq 2\pi/L|(1,1,0)| \approx 2.69\,m_\pi$
- The $a_1$ pole from lattice QCD has a real part overlapping with the PDG mass range but a width at least twice as narrow as the PDG value (which quotes $M \approx 1230$ MeV, $\Gamma \approx 425$ MeV)
- When removing the explicit pole in the parametrization, dynamically generated poles appear concentrated near $\sqrt{s} \approx 1.04$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the FVU formalism (Mai 2017, Mai 2018) to include spin and coupled channels, building on previous lattice QCD studies of three-pion systems at maximal isospin (Culver 2019, Alexandru 2020, Blanton 2021). It goes beyond the approximate two-body Breit-Wigner/Luscher approach used earlier (Lang 2014) by employing the full three-body quantization condition. The results enable future ab-initio studies of exotic and hybrid resonances where three-body dynamics are critical, and motivate further lattice calculations at additional pion masses to map the chiral trajectory of the $a_1(1260)$.
