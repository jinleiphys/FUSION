# Semi-classical and anharmonic quantum models of nuclear wobbling motion

> A semi-classical model for wobbling motion is presented as an extension to the Bohr-Mottelson model of wobbling motion. Using the resultant wobbling potential, a quantum mechanical equation is derived for anharmonic wobbling motion. We then attempt to explain the anharmonicity observed in the excited bands of two wobbling phonons in the A=160 region.

## Key claim

The semi-classical wobbling potential derived from the Bohr-Mottelson model produces at most a 10% reduction in excitation energy between the first and second phonon states (at I=30ℏ), which is insufficient to explain the nearly 50% reduction observed experimentally in the A=160 region; the paper proposes that a phase transition from principal-axis rotation to tilted-axis rotation, modeled via a control parameter a, can reproduce the observed strong anharmonicity.

## Method

The author first derives a semi-classical wobbling potential by taking expectation values of the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian under the high-spin condition I ≈ ⟨Î₁⟩ >> 1, introducing the wobbling angle θ = cos⁻¹(⟨Î₁⟩/I) as a dynamical variable. Re-quantisation is performed via a one-dimensional Schrödinger equation with potential V(θ) ∝ 1−cosθ. To address the discrepancy with experiment, a modified potential Vₐ(θ) ∝ 1−cosθ − a(1−cos²θ) is introduced, where the control parameter a governs a classical phase transition between principal-axis rotation (a < 1/2) and tilted-axis rotation (a > 1/2), and numerical solutions are obtained for the resulting energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The one-phonon excitation energy observed in ¹⁶³Lu is approximately 350 keV, which is set equal to ωₛc (the semi-classical oscillator frequency).
- At I=30ℏ, the mass parameter m is calculated as 6.7 MeV⁻¹; at I=40ℏ, m = 11.9 MeV⁻¹, using rigid-body moment of inertia for ¹⁶³Lu with (β,γ) = (0.4, −20°).
- The anharmonic wobbling potential V(θ) ∝ 1−cosθ gives at most a 10% reduction in excitation energy from the first to the second phonon state at I=30ℏ, and less than 5% reduction at I=40ℏ.
- At the control parameter a = 1.1, the ratio ΔE₂→₁ / ΔE₃→₂ = 335 keV / 167 keV ≈ 2, which agrees well with the experimentally observed anharmonicity.

## Context

This work extends the original Bohr-Mottelson model of wobbling motion (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975) and addresses the strong anharmonicity observed in two-phonon wobbling bands in ¹⁶³,¹⁶⁵Lu (DRJ02, Am03), which neither the particle-rotor model nor the random phase approximation (MSM02) could explain. The paper builds on the suggestion by Matsuzaki and Ohtsubo (2004) that a phase transition from principal-axis rotation to tilted-axis rotation causes the anharmonicity, and provides an analytical model demonstrating that spontaneous symmetry breaking and quantum tunnelling can reproduce the observed spectrum. The critical-point theory developed by Iachello (2000) was useful in constructing the strong anharmonic potential.
