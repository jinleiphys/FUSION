# The isotopic effects in the scattering and the kinetics of the atomic cascade

> {\it Ab initio} quantum-mechanical calculations of the differential and integrated cross sections of the elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb de-excitation at collisions of excited $μ^- p$ and $μ^- d$ atoms with hydrogen isotope atoms in the ground state are performed. The scattering processes are treated in a unified manner in the framework of the close-coupling approach. The used basis includes both open and closed channels corresponding to all exotic atom states with principal quantum numbers from $n=1$ up to $n_{\rm max}=20$. The energy shifts of $ns$ states due to electron vacuum polarization and finite nuclear size are taken into account. The kinetics of atomic cascade of $μ^- p$ and $μ^- d$ atoms are studied in a wide range of relative target densities ($φ= 10^{-8} -1$) within the improved version of the extended cascade model. The results of the numerical quantum-mechanical calculations of the cross sections for quantum numbers and kinetic energies of muonic atoms, that are of interest for the detailed cascade calculations, are used as input data. Initial $(n,l,E)$-distributions of muonic atoms at the instant of their formation and the target motion are taken into account explicitly in present cascade calculations. The comparison of the calculated cross sections, kinetic energy distributions at the instant of the $np\to 1s$ radiative transitions as well as the absolute and relative $x$-ray yields for both muonic hydrogen and muonic deuterium reveals the isotopic effects, which, in principal, may be observed experimentally. The present results are mainly in very good agreement with experimental data available in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that isotopic effects in the scattering cross sections and atomic cascade kinetics of muonic hydrogen and muonic deuterium are significant enough to be experimentally observable, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of a fully quantum-mechanical close-coupling approach with an extended basis including closed channels up to \(n_{\rm max}=20\), which the authors argue is necessary for reliable results.

## Method

The authors performed quantum-mechanical close-coupling calculations of elastic scattering, Stark transitions, and Coulomb de-excitation cross sections for collisions of excited \(\mu^- p\) and \(\mu^- d\) atoms with ground-state hydrogen isotope atoms. The basis included all open and closed channels corresponding to muonic atom states with principal quantum numbers from \(n=1\) to \(n_{\rm max}=20\) (extended to \(n_{\rm max}=30\) for \(2s\) states below the \(2p\) threshold), and the energy shifts of \(ns\) states due to electron vacuum polarization and finite nuclear size were included. The calculated cross sections were then used as input for an improved version of the extended cascade model to simulate the atomic cascade kinetics over a relative target density range of \(\varphi = 10^{-8}\) to \(1\), explicitly accounting for initial \((n,l,E)\)-distributions and target motion.

## Key numbers

* The energy shift of the \(2s\) state due to electron vacuum polarization and finite nuclear size is \(\varepsilon^{\rm Lamb}_2 = 202.08\) meV for muonic hydrogen and \(\varepsilon^{\rm Lamb}_2 = 203.01\) meV for muonic deuterium.
* The convergence of the partial-wave Coulomb de-excitation cross section \(3s \to 2s\) with basis extension to \(n_{\rm max}=20\) leads to variation less than 1% compared to \(n_{\rm max}=15\).
* The maximum number of closed channels in calculations for elastic \(3s-3s\) scattering and CD \(3s\to 2s\) with \(n_{\rm max}=20\) is 754 for \(J=5\) and 959 for \(J=10\).
* The partial-wave elastic cross section for \((\mu^- p)_{2s} + H\) collision shows significant changes at laboratory kinetic energies \(E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 0.4\) eV (below the \(2p\) threshold) when the basis is extended from \(l_{\rm max}=1\) to \(l_{\rm max}=7\).
* The cross sections of \(3s\to 2s\) and \(3s\to 1s\) Coulomb de-excitation are reduced by more than an order of magnitude when the basis is extended from \(n_{\rm max}=3\) to \(n_{\rm max}=20\) at all energies considered.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous close-coupling studies of scattering processes in exotic hydrogen-like atoms (Popov and Pomerantsev, various years) to muonic deuterium, which had not been calculated before, and provides a unified comparison of isotopic effects between muonic hydrogen and muonic deuterium. The results improve upon earlier semiclassical models (Leon and Bethe, 1962) and the extended standard cascade model (ESCM) by including a fully quantum-mechanical treatment with a large basis of closed channels, which is necessary to reproduce experimental data from time-of-flight and x-ray experiments. The study enables more reliable predictions for pionic deuterium and muonic tritium, and supports the analysis of precise spectroscopic experiments with pionic, kaonic, and antiprotonic hydrogen isotopes aimed at extracting strong interaction widths.
