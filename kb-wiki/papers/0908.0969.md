# Epoch Dependent Dark Energy

> We present a model in which the equation of state parameter w approaches -1 near a particular value of z, and has significant negative values in a restricted range of z. For example, one can have w ~ -1 near z = 1, and w > -0.2 from z = 0 to z = 0.3, and for z > 9. The ingredients of the model are neutral fermions (which may be neutrinos, neutralinos, etc) which are very weakly coupled to a light scalar field. This model emphasises the importance of the proposed studies of the properties of dark energy into the region z > 1.

# Epoch Dependent Dark Energy

## Key claim

The authors present a model where the dark energy equation of state parameter w can approach -1 near a specific redshift z (e.g., z = 1) while remaining greater than -0.2 from z = 0 to z = 0.3 and for z > 9, but this behavior depends on choosing parameters that are only weakly constrained and assumes a homogeneous neutral fermion background coupled to a light scalar field.

## Method

The authors model a system of neutral fermions (which could be neutrinos, neutralinos, or other candidates) interacting with a light scalar field via Yukawa coupling, solving the coupled field equations in the adiabatic approximation for a homogeneous Fermi sea. They compute the equation of state parameter w from the total energy density of the coupled fermion-scalar system as a function of the Fermi momentum, which scales with the cosmological scale factor a, then convert this to a function of redshift z by fixing parameters to match the observed dark energy density near the minimum of w.

## Key numbers

- For K0 = 10^6, the minimum value of w is -0.94709, occurring at log10(x_F,m) = -1.809, with the approximate location log10(x_F,1) = -1.841
- For K0 = 10^9, the minimum value of w is -0.99453, occurring at log10(x_F,m) = -2.806, with the approximate location log10(x_F,1) = -2.841
- For K0 = 10^12, the minimum value of w is -0.99945, occurring at log10(x_F,m) = -3.806, with the approximate location log10(x_F,1) = -3.841
- The dark energy density is quoted as rho_DE = (3.20 +/- 0.4) x 10^(-47) GeV^4, corresponding to m_E = 2.4 meV
- For K0 = 10^6, the neutral fermion mass m_n^(0) = 150 meV (neutrino range), with implied fermion density 47 x 10^3 cm^(-3)
- For K0 = 10^54, the neutral fermion mass m_n^(0) = 150 GeV (neutralino range), with implied fermion density 3.5 x 10^(-8) cm^(-3)
- For an example with present neutral fermion density 100 cm^(-3) extrapolated to 800 cm^(-3) at z = 1, m_n^(0) = 9.2 eV, x_F = 3 x 10^(-5), K0 = 9.7 x 10^4, and the minimum w = -0.8

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier neutrino clustering model (Stephenson 1996) and the concept of mass-varying neutrinos interacting with scalar fields introduced by Fardon, Nelson, and Weiner (2003). It contrasts with the approach of Mota et al. (2008, 2009) who introduced neutrino clustering to obtain redshift-dependent dark energy, by showing that a homogeneous model can also produce epoch-dependent w. The model enables future observational tests of whether w varies with redshift, particularly through proposed experiments probing dark energy properties at z > 1.
