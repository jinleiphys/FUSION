# New results for the two neutrino double beta decay in deformed nuclei with angular momentum projected basis

> Four nuclei which are proved to be $2νββ$ emitters ($^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{150}$Nd, $^{238}$U), and four suspected, due to the corresponding Q-values, to have this property ($^{148}$Nd, $^{154}$Sm, $^{160}$Gd, $^{232}$Th), were treated within a proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a projected spherical single particle basis. The advantage of the present procedure over the ones using a deformed Woods Saxon or Nilsson single particle basis is that the actual pnQRPA states have a definite angular momentum while all the others provide states having only K as a good quantum number. The model Hamiltonian involves a mean field term yielding the projected single particle states, a pairing interaction for alike nucleons and a dipole-dipole proton-neutron interaction in both the particle-hole (ph) and particle-particle (pp) channels. The effect of nuclear deformation on the single beta strength distribution as well as on the double beta Gamow-Teller transition amplitude (M$_{\rm GT}$) is analyzed. The results are compared with the existent data and with the results from a different approach, in terms of the process half life T$_{1/2}$. The case of different deformations for mother and daughter nuclei is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using an angular-momentum-projected spherical single-particle basis within a proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) provides a more consistent treatment of deformed double-beta-decay nuclei than approaches using deformed Woods-Saxon or Nilsson bases, because the resulting pnQRPA states have definite angular momentum rather than only the K quantum number. The authors note that this advantage is particularly relevant because double-beta emitters are only moderately deformed, making the strong-coupling approximation used in intrinsic-frame methods questionable.

## Method

The authors constructed a projected spherical single-particle basis from a particle-core Hamiltonian, where the core is described by a harmonic quadrupole boson Hamiltonian and the particle-core interaction yields deformed states that are then angular-momentum-projected. They applied the BCS formalism for pairing and then treated the proton-neutron dipole-dipole interaction in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels within the pnQRPA framework, using a model Hamiltonian with separable interactions. The Gamow-Teller transition amplitude M_GT and half-lives for 2νββ decay were calculated for eight nuclei, including cases where mother and daughter nuclei have different deformations.

## Key numbers

- Four nuclei confirmed as 2νββ emitters were treated: ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹⁵⁰Nd, ²³⁸U; four suspected emitters were also treated: ¹⁴⁸Nd, ¹⁵⁴Sm, ¹⁶⁰Gd, ²³²Th
- The ph interaction strength was taken as χ = 5.2/A⁰·⁷ MeV and the pp strength as χ₁ = 0.58/A⁰·⁷ MeV, following Homma (1996)
- Pairing strengths for mother nuclei follow G_p = 12.186/A + 0.06931 and G_n = 8.2745/A + 0.11266; for daughter nuclei G_p = 13.806/A + 0.06765 and G_n = 8.1563/A + 0.13455
- For ¹⁵⁰Sm, the pairing strengths are equivalent to G_p = 22.86/A and G_n = 25.624/A
- The spherical shell model parameters use ℏω₀ = 41A⁻¹/³, with (κ,μ) values given for three groups of nuclei: (0.08, 0.0) for Ge/Se isotopes, (0.0637, 0.6) for Nd/Sm/Gd isotopes, and (0.0577, 0.65) for Th/U isotopes

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism (Raduta 1991) that first applied pnQRPA with a projected spherical basis to double-beta decay, but now incorporates volume conservation leading to nonlinear deformation dependence of single-particle energies, which was ignored in the earlier linear approximation. The approach contrasts with methods using Nilsson or deformed Woods-Saxon bases (Homma 1996, Sarriguren 1999, Engel 1999) that produce states with only K as a good quantum number rather than definite angular momentum. The results enable more reliable extraction of the pp interaction strength from experimental half-lives, since the deformation dependence pushes the pnQRPA breakdown point to larger χ₁ values, making the fitted strength less ambiguous.
