# J/Psi Propagation in Hadronic Matter

> We study J/$ψ$ propagation in hot hadronic matter using a four-flavor chiral Lagrangian to model the dynamics and using QCD sum rules to model the finite size effects manifested in vertex interactions through form factors. Charmonium breakup due to scattering with light mesons is the primary impediment to continued propagation. Breakup rates introduce nontrivial temperature and momentum dependence into the J/$ψ$ spectral function.

# J/Psi Propagation in Hadronic Matter

## Key claim
The authors find that J/ψ breakup cross sections with light mesons (pions and rho mesons) are several millibarns, producing breakup rates of 5–30 MeV that modify the J/ψ spectral function at finite temperature, but the abstract does not emphasize that these results depend on using QCD sum-rule form factors rather than simpler chiral symmetry or vector meson dominance assumptions, which change coupling constants by tens of percent.

## Method
The authors construct a four-flavor chiral Lagrangian with pseudoscalar and vector meson fields, then extract momentum-dependent vertex form factors from QCD sum rules for the couplings g_{DDJ/ψ}, g_{D*Dπ}, and g_{DDρ}. They compute scattering amplitudes for J/ψ + π → D*D̅ and J/ψ + ρ → D*D̅* using t-channel, u-channel, and four-point diagrams, enforcing gauge invariance via Ward identities, and then evaluate the J/ψ spectral function from the imaginary part of the self-energy at finite temperature.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant g_{D*Dπ} = 6.39, fixed from the measured decay width Γ(D*± → Dπ) = 96 ± 4 ± 22 keV
- Coupling constant g_{DDJ/ψ} = 16.4 from QCD sum rules, compared to 7.7 from strict vector meson dominance
- Coupling constant g_{DDρ} = 4.4 from QCD sum rules, compared to 5.6 from vector meson dominance and chiral symmetry
- Form factor cutoff parameter Λ_D = 3.5 GeV for monopole form factors at D-meson vertices
- Breakup rates fed into the J/ψ spectral function range from 5 to 30 MeV
- Spectral function evaluated at temperatures of 150 MeV and 200 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of φ meson suppression (Haglin 1995, Koch 2002) to the charmonium sector, addressing whether hadronic breakup could mimic the QGP suppression signal proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986). The cross sections are similar in magnitude to other meson-exchange models but differ in energy dependence due to the QCD sum-rule form factors; the authors also note that quark-exchange models (Wong 2002) give cross sections of order 1 mb. The results enable more realistic assessments of J/ψ suppression as a QGP indicator at SPS and RHIC energies.
