# Quark-Gluon Plasma and Nucleons a la Laughlin

> Inspired by Laughlin's theory of the fractional quantum Hall effect, we explore the topological nature of the quark-gluon plasma and the nucleons in the context of the Clifford algebra. In our model, each quark is transformed into a composite particle via the simultaneous attachment of a spin monopole and an isospin monopole. This is induced by a novel kind of meson endowed with both spin and isospin degrees of freedom. The interactions in the strongly coupled quark-gluon system are governed by the topological winding number of the monopoles, which is an odd integer to ensure that the overall wave function is antisymmetric. The states of the quark-gluon plasma and the nucleons are thus uniquely determined by the combination of the monopole winding number m and the total quark number N. The radius squared of the quark-gluon plasma droplet is expected to be proportional to mN. We anticipate the observation of such proportionality in the heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that both quark-gluon plasma droplets and nucleons can be described by a unified wave function where each quark is simultaneously attached to a spin monopole and an isospin monopole, with the topological winding number \(m\) required to be an odd integer to ensure overall antisymmetry of the fermionic wave function; the radius squared of the QGP droplet is expected to be proportional to \(mN\), where \(N\) is the total quark number.

## Method

The author constructs a wave function for strongly coupled quark-gluon systems using the Clifford algebra \(Cl(1,3)\), inspired by Laughlin's wave function for the fractional quantum Hall effect. Each quark is attached to a joint spin-isospin monopole configuration characterized by a topological winding number \(m\), induced by a novel meson field \(\hat{\pi}\) with both spin and isospin degrees of freedom. The wave function is separated into a phase-decorated unitary part (encoding the monopole attachments) and an amplitude-modulation part (encoding interactions via a linear gluon-string potential and a three-dimensional Coulomb-like term). The color degree of freedom is treated implicitly, with the total number of valence quarks \(N\) being a multiple of three and the baryon number \(B\) odd.

## Key numbers

* The topological winding number \(m\) must be an odd integer to ensure the overall fermionic wave function is antisymmetric.
* The total number of valence quarks \(N\) is a multiple of three, i.e., \(N = 3B\), where \(B\) is the total baryon number; \(B\) is assumed to be an odd integer so that \(N = 3, 9, 15, \dots\).
* The quark density is given by \(n(\vec{r}) = 1/(2\pi m l_\sigma^2) \cdot 1/|\vec{r} - \vec{r}_c|\), where \(l_\sigma^2\) reparameterizes the string tension.
* The background charge density is \(\rho(\vec{r}) = 1/(2\pi l_\sigma^2) \cdot 1/|\vec{r} - \vec{r}_c|\).
* The inverse temperature parameter in the amplitude-modulation part is \(1/\beta = m\Lambda\), where \(\Lambda\) is a characteristic QCD energy scale of the order of the Hagedorn temperature.

## Context

This work extends the topological approach of Laughlin (1983) from the fractional quantum Hall effect to the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma and nucleons, drawing on the monopole condensation ideas of 't Hooft (1974) and Mandelstam (1976) for confinement, as well as the Skyrmion model of Skyrme (1961) for nucleons. It differs from prior chromo-magnetic monopole approaches to the QGP (Liao and Shuryak, 2007; Ratti and Shuryak, 2009) by focusing on spin and isospin monopoles rather than color-magnetic monopoles. The framework enables a unified topological description of both hadronic bound states and the QGP, with the predicted proportionality between droplet radius squared and \(mN\) potentially testable in heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.
