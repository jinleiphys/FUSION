# Extracting Parton Distribution Functions from Lattice QCD Calculations

> Parton distribution functions (PDFs) are nonperturbative quantities describing the relation between a hadron and quarks and gluons within it. We propose to extract PDFs from QCD global analysis of "data" generated by lattice QCD calculations of good "lattice cross sections", which are basically single-hadron matrix elements that are lattice QCD calculable and perturbative QCD factorizable into the PDFs. To demonstrate the existence of good "lattice cross sections", we take quasi-quark distribution introduced by Ji [1] as a case study to show that it could be factorized into the PDFs to all orders in perturbation theory if it can be multiplicatively renormalized. We calculate the factorized coefficients at the next-to-leading order in $α_s$.

## Key claim

The authors propose to extract parton distribution functions (PDFs) from QCD global analysis of "data" generated by lattice QCD calculations of "good lattice cross sections", single-hadron matrix elements that are both lattice QCD calculable and perturbatively factorizable into PDFs, rather than from experimental scattering data alone. The program's viability depends on the existence of such good lattice cross sections, and the authors demonstrate that the quasi-quark distribution introduced by Ji (2013) can serve as one, provided it can be multiplicatively renormalized.

## Method

The authors define a "lattice cross section" as a single-hadron matrix element of a composite nonlocal operator with equal-time coordinates, making it calculable in Euclidean lattice QCD. They take the quasi-quark distribution as a case study and prove to all orders in perturbative QCD that a multiplicatively renormalized quasi-quark distribution can be factorized into standard PDFs via a ladder-diagram expansion, with all collinear divergences absorbed into the PDFs. They then calculate the matching coefficient functions at next-to-leading order in α_s to verify that these coefficients are infrared safe and free of collinear divergences.

## Key numbers

- The NLO coefficient function C_{q/q}^{(1)}(t) is calculated with C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c) where N_c = 3 (color factor for SU(3) QCD)
- The coefficient function contains terms proportional to ln(μ̃²/μ²) where μ̃ is the renormalization scale and μ is the factorization scale
- In the limit P_z → ∞, the terms involving Λ_t = √(μ̃²/P_z² + t²) - |t| vanish as O(μ̃²/P_z²), consistent with Ji (2013)
- The quasi-quark distribution's momentum fraction x̃ = k_z/P_z ranges over (-∞, ∞), unlike PDFs where x ∈ [0,1]

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF approach introduced by Ji (2013) by providing a rigorous factorization proof to all orders in perturbation theory, addressing the complication that quasi-PDFs have power ultraviolet divergences while PDFs have only logarithmic UV divergences. The program enables extraction of PDFs from lattice QCD calculations, particularly in the large-x region where PDFs from global fits of experimental data (Gao 2013, Martin 2009, Ball 2011, Alekhin 2013) still have huge uncertainties. The approach can be extended to transverse momentum dependent PDFs, generalized PDFs, and partonic structure of hadrons that are difficult to study with scattering experiments, such as free neutrons or mesons.
