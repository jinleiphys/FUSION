# On the relation between low-energy constants and resonance saturation

> Although there are phenomenological indications that the low-energy constants in the chiral lagrangian may be understood in terms of a finite number of hadronic resonances, it remains unclear how this follows from QCD. One of the arguments usually given is that low-energy constants are associated with chiral symmetry breaking, while QCD perturbation theory suggests that at high energy chiral symmetry is unbroken, so that only low-lying resonances contribute to the low-energy constants. We revisit this argument in the limit of large Nc, discussing its validity in particular for the low-energy constant L8, and conclude that QCD may be more subtle that what this argument suggests. We illustrate our considerations in a simple Regge-like model which also applies at finite Nc.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the standard sum rule relating the low-energy constant \(L_8\) to scalar and pseudoscalar resonance parameters is ill-defined because it involves the difference of two quadratically divergent sums, and they propose a new subtracted sum rule that is finite and universal; they conclude that QCD may be more subtle than the usual argument that only low-lying resonances contribute to low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors revisit the sum rule for \(L_8\) in the large-\(N_c\) and chiral limits, starting from the scalar and pseudoscalar two-point functions \(\Pi_S(q^2)\) and \(\Pi_P(q^2)\). They show that the naive sum rule is not well-defined because each sum over resonances is quadratically divergent, and they derive a new sum rule from a once-subtracted dispersion relation. They illustrate their considerations with a simple Regge-like model that includes finite-\(N_c\) effects through a complex exponent \(\zeta = 1 - a/(\pi N_c)\), and they compare the exact model result with the minimal hadronic approximation (MHA) that uses a finite number of resonances.

## Key numbers

* \(L_8\) in the Regge-like model is given by \(16B^2 L_8 = \kappa (m_P^2 - m_S^2)\), independent of \(N_c\), with \(\kappa\) the asymptotic constant from the parton model.
* Using \(m_S = 0.98\) GeV, \(m_P = 1.3\) GeV, \(\Lambda = 1.2\) GeV, and \(f = 87\) MeV, the model yields \(L_8 = 8 \times 10^{-4}\), compared to \(L_8(M_\rho) = 9 \times 10^{-4}\) from phenomenology.
* For \(L_{10}\) in a similar tower model with \(m_V = 0.77\) GeV, \(m_A = 1.25\) GeV, and \(\Lambda = 1.3\) GeV, the full sum gives \(L_{10} = -7.1 \times 10^{-3}\), while the first few resonances give \(L_{10} = -5.6 \times 10^{-3}\) and the experimental estimate is \(L_{10}(M_\rho) = -5.5 \times 10^{-3}\).
* In the MHA test with physical masses \(\hat M_S = m_S\) and \(\hat M_P = m_P\), the ratio of effective decay constants is \(\hat f_P^2 / \hat f_S^2 \simeq 1/3\), instead of the actual model value of 1.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of resonance saturation for low-energy constants in chiral perturbation theory, specifically addressing the sum rule for \(L_8\) that relates it to scalar and pseudoscalar resonances. The authors build on prior work by the Swiss-Catalan group and the minimal hadronic approximation (MHA), and they contrast the \(L_8\) case with the better-behaved sum rule for \(L_{10}\) involving vector and axial-vector resonances. The results suggest that the usual assumption of saturation by a finite number of resonances may introduce an unknown systematic error, and they enable future studies using Pade approximants (as in the work of Baker and collaborators) to establish a mathematically well-defined connection between resonance parameters and LECs, though this approach is not directly applicable to \(L_8\) because its spectral function is not positive.
