# Inclusive Quasi-Elastic Charged-Current Neutrino-Nucleus Reactions

> The Quasi-Elastic (QE) contribution of the nuclear inclusive electron scattering model developed in Nucl. Phys. A627 (1997) 543 is extended to the study of electroweak Charged Current (CC) induced nuclear reactions, at intermediate energies of interest for future neutrino oscillation experiments. The model accounts for, among other nuclear effects, long range nuclear (RPA) correlations, Final State Interaction (FSI) and Coulomb corrections. Predictions for the inclusive muon capture in $^{12}$C and the reaction $^{12}$C $(ν_μ,μ^-)X$ near threshold are also given. RPA correlations are shown to play a crucial role and their inclusion leads to one of the best existing simultaneous description of both processes, with accuracies of the order of 10-15% per cent for the muon capture rate and even better for the LSND measurement.

## Key claim

The model provides one of the best existing simultaneous descriptions of both inclusive muon capture in ¹²C and the LSND measurement of ¹²C(ν_μ,μ⁻)X near threshold, with accuracies of 10-15% for the muon capture rate, but only when long-range nuclear (RPA) correlations are included; without these correlations the agreement would be significantly worse.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed nuclear inclusive electron scattering model (Nieves, Amaro, and Valverde, 1997) to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions by incorporating axial degrees of freedom. They compute the hadronic tensor from the W-boson selfenergy in nuclear matter using a local Fermi gas description, including Pauli blocking, RPA correlations via a Landau-Migdal effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with explicit π and ρ exchange, final state interactions, and Coulomb corrections. The model is applied to the quasi-elastic region for neutrino energies of interest to oscillation experiments, and predictions are made for muon capture and neutrino-induced reactions on ¹²C.

## Context

This work extends the inclusive electron scattering model of Nieves, Amaro, and Valverde (1997) to charged-current neutrino reactions, building on earlier approaches by Oset and collaborators (1990, 1992) for muon capture and quasi-elastic neutrino scattering. The key improvements over those earlier works include a more complete RPA resummation that accounts for effects in both the vector-isovector and scalar-isovector channels, a more complete tensorial treatment of the RPA response function, and inclusion of final state interaction effects for intermediate nuclear excitation energies. The model enables reliable predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections at the intermediate energies relevant for future neutrino oscillation experiments, where nuclear effects must be controlled to reduce systematic uncertainties.
