# Nucleon axial form factor at large momentum transfers

> Using a Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation and related symmetry-preserving weak interaction current, we deliver parameter-free predictions for the nucleon axialvector form factor, $G_A(Q^2)$, on the domain $0\leq x=Q^2/m_N^2\leq 10$, where $m_N$ is the nucleon mass. We also provide a detailed analysis of the flavour separation of the proton $G_A$ into contributions from valence $u$ and $d$ quarks; and with form factors available on such a large $Q^2$ domain, predictions for the flavour-separated axial-charge light-front transverse spatial density profiles. Our calculated axial charge ratio $g_A^d/g_A^u=-0.32(2)$ is consistent with available experimental data and markedly larger in magnitude than the value typical of nonrelativistic quark models. The value of this ratio is sensitive to the strength of axialvector diquark correlations in the Poincaré-covariant nucleon wave function. Working with a realistic axialvector diquark content, the $d$ and $u$ quark transverse density profiles are similar. Some of these predictions could potentially be tested with new data on threshold pion electroproduction from the proton at large $Q^2$.

## Key claim

Using a Poincare-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation and symmetry-preserving weak interaction current, the authors deliver parameter-free predictions for the nucleon axialvector form factor on the domain \(0 \leq x = Q^2/m_N^2 \leq 10\), where the results are expressed in terms of \(x\) rather than \(Q^2\) alone because the Faddeev equation describes the nucleon's dressed-quark core (mass 1.18 GeV) rather than the physical nucleon, and the "meson cloud" effects that lower the physical mass by roughly 0.2 GeV are not included.

## Method

The calculation solves the Poincare-covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon, which treats the nucleon as a bound state of a dressed-quark and nonpointlike diquark correlations (isoscalar-scalar and isovector-axialvector). The symmetry-preserving axial current is then computed from the resulting Faddeev amplitudes using the diagrams shown in Figure 2 of the paper, ensuring partial conservation of the axialvector current and the Goldberger-Treiman relation. The diquark masses are fixed at \(m_{[ud]_{0^+}} = 0.80\) GeV and \(m_{\{uu\}_{1^+}} = m_{\{ud\}_{1^+}} = m_{\{dd\}_{1^+}} = 0.89\) GeV, with dressed light-quark Euclidean constituent mass \(M_q^E = 0.33\) GeV, and an uncertainty is reported by independently varying the diquark masses by \(\pm 5\%\).

## Key numbers

- Axial charge ratio: \(g_A^d/g_A^u = -0.32(2)\) (Eq. SeparationsC), consistent with empirical SU(3)-flavour estimates of \(-0.27(4)\) and markedly larger in magnitude than the nonrelativistic quark model value of \(-1/4\).
- Total isovector axial charge: \(g_A = g_A^u - g_A^d = 1.25(3)\) (Table I caption).
- Flavour-separated axial charges: \(g_A^u/g_A = 0.76 \pm 0.01\), \(g_A^d/g_A = -0.24 \pm 0.01\) (Eq. Separations).
- Central result interpolation for \(G_A(x)\) on \(0 \leq x \leq 10\): \(G_A(x) = (1.248 + 0.039 x) / (1 + 1.417 x + 0.318 x^2 + 0.071 x^3)\) (Eq. GAcentral).
- Dipole fit to the CSM result on \(x \leq 3\) yields dipole mass \(m_A = 1.24(3) m_N\); at \(x=10\) the dipole overestimates the actual result by \(56(5)\%\) (Fig. LCSRdiff B).
- Lattice QCD points from Jang (2019) on \(0 \leq x \leq 1\) agree with the CSM result with mean \(\chi^2 = 0.79\).
- Scalar-diquark-only case: \(g_A^d/g_A^u = -0.054(13)\) (Fig. GAflavour A caption).

## Context

This work extends continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) calculations of nucleon form factors, previously applied to electromagnetic form factors (Cui 2020) and to the axial form factor at low \(Q^2\) (Chen 2020, Chen 2021), out to \(Q^2/m_N^2 \leq 10\). It provides the only CSM prediction for \(G_A(Q^2)\) at large momentum transfers, complementing light-cone sum rule results (Anikin 2016) which give values roughly a factor of two lower. The predictions can be tested by new data on threshold pion electroproduction from the proton at large \(Q^2\) using detectors like SuperBigBite and CLAS12 at Jefferson Lab, and they enable improved analysis of neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments.
