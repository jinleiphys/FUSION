# Elliptic flow and incomplete equilibration at RHIC

> We argue that RHIC data, in particular those on the anisotropic flow coefficients v_2 and v_4, suggest that the matter produced in the early stages of nucleus-nucleus collisions is incompletely thermalized. We interpret the parameter (1/S)(dN/dy), where S is the transverse area of the collision zone and dN/dy the multiplicity density, as an indicator of the number of collisions per particle at the time when elliptic flow is established, and hence as a measure of the degree of equilibration. This number serves as a control parameter which can be varied experimentally by changing the system size, the centrality of the collision, or the beam energy. We provide predictions for Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC as well as for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that RHIC data on elliptic flow (v₂ and v₄) indicate the matter produced in early nucleus-nucleus collisions is incompletely thermalized, contrary to the common interpretation that ideal hydrodynamics applies. The authors claim that the degree of equilibration can be quantified by the number of collisions per particle, proportional to (1/S)(dN/dy), and that this number is not large enough at RHIC to reach the hydrodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors characterize the degree of thermalization by the Knudsen number K, where K⁻¹ is the typical number of collisions per particle. They derive that K⁻¹ is proportional to (σ/S)(dN/dy)(c_s/c), where σ is the cross section, S is the transverse area of the collision zone, dN/dy is the multiplicity density, and c_s is the speed of sound. They then compare this parameter across different system sizes (Au-Au vs Cu-Cu) and centralities using ideal hydrodynamic calculations and transport model results, examining how v₂/ε and v₄/v₂² scale with the number of collisions per particle.

## Key numbers

- For semi-central Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, with a partonic cross section of 3 mb and c_s ≈ c/√3, K⁻¹ ≈ 2 (not much larger than unity)
- In transport calculations, increasing σ from 3 to 10 mb (K⁻¹ from 2 to 7) yields only a 40% increase in v₂, corresponding to K₀⁻¹ ≈ 1.5
- For Au-Au collisions at b=8 fm: ε = 0.315, R̄ = 1.45 fm, dN/dy = 344, (1/S)(dN/dy) = 1.23 mb⁻¹
- For Cu-Cu collisions at b=5.5 fm: ε = 0.216, R̄ = 1.10 fm, dN/dy = 95, (1/S)(dN/dy) = 0.61 mb⁻¹
- Ideal fluid dynamics yields v₄/v₂² = 1/2, while experimental data show v₄/v₂² ≈ 1.2
- If far from equilibrium, v₂(Cu) = 0.34 v₂(Au); if hydrodynamic regime reached, v₂(Cu) = 0.69 v₂(Au)

## Context

This work challenges the conclusion from earlier hydrodynamic analyses (Kolb 2003, Teaney 2001) that RHIC matter reaches local equilibrium on short time scales (~0.6 fm/c) and behaves as a "perfect fluid." The authors extend the observation by Alt (2003) that v₂/ε scales linearly with (1/S)(dN/dy) across SPS and RHIC energies, interpreting this as evidence against saturation of the hydrodynamic limit. The paper provides testable predictions for Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, and suggests that dropping the assumption of chemical equilibrium (as assumed in thermal model fits) allows a natural explanation of the data without requiring a soft equation of state.
