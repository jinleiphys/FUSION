# Light-Front spin-dependent Spectral Function and Nucleon Momentum Distributions for a Three-Body System

> Poincare' covariant definitions for the spin-dependent spectral function and for the momentum distributions within the light-front Hamiltonian dynamics are proposed for a three-fermion bound system, starting from the light-front wave function of the system. The adopted approach is based on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of the Poincare' generators, that allows one to easily import the familiar and wide knowledge on the nuclear interaction into a light-front framework. The proposed formalism can find useful applications in refined nuclear calculations, like the ones needed for evaluating the EMC effect or the semi-inclusive deep inelastic cross sections with polarized nuclear targets, since remarkably the light-front unpolarized momentum distribution by definition fulfills both normalization and momentum sum rules. It is also shown a straightforward generalization of the definition of the light-front spectral function to an A-nucleon system.

## Key claim

The authors propose Poincaré covariant definitions for the spin-dependent spectral function and momentum distributions within light-front Hamiltonian dynamics for a three-fermion bound system, using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to import non-relativistic nuclear interactions into a light-front framework, with the caveat that only the valence component of the light-front wave function is considered and the intrinsic wave function can be approximated by its non-relativistic counterpart.

## Method

The authors construct light-front spin-dependent spectral functions and momentum distributions for a three-nucleon system (³He and ³H) by defining overlaps between the three-body ground state and tensor products of a free nucleon plane wave with a fully interacting two-body spectator state. They employ the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of Poincaré generators within light-front Hamiltonian dynamics, using Melosh rotations to relate canonical spins (instant form) to light-front spins, and introduce non-symmetric intrinsic variables to describe the kinematics of the knocked-out nucleon and the spectator pair. The intrinsic wave functions with canonical spins are identified with non-relativistic eigenstates of the Schrödinger equation, allowing the use of standard nuclear interactions such as AV18.

## Key numbers

- The two-body mass equation yields the relation \( \epsilon_{int} = (M^2 - 4m^2)/(4m) \), where for the deuteron bound state \( M = 2m - B \) and \( \epsilon_{int} = -B + B^2/(4m) \sim -B \) given the small binding energy relative to nucleon mass.
- The free mass of the three-particle system in terms of non-symmetric intrinsic variables is \( M_0(1,2,3) = \sqrt{m^2 + |\mathbf{k}_1|^2} + \sqrt{M_{23}^2 + |\mathbf{k}_1|^2} \), where \( M_{23} \) is the free mass of the (2,3) pair.
- The three-body intrinsic wave function normalization is \( \sum_{\tau_1,\tau_2,\tau_3}\sum_{\sigma_1,\sigma_2,\sigma_3} \int d\mathbf{k}_1 \int d\mathbf{k}_{23} |\langle \sigma_1,\sigma_2,\sigma_3; \tau_1,\tau_2,\tau_3; \mathbf{k}_1, \mathbf{k}_{23}|j,j_z; \epsilon^3_{int},\Pi; 1/2, T_z \rangle|^2 = 1 \).

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic treatments of DIS and SIDIS off polarized ³He (Pace et al., earlier work; Scopetta et al., earlier work) that used the plane wave impulse approximation with AV18 interaction, and the distorted spin-dependent spectral function approach of Kaptari et al. (recent paper) which included final state interactions but remained non-Poincaré covariant. The formalism enables relativistic calculations of the EMC effect and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross sections with polarized nuclear targets at Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and can be generalized to A-nucleon systems.
