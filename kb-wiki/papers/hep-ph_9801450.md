# Nuclear Dependence in Transverse Momentum Distribution for Drell-Yan Pair

> In terms of multiple scattering picture, I compute the nuclear dependence in Drell-Yan transverse momentum distribution, $dσ/ dQ^2 dq_T^2$, in hadron-nucleus collisions. I present the results for large $q_T$ region and discuss the possible suppression in small $q_T$ region.

## Key claim

The paper computes the nuclear dependence of the Drell-Yan transverse momentum distribution in hadron-nucleus collisions using a multiple-scattering picture, finding that double-scattering produces nuclear enhancement at large \(q_T\) and predicts nuclear suppression at small \(q_T\) due to quantum interference between soft-hard and double-hard subprocesses, though the small-\(q_T\) result requires resummation of large logarithms for a reliable prediction.

## Method

The author calculates double-scattering contributions to the Drell-Yan cross section \(d\sigma/dQ^2 dq_T^2\) in QCD perturbation theory, using the higher-twist factorization formalism developed by Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (LQS). The double-scattering amplitude is decomposed into soft-hard and double-hard subprocesses, and their contributions to the cross section are expressed in terms of four-parton correlation functions, with models for cold nuclei using \(A^{4/3}\) scaling and parameters \(\lambda^2 \sim 0.05-0.1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(C=0.35/(4r_0^2)\) GeV\(^2\) with \(r_0=1.1-1.25\).

## Key numbers

* The average squared transverse momentum \(\langle q_T^2 \rangle\) grows approximately as \(A^{1/3}\), as observed by the NA10 and E772 collaborations.
* For cold nuclei, the double-hard correlation function is modeled as \(T^{DH}_{ab}(x_a,x_b,A)=C A^{4/3} f_{a/N}(x_a) f_{b/N}(x_b)\) with \(C=0.35/(4r_0^2)\) GeV\(^2\) and \(r_0=1.1-1.25\).
* The soft-hard matrix element for cold nuclei is modeled as \(T_a(x_a, A)=\lambda^2 A^{4/3} f_{a/N}(x_a)\) with \(\lambda^2 \sim 0.05-0.1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist QCD factorization approach of Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (LQS) to Drell-Yan production in hadron-nucleus collisions, building on experimental observations from NA10 and E772 that the transverse momentum distribution shows non-linear nuclear dependence not proportional to atomic number \(A\). The calculation enables future systematic resummation of large \(\ln(Q^2/q_T^2)\) logarithms in the small-\(q_T\) region, as discussed by Guo, Qiu, and Sterman (GQS), and provides a framework for understanding nuclear shadowing effects in transverse momentum distributions analogous to those in longitudinal momentum fractions.
