# Role of the Symmetry Energy on the Structure of Neutron Stars with Unified Equations of State

> The role of the symmetry energy on the internal constitution and the global structure of a cold nonaccreted neutron star is studied using a set of unified equations of state. Based on the nuclear energy-density functional theory, these equations of state provide a thermodynamically consistent treatment of all regions of the star and were calculated using the four different Brussels-Montreal functionals BSk22, BSk24, BSk25 and BSk26. Our predictions are compared to various constraints inferred from astrophysical observations including the recent detection of the gravitational wave signal GW170817 from a binary neutron-star merger.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy's density dependence, particularly its slope \(L\), strongly influences the composition and pressure of the inner crust and core of a cold nonaccreted neutron star, but the maximum mass is primarily determined by the choice of the pure neutron matter equation of state rather than by the symmetry energy coefficient \(J\) alone.

## Method

The authors constructed four unified equations of state (BSk22, BSk24, BSk25, BSk26) using the Brussels-Montreal nuclear energy-density functionals, which were fitted to 2353 experimental atomic masses from the 2012 Atomic Mass Evaluation and constrained to two different realistic pure neutron matter equations of state (the stiff 'V18' from Li and Schulze (2008) and the softer 'A18+\(\delta v\)+UIX*' from Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998)). The outer crust was treated with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using experimental masses where available, the inner crust was computed using the fourth-order extended Thomas-Fermi method with Strutinsky-integral shell and pairing corrections, and the core was described by analytic expressions from the same functionals under beta equilibrium. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then solved to obtain the global structure of nonrotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy coefficients \(J\) for the functionals: BSk22 = 32.0 MeV, BSk24 = 30.0 MeV, BSk25 = 29.0 MeV, BSk26 = 30.0 MeV.
- Slope parameters \(L\) of the symmetry energy: BSk22 = 68.5 MeV, BSk24 = 46.4 MeV, BSk25 = 36.9 MeV, BSk26 = 37.5 MeV.
- Nuclear incompressibility \(K_v\) for all functionals lies in the range 236.0–245.9 MeV, consistent with the experimental range \(K_v = 240 \pm 10\) MeV.
- The direct Urca threshold mass for BSk22 is \(M_\mathrm{DU} \simeq 1.151 M_\odot\); for BSk24 it is \(M_\mathrm{DU} \simeq 1.595 M_\odot\); for BSk25 it is \(M_\mathrm{DU} \simeq 1.612 M_\odot\); BSk26 forbids direct Urca for all hydrostatically stable neutron stars.
- The outer crust composition is determined by experimental masses up to a mean baryon density of \(\bar{n} \approx 3 \times 10^{-5}\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the series of Brussels-Montreal unified equations of state (Pearson et al., 2018) by systematically varying the symmetry energy coefficient \(J\) from 29 to 32 MeV while keeping the same pure neutron matter constraint (V18 from Li and Schulze, 2008), and by comparing to the softer pure neutron matter EoS of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) via BSk26. The results are compared to constraints from the gravitational-wave event GW170817, including radius limits from Bauswein et al. (2017) and Koppel et al. (2019), tidal deformability estimates from the LIGO/Virgo collaboration (2018), and direct Urca cooling constraints from observations of neutron star cooling (Yakovlev et al., 2004; Brown et al., 2018). The findings enable tighter discrimination between symmetry energy models and rule out BSk26 based on the absence of direct Urca processes and marginal agreement with tidal deformability constraints.
