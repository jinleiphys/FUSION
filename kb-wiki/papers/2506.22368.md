# Exploring hadronic rescattering effects on resonance productions in pp and p-Pb collisions with PYTHIA8

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the quark-gluon plasma is created, and as the medium cools down, the system transitions into a hadronic phase. While such interactions are well established for large systems, such as Pb-Pb collisions, their relevance in smaller collision systems remains unclear. Consequently, hadronic interactions during the hadronic phase are studied in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV and p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}=5.02$ TeV with the PYTHIA8 event generator. The interaction is studied via the yield ratios between resonances and stable particles with similar quark contents, which are obtained as a function of transverse momentum ($p_{\rm{T}}$) using $\mathrm{ρ(770)^0}$, $\mathrm{K^*(892)^0}$, and $\mathrm{ϕ(1020)}$ mesons and their stable particles, $\mathrm{π^\pm}$ and $\mathrm{K^\pm}$ at midrapidity ($|\rm{y}|<0.5$). Yield ratios are calculated in five multiplicity classes for pp and six for p-Pb collisions, using the 60-100% multiplicity class in pp as a reference. Although rescattering leads to stronger suppression at low $p_{\rm{T}} < 2$ GeV/$c$, a visible suppression remains even when rescattering is turned off. To isolate the rescattering effect, double ratios between the rescattering on and off configurations are obtained. These are then integrated in the full $p_{\rm{T}}$ range ($0<p_{\rm{T}}<6.0$ GeV/$c$). The normalized double ratios show a decreasing trend with increasing multiplicity, independent of the collision system. The lower limit of the hadronic phase lifetimes extracted in the integrated-$p_{\rm{T}}$ region increases with multiplicity in both systems, but with a notable discrepancy between pp and p-Pb collisions.

## Key claim

Hadronic rescattering effects on resonance production in small collision systems (pp and p-Pb) are studied using PYTHIA8, and while rescattering leads to stronger suppression at low transverse momentum (p_T < 2 GeV/c), a visible suppression remains even when rescattering is turned off, indicating that not all observed suppression is due to rescattering alone.

## Method

The authors generated non-diffractive pp events at sqrt(s)=13 TeV and p-Pb events at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV using PYTHIA8 with hadronic rescattering turned on (with and without inelastic scattering) and turned off. Yield ratios of rho(770)^0, K*(892)^0, and phi(1020) mesons to their stable counterparts (pi^pm and K^pm) were extracted via invariant mass analysis at midrapidity (|y|<0.5) in five multiplicity classes for pp and six for p-Pb collisions, using the 60-100% multiplicity class in pp as a reference. Double ratios between rescattering-on and rescattering-off configurations were computed to isolate the rescattering effect, then integrated over the full p_T range (0-6.0 GeV/c).

## Key numbers

* pp collisions at sqrt(s)=13 TeV and p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV were simulated.
* Yield ratios were obtained for rho(770)^0, K*(892)^0, and phi(1020) mesons with lifetimes of 1.3 fm/c, 4.16 fm/c, and 46.3 fm/c, respectively.
* Rescattering leads to stronger suppression at low p_T < 2 GeV/c.
* The full p_T range for integrated double ratios was 0 < p_T < 6.0 GeV/c.
* The normalized double ratios show a decreasing trend with increasing multiplicity, independent of the collision system.
* The lower limit of the hadronic phase lifetime extracted in the integrated-p_T region increases with multiplicity in both systems, with a notable discrepancy between pp and p-Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends studies by the ALICE collaboration (ALICE 2016, ALICE 2019, ALICE 2023) that measured K*(892)^0 and phi(1020) production in pp and p-Pb collisions, finding that yield ratios decrease with increasing multiplicity similarly to large systems. It also builds on transport model studies using EPOS3 with UrQMD (Knospe 2021) that interpreted experimental data on resonance production. The results enable future comparisons between PYTHIA8 predictions and experimental data to constrain the lifetime of the hadronic phase in small collision systems, where QGP-like signatures have been observed (Nagle 2018).
