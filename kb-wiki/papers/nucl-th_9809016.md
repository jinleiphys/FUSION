# Isospin symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potentials and nuclear structure

> Modern nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, which accurately fit the nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, contain terms which break isospin symmetry. The effects of these symmetry violating terms on the bulk properties of nuclear matter are investigated. The predictions of the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) terms are compared with the Nolen-Schiffer (NS) anomaly regarding the energies of neighboring mirror nuclei. We find that, for a quantitative explanation of the NS anomaly, it is crucial to include CSB in partial waves with $L>0$ (besides $^1S_0$) as derived from a microscopic model for CSB of the NN interaction.

## Key claim

The paper finds that, for a quantitative explanation of the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (the energy difference between mirror nuclei beyond electromagnetic effects), it is crucial to include charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in partial waves with orbital angular momentum L>0, not just in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave as is commonly done. The authors show that phenomenological potentials which only adjust the \(^1S_0\) state to fit scattering lengths underestimate the effect, while microscopically-derived CSB in higher partial waves yields agreement.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter at the empirical saturation density (Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\)), using five modern high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (CDBonn, Argonne V18, Nijm1, Nijm2, Reid93) and a refined version (CDBonn99) that includes microscopically-derived isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) in partial waves beyond \(^1S_0\). They compared the binding energies and single-particle potentials obtained when using the full isospin-breaking interactions versus calculations where the proton-proton and neutron-neutron interactions were replaced by the corresponding neutron-proton interaction.

## Key numbers

- The energy loss per nucleon due to isospin symmetry breaking (\(\Delta E_{ISB}\)) at \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\) ranges from 0.218 MeV (Reid93) to 0.370 MeV (CDBonn99), with CDBonn at 0.329 MeV and Argonne V18 at 0.282 MeV.
- The BHF binding energy per nucleon at \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\) ranges from -16.83 MeV (CDBonn99) to -13.71 MeV (Nijm2), with CDBonn at -16.78 MeV and Argonne V18 at -15.58 MeV.
- The difference between proton and neutron single-particle potentials at the Fermi surface (\(\Delta U_{CSB}\)) at empirical saturation density is 0.31 MeV for Argonne V18, 0.28 MeV for CDBonn99, and 0.16 MeV for CDBonn.
- The Hartree-Fock energy per nucleon at \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\) varies dramatically: 6.06 MeV (CDBonn), 13.04 MeV (Nijm1), 31.73 MeV (Argonne V18), 35.46 MeV (Reid93), and 35.62 MeV (Nijm2).
- The symmetry energy at \(k_F = 1.36\) fm\(^{-1}\) ranges from 28.08 MeV (Nijm2) to 30.65 MeV (CDBonn), with negligible ISB effects (changes of at most 0.3 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of modern phase-shift-equivalent nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijmegen group potentials, Argonne V18, CDBonn) to study their isospin symmetry breaking terms, which were previously fitted primarily to reproduce scattering data. The paper directly addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen and Schiffer, 1969) and shows that phenomenological treatments of CSB in the Argonne V18 potential overestimate the effect in higher partial waves by roughly 50% compared to the microscopically-based CDBonn99 potential. The results enable more reliable calculations of nuclear structure for mirror nuclei and demonstrate the need for microscopic models of CSB beyond the \(^1S_0\) partial wave, as provided by the work of Li and Machleidt (1998) and van Kolck et al. (1998).
