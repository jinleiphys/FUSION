# Hydrodynamic Simulations of Oxygen-Neon Classical Novae as Galactic $^7$Li Producers and Potential Accretion Induced Collapse Progenitors

> We report on studies of Classical Nova (CN) explosions where we follow the evolution of thermonuclear runaways (TNRs) on oxygen-neon (ONe) white dwarfs (WDs). Using NOVA, a one-dimensional hydrodynamic computer code, we accrete Solar matter until the TNR is ongoing and then switch to a mixed composition. This approach is guided by the results of multi-dimensional studies of TNRs in WDs which find that sufficient mixing with WD core material occurs after the TNR is well underway, and levels of enrichment of the CNONeMg elements are reached that agree with observations of CN ejecta abundances. Because the amount of accreted material is inversely proportional to the oxygen abundance, by first accreting Solar matter, the amount of accreted material is larger than in those simulations with an initially enriched composition. We vary the mass of the WD (from 0.6 Msun to 1.35 Msun) and the composition of the mixed materials. Our results show large enrichments of 7Be in the ejected gases implying that ONe CNe and CO CNe (Starrfield et al. 2020) may be responsible for a significant fraction (about 100 Msun) of the galactic 7Li ( about 1000 Msun). The production of 22Na and 26Al in CN explosions and the gamma-ray emission predicted by our simulations is discussed. The WDs in all our simulations eject less material than they accrete and we predict that the WD is growing in mass as a consequence of the CN outburst. ONe CNe, therefore, may be an important channel for accretion induced collapse (AIC) events.

## Key claim

Classical novae on oxygen-neon white dwarfs may produce a significant fraction (about 100 solar masses) of the galactic lithium-7 inventory (about 1000 solar masses), and the white dwarfs in all simulations grow in mass rather than lose it, making them potential progenitors of accretion-induced collapse events.

## Method

The authors used the one-dimensional hydrodynamic code NOVA with 300 mass zones, a 187-nuclei reaction network, OPAL opacities, and Timmes equations of state. They accreted solar-composition matter at a rate of 1.6e-10 solar masses per year onto white dwarfs of masses 0.6, 0.8, 1.00, 1.15, 1.25, and 1.35 solar masses, switching to a mixed composition (either 25% or 50% oxygen-neon white dwarf matter mixed with solar matter) only after the thermonuclear runaway was well underway, guided by multi-dimensional simulations showing mixing occurs late.

## Key numbers

- White dwarf masses studied: 0.6, 0.8, 1.00, 1.15, 1.25, and 1.35 solar masses
- Accretion rate: 1.6e-10 solar masses per year
- Peak energy generation threshold for switching composition: 10^11 erg per gram per second
- Surface zone mass: approximately 2e-9 in units of the white dwarf mass
- Peak temperature reached a few hundred seconds after exceeding 10^8 K
- For the 50% WD - 50% Solar mixing-from-beginning simulation on a 1.35 solar mass WD: ejected mass of 1.4e-6 solar masses
- Galactic lithium-7 inventory: approximately 1000 solar masses; nova contribution: approximately 100 solar masses

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of carbon-oxygen classical novae (Starrfield 2020) to oxygen-neon white dwarfs, using the same delayed-mixing approach motivated by multi-dimensional simulations from Casanova (2010, 2011, 2016, 2018) and Jose (2014, 2020). The results contradict earlier mixing-from-beginning simulations (Starrfield 2009) which produced insufficient ejected mass, and enable quantitative predictions for lithium-7, sodium-22, and aluminum-26 yields from oxygen-neon novae as well as assessment of their role as accretion-induced collapse progenitors.
