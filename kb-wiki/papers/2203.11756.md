# Simultaneous Inference of Neutron Star Equation of State and the Hubble Constant with a Population of Merging Neutron Stars

> We develop a method for implementing a proposal on utilizing knowledge of neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) for inferring the Hubble constant from a population of binary neutron star (BNS) mergers. This method is useful in exploiting BNSs as standard sirens when their redshifts are not available. Gravitational wave (GW) signals from compact object binaries provide a direct measurement of their luminosity distances, but not their redshifts. Unlike in the past, here we employ a realistic EoS parametrization in a Bayesian framework to simultaneously measure the Hubble constant and refine the constraints on the EoS parameters. The uncertainty in the redshift depends on the uncertainties in the EoS and the mass parameters estimated from GW data. Combining the inferred BNS redshifts with the corresponding luminosity distances, one constructs a redshift-distance relation and deduces the Hubble constant from it. Here, we show that in the Cosmic Explorer era, one can measure the Hubble constant to a precision of $\lesssim 5\%$ (with a $90\%$ credible interval) with a realistic distribution of a thousand BNSs, while allowing for uncertainties in their EoS parameters. Such a measurement can potentially resolve the current tension in the measurements of the Hubble constant from the early- and late-time universe. The methodology implemented in this work demonstrates a comprehensive prescription for inferring the NS EoS and the Hubble constant by simultaneously combining GW observations from merging NSs, while employing a simple population model for NS masses and keeping the merger rate of NSs constant in redshift. This method can be immediately extended to incorporate merger rate, population properties, and additional cosmological parameters.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that with a realistic distribution of a thousand binary neutron star mergers observed by the third-generation Cosmic Explorer detector, the Hubble constant can be measured to a precision of less than 5% (90% credible interval) while simultaneously allowing for uncertainties in the neutron star equation of state parameters, provided the merger rate is assumed constant in redshift.

## Method

The authors employ a hybrid nuclear plus three-piece piecewise-polytrope parametrization of the neutron star equation of state, using nuclear empirical parameters at low densities and polytropic indices at higher densities. They perform a two-step Bayesian analysis: first, they estimate detector-frame source parameters (redshifted masses, luminosity distance, tidal deformabilities) from gravitational wave signals of individual binary neutron star mergers using the IMRPhenomPv2_NRTidal waveform model; second, they combine these individual posteriors hierarchically to jointly infer the Hubble constant and equation of state parameters, using the fact that tidal deformability depends on source-frame masses while the point-particle phase depends on redshifted masses.

## Key numbers

- Hubble constant measurement precision of less than 5% (90% credible interval) projected for 1000 binary neutron star mergers detected by Cosmic Explorer
- For 50 binary neutron star mergers, the 90% credible interval fractional uncertainty in the Hubble constant is 15-22%, depending on the equation of state prior
- The injected Hubble constant value used in simulations is 70 km/s/Mpc, with a flat LambdaCDM cosmology assuming Omega_m = 0.3
- The injected equation of state parameters include: K0 = 240 MeV, e_sym = 31.7 MeV, L = 58.7 MeV, K_sym = -100 MeV, Gamma1 = 2.5, Gamma2 = 3.5, Gamma3 = 3.0
- The minimum neutron star mass is fixed at 1 solar mass and the maximum mass at approximately 2.27 solar masses for the injected equation of state
- The redshift distribution of sources spans luminosity distances between 100 Mpc and 8 Gpc

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Messenger and Read (2011) to use neutron star equation of state knowledge for determining redshifts of binary neutron star mergers without electromagnetic counterparts. It improves upon Chatterjee et al. (2021) by using a more realistic hybrid nuclear plus piecewise-polytrope equation of state parametrization rather than binary Love relations. The method enables simultaneous inference of the Hubble constant and neutron star equation of state parameters from gravitational wave observations alone, potentially helping to resolve the 4.4-sigma tension between early-universe measurements from Planck (2018) giving 67.36 +/- 0.54 km/s/Mpc and late-universe measurements from Riess et al. (2021) giving 73.04 +/- 1.04 km/s/Mpc.
