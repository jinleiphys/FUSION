# What can we know about hypernuclei via analysis of bremsstrahlung photons?

> We investigate possibility of emission of the bremsstrahlung photons in nuclear reactions with hypernuclei for the first time. A new model of the bremsstrahlung emission which accompanies interactions between $α$ particles and hypernuclei is constructed, where a new formalism for the magnetic momenta of nucleons and hyperon inside hypernucleus is added. For first calculations, we choose $α$ decay of the normal nucleus $^{210}{\rm Po}$ and the hypernucleus $^{211}_Λ{\rm Po}$. We find that (1) emission for the hypernucleus $^{211}_Λ{\rm Po}$ is larger than for normal nucleus $^{210}{\rm Po}$, (2) difference between these spectra is small. We propose a way how to find hypernuclei, where role of hyperon is the most essential in emission of bremsstrahlung photons during $α$ decay. As demonstration of such a property, we show that the spectra for the hypernuclei $^{107}_Λ{\rm Te}$ and $^{109}_Λ{\rm Te}$ are essentially larger than the spectra for the normal nuclei $^{106}{\rm Te}$ and $^{108}{\rm Te}$. Such a difference is explained by additional contribution of emission to the full bremsstrahlung, which is formed by magnetic moment of hyperon inside hypernucleus. The bremsstrahlung emission formed by such a mechanism, is of the magnetic type. A new formula for fast estimations of bremsstrahlung spectra for even-even hypernuclei is proposed, where role of magnetic moment of hyperon of hypernucleus in formation of the bremsstrahlung emission is shown explicitly. Such an analysis opens possibility of new experimental study of properties of hypernuclei via bremsstrahlung study.

## Key claim

The authors propose that bremsstrahlung photons emitted during alpha decay of hypernuclei can serve as a new experimental probe of hypernuclear properties, but the difference between bremsstrahlung spectra of hypernuclei and normal nuclei is small for some cases (e.g., 211_ΛPo vs. 210Po) and only becomes "essentially larger" for specific hypernuclei such as 107_ΛTe and 109_ΛTe, where the hyperon's magnetic moment contributes significantly to magnetic-type emission.

## Method

The authors constructed a new model of bremsstrahlung emission accompanying alpha-particle interactions with hypernuclei, based on a many-nucleon generalization of the Pauli equation. They added a formalism for the magnetic moments of nucleons and the hyperon inside the hypernucleus, including anomalous magnetic moments for protons (2.79284734462 μ_N), neutrons (-1.91304273 μ_N), and Λ-hyperons (-0.613 μ_N). The model was applied to calculate bremsstrahlung spectra for alpha decay of normal nuclei (210Po, 106Te, 108Te) and corresponding hypernuclei (211_ΛPo, 107_ΛTe, 109_ΛTe), using relative coordinates and a new operator of emission expressed in terms of relative momenta.

## Key numbers

- Anomalous magnetic moments used: μ_p^(an) = 2.79284734462 μ_N, μ_n^(an) = -1.91304273 μ_N, μ_Λ^(an) = -0.613 μ_N, where μ_N = 3.1524512550 × 10^(-14) MeV T^(-1) (nuclear magneton)
- The bremsstrahlung emission for hypernucleus 211_ΛPo is larger than for normal nucleus 210Po, but the difference between these spectra is small
- The spectra for hypernuclei 107_ΛTe and 109_ΛTe are "essentially larger" than the spectra for normal nuclei 106Te and 108Te
- The experimental bremsstrahlung data used for model validation were for alpha decay of 211Po (Boie 2007, Boie 2009)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous formalism of bremsstrahlung emission in nuclear reactions (Maydanyuk 2003, Maydanyuk 2009, Maydanyuk 2010, Maydanyuk 2012, Maydanyuk and Zhang 2015, Maydanyuk, Zhang, and Zou 2016, 2018) to include hypernuclei for the first time. It builds on experimental studies of alpha decay of hypernuclei at the Nuclotron accelerator in Dubna (Majling 2004, Majling 2005, Batusov 2005) and on alpha-nucleus interaction potentials from Denisov (2009, 2015) and Santhosh (2018). The work opens the possibility of new experimental studies of hypernuclear properties via bremsstrahlung photon measurements, particularly for hypernuclei where the hyperon's magnetic moment contribution to magnetic-type emission is most essential.
