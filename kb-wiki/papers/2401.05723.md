# Advancing real-time Yang-Mills: towards real-time observables from first principles

> The complex Langevin (CL) method shows great promise in enabling the calculation of observables for theories with complex actions. Nevertheless, real-time quantum field theories have remained largely unsolved due to the particular severity of the sign problem. In this contribution, we discuss our recent progress in applying CL to a thermal SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on a 3+1 dimensional lattice. We present our anisotropic kernel that stabilizes the CL approach for real times longer than the inverse temperature - a first for Yang-Mills theory. We provide explicit evidence of reproducing symmetries and relations among different types of propagators when the complex time path approaches the Schwinger-Keldysh contour. This method paves the way for calculating transport coefficients and other real-time observables from first principles.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the complex Langevin (CL) method, stabilized by an anisotropic kernel and gauge cooling, can compute real-time unequal-time correlation functions in 3+1 dimensional SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on a lattice, but only after extrapolating the tilt angle of the complex time contour to zero (the Schwinger-Keldysh limit).

## Method

The authors discretize SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on a 3+1 dimensional lattice with a tilted complex time contour and solve the complex Langevin equation using the Euler-Maruyama scheme for gauge link fields. They introduce an anisotropic kernel that rescales the Langevin time step differently for temporal and spatial directions, combined with gauge cooling to control non-unitarity of the gauge configurations. The magnetic contribution to the energy density is computed from cloverleaf-improved plaquette variables, and the correlation function C(t,t') is obtained by averaging over spatial lattice sites and extrapolating the tilt angle α to zero using a cubic polynomial fit.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: N_t × N_s^3 with N_s = 16
- Lattice anisotropy: a_s / |a_t| ≈ 16
- Inverse temperature: β/a_s = 1/(T a_s) = 1
- Real-time extent: t_max = 1.5 β
- Gauge coupling: g = 0.5
- Tilt angles α studied: from tan(α) = 1/3 to tan(α) = 1/96
- Langevin step size: ε = 10^{-4}
- Langevin time interval for sampling: θ = 10 to 20, with 100 independent configurations
- Number of independent Langevin trajectories: 100 (for tan(α)=1/3) up to 1000 (for tan(α)=1/96)
- Gauge cooling step size: 0.05, performed once after each CL update

## Context

This work extends earlier CL studies of real-time Yang-Mills theory by Berges (2006) that suffered from numerical instabilities, and builds on the authors' own prior development of the anisotropic kernel (Boguslavski 2022). The method enables the first direct lattice calculation of an unequal-time correlation function in 3+1 dimensional non-Abelian gauge theory, paving the way toward computing transport coefficients (shear and bulk viscosities, speed of sound) and spectral functions for the quark-gluon plasma from first principles. A renormalization procedure for real-time lattice simulations remains an open problem that the authors identify as needed for future work.
