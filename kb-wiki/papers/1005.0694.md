# A Spin-Isospin Dependent 3N Scattering Formalism in a 3D Faddeev Scheme

> We have introduced a spin-isospin dependent three-dimensional approach for formulation of the three-nucleon scattering. Faddeev equation is expressed in terms of vector Jacobi momenta and spin-isospin quantum numbers of each nucleon. Our formalism is based on connecting the transition amplitude $T$ to momentum-helicity representations of the two-body $t$-matrix and the deuteron wave function. Finally the expressions for nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and full breakup process amplitudes are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a spin-isospin dependent three-dimensional (3D) formulation of the Faddeev equation for three-nucleon (3N) scattering, expressed directly in terms of vector Jacobi momenta and spin-isospin quantum numbers, thereby avoiding the involved angular momentum algebra required by partial-wave approaches. The formalism is derived for both nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and the full breakup process, but the authors state that actual numerical calculations of 3N scattering observables are "under preparation" and not presented in this work.

## Method

The authors project the Faddeev equation for three identical nucleons onto a 3D basis of vector Jacobi momenta **p** and **q** combined with explicit spin and isospin projections for each nucleon. They connect the transition amplitude to momentum-helicity representations of the two-body t-matrix and the deuteron wave function, using the antisymmetrized two-body t-matrix and the deuteron wave function in helicity basis. The pole of the two-body t-matrix at the deuteron binding energy is explicitly extracted by defining regularized operators \(\hat{t}^a\) and \(\hat{T}\), and final expressions for the elastic scattering amplitude \(U\) and the breakup amplitude \(U_0\) are derived in terms of these regularized quantities.

## Key numbers

- The total energy in the center-of-mass frame is given by \(E = E_d + \frac{3}{4m} q_0^2 = E_d + \frac{2}{3} E_{\text{lab}}\), where \(E_d\) is the deuteron binding energy and \(\mathbf{q}_0\) is the relative momentum of the projectile nucleon to the deuteron.
- The two-body subsystem energy in the 3N system is \(z = E - \frac{3}{4m} q^2\).
- The free 3N propagator denominator appears as \(E - \frac{1}{m}(q^2 + \mathbf{q} \cdot \mathbf{q}' + q'^2) + i\varepsilon\) in the integral equation.
- The elastic scattering differential cross section formula includes a factor \(\frac{1}{6}\) from averaging over initial spin-isospin states and summing over final states.
- The breakup differential cross section formula includes a factor \(\frac{1}{6}\) from averaging and a factor \(p q^2\) in the phase space.

## Context

This work extends a previously developed 3D formalism for the 3N bound state (Bayegan) to the scattering regime, building on earlier 3D approaches for three identical bosons (Elster, Schadow) and for the leading-order breakup process (Fachruddin et al.). The formalism is designed to accommodate realistic NN forces such as AV18, Bonn-B, and Chiral potentials in their helicity representation (Fachruddin, 2000; Shalchi). By avoiding partial-wave decomposition, this approach aims to simplify the inclusion of three-nucleon forces and to enable calculations above pion production threshold and into the GeV region.
