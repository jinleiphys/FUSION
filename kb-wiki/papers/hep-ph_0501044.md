# Structure of Lambda(1405) and chiral dynamics

> We report on a recent theoretical work on the structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance within a chiral unitary approach, in which the resonance is dynamically generated in meson-baryon scattering. Studying the analytic structure of the scattering amplitude, we have found that there are two poles lying around energies of Lambda(1405) with different widths and couplings to the meson-baryon states. We discuss reactions to conform the double pole structure in experiment and elastic K^- p scattering at low energies.

## Key claim

The Lambda(1405) resonance is not a single state but consists of two poles in the scattering amplitude, located at approximately 1390 - 66i MeV and 1426 - 16i MeV, which are so close that they appear as a single resonance in mass spectra; the double-pole structure depends on the fit to low-energy K- p scattering data and is not seen in all analyses, particularly those that prioritize the DEAR kaonic hydrogen measurements over the elastic K- p total cross section.

## Method

The authors employed a chiral unitary approach for coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering with strangeness S = -1 and isospin I = 0, using the Weinberg-Tomozawa term from lowest-order chiral perturbation theory as the driving force. They included all octet mesons (pi, K, eta) and octet baryons (N, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) respecting flavor SU(3) symmetry, imposed unitarity non-perturbatively via the N/D method, and fitted the subtraction constants to reproduce the threshold branching ratios of K- p to pi Sigma and pi Lambda channels. The analytic structure of the scattering amplitude was then examined in the second Riemann sheet to locate resonance poles.

## Key numbers

- Pole position at 1390 - 66i MeV (lower pole) with coupling strengths |g_i|: pi Sigma = 2.9, anti-K N = 2.1, eta Lambda = 0.77, K Xi = 0.61
- Pole position at 1426 - 16i MeV (higher pole) with coupling strengths |g_i|: pi Sigma = 1.5, anti-K N = 2.7, eta Lambda = 1.4, K Xi = 0.35
- A third pole at 1680 - 20i MeV (identified with Lambda(1670)) with coupling strengths |g_i|: pi Sigma = 0.27, anti-K N = 0.77, eta Lambda = 1.1, K Xi = 3.5

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oller and Meissner (2001) and Bennhold et al. (2003), which first showed that the Lambda(1405) could be dynamically generated in meson-baryon scattering, and confirms earlier suggestions of two poles near the Lambda(1405) from the cloudy bag model of Fink et al. and from other chiral dynamics studies. The double-pole structure enables experimental tests through channel-dependent mass spectra, such as comparing pi Sigma -> pi Sigma versus anti-K N -> pi Sigma reactions, and motivates new measurements of elastic K- p scattering at low energies to resolve inconsistencies with the DEAR kaonic hydrogen data reported by Meissner et al. (2004) and Borasoy et al. (2004).
