# Implications of nonzero $θ_{13}$ for the neutrino mass hierarchy

> The Daya Bay, RENO, and Double Chooz experiments have discovered a large non-zero value for $θ_{13}$. We present a global analysis that includes these three experiments, Chooz, the Super-K atmospheric data, and the $ν_μ\rightarrow ν_e$ T2K and MINOS experiments that are sensitive to the hierarchy and the sign of $θ_{13}$. We report preliminary results in which we fix the mixing parameters other than $θ_{13}$ to those from a recent global analysis. Given there is no evidence for a non-zero CP violation, we assume $δ=0$. T2K and MINOS lie in a region of $L/E$ where there is a hierarchy degeneracy in the limit of $θ_{13}\rightarrow 0$ and no matter interaction. For non-zero $θ_{13}$, the symmetry is partially broken, but a degeneracy under the simultaneous exchange of both hierarchy and the sign of $θ_{13}$ remains. Matter effects break this symmetry such that the positions of the peaks in the oscillation probabilities maintain the two-fold symmetry, while the magnitude of the oscillations is sensitive to the hierarchy. This renders T2K and NO$ν$A, with different baselines and different matter effects, better able in combination to distinguish the hierarchy and the sign of $θ_{13}$. The large value of $θ_{13}$ yields effects from atmospheric data that distinguish hierarchies. We find for normal hierarchy, positive $θ_{13}$, $\sin^22θ_{13}=0.090\pm0.020$ and is 0.2% probable it is the correct combination; for normal hierarchy, negative $θ_{13}$, $\sin^22θ_{13}=0.108\pm0.023$ and is 2.2% probable; for inverse hierarchy, positive $θ_{13}$, $\sin^22θ_{13}=0.110\pm0.022$ and is 7.1% probable; for inverse hierarchy, negative $θ_{13}$, $\sin^22θ_{13}=0.113\pm0.022$ and is 90.5% probable, results that are inconsistent with two similar analyses.

# Implications of nonzero $\theta_{13}$ for the neutrino mass hierarchy

## Key claim
The large measured value of $\theta_{13}$ (sin²2θ₁₃ ≈ 0.1) partially breaks the four-fold degeneracy between neutrino mass hierarchy and the sign of θ₁₃, but a two-fold degeneracy remains under simultaneous exchange of both hierarchy and sign of θ₁₃. The authors find that the inverse hierarchy with negative θ₁₃ is 90.5% probable, while the normal hierarchy with positive θ₁₃ is only 0.2% probable, though they note these results are inconsistent with two similar analyses.

## Method
The authors performed a global analysis of neutrino oscillation data including the Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz, and original Chooz reactor experiments; Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino data; and the νμ → νe appearance data from T2K and MINOS long-baseline experiments. They fixed all mixing parameters except θ₁₃ to values from a recent global analysis and assumed the CP-violating phase δ = 0, since there is no evidence for non-zero CP violation. Bayesian statistics with a constant prior on sin²2θ₁₃ were used to calculate error bars and probabilities.

## Key numbers
- Combined reactor experiments yield sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.097 +0.019 −0.020, excluding θ₁₃ = 0 at 6.2σ (CL = 1.0 − 1 × 10⁻⁹ %)
- Daya Bay alone: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.093 +0.026 −0.027, excluding zero at CL = 1.0 − 6 × 10⁻⁶ %
- RENO alone: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.113 +0.037 −0.038, excluding zero at CL = 1.0 − 1 × 10⁻⁴ %
- Double Chooz alone: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.109 +0.061 −0.064, excluding zero at 99.7% CL
- For the global analysis including all experiments: normal hierarchy, positive θ₁₃: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.090 ± 0.020, 0.2% probable; normal hierarchy, negative θ₁₃: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.108 ± 0.023, 2.2% probable; inverse hierarchy, positive θ₁₃: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.093 ± 0.022, 7.1% probable; inverse hierarchy, negative θ₁₃: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.113 ± 0.022, 90.5% probable
- T2K+MINOS combined: sin²2θ₁₃ = 0.064 +0.103 −0.045 for normal hierarchy, positive θ₁₃, excluding zero at 99.5% CL

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Fogli et al. (2012) and Gonzalez-Garcia et al. (2012) by incorporating the newly measured large value of θ₁₃ from Daya Bay, RENO, and Double Chooz to address the neutrino mass hierarchy question. The authors find a stronger preference for inverse hierarchy with negative θ₁₃ than these prior analyses, attributing the discrepancy to differences in the treatment of Super-Kamiokande atmospheric data. The results demonstrate that the combination of T2K and the future NOνA experiment, with different baselines and matter effects, will be better able to distinguish the hierarchy and sign of θ₁₃. The large θ₁₃ value also enables atmospheric data to distinguish hierarchies through MSW resonance effects in the 3–10 GeV range and through interference terms linear in θ₁₃ at large L/E.
