# The Two-Photon-Exchange and γZ-Exchange Corrections to parity-Violating Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> Leading electroweak corrections play an important role in precision measurements of the strange form factors. We calculate the two-photon-exchange (TPE) and $γZ$-exchange corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry of the elastic electron-proton scattering in a simple hadronic model including the finite size of the proton. We find both can reach a few percent and are comparable in size with the current experimental measurements of strange-quark effects in the proton neutral weak current. The effect of $γZ$-exchange is in general larger than that of TPE, especially at low momentum transfer $Q^2\le 1 GeV^2$. Their combined effects on the values of $G^s_E+βG^s_M$ extracted in recent experiments can be as large as -40% in certain kinematics.

## Key claim

The two-photon-exchange (TPE) and γZ-exchange corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering can each reach a few percent, and their combined effect on the extracted strange form factor combination \(G^s_E + \beta G^s_M\) can be as large as \(-40\%\) in certain kinematics, even though the corrections to the asymmetry itself are at most around \(1\%\).

## Method

The authors calculate the TPE and γZ-exchange box diagrams in a hadronic model with monopole parameterizations of the proton electromagnetic and neutral weak form factors, including only elastic intermediate states. They use the FeynCalc and LoopTools packages to evaluate the full amplitudes numerically, subtract the soft-photon approximation to remove infrared divergences, and then compute the corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_{PV}\) and to the extracted strange form factors.

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 0.1\ \text{GeV}^2\), the full correction \(\delta\) to \(A_{PV}\) reaches about \(1.9\%\) at backward angle \(135^\circ\) (SAMPLE), about \(1.4\%\) at forward angle \(35^\circ\) (A4), and about \(0.36\%\) at forward angle \(6^\circ\) (HAPPEX).
- For HAPPEX data (2004, \(Q^2 = 0.477\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\epsilon = 0.974\)), \(\delta = 0.25\%\) and \(\delta_G = -22.00\%\).
- For HAPPEX data (2007, \(Q^2 = 0.109\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\epsilon = 0.994\)), \(\delta = 0.34\%\) and \(\delta_G = -39.75\%\).
- For A4 data (2004, \(Q^2 = 0.23\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\epsilon = 0.83\)), \(\delta = 0.86\%\) and \(\delta_G = -3.95\%\).
- For A4 data (2005, \(Q^2 = 0.108\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\epsilon = 0.83\)), \(\delta = 1.3\%\) and \(\delta_G = -3.5\%\).
- The γZ-exchange effect is generally larger than the TPE effect, especially at \(Q^2 \le 1\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the hadronic-model calculation of TPE corrections by Blunden (2003) to include γZ-exchange, and contrasts with the parton-model calculation by Afanasev (2005) which was reliable only at high \(Q^2\) and did not include γZ-exchange. The results show that previous estimates of γZ-exchange effects, based on the zero-momentum-transfer calculation by Marciano (1983), greatly overestimate the correction at finite \(Q^2\), and that the combined TPE and γZ corrections are comparable in size to the strange-quark signals measured by the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, A4, and G0 experiments.
