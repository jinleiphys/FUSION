# Signature of Granular Structures by Single-Event Intensity Interferometry

> The observation of a granular structure in high-energy heavy-ion collisions can be used as a signature for the quark-gluon plasma phase transition, if the phase transition is first order in nature. We propose methods to detect a granular structure by the single-event intensity interferometry. We find that the correlation function from a chaotic source of granular droplets exhibits large fluctuations, with maxima and minima at relative momenta which depend on the relative coordinates of the droplet centers. The presence of this type of maxima and minima of a single-event correlation function at many relative momenta is a signature for a granular structure and a first-order QCD phase transition. We further observe that the Fourier transform of the correlation function of a granular structure exhibits maxima at the relative spatial coordinates of the droplet centers, which can provide another signature of the granular structure.

## Key claim

The authors propose that large fluctuations (maxima and minima) in the single-event two-boson correlation function at many relative momenta can serve as a signature for a granular droplet structure in the emitting source, which in turn would indicate a first-order QCD phase transition in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, provided the source is chaotic and the granular density pattern survives post-transition evolution.

## Method

The authors model a chaotic source as a sum of Gaussian droplets with identical spatial and temporal widths, whose centers are randomly distributed according to a Gaussian probability distribution with standard deviation σ_R = 4 fm. They compute the two-boson correlation function C(q) from the Fourier transform of the source density, using the Koonin-Pratt formalism, and examine its structure analytically and numerically for N = 4 and N = 8 droplets with droplet size σ_d = 1.5 fm. They also develop a three-dimensional Fast Fourier Transform method to invert the correlation function and recover the source density distribution.

## Key numbers

- Droplet spatial standard deviation: σ_d = 1.5 fm
- Droplet center distribution standard deviation: σ_R = 4 fm
- Minimum droplet separation: greater than 2√3 σ_d (approximately 5.2 fm)
- Correlation function maximum at q = 0: C(q)|_{q=0} = 2
- For N droplets, there are N(N-1) maxima and N(N-1) minima for each integer n in the correlation function C(q)
- The average correlation function over many events yields ⟨C(q)⟩ = 1 + (1/N) exp(-σ_d² q²) + ((N-1)/N) exp(-(σ_d² + σ_R²) q²)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of granular structure in first-order QCD phase transitions by Witten (1984) and Randrup (2004), who analyzed spinodal instability and momentum-space clumping. It contrasts with the multi-event averaging approach of Pratt et al. (1992), who found smooth correlation functions even for small numbers of droplets. The authors propose single-event HBT interferometry as a method to detect the large event-by-event fluctuations characteristic of a first-order phase transition, and note that such measurements may become feasible with detector upgrades at RHIC or with heavy-ion collisions at LHC.
