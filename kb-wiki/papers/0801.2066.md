# High Energy Break-Up of Few-Nucleon Systems

> We discus recent developments in theory of high energy two-body break-up reactions of few-nucleon systems. The characteristics of these reactions are such that the hard two-body quasielastic subprocess can be clearly separated from the accompanying soft subprocesses. We discuss in details the hard rescattering model (HRM) in which hard photodisintegration develops in two stages. At first, photon knocks-out an energetic quark which rescatters subsequently with a quark of the other nucleon. The latter provides a mechanism of sharing the initial high momentum of the photon by the outgoing two nucleons. Within HRM we discuss hard break-up reactions involving $^2D$ and $^3He$ targets. Another development of HRM is the prediction of new helicity selection mechanism for hard two-body reactions, which was apparently confirmed in the recent JLab experiment.

# High Energy Break-Up of Few-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim
The hard rescattering model (HRM) provides a parameter-free prediction for the unpolarized cross section of deuteron photodisintegration at 90 degrees center-of-mass angle, reproducing the same s^{-11} energy dependence as the quark counting rule without requiring the onset of perturbative QCD. The model's prediction of a large longitudinal polarization asymmetry (C_{z'}) due to a helicity selection mechanism has been apparently confirmed by recent Jefferson Lab data, though the earlier data had large errors indicating vanishing values.

## Method
The author develops the hard rescattering model (HRM) in which high-energy photodisintegration of a two-nucleon system proceeds in two stages: first, the photon knocks out an energetic quark from one nucleon; second, that quark undergoes a hard, high-momentum-transfer rescattering with a quark from the second nucleon via gluon exchange. The soft d→NN transition vertices are evaluated using conventional deuteron wave functions, while the hard quark-interchange rescattering part is expressed through experimentally measured high-momentum-transfer elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. The formalism is extended to two-proton break-up from a ^3He target, where the cross section is expressed in terms of the hard elastic pp scattering cross section and the ^3He ground-state wave function.

## Key numbers
- At photon energy E_γ > 2 GeV, the invariant momentum transfer at 90 degrees CM, -t|_{90^0}, exceeds 4 GeV^2, and the invariant mass of the NN system M_NN = sqrt(s) exceeds 2 GeV.
- The quark counting rule prediction for the differential cross section of γd→pn is dσ/dt ∼ s^{-11}, which was experimentally confirmed starting at E_γ = 1 GeV at SLAC and Jefferson Lab.
- The HRM prediction for the differential cross section at 90 degrees CM is parameter free because the function C(θ_cm=90^0) ≈ 1.
- The HRM predicts that C_{z'} (longitudinal polarization asymmetry) will approach unity with increasing photon energy at 90 degrees CM scattering; data at E_γ = 1.9 GeV appear to confirm large values of C_{z'}.
- For two-proton break-up from ^3He, HRM predicts vanishing C_{z'} due to Pauli principle suppression, in contrast to the large asymmetry for pn break-up.

## Context
This work extends the hard rescattering model previously developed by the author and collaborators (Sargsian, 2001) for deuteron photodisintegration to include ^3He targets and polarization observables. It contrasts with the reduced nuclear amplitude (RNA) formalism and the quark-gluon string (QGS) model, both of which require absolute normalization and describe lower-energy data. The HRM predictions for energy oscillations at 90 degrees CM in pp break-up from ^3He, similar to those observed in elastic pp scattering, offer a means to discriminate between models. A pioneering experiment on high-energy pp break-up from ^3He was recently performed at Jefferson Lab, and the helicity selection mechanism predictions were tested in JLab experiments (2006, 2007).
