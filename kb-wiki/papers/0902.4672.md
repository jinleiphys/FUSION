# Combined analysis of eta and eta-prime hadro- and photo-production off nucleons

> The production of $η$ and $η^\prime$ mesons in photon- and hadron-induced reactions are studied in a combined analysis based on an effective Lagrangian approach in order to learn about the relevant production mechanisms and the possible role of nucleon resonances in these reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a combined analysis of η and η′ meson production in both photon- and hadron-induced reactions can reveal the relevant production mechanisms and the role of nucleon resonances, but the authors emphasize that the existing data are insufficient to establish a unique set of resonance parameters and that the extracted parameters are highly correlated.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic meson-exchange effective Lagrangian approach to describe three reaction types: γN → N M, πN → N M, and NN → NN M (where M = η, η′). Photoproduction is calculated using s-, u-, and t-channel Feynman diagrams plus a generalized contact term to ensure gauge invariance, with final-state interaction (FSI) effects included; πN → Nη is treated at tree level; and NN → NNη is calculated in the Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) including both NN FSI and initial-state interaction (ISI). Free parameters (resonance parameters, NNM coupling constants, and a cutoff parameter at the electromagnetic vector-meson exchange vertex) are fixed by a global fitting procedure to reproduce available data across all three reaction channels.

## Key numbers

- The S11(1535) resonance dominates π−p → nη total cross sections up to W ∼ 1.6 GeV.
- For γp → pη, spin-3/2 resonances (D13(1520), D13(1700), P13(1720)) are important in describing angular distributions in the range Tγ = 1.07 to 1.6 GeV.
- The NNη′ coupling constant is constrained to be not much greater than gNNη′ = 2 from angular distributions at higher energies and large scattering angles in γp → pη′.
- For pp → ppη, the model underestimates total cross section data near threshold for excess energy Q < 30 MeV when based on a stronger P-wave contribution.
- For pp → ppη′, total cross section data exist for excess energies up to Q ≈ 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel and meson-exchange approaches previously applied to η and η′ production (e.g., Nakayama et al. (2008), Haberzettl et al. (2006)) by performing a simultaneous fit to photoproduction, pion-induced, and nucleon-nucleon reactions. The analysis enables stricter constraints on resonance-nucleon-meson coupling strengths, particularly for mesons other than the pion, and highlights the need for more precise data on spin observables and analyzing powers to disentangle competing resonance contributions. The authors note that extending the model to include vector meson (ω, ρ) production is an ongoing effort to further constrain resonance parameters.
