# Minimal Schemes for Large Neutrino Mixings with Inverted Hierarchy

> Existing oscillation data point to nonzero neutrino masses with large mixings. We analyze the generic features of the neutrino Majorana mass matrix with inverted hierarchy and construct realistic {\it minimal schemes} for the neutrino mass matrix that can explain the large (but not maximal) ν_e - ν_mu mixing of MSW-LAM as well as the nearly maximal ν_mu - ν_tau mixing and the small (or negligible) ν_e --> ν_tau transition. These minimal schemes are quite unique and turn out to be extremely predictive. Implications for neutrinoless double beta decay, tritium beta decay and cosmology are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs minimal Majorana mass matrix schemes for the inverted neutrino mass hierarchy that can explain the large but non-maximal solar mixing angle of the MSW-LAM solution (not just maximal mixing), the nearly maximal atmospheric mixing, and the small electron-to-tau transition, using only a minimal set of parameters that are highly predictive.

## Method

The authors start from zeroth-order mass matrices motivated by approximate \(L_e - L_\mu - L_\tau\) symmetry (Type-B1) or a different texture (Type-B2) and add minimal perturbations parameterized by three small dimensionless parameters \((\kappa, \delta, \delta')\) for Type-B1 or \((\delta, \xi)\) for Type-B2. They solve the diagonalization equations for the \(3\times3\) Majorana mass matrix in the CP-conserving limit, expanding in the small parameters \(\delta'\) and \(\sin\theta_{13}\) to first order, and reduce the number of free inputs to a single parameter (the solar mixing angle \(\theta_\odot\)) for Type-B1.

## Key numbers

- Solar mixing angle input range: \(25^\circ \leq \theta_\odot \leq 39^\circ\) at 95% C.L. (MSW-LAM), with central value \(\theta_\odot \simeq 32^\circ\).
- Predicted atmospheric mixing angle for Type-B1: \(39.8^\circ \leq \theta_2 \leq 44.5^\circ\) (consistent with observed \(33^\circ \leq \theta_{\rm atm} \leq 57^\circ\) at 95% C.L.).
- Predicted \(\sin\theta_{13}\) for Type-B1: \(0.046 \leq \theta_3 \leq 0.13\) (consistent with Chooz bound \(\sin^2\theta_{\rm chz} \lesssim 0.04\) at 95% C.L.).
- Predicted mass ratio for Type-B1: \(0.095 \leq \Delta_\odot / \Delta_{\rm atm} \leq 0.28\) (consistent with observed \(4.2\times10^{-3} \leq \Delta_\odot / \Delta_{\rm atm} \leq 0.17\) at 95% C.L.).
- Neutrino mass scale bound for inverted hierarchy: \(0.036\ {\rm eV} \leq m_0 \simeq m_{1,2} \leq 0.074\ {\rm eV}\) at 99% C.L. from \(\Delta_{\rm atm}\).
- Predicted effective Majorana mass for neutrinoless double beta decay: \(0.014\ {\rm eV} \leq m_{ee}[{\rm B1}] \leq 0.029\ {\rm eV}\) and \(0.036\ {\rm eV} \leq m_{ee}[{\rm B2}] \leq 0.074\ {\rm eV}\) at 99% C.L.
- For Type-B2: predicted \(\sin\theta_{13} \leq 0.04\) and ratio \(\delta/\xi\) between 0.3 and 1.2.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of inverted hierarchy neutrino mass matrices by Altarelli and Feruglio (1999) and others, who proposed the zeroth-order forms with approximate \(L_e - L_\mu - L_\tau\) symmetry. The paper addresses the difficulty noted by several authors (e.g., in fits to solar data) that such symmetric forms predict maximal solar mixing, which is excluded by the MSW-LAM solution. The minimal schemes constructed here enable predictions for upcoming neutrinoless double beta decay experiments (Nemo3, Cuore, Genius, Exo, Majorana, Moon) and for long-baseline experiments (MINOS, ICARUS) that may discriminate between Type-B1 and Type-B2 by measuring \(\theta_{13}\) at the level of 0.05.
