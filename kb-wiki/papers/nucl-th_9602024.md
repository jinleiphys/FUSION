# Elastic Pion Scattering on the Deuteron in a Multiple Scattering Model

> Pion elastic scattering on deuterium is studied in the KMT multiple scattering approach developed in momentum space. Using a Paris wave function and the same methods and approximations as commonly used in pion scattering on heavier nuclei excellent agreement with differential cross section data is obtained for a wide range of pion energies. Only for $T_π>250$ MeV and very backward angles, discrepancies appear that are reminiscent of disagreements in pion scattering on $^3$He, $^3$H, and $^4$He. At low energies the second order corrections have been included. Polarization observables are studied in detail. While tensor analyzing powers are well reproduced, vector analyzing powers exhibit dramatic discrepancies.

## Key claim

The KMT multiple scattering approach in momentum space, using the same methods and approximations as for heavier nuclei, achieves excellent agreement with differential cross section data for pion-deuteron elastic scattering across a wide energy range, but only when second-order corrections are included at low energies; discrepancies appear for Tπ > 250 MeV at very backward angles, and vector analyzing powers exhibit dramatic discrepancies that the abstract does not fully capture.

## Method

The authors solved the KMT multiple scattering equations in momentum space using a Paris deuteron wave function, free πN scattering amplitudes from phase-shift analysis with separable off-shell extrapolation, and a modified impulse approximation for the energy relation. They neglected coupling to the break-up channel and included second-order corrections at low energies through a phenomenological ρ² term. The integral equations for partial wave amplitudes were solved by matrix inversion, with Coulomb interaction treated via the Vincent-Phatak matching procedure.

## Key numbers

- πd scattering length from first-order potential: aπd⁽¹⁾ ≈ -0.015 fm
- Experimental πd scattering length: aπdexp = -(0.073 ± 0.02) fm
- Second-order correction to scattering length: aπd⁽²⁾ = -0.034 fm, giving total aπd ≈ -0.049 fm
- Faddeev calculation result for comparison: aπdFad = -0.046 fm
- Mean inverse radius of deuteron: ⟨1/r⟩ = 0.46 fm⁻¹
- Separable form factor range parameter: r₀ = 0.47 fm
- D-state contribution becomes significant at momentum transfers Q > 1.78 fm⁻¹ (corresponding to Tπ > 180 MeV, θπ > 90°)
- At Tπ = 294 MeV and θπ = 180°, T₂₀ ≈ -√2 and τ₂₂ ≈ 2τ₂₁ ≈ -1/√3 when R₀₀⁽⁰⁾ = √2 R₀₂⁽²⁾
- At θπ = 90° with S-state dominance: T₂₀⁽ˢ⁾ ≈ -√2/4, τ₂₂⁽ˢ⁾ ≈ 2τ₂₁⁽ˢ⁾ ≈ -1/√3

## Context

This work extends the KMT momentum-space multiple scattering approach, previously successful for nuclei with A=4-40 (Kamalov, Tiator, Bennhold 1993, 1992), to the deuteron as the simplest few-body system. It provides a simpler alternative to full three-body Faddeev calculations (Garcilazo et al.) while achieving comparable results for differential cross sections in the Δ-resonance region. The approach enables straightforward extension to coherent π⁰ photoproduction on the deuteron, and the unresolved discrepancies in vector analyzing powers point to the need for a consistent description of polarization observables across light nuclei including ³He.
