# Production of spectator hypermatter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the formation of large hyper-fragments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions within two transport models, DCM and UrQMD. Our goal is to explore a new mechanism for the formation of strange nuclear systems via capture of hyperons by relatively cold spectator matter produced in semi-peripheral collisions. We investigate basic characteristics of the produced hyper-spectators and evaluate the production probabilities of multi-strange systems. Advantages of the proposed mechanisms over an alternative coalescence mechanism are analysed. We also discuss how such systems can be detected taking into account the background of free hyperons. This investigation is important for the development of new experimental methods for producing hyper-nuclei in peripheral relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, which are now underway at GSI and are planned for the future FAIR and NICA facilities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new mechanism for producing multi-strange hypernuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions: hyperons produced in the participant zone can be captured by cold spectator matter via secondary interactions, rather than only through direct coalescence. The authors hedge that while the mechanism is promising, the probabilities for capturing multiple hyperons are small (e.g., ~10⁻⁵ for three Λ hyperons) and the results depend on the transport model used, with DCM and UrQMD predictions differing by up to a factor of 2.

## Method

The authors used two independent transport models, the Dubna Cascade Model (DCM) and the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD v2.3), to simulate relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In both models, hyperon absorption by spectator matter was determined by a potential criterion: a hyperon is captured if its kinetic energy in the spectator rest frame is less than the attractive hyperon potential V_Λ(ρ), parameterized with α=57.5 MeV and β=0.522. The DCM included multiple checks of the absorption criterion over time (up to 100 fm/c), while UrQMD considered hyperons as finally absorbed once the criterion was first satisfied.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at 20 A GeV, the probability of capturing one Λ hyperon in a spectator is a few percent, two Λ hyperons have probability ~5×10⁻⁴, and three Λ hyperons have probability ~10⁻⁵ (DCM calculation, minimum bias, 10⁶ events).
- For Au+Au at 20 A GeV, the probability of a Ξ hyperon being absorbed in a spectator is on the order of 10⁻⁵ per event (UrQMD calculation).
- The momentum distribution of absorbed Λ hyperons in Au+Au at 20 A GeV has a smooth cut-off above 250-300 MeV/c, with a Boltzmann fit giving an apparent temperature T ≈ 10 MeV for momenta above 100 MeV/c.
- In the reaction ⁶Li (1.9 A GeV) + ¹²C, the yield of free Λ hyperons in the spectator region is 1-2 orders of magnitude larger than the yield of hypernuclei from the projectile spectator (DCM calculation).
- For Au+Au at 20 A GeV, the excess of free Λ hyperons over hypernuclei in spectator kinematic zones is less than a factor of 2-3 (DCM calculation).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spectator fragmentation by the ALADIN collaboration (1995, 1997) and the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), and builds on the coalescence model approach used by the GiBUU group for hypernucleus production. The mechanism differs from central collision production at RHIC, which can only produce very light hypernuclei (A < 4) due to high excitation energy. The results are relevant for ongoing experiments at GSI (HypHi experiment) and planned experiments at FAIR and NICA facilities, where the longer lifetime of projectile hyper-fragments due to the Lorentz factor enables vertex detector identification.
