# Short range correlations in relativistic nuclear matter models

> Short range correlations are introduced using unitary correlation method in a relativistic approach to the equation of state of the infinite nuclear matter in the framework of the Hartree-Fock approximation. It is shown that the correlations give rise to an extra node in the ground-state wave-function in the nucleons, contrary to what happens in non-relativistic calculations with a hard core. The effect of the correlations in the ground state properties of the nuclear matter and neutron matter is studied. The nucleon effective mass and equation of state (EOS) are very sensitive to short range correlations. In particular, if the pion contact term is neglected a softening of the EOS is predicted. Correlations have also an important effect on the neutron matter EOS which presents no binding but only a very shallow minimum contrary to the Walecka model.

## Key claim

Short range correlations, introduced via a unitary correlation method in a relativistic Hartree-Fock framework, produce an extra node in the ground-state relative wave function of nucleons (unlike the wound found in non-relativistic hard-core calculations), and the equation of state (EOS) is softened when the pion contact term is neglected, but only if the correlation function is treated variationally.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic Hamiltonian including exchange of sigma, omega, rho, and pi mesons. Short range correlations are introduced via a unitary operator method, approximated by a Jastrow-type correlation factor \(f(r)=1-(\alpha+\beta r)e^{-\gamma r}\) acting on the two-body wave function. The correlated ground-state energy is computed in the leading order of a cluster expansion within the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation, with the correlation parameters \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), \(\gamma\) fixed by a normalization condition (ensuring unitarity to leading cluster order), energy minimization, and a healing distance constraint.

## Key numbers

* The sigma and omega meson masses are set to \(m_\sigma = 550\) MeV and \(m_\omega = 783\) MeV; the rho and pion masses are \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV and \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV.
* The rho-meson coupling is taken as \(g_\rho^2/4\pi = 0.55\) and the pion coupling as \(f_\pi^2/4\pi = 0.08\).
* The correlation function parameters \(\alpha\), \(\beta\), and \(\gamma\) are chosen such that \(\gamma\) depends on the Fermi momentum \(k_F\) via \(\gamma = a_1 + a_2\, k_F/k_{F0}\), with \(a_1\) and \(a_2\) conveniently chosen; \(\beta\) is determined by the normalization condition \(\int (f^2(r)-1)\, d^3r = 0\), and \(\alpha\) is fixed by energy minimization.
* The healing distance is taken as approximately 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Panda et al., 2005) of short range correlations in the sigma-omega Walecka model by including exchange of pions and rho mesons. It contrasts with non-relativistic approaches (Coester, 1965) that require explicit three-body repulsive mechanisms to achieve saturation, and with the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) which yields too stiff an EOS and too small an effective mass. The results are compared to the quark-meson-coupling model (QMC) and show that correlations soften the EOS and reduce the effective coupling constants, but the neutron matter EOS still exhibits a shallow minimum (unlike the Walecka model) unless the pion contact term is included. The work enables future studies with more flexible correlation functions (e.g., \(f(r)=1-(1+\alpha r+\beta r^2)e^{-\gamma r}\)) to handle the repulsive contact term properly.
