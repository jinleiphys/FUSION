# Electromagnetic Meson Form Factors in the Salpeter Model

> We present a covariant scheme to calculate mesonic transitions in the framework of the Salpeter equation for $q\bar{q}$-states. The full Bethe Salpeter amplitudes are reconstructed from equal time amplitudes which were obtained in a previous paper\cite{Mue} by solving the Salpeter equation for a confining plus an instanton induced interaction. This method is applied to calculate electromagnetic form factors and decay widths of low lying pseudoscalar and vector mesons including predictions for CEBAF experiments. We also describe the momentum transfer dependence for the processes $π^0,η,η'\rightarrowγγ^*$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a covariant Salpeter model, with no free parameters beyond those already fixed to the mass spectrum and non-hadronic decays, can describe electromagnetic form factors and decay widths of light pseudoscalar and vector mesons, but the authors note that the formalism reaches its limits for the deeply bound pion, where the form factor becomes extremely sensitive to the interaction kernel and shows structures not seen in data.

## Method

The authors reconstruct full Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes from equal-time Salpeter amplitudes (previously obtained by solving the Salpeter equation with a confining potential plus an instanton-induced interaction). They then compute electromagnetic current matrix elements in the Mandelstam formalism, boosting the outgoing vertex function according to the Lorentz transformation properties of the amplitudes, and evaluate the four-dimensional integrals by contour integration and principal-value techniques to handle the off-shell quark propagators.

## Key numbers

- Charged kaon charge radius: calculated \( \langle r^2_{K^{\pm}} \rangle^{1/2}_{\text{calc}} = 0.60\,\text{fm} \), compared to experimental values \( 0.58\pm0.04\,\text{fm} \) (Ame) and \( 0.53\pm0.05\,\text{fm} \) (Dal).
- Neutral kaon charge radius: calculated \( \langle r^2_{K^0} \rangle_{\text{calc}} = -0.070\,\text{fm}^2 \), compared to experimental \( \langle r^2_{K^0} \rangle_{\text{exp}} = -(0.054\pm 0.026)\,\text{fm}^2 \) (Mol).
- Zero in the charged \( K^{*\pm}\rightarrow K^{\pm}\gamma^* \) form factor predicted at \( Q^2_0 = 2.7\,\text{GeV}^2 \) for the default quark mass ratio \( m_s/m_n = 2.3 \).
- Normalized \( \omega\pi\gamma^* \) form factor: calculated pole parameter \( \Lambda_{\omega\pi}^{\text{calc}} = 0.63\,\text{GeV} \), compared to experimental \( \Lambda_{\omega\pi}^{\text{exp}} = (0.65\pm0.03)\,\text{GeV} \) (Dzhel) and VDM value \( 0.77\,\text{GeV} \).
- Decay width \( \Gamma(K^{*\pm}\rightarrow K^{\pm}\gamma) \): calculated 64 keV, experimental \( 50\pm5 \) keV.
- Decay width \( \Gamma(K^{*0}\rightarrow K^{0}\gamma) \): calculated 112 keV, experimental \( 117\pm10 \) keV.
- Decay width \( \Gamma(\phi\rightarrow\eta\gamma) \): calculated 60 keV, experimental \( 56.9\pm2.9 \) keV.
- Decay width \( \Gamma(\rho\rightarrow\eta\gamma) \): calculated 50 keV, experimental \( 58\pm10 \) keV.
- Decay width \( \Gamma(\omega\rightarrow\eta\gamma) \): calculated 5.6 keV, experimental \( 4.0\pm1.7 \) keV.
- Decay width \( \Gamma(\phi\rightarrow\eta'\gamma) \): calculated \( 0.18\pm0.02 \) keV, experimental \( <1.8 \) keV.
- Contribution of negative-energy states to the charged kaon form factor at zero momentum transfer: 25%, giving a radius of \( 0.67\,\text{fm} \) when only positive-energy states are included.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Salpeter model (Mue, 1994) for light meson masses and non-hadronic decays to electromagnetic transitions with hadronic final states, providing predictions for CEBAF experiments. It contrasts with nonrelativistic quark models (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985) and with vector dominance model predictions, showing that the relativistic treatment including the full four-momentum dependence of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is essential for high momentum transfer. The results enable future studies of the complete meson spectrum including one-gluon-exchange for heavy systems, and provide predictions for processes such as \( \rho\rightarrow\pi\gamma^* \) relevant to meson exchange currents in the deuteron form factor.
