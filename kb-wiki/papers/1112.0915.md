# Collective flow in p-Pb and d-Pb collisions at TeV energies

> We apply the hydrodynamic model for the dynamics of matter created in p-Pb collisions at 4.4TeV and d-Pb collisions at 3.11TeV. The fluctuating initial conditions are calculated in the Glauber Monte-Carlo model for several centrality classes. The expansion is performed event by event in 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics. Noticeable elliptic and triangular flows appear in the distributions of produced particles.

# Collective flow in p-Pb and d-Pb collisions at TeV energies

## Key claim

Event-by-event 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations predict noticeable elliptic and triangular flow in p-Pb collisions at 4.4 TeV and d-Pb collisions at 3.11 TeV, but the hydrodynamic model becomes less justified as system size and lifetime decrease, and the results depend on the chosen width of the Gaussian smearing for nucleon contributions (a 0.3 fm width increases integrated flow by approximately 10% compared to 0.4 fm).

## Method

The authors used a Glauber Monte-Carlo model to generate fluctuating initial conditions for the entropy density, proportional to the number of participant nucleons, with asymmetric longitudinal profiles for forward and backward going nucleons. They evolved these initial conditions event-by-event using 3+1-dimensional second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosity, starting at proper time 0.6 fm/c, and performed particle emission at freeze-out temperature 135 MeV using the Cooper-Frye formula with viscous corrections via the THERMINATOR event generator.

## Key numbers

- p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 4.4 TeV: charged particle density at midrapidity dN/deta_PS = 50 +/- 5 for central collisions
- d-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.11 TeV: charged particle density at midrapidity dN/deta_PS = 80 +/- 5 for central collisions
- Elliptic flow v2 in p-Pb collisions: 3-4% with little centrality dependence
- Elliptic flow v2 in d-Pb collisions: reaches almost 10% for central collisions (0-5% centrality bin)
- Triangular flow v3 in d-Pb collisions: similar magnitude as in p-Pb collisions, with almost no centrality variation
- Deconfined phase lifetime: 3-4 fm/c in high-multiplicity events (T > 160 MeV contour shows 3.5 fm/c for a p-Pb event with N_part = 24)
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio: eta/s = 0.08 in the quark-gluon plasma phase, increasing to 0.5 in the hadronic phase
- Initial entropy density normalization: s_0 = 0.72 GeV^3 for both p-Pb and d-Pb collisions
- Gaussian smearing width for nucleon contributions: sigma_w = 0.4 fm (using 0.3 fm increases integrated elliptic and triangular flow by approximately 10%)

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling from heavy-ion collisions (where collective flow is well-established at RHIC and LHC) to small systems (p-Pb and d-Pb), motivated by the observation that central p-Pb and d-Pb collisions produce multiplicities and interaction region sizes similar to peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at 60-80% centrality at 2.76 TeV (Aamodt 2010). The calculations use event-by-event 3+1-D viscous hydrodynamics, which only one other group (Schenke 2010, 2011) had applied to heavy-ion collisions at the time. The results enable quantitative predictions for future LHC p-Pb experiments and provide a baseline for distinguishing collective effects from other nuclear effects on parton distribution functions and saturation (Dumitru 2011, Kharzeev 2004, Salgado 2011).
