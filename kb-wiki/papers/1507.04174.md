# Pion String evolving in a thermal bath

> By using the symmetry improved CJT effective formalism, we study a pion string of the $O(4)$ linear sigma model at finite temperature in chiral limit. In terms of the Kibble-Zurek mechanism we reconsider the production and evolution of the pion string in a thermal bath. Finally, we estimate the pion string density and its possible signal during the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

By using the symmetry improved CJT effective formalism to restore the Goldstone theorem, the authors study pion string production and evolution in a thermal bath during the chiral phase transition, finding that pion strings can form and their density depends sensitively on a model parameter \(\kappa\) that must be constrained by experiment.

## Method

The authors apply the Kibble-Zurek mechanism to the \(O(4)\) linear sigma model at finite temperature in the chiral limit, using a symmetry-improved Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) effective potential that enforces massless pions in the broken phase. They solve the gap equations and equation of motion for the pion string profile at finite temperature, then estimate the string density and loop distribution by matching the Zurek freeze-out time to the chemical freeze-out temperature from heavy-ion collision data.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature \(T_c = 157\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for zero chemical potential, from lattice QCD).
- Chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{\mathrm{ch}} \simeq 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{\mathrm{kin}} \simeq 90\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for LHC Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2760\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- Zurek time \(t_z \simeq 9.60\ \mathrm{fm}\) and critical time \(t_c \simeq 8.37\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Equilibrium correlation length at freeze-out \(\xi_z \simeq 1.81\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Pion string density \(\mathfrak{n} \simeq 0.3\ \mathrm{fm}/\mathrm{fm}^3\) for \(\kappa=1\) and \(\mathfrak{n} \simeq 0.003\ \mathrm{fm}/\mathrm{fm}^3\) for \(\kappa=100\).
- Total number of pion strings created at LHC Pb-Pb collisions varies between \(N \simeq 116\) (for \(\kappa=1\)) and \(N \simeq 1\) (for \(\kappa=100\)).
- Fireball volume at chemical freeze-out \(V(T_{\mathrm{ch}}) \simeq 5380\ \mathrm{fm}^3\) and radius \(r_{\mathrm{ch}} \sim 10.87\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the plasma stabilization mechanism for pion strings proposed by Nagasawa (1999, 2002) and later confirmed by Karouby and Brandenberger (2012, 2013), applying it to the heavy-ion collision environment using the symmetry-improved CJT formalism of Pilaftsis and Teresi (2013). The authors use the Kibble-Zurek mechanism, originally developed for cosmological phase transitions, to estimate pion string production during the QCD chiral phase transition in LHC Pb-Pb collisions. The results enable future experimental searches for pion string signals in heavy-ion collision data, though the authors note that the unstable case (immediate decay after chemical freeze-out) is closer to the experimental situation.
