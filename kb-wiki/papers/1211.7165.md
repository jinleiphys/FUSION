# Low-lying hypernuclei in the relativistic quark-gluon model

> Low-lying hypernuclei $ ^3_őõH$, $ ^3_ő£H$, $ ^3_őõHe$, $ ^3_ő£He$ are described by the relativistic nine-quark equations in the framework of the dispersion relation technique. The approximate solutions of these equations using the method based on the extraction of leading singularities of the amplitudes are obtained. The relativistic nine-quark amplitudes of hypernuclei, including the quarks of three flavors ($u$, $d$, $s$) are calculated. The poles of these amplitudes determine the masses of hypernuclei. The mass of state $ ^3_őõH$ with the isospin I=0 and the spin-parity $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+$ is equal to $M=2991\, MeV$.

# Low-lying hypernuclei in the relativistic quark-gluon model

## Key claim
The relativistic nine-quark equations, solved via extraction of leading singularities of the amplitudes, predict masses for low-lying hypernuclei (¬≥_őõH, ¬≥_ő£H, ¬≥_őõHe, ¬≥_ő£He) that are within approximately 1 MeV of experimental values, but the model relies on only three parameters fitted to the experimental masses of ¬≥He and the hypertriton, and the calculations are approximate rather than exact solutions of the full equations.

## Method
The authors construct relativistic nine-quark amplitudes for hypernuclei using dispersion relation techniques and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach, considering only planar diagrams as dictated by the 1/N_c expansion. They extract leading singularities from the coupled integral equations to obtain reduced amplitudes, where the diquark vertex functions and Chew-Mandelstam phase-space functions are defined with a cutoff őõ = 9 and gluon coupling constant g = 0.2122. The poles of the reduced amplitudes determine the bound-state masses of the hypernuclei.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous relativistic six-quark equations for dibaryons (Gerasyuta and Matskevich, 2012) and nine-quark equations for ¬≥He (Gerasyuta and Matskevich, prior paper) to include strange quarks and describe hypernuclei. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD calculations (e.g., Beane et al., 2011; Yamazaki et al., 2011; Doi et al., 2011) that are performed at unphysically heavy quark masses (m_ŌÄ ‚Čą 800 MeV) and have not yet been extrapolated to the continuum or physical pion masses. The results enable future comparisons with experimental hypernuclear spectroscopy programs at DAő¶NE, FAIR, Jefferson Lab, J-PARC, and Mainz, and suggest that analogous calculations at lighter quark masses in lattice QCD could provide direct predictions for nuclear and hypernuclear physics.
