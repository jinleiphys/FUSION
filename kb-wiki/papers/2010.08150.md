# Microscopic coupled-channel calculation of proton and alpha inelastic scattering to the $4^+_1$ and $4^+_2$ states of $^{24}$Mg

> Background: The triaxial and hexadecapole deformations of the K=0+ and K=2+ bands of 24Mg have been investigated by the inelastic scatterings of various probes, including electrons, protons, and alpha particles, for a prolonged time. However, it has been challenging to explain the unique properties of the scatterings observed for the $4^+_1$ state through reaction calculations. Purpose: To investigate the structure and transition properties of the K=0+ and K=2+ bands of 24Mg employing the microscopic structure and reaction calculations via inelastic proton and alpha-scattering. Particularly, the E4 transitions to the $4^+_1$ and $4^+_2$ states were reexamined. Method: The structure of 24Mg was calculated employing the variation after the parity and total-angular momentum projections in the framework of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). The inelastic proton and alpha reactions were calculated by the microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) approach by folding the Melbourne g-matrix NN interaction with the AMD densities of 24Mg. Results: Reasonable results were obtained on the properties of the structure, including the energy spectra and E2 and E4 transitions of the K=0+ and K=2+ bands owing to the enhanced collectivity of triaxial deformation. The MCC+AMD calculation successfully reproduced the angular distributions of the $4^+_1$ and $4^+_2$ cross sections of proton scattering at incident energies of $E_p$=40--100MeV and alpha-scattering at $E_α$=100--400MeV. Conclusions: This is the first microscopic calculation that described the unique properties of the $0^+_1\to 4^+_1$ transition. In the inelastic scattering to the $4^+_1$ state, the dominant two-step process of the $0^+_1\to 2^+_1\to 4^+_1$ transitions and the deconstructive interference is the weak one-step process were essential.

## Key claim

This is the first microscopic calculation that successfully describes the unique properties of the $0^+_1\to 4^+_1$ transition in $^{24}$Mg, where the dominant two-step process ($0^+_1\to 2^+_1\to 4^+_1$) and destructive interference with the weak one-step process were essential for reproducing the observed angular distributions.

## Method

The structure of $^{24}$Mg was calculated using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after parity and total-angular-momentum projections, employing a fixed-spin version (AMD(fix-s)) rather than the previous spin-optimized version. Inelastic proton and alpha reactions were calculated using the microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) approach, folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with the AMD densities of $^{24}$Mg, with transition densities renormalized by factors $f^\textrm{tr}$ to fit experimental electromagnetic transition strengths and charge form factors.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic deformation parameters for the $0^+_1(K=0)$ state: $\beta=0.35$, $\gamma=10^\circ$ (AMD(fix-s)) compared to $\beta=0.35$, $\gamma=3^\circ$ (AMD(opt-s))
- Intrinsic deformation parameters for the $4^+_1(K=0)$ state: $\beta=0.37$, $\gamma=12^\circ$ (AMD(fix-s)) compared to $\beta=0.29$, $\gamma=7^\circ$ (AMD(opt-s))
- $B(E2;2^+_1\to 0^+_1)$: AMD(fix-s) gives 70.2 $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 88.4(4.1) $e^2$fm$^4$ from Firestone (2007))
- $B(E2;4^+_1\to 2^+_1)$: AMD(fix-s) gives 101.7 $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 160(16) $e^2$fm$^4$ from Firestone (2007))
- $B(\text{IS}4;4^+_1\to 0^+_1)/4$: AMD(fix-s) original 243 $e^2$fm$^8$, normalized to 477 $e^2$fm$^8$ with $f^\textrm{tr}=1.4$ (experimental $(e,e')$: 200(30) $e^2$fm$^8$ from Zarek (1978))
- $B(\text{IS}4;4^+_2\to 0^+_1)/4$: AMD(fix-s) original 3350 $e^2$fm$^8$, normalized to 4050 $e^2$fm$^8$ with $f^\textrm{tr}=1.1$ (experimental $(e,e')$: 4800(600) $e^2$fm$^8$ from Zarek (1978))
- Proton scattering calculations reproduced angular distributions at $E_p=40$--100 MeV
- Alpha scattering calculations reproduced angular distributions at $E_\alpha=100$--400 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous AMD calculations of $^{24}$Mg (Kanada-En'yo, 2019) that underestimated collectivity and failed to reproduce the $0^+_1\to 4^+_1$ charge form factors, and earlier phenomenological coupled-channel calculations by DeLeo (1979, 1981) that suggested two-step contributions but gave unsatisfactory agreement with data. The improved AMD(fix-s) approach, previously applied to $^{28}$Si (Kanada-En'yo, 2020), enhances triaxial collectivity and successfully describes the unique two-peak structure of the $4^+_1$ charge form factors for the first time microscopically. This enables unified treatment of electromagnetic transitions and inelastic scattering without adjustable reaction parameters, and the method can be applied to other $sd$-shell nuclei where shape coexistence and cluster dynamics are important.
