# Intrinsic spin distributions in multinucleon transfer reactions

> Time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TD-CDFT) combined with angular momentum projection is developed and applied to study multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions, with a focus on the intrinsic angular momentum distributions of the final fragments. Using the illustrative reaction $^{40}$Ca + $^{208}$Pb across a range of impact parameters, we find that the MNT process generates broad distributions of intrinsic spins. These distributions arise from the conversion of relative orbital angular momentum into intrinsic spin due to frictional interactions between the colliding nuclei. Additionally, mutual information (entanglement Shannon entropy) is employed to analyze correlations between the intrinsic spins of the fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that multinucleon transfer (MNT) reactions generate broad intrinsic spin distributions in the final fragments, arising from conversion of relative orbital angular momentum into intrinsic spin via frictional interactions, but that the spin-spin correlations between the two fragments are "exceptionally weak" as quantified by mutual information, with negligible dependence on impact parameter.

## Method

Time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TD-CDFT) was combined with angular momentum projection to compute intrinsic spin distributions of projectile-like and target-like fragments after the MNT reaction. The reaction $^{40}$Ca + $^{208}$Pb at laboratory energy 249 MeV was simulated across impact parameters from 4.65 fm to 10 fm, using the relativistic density functional PC-PK1 on a 3D lattice with asymmetric dimensions of $60\times 26\times 60$ and mesh spacing 0.8 fm. Entanglement Shannon entropy (mutual information) was then computed from the spin distributions to quantify correlations between fragment spins and between spin magnitude and its projection.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energy: $E_{\text{lab}} = 249$ MeV for the $^{40}$Ca + $^{208}$Pb reaction.
- Impact parameters studied: from 4.65 fm to 10 fm; fusion occurs for $b < 4.64$ fm, and nucleon transfer becomes negligible for $b \geq 10$ fm.
- At $b = 6$ fm, initial orbital angular momentum $\Lambda = 109.9\ \hbar$; at $b = 5$ fm, $\Lambda = 91.6\ \hbar$; at $b = 4.65$ fm, $\Lambda = 85.2\ \hbar$.
- At $b = 5$ fm, average fragment composition: PLF has $Z \approx 18$, $N \approx 23$; TLF has $Z \approx 84$, $N \approx 123$.
- At $b = 5$ fm, ratio of average spins $S_{\text{PLF}}/S_{\text{TLF}} = 6.73/19.08 = 0.35$, lying between the sticking limit (0.58) and rolling limit (0.07).
- At $b = 4.65$ fm, average fragment composition: PLF has $Z \approx 23$, $N \approx 32$; TLF has $Z \approx 79$, $N \approx 114$.
- Entanglement Shannon entropy $I(S,|K|)$ for the PLF rises to approximately 1.5 at smaller impact parameters, then declines slightly for $b < 6$ fm.
- Mutual information $I(K_{\text{PLF}}, K_{\text{TLF}})$ along both $K$-axis and $y$-axis remains "remarkably small" with negligible dependence on $b$.

## Context

This work extends microscopic studies of spin transfer in near-barrier nuclear reactions, specifically the TD-DFT study by Scamps (2024) which showed that nucleon transfer and neck formation affect spin transfer through tangential friction. The results contrast with fission systems studied by Wilson (2021) and others, where the lighter fragment typically carries higher spin; here the TLF carries larger intrinsic spin than the PLF. The finding of weak inter-fragment spin correlations parallels the experimental result in fission by Wilson (2021) that showed no significant correlation between complementary fragment spins. The approach enables future microscopic studies of angular momentum redistribution in MNT reactions, which are relevant for synthesizing neutron-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei.
