# Understanding Excitations in $^{59,61}$Co, $^{59}$Ni

> High spin states in $^{59}$Co ($Z=27$), $^{59}$Ni ($Z=28$) and $^{61}$Co have been populated by the fusion evaporation reactions, $^{48}$Ti($^{14}$C, p2n)$^{59}$Co, $^{48}$Ti($^{14}$C, 3n)$^{59}$Ni, and $^{50}$Ti($^{14}$C, p2n)$^{61}$Co. The 9 MV tandem accelerator at the John D Fox Laboratory, Florida State University (FSU) was used to accelerate the $^{14}$C beam and the de-exciting $γ$ rays were detected by the FSU detector array consisting of six High Purity Germanium (HPGe) clover detectors, and three single crystals. Directional correlation of the $γ$ rays de-exciting oriented states (DCO ratios) and polarization asymmetry measurements helped to establish spin and parities of the excited states whenever possible. The level scheme of $^{59}$Co has been expanded with the inclusion of positive parity states up to 31/2$^+$ at around 11 MeV. The $^{59}$Ni positive parity states known from previous study were verified with modifications to some of the spins and parities. On the other hand, the negative parity states were extended to 31/2 at an excitation energy of 12 MeV. No new transition was observed for $^{61}$Co, but one of the major bands has been reassigned as consisting of positive parity states by reason of this study which is a candidate for magnetic rotation band. Cross shell excitations were observed in the three nuclei studied and the prominent role of excitation to g$_{9/2}$ orbital crossing the $N=40$ shell gap was established in relation to collective excitation in these nuclei by comparison with large-scale shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper reports that cross-shell excitations into the g9/2 orbital crossing the N=40 shell gap play a prominent role in collective excitations in 59,61Co and 59Ni, but the parity assignments for several states remain tentative where polarization asymmetry could not be measured or statistics were low, and the 61Co level scheme was not extended with new transitions.

## Method

High-spin states in 59Co, 59Ni, and 61Co were populated via fusion-evaporation reactions using a 43 MeV 14C beam from the 9 MV tandem accelerator at Florida State University, with 48Ti and 50Ti targets. Gamma rays were detected using the FSU detector array of six HPGe clover detectors and three single-crystal detectors. Directional correlation of oriented states (DCO ratios) and polarization asymmetry measurements were used to assign spins and parities, and the results were compared with large-scale shell model calculations.

## Key numbers

- 59Co: positive parity states extended up to 31/2+ at around 11 MeV; negative parity states extended to 27/2- at around 9.6 MeV
- 59Co: 22 new transitions observed, resulting in 20 new energy levels; 9 of these new levels are positive parity
- 59Ni: negative parity states extended to 31/2- at an excitation energy of 12 MeV; previously known only to 23/2 at 7954 keV
- 59Ni: positive parity states extended to 17.7 MeV with spin 41/2+; 15 new transitions and 14 new energy levels added
- 61Co: no new transitions observed; one major band reassigned as positive parity states, with the 1808-keV transition confirmed as E1 connecting a 13/2+ state at 3473 keV to an 11/2- state at 1665 keV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of 59Co by Warburton et al. (1970s) which reached a maximum spin of 23/2 at about 7.5 MeV, and of 59Ni by Juutinen et al. (1989) and Yu et al. (more recent), where rotational bands were established up to 43/2. For 61Co, the study builds on the work of Ayangeakaa et al. using Gammasphere, but adds parity information from polarization measurements that was previously unavailable. The results enable testing of large-scale shell model calculations in a valence space including the g9/2 and d5/2 orbitals, and help clarify the onset of collectivity and magnetic rotation in the A≈60 mass region.
