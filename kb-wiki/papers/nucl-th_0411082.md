# Electrodisintegration of $^3$He below and above deuteron breakup threshold

> Recent advances in the study of electrodisintegration of 3He are presented and discussed. The pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonics method is used to calculate the initial and final state wave functions, with a realistic Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The model for the nuclear current and charge operators retains one- and many-body contributions. Particular attention is made in the construction of the two-body current operators arising from the momentum-dependent part of the two-nucleon interaction. Three-body current operators are also included so that the full current operator is strictly conserved. The present model for the nuclear current operator is tested comparing theoretical predictions and experimental data of pd radiative capture cross section and spin observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new model for the nuclear electromagnetic current operator that exactly satisfies the current conservation relation (CCR) with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions, resolving discrepancies in deuteron tensor polarization observables that were present in earlier models where the CCR was only approximately satisfied.

## Method

The authors used the pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonics (PHH) method to calculate bound- and scattering-state wave functions for A=3 systems, with a Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. Two-body current operators were constructed using both the meson-exchange (ME) scheme for momentum-independent parts of the interaction and the minimal-substitution (MS) scheme for momentum-dependent parts, while three-body current operators were included via the ME scheme to ensure exact current conservation. The model was tested by comparing theoretical predictions with experimental data for pd radiative capture cross section and spin observables, as well as for inclusive and exclusive electrodisintegration of 3He.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer values for 3He(e,e') response functions: q = 0.88, 1.64, and 2.47 fm^-1, with excitation energies from two-body threshold up to 20 MeV
- Beam energies for 3He(e,e'd)p reaction: 370 and 576 MeV, with q values of 412, 504, and 604 MeV/c
- Center-of-mass energies for pd radiative capture: E_c.m. = 2 MeV and 3.33 MeV
- Momentum transfer range for validity of charge operators: q < 5 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Viviani et al. (2000) on pd radiative capture and 3He(e,e') reactions below deuteron breakup threshold, which used two-body currents that only approximately satisfied the current conservation relation. The new model resolves discrepancies found in deuteron tensor polarization observables T20 and T21 at E_c.m. = 2 MeV (data from Smith et al., 1999) and 3.33 MeV (data from Goeckner et al., 1992). The calculations also provide the first PHH-method results for the 3He(e,e'd)p reaction above deuteron breakup threshold that include three-nucleon interaction and Coulomb force effects in the final-state interaction, enabling future comparisons with Faddeev calculations that lacked these effects (Spaltro et al., 2002).
