# Fredholm Method for Podolsky Quantum Wave Function

> In this paper we used the Fredholm method in Schroedinger's integral equation in the investigation of the scattering effect near the center of it between a stationary quantum wave function and an electrostatic potential. Two potentials are studied one Coulombian and the other Podolsky. The result shows the importance of the proposal of Podolsky to regularize the effect near the scattering center in the quantum wave function. Being that the coulombian potential presents with strong variation in the amplitude of the wave after the scattering. In the case of Podolsky's potential, this is corrected by adopting a constant that removes this strong variation.

# Fredholm Method for Podolsky Quantum Wave Function

## Key claim

The Fredholm method applied to the Schrödinger integral equation shows that the Podolsky potential, through its constant *a*, removes the strong amplitude variation and undefined behavior at the scattering center (*x* = 0) that appears in the Coulomb potential case, but only for values of *a* greater than zero; for *a* = 0 the Podolsky potential reproduces the Coulomb result.

## Method

The authors solved the stationary Schrödinger equation in integral form using the Fredholm method with arbitrary kernels, constructing the resolvent kernel from Fredholm determinants. They applied this technique to two electrostatic potentials: the Coulomb potential (V = Q²/4πr) and the Podolsky generalized potential (V = (Q²/4π)(1 − e⁻ʳ/ᵃ)/r), computing the scattered wave function ψ(x) for each case. Numerical evaluation was performed with m = 2, E = 1/4, ħ = 1, and Q = 1, giving λ = −1 and k = 1.

## Key numbers

- For the Coulomb potential, the wave function shows undefined behavior at x = 0 with strong amplitude variation between x = 0 and x = 5.
- For the Podolsky potential with constant a = 1, the wave function is close to the Coulomb result; with a = 5, the wave function stabilizes with amplitude tending to values between −1 and 1 before x = 5.
- For values of the Podolsky constant a > 40, the singularity problem in the wave function at x = 0 is eliminated.
- The Podolsky constant a has dimension of length; the potential at the origin is finite: lim_{r→0} V(r) = Q²/(4πa).

## Context

This work extends the Podolsky generalization of Maxwell electrodynamics (Podolsky, 1942, 1944, 1948) to quantum scattering problems, where the standard Coulomb potential produces a singularity at the scattering center. The Fredholm integral equation method (Fredholm, 1900) is used to solve the Schrödinger equation, following the Green function approach of Sakurai. The results enable further investigation of regularized quantum scattering near the interaction center, particularly for potentials that remove the endpoint singularity.
