# Coupled-channels density-matrix approach to low-energy nuclear collision dynamics: A technique for quantifying quantum decoherence effects on reaction observables

> The coupled-channels density-matrix technique for nuclear reaction dynamics, which is based on the Liouville-von Neumann equation with Lindblad dissipative terms, is developed with the inclusion of full angular momentum couplings. It allows a quantitative study of the role and importance of quantum decoherence in nuclear scattering. Formulae of asymptotic observables that can reveal effects of quantum decoherence are given. A method for extracting energy-resolved scattering information from the time-dependent density matrix is introduced. As an example, model calculations are carried out for the low-energy collision of the $^{16}$O projectile on the $^{154}$Sm target.

# Coupled-channels density-matrix approach to low-energy nuclear collision dynamics: A technique for quantifying quantum decoherence effects on reaction observables

## Key claim
Quantum decoherence and dissipation should be simultaneously included in a consistent description of low-energy reaction dynamics when a restricted set of relevant degrees of freedom is employed; the coupled-channels density-matrix (CCDM) approach can quantify these effects, but the model calculations presented are qualitative only, limited by computational capability to a smaller grid (r=0-40 fm with 64 radial points) than would be needed for quantitative predictions.

## Method
The approach solves the Liouville-von Neumann equation with Lindblad dissipative terms for the time-dependent density matrix of the reduced system (relative motion plus a few low-lying collective states of the target), including full angular momentum couplings. The relative motion is treated quantum-mechanically using a wave-packet on a discrete radial grid, while the environment (e.g., fusion or surface processes) is represented by Lindblad operators with radial-dependent decay rates. Energy-resolved scattering information is extracted from the time-dependent density matrix using a window operator technique that yields reflection coefficients via the ratio of final to initial energy spectra.

## Key numbers
- The s-wave Coulomb barrier for the 16O + 154Sm system is V_B = 59.41 MeV at radius R_B = 10.81 fm, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential with (V_0, r_0, a_0) = (-165 MeV, 0.95 fm, 1.05 fm)
- The 154Sm target excitation energies are E_{2+} = 0.08 MeV and E_{4+} = 0.27 MeV, with deformation parameters beta_2 = 0.322 and beta_4 = 0.027
- The fusion environment decay function is a Fermi function with depth 10 MeV and diffuseness 0.1 fm, located at the pocket radius of 5.77 fm
- The surface environment decay function is a Gaussian with width 1 fm, centered at the contact radius estimated as 1.2(16^{1/3} + 154^{1/3}) fm
- Relative partial waves up to 20 hbar are included in the calculations
- The time step for density-matrix propagation was Delta t = 10^{-22} s
- Asymptotic populations with only fusion environment: 0+ = 0.8376, 2+ = 0.1206, 4+ = 0.0418, fusion probability = 3.127E-3
- Asymptotic populations with both fusion and surface environments: 0+ = 0.8770, 2+ = 0.0961, 4+ = 0.0268, fusion probability = 9.106E-4

## Context
This work extends the CCDM approach previously applied to heavy-ion fusion (Diaz-Torres 2008, 2009) by including full angular momentum couplings and a method for extracting energy-resolved scattering information. It addresses the inability of standard stationary-state coupled-channels models (e.g., the CCFULL code of Hagino et al.) to simultaneously describe elastic scattering, quasi-elastic scattering, and fusion measurements (Newton 2001, Nanda 2007), as well as the phenomenological adjustments required to fit precision fusion data (Rae, Esbensen, Hagino). The approach enables future quantitative tests against high-precision measurements of fusion and scattering observables, potentially resolving the long-standing inconsistency between below-barrier quantum tunneling and above-barrier fusion yields within the standard coupled-channels framework.
