# The Specific Heat of Normal, Degenerate Quark Matter: Non-Fermi Liquid Corrections

> In normal degenerate quark matter, the exchange of dynamically screened transverse gluons introduces infrared divergences in the quark self-energies that lead to the breakdown of the Fermi liquid description. If the core of neutron stars are composed of quark matter with a normal component, cooling by direct quark Urca processes may be modified by non-Fermi liquid corrections. We find that while the quasiparticle density of states is finite and non-zero at the Fermi surface, its frequency derivative diverges and results in non-Fermi liquid corrections to the specific heat of the normal, degenerate component of quark matter. We study these non-perturbative non-Fermi liquid corrections to the specific heat and the temperature dependence of the chemical potential and show that these lead to a reduction of the specific heat.

# The Specific Heat of Normal, Degenerate Quark Matter: Non-Fermi Liquid Corrections

## Key claim

While the quasiparticle density of states remains finite and non-zero at the Fermi surface, its frequency derivative diverges, leading to non-Fermi liquid corrections that reduce the specific heat of normal degenerate quark matter; however, these corrections are only a few percent for the temperatures and chemical potentials relevant to young neutron stars, and the reliability of the calculation is questionable for protoneutron stars where corrections could reach 10-15 percent.

## Method

The authors compute the specific heat of normal degenerate quark matter by obtaining the internal energy as the expectation value of the quark Hamiltonian including interactions with gluons, expressed via the spectral density of the quark field. They use a renormalization group resummation of leading logarithmic infrared divergences from dynamically screened transverse gluon exchange, previously developed at T=0 by Boyanovsky and de Vega (2000), to obtain the improved quasiparticle spectral density near the Fermi surface, then evaluate the single quasiparticle density of states and the temperature dependence of the chemical potential to extract the specific heat at constant volume and baryon number.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range for neutron star phenomena: T ≈ 0.1-1 MeV; quark chemical potential: μ_q ≈ 0.3-0.5 GeV
- For μ(0) = 0.5 GeV, the effective coupling λ = 0.152; for μ(0) = 1 GeV, λ = 0.046
- The non-Fermi liquid corrections to the specific heat are of order a few percent for young neutron stars (T ~ 0.1-1 MeV, μ ~ 300-500 MeV)
- For protoneutron stars with T ~ 30-50 MeV and smaller chemical potentials, corrections could be of order 10-15 percent
- The specific heat per unit volume is given by C_v = (μ²(0) T)/3 multiplied by a series in anomalous powers (T/μ(0))² (T/M(0))^{-2λ} and (T/μ(0))² (T/M(0))^{-4λ}, where λ = (α_s/6π) (N_c² - 1)/(2N_c)

## Context

This work extends the non-Fermi liquid analysis of QCD near the Fermi surface by Boyanovsky and de Vega (2000) to finite temperature, applying it to the cooling of neutron stars whose cores may contain normal (non-superconducting) quark matter. It contrasts with the earlier calculation by Iwamoto (1982) of neutrino emissivity via direct quark Urca processes, which assumed Fermi liquid theory, and with the work by Norton (1973) on infrared divergences in the non-relativistic electron gas, which neglected the temperature derivative of the chemical potential and the cancellation between the vanishing quasiparticle residue and group velocity. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutron star cooling histories, particularly for scenarios where color superconductivity leaves a normal quark component, as in the 2SC phase where one color remains ungapped.
