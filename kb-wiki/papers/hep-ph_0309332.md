# Energy Loss of Leading Partons in a Thermal QCD Medium

> We consider bremsstrahlung energy loss for hard partons traversing a quark-gluon plasma. Accounting correctly for the probabilistic nature of the energy loss, and making a leading-order accurate treatment of bremsstrahlung, we find that the suppression of the spectrum is nearly flat, with the most suppression at energies $E \sim 30 T$ ($T$ the QGP temperature), in contrast to previous literature but in agreement with experimental data. This flat pattern should also be observed at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the flat suppression pattern of high-\(p_T\) hadrons observed at RHIC can be explained by LPM-suppressed bremsstrahlung energy loss alone, provided one correctly treats the probabilistic nature of energy loss and does not assume the LPM effect is parametrically large. The authors find that the suppression is most pronounced at energies \(E \sim 30 T\) and predict a similarly flat pattern at the LHC, contradicting earlier claims that additional effects like the Cronin effect and shadowing are necessary.

## Method

The authors compute the bremsstrahlung rate for hard partons in a static thermal QCD medium using the AMY formalism, which treats the LPM effect correctly at all gluon energies down to \(E_{\rm gluon} > g_s T\). They then evolve the momentum distributions of quarks and gluons via coupled Fokker-Planck equations that account for both radiation and absorption, solving the integral equation for the non-collinearity function \(\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{h},p,k)\) by an impact-parameter space method. The initial parton spectrum is taken from a parameterization fitted to \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV \(p\bar p\) data.

## Key numbers

- The LPM suppression factor (actual rate over Bethe-Heitler rate) is only \(1/2\) at \(E_{\rm gluon}=10 T\); for less energetic gluons the LPM suppression is small.
- The ratio of high-\(p_T\) events to the number expected from proton-proton data is \(R \simeq 1/5\) at RHIC.
- The initial parton spectrum is parameterized as \(dN/d^2 p_T \approx C/(p_0^2 + p_T^2)^5\) with \(p_0 \sim 1.75\) GeV, fitted to \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV data.
- The suppression ratio \(R\) reaches a minimum around \(E \sim 30 T\) and then rises slowly; at very large \(p_T\) (a few hundred \(T\)) \(R\) approaches 1.
- With a plasma temperature of about 0.4 GeV, the energy range shown corresponds to about 6 GeV to 20 GeV, with a time range of 0.5 fm to 1.5 fm.
- The authors predict that even at \(p_T = 25\) GeV at the LHC, the suppression pattern will be nearly flat.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of jet quenching via bremsstrahlung energy loss by Baier et al. (2001) and Gyulassy et al. (2000, 2001), who had found that the LPM effect leads to a rising ratio \(R\) with \(p_T\), in tension with RHIC data. The authors show that the discrepancy is largely due to the common approximation of using a mean energy loss \(dE/dt\) rather than evolving the full distribution, and to assuming the LPM effect is parametrically large. The results contradict the claim by Gyulassy and Vitev (2002) that the Cronin effect and nuclear shadowing are essential to explain the flat \(R\) at RHIC, and that these effects would be small at the LHC, leading to a rising \(R\). The present work enables a more quantitative prediction once nuclear geometry and medium expansion are included.
