# Thermal model for RHIC, part I: particle ratios and spectra

> A simple thermal model with single freeze-out and flow is used to analyze the ratios of hadron yields and the hadron transverse-mass spectra measured in 130 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. An overall very good agreement between the model predictions and the data is achieved for all measured hadron species, including hyperons.

## Key claim

A single-freeze-out thermal model with Hubble-like transverse flow can simultaneously describe both hadron yield ratios and transverse-mass spectra at RHIC, provided that resonance decays are fully included; the model's success suggests that the chemical and kinetic freeze-out temperatures are equal, with no need for a separate elastic rescattering phase.

## Method

The authors fit the temperature \(T\) and baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) to nine preliminary hadron yield ratios using a \(\chi^2\) minimization, with \(\mu_S\) and \(\mu_I\) fixed by strangeness neutrality and the baryon-to-charge ratio of the colliding nuclei. They then fit the freeze-out proper time \(\tau\) and maximum transverse radius \(\rho_{\rm max}\) to the transverse-mass spectra of pions, kaons, and antiprotons. All hadronic resonances are included in both the yield and spectrum calculations, with decays treated via semi-analytic formulas using tabulated branching ratios and constant matrix elements for three-body decays.

## Key numbers

* Temperature from the fit to the wider set of hadron ratios: \(T = 168 \pm 5\) MeV.
* Baryon chemical potential from the same fit: \(\mu_B = 41 \pm 4\) MeV.
* Freeze-out proper time for most central events: \(\tau = 7.66\) fm.
* Maximum transverse radius for most central events: \(\rho_{\rm max} = 6.69\) fm.
* Model prediction for \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio: 1.02, compared to experimental values \(1.00 \pm 0.02\) (PHOBOS) and \(0.99 \pm 0.02\) (BRAHMS).
* Model prediction for \(\overline{p}/p\) ratio: 0.65, compared to experimental values \(0.61 \pm 0.07\) (STAR), \(0.64 \pm 0.08\) (PHENIX), \(0.60 \pm 0.07\) (PHOBOS), and \(0.61 \pm 0.06\) (BRAHMS).
* Model prediction for \(\phi/h^{-}\) ratio: 0.020, compared to experimental value \(0.021 \pm 0.001\) (STAR).
* Model prediction for \(\Omega^{-}/h^{-}\) ratio: 0.0011, compared to experimental value \(0.0012 \pm 0.005\) (STAR).
* Resonance decays produce about three-quarters of the observed pions at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the thermal-model approach previously applied by the same authors to lower energies (Florkowski and Broniowski, 2002) and by other groups such as Becattini (2000) and Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001). The extracted freeze-out temperature of 168 MeV is consistent with the QCD lattice deconfinement temperature range of 154–173 MeV (Karsch, 2002), supporting a hadronization scenario. The model's success in describing the \(\phi\) meson spectrum, which receives no resonance-decay contributions, and the \(\Omega\) hyperon spectrum, which is most sensitive to canonical-ensemble corrections, enables future work on elliptic flow and HBT radii presented in the companion paper (Broniowski and Florkowski, 2002).
