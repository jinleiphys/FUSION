# Correlations and realistic interactions in doubly closed shell nuclei

> We review the latest variational calculations of the ground state properties of doubly closed shell nuclei, from $^{12}$C to $^{208}$Pb, with semirealistic and realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions. The studies are carried on within the framework of the correlated basis function theory and integral equations technique, with state dependent correlations having central and tensor components. We report results for the ground state energy, one- and two-body densities and static structure functions. For $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca we use modern interactions and find that the accuracy of the method is comparable to that attained in nuclear matter with similar hamiltonians, giving nuclei underbound by $\sim$2 MeV/A. The computed Coulomb sums are in complete agreement with the latest analysis of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that variational calculations using correlated basis function theory and Fermi hypernetted chain/single operator chain techniques can describe doubly closed shell nuclei with an accuracy comparable to that achieved in nuclear matter, giving nuclei underbound by approximately 2 MeV/A when using modern realistic interactions.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations of ground state properties for doubly closed shell nuclei from 12C to 208Pb using correlated basis function theory with state-dependent correlation operators having central and tensor components. They employed cluster expansions and Fermi hypernetted chain integral equations with the single operator chain approximation to sum the relevant diagrams, and used parametrized correlation functions fixed by minimizing the ground state energy. For 16O and 40Ca, they used the Argonne v14 plus Urbana VII and Argonne v8' plus Urbana IX realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies per nucleon for 16O and 40Ca with Argonne v14 + Urbana VII and Argonne v8' + Urbana IX interactions are reported, with nuclei underbound by approximately 2 MeV/A
- Nuclear matter saturation density is given as 0.16 fm^-3
- The FHNC/SOC approximation accuracy is estimated at approximately 1 MeV/A in nuclear matter at saturation density
- The SOC approximation produces discrepancies of approximately 9% in the worst case for density sum rule normalizations
- Explicit three-body correlations were found to provide approximately 0.85 MeV/A extra binding in the Argonne v14 + Urbana VII case for 16O

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis function and Fermi hypernetted chain approach, previously developed for nuclear matter, to finite doubly closed shell nuclei. It builds upon earlier calculations using semirealistic interactions like the Afnan and Tang S3 potential and its modified version, and compares results with cluster Monte Carlo calculations (CMC) and Coupled Cluster methods (CCM) for 16O. The computed Coulomb sums show complete agreement with experimental data from inclusive quasi-elastic electron scattering experiments on 12C, 40Ca, and 56Fe, as analyzed by Jourdan (1996). This enables future microscopic studies of dynamical quantities such as nuclear cross sections and extension to other systems like hypernuclei.
