# Model study of hot and dense baryonic matter

> The properties of baryonic matter have been investigated at finite density and temperature using different models. The variation of baryon masses and fractional number densities with baryon density and temperature obtained from different models have been compared. The quark hadron phase transition have been studied using Chiral Colour Dieletric (CCD) model in the quark sector. No phase transition has been seen for the different variants of the Zimanyi-Moszkowski model. However, a phase transition is observed for the linear and non-linear Walecka model.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a quark-hadron phase transition occurs in hot and dense baryonic matter using five different hadronic models paired with the Chiral Colour Dielectric (CCD) model for the quark sector; a phase transition is observed only for the linear Walecka (LW) and non-linear Walecka (NW) models, and only at temperatures of 0 MeV and 50 MeV, while none of the three variants of the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model produce a transition for the parameter set used.

## Method

The authors extended the linear Walecka model and three variants of the Zimanyi-Moszkowski model (ZM, ZM2, ZM3) to include hyperons (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻), ρ-meson, and leptons (electrons and muons) in β-equilibrium, and also studied the non-linear Walecka model with cubic and quartic σ self-interactions. All hadronic models were treated in the mean-field approximation to compute baryon effective masses, fractional number densities, and pressure as functions of baryon density and temperature. The quark matter equation of state was calculated from the Chiral Colour Dielectric (CCD) model, with quark-gluon interactions included up to two loops in the thermodynamic potential, and the hadron-quark phase transition was determined using Gibbs' criterion (equality of pressure at fixed baryon chemical potential).

## Key numbers

- The linear Walecka model yields incompressibility K = 524 MeV, compared to the accepted value K = 210 ± 30 MeV.
- The ZM model yields incompressibility K = 224.49 MeV and nucleon effective mass M_N* = 797.64 MeV at nuclear matter saturation density ρ = 0.15 fm⁻³.
- For the LW model at T = 0 MeV, the quark-hadron phase transition occurs at μ_B = 1100 MeV, corresponding to critical density ρ_c = 0.27 fm⁻³; at T = 50 MeV, ρ_c = 0.26 fm⁻³.
- For the NW model, the critical densities are ρ_c = 0.47 fm⁻³ at T = 0 MeV and ρ_c = 0.37 fm⁻³ at T = 50 MeV.
- In the LW model, the nucleon mass goes to zero at about 0.8 fm⁻³; in the other models, the nucleon mass saturates in the range 400–600 MeV.
- In the LW model at T = 0 MeV, the transition from nuclear to hyperonic matter occurs at n_B = 0.4 fm⁻³; in the ZM model it occurs at n_B = 1.1 fm⁻³; in the ZM2 model at n_B = 1.6 fm⁻³; in the NW model at n_B = 0.85 fm⁻³; the ZM3 model never becomes hyperonic.
- The CCD model parameters used are: m_q(u,d) = 92 MeV, m_q(s) = 295 MeV, B^{1/4} = 152 MeV, α = 36, α_s = 0.08.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear matter at finite temperature and density, which were mostly restricted to zero temperature (e.g., Glendenning, Heiselberg et al.), by including hyperon degrees of freedom and comparing five hadronic models systematically. The finding that the ZM model variants do not yield a quark-hadron phase transition for the chosen parameter set implies that, if these models are correct, quark matter may not appear inside neutron stars, in contrast to predictions from the Walecka models. The results enable further calculations of neutron star properties and neutrino emissivities, and highlight the model dependence of the phase transition.
