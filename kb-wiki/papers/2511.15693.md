# Effects of short-range correlations at high densities on neutron stars with and without DM content: role of the repulsive self-interaction

> In this work, we investigate how short-range correlations affect relativistic hadronic models at high densities, with direct consequences for the structure of neutron stars, both with and without dark matter content. Two versions of the model are examined: one with vector self-interactions up to second order ($ω_0^2$) and another including a fourth-order term ($ω_0^4$). We show that SRC tend to soften the equation of state when only the quadratic term is present, but produce a noticeable stiffening once the $ω_0^4$ term is included. The corresponding Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff solutions for pure neutron stars indicate that short-range correlations reduce the maximum mass in the first case but increase it in the second. Extending the analysis to stars containing a fermionic dark matter component, within the two-fluid formalism, we verify that the same features appear in the respective mass-radius diagrams. In particular, the decrease of the maximum mass with increasing dark matter fraction is partly compensated by the SRC effects in the hadronic sector for the model with the fourth-order term. In all cases, the resulting parametrizations are consistent with recent astrophysical constraints, including the joint NICER-XMM-Newton analyses of the pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as the gravitational-wave event GW190425.

# Effects of short-range correlations at high densities on neutron stars with and without DM content: role of the repulsive self-interaction

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) soften the equation of state (EoS) of hadronic matter when only quadratic vector self-interactions ($\omega_0^2$) are present, but produce a noticeable stiffening once a fourth-order term ($\omega_0^4$) is included, with this behavior persisting in neutron stars containing a fermionic dark matter component within the two-fluid formalism.

## Method

The authors implement SRC in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models by replacing the standard step-function nucleon momentum distribution with one containing a high-momentum tail proportional to $1/k^4$ above the Fermi surface, using parameters $C_0 = 0.161$, $C_1 = -0.25$, $\phi_0 = 2.38$, and $\phi_1 = -0.56$ that reproduce a 28% high-momentum tail contribution in symmetric nuclear matter. They examine two versions of the hadronic Lagrangian: one with vector self-interactions up to second order ($C=0$) and another including a fourth-order term ($C \neq 0$). For stars with dark matter, they adopt a two-fluid formalism where the visible and dark sectors interact solely through gravity, with the dark matter modeled as a fermionic relativistic Fermi gas. All parametrizations are calibrated to reproduce key nuclear empirical parameters from the NL3* parametrization and are tested against the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff solutions for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- For the $C=0$ models (NL3*/NL3*-SRC): maximum mass decreases from $2.76 M_\odot$ to $2.73 M_\odot$ when SRC are included
- For the $C=0$ models (NLM/NLM-SRC): maximum mass decreases from $2.27 M_\odot$ to $2.21 M_\odot$ when SRC are included
- For the $C=0$ models (GM3/GM3-SRC): maximum mass decreases from $2.02 M_\odot$ to $1.95 M_\odot$ when SRC are included
- For the $C \neq 0$ models (PK1/PK1-SRC): maximum mass increases from $2.32 M_\odot$ to $2.41 M_\odot$ when SRC are included
- For the $C \neq 0$ models (TMA/TMA-SRC): maximum mass increases from $1.99 M_\odot$ to $2.14 M_\odot$ when SRC are included
- For the $C \neq 0$ models (MB/MB-SRC): maximum mass increases from $1.55 M_\odot$ to $1.76 M_\odot$ when SRC are included
- The high-momentum tail contributes approximately 28% in symmetric nuclear matter and approximately 1.5% in pure neutron matter
- The kinetic contribution to symmetry energy becomes negative in the SRC model, with values ranging from $-13.45$ MeV to $-16.85$ MeV depending on saturation density and effective mass, compared to positive values of $13.68$ MeV to $18.62$ MeV without SRC

## Context

This work extends the analysis of SRC effects in RMF models previously developed by Cai and collaborators (2020) and builds on the nonlinear RMF framework of Muller and Serot (1996), who showed that the quartic $\omega^4$ term arises naturally in effective field theory and significantly affects neutron star mass predictions. The study incorporates the two-fluid formalism for dark matter in neutron stars as used by Goldman and Nussinov (1989), Sandin and Ciarcelluti (2009), and Tolos and collaborators (2015). The results are tested against astrophysical constraints from the NICER and XMM-Newton analyses of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as the gravitational-wave event GW190425. The findings enable future investigations into how SRC effects can partially compensate for the softening introduced by dark matter in neutron star models, particularly when fourth-order vector self-interactions are present.
