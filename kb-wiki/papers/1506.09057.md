# Symmetry energy systematics and its high density behavior

> We explore the systematics of the density dependence of nuclear matter symmetry energy in the ambit of microscopic calculations with various energy density functionals, and find that the symmetry energy from subsaturation density to supra-saturation density can be well determined by three characteristic parameters of the symmetry energy at saturation density $ρ_0 $, i.e., the magnitude $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_0 })$, the density slope $L$ and the density curvature $K_{\text{sym}}$. This finding opens a new window to constrain the supra-saturation density behavior of the symmetry energy from its (sub-)saturation density behavior. In particular, we obtain $L=46.7 \pm 12.8$ MeV and $K_{\text{sym}}=-166.9 \pm 168.3$ MeV as well as $E_{\text{sym}}({2ρ_{0}}) \approx 40.2 \pm 12.8$ MeV and $L({2ρ_{0}}) \approx 8.9 \pm 108.7$ MeV based on the present knowledge of $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_{0}}) = 32.5 \pm 0.5$ MeV, $E_{\text{sym}}({ρ_c}) = 26.65 \pm 0.2$ MeV and $L({ρ_c}) = 46.0 \pm 4.5$ MeV at $ρ_{\rm{c}}= 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$ extracted from nuclear mass and the neutron skin thickness of Sn isotopes. Our results indicate that the symmetry energy cannot be stiffer than a linear density dependence.In addition, we also discuss the quark matter symmetry energy since the deconfined quarks could be the right degree of freedom in dense matter at high baryon densities.

# Symmetry energy systematics and its high density behavior

## Key claim
The symmetry energy from subsaturation to supra-saturation density can be well determined by just three characteristic parameters at saturation density (magnitude, slope, and curvature), provided the systematics derived from 60 energy density functionals hold. The claim is hedged by the fact that the systematics are established only for the specific set of interactions considered and may not be universal; the author notes the relations are approximate and depend on the density region.

## Method
The author selected 60 well-calibrated interactions across Skyrme, Gogny, relativistic mean-field (RMF), density-dependent RMF, point-coupling RMF, and relativistic Hartree-Fock functionals. For each interaction, the symmetry energy magnitude E_sym(ρ) and its density slope L(ρ) were computed at various densities and compared to the second-order Taylor expansion around saturation density using the parameters E_sym(ρ₀), L, and K_sym. Pearson linear correlation coefficients were calculated to quantify the strength of the linear relations between the exact values and the expansion approximations.

## Key numbers
- L = 46.7 ± 12.8 MeV and K_sym = -166.9 ± 168.3 MeV, derived from E_sym(ρ₀) = 32.5 ± 0.5 MeV, E_sym(ρ_c) = 26.65 ± 0.2 MeV, and L(ρ_c) = 46.0 ± 4.5 MeV at ρ_c = 0.11 fm⁻³
- E_sym(2ρ₀) ≈ 40.2 ± 12.8 MeV and L(2ρ₀) ≈ 8.9 ± 108.7 MeV, obtained from the same input constraints
- Pearson linear correlation coefficient r > 0.98 for E_sym(ρ) vs E*_sym(ρ) at ρ = 0.5ρ₀, 2ρ₀, 2.5ρ₀, and 3ρ₀ across all 60 interactions
- Pearson linear correlation coefficient r > 0.93 for L(ρ) vs L*(ρ) at the same four densities
- The systematics for E_sym(ρ) are safely applicable from ρ₀/5 to 3ρ₀ (r > 0.96), and for L(ρ) from ρ₀/2 to 2.5ρ₀ (r > 0.96)

## Context
This work extends the correlation analysis within Skyrme-Hartree-Fock by Chen (2011) to a much larger set of 60 interactions spanning multiple functional families, demonstrating that the curvature parameter K_sym can determine supra-saturation behavior. The results contradict the stiffer symmetry energy dependencies favored by some transport model analyses of π⁻/π⁺ ratios (Xiao 2009, Feng 2010) and n/p elliptic flows (Russo 2011, Cozma 2013), which are mutually contradictory. The extracted values agree with the variational many-body theory calculation using the WFF1 interaction (Wiringa 1988) that describes neutron stars with radius 9.1⁺¹·³₋₁·₅ km (Guillot 2013). The work enables future constraints on supra-saturation symmetry energy from improved measurements of neutron skin thickness and nuclear masses, and opens the question of whether quark matter symmetry energy (discussed in the context of the CIDDM model by Chu 2014) follows similar systematics.
