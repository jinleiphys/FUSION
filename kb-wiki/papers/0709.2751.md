# Instabilities of relativistic mean field models and the role of nonlinear terms

> The instability of nuclear matter due to particle-hole excitation modes has been studied in the frame-work of several relativistic mean field (RMF) models. It is found that both the longitudinal and the transversal modes depend sensitively on the parameter sets used. The important impact of the vector and vector-scalar nonlinear terms on the stability of both modes is demonstrated. Our finding corroborates the result of previous studies, namely that certain RMF models cannot be used in high density applications. However, we show that for certain parameter sets of RMF models this shortcoming can be alleviated by adding these nonlinear terms.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that adding vector and vector-scalar nonlinear terms to relativistic mean field (RMF) models can alleviate the high-density instabilities caused by particle-hole excitations, but only for certain parameter sets; the improvement is not universal and depends sensitively on the specific values of the nonlinear coupling parameters.

## Method

The authors calculated the longitudinal and transversal dielectric functions for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) using the random phase approximation with zero frequency (q0=0) particle-hole excitations. They compared three classes of RMF models: standard RMF (S-RMF) with only scalar nonlinearities, vector-extended RMF (V-RMF) with quartic omega meson self-interactions, and extended RMF (E-RMF) with scalar-vector coupling terms. The instability regimes were identified as regions where the dielectric functions become negative, and the analysis was extended to asymmetric nuclear matter for selected parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- NL-Z parameter set: regime II instability in longitudinal mode starts at critical density ρc ∼ 2.5 ρ0 with qc ≤ 2.5 fm
- NL3 parameter set: regime II instability in longitudinal mode starts at ρc ∼ 6.5 ρ0 with qc ≤ 2.5 fm
- TM1 parameter set: longitudinal instability starts at ρc ∼ 10 ρ0 with qc ∼ 5 fm
- PK1 parameter set: longitudinal instability starts at ρc ∼ 5 ρ0 with qc ∼ 3.5 fm
- G2 parameter set: longitudinal instability starts at ρc ∼ 22 ρ0 with qc ∼ 6.5 fm
- G1 parameter set: longitudinal instability starts at ρc ∼ 3 ρ0 with qc ∼ 3 fm
- For transversal modes: PK1 shows instability at ρc ∼ 5 ρ0 with qc ∼ 3 fm; G2 shows instability at ρc ∼ 8 ρ0 with qc ∼ 4 fm
- TM1 and G1 parameter sets show no unstable regimes in the transversal mode

## Context

This work extends the earlier instability analysis of Piekarewicz (2004) (Ref. PGR2 in the paper), who studied S-RMF models and found that parameterizations with low nucleon effective mass (m*/m ∼ 0.5-0.6) produce critical instabilities in longitudinal modes. The present study shows that V-RMF and E-RMF models (such as TM1 from Toki (1995) and G2 from Furnstahl (1996)) can shift instabilities to physically less important regimes (higher densities and momenta) without sacrificing accuracy in finite nuclei ground-state properties. The results enable more reliable applications of RMF models to high-density astrophysical systems such as neutron stars, where the longitudinal mode sensitivity to the isovector sector can be related to observables like proton fraction and symmetry energy.
