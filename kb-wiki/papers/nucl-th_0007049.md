# Hanbury Brown-Ttiss Correlation Functions from Event Generators: A Reliable Approach to Determine the Size of the Emitting Source in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions?

> Employing NEXUS, one of the most recent simulation programs for heavy ions collisions, we investigate in detail the Hanbury-Twiss correlation function for charged pions for reactions 158 GeV Pb+Pb. For this study we supplement the standard simulation program by electromagnetic interactions. We find that the string fragmentation introduces strong space-momentum correlations and the freeze out times of the correlated pairs have a wide distribution. Both has a strong influence on the apparent source radius determined from the measured correlation correlation function.An equally strong influence is observed for the final state electromagnetic interaction.

## Key claim

The apparent source radius R extracted from HBT correlation functions using a Gaussian fit differs from the true rms radius of the emission points by up to 30%, and the true source density can be overpredicted by up to a factor of two, because string fragmentation introduces strong space-momentum correlations and a wide distribution of freeze-out times that complicate the relation between the measured correlation function and the actual source geometry.

## Method

The authors used the NEXUS event generator (version 2.0 beta) to simulate central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV with impact parameter b ≤ 3.2 fm, supplementing it with classical electromagnetic interactions between all charged particles via the Lorentz force equation. They calculated the two-pion correlation function for charged pions at midrapidity (y_cm - 1 ≤ y ≤ y_cm + 1) by constructing Gaussian wave packets around the classical freeze-out positions and momenta, then fitting the resulting correlation function with the standard Gaussian form C(k) = 1 + λ exp(-4k²R²). They compared results obtained with and without the smoothness approximation, and separately analyzed the effects of space-momentum correlations and Coulomb interactions.

## Key numbers

- The mean source size for correlated pairs (relative momentum < 100 MeV) is 11.3 fm for all pions and 12.4 fm when excluding pions from resonance decay.
- The average relative distance at freeze-out for correlated pairs is √⟨r²(t₂)⟩ = 10.8 fm, compared to 15.6 fm for all pion pairs.
- The apparent source radius R and the true rms radius of emission points differ by up to 30%.
- The true source density can be overpredicted by up to a factor of two if the apparent source radius is taken as the true source size.
- The average change in relative momentum of correlated pairs due to Coulomb interactions after freeze-out is approximately 25 MeV.
- The freeze-out time distribution is almost flat between 3 and 10 fm/c, with transverse distances extending up to 18 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier HBT studies using event generators such as Fritjof by Csörgő et al. and Pratt et al., which assumed the Gamow correction factor adequately accounts for Coulomb effects and that the classical phase space density can be identified with the Wigner density. The authors show that both assumptions are problematic: the Gamow factor overpredicts true Coulomb effects (as previously noted by G. Baym and P. Braun-Munzinger), and the space-momentum correlations inherent in string fragmentation (already present in VENUS and NEXUS) prevent a simple extraction of the source radius. The results enable more careful interpretation of HBT measurements at SPS energies and provide guidance for future analyses at RHIC and LHC, though the authors note that the predictive power of event generators for two-body correlations is limited by the need to introduce the wave packet width L as a free parameter.
