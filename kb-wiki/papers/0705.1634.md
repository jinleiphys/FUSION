# Relativistic BCS-BEC Crossover at Finite Temperature and Its Application to Color Superconductivity

> The non-relativistic $G_0 G$ formalism of BCS-BEC crossover at finite temperature is extended to relativistic fermion systems. The uncondensed pairs contribute a pseudogap to the fermion excitations. The theory recovers the BCS mean field approximation at zero temperature and the non-relativistic results in a proper limit. For massive fermions, when the coupling strength increases, there exist two crossovers from the weak coupling BCS superfluid to the non-relativistic BEC state and then to the relativistic BEC state. For color superconductivity at moderate baryon density, the matter is in the BCS-BEC crossover region, and the behavior of the pseudogap is quite similar to that found in high temperature superconductors.

## Key claim

The paper extends the non-relativistic $G_0 G$ formalism (which includes uncondensed pairs as a pseudogap) to relativistic fermion systems, claiming that for massive fermions there exist two distinct crossovers as coupling increases: first from BCS superfluid to non-relativistic BEC, then to relativistic BEC. The authors hedge that the $G_0 G$ approximation is strictly valid only in the symmetry-breaking phase ($T \leq T_c$), and that above $T_c$ a non-vanishing pair chemical potential must be introduced.

## Method

The authors generalize the non-relativistic $G_0 G$ (asymmetric pair approximation) T-matrix approach to relativistic Dirac fermions with a scalar attractive interaction, using the Nambu-Gorkov formalism. They construct coupled self-consistent equations for the superfluid order parameter $\Delta_\text{sc}$, the pseudogap $\Delta_\text{pg}$ (from uncondensed pairs), and the chemical potential $\mu$, with the total gap $\Delta^2 = \Delta_\text{sc}^2 + \Delta_\text{pg}^2$ entering the fermion quasiparticle energies. The pair susceptibility is expanded around zero momentum to obtain a tractable form for the pseudogap equation, and the theory is regularized via a non-relativistic renormalization relating the bare coupling to an s-wave scattering length.

## Key numbers

- The quark energy gap at moderate baryon density in effective QCD models is about $100$ MeV, which is already of the order of the Fermi energy.
- In the weak coupling BCS region, the critical temperature satisfies $T_c \simeq 0.57 \Delta_0$, where $\Delta_0$ is the zero-temperature gap.
- In the non-relativistic limit with $k_f \ll m$, the critical temperature is given by $T_c = \frac{8e^{\gamma-2}}{\pi} \epsilon_f e^{2\eta/\pi}$, where $\epsilon_f = k_f^2/(2m)$ is the Fermi kinetic energy and $\eta = 1/(k_f a_s)$.
- In the non-relativistic BEC region, the critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation is $T_c = \frac{2\pi}{m_\text{B}} \left( \frac{n_\text{B}}{\zeta(3/2)} \right)^{2/3}$, and in the non-relativistic limit $k_f \ll m$ this becomes $T_c = 0.218 \epsilon_f$.
- At low temperature in the NBEC region, the pseudogap scales as $\Delta_\text{pg} \propto T^{3/4}$, while in the RBEC region it scales as $\Delta_\text{pg} \propto T$.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic $G_0 G$ formalism developed for atomic Fermi gases (BCSBEC6, BCSBEC7, G0G) to relativistic fermion systems, building on prior studies of relativistic BCS-BEC crossover using the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) theory above $T_c$ (RBCSBEC1, RBCSBEC2), boson-fermion models (RBCSBEC3), and BCS-Leggett mean field theory at zero temperature (RBCSBEC4). The theory enables calculation of the critical temperature and pseudogap for color superconductivity at moderate baryon density, where the matter lies in the BCS-BEC crossover region and the pseudogap behavior resembles that found in high-temperature superconductors.
