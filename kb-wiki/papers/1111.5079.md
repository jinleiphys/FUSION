# The Equation of State of the Unitary Fermi Gas: An Update on Lattice Calculations

> The thermodynamic properties of the unitary Fermi gas (UFG) have recently been measured to unprecedented accuracy at the MIT. In particular, these measurements provide an improved understanding of the regime below T/eF ~ 0.20, where a transition into a superfluid phase occurs. In light of this development, we present an overview of state-of-the-art auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) results for the UFG at finite temperature and compare them with the MIT data for the energy, chemical potential, and density. These AFQMC results have been obtained using methods based on the hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm, which was first introduced within the context of lattice QCD.

## Key claim
The auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) results for the unitary Fermi gas (UFG) show satisfactory overall agreement with the MIT experimental data for energy, chemical potential, and density over a wide temperature range, but notable discrepancies persist at low temperatures below T/εF ≈ 0.15, particularly for the chemical potential where AFQMC overpredicts the experimental value by about 5%.

## Method
The authors performed AFQMC calculations using a hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm on a cubic spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions, employing a compact continuous Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation (Type 4) and Luescher's formula to determine the bare coupling constant g ≈ 5.14 for the unitary limit. The system was studied at lattice sizes up to Nx = 16 with particle numbers up to N ≈ 160 and densities in the range n ≈ 0.040-0.045, generating approximately 200 uncorrelated snapshots per temperature value to achieve statistical uncertainty of about 1%. Results were extrapolated to infinite volume before comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature from MIT experiment: Tc/εF = 0.167(15)
- Bertsch parameter from MIT experiment: ξ = 0.376(5)
- Bare lattice coupling constant in unitary limit: g ≈ 5.14
- Effective range parameter in calculations: kF reff ≈ 0.3
- Particle numbers studied: N ≈ 35 for Nx = 8, N ≈ 45 for Nx = 10, N ≈ 75 for Nx = 12, N ≈ 110 for Nx = 14, and N ≈ 160 for Nx = 16
- Density range for Nx = 10-16: n ≈ 0.040-0.045
- Density for Nx = 8: n ≈ 0.070
- Chemical potential from AFQMC at T/εF ≈ 0.1: μ/εF ≈ 0.38

## Context
This work extends the determinantal Monte Carlo (DMC) approach of Bulgac, Drut, and Magierski (2008) by introducing hybrid Monte Carlo methods from lattice QCD, enabling calculations at larger lattice volumes (up to Nx = 16 versus Nx ≈ 10 previously) and lower densities. The results are compared directly with the high-precision MIT measurements by Ku et al. (2011) and with diagrammatic Monte Carlo results by Burovski et al., Goulko et al., and Van Houcke et al. The improved algorithm enables future calculations with Nx > 16 and applications away from the unitary limit, though residual finite-range effects at kF reff ≈ 0.3 remain a systematic uncertainty that may be addressed through improved transfer matrices as proposed by Endres et al. and others.
