# Detection of cosmic neutrino clustering by cosmic ray spectra

> We propose a method to investigate the scenario that cosmic relic neutrinos are highly clustered around stars and galaxies, or dark-matter clusters, rather than uniformly distributed in the universe. Such a scenario can be detected or constrained by the interaction of high energy cosmic ray protons and nuclei with the cosmic neutrinos. There should be observable signature in the energy spectra of cosmic ray protons and nuclei for a neutrino clustering factor beyond $10^{13}$. We provide a relation on the signature onset positions between proton and nuclei spectra, and discuss possible support from existing experiments. It is also suggested that the relative abundance of cosmic ray nuclei may detect or constrain the cosmic neutrinos with smaller clustering.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that if cosmic relic neutrinos are highly clustered (with a clustering factor beyond $10^{13}$), their interaction with high-energy cosmic ray protons and nuclei should produce observable "knee" and "bump" features in cosmic ray energy spectra, and that the relative positions of these features between protons and different nuclear species provide a testable relation.

## Method

The authors calculate the threshold energies for the charged-current interaction \(p + \bar{\nu}_e \to e^+ + n\) and analogous reactions for nuclei (e.g., \(^4\mathrm{He} + \bar{\nu}_e \to e^+ + n + {}^3\mathrm{H}\)), using the center-of-mass energy threshold condition. They derive the mean free path of protons in the cosmic neutrino background as a function of the neutrino clustering factor \(c_\nu\), and compare the predicted threshold energies for protons, helium, carbon, and iron nuclei. They also examine the possibility of detecting smaller clustering factors via enhanced secondary-to-primary abundance ratios, such as \(^3\mathrm{He}/^4\mathrm{He}\).

## Key numbers

- The mean number density of relic neutrinos is \(n_\nu = 56\ \mathrm{cm}^{-3}\) per species (antineutrinos included), and the clustering factor \(c_\nu\) multiplies this to give the local density.
- For zero-mass neutrinos (\(\epsilon_\nu = 4.54 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{eV}\)), the threshold for \(p + \bar{\nu}_e \to e^+ + n\) is \(E_p^{\nu_e} = 1.87 \times 10^{18}\ \mathrm{eV}\); for \(m_{\nu_e} \approx 1\ \mathrm{eV}\) it is \(1.69 \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{eV}\); using the upper limit \(m_{\nu_e} \le 2.2\ \mathrm{eV}\) gives \(E_p^{\nu_e} \ge 7.70 \times 10^{14}\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- A clustering factor \(c_{\nu_e} \sim 10^{13}\) yields a mean free path \(\lambda_p^{\nu_e} \sim 400\ \mathrm{Mpc}\) for protons.
- The predicted threshold energy ratios are \(E_p^{\nu_e} : E_{^4\mathrm{He}}^{\nu_e} : E_{^{12}\mathrm{C}}^{\nu_e} : E_{^{56}\mathrm{Fe}}^{\nu_e} \approx 4 \times 10^{15} : 1.8 \times 10^{17} : 4.4 \times 10^{17} : 1.3 \times 10^{18}\ \mathrm{eV}\) for \(m_{\nu_e} = 0.42\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- Taking the all-particle cosmic ray knee at \(4 \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{eV}\) as \(E_p^{\nu_e}\) gives \(m_{\nu_e} = 0.42\ \mathrm{eV}\).
- For \(^{56}\mathrm{Fe}\), alternative reactions with unstable products may give a lower threshold around \(5 \times 10^{17}\ \mathrm{eV}\).

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Wigmans (2000) that the knee in the all-particle cosmic ray spectrum arises from neutrino interactions, by providing a quantitative relation between the knee positions for protons and different nuclei. It also connects to the Z-burst scenario for ultrahigh-energy cosmic rays (Weiler, 1999; Fargion et al., 1999; Yoshida et al., 1998; McKinsey, 2001) and to earlier suggestions of large neutrino clustering to explain tritium beta-decay anomalies (Mohapatra and Pal, 1997; Robertson et al., 1991). The paper enables future balloon, satellite, and extensive air shower experiments (such as KASCADE) to test neutrino clustering by measuring composition-resolved cosmic ray spectra and isotopic ratios like \(^3\mathrm{He}/^4\mathrm{He}\).
