# Constraints on the Lambda-neutron interaction from charge-symmetry breaking of A=4 hypernuclei

> We include the leading charge symmetry breaking contributions into the hyperon-nucleon interactions derived within chiral effective field theory up to next-to-leading order. Two low energy constants are determined using the experimentally known differences of Lambda separation energies of 4-Lambda-He and 4-Lambda-H. This allows one to predict the Lambda-neutron scattering lengths for the first time based on data. Various sources of uncertainty are discussed.

## Key claim

The leading charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) contributions to hyperon-nucleon interactions, including for the first time two short-range contact terms in chiral effective field theory, are determined from the experimentally known differences of Lambda separation energies of 4-Lambda-He and 4-Lambda-H; this allows the first data-based prediction of the Lambda-neutron scattering lengths, though the results depend on which experimental scenario for the A=4 hypernuclei is adopted.

## Method

The authors include the leading CSB contributions (kaon mass differences, Sigma0-Lambda mixing, pion-eta mixing, and two new contact interactions) into chiral YN interactions at next-to-leading order (NLO13 and NLO19). They determine the two CSB low-energy constants by fitting to the experimental splitting of the 0+ and 1+ state Lambda separation energies of 4-Lambda-He and 4-Lambda-H using Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations in momentum space. The resulting CSB interactions are then used to predict the singlet and triplet Lambda-proton and Lambda-neutron scattering lengths.

## Key numbers

* The experimental CSB splitting used (scenario CSB1) is Delta E(0+) = 233 +/- 92 keV and Delta E(1+) = -83 +/- 94 keV for the difference between 4-Lambda-He and 4-Lambda-H separation energies.
* The fitted CSB contact LECs range from about 4.7e-3 to 1.5e-2 x 10^4 GeV^-2 for the singlet and from about -9.3e-4 to -1.4e-3 x 10^4 GeV^-2 for the triplet, depending on cutoff and interaction version.
* The predicted singlet Lambda-neutron scattering length is approximately -3.2 fm (e.g., -3.267 fm for NLO13(500), -3.202 fm for NLO19(500)), compared to the singlet Lambda-proton scattering length of approximately -2.6 fm.
* The predicted triplet Lambda-neutron scattering length is approximately -1.4 to -1.6 fm (e.g., -1.561 fm for NLO13(500), -1.467 fm for NLO19(500)).
* The variation of Lambda separation energies for the 0+ state of A=4 hypernuclei without CSB is about 240 keV across cutoffs and interaction versions; for the 1+ state the variation is up to 650 keV.

## Context

This work extends chiral EFT studies of YN interactions (Haidenbauer 2013, Haidenbauer 2019) by adding the leading CSB contact terms that were previously neglected. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of charge symmetry breaking in A=4 hypernuclei, which was studied earlier using the Nijmegen SC97 model (Stoks 1999) and LO chiral interactions (Gal 2015, Gazda 2015, Gazda 2016). The results enable future accurate determination of the Lambda-neutron scattering length from improved experimental data on A=4 hypernuclei and provide a foundation for studying CSB in p-shell hypernuclei using the Jacobi no-core shell model.
