# Interplay of baryonic chiral partners in fluctuations of net-baryon number density

> In this contribution, we use the parity doublet model to investigate the fluctuations of the net-baryon number density. We discuss the systematics of the susceptibilities and their ratios for nucleons of positive and negative parity, as well as their correlator. We demonstrate that the fluctuations of positive-parity nucleon do not reflect the fluctuations of the total net-baryon number at the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

The fluctuations of positive-parity nucleons (protons) do not reflect the fluctuations of the total net-baryon number at the chiral phase transition, because the negative correlation between baryonic chiral partners (positive- and negative-parity states) suppresses the net-baryon number susceptibility.

## Method

The authors used the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation to calculate susceptibilities (second-order cumulants) of the net-baryon number density for nucleons of positive parity (N(939)) and negative parity (N*(1535)), as well as their correlator. They computed the susceptibilities and scaled variance ratios as functions of baryon chemical potential at fixed temperatures of T = 30 MeV and T = 100 MeV, examining behavior near both the liquid-gas and chiral phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- At T = 30 MeV, the net-baryon susceptibility develops a peak at μ_B < 1 GeV (remnant of liquid-gas transition) and another peak around μ_B = 1.4 GeV (remnant of chiral phase transition)
- At T = 30 MeV, the correlator χ₂⁺⁻ becomes negative and develops a minimum of similar magnitude to the peaks in χ₂⁺⁺ and χ₂⁻⁻ around the chiral transition
- At T = 100 MeV, the liquid-gas and chiral transitions occur almost simultaneously
- The strongest peak at the chiral transition is developed by the ratio R₂,₁⁻⁻ (scaled variance for negative-parity states)

## Context

This work extends parity doublet model studies (Detar 1988, Jido 1999, Jido 2001) to fluctuations of net-baryon number, building on recent lattice QCD observations of parity doubling in low-lying baryons around the chiral crossover (Aarts 2017). The results address the assumption used in experimental beam energy scan programs at RHIC (STAR 2010, STAR 2020) that net-proton fluctuations reflect net-baryon fluctuations, showing this assumption fails near the chiral phase transition. The work enables more careful interpretation of fluctuation observables in heavy-ion collision experiments searching for the QCD critical point.
