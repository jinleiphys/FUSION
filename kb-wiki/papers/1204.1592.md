# Single Transverse-Spin Asymmetry in Large $P_T$ Open Charm Production at an Electron-Ion Collider

> We discuss the single transverse-spin asymmetry (SSA) to be observed in the $D$-meson production with large transverse-momentum in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, $e p^\uparrow \rightarrow e D X$. This contribution is embodied as a twist-3 mechanism in the collinear factorization, which is induced by purely gluonic correlation inside the transversely-polarized nucleon, in particular, by the three-gluon correlation effects. The complete formula for the corresponding SSA in the leading-order QCD is expressed in terms of the four independent gluonic correlation functions and reveals the five independent structures with respect to the dependence on the azimuthal angle for the produced $D$-meson. We present the numerical calculations of the SSA formula at the kinematics relevant to a future Electron Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The single transverse-spin asymmetry (SSA) in large-transverse-momentum D-meson production at an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) can be described by a complete leading-order QCD twist-3 mechanism involving four independent three-gluon correlation functions, which yields five independent azimuthal structures; the numerical estimates show that the asymmetry can reach several percent, but this depends on the modeling of the nonperturbative gluonic correlation functions and on the specific kinematic region.

## Method

The authors derived the complete leading-order QCD formula for the SSA in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, e p^uparrow -> e D X, using collinear factorization at twist-3 level, where the asymmetry arises from soft-gluon-pole contributions associated with photon-gluon fusion subprocesses. They evaluated the SSA numerically using two models for the C-odd three-gluon correlation functions O(x,x) and O(x,0), with functional forms proportional to x G(x) (Model 1) and sqrt(x) G(x) (Model 2), where G(x) is the CTEQ6L unpolarized gluon density, and used the KKKS08 fragmentation function for D-meson production with charm quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV. The calculations were performed at several EIC kinematic points with S_ep = 1000, 2500, and 5000 GeV^2, Q = 2.5 or 4 GeV, P_{hperp} = 2 GeV, and x_bj = 0.01 or 0.005.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry F_1/sigma_1^U reaches several percent at EIC kinematics with S_ep = 1000 GeV^2, Q = 2.5 GeV, P_{hperp} = 2 GeV, and x_bj = 0.01.
- The asymmetry F_5/(2 sigma_1^U) can reach a few percent for decreasing P_{hperp} at the same kinematics.
- The unpolarized cross section coefficients show a reduction compared to earlier work (Kang 2008) mainly due to using m_c = 1.5 GeV (pole mass) versus the value m_c = 1.3 GeV used in that prior study.
- The lower limits of integration x_min and z_min are given by explicit formulas involving m_c = 1.5 GeV, Q^2, x_bj, z_f, and q_T = P_{hperp}/z_f.

## Context

This work extends the twist-3 mechanism for SSA from pion production in SIDIS (Eguchi, Koike, Tanaka 2007) to D-meson production, correcting earlier incomplete treatments by Kang (2008) that missed many terms and used only two types of gluonic functions with three azimuthal structures. The complete formula reveals four independent gluonic correlation functions and five azimuthal structures, and the numerical estimates demonstrate that measurements at a future EIC can probe multi-gluon correlations inside the nucleon, complementing similar studies for p^uparrow p -> D X at RHIC (Liu 2009).
