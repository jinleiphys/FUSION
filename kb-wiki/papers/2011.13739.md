# Bose-Einstein momentum correlations at fixed multiplicities: Lessons from an exactly solvable thermal model for $pp$ collisions at the LHC

> Two-particle momentum correlations of $N$ identical bosons are studied in the quantum canonical ensemble. We define the latter as a properly selected subensemble of events associated with the grand canonical ensemble which is characterized by a constant temperature and a harmonic-trap chemical potential. The merits of this toy model are that it can be solved exactly, and that it demonstrates some interesting features revealed recently in small systems created in $p+p$ collisions at the LHC. We find that partial coherence can be observed in particle emission from completely thermal ensembles of events if instead of inclusive measurements one studies the two-boson distribution functions related to the events with particle numbers selected in some fixed multiplicity bins. The corresponding coherence effects increase with the multiplicity.

## Key claim

Partial coherence in two-boson momentum correlations can appear in completely thermal ensembles of events if one studies subensembles with fixed particle multiplicities, rather than performing inclusive measurements; the coherence effects increase with multiplicity, even though the underlying system is fully thermal.

## Method

The authors construct an exactly solvable toy model of nonrelativistic ideal bosons in a harmonic trap at fixed temperature, then apply a fixed-particle-number constraint to the grand-canonical statistical operator to define a canonical ensemble. Using the harmonic oscillator basis and recursive formulas for canonical partition functions, they analytically compute one-particle momentum spectra and two-boson momentum correlation functions for fixed multiplicities, and compare these to the corresponding grand-canonical results.

## Key numbers

- Temperature set to 150 MeV, corresponding roughly to system breakup in \(p+p\) collisions at LHC energies.
- Thermal wavelength \(\Lambda_T = 1/\sqrt{mT} \approx 1.36\) fm for pion mass.
- Radius parameter \(R\) set to 1.5 fm and 3.0 fm.
- Particle number \(N\) varied in the range 1 to 20.
- Correlation function intercept \(C_N(\mathbf{k},\mathbf{0})\) is less than 2, indicating partial coherence; smaller \(R\) (1.5 fm) gives smaller intercept values than larger \(R\) (3.0 fm) at fixed \(N\).
- For \(k = 0.15\) GeV/c and \(N = 10\), the correlation function shows non-Gaussian tails beyond the correlation peak, well fitted by a two-Gaussian expression.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Akkelin et al. (Akk-2) on coherence effects in Bose-Einstein correlation functions for thermal systems with fixed multiplicities, moving from a large-volume, low-density approximation to small systems with high particle number densities relevant to high-multiplicity \(p+p\) collisions at the LHC. The model addresses the puzzle that correlation strength parameters measured by ATLAS and CMS are less than unity, contrary to expectations for fully thermalized emission, and suggests that fixed-multiplicity selection effects, not hydrodynamics failure, can produce the observed partial coherence. The results enable future studies of how quantum canonical constraints modify femtoscopic observables in small colliding systems.
