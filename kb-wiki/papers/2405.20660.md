# Factorization of photon induced processes in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions

> In this study, we investigate photon-photon scattering in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions (UPCs). We start by deriving an effective Lagrangian from first principles and then apply factorization techniques from Soft-Collinear effective theory (SCET). This approach allows us to decompose the photon-photon scattering cross-section into two primary factors: the generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) and the hard scattering amplitude. We further analyze the emission of soft photons by final state leptons, incorporating a soft function into the cross section through an evolution method. Our analysis yields detailed predictions for observable angular correlations among the final state leptons. Specifically, we calculate the angular correlations characterized by the azimuthal parameters $\langle\cos 2ϕ\rangle$ and $\langle\cos 4ϕ\rangle$, highlighting the influence of initial photons polarization and recoil effects from final state soft photons.

# Factorization of photon induced processes in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that photon-photon scattering cross-sections in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions can be factorized into generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) and a hard scattering amplitude using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), but this factorization relies on two specific conditions: that the longitudinal momentum of the heavy ions is much larger than their transverse momentum components, and that the impact parameter is much larger than the transverse spatial uncertainty of the wave packets.

## Method

The author constructs an effective Lagrangian from first principles for the process gamma+gamma -> l+anti-l+soft photon, projecting the full QED field onto fields with distinct velocities and separating collinear photon fields from soft photon fields via a decoupling transformation. Using wave packet expansion of the incoming heavy ion states and applying SCET factorization techniques, the S-matrix is decomposed into perturbative high-energy scattering parts and non-perturbative matrix elements corresponding to collinear photon fields. The cross-section is then expressed in terms of GTMDs, a lepton tensor, and a soft function that accounts for final-state soft photon emission, with the impact parameter dependence arising from the wave packet expansion of the initial nuclear states.

## Key numbers

- The total transverse momentum of the lepton pair in UPCs is typically around 10 MeV, significantly smaller than the transverse momentum of each individual lepton
- The paper presents numerical results for angular correlations of muon+muon- pairs from ultra-peripheral Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV center-of-mass energy
- The paper presents numerical results for angular correlations of tau+tau- pairs from ultra-peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at 5020 GeV center-of-mass energy under LHC conditions
- The azimuthal parameters calculated are ⟨cos 2φ⟩ and ⟨cos 4φ⟩, characterizing angular correlations among final state leptons

## Context

This work extends the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) approach used in software packages such as STARlight (Klein 2016) by incorporating photon transverse momentum and polarization information that is missing in the standard EPA framework. The factorization framework connects to the Glauber model (Miller 2007) widely used in heavy-ion collision studies and provides a quantum field theory foundation for impact parameter-dependent scattering cross-sections. The approach enables future studies of quark-gluon plasma effects on final-state lepton pairs in non-central and central collisions, building on preliminary theoretical work by Klein (2019) on transverse momentum broadening in QED media.
