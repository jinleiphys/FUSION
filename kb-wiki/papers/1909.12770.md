# $D$-wave effects in diffractive electroproduction of heavy quarkonia from the photon-like $V\rightarrow Q\bar Q$ transition

> We analyze the validity of a commonly used identification between structures of the virtual photon $γ^*\to Q\bar Q$ and vector meson $V\to Q\bar Q$ transitions. In the existing studies of $S$-wave vector-meson photoproduction in the literature, such an identification is typically performed in the light-front (LF) frame while the radial component of the meson wave function is rather postulated than computed from the first principles. The massive photon-like $V\to Q\bar Q$ vertex, besides the $S$-wave component, also contains an extra $D$-wave admixture in the $Q\bar Q$ rest frame. However, the relative weight of these contributions cannot be justified by any reasonable nonrelativistic $Q\bar Q$ potential model. In this work, we investigate the relative role of the $D$-wave contribution starting from the photon-like quarkonium $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition in both frames: in the $Q\bar Q$ rest frame (with subsequent Melosh spin transform to the LF frame) and in the LF frame (without Melosh transform). We show that the photon-like transition imposed in the $Q\bar Q$ rest frame leads to significant discrepancies with the experimental data. In the second case we find that the corresponding total $J/ψ(1S)$ photoproduction cross sections are very close to those obtained with the "$S$-wave only" $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition, both leading to a good description of the data. However, we find that the "$S$-wave only" transition leads to a better description of photoproduction data for excited heavy quarkonium states, which represent a more effective tool for study of $D$-wave effects. Consequently, the predictions for production of excited states based on the photon-like structure of $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition should be treated with a great care due to a much stronger sensitivity of the $D$-wave contribution to the nodal structure of quarkonium wave functions.

## Key claim

The commonly used identification between the virtual photon \(\gamma^*\to Q\bar Q\) and vector meson \(V\to Q\bar Q\) transitions is not fully justified; the photon-like \(V\to Q\bar Q\) vertex contains an extra \(D\)-wave admixture whose relative weight cannot be justified by any reasonable nonrelativistic \(Q\bar Q\) potential model, and the predictions for excited states based on this photon-like structure should be treated with great care due to stronger sensitivity of the \(D\)-wave contribution to the nodal structure of quarkonium wave functions.

## Method

The authors computed diffractive electroproduction cross sections for heavy quarkonia (J/\(\psi\)(1S), \(\psi\)(2S), \(\Upsilon\)(1S), \(\Upsilon\)(2S), \(\Upsilon\)(3S)) in the color dipole formalism, using two different implementations of the photon-like \(V\to Q\bar Q\) transition: imposed in the \(Q\bar Q\) rest frame with subsequent Melosh spin rotation to the light-front (LF) frame (scenario II), and imposed directly in the LF frame without Melosh transform (scenario I). They employed the same radial wave functions obtained from the Schroedinger equation with Buchmuller-Tye and power-like potentials, and used the GBW and KST parametrizations for the universal dipole cross section.

## Key numbers

- For J/\(\psi\)(1S) photoproduction at \(Q^2 = 0.05\) GeV\(^2\), the photon-like transition imposed in the LF frame (scenario I) yields total cross sections very close to those obtained with the "\(S\)-wave only" transition (scenario III), both leading to a good description of experimental data from HERA and LHC.
- The photon-like transition imposed in the \(Q\bar Q\) rest frame (scenario II) leads to significant discrepancies with experimental data for J/\(\psi\)(1S) photoproduction.
- The slope parameter used in the cross section calculation is \(B = 4.73\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and the photon polarization parameter is \(\tilde{\varepsilon} = 0.99\), both obtained by fitting to H1 data at HERA.
- The scanning radius is defined as \(r_S \approx Y / (2\sqrt{Q^2 z(1-z) + m_Q^2}) \sim Y / \sqrt{Q^2 + M_V^2}\) with \(Y \sim 6\) in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of Melosh spin rotation effects in heavy quarkonium electroproduction (Krelina, Nemchik, Pasechnik 2019) which assumed only \(S\)-wave structure in the \(Q\bar Q\) rest frame. It contradicts the common literature practice of imposing a photon-like \(V\to Q\bar Q\) transition directly in the LF frame (as done by Ryskin 1992, Brodsky 1994, Frankfurt 1995, and Nemchik 1996) by showing that such an approach contains an unjustified \(D\)-wave admixture. The results enable future studies to properly account for \(D\)-wave effects when predicting production of radially excited \(2S\) and \(3S\) heavy quarkonium states, where the sensitivity to the nodal structure of wave functions is much stronger than for ground states.
