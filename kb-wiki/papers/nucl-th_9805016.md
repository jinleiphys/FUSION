# Fine-Tuning Two-Particle Interferometry II: Opacity Effects

> We present a model study of single-particle spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations for opaque sources. We study the transverse mass dependence of the correlation radii R_\perp, R_\parallel and R_0 in the YKP parametrization and find a strong sensitivity of the temporal radius parameter R_0^2 to the source opacity. A simple comparison with the published data from 158 A GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions at CERN indicates that the pion source created in these collisions emits particles from the whole reaction volume and is not opaque. For opaque sources we find certain regions of inapplicability of the YKP parametrization which can be avoided by a slightly different parametrization for the correlator. The physical meaning of the modified parameters is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that opaque sources (which emit particles from a thin surface shell) produce a strong negative signal in the temporal YKP radius parameter \(R_0^2\) that diverges to minus infinity as transverse pair momentum \(K_\perp\) approaches zero, but a comparison with 158 A GeV/c Pb+Pb collision data from CERN indicates the pion source is not opaque and instead emits particles from the whole reaction volume.

## Method

The authors introduced opacity into a previously developed Gaussian emission function by adding an exponential suppression factor \(\exp[-l_{\rm eff}/\lambda]\) that suppresses particle emission from deep inside the source, where \(l_{\rm eff}\) is the effective path length a particle travels outward before leaving the source and \(\lambda\) is the mean free path. They computed single-particle spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation radii in both the Cartesian and Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrizations using model-independent expressions in terms of space-time variances of the emission function, scanning opacities \(\omega = R/\lambda = 0, 1, 10\) with and without transverse flow.

## Key numbers

- Opacity parameter \(\omega = R/\lambda\) values studied: 0 (transparent), 1, and 10
- For the data comparison, the Gaussian transverse radius \(R\) was reduced from 5.74 fm (transparent) to 4.83 fm (\(\omega=1\)) and 3.35 fm (\(\omega=10\)); the transverse flow parameter \(\eta_f\) was reduced from 0.4 to 0.345 to 0.215 to keep average transverse flow velocity \(\langle v_\perp \rangle = 0.44c\) fixed
- Fixed model parameters: temperature \(T = 120\) MeV, average freeze-out proper time \(\tau_0 = 8\) fm/\(c\) (later adjusted to 6.1 fm/\(c\) for data comparison), mean emission duration \(\Delta\tau = 2\) fm/\(c\), geometric Gaussian transverse radius \(R = 6.5\) fm, space-time rapidity width \(\Delta\eta = 1.3\), pion mass \(m_{\pi^\pm} = 139\) MeV/\(c^2\)
- For opaque sources (\(\omega=10\)) with \(\eta_f=0.5\), the average transverse flow velocity needed to reproduce the spectral slope via the blue-shifted temperature formula was \(\langle v_\perp \rangle = 0.49c\), larger than the source-average value of 0.44c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model study (Paper I, Tomasik and Heinz, published in European Physical Journal C) by adding opacity effects that were proposed by Heiselberg and Vischer (1996, 1997) as a mechanism affecting the geometric interpretation of correlation radii. The study contradicts the opaque-source interpretation for SPS Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV/c by showing that the NA49 collaboration data (1997, 1998) for \(R_0^2\) are incompatible with opaque models even at moderate opacity \(\omega=1\), while transparent sources reproduce the data well. The work enables future analyses to use the YKP parametrization's \(R_0^2\) as a sensitive probe of source opacity, though it also identifies a kinematic region where the YKP parametrization becomes ill-defined for opaque sources and proposes a modified parametrization to avoid this defect.
