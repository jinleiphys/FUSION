# The vector and tensor asymmetries and deuteron wave function for different nucleon-nucleon potentials

> Within the framework of the study of radiative corrections to the polarization observed in elastic $ed$-scattering in leptonic variables have been calculated the Born values of vector AB^L, AB^T and tensor AB^LL, AB^TT, AB^LT asymmetries. The deuteron wave functions in coordinate representation for eight nucleon-nucleon potentials (Nijm1, Nijm2, Nijm93, Reid93, Argonne v18, OBEPC, MT and Paris) have been used for numerical calculations of vector AB^i(p,teta) and tensor AB^ij(p,teta) asymmetries. The angular-momentum dependence of values vector and tensor asymmetries have been also evaluated in 3D format for Reid93 potentials.

# The vector and tensor asymmetries and deuteron wave function for different nucleon-nucleon potentials

## Key claim

The Born-level vector and tensor asymmetries in elastic electron-deuteron scattering are calculated using deuteron wave functions from eight different nucleon-nucleon potentials, with the angular-momentum dependence evaluated in 3D format specifically for the Reid93 potential; the results are presented as a baseline for future inclusion of radiative corrections, not as final observable predictions.

## Method

The author computed the Born values of five asymmetries (vector longitudinal A_B^L, vector transverse A_B^T, and tensor A_B^LL, A_B^TT, A_B^LT) using analytic forms of the deuteron wave function in coordinate representation for eight nucleon-nucleon potentials: Nijm1, Nijm2, Nijm93, Reid93, Argonne v18, OBEPC, MT, and Paris. The asymmetries were derived from spin-dependent cross-section expressions in the laboratory system, incorporating deuteron electromagnetic form factors G_C, G_Q, and G_M. The angular dependence of the asymmetries was plotted for all potentials, and the momentum-angle (p, theta) dependence was shown in 3D format for the Reid93 potential only.

## Key numbers

- For the Reid93 potential, the tensor asymmetries A_B^LL and A_B^TT exhibit a peak at 3.5-4 fm^{-1} in the range of angles 0-180 degrees.
- For the Reid93 potential, the tensor asymmetry A_B^LT shows a pit (negative extremum) at the same momentum range of 3.5-4 fm^{-1}.
- The parameterization for the Paris potential uses m_0 = 0.9 fm^{-1} and beta = sqrt(M E_d), where M is the nucleon mass and E_d is the deuteron binding energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of polarization observables in elastic electron-deuteron scattering, building on the formalism for radiative corrections developed in prior work (Zhaba, 2017) and the parameterization of deuteron wave functions for Nijmegen group potentials (Stoks et al., 1994). The calculated Born asymmetries provide a baseline for future inclusion of leptonic radiative corrections, and the results can be compared with experimental data from the BLAST experiment at MIT-Bates and measurements at the VEPP-3 storage ring. The author notes that further study should include lepton mass effects and spin correlation coefficients in the zero-lepton-mass limit.
