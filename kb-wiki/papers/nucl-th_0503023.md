# Inclusive Quasi-Elastic Neutrino Reactions

> The Quasi-Elastic contribution of the nuclear inclusive electron scattering model developed in Nucl. Phys. A 627 (1997) 543 is extended to the study of electroweak Charged Current induced nuclear reactions, at intermediate energies of interest for future neutrino oscillation experiments. The model accounts for long range nuclear (RPA) correlations, Final State Interaction and Coulomb corrections. RPA correlations are shown to play a crucial role in the whole range of neutrino energies, up to 500 MeV, studied in this work. Predictions for inclusive muon capture for different nuclei, and for the reactions $^{12}$C$(ν_μ,μ^-)X$ and $^{12}$C$(ν_e,e^-)X$ near threshold are also given.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously successful model of inclusive electron-nucleus scattering to charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions, but the abstract’s claim that RPA correlations “play a crucial role” is hedged: the model’s predictions are reliable only for nuclear excitation energies below 500 MeV, and the treatment of RPA effects uses a non-relativistic effective force, requiring the authors to neglect terms of order O(p²/M²) for consistency.

## Method

The authors compute the neutrino self-energy in nuclear matter using a many-body expansion, where the W⁺-boson self-energy in the medium is the central object. They evaluate the quasi-elastic (1p1h) contribution to the hadronic tensor using a relativistic Fermi gas with different Fermi momenta for protons and neutrons, then incorporate long-range nuclear correlations via a Landau-Migdal effective particle-hole interaction in the random phase approximation (RPA), including Δ(1232) degrees of freedom in the spin-isospin channel. Final state interactions are included by dressing the outgoing nucleon propagator with a realistic self-energy that couples to 2p2h states, and Coulomb corrections are added via a modified effective momentum approximation.

## Key numbers

- Flux-averaged \(^{12}\text{C}(\nu_e,e^-)X\) cross section: theory gives 0.14 (units of \(10^{-40}\) cm²), compared to KARMEN 0.15±0.01±0.01 and LSND 0.15±0.01±0.01.
- Flux-averaged \(^{12}\text{C}(\nu_\mu,\mu^-)X\) cross section: theory gives 11.9 (units of \(10^{-40}\) cm²), compared to LSND 2002 value of 10.6±0.3±1.8.
- Total muon capture width for \(^{12}\text{C}\): theory with RPA gives 3.21 (units of \(10^4\) s⁻¹), experiment gives 3.78±0.03 (15% discrepancy).
- Total muon capture width for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\): theory with RPA gives 1310 (units of \(10^4\) s⁻¹), experiment gives 1311±8 (0.0% discrepancy).
- For \(^{16}\text{O}(\nu_\mu,\mu^-)X\) at Eν=500 MeV with RPA and FSI: cross section 389.8 (units of \(10^{-40}\) cm²); without RPA and FSI (Pauli only, REL): 460.0.
- RPA+CB corrections suppress cross sections by 20-30% at the highest energies studied (500 MeV for muon neutrinos, 400 MeV for electron neutrinos).
- Relativistic nucleon kinematics reduces cross sections by 4-9% for both neutrino and antineutrino reactions at the energies considered.

## Context

This work extends the inclusive electron scattering model of Gil et al. (1997) and the photo-nuclear model of Carrasco et al. to charged-current neutrino reactions, building on the many-body framework developed by Oset et al. (1982) for pionic reactions. The model successfully reproduces low-energy data from the KARMEN, LSND, and LAMPF experiments on carbon, as well as muon capture rates across the periodic table from carbon to lead. The results enable more precise predictions for future neutrino oscillation experiments by quantifying the systematic uncertainties from nuclear effects, particularly the interplay between RPA correlations and final state interactions that had not been simultaneously treated in prior work such as that of Blomgren et al. (2001).
