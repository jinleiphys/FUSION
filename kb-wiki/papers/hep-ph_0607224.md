# Extraction of Spectral Functions from Dyson-Schwinger Studies via the Maximum Entropy Method

> It is shown how to apply the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) to numerical Dyson-Schwinger studies for the extraction of spectral functions of correlators from their corresponding Euclidean propagators. Differences to the application in lattice QCD are emphasized and, as an example, the spectral functions of massless quarks in cold and dense matter are presented.

# Extraction of Spectral Functions from Dyson-Schwinger Studies via the Maximum Entropy Method

## Key claim
The Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) can be successfully adapted to numerical Dyson-Schwinger studies for extracting spectral functions from Euclidean propagators, and it yields more reliable and stable results than in lattice QCD applications because Dyson-Schwinger calculations can be performed with much higher numerical accuracy and for many more momentum points; however, the systematic error from truncation schemes in Dyson-Schwinger studies remains the dominant uncertainty, making the MEM error negligible in comparison.

## Method
The MEM is applied to numerically determined Euclidean propagators from truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations, using the generalized Källen-Lehmann representation that relates the Euclidean correlator to the spectral function. The inversion of this ill-posed linear relation is regularized by interpreting the spectral function as a probability distribution and implementing a Bayesian approach with an entropy functional, where the most expected spectral function is obtained by integrating over a scaling parameter α. For fermions, the positivity conditions and sum rules of the spectral function are exploited, and the method is adapted to handle matrix-valued spectral functions for massive fermions by diagonalizing the hermitian positive-definite combination ρ(ω,p)γ₄/4.

## Key numbers
- The input data for the gapped channel in the 2SC phase at a quark chemical potential of μ = 1 GeV have errors of less than 1% in absolute value above 30 MeV.
- The spectral function is extracted on an interval [-1.5 GeV, 2 GeV] for momentum p = 0.9 GeV.
- The extracted spectral function is insensitive to variation of the prior estimate m over more than three orders of magnitude (m = 0.001, 0.01, 0.1, and 1.0 GeV⁻¹).
- The BCS-type spectral function for a constant gap Δ is given by ρ⁺(ω,p) = (1/2 + (μ-p)/(2E_Δ)) δ(ω + E_Δ - μ) + (1/2 - (μ-p)/(2E_Δ)) δ(ω - E_Δ - μ), with E_Δ = √((p-μ)² + Δ²).

## Context
This work extends the application of the Maximum Entropy Method, previously used in lattice QCD (Asakawa 2000) and condensed matter physics (Jarrell), to numerical Dyson-Schwinger studies, which are typically performed in Euclidean space (Alkofer 2000, Roberts 2000, Fischer 2006). The method enables the extraction of spectral functions for fermions in cold dense matter, specifically for color-superconducting quark matter in the 2SC phase, without requiring analytic continuation to complex momenta as in previous approaches (Alkofer 2002, Alkofer 2003). This opens the possibility to improve or extend known truncation schemes in Dyson-Schwinger studies and to apply the method to meson and diquark investigations.
