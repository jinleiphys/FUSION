# From Nuclear Matter with Quenched $g_A$ to Compact-Star Matter with a Signal for Emergent Hidden Scale Symmetry

> An ``unorthodox" idea is developed that the long-standing mystery in nuclear physics of the effective axial-current coupling constant in nuclei, $g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1$, could be interpreted in terms of an emerging hidden scale symmetry in dense compact-star matter. Arguments are presented using an effective field theory anchored on a renormalization-group approach to interacting baryons on the Fermi surface coupled with hidden symmetric heavy mesonic degrees of freedom that enables one to go beyond Weinberg's nuclear effective field theory involving nucleon and pion fields only, referred hereon to as $χ$EFT$_π$. Both hidden local and scale symmetries, the former involving the vector mesons $ρ$ and $ω$ and the latter the hidden scalar meson, a dilaton $\hatσ$ (i.e., $f_0(500)$), play the crucial role. Going beyond the density regime applicable to normal nuclear matter $n_0$, the notion of ``hadron-quark continuity HQC)" is brought in via the skyrmion structure of the nucleon argued to be valid in QCD at large $N_c$ limit and the large $N^\prime$ limit of the Grassmannian model $G/H= [O(N^\prime)/O(N^\prime-p) \times O(p)]$ where $N^\prime=4$ and $p=2$ for hidden local symmetry and the IR fixed point in QCD for $N_f \leq 3$ involving ``genuine/QCD-conformal dilaton" for hidden scale symmetry. The connection between the quenched $g_A$ and the sound speed $v^2_{s}/c^2\approx 1/3$ inside dense compact stars could be interpreted as a signal for emergent ``pseudo-conformal" symmetry.

## Key claim

The long-standing mystery of the effective axial-current coupling constant in nuclei, \(g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1\), is interpreted as a signal for an emerging hidden scale symmetry in dense compact-star matter, rather than being explained by conventional nuclear correlations alone. The connection between this quenched \(g_A\) and the sound speed \(v_s^2/c^2\approx 1/3\) inside dense compact stars is proposed as evidence for emergent "pseudo-conformal" symmetry, though the author acknowledges this is an "unorthodox" idea and the density at which the dilaton-limit fixed point sets in is not determined by theory or experiment.

## Method

The author develops a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G\(n\)EFT) by incorporating hidden local symmetry (HLS) for vector mesons \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) and hidden scale symmetry (HSS) for the dilaton scalar \(\hat{\sigma}\) (identified as \(f_0(500)\)) into the chiral Lagrangian, going beyond Weinberg's \(\chi\)EFT\(_\pi\) that includes only nucleon and pion fields. The approach uses a renormalization-group method for interacting baryons on the Fermi surface, mapping the Lagrangian via the Shankar-Polchinski formalism to a Landau Fermi-liquid theory, with the mean-fields taken as Fermi-liquid fixed point quantities and a \(1/\bar{N}\) expansion replacing the chiral expansion. The hadron-quark continuity (HQC) is brought in via the skyrmion crystal structure of the nucleon, exploiting large \(N_c\) properties and the Grassmannian model \(G/H = [O(N^\prime)/O(N^\prime-p) \times O(p)]\) with \(N^\prime=4\) and \(p=2\) for HLS, and an IR fixed point in QCD for \(N_f \leq 3\) involving a "genuine/QCD-conformal dilaton" for HSS.

## Key numbers

- The effective axial-current coupling constant in nuclei is \(g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1\), interpreted as the quasiparticle Landau fixed-point quantity \(g_A^L\) with very little density dependence near equilibrium nuclear matter density \(n_0\sim 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The sound speed in dense compact-star matter is \(v_s^2/c^2\approx 1/3\), which is the conformal velocity in the chiral limit but arises here from "pseudo-conformality" without conformal symmetry restoration.
- The topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions occurs at a density \(n_{1/2}\sim (2-3)n_0\), where the chiral condensate \(\bar{q}q\) is zero space-averaged but the pion decay constant is not zero.
- The dilaton-limit fixed point constraints give \(g_V\to g_A\to 1\) and \(f_\pi\to f_{\bar{\chi}}\neq 0\) at high density near the restoration of scale symmetry, with the quasiparticle mass \(m^\ast_N\to m_0\propto {\rm const.}\) in the half-skyrmion phase.
- The iso-vector quasiparticle gyromagnetic ratio is predicted as \(\delta g_l^{proton}=\frac{4}{9}[1/\Phi -1-\frac{1}{2}\frac{m_N}{m_L} F_1^\pi]\tau_3\), which agrees with available experiments.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposals (Rho, 1991) and the Brown-Rho (BR) scaling idea (Brown and Rho, 1991) by connecting the quenched \(g_A\) problem, first seriously considered by Wilkinson in his Les Houches Lectures, to compact-star physics. It contradicts the standard \(\chi\)EFT\(_\pi\) approach (Weinberg's Folk Theorem) which cannot access densities above \(\sim (2-3)n_0\) and does not predict the cusp in symmetry energy or the pseudo-conformal sound speed. The approach enables a bottom-up description of hadron-quark continuity from normal nuclear matter to superdense matter in compact stars, matching the putative top-down quark-hadron continuity from perturbative QCD, and suggests that the recent RIKEN data on the superallowed Gamow-Teller transition in \(^{100}\)Sn could test the role of the scale anomaly in the axial current.
