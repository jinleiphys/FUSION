# Phi meson production in near threshold proton-nucleus collisions

> The cross section for production of Phi mesons in proton-nucleus reactions is calculated as a function of the target mass. The decay width of the Phi meson is affected by the change of the masses of the Phi, K+ and K- mesons in the medium. A strong attractive K- potential leads to a measurable change of the behavior of the cross section as a function of of the target mass. Comparison between the kaon and electron decay modes are made.

# Phi meson production in near threshold proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The target-mass dependence of the observed phi meson production cross section in subthreshold proton-nucleus reactions is strongly altered if strong changes of the kaon masses in nuclear matter occur, and this effect should be measurable at COSY or SIS accelerators. The abstract glosses over the fact that the observable effect depends critically on the specific choice of in-medium kaon potentials and the selection criterion applied to reconstructed kaon pairs.

## Method

The authors used a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to simulate phi meson production in proton-nucleus collisions at 2.5 GeV beam energy, which is below the free nucleon-nucleon threshold. They included phi production from primary NN collisions and secondary collisions (Delta N, pi N, rho N), implemented in-medium modifications of the phi decay width via Eq. (3) based on changed kaon and phi masses, and tracked rescattering and absorption of kaons and phi mesons. Four sets of K- potentials were tested: zero potential, a moderate -70 MeV potential, a momentum-dependent potential, and a strong -150 MeV potential, with calculations performed for p+C, p+Cu, p+Te, and p+Au targets.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2.5 GeV (below the free NN phi production threshold)
- Vacuum phi mean proper lifetime: about 50 fm/c, decreasing to an order of magnitude smaller in normal nuclear matter
- K+ potential: U_K+(n) = 25 MeV * n/n0 (weakly repulsive, linear in density)
- Phi mass shift parameter alpha tested: alpha = 0 and alpha = 0.033 (corresponding to mass shift up to about 10 MeV at normal density)
- K- potentials tested: (a) 0 MeV, (b) -70 MeV, (c) momentum-dependent with a = -55 MeV, b = -130 MeV, c = 0.0025 MeV^-1, (d) -150 MeV (all at normal nuclear matter density)
- Selection criterion for reconstructed phi from K+K- pairs: |m_K+K- - m_phi^vac| < 0.05 GeV
- For a strong attractive K- potential of 150 MeV, Eq. (3) provides a phi width of 45 MeV in normal nuclear matter
- Gamma_rest in vacuum: 0.7 MeV (15% of total width); if rho mass is reduced by 200 MeV in matter, this partial width increases to 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the BUU transport model previously applied to phi production in heavy-ion collisions (Ref. phiAA, 2003) to the case of proton-nucleus collisions, providing a theoretical framework for interpreting experiments at COSY (FZ Julich) and SIS (GSI Darmstadt). The study is motivated by kaonic atom experiments (Katom, 1990s) and enhanced K- production observed in heavy-ion collisions by FOPI (fopi1, fopi2) and KaoS (kaos1, kaos3) collaborations, which point to a strong attractive K- potential. The results contradict early estimates based on effective chiral Lagrangians (Nelson-Kaplan, 1987) and instead support more sophisticated calculations (Kolomeitsev, 2000; Sibirtsev-Cassing, 2000; Schaffner, 1997; Cieply, 2001; Lutz, 2002) that yield weaker or momentum-dependent K- potentials. The work enables experimental tests of in-medium kaon and phi potentials by comparing the target-mass dependence of phi production reconstructed from K+K- versus e+e- decay channels.
