# Unquenched quark model for baryons: magnetic moments, spins and orbital angular momenta

> We present an unquenched quark model for baryons in which the effects of the quark-antiquark pairs (up, down and strange) are taken into account in an explicit form via a microscopic, QCD-inspired, quark-antiquark creation mechanism. In the present approach, the contribution of the quark-antiquark pairs can be studied for any inital baryon and for any flavor of the pairs. It is shown that, while the inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs does not affect the baryon magnetic moments, it leads to a sizeable contribution of the orbital angular momentum to the spin of the proton and the Lambda hyperon.

## Key claim

The inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs via a QCD-inspired \(^{3}P_0\) pair-creation mechanism does not affect the baryon magnetic moments after renormalization, but it leads to a sizeable contribution of orbital angular momentum to the spin of the proton (32.4%) and the \(\Lambda\) hyperon (14.9%), while preserving the good results of the constituent quark model for magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors extend the constituent quark model by adding quark-antiquark pairs (\(u\bar{u}\), \(d\bar{d}\), \(s\bar{s}\)) as a perturbation using a \(^{3}P_0\) pair-creation operator. The baryon wave function is expanded to include a sum over all possible intermediate baryon-meson states up to five oscillator shells, with harmonic oscillator wave functions and parameters taken from the literature (\(\hbar\omega_{\text{baryon}}=0.32\) GeV, \(\hbar\omega_{\text{meson}}=0.40\) GeV, pair-creation strength \(\gamma_0=2.60\), and pair size \(r_q=0.30\) fm). Matrix elements of observables are computed as the sum of valence and sea contributions, with the sum over intermediate states solved using group-theoretical techniques to ensure completeness.

## Key numbers

- Proton spin decomposition in the unquenched model: \(\Delta u = 1.098\), \(\Delta d = -0.417\), \(\Delta s = -0.005\), \(\Delta\Sigma = 0.676\), \(2\Delta L = 0.324\), with total \(2J = 1.000\).
- \(\Lambda\) hyperon spin decomposition: \(\Delta u = -0.055\), \(\Delta d = -0.055\), \(\Delta s = 0.961\), \(\Delta\Sigma = 0.851\), \(2\Delta L = 0.149\), with total \(2J = 1.000\).
- Quark magnetic moments after unquenching: \(\mu_u = 2.066\ \mu_N\), \(\mu_d = -1.110\ \mu_N\), \(\mu_s = -0.633\ \mu_N\).
- Triplet axial charge \(a_3 = \Delta u - \Delta d = 1.515\) and octet axial charge \(a_8 = \Delta u + \Delta d - 2\Delta s = 0.681\), with ratio \(a_3/a_8 = 2.22\).
- Orbital angular momentum contribution to the proton spin arises almost entirely from the relative motion of nucleon and \(\Delta\) resonance with respect to the \(\pi\)-meson (31.7% of the proton spin), with orbitally excited baryons and mesons contributing only 0.7%.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur (1990s) and the earlier \(^{3}P_0\) model studies by Tornqvist and Zenczykowski, generalizing the approach to any initial baryon and any flavor of quark-antiquark pair. The unquenched quark model provides a microscopic mechanism for the orbital angular momentum contribution to the proton spin, complementing earlier qualitative estimates from the cloudy bag model (CBM) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL), which gave \(\Delta\Sigma\) values of 0.60 and 0.56 respectively. The results enable systematic studies of higher Fock components in baryon spectroscopy and spin structure, and are consistent with the Ellis-Jaffe-Sehgal analysis and the experimental data from HERMES and COMPASS.
