# Nuclear dependence coefficient $α(A,q_T)$ for the Drell-Yan and J/$ψ$ production

> Define the nuclear dependence coefficient $α(A,q_T)$ in terms of ratio of transverse momentum spectrum in hadron-nucleus and in hadron-nucleon collisions: $\frac{dσ^{hA}}{dq_T^2}/ \frac{dσ^{hN}}{dq_T^2}\equiv A^{α(A,q_T)}$. We argue that in small $q_T$ region, the $α(A,q_T)$ for the Drell-Yan and J/$ψ$ production is given by a universal function:\ $a+b q_T^2$, where parameters a and b are completely determined by either calculable quantities or independently measurable physical observables. We demonstrate that this universal function $α(A,q_T)$ is insensitive to the A for normal nuclear targets. For a color deconfined nuclear medium, the $α(A,q_T)$ becomes strongly dependent on the A. We also show that our $α(A,q_T)$ for the Drell-Yan process is naturally linked to perturbatively calculated $α(A,q_T)$ at large $q_T$ without any free parameters, and the $α(A,q_T)$ is consistent with E772 data for all $q_T$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the small transverse momentum ($q_T$) region, the nuclear dependence coefficient $\alpha(A,q_T)$ for both Drell-Yan and J/$\psi$ production follows a universal quadratic function $a + b q_T^2$, where the parameters are fully determined by either perturbatively calculable quantities or independently measurable physical observables, and that this function is insensitive to the atomic number $A$ for normal nuclear targets but becomes strongly $A$-dependent in a color deconfined medium.

## Method

The authors derived the Drell-Yan transverse momentum spectrum in hadron-nucleon and hadron-nucleus collisions at fixed target energies by representing the small $q_T$ region with a Gaussian-like distribution and the large $q_T$ region with perturbatively calculated tails. They expressed the Gaussian width in terms of the measured average transverse momentum square $\langle q_T^2 \rangle$ and a perturbatively calculable correction $\Gamma(q_T^L)$, then used the nuclear broadening $\Delta\langle q_T^2 \rangle_{DY}$ (calculated from quark-gluon correlation functions via generalized QCD factorization) to obtain the hadron-nucleus spectrum. The nuclear dependence coefficient $\alpha(A,q_T)$ was then derived from the ratio of the hadron-nucleus to hadron-nucleon spectra.

## Key numbers

* The Drell-Yan averaged transverse momentum square in hadron-nucleon collisions, $\langle q_T^2 \rangle^{hN}_{DY}$, ranges from 1.5 GeV$^2$ to 1.8 GeV$^2$ for data at fixed target energies.
* The nuclear broadening $\Delta\langle q_T^2 \rangle_{DY}$ is proportional to $A^{1/3}$ for normal nuclear targets, with the form $\Delta\langle q_T^2 \rangle_{DY} = b_{DY} A^{1/3}$ where $b_{DY} = (4\pi^2\alpha_s/3) \lambda^2$.
* The Drell-Yan data from E772 show $\alpha(A,q_T=0)$ is less than one, and $\alpha(A,q_T)$ can be as large as 1.07.
* The contribution from the perturbative tail to the total cross section is less than one percent for both hadron-nucleon and hadron-nucleus collisions at fixed target energies.
* The correction term $\Gamma(q_T^L)^{hN}_{DY}$ is less than or about ten percent of $\langle q_T^2 \rangle^{hN}_{DY}$ depending on the value of $q_T^L$.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculations of nuclear dependence in the large $q_T$ region by Guo (1999) to the small $q_T$ region, where previous quantitative analyses were absent. The authors connect their small-$q_T$ results to the perturbatively calculated $\alpha(A,q_T)$ at large $q_T$ without free parameters, and demonstrate consistency with E772 data across all $q_T$. The approach enables predictions for J/$\psi$ production that are consistent with Fermilab E866 data, and provides a framework for distinguishing normal nuclear matter from a color deconfined medium through the $A$-dependence of $\alpha(A,q_T)$.
