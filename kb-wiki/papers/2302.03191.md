# Enhancement of dilepton production rate and electric conductivity around QCD critical point

> We investigate whether the soft mode that becomes massless at the QCD critical point (CP) causes an enhancement of the dilepton production rate (DPR) and the electric conductivity around the CP through the modification of the photon self-energy. The modification is described by the so-called Aslamazov-Larkin, Maki-Thompson and density of states terms, which have been taken into account in our previous study on the DPR near the color-superconducting phase transition, with a replacement of the diquark modes with the soft mode of the QCD CP. We show that the coupling of photons with the soft modes brings about an enhancement of the DPR in the low invariant-mass region and the conductivity near the CP, which would be observable in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The soft mode that becomes massless at the QCD critical point (CP) causes an enhancement of the dilepton production rate (DPR) in the low invariant-mass region and the electric conductivity near the CP, which would be observable in relativistic heavy-ion collisions; however, the enhancement is calculated using an approximate treatment of the soft mode propagator that accounts for its non-analytic behavior at the origin, unlike the diquark soft modes studied previously.

## Method

The authors adopt a 2-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with mean-field approximation to describe the QCD phase diagram and the soft mode of the QCD CP. They compute the modification of the photon self-energy by the soft mode using the Aslamazov-Larkin (AL), Maki-Thompson (MT), and density of states (DOS) terms, with vertex functions constructed to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity. The DPR and electric conductivity are then calculated from the retarded photon self-energy, with the MT and DOS terms found to cancel, leaving only the AL term contribution.

## Key numbers

- The QCD critical point is located at (T_c, mu_c) approximately equal to (46.757, 329.30) MeV in the NJL model mean-field approximation.
- The constituent quark mass M around the CP is approximately 185 MeV.
- The DPR enhancement from soft modes is significant for omega less than approximately 250 MeV, exceeding the massless free-quark gas contribution.
- The soft mode contribution to the invariant-mass spectrum dGamma/dm_ll^2 is conspicuous for m_ll less than approximately 150 MeV.
- The electric conductivity sigma grows as |T - T_c|^(-2/3) in the vicinity of the critical point in the present approximation.
- At mu = 0.99 mu_c, the conductivity shows a finite peak at T approximately 1.08 T_c; at mu = 1.01 mu_c, sigma shows a cusp-like behavior at T approximately 0.9 T_c.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on DPR enhancement near the two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) phase boundary (Nishimura, 2022), replacing the diquark soft modes with the soft mode of the QCD CP. Unlike the diquark propagator studied in that prior work, the QCD CP soft mode propagator is not analytic at the origin, requiring a different approximation scheme. The results suggest that dilepton measurements and conductivity extraction in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, NA61/SHINE, HADES, FAIR, and J-PARC-HI could serve as signals for the QCD critical point.
