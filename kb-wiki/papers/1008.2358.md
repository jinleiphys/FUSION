# Bound state solutions of the Dirac-Rosen-Morse potential with spin and pseudospin symmetry

> The energy spectra and the corresponding two- component spinor wavefunctions of the Dirac equation for the Rosen-Morse potential with spin and pseudospin symmetry are obtained. The $s-$wave ($κ= 0$ state) solutions for this problem are obtained by using the basic concept of the supersymmetric quantum mechanics approach and function analysis (standard approach) in the calculations. Under the spin symmetry and pseudospin symmetry, the energy equation and the corresponding two-component spinor wavefunctions for this potential and other special types of this potential are obtained. Extension of this result to $κ\neq 0$ state is suggested.

# Bound state solutions of the Dirac-Rosen-Morse potential with spin and pseudospin symmetry

## Key claim

The Dirac equation for the Rosen-Morse potential under spin and pseudospin symmetry conditions admits exact analytical bound-state solutions for the s-wave (kappa = 0 state), obtained via supersymmetric quantum mechanics and function analysis; the authors suggest but do not demonstrate that extension to kappa != 0 states is possible using an improved approximate analytical approach, noting that such solutions were independently obtained by others using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method during the final preparation of this work.

## Method

The authors start from the two-coupled first-order Dirac equations for the upper and lower spinor components, impose spin symmetry (Delta(r) = V(r) - S(r) = constant) or pseudospin symmetry (Sigma(r) = V(r) + S(r) = constant), and reduce the problem to a Schrodinger-like equation for the s-wave (ell = 0). They solve this equation using supersymmetric quantum mechanics: constructing a superpotential W(r) from the Riccati equation, identifying shape invariance between partner potentials, and summing the remainder terms to obtain the energy spectrum. The unnormalized radial wavefunctions are then derived by transforming the differential equation into a hypergeometric form via the substitution x = -exp(-2 alpha r).

## Key numbers

- The Rosen-Morse potential is defined as V(r) = -V1 sech^2(alpha r) + V2 tanh(alpha r), where V1 and V2 are the depth parameters and alpha is the range parameter.
- The spin symmetry energy equation is: M^2 - E_{n,-1}^2 - C_s (M - E_{n,-1}) = (M + E_{n,-1} - C_s)^2 V2^2 / [4 alpha^2 (beta - n)^2] + alpha^2 (beta - n)^2, where beta = (1/2)[-1 + sqrt(1 + 4 V1 (M + E_{n,-1} - C_s)/alpha^2)].
- The pseudospin symmetry energy equation is: M^2 - E_{n,1}^2 + C_ps (M + E_{n,1}) = (M - E_{n,1} + C_ps)^2 V2^2 / [4 alpha^2 (beta - n)^2] + alpha^2 (beta - n)^2, with beta = (1/2)[-1 + sqrt(1 - 4 (M - E_{n,1} + C_ps) V1 / alpha^2)].
- For the reflectionless-type potential (V2 = 0, V1 = (1/2) gamma(gamma+1)), the spin symmetry energy reduces to M^2 - E_{n,-1}^2 - C_s (M - E_{n,-1}) = alpha^2 (beta - n)^2, and the pseudospin symmetry energy to M^2 - E_{n,1}^2 + C_ps (M + E_{n,1}) = alpha^2 (beta - n)^2.
- The quantum number n runs over n = 0, 1, 2, ... for spin symmetry and n = 1, 2, 3, ... for pseudospin symmetry.

## Context

This work extends prior solutions of the Schrodinger equation for the Rosen-Morse potential (Rosen and Morse, 1932) and the Klein-Gordon equation for the Rosen-Morse well (Yi et al., 2004) into the relativistic Dirac framework under spin and pseudospin symmetry conditions. The spin symmetry condition (Delta(r) = constant) is relevant for mesons (Page et al., 2002), while pseudospin symmetry (Sigma(r) = constant) explains quasi-degeneracy of single-nucleon doublets in nuclear shell models (Arima et al., 1969; Hecht and Adler, 1969). The authors note that during final preparation of this work, solutions for kappa != 0 states were obtained using the Nikiforov-Uvarov method (Ikhdair and Sever, 2010), suggesting that the present s-wave results can be extended to nonzero orbital angular momentum via improved approximate analytical approaches for the centrifugal term.
