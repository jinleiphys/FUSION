# Production of doubly charmed baryons nearly at rest

> We investigate the production cross sections, momentum distributions and rapidity distributions for doubly charmed baryons which according to the intrinsic heavy quark mechanism are produced nearly at rest. These events should be measurable at fixed-target experiments like STAR@RHIC and AFTER@LHC.

# Production of doubly charmed baryons nearly at rest

## Key claim

The intrinsic heavy quark mechanism predicts that doubly charmed baryons can be produced nearly at rest from the target in fixed-target experiments, with cross sections of order tens of nanobarns, but only if the experimental acceptance covers low momentum (approximately 1.5 GeV/c) and small rapidity difference relative to the target; the mechanism does not contribute to negative Feynman-x and is not the leading production mechanism at modern colliders.

## Method

The authors apply the BHPS (Brodsky-Hoyer-Peterson-Sakai) model for intrinsic charm Fock states in the nucleon wavefunction, using the probability distribution for two heavy quark pairs as a function of light-front momentum fractions. They combine this with quark-hadron duality to obtain the doubly charmed baryon cross section from the cc pair production cross section, scaling by a color factor of 1/3 for the antitriplet state and an assumed fragmentation ratio of 0.2–0.3. The kinematic limits for production from the target are derived from energy-momentum conservation depending only on the masses of the baryon and target nucleus, independent of beam energy.

## Key numbers

- Production cross section for Xi_cc at STAR@RHIC with a 200 GeV/c proton beam: sigma(Xi_cc) ≈ (0.2–0.3) × 75 nb
- Production cross section for Xi_cc at SMOG@LHCb with sqrt(s) = 110 GeV: sigma(Xi_cc) ≈ (0.2–0.3) × 65 nb
- Mean momentum of doubly charmed baryons produced from the target: approximately 1.5 GeV/c
- Reinterpreted production ratio R = sigma(Xi_cc++) · Br(Xi_cc++ → Lambda_c K- pi+ pi+) / sigma(Lambda_c) in SELEX kinematics: R ∼ (2–3) × 10^{-3}
- Inelastic pp cross section at 200 GeV beam: sigma_inel ≈ 32 mb
- Inelastic pp cross section at sqrt(s) = 110 GeV: sigma_inel ≈ 37 mb
- Effective transverse c-quark mass: hat_m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Squared soft interaction scale: mu^2 ≈ 0.2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic charm mechanism previously applied to single charm production (Brodsky 1980, Brodsky 1981, Vogt 1995) to the production of doubly charmed baryons from the target, following a proposal by Brodsky (2017). It addresses the discrepancy between SELEX data (Mattson 2002) showing evidence for doubly charmed baryons and null results from Belle and LHCb, by reinterpreting the SELEX production ratio as an order of magnitude smaller than previously claimed. The results enable searches for doubly charmed baryons at existing fixed-target programs at STAR@RHIC and SMOG@LHCb, and at the future AFTER@LHC experiment, provided the detectors have acceptance for low-momentum particles near the target rapidity.
