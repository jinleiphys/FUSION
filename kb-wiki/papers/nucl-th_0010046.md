# Nuclear processes in magnetic fusion reactors with polarized fuel

> We consider the processes $d +d \to n +{^3He}$, $d +{^3He} \to p +{^4He}$, $d +{^3H} \to n +{^4He}$, ${^3He} +{^3He}\to p+p +{^4He}$, ${^3H} +{^3He}\to d +{^4He}$, with particular attention for applications in fusion reactors. After a model independent parametrization of the spin structure of the matrix elements for these processes at thermal colliding energies, in terms of partial amplitudes, we study polarization phenomena in the framework of a formalism of helicity amplitudes. The strong angular dependence of the final nuclei and of the polarization observables on the polarizations of the fuel components can be helpful in the design of the reactor shielding, blanket arrangement etc..We analyze also the angular dependence of the neutron polarization for the processes $\vec d +\vec d \to n +{^3He}$ and $\vec d +\vec {^3H} \to n +{^4He}$.

## Key claim

The paper provides a model-independent parametrization of the spin structure for several fusion-relevant nuclear reactions at thermal colliding energies, showing that the strong angular dependence of reaction products and polarization observables on the polarization states of the fuel components can be exploited for reactor design, but this analysis is strictly valid only under the assumption that S-state interaction of the colliding particles dominates, which holds for energies up to about 10 keV typical of fusion reactors.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave analysis of the threshold matrix elements for five reactions (d+d→n+³He, d+³He→p+⁴He, d+³H→n+⁴He, ³He+³He→p+p+⁴He, ³H+³He→d+⁴He) using a helicity-amplitude formalism with quantization axis along the magnetic field direction. They derived the spin structure in terms of a small number of allowed partial amplitudes based on conservation of total angular momentum, parity invariance, and Bose statistics for identical deuterons, then computed the angular dependence of differential cross sections and neutron polarization for various polarization configurations of the colliding nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For d+³H→n+⁴He, the integral coefficient A = (-|g_s|²+|g_d|²)/(|g_s|²+2|g_d|²) reaches a maximum value of 1/2 when the D-wave dominates (fusion resonance region, g_s=0)
- For d+d→n+³He with parallel polarizations (++ collisions), the ratio R_++ = 3/2 f, where f=2|g_d|²/(|g_s|²+2|g_d|²), giving limits 0 ≤ R_++ ≤ 3/2
- For d+d→n+³He with antiparallel polarizations (+- collisions), R_+- = (4-3f)/2, giving limits 1/2 ≤ R_+- ≤ 2
- The lowest ³/₂⁺ level of ⁵He has excitation energy E_x=16.75 MeV, located only 50 keV above the d+³H threshold, with a width of 76 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of d+³He→p+⁴He (Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson, 1998) to additional reactions relevant to magnetic fusion reactors with polarized fuel, following the suggestion by Kulsrud (1982) to use spin-dependent fusion rates for reactor optimization. The paper provides a formalism that corrects and generalizes earlier work by Kulsrud (1982) on the angular dependence of cross sections, showing that the earlier expressions coincide only for the special case of pure D-wave resonance. The results enable design optimization of reactor shielding and blanket arrangements, and can guide the complete experiments needed to determine the threshold amplitudes, which require measurements of polarized beam on polarized target.
