# Anisotropic flow in event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic simulations of sqrt(s_{NN})=200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> We calculate flow observables with the NeXSPheRIO ideal hydrodynamic model and make the first comparison to the complete set of mid-rapidity flow measurements made by the PHENIX collaboration in top energy Au+Au collisions. A simultaneous calculation of v_2, v_3, v_4, and the first event-by-event calculation of quadrangular flow defined with respect to the v_2 event plane (v_4{Psi_2}) gives good agreement with measured values, including the dependence on both transverse momentum and centrality. This provides confirmation that the collision system is indeed well described as a quark-gluon plasma with an extremely small viscosity, and that correlations are dominantly generated from collective effects. In addition we present a prediction for v_5.

## Key claim

Ideal hydrodynamic simulations with fluctuating initial conditions can simultaneously reproduce all measured mid-rapidity flow observables (v2, v3, v4, and v4{Psi2}) in top-energy Au+Au collisions, but this requires negligible viscosity only for the specific NeXus initial conditions used; other initial condition models coupled with viscous hydrodynamics might also fit the data with a small but finite shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO code, which solves ideal relativistic hydrodynamics equations with fluctuating initial conditions from the NeXus event generator. They generated 110 NeXus events in 5% centrality classes up to 60% centrality, solved hydrodynamics independently for each event, and emitted particles via a Monte-Carlo generator at freeze-out. Flow coefficients were calculated by averaging over approximately 6×10^5 particles per event in the pseudorapidity interval -1<η<1, with results presented as both mean and root-mean-square averages to illustrate the effect of event-by-event fluctuations on experimental analyses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum event plane resolution for v2 is 0.74 (around 30% centrality), with much smaller resolution for v3 and v4, implying α>1.81 in the relation v_n{Psi_n}≈⟨v_n^α⟩^(1/α)
- For Gaussian flow fluctuations, the ratio of rms to mean is √(4/π)≈1.13 for v3 and v5, and closer to 1 for v2 and v4
- v4{Psi2} is smaller than v4{Psi4} typically by a factor of 2 for peripheral collisions and by a factor of 5 for central collisions
- Ideal hydrodynamics predicts v4/v2^2≈1/2 at high pT for a single event
- For the most central bin (0-10%), the measured v4/v2^2 is between 1.5 and 2, while v2 fluctuations alone would predict v4/v2^2≈1

## Context

This work extends earlier NeXSPheRIO studies that reproduced rapidity and transverse momentum spectra (Qian 2007), elliptic flow v2 (Andrade 2008), and directed flow v1 (Gardim 2011). It provides the first simultaneous calculation of all measured mid-rapidity flow observables, confirming the paradigm that collective effects generate all long-range correlations in the soft sector. The results enable future work to determine an upper bound on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s, with the authors noting that Schenke (2011) found η/s=0.08 needed with Glauber initial conditions to give reasonable agreement, though that calculation underpredicted v3 for central collisions.
