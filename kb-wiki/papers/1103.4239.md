# Electromagnetic field evolution in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The hadron string dynamics (HSD) model is generalized to include the creation and evolution of retarded electromagnetic fields as well as the influence of the magnetic and electric fields on the quasiparticle propagation. The time-space structure of the fields is analyzed in detail for non-central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200 GeV. It is shown that the created magnetic field is highly inhomogeneous but in the central region of the overlapping nuclei it changes relatively weakly in the transverse direction. For the impact parameter $b=$10 fm the maximal magnetic field - perpendicularly to the reaction plane - is obtained of order $eB_y/m_π^2\sim$5 for a very short time $\sim$ 0.2 fm/c, which roughly corresponds to the time of a maximal overlap of the colliding nuclei. We find that at any time the location of the maximum in the $eB_y$ distribution correlates with that of the energy density of the created particles. In contrast, the electric field distribution, being also highly inhomogeneous, has a minimum in the center of the overlap region. Furthermore, the field characteristics are presented as a function of the collision energy and the centrality of the collisions. To explore the effect of the back reaction of the fields on hadronic observables a comparison of HSD results with and without fields is exemplified. Our actual calculations show no noticeable influence of the electromagnetic fields - created in heavy-ion collisions - on the effect of the electric charge separation with respect to the reaction plane.

## Key claim

The authors generalize the hadron string dynamics (HSD) model to include retarded electromagnetic fields and their back-reaction on quasiparticle propagation, but their actual calculations show no noticeable influence of these fields on the electric charge separation with respect to the reaction plane (the chiral magnetic effect signal).

## Method

The HSD transport model, based on Kadanoff-Baym equations, was extended by coupling the quasiparticle equations of motion to retarded Lienard-Wiechert potentials computed from the charge and current distributions of all moving particles. The electromagnetic fields were calculated on a space grid using the Monte-Carlo parallel ensemble method, with self-interaction excluded for charges within the same Lorentz-contracted hadron volume, and the Lorentz force was included in the quasiparticle propagation.

## Key numbers

* Maximal magnetic field perpendicular to the reaction plane: \(eB_y/m_\pi^2 \sim 5\) for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and impact parameter \(b=10\) fm.
* Duration of maximal magnetic field: \(\sim 0.2\) fm/c, corresponding to the time of maximal nuclear overlap.
* Maximal electric field in the overlap region: \(eE_x/m_\pi^2 \approx 0.5\text{--}0.6\) for \(x \approx 1\text{--}3\) fm, about a factor of 10 smaller than the maximal magnetic field.
* When changing impact parameter from \(b=10\) to 2 fm, the maximal \(eB_y\) decreases by a factor of 5.
* Maximal energy density in the center of the colliding system: \(> 50\) GeV/fm\(^3\) at the moment of maximal overlap.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev et al. (2007), which treated nuclei as infinitely thin pancake-like layers and used a heuristic rapidity-degradation function, and by the UrQMD model calculations of Skokov et al. (2009), which did not include back-reaction. The HSD results show lower magnetic fields than the Kharzeev et al. estimates, especially at later times, due to more realistic treatment of retardation, finite nuclear size, and dynamical charge evolution. The finding that electromagnetic fields have no noticeable effect on charge separation observables provides a baseline for interpreting chiral magnetic effect measurements by the STAR Collaboration (2009) and constrains the role of electromagnetic backgrounds in searches for local parity violation.
