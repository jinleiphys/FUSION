# Error estimates on Nuclear Binding Energies from Nucleon-Nucleon uncertainties

> Despite great theoretical efforts the NN interaction can only be determined with a finite precision, implying an error upper bound for nuclear masses. We analyze for the first time the problem of estimating the systematic errors related to the form of the potential and their impact on nuclear binding. To this end we exploit the concept of coarse grained interactions to typical nuclear wavelengths. Our estimate gives an error of 0.1-0.4 MeV for the binding energy per particle and paves the way for ab initio calculations tailored to such a precision.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first theoretical a priori estimate of systematic errors in nuclear binding energies arising from uncertainties in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, finding that the binding energy per particle carries an intrinsic uncertainty of 0.1-0.4 MeV due to the finite precision with which the NN force can be determined from scattering data.

## Method

The authors exploit the concept of coarse-grained interactions, representing the NN potential as delta-shells at discrete radii up to 3 fm, with parameters fitted to reproduce the spread of phase shifts obtained from seven different high-quality NN potentials (CD Bonn, Nijm-I, Nijm-II, Reid93, AV18, and others) that all fit scattering data with chi-squared per degree of freedom near unity. They propagate the systematic uncertainties in the phase shifts through simple harmonic oscillator shell model calculations for closed-shell nuclei, using Talmi-Moshinsky coefficients to relate the two-body interaction uncertainty to binding energy changes in 3H, 4He, 16O, and 40Ca. For nuclear matter, they use a Skyrme-type effective interaction to estimate the uncertainty from the coarse-grained potential parameters.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle uncertainty: Delta B/A = 0.1-0.4 MeV for nuclei with 4 <= A <= 208
- For triton (3H): Delta B_3/3 = 0.07-0.085 MeV from coarse-grained propagation, compared to 0.11 MeV from Faddeev calculations using seven different potentials
- For alpha particle (4He): Delta B_4/4 = 0.10-0.13 MeV
- For 16O: Delta B_16/16 = 0.26 MeV
- For 40Ca: Delta B_40/40 = 0.32 MeV
- For deuteron: Delta B_2H/2 = 0.1 MeV (compared to experimental precision of 0.005 KeV)
- Coarse-grained Skyrme parameter: t_0 = -0.92(1) GeV fm^3, with Delta t_0 = 10 MeV fm^3
- For nuclear matter at saturation density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3: Delta B/A = 0.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the error analysis tradition of the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993) by addressing systematic rather than statistical uncertainties in NN potentials, which had previously been neglected despite the existence of multiple potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijmegen potentials) all fitting data with chi-squared per degree of freedom near unity. The approach builds on the concept of coarse-grained interactions (Perez 2011, 2012) and V_low k methods (Bogner 2003, 2009), and enables ab initio calculations to be tailored to a precision of 0.1-0.4 MeV per particle, while also providing a lower bound on total uncertainties that will propagate into three-body and higher-body forces.
