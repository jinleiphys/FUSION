# Kaon-Nucleon scattering states and potentials in the Skyrme model

> We study the (anti)kaon nucleon interaction in the Skyrme model. The kaon field is introduced as a fluctuation around the rotating Skyrmion for the nucleon. As an extension of our previous work, we study scattering states and examine phase shifts in various kaon-nucleon channels. Then we study the interaction, where we find that it consists of central and spin-orbit components for isospin channels, $I = 0, 1$, with energy dependence and nonlocality. The interaction is then fitted to a Shrödinger equivalent local potential for s- and p-waves.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model, using a "variation after projection" approach where the kaon fluctuates around a rotating (physically quantized) nucleon rather than a static hedgehog, yields a kaon-nucleon interaction that is energy-dependent and nonlocal; the resulting Schrödinger-equivalent local potential can be fitted by Gaussian-type functions for s- and p-waves, but the model's quantitative predictions for scattering lengths are larger than experimental values, likely because the calculation omits the physically important πΣ channels.

## Method

The authors introduce the kaon field as a small fluctuation around a rotating Skyrmion (the soliton that has already been collectively quantized to represent the physical nucleon), expand the Skyrme Lagrangian (including the Wess-Zumino term) to second order in the kaon field, and derive the equation of motion for each partial wave. They compute phase shifts for s-wave (J^P = 1/2^-) and p-wave (J^P = 3/2^+) kaon-nucleon scattering states for three parameter sets of the pion decay constant F_π and the Skyrme parameter e, then construct a Schrödinger-equivalent local potential by dividing the full nonlocal operator by the kaon wave function, and fit the resulting seven potential components (central isospin-independent and isospin-dependent, spin-orbit isospin-independent and isospin-dependent, from both normal Skyrme terms and the Wess-Zumino term, plus a centrifugal term) to sums of Gaussian-type functions.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy for the anti-kaon-nucleon (K̅N) J^P = 1/2^-, I = 0 channel: 20.6 MeV for Parameter set A (F_π = 205 MeV, e = 4.67); 32.2 MeV for set B (F_π = 186 MeV, e = 4.82); 81.3 MeV for set C (F_π = 129 MeV, e = 5.45).
* For Parameter set B, a very shallow bound state exists for the K̅N I = 1 channel with binding energy 0.2 MeV.
* Scattering lengths for K̅N (J^P = 1/2^-) from Parameter set A: a_0 = 1.56 fm (I = 0 channel), a_1 = -3.38 fm (I = 1 channel), giving a combined K̅N scattering length a_K̅N = -0.91 fm.
* The kaon mass m_K is fixed at the experimental value of 495 MeV throughout.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous bound-state study (Ezoe and Hosaka, 2016) by computing scattering phase shifts and constructing local potentials, using a "variation after projection" approach that differs from the original Callan-Klebanov bound-state method (Callan and Klebanov, 1985) where projection occurs after variation. The resulting potentials are intended for use in few-body nuclear systems with anti-kaons, but the authors note that quantitative comparison with experiment requires the future inclusion of πΣ channels, which are known to be important for describing the Λ(1405) resonance (Dalitz and Tuan, 1960; Dalitz, 1961) and for realistic scattering lengths from other theoretical approaches.
