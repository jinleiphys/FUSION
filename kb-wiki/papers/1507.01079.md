# Halos in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei with covariant density functional theory in continuum

> The covariant density functional theory with a few number of parameters has been widely used to describe the ground-state and excited-state properties for the nuclei all over the nuclear chart. In order to describe exotic properties of unstable nuclei, the contribution of the continuum and its coupling with bound states should be treated properly. In this Topical Review, the development of the covariant density functional theory in continuum will be introduced, including the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory, the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory in continuum, and the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum. Then the descriptions and predictions of the neutron halo phenomena in both spherical and deformed nuclei will be reviewed. The diffuseness of the nuclear potentials, nuclear shapes and density distributions, and the impact of the pairing correlations on nuclear size will be discussed.

## Key claim

This Topical Review argues that covariant density functional theory (CDFT) in continuum, when properly treating the coupling between bound states and the continuum via Bogoliubov transformation, can describe and predict neutron halo phenomena in both spherical and deformed medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, though the authors note that the neutron drip line is experimentally known only up to oxygen (Z=8) and that reaching it for A~100 remains a future goal.

## Method

The authors review three implementations of CDFT in continuum: the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory for spherical nuclei, the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (RHFBc) which includes exchange terms, and the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) for deformed systems. These approaches solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in coordinate space or in a Woods-Saxon basis, using either a zero-range density-dependent pairing force or the finite-range Gogny force, to self-consistently treat the coupling between bound states and the continuum.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear DFT has predicted about 7,000 nuclides are bound with respect to neutron or proton emission (Erler 2012, Afanasjev 2013, Agbemava 2014); when continuum contribution is included, this number may exceed 10,000 (Qu 2013, from O to Ti isotopes).
* Only 288 of the expected thousands of nuclides are stable or have half-lives longer than the expected life of the Solar System.
* The neutron drip line is known experimentally only up to oxygen (Z=8); reaching the neutron drip line up to mass number A~100 is a goal for new generation RIB facilities.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the RCHB theory (Meng 1996, Poschl 1997, Meng 1998) and the extensive review by Meng (2006) by incorporating recent developments in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (Zhou 2010, Li 2012, Chen 2012) and relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (Long 2010). It enables future studies of giant halos predicted in Zr and Ca isotopes (Meng 1998, Meng 2002), shape decoupling between core and halo in deformed nuclei (Zhou 2010, Li 2012), and the extension of the nuclear landscape beyond current predictions.
