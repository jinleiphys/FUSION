# Examination of the H dibaryon within a chiral constituent quark model

> We perform a coupled--channel calculation of the H dibaryon within a chiral constituent quark model. The problem is solved within a quark model constrained by the experimental data of strangeness --1 and --2 two--baryon systems. We examine in detail the role played by the different contributions of the interacting potential as well as the number of coupled channels considered. Special attention has been payed to the parameter dependence, flavor symmetry breaking and spatial configurations. The value extracted for the binding energy of the H dibaryon, being compatible with the restrictions imposed by the Nagara event, falls within a plausible extrapolation of recent lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The H dibaryon binding energy extracted from a chiral constituent quark model constrained by strangeness -1 and -2 experimental data is compatible with the Nagara event restriction (M_H > 2223.7 MeV) and falls within a plausible extrapolation of recent lattice QCD results, but the binding depends sensitively on the scalar meson exchange parameters and the inclusion of coupled channels.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger calculation for the strangeness -2 two-baryon system with quantum numbers (T,S)=(0,0), using a chiral constituent quark model (CCQM) with two variants: Model I (one-sigma exchange) and Model II (full scalar nonet including sigma_0 and scalar octet). The baryon-baryon potentials were derived from the underlying quark-quark interaction via a Born-Oppenheimer approximation, and the coupled-channel problem was solved for the three channels Lambda-Lambda, N-Xi, and Sigma-Sigma, including S-wave and D-wave couplings.

## Key numbers

* Single-channel binding energy of Lambda-Lambda: 1.9 MeV in Model I, no bound state in Model II (Table I).
* Three-channel binding energy of the H dibaryon: 10.0 MeV in Model I, 7.0 MeV in Model II (Table II).
* Two-channel (Lambda-Lambda + N-Xi) binding energy: 8.7 MeV in Model I, 1.6 MeV in Model II (Table II).
* Threshold energies: Lambda-Lambda at 2231 MeV, N-Xi at 2257 MeV, Sigma-Sigma at 2381 MeV (Section III).
* Probabilities from the three-channel calculation in Model II: P(Lambda-Lambda) = 0.177, P(N-Xi) = 0.446, P(Sigma-Sigma) = 0.377 (Section III.D).
* B_H ranges approximately from 4 MeV to 12 MeV for m_sigma0 in the range (3.2, 3.6) fm^{-1} (Section III.B).
* Flavor symmetry breaking reduces binding by about 45%, giving B_H = 10.2 MeV in the exact SU(3) limit (Section III.C).

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model previously applied to strangeness -1 systems (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2007) and strangeness -2 scattering cross sections (Valcarce et al., 2010) to the H dibaryon bound state problem. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations from NPLQCD (Beane et al., 2010) and HAL QCD (Inoue et al., 2010), which found a bound H dibaryon at non-physical pion masses, and with the Nagara event constraint (Takahashi et al., 2001) giving M_H > 2223.7 MeV. The study enables future comparisons with the J-PARC E07 experiment and provides a framework for understanding the role of scalar meson exchange and flavor symmetry breaking in dibaryon binding.
