# The quark mean field model with pion and gluon corrections

> The properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei are studied within the quark mean field (QMF) model by taking the effects of pion and gluon into account at the quark level. The nucleon is described as the combination of three constituent quarks confined by a harmonic oscillator potential. To satisfy the spirit of QCD theory, the contributions of pion and gluon on the nucleon structure are treated in second-order perturbation theory. For the nuclear many-body system, nucleons interact with each other by exchanging mesons between quarks. With different constituent quark mass, $m_q$, we determine three parameter sets about the coupling constants between mesons and quarks, named as QMF-NK1, QMF-NK2, and QMF-NK3 by fitting the ground-state properties of several closed-shell nuclei. It is found that all of the three parameter sets can give satisfactory description on properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei, meanwhile they can also predict the larger neutron star mass around $2.3M_\odot$ without the hyperon degrees of freedom.

# The quark mean field model with pion and gluon corrections

## Key claim
The quark mean field (QMF) model, when augmented with pion and gluon corrections treated in second-order perturbation theory at the quark level, can simultaneously reproduce ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei and predict neutron star masses around 2.3 solar masses without hyperon degrees of freedom, provided that non-linear sigma and omega meson self-interactions are included at the hadron level.

## Method
The nucleon is constructed from three constituent quarks confined by a harmonic oscillator potential, with pion and gluon exchange contributions added via second-order perturbation theory to satisfy QCD chiral symmetry and short-range exchange. The effective nucleon mass in medium is then inserted into a relativistic mean field Lagrangian that includes non-linear sigma and omega meson terms, and the coupled equations for nucleon and meson fields are solved self-consistently for finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. Three parameter sets (QMF-NK1, QMF-NK2, QMF-NK3) are obtained by fitting the binding energies per nucleon and charge radii of 40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb using constituent quark masses of 250, 300, and 350 MeV respectively.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: 0.150–0.154 fm^-3 across QMF-NK1/2/3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.3 MeV for all three parameter sets
- Incompressibility K0: 322–328 MeV across QMF-NK1/2/3
- Symmetry energy J: 30.6 MeV (QMF-NK1), 32.9 MeV (QMF-NK2), 33.6 MeV (QMF-NK3)
- Effective nucleon mass ratio M*_N/M_N at saturation: 0.70 (QMF-NK1), 0.66 (QMF-NK2), 0.64 (QMF-NK3)
- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.25–2.38 solar masses across the three parameter sets
- Binding energy per nucleon for 208Pb: 7.90 MeV (QMF-NK3) vs. experimental 7.87 MeV
- Charge radius for 208Pb: 5.50 fm (QMF-NK3) vs. experimental 5.50 fm
- Spin-orbit splitting for 40Ca proton 1d5/2–1d3/2: -5.1 MeV (QMF-NK3) vs. experimental -7.2 MeV
- Spin-orbit splitting for 208Pb neutron 2f7/2–2f5/2: -1.9 MeV (QMF-NK3) vs. experimental -1.8 MeV

## Context
This work extends the quark mean field approach of Shen and Toki (2000) by incorporating pion and gluon corrections that were previously omitted, and improves upon the MQMC model of Barik et al. (2013) by adding non-linear sigma and omega meson terms. The MQMC model without non-linear terms gave a total energy difference of about 80 MeV for 208Pb, whereas the present QMF-NK3 set reduces this discrepancy to about 6 MeV. The results enable future inclusion of strange quark degrees of freedom to study hyperon degrees of freedom in neutron stars, addressing the "hyperon puzzle" of massive neutron star observations.
