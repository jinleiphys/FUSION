# A new determination of the $γπ\to ππ$ anomalous amplitude via $π^- e^- \to π^- e^- π^{0}$ data

> We discuss the reaction $π^- e^- \to π^- e^- π^{0}$ with the purpose of obtaining information on the $γπ\to ππ$ anomalous amplitude ${\cal F}_{3π}$. We compare a full calculation at ${\cal O}(p^6)$ in chiral perturbation theory and various phenomenological predictions with the existing data of Amendolia {\em et al}. By integrating our theory results using Monte Carlo techniques we obtain $σ= 2.05 $ nb at ${\cal O}(p^6)$ and $σ= 2.17 $ nb after including the dominant electromagnetic correction. Both results are in good agreement with the experimental cross section of $σ= (2.11 \pm 0.47)$ nb. On the basis of the ChPT results one would extract from the the experimental cross section as amplitudes ${\cal F}_{3π}^{(0)\rm extr} = (9.9 \pm 1.1)$ GeV$^{-3}$ and ${\cal F}_{3π}^{(0)\rm extr} = (9.6 \pm 1.1)$ GeV$^{-3}$, respectively, which have to be compared with the low-energy theorem ${\cal F}_{3π}=e/(4π^2 F_π^3)=9.72 {GeV}^{-3}$. We emphasize the need for new data to allow for a comparison of experimental and theoretical distributions and to obtain ${\cal F}_{3π}$ with smaller uncertainty.

# A new determination of the γπ → ππ anomalous amplitude via π⁻ e⁻ → π⁻ e⁻ π⁰ data

## Key claim

The chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculation at O(p⁶) with vector-meson-dominance estimates for the unknown low-energy constants, together with the dominant electromagnetic correction, yields cross sections consistent with the existing 36-event data of Amendolia et al. (1985), but the extracted amplitude F₃π⁽⁰⁾ᵉˣᵗʳ = (9.9 ± 1.1) GeV⁻³ (or (9.6 ± 1.1) GeV⁻³ with electromagnetic corrections) agrees with the low-energy theorem value of 9.72 GeV⁻³ only within the large experimental uncertainty, and the authors emphasize that new high-statistics data are needed to test the theoretical distributions and reduce the uncertainty.

## Method

The authors calculated the γ*π⁻ → π⁰π⁻ amplitude F₃π(s₂, t₂, u₂; q²) at O(p⁶) in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, including one-loop diagrams with one Wess-Zumino-Witten vertex and tree-level contributions from the O(p⁶) anomalous Lagrangian, with the unknown low-energy constants estimated via vector-meson saturation. They also considered several phenomenological models (Terent'ev, Rudaz VMD pole model, and a VMD model with final-state ππ rescattering) and integrated the fourfold differential cross section for π⁻e⁻ → π⁻e⁻π⁰ over the experimental kinematic region using Monte Carlo techniques, comparing the results with the data of Amendolia et al. (1985).

## Key numbers

- ChPT at O(p⁶) total cross section: σ = 2.05 nb
- ChPT at O(p⁶) plus dominant electromagnetic correction: σ = 2.17 nb
- Experimental cross section from Amendolia et al. (1985): σ = (2.11 ± 0.47) nb
- Extracted amplitude from ChPT (without EM correction): F₃π⁽⁰⁾ᵉˣᵗʳ = (9.9 ± 1.1) GeV⁻³
- Extracted amplitude from ChPT (with EM correction): F₃π⁽⁰⁾ᵉˣᵗʳ = (9.6 ± 1.1) GeV⁻³
- Low-energy theorem prediction: F₃π = e/(4π²Fπ³) = 9.72 GeV⁻³
- Previous Serpukhov extraction by Antipov et al. (1986) assuming constant amplitude: F₃πᵉˣᵖ = (12.9 ± 0.9 ± 0.5) GeV⁻³
- Re-analysis by Holstein (1995) with kinematic dependence: F₃πᵉˣᵖ = (11.9 ± 0.9 ± 0.5) GeV⁻³
- Two-loop dispersive analysis by Hannah (2001): F₃πᵉˣᵖ = (11.4 ± 1.3) GeV⁻³
- Hannah result with radiative corrections by Ametller et al. (2001): F₃πᵉˣᵖ = (10.7 ± 1.2) GeV⁻³
- Incident pion beam energy in Amendolia et al. (1985): 300 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of the γπ → ππ anomalous amplitude beyond the earlier O(p⁶) ChPT calculations of Bijnens et al. (1989) and the dispersive two-loop analysis of Hannah (2001), and applies it specifically to the π⁻e⁻ → π⁻e⁻π⁰ reaction rather than the Primakoff reaction on nuclear targets used by Antipov et al. (1986) at Serpukhov. The authors note that the existing data from Amendolia et al. (1985) consist of only 36 events and lack detailed comparisons of experimental and theoretical distributions, and they emphasize that new high-statistics data from the CERN COMPASS experiment (Baum et al., 1996) with 190-300 GeV pion beams would enable a determination of the form factor for πγ* → ππ⁰ with smaller uncertainty, as well as allow kaon-beam measurements to test the expected Nc dependence via three-flavor processes as proposed by Bar and Wiese (2001).
