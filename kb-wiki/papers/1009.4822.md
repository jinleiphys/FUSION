# Gradient corrections to the local density approximation for trapped superfluid Fermi gases

> Two species superfluid Fermi gas is investigated on the BCS side up to the Feshbach resonance. Using the Greens's function technique gradient corrections are calculated to the generalized Thomas-Fermi theory including Cooper pairing. Their relative magnitude is found to be measured by the small parameter $(d/R_{TF})^4$, where $d$ is the oscillator length of the trap potential and $R_{TF}$ is the radial extension of the density $n$ in the Thomas-Fermi approximation. In particular at the Feshbach resonance the universal %constant $A_{TF}$ has the %correction in the center $A=A_{TF}+A_2(d/R_{TF})^4+\...$ corrections to the local density approximation are calculated and a universal prefactor $κ_W=7/27$ is derived for the von Weizsäcker type correction $κ_W(\hbar^2/2m)(\nabla^2 n^{1/2}/n^{1/2})$.

## Key claim

Gradient corrections to the local density approximation for trapped superfluid Fermi gases are derived using Green's function techniques, with the relative magnitude of these corrections measured by the small parameter \((d/R_{TF})^4\) (where \(d\) is the oscillator length and \(R_{TF}\) the Thomas-Fermi radius). At the Feshbach resonance, a universal prefactor \(\kappa_W = 7/27\) is obtained for the von Weizsacker type correction, though the derivation assumes the mean-field BCS model which neglects self-consistent Hartree-type terms.

## Method

The authors used the Green's function technique to expand the normal and anomalous Green's functions in a formal power series in \(\hbar\), then derived gradient corrections up to second order in \(\hbar\) to the generalized Thomas-Fermi theory that includes Cooper pairing. They applied the mean-field BCS (MF-BCS) model with a contact interaction potential \(v(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{r}') = (4\pi\hbar^2 a/m)\delta(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}')\) and evaluated the second-order corrections to the density and gap equations for a general external potential, specializing to the unitary limit at the Feshbach resonance.

## Key numbers

- The small parameter governing gradient corrections is \((d/R_{TF})^4\), where \(d\) is the oscillator length of the trap and \(R_{TF}\) is the radial extension of the density in the Thomas-Fermi approximation.
- At the Feshbach resonance, the universal prefactor for the von Weizsacker type correction is \(\kappa_W = 7/27\).
- The zeroth-order density is given by \(g_0(U(\mathbf{R}),\Delta(\mathbf{R})) = \frac{1}{4\pi^2}\left(\frac{2m\alpha(\mathbf{R})}{\hbar^2}\right)^{3/2} j_1(\Delta(\mathbf{R})/\alpha(\mathbf{R}))\), where \(\alpha(\mathbf{R}) = \mu - U(\mathbf{R})\) is the local chemical potential.
- For negative scattering length, the zeroth-order gap equation yields \(f_0(U(\mathbf{R}),\Delta(\mathbf{R})) = \Delta(\mathbf{R}) \frac{2}{\pi}|a|\sqrt{\frac{2m\alpha(\mathbf{R})}{\hbar^2}} \, i_1(\Delta(\mathbf{R})/\alpha(\mathbf{R}))\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized Thomas-Fermi theory for superfluid Fermi gases originally developed by Szepfalusy (1964) and applies the gradient expansion method previously used for normal electron gases by Baraff and Borowitz (1961) and for superconductors by Werthamer (1963). The results enable systematic corrections to the widely used local density approximation for trapped Fermi gases, particularly relevant for Monte-Carlo simulations with moderate particle numbers where Thomas-Fermi theory requires corrections, and extend the concept of universality at unitarity as discussed by Csordas (2007).
