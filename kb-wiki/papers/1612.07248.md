# Quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions: an open quantum system approach

> We address the evolution of heavy-quarkonium states in an expanding quark-gluon plasma by implementing effective field theory techniques in the framework of open quantum systems. In this setting we compute the nuclear modification factors for quarkonia that are $S$-wave Coulombic bound states in a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma. The calculation is performed at an accuracy that is leading-order in the heavy-quark density expansion and next-to-leading order in the multipole expansion. The quarkonium density-matrix evolution equations can be written in the Lindblad form, and, hence, they account for both dissociation and recombination. Thermal mass shifts, thermal widths and the Lindblad equation itself depend on only two non-perturbative parameters: the heavy-quark momentum diffusion coefficient and its dispersive counterpart. Finally, by numerically solving the Lindblad equation, we provide results for the $Υ(1S)$ and $Υ(2S)$ suppression.

## Key claim
The paper derives quarkonium evolution equations in a strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma that can be written in Lindblad form, accounting for both dissociation and recombination, but the numerical results for bottomonium suppression depend crucially on two non-perturbative parameters (κ and γ) for which only κ has a lattice determination while γ remains unconstrained.

## Method
The authors use potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at next-to-leading order in the multipole expansion to describe heavy quark-antiquark pairs in a strongly-coupled plasma, assuming the hierarchy m ≫ 1/a₀ ∼ mαₛ ≫ πT ∼ m_D. They derive the evolution equations for color-singlet and color-octet density matrices, write them in Lindblad form, and numerically solve them for bottomonium states using Bjorken's model for the plasma expansion with initial temperatures from the Glauber model.

## Key numbers
- Heavy-quark momentum diffusion coefficient range from lattice QCD: 1.8 ≲ κ/T³ ≲ 3.4 (pure SU(3) plasma at about 1.5 T_c)
- For Υ(1S): 3.0 GeV⁻² T³ ≲ Γ_Υ(1S) ≲ 5.7 GeV⁻² T³, giving thermal width around 100 MeV at T ≈ 300 MeV
- Cross-over temperature T_c ≈ 150 MeV from lattice QCD
- Initial temperature for 0-10% centrality: T₀ = 471 MeV at t₀ = 0.6 fm
- For 30-50% centrality: R_AA(1S) = 0.23⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₀₇ and R_AA(2S)/R_AA(1S) = 0.24 ± 0.09
- For 50-100% centrality: R_AA(1S) = 0.80 ± 0.05 and R_AA(2S)/R_AA(1S) = 0.59 ± 0.10

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to quarkonium in a medium, previously applied to weakly-coupled plasmas by Brambilla et al. (2008, 2010, 2011, 2013) and Escobedo (2013), to the strongly-coupled regime relevant for LHC conditions. It differs from earlier open quantum system treatments by Akamatsu (2011) and Katz (2015) by including both color-singlet and color-octet structures, which is essential for non-abelian plasmas. The results enable comparison with CMS bottomonium suppression data at 2.76 TeV (Chatrchyan 2012) and provide a framework that can incorporate any hydrodynamic evolution model and initial conditions.
