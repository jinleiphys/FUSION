# Baryon Number Transport via Gluonic Junctions

> A novel non-perturbative gluon junction mechanism is introduced within the HIJING/B nuclear collision event generator to calculate baryon number transport and hyperon production in pA and AA collisions. This gluonic mechanism can account for the observed large mid-rapidity valence baryon yield in Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV and predicts high initial baryon densities at RHIC. However, the highly enhanced Lambda-Lambdabar yield and the baryon transverse momentum flow observed in this reaction can only be partially described.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a non-perturbative gluonic junction mechanism within the HIJING/B event generator that can account for the observed large mid-rapidity valence baryon yield in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV, but this mechanism can only partially describe the highly enhanced Lambda-AntiLambda yield and baryon transverse momentum flow observed in the same reaction.

## Method

The authors implemented Kharzeev's baryon junction model (with Regge intercept α_M^J_0(0) ≈ 1/2) into the HIJING/B Monte Carlo event generator using a "Y" string configuration for excited baryons, where baryon number is transported via three gluonic Wilson lines linking valence quarks. For reactions without junction exchange, standard diquark-quark (qq-q) strings were used, and the junction exchange was only allowed when the invariant mass of the excited "Y" configuration exceeded 5 GeV. The model assumes that once a baryon junction is exchanged in multiple collisions, it remains stopped in subsequent soft interactions, and no final state interactions were included.

## Key numbers

- Junction exchange cross section σ_BJ = 18 mb was taken to reproduce valence proton data from p+p collisions at 400 GeV/c incident momentum (√s_0 = 27.4 GeV)
- At SPS energies, the kinematic constraint (invariant mass ≥ 5 GeV) reduced the effective junction cross section to approximately 9 mb
- The inverse slope parameter T for proton m_T distributions was found to be T ≈ 151 MeV for p+Pb and T ≈ 168 MeV for S+Pb at 200 GeV/c, compared to measured values T = 195 ± 5 MeV for p+Pb and T = 256 ± 4 MeV for S+Pb from the NA44 collaboration
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV), HIJING/B predicts initial baryon density ρ(τ_0) ≈ 2ρ_0 ≈ 0.3/fm^3
- The strangeness content of junction events is enhanced by a factor of 3 compared to standard strings
- The multiplicity of junction events is enhanced by a factor of 5/4 compared to standard strings

## Context

This work extends the baryon junction mechanism proposed by Kharzeev (1996) and contrasts with the diquark breaking mechanism in the dual parton model (DPM) by Kopeliovich and Zhakharov (1989), which uses a different Regge intercept (α ≈ 1) and predicts a constant, energy-independent annihilation cross section of 1-2 mb. The HIJING/B implementation enables predictions for baryon stopping and hyperon production at RHIC energies, and the authors suggest that the inability of this initial-state mechanism to fully account for observed transverse baryon flow provides evidence for the importance of final state interactions, while also noting that measurements of the impact parameter dependence of baryon stopping could test whether the anomalous J/ψ suppression threshold at b ≈ 8 fm is correlated with the onset of greater baryon stopping.
