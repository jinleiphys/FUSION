# Contribution of the massive photon decay channel to neutrino cooling of neutron stars

> We consider massive photon decay reactions via intermediate states of electron-electron-holes and proton-proton-holes into neutrino-antineutrino pairs in the course of neutron star cooling. These reactions may become operative in hot neutron stars in the region of proton pairing where the photon due to the Higgs-Meissner effect acquires an effective mass $m_γ$ that is small compared to the corresponding plasma frequency. The contribution of these reactions to neutrino emissivity is calculated; it varies with the temperature and the photon mass as $T^{3/2}m_γ^{7/2} e^{-m_γ/T}$ for $T < m_γ$. Estimates show that these processes appear as extra efficient cooling channels of neutron stars at temperatures $T \simeq (10^9-10^{10})$ K.

# Contribution of the massive photon decay channel to neutrino cooling of neutron stars

## Key claim

The authors propose that in hot neutron stars where protons have entered a superconducting phase, massive photons (acquiring a small effective mass via the Higgs-Meissner effect) can decay into neutrino-antineutrino pairs through electron-electron-hole and proton-proton-hole intermediate states, providing an extra efficient cooling channel at temperatures T ≃ (10⁹-10¹⁰) K. The claim is conditional on the existence of proton pairing and on the photon mass being small compared to the plasma frequency, which holds only below the critical temperature for proton superconductivity.

## Method

The authors calculate the neutrino emissivity from massive photon decay via two Feynman diagrams: one with an electron-electron-hole loop and one with a proton-proton-hole loop (using normal Green's functions for protons, neglecting the small gap contribution). They derive the photon dispersion relation in superconducting proton matter, where the photon acquires an effective mass m_γ determined by the paired proton density via the Higgs-Meissner effect, and integrate the squared matrix element over the phase space of the photon, neutrino, and antineutrino to obtain the emissivity as a function of temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- Photon effective mass at nuclear saturation density: m_γ(ρ = ρ₀, T) ≃ 1.6 √((T_{c,p} - T)/T_{c,p}) MeV, which is much smaller than the electron plasma frequency ω_pl(ρ₀) ≈ 4.7 MeV
- Emissivity formula for m_γ > T: ε_ν^γ ≈ 2.6 × 10²⁵ T₉^{3/2} e^{-m_γ/T} (m_γ/MeV)^{7/2} (ρ/ρ₀)^{8/3} (1 + 3T/(2m_γ)) [1 + η] erg cm⁻³ s⁻¹, where T₉ is temperature in units of 10⁹ K
- Ratio of new process emissivity to modified URCA emissivity: R_FM ≈ 1.5 × 10⁴ T₉^{-13/2} e^{(Δ_n + Δ_p - m_γ)/T} (m_γ/MeV)^{7/2} (1 + 3T/(2m_γ)) (ρ/ρ₀)² (m_n³ m_p / m_n^{*3} m_p*) [1 + η]
- The new processes are operative in the temperature range 1 × 10⁹ K ≲ T ≲ 8 × 10⁹ K for the gap parameters of Takatsuka (1992), and 1 × 10⁹ K ≲ T ≲ 4 × 10⁹ K for the parameters of Pines (1991)
- Within these temperature intervals, the new cooling channel may exceed known cooling processes by up to a factor of 10⁶

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino cooling mechanisms in neutron stars beyond the standard modified URCA process (Friman and Maxwell 1979) and plasmon decay (Adams, Ruderman, and Woo 1963; Schinder and Bludman 1989), which are suppressed at temperatures below the nucleon pairing critical temperatures. The authors build on the theory of finite Fermi systems (Migdal 1978; Voskresensky and Senatorov 1986) and previous work on in-medium effects in neutron star cooling (Voskresensky, Senatorov, and collaborators 1990s). The new massive photon decay channel becomes relevant specifically in the proton superconducting phase, where the standard plasmon decay is strongly suppressed by the large plasma frequency gap, and enables more rapid cooling scenarios that could explain observations of fast-cooling neutron stars such as Vela, PSR 2334+61, PSR 0656+14, and Geminga.
