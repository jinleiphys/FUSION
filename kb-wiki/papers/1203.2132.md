# Gaussian Overlap Approxomation for the quadrupole collective states

> The Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) in the Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GOA) is applied to a description of the nuclear quadrupole collective states. The full five-dimensional quadrupole tensor is used as a set of the generator coordinates.The integral Hill-Wheeler equation is reduced to a differential equation by using the Fourier transforms of the overlap and energy kernels. The differential Bohr Hamiltonian obtained this way is compared with that derived by the usual approach to the collective Hamiltonian in the GOA which does contain an additional approximation. The method of calculating the quantities which determine the Bohr Hamiltonian from the set of deformation-dependent intrinsic states is demonstrated. In particular, it appears that the moments of inertia at the quadrupole rotations are of the type of that of Yoccoz.

## Key claim

The Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) in the Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GOA) can be used to derive a five-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian for quadrupole collective states in a purely quantum way, without the additional approximation present in the usual collective approach to the GCM. The derivation relies on the Fourier analysis of the energy kernel, which yields a local distribution containing the Dirac delta function and its second-order derivatives, but the equivalence to the Hill-Wheeler equation is formal because the quantities determining the Hamiltonian can only be determined in an approximation when expanding the exponential operator.

## Method

The full five-dimensional quadrupole tensor is used as the set of generator coordinates, and the integral Hill-Wheeler equation is reduced to a differential equation by introducing new quadrupole variables that diagonalize the exponent of the Gaussian overlap kernel, then performing Fourier transforms of the overlap and energy kernels. The Fourier analysis expresses the narrowed energy kernel as a distribution containing the Dirac delta function and its second-order partial derivatives, which allows the integral equation to be converted into a differential eigenvalue equation for the Bohr Hamiltonian. The method is compared with the usual collective approach to the GCM, which contains an additional approximation beyond the GOA.

## Key numbers

- The overlap kernel is approximated as a Gaussian: \( \mathcal{I}(\boldsymbol{\beta}+\frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\gamma},\boldsymbol{\beta}-\frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\gamma}) = \exp(-\frac{1}{2} g^{\mu\nu}(\boldsymbol{\beta})\gamma_\mu\gamma_\nu) \), where \( g^{\mu\nu} \) is the metric tensor.
- The energy kernel quotient is approximated as: \( \mathcal{H}(\boldsymbol{\beta}+\frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\gamma},\boldsymbol{\beta}-\frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\gamma}) / \mathcal{I}(\boldsymbol{\beta}+\frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\gamma},\boldsymbol{\beta}-\frac{1}{2}\boldsymbol{\gamma}) = v(\boldsymbol{\beta}) - \frac{1}{2} h^{\mu\nu}(\boldsymbol{\beta})\gamma_\mu\gamma_\nu + \dots \).
- The eigenvalue of the Gaussian overlap kernel is: \( j(\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}) = (2\pi)^{5/2} \exp(-\frac{1}{2} \boldsymbol{\varepsilon}\cdot\boldsymbol{\varepsilon}) \).
- The Jacobian of the transformation from deformation parameters and Euler angles to laboratory quadrupole coordinates is: \( W(d,\omega) = |d_2(3d_0^2 - d_2^2)|\sin\omega_2 \).

## Context

This work extends earlier multi-dimensional GCM treatments of quadrupole motion by Kamlah (1973), Onishi and Une (1975), and Gozdz (1985a, 1985b), as well as the review by Reinhard (1987). It provides a consistent derivation of the full five-dimensional Bohr Hamiltonian from the GCM with the GOA, which had not been done previously. The method enables a purely quantum description of quadrupole collective excitations in even-even nuclei, and the moments of inertia obtained are of the Yoccoz type, in contrast to the Thouless-Valatin prescription used in calculations by Bertsch et al. (2007) with Gogny effective forces.
