# Relativistic instant-form approach to the structure of two-body composite systems

> A new approach to the electroweak properties of two-particle composite systems is developed. The approach is based on the use of the instant form of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics. The main novel feature of this approach is the new method of construction of the matrix element of the electroweak current operator. The electroweak current matrix element satisfies the relativistic covariance conditions and in the case of the electromagnetic current also the conservation law automatically. The properties of the system as well as the approximations are formulated in terms of form factors. The approach makes it possible to formulate relativistic impulse approximation in such a way that the Lorentz-covariance of the current is ensured. In the electromagnetic case the current conservation law is ensured, too. The results of the calculations are unambiguous: they do not depend on the choice of the coordinate frame and on the choice of "good" components of the current as it takes place in the standard form of light--front dynamics. Our approach gives good results for the pion electromagnetic form factor in the whole range of momentum transfers available for experiments at present time, as well as for lepton decay constant of pion.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new approach to the electroweak properties of two-particle composite systems using the instant form of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics, where the key novel feature is a method for constructing the matrix element of the electroweak current operator that automatically satisfies relativistic covariance conditions and, for the electromagnetic current, the conservation law. The approach claims to give unambiguous results that do not depend on the choice of coordinate frame or on the choice of "good" components of the current, unlike the standard light-front dynamics approach.

## Method

The authors use the instant form of relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics (RHD) with the Bakamjian-Thomas procedure for including interaction into the mass square operator. They employ the canonical parameterization method of Cheshkov and Shirokov (1963) to construct matrix elements of local operators in terms of relativistic invariant form factors, which are treated as generalized functions. A modified impulse approximation (MIA) is formulated in terms of form factors rather than operators, ensuring Lorentz-covariance and current conservation without requiring exchange currents.

## Context

This work extends the instant form of RHD, which originated with Dirac (1949), as an alternative to the more widely used light-front dynamics approach. The authors note that light-front dynamics calculations for systems with total angular momentum $J=1$ (such as the deuteron and rho meson) show significant variation with coordinate frame rotation, as shown by Grach and Kondratyuk (1984) and Keister (1994). The approach enables a relativistic impulse approximation that avoids the frame-dependence problems of traditional instant form dynamics and does not require the addition of exchange currents to satisfy current conservation, unlike the Bethe-Salpeter and quasipotential equation approaches discussed by Coester and Riska (1994).
