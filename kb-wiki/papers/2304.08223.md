# Quarkyonic Model for Neutron Star Matter: A Relativistic Mean-Field Approach

> The concept of quarkyonic matter presents a promising alternative to the conventional models used to describe high-density matter and provides a more nuanced and detailed understanding of the properties of matter under extreme conditions that exist in astrophysical bodies. The aim of this study is to showcase the effectiveness of utilizing the quarkyonic model, in combination with the relativistic mean-field formalism, to parameterize the equation of state at high densities. Through this approach, we intend to investigate and gain insights into various fundamental properties of a static neutron star, such as its compositional ingredients, speed of sound, mass-radius profile, and tidal deformability. The obtained results revealed that the quarkyonic matter equation of state (EOS) is capable of producing a heavy neutron star with the mass range of $\sim$ $2.8 M_\odot$. The results of our inquiry have demonstrated that the EOS for quarkyonic matter not only yields a neutron star with a significantly high mass but also showcases a remarkable degree of coherence with the conformal limit of the speed of sound originating from deconfined QCD matter. Furthermore, we have observed that the tidal deformability of the neutron star, corresponding to the EOSs of quarkyonic matter, is in excellent agreement with the observational constraints derived from the GW170817 and GW190425 events. This finding implies that the quarkyonic model is capable of forecasting the behavior of neutron stars associated with binary merger systems. This aspect has been meticulously scrutinized in terms of merger time, gravitational wave signatures, and collapse times using numerical relativity simulations.

## Key claim

The quarkyonic matter equation of state, combined with relativistic mean-field formalism, can produce a neutron star with a maximum mass of approximately 2.8 solar masses while remaining consistent with the conformal limit of the speed of sound from deconfined QCD matter, but this result depends on using the RMF formalism to determine the nucleon potential at the transition density rather than the simpler potentials used in prior quarkyonic models.

## Method

The authors combined the quarkyonic model of McLerran and Reddy, as extended by Zhao and Lattimer for beta-equilibrated matter, with the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism using the G3 and FSUGold parameter sets. They enforced beta equilibrium and charge neutrality for both nucleons and quarks simultaneously, using the RMF-derived nucleon potential at the transition density to determine the effective quark masses, then solved for the equation of state and computed static neutron star properties including mass-radius profiles, speed of sound, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass of approximately 2.8 solar masses produced by the quarkyonic matter EOS
- Lambda parameter values of 800 and 1400 MeV adopted for the quarkyonic model, consistent with the work of Zhao and Lattimer
- Nucleon mass M approximately 939 MeV used in the RMF Lagrangian
- Neutron star diameter of only around 10-15 kilometers with mass range 1.1 to 2.5 solar masses cited as typical observational context
- Speed of sound in quarkyonic matter reaches a maximum of 1/sqrt(3) at high densities, consistent with the conformal limit

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic model originally proposed by McLerran and Reddy (2019) and later developed by Zhao and Lattimer (2020) for beta-equilibrated matter, addressing limitations of earlier quark models such as the MIT bag model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model which struggled to satisfy observational constraints from GW170817 and GW190425. The key improvement over Zhao and Lattimer (2020) is using the RMF formalism to provide the nucleon potential at the transition density rather than fitting potentials for pure neutron matter or symmetric nuclear matter, enabling a more consistent treatment of beta-equilibrated matter. The results enable future numerical relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers to test quarkyonic matter predictions against gravitational wave signatures and merger timescales.
