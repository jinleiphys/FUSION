# A quark meson coupling model with density- and temperature- dependent quark masses

> Based on the quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model we suggest a model for nuclear matter where the meson field is introduced to be directly coupled to the quarks. The dynamic formation of the nucleon bag, the saturation properties of nuclear matter as well as equation of state for this model are studies.

# A quark meson coupling model with density- and temperature- dependent quark masses

## Key claim
The authors propose a model for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature that combines the quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model with explicit quark-meson coupling in the mean field approximation, claiming it can reproduce saturation properties comparably to the QMC model, but with the caveat that the result at zero temperature is irrelevant to the temperature-dependent parameters a and b in the bag constant ansatz.

## Method
The authors extend the QMDTD model, where quark masses depend on baryon density and temperature via the bag constant B(T), by introducing scalar (σ) and vector (ω) meson fields coupled directly to quarks in the mean field approximation, following the approach of the QMC model. The nucleon bag energy is computed from the quark single-particle energies, with the bag radius determined by a stability condition δE_Bag/δR = 0. The total energy per nucleon is then obtained by summing nucleon kinetic energies with effective masses given by the bag energy, plus meson field contributions, with the scalar mean field determined self-consistently.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon mass fixed to 939 MeV using B₀ = 173 MeV fm⁻³.
- Saturation binding energy E = -16 MeV at baryon density ρ_B = 0.15 fm⁻³ for symmetric nuclear matter at T = 0 K.
- Quark-meson coupling constants: g_σ²/4π = 14.4, g_ω²/4π = 3.39.
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M* = 802 MeV (QMDTD model) vs. 851 MeV (QMC model).
- Compressibility at saturation: K = 229 MeV (QMDTD model) vs. 200 MeV (QMC model).
- Bag radius at saturation: R₀ = 0.86 fm, with relative modification δR/R₀ = 0.08 (QMDTD model) vs. -0.02 (QMC model).

## Context
This work extends the QMDTD model, previously applied to strange matter and strangelets by the same authors (Qian and Su, 2001), to infinite nuclear matter by incorporating quark-meson coupling in the style of the QMC model (Guichon, 1988). The QMDTD model itself is an extension of the quark mass density-dependent (QMDD) model (Fowler, Raha, and Weiner, 1981) and is based on the Friedberg-Lee non-topological soliton bag model (Lee, 1981), in contrast to the MIT bag model used in QMC. The results enable future studies of finite nuclei and hot nuclear matter within this framework, and the authors note that the calculated quantities differ not very much from those of the QMC model.
