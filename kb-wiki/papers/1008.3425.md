# Binding energy and fission of the heavy charged massive particle - nucleus bound state

> We consider a possibility of capture of a heavy charged massive particle $χ^-$ by the nucleus leading to appearance of a bound state. A simple analytic formula allowing to calculate binding energies of the $Nχ^-$ bound state for different nuclei is derived. If the binding energy is sufficiently large the particle $χ^-$ is stable inside the nucleus. The probabilities of the nuclear fissions for such states are calculated. It is shown that the bound states are more stable to a possible fission in comparison to the bare nucleus. This makes an observation of this hypothetical charged massive particle and the superheavy nuclei more probable.

## Key claim
The paper derives a simple analytic formula for the binding energy of a heavy charged massive particle (χ⁻) bound to a nucleus, and shows that such bound states are more stable against nuclear fission than bare nuclei, with the tunneling probability reduced by 2–3 orders of magnitude. This stability enhancement is conditional on the binding energy being sufficiently large that the χ⁻ particle is stable inside the nucleus (i.e., |E₀| > m_χ⁻ − m_χ⁰).

## Method
The authors model the nucleus as a uniformly charged sphere of radius R ≈ r₀ A^{1/3} and treat the χ⁻ particle bound to it via the Coulomb potential. For heavy nuclei, the wavefunction is localized well inside the nucleus (r_av ≪ R), so the potential reduces to that of a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator plus a constant term. The ground-state binding energy E₀ is then obtained from the known eigenvalues of the harmonic oscillator. The fission probability is estimated using a parabolic barrier model, where the change in Coulomb energy δE between the deformed nuclear shapes at the minimum and maximum of the fission barrier is computed for a prolate spheroidal deformation.

## Key numbers
- For ¹¹Bχ⁻, the binding energy is 2.3 MeV (this work) compared to 2.1 MeV from Pospelov and Ritz (2008); for ¹³²Xeχ⁻, it is 18.6 MeV (this work) vs. 18.4 MeV.
- For ²⁰²Hgχ⁻ (Z=80), the binding energy is 24.2 MeV; for ²⁶⁹Dsχ⁻ (Z=110), it is 30.3 MeV.
- The ratio r_av/R for ²⁶⁹Dsχ⁻ is 0.13, confirming the wavefunction is localized well inside the nucleus.
- For Z=80, δE = 0.315 MeV and P/P₀ = 1.9×10⁻²; for Z=160, δE = 0.501 MeV and P/P₀ = 1.8×10⁻³ (assuming ω_B = 0.5 MeV).
- The tunneling probability of the bound state is 2–3 orders of magnitude smaller than that of the bare nucleus.

## Context
This work extends the numerical calculations of Pospelov and Ritz (2008), who solved the Schrödinger equation for a Gaussian and steplike nuclear charge distribution to find binding energies for several elements. The authors provide an analytic formula that reproduces those results and can be applied to heavy and superheavy nuclei whose properties are less studied. The fission analysis builds on the work of Dzuba and Flambaum (2008), who considered the effect of atomic electrons on nuclear fission and found only a 0.2% change; here, the effect of a bound heavy particle is shown to be much larger. This opens new possibilities for observing both the hypothetical χ⁻ particle and superheavy nuclei that may be too unstable to detect in their bare form.
