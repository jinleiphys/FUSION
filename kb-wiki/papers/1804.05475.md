# Enhanced nuclear spin dependent parity violation effects using the 199HgH molecule

> Electron interactions with the nuclear-spin-dependent (NSD) parity non-conserving (PNC) anapole moment are strongly enhanced within heteronuclear diatomic molecules. A novel, low-energy optical rotation experiment is being proposed with the aim of observing NSD PNC interactions in HgH. Based on the relativistic coupled cluster method we present a complete calculation of the circular polarization parameter $P = 2\,\textrm{Im}(E1_{PNC})/M1 \approx 3 \times 10^{-6}\; κ$ for the $^2Σ_{1/2} \to ^2Π_{1/2}$ optical transition of HgH, where $κ$ is a dimensionless constant determined by the nuclear anapole moment. This provides an improvement in sensitivity to NSD PNC by 2 -- 3 orders of magnitude over the leading atomic Xe, Hg, Tl, Pb and Bi optical rotation experiments, and shows that the proposed measurement will be sensitive enough to extract the $^{199}$Hg anapole moment and shed light on the underlying theory of hadronic parity violation.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that the circular polarization parameter for the proposed optical rotation experiment in the 199HgH molecule is \(P = 3 \times 10^{-6} \kappa\), where \(\kappa\) is a dimensionless constant determined by the nuclear anapole moment; this result is conditional on using the coupled-cluster method and neglecting possible phase differences in the contributing terms, and the authors note that the estimated uncertainty of the calculated weak interaction parameters is 15-20%.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic coupled-cluster (CC) calculations of the effective weak interaction coefficients \(W_a\) for both the ground \(X~^2\Sigma_{1/2}\) and first excited \(A_1~^2\Pi_{1/2}\) electronic states of 199HgH, using the Fock-Space coupled cluster with single and double cluster amplitudes method. They also calculated the perpendicular components of the electric dipole and magnetic dipole transition matrix elements between these states using relativistic linear-response coupled cluster with single and double cluster amplitudes within the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian. These values were combined with experimental rotational constants and energy separations to compute the circular polarization parameter \(P = 2\,\text{Im}(E1_{PNC})/M1\) for the \(^2\Sigma_{1/2} \to ^2\Pi_{1/2}\) optical transition.

## Key numbers

* Weak interaction coefficient for the ground state: \(W_a(^2\Sigma_{1/2}) = 3335\) Hz (coupled-cluster method).
* Weak interaction coefficient for the excited state: \(W_a(^2\Pi_{1/2}) = -419\) Hz (coupled-cluster method).
* Perpendicular electric dipole matrix element: \(D_{+} = 0.7\) a.u.
* Perpendicular magnetic dipole matrix element: \(G_{+} = 1.4\) a.u.
* Energy separation between opposite parity levels in the ground state: \(\Delta E(^2\Sigma_{1/2}) = 8.64\) cm\(^{-1}\).
* Energy separation between opposite parity levels in the excited state: \(\Delta E(^2\Pi_{1/2}) = 3.36\) cm\(^{-1}\).
* Circular polarization parameter: \(P = 3 \times 10^{-6} \kappa\).
* The improvement in sensitivity to NSD PNC is 2 to 3 orders of magnitude over leading atomic experiments in Xe, Hg, Tl, Pb, and Bi.

## Context

This work extends calculations of nuclear-spin-dependent parity violation from atoms to the heteronuclear diatomic molecule HgH, building on the theoretical framework for anapole moments developed by Flambaam and Khriplovich (1985) and the molecular PNC formalism of Kozlov (1991, 1995). The proposed experiment uses the optical cavity technique of Bougas (2012) to enhance sensitivity, and the calculated circular polarization parameter is 2 to 3 orders of magnitude larger than estimated values for NSD PNC in atomic Xe, Hg, Tl, Pb, and Bi (Khriplovich 1991, Dzuba 2012). This enables a proposed low-energy optical rotation experiment that could extract the 199Hg anapole moment and test hadronic parity violation theories, in contrast to atomic experiments which require measurements on multiple hyperfine transitions to isolate the small NSD PNC signal from the larger NSI PNC signal.
