# Probing coherent charmonium photoproduction off light nuclei at medium energies

> We demonstrate how the elementary amplitudes $γN\to ΨN$, the amplitude of the nondiagonal $J/ψN\Leftrightarrow ψ' N$ transition, and the total $J/ψN$ and $ψ' N$ cross sections can be determined from measurements of the coherent $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ photoproduction off light nuclei at moderate energies. For this purpose we provide a detailed numerical analysis of the coherent charmonium photoproduction off silicon within the generalized vector dominance model (GVDM) adjusted to account for the physics of charmonium models and color transparency phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that measurements of coherent charmonium photoproduction off light nuclei (specifically silicon) at moderate photon energies (15–40 GeV) can be used to extract the elementary amplitudes for γN → J/ψ N, γN → ψ′ N, the nondiagonal J/ψ N ↔ ψ′ N transition amplitude, and the total J/ψ N and ψ′ N cross sections, provided that the analysis accounts for color transparency and uses a Generalized Glauber Model rather than the naive Vector Dominance Model.

## Method

The authors combined the multistep Glauber approach with the Generalized Vector Dominance Model (GVDM) restricted to the two lowest charmonium states (J/ψ and ψ′), adjusting for color screening effects. They performed numerical calculations of the forward coherent photoproduction cross sections for J/ψ and ψ′ off a silicon target (A=20–40 being optimal) using a Hartree-Fock-Skyrme nuclear density, for photon energies between 15 and 40 GeV. The input amplitudes were constrained by the measured forward γN → J/ψ N cross section, the relation dσ(γN → ψ′ N)/dt = 0.15·dσ(γN → J/ψ N)/dt, and the J/ψ N cross section σ(J/ψ N) ≈ 3.5 ± 0.8 mb from SLAC data.

## Key numbers

- σ(J/ψ N) = 3.5 ± 0.8 mb, extracted from SLAC A-dependence measurements using a semi-classical model.
- VDM-based estimates give σ(J/ψ N) = 1.3 ± 0.3 mb and σ(ψ′ N) ≈ 0.7 σ(J/ψ N).
- Charmonium-nucleon coupling constants: f_{J/ψ}^2/(4π) = 10.5 ± 0.7, f_{ψ′}^2/(4π) = 30.9 ± 2.6, determined from leptonic decay widths.
- For coherent J/ψ photoproduction off silicon, the GGM cross section deviates from the Impulse Approximation by only 4–5%.
- For coherent ψ′ photoproduction off silicon, the GGM cross section significantly exceeds the Impulse Approximation cross section due to the two-step nondiagonal γA → J/ψ A → ψ′ A process.
- The minimal longitudinal momentum transfers q_L range from 0.35 GeV at 15 GeV to 0.15 GeV at 40 GeV.
- Using naive VDM (neglecting nondiagonal transitions) would predict J/ψ suppression of ≈10–15% for light nuclei and ≈30–40% for heavy nuclei; with nondiagonal transitions included, the suppression does not exceed 5–10% for all nuclei.
- Disregarding the ψ(3770) state can change the J/ψ N amplitude estimate by ≈10%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Frankfurt et al., 2002) which demonstrated that large charmonium masses and QCD color fluctuations lead to significant cross section oscillations in coherent photoproduction. It directly addresses the unsatisfactory situation where extracted σ(J/ψ N) values range from 2 to 6 mb and σ(ψ′ N) from 8 to almost 20 mb (Vogt, 1999). The proposed method is designed for the kinematics of the planned SLAC E160 experiment and enables model-independent extraction of elementary charmonium-nucleon amplitudes, which are urgently needed for interpreting hidden charm production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions (Kharzeev, 1995).
