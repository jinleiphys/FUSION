# The role of the Roper resonance in n p --> d (pi pi)

> In this work, a model for the n p --> d (pi pi) reaction is developed. It is shown that the structure of the deuteron momentum spectra for a neutron beam momentum of 1.46 GeV can be explained as a consequence of the interplay of two mechanisms involving the excitation of the N^*(1440) resonance and its subsequent decay into N (pi pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave} and Delta pi respectively. The relevance of the present analysis for the study of the Roper excitation and decay properties, as well as for the interpretation of other two-pion production experiments is discussed.

## Key claim

The structure of the deuteron momentum spectra in the reaction \(n p \rightarrow d (\pi \pi)^0\) at a neutron beam momentum of 1.46 GeV can be explained as a consequence of the interference of two mechanisms involving the Roper resonance \(N^*(1440)\): its decay into \(N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) and into \(\Delta \pi\), respectively. The model does not fully reproduce the absolute magnitude of the data, and the author notes that the data are underestimated, possibly due to omitted small mechanisms or approximations.

## Method

The author constructed a model for the \(n p \rightarrow d (\pi \pi)^0\) reaction based on a previous microscopic model for \(N N \rightarrow N N \pi \pi\) (Alvarez-Ruso et al., 1998), modified for a deuteron in the final state. The amplitude includes the dominant \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) mechanism, the smaller \(N^* \rightarrow \Delta \pi\) mechanism, and a double-\(\Delta\) mechanism for comparison; the deuteron wave function (Paris, Bonn, or Hulthen) is used, and the \(T=0\) and \(T=1\) meson-exchange potentials include pion, rho, and sigma exchanges with short-range correlations. The differential cross section is calculated in the laboratory frame and compared to data from Hollas et al. (1988) at a neutron beam momentum of 1.463 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Neutron beam momentum: \(p_n = 1.463\) GeV (corresponding to \(T_n = 795\) MeV).
- For the free \(p n \rightarrow p n \pi^+ \pi^-\) reaction at \(T_p = 800\) MeV, the \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) mechanism gives \(\sigma \sim 11\ \mu\)b, while \(N^* \rightarrow \Delta \pi\) gives \(\sigma \sim 0.5\ \mu\)b; all other mechanisms give \(\sigma \lesssim 0.3\ \mu\)b, and double-\(\Delta\) gives \(\sigma \sim 0.1\ \mu\)b.
- Coupling constants used: \(f^* = 2.13\) (for \(\Delta N \pi\)), \(\tilde{f} = 0.477\) (for \(N^* N \pi\)), \(g_{N^* \Delta \pi} = 2.07\); branching ratios for \(N^*\) decays: 65% to \(N \pi\), 25% to \(\Delta \pi\), 7.5% to \(N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\); total \(N^*\) width: 350 MeV.
- For the \(N^* \rightarrow N (\pi \pi)^{T=0}_{S-wave}\) decay, two parameter sets are used: Set I (\(c^*_1 = -7.3\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c^*_2 = 0\)) and Set II (\(c^*_1 = -12.7\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c^*_2 = 2.0\) GeV\(^{-1}\)).
- The neutron beam energy spread is modeled as a Breit-Wigner profile of 40 MeV width centered at \(p_n = 1.46\) GeV; its effect on the spectra is very small.
- For the double-\(\Delta\) mechanism at \(p_n = 1.5\) GeV and \(\theta_{lab} = 4.5^\circ\), the differential cross section is compared to the earlier model of Bar-Nir, Risser, and Shuster (1973).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model for \(N N \rightarrow N N \pi \pi\) (Alvarez-Ruso et al., 1998) to the \(n p \rightarrow d (\pi \pi)^0\) reaction, addressing data from Hollas et al. (1988) that earlier models (Levy, 1973; Bar-Nir, Risser, and Shuster, 1973) could not explain. The results contradict the double-\(\Delta\) formation model of Bar-Nir, Risser, and Shuster (1973) and instead show that the Roper resonance and its interference are essential. The model is relevant for interpreting the MOMO experiment at COSY (\(p d \rightarrow {^3}He \pi^+ \pi^-\)) and for future data on \(p p \rightarrow d \pi \pi\) from CELSIUS at beam energies between 650 and 775 MeV, which could help constrain the properties of the Roper resonance.
