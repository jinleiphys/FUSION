# Continuum Strong QCD: Confinement and Dynamical Chiral Symmetry Breaking

> Continuum strong QCD is the application of models and continuum quantum field theory to the study of phenomena in hadronic physics, which includes; e.g., the spectrum of QCD bound states and their interactions. Herein I provide a Dyson-Schwinger equation perspective, focusing on qualitative aspects of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in cold, sparse QCD, and also elucidating consequences of the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity and features of the heavy-quark limit.

## Key claim

Continuum strong QCD, using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) with a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow truncation, can provide a unified description of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) in cold, sparse QCD, but the approach necessarily involves model-dependence in the infrared behavior of the effective quark-quark interaction, and the truncation's reliability must be judged a posteriori by comparing with experimental data and other theoretical approaches.

## Method

The author employs the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator (the gap equation) in Euclidean space, using a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow truncation where the dressed-gluon propagator and dressed-quark-gluon vertex are replaced by a model effective coupling. The effective coupling combines an integrable infrared singularity, a finite-width Gaussian term, and a perturbative tail matching the one-loop running coupling, with parameters fixed by fitting low-energy pion and kaon properties via the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. The gap equation is solved self-consistently subject to renormalisation boundary conditions at a large spacelike scale (zeta = 19 GeV), and the chiral limit is defined by setting the renormalisation-point-independent current-quark mass to zero.

## Key numbers

* The model effective coupling parameter D = (0.884 GeV)^2, with omega = 0.3 GeV and m_t = 0.5 GeV fixed, and Lambda_QCD^(N_f=4) = 0.234 GeV.
* Renormalised current-quark masses at zeta = 19 GeV: m_{u,d} = 3.74 MeV, m_s = 82.5 MeV, in the ratio 1:22.
* Calculated meson masses and decay constants (in MeV): m_pi = 139, m_K = 497, f_pi = 131, f_K = 154; experimental values: m_pi = 139, m_K = 496, f_pi = 131, f_K = 160.
* Euclidean constituent-quark masses: M^E_u = 0.56 GeV, M^E_s = 0.70 GeV, M^E_c = 1.3 GeV, M^E_b = 4.6 GeV.
* Renormalisation-point-independent vacuum quark condensate: -<qbar q>^0 = (0.227 GeV)^3; one-loop evolved to zeta = 1 GeV gives (0.241 GeV)^3, compared with the phenomenological value (0.236 +/- 0.008 GeV)^3.
* Renormalisation-point-invariant current-quark masses (in MeV): hat m_{u,d} = 6.60, hat m_s = 147, hat m_c = 1030, hat m_b = 6760.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to QCD as reviewed in Bastirev (1999) and Roberts and Williams (1994), providing a concrete model (Maris and Roberts, 1997) that simultaneously describes light meson properties and the quark mass function. The approach enables systematic calculation of hadron observables within a single framework, with the chiral limit analysis showing that DCSB occurs if and only if the quark condensate is nonzero, and that the model-independent large-momentum behaviour of the mass function (Eq. Mchiral) provides a reliable means of extracting the condensate. The results enable future studies of heavy-quark physics via the Bethe-Salpeter equation and further exploration of the infrared behaviour of the gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex.
