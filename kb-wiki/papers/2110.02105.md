# A novel formulation of the unintegrated gluon distribution for DIS

> In this talk, we revisit inclusive DIS in the small $x$ limit and derive a new factorization formula that accounts for leading powers in both Bjorken and Regge limits. In this semi-classical description, we obtain a new unintegrated gluon distribution which encompasses both the dipole operator and the gluon Parton Distribution Function with an explicit dependence on the longitudinal momentum fraction $x$.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new factorization formula for inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) that accounts for leading powers in both the Bjorken and Regge limits simultaneously, going beyond the shock wave approximation by restoring the explicit longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) dependence of the gluonic operator that is lost in the strict Regge limit at leading order.

## Method

The author revisits inclusive DIS in the small \(x\) limit and performs a gradient expansion around the transverse position of quantum fluctuations, relaxing the shock wave approximation that assumes zero longitudinal extent of the target. By extracting the first and last interactions with the target and using an identity relating the gauge field difference to an integral of the field strength tensor, the author obtains a factorization formula involving a new unintegrated gluon distribution. This distribution is defined via a nonlocal operator with staple-shaped gauge links that connect field strength tensors at different light-cone coordinates.

## Key numbers

* The factorization formula (Eq. 4) is exact up to corrections of relative order \(p_\perp/\sqrt{s}\), where \(p_\perp\) is an intrinsic transverse momentum in the proton.
* The delta function in the cross-section formula relates Bjorken \(x\) to Feynman \(x\) in the gluon distribution as \(\delta(x - x_{\rm Bj} - \boldsymbol{\ell}^2/(2z\bar z q^- P^+))\).
* In the strict \(s \to \infty\) limit, the \(x\) integration becomes trivial with \(x = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the shock wave approximation used in the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL), Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK), and Jalilian-Marian-Iancu-McLerran-Weigert-Leonidov-Kovner (JIMWLK) equations that govern small-\(x_{\rm Bj}\) physics, where large collinear logarithms at next-to-leading order require resummation for numerical stability (as observed in work by Beuf and by Edmond and collaborators). The new unintegrated gluon distribution interpolates between the Regge and Bjorken limits, and its quantum evolution is left for future work. The explicit \(x\) dependence results in a non-locality in transverse dipole sizes that was shown to be incompatible with the dipole model by Bialas (2000).
