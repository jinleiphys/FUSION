# Thermodynamic consistency, quark mass scaling, and properties of strange matter

> The previous thermodynamic treatment for models with density and/or temperature dependent quark masses is shown to be inconsistent with the requirement of fundamental thermodynamics. We therefore study a fully self-consistent one according to the fundamental differential equation of thermodynamics. After obtaining a new quark mass scaling with the inclusion of both confinement and leading-order perturbative interactions, we investigate properties of strange quark matter in the fully consistent thermodynamic treatment. It is found that the equation of state become stiffer, and accordingly, the maximum mass of strange stars is as large as about 2 times the solar mass, if strange quark matter is absolutely or metastable.

## Key claim

The paper shows that previous thermodynamic treatments of quark matter with density-dependent quark masses violate fundamental thermodynamics, and presents a fully consistent treatment where the pressure at the minimum energy per baryon is exactly zero, enabling a new quark mass scaling that includes both confinement and leading-order perturbative interactions to produce strange star masses up to about 2 solar masses if strange quark matter is absolutely or metastable.

## Method

The authors derive a self-consistent thermodynamic framework starting from the fundamental differential equation of thermodynamics, using the free-energy density as the characteristic function with temperature, particle number densities, and volume as independent state variables. They introduce an "equiparticle model" where the free-energy density has the same functional form as a free system but with density-dependent equivalent quark masses and effective chemical potentials, with the true chemical potentials differing from the effective ones by a common term. They then obtain a new quark mass scaling that includes both confinement (through a term proportional to the baryon number density to the one-third power) and leading-order perturbative interactions, and apply this to calculate the equation of state and mass-radius relation of strange stars.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of strange stars is about 2 times the solar mass (2 solar masses) if strange quark matter is absolutely or metastable
- For the reciprocity scaling with parameters m_{u0}=m_{d0}=0, m_{s0}=150 MeV, B^{1/4}=170 MeV fm^{-3}, the minimum energy per baryon in TD-3 is 906.3 MeV at density 0.433 fm^{-3} with exactly zero pressure
- For TD-1 with the same parameters, the pressure at the minimum energy per baryon is 90.7 MeV fm^{-3}; for TD-2 it is 113.2 MeV fm^{-3}
- The vacuum limits for TD-1 give energy density = B and pressure = 0; for TD-2 give energy density = 2B and pressure = -B; for TD-3 give energy density = B and pressure = -B

## Context

This work extends the line of quasiparticle models with density-dependent quark masses that began with Fowler (1981) and Chakrabarty (1989), and specifically addresses the thermodynamic inconsistency problems identified in treatments TD-1 through TD-4 by Chakrabarty (1989), Benvenuto (1995), Peng (2000), and Wang (2000). The authors show that the previously used reciprocity scaling and cubic-root scaling cannot satisfy the Cauchy integrability condition required for a consistent thermodynamic potential, and provide a new scaling that includes both confinement and perturbative interactions. This enables strange star models that can simultaneously describe both low-radius stars (as in Dexheimer (2013)) and high-mass stars up to 2 solar masses, consistent with the recent measurements by Antoniadis (2013) and Demorest (2010).
