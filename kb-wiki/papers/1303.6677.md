# GiBUU and Shallow Inelastic Scattering

> In this talk we shortly describe the physics contents of the GiBUU transport code, used to describe lepton scattering off nuclei. Particular attention will be given to validation of the GiBUU in pion-, electron- and photon-induced reactions, which serve as a benchmark for neutrino-induced ones. We mainly concentrate on those properties of benchmark reactions, which are relevant to the region of Shallow Inelastic Scattering (SIS). Our results in this region are presented for integrated and differential cross sections. Comparison with recent MINOS inclusive data, as well as predictions for the differential cross sections measurable in \Minerva and \Nova experiments are made.

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport code provides a unified framework for describing lepton scattering off nuclei across a wide energy range, but its validation relies heavily on benchmark reactions (pion-, electron-, and photon-induced) rather than direct neutrino data; the authors emphasize that the Shallow Inelastic Scattering (SIS) region, where resonance and deep inelastic contributions both matter, requires careful treatment of all reaction channels and final state interactions.

## Method

The GiBUU model solves coupled Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equations for 61 baryons and 21 mesons, implementing a semi-classical transport approach with momentum-dependent mean-field potentials and a local Thomas-Fermi approximation for nuclear density. The code uses PYTHIA for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) processes, with various prescriptions to correct for nuclear binding effects, and includes both resonant and non-resonant (background) pion production mechanisms. Final state interactions are treated dynamically through the BUU equations, accounting for rescattering, absorption, and particle production during hadron propagation through the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For neutrinos on an isoscalar target, the DIS contribution becomes larger than the Delta contribution at about 3 GeV, reaching about 60% of the total cross section at 5 GeV and 95% at higher energies
- For antineutrinos on an isoscalar target, the DIS contribution becomes larger than the Delta channel at about 4 GeV, reaching 40% of the total at 5 GeV and 95% at higher energies
- The difference between various in-medium correction prescriptions for DIS on iron is about 2% at the lowest energies and less at the highest energies
- For pion kinetic energy distributions after FSI, the cross section at T_pi around 0.06 GeV is about 50% higher than before FSI
- The ratio of antineutrino to neutrino cross section on iron rises with energy and approaches an asymptotic value of approximately 0.5 for an isoscalar-corrected target at high energies

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport framework, previously validated for photon-induced reactions (Krusche 2004) and electron-induced reactions (JLab data), to neutrino scattering in the SIS region relevant to MINOS, MINERvA, and NOvA experiments. The paper addresses the controversial topic of nuclear corrections in neutrino reactions, noting that while nuclear parton distributions exist (Hirai 2009), their application to neutrinos remains debated (Paukkunen 2013). The results enable improved modeling of neutrino event generators by demonstrating that final state interactions significantly modify pion and nucleon spectra, and that the characteristic shape of pion spectra after FSI is determined by absorption through the Delta resonance rather than the production mechanism itself.
