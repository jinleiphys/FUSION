# New broad 8Be nuclear resonances

> Energies, total and partial widths, and reduced width amplitudes of 8Be resonances up to an excitation energy of 26 MeV are extracted from a coupled channel analysis of experimental data. The presence of an extremely broad J^pi = 2^+ ``intruder'' resonance is confirmed, while a new 1^+ and very broad 4^+ resonance are discovered. A previously known 22 MeV 2^+ resonance is likely resolved into two resonances. The experimental J^pi T = 3^(+)? resonance at 22 MeV is determined to be 3^-0, and the experimental 1^-? (at 19 MeV) and 4^-? resonances to be isospin 0.

## Key claim

A coupled-channel R-matrix analysis of experimental data on 8Be confirms an extremely broad J^pi = 2^+ "intruder" resonance and discovers a new 1^+ and very broad 4^+ resonance, but the analysis is limited to resonances fairly near the real energy axis and does not include all experimental data, so it is not expected to provide the best parameters for narrow resonances (widths less than 100 keV).

## Method

The analysis used the EDA R-matrix code to fit more than 4700 data points from 69 experimental references covering 11 reactions (including 4He(alpha,alpha0), 7Li(p,p0), and 6Li(d,alpha0)) with about 100 free parameters, achieving a chi^2/(d.o.f.) of 7.4. Resonance parameters (excitation energies, total and partial widths, and reduced width amplitudes) were extracted as complex-energy poles of the S-matrix using the Kapur-Peierls formalism, with isospin symmetry constraints applied to relate reduced width amplitudes for proton and neutron channels.

## Key numbers

- J^pi T = 2^+0 resonance at E_x = 16.40 MeV with total width Gamma = 19200 keV (extremely broad "intruder" resonance)
- New J^pi T = 4^+0 resonance at E_x = 17.59 MeV with total width Gamma = 7900 keV (very broad)
- New J^pi T = 1^+1 resonance at E_x = 20.45 MeV with total width Gamma = 690 keV
- Previously known 2^+0 resonance at E_x = 22.09 MeV (Gamma = 590 keV) likely resolved into two resonances, with a second 2^+0 at E_x = 22.78 MeV (Gamma = 1670 keV)
- J^pi T = 3^-0 resonance at E_x = 21.35 MeV (Gamma = 950 keV), resolving the previous ambiguous J^pi = 3^(+)? assignment
- J^pi = 1^- resonance at 19.33 MeV (Gamma = 650 keV) determined to have isospin 0
- J^pi = 4^- resonance at 21.50 MeV (Gamma = 1060 keV) determined to have isospin 0
- Well-known 2^+0(3) resonance: E_x = 2.77 MeV, Gamma = 1200 keV (R-matrix) versus experimental E_x = 3.03(1) MeV, Gamma = 1513(15) keV
- Well-known 4^+0(11) resonance: E_x = 11.57 MeV, Gamma = 4400 keV (R-matrix) versus experimental E_x = 11.35(15) MeV, Gamma approx 3500 keV

## Context

This work extends previous R-matrix analyses by Barker and collaborators (Barker 1988, 1989, 2000) who found the broad 2^+0 intruder state and a broad 0^+ at about 10 MeV, and provides the most comprehensive global analysis of 8Be resonances up to 26 MeV excitation energy. The results enable direct comparison with ab initio No-Core Shell Model calculations (Navratil 2001) and Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson 2004), which disagree on the existence of intruder states below certain energies, possibly because these calculations treat all states as bound while the intruder states have very large widths. The resonance parameters are advocated for incorporation into future TUNL evaluations and are relevant to sorting out the structure of 8Li and 8B.
