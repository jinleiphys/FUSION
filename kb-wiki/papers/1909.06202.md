# Transport coefficients of hot and dense hadron gas in a magnetic field: a relaxation time approach

> We estimate various transport coefficients of hot and dense hadronic matter in the presence of magnetic field. The estimation is done through solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation.We have investigated the temperature and the baryon chemical potential dependence of these transport coefficients. Explicit calculations are done for the hadronic matter in the ambit of hadron resonance gas model. We estimate thermal conductivity, electrical conductivity and the shear viscosity of hadronic matter in the presence of a uniform magnetic field. Magnetic field, in general, makes the transport coefficients anisotropic. It is also observed that all the transport coefficients perpendicular to the magnetic field are smaller compared to their isotropic counterpart.

## Key claim

The paper estimates thermal conductivity, electrical conductivity, and shear viscosity for hot and dense hadronic matter in a uniform magnetic field using the hadron resonance gas model within the relaxation time approximation, but only for small or moderate magnetic fields where quantum effects from Landau quantization are neglected and the magnetic field effect enters only through the cyclotron frequency of individual hadrons.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation for a multi-component hadron resonance gas, assuming the system is slightly away from equilibrium due to external perturbations (temperature gradient, electric field, or velocity gradient) and that strong interactions dominate thermalization while the electromagnetic field is a perturbation. For thermal and electrical conductivity, they use an ansatz for the deviation from equilibrium distribution that includes terms parallel, perpendicular, and transverse to the magnetic field direction, then extract the transport coefficients by comparing tensor structures. For shear viscosity, they follow the formalism of Landau and Tuchin (2012) to derive four transverse shear viscosity coefficients corresponding to different tensor structures in the presence of a magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength at initial stages of heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be of order several \(m_{\pi}^2\) at RHIC energies and up to 15 \(m_{\pi}^2\) at LHC energies (from cited literature, not original calculation).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio lower bound from AdS/CFT correspondence is \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) (KSS bound), cited as context.
- The paper presents no explicit numerical values for the transport coefficients in the abstract or introduction; numerical results appear only in the truncated sections not provided in the full text.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by the same authors on electrical conductivity and Hall conductivity for hot and dense hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma (Das et al., 2017; Das et al., 2018), where they found that electrical conductivity decreases in a magnetic field while Hall conductivity shows non-monotonic behavior and vanishes at zero baryon chemical potential. The present study generalizes these calculations to arbitrary configurations of electric and magnetic fields, adding the \(\sigma_2\) component of electrical conductivity, and also computes thermal conductivity and shear viscosity in the magnetic field. The formalism follows the approach of Landau and Lifshitz, Tuchin (2012), and Sedrakian (1998) for transport in magnetic fields, and uses the hadron resonance gas model as established by various authors for describing the hadronic phase at chemical freeze-out. The results are relevant for magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions and for understanding how long the initial magnetic field survives in the medium.
