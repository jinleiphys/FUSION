# Comparing partial-wave amplitude parametrization with dynamical models of meson-nucleon scattering

> Relationships between partial-wave amplitude parametrizations, in particular the Chew-Mandelstam approach, and dynamical coupled-channel models are established and investigated. A bare pole corresponding to the Delta(1232) resonance, found in a recent dynamical-model fit to pion- and omega-meson production reactions, compares closely to one found in a unitary multichannel partial-wave amplitude parametrization of SAID. The model dependence of the bare pole precludes a direct connection between the approaches but is suggestive that the dynamical description and the phenomenological parametrization are closely related.

## Key claim

The bare pole position of the Delta(1232) resonance found in a dynamical coupled-channel model (1391 MeV) closely matches the explicit Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix pole position in the SAID parametrization (1381 MeV), suggesting a close dynamical relationship between the two approaches, though the model dependence of bare parameters precludes a direct connection.

## Method

The authors compared the pole structure of the P33 partial wave in the Delta(1232) resonance region between two approaches: a dynamical coupled-channel model that separates the T-matrix into nonresonant and resonant contributions via a Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and the SAID Chew-Mandelstam (CM) parametrization which uses a K-matrix with an explicit pole term plus a polynomial background. They analyzed the relationships between the CM K-matrix, Heitler K-matrix, and T-matrix poles, and compared the inverse Heitler K-matrix elements from both approaches graphically as a function of center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers

- The CM K-matrix pole position for the P33 partial wave in the SAID parametrization: W_p(P33) = 1381 MeV
- The Heitler K-matrix pole position for the P33 partial wave: W_p^H(P33) = 1232 MeV
- The bare resonance mass from the dynamical model (Paris 2008): M^(0)(P33) = 1391 MeV
- The T-matrix pole position (universal across approaches): E = (1210, -51) MeV, within 1 or 2 MeV
- When the background term beta is set to zero in the CM K-matrix, the T-matrix pole shifts to 1297 - i24 MeV
- In the FP10 fit with factorized S-matrix form, the Heitler K-matrix pole remains at 1232 MeV and the T-matrix pole at 1211 - i50 MeV, while the CM K-matrix pole shifts to 1480 MeV
- The Juelich model gives a different bare mass value: 1459 MeV (Gasparyan 2003)
- A precursor model (Sato 1996) gives bare masses of 1299 MeV (model L) and 1319 MeV (model H)

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the SAID Chew-Mandelstam parametrization (Arndt 2006) and dynamical coupled-channel models (Julia-Diaz 2007, Paris 2008) by establishing quantitative relationships between their pole structures for the P33 partial wave. The study addresses a debate (Doering 2009) about the interplay between singularities from iterated nonresonant interactions and explicit bare states, and builds on earlier work (Workman 2008) examining the relation between CM and Heitler K-matrix poles. The results enable future investigation of whether adding explicit CM K-matrix poles to other partial waves could replace resonances generated by non-pole terms, and suggest that the CM parametrization encodes the same dynamics as the model approach despite being free of the model-dependent decomposition into resonant and nonresonant contributions.
