# Theoretical study of the $Ω(2012)$ state in the $Ω_c^0 \to π^+ Ω(2012)^- \to π^+ (\bar{K}Ξ)^-$ and $π^+ (\bar{K}Ξπ)^-$ decays

> We report on a theoretical study of the newly observed $Ω(2012)$ resonance in the nonleptonic weak decays of $Ω_c^0 \to π^+ \bar{K}Ξ^*(1530) (ηΩ) \to π^+ (\bar{K}Ξ)^-$ and $π^+ (\bar{K}Ξπ)^-$ via final-state interactions of the $\bar{K}Ξ^*(1530)$ and $ηΩ$ pairs. The weak interaction part is assumed to be dominated by the charm quark decay process: $c(ss) \to (s + u + \bar{d})(ss)$, while the hadronization part takes place between the $sss$ cluster from the weak decay and a quark-antiquark pair with the quantum numbers $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ of the vacuum, produces a pair of $\bar{K}Ξ^*(1530)$ and $ηΩ$. Accordingly, the final $\bar{K}Ξ^*(1530)$ and $ηΩ$ states are in pure isospin $I= 0$ combinations, and the $Ω_c^0 \to π^+ \bar{K}Ξ^*(1530)(ηΩ) \to π^+ (\bar{K}Ξ)^-$ decay is an ideal process to study the $Ω(2012)$ resonance. With the final-state interaction described in the chiral unitary approach, up to an arbitrary normalization, the invariant mass distributions of the final state are calculated, assuming that the $Ω(2012)$ resonance with spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$ is a dynamically generated state from the coupled channels interactions of the $\bar{K}Ξ^*(1530)$ and $ηΩ$ in $s$-wave and $\bar{K}Ξ$ in $d$-wave. We also calculate the ratio, $R^{\bar{K}Ξπ}_{\bar{K}Ξ} = {\rm Br}[Ω_c^0 \to π^+ Ω(2012)^- \to π^+ (\bar{K}Ξπ)^-] / {\rm Br}[Ω_c^0 \to π^+ Ω(2012)^- \to π^+ (\bar{K}Ξ)^-$]. The proposed mechanism can provide valuable information on the nature of the $Ω(2012)$ and can in principle be tested by future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance, assumed to be a dynamically generated state with $J^P = 3/2^-$ from coupled-channel interactions of $\bar{K}\Xi^*(1530)$ and $\eta\Omega$ in $s$-wave and $\bar{K}\Xi$ in $d$-wave, can be studied via the nonleptonic weak decay $\Omega_c^0 \to \pi^+ \bar{K}\Xi^*(1530) (\eta\Omega) \to \pi^+ (\bar{K}\Xi)^-$ because the final $\bar{K}\Xi^*(1530)$ and $\eta\Omega$ states are in pure isospin $I=0$ combinations, providing an ideal filter for the resonance.

## Method

The weak decay of $\Omega_c^0$ is assumed to proceed via the Cabibbo-favored charm quark decay $c(ss) \to (s+u+\bar{d})(ss)$, followed by hadronization of the $sss$ cluster with a $q\bar{q}$ pair from the vacuum to produce $\bar{K}\Xi^*(1530)$ and $\eta\Omega$ pairs. The final-state interactions of these pairs are described using the chiral unitary approach, where the $\Omega(2012)$ is dynamically generated from coupled channels. The invariant mass distributions for the $\bar{K}\Xi$ and $\bar{K}\Xi\pi$ final states are calculated, and the ratio of branching fractions $R^{\bar{K}\Xi\pi}_{\bar{K}\Xi}$ is computed by integrating over the resonance region.

## Key numbers

- The $\Omega(2012)$ resonance mass is taken as $M_{\Omega^*} = 2012.3$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{\Omega^*} = 8.3$ MeV for Model I with cutoff $\Lambda = 735$ MeV; other models give masses of $2012.2$ to $2012.4$ MeV and widths of $6.4$ to $8.3$ MeV.
- The predicted ratio $R^{\bar{K}\Xi\pi}_{\bar{K}\Xi}$ ranges from $7.3\%$ (for $\Lambda = 900$ MeV) to $13.9\%$ (for $\Lambda = 735$ MeV), in agreement with the Belle upper limit of $11.9\%$ at $90\%$ confidence level.
- The coupling constant $g_{\Xi^*\Xi\pi} = 4.4 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$ is obtained from the $\Xi^*(1530)$ decay width $\Gamma^{\rm on}_{\Xi^*} = 9.5$ MeV and masses $M_{\Xi^*} = 1533.4$ MeV, $M_{\Xi} = 1318.29$ MeV, $m_\pi = 138.04$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance, previously studied by Valderrama (2018), Lin (2018), Pavao (2018), Huang (2018), and Polyakov (2018), and addresses the tension with the Belle measurement of the ratio $R^{\bar{K}\pi\Xi}_{\bar{K}\Xi}$ reported by Jia (2019). The calculations follow the chiral unitary approach used by Lu (2020) and Ikeno (2020) and apply the methodology of using charmed baryon weak decays to study hadron resonances, as done for $\Xi^*(1620)^0$ by the Belle collaboration (Sumihama, 2018). The proposed mechanism enables future experimental tests of the $\Omega(2012)$ nature through $\Omega_c^0$ decays.
