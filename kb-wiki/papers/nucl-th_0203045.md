# Nuclear absorption and $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions

> We have analyzed the NA58 data on $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions. $J/ψ$ production is assumed to be a two step process, (i) formation of $c\bar{c}$ pair, which is accurately calculable in QCD and (ii) formation of $J/ψ$ meson from the $c\bar{c}$ pair, which can be conveniently parameterized. In a pA/AA collision, a $c\bar{c}$ pair gain relative square momentum as it passes through the nuclear medium and some of the $c\bar{c}$ pairs can gain enough momentum to cross the threshold to become an open charm meson, leading to suppression in pA/AA collisions. A new prescription is proposed for the gain in momentum square, consistent with Krammer process. The model without any free parameter could explain the $E_T$ dependence of $J/ψ$ over Drell-Yan ratio.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of J/ψ mesons in Pb+Pb collisions can be explained entirely by conventional nuclear absorption, without invoking a quark-gluon plasma, if the square of the relative momentum gained by a c\(\bar{c}\) pair traversing nuclear matter scales as the square of the path length (L²) rather than linearly with L as previously proposed.

## Method

The author modifies the model of Qiu, Vary, and Zhang (2002) by replacing the prescription for the gain in relative square momentum of a c\(\bar{c}\) pair from \(\varepsilon^2 L\) to \(\varepsilon^2 L^2\), arguing this is consistent with a Krammer-like random walk process. The model's single parameter, \(\varepsilon^2\), was fixed by fitting experimental J/ψ cross sections in pA and AA collisions. The resulting suppression factor was then used to calculate the J/ψ over Drell-Yan ratio as a function of transverse energy (\(E_T\)) in Pb+Pb collisions, including the effect of \(E_T\) fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The model, with no free parameters after fitting the cross sections, reproduced the NA50 data on the \(E_T\) dependence of the J/ψ over Drell-Yan ratio throughout the entire \(E_T\) range, including the rapid fall beyond the knee of the \(E_T\) distribution, when \(E_T\) fluctuations were included.
* The transverse energy distribution in Pb+Pb collisions was fitted with a Gamma distribution with parameters \(\alpha = 3.46 \pm 0.19\) and \(\beta = 0.379 \pm 0.021\).
* The nuclear density profile used a Woods-Saxon form with diffuseness \(a = 0.53\) fm and radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.1 A^{1/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the model of Qiu, Vary, and Zhang (2002) by altering the prescription for momentum gain to be consistent with a Krammer process, and it directly addresses the NA50 data on J/ψ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. The author shows that the data, which had been interpreted as evidence for a deconfinement scenario by Blaizot, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000) and others, can be explained by nuclear absorption alone when \(E_T\) fluctuations are accounted for. This enables a reinterpretation of the NA50 data without requiring a quark-gluon plasma.
