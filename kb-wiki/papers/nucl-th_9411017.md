# On an Extended PCAC Relationship

> We explore a consistent way to extend the partially conserved axial vector current (PCAC) relationship and corresponding current algebra results in two strongly correlated directions: 1) towards a search for a set of systematic rules for the establishment of PCAC related relationships in a finite low momentum transfer region and for the extrapolation of the momentum transfer $q^2$ to zero when deriving the low energy PCAC results that can be compared to experimental data and 2) towards taking into account, besides the conventional one, the only other possibility of the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R \to SU(2)_V$, inside a baryonic system by a condensation (in the sense to be specified in the paper) of diquarks. The paper includes investigations of a chiral Ward--Takahashi identity, the explicit chiral symmetry breaking by a finite current quark mass, the modification of the PCAC relationship and its consequences. It is shown that the signals for a hypothetical diquark condensation inside a nucleon and nucleus is observable in high precision experiments despite the fact that they may evade most of the current observations. We briefly discuss how diquark condensation could provide an answer to the question of where about of pions and quark number in a nucleon and a nucleus, which is raised in explaining puzzles in polarized pion nucleus scattering, violation of Gottfried sum rule and EMC effects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extension of the partially conserved axial vector current (PCAC) relationship to accommodate a hypothetical diquark condensation phase inside nucleons and nuclei, which would represent an alternative form of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking beyond the conventional Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) phase. The author argues that signals of such a diquark condensation could be observable in high-precision experiments, even though they may evade most current observations.

## Method

The author derives a chiral Ward–Takahashi identity for a relativistic fermionic system with both conventional (NJL) and superconducting (diquark condensation) phases, then introduces a finite current quark mass perturbation to study explicit chiral symmetry breaking. The PCAC relation is modified by adding a term for a Goldstone diquark excitation inside the nucleon, and the consequences are worked out for the axial vector form factor \(g_P\), the Goldberger–Treiman relation, the Adler–Weisberger sum rule, and the nucleon \(\Sigma_N\) term. The analysis uses one-loop perturbation calculations and parameterizes the effects of diquark condensation through order parameters \(\phi^c_\mu\) and \(\bar\phi_{c\mu}\).

## Key numbers

- The Goldberger–Treiman relation for single quarks in the superconducting phase is expressed as \(g_{\delta q} f_{\delta} = \sqrt{\phi^2}\), which defines the scale of \(f_\delta\).
- The extrapolation of \(g_A\) from \(q^2 = m_\pi^2\) to \(q^2=0\) introduces an error of order \(m_\pi^2/M_A^2 \sim 1\%\) with \(M_A \sim 1\ \text{GeV}\).
- The Goldberger–Treiman relation is satisfied to about \(5\%\) in experimental observations.
- The value of \(g_A\) from nuclear \(\beta\) decay experiments is \(1.261 \pm 0.004\); the value from the Adler–Weisberger sum rule using pion–nucleon scattering cross sections is about \(1.24 \pm 0.02\).
- The nucleon \(\Sigma_N\) term from pion–nucleon scattering data is \(45\ \text{MeV}\), while from other sources it is \(35\ \text{MeV}\), a discrepancy of order \(25\%\).
- \(\sigma_N(0) = 25\ \text{MeV}\) can be obtained from the baryon spectrum.
- A recent experimental study supports Eq. (PCAC4) within momentum transfer of \(0 < -q^2 < 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard PCAC framework, which is based on the NJL phase characterized by a non-vanishing \(\langle 0|\bar\psi\psi|0\rangle\), by considering the alternative superconducting phase with diquark condensation, as previously explored in a 4-fermion interaction model by Ying (1994). The paper builds on the chiral Ward–Takahashi identity and the Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (GOR) relation, and it addresses puzzles in polarized pion–nucleus scattering, the violation of the Gottfried sum rule (Gottfried, 1967), and the EMC effects (European Muon Collaboration, 1983). The modified PCAC relation and current algebra results provide a framework for interpreting deviations in muon capture experiments (Towner, 1994; Bardin, 1981; Carg, 1990) and for exploring the missing pion and quark number depletion problems in nuclei.
