# A chiral quark model analysis of the $\bar KN$ interaction

> In this work we analyze the $\bar KN$ interaction in the framework of a constituent quark model. The near-threshold elastic and charge exchange cross sections are evaluated, finding a good agreement with the experimental data. Furthermore, the possible existence of $\bar KN$ bound states are explored, finding two poles in the isoscalar $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$ sector that can be interpreted as the experimental $Λ(1405)$ state.

## Key claim
The constituent quark model, with all parameters fixed from prior hadron phenomenology, reproduces near-threshold elastic and charge-exchange cross sections and predicts two isoscalar poles in the $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$ sector that can be interpreted as the $\Lambda(1405)$ state, but only when an optical potential modeling coupled channels is included; without the optical potential, only virtual states (not bound states) are found.

## Method
The authors employed a constituent quark model based on spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, with quark-quark interactions mediated by Goldstone boson exchange (pion and scalar sigma) and one-gluon exchange. The $\bar KN$ interaction was derived using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) to treat the five-quark system, and the scattering problem was solved via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in partial waves up to $J=\frac{9}{2}$. The loss of flux to coupled channels (such as $\pi\Sigma$) and the baryon spectrum was modeled by a complex Gaussian optical potential with isospin dependence, whose parameters were fitted to experimental cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Elastic and charge-exchange cross sections were calculated for $p_{\rm lab}$ from 200 to 700 MeV/c$^2$, with a fit quality of $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}=1.69$.
- Optical potential parameters: $b' = 1.23 \pm 0.7$ fm; $W_i^0 = -0.39 \pm 0.02$ GeV$^{-2}$; $W_i^1 = -1.28 \pm 0.09$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Without optical potential, two virtual states found: $I=0$ at 1405 MeV and $I=1$ at 1414 MeV; including $\bar K^*N$ channel shifts them to 1409 MeV and 1415 MeV respectively.
- With optical potential, isoscalar poles: $z_1 = (1439 \pm 3) - i(22 \pm 2)$ MeV and $z_2 = (1417 \pm 4) - i(55 \pm 7)$ MeV.
- With optical potential, isovector poles: $z_1 = (1444^{+3}_{-2}) - i(7 \pm 1)$ MeV and $z_2 = (1432 \pm 1) - i(57 \pm 8)$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model framework previously applied to $NN$, $N\bar N$, and baryon-baryon systems (Entem 2000, Valcarce 2005, Entem 2006) to the $\bar KN$ system. The two-pole structure found for the $\Lambda(1405)$ in the isoscalar sector agrees with results from chiral SU(3) dynamics studies (Jido 2003, Ikeda 2011, Guo 2012, Mai 2014, Nieves 2024), confirming the molecular interpretation of this resonance. The predicted isovector poles, while consistent with some prior work (Oller 2006), have no experimental counterpart yet, suggesting sensitivity to coupled-channel effects that could be explored in future experiments.
