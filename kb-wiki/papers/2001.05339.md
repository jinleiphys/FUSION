# Impact of the underlying O(4) criticality on net-proton number fluctuations

> We discuss the beam-energy dependence of ratios of the first four cumulants of the net-proton number, calculated using a phenomenologically motivated model in which critical mode fluctuations couple to protons and anti-protons. The model takes into account an impact of the underlying $O(4)$ criticality on the QCD phase diagram in the presence of a $Z(2)$ critical point. This allows us to capture qualitatively both the monotonic behavior of the lowest-order ratio and the non-monotonic behavior of higher-order ratios which are seen in the experimental data from the STAR Collaboration. We also discuss the dependence of our results on the coupling strength between the critical mode and the protons as well as the location of the critical point.

# Impact of the underlying O(4) criticality on net-proton number fluctuations

## Key claim

The model, which incorporates O(4) criticality effects into a phenomenological framework with a Z(2) critical point, can qualitatively reproduce both the monotonic beam-energy dependence of the C2/C1 ratio and the non-monotonic behavior of higher-order cumulant ratios seen in STAR data, but the experimentally observed C3/C2 ratio does not follow the behavior expected if the critical point is close to the freeze-out curve, making such a scenario unlikely.

## Method

The author uses a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model as the baseline for regular contributions to net-proton number cumulants, then couples critical mode fluctuations to protons and anti-protons via a linear sigma-model relation where particle mass depends on the critical mode sigma. The model modifies the approach of Bluhm (2016) by replacing the proton mass in the singular part of the second cumulant with the order parameter g*sigma, following the relation chi_mumu ~ sigma^2 * chi_chiral derived from effective model calculations for O(4) and tricritical points by Hatta (2002) and Sasaki (2006, 2007). Cumulant ratios are calculated at chemical freeze-out using the parametrization of Andronic (2017), with three different critical point locations and coupling strengths g = 2, 3, and 5.

## Key numbers

- Three critical point locations (CP1, CP2, CP3) are considered, with CP3 closest to the freeze-out curve and CP1 farthest; the freeze-out curve follows the parametrization of Andronic (2017).
- Fixed coupling strength g = 5 is used when varying critical point location; for fixed CP3, coupling strengths g = 2, 3, and 5 are tested.
- The C4/C2 ratio shows a strong increase at low beam energies below sqrt(s) = 20 GeV when the critical point is close to the freeze-out curve.
- Model results are compared to preliminary STAR data from Thader (2016); the C2/C1 and C4/C2 ratios show qualitative agreement, while the C3/C2 ratio from the model lies above the HRG baseline whereas the experimental data lie below.

## Context

This work extends the model of Bluhm (2016), which described non-monotonic C3/C2 and C4/C2 ratios but produced a strong non-monotonic C2/C1 ratio not seen experimentally. The modification incorporates O(4) criticality effects on baryon number and chiral susceptibilities following Hatta (2002) and Sasaki (2006, 2007), enabling better description of the C2/C1 ratio. The results suggest the QCD critical point may not be located close to the phenomenological freeze-out curve, but this conclusion requires further investigation due to theoretical and experimental uncertainties.
