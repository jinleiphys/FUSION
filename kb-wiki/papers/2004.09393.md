# Topological susceptibility, divergent chiral density and phase diagram of chirally imbalanced QCD medium at finite temperature

> We show that the nonlocal two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model predicts the enhancement of both chiral and axial symmetry breaking as the chiral imbalance of hot QCD matter, regulated by a chiral chemical potential $μ_5$, increases. The two crossovers are reasonably close to each other in the range of $μ_5$ examined here and the pseudocritical temperatures rise with $μ_5$. The curvatures of the chiral and axial crossovers for the chiral quark chemical potential approximately coincide and give $κ_5 \simeq - 0.011$. We point out that the presence of $μ_5$ in thermodynamic equilibrium is inconsistent with the fact that the chiral charge is not a Noether-conserved quantity for massive fermions. The chiral chemical potential should not, therefore, be considered as a true chemical potential that sets a thermodynamically stable environment in the massive theory, but rather than as a new coupling that may require a renormalization in the ultraviolet domain. The divergence of an unrenormalized chiral density, \corr{coming from zero-point fermionic fluctuations,} is a consequence of this property. We propose a solution to this problem via a renormalization procedure.

## Key claim

The nonlocal two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model predicts that both chiral and axial symmetry breaking are enhanced as the chiral imbalance (regulated by a chiral chemical potential μ₅) increases, with pseudocritical temperatures rising with μ₅; however, the authors point out that the presence of μ₅ in thermodynamic equilibrium is inconsistent because the chiral charge is not a Noether-conserved quantity for massive fermions, so μ₅ should be treated as a new coupling requiring renormalization rather than a true chemical potential.

## Method

The authors employed a nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors, incorporating a chiral chemical potential μ₅ and a topological θ-angle to study the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature. They computed the thermodynamic potential via bosonization at one-loop approximation, using form factors with perturbative QCD tails to match the running of the current quark mass and the quark mass function, and then minimized the potential to obtain mean-field values of the chiral condensate σ and the η condensate. The topological susceptibility was evaluated as a function of temperature T and μ₅ to characterize axial symmetry restoration, and the chiral density n₅ was computed from the thermodynamic potential, revealing an ultraviolet divergence from zero-point fermionic fluctuations that the authors addressed via a renormalization procedure.

## Key numbers

- The curvatures of the chiral and axial crossovers for the chiral quark chemical potential approximately coincide and give κ₅ ≈ -0.011.
- The anomalous dimension of the current quark mass for two-flavor QCD is d_m = 12/29, leading to a power of the logarithm in the divergent chiral density of 1 - 2d_m = 5/29 ≈ 0.17.
- For massless fermions at zero temperature, the chiral density is n₅ = N_c N_f μ₅³ / (3π²), which for N_f = N_c = 1 gives n₅ = μ₅³/(3π²).
- The zero-temperature chiral density for massive fermions diverges as n₅(T=0) ~ (μ₅ m₀² / π²) log(Λ_UV / m₀) for a constant mass m₀.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using nonlocal NJL models (Ruggieri 2016, Frasca 2016) that found the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration increases with μ₅, in agreement with lattice simulations (Braguta 2015, Astrakhantsev 2019) and Schwinger-Dyson equation calculations (Xu 2015, Wang 2015), contrasting with local NJL models (Gatto 2011, Fukushima 2010, Chernodub 2011, Ruggieri 2011, Yu 2015) that predicted a decrease. The paper identifies a fundamental inconsistency in treating μ₅ as a thermodynamic chemical potential for massive fermions and proposes a renormalization procedure to handle the resulting ultraviolet divergence of the chiral density, enabling future studies of the QCD phase diagram with a consistent treatment of chiral imbalance.
