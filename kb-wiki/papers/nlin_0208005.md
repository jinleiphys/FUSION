# Symmetry Breaking and Bifurcations in the Periodic Orbit Theory: II -- Spheroidal Cavity --

> We derive a semiclassical trace formula for the level density of the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity. To overcome the divergences and discontinuities occurring at bifurcation points and in the spherical limit, the trace integrals over the action-angle variables are performed using an improved stationary phase method. The resulting semiclassical level density oscillations and shell energies are in good agreement with quantum-mechanical results. We find that the births of three-dimensional orbits through the bifurcations of planar orbits in the equatorial plane lead to considerable enhancement of shell effect for superdeformed shapes.

## Key claim

The authors derive a semiclassical trace formula for the level density of the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity that remains continuous through bifurcation points and the spherical limit, using an improved stationary phase method rather than the standard stationary phase method which would produce divergences and discontinuities at these critical points.

## Method

The authors evaluate trace integrals over action-angle variables using an improved stationary phase method that keeps exact finite integration limits, rather than extending them to infinity as in the standard stationary phase method. This approach allows them to handle bifurcations where stationary points lie near the ends of the integration region, and to include contributions from "ghost orbits" whose stationary points lie outside the classically accessible region. The method is applied separately to three-dimensional periodic orbits (with degeneracy parameter K=2), meridian-plane two-dimensional periodic orbits (K=2), equatorial-plane periodic orbits (K=1), and the isolated long diameter (K=0).

## Key numbers

- Bifurcation deformations for three-dimensional periodic orbits are given by eta_bif = sin(pi n_phi / n_v) / sin(pi n_u / n_v), with n_u = 1,2,..., n_v >= 2n_phi + 1, n_phi = 2,3,...
- Bifurcation deformations for hyperbolic two-dimensional periodic orbits in the meridian plane are given by eta_bif = [sin(pi n_u / n_v)]^{-1}
- The spheroid is characterized by the ratio of semi-axes eta = b/a, with the prolate case eta > 1 and volume conservation condition a^2 b = R^3 giving b = R eta^{2/3} and a = R eta^{-1/3}
- The action variables I_u, I_v, I_phi are expressed in terms of parameters sigma_1 and sigma_2, with sigma_2 ranging from 0 to 1/(eta^2 - 1) and sigma_1 ranging from sigma_2 to eta^2/(eta^2 - 1) - sigma_2(eta^2 - 1)

## Context

This work extends the improved stationary phase method previously developed for the elliptic billiard by Magner et al. to the three-dimensional spheroidal cavity, which serves as an idealized model for heavy deformed nuclei and deformed metallic clusters. The approach differs from the uniform approximation methods of Richens and Sieber by improving the calculation of the angle part of the phase-space trace integral for orbits with smaller degeneracy. The results enable semiclassical understanding of superdeformed shell structure in nuclei, showing that births of three-dimensional orbits through bifurcations of planar orbits in the equatorial plane lead to considerable enhancement of shell effects for superdeformed shapes.
