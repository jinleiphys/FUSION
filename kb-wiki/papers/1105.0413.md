# Theta vacuum effects on QCD phase diagram

> Theta vacuum effects on the QCD phase structure in the μ-T plane are studied by using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and its extension, where μis the quark chemical potential and T is temperature, respectively. As the parameter θof the theta vacuum increases, the chiral transition becomes stronger. For large θ, it eventually becomes first order even at zero μ.

## Key claim

Using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and its extension (EPNJL), the authors find that as the theta vacuum parameter increases, the chiral transition becomes stronger; in the more reliable EPNJL model, the chiral transition becomes first order even at zero quark chemical potential when theta is large, which is a stronger claim than the abstract's "eventually becomes first order even at zero mu" because it specifies that this result depends on the model choice.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor QCD phase diagram in the plane of quark chemical potential and temperature using both the standard PNJL model and the entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model, where the four-quark coupling constant depends on the Polyakov loop. They incorporate the theta vacuum term via a U(1)_A transformation that removes the theta dependence from the 't Hooft determinant interaction, then compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation and solve the stationary conditions for the chiral condensate, eta condensate, and Polyakov loop.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of parity restoration at zero chemical potential and theta = pi is 202 MeV in the PNJL model and 170 MeV in the EPNJL model.
- In the PNJL model at theta = pi, a tricritical point of parity restoration exists at (mu, T) = (209 MeV, 165 MeV).
- The pion decay constant used for parameter fixing is f_pi = 93 MeV, and the pion mass is m_pi = 139 MeV.
- The current quark mass is set to m_0 = 5.5 MeV, and the parameter c (controlling the strength of the 't Hooft interaction) is taken as c = 0.2.
- The Polyakov-loop potential parameter T_0 is rescaled to 212 MeV in the PNJL model to reproduce the full lattice QCD critical temperature of 173 MeV at zero chemical potential.
- In the EPNJL model, the parameters alpha_1 = alpha_2 = 0.2 and T_0 = 190 MeV are used.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity violation in the NJL model by Fujihara, Inagaki and Kimura (2005) and by Boer and Boomsma (2008) to include Polyakov-loop dynamics, and builds on the authors' own earlier work (Kouno et al., 2010) that investigated spontaneous parity and charge-conjugation violations at finite theta and imaginary chemical potential. The results enable predictions for the QCD phase transition in the early universe and in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, where an effective theta may be of order one, and suggest that a first-order chiral transition at zero chemical potential could change cosmological scenarios such as inhomogeneous Big-Bang nucleosynthesis or baryogenesis.
