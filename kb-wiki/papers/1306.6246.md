# The influence of a repulsive vector coupling in magnetized quark matter

> We consider two flavor magnetized quark matter in the presence of a repulsive vector coupling ($G_V$) devoting special attention to the low temperature region of the phase diagram to show how this type of interaction counterbalances the effects produced by a strong magnetic field. The most important effects occur at intermediate and low temperatures affecting the location of the critical end point as well as the region of first order chiral transitions. When $G_V=0$ the presence of high magnetic fields ($eB \ge 10 m_π^2$) increases the density coexistence region with respect to the case when $B$ and $G_V$ are absent while a decrease of this region is observed at high $G_V$ values and vanishing magnetic fields. Another interesting aspect observed at the low temperature region is that the usual decrease of the coexistence chemical value (Inverse Magnetic Catalysis) at $G_V=0$ is highly affected by the presence of the vector interaction which acts in the opposite way. Our investigation also shows that the presence of a repulsive vector interaction enhances the de Haas-van Alphen oscillations which, for very low temperatures, take place at $eB \lesssim 6 m_π^2$. We observe that the presence of a magnetic field, together with a repulsive vector interaction, gives rise to a complex transition pattern since $B$ favors the appearance of multiple solutions to the gap equation whereas $G_V$ turns some metastable solutions into stable ones allowing for a cascade of transitions to occur.

## Key claim

The repulsive vector coupling \(G_V\) counterbalances the effects of a strong magnetic field on the chiral phase transition in magnetized quark matter, particularly at low temperatures, by shifting the coexistence chemical potential to higher values and weakening the first-order transition, in opposition to the Inverse Magnetic Catalysis (IMC) observed when \(G_V = 0\).

## Method

The authors used the mean-field approximation (MFA) of the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, including a repulsive vector channel parametrized by \(G_V\), to compute the thermodynamical potential for quark matter in a uniform external magnetic field \(B\) at finite temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\). The gap equations for the effective quark mass \(M\) and effective chemical potential \(\tilde{\mu}\) were solved self-consistently, and the phase diagram was mapped in both the \(T-\mu\) and \(T-\rho\) (density) planes for various combinations of \(eB\) and \(G_V\).

## Key numbers

- The scalar coupling and cutoff were fixed to \(\Lambda = 590\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(G_S \Lambda^2 = 2.435\), and \(m = 6.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to reproduce pion mass \(m_\pi \simeq 135\ \mathrm{MeV}\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi \simeq 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and quark condensate \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle^{1/3} \simeq 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At \(G_V = 0.2 G_S\) and \(eB = 5 m_\pi^2\), a cascade of three successive first-order transitions occurs at \(T=0\): the first at \(\mu = 388.55\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (with \(M^H = 409\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M^L = 313\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the second at \(\mu = 389.05\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the third at \(\mu = 402.65\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (with \(M^L = 59\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- At \(G_V = 0.2 G_S\) and \(eB = 7 m_\pi^2\), a single first-order transition occurs at \(T=0\) with \(\mu = 377.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(M^H = 417\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(M^L = 85\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The de Haas–van Alphen oscillations in the high-density branch of the coexistence region \(\rho_B^H\) occur for \(eB \lesssim 9.5 m_\pi^2\) at \(G_V = 0\); for \(eB > 9.5 m_\pi^2\), \(\rho_B^H\) increases almost linearly with \(B\).
- At \(eB = 5.1 m_\pi^2\), \(G_V = 0.2 G_S\), and \(\mu = 388\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (at \(T=0\)), three degenerate minima of the thermodynamical potential appear; at \(eB = 4.56 m_\pi^2\), \(G_V = 0.139 G_S\), and \(\mu = 389\ \mathrm{MeV}\), four degenerate minima appear.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetized quark matter within the NJL model, such as those by the authors of Ref. prd (2012) and Ref. prd12 (2012), which investigated the phase diagram and coexistence region at finite \(B\) but without a vector interaction. It also builds on the known effect that a repulsive vector coupling \(G_V\) weakens the first-order chiral transition at finite density, as established by Buballa (2005) and Fukuoka et al. (2008). The results show that the interplay between \(B\) and \(G_V\) can produce complex transition patterns, including cascades of first-order transitions and multiple degenerate minima, which are relevant for the equation of state of dense matter in compact stars and for understanding the phase structure of QCD at low temperatures and high densities.
