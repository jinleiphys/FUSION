# Ab initio no-core shell-model study of $^{20-23}$Na isotopes

> We have done a systematic no-core shell-model study of $^{20-23}$Na isotopes. The low-energy spectra of these sodium isotopes consisting of natural and un-natural parity states were reported, considering three realistic interactions: inside nonlocal outside Yukawa (INOY), charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K), and the chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO). We also present the mirror energy differences in the low-energy spectra of $|T_z|$ = 1/2 mirror pair ($^{21}$Na - $^{21}$Ne). Apart from the energy spectra, we have also reported the electromagnetic transition strengths and moments. Finally, considering all three realistic interactions, we report the point-proton radii and neutron skin thicknesses.

## Key claim

The authors perform a systematic no-core shell-model (NCSM) study of 20-23Na isotopes using three realistic interactions, but the results are limited to a maximum basis space of Nmax = 4, which is not large enough to achieve full convergence for unnatural parity states and some observables depend on the long-range components of nuclear wavefunctions.

## Method

The authors used the no-core shell-model (NCSM) approach with the Okubu-Lee-Suzuki (OLS) renormalization technique to soften the hard-core part of nuclear interactions, constructing effective Hamiltonians in a many-body harmonic oscillator basis truncated at Nmax = 4. They performed calculations using three realistic NN interactions: inside nonlocal outside Yukawa (INOY), charge-dependent Bonn 2000 (CDB2K), and chiral next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO), computing ground-state energies, low-energy spectra for natural and unnatural parity states, electromagnetic transition strengths and moments, and point-proton radii for 20-23Na isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The maximum basis dimension reached was 1.1 x 10^9 for 23Na at Nmax = 4 model space
- For 20Na natural parity states, the rms deviations in energies for eight lowest states were 1.146 MeV (INOY), 0.720 MeV (CDB2K), and 0.941 MeV (N3LO)
- For 21Na natural parity states, the rms deviations for nine low-lying states were 1.645 MeV (INOY), 0.375 MeV (CDB2K), and 0.439 MeV (N3LO)
- For 22Na natural parity states, the rms deviations for eleven low-lying states were 1.557 MeV (INOY), 0.661 MeV (CDB2K), and 1.077 MeV (N3LO)
- For 23Na natural parity states, the rms deviations for nine states were 0.903 MeV (INOY), 0.811 MeV (CDB2K), and 1.383 MeV (N3LO)
- For 20Na unnatural parity states, the rms energy deviations were 0.226 MeV (INOY), 0.139 MeV (CDB2K), and 0.107 MeV (N3LO)
- For 21Na unnatural parity states, the rms energy deviations were 0.551 MeV (INOY), 0.958 MeV (CDB2K), and 1.437 MeV (N3LO)
- The optimum frequencies for INOY, CDB2K, and N3LO interactions for 21,22Na were 20, 16, and 14 MeV, respectively
- For 23Na ground state at Nmax = 4, a maximum contribution of 18.7% came from the Nmax = 4 space for the N3LO interaction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NCSM study of the neon isotopic chain from A = 18 to 24 (Sarma and Srivastava, 2023) to the sodium isotopes, checking consistency of the NCSM formalism in describing lower sd-shell nuclei. The results are compared to shell-model calculations using the USDB interaction and to recent experimental measurements of E2 transition strengths in 23Na and its mirror nucleus 23Mg using Coulomb excitation, as well as to ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) results reported by other groups. The study enables future investigations of isospin symmetry breaking in mirror energy differences and provides a benchmark for testing whether larger model spaces (Nmax = 5 or greater) can improve convergence, particularly for unnatural parity states.
