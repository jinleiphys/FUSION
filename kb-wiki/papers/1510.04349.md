# Complete Strangeness Measurements in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We discuss strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions within and around the energy range of the planned NICA facility. We describe the minimal statistical model, in which the total strangeness yield is fixed by the observed or calculated $K^+$ multiplicity. We show how the exact strangeness conservation can be taken into account on the event-by-event basis in such a model. We argue that from strange particle yields one can reveal information about the collision dynamics and about possible modifications of particle properties in medium. This can be best achieved if the complete strangeness measurement is performed, i.e. kaons, anti-kaons, hyperons and multistrange hyperons are registered in the same experimental setup. In particular the production of hadrons containing two and more strange quarks, like $Ξ$ and $Ω$ baryons could be of interest.

## Key claim

The paper argues that complete strangeness measurements (simultaneous detection of kaons, antikaons, hyperons, and multistrange baryons in the same experimental setup) are necessary to understand collision dynamics and in-medium modifications of particle properties, but the minimal statistical model systematically underestimates Ξ yields across all collision energies, suggesting that multistrange hyperons may freeze out immediately after production rather than reaching chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors develop a minimal statistical model where the total strangeness yield is normalized to the observed or calculated K⁺ multiplicity, and exact strangeness conservation is enforced event-by-event by dividing collisions into classes with one, two, three, or more s s̄ pairs created. The time evolution of kaon multiplicity is computed from a kinetic equation including production channels (ππ, πB, BB, πY) and neglecting kaon annihilation, while relative chemical equilibrium among strange species is assumed. In-medium potentials for hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) and antikaons are incorporated using relativistic mean-field parameterizations, with hyperon potentials related to nucleon potentials by the number of non-strange quarks.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon scalar potential S_N ≈ −190 MeV × ρ_B/ρ_0 and vector potential V_N ≈ +130 MeV × ρ_B/ρ_0, with ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Hyperon optical potentials at saturation: U_Λ = −27 MeV, U_Σ = +24 MeV, U_Ξ = −14 MeV
- Antikaon effective mass parameter U_ K̄ = −(70–150) MeV
- For Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76 A GeV, exact strangeness conservation reduces the ratio R_Ξ/Λ/K⁺ by a factor ∼0.5, R_Ω/Λ/K⁻/K⁺ by ∼0.2, and R_Ω/Ξ/K⁺ by ∼0.4 compared to average-conservation results
- Kaon production rates shown for Au+Au at 11.6 A GeV (√s = 4.86 A GeV) and 30 A GeV (√s = 7.62 A GeV), with ππ channel dominating

## Context

This work extends the statistical and transport approaches of Fuchs (2004), Hartnack (2012), and Kolomeitsev et al. (1996) for strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions, and applies the event-by-event strangeness conservation scheme introduced by Tomasik et al. (2012) to data from HADES at GSI and AGS-SPS experiments. The persistent underestimation of Ξ yields points to the need for direct production mechanisms rather than chemical equilibrium, and motivates complete strangeness measurements at the planned NICA facility to resolve the dynamics of multistrange baryon formation.
