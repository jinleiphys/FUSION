# Strange Vector Form Factor of Kaons

> Starting from the $ω$-$ϕ$ mixing, further assuming the coupling of the quark-current of some flavour to be of universal strenth exclusively to the component of vector-meson wave function with the same flavour and finally taking numerical values of the coupling constant ratios $(f_{ωK \bar K}/f_ω^e)$, $(f_{ϕK\bar K/f_ϕ^e)$ from the isoscalar part of a realistic six-resonance unitary and analytic model of the kaon electromagnetic structure, the strange-quark vector current form factor behaviour of K-mesons in space-like and time-like regions is predicted.

## Key claim
Using a unitary and analytic model of the kaon electromagnetic form factor, the strange-quark vector current form factor of K-mesons is predicted in both space-like and time-like regions, under the assumption that the quark-current of a given flavour couples with universal strength exclusively to the vector-meson wave-function component of the same flavour, starting from omega-phi mixing.

## Method
The authors construct a unitary and analytic model of the kaon electromagnetic form factor using six vector-meson resonances (omega, omega-prime, omega-double-prime, phi, phi-prime, and rho-family states) in a conformal-mapping representation. They fit this model to all existing data on charged and neutral kaon electromagnetic form factors simultaneously, extracting the isoscalar coupling ratios (f_{omega K K}/f_omega^e) and (f_{phi K K}/f_phi^e). Using omega-phi mixing with a deviation angle epsilon = 3.7 degrees from ideal mixing, they relate these to the corresponding strange-current coupling ratios via derived relations, then construct an analogous unitary and analytic model for F_K^s(t) with the same analytic structure but normalized to -1 and asymptotic behavior ~ t^{-3}.

## Key numbers
- Effective square-root branch point for the isoscalar part: t_in^{I=0} = 1.0480 +/- 0.2800 GeV^2
- Isoscalar coupling ratio for omega: (f_{omega K K}/f_omega^e) = 0.2076 +/- 0.0542
- Isoscalar coupling ratio for phi: (f_{phi K K}/f_phi^e) = 0.3466 +/- 0.0491
- Derived strange-current coupling ratio for omega: (f_{omega K K}/f_omega^s) = -0.0522
- Derived strange-current coupling ratio for phi: (f_{phi K K}/f_phi^s) = -1.0902
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the kaon EM form factor fit: chi^2/ndf = 1.6
- Omega-phi mixing deviation angle: epsilon = 3.7 degrees

## Context
This work extends the dispersion-relation approach of earlier studies (e.g., the authors cite a 1999 approach using Breit-Wigner terms) by applying a more sophisticated unitary and analytic model that unifies all known form factor properties into a single analytic function. The predicted strange kaon form factor is intended for use in estimating the K anti-K contribution to the spectral functions of the strange nucleon electric and magnetic form factors G_E^s and G_M^s, which are relevant to ongoing experimental programs at VEPP-2M and DA Phi NE. The results depend on the precision of existing kaon electromagnetic form factor data and are expected to improve with future measurements.
