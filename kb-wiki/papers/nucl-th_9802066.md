# Orderly spectra from random interactions

> We investigate the low-lying spectra of many-body systems with random two-body interactions, specifying that the ensemble be invariant under particle-hole conjugation.Surprisingly we find patterns reminiscent of more orderly interactions, such as a predominance of J=0 ground states separated by a gap from the excited states, and evidence of phonon vibrations in the low-lying spectra.

## Key claim

Random two-body interactions, when constrained to be invariant under particle-hole conjugation (the "random quasiparticle ensemble" or RQE), produce low-lying spectra with surprising regularities, such as a predominance of J=0 ground states and evidence of phonon vibrations, even though no symmetry is imposed beyond rotational invariance and the two-body character of the interaction.

## Method

The authors constructed an ensemble of two-body Hamiltonians (the RQE) where the variance of two-body matrix elements depends only on total angular momentum J as c_J = v̄²/(2J+1), ensuring invariance under the Pandya transformation (particle-hole conjugation). They diagonalized these Hamiltonians in shell-model spaces with Ω=12 and Ω=20 single-particle states, for systems of N=6 identical particles and for N=4, Z=4 with isospin, computing 1000 spectra per system with all single-particle energies set to zero.

## Key numbers

- For N=6, Ω=12 (²²O): 76% of ground states have J=0, compared to 9.8% of all states in the model space.
- For N=6, Ω=20 (⁴⁶Ca): 75% of ground states have J=0, compared to 3.5% of all states in the model space.
- For N=4, Z=4, Ω=12 (²⁴Mg): 66% of ground states have J=0, T=0, compared to 1.1% of all states in the model space.
- Average energy gap between a J=0 ground state and the first excited state: 0.47 v̄ for N=6, Ω=20; 0.79 v̄ for N=4, Z=4, Ω=12.
- Average fractional collectivity f̄ = 0.52 ± 0.27 for particle-hole phonons between J=0 ground states and first J=2 excited states in the N=4, Z=4, Ω=12 system (compared to 0.87 for a realistic interaction and ~10⁻² for random states).
- For averaged yrast spectra, the long-range behavior of ⟨E_J⟩ versus J(J+1) is roughly linear with slope 0.0539 ± 0.0009 v̄.

## Context

This work extends the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) studied by Wong and French, which used constant c_J and focused on level densities, by imposing particle-hole invariance and examining correlations between states of different quantum numbers. It contrasts with earlier studies by Edwards (1976) and Cortes (1982) that added random Hamiltonians to Hamiltonians with specified symmetries (e.g., SU(3)) to see when randomness overwhelms the symmetry; here the authors look for spontaneous emergence of symmetry markers. The results suggest that basic features of nuclear spectra (J=0 ground states, pairing gaps, vibrational patterns) may arise generically from two-body interactions without fine-tuning, and open the question of whether such regularities persist in the large-N limit and how they depend on the single-particle space.
