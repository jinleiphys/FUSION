# From full stopping to transparency in a holographic model of heavy ion collisions

> We numerically simulate planar shock wave collisions in anti-de Sitter space as a model for heavy ion collisions of large nuclei. We uncover a cross-over between two different dynamical regimes as a function of the collision energy. At low energies the shocks first stop and then explode in a manner approximately described by hydrodynamics, in close similarity with the Landau model. At high energies the receding fragments move outwards at the speed of light, with a region of negative energy density and negative longitudinal pressure trailing behind them. The rapidity distribution of the energy density at late times around mid-rapidity is not approximately boost-invariant but Gaussian, albeit with a width that increases with the collision energy.

## Key claim

The paper uncovers a cross-over between two qualitatively different dynamical regimes in holographic shock wave collisions as a function of the dimensionless product ρw (which decreases with increasing collision energy): a full-stopping scenario for thick shocks (large ρw) and a transparency scenario for thin shocks (small ρw), with the latter exhibiting negative energy density and negative longitudinal pressure trailing behind the receding fragments.

## Method

The authors numerically solve Einstein's equations in anti-de Sitter space (AdS) to simulate collisions of planar shock waves of finite thickness w, characterized by a Gaussian energy density profile with maximum ρ⁴. They vary the dimensionless product ρw over a range from 2 ρw_CY to (1/8) ρw_CY (where ρw_CY ≈ 0.64 from Chesler (2010)), corresponding to low-energy (thick) and high-energy (thin) collisions. Hydrodynamization is assessed by comparing the actual longitudinal pressure to the prediction from first-order viscous hydrodynamics, with applicability defined when 3|ΔP_L^loc|/ε_loc ≤ 0.2.

## Key numbers

- For thick shocks (ρw = 1.28), the maximum energy density ε_max/ρ⁴ = 2.9 occurs at ρt_max = 0.58, with 90% of energy contained in Δz ≈ 2.4 w where flow velocity |v| ≲ 0.1
- For thin shocks (ρw = 0.08), ε_max/ρ⁴ = 2.0 (just the sum of incoming energies) and ε_min/ρ⁴ = -0.1 (negative energy density)
- The hydrodynamization temperature T_hyd/μ ≈ 0.30 across all simulated ρw values (from 0.30 to 0.31 in units of μ)
- The product t_hyd T_hyd ranges from 0.26 to 0.56 across the simulated values
- The ratio P_T/P_L at hydrodynamization time reaches values as large as 15 for the thinnest shocks (ρw = 0.08)
- For thin shocks, the extremum values of pressure/energy ratios approach P_L/ε = -3 and P_T/ε = 2 at early times

## Context

This work extends the holographic shock wave collision approach of Chesler (2010) to a wider range of collision energies, revealing a dynamical cross-over not previously identified. The low-energy regime realizes the Landau model (Landau, 1953) of full stopping followed by hydrodynamic expansion, while the high-energy regime shows transparency with receding fragments moving at the speed of light, contradicting the expectation that infinite coupling necessarily leads to significant stopping. The results enable future studies of how the rapidity distribution (Gaussian rather than boost-invariant, with width increasing with energy) connects to experimental observables at RHIC and LHC.
