# Production Cross Section of Neutron-Rich Calcium Isotopes in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Based on the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics model along with the GEMINI model, heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies are studied. We calculate the production cross sections of different fragments for reactions of 112Sn+112Sn and 124Sn+124Sn at different beam energies. The species and production cross sections of neutron-rich isotopes are generally dependent on the isospin of the system and the incident energies. The nucleon 48Ca and 54Ca are more productive for the neutron-rich system at 30 to 150 MeV/nucleon.

# Production Cross Section of Neutron-Rich Calcium Isotopes in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The production cross sections of neutron-rich calcium isotopes 48Ca and 54Ca are enhanced in neutron-rich colliding systems (124Sn+124Sn) compared to symmetric systems (112Sn+112Sn) at beam energies from 30 to 150 MeV/nucleon, but only when the incident energy is low; the effect diminishes at higher energies.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate the violent stage of heavy-ion collisions, followed by the GEMINI statistical decay model to handle deexcitation of hot prefragments. They calculated fragment production cross sections for 112Sn+112Sn and 124Sn+124Sn reactions at beam energies of 50 and 150 MeV/nucleon, and specifically for 48Ca and 54Ca across the full energy range from 30 to 150 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- Production cross section of 48Ca decreases with increasing beam energy for both 112Sn+112Sn and 124Sn+124Sn systems from 30 to 150 MeV/nucleon.
- Production cross section of 54Ca shows the same decreasing trend with energy as 48Ca for both systems.
- For the neutron-rich system 124Sn+124Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon, 53Ca is produced, while 34-37Ca are not produced in either system.
- At 150 MeV/nucleon, 35Ca, 37Ca, 52Ca, 54Ca, and 56Ca disappear in the 112Sn+112Sn reaction, while 38Ca is not produced for 124Sn+124Sn.
- Peak production cross sections occur at 4He, 16O, and 42Ca, which are relatively stable isotopes.

## Context
This work extends the application of the IQMD+GEMINI model combination, previously validated against experimental data for 197Au+197Au central collisions at 150 MeV/nucleon (from prior experimental work), to study isospin effects in calcium isotope production. The results enable predictions for producing neutron-rich calcium isotopes near the neutron drip line (54Ca is noted as close to the Z=20 drip line), which is relevant for understanding nuclear structure evolution with increasing N/Z ratio and for nucleosynthesis pathway studies. The approach contrasts with statistical abrasion-ablation models (e.g., those used in prior work) by incorporating dynamical transport information through the IQMD model.
