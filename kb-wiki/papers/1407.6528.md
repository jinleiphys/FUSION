# Simulation of stationary Gaussian noise with regard to the Langevin equation with memory effect

> We present an efficient method for simulating a stationary Gaussian noise with an arbitrary covariance function and then study numerically the impact of time-correlated noise on the time evolution of a 1 + 1 dimensional generalized Langevin equation by comparing also to analytical results. Finally, we apply our method to the generalized Langevin equation with an external harmonic and double-well potential.

## Key claim

The paper presents an efficient method for simulating stationary Gaussian noise with an arbitrary covariance function, and shows that time-correlated (colored) noise in the generalized Langevin equation leads to oscillatory transient behavior in the kinetic energy and velocity spectral density, which differs significantly from the exponential relaxation seen with white noise. The method is validated by comparing numerical simulations to analytical results for free particles and harmonic potentials, with the caveat that the noise generation relies on a limit of small variance and large pulse rate.

## Method

The authors construct stationary Gaussian noise by summing discrete pulses with stochastic amplitudes and a chosen pulse shape, then taking the limit of vanishing amplitude variance and infinite pulse rate while keeping their product constant. They relate the pulse shape to the desired covariance function via the Wiener-Khinchin theorem, obtaining the pulse shape as the inverse Fourier transform of the square root of the spectral density. The generalized Langevin equation with a time-dependent friction kernel (related to the noise covariance by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem) is solved numerically using a three-step Adams-Bashforth scheme, with the noise generated by convolving the pulse shape with normalized white noise.

## Key numbers

- For the free particle case with exponential covariance \(C_1(t) = (D/2\tau)\exp(-|t|/\tau)\), the analytical Green's function for \(2Q\tau > 1\) (where \(Q = D/(mT)\)) oscillates with frequency \(\omega_c = \sqrt{2Q\tau - 1}/(2\tau)\).
- The peak frequency of the velocity spectral density for the free particle is \(\omega_{\mathrm{peak}} = \sqrt{2Q\tau - 2}/(2\tau)\); for large \(\tau\), \(\omega_{\mathrm{peak}} \approx \omega_c\).
- Numerical simulations used 8000 realizations to compare analytical and numerical kinetic energy for parameters \(D=2\), \(\tau=2\), \(m=0.1\), \(T=1\).
- For the harmonic oscillator, the spectral density of position shows a peak approaching \(\omega_0 = \sqrt{5}\) for long correlation times \(\tau\), with simulation parameters \(T=1\), \(D=1\), \(m=0.2\).
- The barrier-passing probability for a square-barrier potential uses parameters \(\beta = D/(2mT)\), \(\lambda_1\) as the positive root of \(\lambda^3 + \lambda^2/\tau + (\beta/\tau - \omega_0^2)\lambda - \omega_0^2/\tau = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of non-Markovian Langevin dynamics with colored noise, which has applications in heavy-ion collisions (disoriented chiral condensates, phase transitions), quantum field theory (Kadanoff-Baym equations), condensed matter physics (heat conduction, molecular junctions), and biology (protein fluctuations). The method enables efficient simulation of stationary Gaussian noise with arbitrary covariance, validated against analytical solutions for free particles and harmonic potentials (Kubo 1966), and against the square-barrier diffusion problem solved analytically by Boilley (2006). The approach allows future studies of memory effects in more complex potentials, such as the double-well potential presented numerically in the paper.
