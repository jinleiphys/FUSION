# Ab-initio no-core Gamow shell model calculations of multi-neutron systems

> The existence of multi-neutron systems has always been a debatable question. Indeed, both inter-nucleon correlations and a large continuum coupling occur in these states. We then employ the ab-initio no-core Gamow shell model to calculate the resonant energies and widths of the trineutron and tetraneutron systems with realistic interactions. Our results indicate that trineutron and tetraneutron are both unbound and bear broad widths. The calculated energy and width of tetraneutron are also comparable with recent experimental data. Moreover, our calculations suggest that the energy of trineutron is lower than that of tetraneutron, while its resonance width is also narrower. This strongly suggests that trineutron is more likely to be experimentally observed than tetraneutron. We thus suggest experimentalists to search for trineutron at low energy.

## Key claim
The authors claim that both trineutron and tetraneutron are unbound resonances, but that trineutron is more likely to be experimentally observed because its calculated resonance energy (1.29 MeV) is lower and its width (0.91 MeV) is narrower than those of tetraneutron (2.64 MeV and 2.38 MeV, respectively). The abstract's suggestion to search for trineutron at low energy is conditioned on these specific calculated values and the assumption that narrower width implies greater experimental observability.

## Method
The authors employed the ab-initio no-core Gamow shell model (NCGSM) with a Berggren basis to treat both many-body correlations and continuum coupling simultaneously. They used the chiral two-body N3LO interaction renormalized via the Vlow-k method with a momentum cutoff of 2.1 fm-1. To identify physical resonant states, they added an auxiliary Wood-Saxon confining potential and gradually reduced its depth to zero, tracking the eigenstates via an overlap method. For tetraneutron, full diagonalization was impossible in the large model space, so they performed calculations in a smaller space with NCGSM-4p4h (no truncation) and applied corrections of 360 keV for energy and 380 keV for width to estimate the large-space results.

## Key numbers
- Trineutron resonance energy: 1.29 MeV (unbound, above neutron emission threshold)
- Trineutron resonance width (Gamma_3n): 0.91 MeV
- Tetraneutron resonance energy: 2.64 MeV (unbound)
- Tetraneutron resonance width (Gamma_4n): 2.38 MeV
- Tetraneutron energy correction applied to estimate large-space result: 360 keV too unbound in the small space
- Tetraneutron width correction applied: 380 keV too broad in the small space
- Trineutron energy variation with changes in the central depth V0 of the generating potential: about 50 keV when V0 changes by a few MeV

## Context
This work extends previous ab-initio calculations of multi-neutron systems, including no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using JISP16 interactions (2016) and larger model spaces (2018), quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations with local chiral interactions (2017), and earlier NCGSM calculations using density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) methods by Fossez et al. (2017). The present results for tetraneutron energy and width are comparable with the experimental data from the 4He(8He,8Be) reaction by Kisamori et al. (2016). The calculated tetraneutron width of 2.38 MeV disagrees with the earlier NCGSM-DMRG speculation of a width larger than 3.7 MeV, which the authors attribute to the use of a truncated NCGSM-2p2h space in that prior work. The results enable experimental searches for trineutron at low energy and demonstrate the importance of including continuum coupling and higher-order correlations (NCGSM-4p4h) for accurate resonance widths near the neutron drip line.
