# Centrality fluctuations and decorrelations in heavy-ion collisions

> The centrality or the number of initial-state sources $V$ of the system produced in heavy ion collision is a concept that is not uniquely defined and subject to significant theoretical and experimental uncertainties. We argue that a more robust connection between the initial-state sources with final-state multiplicity could be established from the event-by-event multiplicity correlation between two subevents separated in pseudorapidity, $N_a$ vs $N_b$. This correlation is sensitive to two main types of centrality fluctuations (CF): 1) particle production for each source $p(n)$ which smears the relation between $V$ and $N_a$ used for experimental centrality, and 2) decorrelations between the sources in the two subevents $V_b$ and $V_a$. The CF is analyzed in terms of cumulants of $V_b$ and $N_b$ as a function of $N_a$, i.e. experimental centrality is defined with $N_a$. We found that the mean values $\langle V_b\rangle_{N_a}$ and $\langle N_b\rangle_{N_a}$ increase linearly with $N_a$ in mid-central collisions, but flatten out in ultra-central collisions. Such non-linear behavior is sensitive to the centrality resolution of $N_a$. In the presence of centrality decorrelations, the scaled variances $\langle(δV_b)^2\rangle/\langle V_b\rangle$ and $\langle(δN_b)^2\rangle/\langle N_b\rangle$ are found to decrease linearly with $N_a$ in mid-central collisions, while the $p(n)$ leads to another sharp decrease in the ultra-central region. The higher-order cumulants of $V_b$ and $N_b$ show interesting but rather complex behaviors which deserve further studies. Our results suggest that one can use the cumulants of the two-dimensional multiplicity correlation, especially the mean and variance, to constrain the particle production mechanism as well as the longitudinal fluctuations of the initial-state sources.

# Centrality fluctuations and decorrelations in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that the connection between initial-state sources (participant nucleons) and final-state multiplicity can be more robustly established from event-by-event multiplicity correlations between two pseudorapidity-separated subevents, but this connection is subject to two distinct types of centrality fluctuations: smearing from particle production per source and decorrelations between sources in different pseudorapidity regions. The authors find that the mean values of source number and multiplicity in the second subevent increase linearly with multiplicity in the first subevent for mid-central collisions but flatten in ultra-central collisions, with the non-linear behavior sensitive to the centrality resolution.

## Method

The study uses an independent-source Glauber model framework where particle production per source follows a negative binomial distribution (NBD), tuned to reproduce ATLAS data. For the multiplicity smearing scenario, the same sources (participant nucleons) generate particles in both subevents A and B; for the centrality decorrelation scenario, forward-going and backward-going wounded nucleons serve as separate sources for each subevent. The analysis calculates cumulants (mean, scaled variance, skewness, kurtosis) of the source number and multiplicity distributions as functions of the multiplicity in the centrality-defining subevent A, for Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, Cu+Cu, S+S, and O+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Three NBD parameter sets are used: Par0 (mean n=7.6, RMS/mean=0.65, k2=3.2, k3=17.3, k4=139), Par1 (mean n=7.6, RMS/mean=0.88, k2=5.9, k3=63.8, k4=1031), Par2 (mean n=7.6, RMS/mean=1.35, k2=13.8, k3=368, k4=14692)
- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section of 68 mb is used for collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- Systems studied cover total nucleon numbers 2A = 258 (Xe+Xe), 126 (Cu+Cu), 64 (S+S), and 32 (O+O)
- The knee multiplicity N_knee = 2A * n_bar, where n_bar = 7.6 for the NBD parameters used
- The scaled variance of multiplicity fluctuations in subevent B for mid-central collisions follows: <(delta N_b)^2>/<N_b>^2 = (1/<V>_Na) * (<(delta n_b)^2>/n_bar^2 + <(delta n_a)^2>/n_bar^2)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of centrality fluctuations by Skokov et al. (2012) and Zhou et al. (2018) from mean multiplicity scaling to higher-order cumulants, and incorporates the forward-backward asymmetry of participant nucleons previously studied by Jia et al. (2015) and Jia et al. (2014). The results enable experimental constraints on particle production mechanisms and longitudinal fluctuations of initial-state sources through comparison of two-dimensional multiplicity correlation cumulants between data and model, and are relevant for understanding background sources in searches for the QCD critical point via conserved quantity fluctuations and for interpreting harmonic flow decorrelation measurements by ATLAS and CMS.
