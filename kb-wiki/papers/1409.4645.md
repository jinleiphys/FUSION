# Shape co-existence and parity doublet in Zr isotopes

> We studied the ground and excited states properties for Zr isotopes starting^M from proton to neutron drip-lines using the relativistic and non-relativistic mean field formalisms with BCS and Bogolyubov pairing. The celebrity NL3 and SLy4 parameter sets are used in the calculations. We find spherical ground and low-lying^M superdeformed excited states in most of the isotopes. Several couples of^M $Ω^π=1/2^{\pm}$ parity doublets configurations are found,^M while analyzing the single-particle energy levels of the superdeformed ^M configurations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that shape co-existence (spherical and low-lying superdeformed excited states) occurs in most Zr isotopes from proton to neutron drip-lines, and that several couples of Ω^π = 1/2^± parity doublet configurations appear in the superdeformed configurations. The hedge is that parity doublets are only visible in nuclei with highly deformed shape, not in normal or spherical shapes, and that the origin of this phenomenon is not yet clearly known.

## Method
The authors used relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set and non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) theory with the SLy4 parameter set, both with BCS and Bogoliubov pairing prescriptions. Calculations were performed using axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis expansion with N_F = N_B = 12 oscillator quanta for RMF and N_sh = 20 for SHF, solving the equations self-consistently. Constraint calculations as a function of quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ were performed to obtain potential energy surfaces and identify shape co-existence.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy difference ΔBE ≤ 1 MeV between ground and first/second intrinsic excited states for isotopes ⁹⁶,⁹⁸,¹⁰⁰,¹⁰²,¹⁰⁸Zr, indicating shape co-existence (including triple shape co-existence)
- For ¹¹⁰Zr, three minima located at β₂ = -0.217, 0.0, and 0.398 in the potential energy surface
- For ¹⁰⁰Zr, the ground state binding energy is 847.7 MeV (RMF, β₂ = 0.445) and 849.7 MeV (SHF, β₂ = 0.421), with an oblate solution at β₂ = -0.217 (RMF) and -0.210 (SHF)
- For ⁸²Zr, spherical solution binding energy is 691.7 MeV (RMF) and 694.4 MeV (SHF), compared to experimental value 694.5 MeV
- For ⁹⁸Zr, the superdeformed solution (β₂ = 0.497 in RMF) becomes the ground state
- Pairing gaps for neutrons range from 1.673 MeV (⁸⁰Zr) to 0.685 MeV (¹¹²Zr) in RMF; for protons from 1.673 MeV (⁸⁰Zr) to 0.892 MeV (¹¹²Zr)

## Context
This work extends studies of shape co-existence in the A=80 mass region previously reported using RMF formalism by Patra (1993), Rana, and Chakra (2013), showing that not only neutron-deficient Zr isotopes but also normal and neutron-rich Zr isotopes possess low-lying superdeformed configurations. The paper also investigates parity doublets, a relatively new phenomenon reported by Singh (2014) and Arima (1993), which the authors find to be associated with highly deformed configurations rather than spherical shapes. The results enable further study of shape transitions and parity doublet origins in the Zr isotopic chain, with the authors noting that triaxial calculations (γ ≠ 0) would be welcome for a more detailed account of potential energy surfaces, as suggested by Tomas (2011).
