# Effects of $NN$ potentials on $p$ nuclides in the A$\sim$100-120 region

> Microscopic optical potentials for low energy proton reactions have been obtained by folding density dependent M3Y interaction derived from nuclear matter calculation with densities from mean field approach to study astrophysically important proton rich nuclei in mass 100-120 region. We compare S factors for low-energy $(p,γ)$ reactions with available experimental data and further calculate astrophysical reaction rates for $(p,γ)$ and $(p,n)$ reactions. Again we choose some nonlinear R3Y interactions from RMF calculation and folded them with corresponding RMF densities to reproduce experimental S factor values in this mass region. Finally the effect of nonlinearity on our result is discussed.

## Key claim

Microscopic optical potentials obtained by folding density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) and nonlinear R3Y (NR3Y) interactions with relativistic mean-field (RMF) densities can reproduce experimental S factors for low-energy (p,γ) reactions in the A∼100-120 region, but the nonlinear NR3Y(NL3) potential fails at higher energies (above 6 MeV) because its repulsive component overpowers the attractive part, unlike the DDM3Y and NR3Y(TM1) potentials.

## Method

The authors used the RMF approach with FSUGold, NL3, and TM1 parameter sets to calculate nuclear densities for p-nuclei in the A∼100-120 region. They folded these densities with three types of NN interactions: the phenomenological DDM3Y interaction (derived from Reid potential G-matrix elements with a density-dependent factor), and two nonlinear R3Y interactions (NR3Y(NL3) and NR3Y(TM1)) derived from the RMF Lagrangian including scalar meson self-coupling terms. The resulting microscopic optical potentials were used in the Hauser-Feshbach formalism via the TALYS1.2 code to compute cross sections, S factors, and astrophysical reaction rates for (p,γ) and (p,n) reactions, with comparisons to experimental data and NONSMOKER rates.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for 102Pd: 8.480 MeV (FSUGold), 8.537 MeV (TM1), 8.572 MeV (NL3), compared to experimental 8.580 MeV
- Charge radius for 102Pd: 4.460 fm (FSUGold), 4.476 fm (TM1), 4.483 fm (NL3), compared to experimental 4.483 fm
- Charge radius for 112Sn: 4.595 fm (FSUGold), 4.598 fm (TM1), 4.594 fm (NL3), compared to experimental 4.594 fm
- Binding energy per nucleon for 120Te: 8.461 MeV (FSUGold), 8.461 MeV (TM1), 8.460 MeV (NL3), compared to experimental 8.477 MeV
- Charge radius for 120Te: 4.682 fm (FSUGold), 4.688 fm (TM1), 4.735 fm (NL3), compared to experimental 4.704 fm
- The DDM3Y folded potential for 120Sn elastic scattering at 9.7 MeV was multiplied by factors 0.3 (real) and 0.7 (imaginary) to fit experimental angular distribution data
- For the NR3Y(NL3) interaction, the effective potential coefficients include: 10395 (e^{-3.97r}/4r), 1257 (e^{-3.87r}/4r), -6554 (e^{-2.58r}/4r), 6830 r (e^{-5.15r}/4), and 52384 (e^{-7.73r}/4r) in MeV

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic optical model studies in the A∼55-100 region (Lahiri et al., earlier works; Ganguly et al.) to the A∼100-120 mass range relevant to p-process nucleosynthesis. It builds on the global Hauser-Feshbach calculations of Rauscher et al. (NONSMOKER rates) and the nonlinear R3Y interaction formalism introduced by Sahu et al. The results show that DDM3Y-folded potentials reproduce experimental S factors well and yield reaction rates similar to NONSMOKER values, while the nonlinear NR3Y(NL3) potential requires an upper cutoff on the coupling constants g2 and g3 to avoid excessive repulsion that suppresses higher angular momentum channel contributions at energies above 6 MeV. This enables future application of the microscopic approach to proton-rich nuclei inaccessible to experiment in the p-process network.
