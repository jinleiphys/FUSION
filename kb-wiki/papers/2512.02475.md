# Neutron-deuteron scattering revisited with the EKM chiral nuclear force and the WPCD method

> We revisit the neutron-deuteron scattering using the Wave-Packet Continuum Discretization (WPCD) method with the EKM chiral nuclear force at various chiral orders. We rederive the permutation operator and solve the Faddeev-AGS equations directly, without rewriting the initial Faddeev kernel $tG_0$ and introducing pseudo-states, thereby rendering the approach easily extendable to a relativistic framework. We find that up to the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO), although one can well describe the differential cross sections, one cannot resolve the long-standing $A_y$ puzzle, consistent with previous studies. The fact that the N$^3$LO chiral forces can well describe the $NN$ phase shifts and the results obtained with the EKM and Idaho N$^3$LO chiral forces agree with each other underscores the need for further investigations to resolve the $A_y$ puzzle, e.g., considering three-body forces or relativistic effects.

# Neutron-deuteron scattering revisited with the EKM chiral nuclear force and the WPCD method

## Key claim

Up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO), the EKM chiral nuclear force can describe differential cross sections for neutron-deuteron scattering well, but it cannot resolve the long-standing \(A_y\) puzzle, consistent with previous studies. The authors emphasize that the N³LO chiral forces agree with each other (EKM and Idaho) and can describe NN phase shifts, underscoring the need for further investigations such as three-body forces or relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev-AGS equations for neutron-deuteron scattering using the Wave-Packet Continuum Discretization (WPCD) method with the EKM chiral nuclear force at various chiral orders. They rederive the permutation operator and solve the equations directly using the initial Faddeev kernel \(tG_0\) without introducing pseudo-states, employing a complex spectator momentum contour to handle the deuteron pole singularity. The calculations are performed in a partial-wave basis with \(J \leq 17/2\) and \(j \leq 2\), using the epsilon algorithm to accelerate the Neumann series for the transition operator.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: \(E_d \simeq -2.2245\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Momentum cutoff: \(p_{\mathrm{cut}} = 4.0\ \mathrm{GeV}\), with \(N = 150\) grid points and \(N_{\mathrm{add}} = 2\)
- Phase shifts computed at \(E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 13\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(J^P \leq 7/2^\pm\); eigen phase shifts reproduced with relative error of approximately \(1\%\) compared to standard Faddeev results using the Nijmegen-I potential
- Neutron analyzing power \(A_y(n)\) computed at \(E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(35\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for benchmarking
- Differential cross sections and \(A_y\) studied for \(E_{\mathrm{lab}} \lesssim 50\ \mathrm{MeV}\); deviations in \(A_y\) around \(\theta_{c.m.} \simeq 120^\circ\) for \(E_{\mathrm{lab}} \lesssim 53\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- 18 channels for \(J = 1/2\), 30 channels for \(J = 3/2\), and 34 channels for \(J \geq 5/2\)

## Context

This work extends the WPCD method previously applied by Miller (2022) with Idaho N³LO and N²LO\(_\mathrm{opt}\) interactions, now using the EKM chiral force and solving the Faddeev-AGS equations without pseudo-states to enable future relativistic generalization. The results confirm the persistent \(A_y\) puzzle, which has remained unresolved for over 30 years despite studies with various NN interactions (Gloeckle, 1995) and chiral forces including three-nucleon forces (Golak, 2014; LENPIC, 2015). The framework is designed to be extendable to relativistic chiral forces recently developed by Lu (2021, 2025), which may provide new insights into the puzzle.
