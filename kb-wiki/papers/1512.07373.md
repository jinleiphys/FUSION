# Multiplicity fluctuations of net protons on the hydrodynamic freeze-out surface

> This proceeding briefly summarizes our recent work on calculating the correlated fluctuations of net protons on the hydrodynamic freeze-out surface near the QCD critical point. For both Poisson and Binomial baselines, our calculations could roughly reproduce the energy dependent cumulant $C_4$ and $κσ^2$ of net protons, but always over-predict $C_2$ and $C_3$ due to the positive contributions from the static critical fluctuations.

## Key claim

The authors show that static critical fluctuations added to Poisson or Binomial non-critical baselines can roughly reproduce the energy-dependent cumulant \(C_4\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) of net protons, but always over-predict \(C_2\) and \(C_3\) because the static critical fluctuations contribute positively to these cumulants.

## Method

The authors implemented a freeze-out scheme that couples decoupled classical particles to an order parameter field \(\sigma(x)\) near the QCD critical point, using a modified distribution function expanded to linear order in \(\sigma\). They used the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 to generate the freeze-out surface for Au+Au collisions, then computed the connected 2-point, 3-point, and 4-point correlators of net-proton multiplicity fluctuations by integrating the sigma-field correlators over the freeze-out surface. The total cumulants were taken as the sum of non-critical (Poisson or Binomial) baselines and the static critical fluctuations, with parameters \(g\), \(\tilde{\lambda}_3\), \(\tilde{\lambda}_4\), and correlation length \(\xi\) tuned per collision energy.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4\) and \(200\) GeV, the model calculations with Binomial baselines could nicely fit the energy-dependent \(C_4\) within two different \(p_T\) ranges: \(0.4 < p_T < 0.8\) GeV and \(0.4 < p_T < 2\) GeV.
- With the maximum \(p_T\) increased from \(0.8\) GeV to \(2\) GeV, the averaged numbers of net protons almost increase by a factor of two, leading to large increases in higher cumulants.
- For 30-40% centrality, model calculations with Binomial baselines could nicely fit \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\), but failed to fit \(\kappa\sigma^2\) with Poisson baselines.

## Context

This work extends the static critical fluctuation formalism of Stephanov (2009) by implementing it on a realistic hydrodynamic freeze-out surface from VISH2+1, rather than assuming a static infinite medium. It directly addresses the STAR collaboration measurements of net-proton cumulants in Au+Au collisions at multiple beam energies (Aggarwal 2010, Adamczyk 2013, Luo 2015). The results enable future work to incorporate dynamical evolution of the sigma field and more realistic thermal fluctuation baselines to resolve the sign problem of \(C_2^{\text{critical}}\) and \(C_3^{\text{critical}}\).
