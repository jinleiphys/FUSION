# First-forbidden $\mathbf╬▓$-decay rates, energy rates of $╬▓$-delayed neutrons and probability of $╬▓$-delayed neutron emissions for neutron-rich nickel isotopes

> First-forbidden (FF) transitions can play an important role in decreasing the calculated half-lives specially in environments where allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions are unfavored. Of special mention is the case of neutron-rich nuclei where, due to phase-space amplification, FF transitions are much favored. We calculate the allowed GT transitions in various pn-QRPA models for even-even neutron-rich isotopes of nickel. Here we also study the effect of deformation on the calculated GT strengths. The FF transitions for even-even neutron-rich isotopes of nickel are calculated assuming the nuclei to be spherical. Later we take into account deformation of nuclei and calculate GT + unique FF transitions, stellar $╬▓$-decay rates, energy rate of $╬▓$-delayed neutrons and probability of $╬▓$-delayed neutron emissions. The calculated half-lives are in excellent agreement with measured ones and might contribute in speeding-up of the $r$-matter flow.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including first-forbidden (FF) transitions, particularly unique first-forbidden (U1F) transitions, significantly improves the agreement between calculated and measured ╬▓-decay half-lives for neutron-rich nickel isotopes, with the pn-QRPA(N) model showing "overall excellent agreement" with experimental data. The authors hedge that this improvement is conditional on using mass-dependent interaction constants and that the predictive power of the model improves for shorter half-lives (i.e., with increasing distance from the line of stability).

## Method
The authors performed two sets of calculations using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with different single-particle potentials. The first set used a Woods-Saxon potential with three variants (spherical schematic model SSM, spherical Pyatov's method SPM, and deformed schematic model DSM) to calculate allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and full FF (rank 0, 1, 2) transitions assuming spherical nuclei. The second set used a deformed Nilsson potential [pn-QRPA(N) model] to calculate GT plus U1F transitions, stellar ╬▓-decay rates, energy rates of ╬▓-delayed neutrons, and probabilities of ╬▓-delayed neutron emission for even-even neutron-rich nickel isotopes (РЂи┬▓Ni, РЂиРЂ┤Ni, РЂиРЂХNi, РЂиРЂИNi) under stellar conditions, using mass-dependent interaction constants ¤Є = 4.2/A MeV for allowed and 56.16/A MeV fmРЂ╗┬▓ for U1F transitions.

## Key numbers
- Contribution of U1F rates to total ╬▓-decay half-lives in the pn-QRPA(N) model: 19.7% for РЂи┬▓Ni, 24.0% for РЂиРЂ┤Ni, 18.5% for РЂиРЂХNi, and 17.5% for РЂиРЂИNi.
- For low and intermediate stellar densities (10┬▓ and 10РЂХ g/cm┬│), the U1F phase space is a factor 4 bigger than the allowed phase space at low temperatures, and around a factor 25 bigger at high stellar temperatures (TРѓЅ РЅЦ 1).
- At high stellar density (10┬╣РЂ░ g/cm┬│) and high temperatures, the U1F phase space is around a factor 25 bigger than the allowed phase space.
- For low and intermediate stellar densities, the phase space increases by 4-8 orders of magnitude as stellar temperature goes from TРѓЅ = 0.01 to 1.
- For РЂи┬▓Ni at TРѓЅ = 30 and density 10РЂи g/cm┬│, positron capture rates exceed ╬▓-decay rates by a factor of 25 for allowed GT transitions, and by around three orders of magnitude for U1F transitions.
- The interaction constants used were ¤Є = 4.2/A MeV for allowed transitions and ¤Є = 56.16/A MeV fmРЂ╗┬▓ for U1F transitions, with ╬║ = 0.
- A quenching factor of 0.6 was applied for all pn-QRPA(WS) and pn-QRPA(N) calculations.
- The pairing correlation constants were taken as CРѓЎ = CРѓџ = 12/РѕџA.

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of allowed GT and U1F rates for nickel isotopes in stellar environments by Nabi (2013), which suggested incorporating rank 0 and rank 1 operators for full FF coverage. It also builds on the work of Borzov (2006), who used QRPA with the Fayans energy functional for consistent treatment of allowed and FF contributions to r-process half-lives, and of Zhi (2013), who performed large-scale shell-model calculations including first-forbidden contributions for r-process waiting-point nuclei. The results enable more reliable predictions of ╬▓-decay properties for neutron-rich nickel isotopes near the neutron drip line where no experimental data exist, and may contribute to understanding the speed-up of r-matter flow in explosive nucleosynthesis.
