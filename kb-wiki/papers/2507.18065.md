# Re-examination of fusion hindrance in astrophysical $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C reactions

> To determine the energy dependence of fusion cross sections at extremely low energies is crucial for various astrophysical processes. In the previous study by Jiang et al. [Phys. Rev. C75, 015803 (2007)], it was concluded that fusion cross sections for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system rapidly drop off as the energy decreases. We here re-examine this hindrance phenomenon. While the previous study fitted the logarithmic slope $L(E)$ of fusion cross sections with a function of $L(E)=A+B/E^n$ and searched the optimum value of $A$ and $B$ with $n=1.5$, we refit the data with the same function for $L(E)$ but by releasing the restriction on $n$. We find that the optimum values of $n$ significantly deviates from $n=1.5$, resulting in the absence of hindrance of fusion cross sections both in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C systems.

## Key claim

The authors re-examine the fusion hindrance phenomenon in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C systems and find that, when the exponent $n$ in the fitting function $L(E)=A+B/E^n$ is allowed to vary freely rather than being fixed to $n=1.5$ as in the prior study by Jiang et al. (2007), the optimum values of $n$ deviate significantly from 1.5, resulting in the absence of hindrance in both systems within the error bars.

## Method

The authors fit the astrophysical $S$ factors for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C reactions using the integrated form of the logarithmic slope function $L(E)=A+B/E^n$, treating $A$, $B$, $n$, and the normalization cross section $\sigma'$ as free parameters. Both weighted least squares (WLS) and ordinary least squares (OLS) fitting methods are employed, and the covariance matrices of the fitted parameters are used to propagate uncertainties to the predicted $S$ factors and logarithmic slopes. For the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system, experimental data from Patterson (1969), Mazarakis (1973), High (1977), Aguilera (2006), and Jiang (2018) are used; for the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system, data from Stokstad (1976), Dasmahapatra (1982), ND2012, and Zhang (2020) are adopted.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system, the best-fit value of $n$ is $0.482 \pm 0.055$ (WLS) and $-0.90 \pm 0.73$ (OLS), both deviating significantly from $n=1.5$ used by Jiang et al. (2007).
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system, the best-fit value of $n$ is $1.01 \pm 0.02$ (WLS) and $0.132 \pm 0.072$ (OLS).
- The variance of $n$ for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C WLS fit is $2.97 \times 10^{-3}$; for the OLS fit it is $0.53$.
- The best-fit parameters for the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C WLS fit are: $A = -12.52 \pm 1.83$ MeV$^{-1}$, $B = 34.61 \pm 0.96$ MeV$^{n-1}$, $\sigma' = (1.67 \pm 0.25) \times 10^{-11}$ barn.
- The best-fit parameters for the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C WLS fit are: $A = -5.49 \pm 0.19$ MeV$^{-1}$, $B = 43.94 \pm 0.62$ MeV$^{n-1}$, $\sigma' = (1.21 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{-11}$ barn.

## Context

This work directly re-examines the conclusion of Jiang et al. (2007) that strong fusion hindrance occurs in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system, by relaxing the previously fixed exponent $n=1.5$ in the logarithmic slope fitting function. The results contradict the hindrance model predictions and are consistent with recent indirect measurements using the Trojan Horse method by Tumino (2018) and measurements for the $^{12}$C+$^{13}$C system by Zhang (2020), as well as theoretical calculations based on anti-symmetric molecular dynamics by Taniguchi (2021, 2024). The finding implies that the ignition of superbursts in astrophysical environments may not be suppressed by hindrance, and calls for a careful treatment of the fitting function and data selection in future extrapolations to Gamow energies.
