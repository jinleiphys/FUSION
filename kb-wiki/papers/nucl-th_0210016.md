# Energies of the ground state and first excited $0^{+}$ state in an exactly solvable pairing model

> Several approximations are tested by calculating the ground-state energy and the energy of the first excited $0^{+}$ state using an exactly solvable model with two symmetric levels interacting via a pairing force. They are the BCS approximation (BCS), Lipkin - Nogami (LN) method, random-phase approximation (RPA), quasiparticle RPA (QRPA), the renormalized RPA (RRPA), and renormalized QRPA (RQRPA). It is shown that, in the strong-coupling regime, the QRPA which neglects the scattering term of the model Hamiltonian offers the best fit to the exact solutions. A recipe is proposed using the RRPA and RQRPA in combination with the pairing gap given by the LN method. Applying this recipe, it is shown that the normal-superfluid phase transition is avoided, and a reasonably good description for both of the ground-state energy and the energy of the first excited $0^{+}$ state is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper tests several approximations (BCS, Lipkin-Nogami, RPA, QRPA, renormalized RPA, and renormalized QRPA) against exact solutions of a two-level pairing model, finding that in the strong-coupling regime the QRPA that neglects the scattering term offers the best fit, and proposing a recipe that combines the renormalized RPA or renormalized QRPA with the pairing gap from the Lipkin-Nogami method to avoid the spurious normal-superfluid phase transition and achieve a reasonably good description of both the ground-state energy and the first excited 0+ state energy.

## Method

The paper uses an exactly solvable two-level model with N particles occupying two symmetric levels of degeneracy Ω = N/2, separated by energy ε = 1 MeV, interacting via a pairing force. The exact solutions are obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian using SU(2) algebra, and the ground-state energy and first excited 0+ state energy are compared with results from BCS, Lipkin-Nogami (LN), RPA, QRPA, renormalized RPA (RRPA), and renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) approximations. The QRPA equations are derived in both a boson formalism (using the quasiboson approximation) and a fermion formalism (using exact commutation relations with an average quasiboson approximation), and the renormalized versions incorporate ground-state correlations beyond the quasiboson approximation.

## Key numbers

- The level spacing ε is set to 1 MeV in all calculations.
- The BCS critical coupling constant is G_cr = ε̃/(2Ω), where ε̃ = ε when neglecting the self-energy term or ε̃ = 2Ωε/(2Ω−1) when including it.
- The BCS gap is Δ = GΩ √[1 − (G_cr/G)²], and the LN gap is Δ_LN = GΩ √[1 − ε̄²/(4G²Ω²)] with ε̄ = 2GΩε/(2GΩ+α).
- In the boson formalism, the QRPA phonon energy is ω_QRPA^(b) = 2Δ, and the RPA phonon energy is ω_RPA^(b) = 2ε √(1 − G/G_cr^(b)) with G_cr^(b) = ε/(2Ω).
- In the fermion formalism, the first 0+ excited state energy is ω₁ = 2Δ √[(1 − 3/(4Ω))(1 − Δ²/(4G²Ω³))], and the spurious state energy is ω₀^s = Δ √[(1/Ω)(1 − Δ²/(4G²Ω³))].
- The LN gap Δ_LN vanishes only at G = 0, showing no signature of the superfluid-normal phase transition.

## Context

This work extends tests of RPA-based methods using exactly solvable models, following earlier studies by Hagino (2001) and Stetcu (2001) that tested HF+RPA calculations in schematic models and realistic sd-shell and pf-shell nuclei. The paper proposes a recipe combining the LN method (which removes particle-number fluctuations) with renormalized RPA or renormalized QRPA to avoid the spurious phase transition that plagues standard BCS and RPA calculations, enabling a simultaneous description of ground-state and first excited 0+ state energies in pairing models.
