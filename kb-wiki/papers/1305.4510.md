# A renormalization group approach for QCD in a strong magnetic field

> A Wilsonian renormalization group approach is applied, in order to include effects of the higher Landau levels for quarks into a set of renormalized parameters for the lowest Landau level (LLL), plus a set of operators made of the LLL fields. Most of the calculations can be done in a model-independent way with perturbation theory for hard gluons, thanks to form factors of quark-gluon vertices that arise from the Ritus bases for quark fields. As a part of such renormalization program, we compute the renormalized quark self-energy at 1-loop, including effects from all higher orbital levels. The result indicates that the higher orbital levels cease to strongly affect the LLL at a rather small magnetic field of (0.1 - 0.3) GeV^2.

## Key claim

The authors apply a Wilsonian renormalization group approach to integrate out higher Landau levels (hLLs) for QCD in a strong magnetic field, finding that the higher orbital levels cease to strongly affect the lowest Landau level (LLL) already at a rather small magnetic field of (0.1 - 0.3) GeV^2, due to the small running coupling appearing in the functional integration of the higher orbital levels.

## Method

The authors use Ritus bases to diagonalize the quark action in a uniform magnetic field, then compute 1-loop perturbative contributions from all higher orbital levels to the renormalized LLL quark self-energy. They separate the LLL intermediate states to avoid large logarithms associated with dimensional reduction, and use renormalized perturbation theory with counter terms determined at B=0, reorganizing the self-energy into contributions from l=0 and l>=1 levels. The form factors arising from the Ritus bases suppress soft gluon contributions for hopping between different orbital levels, allowing perturbative treatment of hard gluons.

## Key numbers

* The higher orbital levels cease to strongly affect the LLL at a magnetic field of (0.1 - 0.3) GeV^2
* At |B| = 0.12 GeV^2, the optimized renormalization scale mu* reaches 1.0 GeV for all p_L^2 values
* The optimized renormalization scale mu* for p_L^2=0 is approximately mu*^2 = C|B| with C ~ 8.8 from more precise numerical computations (or ~15 from crude approximation)
* The QCD scale Lambda_MS^{Nf=5} = (213 +/- 8) MeV is used for running coupling and mass calculations
* The beta function coefficients are beta_0 = (11Nc - 2Nf)/(48 pi^2) and gamma_{m0} = 3 C_F/(4 pi)^2 with (Nc, Nf) = (3, 5)

## Context

This work extends the study of QCD in strong magnetic fields beyond previous renormalization group studies (Fukushima 2012, and others) that focused on non-perturbative LLL dynamics of QED or 4-Fermi interaction models. The authors' approach differs by applying RG only to hLLs and hard gluons, not to the LLL dynamics where non-perturbative gluons couple. The results enable justification of the lowest orbital level approximation for non-perturbative phenomena at |eB| > 0.3 GeV^2, and complement lattice QCD results (Bali 2011, Bali 2012) showing linear dependence of chiral condensate on |eB| at large fields.
