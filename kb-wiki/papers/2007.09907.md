# Towards the continuum coupling in nuclear lattice effective field theory I: A three-particle model

> Weakly bound states often occur in nuclear physics. To precisely understand their properties, the coupling to the continuum should be worked out explicitely. In a first step, we use a simple nuclear model in the continuum and on a lattice to investigate the influence of a third particle on a loosely bound state of a particle and a heavy core.

## Key claim

Using a simplified three-particle model (a heavy core A and two nucleons N) on a lattice, the authors show that the influence of an unbound third particle on a weakly bound two-particle state leaves visible traces in the finite-volume energy spectrum, but caution that the model requires much refinement before drawing more definite conclusions about continuum coupling in nuclear lattice effective field theory.

## Method

The authors construct a separable potential for AN → AN scattering, tuned to produce a weakly bound state B with keV binding energy. They then employ a dimer approximation to reduce the full ANN system to an effective BN → BN scattering problem, computing the BN potential from A-exchange (attractive) and from a triangle-loop diagram involving NN interactions (repulsive). Using the Hamiltonian formalism in a finite cubic volume, they calculate energy levels and phase shifts for the BN system, comparing results with the Lüscher equation.

## Key numbers

- The AN binding energy is |E_B| = 11.15 keV for coupling constant g = -31.0
- The A particle mass is set to m_A = 10 GeV, while the N particle mass and regulator cutoff are m = Λ = 1 GeV
- For case 1 (g_NN = 48.34), the BN potential cancels exactly at threshold; the phase shift near threshold increases to about 10°
- For case 2 (g_NN = 96.68), the BN potential is repulsive at threshold; the largest phase shift is around 1°
- Finite-volume calculations are performed at L = 10 fm and L = 17 fm

## Context

This work extends the nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) framework, which has successfully calculated the Hoyle state in ¹²C (Epelbaum, 2011) and low-energy α-α scattering (Elhatisari, 2015), but currently lacks explicit coupling to the continuum. The authors aim to develop a formalism analogous to Lüscher's method (Lüscher, 1986; Lüscher, 1990) for mapping continuum effects onto lattice energy levels. This first step uses a simplified three-particle model; future work requires full three-body ANN calculations, more realistic nuclear potentials, and inclusion of higher partial waves to enable ab initio studies of weakly bound halo nuclei and isotopes near drip lines.
