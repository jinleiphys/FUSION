# Probing nuclear liquid-gas phase transition with isospin correlations

> We investigate the fluctuations of the net-baryon number near the critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition. We use the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation fixed to the zero-temperature properties of nuclear matter to account for critical behavior. We explicitly calculate the fluctuations of the net-proton and net-neutron numbers as well as their correlations in the isospin-symmetric matter. We focus on the qualitative properties and systematics of the first- to fourth-order susceptibilities and their ratios. We demonstrate that the fluctuations of net-proton number do not reflect the total net-baryon number fluctuations in the vicinity of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. We also study the behavior of the baryon and proton number factorial cumulants. We highlight the importance of the non-trivial correlations between protons and neutrons.

# Probing nuclear liquid-gas phase transition with isospin correlations

## Key claim

The net-proton number fluctuations do not reflect the total net-baryon number fluctuations in the vicinity of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, because non-trivial proton-neutron correlations arise from interactions mediated by mean fields. This conclusion holds for isospin-symmetric matter where protons and neutrons are equally populated.

## Method

The authors use the parity doublet model in the mean-field approximation, fixed to zero-temperature nuclear matter properties, to calculate generalized susceptibilities (first through fourth order) and factorial cumulants of net-baryon, net-proton, and net-neutron numbers. The thermodynamic potential includes positive-parity nucleons (N(939)) and their negative-parity chiral partners (N*(1535)), though the latter are thermally suppressed near the liquid-gas transition. The system is restricted to low temperatures and small densities near the liquid-gas critical point, with isospin symmetry assumed throughout.

## Key numbers

- Critical point of the liquid-gas phase transition: T_c = 16 MeV, μ_B = 909 MeV, n_B = 0.053 fm^{-3} = 0.33 n_sat
- Critical point of the chiral phase transition: T_c = 7 MeV, μ_B = 1526 MeV, n_B = 1.25 fm^{-3} = 7.82 n_0
- Vacuum mass of negative-parity nucleons: 1.5 GeV
- At T = 30 MeV, the second-order net-baryon susceptibility χ_2^B shows a clear maximum at the crossover transition
- At small chemical potentials, χ_2^{pn} ≈ 0 and χ_2^B ≈ 2 χ_2^p
- The proton-neutron correlator χ_2^{pn} becomes negative at larger chemical potentials after the crossover
- Ratios R_{3,2}^B and R_{4,2}^B show Skellam behavior (R_{3,2}^α = tanh(μ_B/T), R_{4,2}^α = 1) at small chemical potentials
- R_{3,2}^p is positive at T ≳ 20 MeV and turns negative only in the close vicinity of the critical point

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition fluctuations using the parity doublet model, following the approach of Koch (2023) and Marczenko (2023). It addresses the assumption, common in heavy-ion collision experiments such as the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2010-2022), that net-proton fluctuations reliably reflect net-baryon fluctuations. The results contradict the ideal-gas expectation of Fukushima (2014) where this simplification holds, and show qualitative agreement with quantum van der Waals approaches by Poberezhnyuk (2019). The findings are relevant for interpreting upcoming HADES Collaboration data at GSI probing the liquid-gas phase transition region at beam energies 0.2-1.0A GeV.
