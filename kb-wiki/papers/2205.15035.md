# Systematic study of fusion barrier characteristics within the relativistic mean-field formalism

> Background: The nuclear interaction potential and hence the fusion barrier formed between the interacting nuclei are the keys to understanding the complex fusion process dynamics. Purpose: This work intends to explore the fusion barrier characteristics of different target-projectile combinations within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism. Methods: The density distributions of interacting nuclei and the microscopic R3Y NN interaction are obtained from relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism for non-linear NL1, NL3, TM1, and relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach for DDME2 parameter sets. The fusion and/or capture cross-section for the different reaction systems is calculated using the well-known $\ell$-summed Wong model. Results: The barrier height and position of 24 heavy-ion reaction systems are obtained for different nuclear density distributions and effective NN interaction potentials. The comparison of fusion and/or capture cross-section obtained from the $\ell$-summed Wong model is made with the available experimental data. Conclusions: The phenomenological M3Y NN potential is observed to give higher barrier heights than the relativistic R3Y NN potential for all the reaction systems. The comparison of results obtained from different relativistic parameter sets shows that the densities from NL1 and TM1 parameter sets give the lowest and highest barrier heights for all the systems under study. We observed higher barrier heights and lower cross-sections for DDR3Y NN potential as compared to density-independent R3Y NN potentials obtained for considered non-linear NL1, NL3 and TM1 parameter sets. According to the present analysis, it is concluded that the NL1 and NL3 parameter sets provide comparatively better overlap with the experimental fusion and/or capture cross-section than the TM1 and DDME2 parameter sets.

# Systematic study of fusion barrier characteristics within the relativistic mean-field formalism

## Key claim

The phenomenological M3Y NN potential systematically yields higher fusion barrier heights than the relativistic R3Y NN potential for all 24 reaction systems studied, while among the relativistic parameter sets, NL1 and NL3 provide better agreement with experimental fusion and capture cross-sections than TM1 and DDME2, though the density-dependent DDR3Y potential produces higher barrier heights and lower cross-sections than the density-independent R3Y potentials.

## Method

The nuclear density distributions for projectile and target nuclei were obtained from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism using non-linear NL1, NL3, and TM1 parameter sets, and from the relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach for the DDME2 parameter set. These densities were folded with the microscopic R3Y NN interaction (both density-independent and density-dependent DDR3Y) and the phenomenological M3Y NN potential within the double folding approach to compute the nuclear interaction potential. The fusion and capture cross-sections for 24 heavy-ion reaction systems were then calculated using the ℓ-summed Wong model and compared with available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Barrier heights and positions were obtained for 24 heavy-ion reaction systems, including even-even systems such as ⁴⁸Ca+¹⁵⁴Sm, ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U, ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁸Cm, ²⁶Mg+²⁴⁸Cm; even-odd ⁴⁶K+¹⁸¹Ta; odd-odd ³¹Al+¹⁹⁷Au and ³⁹K+¹⁸¹Ta; and 17 systems for synthesis of superheavy nuclei with Z=120
- The R3Y NN potential for DDME2 was plotted at saturation density ρ_sat = 0.152 fm⁻³
- The M3Y NN potential contains terms 7999 e⁻⁴ʳ/(4r) and -2140 e⁻²·⁵ʳ/(2.5r) plus a delta-function term J₀₀(E)δ(r)
- NL1 and TM1 parameter sets give the lowest and highest central densities respectively for all nuclei studied, with the density at the tail/surface region playing the most crucial role in fusion

## Context

This work extends previous studies that compared M3Y and R3Y NN potentials for Ni-based reactions (Bhuyan 2018, 2020) and for heavy and superheavy nuclei synthesis (Rana 2021), which were limited to the NL3* parameter set. The present systematic analysis includes multiple non-linear RMF parameter sets (NL1, NL3, TM1) and introduces explicit medium dependence of the R3Y NN potential via the DDR3Y potential from the DDME2 parameter set for the first time in fusion studies (Rana 2022). This enables a more comprehensive understanding of how different relativistic density distributions and NN interactions affect fusion barrier characteristics and cross-sections for 24 exotic reaction systems relevant to synthesizing neutron-rich heavy and superheavy nuclei, including the doubly magic shell candidate ³⁰⁴120.
