# Surprises in nonperturbative dynamics in sigma-model at finite density

> The linear $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ sigma-model occupies a unique place in elementary particle physics and quantum field theory. It has been recently realized that when a chemical potential for hypercharge is added, it becomes a toy model for the description of the dynamics of the kaon condensate in high density QCD. We review recent results in nonperturbative dynamics obtained in the ungauged and gauged versions of this model.

## Key claim

The linear SU(2)L × SU(2)R sigma-model at finite chemical potential for hypercharge exhibits unconventional nonperturbative dynamics: in the ungauged version, spontaneous symmetry breaking produces fewer Nambu-Goldstone bosons than the Goldstone theorem requires, and the system is not a superfluid despite having a condensate; in the gauged version, the symmetry breaking is always accompanied by spontaneous breakdown of both rotational symmetry and electromagnetic U(1)em.

## Method

The authors analyze the linear SU(2)L × SU(2)R sigma-model with a chemical potential μ for hypercharge Y, first in its ungauged form and then in a version where the SU(2)L × U(1)Y subgroup is gauged. They derive the ground state solutions by minimizing the effective potential, using an ansatz that allows for spontaneous breaking of rotational invariance, and compute the spectrum of excitations by expanding the Lagrangian to quadratic order in fluctuations around the ground state. For the gauged model with m² > 0 and supercritical μ² > m², they find a ground state with nonzero expectation values for both the scalar field and the longitudinal component of the charged gauge field, breaking SU(2) × U(1)Y × SO(3) down to SO(2).

## Key numbers

- For the ungauged model with μ² > m², the dispersion relation for the K⁺ NG boson is ω₂ ≃ k²/(2μ) for k → 0, while the K⁰ NG boson has ω̃₂ ≃ √[(μ² − m²)/(3μ² − m²)] k for k → 0.
- For the ungauged model with subcritical μ < m, the energy gap of the (K⁻, K̄⁰) doublet is m + μ, and the gap of the (K⁺, K⁰) doublet is m − μ.
- In the gauged model with global U(1)Y and m² > 0, the energy gaps of excitations at zero momentum include: Δ² = 0 (three NG modes), Δ² = 2μϕ (two modes), Δ² = 2μϕ + 3ϕ² (two modes), Δ² = 4μ² (one mode), and Δ² = δ±² (two modes), where ϕ² = g²φ₀²/2.
- For the gauged model with m² < 0, the critical chemical potential for the second stage of symmetry breaking is μ̄²cr = (g²/(4λ))|m²|, where μ̄ = 2μ is the chemical potential for electric charge.

## Context

This work reviews and extends results from the authors' earlier studies (Miransky and Shovkovy; Schafer, Son, Stephanov, and Toublan) on the abnormal number of Nambu-Goldstone bosons in relativistic models at finite density, and from Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy on the gauged version. The model serves as a toy model for kaon condensation in the color-flavor locked phase of high-density QCD (Bedaque and Schafer; Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek), which may exist in compact star cores. The finding of roton-like excitations arising from gauge fields suggests a possible microscopic mechanism for roton excitations in superfluid systems, an open problem dating back to Landau and Feynman.
