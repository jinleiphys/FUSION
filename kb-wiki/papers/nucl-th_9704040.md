# Scaling Properties of Nuclear Giant Resonance Transition Probabilities

> Multifractal scaling analysis of nuclear giant resonance transition probability distributions is performed within the approximation which takes into account the one-particle-one-hole (1p-1h) and 2p-2h states. A new measure to determine the fractal dimensions of the spectra is introduced. It is found that chaotic dynamics governing the decay leads to non-trivial multifractal scaling properties. Such a kind of scaling is absent in the case of regular dynamics. The degree of collectivity is another element which worsens the scaling.

## Key claim

Chaotic dynamics governing the decay of nuclear giant resonances leads to non-trivial multifractal scaling properties in the transition probability distributions, but this scaling is absent in the case of regular dynamics and is worsened by increasing collectivity.

## Method

The authors performed a multifractal scaling analysis of giant quadrupole resonance transition probabilities in 40Ca, computed within a model that includes 1p-1h and 2p-2h states using a Woods-Saxon mean field plus Landau-Migdal residual interaction. They introduced a new measure that weights energy boxes by the transition probabilities rather than by simple point counts, and extracted generalized Renyi dimensions D_q from log-log plots of the partition function versus box number. Two versions of the model were compared: one with no residual interaction in the 2p-2h subspace (regular dynamics) and one with full matrix elements yielding GOE fluctuations (chaotic dynamics).

## Key numbers

- The model for 40Ca with J^pi = 2^+ includes 26 1p-1h states and 3,014 2p-2h states.
- Centroid energy is 31 MeV for isovector transitions and 25 MeV for isoscalar transitions, with dispersion of order 5 MeV.
- For isoscalar transitions, the fractal dimensions D_q are about 5% lower than for isovector transitions.
- The input data consist of approximately 2^11 data points, sufficient to display exponential scaling over about 8 points in the log-log plot for the chaotic case.
- The capacity dimension D_0 is approximately 1 in both cases.

## Context

This work extends the statistical analysis of nuclear spectra beyond nearest-neighbor spacing distributions (Wigner/GOE versus Poissonian, as established by Mehta (1967) and Bohigas, Giannoni and Schmit (1984)) to include wave function information via transition probabilities. The authors build on prior calculations by Drozdz, Nishizaki, Speth and Wambach (1994) showing that the 2p-2h subspace already reproduces GOE characteristics, and on the suggestion by Gorski, Botet, Drozdz and Ploszajczak (1996) that such systems can be simulated by binary self-similar random fragmentation. The results enable further investigation of quantum signatures of classical chaos and suggest that collectivity acts as a regularizing element in nuclear dynamics.
