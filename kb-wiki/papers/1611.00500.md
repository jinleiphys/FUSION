# Heavy-Quark Diffusion Dynamics in Quark-Gluon Plasma under Strong Magnetic Fields

> We discuss heavy-quark dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma under a strong magnetic field induced by colliding nuclei. By the use of the diagrammatic resummation techniques for Hard Thermal Loop and the external magnetic field, we show analytic results of heavy-quark diffusion coefficient and drag force which become anisotropic due to the preferred spatial orientation in the magnetic field. We argue that the anisotropic diffusion coefficient gives rise to an enhancement/suppression of the heavy-quark elliptic flow depending on the transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a strong magnetic field (eB >> T^2) induces a large anisotropy in the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient and drag force in the quark-gluon plasma, with the transverse diffusion coefficient enhanced and the longitudinal diffusion coefficient suppressed; this anisotropy can generate heavy-quark elliptic flow even before the full development of the background fluid flow, but the authors note that the actual enhancement or suppression of elliptic flow depends on the transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors compute the heavy-quark momentum diffusion coefficients at leading order in the strong coupling using diagrammatic resummation techniques that combine Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation with the effects of a strong external magnetic field. They model heavy-quark dynamics via a Langevin equation with anisotropic drag and noise terms, where the diffusion coefficients are obtained from Coulomb scattering amplitudes involving thermal quarks in the lowest Landau level (LLL) and thermal gluons. The Debye screening mass is computed from the gluon self-energy in the LLL approximation, and the kinematics of the scattering process are analyzed using the (1+1)-dimensional dispersion relation of massless fermions in the LLL.

## Key numbers

- The transverse momentum diffusion coefficient from thermal quarks at leading order is κ⊥^LO ∼ α_s^2 T (eB/2π) ln α_s^{-1}, where eB >> T^2.
- The longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficient from thermal quarks in the massless limit is κ∥^LO = 0.
- The gluon contribution to the longitudinal diffusion coefficient is κ∥^LO,gluon ∼ α_s^2 T^3 ln α_s^{-1}.
- The ratio of longitudinal to transverse diffusion coefficients (and corresponding drag coefficients) is κ∥^LO,gluon / κ⊥^LO = η∥^LO,gluon / η⊥^LO ∼ T^2 / eB << 1.
- The Debye screening mass in the strong-field regime scales as m_D^2 ∼ q_f (eB/2π) · (g_s^2/2π), which is much larger than the usual thermal mass squared ∼ (g_s T)^2.

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of heavy-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma (Moore and Teaney, 2004) by incorporating the effects of a strong magnetic field generated in heavy-ion collisions. The authors show that the magnetic field qualitatively changes the diffusion dynamics by making the transport coefficients anisotropic, which can affect the interpretation of open heavy-flavor observables such as R_AA and v_2 measured at RHIC and LHC. The techniques developed here have subsequently been applied to compute the jet quenching parameter (Li, 2016) and electrical conductivity (Hattori, 2016) in strong magnetic fields, and the authors suggest that numerical simulations incorporating these anisotropic coefficients are needed as a next step.
