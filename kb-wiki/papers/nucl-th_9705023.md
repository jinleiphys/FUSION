# Temperature and thermodynamic instabilities in heavy ion collisions

> We investigate thermodynamic properties and instability conditions in intermediate energy heavy ion reactions. We define locally thermodynamic variables, i.e. density, pressure and temperature, directly from the phase space distribution of a relativistic transport calculation. In particular, temperatures are determined by a fit to two covariant hot Fermi distributions thus taking into account possible anisotropic momentum configurations. We define instability independent from the nuclear matter spinodal by the criterion that the effective compressibility becomes negative. The method is applied to a semi-central Au on Au reaction at 600 MeV/nucleon. We investigate in particular the center of the participant and the spectator matter. In the latter we find a clear indication of instability with conditions of density and temperature that are consistent with experimental determinations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that thermodynamic instabilities leading to multifragmentation can be identified within a mean-field transport calculation by a local criterion (negative effective compressibility), without needing to include fluctuations explicitly. The authors hedge that this mean-field approach can determine *when* and *under what conditions* the system enters an instability region, but cannot predict how the unstable system breaks up.

## Method

The authors performed a relativistic Landau-Vlasov (RLV) transport calculation for a semi-central (b=4.5 fm) Au+Au collision at 600 MeV/nucleon, using the NL2 parameterization of the nonlinear Walecka model. Local thermodynamic variables (density, pressure, temperature) were extracted directly from the phase-space distribution at each space-time point, with temperatures obtained by fitting the local momentum distribution to a superposition of two covariant hot Fermi distributions (allowing for anisotropic, non-equilibrium configurations). Instability was identified by the criterion that the effective compressibility K_eff = 9 dP/d(rho_0) becomes negative, rather than by the static nuclear matter spinodal.

## Key numbers

*   Maximum central density reached approximately 2.5 times saturation density (rho_sat = 0.145 fm^-3).
*   Maximum central temperature was about 40 MeV, with a mean temperature of about 30 MeV over the compression phase.
*   Spectator density decreased to about 1/3 rho_sat, where it remained relatively constant for about 30 fm/c.
*   Spectator temperature decreased from about 15 MeV to a stable value around 5 MeV, which the authors identify as the break-up temperature.
*   Break-up density in the spectator was between (1/3 and 1/2) rho_sat.
*   Maximum longitudinal pressure in the center was 80 MeV fm^-3; maximum transverse pressure was 30 MeV fm^-3.
*   The temperature determined from the fit for a cold initialized nucleus was about 0.5 MeV after correcting for the finite width of the momentum-space Gaussians (sigma_k = 0.346 fm^-1).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RLV analysis of Au+Au at 400 MeV (Fuchs 1996) to 600 MeV/nucleon, the energy studied by the ALADIN collaboration (Pochodzalla 1995, Trautmann 1997) and the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf 1997). The method provides a dynamical instability criterion that differs from the static nuclear matter spinodal, and the extracted temperatures are significantly lower than those from particle slope parameters (pion spectra) but qualitatively agree with temperatures from flow analysis in the blast scenario (Reisdorf 1997). The results are intended to provide input for statistical break-up models (Bondorf 1985, Gross) and for dynamical treatments of fluctuations such as the Boltzmann-Langevin approach (Aichelin 1990, Randrup 1990, Abe 1996).
