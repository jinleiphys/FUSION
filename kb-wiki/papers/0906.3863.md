# Adiabatic expansion approximation solutions for the three-body problem

> The motion of a muon in two centers coulomb field is one of the interesting problems of quantum mechanics. The adiabatic expansion method is powerful approach to study the muonic three-body system. In this investigation the three-body problem is studied for short-range interactions. Bound states and energy levels of this system were calculated and compared with their Born-Oppenheimer method counterparts. The obtained results are in good agreement with the previous calculations.

## Key claim
The adiabatic expansion method yields binding energies for the muonic molecule \(dd\mu\) that are in good agreement with previous calculations, but the paper does not claim that the method is more accurate than alternatives; rather, it notes that the Born-Oppenheimer approximation is expected to be poor for muonic molecules and that including adiabatic corrections is necessary to obtain the correct binding for weakly bound states.

## Method
The authors solved the non-relativistic three-body Schrödinger equation for the \(dd\mu\) system using the adiabatic expansion method, in which the total wavefunction is expanded over solutions of the Coulomb two-center problem for the muon at fixed internuclear separation \(R\). After separating the center-of-mass motion and transforming to Jacobi and hyperspheroidal coordinates, they averaged over the muon state and spherical angles to obtain a radial equation for the nuclear motion, from which the adiabatic potential \(V_{Ad}(R)\) and binding energies were computed.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the \((0,0)\) state of \(dd\mu\): 325.06 eV (this work), compared to 325.0735 eV (Ref. [14]) and 325.070540 eV (Ref. [15]).
- Binding energy of the \((0,1)\) state: 35.79 eV (this work), compared to 35.8436 eV (Ref. [14]) and 35.844227 eV (Ref. [15]).
- Binding energy of the \((1,0)\) state: 226.62 eV (this work), compared to 226.6815 eV (Ref. [14]) and 226.679792 eV (Ref. [15]).
- Binding energy of the \((1,1)\) state: 1.73 eV (this work), compared to 1.97475 eV (Ref. [14]) and 1.974985 eV (Ref. [15]).
- Binding energy of the \((2,0)\) state: 86.20 eV (this work), compared to 86.4936 eV (Ref. [14]).

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of muonic molecule binding energies for muon-catalyzed fusion systems, which have been studied with various methods (Refs. [9-12] in the paper). The authors compare their adiabatic expansion results to those from the Born-Oppenheimer method and to earlier calculations by other groups (Refs. [14,15]), noting that the Born-Oppenheimer approximation alone fails to correctly describe the weakly bound \((1,1)\) state. The results enable improved modeling of the resonant formation of muonic molecules such as \(dd\mu\) in low-temperature fusion targets.
