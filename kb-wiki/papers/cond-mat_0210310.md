# Compositeness Effects in the Bose-Einstein Condensation

> Small deviations from purely bosonic behavior of trapped atomic Bose-Einstein condensates are investigated with the help of the quon algebra, which interpolates between bosonic and fermionic statistics. A previously developed formalism is employed to obtain a generalized version of the Gross-Pitaeviskii equation. Two extreme situations are considered, the collapse of the condensate for attractive forces and the depletion of the amount of condensed atoms with repulsive forces. Experimental discrepancies observed in the parameters governing the collapse and the depletion of the condensates can be accounted for by universal fittings of the deformation parameter for each case.

## Key claim

Small deviations from purely bosonic behavior in trapped atomic Bose-Einstein condensates, arising from the composite nature of the atoms, can be described phenomenologically using the quon algebra with a deformation parameter \(q\) close to \(+1\); the authors show that a single deformation parameter can account for experimental discrepancies in both the collapse threshold for attractive interactions and the chemical potential for repulsive interactions, but the parameter must be fitted separately for each case and its value depends on the density regime.

## Method

The authors derive a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation (qGP) by applying the quon algebra, which interpolates between bosonic and fermionic statistics via a deformation parameter \(q\), to a system of composite bosons in a spherical harmonic oscillator trap. They project the quonic Fock space onto the totally symmetric subspace to preserve bosonic exchange symmetry, then use a variational principle with a symmetrized \(N\)-quon state to obtain the qGP equation. Numerical solutions are obtained by expanding the ground-state wavefunction in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with angular momentum \(l=0\), and results are compared to experimental data for both attractive (collapse) and repulsive (chemical potential) interactions.

## Key numbers

* For attractive interactions, the critical collapse constant \(k_c = N_0 |a| / b_t\) (where \(N_0\) is the number of condensed atoms, \(a\) the s-wave scattering length, and \(b_t\) the trap oscillator length) is modified to \(k_c = 0.575 \left([N]/N\right)^{1/4}\); using \(N_0 = [N] = 2000\) and deformation parameter \(x = 1-q = 3\times 10^{-4}\) (i.e., \(q = 0.9997\)), the theoretical value fits the experimental result \(k_c = 0.522\) from Roberts et al. (2001).
* The deformation parameter estimated from composite boson size is \(x = 2\gamma |a|^3 / b_t^3 \sim 10^{-4}\), using \(|a| \sim 15\) Å, \(b_t \sim 100\) Å, and geometrical factor \(\gamma = 100\) from Combescot and Tanguy (2001).
* For repulsive interactions, the chemical potential \(\epsilon\) (in units of \(\hbar\omega_t\)) increases relative to the standard GP result: for \(N_0 = 1000\), \(\epsilon_{\rm GP} = 7.31\) vs. \(\epsilon_{\rm qGP} = 7.47\) with \(x = 1.0\times 10^{-3}\); for \(N_0 = 10000\), \(\epsilon_{\rm GP} = 17.84\) vs. \(\epsilon_{\rm qGP} = 18.32\) with \(x = 1.3\times 10^{-4}\); for \(N_0 = 100000\), \(\epsilon_{\rm GP} = 44.59\) vs. \(\epsilon_{\rm qGP} = 46.69\) with \(x = 2.0\times 10^{-5}\); for \(N_0 = 1000000\), \(\epsilon_{\rm GP} = 111.94\) vs. \(\epsilon_{\rm qGP} = 131.16\) with \(x = 5.1\times 10^{-6}\).

## Context

This work extends the quon algebra formalism of Greenberg (1991) and the symmetric-state construction of Avancini, Marinelli, and Rodrigues (2002) to the specific problem of Bose-Einstein condensation of composite particles. It directly addresses experimental discrepancies in the collapse threshold reported by Roberts et al. (2001) and the beyond-mean-field chemical potential corrections calculated by Fabrocini and Polls (1999). The approach enables a phenomenological description of compositeness effects in trapped atomic BECs without requiring a full microscopic treatment of the constituent fermions, and the authors suggest it could be extended to other composite boson systems such as excitons in semiconductors, following the work of Combescot and Tanguy (2001).
