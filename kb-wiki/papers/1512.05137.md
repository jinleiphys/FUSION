# Coexistence of Kaon Condensation and Hyperons in Hadronic Matter and Its Relevance to Quark Matter

> Coexistence of kaon condensation and hyperons, which may be realized in neutron stars, is investigated on the basis of the relativistic mean-field theory combined with the effective chiral Lagrangian. It is shown that kaon-condensed phase in hyperon-mixed matter is plausible, but it leads to significant softening of the equation of state (EOS).We discuss indispensable effects which make the EOS stiffer so as to be consistent with recent neutron-star observations.

# Coexistence of Kaon Condensation and Hyperons in Hadronic Matter and Its Relevance to Quark Matter

## Key claim

The coexistence of kaon condensation and hyperon-mixed matter in neutron stars is plausible, but it leads to significant softening of the equation of state (EOS); the authors argue that additional stiffening effects (such as many-body forces beyond mean-field approximation) are indispensable to reconcile the softened EOS with the observed existence of neutron stars as massive as 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework for baryon-baryon interactions mediated by σ, σ*, ω, ρ, and φ mesons, including Λ, Σ⁻, and Ξ⁻ hyperons. They couple this with a nonlinear effective chiral Lagrangian for antikaon-baryon and antikaon-antikaon interactions, replacing the original contact interactions with meson-exchange terms; the classical K⁻ field is taken as a plane wave with chiral angle θ and chemical potential μ_K. The ground state is obtained under charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium conditions for weak processes, with the K⁻ optical potential depth U_K set to −120 MeV as a moderately attractive case.

## Key numbers

- Kaon condensation sets in at baryon number density ρ_B = 2.9 ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.153 fm⁻³ is the nuclear saturation density.
- Λ hyperons first appear in the kaon-condensed phase at ρ_B = 4.2 ρ_0.
- Ξ⁻ hyperons set in at ρ_B = 8.8 ρ_0.
- The K⁻ optical potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter is taken as U_K = −120 MeV.
- Most models including the coexistent (Y+K) phase yield maximum neutron-star masses less than 1.85 M_⊙, incompatible with observations of massive neutron stars near 2 M_⊙.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of kaon condensation and hyperon-mixed matter in neutron stars (referenced as "several approaches" from prior literature) by using a combined RMF and chiral Lagrangian framework. The authors note that previous models including the coexistent phase generally fail to reach the 2 M_⊙ threshold from recent neutron-star observations (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013). The paper identifies necessary stiffening effects, such as three-body baryon forces (NNN, YNN, YYN, YYY) beyond mean-field approximation and antisymmetrization effects via tensor coupling of vector mesons, that could enable future models to satisfy observational constraints.
