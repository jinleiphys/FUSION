# Translationally invariant treatment of pair correlations in nuclei: II. Tensor correlations

> We study the extension of our translationally invariant treatment of few-body nuclear systems to include tensor forces and correlations. It is shown that a direct application of our method is not as successful for realistic V6 interactions as our previous results for V4 potentials suggested. We investigate the cause in detail for the case of $^4$He, and show that a combination of our method with that of Jastrow-correlated wave functions seems to be a lot more powerful, thereby suggesting that for mildly to strongly repulsive forces such a hybrid procedure may be an appropriate description.

## Key claim

The authors show that their translationally invariant configuration-interaction (TICI2) method, which worked well for V4 potentials, fails for realistic V6 interactions with tensor forces; they demonstrate that a hybrid approach combining TICI2 with scalar Jastrow correlations significantly improves results, particularly for strongly repulsive potentials.

## Method

The authors extended their TICI2 method, which uses linearized state-dependent pair correlation operators expanded in a Gaussian basis, to include tensor operators (S_ij and S_ij P^tau) needed for V6 interactions. They then introduced a hybrid J-TICI2 wave function that multiplies the TICI2 ansatz by a scalar Jastrow factor g(r)=1+a*exp(-b*r^2) with two variational parameters, and solved the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem for the ground-state energy of 4He, 8Be, 12C, and 16O using several V4 and V6 potentials.

## Key numbers

- For 4He with the V6 part of AV14, TICI2 gives binding energy 14.77 MeV at HO parameter alpha=0.59 fm^-1; J-TICI2 improves this to 20.37 MeV at alpha=0.71 fm^-1
- For 4He with the V6 part of Reid-V6, TICI2 gives only 5.67 MeV binding at alpha=0.43 fm^-1; J-TICI2 dramatically improves to 22.70 MeV at alpha=0.72 fm^-1
- For 4He with the V4 Brink-Boeker B1 potential, TICI2 gives 37.86 MeV, J-TICI2 gives 38.28 MeV, and diffusion Monte Carlo gives 38.32 +/- 0.01 MeV
- For 4He with the V4 Malfliet-Tjon MT V potential, TICI2 gives 29.45 MeV, J-TICI2 gives 31.21 MeV, and DMC gives 31.32 +/- 0.02 MeV
- For 16O with the V6 part of Gogny, TICI2 gives 128.68 MeV binding at alpha=0.64 fm^-1; for AV14/V6, only 14.97 MeV at alpha=0.40 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TICI2 calculations on V4 potentials (Bishop et al., 1990; Guardiola et al., 1996) to include tensor forces, revealing that the method alone is inadequate for realistic V6 interactions. The hybrid J-TICI2 approach combines the strengths of Jastrow correlations (effective for short-range repulsion) with the TICI2 description of medium- and long-range correlations, and the results approach those from variational Monte Carlo and Green function Monte Carlo calculations (Carlson, 1987; Carlson, 1988) for 4He. The method enables future extension to full TICC2 (second-order coupled-cluster) calculations and application to nuclei up to A=16 with realistic forces.
