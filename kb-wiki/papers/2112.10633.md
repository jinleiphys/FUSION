# Predictions for neutron stars from holographic nuclear matter

> We discuss masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities of neutron stars constructed from the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model. Using the same model for crust and core of the star, we combine our theoretical results with the latest astrophysical data, thus deriving more stringent constraints than given by the data alone. For instance, our calculation predicts -- independent of the model parameters -- an upper limit for the maximal mass of the star of about 2.46 solar masses and a lower limit of the (dimensionless) tidal deformability of a 1.4-solar-mass star of about 277.

## Key claim
The paper claims that combining holographic nuclear matter calculations from the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with astrophysical data yields more stringent constraints on neutron star properties than the data alone, but these constraints depend on the model parameters and the assumption of a fixed surface tension in the crust. The authors note that the model cannot simultaneously fit QCD vacuum properties, nuclear saturation properties, and astrophysical constraints with a single parameter set.

## Method
The authors use the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with two flavors (N_f=2) in the confined phase, approximating the Dirac-Born-Infeld action by the Yang-Mills action and implementing baryonic matter via the homogeneous ansatz. They construct neutron stars by combining holographic nuclear matter with a lepton gas (electrons and muons) under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, including a mixed phase for the crust with a fixed surface tension of 1 MeV/fm^2. The resulting equation of state is input into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass, radius, and tidal deformability, which are then compared with astrophysical constraints from gravitational wave detections and the NICER mission.

## Key numbers
- Upper limit for maximal neutron star mass: about 2.46 solar masses (parameter-independent prediction)
- Lower limit for dimensionless tidal deformability of a 1.4-solar-mass star: about 277 (parameter-independent prediction)
- For the QCD window (lambda approximately 7-17, M_KK approximately 949-1000 MeV): maximal mass upper bound 2.40 solar masses, lower bound 2.11 solar masses
- For the QCD window: tidal deformability of 1.4-solar-mass star lower bound 286, upper bound 580
- For the QCD window: radius of 1.4-solar-mass star between 12.4 km and 14.1 km
- For the QCD window: radius of 2.1-solar-mass star between 11.4 km and 13.7 km
- For the QCD window: tidal deformability of 2.1-solar-mass star between 10.1 and 43.7
- Astrophysical constraints used: 70 < Lambda_1.4 < 580 (LIGO/Virgo 2018), 11.5 km < R_1.4 < 14.3 km (NICER 2019), 11.4 km < R_2.1 < 16.3 km (NICER 2021)

## Context
This work extends previous holographic approaches to neutron stars (Hoyos 2016, Jokela 2018, Bitaghsir Fadafan 2020, Jokela 2020) by using the same holographic model for both crust and core, with the crust-core transition determined dynamically rather than added by hand. The paper goes beyond the authors' earlier work (Kovensky 2021) by systematically combining holographic results with astrophysical data to extract parameter-independent predictions. The model enables future improvements including inclusion of strangeness, pion condensation, nonzero temperature, magnetic fields, and phase transitions to chirally restored phases, as well as computation of transport properties.
