# Inhomogeneous instabilities in high-density QCD

> QCD at large densities exhibits a moat regime in the scalar-pseudoscalar sector. The resolution of its dynamics is pivotal for the access to the onset of new phases including the potential critical endpoint of QCD. In this work we present the first selfconsistent analysis of this regime with the functional renormalisation group approach to QCD. We map out the moat regime, including a first analysis of potential inhomogeneous instabilities at baryon chemical potential $μ_B\gtrsim 600$ MeV on the chiral crossover line.

## Key claim
The authors present the first selfconsistent functional renormalisation group analysis of the QCD moat regime at large baryon chemical potential, finding that inhomogeneous instabilities emerge at intermediate momentum scales for \(\mu_B\gtrsim 570\) MeV on the chiral crossover line, though whether these persist to macroscopic scales and imply genuine inhomogeneous long-range order remains an open question.

## Method
The authors employ the functional renormalisation group (fRG) approach to QCD with emergent composite fields (dynamical hadronisation) for the light meson sector and part of the strange-quark sector. They resolve the full spatial momentum dependence of the pion and sigma-meson two-point functions and feed this back into the flow diagrams selfconsistently, while using finite element techniques for the full field-dependence of the effective potential and a flowing gluon mass gap parametrisation for the glue sector.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature at \(\mu_B = 0\): \(T_{pc} = 156.0 \pm 0.4\) MeV
- Curvature of the chiral crossover: \(\kappa = 0.0152(22)\)
- Onset of the moat regime: \(\mu_B/T \gtrsim 4\) on the chiral crossover line
- Onset of inhomogeneous instabilities on the crossover line: \(\mu_B \approx 570\) MeV (with regulator dependence \(\mu^\textrm{on}_B \approx 540 - 620\) MeV)
- Instability onset scale: \(k_{\rm on} \approx 100\) MeV at \(T = 105\) MeV and \(\mu_B = 570\) MeV, with instability occurring at spatial momentum \(\boldsymbol{p}_{M_\pi} \approx 200\) MeV
- Quantitative reliability bound of functional QCD: \(\mu_B/T \approx 4.5\) with 10% accuracy
- Sigma-mode becomes lighter than pion mode for \(\mu_B \gtrsim 550\) MeV on the crossover line

## Context
This work extends the functional QCD phase diagram studies of Fu (2019), Gao (2020), and Gunkel (2021), which mapped the chiral crossover for \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 4\) and excluded a critical endpoint for \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 5.5\). The authors resolve the moat regime first observed in scalar-pseudoscalar correlation functions by Fu (2019, 2024), implementing the full momentum feedback that was absent in those earlier analyses. The results enable future investigations of whether the critical endpoint is replaced by a Lifshitz point or inhomogeneous phase, and provide the foundation for quantitative studies of experimental signatures of the QCD moat and instability regime.
