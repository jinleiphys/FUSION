# T=0 effective interaction in 14N and 10B

> We have calculated the 1+ and 3+, T=0 states in 14N and 10B. In a neutron-proton RPA model these two nuclei are described by the same set of equations. We first show that a bare Minnesota interaction leads to too weak binding in bothnuclei. Furthermore it does not produce a 3+ ground state in 10B as it should. Including medium effects as an exchange of phonons between the neutron-proton pair cures the desagreement in 14N but still gives a 1+ ground state in 10B with the 3+ as an excited state. The same study with a Gogny effective interaction reproduces nicely the properties of both nuclei: same agreement in 14N as previously when medium effeccts were introduced but now the 3+ in 10B becomes the ground state. This success suggests that through its density dependent term the Gogny interaction takes account of the presence of a three-body force which, in a shell model calculation, has been shown to be essential to give a 3+ ground state in 10B.

# T=0 effective interaction in 14N and 10B

## Key claim

The Gogny effective interaction, through its density-dependent term, reproduces the correct 3+ ground state in 10B and the 1+ ground state in 14N, whereas a bare Minnesota interaction plus phonon-exchange medium effects fails to produce the 3+ ground state in 10B. This suggests the Gogny interaction implicitly includes an effective two-body contribution arising from three-body forces, which shell-model calculations have shown to be essential for the 10B ground-state spin.

## Method

The author applied a neutron-proton two-particle RPA model describing 14N and 10B as a 12C core plus or minus a neutron-proton pair, using the same set of RPA equations for both nuclei. Single-particle energies were obtained from a Saxon-Woods potential fitted to experimental neutron energies in the 12C field, with a surface term added to simulate neutron-phonon couplings. The calculations compared three interaction schemes: (1) bare Minnesota interaction, (2) Minnesota interaction plus phonon-exchange contributions from the 2+ and 3- collective states of 12C, and (3) the Gogny D1 and D1S effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- The 0+, T=1 ground state in 14N is calculated at -10.03 MeV (Gogny D1S) versus the experimental -10.14 MeV relative to the 12C ground state.
- The 0+, T=1 ground state in 10B is calculated at 29.24 MeV (Gogny D1S) versus the experimental 29.15 MeV relative to the 12C ground state.
- With the bare Minnesota interaction, the 1+ state in 14N lies at -11.9 MeV and the 3+ at -4.27 MeV; after adding phonon exchange, the 1+ moves to -12.4 MeV and the 3+ to -5.55 MeV, improving agreement with experiment.
- In 10B, the bare Minnesota interaction gives the 1+ at 28.7 MeV and the 3+ at 29.0 MeV; with phonon exchange the 1+ is at 28.1 MeV and the 3+ at 28.6 MeV, still leaving the 1+ as the ground state contrary to experiment.
- With the Gogny D1S interaction (t3=1600 MeV.fm3), the 3+ in 10B becomes the ground state at 27.7 MeV and the 1+ is the first excited state at 28.4 MeV, compared to experimental values of 27.4 MeV and 28.1 MeV respectively.
- The diagonal matrix element for two 1p3/2 nucleons coupled to 3+ is -5.19 MeV with the bare Minnesota interaction, -5.49 MeV with phonon exchange added, and -6.04 MeV in the fitted matrix elements of Warburton and Brown.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous two-neutron RPA study of N=8 nuclei with Pacheco (2005), which successfully described 0+, T=1 states in 14C, 10C, 12Be, 8Be, 11Li, and 7Li using the Gogny interaction. The present study applies the same formalism to the neutron-proton T=0 channel in 14N and 10B. The failure of the bare-plus-phonon-exchange approach in 10B is interpreted in light of shell-model calculations by Caurier et al. (2005) and Navratil and Ormand (2003), who showed that a three-body force (Tucson-Melbourne) is necessary to obtain the correct 3+ ground state in 10B. The results enable a deeper understanding of what the Gogny interaction implicitly contains, suggesting its density-dependent term incorporates both phonon-exchange effects and effective two-body contributions from three-body forces.
