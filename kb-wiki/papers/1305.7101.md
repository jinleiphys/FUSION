# Neutron Skins and Neutron Stars

> The neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei provides a fundamental link to the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, and hence to the properties of neutron stars. The Lead Radius Experiment ("PREX") at Jefferson Laboratory has recently provided the first model-independence evidence on the existence of a neutron-rich skin in 208Pb. In this contribution we examine how the increased accuracy in the determination of neutron skins expected from the commissioning of intense polarized electron beams may impact the physics of neutron stars.

## Key claim
The neutron-skin thickness of heavy nuclei, particularly 208Pb, provides a fundamental link to the equation of state of neutron-rich matter and thus to neutron star properties; the PREX experiment provided the first largely model-independent evidence for a neutron-rich skin in 208Pb, but with limited statistical accuracy that future experiments (PREX-II, CREX) aim to improve.

## Method
The paper reviews the connection between parity-violating electron scattering measurements of neutron densities in 208Pb and 48Ca and the equation of state of neutron-rich matter. It compares predictions from 48 nuclear density functionals for neutron-skin thickness against projected experimental constraints from PREX-II, CREX, and a possible MESA measurement, and examines correlations between the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb and various neutron-star properties using covariance analysis with the FSUGold density functional.

## Key numbers
- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb from PREX: r_skin^208 = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm
- PREX-II goal precision: ±0.06 fm (1% of the neutron radius)
- CREX projected precision for 48Ca: ±0.02 fm
- MESA facility projected precision: ±0.03 fm
- Correlation coefficient between r_skin^208 and the slope of the symmetry energy L: 0.979
- Neutron star mass reported by Demorest et al.: M = (1.97 ± 0.04) M_sun
- Neutron star mass reported by Antoniadis et al.: M = (2.01 ± 0.04) M_sun
- Lower limit on neutron star radius proposed by Suleimanov et al.: 14 km for masses below 2.3 M_sun

## Context
This work extends the connection between laboratory measurements of neutron skins and astrophysical neutron-star properties established by Horowitz (2001) and Lattimer (2006). It builds on the PREX results from Abrahamyan (2012) and Horowitz (2012) and discusses how upcoming experiments (PREX-II, CREX, and a possible MESA measurement) will constrain the isovector sector of nuclear density functionals. The paper also addresses tensions between different X-ray burst analyses: Ozel (2010) finds very small radii (8-10 km) that are difficult to reconcile with models lacking exotic cores, while Steiner, Lattimer, and Brown (2010) find larger radii, and Suleimanov (2010) proposes a lower limit of 14 km.
