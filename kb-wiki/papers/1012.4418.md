# Deflected Jets or Hot Spots? Conical Correlations of Hard Trigger Particles

> The double-peak structure observed in soft-hard dihadron correlations was recently studied intensively in order to learn more about the jet-induced medium excitation in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Experimental data shows that the double-peak structure obtained for soft trigger particles coalesces into one peak for harder trigger particles. We demonstrate that this effect occurs when averaging over many jet events in a transversally expanding background, while a hot spot scenario always leads to two distinct peaks. This suggests to study soft-hard correlations induced by heavy-flavor jets with those generated by light-flavor jets at RHIC and LHC in order to really disentangle medium effects from jets.

# Deflected Jets or Hot Spots? Conical Correlations of Hard Trigger Particles

## Key claim

The double-peak structure in soft-hard dihadron correlations coalesces into a single peak for harder trigger particles when averaging over many jet events in a transversally expanding background, whereas a hot spot scenario always produces two distinct peaks regardless of trigger momentum; this effect may explain experimental observations but cannot by itself distinguish between Mach-cone and hot-spot interpretations.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the SHASTA algorithm with an ideal gas equation of state for massless SU(3) gluons, solving the conservation equations with a source term representing energy and momentum deposited by a jet. Jets were modeled as constant-velocity partons (5 GeV or 11.4 GeV, corresponding to trigger pT of 3.5 GeV and 8 GeV) traversing an expanding medium with initial Glauber energy density profiles for Au+Au (T=200 MeV) and Cu+Cu (T=176 MeV) collisions, and the fluid was converted to particles via the Cooper-Frye prescription at isochronous freezeout. The two-particle correlation function was constructed by averaging over multiple jet trajectories at different azimuthal angles to mimic experimental event averaging, and compared with correlations from hot spot initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Trigger pT of 3.5 GeV (from a 5 GeV parton) produces a double-peak structure in the averaged two-particle correlation function for associated pT of 2 GeV
- Trigger pT of 8 GeV (from an 11.4 GeV parton) produces a coalesced single peak in the averaged two-particle correlation function for associated pT of 2 GeV
- Jets were modeled with velocity v=0.999, energy loss rate dE/dt = 1 GeV/fm, and momentum loss rate dM/dt = 1/v * dE/dt
- The Gaussian smearing width for the jet source was sigma = 0.3 fm
- Initial maximum temperatures: T=200 MeV for Au+Au, T=176 MeV for Cu+Cu
- Temperature cut for energy-momentum deposition: T_cut = 130 MeV
- Jet trajectories were sampled at r=5 fm with azimuthal angles from phi=90 to 165 degrees and phi=195 to 270 degrees in steps of Delta phi = 15 degrees
- Hot spot calculations used Delta e/e0 = 6

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of jet-medium interactions by Casalderrey-Solana (2006), Chaudhuri (2005), Renk (2006), Betz (2008), and others, which showed that conical structures in soft-hard correlations are sensitive to assumptions about jet-medium interaction and that diffusion wakes can overwhelm Mach-cone signals. The paper directly addresses the experimental puzzle from PHENIX and STAR collaborations (2008, 2010) where the away-side double-peak structure for low trigger pT (3-4 GeV) coalesces into one peak for high trigger pT (6-10 GeV). It contrasts the jet-averaging mechanism with the hot-spot explanation proposed by Takahashi (2009) and the triangular flow interpretation suggested by Alver (2010). The results enable future experimental tests using heavy-flavor tagged jets versus light-flavor jets at RHIC and LHC to disentangle medium effects from jet-induced signals.
