# Neutron Star Equation of State and the Possibility of Complex Self-Energy in Landau Theory of Fermi Liquid in Presence of Strong Quantizing Magnetic Field

> Using a relativistic version of Landau theory of Fermi liquid with $σ-ω$ and $ρ$ mesons exchange, we have obtained an equation of state for dense neutron star matter in presence of strong quantizing magnetic field. It is found that in this scenario the self energies of both neutron and proton components of dense neutron star matter become complex under certain physical conditions. To be more specific, it is observed that in the exchange diagrams of $σ$, $ω$ and neutral $ρ$ transfer processes and in the direct interaction diagram with $ρ_\pm$ transfer reactions, the nucleon self-energies become complex in nature.

## Key claim

The authors find that in dense neutron star matter under a strong quantizing magnetic field, the nucleon self-energies become complex in the exchange diagrams of sigma, omega, and neutral rho meson transfer processes, and in the direct interaction diagram with charged rho meson transfer reactions, which is a new result not previously reported.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic version of Landau theory of Fermi liquid for dense neutron star matter at zero temperature, incorporating interactions via sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange in the presence of a strong quantizing magnetic field. They compute the Landau quasi-particle interaction functions from two-particle forward scattering matrices for both direct and exchange diagrams, using modified Dirac spinors for charged particles in a magnetic field and including the effect of the magnetic field on charged meson propagators. The self-energies for neutron and proton components are then obtained from these interaction functions by integrating over the phase space of the quasi-particles.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic fields of magnetars are observed to be greater than or equal to 10^14 to 10^15 G, with theoretical estimates from the virial theorem suggesting core fields up to approximately 10^18 G.
- The quantum critical magnetic field for a charged fermion of mass m_i and charge q_i is given by B_m^(c)(i) = m_i^2 / q_i.
- First-order phase transition to quark matter at the core is completely forbidden if the magnetic field strength exceeds 10^15 G, while second-order phase transition is allowed for field strengths less than 10^20 G.
- The electron gas behaves like a neutral superfluid if the magnetic field strength exceeds 10^16 G.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic formalism of Landau theory of Fermi liquid for dense neutron star matter with sigma-omega meson exchange (Mandal et al., 2005) by incorporating rho meson exchange interactions, and builds upon the foundational work by Baym and Chin (1976) on relativistic Fermi liquid theory. The formalism is applicable to studying the magnetic properties, transport properties, and non-equilibrium properties of dense stellar fermionic plasmas, and can also be extended to electron gas or exotic quark matter in strong magnetic fields.
