# Electrodynamics of dual superconducting chiral medium

> We study the electrodynamics of a chiral medium with electric and magnetic charges using the effective Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory extended to include the magnetic current. The exchange of helicity between the chiral medium and the magnetic field, known as the inverse cascade, is controlled by the chiral anomaly equation. In the presence of the magnetic current, the magnetic helicity is dissipated, so that the inverse cascade stops when the magnetic helicity vanishes while the chiral conductivity reaches a non-vanishing stationary value satisfying $σ_χ^2< 4σ_eσ_m$, where $σ_e$, $σ_m$ and $σ_χ$ are the electric, magnetic and chiral conductivities respectively. We argue that this state is superconducting and exhibits the Meissner effect for both electric and magnetic fields. Moreover, this state is stable with respect to small magnetic helicity fluctuations; the magnetic helicity becomes unstable only when the inequality mentioned above is violated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a chiral medium with both electric and magnetic charges, the inverse cascade of magnetic helicity terminates at a finite chiral conductivity satisfying σχ² < 4σeσm, rather than at zero chiral conductivity as in the monopole-free case; this stationary state is superconducting and exhibits the Meissner effect for both electric and magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors extend the Maxwell-Chern-Simons (MCS) theory by adding a magnetic current density jm = σmB with constant magnetic conductivity σm, alongside the usual electric current je = σeE and the anomalous current jχ = σχB. They analyze the coupled system of Maxwell equations and the chiral anomaly equation, expanding fields into Chandrasekhar-Kendall (CK) eigenstates of the curl operator. To study time evolution, they employ the Fastest Growing State (FGS) model, which assumes late-time dynamics are dominated by the mode with the largest exponential growth rate.

## Key numbers

* The stationary chiral conductivity σ∞ satisfies σ∞² ≤ 4σeσm, where σe and σm are the electric and magnetic conductivities respectively.
* The London penetration length in the superconducting phase is ℓ = 1/√(σeσm − ¼σχ²).
* The fastest growing mode has momentum k⋆ = σχλ/2, independent of σe and σm.
* The magnetic helicity decays at late times as Hem ∼ e^{-2μt} with μ = (4σeσm − σ∞²)/(σe+σm+√((σe−σm)²+σ∞²)).
* The magnetic helicity is stable (monotonically decreasing) when the initial chiral conductivity satisfies σχ²(0) ≤ 4σeσm; it is unstable when σχ² > 4σeσm.

## Context

This work extends the Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory of chiral media (Wilczek 1987, Carroll 1989, Kharzeev 2009) by incorporating magnetic monopole currents, motivated by arguments that magnetic monopoles play an important role in quark-gluon plasma dynamics (Liao 2006, Lopez-Ruiz 2016) and cosmological models (Preskill 1984). Previous studies of the monopole-free MCS theory (Boyarsky 2011, Hirono 2015, Tuchin 2017) found that all helicity transfers from the medium to the magnetic field, with chiral conductivity eventually vanishing. The present paper shows that magnetic currents dissipate magnetic helicity, causing the inverse cascade to terminate at finite chiral conductivity and producing a superconducting state. This enables future studies of chiral magnetic dynamics in systems where magnetic monopoles are present, such as the quark-gluon plasma or early universe.
