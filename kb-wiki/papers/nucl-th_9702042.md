# Equilibrium shapes and high-spin properties of the neutron-rich A~100 nuclei

> Shapes and high-spin properties of nuclei from the neutron-rich (N>56) zirconium region are calculated using the Nilsson-Strutinsky method with the cranked Woods-Saxon average potential and monopole pairing residual interaction. The shape coexistence effects and the competition between rotationally-aligned 1h11/2 neutron and 1g9/2 proton bands is discussed. Predictions are made for the low-lying superdeformed bands in this mass region, characterized by the intruder states originating from the N=5 and 6 oscillator shells.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that shape coexistence and competition between rotationally-aligned \(1h_{11/2}\) neutron and \(1g_{9/2}\) proton bands dominate the high-spin structure of neutron-rich \(A\sim100\) nuclei, but these predictions depend sensitively on the choice of macroscopic energy formula (liquid-drop versus Yukawa-plus-exponential) and on the treatment of pairing correlations (BCS versus particle-number projection), with the FRLD/PNP combination yielding the most pronounced deformed minima.

## Method

The authors employed the macroscopic-microscopic Nilsson-Strutinsky method using a deformed Woods-Saxon average potential with monopole pairing, performing three-dimensional minimizations over \((\beta_2, \beta_4, \beta_6)\) for axial shapes and over \((\beta_2, \gamma, \beta_4)\) for triaxial shapes. Ground-state calculations were carried out with both liquid-drop and Yukawa-plus-exponential (FRLD) macroscopic energies, and with both BCS and particle-number projection (PNP) pairing treatments. High-spin properties were investigated using the total routhian surface (TRS) approach for even-even nuclei with \(Z=38-44\) and \(N=58-66\) at rotational frequencies \(\hbar\omega = 0.075\) to 1.50 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state prolate deformations for Sr and Zr isotopes with \(60 \leq N \leq 72\) range from \(\beta_2 = 0.30\) to 0.40, with the largest values near \(Z=38,40\) and \(N=60,62,64\).
* The oblate-prolate energy difference \(\Delta E_{\rm op}\) for heavy Se isotopes is 0.4–1.4 MeV favoring oblate shapes with \(\beta_2 \approx -0.28\) and \(\beta_6 \approx 0.10\).
* For heavy Kr isotopes, oblate deformations \(\beta_2 \approx -0.31\) with \(\beta_6 \approx 0.10\) are predicted up to \(N\sim66\).
* In \(^{108}\)Ru, the superdeformed minimum with \(\beta_2 \approx 0.5\) lies only \(\approx 2\) MeV above the triaxial yrast line at \(\hbar\omega = 0.6\) MeV.
* Experimentally deduced \(\gamma\) values for \(^{108}\)Ru and \(^{110}\)Ru are \(23^\circ\) and \(24^\circ\) respectively, consistent with the calculated triaxial minima.
* The first band crossing in \(^{100}\)Zr and \(^{102}\)Zr is associated with the \(\nu h_{11/2}\) alignment, with the proton \(1g_{9/2}\) crossing frequency higher than the neutron \(1h_{11/2}\) crossing frequency.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field calculations of shape coexistence and deformation in the \(A\sim100\) region by Nazarewicz (1988), Moller et al. (1995), and Chasman (1991), using an improved Woods-Saxon parameter set adjusted to reproduce the rapid shape transition between \(^{96}\)Zr and \(^{100}\)Zr. The predictions for triaxial ground states in Mo and Ru isotopes and for low-lying superdeformed bands provide guidance for future experiments with new gamma-ray arrays such as GammaSphere and EuroBall, as well as radioactive beam facilities, to expand spectroscopic knowledge toward heavier neutron-rich systems.
