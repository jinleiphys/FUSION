# Contributions of $2π$-exchange, $1π$-exchange, and contact three-body forces in NNLO ChEFT to $_Λ^3$H

> Faddeev calculations of hypertriton ($_Λ^3$H) separation energy are performed, incorporating all next-to-next-to-leading-order $Λ$NN three-body forces (3BFs) in chiral effective field theory: $2π$-exchange, $1π$-exchange, and contact interactions. The $1π$-exchange and contact interactions are rewritten in a form suitable for evaluating partial-wave matrix elements. The $Λ$-deuteron folding potentials constructed from these 3BFs are evaluated to demonstrate their contributions to \h3t. The $1π$-exchange interaction provides an attractive effect in which the d-state component of the deuteron wave function plays an important role. The attractive contribution tends to cancel the repulsive ones from the $2π$-exchange and contact 3BFs. Faddeev calculations show that the net effect of the 3BFs to the \h3t separation energy is small in a range between $-5$ to $+20$ keV, depending on the NN interaction used. Although these results are based on speculative low-energy constants, they can serve as a reference for further investigations.

## Key claim

The net effect of all next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) chiral three-body forces on the hypertriton separation energy is small, ranging from -5 to +20 keV depending on the NN interaction used, but this result is based on speculative low-energy constants (LECs) and serves only as a reference for further investigations.

## Method

Faddeev calculations of the hypertriton separation energy were performed including all three types of NNLO LambdaNN three-body forces (3BFs) in chiral effective field theory: 2pi-exchange, 1pi-exchange, and contact interactions. The 1pi-exchange and contact interactions were rewritten in a form suitable for evaluating partial-wave matrix elements, and Lambda-deuteron folding potentials were constructed to demonstrate their contributions before performing the full Faddeev calculations.

## Key numbers

- The net effect of all NNLO 3BFs on the hypertriton separation energy is between -5 and +20 keV, depending on the NN interaction used.
- The 1pi-exchange 3BF low-energy constant D2' was set to 3.268 x 10^-3 fm^2 MeV^-1, using g_A = 1.29, f_0 = 92.4 MeV, and Delta = 300 MeV.
- The net Lambda-deuteron folding potential from all three 3BFs (with the estimated LECs) is attractive at about -0.35 MeV at the origin.
- The sum of the 1pi-exchange and contact 3BF contributions to the folding potential is attractive at around -300 keV when combined with the repulsive 2pi-exchange contribution.
- The present world average of the hypertriton separation energy is 148 +/- 40 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Kohno, Kamada, Miyagawa 2023) which addressed only the 2pi-exchange LambdaNN 3BF and found a repulsive contribution of about 20 keV. The present paper incorporates the remaining NNLO 3BFs derived by Petschauer et al. (2016, 2017) using a decuplet-dominant model for the LECs. The results enable future ab initio calculations in heavier Lambda-hypernuclei and provide a baseline for understanding how changes in each coupling constant affect the hypertriton binding.
