# Quark-Hadron Duality in the Rein-Sehgal Model

> The quark-hadron duality in CC and NC neutrino interactions is discussed under assumptions that single pion production is described accurately by the Rein-Sehgal model and that it allows reconstruction of the inclusive cross section in the resonance region. The duality is measured by means of integrals of structure functions in the Nachtmann variable for $Q^2<3$ GeV$^2$. The results depend on the precision with which contributions from single pion production channels in the overall cross sections are known. Several approaches to evaluate them are compared. The duality is predicted to be seen for proton target reactions and to be absent for neutron and isoscalar targets. Two-component duality between resonant and valence quark contributions to structure functions is also investigated.

## Key claim

Quark-hadron duality in neutrino-nucleon interactions is predicted to hold for proton targets but to be absent for neutron and isoscalar targets, under the assumptions that the Rein-Sehgal model accurately describes single pion production and that the inclusive cross section in the resonance region can be reconstructed from it using 1-pion functions.

## Method

The authors computed structure functions from the Rein-Sehgal model for charged-current and neutral-current neutrino interactions on proton, neutron, and isoscalar targets, then rescaled them using 1-pion functions (probabilities that the final hadronic state is single pion production at a given invariant mass W) obtained from LUND Monte Carlo simulations. They compared the integrated resonance-region structure functions (W from M+m_pi to 2 GeV) in the Nachtmann variable xi with deep inelastic scattering structure functions based on GRV94 parton distribution functions evaluated at Q^2_DIS = 10 GeV^2, defining ratios R_2 and R_3 to quantify the degree of duality.

## Key numbers

- For CC reactions on proton targets, the duality holds with accuracy ≤ 20% (R_2 and R_3 within 0.8-1.2 for Q^2_RES ≥ 0.5 GeV^2).
- For CC reactions on neutron and isoscalar targets, the average strength of resonance structure functions amounts to only about half of the DIS structure functions (R_2 ≈ 0.5).
- For NC interactions on all targets, DIS contributions are approximately two times as big as resonance ones (R_2 ≈ 0.5).
- The 1-pion function rescaling increases R_2 by a factor of ~1.55 for CC neutron reactions and ~1.39 for CC proton reactions.
- The ambiguity in quantitative duality analysis due to choice of Q^2_DIS is of order 7%, largest for Q^2_RES ~ 1.5 GeV^2.
- For Q^2_RES < 0.5 GeV^2, the ratios R_j vary quickly; for Q^2_RES ≥ 0.5 GeV^2 they become slowly changing.

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron duality analysis of Bloom and Gilman (1970) from electron-nucleon scattering to neutrino-nucleon interactions, using the Rein-Sehgal model that is standard in neutrino Monte Carlo generators. It builds on earlier neutrino duality studies by Matsui, Sato and Lee (2005) and Lalakulich, Paschos and Piranishvili (2004), but uses a more complete set of 18 resonances and addresses the need to account for multi-pion channels via 1-pion functions. The results enable improved estimates of neutrino-nucleon structure functions and cross sections in the few-GeV energy region where data are insufficient, and suggest that two-component duality between resonant/valence quark contributions may hold for proton targets but not for neutron targets.
