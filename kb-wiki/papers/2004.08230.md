# Estimating the values and variations of neutron star observables by dense nuclear matter properties

> Recent NICER observation data on PSR J0030+0451 has recently added a unique mass-radius constraint on the properties of the superdense nuclear matter existing in the interior of compact stars. Such a macroscopic data restrict further the microscopical models, elementary interactions, and their parameters, however, with reasonable margin because of the masquarade problem. Here our goal is to identify the origin and quantify the magnitude of the theoretical uncertainties of the nuclear matter models. A detailed study on the effect of different interaction terms and the nuclear parameter values in the Lagrangian of the extended $σ$-$ω$ model is presented here. The equation of state was inserted to the Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff equation and observable parameters of the neutron star were calculated. We identified, that the optimal Landau effective mass is the most relevant physical parameter modifying the macroscopic observable values. Moreover, the compressibility and symmetry energy terms just generate one-one order of magnitude smaller effect to this, respectively. We calculated the linear relations between the maximal mass of a compact star and these microscopic nuclear parameter values within the physical relevant parameters range. Based on the mass observational data we estimated the magnitude of the radii of PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J0740+6620 including theoretical uncertainties arising from the models' interaction terms and their parameter values choices.

## Key claim

The authors identify that the Landau effective mass is the most relevant microscopic nuclear parameter modifying macroscopic neutron star observables, with compressibility and symmetry energy each generating effects roughly one order of magnitude smaller. The linear relations between maximal compact star mass and these microscopic parameters are calculated within the physically relevant parameter range, but the results are subject to theoretical uncertainties arising from model interaction terms and parameter choices.

## Method

The authors employed the extended σ-ω model in mean field approximation with three variants of scalar interaction terms (U₃(σ), U₄(σ), and U₃₄(σ)) to describe nuclear matter. They fitted model parameters to symmetric nuclear matter saturation data, then incorporated β-equilibrium and charge neutrality for asymmetric matter to generate equations of state. These EoS were inserted into the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to calculate neutron star mass-radius relations, using a "core approximation" that stops integration at the crust boundary to avoid introducing crust EoS parameters.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy at saturation: -16.3 MeV
- Saturation density: 0.156 fm⁻³
- Nucleon effective mass: 0.6 m_N
- Nucleon Landau mass: 0.83 m_N
- Compressibility: 240 MeV (for the U₃₄(σ) model)
- Symmetry energy: 32.5 MeV
- Optimal effective mass for U₃₄(σ) model: 0.6567 m_N ≈ 616 MeV
- Compressibility for original σ-ω model: 563 MeV
- Compressibility for U₃(σ) with effective mass fit: 437 MeV
- Compressibility for U₃(σ) with Landau mass fit: 247 MeV
- Compressibility for U₄(σ) with effective mass fit: 482 MeV
- Compressibility for U₄(σ) with Landau mass fit: 334 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous symmetric nuclear matter analysis (Posfay 2017) by including β-equilibrium and asymmetry relevant for neutron stars. It builds on the σ-ω model framework from Norman and Schmitt and the Boguta-Bodmer approach with cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions. The study is motivated by recent NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (NICER Collaboration 2019) and aims to quantify theoretical uncertainties in connecting microscopic nuclear parameters to macroscopic observables, enabling more informed interpretation of future observations from eROSITA, ATHENA, and SKA, as well as gravitational wave data from LIGO.
