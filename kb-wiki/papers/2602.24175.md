# Theoretical Studies of alpha Clustering in Nuclei and Beyond

> This article comprises three sections. Section 2 starts with a review of ab initio no-core shell model calculations by Monte Carlo Shell Model. Alpha clustering arises for 8,10,12Be and 12C with Daejeon16 and JISP16 interactions, even in the ground state of 12C. Hoyle state is shown to be dominated by alpha clustering in triangular configurations. As the ground and Hoyle states show strong deformations, they are good cases to investigate rotational excitations. As an original work, the recently proposed fully quantum (mechanical) formulation for deformation and rotation is extended to cluster states. Dual rotational modes are proposed: compact-object and distant-object rotations. The former is found in many heavy nuclei, whereas the latter can be found for clustering states. While 8Be is an example for the latter, 12C is a rare example that both modes appear. Atomic molecules and hadrons can be viewed similarly. Possible relevance to fission is mentioned. Section 3 presents a general framework for an extended no-core shell model with cluster-nucleon configuration interaction, combining traditional shell-model-like configurations with explicit microscopic configurations representing cluster degrees of freedom. The section reviews the microscopic origins of cluster substructures in light nuclei, emphasizing how nucleonic degrees of freedom, nucleon-nucleon interactions, and continuum coupling naturally extend the traditional shell model into configuration-interaction frameworks that incorporate clustering and reaction dynamics. Section 4 presents that although the cluster structure is robust in Be-C nuclei, some jj-coupling shell model components are mixed in the ground state of 12C. Using the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model, we can clearly model this competition between the cluster and shell components. The spin-orbit interaction is key.

## Key claim
The paper claims that alpha clustering emerges from first-principles no-core shell model calculations without being assumed a priori, even in well-bound states like the ground state of 12C, contrary to the traditional Ikeda threshold picture that associates clustering with weak binding near alpha emission thresholds. The authors propose dual rotational modes (compact-object and distant-object rotations) as a new framework for understanding rotational excitations in cluster systems.

## Method
The authors performed full Configuration Interaction calculations using the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) with the Daejeon16 interaction for 12C and the JISP16 interaction for Be isotopes, within a model space of seven harmonic oscillator shells. They analyzed intrinsic density profiles from the MCSM wave functions and decomposed basis vectors into three groups based on quadrupole deformation parameters (beta2 and gamma) to separate nuclear-matter-type and cluster-type components. The recently proposed fully quantum mechanical formulation for deformation and rotation was extended to cluster states to analyze rotational modes.

## Key numbers
- The distance between two density peaks in 8Be is approximately 3.6 fm, determined by visual inspection of the density profile
- The ground state of 12C is composed of 94% nuclear-matter-type component (group i, beta2 less than 0.7) and 6% cluster-type component (group ii, beta2 greater than 0.7 with gamma between 6 and 60 degrees)
- The Hoyle state (0+2) is composed of 33% nuclear-matter-type component (group i) and 61% cluster-type component (group ii)
- The unperturbed nuclear-matter component of the ground state lies at -91.0 MeV and the cluster component at -80.4 MeV, with mixing producing a 0.8 MeV additional binding energy
- The ground state and 2+1 state of 12C form an oblate rotational band with deformation parameter beta2 approximately 0.6
- The Hoyle state has quadrupole deformation beta2 greater than or approximately equal to 1.0

## Context
This work extends the ab initio no-core shell model calculations by Wiringa (2000) and the Green's Function Monte Carlo group (2015) that first showed di-alpha clusters in 8Be, and provides first-principles confirmation of the Hoyle state's three-alpha structure previously studied by cluster models such as those by Uegaki (1977), Kamimura (1981), and Bijker (2002). The dual rotational mode concept builds on the recent formulation by Otsuka (2025) for rotational bands in ellipsoidal nuclei, and the hierarchy structure discussion connects to the research project led by Nakamura (2025). The work enables future studies of the crossover between clustering and nuclear matter, and suggests applications to atomic molecules and hadrons.
