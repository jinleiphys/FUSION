# Analysis Method for Jet-Like Three-Particle Azimuthal Correlations

> Jet-like three-particle azimuthal correlations can discriminate various physical scenarios that have been proposed to explain the observed strong modification to two-particle azimuthal correlations. The three-particle correlation analysis is notoriously difficult in heavy-ion collisions due to the large combinatoric backgrounds. We describe the general idea behind the jet-like three-particle azimuthal correlation analysis, with emphasis put on the subtraction of the combinatoric backgrounds. We discuss in detail the various systematic effects in such an analysis.

## Key claim

The paper describes a method for extracting jet-like three-particle azimuthal correlations from heavy-ion collision data by subtracting combinatorial backgrounds, but the method relies on an _ad hoc_ assumption (such as zero-yield-at-1 or zero-yield-at-minimum) to determine the unknown background level, and the precision required (of order \(10^{-4}\)) is unprecedented in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The raw three-particle azimuthal correlation is decomposed into three components: the genuine three-particle jet correlation, the hard-soft background (pairs of one jet-correlated particle and one background particle), and the soft-soft background (pairs of two background particles). The soft-soft background is constructed by mixing trigger particles with inclusive events from the same centrality class, with anisotropic flow modulation added by hand. The hard-soft background is obtained by folding the two-particle jet-correlation signal with the underlying background distribution. The background level is scaled by a factor \(a\) determined from the two-particle correlation using the zero-yield-at-1 (ZYA1) or zero-yield-at-minimum (ZYAM) assumption, and a second scaling factor \(b\) accounts for differences in multiplicity distributions between inclusive events and the underlying background of triggered events.

## Key numbers

* Signal-to-background ratio for two-particle correlations in central Au+Au collisions with associated particle \(p_T\) of 1-2 GeV/\(c\) is of the order of 1/30.
* Signal-to-background ratio for three-particle correlations in central Au+Au collisions with the same associated \(p_T\) range is of the order of \(10^{-3}\).
* The required statistical and systematic precision for three-particle correlation analysis is of the order of \(10^{-4}\).
* In central Au+Au collisions, the number of charged particles at mid-rapidity within \(|\eta|<1\) for \(1<p_T<2\) GeV/\(c\) is about 150.
* The di-jet charged hadron multiplicity within the same \(p_T\) range for a trigger particle \(p_T\) of 3-4 GeV/\(c\) is of the order of a few (\(\sim 5\)).

## Context

This work extends the two-particle azimuthal correlation analysis used at RHIC to study jet-medium interactions, where strong modifications such as broad away-side distributions and possible double-hump structures were observed by experiments like STAR and PHENIX. The method enables discrimination between proposed physical scenarios including jet deflection (Armesto, Hwa), large-angle gluon radiation (Vitev), Mach-cone shock-waves (Stoecker, Shuryak, Muller), and Cherenkov gluon radiation (Dremin, Majumder), which cannot be distinguished by two-particle correlations alone. The technique builds on prior work by Ulery and Wang (2005) and provides a framework for future experimental analyses to extract the speed of sound or cone angle from three-particle correlation data.
