# Evaluation of pion-nucleon sigma term in Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of QCD

> We calculate the variation of the chiral condensate in medium with respect to the quark chemical potential and evaluate the pion-nucleon sigma term via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem. The variation of chiral condensate in medium are obtained by solving the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation for quark propagator at finite chemical potential, with different models for the quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator. We obtain the value of the sigma term $σ_{πN}$ = 62(1)(2) MeV, where the first represents the systematic error due to our different model for the quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator and the second represents a statistical error in our linear fitting procedure.

# Evaluation of pion-nucleon sigma term in Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of QCD

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon sigma term is evaluated as σπN = 62(1)(2) MeV, where the first error (1 MeV) represents systematic uncertainty from different choices of the quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator models, and the second error (2 MeV) represents statistical uncertainty from the linear fitting procedure. The result favors a relatively large value consistent with recent experimental analyses from pion-nucleon scattering and pionic-atom experiments, rather than the smaller values around 30-40 MeV from lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors solve the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator at finite chemical potential, using two different effective interactions (the Gaussian "GS" model and the "QC" model) combined with two quark-gluon vertex ansätze (rainbow approximation and the Ball-Chiu vertex). The variation of the chiral condensate with respect to quark number density is computed, and the pion-nucleon sigma term is extracted via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem using the linear relation between the condensate change and the quark number density, with the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation providing the connection to the current quark mass.

## Key numbers

- σπN = 62(1)(2) MeV, where the systematic error (1 MeV) comes from four different model combinations (DSE1-DSE4) and the statistical error (2 MeV) from the linear fitting procedure
- For DSE1 (rainbow + GS model): slope k = 1.95, σπN = 61 MeV, with ω = 500 MeV, D = 1.00, vacuum chiral condensate −⟨qq⟩₀^{1/3} = 252 MeV, current quark mass mq = 5.2 MeV
- For DSE2 (rainbow + QC model): slope k = 2.04, σπN = 63 MeV, with ω = 678 MeV, D = 1.10, vacuum chiral condensate −⟨qq⟩₀^{1/3} = 253 MeV, mq = 5.2 MeV
- For DSE3 (BC vertex + GS model): slope k = 2.22, σπN = 63 MeV, with ω = 500 MeV, D = 0.50, vacuum chiral condensate −⟨qq⟩₀^{1/3} = 258 MeV, mq = 4.7 MeV
- For DSE4 (BC vertex + QC model): slope k = 2.21, σπN = 62 MeV, with ω = 678 MeV, D = 0.55, vacuum chiral condensate −⟨qq⟩₀^{1/3} = 256 MeV, mq = 4.7 MeV
- For DSE5 (rainbow + GS model, beyond chiral limit): slope k = 1.94, σπN = 61 MeV, with mq = 5.2 MeV
- The pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV and pion mass mπ = 138 MeV are used as experimental inputs via the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to evaluate the pion-nucleon sigma term through the medium dependence of the chiral condensate, rather than directly computing the nucleon mass derivative. It addresses the tension between recent experimental analyses (Hoferichter 2015, Ruiz 2018) giving σπN around 60 MeV with small error bars, and lattice QCD results (Ohki 2008, Bail 2016, Yang 2016, Yamanaka 2018) yielding 30-40 MeV, as well as earlier DSE work (Flambaum 2005) and other models giving values up to 80-95 MeV (Hite 2005, Yamanaka 2014). The result enables improved calculations of the strange quark sigma term σs, which is important for dark matter searches, and can be refined with more sophisticated quark-gluon vertex constructions.
