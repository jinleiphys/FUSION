# Semi-inclusive electron scattering off the deuteron and the neutron structure functions

> The detection of slow nucleons in semi-inclusive electron scattering processes off the deuteron, 2H(e, e'N)X, is a very promising tool for the extraction of model-independent information on the neutron structure function F2n(x, Q**2). The key point is the occurrence of a peculiar scaling property of the semi-inclusive cross section, which holds in the deep-inelastic regime as well as in the nucleon-resonance production regions. Moreover, the possibility to get unique information on the neutron asymmetry A1n(x, Q**2) from polarised 2H_pol(e_pol, e'N)X processes is illustrated.

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive electron scattering off the deuteron with detection of slow recoil nucleons can extract the neutron structure function \(F_2^n\) and the neutron asymmetry \(A_1^n\) in a model-independent way, provided the spectator scaling property holds; this scaling is fulfilled within a few percent in both the deep-inelastic and nucleon-resonance production regions, but only when the detected nucleon is emitted in the backward hemisphere to avoid contamination from target fragmentation and final-state interactions.

## Method

The author calculates the semi-inclusive cross section for \(^2H(e, e'N)X\) using the spectator mechanism, where the recoil nucleon is detected after virtual photon absorption by the other nucleon. The response function \(F^{(s.i.)}\) is evaluated at \(Q^2 = 1\) and \(10\ (GeV/c)^2\) for Bjorken \(x\) from 0.20 to 0.95 and nucleon detection angles \(\theta_2\) from \(10^\circ\) to \(170^\circ\), using the SLAC parameterization of nucleon structure functions with \(R_{L/T}^{N_1} \simeq 0.18\). For the polarised case, the semi-inclusive deuteron asymmetry \(A_1^{D(s.i.)}\) is expressed in terms of the nucleon asymmetry \(A_1^{N_1}\) and a depolarisation factor \(D(\vec{p}_2)\) derived from the deuteron S- and D-wave functions.

## Key numbers

* At \(p_2 = 0.3\ GeV/c\), the spectator scaling is fulfilled within a few percent at both \(Q^2 = 1\ (GeV/c)^2\) and \(Q^2 = 10\ (GeV/c)^2\).
* For \(p_2 \lesssim 0.3\ GeV/c\), the dependence of the nucleon momentum distribution \(n^{(D)}(p_2)\) on the nuclear force model is quite negligible.
* The condition for pions from \(\Delta(1232)\) decay to be emitted only forward in the lab frame is \(q \gtrsim 2\ GeV/c\), corresponding to \(Q^2 \gtrsim 2\ (GeV/c)^2\); for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 1\ (GeV/c)^2\) backward-emitted nucleon final-state interactions drop quickly.
* The depolarisation factor \(D(\vec{p}_2)\) shows remarkable sensitivity to the NN interaction model for \(p_2 \gtrsim 0.2\ GeV/c\).
* The spectator scaling for the asymmetry ratio \(R_A^{D(s.i.)}\) holds for slow nucleons with momentum of the order of \(0.1 \div 0.2\ GeV/c\) detected in the backward hemisphere.

## Context

This work extends the spectator scaling approach proposed by Simula (1996, 1998) for unpolarised scattering to the polarised case, and addresses the extraction of the neutron asymmetry \(A_1^n\). It contrasts with traditional inclusive scattering off deuteron targets, which suffers from ambiguities due to the NN interaction and reaction mechanism (SLAC data analyses). The method enables model-independent extraction of \(F_2^n/F_2^p\) and \(A_1^n/A_1^p\) at both HERA-like (deep-inelastic) and JLAB-like (nucleon-resonance production) kinematics, and can also provide unique information on the deuteron D- to S-wave ratio from polarised \(^2\vec{H}(\vec{e}, e'n)X\) reactions.
