# Higher spin hadrons as relativistic fields

> I discuss the problem of consistent interactions of higher-spin fields and its relevance to resonance physics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that consistent interactions of higher-spin hadron fields (spin ≥ 1) must preserve the number of physical spin degrees of freedom (DOF) of the free theory, which requires the interactions to be invariant under gauge transformations similar to those of the free massless theory; this condition has been largely ignored in formulating couplings of nucleon resonance (N*) fields.

## Method

The author reviews the DOF counting problem for higher-spin fields, showing that free massless theories use gauge symmetries to reduce unphysical components to the physical 2 spin DOF, while mass terms partially break the symmetry to yield 2s+1 DOF. The consistency condition is formulated as requiring that interactions preserve this DOF counting by being gauge-invariant, which at the Feynman-rule level translates into a transversality condition on the vertices (p·Γ = 0). The decoupling of lower-spin sectors from the propagator is demonstrated explicitly for spin-1 and spin-3/2 cases using spin-projection operators.

## Key numbers

- A massless spin-1 particle is described by a vector field with 4 components, reduced to 2 independent components by gauge symmetry; the mass term raises the spin DOF to 3.
- A massless spin-3/2 particle is described by a 16-component Rarita-Schwinger field ψ_μ, reduced to 2 independent components by gauge symmetry; the mass term raises the DOF to 4.
- The spin-3/2 propagator contains a spin-1/2 sector with a factor of 2/(3m²) in the (1/2)22 term and 1/(√3 m) in the (1/2)12 and (1/2)21 terms.
- The conventional γNΔ calculation for Compton scattering on the nucleon in the Delta(1232) resonance region used two off-shell couplings z₁ and z₂; the consistent calculation with chiral one-loop diagrams had two fewer free parameters, using only the well-established m_π, f_π, and g_A.

## Context

This work extends the foundational studies of higher-spin field consistency by Dirac (1936), Fierz and Pauli (1939), Johnson and Sudarshan (1961), and Velo and Zwanziger (1969), who showed that minimally coupling higher-spin fields to electromagnetism leads to pathologies such as negative norm states and superluminal propagation. The paper contrasts the conventional N* couplings (which lack gauge symmetry and introduce off-shell parameters) with the consistent gauge-invariant approach, using the example of Delta(1232) in Compton scattering where the consistent calculation by Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003) with chiral loops outperformed the conventional calculation by Pascalutsa and Scholten (1995) with off-shell couplings. The work enables a cleaner separation of genuine N* contributions from shorter-range effects in hadronic models, relativistic potential models, K-matrix approaches, and chiral perturbation theory.
