# The analytical $ŽĆŽĆ$ scattering amplitude and the light scalars

> In this work we construct the $ŽĆŽĆ$ scattering amplitude $T^0_0$ with regular analytical properties in the $s$ complex plane, which describes simultaneously the data on the $ŽĆŽĆ$ scattering, $ŽĢ\toŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0╬│$ decay, and $ŽĆŽĆ\to K\bar K$ reaction. The chiral shielding of the $Žā(600)$ meson and its mixing with the $f_0(980)$ meson are also taken into account. The data agrees with the four-quark nature of the $Žā(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons. The amplitude in the range $-5 m_ŽĆ^2 < s < 0.64$ GeV$^2$ also agrees with results, obtained on the base of the chiral expansion, dispersion relations, and the Roy equations.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude $T^0_0$ with correct analytical properties in the complex $s$ plane that simultaneously describes data on $\pi\pi$ scattering, $\phi\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma$ decay, and $\pi\pi\to K\bar K$ reaction, while the abstract's claim of agreement with chiral expansion results is qualified by the condition that this agreement holds only in the range $-5 m_\pi^2 < s < 0.64$ GeV$^2$ and that the $\sigma(600)$ pole positions from the fits lie far from those obtained via Roy equations when $K\bar K$ and other high channels are included.

## Method

The authors construct the $\pi\pi$ scattering S-matrix as a product of resonance and background parts, where the background phase $\delta_B^{\pi\pi}$ is parametrized using two complex functions $P_{\pi 1}(s)$ and $P_{\pi 2}(s)$ with integral representations ensuring correct analytic structure including cuts, Adler zero, and absence of poles on the physical sheet. The resonance part includes mixing between the $\sigma(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons via a $2\times2$ inverse propagator matrix, with self-energies computed from two-particle intermediate states ($\pi\pi$, $K\bar K$, $\eta\eta$, $\eta'\eta$, $\eta'\eta'$). The amplitude is fitted simultaneously to KLOE data on $\phi\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma$ decay for $m>660$ MeV, 44 data points on the $\pi\pi$ scattering phase $\delta_0^0$ up to 1.2 GeV, new low-energy $\pi\pi$ scattering data, and the $\pi\pi$ amplitude from Caprini et al. (2006) in the range $-5m_\pi^2 < s < 0.64$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous description of $\phi\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma$ decay (Achasov and Kiselev, 2003-2009) by constructing a $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude with full analytical properties in the complex $s$ plane, enabling direct comparison with the Roy-equation-based results of Caprini et al. (2006) who obtained the $\sigma$ pole at $M_\sigma = 441^{+16}_{-8} - i272^{+9}_{-12.5}$ MeV. The analysis supports the four-quark nature of the $\sigma(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons as proposed by Jaffe (1977), and the new parametrization enables future studies of the role of $K\bar K$ and higher channels in determining the $\sigma$ pole position, which the authors find differs significantly from the Caprini et al. (2006) result when these channels are included.
