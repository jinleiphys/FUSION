# Coulomb final state interaction in heavy ion collisions for Levy sources

> Investigation of momentum space correlations of particles produced in high energy reactions requires taking final state interactions into account, a crucial point of any such analysis. Coulomb interaction between charged particles is the most important such effect. In small systems like those created in e+e- or p+p collisions, the so-called Gamow factor (valid for a point-like particle source) gives an acceptable description of the Coulomb interaction. However, in larger systems such as central or mid-central heavy ion collisions, more involved approaches are needed. In this paper we investigate the Coulomb final state interaction for Levy-type source functions that were recently shown to be of much interest for a refined description of the space-time picture of particle production in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides a parametrization of the Coulomb correction for Bose-Einstein correlations assuming Levy-type source functions, valid for Levy exponent alpha between 0.8 and 1.7 and source radius R between 3 and 12 fm, with relative deviations from the numerical lookup table below about 0.05%.

## Method

The authors numerically computed the source-averaged Coulomb wave function integral for spherically symmetric Levy source functions over a grid of Levy exponent alpha and radius R values, creating a lookup table. They then fitted an ad-hoc parametrization to this table, using a low-q rational function form and an intermediate-q exponential form joined by a Woods-Saxon cutoff, and validated the parametrization by reproducing earlier PHENIX results (Adare 2017).

## Key numbers

- The parametrization is valid for Levy exponent alpha in the range 0.8 to 1.7 and source radius R in the range 3 fm to 12 fm.
- The low-q parametrization is valid for relative momentum q below 0.2 GeV/c, where the Coulomb correction deviates from 1 by more than about 10^-4 to 10^-5.
- The Woods-Saxon cutoff joining the two parametrization regimes uses q0 = 0.08 GeV and Dq = 0.02 GeV.
- The maximum relative deviation of the parametrization from the numerical lookup table is around 0.05%.
- The fine-structure constant alpha_EM is taken as approximately 1/137.

## Context

This work extends the Bowler-Sinyukov method (Bowler 1991, Sinyukov 1998) for Coulomb final state interactions from simple Gaussian or exponential source shapes to the more general Levy-type source functions, which have recently been shown to be relevant for heavy-ion collisions (Csorgo 2003, Adare 2017). The parametrization enables experimental analyses to avoid iterative Coulomb correction procedures by providing a direct analytic form, and was validated by reproducing PHENIX results from Adare (2017).
