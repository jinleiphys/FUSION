# Generalized parton distributions of 3He and the neutron orbital structure

> The two leading twist, quark helicity conserving generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of 3He, accessible, for example, in coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), are calculated in impulse approximation (IA). Their sum, at low momentum transfer, is found to be largely dominated by the neutron contribution, so that 3He is very promising for the extraction of the neutron information. Anyway, such an extraction could be not trivial. A technique, able to take into account the nuclear effects included in the IA analysis in the extraction procedure, even at moderate values of the momentum transfer, is proposed. Coherent DVCS arises therefore as a crucial experiment to access, for the first time, the neutron GPDs and the orbital angular momentum of the partons in the neutron.

## Key claim

The sum of the two leading-twist quark helicity conserving generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of ³He, at low momentum transfer, is largely dominated by the neutron contribution, making ³He promising for extracting neutron GPDs; however, the extraction is not trivial and requires a specific procedure to account for nuclear effects even at moderate momentum transfer.

## Method

The two leading-twist GPDs of ³He, H and E, were calculated in impulse approximation (IA) using a convolution-like equation involving the nucleon GPDs and a spin-dependent one-body off-diagonal spectral function. The spectral function was evaluated using Av18 wave functions for the overlap integrals between the ³He wave function and the final state (a plane wave nucleon plus a two-nucleon system). A simple nucleonic model for the nucleon GPDs was used as input, and the extraction procedure for the neutron GPDs was tested by comparing the extracted neutron quantity with the free neutron input.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic form factor of ³He, G_M³(Δ²), calculated as the x-integral of Σ_q G̃_M³,q, agrees with experimental data for -Δ² ≪ 0.15 GeV².
- The quantity x₃ G̃_M³(x,Δ²,ξ) is shown at Δ² = -0.1 GeV² and ξ₃ = 0.1, with the neutron and proton contributions separated.
- The extraction procedure for the neutron GPDs works for x < 0.7, where data are expected from JLab.
- The ratio r_n(x,Δ²,ξ) = G̃_M^n,extr / G̃_M^n is evaluated at Δ² = -0.1 GeV² and ξ₃ = 0 (forward limit) and at Δ² = -0.1 GeV² and ξ₃ = 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IA calculations of the GPD H for ³He (Scopetta et al.) to the combination G̃_M = H + E, and builds on the special role of ³He for neutron spin structure studies (3He). It proposes a practical extraction technique using point-like magnetic form factors, enabling future coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments at JLab to access neutron GPDs and, ultimately, the orbital angular momentum of partons in the neutron, contributing to the resolution of the "Spin Crisis."
