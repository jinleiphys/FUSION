# Simultaneous analysis of matter radii, transition probabilities, and excitation energies of Mg isotopes by angular-momentum-projected configuration-mixing calculations

> We perform simultaneous analysis of (1) matter radii, (2) $B(E2; 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+ )$ transition probabilities, and (3) excitation energies, $E(2^+)$ and $E(4^+)$, for $^{24-40}$Mg by using the beyond mean-field (BMF) framework with angular-momentum-projected configuration mixing with respect to the axially symmetric $β_2$ deformation with infinitesimal cranking. The BMF calculations successfully reproduce all of the data for $r_{\rm m}$, $B(E2)$, and $E(2^+)$ and $E(4^+)$, indicating that it is quite useful for data analysis, particularly for low-lying states. We also discuss the absolute value of the deformation parameter $β_2$ deduced from measured values of $B(E2)$ and $r_{\rm m}$. This framework makes it possible to investigate the effects of $β_2$ deformation, the change in $β_2$ due to restoration of rotational symmetry, $β_2$ configuration mixing, and the inclusion of time-odd components by infinitesimal cranking. Under the assumption of axial deformation and parity conservation, we clarify which effect is important for each of the three measurements, and propose the kinds of BMF calculations that are practical for each of the three kinds of observables.

## Key claim

The beyond mean-field (BMF) framework with angular-momentum-projected configuration mixing for axially symmetric \(\beta_2\) deformation and infinitesimal cranking successfully reproduces all measured matter radii, \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities, and excitation energies for \(^{24-40}\)Mg, but the agreement depends on the assumption that triaxial deformation is negligible and that infinitesimal cranking is sufficient for low-spin states.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1S effective interaction to generate intrinsic states as functions of the axially symmetric quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta_2\). These states were then angular-momentum-projected and configuration-mixed via the generator coordinate method, with infinitesimal cranking (using \(\hbar\omega_{\rm rot}=10\) keV) applied to include time-odd components. Four levels of BMF calculations were compared: BMF(AMP) with only angular momentum projection, BMF(AMP+CR) adding cranking, BMF(AMP+CM\(\beta_2\)) adding configuration mixing, and the full BMF(AMP+CM\(\beta_2\)+CR) calculation.

## Key numbers

* Matter radii \(r_{\rm m}\) for \(^{24-38}\)Mg were deduced from measured total reaction cross sections \(\sigma_{\rm R}\) using the \(g\)-matrix folding model, with the finite-size effect of nucleons subtracted.
* The empirical rms radius \(r_{\rm m}^{\rm emp}=1.2A^{1/3}\sqrt{3/5}\) fm underestimates the experimental \(r_{\rm m}\) by about 10%, leading to overestimates of \(|\beta_2|\) by about 20% when used instead of measured radii.
* For \(^{32}\)Mg, the spherical barrier (energy difference between prolate and spherical states) is \(\Delta E \approx 2.3\) MeV, and the energy difference between prolate and oblate minima is \(\Delta E \approx 870\) keV.
* For \(^{40}\)Mg, the spherical barrier is \(\Delta E \approx 5.7\) MeV, and the energy difference between prolate and oblate minima is \(\Delta E \approx 1.8\) MeV.
* The effects of \(\beta_2\) configuration mixing on \(B(E2)\) values are about \(8\%-28\%\), and the effects of infinitesimal cranking are about \(7\%-14\%\); the combined effect reduces BMF(AMP) \(B(E2)\) values by about \(15\%-35\%\) except for \(^{26}\)Mg.

## Context

This work extends the beyond mean-field calculations of Rodriguez and Egido (2010) and Rodriguez, Egido, and Robledo (2015) by applying angular-momentum-projected configuration mixing with infinitesimal cranking to simultaneously analyze matter radii, transition probabilities, and excitation energies for a wider range of Mg isotopes (up to \(^{40}\)Mg). The results confirm that the island of inversion extends to \(N=28\) for Mg and demonstrate that the empirical radius formula \(1.2A^{1/3}\) should not be used for extracting deformation parameters in these light, unstable nuclei. The framework enables systematic investigation of which beyond-mean-field correlations are essential for each observable, guiding practical choices for future calculations.
