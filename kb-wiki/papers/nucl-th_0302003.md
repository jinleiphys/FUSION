# Analyses of $dN_{ch}/dη$ and $dN_{ch}/dy$ distributions of BRAHMS Collaboration by means of the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process

> Interesting data on $dN_{\rm ch}/dη$ in Au-Au collisions ($η=-\ln \tan (θ/2)$) with the centrality cuts have been reported by BRAHMS Collaboration. Using the total multiplicity $N_{\rm ch} = \int (dN_{\rm ch}/dη)dη$, we find that there are scaling phenomena among $(N_{\rm ch})^{-1}dN_{\rm ch}/dη= dn/dη$ with different centrality cuts at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 130 GeV and 200 GeV, respectively. To explain these scaling behaviors of $dn/dη$, we consider the stochastic approach named the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process with two sources. The following Fokker-Planck equation is adopted for the present analyses, $$ \frac{\partial P(x,t)}{\partial t} = γ[\frac{\partial}{\partial x}x + \frac 12\frac{σ^2}γ\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}] P(x, t) $$ where $x$ means the rapidity (y) or pseudo-rapidity ($η$). $t$, $γ$ and $σ^2$ are the evolution parameter, the frictional coefficient and the variance, respectively. Introducing a variable of $z_r = η/η_{\rm rms}$ ($η_{\rm rms}=\sqrt{< η^2 >}$) we explain the $dn/d z_r$ distributions in the present approach. Moreover, to explain the rapidity (y) distributions from $η$ distributions at 200 GeV, we have derived the formula as $$ \frac{dn}{dy}=J^{-1}\frac{dn}{d η}, $$ where $J^{-1}=\sqrt{M(1+\sinh^2 y)}/\sqrt{1+M\sinh^2 y}$ with $M = 1 + (m/p_{\rm t})^2$. Their data of pion and all hadrons are fairly well explained by the O-U process. To compare our approach with another one, a phenomenological formula by Eskola et al. is also used in calculations of $dn/dη$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the scaling behavior of charged-particle pseudorapidity density distributions ($dn/d\eta$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV and 200 GeV can be explained by the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (O-U) stochastic process with two sources, but the model systematically overestimates the total charged multiplicity ($N_{\rm ch}^{\rm (Th)}/N_{\rm ch} \approx 1.03$ to $1.07$) because the BRAHMS acceptance is restricted to $|\eta| \le 4.7$.

## Method

The authors fit the BRAHMS $dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta$ data for various centrality bins at both energies using a Fokker-Planck equation (the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process) with two initial sources at $\pm y_{\rm max} = \ln(\sqrt{s_{NN}}/m_N)$. They treat the evolution parameter $p = 1 - e^{-2\gamma t}$ and variance $V^2(t)$ as free parameters, minimizing $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom. For the rapidity distributions, they transform the fitted pseudorapidity distributions using the Jacobian $J^{-1} = \sqrt{M(1+\sinh^2 y)}/\sqrt{1+M\sinh^2 y}$ with $M = 1 + (m/p_t)^2$, and compare with a phenomenological formula by Eskola et al. (2002).

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130$ GeV, the fitted parameters for the 0-5% centrality bin: $p = 0.871 \pm 0.007$, $V^2(t) = 2.56 \pm 0.20$, $\eta_{\rm rms} = 2.21 \pm 0.12$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 0.758/13$ (Table 3).
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the fitted parameters for the 0-5% centrality bin: $p = 0.878 \pm 0.005$, $V^2(t) = 3.08 \pm 0.19$, $\eta_{\rm rms} = 2.32 \pm 0.08$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 5.1/33$ (Table 4).
- The ratio $R = N_{\rm ch}^{\rm (Th)}/N_{\rm ch}$ ranges from 1.03 to 1.05 at 130 GeV and 1.05 to 1.07 at 200 GeV across centrality bins (Tables 3 and 4).
- The frictional coefficient $\gamma \approx 0.1$ fm$^{-1}$ at both energies, assuming an interaction region size of 10 fm (Tables 5 and 6).
- For the $z_r = \eta/\eta_{\rm rms}$ scaling at 130 GeV: $p = 0.889 \pm 0.003$, $V_r^2(t) = 0.527 \pm 0.021$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 5.4/61$ (Figure 6).
- For the $z_r$ scaling at 200 GeV: $p = 0.865 \pm 0.002$, $V_r^2(t) = 0.559 \pm 0.015$, $\chi^2/{\rm n.d.f.} = 32.1/189$ (Figure 6).
- The power-law fit to the intercept data gives $A = 1.64$, $\alpha = 0.109$ at 130 GeV (correlation coefficient 0.970) and $A = 2.09$, $\alpha = 0.08$ at 200 GeV (correlation coefficient 0.740) (Figure 3).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous O-U process analyses of PHOBOS Collaboration data (Biyajima 2002) to BRAHMS data at two collision energies. The $z_r$ scaling observed is consistent with the PHOBOS results (Nouicer 2002). The approach is compared with a phenomenological formula by Eskola et al. (2002, EKRT) for rapidity distributions; the authors note that future measurements near $y \approx 0$ could distinguish between the two approaches by the presence or absence of a small peak from the inverse Jacobian factor. The extracted frictional coefficient $\gamma \approx 0.1$ fm$^{-1}$ agrees with values from proton spectra at SPS energies (Wolschin 1999) and from WA98 Collaboration analyses (Aggarwal 2000).
