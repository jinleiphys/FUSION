# Study of freeze-out dynamics of strange hadrons

> We study the chemical freeze-out dynamics of strange particles ($K,\, Λ,\, Σ$) from a homogeneous and isotropically expanding hadronic system of $π, K, ρ, N, Λ$ and $Σ$ with zero net baryon density. We use the momentum integrated Boltzmann equation and study their evolution over the bulk hadronic matter, a condition being similar to the one created at top RHIC and LHC energies. The cross-sections, which are input to the equations, are taken either from phenomenological models or parameterized by comparing against experimental data. From this microscopic calculation we find that these strange particles freeze-out near transition temperature $\approx T_c$ due to large relaxation time. The continuous cease of the inelastic processes due to gradual fall in the temperature and decrease in the number density, thus lead to early freeze out of strange hadrons $K, Λ$ and $Σ$ which happens sequentially near $T_c$. However, freeze-out of these strange species near Tc appears as a sudden and simultaneous process, which is mostly predicted by thermal model while explaining the yield of identified particles at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors find that strange hadrons (K, Λ, Σ) chemically freeze out near the transition temperature T_c ≈ 155 MeV, which appears as a simultaneous process, but the ratios of scattering rates to expansion rate suggest the freeze-out is actually sequential and continuous rather than sudden, with different species freezing at slightly different temperatures.

## Method

The authors solve momentum-integrated Boltzmann (rate) equations for K, Λ, and Σ in a homogeneous, isotropically expanding hadronic system of π, K, ρ, N, Λ, and Σ at zero net baryon density. They compare two extreme expansion scenarios: Bjorken flow (slowest expansion, rate Γ_e = 1/t) and Hubble-like flow (fastest expansion, rate Γ_e = 3/t). Cross-sections for inelastic processes are taken from phenomenological models or parameterized from experimental data, and the entropy density as a function of temperature is obtained from lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_0 = 355 MeV at time t_0 = 1 fm/c for both expansion models.
- Transition temperature T_c ≈ 155 MeV, used as the starting point for evolution of strange hadron densities.
- For Hubble-like expansion, yields change by about 2% for K and K̄ and about 6% for Λ and Σ after evolution from T_c, indicating freeze-out near T_c.
- Evolution is carried out until x = m_π/T = 3.85, corresponding to T ≈ 36 MeV.

## Context

This work extends microscopic freeze-out studies previously applied to early universe cosmology (Kolb and Turner) to the hadronic system created at top RHIC and LHC energies. It contrasts with thermal model assumptions of sudden, simultaneous freeze-out used in hydrodynamic calculations (Heinz 2006) and with the Cooper-Frye prescription. The results support sequential freeze-out scenarios advocated by lattice calculations (Bellwied 2018) and Hadron Resonance Gas model studies (Sandeep 2017), while providing a microscopic basis for the observation that strange hadrons freeze out near T_c. The approach enables future studies of multi-strange hadron freeze-out and kinetic freeze-out dynamics.
