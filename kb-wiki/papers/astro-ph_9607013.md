# What Can Be Learned by Measuring the Fluxes of the 7-Be and the pep Solar Neutrino Lines?

> Measurements of the interaction rates of the solar neutrino lines of $^7$Be and $pep$ can be used, independent of solar models, to test whether electron flavor is conserved, to determine survival probabilities of electron-type neutrinos at specific energies, and to test for the existence of sterile neutrinos. We present analytic descriptions of these tests. We also illustrate by numerical simulations, assuming matter-enhanced and vacuum neutrino oscillations, what measurements of solar neutrino lines can teach us about neutrino masses and mixing angles.

## Key claim

Measurements of the interaction rates of the 7Be and pep solar neutrino lines can be used, independent of solar models, to test whether electron flavor is conserved, to determine survival probabilities of electron-type neutrinos at specific energies, and to test for the existence of sterile neutrinos, but only if both neutrino absorption and neutrino-electron scattering experiments are performed for the same line.

## Method

The authors calculate the expected electron recoil spectrum for neutrino-electron scattering experiments using the standard solar model and three neutrino oscillation scenarios (small mixing angle MSW, large mixing angle MSW, and vacuum oscillations) with best-fit parameters from the four pioneering solar neutrino experiments. They derive analytic formulas for the survival probability P that depend only on measured absorption and scattering rates, independent of solar physics, and perform numerical simulations with assumed 10% experimental errors to determine 95% confidence limits on neutrino mixing parameters.

## Key numbers

- The 7Be neutrino line has two components: 89.7% at energy 0.862 MeV and 10.3% at 0.384 MeV, with a total standard solar model flux of 5 × 10^9 cm^-2 s^-1
- The pep neutrino line has energy 1.442 MeV with a standard solar model flux of 1.4 × 10^8 cm^-2 s^-1
- For the 7Be line, the ratio of total electron neutrino scattering cross section to neutral current cross section is 4.53; for the pep line this ratio is 4.93
- A measurement of either the 7Be 0.862 MeV line or the pep line to 10% accuracy would essentially eliminate the two competing oscillation scenarios not assumed to be correct
- For MSW oscillations, measuring N(7Be) and N(pep) to ±20% yields a characteristic uncertainty in Δm^2 of about 10%

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework for using solar neutrino lines to test neutrino properties, building on the MSW effect (Mikheyev, Smirnov, Wolfenstein) and vacuum oscillation solutions to the solar neutrino problem. The work provides analytic tools for interpreting future experiments such as BOREXINO (neutrino-electron scattering) and proposed Ga-As or 7Li detectors (neutrino absorption), enabling model-independent tests of electron flavor conservation that complement the approach of Bilenky and Giunti (1993) for 8B continuum neutrinos.
