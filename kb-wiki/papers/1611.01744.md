# Stellar electron capture rates on neutron-rich nuclei and their impact on core-collapse

> During the late stages of gravitational core-collapse of massive stars, extreme isospin asymmetries are reached within the core. Due to the lack of microscopic calculations of electron capture (EC) rates for all relevant nuclei, in general simple analytic parameterizations are employed. We study here several extensions of these parameterizations, allowing for a temperature, electron density and isospin dependence as well as for odd-even effects. The latter extra degrees of freedom considerably improve the agreement with large scale microscopic rate calculations. We find, in particular, that the isospin dependence leads to a significant reduction of the global EC rates during core collapse with respect to fiducial results, where rates optimized on calculations of stable $fp$-shell nuclei are used. Our results indicate that systematic microscopic calculations and experimental measurements in the $N\approx 50$ neutron rich region are desirable for realistic simulations of the core-collapse.

## Key claim

The authors show that incorporating isospin dependence and odd-even effects into analytic parameterizations of electron capture rates leads to a significant reduction of global EC rates during core collapse compared to fiducial results using rates optimized on stable fp-shell nuclei, with reduction factors up to one order of magnitude over certain density domains; however, these quantitative predictions contain uncertainties because the modified rates were added perturbatively without self-consistently updating the thermodynamic trajectory.

## Method

The authors extended the analytic parameterization of electron capture rates from Langanke (2003) by allowing the average Gamow-Teller transition energy ΔE to depend on temperature, electron density, isospin asymmetry I=(N-Z)/A, and odd-even character of nuclei, fitting these parameters to large-scale shell model calculations from the LMP database. They then computed NSE-averaged EC rates along two core-collapse trajectories (15 M⊙ and 25 M⊙ progenitors from Juodagalvis 2010) using an extended NSE model with experimental masses supplemented by the Duflo-Zuker mass model, applying the improved rate parameterizations perturbatively without updating the thermodynamic evolution.

## Key numbers

- For even-even ⁷⁸Ni at n_e=5.93×10⁻⁵ fm⁻³ and T=2.59 MeV: log₁₀(λ_EC)=3.11 (model 0), 1.69 (model 3), 0.32 (model 4)
- For even-odd ⁷⁹Cu at same conditions: log₁₀(λ_EC)=3.34 (model 0), 1.86 (model 3), 0.70 (model 4)
- For odd-even ⁷⁹Zn at same conditions: log₁₀(λ_EC)=3.58 (model 0), 2.49 (model 3), 1.72 (model 4)
- The fitted ΔE values for model (1) lie between 1 and 4 MeV for all electron densities considered
- The isospin range covered by present shell model calculations is -0.07 ≤ I ≤ 0.22
- The reduction factor reaches as high as one order of magnitude over certain density domains

## Context

This work extends the analytic parameterization approach of Langanke (2003) and responds to the sensitivity study of Sullivan (2016), which showed that scaling individual EC rates by factors of 2-10 can change the inner core mass at bounce by +16%/-4% and the neutrino luminosity peak by ±20%. The authors' previous work (Raduta 2016) showed that NSE-averaged EC rates depend strongly on experimentally unknown binding energies of neutron-rich nuclei, with average rates potentially increasing up to 30%. The present results indicate that systematic microscopic calculations and experimental measurements in the N≈50 neutron-rich region are needed for realistic core-collapse simulations, and that the improved parameterizations could enable more reliable extrapolation to regions where large-scale shell model calculations do not yet exist.
