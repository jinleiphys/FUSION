# Nuclear forces and nuclear excited states

> In this work, the role of the central, spin-force and tensor forces of two-nucleon interaction in building the first 2$^+$ and 4$^+$ states of 20 sd-shell nuclei is studied. Calculations are performed within the framework of the nuclear shell model. It is shown that the central force predominantly contributes to the excitation energy. While the spin-orbit and tensor forces contribute relatively less, their roles are noted crucial in a few cases, such as in $^{22}$O. Further, it is demonstrated that the Hamiltonian projection approach should be preferred for rightly assessing the contributions of each force to the excitation energy.

## Key claim

The central force of the two-nucleon interaction predominantly contributes to the excitation energy of the first 2+ and 4+ states in sd-shell nuclei, while the spin-orbit and tensor forces contribute less but are crucial in specific cases such as 22O; however, the Hamiltonian projection approach must be used rather than direct diagonalization of partial Hamiltonians to correctly assess each force's contribution, as the two methods yield discrepant results for the central-plus-tensor force.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations for 20 sd-shell nuclei using the USDB interaction, which was decomposed into central, spin-orbit, and tensor components via spin-tensor decomposition. The complete Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the ANTOINE code with the Lanczos method, and then the Hamiltonians constructed from only the central force and from the central-plus-spin-orbit force were projected onto the resulting eigenvectors to extract the contribution of each force component to the excitation energy, without performing additional diagonalizations.

## Key numbers

- The central-plus-tensor force predicts the 4+ state of 28Si at 2.38 MeV using the projection method, whereas direct diagonalization of the central-plus-tensor Hamiltonian gives a value about 1 MeV higher.
- More than half of the spin-orbit and tensor force two-body matrix elements of the USDB interaction have magnitudes greater than 0.1 MeV.
- The 5555:01, 5555:21, and 5555:41 spin-orbit matrix elements are identified as crucial for restoring the semi-magic character of 22O.

## Context

This work extends the study by Wang et al. (2015) on the effects of central and non-central forces on the 2+ state excitation energy of oxygen isotopes, but uses a different method: Wang et al. performed diagonalization of Hamiltonians containing one force or a sum of two forces at a time, whereas this work uses the Hamiltonian projection approach after full diagonalization. The results agree for oxygen isotopes but show significant discrepancies for the central-plus-tensor force in other nuclei, demonstrating that the projection method should be preferred. The work enables more reliable decomposition of nuclear forces in shell-model calculations and clarifies the role of individual force components in building excited states across the sd-shell.
