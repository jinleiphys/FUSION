# Dispersive analysis of the $\boldsymbol{J/ψ\to γπ^0 π^0}$ process

> We present a dispersive amplitude analysis of the low-energy $π^0π^0$ system in the radiative decay $J/ψ\toγπ^0π^0$, using the mass-independent BESIII $0^{++}$ and $2^{++}$ intensities, the total spectrum, and the measured $0^{++}-2^{++}$ $E1$ phase difference. The isoscalar $S$-wave is described by a coupled-channel $ππ/K\bar K$ Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation, which implements the strong final-state interactions associated with the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$. The $D$-wave is treated with a single-channel $ππ$ Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation, where we identify all kinematic constraints of helicity amplitudes before transforming them to the experimental $E1$, $M2$, and $E3$ multipoles. Smooth short-distance production of a pseudoscalar-meson pair is encoded in subtraction polynomials, while left-hand-cut effects are estimated and found to be numerically subleading. We identify the negative solution of the BESIII $0^{++}-2^{++}$ $E1$ phase ambiguity, after using the modulo-$π$ freedom of production amplitudes, as the phase solution compatible with unitarity constraints, showing that the measured phase information can be accommodated with the Omnès phase motion without requiring large additional phases. By normalizing the BESIII intensities with the extracted branching fraction, we fix the absolute scale of the fitted amplitudes, making them suitable as input for future dispersive studies of two-pion contributions to gravitational form factors.

## Key claim
The dispersive analysis resolves the BESIII phase ambiguity for the $0^{++}-2^{++}$ $E1$ phase difference, identifying the negative solution as the one compatible with unitarity constraints, and shows that the measured phase can be accommodated by the Omnès phase motion without requiring large additional phases.

## Method
The isoscalar $S$-wave was described using a coupled-channel $\pi\pi/K\bar K$ Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation that implements strong final-state interactions associated with the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances. The $D$-wave was treated with a single-channel $\pi\pi$ Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation, with all kinematic constraints of helicity amplitudes identified before transformation to the experimental $E1$, $M2$, and $E3$ multipoles. Smooth short-distance production was encoded in subtraction polynomials, while left-hand-cut effects were estimated and found to be numerically subleading.

## Key numbers
- Branching fraction used for normalization: $\mathcal{B}(J/\psi\to\gamma\pi^{0}\pi^{0})=1.15(0)_\text{stat}(5)_\text{syst}\times10^{-3}$, with a $5.4\%$ overall systematic normalization uncertainty from the BESIII mass-independent analysis.
- The $S$-wave dispersive representation was applied up to $1.05\GeV$ and the $D$-wave representation up to $1.45\GeV$.
- The BESIII data sample contained $1.311(11)\times10^{9}$ $J/\psi$ decays, with a bin width of $\Delta M=15\MeV$ for the $\pi^0\pi^0$ mass bins.

## Context
This work extends previous dispersive analyses of radiative $J/\psi$ decays (Xiao 2019, Sakai 2019) and coupled-channel analyses of the scalar and tensor resonance spectrum (Sarantsev 2021, Rodas 2022). It provides the first dispersive treatment that simultaneously incorporates all three measured $2^{++}$ multipoles, the total intensity spectrum, and the $0^{++}-2^{++}$ $E1$ phase difference from BESIII (2015). The resulting amplitudes are normalized to the branching fraction, making them suitable as input for future dispersive studies of two-pion contributions to gravitational form factors, particularly for the nucleon $D$-term and related quantities in $J/\psi$ photoproduction.
