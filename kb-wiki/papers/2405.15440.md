# Investigation of spectral properties of ^{11}Be in breakup reactions

> We investigate the breakup of the ^{11}Be halo nuclei on a light target ^{12}C within quantum-quasiclassical approach in a wide range of the beam energy (5-67 MeV/nucleon) including bound states and low-lying resonances in different partial and spin states of ^{11}Be. The obtained results are in good agreement with existing experimental data at 67 MeV/nucleon. We also demonstrate that the developed computational scheme can be used for investigation of nuclei spectral properties in low-energy breakup experiments on different targets.

# Investigation of spectral properties of \(^{11}\)Be in breakup reactions

## Key claim
The quantum-quasiclassical approach, which includes the curvature of the projectile trajectory and three low-lying resonances (\(5/2^{+}\), \(3/2^{-}\), \(3/2^{+}\)), provides a slightly better description of experimental breakup data for \(^{11}\)Be on a light \(^{12}\)C target at 67 MeV/nucleon than earlier time-dependent models with linear trajectories, but the improvement is modest and the trajectory-curvature effect is significantly smaller than for heavy targets.

## Method
The authors solved the time-dependent three-dimensional Schrödinger equation for the halo neutron coupled with classical Hamilton equations for the projectile-target relative motion (quantum-quasiclassical approach), using a two-dimensional discrete-variable representation (2D DVR) with \(N_{\theta}=N_{\varphi}=15\) angular grid points and a sixth-order finite-difference scheme on a quasiuniform radial grid up to 600 fm. The nuclear part of the projectile-target interaction was included via optical potentials: the core-target interaction used the Al-Khalili-Tostevin-Brooke (ATB) parametrization for \(^{10}\)Be+\(^{12}\)C, and the neutron-target interaction used the Becchetti-Greenlees (BG) parametrization for n+\(^{12}\)C. Breakup cross sections were computed as a function of the \(^{10}\)Be+n relative energy by projecting the final neutron wave packet onto continuum eigenstates of the n-\(^{10}\)Be Hamiltonian, summing over partial waves up to \(l_{\text{max}}=3\).

## Key numbers
- Ground state binding energy of \(^{11}\)Be: \(E_{1/2^{+}} = -0.503\) MeV; first excited state: \(E_{1/2^{-}} = -0.183\) MeV.
- Resonance positions used: \(E_{5/2^{+}} = 1.232\) MeV, \(E_{3/2^{-}} = 2.789\) MeV, \(E_{3/2^{+}} = 3.367\) MeV.
- At 5 MeV/nucleon beam energy, the difference between cross sections calculated with realistic versus linear trajectories is 6–7% in the region 0.3 MeV \(\leq E \leq\) 1.1 MeV and about 3% for \(E \geq 1.7\) MeV.
- At 20 MeV/nucleon, the CDCC result of Hebborn and Capel (2018) exceeds the present quantum-quasiclassical cross sections by approximately 30% over the whole energy range.
- The time interval for evolution was set to \(T_{\text{in}} = -10\) \(\hbar\)/MeV and \(T_{\text{out}} = 10\) \(\hbar\)/MeV with time step \(\Delta t = 0.02\) \(\hbar\)/MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous quantum-quasiclassical analysis of \(^{11}\)Be breakup on a heavy \(^{208}\)Pb target (Valiolda et al., 2022) to a light \(^{12}\)C target, where the Coulomb interaction is weaker and trajectory-curvature effects are much smaller. The results are compared with experimental data at 67 MeV/nucleon from Fukuda et al. (2004) and with theoretical calculations using CDCC, DEA, and eikonal approximations by Hebborn and Capel (2018, 2019). The study demonstrates that the computational scheme can be used to investigate spectral properties of halo nuclei by manipulating the bound-state spectrum (e.g., removing the \(1/2^{-}\) state or varying the ground-state binding energy) and observing the resulting changes in the breakup cross section, particularly the emergence of a peak near 0.3 MeV at low beam energies.
