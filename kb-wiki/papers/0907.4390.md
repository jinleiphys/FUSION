# High Momentum Probes of Nuclear Matter

> We discuss how the chemical composition of QCD jets is altered by final state interactions in surrounding nuclear matter. We describe this process through conversions of leading jet particles. We find that conversions lead to an enhancement of kaons at high transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, while their azimuthal asymmetry v_2 is suppressed.

# High Momentum Probes of Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

Conversions of leading jet partons in quark-gluon plasma alter the chemical composition of high-transverse-momentum hadrons, leading to an enhancement of kaons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and a suppression of their azimuthal asymmetry v₂, but only under the condition that the strange quark chemical potential in the medium is large enough relative to the initial jet sample.

## Method

The authors model the propagation of leading jet partons through a fireball simulation of quark-gluon plasma, incorporating elastic conversion processes described by rate equations. The conversion cross sections are scaled by a variable K factor (K=0 for no conversions, K=1 for elastic-dominated conversions, K=4 for stronger coupling) to explore different interaction scenarios. The chemical composition of the jet sample is evolved through conversion channels including q+g ↔ g+q and q+¯q ↔ g+g, with strangeness equilibration driven by coupling to the chemically equilibrated medium.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of strange to light quarks in the medium is w_med = s/(u+d) ≈ 0.5, while in the initial jet sample at RHIC energies w_jet < 0.1, creating a large chemical imbalance.
- The suppression of kaon v₂ relative to pions is expected to become noticeable starting at about 6 to 7 GeV/c transverse momentum.
- The color factor 9/4 for the relative quenching strength of gluons versus quarks is cited as the baseline prediction that conversions can wash out.
- At LHC energies, the initial jet sample is already close to chemical equilibrium due to dominance of the g+g fusion channel, making the strangeness enhancement mechanism disappear.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Liu and Fries (2008) that strangeness at RHIC could be a clean observable for measuring conversions of leading jet partons, and builds on prior studies of flavor-changing channels for gluons and quarks by several authors including Wang (2002), Mustafa et al. (2005), and Schäfer and Wang (2007). The paper also connects to earlier work by Fries et al. (2002) on photon production through parton conversions in quark-gluon plasma. The results enable experimental searches for conversion signatures using kaon nuclear modification factors and azimuthal asymmetry at RHIC, while noting that the same mechanism will not be visible at LHC due to different initial conditions.
