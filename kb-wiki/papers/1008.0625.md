# Triangular flow in event-by-event ideal hydrodynamics in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200A$ GeV

> The first calculation of triangular flow $v_3$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200A$ GeV from an event-by-event (3+1)-d transport+hydrodynamics hybrid approach is presented. As a response to the initial triangularity $ε_3$ of the collision zone, $v_3$ is computed in a similar way to the standard event-plane analysis for elliptic flow $v_2$. It is found that the triangular flow exhibits weak centrality dependence and is roughly equal to elliptic flow in most central collisions. We also explore the transverse momentum and rapidity dependence of $v_2$ and $v_3$ for charged particles as well as identified particles. We conclude that an event-by-event treatment of the ideal hydrodynamic evolution starting with realistic initial conditions generates the main features expected for triangular flow.

## Key claim
The first calculation of triangular flow \(v_3\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200A\) GeV from an event-by-event (3+1)-d transport+hydrodynamics hybrid approach shows that \(v_3\) exhibits weak centrality dependence and is roughly equal to elliptic flow in most central collisions, but the authors note that a hadron gas equation of state was used and they are aiming only at qualitative statements, not at quantitative comparisons.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model for initial binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, followed by ideal relativistic fluid dynamics for the hot and dense stage, with a gradual transition back to a hadronic cascade for final rescatterings and resonance decays. The initial state triangularity \(\epsilon_3\) was computed from the spatial distribution of particles, and the final state triangular flow \(v_3\) was extracted using a generalization of the standard event-plane method, where the event plane angle \(\Psi_3\) is defined from the momentum-space distribution of particles. The analysis was performed on an event-by-event basis, with the particle correlated to the event plane removed to eliminate auto-correlations.

## Key numbers
- Triangular flow \(v_3\) is roughly equal to elliptic flow \(v_2\) in most central collisions (impact parameter \(b<3.4\) fm) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200A\) GeV.
- The event-plane resolution for the triangular flow plane angle is around 0.8 radians, and for the \(v_2\) event plane angle it ranges from 0.7 to 0.95 radians depending on centrality.
- The starting time for the hydrodynamic evolution was chosen as \(t_{\rm start}=0.5\) fm to fit the final pion multiplicity at midrapidity.
- The matter around midrapidity (\(|y|<2\)) is considered for the hydrodynamic evolution.
- The triangular flow distribution is almost flat over the whole pseudorapidity range \(\Delta \eta=4\) covered by the calculation.

## Context
This work extends the study of collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions beyond the standard elliptic flow \(v_2\) and higher even harmonics, which had been the focus of prior work such as Ollitrault (1992) and Voloshin (1994). It directly responds to the recent extraction of a triangular flow signal from data by Alver (2010), which suggested that triangular flow could explain structures previously attributed to jet-medium interactions. The calculation enables experimentalists to apply the same event-plane method to measure \(v_3\) differentially, potentially providing information about the amount and size of initial state fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma.
