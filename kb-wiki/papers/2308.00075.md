# Dynamical induced quark spin polarization by magnetic field at the early stage of heavy-ion collisions

> We present a comprehensive analysis of the dynamic process of quark spin polarization induced by magnetic fields at the pre-thermal stage in heavy-ion collisions by using the recently developed theoretical tool of chiral kinetic theory. Our findings demonstrate that the spin polarization of quarks is highly sensitive to the interactions between quarks. These interactions can delay the decay of early spin polarization vector while accelerating the decay of later spin polarization vector. Specifically, our simulations show the detailed process of how magnetic fields polarize quarks within the fireball and reveal that quark interactions lead to an acceleration effect on the average spin. Notably, the fireball of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in its early stages exhibits an incomplete electromagnetic response effect, which differs from the response predicted by Lenz's law. This discrepancy arises from quantum corrections involving the interactions between quark spin and electromagnetic fields.

# Dynamical induced quark spin polarization by magnetic field at the early stage of heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Quark spin polarization induced by magnetic fields at the pre-thermal stage of heavy-ion collisions is highly sensitive to quark interactions, which delay the decay of the early spin polarization vector while accelerating the decay of the later spin polarization vector; the fireball exhibits an incomplete electromagnetic response effect that differs from Lenz's law, arising from quantum corrections involving quark spin–electromagnetic field interactions.

## Method
The authors used chiral kinetic theory coupled with Maxwell equations to simulate the dynamical evolution of quark spin polarization at the pre-thermal stage (0.1 to 0.6 fm/c) in heavy-ion collisions. They solved the chiral transport equation using a split method with finite differences and the Maxwell equations using the Yee-grid algorithm, separating fields into external (from fast-moving protons) and internal (from QGP medium) contributions. The collision term was treated with the relaxation time approximation (RTA), and the initial quark distribution was modeled as a Gaussian in space with a Fermi-Dirac-like momentum distribution characterized by the saturation scale Q_s = 2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The simulation starts at formation time t_in = 0.1 fm/c and ends at t_f = 0.6 fm/c, corresponding to the onset time for hydrodynamic evolution.
- The saturation scale is taken as Q_s = 2 GeV.
- The impact parameter is b = 8.87 fm, corresponding roughly to the 20–50% centrality class.
- The spatial grid uses a volume of 31 x 31 x 81 fm^3 with grid sizes (dx, dy, dz) = (0.8, 0.8, 0.035) fm and time step dt = 0.002 fm/c.
- The momentum volume is 26 x 26 x 26 fm^{-3} with grid size (dp_x, dp_y, dp_z) = (2, 2, 2) fm^{-1}.
- The average spin S_y for all quarks peaks around t = 0.2 fm and then decays monotonically.
- The spin polarization P_y monotonically decays with time for all quarks, consistent with the decay of the magnetic field.
- The induced (internal) magnetic field is very small compared to the external magnetic field at the early stage and can be neglected.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the global spin polarization of Lambda hyperons observed by STAR (2017, 2018, 2019) and the "spin sign puzzle" addressed by shear-induced polarization (Fu 2021, Fu 2020). It builds on chiral kinetic theory developed by Stephanov (2012), Son (2012), and others, and uses the split-method approach for Maxwell equations from McLerran and Skokov (2013). The study enables future quantitative modeling of the spin-splitting puzzle between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons by providing a dynamical description of quark spin evolution in the pre-thermal QGP fireball, rather than only at the freeze-out surface.
