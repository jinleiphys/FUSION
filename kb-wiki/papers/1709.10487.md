# QCD at finite isospin chemical potential

> We investigate the properties of QCD at finite isospin chemical potential at zero and non-zero temperatures. This theory is not affected by the sign problem and can be simulated using Monte-Carlo techniques. With increasing isospin chemical potential and temperatures below the deconfinement transition the system changes into a phase where charged pions condense, accompanied by an accumulation of low modes of the Dirac operator. The simulations are enabled by the introduction of a pionic source into the action, acting as an infrared regulator for the theory, and physical results are obtained by removing the regulator via an extrapolation. We present an update of our study concerning the associated phase diagram using 2+1 flavours of staggered fermions with physical quark masses and the comparison to Taylor expansion. We also present first results for our determination of the equation of state at finite isospin chemical potential and give an example for a cosmological application. The results can also be used to gain information about QCD at small baryon chemical potentials using reweighting with respect to the pionic source parameter and the chemical potential and we present first steps in this direction.

## Key claim

The authors present an update of their lattice QCD study of the phase diagram at finite isospin chemical potential using physical quark masses, showing that the crossover temperature at zero isospin chemical potential lies somewhat below the temperature associated with the melting of the pion condensate at high chemical potentials, with both temperatures increasing slightly toward the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors use 2+1 flavours of stout-improved staggered fermions with physical quark masses on lattices with temporal extents Nt=6, 8, and 10. They introduce a pionic source term with parameter lambda into the action as an infrared regulator, then extrapolate observables to lambda=0 using a singular value decomposition method for the massive Dirac operator. The phase boundary is determined from the onset of non-zero pion condensate, while the crossover temperature is defined by the point where the renormalised light quark condensate reaches a fixed reference value.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass used for normalisation: m_pi = 135 MeV; chiral limit of pion decay constant: f_pi = 86 MeV
* Continuum crossover temperature at mu_I=0: T_c(0) = 155(3)(3) MeV (from Borsanyi et al. 2010)
* On Nt=6 lattices, Taylor expansion to O(mu_I^3) agrees with direct simulation results up to mu_I approximately 50-60 MeV at T=124 MeV and T=155 MeV
* At T=176 MeV on Nt=6 lattices, Taylor expansion to O(mu_I^3) agrees with data up to at least mu_I=200 MeV
* Pion star masses from the TOV equation reach up to approximately three solar masses, with diameters slightly larger than neutron stars
* For the test setup with unimproved staggered fermions: lattice spacing a=0.299(2) fm, bare quark mass am_ud=0.025, pion mass about 260 MeV, pion condensation onset at a mu_I = a m_pi/2 approximately 0.2

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of QCD at finite isospin chemical potential by Kogut et al. (2002-2004), de Forcrand (2007), Detmold (2012), and Endrodi (2014), which used coarse lattices with unphysically large pion masses or unphysical flavour content. The authors previously presented first results with physical quark masses on Nt=6 lattices (Brandt et al. 2016). The results enable testing the range of applicability of Taylor expansion methods used for finite baryon chemical potential studies, and provide a first-principles equation of state for constructing hypothetical pion stars, similar to boson star studies by Kaup (1968) and later reviews by Jetzer (1991) and Liebling (2012). The reweighting technique described can potentially provide information about QCD at small baryon chemical potentials from the sign-problem-free isospin simulations.
