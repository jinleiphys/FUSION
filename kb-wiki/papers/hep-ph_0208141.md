# Dilepton production from the Color Glass Condensate

> We consider dilepton production in high energy proton-nucleus (and very forward nucleus-nucleus) collisions. Treating the target nucleus as a Color Glass Condensate and describing the projectile proton (nucleus) as a collection of quarks and gluons as in the parton model, we calculate the differential cross section for dilepton production in quark-nucleus scattering and show that it is very sensitive to the saturation scale characterizing the target nucleus.

## Key claim

Dilepton production in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions is very sensitive to the saturation scale characterizing the target nucleus, and the differential cross section changes its behavior from a \(1/l_\perp^4\) dependence to a \(1/l_\perp^2\) dependence at transverse momenta near the saturation scale, with the transition occurring at higher transverse momentum in the forward rapidity region.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for dilepton (virtual photon) production in quark-nucleus scattering by treating the target nucleus as a Color Glass Condensate described by a classical color field, while the projectile is described as a collection of quarks and gluons in the parton model. They apply the LSZ reduction formula to obtain the amplitude, replace the photon polarization vectors with the virtual photon propagator and leptonic tensor, and perform the color average over the distribution of hard color sources in the nucleus after squaring the amplitude. The resulting cross section is expressed in terms of a function \(C({\bf l}_\perp)\) that encodes all information about high gluon density effects and the saturation scale \(Q_s\).

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale at mid-rapidity is quoted as \(Q_s^2 \sim 1\text{--}2\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The differential cross section behaves as \(1/{\bf l}_\perp^4\) in the perturbative region \({\bf l}_\perp \gg Q_s\) and as \(1/{\bf l}_\perp^2\) at \({\bf l}_\perp \sim Q_s\).
- The diffractive cross section vanishes exactly when the quark mass \(m\) is neglected.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of real photon production in proton-nucleus collisions (Gelis and Jalilian-Marian, 2002) to the case of virtual photons (dileptons), showing that dileptons provide a more versatile probe of the saturation model than photons and avoid experimental difficulties with photon isolation criteria. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC by predicting that the change in slope of the cross section occurs at higher transverse momentum in the forward rapidity region, and that transverse momentum broadening of the jet+dilepton system will depend on rapidity. The formalism can also be applied to very forward nucleus-nucleus collisions to extract the shadowing function for quarks at large \(x\).
