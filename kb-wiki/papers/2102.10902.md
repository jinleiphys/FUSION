# Forces inside the nucleon on the light front from 3D Breit frame force distributions: Abel tomography case

> We derive simple relations which express 2D light front force distributions in terms of 3D Breit frame pressure and shear force distributions. Mathematically the relations correspond to invertible Abel transformation and they establish one-to-one mathematical equivalence of 3D Breit frame force distributions and 2D light front ones. Any knowledge (model calculation, experimental measurement, etc.) about pressure and shear force distributions in Breit frame can be unambiguously transformed into light front force distributions with the help of Abel transformation. It is important that the transformation ensures 2D stability conditions if the 3D stability conditions are satisfied. As an illustration of how the relations work, we calculated the light front force distributions for a large nucleus as a liquid drop, and for large $N_c$ nucleon as a chiral soliton.

## Key claim

The paper derives invertible Abel transformations that establish a one-to-one mathematical equivalence between 3D Breit frame pressure and shear force distributions and 2D light front force distributions, with the condition that the transformation automatically preserves stability conditions from 3D to 2D.

## Method

The authors start from the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the nucleon, specifically the Druck form factor D(t), which encodes the mechanical properties. They express both the 3D Breit frame force distributions (pressure p(r) and shear s(r)) and the 2D light front force distributions (pressure P(x_perp) and shear S(x_perp)) in terms of the same D(t). Using the mathematical structure of Abel transformations, they derive direct and inverse relations connecting the 3D and 2D distributions, and verify that the 3D von Laue stability condition implies the 2D stability condition. The formalism is illustrated by computing light front force distributions for a large nucleus modeled as a liquid drop and for the nucleon in the large N_c chiral soliton (Skyrme) model.

## Key numbers

- For a large nucleus as a liquid drop: 2D pressure at the center P(0) = gamma^(3D) ~ 1.0 MeV/fm^2 (A-independent)
- For a large nucleus: 2D line tension gamma^(2D) = (pi/4) R_0 gamma^(3D) ~ 0.88 A^(1/3) MeV/fm
- For the nucleon in the Skyrme model: 2D pressure at the center P(0) ~ 70.0 MeV/fm^2
- For the nucleon in the Skyrme model: 2D line tension gamma^(2D) ~ 34.0 MeV/fm
- For the nucleon in the Skyrme model: ratio of 2D mechanical mean square radius to charge mean square radius ~ 0.75
- The 2D mean square mechanical radius relates to the 3D one by a geometric factor of 2/3: <x_perp^2>_mech = (2/3) <r^2>_mech

## Context

This work extends the phase-space Wigner distribution approach of Lorce (2018) and the light-cone quantization method of Freese (2021) by providing explicit invertible Abel transformation relations between 3D Breit frame and 2D light front force distributions. It also builds on the foundational work of Polyakov (2002) on the static energy-momentum tensor and the Druck term. The results enable direct translation between any model calculation or experimental measurement of 3D Breit frame force distributions (such as those from chiral soliton models by Cebulla (2007) or chiral EFT by Alharazin (2020)) into 2D light front distributions with strict probabilistic interpretation, and vice versa.
