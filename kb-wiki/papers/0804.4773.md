# The small axial charge of the N(1535) resonance

> There is a natural cancellation between the contributions of the $qqq$ and $qqqq\bar q$ components to the axial charge of the N(1535) resonance. While the probability of the former is larger than that of the latter, its coefficient in the axial charge expression is exceptionally small. The magnitude of two of the corresponding coefficients of the $qqqq\bar q$ components are in contrast large and have the opposite sign. This result provides a phenomenological illustration of the recent unquenched lattice calculation result that the axial charge of the N(1535) resonance is very small, if not vanishing \cite{takah}. The result sets an upper limit on the magnitude of the probability of $qqqq\bar q$ components as well.

## Key claim

The axial charge of the N(1535) resonance can be made very small or vanishing through a natural cancellation between the contribution of the conventional three-quark (qqq) component and the contributions of five-quark (qqqq\(\bar q\)) sea-quark components, provided the total probability of the sea-quark components is less than 45%.

## Method

The authors computed the diagonal matrix elements of the axial charge operator for the N(1535) resonance in an extended constituent quark model that includes both the conventional qqq configuration and five distinct qqqq\(\bar q\) configurations with appropriate symmetry structures. The axial charge was expressed as a sum of numerical coefficients multiplied by the corresponding probabilities of each configuration, using the wave functions of the five-quark states constructed from S4 Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and SU(3) and SU(2) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient for the conventional qqq configuration in the axial charge expression is \(-1/9\).
- The coefficients for the five qqqq\(\bar q\) configurations are: 0, \(+5/6\), \(-1/9\), \(-4/15\), and \(+17/18\).
- With a qqq probability of 0.55 and qqqq\(\bar q\) configuration (1) probability of 0.45, and configuration (2) probability in the range (0.25-0.3) times that of configuration (1), the axial charge \(g_A(N(1535))\) lies in the range 0.03 to 0.06.
- With equal probabilities for configurations (2) and (3) in the range (0.12-0.15) times the probability of configuration (1), the axial charge falls in the range -0.02 to -0.05.
- The overall range of possible values for \(g_A(N(1535))\) in the extended model is approximately -0.05 to +0.06.
- The total probability of qqqq\(\bar q\) components is constrained to be less than 45%.

## Context

This work provides a phenomenological explanation for the unquenched lattice QCD result by Takahashi (2008) that the axial charge of the N(1535) resonance is very small or vanishing. It extends the constituent quark model approach of Glozman (1998) and the earlier work on five-quark configurations by Helminen (2002) and An (2008), showing that axial charges of baryon resonances can serve as sensitive probes for constraining the probabilities of sea-quark configurations. The result enables future studies to use axial charge measurements as a tool to determine the admixture of sea-quark components in baryon resonances.
