# Isospin Dynamics in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Some isospin dynamics results in heavy ion collisions from low to relativistic energies obtained through transport approaches, largely inspired by David M.Brink, are reviewed. At very low energies, just above the Coulomb barrier, the stimulating implications of the prompt dipole radiation in dissipative collisions of ions with large isospin asymmetries are discussed. We pass then to the very rich phenomenology of isospin effects on heavy ion reactions at intermediate energies (few AGeV range). We show that it can allow a ``direct'' study of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in the hadron medium. We work within a relativistic transport frame, beyond a cascade picture, consistently derived from effective Lagrangians, where isospin effects are accounted for in the mean field and collision terms. Rather sensitive observables are proposed from collective flows (``differential'' flows) and from pion/kaon production ($π^-/π^+$, $K^0/K^+$ yields). For the latter point relevant non-equilibrium effects are stressed. The possibility of the transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase, at high baryon and isospin density, is finally suggested. Some signatures could come from an expected ``neutron trapping'' effect. The importance of {\it violent} collision experiments with radioactive beams, from few AMeV to few AGeV, is stressed.

## Key claim

This review argues that isospin dynamics in heavy ion collisions, studied via transport approaches inspired by David Brink, can provide a "direct" probe of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in the hadron medium, but the sensitivity of observables like kaon yield ratios depends critically on non-equilibrium effects that are absent in chemical equilibrium models.

## Method

The author reviews results from semiclassical transport equations of Vlasov type (non-relativistic) for low-energy reactions and a relativistic transport frame (Relativistic Landau Vlasov method) for intermediate energies, both derived from effective Lagrangians with isospin effects in mean field and collision terms. For low energies, the prompt dipole gamma-ray emission is studied using Skyrme-type effective interactions; for relativistic energies, the isovector interaction is modeled with vector rho and scalar delta mesonic fields within a Hartree (Relativistic Mean Field) approximation, including elastic and inelastic processes for pion and kaon production.

## Key numbers

*   For the reaction $^{32}$S + $^{100}$Mo at 6 A MeV and 9 A MeV (b=2 fm), the dipole mode shows a net increase in collective response when passing from the lower to the higher incident energy.
*   For the $^{40}$Ca + $^{100}$Mo reaction, the percent increase of intensity in the linearized gamma-ray spectra at the compound nucleus GDR energy region (8-18 MeV) is 16%, with an initial dipole moment D(t=0) of 22.1 fm and a mass asymmetry Delta of 0.15.
*   For the $^{16}$O + $^{98}$Mo reaction, the percent increase is 36% (8-20 MeV), with D(t=0)=8.4 fm and Delta=0.29.
*   For the $^{32}$S + $^{100}$Mo reaction at 173.5 MeV excitation energy, the percent increase is 25% (8-21 MeV), with D(t=0)=18.2 fm and Delta=0.19.
*   For central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV, the kaon yield saturates at around 20 fm/c, while the pion yield freezes out at times of the order of 50 fm/c.
*   In the 0.8-1.8 AGeV range for Au+Au, variations in the $K^0/K^+$ ratio between the DDF and $NL\rho\delta$ isovector interaction models are of the order of 14-16%, while variations in the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio are about 8-10%.
*   For the $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U reaction at 1 AGeV (b=7 fm), a transient state is formed with baryon density around 3-4 $\rho_0$, temperature 50-60 MeV, energy density 500 MeV fm$^{-3}$, and proton fraction between 0.35 and 0.40.
*   Moving from symmetric nuclei to nuclei with Z/A ~ 0.3, the critical density for the mixed hadron-quark phase is reduced by roughly 10%.

## Context

This review extends the transport approach to isospin dynamics across a wide energy range, building on the mean-field ideas of David Brink. At low energies, it discusses the prompt dipole radiation as a probe of the symmetry term below saturation, following earlier work by Chomaz (1992) and Bortignon (1993). At relativistic energies, it proposes differential flows and kaon yield ratios as observables to distinguish between different covariant structures of the isovector interaction (e.g., models with only rho meson versus models with both rho and delta mesons), and suggests that experiments with radioactive beams could probe the transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase at high isospin density, extending the work of Mueller (1995).
