# Muon capture in hydrogen

> Theoretical difficulties in reconciling the measured rates for ordinary and radiative muon capture are discussed, based on heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. We also examine ambiguity in our analysis due to the formation of p$μ$p molecules in the liquid hydrogen target.

## Key claim

Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) can reproduce the measured ordinary muon capture (OMC) rate in liquid hydrogen, but cannot simultaneously reproduce the measured radiative muon capture (RMC) branching ratio, even when allowing an extra adjustable parameter for molecular spin-state mixing.

## Method

The authors performed systematic HBChPT calculations for both ordinary and radiative muon capture on protons, expanding the effective Lagrangian in powers of Q/Λ_chiral (where Q ~ m_μ = 105.7 MeV) up to NNLO, including tree diagrams, one-pion-loop diagrams, and nucleon recoil corrections. They then solved coupled kinetic equations describing the time evolution of muons in atomic singlet, ortho-molecular, and para-molecular states in liquid hydrogen, averaging over the finite beam burst duration for OMC to compare with the experimental data of Bardin et al. (1981) and Jonkmans et al. (1996).

## Key numbers

- Experimental OMC rate in liquid hydrogen: Λ_liq^exp = 460 ± 20 s⁻¹ (Bardin et al., 1981)
- Experimental RMC partial branching ratio (E_γ ≥ 60 MeV): R_γ^exp = (2.10 ± 0.22) × 10⁻⁸ (Jonkmans et al., 1996; Wright et al., 1998)
- HBChPT-calculated OMC rate in liquid hydrogen: Λ_liq ≃ 459 s⁻¹, in good agreement with experiment
- HBChPT-calculated atomic singlet OMC rate at NNLO: Λ_s = 687 s⁻¹ (Bernard et al., 2001) and 695 s⁻¹ (Ando et al., 2001)
- HBChPT-calculated atomic triplet OMC rate at NNLO: Λ_t = 13 s⁻¹ (Bernard et al., 2001) and 12 s⁻¹ (Ando et al., 2001)
- PCAC prediction for the induced pseudoscalar coupling: g_P^PCAC = 6.77 g_A(0)
- The g_P value deduced from RMC data is ~1.5 times larger than the PCAC prediction
- Muon decay rate: λ_0 = 0.455 × 10⁶ s⁻¹
- Atomic hyperfine triplet-to-singlet transition rate: λ_10 ≃ 1.7 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹
- Atomic-to-ortho-molecular transition rate: λ_ppμ ~ 2.5 × 10⁶ s⁻¹
- Ortho-to-para molecular transition rate: λ_op ~ (4-7) × 10⁴ s⁻¹

## Context

This work extends earlier HBChPT calculations by Bernard et al. (1994, 2001), Meissner et al. (1998), and Ando and Min (1998) to provide a coherent treatment of both OMC and RMC in liquid hydrogen, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between the g_P values extracted from the two processes. The analysis builds on the classic OMC measurement by Bardin et al. (1981) and the RMC measurement by Jonkmans et al. (1996) and Wright et al. (1998), and incorporates molecular formation effects originally studied by Weinberg (1960) and Bakalov et al. (1982). The results motivate a planned precise OMC measurement in hydrogen gas at PSI (Bailin et al., 1996) to avoid molecular complications, and a TRIUMF experiment (Armstrong et al., 1997) for a more precise measurement of the ortho-para molecular transition rate λ_op.
