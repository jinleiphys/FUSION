# Baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We provide a short introduction to the one-nucleon sector of chiral perturbation theory and address the issue of power counting and renormalization. We discuss the infrared regularization and the extended on-mass-shell scheme. Both allow for the inclusion of further degrees of freedom beyond pions and nucleons and the application to higher-loop calculations. As applications we consider the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass to order ${\cal O}(q^6)$ and the inclusion of vector and axial-vector mesons in the calculation of nucleon form factors. Finally, we address the complex-mass scheme for describing unstable particles in effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory can achieve a consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams through suitable renormalization schemes (infrared regularization or extended on-mass-shell scheme), contrary to earlier claims that the relativistic formulation lacked systematic power counting.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of infrared regularization (Becher and Leutwyler 1999) and the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme (Gegelia 1999, Fuchs 2003) to baryon chiral perturbation theory in the one-nucleon sector. These methods separate loop integrals into infrared-singular parts that satisfy power counting and analytic remainder terms that can be absorbed into counter-term contributions. The complex-mass scheme is introduced for describing unstable particles such as the rho meson and the Roper resonance.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit: m = 882.8 MeV, corresponding to Δm = 55.5 MeV (from EOMS scheme calculation at O(q^6))
- Fifth-order contribution to nucleon mass: k_5 M^5 ln(M/m_N) = -4.8 MeV at physical pion mass with μ = m_N
- Sixth-order contribution: k_6 M^5 = 3.7 MeV or -7.6 MeV depending on choice of third-order LEC d_16
- Non-perturbative sum of iterated diagrams: δm = -0.00233530 MeV, with leading perturbative term -0.00230219 MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.2694(28) from neutron beta decay
- For the rho meson complex-mass scheme: M_ρ^2 = M_χ^2 + 0.019 c_x - 0.0071 + 0.0014 + 0.0013 (GeV^2) and Γ ≈ Γ_χ + 0.21 Γ_χ^3 - 0.016 c_x Γ_χ - 0.0058 Γ_χ + 0.0011 (GeV)

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on baryon chiral perturbation theory by Gasser (1987) and addresses the power-counting problem that had hindered relativistic formulations. The infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) and the EOMS scheme of Gegelia (1999) and Fuchs (2003) enable consistent higher-loop calculations and the inclusion of additional degrees of freedom such as vector mesons, axial-vector mesons, and the Delta(1232) resonance. The complex-mass scheme (Djukanovic 2009) extends the framework to describe unstable particles like the rho meson and Roper resonance, enabling applications to resonance properties and chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results.
