# Threshold-cusp explanation for $X$ and $Z_{cs}$ in $B^+\to J/ψϕK^+$

> Several $X$ and $Z_{cs}$ exotic hadrons were claimed in the LHCb's amplitude analysis on $B^+\to J/ψϕK^+$. The data shows that all the peaks and also dips in the spectra are located at thresholds of seemingly relevant meson-meson channels. While the LHCb analysis fitted the peaks with Breit-Wigner resonances, threshold kinematical cusps might be the cause of such structures. We thus analyze the LHCb data considering the threshold cusps. Our model is simultaneously fitted to $J/ψϕ$, $J/ψK^+$, and $K^+ϕ$ invariant mass distributions. The threshold cusps fit well all the $X$, $Z_{cs}$, and dip structures. Our analysis indicates that spin-parity of the $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$ structures are $0^-$ and $1^-$, respectively. This is different from the LHCb's spin-parity assignments ($1^+$ and $0^+$). The number of fitting parameters can be significantly reduced by considering the relevant threshold cusps. Our analysis shows that $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*}$ scattering lengths are consistent with zero. This disfavors an explanation of $Z_{cs}(4000)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$ as $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*}$ molecules, which is consistent with lattice QCD via the SU(3) relation.

# Threshold-cusp explanation for $X$ and $Z_{cs}$ in $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$

## Key claim

All peaks and dips in the LHCb $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ data can be explained by ordinary $s$-wave threshold kinematical cusps from one-loop diagrams, without requiring Breit-Wigner resonances or virtual poles near thresholds; the spin-parity assignments for the $X(4274)$ and $X(4500)$ structures are $0^-$ and $1^-$ respectively, contradicting the LHCb's $1^+$ and $0^+$ assignments.

## Method

The authors constructed a reaction model for $B^+\to J/\psi\phi K^+$ using one-loop diagrams with $s$-wave meson-meson pairs at thresholds: $D_{sJ}^{(*)+}D_s^{(*)-}$ pairs (Fig. 1a), $D_s^{*+}D_s^{(*)-}$ and $\psi'\phi$ pairs (Fig. 1b), and $D_s^{(*)+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ pairs (Fig. 1c), plus $K^*_J$ resonance mechanisms in Breit-Wigner form (Fig. 1d). The model was simultaneously fitted to the LHCb invariant mass distributions of $J/\psi\phi$, $J/\psi K^+$, and $K^+\phi$ with 29 fitting parameters. To test the molecule interpretation, the $D_s^{(*)+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ interaction strengths were varied and scattering lengths extracted.

## Key numbers

- Fit quality: $\chi^2/{\rm ndf}=1.77$ for the simultaneous fit to $J/\psi\phi$, $J/\psi K^+$, and $K^+\phi$ distributions
- Number of fitting parameters: 29 in the authors' model versus 144 in the LHCb amplitude model
- $D_s^{+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ scattering length: $-0.12 < a {\rm (fm)} < 0.06$ (consistent with zero), corresponding to a virtual pole at 93 MeV below threshold or deeper
- $D_s^{*+}\bar{D}^{*0}$ scattering length: $-0.21 < a {\rm (fm)} < 0.03$ (consistent with zero), corresponding to a virtual pole at 103 MeV below threshold or deeper
- $X(4274)$ assigned $J^P=0^-$ (LHCb assigned $1^+$); $X(4500)$ assigned $J^P=1^-$ (LHCb assigned $0^+$)

## Context

This work extends the threshold-cusp interpretation previously applied to $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_{cs}(3985)$ structures, and directly challenges the LHCb amplitude analysis (LHCb, 2020) which fitted all bumps with Breit-Wigner resonances. The finding that $D_s^{(*)}\bar{D}^{*}$ scattering lengths are consistent with zero disfavors the molecule interpretation of $Z_{cs}(4000)$ and $Z_{cs}(4220)$, consistent with lattice QCD calculations via the SU(3) relation that found weak $D^*\bar{D}$ interactions and no bound state for $Z_c(3900)$. The BESIII collaboration (2020) observed a similar structure $Z_{cs}(3985)$ with mass $3982.5^{+1.8}_{-2.6}\pm 2.1$ MeV but width $12.8^{+5.3}_{-4.4}\pm 3.0$ MeV, much narrower than the LHCb $Z_{cs}(4000)$ width of $131\pm 15\pm 26$ MeV, leaving open the question of whether these are the same state.
