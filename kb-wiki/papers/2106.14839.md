# Cluster and hyper-cluster production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics approach

> We study cluster and hypernuclei production in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies employing the Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (PHQMD) approach, a microscopic n-body transport model based on the QMD propagation of the baryonic degrees of freedom with density dependent 2-body potential interactions. All other ingredients of PHQMD, including the collision integral and the treatment of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase, are adopted from the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach. In PHQMD the cluster formation occurs dynamically, caused by the interactions. The clusters are recognized by the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm. We present the PHQMD results for cluster and hypernuclei formation in comparison with the available experimental data at AGS, SPS, RHIC-BES and RHIC fixed target energies. We also provide predictions on cluster production for the upcoming FAIR and NICA experiments. PHQMD allows to study the time evolution of formed clusters and the origin of their production, which helps to understand how such weakly bound objects are formed and survive in the rather dense and hot environment created in heavy-ion collisions. It offers therefore an explanation of the 'ice in the fire' puzzle.

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (PHQMD) approach can dynamically produce clusters and hypernuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions through n-body potential interactions, without requiring an additional coalescence or statistical model, but the cluster multiplicity depends on a chosen freeze-out time (around 50–60 fm/c) that is calibrated to reproduce experimental yields.

## Method

The PHQMD model propagates baryons as Gaussian wave packets under density-dependent Skyrme-type two-body potentials (compression modulus K = 380 MeV, "hard" equation of state) with relativistic Lorentz contraction modifications, while mesonic and partonic degrees of freedom follow the PHSD transport approach including a quark-gluon plasma phase. Clusters are identified dynamically using the Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithm with a coordinate-space distance cut of r0 = 4 fm in the cluster rest frame, applied at a freeze-out time t0 that is scaled by cosh(y) to account for time dilation. The model is compared to experimental data from AGS (E864 at 10.6 AGeV), SPS (NA49 at 40 AGeV and 158 AGeV), and RHIC-BES energies.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of the nuclear equation of state: K = 380 MeV (hard EOS)
- Gaussian width parameter for baryon wave packets: L = 8.66 fm^2
- MST cluster distance cut: r0 = 4 fm in the cluster rest frame
- Critical energy density for parton-hadron transition: epsilon_C = 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Cluster freeze-out time at midrapidity for deuterons at E_kin = 10.6 AGeV: t0 = 45–50 fm/c
- Cluster freeze-out time at midrapidity for tritons and He-3 at E_kin = 10.6 AGeV: t0 = 55–60 fm/c
- Cluster freeze-out time at midrapidity for deuterons at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 GeV (Pb+Pb): t0 = 53 fm/c
- Binding energy of deuteron: 2.24 MeV; rms radius: 1.95 fm
- Ground state nuclear matter density: rho_0; minimum energy per nucleon: -16 MeV at rho_0

## Context

This work extends the PHQMD approach (Aichelin 2019) to relativistic energies from AGS through RHIC-BES, providing a dynamical alternative to coalescence models (Butler and Pearson 1963; Kapusta 1980; Scheibl 1998) and statistical models (Andronic 2010, 2017) for cluster production. It enables studies of the time evolution and formation mechanisms of weakly bound clusters in hot, dense matter, addressing the "ice in the fire" puzzle, and provides predictions for the upcoming FAIR and NICA experiments.
