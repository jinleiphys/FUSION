# Radiative capture and electromagnetic dissociation involving loosely bound nuclei: the $^8$B example

> Electromagnetic processes in loosely bound nuclei are investigated using an analytical model. In particular, electromagnetic dissociation of $^8$B is studied and the results of our analytical model are compared to numerical calculations based on a three-body picture of the $^8$B bound state. The calculation of energy spectra is shown to be strongly model dependent. This is demonstrated by investigating the sensitivity to the rms intercluster distance, the few-body behavior, and the effects of final state interaction. In contrast, the fraction of the energy spectrum which can be attributed to E1 transitions is found to be almost model independent at small relative energies. This finding is of great importance for astrophysical applications as it provides us with a new tool to extract the E1 component from measured energy spectra. An additional, and independent, method is also proposed as it is demonstrated how two sets of experimental data, obtained with different beam energy and/or minimum impact parameter, can be used to extract the E1 component.

## Key claim

The fraction of the electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) energy spectrum attributable to E1 transitions is nearly model-independent at small relative energies, even though the overall calculated energy spectrum is strongly model-dependent. This provides a new tool to extract the E1 component from measured spectra, and a second independent method using two sets of experimental data with different beam energy or minimum impact parameter can also separate the E1 component.

## Method

The authors developed an analytical two-cluster model for loosely bound nuclei, using a model wave function that reproduces both the correct asymptotic Whittaker tail and the small-distance behavior, with parameters fitted to an "exact" two-body wave function or to three-body overlap functions. They applied this model to compute E1 and E2 strength functions and EMD cross sections for 8B, comparing two-body and three-body treatments, and investigated sensitivity to the rms intercluster distance, few-body behavior, and final-state interaction effects.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 8B: E0 = 137 keV (two-body 7Be + p system)
- rms intercluster distance for 8B: r_rms = 4.57 fm (extracted from Ref. [gri99:60])
- A 15% uncertainty in the rms intercluster distance results in approximately 50% ambiguity in the calculated total EMD cross section
- The analytical E1 strength function agrees with numerical "exact" model results to within less than 2% error in the region of interest
- Three-body components of the 8B ground state: [7Be(3/2-) ⊗ p] with channel spin I=2 and spectroscopic factor a²=0.65; [7Be(3/2-) ⊗ p] with I=1 and a²=0.13; [7Be(1/2-) ⊗ p] with I=1 and a²=0.16; these sum to 94% of the total wave function
- Binding energy for the excited core component: E0 = 566 keV

## Context

This work extends analytical approaches previously applied to one-neutron halo systems (Ber08, Ots94) and two-neutron halo nuclei (Pus96, For02) to proton-rich loosely bound nuclei, using 8B as a test case. The paper addresses the challenge of extracting E1 contributions from EMD experiments, which is critical for determining the astrophysical S17 factor for the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction relevant to solar neutrino production. The proposed methods offer alternatives to the asymmetry-based approach of Esbensen (1995), which was found to be strongly dependent on final-state interactions and higher-order dynamical effects.
