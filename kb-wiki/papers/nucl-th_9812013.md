# Hydrogen-like Atoms from Ultrarelativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The number of hydrogen-like atoms produced when heavy nuclei collide is estimated for central collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider using the sudden approximation of Baym et al. As first suggested by Schwartz, a simultaneous measurement of the hydrogen and hadron spectra will allow an inference of the electron or muon spectra at low momentum where a direct experimental measurement is not feasible.

## Key claim

The number of hydrogen-like atoms (p-e, p-μ, π-e, π-μ) produced in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is estimated using the sudden approximation of Baym et al., with the essential contribution being the calculation of lepton, proton, and pion transverse momentum distributions to then estimate atom yields. The authors note that while a simultaneous measurement of atoms and hadrons could infer low-momentum lepton spectra where direct measurement is infeasible, one still cannot tell whether the leptons were produced in quark-gluon plasma or hadronic matter.

## Method

The lepton production rate from quark-antiquark annihilation in the quark-gluon plasma phase was computed using the reaction rate formula with Fermi-Dirac distributions approximated as Boltzmann factors, integrated over the space-time volume according to the Bjorken model. Contributions from the mixed phase during a first-order phase transition were included by weighting the plasma and hadronic phase rates by their respective volume fractions. The proton and pion distributions were modeled as flat rapidity distributions times exponential falloffs in transverse mass, and the atom yields were obtained by multiplying the hadron and lepton distributions together with the factor from the sudden approximation formula of Baym et al.

## Key numbers

- For the most abundant species, π-μ atoms, about 1000 per unit of rapidity per day are estimated for transverse momenta larger than 1 GeV/c, assuming a beam luminosity of 2×10^26 /cm^2 sec.
- The proton distribution uses Z = 79, R_T = 7 fm, and effective temperature T_p = 480 MeV for a central Au+Au collision at 100 GeV per nucleon per beam.
- The pion rapidity distribution is taken as dN_π/dy = 1000, with T_π = 480 MeV.
- The initial temperature T_0 is set to 3T_c = 480 MeV, with T_c being the critical temperature.
- The ratio of degrees of freedom r = 19/3 for three quark flavors and a hadron gas with g_h = 7.5 effective degrees of freedom.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Baym, Friedman, Hughes, and Jacak (1993), who focused on π-μ atoms and derived the sudden approximation formula relating atom, hadron, and lepton spectra. The present paper applies that formalism to estimate yields for p-e, p-μ, π-e, and π-μ atoms at RHIC, using the Bjorken model (Bjorken, 1983) for the space-time evolution and standard lepton pair production rates from Kapusta, Kerman, Matsui, and McLerran (1985). The results suggest that experimental detection of these atoms is promising, but an efficient detector design remains an open question.
