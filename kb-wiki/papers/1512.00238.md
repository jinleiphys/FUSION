# Role of $Žē$-meson exchange in scaling of the $╬│p\toŽĆ^0 p$ process from a Regge-type model with resonances

> The scaling of photoproduction $╬│p\toŽĆ^0 p$ is investigated in the Reggeized model with $N^*$ and $╬ö$ resonances included to describe resonance peaks up to photon energy $E_╬│$= 3 GeV. Given the $t$-channel exchanges $Žü(770)+Žē(780)+b_1(1235)+h_1(1170)$ Reggeized for the background contribution, the resonances of the Breit-Wigner form are introduced to agree with cross sections for total, differential and beam asymmetry in the low energy region. The scaled differential-cross sections $s^7{dŽā/ dt}$ are reproduced to agree with the recent JLab data, revealing the production mechanism of the big bump structure around $W\approx 2.2$ GeV by the deep-dip pattern of the $Žē$ exchange that originates from the zeros of the trajectory $╬▒_Žē(t)=0$ in the canonical phase, ${1\over2}(-1+e^{-iŽĆ╬▒_Žē(t)})$.

## Key claim

The scaling behavior of the $s^7 d\sigma/dt$ cross section for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 p$ can be reproduced within a hadronic Regge model (without explicit quark degrees of freedom) by attributing the observed bump structure around $W \approx 2.2$ GeV to the deep-dip pattern of the $\omega$-meson exchange, specifically from the nonsense wrong signature zero (NWSZ) of the $\omega$ trajectory $\alpha_\omega(t)=0$ in the canonical phase factor $\frac{1}{2}(-1+e^{-i\pi\alpha_\omega(t)})$, rather than to nucleon resonances.

## Method

The authors constructed a Reggeized model for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 p$ combining $t$-channel Regge pole exchanges ($\rho(770)$, $\omega(780)$, $b_1(1235)$, $h_1(1170)$) as background with $s$-channel nucleon resonances ($N^*$ and $\Delta$) described by Breit-Wigner forms using electric and magnetic multipoles. The resonance parameters (masses, widths, helicity amplitudes, branching ratios) were taken from the Particle Data Group, with only a single dimensionless cutoff parameter $d$ adjusted per resonance, and the double-counting between $t$-channel Regge poles and $s$-channel resonances was neglected.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section calculated up to photon energy $E_\gamma = 3$ GeV, with four prominent resonance peaks represented by $\Delta(1232)$, $N(1520)$, $N(1680)$, and $\Delta(1950)$.
- Scaled differential cross sections $s^7 d\sigma/dt$ (in units of $10^7$ GeV$^{14}$ nb/GeV$^2$) reproduced at $\theta = 90^\circ$, $70^\circ$, and $50^\circ$ up to $W \approx 3$ GeV.
- The NWSZ of the $\omega$ trajectory causes the amplitude to vanish at $W \approx 1.58$ GeV at $\theta = 90^\circ$, with the bump shifting to $W \approx 2.5$ GeV at $\theta = 70^\circ$ and to $W \approx 3.1$ GeV at $\theta = 50^\circ$.
- The linear $\omega$ trajectory is $\alpha_\omega(t) = 0.9t + 0.44$, with saturation limit $\alpha(t) \to -1$ constraining the analysis to $-t < 2$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to an upper bound of $W = 2.38$ GeV at $\theta = 90^\circ$.
- Coupling constants for Regge poles: $g_{\gamma\pi\omega} = 0.723$ GeV$^{-1}$, $g^v_{\omega NN} = 15.6$, $g^t_{\omega NN} = 0$.

## Context

This work extends the Reggeized model of Yu (2015) by including explicit $N^*$ and $\Delta$ resonances to describe the low-energy resonance region, enabling agreement with total cross sections, differential cross sections, and beam asymmetry data from JLab (Bartholomy 2005, Dugger 2007) and other experiments. It contradicts the expectation that scaling in exclusive hadronic processes necessarily signals quark-counting rule behavior (Brodsky 1973, 1974), instead showing that the scaling pattern can arise from hadronic $\omega$-meson exchange dynamics. The approach provides a framework for analyzing photoproduction data from threshold up to the energy region of the 12 GeV-upgrade at JLab for forward angles, and up to 3 GeV for intermediate angles without the $\omega N$ cut.
