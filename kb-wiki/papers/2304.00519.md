# Electric and magnetic conductivities in magnetized fermion systems

> In Wigner function approach with relaxation time approximation, we calculate electric and magnetic conductivities of a fermion system in the strong magnetic field. The linear response has been calculated to the perturbation of electromagnetic fields on the background constant magnetic field. The Wigner function is separated into an equilibrium part in the background magnetic field and an off-equilibrium part induced by perturbative fields. The analytical expression for the equilibrium part and the corresponding equilibrium conditions are given. For the off-equilibrium part, we obtain the kinetic equation at the leading order in $\hbar$ from the master equation of the Wigner function. When perturbative fields only depend on the proper time, the off-equilibrium part can be analytically solved from which the vector and axial vector currents are obtained. We obtain the longitudinal and transverse Ohm conductivities as well as Hall conductivity as the linear response of the vector current to the perturbative electric field. The behaviors of these conductivities as functions of the evolving time, relaxation time, particle mass, and strength of the background magnetic field are investigated both analytically and numerically.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical expressions for electric (Ohm) and Hall conductivities in a strongly magnetized fermion system using the Wigner function approach with relaxation time approximation, but only for the case where perturbative electromagnetic fields depend solely on proper time and at leading order in \(\hbar\), with the background magnetic field treated as constant and spacetime-independent.

## Method

The authors separate the Wigner function into an equilibrium part in a constant background magnetic field and an off-equilibrium part induced by weak perturbative fields. They obtain kinetic equations at leading order in \(\hbar\) from the master equation of the Wigner function with relaxation time approximation, solve them analytically for the vector and axial-vector components when perturbative fields depend only on proper time, and compute the induced vector and axial currents as linear responses.

## Key numbers

* Longitudinal Ohm conductivity \(\sigma_{\parallel}\) and transverse Ohm conductivity \(\sigma_{\perp}\) as well as Hall conductivity \(\sigma_{H}\) are obtained as linear response coefficients of the vector current to the perturbative electric field, with their behaviors investigated as functions of evolving time, relaxation time \(\tau\), particle mass \(m\), and background magnetic field strength \(B_{0}\).
* The background magnetic field strength in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}=200\) GeV at RHIC can reach \(10^{17}\)-\(10^{18}\) Gauss (cited from prior work).
* In Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}=2.76\) TeV at LHC, the magnetic field can reach 10 times as high as that at RHIC (cited from prior work).
* The magnetic field decays with time as \(\sim t^{-3}\) in the early stage, but finite Ohm conductivity slows damping to \(\sim t^{-1}\) in the later stage (cited from prior work).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electrical conductivities in strong magnetic fields that used the lowest Landau level approximation (Hattori 2016) or chiral kinetic theory (Lin 2019), as well as studies in medium magnetic fields solving linearized Boltzmann equations with explicit \(1\leftrightarrow2\) processes (Fukushima 2017) or using relaxation time approximation (Das 2019). The covariant Wigner function approach enables analytical treatment of conductivities including their time dependence, which is relevant for magnetohydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and for understanding transport in compact stars such as magnetars.
