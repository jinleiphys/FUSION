# Time Reversal Invariance Violating and Parity Conserving effects in Proton Deuteron Scattering

> Time reversal invariance violating parity conserving (TVPC) effects are calculated for elastic proton deuteron scattering with proton energies up to $2~$MeV. Distorted Wave Born Approximation is employed to estimate TVPC matrix elements, based on hadronic wave functions, obtained by solving three-body Faddeev-Merkuriev equations in configuration space with realistic potentials.

## Key claim
Time-reversal invariance violating parity conserving (TVPC) effects in elastic proton-deuteron scattering are calculated for proton energies up to 2 MeV using a meson-exchange model for the TVPC interaction, with the caveat that the results depend on the choice of strong interaction potential and on the regularization of short-range singularities in the TVPC potentials.

## Method
The authors solved three-body Faddeev-Merkuriev equations in configuration space with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, AV18UIX, INOY) to obtain non-perturbed wave functions for proton-deuteron scattering. They then employed the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) to compute TVPC scattering amplitudes from matrix elements of TVPC potentials derived from rho and h1 meson exchange, with Coulomb contributions subtracted to avoid divergence at zero scattering angle.

## Key numbers
- At E_cm = 100 keV with AV18UIX potential, the time-reversal invariant amplitude Im(f_+ + f_-) = 1.76 fm^{-1}, and TVPC amplitudes (divided by coupling constants) for operators n=5, n=9, n=18 are 0.437 x 10^{-6} fm^{-1}, 0.348 x 10^{-5} fm^{-1}, and -0.136 x 10^{-6} fm^{-1} respectively.
- At E_cm = 1 MeV with AV18UIX potential, the time-reversal invariant amplitude Im(f_+ + f_-) = 6.75 fm^{-1}, and TVPC amplitudes for operators n=5, n=9, n=18 are 0.118 x 10^{-4} fm^{-1}, -0.658 x 10^{-5} fm^{-1}, and 0.482 x 10^{-5} fm^{-1} respectively.
- At E_cm = 2 MeV with AV18UIX potential, the time-reversal invariant amplitude Im(f_+ + f_-) = 8.04 fm^{-1}, and TVPC amplitudes for operators n=5, n=9, n=18 are 0.327 x 10^{-4} fm^{-1}, -0.229 x 10^{-4} fm^{-1}, and 0.296 x 10^{-5} fm^{-1} respectively.
- For neutron-deuteron scattering at E_cm = 100 keV, the TVPC amplitudes for operators n=5, n=9, n=18 are 0.107 x 10^{-6} fm^{-1}, -0.217 x 10^{-5} fm^{-1}, and -0.711 x 10^{-7} fm^{-1} respectively.
- The parity-violating scattering amplitude (from DDH pion-exchange potential) at E_cm = 1 MeV with AV18UIX is -0.729 x 10^{+0} fm^{-2}.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of TVPC effects in neutron-deuteron scattering by Song (2011) to the proton-deuteron system, and complements earlier TVPC calculations for proton-deuteron scattering at higher energies (hundreds of MeV) by Beyer (1993) and Uzikov (2015) in relation to the COSY experiment. The results show that proton-deuteron and neutron-deuteron scattering have different sensitivities to TVPC interactions from h1 and rho meson exchange, making them complementary probes for new physics beyond the Standard Model. The calculations also provide parity-violating amplitudes that can be compared with future measurements of parity violation in proton-deuteron scattering.
