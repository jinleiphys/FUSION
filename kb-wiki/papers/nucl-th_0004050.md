# Pairing Interaction in N=Z Nuclei with Half-filled High-j Shell

> The role of L=0 pairing interactions (both T=0 and T=1) in three selected bands with the total isospin T=0 in 22Na, 48Cr and 90Rh nuclei is discussed in the spherical shell model. These bands were selected requiring termination in a most unfavourable way. The investigated effects coming from pairing interactions include the change of nuclear wavefunction, quadrupole properties and state energy. To follow the gradual change of nuclear properties, the investigation is performed in a perturbative approach by defining the Hamiltonian with a variable content of the pairing interaction. It is shown that the pairing does not affect the quadrupole properties, if the energy distance to other close lying states is large. The calculated pairing energy is shown to have a similar spin dependence in these three bands. In addition, the influence of the model space as well as of the residual interaction on the derived pairing energy is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in three selected N=Z nuclei with half-filled high-j shells, the L=0 pairing interactions (both T=0 and T=1) affect the selected bands in a similar way, but the pairing does not affect quadrupole properties when the energy distance to other close lying states is large, and the isoscalar (T=0) pairing interaction may be less prominent due to its smooth spin dependence.

## Method

Spherical shell model calculations were performed using the computer code ANTOINE with residual interactions: USD interaction in the full sd-shell for 22Na, KB3 in the full pf-shell for 48Cr, and GF, SLGT0, and SSSV interactions for 90Rh. The role of normalized L=0 pairing interactions was investigated perturbatively by defining a Hamiltonian H_JT(λ) with variable content of the pairing interaction (weight parameter λ from 0 to 1), comparing results from the full Hamiltonian to Hamiltonians with reduced content of either isovector (T=1) or isoscalar (T=0) pairing.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength parameters: G̅_01 = -0.32 ħω and G̅_10 = -0.51 ħω, with resulting values for 22Na: G̅_01 = -3.80, G̅_10 = -6.05; for 48Cr: G̅_01 = -3.19, G̅_10 = -5.09; for 90Rh: G̅_01 = -2.68, G̅_10 = -4.27 (all in MeV)
- Maximum spin states: Imax = 9ħ for 22Na, 16ħ for 48Cr, and 25ħ for 90Rh
- The probability of the configuration where all active particles are in the high-j shell producing spin Imax is >90% for 48Cr and 90Rh (SLGT0 and SSSV calculation), 54% for 22Na, and 62% for 90Rh (GF)
- Excitation energy of the lowest T=0 states in 90Rh is predicted to be approximately 1.3 MeV
- The B(E2) value for the transition 13₂⁺→11₂⁺ in 90Rh (SSSV calculation) is approximately 14.4 W.u.
- The spectroscopic quadrupole moment at I=21 in 90Rh (SSSV) decreases to -38.0 e·fm², while along another band it is approximately constant at -10 e·fm²
- The deformation β for 90Rh ground state (SSSV interaction) is approximately 0.13

## Context

This work extends the discussion of isoscalar and isovector L=0 pairing components in N=Z nuclei, following studies by Martinez-Langanke (year not specified) and Poves and Martinez (year not specified) for nuclei in the middle of the pf-shell. The paper specifically examines three nuclei with half-filled high-j shells (22Na, 48Cr, 90Rh) that exhibit "most unfavoured" band termination, where the last two transitions before termination involve breaking and alignment of two T=1 pairs. The results enable comparison of pairing effects across different mass regions and suggest that only lifetime measurements or calculations in more extended model spaces may resolve whether there is a decrease in B(E2) values at specific spins in 90Rh.
