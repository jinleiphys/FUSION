# Cooling timescale for protoneutron stars and properties of nuclear matter: Effective mass and symmetry energy at high densities

> The cooling process of a protoneutron star is investigated with focus on its sensitivity to properties of hot and dense matter. An equation of state, which includes the nucleon effective mass and nuclear symmetry energy at twice the saturation density as control parameters, is constructed for systematic studies. The numerical code utilized in this study follows a quasi-static evolution of a protoneutron star solving the general-relativistic stellar structure with neutrino diffusion. The cooling timescale evaluated from the neutrino light curve is found to be longer for the models with larger effective masses and smaller symmetry energies at high densities. The present results are compared with those for other equations of state and it is found that they are consistent in terms of their dependences on the effective mass and neutron star radius.

## Key claim

The cooling timescale of a protoneutron star, evaluated from the neutrino light curve, is longer for models with larger nucleon effective masses and smaller symmetry energies at high densities, but this dependence is only significant during the shallow decay phase (after about 20 seconds) and is minor for early times (less than 10 seconds).

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological equation of state (EOS) for hot dense matter using a polynomial expansion with the nucleon effective mass (in units of rest mass, parameter u) and the symmetry energy at twice saturation density (S00) as control parameters, while fixing saturation properties. They performed quasi-static, spherically symmetric, general-relativistic simulations of protoneutron star cooling with multigroup flux-limited neutrino diffusion, starting from initial conditions at 0.3 seconds after bounce from a 15 solar mass progenitor collapse model. The cooling was computed until the electron antineutrino luminosity dropped to 5 × 10^48 erg/s, and the e-folding time of the neutrino luminosity was used to quantify the cooling timescale.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density is set to n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, saturation energy w0 = -16 MeV, and incompressibility K0 = 245 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter.
- The symmetry energy at saturation is S0 = 31 MeV with slope L = 50 MeV; the symmetry energy at twice saturation density S00 is varied among 40, 45, 50, 55, and 60 MeV.
- The effective mass parameter u (ratio of effective mass to rest mass) is set to 0.5, 0.75, and 1.0, giving 15 model combinations.
- The protoneutron star has a baryon mass of 1.47 solar masses, yielding gravitational masses of 1.327–1.336 solar masses for the authors' EOS models.
- The reference model (S00 = 45 MeV, u = 1) has protoneutron star radii of 16.8 km at t = 2 s, 13.8 km at t = 10 s, and 13.1 km at t = 20 s.
- The electron antineutrino luminosity drops to 5 × 10^48 erg/s within 100 seconds for all models.
- The maximum mass of cold neutron stars exceeds 2 solar masses for all considered S00 values.

## Context

This work extends prior protoneutron star cooling studies by Pons et al. (1999) and Roberts et al. (2012) by systematically separating the roles of the effective mass and symmetry energy at high densities using a phenomenological EOS. The results are compared with three established supernova EOSs: the Lattimer and Swesty EOS with incompressibility 220 MeV (LS220 EOS), the Shen EOS, and the Togashi EOS (with the hybrid T+S EOS used for comparison). The findings enable future constraints on hot dense matter properties from observations of supernova neutrino signals, particularly the cooling timescale from the next Galactic supernova detected by SuperKamiokande.
