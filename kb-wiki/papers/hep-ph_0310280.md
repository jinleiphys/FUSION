# $J/ψ$ couplings to open charm mesons from QCD sum rules

> We employ QCD sum rules to calculate the $J/ψ$ form factors and coupling constants with charmed mesons, by studying three-point correlation functions. In particular we consider the $J/ψD^*D$ and $J/ψDD$ vertices. We determine the momentum dependence of the form factors for kinematical conditions where J/ψ, D or D^* are off-shell. Extrapolating to the pole of each one of the so obtained form factors, we determine the coupling constants. For both couplings our results (within the errors) are compatible with estimates based on constituent quark model.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule calculations yield coupling constants for the $J/\psi D^*D$ and $J/\psi DD$ vertices that are compatible with estimates from constituent quark models, but only within the theoretical uncertainties; the results for $g_{\psi DD^*}$ agree with the constituent quark model value of Deandrea (2003) while the $g_{\psi DD}$ result is lower than that model's prediction.

## Method

The authors computed three-point correlation functions in QCD sum rules for the $J/\psi D^*D$ and $J/\psi DD$ vertices, evaluating the operator product expansion up to dimension-three operators (perturbative diagram and quark condensate) plus the gluon condensate contribution. They determined the momentum dependence of the form factors for three different off-shell conditions (off-shell $D$, off-shell $D^*$, and off-shell $J/\psi$) and extrapolated each form factor to the pole of the off-shell meson to extract the coupling constant. The Borel transformed sum rules were used with continuum thresholds $\Delta_s = \Delta_u = (0.5 \pm 0.1)$ GeV to improve the matching between the hadronic and QCD sides.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\psi DD^*} = (4.0 \pm 0.6)$ GeV$^{-1}$ for the $J/\psi D^*D$ coupling constant
- $g_{\psi DD} = 5.8 \pm 0.9$ for the $J/\psi DD$ coupling constant
- $g_{\psi DD}/m_D = (3.1 \pm 0.5)$ GeV$^{-1}$, consistent with the HQET relation $g_{\psi DD^*} = g_{\psi DD}/m_D$
- Input meson masses: $m_D = 1.87$ GeV, $m_{D^*} = 2.01$ GeV, $m_\psi = 3.1$ GeV
- Input decay constants: $f_{J/\psi} = (405 \pm 15)$ MeV, $f_D = (170 \pm 10)$ MeV, $f_{D^*} = (240 \pm 20)$ MeV
- Charm quark mass $m_c = 1.3$ GeV; quark condensate $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.23)^3$ GeV$^3$; gluon condensate $\langle g_s^2 G^2 \rangle = 0.5$ GeV$^4$
- Cutoff parameters: $\Lambda \sim 4.5$ GeV for off-shell $D$ or $D^*$; $\Lambda \sim 7.5$ GeV for off-shell $J/\psi$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule studies of the $D^*D\pi$, $DD\rho$, and $J/\psi DD$ vertices (Navarra et al., 2000; Bracco et al., 2001; Matheus et al., 2002) to the $J/\psi D^*D$ vertex, providing an independent determination that can be compared with the vector meson dominance model plus relativistic potential model of Oh et al. (2001) and the constituent quark model of Deandrea et al. (2003). The results enable more reliable calculations of $J/\psi$ dissociation cross sections by pions and $\rho$ mesons in effective Lagrangian approaches, where different coupling constants have previously led to cross sections differing by more than an order of magnitude.
