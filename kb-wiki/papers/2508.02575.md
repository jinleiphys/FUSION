# Density functional theory of renormalization group in nuclear matter

> The density functional renormalization group (density-fRG) is proposed to investigate the density fluctuations within the functional renormalization group approach, which allows us to quantify the medium effect and study physics of high densities. This method is applied to the nucleon-meson effective field theory, also known as the Walecka model, to study the properties of nuclear matter at high baryon densities. It is found that both the attractive and repulsive nucleon meson interactions are screened by the high density medium, which results in a stiffer equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter in the regime of $ρ_0 \lesssim ρ\lesssim 2.5 ρ_0$, then a softer EoS when $ρ\gtrsim 2.5 ρ_0$. Here $ρ_0$ denotes the saturation baryon density of symmetric nuclear matter. Furthermore, a new phenomenon called the locking of Fermi surface is found. In the locking of Fermi surface the effective energy of quasi-nucleon is always close to the Fermi surface, which are both running with the renormalization group scale.

## Key claim

The density functional renormalization group (density-fRG) method separates density fluctuations from vacuum fluctuations within the functional renormalization group approach, and when applied to the Walecka model, it reveals that both attractive and repulsive nucleon-meson interactions are screened by the high-density medium, leading to a stiffer equation of state between saturation density and approximately 2.5 times saturation density, followed by a softer equation of state beyond that density.

## Method

The authors constructed a density-fRG framework by starting from the Wetterich equation for the nucleon-meson effective field theory (Walecka model) and subtracting the vacuum contributions from the threshold functions, so that only density fluctuations contribute to the RG evolution. They derived flow equations for the effective potential and for the three Yukawa couplings (nucleon-sigma, nucleon-omega, nucleon-rho), solving these coupled equations from the ultraviolet limit (k → ∞) to the infrared limit (k → 0) at fixed baryon densities. The parameters were fitted to reproduce symmetric nuclear matter properties at saturation density: binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV, incompressibility of 215 MeV, symmetry energy of 32 MeV, and saturation density of 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Saturation baryon density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = -16 MeV
- Incompressibility at saturation: K = 215 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_s = 32 MeV
- The equation of state is stiffer in the regime ρ₀ ≲ ρ ≲ 2.5 ρ₀ and softer when ρ ≳ 2.5 ρ₀
- Two parameter sets used: m_σ = 500 MeV (Set I) and m_σ = 600 MeV (Set II); vector meson masses fixed at m_ω = 782 MeV and m_ρ = 775 MeV
- Nucleon mass in vacuum: m_N = 938 MeV

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group approach to nuclear matter by introducing a density-specific formulation that isolates medium effects from vacuum quantum fluctuations, building on the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974) and previous fRG developments (Fu, 2019; Fu, 2022). The method goes beyond the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation, where Yukawa couplings are constant and density-independent, by allowing the coupling strengths to run with the RG scale and thus acquire density dependence self-consistently. This enables more reliable calculations for high-density nuclear matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron star astrophysics, where densities can reach several times saturation density.
