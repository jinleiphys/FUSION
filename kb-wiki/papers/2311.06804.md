# Pion-sigma meson vortices in rotating systems

> Possibilities of the formation of the pion-sigma meson field vortices in a rotating empty vessel (in vacuum) and in the pion-sigma Bose-Einstein condensates at a dynamically fixed particle number are studied within the linear $σ$ model at zero temperature. The charged $σπ^{\pm}$ and neutral $σπ^0$ complex field ansatze (models 1 and 2) are studied. First it is analysed, which of these configurations is energetically favorable in case of the system at rest. Then conditions are found, at which a chiral field storm can arise in a rotating empty vessel. In the model 1 an important role played by the electric field is demonstrated. Its appearance may allow for formation of a supervortex (vortex with a large angular momentum) in case of the empty vessel rotating with an overcritical angular velocity. Influence of magnetic field is also studied. Field configurations in presence and absence of the meson self-interaction are found in both models. Then it is shown that description of the charged (in model 1) and neutral (in model 2) rotating pion-sigma Bose-Einstein condensates is analogous to that for the Bose-Einstein condensates in cold atomic gases. Various field configurations such as vortex lines, rings and spirals are discussed. Conditions for existence of the rigid-body rotation of the vortex lattice are then analysed. Observational effects for vortex fields in rotating vessels, energetic heavy-ion collisions and in rotating superheavy nuclei and nuclearites are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether pion-sigma meson field vortices can form in rotating systems, both in an empty vessel (vacuum) and in Bose-Einstein condensates with a dynamically fixed particle number, using the linear sigma model at zero temperature; the abstract glosses over the condition that the formation of a supervortex in an empty rotating vessel requires an overcritical angular velocity and the presence of an electric field in model 1.

## Method

The study employs the linear sigma model at zero temperature, analyzing two specific ansatze: model 1, which describes a complex charged pion field (σπ±), and model 2, which describes a neutral complex σπ⁰ field. The authors first determine which configuration is energetically favorable for a system at rest, then derive conditions for chiral field vortex formation in a rotating empty vessel, both with and without meson self-interaction, electric fields, and magnetic fields. The description of rotating charged and neutral pion-sigma Bose-Einstein condensates is then shown to be analogous to Bose-Einstein condensates in cold atomic gases, with analysis of vortex lines, rings, spirals, and rigid-body rotation of vortex lattices.

## Key numbers

- The critical rotation frequency for vortex formation in a rotating vessel is given as Ωc1 ∼ ln R/(mR²), where m is the mass of helium atoms and R is the transverse size of the vessel.
- In peripheral heavy-ion collisions of Au + Au at √s = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 10 fm, angular momenta L ∼ 10⁶ ħ are estimated.
- The STAR Collaboration observed global polarization of Λ(1116) hyperons indicating a rotation frequency Ω ≃ (9 ± 1) · 10²¹ Hz ≃ 0.05 mπ.
- Magnetic fields up to ∼ (10¹⁷–10¹⁸) G are predicted for peripheral heavy-ion collisions at energies ∼ GeV per nucleon, with fields H ≲ (10¹⁵–10¹⁶) G at magnetar surfaces and H ≲ 10¹⁸ G in neutron star interiors.
- The pion mass is mπ ≃ 140 MeV, the sigma meson mass is mσ ≃ 600 MeV, and the coupling constant λ ≃ 20.
- For a positively charged nuclear droplet, the critical electric potential for π⁻ level diving is Vc = −2mπ* at gω₀ = 0.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vortex formation in superfluid ⁴He (Pitaevskii, 1984) and Bose-Einstein condensates in cold atomic gases (Baym and Pethick, 2012), as well as earlier investigations of pion condensation in rotating systems (Zahed; Guo). It also builds on the concept of dynamically fixed pion number in heavy-ion collisions, suggested by Voskresensky (1994) and studied by Kolomeitsev (2018, 2019), and on analyses of giant vortex states in rapidly rotating Bose-Einstein condensates (Fischer and Baym, 2003). The results enable future searches for chiral vortex structures in rotating vessels, energetic heavy-ion collisions, and rotating superheavy nuclei and nuclearites.
