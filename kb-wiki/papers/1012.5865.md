# Spin-orbit and orbit-orbit strengths for radioactive neutron-rich doubly magic nucleus $^{132}$Sn in relativistic mean field theory

> Relativistic mean field (RMF) theory is applied to investigate the properties of the radioactive neutron-rich doubly magic nucleus $^{132}$Sn and the corresponding isotopes and isotones. The two-neutron and two-proton separation energies are well reproduced by the RMF theory. In particular, the RMF results agree with the experimental single-particle spectrum in $^{132}$Sn as well as the Nilsson spin-orbit parameter $C$ and orbit-orbit parameter $D$ thus extracted, but remarkably differ from the traditional Nilsson parameters. Furthermore, the present results provide a guideline for the isospin dependence of the Nilsson parameters.

## Key claim
Relativistic mean field (RMF) theory reproduces the experimental single-particle spectrum in the neutron-rich doubly magic nucleus \(^{132}\)Sn and yields Nilsson spin-orbit and orbit-orbit parameters that differ remarkably from traditional Nilsson parameters, but the agreement is limited to the mean-field level and the results show that the reduction of both parameters along the \(N=82\) isotonic chain is predicted rather than definitively measured.

## Method
The authors applied five versions of RMF theory (with effective interactions PC-PK1, NL3*, DD-ME2, PK1, and PKDD) to calculate two-neutron and two-proton separation energies for Sn isotopes and \(N=82\) isotones, and the neutron effective single-particle energies in \(^{132}\)Sn. They then extracted the Nilsson spin-orbit parameter \(C\) and orbit-orbit parameter \(D\) by fitting to the single-particle energies of the \(3p_{1/2}\), \(3p_{3/2}\), \(2f_{5/2}\), and \(2f_{7/2}\) states using a least-squares method, and compared these with experimental data and traditional Nilsson parameters from Nilsson (1969).

## Key numbers
- The re-fitted spin-orbit parameter \(C\) for \(^{132}\)Sn reduces by 50% compared to the traditional Nilsson parameter.
- The re-fitted orbit-orbit parameter \(D\) for \(^{132}\)Sn is one order of magnitude smaller than the traditional Nilsson parameter.
- The RMF calculations show profound jumps in two-neutron separation energies \(S_{2n}\) at \(N=82\) and two-proton separation energies \(S_{2p}\) at \(Z=50\), consistent with \(^{132}\)Sn being doubly magic.
- The non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock results overestimate nearly twice the single-particle energy spacing between the orbitals \(3p_{3/2}\) and \(2f_{7/2}\) compared to experiment.

## Context
This work extends the study of shell structure in exotic nuclei, following the experimental confirmation by Jones (2010) that \(^{132}\)Sn is doubly magic, and the earlier suggestion by Schiffer (2004) that the spin-orbit interaction decreases with neutron excess. The results contradict the traditional Nilsson parameters from Nilsson (1969) and provide a guideline for the isospin dependence of the Nilsson parameters, which is relevant for calculations such as the yrast bands in \(^{136}\)Te and \(^{142}\)Xe using the projected shell model by Zhang (1998). The findings enable improved modeling of neutron-rich nuclei and the \(r\)-process.
