# Quasi-free (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions with unstable nuclei

> We study (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions at proton energies in the range of 100 MeV -- 1 GeV. Our purpose is to explore the most sensitive observables in unpolarized reactions with inverse kinematics involving radioactive nuclei. We formulate a model based on the eikonal theory to describe total cross sections and momentum distributions of the recoiled residual nucleus. The model is similar to the one adopted for knockout reactions with heavy ions. We show that momentum distributions are sensitive to the angular momentum of the ejected nucleon which can be used as an spectroscopic tool. The total cross sections are sensitive to the nucleon separation energies and to multiple scattering effects. Our calculations also indicate that a beam energy around 500 MeV/nucleon has a smaller dependence on the anisotropy of the nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering.

# Quasi-free (p,2p) and (p,pn) Reactions with Unstable Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that momentum distributions in quasi-free (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions with unstable nuclei in inverse kinematics are sensitive to the angular momentum of the ejected nucleon, making them a viable spectroscopic tool, but only if the beam energy is around 500 MeV/nucleon where the dependence on the anisotropy of nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering is minimized.

## Method

The authors formulate a model based on eikonal theory to describe total cross sections and momentum distributions of the recoiled residual nucleus, similar to the approach used for knockout reactions with heavy ions. They employ a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework with eikonal scattering waves, incorporating medium corrections to nucleon-nucleon cross sections via a parametrization based on local nuclear density, and use Woods-Saxon single-particle wavefunctions calculated with central, surface, and spin-orbit potentials.

## Key numbers

- Proton energy range studied: 100 MeV to 1 GeV
- Optimal beam energy identified: approximately 500 MeV/nucleon, where dependence on NN scattering anisotropy is smallest
- Proton rms radius used in calculations: 0.87 fm (corresponding to form factor parameter a² = 0.71 fm⁻²)
- Energy range for NN cross section fits: 10 MeV ≤ E_lab ≤ 5 GeV
- The factorization approximation is stated to be valid for proton energies as low as 75 MeV (citing Sam86)
- Off-energy-shell effects are estimated to be only a few percent uncertainty for proton energies greater than 200 MeV (citing BM61, JM66)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for quasi-free scattering, which was largely developed for stable nuclei in the past 50 years, to the new domain of radioactive beam experiments in inverse kinematics at facilities like GSI and RIKEN. The approach is similar to that used for knockout reactions with heavy ions (Hansen 2003, Gade 2008) but addresses the specific challenges of (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions where the reaction is less surface-dominated than composite-target knockout. The formalism enables systematic studies of single-particle structure and nucleon-nucleon correlations as a function of neutron-to-proton asymmetry, with planned experiments such as those at the R3B setup.
