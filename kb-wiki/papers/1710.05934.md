# Resonances in a sudden chemical freeze-out model

> The prediction for $p_T$ spectra of various resonances produced in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV at the LHC in equilibrium and non-equilibrium models is made. It includes the $η$, $ρ$(770), $Σ$(1385), $Λ$(1520), and $Ξ$(1530). The apparent differences may allow to distinguish between the models.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the transverse momentum spectra of several resonances (eta, rho(770), Sigma(1385), Lambda(1520), and Xi(1530)) produced in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV will show significant differences between equilibrium and non-equilibrium (Bose-Einstein condensate) freeze-out models, but notes that current uncertainties are too large to definitively distinguish between the models.

## Method

The author used the SHARE and THERMINATOR codes to fit mean multiplicities and pT spectra from ALICE data at 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, extracting thermodynamic parameters (temperature T, volume V, and non-equilibrium parameters gamma_s and gamma_q) for equilibrium (EQ), non-equilibrium (NEQ), and non-equilibrium with pion Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) scenarios. The freeze-out hypersurface was described by a Hubble-like single freeze-out with a ratio of freeze-out time tau_f to freeze-out radius r_f as the only extra parameter for spectral shapes. Predictions for resonances not included in the fit were then generated from the fitted parameters.

## Key numbers

- Temperature and pion chemical potential (mu_pi = 2T ln gamma_q) were extracted for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, with the system closer to the BEC scenario in central collisions but with large uncertainties
- Charged pions favor the BEC model at low pT, while the eta/pi0 ratio favors the EQ model, though uncertainties are too large to judge definitively
- K*(892)0 spectra are closer to the BEC prediction than to EQ, despite not being included in the fit of mean multiplicities or spectra
- BEC predicts similar multiplicities and spectra for Lambda(1520) and Xi(1530), while EQ predicts a larger multiplicity difference between these two resonances
- Strange baryons require a different freeze-out hypersurface than pions, kaons, protons, K0_S, K*, and phi, with a mass-dependent slope in BEC where heavier baryons have smaller flow

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Begun 2013, 2014) that successfully described pions, kaons, protons, K*(892)0, and phi(1020) with a single freeze-out non-equilibrium model, questioning the necessity of a long re-scattering phase used by other approaches (Knospe 2015). The predictions for eta, rho(770), Sigma(1385), Lambda(1520), and Xi(1530) provide a test to differentiate between equilibrium with re-scattering and the sudden non-equilibrium freeze-out scenario, which also leads to pion condensation (Begun 2015, 2016). The mass-dependent flow of heavy baryons is consistent with other approaches (Melo 2015, Chatterjee 2014).
