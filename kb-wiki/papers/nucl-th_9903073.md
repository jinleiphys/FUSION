# Baryon, Charged Hadron, Drell-Yan and J/Psi Production in High Energy Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> We show that the distributions of outgoing protons and charged hadrons in high energy proton-nucleus collisions are described rather well by a linear extrapolation from proton-proton collisions. The only adjustable parameter required is the shift in rapidity of a produced charged meson when it encounters a target nucleon. Its fitted value is 0.16. Next, we apply this linear extrapolation to precisely measured Drell-Yan cross sections for 800 GeV protons incident on a variety of nuclear targets which exhibit a deviation from linear scaling in the atomic number A. We show that this deviation can be accounted for by energy degradation of the proton as it passes through the nucleus if account is taken of the time delay of particle production due to quantum coherence. We infer an average proper coherence time of 0.4 +/- 0.1 fm/c, corresponding to a coherence path length of 8 +/- 2 fm in the rest frame of the nucleus. Finally, we apply the linear extrapolation to measured J/Psi production cross sections for 200 and 450 GeV/c protons incident on a variety of nuclear targets. Our analysis takes into account energy loss of the beam proton, the time delay of particle production due to quantum coherence, and absorption of the J/Psi on nucleons. The best representation is obtained for a coherence time of 0.5 fm/c, which is consistent with Drell-Yan production, and an absorption cross section of 3.6 mb, which is consistent with the value deduced from photoproduction of the J/Psi on nuclear targets.

# Baryon, Charged Hadron, Drell-Yan and J/Psi Production in High Energy Proton-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The observed deviation from linear A-scaling in Drell-Yan production and the apparent discrepancy between J/Psi absorption cross sections extracted from proton-nucleus versus photoproduction experiments can both be resolved by a single hadronic model that includes energy degradation of the beam proton, a finite quantum coherence time of approximately 0.4–0.5 fm/c, and, for J/Psi, a nuclear absorption cross section of 3.6 mb. The claim is conditional on the assumption that the linear extrapolation from proton-proton collisions (LEXUS) with a single fitted rapidity shift of 0.16 for charged mesons provides an adequate baseline description of the soft hadronic physics.

## Method

The authors constructed a linear extrapolation model (LEXUS) from proton-proton collisions, parameterizing the beam proton rapidity distribution after each successive nucleon-nucleon collision and folding it over the nuclear density distribution. For charged hadron production, a rapidity shift of 0.16 per struck nucleon was applied to produced mesons. For Drell-Yan and J/Psi production, the model incorporated energy degradation of the beam proton as it cascaded through the nucleus, with a variable time delay (coherence time) that determines how many previous collisions are ignored when computing the available energy for hard production; for J/Psi, an additional absorption cross section on nucleons was included in the final state.

## Key numbers

- Rapidity shift for charged mesons per struck nucleon: 0.16 (fitted from p+S and p+Au data at 200 GeV)
- Average proper coherence time from Drell-Yan analysis: 0.4 +/- 0.1 fm/c, corresponding to a coherence path length of 8 +/- 2 fm in the nucleus rest frame (using total NN cross section of 40 mb and nuclear matter density of 0.155 nucleons/fm^3)
- Best-fit coherence time for J/Psi production: 0.5 fm/c, consistent with the Drell-Yan value
- Best-fit J/Psi absorption cross section on nucleons: 3.6 mb (from combined fit to 200 and 450 GeV/c data)
- Parameter lambda (fraction of non-diffractive, non-elastic collisions): 0.6, independent of beam energy in the 12–400 GeV range
- Average number of negatively charged hadrons per NN collision: given by formula <h^->_NN = 0.784 * (sqrt(s) - 2m_N - m_pi)^(3/4) / s^(1/8)
- J/Psi production cross section parametrization: B * sigma_NN->J/psi(x_F > 0) = 37 * (1 - m_J/psi/sqrt(s))^12 nb, with x_F dependence proportional to (1-|x_F|)^5

## Context

This work extends the LEXUS model previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions (Jeon and Kapusta, 1997) to proton-nucleus data, and addresses the long-standing puzzle of J/Psi absorption cross sections. Prior Glauber analyses without energy loss or coherence time effects (e.g., Alde et al., 1991) had extracted J/Psi absorption cross sections of 6–7 mb, inconsistent with the value of approximately 3–4 mb from photoproduction experiments. The authors show that including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect (quantum coherence time) reconciles these values. The analysis enables more accurate baseline calculations of J/Psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, against which anomalous suppression from quark-gluon plasma formation can be measured. The authors note that a complementary partonic analysis by Gavin and Milana (1992) obtained satisfactory Drell-Yan results with quark energy loss of about 1.5 GeV/fm, and that nuclear shadowing (e.g., as studied by Eskola et al.) also needs to be considered.
