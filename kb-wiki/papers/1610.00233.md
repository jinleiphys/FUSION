# Probing the nature of phases across the phase transition at finite isospin chemical potential

> We compare the low eigenvalue spectra of the Overlap Dirac operator on two sets of configurations at $μ_I/μ_I^c$ = 0.5 and 1.5 generated with dynamical staggered fermions at these isospin chemical potential on $24^3 \times 6$ lattices. We find very small changes in the number of zero modes and low lying modes which is in stark contrast with those across the corresponding finite temperature phases where one sees a drop across the phase transition. Possible consequences are discussed.

## Key claim
The low eigenvalue spectra of the Overlap Dirac operator show very small changes across the isospin chemical potential phase transition at $\mu_I/\mu_I^c = 0.5$ and 1.5, in stark contrast to the sharp drop observed across the finite temperature phase transition, suggesting the nature of the two transitions may differ.

## Method
The authors generated dynamical configurations on $24^3 \times 6$ lattices using a Symanzik improved action with 2 stout steps and quark mass tuned to the physical pion mass. They extracted approximately 500 eigenvalues of the Overlap Dirac operator using the Arnoldi method with a residue $r \le 10^{-10}$, for configurations at $\mu_I/\mu_I^c = 0.5$ and 1.5, each at two values of the pionic source $\lambda_I$ (0.0006 and 0.0025). The eigenvalue distributions and zero mode counts were compared across the transition and with prior finite temperature results.

## Key numbers
- The critical isospin chemical potential is $a\mu_I^c = 0.1$ on the $24^3 \times 6$ lattice, corresponding to $\mu_I^c = m_\pi/2$.
- At $\lambda_I = 0.0006$, the number of zero modes from 50 configurations is 426 at $\mu_I/\mu_I^c = 0.5$ and 451 at $\mu_I/\mu_I^c = 1.5$, showing almost no variation.
- At $\lambda_I = 0.0025$, the number of zero modes from 50 configurations is 416 at $\mu_I/\mu_I^c = 0.5$ and 310 at $\mu_I/\mu_I^c = 1.5$, a reduction of approximately 25%.
- For comparison, at finite temperature with $\mu = 0$, the number of zero modes from 50 configurations drops from 18 at $T/T_c = 1.25$ to 8 at $T/T_c = 1.5$ and 1 at $T/T_c = 2.0$.

## Context
This work extends the investigation of the isospin chemical potential phase transition by Endrodi (2016), who found chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement at the transition point on $8^4$ lattices. The results contrast sharply with finite temperature studies by Edwards et al. (2000) and Gattringer et al. (2001), which showed a steep depletion of zero and near-zero modes above $T_c$. The findings suggest that topological excitations behave differently across the isospin transition compared to the thermal transition, motivating further quantitative studies to understand the difference between the $T$ and $\mu_I$ directions.
