# A new effective theory for stochastic relativistic hydrodynamics

> Thermal fluctuations are a fundamental feature of dissipative systems that are essential for understanding physics near the expected critical point of QCD and in small systems. When such fluctuations are modeled naively in relativistic systems, strange features can appear such as negative self-correlation functions. We construct an effective theory for nonlinear stochastic relativistic hydrodynamics that ensure a well-posed mathematical formulation. Using Crooks fluctuation theorem, we derive a symmetry of the effective action that incorporates fluctuations through a suitable free energy functional. For divergence type theories, the action can then be fully specified using a single vector generating current. The equations of motion obtained using this procedure are guaranteed to be flux conservative and symmetric hyperbolic when the dynamics is causal. This ensures that these equations are well-posed (for suitable initial data) and are in a form that can easily be simulated, including with Metropolis techniques.

# A new effective theory for stochastic relativistic hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The authors construct an effective theory for nonlinear stochastic relativistic hydrodynamics that guarantees well-posedness by enforcing Crooks fluctuation theorem as a symmetry of the effective action, but only for divergence-type theories where the action can be fully specified using a single vector generating current.

## Method
The authors combine divergence-type hydrodynamics (Geroch, 1990) with Crooks fluctuation theorem (Crooks, 1999) to derive a symmetry condition on the effective Lagrangian that enforces nonlinear fluctuation-dissipation relations. The generating current \(X^\mu\) is expanded to include non-equilibrium terms, and the equations of motion take the flux-conservative form \(\nabla_\mu (\partial X^\mu / \partial \phi^A) = I_A(\phi)\), with the entropy current given by \(s^\mu = X^\mu - \phi^A (\partial X^\mu / \partial \phi^A)\). The effective Lagrangian is constructed to leading order as \(\mathcal{L} = -\bar{\phi}^A \nabla_\mu (\partial X^\mu / \partial \phi^A) + i \zeta^{ab}_0(\phi) \bar{\phi}_a (\bar{\phi}_b + i \phi_b)\), where \(\zeta^{ab}_0(\phi)\) is an even function with support only on the dissipative subspace.

## Key numbers
- The effective action is specified by a generating current \(X^\mu\) and a "dissipative potential" \(\zeta^{ab}_0(\phi)\), which is determined by the entropy production.
- For the relativistic diffusion example, the Lagrangian includes a term \(\frac{i}{\kappa T} \bar{j}_\mu (\bar{j}^\mu + i j^\mu)\), where \(\kappa\) is a transport coefficient and \(T\) is the temperature.
- The entropy production rate is \(\sigma \equiv \nabla_\mu s^\mu = \phi^A I_A\), with the leading-order choice \(I_A = \Xi_{AB} \phi^B\) where \(\Xi_{AB}\) is positive semi-definite.

## Context
This work extends the divergence-type hydrodynamics framework of Geroch (1990) by incorporating thermal fluctuations through Crooks fluctuation theorem, addressing issues such as negative self-correlation functions that appear in naive stochastic relativistic models. The formalism enables systematic construction of causal, stable stochastic hydrodynamic theories that can be simulated using Metropolis techniques, and is relevant for studying fluctuations near the QCD critical point and in small systems. The approach contrasts with gradient-expansion-based stochastic hydrodynamics, which faces challenges in incorporating fluctuations in causal versions as noted by Mullins (2023) and Gavassino (2024).
