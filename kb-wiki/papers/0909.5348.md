# Similarity of nuclear structure in 132Sn and 208Pb regions: proton-neutron multiplets

> Starting from the striking similarity of proton-neutron multiplets in 134Sb and 210Bi, we perform a shell-model study of nuclei with two additional protons or neutrons to find out to what extent this analogy persists. We employ effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized by use of the V-low-k approach. The calculated results for 136Sb, 212Bi, 136I, and 212At are in very good agreement with the available experimental data. The similarity between 132Sn and 208Pb regions is discussed in connection with the effective interaction, emphasizing the role of core polarization effects.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the striking similarity between proton-neutron multiplets in the exotic 132Sn region and the stable 208Pb region persists when adding two identical particles (protons or neutrons), but with the hedge that this similarity emerges naturally from realistic shell-model interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential without adjustable parameters, and that core polarization effects (specifically 1p1h excitations) play a crucial role in reproducing the observed level ordering.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations using effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential renormalized via the V-low-k approach, with the effective interaction calculated through a Q-box folded-diagram expansion including second-order terms (V-low-k, core polarization diagrams V_1p1h and V_2p2h, and a ladder diagram). For the 132Sn region, valence protons occupied the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 levels of the 50-82 shell, while neutrons occupied the 1f7/2, 2p3/2, 0h9/2, 2p1/2, 1f5/2, and 0i13/2 levels of the 82-126 shell; for the 208Pb region, valence protons occupied the six levels of the 82-126 shell and neutrons the seven levels of the 126-184 shell. Calculations were performed using the NUSHELLX code.

## Key numbers
- The diagonal matrix elements of V_low-k for the πg7/2 νf7/2 configuration in the 132Sn region range from -0.596 MeV (Jπ=0-) to -0.070 MeV (Jπ=6-), while for the πh9/2 νg9/2 configuration in the 208Pb region they range from -0.468 MeV (Jπ=0-) to -0.035 MeV (Jπ=8-)
- The V_1p1h contribution for the 0- state is +0.089 MeV in the 132Sn region and +0.049 MeV in the 208Pb region, while for the 1- state it is -0.079 MeV and -0.105 MeV respectively, showing opposite signs that bring the 1- state down in energy
- The percentage of the leading configuration in 134Sb ranges from 100% to 88%, and in 210Bi from 100% to 91%
- For 136I, the percentage of the (πg7/2)3 νf7/2 configuration ranges from 64% to 75%; for 212At, the (πh9/2)3 νg9/2 configuration ranges from 66% to 75%
- For 136Sb, the πg7/2 (νf7/2)3 configuration ranges from 58% to 76%; for 212Bi, the πh9/2 (νg9/2)3 configuration ranges from 48% to 75%
- The largest discrepancy between theory and experiment is about 80 keV for the 9- state in 212At
- The V_1p1h matrix element for the (π0g7/2)2 0+ configuration is -0.549 MeV, while for the (ν1f7/2)2 0+ configuration it is only -0.100 MeV, demonstrating the stronger proton-proton pairing from core polarization

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous successful shell-model calculations for 134Sb and 210Bi (Coraggio 2005, 2006, 2007a) to nuclei with two additional protons or neutrons (136I, 136Sb, 212At, 212Bi), building on the observation by Blomqvist (1981) of a correspondence between single-particle states in the 132Sn and 208Pb regions. The study contradicts earlier unsuccessful attempts using the modified Kuo-Herling interaction (Warburton 1991, Shergur 2005) and shows that a consistent Hamiltonian derived from a realistic NN potential can account for the similarity between these regions. The results enable future experimental studies of missing states in these nuclei and provide a theoretical framework for interpreting data from radioactive beam facilities exploring exotic nuclei around 132Sn.
