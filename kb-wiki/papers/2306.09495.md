# Transverse momentum dependent factorization for SIDIS at next-to-leading power

> The semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) is the golden process for investigating the nucleon's transverse momentum-dependent (TMD) structure. We present the complete expression for SIDIS structure functions in the TMD factorization formalism at the next-to-leading power. All perturbative elements of the factorization theorem -- coefficient functions and evolution kernels -- are presented at one-loop accuracy. We found several differences with earlier derivations, which are due to accounting for nontrivial kinematics of the quark-gluon interference terms. As a side result, we present the definition and evolution of twist-three TMD fragmentation functions, including the leading-order evolution kernel.

## Key claim

The authors present the complete expression for SIDIS structure functions in the TMD factorization formalism at next-to-leading power, with all perturbative elements at one-loop accuracy, but note that the derivation does not formally prove the factorization theorem because it assumes hadron wave-functions can be separated without demonstrating the cancellation of Glauber regions.

## Method

The authors apply the TMD operator expansion method, which uses a background-field approach and follows the concept of operator product expansion in position space, to derive the hadronic tensor for SIDIS at NLP. They separate contributions by TMD-twist rather than dynamical twist, which yields independent nonperturbative distributions with distinct coefficient functions and evolution kernels. The genuine NLP term is transformed from complex-valued functions into a form with explicit complex structure, and the resulting coefficient functions and evolution kernels for twist-three TMD distributions are computed at one-loop accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The LP hard coefficient function at NLO: $|C_1(\mu^2,Q^2)|^2 = 1 + 2a_s(\mu)C_F(-\ln^2(Q^2/\mu^2) + 3\ln(Q^2/\mu^2) - 8 + \pi^2/6) + \mathcal{O}(a_s^2)$, where $C_F = (N_c^2-1)/2N_c$ and $a_s = g^2/(4\pi)^2$
- The coefficient function $\mathbb{C}_R(x,u_2)$ at LO: $1/(u_2)_+$, with NLO terms containing $C_F$ and $C_A$ contributions involving $\mathbf{L} = \ln(Q^2/\mu^2)$
- The coefficient function $\mathbb{C}_I(x,u_2)$ at LO: $\delta(u_2)$, with NLO corrections including terms proportional to $C_F$ and $C_A$
- The coefficient function $\mathbb{C}_2(z,w_2)$ at LO: $1$, with NLO corrections proportional to $C_F$ and $C_A$ involving $\mathbf{L} = \ln(Q^2/\mu^2)$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous developments on TMD factorization at sub-leading power (Vladimirov 2021, Rodini 2022) and independently reproduces earlier computations such as Mulders (1995), Boer (2003), and Bacchetta (2006), while uncovering several contributions overlooked in those earlier studies. The primary difference is the inclusion of genuine twist-three distributions with vanishing gluon momentum, which are crucial for theoretical consistency and may have numerical significance for upcoming SIDIS experiments at facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider. The results enable future phenomenological studies of power corrections in the TMD factorization approach and provide the definition and leading-order evolution kernel for twist-three TMD fragmentation functions, which were previously unstudied.
