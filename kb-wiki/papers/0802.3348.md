# Transverse momentum spectra of hadrons produced in central heavy-ion collisions

> In-medium effects of transverse-mass distributions of quarks and gluons are considered assuming a possible local equilibrium for colorless quark objects like mesons and baryons created in central A+A collisions. It is shown that the average square of the transverse momentum for these partons grows and then saturates when the initial energy increases. Within the quark-gluon string model it leads to the energy dependence of hadron transverse mass spectra which is similar to that observed in heavy ion collisions. Comparison with other scenarios is given.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed "anomalous" energy dependence of the effective transverse temperature (inverse slope) of kaons in central heavy-ion collisions can be qualitatively explained by an in-medium effect on quark distributions inside hadrons that are in local thermodynamic equilibrium with the surrounding fireball, without requiring a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors calculate the transverse-momentum distribution of constituent quarks inside a hadron that is embedded in a thermally equilibrated fireball, using a convolution of the quark distribution in a free hadron with the hadron's thermal distribution function. They then apply the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) to compute the inclusive transverse-momentum spectrum of hadrons produced in nucleon-nucleon collisions where one nucleon is locally equilibrated, and extract the average transverse momentum squared of the produced hadrons as a function of the collision energy.

## Key numbers

- The average transverse momentum squared of a quark inside a hadron in equilibrium, \(<p_{q,t}^2>_h^A\), saturates at an asymptotic value of about \(2<p_t^2>_q^h\) at high energies, where \(<p_t^2>_q^h = 1/\gamma_q\) is the value in a free hadron.
- The ratio of the slope parameters for fragmentation and constituent quark distributions is taken as \(r = \gamma_c/\gamma_q = 3-4\), based on QGSM analyses of inclusive hadron spectra.
- The temperature used for the fireball in the numerical estimates is \(T = 150\) MeV.
- The approximate relation for kaons is \(<p_{K,t}^2>^{AA} \simeq T^*\) GeV/\(c^2\), where \(T^*\) is the inverse slope parameter from the exponential fit to the transverse mass spectrum.
- The parameter \(\alpha\) in the Cronin-effect simulation is \(\alpha \approx 0.4\).
- The color rope model gives a hardening of spectra by about \(15\%\) at SPS and RHIC energies.

## Context

This work extends the quark-gluon string model (QGSM) of Kaidalov (1982) and the dual parton model of Capella (1994) by incorporating a thermal in-medium effect on quark distributions, as an alternative to explanations based on a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma (Gorenstein et al., 2003; Mohanty, 2003). The observed saturation of the kaon inverse slope at SPS energies was not reproduced by transport models such as UrQMD and HSD (Bratkovskaya, 2004, 2005), but was successfully described by hydrodynamic models with a phase transition (Bravina, 2004) and also by a 3-fluid hydrodynamic model with a pure hadronic equation of state (Ivanov, 2006). The paper suggests that the proposed thermal collectivity mechanism should be incorporated into dynamical transport models for a definitive test, and notes that the puzzle is relevant to the scientific programs of future accelerators FAIR and NICA.
