# Hydrodynamic Excitations from Chiral Kinetic Theory and the Hydrodynamic Frames

> In the framework of chiral kinetic theory (CKT), we consider a system of right- and left-handed Weyl fermions out of thermal equilibrium in a homogeneous weak magnetic field. We show that the Lorentz invariance implies a modification in the definition of the momentum current in the phase space, compared to the case in which the system is in global equilibrium. Using this modified momentum current, we derive the linearized conservation equations from the kinetic equation up to second order in the derivative expansion. It turns out that the eigenmodes of these equations, namely the hydrodynamic modes, differ from those obtained from the hydrodynamic in the Landau-Lifshitz (LL) frame at the same order. We show that the modes of the former case may be transformed to the corresponding modes in the latter case by a global boost. The velocity of the boost is proportional to the magnetic field as well as the difference between the right- and left-handed charges susceptibility. We then compute the chiral transport coefficients in a system of non-Abelian chiral fermions in the no-drag frame and by making the above boost, obtain the well-known transport coeffiecients of the system in the LL frame. Finally by using the idea of boost, we reproduce the AdS/CFT result for the chiral drag force exerted on a quark at rest in the rest frame of the fluid, without performing any holographic computations.

## Key claim

The authors show that the hydrodynamic modes obtained from chiral kinetic theory (CKT) differ from those obtained in the Landau-Lifshitz (LL) frame at second order in derivatives, but that the two sets of modes can be related by a global boost whose velocity is proportional to the magnetic field and the difference between right- and left-handed charge susceptibilities.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral kinetic theory for right- and left-handed Weyl fermions in a homogeneous weak magnetic field, using the Boltzmann equation with Berry curvature corrections. They derive a modified momentum current in phase space required by Lorentz invariance when the system is out of global equilibrium, then linearize the conservation equations (energy, momentum, and chiral charges) up to second order in the derivative expansion. They compute the hydrodynamic eigenmodes from these equations and compare them to the modes obtained from standard chiral hydrodynamics in the Landau-Lifshitz frame, showing that the two frames are related by a boost. They also compute chiral transport coefficients in the no-drag frame and reproduce the AdS/CFT result for the chiral drag force on a quark at rest without holographic computations.

## Key numbers

* The thermodynamic susceptibilities for chiral charges are given by \(\bar{\boldsymbol{\chi}}_{\chi} = T^2 \left( \frac{\mu_{\chi}^2}{2 \pi^2 T^2} + \frac{1}{6} \right)\) for \(\chi = R, L\).
* The enthalpy density is \(w = T^4 \left( \frac{\mu_R^4 + \mu_L^4}{6 \pi^2 T^4} + \frac{\mu_R^2 + \mu_L^2}{3 T^2} + \frac{7 \pi^2}{45} \right)\).
* The heat capacity is \(C_v = \frac{7 \pi^2 T^3}{15} + \frac{T (\mu_R^2 + \mu_L^2)}{2}\).
* The chiral charge densities are \(n_{\chi} = T^3 \left( \frac{\mu_{\chi}^3}{6 \pi^2 T^3} + \frac{\mu_{\chi}}{6 T} \right)\) for \(\chi = R, L\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Stephanov and Yin (2014) which derived the chiral magnetic wave from chiral kinetic theory while decoupling energy and momentum perturbations from chiral charge perturbations. The present paper includes all hydrodynamic perturbations and shows that the resulting modes differ from those computed in chiral hydrodynamics by Abbasi et al. (2016) in the Landau-Lifshitz frame. The authors demonstrate that the chiral kinetic theory corresponds to a no-drag frame (also called the thermodynamic frame or Lab frame), and that the boost relating frames can reproduce known results for chiral transport coefficients from Neiman and Oz (2010) and the chiral drag force from Rajagopal and Sadofyev (2015) without holographic computation.
