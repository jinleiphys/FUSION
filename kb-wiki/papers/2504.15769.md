# Spin structure of spin-1 charmonium states near $T_c$

> We investigate the spin structure of the $1^{--}$ and $1^{++}$ charmonium states near the critical temperature using QCD sum rules. To this end, we compute the contribution of the dimension-4 twist-2 gluon operator to the two-point function of heavy vector and axial vector currents in a rotating frame. As temperature increases, the quark spin contribution slightly increases, while the quark orbital angular momentum decreases by a comparable amount. The gluon contribution remains nearly unchanged. These thermal changes cancel each other, ensuring that the total spin is preserved even at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The total spin of vector (1⁻⁻) and axial vector (1⁺⁺) charmonium states is preserved even near the critical temperature, because the quark spin contribution increases by a few percent while the quark orbital angular momentum decreases by a comparable amount, and the gluon contribution remains nearly unchanged. This conclusion is based on QCD sum rules incorporating only dimension-4 operators and assumes all temperature dependence is encoded in the gluon condensates, with Wilson coefficients unaffected.

## Method

The author computed the contribution of the dimension-4 twist-2 gluon operator to the two-point function of heavy vector and axial vector currents in a rotating frame, using operator product expansion (OPE) at leading order in αₛ. The spin decomposition was obtained by inserting the total angular momentum density M_z into the two-point function, separating contributions from quark spin (S_q), quark orbital angular momentum (L_q), and gluon total angular momentum (J_g). The Borel-transformed QCD sum rule was then applied to extract ground-state spin components, using Borel windows and continuum thresholds determined from conventional OPE analysis of the inertial-frame two-point function.

## Key numbers

* In vacuum, the vector (1⁻⁻) charmonium state has quark spin contribution s_q = 87.6 ± 1.5%, quark orbital angular momentum l_q = 11.8 ± 2%, and gluon angular momentum j_g = 0.6 ± 0.5%.
* In vacuum, the axial vector (1⁺⁺) charmonium state has s_q = 40.1 ± 1%, l_q = 61.4 ± 0.3%, and j_g = -1.5 ± 0.7%.
* The analysis covers temperatures from 1.00 T_c to 1.04 T_c, using the charm quark mass m_c(p² = -m_c²) = 1.262 GeV and strong coupling αₛ(8m_c²) = 0.21.
* The scalar gluon condensate in vacuum is G₀ = (0.35 GeV)⁴, with G₂ = 0 in vacuum.
* For the vector channel, the continuum threshold √s₀ decreases from 3.57 GeV in vacuum to 3.29 GeV at 1.04 T_c, and the Borel window (M_min, M_max) shifts from (1.08, 2.35) GeV to (0.95, 1.90) GeV.
* For the axial vector channel, √s₀ decreases from 4.02 GeV in vacuum to 3.45 GeV at 1.04 T_c, and the Borel window shifts from (1.39, 2.33) GeV to (1.14, 1.57) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous QCD sum rule analysis of charmonium spin structure in vacuum (Kim 2022) to finite temperature by newly incorporating the dimension-4 twist-2 gluon operator. It builds on earlier thermal QCD sum rule studies of charmonium spectral modifications near T_c by Morita (2009) and Gubler (2011, 2018), and uses gluon condensate values from pure SU(3) lattice gauge simulations by Boyd (1996) and Morita (2009). The results enable future studies incorporating O(αₛ) perturbative corrections and higher-dimensional operators for a more precise understanding of charmonium spin structure in a hot medium.
