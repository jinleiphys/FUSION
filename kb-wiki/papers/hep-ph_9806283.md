# Diffractive Production of Drell-Yan pairs and Heavy Flavors

> Partonic interpretation of high-energy reactions is known to depend on a reference frame. Particularly, in the rest frame of the target Drell-Yan process looks like freeing of the projectile lepton-antilepton fluctuation, rather than q+qbar -> l+lbar annihilation. The light-cone representation for Drell-Yan reaction is very similar to that in DIS and exposes a substantial contamination of soft interactions, which turn out to be dominant in diffractive production of lepton pairs and in nuclear shadowing. We estimate the fraction of diffractive events in the total Drell-Yan cross section, which scales in M^2, and find a substantial deviation from factorization. An analogous approach is developed for diffractive production of heavy flavors.

# Diffractive Production of Drell-Yan pairs and Heavy Flavors

## Key claim
The paper argues that diffractive Drell-Yan production is dominated by soft interactions, not hard perturbative ones, and that the fraction of diffractive events in the total Drell-Yan cross section scales in M² but shows a substantial deviation from factorization compared to deep-inelastic scattering (DIS). The author estimates that the ratio of diffractive to total cross section in Drell-Yan is about half that in DIS, with an additional suppression from survival probability that ranges from a few percent at Tevatron energies to about 20% at lower energies.

## Method
The author develops a light-cone representation for Drell-Yan lepton pair production in the target rest frame, treating the process as the freeing of a projectile lepton-antilepton fluctuation rather than as q+qbar -> l+lbar annihilation. The cross section is expressed in terms of a quark distribution function, a light-cone distribution function for the q l lbar Fock component, and a dipole cross section for a colorless qbar-q pair. For diffractive production, the author uses two-gluon exchange diagrams and introduces a modified dipole cross section that includes an extra color screening factor from spectator partons, then estimates the diffractive fraction by comparing the averaged dipole cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The mean transverse separation squared of the l lbar fluctuation is approximately 1/[(1-alpha) M²], where alpha is the light-cone momentum fraction carried by the pair.
- For heavy pairs with M > 4 GeV at SPS CERN with p_lab = 200 GeV, the longitudinal momentum transfer q_L is large enough to suppress nuclear shadowing via the nuclear formfactor.
- The ratio of diffractive to total cross section in Drell-Yan is approximately (1/2) times that in DIS, based on the comparison of the dipole cross sections in Eqs. (10) and (4a).
- The survival probability factor for diffractive Drell-Yan ranges from a few percent at Tevatron collider energy up to about 20% at lower energies.
- Nuclear suppression for Drell-Yan in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC can be a factor of 0.5 or less.

## Context
This work extends the light-cone approach previously applied to DIS (Bjorken and Kogut, 1973; Kopeliovich, 1995) to Drell-Yan production and heavy flavor production. It contradicts the naive expectation that heavy lepton pair production is dominated by hard scales, showing instead that soft fluctuations with large transverse separations contribute equally to the cross section due to their large interaction cross section. The paper enables predictions for nuclear shadowing in Drell-Yan at RHIC and LHC energies, and provides a framework for calculating diffractive heavy flavor production, though the author notes that comparison with data requires treatment of exclusive channels in a separate publication.
