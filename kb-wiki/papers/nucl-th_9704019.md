# Time invariance violating nuclear electric octupole moments

> The existence of a nuclear electric octupole moment (EOM) requires both parity and time invariance violation. The EOMs of odd $Z$ nuclei that are induced by a particular T- and P-odd interaction are calculated. We compare such octupole moments with the collective EOMs that can occur in nuclei having a static octupole deformation. A nuclear EOM can induce a parity and time invariance violating atomic electric dipole moment, and the magnitude of this effect is calculated. The contribution of a nuclear EOM to such a dipole moment is found, in most cases, to be smaller than that of other mechanisms of atomic electric dipole moment production.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that nuclear electric octupole moments (EOMs) induced by a specific T- and P-odd interaction are generally smaller contributors to atomic electric dipole moments than other mechanisms such as the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment or Schiff moment, except possibly in the case of \(^{229}\)Pa where the contribution may be comparable but whose static octupole deformation is uncertain.

## Method

The authors calculate single-particle EOMs for odd-Z nuclei using a perturbative approach, where a T- and P-odd nucleon-nucleus interaction Hamiltonian \(H_{TP} = \eta \frac{G}{2\sqrt{2}m} \boldsymbol{\sigma} \cdot \nabla \rho\) shifts the unpaired proton's wave function. They also compute collective EOMs for nuclei with static octupole deformation by evaluating the mixing coefficient \(\alpha\) between parity-doublet states induced by \(H_{TP}\), using deformation parameters from the literature. Finally, they compute the atomic EDM induced by these nuclear EOMs via relativistic single-electron matrix elements and compare them to contributions from magnetic quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle EOM: \(\mathcal{O}_{\text{sing}} \approx 8.7 \times 10^{-9} A^{2/3} \eta e (\text{fm})^3\), with expressions depending on whether \(I = l+1/2\) or \(I = l-1/2\).
- Collective EOM estimate: \(|\mathcal{O}_{\text{coll}}| \sim 4 \times 10^{-5} \eta e (\text{fm})^3\) for \(A \approx 225\), using \(\beta_3 \approx 0.1\) and \(|E_+ - E_-| \sim 50\) keV.
- For \(^{209}\)Bi, the matrix element for the EOM-induced atomic EDM is \(\approx 1.9 \times 10^{-13} (\nu_1 \nu_2)^{-3/2} \eta \text{ cm}^{-1}\), compared to \(1.3 \times 10^{-11} (\nu_1 \nu_2)^{-3/2} \eta \text{ cm}^{-1}\) for the magnetic quadrupole moment.
- For \(^{225}\)Ac, the collective EOM matrix element is \(\approx 1.4 \times 10^{-11} (\nu_1 \nu_2)^{-3/2} \eta \text{ cm}^{-1}\), versus \(\sim 6 \times 10^{-11} (\nu_1 \nu_2)^{-3/2} \eta \text{ cm}^{-1}\) for the collective MQM.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of T- and P-odd nuclear moments, particularly the Schiff moment and magnetic quadrupole moment calculations by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984) and Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1986). It also builds on studies of collective octupole deformation in the Ra-Th and Ba-Sm regions reviewed by Ahmad and Butler (1993), and on calculations of parity-doublet mixing by Auerbach, Flambaum, and Sorokin (1996, 1997). The results enable experimental searches for atomic EDMs to prioritize contributions from Schiff moments and magnetic quadrupole moments over those from nuclear EOMs, except possibly in certain deformed nuclei.
