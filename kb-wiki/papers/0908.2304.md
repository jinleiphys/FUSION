# Three-dimensional approach for construction of low-momentum effective interaction from realistic potentials

> The low-momentum effective interaction $V_{low k}$ has been formulated in the three-dimensional momentum-helicity representation as a function of the magnitude of momentum vectors and the angle between them. As an application, AV18 potential has been used in the model space of Lee-Suzuki method and it has been shown that the low-momentum effective interaction, $V_{low k}$ reproduces the same two-body observables obtained by the bare potential $V_{NN}$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a three-dimensional (3D) momentum-helicity formulation of the low-momentum effective interaction \(V_{low k}\) that avoids partial-wave decomposition, and demonstrates that this 3D \(V_{low k}\) reproduces the same two-body observables as the bare AV18 potential when using the Lee-Suzuki method with a momentum cutoff of \(\Lambda = 2.1\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\). The hedge is that the agreement is shown only for the specific AV18 potential and for the chosen cutoff value, and the method is validated by comparing 3D results to partial-wave projections.

## Method

The authors formulate the Lee-Suzuki model-space method in the 3D momentum-helicity representation, using antisymmetrized parity-eigenstate basis states with spin, isospin, and helicity quantum numbers. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the half-on-shell \(T\)-matrix in this 3D representation for both singlet (\(S=0\)) and triplet (\(S=1\)) channels, then compute the \(\omega\) operator from the wave-function components via a completeness relation, and finally obtain \(V_{low k}\) by integrating the bare potential \(V_{NN}\) and the \(\omega\) operator over the high-momentum (\(Q\)-space) region. The AV18 potential is used as the bare interaction, and the momentum cutoff is set to \(\Lambda = 2.1\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- Momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.1\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) used to divide the Hilbert space into low-momentum (\(P\)) and high-momentum (\(Q\)) spaces.
- Typical integration ranges: \(a = 10\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) and \(b = 150\,\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for the hyperbolic and linear mappings of Gauss-Legendre points.
- Numerical grid: 60 grid points for momentum in \([0,a]\), 22 grid points for momentum in \([a,b]\), 40 grid points for the spherical angle, and 10 grid points for the polar angle.
- The AV18 potential is fitted to \(pp\) and \(np\) data below 350 MeV laboratory energy.

## Context

This work extends the 3D momentum-helicity approach previously developed for few-body bound and scattering problems (Elster, 1998; Fachruddin, 2000) to the construction of low-momentum effective interactions, which were previously formulated only in partial-wave decomposition by Bogner et al. (2003, 2005). The paper builds on the authors' own earlier 3D formalism that neglected spin and isospin (Bayegan, 2008) and now includes those degrees of freedom. The resulting 3D \(V_{low k}\) enables future applications to few-body bound and scattering calculations without the need for partial-wave decomposition, automatically including all partial waves.
