# Photodisintegration of light nuclei for testing a correlated realistic interaction in the continuum

> An exact calculation of the photodisintegration cross section of 3H, 3He and 4He is performed using as interaction the correlated Argonne V18 potential, constructed within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (VUCOM). Calculations are carried out using the Lorentz Integral Transform method in conjunction with an hyperspherical harmonics basis expansion. A comparison with other realistic potentials and with available experimental data is discussed. The VUCOM potential leads to a very similar description of the cross section as the Argonne V18 interaction with the inclusion of the Urbana IX three-body force for photon energies 45< w < 120 MeV, while larger differences are found close to threshold.

## Key claim

The VUCOM potential, a correlated non-local two-body interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method, produces photodisintegration cross sections for 3H, 3He, and 4He that are very similar to those from the AV18+UIX model (which includes a three-body force) for photon energies between 45 and 120 MeV, but larger differences appear near the disintegration threshold.

## Method

The total photodisintegration cross section was calculated exactly using the Lorentz Integral Transform method, which reduces the continuum scattering problem to a bound-state-like equation solved with an expansion in hyperspherical harmonics. The VUCOM potential was represented via correlated harmonic oscillator two-body matrix elements of the AV18 potential, and the dipole approximation was employed, which implicitly accounts for meson exchange currents through the Siegert theorem.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 4He with VUCOM: -28.57(3) MeV (HH calculation) compared to -28.30 MeV in nature
- Ground state energy of 3H with VUCOM: -8.27(2) MeV (HH calculation) compared to -8.48 MeV in nature
- For 4He, the VUCOM peak cross section is about 10% lower than with bare AV18, and the tail at omega=60 MeV is about 23% higher than bare AV18
- For 3H, the VUCOM peak cross section is about 4% lower than bare AV18, and the tail at omega=60 MeV is about 15% higher than bare AV18
- For 4He, the TRK sum rule m0 = 117.6 mb MeV for VUCOM, about 20% lower than the AV18+UIX value of 146.2 mb MeV
- Mean square point proton radius of 3H: 2.52(1) fm^2 with VUCOM versus 2.51(1) fm^2 with AV18+UIX
- Mean square radius of 4He: 1.96(1) fm^2 with VUCOM versus 2.05(1) fm^2 with AV18+UIX

## Context

This work extends exact photodisintegration calculations for light nuclei, previously performed with realistic two- and three-body forces by Golak (2002) for 3H/3He and by Gazit (2006) for 4He, to test the VUCOM correlated interaction for the first time in a continuum reaction. The results show that the non-local VUCOM potential can partly simulate the effects of genuine three-body forces at high photon energies, but the differences near threshold indicate the two potential models are not completely equivalent; the calculations provide theoretical guidance for astrophysical studies of gamma-ray absorption in compact sources.
