# Microscopic approach to the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction and nucleon-nucleon scattering in symmetric and isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> After reviewing our microscopic approach to nuclear and neutron-rich matter, we focus on how nucleon-nucleon scattering is impacted by the presence of a dense hadronic medium, with special emphasis on the case where neutron and proton densities are different. We discuss in detail medium and isospin asymmetry effects on the total elastic cross section and the mean free path of a neutron or a proton in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. We point out that in-medium cross sections play an important role in heavy-ion simulations aimed at extracting constraints on the symmetry potential. We argue that medium and isospin dependence of microscopic cross sections are the results of a complex balance among various effects, and cannot be simulated with a simple phenomenological model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that medium and isospin asymmetry effects on nucleon-nucleon cross sections in asymmetric nuclear matter result from a complex balance among various effects and cannot be reproduced by simple phenomenological models, such as scaling with the square of the reduced mass ratio.

## Method

The author uses the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, starting from a realistic one-boson-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential (Bonn B) fit to free-space scattering data, with no additional free parameters in the medium. The in-medium reaction matrix (G-matrix) is solved self-consistently for asymmetric nuclear matter using the Thompson three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with separate single-particle potentials for neutrons and protons determined through iterative solution of the coupled G-matrix equations. The effective masses and single-particle potentials obtained from this procedure are then used to calculate in-medium total elastic cross sections and mean free paths for neutrons and protons.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at nuclear matter density (ρ₀) is typically 30 MeV, with theoretical predictions spreading between approximately 26 and 35 MeV.
- The empirical parametrization of the symmetry energy is e_sym(ρ) = 12.5 MeV (ρ/ρ₀)^(2/3) + 17.5 MeV (ρ/ρ₀)^γ_i, where γ_i is found to be between 0.4 and 1.0.
- Recent measurements of elliptic flows in ¹⁹⁷Au + ¹⁹⁷Au reactions at GSI at 400-800 MeV/nucleon favor a potential energy term with γ_i equal to 0.9 ± 0.4.
- Values for the symmetry energy and the L parameter centered around 32.5 MeV and 70 MeV, respectively, are obtained from nuclear structure and heavy-ion collision measurements for densities between 0.3ρ₀ and ρ₀.
- Predictions of L from phenomenological Skyrme models range from -50 to +100 MeV.
- The parametrization of the in-medium np cross section from Li and Machleidt (1993) is given as σ_np^med = (31.5 + 0.092 |20.2 - E_lab^0.53|^2.9) × (1.0 + 0.0034 E_lab^1.51 ρ²)/(1.0 + 21.55 ρ^1.34).
- The parametrization of the in-medium pp cross section from Li and Machleidt (1993) is given as σ_pp^med = (23.5 + 0.00256 (18.2 - E_lab^0.5)^4) × (1.0 + 0.1667 E_lab^1.05 ρ³)/(1.0 + 9.704 ρ^1.2).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous DBHF calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter (Sammarruca and Alonso 2003; Sammarruca 2010) to focus on in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and mean free paths. It contrasts with phenomenological approaches that scale free-space cross sections by the square of the reduced mass ratio (Prakash and Patra 1992; Gale et al. 1990; Li and Chen 2005) and with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using explicit three-body forces (Zhang et al. 2007). The results are intended for use in isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport models for heavy-ion collisions, particularly those aimed at extracting constraints on the symmetry potential and for determining nuclear radii of exotic neutron-rich nuclei via Glauber-type models.
