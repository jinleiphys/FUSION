# Calculating loops without loop calculations: NLO computation of pentaquark correlators

> We compute next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD corrections to the correlators of interpolating pentaquark currents. We employ modular techniques in configuration space which saves us from the onus of having to do loop calculations. The modular technique is explained in some detail. We present explicit NLO results for several interpolating pentaquark currents that have been written down in the literature. Our modular approach is easily adapted to the case of NLO corrections to multiquark correlators with an arbitrary number of quarks/antiquarks.

## Key claim

The authors compute next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD corrections to pentaquark correlators using a modular technique in configuration space that avoids explicit loop integration, but they note that the NLO corrections are large and that the method does not determine whether the pentaquark mass lies above or below the hadronic threshold.

## Method

The authors employ a modular method in configuration space where the NLO corrections are built from two basic modules: the propagator correction \(S_1(x)\) and the dipropagator correction \(S_2(x)\), expressed in terms of the free massless quark propagator \(S_0(x^2)\). These modules are combined algebraically for any given interpolating pentaquark current, and the spectral densities are obtained by taking the discontinuity of the momentum-space correlation function using Bessel function integrals. Renormalization is performed by subtracting counterterms that account for wave function and vertex renormalization, including mixing effects from gluon exchange between quark lines.

## Key numbers

* For the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark current \(\eta_0\) with quantum numbers \(J^P = 1/2^+\) at \(1530\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the spectral density is \(\rho_{\eta_0}(s) = \frac{31 s^5}{50400 (4\pi)^8} \left\{1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \left( \frac{45}{31} \ln \frac{\bar\mu^2}{s} + \frac{7429}{651} \right) \right\}\), where the finite NLO correction term is \(7429/651 (\alpha_s/\pi)\).
* For the current \(\eta'_1\), the spectral density is \(\rho_{\eta'_1}(s) = \frac{17 s^5}{6300 (4\pi)^8} \left\{1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \left( \frac{2255}{408} \right) \right\}\), with a finite NLO correction of \(2255/408 (\alpha_s/\pi)\).
* For the pentaquark of the second kind \(\theta_1\), the spectral density is \(\rho_{\theta_1}(s) = \frac{s^5}{1575 (4\pi)^8} \left\{1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \left( 2 \ln \frac{\bar\mu^2}{s} + \frac{1021}{70} \right) \right\}\), where the finite NLO correction is \(1021/70 (\alpha_s/\pi)\), the largest among all cases treated.
* For the current \(\eta_2\), the spectral density is \(\rho_{\eta_2}(s) = \frac{s^5}{525 (4\pi)^8} \left\{1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \left( -\frac{7}{6} \ln \frac{\bar\mu^2}{s} + \frac{377}{360} \right) \right\}\).
* For the current \(\eta'_2\), the spectral density is \(\rho_{\eta'_2}(s) = \frac{s^5}{525 (4\pi)^8} \left\{1 + \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \left( -\frac{11}{6} \ln \frac{\bar\mu^2}{s} - \frac{15877}{10080} \right) \right\}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of QCD sum rules to exotic multiquark states, specifically pentaquarks, by providing NLO perturbative corrections that were previously unavailable for these systems. It builds on earlier NLO calculations for baryon correlators by Krasnikov (1980s) and Jamin (1987), and on sum rule analyses of pentaquarks by Zhu (2003) and Matheus (2004), which were limited to leading order. The modular technique developed here enables purely algebraic computation of NLO corrections for any multiquark correlator with an arbitrary number of quarks and antiquarks, and the authors state that the large NLO corrections will significantly affect the relative weight of perturbative and nonperturbative condensate terms in future sum rule analyses of pentaquark masses.
