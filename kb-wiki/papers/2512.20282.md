# Towards compressed baryonic matter densities: thermodynamics and transport coefficients

> We study the thermodynamic and transport properties of hot and dense quantum chromodynamic matter expected to be produced in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, using three different effective quantum chromodynamic frameworks: the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, the chiral effective model, and the hadron resonance gas model. We briefly outline the theoretical formulation of thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients within these approaches, where quarks are treated with effective masses in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio and chiral effective models, and hadronic degrees of freedom are employed in the hadron resonance gas model. The transport coefficients are evaluated using the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation-time approximation. Following the theoretical overview, we present a comprehensive analysis of the behavior of these quantities as functions of the baryon chemical potential or net baryon density. The Lorenz ratio $κ/(σT)$ is found to increase rapidly-indicating a strong violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law in the low-$μ_{B}$ regime--while approaching the universal value at higher baryon chemical potentials or densities. The shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio $η/s$ remains nearly constant at low $μ_{B}$ but exhibits a gradual increase as $μ_{B}$ grows. We also discuss the qualitative similarities of these trends with those observed in the electron-hole plasma of graphene, an emergent quasi-relativistic system characterized by massless energy-momentum dispersion.

## Method

The authors evaluate thermodynamic quantities and transport coefficients (electrical conductivity σ, thermal conductivity κ, and shear viscosity η) using three effective QCD frameworks: the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the chiral effective model, and the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model. Quarks are treated with effective masses in the NJL and chiral effective models, while hadronic degrees of freedom are employed in the HRG model; transport coefficients are calculated using the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation-time approximation with a constant relaxation time of 1.25 fm assumed throughout the entire density range.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ = 135 MeV and pion decay constant fπ = 92.4 MeV used as vacuum inputs for NJL model parameter fitting
- NJL model parameters: current quark mass mq = 0.0056 GeV, coupling constant G = 7.059 GeV⁻², cutoff Λ = 0.5879 GeV
- Vacuum quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩¹/³ = 263.5 MeV and vacuum constituent quark mass Mq = 313 MeV used in the chiral effective model
- Hadron resonance gas model includes all hadronic states with masses up to 2.6 GeV consisting of u and d quarks
- For massless two-flavor quark matter at zero baryon chemical potential: ε = (7/10)π²T⁴, P = (7/30)π²T⁴, s = (14/15)π²T³, n = (18/π²)ζ(3)T³
- Massless limit values: ε/(nT) = 3.15 and P/(nT) = 1.05
- Constant relaxation time τc = 1.25 fm assumed for η/s calculations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermodynamic and transport properties of QCD matter at zero net baryon density (relevant for RHIC and LHC experiments) to the finite baryon density regime relevant for the upcoming Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR and the Nuclotron-based Ion Collider Facility (NICA) at JINR. The authors build upon prior calculations using the NJL model by Ghosh (2015), Bandyopadhyay (2023), Sasaki (2008), Marty (2013), and Islam (2019), and the chiral effective model approach for transport coefficients introduced by Marattukalam (2024, 2025). The work addresses the regime where lattice QCD calculations are hindered by the numerical sign problem, enabling phenomenological predictions for baryon-rich matter that can guide future experiments at FAIR-CBM and NICA.
