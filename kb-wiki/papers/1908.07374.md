# The Integral Over 2 Spherical Bessel Functions Multiplied by a Gaussian

> In this paper, the integral $\pmatrix{λ_1 &λ_2 &λ_3\cr 0 &0 &0\cr}\, \int_0^\infty \, r^{λ_3+2}\, \exp{(-αr^2)}\, j_{λ_1}(k_1r) \,j_{λ_2}(k_2r) \,dr$, where $k_1$, $k_2$ and $α$ are positive, is evaluated analytically. The result is a finite sum over the modified spherical Bessel function of the first kind. This result will be useful for nuclear scattering calculations, where harmonic oscillator nuclear wavefunctions are used or when evaluating momentum space matrix elements for a Gaussian potential.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytic, finite-sum expression for the integral of two spherical Bessel functions multiplied by a Gaussian weight, where the 3j symbol imposes parity and triangle conditions that reduce the result to a finite sum over modified spherical Bessel functions of the first kind, rather than the infinite hypergeometric series found in standard tables such as Gradshteyn and Ryzhik (1965).

## Method

The author uses the closure relation for spherical Bessel functions to rewrite the desired integral as a double integral over a third momentum variable k3, then inserts an analytic expression for the radial integral of a single spherical Bessel function with a Gaussian from Gradshteyn and Ryzhik (1965). The resulting k3 integral is transformed to an integral over the cosine of the angle between k1 and k2, which is evaluated using the plane-wave expansion of an exponential in Legendre polynomials, yielding a finite sum over modified spherical Bessel functions of the first kind i_l(x) weighted by angular-momentum coupling coefficients (3j and 6j symbols).

## Key numbers

- The main result, equation (TWOPNINE), expresses the integral as a finite sum over l of terms containing the factor i_l(k1 k2 / (2 alpha)), with the prefactor sqrt(pi/2) * i^(lambda1+lambda2-lambda3) * k1^lambda3 / (2 alpha)^(lambda3+3/2) * sqrt(2 lambda3+1) * exp(-(k1^2+k2^2)/(4 alpha)).
- For the special case lambda3 = 0 (so lambda1 = lambda2 = lambda), the result reduces to sqrt(pi) / (4 alpha^(3/2)) * exp(-(k1^2+k2^2)/(4 alpha)) * i_lambda(k1 k2 / (2 alpha)), in agreement with Tabakin and Davies (1966).
- The integral is valid under the conditions |lambda2 - lambda1| <= lambda3 <= lambda1 + lambda2 and lambda1 + lambda2 + lambda3 even, with k1, k2, and alpha positive.

## Context

This work extends earlier analytic results for integrals over three spherical Bessel functions by Mehrem, Londergan, and Macfarlane (1991) and Mehrem and Hohenegger (2010) to the case where one Bessel function is replaced by a Gaussian factor. The result is directly applicable to nuclear scattering calculations using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions, as in the work of Mehrem (1992) and Mehrem, Londergan, and Walker (1993), and to momentum-space matrix elements for Gaussian potentials as studied by Tabakin and Davies (1966) and White, Davies, and Siemens (1988). The analytic form avoids the numerical difficulties of oscillatory integrals and provides physical insight into the angular-momentum structure of the interaction.
