# The probe limit in MHD and its implications for magnetic transport

> Many phenomenological and effective field-theoretical (EFT) applications of magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) in the presence of a background magnetic field employ a simplifying assumption whereby the electromagnetic and the energy-momentum fluctuations decouple. In studies of magnetic transport, for example in magnetic diffusion, the conservation of energy and momentum is then neglected. In this paper, we investigate the details and the consistency of this so-called $\textit{probe limit}$ in different parametric regimes of MHD plasmas. In the first part of the paper, our discussion explores the hydrodynamic (higher-form) theory of MHD. In the second part, we then explicitly test the probe limit by using a microscopic holographic (AdS/CFT) model of a strongly coupled plasma. In the process, we develop the holographic Schwinger-Keldysh EFT prescription for describing the bulk 2-form fields and their dual 1-form symmetries. Moreover, we find evidence of a phase transition at low temperatures and show that magnetic Hall transport can emerge as a consequence of background charge density that breaks the charge conjugation symmetry of the state. Finally, we discuss the implications for magnetic transport, with a particular view towards the dynamics of dense nuclear matter in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates the consistency of the "probe limit" in magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), where electromagnetic and energy-momentum fluctuations are assumed to decouple, and demonstrates that any nontrivial probe limit solution strictly violates the conservation of the energy-momentum tensor, making it an approximation rather than a consistent truncation of the full theory.

## Method

The authors formulate MHD as a hydrodynamic effective field theory of a conserved 2-form current coupled with the energy-momentum tensor, using the higher-form formalism developed by Grozdanov and Poovuttikul (2016). They derive the full set of retarded correlators from the linearized conservation equations in the presence of a background magnetic field, using both a variational approach with external metric and 2-form gauge field perturbations and a canonical approach. The transport coefficients are then calculated in a microscopic holographic (AdS/CFT) model of a strongly coupled plasma, comparing results obtained in the probe limit (freezing metric perturbations) against those from the fully backreacting geometry with a bulk massless 2-form gauge field.

## Context

This work extends the higher-form formulation of MHD developed by Grozdanov and Poovuttikul (2016) and the Schwinger-Keldysh EFT approach to magnetic transport by Vardhan, Grozdanov, and Leutheusser (2022, 2024). It provides a detailed comparison between the full theory of MHD and its probe limit, which has been widely used in phenomenological studies including the seminal work by Goldreich and Reisenegger (1992) on magnetic field evolution in neutron stars. The results enable a more rigorous understanding of when the probe limit approximation is valid for applications to dense nuclear matter in neutron stars, and demonstrate that magnetic Hall transport can emerge from background charge density breaking charge conjugation symmetry, filling a gap in the AdS/CMT literature where this phenomenon had previously been studied only in lower dimensions by Hartnoll and Kovtun (2007).
