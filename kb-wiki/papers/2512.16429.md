# Constraining the nuclear equation of state from terrestrial experiments and neutron star observations using relativistic mean-field models

> We investigate the nuclear equation of state (EoS) for isospin-asymmetric matter using a new set of RMF interactions with the $σ$-$δ$ and $ω$-$ρ$ mixing, referred to as the OMEG family. These interactions are optimized so as to reproduce both terrestrial nuclear measurements and astrophysical constraints extracted from NICER and GW170817. The $σ$-$δ$ mixing softens the nuclear symmetry energy and pressure around twice the saturation density, which enables relatively small neutron-star radii and tidal deformabilities while keeping the nuclear EoS sufficiently stiff at high densities to support $2M_{\odot}$ neutron stars. We find that the curvature parameter, $K_{\textrm{sym}}$, plays an important role in realizing the soft-to-hard behavior of the nuclear EoS, and the astrophysical data favor small or even negative values of $K_{\textrm{sym}}$.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new family of relativistic mean-field (RMF) interactions (the OMEG family) that incorporate σ-δ and ω-ρ meson mixing to simultaneously satisfy terrestrial nuclear data and astrophysical constraints from NICER and GW170817. The key hedge is that while the σ-δ mixing softens the symmetry energy around twice saturation density to yield small neutron-star radii and tidal deformabilities, the curvature parameter K_sym must take small or negative values to realize the required soft-to-hard behavior of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors constructed four RMF interactions (OMEG0–OMEG3) by fitting coupling constants to experimental binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell nuclei, while additionally tuning the isoscalar mixing parameter Λ_σδ to satisfy NICER radius constraints and the GW170817 tidal deformability limit. The Lagrangian includes isoscalar (σ, ω) and isovector (δ, ρ) mesons with nonlinear self-interactions (σ³, σ⁴, ω⁴) and mixing terms (σ²δ² and ω²ρ²). The resulting models were used to compute neutron-star mass-radius relations, tidal deformabilities, pressures in pure neutron matter, and neutron skin thicknesses of ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym(ρ₀) ranges from 30.0 MeV (OMEG3) to 35.1 MeV (OMEG1)
- Slope parameter L: ranges from 20.0 MeV (OMEG3) to 70.0 MeV (OMEG1)
- Curvature parameter K_sym: ranges from −384.4 MeV (OMEG0) to −65.9 MeV (OMEG3)
- Dimensionless tidal deformability at 1.4 M_⊙: Λ₁.₄ ranges from 458 (OMEG2) to 515 (OMEG1), all within the GW170817 constraint of Λ₁.₄ = 190⁺³⁹⁰₋₁₂₀
- PREX-2 neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb: R_skin²⁰⁸ = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- CREX neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca: R_skin⁴⁸ = 0.121 ± 0.026(exp.) ± 0.024(model) fm

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies by Miyatsu (2022, 2023, 2024) that incorporated meson mixing, and addresses the tension between the large neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb reported by the PREX-2 experiment and the small neutron-star radii from NICER. The OMEG family improves upon the FSUGarnet interaction by producing softer symmetry energy at intermediate densities while remaining stiff enough at high densities to support 2 M_⊙ neutron stars. The results enable future combined analyses of terrestrial experiments and multi-messenger astrophysical data, though the authors note that simultaneously reproducing both PREX-2 and CREX measurements remains challenging.
