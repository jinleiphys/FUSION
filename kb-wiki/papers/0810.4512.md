# eta and eta-prime production in nucleon-nucleon collisions near thresholds

> The production of eta and eta-prime mesons in nucleon-nucleon collisions near thresholds is considered within a one-boson exchange model. We show the feasibility of an experimental access to transition formfactors.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that isolating $\eta$ and $\eta'$ contributions in nucleon-nucleon collisions near thresholds, within a one-boson exchange model, allows experimental access to the transition formfactors of these mesons, with the caveat that the model parameters must first be adjusted using the $p+p$ channel before predictions for the $n+p$ channel become available.

## Method

The authors employ a one-boson exchange model to compute total and differential cross sections for $\eta$ and $\eta'$ production in $NN \to NN\,ps$ reactions near thresholds, including subsequent Dalitz decay $ps \to \gamma e^+ e^-$. The model incorporates nucleon currents, spin-1/2 resonances ($S_{11}$, $P_{11}$), and spin-3/2 resonances ($D_{13}$, $P_{13}$) with appropriate interaction Lagrangians, strong formfactors for vertex dressing, and initial state interactions accounted for by effective reduction factors for ${}^3 P_0$ and ${}^1 P_1$ waves. Final state interactions are treated via the Jost function formalism.

## Key numbers

- Initial state interaction reduction factors: $\zeta = 0.277$ for $pp$ and $0.243$ for $np$ and $pp$ channels, applied to ${}^3 P_0$ and ${}^1 P_1$ waves.
- For $\eta$ production in $p+p$, data are reproduced with a concern at excess energy $\Delta s^{1/2} \sim 10$ MeV.
- For $\eta'$, the monopole fit parameterization $F_{\eta' \gamma \gamma^*}(Q^2) = (1-Q^2/\Lambda_{\eta'})^{-1}$ is used, where the VMD parametrization differs only slightly.
- Differential cross sections $d\sigma/d s^{1/2}_{\gamma*}$ are presented for $\eta$ at $T_p = 2.2$ GeV and for $\eta'$ at $T_p = 2.5$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-boson exchange model calculations for $\omega$ and $\phi$ production (Kaptari and Kampfer, 2004-2008) to the $\eta$ and $\eta'$ channels, where earlier model predictions for $\omega$ and $\phi$ were later confirmed by improved data from Ankenew (2007) and Newphi (2008). The results enable experimental tests of the vector meson dominance hypothesis for $\eta'$ transition formfactors at facilities such as HADES and FAIR, and provide a necessary background understanding for studies of chiral symmetry restoration in compressed nuclear matter via di-electron measurements.
