# Applications of Symmetry Breaking in Determining PDFs of the Nucleon

> Studying the possible breaking of various parton model symmetries by the parton distribution functions of the nucleon can provide important information for the non-perturbative structure of hadrons and the strong interaction. We review theoretical calculations for the breaking of flavor symmetry, quark-antiquark symmetry and charge symmetry in the unpolarized and polarized nucleons using the meson cloud model. We report an estimation for the total distribution of strange and antistrange quarks in the nucleon by combining theoretical calculations of SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking with light antiquark distributions obtained from global analysis of available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical calculations of symmetry breaking in nucleon parton distribution functions (PDFs) using the meson cloud model, and estimates the total strange and antistrange quark distribution by combining these calculations with light antiquark distributions from global fits. The authors find that the suppression of the strange sea relative to the non-strange light antiquark sea is not as large as previously believed, but note that their calculated strange distribution becomes negative for x > 0.25, indicating possible overestimation of the SU(3) asymmetry or underestimation of the light antiquark sum in the global fit.

## Method

The authors use the meson cloud model (MCM), in which the nucleon fluctuates into virtual baryon-meson Fock states (such as N pi, N rho, Delta pi, Lambda K, Sigma K, and their K* counterparts), with the partons in these states contributing to the nucleon's PDFs. Fluctuation functions are derived from effective meson-nucleon Lagrangians using time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame, and parton distributions for the baryons and mesons are calculated using the MIT bag model. The total strange and antistrange distribution is obtained by subtracting the MCM-calculated SU(3) flavor asymmetry Delta(x) = dbar(x) + ubar(x) - s(x) - sbar(x) from the global fit result for dbar(x) + ubar(x) from the CTEQ6.6M PDF set.

## Key numbers

- The second moment of the strange-antistrange asymmetry is <x(s - sbar)> = 1.43 x 10^{-4} without K* contributions and -1.35 x 10^{-4} with K* contributions, at Q^2 = 16 GeV^2.
- The strange-antistrange asymmetry in the MCM is an order of magnitude too small to significantly affect the NuTeV result for the weak mixing angle.
- The charge symmetry breaking in valence quarks from the MCM is about 10% of the quark model estimations.
- The ratio R_min = delta d_v / d^p_v and R_maj = delta u_v / u^p_v show no large-x enhancement in the MCM, unlike quark model predictions of up to 10% for R_min as x approaches 1.
- The MCM calculations for x(s + sbar) become negative for x > 0.25.

## Context

This work extends previous MCM studies of flavor asymmetry in the unpolarized nucleon sea (Thomas 1983, Kumano, Signal and Thomas 1998) to the polarized sea, and extends calculations of strange-antistrange asymmetry (Signal and Thomas 1987, Cao and Signal 1999) and charge symmetry breaking (Cao and Signal) using the same framework. The results are compared with HERMES measurements (2004, 2008), NuTeV dimuon data analyzed at NLO (Mason et al.), and global PDF sets CTEQ6.5, CTEQ6.6M, and MSTW2008. The work enables improved constraints on the strange sea distribution, which is important for W production cross-sections at the LHC and for interpreting the NuTeV anomaly in sin^2 theta_W extraction from neutrino deep inelastic scattering.
