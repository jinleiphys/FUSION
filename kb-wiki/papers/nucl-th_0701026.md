# Static properties of nuclear matter within the Boson Loop Expansion

> The use of the Boson Loop Expansion is proposed for investigating the static properties of nuclear matter. We explicitly consider a schematic dynamical model in which nucleons interact with the scalar-isoscalar sigma meson. The suggested approximation scheme is examined in detail at the mean field level and at the one- and two-loop orders. The relevant formulas are provided to derive the binding energy per nucleon, the pressure and the compressibility of nuclear matter. Numerical results of the binding energy at the one-loop order are presented for Walecka's sigma-omega model in order to discuss the degree of convergence of the Boson Loop Expansion.

## Key claim

The Boson Loop Expansion (BLE) is proposed as a non-perturbative, renormalization-preserving alternative to the standard Loop Expansion for calculating static properties of nuclear matter, but the paper only presents numerical results for the binding energy at the one-loop order for the sigma-omega model, and the full two-loop results are deferred to a forthcoming publication.

## Method

The authors integrate out the fermionic (nucleon) degrees of freedom in a path-integral formulation of a Lagrangian containing nucleons and a scalar-isoscalar sigma meson, producing a bosonic effective action. They then apply a semiclassical expansion (stationary phase approximation) around the mean field solution, which corresponds to the Relativistic Hartree Approximation, and evaluate the effective action at the one- and two-loop orders in the boson fields. The formalism is developed for a simple sigma-only model, but numerical results for the binding energy are presented for Walecka's sigma-omega model at the one-loop order.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy per nucleon at the one-loop order for the sigma-omega model is presented numerically (exact value not given in the truncated text; the paper states numerical results are shown in Section 5, which is not included).
* The scalar density at the mean field level is given by: \(\rho_{sM} = \frac{m^*}{\pi^2} \left( k_F E_F^* - (m^*)^2 \log\frac{k_F+E_F^*}{m^*} \right)\), where \(m^* = m - g\bar\sigma\) is the nucleon effective mass.
* The nuclear density is \(\rho = 2 k_F^3 / (3\pi^2)\) for symmetric nuclear matter.
* The omega mean field is \(\bar\omega_\mu = (g_\omega / m_\omega^2) \rho \, \delta_{\mu0}\).

## Context

This work extends the Boson Loop Expansion, previously applied to electromagnetic nuclear response functions (Cenni and Saracco, 1994; Cenni, Co, and Saracco, 1997) and hypernuclear weak decay rates (Alberico et al., 2000), to the static properties of nuclear matter. It directly addresses the failure of the standard Loop Expansion at two-loop order in QHD-I, where Furnstahl, Perry, and Serot (1989) found that two-loop corrections were too attractive and precluded convergence. The paper provides the formalism for a future comprehensive application to QHD-I with systematic two-loop calculations, which will be presented in a forthcoming paper.
