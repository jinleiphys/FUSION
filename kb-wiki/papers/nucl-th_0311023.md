# Unexpected goings-on in the structure of a neutron star crust

> We present a brief account of two phenomena taking place in a neutron star crust: the Fermionic Casimir effect and the major density depletion of the cores of the superfluid neutron vortices.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two previously overlooked quantum phenomena, the Fermionic Casimir effect and significant density depletion in superfluid neutron vortex cores, can substantially alter the structure and dynamics of a neutron star crust, potentially "ruining" the perfect crystalline phases predicted by earlier classical liquid-drop models.

## Method

The authors present a theoretical analysis of two effects in neutron star crusts. First, they derive the interaction energy between two hard spheres immersed in a noninteracting Fermi sea, showing that quantum wave interference produces a non-monotonic Casimir-type interaction. Second, they analyze the spatial structure of superfluid neutron vortices in the BCS-to-BEC crossover regime, where the pairing gap becomes comparable to the Fermi energy, using BCS formalism and variational calculations from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The Fermionic Casimir interaction energy between two hard spheres of radius \(R\) at separation \(r\) is approximately \(E_C \approx -\frac{\hbar^2k_F^2}{m} \frac{R^2}{2\pi r(r-2R)} j_1[2k_F(r-2R)]\), where \(j_1\) is the spherical Bessel function, \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum, and \(m\) is the fermion mass.
- The kinetic energy of two penetrable spheres in a superfluid includes a velocity-dependent cross term that decays as \(1/r^3\), with the renormalized mass given by \(M_i^{ren} = M_i \frac{(1-\gamma)^2}{2\gamma+1}\), where \(\gamma = \rho_{out}/\rho_{in}\) and \(\rho_{in,out}\) are the densities inside and outside the nuclei.
- In the BCS-to-BEC crossover, the Cooper pair coherence length is \(\xi \propto \frac{\hbar^2k_F}{m\Delta}\), which is much larger than the inter-particle separation \(\approx \lambda_F = 2\pi/k_F\) in the weakly coupled BCS limit.
- In the BEC limit, dimers repel each other with an estimated scattering length of approximately \(0.6\) to \(2a\), where \(a\) is the two-body scattering length.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron star crust structure by Negele and Vautherin (1973), Ravenhall, Pethick, and Wilson (1983), and others, which used liquid-drop models to predict a sequence of exotic nuclear shapes (rods, plates, tubes, bubbles). The authors argue that these classical approaches missed the Fermionic Casimir effect, which they previously introduced in Bulgac and Wirzba (2001) and related papers. The vortex density depletion result challenges the long-held belief from de Gennes (1966) and others that vortices in Fermi systems show no appreciable normal density variation. The findings enable more realistic models of neutron star crust dynamics, including thermal and electric conductivities relevant to pulsar glitch mechanisms first proposed by Anderson and Itoh (1975).
