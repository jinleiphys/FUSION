# Solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for a two fermion system

> The method of solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, developed previously for spinless particles, is extended to a system of two fermions. The method is based on the Nakanishi integral representation of the amplitude and on projecting the equation on the light-front plane. The singularities in the projected two-fermion kernel are regularized without modifying the original BS amplitudes. The numerical solutions for the J=0 bound state with the scalar, pseudoscalar and massless vector exchange kernels are found. Binding energies are in close agreement with the Euclidean results. Corresponding amplitudes in Minkowski space are obtained.

## Key claim

The method of solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, previously developed for spinless particles, is extended to a system of two fermions by using the Nakanishi integral representation and light-front projection, but the direct application to fermionic kernels introduces singularities that require regularization via a factor \(\eta(k,p)\) that depends on a free parameter \(L\); the resulting binding energies are independent of \(L\) and agree with Euclidean results.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for a two-fermion system with \(J^\pi = 0^+\) is decomposed into four scalar functions \(\phi_i\) multiplying independent spin structures. Each \(\phi_i\) is represented by a Nakanishi integral over weight functions \(g_i(\gamma', z')\). The equation is projected onto the light-front plane, and the singularities in the resulting kernel are regularized by multiplying both sides by a factor \(\eta(k,p)\) that suppresses high off-mass-shell four-momenta. This yields a family of equivalent two-dimensional integral equations for the weight functions, solved numerically for scalar, pseudoscalar, and massless vector exchange kernels.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy \(B = 2m - M\) for scalar exchange with \(\mu = 0.15\), \(\Lambda = 2\), \(L = 1.1\): \(g^2 = 7.813\) at \(B = 0.01\), \(g^2 = 86.95\) at \(B = 0.50\) (in units where \(m = 1\)).
- Binding energy for pseudoscalar exchange with \(\mu = 0.15\), \(\Lambda = 2\), \(L = 1.1\): \(g^2 = 224.8\) at \(B = 0.01\), \(g^2 = 323.1\) at \(B = 0.50\).
- Binding energy for massless vector exchange (positronium) without vertex form factor (\(\Lambda \to \infty\)): \(g^2 = 3.265\) at \(B = 0.01\), \(g^2 = 36.62\) at \(B = 0.50\).
- Critical coupling constant for positronium without form factor: \(g_c = \pi\); binding energy at \(g \to g_c\) is approximately \(B \approx 0.10\).
- Scalar results agree with Euclidean calculations to four digits; pseudoscalar results show discrepancies of approximately \(0.5\%\).
- Nonrelativistic comparison for positronium at \(B = 0.01\): \(g^2_{NR} = 2.51\) versus \(g^2_{BS} = 3.18\).

## Context

This work extends the method of Carbonell and Karmanov (2005, 2006) from spinless particles to two-fermion systems, building on the Nakanishi integral representation approach of Nakanishi (1969) and the light-front projection technique. It improves upon earlier fermionic treatments by Sauli (2004) that kept only one spin component, and it provides Minkowski-space amplitudes that were previously only accessible via Wick rotation to Euclidean space (Dorkin, 2008). The method enables future calculations of bound-state properties for fermion-antifermion systems such as positronium and can be extended to other kernels and higher angular momenta.
