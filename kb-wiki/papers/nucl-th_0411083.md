# Electromagnetic transitions for A=3 nuclear systems

> Recent advances in the study of pd radiative capture in a wide range of center-of-mass energy below and above deuteron breakup threshold are presented and discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new model for the nuclear electromagnetic current operator that satisfies the current conservation relation (CCR) exactly with the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian, and tests it on pd radiative capture observables; the model improves agreement with data for deuteron tensor polarization observables compared to the previous approach of Viviani (2000), but discrepancies remain for deuteron vector analyzing powers at small center-of-mass angles.

## Method
The authors used pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonics (PHH) wave functions from the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions (AV18/UIX). They constructed two-body currents from the momentum-independent part of AV18 via the meson-exchange (ME) scheme and from the momentum-dependent part via the minimal-substitution (MS) scheme, plus model-dependent (MD) terms from rho-pi-gamma, omega-pi-gamma, and Delta-resonance currents; three-body currents were built within the ME scheme to satisfy the CCR with the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. The full current operator was then used to compute pd radiative capture observables at center-of-mass energies from 2.00 to 9.86 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross section, proton vector analyzing power, and deuteron tensor polarization observables (T20, T21) calculated at E_c.m. = 2.00 MeV and 3.33 MeV, compared with experimental data from Smith (1999) and Goeckner (1992).
- Deuteron vector and tensor polarization observables calculated at E_c.m. = 5.83 MeV, compared with data from Akiyoshi (2001).
- Differential cross section calculated at E_c.m. = 6.60 MeV and 9.86 MeV, compared with data from Belt (1970).
- The energy range studied is E_c.m. = 2.00–9.86 MeV, covering below and above the deuteron breakup threshold.

## Context
This work extends the previous study by Viviani (2000) on pd radiative capture below deuteron breakup threshold, which used only two-body currents within the ME scheme and only approximately satisfied the CCR. The new model achieves exact CCR satisfaction by including the MS scheme for momentum-dependent AV18 terms and adding three-body currents from the Urbana IX interaction. The results enable systematic testing of nuclear electromagnetic current operators against experimental data and motivate further investigation of three-nucleon spin-orbit terms, as suggested by Kievsky (1999), to resolve remaining discrepancies in deuteron vector analyzing powers.
