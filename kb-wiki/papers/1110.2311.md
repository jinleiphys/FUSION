# Relativistic mean field interaction with density dependent meson-nucleon vertices based on microscopical calculations

> Although ab-initio calculations of relativistic Brueckner theory lead to large scalar isovector fields in nuclear matter, at present, successful versions of covariant density functional theory neglect the interactions in this channel. A new high precision density functional DD-ME$δ$ is presented which includes four mesons $σ$, $ω$, $δ$, and $ρ$ with density dependent meson-nucleon couplings. It is based to a large extent on microscopic ab-initio calculations in nuclear matter. Only four of its parameters are determined by adjusting to binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei. The other parameters, in particular the density dependence of the meson-nucleon vertices, are adjusted to non-relativistic and relativistic Brueckner calculations of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. The isovector effective mass $m_{p}^{\ast}-m_{n}^{\ast}$ derived from relativistic Brueckner theory is used to determine the coupling strength of the $δ$-meson and its density dependence.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new relativistic mean-field interaction (DD-MEδ) that includes the scalar-isovector δ-meson, which is usually neglected in successful covariant density functionals. The δ-meson parameters are determined not from finite-nucleus data but from microscopic Brueckner calculations of the isovector effective mass splitting, because including the δ-meson has practically no influence on the quality of fits to finite-nuclei data.

## Method
The authors construct a density-dependent relativistic Hartree Lagrangian with four mesons (σ, ω, δ, ρ) using the Typel-Wolter ansatz for the density dependence of all meson-nucleon vertices. The density dependence of the σ, ω, and ρ vertices is adjusted to non-relativistic and relativistic Brueckner calculations of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, while the δ-meson coupling strength and its density dependence are fixed by reproducing the isovector effective mass splitting m_p* - m_n* from relativistic Brueckner theory. Only four parameters are fitted to binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.152 fm⁻³ is used as the reference density for the Typel-Wolter ansatz (close to the actual fitted saturation density).
- The symmetry energy at saturation J, its slope L, and curvature K_sym are derived from the model (numerical values not given in the truncated text).
- The isovector effective mass splitting m_p* - m_n* from relativistic Brueckner theory is used to determine the δ-meson coupling (exact numerical value not provided in the truncated text).

## Context
This work extends the DD-ME model family (DD-ME1, DD-ME2) developed by the Munich group, which previously included only σ, ω, and ρ mesons with all parameters fitted to finite-nuclei data. It follows the approach of the non-relativistic BCP functional (Baldo, Schuck, Viñas, 2008), which used microscopic Brueckner calculations for the bulk part of the energy functional. The new DD-MEδ interaction enables more reliable predictions for nuclei far from stability with large isospin, relevant for rare isotope beam facilities, and provides a consistent treatment of the scalar isovector channel that affects neutron star properties and heavy-ion reaction dynamics.
