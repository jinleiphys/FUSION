# Nuclear dependence of Q^2 evolution in the structure function F_2

> $Q^2$ evolution of the structure functions $F_2$ in tin and carbon nuclei is investigated in order to understand recent NMC measurements. $F_2$ is evolved by using leading-order DGLAP, next-to-leading-order DGLAP, and parton-recombination equations. NMC experimental result $\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C]/ \partial [\ln Q^2]\ne 0$ could be essentially understood by the difference of parton distributions in the tin and carbon nuclei. However, we find an interesting indication that large higher-twist effects on the $Q^2$ evolution could be ruled out. Nuclear dependence of the $Q^2$ evolution could be interesting for further detailed studies. 1. Introduction 2. Q^2 evolution of F_2 in tin and carbon nuclei 3. Conclusions

## Key claim

The NMC observation that the ratio \(F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C\) has a non-zero derivative with respect to \(\ln Q^2\) can be essentially explained by the difference in parton distributions between tin and carbon nuclei, not by modifications to the evolution equations themselves; however, the authors find that large higher-twist effects from parton recombinations can be ruled out, but this does not invalidate the parton-recombination evolution mechanism because the normalization constant \(K_{HT}\) is not yet theoretically determined.

## Method

The authors used input parton distributions at \(Q_0^2 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\) from a hybrid parton model that includes both parton-recombination and \(Q^2\)-rescaling mechanisms, as described in Kumano (1994). They then evolved these distributions to higher \(Q^2\) using three sets of evolution equations: leading-order (LO) DGLAP, next-to-leading-order (NLO) DGLAP, and DGLAP with parton-recombination (PR) terms. The resulting structure functions \(F_2\) for tin and carbon were used to compute the derivative \(\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2]\) at \(Q^2 = 5\ \text{GeV}^2\) and compared with preliminary NMC data.

## Key numbers

- The QCD scale parameter used is \(\Lambda = 0.2\ \text{GeV}\) with three flavors.
- The input parton distributions are defined at \(Q_0^2 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The derivative \(\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2]\) is calculated at \(Q^2 = 5\ \text{GeV}^2\) for the main comparison; additional calculations are performed at \(Q^2 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(Q^2 = 20\ \text{GeV}^2\) to match the varying average \(Q^2\) of the NMC data across \(x\).
- The parton-recombination parameter \(K_{HT}\) is set to 1.68, chosen so that the higher-dimensional gluon distribution contributes 10% of the standard gluon distribution at the input scale.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the EMC effect and nuclear modifications of structure functions, particularly the NMC measurements of \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) as a function of \(Q^2\) (NMC, 1995). It directly addresses the first experimental indication of nuclear dependence in the \(Q^2\) evolution of \(F_2\), reported by NMC for tin versus carbon. The authors use the parton-model framework of Kumano (1994) and the evolution code of Miyama and Kumano (1994) to show that standard DGLAP evolution with nuclear-modified parton distributions can largely explain the data, while large parton-recombination contributions are disfavored. This enables future work to focus on refining nuclear parton distributions, especially sea-quark shadowing, and on theoretically determining the magnitude of higher-twist recombination effects.
