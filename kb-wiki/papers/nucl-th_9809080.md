# Dynamics of dissipative binary collisions

> In this paper we discuss the reaction mechanisms that occur in the overlap zone for semi-peripheral heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. In particular we focus on the development of neck instabilities, that could determine a possible increase of dynamical fluctuations. As observed in recent experimental data, at beam energies just above 10 MeV/A the most relevant expected consequence is the possibility to obtain large variances in the projectile-like and target-like observables. With increasing beam energy we pass to a mid-rapidity fragment production. In this way we predict a smooth transition from a deep-inelastic to a fragmentation reaction mechanism.

# Dynamics of Dissipative Binary Collisions

## Key claim
The paper predicts a smooth transition from deep-inelastic to fragmentation reaction mechanisms in semi-peripheral heavy ion collisions as beam energy increases, but at 14 MeV/A the dynamical fluctuations from neck instabilities are still small compared to statistical fluctuations from nucleon exchange, contrary to the interpretation of some experimental data.

## Method
The authors performed stochastic mean-field (Boltzmann-Langevin type) simulations of the reaction 100Mo + 120Sn at 14 MeV/A using the TWINGO code with a soft equation of state (compressibility modulus K = 200 MeV). For each impact parameter (b = 8.5, 9, 10 fm), multiple events were generated and analyzed for mass exchange, excitation energy sharing, and variances of projectile-like and target-like fragment observables. Statistical fluctuations were reconstructed by random clustering of test particles from the one-body distribution.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 14 MeV/A for the reaction 100Mo + 120Sn
- Impact parameters studied: b = 8.5, 9, and 10 fm
- At b = 8.5 fm: TKEL bin 369.4–404.3 MeV, average exchanged nucleons = 63.7, dynamical variance of PLF mass = 2.69, dynamical variance of TLF mass = 2.69
- At b = 9 fm: TKEL bin 327.2–367.3 MeV, average exchanged nucleons = 50.4, dynamical variance of PLF mass = 4.49, dynamical variance of TLF mass = 4.71
- At b = 10 fm: TKEL bin 177.9–210.7 MeV, average exchanged nucleons = 22.7, dynamical variance of PLF mass = 1.08, dynamical variance of TLF mass = 1.08
- Statistical variance is given by the average number of exchanged nucleons (e.g., 63.7 at b = 8.5 fm), which is larger than the dynamical variances in all cases
- Instability growth time: approximately 100–200 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the study of deep-inelastic collisions at low energies (below 10 MeV/A) into the intermediate energy regime, where two-body collisions begin to play a role. The authors compare their results to experimental data from Casini (1998) at GSI, which showed correlations between net mass transfer and excitation energy that the authors had attributed to dynamical effects. The paper shows that at 14 MeV/A, the Nucleon Exchange Model (NEM) of Randrup (1979) can explain these correlations through statistical processes, while dynamical instabilities become important only at higher energies (around 25 MeV/A). This work enables predictions for the transition from binary deep-inelastic reactions to multifragmentation mechanisms.
