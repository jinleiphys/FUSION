# Directed flow of neutral strange particles at AGS

> Directed flow of neutral strange particles in heavy ion collisions at AGS is studied in the ART transport model. Using a lambda mean-field potential which is 2/3 of that for a nucleon as predicted by the constituent quark model, lambdas are found to flow with protons but with a smaller flow parameter as observed in experiments. For kaons, their repulsive potential, which is calculated from the impulse approximation using the measured kaon-nucleon scattering length, leads to a smaller anti-flow than that shown in the preliminary E895 data. Implications of this discrepancy are discussed.

# Directed flow of neutral strange particles at AGS

## Key claim
Using the ART transport model with a lambda mean-field potential set to 2/3 of the nucleon potential (as predicted by the constituent quark model) and a kaon repulsive potential from impulse approximation using the empirical kaon-nucleon scattering length, the model reproduces the observed lambda directed flow but produces a kaon anti-flow about five times smaller than the preliminary E895 data, suggesting the need for a different density dependence of the kaon potential at high baryon densities.

## Method
The authors used the A Relativistic Transport (ART) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at a beam momentum of 6 GeV/c per nucleon and an impact parameter of 4 fm. For lambdas, the mean-field potential was taken as 2/3 of the nucleon Skyrme-type potential, using both a soft equation of state (compressibility 200 MeV) and a stiff equation of state (compressibility 380 MeV). For kaons, the mean-field potential was calculated from the impulse approximation using the isospin-averaged kaon-nucleon scattering length of -0.255 fm, and charge-exchange reactions were approximated by including Coulomb interaction for neutral kaons.

## Key numbers
- Lambda mean-field potential set to 2/3 of the nucleon potential, based on constituent quark model and hypernuclei phenomenology.
- Nucleon potential compressibilities used: 200 MeV (soft) and 380 MeV (stiff).
- Statistical errors in ART results: approximately 0.1% for proton flow, 0.3% for lambda flow, and 1% for kaon flow.
- Isospin-averaged kaon-nucleon scattering length: a_KN = -0.255 fm.
- Even with all K^0 treated as K^+ (including Coulomb repulsion), the maximum average p_x/m for kaons is about 0.01c, which is almost five times smaller than the preliminary E895 data.
- Lambda to nucleon ratio in the calculation is about 1%.
- The parameter b_K = 3/(8f_pi^2) = 0.333 GeV/fm^3 for the kaon vector interaction; at SIS energies, a_K was found to be 0.22 GeV^2 fm^3 to reproduce K^+ spectra.
- At normal nuclear matter density, the kaon potential from Eq. (6) is about 30 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of kaon collective flow at SIS/GSI energies by the FOPI collaboration (J. Ritman, 1995; D. Best, 1997) and KaoS collaboration (Shin, 1998), where vanishing kaon flow was consistent with a repulsive kaon potential. It directly addresses preliminary E895 data at AGS energies for neutral strange particle flow. The results contradict the RQMD model (Chung, 1998) which showed positive K^0_S flow due to neglect of the kaon mean-field potential. The discrepancy between the ART model and E895 data for kaon flow motivates further study of kaon in-medium properties at high baryon densities, including possible effects from isotropic three-body phase space in kaon production (C. David, 1999) and different density dependencies of the kaon potential as suggested by various models (D. Kaplan, 1992; H. Politzer, 1994; M. Lutz, 1993).
