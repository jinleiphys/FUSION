# First extraction of valence transversities in a collinear framework

> We present an extraction of the valence transversity parton distributions based on an analysis of pion-pair production in deep-inelastic scattering off transversely polarized targets. Recently released data for proton and deuteron targets at HERMES and COMPASS permit a flavor separation of valence transversities. The present extraction is performed in the framework of collinear factorization, where dihadron fragmentation functions are involved. The latter are taken from a previous analysis of electron-positron annihilation measurements.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first extraction of valence transversity parton distributions for up and down quarks using a collinear factorization framework, rather than the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) framework used in prior extractions. The extraction relies on the condition that dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) are known from a previous analysis of electron-positron annihilation data, and the results are valid only within the assumptions of collinear factorization at leading order.

## Method
The authors analyzed single-spin asymmetries (SSAs) for pion-pair production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) off transversely polarized proton and deuteron targets, using data from HERMES and COMPASS. They combined the SSAs for proton and deuteron targets to perform a flavor separation of the valence transversity distributions for up and down quarks, using the collinear factorization framework where the transversity distribution couples to dihadron fragmentation functions. The fitting procedure was carried out using both a standard Hessian method and a Monte Carlo approach with 100 replicas of the data, imposing Soffer's inequality as a theoretical constraint at the starting scale of Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- HERMES proton data at x = 0.068, Q^2 = 2.214 GeV^2: A_SIDIS = 0.035 ± 0.011, giving h1^uv - h1^dv/4 = 0.167 ± 0.069
- COMPASS proton data at x = 0.1613, Q^2 = 10.72 GeV^2: A_SIDIS = 0.108 ± 0.023, giving h1^uv - h1^dv/4 = 0.42 ± 0.12
- COMPASS deuteron data at x = 0.0163, Q^2 = 1.792 GeV^2: A_SIDIS = 0.028 ± 0.010, giving h1^uv + h1^dv = 0.28 ± 0.11
- COMPASS deuteron data at x = 0.0996, Q^2 = 8.890 GeV^2: A_SIDIS = -0.029 ± 0.019, giving h1^uv + h1^dv = -0.30 ± 0.20
- The integrated polarized DiFF n_u^uparrow for COMPASS proton data at Q^2 = 1.232 GeV^2: -0.183 ± 0.031
- The integrated unpolarized DiFF n_u for COMPASS proton data at Q^2 = 1.232 GeV^2: 0.897

## Context
This work extends the earlier extraction of transversity by the Torino group (Anselmino, 2008), which used TMD factorization and data from HERMES, COMPASS, and Belle, but faced difficulties with TMD evolution equations. The present analysis instead uses collinear factorization with dihadron fragmentation functions, following the theoretical framework developed by Collins (1994), Jaffe (1998), and Radici (2001). The DiFFs were previously extracted from Belle data on electron-positron annihilation by Courtoy (2012). This extraction enables future studies of the tensor charge and provides a benchmark for comparisons with lattice QCD calculations and other phenomenological approaches.
