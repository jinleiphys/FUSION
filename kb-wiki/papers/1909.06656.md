# Reply to E. Shuryak's Comments on "Three regimes of QCD"

> In his recent Comments E. Shuryak reiterates old, unfortunately misleading arguments in favor of deconfined Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) immediately above the chiral restoration pseudocritical temperature. In a Comment devoted to our view of QCD at high temperatures he does not address and even mention the essence of our arguments. In recent years a new "hidden" symmetry in QCD was discovered. It is a symmetry of the electric sector of QCD, that is higher than the chiral symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian as the whole. This symmetry was clearly observed above T_c in spatial correlators and very recently also in time correlators. The latter correlators are directly related to observable spectral density. Then in a model-independent way we conclude that degrees of freedom in QCD above T_c, but below roughly 3T_c, are chirally symmetric quarks bound by the chromoelectric field into color-singlet compounds without the chromomagnetic effects. This regime of QCD has been referred to as a Stringy Fluid since such objects are very reminiscent of strings.At higher temperatures there is a very smooth transition to the partonic degrees of freedom, i.e. to the QGP regime. Here we will address some of the points made by Shuryak.

# Three regimes of QCD: Reply to Shuryak

## Key claim

The author argues that above the chiral restoration pseudocritical temperature \(T_c\) but below roughly \(3T_c\), QCD is not in a deconfined Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) regime but rather in a "Stringy Fluid" regime where chirally symmetric quarks are bound by the chromoelectric field into color-singlet compounds without chromomagnetic effects, contradicting Shuryak's defense of the orthodox view that partonic degrees of freedom appear immediately above \(T_c\).

## Method

The author analyzes lattice QCD results on spatial and time correlators that reveal a hidden chiral-spin symmetry \(SU(2)_{CS}\) and its flavor extension \(SU(2N_F)\) above \(T_c\), which is a symmetry of the electric sector of QCD higher than the chiral symmetry of the full QCD Lagrangian. The author also discusses truncation studies of the Dirac operator where removal of the lowest modes restores both \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\) and \(U(1)_A\) symmetries, and uses symmetry classification of the QCD Lagrangian to conclude that chromoelectric interaction is distributed over all Dirac modes while magnetic interaction is localized in near-zero modes.

## Key numbers

- The Stringy Fluid regime exists in the temperature interval \(T_c - 3T_c\), with a smooth transition to QGP above roughly \(3T_c\).
- Removal of order 10 lowest modes of the Dirac operator from quark propagators restores both \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R\) and \(U(1)_A\) symmetries in the hadron spectrum.
- The chiral-spin and \(SU(4)\) symmetries were observed in spatial correlators using \(N_F=2\) QCD with the chirally-symmetric Domain Wall Dirac operator on JLQCD ensembles in the \(T_c - 3T_c\) interval.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Glozman 2015, 2018) on the discovery of chiral-spin symmetry and its prediction to exist above \(T_c\), and directly contradicts the orthodox view defended by Shuryak that deconfined QGP exists immediately above \(T_c\). The author distinguishes this from earlier work by DeTar and Kogut (1987) on screening masses of spatial correlators, noting that the observed \(SU(2)_{CS}\) and \(SU(4)\) symmetries cannot be obtained via dimensional reduction and are not related to 3d physics. The paper enables future investigation of the smooth transition from condensed strings (Stringy Fluid) to partonic degrees of freedom (QGP) at higher temperatures.
