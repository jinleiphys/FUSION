# Elliptic flow of deuterons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the elliptic flow of deuterons in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV per colliding nucleon-nucleon pair and show that it can be used to discriminate between direct statistical production and coalescence. The emission from the fireball is parametrized and tuned to reproduce transverse momentum spectra and the elliptic flow of protons and pions. Coalescence leads to higher deuteron elliptic flow than statistical production and agrees better with experimental data. We attribute this observation to the varying size of the nucleon-producing region for the emission in different azimuthal angles.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of deuterons can discriminate between direct statistical production and coalescence, with coalescence leading to higher deuteron elliptic flow that agrees better with experimental data, provided the fireball anisotropy is large enough. The authors attribute this to the varying size of the nucleon-producing region for emission in different azimuthal angles, which affects coalescence but not thermal production.

## Method

The authors used a parametrized blast-wave model extended with quadratic freeze-out time dependence on radial coordinate, azimuthal anisotropies in both flow and spatial distribution, and viscous corrections to the momentum distribution. The model was tuned to reproduce transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of protons and pions simultaneously for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon-nucleon pair across multiple centrality classes. Deuterons were then generated either thermally via the Cooper-Frye formula with deuteron mass and degeneracy factors, or by coalescence using a Monte Carlo implementation that requires proton-neutron pairs to satisfy maximum relative distance and relative momentum thresholds in their center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: 2.76 TeV per colliding nucleon-nucleon pair in Pb+Pb collisions
- For coalescence, maximum relative distance Δr ≤ 3.5 fm and maximum relative momentum Δp ≤ 0.26 GeV were used to reproduce deuteron yields
- Freeze-out temperature T = 95 MeV for 0-5% centrality, T = 106 MeV for 30-40% centrality, T = 118 MeV for 50-60% centrality
- Transverse size parameter R0 = 15.0 fm for 0-5% centrality, R0 = 10.0 fm for 30-40% centrality, R0 = 6.0 fm for 50-60% centrality
- Spatial anisotropy parameter a2 = 0.016 for 0-5% centrality, a2 = 0.085 for 30-40% centrality, a2 = 0.15 for 50-60% centrality
- Flow anisotropy parameter ρ2 = 0.008 for 0-5% centrality, ρ2 = 0.03 for 30-40% centrality, ρ2 = 0.02 for 50-60% centrality
- Quadratic freeze-out time parameter s2 = -0.02 fm^{-2} for all centrality classes
- Chemical freeze-out temperature assumed at 156 MeV from Melo (2019)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron elliptic flow from coalescence by Oh (2009), Yin (2017), Zhu (2017), Zhao (2018), and Zhu (2015), but provides the first direct comparison between coalescence and statistical production models for this observable. The approach builds on the quantum-mechanical coalescence formalism of Scheibl (1998) and the Monte Carlo implementation of Sombun (2018). The results enable experimental discrimination between production mechanisms using existing ALICE data from Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, and suggest that measurements in more peripheral collisions (50-60% centrality) would provide even clearer discrimination.
