# Meson and Quark Degrees of Freedom and the Radius of the Deuteron

> The existing experimental data for the deuteron charge radius are discussed. The data of elastic electron scattering are inconsistent with the value obtained in a recent atomic physics experiment. Theoretical predictions based on a nonrelativistic description of the deuteron with realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials and with a rather complete set of meson-exchange contributions to the charge operator are presented. Corrections arising from the quark-gluon substructure of the nucleon are explored in a nonrelativistic quark model; the quark-gluon corrections, not accounted for by meson exchange, are small. Our prediction for the deuteron charge radius favors the value of a recent atomic physics experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the discrepancy between the deuteron charge radius extracted from elastic electron scattering (around 2.115 fm) and from a recent atomic physics experiment (around 2.130 fm) is a real experimental inconsistency of about 1%, and that theoretical predictions including meson-exchange corrections favor the larger atomic physics value, while quark-gluon substructure corrections are too small to resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors calculate the deuteron charge radius using nonrelativistic wave functions from six realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Reid soft-core, Paris, and four Bonn potentials), incorporating a complete set of meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions to the charge operator from π, η, ρ, ω, σ, and δ mesons, as well as the ρπγ process. They then explore quark-gluon corrections using a nonrelativistic six-quark Resonating Group Method (RGM) model with a chiral-invariant quark Hamiltonian including confinement, gluon, π, and σ exchange, calculating quark-exchange currents (QEC) and comparing two models (A and B) that differ in their treatment of σ-exchange-induced nonlocal attraction.

## Key numbers

- The atomic physics experiment (Schmidt-Kaler et al., 1993) gives a deuteron charge radius of 2.1303(66) fm, while the elastic electron scattering experiments yield values around 2.115-2.119 fm (Simon et al., 1981) and 2.095-2.125 fm (Berard et al., 1973; Akimov et al., 1979), showing a ~1% discrepancy.
- The meson-exchange correction Δr_MEC ranges from 0.0042 fm (Paris potential) to 0.0057 fm (RSC potential) across the six potentials, with the dominant contribution from the π-pair term (0.0036-0.0050 fm).
- The final theoretical charge radii including MEC are: Paris 2.1372 fm, Bonn Q 2.1349 fm, Bonn A 2.1355 fm, Bonn B 2.1355 fm, Bonn C 2.1345 fm, all favoring the atomic physics value.
- The matter radius r_m derived from the experimental triplet scattering length a_t = 5.419(7) fm via empirical linear relations is 1.969(4) fm, in conflict with the value ~1.950 fm extracted from electron scattering analyses.
- The Darwin-Foldy correction r²_DF = 0.0331 fm² and the spin-orbit correction r²_SO = -0.0015 fm² (for Paris potential with D-state probability P_D = 5.77%) are used.

## Context

This work extends previous meson-exchange calculations by Kohler (1983) that used only pion-range currents, finding that including heavier mesons increases the MEC correction by about 15%. It addresses speculations by Sprung (1990), Kermode (1990), and Wong (1992) that quark-gluon effects might explain the discrepancy between electron scattering and atomic physics data; the authors find such effects are small. The results support the atomic physics experiment of Schmidt-Kaler (1993) and the analysis of Marrs (1995), and suggest that the discrepancy between electron scattering data and theory remains unresolved, requiring further experimental clarification.
