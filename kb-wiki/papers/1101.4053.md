# Three-nucleon force in relativistic three-nucleon Faddeev calculations

> We extend our formulation of relativistic three-nucleon Faddeev equations to include both pairwise interactions and a three-nucleon force. Exact Poincare invariance is realized by adding interactions to the mass Casimir operator (rest Hamiltonian) of the non-interacting system without changing the spin Casimir operator. This is achieved by using interactions defined by rotationally invariant kernels that are functions of internal momentum variables and single-particle spins that undergo identical Wigner rotations. To solve the resulting equations one needs matrix elements of the three-nucleon force with these properties in a momentum-space partial-wave basis. We present two methods to calculate matrix elements of three-nucleon forces with these properties. For a number of examples we show that at higher energies, where effects of relativity and of three-nucleon forces are non-negligible, a consistent treatment of both is required to properly analyze the data.

## Key claim

The paper extends relativistic three-nucleon Faddeev equations to include both pairwise interactions and a three-nucleon force, showing that at higher energies where both relativity and three-nucleon force effects are non-negligible, a consistent treatment of both is required to properly analyze the data, rather than treating either effect in isolation.

## Method

The authors construct a Poincare-invariant three-nucleon mass operator by adding interactions to the mass Casimir operator without changing the spin Casimir operator, using interactions defined by rotationally invariant kernels that are functions of internal momentum variables and single-particle spins that undergo identical Wigner rotations. They present two methods to calculate matrix elements of three-nucleon forces in a momentum-space partial-wave basis, and solve the resulting relativistic Faddeev equations for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering and breakup. The relativistic two-body interactions are obtained from nonrelativistic high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials by solving a nonlinear integral equation that yields phase-equivalent relativistic potentials, avoiding refitting to nucleon-nucleon data.

## Key numbers

- Nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18, CDBonn, Nijm I, II, and 93) underestimate experimental bindings of 3H and 3He by about 0.5-1 MeV when used in three-nucleon calculations
- Elastic nucleon-deuteron angular distribution discrepancies at energies up to approximately 100 MeV nucleon lab energy can be removed by adding a modern three-nucleon force to the nuclear Hamiltonian
- At energies higher than approximately 100 MeV, current three-nucleon forces only partially improve the description of cross section data
- For incoming nucleon laboratory energies below approximately 250 MeV, all partial wave states with total angular momenta of the two-nucleon subsystem up to j_max=5 and all total angular momenta of the three-nucleon system up to J=25/2 must be taken into account, leading to a system of up to 143 coupled integral equations for a given J and parity
- For nucleon-deuteron breakup, large effects due to relativity and three-nucleon forces have been reported for an incoming deuteron energy of 270 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic three-nucleon Faddeev calculations (Witala et al., 2005, 2006) that included only pairwise interactions, by adding a three-nucleon force. It builds on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) for Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics and Coester's formulation (Coester, 1992) of relativistic few-body dynamics. The paper addresses the need identified in earlier studies (Witala et al., 1999, 2001) where nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations with only nucleon-nucleon forces were insufficient to describe neutron-deuteron total cross section data above approximately 100 MeV, and where relativistic effects were found to be comparable to three-nucleon force effects. This work enables consistent relativistic calculations that include both effects simultaneously, which is necessary for analyzing data in regimes where both relativity and three-nucleon forces are significant.
