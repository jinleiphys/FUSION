# Bethe-Salpeter bound-state structure in Minkowski space

> The quantitative investigation of the scalar Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space, within the ladder-approximation framework, is extended to include the excited states. This study has been carried out for an interacting system composed by two massive bosons exchanging a massive scalar, by adopting (i) the Nakanishi integral representation of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and (ii) the formally exact projection onto the null plane. Our analysis, on one hand, confirms the reliability of the method already applied to the ground state and, on the other one, extends the investigation from the valence distribution in momentum space to the corresponding quantity in the impact-parameter space, pointing out some relevant features, like (i) the equivalence between Minkowski and Euclidean transverse-momentum amplitudes, and (ii) the leading exponential fall-off of the valence wave function in the impact-parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Nakanishi integral representation (NIR) method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in Minkowski space from the ground state to excited states, within the ladder approximation for two massive bosons exchanging a massive scalar. The authors claim that the method yields reliable results for excited states, but note that for exchanged-scalar masses below 0.05 times the constituent mass or binding energies below 0.01 times the constituent mass, convergence is slow and extrapolation is needed.

## Method

The authors solve the scalar Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation of the BS amplitude, projecting the equation onto the null plane to obtain a generalized eigenvalue problem for the Nakanishi weight function. The weight function is expanded in Laguerre polynomials for the gamma-dependence (up to 26 terms for excited states) and even Gegenbauer polynomials for the z-dependence (up to 10 terms). The coupling constant alpha is treated as the eigenvalue while the bound-state mass M is a parameter, and excited states are found by searching for masses above the ground-state mass that yield eigenvalue lambda=1.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy fixed at B(0)/m = 1.9 for all comparisons
- For mu/m = 0.05 and alpha_gr = 6.324: first excited state B(1)/m = 0.259 (Minkowski) vs 0.258 (Euclidean); second excited state B(2)/m = 0.090 (both spaces)
- For mu/m = 0.1 and alpha_gr = 6.437: first excited state B(1)/m = 0.221 (Minkowski) vs 0.220 (Euclidean); second excited state B(2)/m = 0.050 (Minkowski) vs 0.051 (Euclidean)
- For mu/m = 0.5 and alpha_gr = 8.047: first excited state B(1)/m = 0.0082 (both spaces)
- Asymptotic behavior of valence LF wave function: psi(xi, k_perp) ~ k_perp^{-4} for large transverse momentum
- Ground state convergence achieved with 14 Laguerre and 10 Gegenbauer polynomials; excited states required 26 Laguerre and 10 Gegenbauer polynomials

## Context

This work extends the NIR-based approach previously applied only to the ground state by Frederico, Salme, and Viviani (2014) to include excited states of the scalar BSE in ladder approximation. The results are compared with Euclidean-space calculations that do not assume the NIR, showing agreement that validates the NIR method. The study enables future application to fermionic systems, kernels beyond ladder approximation, and actual physical systems such as hadrons, where the valence wave function in momentum and impact-parameter spaces is relevant for parton distributions and generalized parton distributions.
