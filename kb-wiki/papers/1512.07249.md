# Charge transport and vector meson dissociation across the thermal phase transition in lattice QCD with two light quark flavors

> We compute and analyze correlation functions in the isovector vector channel at vanishing spatial momentum across the deconfinement phase transition in lattice QCD. The simulations are carried out at temperatures $T/T_c=0.156, 0.8, 1.0, 1.25$ and $1.67$ with $T_c\simeq203$MeV for two flavors of Wilson-Clover fermions with a zero-temperature pion mass of $\simeq270$MeV. Exploiting exact sum rules and applying a phenomenologically motivated ansatz allows us to determine the spectral function $ρ(ω,T)$ via a fit to the lattice correlation function data. From these results we estimate the electrical conductivity across the deconfinement phase transition via a Kubo formula and find evidence for the dissociation of the $ρ$ meson by resolving its spectral weight at the available temperatures. We also apply the Backus-Gilbert method as a model-independent approach to this problem. At any given frequency, it yields a local weighted average of the true spectral function. We use this method to compare kinetic theory predictions and previously published phenomenological spectral functions to our lattice study.

## Key claim

The paper finds evidence for the dissociation of the ρ meson across the deconfinement phase transition and estimates the electrical conductivity via a Kubo formula, but these results depend on a phenomenologically motivated ansatz for the spectral function fitted to lattice correlation function data, and the authors note that the Backus-Gilbert method provides a complementary model-independent comparison.

## Method

The authors compute Euclidean-time correlation functions in the isovector vector channel at vanishing spatial momentum using lattice QCD with two flavors of Wilson-Clover fermions at a zero-temperature pion mass of approximately 270 MeV. They apply exact sum rules and a phenomenologically motivated ansatz for the spectral function to fit the lattice correlation function data, and also employ the Backus-Gilbert method to obtain a local weighted average of the true spectral function without assuming a specific parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures studied: T/T_c = 0.156, 0.8, 1.0, 1.25, and 1.67, with T_c approximately 203 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0486(4)(5) fm
- Zero-temperature pion mass: approximately 270 MeV
- Vector renormalization constant: Z_V = 0.768(5)
- Area under the ρ resonance peak in the vacuum spectral function up to 1 GeV: 0.114 GeV^2
- HRG estimates for χ_s ⟨v^2⟩: 0.00236 GeV^2 at T=100 MeV, 0.0042 GeV^2 at T=120 MeV, 0.0069 GeV^2 at T=140 MeV, 0.0111 GeV^2 at T=160 MeV
- Temperatures in MeV: T=169(3) MeV (N_τ=24), T=203(4) MeV (N_τ=20), T=254(4) MeV (N_τ=16), T=338(5) MeV (N_τ=12)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation at T/T_c=1.25 (Brandt 2012) to a wider temperature range across the deconfinement phase transition, using the same fixed-scale approach with improved statistics. The results enable comparison with hadron resonance gas model predictions and with phenomenological spectral functions (Hohler 2013), and provide input for dilepton production rates in heavy-ion collisions and for understanding charge transport in the quark-gluon plasma.
