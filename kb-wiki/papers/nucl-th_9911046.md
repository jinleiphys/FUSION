# Quark mean field model for nuclear matter and finite nuclei

> We study nuclear matter and finite nuclei in terms of the quark mean field (QMF) model, in which we describe the nucleon using the constituent quark model. The meson mean fields, in particular the sigma meson, created by other nucleons act on quarks inside a nucleon and change the nucleon properties in nuclear medium. The QMF model predicts an increasing size of the nucleon as well as a reduction of the nucleon mass in the nuclear environment. The present model is applied to study the properties of finite nuclei after fixing all the parameters by the nuclear matter properties, and it is found to give satisfactory results on the nuclear properties.

# Quark mean field model for nuclear matter and finite nuclei

## Key claim

The quark mean field (QMF) model, which describes the nucleon using constituent quarks rather than the MIT bag model, predicts that nucleon properties change in nuclear medium, specifically an increasing nucleon size and a reduction of nucleon mass, and that these changes, when applied to finite nuclei, yield results close to experimental values, though the quality of agreement depends on the Lorentz structure chosen for the confinement potential (scalar versus scalar-vector) and the oscillator strength.

## Method

The authors construct a two-step model: first, they solve the Dirac equation for constituent quarks inside a nucleon under the influence of constant meson mean fields (sigma, omega, rho), using a harmonic oscillator confining potential with either scalar or scalar-vector Lorentz structure; second, they embed the resulting density-dependent effective nucleon mass into a relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian for the nuclear many-body system, solving the Euler-Lagrange equations self-consistently for nuclear matter and for spherically symmetric finite nuclei using a local density approximation. The five free parameters (quark-meson couplings and nonlinear meson self-coupling constants) are fixed by reproducing five equilibrium properties of nuclear matter at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- The constituent quark mass is taken as one third of the free nucleon mass: m_q = M_n/3 = 313 MeV.
- The free nucleon mass is fixed at M_n = 939 MeV.
- The oscillator strength for the confining potential is taken as k = 700 and 1000 MeV/fm^2 for two test cases.
- Nuclear matter saturation density is set at rho_0 = 0.145 fm^{-3}.
- The omega meson mass is m_omega = 783 MeV; the rho meson mass is m_rho = 770 MeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density, the nucleon radius increases by about 5% to 9% relative to free space, depending on the confinement parameters and potential type.
- The charge radius of 40Ca is reproduced to be around 3.45 fm by tuning the sigma meson mass.
- For the scalar-vector confinement potential, the calculated binding energies per nucleon and charge radii for finite nuclei are almost the same as the RMF(TM1) results; for the scalar potential, binding energies are somewhat underestimated.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) and Thomas and collaborators, replacing the MIT bag model description of the nucleon with the constituent quark model, which avoids conceptual problems such as having non-perturbative mesons inside a perturbative bag vacuum or negative quark masses. The model connects to the one-boson exchange model (Bonn potential) and is compared with the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and the RMF(TM1) parameter set of Sugahara and Toki (1994). The predicted swelling of the nucleon radius (5-9% at saturation) is significantly larger than in the original QMC model, and the authors suggest this could be relevant for calculating the EMC effect.
