# Tidal deformability and compactness of neutron stars and massive pulsars from semi-microscopic equations of state

> Tidal deformability measures how NS can comfortably deform as a response to an applied tidal field. We use updated constraints on the mass, radius, and tidal deformability of neutron star (NS) objects and pulsars to examine nuclear equations of state (EOS) based on realistic finite-range M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction, which have been successfully used to describe low- and high-dense nuclear matter (NM). We then employ these EOSs to examine the impact of tidal deformability and compactness of NSs on their structure. We found that the EOSs from CDM3Y-230 to CDM3Y-330 characterized with the saturation incompressibility ${K}_{0}=230-330$ MeV together yield more limited ranges of tidal deformability and radius for NS objects than their experimentally inferred ranges. For light NS ($M<\text{M}_{\odot}$), both ${k}_{2}$ and $\mathcal{C}$ decreases upon decreasing the NS mass, which enhances its tidal deformability. The stiffness of the NS core matter has shown a minor effect on the tidal deformability of such NS ($M<\text{M}_{\odot}$). An opposite behavior is obtained as an increase in the tidal Love number but a decrease in the more effective compactness of NS ($M>\text{M}_{\odot}$), upon increasing (decreasing) the stiffness of the employed EOS (its mass). This appears as enhanced tidal deformability indicated at a larger radius for NS of stiffer NM and for the lighter NS above $\text{M}_{\odot}$. Unified description of some correlations between tidal deformability, tidal Love number, and NS compactness is provided independent of the details of the considered EOS.

# Tidal deformability and compactness of neutron stars and massive pulsars from semi-microscopic equations of state

## Key claim
The CDM3Y equations of state with saturation incompressibility K0 = 230–330 MeV together yield more limited ranges of tidal deformability and radius for neutron star objects than their experimentally inferred ranges, but the authors qualify that only the EOSs from CDM3Y-230 to CDM3Y-270 successfully reproduce most of the mass-radius constraints from 15 neutron star objects and pulsars, while softer EOSs (K0 ≤ 240 MeV) do not support neutron stars heavier than 2 solar masses.

## Method
The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the differential equation for the tidal Love number using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta technique, employing semi-microscopic equations of state based on the finite-range M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction in its CDM3Y density-dependent form. The equations of state were derived from non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations for beta-stable npeμ matter, with the outer crust described by the Haensel-Zdunik EOS and the inner crust by the Douchin-Haensel EOS. The tidal deformability and compactness were computed for neutron stars with masses ranging from light objects below one solar mass up to the maximum mass for each EOS, using CDM3Y-Paris and CDM3Y-Reid parameterizations with saturation incompressibility values from 200 MeV to 330 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the dimensionless tidal deformability increases from 32 (98) to 413 (428) when increasing the saturation incompressibility of the CDM3Y-Paris (Reid) EOS from K0 = 200 MeV to 330 MeV.
- The CDM3Y-Paris EOSs with K0 = 217–257 MeV yield tidal deformability values of 142–348 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, while the BDM3Y-Paris EOS with K0 = 270 MeV gives 407.
- For a neutron star of 1.4 solar masses, the central isospin-asymmetry increases from 0.35 to about 0.84 with increasing K0 from 200 MeV to 330 MeV for the CDM3Y-Paris EOS.
- The density-slope of the symmetry energy at the central isospin-asymmetry density decreases from 44.71 MeV to 35.68 MeV for the CDM3Y-Paris EOS when K0 increases from 200 MeV to 330 MeV for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.
- A decrease in neutron star mass of about 0.5 solar masses below the maximum mass yields an increase in radius of about 1.4 km for the EOS with K0 = 300 MeV and about 2.4 km for the EOS with K0 = 200 MeV.
- The symmetry energy coefficient at saturation density is 29.10 MeV (30.98 MeV) and the density-slope of the symmetry energy is 48.18 MeV (50.81 MeV) for the CDM3Y-Paris (Reid) EOSs.

## Context
This work extends previous studies using CDM3Y-Paris EOSs with K0 = 217–257 MeV and BDM3Y EOSs (Tan and Khoa, 2021) to a wider spectrum of saturation incompressibility from extremely soft (200 MeV) to extremely stiff (330 MeV) limits. The results are compared with constraints from the GW170817 and GW190425 gravitational wave events analyzed by Abbott et al. (2019) and Landry et al. (2020), as well as with NICER observations of pulsars PSR J0030+0451, J0740+6620, J0437+4715, and J1231+1411 reported by Riley et al. (2021), Miller et al. (2019, 2021), and Salmi et al. (2024). The findings enable unified description of correlations between tidal deformability, tidal Love number, and neutron star compactness independent of the details of the considered equation of state, and raise questions about the possible existence of hybrid neutron stars with phase transitions to quark matter or hyperons.
