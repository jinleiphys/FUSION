# Quantifying properties of hot and dense QCD matter through systematic model-to-data comparison

> We systematically compare an event-by-event heavy-ion collision model to data from the Large Hadron Collider. Using a general Bayesian method, we probe multiple model parameters including fundamental quark-gluon plasma properties such as the specific shear viscosity $η/s$, calibrate the model to optimally reproduce experimental data, and extract quantitative constraints for all parameters simultaneously. The method is universal and easily extensible to other data and collision models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bayesian parameter estimation with Gaussian process emulators can simultaneously constrain multiple heavy-ion collision model parameters, including the specific shear viscosity η/s, from LHC data, but the extracted constraints depend on the choice of initial condition model (Glauber vs. KLN) and subjective weighting of observables.

## Method

The authors constructed a full event-by-event heavy-ion collision model chain consisting of Monte Carlo Glauber or KLN initial conditions, VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics, a Cooper-Frye particlization sampler, and the UrQMD hadronic afterburner. They evaluated the model at 254 Latin hypercube design points spanning five input parameters (normalization, initial condition parameter, thermalization time τ₀, specific shear viscosity η/s, and shear relaxation time coefficient k_π), then built Gaussian process emulators on the principal components of the model outputs (charged-particle multiplicity, elliptic flow v₂{2}, and triangular flow v₃{2}) to act as fast surrogates. Bayesian calibration via Markov chain Monte Carlo was used to extract posterior probability distributions for all parameters simultaneously by comparing emulator predictions to ALICE Pb-Pb data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The input parameter ranges were: τ₀ from 0.2 to 1.0 fm, η/s from 0 to 0.3, and k_π from 0.2 to 1.1; Glauber normalization 20–60, Glauber α 0.05–0.30; KLN normalization 5–15, KLN λ 0.1–0.3.
- The Latin hypercube design used 256 points (reduced to 254 after discarding two non-physical points) across 5 input parameters, with approximately 10³ events per design point and 10⁷ events total.
- The emulator used q = 5 principal components, retaining over 99% of the variance from the original 18-dimensional output space.
- A separate 64-point validation design confirmed emulator accuracy, with most uncertainty attributed to statistical noise in the flow cumulants, especially v₃{2}.
- Subjective weight factors of 1.2 : 1.0 : 0.6 were applied to ⟨N_ch⟩ : v₂{2} : v₃{2} respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier Bayesian calibration efforts by Novak et al. (2013) and Pratt et al. (2014, 2015) by applying the methodology to a full event-by-event model rather than event-averaged calculations, enabling study of flow fluctuations. It also generalizes previous η/s constraints from Luzum (2008), Song (2010), Schenke (2010), and Luzum (2012), which used manual tuning of η/s alone to match flow coefficients, by simultaneously constraining multiple parameters including initial condition parameters and thermalization time. The framework enables future systematic comparisons between different initial condition models and can be extended to additional observables and collision systems.
