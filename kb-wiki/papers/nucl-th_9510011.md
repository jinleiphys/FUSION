# Hot nuclear matter with dilatons

> We study hot nuclear matter in a model based on nucleon interactions deriving from the exchange of scalar and vector mesons. The main new feature of our work is the treatment of the scale breaking of quantum chromodynamics through the introduction of a dilaton field. Although the dilaton effects are quite small quantitatively, they affect the high-temperature phase transition appreciably. We find that inclusion of the dilaton leads to a metastable high-density state at zero pressure, similar to that found by Glendenning who considered instead the admixture of higher baryon resonances.

# Hot nuclear matter with dilatons

## Key claim

The inclusion of a dilaton field to represent QCD scale-breaking in a relativistic mean-field model of hot nuclear matter produces only small quantitative effects overall, but it appreciably alters the high-temperature phase transition and leads to a metastable high-density state at zero pressure, similar to that found by Glendenning who instead considered admixture of higher baryon resonances.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian with nucleons coupled to scalar and vector mesons, and introduce a dilaton field with a logarithmic potential to mimic the QCD trace anomaly. They solve the mean-field equations for infinite nuclear matter at finite temperature, computing the scalar and vector densities, effective nucleon mass, energy density, and pressure as functions of chemical potential and temperature. Phase transitions are identified by comparing pressures (grand potentials) of different branches at given chemical potential and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.153$ fm$^{-3}$
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: $E_B = -16.5$ MeV
- Compressibility without dilaton: $K = 212$ MeV; with dilaton: $K = 234$ MeV
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: $M^* = 0.57 M$
- Scalar meson mass: $m_s = 492$ MeV; vector meson mass: $m_\omega = 795$ MeV
- Scalar coupling without dilaton: $g_s = 10.1$; with dilaton: $g_s = 9.6$
- Vector coupling: $g_\omega = 13.3$
- Dilaton "bag" constant: $B = (200\ \mathrm{MeV})^4$
- Phase boundary intercepts the $T$ axis at $T \simeq 185$ MeV (with dilaton) and $T \simeq 189$ MeV (without dilaton)
- Critical point with dilaton: $(T, \mu) \simeq (170\ \mathrm{MeV}, 565\ \mathrm{MeV})$
- Critical point without dilaton: $(T, \mu) \simeq (185\ \mathrm{MeV}, 300\ \mathrm{MeV})$
- Dilaton field drops by approximately 10% in the abnormal state

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean-field treatments of hot nuclear matter (Walecka 1974, Serot and Walecka 1986, and others) by incorporating broken scale invariance of QCD via a dilaton field, following the approach of Schechter (1980) and the zero-temperature work of Rusnak and Furnstahl (1995) and Mishustin, Bondorf, and Rho (1993). The metastable zero-pressure state found here parallels the result of Glendenning (1989) who obtained similar behavior by including higher baryon resonances rather than the dilaton. The results enable future studies of baryons immersed in hot hadronic matter, particularly in skyrmion approaches (Mishustin 1991, Eisenberg and Kalbermann 1995), where dilaton effects may prove important.
