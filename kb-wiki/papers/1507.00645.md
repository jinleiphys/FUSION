# The Skyrme Tensor Force in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Background: [elided for arXiv character count] Purpose: With the advancement of computational power it is now possible to undertake TDHF calculations without any symmetry assumptions and incorporate the major strides made by the nuclear structure community in improving the energy density functionals used in these calculations. In particular, time-odd and tensor terms in these functionals are naturally present during the dynamical evolution, while being absent or minimally important for most static calculations. The parameters of these terms are determined by the requirement of Galilean invariance or local gauge invariance but their significance for the reaction dynamics have not been fully studied. This work addresses this question with emphasis on the tensor force. Method: The full version of the Skyrme force, including terms arising only from the Skyrme tensor force, is applied to the study of collisions within a completely symmetry-unrestricted TDHF implementation. Results: We examine the effect on fusion thresholds with and without the tensor force terms and find an effect on the fusion threshold energy of the order several MeV. Details of the distribution of the energy within terms in the energy density functional is also discussed. Conclusions: Terms in the energy density functional linked to the tensor force can play a non-negligible role in dynamic processes in nuclei, including those in which the terms do not affect ground state properties.

# The Skyrme Tensor Force in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The tensor terms of the Skyrme energy density functional, which have negligible effect on static ground-state properties of doubly-magic nuclei like 16O, can shift the upper fusion threshold in 16O+16O collisions by several MeV, demonstrating that these terms play a non-negligible role in dynamical processes even when they do not affect ground-state observables.

## Method
The authors performed fully symmetry-unrestricted time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the Sky3D code, implementing the complete Skyrme energy density functional including all time-odd and tensor terms. They used the SLy5 force as a baseline, with tensor terms added perturbatively (SLy5t), and also tested the TIJ family of parameter sets in which isoscalar and isovector tensor strengths are systematically varied. The study focused on head-on and off-axis collisions of 16O on 16O, comparing fusion thresholds and energy contributions from different terms in the functional.

## Key numbers
- Upper fusion threshold for SLy5 (full): 68 MeV; for SLy5t (with tensor): 65 MeV, a reduction of approximately 5%
- Upper fusion threshold for SkM* (basic): 77 MeV; SkM* (including J² terms): 71 MeV; SkM* (full with time-odd terms): 73 MeV
- Upper fusion thresholds for TIJ parameter sets range from 61 MeV (T12) to 87 MeV (T46), a variation of about 25 MeV
- In 100 MeV deep-inelastic collisions, the s·F term (activated only by tensor parameters) contributes a few hundred keV during the collision
- For off-axis collisions at 34 MeV center-of-mass energy and impact parameter b = 6.65 fm, the J² terms contribute up to several MeV of energy during the collision, with sign depending on the tensor parameter set

## Context
This work extends previous TDHF studies of fusion and dissipation in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the analysis of time-odd terms by Umar and collaborators (2006) and the inclusion of spin-orbit effects by Reinhard and Umar (1989) and Umar and Strayer (1989). It builds on the comprehensive treatment of the Skyrme tensor force in nuclear structure by Lesinski et al. (2007) and the perturbative tensor addition to SLy5 by Colò et al. (2007). The results show that tensor terms, which have been studied in giant resonances by Fracasso and collaborators, can significantly affect fusion dynamics even in systems where they leave ground states unchanged, opening the possibility of constraining tensor parameters using reaction data rather than only structure data.
