# Modification of Z Boson Properties in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We calculate the change in the effective mass and width of a Z boson in the environment of a quark-gluon plasma under the conditions expected in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The change in width is predicted to be only about 1 MeV at a temperature of 1 GeV, compared to the natural width of 2490$\pm$7 MeV. The mass shift is even smaller. Hence no observable effects are to be expected.

## Key claim

The change in the Z boson width due to immersion in a quark-gluon plasma at temperatures achievable in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC is predicted to be only about 1 MeV at a temperature of 1 GeV, making the effect unobservable in dimuon invariant mass spectra despite the Z boson's lifetime matching the plasma thermalization time.

## Method

The authors computed the finite-temperature self-energy of the Z boson in a quark-gluon plasma using one-loop and two-loop diagrams in the limit that the Z moves non-relativistically relative to the plasma rest frame. The dominant contribution to the imaginary part of the self-energy came from two-loop diagrams involving gluon exchange, which were evaluated by cutting the diagrams to obtain scattering amplitudes for processes such as Compton scattering (q+Z → q+g), gluon fusion (g+Z → q+anti-q), and three-body decay (Z → q+anti-q+g), including interference terms. Thermal distributions of quarks and gluons were included, and an infrared resummation scheme was used to handle infrared divergences, with the cutoff interpreted as the effective thermal mass of quarks in the plasma.

## Key numbers

- Natural width of Z boson in vacuum: Γ_vac = 2.490 ± 0.007 GeV
- Z boson mass: m_Z = 91.153 ± 0.007 GeV
- Predicted thermal contribution to Z width at T = 1 GeV: Γ_T ≈ 1.5 MeV (using α_s ≈ 1/4 at T = 1 GeV)
- Mass shift from one-loop self-energy: Δm_Z² = -1.56 × 10⁻⁴ T⁴_GeV (with T in GeV)
- Thermalization time for central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC: t₀ = 0.07 fm/c
- Initial temperature estimate for central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC: T₀ = 1 GeV
- Z boson creation time: 1/m_Z ≈ 0.002 fm/c
- Z boson vacuum lifetime: 0.08 fm/c
- Hadronization temperature: T_c ≈ 160 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of finite-temperature effects on electroweak bosons in heavy-ion collisions, specifically applying techniques from thermal field theory (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990) to the Z boson case. The calculation shows that unlike the photon or dilepton probes that have been studied for quark-gluon plasma characterization, the Z boson's properties are too weakly modified to serve as a practical probe of the initial plasma temperature at the LHC. The result enables future work to focus on other observables for studying the early-stage quark-gluon plasma, as the Z boson's potential as a "thermometer" is ruled out by this calculation.
