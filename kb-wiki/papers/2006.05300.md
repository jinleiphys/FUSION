# Two-current correlations in the pion in the Nambu and Jona-Lasinio model

> We present an analysis of two-current correlations for the pion in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, with Pauli--Villars regularization. We provide explicit expressions in momentum space for two-current correlations corresponding to the zeroth component of the vector Dirac bilinear in the quark vertices, which has been evaluated on the lattice. The numerical results show a remarkable qualitative agreement with recent lattice data. The factorization approximation into one-body currents is discussed.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, with Pauli-Villars regularization, yields two-current correlation functions for the pion that show "remarkable qualitative agreement" with recent lattice data, though the agreement is described as "encouraging" and "tentative" rather than quantitative, and the comparison is limited to the time-time component of the vector current.

## Method

The authors compute two-current correlation functions for the pion in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model using Pauli-Villars regularization, evaluating the leading-order contributions from rhombus and trapezoid Feynman diagrams as well as a pole contribution from vertex dressing. The calculation is performed in momentum space for the zeroth component of the vector Dirac bilinear, with the results Fourier-transformed to coordinate space for comparison with lattice data from Bali (2018). The isospin decomposition follows the treatment of Bali (2018), yielding the functions F0, F1, and F2.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters are adjusted to produce pion masses of approximately 300 MeV for comparison with lattice data (the specific mass value is given as "m_pi = 300 MeV NJL-Latt" in figure labels)
- The integrals I_V0^r(m,q) and I_V0^t(m,q) are evaluated with regularization coefficients c_i for i=0,1,2, where the quark mass m and regulator masses M_i are specified in the appendix
- The pole contribution X_V0^p(q) involves the factor [m_pi^2 I_3(m_pi^2) - I_2(0)] / [m_pi^2 I_2(m_pi^2) + 4m^2 I_2(0)] where m is the quark mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NJL calculation of pion double parton distributions (Courtoy, Noguera, Scopetta, 2019) to two-current correlations that can be directly compared with lattice data from Bali (2018). The study enables testing the factorization approximation into one-body currents, which has been used for double parton distributions following arguments by Diehl (2003) and Burkardt (1994). The results complement lattice QCD evaluations of pion structure and provide a field-theoretical benchmark for approximations commonly used in phenomenological analyses of double parton scattering.
