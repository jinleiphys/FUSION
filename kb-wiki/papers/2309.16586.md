# Signatures of $α$-clustering in $^{12}$C and $^{13}$C

> We study the cluster structure of $^{12}$C and $^{13}$C in the framework of the cluster shell model. Simple relations are derived for ratios of longitudinal form factors as well as transition probabilities. It is shown that the available experimental data for $^{12}$C and $^{13}$C can be well described by a triangular structure with ${\cal D}_{3h}$ and ${\cal D}'_{3h}$ symmetry, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the available experimental data for \(^{12}\)C and \(^{13}\)C can be well described by a triangular structure with \(\mathcal{D}_{3h}\) and \(\mathcal{D}'_{3h}\) symmetry, respectively, but the analysis uses a single value of the cluster size parameter \(\beta\) for all states, whereas the authors note that a state-dependent \(\beta\) (e.g., 10% larger for the \(3^-\) state) would improve agreement with experimental \(B(E3)\) values.

## Method

The authors employ the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) for \(^{12}\)C and the Cluster Shell Model (CSM) for \(^{13}\)C, treating \(^{13}\)C as three \(\alpha\)-particles in a triangular configuration plus an extra neutron moving in the deformed field of the cluster. They derive explicit relations between longitudinal (Coulomb) form factors and transition probabilities in both nuclei, comparing the collective (cluster) contribution to the single-particle contribution, and fit the parameters \(\alpha\) (Gaussian width) and \(\beta\) (cluster radius) to elastic form factor data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C: \(\alpha = 0.56\) 1/fm\(^2\) and \(\beta = 1.74\) fm from elastic form factors.
- For \(^{13}\)C: \(\alpha = 0.58\) 1/fm\(^2\) and \(\beta = 1.71\) fm from elastic form factors.
- \(B(E2;2^+,0 \rightarrow 0^+,0)\) in \(^{12}\)C: theory 6.6 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\), experiment \(7.63 \pm 0.19\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(E3;3^-,3 \rightarrow 0^+,0)\) in \(^{12}\)C: theory 49.7 \(e^2\)fm\(^6\), experiment \(103 \pm 17\) \(e^2\)fm\(^6\).
- \(B(E2;\frac{3}{2}^-,\frac{1}{2} \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}^-,\frac{1}{2})\) in \(^{13}\)C: theory 6.1 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\), experiment \(6.4 \pm 1.5\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(E2;\frac{5}{2}^-,\frac{1}{2} \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}^-,\frac{1}{2})\) in \(^{13}\)C: theory 6.1 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\), experiment \(5.6 \pm 0.4\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- \(B(E3;\frac{5}{2}^+,-\frac{5}{2} \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}^-,\frac{1}{2})\) in \(^{13}\)C: theory 44.7 \(e^2\)fm\(^6\), experiment \(100 \pm 40\) \(e^2\)fm\(^6\).
- Quadrupole moment \(Q(2^+,0)\) in \(^{12}\)C: theory 5.2 \(e\)fm\(^2\), experiment \(5.97 \pm 0.30\) \(e\)fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM) for \(^{12}\)C, previously developed by the same group (Bijker and Iachello, 2000-2014), to the neighboring odd nucleus \(^{13}\)C using the Cluster Shell Model (CSM) introduced by the authors. The study provides explicit relations between form factors in the two nuclei analogous to the Alaga rules (Alaga, 1955) in the collective geometric model, and suggests that the \(\frac{1}{2}^-_2\) state at 8.86 MeV in \(^{13}\)C may be the analog of the Hoyle state in \(^{12}\)C, consistent with earlier energy systematics (Bijker, 2015) and charge radius analyses (Demyanova, 2015). The results enable future searches for cluster structure in neighboring nuclei and help identify analogs of the Hoyle state in \(^{13}\)C.
