# Solving the puzzle of nuclear stopping power

> This paper reviews our recent findings on the dynamics of transport of baryon number in proton-induced reactions at the CERN SPS. These are put in a more general context of the present understanding of baryon stopping phenomena up to LHC and cosmic ray energies. The implications of our studies, advantages to be provided by modern and high quality experimental data, and perspectives of new measurements are shortly discussed.

# Solving the puzzle of nuclear stopping power

## Key claim

The puzzle of nuclear stopping power, the paradox that the Dual Parton Model predicted far weaker baryon stopping in proton-nucleus collisions than experimental data indicated, has been solved by incorporating a rigorous treatment of color quantum number and a complete description of the proton's Fock state structure, including sea partons. The key elements are color octet and decuplet exchanges that become available in multiple proton-nucleon collisions, which the authors argue were not accessible in earlier model formulations.

## Method

The authors analyzed NA49 experimental data on net proton and net neutron rapidity distributions in proton-proton and proton-carbon collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV, specifically selecting events where the projectile proton collides with more than one target nucleon (average number of collisions about 2.6). They compared these data to simulations based on the Dual Parton Model (DPM) of Capella and Tran Thanh Van, and then developed the Gluon Exchange Model (GEM) which introduces event classes defined by the number of color octets (gluons) exchanged between projectile and target constituents, including color decuplet exchange in multiple collisions.

## Key numbers

- The average number of proton-nucleon collisions in the "multiple collision" pC sample is about 2.6.
- The analysis is performed at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV (CERN SPS energy).
- The DPM simulation scaled by 0.6 gives the upper limit for the contribution of the diquark-preserving mechanism to the total net baryon spectrum in multiple-collision pC reactions.
- For the "multiple" part of pC reactions, the lower limit for diquark disintegration (event classes 3 and 4) varies from one third to one half depending on the model applied.

## Context

This work extends and resolves a long-standing controversy between the nuclear stopping power extracted by Busza and Goldhaber from FNAL experimental data and the model simulations by Jezabek and Rozanska (1990s), which showed that the original Dual Parton Model predicted far weaker stopping. The authors build on the earlier bremsstrahlung analogy by Stodolsky and the work of Bialas and Stodolsky on pA collisions, as well as the Wounded Quark Model of Bialas and Czyz and the Wounded Constituent Model of Bialas, Bzdak, and collaborators. The new experimental data from NA49 (2010 and 2013) provide simultaneous proton and neutron spectra, which the authors argue was essential to resolve the puzzle. The work enables improved phenomenological models for baryon transport in soft hadron-nucleus collisions relevant to cosmic ray interactions and heavy-ion physics.
