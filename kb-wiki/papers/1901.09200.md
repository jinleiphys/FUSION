# Transverse momentum distributions of charmonium states with the statistical hadronization model

> Calculations and predictions are presented within the framework of the statistical hadronization model for transverse momentum spectra of the charmonium states J/$ψ$, $ψ(2S)$ and $X(3872)$ produced in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies. The results are confronted with available data and exhibit very good agreement by using particle flow profiles from state-of-the-art hydrodynamic calculations. For $X(3872)$ production in Pb-Pb collisions we predict a transverse momentum distribution similar in shape to that for J/$ψ$ with a strong enhancement at low transverse momenta and a production yield of about 1\% relative to that for J/$ψ$.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model for charm quarks (SHMC) can describe the transverse momentum spectra of J/ψ, ψ(2S), and X(3872) in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies, but the agreement with data for J/ψ at forward rapidity holds only for pT below about 4.5 GeV, with the model falling below data at higher transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors compute charmonium yields using the statistical hadronization model with charm quark fugacity fixed by the measured charm cross section, then generate transverse momentum spectra by convolving the thermal yields with a blast-wave function whose flow velocity profile is taken from (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulations (MUSIC with IP-Glasma initial conditions). A core-corona approach separates the thermal QGP contribution from a scaled proton-proton component, where the pp spectra for J/ψ and ψ(2S) are taken from ALICE measurements at √sNN = 5 TeV and for X(3872) from CMS data at √s = 7 TeV scaled to 5 TeV.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ transverse momentum spectrum in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5 TeV at forward rapidity shows good agreement with ALICE data for pT below approximately 4.5 GeV, but the model falls below data for pT ≳ 4.5 GeV
- The predicted production yield of X(3872) relative to J/ψ in Pb-Pb collisions is about 1%
- For X(3872) in Kr-Kr collisions at √sNN = 5 TeV, yields are a factor of 4-5 lower than for Pb-Pb collisions for pT ≲ 5 GeV
- The branching ratio BR(X(3872) → J/ψ π+ π-) is constrained between 3.2% and about 20%, with results shown assuming 10%

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model for charm (SHMC), previously applied successfully to charmonium yields by Andronic et al. (2006, 2007, 2018), to differential transverse momentum spectra. It builds on the observation that charm quarks thermalize in the quark-gluon plasma and hadronize at the QCD phase boundary, with flow profiles from state-of-the-art hydrodynamic calculations (MUSIC framework by Schenke et al., 2010, 2012). The predictions for ψ(2S) and X(3872) enable future tests with LHC Run 3 and Run 4 data, and the discrepancy at high pT for J/ψ suggests additional production mechanisms such as gluon fragmentation in jets beyond the regeneration process.
