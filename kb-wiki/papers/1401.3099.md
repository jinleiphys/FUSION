# A non-perturbative effect of gluons for scalar diquark in the Schwinger-Dyson formalism

> The diquark has been considered to be important effective degrees of freedom in hadron physics, especially for multi-quark physics and the structure of heavy hadronic states. Using the Schwinger-Dyson formalism, we investigate the non-perturbative effect of gluons for scalar diquarks with renormalization-group improved coupling in the Landau gauge. Here, we treat the scalar diquark as an effective degree of freedom with a peculiar size, while the diquark is originally a bound-state-like object of two quarks. Since the diquark has a non-zero color charge, it strongly interacts with gluons. We evaluate the gluonic non-perturbative effect to the diquark, considering the size effect of the diquark. We investigate the mass function of the diquark in both cases with a constant bare diquark mass and twice of the running quark self-energy. It is found that the diquark, especially the small diquark, obtains a large effective mass by the gluonic dressing effect. The scalar diquark mass seems to be dynamically generated by the non-perturbative effect, although it does not have the chiral symmetry.

## Key claim

The scalar diquark, despite lacking chiral symmetry, can acquire a large effective mass through non-perturbative gluonic dressing effects, with the magnitude of this mass generation depending critically on the assumed diquark size (the inverse of a momentum cutoff introduced to model the diquark's finite spatial extent).

## Method

The authors solved the Schwinger-Dyson (SD) equation for a scalar diquark field in the Landau gauge, using the rainbow-ladder truncation with the Higashijima-Miransky approximation and a renormalization-group-improved running coupling. A simple form factor \( f_{\Lambda}(p_E^2) = (\Lambda^2/(p_E^2+\Lambda^2))^2 \) was introduced at the diquark-gluon vertex to model the diquark's finite size \( R = \Lambda^{-1} \). The SD equation was solved numerically for three choices of the bare diquark mass: a constant 600 MeV, twice the running quark self-energy from a separate quark SD equation, and zero.

## Key numbers

*   The infrared regularization of the running coupling uses a cutoff at \( p_{\rm IR} \simeq 640 \) MeV, with the QCD scale parameter \( \Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 500 \) MeV.
*   For the constant bare mass case (\( m_{\phi}=600 \) MeV), the diquark self-energy \( \Sigma(p_E^2) \) is always larger than the bare mass and strongly depends on the size: a "compact diquark" with \( R \simeq 0.3 \) fm (cutoff \( \Lambda=600 \) MeV) obtains a large effective mass, while a larger diquark with \( R=1 \) fm (\( \Lambda=200 \) MeV) shows a smaller dressing.
*   In the running bare mass case, for \( R=1 \) fm the gluonic effect is small, with \( \Sigma(p_E^2) \approx 2\Sigma_q(p_E^2) \); for \( R \simeq 0.3 \) fm the self-energy remains large at high momentum even as the bare mass goes to zero.
*   For the zero bare-mass case (\( m_{\phi} \equiv 0 \)), the self-energy \( \Sigma(p_E^2) \) is always finite and takes a large value for both size cases (\( R=1 \) fm and \( R \simeq 0.3 \) fm).

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Dyson formalism, previously applied to quarks (Higashijima, 1984; Aoki, 1990; Yamanaka, 2013), to the scalar diquark as an effective degree of freedom, motivated by the need to understand multi-quark and exotic hadron states discovered in recent experiments (Beringer, 2012). The study introduces a size-dependent form factor to account for the diquark's bound-state nature, building on earlier work on diquark-gluon interactions (Iida, 2007). The finding of dynamical mass generation without chiral symmetry is contextualized as a general property of strongly interacting theories, analogous to the non-perturbative mass generation seen for gluons (Iritani, 2009) and heavy quarks. This enables future work on color-singlet states such as tetraquarks, where the dressed diquark mass would be a key input.
