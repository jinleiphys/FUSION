# The Nuclear Contact Formalism - the Deuteron Channel

> The contact formalism, devised to elucidate the important role of short-range correlations, was recently generalized for systems with coupled channels, such as the deuteron channel, where s and d waves are coupled into a $J=1$ state. The coupling of the two channels implies two independent asymptotic solutions and results a 2x2 contact matrix. For a strong coupling, with appropriate boundary conditions, such as in nuclear physics, the two solutions degenerate into a single asymptotic wave function. Here, we explore the asymptotic behavior of the correlated neutron-proton pairs in the deuteron channel using both schematic models and realistic nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent contradiction between the coupled-channels contact formalism (which predicts a 2x2 contact matrix requiring two independent asymptotic functions) and the observed universal deuteron-like behavior of nuclear short-range correlations is resolved by imposing a boundary condition (such as a hard wall or mean-field potential) that, in the strong-coupling limit characteristic of nuclear physics, collapses the two solutions into a single asymptotic wave function proportional to the deuteron bound state.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Schrödinger equation for the s-wave and d-wave channels of a neutron-proton pair in a J=1 state, using both a schematic Gaussian "toy model" potential with variable s-d coupling strength and realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (AV18 and chiral EFT N3LO(600)). For the toy model, they imposed a hard-wall spherical box boundary condition at radius R and extracted the ratio η = c₂/c₁ for the first two energy levels as a function of coupling strength S. For the realistic potentials, they solved the two-body problem in an external harmonic oscillator potential (ħω_HO = 0.1 MeV) to simulate the mean field inside a nucleus, then compared the momentum distributions of the first 10 energy levels to the deuteron bound-state momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- The high-momentum tail of nuclear momentum distributions begins at k > k_F ≈ 1.26 fm⁻¹ (from the introduction, citing prior work)
- For the toy model, calculations used scattering length a_s = 10 fm and Λ² = 3 fm⁻², with box radii R = 15, 25, and 35 fm
- The ratio |η₁(S)-η₂(S)|/|η₁(S)+η₂(S)| approaches 1 in the non-coupling limit S→0 and approaches 0 as coupling S becomes strong (S=2 shown in figures)
- For realistic potentials with HO confinement, the first 10 levels span an energy range of -2.2 MeV ≤ E_l ≤ 20 MeV
- At k ≈ 4 fm⁻¹, the s-wave and d-wave contributions to the momentum distribution n(k) are roughly equal
- For strong coupling (S=2), the densities of the two lowest energy states coincide up to almost r = 0.8 fm; for weak coupling (S=0.2), they diverge starting around r ≈ 0.3 fm

## Context

This work extends the contact formalism developed by Tan (2008) for zero-range interactions and generalized to nuclear systems by Weiss, Bazak, Barnea (2015-2017) and others, specifically addressing the coupled-channels generalization introduced by Weiss and Barnea (2017). The paper resolves a tension between the coupled-channels contact theory (which predicts a 2x2 contact matrix with two independent asymptotic functions) and the well-established experimental and theoretical observation that nuclear short-range correlations are dominated by the single deuteron bound-state wave function, as shown in experiments by Piasetzky (2006), Subedi (2008), and Hen (2014), and in calculations by Wiringa, Schiavilla, and Pieper (2014). The results enable the continued use of the single-deuteron approximation for describing short-range correlations in finite nuclei while providing a theoretical justification for why the contact matrix collapses to a single contact in the strong-coupling regime relevant to nuclear physics.
