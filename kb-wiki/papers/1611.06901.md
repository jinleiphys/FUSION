# Gluon momentum fraction of the nucleon from lattice QCD

> We perform a direct calculation of the gluon momentum fraction of the nucleon using maximally twisted mass fermion ensembles with $N_f=2+1+1$ flavors at a pion mass of about $370\,\mathrm{MeV}$ and a lattice spacing of $a\approx 0.082\,\mathrm{fm}$ and with $N_f=2$ flavors at the physical pion mass and a lattice spacing of $a\approx 0.093\,\mathrm{fm}$. In the definition of the gluon operator we employ stout smearing to obtain a statistically significant result for the bare matrix elements. In addition, we perform a lattice perturbative calculation including 2 levels of stout smearing to carry out the mixing and the renormalization of the quark and gluon operators. We find, after conversion to the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme at a scale of $2\,\mathrm{GeV}$: $\langle x\rangle^R_g {=} 0.284(23)(23)$ for pion mass of about $370\,\mathrm{MeV}$ and $\langle x\rangle^R_g {=} 0.283(23)(15)$ for the physical pion mass.

## Key claim

The authors perform the first direct lattice QCD calculation of the gluon momentum fraction of the nucleon with dynamical quarks, finding values around 0.28-0.29 after renormalization, but the result depends on a one-loop perturbative renormalization procedure that includes mixing with the quark singlet operator and uses stout smearing to achieve a statistically significant signal.

## Method

The gluon momentum fraction was extracted from the ratio of a three-point to a two-point nucleon correlation function at zero momentum, using the operator O_B constructed from plaquette terms with stout smearing applied to improve the signal-to-noise ratio. The calculation used maximally twisted mass fermion ensembles: an N_f=2+1+1 ensemble at a pion mass of about 370 MeV with 34470 measurements, and an N_f=2 ensemble at the physical pion mass with 209400 measurements. Renormalization was performed using a one-loop lattice perturbative calculation that included two levels of stout smearing to determine the mixing coefficients between the gluon and quark singlet operators.

## Key numbers

- For the B55.32 ensemble (pion mass ~370 MeV, lattice spacing a≈0.082 fm): ⟨x⟩_g^R = 0.284(27)(17)(24) in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV, where the first parenthesis is statistical, the second is systematic from excited states, and the third is systematic from renormalization
- For the cA2.09.48 ensemble (physical pion mass ~130 MeV, lattice spacing a≈0.093 fm): ⟨x⟩_g^R = 0.267(22)(19)(24) in the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV
- Bare results before renormalization: ⟨x⟩_g^bare = 0.290(27)(17) for B55.32 and 0.311(22)(20) for cA2.09.48
- The B55.32 ensemble used 2298 configurations on a 32^3 × 64 lattice with β=1.95; the cA2.09.48 ensemble used 2094 configurations on a 48^3 × 96 lattice with β=2.1

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice calculations of the gluon momentum fraction by Gockeler (1996), Horsley (2012), Liu (2012), and Deka (2013) to include dynamical quarks for the first time. The results are consistent with phenomenological parton distribution function analyses by Alekhin (2013) that suggest gluons carry a significant fraction of the nucleon momentum, though with larger uncertainties. The method enables future non-perturbative renormalization and application to other gluonic observables such as the gluon contribution to the nucleon spin.
