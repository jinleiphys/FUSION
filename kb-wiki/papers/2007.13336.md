# Simulating hadron test beams in liquid argon

> Thorough modeling of the physics involved in liquid argon calorimetry is essential for accurately predicting the performance of DUNE and optimizing its design and analysis pipeline. At the fundamental level, it is essential to quantify the detector response to individual hadrons---protons, charged pions, and neutrons---at different injection energies. We report such a simulation, analyzed under different assumptions about event reconstruction, such as particle identification and neutron detection. The role of event containment is also quantified. The results of this simulation can help inform the ProtoDUNE test-beam data analysis, while also providing a framework for assessing the impact of various cross section uncertainties.

# Simulating hadron test beams in liquid argon

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates through simulation that the energy resolution for hadrons in liquid argon detectors depends critically on particle identification (PID) capabilities and neutron detection efficiency, with the resolution following an approximate \(E^{-1/2}\) scaling at high energies but improving at low energies where hadronic interactions become less frequent.

## Method

The authors used FLUKA (version 2011.2x.6) to simulate individual protons, charged pions, and neutrons injected into an unlimited liquid argon volume at energies from 0.01 to 3.0 GeV, generating \(10^4\) events per energy value. They analyzed the simulated events under three reconstruction scenarios: (1) full PID information with low detection thresholds, (2) charge-only reconstruction with no PID, and (3) charge-only reconstruction with all neutron energy considered lost. They also studied the effects of finite detector volume using cubic volumes of 2 m, 3 m, and 5 m side length for 2 GeV and 7 GeV proton beams.

## Key numbers

- Protons: fractional energy resolution of \(42\%/\sqrt{E}\) for charge-only method and \(25\%/\sqrt{E}\) for best-reconstruction method at high energies
- Charged pions: fractional energy resolution of \(42\%/\sqrt{E}\) for charge-only method and \(21\%/\sqrt{E}\) for best-reconstruction method at high energies
- Neutrons: fractional energy resolution of \(40\%/\sqrt{E}\) for charge-only method and \(23\%/\sqrt{E}\) for best-reconstruction method at high energies
- For a 300 MeV neutron, on average 34% of energy goes into knockout protons above 30 MeV threshold, 4% into subthreshold protons, 40% to nuclear breakup, 14% into gammas, 4% into heavy ions, 2% to nuclear recoil, and 2% into pions
- For 2 GeV protons in a 2 m cubic volume, about 22% of ionization charge is created outside the volume; for a 3 m cube, 8% is outside
- Mean free path for hadronic interactions is approximately 80 cm
- For 1 GeV neutrons, 19% of energy goes into pions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous full-event simulation study (Friedland 2018) by deconstructing the detector response to individual hadron types, providing "virtual test-beam data" that can be compared with actual ProtoDUNE test-beam data (Abi 2020). The results are intended to inform ProtoDUNE data analysis and provide a framework for assessing cross section uncertainties. The study also supports the FastMC approach used in DUNE CDR documents (Acciarri 2015, Acciarri 2016) and can help validate the simulation pipeline for full neutrino events. The authors note that ArgoNeuT (Acciarri 2018) has demonstrated detection of nuclear de-excitation charge "blips" and MicroBooNE (MicroBooNE 2018) has detected \(^{39}\)Ar beta decay products, supporting the feasibility of neutron detection.
