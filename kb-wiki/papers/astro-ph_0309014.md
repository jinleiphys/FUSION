# Structure of pair winds from compact objects with application to emission from hot bare strange stars

> We consider a stationary, spherically outflowing wind consisting of electron-positron pairs and photons. We do not assume thermal equilibrium, and include the two-body processes that occur in such a wind: Moller and Bhaba scattering of pairs, Compton scattering, two-photon pair annihilation, and two-photon pair production, together with their radiative three-body variants: bremsstrahlung, double Compton scattering, and three-photon pair annihilation, with their inverse processes. In the concrete example described here, the wind injection source is a hot, bare, strange star. Such stars are thought to be powerful sources of pairs created by the Coulomb barrier at the quark surface. We present a new, finite-difference scheme for solving the relativistic kinetic Boltzmann equations for pairs and photons. Using this method we study the kinetics of the wind particles and the emerging emission for total luminosities of L=10^{34}-10^{42} ergs/s. We find the rates of particle number and energy outflows, outflow velocities, number densities, energy spectra, and other parameters for both photons and pairs as functions of the distance. We find that for L>2x10^{35} ergs/s, photons dominate the emerging emission. As L increases from ~ 10^{34} to 10^{42} ergs/s, the mean energy of emergent photons decreases from ~400-500 keV to 40 keV, as the spectrum changes in shape from that of a wide annihilation line to nearly a blackbody spectrum with a high energy (> 100 keV) tail. These results are pertinent to the deduction of the outside appearance of hot bare strange stars, which might help discern them from neutron stars.

# Structure of pair winds from compact objects with application to emission from hot bare strange stars

## Key claim

For a stationary, spherically outflowing electron-positron pair wind from a hot bare strange star with total luminosities L = 10^34–10^42 ergs/s, the emerging emission transitions from pair-dominated to photon-dominated above L ≈ 2×10^35 ergs/s, and the mean photon energy decreases from ~400–500 keV to ~40 keV as luminosity increases, but these results are limited by computational constraints at the upper end of the luminosity range and assume no magnetic field and negligible gravity.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic kinetic Boltzmann equations for electron-positron pairs and photons using a new finite-difference conservative scheme on a phase-space grid (8 energy bins, 8 angle bins, 100 radial shells) with Gear's implicit method for stiff equations. They included two-body processes (Møller and Bhaba scattering, Compton scattering, two-photon pair annihilation, two-photon pair production) and their radiative three-body variants (bremsstrahlung, double Compton scattering, three-photon pair annihilation) with inverse processes, without assuming thermal equilibrium. The wind was injected from a spherical surface at r = 10^6 cm with a thermal pair spectrum at the strange star surface temperature, and the system was evolved until steady state for each luminosity from 10^34 to 10^42 ergs/s in decade steps.

## Key numbers

- The transition luminosity where photons begin to dominate the emerging emission is L_eq ≈ 2×10^35 ergs/s; for L < L_eq pairs dominate, for L > L_eq photons dominate.
- The mean energy of emergent photons decreases from ~400–500 keV at L ~ 10^34 ergs/s to ~40 keV at L = 10^42 ergs/s.
- The emerging pair number rate is bounded: dot N_e ≲ dot N_e^max ≈ 10^43 s^-1.
- The emerging pair luminosity is bounded: L_e^max ≈ 2×10^37 ergs/s (including rest energies).
- The pair wind becomes optically thick [tau_gamma(R) > 1] for dot E > 10^37 ergs/s; the photosphere radius varies from ~R at 10^37 ergs/s to ~10R ≈ 10^7 cm at 10^42 ergs/s.
- For dot E = 10^42 ergs/s, the bulk outflow velocity of the pair plasma decreases to ~2×10^7 cm/s near the star.
- The annihilation length near the surface is l_ann ≈ 10^-2 (dot E / 10^42 ergs/s)^-1 cm.
- The Eddington luminosity for the pair plasma is ~7×10^34 (M/M_sun) ergs/s.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pair plasmas in thermal equilibrium (Bisnovatyi-Kogan, Zeldovich, & Sunyaev 1971; Lightman 1981; Svensson 1982) and relativistic hydrodynamic treatments of very powerful pair winds (Paczyński 1986, 1990; Goodman 1986) into the non-equilibrium regime at lower luminosities (dot E < 10^42 ergs/s) where thermalization times exceed escape times. The results enable predictions for the observational appearance of hot bare strange stars, which may help distinguish them from neutron stars, and follow from earlier work on pair creation at the quark surface of strange stars (Usov 1998, 2001a) and cooling calculations (Page & Usov 2002). A brief account of the emerging emission was previously given by Aksenov, Milgrom, & Usov (2003).
