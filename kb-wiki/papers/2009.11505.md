# Proximity effect of pair correlation in the inner crust of neutron stars

> We study proximity effect of pair correlation in the inner crust of neutron stars by means of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory formulated in the coordinate space. We describe a system composed of a nuclear cluster immersed in neutron superfluid, which is confined in a spherical box. Using a density-dependent effective pairing interaction which reproduces both the pair gap of neutron matter obtained in ab initio calculations and that of finite nuclei, we analyze how the pair condensate in neutron superfluid is affected by the presence of the nuclear cluster. It is found that the proximity effect is characterized by the coherence length of neutron superfluid measured from the edge position of the nuclear cluster. The calculation predicts that the proximity effect has a strong density dependence. In the middle layers of the inner crust with baryon density $5 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3} < ρ_b < 2\times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-3}$, the proximity effect is well limited in the vicinity of the nuclear cluster, i.e. in a sufficiently smaller area than the Wigner-Seitz cell. On the contrary, the proximity effect is predicted to extend to the whole volume of the Wigner-Seitz cell in shallow layers of the inner crust with $ρ_b < 2 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$, and in deep layers with $ρ_b > 5 \times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The proximity effect of pair correlation in the inner crust of neutron stars is characterized by the coherence length of neutron superfluid measured from the edge position of the nuclear cluster, and its spatial extent depends strongly on the baryon density of the surrounding neutron superfluid, with the effect being limited to a small region near the cluster in middle layers but extending to the whole Wigner-Seitz cell in shallow and deep layers.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in a spherical coordinate space with a large box (100-200 fm) to reduce finite-size effects, placing a nuclear cluster with proton number Z=28 at the center immersed in neutron superfluid. They used three density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) parameter sets (DDDI-b, DDDI-a1, DDDI-a2) that reproduce both ab initio pairing gaps of neutron matter and experimental pairing gaps in finite nuclei, with the Skyrme parameter set SLy4 for the Hartree-Fock potential. The range of the proximity effect was quantified by comparing the neutron pair density profile to the uniform-BCS result and identifying the distance from the nuclear cluster edge where convergence occurs.

## Key numbers

- The proximity effect is limited to a region smaller than the Wigner-Seitz cell in middle layers with baryon density $5 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3} < \rho_b < 2\times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-3}$
- The proximity effect extends to the whole Wigner-Seitz cell in shallow layers with $\rho_b < 2 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$ and in deep layers with $\rho_b > 5 \times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-3}$
- The minimum coherence length values are: $\xi\sim 3.6$ fm for DDDI-b at $\lambda_n \approx 5$ MeV, $\xi\sim 4.6$ fm for DDDI-a1 at $\lambda_n \approx 6$ MeV, and $\xi\sim 6.1$ fm for DDDI-a2 at $\lambda_n \approx 5$ MeV
- For very low density $\rho_n\sim 10^{-5}-10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$, the coherence length ranges from $\xi=5-20$ fm for DDDI-b, $\xi=10-36$ fm for DDDI-a1, and $\xi=11-37$ fm for DDDI-a2
- The average neutron pairing gap in $^{120}$Sn calculated with DDDI-a1 and DDDI-a2 is 1.28 MeV, reproducing the experimental gap of approximately 1.3 MeV, while DDDI-b gives 0.48 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous qualitative discussions of the proximity effect in inner crust matter by Chamel (2010), Sandulescu (2004), and Barranco (1998) by providing a quantitative characterization using the coherence length as a measure. The study builds on the authors' previous HFB-based investigations of collective excitations in the inner crust (Inakura 2017, Inakura 2019) and introduces new DDDI parameter sets that simultaneously reproduce ab initio neutron matter pairing gaps from Gezerlis and Carlson (2010) and Abe and Seki (2009) as well as experimental pairing gaps in Sn isotopes. The results enable more accurate modeling of how the inhomogeneous structure of the inner crust affects superfluid properties relevant to neutron star cooling, thermal conductivity, and vortex pinning.
