# On Fixed-multiplicity Corrections to Correlators

> Correction terms generated in the correlator analysis due to multiplicity-dependent observable mean are investigated. A procedure for subtraction of such terms from calculated correlator estimates is suggested and the obtained results are discussed.

## Key claim

Correlator estimates calculated using a global observable mean are systematically biased when the observable mean depends on event multiplicity; this bias can be subtracted by replacing the global mean with a multiplicity-adjusted mean, yielding a "true" correlator that is free of such systematic shifts.

## Method

The paper derives analytical expressions for the correction terms Δ_K that must be subtracted from global K-th order correlators C_K^calc to obtain true correlators C_K^true, starting from the 2-particle case and generalizing to higher orders. For the 2-particle correlator, the correction is the variance of the multiplicity-dependent observable mean over the event sample; for higher orders, additional terms involving lower-order true correlators appear. The analysis is extended to account for fluctuations of total multiplicity at fixed measured multiplicity (detector acceptance effects), yielding fixed-multiplicity correction terms expressed in terms of experimentally accessible quantities: the slope k of the observable mean versus measured multiplicity, the slope ω of the multiplicity distribution, and the width σ of multiplicity fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- For a linear multiplicity dependence of the observable mean and constant P(n_k) distribution, the correction to C_2 is Δ_2 = k̃²(n₂−n₁)²/12 = (Δ⟨x_G⟩)²/3, where k̃ is the slope of x(n_k) and Δ⟨x_G⟩ is the difference in mean between the highest and lowest multiplicity subsamples.
- For the fixed-multiplicity correction to C_2 with linear approximation x(n^tot) = x₀ + k̃·n^tot, the correction is Δ̃₂ = k²·ñ_k·⟨σ̃⟩², where k is the slope of x(ñ_k) measured experimentally, ñ_k is the measured multiplicity, and ⟨σ̃⟩ ≈ 1 is obtained from MC simulation.
- For the fixed-multiplicity correction to C_4 with C_2^true = 0, the correction is Δ̃₄ = 3k⁴σ⁴_ñ_k, where σ_ñ_k = √ñ_k·⟨σ̃⟩.
- For non-constant prior probability P(n^tot) = 2ω̄(1−ω̄·n^tot), the fixed-multiplicity correction to C_2 is Δ̃₂^ω ≈ k²σ²_ñ_k [1−ω²σ²_ñ_k], where ω is the slope of the measured multiplicity distribution P(ñ_k).
- For the same non-constant prior, the fixed-multiplicity correction to C_3 is Δ̃₃^ω ≈ −2k³ω³σ⁶_ñ_k.

## Context

This work extends the correlator analysis framework developed by Amelin (2005) and applied by the STAR collaboration (2005) to p_t correlations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The paper addresses a systematic bias in correlator calculations that was not fully treated in prior work, where multiplicity-dependent observable means were assumed constant. The results enable experimentalists to subtract these biases using only experimentally measurable quantities (slope of observable mean vs. multiplicity, slope of multiplicity distribution, and width of multiplicity fluctuations from MC simulation), allowing extraction of true correlator values that reflect genuine particle correlations rather than artifacts of multiplicity-dependent means.
