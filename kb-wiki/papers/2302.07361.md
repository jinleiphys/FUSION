# Empirical Determination of the Pion Mass Distribution

> Existing pion+nucleus Drell-Yan and electron+pion scattering data are used to develop ensembles of model-independent representations of the pion generalised parton distribution (GPD). Therewith, one arrives at a data-driven prediction for the pion mass distribution form factor, $θ_2$. Compared with the pion elastic electromagnetic form factor, $θ_2$ is harder: the ratio of the radii derived from these two form factors is $r_π^{θ_2}/r_π= 0.79(3)$. Our data-driven predictions for the pion GPD, related form factors and distributions should serve as valuable constraints on theories of pion structure.

## Key claim

Using existing pion+nucleus Drell-Yan and electron+pion scattering data, the authors produce data-driven predictions for the pion mass distribution form factor, finding that the mass distribution is more compact than the charge distribution; the abstract's claim that this is an "empirical determination" is qualified by the fact that the result depends on a factorized light-front wave function ansatz and on the specific statistical ensembles generated from two different analyses of the E615 Drell-Yan data.

## Method

The authors first generate ensembles of pion valence-quark distribution functions (DFs) at the hadron scale by fitting to E615 Drell-Yan data using two different analyses (Aicher et al. 2010 and Barry et al. 2021), then evolve these DFs to the hadron scale using a nonperturbative DGLAP evolution scheme based on an effective charge. They reconstruct the pion generalized parton distribution (GPD) using a factorized light-front wave function overlap representation, where the transverse profile is constrained by fitting to pion elastic electromagnetic form factor data and the pion charge radius. The mass distribution form factor is then obtained as the first Mellin moment of the GPD at zero skewness.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of mass radius to charge radius: \(r_\pi^{\theta_2}/r_\pi = 0.79(3)\)
- Mass radius from ensemble A: \(r_\pi^{\theta_2} = 0.518(16)\) fm
- Mass radius from ensemble B: \(r_\pi^{\theta_2} = 0.498(14)\) fm
- Mass radius from lattice QCD ensemble: \(r_\pi^{\theta_2} = 0.512(21)\) fm
- Charge radius used: \(r_\pi = 0.64(2)\) fm (from Cui et al. 2021)
- Volume ratio: \(0.49(6)\)
- Bounds on the radii ratio: \(1/\sqrt{2} \leq r_\pi^{\theta_2}/r_\pi \leq 1\)

## Context

This work extends the program of using experimental data to constrain pion structure, building on prior analyses of Drell-Yan data by Aicher et al. (2010) and Barry et al. (2021), and on continuum Schwinger function method predictions by Zhang et al. (2021) and Raya et al. (2021). The data-driven GPDs and mass distribution provide constraints for theories of emergent hadron mass and can be tested by future experiments at facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider. The result that the mass distribution is more compact than the charge distribution is consistent with continuum Schwinger function method predictions (ratio 0.81(3)) but inconsistent with some momentum-independent interaction model results (ratios of 0.71 and 0.53).
