# Covariant model for proton-proton bremsstrahlung: Comparison with high-precision data

> We compare a relativistic covariant model for proton-proton bremsstrahlung with high-quality data from KVI. The agreement in large parts of phase space is satisfactory. However, remarkably large discrepancies are observed for specific kinematic regions. These failures are shown to occur primarily when the final two-nucleon system has energies less than about 15 MeV.

## Key claim

The relativistic covariant model for proton-proton bremsstrahlung shows satisfactory agreement with high-precision KVI data over large parts of phase space, but large discrepancies appear specifically in kinematic regions where the final two-nucleon system has energies below about 15 MeV, indicating that the problem resides primarily in the low-energy behavior of the nucleon-nucleon interaction models used.

## Method

The authors compared a relativistic covariant model for proton-proton bremsstrahlung (based on solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the equal-time approximation with a one-boson exchange kernel) to high-precision data from the KVI experiment at 190 MeV incoming proton energy. They analyzed the contributions from different partial waves (particularly the 1S0 and 3P waves) and the energy at which the nucleon-nucleon T-matrix is evaluated in the single-scattering and rescattering diagrams. A preliminary refit of the nucleon-nucleon model was performed to improve agreement with the Nijmegen pp partial-wave analysis from 10 MeV to 215 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The KVI experiment measured pp gamma cross sections at 190 MeV incoming proton energy, with scattered protons detected at small forward angles and photons emitted in the backward hemisphere.
- For the asymmetric kinematics (theta1=8 degrees, theta2=16 degrees), the T-matrix is evaluated at 7 and 11 MeV kinetic energy at the cross section peaks (theta_gamma=20 degrees and 140 degrees respectively), and at 24 MeV at the minimum (theta_gamma=75 degrees).
- For the symmetric kinematics (theta1=theta2=16 degrees), the T-matrix is evaluated at more than 20 MeV for theta_gamma=160 degrees and at more than 40 MeV for the minimum around theta_gamma=75 degrees.
- The 1S0 partial wave contribution to the cross section at theta1=8 degrees, theta2=16 degrees, theta_gamma=140 degrees was 1.90 microbarn/sr^2/rad in the original model and 1.18 microbarn/sr^2/rad after the refit.
- The total cross section at theta1=8 degrees, theta2=16 degrees, theta_gamma=140 degrees was 2.40 microbarn/sr^2/rad in the original model and 1.98 microbarn/sr^2/rad after the refit.
- The total cross section at theta1=16 degrees, theta2=16 degrees, theta_gamma=160 degrees was 1.44 microbarn/sr^2/rad in the original model and 1.38 microbarn/sr^2/rad after the refit.

## Context

This work extends the covariant bremsstrahlung model of Martinus et al. (1997) by comparing it to the first high-precision KVI data at 190 MeV from Huisman et al. (1999), which revealed discrepancies not seen in earlier comparisons with TRIUMF data at 280 MeV from Michaelian et al. (1990). The study demonstrates that the discrepancies arise from inadequate description of the low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction, and shows that a preliminary refit of the one-boson exchange model (originally fitted to the Virginia Tech np partial-wave solution from Arndt et al. (1987)) to the Nijmegen pp partial-wave analysis from Stoks et al. (1993) substantially improves agreement. The sensitivity of bremsstrahlung cross sections to low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction raises questions about the relation between microscopic models and soft-photon descriptions such as that of Liou et al. (1995), which gives good agreement with KVI data but uses on-shell points that do not correspond to low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction.
