# Calculation of the Momentum Dependence of Hadronic Current Correlation Functions at Finite Temperature

> We have calculated spectral functions associated with hadronic current correlation functions for vector currents at finite temperature. We made use of a model with chiral symmetry, temperature-dependent coupling constants and temperature-dependent momentum cutoff parameters. Our model has two parameters which are used to fix the magnitude and position of the large peak seen in the spectral functions. In our earlier work, good fits were obtained for the spectral functions that were extracted from lattice data by means of the maximum entropy method (MEM). In the present work we extend our calculations and provide values for the three-momentum dependence of the vector correlation function at T=1.5T_c. These results are used to obtain the correlation function in coordinate space, which is usually parametrized in terms of a screening mass. Our results for the three-momentum dependence of the spectral functions are similar to those found in a recent lattice QCD calculation for charmonium [S. Datta, F. Karsch, P. Petreczky and I. Wetzorke, hep-lat/0312037]. For a limited range we find the exponential behavior in coordinate space that is usually obtained for the spectral function for T>T_c and which allows for the definition of a screening mass.

## Key claim
The authors extend their earlier chiral model, which successfully reproduced spectral functions extracted from lattice QCD via the maximum entropy method (MEM), to compute the three-momentum dependence of the vector correlation function at T=1.5 T_c. The exponential behavior in coordinate space that allows definition of a screening mass is found only for a limited range of z (1 GeV^{-1} < z < 3 GeV^{-1}), not for all z values.

## Method
The authors use a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with chiral symmetry, temperature-dependent coupling constants G_S(T) and G_V(T), and a temperature-dependent momentum cutoff parameter k_max. They calculate the imaginary part of the vector polarization function using the real-time finite-temperature formalism, then obtain the real part via a twice-subtracted dispersion relation. The spectral functions sigma(omega, P) are computed for various values of the total three-momentum |P| ranging from 0.10 GeV to 2.10 GeV, and the coordinate-space correlation function C(z) is obtained by Fourier transforming the momentum-space results.

## Key numbers
- Temperature: T = 1.5 T_c, with T_c = 270 MeV (quenched approximation value)
- Momentum cutoff parameter: k_max = 1.22 GeV
- Coupling constant G_S = 1.2 GeV^{-2}
- Screening mass obtained from exponential fit: m_sc = 1.02 GeV, compared to pi T = 1.27 GeV
- Values of |P| studied: 0.10, 0.30, 0.50, 0.70, 0.90, 1.10, 1.30, 1.50, 1.70, 1.90, and 2.10 GeV
- Range of exponential behavior in coordinate space: 1 GeV^{-1} < z < 3 GeV^{-1}

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier calculations (Li et al., 2004) that fitted spectral functions extracted from lattice QCD data using the maximum entropy method (MEM) at T/T_c = 1.5 and 3.0. The results for the three-momentum dependence of the spectral functions are similar to those found in a lattice QCD calculation for charmonium by Datta, Karsch, Petreczky and Wetzorke (2003). The model enables further study of screening masses in the quark-gluon plasma and supports the picture of strongly interacting quasiparticles in the region T_c < T < 4 T_c, as discussed by Shuryak (2003).
