# Renormalization of Singular Potentials and Power Counting

> We analyze the renormalization of the nucleon--nucleon interaction at low energies in coordinate space for both one and two pion exchange chiral potentials. The singularity structure of the long range potential and the requirement of orthogonality respectively determines, once renormalizability is imposed, the minimum and maximum number of counterterms allowed in the effective description of the nucleon--nucleon interaction in a non-perturbative context.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for singular chiral potentials (such as the NNLO $^1S_0$ channel with a $-1/r^6$ attraction), renormalizability alone does not fix the number of counterterms; the additional requirement of orthogonality between different energy solutions forces the boundary condition to be energy-independent, reducing the allowed counterterms below what Weinberg power counting would suggest.

## Method

The authors analyze the nucleon–nucleon interaction in coordinate space using the reduced s-wave Schrödinger equation for the $^1S_0$ channel, with long-range potentials from one-pion and two-pion exchange chiral EFT at LO, NLO, and NNLO. They impose renormalization by introducing a short-distance cutoff and study the behavior of linearly independent zero-energy wave functions near the origin, determining the minimum and maximum number of counterterms from the singularity structure and the orthogonality condition between different energy solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the $^1S_0$ singlet channel at NNLO, the effective range predicted from the orthogonality condition is $r_0 = 2.86\ \mathrm{fm}$, compared to the experimental value $2.77 \pm 0.05\ \mathrm{fm}$.
- The scattering length used in the effective range calculation is $\alpha_0 = -23.74\ \mathrm{fm}$.
- The long-range potential at NNLO displays a $-1/r^6$ singularity near the origin.

## Context

This work extends the debate on power counting in nuclear EFT, specifically challenging the assumption that long-range and short-range power countings are independent, as argued in standard EFT reviews such as Bedaque (2002). It directly addresses the discussion in Nogga (2005), Epelbaum (2006), and Birse (2005), and builds on the authors' own prior work (Pavon Valderrama 2005, Valderrama 2005) showing that singular potentials require boundary conditions rather than the usual regularity condition $u(0)=0$. The result enables a more constrained power counting for NN interactions where orthogonality reduces the number of allowed counterterms.
