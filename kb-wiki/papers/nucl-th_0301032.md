# Microscopic calculations in asymmetric nuclear matter

> A microscopic calculation of the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter is presented. We employ realistic nucleon-nucleon forces and operate within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach to nuclear matter. The focal point of this paper is a (momentum-space) G-matrix which properly accounts for the asymmetry between protons and neutrons. This will merge naturally into the development of an effective interaction suitable for applications to asymmetric nuclei, which will be the object of extensive study in the future.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic calculation of the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, with the key advance being a momentum-space G-matrix that properly accounts for the asymmetry between protons and neutrons. The authors note that while similar calculations have been done in Ref. Oslo, their purpose is to produce a self-contained report and examine the effect of asymmetry on the in-medium NN interaction, serving as a baseline for future applications to asymmetric nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the Thompson relativistic three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for nuclear matter, using a relativistic one-boson-exchange potential (OBEP) with pseudo-vector coupling for the pi and eta mesons. They perform a self-consistent calculation where the G-matrix equation and the single-particle potentials for neutrons and protons (which are coupled through the np component) are solved simultaneously, with the single-particle potential parametrized in terms of scalar and vector constants that are iterated until convergence. The Pauli operator is treated with an angle-average approximation and an average center-of-mass momentum is introduced to simplify the integration.

## Key numbers

- Symmetric matter saturates at k_F approximately 1.4 fm^{-1} with a value of 16.7 MeV, in good agreement with empirical values
- Compression modulus of saturated symmetric matter is 233 MeV, in excellent agreement with the empirical determination of 225 plus or minus 15 MeV (Ref. PD02)
- DBHF prediction for the symmetry energy at saturation density is about 30 MeV
- For asymmetry parameter alpha larger than about 0.8, the system is no longer bound
- The chiral potential cutoff is 460 MeV/c (equivalent to 2.3 fm^{-1}), which sets the reliability limit for its predictions

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, which was developed in the 1980s (Refs. 7-9) and successfully described saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, to the asymmetric case. The authors compare their DBHF results with conventional Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations and with a chiral potential from Ref. EM, noting that the DBHF predictions produce a reasonable amount of repulsion compared to experimental constraints from elliptic flow measurements at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (Ref. nscl). The work enables future applications including reaction studies using effective interactions for scattering on asymmetric nuclei, calculations of neutron radii and neutron skins, and studies of highly asymmetric nuclei relevant to the proposed Rare Isotope Accelerator.
