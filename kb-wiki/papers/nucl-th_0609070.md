# A critical analysis on deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei and the KEK experiment

> We critically discuss the theoretical developments that led to predictions of very deeply bound kaon states and then revise the data of the KEK experiment from where claims for evidence of deeply bound kaon atoms in nuclei were made. We conclude that the peaks seen in the experiment correspond to the absorption of kaons by a pair of nucleons leading to $Σp$ and $Λp$ and leaving the daughter nucleus as a spectator. These conclusions have been reconfirmed by a recent experiment at FINUDA.

# A critical analysis on deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei and the KEK experiment

## Key claim
The peaks previously interpreted as evidence for deeply bound kaonic states in the KEK experiment actually correspond to kaon absorption by a pair of nucleons producing Σp and Λp pairs, with the remaining nucleons acting as spectators. This reinterpretation is supported by the observation that the theoretical predictions for deeply bound kaon states relied on unreliable approximations including compression of nuclear matter to ten times normal density.

## Method
The authors performed a critical review of the chiral unitary theory calculations for kaon-nucleus interactions and compared them with the approach of Akaishi and collaborators, which used only the K̄N, πΛ, and πΣ channels while neglecting self-consistency in the kaon propagator. They then reanalyzed the KEK experiment data on stopped K⁻ absorption in ⁴He leading to proton emission, testing alternative reaction mechanisms and comparing predicted proton momenta from K⁻NN → ΣN and K⁻NN → ΛN processes against the observed peaks at 475 MeV/c and 545 MeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The peak in the proton momentum spectrum from the KEK experiment appears at 475 MeV/c before proton energy loss corrections, matching the 488 MeV/c predicted for K⁻NN → ΣN events after accounting for approximately 13 MeV/c energy loss in the target.
- A second peak is observed around 545 MeV/c, consistent with the predicted 562 MeV/c for K⁻NN → ΛN events.
- The Σ⁻p d reaction occurs at a rate of 1.6% per stopped K⁻, while Λ(Σ⁰)pnn occurs at 11.7% per stopped K⁻, with errors of 30-40%.
- For K⁻ absorption in ⁷Li, the predicted proton momenta are 502 MeV/c for pΣ emission and 574 MeV/c for pΛ emission, with FINUDA data showing peaks around 505 MeV/c and 570 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the critical analysis previously published by the same authors (Oset and Toki, 2006) and directly contradicts the predictions of Akaishi and collaborators (2002, 2005), who claimed deeply bound kaonic states with 108 MeV binding in ³He and later a potential of 618 MeV attraction at ten times nuclear matter density. The analysis also contradicts the interpretation of Suzuki and collaborators (2004), who claimed discovery of a strange tribaryon. The conclusions are reconfirmed by the FINUDA experiment (Piano, 2006; published as Lastfinuda, 2006), which showed the same peaks in other light nuclei with the predicted narrowing and diminishing strength for heavier nuclei. This work enables a reinterpretation of kaon absorption experiments and suggests that future experiments should search for Σ or Λ in coincidence with protons to confirm the spectator mechanism.
