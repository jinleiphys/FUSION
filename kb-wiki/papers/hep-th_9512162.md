# Scale Anomaly and Quantum Chaos in the Billiards with Pointlike Scatterers

> We argue that the random-matrix like energy spectra found in pseudointegrable billiards with pointlike scatterers are related to the quantum violation of scale invariance of classical analogue system. It is shown that the behavior of the running coupling constant explains the key characteristics of the level statistics of pseudointegrable billiards.

# Scale Anomaly and Quantum Chaos in the Billiards with Pointlike Scatterers

## Key claim
The random-matrix-like energy spectra observed in pseudointegrable billiards with pointlike scatterers arise from the quantum violation of scale invariance (scale anomaly), and the energy dependence of the running coupling constant explains the level statistics, specifically, the effective coupling strength vanishes at high energy, causing a transition toward Poisson statistics, regardless of the formal coupling strength.

## Method
The authors consider a quantum particle of unit mass moving freely inside a hard-wall boundary in two dimensions, with pointlike scatterers represented by Dirac delta potentials. They regularize the divergent Green's function using self-adjoint extension theory, introducing a regularization scale Λ and a self-adjoint extension parameter Θ. The effective (running) coupling strength v_eff(ω, v̄) is derived as a function of energy ω by truncating the mode sum at n_x(ω) = αω, and its high-energy asymptotic behavior is analyzed. The formalism is extended to two and then an arbitrary number of pointlike scatterers to examine the dependence of level statistics on scatterer number and formal strength.

## Key numbers
- The effective coupling strength at high energy behaves as v_eff(ω, v̄) ≈ -1 / (⟨φ(x₀)²⟩ ρ₀ log ω) as ω → ∞, where ρ₀ is the constant density of states and ⟨φ(x₀)²⟩ is the average squared wavefunction at the scatterer position.
- For two closely placed scatterers with equal formal strength v̄₀, the combined effective coupling is v_eff⁽²⁾(ω, v̄₀, v̄₀) = 2 v_eff(ω, v̄₀), which at high energy gives v_eff⁽²⁾ ≈ -2 / (⟨φ(x₀)²⟩ ρ₀ log ω).
- The truncation condition for the mode sum is n_x(ω) = α ω, where α is inversely proportional to the allowable error δḠ.
- The condition for two scatterers to be considered "closely placed" is |x₁ − x₀| ≈ √(ρ₀ / n_x(ω)).

## Context
This work extends the study of pseudointegrable billiards, previously examined by Richens and Berry (1981), Cheon and Cohen (1989), and Shigehara and Harada (1994), who found puzzling random-matrix-like level statistics in systems lacking classical chaos. The paper provides a fully quantum mechanical explanation, without semiclassical periodic orbit theory (Gutzwiller 1970, Berry 1989), for the energy dependence of level statistics and the effect of increasing the number of singular scatterers. It enables a direct connection between scale anomaly (Jackiw 1995) and quantum chaos, and suggests that the system serves as a pedagogical example of asymptotic freedom outside of gauge field theories.
