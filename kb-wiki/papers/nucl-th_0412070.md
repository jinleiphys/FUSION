# The complement: a solution to liquid drop finite size effects in phase transitions

> The effects of the finite size of a liquid drop undergoing a phase transition are described in terms of the complement, the largest (but still mesoscopic) drop representing the liquid in equilibrium with the vapor. Vapor cluster concentrations, pressure and density from fixed mean density lattice gas (Ising) model calculations are explained in terms of the complement. Accounting for this finite size effect is key to determining the infinite nuclear matter phase diagram from experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the "complement" concept to account for finite-size effects in liquid drops undergoing phase transitions, showing that the free energy change when a cluster moves from the liquid to the vapor must include changes in both the vapor cluster and the residual liquid drop (the complement). This generalization of Fisher's model explains vapor cluster concentrations, pressure, and density from fixed mean density lattice gas (Ising) model calculations, and is presented as a necessary step for extracting the infinite nuclear matter phase diagram from experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed canonical lattice gas (Ising) model calculations on square (d=2, L=80) and simple cubic (d=3, L=25) lattices with periodic boundary conditions, using fixed magnetization (fixed mean occupation density) to simulate a finite liquid drop in equilibrium with its vapor. They identified clusters via the Coniglio-Klein algorithm to ensure physical behavior, identified the largest cluster geometrically as the liquid drop, and compared cluster concentrations, pressure, and density to the predictions of Fisher's model both with and without the complement correction. For each temperature and fixed density, over 10^5 thermalized realizations were generated.

## Key numbers

- For d=2 free magnetization calculations, the fit returned Tc = 2.283 +/- 0.004 (compared to Onsager value 2.26915), c0 = 8.6 +/- 0.2, sigma = 0.56 +/- 0.01, tau = 2.071 +/- 0.002
- For d=3 free magnetization calculations, the fit returned Tc = 4.533 +/- 0.002 (compared to theoretical 4.51152), c0 = 12.63 +/- 0.04, sigma = 0.725 +/- 0.003, tau = 2.255 +/- 0.001
- For d=2 fixed magnetization with Ad^0 = 640, chi-squared per degree of freedom improved from 10.3 (without complement) to 1.7 (with complement)
- For d=3 fixed magnetization with Ad^0 = 468, chi-squared per degree of freedom improved from 14,825.9 (without complement) to 1,553.4 (with complement)
- The lattice size shift in Tc was less than 0.5% for both d=2 and d=3 calculations

## Context

This work extends Fisher's model (Fisher, 1969) for cluster concentrations at liquid-vapor coexistence to finite systems, and generalizes the Gibbs-Thomson formulae (Krishnamachari, 1996) for finite drop vapor pressure. The method is demonstrated on the lattice gas (Ising) model (Lee and Yang, 1952) and is intended to enable extraction of the infinite symmetric nuclear matter liquid-vapor phase diagram from experimental nuclear data, building on prior work using heat capacity measurements (D'Agostino, 2000) and fragment distributions (Elliott, 2002). The approach can be generalized to include symmetry, Coulomb, and angular momentum energies relevant to nuclear systems.
