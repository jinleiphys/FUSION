# GUMP1.0 -- First global extraction of generalized parton distributions from experiment and lattice data with NLO accuracy

> We report the first global extraction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), GUMP1.0, by combining deeply virtual Compton scattering and $ρ$-meson production data from Jefferson Lab and Hadron-Electron Ring Accelerator with global fits of parton distribution functions, charge form factors, and lattice quantum chromodynamics simulations. Using a conformal moment space parametrization, we achieve a unified description across low- and high-$x$ regions at next to leading order (NLO) accuracy in perturbative corrections. The results provide state-of-the-art GPDs consistent with almost all known facts, enabling three-dimensional nucleon imaging in impact parameter space and, at the same time, establishing a benchmark for future theoretical and experimental studies of the nucleon structure.

# GUMP1.0 -- First global extraction of generalized parton distributions from experiment and lattice data with NLO accuracy

## Key claim
The authors report the first global extraction of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that simultaneously fits deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and rho-meson production data from Jefferson Lab and HERA, global parton distribution function (PDF) fits, charge form factors, and lattice QCD simulations, achieving a unified description across low- and high-x regions at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy. The hedge is that the extraction relies on a conformal moment space parametrization with specific assumptions about the t-dependence and off-forward moments, and the E and tilde-E GPDs remain poorly constrained, requiring empirical constraints such as E_g proportional to H_g.

## Method
The authors parametrize GPDs in conformal moment space, expanding the moments as polynomials in the skewness parameter xi truncated to order xi^4, with forward moments modeled using a Regge-inspired ansatz with up to two terms for sea quarks and gluons and one term for valence quarks. The t-dependence is implemented via a non-factorized Regge trajectory plus a factorized exponential form for sea quarks and gluons or a dipole form for valence quarks. A chi-squared minimization using Minuit2 is performed against 2,646 data points from JLab DVCS differential cross sections and asymmetries, HERA DVCS and deeply virtual rho-meson production, JAM global PDFs, lattice QCD simulations of GPDs and generalized form factors, and nucleon charge form factors, with NLO Wilson coefficients and GPD evolution applied throughout.

## Key numbers
- Total of 2,646 data points from experiments, lattice simulations, and global PDFs and form factors were included in the analysis.
- The overall chi-squared per degree of freedom is about 1.09.
- The extracted proton angular momentum decomposition is: J_u = 0.334(9), J_d = -0.108(12), J_g = 0.258(8), with J_tot = 0.484(17), where uncertainties include Hessian errors only.
- A moderate relative 30% uncertainty was added to the reported uncertainty of lattice results to account for systematic uncertainties not included in the original analysis.
- The analysis covers x in the range [0.0005, 0.6] for the H GPD of anti-u, anti-d, and gluons.

## Context
This work extends the GPD through Universal Moment Parametrization (GUMP) program (Guo 2022, Guo 2023, Guo 2024) and builds on the Kumericki-Muller (KM) model (Kumericki 2007, Kumericki 2009, Cuic 2023) which focused on sea distributions, by incorporating both sea and valence regions from extensive experimental and lattice inputs. The extraction enables three-dimensional nucleon imaging in impact parameter space and establishes a benchmark for future GPD studies at Jefferson Lab, the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and the Electron-Ion Collider in China (EIcC). The results show tension with lattice simulations (Hackett 2023) regarding the B^{u+d}_{10}(t) form factor, which the authors attribute to global charge form factors favoring a negative value while lattice favors positive or near-zero.
