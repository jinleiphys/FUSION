# Medium effects on phi decays to dilepton and kaon-antikaon pairs in relativistic heavy ion reactions

> We consider the role of rescattering of secondary kaons on the dilepton branching ratio of the phi meson. In-medium mass modifications and broadening of kaons and phi mesons are taken into account. We find in the framework of a Bjorken scenario for the time evolution of the expanding fireball that the phi yield from dimuons is moderately or at least only slightly enhanced compared to that from kaon-antikaon pairs. The relation to experimental yields measured by the NA49, NA50 and CERES Collaborations at CERN SPS and the PHENIX Collaboration at RHIC is discussed.

## Key claim

The apparent enhancement of the phi meson yield from dileptons compared to kaon-antikaon pairs is only moderate (factor between about 1.1 and 1.6) when kaon rescattering and in-medium modifications are included in a Bjorken fireball expansion, contradicting the much larger enhancement factor of 2-4 previously reported from comparing NA50 and NA49 data.

## Method

The authors calculated kaon collision widths and in-medium phi meson decay widths in an isotopically symmetric hot pion gas using chiral perturbation theory extended to RHIC temperatures via experimental pi-K scattering phase shifts. They then modeled the time evolution of the expanding hadronic fireball using a Bjorken scenario with temperature-dependent widths, integrating over the fireball lifetime to compute the number of phi mesons reconstructed from dilepton versus kaon-antikaon decays.

## Key numbers

- Apparent dilepton branching ratio enhancement factor B^app/B ranges between about 1.1 and 1.6 for reasonable parametrizations of the cooling scenario
- For fast cooling with T_h = 200 MeV and t_h = 1 fm/c, the enhancement factor is about 1.25 at the physically interesting tau region around 32 fm/c
- For slower cooling with T_h = 150 MeV and t_h = 5 fm/c, the enhancement factor is about 1.5-1.6 at tau ≈ 32 fm/c
- The CERES Collaboration excludes an enhancement factor larger than 1.6 at 95% CL, with a measured ratio of 1.0 ± 0.31 (stat) ± 0.28 (syst)
- The phi meson mean free path at temperatures above 170 MeV is between 2.4 fm and 1 fm, based on calculations from a Hidden Local Symmetry Lagrangian (koch02)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Johnson (1999), Filip (2001), and Pal (2002) that investigated kaon rescattering effects on phi meson reconstruction, which could not explain the factor 2-4 discrepancy between NA49 (K+K- channel) and NA50 (dimuon channel) measurements at CERN SPS. The results agree with recent CERES Collaboration data (Adamova 2005) that exclude enhancement above 1.6, and provide a framework for interpreting upcoming PHENIX data at RHIC where preliminary results show larger errors in the dielectron channel. The inclusion of additional collisional broadening from rho mesons does not significantly change the results, as the integration over the fireball volume is dominated by lower temperatures where heavier mesons are Boltzmann-suppressed.
