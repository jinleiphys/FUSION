# Open $sd$-shell nuclei from first principles

> We extend the ab initio coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) method to deformed open-shell nuclei with protons and neutrons in the valence space, and compute binding energies and excited states of isotopes of neon and magnesium. We employ a nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory evolved to a lower cutoff via a similarity renormalization group transformation. We find good agreement with experiment for binding energies and spectra, while charge radii of neon isotopes are underestimated. For the deformed nuclei $^{20}$Ne and $^{24}$Mg we reproduce rotational bands and electric quadrupole transitions within uncertainties estimated from an effective field theory for deformed nuclei, thereby demonstrating that collective phenomena in $sd$-shell nuclei emerge from complex ab initio calculations.

## Key claim
The coupled-cluster effective interaction (CCEI) method is extended to deformed open-shell nuclei with both protons and neutrons in the valence space, reproducing rotational bands and electric quadrupole transitions in 20Ne and 24Mg within uncertainties estimated from an effective field theory for deformed nuclei, thereby demonstrating that collective phenomena in sd-shell nuclei emerge from ab initio calculations.

## Method
The authors start from an intrinsic A-dependent Hamiltonian with a next-to-next-to-next-to leading order two-body chiral interaction and a next-to-next-to leading order three-body chiral interaction, evolved to a lower cutoff via a similarity renormalization group transformation. They perform coupled-cluster calculations in thirteen major oscillator shells with frequency 20 MeV, using the normal-ordered two-body approximation for the three-nucleon force with an additional three-body energy cut E3max = N1+N2+N3 ≤ 12. The CCEI method uses a valence-cluster expansion truncated at the two-body level, with the core ground state computed via coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles with perturbative Lambda-triples correction (Lambda-CCSD(T)), and one- and two-particle-attached equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) methods for the A_c+1 and A_c+2 nuclei, with the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation projecting onto the sd-shell valence space.

## Key numbers
- For 22O, CCEI yields a J^pi = 2+ excited state at 2.6 MeV and a J^pi = 3+ excited state at 3.8 MeV, while full-space EOM-CCSD yields 2.5 MeV and 3.8 MeV, respectively.
- For 24O, CCEI gives J^pi = 2+ at 5.7 MeV and J^pi = 1+ at 6.4 MeV, while EOM-CCSD yields 6.0 MeV and 6.4 MeV, respectively.
- CCEI binding energies for 22O and 24O are 162.2 MeV and 168.1 MeV, while full-space Lambda-CCSD(T) results are 162.0 MeV and 170.2 MeV, respectively.
- CCEI binding energies for 24F and 24Ne are 179.4 MeV and 192.9 MeV, in good agreement with full-space coupled-cluster results of 181.0 MeV and 190.8 MeV, and experimental values of 179.9 MeV and 191.8 MeV.
- Root-mean-squared deviations from 144 experimental levels in the lower sd-shell: 591 keV for oxygen, 452 keV for fluorine, 422 keV for neon, 529 keV for sodium, and 760 keV for magnesium.
- For deformed nuclei, the rotational energy scale xi is approximately 1.5 MeV and the breakdown energy Omega is approximately 6 MeV, with low-energy constants alpha_2 and beta_2 of order (xi/Omega)^2.

## Context
This work extends the CCEI method previously applied to p-shell nuclei (Navratil 1997, Lisetskiy 2008) and oxygen isotopes (Jansen 2014) to deformed sd-shell nuclei with both protons and neutrons in the valence space. It builds on recent developments in ab initio methods including in-medium similarity renormalization group (Hergert 2014) and Bogoliubov coupled cluster theory (Signoracci 2015), while addressing the challenge that deformed open-shell nuclei are inaccessible to typical spherical closed-shell implementations. The results enable non-perturbative shell-model Hamiltonians tied to chiral effective field theory with predictive power in the sd-shell and beyond, and allow comparison with an effective field theory for deformed nuclei (Papenbrock 2011, Papenbrock 2014, Coello Perez 2015).
