# Halo effective field theory constrains the solar Beryllium-7 + proton -> Boron-8 + photon rate

> We report an improved low-energy extrapolation of the cross section for the process Beryllium-7+proton -> Boron-8+photon, which determines the Boron-8 neutrino flux from the Sun. Our extrapolant is derived from Halo Effective Field Theory (EFT) at next-to-leading order. We apply Bayesian methods to determine the EFT parameters and the low-energy S-factor, using measured cross sections and scattering lengths as inputs. Asymptotic normalization coefficients of Boron-8 are tightly constrained by existing radiative capture data, and contributions to the cross section beyond external direct capture are detected in the data at E < 0.5 MeV. Most importantly, the S-factor at zero energy is constrained to be S(0)= 21.3 + - 0.7 eV b, which is an uncertainty smaller by a factor of two than previously recommended. That recommendation was based on the full range for S(0) obtained among a discrete set of models judged to be reasonable. In contrast, Halo EFT subsumes all models into a controlled low-energy approximant, where they are characterized by nine parameters at next-to-leading order. These are fit to data, and marginalized over via Monte Carlo integration to produce the improved prediction for S(E).

## Key claim

The S-factor at zero energy for the reaction Beryllium-7 + proton -> Boron-8 + photon is constrained to S(0) = 21.3 ± 0.7 eV b, an uncertainty smaller by a factor of two than previously recommended, but this result depends on the assumption that Halo Effective Field Theory at next-to-leading order subsumes all plausible low-energy models into a controlled approximant with nine parameters.

## Method

The authors applied Halo Effective Field Theory (EFT) at next-to-leading order to model the Beryllium-7 + proton system as two interacting particles, with the Coulomb interaction treated exactly and the strong interaction expanded in powers of relative momentum. They used Bayesian methods with Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling to fit the nine EFT parameters (asymptotic normalization coefficients, scattering lengths, effective ranges, short-distance parameters, and a core-excitation parameter) to 42 data points from five modern experiments in the low-energy region below 0.5 MeV, marginalizing over common-mode errors for each experiment.

## Key numbers

- S(0) = 21.3 ± 0.7 eV b (median and 68% interval), compared to the previous recommendation of 20.8 ± 1.6 eV b from Adelberger (2010)
- S(20 keV) median = 20.67 eV b, with upper 68% limit +0.60 eV b and lower limit -0.63 eV b
- Sum of asymptotic normalization coefficients C1^2 + C2^2 = 0.564 ± 0.023 fm^{-1}
- Beryllium-8 ground state is 0.1364 ± 0.0010 MeV below the Beryllium-7-proton scattering continuum
- The leading-order curve differs from the next-to-leading-order curve by less than 2% at E = 0 and less than 10% at E = 0.5 MeV
- The 1 keV uncertainty in the Boron-8 proton-separation energy shifts S(20 keV) by approximately 0.75%

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT approach previously applied to low-energy radiative capture by Zhang (2014, 2015) and Ryberg (2013, 2014), and directly addresses the extrapolation uncertainty identified in the Adelberger (2010) evaluation, which assigned a 1.4 eV b model uncertainty based on the spread among discrete models. The result enables more precise predictions of the Boron-8 neutrino flux from the Sun and provides a formalism applicable to other astrophysical radiative capture processes with similar physics.
