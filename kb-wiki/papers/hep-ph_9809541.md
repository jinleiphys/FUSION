# Evolution of the EMC Effect in Light Nuclei

> The evolution of the EMC effect as a function of atomic mass $A$ is considered for the first time for the lightest nuclei, D, $^3H$, $^3He$ and $^4He$, with an approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. We show that the pattern of the oscillation of the ratio $r^A(x) = F_2^A/F_2^N(D)$ varies with $A$, unlike the pattern for nuclei with masses $A > 4$, where only the amplitude of the oscillation changes.

# Evolution of the EMC Effect in Light Nuclei

## Key claim
The pattern of the oscillation of the ratio r^A(x) = F_2^A/F_2^N(D) varies with atomic mass A for the lightest nuclei (D, ³H, ³He, ⁴He), unlike for nuclei with A > 4 where only the amplitude of the oscillation changes. This pattern change is attributed to the saturation of short-range binding forces in 3- and 4-nucleon systems, not to a simple correlation with binding energy per nucleon.

## Method
The authors extended the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, previously applied to the deuteron, to compute nuclear structure functions for A = 3 and A = 4 nuclei. They expressed the nuclear Compton amplitude in terms of off-mass-shell nucleon Compton amplitudes and introduced "bare" Bethe-Salpeter vertex functions to handle singularities from nucleon-nucleon bound states. The resulting expressions for F_2^A involve three-dimensional momentum distributions extracted from experimental data, with calculations performed at fixed Q² = 10 GeV² using parameterizations of free proton and neutron structure functions.

## Key numbers
- The cross-over point x₃ (where r^A(x) = 1) for ⁴He was calculated as x₃ = 0.919, in reasonable agreement with extrapolated data from Gomez (1994) and Amaudruz (1995).
- The cross-over point x₃ for A = 3 was calculated as x₃ = 0.855, falling within the interval 0.84–0.86 where ratios r^A(x) for A > 4 cross unity.
- Numerical calculations were performed for a fixed Q² of 10 GeV².
- The considered x range is 0.3 < x < 0.9, where experiments show no Q² dependence of r^A(x).

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter formalism approach previously applied to the deuteron by the same authors (Smirnov 1994, 1995) to the lightest nuclei A = 3 and 4, where no prior EMC effect data existed. It contradicts the statement by Gomez (1994) that r^A(x) does not directly correlate with binding energy per nucleon, instead showing that saturation of short-range binding forces manifests in the pattern of x-dependence rather than amplitude. The results enable future experimental tests at HERA or CEBAF using ³He and ³H targets to measure nucleon isospin asymmetry independently of binding corrections, and suggest that the EMC effect in heavy nuclei can be understood as distortions of nucleon parton distributions in ³He or ³H modified by nuclear density effects.
