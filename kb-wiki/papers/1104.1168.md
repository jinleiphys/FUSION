# Longitudinal Rescaling of Quantum Electrodynamics

> We investigate quantum longitudinal rescaling of electrodynamics, transforming coordinates as $x^{0,3}\toλx^{0,3}$ and $x^{1,2}\to x^{1,2}$, to one loop. We do this by an aspherical Wilsonian renormalization, which was applied earlier to pure Yang-Mills theory. We find the anomalous powers of $λ$ in the renormalized couplings. Our result is only valid for $λ\lesssim 1$, because perturbation theory breaks down for $λ\ll 1$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that under longitudinal rescaling of quantum electrodynamics (QED), the renormalized couplings acquire anomalous powers of the rescaling parameter λ, but the result is only valid for λ ≲ 1 because perturbation theory breaks down for λ ≪ 1.

## Method

The authors apply an aspherical Wilsonian renormalization procedure to QED, using ellipsoidal momentum cut-offs that treat longitudinal and transverse momenta differently. They integrate out high-momentum modes in a shell between two ellipsoidal surfaces, compute the one-loop corrections to the photon polarization tensor, electron self-energy, and vertex function, then rescale the coordinates and fields to restore isotropy.

## Key numbers

- The QED beta function from spherical cut-offs is β(g̃) = g³/(12π²)
- The effective gauge coupling after rescaling satisfies 1/g'²_eff = 1/ñ² λ^(-8ñ²/(9π²) + g²/(4π²R̃)) where R̃ = 1 + g²/(8π²) ln(Λ/Λ̃)
- The transverse electric charge e_⊥ = ñ λ^(-1 + ñ²/(18π²) - g²/(8π²R̃)) diverges as λ → 0, enhanced by quantum corrections
- The anomalous power of λ in the Dirac kinetic term for transverse directions is λ^(1 - g²/(12π²R̃))

## Context

This work extends the aspherical Wilsonian renormalization method previously applied to pure Yang-Mills theory by the same authors (PhysRevD80, 2009) to the Abelian case of QED. The quantum corrections from fermions have the opposite sign to those from non-Abelian gauge fields, and the authors intend to study QCD with quarks to see how anomalous dimensions depend on the number of flavors. The high-energy limit of QED presents a problem because the effective transverse electric charge diverges, and a Landau pole is encountered when extrapolating the renormalization group equations.
