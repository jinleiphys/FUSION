# A linearized Boltzmann--Langevin model for heavy quark transport in hot and dense QCD matter

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the production of heavy quarks at large transverse momenta is strongly suppressed compared to proton-proton collisions. In addition an unexpectedly large azimuthal anisotropy was observed for the emission of charmed hadrons in non-central collisions. Both observations pose challenges to the theoretical understanding of the coupling between heavy quarks and the quark-gluon plasma produced in these collisions. Transport models for the evolution of heavy quarks in a QCD medium offer the opportunity to study these effects - two of the most successful approaches are based on the linearized Boltzmann transport equation and the Langevin equation. In this work, we develop a hybrid transport model that combines the strengths of both of these approaches: heavy quarks scatter with medium partons using matrix-elements calculated in perturbative QCD, while between these discrete hard scatterings they evolve using a Langevin equation with empirical transport coefficients to capture the non-perturbative soft part of the interaction. With the hybrid transport model coupled to a state-of-the-art event-by-event bulk evolution model based on 2+1D relativistic viscous fluid dynamics, we study the azimuthal anisotropy and nuclear modification factor of heavy quarks in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 5.02$ TeV. The parameters of our model are calibrated using a Bayesian analysis comparing to available $D$-meson and $B$-meson data at the LHC. Using the calibrated model, we study the implications on heavy-flavor transport properties and predict novel observables.

## Key claim

The authors develop a hybrid transport model that combines linearized Boltzmann scattering with pQCD matrix elements and a Langevin diffusion component with empirical transport coefficients, claiming this approach captures both hard perturbative and soft non-perturbative interactions of heavy quarks with the quark-gluon plasma. The model's parameters are calibrated via Bayesian analysis to D-meson and B-meson data, but the authors acknowledge that the separation between scattering and diffusion components is not rigorously perturbative and that the medium scale parameter μ encodes uncertainty in the pQCD matrix-element approach.

## Method

The model, called Lido (Linearized Boltzmann with diffusion model), evolves heavy quarks using a split-step method: within each time step, heavy quarks first undergo discrete scatterings with medium partons via a linearized Boltzmann equation using leading-order pQCD matrix elements (elastic 2→2 and inelastic 2→3 processes with gluon absorption 3→2 for detailed balance), then propagate between scatterings via a Langevin equation with isotropic momentum diffusion coefficient κ and drag satisfying the Einstein relation. The medium evolution is provided by a calibrated event-by-event 2+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model with Trento initial conditions, free-streaming pre-equilibrium stage, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner. Model parameters (energy-loss start time τ₀, medium scale μ, diffusion strength κ_D, and energy-dependence fraction x_D) are calibrated via Bayesian inference comparing to LHC D-meson and B-meson data.

## Key numbers

- Heavy quark thermalization time in static medium at T = 0.4 GeV: approximately 50 fm/c with elastic processes only, approximately 20 fm/c with elastic, radiation, and absorption processes included (initial charm quark energy E₀ = 10 GeV)
- Elastic energy loss fraction ΔE/E shown for path length 5 fm at temperatures T = 0.2 and 0.4 GeV as function of energy; inelastic energy loss shows non-linear path length dependence for small L transitioning to linear at large L
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA calculated for charm and bottom quarks in static medium at T = 0.4 GeV after 3 fm/c evolution, with absorption processes affecting only p_T < 10 GeV
- Model parameters calibrated with ranges: τ₀ from 0.1 to 1.0 fm/c, μ from 1/3 to 4, κ_D from 0 to 8, x_D from 0 to 1
- Hadronization temperature set at T = 0.154 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior heavy quark transport approaches by combining the linearized Boltzmann method (used by Cao et al. (2013, 2016, 2017) and Auvinen (2009)) with the Langevin equation approach (used by Moore (2004), Rapp (2009), and the authors' own group in Xu (2017) for Bayesian extraction of transport coefficients). The hybrid model enables systematic calibration of both perturbative and non-perturbative heavy quark transport parameters from LHC data, and the authors plan to use the calibrated model to predict novel observables and compare with previous extractions of the heavy quark momentum diffusion parameter.
