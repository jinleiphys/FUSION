# Chiral extrapolation of hadronic vacuum polarization

> We study the pion-mass dependence of the two-pion channel in the hadronic-vacuum-polarization (HVP) contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ^\text{HVP}$, by using an Omnès representation for the pion vector form factor with the phase shift derived from the inverse-amplitude method (IAM). Our results constrain the dominant isospin-$1$ part of the isospin-symmetric light-quark contribution, and should thus allow one to better control the chiral extrapolation of $a_μ^\text{HVP}$, required for lattice-QCD calculations performed at larger-than-physical pion masses. In particular, the comparison of the one- and two-loop IAM allows us to estimate the associated systematic uncertainties and show that these are under good control.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the chiral extrapolation of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization (HVP) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment can be controlled using the inverse-amplitude method (IAM) combined with an Omnès representation, contrary to earlier concerns that chiral perturbation theory alone is insufficient. The comparison of one- and two-loop IAM implementations allows estimation of systematic uncertainties, which the authors show are under good control.

## Method
The authors use an Omnès representation for the pion vector form factor (VFF) with the ππ P-wave phase shift derived from the inverse-amplitude method at one- and two-loop order. The quark-mass dependence of the phase shift is taken from IAM fits to lattice data for ππ scattering from Andersen (2018) and the physical pion decay constant, combined with the physical phase shift from Colangelo (2018). The pion-mass dependence of the charge radius is included via two-loop chiral perturbation theory, with the NNLO low-energy constant rV1 estimated from resonance saturation.

## Key numbers
- Physical two-pion contribution to aμHVP up to 1 GeV (no final-state radiation, no ρ-ω mixing): 486.3(1.4)(2.1)×10−10
- NNLO IAM result for the same quantity using lattice phase-shift data and physical phase-shift input: 482.4(0.1)(0.7)(8.0)×10−10
- NLO IAM result for the same quantity: 460.4(0.3)(14.9)(7.2)×10−10
- NNLO IAM result for the full ππ contribution (Λ→∞): 490.8(0.1)(0.7)(8.4)×10−10
- Pion charge radius at the physical point: 0.429(4) fm²
- Resonance-saturation estimate for rV1: 2.0×10−5
- ChPT prediction for the charge radius at Mπ=340.9 MeV (NNLO): 0.350(4)(13)(7) fm², compared to lattice result 0.3485(27) fm² from Feng (2019)

## Context
This work addresses the problem raised by Golterman (2017) that chiral perturbation theory alone cannot control the quark-mass extrapolation of HVP because the muon mass is lighter than the pion mass. The approach extends the IAM framework developed by Truong (1988), Dobado (1989), and others, and builds on the Omnès representation of the pion VFF used in numerous analyses including Colangelo (2018). The results enable lattice-QCD calculations performed at larger-than-physical pion masses to perform controlled chiral extrapolations of the dominant I=1 component of the isospin-symmetric ud contribution to aμHVP, with systematic uncertainties quantified by comparing one- and two-loop IAM implementations.
