# A Halo: The Trigger to a New Era of Nuclear Correlations

> In this contribution to the Halo-40 Proceedings, we discuss two topics regarding halo phenomena: The first is the pairing anti-halo effect on the neutron radius of halo nuclei and its restoration due to the coupling to the continuum; the second is the soft dipole excitation of deformed halo nuclei. We demonstrate the importance of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum for properly taking into account the halo nature of extended wave functions in calculations of neutron radii, as well as the soft dipole excitations of halo nuclei. It was shown that the anti-halo effect is very sensitive to the continuum coupling induced by Bogoliubov-type quasi-particles, which largely cancels the anti-halo effect on the neutron radius. The soft dipole excitations of deformed halo nuclei Ne-31 and Mg-37 are discussed within the deformed Woods-Saxon model. We point out that the sharp peak just above the threshold in the dipole response is created by the halo effect, and its strength can be used to identify the magnitude of deformation and the halo configuration in the Nilsson level scheme.

## Key claim

The pairing anti-halo effect, which suppresses the spatial extension of halo wave functions when the gap parameter remains finite as the single-particle energy approaches zero, can be largely cancelled by continuum coupling induced by Bogoliubov-type quasi-particles, leading to a restoration of the halo radius in certain nuclei such as \(^{11}\)Li while the anti-halo effect survives in others such as \(^{32}\)Ne.

## Method

The authors employed the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theories in coordinate space to study the pairing anti-halo effect and its restoration via continuum coupling in neutron-rich nuclei. For deformed halo nuclei \(^{31}\)Ne and \(^{37}\)Mg, they used a deformed Woods-Saxon model and the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) to calculate soft dipole excitation strengths and analyze the role of deformation and halo configurations in the Nilsson level scheme.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Li is \(S_{2n}=378 \pm 5\) keV, which drives its anomalously large matter radius.
- For \(^{31}\)Ne, the one-neutron separation energy is set to \(S_n \approx 150\) keV in the deformed Woods-Saxon model calculations.
- The \(p_{3/2}\) component probability in the \([321]3/2\) Nilsson orbit of \(^{31}\)Ne is 32.7%, in agreement with the experimental value of 32% from Coulomb breakup measurements.
- For \(^{37}\)Mg, the one-neutron separation energy is constrained to \(S_n \approx 220\) keV at a deformation of \(\beta_2 = 0.46\).
- The peak height for the deformed \([321]3/2\) configuration in \(^{31}\)Ne is quenched to about 20% of the spherical \(2p_{3/2}\) limit due to deformation effects.

## Context

This work extends the original proposal of the pairing anti-halo effect by Bennaceur et al. (2000) and the subsequent studies of Ne and Mg isotope reaction cross sections by Takechi et al. (2012) and others. The authors address the competing effects of pairing correlations that both suppress halo extension (anti-halo effect) and enhance it through continuum coupling, as previously studied by Chen et al. (2014) using relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory. The soft dipole excitation calculations for deformed halo nuclei build on the deformed Woods-Saxon model framework of Hamamoto (2005, 2012) and the DRHBc theory developed by Zhou et al. (2010) and Li et al. (2012). The results enable identification of deformation magnitude and halo configurations in the Nilsson level scheme through analysis of dipole response strengths.
