# Precision Low-Energy Measurements at DA$Φ$NE and Their Impact on $\bar{K}N$ Physics

> 4 pages, latex, no figures, uses style file menu97.sty (latex twice to get the references right); presented by P.M. Gensini at the "7th International Symposium on Meson-Nucleon Physics and the Structure of the Nucleon, MENU '97", Vancouver, B.C., July 28th - August 1st, 1997, to be published in the Proceedings

# Precision Low-Energy Measurements at DAΦNE and Their Impact on KN Physics

## Key claim

The authors propose a "theoretical χ²-functional technique" to implement fixed-t analyticity on invariant amplitudes for the coupled KN, πΛ, πΣ channels, which can test the analyticity of parametrizations without requiring external parameters such as coupling constants or subtraction constants. The method is presented as exploratory in the absence of new data but is intended to be applied once the Frascati DAΦNE φ-factory provides high-statistics kaon-nucleon scattering data.

## Method

The authors map the squared-energy variable ω² into a new variable z(ω²) such that the physical sheet maps onto the unit disk, then construct a family of analytic functions G_n(z) with zeros at the Born-term pole positions. They define a χ²-functional from contour integrals of the product of the amplitude F(z) with G_n(z) over the part of the contour where data exist, providing a measure of the lack of analyticity of the parametrization. A further refinement uses a weight function w(z), constructed via the Schwarz-Villat formula, to suppress contributions from the unmeasured region and weight inputs according to their statistical or systematic accuracies.

## Key numbers

- At DAΦNE operating at the φ-resonance peak, K± momenta are 126 MeV/c and K_L momenta are 110 MeV/c at the interaction point; with moderators, K± momenta can span from about 115 MeV/c down to about 80 MeV/c.
- For a reference luminosity of 5×10³² cm⁻²s⁻¹, expected rates are around 1,200 K±/s and 830 K_L/s.
- The K± decay length is about 90 cm.
- The only available P-wave information below 250 MeV/c consists of 4 data points on the 1st moment of the angular distribution for each of the π±Σ∓ channels, all consistent with zero within 2σ.
- For analysis below 550 MeV/c, at least four partial waves per isospin channel are required, totaling 36 two-channel K- or M-matrix elements, requiring more than a hundred parameters in a self-consistent amplitude expansion to order p⁴.
- The proposed analyticity test can be run for |t| ≤ 2 m_π² and for five out of the ten S = -1 two-body isospin eigenchannels.

## Context

This work extends the S-wave dispersion-relation approach of A.D. Martin (1981), who used once-subtracted forward dispersion relations for K±p and K±n elastic scattering with additional parameters (subtraction constants and KNY couplings). The authors aim to extend such constraints to other amplitudes and channels, including B amplitudes dominated by P-waves and "unphysical" channels such as πY → πY' or KN → πY. The technique builds on earlier mapping methods by Ciuﬁ et al. (1970) and Nenciu (1970) and is intended for use with the high-statistics data expected from the DAΦNE φ-factory, which should bring event counts from the few hundred events of the TST Collaboration at NIMROD to 10⁵–10⁶ events/year. The work is part of the EuroDAΦNE Network and is to be summarized in successive editions of the "DAΦNE Physics Handbook."
