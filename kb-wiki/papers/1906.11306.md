# Critical behavior of the bulk viscosity in QCD

> We study the behavior of the bulk viscosity $ζ$ in QCD near a possible critical endpoint in the phase diagram. We verify the expectation that $(ζ/s)\sim a(ξ/ξ_0)^{x_ζ}$, where $s$ is the entropy density, $ξ$ is the correlation length, $ξ_0$ is the non-critical correlation length, $a$ is a constant and $x_ζ\simeq 3$. Using a recently developed equation of state that includes a critical point in the universality class of the Ising model we estimate the constant of proportionality $a$. We find that $a$ is typically quite small, $a\sim O(10^{-4})$. We observe, however, that the result is sensitive to the commonly made assumption that the Ising temperature axis is approximately aligned with the QCD baryon chemical potential axis. If this is not the case, then the critical $ζ/s$ can approach the non-critical value of $η/s$, where $η$ is the shear viscosity, even if the enhancement of the correlation length is modest, $ξ/ξ_0\sim 2$.

# Critical behavior of the bulk viscosity in QCD

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity near the QCD critical point can become comparable to the non-critical shear viscosity, even for a modest correlation length enhancement of ξ/ξ₀ ∼ 2, but only if the Ising temperature axis is not approximately aligned with the QCD baryon chemical potential axis; under the commonly made assumption of alignment, the critical ζ/s is suppressed by a factor (n/s)² and remains very small, of order 10⁻⁴.

## Method

The authors used the Ising equation of state parameterized by Zinn-Justin (2002) and the QCD-to-Ising mapping of Parotto et al. (2018) to compute the tri-linear coupling γ in the critical entropy functional. They then evaluated the Kubo formula for the bulk viscosity using the order parameter correlation functions from model H of dynamical critical phenomena, both in a simple diffusion approximation and in the Kawasaki approximation that accounts for coupling to the fluid momentum density. The orientation of the Ising axes in the QCD phase diagram was varied, using a random matrix model to determine a realistic misalignment angle.

## Key numbers

- The critical exponent for the bulk viscosity scaling is x_ζ ≃ 2.8 in the Kawasaki approximation (model H), giving ζ/s ∼ (ξ/ξ₀)^(2.8); in the simple diffusion approximation the exponent is 3.92.
- Under the standard assumption of Ising temperature axis aligned with the QCD chemical potential axis, the prefactor is ζ/s = 8.0 × 10⁻⁵ (ξ/ξ₀)^(2.8) on the crossover side (r > 0) and 5.2 × 10⁻⁴ (ξ/ξ₀)^(2.8) on the first-order side (r < 0), using η₀/s = 1/(4π).
- With a misaligned orientation (α₁ ≃ 105° from a random matrix model), the prefactor becomes ζ/s = sin²(α₁) × 5.1 × 10⁻² (ξ/ξ₀)^(2.8) for r > 0 and 3.2 × 10⁻¹ (ξ/ξ₀)^(2.8) for r < 0; with sin²(α₁) ≃ 1/4, this yields ζ/s ∼ 0.08 (ξ/ξ₀)^(2.8) on the first-order side.
- The Kibble-Zurek scale from Akamatsu et al. (2018) limits the correlation length enhancement to ξ_KZ/ξ₀ ∼ 1.33 in a heavy ion collision.
- The tri-linear coupling constants are γ₊ = 0.43 r^(1-2β) for r > 0 and γ₋ = 1.10 |r|^(1-2β) for r < 0, with 1-2β ≃ 0.34.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical expectation that ζ/s ∼ (ξ/ξ₀)^(x_ζ) by providing the first quantitative estimate of the proportionality constant using a realistic QCD equation of state with a critical point (Parotto et al., 2018). It contradicts the scaling relation ζ/s ∼ (1/c_s² - 3) proposed by Karsch et al. (2007), which predicts a much weaker divergence ζ/s ∼ ξ^(α/ν), and also differs from Weinberg's (1972) relation ζ ∼ (c_s² - 1/3)² η which has no critical divergence. The results enable more realistic modeling of bulk viscous effects in heavy ion collisions near a critical point, and show that the relaxation of the bulk pressure is governed by a slow 1/√t tail rather than a simple exponential, which has implications for second-order relativistic fluid dynamics implementations that assume τ_ζ ∼ ζ/P.
