# A Hydrodynamic and Transport Model for Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and LHC Energies

> Combining the hydrodynamic model (Hydro code) and the transport model (PACIAE model), we present the Hydro-PACIAE hybrid model. We use the Hydro-PACIAE hybrid model to calculate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=130 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV. The obtained pseudo-rapidity and transverse momentum distributions well reproduce the experimental data. This shows that the Hydro-PACIAE hybrid model is useful to describe the ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC and LHC Energies. We used the hybrid model to calculate the elliptical flow for the Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV. The $v_2$ values are bigger than the experimental data at high $p_T$, and will become bigger when the Hydro evolution time is longer. The results indicate that the selection of the transition hypersurface has significant effect on the observable quantities.

## Key claim
The Hydro-PACIAE hybrid model reproduces experimental pseudo-rapidity and transverse momentum distributions for Au+Au collisions at 130 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the elliptic flow (v2) values exceed experimental data at high transverse momentum and increase further with longer hydrodynamic evolution time, indicating that the choice of transition hypersurface significantly affects observables.

## Method
The authors combined a 3+1 dimensional relativistic perfect fluid hydrodynamic model (using the MIT Bag Model equation of state and solved via the Particle In Cells method) with the PACIAE parton and hadron cascade model. The transition from fluid to partonic matter occurred on a constant-time hypersurface, where each fluid cell was converted to partons using the Cooper-Frye formula while conserving baryon charge, energy, and momentum. The resulting partons then underwent parton rescattering, hadronization via coalescence, and final-state hadron rescattering within the PACIAE framework.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV: impact parameter b=5.5 fm corresponds to approximately 20% centrality, b=8.2 fm corresponds to approximately 40% centrality
- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV: hydrodynamic evolution times compared for v2 were 0.423, 2.54, and 5.08 fm/c
- Directed flow v1(eta) showed random statistical fluctuation amplitude of 0.1% for |eta| < 4, increasing to 0.2% for |eta| > 4
- Directed flow v1(pT) reached 1% at pT = 3 GeV/c and 3% at pT = 4 GeV/c due to statistical fluctuations
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_s = 0.3 used in parton generation

## Context
This work extends the hybrid model approach previously developed by Petersen, Bass, Hirano, Bauchle, and Abelev (all cited as prior work) by combining the specific Hydro code with the PACIAE transport model. The Effective String Rope model of Magas (cited as prior work) was used for initial state generation. The paper enables future studies where the transport model PACIAE itself generates the initial state for the hydrodynamic stage, creating a full transport model with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage to study relativistic heavy ion collisions with greater flexibility in model assumptions.
