# Variation of Tensor Force due to Nuclear Medium Effect

> The enhancement of $J^π(T)$=3$^{+}$(0) state with isospin $T=0$ excited by the tensor force in the free $^{6}$Li nucleus has been observed, for the first time, relative to a shrinkable excitation in the $^{6}$Li cluster component inside its host nucleus. Comparatively, the excitation of $J^π(T)$=0$^{+}$(1) state with isospin $T=1$ for these two $^{6}$Li formations take on an approximately equal excitation strength. The mechanism of such tensor force effect was proposed due to the intensive nuclear medium role on isospin $T$=0 state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the tensor force excitation of the \(J^{\pi}(T)=3^{+}(0)\) state in \({}^{6}\)Li is enhanced in the isolated nucleus but suppressed when \({}^{6}\)Li is embedded as a cluster inside \({}^{11}\)C, with the mechanism attributed to nuclear medium effects that selectively hinder the isospin \(T=0\) channel.

## Method

The experiment used a 60 MeV/nucleon \({}^{12}\)C beam on a \({}^{9}\)Be target to produce a \(\sim\)25 MeV/nucleon \({}^{11}\)C secondary beam, which was then directed onto a carbon target to induce breakup reactions. The outgoing deuteron (\(d\)) and alpha (\(\alpha\)) fragments were detected by a zero-degree telescope consisting of a double-sided silicon strip detector (DSSD) and a CsI(Tl) scintillator array, and the \({}^{6}\)Li excitation energy spectra were reconstructed via invariant mass of the \(d+\alpha\) final state. The relative emission angle \(\Theta_{CM}\) between \(d\) and \(\alpha\) in the \({}^{6}\)Li center-of-mass system was used to separate events from the \({}^{6}\)Li cluster inside \({}^{11}\)C (\(\Theta_{CM}<120^{\circ}\)) from those from isolated \({}^{6}\)Li (\(\Theta_{CM}>120^{\circ}\)).

## Key numbers

*   Cross section ratio between the \(J^{\pi}(T)=3^{+}(0)\) state and the \(J^{\pi}(T)=0^{+}(1)\) state: 2.7 for \(\Theta_{CM}<120^{\circ}\) (\({}^{6}\)Li inside \({}^{11}\)C) and 5.5 for \(\Theta_{CM}>120^{\circ}\) (isolated \({}^{6}\)Li).
*   Mean relative momentum \(p_{rel}\) between \(d\) and \(\alpha\) for the \(3^{+}(0)\) state: 361.6 \(\pm\) 12.7 MeV/c, with a standard deviation \(\sigma\) of 77.2 \(\pm\) 5.5 MeV/c.
*   Mean relative momentum \(p_{rel}\) for the mixing state of \(2^{+}(0)\) and \(1^{+}(0)\): 517.7 \(\pm\) 9.1 MeV/c, with \(\sigma\) of 35.5 \(\pm\) 2.8 MeV/c.
*   Energy resolution (sigma) of the \(\Delta E\)-E detection system: \(\sim\)0.8 MeV from numerical simulation.

## Context

This work extends studies of the tensor force in nuclear structure, particularly its role in shell evolution (Otsuka et al.) and short-range correlations (Hen et al.), by providing the first experimental observation of a medium-induced suppression of the \(T=0\) tensor channel. The results are interpreted using a nuclear medium effect for tensor force (NMET) model, which parameterizes the reduction of tensor force strength with increasing nuclear density. The finding enables future investigations of tensor force variation in extreme density environments such as neutron stars or heavy-ion collisions, and tests of cluster models for light nuclei like those of Nishioka et al. (1979) for \({}^{6}\)Li.
