# Chiral spin symmetry and the QCD phase diagram

> Lattice QCD simulations with chirally symmetric quarks have recently established approximate $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(2N_F)$ symmetries of the quantum effective action in a temperature range above the chiral crossover $T_\mathrm{ch}$, in which color-electric interactions between quarks dominate the dynamics. We show that such an intermediate temperature range between the chirally broken and plasma regimes is fully consistent with published screening mass spectra, which demonstrate the breakdown of thermal perturbation theory at the crossover between the partonic and the chiral spin symmetric regime at $T_s\sim (2-3)T_\mathrm{ch}$. From the known behavior of screening masses with baryon chemical potential, we deduce qualitatively how this chiral spin symmetric band extends into the QCD phase diagram. In the cold and dense region, we propose parity doubled baryons as possible candidates for chiral spin symmetric matter. This represents a special case of quarkyonic matter with confinement and restored chiral symmetry, and can smoothly transform to quark matter at sufficiently high densities. Finally, we discuss the potential of dilepton spectra to identify such matter forms.

## Key claim

The paper argues that an intermediate temperature range between the chirally broken phase and the quark-gluon plasma, characterized by approximate $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(2N_F)$ chiral spin symmetries, is fully consistent with published screening mass spectra and extends into the QCD phase diagram as a band at finite baryon chemical potential, with the caveat that the boundaries are smooth crossovers rather than sharp phase transitions and their precise numerical values depend on definition and quark masses.

## Method

The authors analyze published lattice QCD screening mass data for mesons in 12 different quantum number channels across a wide temperature range, comparing them to perturbative predictions from resummed thermal perturbation theory and EQCD. They identify the breakdown of perturbative behavior at a characteristic temperature $T_s$ where screening masses bend sharply, and use the known quadratic dependence of screening masses on baryon chemical potential to deduce the curvature of the upper boundary of the chiral spin symmetric band. They also review lattice results on gluonic correlation functions and Debye mass calculations to characterize the dominance of chromoelectric over chromomagnetic interactions in this regime.

## Key numbers

- The chiral crossover temperature for $N_F=2+1$ QCD at the physical point in the continuum is $T_\mathrm{pc} = 157(2)$ MeV
- The upper boundary of the chiral spin symmetric regime occurs at $T_s \sim (2-3)T_\mathrm{ch}$, where $T_\mathrm{ch} \approx 200$ MeV
- The Debye mass from gauge-invariant lattice evaluations at $T\approx 2T_\mathrm{ch}$ gives $m_D^\mathrm{gi}\approx 7.5 T$, corresponding to a Debye radius of $r_D\approx 0.09$ fm
- The pole mass definition of the Debye mass gives $m_D^\mathrm{pole}\approx 2.5 T$, corresponding to $r_D\approx 0.27$ fm
- The chiral crossover temperature at finite chemical potential follows $T_\mathrm{pc}(\mu_B)/T_\mathrm{pc}(0) = 1-0.016(5)(\mu_B/T_\mathrm{pc}(0))^2 + \ldots$
- Lattice screening mass data for pseudo-scalar and vector mesons extend to $T=1-160$ GeV, with the bending structure occurring within $T\approx 0.5-0.7$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the discovery of approximate $SU(2)_{CS}$ and $SU(2N_F)$ symmetries in lattice QCD simulations by Rohrhofer (2019) and Glozman (2014, 2015) from zero density to finite baryon chemical potential, and reconciles these findings with published screening mass data from Bazavov (2019) and Brida (2021). The paper connects to the concept of quarkyonic matter and parity doubled baryons as candidates for cold dense matter with restored chiral symmetry, and discusses how dilepton spectra could probe such matter forms. The chiral spin symmetric band is distinguished from the semi-QGP concept proposed by Dumitru (2012) and Pisarski (2016), and from observations of thermal monopole condensation at $T\approx 275$ MeV by Cardinali (2021), noting that the relationship between these phenomena remains unclear.
