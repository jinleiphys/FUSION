# Description of nuclear systems with a self-consistent configuration-mixing approach. II: Application to structure and reactions in even-even sd-shell nuclei

> The variational multiparticle-multihole configuration mixing approach (MPMH) to nuclei has been proposed about a decade ago. While the first applications followed rapidly, the implementation of the full formalism of this method has only been recently completed and applied in [C. Robin, N. Pillet, D. Peña Arteaga and J.-F. Berger, Phys. Rev. C 93, 024302 (2016)] to $^{12}$C as a test-case. The main objective of the present paper is to carry on the study that was initiated in that reference, in order to put the MPMH method to more stringent tests. To that aim we perform a systematic study of even-even sd-shell nuclei. The wave function of these nuclei is taken as a configuration mixing built on orbitals of the sd-shell, and both the mixing coefficients of the nuclear state and the single-particle wave functions are determined consistently from the same variational principle. The calculations are done using the D1S Gogny force. Various ground-state properties are analyzed. In particular, the correlation content and composition of the wave function as well as the single-particle orbitals and energies are examined. Binding energies and charge radii are also calculated and compared to experiment. The description of the first excited state is also examined and the corresponding transition densities are used as input for the calculation of inelastic electron and proton scattering. Special attention is paid to the effect of the optimization of the single-particle states consistently with the correlations of the system. Globally, the results are satisfying and encouraging. In particular, charge radii and excitation energies are nicely reproduced. However, the chosen valence-space truncation scheme precludes achieving maximum collectivity in the studied nuclei. Further refinement of the method and a better-suited interaction are necessary to remedy this situation.

## Key claim

The variational multiparticle-multihole (MPMH) configuration mixing approach, when applied systematically to even-even sd-shell nuclei using the D1S Gogny force, reproduces charge radii and excitation energies well, but the valence-space truncation scheme (restricting excitations to the sd-shell on top of a 16O core) prevents achieving maximum collectivity, and further refinement of the method and a better-suited interaction are necessary.

## Method

The nuclear wave function is taken as a superposition of Slater determinants built on single-particle states expanded on axially deformed harmonic oscillator states (N0=9 major shells) at the spherical point. Both the mixing coefficients of the nuclear state and the single-particle wave functions are determined consistently from the same variational principle using the D1S Gogny force, solving coupled equations for the configuration-mixing coefficients and the natural orbitals. The calculations are performed at three levels of self-consistency: (1) a single diagonalization using pure Hartree-Fock orbitals and density, (2) iterative solution of the configuration-mixing equation alone including the correlated density and rearrangement terms, and (3) full self-consistency where both the configuration-mixing and orbital equations are solved together.

## Key numbers

* Correlation energy E_corr = E_HF - E_0 for 20Ne: 10.93 MeV at level 1, 11.54 MeV at level 2, and 13.30 MeV at level 3 (full self-consistency).
* Correlation energy for 28Si: 5.89 MeV at level 1, 6.25 MeV at level 2, and 8.08 MeV at level 3 (full self-consistency), with the orbital optimization adding 1.83 MeV.
* Correlation energy for 24Mg: 14.24 MeV at level 1, 15.06 MeV at level 2, and 16.04 MeV at level 3.
* Weight of the 0p-0h configuration in the ground-state wave function of 20Ne: 45.36% at level 1, 43.05% at level 2, and 33.05% at level 3.
* Weight of the 0p-0h configuration in 28Si: 26.02% at level 1, 38.68% at level 2, and 17.80% at level 3.
* Proton source term coupling between 1s and 0s shells: 2.02 MeV in 24Mg and 2.08 MeV in 28Si at full self-consistency.
* Minimum number of configurations: 418 for 20Ne; maximum number: 56,937 for 28Si.
* Convergence criterion for the one-body density matrix: difference between iterations less than 1.0 x 10^{-5}.

## Context

This work extends the full implementation of the MPMH formalism previously applied to 12C in Robin (2016) to a systematic study of even-even sd-shell nuclei. It builds on earlier partial applications that used frozen Hartree-Fock orbitals (Pillet 2011, Le Bloas 2011) or made approximations in determining single-particle states (Pillet 2008). The approach unifies shell-model and self-consistent mean-field methods without symmetry-breaking and projection techniques, and the results enable future calculations of inelastic electron and proton scattering using the transition densities obtained from the first excited states.
