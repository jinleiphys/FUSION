# Polarization observables in the semiexclusive photoinduced three-body breakup of 3He

> The photon and 3He analyzing powers as well as spin correlation coefficients in the semiexclusive three-body photodisintegration of 3He are investigated for incoming photon laboratory energies E=12, 40 and 120 MeV. The nuclear states are obtained by solving three-body Faddeev equations with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential alone or supplemented with the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force. Explicit pi- and rho-meson exchange currents are taken into account, but we also compare to other models of the electromagnetic current. In some kinematical conditions we have found strong effects of the three-nucleon force for the 3He analyzing power and spin correlation coefficients, as well strong sensitivities to the choice of the currents. This set of predictions should be a useful guidance for the planning of measurements. In addition, we compare our results for two-body 3He breakup induced by polarized photons with a few existing data.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that for the semiexclusive three-body photodisintegration of 3He, the 3He analyzing power and spin correlation coefficients show strong sensitivity to the three-nucleon force (UrbanaIX) and to the choice of the electromagnetic current operator, but only under specific kinematic conditions; the photon analyzing power is largely insensitive to the three-nucleon force except at the highest photon energy studied (120 MeV).

## Method

The authors solved three-body Faddeev equations in momentum space using partial wave decomposition with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential alone or supplemented with the UrbanaIX three-nucleon force. The electromagnetic current operator included explicit pi- and rho-meson exchange currents, and was compared to a single nucleon current and to a Siegert-approach current. The nuclear matrix elements were computed for the semiexclusive reaction where only the outgoing nucleon (proton or neutron) is detected, and the four nonvanishing spin observables (photon analyzing power, 3He analyzing power, and two spin correlation coefficients) were extracted from the amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Photon laboratory energies studied: E_gamma = 12, 40, and 120 MeV
- At E_gamma = 120 MeV, the three-nucleon force changes the photon analyzing power by up to approximately 10% for proton detection at energies around 25-50 MeV
- The 3He analyzing power shows three-nucleon force effects of up to 15% for detected neutrons at E_gamma = 12 MeV, and above approximately 20% for detected protons at E_gamma = 120 MeV
- The spin correlation coefficient C_{x,y}^{gamma,3He} shows three-nucleon force effects of approximately 15% in the whole neutron spectrum at E_gamma = 12 MeV
- Absolute values of C_{x,y}^{gamma,3He} for detected protons stay below approximately 0.25 at E_gamma = 12 MeV and approximately 0.4 at 40 and 120 MeV; for detected neutrons below approximately 0.1 at 12 MeV and approximately 0.25 at 40 and 120 MeV
- Absolute values of C_{y,x}^{gamma,3He} for neutron detection are below approximately 0.1 at E_gamma = 12 and 40 MeV, and approach approximately 0.4 at E_gamma = 120 MeV; for proton detection reach 0.25, 0.5, and 0.25 at E_gamma = 12, 40, and 120 MeV respectively
- At E_gamma = 120 MeV, the difference between single nucleon current predictions and explicit pi- and rho-current results for the photon analyzing power amounts up to 140% (180%) at neutron energy approximately 20 MeV (proton energy approximately 17 MeV)
- For the 3He analyzing power at E_gamma = 40 MeV, differences between Siegert and meson exchange current predictions reach up to approximately 50% for neutron energies around 5-10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of two-body photodisintegration of 3He (Skibinski et al., 2003) to the semiexclusive three-body breakup channel, providing the first published predictions for these polarization observables in that reaction. The calculations use the same Faddeev framework and nuclear potentials (AV18 and UrbanaIX) as earlier work on Nd elastic scattering and radiative capture (Golak et al., 2000; Witala et al., 2001). The results are intended to guide planned experiments using polarized photon beams from Compton backscattering at facilities such as TUNL (Tornow et al., 2003), and the authors compare their two-body breakup predictions to existing data from Belyaev et al. (1986).
