# Radiative hadronization: Photon emission at hadronization from quark-gluon plasma

> We investigate photon emission at the hadronization stage from a quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. A recombination-model picture suggests that a quark and an antiquark bind into a meson state in hadronization, which would apparently violate the energy conservation if there is nothing else involved. We consider here a hadronization process where the recombination accompanies a photon emission. This is an analog of the "{\it radiative recombination}" known in plasma physics, such as $e^- + p^+ \to {\rm H}^0 +γ$, which occurs when an electromagnetic plasma goes back to a neutral atomic gas. The "radiative hadronization" picture will bring about (i) an enhancement of the photon yield, (ii) significant flow of photons similar to that of hadrons, and (iii) the photon transverse momentum ($p_T$) distribution with a thermal profile whose effective temperature is given by blue-shifted temperature of quarks. Here as a simplest and phenomenological realization of the radiative hadronization, we modify the recombination model to involve a photon emission and evaluate the photon yield with this modified model. Adding this contribution to the direct photon yield along with thermal photon contribution calculated with a hydrodynamic model and a parametrized contribution of prompt photons, we study the $p_T$ spectrum and elliptic flow of the photons produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that photon emission during hadronization of quark-gluon plasma, analogous to radiative recombination in electromagnetic plasmas, can contribute to the observed direct photon yield and elliptic flow, potentially helping to resolve the "direct photon puzzle" where hydrodynamic models underpredict both quantities simultaneously. The authors hedge that this is a phenomenological model with an overall normalization parameter κ that must be determined by experimental data, and that the radiative hadronization contribution is subdominant to the original recombination model for hadron yields.

## Method

The authors modify the recombination/coalescence (ReCo) model to include photon emission in the process q + q̄ → M + γ, treating the two-parton system as a "preformed state" with invariant mass M* that decays via photon emission to satisfy energy conservation. They compute the photon yield by multiplying the number of preformed states (evaluated using the original ReCo model with thermal quark distributions and collective flow) by a photon distribution from the decay M* → M + γ, assuming isotropic emission in the preformed-state rest frame and approximating the invariant mass distribution as a delta function at the threshold M* = m₁ + m₂. They add this contribution to thermal photon yields from a hydrodynamic model and a parametrized prompt photon contribution, then study the pT spectrum and elliptic flow v₂ for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperatures from exponential slopes of pT distributions: Teff ∼ 230 MeV at RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV) and Teff ∼ 300 MeV at LHC (√sNN = 2.76 TeV)
- The recombination model predicts proton-to-pion yield ratio Rp/π ∼ 2 at common PT, compared to ∼ 0.2 from parton fragmentation
- The photon distribution from preformed-state decay uses photon energy k₀ = (M*² − M²)/(2M*) in the center-of-mass frame
- The overall factor κ is expected to be of order αem = 1/137 from QED coupling, but left as a free parameter
- The recombination model applies in the intermediate pT region 2 ≲ pT ≲ 5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the recombination/coalescence model approach (Fries 2003, Greco 2003, Molnar 2003, Hwa 2003) to include photon emission at hadronization, addressing the "direct photon puzzle" where hydrodynamic models (Chatterjee 2021, Paquet 2017, Gale 2021) and the PHSD model (Linnyk 2015) underpredict both the photon yield and elliptic flow observed by PHENIX and ALICE. The radiative hadronization picture is analogous to radiative recombination in electromagnetic plasmas (e.g., e⁻ + p⁺ → H⁰ + γ) and to the "radiative return" process in e⁺e⁻ collisions (e⁺ + e⁻ → meson + γ) studied at BaBar and BESIII. This work enables future inclusion of this overlooked photon source in comprehensive models of heavy-ion collisions, potentially resolving discrepancies between theory and experiment for direct photon observables.
