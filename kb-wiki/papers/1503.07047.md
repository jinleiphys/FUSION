# Generalized nuclear contacts and the nucleon's momentum distributions

> The general nuclear contact matrices are defined, taking into consideration all partial waves and finite-range interactions, extending Tan's work for the zero range model. The properties of these matrices are discussed and the relations between the contacts and the one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions are derived. Using these relations, a new asymptotic connection between the one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions, describing the two-body short-range correlations in nuclei, is obtained. Using available numerical data, we extract few connections between the different contacts and verify their relations to the momentum distributions. The numerical data also allows us to identify the main nucleon momentum range affected by two-body short-range correlations. Utilizing these relations and the numerical data, we also verify a previous independent prediction connecting between the Levinger constant and the contacts. This work provides an important indication for the relevance of the contact formalism to nuclear systems, and should open the path for revealing more useful relations between the contacts and interesting quantities of nuclei and nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Tan's contact formalism from ultracold atomic gases to nuclear systems by defining matrices of nuclear contacts that account for all partial waves and finite-range interactions, rather than assuming zero-range s-wave interactions. The authors show that the contact formalism is relevant for nuclear systems in the momentum range 4 fm^{-1} < k < 5 fm^{-1}, which is higher than the range typically studied in short-range correlation experiments.

## Method

The authors define generalized nuclear contact matrices C_{ij}^{αβ} for proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and neutron-proton pairs, incorporating all partial waves (s_2, ℓ_2, j_2, m_2) and finite-range interactions. They derive asymptotic relations connecting these contacts to the one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions for k → ∞. Using Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) numerical data from Wiringa et al. for nuclei with A ≤ 12 (⁴He, ⁶He, ⁸He, ⁶Li, ⁸Be, ¹⁰B), they verify the predicted relations between momentum distributions and extract information about the dominant contact contributions.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic relations between one-nucleon and two-nucleon momentum distributions hold for 4 fm^{-1} < k < 5 fm^{-1} for all studied nuclei (⁴He, ⁶He, ⁸He, ⁶Li, ⁸Be, ¹⁰B)
- The ratio F_{pp}/F_{nn} for non-symmetric nuclei (⁶He, ⁸He) is approximately constant in the range 4 fm^{-1} < k < 5 fm^{-1} and close to the ratio Z/N (number of protons to neutrons)
- The ratio between F_{pp} of different nuclei compared to ⁴He shows flattening in the range 4 fm^{-1} < k < 5 fm^{-1}
- The s-wave spin-singlet and spin-triplet scattering lengths in nuclear systems are about -20 fm and 5.38 fm, respectively, with average inter-nucleon distance about 2.4 fm and pion-exchange range about 1.4 fm

## Context

This work extends Tan's contact formalism (Tan 2008) from ultracold two-component Fermi gases to nuclear systems with four-component fermions (protons and neutrons with spin) and finite-range interactions. It builds on the authors' previous work (Weiss, Bazak, Barnea 2014) where s-wave nuclear contacts were defined and related to the Levinger constant. The paper provides theoretical support for the picture of short-range correlated nucleon pairs with back-to-back momentum, which is a main feature in studies of two-body short-range correlations by Wiringa et al. (2014), Hen et al. (2014), and others. The results enable future extraction of nuclear contacts from experimental data and derivation of relations between contacts and other nuclear observables.
