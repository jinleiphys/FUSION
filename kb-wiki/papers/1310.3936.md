# Isospin mixing within the symmetry restored density functional theory and beyond

> We present results of systematic calculations of the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the superallowed I=$0+,T=1 --> I=0+,T=1 beta-decays, based on the self-consistent isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory (DFT). We discuss theoretical uncertainties of the formalism related to the basis truncation, parametrization of the underlying energy density functional, and ambiguities related to determination of Slater determinants in odd-odd nuclei. A generalization of the double-projected DFT model towards a no core shell-model-like configuration-mixing approach is formulated and implemented. We also discuss new opportunities in charge-symmetry- and charge-independence-breaking studies offered by the newly developed DFT formalism involving proton-neutron mixing in the particle-hole channel.

## Key claim

The isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections to superallowed Fermi beta-decays calculated within the isospin- and angular-momentum-projected density functional theory (DFT) are consistent with the shell-model results of Towner and Hardy (2008) within 2-sigma, but the theoretical uncertainties are larger than previously estimated, particularly due to ambiguities in the reference Slater determinant for odd-odd N=Z nuclei and the limited availability of density-independent Skyrme functionals.

## Method

The authors solve Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) equations without pairing using an isospin-invariant kinetic energy and Skyrme interaction plus the Coulomb force as the only explicit source of isospin violation. They then apply simultaneous isospin and angular-momentum projection operators to the resulting self-consistent Slater determinants, followed by a rediagonalization in the basis of good-isospin good-angular-momentum states to remove spurious isospin mixing. For the dynamical extension, they mix states projected from multiple self-consistent Slater determinants representing low-lying particle-hole excitations by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation in the collective space.

## Key numbers

* The isospin impurities calculated with the isospin-only-projected variant are consistent with data from giant-dipole-resonance decay in 80Zr and isospin-forbidden E1 decay in 64Ge, while standard mean-field results are lower by almost 30 percent due to spurious contaminations.
* The symmetry energy of the SHZ2 functional is a_sym = 42.2 MeV, exceeding the commonly acceptable value by almost 30 percent.
* The two sets of calculated ISB corrections lead to |V_ud| = 0.97397(27) for the SV functional and |V_ud| = 0.97374(27) for the SHZ2 functional, both resulting in CKM matrix unitarity up to 0.1 percent.
* For the 32Cl to 32S Fermi decay between isobaric analogue I=1+ states, the calculated ISB correction is delta_C approximately 6(2) percent, consistent with the anomalously large experimental value of 5.3(9) percent reported by Melconian et al. (2011).
* The estimated errors on stable eigenvalues from the configuration-mixing calculations rarely exceed plus or minus 150 keV.
* The unaccounted isotensor effect in triplet binding energy differences (TED) is approximately 450 keV and appears to be almost A-independent and Skyrme-force independent.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of isospin- and angular-momentum-projected DFT (Satula et al., 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012) to include configuration mixing, addressing the long-standing obstacle that DFT cannot directly compute isospin impurities due to spontaneous isospin violation (Engel, 1970). The results are compared to the shell-model calculations of Towner and Hardy (2008) and are at variance with the RPA-based study of Liang et al. (2009). The new formalism involving proton-neutron mixing in the particle-hole channel enables studies of the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (Nolen and Schiffer, 1969) and the isotensor component of the strong interaction, which is needed to explain triplet binding energy differences.
