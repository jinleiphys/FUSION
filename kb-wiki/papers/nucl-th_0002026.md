# Quark Substructure and Isobar Effects on Deuteron Form-Factors

> Elastic ed scattering, with deuteron polarization, up to high momentum transfer provides detailed information on the deuteron wave function. This determines the range dependence of the orbital and spin components of the one- and two-body currents, restricting contributions of isobar and meson-exchange currents and of quark/gluon degrees of freedom, as well as the nucleon component. The R-matrix boundary condition model combines all these effects, predicting nucleon-nucleon reactions and the deuteron form-factors simultaneously. A brief description of the model is followed by a comparison of its results with data, emphasizing the restrictions placed on the model by ed elastic form-factors.

# Quark Substructure and Isobar Effects on Deuteron Form-Factors

## Key claim

The R-matrix boundary condition model, which combines quark/gluon degrees of freedom, isobar components, and meson-exchange currents, can simultaneously predict nucleon-nucleon reactions and deuteron form-factors, but the full model (Model E) fails to reproduce the magnetic form-factor B(q²) minimum and the tensor-polarization form-factor t₂₀(q²) maximum as well as simpler models do, indicating that the ratio of ΔΔ(⁷D₁) to ΔΔ(³D₁) couplings and the balance of N*(1440) coupling require further optimization.

## Method

The R-matrix boundary condition method connects an interior region (described by quark/gluon degrees of freedom with a Cloudy bag model at separation radius r₀ = 1.05 fm) to an exterior region where hadrons interact via meson exchange potentials with known masses and coupling constants. Three models were compared: Model C' (no quark configurations, r₀ = 0.74 fm), Model D' (including the lowest f-pole from the [q(1s₁/₂)]⁶ configuration), and Model E (adding NN*(1440), NS₁₁(1535), NS₁₁(1650), and ΔS₃₁(1620) channels). The deuteron form-factors A(q²), B(q²), and t₂₀(q²) were calculated using nonrelativistic coupled channel impulse approximation with first-order relativistic corrections and meson-exchange currents including π, ρ, ω, pair corrections, and ρπγ terms.

## Key numbers

- Separation radius r₀ = 1.05 fm for the Cloudy bag model (Models D' and E), compared to r₀ = 0.74 fm for Model C'
- The first f-pole in the NN system lies at 0.76 GeV above the deuteron mass, with width 0.03 GeV
- Interior wave function probability estimated to be ≤ 0.004
- Model E static deuteron properties: binding energy 2.2247 MeV (experimental 2.2246 MeV), D-state probability 5.21%, quadrupole moment 0.273 fm² (experimental 0.286 fm²), magnetic moment 0.860 μ_N (experimental 0.857 μ_N)
- Model E ΔΔ probabilities: ΔΔ(³S₁) 0.006%, ΔΔ(³D₁) 3.24%, ΔΔ(⁷D₁) 1.79%, N*(1440) probability 0.71%
- A(q²) data available up to 6 (GeV/c)², B(q²) data up to 2.8 (GeV/c)², t₂₀(q²) data up to 1.8 (GeV/c)²
- Model E minimum of B(q²) occurs at q² = 1.3 (GeV/c)², which is at much too small a value
- Model E fits A(q²) reasonably well for q² < 2.5 (GeV/c)² with either nucleon form-factor choice, but only large enough at higher q² with the Gari-Krumpelmann choice

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix boundary condition approach previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering (Paul 1986, Pedro 1987) and earlier deuteron form-factor calculations (Green, Lomon 1998) by adding more isobar channels and relativistic corrections. The model contrasts with simpler repulsive core models by using a larger separation radius (2-3 times larger than typical core radii) compensated by discontinuous wave function matching at r₀. The results enable future optimization of the ΔΔ(⁷D₁) to ΔΔ(³D₁) coupling ratio and N*(1440) magnetic moment to improve agreement with the full set of elastic electron-deuteron form-factor data up to 6 (GeV/c)².
