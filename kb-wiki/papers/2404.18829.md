# Disentangling the development of collective flow in high energy proton proton collisions with a multiphase transport model

> In this work, we investigate the collective flow development in high energy proton proton (pp) collisions with a multiphase transport model (AMPT) based on PYTHIA8 initial conditions with a sub-nucleon structure. It is found that the PYTHIA8 based AMPT model can reasonably describe both the charged hadron productions and elliptic flow experimental data measured in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV. By turning on the parton and hadron rescatterings in AMPT separately, we find that the observed collective flow in pp collisions is largely developed during the parton evolution, while no significant flow effect can be generated with the pure hadronic rescatterings. It is also shown that the parton escape mechanism is important for describing both the magnitude of the two-particle cumulant and the sign of the four-particle cumulants. We emphasize that the strong mass ordering of the elliptic flow results from the coalescence process in the transport model and can thus be regarded as unique evidence related to the creation of deconfined parton matter in high energy pp collisions.

## Key claim

The observed collective flow in high-energy proton-proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV is largely developed during the parton evolution stage (parton rescatterings), while pure hadronic rescatterings generate no significant flow effect; the strong mass ordering of elliptic flow results from the coalescence process and can be regarded as unique evidence for the creation of deconfined parton matter in these collisions.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with PYTHIA8/Angantyr initial conditions incorporating sub-nucleon structure (three constituent quarks per proton) to simulate pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV. Parton rescatterings were modeled via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) with a cross section of 0.15 mb, followed by spatial coalescence hadronization and hadron rescatterings via the ART model. Elliptic flow and multi-particle cumulants were calculated using the Q-cumulant method with a pseudorapidity gap |Δη| > 2 and non-flow subtraction using low-multiplicity events (10 < M < 20).

## Key numbers

* Parton rescattering cross section in ZPC set to 0.15 mb to reproduce charged hadron elliptic flow data at √s = 13 TeV
* Reference flow particles defined as charged hadrons within |η| < 2.4 and 0.3 < pT < 3 GeV/c
* Low multiplicity events for non-flow subtraction required reference particle number 10 < M < 20
* High multiplicity events for differential flow analysis required M > 100
* Pseudorapidity gap |Δη| > 2 applied for two-particle cumulant calculations using the sub-event method
* Charged particle density distributions per unit pseudorapidity and transverse momentum spectra at √s = 13 TeV were well described by the model compared to ALICE data (Adam 2015)
* Four-particle cumulants c2{4} become negative at high multiplicity, predominantly from parton evolution stage, qualitatively similar to ATLAS data (ATLAS 2017)

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT studies using HIJING initial conditions (Lin 2004) to a PYTHIA8-based framework with sub-nucleon structure, building on earlier findings that such initial conditions describe multiplicity-dependent identified hadron pT spectra (Zheng 2021). The results contradict models that attribute collectivity solely to initial-state correlations (Schenke 2014) or hadronic final-state interactions (Bierlich 2017), instead supporting the interpretation that a deconfined parton phase is necessary. The finding that parton escape mechanisms are important for both two- and four-particle cumulants enables future discrimination between hydrodynamic and transport descriptions of small-system collectivity.
