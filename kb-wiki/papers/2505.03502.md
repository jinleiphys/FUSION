# Nuclear structure and weak rates of heavy waiting point nuclei under rp-process conditions

> The structure and the weak interaction mediated rates of the heavy waiting point (WP) nuclei $^{80}$Zr, $^{84}$Mo, $^{88}$Ru, $^{92}$Pd and $^{96}$Cd along $N = Z$ line were studied within the interacting boson model-1 (\mbox{IBM-1}) and the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (\mbox{pn-QRPA}). The energy levels of the $N$ = $Z$ WP nuclei were calculated by fitting the essential parameters of \mbox{IBM-1} Hamiltonian and their geometric shapes were predicted by plotting potential energy surfaces (PESs). Half-lives, continuum electron capture rates, positron decay rates, electron capture cross sections of WP nuclei, energy rates of $β$-delayed protons and their emission probabilities were later calculated using the \mbox{pn-QRPA}. The calculated Gamow-Teller strength distributions were compared with previous calculation. We present positron decay $\&$ continuum electron capture rates on these WP nuclei under $rp$-process conditions using the same model. For the $rp$-process conditions, the calculated total weak rates are twice the Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA rates for $^{80}$Zr. For remaining nuclei the two calculations compare well. The electron capture rates are significant and compete well with the corresponding positron decay rates under $rp$-process conditions. The finding of the present study supports that electron capture rates form an integral part of the weak rates under $rp$-process conditions and has an important role for the nuclear model calculations.

# Nuclear structure and weak rates of heavy waiting point nuclei under rp-process conditions

## Key claim
The total weak rates calculated using the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) and proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) are twice the Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA rates for $^{80}$Zr under rp-process conditions, while for the remaining four waiting point nuclei the two calculations compare well. The electron capture rates are significant and compete with positron decay rates under rp-process conditions, supporting that electron capture forms an integral part of the weak rates.

## Method
The authors used the IBM-1 to calculate energy levels and potential energy surfaces for the even-even waiting point nuclei $^{80}$Zr, $^{84}$Mo, $88$Ru, $^{92}$Pd, and $^{96}$Cd along the N=Z line, fitting Hamiltonian parameters to experimental data for known nuclei and to shell model results for $^{96}$Cd. They then employed the pn-QRPA model with a separable Gamow-Teller residual interaction, using deformation parameters from relativistic mean-field calculations and a quenching factor of (0.6)$^2$, to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions, half-lives, continuum electron capture rates, positron decay rates, electron capture cross sections, and beta-delayed proton energy rates and emission probabilities.

## Key numbers
- Total weak rates for $^{80}$Zr under rp-process conditions are twice the Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA rates.
- The interaction strength parameter $\chi$ was optimized at $4.2/A$ to best reproduce experimental half-lives.
- The particle-particle interaction strength constant $\kappa$ was fixed at 0.1 MeV.
- A quenching factor of $f_q^2 = (0.6)^2$ was applied to the pn-QRPA calculated Gamow-Teller strength.
- The energy ratio $R_{4/2} = E(4^+)/E(2^+)$ values are: $^{80}$Zr lies between O(6) and SU(3) limits, $^{84}$Mo is close to O(6) ($R_{4/2} \approx 2.50$), $^{88}$Ru lies between U(5) and O(6) ($R_{4/2}^{EXP}=2.30$, $R_{4/2}^{IBM}=2.29$), and $^{92}$Pd and $^{96}$Cd are close to U(5) ($R_{4/2} \approx 2.00$).
- $^{88}$Ru has an energy ratio $R_{4/2}^{E(5)} = 2.19$, placing it near the E(5) critical point.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Nabi 2016) of waiting point nuclei along the N=Z line by adding electron capture cross section calculations for the first time and completing the investigation of these five nuclei. The results are compared with Gamow-Teller strength distributions calculated by Sarriguren (2011) using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock + BCS + QRPA model, which used a quenching factor of 0.55 versus the 0.6 used here. The calculated weak rates and beta-delayed proton emission probabilities are needed for modeling the rp-process in Type I X-ray bursts, where the lifetimes of waiting point nuclei determine the composition of burst ashes that serve as input for neutron star crust models (Gupta 2007).
