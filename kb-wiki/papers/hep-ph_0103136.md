# Helicity and Transversity Distribution of Nucleon and $Λ$-Hyperon from $Λ$ Fragmentation

> It is shown that $Λ$-hyperon fragmentation in charged lepton deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a polarized nucleon target can provide sensitive information concerning the quark helicity and transversity distributions for both nucleon and $Λ$-hyperon at large $x$. Numerical predictions are given for the spin transfers of the produced $Λ$, when the target nucleon is polarized either longitudinally or transversely, and with the nucleon and $Λ$ quark distributions evaluated both in an SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model and in a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based model. It is also shown that the predicted spin transfers have different behaviors for proton and neutron targets, and this can provide sensitive tests of different predictions for the quark helicity and transversity distributions of the $d$ valence quark of the proton at large $x$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that measuring the spin transfer to Λ-hyperons produced in charged lepton deep inelastic scattering on polarized nucleon targets can distinguish between two competing predictions for the d valence quark helicity and transversity distributions at large x, specifically the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model prediction (Δd/d → -1/3) versus the pQCD counting rule prediction (Δd/d → 1), but only if the target is polarized and the lepton beam is unpolarized, the authors note in an erratum that existing data from HERMES and E665 used a different experimental configuration and therefore do not test their predictions.

## Method

The authors calculate spin transfers A^Λ(x,z) for longitudinal target polarization and Â^Λ(x,z) for transverse target polarization using the standard factorized expressions involving quark distribution functions of the target nucleon and fragmentation functions for quark to Λ. They obtain the quark helicity and transversity distributions for both nucleon and Λ in two models: a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model incorporating Melosh-Wigner rotation effects, and a pQCD-based counting rule model. The fragmentation functions are connected to the Λ quark distributions via the Gribov-Lipatov relation D_q^h(z) ∼ z q_h(z), valid near z → 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful predictions of longitudinal Λ polarizations in e+e- annihilation (ALEPHI 1996, DELPHI 1995, OPAL 1997), polarized DIS (HERMES, E665), and neutrino DIS (NOMAD) to the transverse polarization case, using the same Gribov-Lipatov relation and the two competing models for quark distributions. The approach builds on earlier suggestions by Artru and Mekhfi (1990) and Jaffe (1996) that Λ transverse polarization in DIS on transversely polarized targets can probe quark transversity, and by Lu (1995) for longitudinal polarization probing strange quark polarization. The predictions are intended to guide future experiments at COMPASS, HERMES, and SMC, with the key test being the different behavior of spin transfers for proton versus neutron targets between the two models.
