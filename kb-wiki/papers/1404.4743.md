# Predictions for the Spatial Distribution of Gluons in the Initial Nuclear State

> We make predictions for the t-differential cross section of exclusive vector meson production (EVMP) in electron-ion collisions, with the aim of comparing DGLAP evolution to CGC models. In the current picture for the high-energy nucleus, nonlinear effects need to be understood in terms of low-$x$ gluon radiation and recombination as well as how this leads to saturation. EVMP grants experimental access to the edge region of the highly-boosted nuclear wavefunction, where the saturation scale for CGC calculations becomes inaccessible to pQCD. On the other hand, DGLAP evolution requires careful consideration of unitarity effects. The existing $J/ψ$ photoproduction data in ep collisions provides a baseline for these theoretical calculations. Under different small-$x$ frameworks we obtain a measurable distinction in both the shape and normalization of the differential cross section predictions. These considerations are relevant for heavy ion collisions because the initial state may be further constrained, thus aiding in quantitative study of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that DGLAP evolution and running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) evolution produce measurably different predictions for the t-differential cross section of exclusive vector meson production in electron-ion collisions, but only after first confirming that both approaches give reasonable agreement with existing electron-proton data in the relevant x range.

## Method

The authors compute the t-differential cross section for exclusive vector meson production using the dipole picture, where the amplitude factorizes into a vector-meson photon overlap and a dipole-proton cross section. For the nucleon baseline, they compare the Glauber-Mueller formula with a linearized unitary-cutoff model (theta-CK), using DGLAP evolution via MSTW parton distribution functions and a simplified MNRT parametrization. For nuclear targets, they extend these models by treating the nucleus as a collection of nucleons with Woods-Saxon distributions, comparing smooth nucleus approximations to a "lumpy nucleus" calculation, and also implement the running-coupling BK equation following the numerical model of Albacete (2007).

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for gamma* + p -> V + p is computed as a function of W^2, with mean Q^2 approximately 0.1 GeV^2, consistent with data from ZEUS (2002) and H1 (2005)
- The differential cross section predictions are shown at x = 10^{-3} and x = 10^{-4} for nuclear targets
- The opacity is defined as Omega = r^2 F(x,r) T(b), where F(x,r) = (pi^2 / 2 N_c) alpha_s(mu^2) xg(x,mu^2)
- The momentum scale of the interaction is parameterized as mu^2(r) = C/r^2 + mu_0^2, with best-fit values for C and mu_0 obtained from the data

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture approach of Kowalski and Teaney (2003) and the impact-parameter dependent DGLAP models of Kowalski, Motyka, and Watt (2006) to nuclear targets, comparing them with CGC-based calculations using the running-coupling BK equation from Albacete (2007). The predictions are relevant for future electron-ion collider facilities, as exclusive vector meson production provides a testing ground for distinguishing between DGLAP and CGC evolution of gluon densities. The authors note that including nuclear shadowing effects through newer nPDF software and extending the analysis to phi-meson production (with larger dipole size) would provide more sensitive probes of the saturation region.
