# Is there a "$Δ$-isobar puzzle" in the physics of neutron stars?

> We discuss the formation of $Δ$ isobars in neutron star matter. We show that their threshold density strictly correlates with the density derivative of the symmetry energy of nuclear matter, the $L$ parameter. By restricting $L$ to the range of values indicated by recent experimental and theoretical analysis, i.e. $40$ MeV $\lesssim L \lesssim 62$ MeV, we find that $Δ$ isobars appear at a density of the order of 2$÷$3 times nuclear matter saturation density, i.e. the same range for the appearance of hyperons. The range of values of the couplings of the $Δ$s with the mesons is restricted by the analysis of the data obtained from photoabsorption, electron and pion scattering on nuclei. If the potential of the $Δ$ in nuclear matter is close to the one indicated by the experimental data then the equation of state becomes soft enough that a "$Δ$ puzzle" exists, similar to the "hyperon puzzle" widely discussed in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the density derivative of the symmetry energy (the \(L\) parameter) is restricted to the range 40–62 MeV indicated by recent experiments, then \(\Delta\) isobars appear at densities of 2–3 times nuclear saturation density, the same range as hyperons, and if the \(\Delta\) potential in nuclear matter is close to experimental indications, the equation of state becomes soft enough that a "\(\Delta\) puzzle" exists, analogous to the hyperon puzzle.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field model, first with the GM3 model modified by a density-dependent baryon-\(\rho\) meson coupling to vary the \(L\) parameter, then with the more sophisticated SFHo equation of state (Steiner 2012) that includes self-interaction and mixing terms for vector mesons. They compute threshold densities for \(\Delta\) isobars and hyperons under \(\beta\)-equilibrium conditions, and constrain the \(\Delta\)-meson coupling ratios \(x_{\sigma\Delta}\), \(x_{\omega\Delta}\), and \(x_{\rho\Delta}\) using experimental data from photoabsorption, electron scattering, and pion scattering on nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The \(L\) parameter is constrained to \(40\ \mathrm{MeV} \lesssim L \lesssim 62\ \mathrm{MeV}\) based on the analysis of Lattimer (2012).
- Nuclear matter saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- In the SFHo model (\(L = 47\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), the \(\Delta^-\) appears at a density of about \(0.4\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (approximately \(2.5\, n_0\)).
- The \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi^-\) hyperons appear at about \(0.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) when \(\Delta\)s are excluded; including \(\Delta\)s shifts the \(\Xi^-\) threshold higher by about \(0.15\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The symmetry energy at saturation is \(S = 32\ \mathrm{MeV}\) in the SFHo model.
- The \(\Delta\) potential in nuclear matter falls in the range \(-30\ \mathrm{MeV} + V_N \lesssim V_{\Delta} \lesssim V_N\), where \(V_N\) is the nucleon potential.
- For \(x_{\omega\Delta} \lesssim 1\), the maximum mass of neutron stars including \(\Delta\)s falls below the \(2 M_{\odot}\) limit from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013).
- Radii of maximum-mass configurations with only \(\Delta\)s are \(R \lesssim 10.5\ \mathrm{km}\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the "hyperon puzzle", the inconsistency between the \(2 M_{\odot}\) mass limit and the softening of the equation of state from hyperons, to \(\Delta\) isobars, which were previously thought to appear only at densities too high to affect neutron stars (Glendenning 1984). The authors show that recent constraints on the symmetry energy derivative (Lattimer 2012) force \(\Delta\) isobars to appear at the same densities as hyperons, creating a similar puzzle. The paper enables future work to incorporate \(\Delta\) isobars into neutron star equations of state and to refine their couplings through new experiments, particularly on neutron-rich nuclei.
