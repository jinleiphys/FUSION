# Constraining isovector nuclear interactions with giant dipole resonance and neutron skin in $^{208}$Pb from a Bayesian approach

> The remaining uncertainties of isovector nuclear interactions call for reliable experimental measurements of isovector probes in finite nuclei. Based on the Bayesian analysis, although the neutron-skin thickness data or the isovector giant dipole resonance data in $^{208}$Pb can constrain only one isovector interaction parameter, correlations between other parameters are built. Using combined data of both the neutron-skin thickness and the isovector giant dipole resonance helps to constrain significantly all isovector interaction parameters, thus serves as a useful way in the future analysis.

## Key claim

Using combined data of both the neutron-skin thickness and the isovector giant dipole resonance in $^{208}$Pb helps to constrain significantly all isovector interaction parameters, whereas each observable alone can constrain only one parameter individually, though correlations between other parameters are built.

## Method

The author employed the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model to calculate nucleon density distributions and the neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb, and the random-phase approximation (RPA) method to compute the strength function of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR), from which the centroid energy $E_{-1}$ and electric polarizability $\alpha_D$ were obtained. A Bayesian analysis using the Markov-Chain Monte Carlo approach with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm was then applied to derive posterior probability distribution functions of the isovector interaction parameters $E_{sym}^0$, $L$, and $m_v^\star$ from imagined neutron-skin thickness data and real IVGDR data.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density from a 2016 survey of 53 analyses: $E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0)=31.7 \pm 3.2$ MeV.
- The slope parameter of the symmetry energy from the same survey: $L = 58.7 \pm 28.1$ MeV.
- The IVGDR centroid energy in $^{208}$Pb: $E_{-1}=13.46$ MeV, measured from photoneutron scatterings.
- The electric polarizability of $^{208}$Pb: $\alpha_D=19.6 \pm 0.6$ fm$^3$, from polarized proton inelastic scatterings.
- From neutron-skin thickness data alone ($\Delta r_{np}=0.15 \pm 0.02$ fm), $L$ is constrained to $35^{+19}_{-26}$ MeV at 68% confidence level around its maximum a posteriori (MAP) value.
- From neutron-skin thickness data alone ($\Delta r_{np}=0.20 \pm 0.02$ fm), $L$ is constrained to $74^{+17}_{-25}$ MeV at 68% confidence level around its MAP value.
- From IVGDR data alone, $m_v^\star/m$ is constrained to $0.79\pm0.04$ at 68% confidence level around its MAP value.
- Combining $\Delta r_{np}=0.15 \pm 0.02$ fm and IVGDR data reduces the MAP value of $L$ from about 35 MeV to 31 MeV.
- Combining $\Delta r_{np}=0.20 \pm 0.02$ fm and IVGDR data reduces the MAP value of $L$ from about 74 MeV to 55 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior Bayesian analyses of isovector nuclear interactions by demonstrating that combining neutron-skin thickness and IVGDR data from the same nucleus ($^{208}$Pb) can simultaneously constrain the symmetry energy $E_{sym}^0$, its slope $L$, and the isovector nucleon effective mass $m_v^\star$, whereas each observable alone constrains only one parameter. The study builds on earlier constraints of the symmetry energy from surveys of terrestrial and astrophysical data (BAL13, Oer17) and on previous work linking the IVGDR to the symmetry energy (Tri08, Rei10, Pie12, Vre12, Roc13b, Col14, Roc15, zhangzhen15, zhenghua16, Xu20a) and the nucleon effective mass (Zha16, Kon17). The results call for more reliable experimental measurements of the neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb, such as those anticipated from the Jefferson Laboratory and the Mainz energy recovery superconducting accelerator (Thiel).
