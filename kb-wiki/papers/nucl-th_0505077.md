# New Treatment of Resonances with Bound State Approximation by Using Pseudo Potential

> We propose a new approach to extract the wave functions of resonances by the bound state approximation which gives the mixed states of the resonance components and the continuum ones. In our approach, on the basis of the method of analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC), we construct Padé rational function by adopting the positive energies as well as the negative ones. We report the result of the application of this new method to the second $2^+$ state of $^{12}$C which was studied with the ACCC method in our previous work. It is found that the resonance parameters obtained by the ACCC method are well reproduced by the new method. Some advantages over the ACCC method are also shown.

## Key claim

The authors propose an extended bound-state approximation that can extract resonance wave functions and widths by using a pseudo potential with a weak coupling constant, avoiding the need to convert the resonance into a bound state as required by the original analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) method.

## Method

The method adds an attractive pseudo potential \(V\) to the original Hamiltonian, \(H'(\delta) = H + \delta V\), and diagonalizes it with square-integrable basis functions. For coupling constants \(\delta\) where the resonance state mixes strongly with continuum states, the pure resonance component is extracted by diagonalizing the squared-radius operator \(\sum r_j^2\) over the mixed eigenstates and selecting the eigenvector with the smallest eigenvalue. The resonance energies obtained at several \(\delta\) values (both in the bound-state region \(\delta > \delta_0\) and in the resonance region \(\delta < \delta_0\)) are then used to determine the coefficients of a Pade rational function for the complex eigen-energy via a frictional cooling method, from which the resonance width is obtained.

## Key numbers

* The calculated resonance energy of the \(2_2^+\) state of \(^{12}\)C, measured from the \(3\alpha\) threshold, is \(1.59\) MeV using the new method, compared to \(1.55\) MeV from the original ACCC method.
* The calculated width is \(0.15\) MeV using the new method, compared to \(0.10\) MeV from the original ACCC method.
* The experimental values for this state are \(2.6 \pm 0.3\) MeV above the \(3\alpha\) threshold with a width of \(1.0 \pm 0.3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the ACCC method previously applied by the same authors (Funaki et al., 2005) to the \(2_2^+\) state of \(^{12}\)C. The new method allows the use of both positive and negative energy eigenstates from the bound-state approximation to construct the Pade approximant, whereas the original ACCC method could only use bound-state energies. This enables the extraction of resonance wave functions, which is not possible with the original ACCC method, and requires less numerical precision than the original approach.
