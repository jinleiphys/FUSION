# $P$- and $T$-odd two-nucleon interaction and the deuteron electric dipole moment

> The nuclear physics relevant to the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the deuteron is addressed. The general operator structure of the $P$- and $T$-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction is discussed and applied to the two-body contributions of the deuteron EDM, which can be calculated in terms of $P$- and $T$-odd meson-nucleon coupling constants with only small model dependence. The one-body contributions, the EDMs of the proton and the neutron, are evaluated within the same framework. Although the total theoretical uncertainties are sizable, we conclude that, compared to the neutron, the deuteron EDM is competitive in terms of sensitivity to $C P$ violation, and complementary with respect to the microscopic sources of $C P$ violation that can be probed.

## Key claim

The deuteron electric dipole moment (EDM) is competitive with the neutron EDM in sensitivity to CP violation and complementary regarding the microscopic sources of CP violation that can be probed, although the total theoretical uncertainties are sizable.

## Method

The authors constructed the general operator structure of the P- and T-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction from symmetry considerations and one-meson exchange (π, η, ρ, ω). They calculated the two-body contributions to the deuteron EDM using this potential in combination with three high-quality local potential models (Argonne v18, Reid93, and Nijmegen II), and evaluated the one-body contributions (proton and neutron EDMs) within the same meson-exchange framework using hadronic loop diagrams.

## Key numbers

- For the two-body polarization contribution using Argonne v18: d_D^(pol) = 1.43×10⁻² Ḡ_π^(1) + 1.59×10⁻³ Ḡ_η^(1) + 6.25×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_ρ^(1) − 5.96×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_ω^(1) (in e·fm units)
- For the two-body polarization contribution using Reid93: d_D^(pol) = 1.45×10⁻² Ḡ_π^(1) + 1.68×10⁻³ Ḡ_η^(1) + 6.83×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_ρ^(1) − 6.53×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_ω^(1)
- For the two-body polarization contribution using Nijm II: d_D^(pol) = 1.47×10⁻² Ḡ_π^(1) + 1.72×10⁻³ Ḡ_η^(1) + 7.50×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_ρ^(1) − 7.19×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_ω^(1)
- The exchange current contribution: d_D^(ex) ≃ 9.40×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_π^(1) − 5.28×10⁻⁴ Ḡ_π^(0)
- The total two-body contribution: d_D^(2) ≃ 0.20 ḡ_π^(1) + O(ḡ_π^(0), ḡ_η,ρ,ω^(1)), with error estimated as less than 5%
- The total one-body contribution: d_D^(1) = 0.03 ḡ_π^(1) + 0.09 ḡ_π^(0) + 0.04 ḡ_ρ^(1) + 0.01 ḡ_ω^(0) + O(ḡ_η^(0,1))
- The final deuteron EDM estimate: d_D = (0.20 + 0.03) ḡ_π^(1) + 0.09 ḡ_π^(0) + 0.04 ḡ_ρ^(1) + 0.01 ḡ_ω^(0)

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the deuteron EDM by Avishai (1985) and Khriplovich and Korkin (1999), providing a more complete treatment with modern nucleon-nucleon potentials and including both one- and two-body contributions within a consistent meson-exchange framework. The results enable microscopic calculations of EDMs for more complex systems such as the mercury atom, and support the proposed storage-ring experiment to measure the deuteron EDM at the 10⁻²⁷ e·cm level.
