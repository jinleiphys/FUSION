# Statistical Mechanics of Quarkyonic Matter

> We extend the theoretical formulation of Quarkyonic Matter within the IdylliQ model framework proposed in [Y. Fujimoto et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 132, 112701 (2024) [1]] for zero temperature to non-zero temperatures. To this end, we develop a consistent statistical mechanics and grand canonical ensemble description of Quarkyonic Matter as a quantum system subject to additional inequality constraints due to the Pauli exclusion principle acting simultaneously on baryons and their constituent quarks. These constraints result in a significant reduction in the number of physically available baryon states compared to an ideal Fermi gas. As a consequence, the one-particle baryon distribution function factorizes into a thermal Fermi-Dirac distribution and a momentum-dependent density of states. This separation allows us to derive a proper definition of the entropy density that satisfies the third law of thermodynamics in the zero-temperature limit. Moreover, we find that inside Quarkyonic Matter the physical temperature and the physical baryon chemical potential differ from the Lagrange multipliers appearing in the Fermi-Dirac distribution which may have important consequences for the thermodynamics of Quarkyonic Matter.

## Key claim

The authors extend the IdylliQ model of Quarkyonic Matter from zero to non-zero temperature by developing a statistical mechanics framework where the baryon distribution function factorizes into a thermal Fermi-Dirac distribution and a momentum-dependent density of states, resolving the problem that a naive extension would produce non-vanishing entropy at zero temperature in violation of the third law of thermodynamics.

## Method

The authors construct a grand canonical ensemble for a quantum gas of baryons subject to an additional external constraint from the Pauli exclusion principle acting simultaneously on quarks confined inside baryons. This constraint is implemented by introducing a projector factor \(g_i\) that eliminates low-lying baryonic states forbidden by quark saturation, leading to a modified partition function and a momentum-dependent density of states \(g(k)\) in the thermodynamic limit. The density of states is then determined by maximizing the entropy density under inequality constraints from both baryonic and quark Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

* For \(N_c=3\) colors, \(\Lambda=0.3\) GeV, \(d=4\) degrees of freedom, and nucleon mass \(M_N=0.94\) GeV, the zero-temperature baryon distribution function in Quarkyonic Matter has a bulk part suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_c^3 = 1/27\) for momenta below \(k_{\rm bu}\).
* The onset of Quarkyonic Matter for neutron star physics is expected at densities around 1.5–1.8 times nuclear saturation density, based on constraints from chiral effective field theory and the GW170817 tidal deformability observation.
* The effective confinement scale parameter \(\Lambda\) is set to 0.3 GeV for all numerical results shown in the paper.

## Context

This work extends the IdylliQ model framework originally proposed by Fujimoto et al. (2024) for zero-temperature Quarkyonic Matter to finite temperatures, going beyond earlier ad hoc thermal treatments by Sen (2020) and Folias (2025) that lacked a microscopic statistical-mechanical foundation. The formalism enables systematic calculation of thermodynamic quantities including entropy density, physical temperature, and physical baryon chemical potential in Quarkyonic Matter, with implications for understanding the rapid stiffening of the equation of state needed to explain neutron star observations and the hyperon puzzle.
