# Eigenstate Thermalization in 2+1 dimensional SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory

> We present preliminary numerical evidence for the hypothesis that the Hamiltonian SU(2) gauge theory discretized on a lattice obeys the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (ETH). To do so we study three approximations: (a) a linear plaquette chain in a reduced Hilbert space limiting the electric field basis to $j=0,\frac{1}{2}$ , (b) a two-dimensional honeycomb lattice with periodic or closed boundary condition and the same Hilbert space constraint, and (c) a chain of only three plaquettes but such a sufficiently large electric field Hilbert space ($j \leq \frac{7}{2})$ that convergence of all energy eigenvalues in the analyzed energy window is observed. While an unconstrained Hilbert space is required to reach the continuum limit of SU(2) gauge theory, numerical resource constraints do not permit us to realize this requirement for all values of the coupling constant and large lattices. In each of the three studied cases we check first for random matrix theory (RMT) behavior in the eigenenergy spectrum and then analyze the diagonal as well as the off-diagonal matrix elements between energy eigenstates for a few operators. Within current uncertainties all results for (a), (b) and (c) agree with ETH predictions. Furthermore, we find the off-diagonal matrix elements of the electric energy operator exhibit RMT behavior in frequency windows that are small enough in (b) and (c). To unambiguously establish ETH behavior and determine for which class of operators it applies, an extension of our investigations is necessary.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary numerical evidence that Hamiltonian SU(2) lattice gauge theory obeys the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis (ETH), but only within the constraints of truncated Hilbert spaces and small lattices; the authors explicitly caution that an unconstrained Hilbert space is required to reach the continuum limit and that unambiguous establishment of ETH behavior requires further investigation.

## Method

The authors study three approximations of 2+1 dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian: (a) a linear plaquette chain with electric field basis truncated to j=0,1/2, mapped onto an Ising spin chain; (b) a two-dimensional honeycomb lattice with the same j_max=1/2 truncation under periodic or closed boundary conditions; and (c) a three-plaquette chain with a larger electric field cutoff j_max=7/2 to achieve convergence of energy eigenvalues. For each system, they diagonalize the Hamiltonian numerically, check for random matrix theory (GOE) behavior in the eigenenergy spectrum via level spacing statistics and the gap ratio r_alpha, and analyze the diagonal and off-diagonal matrix elements of operators such as the electric energy operator, fitting the spectral function f(E,omega) and testing the GOE measure Lambda^T.

## Key numbers

* For the N=19 plaquette chain with j_max=1/2 and g^2=1.2, the mean restricted energy gap ratio is <r> = 0.5340, compared to the GOE prediction of approximately 0.5307.
* The level density in the k=1 momentum sector of the N=19 chain is fit by rho(E) = A/cosh^2[(E-E_tilde)/Delta] with A=2250, Delta=6.276, E_tilde=0.342 (in units of a^{-1}).
* The spectral function f_el(E,omega) for the electric energy operator in the N=19 chain is fit by f(E,omega) = a/(omega^2+b^2) + c for small |omega| < 0.1 (in units of a^{-1}) across three energy windows.
* The GOE measure Lambda^T for the electric energy operator in the N=19 chain reaches values of approximately 0.45-0.47 for the highest energy window (2 < E < 3), closest to the GOE limit of 0.5.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quantum chaos in SU(2) lattice gauge theory, which established classical ergodicity (Bolte 1999) and random matrix theory behavior in Euclidean gauge theories (Berbenni-Bitsch 1997, Halasz 1998, Verbaarschot 2000). It builds on the mapping of the truncated SU(2) gauge theory onto an Ising spin chain proposed by Yao (2023) and the honeycomb lattice formulation by Muller (2023). The results enable future investigations into whether ETH holds for gauge-invariant operators in the continuum limit, and whether thermalization times for different operators (e.g., shear viscosity-related modes in 2D) exhibit distinct behavior, as suggested by holographic calculations (Balasubramanian 2010, 2011).
