# Progress in correlation femtoscopy

> Recent results on particle momentum and spin correlations are discussed, particularly, in view of the role played by the effect of final state interaction. It is demonstrated that this effect allows for (i) correlation femtoscopy with unlike particles; (ii) study of the relative space--time asymmetries in the production of different particle species (including relative time delays); (iii) study of the particle strong interaction hardly accessible by other means (e.g., in $ΛΛ$ system).

# Progress in correlation femtoscopy

## Key claim

Final state interaction (FSI) effects in particle momentum and spin correlations enable correlation femtoscopy with unlike particles, the study of relative space-time asymmetries in particle production (including relative time delays), and access to particle strong interaction properties that are otherwise difficult to measure, but the analysis requires careful treatment of non-Gaussian tails in the emission source distribution and model-dependent scaling of emission coordinates.

## Method

The two-particle correlation function is calculated as the square of the properly symmetrized stationary solution of the scattering problem, averaged over the relative distance of the emitters in the pair center-of-mass system and over the pair total spin. For unlike particles, transport code simulations (specifically RQMD v.2.3) are used to account for the dynamical evolution of the emission process, with the space-time coordinates of emission points scaled by factors of 0.7, 0.8, and 1.0 to fit the correlation data. For systems with poorly known strong interactions (such as Lambda-Lambda), the correlation function is analyzed using analytical expressions that depend on s-wave scattering amplitudes, assuming the effective emission radius is much larger than the range of the strong interaction potential.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV (NA49), RQMD overestimates the relative distances r* by 10-20% based on fits of pi+pi-, pi+p and pi-p correlation functions
- Combined (4, 6, and 8 AGeV) p-Lambda correlation function from Au+Au experiment E985 at AGS yields a Gaussian radius r0 = 4.5 +/- 0.7 fm, consistent with radii of 3-4 fm from pp correlations at GSI, AGS, and SPS energies
- For the pi+pi- system in NA49 data, the fitted strong interaction scale (redefining the original scattering length f0 = 0.232 fm) appeared significantly lower than unity: 0.63 +/- 0.08
- For the Lambda-Lambda system in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, the singlet s-wave scattering length f0 is estimated as 2.4 +/- 2.1 fm for r0 = 2 fm and 3.2 +/- 5.7 fm for r0 = 4 fm
- For pi-p systems in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, RQMD yields <Delta x> = -5.2 fm, <Delta t> = 2.9 fm/c, and <Delta x*> = -8.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the foundations of correlation femtoscopy established by Kopylov and Podgoretsky (1972), who clarified the role of space-time characteristics in particle production and suggested studying interference effects in terms of the correlation function. The paper reviews recent results that build on earlier formalism for final state interaction effects (Koonin, 1977; Lednicky and Lyuboshitz, 1981) and the extension to unlike particle correlations (Lednicky and Lyuboshitz, 1981; Biyajima, 1986). The results enable further study of collective flows in heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC, investigation of quark-gluon plasma phase transition effects through correlation asymmetries, and measurement of strong interaction parameters for exotic systems like Lambda-Lambda that are otherwise inaccessible.
