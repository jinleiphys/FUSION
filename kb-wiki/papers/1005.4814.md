# The phase diagram of dense QCD

> Current status of theoretical researches on the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential is reviewed with special emphasis on the origin of various phases and their symmetry breaking patterns. Topics include; quark deconfinement, chiral symmetry restoration, order of the phase transitions, QCD critical point(s), colour superconductivity, various inhomogeneous states and implications from QCD-like theories.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current theoretical understanding of the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, emphasizing that relatively firm statements can be made only in limited cases, the phase structure at finite temperature with small baryon density (μB ≪ T) and that at asymptotically high density (μB ≫ ΛQCD), while the intermediate density region remains largely conjectural and not fully established.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches including lattice QCD simulations, effective chiral models, the Ginzburg-Landau-Wilson renormalization group analysis, weak-coupling QCD methods at asymptotically high density, and large-Nc arguments. They classify phases according to symmetry breaking patterns using order parameters: the Polyakov loop for deconfinement, the chiral condensate for chiral symmetry restoration, and the diquark condensate for colour superconductivity.

## Key numbers

- The QCD scale ΛQCD ≈ 200 MeV sets the intrinsic scale for phase transitions.
- The Hagedorn temperature TH ≈ 0.19 GeV (190 MeV) from the Regge slope parameter.
- The chiral condensate in vacuum is about −(0.24 GeV)^3.
- The pion decay constant fπ ≈ 93 MeV.
- The π-N sigma term σπN ≈ 40 MeV.
- The critical temperature for deconfinement in pure gluonic theory (Nf=0) is Tc ≈ 270 MeV.
- The pseudo-critical temperature Tpc for crossover with realistic quark masses lies in the range 150–200 MeV.
- The normal nuclear matter density n0 ≈ 0.17 fm^(−3).
- The nucleon mass mN ≈ 939 MeV; the binding energy in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is around 16 MeV.
- The threshold chemical potential for nuclear matter at T=0 is μNM ≈ 924 MeV.
- The liquid-gas critical point temperature TG = 15–20 MeV.
- The transitional point (μH, TH) where baryon importance surpasses mesons occurs around μH = 350–400 MeV and TH = 150–160 MeV from Statistical Model analysis.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the QCD phase diagram including the Hagedorn/percolation picture (Hagedorn 1965), chiral symmetry restoration studies (Hatsuda 1985), and colour superconductivity analyses (Rajagopal 2000, Alford 2007). It synthesizes results from lattice QCD simulations (Aoki 2006, DeTar 2009), effective model predictions for the QCD critical point (Asakawa 1989, Barducci 1989, Berges 1998), and large-Nc arguments for quarkyonic matter (McLerran 2007). The review enables future experimental searches at facilities such as FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC, and guides theoretical work on the intermediate-density region where inhomogeneous phases and the quark-hadron continuity remain open questions.
