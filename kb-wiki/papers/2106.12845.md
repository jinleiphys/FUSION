# Influence of the crust on the neutron star macrophysical quantities and universal relations

> Measurements of neutron-star macrophysical properties thanks to multimessenger observations offer the possibility to constrain the properties of nuclear matter. Indeed cold and dense matter as found inside neutron stars, in particular in their core, is not accessible to terrestrial laboratories.We investigate the consequences of using equations of state that employ models for the core and the crust that are not calculated consistently on the neutron-star macrophysical properties, on some of the so-called universal relations and on the constraints obtained from gravitational-wave observations. We use various treatments found in the literature to connect together nonconsistent core and crust equations of state. We then compute the mass, the radius, the tidal deformability, and the moment of inertia for each model. Finally, we assess the discrepancies in the neutron-star macrophysical properties obtained when consistent models for the whole star and nonconsistent ones are employed. The use of crust models nonconsistent with the core introduces an error on the macrophysical parameters which can be as large as the estimated accuracy of current and next generation telescopes. The precision of some of the universal relations reported in the literature is found to be overestimated. We confirm that the equation of the crust has limited influence on the macrophysical properties. The discrepancy between results obtained for a fully consistent equation of state and a nonconsistent one can be reduced if one connects the core and the crust models at baryon densities around 0.08-0.1 fm$^{-3}$. The equation of the crust cannot be probed with current multimessenger observations and near-future ones.

## Key claim

The use of crust models that are not thermodynamically consistent with the core equation of state introduces errors in neutron-star macrophysical parameters (radius, tidal deformability, moment of inertia) that can be as large as the estimated accuracy of current and next-generation telescopes, but these discrepancies can be minimized if the core and crust are connected at baryon densities around 0.08-0.1 fm$^{-3}$.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron-star equations of state by gluing three different relativistic mean-field core models (NL3, BSR6, DD2) to the DH crust model at various baryon-number densities (the core-crust transition density, n0, n0/2, and 0.1 fm$^{-3}$), as well as using a cubic spline interpolation method. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute the mass, radius, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia for each matched equation of state, then compared these results to those obtained from fully unified equations of state where the same nuclear model describes both core and crust. They also tested the influence of different crust models (DH, BSk21 catalyzed, BSk21 accreted) and evaluated how the matching procedure affects the precision of several universal relations between compactness, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia reported in the literature.

## Key numbers

- Relative differences in radius between matched and unified equations of state reach up to approximately 5% for NL3 core matched to DH crust, and up to 10% (or 1.5 km for M ≈ 1 M⊙) when using a cubic spline matching
- Relative differences in tidal deformability reach up to approximately 20% for NL3 core, 10% for BSR6 core, and 6% for DD2 core when using nonconsistent matching
- Relative differences in moment of inertia reach up to approximately 10% for NL3 core, 3% for BSR6 core, and 1.5% for DD2 core
- The maximum masses of the three core models are: NL3 = 2.77 M⊙, DD2 = 2.42 M⊙, BSR6 = 2.43 M⊙
- The Yagi and Yunes fit for the Λ-C relation gives a maximum relative error of approximately 3% across all matchings, while the Maselli et al. fit gives up to approximately 6%
- The Yagi and Yunes fit for the Λ-I relation gives a maximum relative difference of at most 0.4%, while the Maselli et al. fit reaches up to 7% for the stiffest equation of state

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Fortin (2016) on the influence of crust-core matching on neutron-star radii, now including tidal deformability and moment of inertia, and systematically testing the impact on universal relations from Maselli (2013), Yagi and Yunes (2017), Breu and Rezzolla (2016), and Zhao and Lattimer (2018). The results confirm that the crust equation of state has limited influence on macrophysical properties and cannot be probed with current or near-future multimessenger observations, but they show that the precision of some universal relations reported in the literature is overestimated when nonconsistent equations of state are used. The findings enable more reliable uncertainty quantification when using nonunified equations of state for interpreting gravitational-wave observations from LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA and X-ray observations from NICER, Athena, and eXTP.
