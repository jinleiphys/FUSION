# Benchmark of many-body approaches for magnetic dipole transition strength

> The low-energy enhancement observed recently in the deexcitation gamma-ray strength functions, suggested to arise due to the magnetic dipole radiation, motivates theoretical efforts to improve the description of M1 strength in available nuclear structure models. Reliable theoretical predictions of nuclear dipole excitations are of interest for different nuclear applications and in particular for nuclear astrophysics, where the calculations of radiative capture cross sections often resort to theoretical strength functions. We aim to benchmark many-body methods in their description of the M1 strength functions, with a special emphasis on the low-energy effects observed in the deexcitation strength. We investigate the zero-temperature and finite-temperature magnetic dipole strength functions computed within the quasiparticle random-phase approximation and compare them to those from exact diagonalizations of the same Hamiltonian in restricted orbital spaces. The study is carried out for a sample of 25 spherical and deformed nuclei which can be described by diagonalization of the respective effective Hamiltonian in three different valence spaces. A reasonable agreement is found for the total photoabsorption strengths while the QRPA distributions are shown to be systematically shifted down in energy with respect to exact results. Photoemission strengths obtained within the FT-QRPA appear insufficient to explain the low-energy enhancement of the M1 strength functions. The problems encountered in QRPA calculations are ascribed to the lack of correlations in the nuclear ground state and to the truncation of the many-body space. In particular, the latter prevents obtaining the sufficiently high level density to produce the low-energy enhancement of the strength function, making the (FT-)QRPA approach unsuitable for predictions of such effects across the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and its finite-temperature extension (FT-QRPA) systematically underestimate the centroid energy of M1 strength distributions compared to exact shell-model diagonalizations using the same Hamiltonian and model space, and the FT-QRPA photoemission strength is insufficient to explain the low-energy enhancement of M1 strength functions observed in exact calculations.

## Method

The authors computed zero-temperature and finite-temperature magnetic dipole strength functions using the QRPA and FT-QRPA approaches, implemented via the Finite Amplitude Method, and compared them to results from exact Lanczos diagonalizations of the same effective Hamiltonians in three valence spaces (USDb for the 1s0d shell, LNPS for the 1p0f shell, and GCN5082 for the 0g7/2 1d 2s1/2 0h11/2 shell). The study covered 25 spherical and deformed even-even nuclei with masses from A=26 to A=136, using the same M1 operator with bare orbital g-factors and spin g-factors quenched by a factor of 0.75 in all approaches.

## Key numbers

* Total M1 photoabsorption strengths from QRPA and shell model agree within 20% for the majority of the 25 nuclei studied, with the largest discrepancy in 108Xe where QRPA misses nearly twice the shell-model strength.
* The QRPA centroid energies are systematically shifted to lower energies than shell-model centroids across all three model spaces, with the shift not correlated with quadrupole deformation.
* In spherical 32S, the RPA (zero-pairing limit of QRPA) gives a single dominant peak at 11.1 MeV, while the shell model with restricted 0p-0h ground state and 1p-1h excitations gives the major peak at 11.4 MeV.
* In 56Ni, the RPA gives two peaks with the dominant one at 10.82 MeV carrying 99% of the M1 strength, in good agreement with the restricted-space diagonalization.
* Ground state energy differences between HFB and exact diagonalization range from -1.25 MeV (132Te) to -8.56 MeV (64Ge).

## Context

This work extends the earlier benchmark of Stetcu (2003), which compared RPA and shell-model results for Gamow-Teller, spin-flip, and quadrupole transitions in a few sd-shell and pf-shell nuclei, by including pairing via QRPA, covering a wider mass range (A=20 to 136), and focusing on the M1 operator. The study directly addresses the low-energy enhancement (LEE) of gamma-ray strength functions observed in Oslo experiments and previously reproduced by shell-model calculations (Schwengner 2010, Sieja 2015), showing that the (FT-)QRPA approach is unsuitable for predicting such effects across the nuclear chart due to insufficient level density from the truncation of the many-body space. The results motivate development of computationally feasible methods that include higher-order correlations beyond QRPA, such as second RPA or quasiparticle-vibration coupling, to achieve reliable microscopic predictions of M1 strength functions for nuclear astrophysics applications.
