# Triton-$^3$He relative and differential flows as probes of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities

> Using a transport model coupled with a phase-space coalescence after-burner we study the triton-$^3$He ratio, relative and differential transverse flows in semi-central $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ reactions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/A. The neutron-proton ratio, relative and differential flows are also discussed as a reference. We find that similar to the neutron-proton pairs the triton-$^3$He pairs also carry interesting information about the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. Moreover, the nuclear symmetry energy affects more strongly the t-$^3$He relative and differential flows than the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in the same reaction. The t-$^3$He relative flow can be used as a particularly powerful probe of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim
The triton-³He relative transverse flow is a more sensitive probe of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy than the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in the same reaction, and can be used to test the soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities previously inferred from FOPI π⁻/π⁺ data (Xiao 2009). This claim is conditional on the use of a specific transport model (IBUU04) coupled to a simple phase-space coalescence after-burner, and on the choice of two extreme symmetry energy parameterizations (x=1 for soft, x=-1 for stiff).

## Method
The authors used the IBUU04 isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model with a modified Gogny effective interaction (MDI) single-nucleon potential, coupled to a simple phase-space coalescence after-burner (with coalescence parameters P₀=263 MeV/c and R₀=3 fm) to simulate semi-central ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reactions at 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter 5 fm. They calculated the transverse collective flows of tritons and ³He individually, then constructed the relative flow (difference of mean transverse momenta per nucleon) and differential flow (weighted difference) for t-³He pairs, comparing them to the corresponding n-p flows and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio.

## Key numbers
- The slope F of the t-³He relative flow at mid-rapidity is approximately -74 MeV/c for the soft symmetry energy (x=1) and approximately -22 MeV/c for the stiff symmetry energy (x=-1).
- The slope F of the t-³He differential flow at mid-rapidity is approximately 21 MeV/c for x=1 and approximately 42 MeV/c for x=-1.
- The slope F of the n-p relative flow at mid-rapidity is approximately -53 MeV/c for x=1 and approximately -25 MeV/c for x=-1.
- The slope F of the n-p differential flow at mid-rapidity is approximately 20 MeV/c for x=1 and approximately 36 MeV/c for x=-1.
- The effect of the symmetry energy on the total π⁻/π⁺ yield ratio is about 10% when varying x from -1 to 1 in this reaction, compared to about 20% in head-on Au+Au collisions at the same beam energy.
- The maximum density reached in the ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon and b=5 fm is about 2ρ₀ (where ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³).
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is about 31.6 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier finding by Xiao (2009) that the FOPI π⁻/π⁺ data favor a soft symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, by proposing an independent observable (t-³He relative flow) that shows stronger sensitivity to the symmetry energy than the pion ratio. It also quantitatively addresses the question, previously raised by experimentalists, of whether t-³He pairs carry the same information as n-p pairs, which had been proposed as a probe by Li (2000). The results enable future experimental tests at FAIR/GSI using the ¹³²Sn+¹²⁴Sn reaction, and can help resolve the tension between the soft symmetry energy indicated by FOPI pion data and the stiffer symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities favored by NSCL/MSU isospin diffusion data (Chen 2004, Li 2005).
