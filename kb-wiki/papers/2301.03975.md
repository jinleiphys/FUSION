# Nuclear $β$ decay as a probe for physics beyond the Standard Model

> This white paper was submitted to the 2022 Fundamental Symmetries, Neutrons, and Neutrinos (FSNN) Town Hall Meeting in preparation for the next NSAC Long Range Plan. We advocate to support current and future theoretical and experimental searches for physics beyond the Standard Model using nuclear $β$ decay.

## Key claim

This white paper advocates for sustained support of theoretical and experimental searches for physics beyond the Standard Model using nuclear beta decay, arguing that the field is at an opportune moment where recent theoretical advances in ab initio many-body methods and radiative corrections, combined with high-precision experimental campaigns, can resolve the current hints of CKM unitarity violation and probe new physics at the TeV scale.

## Method

The paper surveys the current status and future prospects of three main research directions using nuclear beta decay: tests of CKM matrix unitarity via superallowed Fermi and mirror transitions; searches for scalar and tensor currents through angular correlation and Fierz interference measurements; and neutrino physics including absolute neutrino mass measurements and sterile neutrino searches. It reviews recent experimental achievements using atom traps, ion traps, cyclotron radiation emission spectroscopy (CRES), and superconducting tunnel junctions, alongside theoretical progress in ab initio nuclear structure calculations and electroweak radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

- The most precise determination of V_ud is obtained from 0+ to 0+ nuclear decays, using a collection of more than 200 measurements in 21 different nuclei
- The KATRIN experiment has set the most stringent limit on the neutrino mass scale of m_beta less than or equal to 0.8 eV/c^2 at 90% C.L. using molecular tritium, with a final target sensitivity of m_beta less than or equal to 0.2 eV/c^2 at 90% C.L.
- The Project 8 experiment targets a sensitivity of m_beta less than or equal to 0.04 eV/c^2 at 90% C.L. using an atomic tritium source
- The BeEST experiment currently sets the best laboratory limits in the 100 to 850 keV mass range for sterile neutrinos
- A recent global fit of nuclear and neutron beta decay data shows a hint of BSM tensor coupling to right-handed neutrinos at the 3-sigma level
- Angular correlation experiments have achieved precision as good as 0.3%, placing stringent limits on tensor interactions and right-handed currents
- New experiments will achieve precision of 0.1% or better, enabling them to reach TeV-scale physics

## Context

This white paper was submitted to the 2022 Fundamental Symmetries, Neutrons, and Neutrinos (FSNN) Town Hall Meeting in preparation for the next NSAC Long Range Plan. It extends the work of Towner and Hardy (2010) on superallowed decays and Hardy (2014) on ft-value measurements, while building on recent theoretical breakthroughs in radiative corrections by Seng (2018, 2019) and others that produced a 3-sigma shift in V_ud compared to previous state-of-the-art calculations. The paper enables a coordinated community effort to resolve the Cabibbo angle anomaly through the proposed VUDU (Vud Unitarity) alliance and to push experimental sensitivity beyond current LHC reach for scalar and tensor currents.
