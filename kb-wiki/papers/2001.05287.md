# Spectrum and rearrangement decays of tetraquark states with four different flavors

> We have systematically investigated the mass spectrum and rearrangement decay properties of the exotic tetraquark states with four different flavors using a color-magnetic interaction model. Their masses are estimated by assuming that the $X(4140)$ is a $cs\bar{c}\bar{s}$ tetraquark state and their decay widths are obtained by assuming that the Hamiltonian for decay is a constant. According to the adopted method, we find that the most stable states are probably the isoscalar $bs\bar{u}\bar{d}$ and $cs\bar{u}\bar{d}$ with $J^P=0^+$ and $1^+$. The width for most unstable tetraquarks is about tens of MeVs, but that for unstable $cu\bar{s}\bar{d}$ and $cs\bar{u}\bar{d}$ can be around 100 MeV. For the $X(5568)$, our method cannot give consistent mass and width if it is a $bu\bar{s}\bar{d}$ tetraquark state. For the $I(J^P)=0(0^+),0(1^+)$ double-heavy $T_{bc}=bc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ states, their widths can be several MeVs.

## Key claim

The authors systematically estimate the masses and rearrangement decay widths of tetraquark states with four different flavors using a color-magnetic interaction (CMI) model, assuming the X(4140) is a \(cs\bar{c}\bar{s}\) tetraquark and that the decay Hamiltonian is a constant; they find that the most stable states are probably the isoscalar \(bs\bar{u}\bar{d}\) and \(cs\bar{u}\bar{d}\) with \(J^P=0^+\) and \(1^+\), but note that for the X(5568) their method cannot give consistent mass and width if it is a \(bu\bar{s}\bar{d}\) tetraquark.

## Method

The authors construct flavor-spin-color wavefunction bases for nine tetraquark systems (e.g., \(bc\bar{s}\bar{n}\), \(bu\bar{s}\bar{d}\), \(cs\bar{u}\bar{d}\)) and diagonalize the CMI Hamiltonian to obtain eigenvalues and mixing coefficients. Tetraquark masses are estimated using a reference mass formula based on the X(4140) mass (4146.5 MeV) and quark mass differences extracted from conventional hadrons (e.g., \(\Delta_{bc}=3340.2\) MeV, \(\Delta_{cn}=1280.7\) MeV, \(\Delta_{sn}=90.6\) MeV). Decay widths are calculated via a simple rearrangement model where the decay Hamiltonian is a constant, with the coupling \(\alpha\) extracted by assuming the total width of X(4140) equals 83 MeV.

## Key numbers

*   The extracted effective quark masses are \(m_n=361.8\) MeV, \(m_s=542.4\) MeV, \(m_c=1724.1\) MeV, and \(m_b=5054.4\) MeV.
*   The mass of X(4140) is taken as 4146.5 MeV, leading to \(M_{X(4140)}-(E_{CMI})_{X(4140)}=4231.0\) MeV.
*   For the \(F_9=cs\bar{u}\bar{d}\) system, the lowest \(0(0^+)\) state has a mass estimated at 2320 MeV (using the X(4140) scale) and the lowest \(0(1^+)\) state at 2954 MeV.
*   For the \(F_7=bs\bar{u}\bar{d}\) system, the lowest \(0(0^+)\) state has a mass of 5755 MeV and the lowest \(0(1^+)\) state at 6285 MeV (using the X(4140) scale).
*   The extracted coupling constant \(\alpha\) for the decay Hamiltonian is 7.27 GeV, obtained by setting \(\Gamma_{sum}=83\) MeV for X(4140).
*   For the \(F_8=cu\bar{s}\bar{d}\) system, the lowest \(0^+\) state has a mass of 2213 MeV (X(4140) scale) and the lowest \(1^+\) state at 2385 MeV.
*   For the \(F_6=bu\bar{s}\bar{d}\) system, the lowest \(0^+\) state has a mass of 5640 MeV and the lowest \(1^+\) state at 5697 MeV (X(4140) scale).

## Context

This work extends previous CMI model studies of tetraquarks with four different flavors, such as those by Liu (2016) on \(qq\bar{q}\bar{Q}\) systems and Wu (2018) on \(bs\bar{c}\bar{n}\), \(bn\bar{c}\bar{s}\), and \(bn\bar{c}\bar{n}\) systems. The authors use an updated mass estimation method that references the X(4140) state to reduce uncertainties, and they apply a simple constant-decay Hamiltonian previously used for pentaquark decays by Cheng (2019). The results provide predictions for the masses and widths of these exotic tetraquarks, which can guide future experimental searches, especially for the most stable isoscalar \(bs\bar{u}\bar{d}\) and \(cs\bar{u}\bar{d}\) states, and they highlight the difficulty of explaining the X(5568) as a tetraquark within this model.
