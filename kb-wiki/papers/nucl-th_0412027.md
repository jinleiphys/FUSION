# Study of the $Σ$(1385) and $Λ$(1405) resonances in K$^+$ photoproduction processes

> The $γp \to K^+ π^0 Λ$ and $γp \to K^+ πΣ$ reactions are studied in the kinematic region where the $π^0 Λ$(1116) and $πΣ$(1192) pairs originate dominantly from the decay of the $Σ$(1385) and $Λ$(1405) resonances. We consider laboratory photon energies around 2 GeV, i.e. total center of mass energies above the known resonance region. We compute the t-channel kaon-exchange contribution to these reactions using $K^- p \to π^0 Λ$ and $K^- p \to πΣ$ amplitudes calculated in the framework of a chiral coupled-channel effective field theory of meson-baryon scattering. We extract from the calculated cross section the gauge-invariant double kaon pole term. We find this term to be large and likely to drive significantly the $γp \to K^+ π^0 Λ$ and $γp \to K^+ πΣ$ reactions in the kinematics under investigation. Accurate measurements of t-distributions for these processes, in progress or planned at ELSA and at SPring-8, are needed to confirm this expectation and assess the possibility of studying antikaon-nucleon dynamics just below threshold through these reactions.

## Key claim

The gauge-invariant double kaon pole term, extracted from the t-channel K⁻-exchange contribution to the γp → K⁺π⁰Λ and γp → K⁺πΣ reactions, is large and likely to drive these reactions significantly at laboratory photon energies around 2 GeV, but accurate measurements of t-distributions (in progress or planned at ELSA and SPring-8) are needed to confirm this expectation.

## Method

The authors computed the t-channel kaon-exchange contribution to the γp → K⁺π⁰Λ and γp → K⁺πΣ reactions using K⁻p → π⁰Λ and K⁻p → πΣ amplitudes obtained from a chiral coupled-channel effective field theory of meson-baryon scattering. They extracted the gauge-invariant double kaon pole term from the calculated cross section by applying the pole scheme method, which isolates the residue at the double pole and ensures gauge invariance. The K⁻p → πY amplitudes used were those from Lutz (2004), which describe available data on K⁻p elastic and inelastic processes up to laboratory K⁻ momenta of about 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The Σ⁰(1385) decays primarily into the π⁰Λ channel at (88±2)% and into the πΣ channel at (12±2)%.
- The Λ(1405) decays entirely into the πΣ channel.
- The K⁻N threshold is at √s ≈ 1.435 GeV.
- The threshold photon energies for producing K⁺Σ(1385) and K⁺Λ(1405) final states are Eγᵗʰʳᵉˢʰ = 1.41 GeV and Eγᵗʰʳᵉˢʰ = 1.45 GeV, respectively.
- The double kaon pole term contributions to differential cross sections were computed at Eγ = 2.1 GeV as functions of the πY total center-of-mass energy.
- At Eγ = 1.7 GeV, the calculated cross sections for the πΣ channels are roughly twice larger than predictions from the model of Nacher (1999), and the cross section for the π⁰Λ channel is about an order of magnitude larger than that result.

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channel approach of Lutz (2004) for K⁻p scattering to photoproduction reactions, offering a way to test K⁻p amplitudes below the K⁻N threshold where the Σ(1385) and Λ(1405) resonances dominate. The study is motivated by new-generation experiments at ELSA (SAPHIR and Crystal Barrel) and SPring-8/LEPS that can measure multiparticle final states exclusively, enabling separation of the Σ(1385) and Λ(1405) channels. The approach contrasts with earlier electroproduction data from DESY (Azemoon, 1970s) that could not separate the two resonances, and with the model of Nacher (1999) which neglected the Σ(1385) resonance.
