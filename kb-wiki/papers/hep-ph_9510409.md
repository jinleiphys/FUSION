# Dilepton bremsstrahlung from pion-pion scattering in a relativistic OBE model

> We have made a detailed and quantitative study of dilepton production via bremsstrahlung of a virtual photon during pion-pion collisions. Most calculations of electromagnetic radiation from strong interaction processes rely on the soft photon approximation (SPA). The conditions underlying this approximation are generally violated when dilepton spectra are calculated in terms of their invariant mass, so that an approach going beyond the SPA becomes necessary. Superseding previous derivations, we derive an exact formula for the bremsstrahlung cross section. The resulting formulation is compared to various forms based on the SPA, the two-particle phase space approximation and Rückl's formula using a relativistic One Boson Exchange (OBE) model. Within the OBE approach, we show that approximations to the bremsstrahlung dilepton cross sections often differ greatly from the exact result; discrepancies become greater both with rising temperature and with invariant mass. Integrated dilepton production rates are overestimated by Rückl-based approximations by factors 1.5--8.0. The largest discrepancies occur for the reaction $π^+π^+ \to π^+π^+\ell^+\ell^-$, where such approximations overestimate the exact rate by factors ranging from 2 to 30 for invariant masses between 10 and 500 MeV. Our findings, combined with recent estimates of the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect, indicate that bremsstrahlung dileptons rates in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions should be even more suppressed than had been thought before.

## Key claim

The soft photon approximation (SPA) is generally invalid for dilepton production cross sections expressed in terms of invariant mass, even at small invariant masses, because the kinematic range of the photon energy \(q_0\) is never much smaller than the SPA validity limits; the authors derive an exact formula for the bremsstrahlung cross section and show that SPA-based approximations overestimate the exact result by factors ranging from 1.5 to 30 depending on the reaction channel and invariant mass.

## Method

The authors derived an exact, fully covariant formula for the dilepton bremsstrahlung cross section \(\pi\pi \to \pi\pi\ell^+\ell^-\) that makes no approximations regarding photon momentum in the hadronic matrix element or phase space, using a relativistic One Boson Exchange (OBE) model for the pion-pion interaction. They compared this exact result to several approximations: the soft photon approximation (SPA), the two-particle phase space approximation, and Ruckl's formula, by computing cross sections \(d\sigma(s)/dM\) and thermal dilepton production rates \(dN/d^4x\, dM\) for a Boltzmann gas of pions at temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- Integrated dilepton production rates are overestimated by Ruckl-based approximations by factors of **1.5 to 8.0**.
- For the reaction \(\pi^+\pi^+ \to \pi^+\pi^+\ell^+\ell^-\), Ruckl-based approximations overestimate the exact rate by factors ranging from **2 to 30** for invariant masses between **10 and 500 MeV**.
- The SPA validity condition in the center-of-mass frame requires \(q_0^* \ll \sqrt{s}/2\) and \(q_0^* \ll m_Y \sqrt{1 - 4m^2/s}\), where \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV and \(m_Y = m_\sigma \simeq 500\) MeV; these conditions are violated even for \(M = 10\) MeV.
- The kinematic range for \(q_0\) is \(M \leq q_0 \leq (s - 4m^2 + M^2)/(2\sqrt{s})\).

## Context

This work extends earlier bremsstrahlung calculations by Gale and Haglin (1993) and Lichard (1995) by providing an exact treatment that supersedes the soft photon approximation and Ruckl's formula (Ruckl, 1976), which had been widely used for dilepton production in heavy ion collisions. The findings, combined with recent estimates of the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect, indicate that bremsstrahlung dilepton rates in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions should be even more suppressed than previously thought, and the exact formalism enables future work that includes spacetime integration over the fireball region, Dalitz decays, and the Landau-Pomeranchuk effect for direct comparison to experimental data.
