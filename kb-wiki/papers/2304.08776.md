# Hot and highly magnetized neutron star matter properties with Skyrme interactions

> We study the properties of hot and dense neutron star matter under the presence of strong magnetic fields using two Skyrme interactions, namely the LNS and the BSk21 ones. Asking for $β$--stability and charge neutrality, we construct the equation of state of the system and analyze its composition for a range of densities, temperatures and magnetic field intensities of interest for the study of supernova and proto-neutron star matter, with a particular interest on the degree of spin-polarization of the different components. The results show that system configurations with larger fractions of spin up protons and spin down neutrons and electrons are energetically favored over those with larger fractions of spin down protons and spin up neutrons and electrons. The effective mass of neutrons and protons is found to be in general larger for the more abundant of their spin projection component, respectively, spin down neutrons and spin up protons. The effect of the magnetic field on the Helmhotz total free energy density, pressure and isothermal compressibility of the system is almost negligible for all the values of the magnetic field considered.

# Hot and highly magnetized neutron star matter properties with Skyrme interactions

## Key claim

The effect of magnetic fields up to 10^18 G on the Helmholtz total free energy density, pressure, and isothermal compressibility of beta-stable neutron star matter is almost negligible for all field strengths considered, despite the fact that fields of at least 10^17 G do induce appreciable spin polarization of neutrons, protons, and electrons. The authors qualify that the weak influence on bulk thermodynamics arises primarily because the nuclear magneton is small and the total electron fraction is very low.

## Method

The authors constructed the equation of state of hot, beta-stable, charge-neutral neutron-proton-electron matter under strong magnetic fields using two Skyrme interactions (LNS and BSk21) in the Hartree-Fock approximation. They solved the coupled system of chemical potential relations, baryon number conservation, and charge neutrality to determine the composition, including spin-resolved partial densities for neutrons, protons, and electrons with Landau quantization for charged particles. The Helmholtz free energy density, pressure, and isothermal compressibility were computed for densities up to 0.4 fm^-3, temperatures of 5, 15, and 30 MeV, and magnetic field strengths of 10^16, 10^17, and 10^18 G.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: 10^16 G, 10^17 G, and 10^18 G
- Temperatures considered: 5 MeV, 15 MeV, and 30 MeV
- Density range explored: up to 0.4 fm^-3
- Nuclear magneton value used: mu_N = 3.15245 x 10^-18 MeV/G
- Bohr magneton value used: mu_B = 5.78838 x 10^-15 MeV/G
- Neutron g-factor: g_n = -3.826; proton g-factor: g_p = 5.586; electron g-factor: g_e = -2
- A magnetic field of 10^16 G induces only extremely low spin polarization at very low densities; fields of at least 10^17 G are needed to see appreciable differences in spin-resolved fractions
- For the LNS interaction at T = 5 MeV and B = 10^17 G, the proton spin asymmetry W_p/rho_p decreases up to a density of approximately 0.2 fm^-3 before increasing
- For the BSk21 interaction, the proton effective mass splitting reverses at approximately 0.1 fm^-3, with m*_p_down > m*_p_up below this density and m*_p_up > m*_p_down above it
- The isothermal compressibility becomes very small for densities above approximately 0.3 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetized neutron star matter by Blandford and Hernquist (1982), Broderick et al. (2000), and Dong et al. (2013) by using non-relativistic Skyrme interactions (LNS and BSk21) rather than relativistic mean-field approaches, and by including finite temperature effects relevant for supernova and proto-neutron star matter. The results contrast with those of Rabhi et al. (2015) and the Coimbra group (Fang et al., 2016-2017; Avancini et al., 2018; Sengo et al., 2020; Pais et al., 2021; Ferreira et al., 2021), who found noticeable effects of strong magnetic fields on the crust-core transition using RMF models; the present work finds negligible magnetic effects on bulk thermodynamics for the Skyrme interactions considered. The framework is intended to enable future studies of neutrino propagation in hot, dense, magnetized neutron star matter, extending the recent work by Torres and Patino (2019) on neutrino mean free paths in pure neutron matter.
