# Chiral SU(3) theory of antikaon-nucleon interactions with improved threshold constraints

> $\bar{K}$-nucleon interactions are investigated in the framework of coupled-channels dynamics based on the next-to-leading order chiral SU(3) meson-baryon effective Lagrangian. A recent determination of the 1s shift and width of kaonic hydrogen enables us to set accurate constraints on the coupled-channels meson-baryon amplitudes in the strangeness $S=-1$ sector. Theoretical uncertainties in the subthreshold extrapolation of the coupled-channels amplitudes are discussed. Using this framework, we give predictions for $K^-$-neutron interactions and for the spectrum of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance. A simplified, effective three-channel model using leading order chiral SU(3) meson-baryon interactions is also constructed for convenient application in $\bar{K}$-nuclear few-body calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the new SIDDHARTA measurements of kaonic hydrogen (2011) provide sufficiently precise constraints to reduce theoretical uncertainties in the subthreshold extrapolation of antikaon-nucleon amplitudes, but only when next-to-leading order chiral SU(3) terms are included; leading-order models alone cannot simultaneously reproduce the kaonic hydrogen shift and the scattering data.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson-baryon T-matrix in the strangeness S=-1 sector, using an interaction kernel derived from the chiral SU(3) meson-baryon effective Lagrangian at next-to-leading order (NLO). They perform systematic chi-squared fits to the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen data (energy shift and width), threshold branching ratios, and total cross section data from K^-p scattering, progressively improving the interaction kernel from leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg terms alone, through inclusion of Born terms, to the full NLO scheme. The subtraction constants in the dimensional regularization of the loop functions and the NLO low-energy constants are treated as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen 1s state: energy shift ΔE = 283 ± 36 (stat) ± 6 (syst) eV, width Γ = 541 ± 89 (stat) ± 22 (syst) eV (SIDDHARTA measurements)
- Best-fit NLO model yields ΔE = 306 eV and Γ = 591 eV, within experimental error bars
- Threshold branching ratios from best-fit NLO model: γ = 2.37, R_n = 0.19, R_c = 0.66
- Pole positions of the isospin I=0 amplitude in the NLO scheme: 1424 − 26i MeV and 1381 − 81i MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for NLO fit: 0.96
- Subtraction constants at scale μ = 1 GeV: a_{Kbar N} = −2.38^{+0.17}_{-0.84} × 10^{-3}, a_{πΣ} = 4.35^{+0.31}_{-0.77} × 10^{-3}
- Meson decay constants in NLO fit: f_K = 110.0 MeV, f_η = 118.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral coupled-channels approach previously developed by Kaiser (1995), Oller (2001), and Borasoy (2005-2006), which suffered from large uncertainties due to insufficiently precise kaonic hydrogen data from earlier experiments (Iwasaki 1997, Beer 2005/DEAR). The new SIDDHARTA constraints resolve a quantitative inconsistency noted between DEAR measurements and scattering data in those earlier studies. The improved amplitudes enable reliable predictions for K^-neutron scattering lengths and provide a foundation for Kbar-nuclear few-body calculations, for which a simplified effective three-channel model using leading-order interactions is also constructed.
