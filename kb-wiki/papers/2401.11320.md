# The $X(3872)$ to $ψ(2S)$ yield ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> In this work we evaluate the $X(3872)$ to $ ψ(2S) $ yield ratio ($N_X/N_{ψ(2S)}$) in Pb Pb collisions, taking into account the interactions of the $ψ(2S) $ and $ X(3872)$ states with light mesons in the hadron gas formed at the late stages of these collisions. We employ an effective Lagrangian approach to estimate the thermally-averaged cross sections for the production and absorption of the $ψ(2S)$ and use them in the rate equation to determine the time evolution of $N_{ψ(2S)}$. The multiplicity of these states at the end of mixed phase is obtained from the coalescence model. The multiplicity of $X(3872)$, treated as a bound state of $(D\bar D^{*} + c.c.)$ and also as a compact tetraquark, was already calculated in previous works. Knowing these yields, we derive predictions for the ratio ($N_X/N_{ψ(2S)}$) as a function of the centrality, of the center-of-mass energy and of the charged hadron multiplicity measured at midrapidity $[dN_{ch}/dη\,(η<0.5)]$. Finally, we make predictions for this ratio in Pb Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV to be measured by the ALICE Collaboration in the Run 3.

## Key claim

The ratio of X(3872) to psi(2S) yields in PbPb collisions depends on whether the X(3872) is a molecular bound state of D and D* mesons or a compact tetraquark, because the two configurations interact differently with the hadron gas formed in the late stages of the collision, and this difference modifies the final yield ratio.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach to compute vacuum cross sections for psi(2S) absorption and production reactions with pions and rho mesons (psi(2S) + pi/rho -> D(*) D(*)), then thermally averaged these cross sections over Bose-Einstein distributions. They employed these thermally-averaged cross sections in rate equations to determine the time evolution of the psi(2S) multiplicity in the hadron gas, while using previously calculated multiplicities for X(3872) in both molecular and tetraquark configurations. The initial multiplicities at the end of the mixed phase were obtained from a coalescence model.

## Key numbers

* The CMS Collaboration measured the prompt X(3872) to psi(2S) yield ratio in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV as 1.08 +/- 0.49(stat) +/- 0.52(syst), for |y| < 1.6 and 15 < pT < 50 GeV.
* The LHCb Collaboration measured the ratio in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV as 0.27 +/- 0.08 +/- 0.05 (forward) and 0.36 +/- 0.15 +/- 0.11 (backward), for pT > 5 GeV.
* The ratio observed in pp collisions is approximately 0.09.
* The psi(2S) absorption cross sections by pions and rho mesons range from about 10^-4 mb to 10^1 mb in the region up to sqrt(s) - sqrt(s0) ~ 500 MeV above threshold.
* The production cross sections for D(*) D(*) -> psi(2S) + light meson are of order 10^-4 mb to 10^0 mb in the same energy range.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of X(3872) interactions in hadron gas (Houng 2013, Torres 2014, Abreu 2016, QCDSR 2021) by adding the psi(2S) interactions with light mesons, which were not included in the coalescence model calculation of Yun (2022). The results enable predictions for the ALICE Collaboration Run 3 measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, and the comparison between molecular and tetraquark predictions for X(3872) may help discriminate between these two configurations.
