# Bayesian inference of neutron-star observables based on effective nuclear interactions

> Based on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model (SHF) as well as its extension (the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) model) and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, we have studied the constraints on the parameters of the nuclear matter equation of state (EOS) from adopted astrophysical observables using a Bayesian approach. While the masses and radii of neutron stars generally favors a stiff isoscalar EOS and a moderately soft nuclear symmetry energy, model dependence on the constraints is observed and mostly originates from the incorporation of higher-order EOS parameters and difference between relativistic and non-relativistic models. At twice saturation density, the value of the symmetry energy is constrained to be $48^{+15}_{-11}$ MeV in the standard SHF model, $48^{+8}_{-15}$ MeV in the KIDS model, and $48^{+5}_{-6}$ MeV in the RMF model, around their maximum {\it a posteriori} values within $68\%$ confidence intervals. Our study helps to obtain a robust constraint on nuclear matter EOS, and meanwhile, to understand the model dependence of the results.

# Bayesian inference of neutron-star observables based on effective nuclear interactions

## Key claim

The authors find that neutron-star masses and radii favor a stiff isoscalar equation of state and a moderately soft nuclear symmetry energy, but the constraints are model-dependent, with the differences originating from how higher-order EOS parameters are incorporated and from the distinction between relativistic and non-relativistic models.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference on the parameters of the nuclear matter equation of state using three effective nuclear interaction models: the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model, its extension the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) model, and the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. They sampled over EOS parameters (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, skewness, symmetry energy and its slope, curvature, and skewness) and compared predicted neutron-star radii and tidal deformabilities against astrophysical data from NICER, LIGO/Virgo, and pulsar timing measurements, using a likelihood function that also enforced a maximum neutron-star mass above 2.08 solar masses and subluminal sound speed.

## Key numbers

- At twice saturation density, the symmetry energy is constrained to 48^{+15}_{-11} MeV in the standard SHF model, 48^{+8}_{-15} MeV in the KIDS model, and 48^{+5}_{-6} MeV in the RMF model, all at 68% confidence intervals.
- The incompressibility K0 of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation was varied within a prior range of 220 to 260 MeV, based on isoscalar giant monopole resonance studies.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy at saturation was varied within a prior range of 30 to 90 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at saturation Esym0 was varied within a prior range of 28.5 to 34.9 MeV.
- The canonical neutron-star radius R1.4 was taken as 11.725 ± 1.105 km from photospheric radius expansion bursts and thermal emissions.
- The tidal deformability of a canonical neutron star Lambda1.4 was taken as 190^{+390}_{-120} from GW170817 analysis.
- The radius of PSR J0740+6620 (mass 2.08 solar masses) was taken from two NICER analyses: 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5} km and 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of neutron-star observables by using effective nuclear interaction models (SHF, KIDS, RMF) rather than purely parameterized or polytropic EOS forms, allowing a more theoretically grounded connection to nuclear forces. It builds on prior constraints of EOS parameters from terrestrial experiments, such as the incompressibility from ISGMR studies (PhysRevLett.109.092501, PhysRevC.97.025805) and the symmetry energy parameters from surveys (LI2013276, RevModPhys.89.015007). The results enable future combined analyses of neutron-star, heavy-ion reaction, and nuclear structure data within the same effective interaction framework, and highlight the need to account for model dependence when extracting robust EOS constraints.
