# Low energy scattering and photoproduction of $η$-mesons on deuterons

> Photo-production of $η$-mesons and their collisions with light nuclei are studied within the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) formalism for different parameters of $ηN$ interaction. A three-body resonance or a quasi-bound state is found close to the $ηd$ threshold. Expected manifestation of this resonant behavior of $ηd$ elastic scattering in various processes involving $ηd$-system in their final states was found in calculations of the $γd \to ηd$ reaction in the framework of modified AGS equations. These calculations revealed peaks in the energy dependence of the total cross-section almost at the same energies as in the elastic $ηd$ scattering. Meanwhile, such peaks did not appear in the impulse approximation (i.e. when $ηd$ resonance was not taken into account). This sheds some light on the nature of the enhancement of $η$ photo-production on deuteron observed recently in the low energy experiments.

# Low energy scattering and photoproduction of η-mesons on deuterons

## Key claim

The authors find that a three-body resonance or quasi-bound state exists close to the ηd threshold, and that this resonance manifests as peaks in the energy dependence of the total cross-section for γd → ηd, but only when full rescattering is included via the AGS formalism, not in the impulse approximation. The resonance position and width depend sensitively on the poorly-constrained real part of the ηN scattering length, and the calculated resonant peak lies about 15 MeV below the experimental peak, suggesting that an ηN repulsion at short distances may be needed.

## Method

The authors solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-body equations in momentum space for the ηd system, using separable two-body T-matrices for both NN and ηN interactions. The ηN T-matrix is modeled via the S11(1535) resonance propagator with a Yamaguchi form factor, and the NN interaction uses a modified separable potential from Garcilazo (1988). For photoproduction, the AGS formalism is extended to include the electromagnetic interaction in first order, with the γN → ηN amplitude taken from a multi-channel analysis and extended off-shell using Yamaguchi form factors.

## Key numbers

- The critical value of Re aηN for binding the η inside the deuteron is 0.733 fm when Im aηN = 0.3 fm; for Im aηN = 0.20, 0.25, and 0.35 fm, the critical Re aηN values are 0.788, 0.761, and 0.698 fm respectively.
- The ηd resonance energy ranges from 8.24 MeV (for Re aηN = 0.55 fm) down to 3.73 MeV (for Re aηN = 0.90 fm), with corresponding widths from 9.15 MeV to 6.81 MeV.
- The ηN scattering length uncertainty interval is 0.27 fm ≤ Re aηN ≤ 0.98 fm and 0.19 fm ≤ Im aηN ≤ 0.37 fm.
- The ratio of neutron to proton photoproduction amplitudes is A = −0.84 ± 0.15 from multipole analysis.
- The range parameter α for the ηN vertex is 3.316 fm⁻¹.
- The S11 resonance parameters are E0 = 1535 MeV − (mN + mη) and Γ = 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier few-body calculations of η-nucleus systems (Ueda, 1992; Shevchenko, 1998) and addresses the long-standing problem of whether η-mesic nuclei can exist, which was previously studied via optical potential methods (Haider and Liu, 1986), Green's function methods, and multiple scattering theory. The authors provide the first exact AGS treatment of ηd scattering and photoproduction, showing that the resonant enhancement seen in experimental data (reported in 1998) cannot be reproduced by impulse approximation alone. The results enable future extraction of the ηN scattering length from ηd data and motivate searches for η-mesic nuclei in other light systems.
