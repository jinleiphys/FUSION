# On the role of pairing correlations in calculation of \b{eta}-decay half-lives within QRPA formalism

> In this paper we show that the proton-neutron residual interaction can play an important role in the reliability of calculated $β$-decay half-lives. It may also improve the prediction power of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) model. We further demonstrate that a reasonable choice of the particle-particle (attractive) and particle-hole force (repulsive) parameters can result in calculated half-lives in very good comparison with the measured ones. Pairing gaps have affect on calculated half-lives which we explore in this paper. We present our half-lives calculation using the proton-neutron QRPA (pn-QRPA) model possessing a multi-shell single-particle deformed space including a schematic interaction for some medium mass neutron-deficient nuclei undergoing $β^{+}$/EC decay. Our study shows a better agreement with the available experimental data as compared to former calculations.

# On the role of pairing correlations in calculation of β-decay half-lives within QRPA formalism

## Key claim
The proton-neutron residual interaction, specifically the choice of particle-particle (attractive) and particle-hole (repulsive) force parameters, can significantly affect the reliability of calculated β-decay half-lives within the pn-QRPA model, but only when pairing gaps are determined using an empirical formula based on separation energies (Emp-1) rather than the traditional mass-dependent formula (Emp-2). The authors show that pairing gaps have a larger effect on calculated half-lives than previously assumed in earlier pn-QRPA calculations.

## Method
The authors employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model in a deformed multi-shell single-particle space using Nilsson+BCS formalism with a schematic and separable Gamow-Teller (GT) potential. They calculated β+/EC decay half-lives for even-even medium-mass neutron-deficient nuclei with atomic numbers Z = 24-34 and neutron numbers N = 18-36, using two different empirical formulas for pairing gaps: Emp-1 (based on neutron and proton separation energies) and Emp-2 (the traditional 12/√A formula). The GT force parameters were fixed at χ = 61.20/A MeV and κ = 4.85/A MeV, and deformation parameters were taken from Moller (2016) while Q-values came from NUBASE2016.

## Key numbers
- The root mean square (RMS) deviation from measured data for the Emp-1 scheme was 272.6 s, compared to 5488.1 s for Emp-2, 1734.6 s for FRDM, 3869.3 s for EQRPA (no pn-pairing), and 3229.3 s for EQRPA (with pn-pairing)
- The GT force parameters used were χ = 61.20/A MeV and κ = 4.85/A MeV, displaying a 1/A dependence
- The constant K in the partial half-life formula was taken as 6143 s
- The Emp-2 formula for pairing gaps was Δ_pp = Δ_nn = 12/√A (in MeV)
- Up to a factor of four difference was observed between calculated total GT strengths using the two different pairing gap schemes
- For the case of 62Zn, a branching ratio of 67% at 0.068 MeV excitation in daughter 62Cu led to a considerable decrease in total half-life in the Emp-2 scheme

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations by Staudt (1990) and Hirsch (1993), who argued that pairing gap values have very little effect on calculated β-decay half-lives, a claim the present study contradicts. The authors compare their results with the recent extended QRPA (EQRPA) model calculations by Tan (2017), which used charge-dependent Bonn forces with and without proton-neutron pairing, and with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) calculations by Moller (1997). The study enables more reliable predictions of β-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient fp-shell nuclei important for the astrophysical rp-process, and the authors note their model can be applied to any arbitrary heavy nucleus due to its large model space (up to 7ħω).
