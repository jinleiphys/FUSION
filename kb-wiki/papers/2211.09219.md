# Helicity and vorticity in heavy-ion collisions at NICA energies

> Heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass nucleon collision energies 4.5--11.5 GeV are analyzed within the PHSD transport model. Spectator nucleons are separated, and the transfer of the initial angular momentum of colliding nuclei to the fireball formed by participants is studied. The maximal angular momentum is carried by the fireball in gold-gold collisions with the impact parameter about 5 fm corresponding to centrality class 10--20\%. The obtained participant distributions were fluidized and the energy and baryon number densities, temperature, and velocity fields are obtained in the Landau frame. It is shown that the velocity field has dominantly Hubble-like transversal and longitudinal expansion with the vortical motion being only a small correction on top of it. The vorticity field is calculated and illustrated in detail. The formation of two oppositely-rotating vortex rings moving in opposite directions along the $z$ axis is demonstrated. Other characteristics of the vortical motion such as the Lamb vector field and the kinematic vorticity number are considered. The magnitude of the latter one is found to be smaller than that for the Poiseuille flow and close to the pure shear deformation corresponding to just a flattening of fluid cells. The field of hydrodynamic helicity, which is responsible for the axial vortex effect, is calculated. The separation of positive and negative helicities localized upper and lower semi-planes with respect to the reaction plane is shown. It is proved that the areas with various helicity signs can be probed by the selection of $Λ$ hyperons with positive and negative projections of their momenta orthogonal to the reaction plane.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in heavy-ion collisions at NICA energies (4.5–11.5 GeV), the velocity field is dominated by Hubble-like expansion with vortical motion being only a small correction, and that the hydrodynamic helicity field separates into positive and negative regions in opposite half-planes relative to the reaction plane, which can be probed by selecting Λ hyperons with specific momentum projections.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass nucleon collision energies from 4.5 to 11.5 GeV. Spectator nucleons were separated from participants using a rapidity cut, and the participant distributions were fluidized using the particle-in-cell method with a square-law spline kernel on a spatial grid. The energy-momentum tensor and baryon current were computed from the test-particle distributions, and the flow velocity was determined in the Landau frame; the equation of state of a hadron resonance gas with a density-dependent mean field was used to obtain temperature fields.

## Key numbers

- Maximal angular momentum transferred to the fireball occurs in Au+Au collisions at impact parameter about 5 fm, corresponding to centrality class 10–20%
- For √sNN = 7.7 GeV and b = 7.5 fm, the maximum energy density at t ≈ 5 fm/c is approximately 12 GeV/fm³ and maximum baryon number density is 2.1/fm³ at t ≈ 3.4 fm/c
- For √sNN = 11.5 GeV, the maximum energy density is 12 GeV/fm³ at t = 3.3 fm/c and maximum baryon number density is 2.1/fm³ at t = 3.4 fm/c
- The kinematic vorticity number magnitude is found to be smaller than that for Poiseuille flow and close to pure shear deformation
- For √sNN = 7.7 GeV and b = 7.5 fm, the fireball fluid fully disintegrates at approximately 15–16 fm/c
- The transferred angular momentum at b ≈ 5 fm for √sNN = 4.5 GeV is about 7% smaller than for √sNN = 7.7 and 11.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions performed using AMPT at higher RHIC and LHC energies (XTW18) and using QGSM at √sNN = 5 GeV (BGST-Hseparation, BGST-Vsheet). It also builds on the three-fluid hydrodynamic approach (Ivanov, Soldatov, PRC97) which found a structure of two vortex rings at √sNN = 39 GeV. The detailed characterization of helicity fields at NICA energies enables future experimental tests of the axial vortical effect via Λ hyperon polarization measurements, particularly the separation of positive and negative helicity regions that can be probed by selecting hyperons with specific momentum projections orthogonal to the reaction plane.
