# Imaging Freeze-out Sources and Extracting Strong Interaction Parameters in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> By combining femtoscopic interferometry with an optical deblurring algorithm, we present a novel method to image the source in heavy-ion collisions (HICs) while simultaneously determining the interaction strength between particle pairs. The spatial distribution of the emission source has been reconstructed for protons ($p$) and antiprotons ($\bar{p}$) from the respective $pp$ and $\bar{p}\bar{p}$ correlation functions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV. Within experimental uncertainties, protons and antiprotons share the same freeze-out distribution showing higher density in the center compared to the widely assumed Gaussian shape. The results evidence the matter-antimatter symmetry in coordinate space at the moment of freeze-out before the nucleons are fully randomized in the collisions.

## Key claim

By combining the Richardson-Lucy deblurring algorithm with the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz model, the authors simultaneously reconstruct the freeze-out source spatial distribution and extract the strong interaction scattering length for proton-proton and antiproton-antiproton pairs, without assuming a Gaussian source shape. Within experimental uncertainties, protons and antiprotons share the same freeze-out distribution, which shows higher density in the center compared to the widely assumed Gaussian shape, evidencing matter-antimatter symmetry in coordinate space at freeze-out.

## Method

The authors apply the Richardson-Lucy iterative deblurring algorithm to the Koonin-Pratt formula, which relates the two-particle correlation function to the source function via a kernel containing the pair wave function. The wave function is computed using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz model with the scattering length \(f_0\) and effective range \(d_0\) as free parameters. The algorithm iteratively reconstructs the source function from experimental correlation functions while scanning over (\(f_0\), \(d_0\)) to minimize \(\chi^2\), thereby extracting both the source image and the interaction parameters simultaneously. The method is applied to \(pp\) and \(\bar{p}\bar{p}\) correlation functions from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{S_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV measured by the STAR collaboration, with the effective range fixed to \(d_0 = 2.8\) fm.

## Key numbers

* Scattering length for \(pp\) pairs: \(f_{0(pp)} = (7.7 \pm 0.2)\) fm, extracted from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{S_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV.
* Scattering length for \(\bar{p}\bar{p}\) pairs: \(f_{0(\bar{p}\bar{p})} = (7.9 \pm 0.3)\) fm, extracted from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{S_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV.
* Probability that the reconstructed source function for protons is Gaussian: 0 (quantitative calculation).
* Probability that the reconstructed source function for antiprotons is Gaussian: \(2.2 \times 10^{-14}\).
* Convergence criterion for the iterative algorithm: change in \(\chi^2\) between successive iterations less than \(1 \times 10^{-4}\) for model tests, and \(1 \times 10^{-2}\) for experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analysis by the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk 2015), which assumed a Gaussian source shape and found similar source sizes and interaction parameters for \(pp\) and \(\bar{p}\bar{p}\) pairs. The direct imaging approach reveals non-Gaussian source distributions, consistent with recent observations of Levy-stable source shapes in heavy-ion collisions (Adare 2018, Mukherjee 2023, Kovacs 2023, Kincses 2024). The method enables future applications to probe exotic nuclear structures such as bubble nuclei (Mutschler 2017, Natowitz 2024) and, when extended to three dimensions, to investigate higher-order nuclear deformations at facilities like RHIC.
