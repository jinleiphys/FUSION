# Gluon Unpolarized, Polarized, and Transversity GPDs from Lattice QCD: Lorentz-Covariant Parametrization (Part I)

> We identify the matrix elements necessary to determine the leading-twist gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) $H_g,~E_g,~\wt{H}_g,~\wt{E}_g,~H^T_g,~E^T_g, \wt{H}^T_g ,~\wt{E}^T_g$ in lattice QCD calculations. We present a method to achieve a Lorentz-covariant parameterization of the matrix elements in terms of a linearly independent basis of tensor structures. This parameterization is crucial for projecting lattice QCD matrix elements onto light cone distributions. For the first time, we determine the corresponding components that project onto the linear combinations of invariant amplitudes, which reduce to the different gluon GPDs in the light cone limit and enable their separation in a lattice QCD calculation for spin-$0$ and spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ hadrons. Hence, this work lays the foundation for the numerical determination of the gluon GPDs from first-principle lattice QCD calculations, directly advancing our understanding of the mass and spin structures and mechanical properties of the nucleon, as well as the physics underlying deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production in a range of experimental processes.

## Key claim

The paper provides a Lorentz-covariant parameterization of off-forward gluonic matrix elements in terms of a linearly independent basis of tensor structures, enabling for the first time the projection of lattice QCD matrix elements onto all eight leading-twist gluon GPDs for spin-0 and spin-1/2 hadrons, but the projections are not unique and depend on the chosen basis for the Lorentz decomposition due to an overcompleteness of possible tensor structures.

## Method

The authors decompose the off-forward hadronic matrix element of a bilocal gluon operator into a sum of Lorentz-covariant tensor structures built from the metric, the hadron momenta P and Δ, and the displacement vector z, with coefficients being Lorentz-invariant amplitudes. For the spin-0 case, they identify 19 possible tensor structures, find one linear relation among them using a Gram-Schmidt procedure, and then solve a system of linear equations to obtain projection tensors that isolate the generalized Ioffe-time distribution at z²=0. For the spin-1/2 case, they extend this approach by including Dirac matrix structures and the Levi-Civita tensor, determining projection components that separate the linear combinations of invariant amplitudes corresponding to each of the eight gluon GPDs in the light-cone limit.

## Key numbers

- Eight leading-twist gluon GPDs are identified: H_g, E_g, \widetilde{H}_g, \widetilde{E}_g, H^T_g, E^T_g, \widetilde{H}^T_g, \widetilde{E}^T_g
- For the spin-0 hadron, 19 tensor structures are listed, with one linear relation (Eq. 4.18) reducing the basis to 18 independent elements
- The skewness ξ is defined as ξ = -Δ⁺/(2P⁺), and the Ioffe time ω = -z·P
- The quasi-skewness η is defined as η = -(z·Δ)/(2z·P) = (z·Δ)/(2ω)
- For the spin-1/2 case, the paper identifies the components that project onto the linear combinations of invariant amplitudes that reduce to the different gluon GPDs in the light-cone limit

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz-covariant parameterization approach first introduced by Balitsky et al. (2019) for the forward unpolarized gluon PDF to the off-forward case for generic polarizations, addressing the significantly more complicated gluonic matrix elements that had not been explored in the literature. The paper lays the foundation for numerical lattice QCD calculations of gluon GPDs using the quasi-PDF and large momentum effective theory (LaMET) formalism of Ji (2013, 2014) and the pseudo-PDF formalism of Radyushkin (2017), which will enable first-principle determinations of the gluon contributions to nucleon mass, spin, and mechanical properties, as well as support experimental programs at Jefferson Laboratory and the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider.
