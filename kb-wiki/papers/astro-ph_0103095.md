# Neutrino Propagation in Dense Astrophysical Systems

> Even the elusive neutrinos are trapped in matter, albeit transiently, in several astrophysical circumstances. Their interactions with the ambient matter not only reveal the properties of such exotic matter itself, but also shed light on the fundamental properties of the neutrinos themselves. The physical sites of interest include the early universe, supernovae, and newly-born neutron stars. Detection of neutrinos from these vastly different eras using the new generation of neutrino detectors holds great promise for enhancing our understanding of neutrino-matter interactions and astrophysical phenomena.

## Key claim

This review argues that neutrino propagation in dense astrophysical environments (supernovae, proto-neutron stars, and the early universe) requires a unified treatment of neutrino flavor mixing, forward-scattering refractive effects, and collective scattering processes, but cautions that "in most of the problems that we shall discuss there are, at present, few definitive results" due to undetermined mixing parameters, the need to follow non-equilibrium distributions of multiple species simultaneously, and the dependence of supernova applications on hydrodynamical calculations.

## Method

The authors derive a quantum kinetic equation (a non-Abelian Boltzmann equation) for the neutrino momentum-flavor density matrix that includes transport, flavor precession from mass and refractive effects, and collision terms from scattering, pair production, and annihilation. They then show how the rate functions in this equation are determined from current correlators in the medium, and describe the ring-graph (RPA) summation method to incorporate strong and electromagnetic correlations among nucleons, including the long-wavelength limit where the structure factor connects to the equation of state via the isothermal bulk modulus.

## Key numbers

- Weak coupling constant: \(G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-11}~\text{(MeV)}^{-2}\)
- Solar center electron density: \(n_e \approx 6 \times 10^{25}~\text{cm}^{-3} \approx 5 \times 10^{-7}~\text{(MeV)}^{3}\)
- Supernova-core to neutrinosphere nucleon density: \(n_N = [10^4 - 10^6]~\text{(MeV)}^{3}\)
- Solar center mean free path for neutrino scattering: \(\lambda_f \approx 4 \times 10^{17}~(\text{MeV}/E_\nu)^2~\text{cm}\)
- Supernova center mean free path for neutrino scattering: \(\lambda_f \approx 2 \times 10^{5}~(\text{MeV}/E_\nu)^2~\text{cm}\)
- Neutrino decoupling temperature in the early universe: \(T \approx 1~\text{MeV}\)
- Neutrino collision time in the early universe: \(\lambda_f/c \approx (T/\text{MeV})^{-3}~\text{s}\)
- Expansion time in the early universe: \(t(\text{s}) \approx [T(\text{MeV})]^{-2}\)
- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16~\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Neutrinosphere density: approximately \(0.01 n_0\) with \(T \approx 5~\text{MeV}\)
- Coherent scattering enhancement factor from heavy nuclei: \(N^2/6A \approx 100\) compared to the same mass density of nucleons

## Context

This review extends the line of work on neutrino transport in dense matter that began with the MSW effect (Haxton, year not given) and the seminal treatment of coherent scattering from nuclei by Freedman (year not given). It synthesizes and builds upon prior calculations of neutrino opacities using Fermi liquid parameters (Fermiliquid, year not given) and ring-graph methods (IP, rfs, redd98, redd99a, burr98), and connects to the quantum kinetic equation derivations of McKellar and Thomson (year not given), Raffelt, Sigl, and Stodolsky (year not given), and Loreti and Balantekin (year not given). The work enables future numerical simulations of neutrino transport with flavor mixing in supernovae and proto-neutron stars, and provides the formalism needed to compute corrections to primordial helium abundance predictions from non-equilibrium neutrino distributions in the early universe.
