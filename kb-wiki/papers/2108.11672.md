# Renormalization of NN Chiral EFT, my personal mixed feelings

> We are celebrating thirty-years from the seminal papers from Weinberg proposing to use the rules of Chiral Perturbation Theory to nucleon systems. His proposal was to build an Effective Field Theory (EFT) with Chiral symmetry as a key property. A big effort on this line have been made during these three decades, however the issue of renormalization of the theory is not settled. I will briefly review my path on this issue and give my personal view.

## Key claim

The renormalization of nucleon-nucleon (NN) chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) remains unsettled after thirty years; the author argues that while a pragmatic approach using finite cut-offs works phenomenologically, a fully regulator-independent renormalization program may be achievable through the exact N/D method, though it is not yet clear if a potential can be built from such calculations.

## Method

The author reviews two approaches to renormalization in NN chiEFT: (1) the standard method using finite cut-offs or regulator functions to cut loop integrals, which introduces regulator artifacts but yields successful NN potentials; and (2) the non-perturbative renormalization approach using the exact N/D method, which solves an integral equation for the left-hand discontinuity and allows multiple subtractions even for singular interactions. The exact N/D method was tested against a "full theory" potential with known regular behavior, where short-range contributions were removed to mimic the singular interactions of chiEFT.

## Key numbers

- The chiral-symmetry-breaking scale Lambda_chi is given as the hard energy scale of the theory, providing the ratio of low-energy external momenta over Lambda_chi for the low-energy expansion.
- The exact N/D method was shown to reproduce results from previous approaches when using only one or no subtractions, with "perfect" agreement.
- For the test potential with singular interactions, using more than one subtraction gave "very good" agreement with the full theory at low energies, for both singular attractive and singular repulsive cases.

## Context

This paper extends the thirty-year line of work initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991) proposing chiral perturbation theory rules for nuclear systems, and the subsequent development of NN potentials by Ordonez et al. (1992), Kaiser et al. (1997), Epelbaum et al. (1998, 2000), and Machleidt (2011). The author's exact N/D method, developed with Oller (2017, 2019), builds on earlier non-perturbative renormalization work by Ruiz-Arriola and Valderrama (2006) and Nogga et al. (2005), and addresses the problem that singular attractive interactions previously allowed only one low-energy constant to be fixed, making a good description of NN data impossible. The approach enables the possibility of complete regularization independence in chiEFT, though it remains unclear whether a usable potential can be constructed from the N/D method.
