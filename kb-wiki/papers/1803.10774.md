# Holographic Heavy-Ion Collisions: Analytic Solutions with Longitudinal Flow, Elliptic Flow and Vorticity

> We consider phenomenological consequences arising from simple analytic solutions for holographic heavy-ion collisions. For these solutions, early-time longitudinal flow is initially negative (inward), sizable direct, elliptic, and quadrangular flow is generated, and the average vorticity of the system is tunable by a single parameter. Despite large vorticity and angular momentum, we show that the system does not complete a single rotation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new two-parameter family of analytic solutions to ideal relativistic hydrodynamics that arise naturally from off-center black hole collisions in global AdS, but the authors explicitly state these solutions provide only *qualitative* or *semi-quantitative* insights for real-world heavy-ion collisions, not quantitatively accurate predictions, because the gauge/gravity duality solves strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory rather than QCD.

## Method

The authors derive analytic solutions for the energy density and fluid four-velocity in Minkowski space (Milne coordinates) by transforming stationary Myers-Perry-AdS black hole solutions from global AdS coordinates. They verify that these solutions satisfy the ideal conformal hydrodynamic equations of motion (with pressure P = ε/3). To convert the fluid velocity fields to particle observables, they apply the standard Cooper-Frye decoupling procedure using classical statistics and isochronous decoupling at constant proper time τ_f = 10 L, computing anisotropic flow coefficients v_n and vorticity-related quantities.

## Key numbers

- The analytic solution contains two angular rotation frequencies ω₁ and ω₂, both constrained to |ω₁,₂| < 1, with ω₁,₂ = 0 corresponding to no rotation and ω₁,₂ = ±1 corresponding to the mass-shedding limit of a Myers-Perry-AdS black hole.
- For the flow coefficient calculations, the authors used ω₁ = 0.5 and ω₂ = 0.05 as illustrative parameter values.
- The decoupling hypersurface was chosen at τ_f = 10 L, with results reported as essentially insensitive to this choice.
- The p⊥-integrated flow results were calculated with a transverse momentum cut of p⊥ L > 1.
- The rapidity distribution from the analytic solution is narrower than in heavy-ion experiments but consistent with numerical holography results for shock-wave collisions in Casalderrey-Solana (2013).

## Context

This work extends the holographic heavy-ion collision program beyond head-on collisions (Bantilan 2014) and collisions with negligible spatial eccentricity (Chesler 2015) to include off-center collisions that generate elliptic flow, triangular flow, and vorticity. The analytic solution generalizes previous exact hydrodynamic solutions (Nagy 2009, Hatta 2014) to the case of rotation around two axes, and is similar to other exact solutions in the literature (Csörgő 2003, 2006; Friess 2006). The solutions enable future studies of initial state fluctuations on smooth backgrounds, thermal fluctuations, fluid turbulence, and connections between quantum informational tools and real-time evolution in heavy-ion collisions.
