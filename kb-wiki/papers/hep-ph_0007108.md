# The initial gluon multiplicity in heavy ion collisions

> The initial gluon multiplicity per unit area per unit rapidity, dN/L^2/dη, in high energy nuclear collisions, is equal to f_N (g^2μL) (g^2μ)^2/g^2, with μ^2 proportional to the gluon density per unit area of the colliding nuclei. For an SU(2) gauge theory, we compute f_N (g^2μL)=0.14\pm 0.01 for a wide range in g^2μL. Extrapolating to SU(3), we predict dN/L^2/dηfor values of g^2μL in the range relevant to the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider. We compute the initial gluon transverse momentum distribution, dN/L^2/d^2 k_\perp, and show it to be well behaved at low k_\perp.

## Key claim

The initial gluon multiplicity per unit area per unit rapidity in high energy nuclear collisions can be expressed as a dimensionless function f_N(g^2μL) times (g^2μ)^2/g^2, where the function f_N is computed non-perturbatively for SU(2) and found to be 0.14 ± 0.01 over a wide range of g^2μL, with the caveat that the result is for SU(2) gauge theory and extrapolated to SU(3) for phenomenological predictions.

## Method

The classical effective field theory (EFT) approach to high energy nuclear scattering was discretized on a lattice in the transverse plane with boost invariance and periodic boundary conditions, using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian in 2+1 dimensions coupled to an adjoint scalar field. The Yang-Mills equations were solved numerically with initial conditions matching the pure gauge fields of the two nuclei before collision, averaging over random color charge sources distributed according to a Gaussian weight. Two independent methods were used to define the gluon number: Coulomb gauge fixing in the transverse plane with the free-field particle number formula, and a cooling method based on relaxation equations that yields a gauge-invariant total particle number.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) gauge theory, f_N(g^2μL) = 0.14 ± 0.01 for a wide range in g^2μL
- For Au-Au central collisions at RHIC energies (g^2μL ≈ 120), predicted dN/dη ≈ 950 using f_N = 0.132 ± 0.006
- For LHC energies (g^2μL ≈ 255), predicted dN/dη ≈ 4300 using f_N = 0.148 ± 0.002
- The non-perturbative coefficient c_N = 1.29 ± 0.09, obtained from f_N = 0.14 ± 0.01, compared to Mueller's estimate of order unity
- At RHIC energies, Q_s ∼ 1 GeV; at LHC, Q_s ∼ 2-3 GeV
- For g^2μL = 35.35, the finest lattice spacing used was g^2μa = 0.138
- Values of f_N from cooling method range from 0.116 ± 0.001 at g^2μL = 35.36 to 0.151 ± 0.001 at g^2μL = 297.0

## Context

This work extends the classical effective field theory approach developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the Wilson renormalization group techniques of Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, Leonidov, and Weigert (1997-1999) to compute non-perturbative gluon multiplicities in heavy ion collisions. The results provide initial conditions for subsequent transport approaches to quark-gluon plasma evolution, as discussed by Mueller (2000) and by Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2000). The predictions are in rough agreement with pQCD-based models but differ significantly from the theta-function transverse momentum distribution guessed by Mueller (2000), and the framework enables future quantitative studies of final state observables at RHIC and LHC.
