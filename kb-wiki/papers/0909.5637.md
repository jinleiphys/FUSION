# Nuclear Forces from Lattice QCD

> A method to extract nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in lattice QCD is presented. It is applied to the two nucleons on the lattice with quenched QCD simulations. By disentangling the mixing between the S-state and the D-state, we obtain central and tensor potentials in the leading order of the velocity expansion of the non-local NN potential. The spatial structure, the quark mass dependence and the velocity dependence of the NN potential are analyzed. Preliminary result in (2+1)-flavor QCD simulations is also shown.

# Nuclear Forces from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

A non-local nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential can be extracted from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in lattice QCD, with the leading-order central and tensor potentials obtained by disentangling S-state and D-state mixing in the velocity expansion. The resulting potential reproduces the characteristic repulsive core and attractive well seen in phenomenological NN potentials, but the comparison of spatial structures should be made only qualitatively because the lattice potential depends on the choice of nucleon interpolating operator.

## Method

The equal-time Bethe-Salpeter wave function for two nucleons is computed from four-point correlators on the lattice using wall sources with Coulomb gauge fixing. A non-local potential is defined from the wave function inside the interaction region (r < 2 fm) via a Schrodinger-type equation, and the leading-order velocity expansion yields central and tensor potentials by projecting onto S-wave and D-wave components in the J^P=1^+ channel. Quenched QCD simulations are performed on a 32^4 lattice with lattice spacing a=0.137 fm and pion masses of 380, 529, and 731 MeV, with additional (2+1)-flavor full QCD results at m_pi=702 MeV on a 32^3x64 lattice.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: 1/a = 1.44(2) GeV (a = 0.137 fm) determined from rho meson mass in the chiral limit, on a 32^4 lattice with beta = 5.7
- Physical lattice size: L = 4.4 fm
- Pion masses studied in quenched QCD: m_pi = 731, 529, and 380 MeV, corresponding to hopping parameters kappa = 0.1640, 0.1665, 0.1678
- Lowest effective center-of-mass energy in quenched simulations: E ranges from -0.4 MeV to -1 MeV
- For velocity dependence study: anti-periodic boundary condition yields minimum momentum |p_min| = 240 MeV and E = 50 MeV on a 32^3 x 48 lattice
- Full QCD lattice scale: a = 0.091 fm from m_pi, m_K, and m_Omega on 32^3 x 64 lattice with Iwasaki gauge action at beta = 1.90, giving spatial extension L = 2.90 fm
- Full QCD pion mass: m_pi = 702 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Luscher method (Luscher, 1991) for extracting scattering phase shifts from finite-volume lattice spectra by instead defining a non-local potential from the Bethe-Salpeter wave function in the interaction region, an approach introduced by Ishii (2007) and Aoki (2008). The method enables first-principles determination of NN potentials from QCD, complementing phenomenological potentials that fit experimental NN data. The results enable future studies of hyperon-nucleon potentials (Nemura, 2008; Nemura, 2009), three-nucleon forces, and applications to neutron star matter, with ongoing full QCD simulations by the PACS-CS collaboration needed to reach physical quark masses.
