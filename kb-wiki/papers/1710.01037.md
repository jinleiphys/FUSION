# Transverse expansion of hot magnetized Bjorken flow in heavy ion collisions

> We argue that the existence of an inhomogeneous external magnetic field can lead to radial flow in transverse plane. Our aim is to show how the introduction of a magnetic field generalizes the Bjorken flow. We investigate the effect of an inhomogeneous weak external magnetic field on the transverse expansion of in-viscid fluid created in high energy nuclear collisions. In order to simplify our calculation and compare with Gubser model, we consider the fluid under investigation to be produced in central collisions, at small impact parameter; azimuthal symmetry has been considered. In our model, we assume an inhomogeneous external magnetic field following the power-law decay in proper time and having radial inhomogeneity perpendicular to the radial velocity of the in-viscid fluid in the transverse plane; then the space time evolution of the transverse expansion of the fluid is obtained. We also show how the existence of an inhomogeneous external magnetic field modifies the energy density. Finally we use the solutions for the transverse velocity and energy density in the presence of a weak magnetic field, to estimate the transverse momentum spectrum of protons and pions emerging from the Magneto-hydrodynamic solutions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that an inhomogeneous external magnetic field, even when weak, can generate non-zero radial flow in the transverse plane of a Bjorken-expanding fluid, thereby generalizing the standard Bjorken model. The claim is hedged by the condition that the magnetic field is treated perturbatively and that the fluid is inviscid with infinite electrical conductivity, with the magnetic field energy density assumed to be much smaller than the fluid energy density (ratio \(B_c^2/\epsilon_c = 0.1\)).

## Method

The authors solve the ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamic (RMHD) conservation equations perturbatively to second order in a small parameter \(\lambda\), assuming a (2+1)-dimensional, longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken flow with azimuthal symmetry. They consider an external magnetic field pointing in the azimuthal (\(\phi\)) direction, with a power-law decay in proper time (\(\tau^n\) with \(n = -1\) or \(n = -4/3\)) and a radial inhomogeneity of the form \(b_\phi^2 \propto x_\perp e^{-\alpha x_\perp^2}\). The magnetic field is expanded in a series of Bessel functions, and the partial differential equations for the transverse velocity \(u_\perp\) and energy density correction \(\epsilon_1\) are reduced to ordinary differential equations solved analytically in terms of Bessel and hypergeometric functions, with integration constants fixed by requiring finite transverse velocity at late times.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of magnetic field energy to fluid energy density is set to \(B_c^2/\epsilon_c = 0.1\), corresponding to \(\sigma \sim 0.015\).
- The initial energy density at proper time \(\tau = 1\) fm is taken as \(\epsilon = 5.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\), from Gubser (2010).
- For the case \(n = -1\), the first ten Fourier-Bessel coefficients \(B_k^2\) (for \(\alpha = 0.1\)) are: 0.112499, 0.111212, 0.0707575, 0.0391739, 0.0231679, 0.0153799, 0.0110821, 0.0084056, 0.00661182, 0.00534074.
- For the case \(n = -4/3\), the first ten Fourier-Bessel coefficients \(B_k^2\) (for \(\alpha = 0.1\)) are: 0.04745, 0.0371507, -0.00832931, -0.0215885, -0.0146208, -0.00825935, -0.00515007, -0.00362071, -0.00269932, -0.00212629.
- The typical magnetic field in Au-Au peripheral collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is \(|eB| \sim 10 m_\pi^2\), with \(m_\pi \approx 150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analytical perturbative approach of Pu et al. (2015) for (1+1)-dimensional transverse RMHD to the case of central heavy-ion collisions with azimuthal symmetry, providing an analytical solution for the transverse expansion of a magnetized QGP. It contrasts with the Gubser flow model (Gubser, 2010), where radial flow arises from the finite transverse size of the nuclei, by showing that an inhomogeneous external magnetic field can also generate radial flow. The results enable analytical estimates of the transverse momentum spectrum of protons and pions from magnetohydrodynamic solutions, and can be compared with numerical RMHD codes such as that developed in the authors' previous work (Haddadi et al., 2016).
