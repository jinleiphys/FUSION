# Anti-flow of Mesons in the High Baryon Density Region

> The E895 and STAR experiments demonstrate that the slopes of directed flow with respect to rapidity ($dv_1/dy|_{y=0}$) of mesons are negative in the low transverse momentum ($p_T$) region, $p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$, in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0 - 3.9$ GeV. Using the transport model JAM, we investigate the directed flow of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, and $K^0$ as functions of rapidity, $p_T$, and collision energy in Au + Au collisions at the same energies as those in the E895 and STAR experiments. We find that the JAM model can qualitatively reproduce the anti-flow of $K^0_S$ observed in the E895 experiment. The $v_1$ slopes of pions and kaons are analyzed as functions of the $p_T$ window, revealing a strong $p_T$ dependence of the $v_1$ slopes. Negative $v_1$ slopes are observed in the low $p_T$ region, $p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$, while positive slopes are shown in the higher $p_T$ region. We find that the shadowing effect from spectators is crucial in generating the anti-flow of mesons at low $p_T$ in the high baryon density region.

## Key claim
Using the JAM transport model in cascade mode, the authors find that the spectator shadowing effect, rather than the kaon potential alone, is crucial for generating the anti-flow (negative $dv_1/dy|_{y=0}$) of mesons at low transverse momentum in the high baryon density region, though the model qualitatively reproduces the $K^0_S$ anti-flow observed by E895 but underestimates its strength.

## Method
The authors used the JAM hadronic transport model (version 2.1) in cascade mode to simulate Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0, 3.2, 3.5, 3.9$ GeV. They calculated the directed flow $v_1 = \langle \cos(\phi) \rangle$ for $\pi^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, and $K^0$ as functions of rapidity and transverse momentum, comparing results from default cascade mode with those from a cascade mode where spectators were removed to isolate the shadowing effect. Event centrality was defined by reference multiplicity counting charged pions, charged kaons, protons, and anti-protons within $|\eta| < 0.5$.

## Key numbers
- The spectator passing time $t_p$ ranges from 7.59 fm/$c$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.9$ GeV to 11.00 fm/$c$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0$ GeV, while the hadron freeze-out mean time $t_m$ ranges from 16.01 to 16.99 fm/$c$, giving $t_p/t_m$ ratios from 0.47 to 0.65.
- Negative $v_1$ slopes for pions and kaons are observed in the low $p_T$ region $p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$ at all four collision energies.
- The $v_1$ slope of $K^0$ becomes more negative in peripheral collisions: anti-flow occurs at $p_T < 0.8$ GeV/$c$ for 0-10% centrality, $p_T < 0.6$ GeV/$c$ for 10-40%, and $p_T < 0.4$ GeV/$c$ for 40-60% at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.9$ GeV.
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0$ GeV, the anti-flow of $K^0$ disappears entirely in the JAM cascade mode without spectators across the shown $p_T$ range.

## Context
This work extends the experimental observations of $K^0_S$ anti-flow by the E895 Collaboration (E895, 2000) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.83$ GeV and the preliminary STAR results (Liu, 2023) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0-3.9$ GeV. It contradicts the prevailing attribution of kaon anti-flow primarily to repulsive kaon potentials (Kaplan, 1986; Brown, 1991; Li, 1994; Pal, 2000) by demonstrating that spectator shadowing, previously studied for elliptic flow (STAR, 2021), is a dominant mechanism for meson anti-flow at low $p_T$ in the high baryon density region. The findings enable future studies to disentangle the contributions of nuclear mean-field effects and spectator shadowing in collective flow observables.
