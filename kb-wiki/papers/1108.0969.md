# R$ν$MDM and Lepton Flavor Violation

> A model relating radiative seesaw and minimal dark matter mass scales without beyond the standard model (SM) gauge symmetry (R$ν$MDM) is constructed. In addition to the SM particles, the R$ν$MDM contains, a Majorana fermion multiplet $N_R$ and scalar multiplet $χ$ that transform respectively as $(1,5,0)$ and $(1,6,-1/2)$ under the SM gauge group $SU(3)_C\times SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y$. The neutral component $N_R^0$ plays the role of dark matter with a mass in the range of 9 to 10 TeV. This scale also sets the lower limit for the scale for the heavy degrees of freedom in $N_R$ and $χ$ which generate light neutrino masses through the radiative seesaw mechanism. The model predicts an $N_R^0$-nucleus scattering cross section that would be accessible with future dark matter direct detection searches as well as observable effects in present and searches for charged lepton flavor violating processes, such as $l_i\to l_j γ$ and $μ- e$ conversion.

## Key claim

The RνMDM model relates the radiative seesaw and minimal dark matter mass scales without imposing any beyond-Standard-Model gauge symmetry, but the dark matter candidate (the neutral component of a Majorana fermion multiplet) must have a mass in the 9–10 TeV range to saturate the observed relic density, and the model's testability in lepton flavor violation depends on the Yukawa couplings being large enough while remaining consistent with neutrino mass constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a model extending the Standard Model with a Majorana fermion multiplet \(N_R\) transforming as \((1,5,0)\) and a scalar multiplet \(\chi\) transforming as \((1,6,-1/2)\) under \(SU(3)_C\times SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y\). Neutrino masses are generated at one-loop order via the radiative seesaw mechanism, while the neutral component \(N_R^0\) is stabilized as dark matter by an accidental \(Z_2\) symmetry that follows from gauge invariance and renormalizability. The authors then compute the dark matter relic density, neutrino mass matrix, and charged lepton flavor violating processes such as \(\mu \to e \gamma\) and \(\mu-e\) conversion.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter mass \(m_N = (9.6 \pm 0.2)\) TeV when Sommerfeld enhancement is included, saturating the relic density \(\Omega_{DM}h^2 = 0.110 \pm 0.006\).
- Direct detection cross section predicted to be \(10^{-44}\) cm\(^2\), below current CDMSII and Xenon100 limits but testable at future superCDMS and xenon-1ton experiments.
- For a sample normal hierarchy, Yukawa couplings are of order \(Y \sim 0.03\)–\(0.1\) when \(\tilde{\lambda}_{H\chi} = 10^{-7}\) and \(m_N = m_\chi = 9.6\) TeV.
- For a sample inverted hierarchy, Yukawa couplings are of order \(Y \sim 0.05\)–\(0.11\) under the same conditions.

## Context

This work extends the minimal dark matter idea of Cirelli et al. (2006) and the radiative seesaw model of Ma (2006) by combining them without imposing discrete symmetries beyond the Standard Model gauge group. The model predicts observable signals in future dark matter direct detection experiments and in charged lepton flavor violation searches such as MEG, Mu2E, COMET, and PRISM, while being unlikely to be testable at the LHC due to the multi-TeV mass scale.
