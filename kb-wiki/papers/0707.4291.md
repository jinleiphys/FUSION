# Specific Density Of Binding Enerfy Of Core In Beta - Stable Nuclei is 2.57 MeV/fm^3

> Recently an alpha-cluster model based on the pn-pair interactions with using the isospin invariance of nuclear force has been proposed. According to the model the excess neutron pairs fill out the free space in the core determined by the difference in the charge and matter radii of the alpha-clusters. Then the number of excess neutrons in beta-stable nuclei depends on the number of the core alpha-clusters. In such a representation the specific density of binding energy of core is the only parameter to fit the experimental binding energies of beta-stable nuclei and it turned out to be a constant value equal to 2.57 MeV/fm^3. Knowing the value allows one to estimate the size of a nucleus from its experimental binding energy.

# Specific Density of Binding Energy of Core in Beta-Stable Nuclei is 2.57 MeV/fm³

## Key claim

The specific density of binding energy of the core (the interior alpha-cluster structure) in beta-stable nuclei is claimed to be a constant value of 2.57 MeV/fm³, but this holds only under the model's assumption that the number of excess neutron pairs is determined by the number of core alpha-clusters and that the peripheral compound cluster configuration is correctly identified for each nucleus.

## Method

The author extends a previously proposed alpha-cluster model (Nie, earlier works) by dividing each nucleus into a core of alpha-clusters and a peripheral compound cluster of 1 to 8 alpha-clusters. The binding energy is calculated as the sum of the core binding energy (obtained from the specific density multiplied by core volume) plus the experimental binding energy of the peripheral compound cluster, minus the Coulomb energy between the peripheral cluster and the core. The number of excess neutron pairs is determined from a phenomenological formula fitted to experimental separation energies of 27 nn-pairs, and the specific density is the single free parameter adjusted to reproduce experimental binding energies across the beta-stability valley.

## Key numbers

- Specific density of core binding energy: ρ = 2.57 MeV/fm³
- Radius of an alpha-cluster in the core: r_α = 1.60 fm (obtained from fitting experimental radii of nuclei with N_coreα >> N_pα)
- Experimental radius of ⁴He (peripheral alpha-cluster): r_⁴He = 1.71 fm
- Binding energy of ⁸Be (N_pα = 2): 56.5 MeV; ¹²C (N_pα = 3): 92.2 MeV; ¹⁶O (N_pα = 4): 127.6 MeV; ²⁰Ne (N_pα = 5): 160.6 MeV
- Binding energy of ⁷Li (N_1pα = 1.5): 39.2 MeV; ¹¹B (N_1pα = 2.5): 76.2 MeV; ¹⁵N (N_1pα = 3.5): 115.5 MeV; ¹⁹F (N_1pα = 4.5): 147.8 MeV; ²³Na (N_1pα = 5.5): 186.6 MeV
- Energy of an alpha-cluster: ε_α = 28.296 MeV; energy per short-range bond between alpha-clusters: ε_αα = 2.425 MeV
- Nuclear force energy of an alpha-cluster: ε^{nuc}_α = 29.060 MeV; nuclear force interaction energy between two alpha-clusters: ε^{nuc}_αα = 4.350 MeV; Coulomb energy between two alpha-clusters: ε^C_αα = 1.925 MeV
- Formula for excess neutron pair energy: E_ΔN = (21.93 - 0.762 N_nn^{2/3}) N_nn (in MeV)
- Coulomb energy formula: E^C = 1.849 (N_α)^{5/3} (in MeV)
- Radius of last alpha-cluster position: R_p = 2.168 (N_α - 4)^{1/3} (in fm)
- Example calculation: ¹⁴²Nd (Z=60) has E_exp = 1185 MeV, yielding N_pα = 4 and calculated radius R = 5.02 fm

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model previously developed by the same author (Nie, earlier works 2005-2006) which was based on pn-pair interactions and isospin invariance of nuclear force. The model provides an alternative to the liquid-drop model (Weizsäcker formula) for calculating binding energies and radii of beta-stable nuclei, claiming comparable accuracy (a few MeV). The key finding that the specific density of core binding energy is constant enables a new method to estimate nuclear size from experimental binding energy, and the model predicts that the number of peripheral alpha-clusters increases from 2-5 for stable nuclei to 5-8 for nuclei with Z ≥ 80 due to Coulomb repulsion pushing clusters outward.
