# Analytic solution for relativistic transverse flow at the softest point

> We obtain an extension of Bjorken's 1+1 dimensional scaling relativistic flow solution to relativistic transverse velocities with cylindrical symmetry in 1+3 dimensions at constant, homogeneous pressure (vanishing sound velocity). This can be the situation during a first order phase transition converting quark matter into hadron matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

This paper presents an analytic solution for relativistic transverse flow with cylindrical symmetry in 1+3 dimensions, but only for the special case of constant, homogeneous pressure (vanishing sound velocity), which can occur during a first-order phase transition in heavy ion collisions. The solution is not generalizable to arbitrary equations of state; for a general equation of state only a perturbative expansion in mild transverse velocities is possible.

## Method

The author extends Bjorken's 1+1 dimensional scaling flow solution by introducing a vierbein basis adapted to cylindrical symmetry and longitudinal boost invariance. The relativistic Euler and continuity equations are solved analytically under the condition of constant pressure (zero sound velocity), using a factorizing ansatz for the transverse velocity. The resulting partial differential equation yields a closed-form solution for the transverse velocity field and the energy density as functions of longitudinal proper time and cylindrical radius.

## Key numbers

- The transverse flow velocity solution is given by \( v = \alpha r / (1 + \alpha \tau) \), where \( \tau \) is longitudinal proper time and \( r \) is cylindrical radius.
- For a bag-model quark-gluon plasma with \( \sigma = 37 \) degrees of freedom and a pion gas with \( h = 3 \) degrees of freedom, the mixed phase lifetime is \( \tau_1 \approx 2.31 \tau_0 \).
- The maximum radial extent of the mixed phase is \( r_b^{\text{max}} \approx 1.14 R_1 \), occurring at \( \tau^{\text{max}} \approx 1.45 \tau_0 \).
- For a CERN SPS Pb+Pb experiment with \( \tau_0 \approx 5 \) fm/c, the conversion to hadrons reaches zero radius in about 11.5 fm/c.
- For an initial transverse velocity \( v_0 = 0.6 \) at the radial edge \( R_0 = 7 \) fm, the mixed phase lasts about \( \tau_b \approx 17.5 \) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends Bjorken's (1983) 1+1 dimensional scaling flow solution to include relativistic transverse flow, and generalizes earlier nonrelativistic analytic solutions by Csorgo and Csernai (various years) and perturbative relativistic treatments by Russian authors. The solution applies specifically at the "softest point" of the equation of state, a concept suggested by Shuryak as a signal of the quark-hadron phase transition and investigated numerically by Rischke and Dumitru. The analytic result enables estimation of the mixed-phase lifetime and radial evolution during hadronization in heavy ion collisions, showing that the cooling law interpolates between one-dimensional Bjorken scaling (\( 1/\tau \)) and three-dimensional spherical scaling (\( 1/\tau^3 \)).
