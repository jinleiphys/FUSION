> The restriction of $Ïƒ^{*}$ and $Ï•$ on the protoneutron star (PNS) PSR J0348+0432 is described by the relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) through choosing the effective coupling constants. ## Key claim

The paper claims that including the strange mesons $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$ in the relativistic mean field description of the protoneutron star PSR J0348+0432 (at an entropy per baryon S=1) produces only very small changes in the star's radius, moment of inertia, and surface gravitational redshift, indicating that these mesons play a minor role in the protoneutron star core.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) with the GL85 nucleon coupling parameter set to model the protoneutron star PSR J0348+0432 at finite temperature, described by a fixed entropy per baryon S=1. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, and computed the moment of inertia and surface gravitational redshift for two cases: one including the strange mesons $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$ and one without them. The hyperon coupling constants were tuned so that the maximum mass of the protoneutron star matched the observed mass of PSR J0348+0432 (2.010 $M_{\odot}$) in both cases.

## Key numbers

- Mass-radius relation with $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$: (2.010 $M_{\odot}$, 12.6520 km)
- Mass-radius relation without $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$: (2.010 $M_{\odot}$, 12.6170 km)
- Moment of inertia with $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$: (2.010 $M_{\odot}$, 1.559 $\times 10^{45}$ g.cm$^{2}$)
- Moment of inertia without $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$: (2.010 $M_{\odot}$, 1.510 $\times 10^{45}$ g.cm$^{2}$)
- Surface gravitational redshift with $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$: (2.010 $M_{\odot}$, 0.3747)
- Surface gravitational redshift without $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$: (2.010 $M_{\odot}$, 0.3701)
- The hyperon coupling constant $x_{\omega}$ (ratio of hyperon to nucleon omega coupling) was tuned to 0.847 for the case without $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$, and to 0.874 for the case with $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$, to reproduce the observed mass of 2.010 $M_{\odot}$.
- The first hyperon to appear ($\Lambda$) appears at baryon density 0.248 fm$^{-3}$ (1.71 $\rho_0$) in both cases.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron stars at zero temperature, such as the work by Xian-Feng Zhao (2016) on the effect of $\sigma^{*}$ and $\phi$ on the surface gravitational redshift of PSR J0348+0432, by including finite-temperature effects described by an entropy per baryon S=1. The paper also builds on the observation of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 by Antoniadis et al. (2013) and uses the GL85 and GL97 parameter sets from the relativistic mean field literature. The results enable future studies of the role of strange mesons in protoneutron star interiors and suggest that their influence is minor for this particular star, leaving open the question of whether they become more important for other masses or entropies.
