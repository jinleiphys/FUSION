# A systematic study of the sensitivity of triangular flow to the initial state fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Experimental data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) suggests that the quark gluon plasma behaves almost like an ideal fluid. Due to its short lifetime, many QGP properties can only be inferred indirectly through a comparison of the final state measurements with transport model calculations. Among the current phenomena of interest are the interdependencies between two collective flow phenomena, elliptic and triangular flow. The former is mostly related to the initial geometry and collective expansion of the system whereas the latter is sensitive to the fluctuations of the initial state. For our investigation we use a hybrid transport model based on the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport approach using an ideal hydrodynamic expansion for the hot and dense stage. Using UrQMD initial conditions for an Au-Au collision, particles resulting from a collision are mapped into an energy density distribution that is evolved event-by-event with a hydrodynamic calculation. By averaging these distributions over different numbers of events, we have studied how the granularity/smoothness of the distribution affects the initial eccentricity, the initial triangularity, and the resulting flow components. The average elliptic flow in non central collisions is not sensitive to the granularity, while triangular flow is. The triangularity might thus provide a good measure of the amount of initial state fluctuations that is necessary to reproduce the experimental data.

## Key claim

Triangular flow is directly sensitive to the granularity of the initial state energy density distribution, while elliptic flow in non-central collisions is not; the full event-by-event setup (no averaging) provides the best description of PHENIX triangular flow data, though this constitutes a lower bound on initial state granularity because viscosity would dilute fluctuations faster.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid transport model combining the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) approach with a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic expansion for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV. They varied the initial state granularity by averaging over different numbers of events (n=1 to n=25) before mapping particles into an energy density distribution for the hydrodynamic evolution, then computed elliptic and triangular flow coefficients via the event-plane method matching the PHENIX analysis.

## Key numbers

- 300 initial profiles were generated for each of 6 different granularities at two impact parameters (b=2 fm and b=7 fm) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200A\) GeV.
- The hydrodynamic evolution used a freeze-out criterion of \(\epsilon \leq 1.022\) GeV/fm\(^3\) and the DE-EoS from Petersen (2010).
- The transition from UrQMD to hydrodynamics occurred at \(t_{\rm start}=0.5\) fm after the nuclei passed through each other.
- Triangular flow from the full event-by-event setup (n=1) was closest to PHENIX data; averaging over n=5 events still gave reasonable event-plane resolution, while n=25 gave practically zero triangular flow.

## Context

This work extends the study of higher-order flow coefficients (triangular flow \(v_3\)) as a probe of initial state fluctuations, building on prior work by Alver (2010), Schenke (2010), and Petersen (2010) that recognized the importance of event-by-event fluctuations. The approach contrasts with simplified models using linearized hydrodynamics or rotated averaged initial conditions (Teaney 2010, Qin 2010, Qiu 2011, Staig 2011). The results enable future multi-parameter sensitivity analyses to constrain both initial state granularity and viscosity by comparing to experimental data from PHENIX and ALICE.
