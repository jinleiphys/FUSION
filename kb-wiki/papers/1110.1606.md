# Comparison of kaon and pion valence quark distributions in a statistical model

> We have calculated the Bjorken-x dependence of the kaon and pion valence quark distributions in a statistical model. Each meson is described by a Fock state expansion in terms of quarks, antiquarks and gluons. Although Drell-Yan experiments have measured the pion valence quark distributions directly, the kaon valence quark distributions have only been deduced from the measurement of the ratio $\bar{u}_K(x)/\bar{u}_π(x)$. We show that, using no free parameters, our model predicts the decrease of this ratio with increasing x.

## Key claim

Using a statistical model with no free parameters, the authors predict that the ratio of kaon to pion valence quark distributions, \(u_K(x)/u_\pi(x)\), decreases with increasing Bjorken-\(x\), but the agreement with experimental data depends on whether the strange antiquark is treated as massless or given a current quark mass of 100 MeV, with the massless calculation agreeing better with experiment.

## Method

The authors applied a statistical model originally developed for the proton by Zhang and collaborators (2001-2002) to the pion and kaon. Each meson is described by a Fock state expansion in terms of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, with state probabilities determined by detailed balance between splitting and recombination processes. Momentum distributions for each \(n\)-parton state were generated using the Monte Carlo event generator RAMBO, and the resulting parton distribution functions (PDFs) were evolved from a starting scale of \(Q_0^2 = 1.96\) GeV\(^2\) using Miyama and Kumano's BF1 evolution code.

## Key numbers

- Average number of partons in the pion: \(\bar{n}_\pi = 4.74\); in the kaon: \(\bar{n}_K = 5.23\).
- Momentum fraction carried by valence quarks in the pion: \(\int_0^1 x u_\pi(x) dx = 0.50\); in the kaon (massless \(\bar{s}\)): \(\int_0^1 x u_K(x) dx = 0.46\).
- Probability of finding the meson in the leading valence Fock state: 0.14 for the pion, 0.10 for the kaon.
- For the kaon with a massive \(\bar{s}\) (100 MeV), the momentum fraction carried by the \(u\) valence quark drops to 0.20, and the \(\bar{s}\) carries 0.35.
- Starting scale for evolution: \(Q_0^2 = 1.96\) GeV\(^2\), determined by matching first and second moments of the pion valence distribution to those of Sutton et al. (1992) at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the statistical model of Zhang and collaborators (2001-2002), which successfully predicted the integrated asymmetry \(\bar{d} - \bar{u} = 0.124\) in the proton, to meson systems. The authors compare their results to Drell-Yan measurements by Badier et al. (1980) and to other theoretical calculations including the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach of Nguyen et al. (2011), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of Davidson and Arriola (2001), and the valon model of Arash (2004). The paper enables future higher-statistics measurements of pion and kaon parton distributions, especially at low \(x\) where current data are insufficient to distinguish between models.
