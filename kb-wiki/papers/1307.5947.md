# Anomalous-Magnetic-Moment Effects in a Strongly Magnetized and Dense Medium

> We investigate the quantum corrections of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for fermions in the presence of a strong magnetic field using the Ritus's approach. At strong fields the particles get different AMM's depending on the LL's. This result is different from what is obtained with the Schwinger's approximation at weak field where the AMM is independent of the LL. We analyze the significance of the AMM contribution to the Equation of State (EoS) of the magnetized system, in the weak and strong field approximations.

## Key claim

The anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of fermions in a strong magnetic field depends on the Landau level (LL) index, with only the lowest Landau level (LLL) acquiring a nonzero AMM that grows as the square logarithm of the field; this contradicts the Schwinger approximation (valid only at weak field) where the AMM is independent of LL and grows linearly with the field, and the authors find that the AMM's effect on the equation of state is negligibly small in both regimes.

## Method

The authors used Ritus's approach to diagonalize the one-loop fermion self-energy in a constant uniform magnetic field, extracting the AMM coefficient \(T^l\) for each Landau level from the Schwinger-Dyson equations. They then derived the dispersion relations including the AMM, computed the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature including vacuum and statistical contributions, and numerically evaluated the magnetization, energy density, and parallel/perpendicular pressures in the strong-field limit (\(eB \gg \mu^2 \gg m^2\)) where only the LLL contributes significantly.

## Key numbers

- The LLL AMM contribution to the self-energy in the strong-field limit is \(E^0 \simeq \frac{\alpha}{4\pi} \log^2(\widehat{m}^2)\), where \(\widehat{m}^2 = m^2/(2eB)\).
- Using the Schwinger AMM in QED, the threshold field for zero rest energy is \(B \simeq 10^{15}\) G; in QCD for u-quark mass, the threshold is \(B \simeq 10^{18}\) G.
- The numerical calculations were performed with \(m/\mu \simeq 0.02\) for the ratio of fermion mass to chemical potential.
- The renormalized vacuum thermodynamic potential at zero AMM in the strong-field limit is \(\Omega_{AMM=0}^R(B,0,0) = -\frac{\alpha}{6\pi} B^2 \ln\left(\frac{eB}{m^2}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetized fermion systems by calculating the AMM beyond the Schwinger approximation (Schwinger, 1948), which is only valid for \(eB \ll m^2\). The authors note that earlier works (e.g., EoS-AMM1, EoS-AMM2) claimed significant AMM effects on the equation of state at strong fields, but the present results contradict those claims, finding negligible AMM effects on the energy density, pressures, and magnetization. The results enable more reliable modeling of neutron star interiors where strong magnetic fields are present, by providing the correct AMM behavior in the strong-field regime.
