# Radial and elliptic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider from viscous hydrodynamic

> A comprehensive viscous hydrodynamic fit of spectra and elliptic flow for charged hadrons and identified pions and protons from Au+Au collisions of all centralities measured at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider is performed and used as the basis for predicting the analogous observables for Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider at sqrt(s)=2.76 and 5.5 A TeV. Comparison with recent measurements of the elliptic flow of charged hadrons by the ALICE experiment shows that the model slightly over-predicts the data if the same (constant) specific shear viscosity eta/s is assumed at both collision energies. In spite of differences in our assumptions for the equation of state, the freeze-out temperature, the chemical composition at freeze-out, and the starting time for the hydrodynamic evolution, our results agree remarkably well with those of Luzum [M. Luzum, Phys. Rev. C 83, 044911 (2011)], indicating robustness of the hydrodynamic model extrapolations. Future measurements of the centrality and transverse momentum dependence of spectra and elliptic flow for identified hadrons predicted here will further test the model and shed light on possible variations of the quark-gluon transport coefficients between RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim
The authors predict that a constant specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.20, fitted to RHIC Au+Au data, slightly over-predicts the charged hadron elliptic flow measured by ALICE in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 A TeV, indicating that the same (constant) η/s may not hold at both collision energies.

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the lattice QCD-based equation of state s95p-PCE with MC-KLN initial conditions, a constant specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.20, thermalization time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, chemical freeze-out at Tchem = 165 MeV, and kinetic decoupling at Tdec = 120 MeV. They fitted the model to charged hadron spectra and elliptic flow from 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, then extrapolated to Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 and 5.5 A TeV keeping all parameters unchanged, and compared with ALICE data.

## Key numbers
- η/s = 0.20 (constant, temperature-independent) used for both RHIC and LHC calculations, fitted to RHIC Au+Au data with MC-KLN initial conditions.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, the fireball lifetime increases by 19% (at 2.76 A TeV) and 24% (at 5.5 A TeV) relative to RHIC Au+Au; for peripheral (70-80%) collisions, the increase is 34% and 41%, respectively.
- Average radial flow velocity in central (0-5%) collisions increases by 5% (2.76 A TeV) and 7% (5.5 A TeV) relative to RHIC; in peripheral (70-80%) collisions, by 9% and 11%.
- The hydrodynamic model over-predicts the pT-integrated charged hadron v2 at LHC by 10-15% compared to ALICE v2{4} data.
- From RHIC to LHC, the hydrodynamically computed integrated v2 in mid-central to mid-peripheral collisions increases by about 30%.
- The MC-KLN model over-predicts the measured charged multiplicity from central 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions by about 10% before renormalization.

## Context
This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic analysis of Song et al. (2010) from RHIC Au+Au collisions to LHC Pb+Pb collisions, using the same constant η/s = 0.20 and MC-KLN initial conditions. The results agree remarkably well with those of Luzum (2011) despite differences in equation of state, freeze-out temperature, chemical composition, and starting time, indicating robustness of hydrodynamic extrapolations. Future measurements of centrality and pT dependence of spectra and elliptic flow for identified hadrons will test the model and probe possible variations of quark-gluon transport coefficients between RHIC and LHC energies.
