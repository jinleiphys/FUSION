# Spin Polarized versus Chiral Condensate in Quark Matter at Finite Temperature and Density

> It is shown that the spin polarized condensate appears in quark matter at high baryon density and low temperature due to the tensor-type four-point interaction in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type model as a low energy effective theory of quantum chromodynamics. It is indicated within this low energy effective model that the chiral symmetry is broken again by the spin polarized condensate as increasing the quark number density, while the chiral symmetry restoration occurs in which the chiral condensate disappears at a certain density.

# Spin Polarized versus Chiral Condensate in Quark Matter at Finite Temperature and Density

## Key claim

Within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a tensor-type four-point interaction, the spin polarized condensate appears at high baryon density and low temperature, and the chiral condensate is broken again by this spin polarized condensate as quark number density increases, even after chiral symmetry restoration has occurred at a lower density. The two phases (chiral condensed and spin polarized) do not coexist under the parameter set adopted, and the spin polarized phase is realized instead of the two-flavor color superconducting phase in the case with only two flavors.

## Method

The authors use an NJL-type model with SU(2)-flavor symmetry, including a tensor-type four-point interaction term in addition to the standard scalar-pseudoscalar interaction. They apply the mean field approximation to obtain the thermodynamic potential at both zero and finite temperature, introducing a quark chemical potential to study finite density. The chiral condensate M and the spin polarized condensate F are treated as order parameters, and the thermodynamic potential is minimized numerically as a function of these condensates for various chemical potentials and temperatures, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- The three-momentum cutoff parameter is set to Λ = 0.631 GeV, the scalar coupling constant G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2}, and the tensor coupling constant G_T = 11.0 GeV^{-2} (ratio G_T/G_S = 2.0).
- The current quark mass is taken as m_0 = 0.0 GeV (chiral limit).
- For the chiral condensate at zero temperature with F=0, the thermodynamic potential shows two local minima when μ = 0.33–0.34 GeV, indicating a first-order phase transition.
- The spin polarized phase appears at zero temperature when the chemical potential exceeds approximately μ = 0.42 GeV (with M=0).
- For the spin polarized phase at zero temperature, the critical chemical potential decreases as G_T increases: at G_T/G_S = 1.5 the transition occurs around μ = 0.54 GeV, while at G_T/G_S = 2.5 it occurs around μ = 0.33 GeV.
- At finite temperature, the phase diagram shows a critical endpoint for the chiral condensed to normal phase transition near μ = 0.31 GeV and T = 0.046 GeV.
- The phase transition from the normal quark phase to the spin polarized phase is always of second order with no endpoint.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on spin polarized phases in two-flavor (Matsuoka et al., 2014) and three-flavor (Matsuoka et al., 2015) quark matter at zero temperature, now including finite temperature effects. It also builds on earlier work (Matsuoka et al., 2013) that introduced the tensor-type interaction. The results indicate that the spin polarized phase may compete with the color superconducting phase (Alford et al., 2008; Rapp et al., 2000; Iida and Baym, 2001) in the high-density, low-temperature region of the QCD phase diagram, and may have implications for the spontaneous magnetization of compact stars such as magnetars (Duncan and Thompson, 1992; Thompson and Duncan, 1993; Kouveliotou et al., 1998). Future work should investigate the role of charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium for neutron star physics, and whether the spin polarized phase survives at finite temperature instead of the color superconducting phase.
