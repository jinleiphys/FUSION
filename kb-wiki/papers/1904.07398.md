# Large-amplitude quadrupole shape mixing probed by the $(p,p^\prime)$ reaction : a model analysis

> To discuss a possible observation of large-amplitude nuclear shape mixing by nuclear reaction, we employ a simple collective model and evaluate transition densities, with which the differential cross sections are obtained through the microscopic coupled-channel calculation. Assuming the spherical-to-prolate shape transition, we focus on large-amplitude shape mixing associated with the softness of the collective potential in the $β$ direction. We introduce a simple model based on the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, which simulates a chain of isotopes that exhibit spherical-to-prolate shape phase transition. Taking $^{154}$Sm as an example and controlling the model parameters, we study how the large-amplitude shape mixing affects the elastic and inelastic proton scatterings. The calculated results suggest that the inelastic cross section of the $2_2^+$ state tells us an important role of the quadrupole shape mixing.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the inelastic differential cross section for the $2_2^+$ state in proton scattering can reveal the role of large-amplitude quadrupole shape mixing, but this is a model-based analysis using a simple phenomenological collective Hamiltonian rather than a direct experimental observation, and the authors emphasize their goal is to grasp gross features rather than reproduce specific experimental data.

## Method

The authors constructed a five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian with a phenomenological collective potential and inertial masses to simulate spherical, prolate, and spherical-to-prolate transitional nuclei. They solved the collective Schrodinger equation to obtain wave functions and transition densities, then used these as input for microscopic coupled-channel calculations with the JLM complex nucleon-nucleon interaction to compute proton elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections for $^{154}$Sm at incident energies of 35, 65, and 66.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The $R_{4/2} = E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)$ ratios obtained were 2.0 for the spherical, 2.2 for the transitional, and 3.0 for the prolate parameter sets
- The deformed Woods-Saxon potential used mesh points with $\Delta\beta = 0.03$ and $\Delta\gamma = 3.0^\circ$ over 20 points each
- The collective potential for the prolate case had an absolute minimum around $\beta = 0.35$
- The inertial mass parameter $D$ was set to 50 MeV$^{-1}$ for all three calculations
- The range parameter $t$ of the JLM interaction was fixed to 1.2 according to the original JLM reference
- The experimental $R_{4/2}$ for $^{154}$Sm is 3.2 and the experimental $\beta_2$ is 0.34
- The experimental $R_{4/2}$ for $^{150}$Sm is 2.3

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear shape mixing dynamics using electron and nucleon scatterings, referencing the work of Phan (1988), Boeglin (1988), and Sandor (1991) who used microscopically derived collective Hamiltonians for electron scattering, as well as Yao (2015) who developed a beyond-mean-field framework. It also builds on the phenomenological approach of Sato (2010) for triaxial deformation dynamics, and connects to 1980s nucleon scattering analyses by Mirzaa (1985), Delaroche (1987), Clegg (1989), and Hicks (1989) that investigated gamma-softness in the Pt-Os region. The results suggest that measuring inelastic scattering to the $2_2^+$ state in transitional nuclei could provide a probe of large-amplitude shape mixing, enabling future experimental tests of collective model predictions.
