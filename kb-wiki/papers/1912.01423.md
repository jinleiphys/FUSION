# Photon spectra and anisotropic flow in heavy ion collisions at the top RHIC energy within the integrated hydrokinetic model with photon hadronization emission

> The integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKM) is applied to analyse the results of direct photon spectra as well as elliptic and triangular flow measurements in 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC for different centrality bins. Experiments detect the strong centrality dependence of photon elliptic and triangular flow as increasing $v_n(p_T)$-coefficients towards peripheral collisions. The photon production in the model is accumulated from the different sources along with the process of relativistic heavy ion collision developing. Those include the primary hard photons from the parton collisions at the very early stage of the process, the photons generated at the pre-thermal phase of dense matter evolution, then thermal photons at partially equilibrated hydrodynamic quark-gluon stage, together with radiation displaying a confinement and, finally, from the hadron gas phase. Along the way a hadronic medium evolution is treated in two distinct, in a sense opposite, approaches: chemically equilibrium and chemically non-equilibrium, namely, chemically frozen expansion. We find the description of direct photon spectra, elliptic and triangular flow are significantly improved, similar to that found in iHKM for the LHC energies, if an additional portion of photon radiation associated with the confinement processes, the "hadronization photons", is included into consideration.

## Key claim

The integrated HydroKinetic Model (iHKM) can describe direct photon spectra, elliptic flow, and triangular flow in 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC significantly better when an additional "hadronization photon" component is included, but the effective intensity parameter for this component differs between RHIC (beta = 0.04) and LHC (beta = 0.02), possibly due to different transverse expansion rates affecting the coalescence mechanism.

## Method

The iHKM was applied to 200A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC, modeling matter evolution through five stages: initial state generation via Monte-Carlo Glauber (GLISSANDO) at tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c, a prethermal stage with gradual thermalization up to tau_th = 1 fm/c, viscous hydrodynamic evolution (eta/s = 0.08) with Laine-Schroder equation of state, particlization at T = 165 MeV, and hadron gas evolution. Photon production was accumulated from prompt hard photons (pQCD via JETPHOX), prethermal photons from the hydrodynamic component, thermal photons from QGP and hadron gas using rates from Arnold (2001) and Turbide (2004), and hadronization photons parametrized as a thermal-like emission from the T = 165 MeV hypersurface. The hadronic stage was treated in two opposite approaches: chemically equilibrated evolution and chemically frozen (non-equilibrium) evolution using the earlier HKM formalism.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c; thermalization time tau_th = 1 fm/c; relaxation parameter tau_rel = 0.25 fm/c
- Maximal initial energy density epsilon_0 = 235 GeV/fm^3 for most central collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.08
- Particlization/hadronization temperature T_h = 165 MeV (corresponding to energy density epsilon = 0.5 GeV/fm^3)
- Hadronization photon intensity parameter beta = d_c * gamma_hadr = 0.04 for RHIC (compared to beta = 0.02 for LHC)
- Prompt photon p_T range calculated: 0.5 GeV/c to 6 GeV/c
- Prethermal photon emission time interval: 0.1 fm/c to 1.0 fm/c
- Hadron gas photon emission calculated down to T = 100 MeV (with checks at 115 MeV and 80 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the iHKM approach previously applied to LHC energies (ihkm-photon, 2018) to RHIC top energy, addressing the "direct photon flow puzzle" observed by PHENIX Collaboration. The model had already successfully described bulk hadron observables at RHIC (ihkm-rhic, 2018) including particle yields, spectra, and elliptic flow. The hadronization photon mechanism builds on phenomenological proposals from Campanelli (2009), Pratt (2010), and Itakura (2015) for soft photon radiation during confinement. The results enable systematic comparison of photon production mechanisms across RHIC and LHC energies, though the energy-dependent hadronization parameter requires further theoretical elaboration.
