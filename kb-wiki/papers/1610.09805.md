# Efimov Physics: a review

> This article reviews theoretical and experimental advances in Efimov physics, an array of quantum few-body and many-body phenomena arising for particles interacting via short-range resonant interactions, that is based on the appearance of a scale-invariant three-body attraction theoretically discovered by Vitaly Efimov in 1970. This three-body effect was originally proposed to explain the binding of nuclei such as the triton and the Hoyle state of carbon-12, and later considered as a simple explanation for the existence of some halo nuclei. It was subsequently evidenced in trapped ultra-cold atomic clouds and in diffracted molecular beams of gaseous helium. These experiments revealed that the previously undetermined three-body parameter introduced in the Efimov theory to stabilise the three-body attraction typically scales with the range of atomic interactions. The few- and many-body consequences of the Efimov attraction have been since investigated theoretically, and are expected to be observed in a broader spectrum of physical systems.

## Key claim

This review argues that Efimov physics, a set of quantum few-body and many-body phenomena based on a scale-invariant three-body attraction, has been confirmed experimentally in ultracold atomic gases and helium trimers, but the strictest evidence for the discrete scale invariance (the geometric series of consecutive three-body bound states) was only obtained in 2014, 44 years after Efimov's original prediction.

## Method

The authors provide a comprehensive theoretical review organized by physical situation (identical bosons, mass-imbalanced systems, different dimensions, etc.), presenting the Efimov universal theory via hyperspherical coordinates and the Bethe-Peierls boundary condition. They derive the effective three-body attraction from the free Schrödinger equation with Faddeev decomposition, and survey experimental observations across nuclear physics, ultracold atoms, and helium cluster experiments, comparing results to the universal scaling predictions.

## Key numbers

* The universal scaling factor between consecutive Efimov trimers for identical bosons is a geometric series, with the energy spectrum accumulating at the zero-energy threshold.
* The universal dimer binding energy near unitarity is approximately ħ²/(ma²), where a is the scattering length and m the particle mass.
* The resonance condition for Efimov physics requires |a| ≫ b, where b is the range of the interaction.
* The s-wave phase shift at low energy is δ₀ ∼ −arctan(ka) for k ≪ b⁻¹.
* The scattering cross section at unitarity reaches its maximal value σ = 4π/k² × sin²δ₀ = 4π/k².
* The two-body wave function in the region b ≪ r ≪ k⁻¹ has the form ψ(r) ∝ 1/r − 1/a.
* The hyper-radius is defined as R² = r² + ρ² = (2/3)(r₁₂² + r₂₃² + r₃₁²).

## Context

This review extends the original theoretical work of Efimov (1970) and the subsequent analytical confirmation by Amado and Noble (1972). It covers the experimental breakthrough by Kraemer et al. (2006) observing the first Efimov trimer in ultracold caesium, the observation of four-body bound states tied to Efimov states by Ferlaino et al. (2009), and the 2014 observations of consecutive Efimov states by Huang et al. (Innsbruck), Tung et al. (Chicago), and Pires et al. (Heidelberg), as well as the imaging of the helium-4 second trimer by Kunitski et al. (2015). The review enables future work on Efimov physics in broader physical systems including nuclear, condensed matter, and high-energy physics, and identifies the van der Waals universality of the three-body parameter as a key open question resolved by Wang (2012) and Naidon (2014).
