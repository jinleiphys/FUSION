# Pion photo- and electroproduction in relativistic baryon ChPT

## Key claim
The authors present a manifestly Lorentz-invariant (relativistic) baryon chiral perturbation theory (RChPT) calculation up to order q^4 that can describe all pion photo- and electroproduction channels in the threshold region, but the calculation does not include the Delta resonance explicitly and therefore cannot fully describe its impact on the M_{1+} multipole above 170 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed a manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation up to and including chiral order q^4, using the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme to solve the power-counting problem. They fixed the low-energy constants (LECs) by fitting experimental data in all available reaction channels for pion photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon in the threshold region. The results are made accessible via a web interface called chiral MAID.

## Key numbers
- The M_{1+} multipole, related to the Delta resonance, shows rising data above 170 MeV that the calculation cannot fully describe due to the absence of an explicit Delta resonance.
- For electroproduction, the beam asymmetry A_{LT'} is shown at constant Q^2 = 0.05 GeV^2, Theta_pi = 90 degrees, Phi_pi = 90 degrees, and epsilon = 0.93 as a function of Delta W above threshold.
- The total cross section sigma_total = sigma_T + epsilon sigma_L is shown as a function of Q^2 for different center-of-mass energies above threshold Delta W in MeV.

## Context
This work extends the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner for pion photo- and electroproduction near threshold, which had been the standard tool. The authors compare their results with the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model of Kamalov (2001) and the covariant, unitary chiral approach of Gasparyan and Lutz (2010). The calculation enables a consistent description of all pion photo- and electroproduction processes in the threshold region within a single chiral perturbation theory framework, and the results are publicly available through the chiral MAID web interface.
