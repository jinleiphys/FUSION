# Status of three flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory

> I review the present status of three flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory in the heavy fermion formalism. It is argued that precise calculations have to include all terms quadratic in the quark masses. As examples, I consider the chiral expansion of the octet baryon masses, the baryon magnetic moments and kaon photoproduction off nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that precise calculations in three-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory must include all terms quadratic in the quark masses (order \(q^4\)), but the author acknowledges that the convergence of the chiral expansion in the three-flavor sector is not yet definitively established and that the results presented should be considered indicative rather than definitive.

## Method

The author constructs the effective meson-baryon Lagrangian using the heavy fermion formalism, expanding in chiral dimension up to order \(q^4\). The generating functional is renormalized at one-loop order to \(q^3\) by introducing 102 counterterms, and the fourth-order Lagrangian is assembled from symmetry-breaking terms for the scalar sector and magnetic moments. The low-energy constants are estimated via resonance saturation, including contributions from the spin-3/2 decuplet and spin-1/2 Roper octet, with a consistent scheme to avoid double-counting.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energies for kaon photoproduction: 1046 MeV (\(\gamma p \to \Sigma^+ K^0\)), 1048 MeV (\(\gamma p \to \Lambda K^0\)), and 911 MeV (\(\gamma p \to \Sigma^0 K^+\))
- Electric dipole amplitude at threshold for \(K^0 \Sigma^+\): \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr} = 1.07 \times 10^{-3}/M_{\pi}\)
- Total cross section for \(\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda\) in the energy bin \(E_\gamma \in [0.96,1.01]\,{\rm GeV}\): \(\sigma_{\rm tot} = (1.43 \pm 0.14)\,\mu{\rm b}\)
- Octet baryon mass in the chiral limit: \(\krig{m} = 770 \pm 110\,{\rm MeV}\)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term at \(t=0\): \(\sigma_{\pi N}(0) = 45\,{\rm MeV}\) (from the expansion \(58.3\,(1 - 0.56 + 0.33)\,{\rm MeV}\))
- Strangeness fraction: \(y = 0.21 \pm 0.20\)
- \(\hat \sigma = 36 \pm 7\,{\rm MeV}\)
- Upper limit on the remainder at the Cheng-Dashen point: \(\Delta_R \le 2\,{\rm MeV}\)
- Transition axial radius for \(p \to \Lambda K^+\): \(\langle r_A^2 \rangle_{p \to \Lambda K^+} = 0.23 \ldots 0.70\,{\rm fm}^2\)

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory (as reviewed by Bernard at the same workshop) to the three-flavor case, addressing the long-standing question of why SU(3) flavor symmetry works well in some observables but not others. The calculation of octet baryon masses to order \(q^4\) contradicts the earlier claim by Jurgens (1995) that three-flavor CHPT breaks down, and does not support the conjecture by Jenkins and Manohar (1992) that only the leading non-analytic corrections are large. The results enable systematic studies of kaon photoproduction, hyperon decays, and quark mass ratio extraction, though the author notes that more data closer to threshold are needed to test the scheme.
