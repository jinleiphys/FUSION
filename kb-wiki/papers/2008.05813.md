# Particle Production in AgAg Collisions at $E_{\rm Kin}=1.58A$ GeV within a Hadronic Transport Approach

> Heavy-ion collisions at low beam energies explore the high density regime of strongly-interacting matter. The dynamical evolution of these collisions can be successfully described by hadronic transport approaches. In March 2019, the HADES collaboration has taken data for AgAg collisions at $E_{\rm Kin}=1.58A$ GeV and in this work, we provide predictions for particle production and spectra within the Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons (SMASH) approach. The multiplicities and spectra of strange and non-strange particles follow the expected trends as a function of system size. In particular, in AuAu collisions, much higher yields of double-strange baryons were observed experimentally than expected from a thermal model. Therefore, we incorporate a previously suggested mechanism to produce $Ξ$ baryons via rare decays of high mass $N^*$ resonances and predict the multiplicities. In addition, we predict the invariant mass spectrum for dilepton emission and explore the most important sources of dileptons above 1 GeV, that are expected to indicate the temperature of the medium. Interestingly, the overall dilepton emission is very similar to the one in AuAu collisions at $1.23 A$ GeV, a hint that the smaller system at a higher energy behaves very similar to the larger system at lower beam energy.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the overall dilepton emission in AgAg collisions at 1.58A GeV is very similar to that in AuAu collisions at 1.23A GeV, suggesting that the smaller system at a higher energy behaves similarly to the larger system at lower beam energy, though this is a prediction awaiting experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors used the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) hadronic transport approach in version 1.6, operating in cascade mode without mean-field potentials for most results. They incorporated a previously suggested mechanism to produce Xi baryons via rare decays of high-mass N* resonances, tuning the branching ratio to match experimental data from pNb reactions. For dilepton production, they combined perturbative emission from microscopic transport with a coarse-graining approach for thermal emission from hot cells.

## Key numbers

- Xi- yield in AgAg collisions at 1.58A GeV (0-10% centrality): 1.78 x 10^-3 with modified branching ratios, compared to 8.50 x 10^-6 without the N* decay mechanism
- Xi-/(Lambda+Sigma0) ratio in AgAg collisions: 5.63 x 10^-3 with modified branching ratios, compared to 2.71 x 10^-5 without
- Branching ratio for N* -> Xi KK decays tuned to 0.5 based on pNb data at 3.5 GeV
- Effective temperatures Teff for various particles in AgAg collisions at 1.58A GeV (0-10% centrality): pions 97.5-98.1 MeV, protons 114.2 MeV, K+ 97.4 MeV, K- 93.9 MeV, eta 101.4 MeV, Lambda 105.0 MeV
- System size scaling exponents alpha: K+ 1.55 +/- 0.02, Lambda+Sigma0 1.53 +/- 0.02, phi 1.71 +/- 0.03, K- 1.81 +/- 0.03

## Context

This work provides predictions for the HADES collaboration's AgAg collision data taken in March 2019 at 1.58A GeV, extending previous SMASH studies of strangeness and dilepton production (Steinberg 2018, Staudenmaier 2017). The Xi production mechanism follows the idea of Steinheimer (2015) and is analogous to the N* -> N phi mechanism already implemented in SMASH. The results can be compared to existing HADES data for ArKCl and AuAu collisions, and the predictions will test whether the N* decay mechanism can explain the experimentally observed excess of double-strange baryons over thermal model expectations.
