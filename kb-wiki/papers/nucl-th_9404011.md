# SDG fermion-pair algebraic SO(12) and Sp(10) models and their boson realizations

> It is shown how the boson mapping formalism may be applied as a useful many-body tool to solve a fermion problem. This is done in the context of generalized Ginocchio models for which we introduce S-, D-, and G-pairs of fermions and subsequently construct the sdg-boson realizations of the generalized Dyson type. The constructed SO(12) and Sp(10) fermion models are solved beyond the explicit symmetry limits. Phase transitions to rotational structures are obtained, also in situations where there is no underlying SU(3) symmetry.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that boson mapping can be used as a practical many-body tool to solve fermion problems beyond explicit symmetry limits, specifically for generalized Ginocchio models incorporating S-, D-, and G-pairs of fermions. The hedge is that this approach works for the constructed SO(12) and Sp(10) models, and phase transitions to rotational structures are obtained even in situations where there is no underlying SU(3) symmetry.

## Method
The authors construct generalized Ginocchio fermion-pair algebraic models by introducing S-, D-, and G-pairs (with maximum angular momentum J_max=4) within a single rr-pp-shell framework, where rr is the inactive angular momentum and pp is the active angular momentum. They derive the SO(12) and Sp(10) dynamical symmetries from the underlying single-pp shell structure, with the former arising for half-integer pp and the latter for integer pp. The fermion problem is then transformed to an equivalent boson description using Dyson boson mapping of the pair and multipole operators, allowing solutions beyond the explicit symmetry limits.

## Key numbers
- The SO(12) model corresponds to active angular momentum pp=5/2 (half-integer), giving the orthogonal algebra so(4pp+2)=so(12)
- The Sp(10) model corresponds to active angular momentum pp=2 (integer), giving the symplectic algebra sp(4pp+2)=sp(10)
- The maximum angular momentum of fermion pairs is J_max=2[pp], which equals 4 for both models (giving S, D, and G pairs)
- For the Sp(10) case, allowed SU(5) active space representations have Young labels [2N-2p-4q-6r-8s,2p+2q+2r+2s,2q+2r+2s,2r+2s,2s] with the constraint 4p+6q+8r+10s <= 2N
- For the SO(12) case, allowed SU(6) active space representations have Young labels [N-j-2k,N-j-2k,j+k,j+k,k,k] with the constraint 2j+3k <= N
- The Sp(10) SDG models can be constructed for oscillator shells N0=4 and 5; the SO(12) SDG models for N0=5 and 6

## Context
This work extends the original Ginocchio SO(8) and Sp(6) models (Ginocchio 1980) and the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (FDSM) by incorporating G-pairs to reach SO(12) and Sp(10) dynamical symmetries. It goes beyond the analytic symmetry-limit solutions previously presented by Feng et al. (1991) for the Sp(10) case, using boson mapping methods to obtain solutions in regions without SU(3) symmetry. The results enable the study of phase transitions from anharmonic vibrations to rotations in nuclear collective models, and provide a framework for comparing all four models (SO(8), Sp(6), SO(12), and Sp(10)) in the same major oscillator shells (N0=4, 5, and 6).
