# Density dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections

> The lowest-order correction of the density dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections is obtained from geometrical considerations of Pauli-blocking effects. As a by-product, it is shown that the medium corrections imply an 1/E energy dependence of the density-dependent term.

# Density dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections

## Key claim
Pauli-blocking effects alone, treated via geometrical considerations of momentum-space phase space restrictions, can account for almost the entire lowest-order density correction to in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections. The correction term scales as ρ^(2/3) rather than the linear ρ dependence commonly used in the literature, and its coefficient carries a 1/E energy dependence.

## Method
The author derives the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section by averaging the free cross section over the Fermi spheres of two colliding nuclei, with the Pauli principle restricting the final-state scattering solid angle. Two simplifying approximations are adopted: symmetric Fermi momenta (K_F1 = K_F2) and taking the free cross section outside the integral, which yields an analytic expression for the Pauli-blocked solid angle. The local density approximation then connects the Fermi momentum to the nuclear density, producing a density-dependent correction term.

## Key numbers
- The in-medium cross section is about 1/2 of its free value for k = 2K_F, corresponding to E/A ≈ 150 MeV.
- The density-dependent coefficient is α' = -48.4 / E (MeV), where E is the bombarding energy per nucleon.
- For E/A = 100–200 MeV and ρ ≈ ρ_0, the coefficient α' lies between -0.2 and -0.5.
- Values of α between -0.4 and -0.2 from the linear density expansion (σ_NN = σ_NN^free (1 + α ρ̄)) yield the best agreement with G-matrix calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Context
This work extends earlier approaches that multiplied the nucleon-nucleon cross section by a constant scaling factor to account for medium corrections, as done by Bertsch, Lynch, and Tsang (1987) and Bauer (1988). It improves upon the linear density expansion of Klakow, Welke, and Bauer (1993) by deriving a ρ^(2/3) dependence from Pauli-blocking geometry and predicting an explicit 1/E energy dependence not previously accounted for. The results enable more realistic treatment of in-medium cross sections in transport equations (e.g., BUU) used to extract the nuclear equation of state from intermediate-energy heavy-ion collision data, and call for further study of the consequences of this energy dependence in such analyses.
