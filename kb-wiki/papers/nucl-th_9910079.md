# Form Factors of Baryons in a Confining and Covariant Diquark-Quark Model

> We treat baryons as bound states of scalar or axialvector diquarks and a constituent quark which interact through quark exchange. This description results as an approximation to the relativistic Faddeev equation for three quarks which yields an effective Bethe-Salpeter equation. Octet and decuplet masses and fully four-dimensional wave functions have been computed for two cases: assuming an essentially pointlike diquark on the one hand, and a diquark with internal structure on the other hand. Whereas the differences in the mass spectrum are fairly small, the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are greatly improved assuming a diquark with structure. First calculations to the pion-nucleon form factor also suggest improvements.

# Form Factors of Baryons in a Confining and Covariant Diquark-Quark Model

## Key claim
The nucleon electromagnetic form factors are greatly improved when the diquark is given an internal structure (parameterized by a momentum-dependent vertex function) compared to the pointlike diquark case, even though the mass spectrum differences between the two cases are fairly small. The authors note that the neutron electric form factor appears "quenched too strongly" compared to data, which they attribute to overestimated axialvector diquark correlations within the nucleon.

## Method
The authors treat baryons as bound states of scalar or axialvector diquarks and a constituent quark interacting through quark exchange, which approximates the relativistic Faddeev equation for three quarks and yields an effective Bethe-Salpeter equation. Confinement is parameterized by modifying the quark and diquark propagators to remove free-particle poles, replacing them with essential singularities at time-like infinity. The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved in the nucleon rest frame by decomposing the vertex into eight independent amplitudes (scalar functions) and expanding in Chebyshev polynomials to reduce the four-dimensional equation to coupled one-dimensional integral equations solved iteratively.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon and delta masses used as input to determine normalization constants; constituent quark mass $m_u = 0.5$ GeV (pointlike diquark) or 0.56 GeV (extended diquark); strange quark mass $m_s = 0.63$ GeV (pointlike) or 0.68 GeV (extended)
- Diquark extension parameter $\gamma = 0.5$ GeV for the extended case with vertex function $P(p) = \left( \frac{\gamma^2}{\gamma^2 + p^2} \right)^4$
- Octet masses (extended diquark): $M_\Lambda = 1.098$ GeV (exp. 1.116 GeV), $M_\Sigma = 1.129$ GeV (exp. 1.193 GeV), $M_\Xi = 1.279$ GeV (exp. 1.315 GeV)
- Decuplet masses (extended diquark): $M_{\Sigma^*} = 1.396$ GeV (exp. 1.384 GeV), $M_{\Xi^*} = 1.572$ GeV (exp. 1.530 GeV), $M_\Omega = 1.766$ GeV (exp. 1.672 GeV)
- Nucleon magnetic moments are about 13% too small compared to experiment, though the ratio $\mu_p/\mu_n$ is reproduced nicely
- The pion-nucleon form factor $g_{\pi NN}$ for the extended diquark falls slightly stronger than a monopole with a width parameter around 360 MeV
- The exchange kernel contribution makes up more than 30% of the total magnetic moment

## Context
This work extends the diquark-quark approach to baryons previously developed within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Rei90, Ish95) by incorporating confinement via modified propagators and by introducing extended (rather than pointlike) diquark correlations. The authors note that within the NJL model, diquarks appear as poles in quark-quark scattering and become confined only beyond ladder approximation (Hel97b). The present model enables calculation of electromagnetic form factors for momentum transfers between 1 and 5 GeV$^2$, bridging low-energy and perturbative QCD regimes. The authors identify as next steps the calculation of associated strangeness production ($pp \rightarrow pK\Lambda$) and kaon photoproduction ($\gamma p \rightarrow K\Lambda$) (Alk99), and note that the magnetic moments could be improved by properly resolving the diquark's internal electromagnetic structure via Mandelstam's formalism.
