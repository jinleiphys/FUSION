# Light front Bethe-Salpeter equation applied to form factors, generalized parton distributions and generalized distribution amplitudes

> We present an intuitive, light front reduction scheme for the Bethe-Salpeter equation that consists of iterations of the (light front) energy dependent covariant equation followed by an instantaneous approximation. This scheme is equivalent to and motivates the form of a standard reduction that reproduces light front, time ordered perturbation theory for bound states. We use this three-dimensional light front formalism to compute electromagnetic form factors between bound states to show how higher, light front Fock components arise from the covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave function. Moreover, we show that non-wave function vertices, which arise after integration over the minus momenta, are completely removed in favor of higher Fock states when one correctly takes into account all contributions at a given order in perturbation theory. Such non-wave function vertices are shown only to occur in models in which the interactions are instantaneous. These models lack true higher Fock states. The normalization of the reduced wave function is derived from the covariant framework and related to nonvalence probabilities using familiar Fock space projection operators. Using a simple model, we obtain expressions for generalized parton distributions that are continuous. The nonvanishing of these distributions at the crossover between kinematic regimes (where the plus component of the struck quark's momentum is equal to the plus component of the momentum transfer) is tied to higher Fock components. Lastly we apply the light front reduction to timelike form factors and derive expressions for the generalized distribution amplitudes in this model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-wave-function vertices, which appear after integrating over minus momenta in light front reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, are exactly canceled when one works completely to a given order in perturbation theory, and are replaced by contributions from higher Fock states. This cancellation is shown using a simple scalar model with one-boson exchange, and the authors argue that models with instantaneous interactions lack true higher Fock states and therefore produce spurious non-wave-function vertices.

## Method

The authors develop an iterative reduction scheme for the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation for two scalar quarks exchanging a massive scalar boson. They first project the covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave function onto the light front by integrating over the minus momentum component, using an instantaneous approximation for the vertex function to perform the integrals. They then iterate the covariant equation one or more times before applying the instantaneous approximation, systematically improving the approximation to the light front wave function. The scheme is applied to compute electromagnetic form factors, generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) in a simple scalar model, working to next-to-leading order in perturbation theory to show the cancellation of non-wave-function vertices.

## Context

This work extends the light front reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation introduced by Sales et al. (1999) and builds on the earlier work of Weinberg (1966) on time-ordered perturbation theory for bound states. The authors contrast their approach with that of Ji et al. (2000), who proposed replacing nonvalence vertices with a constant, and Hwang (2001), who treated the nonvalence piece as a new wave function with an extra parameter. The paper shows that such approximations ignore the true nonvalence correlations from higher Fock states, and that a proper treatment requires including all contributions at a given order in perturbation theory. The results enable a consistent field-theoretic treatment of GPDs and GDAs in light front dynamics, with continuous behavior at kinematic crossovers tied to higher Fock components.
