# The property difference between the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 and its proto neutron star

> The property difference between the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 and its proto neutron star is studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory considering neutrino trapping. We see that the central baryon number density of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is in the range $ρ_{c, PNS}=0.539\sim0.698$ fm$^{-3}$, which is smaller than that of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 $ρ_{c, NS}=0.634\sim0.859$ fm$^{-3}$. Inside the neutron star PSR J0348+0432, only the neutrons, protons, $Λ$ and $Ξ^{-}$ produce, whereas the hyperons $Σ^{-}, Σ^{0}, Σ^{+}$ and $Ξ^{0}$ all do not appear. But in the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432, hyperons $Σ^{-}$, $Σ^{0}$, $Σ^{+}$ and $Ξ^{0}$ all will produce, though their relative particle number density are still very small, no more than 2\%. This shows that higher temperature will be advantageous to the hyperon production.

## Key claim
The proto neutron star (PNS) PSR J0348+0432 has a lower central baryon number density (0.539–0.698 fm⁻³) than the cold neutron star (NS) PSR J0348+0432 (0.634–0.859 fm⁻³), and the higher temperature of the PNS enables the production of hyperons Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, and Ξ⁰ that are absent in the cold NS, though their relative number densities remain below 2%. This conclusion depends on the specific choice of hyperon coupling constants (parameter set 44) and the assumption of neutrino trapping with lepton fraction Y_le = 0.1 and entropy per baryon S = 2.

## Method
The study uses relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the GL85 nucleon coupling set, including hidden-strangeness mesons f₀(975) and φ(1020), to compute the equation of state for both cold NS and hot PNS matter with neutrino trapping. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain mass and radius for the star PSR J0348+0432, with hyperon coupling constants fitted to experimental well depths (U_Λ = −30 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = −18 MeV) and varied across 44 parameter combinations to find those yielding a maximum mass above 2.05 M_⊙.

- Central baryon number density of the NS PSR J0348+0432: ρ_c,NS = 0.634–0.859 fm⁻³
- Central baryon number density of the PNS PSR J0348+0432: ρ_c,PNS = 0.539–0.698 fm⁻³
- Mass range of both NS and PNS: M = 1.97–2.05 M_⊙
- Central temperature of the PNS: T_c,PNS = 41.662–45.685 MeV
- Central field strength of meson σ in NS: σ_NSc = 0.321–0.366 fm⁻¹; in PNS: σ_PNSc = 0.290–0.326 fm⁻¹
- Central field strength of meson ω in NS: ω_NSc = 0.367–0.492 fm⁻¹; in PNS: ω_PNSc = 0.311–0.401 fm⁻¹
- Central field strength of meson ρ in NS: ρ_NSc = 0.087–0.091 fm⁻¹; in PNS: ρ_PNSc = 0.079 fm⁻¹
- Central chemical potential of neutrons in NS: μ_nc,NS = 6.957–7.906 fm⁻¹; in PNS: μ_nc,PNS = 6.395–7.046 fm⁻¹
- Central chemical potential of electrons in NS: μ_ec,NS = 1.314–1.294 fm⁻¹; in PNS: μ_ec,PNS = 1.057–1.142 fm⁻¹
- Central relative number density of Σ⁻ in PNS: 0.06%–0.1%; Σ⁰: 0.2%–0.4%; Σ⁺: 0.8%–1.4%; Ξ⁰: 0.7%–1.2%
- Central relative number density of Ξ⁻ in NS: 0%–6.5%; in PNS: 6.3%–8.4%
- Central relative number density of Λ in NS: 14.4%–23.8%; in PNS: 13.5%–17.6%

## Context
This work extends the study of massive neutron stars following the discovery of PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) and PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010), applying RMF theory with hyperons to compare cold NS and hot PNS properties. It builds on prior work by Burgio (2007) and Nicotra (2006) showing that neutrino trapping stiffens the equation of state and shifts hyperon appearance to higher densities, and by Bednarek (2012) demonstrating that nonlinear RMF models can support neutron stars with hyperon cores above 2 M_⊙. The results enable future studies of rotational effects on PNS properties, as noted by Franzon (2016) and Dexheimer (2008).
