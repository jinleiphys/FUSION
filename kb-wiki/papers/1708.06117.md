# Yrast band of 109Ag described by tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory with a separable pairing force

> A separable form of the Gogny pairing force is implemented in tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory for the description of rotational bands in open shell nuclei. The developed method is used to investigate the yrast sequence of 109Ag for an example. The experimental energy spectrum, angular momenta, and electromagnetic transition probabilities are well reproduced by taking into account pairing correlations with the separable pairing force. An abrupt transition of the rotational axis from the long-intermediate plane to the long-short one is obtained and discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper implements a separable form of the Gogny pairing force in tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) and demonstrates that it reproduces the experimental energy spectrum, angular momenta, and electromagnetic transition probabilities for the yrast band of 109Ag, but the description of the energy differences between the bandheads of the 1-quasiparticle and 3-quasiparticle configurations is improved significantly only with the inclusion of pairing correlations, and the separable pairing force is not strong enough to reproduce those energy differences exactly.

## Method

The authors solve the tilted axis cranking relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equation for quasiparticles in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 12 major shells, using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 in the particle-hole channel and the separable pairing force with parameters G = -738 MeV fm3 and a = 0.636 fm in the particle-particle channel. The method is applied to both the 1-quasiproton configuration (πg9/2−1) and the 3-quasiparticle configuration (πg9/2−1 ⊗ νh11/22) of 109Ag, with the rotational axis orientation determined self-consistently by minimizing the total Routhian along the band.

## Key numbers

- The separable pairing force parameters are G = -738 MeV fm3 and a = 0.636 fm, derived from fitting to the density dependence of pairing gaps at the Fermi surface for nuclear matter obtained with the Gogny force.
- Increasing the number of major shells from 12 to 14 changes the total energy by 0.021% and the total angular momentum by 0.020% at a rotational frequency of 0.35 MeV.
- For the 3-quasiparticle configuration, the neutron pairing gap obtained for 109Ag is 0.87 MeV, which is 20% smaller than the 1.26 MeV value for 135Nd at the same frequencies.
- The Dirac effective mass is approximately 0.58m, and scaling it to the nucleon mass by a factor of 0.58 accounts for back-flow effects in the B(M1) calculations.
- Converged results for the 1-quasiparticle configuration can be obtained up to a rotational frequency of approximately 0.5 MeV when pairing correlations are included.

## Context

This work extends the TAC-CDFT framework by replacing the zero-range monopole pairing force used in Zhao (2015) with a separable form of the Gogny pairing force, which avoids the ultraviolet divergence problem and requires less computational time than other finite-range pairing forces such as the full Gogny force. The method is applied to 109Ag, following experimental data from Datta (2008), and the results show similar improvements in the description of rotational bands as previously found for 135Nd with a monopole pairing force. The approach enables future studies of rotational bands in open-shell nuclei with a universal pairing force that does not require adjustment to odd-even mass differences.
