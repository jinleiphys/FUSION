# Model Independent Tests of Skyrmions and Their Holographic Cousins

> We describe a new exact relation for large $N_c$ QCD for the long-distance behavior of baryon form factors in the chiral limit. This model-independent relation is used to test the consistency of the structure of several baryon models. All 4D semiclassical chiral soliton models satisfy the relation, as does the Pomarol-Wulzer holographic model of baryons as 5D Skyrmions. However, remarkably, we find that the holographic model treating baryons as instantons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model does not satisfy the relation.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new model-independent relation for large \(N_c\) QCD that constrains the long-distance behavior of baryon form factors in the chiral limit, and uses it to test whether holographic baryon models correctly capture large \(N_c\) chiral physics. The hedge is that the relation is exact only when the large \(N_c\) limit is taken before the chiral limit, and the semiclassical treatment of soliton models corresponds to this ordering.

## Method

The authors derive an exact relation for the ratio of position-space electric and magnetic baryon form factors in the large-distance limit \(r \rightarrow \infty\), using large \(N_c\) chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi PT\)) to show the result is independent of low-energy constants. They then compute this ratio explicitly in three baryon models: the 4D Skyrme model, the Pomarol-Wulzer holographic model of baryons as 5D Skyrmions, and the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model treating baryons as instantons. The ratio is evaluated by extracting the asymptotic large-\(r\) behavior of the form factors from each model's field configurations and currents.

## Key numbers

- The model-independent relation is: \(\lim_{r\rightarrow \infty} \frac{\tilde{G}_E^{I=0} \tilde{G}_E^{I=1}}{\tilde{G}_M^{I=0} \tilde{G}_M^{I=1}} = \frac{18}{r^2}\).
- In the Skyrme model, the long-distance form factors scale as: \(G_{I=0}^{E} \propto 1/r^9\), \(G_{I=0}^{M} \propto 1/r^7\), \(G_{I=1}^{M} \propto 1/r^4\), \(G_{I=1}^{E} \propto 1/r^4\).
- In the Pomarol-Wulzer model, the long-distance form factors scale as: \(G_{I=0}^{E} \propto 1/r^9\), \(G_{I=0}^{M} \propto 1/r^7\), \(G_{I=1}^{E} \propto 1/r^4\), \(G_{I=1}^{M} \propto 1/r^4\).
- In the Sakai-Sugimoto instanton model, the long-distance form factors scale as: \(G_E^{I=0}(r) \propto e^{- \rho_1 r}/r\), \(G_M^{I=0}(r) \propto r e^{-\rho_1 r}\), \(G_E^{I=1}(r) \propto e^{- \rho_1 r}/r\), \(G_M^{I=1}(r) \propto e^{-{\rho_1}r}\), where \(\rho_1\) is the lowest vector meson mass eigenvalue.
- The ratio in the Sakai-Sugimoto model is \(\frac{\lambda \sqrt{40/3}  }{\pi \rho^{2}_{1} r^2}\), which depends on model parameters \(\lambda\) and \(\rho_1\), unlike the universal \(18/r^2\) from QCD.

## Context

This work extends the program of testing baryon models against model-independent large \(N_c\) QCD relations, following earlier work by Cohen (2003) and Cohen (2004) on chiral and isovector relations. The relation derived here is sensitive to the anomalous coupling of the baryon current to three pions, which distinguishes it from previous tests. The finding that the Sakai-Sugimoto instanton model (Hashimoto, Sakai, and Sugimoto, 2008) fails the test while the Pomarol-Wulzer model (Pomarol and Wulzer, 2008) passes suggests that the instanton model may have a structural issue related to the non-commutativity of the large \(\lambda\) and chiral limits, which the authors indicate will be discussed in a future work (Cherman, Cohen, and Nielsen, 2009).
