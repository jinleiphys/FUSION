# Ab initio nuclear structure calculations of light nuclei

> We perform no-core configuration interaction calculations for nuclei in the p-shell. We show that for typical light nuclei, a truncation on the total number of quanta in the many-body system converges much more rapidly than a full configuration interaction (FCI) truncation, which is a truncation on the single-particle basis space. We present new results for the ground state energies of the Be isotopes with the nonlocal two-body potential JISP16, and discuss emerging phenomena such as clustering and rotational band structures in 9Be. We also show that the anomalously suppressed beta decay of 14C to the ground state of 14N can be reproduced using two- and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory. In particular the structure of the ground state of 14N is sensitive to the three-nucleon force.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for light p-shell nuclei, a truncation on the total number of quanta in the many-body system (Nmax truncation) converges much more rapidly than a full configuration interaction (FCI) truncation on the single-particle basis space, but the authors note that for larger nuclei like those in the sd-shell, achieving convergence requires basis dimensions of order 10 billion or more, making high-performance computing essential.

## Method
The authors performed no-core configuration interaction calculations using the M-scheme with a harmonic oscillator single-particle basis, expanding the nuclear wavefunction in Slater determinants and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian matrix using an iterative Lanczos algorithm with the code MFDn. They compared convergence rates between FCI truncation (limiting the single-particle basis) and Nmax truncation (limiting the total number of harmonic oscillator quanta in the many-body system), and used exponential extrapolation from three consecutive Nmax values at fixed basis parameter hbar-omega to estimate converged binding energies in the infinite-basis limit. For the 14C beta decay study, they employed chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon forces with Lee-Suzuki renormalization.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy of 4He with JISP16 converges to -28.299 MeV in the infinite-basis limit
- For 4He, an Nmax=8 calculation with basis dimension less than 50,000 gives ground state energy within 100 keV of the exact result, while FCI requires Nshell=6 with basis dimension of almost 2 million for similar convergence
- For 6Li, an FCI calculation with basis dimension over 1 billion is still more than 3 MeV from the exact ground state energy, while an Nmax=10 calculation with about 10 million basis states is within 1 MeV of convergence
- Ground state energy of 9Be with JISP16 is -57.2(2) MeV, compared to experimental -58.167 MeV (underbinding by about 1 MeV)
- The two-alpha threshold for 9Be is at -56.6 MeV, confirming the calculated state is a true bound state
- The anomalously long lifetime of 14C is 5730 +/- 30 years
- Two sets of chiral 3NF coupling constants were used: (cD,cE)=(-0.2,-0.205) and (-2.0,-0.501); the latter is preferred by the 14C half life

## Context
This work extends the no-core shell model approach developed by the UNEDF SciDAC collaboration (Bertsch 2007, Furnstahl 2011) and builds on prior calculations with JISP16 (Shirokov 2005, Maris 2008) that demonstrated good convergence for nuclei with A less than or equal to 16. The clustering results for 9Be are consistent with alpha-cluster models, and the rotational band analysis follows the textbook rotational model. The 14C beta decay results extend earlier work by Aroua (2002) and Negret (2006) showing that realistic NN forces alone are insufficient, and confirm the findings of Maris (2011) that chiral three-nucleon forces are essential to reproduce the anomalously small transition matrix element. The work enables future studies of parity inversion in neutron-rich beryllium isotopes and provides a framework for testing improved NN and 3NF potentials.
