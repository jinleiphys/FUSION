# Energy dependence of rescattering effect on vector mesons spin alignment at RHIC

> Spin alignment of vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions provides a novel probe of quark polarization and hadronization mechanism in quark-gluon plasma. Hadronic rescattering may affect the measured spin alignment of vector mesons due to non-uniform rescattering probability in non-central heavy-ion collisions. Using the UrQMD model, we systematically investigated the hadronic rescattering effect on the measurement of $ρ_{00}$, the spin alignment parameter, for $K^{*0}$, $ϕ$, and $ρ^{0}$ mesons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 7.7 - 200 GeV. Our results reveal that the measurable $ρ_{00} - 1/3$ remains unaffected for $ϕ$, while shows significantly negative (positive) deviations for $K^{*0}$ and $ρ^0$ with respect to the reaction (production) plane. Quantitatively, the maximum deviation reaches $-0.0056$ ($0.0268$) for $K^{*0}$ and $-0.0122$ ($0.0414$) for $ρ^{0}$ with respect to the reaction (production) plane. Notably, the deviations in $ρ_{00}$ for both $K^{*0}$ and $ρ^{0}$ increase monotonically with increasing collision energy. These findings underscore the critical necessity of accounting for rescattering effects when interpreting spin alignment measurements of short-lived vector mesons in heavy-ion collisions.

# Energy dependence of rescattering effect on vector mesons spin alignment at RHIC

## Key claim
Using the UrQMD model, the authors show that hadronic rescattering produces artificial spin alignment signals for short-lived vector mesons (K*0 and rho0) in non-central Au+Au collisions, while the phi meson remains unaffected. The deviation of rho00 from 1/3 increases monotonically with collision energy, meaning that rescattering effects must be accounted for when interpreting experimental spin alignment measurements, particularly at higher RHIC energies.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD (ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics) microscopic transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 200 GeV. They tracked vector mesons (K*0, phi, rho0) and their decay daughters through the full hadronic evolution, classifying mesons as "reconstructable" if neither decay daughter underwent any subsequent scattering. The spin alignment parameter rho00 was extracted by fitting the angular distribution of decay daughters for both all mesons and reconstructable mesons, with respect to both the reaction plane and the production plane.

## Key numbers
- Maximum deviation of rho00 - 1/3 for K*0 with respect to reaction plane: -0.0056 +/- 0.0005 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in 0-20% central collisions
- Maximum deviation of rho00 - 1/3 for rho0 with respect to reaction plane: -0.0122 +/- 0.0004 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in 0-20% central collisions
- Maximum deviation of rho00 - 1/3 for K*0 with respect to production plane: +0.0268
- Maximum deviation of rho00 - 1/3 for rho0 with respect to production plane: +0.0414
- For reconstructable K*0 at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV: rho00 - 1/3 = -0.0003 +/- 0.0012 (consistent with zero)
- For reconstructable K*0 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: rho00 - 1/3 = -0.0039 +/- 0.0007 (greater than 5 sigma significance)
- For reconstructable rho0 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: rho00 - 1/3 = -0.0080 +/- 0.0005 (approximately 16 sigma significance)
- Lifetime of K*0: 4.2 fm/c; lifetime of rho0: 1.3 fm/c; lifetime of phi: 46.3 fm/c

## Context
This work extends previous UrQMD studies of rescattering effects on K*0 spin alignment at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (Liu et al., prior work) to the full RHIC beam energy scan range (7.7-200 GeV) and to additional vector mesons (phi and rho0). The results are compared with STAR measurements of K*0 and phi spin alignment at BES-I energies (STAR Collaboration, 2023) and with ALICE measurements at 2.76 TeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2020). The study enables quantitative corrections for rescattering-induced artificial spin alignment when interpreting experimental rho00 measurements, particularly for short-lived resonances like K*0 and rho0, and is relevant for background estimation in chiral magnetic effect measurements involving pions.
