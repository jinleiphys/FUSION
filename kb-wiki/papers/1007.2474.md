# Di-neutron clustering and deuteron-like tensor correlation in nuclear structure focusing on 11Li

> 11Li is a Borromean nucleus, where two out of three objects as 9Li + n and two neutrons independently do not form bound systems. Two neutrons should form a di-neutron cluster in the nuclear field generated by the 9Li core nucleus. We treat di-neutron clustering by solving the two neutron relative wave function precisely by using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction so that the spatial clustering structure is obtained quantitatively within the whole 11Li nucleus. This di-neutron clustering is an essential dynamics to form the halo structure by making a compact di-neutron cluster, which distributes loosely around the 9Li core. The di-neutron clustering is a new concept and is a general phenomenon in neutron skin and neutron halo nuclei. This quantitative description of di-neutron clustering has made it necessary to consider another important deuteron-like tensor correlation, which is caused by strong tensor interaction in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The tensor interaction originates from pion exchange and known to provide large attraction to form the 4He nucleus. The unique feature of the tensor correlation is to make highly correlated deuteron-like excitation, which interferes with shell model like structure in a unique way. This dynamical effect removes the magic number effect and makes easy the participation of the s-wave neutrons. Hence, there are pairing and deuteron-like tensor correlations in addition to the mean field structure in 9Li. The combined system of two additional neutrons with the correlated 9Li provides the halo phenomenon, in which the di-neutron clustering develops with the help of large s-wave component caused by the deuteron-like tensor correlation. In this lecture note, we would like to introduce these two new concepts in a systematic manner and fill a gap between the halo phenomenon and the microscopic reason for this interesting phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the halo structure of 11Li arises from two cooperating mechanisms: di-neutron clustering (a spatial correlation between the two valence neutrons in the 1S0 channel) and deuteron-like tensor correlation (a proton-neutron pair excitation in the 3S1 channel caused by the tensor interaction), which together wash out the N=8 magic shell structure and allow large s-wave neutron components. The authors present this as a new conceptual framework, but note that the existence of a soft dipole resonance in 11Li remains debated despite experimental evidence of a bump structure just above threshold.

## Method

The authors develop the hybrid-VT model, which combines V-type basis states (cluster orbital shell model, COSM) describing the mean-field motion of each valence neutron relative to the 9Li core with T-type basis states describing explicit neutron-neutron correlations. They solve the three-body core+n+n system using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) with Gaussian expansion of the relative wave functions, employing the bare AV8' nucleon-nucleon interaction. The core 9Li wave function is expanded in shell-model-like configurations including pairing (Jπ=0+ neutron pairs) and deuteron-like tensor correlation (Jπ=1+ proton-neutron pairs) components.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of 11Li: 0.32 MeV
- Single-neutron separation energy of 11Li: 0.62 MeV
- Single-neutron separation energy of 10Li: -0.3 MeV (unbound)
- Two-neutron separation energy of 9Li: 6.10 MeV
- Single-neutron separation energy of 9Li: 4.07 MeV
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: -18.5 ± 0.4 fm
- Neutron-9Li scattering length: approximately -20 fm
- (1s1/2)2 component contribution to 11Li ground state: (45 ± 10)% from momentum distribution measurements
- Deuteron binding energy from AV8' potential: -2.24 MeV
- Deuteron tensor interaction expectation value: -16.64 MeV (four times the central interaction contribution)
- Deuteron D-state probability: 5.78%
- Deuteron radius: 1.96 fm (S-wave component 2.00 fm, D-wave component 1.22 fm)

## Context

This work extends the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) developed by Suzuki and Ikeda (1988) and earlier three-body calculations by Thompson and Zhukov (1994) and Esbensen and Bertsch (1992), which required phenomenological adjustments to bring down the s1/2 orbit. It contrasts with the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach of Meng and Ring, which gave only about one-quarter s-wave contribution, and with the cluster model of Varga, Suzuki and Lovas (2002) that breaks the 9Li core into 4He+t+4n. The hybrid-VT model enables quantitative treatment of di-neutron clustering and deuteron-like tensor correlation using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the authors connect this to the BCS-BEC crossover concept discussed in recent literature by Hagino, Matsuo, and Sun.
