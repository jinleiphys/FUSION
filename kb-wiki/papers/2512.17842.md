# Active learning emulators for nuclear two-body scattering in momentum space

> We extend the active learning emulators for two-body scattering in coordinate space with error estimation, recently developed by Maldonado et al. [Phys. Rev. C 112, 024002], to coupled-channel scattering in momentum space. Our full-order model (FOM) solver is based on the Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for the scattering $t$-matrix as opposed to the radial Schrödinger equation. We use (Petrov-)Galerkin projections and high-fidelity calculations at a few snapshots across the parameter space of the interaction to construct efficient reduced-order models (ROMs), trained by a greedy algorithm for locally optimal snapshot selection. Both the FOM solver and the corresponding ROMs are implemented efficiently in Python using Google's JAX library. We present results for emulating scattering phase shifts in coupled and uncoupled channels and cross sections, and assess the accuracy of the developed ROMs and their computational speedup factors. We also develop emulator error estimation for both the $t$-matrix and the total cross section. The software framework for reproducing and extending our results is publicly available. Together with our recent advances in developing active-learning emulators for three-body scattering, these emulator frameworks set the stage for full Bayesian calibrations of chiral nuclear interactions and optical models against scattering data with quantified emulator errors.

## Key claim

The authors extend active-learning emulators for two-body scattering from coordinate-space uncoupled channels to momentum-space coupled-channel scattering, demonstrating that reduced-order models (ROMs) based on (Petrov-)Galerkin projections and greedy snapshot selection can accurately emulate scattering phase shifts and cross sections for chiral nuclear interactions, with the caveat that the emulator error estimation relies on the assumption that the residual norm is approximately proportional to the true error rather than a rigorous upper bound.

## Method

The full-order model (FOM) solves the Lippmann-Schwinger integral equation for the scattering t-matrix in momentum space, discretized on a grid of N=80 points with a maximum momentum of 20 fm^-1. Reduced-order models are constructed using Galerkin and Least-Squares Petrov-Galerkin projections, with snapshot locations selected by a greedy algorithm that iteratively places high-fidelity calculations where the estimated emulator error (based on the residual norm) is largest. Both the FOM solver and ROMs are implemented in Python using Google's JAX library.

## Key numbers

- Momentum grid size: N=80 points, with maximum momentum k_max = 20 fm^-1
- Minnesota potential best-fit parameters: V_R = 200 MeV, V_s = -91.85 MeV, kappa_R = 1.487 fm^-2, kappa_s = 0.465 fm^-2
- Greedy algorithm tolerance threshold: epsilon < 10^-8 for the emulator error
- Final emulator basis size for Minnesota potential test case: 11 snapshots (2 initial + 9 greedy iterations)
- Laboratory energy for Minnesota potential demonstration: E_lab = 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the active-learning emulator framework developed by Maldonado et al. (2025) for coordinate-space two-body scattering to momentum-space coupled-channel scattering, enabling application to a wider range of modern chiral interactions. Together with recent advances in three-body scattering emulators by Gnech et al. (2025), this framework sets the stage for full Bayesian calibrations of chiral nuclear interactions and optical models against scattering data with quantified emulator errors.
