# Lambda flow in heavy-ion collisions: the role of final-state interactions

> Lambda flow in Ni+Ni collisions at SIS energies is studied in the relativistic transport model (RVUU 1.0). It is found that for primordial lambdas the flow is considerably weaker than proton flow. The inclusion of final-state interactions, especially the propagation of lambdas in mean-field potential, brings the lambda flow close to that of protons. An accurate determination of lambda flow in heavy-ion experiments is shown to be very useful for studying lambda properties in dense matter.

# Lambda flow in heavy-ion collisions: the role of final-state interactions

## Key claim
Primordial lambdas produced in Ni+Ni collisions at SIS energies show considerably weaker flow than protons, but the inclusion of final-state interactions, especially propagation in an attractive mean-field potential, brings lambda flow close to that of protons. The study shows that accurate experimental determination of lambda flow can be used to infer lambda properties in dense matter, but the authors note that quantitative extraction of the lambda potential requires both more accurate data and better theoretical understanding of in-medium modifications to the Lambda-Nucleon cross section.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic transport model (RVUU 1.0) to simulate Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b ≤ 4 fm, corresponding to FOPI centrality selection. Lambda production was treated via baryon-baryon (BB → BΛK) and pion-nucleon (πN → ΛK) channels with isospin-averaged cross sections parameterized from Randrup and Cugnon, including medium modifications of hadron masses. After production, lambdas underwent elastic ΛN scattering (using a fitted cross section from experimental data) and propagation in a mean-field potential derived from the constituent quark model with a variable vector potential strength parameter α.

## Key numbers
- Primordial lambda flow parameter: about 60 MeV, compared to proton flow parameter of about 140 MeV.
- Including elastic ΛN scattering increases the lambda flow parameter to about 80 MeV.
- Including both scattering and mean-field propagation (α = 1.0) gives a lambda flow parameter of about 115 MeV, close to the proton value.
- The maximum average transverse momentum ⟨p_x⟩_max for primordial lambdas is about 45 MeV, increasing to about 60 MeV with scattering, and to about 100 MeV with both scattering and mean-field propagation.
- Changing the ΛN cross section by a factor of 3 (0.5× to 1.5×) modifies the flow parameter by only about 15 MeV; each lambda undergoes on average 1.3, 2.5, and 3.6 collisions for the three cross section values respectively.
- The flow parameter F changes from about 95 MeV (α = 1.2, less attractive potential) to about 135 MeV (α = 0.85, more attractive potential).
- The azimuthal anisotropy parameter R changes from about 1.9 (α = 1.2) to about 2.6 (α = 0.85); without lambda potential R is about 1.4; for nucleons R ≈ 3.0.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of kaon flow in Ni+Ni collisions at similar energies (Li, Ko, and collaborators, 1995-1996), where primordial kaons showed weak flow that was further reduced by a repulsive kaon potential. The study directly addresses the experimental findings of the FOPI collaboration (Ni+Ni at 1.93 GeV/nucleon) and the EOS collaboration (Ni+Cu at 2.0 GeV/nucleon), both of which observed lambda flow in the same direction and with similar strength as proton flow. The results enable future extraction of the lambda mean-field potential at densities above normal nuclear matter density, which is relevant for understanding neutron star properties and complements information from hypernuclei studies (Dover and collaborators; hypernuclei references) that only probe lambda properties at and below normal nuclear matter density.
