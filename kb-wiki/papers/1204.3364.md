# Hadronic Molecular States Composed of Heavy Flavor Baryons

> We investigate the possible molecules composed of two heavy flavor baryons such as "$A_QB_Q$"(Q=b, c) within the one-pion-exchange model (OPE). Our results indicate that the long-range $π$ exchange force is strong enough to form molecules such as $[Σ_QΞ_Q^{'}]^{I=1/2}_{S=1}$(Q=b, c), $[Σ_QΛ_Q]^{I=1}_{S=1}$(Q=b, c), $[Σ_bΞ_b^{'}]^{I=3/2}_{S=1}$ and $[Ξ_bΞ^{'}_b]^{I=0}_{S=1}$ where the S-D mixing plays an important role. In contrast, the $π$ exchange does not form the spin-singlet $A_QB_Q$ bound states. If we consider the heavier scalar and vector meson exchanges as well as the pion exchange, some loosely bound spin-singlet S-wave states appear while results of the spin-triplet $A_QB_Q$ system does not change significantly, which implies the pion exchange plays an dominant role in forming the spin-triplet molecules. Moreover, we perform an extensive coupled channel analysis of the $Λ_QΛ_Q$ system within the OPE and one-boson-exchange (OBE) framework and find that there exist loosely bound states of $Λ_QΛ_Q$(Q=b,c) with quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(0^+)$, $0(0^-)$ and $0(1^-)$. The binding solutions of $Λ_QΛ_Q$ system mainly come from the coupled-channel effect in the flavor space. Besides the OPE force, the medium- and short-range attractive force also plays a significant role in the formation of the loosely bound $Λ_cΛ_c$ and $Λ_bΛ_b$ states. Once produced, they will be very stable because such a system decays via weak interaction with a very long lifetime around $10^{-13}\sim10^{-12}$s.

## Key claim

The one-pion-exchange (OPE) force alone is sufficient to form spin-triplet hadronic molecules composed of two heavy flavor baryons (such as $[\Sigma_Q\Xi_Q^{'}]^{I=1/2}_{S=1}$ and $[\Sigma_Q\Lambda_Q]^{I=1}_{S=1}$ for Q=b,c), but does not form spin-singlet $A_QB_Q$ bound states; when heavier scalar and vector meson exchanges are added, some loosely bound spin-singlet S-wave states appear, while the spin-triplet results remain largely unchanged, indicating that pion exchange dominates the spin-triplet binding.

## Method

The authors employed the one-pion-exchange (OPE) model and the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, deriving effective interaction potentials from SU(3)-flavor-symmetric Lagrangians for pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector meson exchanges between heavy flavor baryons (charm and bottom sectors). Coupling constants were obtained from the quark model by scaling nucleon-meson couplings, and a monopole form factor with a cutoff parameter was used at each vertex. The multichannel Schrodinger equation was solved numerically using the Fortran program FESSDE, including coupled-channel analysis for the $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ system with channels involving $\Sigma_Q$, $\Sigma_Q^*$, and $\Lambda_Q$ baryons.

## Key numbers

- Binding solutions found for $[\Sigma_Q\Xi_Q^{'}]^{I=1/2}_{S=1}$ (Q=b,c), $[\Sigma_Q\Lambda_Q]^{I=1}_{S=1}$ (Q=b,c), $[\Sigma_b\Xi_b^{'}]^{I=3/2}_{S=1}$, and $[\Xi_b\Xi^{'}_b]^{I=0}_{S=1}$ within the OPE model
- Loosely bound states of $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ (Q=b,c) found with quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(0^+)$, $0(0^-)$, and $0(1^-)$ within the OPE and OBE framework
- Predicted lifetime of $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ systems around $10^{-13}\sim10^{-12}$ seconds, decaying via weak interaction
- Coupling constants used include $g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi=13.6$, $g_{\sigma NN}^2/4\pi=5.69$, $g_{\rho NN}^2/4\pi=0.84$, $f_{\rho NN}/g_{\rho NN}=\kappa_{\rho}=6.1$, $g_{\omega NN}^2/4\pi=20.0$, $f_{\omega NN}/g_{\omega NN}=\kappa_{\omega}=0$
- Masses used: $\Lambda_c^+$ = 2286.5 MeV, $\Lambda_b^0$ = 5620.2 MeV, $\Sigma_c^{++}$ = 2454.02 MeV, $\Sigma_b^+$ = 5807.8 MeV, $\Sigma_c^*$ = 2518.0 MeV, $\Sigma_b^*$ = 5832.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular approach, previously applied to meson-antimeson systems such as $X(3872)$ near the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold (Choi 2003) and $Z_b(10610)$/$Z_b(10650)$ near $B\bar{B}^*$/$B^*\bar{B}^*$ thresholds (Belle Collaboration 2011), to the heavy baryon sector. It builds on earlier studies of double-charmed hyperon bound states by Julia-Diaz (2004) and the authors' own previous work on $\Lambda_c\Lambda_c$, $\Xi_c\Xi_c$, $\Sigma_c\Sigma_c$, $\Xi_c^{'}\Xi_c^{'}$, and $\Omega_c\Omega_c$ systems (Lee 2011). The $\Lambda_Q\Lambda_Q$ system is treated as the heavy analogue of the H dibaryon originally proposed by Jaffe (1976), and the results enable future experimental searches for these weakly-decaying, long-lived dibaryon-like states at facilities such as LHCb or Belle II.
