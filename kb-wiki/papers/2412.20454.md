# Transport coefficients of dense nucleon matter at low temperature

> The transport property of cold and dense nucleon matter is important for nuclear physics but is relatively less studied than that at finite temperatures. In this paper, we present a primary study of bulk and shear viscosities in the limit $T/μ_B \ll 1$, where $T$ and $μ_B$ are the temperature and the baryon chemical potential. The analysis is performed for a generic system where nucleons are dressed by the condensation of both scalar and vector interactions. Under the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann equation, we compute the viscosities of the system to leading power in $T/μ_B$ expansion and establish a relation between the thermodynamic potential and transport coefficients, including bulk viscosity ($ζ$) and shear viscosity ($η$). It is found that hydrodynamic stability ($ζ>0$) imposes additional constraints on the thermodynamic potential. As an example, these relations are applied to the Walecka model. The fluid properties of the cold and dense nucleon matter are characterized by the dimensionless combination of viscosities times the quasi-Fermi momentum over the enthalpy. Furthermore, we discuss the implication of the stability condition on the range of applicability of the model.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for bulk and shear viscosities of cold dense nucleon matter in the limit \(T/\mu_B \ll 1\), establishing that the positivity of bulk viscosity (\(\zeta > 0\)) imposes a constraint on the thermodynamic potential that is independent of the usual thermodynamic stability conditions, which the authors note may be an artifact of the relaxation time approximation.

## Method

The authors use the relaxation time approximation of the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a fermionic system with quasiparticles dressed by scalar and vector condensates, expanding the distribution function to leading order in \(T/\mu_B\). They compute the shear and bulk viscosities by solving for the nonequilibrium correction to the Fermi-Dirac distribution, then apply the formalism to the Walecka model (a relativistic mean-field theory with \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) mesons) to obtain explicit expressions for the transport coefficients in terms of the thermodynamic potential and its derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity: \(\eta = \frac{{p^*_\mathrm{F}}^5}{30\pi^2\mu^*} \tau_\text{rel}(\mu^*)\), where \(p^*_\mathrm{F} = \sqrt{{\mu^*}^2 - {m^*}^2}\) is the quasi-Fermi momentum and \(\tau_\text{rel}\) is the relaxation time.
- Bulk viscosity: \(\zeta = -\frac{{p^*_\mathrm{F}}^5}{18\pi^2{\mu^*}} \frac{m^*\mathcal{f}^\prime(\bar{\sigma})}{\mu^*\mathcal{g}^\prime(\bar{\omega}_0)} \frac{\partial^2\bar{\mathcal{V}}_\mathrm{eff}}{\partial\bar{\omega}_0\partial\bar{\sigma}} \left(\frac{\partial^2\bar{\mathcal{V}}_\mathrm{eff}}{\partial\bar{\sigma}^2}\right)^{-1} \tau_\text{rel}(\mu^*)\).
- For a free relativistic fermion gas, the squared speed of sound is \(v_s^2 = \frac{1}{3}\left(1 - \frac{m^2}{\mu^2}\right)\) for \(|\mu| > m\), approaching \(1/3\) in the massless limit.
- The sign of \(\zeta\) is determined by \(\dif m^* / \dif \mu^*\), which is related to the mixed second derivative of the effective potential.

## Context

This work extends Fermi liquid theory (Landau, 1956) to compute transport coefficients of cold dense nucleon matter, a regime less studied than finite-temperature nuclear matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions. The formalism is applied to the Walecka model (Walecka, 1974), a standard relativistic mean-field approach for nuclear matter. The results enable future studies of dissipative hydrodynamics in low-energy nuclear reactions and electron-ion collisions at facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider and the Electron-Ion Collider in China, though the authors note that the strong-interaction viscosities considered here do not play a significant role in the long-term evolution of neutron stars.
