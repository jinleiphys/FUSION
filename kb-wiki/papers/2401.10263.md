# Microscopic Encoding of Macroscopic Universality in QCD Chiral Phase Transition

> We reveal that the universal scaling properties of the chiral phase transition in quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at the macroscale are, in fact, encoded within the microscopic energy levels of its fundamental constituents, the quarks. We introduce a novel relation between the cumulants of the chiral order parameter, i.e., the chiral condensate, and the correlations among the energy levels of quarks, i.e., the eigenspectra of the massless QCD Dirac operator. This relation elucidates how the fluctuations of the chiral condensate arise from the correlations within the infrared part of the energy spectra of quarks, and naturally leads to a generalization of the Banks-Casher relation for the cumulants of the chiral condensate. Then, through (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using HISQ action with varying light quark masses around the chiral phase transition temperature, we demonstrate that the correlations among the infrared part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectra exhibit same universal scaling behaviors as expected of the cumulants of the chiral condensate. We find that these universal scaling behaviors extend up to the physical values of the up and down quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the universal scaling properties of the chiral phase transition in QCD are encoded in the correlations among the infrared part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectra, and that these scaling behaviors extend up to the physical values of the up and down quark masses (corresponding to a pion mass of approximately 140 MeV), not just in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors derived a theoretical relation connecting the nth-order cumulant of the chiral condensate to n-point correlations of the quark energy levels (eigenvalues of the massless QCD Dirac operator), leading to a generalized Banks-Casher relation. They then performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using the HISQ action with light quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 140, 110, 80, and 55 MeV, at temperatures between 135 and 176 MeV, and computed the eigenvalue correlations using Chebyshev filtering combined with stochastic estimation on approximately 3000 configurations.

## Key numbers

- The chiral phase transition temperature was determined as T_c = 144.2(6) MeV, with a scale parameter z_0 = 1.83(9), taken from Clarke (2020) based on scaling fits to the static quark free energy.
- Lattice calculations were performed at temperatures between 135 and 176 MeV, with temporal extent N_tau = 8 and spatial extents N_sigma = (4-7) N_tau.
- The light quark masses used were m_l = m_s/27, m_s/40, m_s/80, and m_s/160, corresponding to Goldstone pion masses of approximately 140, 110, 80, and 55 MeV, respectively.
- The universal scaling of the rescaled eigenvalue correlation functions was observed for |z/z_0| less than approximately 0.2, where z is the scaling variable.
- The critical exponents adopted were beta = 0.349 and delta = 4.78, corresponding to the 3-dimensional O(2) universality class.

## Context

This work extends the Banks-Casher relation (Banks 1979) from the chiral condensate to higher-order cumulants, establishing a direct connection between macroscopic universal scaling and microscopic quark energy levels. It builds on lattice QCD studies of the chiral phase transition using staggered fermions (Ejiri 2009, Clarke 2020) and demonstrates that the universal O(2) scaling previously observed in macroscopic observables is also present in the correlations of Dirac eigenvalue spectra. This enables future detailed scaling studies using eigenvalue correlations, which may be advantageous because they are sensitive only to deep infrared physics (lambda ~ m_l) and are fairly insensitive to the exact values of T_c and z_0.
