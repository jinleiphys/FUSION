# Realistic Shell-Model Calculations for 208Pb Neighbors

> We have performed a shell-model study of the two nuclei 210Po and 206Hg, having and lacking two protons with respect to doubly magic 208Pb. In our calculations we have employed realistic effective interactions derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential. The calculated results are compared with the available experimental data are, however, very scanty for 206Hg. The very good agreement obtained for 210Po supports confidence in our predictions for 206Hg.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential, using a G-matrix folded-diagram method, yields very good agreement with experimental data for 210Po, and that this supports confidence in their predictions for 206Hg, where experimental data are very scanty.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for the N=126 isotones 210Po (two valence protons) and 206Hg (two proton holes) relative to doubly magic 208Pb. They derived a realistic effective interaction from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential using a G-matrix folded-diagram formalism, including renormalizations from core polarization and folded diagrams, with the Q-box composed of G-matrix diagrams through second order in G. Single-particle energies were taken from experimental spectra of 209Bi and 207Tl, and the shell-model oscillator parameter was set to 6.88 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square deviation σ for 25 identified excited states in 210Po: 92 keV
- Calculated ground-state binding energy relative to 208Pb for 210Po: 8.871 MeV (experimental: 8.783 ± 0.004 MeV)
- Calculated ground-state binding energy relative to 208Pb for 206Hg: -15.165 MeV (experimental: -15.382 ± 0.021 MeV)
- Calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) in 210Po: 3.62 W.u. (experimental: 0.56 ± 0.12 W.u.)
- Calculated B(E2; 4₁⁺ → 2₁⁺) in 210Po: 4.49 W.u. (experimental: 4.53 ± 0.15 W.u.)
- Calculated B(E2; 6₁⁺ → 4₁⁺) in 210Po: 3.08 W.u. (experimental: 3.00 ± 0.12 W.u.)
- Calculated B(E2; 8₁⁺ → 6₁⁺) in 210Po: 1.25 W.u. (experimental: 1.10 ± 0.05 W.u.)
- Calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) in 206Hg: 5.2 W.u. (experimental: > 0.00027 W.u.)
- Calculated B(E3; 5₁⁻ → 2₁⁺) in 206Hg: 0.432 W.u. (experimental: 0.182 ± 0.018 W.u.)
- Calculated quadrupole moment Q(8₁⁺) in 210Po: -58.8 e·fm² (experimental: -55.2 ± 2.0 e·fm²)
- Calculated quadrupole moment Q(11₁⁻) in 210Po: -97 e·fm² (experimental: -86 ± 11 e·fm²)
- Calculated quadrupole moment Q(5₁⁻) in 206Hg: 53 e·fm² (experimental: 74 ± 15 e·fm²)
- Calculated g-factor for 6₁⁺ in 210Po: 0.906 (experimental: 0.913 ± 0.006)

- Calculated g-factor for 11₁⁻ in 210Po: 1.140 (experimental: 1.108 ± 0.012)
- Calculated g-factor for 5₁⁻ in 206Hg: 1.17 (experimental: 1.09 ± 0.01)

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of the lead region that used empirical effective interactions, such as those by Glaudemans (1985) and Ma (1973), and earlier realistic interactions from the Hamada-Johnston potential by Kuo (1971, 1972, 1975). The calculations are among the first in the lead region to derive the effective interaction from a modern nucleon-nucleon potential (Bonn A) using a G-matrix folded-diagram method, and they are part of a broader study by the same authors (Andr 1996, 1997a, 1997b; Coraggio 1998). The results enable future predictions for other N=126 isotones and provide a test of the Bonn potential's reliability in nuclear structure calculations.
