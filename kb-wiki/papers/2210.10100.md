# A path to the Nuclear Equation of State within the frameworks of Mean-Field and Fermionic Dynamics

> The nuclear Equation of State (EoS) lies in the center of the nuclear N-body problem as it describes the properties of the Nuclear Matter (NM) and determines the parameters of the nuclear interaction. In this work, we propose a theoretical description of the EoS of both Symmetric (SNM) and Asymmetric (ANM) nuclear matter within the framework of Fermionic Dynamics. With this description we produce several new semi-hard EoS with density dependent effective mass. Finally, we transform the aforementioned theory in order to be consistent with Mean-Field dynamics. We use this approach to accurately calculate the binding energies and charge radii of nuclei in the A= 40--238 region with the Skyrme Hartree Fock (SHF) model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a theoretical description of the nuclear Equation of State for both symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter within the Fermionic Dynamics framework, and then transforms this description to be consistent with Mean-Field dynamics, but the resulting EoS are described as "semi-hard" and the approach relies on freely selecting the parameter sigma to solve the system of equations, meaning the results are not uniquely determined by the input data alone.

## Method

The authors derive the EoS for Symmetric Nuclear Matter (SNM) using the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) functional, first with free nucleon mass and then with a density-dependent effective mass parameter. They extend this to Asymmetric Nuclear Matter (ANM) by including kinetic and potential symmetry energy terms, and determine the asymmetry parameter range. Finally, they transform the CoMD-consistent EoS into a Mean-Field consistent form using a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) functional and calculate binding energies and charge radii for nuclei in the A=40-238 region.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium density of SNM taken as rho_0 = 0.165 fm^{-3} and equilibrium energy per nucleon E_0 = -16 MeV
- Three EoS without effective mass: K_NM = 220 MeV (rho_0 = 0.165 fm^{-3}), K_NM = 254 MeV (rho_0 = 0.160 fm^{-3}), K_NM = 308 MeV (rho_0 = 0.160 fm^{-3})
- Three EoS with effective mass m*/m = 0.85 at rho_0 = 0.165 fm^{-3}: K_NM = 258 MeV (sigma = 1.40), K_NM = 292 MeV (sigma = 1.58), K_NM = 315 MeV (sigma = 1.70)
- The asymmetry parameter delta_x for nuclear matter constrained into the range +/- 0.72
- The slope parameter lambda = 1.07 for the effective mass density dependence when m*/m = 0.85 at saturation

## Context

This work extends the approach of Papa (2013) and the CoMD model of Bonasera (2001) by incorporating a density-dependent effective mass into the Fermionic Dynamics EoS, which was previously treated as a free variable decoupled from compressibility in the authors' prior work (Depastas, 2021). The new EoS are intended to be used for simulating Giant Dipole and Giant Monopole Resonances with the CoMD model, and the transformation to Mean-Field dynamics enables calculation of binding energies and charge radii for finite nuclei using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, with a reduced Coulomb potential due to Pauli correlations.
