# Charmonium spectral functions in two-flavour QCD

> We compute charmonium spectral functions in 2-flavour QCD using the maximum entropy method and anisotropic lattices. We find that the S-waves (J/psi and eta_c) survive up to temperatures close to 2T_c, while the P-waves (chi_c0 and chi_c1) melt away below 1.3T_c.

# Charmonium spectral functions in two-flavour QCD

## Key claim
S-wave charmonium states (J/ψ and η_c) survive virtually unchanged up to at least 1.4T_c and possibly close to 2T_c, while P-wave states (χ_c0 and χ_c1) melt away below 1.4T_c; however, the survival of S-waves at 2.1T_c shows a possible mass dependence that cannot be definitively ascertained from the present data.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two dynamical quark flavours using anisotropic lattices (TSI gauge action with coarse Wilson, fine Hamber-Wu fermion action and stout-link smearing) at spatial lattice spacing a_s ≈ 0.18 fm and anisotropy ξ = 6. Charmonium correlators were computed using all-to-all propagators on 8³ × N_τ lattices with N_τ = 32, 24, and 16 (corresponding to T/T_c ≈ 1.05, 1.4, and 2.1 respectively), and spectral functions were reconstructed via Bryan's maximum entropy method (MEM) using the free continuum spectral function ω² as the default model.

## Key numbers
- Spatial lattice spacing a_s ≈ 0.18 fm, anisotropy ξ = 6
- Pion-to-rho mass ratio m_π/m_ρ = 0.54
- Critical temporal extent N_τ^c = 1/(aT_c) ≈ 34
- 500 configurations generated on each of three lattice sizes: 8³×32, 8³×24, and 8³×16, corresponding to T/T_c ≈ 1.05, 1.4, and 2.1 respectively
- Bare charm quark masses a_t m = 0.08 and 0.092
- P-wave axial-vector channel shows a peak at a_t ω ≈ 0.45 at T/T_c = 1.05, consistent with a modified χ_c1
- Physical volume of (1.4 fm)³ on the 8³ lattice

## Context
This work extends quenched (zero-flavour) lattice studies of charmonium spectral functions (Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003, Umeda 2002) by including two dynamical quark flavours, which had been previously impractical due to the computational cost of achieving the required temporal lattice spacing. The authors had presented preliminary results (Morrin 2005, Aarts 2005) with systematic uncertainties at the 20% level; the present study uses anisotropies tuned to within 3% and includes P-wave channels for the first time in dynamical simulations. The results support the qualitative picture from quenched calculations but require further work on larger volumes (12³ lattices are underway), a more detailed temperature scan, and systematic studies of MEM default model dependence before quantitative conclusions can be drawn.
