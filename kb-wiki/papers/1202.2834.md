# Axial charges of excited nucleons from CI-fermions

> We report lattice QCD results on the axial charges of ground and excited nucleon states of both parities. This is the first study of these quantities with approximately chiral (CI) fermions. Two energy levels in the range of the negative parity resonances N*(1535) and N*(1650) are observed and we determine the axial charge for both. We obtain a small axial charge for one of them, which is consistent with the chiral symmetry restoration in this state as well as with the small axial charge of the N*(1535) predicted within the quark model. This result agrees with the findings of Takahashi et al. obtained with Wilson quarks which violate chiral symmetry for finite lattice spacing. At the same time for the other observed negative parity state we obtain a large axial charge, that is close to the axial charge of the nucleon. This is in disagreement both with the quark model prediction as well as with the chiral restoration but allows for an interpretation as an s-wave π N state.

## Key claim

This lattice QCD study reports the first determination of axial charges for excited nucleon states using approximately chiral (CI) fermions, finding that one of the two observed negative-parity states has a very small axial charge consistent with chiral symmetry restoration, while the other has a large axial charge close to that of the ground-state nucleon.

## Method

The authors employed a variational analysis of a 6×6 correlation matrix built from two different nucleon interpolator structures (scalar and pseudoscalar diquark types) combined with three levels of Jacobi smearing, on four ensembles of 16³×32 lattices with n_F=2 dynamical CI fermions. They computed three-point correlation functions with vector and axial-vector currents at multiple source-sink separations (τ=2,3,4) and extracted axial charges from ratios of three-point to two-point functions using eigenvectors from the variational method.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state positive parity nucleon axial charge: g_A = 1.137(13) for ensemble C77, 1.164(10) for C72, 1.175(11) for C64, 1.178(9) for A50 (renormalized values)
- First negative-parity state axial charge: g_A = 0.121(128) for C77, -0.053(78) for C72, -0.022(82) for C64, -0.145(71) for A50 (renormalized values)
- Second negative-parity state axial charge: g_A = 1.032(99) for C77, 0.897(65) for C72, 0.858(73) for C64, 0.891(48) for A50 (renormalized values)
- Lattice spacings: 0.14487(95) fm (C77), 0.15051(115) fm (C72), 0.15831(127) fm (C64), 0.15032(112) fm (A50)
- AWI quark masses: 14.35(26) MeV (C77), 22.65(21) MeV (C72), 37.33(25) MeV (C64), 39.73(10) MeV (A50)

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice study by Takahashi et al. (2009) who found a small axial charge for one negative-parity nucleon resonance using Wilson fermions, which violate chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacing. The present results with approximately chiral fermions confirm that finding and support the hypothesis of effective chiral symmetry restoration proposed by Glozman (1999) and Cohen (2001), which predicts vanishing axial charges for parity-doublet partners. The small axial charge is also consistent with quark model predictions for the N*(1535) resonance, while the large axial charge of the other state allows interpretation as an s-wave πN state, helping to resolve whether the two observed lattice states represent the N*(1535) and N*(1650) resonances or one resonance plus a scattering state.
