# Collapse of the $N=28$ shell closure in the newly discovered $^{39}$Na and the development of deformed halos towards the neutron dripline

> Halos and changes of nuclear magicities have been extensively investigated in exotic nuclei during past decades. The newly discovered $^{39}$Na with the neutron number $N=28$ provides a new platform to explore such novel phenomena near the neutron dripline of the sodium isotopic chain. We study the shell property and the possible halo structure in $^{39}$Na within the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum. It is found that the lowering of $2p$ orbitals in the spherical limit results in the collapse of the $N=28$ shell closure in $^{39}$Na, and a well deformed ground state is established. The pairing correlations and the mixing of $pf$ components driven by deformation lead to the occupation of weakly bound or continuum $p$-wave neutron orbitals. An oblate halo is therefore formed around the prolate core in $^{39,41}$Na, making $^{39}$Na a single nucleus with the coexistence of several exotic structures, including the quenched $N=28$ shell closure, Borromean structure, deformed halo, and shape decoupling. The microscopic mechanisms behind the shape decoupling phenomenon and the development of halos towards dripline are revealed.

## Key claim

The newly discovered nucleus $^{39}$Na exhibits a collapse of the $N=28$ shell closure due to the lowering of $2p$ orbitals in the spherical limit, leading to a well-deformed prolate ground state; the paper claims that an oblate neutron halo forms around this prolate core, but this result is conditional on the specific density functional (PC-PK1) and pairing interaction used in the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) calculations.

## Method

The authors performed deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) calculations using the PC-PK1 density functional (with additional checks using PC-F1, NL3*, NL-SH, and PK1 functionals) and a surface pairing interaction. They solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with energy cutoff 300 MeV and angular momentum cutoff 23/2 hbar, expanding densities and potentials in Legendre polynomials up to lambda_max=6. Constrained calculations were used to map single-neutron levels as a function of quadrupole deformation, and the neutron density distributions, root-mean-square radii, and spherical components of single-neutron levels were analyzed for neutron-rich sodium isotopes from $^{31}$Na to $^{41}$Na.

## Key numbers

- $^{39}$Na has a prolate ground-state deformation $\beta_2 \approx 0.45$ and a two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n} \lesssim 1$ MeV, consistent with a Borromean structure.
- $^{41}$Na is less deformed with $\beta_2 = 0.37$ and is the last bound sodium isotope in PC-PK1 results, with $S_{2n} = 0.49$ MeV.
- In $^{39}$Na, the halo orbitals contribute 0.56 $p$-wave neutrons total, about 30% of the valence neutrons; in $^{41}$Na, more than 1.2 $p$-wave neutrons are contributed.
- The pairing strength used was $V_0 = -325$ MeV fm$^3$, with saturation density $\rho_{\mathrm{sat}} = 0.152$ fm$^{-3}$ and a pairing window of 100 MeV.
- The $1f_{7/2}$-$2p_{3/2}$ energy difference in the spherical limit is smaller than 1.0 MeV for $^{40}$Mg and smaller than 2.5 MeV for $^{42}$Si in DRHBc calculations with NL3* and PK1 functionals.

## Context

This work extends the study of $N=28$ shell quenching previously suggested for $^{40}$Mg and $^{42}$Si (Bastin 2007, Doornenbal 2013) to the newly discovered $^{39}$Na (Ahn 2022), and adds $^{39,41}$Na as new candidates for deformed halo nuclei with shape decoupling. The results build on prior DRHBc studies of halo nuclei such as $^{31}$Ne (Zhong 2022) and $^{42,44}$Mg (Zhou 2010, Li 2012), and the microscopic mechanism of oblate halo formation from $p$-wave neutron orbitals is revealed. Future experimental measurements of reaction cross sections, core momentum distributions, and Coulomb dissociation are anticipated to test these predictions.
