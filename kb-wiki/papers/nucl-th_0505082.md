# Soft-core meson-baryon interactions. I. One-hadron-exchange potentials

> The Nijmegen soft-core model for the pseudoscalar-meson baryon interaction is derived, analogous to the Nijmegen NN and YN models. The interaction Hamiltonians are defined and the resulting amplitudes for one-meson-exchange and one-baryon-exchange in momentum space are given for the general mass case. The partial wave projection is carried through and explicit expressions for the momentum space partial wave meson-baryon potentials are presented.

## Key claim

The paper derives the theoretical framework for the Nijmegen soft-core meson-baryon model, analogous to the previously developed Nijmegen NN and YN models, but the actual construction and fitting to data is deferred to a companion paper (Paper II). The model assumes "dynamical pair suppression" (neglecting negative-energy baryon states) and uses Gaussian form factors, with the explicit claim that this approach provides a natural starting point for constructing a kaon-nucleon model via SU_f(3) extension.

## Method

The authors define effective baryon-baryon-meson and meson-meson-meson interaction Hamiltonians and derive the resulting one-meson-exchange and one-baryon-exchange Feynman diagrams in momentum space for general baryon and meson masses. They perform partial wave projections of these potentials and present explicit expressions for the momentum space partial wave potentials for scalar-meson, vector-meson, tensor-meson, and baryon exchanges. The integral equation for the amplitude is a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, solved on a partial wave basis to obtain phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* The model is intended to fit empirical pion-nucleon S- and P-wave phase shifts up to a pion laboratory energy of 600 MeV (stated in the abstract as a result, though the full fitting is in Paper II).
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is given as Lambda_{chi SB} approximately 1 GeV^2, where the QCD vacuum becomes unstable and constituent quark masses of about 300 MeV are generated.
* The effective field theory includes meson and baryon masses with M less than or equal to 1.5 GeV.
* The kaon beams at J-PARC and GSI (FAIR) are expected to have approximately ten times higher intensity than currently available beams at Brookhaven National Laboratory and KEK.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core approach, previously successful for nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions as developed in NRS78 and MRS89, to meson-baryon systems. The model provides a theoretical framework that can be extended via SU_f(3) symmetry to construct kaon-nucleon potentials, addressing the lack of accurate kaon-nucleon scattering data. The paper sets up the formalism for subsequent fitting to pion-nucleon data (Paper II), which will then serve as input for kaon-nucleon models needed for interpreting experiments at new facilities like J-PARC and FAIR.
