# Differential Cross Section of DP-Elastic Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The deuteron-proton elastic scattering is studied in the multiple scattering expansion formalism. The contributions of the one-nucleon-exchange, single- and double scattering are taken into account. The Love and Franey parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon $t$-matrix is used, that gives an opportunity to include the off-energy-shell effects into calculations. Differential cross sections are considered at four energies, $T_d=390, 500, 880, 1200$ MeV. The obtained results are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The multiple scattering expansion formalism, including one-nucleon-exchange, single-scattering, and double-scattering terms with the Love and Franey parameterization of the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix, can describe deuteron-proton elastic scattering differential cross sections at intermediate energies (deuteron kinetic energies 390–1200 MeV) up to scattering angles of about 140° in the center-of-mass, but the agreement degrades at backward angles above 140° where the multiple scattering series may not be converged or three-nucleon forces may be needed.

## Method

The author iterates the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations up to second order in the two-nucleon t-matrix to obtain the reaction amplitude as a sum of one-nucleon-exchange, single-scattering, and double-scattering contributions. Calculations are performed in the deuteron Breit frame using the CD-Bonn non-relativistic deuteron wave function, with Lorentz transformations and Wigner rotations applied to relate the wave function and the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix between frames. The Love and Franey parameterization of the NN t-matrix, with a new fit to the SP07 phase-shift analysis, is used to include off-energy-shell effects and the full three-nucleon propagator (including the principal value part) is retained in the double-scattering term.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections calculated at four deuteron kinetic energies: T_d = 390, 500, 880, and 1200 MeV.
- At 500 MeV, including double scattering changes the cross section by a factor of about 2 in the minimum compared to the one-nucleon-exchange plus single-scattering result.
- At 880 MeV, the double-scattering effect increases the cross section by a factor of about 10 in the minimum.
- At 1200 MeV, the double-scattering effect increases the cross section by a factor of about 20 in the minimum.
- The "full" calculations (including the principal value part of the three-nucleon propagator) agree with experimental data up to scattering angles of about 140° in the center-of-mass at all four energies.

## Context

This work extends the multiple scattering expansion approach previously applied to polarization observables by the same author (Ladygina, 2009) to differential cross sections at intermediate energies (200–600 MeV per nucleon), a region where Glauber theory is not valid and Faddeev calculations do not work properly. The results are compared with experimental data from Ermish (year not given), Adelberger (year not given), and others at the four energies. The paper enables future investigations of backward-angle scattering where three-nucleon forces or higher-order multiple scattering terms, as studied by Witala (year not given) and Deltuva (year not given) at lower energies, may be needed.
