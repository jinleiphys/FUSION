# Nucleon-nucleon potential in finite nuclei

> We consider the spin-isospin-independent central part of the residual nucleon-nucleon potential in finite spherical nuclei taking into account the deformation effects of the nucleons within the surrounding nuclear environment. It is shown that inside the nucleus the short-range repulsive contribution of the potential is increased and the intermediate attraction is decreased. We identify the growth of the radial component of the spin-isospin independent short-range part of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon interaction as the responsible agent that prevents the radial collapse of the nucleus.

# Nucleon-nucleon potential in finite nuclei

## Key claim

The spin-isospin-independent central part of the residual nucleon-nucleon potential in finite spherical nuclei shows increased short-range repulsion and decreased intermediate attraction when nucleon deformation effects from the surrounding nuclear environment are included. This growth of the radial component of the short-range repulsion is identified as the mechanism that prevents radial collapse of the nucleus, compensating the attractive mean-field potential that would otherwise draw nucleons to the center.

## Method

The authors use a scale-invariant version of the in-medium Skyrme model including a dilaton field, with nuclear density dependence incorporated through in-medium coefficients α_p and α_s derived from pion-nucleus scattering data. They minimize the mass functional for single skyrmions at various distances from the center of finite spherical nuclei (¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca) to obtain deformed chiral and dilaton profile functions, then insert these deformed profiles into a product ansatz to compute the residual NN potential in the radial direction via the standard projection procedure for the spin-isospin-independent central part.

## Key numbers

- The free-space sigma meson mass is taken as m_σ = 550 MeV, corresponding to a free-space gluon condensate parameter C_g = (260 MeV)⁴
- The in-medium sigma meson mass is parameterized as m_σ*(x) = (1 - 0.12 ρ_{A-B}(x)/ρ_0) m_σ, where ρ_0 = 0.5 m_π³ is normal nuclear matter density
- For ¹²C, the nuclear density parameterization uses r_0 = 1.635 fm; for A ≥ 40, parameters are a = 0.58 fm, R' = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm
- Input Skyrme parameters: F_π = 186 MeV, e = 2π
- The empirical parameters from pionic atom analyses: b_0 = -0.024 m_π^{-1}, c_0 = 0.21 m_π^{-3}, with Lorentz-Lorenz factor g_0' = 1/3
- For a single skyrmion in ¹²C at the center (R_k = 0 fm), the chiral radial parameter r_S = 0.512 fm and dilaton radial parameter r_d = 0.843 fm; at R_k = ±2.595 fm, r_S = 0.435 fm and r_d = 0.776 fm
- For a single skyrmion in ⁴⁰Ca at the center (R_k = 0 fm), r_S = 0.597 fm and r_d = 0.983 fm; at R_k = ±6.819 fm, r_S = 0.398 fm and r_d = 0.741 fm
- Free-space values: r_S = 0.398 fm, χ_d = 0.877, r_d = 0.740 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium nucleon properties using the Skyrme model, particularly the finding that bound nucleons acquire an intrinsic quadrupole moment due to deformation effects in finite nuclei (Yakhshiev et al., 2003; earlier work in Nuclear Physics A). It contrasts with studies of the tensor part of the NN potential in infinite nuclear matter (prc001, 2003) by focusing on the spin-isospin-independent central part in finite nuclei. The results are consistent with those of Su et al. (1994) using a chiral σ-ω model under the imaginary-time Green's function method, and with quantum hadrodynamics studies (Gao, 1995) and thermo field dynamics approaches (sgao, 1997; Rakhimov, 1999; Yakhshiev, 2003) that found the NN potential well becomes shallower with increasing temperature or density. The work enables further investigation of nuclear saturation mechanisms and shell structure formation within Skyrme-type models, which are unique in simultaneously describing single-baryon and multi-baryon properties using hadronic degrees of freedom.
