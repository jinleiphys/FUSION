# Entropy production at freeze-out from dissipative fluids

> Entropy production due to shear viscosity during the continuous freeze-out of a longitudinally expanding dissipative fluid is addressed. Assuming the validity of the fluid dynamical description during the continuous removal of interacting matter we estimated a small entropy production as function of the freeze-out duration and the ratio of dissipative to non-dissipative quantities in case of a relativistic massless pion fluid.

## Key claim
Entropy production due to shear viscosity during continuous freeze-out of a longitudinally expanding dissipative fluid is estimated to be small, and is independent of the continuous removal of interacting matter, depending only on the freeze-out duration and the ratio of dissipative to non-dissipative quantities.

## Method
The author uses the relativistic Navier-Stokes (first-order) equations for a 1+1D boost-invariant Bjorken model of a massless pion fluid, incorporating a continuous freeze-out term via an escape probability. The evolution equations for particle number, energy, and entropy densities are solved analytically for a fixed relaxation time, and the entropy production is computed by comparing the entropy density of a viscous fluid to that of a perfect fluid.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out duration: \( L = 4 \) fm/c, starting at proper time \( \tau_0 = 5 \) fm/c.
- Initial thermalization time and freeze-out characteristic time: \( \tau_{th}(\tau_0) \sim \tau'(\tau_0) \approx 1 \) fm/c.
- Initial temperature: \( T_0 = 160 \) MeV.
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density is initially close to the conjectured minimal bound \( \eta/s \geq 1/(4\pi) \).
- Entropy production at freeze-out is up to two orders of magnitude less than entropy produced in the initial stage over a similar timescale of \( \simeq 1 \) fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the fluid dynamical modeling of freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating shear viscosity into the continuous freeze-out framework, building on prior work by Gavin (shear viscosity for massless pions), Hirano and Gyulassy, and Dumitru et al. It enables more accurate estimates of total entropy production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the late-stage evolution where dissipative effects become relevant. The results suggest that entropy production from shear viscosity at freeze-out is small compared to initial-stage entropy production, but the author notes that different equations of state or inclusion of transverse expansion could modify this conclusion.
