# What makes the peak structure of the $╬øp$ invariant-mass spectrum in the $K^{-} {}^{3} {\rm He} \to ╬øp n$ reaction?

> Recently a peak structure was observed near the $K^{-} p p$ threshold in the in-flight ${}^{3} {\rm He} (K^{-} , \, ╬øp) n$ reaction of the E15 experiment at J-PARC, which could be a signal of a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state. In order to investigate what is the origin of this peak, we calculate the cross section of this reaction, in particular based on the scenario that the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is indeed generated and decays into $╬øp$. We find that the numerical result of the $╬øp$ invariant-mass spectrum in the $\bar{K} N N$ bound scenario is consistent with the J-PARC E15 data.

## Key claim

The peak structure observed near the $K^{-}pp$ threshold in the J-PARC E15 experiment is consistent with the interpretation that it originates from a $\bar{K}NN$ bound state, but only when the full multiple-scattering series (including the bound-state pole) is summed; the simpler uncorrelated $\Lambda(1405)p$ scenario produces a spectrum that falls short of the data at lower invariant masses.

## Method

The authors calculated the cross section of the $K^{-} {}^{3}{\rm He} \to \Lambda p n$ reaction at a kaon laboratory momentum of $1~{\rm GeV}/c$ using a diagrammatic approach where the initial kaon first undergoes a quasi-elastic scattering ($K^{-}p \to \bar{K}^{0}n$ or $K^{-}n \to K^{-}n$) to produce a forward neutron and a slow $\bar{K}$, which then propagates and is absorbed by two nucleons. The $\bar{K}NN$ absorption vertex was treated via the fixed-center approximation to the Faddeev equation, summing multiple $\bar{K}$ scatterings between the two nucleons, which generates a $\bar{K}NN$ bound-state pole. The elementary $K^{-}N$ amplitudes were fitted to reproduce experimental cross sections at the relevant momentum.

## Key numbers

- The $\bar{K}NN$ bound-state pole was found at $2354 - 36i~{\rm MeV}$ in the scattering amplitude.
- The calculated total cross section for the reaction is $7.6~\mu{\rm b}$, consistent with the empirical value $7 \pm 1~\mu{\rm b}$ from the E15 experiment.
- The uncorrelated $\Lambda(1405)p$ scenario produced a peak at $2370~{\rm MeV}$ in the $\Lambda p$ invariant-mass spectrum.
- The kinematic peak from quasi-elastic kaon scattering appears at a minimum $\Lambda p$ invariant mass of approximately $2.40~{\rm GeV}$ for forward neutron emission ($\theta_n^{\rm cm} = 0^\circ$).
- The experimental peak from E15 has mass $M_X = 2355^{+6}_{-8}~({\rm stat.}) \pm 12~({\rm sys.})~{\rm MeV}$ and width $\Gamma_X = 110^{+19}_{-17}~({\rm stat.}) \pm 27~({\rm sys.})~{\rm MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of kaonic nuclei, specifically the $\bar{K}NN$ ($I=1/2$) system, following the J-PARC E15 experiment by Sada et al. (2016) which observed a peak structure in the $\Lambda p$ invariant-mass spectrum. The authors previously developed the uncorrelated $\Lambda(1405)p$ scenario (Sekihara et al., 2016), which produced a peak at 2370 MeV but failed to match the low-energy tail of the data; the present full calculation including the $\bar{K}NN$ bound state resolves this discrepancy. The results enable future analysis of high-statistics data from the second run of the E15 experiment (Iwasaki, 2017), which will allow study of angular dependence and the $\Lambda p / \Sigma^0 p$ branching ratio to further confirm the existence of the $\bar{K}NN$ bound state.
