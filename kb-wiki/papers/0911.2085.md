# Strange and charm mesons at FAIR

> We study the properties of strange and charm mesons in hot and dense matter within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach for the experimental conditions of density and temperature expected for the CBM experiment at FAIR/GSI. The in-medium solution at finite temperature accounts for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding of all the baryons involved, and meson self-energies. We analyze the behaviour in this hot and dense environment of dynamically-generated baryonic resonances together with the evolution with density and temperature of the strange and open-charm meson spectral functions. We test the spectral functions for strange mesons using energy-weighted sum rules and finally discuss the implications of the properties of charm mesons on the D_{s0}(2317) and the predicted X(3700) scalar resonances.

## Key claim

The paper studies strange and charm meson properties in hot and dense matter under conditions expected for the CBM experiment at FAIR, but the results are obtained from a self-consistent coupled-channel model that incorporates Pauli blocking, mean-field binding, and meson self-energies, rather than from direct experimental measurement.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels using a transition potential from effective Lagrangians, then obtain the meson self-energy by summing the in-medium T-matrix over the nucleon Fermi distribution at finite temperature. For strange mesons, a chiral unitary approach incorporates s-wave and p-wave kaon-nucleon interactions, while for charm mesons, two approaches are used: a broken SU(4) s-wave Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term, and an SU(8) spin-flavor scheme incorporating heavy-quark symmetry. The self-energy is determined self-consistently since the in-medium amplitude contains the meson-baryon loop function which depends on the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Antikaon attractive potential of approximately -50 MeV at normal nuclear matter density from prior approaches
- Beam energy range for CBM experiment at FAIR: 10 to 40 GeV/u
- The D_{s0}(2317) and X(3700) resonances develop widths of order 100 MeV and 200 MeV respectively at normal nuclear matter density
- Momentum values studied: q = 0 MeV/c and q = 450 MeV/c for antikaon spectral functions; q = 150 MeV/c for sum rule analysis; q = 0 MeV/c for D and D* spectral functions
- Temperatures studied: T = 0 MeV and T = 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of strangeness in exotic atoms (Friedman and Gal, 2007) and heavy-ion collisions at SIS/GSI energies (Fuchs, 2005) to include charm degrees of freedom for the CBM experiment at FAIR. The approach builds on earlier self-consistent coupled-channel calculations for antikaons in dense matter (Ramos and Oset, 2000; Tolos, Ramos and Oset, 2006) and extends them to finite temperature and to the charm sector. The results enable predictions for the in-medium behavior of dynamically-generated resonances such as the D_{s0}(2317) and X(3700), and the authors suggest using transparency ratios to investigate the in-medium widths of these resonances in nuclear matter.
