# On the Spectacular Large Asymmetry in $\pbarppipi$ and $\pbarpKK$ Reactions}

> An illustrative analysis is presented to show the origin of the energy-independent maximal asymmetry observed for wide ranges of angles in the reactions $\pbarppipi$ and $\pbarpKK$. % The general nature of our simple relation between helicity -flip and -nonflip partial wave amplitudes enforces the notion that % these features of the asymmetry for these two annihilation reactions are likely to persist within the hadronic regime. % At higher energies these features of the asymmetry will probably be modified significantly, signaling the onset of perturbative QCD. % Our study supports the arguments that the final $\KbarK$ state originates from a more central reaction than the $\pipi$ final state.

## Key claim

The energy-independent maximal asymmetry observed in $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$ and $\overline{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^+$ reactions over wide angular ranges can be explained by a simple differential relation between helicity-flip and helicity-nonflip partial wave amplitudes, without requiring explicit meson resonances; however, this maximal asymmetry is expected to break down at higher energies, signaling the onset of perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors performed a diffraction model analysis using partial wave expansions of the two independent helicity amplitudes ($f_{++}$ for helicity non-flip and $f_{+-}$ for helicity flip). They assumed that the helicity-flip amplitude $T_-^J$ is proportional to the derivative of the helicity-nonflip amplitude $T_+^J$ with respect to the impact parameter $b$, leading to a relation $T_-^J \propto \Delta T_+^J / \Delta J$. This assumption was further examined using a DWBA approach with an optical potential for the initial $\overline{N}N$ channel and a parametrized effective transition operator.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry $A_{0n}$ reaches the maximal possible value of 1 over wide ranges of angles and laboratory momenta $p_{lab}$ between about 1 GeV/c and 2.2 GeV/c.
- For $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$, the asymmetry is very large for $p_{lab} \gtrsim 1.5$ GeV/c.
- For $\overline{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^+$, the asymmetry has values close to 1 for $p_{lab} \gtrsim 1$ GeV/c.
- To reproduce the observed differential cross section for $\overline{p}p \rightarrow \pi^-\pi^+$, the model requires $a \approx 0$ and $J_{max} = 4$ (a "black" sphere amplitude with radius $J_{max}/q$).
- For $\overline{p}p \rightarrow K^-K^+$, the data requires $a \approx 0.5$ (a "grey" sphere), meaning the lowest partial waves (J=0 and 1) dominate.
- With an imaginary $\beta (=i)$, the asymmetry from the model is larger than 0.9 over a very wide angular range ($|\cos\theta| \sim 0.8$).

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological analyses that speculated about possible J=3, 4 and 5 meson resonances (Ca77) and a dispersion relation partial wave analysis (MO88) that was not incompatible with resonance activity. The authors' "geometrical" analysis provides an alternative explanation without requiring explicit resonances. The study supports arguments by Richard that the final $\overline{K}K$ state originates from a more central reaction than the $\pi\pi$ final state, consistent with the idea that annihilating more valence quark-antiquark pairs leads to more central reactions. The measurement of asymmetry for $p_{lab} > 2$ GeV/c is expected to be useful for monitoring the possible onset of perturbative QCD, as discussed by Carlson et al. (1992) and Myhrer (1993).
