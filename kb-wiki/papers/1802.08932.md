# Auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of light and medium-mass nuclei with local chiral interactions

> Quantum Monte Carlo methods have recently been employed to study properties of nuclei and infinite matter using local chiral effective field theory interactions. In this work, we present a detailed description of the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo algorithm for nuclei in combination with local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order. We show results for the binding energy, charge radius, charge form factor, and Coulomb sum rule in nuclei with $3\le A\le16$. Particular attention is devoted to the effect of different operator structures in the three-body force for different cutoffs. The outcomes suggest that local chiral interactions fit to few-body observables give a very good description of the ground-state properties of nuclei up to $^{16}$O, with the exception of one fit for the softer cutoff which predicts overbinding in larger nuclei.

## Key claim

Local chiral interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order, fitted to few-body observables, provide a very good description of ground-state properties of nuclei up to oxygen-16, with the exception of one fit for the softer cutoff (R0 = 1.2 fm) which predicts overbinding in larger nuclei.

## Method

The authors implemented the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) algorithm for nuclei using local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO). The three-body force was treated by including terms with quadratic spin and isospin operators directly in the propagator via Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, while the cubic spin-isospin term (Vc) was handled perturbatively by adjusting three constants to minimize its contribution. The method was applied to compute binding energies, charge radii, charge form factors, and Coulomb sum rules for nuclei with mass numbers from 3 to 16.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies calculated for nuclei with 3 ≤ A ≤ 16 using local chiral interactions at N2LO with two cutoff values: R0 = 1.0 fm (approximately 500 MeV momentum cutoff) and R0 = 1.2 fm (approximately 400 MeV momentum cutoff)
- The three-body force contact terms were fit to the alpha particle binding energy and to the spin-orbit splitting in the neutron-alpha P-wave phase shifts
- The pion decay constant used was fπ = 92.4 MeV, the averaged pion mass was mπ = 138.03 MeV, and the axial-vector coupling constant was gA = 1.267
- The chiral breakdown scale was taken as Λχ = 700 MeV
- The coordinate-space cutoffs were R0 = 1.0 fm and R0 = 1.2 fm

## Context

This work extends the application of AFDMC methods from phenomenological interactions like the Argonne v18 potential combined with Urbana/Illinois three-nucleon forces (Carlson 2015) to local chiral effective field theory interactions developed by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) and Lynn (2016, 2017). The approach enables systematic improvement of nuclear interactions order by order and provides a way to estimate theoretical uncertainties, overcoming limitations of phenomenological models that cannot describe heavy neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The method allows calculations beyond A = 12, which is the current limit for Green's function Monte Carlo calculations that explicitly sum over all spin and isospin states.
