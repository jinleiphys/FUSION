# Chiral Thermodynamics in a finite box

> Finite-volume modifications of the two-flavor chiral phase diagram are investigated within an effective quark-meson model in various mean-field approximations. The role of vacuum fluctuations and boundary conditions, their influence on higher cumulants and signatures of a possible pseudo-critical endpoint are amplified with smaller volumes.

# Chiral Thermodynamics in a finite box

## Key claim

Finite-volume effects in the two-flavor chiral phase diagram are studied within a quark-meson model, and the authors find that when vacuum fluctuations are included (renormalized mean-field approximation), the pseudocritical endpoint moves to higher temperatures and then vanishes at larger chemical potentials for decreasing box sizes, which would be problematic for experimental searches if this behavior is generic.

## Method

The authors employ the two-flavor quark-meson model in a spatial box of equal length L with periodic, periodic without zero mode, and antiperiodic boundary conditions. They compare two mean-field approximations: the standard mean-field approximation (sMFA), which ignores the divergent vacuum quark-loop contribution, and the renormalized mean-field approximation (rMFA), which fully includes the renormalized vacuum term. The grand potential is computed by performing only the quark path integral, and the resulting phase diagram is studied as a function of box size.

## Key numbers

- For periodic boundary conditions without the zero mode (PBC*), a chiral symmetry restoration phenomenon occurs around 1/L ~ 0.4 fm^{-1} for decreasing volumes.
- For box sizes L >= 10 fm, both ABC and PBC* boundary conditions reproduce the infinite-volume limit in both sMFA and rMFA.
- In rMFA, the crossover temperature at mu=0 decreases by about 20% for smaller systems when the zero mode is excluded, then turns back for L < 2 fm towards larger values.
- In rMFA, the pseudocritical endpoint disappears from the phase diagram around L ~ 2.4 fm, and the negative region of the first three even cumulants (chi_4, chi_6, chi_8) is drastically washed out.

## Context

This work extends finite-volume studies of the QCD phase diagram using effective models, following earlier investigations by Braun (2005) on critical endpoint singularities and by Palhares (2009) on finite-size effects. The results agree qualitatively with functional renormalization group calculations in a similar quark-meson model truncation by Braun (2011), which found that for periodic boundary conditions the crossover curvature decreases for decreasing volumes until m_pi L < 2, then turns back and increases. The findings suggest that finite-size corrections may push the critical endpoint to regimes inaccessible to current heavy-ion experiments, and that higher cumulants of particle multiplicity fluctuations are smoothed out in small volumes, complicating experimental searches for critical behavior.
