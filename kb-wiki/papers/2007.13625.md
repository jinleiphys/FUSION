# Coherent and incoherent $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in $PbPb$ collisions at the LHC, HE -- LHC and FCC

> The coherent and incoherent $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in $PbPb$ collisions are investigated considering the possible states of nucleon configurations in the nuclear wave function and taking into account of the non - linear corrections to the QCD dynamics. The cross sections and the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions are estimated for the energies of the next run of the LHC, High -- Energy LHC and Future Circular Collider. Our results indicate that a future experimental analysis of these processes will be useful to discriminate between different approaches for the QCD dynamics as well to improve our description of the gluon saturation effects.

## Key claim

The coherent and incoherent J/Psi photoproduction cross sections and rapidity and transverse momentum distributions in PbPb collisions at LHC, HE-LHC, and FCC energies are sensitive to non-linear (saturation) effects in QCD dynamics, but the discrimination between saturation and non-saturation scenarios depends on the specific observable and kinematic range, with the transverse momentum distribution being particularly useful at larger energies.

## Method

The authors used the color dipole formalism within the Glauber-Gribov framework to compute coherent and incoherent J/Psi photoproduction cross sections in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions. They modeled the nuclear profile by sampling nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution, each with a Gaussian profile of width B_p, and generated 500 distinct nucleon configurations using the Sartre event generator. The dipole-proton cross section was described using the IP-SAT model (b-Sat) including multiple elastic rescatterings, and compared with the IP-NONSAT model (b-Non Sat) that disregards these non-linear corrections.

## Key numbers

- For coherent J/Psi photoproduction at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV (LHC): total cross section 22.6 mb (b-Sat) vs 36.8 mb (b-Non Sat)
- For coherent J/Psi photoproduction at sqrt(s) = 39 TeV (FCC): total cross section 98.7 mb (b-Sat) vs 184.4 mb (b-Non Sat)
- For incoherent J/Psi photoproduction at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV (LHC): total cross section 4.2 mb (b-Sat) vs 9.0 mb (b-Non Sat)
- For incoherent J/Psi photoproduction at sqrt(s) = 39 TeV (FCC): total cross section 17.3 mb (b-Sat) vs 45.6 mb (b-Non Sat)
- Maximum photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy W_max = 0.95 TeV at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV) and 6.8 TeV at FCC (sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV)
- The b-Sat predictions for midrapidity are a factor greater than or approximately 1.5 smaller than the b-Non Sat predictions
- Expected event rates per year: greater than 10^6 at LHC, greater than 10^7 at HE-LHC, greater than 10^8 at FCC (assuming integrated luminosities of 3.0, 10, and 110 nb^{-1} respectively)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of J/Psi photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions at lower center-of-mass energies by Goncalves et al. (2014-2019), Lappi and Mantysaari (2013), Guzey et al. (2018), and Contreras (2017) to the higher energies of the next LHC run (5.5 TeV), HE-LHC (10.6 TeV), and FCC (39 TeV). The results enable future experimental analyses to discriminate between saturation and non-saturation scenarios in QCD dynamics, which is fundamental for understanding the initial conditions of the quark-gluon plasma produced in central and semi-central heavy ion collisions.
