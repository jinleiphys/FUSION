# Nuclear symmetry energy probed by neutron skin thickness of nuclei

> We describe a relation between the symmetry energy coefficients c_sym(rho) of nuclear matter and a_sym(A) of finite nuclei that accommodates other correlations of nuclear properties with the low-density behavior of c_sym(rho). Here we take advantage of this relation to explore the prospects for constraining c_sym(rho) of systematic measurements of neutron skin sizes across the mass table, using as example present data from antiprotonic atoms. The found constraints from neutron skins are in harmony with the recent determinations from reactions and giant resonances.

# Nuclear symmetry energy probed by neutron skin thickness of nuclei

## Key claim
The symmetry energy coefficient of nuclear matter at subsaturation density (around 0.1 fm⁻³) equals the symmetry energy coefficient of heavy finite nuclei, a relation that holds universally across mean field theories. This relation allows neutron skin thickness measurements from antiprotonic atoms to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, yielding results consistent with constraints from heavy-ion reactions and giant resonances, though the probed densities and energies are not necessarily the same.

## Method
The authors employed the nuclear droplet model to derive analytical expressions linking the neutron skin thickness to the symmetry energy parameters of nuclear matter. They applied these formulas to fit the experimental neutron skin data from 26 stable nuclei (⁴⁰Ca to ²³⁸U) measured in antiprotonic atoms, using a power-law parametrization of the symmetry energy density dependence. The analysis was performed both with and without a correction term for differences in neutron and proton surface widths, with the latter evaluated from asymmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter calculations.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy coefficient at saturation density J is about 32 MeV, with the slope parameter L governing its density dependence around ρ₀
- From antiprotonic atom neutron skin data with the surface width correction set to zero: L = 75 ± 25 MeV (γ = 0.79 ± 0.25)
- The symmetry term of the incompressibility Kτ from neutron skins: Kτ = -500⁺¹²⁵₋₁₀₀ MeV
- Including the surface width correction shifts L by up to -25 MeV and Kτ by up to +125 MeV
- The transition density between crust and core of a neutron star estimated from the neutron skin constraints: ρt ≈ 0.095 ± 0.01 fm⁻³
- The relation csy(ρ) = asy(A) holds for ²⁰⁸Pb at ρ ≈ 0.1 fm⁻³, for A=116 at ρ ≈ 0.093 fm⁻³, and for A=40 at ρ ≈ 0.08 fm⁻³ across multiple nuclear models

## Context
This work extends the understanding of the symmetry energy density dependence by establishing a direct connection between the finite-nucleus symmetry energy coefficient and the nuclear matter symmetry energy at subsaturation density. The results are consistent with constraints from isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions (Li 2005, Li 2008) giving L = 88 ± 25 MeV, isoscaling (Shetty 2007) favoring L ≈ 65 MeV, and the giant dipole resonance analysis of ²⁰⁸Pb (Trippa 2008) constraining csy(0.1 fm⁻³) = 23.3–24.9 MeV. The findings enable tighter constraints on the nuclear equation of state for neutron-rich matter, with implications for neutron star crust-core transition density and cooling processes, and motivate further measurements of neutron skins using both hadronic and electroweak probes such as the PREX experiment.
