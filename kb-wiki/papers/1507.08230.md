# Numerical study of the unitary Fermi gas across the superfluid transition

> We present results from Monte Carlo calculations investigating the properties of the homogeneous, spin-balanced unitary Fermi gas in three dimensions. The temperature is varied across the superfluid transition allowing us to determine the temperature dependence of the chemical potential, the energy per particle and the contact density. Numerical artifacts due to finite volume and discretization are systematically studied, estimated, and reduced.

## Key claim

The paper presents Monte Carlo calculations of thermodynamic properties of the homogeneous, spin-balanced unitary Fermi gas across the superfluid transition, with the hedge that results are obtained after systematic extrapolations to the thermodynamic and continuum limits, and that agreement with experiment is found generally but not for every observable detail (e.g., the authors do not observe the sharp drop in contact density around T_c reported by some experiments).

## Method

The authors use the Diagrammatic Determinant Monte Carlo (DDMC) algorithm on a three-dimensional periodic lattice with the Fermi-Hubbard model, tuning the coupling constant to U = -7.914/b^2 to achieve infinite scattering length. They perform calculations for multiple lattice sizes (L^3 sites, with L ranging from 6 to 32) and extrapolate results to the thermodynamic limit (1/L → 0), then take the continuum limit by extrapolating to zero filling factor ν → 0 (equivalent to zero lattice spacing) using a fit function linear in ν^{1/3} with Bayesian priors for higher-order terms. The temperature range studied is approximately T_c/2 ≤ T ≤ 2 T_c.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c from previous work (Goulko and Wingate, 2010) is used as the reference point; the gray bar in figures indicates the critical point with error margin.
- The coupling constant used throughout is U = -7.914/b^2, corresponding to infinite scattering length in the infinite-volume limit.
- At the lowest filling factor, volumes up to V = 32^3 were used, corresponding to up to 1000 particles; typical ranges were L = 6 to L = 14 at higher filling factors (about 40 to 700 particles) and L = 10 to L = 32 at lower filling factors (about 100 to 1000 particles).
- For the contact density at zero temperature, the authors cite a value of C/ε_F^2 = 2ζ/5π = 0.1184(64) from experimental work (Salomon et al., 2010).
- The fit parameter c_2 for μ/ε_F at low βμ was found to be approximately 0.3, used as the width of Bayesian priors for higher-order continuum extrapolation terms.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of the critical temperature and thermodynamic observables at T_c (Goulko and Wingate, 2010) to a range of temperatures on both sides of the superfluid transition. It complements bold diagrammatic Monte Carlo calculations (Van Houcke et al., 2012) which are restricted to temperatures above T_c due to singularities in the pair propagator in the superfluid phase, and improves upon earlier lattice hybrid Monte Carlo work (Drut, 2011) by presenting results at multiple lattice spacings and performing systematic continuum extrapolations. The results provide benchmarks for future studies, particularly below T_c where few accurate predictions and measurements are available.
