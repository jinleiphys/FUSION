# The Gluon Spin in the Chiral Bag Model

> We study the gluon polarization contribution at the quark model renormalization scale to the proton spin, $Γ$, in the chiral bag model. It is evaluated by taking the expectation value of the forward matrix element of a local gluon operator in the axial gauge $A^+=0$. It is shown that the confining boundary condition for the color electric field plays an important role. When a solution satisfying the boundary condition for the color electric field, which is not the conventionally used but which we favor, is used, the $Γ$ has a positive value for {\it all} bag radii and its magnitude is comparable to the quark spin polarization. This results in a significant reduction in the relative fraction of the proton spin carried by the quark spin, which is consistent with the small flavor singlet axial current measured in the EMC experiments.

## Key claim

The gluon spin contribution to the proton spin, \(\Gamma\), is positive for all bag radii in the chiral bag model when a specific boundary condition for the color electric field (\(Q_R(r)\)) is used, contrary to the negative value obtained with the conventionally used solution (\(Q_0(r)\)). This positive gluon spin results in a significant reduction of the quark spin fraction, consistent with the small flavor-singlet axial current measured in EMC experiments.

## Method

The gluon spin \(\Gamma\) was evaluated by taking the expectation value of the forward matrix element of a local gluon operator in the axial gauge \(A^+=0\), using static gluon fields generated by color charge and current distributions of valence quarks in the chiral bag model. The calculation incorporated the exchange of static gluon fields responsible for the \(N-\Delta\) mass splitting, and two different solutions for the color electric field were compared: the conventional \(Q_0(r)\) (regular at the origin) and the alternative \(Q_R(r)\) (satisfying the local boundary condition \(\mathbf{r}\cdot\mathbf{E}=0\) on the bag surface). The resulting gluon moment of inertia \(\mathcal{I}_G\) was then used to determine the fraction of proton spin carried by gluons via collective coordinate quantization.

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma_\lambda = \frac{4}{3} \alpha \int_0^R r dr \, Q_\lambda(r) (h(R)-2h(r))\), where \(\alpha = g_s^2/4\pi\) and the numerical factor arises from color matrix elements \(\sum_a \langle \lambda_i^a \lambda_j^a \rangle_{\text{baryon}} = -8/3\) for \(i \neq j\).
- The expectation value \(\sum_{i\neq j}\sum_{a=1}^8 \langle p, \uparrow | S_3 \frac{\lambda_i^a}{2} \frac{\lambda_j^a}{2} |p,\uparrow \rangle = -2\) in the chiral bag model, differing from the MIT bag model value of \(-4/3\).
- For the alternative solution \(Q_R(r)\), the gluon moment of inertia \(\mathcal{I}_G\) is positive for all bag radii and comparable in size to the quark moment of inertia \(\mathcal{I}_Q\).
- For the conventional solution \(Q_0(r)\), \(\mathcal{I}_G\) is negative, leading to gluons anti-aligned with the proton spin.
- The flavor-singlet axial current including the \(U_A(1)\) anomaly is given by \(a_0 = \Sigma - (N_f \alpha / 2\pi) \Gamma'_\lambda\), where \(\Gamma'_\lambda = -\frac{4}{3}\alpha \int_0^R r dr \, Q_\lambda(r) h(r)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gluon spin in the MIT bag model by Jaffe (1996), which found a negative \(\Gamma \sim -0.1 \alpha_{\text{bag}}\), and the QCD sum rule calculation by Mankiewicz, Piller, and Schäfer (1997) yielding a positive value \(2\Gamma \sim 2.1 \pm 1.0\) at 1 GeV\(^2\). It also relates to the calculation by Barone, Calarco, and Drago (1998), who obtained a positive \(\Gamma \sim +0.12\) in the Isgur-Karl quark model by including self-interaction contributions. The results enable a consistent description of the proton spin crisis within the chiral bag model, where the positive gluon spin reduces the quark spin fraction and reproduces the small flavor-singlet axial current measured by the EMC experiment, while also highlighting the role of boundary conditions and gauge dependence in the "Cheshire Cat" picture.
