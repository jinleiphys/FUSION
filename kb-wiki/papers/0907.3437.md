# Two-pion exchange electromagnetic current in chiral effective field theory using the method of unitary transformation

> We derive the leading two-pion exchange contributions to the two-nucleon electromagnetic current operator in the framework of chiral effective field theory using the method of unitary transformation. Explicit results for the current and charge densities are given in momentum and coordinate space.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading two-pion exchange contributions to the two-nucleon electromagnetic current operator in chiral effective field theory using the method of unitary transformation, but the derivation is restricted to photon momenta of order the pion mass in the two-nucleon rest frame, with the time component of the photon momentum being much smaller than the pion mass.

## Method

The authors employ the method of unitary transformation to decouple the purely nucleonic subspace from the pionic part of the Fock space, then compute the effective two-nucleon electromagnetic current operator perturbatively using the chiral Lagrangian at leading one-loop order. They evaluate all loop integrals analytically using dimensional regularization, retaining only the non-polynomial (long-range) parts, and perform the Fourier transformation to obtain compact coordinate-space expressions for both the current and charge densities. The calculation distinguishes seven classes of diagrams based on powers of the axial coupling constant \(g_A\) and the type of hadronic current vertex.

## Key numbers

- The leading two-pion exchange current is computed at order \(\mathcal{O}(e Q)\), where \(Q\) is the soft scale of order the pion mass.
- The loop functions \(L(q)\) and \(A(q)\) are defined as \(L(q) = \frac{1}{2}\frac{s}{q}\ln\left(\frac{s+q}{s-q}\right)\) with \(s = \sqrt{q^2+4M_\pi^2}\) and \(A(q) = \frac{1}{2q}\arctan\left(\frac{q}{2M_\pi}\right)\).
- The coordinate-space current density for class c1 is proportional to \(\frac{g_A^2 M_\pi^7}{128 \pi^3 F_\pi^4} \frac{K_1(2x_{10})}{x_{10}^2}\), where \(x_{10} = M_\pi |\vec{r}_{10}|\) and \(K_1\) is a modified Bessel function.
- The coordinate-space charge density for class c4 is proportional to \(\frac{g_A^2 M_\pi^7}{256 \pi^2 F_\pi^4} \tau_1^3 \, \delta(\vec{x}_{20}) \, (\nabla_{10}^2 - 2) \frac{e^{-2x_{10}}}{x_{10}^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach to nuclear currents previously applied to nuclear forces by Epelbaum et al. (2007) and provides an independent check of the two-pion exchange current results obtained by Pastore et al. (2008, 2009) using time-ordered perturbation theory. Unlike Pastore et al., the authors also present the exchange charge density for the first time and give fully analytic coordinate-space expressions. The results enable future calculations of electron and photon inelastic reactions on few-nucleon systems at momentum transfers of order the pion mass, where experimental data are available, using consistent chiral EFT currents and potentials.
