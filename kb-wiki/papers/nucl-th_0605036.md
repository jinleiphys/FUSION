# Propagation of broad meson resonances in a BUU type transport model: Application to di-electron production

> We apply a BUU type transport model for the interpretation of the di-electron invariant mass spectrum measured by the HADES collaboration for the reaction C(2 AGeV) + C. Our model incorporates the propagation of broad meson resonances emerging from the decay of baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The BRoBUU transport model, which incorporates the propagation of broad meson resonances via a spectral-function technique, can reproduce the di-electron invariant mass spectrum measured by HADES for C(2 AGeV) + C, but a conclusive decision on medium effects cannot be made because the fine structure in the data is not yet resolved.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation using a parallel-ensemble test-particle method for nucleons, 24 Delta and N* resonances, and pi, eta, sigma, omega, and rho mesons. Vector mesons are produced primarily from baryon resonance decays, and their in-medium spectral functions are propagated by evolving test-particle masses according to a derived equation based on the self-energies. The di-electron yield from vector meson decays and from the pi pi to rho to e+e- channel is integrated along particle trajectories, with a HADES acceptance filter applied for comparison to data.

## Key numbers

- The measured pi0 production cross section is 707 +/- 72 mb; the calculation yields 700 mb.
- The measured eta production cross section is 25 +/- 4 mb; the calculation yields 20 mb.
- A mass shift of Delta m_omega = -50 MeV is used for the omega meson, suggested by CB-TAPS data.
- A mass shift of Delta m_rho = -200 MeV is used for the rho meson.
- The vacuum rho meson parameters used are: tilde m_rho = 775 MeV, hat m_rho = 761 MeV, tilde Gamma_rho = 118 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BUU-type transport approach of Wolf (1993) and Wolf (1997) by incorporating the spectral-function propagation technique developed by Cassing and Juchem (2000) and Leupold (2000) for broad resonances. The results are compared to the first HADES data for C(2 AGeV) + C (Sturm, 2006), and the medium modifications are guided by QCD sum rules and the CB-TAPS data of Trnka (2005). The study enables future systematic exploration of in-medium vector meson properties in the baryon-dense region accessible at few-AGeV beam energies.
