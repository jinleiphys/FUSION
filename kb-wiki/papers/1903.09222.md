# Gravitational form factors of light mesons

> We calculate the gravitational form factors of the pion, sigma meson, and rho meson in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of quantum chromodynamics. The canonical energy-momentum tensor (EMT) is used in their derivation, allowing the possibility of an antisymmetric contribution when the hadron has intrinsic spin. We show that the asymmetric graviton vertex arising from the canonical EMT satisfies a simpler Ward-Takahashi identity (WTI) than the symmetric graviton vertex of the Belinfante EMT. The necessity of fully dressing the graviton vertex through the relevant Bethe-Salpeter equation is demonstrated for observing both the WTI and a low-energy pion theorem. Lastly, we calculate static moments of the meson EMT decompositions, obtaining predictions for the meson mass radii. We find light cone mass radii of 0.27 fm for the pion, 0.32 fm for the sigma, and 0.25 fm for the rho. For the pion and rho, these are smaller than the light cone charge radii, respectively 0.51 fm and 0.45 fm, while we have a sigma charge radius of zero. Our light cone pion mass radius agrees with a phenomenological extraction from KEKB data.

## Key claim

The authors calculate gravitational form factors of light mesons using the canonical (asymmetric) energy-momentum tensor rather than the symmetric Belinfante-improved tensor, arguing that the canonical tensor satisfies a simpler Ward-Takahashi identity and that adding total derivatives to the EMT can change measurable quantities such as the D-term.

## Method

The authors employ the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as a low-energy effective field theory of QCD, using proper-time regularization with both infrared and ultraviolet regulators. They solve the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-graviton vertex, then compute the meson-graviton coupling through triangle diagrams and a bicycle diagram arising from the four-fermi contact interaction. The gravitational form factors are extracted from the decomposition of the EMT matrix elements for spin-zero (pion, sigma) and spin-one (rho) mesons.

## Key numbers

- Light cone mass radii: 0.27 fm for the pion, 0.32 fm for the sigma, and 0.25 fm for the rho
- Light cone charge radii: 0.51 fm for the pion and 0.45 fm for the rho; the sigma charge radius is zero
- The pion mass radius of 0.27 fm agrees with a phenomenological extraction from KEKB data
- NJL model parameters: infrared regulator 0.240 GeV, ultraviolet regulator 0.645 GeV, dressed quark mass 0.4 GeV, G_pi = 19.0 GeV^{-2}, G_rho = 11.0 GeV^{-2}, G_omega = 10.4 GeV^{-2}, G_f = 0.82 GeV^{-2}, G_T = 0

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of meson properties (Cloet 2014) to the gravitational form factors, complementing lattice QCD calculations of the pion EMT (Brommel 2007, Shanahan 2018) and empirical extractions from KEKB data (Kumano 2017). The results enable comparison between model predictions and phenomenological extractions of meson mass distributions, and provide a framework for understanding how dynamical chiral symmetry breaking constrains the D-term of Nambu-Goldstone bosons as predicted by Novikov (1980) and Voloshin (1980).
