# Single j Shell Observations

> In this report we examine topics relating to previous single j shell model results. A common thread in the two problems is that they both involve the isospin variable in an important way.

# Single j Shell Observations

## Key claim

The authors show that in a single-j shell model, if one restricts the ground-state wavefunction of even-even Ti isotopes to only Jp=Jn=0 and Jp=Jn=2 couplings, the relative amounts of these two components are completely fixed by the orthogonality condition between T=Tmin and T=Tmin+2 states, leaving no freedom; a small admixture of higher-J couplings restores that freedom.

## Method

The authors performed a linear fit to excitation energies of T=Tmin+1 states in odd-A nuclei and T=Tmin+2 states in even-even nuclei in the f-p shell, using a formula E = b(T+X) for single analogs and E = 2b(T+X+1/2) for double analogs. They then converted this to a binding energy formula E = (b/2)T(T+Y) and extracted parameters from single-j shell calculations. Separately, they defined a "single-j IBA" model for Ti isotopes by truncating the MBZ wavefunctions to only Jp=Jn=0 and Jp=Jn=2 couplings, and compared the resulting summed B(M1) strengths with full MBZ results.

## Key numbers

- Linear fit to single-j shell calculations gave b = 2.32 and X = 1.3, leading to binding energy formula E = (b/2) T(T + 1.6)
- For the t(1)·t(2) interaction, X = 1 and Y = 1; for the Wigner SU(4) limit, X = 2.5 and Y = 4
- Single-j IBA wavefunction components for 44Ti: (0.5976, 0.8018); for 46Ti: (0.7906, 0.6124); for 48Ti: (0.9129, 0.4082)
- Summed B(M1) strengths (in μN²) using effective (gp-gn) = 1.89: for 44Ti, MBZ = 2.881 and single-j IBA = 3.289; for 46Ti, MBZ = 1.919 and single-j IBA = 1.9767; for 48Ti, MBZ = 0.8528 and single-j IBA = 0.8568

## Context

This work extends earlier single-j shell calculations by Devi et al. (2004) on excitation energies of high-isospin states, and connects to the binding energy formulas discussed in Talmi's book, which gives T(T+4) for the SU(4) limit and T(T+1) for the seniority-conserving limit. The authors note that McCullen et al. (1964) showed seniority is badly broken when both protons and neutrons are in open shells, and the extracted T(T+1.6) lies between these two extremes. The single-j IBA model defined here most closely resembles IBA2 and enables simplified calculations of M1 transitions that reproduce full MBZ summed strengths remarkably well for 46Ti and 48Ti.
