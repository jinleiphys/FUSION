# Skyrmions with vector mesons revisited

> In order to develop a model that can describe both a single baryon and multi-baryon systems on the same footing, we re-investigate the Skyrme model in a chiral Lagrangian derived from the hidden local symmetry (HLS) up to $O(p^4)$ including the homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms. We use the master formulas that connect the parameters of the HLS Lagrangian and a class of holographic QCD models, which provides a controllable way to determine the low-energy constants of the Lagrangian once the pion decay constant and the vector meson mass are given. Therefore, this model allows us to study the role of vector mesons in the skyrmion structure. We find that the $ρ$ and $ω$ vector mesons have different roles in the skyrmion structure and that the $ω$ meson has an important role in the properties of the nucleon.

# Skyrmions with vector mesons revisited

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ρ and ω vector mesons have opposite roles in the skyrmion structure: the ρ meson reduces the soliton mass and shrinks the soliton wave functions, while the ω meson increases the soliton mass and has the opposite effect on wave functions; furthermore, the ω meson is essential for justifying the standard collective quantization method, because without it the Δ-N mass difference becomes larger than the soliton mass itself.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian up to O(p⁴) using hidden local symmetry (HLS) including homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms, with parameters fixed by master formulas derived from holographic QCD (specifically the Sakai-Sugimoto model). They solve the equations of motion for the hedgehog ansatz with pion, ρ, and ω fields, then quantize the soliton via standard collective coordinate rotation. To isolate the role of each vector meson, they compare three models: HLS₁(π,ρ,ω) (full model), HLS₁(π,ρ) (ω decoupled by omitting homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms), and HLS₁(π) (ρ integrated out).

## Key numbers

- Soliton mass M_sol = 1184 MeV in the full HLS₁(π,ρ,ω) model, compared to 834 MeV in HLS₁(π,ρ) and 922 MeV in HLS₁(π)
- Δ-N mass difference Δ_M = 448 MeV in the full model, compared to 1707 MeV in HLS₁(π,ρ) and 1014 MeV in HLS₁(π)
- RMS energy radius √⟨r²⟩_E = 0.608 fm in the full model, compared to 0.371 fm in HLS₁(π,ρ) and 0.417 fm in HLS₁(π)
- Input parameters: f_π = 92.4 MeV, m_ρ = 775.5 MeV, with the Sakai-Sugimoto model and HLS parameter a = 2

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach of Balachandran et al. (1983) and the hidden local symmetry framework by including vector mesons up to O(p⁴) with parameters fixed by holographic QCD master formulas, following a series of publications by the author and collaborators (2012-2013). It contrasts with earlier minimal models such as Meissner et al. (1987) and Igarashi et al. (1985) that used only O(p²) terms. The results enable systematic study of dense baryonic matter via skyrmion crystals, as reported in a companion paper (2014), and highlight the need for one-loop corrections as shown in the pion-only work of Meissner and Weigel (1996).
