# Dynamical symmetry in spinor Bose-Einstein condensates

> We demonstrate that dynamical symmetry plays a crucial role in determining the structure of the eigenspectra of spinor Bose-Einstein condensates (BECs). In particular, the eigenspectra of spin- 1 and spin-2 BECs in the single-mode approximation are shown to be completely determined by dynamical symmetries, where a spin-2 BEC corresponds to the U(5) limit of the interacting boson model in nuclear physics. The eigenspectrum of a spin-3 BEC is solved analytically for a specific class of coupling constants, while it is shown that dynamical symmetry alone is not enough to determine the spectrum for arbitrary coupling constants. We also study the low-lying eigenspectra of spin-1 and spin-2 BECs in the absence of external magnetic field, and find, in particular, that the quasidegenerate spectra emerge for antiferromagnetic and cyclic phases. This implies that these systems are highly susceptible to external perturbations and may undergo symmetry-breaking transitions to other states upon increasing the size of system.

# Dynamical symmetry in spinor Bose-Einstein condensates

## Key claim

The eigenspectra of spin-1 and spin-2 Bose-Einstein condensates (BECs) in the single-mode approximation are completely determined by dynamical symmetries, with a spin-2 BEC corresponding to the U(5) limit of the interacting boson model from nuclear physics; however, for spin-3 BECs, dynamical symmetry alone is not sufficient to determine the spectrum for arbitrary coupling constants, and analytic solutions exist only for a specific class of coupling constants where the coefficient e₃ vanishes.

## Method

The authors express the spin-dependent interaction Hamiltonian of a spin-f BEC in terms of spherical tensor operators and Racah forms of the unitary group U(2f+1). They rewrite the Hamiltonian using Casimir operators of subgroup chains (U(2f+1) ⊃ SO(2f+1) ⊃ ... ⊃ SO(3) ⊃ SO(2)) and identify conditions under which the Hamiltonian becomes a linear combination of these Casimir operators, enabling algebraic solution. For spin-3 BECs, they show that an identity relating the scalar products of spherical tensor operators reduces the number of independent quadratic Casimir operators from four to three, preventing a general dynamical symmetry solution.

## Context

This work extends the application of dynamical symmetry methods, previously used in nuclear physics (Arima and Iachello's interacting boson model) and molecular physics, to spinor BECs. The authors build on prior mean-field studies of spin-1 BECs (Ho 1998, Ohmi and Machida 1998), spin-2 BECs (Koashi and Ueda 2000, Ciobanu et al. 2000, Ueda and Koashi 2002), and spin-3 BECs (Diener and Ho 2006, Santos and Pfau 2006, Makela and Suominen 2007). The exact eigenspectra for spin-1 and spin-2 BECs had been obtained previously (Koashi and Ueda 2000, Law et al. 1998, Pu et al. 1999, Ho and Yip 2000), but this paper provides a unified group-theoretic framework and reveals the connection to nuclear physics models. The finding that quasi-degenerate spectra emerge for antiferromagnetic and cyclic phases implies these systems are highly susceptible to external perturbations and may undergo symmetry-breaking transitions as system size increases.
