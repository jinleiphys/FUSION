# The 1-loop correction of the QCD energy momentum tensor with the overlap fermion and HYP smeared Iwasaki gluon

> We present the 1-loop renormalization of the energy momentum tensor using the overlap fermion and a HYP-smeared Iwasaki gauge action. We also calculate the 1-loop matching coefficient that convert the lattice simulation results renormalized in the RI/MOM scheme to the $\overline{\textrm{MS}}$ scheme. The dependence of the renormalization on the gauge action and the number of HYP smearing steps are also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper presents the 1-loop renormalization and matching coefficients for the QCD energy momentum tensor (EMT) using overlap fermions and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gauge action, but the results for the gluon EMT operator vertex correction involving the unknown $G^{(2)}_{\mu\nu}$ term rely on an assumption that the ratio of contributions from different color structures is unchanged by the $O(a^2)$ improvement and HYP smearing, with 100% uncertainty assigned to those estimated contributions.

## Method

The authors computed 1-loop Feynman diagrams for the quark and gluon self-energies and the EMT vertex corrections using lattice perturbation theory with numerical integration in Mathematica, employing the ClenshawCurtisOscillatoryRule integrator. They performed fits of the numerical loop integrals to functional forms including logarithmic and polynomial terms in $a^2p^2$ to extract the finite pieces $B_{ij}$, using 16 external momentum values with $a^2p^2 < 2\times10^{-4}$ and fitting with up to three polynomial terms to estimate systematic uncertainties. The calculation covered Wilson fermions, overlap fermions, and the chiral fermion $D_c$, combined with Wilson gluon, Iwasaki gluon, and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gluon actions, with the gluon EMT operator defined via the clover discretization.

## Key numbers

- For the quark self-energy finite piece $B_Q$ with overlap fermion and Iwasaki gluon: -13.58
- For the quark self-energy finite piece $B_Q$ with overlap fermion and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gluon: -7.56
- For the gluon self-energy finite piece $B_G$ with Wilson fermion and Wilson gluon: -19.49 (split into $B^t_G = -8.53$ and $\frac{2N_c^2-3}{24N^2_c}B^s_G = -10.97$)
- For the gluon self-energy finite piece $B_G$ with Wilson fermion and Iwasaki gluon: 6.56 (split into $B^t_G = -0.05$ and $\frac{2N_c^2-3}{24N^2_c}B^s_G = 6.61$)
- For the quark EMT operator finite piece $B_{QQ}$ with overlap fermion and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gluon (off-diagonal): -3.16
- For the mixing coefficient $B_{GQ}$ with overlap fermion and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gluon (off-diagonal): -0.72
- For the mixing coefficient $B_{QG}$ with overlap fermion and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gluon (off-diagonal): -2.42
- For the gluon EMT operator finite piece $B_{GG}$ with overlap fermion and HYP-smeared Iwasaki gluon (off-diagonal): -1.64

## Context

This work extends the 1-loop renormalization of the QCD EMT beyond the earlier calculations using Wilson fermions and Wilson gluons by Capitani (1994) and the more recent work by Glatzmaier (2014) for Wilson fermions with overlap-defined gluon operators. The results enable lattice QCD simulations using overlap fermions and improved gauge actions to extract the quark and gluon momentum fractions $\langle x \rangle_{Q,G}$ and angular momentum contributions $J_{Q,G}$ in the nucleon with proper renormalization, which is timely given that recent simulations by Horsley (2012), Deka (2013), and Yang (2016) have achieved gluonic matrix element uncertainties at the 20% level or better.
