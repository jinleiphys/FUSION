# Transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow in ideal hydrodynamics and geometric scaling

> In an ideal hydrodynamic model, with an equation of state where the confinement-deconfinement transition is a cross-over at $T_{co}=196 MeV$, we have simulated $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions. Simultaneous description of the experimental charged particle's $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow require that in central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions, initial energy density scales with the binary collision number density. In less central collisions, experimental data demand scaling with the participant density. Simulation studies also indicate that in central collisions viscous effects are minimal.

## Key claim

Simultaneous description of charged particle transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow in central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV requires initial energy density to scale with binary collision number density (hard scattering fraction x=1), while in less central collisions the data demand scaling with participant density (x=0); this centrality-dependent geometric scaling is not captured by a single fixed hard scattering fraction.

## Method

The authors used the ideal hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA with longitudinal boost-invariance to simulate √s=200 GeV Au+Au collisions, assuming a baryonless QGP fluid thermalized at τ_i=0.6 fm. The equation of state was a lattice+hadron resonance gas (HRG) EOS with a confinement-deconfinement cross-over at T_co=196 MeV. Initial energy density in the transverse plane was parameterized via a Glauber model with two extreme limits: scaling with binary collision number density (x=1) or with participant density (x=0); central energy density ε_0 was fixed by fitting PHENIX charged particle p_T spectra in 0-10% centrality, and kinetic freeze-out was set at T_F=150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density for participant scaling (x=0): ε_0 = 36.1 GeV/fm³, fitted to PHENIX 0-10% Au+Au p_T spectra.
- Central energy density for binary collision scaling (x=1): ε_0 = 48 GeV/fm³, ~30% higher than the x=0 case.
- Confinement-deconfinement cross-over temperature: T_co = 196 MeV.
- Thermalisation time: τ_i = 0.6 fm.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_F = 150 MeV.
- In 0-10% Au+Au collisions, Glauber initialisation with x=1 explains elliptic flow; with x=0, elliptic flow at p_T~1.5 GeV is underpredicted by ~35%.
- In less central collisions with x=1, elliptic flow at p_T~1.5 GeV is overpredicted by ~20% (10-20%), 25% (20-30%), 35% (30-40%), 45% (40-50%), and 60% (50-60%).
- With x=0 in peripheral collisions, elliptic flow at p_T~1.5 GeV is overpredicted by ~15% (30-40%), 25% (40-50%), and 50% (50-60%).

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic studies using Glauber model initialisation with fixed hard scattering fractions (e.g., x=0.25 in Kolb et al. (2001) and x=0.13 in Hirano (2009)), which underpredicted elliptic flow in very central Au+Au collisions. The finding that central collisions require binary collision scaling while peripheral collisions require participant scaling suggests a centrality-dependent geometric scaling of initial energy density, with implications for the pre-equilibrium stage and the role of viscous effects. The authors note that in central collisions viscous effects appear minimal, while peripheral collisions may demand viscosity, consistent with centrality-dependent viscosity-to-entropy ratio analyses.
