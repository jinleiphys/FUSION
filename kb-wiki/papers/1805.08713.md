# Axial anomaly and hadronic properties in a nuclear medium

> We investigate meson and nucleon dynamics at finite baryon density and temperature by coupling the nucleon field and the omega meson to the three-flavor linear sigma model and calculate hadronic properties around the nuclear liquid-gas transition. We apply the functional renormalization group method, and find that mesonic fluctuations increase the strength of the coefficient of the $U_A(1)$ breaking determinant operator as a function of the chiral condensate. As a consequence, we find that the actual value of the anomaly increases discontinuously at the first order nuclear liquid-gas transition. We calculate how mesonic masses and partial restoration of chiral symmetry are modified due to such an effect.

## Key claim

The paper claims that mesonic fluctuations, treated via the functional renormalization group, increase the strength of the $U_A(1)$-breaking determinant operator coefficient as a function of the chiral condensate, and that the actual value of the anomaly increases discontinuously at the first-order nuclear liquid-gas transition. This result is conditional on the inclusion of the omega meson to model repulsive nucleon-nucleon interactions, which was absent in the authors' prior work (Fejos 2017) and is necessary to properly describe the liquid-gas transition.

## Method

The authors couple the nucleon field and the omega meson to a three-flavor linear sigma model and apply the functional renormalization group (FRG) method to compute the scale-dependent effective potential. They solve flow equations for the chiral potential and the $U_A(1)$ anomaly coefficient on a grid in field space, using a Litim-type regulator, while neglecting wave-function renormalizations and the flow of the Yukawa coupling and omega coupling. The two-flavor nucleon sector is treated via a separate effective potential that includes nonrenormalizable interactions tuned to reproduce nuclear matter properties, and the omega meson is treated as a background field whose expectation value is determined by an equation of state.

## Key numbers

* Normal nuclear density: $n_N \approx 0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3} \approx (109.131\ \mathrm{MeV})^3$, corresponding to Fermi momentum $p_F \approx 267.9\ \mathrm{MeV} \approx 1.36\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$.
* Nucleon mass in vacuum: $m_N(f_\pi) \approx 939\ \mathrm{MeV}$, giving Yukawa coupling $g_Y \approx 20.19$.
* Landau mass in the liquid phase: $M_L \approx 0.8 m_N(f_\pi) \approx 751.2\ \mathrm{MeV}$, with corresponding nonstrange condensate $v_{\mathrm{ns,liq}} \approx 69.52\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Critical chemical potential at $T=0$: $\mu_{B,c} \approx 922.7\ \mathrm{MeV}$, with omega condensate $\omega_c \approx -171.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and binding energy $B \approx 16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Compression modulus of nuclear matter: $K \approx 287\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Critical end point temperature: $T_{\mathrm{cep}} = 18\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Pion mass: $m_\pi = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}$, kaon mass: $m_K = 494\ \mathrm{MeV}$, decay constants $f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $f_K = 113\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
* Ultraviolet cutoff: $\Lambda = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier FRG study of the three-flavor linear sigma model with nucleons (Fejos 2016, 2017) by including the omega vector meson, which is indispensable for describing the nuclear liquid-gas transition and the associated first-order phase boundary. The results contradict mean-field predictions from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Costa 2005, Nagahiro 2006) and linear sigma model (Sakai 2013, 2016) regarding the magnitude of the eta-prime mass drop in medium, suggesting that the anomaly strengthens rather than weakens across the transition. The work enables future studies of in-medium meson properties, particularly the eta-prime-nucleon interaction relevant to experiments at LEPS2 (SPring-8), BGO-OD (ELSA), and the JAEA Heavy Ion project.
