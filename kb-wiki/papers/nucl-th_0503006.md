# An alternative understanding of mass formulas in terms of nuclear structure

> A typical form of mass formula is re-explained in terms of nuclear structure. For $ N \approx Z $ nuclei, we propose to start with the shell model picture and to consider the T=0 $2n-2p$ ($α$-like) correlations as the fundamental concept, instead of the symmetry energy. Subsequently, the symmetry energy is described on the basis of the $α$-like superfluidity caused by the T=0 $2n-2p$ correlations, in parallel with the pairing energy described on the basis of the pairing superfluidity. This re-explanation gives useful insight for understanding the nuclear mass formula. The origin of the Wigner energy is also explained in an interacting boson model for the Cooper pairs in the $α$-like superfluid vacuum. Adding a correction term due to the T=0 $2n-2p$ correlations, which determines the T=0 base level for nuclear masses, can improve the mass formulas in practice.

# An Alternative Understanding of Mass Formulas in Terms of Nuclear Structure

## Key claim

The authors propose that the symmetry energy in nuclear mass formulas should not be treated as a fundamental concept, but instead should be derived from T=0 2n-2p (alpha-like) correlations, in parallel with how pairing energy is derived from T=1 pair correlations. This re-explanation applies specifically to N ≈ Z nuclei and is presented as a conceptual understanding rather than a new mass formula that outperforms existing sophisticated ones.

## Method

The authors extract the energy of valence nucleons by subtracting a volume-surface-Coulomb term B_VSC(A) plus an A^{-3/4} adjustment from experimental binding energies for A_0+mα nuclei (doubly-closed-shell core plus m alpha-like quartets). They then compare these extracted energies with shell model calculations using the Honma interaction in the pf shell, decomposing the effective interaction into a T=0 monopole field and a residual part. The T=0 2n-2p correlations are analyzed through an interacting boson model where the alpha-like quartet is treated as a boson with an attractive interaction between quartets.

## Key numbers

- The adjustment term for the shell model potential depth is δU_pot(A) = -46.4 / A^{3/4} MeV
- For the pf shell nuclei, the shell model parameters used are p=7.279, q=0.15, r=-0.065 and λ=-12.45 MeV; for the sd shell nuclei, p=3.54, q=0.20, r=0.0 and λ=-11.2 MeV
- The monopole field strength k^0 = 1.44 MeV for ^56Ni
- In ^56Ni, the residual interaction energy ⟨H_res⟩ is approximately 20 MeV, which overwhelms the single-particle energy gap between f_{7/2} and p_{3/2}
- For the interacting alpha-boson model in the sd shell: e_α=2.75, g_{αα}=5.3, C_α=0.21 in MeV (for ^20Ne to ^28Si)
- For the interacting alpha-boson model in the pf shell: e_α=1.35, g_{αα}=3.75, C_α=0.18 in MeV (for ^44Ti to ^56Ni)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of mass formulas beyond the traditional Weizsacker-Bethe approach by connecting the symmetry energy to shell model correlations, treating it symmetrically with pairing energy. It builds on the detailed mass formula work of Duflo (2000) and the microscopic guidelines of Ref. 5, while also referencing the study of alpha-like superfluidity by Gambhir, Ring and Schuck (1983). The paper enables a unified description where the symmetry energy and pairing energy are both derived from nuclear correlations rather than treated as separate phenomenological terms, and suggests that the T=0 base level determined by alpha-like correlations should be explicitly included in mass formulas for N ≈ Z nuclei.
