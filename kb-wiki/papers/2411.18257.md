# Production of exotic hadrons in $pp$ and nuclear collisions

> Exotic hadrons beyond the conventional quark model have been discovered in the past two decades. Investigations of these states can lead to deep understanding of nonperturbative dynamics of the strong interaction. In this concise review, we focus on the productions of exotic hadrons in $pp$, $p\bar p$, and nuclear collisions. Experimental observations of light nuclei and hypernuclei, as prototypes of hadronic molecules, in heavy ion collisions will also be briefly discussed.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that the production of exotic hadrons in hadron-hadron and nuclear collisions provides crucial insights into nonperturbative QCD dynamics, but the interpretation of these states remains ambiguous because the internal structure of candidates like the X(3872) is still debated between hadronic molecule and compact tetraquark models, with no universal description yet achieved.

## Method

The authors survey experimental results from pp, pbar p, and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, focusing on the production mechanisms of exotic hadrons including hidden-charm XYZ states, pentaquarks, double-charm tetraquarks, and light (anti)hypernuclei. They compare production yields, nuclear modification factors, and decay branching fractions across different collision systems, and discuss how these measurements constrain theoretical models such as the coalescence model, statistical thermal model, and hadronic molecule picture.

- X(3872) branching fraction to pi+ pi- J/psi: (4.1 ± 1.3)% from BaBar, and (4.1^{+1.9}_{-1.1})% from global analysis; dominant decay to D*0 Dbar0 + c.c. at (52.4^{+25.3}_{-14.3})%
- Ratio R_{psi gamma} = Gamma(X(3872)->psi(2S)gamma)/Gamma(X(3872)->J/psi gamma) = 1.67 ± 0.21 ± 0.12 ± 0.04 from LHCb, versus BESIII upper limit R_{psi gamma} < 0.59 at 90% confidence level
- Prompt X(3872) to psi(2S) yield ratio in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: rho_Pb-Pb = 1.08^{+0.49}_{-0.52}, compared to typical value of 0.1 in pp collisions
- Nuclear modification factors for psi(nS) in Pb-Pb: R_AA = 0.56 ± 0.08 (stat) ± 0.07 (syst) for J/psi, 0.12 ± 0.04 (stat) ± 0.02 (syst) for psi(2S), and less than 0.10 at 95% CL for psi(3S)

## Context

This review extends the extensive literature on exotic hadron spectroscopy, including the Belle (2003) discovery of X(3872), LHCb (2015, 2019) discoveries of hidden-charm pentaquarks, and LHCb (2021) discovery of the double-charm tetraquark T_cc(3875)+. It connects these findings to heavy-ion collision studies of (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypernuclei from STAR and ALICE, and to measurements of quarkonium suppression patterns from CMS. The work enables future searches for exotic hadrons in nuclear collisions at LHC Run 3 and Belle II, and tests of hadronic molecule versus compact tetraquark interpretations through production yields and nuclear modification factors.
