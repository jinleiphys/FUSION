# Probing the nuclear equation of state by $K^+$ production in heavy ion collisions

> The dependence of $K^+$ production on the nuclear equation of state is investigated in heavy ion collisions. An increase of the excitation function of $K^+$ multiplicities obtained in heavy ($Au+Au$) over light ($C+C$) systems when going far below threshold which has been observed by the KaoS Collaboration strongly favours a soft equation of state. This observation holds despite of the influence of an in-medium kaon potential predicted by effective chiral models which is necessary to reproduce the experimental $K^+$ yields.

## Key claim

The ratio of $K^+$ multiplicities in heavy ($Au+Au$) over light ($C+C$) systems, measured far below the free production threshold, strongly favors a soft nuclear equation of state (EOS) with a compression modulus of K=200 MeV over a hard EOS with K=380 MeV, even when an in-medium kaon potential is included.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport model with soft and hard Skyrme forces (compression moduli K=200 MeV and 380 MeV) and momentum dependence adjusted to the empirical optical nucleon-nucleus potential. They calculated $K^+$ production via baryon-baryon collisions ($BB \rightarrow BYK^+$) and pion-induced processes ($\pi B \rightarrow YK^+$), including an in-medium kaon potential from effective chiral models, and compared the excitation function of the ratio of $K^+$ multiplicities in $Au+Au$ over $C+C$ reactions to data from the KaoS Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Compression modulus of the soft EOS: K=200 MeV; hard EOS: K=380 MeV.
- Saturation point of nuclear matter: binding energy $E_B = -16$ MeV at density $\rho_{\rm sat}= 0.17~{\rm fm}^{-3}$.
- Free $K^+$ production threshold energy: $E_{thr}= 1.58$ GeV.
- In $C+C$ reactions at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV, the in-medium kaon potential reduces the $K^+$ yield by about $50\%$.
- In central $Au+Au$ at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV, the average density at kaon production increases from 1.47 $\rho_{\rm sat}$ (hard EOS) to 1.57 $\rho_{\rm sat}$ (soft EOS).
- The average shift of the in-medium kaon mass relative to vacuum is 55 MeV (hard EOS) and 61 MeV (soft EOS), a relative shift of 6 MeV.
- The available energy $<\sqrt{s}>$ in central $Au+Au$ at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV is enhanced by 90 MeV when using the soft EOS compared to the hard EOS.
- In $C+C$ at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV, the available energy $<\sqrt{s}>$ is reduced by 45 MeV with the soft EOS.
- Mean densities at kaon creation in central collisions at 0.8 A$\cdot$GeV: $<\rho /\rho_{\rm sat} >$ = 1.46/1.40 ($C+C$, hard/soft) and 1.47/1.57 ($Au+Au$, hard/soft).
- In $Au+Au$, kaons are created at densities up to three times saturation density.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model studies by Aichelin and Ko (1985) and Li et al. (1995) that first noted the sensitivity of $K^+$ yields to the nuclear EOS, and by Li et al. (1997) and Cassing et al. (1999) who included pionic channels and momentum-dependent forces. The paper uses new precise data from the KaoS Collaboration (Sturm et al., 2000) on $K^+$ production far below threshold in $Au+Au$ and $C+C$ systems, and employs improved elementary cross sections from Sibirtsev et al. (1995) and Tsushima et al. (1999). The ratio method cancels theoretical uncertainties and enables a clear conclusion favoring a soft EOS, which is relevant for understanding the properties of dense nuclear matter in neutron stars and supernovae.
