# Fission modes in fermium isotopes with Brownian shape-motion model

> Fission-fragment mass and total-kinetic-energy distributions following fission of the fermium isotopes $^{256,258,260}\text{Fm}$ at low excitation energy have been calculated using the Brownian shape-motion model. A transition from asymmetric fission in $^{256}\text{Fm}$ to symmetric fission in $^{258}\text{Fm}$ is obtained. The total-kinetic-energy distributions for the three isotopes show radically different behaviour due to varying contributions from different fission modes, with a double-humped distribution for $^{258}\text{Fm}$.

## Key claim

The Brownian shape-motion model predicts a transition from asymmetric fission in 256Fm to symmetric fission in 258Fm, with a double-humped total-kinetic-energy distribution for 258Fm, but the high-TKE component from the super-short fission mode is slightly less pronounced in the calculations than in experimental spontaneous-fission data because the calculations start from excited states above the outer barrier rather than from the ground state.

## Method

Fission-fragment mass and total-kinetic-energy distributions were calculated using the Brownian shape-motion model on five-dimensional potential-energy surfaces from the macroscopic-microscopic model (finite-range liquid-drop model plus folded-Yukawa single-particle model with Strutinsky shell corrections). Dynamical simulations started from the isomer minimum at low compound excitation energies (1.71–3.88 MeV) with 10000 walks per nucleus, using a bias potential of 60 MeV and a critical neck radius of 1.5 fm for scission. The total kinetic energy was determined from the sum of compound excitation energy and Q-value, shared between kinetic and excitation energy of the fragments.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies for the dynamical simulations: 3.88 MeV (256Fm), 2.77 MeV (258Fm), and 1.71 MeV (260Fm)
- Critical neck radius for scission: 1.5 fm
- Maximum number of steps per walk: 2 × 10^6 steps; walks exceeding this or reaching maximum elongation are discarded
- Total walks per nucleus: 10000, with less than 1% discarded
- Average TKE for 256Fm: close to the measured value of 197.9 MeV from spontaneous fission (Hoffman 1980)
- TKE distribution for 258Fm shows peaks near 200 and 235 MeV, consistent with measurements of spontaneous fission (Hulet 1986)
- TKE distribution for 260Fm is peaked around 235 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Brownian shape-motion model (Randrup 2011, Randrup 2013) to neutron-rich fermium isotopes, building on earlier macroscopic-microscopic calculations (Moller 1989, Moller 1994) and experimental observations of bimodal fission in the fermium region (Hulet 1986). The results enable systematic studies of fission modes in superheavy nuclei (Albertsson 2019) and demonstrate how shell effects from the doubly magic 132Sn produce compact scission configurations with high TKE, while liquid-drop-like elongated scission produces lower TKE.
