# Perturbation Scheme for the Effective Nuclear Force

> Based on a systematic Hartree-Fock+BCS calculation, new perturbation scheme is proposed to modify the existing Skyrme-type effective nuclear force. Much attention is paid to have a better description of fission property of heavy nuclei. In terms of incorporating the nuclear medium effects on the fission barrier height more correctly, two typical Skyrme parameter sets (SkM* and SLy4) are modified. In conclusion, according to the comparison to experiments, the calculated fission barrier heights are improved by almost 90$\%$.

# Perturbation Scheme for the Effective Nuclear Force

## Key claim
The authors propose a perturbation scheme that modifies the density-dependent term of Skyrme-type effective nuclear forces (specifically the $t_3 \rho^{2+\alpha}$ term) to improve fission barrier height calculations. The claimed 90% improvement in fission barrier heights is an average over many isotopes and is conditional on the perturbation satisfying a 5% tolerance condition on the expectation value of the modified interaction relative to the original.

## Method
Constrained Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations were performed using the SkyAX code on a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate grid with axial symmetry, imposing a quadrupole deformation constraint. The perturbation scheme first adds a small change $\delta \alpha$ to the density-dependence exponent $\alpha$ to fit experimental fission barrier heights, then adjusts the $t_3$ parameter to preserve the original interaction's quality in reproducing static nuclear properties (binding energies and radii). The modified interactions mSkM* and mSLy4 were obtained by applying this scheme to the existing SkM* and SLy4 parameter sets, using the experimental inner fission barrier height of $^{236}$U (5.00 MeV) as the primary reference.

## Key numbers
- Experimental inner fission barrier height of $^{236}$U: 5.00 MeV (from RIPL-2 database)
- Difference between calculated and experimental fission barrier height for $^{236}$U inner barrier: 2.36 MeV for SLy4, 2.53 MeV for SkM*
- Modified parameters: $\alpha$ changed from 0.166667 to 0.195967 for mSLy4, and from 0.166667 to 0.179000 for mSkM*; $t_3$ changed from 13777.000 to 14645.710 MeV$\cdot$fm$^{3(1+\alpha)}$ for mSLy4, and from 15595.000 to 16004.450 MeV$\cdot$fm$^{3(1+\alpha)}$ for mSkM*
- Nuclear matter properties for mSkM*: $E/A = -15.39$ MeV, $K = 234$ MeV, $S = 30$ MeV, $L = 44$ MeV
- Nuclear matter properties for mSLy4: $E/A = -15.21$ MeV, $K = 269$ MeV, $S = 31$ MeV, $L = 32$ MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon for $^{208}$Pb: 7.86 MeV (mSLy4), 7.80 MeV (mSkM*); experimental value 7.87 MeV
- Proton radius of $^{208}$Pb: 5.70 fm (mSLy4), 5.58 fm (mSkM*); experimental value 5.50 fm
- Perturbation condition: $\left| 1 - \frac{\langle \psi | t_3' \rho^{2+\alpha+\delta\alpha} | \psi \rangle}{\langle \psi | t_3 \rho^{2+\alpha} | \psi \rangle} \right| < 0.05$

## Context
This work extends the long line of Skyrme effective interaction development, specifically modifying the SkM* parameter set (Bartel 1982) and the SLy4 parameter set (Chabanat 1998). The authors address the known problem that standard Skyrme forces systematically overestimate fission barrier heights, which limits predictive power for nuclear fission processes. The proposed perturbation scheme enables systematic improvement of fission properties while preserving the quality of static nuclear structure calculations (binding energies and radii of $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb). The modified interactions are intended for future applications in heavy nuclear physics, though the authors note that the saturation density is somewhat underestimated and requires further work.
