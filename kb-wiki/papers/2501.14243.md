# Longitudinal flow decorrelations in light ion collisions

> This study presents a detailed analysis of longitudinal flow decorrelations in light ion collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider(LHC) energies for the first time. We compare different theoretical models of the oxygen structure and find that certain observables, such as the flow vector correlator, can distinguish between them, particularly highlighting differences between the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) structure and other models, such as Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and the Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM), which behave similarly. We further examine flow decorrelations across different systems, including d+Au and O+O collisions at 200 GeV, as well as Ne+Ne and O+O collisions at 6.37 TeV, indicating a hierarchy in decorrelations that underscores the complexity of these interactions. Our findings emphasize that the role of asymmetry in d+Au collisions and its impact on flow correlations is mitigated using a modified flow correlation defined as a ratio of two flow vector covariances derived from different pairs of pseudorapidity bins. Additionally, our analysis of flow angle decorrelations shows diverse distributions across various systems, finding distinct patterns in d+Au collisions compared to other collision systems.

## Key claim
This study presents the first analysis of longitudinal flow decorrelations in light ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, finding that the flow vector correlator can distinguish between different theoretical models of oxygen structure, particularly separating the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) structure from Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and the Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM), which behave similarly.

## Method
The authors used a combination of the 3D-Glauber Monte-Carlo model, (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC), and UrQMD to generate 10000 fluctuating events for each collision system: d+Au and O+O at 200 GeV, and O+O and Ne+Ne at 6.37 TeV. They computed two-particle correlations as a function of pseudorapidity to obtain flow vector moments and decorrelation coefficients, including the modified correlators r_{2;V} and r_{2;psi} defined as ratios of flow vector covariances from different pairs of pseudorapidity bins. The analysis was performed in the 0-5% centrality range with transverse momentum 0.2 to 3 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For O+O at 200 GeV, the deviation of v_2{2}(eta) obtained by VMC configurations differs up to 10% from NLEFT and PGCM configurations
- The deviation from spherical structure (3pF) is approximately 3% for NLEFT (and PGCM) and approximately 9% for VMC in O+O collisions at 200 GeV
- For the ratio R(-eta,eta)_{configs}/R(-eta,eta)_{3pF}, VMC shows 1% difference for Delta-eta less than 4, and 5% with PGCM and 7% with NLEFT at Delta-eta=8
- The ratio v_2{2}_{Ne+Ne}/v_2{2}_{O+O} is approximately constant in the range -2.5 less than eta less than 2.5 with a difference around 5%
- For r_{2;V}^{Ne+Ne}/r_{2;V}^{O+O} at 6.37 TeV, the difference for NLEFT structures is obtained up to 1.5%

## Context
This work extends previous studies of light nuclear structures in high-energy collisions, particularly following investigations of oxygen and neon structures using NLEFT, PGCM, and VMC models by Zhang (2024) and Giacalone (2024). The authors build on prior analyses of flow decorrelations in heavy ion collisions by Bozek (2010, 2015) and the CMS Collaboration (2015), and apply these techniques to small systems for the first time. The results enable future experimental comparisons at RHIC and LHC to discriminate between nuclear structure models using longitudinal flow observables.
