# The chromoelectric adjoint correlators in Euclidean space at next-to-leading order

> The physics of quarkonium created in heavy-ion collisions is intrinsically connected to the correlation functions of adjoint chromoelectric fields in quantum chromodynamics. We study such correlation functions in a weak-coupling expansion in a thermal medium. We identify three distinct gauge-invariant correlators, and evaluate them to next-to-leading order. Two of the resulting correlators turn out to be asymmetric. We pinpoint the source of this asymmetry to Matsubara zero modes associated with Wilson lines. The results are shown to agree well with recent lattice calculations at high temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first next-to-leading order (NLO) evaluation of three distinct gauge-invariant chromoelectric adjoint correlators in Euclidean thermal QCD, showing that two of them are asymmetric on the thermal circle due to Matsubara zero modes associated with Wilson lines, and that these perturbative results agree well with recent lattice calculations at high temperatures.

## Method

The authors define three gauge-invariant adjoint chromoelectric correlators (upper, lower, and symmetric) and evaluate them using weak-coupling perturbation theory in the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism of thermal QCD. They compute all nine distinct diagram topologies contributing at NLO, perform colour and Lorentz contractions to scalarise the diagrams, and use integration-by-parts methodology to factorise the two-loop sum-integrals into products of one-loop integrals with known closed-form analytic expressions involving polylogarithms.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order result for the upper/lower correlator: $\langle EE\rangle_{\mathrm{LO}} = d_A g_s^2 \pi^2 [\cos(2\pi T t) + 2] \csc^4(\pi T t) + O(\varepsilon)$, where $d_A = N_c^2 - 1$ is the dimension of the adjoint representation
- The generic one-loop sum-integral evaluated analytically: $\mathcal{E}^{a}_{m}(t) = \frac{T^{4-2m+a}}{(2\pi)^{2m-a}} \frac{\Gamma(m-d/2)}{\Gamma(m)} \pi^{3/2} (e^{\gamma_E}\bar{\Lambda}^2/(4\pi^2 T^2))^{(3-d)/2} \times [\mathrm{Li}_{2m-a-d}(e^{2\pi i T t}) + (-1)^a \mathrm{Li}_{2m-a-d}(e^{-2\pi i T t})]$
- The integration-by-parts identity used: $\int_{\mathbf{pq}} \frac{1}{(p_0^2+p^2)(q_0^2+q^2)[(p_0+q_0)^2+(\mathbf{p}+\mathbf{q})^2]} = -\frac{d-2}{2(d-3)} [\frac{I_1(p_0)I_1(p_0+q_0)}{p_0(p_0+q_0)} + \frac{I_1(q_0)I_1(p_0+q_0)}{q_0(p_0+q_0)} - \frac{I_1(p_0)I_1(q_0)}{p_0 q_0}]$

## Context

This work extends the perturbative study of chromoelectric correlators relevant to quarkonium transport in the quark-gluon plasma, which were first connected to transport coefficients in pNRQCD by Brambilla (2016). It provides a Euclidean-space counterpart to real-time calculations by Binder (2021) and Eller (2019), and offers a perturbative explanation for the asymmetry observed in the first lattice calculation of these correlators by Brambilla (2025). The results enable direct comparison with lattice QCD at high temperatures and provide analytic expressions for the transport coefficients governing quarkonium dissociation and recombination in the open quantum system framework.
