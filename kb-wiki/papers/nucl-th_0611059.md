# Wobbling excitations in 156Dy and 162Yb

> We study in the cranked Nilsson plus random phase approximation low-lying quadrupole excitations of positive parity and negative signature in 156Dy and 162Yb at high spins. Special attention is paid to a consistent description of wobbling excitations and their identification among excited states. A good agreement between available experimental data and the results of calculations is obtained. We found that in 156Dy the lowest odd spin gamma-vibrational states transform to the wobbling excitations after the backbending, associated with the transition from axially-symmetric to nonaxial shapes. Similar results are predicted for 162Yb. The analysis of electromagnetic transitions, related to the wobbling excitations, determines uniquely the sign of the gamma-deformation in 156Dy and 162Yb after the transition point.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in 156Dy and 162Yb, the lowest odd-spin gamma-vibrational states transform into wobbling excitations after the backbending, but only when the transition from axially-symmetric to nonaxial shapes occurs; in 156Dy this is a first-order shape-phase transition, while in 162Yb it is a second-order transition driven by neutron alignment, and the identification of wobbling modes requires a self-consistent microscopic description of all three moments of inertia.

## Method

The authors used the cranked Nilsson plus random phase approximation (CRPA) with a separable residual interaction, including monopole pairing and doubly-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, to describe low-lying negative-signature quadrupole excitations in 156Dy and 162Yb at high spins. They solved nonlinear Hartree-Bogoliubov equations on a mesh of deformation parameters β and γ at each rotational frequency, enforcing self-consistency between the mean field and the RPA via the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia test. The wobbling excitations were identified by comparing the CRPA results with the asymmetric rotor model (ARM) and by analyzing the Janssen-Mikhailov equation and Marshalek moments of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Critical rotational frequency for backbending in 162Yb: ħΩc ≈ 0.25 MeV (corresponding to angular momentum change from 10ħ to 12ħ)
- Critical rotational frequency for backbending in 156Dy: ħΩc ≈ 0.3 MeV (corresponding to angular momentum change from 14ħ to 16ħ)
- In 156Dy, the difference between HB energy minima for γ = ±20° is about 0.8 MeV
- The maximal difference between minimal HB energies at positive and negative equilibrium γ-values does not exceed about 1 MeV for 156Dy

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CRPA analysis (Kvasil and Nazmitdinov, 2005) of shape-phase transitions and positive-signature excitations at backbending in 156Dy and 162Yb. It contrasts with earlier non-self-consistent microscopic analyses by Matsuzaki et al. (2002, 2003) and Shimizu and Matsuzaki (1995), which used constant mean field parameters and did not properly describe the kinematic moment of inertia. The paper also builds on the theoretical framework of Marshalek (1979) for wobbling excitations in the time-dependent Hartree-Bogoliubov approach and on the asymmetric rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The results enable future identification of wobbling excitations in experimental data through analysis of electromagnetic transitions and inertial properties.
