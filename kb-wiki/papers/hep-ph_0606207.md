# Energy density of the Glasma

> The initial energy density produced in an ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision can, in the color glass condensate framework, be factorized into a product of the integrated gluon distributions of the nuclei. Although this energy density is well defined without any infrared cutoff besides the saturation scale, it is apparently logarithmically ultraviolet divergent. We argue that this divergence is not physically meaningful and does not affect the behavior of the system at any finite proper time.

# Energy density of the Glasma

## Key claim

The initial energy density of the Glasma in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model factorizes into a product of the integrated gluon distributions of the two colliding nuclei, but only strictly at proper time τ=0. Although this energy density is logarithmically ultraviolet divergent at τ=0, this divergence is not physically meaningful because it does not persist when the equations of motion are solved to times larger than the inverse ultraviolet cutoff.

## Method

The author computes the initial energy density of the classical gauge fields in the color glass condensate framework using the MV model, where color charge densities are stochastic Gaussian random variables on the transverse plane. The fields are solved in Schwinger gauge from the classical Yang-Mills equation with sources on the light cones, and the initial condition on the future light cone is expressed in terms of the pure gauge fields of the two colliding nuclei. The energy density is then evaluated analytically in terms of the gauge field correlator G(p_T), and its ultraviolet behavior is studied through both perturbative Bessel function solutions and numerical lattice calculations on transverse lattices ranging from 100^2 to 700^2 points.

## Key numbers

- The initial energy density at τ=0 factorizes as ε = (g^2/2) N_c (N_c^2 - 1) [∫ d^2 p_T/(2π)^2 G(p_T)]^2
- For RHIC energies, the color charge density parameter is estimated as g^2 μ = 2 GeV (considered an upper bound)
- For LHC energies, the estimate based on HERA scaling gives g^2 μ ≈ 3 GeV, with scaling exponent λ = 0.288 and λ̄ ≈ 0.25
- The continuum extrapolated energy density at τ = 1/g^2 μ is ε = 0.26 (g^2 μ)^4 / g^2
- Using g=2, this yields ε(τ = 0.1 fm) ≈ 130 GeV/fm^3 for RHIC and ε(τ = 0.07 fm) ≈ 700 GeV/fm^3 for the LHC

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) and earlier numerical calculations of Glasma fields by Krasnitz, Lappi, and Venugopalan (1998-2004). The paper clarifies the ultraviolet behavior of the initial energy density, which had been a concern for using these fields as initial conditions for hydrodynamic calculations such as those by Hirano (2004). The result enables the use of the Glasma energy density at finite proper times as a well-defined initial condition for hydrodynamics, and shows that introducing a finite initial time (as done by Romatschke and collaborators, 2005-2006) does not add another physical parameter to the model.
