# Universal laws for nuclear contacts

> The nuclear contact characterizes the nucleon-nucleon pairs in close proximity and serves as an important tool for studying the short-range correlations (SRCs) within atomic nuclei. While they have been extracted for selected nuclei, the investigation of their behavior across the nuclear chart remains limited. Very recently, Yankovich, Pazy, and Barnea have proposed a set of universal laws (YPB laws) to describe the correlation between nuclear contacts and nuclear radii and tested their laws for a small number of nuclei by using the Woods-Saxon mean-field model~[R.\ Yankovich, E.\ Pazy, and N.\ Barnea, arXiv:2407.15068 (2021)]. In this Letter, we extend their study to a majority part of the chart of nuclides within the framework of the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov model, which incorporates several essential beyond-mean-field features and offers a more accurate description of the bulk properties of atomic nuclei. Our results suggest that the YPB laws hold as a good approximation for different nuclear mass regions, with minor deviations attributed to, e.g., isospin-breaking effects. Our work lays a firm foundation for future applications of the YPB laws in finite nuclei and provides new evidence for the long-range nature of the relative abundance of short-range pairs.

# Universal laws for nuclear contacts

## Key claim

The YPB laws, which posit that spin-zero nuclear contacts for proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs scale as the square of the nucleon number divided by the cube of the corresponding root-mean-square radius, hold as a good approximation across the nuclear chart for nuclei with mass number 40 < A < 240, with minor deviations attributed primarily to isospin-breaking effects from the Coulomb force.

## Method

The authors combine the generalized contact formalism (GCF) with the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) model to compute point-proton and point-neutron density distributions for nuclei across the chart of nuclides. Spin-zero nuclear contacts are extracted by matching the asymptotic two-body pair-density constructed from one-body densities (with Pauli exclusion corrections) to the short-range form given by the asymptotic two-body wave function at a matching distance r0, using the AV18 potential to solve the zero-energy two-body Schrodinger equation. Five Skyrme parameter sets (SIII, SKM*, SLY4, SKI3, SKX) are used to test model independence.

## Key numbers

* Generalized Levinger constant for nn pairs across all nuclei: L0_nn = 0.01659 fm^3 (global fit)
* Generalized Levinger constant for pp pairs across all nuclei: L0_pp = 0.01626 fm^3 (global fit)
* For Pb isotopes (Z=82, N=96-138), L0_nn ranges from 0.01669 to 0.01678 fm^3 across five Skyrme parameter sets
* For Pb isotopes, L0_pp = 0.01639 fm^3 (fit to all five data sets)
* L0_nn values across the nuclear chart range from 0.0155 to 0.0169 fm^3
* L0_pp values across the nuclear chart range from 0.0151 to 0.0165 fm^3
* For N=82 isotones (Z=48-72), L0_nn = 0.01653 fm^3 and L0_pp ranges from 0.01618 to 0.01633 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the YPB laws proposed by Yankovich, Pazy, and Barnea (2024), who tested them for a small number of nuclei using the Woods-Saxon mean-field model. The present study uses the Skyrme HFB model, which incorporates beyond-mean-field features like pairing correlations, to verify the laws across a much larger portion of the nuclear chart. The results provide a practical method to evaluate nuclear contacts from nuclear radii, which can be extracted from elastic electron scattering experiments for both stable and unstable nuclei, and suggest new connections between neutron skin, short-range correlations, and the equation of state of neutron stars.
