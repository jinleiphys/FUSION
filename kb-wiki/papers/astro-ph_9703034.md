# Dense nuclear matter in a strong magnetic field

> We investigate in a relativistic Hartree theory the gross properties of cold symmetric nuclear matter and nuclear matter in beta equilibrium under the influence of strong magnetic fields. If the field strengths are above the critical values for electrons and protons, the respective phase spaces are strongly modified. This results in additional binding of the systems with distinctively softer equations of state compared to the field free cases. For magnetic field $\sim 10^{20}$ Gauss and beyond, the nuclear matter in beta equilibrium practically converts into a stable proton rich matter.

## Key claim

For magnetic fields at or above the proton critical field (~10^20 G), nuclear matter in beta equilibrium becomes strongly bound and converts into a stable proton-rich matter, with proton fractions reaching up to 0.98; however, this qualitative result is derived within a simple linear sigma-omega-rho relativistic Hartree model, and the authors note that the features may survive in more sophisticated calculations.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic Hartree approach in the linear sigma-omega-rho model to study cold symmetric nuclear matter and n-p-e (neutron-proton-electron) matter in beta equilibrium under uniform magnetic fields. For protons and electrons, the Dirac equation was solved in the presence of a constant magnetic field along the z-axis, yielding Landau-level quantized solutions, while neutrons were treated as plane waves. The effective nucleon mass, scalar and vector densities, and total energy density were computed self-consistently for given baryon density and magnetic field strength, with the equation of state obtained from the energy per baryon.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for electrons: \(B_m^{(e)(c)} = 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G; for protons: \(B_m^{(p)(c)} \sim 10^{20}\) G.
- For symmetric nuclear matter at \(B_m = 10 B_m^{(p)(c)}\), the binding energy per baryon reaches \(E/A \approx -41\) MeV.
- For the n-p-e system at \(B_m = 10 B_m^{(p)(c)}\), the proton fraction saturates to 0.98 at baryon densities \(n_B \geq 2 n_0\) (where \(n_0 = 0.1484\) fm\(^{-3}\)).
- Maximum neutron star masses for different fields: \(M_{\rm max} = 3.10 M_\odot\) (B=0), \(2.99 M_\odot\) (\(10^4 B_m^{(e)(c)}\)), \(2.91 M_\odot\) (\(B_m^{(p)(c)}\)), and \(2.86 M_\odot\) (\(10 B_m^{(p)(c)}\)).
- Corresponding radii at maximum mass: 15.02 km (B=0), 14.95 km (\(10^4 B_m^{(e)(c)}\)), 12.25 km (\(B_m^{(p)(c)}\)), and 12.00 km (\(10 B_m^{(p)(c)}\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of free electron gas in intense magnetic fields relevant to neutron star crusts (Lai 1991; Fushiki et al. 1992) and investigations of neutron matter in strong fields (Shu 1995) by incorporating a relativistic Hartree treatment of nuclear matter with both protons and electrons quantized. The results enable predictions for neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process, which requires a threshold proton fraction (Lattimer et al. 1991), and suggest that sufficiently strong interior fields (~10^20 G) could produce stable proton-rich matter, with implications for neutron star mass-radius relationships and compactness.
