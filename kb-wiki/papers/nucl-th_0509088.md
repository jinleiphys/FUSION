# $Λ(1520)$ and $Σ(1385)$ in the nuclear medium

> Recent studies of the $Λ(1520)$ resonance within chiral unitary theory with coupled channels find the resonance as a dynamically generated state from the interaction of the decuplet of baryons and the octet of mesons, essentially a quasibound state of $πΣ^*(1385)$ in this case, although the coupling of the $Λ(1520)$ to the $\bar{K}N$ and $πΣ$ makes this picture only approximate. The $πΣ^*(1385)$ decay channel of the $Λ(1520)$ is forbidden in free space for the nominal mass of the $Σ^*(1385)$, but the coupling of the $π$ to $ph$ components in the nuclear medium opens new decay channels of the $Λ(1520)$ in the nucleus and produces a much larger width. Together with medium modifications of the $\bar{K}N$ and $πΣ$ decay channels, the final width of the $Λ(1520)$ at nuclear matter density is more than five times bigger than the free one. We perform the calculations by dressing simultaneously the $Λ(1520)$ and the $Σ^*(1385)$ resonances, finding moderate changes in the mass but substantial ones in the width of both resonances.

## Key claim

The $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance, dynamically generated as a quasibound state of $\pi \Sigma^*(1385)$ in chiral unitary theory, acquires a width at nuclear matter density more than five times larger than its free width because the $\pi \Sigma^*(1385)$ decay channel, forbidden in free space for the nominal $\Sigma^*(1385)$ mass, opens in the medium through pion coupling to particle-hole excitations.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium self-energies of both the $\Lambda(1520)$ and $\Sigma^*(1385)$ simultaneously using a coupled-channel approach. The $\Lambda(1520)$ self-energy receives contributions from the $S$-wave $\pi \Sigma^*(1385)$ channel and the $D$-wave $\bar{K}N$ and $\pi \Sigma$ channels, where meson propagators are dressed by coupling to particle-hole and hyperon-hole excitations in nuclear matter via the Lindhard function formalism with Landau-Migdal parameter $g'=0.7$. The $\Sigma^*(1385)$ is similarly dressed in its $P$-wave $\pi \Lambda$ and $\pi \Sigma$ decay channels, with short-range correlations treated by separating longitudinal and transverse parts of the pion effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- The free width of the $\Lambda(1520)$ is 15 MeV.
- At nuclear matter density $\rho_0$, the width of the $\Lambda(1520)$ becomes more than five times larger than the free width.
- The coupling constant $g_{\Lambda^*\Sigma^*\pi}=0.89$ is used for the $\Lambda(1520)\to\pi\Sigma^*(1385)$ vertex, taken from the combined analysis of photoproduction and $\bar{K}N$ scattering data.
- The $D$-wave coupling constants are $g_{\Lambda^*\pi\Sigma}=10.75$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $g_{\Lambda^*\bar{K}N}=16.01$ GeV$^{-2}$, corresponding to dimensionless couplings $\tilde{g}_{\Lambda^*\pi\Sigma}=0.44$ and $\tilde{g}_{\Lambda^*\bar{K}N}=0.54$ at the $\Lambda(1520)$ pole.
- The pion decay constant is $f_\pi=93$ MeV, and the axial coupling constants are $F\simeq0.51$, $D\simeq0.76$, giving $g_A=D+F\simeq1.27$.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter is $g'=0.7$ for both pion and kaon polarization operators.
- The nucleon binding potential is $V_N(\rho)\simeq -60\rho/\rho_0$ MeV, and the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ binding potentials are $V_\Lambda(\rho)=V_\Sigma(\rho)=-30\rho/\rho_0$ MeV.
- The static form factor cutoff is $\Lambda=1$ GeV for both pion and antikaon vertices.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary description of the $\Lambda(1520)$ as a dynamically generated resonance from the interaction of the baryon decuplet and meson octet, as developed by Kolo (2004), Sarkar (2005), and Souravreac (2005). The medium modifications are analogous to those of the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance studied by Oset (1987), where pion renormalization via particle-hole and $\Delta$-hole excitations leads to absorption mechanisms. The predicted large in-medium width of the $\Lambda(1520)$ would provide strong support for its dynamically generated nature and could be tested in heavy-ion reactions as analyzed by Rapp (2004) and other experiments.
