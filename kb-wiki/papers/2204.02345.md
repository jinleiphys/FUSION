# Splitting of elliptic flow in a tilted fireball

> The splitting of elliptic flow measured in different regions of the momentum space of produced hadrons has been recently studied in transport models and proposed as a sensitive probe of the angular momentum carried by the fireball produced in a relativistic heavy ion collision. The initial state angular momentum also gives rise to rapidity odd directed flow which has been measured. We consider a relativistic hydrodynamic framework with the initial matter distribution suitably calibrated to describe the observed directed flow and apply it to study the spilt in the elliptic flow. Our study suggests that the split in the elliptic flow is mostly driven by directed and triangular flows and may be used to constrain models of initial state rapidity distribution of matter in the fireball.

# Splitting of elliptic flow in a tilted fireball

## Key claim

The split in elliptic flow (Δv₂) between momentum-space regions parallel and anti-parallel to the impact parameter is mostly driven by directed flow (v₁) and triangular flow (v₃), and may be used to constrain models of the initial-state rapidity distribution of matter in the fireball, provided the hydrodynamic model first reproduces the measured directed flow data.

## Method

The authors initialize a 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamic evolution (MUSIC code) with two different initial energy-density profiles for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV: a shifted initial condition (SIC) and a tilted initial condition (TIC), both derived from the Optical Glauber model. The TIC includes a free parameter ηm = 2.5 that controls forward-backward asymmetry in energy deposition, calibrated to reproduce STAR directed flow data. After hydrodynamic evolution with η/s = 0.08 and a lattice QCD equation of state, hadrons are produced via Cooper-Frye freezeout at T = 150 MeV followed by resonance decays, and the elliptic flow split Δv₂ = v₂ᴿ − v₂ᴸ is computed from the azimuthal distributions in the two half-planes defined relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV, 5–40% centrality, the TIC with ηm = 2.5 describes the STAR v₁ data, while SIC fails.
- For |η| < 1, Δv₂ ∼ 10⁻⁴ under TIC; the magnitude grows substantially for larger |η|.
- Δv₂ crosses zero at pT ∼ 1 GeV and becomes positive; for pT > 1.5 GeV, v₃ becomes the dominant contributor to Δv₂.
- For |η| < 1, Δv₂ in SIC is 10–20 times larger than in TIC and of opposite sign.
- The ratio |Δv₂/v₂| stays below 0.005 for |η| < 1 under TIC.
- At pT ∼ 1.5 GeV, Δv₂/v₂ ≈ 3% for 0 < η < 1.3 and ≈ 5% for 1.3 < η < 2.5.
- Phase-space integrated Δv₂/v₂ = −0.0019 for 0 < η < 1.3 and −0.0268 for 1.3 < η < 2.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport-model studies of elliptic flow splitting by Chen (2021) and Zhang (2021), which did not describe the directed flow data from STAR (2008). The authors adopt the tilted initial condition (TIC) of Bozek (2010), which successfully reproduces v₁ data, in contrast to the shifted initial condition (SIC) of Hirano (2002). The results show that Δv₂ arises from competition between v₁ and v₃, with v₃ becoming dominant at high pT under TIC, unlike in the AMPT transport model where v₃ was negligible. The study enables future experimental measurements of Δv₂ to discriminate between longitudinal initial-condition models, complementing existing v₁ and polarization observables.
