# J/Psi production in nuclear collisions: measuring the transport coefficient

> The observed pT-dependence of nuclear effects for J/Psi produced in heavy ion collisions at RHIC might look puzzling, since the nuclear suppression seems to fade at large pT. We explain this by the interplay of three mechanisms: (i) attenuation of J/Psi in the hot medium created in the nuclear collision; (ii) initial state shadowing of charmed quarks and attenuation of a c-cbar dipole propagating through the colliding nuclei; (iii) a strong Cronin effect for J/Psi caused by saturation of gluons in the colliding nuclei. All three effects are well under control and calculated in a parameter free way, except for the transport coefficient q_0-hat characterizing the medium. This is adjusted to the J/Psi data and found to be in good agreement with the pQCD prediction, but more than an order of magnitude smaller than what was extracted from jet quenching data within the energy loss scenario.

# J/Psi production in nuclear collisions: measuring the transport coefficient

## Key claim
The observed rise of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for J/Psi with transverse momentum at RHIC is explained by the interplay of final-state attenuation in the hot medium, initial-state shadowing and absorption of the c-cbar dipole, and a strong Cronin effect from gluon saturation. The transport coefficient q_0-hat extracted from J/Psi data (0.3-0.5 GeV^2/fm) agrees with pQCD predictions but is more than an order of magnitude smaller than values extracted from jet quenching data within the energy loss scenario.

## Method
The authors calculate three contributions to J/Psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions: (i) final-state interaction (FSI) of a fully formed J/Psi propagating through the expanding medium, using the dipole cross section related to the transport coefficient; (ii) initial-state interaction (ISI) including higher-twist charm quark shadowing and dipole attenuation in cold nuclear matter, constrained by d-Au data; (iii) the Cronin effect from gluon broadening in nuclei, implemented via a shift in the mean p_T^2 distribution. The transport coefficient q_0-hat is the only free parameter, adjusted to reproduce the p_T-dependent nuclear modification factor R_AA(p_T) for central Cu-Cu and Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Transport coefficient extracted from RHIC J/Psi data: q_0-hat = 0.3-0.5 GeV^2/fm (central Au-Au at b=0, tau=0)
- Transport coefficient extracted from SPS NA60 data: q_0-hat = 0.23 +/- 0.07 GeV^2/fm (central Pb-Pb)
- Mean J/Psi radius: sqrt(<r^2>) = 0.42 fm
- J/Psi formation time in c.m. frame: t*_f < 0.6 fm for p_T up to 5 GeV
- Effective cross section for ISI suppression extracted from d-Au data: sigma_eff = 2.3 mb
- Theoretical expectation for sigma_eff from dipole approach: 2.0-2.5 mb
- Broadening in Cu-Cu collisions: Delta = 0.64 GeV^2
- Broadening in Au-Au collisions: Delta = 0.93 GeV^2
- Mean p_T^2 in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: <p_T^2> = 4 GeV^2
- Coherence time for c-cbar production at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: 4-13 fm for p_T=0-5 GeV
- FSI suppression factor from NA60: 0.75 +/- 0.07

## Context
This work extends the dipole approach to J/Psi production in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior calculations of broadening and shadowing in pA collisions by Kopeliovich et al. (2002, 2007) and the transport coefficient formalism of Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (1997). It contradicts the interpretation of Wu and Ma (2009), who overestimated q_0-hat by a factor of 5 by ignoring ISI suppression and assuming fixed c-cbar separation. It also contrasts with the approach of Satz (2009), who assumed no FSI attenuation in the hot medium. The extracted q_0-hat is more than an order of magnitude smaller than values from jet quenching analyses by D'Enterria (2007) and Urs (2008). The results enable future calculations of centrality and rapidity dependence, azimuthal asymmetry, and studies of heavier quarkonia (Psi', chi, Upsilon) as probes of the dense medium.
