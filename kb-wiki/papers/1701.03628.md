# In medium dispersion relation effects in nuclear inclusive reactions at intermediate and low energies

> In a well-established many-body framework, successful in modeling a great variety of nuclear processes, we analyze the role of the spectral functions (SFs) accounting for the modifications of the dispersion relation of nucleons embedded in a nuclear medium. We concentrate in processes mostly governed by one-body mechanisms, and study possible approximations to evaluate the particle-hole propagator using SFs. We also investigate how to include together SFs and long-range RPA-correlation corrections in the evaluation of nuclear response functions, discussing the existing interplay between both type of nuclear effects. At low energy transfers (\le 50 MeV), we compare our predictions for inclusive muon and radiative pion captures in nuclei, and charge-current (CC) neutrino-nucleus cross sections with experimental results. We also present an analysis of intermediate energy quasi-elastic neutrino scattering for various targets and both neutrino and antineutrino CC driven processes. In all cases, we pay special attention to estimate the uncertainties affecting the theoretical predictions. In particular, we show that errors on the σ_μ/σ_e ratio are much smaller than 5%, and also much smaller than the size of the SF+RPA nuclear corrections, which produce significant effects, not only in the individual cross sections, but also in their ratio for neutrino energies below 400 MeV. These latter nuclear corrections, beyond Pauli blocking, turn out to be thus essential to achieve a correct theoretical understanding of this ratio of cross sections of interest for appearance neutrino oscillation experiments. We also briefly compare our SF and RPA results to predictions obtained within other representative approaches.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spectral function (SF) corrections for in-medium nucleon dispersion relations and long-range RPA correlations must be included together to correctly predict neutrino-nucleus cross sections and their ratios, because the two effects partially cancel each other in integrated rates while still significantly modifying differential distributions; the uncertainty on the \(\sigma_\mu/\sigma_e\) ratio is much smaller than 5% and also much smaller than the size of the combined SF+RPA nuclear corrections for neutrino energies below 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive lepton-nucleus cross sections (muon capture, radiative pion capture, and charge-current neutrino scattering) using a many-body framework based on the local density approximation, where the hadron tensor is related to the imaginary part of the gauge boson self-energy in nuclear matter. They incorporate dressed nucleon propagators via spectral functions derived from a semi-phenomenological nucleon self-energy model (FernandezdeCordoba 1991) and include long-range RPA correlations in the particle-hole propagator, evaluating the interplay between both corrections for energy transfers up to 400 MeV and for low-energy transfers below 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty on the \(\sigma_\mu/\sigma_e\) ratio is much smaller than 5% for neutrino energies below 400 MeV.
- The combined SF+RPA nuclear corrections produce significant effects in the \(\sigma_\mu/\sigma_e\) ratio for neutrino energies below 400 MeV.
- A form-factor error in prior work (Nieves 2004) produced numerical effects of at most 20% in quasi-elastic cross sections (higher cross sections), corrected in this paper.
- Low-energy comparisons are made for inclusive muon and radiative pion captures and for \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(\nu_\mu,\mu^-)X\) and \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(\nu_e,e^-)X\) cross sections near threshold, with energy transfers \(\le 50\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the many-body model previously applied to photon, electron, pion, kaon, and hyperon interactions with nuclei (Oset 1981, Carrasco 1989, Gil 1997) and to neutrino-nucleus reactions (Nieves 2004, Nieves 2005) by including full spectral functions (including hole-state widths) and studying their interplay with RPA correlations, which had not been addressed at low energies. It improves upon the superscaling approach (SuSA) of Amaro (2004) and Donnelly (1999), which cannot incorporate RPA effects, and corrects a form-factor error in earlier neutrino cross-section calculations (Nieves 2004). The results enable more accurate predictions for the \(\sigma_\mu/\sigma_e\) ratio relevant to appearance neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, MINOS, NOvA, and DUNE.
