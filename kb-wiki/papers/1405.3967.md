# Towards an effective action for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics

> We propose an effective action for first order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics that can be used to evaluate n-point symmetrized correlation functions, taking into account thermal fluctuations of the hydrodynamic variables.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an effective action for first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics that can be used to evaluate symmetrized n-point correlation functions including thermal fluctuations, but the authors note that the derivation has unresolved issues including the treatment of multiplicative noise, the Jacobian from the change of variables, and UV divergences in the loop expansion.

## Method

The authors start from classical relativistic hydrodynamics augmented with stochastic noise terms, convert the stochastic differential equations to a functional integral using auxiliary fields, and integrate out the noise fields to obtain an effective action. They work in the Landau-Lifshitz frame with first-order gradient terms in the constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and current, and they discuss the need for a specific discretization prescription to resolve ambiguities from multiplicative noise.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity Kubo formula gives \(G_{xy,xy}^{S}(\omega,\mathbf{k}) = 2\bar{T}\bar{\eta}\) for the symmetrized function of \(T_{xy}\)
- The noise correlations are \(\langle r_i(x) r_j(y) \rangle = 2\bar{T}\bar{\sigma} \delta_{ij} \delta(x-y)\) and \(\langle \tau_{ij}(x) \tau_{kl}(y) \rangle = 2\bar{T} G_{ijkl} \delta(x-y)\)
- For a scale-invariant uncharged fluid, the dimensionless combination controlling fluctuation corrections is \(\lambda \equiv (T/s^{1/d})\, (\eta/s)^{-1}\), which vanishes as \(N\to\infty\) in large-\(N\) gauge theories

## Context

This work extends the stochastic hydrodynamic framework of Landau and Lifshitz (1957) and the functional integral formulation of Martin, Siggia, and Rose (1973) to relativistic fluids, addressing limitations of prior effective actions that only captured non-dissipative physics (Dubovsky 2011, Bhattacharya 2012, Banerjee 2012, Jensen 2012). The proposed action enables systematic calculation of fluctuation corrections to transport coefficients such as shear viscosity, which were previously studied at one-loop level without a complete field-theoretic framework (Kovtun 2003, Kovtun 2011, Kovtun 2012).
