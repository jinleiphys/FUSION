# Can the "Darmstadt oscillations" be treated as two closely spaced mass-eigenstates of the H-like mother ions ?

> We discuss the proposal that the "Darmstadt oscillations" of the orbital K-shell electron capture decay (EC) rate of the H-like heavy ions are caused by quantum beats of two coherently excited, closely spaced mass-eigenstates of decaying H-like heavy ions. We show that such a mechanism to explain the time modulation of the EC-decay rates of the H-like heavy ions leads to the time modulation of the beta^+ decay rate of these ions with the same period. Such a time-dependence of the beta^+ decay rate of the H-like heavy ions contradicts the experimental data of GSI.

# Can the "Darmstadt oscillations" be treated as two closely spaced mass-eigenstates of the H-like mother ions?

## Key claim
The authors show that the proposal by Giunti (2006) and Kienert et al. (2008) to explain the periodic time modulation of electron capture decay rates of H-like heavy ions as quantum beats of two closely spaced mass-eigenstates of the mother ions necessarily predicts the same periodic time modulation for the beta+ decay rates of these ions, which contradicts preliminary GSI experimental data showing no such modulation.

## Method
The authors calculate the time-dependent EC and beta+ decay rates for H-like heavy ions prepared in a coherent superposition of two mass-eigenstates |m'> and |m''> with masses M_m' and M_m'', using standard weak interaction decay formalism. They then compute the total decay rates averaged over the two possible coherent states that can be statistically populated by projectile fragmentation, showing that for equal production probabilities the interference terms cancel completely.

## Key numbers
- The observed EC decay period T_EC = 2π/ω_EC ≈ 7 sec, with amplitude a_EC ≈ 0.20
- The Lorentz factor of the stored ions is γ = 1.43
- The energy difference between mass-eigenstates ΔE_m'm'' determines the oscillation period via ω_EC = ΔE_m'm''
- The mixing angle θ characterizes the superposition |m⟩ = cosθ |m'> + sinθ |m''>

## Context
This work extends the debate over the "Darmstadt oscillations" observed by Litvinov et al. (2008) in the EC decay rates of H-like 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ ions at GSI. The authors contrast their own earlier explanation (Ivanov et al., 2008) based on interference of neutrino mass-eigenstates with the alternative proposal by Giunti (2006) and Kienert et al. (2008) of mother-ion mass-eigenstates. The analysis shows that the mother-ion mass-eigenstate mechanism is ruled out by the absence of time modulation in beta+ decay, while the neutrino mass-eigenstate mechanism remains viable. The statistical population argument provides an additional reason why such oscillations would be unobservable in the mother-ion mass-eigenstate picture.
