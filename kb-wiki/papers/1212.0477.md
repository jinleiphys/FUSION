# Efimov States of Heavy Impurities in a Bose-Einstein Condensate

> We consider the problem of two heavy impurity particles embedded in a gas of weakly-interacting light mass bosonic particles in the condensed state. Using the Bogoliubov approach to describe the bosonic gas and the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for the three-body dynamics, we calculate the modification to the heavy-heavy two-body potential due to the presence of the condensate. For the case of resonant interaction between the light bosons and the impurities, we present (semi)-analytical results for the potential in the limit of a large condensate coherence length. In particular, we find a formula for the modification of the Efimov scaling factor due to the presence of a degenerate bosonic gas background.

# Efimov States of Heavy Impurities in a Bose-Einstein Condensate

## Key claim
The presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate background modifies the effective heavy-heavy potential at distances comparable to the condensate coherence length, suppressing the attractive inverse-square potential that is necessary for the Efimov effect. The coherence length replaces the scattering length in the Efimov formula for the number of universal three-body bound states when the heavy-light interaction is resonant and the condensate density is sufficiently high.

## Method
The author uses the Bogoliubov approximation to describe the weakly-interacting Bose gas of light particles and the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to treat the three-body dynamics of two heavy impurities and one light bosonic quasiparticle. The heavy-light interaction is modeled as a zero-range contact interaction, and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is used to relate the interaction strength to the vacuum scattering length. The effective heavy-heavy potential is derived by solving the Schrodinger equation for the light particle in the field of two fixed impurities, with the condensate effects entering through the Bogoliubov dispersion relation E(k) = sqrt(U_B n_0 hbar^2 k^2 / m + (hbar^2 k^2 / 2m)^2).

## Key numbers
- The Efimov scaling factor for equal mass particles is e^(pi/s_0) approximately 22.7, while for heavy-heavy-light systems it can be much smaller.
- In the non-interacting boson limit (a_B to 0, xi to infinity), the eigenvalue equation reduces to alpha R = R/a + e^(-alpha R), with solution alpha R = x_0 approximately 0.567 at unitarity (|a| = infinity).
- For the 4He*-87Rb system with a_B approximately 142 a_0 and r_vdW(87Rb) approximately 83 a_0, the estimated number of Efimov states at unitarity is N_B approximately 3.2 at condensate density n_0 = 10^13 cm^-3, decreasing to N_B approximately 2.5 for a tenfold increase in n_0 or a_B.
- The scaling parameter s_0 = 1.98 for mass ratios comparable to 4He-87Rb or 7Li-133Cs systems.

## Context
This work extends the study of Efimov physics (Efimov 1970) from isolated three-body systems to systems embedded in a many-body background, specifically a Bose-Einstein condensate. It contrasts with studies of fermionic backgrounds (Nishida 2009, MacNeill 2011, Nygaard 2011, Song 2011, Niemann 2012) where the Fermi sea can enhance binding, whereas the condensate background suppresses binding. The approach is related to the bosonic Kondo problem with two impurities (Affleck 1995, Smith 1999, Sengupta 2000, Vojta 2000, Zarand 2002, Florens 2006) and to recent work on impurities in condensates (Cucchietti 2006, Kalas 2006, Tempere 2009, Casteels 2011, Santamore 2011). The results enable experimental manipulation of the number of universal three-body states by tuning condensate density and interaction strengths in systems such as 4He*-87Rb (Knoop 2012) or 7Li-133Cs (Mudrich 2002, Marzok 2009).
