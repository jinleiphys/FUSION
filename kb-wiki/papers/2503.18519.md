# Effective field theory for weakly bound two-neutron halo nuclei: corrections from neutron-neutron effective range

> Using an effective field-theoretical approach, we investigate the properties of weakly bound two-neutron halo nuclei (also known as Borromean nuclei) that do not support a low-energy $s$-wave core-neutron resonance. Extending the recently formulated effective field theory for weakly bound Borromean nuclei, we incorporate corrections arising from the effective range of neutron-neutron scattering and evaluate their impact on the mean-square radii and electromagnetic response. In particular, we compute the ratio of the matter and charge radii, the shape of the $E1$ dipole strength function, and the electric polarizability. Our results indicate that these corrections remain numerically small when the two-neutron separation energy of the Borromean nucleus is much less than 1~MeV.

## Key claim

The paper extends the recently formulated effective field theory for weakly bound Borromean nuclei by incorporating corrections from the neutron-neutron effective range, and finds that these corrections remain numerically small when the two-neutron separation energy of the Borromean nucleus is much less than 1 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for a three-body system consisting of a compact core and two loosely bound neutrons, extending the theory of Hongo (2022) by adding a dimension-6 operator proportional to the neutron-neutron effective range \(r_0\). They treat the effective-range correction perturbatively, expanding the dimer propagator to first order in \(r_0\), and compute the charge, dineutron, and matter mean-square radii as well as the E1 dipole strength function and electric polarizability using Feynman diagram techniques with on-shell renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length \(a \approx -19\) fm, corresponding to a virtual energy \(\epsilon_n = \hbar^2/(m_n a^2) \approx 0.12\) MeV
- Neutron-neutron effective range \(r_0 \approx 2.75\) fm, corresponding to an energy scale \(\epsilon_0 = \hbar^2/(m_n r_0^2) \approx 5.5\) MeV
- For the case of \(^6\)He, the s-wave core-neutron scattering length is \(a_{cn} \approx 2.6\) fm
- The running coupling constant is given by \(g^2(E) = \frac{\pi}{4} \left( \frac{A}{A+2} \right)^{3/2} \left[1 + \frac{r_0}{a} + O\left( \left( \frac{r_0}{a} \right)^2 \right) \right] \frac{1}{\ln \frac{E_0}{E}}\)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory for weakly bound Borromean nuclei formulated by Hongo (2022), which assumed a small core-neutron scattering length in contrast to standard Halo EFT approaches that assume a resonant s-wave core-neutron interaction. The paper focuses on the carbon isotope \(^{22}\)C as the best candidate for this EFT framework, since experimental evidence from Mosby (2013) suggests the n-\(^{20}\)C scattering length is small. The results enable systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections to predictions for Borromean halo nuclei with small core-neutron scattering lengths, with the shape of the E1 dipole strength function remaining nearly unchanged when the effective-range correction is included.
