# Near-barrier Fusion Induced by Stable Weakly Bound and Exotic Halo Light Nuclei

> The effect of breakup is investigated for the medium weight $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co system in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier. The strong coupling of breakup/transfer channels to fusion is discussed within a comparison of predictions of the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels model which is also applied to $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co a reaction induced by the borromean halo nucleus $^{6}$He.

## Key claim

The Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) model can provide a consistent description of elastic scattering, total fusion, and breakup for reactions induced by stable weakly bound projectiles like \(^{6,7}\)Li, but its application to the halo nucleus \(^{6}\)He using a dineutron model is preliminary and the role of neutron-transfer channels remains to be quantified.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations using the FRESCO code for the \(^{6,7}\)Li+\(^{59}\)Co systems, treating the projectiles as two-body clusters (\(\alpha+d\) for \(^{6}\)Li, \(\alpha+t\) for \(^{7}\)Li) with full continuum couplings up to 8 MeV. For the \(^{6}\)He+\(^{59}\)Co system, a dineutron model (\(^{6}\)He = \(^{4}\)He+\(^{2}\)n) was adopted, including couplings to resonant (2\(^{+}\), E\(_{ex}\) = 0.826 MeV) and non-resonant continuum states up to f-waves, with short-range imaginary fusion potentials for each fragment.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{6}\)Li+\(^{59}\)Co, total calculated breakup cross sections were obtained by integrating contributions from continuum states up to 8 MeV.
- The \(^{6}\)He+\(^{59}\)Co CDCC calculations included couplings to the resonant state at E\(_{ex}\) = 0.826 MeV (2\(^{+}\)).
- The CDCC calculations for \(^{6}\)He+\(^{59}\)Co predicted much larger total fusion cross sections compared to \(^{4}\)He+\(^{59}\)Co experimental data (McMahan 1980) across all energies.
- The inclusion of couplings to breakup channels notably increased the total fusion cross section for \(^{6}\)He+\(^{59}\)Co for all energies.

## Context

This work extends previous CDCC studies of \(^{6,7}\)Li+\(^{59}\)Co reactions (Beck 2003, Diaz-Torres 2003) and compares with optical-model analyses (Souza 2004). The preliminary \(^{6}\)He+\(^{59}\)Co results are consistent with data for other medium-mass targets such as \(^{64}\)Zn (DiPietro 2006) and \(^{63,65}\)Cu (Navin 2006). The paper highlights the need for four-body CDCC models (Matsumoto 2004, Thompson 2005) and high-intensity radioactive beams to fully understand the competition between breakup, transfer, and fusion in halo-induced reactions.
