# Collision rates for $\bboxρ$, $\bboxω$ and $\bboxϕ$ mesons at nonzero temperature

> The $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons are exceptionally narrow in vacua being about 8 and 4 MeV. At finite temperature they will scatter with other hadrons in their approach to equilibrium and thereafter. These dynamics induce broadening to the already unstable vector mesons which is calculated in the framework of relativistic kinetic theory. A rather complete set of low-lying mesons provide reaction partners whose interactions are modeled using effective Lagrangians. Collision rates for $ρ$ and $ω$ are found to be $\sim$ 100 MeV at $T$=200 MeV while the rate for $ϕ$ is $\sim$ 25 MeV. Corresponding mean free paths are about 1 and 4 fm! Possible observable consequences are discussed.

## Key claim

Collision rates for ρ and ω mesons at T=200 MeV are approximately 100 MeV, while the rate for φ mesons is about 25 MeV, with corresponding mean free paths of about 1 fm and 4 fm respectively; these results depend on including a temperature- and energy-dependent width for exchanged pions and kaons in the propagators, which the authors note is a phenomenological estimate rather than a full two-loop field-theoretic calculation.

## Method

The author computed collision rates for ρ, ω, and φ mesons in a thermal hadron gas using relativistic kinetic theory, modeling interactions with effective Lagrangians (VPP, VVP, AVP types) and including s-channel and t-channel diagrams with monopole form factors (Λ = 1.8 GeV for most vertices, Λ = mρ for ρππ vertices). The imaginary parts of intermediate pion and kaon propagators were estimated by computing their momentum- and temperature-dependent scattering rates using Breit-Wigner cross sections for resonance formation (ρ, K*, K1(1270)), then inserted by hand into the propagators as mΓ terms.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum widths: ω meson ≈ 8 MeV, φ meson ≈ 4 MeV
- ρ and ω collision rates ≈ 100 MeV at T = 200 MeV
- φ collision rate ≈ 25 MeV at T = 200 MeV (specifically 27.4 MeV from sum of all contributions)
- ρ and ω mean free paths ≈ 1 fm at T = 200 MeV
- φ mean free path ≈ 4.4 fm at T = 200 MeV (38.2 fm at T = 100 MeV, 14.9 fm at T = 150 MeV)
- ρ scattering rate from kaon contribution alone: 66 MeV at T = 200 MeV; sum of pion and kaon contributions: 115.5 MeV
- φ collision rate at T = 100 MeV: 2.4 MeV; at T = 150 MeV: 8.6 MeV
- Average φ velocity: 0.46c at T = 100 MeV, 0.54c at T = 150 MeV, 0.61c at T = 200 MeV
- Pion and kaon widths from scattering vary from 20 to 200 MeV depending on temperature and momentum

## Context

This work extends prior studies of vector meson behavior at finite temperature that focused on mass shifts and modified dispersion relations (e.g., studies by C. Gale, S. Gottlieb, M. Dey, R. Furnstahl, and others cited in the paper), specifically addressing the previously neglected two-loop-level collisional broadening. The author improves upon the calculation by Ko and Seibert (1994) for the φ meson, who reported full widths below 10 MeV but neglected some contributions and used fixed kaon widths of 20 or 30 MeV rather than energy-dependent ones. The results enable estimates of how resonance broadening affects dilepton invariant mass spectra and the visibility of ω and φ peaks in heavy-ion collisions.
