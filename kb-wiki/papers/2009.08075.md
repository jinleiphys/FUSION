# Covariant density functional theory for nuclear chirality in $^{135}$Nd

> The three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (3D-TAC CDFT) is used to study the chiral modes in $^{135}$Nd. By modeling the motion of the nucleus in rotating mean field as the interplay between the single-particle motions of several valence particle(s) and hole(s) and the collective motion of a core-like part, a classical Routhian is extracted. This classical Routhian gives qualitative agreement with the 3D-TAC CDFT result for the critical frequency corresponding to the transition from planar to aplanar rotation. Based on this investigation a possible understanding of tilted rotation appearing in a microscopic theory is provided.

## Key claim

The three-dimensional tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (3D-TAC CDFT) calculations for $^{135}$Nd show a transition from planar to aplanar rotation for one configuration (config1, bands D3/D4) but only planar rotation for another (config2, bands D5/D6), and the extracted classical Routhian gives only *qualitative* agreement with the microscopic 3D-TAC CDFT result for the critical frequency of that transition.

## Method

The 3D-TAC CDFT with the effective point-coupling interaction PC-PK1 was used to solve the Dirac equation in a three-dimensional Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with ten major shells, neglecting pairing correlations. The orientation of the angular velocity vector $\bm{\omega}$ (defined by polar angle $\theta$ and azimuthal angle $\varphi$) was determined self-consistently by minimizing the total Routhian for two configurations in $^{135}$Nd: $\pi [h^1_{11/2}(gd)^1] \otimes \nu h^{-1}_{11/2}$ (config1) and $\pi h^2_{11/2}\otimes \nu h^{-1}_{11/2}$ (config2). A classical Routhian was then extracted by separating the nucleus into valence nucleons and a core-like part, using the single-particle Routhians and the core angular momenta from the 3D-TAC CDFT results.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for config1: $(\beta, \gamma) \approx (0.23, 21^\circ)$; for config2: $(\beta, \gamma) \approx (0.24, 22^\circ)$, both stable within $\Delta\beta = 0.02$ and $\Delta\gamma = 3^\circ$ with rotation.
- For config1, two degenerate minima at $|\varphi_{\min}| \sim 15^\circ$ appear on the Routhian curve at $\hbar\omega = 0.50$ MeV; the barrier height at $\varphi=0$ reaches $\sim 150$ keV at $\hbar\omega = 0.60$ MeV.
- For config2, convergent results were attained only up to $\hbar\omega = 0.55$ MeV (corresponding to $I \sim 21\hbar$); the critical frequency $\omega_{\text{crit}}$ is larger than $\hbar\omega = 0.55$ MeV in this calculation, compared to $\hbar\omega = 0.50$ MeV in a prior 3D-TAC calculation by S. Zhu (2003).
- At $\hbar\omega = 0.30$ MeV, the ratio of core moments of inertia $\mathcal{J}_m : \mathcal{J}_s : \mathcal{J}_l$ at $\varphi=0$ is about $1.00 : 0.65 : 0.50$ for config1 and $1.00 : 0.78 : 0.76$ for config2.
- The valence neutron in the $h_{11/2}$ shell contributes roughly $4.5\hbar$ of angular momentum along the $l$-axis.

## Context

This work extends the 3D-TAC CDFT approach previously applied to chirality in $^{106}$Rh (Zhao 2017), $^{136}$Nd (Petrache 2018), and $^{106}$Ag (Zhao 2019) to the nucleus $^{135}$Nd, which has two reported chiral doublet band candidates (bands D3/D4 and D5/D6). The study provides a microscopic justification for the assumptions of the phenomenological particle-rotor model (PRM) of Frauendorf (1997) and the analytical formula for the critical frequency by Olbratowski (2004), by demonstrating that a core-like part with the largest moment of inertia along the medium ($m$-) axis indeed emerges from the self-consistent 3D-TAC CDFT calculations. This enables a deeper understanding of how tilted rotation arises in a fully microscopic theory and reinforces the duality of single-particle and collective properties in atomic nuclei.
