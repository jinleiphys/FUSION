# Constraining the high-density nuclear symmetry energy with the transverse-momentum dependent elliptic flow

> Within the newly updated version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, the transverse-velocity dependence of the elliptic flow of free nucleons from $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at the incident energy 400 MeV$/$nucleon is studied within different windows of the normalized c.m. rapidity $y_0$. It is found that the elliptic flow difference $v_{2}^{n}$-$v_{2}^{p}$ and ratio $v_{2}^{n}$/$v_{2}^{p}$ of neutrons versus protons are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, especially the ratio $v_{2}^{n}$/$v_{2}^{p}$ at small transverse velocity in the intermediate rapidity intervals $0.4<|y_0|<0.6$. By comparing either transverse-momentum dependent or integrated FOPI/LAND elliptic flow data of nucleons and hydrogen isotopes with calculations using various Skyrme interactions, all exhibiting similar values of isoscalar incompressibility but very different density dependences of the symmetry energy, a moderately soft to linear symmetry energy is extracted, in good agreement with previous UrQMD or Tübingen QMD model calculations but contrasting results obtained with $π^-/π^+$ yield ratios available in the literature.

# Constraining the high-density nuclear symmetry energy with the transverse-momentum dependent elliptic flow

## Key claim
The transverse-momentum dependent elliptic flow ratio of neutrons to hydrogen isotopes from 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon constrains the slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy to L = 89 ± 45 MeV (2-sigma uncertainty), indicating a moderately soft to linear density dependence. This result agrees with previous UrQMD and Tübingen QMD model analyses but contrasts with constraints extracted from π−/π+ yield ratios using IBUU04 and LQMD models.

## Method
The authors used the updated ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model with a Skyrme potential energy density functional to simulate semi-central 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. They compared calculated elliptic flow ratios v2n/v2H and differences v2n−v2p with FOPI/LAND experimental data using 21 different Skyrme interactions that have similar isoscalar incompressibility (K0 ≈ 230±10 MeV) but widely varying symmetry energy slope parameters L. A chi-squared analysis of the transverse-momentum dependent flow ratio was performed to extract the best-fit L value.

## Key numbers
- Slope parameter L = 89 ± 45 MeV (2-sigma uncertainty) extracted from transverse-momentum dependent v2n/v2H ratio
- Numerical average L ≈ 85 MeV from four transverse-momentum integrated observables (v2n−v2p, v2n−v2H, v2n/v2p, v2n/v2H)
- Slightly larger L ≈ 110 MeV obtained when using ERAT-based impact parameter sorting instead of multiplicity sorting
- Previous UrQMD result: L = 83 ± 52 MeV (2-sigma) and γ = 0.98 ± 0.35 (Russotto et al., 2011)
- Previous Tübingen QMD result: L = 122 ± 57 MeV (Cozma et al., 2013)
- Selected Skyrme forces cover L values from 5.75 MeV (Skz2) to 161.05 MeV (SkI1)
- Saturation properties: S0 values range from 30.00 MeV (SV-mas08, MSL0) to 37.53 MeV (SkI1)
- Incident energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for 197Au+197Au collisions

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Russotto et al. (2011) by using the updated UrQMD model with Skyrme interactions rather than the earlier γ-exponent parameterization of the symmetry energy potential. The results agree with previous UrQMD and Tübingen QMD model analyses (Cozma et al., 2013) indicating moderately soft to linear symmetry energy, but contrast with extremely soft or extremely stiff behaviors extracted from π−/π+ yield ratios using the IBUU04 model (Xiao et al., 2009) and LQMD model (Feng et al., 2009). The extracted L ≈ 90-110 MeV is larger than the typical L ≈ 60 MeV from nuclear structure studies and lower-energy reactions, which may reflect the higher densities probed at this beam energy. The authors note that new experiments such as the ASY-EOS (S394) experiment at GSI will be important for testing consistency of model predictions.
