# Microscopic investigation of $E2$ matrix elements in atomic nuclei -- II

> The present work is a continuation of our earlier investigation with the primary objective to systematically calculate the $E2$ matrix elements using the microscopic approach of the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM). In the earlier work, we studied nine nuclides of $^{72}$Ge, $^{76}$Ge, $^{104}$Ru, $^{168}$Er, $^{186}$Os, $^{188}$Os, $^{190}$Os, $^{192}$Os, and $^{194}$Pt. In the present work six more nuclides of $^{70}$Ge, $^{76,78,80,82}$Se, and $^{100}$Mo have been investigated. The Coulomb excitation data has recently become available for $^{70}$Ge and other nuclides were inadvertently omitted in our earlier investigation. It is demonstrated that TPSM approach provides a good description of the available experimental data and most of the nuclides, except for $^{76}$Se and $^{100}$Mo, are shown to have $γ$ soft behaviour. Further, it is demonstrated that in contrast to the predictions of the phenomenological collective model, TPSM calculations depict no clear correlation between the energy staggering pattern of the $γ$ band and the deduced shape invariant quantities using the Kumar-Cline sum rules.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) provides a good description of the available experimental E2 matrix elements for the six nuclides studied, and most of these nuclei exhibit gamma-soft behavior; however, the TPSM calculations show no clear correlation between the energy staggering pattern of the gamma band and the shape invariant quantities deduced from Kumar-Cline sum rules, in contrast to predictions of the phenomenological collective model.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) calculations for six nuclides (70Ge, 76,78,80,82Se, and 100Mo) using a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian with triaxially deformed Nilsson basis states. Angular momentum projection was applied to the triaxial vacuum and multi-quasiparticle configurations, followed by diagonalization to obtain wave functions and E2 matrix elements. The deformation parameters were chosen to reproduce experimental gamma-band head energies and B(E2;2_1+ to 0_1+) values, and shape invariant quantities were derived from the calculated E2 matrix elements using the Kumar-Cline sum rules via the GOSIA code.

## Key numbers

* 70Ge: centroid ⟨Q²⟩ ≈ 0.29 e²b² for the yrast configuration, corresponding to deformation β ≈ 0.289; ⟨cos3δ⟩ ≈ -0.18 for yrast states, corresponding to δ ≈ 33°
* 76Se: yrast ⟨cos3δ⟩ = 0.01 for I=0, corresponding to δ = 29.7°, increasing with spin toward the prolate shape
* 78Se: yrast ⟨cos3δ⟩ = 0.383 for I=0, corresponding to δ = 22.5°, located in the prolate sector
* 80Se: yrast ⟨cos3δ⟩ = 0.0505 at I=0, corresponding to δ = 29°
* 100Mo: even-spin-down staggering pattern appears at I=8 in TPSM calculations
* Diagonal yrast E2 matrix elements are about 5-8 times smaller than the 3_1 to 2_2 values, indicating gamma values not far from 30°

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TPSM analysis (Qureshie et al., 2025; Rouoof et al., 2024) of nine nuclides (72Ge, 76Ge, 104Ru, 168Er, 186,188,190,192Os, 194Pt) to six additional nuclides, including 70Ge for which Coulomb excitation data recently became available (Cline et al., 2023). The study enables systematic comparison of microscopic TPSM predictions with the phenomenological collective model, particularly regarding the relationship between gamma-band energy staggering and shape invariants. The results challenge the collective model expectation that energy staggering patterns correlate with triaxial softness, as demonstrated previously for Os and Pt isotopes (Rouoof et al., 2024).
