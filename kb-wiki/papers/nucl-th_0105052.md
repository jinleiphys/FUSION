# Neutron structure function and inclusive DIS from H-3 and He-3 at large Bjorken-x

> A detailed study of inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from mirror A = 3 nuclei at large values of the Bjorken variable x is presented. The main purpose is to estimate the theoretical uncertainties on the extraction of the neutron DIS structure function from such nuclear measurements. On one hand, within models in which no modification of the bound nucleon structure functions is taken into account, we have investigated the possible uncertainties arising from: i) charge symmetry breaking terms in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, ii) finite Q**2 effects neglected in the Bjorken limit, iii) the role of different prescriptions for the nucleon Spectral Function normalization providing baryon number conservation, and iv) the differences between the virtual nucleon and light cone formalisms. Although these effects have been not yet considered in existing analyses, our conclusion is that all these effects cancel at the level of ~ 1% for x < 0.75 in overall agreement with previous findings. On the other hand we have considered several models in which the modification of the bound nucleon structure functions is accounted for to describe the EMC effect in DIS scattering from nuclei. It turns out that within these models the cancellation of nuclear effects is expected to occur only at a level of ~ 3%, leading to an accuracy of ~ 12 % in the extraction of the neutron to proton structure function ratio at x ~ 0.7 -0.8$. Another consequence of considering a broad range of models of the EMC effect is that the previously suggested iteration procedure does not improve the accuracy of the extraction of the neutron to proton structure function ratio.

# Neutron structure function and inclusive DIS from H-3 and He-3 at large Bjorken-x

## Key claim

The super-ratio of He-3 to H-3 DIS structure functions, which was previously expected to be close to unity and thus allow a clean extraction of the neutron structure function, deviates from unity by approximately 3% when realistic models of the EMC effect are included, limiting the accuracy of the extracted neutron-to-proton structure function ratio to about 12% at x ~ 0.7–0.8. This cancellation of nuclear effects is significantly worse than the ~1% level found when no bound nucleon modification is assumed.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of inclusive deep inelastic scattering from He-3 and H-3 using both the Virtual Nucleon Convolution (VNC) model and the light-cone (LC) formalism, incorporating realistic three-body spectral functions based on the Reid Soft Core (RSC) nucleon-nucleon interaction. They evaluated the super-ratio of the EMC ratios for the two mirror nuclei under two classes of models: (i) those with no modification of bound nucleon structure functions, where they studied charge symmetry breaking, finite Q^2 effects, spectral function normalization prescriptions, and differences between VNC and LC formalisms; and (ii) several models incorporating modifications of bound nucleon structure functions to describe the EMC effect.

## Key numbers

- For x < 0.75, all effects studied within models with no bound nucleon modification (charge symmetry breaking, finite Q^2 effects, normalization prescriptions, VNC vs LC formalisms) cancel at the level of approximately 1%.
- Within models that include modification of bound nucleon structure functions (EMC effect models), the cancellation of nuclear effects in the super-ratio occurs only at the level of approximately 3%.
- This leads to an accuracy of approximately 12% in the extraction of the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio at x ~ 0.7–0.8.
- The minimum removal energy for He-3 is approximately 5.49 MeV, while for H-3 it is approximately 6.26 MeV.
- The three-body break-up thresholds are 7.72 MeV in He-3 and 8.48 MeV in H-3.
- The probabilities of partial waves in the three-nucleon ground state are: S'-wave 1.2–1.5%, P-wave less than 0.2%, and D-wave 7–9%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Wally (1999) and Pace (2000), who found deviations of the super-ratio from unity of 2% and 1% respectively, by including additional effects not previously considered. The paper contradicts the expectation that the mirror symmetry of A=3 nuclei would allow a nearly model-independent extraction of the neutron structure function, as had been suggested in those earlier works. The results show that the iteration procedure previously suggested by FS85 and S92 does not improve the accuracy of the extraction. This work enables future experimental programs at Jefferson Lab and elsewhere to set realistic expectations for the precision achievable in neutron structure function measurements from A=3 targets.
