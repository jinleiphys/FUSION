# Elliptic Flow from a Hybrid CGC, Full 3D Hydro and Hadronic Cascade Model

> We investigate the robustness of the discovery of the perfect fluid through comparison of hydrodynamic calculations with the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV. Employing the Glauber model for initial entropy density distributions, the centrality dependence of v_2 is reasonably reproduced by using an ideal fluid description of the early QGP stage followed by a hadronic cascade in the late hadronic stage. On the other hand, initial conditions based on the Colour Glass Condensate model are found to generate larger elliptic flow due to larger initial eccentricity epsilon. We further predict v_2/epsilon at a fixed impact parameter as a function of collision energy sqrt{s_{NN}} up to the LHC energy.

# Elliptic Flow from a Hybrid CGC, Full 3D Hydro and Hadronic Cascade Model

## Key claim
The centrality dependence of elliptic flow v2 at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV can be reproduced by ideal fluid dynamics with Glauber initial conditions followed by hadronic cascade, but Colour Glass Condensate initial conditions generate larger elliptic flow than observed even after accounting for hadronic dissipation, implying that either significant shear viscosity in the QGP stage or a better understanding of initial conditions is required.

## Method
The authors formulated a unified dynamical model combining fully three-dimensional ideal fluid dynamics for the QGP stage with a hadronic cascade model (JAM) for the late hadronic stage below a switching temperature of 169 MeV. They compared two sets of initial conditions for the early stage: a conventional Glauber model parametrization of initial entropy density and a Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) picture calculating produced gluon distributions. The centrality dependence of charged particle multiplicity and elliptic flow v2 were computed and compared to PHOBOS experimental data.

## Key numbers
- At sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV in Au+Au collisions, the eccentricity from CGC initial conditions is 20-30% larger than that from the Glauber model
- The switching temperature between ideal fluid QGP and hadronic cascade is T_sw=169 MeV
- For the excitation function calculation, impact parameter is fixed at b=6.3 fm in Au+Au collisions
- At RHIC energy, hadronic dissipation reduces v2 by approximately 20%
- The hybrid model predicts a monotonically increasing excitation function for v2/epsilon from RHIC to LHC energies, in contrast to the ideal fluid approach whose excitation function almost saturates above RHIC energies

## Context
This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamic+cascade approach of Hirano, Heinz, Kharzeev, Lacey, and Nara (2006) to systematically compare Glauber and CGC initial conditions for elliptic flow at RHIC. It contradicts the assumption that ideal fluid dynamics alone can explain all v2 data, showing that CGC initial conditions overshoot the PHOBOS data unless additional shear viscosity in the QGP is assumed. The analysis enables future studies of QGP viscosity by highlighting the need to constrain initial conditions, and references prior work by Kolb (2001) on the phase-transition bump in v2/epsilon and by Teaney (2003) on hadronic dissipation effects. The approach predicts that v2/epsilon continues to increase toward LHC energies, with hadronic dissipation washing out the phase-transition bump predicted in earlier purely hydrodynamic calculations.
