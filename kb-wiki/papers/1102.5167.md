# Dense Stellar Matter with Strange Quark Matter Driven by Kaon Condensation

> The core of neutron-star matter is supposed to be at a much higher density than the normal nuclear matter density for which various possibilities have been suggested such as, for example, meson or hyperon condensation and/or deconfined quark or color-superconducting matter. In this work, we explore the implication on hadron physics of a dense compact object that has three "phases", nuclear matter at the outer layer, kaon condensed nuclear matter in the middle and strange quark matter at the core. Using a drastically simplified but not unreasonable model, we develop the scenario where the different phases are smoothly connected with the kaon condensed matter playing a role of "doorway" to a quark core, the equation of state (EoS) of which with parameters restricted within the range allowed by nature could be made compatible with the mass vs. radius constraint given by the 1.97-solar mass object PSR J1614-2230 recently observed.

## Key claim

The authors explore whether a neutron star with three smoothly connected phases, nuclear matter, kaon-condensed matter, and strange quark matter, can be compatible with the observed 1.97-solar-mass object PSR J1614-2230, using a simplified model with parameters restricted within ranges allowed by nature, rather than claiming definitive proof of such a structure.

## Method

The authors construct a hadronic effective Lagrangian with kaon-nucleon interactions limited to the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and a sigma term treated as a free parameter (200–400 MeV), combined with a phenomenological nuclear matter parametrization (LCK/MDI) for the symmetry energy and potential energy. They solve the chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions for kaon-condensed nuclear matter (KNM) and then connect it to strange quark matter (SQM) via an MIT bag model, integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation to obtain stellar mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The observed neutron star PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of 1.97 solar masses.
- The kaon condensation threshold density is approximately 3 times the normal nuclear matter density (3ρ₀).
- The KN sigma term Σ_KN is constrained by lattice QCD to be less than or equal to 200 MeV, with a possible range from approximately 130 MeV to 400 MeV.
- The symmetry energy parameters for the LCK model are given: for η=-1, F=3.673 MeV and G=1.569; for η=0, F=129.981 MeV and G=1.059.
- The nuclear matter potential parameters are α=-298.25 MeV, β=244.99 MeV, and γ=1.21.
- The non-interacting quark gas symmetry energy at critical density is given as S_N(ρ_c) = 24.6 (ρ_c/ρ₀)^(1/3) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the scenario of Bethe and Brown (1990s) where kaon condensation limits neutron star maximum masses below 2 solar masses, and addresses the challenge posed by the observation of PSR J1614-2230 by Demorest et al. (2010). It contrasts with approaches that treat hyperon condensation as the primary strangeness mechanism, and builds on the lattice QCD results from the NPLQCD collaboration (2010) showing that the strangeness content of the nucleon is much smaller than previously assumed. The paper enables future tests of whether kaon condensation can serve as a "doorway" to quark matter in compact stars, with predictions that could be falsified by experiments at FAIR/GSI and J-PARC.
