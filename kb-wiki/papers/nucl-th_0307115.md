# Nuclear absorption and anomalous $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions

> We discuss $J/ψ$ suppression in a QCD based nuclear absorption model. Centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in S+U and in Pb+Pb collisions are explained in the model. However, the model fails to explain the centrality dependence of $ψ\prime$ suppression. $ψ\prime$ suppression in S+U or in Pb+Pb collisions require additional suppression. Additional suppression of $ψ\prime$, due to hadronic comovers or due to QGP formation could not be distinguished in Pb+Pb collisions. We then show that the centrality dependence of the ratio, $ψ\prime$ over $J/ψ$, could possibly distinguish two scenario (e.g. QGP or hadronic comover) at RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The QCD-based nuclear absorption model explains the centrality dependence of \(J/\psi\) suppression in both S+U and Pb+Pb collisions, but it fails to explain \(\psi\prime\) suppression in those systems, requiring additional suppression mechanisms (QGP formation or hadronic comovers) that cannot be distinguished at SPS energies; the ratio \(\psi\prime / J/\psi\) as a function of transverse energy could distinguish these two scenarios at RHIC energy.

## Method

The author uses a QCD-based nuclear absorption model where \(J/\psi\) production is a two-step process: perturbative production of a \(c\bar{c}\) pair, followed by non-perturbative formation of the \(J/\psi\) meson. The nuclear medium increases the relative 4-square momentum of the pair, parameterized by a gain per unit length \(\varepsilon^2 = 0.185\ \mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}\) obtained from fits to high-statistics pA data. For \(\psi\prime\) suppression, additional suppression factors are introduced: a QGP suppression factor (dissolving all \(\psi\prime\) above a threshold density \(n_c\)) and a comover suppression factor (using a \(\psi\prime\)-comover absorption cross section \(\sigma_{co}\)), each fitted to the experimental \(\psi\prime\) data.

## Key numbers

- \(\varepsilon^2 = 0.185\ \mathrm{GeV}^2/\mathrm{fm}\): the gain in 4-square momentum per unit length, obtained from a fit to high-statistics pA data on total \(J/\psi\) cross section.
- \(\sigma^{J/\psi N}_{abs} = 4.4 \pm 1\ \mathrm{mb}\): the \(J/\psi\)-nucleon absorption cross section from high-statistics pA data, much less than the earlier estimate of 6-7 mb.
- \(\sigma_{co} = 8\ \mathrm{mb}\): the \(\psi\prime\)-comover absorption cross section used in the comover scenario fit.
- \(n_c = 2.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\): the threshold density above which all \(\psi\prime\) are dissolved in the QGP scenario, obtained from a fit to Pb+Pb data.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, the ratio \(\psi\prime / J/\psi\) shows a tendency of saturation beyond \(E_T > 70\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- At RHIC, with 37% hard scattering, the nuclear+QGP scenario predicts the ratio remains constant for \(E_T > 70\ \mathrm{GeV}\), while the nuclear+comover scenario predicts a continual decrease.

## Context

This work extends the QCD-based nuclear absorption model previously applied to \(J/\psi\) suppression in Chaudhuri (2002) and to \(p_T\) broadening in Chaudhuri (2002a), and addresses the anomalous suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration (2000). The analysis follows the approach of Blaizot et al. (2000) for QGP suppression and Gavin et al. (1996) for comover suppression, and builds on the proposal by Gupta and Satz (2000) to use the \(\psi\prime / J/\psi\) ratio as a QGP signal. The model successfully explains the latest NA50 data from the 2000 Pb+Pb run and high-statistics pA data, but cannot distinguish between QGP and comover scenarios at SPS energies; it predicts that RHIC energy measurements of the \(\psi\prime / J/\psi\) ratio could resolve this ambiguity.
