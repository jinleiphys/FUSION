# In-Medium Pion Valence Distributions in a Light-Front Model

> Pion valence distributions in nuclear medium and vacuum are studied in a light-front constituent quark model. The in-medium input for studying the pion properties is calculated by the quark-meson coupling model. We find that the in-medium pion valence distribution, as well as the in-medium pion valence wave function, are substantially modified at normal nuclear matter density, due to the reduction in the pion decay constant.

## Key claim

The in-medium pion valence distribution and valence wave function are substantially modified at normal nuclear matter density due to the reduction in the pion decay constant, but the pion mass is assumed unchanged up to that density based on pionic-atom experiments.

## Method

The authors used a light-front constituent quark model with a symmetric vertex function to calculate the pion valence wave function and distribution amplitude in symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium input for the constituent light-quark properties (effective mass and vector potential) was obtained from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which self-consistently reproduces nuclear matter saturation properties with a vacuum constituent quark mass of 220 MeV. The in-medium pion decay constant was then used to normalize the valence wave function, and the distribution amplitude was obtained by integrating the wave function over transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum constituent quark mass: 220 MeV; at normal nuclear matter density (rho/rho0 = 1.00), effective quark mass drops to 79.5 MeV
- Vacuum pion decay constant: 93.1 MeV; at normal nuclear matter density, pion decay constant drops to 40.2 MeV
- Vacuum pion charge radius: 0.73 fm; at normal nuclear matter density, charge radius increases to 1.96 fm
- Valence component probability in vacuum: 0.782; at normal nuclear matter density, valence probability increases to 0.930
- Nuclear incompressibility obtained with 220 MeV quark mass: K = 320.9 MeV
- Regulator mass used in vertex function: m_R = 600 MeV (unchanged in medium)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of in-medium pion properties (de Melo 2014, pimedium2) from electromagnetic form factors and decay constants to the valence distribution amplitude. It uses the same QMC model framework developed by Guichon and Saito (2007) that provides a saturation mechanism for nuclear matter based on quark degrees of freedom. The results enable future studies of in-medium parton distribution functions for the pion and can be extended to kaon, D-meson, and rho-meson in nuclear medium.
