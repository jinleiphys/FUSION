# Effects of perturbative exchanges in a QCD-string model

> The QCD-string model for baryons derived by Simonov and used for the calculation of baryon magnetic moments in a previous paper is extended to include also perturbative gluon and meson exchanges. The mass spectrum of the baryon multiplet is studied. For the meson interaction either the pseudoscalar or pseudovector coupling is used. Predictions are compared with the experimental data. Besides these exchanges the influence of excited quark orbitals on the baryon ground state are considered by performing a multichannel calculation. The nucleon-Delta splitting increases due to the mixing of higher quark states while the baryon magnetic momenta decrease. The multichannel calculation with perturbative exchanges is shown to yield reasonable magnetic moments while the mass spectrum is close to experiment.

## Key claim

The QCD-string model for baryons, previously used to calculate magnetic moments without constituent quark masses, is extended to include perturbative one-gluon and pseudoscalar meson exchanges; the multichannel calculation with these exchanges yields reasonable magnetic moments and a mass spectrum close to experiment, but the results depend on the choice of string tension and on whether pseudoscalar or pseudovector coupling is used for the meson interaction.

## Method

The authors start from the field correlator method (FCM) to obtain single-quark orbitals in a confining, Lorentz-scalar potential characterized by a string tension σ and a gluon correlation length Tg = 0.24 fm. They then add perturbative one-gluon-exchange (OGE) and one-pion-exchange (OPE) potentials, using either pseudoscalar (PS) or pseudovector (PV) coupling for the pion, and treat these exchanges in first-order perturbation theory on the baryon ground-state wave functions. To account for mixing of excited quark orbitals, they perform a multichannel calculation by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis that includes single-quark states such as 1(1/2)++, 0(3/2)++, and 0(1/2)-+, and compute the resulting mass spectrum and magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- String tension values used: σ = 0.06 GeV², 0.09 GeV², and 0.12 GeV².
- Gluon correlation length: Tg = 0.24 fm.
- For the running coupling constant: ΛV = 385 MeV, mB = 1.0 GeV, nf = 3.
- Pseudoscalar meson decay constants: fπ = 93 MeV, fκ = 113 MeV, fη = 112 MeV.
- Cutoff mass parameters for meson exchanges: Λ0 = 565 MeV, κ = 0.81.
- The nucleon-Δ splitting increases by about 100 MeV due to mixing of higher quark states.
- For PS coupling, a constant C0 of about 170–190 MeV is needed; for PV coupling, C0 ≈ 140–160 MeV.
- The PS coupling at σ = 0.06 GeV² gives baryon octet masses close to experiment; the PV coupling at σ = 0.09 GeV² gives reasonable decuplet masses, missing about 100 MeV.
- The multichannel calculation suggests a preferred string tension of about σ = 0.08 GeV² with a slight preference for PV coupling.

## Context

This work extends the QCD-string model for baryons developed by Simonov (2001) using the field correlator method, which previously calculated baryon magnetic moments without constituent quark masses. It adds perturbative one-gluon and meson exchanges following the approach of Isgur (1978, 1979) and Capstick (1986) for including excited quark orbitals via multichannel mixing. The results enable future studies of baryon structure within a framework that combines confinement from the field correlator method with perturbative spin-dependent interactions, and the authors note that lattice simulations on static quarks (Takahashi, 2000) suggest string tensions as low as 0.06 GeV² are appropriate for their constant-r0 approximation.
