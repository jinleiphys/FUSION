# Understanding saturation and AA collisions with an eA collider

> The initial conditions in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions are determined by the small momentum fraction part of the nuclear wavefunction. This is the regime of gluon saturation and the most direct way to experimentally study it would be deep inelastic scattering at a high energy electron ion collider (EIC). This talk discusses some of the connections between physics at the EIC and the initial stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We argue that measurements at an EIC will provide detailed high-precision information about the parameters for the initial conditions, transverse geometry and longitudinal correlations that will be crucial in understanding the initial stage of a heavy ion collision.

## Key claim

Measurements at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will provide high-precision information about the parameters for the initial conditions, transverse geometry, and longitudinal correlations that are crucial for understanding the initial stage of a heavy ion collision, but the actual value of the saturation scale Q_s depends on a nonperturbative input that must be obtained from experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical connections between deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at an EIC and the initial stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions, focusing on the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory. It discusses how the saturation scale Q_s, which governs bulk particle production in heavy ion collisions, can be constrained by dipole model fits to existing HERA data on protons and then extended to nuclei using basic nuclear geometry. The analysis compares different parametrizations of nuclear gluon distributions and extrapolates saturation scale evolution from RHIC to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale for the central rapidity region at RHIC is estimated as Q_s ≈ 1.2 GeV, based on dipole model fits to HERA data extended to nuclei.
- Estimates from fits to HERA inclusive data for the exponent λ in Q_s² ∼ x^{-λ} vary in the range λ = 0.2 to 0.3.
- At LHC energies, partons with x ≪ 10^{-2} can produce jets with p_T > 10 GeV, making jet production sensitive to a region where the nuclear gluon distribution is virtually unknown.
- According to one recent estimate, 20-25% of events at an EIC could be diffractive, compared to approximately 15% at HERA for protons.
- The initial gluon multiplicity in a heavy ion collision is approximately c Q_s²(x) π R_A² / α_s, yielding around 1000 gluons per unit rapidity in the initial state.

## Context

This talk extends the line of work connecting DIS measurements to heavy ion collision initial conditions, building on the CGC framework reviewed by Iancu (2003) and Weigert (2005). It contrasts the poorly constrained nuclear gluon distributions at small x (shown in parametrizations by Eskola (2009)) with the more precise constraints that would come from an EIC, and discusses how measurements of charged hadron multiplicity at LHC will constrain the interpretation of HERA data. The paper enables future work by identifying specific observables, diffractive structure functions, longitudinal correlations, and cold nuclear matter energy loss, where EIC measurements would directly inform the initial conditions for heavy ion collisions.
