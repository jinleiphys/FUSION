# Analysis of the intermediate-state contributions to neutrinoless double beta-minus decays

> A comprehensive analysis of the structure of the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of neutrinoless double beta-minus decays to the 0^+ ground and first excited states is performed in terms of the contributing multipole states in the intermediate nuclei of neutrinoless double beta-minus transitions. We concentrate on the transitions mediated by the light (l-NMEs) Majorana neutrinos. As nuclear model we use the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a realistic two-nucleon interaction based on the Bonn one-boson-exchange G matrix. In the computations we include the appropriate short-range correlations, nucleon form factors, higher-order nucleonic weak currents and restore the isospin symmetry by the isoscalar-isovector decomposition of the particle-particle proton-neutron interaction parameter g_{pp}.

# Analysis of the intermediate-state contributions to neutrinoless double beta-minus decays

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta-minus decays mediated by light Majorana neutrinos can be decomposed into contributions from specific multipole states in the intermediate nucleus, and that often only a few intermediate states dominate the strength for a given multipole, with the specific pattern depending on the nuclear mass region and whether the transition goes to the ground state or first excited 0+ state of the daughter nucleus.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a realistic two-nucleon interaction based on the Bonn one-boson-exchange G matrix, including short-range correlations, nucleon form factors, higher-order nucleonic weak currents, and isospin symmetry restoration via isoscalar-isovector decomposition of the particle-particle interaction parameter g_pp. They computed the multipole decomposition of the light-neutrino NMEs for ground-state-to-ground-state and ground-state-to-first-excited-state 0+ transitions for 11 nuclear systems, then extracted the most important multipole components and further decomposed them into contributions from individual intermediate states. A quenched axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.00 was used throughout.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge ground-state-to-ground-state decay: the 2- multipole contribution is 0.748 (79% of total 2- strength) from a state at 0.22 MeV; the 1+ multipole contribution is 0.668 (88% of total 1+ strength) with the largest single state at 4.94 MeV contributing 0.578
- For 96Zr ground-state-to-ground-state decay: the 1- multipole contribution is 0.728 (83% of total 1- strength) with the largest state at 5.27 MeV contributing 0.209; the 2- multipole contribution is 0.666 (96% of total 2- strength) with the largest state at 0.92 MeV contributing 0.498
- For 136Xe ground-state-to-ground-state decay: the 2+ multipole contribution is 0.238 (62% of total 2+ strength); the 1- multipole contribution is 0.238 (76% of total 1- strength) with the largest state at 6.65 MeV contributing 0.141
- For 116Cd ground-state-to-excited-state decay: the 1+ multipole contribution is 0.735 (81% of total 1+ strength) from a single state at 0.00 MeV (the intermediate ground state)
- For 124Sn ground-state-to-excited-state decay: the 1+ multipole contribution is 2.968 (94% of total 1+ strength) with the largest state at 3.31 MeV contributing 0.664; the 0+ multipole contribution is 1.322 (94% of total 0+ strength) with the largest state at 2.70 MeV contributing 0.667
- For 130Te ground-state-to-excited-state decay: the 1+ multipole contribution is 2.882 (95% of total 1+ strength) with the largest state at 15.07 MeV contributing 0.773; the 0+ multipole contribution is 1.351 (99.6% of total 0+ strength)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses (Hyvarinen 2015, Hyvarinen 2016) which performed multipole decomposition for ground-state decays and excited-state decays, respectively, by providing a more detailed scrutiny of individual intermediate-state contributions. The pnQRPA approach used here is the only nuclear model that avoids the closure approximation and retains contributions from individual intermediate states, unlike other models discussed in reviews by Vergados (2012), Suhonen (2012), Vogel (2012), Barea (2013), Neacsu (2014), and Engel (2015). The finding of single-state dominance for the 2- mode in 76Ge, 82Se, and 96Zr connects to an analysis by Ejiri (2014) of unique first-forbidden single beta transitions, which suggested that strong renormalization of axial-vector 2- matrix elements might also affect 0νββ NMEs. The results enable improved understanding of which intermediate nuclear states contribute most to 0νββ decay rates and may guide experimental searches and theoretical refinements.
