# Temperature dependence of nuclear spin-isospin response and beta decay in hot astrophysical environments

> A microscopic approach to the proton-neutron nuclear response is formulated in the finite-temperature relativistic nuclear field theory framework. The approach is based on the meson-nucleon Lagrangian of quantum hadrodynamics and advances the relativistic field theory for spin-isospin response beyond the finite-temperature random phase approximation. The dynamical contribution to the in-medium proton-neutron interaction amplitude is described in a parameter-free way by the coupling between the single nucleons and strongly-correlated particle-hole excitations (phonons) within the newly developed finite-temperature formalism. In this framework we investigate temperature dependence of the Gamow-Teller and spin dipole resonances in the closed-shell nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{78}$Ni, and $^{132}$Sn. Broader impacts of their temperature dependence are illustrated for the associated beta decay rates and lifetimes of $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn in hot astrophysical environments. We found a remarkable sensitivity of the beta decay rates to the enhanced low-energy spin-isospin strength at finite temperature, in particular, to the contribution of the first-forbidden transitions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the temperature dependence of nuclear spin-isospin response, computed via a novel finite-temperature relativistic time blocking approximation (FT-RTBA), reveals a remarkable sensitivity of beta decay rates to enhanced low-energy Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden strength at finite temperature, particularly for the waiting-point nuclei $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn in hot astrophysical environments.

## Method

The authors formulate a finite-temperature extension of relativistic nuclear field theory (RNFT) based on a meson-nucleon Lagrangian from quantum hadrodynamics. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the proton-neutron response using the finite-temperature time blocking approximation, which includes both a static meson-exchange interaction (pi, rho, and Landau-Migdal terms) and a dynamical particle-vibration coupling (PVC) amplitude. The temperature-dependent mean field is obtained from the finite-temperature relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory by minimizing the grand potential, and the phonon vertices and frequencies are extracted from the finite-temperature relativistic random phase approximation (FT-RRPA). The approach is applied to compute Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole strength distributions in the closed-shell nuclei $^{48}$Ca, $^{78}$Ni, and $^{132}$Sn at various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- For $^{132}$Sn at T=0, the three lowest GT states have reduced transition probabilities of $B_{GT}^{(1)}$=0.037, $B_{GT}^{(2)}$=0.607, and $B_{GT}^{(3)}$=0.147 units, with excitation energies of 0.01 MeV, 1.11 MeV, and 2.08 MeV, respectively.
- The experimentally observed states in $^{132}$Sn are at $E^{(1)}$ = 1.325 MeV with $B_{GT}^{(1)}$=0.364 and $E^{(2)}$ = 2.268 MeV with $B_{GT}^{(2)}$=0.0577.
- The low-energy GT strength distributions in $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn change notably between T = 0.5 MeV and T = 0.75 MeV, indicating increased beta instability starting from these temperatures.
- The smearing parameter used for strength distributions is $\Delta$ = 200 keV for broad features and $\Delta$ = 20 keV for fine structure calculations.
- The neutron-proton mass splitting used is $M_{np} = 1.293$ MeV.
- The beta decay half-lives are computed for electron density $\text{lg}(\rho Y_e)$ = 7 from the Fuller, Fowler and Newman (FFN) temperature-density grid.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature relativistic nuclear field theory (RNFT) framework, previously developed by Litvinova, Ring, Tselyaev, and others for neutral and charge-exchange channels, to finite temperatures. It goes beyond the finite-temperature random phase approximation (FT-RRPA) and finite-temperature quasiparticle RPA (FT-QRPA) approaches used by Minato (2009), Dzhioev (2010), and Niu (2011), which are limited to the one-fermion loop approximation and cannot account for retardation effects from particle-vibration coupling. The results are consistent with the thermal unblocking mechanisms described by Lacroix, Chomaz, and Ayik (1998, 2000) and with model analyses by Sokolov (1997) and Stoyanov (2004). The temperature-dependent strength functions enable more reliable predictions of beta decay rates for r-process waiting-point nuclei like $^{78}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn in core-collapse supernovae and neutron star merger environments, where the temperature dependence of nuclear transitions must be considered alongside environmental thermal effects.
