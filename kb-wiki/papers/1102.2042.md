# Ab initio many-body calculations of deuteron-4He scattering and 6Li states

> We extend the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) to projectile-target binary-cluster states where the projectile is a deuteron. We discuss the formalism in detail and give algebraic expressions for the integration kernels. Using a soft similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, we calculate deuteron-4He scattering and investigate 6Li bound and unbound states. Virtual three-body breakup effects are obtained in an approximated way by including excited pseudo-states of the deuteron in the calculation. We compare our results to experiment and to a standard NCSM calculation for 6Li.

## Key claim

The authors extend the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) to treat deuteron-nucleus scattering, but virtual three-body breakup effects (neutron-proton-nucleus continuum) are only approximated by including excited deuteron pseudo-states rather than treated exactly.

## Method

The NCSM/RGM formalism was generalized from single-nucleon projectile to two-nucleon (deuteron) projectile by deriving algebraic expressions for the norm and Hamiltonian integration kernels, including one-nucleon-exchange, two-nucleon-exchange, and potential terms that depend on one-, two-, and three-body density matrix elements of the target nucleus. For the specific case of deuteron-4He, the calculation used a similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potential with evolution parameter Λ=1.5 fm⁻¹, and the localized parts of the kernels were expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis up to Nmax=12 excitations. The coupled-channel equations were solved using the microscopic R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh.

## Key numbers

- Calculated 4He ground state energy: -28.22 MeV (experiment: -28.30 MeV) using SRG-N3LO potential with Λ=1.5 fm⁻¹
- Calculated 6Li ground state energy from NCSM/RGM: -32.25 MeV; from standard NCSM: -32.87 MeV (experiment: -31.99 MeV)
- NCSM/RGM calculation limited to deuteron ground state only binds 6Li by about 200 keV, compared to the NCSM result of 2.4 MeV binding relative to the d+α threshold using the same Hamiltonian
- Including 7 deuteron pseudo-states in the ³S₁-³D₁ channel and 5 in the ³D₂ channel, the NCSM/RGM ground state energy converges to within 30 keV of the Nmax=9 result
- The 6Li NCSM/RGM ground state is about 600 keV (approximately 2%) less bound than the full NCSM calculation

## Context

This work extends the NCSM/RGM approach previously applied to single-nucleon projectiles (n-³H, n-⁴He, p-³,⁴He, n-¹⁰Be, n-⁷Li, p-⁷Be, and nucleon scattering on ¹²C and ¹⁶O) to the case of a two-nucleon projectile. Earlier binary-cluster RGM studies of deuteron-4He scattering (Thompson 1973, Kanada 1982, 1985) did not use accurate nucleon-nucleon interactions or many-body cluster wave functions from the same Hamiltonian. The results enable future extension to three-cluster (n-p-α) basis states for proper treatment of deuteron breakup, and the formalism is general for any deuteron-nucleus system.
