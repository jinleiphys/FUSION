# The p(d,p)d and p(d,p)pn reactions as a tool for the study of the short range internal structure of the deuteron

> In recent time the deuteron structure at short distances is often treated from the point of view nonnucleonic degrees of freedom. In this paper the measurements of T-odd polarization observables using tensor polarized deuteron beam and polarized proton target or proton polarimeter are proposed to search the quark configurations inside the deuteron.

## Key claim

Measurements of T-odd polarization observables (tensor-vector spin correlations and polarization transfer coefficients) in the p(d,p)d and p(d,p)pn reactions can serve as a clear signature of exotic 6-quark configurations inside the deuteron, provided these observables show large values at internal momenta above 600 MeV/c, where conventional one-nucleon-exchange mechanisms contribute negligibly.

## Method

The author calculates the matrix elements for deuteron breakup in collinear geometry (d + p → p(0°) + p(180°) + n) and for deuteron-proton backward elastic scattering (d + p → p + d) using a hybrid deuteron wave function that adds a complex 6-quark admixture to the standard S and D components from NN potentials (Paris, Reid Soft Core, and Bonn C). The T-odd observables C_{0,SL,N,0} (polarization transfer from tensor-polarized deuteron to proton) and C_{N,SL,0,0} (tensor-vector spin correlation) are derived analytically and evaluated numerically for two different models of the 6-quark admixture, with parameters obtained from fits to existing inclusive breakup data on momentum density, tensor analyzing power T_{20}, and polarization transfer coefficient κ₀.

## Key numbers

* Probability of the 6-quark admixture found to be 3-4% from fits to inclusive breakup data using Paris, RSC, and Bonn C deuteron wave functions.
* Relative phase χ between the 6-quark component and the S-wave of the standard deuteron wave function is approximately 40° for RSC and Paris potentials, and 55° for Bonn C potential.
* Radius of the 6-quark configuration r ≈ 0.6 fm for all used deuteron wave functions.
* Polarization transfer coefficient C_{0,SL,N,0} predicted to reach about -1 at internal momentum k ∼ 600 MeV/c, independent of initial energy.
* Spin correlation C_{N,SL,0,0} in dp backward elastic scattering predicted to reach a minimum of approximately -0.7 to -0.8 at k ∼ 400 MeV/c.
* Probability of the D-wave from s⁴p² six-quark configurations found to be about 0.5% (a small part of the total D-wave probability of ∼6%).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the deuteron, following observations that tensor analyzing power T_{20} in inclusive deuteron breakup and dp backward elastic scattering reaches negative values of -0.3 to -0.4 at high internal momenta, incompatible with predictions using standard nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, RSC, Bonn). The paper builds on the hybrid deuteron wave function approach of Kobushkin and Vizireva (1990) and the non-nucleon component model of Lykasov and Efremov (1990s), which successfully described T_{20} and κ₀ data up to k ∼ 1 GeV/c. The proposed T-odd observables offer a new experimental test that could distinguish between different models of 6-quark configurations and potentially reveal dibaryon resonances, with measurements feasible at COSY (ANKE facility) or JINR using existing polarized beam and target techniques.
