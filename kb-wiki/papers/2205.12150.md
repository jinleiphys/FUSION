# Extending a Scaling Equation of State to QCD

> Whether Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) exhibits a phase transition at finite temperature and density is an open question. It is important for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy ion collisions and neutron star mergers. Lattice QCD simulations have definitively shown that the transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons is a crossover when the baryon chemical potential is zero or small. We combine the parametric scaling equation of state, usually associated with the 3D Ising model, with a background equation of state based on a smooth crossover from hadrons to quarks and gluons. Comparison to experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan II at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider or in heavy ion experiments at other accelerators may allow the critical exponents and amplitudes in the scaling equation of state to be determined for QCD if a critical point exists.

## Key claim
The authors propose a method to embed a critical point (with 3D Ising universality-class exponents) into a smooth QCD equation of state, but emphasize that this construction only allows determination of critical exponents and amplitudes from experimental data *if* a critical point actually exists in QCD.

## Method
The authors combine the Schofield parametric scaling equation of state (using baryon chemical potential and density as conjugate variables, rather than the Ising model magnetic field and magnetization) with a smooth background equation of state that interpolates between a hadron resonance gas and perturbative QCD. The critical contribution is multiplied by a Gaussian-like window function centered on the coexistence curve to suppress it away from the critical region, and the background equation of state is constructed to match lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Critical exponents taken as universal 3D Ising values: β = 0.3264, γ = 1.2371, α ≈ 0.1101, δ ≈ 4.7901
- For illustration, the critical point is placed at T_c = 120 MeV and μ_c = 750 MeV
- The critical density for the illustrative parameters is n_c = 1.31 fm^{-3}
- The background switching function parameters are T_s = 145.33 MeV and μ_s = 3π T_s
- The illustrative parameters include m_0 = 0.5, h_0 = 0.2, n_0 = 0.1 n_c, P_0 = 0.05 P_c, c_* = 0.3
- The ratio of critical amplitudes for susceptibility is C_+/C_- = 4.764 and for heat capacity is A_+/A_- = 0.5302

## Context
This work extends the parametric scaling equation of state approach of Guida and collaborators to QCD, improving upon the constructions of Refs. [attract] and [Taylor1] by working directly in chemical potential and density variables rather than mapping from Ising model variables. The method is complementary to the approach of Ref. [ourletter] and is designed to enable hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions (particularly Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC) and neutron star mergers to search for evidence of a QCD critical point.
