# Medium Induced Transverse Momentum Broadening in Hard Processes

> Using deep inelastic scattering on a large nucleus as an example, we consider the transverse momentum broadening of partons in hard processes in the presence of medium. We find that one can factorize the vacuum radiation contribution and medium related $P_T$ broadening effects into the Sudakov factor and medium dependent distributions, respectively. Our derivations can be generalized to other hard processes, such as dijet productions, which can be used as a probe to measure the medium $P_T$ broadening effects in heavy ion collisions when Sudakov effects are not overwhelming.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in hard processes such as deep inelastic scattering on a large nucleus, the transverse momentum broadening of partons can be factorized into a vacuum Sudakov contribution and medium-dependent effects, but this factorization holds specifically when the Sudakov effects are not overwhelming compared to the medium-induced broadening.

## Method

The authors analyze forward jet production in deep inelastic scattering on a large nucleus, considering two regimes: where the jet formation time is much less than the nuclear medium length, and where it is much greater. They derive the transverse momentum spectrum using a dipole model formalism, incorporating multiple scattering via the transport coefficient $\hat q$, Sudakov double-logarithmic radiation from the hard scattering, and medium-induced radiative corrections to $\hat q$. The analysis includes fixed coupling and running coupling treatments, with resummation of double-logarithmic corrections to $\hat q$ via evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- The Sudakov factor is given by $\mathcal{E}_{\textrm{Sud}} = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \ln^2(Q^2 x_\perp^2)$, where $Q^2$ is the photon virtuality and $x_\perp$ is the dipole transverse size.
- For dijet production at the LHC with jet transverse momentum $P_T \sim 100$ GeV, the Sudakov broadening is $\langle \triangle P_T^2\rangle \sim 100$ GeV$^2$, while the medium-induced broadening is $\langle \triangle P_T^2\rangle \sim \hat q L \sim 10$ GeV$^2$.
- The transport coefficient is defined as $\hat q = \frac{C_F}{N_c} \frac{4\pi^2 \alpha_s N_c}{N_c^2-1} \rho \, xG(x)$, where $\rho$ is nucleon density and $xG$ is the gluon distribution.
- For small-$x$ forward jet production with fixed coupling, the transverse momentum spectrum in the scaling region (no Sudakov effects) behaves as $\frac{dN}{d^2b d^2 k_\perp} \propto (Q_s^2/Q^2)^{1-\lambda_0}$ with $Q_s$ the saturation scale and $\lambda_0$ the anomalous dimension.
- The radiative correction to $\hat q$ in the double-logarithmic limit yields $\hat{q}_t = \hat{q} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_s}K} I_1(2\sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_s}K)$ for $0 \leq K \leq \tau$, where $K = \ln(1/(\hat q r_0 x_\perp^2))$, $\tau = \ln(L/r_0)$, and $\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c/\pi$.

## Context

This work extends the Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS) approach to transverse momentum broadening by incorporating Sudakov effects alongside medium-induced broadening, building on prior studies of Sudakov resummation in back-to-back dijet correlations by Mueller (2012, 2013) and Sun (2014). The formalism enables quantitative predictions for dijet angular correlations in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the interplay between Sudakov and medium effects determines whether medium modifications are observable; the authors note that at LHC energies with $P_T \sim 100$ GeV, Sudakov effects dominate and mask medium broadening, while at RHIC with $P_T \sim 35$ GeV, medium effects become measurable.
