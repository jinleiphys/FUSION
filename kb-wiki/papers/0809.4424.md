# Flavor asymmetry of the nucleon

> The flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea is discussed in an unquenched quark model for baryons in which the effects of quark-antiquark pairs (u anti-u, d anti-d and s anti-s) are taken into account in an explicit form. The inclusion of q anti-q pairs leads automatically to an excess of anti-d over anti-u quarks in the proton, in agreement with experimental data.

# Flavor asymmetry of the nucleon

## Key claim

The inclusion of quark-antiquark pairs ($u\bar{u}$, $d\bar{d}$, $s\bar{s}$) in an unquenched quark model leads automatically to an excess of $\bar{d}$ over $\bar{u}$ quarks in the proton, in qualitative agreement with experimental data from the New Muon Collaboration (NMC), but the calculation uses parameters taken from the literature with no attempt to optimize them.

## Method

The authors extend the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur (1996) by generalizing the formalism to include $u\bar{u}$ and $d\bar{d}$ loops in addition to $s\bar{s}$ loops, using a $^{3}P_0$ pair-creation operator. They employ group-theoretical techniques to handle permutation symmetry between identical quarks and an algorithm to generate a complete set of intermediate baryon-meson states up to four oscillator shells, using harmonic oscillator wave functions with a baryon oscillator parameter of 0.32 GeV and a meson oscillator parameter of 0.40 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Proton flavor asymmetry $A_{\rm asym}(p) = N_{\bar{d}} - N_{\bar{u}} = 0.21$, corresponding to a Gottfried integral $S_G = 0.19$.
- Neutron flavor asymmetry $A_{\rm asym}(n) = N_{\bar{u}} - N_{\bar{d}} = 0.21$ from isospin symmetry.
- Experimental NMC value for the Gottfried integral: $S_G = 0.2281 \pm 0.0065$ at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 4$ (GeV/c)$^2$ over $0.004 \leq x \leq 0.8$.
- Experimental HERMES value: $S_G = 0.23 \pm 0.02$ at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 2.3$ (GeV/c)$^2$ over $0.020 < x < 0.30$.
- Experimental E866/NuSea value: $S_G = 0.255 \pm 0.008$ at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 54$ (GeV/c)$^2$ over $0.015 < x < 0.35$.
- Intrinsic pair creation strength $\gamma_0 = 2.60$ from strong decays of baryons.
- Width of Gaussian quark-antiquark creation vertex function $r_q = 0.30$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube breaking model of Geiger and Isgur (1996), who studied $s\bar{s}$ loops in the proton and emphasized the need to include $u\bar{u}$ and $d\bar{d}$ loops to address the violation of the Gottfried sum rule. The unquenched quark model provides a mechanism for flavor asymmetry that complements earlier explanations based on the pion cloud in the cloudy bag model by Thomas (1983). The results enable systematic future studies of electromagnetic and strong couplings, elastic and transition form factors of baryon resonances, and their sea quark content and flavor decomposition.
