# Novel Cross Section Ratios as Possible Signals of Saturation in UPCs

> We propose new cross section ratios in ultra-peripheral A$+$A and $p+$A collisions (UPCs) as a potential experimental signal of saturation physics. We consider the ratio $R_1$ of elastic vector meson photoproduction cross section to the inclusive hadron or jet photoproduction cross section. The ratio can be measured in the $γ+$A and $γ+ p$ collisions taking place in the UPCs. We label the ratios $R_1 ({\rm A})$ and $R_1 (p)$, respectively. Constructing the double ratio $R_{\rm{UPC}} ({\rm A}) = R_1 ({\rm A})/R_1 (p)$, and performing a small-$x$ calculation both in the quasi-classical approximation and by including small-$x$ evolution, we observe that $R_{\rm{UPC}} ({\rm A})$ exhibits a markedly different dependence on the nuclear atomic number inside and outside the saturation region. This result indicates that $R_{\rm{UPC}} ({\rm A})$ and $R_1 ({\rm A})$ measurements in UPCs may help in the experimental searches for the evidence of saturation physics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the double ratio \(R_{\rm{UPC}}(A) = R_1(A)/R_1(p)\), constructed from elastic vector meson photoproduction and inclusive hadron or jet photoproduction cross sections in ultra-peripheral collisions, exhibits a markedly different dependence on the nuclear atomic number \(A\) inside versus outside the saturation region, potentially serving as an experimental signal of saturation physics even though \(R_{\rm{UPC}}\) is not necessarily greater than one in the saturation picture.

## Method

The authors perform leading-order small-\(x\) calculations of the elastic vector meson photoproduction cross section and the inclusive hadron or jet photoproduction cross section within the saturation/color glass condensate formalism, using the dipole scattering amplitude. They evaluate the double ratio \(R_{\rm{UPC}}\) first in the quasi-classical Glauber-Gribov-Mueller/McLerran-Venugopalan approximation and then by including small-\(x\) evolution via the geometric scaling solution of the leading-order Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, analyzing the \(A\)-dependence for both \(\rho\) and \(J/\psi\) meson production.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale grows approximately as \(Q_s^2 \propto A^{1/3} \, (1/x)^\lambda\) with the power \(\lambda \approx 0.3\).
* In ultra-peripheral collisions, the photon virtuality \(Q^2\) is around \(10^{-4}-10^{-3}~\rm{GeV^2}\) and the typical photon transverse momentum is \(\sim 20-40~\rm{MeV/c}\).
* The center-of-mass energy between the photon and per nucleon in the nucleus, \(W_{\gamma*N}\), ranges from less than 10 GeV (RHIC) to \(\sim 1~\rm{TeV}\) (LHC), corresponding to momentum fraction \(x\) in the range from \(10^{-1}\) to \(10^{-5}\).
* The diffractive over total DIS cross section ratio in electron-proton collisions at HERA is found to be \(\sim 13\%\) (Abramowicz, 1998).

## Context

This work extends the concept of the double ratio of diffractive to total DIS cross sections proposed for the Electron-Ion Collider (Accardi, 2012) to ultra-peripheral collisions at RHIC and the LHC, where the same double ratio cannot be directly measured. The proposed observable \(R_{\rm{UPC}}\) replaces the diffractive DIS cross section with elastic vector meson photoproduction and the total DIS cross section with inclusive hadron or jet photoproduction, and its \(A\)-dependence is predicted to differ between saturation calculations and the leading-twist nuclear shadowing model (Frankfurt, 2003; Frankfurt, 2011). The measurements could lay groundwork for the future EIC double ratio measurement and provide complementary information needed for saturation discovery.
