# Non-resonant kaon pair production and medium effects in proton-nucleus collisions

> We study the non-resonant (non-$ϕ$) production of $K^+K^-$ pairs by protons of 2.83 GeV kinetic energy on C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets within the collision model, based on the nuclear spectral function, for incoherent primary proton--nucleon and secondary pion--nucleon creation processes. The model takes into account the initial proton and final kaon absorption, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion as well as nuclear mean-field potential effects on these processes. We calculate the antikaon momentum dependences of the exclusive absolute and relative $K^+K^-$ pair yields in the acceptance window of the ANKE magnetic spectrometer, used in a recent experiment performed at COSY, within the different scenarios for the antikaon-nucleus optical potential. We demonstrate that the above observables are strongly sensitive to this potential. Therefore, they can be useful to help determine the $K^-$ optical potential from the direct comparison of the results of our calculations with the data from the respective ANKE-at-COSY experiment. We also show that the pion--nucleon production channels dominate in the low-momentum $K^-$, $K^+$ production in the considered kinematics and, hence, they have to be accounted for in the analysis of these data.

## Key claim

The exclusive absolute and relative yields of non-resonant (non-$\phi$) $K^+K^-$ pairs produced in proton-nucleus collisions at 2.83 GeV are strongly sensitive to the antikaon-nucleus optical potential, but the pion-nucleon production channels dominate in the low-momentum $K^-$, $K^+$ production and must be accounted for in the analysis of the ANKE-at-COSY data.

## Method

The authors employed a collision model based on the nuclear spectral function to calculate incoherent primary proton-nucleon and secondary pion-nucleon $K^+K^-$ creation processes on C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets. The model incorporates initial proton and final kaon absorption, target nucleon binding and Fermi motion, and nuclear mean-field potential effects, using four different scenarios for the $K^-$ nuclear potential depth at saturation density: 0, -60, -126, and -180 MeV. The exclusive differential cross sections were computed within the acceptance window of the ANKE magnetic spectrometer (polar angles $0^{\circ} \le \theta_{K^{\pm}} \le 12^{\circ}$, $K^+$ momenta 0.2–0.6 GeV/c, $K^+K^-$ invariant mass below 1.005 GeV) and averaged over the experimental phase space.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton kinetic energy: 2.83 GeV on C, Cu, Ag, and Au targets.
- $K^+$ nuclear potential depth at saturation density: $U_{K^+}^0 = 22$ MeV.
- $K^-$ nuclear potential depth options at saturation density: $U_{K^-}^0 = 0$, -60, -126, and -180 MeV.
- Coulomb potential at $R_{1/2}$ for kaons: approximately 3.5, 9.7, 12.3, and 16.6 MeV for C, Cu, Ag, and Au nuclei, respectively.
- Ratio of free total cross sections for $K^+K^-$ production in $pn$ vs $pp$ interactions: 4.5, derived from a one-pion-exchange model.
- $K^+$ momentum acceptance window: 0.2 GeV/c $\le p_{K^+} \le$ 0.6 GeV/c.
- $K^+K^-$ invariant mass cut: $IM(K^+K^-) \le 1.005$ GeV.
- Polar angular acceptance for both $K^+$ and $K^-$: $0^{\circ} \le \theta_{K^{\pm}} \le 12^{\circ}$.
- $K^-$ absorption by two-nucleon mechanism yields corrections within 1–3% for gold nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium antikaon properties from inclusive measurements (e.g., KaoS data analyzed with BUU transport model by Hartnack et al. (2006), and ITEP data analyzed by Paryev (2004)) to exclusive $K^+K^-$ pair production, providing a tool to determine the $K^-$ optical potential. The calculations are designed for direct comparison with the ANKE-at-COSY experiment (Hartmann et al., 2014) and address the unresolved tension between shallow potentials (about -50 to -80 MeV from chiral coupled-channel calculations) and deep potentials (about -200 MeV from kaonic atom fits). The results enable extraction of the antikaon-nucleus optical potential from the forthcoming ANKE data on non-resonant $K^+K^-$ pair yields.
