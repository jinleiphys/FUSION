# Spin dependent ab initio nonlocal No-Core Shell-Model One-Body Densities

> Constructing microscopic effective interactions (`optical potentials') for nucleon-nucleus (NA) elastic scattering requires in first order off-shell nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering amplitudes between the projectile and the struck target nucleon and nonlocal one-body density matrices. While the NN amplitudes and the {\it ab intio} no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations always contain the full spin structure of the NN problem, one-body density matrices used in traditional microscopic folding potential neglect spin contributions inherent in the one-body density matrix. Here we derive and show the expectation values of the spin-orbit contribution of the struck nucleon with respect to the rest of the nucleus for $^{4}$He, $^{6}$He, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O and compare them with the scalar one-body density matrix.

## Key claim

The paper derives and presents the expectation values of the spin-orbit contribution of the struck target nucleon with respect to the rest of the nucleus for several light nuclei, showing that while the spin-orbit density is at least two orders of magnitude smaller than the scalar one-body density, it may still produce visible effects on nucleon-nucleus scattering observables because the scalar density's maximum is far off-shell under on-shell scattering conditions.

## Method

The authors extract scalar (K_s=0) and spin-dependent (K_s=1) nonlocal one-body density matrices from ab initio no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations, using the NNLO_opt interaction. They evaluate the expectation value of the scalar product of the struck nucleon's spin with the normal to the scattering plane (the spin-orbit operator) in momentum space, expressed in terms of momentum transfer q and total momentum K, and compare it to the scalar one-body density for the neutron distributions of 4He, 6He, 12C, and 16O.

## Key numbers

- For 4He, the spin-orbit contribution is at least three orders of magnitude smaller than the scalar part; its maximum strength is at about q = 1.5 fm^-1 and 2K = 1.5 fm^-1.
- For 6He, the maximum value of the spin-orbit contribution is only two orders of magnitude smaller than the scalar contribution, with maximum strength at about q = 0.7 fm^-1 and 2K = 0.7 fm^-1.
- For 12C, the maximum spin-orbit contribution is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the scalar density, at about q = 1 fm^-1 and 2K = 1 fm^-1.
- For 16O, the maximum spin-orbit contribution is negative and about two orders of magnitude smaller than the scalar one, at about q = 0.7 fm^-1 and 2K = 0.7 fm^-1.
- For 4He with N_max=10, the maximum spin-orbit contribution is about 4.8 x 10^-4; with N_max=18 it increases slightly to 4.9 x 10^-4.
- For 6He, the spin-orbit strength changes by about 15% (from 4.0 x 10^-2 to 4.6 x 10^-2) when increasing N_max from 10 to 18.

## Context

This work extends the traditional first-order folding potentials developed in the 1990s, which assumed spin-saturation and were most applicable to closed-shell nuclei, by incorporating spin-dependent one-body densities from ab initio NCSM calculations. It builds on a first step by Orazbayev (2013) that used a simple model density, and on the consistent ab initio folding approach of Burrows (2018) and Gennari (2017). The results enable future calculations of nucleon-nucleus scattering that include the Wolfenstein amplitudes C and M in the folding effective interaction, potentially adding contributions to the central potential (through C) and the spin-orbit potential (through M) for proton scattering from spin-zero nuclei.
