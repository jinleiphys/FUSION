# The Ridge from the BFKL evolution and beyond

> We show that the long-range rapidity correlations between the produced charged-hadron pairs from two BFKL parton showers generate considerable azimuthal angle correlations. These correlations have no 1/N_c suppression. The effect of gluon saturation on these correlations are discussed and we show that it is important. We show that a pronounced ridge-like structure emerges by going from the BFKL to the saturation region. We show that the ridge structure at high-energy proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions has the same origin and its main feature can be understood due to initial-state effects. Although the effects of final-state interactions in the latter case can be non-negligible.

## Key claim
The authors show that long-range rapidity correlations between charged-hadron pairs produced from two BFKL parton showers generate azimuthal angle correlations that have no 1/N_c suppression, and that a pronounced ridge-like structure emerges when going from the BFKL region to the saturation region, with the same origin in both proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions primarily due to initial-state effects, although final-state interactions in the latter case can be non-negligible.

## Method
The authors compute double inclusive gluon production cross-sections using Mueller diagrams for two parton showers within the BFKL Pomeron calculus, expanding the BFKL kernel and unintegrated gluon densities to extract azimuthal correlations. They derive the two-particle correlation function R in the leading log-s approximation, then extend the formalism to the saturation region by replacing average transverse momenta with the saturation scale Q_s. Estimates are made for both the BFKL kinematic region (using a quasi-eikonal approximation for the Pomeron-hadron amplitude) and the saturation region.

## Key numbers
- The correlation function R(Δφ; y1, y2) for high-multiplicity events in the saturation region is given by R = [n̄(n̄-1)/(2n̄²)] {1 + ½(2 + cos(2Δφ))} - 1, where n̄ is the relative average number of Pomeron parton showers.
- In the BFKL region, the correlation function becomes R = [n̄(n̄-1)/(2n̄²)] {1 + (m⁴/(30 Q_s⁴))(2 + cos(2Δφ))} - 1, with m² = 0.8 GeV² from HERA diffractive vector meson data or m² = 1.6 GeV² from CDF double jet data.
- The average ⟨Q_T²⟩ = m²/6 and ⟨Q_T⁴⟩ = m²/15 from the dipole form factor g_{Pp}(Q_T) = 1/(1 + Q_T²/m²)².
- The ridge was observed at the LHC in √s = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions and in √s = 7 TeV pp collisions for high multiplicity N ≥ 90 event selections.
- The ridge was previously seen at RHIC in central Cu+Cu collisions at √s = 62.4 GeV and in Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of the ridge phenomenon observed by the CMS collaboration (2010) in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV and previously at RHIC in Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions. It introduces a new source of long-range azimuthal correlations from two BFKL parton showers, complementing earlier CGC-based approaches by Kovner and Lublinsky (2010) and arguments in Ref. [NEWCORPP] about initial-state effects. The paper enables a unified description of the ridge in both pp and AA collisions based on initial-state effects, while noting that final-state interactions in AA collisions may also contribute.
