# Probing Proton Strangeness with Time-Like Virtual Compton Scattering

> We document that p(gamma,e+e-)p measurements will yield new, important information about the off-shell time-like nucleon form factors, especially in the phi meson region (q^2 = M^2_{phi}) governing the phi N couplings g^{V,T}_{ϕN N}. Calculations for p(gamma,e+e-)p, utilizing vector meson dominance, predict measurable phi enhancements at high |t| compared to the expected phi background production from pi, eta and Pomeron exchange. The phi form factor contribution generates a novel experimental signature for OZI violation and the proton strangeness content. The phi N couplings are determined independently from a combined analysis of the neutron electric form factor and recent high |t| phi photoproduction. The pi, eta and Pomeron transition form factors are also predicted and the observed pi and eta transition moments are reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that measurements of the reaction p(gamma,e+e-)p will yield new information about off-shell time-like nucleon form factors, especially in the phi meson region (q^2 = M^2_phi), and that the phi form factor contribution generates a novel experimental signature for OZI violation and the proton strangeness content. The authors hedge that these predictions depend on the validity of vector meson dominance (VMD) and that similar findings will occur for other models, but the specific dual-peak signature and magnitude follows from VMD only.

## Method

The authors used a Quantum Hadrodynamic (QHD) model with vector meson dominance (VMD) to calculate cross sections for time-like virtual Compton scattering (TVCS), p(gamma,e+e-)p. They computed hadronic current tensors from s, t, and u channel diagrams involving proton propagation, Pomeron exchange, and pseudoscalar meson (pi, eta) exchange, with the phi N couplings determined from a combined analysis of the neutron electric form factor and recent high |t| phi photoproduction data. The model also incorporated Regge theory for the Pomeron trajectory and a self-energy correction for the off-shell proton propagator.

## Key numbers

- The phi N vector coupling extracted is g^V_phiNN = 1.3, with tensor coupling g^T_phiNN = 2.3 and tensor moment kappa^T_phi = 1.8
- The ratio g^2_phiNN / g^2_omegaNN = 0.14, compared to the OZI prediction of 0.0042 f (where f is a kinematic quantity of order unity)
- The predicted cross section ratio R = sigma(q^2 = M^2_phi) / sigma(q^2 = M^2_omega) = 0.14 f, over an order of magnitude larger than the OZI prediction
- The Pomeron trajectory is alpha(t) = 0.999 + 0.27 GeV^{-2} t, with Pomeron mass M_P = 1.7 GeV and slope beta = 0.27 GeV^{-2}
- The pseudoscalar t-channel form factor cutoff parameter is lambda = 0.7 GeV
- The off-shell proton self-energy parameter is alpha_off = 1.29
- VMD predicts transition moments kappa_pigamma_gamma = 0.30, kappa_etagamma_gamma = 0.27, kappa_Pgamma_gamma = 0.11, in agreement with observed values kappa_pigamma_gamma = 0.27 and kappa_etagamma_gamma = 0.26
- The vector meson-nucleon couplings are C_rho(N) = 0.4, C_omega(N) = 0.2, C_phi(N) = -0.1
- The phi decay constant is f_phi = -13.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of p(pi-,e+e-)n (Cotanch and Williams, 2000) to electromagnetic facilities, and uses VMD as developed by Gari and Krumpelmann (1992) and extended by Williams, Krusche, and collaborators. The paper addresses the hidden strangeness in the nucleon, which has been observed in p-pbar annihilation experiments at LEAR (ASTERIX, Crystal Barrel, OBELIX collaborations), deep inelastic muon-proton scattering (EMC collaboration), elastic neutrino-proton scattering at BNL (Ahrens, 1987), and pi-N sigma term measurements (Donoghue, 1987). The results enable direct extraction of phi N couplings from TVCS experiments and provide a means to probe the time-like nucleon form factors below q^2 = 4 M_N^2, a region currently unmeasured.
