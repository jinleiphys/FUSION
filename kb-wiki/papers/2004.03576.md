# Non-perturbative renormalization scheme for the CP-odd three-gluon operator

> We define a regularization-independent momentum-subtraction scheme for the $CP$-odd three-gluon operator at dimension six. This operator appears in effective field theories for heavy physics beyond the Standard Model, describing the indirect effect of new sources of $CP$-violation at low energies. In a hadronic context, it induces permanent electric dipole moments. The hadronic matrix elements of the three-gluon operator are non-perturbative objects that should ideally be evaluated with lattice QCD. We define a non-perturbative renormalization scheme that can be implemented on the lattice and we compute the scheme transformation to $\overline{\text{MS}}$ at one loop. Our calculation can be used as an interface to future lattice-QCD calculations of the matrix elements of the three-gluon operator, in order to obtain theoretically robust constraints on physics beyond the Standard Model from measurements of the neutron electric dipole moment.

## Key claim

The paper defines a regularization-independent momentum-subtraction (RI-$\tilde{\text{S}}$MOM) scheme for the $CP$-odd three-gluon operator at dimension six, which can be implemented on the lattice, and computes the one-loop matching to the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme, but only under the condition that the lattice regulator preserves chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct the complete basis of operators up to dimension six that can mix with the $CP$-odd three-gluon operator under renormalization, using chiral symmetry and BRST symmetry constraints to identify gauge-invariant operators, operators that vanish by equations of motion (class IIa), and gauge-variant nuisance operators (class IIb). They then define a regularization-independent momentum-subtraction (RI-$\tilde{\text{S}}$MOM) scheme for the three-gluon operator and compute the one-loop conversion factor to the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The operator basis at dimension six contains 10 physical operators ($\mathcal{O}_1^{(6)}$ through $\mathcal{O}_{10}^{(6)}$), 10 class-IIa nuisance operators ($\mathcal{N}_1^{(6)}$ through $\mathcal{N}_{10}^{(6)}$), and 10 class-IIb nuisance operators ($\mathcal{N}_{11}^{(6)}$ through $\mathcal{N}_{20}^{(6)}$)
- At dimension four, there are 2 physical operators ($\mathcal{O}_1^{(4)}$, $\mathcal{O}_2^{(4)}$) and 1 nuisance operator ($\mathcal{N}_1^{(4)}$)
- At dimension five, there is 1 physical operator ($\mathcal{O}_1^{(5)}$)
- The number of quark flavors used in the basis construction is $N_f = 3$

## Context

This work extends the program of non-perturbative renormalization for $CP$-violating operators relevant to electric dipole moment calculations, complementing prior work on the quark chromoelectric dipole moment (qCEDM) by Bhattacharya et al. (2015). The defined scheme provides an interface between future lattice QCD calculations of the three-gluon operator matrix elements and the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme used in phenomenological analyses, enabling theoretically robust constraints on beyond-Standard-Model $CP$ violation from neutron electric dipole moment measurements.
