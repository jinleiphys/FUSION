# Difference in peripherality of the inclusive (p, p'x) and (d, d'x) reactions and its implications for phenomenological reaction model

> Previous studies have revealed the importance of introducing surface correction into a phenomenological model for inclusive (n, n'x) and (p, p'x) reactions. These findings have contributed significantly to the improvement of nuclear data evaluation. However, the necessity for the surface correction in an inclusive (d, d'x) reaction has hardly been investigated. The purpose of this study is to investigate the difference in the peripherality of the (p, p'x) and (d, d'x) reactions by a theoretical analysis using a quantum mechanical model, and to obtain a theoretical basis on the surface correction in the (d, d'x) reaction. The energy spectra and their radial distributions for the (p, p'x) and (d, d'x) reactions are calculated by the one-step semiclassical distorted wave model. The radial distribution of the energy spectra for the (d, d'x) reaction is shifted toward the outer region of the nucleus compared to the (p, p'x) reaction. Based on this finding, we consider a larger surface correction into a phenomenological model for the (d, d'x) reaction than that for the (p, p'x) reaction, and calculated values reproduce the experimental (d, d'x) spectra well. The peripherality of the (d, d'x) reaction is more prominent than that of the (p, p'x) reaction. The stronger surface correction thus should be introduced for the (d, d'x) reaction than for the (p, p'x) reaction.

## Key claim

The inclusive (d,d'x) reaction exhibits stronger peripherality (surface localization) than the (p,p'x) reaction, and therefore a stronger surface correction (smaller finite well depth V) should be introduced into phenomenological models for deuteron-induced reactions than for proton-induced reactions, specifically with an optimized well depth of 10 MeV for deuterons versus 17-25 MeV for protons.

## Method

The authors calculated energy spectra and radial distributions for inclusive (p,p'x) and (d,d'x) reactions using the one-step semiclassical distorted wave (SCDW) model, which represents the inclusive cross section as an incoherent integral over collision points. They compared calculations using local Fermi gas (LFG) and uniform Fermi gas (FG) to isolate surface contributions, then applied the findings to the phenomenological Kalbach model by adjusting the finite well depth parameter V that controls the surface correction.

## Key numbers

- For the (d,d'x) reaction on 58Ni at 80 MeV, the optimized finite well depth V was 10 MeV, compared to 17 MeV proposed for (p,p'x) reactions in Kalbach (2000) and approximately 25 MeV from the Koning (2004) parameterization for proton-induced reactions
- The radial peak position for the integrated energy spectra (omega = 5-15 MeV) was about 4.3 fm for the (p,p'x) reaction and about 6.0 fm for the (d,d'x) reaction on 54Fe at 62 MeV per nucleon
- The Fermi momentum of 58Ni becomes zero at R approximately 4.4 fm in the FG model, while LFG allows reactions beyond this boundary
- The SCDW calculations used a Woods-Saxon nuclear density with radial parameter r_rho = 1.15 fm and diffuseness a_rho = 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the surface correction concept previously established for (n,n'x) and (p,p'x) reactions by Kalbach (1985, 2000) and Koning (2004) to deuteron-induced reactions, which had hardly been investigated. The SCDW analysis follows the approach of Watanabe (1999) for (p,p'x) reactions and Nakada (2023) for (d,d'x) reactions. The findings enable improved nuclear data evaluation for deuteron-induced reactions, which are increasingly important for deuteron accelerator-based neutron sources as discussed by Moeslang (2006) and Ledoux (2014). Future work should extend the one-step SCDW model to multi-step processes and apply similar analyses to other composite particles such as alpha particles.
