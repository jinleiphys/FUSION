# Hadron-hadron potentials coupled to quark degrees of freedom for X(3872)

> To study the internal structure of the exotic hadron $X(3872)$, considering the coupled-channel potential between quarks and hadrons is necessary because $X(3872)$ is regarded as a mixture state of $c\bar{c}$ and $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$. In this work, we construct the hadron-hadron potentials coupled to the quark channel and study the properties of the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ potentials for $X(3872)$. In particular, we compare various local approximations by focusing on the scattering phase shifts.

## Key claim

The effective single-channel hadron-hadron potential derived from coupling quark and hadron degrees of freedom is inherently nonlocal and energy dependent; the HAL QCD method provides a better local approximation to this nonlocal potential than the formal derivative expansion, especially for reproducing the exact scattering phase shifts at low momenta.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the X(3872) system, coupling a confined c-cbar quark channel (in the 3P1 state) with a D0-D*0 hadron channel (s-wave) via a Yukawa-type transition potential. They eliminate the quark channel using Feshbach's method to obtain an effective nonlocal, energy-dependent D0-D*0 potential, then apply two local approximation schemes: the formal derivative expansion and the HAL QCD method. The local potentials and resulting scattering phase shifts are compared numerically against the exact solution from the original nonlocal potential.

## Key numbers

* The binding energy of X(3872) is set to B = 40 keV.
* The quark channel eigenenergy E0 is approximately 0.078 GeV above the D0-D*0 threshold, taken from the quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985).
* The cutoff parameter mu for the Yukawa transition form factor is set to 0.14 GeV, corresponding to pion exchange.
* Quantitative differences between the two local potentials are pronounced at short distances r less than about 0.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of channel coupling in hadron potentials, previously explored for string breaking in lattice QCD by Bali et al. (2005), to the specific case of the exotic hadron X(3872). The framework generalizes the derivative expansion methods of Aoki et al. (2021) to energy-dependent nonlocal potentials. The results enable more realistic modeling of X(3872) by incorporating additional channels (charged D-D*, J/psi rho decay channels), multiple c-cbar bound states, and more sophisticated transition potentials such as the 3P0 model.
