# The origin of light $0^{+}$ scalar resonances

> We demonstrate how most of the light $J^{P}=0^{+}$ spectrum below $2.0\,\mathrm{GeV}$ and their decays can be consistently described by the unitarized quark model incorporating the chiral constraints of Adler zeros and taking SU(3) breaking effects into account. These resonances appear as poles in the complex $s$ plane in a unified picture as $q\bar{q}$ states strongly dressed by hadron loops. Through the large $N_c$ analysis, these resonances are found to naturally separate into two kinds: $σ, κ, f_0(980), a_0(980)$ are dynamically generated and run away from the real axis as $N_c$ increases, while the others move towards the $q\bar q $ seeds. In this picture, the line shape of $a_0(980)$ is produced by a broad pole below the $K\bar{K}$ threshold, and exhibits characteristics similar to the $σ$ and $κ$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that most light scalar resonances below 2.0 GeV can be described in a unified unitarized quark model with only seven parameters, where the resonances appear as poles of the S-matrix from q̅q seeds dressed by hadron loops. The key hedge is that the f₀(1710) is excluded as a possible glueball candidate, and the description relies on a Gaussian form factor as the most drastic assumption.

## Method

The authors apply the unitarized quark model (UQM) of Törnqvist (1995) with three improvements: incorporating SU(3) breaking effects in the coupling constants via a parameter ρ for the ratio of s̅s to u̅u creation rates, fixing Adler zeros from chiral perturbation theory rather than leaving them free, and performing a pole analysis on the complex s-plane. They fit the model to experimental data for I=1/2 Kπ S-wave scattering, I=0 ππ S-wave phase shifts, and ππ→KK̅ data below 1.5 GeV, then extract pole positions on various Riemann sheets and study their large N_c trajectories.

- I=1/2 Kπ poles: κ at √s = 0.767±0.009 − i 0.308±0.035 GeV (second sheet), K₀*(1430) at 1.456±0.018 − i 0.164±0.026 GeV (third sheet), K₀*(1950) at 1.890±0.029 − i 0.296±0.014 GeV (fourth sheet)

- Fit quality: χ²/d.o.f. ≃ 0.8

## Context

This work extends the unitarized quark model of Törnqvist (1995) by incorporating SU(3) breaking and Adler zeros from chiral perturbation theory (Gasser 1983, 1984), enabling the κ resonance to be found where it was previously absent. The large N_c analysis confirms the separation of σ, κ, f₀(980), a₀(980) as dynamically generated resonances (consistent with tetraquark interpretations by Jaffe (1976) and Maiani (2004)) while heavier states move toward q̅q seeds, unifying the two classes in a single framework. The results enable future extension to other spectra such as charmonium states (Pennington 2007) and provide theoretical guidance for experimental investigation of higher scalar resonances.
