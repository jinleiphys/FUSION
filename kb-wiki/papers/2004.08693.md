# Induced surface and curvature tensions equation of state of hard spheres and its virial coefficients

> Here we present new results obtained for the equation of state with induced surface and curvature tensions. The explicit formulas for the first five virial coefficients of system pressure and for the induced surface and curvature tension coefficients are derived and their possible applications are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit virial expansions for the pressure, induced surface tension coefficient, and induced curvature tension coefficient of the hard-sphere gas, showing that with three adjustable parameters one can exactly reproduce the first five virial coefficients of the Carnahan-Starling equation of state or of Monte-Carlo simulations, but the physical solution requires the coefficient \(A = 1 - \beta_p\) to be positive to avoid negative surface tension.

## Method

The authors start from the system of three equations defining the induced surface and curvature tensions (ISCT) equation of state for one-component hard spheres. They expand the pressure \(p\), surface tension coefficient \(\Sigma\), and curvature tension coefficient \(K\) as Taylor series in the particle number density \(\rho\) up to fifth order. By substituting these expansions into the ISCT equations and using the relation \(B = 1 - A\), they obtain explicit formulas for the virial coefficients \(a_k\), \(b_k\), and \(c_k\) in terms of the parameters \(\gamma\), \(\delta\), and \(\beta_p\). These parameters are then fixed by matching the known virial coefficients of the Carnahan-Starling equation of state or Monte-Carlo data for hard spheres.

## Key numbers

* The Carnahan-Starling compressibility factor virial expansion is \(Z_{CS} = 1 + 4\eta + 10\eta^2 + 18\eta^3 + 28\eta^4 + 40\eta^5 + 54\eta^6 + 70\eta^7\), where \(\eta = \rho v\) is the packing fraction.
* The Monte-Carlo virial expansion for hard spheres is \(Z_{MC} = 1 + 4\eta + 10\eta^2 + 18.36\eta^3 + 28.23\eta^4 + 39.74\eta^5 + 53.5\eta^6 + 70.8\eta^7\).
* The physical solution reproducing the Carnahan-Starling five virial coefficients gives \(\gamma = 0.302706 v\), \(\delta = 2.22698 v\), \(\beta_p = 0.313112\), which yields \(A \simeq 0.69\), \(\alpha \simeq 1.14\), \(\beta \simeq 3.37\).
* The physical solution reproducing the Monte-Carlo five virial coefficients gives \(\gamma = 0.316292 v\), \(\delta = 2.28784 v\), \(\beta_p = 0.298844\).
* The ISCT equation of state accurately reproduces the Carnahan-Starling compressibility factor up to \(\eta \simeq 0.42\), while the virial expansion truncated at fifth order works up to \(\eta \simeq 0.25\).

## Context

This work extends the induced surface tension (IST) concept previously applied to hadron resonance gas models (Bugaev et al., 2016-2018) and nuclear matter (Ivanytskyi, 2018) by including curvature tension, following the ISCT formalism of Yakovenko et al. (2019). The derived virial expansions allow quantitative study of how the effective excluded volume and effective surface of hard spheres decrease with density, which the authors note is absent in the Van der Waals equation of state and the earlier IST equation of state. This enables development of more realistic equations of state for nuclear and neutron matter, particularly for describing the vanishing of surface tension at the critical endpoint.
