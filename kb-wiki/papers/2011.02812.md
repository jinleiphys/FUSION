# Comparing conserved charge fluctuations from lattice QCD to HRG model calculations

> We present results from lattice QCD calculations for $2^{nd}$ and $4^{th}$ order cumulants of conserved charge fluctuations and correlations, and compare these with various HRG model calculations. We show that differences between HRG and QCD calculations already show up in the second order cumulants close to the pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD and quickly grow large at higher temperatures. We also show that QCD results for strangeness fluctuations are enhanced over HRG model calculations which are based only on particles listed in the Particle Data Group tables as 3-star resonances. This suggests the importance of contributions from additional strange hadron resonances. We furthermore argue that additional (repulsive) interactions, introduced either through excluded volume (mean field) HRG models or the S-matrix approach, do not improve the quantitative agreement with $2^{nd}$ and $4^{th}$ order cumulants calculated in lattice QCD. HRG based approaches fail to describe the thermodynamics of strongly interacting matter at or shortly above the pseudo-critical temperature of QCD.

## Key claim

Differences between Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model calculations and lattice QCD results for conserved charge fluctuations already appear in second order cumulants close to the pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD, and these deviations grow large at higher temperatures; the authors argue that including repulsive interactions through excluded volume or S-matrix approaches does not improve quantitative agreement with lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors performed continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD calculations using the HISQ action on lattices of size Nσ³ × Nτ with Nσ = 4 Nτ for (2+1)-flavor QCD, computing second and fourth order generalized susceptibilities (cumulants) of net baryon-number, strangeness, and electric charge fluctuations and their correlations. They compared these results to several HRG model variants: a non-interacting HRG using only PDG-listed 3-star resonances (PDG-HRG), an HRG extended with additional strange resonances from quark model calculations (QM-HRG), an excluded volume HRG with repulsive interactions among baryons (EV-HRG), and an S-matrix approach incorporating Δ++ and N* resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral transition is Tpc = (156.5 ± 1.5) MeV.
- At Tpc, the ratio χ₂B/χ₂S = 0.417(18).
- Continuum extrapolated ratios of second order cumulants at Tpc: χ₁₁BS/χ₂S = -0.241(4), χ₂B/χ₁₁QS = 1.10(4), χ₁₁BQ/χ₂Q = 0.059(14).
- An excluded volume of size b ≃ 0.4/T³ (corresponding to hadron radius rT ≃ 0.3, or r in the range 0.45-0.34 fm over 130-175 MeV) reduces χ₂B by about 20% and χ₂S by about 8% in the transition region.
- The non-interacting quark gas limit for χ₂B/χ₂S at infinite temperature is 1/3.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of conserved charge fluctuations (Bazavov 2014, Bazavov 2017) by providing a systematic comparison with multiple HRG model variants, including those with additional strange resonances from quark model calculations (Isgur, Ebert) and repulsive interactions via excluded volume (Yen 1997, Vovchenko 2016, Vovchenko 2017) or mean field approaches (Huovinen 2017). The results demonstrate that HRG-based approaches fail to describe QCD thermodynamics at or shortly above Tpc, and that the inclusion of additional strange resonances improves agreement for strangeness fluctuations while repulsive interactions tend to worsen it. This provides quantitative benchmarks for heavy-ion collision experiments and motivates further development of effective theories beyond the HRG framework.
