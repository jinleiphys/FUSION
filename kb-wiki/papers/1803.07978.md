# Collective flows of $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O collisions with $α$-clustering configurations

> The main purpose of the present paper is to discuss whether or not the collective flows in heavy-ion collision at Fermi energy can be taken as a tool to investigate the cluster configuration in light nuclei. In practice, within an Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics model, four $α$-clustering (linear chain, kite, square, and tetrahedron) configurations of $^{16}$O are employed in the initialization, $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O around Fermi energy (40 - 60 MeV$/$nucleon) with impact parameter 1 - 3 fm are simulated, the directed and elliptic flows are analyzed. It is found that collective flows are influenced by the different $α$-clustering configurations, and the directed flow of free protons is more sensitive to the initial cluster configuration than the elliptic flow. Nuclear reaction at Fermi energy can be taken a useful way to study cluster configuration in light nuclei.

## Key claim

Collective flows in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies can serve as a tool to probe alpha-clustering configurations in light nuclei, but the sensitivity is conditional: the directed flow of free protons is more sensitive to the initial cluster configuration than the elliptic flow, and the effect is most pronounced at larger impact parameters and lower beam energies (around 40 MeV/nucleon).

## Method

The authors used the Extended Quantum Molecular Dynamics (EQMD) model to simulate 16O + 16O collisions at beam energies of 40-60 MeV/nucleon with impact parameters of 1-3 fm. Four different 4-alpha clustering configurations of 16O (linear chain, kite, square, and tetrahedron) were employed in the initialization, with random orientations for each event; more than 600,000 events per configuration were simulated to reduce statistical error. The directed flow (v1) and elliptic flow (v2) of free protons were analyzed as functions of longitudinal rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: 40 - 60 MeV/nucleon
- Impact parameter range: 1 - 3 fm
- More than 600,000 events simulated for each of the four alpha-clustering configurations
- At 40 MeV/nucleon and b = 3 fm, the directed flow v1 of free protons from the tetrahedron configuration is more negative than that from the chain configuration
- At 40 MeV/nucleon and b = 3 fm, the elliptic flow v2 at mid-rapidity from the tetrahedron configuration is slightly smaller than that from the chain configuration
- At 60 MeV/nucleon, the directed flow shows almost no sensitivity to different cluster configurations

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Broniowski (2013) and Bozek (2014) to use collective flows in relativistic nuclear collisions to probe alpha-clustering, now applied at Fermi energies. It builds on prior EQMD studies by He (2014, 2016) and Huang that investigated alpha-clustering effects on dipole resonance and photonuclear reactions of 16O and 12C. The results suggest that future experiments should measure directed flow at larger impact parameters and beam energies around 40 MeV/nucleon, and the authors propose extending the study to asymmetric systems such as 16O + 124Sn or 12C + 197Au, as well as investigating higher-order flows (v3, v4) for triangular and tetrahedral cluster configurations.
