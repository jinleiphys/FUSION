# Parity conserving gamma asymmetry in n-p radiative capture

> The importance of n-p radiative capture, utilizing polarized cold neutrons, as a means of measuring the weak pion coupling constant is reviewed. Parity conserving processes of the form k_gamma*[s_n,k_n] can contribute to the s_n*k_gamma photon asymmetry in any such experiment, if the apparatus is not perfectly symmetric. For an incident laboratory neutron energy of 0.003 eV a value of A^{PC}_{gamma}=0.67*10^{-8} is obtained for two different potential models (Argonne AV14 and Nijmegen Reid93). Serving as an extreme test case, the Reid soft core potential yields 0.61*10^{-8}, close to the result of the contemporary forces. Implications for extracting the weak pion coupling constant and for monitoring the beam polarization are discussed.

# Parity conserving gamma asymmetry in n-p radiative capture

## Key claim

Parity-conserving processes of the form **k**_γ · (**s**_n × **k**_n) can produce a background asymmetry in measurements of the parity-violating photon asymmetry **s**_n · **k**_γ if the detector has imperfect left-right spatial acceptance; for an incident neutron energy of 0.003 eV, this parity-conserving asymmetry is calculated to be approximately 0.67 × 10⁻⁸, which should not significantly contaminate a parity-violation measurement unless the latter proves to be an order of magnitude smaller than expected.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Schrödinger equation numerically for each partial wave using a cubic spline expansion and collocation method, computing electric and magnetic multipole matrix elements for n-p radiative capture at cold neutron energies. They employed three different nucleon-nucleon potential models (Reid Soft Core, Argonne AV14, and Nijmegen Reid93) to evaluate the parity-conserving photon asymmetry from the E1 × M1 interference term in the angular distribution. The wave functions were validated by reproducing Partovi's (1964) published matrix elements to better than 1% agreement and by matching the total thermal neutron capture cross section reported by Arenhövel and Sanzone (1991).

## Key numbers

- For an incident laboratory neutron energy of 0.003 eV, the parity-conserving gamma asymmetry is A^{PC}_γ = 0.668 × 10⁻⁸ using the Argonne AV14 potential and 0.665 × 10⁻⁸ using the Nijmegen Reid93 potential
- The Reid Soft Core potential, serving as an extreme test case, yields A^{PC}_γ = 0.607 × 10⁻⁸
- The parity-violating asymmetry A^{PNC}_γ is expected to be of order 10⁻⁷ based on the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (1980) prediction for the weak pion coupling constant f_π ≈ −0.5 × 10⁻⁷
- The previous experimental upper limit from Alberi et al. (1988) was A_γ = (−1.5 ± 4.7) × 10⁻⁸
- The spin-singlet n-p scattering length is a^s_np ≈ −23.7 fm, compared to the Coulomb-corrected p-p scattering length a^C_pp ≈ −17 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Partovi (1964) for n-p radiative capture to evaluate a potential systematic background in parity-violation experiments. The calculation addresses a concern raised by the ILL experiment (Alberi et al., 1988), which assumed that parity-conserving contamination was negligible when setting an upper limit on A^{PNC}_γ. The results enable future measurements of the weak pion coupling constant f_π, which Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) predicted to be enhanced by neutral currents, and suggest that A^{PC}_γ could serve as an in situ polarization monitor for cold neutron beams if experimental sensitivity reaches a few times 10⁻⁹.
