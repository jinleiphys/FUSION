# Deuteron-deuteron scattering above four-nucleon breakup threshold

> Deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering and transfer reactions in the energy regime above four-nucleon breakup threshold are described by solving exact four-particle equations for transition operators. Several realistic nuclear interaction models are used, including the one with effective many-nucleon forces generated by the explicit $Δ$-isobar excitation; the Coulomb force between protons is taken into account as well. Differential cross sections, deuteron analyzing powers, outgoing nucleon polarization, and deuteron-to-neutron polarization transfer coefficients are calculated at 10 MeV deuteron energy. Overall good agreement with the experimental data is found. The importance of breakup channels is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact four-particle scattering calculations for deuteron-deuteron reactions above the four-nucleon breakup threshold are now possible with realistic nuclear interactions, achieving overall good agreement with experimental data at 10 MeV deuteron energy, though the abstract's claim of "good agreement" is hedged by noting that some discrepancies remain, particularly for vector analyzing powers and at intermediate angles where cross sections are underpredicted by up to 6%.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for symmetrized transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave framework, including partial waves with orbital angular momenta up to 6-7 and total four-nucleon angular momentum up to 7. They used the complex energy method with special integration weights to handle quasisingularities from open breakup channels, and included the proton-proton Coulomb force via the screening and renormalization method with screening radius R=13 fm. Three realistic nuclear interaction models were employed: the INOY04 potential, the CD Bonn potential, and the CD Bonn + Delta potential that generates effective three- and four-nucleon forces through explicit Delta-isobar excitation.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section for d+d elastic scattering at 10.5 MeV: calculations overpredict data from Wilson (1969) by 3% at center-of-mass angle 90 degrees
- Differential cross section for d+d elastic scattering at 12.3 MeV: calculations underpredict data from PhysRevC.10.54 by 4% at center-of-mass angle 90 degrees
- Deuteron analyzing powers in d+d elastic scattering at 10 MeV: absolute values are of order 0.02
- Total cross section for 2H(d,p)3H transfer at 10 MeV: 77.6 mb (INOY04), 79.8 mb (CD Bonn + Delta), 83.4 mb (CD Bonn)
- Total cross section for 2H(d,n)3He transfer at 10 MeV: 81.3 mb (INOY04), 83.9 mb (CD Bonn + Delta), 87.1 mb (CD Bonn)
- Total breakup cross section at 10 MeV: 112 mb (INOY04), 113 mb (CD Bonn + Delta), 110 mb (CD Bonn)
- Differential cross section for transfer reactions at 10 MeV: INOY04 results underpredict data by roughly 6% at center-of-mass angle 90 degrees
- Complex energy parameter epsilon used: between 1.2 and 3.0 MeV in 0.3 MeV steps, with about 30 grid points per momentum variable
- Screening radius for Coulomb treatment: R=13 fm

## Context

This work extends the Lisbon group's previous exact four-nucleon calculations for nucleon-trinucleon scattering (Deltuva 2007, 2014) to the more challenging deuteron-deuteron system, which mimics the scattering of two halo nuclei due to the deuteron's weak binding and large spatial size. The calculations go beyond earlier work by the Pisa group (Viviani 2001, 2013) and Grenoble-Strasbourg group (Lazauskas 2004, 2009) that had difficulty with multichannel reactions above breakup threshold and with Coulomb inclusion. The results enable future studies of four-nucleon reactions over wider energy ranges and provide a benchmark for testing nuclear force models, while also demonstrating that all two-cluster reactions in the four-nucleon system can now be calculated exactly with realistic interactions including Coulomb repulsion.
