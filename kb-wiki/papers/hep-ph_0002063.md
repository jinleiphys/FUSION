# MSW Effects in Vacuum Oscillations

> We point out that for solar neutrino oscillations with the mass-squared difference of Delta m^2 ~ 10^-10 - 10^-9 eV^2, traditionally known as ``vacuum oscillation'' range, the solar matter effects are non-negligible, particularly for the low energy pp neutrinos. One consequence of this is that the values of the mixing angle theta and pi/2-theta are not equivalent, leading to the need to consider the entire physical range of the mixing angle 0<=theta<=pi/2 when determining the allowed values of the neutrino oscillation parameters.

## Key claim

For solar neutrino oscillations with mass-squared differences traditionally considered the "vacuum oscillation" range (Δm² ~ 10⁻¹⁰–10⁻⁹ eV²), solar matter effects are non-negligible, especially for low-energy pp neutrinos, breaking the symmetry between mixing angle θ and π/2−θ and requiring consideration of the full physical range 0 ≤ θ ≤ π/2.

## Method

The author numerically solved the Schrödinger equation for neutrino propagation through the Sun using the BP2000 solar profile to compute the level-crossing probability P_c for two-generation mixing, then used the resulting survival probability (including the oscillatory term) to fit the total event rates from the Homestake, GALLEX, SAGE, and Super-Kamiokande experiments. The fits integrated over neutrino energy spectra and over the annual variation of the Earth–Sun distance due to orbital eccentricity, and used the analytical exponential-profile formula for P_c with an adjusted scale height r₀ = R⊙/18.4 = 3.77 × 10⁴ km for the outer solar regions.

## Key numbers

- Solar matter effects become significant for Δm² ≳ 2 × 10⁻¹⁰ eV² in gallium experiments (sensitive to pp neutrinos with E_ν ≤ 0.42 MeV).
- For the combined fit of GALLEX, SAGE, and Super-Kamiokande, matter effects are noticeable for Δm² > 6 × 10⁻¹⁰ eV².
- The exponential profile formula for P_c with r₀ = 3.77 × 10⁴ km agrees well with numerical results for Δm² ≲ 4 × 10⁻⁹ eV².
- The first-order correction to the vacuum oscillation probability contains a term (γ/4) cos 2θ, where γ = 2π r₀ Δm²/(2E_ν), making the correction non-negligible for pp neutrinos at Δm² ∼ 10⁻¹⁰–10⁻⁹ eV².
- The neutrino survival probability becomes independent of Earth–Sun distance L for Δm² ≳ 6 × 10⁻⁹ eV² due to loss of coherence and adiabatic evolution.
- The best fit to the Super-Kamiokande electron recoil spectrum from prior work is at Δm² = 6.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ eV², sin²2θ = 1.

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of solar neutrino oscillations, which traditionally separates the "vacuum oscillation" region (Δm² ∼ 10⁻¹¹–10⁻⁹ eV²) from the MSW region (Δm² ∼ 10⁻⁸–10⁻³ eV²) and uses the canonical vacuum survival probability P = 1 − sin²2θ sin²(1.27 Δm² L/E), which is invariant under θ → π/2−θ. The author shows that this separation is artificial for Δm² ≳ 10⁻¹⁰ eV², building on prior work by the same author (Friedland, 1999) on the "dark side" of parameter space and on regeneration effects. The results imply that future fits to solar neutrino data (e.g., from Super-Kamiokande, SNO, Borexino) should extend the mixing angle range to 0 ≤ θ ≤ π/2 and present results up to Δm² ∼ 10⁻⁸ eV², as previously advocated by Friedland (1999).
