# AMY Lorentz invariant parton cascade -- the thermal equilibrium case

> We introduce ALPACA, a Lorentz invariant parton cascade encoding the AMY effective kinetic theory of QCD at high temperatures. It solves the Boltzmann equation by explicitly simulating the evolution of parton ensembles corresponding to single events. We discuss how the effective masses and temperature entering the elastic collision and splitting/merging rates can be estimated from just a single event. We perform an extensive validation of the framework by showing that it reproduces the expected behaviour in thermal equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper introduces ALPACA, a Lorentz invariant parton cascade that encodes the AMY effective kinetic theory of QCD at high temperatures, and validates it by showing that ensembles sampled from a thermal distribution remain in thermal equilibrium after long simulation times, which requires all parts of the simulation (elastic scattering, splitting, and merging) to intertwine correctly to respect detailed balance.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo event generator that evolves parton ensembles by explicitly simulating elastic 2-to-2 scattering, quasi-collinear 1-to-2 splitting, and 2-to-1 merging processes according to the AMY collision kernels. The evolution uses a Lorentz invariant framework where interactions are ordered by a scalar parameter tau rather than observer time, with particle positions and momenta evolved in an 8N-dimensional phase space. For thermal equilibrium validation, the system is placed in a box with periodic boundary conditions, and effective masses and temperature needed for the scattering rates are estimated dynamically from each single event's parton ensemble rather than from the phase space density.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for the QCD phase transition is given as T_c approximately 160 MeV, citing HotQCD (2014).
- The number of spin and color states is nu_q = 6 for quarks and anti-quarks and nu_g = 16 for gluons.
- The coupling is defined as lambda = g^2 N_c = 4 pi alpha_s N_c, where the theory is leading-order accurate in this coupling for hard partons.
- A minimum momentum cutoff p_min is introduced for all particles to handle infrared divergences in splitting/merging rates.

## Context

This work extends the AMY effective kinetic theory of QCD (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002) by providing a Monte Carlo event generator representation that can go beyond mean-field evolution to produce fully exclusive final states with fluctuations and correlations. It differs from existing parton cascade codes (Geiger 1991, Zhang 1997, Molnar 2000, Bass 2002, Xu 2007, and others) by exactly reproducing the AMY kernels and being Lorentz invariant by construction, avoiding problems with residual causality violation and altered correlations from particle subdivision. The framework enables future apples-to-apples comparisons between theory and experimental data for both small and large collision systems, with planned integration into the Sherpa event generator for hadronisation and hard processes.
