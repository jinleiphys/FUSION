# Dynamical magnetic enhancement of light and heavy quark jet quenching at RHIC

> A new Monte Carlo implementation of Djordjevic's dynamical scattering generalization of the DGLV radiative energy loss opacity series is used with a hybrid interpolation scheme to compute both light and heavy quark jet quenching up to third order in opacity. The enhancement of the ratio of bottom to charm quark energy loss due to perturbative long range color magnetic effects in nonuniform Bjorken expanding geometries is found to reduce the significance of the heavy quark jet puzzle posed by the observed near equality (within sizeable errors) of pion and nonphotonic electron nuclear modification at RHIC. Jet Flavor Spectroscopy discussed below will be a powerful tool to differentiate competing dynamical models of the QGP produced in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of perturbative long-range color magnetic scattering effects (via the dynamical MD model) in a Bjorken-expanding QGP geometry reduces the significance of the heavy quark jet puzzle at RHIC, but the ratio of bottom to charm quark energy loss remains well below unity (about 0.5), so the puzzle is not fully resolved within this pQCD framework.

## Method

The authors developed a hybrid Monte Carlo interpolation of the DGLV-BFW-MC1.0 code to compute radiative energy loss up to third order in opacity, replacing the static Debye-screened GW momentum distributions with a dynamical magnetic-enhanced transverse distribution parameterized by the ratio \(r_m = \mu_m/\mu_e\) (color magnetic to electric screening length). They applied this to both a uniform static "brick" geometry and a more realistic Bjorken-expanding geometry with Wood-Saxon participant density and Glauber initial jet production profiles, computing the probability distribution \(P(\epsilon)\) of fractional energy loss and the partonic \(R_{AA}\) using a power-law approximation with index \(n=4\).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of charm to bottom quark radiative energy loss at first order in opacity is reduced by approximately 20% in the dynamical MD model (\(r_m=0\)) compared to the static GW model (\(r_m=1\)) for a uniform static QGP at \(T=250\) MeV.
- For path lengths \(L > 2\) fm, the ratio of light (up) quark to bottom quark energy loss remains well above unity for all \(r_m\) values (1, 1/2, 1/3, 0) and up to third order in opacity.
- The dynamical MD model increases the absolute energy loss fraction \(\Delta E/E\) by about a factor of two in a static plasma compared to the static GW model, but Bjorken expansion reduces it back toward the static GW value.
- The ratio \(\Delta E(b)/\Delta E(c)\) is found to be no more than about 0.5 after including magnetic effects and expansion.
- The pQCD framework predicts \(R_{AA}(e^-)/R_{AA}(\pi) \approx 2\) due to the highly quenched gluon jet component of the pion yield.

## Context

This work extends the DGLV opacity series approach (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000) and the WHDG model (Wicks, Horowitz, Djordjevic, Gyulassy, 2007) by implementing the dynamical magnetic scattering proposal of Djordjevic (2009) to address the heavy quark jet puzzle, where pQCD predictions over-quenched bottom quarks compared to PHENIX and STAR nonphotonic electron data. The results suggest that flavor-tagged charm and bottom jet measurements (Jet Flavor Spectroscopy) are needed to differentiate pQCD from holographic gravity dual models (Horowitz, 2007) and to resolve the remaining discrepancy.
