# One Body Density Matrix, Natural Orbits and Quasi Hole States in 16O and 40Ca

> The one body density matrix, momentum distribution, natural orbits and quasi hole states of 16O and 40Ca are analyzed in the framework of the correlated basis function theory using state dependent correlations with central and tensor components. Fermi hypernetted chain integral equations and single operator chain approximation are employed to sum cluster diagrams at all orders. The optimal trial wave function is determined by means of the variational principle and the realistic Argonne v8' two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The correlated momentum distributions are in good agreement with the available variational Monte Carlo results and show the well known enhancement at large momentum values with respect to the independent particle model. Diagonalization of the density matrix provides the natural orbits and their occupation numbers. Correlations deplete the occupation number of the first natural orbitals by more than 10%. The first following ones result instead occupied by a few percent. Jastrow correlations lower the spectroscopic factors of the valence states by a few percent (~1-3%) and an additional ~8-12% depletion is provided by tensor correlations. It is confirmed that short range correlations do not explain the spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) experiments. 2h-1p perturbative corrections in the correlated basis are expected to provide most of the remaining strength, as in nuclear matter.

# One Body Density Matrix, Natural Orbits and Quasi Hole States in 16O and 40Ca

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (Jastrow and tensor) deplete the occupation numbers of the first natural orbitals by more than 10% and lower spectroscopic factors of valence states by a total of approximately 9-15%, but this variational treatment (zeroth-order correlated basis function theory) does **not** explain the spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) experiments; two-hole one-particle perturbative corrections in the correlated basis are expected to provide most of the remaining strength, as in nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors employed correlated basis function (CBF) theory with state-dependent correlations containing central and tensor components (f6 model, omitting spin-orbit terms) for 16O and 40Ca. Fermi hypernetted chain (FHNC) integral equations with the single operator chain (SOC) approximation were used to sum cluster diagrams at all orders, using the Argonne v'8 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions. The optimal trial wave function was determined variationally, and the one-body density matrix was diagonalized to obtain natural orbits and their occupation numbers.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon: 5.48 MeV in 16O and 6.97 MeV in 40Ca (experimental: 7.97 MeV and 8.55 MeV, respectively)
- Root mean square radii: 2.83 fm in 16O and 3.66 fm in 40Ca (experimental: 2.73 fm and 3.48 fm, respectively)
- Jastrow correlations lower spectroscopic factors of valence states by approximately 1-3% with respect to unity
- Tensor correlations provide an additional depletion of approximately 8-12%
- Occupation number depletion of the first natural orbitals: more than 10%; the 2s state in 40Ca is depleted by as much as 22%
- Momentum distribution normalization satisfied within 0.2-0.3% for Jastrow correlations and within approximately 3% (16O) and 2% (40Ca) for the f6 model
- Kinetic energy from momentum distribution (T_MD) vs. FHNC value: 29.42 MeV vs. 32.64 MeV in 16O (9% disagreement); 36.63 MeV vs. 38.15 MeV in 40Ca (9.6% disagreement)
- Nuclear matter energy per nucleon at empirical saturation density: -10.9 MeV (empirical: -16 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the FHNC/SOC methodology, previously applied to nuclear matter by Fantoni (1978) and Fantoni and Pandharipande (1983), to finite doubly closed shell nuclei with state-dependent correlations including tensor components. It builds on earlier FHNC calculations for Jastrow correlations in finite nuclei by Co' et al. (1994) and Arias de Saavedra et al. (1996). The results are compared with variational Monte Carlo calculations for 16O by Pieper et al. (1992) using the Argonne v14 potential, and with Green function method results by Polls et al. (1995) using the Bonn B potential. The work confirms that short-range correlations alone cannot account for the spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) experiments (typically 0.6-0.7), and points toward the need for two-hole one-particle perturbative corrections in the correlated basis to recover the missing strength, analogous to the nuclear matter case.
