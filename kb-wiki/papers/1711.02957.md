# Exclusive vector meson photoproduction with a leading baryon in photon - hadron interactions at hadronic colliders

> Exclusive vector meson photoproduction associated with a leading baryon ($B = n, Δ^+, Δ^0$) in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is investigated using the color dipole formalism and taking into account nonlinear effects in the QCD dynamics. In particular, we compute the cross sections for $ρ$, $ϕ$ and $J/Ψ$ production together with a $Δ$ and compare the predictions with those obtained for a leading neutron. Our results show that the $V + Δ$ cross section is almost 30 % of the $V + n$ one. Our results also show that a future experimental analysis of these processes is, in principle, feasible and can be useful to study leading particle production.

## Key claim

Exclusive vector meson photoproduction associated with a leading baryon (neutron or delta) in pp and pA collisions is feasible for experimental analysis, but the cross sections are smaller than the usual exclusive vector meson photoproduction by approximately two orders of magnitude for a leading neutron and three orders for a leading delta.

## Method

The authors used the color dipole formalism to compute cross sections for exclusive rho, phi, and J/psi photoproduction in association with a leading baryon (neutron, Delta+, Delta0) in pp and pA collisions. The photon-target interaction was factorized into three subprocesses: photon fluctuation into a quark-antiquark dipole, diffractive dipole-pion scattering, and formation of the vector meson and leading particle, with the pion flux described by a splitting function and the dipole-pion amplitude related to the dipole-proton amplitude via the additive quark model (Rq = 2/3). Absorptive corrections were approximated by a constant factor K = 0.179, fixed from HERA data on leading neutron production.

## Key numbers

* For rho production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, the total cross section with a leading neutron is 121.34 nb, with a leading Delta+ is 45.59 nb, and with a leading Delta0 is 22.45 nb.
* For J/psi production in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, the total cross section with a leading neutron is 0.45 nb, with a leading Delta+ is 0.160 nb, and with a leading Delta0 is 0.079 nb.
* For rho production in pPb collisions at sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV, the total cross section with a leading neutron is 163.12 microbarn, with a leading Delta+ is 59.56 microbarn, and with a leading Delta0 is 29.35 microbarn.
* The V + Delta cross section is almost 30% of the V + n cross section.
* The channels Delta0 -> n pi0 and Delta+ -> n pi+ together have a cross section about 20-28% of the direct leading neutron production cross section.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Carvalho et al., 2016) on exclusive vector meson photoproduction with a leading neutron to include delta baryon production, which is the next most important proton dissociation process after p -> n pi. The approach is based on the same formalism used to successfully describe exclusive vector meson photoproduction without proton dissociation (Goncalves et al., 2014) and inclusive/exclusive gamma p interactions with a leading neutron at HERA (Goncalves et al., 2015). The results enable future experimental analyses using forward detectors such as the CMS-TOTEM Precision Proton Spectrometer and ATLAS + LHCf experiments, and can help constrain the theoretical description of leading particle production and QCD dynamics at high energies.
