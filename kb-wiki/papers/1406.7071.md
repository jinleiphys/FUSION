# Reaction ${\bar p}p \to {\bar Λ}_c^-Λ_c^+$ within an effective Lagrangian model

> We study the charmed baryon production reaction ${\bar p} p \to {\bar Λ}_c^- Λ_c^+$ using the $t$-channel $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ meson exchange diagrams within an effective Lagrangian model involving the physical hadron masses and the coupling constants determined from SU(4) flavor symmetry. The initial and final state distortion effects are accounted for by using a simple eikonal approximation based procedure. The vertex parameters of our model have been checked by employing them to calculate the cross sections for the ${\bar p} p \to {\bar Λ} Λ$ reaction within a similar model. We predict the ${\bar Λ}_c^- Λ_c^+$ production cross sections in the range of 1-30 $μb$ for antiproton beam momenta varying between threshold to 20 GeV/c. The respective roles of $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ meson exchanges and also those of the vector and tensor components of the $D^{*0}$ coupling have been investigated

## Key claim
The authors predict that the total cross section for the reaction \(\bar p p \to \bar \Lambda_c^- \Lambda_c^+\) is in the range of 1–30 \(\mu b\) for antiproton beam momenta from threshold to 20 GeV/\(c\), but they note that the absolute magnitudes carry uncertainty because the initial and final state distortion parameters are not constrained by experimental data for this specific channel.

## Method
The reaction is described by \(t\)-channel \(D^0\) and \(D^{*0}\) meson exchange diagrams within an effective Lagrangian model, using coupling constants determined from SU(4) flavor symmetry and taken from prior \(DN\) and \(\bar D N\) scattering analyses. Initial and final state distortion effects are treated via an eikonal approximation with a Gaussian absorptive potential, and the vertex off-shell behavior is regulated by a monopole form factor with a cutoff parameter of 3.0 GeV. The model parameters are validated by applying the same framework to the \(\bar p p \to \bar \Lambda \Lambda\) reaction and comparing with experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for \(\bar p p \to \bar \Lambda_c^- \Lambda_c^+\) is predicted to be in the range 1–30 \(\mu b\) for antiproton beam momenta from threshold to 20 GeV/\(c\).
- The cross section peaks around \(\bar p_{\text{lab}} = 15\) GeV/\(c\) with a peak value of approximately 30 \(\mu b\).
- Near-threshold (excess energy between 1 and 100 MeV), the total cross section varies between 1 and 8 \(\mu b\).
- The cutoff parameter \(\lambda\) for the monopole form factor is set to 3.0 GeV for the charmed baryon production channel.
- The ratio of tensor to vector coupling at the \(D^{*0}BN\) vertex is 3.26 (from Table I: \(g_{D^{*0}BN}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 1.590\), \(f_{D^{*0}BN}/\sqrt{4\pi} = 5.183\)).
- For the \(\bar p p \to \bar \Lambda \Lambda\) validation, the cutoff parameter is 1.7 GeV, and the absorptive potential parameters are \(V_0 = 0.8965\) GeV and \(\mu = 0.3369\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends the meson-exchange approach previously applied to \(\bar p p \to \bar \Lambda \Lambda\) by Haidenbauer et al. (1992) and to charmed baryon production by Haidenbauer et al. (2010, 2011) using a coupled-channels framework. The predicted cross sections are substantially larger (by factors of 10–100) than those from quark-based models such as the handbag approach of Gornicki et al. (2009), the quark-gluon string model of Kaidalov et al. (1994), and the Regge-based calculation of Kholodenko et al. (2012). The results are intended to guide planning for the \(\bar P\)ANDA experiment at FAIR, where the reaction will be measured.
