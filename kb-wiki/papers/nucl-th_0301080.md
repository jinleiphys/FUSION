# Renormalization of the 1S0 One-Pion-Exchange NN Interaction in Presence of Derivative Contact Interactions

> We use standard distorted wave theory techniques and dimensional regularization to find out solutions of the nucleon-nucleon Lippman--Schwinger equation with a kernel determined by the Weinberg's next-to-leading potential, which consists of one--pion exchange and additional contact terms with derivatives. Though for simplicity, we restrict the discussion to the $^1S_0$ channel and to contact terms containing up to two derivatives, the generalization to higher waves and/or number of derivatives is straightforward. The undetermined low energy constants emerging out of the renormalization procedure are fitted to data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the one-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction in the 1S0 channel, combined with contact terms containing up to two derivatives, can be renormalized using dimensional regularization and a scheme that absorbs the remaining logarithmic divergence into a single additional parameter, yielding a description of phase shifts up to center-of-mass momenta of about 260 MeV.

## Method

The author solves the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the 1S0 partial wave using distorted wave theory, treating the one-pion-exchange potential exactly and the short-range contact terms (with up to two derivatives) as the perturbation. Dimensional regularization is used to handle ultraviolet divergences, and the remaining logarithmic divergence from the integral J0(0) is absorbed into a renormalized parameter J0R(0), which is fitted along with the two contact-term low-energy constants g0 and g1 to Nijmegen phase-shift data.

## Key numbers

- Reduced nucleon mass: m = 469.46 MeV (neutron-proton system)
- Pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV; axial coupling: g_A = 1.25
- Best-fit parameters: g0 m = -0.276(6), g1 m^3 = 0.347(14), J0R(0)/m = -3.21(8), with chi^2/dof = 0.016 for 62 phase-shift values from E_Lab = 0.01 to 142 MeV
- Fitted scattering length: a = -23.65 fm; effective range: r0 = 2.63 fm (Nijmegen results: -23.7 fm and 2.73 fm)
- One-pion-exchange-only effective range parameters: a_pi = -0.88 fm, r0_pi = 12.38 fm
- The scheme describes data up to CM nucleon momentum k_max ~ 260 MeV; the ratio sqrt(|g0/2g1|) is approximately 295 MeV

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to pi-pi scattering (Nieves and Ruiz-Arriola, 1999) and meson-baryon scattering (Nieves and Ruiz-Arriola, 2001) to the nucleon-nucleon system. It contrasts with the Kaplan-Savage-Wise scheme (1998), which treats pions perturbatively and converges slowly in the 1S0 channel, and with the cutoff-based approach of Epelbaum, Gloeckle, and Meissner (1998), which uses UV cutoffs in the 0.6-1 GeV range. The proposed scheme enables systematic improvement by including higher-derivative contact terms and can be generalized to higher partial waves and more derivatives.
