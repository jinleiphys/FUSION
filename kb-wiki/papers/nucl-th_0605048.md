# Local Fermi gas in inclusive muon capture from nuclei

> We compare local Fermi gas and shell model in muon capture in nuclei in order to estimate the effect of finite nuclear size in low energy weak reactions.

## Key claim

The local Fermi gas (LFG) model, despite being a poor description of the detailed nuclear excitation spectrum at the low energies (0–25 MeV) relevant for muon capture, yields integrated muon capture widths that agree with a simple shell model (SM) to within 3–6% for light nuclei and 1–7% for medium and heavy nuclei, provided the Woods-Saxon potential parameters produce similar neutron and proton densities.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive muon capture widths for closed-shell nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb) using two uncorrelated models: a local Fermi gas (LFG) and an extreme shell model (SM) with single-particle states in a Woods-Saxon (WS) potential. Both models used the same nuclear densities (from the WS wave functions) and included Pauli blocking and correct energy balance, but omitted RPA correlations; the SM additionally accounted for discrete bound-state excitations. The integrated widths from the two models were compared for three different WS parameter sets (WS1, WS2, WS3) to isolate the effect of finite nuclear size.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C with WS1 and WS2 parameters, the LFG and SM integrated widths differ by only 3.2% and 1.7%, respectively; with WS3 the difference is 14.1%.
- For ¹⁶O with WS1 and WS3, the LFG and SM integrated widths agree within 6.2% and 4.3%, respectively; with WS2 the discrepancy is 24.4%.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, the LFG integrated width is within 8% of the SM result for all three parameter sets (0.1%, 4.3%, 8.3%).
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with WS1 and WS2, the LFG and SM integrated widths differ by 1.9% and 12.2%, respectively.
- The full LFG model (including RPA) reproduces experimental muon capture widths for ¹²C (3.21 × 10⁴ s⁻¹ vs. 3.78 ± 0.03 × 10⁴ s⁻¹), ¹⁶O (10.41 × 10⁴ s⁻¹ vs. 10.24 ± 0.06 × 10⁴ s⁻¹), and ²⁰⁸Pb (1310 × 10⁴ s⁻¹ vs. 1311 ± 8 × 10⁴ s⁻¹) with relative discrepancies of 0.15, −0.02, and 0.00, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the LFG-based model of Nieves et al. (2004), which successfully described inclusive neutrino-nucleus cross sections and muon capture, by quantifying the uncertainty due to finite nuclear size effects that the LFG neglects. The comparison with an extreme shell model (using Woods-Saxon potentials) shows that the integrated capture width is robust against these finite-size details, validating the LFG approach for integrated observables. The results enable the use of the simpler LFG model for neutrino-nucleus reaction calculations near threshold, while noting that detailed spectral shapes (resonances, discrete states) require a more realistic shell-model treatment.
