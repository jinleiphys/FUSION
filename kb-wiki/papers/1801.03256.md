# Multifractal Characteristics of Multiparticle Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions at SPS Energies

> Entropy, dimensions and other multifractal characteristics of multiplicity distributions of relativistic charged hadrons produced in ion-ion collisions at SPS energies are investigated. The analysis of the experimental data is carried out in terms of phase space bin-size dependence of multiplicity distributions following the Takagi's approach. Yet another method is also followed to study the multifractality which, is not related to the bin-width and (or) the detector resolution, rather involves multiplicity distribution of charged particles in full phase space in terms of information entropy and its generalization, Rényi's order-q information entropy. The findings reveal the presence of multifractal structure-- a remarkable property of the fluctuations. Nearly constant values of multifractal specific heat, 'c' estimated by the two different methods of analysis followed indicate that the parameter 'c' may be used as a universal characteristic of the particle production in high energy collisions. The results obtained from the analysis of the experimental data agree well with the predictions of Monte Carlo model AMPT.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the multifractal specific heat parameter 'c' is nearly constant (approximately 0.2) across different collision systems and energies, suggesting it may serve as a universal characteristic of particle production, but this claim is hedged by the observation that the intercept values (a-c) differ between data sets.

## Method
The authors analyzed experimental data from 200A GeV/c 16O-AgBr, 32S-AgBr, and 32S-Gold collisions recorded by the EMU01 collaboration, using two complementary methods. First, they applied Takagi's approach, examining the bin-size dependence of multiplicity distributions in cumulative X(eta) and X(phi) space to extract generalized dimensions Dq. Second, they studied Renyi's order-q information entropy from full phase space multiplicity distributions to independently estimate Dq values and the multifractal specific heat. Results were compared with predictions from the AMPT Monte Carlo model.

## Key numbers
- Multifractal specific heat 'c' approximately 0.2 for all three data sets (16O-AgBr, 32S-AgBr, 32S-Gold) in both eta and phi spaces, as estimated from both Takagi's method and entropy studies.
- Number of events analyzed: 223 (16O-AgBr), 452 (32S-AgBr), and 542 (32S-Gold) at 200A GeV/c.
- Generalized dimensions Dq were calculated for q = 2-6, with Dq values decreasing as q increases, indicating multifractality.
- The pseudorapidity range considered was eta_c - 3.0 to eta_c + 3.0, with phi range 0 to 2pi.
- Bin number M was varied from 2 to 30 in steps of 2 for the Takagi analysis.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of multifractality in heavy-ion collisions, particularly following the Takagi approach (Takagi, 1993) and the constant specific heat approximation (Bershadskii, 1999). The results are consistent with earlier findings by the same authors (Khan and Ahmad, 2017) for 14.5A GeV/c Si-AgBr and 10.6A GeV/c Au-AgBr collisions, and with Bershadskii (1999) for 10.6A GeV/c Au-nucleus collisions. The nearly constant value of 'c' (approximately 0.2) across hh, hA, and AA collisions suggests the parameter may be a universal characteristic, enabling future comparisons with QGP phase transition signatures and providing a benchmark for Monte Carlo models like AMPT.
