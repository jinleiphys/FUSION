# Hydrodynamical noise and Gubser flow

> Hydrodynamical noise is introduced on top of Gubser's analytical solution to viscous hydrodynamics. With respect to the ultra-central collision events of Pb-Pb, p-Pb and p-p at the LHC energies, we solve the evolution of noisy fluid systems and calculate the radial flow velocity correlations. We show that the absolute amplitude of the hydrodynamical noise is determined by the multiplicity of the collision event. The evolution of azimuthal anisotropies, which is related to the generation of harmonic flow, receives finite enhancements from hydrodynamical noise. Although it is strongest in the p-p systems, the effect of hydrodynamical noise on flow harmonics is found to be negligible, especially in the ultra-central Pb-Pb collisions. For the short-range correlations, hydrodynamical noise contributes to the formation of a near-side peak on top of the correlation structure originated from initial state fluctuations. The shape of the peak is affected by the strength of hydrodynamical noise, whose height and width grow from the Pb-Pb system to the p-Pb and p-p systems.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamical noise in heavy-ion collisions is controlled by the total multiplicity of the collision event rather than by the system size, and although its effect on flow harmonics is strongest in p-p systems, it is negligible overall, especially in ultra-central Pb-Pb collisions.

## Method

The authors introduce hydrodynamical noise as a stochastic term on top of Gubser's analytical solution to Navier-Stokes viscous hydrodynamics for a conformal fluid. They decompose fluctuations into scalar and vector modes using spherical harmonics and Fourier modes in rapidity, then solve the resulting linearized Langevin equations mode-by-mode for Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p systems at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The two-point autocorrelation of hydrodynamical noise modes scales as $\pi \nu / (\hat w \cosh^2 \rho)$, where $\nu = (4/3)(\eta/s)$ and $\hat w$ is the enthalpy density in the de Sitter coordinate system.
- For Bjorken flow, the noise autocorrelation amplitude is proportional to $1/(A_\perp w \tau)$, which parametrically equals $1/(dE_\perp/dy)$, the inverse of transverse energy per rapidity.
- The energy density in the Gubser solution is $\hat \epsilon(\rho) = (\cosh \rho)^{-8/3} [\hat T_0 + (1/3) H_0 F(\rho)]^4$, where $H_0$ is proportional to $\eta/s$ and $\hat T_0$ is constrained by multiplicity.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of hydrodynamical noise from the 1+1D Bjorken flow case studied by Kapusta, Muller and Stephanov (2011) to the more realistic Gubser flow which includes transverse expansion. It provides a quantitative estimate of thermal noise effects in small colliding systems, testing the applicability of noisy viscous hydrodynamics in Pb-Pb, p-Pb, and p-p collisions. The results enable future work on whether hydrodynamical noise must be included in event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of small systems.
