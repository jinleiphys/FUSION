# Tensor interaction in mean-field and density functional theory approaches to nuclear structure

> The importance of the tensor force for nuclear structure has been recognized long ago. Recently, the interest for this topic has been revived by the study of the evolution of nuclear properties far from the stability line. However, in the context of the effective theories that describe medium-heavy nuclei, the role of the tensor force is still debated. This review focuses on ground-state properties like masses and deformation, on single-particle states, and on excited vibrational and rotational modes. The goal is to assess which properties, if any, can bring clear signatures of the tensor force within the mean-field or density functional theory framework. It will be concluded that, while evidences for a clear neutron-proton tensor force exist despite quantitative uncertainties, the role of the tensor force among equal particles is less well established.

## Key claim

This review concludes that, while there is evidence for a clear neutron-proton tensor force despite quantitative uncertainties, the role of the tensor force among equal particles (proton-proton or neutron-neutron) is less well established within mean-field or density functional theory frameworks.

## Method

The authors review the implementation of tensor terms in both relativistic (Relativistic Mean Field and Relativistic Hartree-Fock) and nonrelativistic (Skyrme zero-range and finite-range) mean-field and density functional theory approaches. They analyze results from various groups concerning ground-state properties (masses, deformation), single-particle states, and excited vibrational and rotational modes, and also discuss the role of the tensor force in driving ferromagnetic instabilities in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

* The one-pion exchange potential uses a pseudo-vector coupling constant \(f_{\pi}^2 = 0.08\) and a pion mass \(m_{\pi} = 138\) MeV.
* The \(\rho\)-meson exchange potential has a mass \(m_{\rho} = 770\) MeV and a coupling constant \(f_{\rho}^2 = 4.86\).
* The \(\omega\)-meson mass is \(m_{\omega} = 783\) MeV.
* The Skyrme tensor interaction parameters \(T\) and \(U\) denote the strength of the triplet-even and triplet-odd tensor interactions, respectively, with the tensor contributions to the spin-orbit potential given by \(\alpha_T = (5/12)U\) and \(\beta_T = (5/24)(T+U)\).

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the tensor force in nuclear structure, including the early Skyrme parametrization by Skyrme (1959) and the Hartree-Fock calculations by Stancu (1977). It also builds on the relativistic Hartree-Fock model introduced by Bouyssy (1987) and the density-dependent coupling model by Long (2006). The paper enables future systematic studies of tensor effects in exotic nuclei far from stability, particularly for shell evolution, spin-isospin excitations, and the removal of artificial shell closures in mean-field calculations.
