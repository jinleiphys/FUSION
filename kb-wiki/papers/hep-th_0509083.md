# Transport properties of cascading gauge theories

> Cascading gauge theories of Klebanov et.al. provide a model within a framework of gauge theory/string theory duality for a four dimensional non-conformal gauge theory with a spontaneously generated mass scale. Using the dual supergravity description we study sound wave propagation in strongly coupled cascading gauge theory plasma. We analytically compute the speed of sound and the bulk viscosity of cascading gauge theory plasma at a temperature much larger than the strong coupling scale of the theory. The sound wave dispersion relation is obtained from the hydrodynamic pole in the stress-energy tensor two-point correlation function. The speed of sound extracted from the pole of the correlation function agrees with its value computed in [hep-th/0506002] using the equation of state. We find that the bulk viscosity of the hot cascading gauge theory plasma is non-zero at the leading order in the deviation from conformality.

## Key claim

The paper analytically computes the speed of sound and bulk viscosity of strongly coupled cascading gauge theory plasma at temperatures much larger than the strong coupling scale, finding that the bulk viscosity is non-zero at leading order in the deviation from conformality and that the ratio of bulk to shear viscosity is proportional to the deviation of the speed of sound squared from its conformal value.

## Method

Using the dual supergravity description of cascading gauge theories, the author studies sound wave propagation by extracting the dispersion relation from the hydrodynamic pole in the stress-energy tensor two-point correlation function. The computation involves analyzing fluctuations of the black brane geometry dual to the thermal gauge theory, introducing gauge invariant fluctuations, and solving their equations of motion in the hydrodynamic limit (small frequency and momentum compared to temperature) at high temperature where the deformation parameter is small.

## Key numbers

* Speed of sound: \(v_s^2 = \frac{1}{3} - \frac{4}{9} \dd_{cascade} + \mathcal{O}(\dd_{cascade}^2)\), where \(\dd_{cascade} \equiv P^2/K_*\) is the deformation parameter
* Dispersion relation: \(\omega(q) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\left(1-\frac{2}{3}\dd_{cascade}\right)q - i\frac{q^2}{6\pi T}\left(1+\frac{2}{3}\dd_{cascade}\right) + \mathcal{O}(q^3/T^2, \dd_{cascade}^2)\)
* Ratio of bulk to shear viscosity: \(\frac{\zeta}{\eta} = -2\left(v_s^2 - \frac{1}{3}\right) + \mathcal{O}(\dd_{cascade}^2)\)
* Universal shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: \(\frac{\eta}{s} = \frac{1}{4\pi}\)
* For the mass-deformed \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM (comparison case): \(\frac{\zeta}{\eta} \simeq -\kappa\left(v_s^2 - \frac{1}{3}\right)\) with \(\kappa \approx 4.558\) for \(m_b=0\) and \(\kappa \approx 4.935\) for \(m_f=0\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of transport properties in strongly coupled non-conformal gauge theory plasma from theories with explicit mass deformation (the \(\mathcal{N}=2^*\) theory studied by Buchel, Buchel, and collaborators in 2005) to theories with spontaneously generated mass scale, specifically the cascading gauge theories of Klebanov, Nekrasov, and Strassler (2000). The computation uses the finite temperature gauge/gravity correspondence developed by Buchel et al. (2001-2005) and the holographic renormalization framework established by Buchel (2005). The result establishes that the phenomenological relation \(\zeta/\eta \sim -\mathcal{O}(1)\times(v_s^2 - 1/3)\) appears to be a robust prediction for hot strongly coupled non-conformal gauge theory plasma, whether scale invariance is broken explicitly or spontaneously, and is expected to be relevant to real QCD quark-gluon plasma.
