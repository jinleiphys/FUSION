# Optimizing relativistic energy density functionals: covariance analysis

> The stability of model parameters for a class of relativistic energy density functionals, characterized by contact (point-coupling) effective inter-nucleon interactions and density-dependent coupling parameters, is analyzed using methods of statistical analysis. A set of pseudo-observables in infinite and semi-infinite nuclear matter is used to define a quality measure $χ^2$ for subsequent analysis. We calculate uncertainties of model parameters and correlation coefficients between parameters, and determine the eigenvectors and eigenvalues of the matrix of second derivatives of $χ^2$ at the minimum. This allows to examine the stability of the density functional in nuclear matter, and to deduce weakly and strongly constrained combinations of parameters. In addition, we also compute uncertainties of observables that are not included in the calculation of $χ^2$: binding energy of asymmetric nuclear matter, surface thickness of semi-infinite nuclear matter, binding energies and charge radii of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The stability of the DD-PC1 relativistic energy density functional is assessed using pseudo-observables from infinite and semi-infinite nuclear matter with a uniform 2% assigned uncertainty, rather than direct experimental data; the analysis reveals that while the coupling values at saturation are well-constrained, their density derivatives and the parameter \(d_s\) are poorly determined, leading to large model uncertainties.

## Method

A \(\chi^2\) quality measure was constructed from ten pseudo-observables of infinite nuclear matter (saturation density, binding energies at three densities, compressibility, Dirac mass, effective mass, symmetry energy and its slope, and symmetry energy coefficient) and, in a second step, the surface energy of semi-infinite nuclear matter was added. The curvature matrix \(\mathcal{M}\) of second derivatives of \(\chi^2\) with respect to nine (or ten) dimensionless model parameters was diagonalized to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, identifying stiff and soft directions in parameter space. Uncertainties and correlation coefficients for parameters and for observables not included in the fit (binding energy of asymmetric matter, surface thickness, finite-nuclei properties) were computed from the covariance matrix \(\mathcal{M}^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of DD-PC1: \(\rho_\mathrm{sat} = 0.152\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
* Volume energy at saturation: \(a_v = -16.06\ \text{MeV}\)
* Symmetry energy coefficient: \(a_4 = 33\ \text{MeV}\)
* Nuclear matter compression modulus: \(K_{nm} = 230\ \text{MeV}\)
* Symmetry energy at sub-saturation density \(\rho_\mathrm{sub}=0.12\ \text{fm}^{-3}\): \(S_2 = 27.8\ \text{MeV}\)
* Slope of symmetry energy at \(\rho_\mathrm{sub}\): \(L = 57.2\ \text{MeV}\)
* Dirac mass at saturation: \(m_D = 0.58\ m\)
* Effective mass at saturation: \(m^* = 0.66\ m\)
* Binding energy at low density \(\rho_{low}=0.04\ \text{fm}^{-3}\): \(-6.48\ \text{MeV}\)
* Binding energy at high density \(\rho_{high}=0.56\ \text{fm}^{-3}\): \(34.38\ \text{MeV}\)
* Uncertainties of couplings at saturation: \(\leq 5\%\); uncertainties of their first and second derivatives: larger, with \(d_s\) uncertainty \(> 10\%\)

## Context

This work extends the covariance analysis methodology previously applied to non-relativistic Skyrme functionals (Gao et al., 2013) and to a general model study using pseudo-data (Fattoyev et al., 2011) to the relativistic point-coupling functional DD-PC1 (Niksic et al., 2008). By identifying soft parameter combinations, the analysis enables improved calibration strategies for relativistic energy density functionals and provides uncertainty estimates for predictions of neutron-rich matter and finite nuclei, which are critical for extrapolations to astrophysical systems.
