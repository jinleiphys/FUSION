# Measuring the vortex-nucleus pinning force from pulsar glitch rates

> Superfluid vortex avalanches are one plausible cause of pulsar glitch activity. If they occur according to a state-dependent Poisson process, the measured long-term glitch rate is determined by the spin-down rate of the stellar crust, $\dotΩ_{\rm c}$, and two phenomenological parameters quantifying the vortex-nucleus pinning force: a crust-superfluid angular velocity lag threshold, $X_{\rm cr}$, and a reference unpinning rate, $λ_0$. A Bayesian analysis of 541 glitches in 177 pulsars, with $N_{\rm g} \geq 1$ events per pulsar, yields $X_{\rm cr} = 0.15^{+0.09}_{-0.04} \, {\rm rad \, s^{-1}}$, $λ_{\rm ref} = 7.6^{+3.7}_{-2.6} \times 10^{-8} \, {\rm s^{-1}}$, and $a = -0.27^{+0.04}_{-0.03}$ assuming the phenomenological rate law $λ_0 = λ_{\rm ref} [τ/(1 \, {\rm yr})]^a$, where $τ$ denotes the characteristic spin-down age. The results are broadly similar, whether one includes or excludes quasiperiodic glitch activity, giant glitches, or pulsars with $N_{\rm g}=0$, up to uncertainties about the completeness of the sample and the total observation time per pulsar. The $X_{\rm cr}$ and $λ_0$ estimates are consistent with first-principles calculations based on nuclear theory, e.g. in the semiclassical local density approximation.

## Key claim

The vortex-nucleus pinning force can be inferred from pulsar glitch statistics by modeling glitch activity as a state-dependent Poisson process, where the long-term glitch rate is determined by the spin-down rate of the stellar crust and two phenomenological parameters: a crust-superfluid angular velocity lag threshold and a reference unpinning rate. The authors acknowledge that superfluid vortex avalanches are only one of several plausible mechanisms for pulsar glitches, and different mechanisms may operate in different pulsars or simultaneously in the same pulsar.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian analysis of 541 glitches detected in 177 pulsars (with at least one glitch per pulsar) using a hierarchical model where the per-pulsar glitch rate follows a state-dependent Poisson process with mean rate γ = λ₀ + |Ω̇c|/Xcr. The reference unpinning rate λ₀ was modeled as a power law in characteristic spin-down age, λ₀ = λref [τ/(1 yr)]^a, while Xcr was assumed constant across the population. Parameter estimation was carried out via Markov chain Monte Carlo using the Stan programming language, with broad priors consistent with nuclear theory.

## Key numbers

- Xcr = 0.15^{+0.09}_{-0.04} rad s⁻¹ (median and 90% confidence interval) for the 177 pulsars with N_g ≥ 1
- λref = 7.6^{+3.7}_{-2.6} × 10⁻⁸ s⁻¹ (median and 90% confidence interval) for the 177 pulsars with N_g ≥ 1
- a = -0.27^{+0.04}_{-0.03} (median and 90% confidence interval) for the 177 pulsars with N_g ≥ 1
- When including 233 pulsars with N_g = 0, the posterior peaks at λref = 5.5^{+2.3}_{-1.6} × 10⁻⁷ s⁻¹, a = -0.48^{+0.03}_{-0.03}, and Xcr = 0.22^{+0.29}_{-0.08} rad s⁻¹
- The model rules out a = 0 with more than 99% confidence

## Context

This work extends the state-dependent Poisson process framework developed by Fulgenzi et al. (2017) for modeling pulsar glitch activity as a stress-relax process, and builds on empirical scaling relations between glitch rate and spin-down rate discovered by McKenna and Lyne (1990), Lyne et al. (2000), Espinoza et al. (2011), and Fuentes et al. (2017). The inferred pinning parameters are consistent with first-principles nuclear theory calculations using the semiclassical local density approximation (Alpar et al. 1984, Epstein and Baym 1988, Donati and Pizzochero 2006, Avogadro et al. 2008, Seveso et al. 2016, Link 2022). The results enable future comparisons with microscopic calculations of the vortex-nucleus pinning force at neutron drip densities, though the authors caution that the analysis is limited by uncertainties in catalog completeness and observation time estimates.
