# Axial anomaly effects in finite isospin $χ$PT in a magnetic field

> In this paper, we consider finite isospin chiral perturbation theory including the effects of the axial anomaly (through the Wess-Zumino-Witten term) in a strong magnetic field. We firstly prove that in a strong external magnetic field ($H_{\rm ext}$) or more precisely the Schwinger limit, where photon back-reactions are suppressed, only neutral pions can condense and the condensation of charged pions is forbidden. Secondly, we find that the $π^{0}$ domain wall is an example of a phase that can exist in a strong magnetic field and suggest the existence of a new phase transition line from the normal vacuum state to the $π^{0}$ domain wall state. This phase transition exists for non-zero pion masses if the baryon chemical potential exceeds a critical value $16πf_π^{2}m_π/eH_{\rm ext}$. The phase transition line persists away from the Schwinger limit when the photons can back-react to the external magnetic field.

# Axial anomaly effects in finite isospin χPT in a magnetic field

## Key claim

The paper proves that in the Schwinger limit (where photon back-reactions are suppressed), only neutral pions can condense and charged pion condensation is forbidden in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory; a π⁰ domain wall phase becomes stable above a critical baryon chemical potential, introducing a new phase transition line from the normal vacuum state that persists even when photons can back-react to the external magnetic field.

## Method

The authors work at leading order in chiral perturbation theory with an SU(2) matrix parametrization of the pion fields, coupling the Lagrangian to a uniform external magnetic field and a finite isospin chemical potential. They derive the equations of motion for the gauge fields and the pion fields, then systematically exclude possible phases (chirally restored phase and a phase with constrained complex phase of charged pions) to prove that only neutral pion condensation is possible in the Schwinger limit. The stability of the π⁰ domain wall solution is analyzed by expanding the Lagrangian around the domain wall configuration and computing the dispersion relation for charged pion fluctuations, including the Wess-Zumino-Witten term for axial anomaly effects.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryon chemical potential for π⁰ domain wall stability: μ_B^c = 16π f_π² m_π / (e H_ext)
- Metastability condition for π⁰ domain wall at zero isospin: H_ext > 3m_π² / e
- Metastability condition for π⁰ domain wall at finite isospin: H_ext > H_c3 = (3m_π² + μ_I²) / e
- Upper critical field for vortex-to-normal transition: e H_c2 = μ_I² - m_π²
- Difference between critical fields: H_c3 - H_c2 = 4m_π² / e
- Energy per unit area of the π⁰ domain wall: E_dw / S = 8 f_π² m_π
- Magnetization per unit area of the domain wall: M_dw / S = ẑ (e / 2π) μ_B

## Context

This work extends the finite isospin chiral perturbation theory analysis of Adhikari (2015), which showed type-II superconducting behavior through charged pion condensation with back-reacting photons, by considering the Schwinger limit where photon back-reactions are absent. The π⁰ domain wall phase was first considered by Son (Son4) for zero isospin chemical potential; the present paper generalizes this to finite isospin and shows a new phase transition line emerges above the critical baryon chemical potential. The results enable construction of the full phase diagram of finite isospin QCD in a magnetic field, including the normal vacuum, uniform superconducting state, vortex state, and π⁰ domain wall state, with the chiral limit showing a direct transition from vortex to domain wall phase without an intervening normal phase.
