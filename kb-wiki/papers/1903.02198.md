# A semi-classical transport equation for the chiral quarks surrounding a SU(2) t'Hooft-Polyakov monopole

> A transport equation of the representative quasi-particles is derived, by solving Dirac (or Dyson) equations under the semi-classical approximation, for describing the entangled motions of the red and blue chiral quarks in the vicinity of a SU(2) t'Hooft-Polyakov monopole. The representative quasi-particles have two states characterized by different dispersion relations. A ground state quasi-particle has a zero pole mass, a zero longitudinal inertial mass and a finite transverse inertial mass, while an excited quasi-particle has a finite pole mass and finite inertial masses. At equilibrium, the red and blue currents flow against each other in the transverse direction, and the red and blue axial currents flow against each other in the radial direction.

## Key claim

The paper derives a semi-classical transport equation for chiral quarks near a SU(2) t'Hooft-Polyakov monopole, but the result is not gauge invariant because the derivation relies on a specific gauge choice where the field lines of the monopole configuration are circles perpendicular to and symmetric about each coordinate axis.

## Method

The author starts from a pair of Dirac equations for the Keldysh Green's function, applies a Wigner transformation, and expands to leading order in ℏ (semi-classical approximation). The resulting equations are decomposed in both spinor and color spaces, and solved for the monopole background field configuration Ω⁰_A = 0, Ωⁱ_A = ε_{Aik}ω_k. The solution expresses all components of the Wigner function in terms of a single distribution function per chirality, yielding a Vlasov-type transport equation for representative quasi-particles.

## Key numbers

- Ground state quasi-particle: zero pole mass, zero longitudinal inertial mass, finite transverse inertial mass = |ω| (where ω is the monopole field vector)
- Excited state quasi-particle: pole mass = 2|ω|, longitudinal inertial mass = 2|ω|, transverse inertial mass = |ω|
- For |p| ≪ |ω|, the dispersion relation is p⁰ ≈ √(p_⊥² + p_∥² + 2ω² ± (2ω² + p_⊥² − p_⊥⁴/4ω²))
- The rank of the linear equations for the Wigner components is 15 for the monopole configuration (compared to rank 14 for an abelian background field)

## Context

This work extends the Wigner function formalism previously applied to partonic transport equations (Elze 1986, Vasak 1987, Klevansky 1997, Li 2019) to the specific case of a t'Hooft-Polyakov monopole background. The monopole configuration follows the original solutions of Polyakov (1974) and t'Hooft (1974). The entangled motion of red and blue quarks, described by a single representative quasi-particle distribution, is presented as a feature relevant to confinement. The derived transport equation can be solved using the test particle method of Wong (1982), and the author plans to extend the derivation to order ℏ to study quantum effects.
