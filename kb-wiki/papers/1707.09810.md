# Lattice Quantum Monte Carlo Study of Chiral Magnetic Effect in Dirac Semimetals

> In this paper Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in Dirac semimetals is studied by means of lattice Monte Carlo simulation. We measure conductivity of Dirac semimetals as a function of external magnetic field in parallel $σ_{\parallel}$ and perpendicular $σ_{\perp}$ to the external field directions. The simulations are carried out in three regimes: semimetal phase, onset of the insulator phase and deep in the insulator phase. In the semimetal phase $σ_{\parallel}$ grows whereas $σ_{\perp}$ drops with magnetic field. Similar behaviour was observed in the onset of the insulator phase but conductivity is smaller and its dependence on magnetic field is weaker. Finally in the insulator phase conductivities $σ_{\parallel, \perp}$ are close to zero and do not depend on magnetic field. In other words, we observe manifestation of the CME current in the semimetal phase, weaker manifestation of the CME in the onset of the insulator phase. We do not observe signatures of CME in the insulator phase. We believe that the suppression of the CME current in the insulator phase is connected to chiral symmetry breaking and generation of dynamical fermion mass which take place in this phase.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in Dirac semimetals is clearly observable in the semimetal phase where chiral symmetry is not dynamically broken, but is suppressed in the insulator phase where chiral symmetry is dynamically broken and a dynamical fermion mass is generated; the CME conductivity drops rapidly in the transition region between these phases.

## Method

The authors performed lattice quantum Monte Carlo simulations of Dirac semimetals with two Fermi points and small isotropic Fermi velocity, using staggered fermions coupled to an Abelian lattice gauge field. They measured the Euclidean correlator of spatial electric current components and applied the midpoint calculation method to extract the conductivity parallel and perpendicular to an external magnetic field. Simulations were carried out on a 20x20x20x20 lattice with temporal-spatial anisotropy ξ=1/4 and fermion mass m=0.0025, for effective coupling constants α_eff = 0.32 (semimetal phase), 1.27 (onset of insulator phase), and 2.55 (deep in insulator phase), across multiple magnetic field values.

## Key numbers

- The critical effective coupling constant for the semimetal-to-insulator transition is α_eff^c = 1.14, as reported in Braguta (2017).
- For α_eff = 0.32 (semimetal phase), σ_∥ grows with magnetic field while σ_⊥ drops, showing negative magnetoresistance for parallel conductivity and positive magnetoresistance for perpendicular conductivity.
- For α_eff = 1.27 (onset of insulator phase), σ_∥ and σ_⊥ behave similarly to the semimetal phase but with smaller absolute values and weaker CME.
- For α_eff = 2.55 (deep in insulator phase), conductivities σ_∥ and σ_⊥ are close to zero and independent of magnetic field.
- At magnetic field eB (a_s L_s)^2/(2π) = 40, the CME conductivity σ^CME drops rapidly for α_eff > 1 and is zero within uncertainty for α_eff > 1.4, coinciding with the region where the chiral condensate rises.

## Context

This work extends lattice Monte Carlo studies of strongly correlated condensed matter systems, following the approach used by Braguta (2016, 2017) to study dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and the semimetal-insulator transition in Dirac semimetals. The results confirm experimental observations of CME in Dirac semimetals reported by Li (2014, 2015, 2016) and provide a theoretical framework for understanding how strong interactions and chiral symmetry breaking suppress the CME. The findings enable future studies of CME in systems with dynamically generated fermion mass and establish lattice QCD-inspired methods as a tool for condensed matter physics.
