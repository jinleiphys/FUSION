# Isospin asymmetry and type-I superconductivity in neutron star matter

> It has been argued by Buckley et. al.(Phys. Rev. Lett. 92, 151102, 2004) that nuclear matter is a type-I rather than a type-II superconductor. The suggested mechanism is a strong interaction between neutron and proton Cooper pairs, which arises from an assumed U(2) symmetry of the effective potential, which is supposed to originate in isospin symmetry of the underlying nuclear interactions. To test this claim, we perform an explicit mean-field calculation of the effective potential of the Cooper pairs in a model with a simple four-point pairing interaction. In the neutron star context, matter is very neutron rich with less than 10% protons, so there is no neutron-proton pairing. We find that under these conditions our model shows no interaction between proton Cooper pairs and neutron Cooper pairs at the mean-field level. We estimate the leading contribution beyond mean field and find that it is is small and attractive at weak coupling.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the U(2) symmetry of the effective potential for neutron and proton Cooper pairs assumed by Buckley et al. (2004) is not present in neutron star matter, because the large disparity between neutron and proton Fermi momenta (due to electrical neutrality and beta-equilibrium) provides a strong explicit breaking of that symmetry. In their mean-field calculation, there is no interaction between proton and neutron Cooper pairs at all, and the leading beyond-mean-field correction is negligible.

## Method

The authors perform a mean-field calculation of the thermodynamic potential for a simple four-point pairing interaction model of neutrons and protons, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce bosonic Cooper-pair fields. They then expand the thermodynamic potential around the mean-field ground state to obtain the quartic couplings between fluctuations in the neutron and proton condensates, and calculate the leading beyond-mean-field correction diagrammatically using Nambu-Gorkov Green's functions. They also perform a Landau-Ginzburg analysis near the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- Neutron chemical potential: μ_n ~ 60 MeV (k_Fn ~ 335 MeV); proton chemical potential: μ_p ~ 8 MeV (k_Fp ~ 123 MeV)
- Momentum cutoff: Λ = 750 MeV
- Four-fermion couplings giving Δ_nn ~ 1 MeV and Δ_pp ~ 1 MeV: G_nn ~ 1 × 10^(-5) MeV^(-2) and G_pp ~ 2 × 10^(-5) MeV^(-2)
- At mean-field level, the asymmetry parameter ε = 1 (no interaction between neutron and proton Cooper pairs)
- Beyond-mean-field corrections keep ε within 10^(-4) of unity
- Near critical temperature (T_c ~ 0.6 MeV), at T = 0.4 MeV: μ^c_nn = 5520 MeV^2, μ^c_pp = 2010 MeV^2, a^c_nn = 2627, a^c_pp = 968, a^c_np = -3 (G_np/G_nn)

## Context

This paper directly tests the claim by Buckley et al. (2004) that nuclear matter is a type-I superconductor due to a U(2) symmetry of the effective potential for Cooper pairs. The authors find that the assumed symmetry is strongly broken by the Fermi momentum disparity in neutron star matter, contradicting the earlier work. The result enables future calculations of vortex structure in neutron star cores using the Landau-Ginzburg framework near T_c, though the authors note that non-perturbative nuclear interactions could still allow strong coupling between neutron and proton superfluids.
