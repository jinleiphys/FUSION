# Role of octupole shape degree of freedom in neutron-rich odd-mass xenon isotopes

> Influences of the octupole shape degree of freedom on low-energy spectra of neutron-rich odd-mass xenon isotopes are studied within the interacting boson-fermion model that is based on the nuclear density functional theory. The interacting-boson Hamiltonian describing low-energy quadrupole and octupole collective states of the even-even nuclei $^{140,142,144}$Xe, single-particle energies, and occupation probabilities for an unpaired neutron in the odd-mass nuclei $^{141,143,145}$Xe, are determined based on the axially symmetric quadrupole-octupole deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations with a choice of the energy density functional and pairing interaction. Strength parameters of the boson-fermion interactions are empirically determined to reproduce a few low-lying levels of each odd-mass nucleus. The mean-field calculation predicts for $^{142}$Xe a potential energy surface that is notably soft in the octupole deformation with a non-zero octupole global minimum. The octupole correlations are shown to be relevant in positive-parity excited states of $^{143,145}$Xe.

## Key claim

Octupole correlations are shown to be relevant in positive-parity excited states of the neutron-rich odd-mass nuclei $^{143,145}$Xe, but the paper hedges that the octupole degree of freedom is most pronounced at the neutron number $N=88$ (in $^{142}$Xe) and that the potential energy surface for $^{142}$Xe is notably soft in octupole deformation with a non-zero octupole global minimum only 32 keV lower than the $\beta_3=0$ axis, rather than exhibiting a rigid static octupole deformation.

## Method

The author performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliogov model using the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to obtain potential energy surfaces in the axially symmetric quadrupole ($\beta_2$) and octupole ($\beta_3$) deformation plane for the even-even nuclei $^{140,142,144}$Xe. These surfaces were then mapped onto the expectation value of an interacting boson model Hamiltonian with $s$, $d$, and $f$ bosons ($sdf$-IBM), and the resulting core Hamiltonian was coupled to a single neutron in the $N=82-126$ major shell via the interacting boson-fermion model ($sdf$-IBFM), with boson-fermion interaction strengths empirically adjusted to reproduce a few low-lying levels of each odd-mass nucleus $^{141,143,145}$Xe.

## Key numbers

- For $^{142}$Xe ($N=88$), the global minimum of the RHB-SCMF potential energy surface is found at $(\beta_2,\beta_3)=(0.2,0.05)$, and the energy at this minimum is only 32 keV lower than the lowest energy along the $\beta_3=0$ axis.
- The equilibrium quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ is approximately 0.15 for $^{140}$Xe and 0.20 for both $^{142}$Xe and $^{144}$Xe.
- The $sdf$-IBM calculation for $^{140}$Xe reproduces the experimental $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value of $24.0 \pm 0.7$ W.u. with a calculated value of 24.1 W.u.
- For $^{142}$Xe, the expectation value of the $f$-boson number $\langle \hat n_f \rangle$ in the $0^+_1$ ground state is 1.121, indicating significant octupole correlations even in the ground state.
- The quasiparticle energy for the $1i_{13/2}$ orbital in $^{141}$Xe is 0.981 MeV, in $^{143}$Xe is 0.974 MeV, and in $^{145}$Xe is 0.966 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the $sdf$-IBFM framework previously developed by Nomura (2018) for neutron-rich odd-mass barium isotopes ($^{143,145,147}$Ba) to the neighboring xenon isotopes, using the same relativistic EDF (DD-PC1) and similar model parameters. The study is motivated by recent experimental data from the EURICA project at RIKEN on $^{140}$Xe (Yagi, 2022) and $^{141,143}$Xe (Nor, 2024), and it enables systematic predictions for low-lying structures of these neutron-rich nuclei, including the role of octupole correlations in odd-mass systems where the even-even core exhibits octupole softness.
