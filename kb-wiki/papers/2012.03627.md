# Cooper Triples in Attractive Three-Component Fermions: Implication for Hadron-Quark Crossover

> We investigate many-body properties of equally populated three-component fermions with attractive three-body contact interaction in one dimension. A diagrammatic approach suggests the possible occurrence of Cooper triples at low temperature, which are three-body counterparts of Cooper pairs with a two-body attraction. We develop a minimal framework that bridges the crossover from tightly-bound trimers to Cooper triples with increasing chemical potential and show how the formation of Cooper triples occurs in the grand-canonical phase diagram. Moreover, we argue that this non-trivial crossover is similar to the hadron-quark crossover proposed in dense matter. A coexistence of medium-induced triples and the underlying Fermi sea at positive chemical potential is analogous to quarkyonic matter consisting of baryonic excitations and the underlying quark Fermi sea. The comparison with the existing quantum Monte Carlo results implies that the emergence of these kinds of three-body states can be a microscopic origin of the peak of the sound velocity along the crossover.

## Key claim

Using a diagrammatic approach for one-dimensional three-component fermions with attractive three-body contact interaction, the authors suggest the possible occurrence of Cooper triples at low temperature, which are three-body counterparts of Cooper pairs, but only when the chemical potential is sufficiently large to ensure coexistence of the Fermi sea and loosely-bound triple states.

## Method

The authors analyze the in-medium three-body T-matrix for non-relativistic three-component fermions with a contact-type three-body interaction in one dimension, incorporating Pauli-blocking and thermal agitation effects via statistical factors in the three-particle propagator. They determine the temperature T* where the in-medium trimer binding energy disappears and construct the grand-canonical phase diagram, also calculating three-body spectral functions at fixed temperature.

## Key numbers

- The in-vacuum trimer binding energy is given by \(E_{\rm B}=\frac{\Lambda^2}{m}e^{\frac{2\sqrt{3}\pi}{mg_3}}\)
- For numerical calculation of T*, the authors take \(E_{\rm B}^{\rm M}=10^{-2}E_{\rm B}\) and \(\delta=10^{-3}E_{\rm B}\)
- Three-body spectral functions are calculated at temperature \(T/E_{\rm B}=0.1\) for chemical potentials \(\mu/E_{\rm B}=-1\) and \(\mu/E_{\rm B}=2\)

## Context

This work extends the concept of Cooper triples previously predicted in three-dimensional three-component Fermi gases with two-body attraction (Niemann and Tajima, 2021) to one-dimensional systems with three-body attraction. The crossover from tightly-bound trimers to Cooper triples is analogous to the hadron-quark crossover in dense QCD, where quarkyonic matter (McLerran, 2007) features coexistence of baryonic excitations and the underlying quark Fermi sea. The comparison with quantum Monte Carlo results (McKenny, 2020) suggests that three-body states may be the microscopic origin of the sound velocity peak along the crossover.
