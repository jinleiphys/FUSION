# Octupole deformation and Ra puzzle in reflection asymmetric covariant density functional theory

> Reflection asymmetric covariant density functional theory(CDFT) based on the point-coupling interaction is established on a two-center harmonic-oscillator basis and applied to investigate the Ra puzzle, i.e., the anomalous enhancement of the residual proton-neutron interactions for Ra isotopes around N=135. The octupole deformation and shape evolution in the Ra and Rn isotopes are examined in the potential energy surfaces in(beta2, beta3) plane by the constrained reflection asymmetric calculations. The residual proton-neutron interactions extracted from the double difference of the binding energies for Ra isotopes are compared with the data as well as the axial and the triaxial calculations. It is found that the octupole deformation is responsible for the Ra puzzle in the microscopic CDFT.

# Octupole deformation and Ra puzzle in reflection asymmetric covariant density functional theory

## Key claim

The anomalous enhancement of residual proton-neutron interactions (δVpn) for Ra isotopes around N=135, the "Ra puzzle", is explained by octupole deformation in microscopic covariant density functional theory, but only when reflection asymmetry is explicitly included; axial and triaxial calculations without octupole degrees of freedom fail to reproduce the observed peak.

## Method

The authors developed reflection asymmetric covariant density functional theory (RAS-RMF-PC) using a point-coupling interaction (PC-PK1) solved on a two-center harmonic-oscillator (TCHO) basis. Constrained calculations in the (β2, β3) plane were performed to obtain potential energy surfaces for 210-229Ra and 208-227Rn, and residual proton-neutron interactions were extracted from the double difference of binding energies using Eq. (1). The results were compared with experimental data and with axial and triaxial RMF calculations that lack octupole degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state octupole deformation parameters for 220-229Ra: 0.1 ≤ β3 ≤ 0.18, increasing with neutron number
- Ground-state quadrupole deformation parameters for 220-229Ra: 0.15 < β2 < 0.23
- For 210-219Ra: ground-state β2 between 0.04 and 0.1, with β3 = 0
- For 218Ra: a local minimum appears with octupole deformation β3 = 0.13
- For Rn isotopes: octupole deformation β3 increases from 0.02 (218Rn) to 0.08 (223Rn), then drops to 0.02 (225Rn)
- Binding energy of 212Ra changes less than 0.01% when increasing major shell number from Nf=16 to 18 for axial and triaxial calculations, and less than 0.02% for RAS-RMF-PC calculations
- Major shell number Nf=16 was adopted for all calculations

## Context

This work extends the reflection asymmetric relativistic mean-field theory previously developed on a TCHO basis by Geng (2007) and applied to 226Ra, and by Zhang (2010) and Guo (2010) for Sm and Th isotopes. It directly addresses the "Ra puzzle" identified by Brenner (2006) and confirmed by Neidherr (2009) via precise Penning-trap mass measurements on 223-229Rn. The results show that octupole deformation, rather than quadrupole deformation or triaxiality, is responsible for the anomalous δVpn peak, and that including pairing correlations may further improve agreement for even-even Ra isotopes.
