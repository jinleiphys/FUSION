# Nucleon deformation in finite nuclei

> The deformation of a nucleon embedded in various finite nuclei is considered by taking into account the distortion of the chiral profile functions under the action of an external field representing the nuclear density. The baryon charge distribution of the nucleon inside light, medium-heavy and heavy nuclei is discussed. The mass of the nucleon decreases as it is placed deeper inside the nucleus and reaches its minimum at the center of the nucleus. We discuss the quantization of non-spherical solitons and its consequences for the mass splitting of the delta states. We show that bound nucleons acquire an intrinsic quadrupole moment due to the deformation effects. These effects are maximal for densities of nuclei about ρ(R)\sim 0.3...0.35 ρ(0). We also point out that scale changes of the electromagnetic radii can not simply be described by an overall swelling factor.

## Key claim

The deformation of a nucleon inside a finite nucleus is maximal not at the center but at densities around \(\rho(R)\sim 0.3\ldots 0.35\,\rho(0)\), and the resulting intrinsic quadrupole moment cannot be captured by a simple overall swelling of the electromagnetic radii.

## Method

The authors embed a Skyrme-type soliton (baryon number \(B=1\)) in a finite nucleus by coupling the chiral profile functions to distance-dependent nuclear densities via medium functionals \(\alpha_p(\vec r)\) and \(\alpha_s(\vec r)\) derived from pion-nucleon scattering lengths. They minimize the static mass functional using a variational ansatz that allows both radial breathing (parameter \(\delta r_S\)) and angular deformation (parameters \(\gamma_1,\gamma_2,\delta_1\)) of the profile function \(F(r',\theta)\) and the angular function \(\Theta(\theta)\). The resulting non-spherical soliton is then quantized adiabatically, accounting for the axial symmetry of the system, to obtain the masses of nucleon and \(\Delta\) states and the intrinsic quadrupole moment.

## Key numbers

- Free-space Skyrme parameters: \(F_\pi = 108\) MeV, \(e = 5.265\), chosen to reproduce free nucleon and \(\Delta\) masses.
- Normal nuclear matter density: \(\rho_0 = 0.5\,m_\pi^{-3}\).
- Deformation effects are maximal at nuclear densities \(\rho(R) \sim 0.3\ldots 0.35\,\rho(0)\).
- For light nuclei (\({}^{12}\)C, \({}^{16}\)O), the leading angular deformation parameter \(\gamma_1\) peaks where the density falls by \(\sim 20\%\); for medium-heavy and heavy nuclei (\({}^{40}\)Ca, \({}^{56}\)Fe, \({}^{198}\)Au, \({}^{208}\)Pb) it peaks where the density falls by \(\sim 40\%\).
- The parameters \(\gamma_2\) and \(\delta_1\) have maxima where the density falls by \(\sim 80\%\).
- Free-space skyrmion size parameter: \(r_S^f = 0.6\) fm.
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV; nucleon mass: \(M_N = 938\) MeV.
- Empirical parameters for medium functionals: \(b_0 = -0.024\,m_\pi^{-1}\), \(c_0 = 0.21\,m_\pi^{-3}\), Migdal parameter \(g_0' = 1/3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Skyrme solitons in infinite nuclear matter (e.g., the authors' own work in prc001 and npa001) by introducing a finite-nucleus density profile and allowing non-spherical deformation of the soliton. It contrasts with the infinite-matter approach, which only treats nucleons at the center of heavy nuclei, and with earlier deformation studies for \(B>1\) systems (Kopeliovich, Nikolaev, Otofuji). The results enable a more realistic description of nucleon properties (mass, radii, quadrupole moment) as a function of position inside light, medium-heavy, and heavy nuclei, and provide a framework for future inclusion of Fermi motion and more sophisticated medium modifications.
