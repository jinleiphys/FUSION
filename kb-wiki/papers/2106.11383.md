# Model dependence of the polarization asymmetries in weak pion production off the nucleon

> The work presents the studies of the polarization observables in the single pion production (SPP) induced by the interaction of the muon neutrino (antineutrino) with nucleons. The SPP cross-sections and spin asymmetries are computed within two phenomenological models. One is basing on the nonlinear sigma model \cite{Hernandez:2007qq}; the other has origin in linear sigma model~\cite{Fogli:1979cz}. Firstly, we show that the final nucleon polarization and target spin asymmetries are good observables to obtain information about the $C_5^A$ axial form factor. Secondly, we demonstrate that the nucleon polarization and the target spin asymmetries are sensitive to the relative phase between resonance and nonresonance amplitudes. We conclude that the polarization of the final nucleon and the target spin asymmetry are promising observables for testing SPP models, including studies of the axial content of $Δ(1232)$ resonance and unitarization procedures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final nucleon polarization and target spin asymmetries in charged-current single pion production (SPP) are sensitive to the axial form factor \(C_5^A\) and to the relative phase between resonance and nonresonance amplitudes, but only under the condition that the muon polarization is largely insensitive to these hadronic model details, making the nucleon and target observables the promising ones for testing SPP models.

## Method

The authors computed cross-sections and spin asymmetries for six charged-current SPP processes induced by muon neutrinos and antineutrinos on nucleons, using two phenomenological models: the HNV model (based on a nonlinear sigma model with pseudovector \(\pi NN\) vertex) and the FN model (based on a linear sigma model with pseudo-axial \(\pi NN\) vertex). They varied the dipole parameters of the \(C_5^A\) axial form factor (axial mass \(M_A\) and \(C_5^A(0)\)) and introduced a toy-model relative phase \(\Phi\) between resonance and nonresonant amplitudes, computing longitudinal, transverse, and normal components of lepton polarization, final nucleon polarization, and target spin asymmetry for neutrino energy \(E=1\) GeV and energy transfer \(\omega=0.5\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* The normal component of final nucleon polarization \(\mathcal{P}_{N'}^N\) reaches about 0.4 for the HNV model and 0.3 for the FN model at a slight scattering angle, for \(E=1\) GeV and \(\omega=0.5\) GeV.
* The default axial mass used is \(M_A = 1.0\) GeV.
* The default \(C_5^A(0)\) values are 1.10 for the HNV model and 1.18 for the FN model.
* The hadronic invariant mass is limited to \(W < 1.4\) GeV in all numerical experiments.
* The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation gives \(C_5^A(0) = 1.15 \pm 0.01\) from \(g_{\pi N\Delta}(q^2 = m_\pi^2) = 28.6 \pm 0.3\) and \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of polarization observables in SPP (Graczyk 2017, Graczyk 2019) by systematically studying sensitivity to the axial form factor \(C_5^A\) and to the relative phase between resonance and nonresonant amplitudes. It contrasts with earlier analyses of ANL and BNL bubble chamber data (Radecky 1981, Kitagaki 1986) which provided spin-averaged cross-sections but insufficient information to thoroughly constrain SPP models. The results suggest that combining polarization observables with spin-averaged cross-section data can provide complete information about the axial structure of the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance and enable testing of unitarization procedures such as those applied to the HNV model by Alvarez-Ruso (2015).
