# A large Nc perspective on the QCD phase diagram

> The transition between the hadronic phase and the quark gluon plasma phase at nonzero temperature and quark chemical potentials is studied within the large-Nc expansion of QCD.

# A large Nc perspective on the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim
The large-Nc expansion of QCD predicts that the critical temperature for the transition between the hadronic phase and the quark-gluon plasma phase at nonzero baryon chemical potential differs from that at nonzero isospin chemical potential only at order 1/Nc^2, and that if a first order phase transition exists at temperatures below approximately 140 MeV for one chemical potential configuration, it must also exist for the other configuration up to the same order of corrections.

## Method
The author uses the large-Nc expansion of QCD, where the pressure is expanded as p = Nc^2 p0(T) + Nc sum_f p1(T, m_f, mu_f^2) + O(1/Nc^0), with the key property that quark chemical potentials do not mix at next-to-leading order because planar diagrams with a single quark loop are even in each flavor's chemical potential separately. The critical temperature is defined by the peak of the specific heat C_V, and the author analyzes the Taylor expansion of dC_V/dT around zero chemical potential to extract the chemical potential dependence of T_c. For the order of the phase transition, the Clausius-Clapeyron relation connects the latent heat to the discontinuity of the quark-antiquark condensate.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature for pure Yang-Mills theory (Nc=3) is approximately 270 MeV, while for QCD with three flavors it is approximately 175 MeV, giving a relative difference of order 1/Nc.
- For pure Yang-Mills theories with varying Nc, the critical temperature follows T_c^YM(Nc)/T_infinity^YM = 1 + 0.76(6)/Nc^2 + ..., where T_infinity^YM is the Nc to infinity limit.
- The relation T_c(mu,mu,mu_s) - T_c(mu,-mu,mu_s) divided by T_c(mu,mu,mu_s) is of order 1/Nc^2.
- The quark-antiquark condensate at nonzero temperature in the hadronic phase remains at least 80% of its zero-temperature value for temperatures below approximately 140 MeV, based on chiral perturbation theory results for two massive quarks.
- The latent heat for a first order phase transition at temperatures below approximately 140 MeV is of order Nc^2.

## Context
This work extends the large-Nc framework developed by Cohen (2004) for nonzero temperature and chemical potential to study the QCD phase diagram, and it explains puzzling lattice results from Kogut et al. and the Bielefeld group showing that T_c(mu,mu,0) is approximately equal to T_c(mu,-mu,0). The paper provides a theoretical justification for using lattice simulations at nonzero isospin chemical potential (which have no sign problem) to infer properties of the phase transition at nonzero baryon chemical potential (which suffers from the sign problem). This enables a practical strategy for determining the location of a possible critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, complementing direct lattice approaches at small chemical potential from Fodor and Katz, the Bielefeld group, and others.
