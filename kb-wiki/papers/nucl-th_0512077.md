# Variational Calculation on A=3 and 4 Nuclei with Non-Local Potentials

> The application of the hyperspherical harmonic approach to the case of non-local two-body potentials is described. Given the properties of the hyperspherical harmonic functions, there are no difficulties in considering the approach in both coordinate and momentum space. The binding energies and other ground state properties of A=3 and 4 nuclei are calculated using the CD Bonn 2000 and N3LO two-body potentials. The results are shown to be in excellent agreement with corresponding ones obtained by other accurate techniques.

## Key claim

The hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method, previously limited to local nuclear potentials, can be successfully extended to non-local two-body potentials such as CD Bonn 2000 and N3LO, with the caveat that three-nucleon interactions (TNIs) were not included in the calculations reported here.

## Method

The authors expanded the A=3 and A=4 nuclear wave functions in a basis of hyperspherical harmonic functions combined with spin-isospin states, using the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle to solve the Schrodinger equation. The matrix elements of non-local potentials were computed in momentum space using the Fourier-transformed HH basis, while local Coulomb interactions were treated in coordinate space. Two choices for the hyperradial basis functions were tested: exponential functions and Laguerre polynomials, with the Laguerre basis proving more suitable for the N3LO potential.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy with CD Bonn 2000 potential using only the first 3 channels: 7.699 MeV, accurate at the 1 keV level
- Triton binding energy convergence from G=60 to G=70: gain of 1.3 keV; from G=70 to G=80: gain of 0.5 keV (exponential basis)
- Triton binding energy convergence from G=60 to G=70: gain of 1.3 keV; from G=70 to G=80: gain of 0.6 keV (Laguerre basis)
- Maximum grand angular momentum G_max = 80 for the first 3 channels with CD Bonn 2000, compared to G_max = 40 for the first 3 channels with N3LO
- Non-linear parameter gamma for Laguerre basis: 3.5-4.5 fm^{-1} for CD Bonn 2000, 6-8 fm^{-1} for N3LO potential
- Partial wave decomposition truncated at two-body total angular momentum j_max = 6, giving accuracy better than 1 keV in triton binding energy

## Context

This work extends the HH method, previously applied to local potentials like Argonne v18 (Viviani et al., 2005; Kievsky et al., 1997), to non-local potentials derived from chiral perturbation theory (N3LO by Entem and Machleidt, 2003) and the CD Bonn 2000 potential (Machleidt, 2001). The results enable future application of the HH method to A=3 and 4 scattering states with non-local interactions, where only the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach (Nogga et al., 2000; Delves, 2003) and No Core Shell Model (Navratil et al., 2004) had previously been employed.
