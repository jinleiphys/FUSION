# The nucleon axial charge from lattice QCD with controlled errors

> We report on our calculation of the nucleon axial charge gA in QCD with two flavours of dynamical quarks. A detailed investigation of systematic errors is performed, with a particular focus on contributions from excited states to three-point correlation functions. The use of summed operator insertions allows for a much better control over such contamination. After performing a chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass, we find gA=1.223 +/- 0.063 (stat) +0.035 -0.060 (syst), in good agreement with the experimental value.

## Key claim

The nucleon axial charge gA is determined from lattice QCD with two dynamical quark flavours to be 1.223 +/- 0.063 (stat) +0.035 -0.060 (syst), which agrees with the experimental value only after the use of summed operator insertions to control excited-state contamination; without this method, the plateau method yields a lower value of 1.173 +/- 0.038 (stat) that fails to reproduce experiment.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with Nf=2 flavours of O(a) improved Wilson fermions and the Wilson plaquette action, using ensembles generated by the CLS initiative with lattice spacings of approximately 0.079 fm, 0.063 fm, and 0.050 fm. The axial charge was extracted from three-point correlation functions using the method of summed operator insertions, where the ratio R(t,ts) is summed over the insertion time t and the slope of the resulting S(ts) versus ts is fitted linearly, providing parametric suppression of excited-state contributions compared to the conventional plateau method. Chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass were performed using several ansatze including linear fits, logarithmic chiral perturbation theory forms, and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Final result: gA = 1.223 +/- 0.063 (stat) +0.035 -0.060 (syst), agreeing with the experimental value 1.2701(25) at the 6-7% level
- Plateau method result from the same chiral extrapolation: gA = 1.173 +/- 0.038 (stat), which fails to reproduce the experimental value by two standard deviations
- For ensemble A5 (m_pi = 312 MeV), the summation method gives gA = 1.382(127) while the plateau method gives gA = 1.228(61), a difference of about 10%
- Source-sink separations ts ranged from approximately 0.7 fm to 1.3 fm for the summation method fits
- Pion masses across ensembles ranged from 277 MeV to 649 MeV
- Finite-volume corrections based on HBChPT were at the sub-percent level for all ensembles (m_pi L > 4, L between 2 fm and 3 fm)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy where lattice calculations of the nucleon axial charge typically lie 10-15% below the experimental value, with no tendency for the gap to narrow as the pion mass decreases, as seen in prior results from LHPC (2005, 2010), RBC/UKQCD (2004, 2008, 2009), QCDSF (2006, 2009, 2010), BGR (2009), and ETMC (2010). The use of summed operator insertions, following the approach of Maiani (1987) and Bulava (2011), provides a method to separate excited-state contamination from finite-volume effects, which had been conflated in earlier analyses such as the RBC/UKQCD work that required a large downward curvature subtraction to achieve agreement with experiment. The results enable future studies of other nucleon structure quantities such as vector and axial vector form factors, where excited-state contamination is expected to be a generic problem.
