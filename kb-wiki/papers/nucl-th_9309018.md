# Microscopic approach to collective motion

> An overview of a microscopic framework based on the Hartree-Fock description of the mean field is presented which, starting from an effective interaction allows a description of collective motions. A study of the isotope shifts in the Pb region illustrates the importance of the effective interactions and points to their limitations. Such forces should be improved in order to achieve a better description of nuclei properties especially with the coming availability of exotic beam facilities. The coupling of collective quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom in $^{194}$Pb is analyzed within the Generator Coordinate Method, which represents a step beyond the simple mean-field approximation. As a last example, we present a study of nuclear rotations. First we discuss results on superdeformed rotating bands in $^{192}$Hg, $^{194}$Hg and $^{194}$Pb obtained without including a treatment of pairing correlations. Preliminary calculations are also presented with these correlations included as well as an approximate projection on nucleon number. Email contact: paul@amoco.saclay.cea.fr

# Microscopic approach to collective motion

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock mean-field framework with effective Skyrme interactions can describe collective motions in nuclei, but the symmetry-energy properties of commonly used forces (SIII, SkM*, SGII) are insufficient to reproduce the isotope shifts in Pb nuclei, particularly the kink at the N=126 shell closure. Even after including beyond-mean-field corrections via the Generator Coordinate Method and density-dependent pairing, the overall trend of the isotope shifts remains incorrect unless the effective interaction itself is refitted to neutron matter equations of state.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock plus BCS (HFBCS) calculations with three Skyrme forces (SIII, SkM*, SGII) to compute proton and neutron mean-square radii for Pb isotopes from A=194 to A=214. They then extended the calculations using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with quadrupole and octupole moments as generating variables to study collective correlations in 194Pb, and performed cranked Hartree-Fock calculations for superdeformed rotational bands in 192Hg, 194Hg, and 194Pb, both without and with pairing correlations and approximate particle-number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami prescription.

## Key numbers

- The experimental kink parameter k_p for proton radii, defined from 194Pb, 208Pb, and 214Pb, is 0.0529 fm^2; SkM* gives k_p = 0.0050 fm^2, SIII gives 0.0127 fm^2, and SGII gives 0.0231 fm^2.
- For SkM*, the proton Delta r^2 values are -0.7573 fm^2 for 194Pb and 0.3545 fm^2 for 214Pb; experimental values are -0.6830 fm^2 and 0.6099 fm^2 respectively.
- The GCM ground state of 194Pb lies 2.8 MeV below the absolute HFBCS minimum, with 1.4 MeV coming from quadrupole correlations and an additional 1.4 MeV from octupole correlations.
- The superdeformed minimum in 194Pb at q30 = 0 is 4.67 MeV above the absolute minimum; at q30 = 2000 fm^3 it is 1.75 MeV higher, and at q30 = 3000 fm^3 the SD minimum becomes lower than the ground-state minimum by 0.9 MeV.
- The SD quadrupole moment in 194Pb increases smoothly from 46 to 58 barn as q30 increases from 0 to 7000 fm^3.
- A third hyperdeformed minimum exists at 82 barns with a 2.1 to 1 axis ratio and a well depth of 0.8 MeV at q30 = 0.
- For the odd SD rotational band in 194Pb, E1 transitions to the even SD band are strong, causing depopulation between 24 hbar and 36 hbar.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field approach of Bonche et al. (1989) and earlier constrained Hartree-Fock studies of collective motion. The isotope shift analysis reveals limitations of standard Skyrme forces (SIII from 1980, SkM* from 1982, SGII) that were fitted primarily to symmetric nuclear matter and closed-shell nuclei, motivating a refit to neutron matter equations of state from Wiringa (1988). The two-dimensional GCM calculation of quadrupole-octupole coupling in 194Pb goes beyond the one-dimensional GCM studies of Heenen et al. (1992) and the parity-projected calculations of Vautherin (1970s). The superdeformed band calculations in the Hg-Pb region enable comparison with experimental identical bands observed by Riley et al. (1990), Henry et al. (1990), and others, and provide a framework for future studies with exotic beam facilities.
