# ABC effect as a signal of chiral symmetry restoration in hadronic collisions

> A new nonconventional mechanism for the basic 2π-fusion reaction pn --> d+(ππ)_0 in the energy region T_p = 1.0-1.4 GeV is suggested. The mechanism is aimed at providing a consistent explanation for the comprehensive experimental studies of this reaction in exclusive setting done recently by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration. The basic assumption of the model proposed is the production of the I(JP) = 0(3+) dibaryon resonance D03 in the pn collision. The interference of two decay channels of this resonance: D_03 --> d + σ --> d + (ππ)_0 and D_03 --> D_12 + π --> d + (ππ)_0 is shown to give a strong near-threshold enhancement in the ππ invariant mass spectrum, which is well known as the ABC effect. The σ-meson parameters found to reproduce the ABC enhancement are in a general agreement with models which predict the chiral symmetry restoration at high excitation energy and/or high density of matter, although they are essentially less than those accepted for the free σ meson. So, this result might be considered as an indication of partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense and excited quark matter.

# ABC effect as a signal of chiral symmetry restoration in hadronic collisions

## Key claim
The ABC effect in the reaction pn → d+(ππ)₀ can be explained by the production and decay of the I(J^P)=0(3⁺) dibaryon resonance D₀₃ via two interfering channels: σ-meson emission and sequential pion emission through an intermediate isovector dibaryon D₁₂. The σ-meson parameters extracted from the fit (m_σ ≈ 300 MeV, Γ_σ ≈ 100 MeV) are significantly lower than free-space values, which the authors interpret as a possible indication of partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense and excited quark matter, though they note this conclusion is conditional on the model assumptions.

## Method
The authors constructed a resonance model for the reaction pn → d+π⁰π⁰ at T_p = 1.0–1.4 GeV, assuming s-channel production of the D₀₃ dibaryon resonance. The amplitude includes two decay mechanisms: D₀₃ → d+σ → d+(ππ)₀ (σ emission) and D₀₃ → D₁₂+π → d+π+π (sequential pion emission via the isovector dibaryon D₁₂), with the total amplitude given by their coherent sum. The model parameters (σ mass and width, relative weight of the two amplitudes) were fitted to the experimental M_{ππ}² spectrum from the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration, and the resulting M_{dπ}² and angular distributions were then calculated without additional free parameters.

## Key numbers
- D₀₃ dibaryon resonance mass: m_{D₀₃} = 2370 MeV, total width: Γ_{D₀₃} = 70 MeV
- D₁₂ dibaryon resonance mass: m_{D₁₂} = 2150 MeV, total width: Γ_{D₁₂} = 110 MeV
- σ-meson parameters extracted from fit to ABC peak: m_σ ≈ 300 MeV, Γ_σ ≈ 100 MeV
- Free σ-meson pole parameters from dispersion analysis (Caprini): m_σ⁽⁰⁾ = 441⁺¹⁶₋₈ MeV, Γ_σ⁽⁰⁾ = 544⁺¹⁸₋₂₅ MeV
- PDG range for σ (f₀(500)): m_σ = 400–550 MeV, Γ_σ = 400–700 MeV
- Peak energy for total cross section: √s = 2.38 GeV (T_p = 1.14 GeV)
- Total cross section at peak: (σ_T)_peak ≈ 0.43 mb
- D₀₃ binding energy in ΔΔ channel: ε_B(D₀₃) ≈ 90 MeV
- Cut-off parameter for D₀₃ → ΔΔ vertex in alternative model: Λ_{ΔΔ} = 0.15 GeV/c
- Quark model predictions for D₀₃ radius: r(D₀₃) ≈ 0.7–0.9 fm

## Context
This work extends the dibaryon model for short-range nuclear force developed by the authors (Platonova and Kukulin, earlier works) and applies it to the ABC effect, which was discovered by Abashian, Booth, and Crowe (1960) in double-pionic fusion reactions. The model directly addresses the exclusive experimental data from the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (Adlarson et al., 2011), who observed the D₀₃ resonance and interpreted the ABC effect via an s-channel ΔΔ mechanism requiring an unnaturally soft form factor. The present work offers an alternative to that ΔΔ model and partially rehabilitates the original σ-meson interpretation of the ABC effect, while connecting to theoretical studies of chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter (Hatsuda and Kunihiro; Volkov) and in excited hadrons (Glozman). The results suggest that ABC-type experiments could serve as a direct probe of σ-meson production and chiral symmetry restoration in hadronic collisions.
