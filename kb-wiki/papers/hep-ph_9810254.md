# Isospin violation and the proton's neutral weak magnetic form factor

> The effects of isospin violation on the neutral weak magnetic form factor of the proton are studied using two-flavour chiral perturbation theory. The first nonzero contributions appear at O(p^4) in the small-momentum expansion, and the O(p^5) corrections are also calculated. The leading contributions from an explicit Delta(1232) isomultiplet are included as well. At such a high order in the chiral expansion, one might have expected a large number of unknown parameters to contribute. However, it is found that no unknown parameters can appear within loop diagrams, and a single tree-level counterterm at O(p^4) is sufficient to absorb all divergences. The momentum dependence of the neutral weak magnetic form factor is not affected by this counterterm.

## Key claim

Using two-flavour heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), the authors show that isospin-violating contributions to the proton's neutral weak magnetic form factor first appear at order O(p^4), and that at O(p^5) no new unknown parameters enter the calculation, with only a single tree-level counterterm at O(p^4) needed to absorb all divergences.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic chiral expansion of the isospin-violating neutral weak vector form factors of the nucleon using two-flavour heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), including virtual photon effects and explicit Delta(1232) resonance contributions. They calculated all one-pion-loop diagrams at O(p^4) and O(p^5), evaluated wave function renormalization constants, and determined the momentum dependence of the isospin-violating form factors G_M^{u,d}(q^2) up to next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of next-to-leading order (NLO) to leading order (LO) contributions to the derivative of G_M^{u,d}(q^2) at q^2=0 is approximately 1/2, given by 9*pi*m_pi+/(8*M_N)
- The NLO corrections to G_M^{u,d}(q^2) total roughly 0.01 at q^2=0 and grow to about 0.02 near q^2=0.1 GeV^2
- The isospin-violating form factors remain zero up to and including O(p^3) in the chiral expansion
- The leading contributions from the Delta(1232) isomultiplet appear at O(p^4)

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of isospin-violating effects in the proton's neutral weak magnetic form factor that used constituent quark models (Dmitrasinovic and Pollock, 1995; Capstick and Roberts, 1996; Miller, 1996) and a light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model (Ma, 1996). The chiral perturbation theory approach provides a systematic framework for calculating the momentum dependence of these effects, which is relevant for interpreting precision parity-violating electron scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab and the SAMPLE experiment at MIT/Bates that aim to extract the strange quark contribution to the nucleon's form factors.
