# Thermodynamics of SU(3) Gauge Theory from Gradient Flow

> A novel method to study the bulk thermodynamics in lattice gauge theory is proposed on the basis of the Yang-Mills gradient flow with a fictitious time t. The energy density (epsilon) and the pressure (P) of SU(3) gauge theory at fixed temperature are calculated directly on 32^3 x (6,8,10) lattices from the thermal average of the well-defined energy-momentum tensor (T_{mu nu}^R(x)) obtained by the gradient flow. It is demonstrated that the continuum limit can be taken in a controlled manner from the t-dependence of the flowed data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the energy density and pressure of SU(3) gauge theory at fixed temperature can be extracted directly from lattice simulations using the Yang-Mills gradient flow, with the continuum limit taken in a controlled manner from the flow-time dependence of the data, provided one works within a fiducial window where lattice artifacts and non-perturbative corrections are both suppressed.

## Method

The authors generate gauge configurations on \(32^3 \times (6,8,10)\) lattices using the Wilson plaquette action, then solve the Yang-Mills gradient flow for each configuration to obtain flowed link variables. They construct the gauge-invariant local operators \(U_{\mu\nu}(t,x)\) and \(E(t,x)\) from the flowed field strength, and use perturbative matching coefficients \(\alpha_U(t)\) and \(\alpha_E(t)\) (computed to two-loop order in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme) to extract the renormalized energy-momentum tensor \(T_{\mu\nu}^R(x)\). The thermal averages of the diagonal components give the interaction measure \(\Delta = \varepsilon - 3P\) and the entropy density \(s = (\varepsilon + P)/T\), with a vacuum subtraction for \(\Delta\) obtained from separate \(32^4\) zero-temperature simulations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice sizes: \(32^3 \times N_\tau\) with \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 10\); gauge coupling \(\beta\) values chosen to give \(T/T_c = 0.99, 1.24, 1.65\) (see Table I).
- Number of gauge configurations for finite-temperature measurements: 300.
- Statistical (systematic) errors for \(N_\tau = 8\) at \(\sqrt{8t}T = 0.40\) and \(T = 1.65 T_c\): \(2.5\%\) (\(0.11\%\)) for \(\Delta/T^4\) and \(0.83\%\) (\(4.4\%\)) for \(s/T^3\).
- Statistical error of the present result on \(32^3 \times 8\) lattice for \(\beta = 6.4\) (\(\beta = 6.2\)) is about 3.33 (2.69) times smaller than the corresponding result in Boyd (1996) on the same lattice.
- Continuum extrapolation error from scaling violation: \(\pm 5\%\) at high temperature and \(\pm 25\%\) at low temperature; overall error within \(\pm 12\%\) even at the lowest temperature.

## Context

This work extends the gradient-flow approach to energy-momentum tensor renormalization proposed by Suzuki (2013) to finite-temperature SU(3) gauge theory, providing a direct alternative to the traditional integral method used by Boyd (1996), Okamoto (1999), and Borsanyi (2012). The results are consistent with those earlier integral-method calculations within statistical errors, but the new method avoids the need for integration over \(\beta\) or temperature and substantially reduces statistical noise at finite flow time. This enables future studies of transport coefficients (shear and bulk viscosities), fluctuation observables, and glueball properties, and can be extended to include fermions following Makino and Suzuki.
