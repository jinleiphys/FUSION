# The Isovector Quadrupole-Quadrupole Interaction Used in Shell Model Calculations with $ΔN=2$ Excitations

> The effect of the ``$B$'' term in the interaction $-χQ\cdot Q(1+B\vecτ(1)\cdot \vecτ(2))$, which was previously considered in the $0p$ shell, is now studied in a larger space which includes $ΔN=2$ excitations. We still get a collapse of low-lying states below the conventional $J=0^+$ ground state when $B$ is made sufficiently negative.

# The Isovector Quadrupole-Quadrupole Interaction Used in Shell Model Calculations with ΔN=2 Excitations

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including ΔN=2 excitations in shell model calculations does not resolve the unphysical collapse of low-lying states (an L=1 S=1 triplet becoming the ground state) when the isovector strength parameter B in the interaction −χQ·Q(1+Bτ(1)·τ(2)) is made sufficiently negative; the collapse still occurs, albeit at a larger negative B value (B ≈ −1.6) compared to the 0p-shell-only calculation (B ≈ −0.74), and the scale change is attributed to renormalization effects discussed by Bes, Broglia and Nilsson (1975).

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed for ⁶He, ⁸Be, ¹⁰Be, and ²⁰Ne using the interaction −χQ·Q(1+Bτ(1)·τ(2)), with the model space extended from the 0p shell to include all 2ħω (ΔN=2) excitations. The energy dependence of selected states (2⁺ states, L=1 S=1 triplets, and scissors mode states) was tracked as a function of the parameter B, and the summed isovector orbital M1 strength and energy-weighted strength were computed for ¹⁰Be and ⁸Be. A small-space (ΔN=0) calculation was also performed for ²²Ne in the 1s-0d shell for comparison.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁰Be with ΔN=2 excitations, the L=1 S=1 triplet becomes the ground state at B ≈ −1.6; in the 0p-shell-only space this occurred at B ≈ −0.74.
- For ¹⁰Be at B=0 in the large space, the 2₁⁺ state is at 2.186 MeV and the 2₂⁺ state at 3.400 MeV.
- For ¹⁰Be at B=0, isoscalar B(E2) values (eₚ=eₙ=1) are 63.76 e²fm⁴ for the 2₁⁺ state and 113.34 e²fm⁴ for the 2₂⁺ state; isovector B(E2) values (eₚ=1, eₙ=−1) are 12.49 and 9.52 e²fm⁴ respectively.
- For ¹⁰Be at B=−1.5 (just before collapse), the corresponding states are at 0.717 and 4.199 MeV; isoscalar B(E2) values are 32.86 and 168.4 e²fm⁴; isovector B(E2) values are 13.28 and 0.193 e²fm⁴.
- The χ values used (in MeV fm⁴) are: 0.6914 for ⁶He, 0.57619 for ⁸Be, 0.36146 for ¹⁰Be, and 0.13403 for ²⁰Ne; the corresponding χ̄ values are 0.200, 0.1865, 0.1286, and 0.0691.
- For ²²Ne in the small space at B=−1, the 2⁺ state coming down is at 0.323 MeV with isoscalar B(E2)=29.20 e²fm⁴ and isovector B(E2)=27.39 e²fm⁴; the L=1 S=1 triplet is at 0.107 MeV and becomes the ground state for |B| ≥ 1.005.
- In ⁸Be, no states collapse; both the 2₁⁺ T=0 and 1⁺ T=1 states increase in energy as B becomes more negative.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 0p-shell study of ¹⁰Be (Fayache, Sharma and Zamick, 1995) to a larger space including 2ħω excitations, and also examines ⁶He, ⁸Be, and ²⁰Ne. The interaction form is a generalization of the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958), which corresponds to B=0. The paper addresses the conflict between the large negative B value (B=−3.6) needed to fit the isovector-isoscalar splitting of giant quadrupole resonances in RPA calculations and the much smaller B values required to avoid unphysical ground states in shell model calculations. The authors suggest that effective mass effects, as discussed by Bes, Broglia and Nilsson (1975), could reduce the required magnitude of B. The results set limits on acceptable B values and enable further work on the interplay between effective interactions and model space truncation in nuclear structure calculations.
