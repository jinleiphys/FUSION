# Rescattering Effects on Intensity Interferometry

> We derive a general formula for the correlation function of two identical particles with the inclusion of multiple elastic scatterings in the medium in which the two particles are produced. This formula involves the propagator of the particle in the medium. As illustration of the effect we apply the formula to the special case where the scatterers are static, localized 2-body potentials. In this illustration both $R^2_{\rm side}$ and $R^2_{\rm out}$ are increased by an amount proportional to the square of the spatial density of scatterers and to the differential cross section. Specific numbers are used to show the expected magnitude of the rescattering effect on kaon interferometry.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formula for the two-particle intensity interferometry correlation function that includes the effect of multiple elastic scatterings in the medium where the particles are produced, but the numerical illustration is restricted to the special case of static, localized two-body potentials and does not include collective flow or moving scattering centers.

## Method

The authors derive a nonrelativistic formula for the correlation function using the full propagator of the particle in the medium, then generalize to relativistic bosons via the Klein-Gordon equation and to relativistic fermions via the Dirac equation. As an illustration, they apply the formula to the case of static, localized weak potentials in three dimensions, assuming a Gaussian distribution of scattering centers and a Gaussian source function, and compute the resulting HBT radius parameters \(R_{\rm out}\) and \(R_{\rm side}\).

## Key numbers

- Forward differential cross section for kaon-nucleon scattering: 1 mb/sr.
- Central density of nucleons: 0.15 nucleons/fm\(^3\).
- Radii of the production source and scattering center distribution: \(R_P = R_c = 5\) fm.
- Duration of emission: \(\tau = 2\) fm/c.
- The ratio \(R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}\) is less than 1 for kaon momentum \(k < 150\) MeV and greater than 1 for \(k > 150\) MeV.
- Both \(R^2_{\rm side}\) and \(R^2_{\rm out}\) are increased by an amount proportional to the square of the spatial density of scatterers and to the differential cross section.

## Context

This work extends the standard Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (HBT) formalism, originally developed for astronomy and later applied to subatomic physics by Goldhaber et al. (GGLP), to include rescattering effects that are negligible in elementary particle collisions but may be significant in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The derived formulas enable future calculations of HBT radii that account for multiple elastic scattering, and the authors note that complications such as moving scattering centers, flow, and partial coherence must be included for comparison with experimental data.
