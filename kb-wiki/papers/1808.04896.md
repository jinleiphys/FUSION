# Multiparticle Production at Mid-Rapidity in the Color-Glass Condensate

> In this paper, we compute a number of cross sections for the production of multiple particles at mid-rapidity in the semi-dilute / dense regime of the color-glass condensate (CGC) effective field theory. In particular, we present new results for the production of two quark-antiquark pairs (whether the same or different flavors) and for the production of one quark-antiquark pair and a gluon. We also demonstrate the existence of a simple mapping which transforms the cross section to produce a quark-antiquark pair into the corresponding cross section to produce a gluon, which we use to obtain various results and to cross-check them against the literature. We also discuss hadronization effects in the heavy flavor sector, writing explicit expressions for the production of various combinations of $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons, $J/ψ$ mesons, and light hadrons. The various multiparticle cross sections presented here contain a wealth of information and can be used to study heavy flavor production, charge-dependent correlations, and "collective" flow phenomena arising from initial-state dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper presents new complete expressions for multiparticle production cross sections at mid-rapidity in the semi-dilute/dense regime of the color-glass condensate (CGC) effective field theory, including a mapping that transforms quark-antiquark pair production cross sections into gluon production cross sections, but these results are derived under the specific hierarchy of scales where the projectile saturation momentum is much smaller than the target saturation momentum (Q_{s,p}^2 << Q_{s,t}^2) and where the projectile density corrections are treated perturbatively.

## Method

The authors compute scattering amplitudes for soft particle production in momentum space using light-front wave functions within the CGC framework, where the dense target's color fields are resummed to all orders via Wilson lines while the semi-dilute projectile's charge density is treated perturbatively. They derive the complete double quark-antiquark pair production cross section dσ^{(q\bar{q})(q\bar{q})} at exact N_c in momentum space, then demonstrate a mapping that transforms the quark-antiquark pair production amplitude into the corresponding gluon production amplitude, which they validate by reproducing known single-gluon and double-gluon production results from the literature. The partonic-level cross sections are then convolved with fragmentation functions and long-distance matrix elements to obtain hadronic-level expressions for D mesons, J/ψ mesons, and light hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The semi-dilute/dense regime is quantified by the hierarchy α_s^2 A^{1/3} ~ O(1) and α_s << α_s^2 a^{1/3} << 1, where A is the target nucleon number and a is the projectile nucleon number
- The saturation momenta satisfy Λ_QCD^2 << Q_{s,p}^2 << Q_{s,t}^2 for the semi-dilute/dense regime
- The light-front wave function for gluon emission squared and spin-averaged gives tr_D[φ(q_1) φ^†(q_2)] = 4 (q_1·q_2)/(q_{1T}^2 q_{2T}^2)
- The light-front wave function for gluon emission in coordinate space squared and spin-averaged gives tr_D[φ(x) φ^†(y)] = (1/π^2) (x·y)/(x_T^2 y_T^2)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of multiparticle production in the CGC framework, particularly building on the semi-dilute/dense formalism developed by Kovchegov and collaborators (2012) for double-gluon production. The authors significantly generalize the partial calculation of double-pair production by Altinoluk and collaborators (2016) by including contributions intentionally omitted there, working at exact N_c, and keeping multiple scattering corrections to all orders. The results enable systematic studies of initial-state contributions to collective flow phenomena, charge-dependent correlations, and heavy flavor production that are essential for disentangling initial-state effects from final-state quark-gluon plasma dynamics in proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions.
