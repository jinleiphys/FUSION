# Comprehending Isospin breaking effects of $X(3872)$ in a Friedrichs-model-like scheme

> Recently, we have shown that the $X(3872)$ state can be naturally generated as a bound state by incorporating the hadron interactions into the Godfrey-Isgur quark model using the Friedrichs-like model combined with the QPC model, in which the wave function for the $X(3872)$ as a combination of the bare $c\bar c$ state and the continuum states can also be obtained. Under this scheme, we now investigate the isospin breaking effect of $X(3872)$ in its decays to $J/ψπ^+π^-$ and $J/ψπ^+π^-π^0$. By Considering its dominant continuum parts coupling to $J/ψρ$ and $J/ψω$ through the quark rearrangement process, one could obtain the reasonable ratio of $\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/ψπ^+π^-π^0)/\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/ψπ^+π^-)\simeq (0.58\sim 0.92)$. It is also shown that the $\bar D D^*$ invariant mass distributions in the $B\rightarrow \bar D D^* K$ decays could be understood qualitatively at the same time. This scheme may provide more insight to understand the enigmatic nature of the $X(3872)$ state.

## Key claim
The X(3872) state is generated as a bound state from the coupling of a bare \(c\bar c\) state with meson-meson continuum channels, and its isospin-breaking decay ratio \(\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)/\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-)\) can be reproduced without invoking OZI-suppressed \(c\bar c\) contributions, using only the continuum components of the wave function and the quark rearrangement model.

## Method
The authors combine the Friedrichs-like model with the quark-pair-creation (QPC) model to couple a bare \(2^3P_1\) \(c\bar c\) state from the Godfrey-Isgur quark model to \(D\bar D^*\) and \(D^*\bar D^*\) continuum channels. The resulting bound-state wave function for X(3872) is then used to compute transition amplitudes to \(J/\psi\rho\) and \(J/\psi\omega\) via the Barnes-Swanson quark rearrangement model, which includes color Coulomb, spin-spin hyperfine, and linear confinement interactions. Only the continuum components of the X(3872) wave function contribute, as the \(c\bar c\) to \(J/\psi\rho/\omega\) couplings are OZI-suppressed.

## Key numbers
- The ratio \(\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0)/\mathcal{B}(X(3872)\rightarrow J/\psi\pi^+ \pi^-)\) is calculated to be \(0.58\sim 0.92\).
- The X(3872) bound-state pole is found at around \(3871.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), just below the \(D^0\bar D^{0*}\) threshold.
- The QPC coupling strength \(\gamma\) is set to approximately \(4.0\) to simultaneously reproduce the X(3872) and \(\chi_{c2}(2P)\) masses.
- A resonance pole at around \(3.934\pm 0.040 i\ \mathrm{GeV}\) on the unphysical sheet is generated from the bare \(\chi_{c1}(2P)\) state, possibly related to the observed X(3940).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel analysis (Zhou and Xiao, 2017) of excited \(P\)-wave charmonium states using the Friedrichs model, where X(3872) emerged as a dynamically generated bound state rather than a pure molecular state from pion exchange (Tornqvist, 1993) or a tetraquark (Maiani, 2004). It addresses the isospin-breaking puzzle noted by Suzuki (2005) and later studied via effective Lagrangians (Meng and Chao, 2007) and one-meson-exchange models (Li and Zhu, 2012). The scheme provides a unified framework to compute both the spectrum and decay properties of X(3872) from first-principles quark-model inputs, enabling future studies of other exotic candidates.
