# On the importance of gluon contributions to timelike and spacelike DVCS

> We emphasize how large O(α_s) corrections to timelike and spacelike virtual Compton scattering amplitudes are in the generalized Bjorken scaling regime, and in particular the gluonic contributions, even in the medium energy range which will be studied intensely at JLab12 and in the COMPASS-II experiment at CERN. We stress that the timelike and spacelike cases are complementary and that their difference deserves much special attention.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that next-to-leading-order (NLO) gluonic contributions to both spacelike and timelike deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS and TCS) are so large that they can change the sign of the real part of the Compton form factor, and remain sizeable for the imaginary part up to skewness values of 0.3, even at the medium energies accessible at JLab12 and COMPASS-II.

## Method

The authors computed the NLO coefficient functions for both DVCS and TCS, using the analyticity relation between the two processes to relate the timelike coefficient functions to the spacelike ones. They evaluated the Compton form factors using two different generalized parton distribution (GPD) models based on double distributions: the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model and a model based on the MSTW08 parton distribution function parametrization, at a factorization scale and virtuality of \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and momentum transfer \(t = -0.1\) GeV\(^2\) or \(t = -0.2\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- The gluonic contribution changes the sign of the real part of the spacelike and timelike Compton form factor \(\mathcal{H}\) for almost all values of the skewness parameter \(\xi\) or \(\eta\) at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(t = -0.1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The gluonic contribution to the imaginary part of the CFF remains sizeable for skewness values up to 0.3 at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(t = -0.1\) GeV\(^2\).
- For the photon beam circular polarization asymmetry in TCS, NLO corrections range from 10% at \(\eta = 0.1\) (relevant for JLab) through 30% at \(\eta = 0.05\) (relevant for COMPASS) up to 100% at very small \(\eta\), with the gluon contribution important for \(\eta < 0.05\).
- The ratio \(R(\eta)\) for TCS, defined as the ratio of weighted cross sections, shows a dramatic change of sign when NLO gluonic corrections are included at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and \(t = -0.2\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations of coefficient functions for DVCS from the early days of GPD studies and the more recent NLO calculations for TCS by Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner (2013), using the analyticity relation established by Muller (2012). The results demonstrate that current attempts to extract GPDs from DVCS data, such as those by Guidal (2009), Kroll (2012), Guidal (2013), and Kumericki (2009, 2013), represent only a first step, and that gluonic NLO corrections must be included in analyses of data from JLab12, COMPASS-II, and future higher-energy facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider.
