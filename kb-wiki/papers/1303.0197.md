# Deformations and quasiparticle spectra of nuclei in the nobelium region

> We have performed self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations for nuclei close to $^{254}$No. Self-consistent deformations, including $β_{2,4,6,8}$ as functions of the rotational frequency, were determined for even-even nuclei $^{246,248,250}$Fm, $^{252,254}$No, and $^{256}$Rf. The quasiparticle spectra for N=151 isotones and Z=99 isotopes were calculated and compared with experimental data and the results of Woods-Saxon calculations. We found that our calculations give high-order deformations similar to those obtained for the Woods-Saxon potential, and that the experimental quasiparticle energies are reasonably well reproduced.

# Deformations and quasiparticle spectra of nuclei in the nobelium region

## Key claim

Self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations using the newly tuned UNEDF1$^{\textrm{SO}}_L$ parameter set reproduce experimental quasiparticle energies reasonably well and yield high-order deformations similar to those from Woods-Saxon calculations, but the agreement is conditional: the calculations underestimate the energies of the 7/2[624] and 1/2[620] states in N=151 isotones and fail to reproduce the increase of excitation energy of the 7/2[514] orbital with neutron number in Es isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed symmetry-unrestricted cranking-plus-blocking calculations using the HFODD solver (v2.52j) with the Skyrme UNEDF1 functional, whose spin-orbit coupling constants ($C_0^{\nabla J}$, $C_1^{\nabla J}$) and pairing strengths ($V_0^{\nu}$, $V_0^{\pi}$) were readjusted to match experimental excitation energies of the $11/2^-$ state in $^{251}$Cf and $7/2^+$ state in $^{249}$Bk. Deformations $\beta_{2,4,6,8}$ were extracted from microscopic multipole moments for six even-even nuclei, and quasiparticle spectra for N=151 isotones and Z=99 Es isotopes were computed using the blocking approximation with the Lipkin-Nogami formalism for approximate particle-number projection.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit coupling constants of the UNEDF1$^{\textrm{SO}}_L$ parameter set: $C_0^{\nabla J} = -88.05040$ MeV fm$^5$, $C_1^{\nabla J} = 8.45838$ MeV fm$^5$
- Pairing strengths: $V_0^{\nu} = -205.05$ MeV, $V_0^{\pi} = -252.48$ MeV
- Basis parameters: 680 deformed harmonic-oscillator states with $\hbar \omega_x = \hbar \omega_y = 8.5049$ MeV and $\hbar \omega_z = 6.4823$ MeV; quasiparticle cutoff energy $E_c = 60$ MeV
- In N=151 isotones, the 5/2[622] state is located at approximately 200 keV in experiment; the 7/2[624] and 9/2[734] states are separated by only several tens of keV and change order with increasing proton number
- Deformations $\beta_2$ at $\omega=0$ are systematically higher than those from macroscopic-microscopic Woods-Saxon calculations and lower than those from other relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field calculations; $\beta_2$ begins to decrease only at rotational frequencies $\omega \sim 0.3$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous readjustment of the Skyrme UNEDF1 functional (Shi 2013) to improve deformed shell gaps at N=152 and Z=100, which macroscopic-microscopic calculations predict but standard self-consistent mean-field calculations typically miss (Bender 2012). The calculations address the role of high-order deformations ($\beta_6$) in explaining high-spin phenomena such as the delayed upbending of moments of inertia in $^{254}$No compared to $^{252}$No (Liu 2012). The results enable systematic comparison with experimental quasiparticle spectra for odd-A nuclei in the nobelium region, though further work is needed to resolve discrepancies in the 7/2[514] orbital energies and the overestimation of the 5/2[622] state energy.
