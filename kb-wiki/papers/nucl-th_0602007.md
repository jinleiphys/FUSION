# Open questions and perspectives for hadron electromagnetic form factors in space-like and time-like regions

> An overview of recent data on electromagnetic hadron form factors is presented and discussed in terms of selected nucleon models in space-like and time-like regions. Model independent properties connecting scattering and annihilation channels are presented and give a deeper understanding of the underlying physics. Radiative corrections are discussed. The interest of polarization observables with respect to model predictions and reaction mechanism (one photon versus two photon exchange) is highlighted.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while recent polarization measurements of the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio deviate strongly from the traditional dipole scaling and Rosenbluth-extracted values, this discrepancy may be resolved by improved radiative corrections or two-photon exchange contributions, rather than necessarily indicating a zero in the electric form factor.

## Method
The author reviews world data on nucleon electromagnetic form factors in both space-like (SL) and time-like (TL) regions, comparing them to three phenomenological models: a vector meson dominance (VMD) model from Iachello (1973), an extended VMD model from Lomon (GKex(02L)), and a pQCD-inspired parametrization. Model-independent constraints from analyticity, crossing symmetry, and C-parity are applied to connect the SL and TL regions, and the role of radiative corrections and two-photon exchange in the Rosenbluth versus polarization method discrepancy is analyzed.

## Key numbers
- The proton magnetic form factor has been extracted up to Q^2 = 31 GeV^2 (Arnold et al., 1975), while individual determination of both proton form factors was possible only up to 8.9 GeV^2 (Andivahis et al., 1994).
- The dipole form factor parametrization is G_D(Q^2) = (1 + Q^2 [GeV^2]/0.71)^{-2}, with the proton magnetic moment mu_p = 2.79.
- At Q^2 = 2 GeV^2 and epsilon = 0.2, the electric contribution to the reduced cross section falls below 3% under the polarization-method hypothesis.
- The relative difference between two radiative correction methods reaches 7% at the largest epsilon values for Q^2 = 1, 3, and 5 GeV^2.
- The model from Iachello (1973) predicts the largest absolute polarization observables at q^2 = 15 GeV^2 for the process p + anti-p -> e+ + e-.

## Context
This review extends the analysis of Tomasi-Gustafsson (2005) by applying phenomenological models fitted to SL data to predict TL observables, and by examining the two-photon exchange mechanism as a possible explanation for the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization measurements first highlighted by Jones et al. (2000). The work enables future polarization experiments at FAIR, Frascati, and Beijing to distinguish between VMD-inspired models and pQCD predictions, and to determine the relative phase of the complex form factors in the TL region.
