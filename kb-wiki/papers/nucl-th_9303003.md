# Atomic parity nonconservation and neutron radii in cesium isotopes

> The interpretation of future precise experiments on atomic parity violation in terms of parameters of the Standard Model could be hampered by uncertainties in the atomic and nuclear structure. While the former can be overcome by measurement in a series of isotopes, the nuclear structure requires knowledge of the neutron density. We use the nuclear Hartree-Fock method, which includes deformation effects, to calculate the proton and neutron densities in {125}Cs-{139}Cs. We argue that the good agreement with the experimental charge radii, binding energies, and ground state spins signifies that the phenomenological nuclear force and the method of calculation that we use is adequate. Based on this agreement, and on calculations involving different effective interactions, we estimate the uncertainties in the differences of the neutron radii delta<r^2>_{N,N'} and conclude that they cause uncertainties in the ratio of weak charges, the quantities determined in the atomic parity nonconservation experiments, of less than 10^{-3}. Such an uncertainty is smaller than the anticipated experimental error.

## Key claim

The uncertainty in the differences of neutron mean square radii between cesium isotopes, as calculated by the nuclear Hartree-Fock method including deformation effects, causes uncertainties in the ratio of weak charges of less than 10^{-3}, which is smaller than the anticipated experimental error in future atomic parity nonconservation experiments.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear Hartree-Fock calculations with BCS pairing correlations using two effective Skyrme interactions (Skyrme III and SkyrmeM*) to compute proton and neutron densities for cesium isotopes from ^{125}Cs to ^{139}Cs. The calculations included deformation effects by constraining the nuclear shape to be axially symmetric and solving on a three-dimensional rectangular mesh with 0.8 fm spacing. Zero-point quadrupole vibrational motion corrections were applied using B(E2) transition matrix elements from neighboring Xe and Ba isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty in the shift of the proton mean square radius δr_p^2 between isotopes is at most 0.2 fm^2, and the same uncertainty is assigned to neutron shifts δr_n^2
- The uncertainty in the factors q_{n,p} (integrals of formfactors with nuclear densities) is δq_{n,p} = 4.6 × 10^{-4}, representing about 1% of the deviation of q_{n,p} from unity
- The relative uncertainty in the weak charge Q_W from nuclear structure alone for a single isotope is about 2.5 × 10^{-3}
- The nuclear structure contribution to the uncertainty in the ratio R(N',N) of weak charges between isotopes is roughly 7 × 10^{-4}
- For ^{133}Cs, the experimental charge radius ⟨r_p^2⟩ = 23.04 fm^2; the Hartree-Fock calculation with Skyrme III gives ⟨r_p^2⟩_{HF} = 23.27 fm^2 and with SkyrmeM* gives 22.69 fm^2
- The zero-point vibrational correction to r^2 is 0.2124 fm^2 in ^{133}Cs, 0.1325 fm^2 in ^{135}Cs, 0.0724 fm^2 in ^{137}Cs, and 0.1263 fm^2 in ^{139}Cs

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear structure corrections to atomic parity nonconservation (PNC) measurements previously performed by Pollock, Fortson, and Wilets (1992), who estimated uncertainties using a wider variety of interactions. The authors build on the suggestion by Wieman, Monroe, and Cornell (1991) for PNC measurements in unstable cesium isotopes. The results enable future high-precision tests of the Standard Model electroweak parameters, particularly the weak mixing angle sin^2 θ_W, by showing that nuclear structure uncertainties in isotope ratio measurements will not limit the interpretation of atomic PNC experiments.
