# Nucleon distribution in nuclei beyond β-stability line

> The radii of nucleon distribution, bulk density, and neutron skin in nuclei beyond the β-stability line are studied within the direct variational method. We evaluate the partial equation of state of finite nuclei and demonstrate that the bulk density decreases beyond the beta stability line. We show that the growth of the neutron skin in unstable nuclei does not obey the saturation condition because of the polarization effect. The value of the neutron-skin thickness Δr_{np}=\sqrt{<r_n^2>}-\sqrt{<r_p^2>} is caused by the different radii (skin effect) and only slightly by the different shapes (halo effect) of neutron and proton distributions. The relative contribution of both effects depends on the competition between the symmetry energy, and the spin-orbit and Coulomb interactions. The calculations of the isovector shift of the nuclear radius Δr_{np} show its primarily linear dependence on the asymmetry parameter X=(N-Z)/A.

# Nucleon distribution in nuclei beyond β-stability line

## Key claim

The authors show that the bulk density of finite nuclei decreases beyond the beta-stability line, and that the growth of the neutron skin in unstable nuclei does not obey the saturation condition because of the polarization effect. The neutron-skin thickness Δr_np is caused primarily by the different radii (skin effect) and only slightly by the different shapes (halo effect) of neutron and proton distributions, with the relative contribution depending on the competition between symmetry energy, spin-orbit, and Coulomb interactions.

## Method

The authors combine the extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation with corrections up to order ℏ² and a direct variational method, using trial profile functions of the Fermi type for neutron and proton densities. They apply the Skyrme-type effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and perform variational calculations to determine the parameters of the density distributions (bulk density, radius, diffuseness, and skin parameters) for nuclei with varying asymmetry parameter X=(N-Z)/A. The calculations are carried out for several isotopic chains (Na, Sn, Pb) using the SkM and SLy230b Skyrme force parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium bulk density for the SkM parametrization is ρ₀,eq ≈ 0.153 fm⁻³ (for ¹²⁰Sn).
- The fitted dependence of the skin parameters on asymmetry X for A=120 with SkM forces: Δ_R(X) ≈ 1.34 X + 0.07 X² fm, Δ_a(X) ≈ 0.36 X + 0.53 X² fm.
- The beta-stability line is parametrized as X*(A) = 0.17 A^(2/3) / (26.5 − 25.6 A^(−1/3) + 0.17 A^(2/3)).
- The spinodal instability border occurs at ρ₀/ρ₀,eq = 0.62, where the finite-nucleus incompressibility K_A = 0.
- For the semiclassical gradient parameter β = 1/36, the halo effect contribution to Δr_np is small near the beta-stability line and becomes appreciable only near the drip line; for β = 1/9, the halo effect contribution is more significant.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron-rich nuclei beyond the beta-stability line using the direct variational method, building on earlier ETF approaches by Kirzhnits (1967) and Brack et al. (1985). The authors derive a partial equation of state for finite nuclei and relate the neutron-skin thickness to the surface symmetry energy, following the framework of Myers and Swiatecki (1969, 1974) for the advanced mass formula. The results enable improved fitting of Skyrme force parameters by comparing calculated neutron and proton rms radii with experimental data from Suzuki et al. (1995) and others, and provide a basis for extrapolating nuclear properties toward neutron matter.
