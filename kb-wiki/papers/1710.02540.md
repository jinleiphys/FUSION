# Spectroscopy and decays of the fully-heavy tetraquarks

> We discuss the possible existence of the fully-heavy tetraquarks. We calculate the ground-state energy of the $bb \bar b \bar b$ bound state, where $b$ stands for the bottom quark, in a nonrelativistic effective field theory framework with one-gluon-exchange (OGE) color Coulomb interaction, and in a relativized diquark model characterized by OGE plus a confining potential. Our analysis advocates the existence of uni-flavor heavy four-quark bound states. The ground state $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquark mass is predicted to be $(18.72\pm0.02)$~GeV. Mass inequality relations among the lowest $QQ\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$ state, where $Q\in \{c, b\}$, and the corresponding heavy quarkonia are presented, which give the upper limit on the mass of ground state $QQ\bar{Q}\bar{Q}$. The possible decays of the lowest $bb\bar{b}\bar{b}$ are highlighted, which might provide useful references in the search for them in ongoing LHC experiments, and its width is estimated to be a few tens of MeV.

## Key claim

The paper advocates for the existence of fully-heavy tetraquark bound states, specifically the $bb\bar b\bar b$ system, but the analysis is performed at leading order (LO) in a nonrelativistic effective field theory that neglects all spin-dependent and confining interactions, and the ground-state mass prediction carries an uncertainty of $\pm 0.02$ GeV from the product of binding energy and $\alpha_s$.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body problem for the $bb\bar b\bar b$ system using a nonrelativistic effective field theory with only the one-gluon-exchange color Coulomb potential at leading order, employing Gaussian trial wave functions in a variational approach with Jacobi coordinates. They also computed the ground-state mass in a relativized diquark model where the tetraquark is treated as an axial-vector diquark ($bb$) and antidiquark ($\bar b \bar b$) bound by OGE plus a confining potential. The quark mass $m_b = 4.82$ GeV and the strong coupling $\alpha_s(1.5~\text{GeV}) = 0.31$ were extracted from the spin-averaged bottomonium mass $M_{b\bar b}(1S) = 9.45$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state mass of $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquark from NREFT: $M_{bb\bar b\bar b}^{\text{gs, NR}} = (18.72 \pm 0.02)$ GeV
- Binding energy of the $bb\bar b\bar b$ ground state: $E_{bb\bar b\bar b}(1S) = (0.56 \pm 0.02)$ GeV
- Ground-state mass from relativized diquark model: $M_{bb\bar b\bar b}^{\text{gs, REL}} = 18.75$ GeV
- Axial-vector diquark mass: $M_{\text{av}, bb} = 9.85$ GeV
- Spin-averaged binding energy of $1S$ bottomonium: $E_{b\bar b}(1S) = (0.20 \pm 0.01)$ GeV
- Variational parameters: $\beta_1 = \beta_2 = 0.77$ GeV, $\beta_3 = 0.60$ GeV
- Upper bound on $X_{bb\bar{b}\bar{b}}$ mass from inequality: $\lesssim 2 M_{b\bar b}(1S) \lesssim 18.89$ GeV
- Upper bound on $X_{cc\bar{c}\bar{c}}$ mass from inequality: $\lesssim 2 M_{c\bar c}(1S) \lesssim 6.14$ GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of fully-heavy tetraquarks by Karliner et al. (2016), Bai et al. (2016), and Berezhnoy et al. (2011), all of which predicted the existence of $bb\bar b\bar b$ bound states with masses around $18.7$ to $18.9$ GeV, while the chromo-electric potential model of Richard (2017) argued against stability. The NREFT result agrees well with the phenomenological potential calculation of Bai et al. (2016), and the mass inequality relations provide upper limits that can be tested by lattice QCD or future LHC searches for narrow resonances in the $18$ to $19$ GeV region.
