# New Theoretical Results on Event-by-Event Fluctuations

> Several theoretical results concerning event-by-event fluctuations are discussed: (1) a role of the global conservation laws and concept of statistical ensembles; (2) strongly intensive measures are introduced; they give a possibility to study e-by-e fluctuations for physical systems with volume fluctuations; (3) identity method for chemical fluctuations in a case of incomplete particle identification; (4) the example of particle number fluctuations in a vicinity of the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while mean hadron multiplicities in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions become equivalent across different statistical ensembles in the thermodynamic limit, the scaled variances of multiplicity distributions remain ensemble-dependent even in that limit, meaning the equivalence of ensembles holds for mean values but does not extend to fluctuations.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for scaled variances of multiplicity distributions within the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), canonical ensemble (CE), and micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) for ideal Boltzmann gases, using partition functions with charge conservation constraints and fixed energy conditions. For the hadron resonance gas model, the scaled variances for negatively and positively charged hadrons are calculated along the chemical freeze-out line for central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions across the energy range from SIS to LHC, with model parameters fitted to mean hadron multiplicities. The strongly intensive measures Δ[A,B] and Σ[A,B] are introduced as functions of first and second moments of extensive quantities A and B, with normalization factors chosen to yield unity in the independent particle model.

## Key numbers

- For the ideal gas of massless neutral Boltzmann particles, the scaled variance in the GCE is ω_gce = 1, while in the MCE it is ω_mce = 1/4, a factor of four smaller.
- For the ideal gas of positively and negatively charged particles with total charge zero, in the large volume limit the CE scaled variance approaches ω_ce^± ≅ 1/2, which is half the GCE value of ω_gce^± = 1.
- For Bose statistics of pions at temperature T ≅ 150 MeV, Δ[P_T,N] decreases by about 20% and Σ[P_T,N] increases by about 10% relative to the independent particle model values of unity.
- The NA49 data at collision energies 20, 30, 40, 80 and 158 A GeV for the 1% most central Pb+Pb collisions show that among GCE, CE, and MCE results, the MCE is in better agreement with the data.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of event-by-event fluctuations in statistical mechanics, building on prior studies of ensemble dependence in fluctuation observables (fluc-1 through fluc-6, mce-1, mce-2). The strongly intensive measures Δ and Σ introduced here generalize earlier fluctuation measures such as the Φ measure from Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992) and provide tools for the energy and system size scan programs of NA61/SHINE at CERN SPS and STAR/PHENIX at BNL RHIC. The results enable experimental extraction of resonance abundances from fluctuation measurements, following the idea first suggested by Jeon and Koch, and allow comparison of statistical model predictions with data from the NA49 Collaboration.
