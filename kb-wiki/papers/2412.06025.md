# Mapping spatial distributions within pseudoscalar mesons

> Several aspects of the internal structure of pseudoscalar mesons, accessible through generalized parton distributions in their zero-skewness limit, are examined. These include electromagnetic and gravitational form factors related to charge and mass densities; and distributions in the impact parameter space. To this end, we employ an algebraically viable framework that is based upon the valence-quark generalized parton distribution expressed explicitly in terms of the associated distribution function and a profile function that governs the off-forward dynamics. The predominantly analytical nature of this scheme yields several algebraic results and relations while also facilitating the exploration of insightful limiting cases. With a suitable input distribution function, guided either by experiment or theory, and with an appropriate choice of the profile function, it is possible to provide testable predictions for spatial distributions of valence quarks inside pseudoscalar mesons. When comparison is possible, these predictions align well with existing experimental data as well as the findings of reliable theoretical approaches and lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraically viable framework (the Exponential Representation Scheme, or ERS) for modeling valence-quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of pseudoscalar mesons in the zero-skewness limit, which yields testable predictions for spatial distributions that align with existing experimental data and lattice QCD results when comparison is possible, but only with a suitable input distribution function and an appropriate choice of the profile function.

## Method

The authors construct the valence-quark GPD as a product of the forward parton distribution function and an exponential factor containing a profile function that governs off-forward dynamics, parameterized as \(\hat{\phi}_\textbf{P}(x;\zeta_H) = (1-x)^2/\Lambda_\textbf{P}^2\). Using this representation, they derive analytic expressions for the impact parameter space GPD, electromagnetic and gravitational form factors, charge and mass radii, and two-dimensional charge and mass distributions. They then apply the scheme to pseudoscalar mesons including the pion, kaon, and D meson, as well as illustrative \(\pi_s\) and \(\pi_c\) systems, using realistic input distribution functions guided by prior continuum QCD studies.

## Key numbers

* For the flat-top (contact interaction) DF, the charge radius is \((r_E^{ci})^2 = 2/\Lambda_{ci}^2\) and the mass radius is \((r_M^{ci})^2 = 1/\Lambda_{ci}^2\), yielding a mass-to-charge ratio \(\mathcal{R}_0^{ci} = 1/2\).
* For the scale-free DF \(q_{sf}(x) = 30x^2(1-x)^2\), the charge radius is \((r_E^{sf})^2 = 12/(7\Lambda_{sf}^2)\) and the mass radius is \((r_M^{sf})^2 = 9/(7\Lambda_{sf}^2)\), yielding \(\mathcal{R}_0^{sf} = 3/4\).
* For infinitely heavy systems with DF \(q_{nr}(x) = \delta(x-1/2)\), the mass-to-charge ratio is \(\mathcal{R}_0^{nr} = 1\).
* The global maximum of the impact parameter space distribution for the scale-free case occurs at \((x^{\text{max}}, |b_\perp^{\text{max}}|) = (2/3, \sqrt{2}/(3\Lambda_{sf}))\).
* The Mellin moments of the flat-top DF are \(\langle x^n \rangle_{q_{ci}} = 1/(n+1)\); for the scale-free DF, \(\langle x^n \rangle_{q_{sf}} = 60/[(3+n)(4+n)(5+n)]\); for the infinitely heavy DF, \(\langle x^n \rangle_{q_{nr}} = 1/2^n\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic modeling of meson GPDs previously developed for the pion and kaon by Raya et al. (2021) and Xu et al. (2023), and is consistent with more sophisticated continuum QCD approaches such as those of Ding et al. (2022) and Raya et al. (2024). The ERS framework enables direct derivation of measurable quantities including electromagnetic and gravitational form factors, and provides a bridge between parton distribution functions, distribution amplitudes, and spatial charge and mass distributions, facilitating comparisons with lattice QCD results from Lu et al. (2023) and Cui et al. (2021, 2022).
