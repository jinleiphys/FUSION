# Benchmark neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements in a light nucleus

> We compute nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-beta decay mediated by light Majorana-neutrino exchange in the A = 6 system. The goal is to benchmark two many-body approaches, the No-Core Shell Model and the Multi-Reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group. We use the SRG-evolved chiral N3LO-EM500 potential for the nuclear interaction, and make the approximation that isospin is conserved. We compare the results of the two approaches as a function of the cutoff on the many-body basis space. Although differences are seen in the predicted nuclear radii, the ground-state energies and neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements produced by the two approaches show significant agreement. We discuss the implications for calculations in heavier nuclei.

## Key claim

The neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements for the $A=6$ system, calculated with the No-Core Shell Model and the Multi-Reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group using the SRG-evolved chiral N3LO-EM500 potential under the approximation of conserved isospin, show significant agreement between the two approaches, though differences are seen in predicted nuclear radii.

## Method

The authors computed the ground-state-to-ground-state $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay matrix element for $^{6}\text{He}\rightarrow{}^{6}\text{Be}$ using two *ab initio* many-body methods: the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) and the Multi-Reference In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (MR-IMSRG) truncated at the two-body level (MR-IMSRG(2)). Both methods used the same SRG-evolved chiral N3LO-EM500 nucleon-nucleon potential in a harmonic oscillator basis with $\hbar\Omega=20$ MeV and SRG evolution scale $\lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$, and assumed good isospin symmetry to relate the initial and final nuclear states. The results were compared as a function of the basis cutoff parameters $N_{\text{max}}$ (NCSM) and $e_{\text{max}}$ (MR-IMSRG), with the mapping $e_{\text{max}}\equiv N_{\text{max}}+1$, and extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{6}\text{He}$: NCSM extrapolated value is approximately $-28.5$ MeV; MR-IMSRG(2) extrapolated value is approximately $-28.0$ MeV (from Fig. 1(a), approximate values).
- Matter square radius of $^{6}\text{He}$: NCSM extrapolated value $\langle r_m^2\rangle_\infty \approx 6.0$ fm$^2$; MR-IMSRG(2) extrapolated value $\langle r_m^2\rangle_\infty \approx 5.5$ fm$^2$ (from Fig. 1(b) legend, approximate values).
- The nuclear radius parameter $R = 1.2 A^{1/3} \approx 2.2$ fm is used to make matrix elements dimensionless.
- The average intermediate-state energy parameter $\bar{E} - (E_i + E_f)/2 \equiv 5$ MeV is employed throughout.
- The vector and axial masses used in form factors are $\Lambda_V = 850$ MeV and $\Lambda_A = 1040$ MeV.
- The axial coupling constant at zero momentum transfer is $g_A \simeq 1.27$.

## Context

This work benchmarks two many-body approaches for $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay matrix elements in a light nucleus, extending prior NCSM studies of the $A=6$ system by Cockrell (2012) and Shin (2016) by focusing on $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay and comparing with the MR-IMSRG method. It also offers a point of comparison to Variational Monte Carlo calculations of $0\nu\beta\beta$-decay NMEs in light nuclei by Pastore (2017), though using a different nucleon-nucleon interaction and focusing solely on light Majorana-neutrino exchange. The results enable future benchmarking of these methods in heavier nuclei, where basis truncation is more severe and where calculated NMEs currently differ by a factor of two to three.
