# Structure of vortices in rotating Bose-Einstein condensates

> We calculate the structure of individual vortices in rotating Bose-Einstein condensates in a transverse harmonic trap. Making a Wigner-Seitz approximation for the unit cell of the vortex lattice, we derive the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the condensate wave function in each cell of the lattice, including effects of varying coarse grained density. We calculate the Abrikosov parameter, the fractional core area, and the energy of individual cells.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the internal structure of individual vortices in a rotating Bose-Einstein condensate for arbitrary rotation velocities, not just in the limits of slow rotation or the lowest Landau level regime, and finds that the vortex structure depends not only on rotation rate and local density but also on derivatives of the local density, an effect previously taken into account only globally.

## Method

The authors treat a unit cell of the vortex lattice in the Wigner-Seitz approximation, replacing the hexagonal unit cell by a circular one of the same area. They derive the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the condensate wave function in each cell, including effects of varying coarse-grained density, and solve it numerically by expanding the wave function in a basis of eigenfunctions of the free Hamiltonian and propagating the solution in imaginary time.

## Key numbers

- The Abrikosov parameter b approaches 1.158 as Ω → ω (the rapidly rotating limit), close to the exact LLL value of 1.1596 for a triangular lattice.
- For the central cell at Ω/ω = 0.99 and κ₀ = 10, the Abrikosov parameter b = 1.141, the fractional core area r_c²/ℓ² = 0.217, and r_c'²/ℓ² = 0.316.
- At Ω/ω = 0.90 and κ₀ = 10, b = 1.115, r_c²/ℓ² = 0.199, and r_c'²/ℓ² = 0.231.
- At Ω/ω = 0.50 and κ₀ = 10, b = 1.056, r_c²/ℓ² = 0.117, and r_c'²/ℓ² = 0.103.
- As Γ_LLL⁻¹ → ∞, r_c²/ℓ² approaches 0.225 and r_c'²/ℓ² approaches 0.368.
- The amplitudes of higher Landau level components (i ≥ 2) are less than 5% at Ω = 0.9ω and less than 10% at Ω = 0.5ω for κ₀ = 10.

## Context

This work generalizes an earlier account by Baym and Pethick (2004), which assumed the vortex structure was the same throughout the lattice, and is complementary to the numerical work of Cozzini, Stringari, and Tozzo (2004) for the case when rotation equals the transverse trapping frequency. The calculations enable analysis of current experiments in the intermediate regime where the interaction energy is comparable to ℏΩ, and provide a framework for future calculations of equilibrium lattice distortions and elastic constants.
