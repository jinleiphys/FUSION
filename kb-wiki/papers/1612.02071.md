# Reggeized model for $╬│p \to Žü^- ╬ö^{++}(1232)$ photoproduction

> A model for the reaction $╬│p\toŽü^-╬ö^{++}$ is presented with the $t$-channel $ŽĆ+Žü$ exchanges reggeized to describe the reaction up to high energies. Gauge invariance of $Žü$ exchange is discussed in connection with the convergence of the reaction cross section at high energy. The roles of electromagnetic (EM) multipole moments of $╬ö^{++}$ baryon and of $Žü^-$ meson are analyzed in total and differential cross sections and spin density matrix elements. Photon polarization asymmetry $╬Ż$ is predicted for a measurement of electromagnetic moments of $Žü^-$ and of $╬ö^{++}$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Reggeized model for $\gamma p \to \rho^- \Delta^{++}(1232)$ photoproduction that achieves convergent cross sections up to high energies, but only under specific gauge prescriptions: the conventional approach (Model I) converges up to about 8 GeV, while the minimal gauge prescription (Model II) extends convergence to 16 GeV, with the caveat that the $\Delta^{++}$ electromagnetic multipole moments $\lambda_{\Delta^{++}}$ and $\chi_{\Delta^{++}}$ must be turned off in Model I to maintain convergence.

## Method

The authors construct a gauge-invariant Born amplitude for $\gamma p \to \rho^- \Delta^{++}$ using $t$-channel $\pi$ and $\rho$ exchanges, with the $\rho$ exchange reggeized following standard Regge-pole formalism. Two models are compared: Model I uses the full $\gamma\Delta\Delta$ and $\gamma\rho\rho$ vertices including all electromagnetic multipole moments, while Model II applies a minimal gauge prescription retaining only charge couplings in the $s$- and $u$-channel poles. The strong $\rho N\Delta$ coupling is taken from quark model predictions, and the $\pi N\Delta$ coupling is fitted to data, with the $a_2$ exchange found negligible at the $10^{-2}$ level.

## Key numbers

- $f_{\rho^- p\Delta^{++}} = 5.5$ (Model I) and $8.57$ (Model II) for the strong $\rho N\Delta$ coupling constant
- $f_{\pi^- p\Delta^{++}} = 1.7$ (Model I) and $2$ (Model II) for the $\pi N\Delta$ coupling constant
- $\kappa_{\Delta^{++}} = 4.34$ (Model I), $\lambda_{\Delta^{++}} = 6.18$ (Model I, turned off for convergence), $\chi_{\Delta^{++}} = 12.34$ (Model I, turned off for convergence)
- $\kappa_\rho = 1.01$ and $\lambda_\rho = -0.41$ for the $\rho^-$ meson electromagnetic multipole moments (fixed in both models)
- $g_{\gamma\pi\rho} = \pm 0.224$ from the measured decay width
- The $\rho$ trajectory: $\alpha_\rho(t) = 0.9(t - m_\rho^2) + 1$; the $\pi$ trajectory: $\alpha_\pi(t) = 0.7(t - m_\pi^2)$
- Model I total cross section converges up to $E_\gamma \approx 8$ GeV; Model II converges up to $E_\gamma = 16$ GeV
- The dip in differential cross section appears at $-t \approx 0.4$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Reggeized models for $\gamma N \to \rho^\pm N$ (Yu and Kong, 2015) and $\gamma p \to \pi^\pm \Delta$ (Yu and Kong, 2015) to the combined $\gamma p \to \rho^- \Delta^{++}$ process, addressing the divergence issues that arise from spin-1 and spin-3/2 propagators. It improves upon earlier attempts by Clark and Kramer (1990) and by McLark (1994) by providing a gauge-invariant effective Lagrangian formulation without ad hoc counter terms or cutoff functions. The framework is intended as a tool for analyzing multipion photoproduction data from the GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab, particularly for searches of exotic mesons such as $\pi_1(1400)$ and $\pi_1(1600)$.
