# On The Structure of A=3 Nuclei

> The hole in the charge distribution of $^3{\text He}$ is a major problem in A=3 nuclei. The canonical wavefucntion of A=3 nuclei which does well for electromagnetic properties of A=3 nuclei fails to produce the hole in A=3 nuclei. The hole is normally assumed to arise from explicit quark degree of freedom. Very often quark degrees of freedom are imposed to propose a different short range part of the wavefunction for A=3 to explain the hole in $^3{\text He}$. So an hybrid model with nucleonic degree of freedom in outer part and quark degrees of freedom in the inner part of the nucleus have been invoked to understand the above problem. Here we present a different picture with a new wavefunction working at short range within nucleonic degrees of freedom itself. So the above problem is explained here based entirely on the nucleonic degree of freedom only.

# On The Structure of A=3 Nuclei

## Key claim
The hole in the charge distribution of ³He can be explained entirely within nucleonic degrees of freedom, without invoking explicit quark degrees of freedom, by using an antisymmetric orbital wavefunction at short range (r < 1 fm) while retaining the canonical symmetric wavefunction at larger distances.

## Method
The authors split the A=3 wavefunction into two spatial regions: for r > r₀ (~1 fm) they use the standard fully symmetric Faddeev orbital wavefunction with antisymmetric spin-isospin part, which reproduces the magnetic moments; for r < r₀ they propose a completely antisymmetric orbital wavefunction combined with a symmetric spin-isospin wavefunction. The antisymmetric orbital wavefunction forces the charge density to vanish at r=0, producing the observed central hole.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic moment of ³H: predicted 2.79 nm (experimental 2.97 nm)
- Magnetic moment of ³He: predicted -1.91 nm (experimental -2.12 nm)
- The transition radius between the two wavefunction regimes is r₀ ~ 1 fm for ³He

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study of A=3 clustering (Abbas and Ahmad, 2011) and directly challenges the prevailing hybrid models that invoke 6-quark clusters (Hoodbhoy, 1980s) or 9-quark clusters (Abbas, 1986) to explain the central hole in ³He and ³H charge distributions. The approach takes inspiration from early quark model attempts at antisymmetric orbital wavefunctions (Mitra and Majumdar, 1966; Kretschmar, 1967) which failed for baryons but are shown here to succeed for A=3 nuclei. This enables a purely nucleonic description of the first "genuine nucleus" with both interior and surface structure.
