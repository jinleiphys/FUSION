# Event-by-event azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In this paper we present with full details a systematic quantification, on an even-by-event basis, of the hard probe response to the geometry and fluctuations of the hot QCD matter created in heavy ion collisions at both the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching is extracted and decomposed as harmonic responses (for n=1,2,3,4,5,6) to the corresponding harmonics in the fluctuating initial condition. We show that such jet response harmonics are sensitive to the jet quenching models as well as to the bulk matter initial compositions. By studying these for all centralities at both RHIC and LHC energies we put a strong constraint on the path-length and medium-density dependence of jet energy loss. We also examine the hard-soft di-hadron correlation arising from the hard and soft sectors' responses to the common initial fluctuations. We demonstrate that the experimentally observed "hard-ridge" can be explained this way and that its dependence on the trigger-azimuthal-angle could also be qualitatively understood.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies, on an event-by-event basis, how the azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching (decomposed into harmonic coefficients for n=1-6) responds to fluctuating initial conditions in heavy ion collisions, and demonstrates that only the near-Tc enhancement (NTcE) model for jet energy loss survives the second-harmonic test when comparing with data from both RHIC and LHC, while other harmonics from this model are consistent with available data.

## Method

An event-by-event Monte Carlo simulation was employed using a Glauber model to generate fluctuating initial conditions for the medium entropy density and binary collision density. Three geometric jet-quenching models were tested: the near-Tc enhancement (NTcE) model with m=1 and density-dependent jet-medium coupling, the L^2 model (m=1, constant coupling), and the L^3 model (m=2, constant coupling). The azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching was extracted by Fourier-decomposing the azimuthal-angle-dependent nuclear modification factor R_AA(phi) into harmonic coefficients v_n^h for n=1-6, and the hard-soft dihadron correlation was computed from the common response of hard and soft sectors to the same fluctuating initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- The NTcE model parameters: enhancement factor xi=6, central density s_c=7/fm^3, width s_w=2/fm^3
- The NTcE model predicts a reduction of about 30% for the average jet-medium interaction from RHIC to LHC: <kappa>_RHIC : <kappa>_LHC ≈ 1 : 0.72
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA ≈ 0.18 in the 0-5% most central collisions at RHIC sqrt(s)=0.2 TeV was used to fix the parameter kappa_0 in each model
- The exponent n (spectrum index) values used: 8.1 at RHIC 0.2 TeV, 6.0 at LHC 2.76 TeV, 5.4 at LHC 5.5 TeV
- The second harmonic v_2^h from the NTcE model is consistent with RHIC and LHC data; the L^3 model matches RHIC v_2^h data while the L^2 model matches LHC v_2^h data

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Liao (2008) that proposed the near-Tc enhancement model to resolve the discrepancy between predicted and measured elliptic flow of high-pT hadrons at RHIC, and follows the initial investigation by Zhang and Liao (2012) on the connection between initial state fluctuations and jet azimuthal anisotropy. The study enables a new approach to jet quenching physics by shifting from studying average anisotropy patterns to event-by-event extraction of full harmonic information, providing a sensitive tool for probing initial conditions that is complementary to what can be learned from studying bulk expansion responses as done in hydrodynamic calculations by Alver (2010), Luzum (2011), and Qiu (2011).
