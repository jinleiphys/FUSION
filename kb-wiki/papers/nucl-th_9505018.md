# On the impossibility of temperature extraction from heavy ion induced particle spectra

> Spectra of various particle species have been calculated with the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model for very central collisions of Au+Au. They are compatible with the idea of a fully stopped thermal source which exhibits a transversal expansion besides the thermal distribution of an ideal gas. However, the microscopic analyses of the local flow velocities and temperatures indicate much lower temperatures at densities associated with the freeze-out. The results express the overall impossibility of a model-independent determination of nuclear temperatures from heavy ion spectral data, also at other energies e.g. CERN or for other species (i.e. pions, kaons, hyperons)!

# On the impossibility of temperature extraction from heavy ion induced particle spectra

## Key claim
Temperatures extracted from the slopes of particle spectra in heavy ion collisions, even when collective flow is accounted for, are systematically 2-3 times higher than the true local temperatures at freeze-out densities. This discrepancy arises because the high transverse momentum components of the spectra originate from collective motion of fast, low-temperature cells rather than from the high-momentum tail of a hot thermal source.

## Method
The authors performed Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) calculations for central Au+Au collisions at 150 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b=0, propagating the system for 300 fm/c and then applying a 3 fm configuration space coalescence to form fragments. The resulting triple-differential spectra were fitted with a transversally expanding, thermally equilibrated source model assuming a homogeneous sphere with linearly increasing flow velocity. Separately, the local flow velocities and temperatures were extracted microscopically by dividing configuration space into cylindrical cells and computing the mean and spread of the local velocity distribution for each cell.

## Key numbers
- Fitted slope temperatures from the macroscopic analysis: 20–25 MeV for all fragment species
- Averaged collective flow velocities from the macroscopic fit: 0.1–0.13 c (10–13% of speed of light)
- Microscopically determined true local temperatures at densities below 0.5 ρ₀ (freeze-out densities): below 10 MeV
- The macroscopic slope temperatures are 2–3 times higher than the microscopic local temperatures
- After 100 fm/c, about one third of the system remains in the central reaction zone (defined as cells with density at least half the maximum density)
- In the intermediate reaction stages (t=40–60 fm/c), the longitudinal spread of the velocity distribution is 50% higher than the transverse spread

## Context
This work extends the debate on temperature determination in heavy ion collisions, specifically addressing the discrepancy between chemical thermometers (e.g., isotope ratios and fragment abundances) and spectral slope thermometers. The authors note that chemical analyses by Kuh93 yield temperatures of about 8 MeV from fragment abundances in the same Au+Au system at 150 MeV/nucleon, while spectral analyses by Cof94, Jeo93, and Rei94 suggest higher temperatures. The QMD analysis shows that the spectral slope method systematically overestimates temperatures, and the authors argue that this finding applies also to higher-energy collisions at AGS and CERN (Hei92). The work enables a more cautious interpretation of temperature extractions and suggests that chemical thermometers (e.g., π/p ratios accounting for feeding from unstable states and absorption) may be more reliable than spectral slope methods.
