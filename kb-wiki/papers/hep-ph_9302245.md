# Strong and Radiative Meson Decays in a Generalized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> We investigate strong and radiative meson decays in a generalized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The one loop order calculation provides a satisfactory agreement with the data for the mesonic spectrum and for radiative decays. Higher order effects for strong decays of $ρ$ and $K^*$ are estimated to be large. We also discuss the role of the flavour mixing determinantal interaction.

## Key claim

The generalized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, when evaluated at one-loop order with full Bethe-Salpeter vertex functions, gives satisfactory agreement with experimental data for the meson spectrum and radiative decays, but the one-loop calculation is clearly insufficient for the strong decays of the ρ and K* mesons, for which higher-order (two-loop) corrections are estimated to be large.

## Method

The authors work in flavour SU(3) with a Lagrangian containing two four-fermion coupling constants (G₁, G₂), a six-fermion 't Hooft determinantal interaction (K), and a covariant four-momentum cutoff Λ = 1 GeV. Meson masses and decay constants are obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equations for quark-antiquark bound states at one-loop order. Strong and radiative decay amplitudes are computed from triangle diagrams with the full momentum-dependent vertex functions, and higher-order corrections for the ρ and K* strong decays are estimated by dressing the vector meson propagator with a pseudoscalar-meson loop.

## Key numbers

- Meson masses (all in MeV) obtained with Λ = 1 GeV, G₁Λ² = 3.95, G₂Λ² = 5.43, KΛ⁵ = 42, m_u = m_d = 4 MeV, m_s = 115 MeV: M_π = 136.5 (exp. 139.6), M_K = 497.5 (497.7), M_η = 549 (548.8), M_η' = 936 (957.5), M_ρ = 775 (768.3), M_ω = 764 (781.95), M_K* = 898 (891.6), M_Φ = 990 (1019.41), M_a0 = 970 (983.3)
- Pion decay constant F_π = 93.9 MeV; kaon decay constant F_K = 96.6 MeV
- Vacuum expectation values: -⟨ūu⟩^{1/3} = 272 MeV (experimental 225 ± 35); ⟨s̄s⟩/⟨ūu⟩ = 0.74 (experimental 0.8 ± 0.2)
- The one-loop strong decay widths for ρ → ππ and K* → Kπ are much smaller than the empirical values; including the two-loop estimate improves the results by about a factor of 2 but still leaves them too small

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of meson properties (Bernard et al., various years; Vogl and Weise, various years) by performing the first systematic one-loop calculation of three-point functions (strong and radiative decays) with full Bethe-Salpeter vertex functions, rather than using the leading term in a momentum expansion as in earlier attempts (ref. [5] in the paper). The results show that the simple quark-antiquark description fails for ρ and K* strong decays, supporting the earlier observation by Krewald et al. (ref. [9]) regarding the pion electromagnetic form factor. The authors disagree with the conclusions of Takizawa and Krewald (ref. [10]) on radiative decays π⁰ → γγ and η → γγ, arguing that their procedure of removing the cutoff and using the empirical η mass is inconsistent. The work enables future studies of isospin-breaking effects and SU(3) breaking in low-energy meson interactions.
