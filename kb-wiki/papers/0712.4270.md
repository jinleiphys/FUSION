# Charge form factors and nucleon shape

> To obtain further information on the geometric shape of the nucleon, the proton charge form factor is decomposed into two terms, which are connected respectively with a spherically symmetric and an intrinsic quadrupole part of the proton's charge density. Quark model relations are employed to derive expressions for both terms. In particular, the proton's intrinsic quadrupole form factor is obtained from a relation between the N -> Delta and neutron charge form factors. The proposed decomposition shows that the neutron charge form factor is an observable manifestation of an intrinsic quadrupole form factor of the nucleon. Furthermore, it affords an interpretation of recent electron-nucleon scattering data in terms of a nonspherical distribution of quark-antiquark pairs in the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the neutron charge form factor is an observable manifestation of an intrinsic quadrupole form factor of the nucleon, and that the scaling law violation between proton charge and magnetic form factors at high momentum transfers is a consequence of the proton's nonspherical charge distribution.

## Method

The author decomposes the proton charge form factor into a spherically symmetric term and an intrinsic quadrupole term, where the latter is obtained from a relation between the N to Delta charge quadrupole transition form factor and the neutron charge form factor. Quark model relations based on SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry and its breaking by two-body exchange currents are employed to derive expressions for both terms, and the resulting C2/M1 ratio for the N to Delta transition is expressed in terms of the neutron form factor ratio G_C^n/G_M^n.

## Key numbers

- The static C2/M1 ratio at Q^2=0 is calculated as -0.031, in good agreement with experimental E2/M1 ratios from pion photoproduction
- The SU(6) relation between magnetic dipole transition form factor and elastic neutron magnetic form factor: G_{M1}^{N to Delta}(Q^2) = -sqrt(2) * G_M^n(Q^2)
- The relation between charge quadrupole transition form factor and elastic neutron charge form factor: G_{C2}^{N to Delta}(Q^2) = -(3*sqrt(2)/Q^2) * G_C^n(Q^2)
- The static quadrupole moment relation: Q_{N to Delta} = (1/sqrt(2)) * r_n^2
- The asymptotic C2/M1 ratio for Q^2 to infinity ranges between -0.06 and -0.21 depending on the Galster parameter d
- The dipole form factor parameter Lambda^2 = 0.71 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Buchmann 1997, 2000, 2004, 2005) on relations between elastic and inelastic nucleon form factors, and builds on the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry framework developed by Gursey and Radicati (1964), Beg et al. (1964), and Sakita (1964). The paper provides a theoretical interpretation of recent polarized electron-nucleon scattering experiments (Jones 2000, Gayou 2003) that showed large deviations from the scaling law, and enables future extraction of the nucleon's intrinsic quadrupole deformation from elastic form factor data.
