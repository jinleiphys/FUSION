# Coupled-channel analysis of $D^+\to K^- π^+π^+$ decay

> We perform a coupled-channel analysis of pseudodata for the D+-> K-pi+pi+ Dalitz plot. The pseudodata are generated from the isobar model of the E791 Collaboration, and are reasonably realistic. We demonstrate that it is feasible to analyze the high-quality data within a coupled-channel framework that describes the final state interaction of D+-> K-pi+pi+ as multiple rescatterings of three pseudoscalar mesons through two-pseudoscalar-meson interactions in accordance with the two-body and three-body unitarity. The two-pseudoscalar-meson interactions are designed to reproduce empirical pi pi and pi bar K scattering amplitudes. Furthermore, we also include mechanisms that are beyond simple iterations of the two-body interactions, i.e., a three-meson force, derived from the hidden local symmetry model. A picture of hadronic dynamics in D+-> K-pi+pi+ described by our coupled-channel model is found to be quite different from those of the previous isobar-type analyses. For example, we find that the D+-> K-pi+pi+ decay width can get almost triplicated when the rescattering mechanisms are turned on. Among the rescattering mechanisms, those associated with the rho(770) bar K0 channel, which contribute to D+-> K-pi+pi+ only through a channel coupling, give a large contribution, and significantly improve the quality of the fits. The K-pi+ s-wave amplitude from our analysis is reasonably consistent with those extracted from the E791 model independent partial-wave analysis; the hadronic rescattering and the coupling to the rho(770) bar K0 channel play a major role here. We also find that the dressed D+ decay vertices have phases, induced by the strong rescatterings, that strongly depend on the momenta of the final pseudoscalar mesons. Although the conventional isobar-type analyses have assumed the phases to be constant, this common assumption is not supported by our more microscopic viewpoint.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a coupled-channel framework incorporating three-body unitarity and final-state rescattering can quantitatively fit $D^+\to K^-\pi^+\pi^+$ Dalitz plot pseudodata, but the resulting hadronic dynamics differ substantially from conventional isobar-type analyses; the common isobar-model assumption that the phases of dressed $D^+$ decay vertices are constant is not supported by this more microscopic approach.

## Method

The authors construct a unitary coupled-channel model for the three-pseudoscalar-meson final state by first fitting two-body $\pi\pi$ and $\pi\bar K$ scattering amplitudes (including $I=1/2$ $s$-, $p$-, $d$-waves, $I=3/2$ $\bar K\pi$ $s$-wave, $I=2$ $\pi\pi$ $s$-wave, and $I=1$ $\pi\pi$ $p$-wave) to empirical data from LASS and CERN-Munich. They then solve the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas form of the Faddeev equation for three-meson scattering, including a three-meson force derived from the hidden local symmetry model, and fit the $D^+\to K^-\pi^+\pi^+$ decay pseudodata (generated from the E791 isobar model) by adjusting only the bare $D^+\to Rc$ vertex strengths and phases while keeping the two-meson interactions fixed.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier coupled-channel formalism (Nakamura, 2015) for heavy-meson decays into three pseudoscalar mesons, applying it for the first time to a realistic $D$-meson decay. It directly addresses inconsistencies noted in prior isobar-type analyses by E791 (2002), FOCUS (2005, 2009), and CLEO (2007), where the $K^-\pi^+$ $s$-wave phase from model-independent partial-wave analysis disagreed with Watson theorem expectations based on LASS scattering data. The analysis also builds on the work of Magalhães et al. (2011) who studied three-body final-state interactions for this decay but did not perform a direct Dalitz plot fit. The results enable future extraction of genuine $K\pi$ scattering amplitudes from $D$-decay data without isobar-model artifacts, and suggest that $D\to \bar K\pi\pi$ and $D\to \pi\pi\pi$ decays could serve as a laboratory to study three-meson forces.
