# Erratum: Eur. Phy. J. C 12 (2000) 109 (A Second Look at Single Photon Production in $S+Au$ Collisions at 200 A$\cdot$GeV and Implications for Quark Hadron Phase Transition)

> It has been shown recently that the values of $J_T$ and $J_L$ which appear in Eqs.(2) and (5) of the above paper and which are taken from the work of Aurenche et al are too large by a factor of 4. Correcting for this changes the Fig.~2. More-over, now the emissions from the quark matter dominate only for $p_T <$ 1 GeV/$c$. The basic result of the paper, that the upper limit of the single-photon production in the WA80 experiment is consistent with the rates for photon production at two-loop level remains valid.

## Key claim
The original paper's conclusion that the WA80 upper limit on single-photon production is consistent with two-loop photon production rates remains valid, but only after correcting that the values of \(J_T\) and \(J_L\) from Aurenche et al. were too large by a factor of 4; with this correction, quark-matter photon emission dominates only for \(p_T < 1\) GeV/\(c\).

## Method
The authors corrected a factor-of-4 error in the integrals \(J_T\) and \(J_L\) used in Eqs. (2) and (5) of the original paper, which were taken from the work of Aurenche et al. They recalculated the transverse-momentum dependence of single-photon production in \(S+Au\) collisions at 200 \(A\cdot\)GeV and updated Fig. 2 accordingly.

## Key numbers
- The values of \(J_T\) and \(J_L\) from Aurenche et al. were too large by a factor of 4.
- After correction, quark-matter photon emission dominates only for \(p_T < 1\) GeV/\(c\).
- The upper limit of single-photon production in the WA80 experiment remains consistent with two-loop photon production rates.

## Context
This erratum corrects a numerical error in the authors' earlier paper (Srivastava and Sinha, 2000) that used integrals from Aurenche et al. The correction narrows the regime where quark-matter emission dominates to low transverse momentum, but does not alter the original conclusion that the WA80 data are consistent with two-loop rates. This work enables more precise comparisons between photon data and theoretical rates for the quark-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions.
