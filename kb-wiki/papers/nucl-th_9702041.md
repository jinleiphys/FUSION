# $Σ$ Like Term in Pion Electroproduction Near Threshold

> Sum of the time component of $Σ$ term and the induced pseudo scalar term in axial current is shown to be the t-channel pion pole in Born terms for pion electroproduction near threshold. We also show that this $Σ$ term represents the charged pseudo scalar quark density matrix elements in nucleon and manifests itself in the $L_0^+$ amplitude on this reaction.

# Σ Like Term in Pion Electroproduction Near Threshold

## Key claim

The time component of the Σ term (a chiral symmetry breaking term) and the induced pseudo-scalar term in the axial current together account for exactly the t-channel pion pole in Born terms for pion electroproduction near threshold, with each contributing one half of the t-pole contribution. This equivalence holds specifically at the pion threshold, and the Σ term manifests itself in the longitudinal multipole amplitude L₀⁺.

## Method

The authors rederive the transition amplitude for pion electroproduction using the real pion limit (rather than the soft pion limit) within a current algebra (CA) approach based on PCAC (partial conservation of axial current). They separate the amplitude into contributions from the equal-time commutator C_ν^a, the axial-current correlation function T_{μν}^a, and the Σ term, then evaluate the time component of the Σ term using CVC (conservation of vector current) and the model-independent commutator [D^a(x), V_0(0)]. The resulting expression is compared to the t-channel pion pole from the Born amplitude, and the contribution to the L₀⁺ multipole amplitude is estimated using experimental form factors for G_A(t) and the pion-pole dominance assumption for G_P(t).

## Key numbers

- The value of |i Σ̄₀^α(γ*π^±)| at the γ point (k² = 0) is estimated as 38.1 MeV, using the experimental axial form factor G_A(t) and the pion-pole dominance relation for G_P(t).
- The ratio α_theory = ΔL₀⁺(σ-pole) / L₀⁺ for γ*π⁺ at the γ point is approximately 1/2, meaning half of the total L₀⁺ amplitude at threshold comes from the σ-pole contribution.
- The momentum transfer at the pion threshold is given by t = (k² − m_π²)/(1 + μ), where μ = m_π/M (with M the nucleon mass).
- The axial form factor is parameterized as G_A(t) = g_A/(1 − t/Λ²)² with Λ ∼ 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the current algebra approach to pion electroproduction by resolving a long-standing flaw: the inability to derive the t-channel pion pole from CA methods, which was not problematic for photoproduction (where the t-pole vanishes at threshold) but becomes essential for electroproduction due to Coulomb polarization of the virtual photon. The authors build on earlier work by Furlan et al. (1974) on the Σ term concept, and by Nath and Singh (1989) on its spatial component, while addressing criticisms by Kamal (1989) and Bernstein and Holstein (1991) regarding model dependence. The result enables extraction of the charged pseudo-scalar quark density in the nucleon from future measurements of the L₀⁺ amplitude in π⁺ electroproduction near threshold, analogous to how the σ term in π-N scattering (Cheng and Li, 1990) provides information about scalar quark densities.
