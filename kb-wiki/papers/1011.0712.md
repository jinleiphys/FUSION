# Statistical and dynamical fluctuations in the ratios of higher cumulants in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> With the help of transport and statistical models, we find that the ratios of higher net-proton cumulants measured at RHIC are dominated by the statistical fluctuations. Future measurements should focus on the dynamical fluctuations, which are relevant to the underlying mechanisms of particle production, the critical phenomena in particular. We also demonstrate that a proton-antiproton correlation directly show if protons and antiprotons are emitted independently.

## Key claim

The ratios of higher net-proton cumulants measured at RHIC are dominated by statistical fluctuations, meaning that the observed centrality and energy dependence of Skewness and Kurtosis can be reproduced by a simple Skellam distribution of independent protons and antiprotons, without requiring critical phenomena. The paper argues that future measurements must focus on dynamical fluctuations (the deviation from statistical baselines) to access the underlying particle production mechanisms.

## Method

The authors compare net-proton cumulant ratios from the STAR experiment with results from three models: AMPT (a transport model in two versions, default and string melting), UrQMD (another transport model), and Therminator (a statistical hadron-resonance-gas model). They compute the statistical baseline using the Skellam distribution, which describes the net-proton distribution if protons and antiprotons are emitted independently as Poisson variables. Dynamical ratios are defined as the measured ratio minus the Skellam expectation, and a proton-antiproton correlation function is also introduced to test independence directly.

## Key numbers

*   Net-proton Kurtosis from STAR at 200 GeV Au+Au collisions follows the same trend as the Skellam statistical baseline as a function of number of participants (N_part), with both decreasing with centrality.
*   The statistical Kurtosis for a Skellam distribution is K_stat = 1/(<N_p> + <N_antip>), where <N_p> and <N_antip> are the mean proton and antiproton multiplicities.
*   The statistical ratio R_{4,2,stat} = 1 exactly, meaning the Kurtosis ratio is purely statistical in the Skellam limit.
*   At 200 GeV, dynamical Kurtosis and Skewness from Therminator are zero at all centralities; from AMPT default they are positive in peripheral collisions and approach zero in central collisions.
*   Dynamical Skewness from AMPT default shows significant dependence on incident energy from 39 to 200 GeV, especially in peripheral collisions, while dynamical Kurtosis is almost independent of incident energy over the same range.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Karsch and Redlich (2010) that HRG model results match STAR data, by showing that the agreement arises because the data are dominated by statistical fluctuations rather than dynamical physics. It also builds on the STAR measurement of net-proton Skewness and Kurtosis (STAR Collaboration, 2010) and on earlier work by Bialas and Peschanski (1986) on factorial moments for removing statistical fluctuations. The paper proposes dynamical cumulant ratios and proton-antiproton correlations as observables for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, where a sign change in the dynamical third or fourth cumulant could signal proximity to the QCD critical point.
