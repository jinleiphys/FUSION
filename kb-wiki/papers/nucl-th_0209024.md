# Viscous Corrections to Spectra, Elliptic Flow, and HBT Radii

> I compute the first viscous correction to the thermal distribution function. With this correction, I calculate the effect of viscosity on spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii. Indicating the breakdown of hydrodynamics, viscous corrections become of order one for $p_{T}\sim 1.5$ GeV. Viscous corrections to HBT radii are particularly large and reduce the outward and longitudinal radii. This reduction is a direct consequence of the reduction in longitudinal pressure.

# Viscous Corrections to Spectra, Elliptic Flow, and HBT Radii

## Key claim
Viscous corrections to the thermal distribution function become of order one for transverse momentum around 1.4–1.5 GeV, signaling the breakdown of hydrodynamics; the paper shows that these corrections naturally explain why ideal hydrodynamics fails to reproduce single-particle spectra above this momentum and why HBT radii from ideal hydrodynamics are too large.

## Method
The author computes the first viscous correction to the thermal distribution function for a Boltzmann gas, relating the correction coefficient to the shear viscosity via the sound attenuation length Γ_s = (4/3 η)/(sT). Using a generalized blast wave model with boost-invariant Bjorken expansion (decoupling at τ_o = 6.5 Fm, T_o = 160 MeV, R_o = 10.0 Fm, maximum radial velocity u^r_o = 0.5 c), the Cooper-Frye formula is applied to calculate viscous corrections to particle spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii.

## Key numbers
- Viscous corrections become of order one when p_T approaches 1.4 GeV, consistent with the breakdown of ideal hydrodynamics above p_T ~ 1.5 GeV.
- The sound attenuation length is taken as Γ_s/τ = 1/3, based on perturbative estimates giving Γ_s/τ ~ 1 and assuming non-perturbative effects shorten equilibration times.
- Blast wave parameters for central collisions: τ_o = 6.5 Fm, T_o = 160 MeV, R_o = 10.0 Fm, u^r_o = 0.5 c.
- For non-central collisions (AuAu at impact parameter b = 6 Fm): u_o = 0.5, u_2 = 0.1, R_o = 6 Fm, τ_o = 4.0 Fm.
- Viscosity reduces elliptic flow by a factor of three in the non-central blast wave model.
- For large p_T, the ratio of corrected to uncorrected spectrum scales as δdN/dN_o ≈ (Γ_s/(4τ)) (p_T/T)^2.

## Context
This work extends the ideal hydrodynamic calculations of Teaney (2001) and Kolb (2001), which successfully predicted elliptic flow but failed to reproduce spectra above p_T ~ 1.5 GeV and gave HBT radii significantly too large compared to data (Bass and Dumitru, 2000). The viscous correction framework provides a natural explanation for both failures by reducing longitudinal pressure and thereby narrowing the particle distribution in the longitudinal direction, which directly reduces the outward and longitudinal HBT radii. The paper enables future viscous hydrodynamic simulations to systematically compare with heavy-ion collision data across multiple observables.
