# The strong $ρNN$ coupling derived from QCD

> We study the two point correlation function of two nucleon currents sandwiched between the vacuum and the rho meson state. The light cone QCD sum rules are derived both for $ρNN$ vector and tensor couplings simultaneously. The contribution from the excited states and the continuum is subtracted cleanly through the double Borel transform with respect to the two external momenta, $p_1^2$, $p^2_2=(p-q)^2$. Our results are $g_ρ=(2.5\pm 0.2)$, $κ_ρ=(8.0\pm 2.0), in good agreement with the values used in the nuclear forces.

## Key claim

The light cone QCD sum rules yield the vector coupling \(g_\rho = 2.5 \pm 0.2\) and the tensor-vector ratio \(\kappa_\rho = 8.0 \pm 2.0\), which agree with the large values used in nuclear forces but deviate strongly from the vector meson dominance model prediction of \(\kappa_v = 3.7\). The result depends on the model rho wave functions and the truncation of the operator product expansion on the light cone.

## Method

The author calculates the two-point correlation function of two nucleon currents sandwiched between the vacuum and the rho meson state, using light cone QCD sum rules. The rho meson wave functions up to twist four are employed, and the double Borel transform with respect to the two external momenta \(p_1^2\) and \(p_2^2\) is applied to subtract the contributions from excited states and the continuum. The sum rules are derived for the chiral odd tensor structures, and the results are divided by Ioffe's mass sum rule for the nucleon to reduce the uncertainty from the nucleon overlapping amplitude \(\lambda_N\).

## Key numbers

*   Vector coupling: \(g_\rho = 2.5 \pm 0.2\).
*   Tensor-vector ratio: \(\kappa_\rho = 8.0 \pm 2.0\).
*   Combined coupling squared: \(g_\rho^2 (1+\kappa_\rho)^2 / 4\pi = 39 \pm 10\).
*   Intermediate result: \(g_\rho (1+\kappa_\rho) = 22 \pm 3\).
*   Intermediate result: \(1+\kappa_\rho = 9.0 \pm 2.0\).
*   Borel parameter working interval: \(0.9\ \text{GeV}^2 \leq M_B^2 \leq 1.8\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   Continuum threshold: \(s_0 = 2.25\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   Rho meson mass: \(m_\rho = 770\ \text{MeV}\).
*   Rho meson decay constant: \(f_\rho = 198 \pm 7\ \text{MeV}\).
*   Nucleon mass: \(m_N = 0.938\ \text{GeV}\).
*   Nucleon overlapping amplitude: \(\lambda_N = 0.026\ \text{GeV}^3\) at scale \(\mu = 1\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the light cone QCD sum rule method, previously applied to pion couplings with hadrons (Zhu 1995, 1996, 1997) and to rho meson decays (Zhu 1998), to the direct calculation of the \(\rho NN\) couplings from QCD. It contradicts the result of Wen and Hwang (1997), who obtained \(\kappa_\rho = 3.6\) using an external field method with a vector-like \(\rho q q\) interaction, which the author argues implicitly assumes vector meson dominance. The calculated large tensor-vector ratio supports the phenomenological values used in nuclear forces by Brown and Machleidt (1994) and enables a first-principles QCD-based input for meson-exchange models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
