# Implementing the exact kinematical constraint in the saturation formalism

> We revisit the issue of the large negative next-to-leading order (NLO) cross section for single inclusive hadron production in $pA$ collisions in the saturation formalism. By implementing the exact kinematical constraint in the modified dipole splitting functions, two additional positive NLO correction terms are obtained. In the asymptotic large $k_\perp$ limit, we analytically show that these two terms become as large as the negative NLO contributions found in our previous calculation. Furthermore, the numerical results demonstrate that the applicable regime of the saturation formalism can be extended to a larger $k_\perp$ window, where the exact matching between the saturation formalism (in the asymptotic $k_\perp$ regime) and the collinear factorization calculations will have to be performed separately. In addition, after significantly improving the numerical accuracy of the NLO correction, we obtain excellent agreement with the LHC and RHIC data for forward hadron productions.

## Key claim

By implementing the exact kinematical constraint in the modified dipole splitting functions for single inclusive hadron production in pA collisions, two additional positive NLO correction terms are obtained that partially cancel the large negative NLO cross section found in previous calculations, but only when the transverse momentum k_perp exceeds approximately 2 Q_s; the effect is negligible when k_perp is below the saturation scale.

## Method

The authors modified the dipole splitting functions for the q→qg and g→g channels by incorporating a Bessel function term arising from the exact kinematical constraint derived from conservation of the minus component of four-momentum in 2→2 processes. After subtracting the small-x logarithm through the BK evolution equation, they obtained additional hard factors L_q(k_perp) and L_g(k_perp) which were evaluated analytically in the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) model and numerically using both Mathematica and a C++ code (SOLO). The full NLO cross section including these terms was then compared to RHIC and LHC data for forward hadron production.

## Key numbers

- The additional term L_q(k_perp) asymptotically behaves as (alpha_s N_c S_perp)/(4 pi^2) * (4 Q_s^2)/(k_perp^4) in the large k_perp limit, compared to the NLO quark channel hard factor which behaves as -(alpha_s N_c)/(4 pi^2) * x q(x) * (61/12) * (Q_s^2)/(k_perp^4)
- L_q(k_perp) becomes important when k_perp rises to approximately 2 Q_s and higher, while being negligible when k_perp < Q_s
- The kinematical constraint modifies the rapidity divergent integral from ∫ dξ/(1-ξ) = ln(1/x_g) to ∫ dξ/(1-ξ) = ln(1/x_g) + ln(k_perp^2/l_perp^2), where x_g ≡ k_perp^2/(x_p s)
- The squared saturation momentum Q_s^2 in nuclear targets is A^(1/3) times that in protons

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation of single inclusive hadron production in pA collisions by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2011, 2012) and the numerical analysis by Stasto, Xiao, and Zaslavsky (2013, 2014), which found that the NLO cross section becomes negative above a cutoff momentum slightly greater than Q_s. The implementation of the kinematical constraint was motivated by independent NLO calculations by Beuf (2014) and Altinoluk et al. (2014) discussing Ioffe time dependence. The additional terms enable extending the saturation formalism's applicability to larger k_perp values, where matching to collinear factorization calculations will need to be performed separately.
