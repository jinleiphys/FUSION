# The electromagnetic Sigma-to-Lambda hyperon transition form factors at low energies

> Using dispersion theory the low-energy electromagnetic form factors for the transition of a Sigma to a Lambda hyperon are related to the pion vector form factor. The additionally required input, i.e. the two-pion--Sigma--Lambda amplitudes are determined from relativistic next-to-leading-order (NLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory including the baryons from the octet and optionally from the decuplet. Pion rescattering is again taken into account by dispersion theory. It turns out that the inclusion of decuplet baryons is not an option but a necessity to obtain reasonable results. The electric transition form factor remains very small in the whole low-energy region. The magnetic transition form factor depends strongly on one not very well determined low-energy constant of the NLO Lagrangian. One obtains reasonable predictive power if this low-energy constant is determined from a measurement of the magnetic transition radius. Such a measurement can be performed at the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR).

## Key claim

Using dispersion theory combined with next-to-leading-order (NLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory, the low-energy electromagnetic Sigma-to-Lambda transition form factors can be related to the pion vector form factor, but only if decuplet baryons are included as explicit degrees of freedom; the magnetic transition form factor depends strongly on one poorly determined low-energy constant, and predictive power is only obtained if that constant is fixed by a measurement of the magnetic transition radius.

## Method

The authors set up subtracted dispersion relations for the electric and magnetic transition form factors of the Sigma-to-Lambda hyperon, saturating the inelasticity by a two-pion intermediate state. The required bare four-point amplitudes for Sigma-bar-Lambda to two pions are computed from relativistic three-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory at NLO, including both octet-only and octet-plus-decuplet baryon contributions. Pion rescattering is incorporated via the Omnès function, using the experimentally known pion p-wave phase shift, and the resulting scattering amplitudes are then used in the dispersive integrals to obtain the form factors.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic transition form factor at the photon point is fixed to κ ≈ 1.98, extracted from the PDG value for the Σ⁰ → Λ γ decay width.
- The upper limit of available invariant masses for the Dalitz decay Σ⁰ → Λ e⁺ e⁻ is given by m_Σ⁰ − m_Λ ≈ 77 MeV.
- The magnetic moment μ is related to κ by μ = κ × (2 m_p)/(m_Λ + m_Σ) × e/(2 m_p) ≈ 1.61 × e/(2 m_p).
- The electric transition form factor vanishes at the photon point: G_E(0) = 0.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach previously applied to nucleon form factors (e.g., Hoferichter (2016), Mergell (1995)) to the hyperon sector, specifically the purely isovector Sigma-to-Lambda transition. It builds on a direct chiral perturbation theory calculation by Kubis (2000) and provides a framework that can be extended to other hyperon transitions, such as decuplet Sigma-to-Lambda and Delta-to-nucleon, and to elastic form factors of octet and decuplet hyperons. The results are directly relevant for planned measurements at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) via the Dalitz decay Σ⁰ → Λ e⁺ e⁻.
