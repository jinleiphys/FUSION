# A Note on the External-Field Method in QCD Sum Rules

> The external-field method has been used extensively in the QCD sum-rule approach to explore various hadron static properties. In the traditional formalism of this method, the transitions from the ground state hadron to excited states are not exponentially suppressed relative to the ground state term and thus contaminate the ground state hadron property to be extracted. In this paper, we suggest a modified formalism, in which the transition terms are exponentially suppressed relative to the ground state term. As such, the pole plus continuum spectral model, traditionally invoked in QCD sum-rule approach, can be adopted. Thus, this modified formalism has potential to improve the predictability and reliability of external-field sum-rule calculations, which is illustrated in an explicit example.

## Key claim

The traditional external-field QCD sum-rule formalism suffers from contamination because transitions from the ground state hadron to excited states are not exponentially suppressed relative to the ground state term after Borel transformation; the paper proposes a modified formalism using the combination \((M_0^2 - q^2) \Pi_1(q^2)\) instead of \(\Pi_1(q^2)\) alone, which exponentially suppresses those transition terms and allows the standard pole-plus-continuum spectral model to be adopted.

## Method

The author starts from the linear response term \(\Pi_1(q)\) of the hadron correlation function in an external field and multiplies it by \((M_0^2 - q^2)\) to obtain a modified correlation function \(\widetilde{\Pi}_1(M^2)\). After Borel transformation, the transition terms from ground to excited states acquire an exponential factor \(e^{-M_i^2/M^2}\) relative to the ground state term \(e^{-M_0^2/M^2}\), making them exponentially suppressed. The method is illustrated by applying both the traditional and modified formalisms to the proton matrix element of the isovector-scalar current, \(H \equiv \langle p|\overline{u}u - \overline{d}d |p\rangle/2 M_p\), using the same proton interpolating field and numerical optimization procedure as in Jin (1995).

## Key numbers

* Proton matrix element \(H\) extracted from the traditional formalism (Eq. sr-old): \(H \simeq 0.60\) (dimensionless, from sum rule with constant transition parameter \(\widetilde{A}\)).
* Proton matrix element \(H\) extracted from the new modified formalism (Eq. sr-new): \(H \simeq 0.97\) (dimensionless, from sum rule with no transition parameter).
* Proton matrix element \(H\) extracted from the traditional formalism with a complete phenomenological representation (Eq. sr-3): \(H \simeq 0.98\) (dimensionless, including Borel-mass-dependent transition term \(\widetilde{B}\)).
* Input parameters used: \(\widetilde{\lambda}_p^2 \simeq 1.85\) GeV\(^6\), \(s_0 \simeq 2.14\) GeV\(^2\), \(\chi = 2.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the external-field QCD sum-rule method originally developed by Ioffe and Smilga (1984) and addresses a known drawback identified in later studies by Ioffe (1995) and Jin (1995a): the transition terms from ground to excited states are not exponentially suppressed and their Borel-mass dependence can significantly affect extracted hadron properties. The modified formalism enables the use of the standard pole-plus-continuum spectral model, reducing the number of phenomenological parameters to be extracted. A similar procedure was proposed by Balitsky and Yung (1983) for three-point function calculations but had not been applied in the external-field method; the present work clarifies that transition contributions are not eliminated but are instead included in the continuum model, and that the continuum threshold should not be assumed identical to that in mass sum rules.
