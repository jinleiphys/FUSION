# Slow proton production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and the pion cloud in the nucleon

> The semi-inclusive cross section for producing slow protons in charged current deep inelastic (anti-) neutrino scattering on protons and neutrons is calculated as a function of the Bjorken $x$ and the proton momentum. The standard hadronization models based upon the colour neutralization mechanism appear to underestimate the rate of slow proton production on hydrogen. The presence of the virtual mesons (pions) in the nucleon leads to an additional mechanism for proton production, referred to as spectator process. It is found that at low proton momenta both mechanisms compete, whereas the spectator mechanism dominates at very small momenta, while the color neutralization mechanism dominates at momenta larger than 1-2 \, GeV$/c$. The results of the calculations are compared with the CERN bubble chamber (BEBC) data. The spectator model predicts a sharp increase of the semi-inclusive cross section at small $x$ due to the sea quarks in virtual mesons.

# Slow proton production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and the pion cloud in the nucleon

## Key claim

Standard hadronization models based on colour neutralization underestimate the rate of slow proton production in charged current deep inelastic (anti-)neutrino scattering on hydrogen; the presence of virtual pions in the nucleon provides an additional spectator mechanism that can account for part of the deficit, though even the combination of both mechanisms may still fail to fully describe the total rate from the CERN WA21 experiment (Guy 1989) at small Bjorken x and low invariant mass.

## Method

The authors calculate the semi-inclusive cross section for slow proton production in charged current (anti-)neutrino scattering on protons and neutrons by combining two mechanisms: (1) a standard colour neutralization (hadronization) model using quark-diquark fragmentation functions and the Lund string model, and (2) a spectator mechanism arising from the pion cloud in the nucleon, where the virtual W-boson scatters off a virtual pion and the associated baryon (nucleon or delta) emerges as a spectator. The spectator contribution is computed using splitting functions for the πN and πΔ Fock components of the nucleon, with exponential vertex form factors whose cut-off parameters (ΛπN = 1.10 GeV, ΛπΔ = 0.98 GeV) are determined from high-energy neutron and Δ production data and from reproducing the NMC Gottfried sum rule result (Amaudruz 1991). The results are compared with CERN BEBC bubble chamber data (Allen 1983; Guy 1989).

## Key numbers

- The spectator mechanism dominates at very small proton momenta, while the colour neutralization mechanism dominates at momenta larger than 1–2 GeV/c.
- For slow protons in the momentum interval 0.15–0.60 GeV/c, the Lund string model predicts a rate below the experimental data from Guy (1989); the spectator mechanism contributes additional strength but the combined total may still fall short.
- The cut-off parameters used in the exponential vertex form factors are ΛπN = 1.10 GeV and ΛπΔ = 0.98 GeV, determined from analysis of particle spectra for high-energy neutron and Δ production.
- The transverse momentum distribution in the fragmentation function is parametrized as a Gaussian with σ² = 0.3 (GeV/c)².
- The four-quark fragmentation contribution must be reduced by a factor of 0.5–0.7 at Q² = 4 (GeV/c)² to avoid double-counting with the pionic component of the sea.
- For the proton momentum distribution with cuts W > 3 GeV and x > 0.1 (Allen 1983), the spectator mechanism gives almost the total missing strength not accounted for by the Lund string model.

## Context

This work extends the pion cloud model previously applied to inclusive deep inelastic scattering to explain the flavour asymmetry of the light quark sea (Henley and Miller 1990; Kumano 1991; Szczurek and Speth 1993; Szczurek et al. 1993) and the violation of the Gottfried sum rule observed by NMC (Amaudruz 1991). It addresses the deficit of slow protons in standard hadronization models such as the quark-diquark model (Baldini et al. 1993) and the Lund string model (Ingelman 1992), which had been used to reproduce the WA21 data (Guy 1989) with a constant fragmentation function (Mankiewicz et al. 1992). The results enable future experimental tests of the spectator mechanism through measurements of charged pion asymmetries in backward production, where the spectator model predicts a large difference between π⁺ and π⁻ yields that could confirm the existence of virtual pions in the nucleon.
