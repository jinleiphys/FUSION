# Neutron stars and stellar mergers as a laboratory for dense QCD matter

> Neutron star observations, including direct mass and radius measurements as well as the analysis of gravitational wave signals emitted by stellar mergers, provide valuable and unique insights into the properties of strongly interacting matter at high densities. In this proceedings contribution, I review recent efforts to systematically constrain the equation of state (EoS) of dense nuclear and quark matter using a combination of ab initio particle and nuclear physics calculations and astrophysical data. In particular, I discuss the constraints that the gravitational wave observation GW170817 has placed on the EoS, and comment on the future prospects of improving the accuracy, to which this quantity is known.

## Key claim

The gravitational wave observation GW170817, combined with the two-solar-mass constraint, rules out both very soft and very stiff equations of state, effectively acting as a radius constraint on neutron stars; the results assume that all observed neutron stars are main sequence stars, not "twin stars" from a possible first-order phase transition.

## Method

The author reviews a systematic interpolation scheme that constructs families of neutron star matter equations of state (EoS) using piecewise polytropes (2-, 3-, and 4-part) that smoothly connect a low-density Chiral Effective Theory (CET) EoS to a high-density perturbative QCD (pQCD) EoS, enforcing thermodynamic consistency and subluminality. Over 200,000 such EoSs are generated and then filtered by requiring they support a maximum mass of at least two solar masses and by comparing the tidal deformability \(\Lambda(1.4M_\odot)\) predicted by each EoS to the limits from the LIGO/Virgo analysis of GW170817.

## Key numbers

* The radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star, before any astrophysical constraints, varies between 6.6 km and 14.7 km across the interpolated EoS family.
* The two-solar-mass constraint alone raises the lower bound on the radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star to 9.9 km (or 10.7 km using only tritropic interpolations).
* The LIGO/Virgo 90% credence upper limit on the tidal deformability of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) star is \(\Lambda(1.4M_\odot) < 800\); the 50% credence limit is \(\Lambda(1.4M_\odot) < 400\).
* A direct X-ray measurement gives the circumferential radius of a \(1.9 \pm 0.3 M_\odot\) neutron star as \(12.4 \pm 0.4\) km at a 68% credibility level (Nattila, 2017).

## Context

This proceedings paper reviews the model-independent EoS constraints developed in a series of works by Hebeler (2013), Kurkela (2014), and Annala (2017), extending earlier piecewise-polytrope extrapolations by incorporating the high-density pQCD limit. The work directly uses the first binary neutron star merger GW170817 to set tidal deformability limits, and it enables future studies to search for signatures of a first-order deconfinement phase transition or the existence of deconfined quark matter inside neutron star cores, especially as more precise mass and radius measurements from missions like NICER become available.
