# Longitudinal fluctuations of the fireball density in heavy-ion collisions

> We show that fluctuations of the fireball shape in the longitudinal direction generate nontrivial rapidity correlations that depend not only on the rapidity difference, y_{1} - y_{2}, but also on the rapidity sum, y_{1} + y_{2}. This is explicitly demonstrated in a simple wounded nucleon model, and the general case is also discussed. We show how to extract different components of the fluctuating fireball shape from the measured two-particle rapidity correlation function. The experimental possibility of studying the longitudinal initial conditions in heavy-ion and proton-proton collisions is emphasized.

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations of the fireball shape in the longitudinal direction generate two-particle rapidity correlations that depend not only on the rapidity difference \(y_{1}-y_{2}\) but also on the rapidity sum \(y_{1}+y_{2}\), which can distinguish these shape fluctuations from other correlation sources such as resonance decays.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate the effect in a simple wounded nucleon model, where the single-particle rapidity distribution from a single wounded nucleon is approximated as a linear function of rapidity, and the event-by-event asymmetry arises from fluctuations in the difference between left-moving and right-moving wounded nucleons (\(w_{L}-w_{R}\)). They then generalize the analysis by expanding the fluctuating single-particle rapidity density in Chebyshev polynomials \(T_i(y/Y)\), where each polynomial corresponds to a different component of the fireball shape (e.g., asymmetry, butterfly), and show how to extract the coefficients \(\langle a_i a_k \rangle\) from the measured two-particle correlation function using orthogonality relations.

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, \(\langle (w_{L}-w_{R})^{2} \rangle\) is comparable in magnitude to the total number of wounded nucleons \(\langle w_{L}+w_{R} \rangle\), making event-by-event rapidity fluctuations of order \(\sim 1/(w_L+w_R)^{1/2}\) relative to the average.
- For the asymmetric component at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the single-particle distribution in d+Au collisions is approximately linear for \(|y| < 2\), so \(Y \approx 2\) is a reasonable choice for the rapidity scale parameter.
- The asymmetric component in p+p collisions was found to be significantly stronger than in central Au+Au collisions (citing Bialas (2011)).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bialas (2011), who argued that long-range rapidity correlations can be interpreted in terms of fluctuating rapidity density of the fireball and found a significant asymmetric component in central Au+Au collisions using STAR data. The paper generalizes that approach to include higher-order shape components (such as the "butterfly" component suggested by RHIC measurements) and provides a systematic method using Chebyshev polynomials to extract these components from data. This enables detailed comparisons of longitudinal initial conditions between heavy-ion and proton-proton collisions, and can test models of particle production such as the wounded nucleon model and the CGC/Glasma approach.
