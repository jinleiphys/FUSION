# Effective pairing interactions with isospin density dependence

> We perform Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for semi-magic Calcium, Nickel, Tin and Lead isotopes and $N$=20, 28, 50 and 82 isotones using density-dependent pairing interactions recently derived from a microscopic nucleon-nucleon interaction. These interactions have an isovector component so that the pairing gaps in symmetric and neutron matter are reproduced. Our calculations well account for the experimental data for the neutron number dependence of binding energy, two neutrons separation energy, and odd-even mass staggering of these isotopes. This result suggests that by introducing the isovector term in the pairing interaction, one can construct a global effective pairing interaction which is applicable to nuclei in a wide range of the nuclear chart. It is also shown with the local density approximation (LDA) that the pairing field deduced from the pairing gaps in infinite matter reproduces qualitatively well the pairing field for finite nuclei obtained with the HFB method.

## Key claim

The paper shows that introducing an isovector (isospin-dependent) term in density-dependent pairing interactions, fitted to reproduce pairing gaps in both symmetric and neutron matter from microscopic nucleon-nucleon calculations, allows a global effective pairing interaction to describe finite nuclei across a wide range of the nuclear chart; however, the interaction fitted to the "screened" (medium-polarization-included) pairing gap (IS+IV Induced) produces systematically too weak pairing in finite nuclei, while the interaction fitted to the "bare" pairing gap (IS+IV Bare) agrees well with experimental data.

## Method

Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed for semi-magic Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes and N=20, 28, 50, and 82 isotones using density-dependent contact pairing interactions with an isovector component, whose parameters were adjusted to reproduce the pairing gaps in symmetric and neutron matter obtained from microscopic nucleon-nucleon interaction calculations (both bare and including medium polarization effects). The mean field was described by the SLy4 Skyrme force, and the pairing field was computed from the density-dependent contact interaction with a cutoff energy of 40 MeV, using finite-box boundary conditions (R_box=25 fm) to discretize continuum states. The results were compared to experimental binding energies, two-neutron separation energies, and odd-even mass staggering (OES).

## Key numbers

- The bare pairing gap maximum is located at neutron Fermi momentum k_Fn = 0.87 fm^{-1} with Δ_n = 3.1 MeV for both symmetric and neutron matter.
- The screened pairing gap maximum is at k_Fn = 0.60 fm^{-1} with Δ_n = 2.70 MeV for symmetric matter and at k_Fn = 0.83 fm^{-1} with Δ_n = 1.76 MeV for neutron matter.
- The cutoff energy used for the pairing interactions is E_c = 40 MeV.
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The IS+IV Bare interaction parameters are: η_s = 0.664, α_s = 0.522, η_n = 1.01, α_n = 0.525, η_2 = 0.0.
- The IS+IV Induced interaction parameters are: η_s = 1.80, α_s = 0.27, η_n = 1.61, α_n = 0.122, η_2 = 0.8.
- The IS Bare interaction parameters are: η_s = 0.664, α_s = 0.522, η_n = 0.664, α_n = 0.522, η_2 = 0.0.
- The alternative parameterization g_n^3 uses: η_1 = 1.01, α_1 = 0.525, η_2 = -0.058, α_2 = 0.559, with cutoff E_c = 40 MeV.
- The phenomenological isoscalar fit from Vogt (1984) is Δ_n^IS = 13.3/A^{1/2} MeV; the isovector fit is Δ_n^{IS+IV} = [7.2 - 44(1-2Z/A)^2]/A^{1/3} MeV.

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent pairing interactions proposed by Margueron, Sagawa, and Hagino (2007) that reproduce pairing gaps in uniform matter, applying them to finite nuclei for the first time. It builds on microscopic calculations of pairing gaps in symmetric and neutron matter by Cao et al. (2006), which showed that medium polarization effects act differently in neutron matter (reducing the gap) versus symmetric matter (enhancing the gap at low density). The results contradict the expectation that the medium-polarization-included (screened) pairing interaction from infinite matter would work well in finite nuclei; instead, the bare interaction fits experimental data better, suggesting that the medium polarization effects estimated in infinite matter give too weak pairing in finite nuclei. This enables construction of a global effective pairing interaction applicable across the nuclear chart, with the isovector component shown to be essential for reproducing isotopic trends in pairing gaps.
