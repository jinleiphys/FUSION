# Collective Excitations and Pairing Effects in Drip-Line Nuclei -- Continuum RPA in Coordinate-Space HFB --

> We discuss novel features of a new continuum RPA formulated in the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework. This continuum quasiparticle RPA takes into account both the one- and two-particle escaping channels. The theory is tested with numerical calculations for monopole, dipole and quadrupole excitations in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes near the drip-line. Effects of the particle-particle RPA correlation caused by the pairing interaction are discussed in detail, and importance of the selfconsistent treatment is emphasized.

# Collective Excitations and Pairing Effects in Drip-Line Nuclei, Continuum RPA in Coordinate-Space HFB

## Key claim

The paper presents a new continuum quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) formulated in the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework that, for the first time, consistently treats both one- and two-particle escaping channels and the dynamical pairing correlation in the particle-particle channel, but the numerical tests are performed using a Woods-Saxon potential and Skyrme-type residual interactions rather than a fully selfconsistent Hartree-Fock treatment for the particle-hole channel.

## Method

The authors formulate the linear response theory by expressing the unperturbed response function as a contour integral of products of the exact HFB Green function, which incorporates both discrete bound quasiparticle states and continuum states via outgoing-wave boundary conditions. The RPA linear response equation is then solved for the transition densities (normal density and pair densities) using residual interactions in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels, with the same density-dependent delta force used for the pairing field in the HFB ground state and the dynamical pairing correlation in the response equation. Numerical calculations are performed for monopole, dipole, and quadrupole excitations in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes \(^{18-24}\)O using a Woods-Saxon potential with Skyrme-type density-dependent delta forces for the particle-hole residual interaction and a density-dependent delta force for the pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- Average neutron pairing gaps: \(\left<\Delta\right> = 2.37, 2.83, 2.84, 2.89\) MeV for \(^{18-24}\)O, with zero gap for protons; pairing force strength \(V_0 = 520\) fm\(^{-3}\)MeV.
- Low-lying \(2^+\) state in \(^{24}\)O at excitation energy \(E = 3.6\) MeV, very close to the one- and two-neutron threshold energy \(E_{th,1n} = E_{th,2n} = 4.08\) MeV; its energy-weighted isoscalar sum is 2330 fm\(^4\)MeV with full pairing, versus 646 fm\(^4\)MeV without pairing.
- \(B(E2)\) values for the low-lying \(2^+\) state: 17, 18, 20, 18 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) for \(A = 18-24\).
- Energy-weighted E1 sum below 15 MeV: 7, 11, 16, 21% of the TRK sum-rule value for \(A = 18-24\).
- Energy-weighted isoscalar quadrupole sum for the low-lying \(2^+\) state: 730, 1220, 1680, 2330 fm\(^4\)MeV for \(A = 18-24\) with full pairing; without pairing these become 73, 132, 277, 646 fm\(^4\)MeV.

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA approach of Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) by incorporating pairing correlations within the coordinate-space HFB framework, going beyond previous continuum QRPA approaches that employed the BCS approximation (e.g., Platchkov et al. 1988, Kamerdzhiev et al. 1998, Hagino and Sagawa 2001) or that used discretized canonical bases (Engel et al. 1999). The formalism enables microscopic description of collective excitations in drip-line nuclei where both pairing and continuum coupling are essential, and the numerical results for oxygen isotopes demonstrate the importance of dynamical pairing correlations for low-lying quadrupole neutron vibrations and low-energy dipole strength (pygmy resonance), with implications for future fully selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-based calculations.
