# Spectral properties and spin alignment of $ϕ$ meson in QCD Matter

> As the spin alignment of a vector meson is predicted to be correlated with its spectral properties, we study the spectral properties of the $ϕ$ meson using two microscopic Lagrangians based either on chiral effective field theory or the quark-meson model. We calculate the self-energies and spectral functions of the $ϕ$ meson for these two Lagrangians at the one-loop level within the Matsubara formalism of finite-temperature quantum field theory, employing various parameters to represent different physical scenarios. Using these spectral functions, transport coefficients related to the spin alignment and tensor polarization are obtained, connecting the spin alignment of the $ϕ$ meson to hydrodynamic gradients. Using the standard freeze-out picture within the relativistic hydrodynamic model, we explore how the possible pattern of the spin alignment can be generated using these microscopically calculated spectral functions. We discover that, with certain assumptions, these simple microscopic model-based calculations can produce sizable spin alignments with a sign-flipping behavior in their $p_T$ and centrality dependence, similar to those observed in experiments.

## Key claim
Using two simple one-loop microscopic models (hadronic chiral effective field theory and a quark-meson model), the authors show that the calculated spectral functions of the phi meson can produce sizable spin alignments with a sign-flipping behavior in their transverse momentum and centrality dependence, similar to experimental observations, but only under certain assumptions about the freeze-out conditions.

## Method
The authors calculated the self-energies and spectral functions of the phi meson at the one-loop level within the Matsubara formalism of finite-temperature quantum field theory, using two distinct Lagrangians: one from SU(3) chiral perturbation theory with kaon loops (hadronic phase) and one from a quark-meson model with strange quark loops (partonic phase). From these spectral functions, they derived transport coefficients (alpha_sh, alpha_sp, alpha_0) that connect the spin alignment of the phi meson to hydrodynamic gradients, and then applied a standard freeze-out picture within a relativistic hydrodynamic model to generate the spin alignment patterns.

## Key numbers
* The coupling constant for the hadronic model is chosen as g_phi = 4.55, which matches the phi to K Kbar branching ratio.
* The vacuum kaon mass used is m_K = 0.494 GeV, with an alternative value of m_K = 0.47 GeV to illustrate in-medium mass shift effects.
* The phi meson mass is 1.02 GeV, and with vacuum kaon mass the width is only around 4 MeV, close to the vacuum width.
* The in-medium mass shift of the phi meson is a few MeV, and the mass shift of the kaon (at rest) is of the order of 10 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework connecting vector meson spin alignment to spectral properties, as proposed in Li (2022) and Li (2025), by providing the first microscopic calculations using pure hadronic degrees of freedom for phi meson spin alignment. It builds on earlier studies of the phi meson spectral function using thermal field theory by Haglin (1994) and Song (1996), and the many-body approach by Rapp (2000) and van Hees (2007). The results aim to help resolve the "spin-sign puzzle" observed in experiments by STAR and ALICE, where measured spin alignments are orders of magnitude larger than predictions from earlier theories by Becattini (2007) and Xia (2020).
