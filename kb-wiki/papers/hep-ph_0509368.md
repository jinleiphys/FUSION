# A QCD analysis of \bar p N -> gamma^* pi and \bar p N -> gamma^* gamma. Where is the pion in the proton ?

> We study the scaling regime of nucleon-anti-nucleon annihilation into a deeply virtual photon and a photon or meson, \bar p N -> gamma ^* pi, \bar p N -> gamma ^* gamma, in the forward direction. The leading twist amplitude factorizes into an antiproton distribution amplitude, a short-distance matrix element and a long-distance dominated transition distribution amplitude (TDA) which describes the nucleon to meson or photon transition. The impact representation of this TDA maps out the transverse locations of the small size core and the meson or photon cloud inside the proton.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the leading-twist amplitude for nucleon-antinucleon annihilation into a deeply virtual photon and a pion or photon factorizes into an antiproton distribution amplitude, a short-distance hard scattering amplitude, and a long-distance transition distribution amplitude (TDA), but this factorization is argued by generalizing existing proofs from meson-meson and meson-baryon cases rather than proven directly for the baryon-antibaryon case.

## Method

The authors generalize the factorization proof for deep exclusive meson electroproduction on a meson (gamma* M1 -> M2 M3) in the forward direction, first to its time-reversed process M2 M1 -> gamma* M3, then to meson-meson annihilation M2 M1 -> gamma* gamma using the meson-photon analogy from photon structure functions, and finally to the baryon case involving three-quark exchanges based on the QCD analysis of baryon form factors. They define the eight leading-twist TDAs for the proton-to-pion transition in terms of helicity amplitudes and derive their soft-pion limit relations to the standard nucleon distribution amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, used in the normalization of the TDA parametrization.
- The nucleon wave function constant f_N is estimated through QCD sum rules to be of order 5.3 x 10^{-3} GeV^2, used in the TDA normalization.
- The skewedness variable xi = -Delta^+ / (2P^+) describes the loss of plus-momentum of the incident hadron in the proton-to-meson transition, with quark momentum fractions x_i supported within [-1+xi, 1+xi] and satisfying sum_i x_i = 2 xi.

## Context

This work extends the factorization approach of deep virtual Compton scattering and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) to antiproton-proton annihilation channels, building on prior factorization proofs by Collins, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1997) for exclusive meson electroproduction. The formalism enables future experiments at intense antiproton facilities (such as PAX) to probe the transverse spatial distribution of the pion cloud inside the proton, and also applies to related spacelike processes such as backward virtual Compton scattering and backward meson electroproduction where data exist at moderate Q^2.
