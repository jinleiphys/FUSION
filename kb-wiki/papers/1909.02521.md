# Patterns of flavour symmetry breaking in hadron matrix elements involving u, d and s quarks

> By considering a flavour expansion about the SU(3)-flavour symmetric point, we investigate how flavour-blindness constrains octet baryon matrix elements after SU(3) is broken by the mass difference between quarks. Similarly to hadron masses we find the expansions to be constrained along a mass trajectory where the singlet quark mass is held constant, which provides invaluable insight into the mechanism of flavour symmetry breaking and proves beneficial for extrapolations to the physical point. Expansions are given up to third order in the expansion parameters. Considering higher orders would give no further constraints on the expansion parameters. The relation of the expansion coefficients to the quark-line-connected and quark-line disconnected terms in the 3-point correlation functions is also given. As we consider Wilson clover-like fermions, the addition of improvement coefficients is also discussed and shown to be included in the formalism developed here. As an example of the method we investigate this numerically via a lattice calculation of the flavour-conserving matrix elements of the vector first class form factors.

## Key claim

By expanding octet baryon matrix elements about the SU(3)-flavour symmetric point along a trajectory where the average bare quark mass is held constant, the expansions are constrained such that flavour-singlet quantities become largely constant, providing a complementary approach to chiral perturbation theory for extrapolating lattice QCD results to the physical point.

## Method

The authors perform a group-theoretic classification of three-index tensors for the product 8 ⊗ 8 ⊗ 8 to determine the allowed quark-mass dependence of octet baryon matrix elements, expanding in polynomials of δm_q = m_q − m̄ up to third order. They then compute the flavour-conserving vector current form factors F₁ and F₂ for octet baryons using n_f = 2+1 flavour Wilson clover fermions on the lattice, extracting matrix elements from ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The expansion is constrained along a trajectory where the singlet quark mass m̄ = (m_u + m_d + m_s)/3 is held constant, reducing the number of independent mass polynomials.
- Expansions are given up to third order in δm_q; higher orders give no further constraints on the expansion parameters.
- For the n_f = 2+1 case with m_u = m_d ≡ m_l, the identity 2δm_l + δm_s = 0 reduces the expansion to a single variable δm_l.
- At the SU(3) symmetric point, second class form factors (F₃, G₂, h₄) vanish for diagonal currents and for transitions within isospin multiplets.

## Context

This work extends the flavour expansion programme previously applied to meson and baryon masses by Bietenholz et al. (2010, 2011) to include matrix elements, and is complementary to chiral perturbation theory approaches. The method enables systematic extrapolations to the physical point for lattice QCD calculations of semileptonic decay form factors (relevant for |V_us| determination) and generalised form factors for the Electron Ion Collider programme described by Accardi et al. (2012).
