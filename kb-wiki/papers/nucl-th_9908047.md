# Enhancement of parity violating mixing in halo nuclei and the problem of neutron weak parity nonconserving potential constant

> We consider the Parity Nonconserving (PNC) mixing in the ground state of exotic (halo) nuclei caused by the PNC weak interaction between outer neutron and nucleons within nuclear interior. For the nucleus $^{11}Be$ as an example of typical nucleus with neutron halo, we use analytical model for the external neutron wave functions to estimate the scale of the PNC mixing. The amplitude of the PNC mixing in halo state is found to be an order of magnitude bigger than that of typical PNC mixing between the ``normal'' nuclear states in nearby nuclei. The enhanced PNC mixing in halo cloud is proportional to the neutron weak PNC potential constant $g^W_n$ only.

# Enhancement of parity violating mixing in halo nuclei and the problem of neutron weak parity nonconserving potential constant

## Key claim

The parity nonconserving (PNC) mixing amplitude in the ground state of the halo nucleus ¹¹Be is found to be approximately 0.6 × 10⁻⁶ × gⁿ_W, which is about one order of magnitude larger than typical PNC mixing in normal nuclei (≈ 0.7 × 10⁻⁷), and this enhanced mixing is proportional only to the neutron weak PNC potential constant gⁿ_W, not the proton constant.

## Method

The authors constructed an analytical single-particle model for the halo neutron wave functions in ¹¹Be, using a 2s₁/₂ ground state and a 1p₁/₂ excited state with experimentally known energy separation of 0.32 MeV. They calculated the matrix element of the effective one-body weak PNC potential between these states, using a Gaussian core density fitted to Hartree-Fock results from Sagawa, and evaluated the resulting parity mixing coefficient via first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- PNC mixing coefficient in ¹¹Be halo ground state: |f^{HALO}_{sp}| ≈ 0.6 × 10⁻⁶ × gⁿ_W (for gⁿ_W ≈ 1, this is 0.6 × 10⁻⁶)
- PNC mixing coefficient for normal nuclear states in the same mass region (A ≈ 11): |f^{normal}_{sp}| ≈ 0.7 × 10⁻⁷ × gⁿ_W
- Enhancement factor of halo PNC mixing over normal PNC mixing: factor ≈ 9
- Energy separation between 2s₁/₂ ground state and 1p₁/₂ excited state in ¹¹Be: |ΔE_{HALO}| = 0.32 MeV
- Matrix element of weak potential between halo states: ⟨1p|W_sp|2s⟩_{HALO} = -i 0.2 eV × gⁿ_W
- Best-fit halo wave function parameters: r₀ = 1.45 fm (2s state), r₁ = 1.80 fm (1p state)
- Halo root-mean-square radii from model: √⟨r²_{2s}⟩ = 5.9 fm, √⟨r²_{1p}⟩ = 4.9 fm
- Core density parameters: ρ₀ = 0.2 fm⁻³, R_c = 2 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of parity violation from normal nuclei to exotic halo nuclei, motivated by the fact that the neutron weak constant gⁿ_W remains poorly constrained (gⁿ_W = 1 ± 1.5 from DDH analysis) compared to the proton constant gⁿ_W = 4.5 ± 2. Previous work by Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1980) established the DDH Hamiltonian for PNC nucleon-nucleon interactions, and Adelberger and collaborators reviewed PNC effects in normal nuclei. The authors use halo structure calculations by Sagawa and the node restoration analysis from scattering data. This result enables future experimental probes of the neutron weak constant using halo nuclei, and suggests that anapole moment measurements in halo systems could provide enhanced sensitivity due to the extended halo cloud.
