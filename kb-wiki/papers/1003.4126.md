# Separable form of low-momentum realistic NN interaction

> The low-momentum interaction $V_{\text{low-k}}$ derived from realistic models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is presented in a separable form. This separable force is supported by a contact interaction in order to achieve the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter. Bulk properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei are investigated for the separable form of $V_{\text{low-k}}$ and two different parameterizations of the contact term. The accuracy of the separable force in Hartree-Fock calculations with respect to the original interaction $V_{\text{low-k}}$ is discussed. For a cutoff parameter $Λ$ of 2 fm$^{-1}$ a representation by a rank 2 separable force yields a sufficient accuracy, while higher ranks are required for larger cut-off parameters. The resulting separable force is parameterized in a simple way to allow for an easy application in other nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The low-momentum NN interaction \(V_{\text{low-k}}\) can be accurately represented by a low-rank separable force, but only for a cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) of 2 fm\(^{-1}\) does a rank-2 separable force yield sufficient accuracy; larger cutoffs require higher ranks, and the separable force must be supplemented by a density-dependent contact interaction to achieve nuclear matter saturation.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the \(V_{\text{low-k}}\) matrix in momentum space for each partial wave channel, retaining only the largest eigenvalues to construct a low-rank separable approximation \(V^{[n]}_{\text{low-k}}\). They then perform Hartree-Fock calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei using this separable form, and supplement it with a Skyrme-like contact interaction (with parameters \(t_0\), \(t_3\), \(x_3\)) fitted to reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- For \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\), the \(^1S_0\) channel yields only 11 nonzero eigenvalues from a \(20\times20\) matrix; a rank-2 separable approximation achieves fast convergence to zero deviation.
- At \(\Lambda = 3\) fm\(^{-1}\), good accuracy in the \(^1S_0\) channel requires starting from rank \(n=5\).
- The separable form \(V^{[2,3]}_{\text{low-k}}\) deviates from the original \(V_{\text{low-k}}\) by less than \(1\%\) at saturation density \(\rho_0\) and \(1.7\%\) at density \(2\rho_0\) in symmetric nuclear matter.
- The fitted separable potential \(V^{[2,3]}_{\text{fit}}\) + CT1 gives binding energy per nucleon E/A = -16.1 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The fitted separable potential \(V^{[2,3]}_{\text{fit}}\) + CT2 gives E/A = -16.0 MeV at \(\rho_0 = 0.156\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Compressibility modulus \(K\) ranges from 240.5 to 258 MeV for the three contact interaction parameter sets (CT, CT1, CT2).

## Context

This work extends the \(V_{\text{low-k}}\) approach of Bogner (2001) and others by providing a separable parameterization that simplifies many-body calculations, similar in spirit to the separable representations used by Baldo (1988) and Tian (2009). The separable form enables Hartree-Fock calculations for large systems such as Wigner-Seitz cells in neutron star crusts, which were previously impractical with the nonlocal \(V_{\text{low-k}}\) matrix. The paper also addresses the known problem that \(V_{\text{low-k}}\) alone cannot reproduce nuclear matter saturation (Kuckei 2003, Bogner 2005), and provides two contact-term parameterizations (CT1 and CT2) that restore saturation while maintaining a soft equation of state consistent with heavy-ion data from Stoicea (2004) and Sturm (2001).
