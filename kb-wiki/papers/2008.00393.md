# Comment on "Breakdown of the tensor component in the Skyrme energy density functional''

> In a recent paper [Phys. Rev. C 101, 014305 (2020)], Dong and Shang claim that the Skyrme original tensor interaction is invalid. Their conclusion is based on the misconception that the Fourier transform of tensor interaction is difficult or even impossible, so that the Skrme-type tensor interaction was introduced in an unreasonable way. We disagree on their claim. In this note, we show that one can easily get the Skyrme force in momentum space by Fourier transformation if one starts from a general central, spin-orbit or tensor interaction with a radial dependence.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Skyrme original tensor interaction is valid, contrary to the claim by Dong and Shang (2020) that it is invalid due to difficulties with Fourier transformation. They show that the Fourier transform of a general tensor interaction with a radial dependence can be performed straightforwardly, producing the Skyrme-type tensor interaction as the lowest-order expansion of a finite-range tensor force.

## Method

The authors perform the Fourier transform of a general central, spin-orbit, or tensor interaction with a radial dependence, using the multipole expansion of a plane wave in terms of spherical Bessel functions and spherical harmonics. For the tensor interaction specifically, they start from the coordinate-space tensor operator \(S_{12}(\mathbf{r})\) expressed with spherical harmonics \(Y_{2\mu}\), perform the Fourier transform, and expand the resulting spherical Bessel function \(j_2(qr)\) to lowest order in \(q^2\). This yields the momentum-space tensor operator \(S_{12}(\mathbf{q})\) that matches the standard Skyrme tensor form, including both the triplet-even (T-term) and triplet-odd (U-term) contributions.

## Key numbers

* The spherical Bessel function \(j_2(qr)\) is proportional to \(q^2\) in the lowest order of expansion, with \(j_2(qr) \sim (qr)^2/5!!\).
* The resulting momentum-space tensor interaction contains the factor \(-\frac{4\pi}{15}\sqrt{\frac{8\pi}{3}}\) times the angular-momentum-coupled operator \([(\sigma_1\cdot\sigma_2)^{(2)}\times q^2Y_2(\hat q)]^{(0)}\) and the radial integral \(\int r^6 f_T(r) dr\).

## Context

This comment directly contradicts the claim of Dong and Shang (2020) that the Skyrme tensor interaction is invalid. It builds on the original work by Skyrme (1956, 1959) and Bell (1959), who established that effective potentials for finite nuclei must depend on both momenta and coordinates. The authors note that their demonstration validates the approach used in approximately 30-40 papers on the Skyrme tensor force reviewed by Sagawa and Colo (2014), and that if the Skyrme tensor were invalid, it would impact roughly \(10^3\) papers using Skyrme forces in general.
