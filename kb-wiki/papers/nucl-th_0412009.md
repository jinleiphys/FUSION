# Magnetic Properties of Precession Modes Built on High-K Multi-quasiparticle States in ^{178}W

> We present an example that shows that the random phase approximation performed on high-K multi-quasiparticle configurations leads to a rotor picture by calculating excitation energies and magnetic properties of ^{178}W. Then we deduce the effective g_R of the high-K rotors and compare it with that of the low-K one.

## Key claim

The random phase approximation (RPA) performed on high-K multi-quasiparticle configurations in 178W yields a rotor picture, and the effective g_R of these high-K rotors can be deduced and compared with that of the low-K rotor; the agreement is semi-quantitative and shows that high-K and low-K rotors are similar unless the shape-driving π h9/2 orbital is included.

## Method

The authors performed RPA calculations on 11 high-K multi-quasiparticle configurations (2QP through 6QP) in 178W using a Nilsson Hamiltonian with pairing and doubly-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. They calculated excitation energies of the first rotational band members (I=K+1 → K) and B(M1) transition strengths, then extracted |g_K - g_R| by equating the RPA result with the rotor model expression. The intrinsic g-factor of each high-K configuration was calculated at the mean-field level, and g_R was deduced by combining these two quantities using the rotor model relation.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation ε2 = 0.235, chosen to reproduce Q0 = 7.0 eb assumed in experimental analyses
- Pairing gaps: 0.5 MeV for 2QP configurations and 0.01 MeV for 4QP and 6QP configurations, for both neutrons and protons
- Spin g-factor: g_s^(eff) = 0.7 g_s^(free)
- Average deduced g_R for high-K rotors: approximately 0.29
- Calculated g values for the ground state band at 2+ (0.218), 4+ (0.216), and 6+ (0.214) states, with pairing gaps Δn = 0.883 MeV and Δp = 1.026 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical discussion by Andersson et al. (1976) that RPA on high-K configurations leads to a rotor picture via E2 properties, by now including M1 properties and magnetic moments. The method builds on the rotor model formalism of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the RPA framework for precession phonons developed by Marshalek (1977). The results enable future detailed investigations of g_R variations across different high-K configurations and their comparison with low-K rotors, particularly regarding the influence of the π h9/2 orbital.
