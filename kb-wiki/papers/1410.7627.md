# Microscopic positive-energy potential based on Gogny interaction

> We present nucleon elastic scattering calculation based on Green's function formalism in the Random-Phase Approximation. For the first time, the Gogny effective interaction is used consistently throughout the whole calculation to account for the complex, non-local and energy-dependent optical potential. Effects of intermediate single-particle resonances are included and found to play a crucial role in the account for measured reaction cross section. Double counting of the particle-hole second-order contribution is carefully addressed. The resulting integro-differential Schrödinger equation for the scattering process is solved without localization procedures. The method is applied to neutron and proton elastic scattering from $^{40}$Ca. A successful account for differential and integral cross sections, including analyzing powers, is obtained for incident energies up to 30 MeV. Discrepancies at higher energies are related to much too high volume integral of the real potential for large partial waves. Moreover, this works opens the way for future effective interactions suitable simultaneously for both nuclear structure and reaction.

## Key claim

For the first time, the Gogny effective interaction is used consistently throughout the entire calculation of a nucleon-nucleus optical potential within the Green's function formalism and Random-Phase Approximation, yielding successful accounts for elastic scattering observables on $^{40}$Ca up to 30 MeV incident energy, but with discrepancies at higher energies attributed to the volume integral of the real potential for large partial waves.

## Method

The authors compute the optical potential as the sum of a Hartree-Fock (HF) term and a polarization term from Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) target excitations, using the D1S Gogny interaction throughout. The uncorrelated particle-hole second-order contribution is subtracted to avoid double counting, and the resulting non-local, energy-dependent, complex potential is used to solve the integro-differential Schrödinger equation without localization. The method is applied to neutron and proton elastic scattering from $^{40}$Ca, with RPA excited states up to spin $J=8$ included and phenomenological widths assigned to each RPA state to smooth the potential.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies up to 30 MeV: successful account for differential and integral cross sections and analyzing powers for neutron and proton elastic scattering from $^{40}$Ca.
- Beyond 30 MeV: backward-angle cross sections are overestimated.
- Phenomenological widths $\Gamma(E_N)$ assigned to RPA states: 2 MeV at excitation energy 20 MeV, 5 MeV at 50 MeV, 15 MeV at 100 MeV, and 50 MeV at 200 MeV.
- For neutron scattering from $^{40}$Ca, the HF potential's volume integral matches the Perey-Buck non-local potential up to about the twelfth partial wave (approximately 17 MeV), then saturates.
- At 9.91 MeV neutron scattering from $^{40}$Ca, the two approximations for $\Delta V$ (full subtraction vs. $\text{Im}[V^{RPA}/2]$) yield very similar shapes for the imaginary part of the potential across partial waves.

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Structure Method (NSM) for scattering, originally developed by Vinh Mau (1970) and others, by applying it with the Gogny effective interaction for the first time. It contrasts with approaches using Skyrme interactions, such as the continuum particle-vibration coupling by Mizuyama (2012), and with Self-Consistent Green's Function methods applied to $^{16}$O by Barbieri (2005) and Dussan (2011). The results open the way for future effective interactions suitable simultaneously for nuclear structure and reactions, and can be extended to deformed nuclei using Quasi-particle RPA.
