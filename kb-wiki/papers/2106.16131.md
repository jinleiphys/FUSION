# Impact of three-nucleon forces on gravitational wave emission from neutron stars

> The detection of gravitational radiation, emitted in the aftermath of the excitation of neutron star quasi-normal modes, has the potential to provide unprecedented access to the properties of matter in the star interior, and shed new light on the dynamics of nuclear interactions at microscopic level. Of great importance, in this context, will be the sensitivity to themodelling of three-nucleon interactions, which are known to play a critical role in the high-density regime. We report the results of a calculation of the frequencies and damping times of the fundamental mode, carried out using the equation of state of Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall as a baseline, and varying the strength of the isoscalar repulsive term the Urbana IX potential within a range consistent with multimessenger astrophysical observations. The results of our analysis indicate that repulsive three-nucleon interactions strongly affect the stiffness of the equation of state, which in turn determines the pattern of the gravitational radiation frequencies, largely independent of the mass of the source. The observational implications are also discussed.

# Impact of three-nucleon forces on gravitational wave emission from neutron stars

## Key claim

The frequencies of neutron star fundamental oscillation modes are significantly affected by the strength of repulsive three-nucleon interactions, with the effect being largely independent of the stellar mass; however, the damping times of these modes are largely independent of the three-nucleon force strength, limiting the observational discriminability to frequency measurements alone.

## Method

The authors used the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) equation of state as a baseline, modifying the strength of the repulsive term in the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential through a parameter α (defined by V^R → α V^R). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star equilibrium configurations, then computed the frequencies and damping times of the fundamental (f) oscillation mode by solving the linearized Einstein equations coupled with hydrodynamics for non-radial perturbations, imposing outgoing gravitational wave boundary conditions via the Zerilli equation.

## Key numbers

- At M = 1.4 M⊙, increasing α from 1.0 to 1.6 leads to approximately 10% increase of the star radius
- At M = 1.4 M⊙, the f-mode frequency decreases from 2.1 kHz (α = 0.8) to 1.68 kHz (α = 1.8), a decrease of approximately 20%
- At M = 2.0 M⊙, the f-mode frequency decreases from 2.42 kHz (α = 0.8) to 1.92 kHz (α = 1.8), also approximately 20%
- The equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter is ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The mass density at nuclear saturation is ε₀ = 2.67 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³
- For α = 1, causality violation (v_s > 1) in β-stable matter occurs at energy density ε ≳ 6.5 ε₀; for larger α, this threshold drops to ε ∼ 4 ε₀

## Context

This work extends the pioneering study of Ref. MSB2021, which constrained the α parameter using multimessenger data including the GW170817 gravitational wave event, NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, and the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620. The present study is complementary, focusing specifically on gravitational wave signatures from neutron star oscillations rather than static properties like mass-radius relations or tidal deformabilities. The results enable future gravitational wave detectors to potentially constrain three-nucleon forces through detection of f-mode frequencies from supernovae or binary merger events, with the authors noting that dynamical tides in inspiraling binaries may offer the most promising observational scenario.
