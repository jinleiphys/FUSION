# Ground state of medium-heavy doubly-closed shell nuclei in correlated basis function theory

> The correlated basis function theory is applied to the study of medium-heavy doubly closed shell nuclei with different wave functions for protons and neutrons and in the jj coupling scheme. State dependent correlations including tensor correlations are used. Realistic two-body interactions of Argonne and Urbana type, together with three-body interactions have been used to calculate ground state energies and density distributions of the 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb nuclei.

## Key claim

The correlated basis function (CBF) theory with Fermi Hypernetted Chain/Single Operator Chain (FHNC/SOC) resummation is extended to treat medium-heavy doubly closed shell nuclei with different proton and neutron wave functions in the jj coupling scheme, but the calculations keep the single-particle wave functions fixed and vary only the correlation functions, which the authors estimate places their results only a few percent above a full variational minimum.

## Method

The authors apply the FHNC/SOC formalism to evaluate the ground-state energy expectation value of a Hamiltonian containing realistic two-body interactions (Argonne v'8 or Urbana v14, truncated to the first eight operator channels) plus three-body interactions (Urbana IX or Urbana VII). The many-body wave function is a correlated product of a Slater determinant (built from Woods-Saxon single-particle states with spin-orbit splitting, different for protons and neutrons) and a state-dependent two-body correlation operator including central, spin, and tensor terms. The cluster expansion is resummed via FHNC techniques with the SOC approximation for operator chains, and the energy is minimized by varying the healing distances of the central and tensor correlation channels.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon for the five nuclei with Argonne v'8 + Urbana IX: 12C (barely bound), 16O (~5.5 MeV), 40Ca (~6.5 MeV), 48Ca (~6.5 MeV), 208Pb (~5.5 MeV), all underbinding experiment by about 3-4 MeV per nucleon.
- Kinetic energy per nucleon saturates around 40 MeV for 40Ca and heavier nuclei.
- The scalar-only (Jastrow) FHNC calculations produce density sum rules deviating by few parts per thousand from unity; inclusion of state-dependent correlations increases deviations up to a maximum of 5.5%.
- Healing distances for central correlations (d_c) are the same for both interactions across all nuclei except 12C; tensor healing distances (d_t) for v14+UVII are slightly larger than for v'8+UIX.
- Coulomb energy for 40Ca is 78.40 MeV total, for 48Ca is 75.36 MeV total (3.8% difference due to structure).
- Differences in total energies between the two interaction sets range from 5% (16O) to 18% (208Pb).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CBF/FHNC calculations from spin- and isospin-saturated systems in ls coupling (Co' et al., 1992, 1994; Arias de Saavedra et al., 1996) to realistic jj coupling with distinct proton and neutron wave functions, building on the FHNC/SOC treatment of tensor and spin-orbit forces (Fabrocini et al., 1998, 2000). It contrasts with Quantum Monte Carlo methods (Pudliner et al., 1995, 1997; Pieper et al., 2005), which are exact but limited to A ≤ 12, and with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Schulze et al., 1991; Dickhoff et al., 2004), no-core shell model (Navratil et al., 2000; Forssen et al., 2005), coupled cluster (Heisenberg et al., 1999; Mihaila et al., 1999; Dean et al., 2005), and Unitary Correlation Operator Method (Neff et al., 2003; Roth et al., 2004) approaches applied to medium and heavy nuclei. The results enable systematic variational studies of ground-state properties of doubly magic nuclei up to 208Pb with realistic interactions, though the persistent underbinding of 3-4 MeV per nucleon and the anomalous behavior of 12C indicate missing physics (possibly soft deformations) that future work must address.
