# On the structure of the X(1835) baryonium

> The measurement by the BES collaboration of J/psi -> gamma p pbar decays indicates an enhancement at the p-pbar threshold. In another experiment BES finds a peak in the invariant mass of pi-mesons produced in the possibly related decay J/psi -> gamma pi+ pi- eta'. Using a semi-phenomenological potential model which describes all the N-Nbar scattering data, we show that the explanation of both effects may be given by a broad quasi-bound state in the spin and isospin singlet S wave. The structure of the observed peak is due to an interference of this quasi-bound state with a background amplitude and depends on the annihilation mechanism.

## Key claim

The X(1835) peak observed by BES in the decay \(J/\psi \rightarrow \gamma \pi^+ \pi^- \eta'\) can be explained not as a new baryon state but as an interference effect between a broad quasi-bound \(N\bar{N}\) state in the spin and isospin singlet \(S\)-wave (the \(N\bar{N}_S(1870)\)) and a background amplitude, with the exact shape depending on the annihilation mechanism.

## Method

The authors used the semi-phenomenological Paris potential model, which describes all \(N\bar{N}\) scattering data, to calculate the subthreshold \(N\bar{N}\) scattering amplitude in the \(^{11}S_0\) partial wave. They then constructed a transition amplitude for the decay \(J/\psi \rightarrow \gamma \pi^+ \pi^- \eta'\) by assuming an intermediate \(N\bar{N}\) state, using a source function for pair formation, a Gaussian annihilation amplitude for the meson production, and the full Green's function of the \(N\bar{N}\) system. The spectral function for the X(1835) was computed from the squared transition amplitude multiplied by the four-body phase space.

## Key numbers

- The Paris potential generates a quasi-bound state in the \(^{11}S_0\) wave with a width of \(\Gamma = 52\) MeV and a binding energy of \(4.8\) MeV below the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold, named \(N\bar{N}_S(1870)\).
- The pole of this bound state in the complex energy plane is at Re \(E = 1871.7\) MeV.
- The best description of the BES data for the X(1835) shape is obtained with the annihilation range parameter \(r_f \approx 0.4 - 0.5\) fm.
- The formation amplitude range parameter was fixed at \(r_i = 0.55\) fm from previous work on the final-state interactions above threshold.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dedonder et al. (2005) which attributed the threshold enhancement in \(J/\psi \rightarrow \gamma p\bar{p}\) to the same quasi-bound state. It contradicts the interpretation of the BES collaboration that X(1835) is a new baryon state, and also differs from the view of the Julich group (2006) who questioned the X(1835) interpretation but did not attribute it to the same mechanism. The results enable future experimental tests at the FAIR facility with the PANDA detector or at the CERN AD ring with the PAX apparatus, where reactions such as \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \pi^+ \pi^- X\) or \(\bar{p}p \rightarrow \gamma X(1835)\) could confirm the link between the \(p\bar{p}\) system and the X(1835).
