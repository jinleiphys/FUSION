# The Role of Ground State Correlations in the Single-Particle Strength of Odd Nuclei with Pairing

> A method based on the consistent use of the Green function formalism has been developed to calculate the distribution of the single-particle strength in odd nuclei with pairing. The method takes into account the quasiparticle-phonon interaction, ground state correlations and a "refinement" of phenomenological single-particle energies and pairing gap values from the quasiparticle-phonon interaction under consideration. The calculations for 121Sn and 119Sn that were performed in the quasiparticle$\otimes$phonon approximation, have shown a reasonable agreement with experiment. The ground state correlations play a noticeable role and mostly improve the agreement with experiment or shift the results to the right direction.

## Key claim

The authors develop a Green function formalism that consistently includes quasiparticle-phonon interaction, ground state correlations, and a "refinement" procedure for phenomenological single-particle energies and pairing gaps; they find that ground state correlations play a noticeable role and mostly improve agreement with experiment or shift results in the right direction, but the method is limited to the quasiparticle-phonon approximation (g^2 approximation) and the refinement procedure is phenomenological rather than fully self-consistent.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the normal and anomalous Green functions in a superfluid nucleus, using mass operators that include the quasiparticle-phonon interaction in the g^2 approximation (1qp + 1qp-phonon configurations). They extract phenomenological single-particle energies and pairing gaps from experimental excitation energies of 119Sn and 121Sn, then "refine" these quantities by solving nonlinear equations that remove the contribution of the quasiparticle-phonon interaction to avoid double counting. The resulting refined single-particle energies and gaps are used as input to solve the secular equation and compute spectroscopic factors for the odd-mass tin isotopes.

## Key numbers

- For 120Sn, the mean value of the quasiparticle-phonon pairing contribution (gamma_bar) is 32%, calculated over all 8 levels under consideration.
- For the 2d5/2 level in 120Sn, the refined single-particle energy changes from -2.67 MeV to -3.69 MeV (21 phonons) or -3.41 MeV (3 phonons), and the gap changes from 1.35 MeV to -0.23 MeV (21 phonons), giving gamma = 117%.
- For the 1g7/2 level in 120Sn, the refined single-particle energy changes from -1.36 MeV to -3.27 MeV (21 phonons) or -2.54 MeV (3 phonons), and the gap changes from 1.58 MeV to 0.76 MeV (21 phonons), giving gamma = 52%.
- For the 2d3/2 level in 120Sn, gamma = 20% (21 phonons) and 18% (3 phonons).
- For the 3s1/2 level in 120Sn, gamma = 26% (21 phonons) and 16% (3 phonons).
- For the 1h11/2 level in 120Sn, gamma = 13% (21 phonons) and 9% (3 phonons).
- The experimental average excitation energy for the 1g9/2 level in 119Sn is 5.41 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Green function approach previously applied to magic and semi-magic nuclei (e.g., by the same group in earlier work) to non-magic nuclei with pairing, where Cooper pairing in the ground state must be included. The method builds on the quasiparticle-phonon coupling model and the theory of finite Fermi systems, and it introduces a refinement procedure analogous to that used for single-particle energies in magic nuclei (as in earlier work by the authors). The calculations for 119Sn and 121Sn show reasonable agreement with experimental data from Gales (1980) and others, except for the 2d5/2 and 1g7/2 levels in 121Sn where the strength is very fragmented. This work enables more accurate calculations of single-particle strength distributions in odd-mass deformed and spherical nuclei with pairing, and the refinement procedure can be extended to include pairing phonons in future studies.
