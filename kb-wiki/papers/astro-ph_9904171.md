# Nucleosynthesis in Supernovae

> Core collapse supernovae are dominated by energy transport from neutrinos. Therefore, some supernova properties could depend on symetries and features of the standard model weak interactions. The cross section for neutrino capture is larger than that for antineutrino capture by one term of order the neutrino energy over the nucleon mass. This reduces the ratio of neutrons to protons in the $ν$-driven wind above a protoneutron star by approximately 20 % and may significantly hinder r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The difference between neutrino and antineutrino cross sections, arising from standard model weak interactions, reduces the equilibrium neutron-to-proton ratio in the neutrino-driven wind above a protoneutron star by approximately 20%, which may significantly hinder r-process nucleosynthesis; this result depends on the assumption that neutrino capture rates dominate over other reactions such as electron/positron capture.

## Method

The authors calculated the charged-current cross sections for ν_e + n → p + e^- and \barν_e + p → n + e^+ to first order in E/M (neutrino energy over nucleon mass), including the weak magnetism term δ that violates C and P. They averaged these cross sections over the neutrino and antineutrino spectra, then took the ratio of the averaged rates to compute the equilibrium electron fraction Y_e and the n/p ratio in the wind, using typical spectral parameters from supernova simulations by J.R. Wilson.

## Key numbers

- The weak magnetism coefficient δ = 4g_a(1+2F_2)/(1+3g_a^2) ≈ 4.12, which increases the ν cross section and decreases the \barν cross section.
- The recoil correction coefficient γ = (2+10g_a^2)/(1+3g_a^2) ≈ 3.10, which is the same for ν and \barν.
- The C-violating factor C ≈ 0.8 for typical parameters, reducing the equilibrium n/p ratio by approximately 20%.
- The neutron-proton mass difference Δ = 1.293 MeV.
- For a model without the C term yielding Y_e = 0.42, including the C term gives Y_e ≈ 0.48.
- The shape factor a_2 = 1.23 for a Fermi-Dirac distribution with chemical potential η=0, and a_2 = 1.15 for η=3.5.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nucleosynthesis in supernovae, which had assumed equal neutrino and antineutrino interactions aside from the neutron-proton mass difference (Qian and Woosley 1996; Woosley and Hoffman 1992). The paper shows that the standard model weak interaction asymmetry makes it very unlikely that present wind models produce a successful r-process, and suggests that modifications to the neutrino fluxes (e.g., via neutrino oscillations as discussed by Qian and Fuller 1995) or changes in the astrophysical simulations are needed. The result also implies that differences between ν and \barν interactions may be important for any nucleosynthesis site involving neutrinos.
