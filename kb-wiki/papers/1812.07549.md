# A transverse momentum dependent framework for back-to-back photon+jet production

> We propose the $pp \rightarrow \text{jet} + γ$ as a new process for studying the Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Functions (TMDs). To do so, we developed a novel framework for the jet-$γ$ imbalance in $pp \rightarrow \text{jet} + γ$ using Soft Collinear Effective Theory. The new framework opens up many new insights to the TMDs which the current TMD studies confined to the Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes cannot achieve. The established formalism provides, for the first time, a first principle prediction of the jet-$γ$ imbalance in $pp$ collisions and therefore a first direct probe of the factorization breaking effects when compared with the future experimental data. If the factorization breaking effects are found small, the process will offer the unique sensitivities to both the polarized and un-polarized quark/gluon TMDs. We demonstrate the predictive power of the framework by calculating each component in the formalism to the next-to-leading order accuracy and by realizing the next-to-leading-logarithmic evolution. We provide the first numerical estimates for this process, for both the unpolarized cross section and the Sivers asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that back-to-back photon+jet production in proton-proton collisions can serve as a new process for studying Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Functions (TMDs), provided that factorization breaking effects are found to be small when compared with future experimental data.

## Method

The authors develop a factorized framework using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the jet-photon transverse momentum imbalance in proton-proton collisions, focusing on the back-to-back region where the imbalance is much smaller than the average transverse momentum. They compute all components in the formalism to next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy and implement next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) evolution. Numerical estimates are obtained by combining standard TMDs extracted from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) with the newly derived soft functions, and comparing with Pythia 8 simulations for RHIC kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized cross section is computed for RHIC energy sqrt(s) = 500 GeV, with jet transverse momentum 10 GeV < p_perp < 50 GeV, rapidities |y_gamma,J| < 2.0, and jet radius R = 0.7
- The Sivers asymmetry A_N is found to be on the order of 1% for the same RHIC energy, with jet transverse momentum 10 GeV < p_perp < 50 GeV and rapidities y_gamma = y_J = 1
- The hard functions for the two relevant partonic channels (q qbar -> g gamma and q g -> q gamma) are known to two loops (Becher 2009)
- The collinear-soft function at NLO in b-space contains a term with ln^2(i 2 mu c_phi_bj / (mu_b R)) where mu_b = 2 e^{-gamma_E} / b

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization framework beyond the two established processes of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan, which have been the only processes with well-established TMD factorization (Collins 2011). The process was proposed earlier by Bacchetta (2007) using a leading-order parton model, but later theoretical studies by Collins (2007) and Rogers (2010) found potential factorization breaking effects that hindered further investigation. The framework enables first-principle predictions that can be compared with experimental data from RHIC and LHC to assess factorization breaking, and if such effects are small, it provides unique sensitivity to gluon TMDs and Sivers functions that cannot be probed in SIDIS or Drell-Yan processes.
