# Covariant three-body equations in phi^3 field theory

> We derive four-dimensional relativistic three-body equations for the case of a field theory with a three-point interaction vertex. These equations describe the coupled 2->2, 2->3, and 3->3 processes, and provide the means of calculating the kernel of the 2->2 Bethe-Salpeter equation. Our equations differ from all previous formulations in two essential ways. Firstly, we have overcome the overcounting problems inherent in earlier works. Secondly, we have retained all possible two-body forces when one particle is a spectator. In this respect, we show how it is necessary to also retain certain three-body forces as these can give rise to (previously overlooked) two-body forces when used in a 2->3 process. The revealing of such hidden two-body forces gives rise to a further novel feature of our equations, namely, to the appearance of a number of subtraction terms. In the case of the piNN system, for example, the NN potential involves a subtraction term where two pions, exchanged between the nucleons, interact with each other through the pi-pi t-matrix. The necessity of an input pi-pi interaction is surprising and contrasts markedly with the corresponding three-dimensional description of the piNN system where no such interaction explicitly appears. This illustrates the somewhat unexpected result that the four-dimensional equations differ from the three-dimensional ones even at the operator level.

## Key claim

The authors derive four-dimensional relativistic three-body equations for a φ³ field theory that avoid both the overcounting problems of earlier formulations (Taylor, 1974; subsequent works) and the undercounting of two-body forces that occurs when three-body forces are neglected without careful analysis. The equations differ from three-dimensional descriptions even at the operator level, requiring input (such as a π-π interaction in the πNN system) that is not needed in the corresponding three-dimensional equations.

## Method

The authors analyze skeleton Feynman diagrams for the coupled 2→2, 2→3, and 3→3 processes in a φ³ field theory with dressed vertices and propagators, using the concept of r-particle irreducibility. They overcome the non-uniqueness of the right-most three-particle cut in 2→3 amplitudes by "pulling out" a vertex on one of the two initial particles, and they expose hidden two-body forces that arise from three-body forces when particles are absorbed through a φ³ vertex. The resulting integral equations for the 3→3 amplitude T and the 2→3 amplitude M are expressed in terms of two-body t-matrices and include explicit subtraction terms.

## Key numbers

- The counting factor for exposing n particles between appropriately symmetric amplitudes is 1/n! (e.g., factor 1/2 for two particles, factor 1/3! = 1/6 for three particles).
- The three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation for the 1-, 2-, and 3-particle irreducible amplitude T⁽²⁾ is written as T⁽²⁾ = K + (1/3!) K G₀ T⁽²⁾, where K is the sum of all 3→3 diagrams that are simultaneously 1-, 2-, and 3-particle irreducible.
- The full 3→3 amplitude T is given by T = T⁽²⁾ + (1/4) M⁽²⁾ D M̅⁽²⁾, where D = D₀ᴾ + D₀ t D₀.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering four-dimensional three-body formulation of Taylor (1974) by correcting the overcounting problem that arose from non-unique three-particle cuts in 2→3 amplitudes, a difficulty previously addressed only by Tucciarone (whose equations required explicit perturbation expansions). The authors also correct the undercounting problem present in all earlier works (Taylor, 1974; references 2-5) that neglected three-body forces without realizing that such forces can generate two-body forces when used in 2→3 processes. The equations enable a consistent four-dimensional treatment of systems such as πNN, where the NN potential acquires a subtraction term involving π-π interaction, and can be compared with the three-dimensional convolution equations of Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1993).
