# Linear square-mass trajectories of radially and orbitally excited hadrons in holographic QCD

> We consider a new approach towards constructing approximate holographic duals of QCD from experimental hadron properties. This framework allows us to derive a gravity dual which reproduces the empirically found linear square-mass trajectories of universal slope for radially and orbitally excited hadrons. Conformal symmetry breaking in the bulk is exclusively due to infrared deformations of the anti-de Sitter metric and governed by one free mass scale proportional to Lambda_QCD. The resulting background geometry exhibits dual signatures of confinement and provides the first examples of holographically generated linear trajectories in the baryon sector. The predictions for the light hadron spectrum include new relations between trajectory slopes and ground state masses and are in good overall agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a holographic dual of QCD with a single free mass scale (proportional to \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\)) can reproduce the empirically observed linear square-mass trajectories of universal slope for both radially and orbitally excited hadrons, including the baryon sector where such trajectories had not previously been generated holographically. The hedge is that this is achieved through an approximate, bottom-up construction based on infrared deformations of the anti-de Sitter metric, not from an exact top-down derivation.

## Method

The authors start from the five-dimensional wave equations for string modes dual to mesons and baryons in an AdS\(_5\) background with a general warp factor \(e^{2A(z)}\). They heuristically replace the twist dimension \(\bar{\tau}\) of the dual QCD operators by \(\bar{\tau} + \lambda^2 z^2\) in the five-dimensional mass terms, which yields Schrödinger-type potentials of oscillator form. They then equate these heuristic potentials to the general expressions derived from the metric with an unspecified warp factor, obtaining differential equations for \(A(z)\). Solving these equations with the conformal boundary condition \(A(0)=0\) yields explicit analytic forms for the warp factor that generate the linear trajectories.

## Key numbers

* Trajectory slope: \(W = 4\lambda^2\), where \(\lambda\) is the single free IR mass scale.
* Meson square-mass spectrum: \(M_M^2 = 4\lambda^2 (N + L + 1/2)\).
* Baryon square-mass spectrum: \(M_B^2 = 4\lambda^2 (N + L + 3/2)\).
* Experimental meson trajectory slopes from fits: \(W = 1.25 \pm 0.15\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (Anisovich 2000) and \(W = 1.14 \pm 0.013\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (Bugg 2004).
* Experimental baryon trajectory slope from fit: \(W = 1.081 \pm 0.035\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) (Klempt 2002).
* Ground-state mass relations: \(M_{M,0}^2 = W/2\) and \(M_{B,0}^2 = 3W/2\).

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up AdS/QCD program beyond the hard-wall model of Polchinski (2002), which predicted quadratic instead of linear square-mass trajectories for excited hadrons. It addresses a limitation identified in earlier holographic approaches: while linear meson trajectories had been reproduced by models such as Karch et al. (2006), Andreev (2006), and Kruczenski et al. (2005), no holographic dual had previously generated linear baryon trajectories. The results enable further study of holographic confinement mechanisms and provide testable relations between trajectory slopes and ground-state masses that can be compared with experimental data from the Particle Data Group.
