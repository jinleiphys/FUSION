# Electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma investigated using a perturbative QCD based parton cascade

> Electric conductivity is sensitive to effective cross sections among the particles of the partonic medium. We investigate the electric conductivity of a hot plasma of quarks and gluons, solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation. In order to extract this transport coefficient, we employ the Green-Kubo formalism and, independently, a method motivated by the classical definition of electric conductivity. To this end we evaluate the static electric diffusion current upon the influence of an electric field. Both methods give identical results. For the first time, we obtain numerically the Drude electric conductivity formula for an ultrarelativistic gas of quarks and gluons employing constant isotropic binary cross sections. Furthermore, we extract the electric conductivity for a system of massless quarks and gluons including screened binary and inelastic, radiative $2\leftrightarrow 3$ perturbative QCD scattering. Comparing with recent lattice results, we find an agreement in the temperature dependence of the conductivity.

## Key claim

The paper numerically extracts the electric conductivity of a quark-gluon plasma using a parton cascade solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation, and finds agreement with lattice QCD results specifically in the *temperature dependence* of the conductivity, not necessarily in the absolute magnitude.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation using the parton cascade BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings) for a static box of massless quarks, antiquarks, and gluons with periodic boundary conditions. They extract the electric conductivity via two independent methods: the Green-Kubo formalism (fitting an exponential to the electric current autocorrelation function) and a direct method applying a small static electric field and measuring the resulting steady-state current. The simulations employ constant isotropic binary cross sections for validation, then proceed to leading-order perturbative QCD cross sections including elastic 2-to-2 and inelastic 2-to-3 processes with running coupling and a Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression parameter fixed to X_LPM = 0.3.

## Key numbers

* For constant isotropic binary cross sections of 3 mb, the numerically obtained Drude-type formula gives a correction factor of 0.9 ± 0.01 relative to the simple relaxation-time approximation.
* The electric conductivity over temperature ratio σ_el/T lies between 0.04 and 0.08 for temperatures from 0.2 GeV to 0.6 GeV in the full pQCD scenario with elastic and inelastic scattering and running coupling.
* Changing the LPM cutoff parameter from X_LPM = 0.3 to X_LPM = 0.5 increases the electric conductivity by about 16%, and to X_LPM = 1.0 increases it by about 40%.
* Including inelastic collisions decreases the electric conductivity by about 40% compared to the elastic-only scenario.

## Context

This work extends previous BAMPS studies of shear viscosity (Wesp 2011, Reining 2012) and heat conductivity (Greif 2013) to the electric conductivity of a partonic plasma. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations (Aarts 2007, Brandt 2013, Amato 2013, Gupta 2004, Buividovich 2010, Burnier 2012, Ding 2011), the transport model PHSD (Cassing 2013, Steinert 2013), and holographic computations (Finazzo 2014). The study enables microscopic understanding of effective scattering rates in pQCD and provides a benchmark for comparing effective cross sections between transport models, lattice QCD, and Dyson-Schwinger approaches.
