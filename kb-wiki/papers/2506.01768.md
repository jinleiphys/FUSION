# Toward extracting scattering phase shift from integrated correlation functions IV: Coulomb corrections

> The formalism developed in Refs.~\cite{Guo:2023ecc,Guo:2024zal,Guo:2024pvt} that relates the integrated correlation functions for a trapped system to the infinite volume scattering phase shifts through a weighted integral is further extended to include Coulomb interaction between charged particles. The original formalism cannot be applied due to different divergent asymptotic behavior resulting from the long-range nature of the Coulomb force. We show that a modified formula in which the difference of integrated correlation functions between particles interacting with Coulomb plus short-range interaction and with Coulomb interaction alone is free of divergence, and has rapid approach to its infinite volume limit. Using an exactly solvable model, we demonstrate that the short-range potential scattering phase shifts can be reliably extracted from the formula in the presence of Coulomb interaction.

## Key claim

The paper extends the integrated correlation function formalism to systems with Coulomb interactions by showing that the difference between correlation functions for particles interacting via Coulomb plus short-range potentials and those interacting via Coulomb alone remains finite and converges rapidly to the infinite-volume limit, unlike the original formalism which fails due to divergent asymptotic behavior from the long-range Coulomb force.

## Method

The authors derive a modified relation in which the difference of integrated correlation functions between particles with Coulomb plus short-range interaction and with pure Coulomb interaction replaces the original difference between interacting and non-interacting systems. They verify the formalism using an exactly solvable model with a contact short-range interaction and a spherical hard-wall trap, solving the quantization condition that connects trapped energy levels to infinite-volume scattering phase shifts in the presence of Coulomb forces.

## Key numbers

* Contact interaction renormalized strength \(V_R = 0.5\) used in numerical verification with reduced mass \(\mu = 1\) and Coulomb strength \(Z = 0.1\)
* Trap radii of \(R = \pi\) and \(R = 4\pi\) used to demonstrate convergence of the integrated correlation function difference to the infinite-volume limit
* Coulomb phase shift given analytically as \(\delta^{(C)}_l(\epsilon) = \arg \Gamma(l+1 + i\gamma)\) where \(\gamma = -Z\mu/k\)
* Short-range S-wave phase shift given by \(\delta^{(S)}_0(\epsilon) = \cot^{-1}(-1/(2\mu k V_R))\)

## Context

This work extends the integrated correlation function formalism developed by Guo and Lee (2023, 2024) for extracting scattering phase shifts from trapped systems, which previously excluded long-range interactions. The approach complements Luscher-like quantization conditions and the HALQCD potential method for lattice QCD, and addresses the need for Coulomb corrections in charged-hadron interactions. The formalism enables extraction of short-range phase shifts from correlation functions without requiring energy spectrum determination, potentially overcoming signal-to-noise problems in multi-nucleon lattice simulations.
