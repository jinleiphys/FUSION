# Structure change of Cooper pairs in color superconductivity -- Crossover from BCS to BEC ? --

> We discuss a possibility of transition from color superconductivity of the standard BCS type at high density, to Bose-Einstein Condensation (BEC) of Cooper pairs at lower density. Examining two-flavor QCD over a wide range of baryon density, we found the size of a Cooper pair becomes small enough to be comparable to the averaged quark-quark distance at lower density. We also consider the same problem in two-color QCD.

# Structure change of Cooper pairs in color superconductivity, Crossover from BCS to BEC ?

## Key claim
The paper argues that as baryon density decreases in two-flavor QCD, color superconductivity may undergo a smooth crossover from standard BCS-type pairing (with large, loosely bound Cooper pairs) to Bose-Einstein Condensation (BEC) of tightly bound Cooper pairs, provided the system remains in the deconfined phase at lower densities. The claim is conditional: the extrapolation to the BEC regime depends on whether the deconfined phase persists at chemical potentials below the lowest value explicitly computed (μ = 800 MeV).

## Method
The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson gap equation for the momentum-dependent superconducting gap Δ(k) in the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, using a running coupling in the improved ladder approximation with an infrared regulator. For two-flavor, three-color QCD, they compute the Cooper pair wavefunction from the gap and extract the coherence length (root-mean-square radius) as a function of chemical potential μ. For two-color QCD, they solve coupled gap equations for both the chiral condensate M(p) and the diquark gap Δ(p) to study the symmetry structure at low density.

## Key numbers
- At the lowest density computed (μ = 800 MeV), the Cooper pair size is less than 4 fm.
- The ratio ξ_c/d_q of Cooper pair size to average inter-quark distance is less than 10 at μ = 800 MeV, compared to approximately 10^5 at the highest density.
- The running coupling uses Λ = 400 MeV and p_c^2 = 1.5 Λ^2, determined to reproduce low-energy meson properties for N_f = 2, N_c = 3 vacuum.
- Densities are scanned over μ = 2^n Λ with n = 1, 2, 3, 12 (i.e., μ = 800 MeV, 1.6 GeV, 3.2 GeV, and extremely high density).

## Context
This work extends the study of color superconductivity beyond the weak-coupling regime, where previous treatments (reviewed in the paper) assumed BCS-like pairing valid only at asymptotically high density. The authors build on the improved ladder approximation of Higashijima (1987) for the running coupling. For two-color QCD, the analysis connects to the work of Kogut et al. (2000) on the Pauli-Gursey symmetry and the Nambu-Goldstone boson structure at μ = 0. The results suggest that future work should investigate the detailed nature of the BCS-BEC crossover and its signatures in observables such as the occupation number and the diffuse Fermi surface.
