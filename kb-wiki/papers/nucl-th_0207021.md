# Effective DBHF Method for Asymmetric Nuclear Matter and Finite Nuclei

> A new decomposition of the Dirac structure of nucleon self-energies in the Dirac Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach is adopted to investigate the equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter. The effective coupling constants of $σ$, $ω$, $δ$ and $ρ$ mesons with a density dependence in the relativistic mean field approach are deduced by reproducing the nucleon self-energy resulting from the DBHF at each density for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. With these couplings the properties of finite nuclei are investigated. The agreement of charge radii and binding energies of finite nuclei with the experimental data are improved simultaneously in comparison with the projection method. It seems that the properties of finite nuclei are sensitive to the scheme used for the DBHF self-energy extraction. We may conclude that the properties of the asymmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei could be well described by the new decomposition approach of the G matrix.

## Key claim

The new decomposition of the DBHF G-matrix into a bare OBEP and a correlation term parameterized by four pseudo-mesons yields effective nucleon-meson coupling constants that improve the simultaneous description of charge radii and binding energies of finite nuclei compared to the projection method, although the properties of finite nuclei remain sensitive to the specific scheme used for self-energy extraction.

## Method

The DBHF G-matrix was split into the bare one-boson-exchange potential (Bonn A) and a correlation term, with the latter parameterized by four pseudo-mesons (sigma', omega', delta', rho') of infinite mass. The Dirac structure of the nucleon self-energy was extracted from the G-matrix, and effective density-dependent coupling constants for sigma, omega, delta, and rho mesons were determined by reproducing the DBHF scalar and vector self-energies (Fit.A) or the DBHF scalar potential and binding energy per nucleon (Fit.B) at each density for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. These couplings were then used in relativistic mean-field calculations for doubly magic and semi-magic nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties from DBHF with Bonn A: E/A = -15.6 MeV at rho_B0 = 0.185 fm^{-3}; with Groningen interaction: E/A = -15.5 MeV at rho_B0 = 0.182 fm^{-3}
- Deviations of binding energies and charge radii for magic and semi-magic nuclei from experimental data are within 5%
- For asymmetry parameter beta < 0.8, the energy per nucleon as a function of density exhibits a curve with a minimum; for beta = 1 (neutron matter) no minimum is found
- The deviation of the binding energy at the saturation point between the RMF with effective couplings and the DBHF is within 2%

## Context

This work extends the new decomposition approach of Schiller and Muether (2001) from symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric nuclear matter and finite nuclei. It addresses ambiguities in earlier methods: the simple method of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) that extracted self-energies from the momentum dependence of single-particle energy gave isovector coupling constants with a wrong sign (Ulrych and Muether, 1997), while the projection method of Boersma and Malfliet (1994) and de Jong and Lenske (1998) introduced ambiguities from pseudo-scalar couplings. The results improve upon those of Hofmann, Keil, and Lenske (2001) who used a projection method with momentum correction, achieving better simultaneous agreement for binding energies and charge radii without introducing additional parameters. The approach enables future applications to neutron star physics and exotic nuclei.
