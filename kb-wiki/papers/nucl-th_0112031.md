# The size of two-body weakly bound objects : short versus long range potentials

> The variation of the size of two-body objects is investigated, as the separation energy approaches zero, with both long range potentials and short range potentials having a repulsive core. It is shown that long range potentials can also give rise to very extended systems. The asymptotic laws derived for states with angular momentum l=1,2 differ from the ones obtained with short range potentials. The sensitivity of the asymptotic laws on the shape and length of short range potentials defined by two and three parameters is studied. These ideas as well as the transition from the short to the long range regime for the l=0 case are illustrated using the Kratzer potential.

## Key claim

Long-range potentials (where the zero-energy limit is reached as the potential depth goes to zero) can also produce very extended two-body systems, and their asymptotic laws for the root-mean-square (rms) radius differ from those of short-range potentials for angular momentum ℓ = 1, 2, not just for ℓ = 0. The authors show that for short-range potentials with a repulsive core, the asymptotic laws depend on the shape and length of the potential when defined by three parameters, not just two.

## Method

The authors start from the radial Schrödinger equation (with ℏ = 2m = 1) for spherical symmetric potentials and derive general inequalities relating the rms radius to the energy. They then derive asymptotic laws for the rms radius as the separation energy approaches zero, using the asymptotic form of the wavefunctions: spherical Hankel functions for short-range potentials and spherical Bessel functions for long-range potentials. To illustrate the transition between regimes and the effect of a repulsive core, they solve the Schrödinger equation analytically for the Kratzer potential (long-range with a repulsive core) and for a cut version of this potential (short-range with a repulsive core, defined by three parameters), comparing results to a square well (two-parameter short-range potential).

## Key numbers

* For the Kratzer potential (long-range), as Eℓ → 0: ⟨r²⟩₀ ≈ 3/|E₀|, ⟨r²⟩₁ ≈ 7.5/|E₁|, ⟨r²⟩₂ ≈ 14/|E₂| (in units where ℏ = 2m = 1).
* For the cut Kratzer potential (short-range with core), the asymptotic laws are: ⟨r²⟩₀ ≈ c₀/|E₀|, ⟨r²⟩₁ ≈ c₁ R_cut / √|E₁|, ⟨r²⟩₂ ≈ c₂ R_cut², with c₀ = 1/2 exactly (as predicted by a general lower bound).
* For the ¹¹Be halo nucleus, using experimental separation energy 0.5 MeV and rms radius ≈ 7 fm, the extracted c₀ ≈ 1.1.
* For the ⁴He-⁴He dimer, using separation energy 10⁻¹ μeV and radius 70 Å, the extracted c₀ ≈ 0.52.
* For the calculated ³He-⁶Li dimer, using separation energy 10⁻² μeV and radius 210 Å, the extracted c₀ ≈ 0.5.
* The transition between short-range and long-range regime for ℓ = 0 occurs at around √|E₀| R_cut = 2 (half-point between c₀ = 1/2 and c₀ = 3).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of asymptotic laws for weakly bound two-body systems, particularly the work of Riisager, Jensen, and Møller (1992) who derived asymptotic laws for short-range potentials defined by two parameters (depth and range). The authors show that long-range potentials (such as the Kratzer potential) produce a different energy dependence of the rms radius for p- and d-states, and that short-range potentials with a repulsive core (three parameters) exhibit a "quasi-scaling" where the dependence on the range is similar to the two-parameter case but the coefficients cℓ depend on the potential shape. The results enable a better understanding of halo nuclei and loosely bound molecular dimers, showing that while dimers like ⁴He-⁴He are in the asymptotic region, halo nuclei like ¹¹Be remain at the threshold where the size is sensitive to the potential details.
