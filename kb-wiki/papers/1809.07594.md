# Transport responses from rate of decay and scattering processes in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We have calculated quark and anti-quark relaxation time by considering different possible elastic and inelastic scatterings in the medium. Comparative role of these elastic and inelastic scatterings on different transport coefficients are explored. The quark-meson effective interaction Lagrangian density in the framework of Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model is used for calculating both type of scatterings. Owing to a kinetic threshold, inelastic scatterings can only exist beyond the Mott line in temperature and chemical potential plane, whereas elastic scatterings occur in the entire plane. Interestingly, the strength of inelastic scatterings near and above Mott line becomes so strong that medium behaves like a perfect fluid, in that all transport coefficients become very small.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the combined effect of elastic (2↔2) and inelastic (1↔2) scatterings in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model produces a dramatic reduction in transport coefficients near and above the Mott temperature, but this perfect-fluid behavior is conditional on the inelastic processes having a kinetic threshold, they only contribute beyond the Mott line in the temperature-chemical potential plane, whereas elastic scatterings operate everywhere.

## Method

The authors used the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors (u, d) in the quasi-particle (Hartree) approximation to compute quark and antiquark thermal widths from both elastic 2↔2 quark-quark and quark-antiquark scatterings mediated by σ and π meson exchanges, and inelastic 1↔2 quark-meson processes (Q ↔ QM) obtained from the Landau cut of the quark self-energy. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity η, electrical conductivity σ, and thermal conductivity κ) were then calculated using the Kubo formalism in the relaxation time approximation, with the thermal width entering as the inverse relaxation time. The model parameters were fixed to reproduce the quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = (−252 MeV)³, pion decay constant fπ = 94 MeV, and pion mass mπ = 142 MeV, yielding constituent quark mass MQ = 328 MeV and σ meson mass mσ = 663 MeV at T=0, μ=0.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass MQ = 328 MeV and σ meson mass mσ = 663 MeV at T=0, μ=0, with current quark masses mQ = 5 MeV, coupling GΛ² = 2.14, and cutoff Λ = 653 MeV
- Pion mass mπ = 142 MeV and pion decay constant fπ = 94 MeV used as fit constraints
- Quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = (−252 MeV)³ used as fit constraint
- At μ = 0.150 GeV and T = 0.150 GeV, the Mott chemical potential μM ∼ 0.2 GeV
- The η/s ratio approaches the KSS holographic bound of 1/(4π) near T ∼ 0.275 GeV when inelastic scatterings are included
- For μ = 0, the Mott temperature TM is the threshold beyond which mπ(T ≥ TM) ≥ 2MQ(T ≥ TM), and TM decreases with increasing μ

## Context

This work extends prior NJL model calculations that separately studied either elastic 2↔2 scatterings (Klevansky (1993), Marty (2013), Deb (2016)) or inelastic 1↔2 processes (Ghosh (2015), Weise (2015), Harutyunyan (2017)) by providing the first simultaneous treatment of both scattering types within the same model. The results enable quantitative understanding of how the Mott transition, where pions delocalize into their constituent quarks, produces a sudden enhancement of thermal width that drives transport coefficients toward the perfect-fluid limit observed in RHIC heavy-ion collisions, while also showing that below the Mott temperature the η/s ratio is much larger than the KSS bound, indicating non-perfect-fluid behavior.
