# Supercooled Goldstone Bosons at the QCD Chiral Phase Transition

> We discuss a universal non-equilibrium enhancement of long-wavelength Goldstone bosons induced by quenches to the broken phase in Model G -- the dynamical universality class of an $O(4)$-antiferromagnet and the chiral phase transition in QCD. Scaling arguments for the coarsening dynamics describing the formation of the chiral condensate predict a parametric enhancement in the infrared spectra of Goldstones, a prediction confirmed by stochastic simulations of the transition. The details of the enhancement are determined by the non-linear dynamics of a superfluid effective theory, which is a limit of Model G reflecting the broken $O(4)$ symmetry. Our results translate to a parametric enhancement of low-momentum pions in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, which are underpredicted in current hydrodynamic models without critical dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that during instantaneous quenches to the broken phase of the QCD chiral phase transition, the infrared spectrum of Goldstone bosons (pions) is parametrically enhanced over equilibrium values by a factor of 1/(kξ) for massless pions or 1/(mξ) for massive pions, but this enhancement only persists for a parametrically long but finite time window between the ballistic timescale t_B ~ L/v and the diffusive timescale t_D ~ L²/D_A.

## Method

The authors perform stochastic simulations of Model G, the dynamical universality class of an O(4) antiferromagnet that describes the QCD chiral phase transition, using instantaneous quenches from the restored to the broken phase. They analyze the non-equilibrium evolution of the magnetization (zero-mode of the order parameter) and the pion-pion two-point correlation function G_ππ(t,k) at small momenta, comparing their scaling behavior to equilibrium expectations. The simulations are conducted for several reduced temperatures t_r, with correlation lengths scaling as ξ ~ |t_r|^{-ν} where ν = 0.7377(41).

## Key numbers

- The enhancement factor for massless pions at times t ~ 1/(vk) is 1/(kξ) in d=3 dimensions
- The enhancement factor for massive pions with k ≪ m is 1/(mξ) in d=3 dimensions
- The critical exponent ν = 0.7377(41) from Engels (2014) is used for the correlation length scaling ξ ~ |t_r|^{-ν}
- The finite-size corrections to equilibrium magnetization are parametrized as √⟨M_a M_a⟩_eq ≈ σ_eq (1 + 0.706 ξ/L)
- The data collapse for the pion correlator is observed for 1 ≲ tvk ≲ 4 after rescaling

## Context

This work extends the dynamical universality class analysis of Model G for the QCD chiral phase transition, originally identified by Rajagopal (1992), and builds on earlier studies of disoriented chiral condensates by Bjorken (1991) and Blaizot (1992). The results address a persistent tension between hydrodynamic models and LHC data showing a ~50% enhancement of low-momentum pions near p_T ~ m_π (Lu, 2024), which current models without critical dynamics underpredict. The work enables future implementation of stochastic Model G dynamics within realistic hydrodynamic codes to quantify whether non-equilibrium critical dynamics can account for the observed low-p_T excess in heavy-ion collisions at ALICE3.
