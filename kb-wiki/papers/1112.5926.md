# Two-Baryon Potentials and H-Dibaryon from 3-flavor Lattice QCD Simulations

> Baryon-baryon potentials are obtained from 3-flavor QCD simulations with the lattice volume L ~ 4 fm, the lattice spacing a ~ 0.12 fm, and the pseudo-scalar-meson mass M_ps =469 - 1171 MeV. The NN scattering phase shift and the mass of H-dibaryon in the flavor SU(3) limit are extracted from the resultant potentials by solving the Schrodinger equation. The NN phase shift in the SU(3) limit is shown to have qualitatively similar behavior as the experimental data. A bound H-dibaryon in the SU(3) limit is found to exist in the flavor-singlet J^P=0^+ channel with the binding energy of about 26 MeV for the lightest quark mass M_ps = 469 MeV. Effect of flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking on the H-dibaryon is estimated by solving the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for Lambda Lambda - N Xi - Sigma Sigma with the physical baryon masses and the potential matrix obtained in the SU(3) limit: a resonant H-dibaryon is found between Lambda Lambda and N Xi thresholds in this treatment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a bound H-dibaryon exists in the flavor SU(3) limit with a binding energy of about 26 MeV at the lightest quark mass studied (M_ps = 469 MeV), but when flavor SU(3) symmetry breaking is introduced via baryon mass splittings, the H-dibaryon becomes a resonance located between the Lambda-Lambda and N-Xi thresholds, rather than a deeply bound state.

## Method

The authors performed 3-flavor lattice QCD simulations in the flavor SU(3) limit on a 32^3 x 32 lattice with lattice spacing a = 0.121 fm and volume L = 3.87 fm, at five pseudo-scalar meson masses ranging from 469 to 1171 MeV. They extracted baryon-baryon potentials using the time-dependent HAL QCD method, which solves a time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function, and then solved the Schrodinger equation in infinite volume to obtain NN phase shifts and H-dibaryon binding energies. To estimate SU(3) breaking effects, they solved the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the Lambda-Lambda, N-Xi, and Sigma-Sigma system using the SU(3)-limit potential matrix together with physical baryon masses introduced via a linear interpolation parameter.

## Key numbers

* NN phase shifts in the SU(3) limit show qualitatively similar behavior to experimental data, with no bound two-nucleon states found for any quark mass in the range M_ps = 469 - 1172 MeV.
* H-dibaryon binding energy in the SU(3) limit at M_ps = 469 MeV: 26.0 (4.4)(4.8) MeV (statistical and systematic errors).
* H-dibaryon binding energies at other masses: 33.6 (4.8)(3.5) MeV at M_ps = 672 MeV; 37.8 (3.1)(4.2) MeV at M_ps = 837 MeV; 37.2 (3.7)(2.4) MeV at M_ps = 1015 MeV; 49.1 (3.4)(5.5) MeV at M_ps = 1171 MeV.
* With physical SU(3) breaking (x=1), the H-dibaryon exists as a resonance at 3 MeV below the N-Xi threshold with a width of 2.7 MeV.
* Scattering length for Lambda-Lambda at x=0.4: a = -0.91 fm; at x=0.6: a = -29.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exploratory studies of baryon-baryon potentials in the flavor SU(3) limit (Inoue 2010) from smaller volumes (L=1.93 fm) and heavier quark masses to a larger volume (L=3.87 fm) and lighter quark masses down to M_ps = 469 MeV. The results are consistent with independent full QCD simulations using Luscher's method (Beane 2010, Beane 2011) which found an H-dibaryon binding energy of 13.2 MeV at (M_pi, M_K) approximately (389, 544) MeV. The phenomenological estimate of SU(3) breaking effects, producing a resonant H-dibaryon near the N-Xi threshold, agrees with earlier coupled-channel quark model analyses (Oka 1983) and contrasts with recent chiral effective field theory studies (Beane 2011, Shanahan 2011, Haidenbauer 2011). The results enable experimental searches for the resonant H-dibaryon in double-Lambda production experiments at J-PARC and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
