# Studies of the structure of massive hybrid stars within a modified NJL model

> In this paper, we use the equation of state based on a modification of 2+1 flavors Nambu-Jona- Lasinio (NJL) model to study the quark matter of hybrid stars. For comparison, we utilize five EOSs of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model to describe the hadronic phase. With the 3- window crossover interpolation approach, we try to construct relatively soft hybrid EOSs but find the maximum masses of hybrid stars do not differ too much. The results are quite close to two solar mass, which is consistent with the mass constraint of PSR J0348+0432. Furthermore it is noteworthy that the heaviest stable stars have central densities higher than that of the deconfinement transition thus suggesting a pure quark core in the hybrid star.

## Key claim

The maximum masses of hybrid stars constructed using a modified NJL model with a crossover deconfinement phase transition are all close to two solar masses, consistent with the mass constraint of PSR J0348+0432, but the authors note that the hybrid equations of state are relatively soft despite these high masses.

## Method

The authors used a modified 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a four-fermion coupling constant that depends on the quark condensate, inspired by the operator product expansion approach, to describe quark matter. For the hadronic phase, they adopted five relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (NL3, NL3ωρ, DD2, DDME2, BSR6). They connected the hadronic and quark phases using a 3-window crossover interpolation approach in the pressure-chemical potential plane, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

* Maximum masses of hybrid stars: 2.03, 2.05, 2.01, 2.03, and 1.99 solar masses for the NL3, NL3ωρ, DD2, DDME2, and BSR6 hadronic models respectively, with corresponding radii of 14.21, 13.61, 12.90, 12.99, and 13.16 kilometers.
* Central densities of the heaviest stable stars: 5.15, 5.30, 6.33, 6.04, and 5.98 times the saturation nuclear density for the five models respectively, all exceeding the deconfinement transition density.
* The bag constant B was fixed at (137 MeV)^4, within the range (134.1 MeV)^4 to (141.4 MeV)^4 from constraints including GW170817.
* The critical chemical potential for u and d quarks is about 340 MeV, while for s quarks it is about 410 MeV.
* The interpolation window parameter Γ was set to 0.4 GeV, with the deconfinement transition region located at chemical potentials from about 1 GeV to about 1.8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends studies using modified NJL models (Li et al., 2016; 2017) and crossover interpolation approaches (Masuda et al., 2012; 2013) to construct hybrid star equations of state. The results are consistent with the mass constraints from PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, and the finding that maximum masses are similar across different interpolation approaches agrees with the work of Alvarez-Castillo and Blaschke (2015). The study enables further investigation of hybrid star structure with a pure quark core, and the modified NJL model provides a method to incorporate quark feedback into the effective gluon propagator, addressing limitations of conventional NJL models.
