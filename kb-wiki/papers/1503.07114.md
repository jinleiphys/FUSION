# Equilibration rates in a strongly coupled nonconformal quark-gluon plasma

> We initiate the study of equilibration rates of strongly coupled quark-gluon plasmas in the absence of conformal symmetry. We primarily consider a supersymmetric mass deformation within ${\cal N}=2^{*}$ gauge theory and use holography to compute quasinormal modes of a variety of scalar operators, as well as the energy-momentum tensor. In each case, the lowest quasinormal frequency, which provides an approximate upper bound on the thermalization time, is proportional to temperature, up to a pre-factor with only a mild temperature dependence. We find similar behaviour in other holographic plasmas, where the model contains an additional scale beyond the temperature. Hence, our study suggests that the thermalization time is generically set by the temperature, irrespective of any other scales, in strongly coupled gauge theories.

## Key claim
The paper claims that in strongly coupled nonconformal plasmas, the thermalization time is generically set by the temperature alone, regardless of any additional mass scales, based on quasinormal mode frequencies that change by at most a factor of two across a wide range of the dimensionless mass-to-temperature ratio \(m/T\).

## Method
The authors use holographic duality to model the nonconformal \({\cal N}=2^{*}\) gauge theory as a five-dimensional black hole with scalar fields, solving the equations of motion derived from the Pilch-Warner action. They compute the lowest quasinormal mode frequencies for scalar operators of dimensions \(\Delta = 2, 3, 4\) and for the energy-momentum tensor by imposing ingoing boundary conditions at the horizon and using a shooting method to find discrete complex frequencies. They also extend the analysis to a conformal plasma with nonzero chemical potential using the anti-de Sitter Reissner-Nordstrom black hole background.

## Key numbers
- The ratio \(m/T\) is varied from 0 to 25, with the strongest deviation from conformality occurring at \(m/T \approx 4.8\), where \((\epsilon-3P)/\epsilon \approx 20\%\).
- The lowest quasinormal mode frequencies for phenomenological scalar operators of dimensions \(\Delta = 2, 3, 4\) change by no more than a factor of two as a function of \(m/T\).
- At \(m/T = 4.8\), the momentum dependence of the quasinormal mode frequencies for \(\Delta = 2, 3, 4\) operators is very close to the \({\cal N}=4\) SYM result (dashed curves in Fig. k).
- For large \(m/T\), the quasinormal mode frequencies asymptote to those of a massless scalar field in a six-dimensional AdS-Schwarzschild geometry, consistent with a five-dimensional CFT compactified on a circle.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of equilibration in conformal \({\cal N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory (Kovtun 2005, Chesler 2008) to nonconformal plasmas, addressing the question of whether the temperature alone sets the thermalization time when additional scales are present. The results contradict the naive expectation that conformal symmetry breaking could significantly alter equilibration rates, and they enable simplified modeling of heavy ion collisions using \({\cal N}=4\) SYM dynamics rather than complicated nonconformal codes. The paper also notes agreement with upcoming results from a bottom-up holographic QCD model (Janik and Spalinski).
