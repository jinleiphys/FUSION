# Isospinning Skyrmions

> In the Skyrme model atomic nuclei are modelled as quantized soliton solutions in a nonlinear field theory of pions. The mass number is given by the conserved topological charge $B$ of the solitons. Conventionally, Skyrmions are semiclassically quantized within the rigid body approach. In this approach Skyrmions are effectively treated as rigid rotors in space and isospace that is it is assumed that Skyrmions do not deform at all when they spin and isospin. This approximation resulted in qualitative and encouraging quantitative agreement with experimental nuclear physics data. In this talk, we point out that the theoretical agreement could be further improved by allowing classical Skyrmion solutions to deform as they spin and isospin. As a first step towards a better understanding of how nuclei can be approximated by classically spinning and isospinning soliton solutions, we study how classical Skyrmion solutions of topological charges $B=1-4,8$ deform when classical isospin is added.

# Isospinning Skyrmions

## Key claim
The rigid body approximation, which treats Skyrmions as non-deforming rotors in space and isospace, yields qualitative agreement with nuclear data but can be improved by allowing classical Skyrmion solutions to deform as they spin and isospin. As a first step, the authors study how classical Skyrmion solutions of topological charges B=1-4,8 deform when classical isospin is added, without imposing any spatial symmetry assumptions.

## Method
The authors construct stationary isospinning soliton solutions by numerically solving an energy minimization problem using a second-order gradient flow algorithm with friction on cubic grids of (200)^3 or (201)^3 points with lattice spacing Δx=0.1. They fix the isospin vector K constant and minimize the pseudoenergy functional F_ω = M_B - (1/2) ω_i U_ij ω_j, where U_ij is the isospin inertia tensor, recalculating ω at each step via K_i = U_ij ω_j. Initial configurations are generated using the rational map ansatz, and minimal energy solutions at lower |K| are used as initial conditions for higher |K|.

## Key numbers
- For B=1 Skyrmion with μ=1, the critical isospin is K_crit = 6.5 × 4π (ω_crit = μ = 1); at this point the rigid body approximation gives approximately 7% larger energy than the deforming solution.
- For B=1 with μ=1, the diagonal inertia tensor elements at ω_crit are U_11 = U_22 = 66.7, U_33 = 80.9, V_11 = V_22 = 67.9, W_11 = W_22 = 58.52 (in Skyrme units).
- For B=1 static solution (μ=1), the moment of inertia Λ = 47.5 (in Skyrme units).
- For B=2 isospinning about (0,0,1) with μ=1, the spin-to-isospin ratio L/K = -W_33/U_33 = -2 (numerically -1.97).
- For B=8 D_6d-symmetric solution isospinning about (0,0,1), the linear regime extends to K ≤ 17 × 4π (L ≥ -6.6 × 4π), beyond which the solution breaks into two B=4 cubes.
- Energies of static Skyrmions (μ=1): M_1 = 1.415, M_2 = 2.720, M_3 = 3.969, M_4 = 5.177, M_8 = 10.235 (all in units of 12π²).

## Context
This work extends the rigid body quantization approach of Adkins (1983) and the symmetry-based quantization of Irwin (1998) and Krusch (2002, 2005) by allowing centrifugal deformations that were previously neglected. It builds on earlier studies of spinning Skyrmions by Battye (2005), Houghton (2005), and Fortier (2008) that considered axial symmetry, and on investigations of isospinning solitons in related models such as baby Skyrmions by Battye (2013) and Hopf solitons by Harland (2013). The results demonstrate that the rigid body approximation overestimates energies for high isospin and that symmetry changes from isospinning can affect allowed quantum states, which is relevant for improving Skyrme model predictions for odd-mass nuclei such as Lithium-7 and Beryllium-7, where Manko (2007) found the static icosahedral B=7 solution too symmetric.
