# Consistent description of 12C and 16O using finite range three-body interaction

> Consistent description of 12C and 16O has been a long standing problem of microscopic alpha cluster models, where the wave function is fully antisymmetrized and the effective interaction is applied not between alpha clusters but between nucleons. When the effective interaction is designed to reproduce the binding energy of 16O (four alpha), the binding energy of 12C (three alpha) becomes underbound by about 10 MeV. In the present study, by taking into account the coupling with the jj-coupling shell model components and utilizing Tohsaki interaction, which is phenomenological but has finite-range three-body interaction terms, we show that consistent understanding of these nuclei can be achieved. The original Tohsaki interaction gives small overbound of about 3 MeV for 16O, and this is improved by slightly modifying three-body Majorana exchange parameter. Also, the coupling with the jj-coupling shell model wave function strongly contributes to the increase of the binding energy of 12C. So far the application of Tohsaki interaction has been limited to 4N nuclei, and here, we add Bartlet and Heisenberg exchange terms in the two-body interaction for the purpose of applying it to neutron-rich systems, and it is applied to 6He.

## Key claim

By coupling the Tohsaki finite-range three-body interaction with the antisymmetrized quasi-cluster model (AQCM) to include jj-coupling shell model components, the author shows that consistent binding energies for both 12C and 16O can be achieved, resolving a long-standing discrepancy where previous interactions underbound 12C by about 10 MeV when 16O was correctly bound.

## Method

The author uses the Tohsaki interaction, which contains finite-range two-body and three-body terms, for the central part of the Hamiltonian and the G3RS interaction for the spin-orbit part. For 16O, a tetrahedron configuration of four alpha clusters is used with generator coordinate method (GCM) superposition over alpha-alpha distances. For 12C, the AQCM is employed to generate basis states that interpolate between pure three-alpha clusters and jj-coupling shell model configurations by varying the dissolution parameter Lambda, and 40 basis states are superposed via GCM. The three-body Majorana exchange parameter is slightly modified from the original F1 set to create the F1' parameter set, and Bartlet and Heisenberg exchange terms are added to the two-body interaction for application to neutron-rich systems.

## Key numbers

- Original Tohsaki interaction gives small overbound of about 3 MeV for 16O
- With the F1' parameter set, the ground state of 16O is obtained at -126.9 MeV compared to the experimental value of -127.619293 MeV
- The ground state of 16O gives a matter rms radius of 2.49 fm, corresponding to a charge radius of 2.64 fm compared to the experimental charge radius of 2.69 fm
- For 12C, the ground 0+ state is obtained at -85.2 MeV without spin-orbit interaction, and at -87.8 MeV, -88.9 MeV, and -90.5 MeV with spin-orbit strengths of 1600 MeV, 1800 MeV, and 2000 MeV respectively (experimental value: -92.161726 MeV)
- The binding energy from the three-alpha threshold for 12C is -3.3 MeV without spin-orbit, and -5.9 MeV, -7.0 MeV, and -8.6 MeV with spin-orbit strengths of 1600 MeV, 1800 MeV, and 2000 MeV respectively (experimental value: -7.2747 MeV)
- The Hoyle state (second 0+ state) appears at excitation energies of 6.1 MeV, 7.5 MeV, 7.9 MeV, and 8.6 MeV for the four spin-orbit strengths (experimental: 7.65420 MeV)
- The 0+-2+ level spacing in 12C becomes 3.1 MeV, 3.7 MeV, and 4.6 MeV with spin-orbit strengths of 1600 MeV, 1800 MeV, and 2000 MeV (experimental: 4.4389131 MeV)
- Negative parity states 3- and 1- in 12C are reproduced at 9.8 MeV and 12.6 MeV with spin-orbit strength of 1800 MeV (experimental: 9.6415 MeV and 10.84416 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the earlier use of the Tohsaki interaction by Itagaki, Ohnishi, and Kato (1995), which had not fully solved the binding energy discrepancy between 12C and 16O. The inclusion of spin-orbit effects via the AQCM method, previously developed by Itagaki and collaborators, allows the coupling of jj-coupling shell model components that are essential for 12C but cancel in 16O. The modified interaction (F1') and the addition of Bartlet and Heisenberg exchange terms enable application to neutron-rich systems such as 6He, extending beyond the 4N nuclei to which the Tohsaki interaction had previously been limited.
