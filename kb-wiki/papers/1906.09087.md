# Effects of a dense medium on parameters of doubly heavy baryons

> The spectroscopic properties of the doubly heavy spin-$1/2$ baryons $Ξ_{QQ'}$, $Ξ'_{QQ'}$, $Ω_{QQ'}$ and $Ω'_{QQ'}$, with heavy quarks $Q$ and $Q'$ being $b$ or/and $c$, are investigated in cold nuclear matter. In particular, the behavior of the mass of these particles with respect to the density of the medium in the range $ρ\in [0,1.4] ~ρ_{sat}$, with $ρ_{sat}=0.11^3 ~GeV^3$ being the saturation density of nuclear matter, is investigated. From the shifts in the mass and vector self energy of the states under consideration, it is obtained that $Ξ_{QQ'}$ and $Ξ'_{QQ'}$ baryons with two heavy quarks and one $u$ or $d$ quark are affected by the medium, considerably. It is also seen that the $Ω_{QQ'}$ and $Ω'_{QQ'}$ states, containing two heavy quarks and one $s$ quark do not see the dense medium, at all. The value of mass for the $Ξ_{cc}$ state obtained at $ρ\rightarrow 0$ limit is nicely consistent with the experimental data. Our results on parameters of other members can be useful in the search for these states. The obtained results may also shed light on the future in-medium experiments aiming to search for the behavior of the doubly heavy baryons under extreme conditions.

## Key claim

The spectroscopic properties of doubly heavy spin-1/2 baryons (Ξ_QQ', Ξ'_QQ', Ω_QQ', Ω'_QQ') are investigated in cold nuclear matter, with the key finding that baryons containing a u or d light quark (Ξ and Ξ' states) are considerably affected by the medium, while those containing an s quark (Ω and Ω' states) show no sensitivity to the dense medium at all, over the density range ρ ∈ [0, 1.4] ρ_sat.

## Method

The authors employed in-medium QCD sum rules, calculating the correlation function of interpolating currents for symmetric (unprimed) and anti-symmetric (primed) doubly heavy baryon states. The spectral densities were derived from the imaginary parts of the correlation function using in-medium light and heavy quark propagators, with the Borel transformation and continuum subtraction applied to extract the masses and vector self-energies as functions of nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of nuclear matter is taken as ρ_sat = 0.11³ GeV³.
- For the Ξ_cc baryon, the vacuum mass (ρ → 0 limit) is obtained with a Borel window M² ∈ [3–5] GeV² and continuum threshold s₀* ∈ [15.4–17.0] GeV².
- The working Borel mass ranges for different channels: Ξ_cc and Ω_cc: [3–5] GeV²; Ξ_bc and Ω_bc: [6–8] GeV²; Ξ'_bc and Ω'_bc: [6–8] GeV²; Ξ_bb and Ω_bb: [8–12] GeV².
- The continuum threshold windows are set as m_QQ' + 0.3 GeV ≤ √s₀ ≤ m_QQ' + 0.5 GeV for all channels.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of doubly heavy spin-3/2 baryons (Ξ*_QQ' and Ω*_QQ') in cold nuclear matter (PhysRevD.99.074012, 2019) to the spin-1/2 sector. It builds on vacuum studies of doubly heavy baryons using QCD sum rules (Aliev et al., 2012; Wang, 2018) and addresses the limited number of in-medium investigations for these states, following earlier work by Wang (2012). The results are intended to guide future in-medium experiments searching for doubly heavy baryons under extreme conditions, such as those in heavy-ion collisions or neutron star interiors.
