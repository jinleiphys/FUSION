# A new approach to resummation: Parametric Perturbation Theory

> We present a {\sl non--perturbative} method, called {\sl Parametric Perturbation Theory} (PPT), which is alternative to the ordinary perturbation theory. The method relies on a principle of simplicity for the observable solutions, which are constrained to be linear in a certain (unphysical) parameter. The perturbative expansion is carried out in this parameter and not in the physical coupling (as in ordinary perturbation theory). We show that the method is capable to resum the divergent perturbative series, to {\sl extract} the leading asymptotic (strong coupling) behavior and {\sl predict} with high accuracy the coefficients of the perturbative series.

# A new approach to resummation: Parametric Perturbation Theory

## Key claim
Parametric Perturbation Theory (PPT) can resum divergent perturbative series and extract leading strong-coupling behavior by imposing that the observable be linear in an unphysical parameter, rather than expanding in the physical coupling. The method's success depends on the chosen functional relation between the physical coupling and the unphysical parameter, and on the integer parameters that determine the asymptotic behavior of the solution.

## Method
The method introduces an unphysical parameter ρ and a functional relation g = g(ρ) between the physical coupling and ρ, typically a rational function with unknown coefficients. The Principle of Absolute Simplicity requires that the observable be linear in ρ (O = b₀ + b₁ρ). By substituting the g(ρ) relation into the known perturbative series and expanding around ρ = 0, the unknown coefficients are determined by requiring that all nonlinear terms in ρ vanish up to a given order. The integers specifying the numerator and denominator powers in g(ρ) determine the asymptotic behavior of the observable as g → ∞.

## Key numbers
- For the zero-dimensional φ⁴ model: using N̄=3, the transformation g(ρ) yields a denominator zero at ρ₀ = -0.0593, signaling a branch point near g=0.
- For g = -1 in the zero-dimensional φ⁴ model: exact imaginary part Im E(g) = -0.37679; PPT with the first root gives Im E(g)ᵖᵖᵀ₁ = -0.36488.
- For the lattice φ⁴ model in 1+1 dimensions: the set [3,2] predicts perturbative coefficients b₇ through b₁₁ with the largest error 0.11851% for b₁₁; the resummed energy has a branch point at g ≈ -1.025.
- For hard sphere virial coefficients in D=2: the set [3,4] gives ḡ ≈ 1.1625; the set [2,3] predicts B₉ with error about 0.035%.
- For hard sphere virial coefficients in D=3: the set [3,4] gives ḡ ≈ 1.43439; the set [2,3] predicts B₉ with error about 0.54%.

## Context
This work extends the resummation techniques for divergent perturbative series, such as those appearing in φ⁴ field theory and lattice models. The method is applied to a zero-dimensional φ⁴ model previously studied by J. Zinn-Justin, to a 1+1 dimensional lattice φ⁴ model where Nishiyama (2001) used linked cluster expansions and DMRG, and to virial coefficients of hard sphere gases in D=2 and D=3 dimensions studied by Clisby and McCoy (2006). The approach enables prediction of unknown perturbative coefficients and extraction of asymptotic behavior, and can describe multivalued functions and metastable states through complex values of the unphysical parameter.
