# Using EFT to analyze low-energy Compton scattering from protons and light nuclei

> We discuss the application of an effective field theory (EFT) which incorporates the chiral symmetry of QCD to Compton scattering from the proton and deuteron. We describe the chiral EFT analysis of the proton Compton scattering database presented in our recent review (arXiv:1203.6834), which gives: alpha^{(p)}=10.5 +/- 0.5(stat) +/- 0.8(theory); beta^{(p)}= 2.7 +/- 0.5(stat) +/- 0.8(theory), for the electric and magnetic dipole polarizability of the proton. We also summarize the chiral EFT analysis of the world data on coherent Compton scattering from deuterium presented in arXiv:1203.6834. That yields: alpha^{(s)}=10.5 +/- 2.0(stat) +/- 0.8(theory); beta^{(s)}=3.6 +/- 1.0(stat) +/- 0.8(theory).

## Key claim

The paper presents chiral effective field theory (chi-EFT) analyses of Compton scattering from the proton and deuteron, extracting the electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities with statistical and theoretical uncertainties, but the results depend on excluding certain data sets (the Hallin data for the proton) and on a specific fitting strategy that iterates between low-energy and medium-energy regions.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-EFT analysis of the world gamma-proton Compton scattering database, including nucleon Born graphs, t-channel pi-zero pole graphs, Delta-pole graphs with finite width from pi-N loops, and pi-N and pi-Delta loop graphs up to next-to-next-to-leading order in delta-counting. For the deuteron, they used a chi-EFT treatment valid from threshold to about 100 MeV, employing a chi-EFT deuteron wave function at next-to-next-to-leading order, and fit the isoscalar combinations of scalar dipole polarizabilities to the gamma-deuteron elastic data.

## Key numbers

* Proton electric dipole polarizability: alpha_p = 10.5 +/- 0.5 (stat) +/- 0.8 (theory) (units: 10^-4 fm^3)
* Proton magnetic dipole polarizability: beta_p = 2.7 +/- 0.5 (stat) +/- 0.8 (theory) (units: 10^-4 fm^3)
* Isoscalar electric dipole polarizability: alpha_s = 10.5 +/- 2.0 (stat) +/- 0.8 (theory) (units: 10^-4 fm^3)
* Isoscalar magnetic dipole polarizability: beta_s = 3.6 +/- 1.0 (stat) +/- 0.8 (theory) (units: 10^-4 fm^3)
* Baldin sum-rule constraint: alpha_p + beta_p = 13.8 +/- 0.4 (units: 10^-4 fm^3)
* Baldin-constrained fit yields alpha_p - beta_p = 7.7 +/- 0.6 (units: 10^-4 fm^3)
* Delta(1232) mass difference: M_Delta - M_N = 293 MeV
* Ratio of E2 to M1 couplings: b2/b1 = -0.34
* Fitted gamma-N-Delta M1 coupling: b1 = 3.66 +/- 0.03
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom for proton fit (without Hallin data): 106.1/124
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom for deuteron fit: 24.3/24

## Context

This work extends the pioneering chi-EFT calculations of Compton scattering by Bernard et al. (1991, 1995) and Beane et al. (2003, 2005) by including the Delta(1232) as an explicit degree of freedom, which is essential for describing data at photon energies approaching the Delta resonance. The analysis enables precise extraction of nucleon polarizabilities from existing experimental data at MAMI (Mainz), HI-gamma-S at TUNL, and MAX-Lab at Lund, and provides a framework for analyzing future measurements to further constrain these fundamental hadron-structure parameters.
