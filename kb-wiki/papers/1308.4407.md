# Angular Distributions of Higher Order Splitting Functions

> We study the angular distributions of the splitting functions for processes for which a parton splits into three partons. Unlike the case of coherent branching, we find that both in vacuum and in the presence of the dense QCD matter, such collinear splitting functions are neither ordered, nor anti-ordered. In the medium-induced splitting functions the angular distributions are broader compared to the similar vacuum distribution, a feature previously noticed from the lowest order medium-induced splitting functions.

## Key claim

The paper finds that higher-order collinear splitting functions (specifically \(q\rightarrow ggq\)) are neither angular-ordered nor anti-ordered, contradicting the coherent branching picture that holds for soft, long-distance physics; this result applies both in vacuum and in the presence of dense QCD matter, with the caveat that the analysis is performed in the collinear regime and assumes the hard process \(J\) is a pure QCD interaction.

## Method

The authors calculate the \(q\rightarrow ggq\) splitting function in vacuum using SCET (Soft Collinear Effective Theory) in light-cone gauge, reproducing the known results of Catani (1999) and Catani (1998). For the medium-induced case, they compute to first order in opacity using \(\text{SCET}_{\text{G}}\) with a hybrid gauge (collinear gluons in light-cone gauge, Glauber gluons in covariant gauge), summing 19 single-Born and 34 double-Born diagrams. The angular distributions are studied by taking the limit \(z_1 \rightarrow 0\) (soft gluon 1) and examining the resulting antenna functions.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum splitting: initial quark energy \(E_0 = 100\ \text{GeV}\), with \(z_1 = 0.03\), \(z_2 \approx 2/3\), \(z_3 \approx 1/2\); the non-abelian part falls as \(1/\theta_{01}^4\) (ordered) while the abelian part falls as \(1/\theta_{01}^2\) (not ordered).
- Medium-induced splitting: medium parameters \(\mu = 0.75\ \text{GeV}\), \(L = 5\ \text{fm}\), gluon scattering length \(\lambda_g = 1\ \text{fm}\); initial quark energy \(E_0 = 100\ \text{GeV}\).
- Scenario 1: \(z_1 = 0.03\), \(z_2 = 0.643\), \(\theta_{20} = 10^\circ\), \(\theta_{30} = 20^\circ\).
- Scenario 2: \(z_1 = 0.03\), \(z_2 = 0.282\), \(\theta_{20} = 25^\circ\), \(\theta_{30} = 10^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the study of angular distributions of collinear splitting functions first performed in Fickinger (2013) and contrasts with the coherent branching results of Marchesini (1983) and Marchesini (1987), which established angular ordering for soft gluon emission. The medium-induced results show broader angular distributions than vacuum, a feature previously noted for the lowest-order \(q\rightarrow qg\) splitting by Vitev (2005). The findings enable future studies of parton shower phenomenology, particularly identifying observables sensitive to the collinear versus soft regimes and testing the effect of turning angular ordering on and off in shower simulations.
