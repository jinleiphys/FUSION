# Proof of NRQCD Factorization at All Order in Coupling Constant in Heavy Quarkonium Production

> Recently the proof of factorization in heavy quarkonium production in NRQCD color octet mechanism is given at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in coupling constant by using diagrammatic method of QCD. In this paper we prove factorization in heavy quarkonium production in NRQCD color octet mechanism at all order in coupling constant by using path integral method of QCD. Our proof is valid to all powers in the heavy quark relative velocity. We find that the gauge invariance and the factorization at all order in coupling constant require gauge-completed non-perturbative NRQCD matrix elements that were introduced previously to prove factorization at NNLO.

## Key claim

The paper proves that NRQCD factorization for heavy quarkonium production in the color octet mechanism holds at all orders in the coupling constant, not just at NNLO as previously shown, and that this requires gauge-completed non-perturbative NRQCD matrix elements containing light-like Wilson lines in the adjoint representation.

## Method

The author uses the path integral formulation of QCD, extending a prior path integral approach originally developed for QED by Tucci to the non-abelian case. The infrared behavior of non-perturbative NRQCD matrix elements is studied by placing the heavy quark fields in an SU(3) pure gauge background field, which is shown to be equivalent to the effect of soft gluon exchange with a light-like Wilson line. The proof demonstrates that the long-distance behavior of the gauge-completed NRQCD matrix element is independent of the light-like vector \(l^\mu\) at all orders in the coupling constant and to all powers in the heavy quark relative velocity.

## Key numbers

- The NNLO infrared pole contribution to order \(\vec{v}^2\) is \(\Sigma(P,v,l) = \alpha_s^2 \frac{1}{3\epsilon} \frac{\vec{v}^2}{4}\), computed in the rest frame of the heavy quarkonium (\(\vec{P}=0\)) with \(l^\mu = \delta^{\mu -}\).
- The proof is valid to all orders in the coupling constant and to all powers in the heavy quark relative velocity \(v\).

## Context

This work extends the proof of NRQCD factorization from next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in coupling constant, previously established by Nayak, Nayak and Sterman (2010-2015) using diagrammatic methods, to all orders using path integral methods. The path integral approach was originally developed for QED by Tucci and extended to QCD by Nayak (2015). The result enables rigorous factorization calculations for heavy quarkonium production at high energy colliders such as the Tevatron and LHC, and confirms that the gauge-completed NRQCD matrix elements introduced at NNLO are necessary and sufficient at all orders.
