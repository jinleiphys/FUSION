# Open problems in theory of nuclear open quantum systems

> Is there a connection between the branch point singularity at the particle emission threshold and the appearance of cluster states which reveal the structure of a corresponding reaction channel? Which nuclear states are most impacted by the coupling to the scattering continuum? What should be the most important steps in developing the theory that will truly unify nuclear structure and nuclear reactions? The common denominator of these questions is the continuum shell-model approach to bound and unbound nuclear states, nuclear decays, and reactions.

# Open problems in theory of nuclear open quantum systems

## Key claim
The paper argues that branch point singularities at particle emission thresholds and exceptional points (double-poles of the S-matrix) are generic mechanisms that induce configuration mixing and clustering in nuclear states, and that these effects should be present in any open quantum many-body system with complex reaction channels, not just in nuclei.

## Method
The authors use the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) framework, which couples the standard interacting shell model (ISM) Hilbert space to scattering continua via an energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian. They compute phase rigidity and continuum-coupling energy corrections for states in 16Ne and oxygen isotopes to quantify how the coupling to reaction channels mixes bound and unbound eigenfunctions near thresholds and at avoided level crossings.

## Key numbers
- Phase rigidity for the 0+1 and 0+2 states in 16Ne shows two local variations: one near the elastic channel threshold (E=0 MeV) and another at the avoided level crossing at higher energy.
- The exceptional point for 2+3 and 2+4 states in 16Ne appears at E ≈ 4.8 MeV, where phase rigidity drops abruptly to 0.
- Continuum-coupling energy correction E_corr varies from ±4 MeV in 28O to ±2 MeV in 26O and 24O in a broad interval around the neutron emission threshold.
- The contact force strength used for 16Ne calculations is V0 = -1100 MeV fm3 (for 0+ states) and V0 = -1357 MeV fm3 (for 2+ states).
- The Wigner-Bartlett interaction strength for oxygen isotope calculations is V0 = -414 MeV fm3.
- In 11Li, the two-neutron and one-neutron emission thresholds are separated by less than 30 keV.

## Context
This paper extends the continuum shell model framework developed by Feshbach (1958, 1962) and later formulations such as SMEC (Bennaceur, 1999) and the Gamow Shell Model (Michel, 2002, 2003, 2004). It builds on the Ikeda systematics (Ikeda, 1968) of cluster states near decay thresholds and connects threshold phenomena to Wigner's cusp effect (Wigner, 1948) and Baz' threshold laws (Baz, 1957). The work identifies open problems for a unified theory of nuclear structure and reactions, particularly the need to extend GSM to reaction problems via coupled-channel formulations and to develop faster algorithms for calculating overlaps between reaction channels and many-body states.
