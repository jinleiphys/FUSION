# Resonance interpretation of the bump structure in the $γp \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ differential cross section

> We investigate the $Λ(1520)$ photoproduction in the $\vecγ p \to K^+ Λ(1520)$ reaction within the effective Lagrangian method near threshold. In addition to the "background" contributions from the contact, $t-$channel $K$ exchange, and $s-$channel nucleon pole terms, which were already considered in previous works, the contribution from the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ (spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$) is also considered. We show that the inclusion of the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ leads to a fairly good description of the new LEPS differential cross-section data, and that these measurements can be used to determine some of the properties of this latter resonance.

## Key claim

The bump structure observed in the LEPS differential cross section for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1520)$ at forward $K^+$ angles can be described by including the nucleon resonance $N^*(2080)$ (spin-parity $J^P = 3/2^-$), but the authors note that other explanations are also possible, such as a $J^P = 3/2^+$ resonance not listed in the PDG or interference with $\phi$ photoproduction.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams including contact, $t$-channel $K$ exchange, and $s$-channel nucleon pole terms as background, plus an additional $s$-channel $N^*(2080)$ resonance contribution. They performed eight-parameter $\chi^2$ fits to the LEPS differential cross section data at forward $K^+$ angles, fitting the resonance mass, width, hadronic couplings, and electromagnetic couplings, with form factors applied to maintain gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- Fitted $N^*(2080)$ mass: $M_{N^*} = 2115 \pm 8$ MeV
- Fitted $N^*(2080)$ width: $\Gamma_{N^*} = 254 \pm 24$ MeV
- Branching fraction of $N^*(2080)$ decay into $\Lambda(1520)K$: $7.5\% \pm 2.8\%$
- Fitted cut-off parameters: $\Lambda_s = \Lambda_t = 604 \pm 2$ MeV for background terms; $\Lambda_R = 909 \pm 55$ MeV for the resonance term
- Fitted electromagnetic couplings: $ef_1 = 0.177 \pm 0.023$, $ef_2 = -0.082 \pm 0.023$
- Fitted hadronic couplings: $g_1 = 1.4 \pm 0.3$, $g_2 = 5.5 \pm 1.8$
- Data set: 59 data points from LEPS at SPring-8, for photon energies below 2.4 GeV, covering four intervals of $\cos\theta_{\rm C.M.}$ from 1 down to 0.6

## Context

This work extends previous models of $\Lambda(1520)$ photoproduction by Titov (2005), Nam (2005), and Toki (2006), which included only background contributions and failed to describe the forward bump structure in the new LEPS data. The analysis enables determination of properties of the poorly known $N^*(2080)$ resonance, which is listed as a two-star resonance in the PDG. The large $\Lambda(1520)K$ branching fraction found supports predictions from the quark model approach by Simon (1998).
