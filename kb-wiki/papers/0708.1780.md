# Two-particle scattering on the lattice: Phase shifts, spin-orbit coupling, and mixing angles

> We determine two-particle scattering phase shifts and mixing angles for quantum theories defined with lattice regularization. The method is suitable for any nonrelativistic effective theory of point particles on the lattice. In the center-of-mass frame of the two-particle system we impose a hard spherical wall at some fixed large radius. For channels without partial-wave mixing the partial-wave phase shifts are determined from the energies of the nearly-spherical standing waves. For channels with partial-wave mixing further information is extracted by decomposing the standing wave at the wall boundary into spherical harmonics, and we solve coupled-channels equations to extract the phase shifts and mixing angles. The method is illustrated and tested by computing phase shifts and mixing angles on the lattice for spin-1/2 particles with an attractive Gaussian potential containing both central and tensor force parts.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for extracting two-particle scattering phase shifts and mixing angles on the lattice by imposing a hard spherical wall boundary condition in the center-of-mass frame, which avoids artifacts from periodic cubic boundaries that limit Luscher's method at higher energies and orbital angular momenta. The method is demonstrated for spin-1/2 particles with an attractive Gaussian potential containing both central and tensor force parts, but the authors note it is suitable for any nonrelativistic effective theory of point particles on the lattice.

## Method

The authors impose a hard spherical wall at a fixed large radius in the center-of-mass frame of the two-particle system, solving the Schrodinger equation for spherical standing waves that vanish at the wall. For channels without partial-wave mixing, phase shifts are determined directly from the energies of the standing waves using the relation between spherical Bessel functions and the wall radius. For channels with partial-wave mixing, the standing wave at the wall boundary is decomposed into spherical harmonics, and coupled-channels equations are solved to extract the phase shifts and mixing angles using the Stapp parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The test potential parameters are: C = -2 MeV, R0 = 2 x 10^-2 MeV^-1, and m = 938.92 MeV, producing a shallow bound state in the 3S(D)1 channel with energy -0.155 MeV (exact continuum result) and -0.170 MeV (lattice result with wall radius 10+epsilon lattice units).
- The spatial lattice spacing is a = (100 MeV)^-1 and the temporal lattice spacing is a_t = (70 MeV)^-1.
- For the 1S0 state with wall radius 10+epsilon lattice units: free particle energy 0.9280 MeV (k_free = 29.52 MeV), interacting energy 0.6445 MeV (k = 24.60 MeV), giving a phase shift delta(1S0) = 30.0 degrees.
- Lattice results for S=0 and uncoupled S=1 channels are within a few percent of exact results for momenta below 80 MeV, and within 10% to 15% for momenta near 120 MeV.

## Context

This work extends Luscher's method (Luscher, 1985, 1986, 1991) for relating two-body energy levels in finite-volume cubic boxes to infinite-volume scattering matrices, addressing the limitations of that approach at higher energies and for spin-orbit coupling and partial-wave mixing. The method is intended for lattice effective field theory simulations of few- and many-body systems, where the full two-particle spectrum is relatively easy to compute, unlike in lattice quantum chromodynamics where hadron-hadron scattering states well above threshold produce weak Monte Carlo signals. The technique enables direct measurement of phase shifts and mixing angles for higher orbital angular momenta and coupled channels, which previous methods could not accurately extract due to artifacts from periodic cubic boundaries.
