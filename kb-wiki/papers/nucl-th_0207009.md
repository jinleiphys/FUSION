# Quark interchange effects in the KN interaction

> We study the short range repulsion in the KN system due to quark-gluon exchange. Phase shifts for spin-spin, color Coloumb and spin-orbit interactions are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the short-range repulsion in the KN system can be studied via quark-gluon exchange using the Fock-Tani formalism, and presents phase shifts for S- and P-wave partial interactions, though details of the calculation for higher partial waves are shown elsewhere.

## Method

The authors apply the Fock-Tani formalism to transform composite-particle operators into ideal operators that obey canonical (anti)commutation relations, then use a unitary transformation to obtain an effective meson-baryon potential from a microscopic quark-quark Hamiltonian. The potential is specialized to the KN system using a one-gluon exchange potential (OGEP) that includes color Coulomb, spin-spin, and spin-orbit terms. Phase shifts are calculated in the Born approximation from the scattering amplitude derived from the Fock-Tani formalism.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts are presented for partial waves: \(S_{01}\), \(S_{11}\), \(P_{01}\), \(P_{11}\), \(P_{03}\), \(P_{13}\) (notation \(L_{I,2J}\)).
- The phase shift formula uses \(\delta _{l}^{{\rm KN}}=-\frac{2\pi ^{2}P_{cm}E_{K}\,E_{N}}{\ E_{K}+E_{N}}\int_{-1}^{1}d\mu \;h_{fi}^{{\rm KN}}P_{l}(\mu )\), where \(E_{i}=\sqrt{P^2_{cm} +m^2_i}\).
- The OGEP includes terms proportional to \(\alpha_s\) with factors such as \(1/q^2\) for color Coulomb, \(-2/(3m_1m_2) \mathbf{S}_i \cdot \mathbf{S}_j\) for spin-spin, and spin-orbit terms involving \(\mathbf{S}_1 \cdot (\mathbf{q} \times \mathbf{p}_1)/(2m_1^2)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the KN system in the S-wave (cited as "many groups" in the text) to include higher partial waves such as P-waves, using the Fock-Tani formalism developed by previous authors (cited as "ft" and "dimi0"). The approach enables a more detailed investigation of short-range hadronic repulsion in the KN system, which the authors argue is more suitable than other hadronic systems for studying this effect.
