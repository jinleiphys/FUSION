# Randomized Low-Rank Decompositions of Nuclear Three-Body Interactions

> First-principles simulations of many-fermion systems are commonly limited by the computational requirements of processing large data objects. As a remedy, we propose the use of low-rank approximations of three-body interactions, which are the dominant such limitation in nuclear physics. We introduce a novel randomized decomposition technique to handle the excessively large matrix dimensions and study the sensitivity of low-rank properties to interaction details. The developed low-rank three-nucleon interactions are benchmarked in ab initio simulations of few- and many-body systems. Exploiting low-rank properties provides a promising route to extend the microscopic description of atomic nuclei to large systems where storage requirements exceed the computational capacities of the most advanced high-performance computing facilities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three-nucleon (3N) interactions admit excellent low-rank approximations via a randomized singular value decomposition (rSVD), enabling data compression ratios exceeding 100 with negligible errors on few- and many-body observables, provided the decomposition rank is kept at approximately 1% of the original matrix dimension.

## Method

The authors apply a randomized SVD algorithm to partial-wave-decomposed momentum-space matrix elements of chiral three-nucleon interactions, using 15 mesh points for each of the two Jacobi momenta and including intermediate two-body quantum numbers up to J_max=8. The low-rank 3N interactions are then benchmarked in Faddeev calculations for the triton and in in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) calculations at the IMSRG(2) level for closed-shell nuclei from 16O to 132Sn, using the "1.8/2.0" and "EMN450" chiral interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the triton, keeping the leading 1% of singular values yields absolute differences of 10^-4 MeV for the binding energy and 10^-4 fm for the point-proton radius.
- The 3N contribution to the triton binding energy is 0.19 MeV for the "1.8/2.0" interaction and 0.67 MeV for the "EMN450" interaction.
- Matrix dimensions among partial-wave channels range from N=7650 (for J^Pi T = 1/2^+ 3/2) up to N=40050 (for J^Pi T = 5/2^+ 1/2).
- For medium-mass nuclei up to 132Sn, the relative error on ground-state energies is well below 0.1% when keeping 0.2% to 1.0% of singular values, with errors at the few hundred keV level for the heaviest systems.
- A relative SVD-rank of 1.0% corresponds to a compression ratio C of approximately 100; a relative SVD-rank of 0.1% corresponds to C of approximately 1000.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of low-rank approximations for nucleon-nucleon interactions by Tichai et al. (2021) and Zhu et al. (2021) to the three-body sector, which poses the dominant computational bottleneck in nuclear ab initio calculations. The randomized decomposition technique, based on the algorithm of Halko et al. (2011), overcomes the limitations of traditional SVD algorithms for the large matrix dimensions encountered in three-body interactions. The results enable new efficient storage formats for many-body operators and motivate the development of factorized many-body solvers that could extend microscopic descriptions to larger nuclear systems beyond current high-performance computing capabilities.
