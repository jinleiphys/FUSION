# Thermalization of the world's smallest fluids: recent developments

> The late Gerry Brown was not shy to tackle complex scientific problems that took time to play out but yielded in the end a deeper understanding of many-body phenomena. In this note, prepared for a memorial volume in his honor, we provide a perspective on a couple of outstanding scientific puzzles that have their origin in our understanding of the thermalization of matter in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, and possibly, in high multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions proceeds through a universal nonthermal fixed point characterized by self-similar scaling behavior, but notes that this weak-coupling picture may be fragile when extended to later times and that the applicability of hydrodynamics at very early times (less than 1 fm) remains an open question.

## Method

The author reviews classical-statistical Yang-Mills simulations of longitudinally expanding non-Abelian plasmas, where the gauge fields are initialized with Gaussian-distributed fluctuations on top of classical Color Glass Condensate (CGC) background fields. The evolution of the system is followed by solving 3+1-D classical Yang-Mills equations, and single-particle distributions are extracted to study scaling behavior. The results are interpreted within the framework of wave turbulence and compared to the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001).

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_S is the dynamical (semi-)hard scale at which maximal occupancy of 1/alpha_S is attained for a given x
- At proper time tau ~ 1/Q_S, the system is highly anisotropic with P_T^x = P_T^y = epsilon/2 and P_L = 0
- The classical-statistical simulations use alpha_S = 10^{-5} corresponding to Q*tau_0 ~ 100
- The scaling exponents extracted from lattice simulations are beta ~ 0, gamma ~ 1/3, and alpha ~ -2/3
- The ratio P_L/P_T decreases as tau^{-2/3} in the classical regime, not as tau^{-2} as in free-streaming
- The classical regime lasts until Q*tau ~ alpha_S^{-3/2}
- In the bottom-up scenario, thermalization occurs at Q*tau = alpha_S^{-13/5} at a temperature T = alpha_S^{1/2} * Q
- For alpha_S = 1/50, the lifetime of the classical regime is Q*tau_class ~ 350
- The Glasma graph contribution in high multiplicity proton-proton events is enhanced by alpha_S^{-8}, corresponding to a factor of ~10^5 for typical alpha_S values

## Context

This perspective piece extends the author's earlier work on CGC and Glasma dynamics (Dusling 2012, Berges 2013) and directly addresses the thermalization problem in heavy ion collisions. It contrasts the classical-statistical approach of Berges et al. (2013) with the alternative numerical simulations of Gelis et al. (2013) that suggest rapid isotropization. The paper enables future work on matching classical field dynamics to kinetic theory or hydrodynamics, and on interpreting the "ridge" phenomenon in small collision systems (p+p, p+A) as either initial-state CGC effects or final-state hydrodynamic flow.
