# Effect of deuteron breakup on the deuteron-$Ξ$ correlation function

> The hadron-deuteron correlation function has attracted many interests as a potential method to access the three-hadron interactions. However, the weakly-bound nature of deuteron has not been considered in the preceding studies. In this study, the breakup effect of deuteron on the deuteron-$Ξ^-$ ($d$-$Ξ^-$) correlation function $C_{dΞ^-}$ is investigated. The $d$-$Ξ^-$ scattering is described by a nucleon-nucleon-$Ξ$ three-body reaction model. The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method, which is a fully quantum-mechanical and non-perturbative reaction model, is adopted. $C_{dΞ^-}$ turns out to be sensitive to the strong interaction and enhanced by the deuteron breakup effect by 6--8 % for the $d$-$Ξ^-$ relative momentum below about 70 MeV/$c$. Low-lying neutron-neutron continuum states are responsible for this enhancement. Within the adopted model, the deuteron breakup effect on $C_{dΞ^-}$ is found to be appreciable but not very significant. Except for the enhancement by several percent, studies on $C_{dΞ^-}$ without the deuteron breakup effect can be justified.

## Key claim

The deuteron breakup effect enhances the deuteron-Ξ⁻ correlation function by 6–8% for relative momenta below about 70 MeV/c, but within the adopted three-body model this effect is "appreciable but not very significant," meaning that studies neglecting breakup can be justified except for a several percent enhancement.

## Method

The authors describe the d-Ξ⁻ scattering using a nucleon-nucleon-Ξ three-body reaction model and apply the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), a fully quantum-mechanical and non-perturbative approach. They employ the Argonne V4' nucleon-nucleon interaction and the N-Ξ interaction obtained from lattice QCD by the HAL QCD Collaboration, including only s-wave channels for both the NN relative motion and the Ξ relative motion to the NN center-of-mass. The correlation function is computed by integrating the squared three-body scattering wave function with a Gaussian source function of size parameter b = 1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron breakup effect enhances C_dΞ⁻ by 6–8% for d-Ξ⁻ relative momentum between 30 and 70 MeV/c.
- The enhancement drops below 1% for relative momenta above 100 MeV/c.
- The NN continuum states are truncated at k_max = 2.0 fm⁻¹ (approximately 400 MeV/c) to achieve convergence.
- The bin size for the nn (³¹S₀) continuum is set to 0.005 fm⁻¹ (approximately 1 MeV/c) to obtain a smooth correlation function.
- The source size parameter b is taken as 1.2 fm, with additional calculations shown for b = 1.6 fm and b = 3.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies of hadron-hadron interactions, which have been applied to pairs such as pΛ, ΛΛ, pΞ⁻, and pΩ by ALICE and STAR, to the hadron-deuteron case. Previous exploratory studies of pd, K⁻d, Λd, and Ω⁻d correlation functions by Morita and Sako (2020), Haidenbauer and collaborators, and Etminan and collaborators ignored deuteron breakup effects, sometimes conjecturing they could be absorbed into an effective source size. The present calculation provides a benchmark for the magnitude of breakup effects and enables future work to incorporate three-body source functions and more realistic treatments of the NNΞ system.
