# Navier-Stokes, Gross-Pitaevskii and Generalized Diffusion Equations using Stochastic Variational Method

> The stochastic variational method is applied to particle systems and continuum mediums. As the brief review of this method, we first discuss the application to particle Lagrangians and derive a diffusion-type equation and the Schrödinger equation with the minimum gauge coupling. We further extend the application of the stochastic variational method to Lagrangians of continuum mediums and show that the Navier-Stokes, Gross-Pitaevskii and generalized diffusion equations are derived. The correction term for the Navier-Stokes equation is also obtained in this method. We discuss the meaning of this correction by comparing with the diffusion equation.

# Navier-Stokes, Gross-Pitaevskii and Generalized Diffusion Equations using Stochastic Variational Method

## Key claim

The stochastic variational method (SVM) can derive the Navier-Stokes, Gross-Pitaevskii, and generalized diffusion equations from continuum medium Lagrangians, but the derivation assumes that microscopic degrees of freedom can be approximated by Gaussian white noise and that there is a clear separation of microscopic and macroscopic time scales; the resulting Navier-Stokes equation contains a correction term whose physical meaning is discussed by comparison with the diffusion equation.

## Method

The authors apply the stochastic variational method to continuum medium Lagrangians expressed in Lagrangian coordinates, where each fluid element's trajectory follows forward and backward stochastic differential equations with Gaussian white noise. They replace the classical kinetic term in the action with mean forward and/or backward derivatives, then perform stochastic variation to obtain equations of motion for the mean velocity field and density. The consistency condition relating forward and backward drift velocities is enforced through the Fokker-Planck equations for the probability density.

## Key numbers

- The noise magnitude is characterized by a constant parameter ν, with the Wiener process satisfying E[dW_i dW_j] = δ_ij dt
- The Schrödinger equation is recovered from SVM when ν = ħ/(2m), with the wave function defined as ψ = √ρ e^{iθ}
- For the case of a charged particle in gauge fields, the same relation ν = ħ/(2m) yields the Schrödinger equation with minimal coupling: iħ ∂_t ψ = [(1/(2m))(-iħ∇ - (e/c)A)^2 + V + eφ] ψ
- The correction term to the Navier-Stokes equation involves the parameter ν and spatial derivatives of the density and velocity fields

## Context

This work extends the stochastic variational method, previously applied exclusively to particle Lagrangians by Yasue (1981) and Hasegawa, to continuum mediums. It builds on prior derivations showing that SVM yields diffusion-type equations and the Schrödinger equation for particle systems, and follows the authors' earlier report (Koide and Kodama, 2011) on deriving the Navier-Stokes equation for compressible fluids. The results enable a unified variational framework for dissipative fluid dynamics and quantum mechanics, and suggest a connection between the correction term in the Navier-Stokes equation and the diffusion equation that may be explored in future work on non-equilibrium systems.
