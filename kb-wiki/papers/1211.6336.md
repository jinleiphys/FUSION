# Kaonic atoms and in-medium K-N amplitudes II: interplay between theory and phenomenology

> A microscopic kaonic-atom optical potential $V^{(1)}_{K^-}$ is constructed, using the Ikeda-Hyodo-Weise NLO chiral $K^-N$ subthreshold scattering amplitudes constrained by the kaonic hydrogen SIDDHARTA measurement, and incorporating Pauli correlations within the Waas-Rho-Weise generalization of the Ericson-Ericson multiple-scattering approach. Good fits to kaonic atom data over the entire periodic table require additionally sizable $K^-NN$--motivated absorptive and dispersive phenomenological terms, in agreement with our former analysis based on a post-SIDDHARTA in-medium chirally-inspired NLO separable model by Cieplý and Smejkal. Such terms are included by introducing a phenomenological potential $V^{(2)}_{K^-}$ and coupling it self consistently to $V^{(1)}_{K^-}$. Properties of resulting kaonic atom potentials are discussed with special attention paid to the role of $K^-$-nuclear absorption and to the extraction of density-dependent amplitudes representing $K^-$ multi-nucleon processes.

## Key claim

Good fits to kaonic atom data across the periodic table require sizable phenomenological $K^-NN$-motivated absorptive and dispersive terms added to the microscopic single-nucleon optical potential, even when using the most recent chiral $K^-N$ amplitudes constrained by the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement; the single-nucleon potential alone cannot reproduce the experimental strong-interaction level shifts and widths.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic kaonic-atom optical potential $V^{(1)}_{K^-}$ using the Ikeda-Hyodo-Weise (IHW) NLO chiral $K^-N$ subthreshold scattering amplitudes, incorporating Pauli correlations via the Waas-Rho-Weise (WRW) generalization of the Ericson-Ericson multiple-scattering approach. They added a phenomenological potential $V^{(2)}_{K^-}$ with linear and power-law density terms to represent multi-nucleon processes, and coupled the two potentials self-consistently through the subthreshold energy-density relation. Global fits to 65 data points for strong-interaction observables in kaonic atoms across the periodic table were performed by varying the parameters of $V^{(2)}_{K^-}$.

## Key numbers

- Best fit with Im $b$ = 0 gave $\chi^2$ = 118 for 65 data points, with parameters Re $b$ = $-0.34 \pm 0.07$ fm, Re $B$ = $1.94 \pm 0.16$ fm, Im $B$ = $0.83 \pm 0.16$ fm, and exponent $\alpha$ = 1.2
- The IHW-based potential for Ni has central values $V_R(0)$ = $-191$ MeV and $V_I(0)$ = $-79$ MeV, with r.m.s. radii $r_R$ = 3.34 fm and $r_I$ = 3.73 fm (point-proton r.m.s. radius of Ni is 3.69 fm)
- The subthreshold energy downward shift at 50% of central nuclear density remains unchanged at 40 MeV below threshold
- For kaonic Ni, removing the 1$N$ absorption depletes the upper $5g$ level width by 60% (from 2.7 eV to 1.1 eV), while removing the $mN$ absorption depletes it by only 27% (to 2.0 eV); for the lower $4f$ level, removing 1$N$ absorption reduces width by 26% (from 0.97 keV to 0.72 keV), while removing $mN$ absorption nearly doubles the width (to 1.54 keV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Friedman and Gal, 2012) which used the Cieplý-Smejkal NLO30 separable model, by instead employing the IHW free-space amplitudes that are free of phenomenologically adjusted momentum-space form factors. The present approach achieves significantly better fits ($\chi^2$ = 118 versus 148 for NLO30) by including $V^{(2)}_{K^-}$ in the self-consistent procedure from the outset, creating implicit coupling between the single-nucleon and multi-nucleon components. The results enable extraction of density-dependent amplitudes representing $K^-$ multi-nucleon processes and demonstrate that multi-nucleon absorption dominates at densities above half nuclear matter density, while single-nucleon absorption dominates at larger radii.
