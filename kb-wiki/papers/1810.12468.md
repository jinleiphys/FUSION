# Swimming and swirling colorful ghosts

> We show that the ideal fluid limit, defined as the existance of a flow frame $u_μ$ with respect to which the fluid is homogeneus and isotropic, and the consequent independence of the equation of state on $u_μ$, is incompatible with non-Abelian gauge theory. Instead, the equation of state becomes dependent on $u_μ$ via modes which are roughly equivalent to ghost modes in the hydrodynamic limit. These modes can be physically imagined as a field of 'purcell swimmers' whose 'arms and legs' are outstretched in Gauge space. Also, vorticity should couple to the Wilson loop via the chromo-electro-magnetic field tensor, which in this limit is not a 'force' but instead represents the polarization tensor of the gluons. We show that because of this coupling vorticity also aquires swirling non-hydrodynamic modes. We then argue that these swirling and swimming non-hydodynamic modes are the manifestation of gauge redunancy within local equilibrium, and speculate on their role in quark-gluon plasma thermalization

## Key claim

The ideal fluid limit, defined by the existence of a flow frame \(u_\mu\) with respect to which the fluid is homogeneous and isotropic, is incompatible with non-Abelian gauge theory when local color charge density is non-zero; the equation of state becomes dependent on \(u_\mu\) via modes roughly equivalent to ghost modes in the hydrodynamic limit.

## Method

The author treats hydrodynamics as a bottom-up effective theory based on symmetries, extending the Lagrangian formulation of ideal fluids (shift symmetry and volume-preserving diffeomorphisms) to include non-Abelian gauge symmetry. The chemical shift symmetry is generalized from a \(U(1)\) phase to a matrix \(y_{ab}\) representing color charge chemical potentials, and gauge invariance is imposed on the free energy. The resulting Lagrangian is expanded around the hydrostatic limit to identify non-hydrodynamic modes arising from gauge redundancy.

## Key numbers

- The three relevant length scales are given as: \(L_{color}^{-1} \sim \{ Q_s - \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} \}\), \(L_{mfp}^{-1} \sim \{ \lambda^2 T - 4\pi T - \tau_0^{-1} \sim Q_s \}\), and \(L_{debye}^{-1} \sim \{ \sqrt{\lambda} T - 3.4\pi T \}\), where \(Q_s\) is the saturation momentum, \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) the QCD non-perturbative scale, \(T\) the temperature, and \(\lambda\) the 't Hooft coupling.
- For pp collisions, Bjorken formula extrapolations give: \(\order{0.1}\tau_0 R^2 T^3 \sim dN/dy\), \(R \sim \order{1}\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(dN/dy \sim \order{10-50}\), and \(Q_s \sim \order{1}\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The transverse size of color domains in the initial state is estimated as 0.5–0.7 times the baryonic transverse size if the semiclassical regime is not reached.

## Context

This work extends the Lagrangian approach to ideal hydrodynamics (Nicolis et al., 2015; shift symmetry) to non-Abelian gauge theories, contrasting with prior Vlasov-type approaches (Jackiw, 1980; Wong, 1970; Heinz, 1980) that couple a thermalized fluid to a coherent classical Yang-Mills field. The paper argues that color charge density can be non-zero in the initial stages of quark-gluon plasma evolution, and that this regime requires a hydrodynamic theory where gauge redundancy introduces non-hydrodynamic "swimming" and "swirling" ghost modes. This enables future investigation of whether such modes affect quark-gluon plasma thermalization and whether they resolve paradoxes related to color neutralization at large scales (Alkofer, 2000; Linde, 1980).
