# Pion correlation from Skyrmion-AntiSkyrmion annihilation

> We study two pion correlations from Skyrmion and antiSkyrmion collision, using the product ansatz and an approximate random grooming method for nucleon projection. The spatial-isospin coupling inherent in the Skyrme model, along with empirical averages, leads to correlations not only among pions of like charges but also among unlike charge types.

## Key claim

Pion correlations in nucleon-antinucleon annihilation arise not only from thermal or statistical Bose-Einstein effects but also from the spatial-isospin coupling inherent in the Skyrme model, producing correlations among both like-charge and unlike-charge pions, even without full dynamical evolution or final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors approximate a Skyrmion-antiSkyrmion collision at the point of closest approach using the product ansatz, with no subsequent pion dynamics. They construct a quantum coherent state from the classical pion field of the moving hedgehog and antihedgehog, then introduce spin and isospin by random grooming (averaging over independent random orientations of the Skyrmion and antiSkyrmion). The two-pion correlation functions are computed for both Galilean and Lorentz-boosted hedgehogs, with an average over impact parameter using an exponential weight.

## Key numbers

- The annihilation time scale is estimated as \( T_c = 1/m \), where \( m \) is the pion mass.
- The rotation period of the hedgehog is estimated as \( T_r \approx 1/m \sqrt{M/m} \), giving a ratio \( T_r/T_c \sim \sqrt{M/m} \approx 3 \); an alternative estimate gives \( T_r/T_c \sim M/m \approx 7 \).
- The impact parameter averaging uses a probability \( e^{-\lambda b} \) with \( \lambda = 1\ \text{fm}^{-1} \).
- The correlation function \( R_2 \) is studied for two incident velocities: \( v = 0.1c \) and \( v = 0.9c \).
- The range over which \( R_2 \) differs significantly from unity is \( 1\ \text{fm}^{-1} \), set by the chosen scale parameters \( \mu = \lambda = 1\ \text{fm}^{-1} \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous large-\( N_c \) QCD studies of nucleon-antinucleon annihilation (Lu and Amado, 1995) by incorporating spin and isospin via random grooming, moving beyond the spherically symmetric initial baryon density used earlier. It contrasts with the full classical Skyrmion dynamics calculations of Seki (1995) and with the thermal decoherence picture of Bose-Einstein correlations. The results enable future studies of quantum corrections, time-dependent rotating Skyrmions, and pion interactions in annihilation, and may help explain experimental findings such as the two-pion charge-spin asymmetry observed by Hasan (1995) and Myhrer (1995).
