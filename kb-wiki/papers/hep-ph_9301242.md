# Nonabelian Berry Phases in Baryons

> We show how generic nonabelian gauge fields can be induced in baryons when a hierarchy of fast degrees of freedom is integrated out. We identify them with nonabelian Berry potentials and discuss their role in transmuting quantum numbers in bag and soliton models of baryons. The resulting baryonic spectra for both light and heavy quark systems are generic and resemble closely the excitation spectrum of diatomic molecules. The symmetry restoration in the system, i.e., the electronic rotational invariance in diatomic molecules, the heavy-quark symmetry in heavy baryons etc. is interpreted in terms of the vanishing of nonabelian Berry potentials that otherwise govern the hyperfine splitting.

## Key claim

Nonabelian Berry potentials arise generically in baryons when fast degrees of freedom (sea quarks or heavy mesons) are integrated out, and these potentials govern the hyperfine splitting and quantum number transmutation in both light and heavy quark systems; the symmetry restoration observed in heavy-quark baryons corresponds to the vanishing of these nonabelian Berry potentials, analogous to electronic rotational invariance in diatomic molecules at large internuclear distances.

## Method

The authors integrate out fast degrees of freedom (Dirac sea quarks in the topological chiral bag model, or heavy meson degrees of freedom in soliton-heavy meson systems) using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation, treating the slow adiabatic rotation of the solitonic cloud or the heavy meson as the slow variable. They derive the induced nonabelian Berry potential from the mixing of degenerate quark levels in each grand-spin K-band, obtaining an effective Hamiltonian on the SU(2) group manifold that resembles a spinning particle coupled to an instanton-like gauge field. The spectrum is then constructed by quantizing the system using canonical momenta and identifying the conserved angular momentum and isospin operators.

## Key numbers

- The induced Berry charge for a quark in the K=1 band is given by \( g_K = \frac{1}{K}\left( \frac{1}{1+y} \right) - \frac{1}{K+1}\left(\frac{y}{1+y} \right) \), where y is a function of spherical Bessel functions \( j_K \) evaluated at \( x = \omega R \) (the lowest energy solution for fixed K and parity) and the chiral angle F at the bag surface.
- The effective Hamiltonian for a singly excited quark takes the form \( H^* = \epsilon_K {\bf 1} + \frac{1}{8\I}\left(\vec{R}^2 -2g_K\vec{R}\cdot\vec{T}_K + g_K^2\vec{T}_K^2\right) \), where \( \I \) is the moment of inertia of the bag and \( \vec{T}_K \) are the generators in the K representation.
- The field strength tensor vanishes for \( g_K = 0 \) (trivial case) and for \( g_K = 2 \) (pure gauge), but the authors state that \( g_K \) cannot reach 2 in their system.
- For the diatomic molecule example, the Berry connection yields a magnetic field \( \vec{B} = -\Omega\frac{\vec{N}}{R^3} \sigma_3 \), corresponding to a Dirac monopole with charge \( eg = -\Omega \), and the effective rotational Hamiltonian is \( H^{eff} = \frac{1}{2MR^2}(\vec{J}^2 - \Omega^2) \).

## Context

This work extends the concept of Berry phases from simple quantum systems (Berry 1984) and the nonabelian generalization by Wilczek and Zee (1984) to models of strong interaction physics, specifically the topological chiral bag model (Brown and Rho) and soliton-heavy meson systems. The authors show that the induced gauge structures produce mass relations identical to those in the Callan-Klebanov model of hyperon skyrmions (Callan and Klebanov), and that the heavy-quark symmetry discovered by Isgur and Wise corresponds to the vanishing of the nonabelian Berry potentials. The work enables a unified description of baryon spectra across light and heavy quark sectors, with the generic spectrum resembling that of diatomic molecules, and opens the possibility of interpreting symmetry restoration in terms of gauge field dynamics.
