# Highlights from the PHENIX experiment

> PHENIX has performed an extensive study on the evolution of medium effects from small to large systems. PHENIX has continued searching for Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in small systems by measuring collectivity, modification of light hadron and quarkonia production, and jet substructure. In large systems, detailed studies on the property of the QGP have been done using direct photon, $π^{0}$-hadron correlation, heavy-flavor electron, and $J/ψ$ flow with a large statistics of data collected in 2014. This report covers new results from the PHENIX experiment in various collision systems.

## Key claim
PHENIX reports new results on the evolution of medium effects across collision system sizes, claiming evidence for Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) signatures in small systems through measurements of collectivity, modified hadron and quarkonia production, and jet substructure, while in large systems the properties of QGP are studied using direct photon, pi0-hadron correlation, heavy-flavor electron, and J/psi flow data from the 2014 dataset.

## Method
The PHENIX experiment analyzed data from various collision systems at RHIC, measuring collective flow patterns, modification of light hadron and quarkonium production yields, and jet substructure modifications. For large systems, the analysis used high-statistics data collected in 2014 to study direct photon emission, pi0-hadron correlations, heavy-flavor electron production, and J/psi elliptic flow as probes of QGP properties.

## Context
This work extends the PHENIX program's systematic study of medium effects from small to large collision systems, continuing the search for QGP signatures in small systems that was pioneered by earlier RHIC experiments. The results enable comparisons between small-system collectivity measurements and large-system QGP properties, addressing open questions about the minimum system size required for QGP formation. The paper was presented at Quark Matter 2022.
