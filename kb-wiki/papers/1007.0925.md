# One pion production in neutrino reactions: including non-resonant background

> We investigate neutrino induced one pion production on nucleons. The elementary neutrino--nucleon cross section is calculated as the sum of the leading Delta pole diagram and several background diagrams obtained within the non-linear sigma model. This approach does not introduce any new adjustable parameters, which allows unambiguous predictions for the observables. Considering electroproduction experiments as benchmark, the model is shown to be applicable up to pion-nucleon invariant mass $W<1.4\GeV$ and provides a good accuracy. With respect to the total one pion cross section, the model predicts the background at the level of $=10% for the $pπ^+$, 30% for $pπ^0$ and 50% for $nπ^+$ final states. The results are compared with experimental data for various differential cross sections. Distributions with respect to muon-nucleon and muon-pion invariant masses are presented for the first time. The model describes the data quite well, with the discrepancies being of the same order as those between different data sets.

## Key claim

The model predicts the non-resonant background contribution to neutrino-induced one-pion production at the level of 10% for the \(p\pi^+\) final state, 30% for \(p\pi^0\), and 50% for \(n\pi^+\), but this result is conditional on the model being applicable only up to a pion-nucleon invariant mass \(W < 1.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and on the use of a specific fit for the axial form factor \(C_5^A\) that does not satisfy the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Method

The elementary neutrino-nucleon cross section was calculated as the coherent sum of a leading Delta pole diagram and six background diagrams (crossed Delta pole, nucleon pole, crossed nucleon pole, contact term, pion pole, and pion in flight) obtained from the \(SU(2)\) nonlinear sigma model. The model introduced no new adjustable parameters, using known form factors from electroproduction data and a dipole parameterization for the axial form factors, with the vector form factors taken from Lalakulich (2006) and the axial form factors from Hernandez (2007). The hadronic current was constructed with phenomenological form factors for the nucleon vertex and a \(\rho\)-meson dominance factor for the contact term and pion pole, and the model was validated against electroproduction data before application to neutrino reactions.

## Key numbers

- Background contribution to the total one-pion cross section: 10% for the \(p\pi^+\) final state, 30% for \(p\pi^0\), and 50% for \(n\pi^+\) (at unspecified neutrino energy).
- Axial form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(C_5^A(0) = 0.867\), fitted to the ANL cross section, with axial mass \(M_A = 0.985\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- Vector mass parameter: \(M_V = 0.84\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for the dipole function in the vector form factors.
- Axial mass for nucleon form factors: \(M_A = 0.999\ \mathrm{GeV}\) from Kuzmin (2007).
- Model applicability limit: pion-nucleon invariant mass \(W < 1.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\), validated against electroproduction data at electron energies \(E_e = 1.884\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(E_e = 2.238\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- Integrated cross section for the \(n\pi^+\) channel at \(E_\nu = 1.6\ \mathrm{GeV}\): \(0.12 \times 10^{-38}\ \mathrm{cm}^2\) (full model) versus \(0.08 \times 10^{-38}\ \mathrm{cm}^2\) (HNV result).

## Context

This work extends the HNV model of Hernandez (2007) by applying it to both electron and neutrino scattering on nucleons, providing the first predictions for muon-nucleon and muon-pion invariant mass distributions. It contradicts the assumption in earlier works by Rein (1980), Alvarez-Ruso (1998), and Paschos (2003) that there is no background in the isospin-3/2 channel for \(\Delta^{++}\) production, though it confirms that the background in that channel is small (10%). The model enables improved treatment of pion production in the GiBUU transport model of Leitner (2008) and provides a benchmark for future neutrino-nucleus calculations, with nuclear interactions to be addressed in a forthcoming publication.
