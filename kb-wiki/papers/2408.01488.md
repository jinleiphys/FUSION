# Restricting Sterile Neutrinos by Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> The bounds on parameters of the eV and higher scale sterile neutrinos from the $0νββ$ decay have been refined and updated. We present a simple and compact analytic expression for the bound in the $Δm^2_{41} - \sin^2 2θ_{14}$ plane, which includes all relevant parameters. Dependencies of the bound on unknown CP-phases and the type of mass spectrum of light neutrinos (mass ordering and level of degeneracy) are studied in detail. We have computed the bounds using the latest and most stringent data from KamLAND-Zen. The projected constraints from future experiments are estimated. The obtained bounds are confronted with positive indications of the presence of sterile neutrinos as well as with the other existing bounds. The $0νββ$ decay results exclude the regions of parameters implied by BEST and Neutrino-4, and the regions indicated by LSND and MiniBooNE are in conflict with $0νββ$ results combined with $ν_μ-$ disappearance bounds.

## Key claim

The bounds on sterile neutrino parameters from neutrinoless double beta decay are refined and updated, showing that the KamLAND-Zen data exclude the parameter regions implied by BEST and Neutrino-4, while the LSND and MiniBooNE regions are in conflict when combined with muon neutrino disappearance bounds; the strength of the bound depends on the unknown CP phase difference Delta and the mass spectrum of light neutrinos.

## Method

The authors derive a compact analytic expression relating the sterile neutrino mass-squared difference Delta m^2_41 and mixing sin^2 2theta_14 to the effective Majorana mass m_betabeta from 0nubb decay and the contribution of the three active neutrinos |m_ee|. They compute bounds using the latest KamLAND-Zen limit on m_betabeta (28-122 meV at 90% C.L., depending on nuclear matrix element uncertainty) and study the dependence on the unknown relative phase Delta between the active and sterile contributions, as well as on the mass ordering and absolute mass scale of the three light neutrinos. The bounds are then confronted with the parameter regions favored by the Gallium anomaly, BEST, Neutrino-4, LSND, and MiniBooNE experiments.

## Key numbers

- KamLAND-Zen bound on effective Majorana mass: m_betabeta < (28 - 122) meV at 90% C.L., where the interval reflects nuclear matrix element uncertainty
- For normal mass hierarchy with m_1 = 0, the maximal |m_ee| = 3.7 meV, and the conservative bound extrapolates as Delta m^2_41 = 25.3 eV^2 / (sin^2 2theta_14)^2
- For normal ordering with m_1 = 30 meV, |m_ee|^max = 31.0 meV and |m_ee|^min = 9.6 meV
- For inverted hierarchy with m_3 = 0, |m_ee|^max = 48.3 meV and |m_ee|^min = 18.3 meV
- For m_betabeta = 28 meV and Delta m^2_41 = 10 eV^2, the upper bounds on sin^2 2theta_14 are 0.04 (normal hierarchy), 0.07 (normal ordering), and 0.10 (inverted hierarchy)
- The 0nubb bound dominates over oscillation bounds at Delta m^2_41 > (4 - 10) eV^2

## Context

This work refines and updates earlier constraints on sterile neutrinos from 0nubb decay (Benes 2005, Goswami 2005, Giunti 2012, Jang 2018) by using the latest KamLAND-Zen data (Abe 2024) and incorporating the cosmological bound on the sum of neutrino masses from Planck (2018). The derived bounds are more stringent than prior studies and are confronted with positive signals from LSND (2001), MiniBooNE (2020), BEST (2022), and Neutrino-4 (2020), as well as with disappearance bounds from experiments such as Daya Bay (2024), PROSPECT (2024), and KATRIN (2022). The results enable future experiments like LEGEND-1000 (projected m_betabeta <= 10 meV) to further test the sterile neutrino parameter space.
