# Modification of triaxial deformation and change of spectrum in $^{25}_{\ Λ}$Mg caused by $Λ$ hyperon

> The positive-parity states of $^{25}_{\ Λ}$Mg with a $Λ$ hyperon in $s$ orbit were studied with the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei. We discuss two bands of $^{25}_{\ Λ}$Mg corresponding to the $K^π=0^+$ and $2^+$ bands of $^{24}$Mg. It is found that the energy of the $K^π= 2^+ \otimes Λ_s$ band is shifted up by about 200 keV compared to $^{24}$Mg. This is because the $Λ$ hyperon in $s$ orbit reduces the quadrupole deformation of the $K^π= 0^+ \otimes Λ_s$ band, while it does not change the deformation of the $K^π= 2^+ \otimes Λ_s$ band significantly.

## Key claim

The excitation energy of the \(K^\pi = 2^+ \otimes \Lambda_s\) band in \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg is shifted upward by about 200 keV compared to the corresponding \(K^\pi = 2^+\) band in \(^{24}\)Mg, but only because the \(\Lambda\) hyperon reduces the quadrupole deformation of the \(K^\pi = 0^+ \otimes \Lambda_s\) band while leaving the deformation of the \(K^\pi = 2^+ \otimes \Lambda_s\) band essentially unchanged.

## Method

The authors applied the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics for hypernuclei (HyperAMD) combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg. They performed variational calculations for the core nucleus \(^{24}\)Mg under constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\), then added a \(\Lambda\) hyperon in the \(s\) orbit and varied the hyperon wave function at each grid point on the \(\beta\)-\(\gamma\) plane. Three-dimensional angular momentum projection and \(K\)-mixing were carried out, followed by GCM superposition of states with different deformations to obtain the final wave functions and excitation spectra.

## Key numbers

* The excitation energy of the \(K^\pi = 2^+ \otimes \Lambda_s\) band is shifted up systematically by about \(\Delta E_x = 200\) keV relative to the \(K^\pi = 2^+\) band of \(^{24}\)Mg.
* The \(\Lambda\) binding energy \(B_\Lambda\) for the \(K^\pi = 0^+ \otimes \Lambda_s\) band is 15.98 MeV (for the \(1/2^+_1\) state), while for the \(K^\pi = 2^+ \otimes \Lambda_s\) band it is 15.80 MeV (for the \(3/2^+_2\) state); the difference is about 200 keV.
* The peak of the GCM overlap for the \(2^+_1\) state of \(^{24}\)Mg is at \((\beta, \gamma) = (0.48, 21^\circ)\); for the \(3/2^+_1\) state of \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg it shifts to \((\beta, \gamma) = (0.43, 15^\circ)\), while for the \(3/2^+_2\) state it remains at \((0.48, 21^\circ)\).
* The intra-band \(B(E2)\) values change by less than 10% due to the \(\Lambda\) hyperon; for example, the \(2^+_1 \to 0^+_1\) transition in \(^{24}\)Mg has \(B(E2) = 98\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\), and the corresponding \(3/2^+_1 \to 1/2^+_1\) transition in \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg has \(B(E2) = 92\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) (a change of \(-6.1\%\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of structure changes in \(sd\)-shell hypernuclei, particularly the prediction by Myaing (2011) that a \(\Lambda\) hyperon makes \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg slightly soft against \(\gamma\) deformation and the prediction by Yao (2011) that the \(\Lambda\) hyperon slightly stretches the ground band and reduces \(B(E2)\). The present calculation uses a more sophisticated treatment (HyperAMD + GCM) that does not assume axial symmetry and treats triaxial deformation explicitly. The results enable quantitative predictions for the low-lying spectra and \(B(E2)\) values of \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg, and they show that the response of triaxially deformed nuclei to a \(\Lambda\) hyperon differs from that of axially symmetric nuclei such as \(^{21}_{\Lambda}\)Ne, where larger \(B(E2)\) reductions (about 20%) were predicted.
