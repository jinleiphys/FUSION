# Generalized Parton Distributions: visions, basics, and realities

> An introductory to generalized parton distributions is given which emphasizes their spectral property and its uses as well as the equivalence of various GPD representations. Furthermore, the status of the theory and phenomenology of hard exclusive processes is shortly reviewed.

## Key claim

This introductory review argues that while generalized parton distributions (GPDs) promise a three-dimensional tomographic picture of the nucleon and access to the partonic spin decomposition, the quantitative description of hard exclusive processes using simple factorized GPD models is not satisfactory, and a global fitting framework based on flexible GPD models is under development to overcome these phenomenological issues.

## Method

The paper reviews the definition of leading twist-two quark and gluon GPDs as hadronic matrix elements of light-ray operators, emphasizing their spectral (double distribution) representation and its uses. It discusses the equivalence of various GPD representations, including the double distribution representation, conformal partial wave expansion, and the dissipative framework, and illustrates these concepts with a toy example of a photon twist-two GPD derived from a box diagram.

## Key numbers

- The second Mellin moment of GPDs with respect to momentum fraction \(x\) gives the fraction of angular momentum carried by a particular parton flavor (Ji sum rule).
- For vanishing skewness (\(\eta=0\)), the Fourier transform of GPDs to impact parameter space gives the probability to find a parton with a given momentum fraction \(x\) and transverse distance from the nucleon center.
- The paper notes that cross section normalization predictions for hard exclusive processes at large energies at leading order overshoot the measured ones from H1 and ZEUS collider experiments.
- A good description of the unpolarized DVCS world data set, including DVMP data at small \(\xB\), has been reached using flexible GPD models (Kumericki 2009, Kumericki 2011, Lautenschlager 2013).

## Context

This review extends the extensive studies of form factors and parton distribution functions (PDFs) by introducing GPDs as a complementary tool for nucleon structure, building on the foundational factorization theorems for deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) established by Collins (1996), Radyushkin (1996), and Ji (1996). It enables the development of global GPD fitting frameworks that can resolve the spin decomposition of the proton and provide a three-dimensional partonic picture of hadrons, with further measurements planned after the 12 GeV upgrade at JLab and by the COMPASS II collaboration at CERN.
