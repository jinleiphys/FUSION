# Gradient corrections to the local-equilibrium energy-momentum tensor in the Zubarev approach

> We compute the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor of a real scalar field in an approximation which accounts for spacetime gradients of the hydrodynamical variables in local thermodynamical equilibrium. We show that the energy-momentum tensor receives corrections with respect to the standard local-equilibrium result. Notably, the relation between the energy density and pressure, i.e., the equation of state, is modified with respect to the one in global equilibrium. The obtained corrections might be relevant for systems created in relativistic hadron and heavy-ion collisions.

# Gradient corrections to the local-equilibrium energy-momentum tensor in the Zubarev approach

## Key claim

The authors show that the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor computed with the local-equilibrium statistical operator (which is commonly identified with the ideal-fluid form) actually contains additional gradient corrections, and that the equation of state relating energy density and pressure is modified relative to global equilibrium. These corrections scale as the square of the ratio of the thermal wavelength to the typical spacetime homogeneity length, and may become relevant for small, highly inhomogeneous systems such as those created in relativistic hadron and heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors compute the expectation value of the energy-momentum tensor for a real scalar field by first taking spacetime derivatives outside the statistical averaging (using stationarity of the nonequilibrium statistical operator), then replacing the nonequilibrium average with a thermal average using the spacetime-dependent global-equilibrium operator at point z (neglecting dissipative corrections). The resulting expression is expanded in gradients of the hydrodynamical variables, and the gradient corrections are isolated and evaluated using the on-mass-shell approximation for the statistical two-point function, with interactions set to zero for simplicity.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the gradient correction to the pressure is given by |Λ|/P_{z-geq} = (3/8) * 1/(\overline{p*^2} L^2), where \overline{p*^2} is the mean squared momentum and L is the spacetime homogeneity length.
- In the nonrelativistic limit (m/T >> 1), the correction scales as |Λ|/P_{z-geq} ~ λ_th^2 / L^2, where λ_th ~ 1/√(mT) is the thermal wavelength.
- In the ultrarelativistic limit (m/T << 1), the correction scales as |Λ|/P_{z-geq} ~ λ_th^2 / L^2, where λ_th ~ 1/T is the thermal wavelength.
- The condition for neglecting corrections is λ_th / L << 1; when λ_th / L ≳ 1, the energy density or pressure can become negative, indicating inapplicability of hydrodynamics.

## Context

This work extends the Zubarev nonequilibrium statistical operator formalism (Zubarev, 1974) and its reformulation for transient evolution in heavy-ion collisions (Zubarev, 2013) by computing gradient corrections that are usually neglected when approximating the local-equilibrium statistical operator by the spacetime-dependent global-equilibrium operator. The corrections are distinct from standard dissipative terms (which are proportional to the Knudsen number and involve transport coefficients via Kubo relations) and instead arise from the nonlocality of the local-equilibrium statistical operator itself. The results support the view that hydrodynamics requires a clear separation between microscopic scales (thermal wavelength) and macroscopic scales (homogeneity length), and may help define the domain of validity of hydrodynamic descriptions for small systems, as considered by Ref.
