# Fusion reactions in multicomponent dense matter

> We analyze thermonuclear and pycnonuclear fusion reactions in dense matter containing atomic nuclei of different types. We extend a phenomenological expression for the reaction rate, proposed recently by Gasques et al. (2005) for the one-component plasma of nuclei, to the multi-component plasma. The expression contains several fit parameters which we adjust to reproduce the best microscopic calculations available in the literature. Furthermore, we show that pycnonuclear burning is drastically affected by an (unknown) structure of the multi-component matter (a regular lattice, a uniform mix, etc.). We apply the results to study nuclear burning in a carbon_12-oxygen_16 mixture. In this context we present new calculations of the astrophysical S-factors for carbon-oxygen and oxygen-oxygen fusion reactions. We show that the presence of a CO lattice can strongly suppress carbon ignition in white dwarf cores and neutron star crusts at densities > 3e9 g cm^{-3} and temperatures T<1e8 K.

## Key claim

The paper extends a phenomenological expression for fusion reaction rates from one-component plasmas (OCP) to multi-component plasmas (MCP), but the authors show that pycnonuclear burning is drastically affected by the unknown structure of the multi-component matter (regular lattice, uniform mix, etc.), meaning the results carry significant uncertainty depending on the assumed spatial arrangement of nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated astrophysical S-factors for the 12C+16O and 16O+16O fusion reactions using a one-dimensional barrier penetration formalism with the Sao Paulo potential, then fitted these S-factors to an analytic expression. They extended a phenomenological reaction rate formula from OCP to MCP by adjusting fit parameters to reproduce the best microscopic calculations available, and applied the results to study nuclear burning in a 12C-16O mixture across five burning regimes (two thermonuclear, two pycnonuclear, and one intermediate).

## Key numbers

- The S-factor fit for 12C+12C has maximum formal error 7.2% at E = 19.8 MeV; for 12C+16O, 6.3% at E = 7.5 MeV; for 16O+16O, 3.9% at E = 8.2 MeV
- The presence of a CO lattice can strongly suppress carbon ignition at densities > 3e9 g cm^{-3} and temperatures T < 1e8 K
- The classical OCP of ions solidifies at Gamma approximately 175 (dimensionless Coulomb coupling parameter)
- The neutron drip density is approximately 4e11 g cm^{-3}
- The ion-sphere model underestimates the enhancement factor by at most a factor of 1.5 for Gamma_ij between 1 and 10 with charge ratios 1/5 to 5
- For Gamma_ij = 170, the enhancement factor itself is as large as approximately 10^{74}

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological reaction rate expression of Gasques et al. (2005) from one-component plasmas to multi-component plasmas, building on the foundational theory of thermonuclear burning with strong plasma screening by Salpeter and the theory of pycnonuclear burning by Salpeter and Van Horn (1969). The results enable more accurate modeling of carbon ignition in white dwarf cores and neutron star crusts, particularly for triggering type Ia supernova explosions, and highlight the need for better understanding of the microscopic structure of cold multi-component matter through Monte Carlo simulations.
