# Bulk viscosity of strange quark matter: Urca versus non-leptonic processes

> A general formalism for calculating the bulk viscosity of strange quark matter is developed. Contrary to the common belief that the non-leptonic processes alone give the dominant contribution to the bulk viscosity, the inclusion of the Urca processes is shown to play an important role at intermediate densities when the characteristic r-mode oscillation frequencies are not too high. The interplay of non-leptonic and Urca processes is analyzed in detail

## Key claim

Contrary to the common belief that non-leptonic weak processes alone dominate the bulk viscosity of strange quark matter, the inclusion of Urca (semi-leptonic) processes plays an important role at intermediate densities when the characteristic r-mode oscillation frequencies are not too high, with the caveat that this enhancement is significant only for sufficiently large in-medium strange quark masses (e.g., 300 MeV) and lower baryon densities (e.g., 5 times nuclear saturation density).

## Method

The authors developed a general formalism for calculating the bulk viscosity of three-flavor strange quark matter by solving coupled rate equations for the electron and strangeness fractions under periodic density oscillations, incorporating both non-leptonic processes (u + d ↔ u + s) and Urca processes (semi-leptonic weak interactions). They used zero-temperature expressions for quark and electron number densities including leading-order strong interaction corrections, and employed approximate analytic expressions for the weak process rates from the literature. Numerical calculations were performed for two representative parameter sets corresponding to different baryon densities and strange quark masses.

## Key numbers

- For parameter set A (n = 5ρ₀, m_s = 300 MeV, α_s = 0.2): electron chemical potential μ_e = 39.139 MeV, up quark chemical potential μ_u = 402.463 MeV, down/strange chemical potential μ_d = μ_s = 441.602 MeV
- For parameter set B (n = 10ρ₀, m_s = 140 MeV, α_s = 0.1): electron chemical potential μ_e = 7.396 MeV, up quark chemical potential μ_u = 495.275 MeV, down/strange chemical potential μ_d = μ_s = 502.671 MeV
- The characteristic r-mode oscillation frequencies of interest range from 1 s⁻¹ to 10³ s⁻¹, with the fastest-spinning pulsar PSR J1748-2446ad having ω ≈ 4.5 × 10³ s⁻¹ (ν = 716 Hz)
- The ratio ζ/ζ_non (complete bulk viscosity versus non-leptonic-only bulk viscosity) for set A shows that neglecting Urca processes can underestimate the bulk viscosity by up to an order of magnitude at intermediate densities
- For set B (smaller strange quark mass), the ratio ζ/ζ_non deviates from 1 by at most 28%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of bulk viscosity in two-flavor quark matter (Sa'd, Shovkovy, Rischke, 2006) to three-flavor strange quark matter, and augments existing studies by Wang (1984), Sawyer (1989), Madsen (1992), and others that neglected the role of Urca processes. The results are relevant for understanding r-mode damping in young neutron stars, where temperatures between 0.1 MeV and 1 MeV are of interest. The formalism enables future calculations of bulk viscosity in color-superconducting phases of quark matter.
