# Effect of Rescattering on Forward-Backward Correlations

> We use the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamic model (UrQMD v2.2) to study forward-backward fluctuations and compare our results with the data published by the PHOBOS experiment. The extracted effective cluster multiplicities show a clear centrality dependence within the present hadron-string transport approach. This behavior is not reproduced with models not taking into account final state rescattering.

## Key claim

The hadron-string transport model UrQMD reproduces mid-peripheral PHOBOS data for forward-backward fluctuations but fails to describe central Au+Au collisions, which the authors interpret as evidence for additional cluster melting not accounted for by final-state rescattering in the model.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v2.2 hadron-string transport model to simulate 5×10^5 pp and minimum bias Au+Au events at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They computed the dynamical fluctuation variable σ²_C (variance of the asymmetry C = (N_F - N_B)/√(N_F+N_B)) as a function of pseudorapidity η and window size Δη, and extracted effective cluster multiplicities K_eff from the results. The calculations were compared to PHOBOS experimental data and HIJING model results.

## Key numbers

- For mid-peripheral (40%-60%) Au+Au events, σ²_C increases from ≈1 to ≈1.75 as a function of η (Δη=0.5)
- For central (0%-20%) Au+Au events, σ²_C increases from ≈1 to ≈1.6 as a function of η (Δη=0.5)
- For mid-peripheral events, σ²_C reaches up to 2.8 as a function of Δη (η=2)
- For central events, σ²_C reaches 2.2 as a function of Δη (η=2)
- For pp collisions, σ²_C = 1.1 at η=2.75 (Δη=0.5)
- For 20%-40% Au+Au events, σ²_C = 1.8 at η=2.75 (Δη=0.5)
- For 0%-20% Au+Au events, σ²_C = 1.6 at η=2.75 (Δη=0.5)
- For pp collisions, σ²_C = 1.25 at Δη=2 (η=2)
- For 20%-40% Au+Au events, σ²_C = 2.75 at Δη=2 (η=2)
- With fixed η=2 and Δη=2, σ²_C increases from 2.1 for peripheral events to 3 for 20%-30% central events, then decreases to 2.7 for 0%-10% central events

## Context

This work extends the UA5 cluster analysis from p-pbar collisions (Alpgard 1983) and the PHOBOS forward-backward fluctuation measurements (Back 2006) by providing a hadronic transport baseline. The authors previously extracted effective cluster multiplicities K_eff of 2.7 for mid-peripheral and 2.2 for central Au+Au events (Abdel-Aziz 2006, Haussler 2006), values larger than the hadron resonance gas prediction of 1.5 (Stephanov 1999). The failure of UrQMD for central events suggests the need for additional physics beyond hadron-string dynamics, potentially related to quark-gluon plasma formation, and enables future comparisons with models incorporating partonic degrees of freedom or more sophisticated rescattering.
