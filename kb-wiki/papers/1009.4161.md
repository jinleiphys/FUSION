# Low scale saturation of Effective NN Interactions and their Symmetries

> The Skyrme force parameters can be uniquely determined by coarse graining the NN interactions at a characteristic momentum scale. We show how exact Vlowk potentials to second order in momenta are accurately and universally saturated with physical NN scattering threshold parameters at CM momentum scales of about Lambda=250 MeV for the S-waves and Lambda=100 MeV for the P-waves. The pattern of Wigner and Serber symmetries unveiled previously is also saturated at these scales.

## Key claim
The Skyrme force parameters can be uniquely determined from NN scattering threshold parameters by coarse graining the interaction at a characteristic momentum scale, but this uniqueness holds only when the expansion is truncated to second order in momenta and the scale is chosen such that the counterterms saturate, specifically at CM momentum scales of about Λ=250 MeV for S-waves and Λ=100 MeV for P-waves.

## Method
The author uses the Vlowk method to integrate out high-momentum modes of NN interactions above a cutoff Λ, then represents the resulting effective potential as a polynomial in momenta up to second order. The coefficients of this polynomial (the Skyrme force parameters) are related analytically to the low-energy threshold parameters (scattering lengths, effective ranges, and scattering volumes) from the effective range expansion, using algebraic solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The scale dependence of these coefficients is computed and compared with exact Vlowk results from the Argonne-V18 potential at Λ=420 MeV.

## Key numbers
- S-wave counterterms saturate at Λ=250 MeV, where the coefficients C_{^1S_0} and C_{^3S_1} become equal, exhibiting Wigner symmetry.
- P-wave counterterms saturate at Λ=100 MeV, a much lower scale than for S-waves.
- The spin-orbit parameter W0, evaluated in the Λ→0 limit, yields W0 = 72 MeV fm^4, reproducing the exact Vlowk value within less than 10%.
- The scattering lengths and volumes used are from Pavon Valderrama (2005) for the NijmII and Reid93 potentials; specific numerical values are referenced but not reproduced in the text.
- The identity C_{^1S_0}(Λ)=C_{^3S_1}(Λ) holds for Λ ≥ 250 MeV only when the ^3S_1-^3D_1 mixing term D_{E_1} is included.

## Context
This work extends the Vlowk approach of Bogner (2003) by showing that Skyrme force parameters can be determined directly from NN threshold parameters rather than from detailed potential models, and that the Wigner and Serber symmetries previously identified by Calle Cordon (2008, 2009) and Ruiz Arriola (2009) are saturated at low scales independent of the specific bare potential. The results enable a systematic connection between two-body scattering data and many-body nuclear structure calculations, though the author notes that going beyond second order requires three-body forces and that chiral interactions with cutoffs around 600 MeV (Entem, 2003) tend to violate Wigner symmetry, suggesting lower chiral cutoffs around 450 MeV are preferred.
