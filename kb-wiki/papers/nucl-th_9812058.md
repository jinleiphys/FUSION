# The role of trapped neutrino in dense stelllar matter and kaon condensation

> We investigate the effect of neutrino trapping on kaon condensation process and Equation of State (EOS) in a newly formed neutron star which is less than several seconds old. Using nonlinear relativistic mean field model, we find that the presence of neutrino shifts the threshold for kaon condensation and muon production to much higher density. We also studied the energy density and pressure of the syst under trapped neutrino condition and found that it stiffens the EOS of the system which may be responsible for the delayed exploision mechanism of supernovae.

# The role of trapped neutrino in dense stellar matter and kaon condensation

## Key claim
Neutrino trapping in protoneutron star matter shifts the threshold density for kaon condensation to much higher densities (around 8 times nuclear saturation density for an electron-lepton fraction of 0.3), which may explain the delayed explosion mechanism of supernovae by stiffening the equation of state while neutrinos remain trapped.

## Method
The author uses a nonlinear relativistic mean field model with baryons interacting via sigma, omega, and rho meson exchange, combined with the Kaplan-Nelson SU(3)xSU(3) chiral Lagrangian for kaon-baryon interactions. The hadronic and leptonic densities are calculated self-consistently under beta equilibrium, charge neutrality, and total baryon number conservation, with neutrino trapping imposed by fixing the electron-lepton fraction Y_le at a constant value.

## Key numbers
- Threshold density for muon production: shifts from approximately 1.05 rho_0 (neutrino-free) to approximately 5 rho_0 (with trapped neutrinos, Y_le = 0.3)
- Threshold density for kaon condensation: shifts from approximately 4 rho_0 (neutrino-free) to approximately 8 rho_0 (with trapped neutrinos, Y_le = 0.3)
- Kaon chemical potential mu: approximately 49 MeV for trapped neutrinos (Y_le = 0.3) compared to approximately 108 MeV in the untrapped case
- Coupling constants used: (g_sigma/m_sigma)^2 = 10.138, (g_omega/m_omega)^2 = 13.285, (g_rho/m_rho)^2 = 4.975, b = 0.003478, c = 0.01328
- Constants for kaon sector: a_1 m_s = -67.0 MeV, a_2 m_s = 134.0 MeV, a_3 m_s = -222.0 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of kaon condensation in neutron star matter by including neutrino trapping, building on the foundational work of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) who first proposed kaon condensation using chiral Lagrangian methods. It contrasts with the study by Chiapparini et al. (1996) who examined trapped neutrino matter without kaon condensation and hyperons, finding almost no effect on the equation of state. The results suggest that the delayed softening of the equation of state from shifted kaon condensation thresholds could explain the delayed explosion mechanism in supernovae, and that kaon condensates may not be present in the core of protoneutron stars during the early trapped-neutrino phase.
