# Chaos Thresholds in finite Fermi systems

> The development of Quantum Chaos in finite interacting Fermi systems is considered. At sufficiently high excitation energy the direct two-particle interaction may mix into an eigen-state the exponentially large number of simple Slater-determinant states. Nevertheless, the transition from Poisson to Wigner-Dyson statistics of energy levels is governed by the effective high order interaction between states very distant in the Fock space. The concrete form of the transition depends on the way one chooses to work out the problem of factorial divergency of the number of Feynman diagrams. In the proposed scheme the change of statistics has a form of narrow phase transition and may happen even below the direct interaction threshold.

## Key claim

The transition from Poisson to Wigner-Dyson level statistics in finite interacting Fermi systems may occur as a narrow phase transition at an energy below the direct interaction threshold, governed by the factorial divergence of high-order effective interaction diagrams rather than by direct two-particle mixing.

## Method

The author analyzes the inverse participation ratio (IPR) for many-particle excited states using perturbation theory, computing the density of accessible states and the averaged modulus of effective matrix elements for n-particle interactions via tree-type Feynman diagrams. The scaling behavior of the IPR is derived by exponentiating contributions from independent direct transitions and then incorporating high-order effective interactions, with the number of diagrams counted using Cayley's tree formula. The factorial divergence of the series coefficients is examined to determine the chaos threshold.

## Key numbers

- The direct two-particle interaction matrix elements satisfy \(\overline{V^2} = \Delta^2/g^2\) with \(g \gg 1\), where \(\Delta\) is the single-particle level spacing and \(g\) is the dimensionless conductance.
- The density of directly accessible two-particle states is \(1/\Delta_2 = (T^3/\Delta^4)(\pi^2/6)\), where \(T = \sqrt{6\mathcal{E}\Delta}/\pi\) is the effective temperature.
- The IPR from direct interaction exponentiates as \(I = \exp\{-(4\sqrt{3}/\sqrt{\pi}g)(\mathcal{E}/\Delta)^{3/2}\}\) for \(\mathcal{E} > \Delta g^{2/3}\).
- The averaged modulus of the n-particle effective matrix element scales as \(\overline{|V_{eff}^{(n)}|} \sim \Delta (\ln g)^{n-1}/g^n\).
- The number of tree-type Feynman diagrams for n-particle effective interaction is \(N_d = n^{n-2}(n-1)!n!\).
- The chaos threshold from the factorial scenario is \(\mathcal{E}_c \sim \Delta (g/\ln g)^{2/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum chaos in finite Fermi systems initiated by Wigner (1950s), Bohigas (1980s), and the random matrix theory approach reviewed by Weidenmuller. It builds on the analysis of quasiparticle disintegration in quantum dots by Sivan (1994), Blanter (1996), and the author's own prior work (1996) on below-golden-rule decay. The paper introduces a new mechanism for the Poisson-to-Wigner-Dyson transition based on factorial divergence of perturbation series, contrasting with the conventional picture where chaos develops gradually between \(\mathcal{E} \sim \Delta g/\ln g\) and \(\mathcal{E} \sim \Delta g\). The results enable predictions for level statistics in mesoscopic systems such as quantum dots, atomic clusters, and helium droplets.
