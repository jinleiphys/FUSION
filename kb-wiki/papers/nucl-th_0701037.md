# Systematics of the first 2+ excitation with the Gogny interaction

> We examine the global performance of an extended self-consistent mean field theory of 2+ excitations in even-even nuclei. 518 nuclei are included in our survey, comprising all but 39 of the known nuclei tabulated by Raman et al. The theory is based on a quantal collective Hamiltonian in five dimensions in which the potential energy and the tensor of inertia are obtained from constrained triaxial Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations. The only parameters in theory are those of the finite-range, density-dependent Gogny D1S interaction. The following properties of the lowest 2+ excitations are calculated: excitation energy, reduced transition probability, and spectroscopic quadrupole moment. We find that the theory is very reliable to classify the nuclei by shape. Quantitatively the performance of the theory in deformed nuclei is excellent: average excitation energies and transition quadrupole moments are within 5% of the experimental values, and the dispersion about the averages are 20% and 10%, respectively. The performance is not as good on spherical and soft nuclei. Including all nuclei in the performance evaluation, the average transition quadrupole moment is 10% too high and the dispersion about the mean is 20%. For the energies, the average is 13% too high and the dispersion is 40%.

# Systematics of the first 2+ excitation with the Gogny interaction

## Key claim

The five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) based on constrained triaxial Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction can predict the properties of the first 2+ excitation in over 90% of known even-even nuclei, but the quantitative accuracy depends strongly on nuclear shape: for deformed nuclei the average excitation energies and transition quadrupole moments are within 5% of experiment, while for spherical and soft nuclei the performance is significantly worse, with average energies 13% too high overall.

## Method

The authors performed constrained triaxial Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations using the finite-range, density-dependent Gogny D1S interaction to construct potential energy surfaces and the tensor of inertia. From these inputs they built a quantal collective Hamiltonian in five dimensions (5DCH), formally similar to the Bohr Hamiltonian, with rotational moments of inertia obtained via the Thouless-Valatin prescription and mass parameters calculated by the Inglis-Belyaev formula. The theory was applied to 519 even-even nuclei (all but 38 of the nuclei tabulated by Raman et al. (2001), excluding very light nuclei with Z or N below 8 and doubly magic nuclei where the mapping breaks down), and the excitation energy, reduced transition probability, and spectroscopic quadrupole moment of the lowest 2+ state were computed and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- 519 nuclei were included in the survey, comprising all but 38 of the 557 even-even nuclei with known 2+ excitation energies tabulated by Raman et al. (2001)
- For all 519 nuclei, the average logarithmic error in excitation energy is 0.12, corresponding to a systematic overestimate of 12%; the root mean square deviation is 0.33, implying typical errors from -30% to +40% after correcting for bias
- For the 319 nuclei with measured transition quadrupole matrix elements, the average logarithmic error is 0.10 (matrix elements 11% too large on average), with a dispersion of 0.21 (range of +20% to -19% about the center)
- For the 146 deformed nuclei, the average excitation energy error is -0.05 (slightly underpredicted) with a dispersion of 0.19; for the 106 deformed nuclei with transition matrix elements, the average error is 0.035 with a dispersion of 0.09
- For the 73 semimagic nuclei, the average excitation energy error is 0.02 but the dispersion is 0.51; for the 43 semimagic nuclei with transition matrix elements, the average error is 0.42 with a dispersion of 0.23
- 93% of the 306 B(E2) values shown in the scatter plot fall within a factor of two of experiment

## Context

This work provides the first comprehensive test of a microscopic collective Hamiltonian theory for 2+ excitations across the entire periodic table, extending previous successful applications to strongly deformed nuclei by Delaroche et al. (2006), Libert et al. (1999), and Goutte et al. (2005) as well as to soft nuclei by Bonche et al. (1988) and Flores et al. (2004). The results are compared to a parallel study by Sabbey et al. (2006) using a Skyrme energy functional with angular-momentum-projected generator coordinate method, which achieved similar accuracy for quadrupole properties of deformed nuclei but produced excitation energies systematically higher by about 50%. The present work enables future systematic studies of gamma vibrations as higher-lying 2+ excitations and beta vibrations as 0+ excitations, and identifies the need for improved treatment of collective masses beyond the Inglis-Belyaev approximation, such as QRPA-based methods.
