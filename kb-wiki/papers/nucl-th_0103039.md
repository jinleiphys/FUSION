# Charged-current neutrino-208Pb reactions

> We present theoretical results on the non flux-averaged $^{208}Pb(ν_{e},e^-)^{208}Bi$ and $^{208}Pb(ν_μ,μ^-)^{208}Bi$ reaction cross sections, obtained within the charge-exchange Random-Phase-Approximation. A detailed knowledge of these cross sections is important in different contexts. In particular, it is necessary to assess the possibility of using lead as a detector in future experiments on supernova neutrinos, such as OMNIS and LAND, and eventually detect neutrino oscillation signals by exploiting the spectroscopic properties of $^{208}Bi$. We discuss the present status on the theoretical predictions of the reaction cross sections.

# Charged-current neutrino-208Pb reactions

## Key claim

The authors present non flux-averaged cross sections for charged-current neutrino-lead reactions calculated within a self-consistent charge-exchange Random-Phase-Approximation, emphasizing that forbidden transitions contribute significantly even at astrophysical neutrino energies and that theoretical predictions for these cross sections show substantial variation (20-30% or more) depending on the treatment of Coulomb corrections and the detailed strength distributions in 208Bi.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent charge-exchange Random-Phase-Approximation (RPA) calculations using SIII and SGII Skyrme effective forces, with Hartree-Fock single-particle energies and wavefunctions derived from the same forces. Coulomb corrections for the outgoing lepton were treated by interpolating between the Fermi function at low electron energies and the Modified Effective Momentum Approximation at high lepton energies. All multipolarities up to J=6 were included, and the axial vector coupling constant was taken as 1.26.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow-Teller strength distribution is peaked at 19.2 MeV, exhausting about 60% of the Ikeda sum rule
- The Isobaric Analogue State is found at 18.4 MeV (compared to the experimental value of 18.8 MeV)
- For the DAR (Decay-At-Rest) of muon neutrinos, the flux-averaged cross section is 44.39 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- For the DIF (Decay-In-Flight) of pion neutrinos, the flux-averaged cross section is 399.2 × 10^{-40} cm^2
- The 2-neutron emission threshold in 208Bi is 14.98 MeV; the 1-neutron emission threshold is 6.9 MeV
- A peak in the Gamow-Teller strength distribution is found at around 7.5 MeV (above the 1n threshold)
- Using only the Fermi function for Coulomb corrections increases the reaction cross sections by about 50% on average

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Hax (2000) and Langanke et al. (2000) for neutrino-lead reactions, which are needed to assess lead-based detectors for supernova neutrinos such as OMNIS and LAND. The authors find close agreement (20-30% difference) with the CRPA calculations of Langanke et al. (2000) but significant discrepancies (up to a factor of 2) with the allowed-approximation calculations of Hax (2000), which they attribute mainly to different treatments of Coulomb corrections and sensitivity to the detailed strength distributions. The results enable more reliable estimates of backgrounds in LSND experiments and evaluation of proposed neutrino oscillation signals based on neutron emission from 208Bi.
