# J/Psi and Psi' Polarizations in Polarized Proton-Proton Collisions at the RHIC

> We study inclusive heavy quarkonium production with definite polarizations in polarized proton-proton collisions using the non-relativistic QCD color-octet mechanism. We present results for rapidity distributions of cross sections and spin asymmetries for the production of J/psi and psi' with specific polarizations in polarized p-p collisions at \sqrt s = 200 GeV and 500 GeV at the RHIC within the PHENIX detector acceptance range.

# J/Psi and Psi' Polarizations in Polarized Proton-Proton Collisions at the RHIC

## Key claim

The leading-order NRQCD color-octet mechanism predicts that in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC energies, the spin asymmetries for J/psi and psi' production with definite helicities are rather flat over the central rapidity range, but the polarized cross sections are at least a factor of 200-1000 smaller than the unpolarized ones and could be even smaller or negative due to cancellations between color-octet contributions, depending on the relative sizes of the non-perturbative matrix elements.

## Method

The authors used the helicity decomposition method within the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) color-octet mechanism to calculate partonic-level cross sections for the processes q qbar → H(λ) and gg → H(λ), where λ is the helicity of the produced heavy quarkonium state. These partonic cross sections were then folded with polarized parton distribution functions (GRV unpolarized LO and GRSV polarized densities) to compute rapidity distributions and spin asymmetries A_LL for J/psi and psi' production in longitudinally polarized p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV and 500 GeV within the PHENIX detector acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass taken as m = 1.5 GeV with mass factorization scale equal to 2m
- For J/psi: <O^J/psi_8(^3S_1)> = 0.0112 GeV^3; three scenarios for the combination M^J/psi(^1S_0^(8), ^3P_0^(8)) ≈ 0.039 GeV^3 with individual values: Scenario 1: both <O^J/psi_8(^1S_0)> and <O^J/psi_8(^3P_0)>/m^2 set to 0.0087 GeV^3; Scenario 2: <O^J/psi_8(^1S_0)> = 0.039 GeV^3, <O^J/psi_8(^3P_0)>/m^2 = 0; Scenario 3: <O^J/psi_8(^1S_0)> = 0, <O^J/psi_8(^3P_0)>/m^2 = 0.01125 GeV^3
- For psi': <O^psi'_8(^3S_1)> = 0.0046 GeV^3; rapidity distributions are a factor of 3.5 smaller than for J/psi
- Lambda^LO_4 = 175 MeV, giving alpha_s^LO(m_Z) = 0.121
- PHENIX detector acceptance: central arm covers |y| < 0.5, forward arms cover 1 < |y| < 2

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD color-octet mechanism calculations of heavy quarkonium production, previously applied to unpolarized collisions at the Tevatron (CDF), HERA, LEP, and fixed-target experiments, to the case of polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC. The authors build on the helicity decomposition method of Braaten (1994) and the polarized partonic cross section calculations of Godbole, Misra, and Mukherji (2002). The study is motivated by the fact that Tevatron data on heavy quarkonium polarization (CDF) is not explained by the color-octet mechanism, making RHIC measurements a crucial test. The results enable experimental comparisons with future PHENIX data to test the NRQCD color-octet production mechanism with respect to polarization and to probe spin transfer processes in perturbative QCD.
