# Influence of statistical sequential decay on isoscaling and symmetry energy coefficient in a GEMINI simulation

> Extensive calculations on isoscaling behavior with the sequential-decay model gemini are performed for the medium-to-heavy nuclei in the mass range A = 60-120 at excitation energies up to 3 MeV/nucleon. The comparison between the products after the first-step decay and the ones after the entire-steps decay demonstrates that there exists a strong sequential decay effect on the final isoscaling parameters and the apparent temperature. Results show that the apparent symmetry energy coefficient $γ_{app}$ does not reflect the initial symmetry energy coefficient $C_{sym}$ embedded in the mass calculation in the present GEMINI model.

## Key claim

The apparent symmetry energy coefficient extracted from final cold fragments after multi-step sequential decay (gamma_app = 19.65 +/- 0.25 MeV using initial temperature, or 15.84 +/- 0.18 MeV using apparent temperature) does not reflect the initial symmetry energy coefficient C_sym = 24.2 +/- 0.3 MeV embedded in the GEMINI mass calculation, even though isoscaling behavior is preserved after entire-steps decay.

## Method

The authors performed GEMINI statistical sequential decay simulations for equilibrated source pairs with Z_s = 30 (A_s = 60, 63, 66, 69) and Z_s = 50 (A_s = 100, 105, 110, 115) at excitation energies from 1.0 to 3.0 MeV/nucleon. They compared isoscaling parameters alpha and beta extracted from fragments after only the first-step decay versus after the entire multi-step decay chain, and extracted the apparent symmetry energy coefficient gamma_app by fitting alpha T versus Delta(Z/A)_s^2 and beta T versus Delta(N/A)_s^2.

## Key numbers

- Initial symmetry energy coefficient C_sym = 24.2 +/- 0.3 MeV, obtained from linear fit of first-step decay data using initial source temperature T_i
- Apparent symmetry energy coefficient gamma_app = 19.65 +/- 0.25 MeV, obtained from entire-steps decay data using the same initial temperature T_i
- Apparent symmetry energy coefficient gamma_app = 15.84 +/- 0.18 MeV, obtained from entire-steps decay data using the apparent temperature T_r from neutron and proton spectra fitting
- Reduction of isoscaling parameters alpha and |beta| by about 20% on average when entire-steps decay is included compared to first-step only
- Initial temperatures T_i range from 2.8 to 5.3 MeV across systems (Z_s=30, A_s=60-69) at excitation energies 1.0-3.0 MeV/nucleon
- Apparent temperatures T_r range from 2.3 to 4.5 MeV for the same systems

## Context

This work extends earlier GEMINI studies by Tian et al. (2007) that confirmed isoscaling for first-step decay products, and addresses the debate between studies finding negligible sequential decay effects versus those finding distortion of symmetry energy extraction (Fe05, Co06). The results show that multi-step sequential decay systematically reduces the extracted symmetry energy coefficient, meaning experimental extractions from cold fragments must account for this effect. The work enables more careful interpretation of symmetry energy coefficients derived from experimental isoscaling data using statistical decay codes.
