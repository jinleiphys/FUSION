# Realistic Shell-Model Calculations for Proton-Rich N=50 Isotones

> The structure of the N=50 isotones 98Cd, 97Ag, and 96Pd is studied in terms of shell model employing a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential. The single-hole energies are fixed by resorting to an analysis of the low-energy spectra of the isotones with A>= 91. Comparison shows that our results are in very satisfactory agreement with the available experimental data. This supports confidence in the predictions of our calculations

# Realistic Shell-Model Calculations for Proton-Rich N=50 Isotones

## Key claim

The shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential reproduce the available experimental data for 98Cd, 97Ag, and 96Pd with rms deviations of 107, 108, and 122 keV respectively, but the single-hole energies for the p3/2 and f5/2 orbits could not be determined individually from the existing data and had to be fixed by linear extrapolation from lighter isotones, introducing unavoidable uncertainty.

## Method

The authors derived a two-hole effective interaction from the Bonn-A free nucleon-nucleon potential using a G-matrix formalism with renormalizations from core polarization and folded diagrams, assuming 100Sn as a closed core. Single-hole energies for the p1/2, p3/2, and f5/2 orbits relative to the g9/2 level were determined by analyzing the spectra of 98Cd, 97Ag, and 96Pd combined with linear extrapolation of one-hole state energies from 89Y, 91Nb, and 93Tc. Shell-model calculations were performed using the OXBASH code, and electromagnetic observables were calculated using effective operators including first-order core-polarization diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Single-hole energies adopted (in MeV): epsilon_g9/2 = 0.0, epsilon_p1/2 = 0.7, epsilon_p3/2 = 2.1, epsilon_f5/2 = 3.1; the sum epsilon = epsilon_p3/2 + epsilon_f5/2 was fixed at 5.2 MeV
- rms deviation sigma = 107 keV for 98Cd, 108 keV for 97Ag, and 122 keV for 96Pd
- Calculated B(E2; 8+ -> 6+) in 98Cd = 1.20 W.u., compared to experimental values 1.17(+0.14/-0.11) W.u. (Grzywacz 1998) and 0.54(+0.28/-0.13) W.u. (Gorska 1997)
- Calculated B(E2; 6+ -> 4+) in 96Pd = 0.51 W.u., compared to experimental 0.78 +/- 0.11 W.u.
- Calculated B(E2; 8+ -> 6+) in 96Pd = 0.20 W.u., compared to experimental 0.34 +/- 0.05 W.u.
- Calculated magnetic moment of the 8+ state in 96Pd = 10.54 nm, compared to experimental 10.97 +/- 0.06 nm
- Harmonic oscillator parameter hbar-omega = 8.5 MeV for A=100
- Predicted first excited state in 97Ag: a seniority-one 1/2- state at about 0.5 MeV
- Predicted 5- state in 98Cd at 2.705 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' program of realistic shell-model calculations (Andreozzi 1996, 1997; Covello 1997, 1998; Coraggio 1998, 1999) from the 132Sn and 208Pb regions to the proton-rich N=50 isotones below 100Sn, which is the heaviest N=Z doubly magic nucleus. Previous shell-model calculations for N=50 isotones (Blomqvist and Rydstrom 1985; Ji and Wildenthal 1988a, 1988b; Sinatkas 1992; Ghugre and Datta 1995; Johnstone and Skouras 1997; Zhang 1999) mostly used empirical two-body matrix elements or, in the case of Sinatkas (1992), the Sussex interaction without folded-diagram renormalization. The present calculations are the first to use an effective interaction derived from a modern free nucleon-nucleon potential for these nuclei. The results enable predictions for unobserved states in 98Cd, 97Ag, and 96Pd that may guide future experiments with radioactive ion beams and large gamma-ray arrays.
