# Dynamical coupled-channels approach to electroweak meson productions on nucleon and deuteron

> I overview our recent activity with the Argonne-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) approach that provides a unified description of various electroweak meson productions on single nucleon and nucleus. First I discuss the DCC model of a single nucleon. The DCC model has been developed through a comprehensive analysis of $πN, γN\to πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ$ reaction data. The model has been further extended to finite $Q^2$ region by analyzing pion electroproduction data, and to neutrino-induced reactions using the PCAC relation. Next I discuss applications of the DCC model to electroweak meson productions on the deuteron. We consider impulse mechanism supplemented by final state interactions (FSI) due to $NN$ and meson-nucleon rescatterings. Using this model, I discuss FSI corrections needed to extract $γ$-neutron reaction observables from $γd\to πNN$, and a novel method to extract $ηN$ scattering length from $γd\to ηpn$. I also discuss FSI corrections on the existing neutrino-nucleon pion production data that had been extracted from neutrino-deuteron data.

# Dynamical coupled-channels approach to electroweak meson productions on nucleon and deuteron

## Key claim

The Argonne-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) approach provides a unified description of electroweak meson productions on single nucleon and deuteron, but the model's predictions for neutrino-induced reactions rely on the PCAC relation to derive axial current form factors at Q²~0, with a conventional dipole form factor assumption for the Q²-dependence due to scarce neutrino data.

## Method

The DCC model is based on a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation that couples πN, ππN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, πΔ, ρN, and σN channels to satisfy unitarity, with electroweak couplings treated perturbatively and meson-baryon interactions consisting of non-resonant meson-exchange and resonant bare N*-excitation mechanisms. For deuteron reactions, the model implements impulse, NN rescattering, and meson-nucleon rescattering mechanisms using elementary amplitudes from the DCC model, NN scattering amplitudes, and the CD-Bonn potential for the deuteron wave function. The axial current for neutrino reactions is derived using the PCAC relation to relate axial N→N* transition form factors at Q²~0 to πN→N* coupling strengths.

## Key numbers

- The DCC model was developed through analysis of approximately 27,000 data points from πN, γN→πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ reactions
- For γd→ηpn at the ELPH kinematics (Eγ=0.94 GeV, θp=0°), the ηn→ηn FSI mechanism changes the cross section by -40% to +20%
- The πn→ηn FSI changes cross sections by ≤9% at the ELPH kinematics
- For the ηN scattering length extraction, varying Re[aηN] by ±0.1 fm from 0.6 fm changes cross sections by ~5% at the quasi-free peak (Mηn~1.488 GeV)
- FSI corrections to neutrino-nucleon total cross sections from deuterium data enhance cross sections by factors of 1.05-1.12 for νμp→μ-π+p, 1.10-1.27 for νμn→μ-π+n, and 1.01-1.02 for νμn→μ-π0p, with larger corrections at smaller neutrino energies
- The imaginary part of aηN from prior coupled-channel analyses falls into Im[aηN]=0.2-0.3 fm, while the real part ranges Re[aηN]=0.2-0.9 fm

## Context

This work extends the Argonne-Osaka DCC approach, previously successful in analyzing πN and γN reactions (Kamano et al. 2013, 2016), to finite Q² region and neutrino-induced reactions, and applies it to deuteron targets. The DCC model is contrasted with the K-matrix model (Batinic and Golli 2012) as providing reaction mechanisms in terms of hadronic degrees of freedom and being compatible with multiple scattering theory for nuclear applications. The deuteron reaction model enables extraction of neutron-target observables from γd→πNN data (relevant to JLab and MAMI analyses), a novel method to determine the ηN scattering length from γd→ηpn experiments at ELPH, Tohoku University, and FSI corrections to historical neutrino-nucleon cross section data from ANL and BNL deuterium bubble chamber experiments. The approach enables improved neutrino-nucleus reaction models needed for T2K and DUNE oscillation experiments.
