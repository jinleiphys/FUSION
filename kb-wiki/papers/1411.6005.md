# BBN And The CMB Constrain Neutrino Coupled Light WIMPs

> (abridged) In the presence of a light WIMP (mass m_chi < 30 MeV), there are degeneracies among the WIMP's nature, its couplings to standard model particles, its mass, and the number of equivalent (additional) neutrinos, Delta N_nu. These degeneracies cannot be broken by the CMB constraint on the effective number of neutrinos, N_eff. However, since big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) is also affected by a light WIMP and equivalent neutrinos, complementary BBN and CMB constraints can break some of the degeneracy. In a previous paper BBN and CMB were combined to explore allowed ranges for m_chi, Delta N_nu, and N_eff for light WIMPs that annihilate electromagnetically (EM) to photons and/or electrons/positrons. In this paper BBN predictions with a light WIMP that only couples to neutrinos are calculated. Recent observed abundances of ^2H and ^4He are used to limit m_chi, Delta N_nu, N_eff, and the present-day baryon density. Allowing for a neutrino coupled light WIMP and nonzero Delta N_nu, combined BBN and CMB data give lower limits to m_chi, with a best fit m_chi > 35 MeV, equivalent to no light WIMP at all. All masses below 4--9 MeV (depending on spin) are excluded. Without any light WIMP, BBN alone prefers Delta N_nu = 0.50 +- 0.23, favoring neither Delta N_nu = 0, nor a fully thermalized sterile neutrino (Delta N_nu = 1). This result is consistent with the CMB constraint, N_eff = 3.30 +- 0.27, limiting "new physics" between BBN and recombination. Combining BBN and CMB data gives Delta N_nu = 0.35 +- 0.16 and N_eff = 3.40 +- 0.16; while BBN and the CMB combined require Delta N_nu > 0 at ~98% confidence, they disfavor Delta N_nu > 1 at > 99% confidence. Allowing a neutrino-coupled light WIMP extends the allowed range slightly downward for Delta N_nu and slightly upward for N_eff simultaneously, leaving best-fit values unchanged.

## Key claim

Combined BBN and CMB data give a best-fit lower limit on the mass of a neutrino-coupled light WIMP of m_chi > 35 MeV, effectively excluding any such light WIMP; all masses below 4–9 MeV (depending on whether the WIMP is a Majorana or Dirac fermion, or a real or complex scalar boson) are excluded.

## Method

The authors computed BBN predictions for primordial abundances of deuterium and helium-4 in the presence of a light WIMP that annihilates only to standard-model neutrinos (or to both SM and equivalent neutrinos), using the instantaneous neutrino decoupling approximation. They then performed likelihood analyses to map the observed D and He-4 abundances into constraints on the WIMP mass (m_chi), the number of equivalent neutrinos (Delta N_nu), the effective number of neutrinos at recombination (N_eff), and the baryon density (Omega_B h^2), and compared these with independent Planck CMB constraints.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit m_chi > 35 MeV for neutrino-coupled WIMPs, equivalent to no light WIMP at all.
- All neutrino-coupled WIMP masses below 4–9 MeV are excluded, with specific limits: 4 MeV for a real scalar, up to 9 MeV for a Dirac fermion.
- Without any light WIMP, BBN alone prefers Delta N_nu = 0.50 ± 0.23 and N_eff = 3.56 ± 0.23.
- Combining BBN and CMB data gives Delta N_nu = 0.35 ± 0.16 and N_eff = 3.40 ± 0.16.
- BBN and CMB combined require Delta N_nu > 0 at ~98% confidence and disfavor Delta N_nu > 1 at >99% confidence.
- The CMB constraint alone gives N_eff = 3.30 ± 0.27 (from Planck).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Nollett and Steigman, 2014) of electromagnetically coupled light WIMPs to the case of neutrino-coupled WIMPs, and updates constraints using a new deuterium abundance determination from Cooke et al. (2014). It builds on earlier studies by Kolb et al., Serpico and Raffelt, and Boehm et al. that showed light WIMPs modify BBN yields. The results enable joint BBN+CMB tests of "new physics" between BBN and recombination, and constrain scenarios such as sterile neutrinos or neutrino secret interactions proposed by Ng and Ahlgren et al.
