# An effective field theory of QCD at high density

> We derive a (Wilsonian) effective field theory of QCD at finite density by integrating out the states in the Dirac sea when the chemical potential $μ\ggΛ_{\rm QCD}$. The quark-gluon coupling is effectively (1+1)-dimensional and the theory contains four-quark operators which become relevant as we approach to the Fermi sea. By calculating the one-loop vacuum polarization tensor in the effective theory, we find the electric gluons have a screening mass, $M\sim g_sμ$, while the static magnetic gluons are unscreened. We then investigate the gap equations for color anti-triplet Cooper pairs by including both gluon-exchange interactions and the marginal four-quark interactions in the effective theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Wilsonian effective field theory of QCD at high density (chemical potential μ ≫ Λ_QCD) by integrating out Dirac-sea states, finding that the resulting gap for color anti-triplet Cooper pairs behaves as Δ ∼ μ g_s^{-5} exp[-3π²/(√2 g_s)], which agrees with Son (1999) and other Schwinger-Dyson approaches, but only under the condition that the leading contribution comes from Landau-damped magnetic gluons while four-quark operators are negligible at leading order.

## Method

The author integrates out the fast modes (ψ_- states in the Dirac sea) from the QCD Lagrangian at finite chemical potential, matching one-light-particle-irreducible amplitudes in loop expansion to obtain a low-energy effective Lagrangian for quarks near the Fermi surface and gluons. The effective theory contains (1+1)-dimensional quark-gluon couplings and marginal four-quark operators; the author then computes the one-loop vacuum polarization to determine gluon screening masses, solves the renormalization group equations for the four-quark couplings, and finally solves the gap equation for color anti-triplet Cooper pairs in the hard dense loop (HDL) approximation including both gluon-exchange and four-quark interactions.

## Key numbers

- Electric gluon screening mass: M ∼ g_s μ (from one-loop vacuum polarization)
- At scale Λ ≪ μ, the renormalized four-quark couplings are: g_{3̄}(Λ) ≈ 0.04 α_s(Λ), g_6(Λ) ≈ 0.29 α_s(Λ), h_{3̄}(Λ) ≈ 0.4 α_s(Λ), h_6(Λ) ≈ 0.81 α_s(Λ)
- After integrating out electric gluons at scale M, the color anti-triplet four-quark coupling shifts to g_{3̄}(M) + 2√2 g_s²(M)/3 ≈ 0.95 g_s²(M)
- The Cooper-pair gap from four-quark interactions alone: Δ ≈ M exp(-4π²/(0.95 g_s²(M)))
- The full gap from magnetic gluon exchange: Δ ≈ c · μ g_s^{-5} exp(-3π²/(√2 g_s)), where c = 2⁷π⁴ N_f^{-5/2} e^{3ξ/2+1} (ξ is gauge parameter)

## Context

This work extends the program of deriving effective field theories for dense QCD, following the approach used by Hong (1998) for QED under external magnetic fields, and builds on the BCS-type analysis of color superconductivity by Bailin and Love (1984), Rapp, Schäfer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998), and Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998). The result for the gap agrees with the renormalization group analysis of Son (1999) and with Schwinger-Dyson calculations in full QCD by Hong, Miransky, Shovkovy, and Wijewardhana (1999), Schäfer and Wilczek (1999), and Pisarski and Rischke (1999); the effective theory enables systematic inclusion of subleading corrections and provides a framework to study the competition between four-quark operators and gluon-exchange interactions in determining the superconducting gap.
