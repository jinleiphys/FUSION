# Geometry of the shears mechanism in nuclei

## Key claim

The geometry of the shears mechanism in nuclei can be derived directly from the nuclear shell model by taking the classical limit (large angular momenta) of shell-model matrix elements, yielding an energy expression that depends only on the shears angle. However, the minimum energy of a shears band occurs for a non-orthogonal configuration when the isoscalar and isovector interaction strengths are different, contrary to the intuitive picture that the blades are orthogonal at the band head.

## Method

The authors consider a 2-particle neutron and 2-hole proton configuration coupled to angular momenta J_nu and J_pi, respectively, and compute the shell-model matrix element of a generic Hamiltonian for states of total angular momentum J. They introduce the surface delta interaction (SDI) for the neutron-proton interaction and evaluate the resulting 12j-symbol expression in the classical limit by replacing the relevant 3j symbols with their classical approximations based on the area of a triangle formed by the angular momentum vectors. The classical limit yields a closed-form expression for the shears matrix element as a function of the shears angle theta_nu_pi.

## Key numbers

- For neutron and proton blade angular momenta J_nu = J_pi = 20, the classical approximation is good for the stretched case J_rho = j_1_rho + j_2_rho, but deteriorates as J_rho diminishes; in the region J^2 ≈ J_nu^2 + J_pi^2 the approximation remains good even for J_rho = j_1_rho + j_2_rho - 1.
- For J_nu = J_pi = 12 with aligned single-particle angular momenta j_1_rho = 11/2 and j_2_rho = 13/2, the classical approximation is reasonable; with larger unaligned single-particle angular momenta j_1_rho = j_2_rho = 21/2 and J_rho = j_1_rho + j_2_rho - 9, the shears interpretation breaks down.
- In 199Pb, the classical approximation for B(M1) values using g factors g_nu = -0.29 μ_N and g_pi = 1.03 μ_N with J_nu = 33/2 and J_pi = 11 agrees with the exact result and with experimental data in the spin region 47/2 ≤ J ≤ 53/2.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical analysis of shears bands by Clark and Macchiavelli (2000) and Hubel (2005), which assumed an interaction of the form V_0 + V_2 P_2(cos theta_nu_pi) between the blades. The authors provide the first direct derivation of the shears geometry from the shell model, confirming the validity of the shears picture in numerical shell-model calculations by Frauendorf, Reif, and Winter (1996). The results enable a microscopic understanding of when the geometric shears picture applies and when it breaks down, and suggest that energy formulas with constant coefficients cannot describe an entire shears band due to changing neutron and proton configurations.
