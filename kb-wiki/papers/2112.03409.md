# Sum rule for the partial decay rates of bottom hadrons based on the dynamical supersymmetry of the $\bar s$ quark and the $ud$ diquark

> We investigate the weak decays of $\bar B_{s}^{0}$ and $Λ_{b}$ to charm hadrons based on the dynamical supersymmetry between the $\bar s$ quark and the $ud$ diquark. We derive a new sum rule relating the decay rates of the processes $\bar B_{s}^{0} \to D_{s}^{+} P^{-}$, $\bar B_{s}^{0} \to D_{s}^{*+} P^{-}$ and $Λ_{b} \to Λ_{c} P^{-}$, where $P^{-}$ is a negatively charged meson, such as $π^{-}$ and $K^{-}$. It is found that the observed decay rates satisfy the sum rule very well. This implies that the supersymmetry between the $\bar s$ quark and the $ud$ diquark is also seen in the wavefunctions of the heavy hadrons and suggests that the $ud$ diquark can be regarded as a valid effective constituent for heavy hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper derives a sum rule relating the partial decay rates of $\bar B_{s}^{0}$ and $\Lambda_{b}$ to charm hadrons, based on a dynamical supersymmetry between the $\bar s$ constituent quark and the $ud$ scalar diquark. The sum rule is found to be satisfied extremely well by observed pionic decays, but the authors note that for kaonic decays the experimental data cannot discriminate between $\bar B_{s}^{0}$ and $B_{s}^{0}$ decays, so those results are treated as upper limits.

## Method

The authors consider weak decays of $\bar B_{s}^{0}$ and $\Lambda_{b}$ where the $\bar s$ quark or $ud$ diquark acts as a spectator, using an effective Hamiltonian for the $b \to c$ transition. They calculate the hadronic matrix elements for decays to pseudoscalar $D_{s}^{+}$, vector $D_{s}^{*+}$, and baryonic $\Lambda_{c}$ final states, assuming the spin wavefunctions of the heavy hadrons are determined by the supersymmetry. The sum rule $\Gamma_{\bar B_{s}^{0} \to D_{s}^{+}} + \Gamma_{\bar B_{s}^{0}\to D_{s}^{*+}} = \Gamma_{\Lambda_{b}\to \Lambda_{c}}$ emerges from the equality of the spin-summed squared matrix elements for the meson and baryon decays.

## Key numbers

* For pionic decays: sum of $\bar B_{s}^{0} \to \pi^{-} D_{s}^{+}$ and $\bar B_{s}^{0} \to \pi^{-} D_{s}^{*+}$ rates is $(3.3 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{9} / \mathrm{s}$, while $\Lambda_{b} \to \pi^{-} \Lambda_{c}$ rate is $(3.3 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{9} / \mathrm{s}$.
* For kaonic decays: sum of $\bar B_{s}^{0}$ rates to $K^{\mp} D_{s}^{\pm}$ and $K^{\mp} D_{s}^{*\pm}$ is $(2.37 \pm 0.26) \times 10^{8} / \mathrm{s}$, while $\Lambda_{b} \to K^{-} \Lambda_{c}$ rate is $(2.44 \pm 0.020) \times 10^{8} / \mathrm{s}$.
* The symmetric mass for bottom hadrons is $M_{b}=5475$ MeV and for charm hadrons $M_{c}=2146$ MeV, obtained from spin averages.
* The deviation of the kinematical factor from the symmetric limit is at most 5% for the decay modes considered.
* Predicted partial decay rate for $\Lambda_{b} \to \rho^{-} \Lambda_{c}$ is $[10.9 \pm 1.7] \times 10^{9} / \mathrm{s}$.
* Predicted partial decay rate for $\Lambda_{b} \to D_{s}^{-} \Lambda_{c}$ is $(14.1\pm 1.9) \times 10^{9} / \mathrm{s}$.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical supersymmetry between the $\bar s$ quark and $ud$ diquark proposed by Amano, Jido, and Leupold (2019) from hadron spectra to weak decay wavefunctions. The supersymmetry is related to the V(3) algebra introduced by Miyazawa for mesons and baryons, and the authors use the topological classification of weak decays from Ref. WeakPro. The sum rule enables predictions for unmeasured decay rates of $\bar B_{s}^{0}$ and $\Lambda_{b}$, and its verification would support the diquark as a valid effective constituent in heavy hadrons.
