# System dependence of away-side broadening and $α$-clustering light nuclei structure effect in dihadron azimuthal correlations

> A collision system scan involving $α$-clustered $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O is studied by using a multiphase transport model for central collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.37$ TeV. Background subtracted away-side dihadron azimuthal correlation is performed via the zero yield at minimum (ZYAM) method from raw signals, and the quantitative parameters, such as RMS width and Kurtosis, seem nicely follow the $A^{-1/3}$ law of the system size if the nucleus has the normal Woods-Saxon nucleon distribution. However, for $α$-clustering light nuclei, specifically for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, the RMS width and Kurtosis of away-side azimuthal correlation are deviated from the baseline of $A^{-1/3}$ law. In addition, the momentum dependence of away-side broadening parameters is also presented. The results show that there is a distinction in away-side broadening parameters of dihadron correlation function between the Woods-Saxon distribution and the $α$-clustered structures, which sheds light on that the collision system scan for dihadron azimuthal correlation as a potential probe to distinguish $α$-clustered nuclei.

## Key claim

The RMS width and Kurtosis of away-side dihadron azimuthal correlations follow the \(A^{-1/3}\) law of system size for nuclei with normal Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions, but for \(\alpha\)-clustered \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O these parameters deviate from that baseline, suggesting dihadron azimuthal correlation as a potential probe to distinguish \(\alpha\)-clustered nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the multiphase transport (AMPT) model to simulate central collisions for systems from \(^{10}\)B + \(^{10}\)B to \(^{197}\)Au + \(^{197}\)Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.37\) TeV. Dihadron azimuthal correlation functions were constructed from charged hadrons (\(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), \(p\), \(\bar{p}\)) with trigger particles in \(2 < p_T < 6\) GeV/\(c\) and associated particles in \(0.2 < p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\) within \(|y| < 1\); background from collective flow was subtracted using the zero yield at minimum (ZYAM) method with mixed events matched by second and third order event plane angles. For \(\alpha\)-clustered \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O, initial nucleon distributions were set as triangular (3\(\alpha\)) and tetrahedral (4\(\alpha\)) configurations, respectively, with cluster positions from the EQMD model.

## Key numbers

* RMS width of away-side correlation for Woods-Saxon nuclei follows \(k A^{-1/3} + c\) with \(k = -0.60\) and \(c = 1.00\) (string melting AMPT) or \(k = -0.45\) and \(c = 0.93\) (default AMPT) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.37\) TeV.
* Kurtosis of away-side correlation for Woods-Saxon nuclei follows \(k A^{-1/3} + c\) with \(k = 0.94\) and \(c = -1.22\) (string melting) or \(k = 1.05\) and \(c = -1.28\) (default AMPT) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.37\) TeV.
* Ratio \(|\text{Kurtosis}|/\text{RMS}\) for Woods-Saxon nuclei follows \(k A^{-2} + c\) with \(k = -1.02\) and \(c = 1.17\) (string melting) or \(k = -2.05\) and \(c = 1.25\) (default AMPT) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.37\) TeV.
* For \(\alpha\)-clustered \(^{12}\)C, the triangular arrangement places \(\alpha\) clusters 3.10 fm apart; for \(\alpha\)-clustered \(^{16}\)O, the tetrahedral side length is 3.42 fm.
* The \(\alpha\)-clustered \(^{12}\)C + \(^{12}\)C system shows a smaller RMS width and higher correlation yield around \(\Delta\phi \approx \pi\) compared to its Woods-Saxon counterpart; the \(\alpha\)-clustered \(^{16}\)O + \(^{16}\)O system shows a larger RMS width and lower yield at the peak.

## Context

This work extends previous studies on jet quenching and dihadron azimuthal correlations in heavy-ion collisions (Zhang 2011, Aggarwal 2010) and builds on earlier proposals that initial \(\alpha\)-clustering nuclear structure could be distinguished via high-energy collisions (Broniowski 2014). The system scan approach was proposed in prior work by the same group using collective flow ratios and forward-backward multiplicity correlations (Li 2020, Li 2021). The results suggest that away-side broadening parameters, particularly kurtosis, can serve as a probe for \(\alpha\)-clustering in light nuclei such as \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O, and the authors note that the distinction is more pronounced in the default AMPT model (without partonic interactions) than in the string-melting version, indicating sensitivity to the partonic evolution stage.
