# Determination of the strong coupling constant and the Collins-Soper kernel from the energy-energy correlator in $e^+e^-$ collisions

> We have conducted the first simultaneous global fit of the strong coupling constant $α_s$ and the Collins-Soper (CS) kernel using the energy-energy correlators (EEC) of $e^{+} e^{-}$ collisions in the back-to-back limit. This analysis, based on the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization of EEC at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic ($\rm{N}^{3} \rm{LL}$) accuracy, yields $α_s$ consistent with the world average. We have tested two different parametrizations for the non-perturbative CS kernel and found both to align with results obtained from lattice QCD and fits on semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan process.

## Key claim

The authors performed the first simultaneous global fit of the strong coupling constant and the Collins-Soper kernel using energy-energy correlator data from e+e- collisions in the back-to-back limit, finding that the extracted strong coupling constant is consistent with the world average and that two different non-perturbative parametrizations of the CS kernel both align with lattice QCD and other phenomenological extractions.

## Method

The analysis uses transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization of the energy-energy correlator at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (N3LL) accuracy, with the non-singular contribution computed at next-to-leading order. The authors performed a chi-squared minimization fit of four parameters (the strong coupling constant at the Z-boson mass, two parameters for the non-perturbative Sudakov factor, and one parameter for the non-perturbative CS kernel) to 93 data points from 8 datasets across center-of-mass energies from 29.0 GeV to 91.2 GeV, restricting the fit to the back-to-back region of 145 degrees to 175 degrees. Fit uncertainties were quantified using the replica method with 200 replicas, and theoretical uncertainties from scale variations were assessed using the scan method with 200 sets of initial scales varied within a factor of two.

## Key numbers

- Strong coupling constant: alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.1193 (+0.0009/-0.0009 fit uncertainty, +0.0008/-0.0013 theory uncertainty) for Fit 1; 0.1193 (+0.0010/-0.0008 fit, +0.0009/-0.0013 theory) for Fit 2
- Total chi-squared per degree of freedom: 0.95 for both fits (84.8 for Fit 1, 84.9 for Fit 2 over 93 data points)
- Non-perturbative Sudakov parameter a_2 = 1.152 for both fits, indicating near-linear behavior
- Non-perturbative CS kernel parameter g_2 = 0.076 (+0.014/-0.013 fit, +0.015/-0.012 theory) GeV^2 for Fit 1
- Non-perturbative CS kernel parameter g'_2 = 0.194 (+0.019/-0.020 fit, +0.037/-0.028) for Fit 2
- Center-of-mass energies of fitted datasets: 91.2 GeV (OPAL, SLD), 59.5 GeV and 53.3 GeV (TOPAZ), 43.5 GeV and 34.8 GeV (TASSO), 29.0 GeV (MARKII and MAC)

## Context

This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators in the back-to-back limit using TMD factorization, building on the theoretical framework of Ebert et al. (2020) and the N3LL resummation techniques. The extracted Collins-Soper kernel is compared with phenomenological extractions from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes by Scimemi and Vladimirov (2019), Sun et al. (2014), Moos et al. (2023), and Bacchetta et al. (2024), as well as with lattice QCD calculations by Avkhadiev et al. (2024), Bollweg et al. (2024), and the Lattice Parton Collaboration (2023). The results enable joint fits of EEC data with other TMD processes for future studies at the Large Hadron Collider and the Electron-Ion Collider.
