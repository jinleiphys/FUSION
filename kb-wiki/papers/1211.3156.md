# Quarkonium at non-zero isospin density

> We calculate the energies of quarkonium bound states in the presence of a medium of nonzero isospin density using lattice QCD. The medium, created using a canonical (fixed isospin charge) approach, induces a reduction of the quarkonium energies. As the isospin density increases, the energy shifts first increase and then saturate. The saturation occurs at an isospin density close to that where previously a qualitative change in the behaviour of the energy density of the medium has been observed, which was conjectured to correspond to a transition from a pion gas to a Bose-Einstein condensed phase. The reduction of the quarkonium energies becomes more pronounced as the heavy-quark mass is decreased, similar to the behaviour seen in two-colour QCD at non-zero quark chemical potential. In the process of our analysis, the $η_b$-$π$ and $Υ$-$π$ scattering phase shifts are determined at low momentum. An interpolation of the scattering lengths to the physical pion mass gives $a_{η_b,π} = 0.0025(8)(6)$ fm and $a_{Υ,π} = 0.0030(9)(7)$ fm.

## Key claim

The paper finds that quarkonium bound state energies are reduced in a medium of nonzero isospin density, with the energy shift first increasing and then saturating at an isospin density close to where the medium is conjectured to transition from a pion gas to a Bose-Einstein condensed phase; the saturation and the magnitude of the shift depend on the heavy-quark mass, with lighter quarks showing more pronounced effects.

## Method

The authors use lattice QCD with a canonical (fixed isospin charge) approach to create a medium of pions at nonzero isospin density, and compute quarkonium correlation functions using anisotropic lattice NRQCD for the heavy quarks. They construct ratios of correlators involving both the quarkonium state and the many-pion system to extract the energy shift of the combined system relative to the sum of individual energies, and also determine quarkonium-pion scattering phase shifts using the finite-volume Lüscher formalism.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length at physical pion mass for η_b-π: a_{η_b,π} = 0.0025(8)(6) fm
- Scattering length at physical pion mass for Υ-π: a_{Υ,π} = 0.0030(9)(7) fm
- At m_π ≈ 390 MeV, m_π a_{η_b,π} = 0.039(13) and m_π r_{η_b,π} = 4.7(3.7)
- At m_π ≈ 390 MeV, m_π a_{Υ,π} = 0.047(14) and m_π r_{Υ,π} = 5.8(3.3)
- The saturation of the energy shift occurs at an effective isospin chemical potential μ_I ∼ μ_{I,peak} = 1.3 m_π
- The screening shift in the static potential from a potential model fit gives α = -8(3) MeV fm² at m_π ∼ 320 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the static quark-antiquark potential in a pionic medium by Detmold (2008) and investigations of the energy density of isospin-charged matter by Detmold (2012), which suggested a transition from a pion gas to a Bose-Einstein condensed phase. The results enable future studies of quarkonium as a probe of dense QCD matter, and the extracted scattering lengths provide input for effective field theory descriptions of quarkonium-pion interactions, complementing earlier lattice calculations of charmonium-pion scattering by Yokokawa (2006) and Liu (2008, 2009).
