# Non-strange partner of strangeonium-like state Y(2175)

> Inspired by the observed Y(2175) state, we predict its non-strange partner Y(1915), which has a resonance structure with mass around 1915 MeV and width about $317\sim 354$ MeV. Experimental search for Y(1915) is proposed by analyzing the $ωf_0(980)$ or $ωππ$ invariant mass spectrum of the $e^+e^-\to ωf_0(980), ωππ$ and $J/ψ\to ηωf_0(980)$ processes, which are accessible at Belle, BaBar, BESIII and forthcoming BelleII. Considering similarity between two families, the comparison of the mass spectra of $ω$ and $ϕ$ families can provide important information on the 1D state of $ϕ$ family, $ϕ(1910)$, which has a very broad resonance structure with mass around 1910 MeV regarded as the strangeonium partner of $ω(1650)$. This also answers the question why the 1D state $ϕ(1910)$ is still missing in experiment. This is supported by our former study on the properties of Y(2175), which explains Y(2175) as the 2D strangeonium because our theoretical total width is comparable with the Belle data.

## Key claim
The paper predicts a non-strange partner of the observed Y(2175) state, called Y(1915), with mass around 1915 MeV and width 317–354 MeV, but this prediction depends on the assumption that Y(2175) is a conventional 2D strangeonium and that the mass difference between the non-strange partner and ω(1420) equals the mass difference between Y(2175) and φ(1680) (about 495 MeV).

## Method
The authors use the quark pair creation model (also known as the 3P0 model) to calculate Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka-allowed strong decay widths of the predicted Y(1915), ω(1650), ω(1420), φ(1910), Y(2175), and φ(1680). Harmonic oscillator wave functions are adopted for meson spatial wave functions, with the oscillator parameter R varied over a range (3.5–4.5 GeV⁻¹ for ω-family states, 3.94–4.84 GeV⁻¹ for Y(2175)) to produce the decay width dependence. Mass estimates are obtained by comparing mass spectra of the ω and φ meson families and by analyzing Regge trajectories on the (n, M²) plane.

## Key numbers
- Predicted mass of Y(1915): around 1915 MeV.
- Predicted total width of Y(1915): 317–354 MeV (for R = 3.5–4.5 GeV⁻¹).
- Partial decay width of Y(1915) → f₀(980)ω: 5.86–16.22 MeV.
- Partial decay width ratios for Y(1915): Γ(b₁(1235)π)/Γ(ρ(1450)π) ≈ 1.3–3.5; Γ(a₀(980)ρ)/Γ(f₀(980)ω) ≈ 9–19.
- Predicted mass of φ(1910): around 1910 MeV.
- Predicted total width of φ(1910): 822–1047 MeV.
- Branching ratio of φ(1910) → K₁(1270)K: (79–84)% of total width.
- Mass difference between Y(2175) and φ(1680): about 495 MeV.
- Mass difference between ω(1650) and ω(1420): about 230 MeV.
- Mass difference between ω(782) and φ(1020): about 240 MeV.
- Mass difference between ω(1420) and φ(1680): about 260 MeV.
- Regge trajectory slope parameter μ² = 1.36 GeV².
- Predicted masses from Regge trajectories: m_ω(3S) = 1830 MeV; m_φ(3S) = 1940 MeV.
- Total width of φ(1680) at R = 3.4 GeV⁻¹: 93 MeV; ratio Γ(KK)/Γ(K*K) = 0.07.

## Context
This work extends the study of the strangeonium-like state Y(2175) observed by BaBar (Aubert 2006), BESII (2007), and Belle (Shen 2009), and is motivated by the similarity between the ω and φ meson families. The authors previously explained Y(2175) as a 2D strangeonium (Chen 2011). The prediction of Y(1915) as a non-strange partner completes a flavor series analogous to Y(4260) and Υ(10860). The predicted φ(1910) is identified as the missing 1D strangeonium partner of ω(1650) (Barnes 1996), and its very broad width (822–1047 MeV) explains why it has not been observed. The results enable experimental searches at Belle, BaBar, BESIII, and Belle II via e⁺e⁻ → ωf₀(980), ωππ, and J/ψ → ηωf₀(980) processes.
