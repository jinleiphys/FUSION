# Electromagnetic Radiation and in-Medium Effects

> The theory of thermal photon and dilepton emission from a hot and dense hadronic gas, as well as from the Quark-Gluon Plasma, is reviewed in the context of extracting in-medium properties of the matter constituents. In phenomenological applications to ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions we focus on recent photon and dilepton spectra as measured by WA98 and CERES/NA45, respectively, at CERN-SPS energies.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theory of thermal photon and dilepton emission from hot and dense hadronic gas and Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), arguing that while current hadronic calculations with medium-modified ρ-meson spectral functions are in reasonable agreement with SPS dilepton data, new low-transverse-momentum photon data from WA98 show an appreciable excess beyond theoretical expectations that may require new mechanisms in the emission rates or space-time evolution.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical frameworks for evaluating electromagnetic emission rates, including hadronic many-body theory calculations of the ρ-meson propagator and complete leading-order perturbative QCD results for the QGP. These rates are then convoluted over a simple expanding isentropic and isotropic fireball model, consistent with measured particle yields and radial flow, to calculate photon and dilepton spectra for comparison with WA98 and CERES/NA45 data at CERN-SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures of 200-250 MeV are inferred from thermal source fits to intermediate dilepton masses (1 GeV < M < 3 GeV) and direct photon spectra
- The critical energy density for QGP formation is approximately 1 GeV/fm³, while RHIC collisions produce energy densities of about 20 GeV/fm³
- The ρ-meson spectral function calculations use baryon densities up to 2.6 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) and temperatures up to 180 MeV
- The fine structure constant α_em = 1/137 is used in the emission rate formulas
- The vacuum ρ→ee decay width is approximately 11 times the ω→ee width and 5.5 times the φ→ee width
- The matching scale for operator product expansion is Λ ≈ 1.1 GeV
- The quark condensate in vacuum is ⟨q̄q⟩ ≈ (-250 MeV)³
- The dual mass scale M_dual ≈ 1.5 GeV separates the perturbative q̄q continuum from the vector meson dominated region
- The standard initial temperature for SPS thermal yield is T̄₀ ≈ 205 MeV with formation time τ₀ = 1 fm/c
- At T = 170 MeV, nucleon and Δ(1232) densities are approximately equal
- For RHIC, thermal radiation at low mass is expected to exceed open-charm contributions by a factor of 2-3

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions previously developed by Rapp and Wambach (2000), Alam et al. (2001), Gale and Haglin (2003), and Brown and Rho (2004). The paper addresses new data from WA98 (2003) and CERES/NA45 (2004) that show excess radiation at low transverse momenta and high centrality, respectively, potentially challenging existing theoretical models. The work enables future investigations into whether nonperturbative interactions in the QGP modify the picture of matching hadronic and QGP electromagnetic correlators, and at what mass scale such effects appear.
