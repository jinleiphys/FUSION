# Low-energy ${}^{12}$C continuum states in three-$α$ model

> The electric multipole strength distributions for transitions from the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+)$ ground state to $3α$ ($0^+$, $1^-$, $2^+$, and $3^-$) continuum states are studied in terms of $3α$ model. Several sets of the $3α$ Hamiltonian are introduced phenomenologically with conventional $α$-$α$ interaction potentials and $3α$ potentials with different range parameters. The transition strength distributions are obtained from wave functions of $3α$ bound- and continuum states calculated by the Faddeev three-body formalism. From these strength distributions, the resonance parameters (energy, $3α$-decay width, and transition strength) of $3α$ resonance states are extracted. Dependencies of these observables on interaction potential models are studied to examine the introduced interaction models. Using the transition strength distributions, excitation-energy spectra in the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(α,α^\prime)3α$ reaction are calculated to compare with experimental data.

# Low-energy ${}^{12}$C continuum states in three-$\alpha$ model

## Key claim
The electric multipole strength distributions for transitions from the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+)$ ground state to $3\alpha$ continuum states reveal resonance structures whose properties (energy, width, transition strength) depend significantly on the choice of phenomenological $2\alpha$ and $3\alpha$ interaction potentials, particularly the range parameter of the three-body potential. The $0_3^+$ peak around $E=1.5$ MeV is shown to be predominantly a genuine resonance rather than a "Ghost Anomaly" from the Hoyle state, as R-matrix calculations with channel radii up to 11 fm produce Ghost peaks with insufficient strength to explain the $3\alpha$ model results.

## Method
The Faddeev three-body formalism was used to calculate $3\alpha$ bound and continuum wave functions for $0^+$, $1^-$, $2^+$, and $3^-$ states, employing phenomenological Hamiltonians with Ali-Bodmer $2\alpha$ potentials (AB(A') and AB(D) versions) and Gaussian $3\alpha$ potentials with four different range parameters ($a_3 = 4.0$, 3.4, 3.0, and 2.6 fm). Electric multipole transition strength distributions (E0, E1, E2, E3) from the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+)$ ground state to $3\alpha$ continuum states were computed, and resonance parameters were extracted by fitting peaks with a Breit-Wigner formula. Excitation-energy spectra for the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\alpha^\prime)3\alpha$ reaction were calculated using plane-wave impulse approximation and compared with experimental data from Itoh et al. (2011).

## Key numbers
- Hoyle state ($0_2^+$): resonance energy $E_r = 0.3795$ MeV (fitted to experiment); calculated $3\alpha$-decay widths $\Gamma_{3\alpha} = 9.9$-$10.1$ eV for AB(A') models and $6.0$-$6.8$ eV for AB(D) models; experimental value is $9.3 \pm 0.9$ eV (Ke17)
- Hoyle state E0 strength: $B(\mathrm{E0}:0_1^+ \to 0_2^+) = 40.1$-$50.3$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ for AB(A') models and $17.1$-$34.8$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ for AB(D) models; experimental value is $29.9 \pm 1.0$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (Ch10)
- ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+)$ ground state binding energy: $6.337$-$6.725$ MeV for AB(A') models and $7.281$-$7.789$ MeV for AB(D) models; experimental value is $7.2746$ MeV
- ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_1^+)$ r.m.s. radius: $1.69$-$1.96$ fm across all models; experimental value is $1.8823 \pm 0.0036$ fm
- $B(\mathrm{E2}:2_1^+ \to 0_1^+)$: $4.66$-$8.68$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ for AB(A') models and $6.15$-$9.43$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ for AB(D) models; experimental value is $7.63 \pm 0.19$ $e^2\mathrm{fm}^4$ (DA20)
- Quadrupole moment $Q_\mathrm{S}(2_1^+)$: $5.43$-$6.65$ $e\mathrm{fm}^2$ for AB(A') models and $5.78$-$6.46$ $e\mathrm{fm}^2$ for AB(D) models; experimental value is $+6 \pm 3$ $e\mathrm{fm}^2$ (Ve83)
- $0_3^+$ peak located around $E \approx 1.5$ MeV; $0_4^+$ peak located in range $E = 3.5$-$4.5$ MeV (model dependent)
- E2-TSD shows peaks at $E \approx 2$ MeV and $E \approx 7$ MeV, with a tiny structure around $E = 4.5$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the author's previous $3\alpha$ model calculations (Ishikawa 2013, 2014a, 2014b, 2016) that studied $0^+$ and $2^+$ continuum states, now including $1^-$ and $3^-$ multipoles and systematic variation of the $3\alpha$ potential range. The results address ongoing debates about unestablished ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ resonance states around $E=3$ MeV, where compilations (Ajzenberg-Selove 1985, 1990; Kelley et al. 2017) list a tentative $0^+$ state at $E=3.0$ MeV with large width, while recent R-matrix analyses (Li et al. 2022a, 2022b) suggest a doublet structure. The calculated $0_3^+$ peak is shown to be a genuine resonance rather than a Ghost Anomaly, consistent with the R-matrix findings. The work enables improved calculations of reactions involving $3\alpha$ breakup final states, demonstrated here by comparison with ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\alpha^\prime)3\alpha$ experimental data from Itoh et al. (2011).
