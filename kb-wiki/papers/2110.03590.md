# Jet Drift and Collective Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the tomographic applications of a new phenomenon we dub "jet drift" -- the deflection of high-energy particles and jets toward the direction of a flowing medium -- to the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions. While the physics of jet drift is quite general, for specificity we consider the case of photon-jet production at mid-rapidity. Beginning with the simplest possible model, a large slab of uniformly flowing plasma, we systematically introduce the geometrical elements of a heavy-ion collision in a simple optical Glauber model. We find that the moving medium causes the jet to drift in the direction of the flow, bending its trajectory and leaving detailed signatures of the flow pattern in the distribution of $γ+ \: \mathrm{jet}$ acoplanarities. In the elliptical geometries produced in non-central collisions, this drift effect leads to a strong geometry coupling which persists despite the addition of event-by-event fluctuations in the jet production point, impact parameter, and acoplanarity. We propose a new observable to measure the jet drift effect through the correlation of $γ+ \: \mathrm{jet}$ acoplanarities with the elliptic flow of soft particles. Preliminary estimates suggest this correlation may be studied at sPHENIX and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the deflection of high-energy jets toward the direction of a flowing medium ("jet drift") produces measurable asymmetries in the photon-jet acoplanarity distribution, and that this effect persists despite event-by-event fluctuations in the jet production point, impact parameter, and acoplanarity, provided the medium has a nonzero collective velocity.

## Method

The authors start from the first-order-in-opacity expression for the final-state jet distribution in a moving medium derived by Sadofyev et al. (2021), using the Gyulassy-Wang potential and a tree-level initial jet distribution. They evaluate vector-valued (odd) moments of the acoplanarity distribution for a constant slab of uniformly flowing plasma, then systematically introduce the geometrical elements of a heavy-ion collision using a simple optical Glauber model with an elliptical Gaussian toy model for the medium density and flow profile.

## Key numbers

* The mean acoplanarity ⟨Δθ⟩ is given by 3 ∫ (dt/λ(t)) (u⊥(t)/(1-u∥(t))) (μ²(t)/E²) ln(E/μ(t)) within the leading-logarithmic approximation, where μ is the Debye mass, λ the mean free path, and u the fluid velocity.
* For a constant slab of length L, the odd moment ⟨p⊥ p⊥ᵏ⟩ = (u⊥/(1-u∥)) (L/λ) (μᵏ⁺²/E) I(k), with I(k) = -(π/8)(k+2)(k+12) csc(kπ/2).
* The extrema of the moment as a function of jet angle θ occur at θ_peak = α ± arccos(u), where α is the fluid direction and u the fluid speed.
* The ratio of slopes at the two zero crossings is m(θ=α±π)/m(θ=α) = (u-1)/(u+1), which isolates the fluid speed from all other medium properties.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for sub-eikonal jet-flow coupling constructed by Sadofyev et al. (2021) and the concept of antisymmetric jet observables proposed by He et al. (2020) and Sadofyev et al. (2021). It provides a systematic study of how jet drift couples to the elliptical geometry of non-central heavy-ion collisions, proposing a new observable, the correlation of photon-jet acoplanarity with the elliptic flow of soft particles, that could be studied at sPHENIX and the LHC. The paper enables future work incorporating state-of-the-art hydrodynamic backgrounds and other jet processes beyond photon-jet production.
