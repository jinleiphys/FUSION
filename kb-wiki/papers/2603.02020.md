# Explicit asymptotics of coupling matrix elements for central potentials in the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method

> The analytic structure and asymptotic behavior of channel-coupling potentials in three-body systems are investigated within the framework of the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method. The coupling between different Jacobi partitions is expressed using Raynal--Revai transformation coefficients and a reduced hyperangular integral that contains the two-body interaction. For central potentials, this integral is factorised into geometric and dynamical components. Explicit asymptotic scaling laws are derived for the hyperradial coupling strength in the limit of large hyperradius $ρ\to \infty$ for representative nuclear potentials: Gaussian, Yukawa, and Woods--Saxon potentials (short-range), and Coulomb potential (long-range). These short-range potentials are found to exhibit an algebraic decay $\propto ρ^{-(2\ell_{η_i}+3)}$, where $\ell_{η_i}$ is the orbital angular momentum of the interacting pair. This decay is shown to lead to efficient asymptotic decoupling of hyperspherical channels. In contrast, the Coulomb interaction yields couplings that decay only as $1/ρ$, indicating persistent channel coupling at large distances and explaining the slow convergence of hyperspherical expansions for charged systems. These results provide a quantitative basis for truncating the hyperradial domain, for example, in choosing a matching radius for scattering calculations or an upper integration limit in bound-state problems.

# Explicit asymptotics of coupling matrix elements for central potentials in the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method

## Key claim

For short-range central potentials (Gaussian, Yukawa, and Woods-Saxon), the hyperradial coupling matrix elements decay algebraically as $\rho^{-(2\ell_{\eta_i}+3)}$ in the limit of large hyperradius $\rho\to\infty$, where $\ell_{\eta_i}$ is the orbital angular momentum of the interacting pair; this asymptotic decoupling is independent of the detailed shape of the short-range potential. In contrast, the Coulomb potential yields couplings that decay only as $1/\rho$, indicating persistent channel coupling at large distances that explains the slow convergence of hyperspherical expansions for charged systems.

## Method

The authors express channel-coupling potentials between different Jacobi partitions in three-body systems using Raynal-Revai transformation coefficients and a reduced hyperangular integral containing the two-body interaction. For central potentials, the five-dimensional angular integral factorises into geometric and dynamical components, with the hyperangular integration over the four spherical angles yielding Kronecker deltas that enforce diagonality in the orbital angular momentum quantum numbers. The asymptotic scaling of the remaining one-dimensional hyperangular integral is then derived analytically for Gaussian, Yukawa, Woods-Saxon, and Coulomb potentials by evaluating the integral in the small-hyperangle limit ($\theta_i\to 0$) as $\rho\to\infty$.

## Key numbers

- Gaussian potential coupling: $J^{\ell_{\eta_i},\ell_{\lambda_i}}_{KK'}(\rho) \propto \rho^{-(2\ell_{\eta_i}+3)}$, with explicit prefactor containing $\frac{1}{2}\,\Gamma\!\left(\ell_{\eta_i}+\frac{3}{2}\right) \left(a_0\right)^{2\ell_{\eta_i}+3}$ where $a_0$ is the range parameter
- Yukawa potential coupling: $J^{\ell_{\eta_i},\ell_{\lambda_i}}_{KK'}(\rho) \propto \rho^{-(2\ell_{\eta_i}+3)}$, with prefactor containing $\frac{\Gamma(2\ell_{\eta_i}+2)}{\mu^{2\ell_{\eta_i}+2}}$ where $\mu$ is the inverse range parameter
- Woods-Saxon potential coupling: $J^{\ell_{\eta_i},\ell_{\lambda_i}}_{KK'}(\rho) \propto \rho^{-(2\ell_{\eta_i}+3)}$, with prefactor containing $e^{R/a} \, a^{2\ell_{\eta_i}+3} \, \Gamma(2\ell_{\eta_i}+3)$ where $R$ is the radius parameter and $a$ the surface diffuseness
- Coulomb potential coupling: $J^{\ell_{\eta_i},\ell_{\lambda_i}}_{KK'}(\rho) = \frac{Z_j Z_k e^2}{\rho}\, C^{\ell_{\eta_i},\ell_{\lambda_i}}_{KK'}$, decaying as $1/\rho$ with a purely geometric coefficient $C^{\ell_{\eta_i},\ell_{\lambda_i}}_{KK'}$ independent of $\rho$

## Context

This work extends the numerical demonstrations of Kleinfeld (1990) and the adiabatic hyperspherical analysis of Kvinikhidze (1991), who showed that coupling matrix elements for short-range interactions decay algebraically in the hyperradius. The explicit asymptotic formulas derived here provide quantitative criteria for truncating the hyperradial domain in practical calculations, such as choosing a matching radius for scattering calculations or an upper integration limit in bound-state problems. The results enable more efficient numerical strategies for solving three-body Faddeev equations, both for bound states and scattering processes, by quantifying when hyperspherical channels become effectively decoupled.
