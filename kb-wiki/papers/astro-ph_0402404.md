# Quark deconfinement and implications for the radius and the limiting mass of compact stars

> We study the consequences of the hadron-quark deconfinement phase transition in stellar compact objects when finite size effects between the deconfined quark phase and the hadronic phase are taken into account. We show that above a threshold value of the central pressure (gravitational mass) a neutron star is metastable to the decay (conversion) to a hybrid neutron star or to a strange star. The "mean-life time" of the metastable configuration dramatically depends on the value of the stellar central pressure. We explore the consequences of the metastability of ``massive'' neutron stars and of the existence of stable compact quark stars (hybrid neutron stars or strange stars) on the concept of limiting mass of compact stars. We discuss the implications of our scenario on the interpretation of the stellar mass and radius extracted from the spectra of several X-ray compact sources. Finally, we show that our scenario implies, as a natural consequence a two step-process which is able to explain the inferred ``delayed'' connection between supernova explosions and GRBs, giving also the correct energy to power GRBs.

## Key claim

The paper argues that pure hadronic neutron stars above a threshold central pressure are metastable to conversion into hybrid or strange quark stars, with a mean-life time that depends dramatically on the central pressure; this metastability implies that the effective maximum mass of compact stars may be lower than the classical Oppenheimer-Volkoff limit, depending on the bag constant and surface tension.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase using a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian (GM1 and GM3 parameter sets from Glendenning & Moszkowski 1991) and the quark phase using the MIT bag model with bag constant B, strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV, and α_s = 0. They apply the Lifshitz-Kagan quantum nucleation theory in the relativistic formulation of Iida & Sato (1997) to compute the nucleation time for a critical-size droplet of deconfined quark matter in cold (T=0), neutrino-free hadronic matter, accounting for finite-size effects including surface tension σ and curvature energy γ.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark matter nucleation in neutron stars (Horvath et al. 1992; Horvath 1994; Olesen & Madsen 1994; Heiselberg 1995; Grassi 1998) by focusing on quantum nucleation in cold, neutrino-free matter rather than thermal nucleation in hot protoneutron stars. It builds on the Gibbs criterion for phase equilibrium (Glendenning 1992; Müller & Serot 1995) and the finite-size effects formalism of Iida & Sato (1997). The scenario enables a reinterpretation of mass-radius constraints from X-ray sources (e.g., SAX J1808.4-3658, EXO 0748-676, 1E 1207.4-5209, RX J1856.5-3754) and provides a two-step mechanism for the delayed connection between supernovae and gamma-ray bursts.
