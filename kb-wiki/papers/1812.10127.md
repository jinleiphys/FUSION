# Chiral kinetic theory in curved spacetime

> Many-body systems with chiral fermions exhibit anomalous transport phenomena originated from quantum anomalies. Based on quantum field theory, we derive the kinetic theory for chiral fermions interacting with an external electromagnetic field and a background curved geometry. The resultant framework respects the covariance under the U(1) gauge, local Lorentz, and diffeomorphic transformations. It is particularly useful to study the gravitational or non-inertial effects for chiral systems. As the first application, we study the chiral dynamics in a rotating coordinate and clarify the roles of the Coriolis force and spin-vorticity coupling in generating the chiral vortical effect (CVE). We also show that the CVE is an intrinsic phenomenon of a rotating chiral fluid, and thus independent of observer's frame.

## Key claim

The authors derive a chiral kinetic theory in arbitrary curved spacetime that is U(1) gauge invariant and local Lorentz and diffeomorphism covariant, showing that the chiral vortical effect (CVE) is an intrinsic phenomenon of a rotating chiral fluid and thus independent of the observer's frame, with the Coriolis force and spin-vorticity coupling playing different roles depending on the observer's choice.

## Method

The authors start from quantum field theory and use the Wigner function formalism in curved spacetime, employing the horizontal lift of the covariant derivative to the cotangent bundle to maintain diffeomorphism covariance. They perform a semiclassical expansion in powers of Planck's constant to obtain the chiral kinetic equation at order O(hbar), then apply the framework to a rotating coordinate system to study the chiral vortical effect with different choices of the spin-frame vector n^mu and fluid velocity U^mu.

## Key numbers

* The CVE current in a rotating coordinate is J_CVE = hbar * omega * (mu^2 / (4 pi^2) + T^2 / 12), where omega is the angular velocity, mu is the chemical potential, and T is the temperature.
* For a rotating fluid with n^mu = U^mu_in (inertial observer), the first term in the current expression gives 1/3 of the total CVE current, while the second term (magnetization current) gives 2/3.
* The rotating coordinate metric has an infinite red-shift surface at distance r = 1/|omega| from the rotating axis, requiring |omega| much less than T or |omega| much less than mu for validity.
* The modified energy dispersion for the rotating observer is epsilon_q = |q| - (hbar/2) * (q-hat) * omega, where q is the momentum observed by the rotating observer.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory, previously restricted to flat spacetime (Stephanov 2012, Son 2012, Gao 2012, Chen 2012, Huang 2015, Hidaka 2016), to arbitrary curved backgrounds, enabling the study of gravitational and non-inertial effects on chiral transport. The framework generalizes earlier attempts that considered only rotating coordinates (Basar 2013, Dayi 2016, Huang 2018) and lacked diffeomorphism covariance, and it provides a rigorous foundation for computing the chiral vortical effect and spin-curvature forces in systems such as heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical environments.
