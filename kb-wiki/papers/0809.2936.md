# What we (would like to) know about the neutrino mass

> We present updated values for the mass-mixing parameters relevant to neutrino oscillations, with particular attention to emerging hints in favor of theta_13>0. We also discuss the status of absolute neutrino mass observables, and a possible approach to constrain theoretical uncertainties in neutrinoless double beta decay. Desiderata for all these issues are also briefly mentioned.

## Key claim
The paper presents an updated global analysis of neutrino oscillation parameters, finding a hint of a non-zero mixing angle θ₁₃ at about 1.6σ confidence level (approximately 90% C.L.), which is a tentative indication rather than a definitive discovery. The authors note that this hint emerges from combining multiple data sets and could be corroborated by future experiments.

## Method
The authors performed a global χ² analysis of neutrino oscillation data, combining solar neutrino data (including the latest SNO-III results), KamLAND reactor data, atmospheric neutrino data, long-baseline accelerator data, and CHOOZ reactor data. For absolute neutrino mass observables, they combined oscillation constraints with cosmological data sets (CMB, HST, SN-Ia, BAO, Lyα) and neutrinoless double beta decay results from the Heidelberg-Moscow and Cuoricino experiments. They also developed a method to constrain nuclear matrix element uncertainties in neutrinoless double beta decay by using multiple independent decay data (2ν2β, EC, β⁻) to overconstrain the QRPA parameters (g_pp, g_A) for ¹⁰⁰Mo and ¹¹⁶Cd.

## Key numbers
- sin²θ₁₃ best fit: 0.016 ± 0.010 (1σ), with a 1.6σ preference for θ₁₃ > 0
- δm² (solar mass splitting) best fit: 7.67 × 10⁻⁵ eV² (1σ range: 7.48–7.83 × 10⁻⁵ eV²)
- Δm² (atmospheric mass splitting) best fit: 2.39 × 10⁻³ eV² (1σ range: 2.31–2.50 × 10⁻³ eV²)
- sin²θ₁₂ best fit: 0.312 (1σ range: 0.294–0.331)
- sin²θ₂₃ best fit: 0.466 (1σ range: 0.408–0.539)
- Heidelberg-Moscow 0ν2β claim: half-life T⁰ν₁/₂ = 2.23⁺⁰·⁴⁴₋₀·₃₁ × 10²⁵ y (1σ), implying m_ββ range 0.16–0.52 eV (2σ)
- Cuoricino 0ν2β limit: T⁰ν₁/₂ > 2.5 × 10²⁴ y at 95% C.L., implying m_ββ < 0.23 eV (favorable NME) or < 0.85 eV (unfavorable NME) at 2σ
- Cosmological upper limits on Σ (sum of neutrino masses) at 2σ: < 1.19 eV (CMB only), < 0.75 eV (CMB+HST+SN-Ia), < 0.60 eV (adding BAO), < 0.19 eV (adding Lyα)
- For the degenerate mass scenario compatible with oscillation+CMB+0ν2β claim: m₁ ≈ m₂ ≈ m₃ ∈ [0.15, 0.46] eV (2σ)

## Context
This work extends the global oscillation parameter analyses previously performed by the same group (Fogli et al., 2008) and others, incorporating the latest solar neutrino data from SNO-III and updated cosmological constraints from WMAP 5-year data. The hint of θ₁₃ > 0 builds on earlier indications from atmospheric neutrino data (Fogli et al., 2008) and from the tension between solar and KamLAND data. The paper enables future comparisons between oscillation parameters, neutrinoless double beta decay searches, and cosmological mass limits, while highlighting the need for independent verification of the Heidelberg-Moscow 0ν2β claim and for reduction of nuclear matrix element uncertainties.
