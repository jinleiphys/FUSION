# The nuclear physics of OHe

> A recent composite-dark-matter scenario assumes that the dominant fraction of dark matter consists of O-helium (OHe) dark atoms, in which a lepton-like doubly charged particle O is bound with a primordial helium nucleus. It liberates the physics of dark matter from unknown features of new physics, but it demands a deep understanding of the details of known nuclear and atomic physics, which are still unclear. Here, we consider in detail the physics of the binding of OHe to various nuclei of interest for direct dark matter searches. We show that standard quantum mechanics leads to bound states in the keV region, but does not seem to provide a simple mechanism that stabilizes them. The crucial role of a barrier in the OHe-nucleus potential is confirmed for such a stabilization.

## Key claim

Standard quantum mechanics produces bound states of OHe with nuclei in the keV energy region, but does not provide a simple mechanism to stabilize them; the existence of a barrier in the OHe-nucleus potential is confirmed as crucial for such stabilization, yet neither classical nor perturbative treatments reproduce such a barrier.

## Method

The authors treated the OHe atom as a hydrogenoid system with a Bohr radius of approximately 1.81 fm and considered its interaction with various target nuclei. They used a semiclassical WKB method to solve the radial Schrodinger equation for the OHe-nucleus system, incorporating a screened Coulomb potential and a Stark-effect polarization term, together with a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential parametrized from alpha-nucleus scattering experiments. They also performed a perturbative analysis up to third order in the interaction between the OHe atom and an approaching nucleus, calculating the energy shift and polarization of the helium wavefunction as a function of nuclear distance.

## Key numbers

- OHe Bohr radius: approximately 1.81 fm, derived from the formula \(r_0 = 1/(m_{He} Z_O Z_{He} \alpha)\)
- Unperturbed ground-state energy of the OHe atom: approximately -1.58 MeV
- For the OHe-\(^{23}\)Na system with a screened Coulomb potential only, the highest bound state at \(l=0\) lies at -3 keV when the nuclear well depth \(V_0\) is set to 31.9 MeV
- For the OHe-\(^{74}\)Ge system with a screened Coulomb potential only, the highest bound state at \(l=0\) lies at 3.88 keV with \(V_0 = 45\) MeV
- For the OHe-\(^{127}\)I system with a screened Coulomb potential only, the highest bound state at \(l=0\) lies at 0.500 keV with \(V_0 = 45\) MeV
- For the OHe-\(^{132}\)Xe system with a screened Coulomb potential only, the highest bound state at \(l=0\) lies at 540 keV with \(V_0 = 45\) MeV
- For the OHe-\(^{201}\)Tl system with a screened Coulomb potential only, the highest bound state at \(l=0\) lies at 15.6 keV with \(V_0 = 45\) MeV
- When the Stark potential is added, the highest bound state at \(l=0\) for \(^{74}\)Ge becomes 1.16 keV, for \(^{127}\)I becomes 2.31 keV, for \(^{132}\)Xe becomes 2.33 keV, for \(^{184}\)W becomes 1.86 keV, and for \(^{201}\)Tl becomes 52.7 keV, all with \(V_0 = 45\) MeV
- The Woods-Saxon nuclear potential parameters used are \(V_0 = 30\) MeV for nuclei with \(Z_A \leq 25\) and \(V_0 = 45\) MeV for \(Z_A > 25\), with diffuseness \(a = 0.5\) fm and radius \(R_*(\text{fm}) = 1.35 \times A^{1/3} + 1.3\) fm

## Context

This work extends the composite-dark-matter scenario proposed by Khlopov and collaborators (2006 onward), in which dark matter consists of OHe atoms formed by a doubly charged heavy particle O\(^{--}\) bound to a primordial helium nucleus. The paper directly addresses the problem of OHe-nucleus interactions that was previously assumed to feature a repulsive barrier, as needed to explain the positive signals from DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA and the null results from XENON100 and CDMS. The authors show that neither classical nor perturbative quantum treatments produce such a barrier, leaving the resolution to a full numerical solution of the three-body Schrodinger equation in the regime where perturbative approaches break down.
