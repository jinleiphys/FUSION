# Nuclear equation of state at high density and the properties of neutron stars

> We discuss the relativistic nuclear equation of state (EOS) using a relativistic transport model in heavy-ion collisions. From the baryon flow for $Au + Au$ systems at SIS to AGS energies and above we find that the strength of the vector potential has to be reduced moderately at high density or at high relative momenta to describe the flow data at 1-10 A GeV. We use the same dynamical model to calculate the nuclear EOS and then employ this to calculate the gross structure of the neutron star considering the core to be composed of neutrons with an admixture of protons, electrons, muons, sigmas and lambdas at zero temperature. We then discuss these gross properties of neutron stars such as maximum mass and radius in contrast to the observational values.

# Nuclear equation of state at high density and the properties of neutron stars

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state (EOS) derived from heavy-ion collision flow data, when implemented with a momentum-dependent reduction of the vector potential at high density, yields neutron star maximum masses (1.94–1.97 M⊙) that fall within the observationally allowed range (1.4–2.2 M⊙), but only if the vector potential strength is substantially reduced at high density relative to the standard NL3 model.

## Method

The author extends the relativistic mean-field model by introducing phenomenological momentum-dependent form factors at the vector and scalar vertices, with cut-off parameters Λv1=0.37 GeV, Λv2=0.9 GeV for the vector potential and Λs1=0.71 GeV, Λs2=1.0 GeV for the scalar potential. These form factors are fitted to reproduce the Schrödinger-equivalent potential up to 1 GeV and the baryon flow data for Au+Au collisions at SIS to AGS energies (1–10 A GeV). The resulting nuclear EOS is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for neutron star structure, assuming a core composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and hyperons (Λ and Σ−) at zero temperature, with hyperon-meson couplings fixed by hypernuclei data.

## Key numbers

- Vector potential cut-off parameters: Λv1=0.37 GeV, Λv2=0.9 GeV; scalar potential cut-off parameters: Λs1=0.71 GeV, Λs2=1.0 GeV
- At ρ=8ρ0, vector potential = 1250 MeV and scalar potential = −511 MeV for the momentum-dependent NLE model, compared to 1740 MeV and −735 MeV respectively for the NL3 model
- Maximum neutron star masses: 2.18 M⊙ (NL3), 1.94 M⊙ (NLE1), 1.97 M⊙ (NLE2)
- Corresponding radii at maximum mass: 11.9 km (NL3), 10.7 km (NLE1), 10.8 km (NLE2)
- Central densities at maximum mass: 2.0×10^15 g cm−3 (NL3), 2.2×10^15 g cm−3 (NLE1 and NLE2)
- Hyperons (Σ− and Λ) appear above 2 times nuclear matter density in all models
- Proton concentration crosses the threshold value 0.11 at approximately 1.5 times nuclear matter density, enabling direct URCA cooling

## Context

This work extends relativistic mean-field calculations of neutron star structure (Glendenning 1992, Ghosh et al. 1995) by incorporating momentum-dependent forces derived from heavy-ion collision flow data at SIS to AGS energies (Liu et al. 1998, Pinkenburg et al. 1999). The results support the conclusion that the nuclear EOS must be soft at high density (Heiselberg 1999, Balberg & Gal 1997, Shen et al. 1998) to keep neutron star masses below 2 M⊙ as indicated by observations (Thorsett 1999). The approach enables future work incorporating kaon condensation and momentum-dependent kaon potentials from heavy-ion data (work in progress by the author).
