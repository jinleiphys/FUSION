# A study of the $KN$-$K^*N$ coupled systems

> We study the strangeness $+1$ meson-baryon systems to obtain improved $KN$ and $K^*N$ amplitudes and to look for a possible resonance formation by the $KN$-$K^*N$ coupled interaction. We obtain amplitudes for light vector meson-baryon systems by implementing the $s$-, $t$-, $u$- channel diagrams and a contact interaction. The pseudoscalar meson-baryon interactions are obtained by relying on the Weinberg-Tomozawa theorem. The transition amplitudes between the systems consisting of pseudoscalars and vector mesons are calculated by extending the Kroll-Ruderman term for pion photoproduction replacing the photon by a vector meson. We fix the subtraction constants required to calculate the loops by fitting our $KN$ amplitudes to the data available for the isospin 0 and 1 $s$-wave phase shifts. We provide the scattering lengths and the total cross sections for the $KN$ and $K^* N$ systems obtained in our model, which can be useful in future in-medium calculations. Our amplitudes do not correspond to formation of any resonance in none of the isospin and spin configurations.

## Key claim

The authors find that coupling the $KN$ and $K^*N$ channels does not produce any resonance in the strangeness +1 sector, despite the attractive contributions from some diagrams, once the subtraction constants are constrained by fitting to experimental $KN$ phase shift data.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in its on-shell factorization form for the $KN$-$K^*N$ coupled system in s-wave. The $KN$ kernel is obtained from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian using the Weinberg-Tomozawa theorem, while the $K^*N$ kernel includes $s$-, $t$-, $u$-channel diagrams and a contact interaction derived from hidden local symmetry. The transition amplitude between $KN$ and $K^*N$ is calculated by extending the Kroll-Ruderman term for pion photoproduction, replacing the photon by a vector meson. Subtraction constants for the loop functions are fixed by fitting the model's $KN$ amplitudes to available data on isospin 0 and 1 s-wave phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The kaon decay constant used is $f_K = 113.46$ MeV, and the $K^*$ decay constant is $f_{K^*} = 171.12$ MeV.
- The Kroll-Ruderman coupling constant for the $KN$-$K^*N$ transition is $g_{\textrm{KR}} \sim 4.53$.
- The isospin coefficient $C^I_{KN}$ in the Weinberg-Tomozawa term is $0$ for isospin 0 and $-2$ for isospin 1.
- The isospin coefficient $C^I_{t, K^*N}$ for the $t$-channel $K^*N$ amplitude is $0$ for isospin 0 and $-2$ for isospin 1.
- The $u$-channel form factor cut-off $\Lambda$ is varied in the range 650-1000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel formalism developed for vector meson-baryon and pseudoscalar meson-baryon systems in Refs. [vbvb, pbvb, hyperons, more] to the strangeness +1 sector. It contradicts the earlier study by Garcia-Recio et al. (2005), which found an isoscalar resonance with spin-parity $3/2^-$ and mass between 1.7-1.8 GeV using a SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry approach. The present results enable future in-medium calculations of $K$ and $K^*$ interactions with nuclear matter by providing scattering lengths and total cross sections for the $KN$ and $K^*N$ systems.
