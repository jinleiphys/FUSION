# The Ramsauer model for the total cross sections of neutron nucleus scattering

> Theoretical study of systematics of neutron scattering cross sections on various materials for neutron energies up to several hundred MeV are of practical importance. In this paper, we analysed the experimental neutron scattering total cross sections from 20MeV to 550MeV using Ramsauer model for nuclei ranging from Be to Pb.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Ramsauer model, using a single set of twelve global parameters (with a separate radius parameter for light nuclei), can reproduce experimental neutron total cross sections for nuclei from Be to Pb across the energy range 20–550 MeV, but notes that for a few specific nuclei (Al, Th, U) the radius parameter had to be individually adjusted to achieve good fits.

## Method

The authors performed a Ramsauer model analysis of experimental neutron total cross section data for nuclei ranging from Be to Pb, using an angular-momentum-independent phase shift approximation. They parameterized the total cross section as a function of atomic mass number (A) and center-of-mass energy (E) using twelve free parameters, with separate radius parameters for light and heavy nuclei. The fits were obtained by adjusting the parameters to reproduce experimental data across the energy range 20–550 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The global radius parameter for heavy nuclei: r₀ = 1.42988 fm
- The global radius parameter for light nuclei: r₀ = 1.53091 fm
- Adjusted radius parameter for Al: r₀ = 1.3892 fm
- Adjusted radius parameter for Th: r₀ = 1.4488 fm
- Adjusted radius parameter for U: r₀ = 1.44641 fm
- The energy range covered: 20 MeV to 550 MeV (center-of-mass energy)
- The nuclear mass range covered: from Be to Pb (⁹Be to ²⁰⁸Pb)

## Context

This work extends earlier Ramsauer model analyses by Grimes et al. (1998, 2000) and Deb and Amos (2003, 2004) by proposing a new functional form for the energy and mass dependence of the Ramsauer model parameters. The study is motivated by the need for neutron-nucleus scattering cross sections up to 500 MeV for accelerator driven sub-critical (ADS) system design, and provides a simpler parameterization alternative to the more complex optical model potential fits by Koning and Delaroche (2003). The results enable rapid estimation of total cross sections for reactor physics calculations of ADS systems without performing full optical model calculations.
