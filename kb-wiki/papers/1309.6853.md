# Extention of the Time-Dependent Multi-Determinant approach to propagators

> We extend the recently proposed Time-Dependent Multi-Determinant approach (ref.[1]) to the description of fermionic propagators. The method hinges on equations of motions obtained using variational principles of Dirac type. In particular we focus on traces of imaginary time propagators, i.e. the partition function. The method is equally applicable with or without projectors to good quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Time-Dependent Multi-Determinant (TDMD) approach from wave functions to fermionic propagators, using variational principles of Dirac type to derive equations of motion; the method works for both real and imaginary time propagators and can incorporate projectors to good quantum numbers, but the numerical solution is not guaranteed to preserve unitarity at finite times, only the norm and energy-like quantities are conserved.

## Method

The propagator is written as a sum of time-dependent elementary propagators (EPs) of the form exp(∑ᵢⱼ a†ᵢ uᵢⱼ aⱼ). Variational principles for the trace of the propagator (eqs. 8a-9b) yield coupled equations of motion (EOM1 and EOM2) for the matrices u(t) representing each EP. The equations involve determinants and matrix inverses built from the single-particle matrices S, T, and rotation operators R from projectors; the linear system for the time derivatives is solved using the generalized inverse of the L matrix, discarding zero eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

* The numerical example uses 6 neutrons in the 1s1p harmonic oscillator shells with ℏΩ = 12 MeV and the neutron-neutron part of the N3LO interaction renormalized to that space.
* The model space contains 28 states total; with Jz=0 projection there are 10 states, and with full J=0 projection there are 4 states.
* Time steps δt were taken in the range 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁵ (in units of MeV⁻¹, with ℏ=1).
* The Fourier transform parameter γ in eq.(44) was set to 0.1 MeV for extracting the level density.
* The energy-like quantity CE(t) is conserved for very long times even when unitarity is broken (deviation from unitarity shown in fig.2).

## Context

This work extends the TDMD approach of Puddu (2013, ref.[1]) from wave functions to propagators, enabling computation of the micro-canonical level density via Fourier transform of real-time propagators. The method differs fundamentally from Hubbard-Stratonovich functional integrals (ref.[2]) and from free-energy minimization approaches (ref.[3]), which cannot handle projectors to good quantum numbers due to entropy computation difficulties. The formalism enables future extension to neutron-proton systems in reasonably large shell model spaces, with conservation laws providing a test of numerical accuracy.
