# Dispersion relations and the spin polarizabilities of the nucleon

> A forward dispersion calculation is implemented for the spin polarizabilities $γ_1, ... ,γ_4$ of the proton and the neutron. These polarizabilities are related to the spin structure of the nucleon at low energies and are structure-constants of the Compton scattering amplitude at ${\cal O}(ω^3)$. In the absence of a direct experimental measurement of these quantities, a dispersion calculation serves the purpose of constraining the model building, and of comparing with recent calculations in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

A forward dispersion relation calculation for the spin polarizabilities of the proton and neutron, using photoproduction multipoles up to 500 or 1500 MeV, predicts values that disagree with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) calculations for the combinations \(\gamma = \gamma_1 - \gamma_2 - 2\gamma_4\) and \(\gamma_{14} = \gamma_1 - 2\gamma_4\), showing an opposite sign compared to the HBChPT results.

## Method

The authors used a finite-size contour dispersion relation approach following L'vov et al., where the real parts of the Compton scattering amplitudes are expressed as a sum of Born terms, a finite-range dispersion integral over photoproduction multipoles, and asymptotic \(t\)-channel contributions modeled by \(\sigma\) and \(\pi^0\) exchanges. The dispersion integrals were saturated by one-pion photoproduction amplitudes from two analyses: the HDT multipoles (Hanstein, Drechsel, Tiator) up to 500 MeV, and the VPI-SP97K SAID multipoles up to both 500 MeV and 1500 MeV. The spin polarizabilities \(\gamma_1\) through \(\gamma_4\) were extracted from the third derivative of the non-Born amplitudes at zero energy.

## Key numbers

*   For the proton, the dispersion calculation using HDT multipoles gives \(\gamma^{(p)} = -0.6\) (in units of \(10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^4\)), compared to the HBChPT value of \(+2.0\) from Holstein et al.
*   The forward spin polarizability \(\gamma = \gamma_1 - \gamma_2 - 2\gamma_4\) from the SAID multipoles up to 1500 MeV is practically identical to the value from the earlier VPI-FA93 analysis, which gave \(\gamma = -1.3\).
*   The \(E_{0+}(n\pi^+)\) amplitude at threshold is \(28.3 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) for HDT and \(24.9 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\) for SAID, with the HDT value much closer to the ChPT prediction of \(28.4 \times 10^{-3}/m_{\pi^+}\).
*   The separate contributions to \(\gamma\) from the HDT multipoles are \(+2.5\) from \(E_{0+}\), \(-3.0\) from \(E_{1+},M_{1+}\), and \(-0.1\) from the rest, summing to \(-0.6\).

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of L'vov et al. to the spin polarizabilities \(\gamma_1\) through \(\gamma_4\), which were introduced by Ragusa as structure constants of the Compton scattering amplitude at \({\cal O}(\omega^3)\). The results contradict the HBChPT calculations of Hemmert et al. (1997) and Holstein et al., which predicted a positive value for \(\gamma\), while the dispersion calculation (in agreement with the multipole analysis of Sandorfi, Whisnant, and Khandaker) finds a negative value. The calculation enables future experimental determination of these polarizabilities and constrains higher-order HBChPT calculations, particularly at \({\cal O}(\epsilon^4)\).
