# Reply to the "Comments to the paper "Detailed study of the astrophysical direct capture reaction $^{6}{\rm Li}(p, γ)^{7}{\rm Be}$ in ..." " by S.B. Dubovichenko, A.S. Tkachenko, R. Ya. Kezerashvili, arXiv:2401.04281 (2024)

> The differences in potential models used in our work and in the original paper of the authors of the Comment are discussed. The neglecting of simple rules of reaction calculations is shown as a possible origin of the defect in the temperature dependence of the reaction rates in the work of the authors of the Comment.

# Reply to "Comments to the paper 'Detailed study of the astrophysical direct capture reaction $^{6}{\rm Li}(p, \gamma)^{7}{\rm Be}$ in a potential model approach'"

## Key claim
The authors argue that the Comment's criticism is unfounded because the Gaussian potentials used in their original work (Tursunov et al., 2023) were adjusted to experimental data from the LUNA collaboration and recent ANC measurements, whereas the Comment's authors (Dubovichenko et al., 2022) used inconsistent unit conventions that produce a non-parallel temperature dependence of reaction rates.

## Method
The authors compare the Gaussian potential parameters for the $p+^6$Li system in the initial $S$-wave and final $P$-wave states between their work (Tursunov et al., 2023) and the Comment's original paper (Dubovichenko et al., 2022). They compute scattering lengths and asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from each potential set and evaluate how these quantities affect the astrophysical $S$-factor and reaction rates. They also check the consistency of the reduced mass constant $\hbar^2/m_0$ against the chosen atomic mass units.

## Key numbers
- $S$-wave scattering length in Tursunov et al. (2023): $a_{01} = 10.01$ fm, versus $a_{01} = 11.45$ fm in Dubovichenko et al. (2022), yielding a ~30% difference in the low-energy scattering cross-section.
- $S$-wave Gaussian potential parameters in Tursunov et al. (2023): $V_0 = -52.0$ MeV, $\alpha_0 = 0.297$ fm$^{-2}$; in Dubovichenko et al. (2022): $V_0 = -58.0$ MeV, $\alpha_0 = 0.4$ fm$^{-2}$.
- Final-state $^2P_{3/2}$ potential in Tursunov et al. (2023): $V_0 = -76.6277$ MeV, $\alpha_0 = 0.175$ fm$^{-2}$, adjusted to ANC = 4.81 fm$^{-1}$; $^2P_{1/2}$ potential: $V_0 = -74.8169$ MeV, $\alpha_0 = 0.1731$ fm$^{-2}$, adjusted to ANC = 4.29 fm$^{-1}$.
- Corresponding ANCs in Dubovichenko et al. (2022) versions 2 and 5: 5.1076 fm$^{-1}$ and 4.6656 fm$^{-1}$, giving ~20% difference in the squared ANC and thus in the $S$-factor.
- Dubovichenko et al. (2022) use $\hbar^2/m_0 = 41.4686$ MeV fm$^2$ with atomic masses $m_p = 1.00727647$ amu and $m(^6$Li) = 6.01347746$ amu, whereas the consistent value should be $\hbar^2/m_0 = 41.8016$ MeV fm$^2$.

## Context
This reply addresses a Comment by Dubovichenko, Tkachenko, and Kezerashvili (2024) on the original paper by Tursunov et al. (2023) which studied the $^6$Li(p,$\gamma$)$^7$Be direct capture reaction using a potential model. The work extends the LUNA collaboration (2020) experimental data for the astrophysical $S$-factor and the empirical ANC values from Kiss et al. (2021). The authors identify an inconsistency in the reduced mass constant used by Dubovichenko et al. (2022) as the likely cause of the non-parallel temperature dependence of their reaction rates, and they reaffirm that their own potentials reproduce both the absolute value and energy/temperature dependence of the LUNA data.
