# Bayesian Data Fusion of Imperfect Fission Yields for Augmented Evaluations

> We demonstrate that Bayesian machine learning can be used to treat the vast amount of experimental fission data which are noisy, incomplete, discrepant, and correlated. As an example, the two-dimensional cumulative fission yields (CFY) of neutron-induced fission of $^{238}$U are evaluated with energy dependencies and uncertainty qualifications. For independent fission yields (IFY) with very few experimental data, the heterogeneous data fusion of CFY and IFY is employed to interpolate the energy dependence. This work shows that Bayesian data fusion can facilitate the further utilization of imperfect raw nuclear data.

## Key claim

Bayesian machine learning can fuse noisy, incomplete, discrepant, and correlated experimental fission data to produce evaluated fission yields with energy-dependent uncertainty quantification, but the method's reliability depends on the inclusion of evaluated data (e.g., from JENDL) as learning constraints and on the availability of sufficient data to capture non-local correlations.

## Method

The authors employed a Bayesian neural network (BNN) with a single hidden layer (10 neurons) for small test datasets and a double-layer network (20-20 neurons) for the full data fusion. Input variables were the fragment atomic number Z, neutron number N, and incident neutron energy E. The likelihood function incorporated both a hyperparameter noise scale and experimental uncertainties, and the posterior distribution was used to infer fission yields with associated uncertainties. For independent fission yields (IFY), heterogeneous data fusion was performed by training the same network on both cumulative fission yield (CFY) and IFY data, with the proton numbers deliberately separated into two groups to capture correlations between the two yield types.

## Key numbers

- The BNN evaluation of fission mass yields for neutron-induced fission of 238U used a test dataset of 309 data points from Ramos et al.
- The full CFY data fusion for 238U included 33 experiments with about 1221 scattering data points from EXFOR, plus 2064 evaluated CFY data points from JENDL at 0.5 and 14 MeV.
- The overall noise scale for the CFY evaluation was approximately 0.6.
- Average BNN uncertainties for the two-dimensional CFY distributions at 2, 8, and 14 MeV were 0.789, 0.805, and 0.793, respectively, for 1032 points each.
- Some fission product yields had uncertainties as large as 1.4 where CFY values were large.
- For IFY, only 211 experimental data points from EXFOR and 2064 evaluated points from JENDL were available for 238U.
- The heterogeneous fusion of CFY and IFY used a 20-20 network and produced an overall background noise scale of about 0.2.

## Context

This work extends prior Bayesian neural network evaluations of incomplete fission mass yields (Wang et al., 2020) and discrepant charge yields (Qiao et al., 2020) to the full two-dimensional cumulative and independent fission yield problem. It contrasts with conventional semi-empirical models such as Brosa and GEF, which invoke many parameters and lack uncertainty quantification, and with Gaussian process regression, which assumes local correlations. The method enables the interpolation of energy-dependent fission yields between the standard evaluated energies (thermal, 0.5 MeV, and 14 MeV) and provides uncertainty-quantified yield-energy relations for key fragments like 99Mo, 135Xe, 140Ba, and 147Nd, which are important for reactor monitoring and the reactor anti-neutrino anomaly.
