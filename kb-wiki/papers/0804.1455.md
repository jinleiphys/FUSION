# Sequential melting of charmonium states in an expanding Quark Gluon Plasma and $J/ψ$ suppression at RHIC and LHC energy collisions

> We have developed a hydrodynamic model to study sequential melting of charmonium states in an expanding QGP medium. According to the initial fluid temperature profile, $J/ψ$'s are randomly distributed in the transverse plane. As the fluid evolve in time, the free streaming $J/ψ$'s are suppressed if the local fluid temperature exceed a critical temperature. PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Au+Au collisions at mid-rapidity are explained by sequential melting of the charmonium states, $χ_c$, $ψ\prime$ and $J/ψ$, in the expanding medium. The critical temperatures $T_{J/ψ} \approx2.09T_c$ and $T_χ=T_{χ_c}=T_{ψ\prime} \approx 1.1T_c$ agree with lattice motivated calculations. The feed-down fraction $F$ depend on whether the cold nuclear matter effect is included or not. It changes from $F=0.3$ with cold nuclear matter effect included to $F=0.5$ when the effect is neglected. Model fails to reproduce the PHENIX data on the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Cu+Cu collisions at mid-rapidity, indicating that the mechanism of $J/ψ$ suppression is different in Au+Au and in Cu+Cu collisions. We also use the model to predict for the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, $\sqrt{s}$=5500 GeV. In LHC energy, $J/ψ$'s are more suppressed in mid central collisions than in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy.

# Sequential melting of charmonium states in an expanding Quark Gluon Plasma and J/ψ suppression at RHIC and LHC energy collisions

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic model explains PHENIX data on J/ψ suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC via sequential melting of χc, ψ′, and J/ψ states, but the model fails to reproduce the centrality dependence of J/ψ suppression in Cu+Cu collisions at the same energy, indicating that the suppression mechanism differs between the two collision systems. The feed-down fraction F depends on whether cold nuclear matter effects are included, ranging from F=0.3 (with cold nuclear matter) to F=0.5 (without).

## Method

The author solved 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamic equations assuming longitudinal boost-invariance, with initial energy density in the transverse plane proportional to a Glauber model calculation using 75% soft collisions and 25% hard collisions. J/ψ mesons were randomly distributed in the transverse plane according to the initial fluid temperature profile, assigned a random direction vector, and assumed to follow free-streaming paths; they were suppressed if the local fluid temperature exceeded a critical melting temperature, using a step-function decay width that changes from infinity to zero at the melting temperature.

## Key numbers

- Melting temperature for J/ψ: T_{J/ψ} ≈ 2.09 T_c (with T_c = 164 MeV)
- Melting temperature for χc and ψ′: T_χ = T_{χc} = T_{ψ′} ≈ 1.1 T_c
- Feed-down fraction F = 0.3 when cold nuclear matter effect is included (σ_abs = 2 mb)
- Feed-down fraction F = 0.5 when cold nuclear matter effect is neglected
- Initial central energy density in Au+Au collisions: ε = 30 GeV/fm³ at τ_i = 0.6 fm/c
- Initial central entropy density in Au+Au collisions: S_ini = 110 fm⁻³
- Peak temperature in 0-5% centrality Au+Au collisions: 2.16 T_c
- Peak temperature in most peripheral (60-93%) Au+Au collisions: 1.16 T_c
- For LHC predictions: central entropy density S_ini = 180 fm⁻³, central temperature T_i = 421 MeV
- Extrapolated charged particle multiplicity at LHC for N_part = 350: 927 ± 70

## Context

This work extends the "Hydro+J/ψ" model of Gunji et al. (2007) by using a 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamic approach with longitudinal boost-invariance rather than full 3+1 dimensions, and applies it to both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC as well as Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. The melting temperatures agree with lattice-motivated calculations by Satz (2006). The model contradicts the threshold model of Blaizot et al. (2000) which ignored medium expansion; unlike the threshold model, the hydrodynamic model predicts enhanced J/ψ suppression at LHC compared to RHIC due to higher local fluid temperatures. The failure to describe Cu+Cu data suggests that recombination effects or other mechanisms not included in the model may be important, and the work enables future studies to incorporate regeneration of charmonium states in the quark-gluon plasma.
