# Decoding the QCD critical behaviour in A+A collisions

> In a systematic search for the QCD critical point in nuclear collisions, at the CERN SPS, it was found that intermittency measurements in the freeze-out state of central Si+Si collisions, at the maximum SPS energy, provide us with an indication of sizeable critical fluctuations. Also, rather recently, a weaker effect was traced in preliminary data of the Ar+Sc reaction for 10-20% most central collisions at (approximately) the same energy. However, the uncertainties in the analysis and the limitations of the experimental event statistics make the interpretation of the above measurements (NA49, NA61/SHINE) rather inconclusive, inviting for a further, phenomenological investigation with complementary tools and theoretical ideas. To this end, in the present work, we employ intermittency techniques within a model-independent analysis scheme (AMIAS), a novel method from Data Science [arXiv:1205.6505], in order to produce unbiased results for the parameters of the power-laws and in particular for the associated power-law exponent (intermittency index) $ϕ_2$. Using data-sets at different peripheralities, we also study the dependence of the $ϕ_2$-value on the number of wounded nucleons, in order to uncover the approach to the critical point. With these findings and the help of Ising-QCD partition function, the interpretation of SPS intermittency measurements and their links to the critical region, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that intermittency measurements in central Si+Si collisions at maximum SPS energy show sizeable critical fluctuations, and a weaker effect appears in preliminary Ar+Sc data for 10-20% most central collisions at similar energy; however, the authors hedge that uncertainties in the analysis and limited experimental event statistics make the interpretation of these NA49 and NA61/SHINE measurements rather inconclusive, motivating a further phenomenological investigation with the AMIAS method.

## Method

The authors applied the AMIAS (a model-independent analysis scheme from Data Science) to extract unbiased estimates of the intermittency index φ₂ from the proton transverse momentum correlator ΔF₂(M) in Si+Si and Ar+Sc collisions at SPS energies. They used the Ising-QCD partition function to relate the measured φ₂ values and the number of wounded nucleons N_w to the location of freeze-out states in the (μ_B, T) plane relative to the QCD critical point. The analysis covered different peripherality bins for Ar+Sc collisions at 150A GeV/c beam momentum (√s_NN = 16.8 GeV) and Si+Si collisions at 158A GeV/c.

## Key numbers

- For Si+Si 0-12% most central collisions at 158A GeV/c: ⟨φ₂⟩ = 0.92 ± 0.18, with N_w = 37 ± 3
- For Ar+Sc 10-20% most central collisions at 150A GeV/c: ⟨φ₂⟩ = 0.39 ± 0.08, with N_w = 45.9 ± 0.5
- For Ar+Sc 10-15% most central: ⟨φ₂⟩ = 0.38 ± 0.10, with N_w = 49.4 ± 0.4
- For Ar+Sc 15-20% most central: ⟨φ₂⟩ = 0.41 ± 0.12, with N_w = 42.4 ± 0.5
- For Ar+Sc 0-20% most central: ⟨φ₂⟩ = 0.16 ± 0.06, with N_w = 54 ± 0.6
- For Ar+Sc 0-10% most central: ⟨φ₂⟩ = -0.77 ± 0.49, with N_w = 62 ± 0.6
- The critical φ₂ value from 3d-Ising universality class prediction is φ₂ = 5/6 ≈ 0.83
- AMIAS test on Critical Monte Carlo (CMC) generated events with 0.7% critical component and 99.3% noise recovered φ₂ = 0.83 ± 0.06, consistent with the input value

## Context

This work extends the systematic search for QCD critical fluctuations performed by the NA49 and NA61/SHINE experiments at CERN SPS over the last decade (2010-2020), where intermittency in π⁺π⁻ pairs and protons was measured in C+C, Si+Si, Pb+Pb, Be+Be, and Ar+Sc collisions. The authors build on the finding that Si+Si collisions at maximum SPS energy showed a sizeable intermittency effect (NA49, 2010s), while the Ar+Sc data from NA61/SHINE showed a weaker effect only in 10-20% centrality. The AMIAS method, previously applied in pion photoproduction analysis and lattice QCD investigations, is used here to overcome the problem of correlated bins in the intermittency treatment. The results enable a mapping of freeze-out states in the QCD phase diagram relative to the critical region, with the Ising-QCD partition function predicting a critical region a few MeV wide along the chemical potential direction.
