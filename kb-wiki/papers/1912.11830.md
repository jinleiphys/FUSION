# Triangle singularity appearing as an $X(3872)$-like peak in $B\to (J/ψπ^+π^-) Kπ$

> We consider a triangle diagram for $B^0\to (J/ψπ^+π^-) K^+π^-$ where an $X(3872)$ peak has been observed experimentally. We demonstrate that a triangle singularity inherent in the triangle diagram creates a sharp peak in the $J/ψπ^+π^-$ invariant mass distribution when the final $(J/ψπ^+π^-)π$ invariant mass is at and around the $D^*\bar D^*$ threshold. The position and width of the peak is 3871.68 MeV (a few keV above the $D^{*0}\bar{D}^0$ threshold) and $\sim$0.4 MeV, respectively, in perfect agreement with the precisely measured $X(3872)$ mass and width: $3871.69\pm 0.17$ MeV and $< 1.2$ MeV. This remarkable agreement is virtually parameter-free. The result indicates that the considered mechanism has to be understood in advance when separating an $X(3872)$-pole contribution from $B^0\to (J/ψπ^+π^-) K^+π^-$ data; the separation yields an $X(3872)π$ lineshape that could be used to determine the $X(3872)$ mass. We suggest a method to set a constraint on the triangle mechanism by analyzing a charge analogous process $B^0\to (J/ψπ^0π^-) K^+π^0$ where a similar triangle singularity generates an $X^-(3876)$-like peak.

## Key claim

The triangle singularity inherent in a specific triangle diagram for $B^0\to (J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-) K^+\pi^-$ creates a sharp peak in the $J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution whose position and width agree perfectly with the precisely measured $X(3872)$ mass and width, but only when the final $(J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-)\pi$ invariant mass is at and around the $D^*\bar D^*$ threshold; this agreement is virtually parameter-free.

## Method

The author computed the double differential decay width for $B^0\to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-K^+\pi^-$ from a triangle diagram where $B^0$ decays to $D^{*-}D^{*0}K^+$, the $D^{*-}$ decays to $\bar D^0\pi^-$, and the $D^{*0}\bar D^0$ pair rescatters into $J/\psi\rho^0$ via a contact interaction with dipole form factors. The amplitude was evaluated in the $J/\psi\rho^0\pi^-$ center-of-mass frame, and the $\rho^0\to\pi^+\pi^-$ decay was folded in using a Breit-Wigner lineshape. The $D^*$ decay widths were taken from PDG averages and from isospin-symmetry-based calculations, and the $B^0\to D^{*-}D^{*0}K^+$ vertex strength was fixed using the measured branching ratio of $1.06\%$.

## Key numbers

* The triangle singularity peak from diagram A appears at $M_{J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-}=3871.68$ MeV, which is a few keV above the $D^{*0}\bar D^0$ threshold of $3871.7$ MeV.
* The Breit-Wigner fit of the peak in the triangle singularity region gives mass $3871.68\pm 0.00$ MeV and width $0.42\pm 0.01$ MeV, compared to the PDG $X(3872)$ values of $3871.69\pm 0.17$ MeV and $<1.2$ MeV.
* The triangle singularity condition for diagram A requires $0 < M_{J/\psi\rho} - (m_{D^{*0}}+m_{D^0}) \leq 0.2$ MeV and $0 < W - (m_{D^{*-}}+m_{D^{*0}}) \lesssim 1.0$ MeV, where $W$ is the $J/\psi\rho\pi$ invariant mass.
* For the charge analogous process $B^0\to (J/\psi\pi^0\pi^-)K^+\pi^0$, the triangle diagrams C and D produce peaks at $m_{D^{*0}}+m_{D^-}=3876.4$ MeV and $m_{D^{*-}}+m_{D^0}=3875.1$ MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of triangle singularities as potential fakes of exotic hadron signals, following earlier work on triangle singularities in $XYZ$ candidates by various authors (e.g., Szczepaniak, Liu, and others). The result directly impacts the $X(3872)$ mass determination method proposed by Guo (2019) and collaborators, which analyzes $X(3872)\pi$ and $X(3872)\gamma$ lineshapes from triangle diagrams that include an $X(3872)$-pole propagation. The paper suggests that the non-resonant triangle mechanism must be understood and subtracted before extracting the $X(3872)$-pole contribution from $B^0\to (J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-) K^+\pi^-$ data, and proposes analyzing the charge analogous $B^0\to (J/\psi\pi^0\pi^-) K^+\pi^0$ process to constrain the triangle mechanism.
