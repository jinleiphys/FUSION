# Lepton $g-2$ non-universality of hadronic contributions and a sub-GeV window to New Physics

> We propose the linear combination of the anomalous magnetic moments of the muon and electron, $a_{μ-e} \equiv a_μ- (m_μ/m_e)^2 a_e$, as a natural low-energy window quantity, with enhanced sensitivity to the current discrepancy between the data-driven and lattice-QCD evaluations of hadronic contributions. The rescaling ensures an exact cancellations the short-range effects, thereby improving the UV behavior and bypassing a number of issues that arise in $a_μ$ or $a_e$ separately. The hadronic-vacuum-polarization effect in $a^{\rm HVP}_{μ-e}$, together with its uncertainty, is reduced as compared to $a^{\rm HVP}_μ$ by $\sim 85\%$. This is promising for tests of New Physics, conditional to significant improvements in experimental measurements of $a_e$ and $α$. One can foresee the improvements in tests of New Physics with some degree of flavor non-universality, as well as for the flavor-universal sub-GeV states.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the linear combination \(a_{\mu-e} \equiv a_\mu - (m_\mu/m_e)^2 a_e\) serves as a natural low-energy window quantity that cancels short-range effects and reduces the hadronic-vacuum-polarization (HVP) contribution and its uncertainty by about 85% compared to \(a_\mu\) alone, but this is conditional on significant future improvements in experimental measurements of \(a_e\) and the fine-structure constant \(\alpha\).

## Method

The authors construct the rescaled difference \(a_{\mu-e}\) by subtracting the electron anomalous magnetic moment multiplied by the squared mass ratio \((m_\mu/m_e)^2\) from the muon anomalous magnetic moment. They analyze the HVP contribution using the dispersive representation with a modified kernel function \(K_{\mu-e}(s)\) that contains an additional power of \(1/s\), suppressing high-energy contributions. They illustrate the cancellation using a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model for the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) channel and a chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) calculation for the \(\pi^0\gamma\) contribution, and then apply the combination to constrain dark photon parameter space.

## Key numbers

* The HVP contribution to \(a_{\mu-e}\) from lattice QCD is \(-96.1(29.0) \times 10^{-10}\), compared to the data-driven value of \(-102.8(4) \times 10^{-10}\), yielding a discrepancy of \(6.7(29.0) \times 10^{-10}\).
* The HVP effect in \(a^{\rm HVP}_{\mu-e}\) is reduced by approximately 85% relative to \(a^{\rm HVP}_\mu\).
* The \(\pi^0\gamma\) contribution to \(a_{\mu-e}\) from \(\chi\)PT is \(-0.34(5) \times 10^{-11}\), compared to \(-0.45 \times 10^{-11}\) when using an empirical pion transition form factor.
* The \(\pi^+\pi^-\) channel contribution to \(a_{\mu-e}\) from the VMD model is \(-90.0 \times 10^{-10}\), while the data-driven evaluation (KNT) gives \(-89.1(4) \times 10^{-10}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using rescaled differences between electronic and muonic observables, analogous to the treatment of muonic-hydrogen hyperfine transitions that cancel short-distance proton structure effects (Antognini 2022). It contrasts with the previously considered combination \(R_{e/m} \equiv (m_\mu^2/m_e^2)(a_e/a_\mu)\) (Giusti 2020), which does not achieve exact cancellation of high-energy contributions. The quantity \(a_{\mu-e}\) enables direct comparison between lattice-QCD and data-driven evaluations of HVP, and can be used to probe sub-GeV New Physics such as dark photons (Fayet 2007, Pospelov 2008), with improved sensitivity over \(a_\mu\) or \(a_e\) separately for flavor-non-universal scenarios.
