# Once more on mixing of the a_0(980) and f_0(980) mesons

> We show that a recent proposal by Close and Kirk fails to describe a_0(980) and f_0(980) mixing when physical masses and widths are included.

# Once more on mixing of the a_0(980) and f_0(980) mesons

## Key claim

The proposal by Close and Kirk (2001) that a_0(980) and f_0(980) mixing in the K-bar-K molecule model can produce arbitrarily large effects even for equal production amplitudes of I=0 and I=1 resonances is incorrect; when physical masses and widths are included, the mixing effect on branching ratios is less than 10% and is practically compensated by interference between the two resonances in the pi-eta and pi-pi mass spectra.

## Method

The authors analyze the Close-Kirk (2001) mixing scheme by constructing the full production amplitudes for phi radiative decays to gamma pi-eta and gamma pi-pi, including the propagators D_R(m) = m_R^2 - m^2 - i m Gamma_R(m) for both resonances and the interference terms arising from isospin-breaking decays f_0 -> pi-eta and a_0 -> pi-pi. They evaluate the mixing corrections numerically using the mass difference m_a0 - m_f0 = 4 MeV and width difference Gamma_a0 - Gamma_f0 = +/- 10 MeV, then extend the same interference analysis to Pomeron-Pomeron production in central collisions.

## Key numbers

- Mass difference m_a0 - m_f0 = 4 MeV, derived from the kaon mass splitting as cos(2 theta) * (2 m_K0 - 2 m_K+) with theta = 30 degrees
- Conservative width difference Gamma_a0(m_a0) - Gamma_f0(m_f0) = +/- 10 MeV
- Mixing correction in the phi -> gamma (a_0 + f_0) -> gamma pi-eta amplitude is less than 0.08 (using m = m_f0, Gamma_f0 = 50 MeV)
- Mixing correction in the phi -> gamma (f_0 + a_0) -> gamma pi-pi amplitude is less than 0.05 (using m = m_a0, Gamma_a0 = 50 MeV)
- Mixing effect correction in the ratio S_a0(m)/S_f0(m) is less than 0.1
- Predicted cross-section ratio sigma(PP -> a_0)/sigma(PP -> f_0) < 4 x 10^{-3} from the interference analysis
- Experimental value from Close and Kirk (2000): sigma(PP -> a_0)/sigma(PP -> f_0) = (8 +/- 3) x 10^{-2}
- SND (2000) measured m_a0 = 994 (+33, -8) MeV, m_f0 = 0.9698 +/- 0.0045 GeV
- CMD (1999) measured m_f0 = 0.969 +/- 0.005 GeV
- SND (2000) measured g_a0K+K-^2/4pi = 1.05 (+0.36, -0.25) GeV^2 and g_f0K+K-^2/4pi = 2.47 (+0.73, -0.51) GeV^2
- CMD (1999) measured g_f0K+K-^2/4pi = 1.49 +/- 0.36 GeV^2
- Estimated mixing parameter |M_f0a0(m_f0)/Gamma_f0(m_f0)| is less than 0.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier theoretical discovery of a_0(980)-f_0(980) mixing as a threshold phenomenon (Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov, 1979) and their subsequent proposals for experimental tests (Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov, 1981). It directly contradicts the claim by Close and Kirk (2001) that the K-bar-K molecule model allows arbitrarily large mixing effects, showing instead that the interference between overlapping resonances in the pi-eta and pi-pi channels compensates the mixing influence on production amplitudes. The analysis enables proper treatment of mixing in phi radiative decay data from SND, CMD, and future KLOE results, and clarifies that the earlier prediction of sigma(PP -> f_0 -> a_0 -> pi-eta)/sigma(PP -> f_0 -> pi-pi) = (0.5 - 2) x 10^{-2} (Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov, 1981) differs from the experimental value by 1.71 - 2.46 experimental errors.
