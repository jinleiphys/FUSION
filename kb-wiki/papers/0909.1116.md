# Dense stellar matter with trapped neutrinos under strong magnetic fields

> We investigate the effects of strong magnetic fields on the equation of state of dense stellar neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped matter. Relativistic nuclear models both with constant couplings (NLW) and with density dependent parameters (DDRH) and including hyperons are considered . It is shown that at low densities neutrinos are suppressed in the presence of the magnetic field. The magnetic field reduces the strangeness fraction of neutrino-free matter and increases the strangeness fraction of neutrino-trapped matter. The mass-radius relation of stars described by these equations of state are determined. The magnetic field makes the overall equation of state stiffer and the stronger the field the larger the mass of maximum mass star and the smaller the baryon density at the center of the star. As a consequence in the presence of strong magnetic fields the possibility that a protoneutron star evolves to a blackhole is smaller.

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields suppress neutrinos at low densities in neutrino-trapped matter, and the effect is strong enough that for the largest field considered (B* = 3 × 10⁵, corresponding to B ≈ 1.3 × 10¹⁹ G) with anomalous magnetic moments included, it becomes impossible to obtain an equation of state at low densities for the fixed lepton fraction Y_L = 0.4.

## Method

The authors solve the mean-field equations for a Lagrangian density including the baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Σ⁺, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) and leptons (e⁻, μ⁻) under a constant external magnetic field along the z-axis, using two relativistic nuclear models: the GM1 parametrization with constant couplings and the TW density-dependent relativistic hadronic (DDRH) model. For neutrino-trapped matter, the lepton fraction is fixed at Y_Le = 0.4 and Y_Lμ = 0, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field used as a unit is B^c_e = 4.414 × 10¹³ G, so that B = B* × B^c_e.
- For neutrino-free matter with B* = 3 × 10⁵ and the TW model (x_σ = 0.6, without AMM), the hyperon onset density is 0.358 fm⁻³ (Σ⁻ is the first hyperon).
- For neutrino-trapped matter with B* = 3 × 10⁵ and the TW model (x_σ = 0.6, without AMM), the hyperon onset density is 0.445 fm⁻³ (Σ⁻ is the first hyperon).
- The threshold density for the neutrino chemical potential to vanish reaches 0.5 ρ₀ for GM1 and 1.35 ρ₀ for TW at B* = 5 × 10⁵ (with x_σ = 0.6, without AMM).
- The strangeness fraction for neutrino-free matter at high density reaches approximately 0.275 (GM1) and 0.23 (TW) for x_σ = 0.6 at B = 0.
- The nuclear saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the study of Prakash et al. (1997) on neutrino-trapped matter and Prakash et al. (2002) on magnetic field effects in hyperonic matter, which did not consider neutrino trapping. It also contrasts with Chakrabarty et al. (1998), who found that strong magnetic fields soften the equation of state and reduce maximum masses, whereas the present work finds the opposite effect. The results enable more realistic modeling of protoneutron star evolution and the conditions under which a protoneutron star may collapse to a black hole after neutrino outflow.
