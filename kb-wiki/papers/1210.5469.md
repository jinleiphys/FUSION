# Shape transitions in exotic Si and S isotopes and tensor-force-driven Jahn-Teller effect

> We show how shape transitions in the neutron-rich exotic Si and S isotopes occur in terms of shell-model calculations with a newly constructed Hamiltonian based on V_MU interaction. We first compare the calculated spectroscopic-strength distributions for the proton 0d_5/2,3/2 and 1s_1/2 orbitals with results extracted from a 48Ca(e,e'p) experiment to show the importance of the tensor-force component of the Hamiltonian. Detailed calculations for the excitation energies, B(E2) and two-neutron separation energies for the Si and S isotopes show excellent agreement with experimental data. The potential energy surface exhibits rapid shape transitions along the isotopic chains towards N=28 that are different for Si and S. We explain the results in terms of an intuitive picture involving a Jahn-Teller-type effect that is sensitive to the tensor-force-driven shell evolution. The closed sub-shell nucleus 42Si is a particularly good example of how the tensor-force-driven Jahn-Teller mechanism leads to a strong oblate rather than spherical shape.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the tensor-force-driven shell evolution, previously inferred from reaction data, is directly confirmed by comparing shell-model calculations with spectroscopic factors from the 48Ca(e,e'p) experiment, and that this same mechanism drives rapid shape transitions in neutron-rich Si and S isotopes through a Jahn-Teller-type effect that favors oblate deformation at sub-shell closures like 42Si.

## Method

The authors constructed a new shell-model Hamiltonian (SDPF-MU) based on the VMU interaction, with the sd and pf shells as valence space, and performed large-scale diagonalization using the mshell64 code. They calculated spectroscopic strength distributions for proton hole states in 48Ca and compared them to (e,e'p) data, then computed excitation energies, B(E2) values, two-neutron separation energies, and potential energy surfaces for even-N Si and S isotopes from N=22 to 28, both with and without the cross-shell tensor force.

## Key numbers

- The 0d3/2-0d5/2 gap in 48Ca is approximately 5 MeV in the full calculation, but becomes about 2 MeV larger when the cross-shell tensor force is switched off.
- The 0d5/2-1s1/2 spacing in 42Si is 7.8 MeV with the SDPF-MU Hamiltonian, and the ground-state expectation value of the multipole proton-neutron interaction is -13.2 MeV.
- The tensor force reduces the 0d5/2-1s1/2 spacing in 42Si by 1.1 MeV.
- The ratio Ex(2+2)/Ex(2+1) is 1.5 for 40Si and 4.4 for 42Si.
- Two-neutron separation energies agree with experiment within about 0.5 MeV for the full calculation, except for N=22 Si with a discrepancy of 1.2 MeV; without the tensor force, deviations of 1-3 MeV occur for Si and S isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the tensor-force-driven shell evolution mechanism proposed by Otsuka et al. (2005) by providing the first direct test using spectroscopic factors from the 48Ca(e,e'p) experiment of Kramer et al. (2001). The results contradict density-functional calculations by Li et al. and Rodriguez et al. that do not reproduce the tensor-force-driven reduction of the spin-orbit splitting seen in the (e,e'p) data, and improve upon earlier shell-model calculations with empirical interactions by SDPF-U and Kaneko et al. that gave larger deviations from experiment. The findings enable predictions for shape transitions in the 78Ni region and demonstrate that the SDPF-MU Hamiltonian can describe a wide variety of nuclei beyond those studied here.
