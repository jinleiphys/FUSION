# Double Decimation and Sliding Vacua in the Nuclear Many-Body System

> We propose that effective field theories for nuclei and nuclear matter comprise of "double decimation": (1) the chiral symmetry decimation (CSD) and (2) Fermi liquid decimation (FLD). The Brown-Rho scaling recently identified as the parametric dependence intrinsic in the "vector manifestation" of hidden local symmetry theory of Harada and Yamawaki results from the first decimation. This scaling governs dynamics down to the scale at which the Fermi surface is formed as a quantum critical phenomenon. The next decimation to the top of the Fermi sea where standard nuclear physics is operative makes up the Fermi liquid decimation. Thus nuclear dynamics is dictated by two fixed points, namely, the vector manifestation fixed point and the Fermi liquid fixed point. It has been a prevalent practice in nuclear physics community to proceed with the second decimation only, assuming density independent masses. We show why most nuclear phenomena can be reproduced by theories using either density-independent, or density-dependent masses, a grand conspiracy of nature that is an aspect that could be tied to the Cheshire-Cat phenomenon in hadron physics. We identify what is left out in the Fermi liquid decimation that does not incorporate the CSD. Experiments such as the dilepton production in relativistic heavy ion reactions, which are specifically designed to observe effects of dropping masses, could exhibit large effects from the reduced masses. However they are compounded with effects that are not directly tied to chiral symmetry. We discuss a recent STAR/RHIC observation where BR scaling can be singled out in a pristine environment.

## Key claim

The authors propose that effective field theories for nuclei and nuclear matter require a "double decimation" procedure consisting of chiral symmetry decimation (CSD) and Fermi liquid decimation (FLD), and that most nuclear phenomena can be reproduced using either density-independent or density-dependent masses due to a "grand conspiracy of nature" related to the Cheshire-Cat phenomenon, meaning that the need for density-dependent hadron masses (Brown-Rho scaling) was historically missed because it does not manifest in most observables.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework combining hidden local symmetry theory with vector manifestation (HLS/VM) matched to QCD at the chiral scale, then apply a two-step decimation: first from the chiral scale down to the Fermi surface scale (CSD), then from the Fermi surface to the top of the Fermi sea (FLD). They map the mean-field solutions of effective Lagrangians (type-I with only pions and nucleons, or type-II with explicit vector mesons and a dilaton scalar) to Landau Fermi-liquid theory, using the Walecka mean-field approach as a short-cut to describe nuclear matter as a soliton solution.

## Key numbers

* The tensor interaction in medium weakens when the rho meson mass drops to approximately 0.8 times its free-space mass at nuclear matter density n0, as early evidence for Brown-Rho scaling.
* The renormalization-group cutoff Lambda for the effective interaction V_low-k is chosen at approximately 350 MeV, corresponding to the upper scale to which nucleon-nucleon phase shifts have been obtained.
* The Fermi surface scale Lambda_FS is typically less than or about 1.5 fm^{-1}, which is the scale relevant to nuclear physics.
* At low densities and temperatures, hadron masses scale as m*/m approximately equal to [<qbar q>* / <qbar q>]^{1/2}, while at higher densities and temperatures they scale as [<qbar q>* / <qbar q>].

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling proposal (Brown and Rho, 1991) by connecting it to the vector manifestation fixed point of Harada and Yamawaki in hidden local symmetry theory, and to the Fermi liquid fixed point description of nuclear matter (Shankar, 1994; Friman and collaborators). The paper synthesizes the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) with modern effective field theory, showing that the successful Kuo-Brown interactions and V_low-k effective interactions can be understood within this framework. The approach enables a unified description of finite nuclei, nuclear matter, and dense matter up to chiral restoration, and identifies specific observables such as dilepton production in heavy ion collisions and axial charge transitions in heavy nuclei where Brown-Rho scaling can be isolated.
