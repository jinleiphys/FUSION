# Non-monotonic energy dependence of net-proton number fluctuations

> Non-monotonic variation with collision energy ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$) of the moments of the net-baryon number distribution in heavy-ion collisions, related to the correlation length and the susceptibilities of the system, is suggested as a signature for the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) critical point. We report the first evidence of a non-monotonic variation in kurtosis times variance of the net-proton number (proxy for net-baryon number) distribution as a function of \rootsnn with 3.1$σ$ significance, for head-on (central) gold-on-gold (Au+Au) collisions measured using the STAR detector at RHIC. Data in non-central Au+Au collisions and models of heavy-ion collisions without a critical point show a monotonic variation as a function of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first evidence of a non-monotonic variation in kurtosis times variance (κσ²) of the net-proton number distribution as a function of collision energy √sNN, with 3.1σ significance for central Au+Au collisions, but this non-monotonic behavior is observed only for the highest-order moment and is absent in peripheral collisions and in models without a critical point.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured event-by-event net-proton (p − p̄) distributions in Au+Au collisions at nine beam energies from √sNN = 7.7 to 200 GeV, using the Time Projection Chamber and Time Of Flight detector for proton identification over 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c and |y| < 0.5. Cumulants C₁ through C₄ of the net-proton distributions were calculated for central (0-5% most central) and peripheral (70-80%) collisions, corrected for volume fluctuations and detector efficiencies, and the ratios κσ² = C₄/C₂ and Sσ = C₃/C₂ were examined for energy dependence.

## Key numbers

- Significance of non-monotonic variation in κσ² versus √sNN: 3.1σ, determined from a fourth-order polynomial fit to data from √sNN = 7.7 to 62.4 GeV
- Collision energies studied: 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 54.4, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Baryon chemical potential range covered: 20 to 420 MeV at chemical freeze-out
- Number of minimum bias events: 3 million at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 585 million at √sNN = 54.4 GeV
- Proton identification purity above 97% for pT up to 2 GeV/c using TOF
- TPC efficiency for protons in 0-5% central collisions: 83% at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, 62% at √sNN = 200 GeV
- TOF-matching efficiency for protons: 72% at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, 69% at √sNN = 200 GeV
- p-value from χ² test between data and HRG GCE model for κσ² over √sNN = 7.7-27 GeV: 0.00553
- p-value from χ² test between data and UrQMD model for κσ² over √sNN = 7.7-27 GeV: 0.0221

## Context

This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, improving upon previous STAR measurements (Adamczyk et al., 2013) by including TOF identification for higher pT protons and achieving greater statistical precision. The non-monotonic signal in κσ² is qualitatively consistent with predictions from QCD-based models including a critical point (Stephanov, 1999; Stephanov, 2011), while transport models without a critical point (UrQMD, JAM) and hadron resonance gas models (Garg et al., 2013; Bhattacharyya et al., 2013; Braun-Munzinger et al., 2020) show monotonic behavior. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD susceptibilities and motivate higher-statistics measurements in BES Phase II to establish the critical point.
