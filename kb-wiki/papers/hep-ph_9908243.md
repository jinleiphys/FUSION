# J/Psi and Psi' total cross sections and formation times from data for charmonium suppression in $pA$ collisions

> The recent data for E866 experiment on the x_F dependence for charmonium suppression in pA collisions at 800 GeV are analyzed using a time- and energy-dependent preformed charmonium absorption cross section σ_{abs}^ψ(τ,\sqrt{s}). For \sqrt{s}=10 GeV the initially (τ=0) produced premeson has an absorption cross section of σ_{pr}~3mb. At the same energy but for τ-> \infty one deduces for the total cross sections σ_{tot}^{J/Psi N}=(2.8\pm 0.3)mb, σ_{tot}^{J/Psi N}= (10.5\pm 3.6)mb. The date are compatible with a formation time τ_{1/2}=0.6 fm/c.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed $x_F$ dependence of charmonium suppression in $pA$ collisions at 800 GeV can be explained entirely by a time- and energy-dependent effective absorption cross section for a premeson evolving during its passage through the nucleus, but the authors caution that the analysis relies on several unverified hypotheses and approximations whose accuracies cannot be estimated.

## Method

The authors fit a classical expression for the nuclear suppression $S_\psi(x_F)$ to the E866 data for $J/\psi$ and $\psi^\prime$ production on tungsten and beryllium targets in the $x_F$ interval $[-0.1, 0.25]$, using a parameterized time-dependent absorption cross section $\sigma_{\rm abs}^{\psi N}(\tau,\sqrt{s_{\psi N}})$ with two adjustable parameters $\Sigma_0$ (short-time limit) and $\Sigma_\infty$ (long-time limit). The time dependence is derived from a two-channel quantum mechanical model coupling the $J/\psi$ and $\psi^\prime$ states, with the oscillatory term governed by the mass difference $\Delta M = M_{\psi^\prime} - M_{J/\psi}$. The fits are performed using MINUIT-Hesse from CERNLIB, and the extracted parameters are then tested against data from NA3 (200 GeV) and NA38/NA50 (200 and 450 GeV).

## Key numbers

- For $\sqrt{s}=10$ GeV and $\tau \rightarrow \infty$, the total cross section for the fully developed $J/\psi$ on a nucleon is $\sigma_{tot}^{J/\psi N} = (2.8 \pm 0.3)$ mb, after correcting for feed-down from $\chi_c$ and $\psi^\prime$ decays.
- For $\sqrt{s}=10$ GeV and $\tau \rightarrow \infty$, the total cross section for the fully developed $\psi^\prime$ on a nucleon is $\sigma_{tot}^{\psi^\prime N} = (10.5 \pm 3.6)$ mb.
- The ratio of these total cross sections is $\sigma_{tot}^{\psi^\prime N} / \sigma_{tot}^{J/\psi N} = 3.8 \pm 1.3$.
- For the initially produced premeson at $\tau=0$ and $\sqrt{s}=10$ GeV, the absorption cross section is $\sigma_{\rm pr} = 2.7 \pm 0.1$ mb when $J/\psi$ is observed, and $\sigma_{\rm pr} = 3.8 \pm 0.6$ mb when $\psi^\prime$ is observed.
- The formation time $\tau_{1/2}$, defined as the time at which the oscillating term in the cross section is reduced by 50%, is $\tau_{1/2} = 0.6$ fm/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of charmonium suppression in $pA$ collisions by using the E866 data to extract both the time-dependent absorption cross section and the asymptotic total cross sections for $J/\psi$ and $\psi^\prime$, building on earlier theoretical frameworks by Kopeliovich (1991) and Huefner (1996). The extracted total cross sections agree with values from photoproduction experiments analyzed via the modified vector dominance model (Huefner, 1998) and follow the systematics of hadron-nucleon total cross sections as a function of $\langle r^2 \rangle$ from Buchmueller (1981). The results enable a consistent description of data from E866, NA3, and NA38/NA50, and provide a benchmark for future studies of charmonium formation times in nuclear matter, though the authors note that a subsequent classical analysis by another group (1999) covers the full $x_F$ range with a different approach.
