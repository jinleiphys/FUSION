# Microscopic Calculation of Total Ordinary Muon Capture Rates for Medium - Weight and Heavy Nuclei

> Total Ordinary Muon Capture (OMC) rates are calculated on the basis of the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation for several spherical nuclei from 90^Zr to 208^Pb. It is shown that total OMC rates calculated with the free value of the axial-vector coupling constant g_A agree well with the experimental data for medium-size nuclei and exceed considerably the experimental rates for heavy nuclei. The sensitivity of theoretical OMC rates to the nuclear residual interactions is discussed.

## Key claim

Total Ordinary Muon Capture (OMC) rates calculated with the free value of the axial-vector coupling constant g_A agree well with experimental data for medium-mass nuclei but exceed the experimental rates considerably for heavy nuclei, indicating that the free coupling constant may not be appropriate for heavy systems.

## Method

The authors calculated total OMC rates for spherical nuclei from 90Zr to 208Pb using the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with a separable residual interaction. The nuclear Hamiltonian included Woods-Saxon mean fields, monopole pairing interactions, and isovector residual interactions with two different radial form factors (r^L and dU/dr). The bound muon wave function and binding energy were calculated approximately accounting for finite nuclear size, and the total capture rate was obtained by summing over all final nuclear states with J^pi = 0^+, 1^+, 2^+, and 3^+.

## Key numbers

- For 90Zr, the calculated total OMC rate with both residual interaction types agrees with the experimental value of 86.6 x 10^5 s^-1 (Suzuki et al., 1987) to within less than 5%
- For 90Zr, varying g_P/g_A from 4.0 to 12.0 changes the theoretical capture rate from 85.5 x 10^5 s^-1 to 76.8 x 10^5 s^-1
- For 90Zr, the total B(GT) strength calculated with the dU/dr residual interaction is 32.0, compared to the experimental value of 34.2 +/- 1.6 from 90Zr(p,n)90Nb measurements
- For natural Sn, the experimental capture rate is 106.7 x 10^5 s^-1 (Suzuki et al., 1987)
- For 90Zr, the isobar analog state (IAS) energy is reproduced at 12.0 MeV with coupling constants kappa^0_1 = -0.43/A (dU/dr interaction) or kappa^0_1 = -35.0/A (r^L interaction)

## Context

This work extends the microscopic QRPA approach previously applied by the authors (EKT1998, KLV2000) to calculate OMC rates across a wider mass range, from medium-weight to heavy nuclei. The study contrasts with earlier closure approximation methods (GP1974, NBGM1987) and local density approximation approaches (COF1990, FW1992) by providing a fully microscopic description that preserves the collective nature of nuclear response. The results enable future consistent calculations of both ordinary and radiative muon capture rates within the same nuclear model, which could help determine the pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P from the ratio of these rates as suggested by GOT1986 and EKT1998.
