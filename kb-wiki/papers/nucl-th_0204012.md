# In medium T matrix for neutron matter

> We calculate the equation of state of pure neutron matter, comparing the G-matrix calculation with the in-medium T-matrix result. At low densities, we obtain similar energies per nucleon, however some differences appear at higher densities. We use the self-consistent spectral functions from the T-matrix approach to calculate the 1S0 superfluid gap including self-energy effects. We find a reduction of the superfluid gap by 30%.

# In medium T matrix for neutron matter

## Key claim

The self-consistent in-medium T-matrix approximation yields a stiffer equation of state for neutron matter at higher densities compared to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, and including self-energy effects via dressed spectral functions reduces the 1S0 superfluid gap by 30% in the region of maximal gap, though the reduction is stronger near gap closure.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent iterations of the in-medium T-matrix equations for pure neutron matter using the separable Paris interaction, solving for the Green's functions and self-energy in the real-time formalism. They compared the resulting energy per nucleon with a BHF calculation using the continuous choice of single-particle energy with the same interaction and numerical grids. The self-consistent spectral functions from the T-matrix approach were then used to solve the superfluid gap equation with dressed propagators for the 1S0 channel, using both the CD-Bonn and Argonne V18 interactions.

## Key numbers

- Below Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.3 \text{ fm}^{-1}\), the difference in energy per particle between the T-matrix and BHF calculations is less than 2 MeV.
- The effective mass in the T-matrix calculation decreases from 1.07 to 1.02 nucleon masses with increasing density, about 0.05 more than the corresponding BHF effective mass.
- The quasiparticle strength factor \(Z_p\) at the Fermi momentum is approximately 0.9 for the range of densities studied.
- The average reduction factor for the superfluid gap is 0.7 in the region of the maximal gap (i.e., a 30% reduction).
- The self-consistent T-matrix calculation could not be performed at densities below \(0.016 \text{ fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons between BHF and T-matrix approaches for nuclear matter, providing a quantitative estimate of their differences for pure neutron matter. The self-energy effects on the superfluid gap are studied in the context of prior work by Baldo (2000), Bozek (2000), and Lombardo (2001), who found that in-medium dressing generally reduces the superfluid gap. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star properties, particularly the equation of state for mass calculations and superfluid parameters relevant for cooling and rotation, though the authors note that higher partial waves and three-body forces are needed for reliable results at higher densities.
