# Spectral Functions in V-QCD with Matter: Masses, Susceptibilities, Diffusion and Conductivity

> We consider a holographic model of QCD in the Veneziano limit of a large number of colors $N_c$ and flavors $N_f$ but fixed $x=N_f/N_c$ (V-QCD). The model exhibits a first order deconfined but chirally broken transition, followed by a second order chirally restored transition in the $μ-T$ plane for a range of plausible holographic parameters. We study the quasi-normal mode spectrum, and derive the pertinent vector and axial spectral functions across the transition regions. The pole masses, susceptibilities, diffusion constants and electric conductivity are also discussed. In particular, the pole masses are found to survive the deconfining transition, to quickly dissolve in the the chirally restored phase by developing substantial widths. The flavor electric conductivities arise sharply in the transition region. The flavor susceptibility is shown to be consistent with the one derived from bulk thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The V-QCD holographic model exhibits a first-order deconfining (but chirally broken) transition followed by a second-order chiral restoration transition in the μ–T plane for massless quarks with Nf/Nc = 1, but contrary to earlier reports from the same group, the corrected numerical phase diagram contains no critical point and the chiral transition is second order for the entire temperature range.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic dual (V-QCD) in the Veneziano limit of large Nc and Nf with fixed x = Nf/Nc, combining an improved holographic QCD action for the glue sector with a DBI action for flavor brane-antibrane pairs including a bulk tachyon field. They numerically solve the coupled Einstein-dilaton-tachyon equations for charged black hole backgrounds with scalar hair at finite temperature and chemical potential, then expand the flavor action to quadratic order around these backgrounds to derive the equations of motion for vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar fluctuations. The quasi-normal mode spectra are obtained by solving these fluctuation equations with infalling boundary conditions at the black hole horizon, and the retarded correlators are used to extract transport coefficients via standard holographic prescriptions.

## Key numbers

* Deconfinement transition temperature at μ = 0: Tc = 0.141 Λ
* Chiral restoration transition temperature at μ = 0: Tχ = 0.148 Λ
* The model exhibits chiral symmetry breaking in vacuum for x ≤ 4 and restores chiral symmetry for 4 ≤ x ≤ 5.5
* The 5-dimensional Planck mass is fixed by requiring the large-temperature pressure to match free fermions and bosons, giving (M ℓ)³ = (1 + 7x/4) / (45π²)

## Context

This work extends the V-QCD model previously developed by Jarvinen and Kiritsis (2012) and studied at finite temperature and chemical potential by Alho et al. (2014), correcting the earlier phase diagram which had reported a critical point. The analysis of quasi-normal modes and spectral functions in the deconfined phase provides time-like information about QCD matter that is inaccessible to lattice simulations due to the sign problem at finite baryon density (Barbour 1997, Endrodi 2011). The results enable comparison with future lattice data and heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and the verification of the Einstein relation for diffusion and susceptibility extends similar checks performed in the probe D3/D7 model by Mas et al. (2009).
