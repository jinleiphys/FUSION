# $K_{1}^{\pm}$ mesons moving in nuclear matter

> Observing the mass shifts of mesons immersed in nuclear matter is interesting, as the changes are expected to shed light on the effects of chiral symmetry breaking on the origin of hadron masses. At the same time, it is important to understand the momentum dependence of the masses for spin-1 mesons, as the changes manifest differently across the two polarization modes. Here, the mass shifts of $K_{1}^{\pm}$ mesons with finite three-momentum in nuclear medium are studied in the QCD sum rule approach. We find that the mass of $K_{1}^{+}$($K_{1}^{-}$) meson is increased(decreased) by the non-trivial momentum effect in both the transverse and longitudinal modes. Specifically, compared to its rest mass in the nuclear medium, in the transverse mode, the mass of $K_{1}^{+}(K_{1}^{-})$ is observed to shift by +2(-55) MeV, while in the longitudinal mode, the mass shift is +13(-11) MeV, all at a momentum of 0.5 GeV. Exploring the medium modifications of $K_{1}$ meson through kaon beams at J-PARC will provide insights on the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The mass of the $K_{1}^{+}$ meson increases while the mass of the $K_{1}^{-}$ meson decreases due to non-trivial momentum effects in both transverse and longitudinal polarization modes, with the magnitude of the shift depending on the charge state and polarization direction; these momentum-dependent shifts are in addition to the density-dependent mass shifts at zero momentum.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rule analysis with operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 6, including twist-2 terms, to calculate the two-current correlation function for $K_{1}^{\pm}$ mesons with finite three-momentum in nuclear matter at normal nuclear density. The correlation function was decomposed into transverse and longitudinal polarization modes with respect to the momentum direction, and the mass shifts were extracted by minimizing a function of the linear density parameters for the mass, residue, and continuum threshold within a Borel window of $0.97 \leq M^{2} \leq 2.18$ GeV$^{2}$.

## Key numbers

- At momentum $|\vec{q}| = 0.5$ GeV in the transverse mode: $K_{1}^{+}$ mass shift = +2 MeV, $K_{1}^{-}$ mass shift = -55 MeV
- At momentum $|\vec{q}| = 0.5$ GeV in the longitudinal mode: $K_{1}^{+}$ mass shift = +13 MeV, $K_{1}^{-}$ mass shift = -11 MeV
- At zero momentum in nuclear matter ($\vec{q}=0$): $K_{1}^{-}$ mass shift = -257 MeV, $K_{1}^{+}$ mass shift = +65 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density used: $\rho_{0} = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$
- Vacuum continuum threshold: $s_{0} = 2.412$ GeV$^{2}$
- Vacuum overlap strength: $F_{K_{1}} = 0.049$ GeV$^{2}$

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule studies of momentum-dependent polarization modes previously applied to light vector mesons ($\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$) by Lee (1998) and later revised for the $\phi$ meson by Gubler (2014) and Kim (2020), and to heavy quarkonium by Kim (2023). The authors build on the zero-momentum nuclear matter results for $K_{1}$ mesons from Song (2019) and the chiral partner framework connecting $K_{1}$ and $K^{*}$ mesons from Lee (2023). The results provide guidelines for future experiments at J-PARC using kaon beams to observe $K_{1}$ meson modifications in nuclear targets, where the different polarization modes could be distinguished through angular dependence of decay particles.
