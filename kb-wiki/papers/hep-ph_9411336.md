# Thermal photon production in heavy ion collisions

> Using a three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation of the collision and an equation of state containing a first order phase transition to the quark-gluon plasma, we study thermal photon production for $Au+Au$ collisions at $E_{lab}=11.5$ AGeV and for $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $160$ AGeV. We obtain surprisingly high rates of thermal photons even at the lower energy, suggesting that, contrary to what was expected so far, photon production may be an interesting topic for experimental search also at the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron. When applied to the reaction $S+Au$ at $200$ AGeV, our model can reproduce preliminary data obtained by the WA80 Collaboration without having to postulate the existence of an extremely long-lived mixed phase as was recently proposed.

## Key claim
Thermal photon production rates in heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies (11.5 AGeV) are surprisingly high, suggesting that experimental searches for direct photons are feasible at these lower energies, contrary to prior expectations. The model reproduces WA80 preliminary data for S+Au at 200 AGeV without requiring an extremely long-lived mixed phase as had been proposed.

## Method
The authors used a three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulation (HYLANDER code) with full stopping initial conditions and an equation of state from lattice QCD that includes a first-order phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at a critical temperature of 200 MeV. They integrated thermal photon production rates from the QGP phase, the mixed phase, and the hadronic phase (including A1-meson contributions) over the space-time evolution of the expanding fireball for Au+Au at 11.5 AGeV, Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV, and S+Au at 200 AGeV.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at 11.5 AGeV: total lifetime of thermalized matter is ~12 fm/c; pure QGP and mixed phase lifetime is about 7 fm/c.
- For Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV: total lifetime of thermalized matter is ~16 fm/c; pure QGP and mixed phase lifetime is about 7 fm/c.
- Critical temperature used in the equation of state: T_c = 200 MeV.
- Initial temperatures at SPS energies: T_i ~ 300 MeV.
- At temperatures T ~ 0.2 GeV, the hadron gas outshines the QGP by a factor of about two in photon production rates.
- For S+Au at 200 AGeV, the model reproduces WA80 preliminary data without assuming mixed phase lifetimes of 30-40 fm/c as proposed in prior work.

## Context
This work extends prior thermal photon calculations (Shuryak, 1993; Kapusta, 1991) from SPS energies down to AGS energies, where photon production had been considered too low to measure. The results contradict the proposal by Shuryak (1993) that an extremely long-lived mixed phase (30-40 fm/c) was needed to explain the WA80 data. The predictions are directly testable by the WA98 Collaboration at the SPS and by the PHENIX detector at the AGS, enabling experimental searches for thermal photons as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation at both energy regimes.
