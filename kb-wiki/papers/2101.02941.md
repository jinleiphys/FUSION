# Fermi polaron in low-density spin-polarized neutron matter

> We study the properties of a spin-down neutron impurity immersed in a low-density free Fermi gas of spin-up neutrons. In particular, we analyze its energy ($E_\downarrow$), effective mass ($m^*_\downarrow$) and quasiparticle residue ($Z_\downarrow$). Results are compared with those of state-of-the-art quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the attractive Fermi polaron realized in ultracold atomic gases experiments, and with those of previous studies of the neutron polaron. Calculations are performed within the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach using the chiral two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem and Machleidt at N$^3$LO with a 500 MeV cut-off and the Argonne V18 phenomenological potential. Only contributions from the $^1S_0$ partial wave, which is the dominant one in the low-density region considered, are included. Contributions from three-nucleon forces are expected to be irrelevant at these densities and, therefore, are neglected in the calculation. Our results show that for Fermi momenta between $\sim 0.25$ and $\sim 0.45$ fm$^{-1}$ the energy, effective mass and quasiparticle residue of the impurity vary only slightly, respectively, in the ranges $-0.604\,E_F < E_\downarrow < -0.635\,E_F $, $1.300\,m < m^*_\downarrow < 1.085\, m$ and $0.741 <Z_\downarrow< 0.836$ in the case of the chiral interaction, and $-0.621\,E_F < E_\downarrow < -0.643\,E_F $, $1.310\,m < m^*_\downarrow < 1.089\, m$ and $0.739 <Z_\downarrow< 0.832$ when using the Argonne V18 potential. These results are compatible with those derived from ultracold atoms and show that a spin-down neutron impurity in a free Fermi gas of spin-up neutrons with a Fermi momentum in the range $0.25\lesssim k_F \lesssim 0.45$ fm$^{-1}$ exhibits properties very similar to those of an attractive Fermi polaron in the unitary limit.

## Key claim

A spin-down neutron impurity in a low-density free Fermi gas of spin-up neutrons with Fermi momentum between 0.25 and 0.45 fm⁻¹ exhibits properties very similar to those of an attractive Fermi polaron in the unitary limit, but the paper's hedge is that low-density neutron matter never actually reaches the unitary limit despite having properties "close" to it in the range where the average interparticle spacing lies between the effective range and the scattering length.

## Method

The author solved the coupled-channel Bethe-Goldstone integral equation within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, using only the ¹S₀ partial wave contribution from either the chiral two-body nucleon-nucleon interaction of Entem and Machleidt at N³LO with a 500 MeV cut-off (EM500) or the Argonne V18 phenomenological potential. The on-shell BHF potential for the impurity was computed self-consistently, and the effective mass was extracted by fitting the calculated BHF energy to a quadratic form at low momentum, while the quasiparticle residue was obtained from the off-shell BHF potential.

## Key numbers

- For the EM500 interaction at Fermi momenta between 0.25 and 0.45 fm⁻¹: impurity energy ranges from -0.604 E_F to -0.635 E_F; effective mass ranges from 1.300 m to 1.085 m; quasiparticle residue ranges from 0.741 to 0.836.
- For the Argonne V18 potential at Fermi momenta between 0.25 and 0.45 fm⁻¹: impurity energy ranges from -0.621 E_F to -0.643 E_F; effective mass ranges from 1.310 m to 1.089 m; quasiparticle residue ranges from 0.739 to 0.832.
- The ¹S₀ neutron-neutron scattering length is a_S = -18.5 fm; the effective range is r_e = 2.75 fm.
- State-of-the-art quantum Monte Carlo calculations (van Houcke et al.) predict a proportionality constant eta = -0.61565(4) for the polaron energy in the unitary limit; experiments with ⁶Li atoms give eta = -0.58(5) (Shin et al.) and eta = -0.64(7) (Schirotzek et al.).
- The full many-body analysis of Combescot and Giraud found m*_↓ = 1.197 m; the diagrammatic Monte Carlo method of Vlietinck et al. obtained Z_↓ = 0.759.

## Context

This work extends the polaron concept to neutron matter, following earlier quantum Monte Carlo studies by Forbes et al. (2014) who analyzed the neutron polaron energy using Argonne V18 and modified Poschl-Teller potentials, and by Roggero et al. (2015) who studied a proton impurity in low-density neutron matter with chiral interactions. The results are compared with ultracold atomic gas experiments and quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the attractive Fermi polaron, and the findings confirm that a spin-down neutron impurity in this density regime behaves similarly to a Fermi polaron in the unitary limit, which may help constrain nuclear energy density functionals for polarized nuclear systems.
