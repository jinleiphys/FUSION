# Next-to-leading order transverse momentum broadening for Drell-Yan production in p+A collisions

> We present the nuclear transverse momentum broadening for Drell-Yan lepton pair production in p+A collisions at next-to-leading order (NLO) in powers of strong coupling constant $α_s$. We verify that the transverse momentum weighted differential cross section in NLO perturbative QCD (pQCD) at twist-4 can be factorized into the convolution of parton distribution function of an active parton in the projectile proton, a twist-4 multiparton correlation function of the target nucleus, and perturbatively calculable hard coefficient function. We identify a QCD evolution equation for such a twist-4 nuclear gluon-quark correlation function, and verify its universality -- its process independence. This finding demonstrates the prediction power of pQCD factorization approach for studying parton multiple scattering in nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the nuclear transverse momentum broadening for Drell-Yan lepton pair production in p+A collisions can be calculated at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD within a twist-4 factorization formalism, and that the associated twist-4 gluon-quark correlation function satisfies a universal QCD evolution equation independent of the process, but only for the gluon-quark and gluon-gluon double scattering channels (the quark-quark channel is left for future work).

## Method

The authors perform a collinear expansion within the generalized high-twist factorization formalism to compute the transverse momentum weighted differential cross section for Drell-Yan production at NLO in the strong coupling constant α_s. They evaluate four subprocesses for gluon-quark double scattering (soft-soft, hard-hard, soft-hard, and hard-soft) and the quark-gluon Compton scattering channel involving gluon-gluon correlation functions, using dimensional regularization in n=4-2ε dimensions to isolate soft and collinear divergences. The soft divergences cancel between real and virtual diagrams, while collinear divergences are absorbed into the parton distribution functions of the projectile proton and the twist-4 multiparton correlation functions of the target nucleus, from which the QCD evolution equations for these correlation functions are derived.

## Key numbers

- The LO transverse momentum broadening is given by Δ⟨q_T²⟩ = (4π²α_s/N_c) × [∑_q e_q² ∫(dx'/x') f_{q̄/p}(x') T_{gq}(x_B,0,0)] / [∑_q e_q² ∫(dx'/x') f_{q̄/p}(x') f_{q/A}(x_B)], where α_s is the strong coupling constant, N_c=3, and T_{gq} is the twist-4 gluon-quark correlation function.
- The NLO calculation is performed in n=4-2ε dimensions, with the two-body final-state phase space given by dPS^(2) = (1/16π²) ∫ dx δ(x₁+x₂-x-x_e) (4π/Q²)^ε (1/Γ(1-ε)) z^ε (1-z)^{1-2ε} ∫₀¹ dv [v(1-v)]^{-ε}.
- The soft-soft double scattering divergent terms contain T_{gq}⊗H_{d2}^{ss}=T_{gq}(x,0,0)C_F and T_{gq}⊗H_{d1}^{ss}=T_{gq}(x,0,0)C_F z²(1+z²)/(1-z)_+.
- The hard-hard double scattering divergent terms contain T_{gq}⊗H_{d2}^{hh}=T_{gq}(x,0,0)C_A and T_{gq}⊗H_{d1}^{hh}=T_{gq}(x,0,0)(1+z²)/(1-z)_+[C_A z+C_F(1-z)²].
- The soft-hard double scattering divergent terms contain T_{gq}⊗H_{d2}^{sh}=-T_{gq}(x,0,0)C_A/2.

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order calculations of Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening by Guo (1998), Kang (2008, 2011, 2012), and others to NLO in α_s, following the same high-twist factorization approach developed by Luo (1992, 1994) and Qiu (1990). It complements the authors' earlier NLO analysis for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) by Kang (2013, 2014), demonstrating the universality of the twist-4 multiparton correlation functions across different processes. The results enable more precise determination of nuclear medium properties from future measurements at Fermilab-E906, RHIC, and the LHC, and provide a theoretical foundation for studying parton multiple scattering in nuclear matter at higher perturbative orders.
