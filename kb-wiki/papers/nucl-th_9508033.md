# Nuclear Structure Corrections to Deuterium Hyperfine Structure and Lamb Shift

> The low-energy theorem for the forward Compton scattering is generalized to the case of an arbitrary target spin. The generalization is used to calculate the corresponding contribution to the deuterium hyperfine structure. The nuclear-structure corrections are quite essential in this case due to the deuteron large size. Corrections of this type calculated here remove the discrepancy between the theoretical and experimental values of the deuterium hyperfine splitting. Explicit analytical result is obtained also for the deuteron polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift.

## Key claim

The nuclear-structure corrections calculated in this paper remove the 45 kHz discrepancy between the theoretical pure QED value and the experimental value of the deuterium hyperfine splitting, yielding a correction of 43 kHz that is considered satisfactory given the approximations of the zero-range nuclear model.

## Method

The authors generalize the low-energy theorem for forward Compton scattering to arbitrary target spin, then apply it to calculate two-photon exchange contributions to the electron-deuteron scattering amplitude. The calculation is performed with logarithmic accuracy, separating the integration region into low momentum transfers (cut off at the inverse deuteron size κ = 45.7 MeV) where the deuteron is treated as a composite object, and high momentum transfers (cut off at the hadronic scale m_ρ = 770 MeV) where the amplitude is the coherent sum of free proton and neutron contributions. Additional contributions from deuteron virtual excitations and finite charge/magnetic moment distributions are computed in the zero-range approximation (zra) for the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

* Experimental deuterium hyperfine splitting: ν_exp = 327,384.352,522,2(17) kHz
* Pure QED theoretical value: ν_QED = 327,339.27(7) kHz
* Discrepancy between experiment and QED: ν_exp - ν_QED = 45 kHz
* Calculated nuclear-structure correction to hyperfine splitting: Δν = 43 kHz
* Inverse deuteron size: κ = 45.7 MeV
* Hadronic cutoff scale: m_ρ = 770 MeV
* Deuteron magnetic moment: μ_d = 0.857 (in nuclear magnetons)
* Proton magnetic moment: μ_p = 2.79
* Neutron magnetic moment: μ_n = -1.91
* Calculated static deuteron electric polarizability: α_d(0) = 0.64 fm³
* Experimental deuteron electric polarizability: 0.70(5) fm³
* Deuteron polarizability contribution to Lamb shift: -22.3 kHz (Coulomb: -19.7 kHz, magnetic: -2.6 kHz)
* Deuteron magnetic polarizability contribution to Lamb shift: 0.31 kHz

## Context

This work extends earlier intuitive arguments by Bohr (year not given) and more detailed discussions by Low (year not given), Loskutov (year not given), and Grotch and Feynman (year not given) regarding nuclear-structure contributions to deuterium hyperfine splitting. The calculation of the deuteron polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift improves upon earlier square-well potential calculations by Pachucki and others (year not given) and separable potential calculations by Leidemann and Rosenfelder (year not given), providing a closed analytical result in the zero-range approximation. The results enable more precise tests of deuteron structure models through atomic spectroscopy.
