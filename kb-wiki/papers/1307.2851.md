# Thermodynamics of (2+1)-flavor strongly interacting matter at nonzero isospin

> We investigate the phase structure of strongly interacting matter at non-vanishing isospin before the onset of pion condensation in the framework of the unquenched Polyakov-Quark-Meson model with 2+1 quark flavors. We show results for the order parameters and all relevant thermodynamic quantities. In particular, we obtain a moderate change of the pressure with isospin at vanishing baryon chemical potential, whereas the chiral condensate decreases more appreciably. We compare the effective model to recent lattice data for the decrease of the pseudo-critical temperature with the isospin chemical potential. We also demonstrate the major role played by the value of the pion mass in the curvature of the transition line, and the need for lattice results with a physical pion mass. Limitations of the model at nonzero chemical potential are also discussed.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-Quark-Meson model with 2+1 quark flavors, when unquenched and including the fermionic vacuum loop, reproduces the qualitative decrease of the pseudo-critical temperature with isospin chemical potential seen in lattice data, but the curvature of the transition line is much more sensitive to the value of the pion mass than to the number of quark flavors, and the model's results for the slope are significantly larger than lattice results when using a pion mass of 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the (2+1)-flavor Polyakov-Quark-Meson model to nonzero isospin chemical potential by generalizing the meson self-interaction potential to distinguish up and down quark condensates, and by including the fermionic vacuum loop contribution and an unquenched Polyakov-loop potential with a running critical temperature that depends on the quark chemical potentials. They computed the grand canonical potential in a mean-field approximation, treating the Polyakov-loop variables as independent real quantities to avoid the sign problem, and determined the order parameters and thermodynamic quantities as functions of temperature and isospin chemical potential for values below the onset of pion condensation.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature at zero isospin and baryon chemical potential is \(T_c^0 = 164\) MeV.
- The transition region for chiral and deconfinement observables at zero density is 152-164 MeV, consistent with lattice data from Borsanyi (2010).
- For 2+1 flavors with physical pion mass (138 MeV), the transition temperature just before the onset of pion condensation is about two percent lower than the 2-flavor result.
- For 2 flavors with a pion mass of 400 MeV, the decrease of the pseudo-critical temperature with isospin chemical potential is significantly larger than the lattice results of Cea (2012).
- The vacuum pion mass used is \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, the kaon mass is 495 MeV, the eta mass is 548 MeV, and the sigma meson mass is 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor studies of nonzero isospin in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Toublan 2003, Frank 2003, He 2005) and the Quark-Meson model (Kamikado 2012) to include strange quarks and an unquenched Polyakov-loop potential. It also builds on the combination of chiral perturbation theory with the fuzzy bag model by Fraga (2008) and compares directly to lattice data from Cea (2012) and Kogut (2004). The results enable future investigations of pion condensation at larger isospin and applications to the cosmological QCD phase transition at nonzero lepton number.
