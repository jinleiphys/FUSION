# Constraining the $^{30}$P($p,Î³)^{31}$S reaction rate in ONe novae via the weak, low-energy, $Î²$-delayed proton decay of $^{31}$Cl

> The $^{30}$P$(p,Î³)^{31}$S reaction plays an important role in understanding nucleosynthesis of $A\geq 30$ nuclides in oxygen-neon novae. ## Key claim

The 260-keV resonance in the $^{30}$P($p,\gamma)^{31}$S reaction, previously identified as potentially dominant for ONe nova nucleosynthesis, has been directly constrained via the weak $\beta$-delayed proton decay of $^{31}$Cl, yielding a resonance strength of $\omega\gamma = 80(48)$ $\mu$eV; this measurement reduces the dominant nuclear physics uncertainty for modeling $A \geq 30$ nucleosynthesis in ONe novae, but the final rate uncertainty is now limited by the theoretical $\gamma$-decay width of the 6390-keV state in $^{31}$S.

## Method

The Gaseous Detector with Germanium Tagging (GADGET) system, combining a gas-filled proportional counter with the Segmented Germanium Array (SeGA), was used to measure $\beta$-delayed protons from $^{31}$Cl implanted in P10 gas at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory. The 6390-keV $J^\pi = 3/2^+$ state in $^{31}$S, populated by $^{31}$Cl $\beta^+$ decay, was observed to emit protons to the $^{30}$P ground state with a decay energy of 273(10) keV; the proton branching ratio was extracted by comparing the 260-keV proton peak intensity to the known 1-MeV $\beta$-delayed proton branch, and the $\gamma$-decay width was calculated using shell-model theory with the USDC Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- $\beta$-delayed proton decay intensity for the 260-keV resonance: $I^{260}_{\beta p} = 8.3^{+1.2}_{-0.9} \times 10^{-6}$, the weakest $\beta$-delayed charged-particle emission ever measured below 400 keV.
- Proton branching ratio: $\Gamma_p / \Gamma = 2.5^{+0.4}_{-0.3} \times 10^{-4}$.
- Resonance strength: $\omega\gamma = 80(48)$ $\mu$eV.
- Adopted $\gamma$-decay width for the 6390-keV state: $\Gamma_\gamma = 490(280)$ meV.
- Predicted silicon isotopic ratio in ONe nova ejecta: $\delta(^{30}\text{Si}/^{28}\text{Si}) = +1.14^{+0.93}_{-0.35} \times 10^4$ per mil deviation from solar abundances.
- For V838 Herculis (peak temperature 0.30-0.31 GK), predicted abundance ratios O/S = $0.86^{+0.12}_{-0.04}$ and S/Al = $15.7^{+0.9}_{-2.2}$ agree with astronomical observation to within $1\sigma$.

## Context

This work directly constrains the $^{30}$P($p,\gamma)^{31}$S reaction rate, which sensitivity studies by Jose (2001, 2006) identified as the dominant nuclear physics uncertainty for $A \geq 30$ nucleosynthesis in ONe novae. The measurement resolves a long-standing ambiguity: previous Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations (Rauscher 2000) were not expected to be accurate at nova temperatures, and earlier simulations by Jose (2004) found that $^{30}$Si excesses varied by a factor of about 6 between rate limits. The new rate enables concrete predictions of $^{30}$Si/$^{28}$Si excesses in presolar nova grains (previously ambiguous since Amari 2001) and reduces uncertainties in nova thermometers based on O/S, S/Al, O/P, and P/Al ratios by factors of 2-4, with the main limitation now being observational precision rather than nuclear physics.
