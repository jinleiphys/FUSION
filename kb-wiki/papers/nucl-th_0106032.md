# Relativistic approaches to structure functions of nuclei

> We employ a propagator technique to derive a new relativistic $1/\qq$ expansion of the structure function of a nucleus, composed of point-nucleons. We exploit non-relativistic features of low-momentum nucleons in the target and only treat relativistically the nucleon after absorption of a high-momentum virtual photon. The new series permits a 3-dimensional reduction of each term and a formal summation of all Final State Interaction terms. We then show that a relativistic structure function can be obtained from its non-relativistic analog by a mere change of a scaling variable and an addition of an energy shift. We compare the obtained result with an ad hoc generalized Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith theory, previously used in computations of nuclear structure functions.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new relativistic expansion of the nuclear structure function in inverse powers of the three-momentum transfer, exploiting the fact that only the struck nucleon after absorbing the virtual photon needs relativistic treatment while all other nucleons in the target remain non-relativistic. The key result is that the relativistic structure function can be obtained from its non-relativistic analog by a change of scaling variable and an addition of an energy shift, but this equivalence holds only under the assumption that the residual interaction between the struck nucleon and spectator is local and that negative-energy pole contributions in the relativistic impulse series can be neglected.

## Method

The authors employ a propagator technique to derive a relativistic generalization of the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) expansion for the nuclear structure function of a system of point-nucleons. They start from the relativistic forward Compton amplitude expressed in terms of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nucleon-spectator scattering operator, then apply non-relativistic limits to all quantities involving low-momentum nucleons (target ground state, spectator, struck nucleon before photon absorption). The resulting series permits a three-dimensional reduction of each term and a formal summation of all Final State Interaction (FSI) terms into a closed expression involving a three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum of nucleons in nuclear ground states: \(\langle \mathbf{p}^2 \rangle^{1/2} \lesssim p_F \approx 0.3\) GeV
- The relativistic scaling variable for the GRS series: \(y_G = \frac{M}{q} \left[ \nu \left(1 - \frac{\Delta}{M}\right) - \frac{Q^2}{2M} \right]\), where \(Q^2 = \mathbf{q}^2 - \nu^2\)
- The non-relativistic GRS scaling variable: \(y_W = \frac{M}{q} \left( \nu - \frac{q^2}{2M} \right)\)
- The relativistic IA scaling variable: \(\bar{y}_0 = -q + \sqrt{2M(\nu - \Delta) + (\nu - \Delta)^2}\)
- The asymptotic limit of the maximum excitation energy in the relativistic GRS series: \(\lim_{q \to \infty} \tilde{E}_{\text{max}}(q, y_G, p) = y_G + p\)
- The asymptotic limit of the maximum excitation energy in the relativistic IA: \(\lim_{q \to \infty} \bar{E}_{\text{max}}(q, \bar{y}_0, p) = \bar{y}_0 + p\)

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory (Gersch, Rodriguez, and Smith, 1973) to the relativistic regime needed for high-energy electron scattering data. It contrasts with the relativistic Impulse Series (IS) approach, where three-dimensional reduction is complicated by negative-energy poles in relativistic nucleon propagators. The result enables practical calculations of nuclear structure functions using the simpler non-relativistic formalism with modified scaling variables, and the authors note that the leading GRS term is a better approximation to the total structure function than the Impulse Approximation, based on comparisons with exactly solvable models from prior work (Koh, 1976; Gurvitz and Rinat, 1994, 1995).
