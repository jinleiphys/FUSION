# Annihilation Mechanism of Dilepton Emission from Finite Fireball

> Medium-induced modifications of the pion and quark-antiquark annihilation mechanisms of dilepton production during relativistic heavy ion collisions are considered. Due to the dense hadron environment, the pions produced during a collision are effectively confined in a finite volume, in which they live for a finite time which is scaled as the lifetime of a fireball. Our results indicate that, due to the space-time finiteness of the pion system which generates the corresponding quantum randomization, the dilepton rates are finite in the invariant low-mass region M<2m_π. It is found that the spatial finiteness of quark wave functions and the finiteness of the lifetime of excited states result in the same effect for the quark-antiquark annihilation to dileptons. The breaking of the detailed energy-momentum conservation due to the broken translation invariance is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the finite space-time volume of the pion system produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions causes a quantum smearing of energy-momentum conservation, which makes dilepton production rates finite below the standard threshold of \(M < 2m_\pi\), down to the two-lepton mass scale. This effect arises from the uncertainty principle rather than from medium modifications of the rho-meson properties themselves.

## Method

The authors compute the dilepton production rate from pion-pion annihilation (\(\pi^+ \pi^- \to \rho \to \gamma^* \to l\bar{l}\)) and quark-antiquark annihilation, replacing the infinite-volume delta function \(\delta^4(K-P)\) with a finite-volume form-factor \(\rho(K-P)\) that smears the relation between the total 4-momentum of the incoming pair \(K\) and the outgoing lepton pair \(P\). They adopt a Gaussian form-factor for the pion source with lifetime \(\tau\) and radius \(R\), keep the vacuum rho-meson mass and width fixed, and compare two models: model (a) where the rho-meson has zero mean free path and model (b) where it propagates as in vacuum. The thermal averaging over pion momenta uses a Boltzmann distribution at temperature \(T\).

## Key numbers

- The rho-meson form-factor parameters used are \(m_\rho = 775\) MeV, \(m'_\rho = 761\) MeV, and \(\Gamma_\rho = 118\) MeV.
- The fireball size is taken as \(R_0 \propto 4\div 10\) fm, with the pion subsystem size \(R \propto 1\div 5\) fm.
- The lifetime of the pion system is taken as \(\tau = 2\) fm/c, giving an energy uncertainty up to \(\Delta E = 100\) MeV, which allows dilepton production down to \(M \propto 2m_\pi - 100\) MeV.
- For electron-positron pairs, the rate becomes measurable down to \(2m_e \approx 1\) MeV.
- The lifetime range considered is \(\tau = 4\div 10\) fm/c and the spatial range is \(R = 4\div 10\) fm for the fireball.

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of dilepton production from pion annihilation (Domokos, McLerran and Toimela (1985), Gale and Kapusta (1987), Wong (1994)) by incorporating the finite space-time volume of the hadron fireball, which is motivated by experimental observations from the CERES collaboration (Agakichiev (1995, 1998)) showing an enhancement in the 200-800 MeV invariant mass region. The paper contrasts with approaches that modify the pion dispersion relation in medium (Rapp and Wambach (1996), Song (1996)) or include baryon resonance effects (Rapp and Wambach (1997, 1999)), and instead attributes the sub-threshold yield to quantum uncertainty effects from finite system size and lifetime. The results enable future calculations using more realistic source form-factors and can be applied to both pion-pion and quark-antiquark annihilation channels.
