# Quantum number projection at finite temperature via thermofield dynamics

> Applying the thermo field dynamics, we reformulate exact quantum number projection in the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. Explicit formulae are derived for the simultaneous projection of particle number and angular momentum, in parallel to the zero-temperature case. We also propose a practical method for the variation-after-projection calculation, by approximating entropy without conflict with the Peierls inequality. The quantum number projection in the finite-temperature mean-field theory will be useful to study effects of quantum fluctuations associated with the conservation laws on thermal properties of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates exact quantum number projection (particle number and angular momentum) in finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory using thermo field dynamics, deriving explicit formulae that maintain complete parallel to the zero-temperature formalism. The authors also propose an approximate entropy expression that preserves the Peierls inequality, thereby enabling variation-after-projection calculations at finite temperature.

## Method

The authors employ thermo field dynamics (TFD) to enlarge the quasiparticle operator space by introducing tilded operators, allowing thermal expectation values to be expressed as vacuum expectation values in an enlarged TFD space. They then derive the extended Bogoliubov transformation that unifies the standard generalized Bogoliubov transformation with the TFD thermal transformation, and use this framework to compute projected ensemble averages for the simultaneous projection of angular momentum (SU(2)_J), proton number (U(1)_Z), and neutron number (U(1)_N). The generalized Wick's theorem is applied in the TFD space to reduce the vacuum expectation values of projected operators, and an approximation for the entropy is constructed that maintains the variational principle for free energy via the Peierls inequality.

## Key numbers

- The group order for SU(2)_J angular momentum projection is g = 16π², obtained from the volume integral over Euler angles (α from 0 to 2π, β from 0 to π, γ from 0 to 4π for half-odd-integer spin)
- The character of the SU(2)_J representation matrix is given by χ_J(ω) = sin[(J+1/2)ω]/sin(ω/2)
- The quasiparticle occupation number is f_μ = 1/(e^{E_μ/T} + 1), where E_μ are the quasiparticle energies and T is the temperature
- The entropy is expressed as S = -∑_μ [f_μ ln f_μ + (1-f_μ) ln(1-f_μ)]

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature quantum number projection methods developed by Hara and Iwasaki (1979) and others to finite temperature, addressing the complication that the projection operator does not commute with the Boltzmann-Gibbs operator in the mean-field approximation. The formalism is equivalent to that of Rossignoli and Ring (1994) but offers advantages in straightforward derivation and identification of variational parameters. The method enables studies of quantum fluctuations associated with conservation laws on thermal properties of nuclei, and the proposed entropy approximation makes variation-after-projection calculations practical for finite-temperature nuclear systems.
