# Databases and tools for nuclear astrophysics applications BRUSsels Nuclear LIBrary (BRUSLIB), Nuclear Astrophysics Compilation of REactions II (NACRE II) and Nuclear NETwork GENerator (NETGEN)

> An update of a previous description of the BRUSLIB+NACRE package of nuclear data for astrophysics and of the web-based nuclear network generator NETGEN is presented. The new version of BRUSLIB contains the latest predictions of a wide variety of nuclear data based on the most recent version of the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme-HFB model. The nuclear masses, radii, spin/parities, deformations, single-particle schemes, matter densities, nuclear level densities, E1 strength functions, fission properties, and partition functions are provided for all nuclei lying between the proton and neutron drip lines over the 8<=Z<=110 range, whose evaluation is based on a unique microscopic model that ensures a good compromise between accuracy, reliability, and feasibility. In addition, these various ingredients are used to calculate about 100000 Hauser-Feshbach n-, p-, a-, and gamma-induced reaction rates based on the reaction code TALYS. NACRE is superseded by the NACRE II compilation for 15 charged-particle transfer reactions and 19 charged-particle radiative captures on stable targets with mass numbers A < 16. NACRE II features the inclusion of experimental data made available after the publication of NACRE in 1999 and up to 2011. In addition, the extrapolation of the available data to the very low energies of astrophysical relevance is improved through the systematic use of potential models. Uncertainties in the rates are also evaluated on this basis. The latest release of the web-based tool NETGEN is presented. It contains in a fully documented form the new BRUSLIB and NACRE II data, as well as new experiment-based radiative neutron capture cross sections. The full new versions of BRUSLIB, NACRE II, and NETGEN are available electronically at http://www.astro.ulb.ac.be/NuclearData. The nuclear material is presented in an extended tabular form complemented with a variety of graphical interfaces.

## Key claim

The paper presents an update of the BRUSLIB+NACRE package and the NETGEN web tool, claiming that the new BRUSLIB data are based on a unique microscopic model (the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme-HFB model) that ensures a good compromise between accuracy, reliability, and feasibility for all nuclei between the proton and neutron drip lines over the 8 ≤ Z ≤ 110 range, while the NACRE II compilation improves upon NACRE by including experimental data up to 2011 and using systematic potential models for extrapolation to astrophysically relevant low energies.

## Method

The authors used the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) model to predict nuclear ground-state properties (masses, radii, spins, parities, deformations, single-particle schemes, matter densities) for 8508 nuclides with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 110 lying between the proton and neutron drip lines. These properties were then used as input to the reaction code TALYS to calculate about 100,000 Hauser-Feshbach neutron-, proton-, alpha-, and gamma-induced reaction rates. For NACRE II, the authors compiled experimental data published between 1999 and 2011 for 34 charged-particle reactions on targets with A < 16, and used phenomenological potential models (DWBA for transfer reactions, potential model for radiative captures) to extrapolate cross sections to low energies and evaluate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- 8508 nuclear masses are provided for all nuclides with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 110 lying between the proton and neutron drip lines, derived from 21 HFB models
- About 100,000 Hauser-Feshbach reaction rates are calculated using TALYS for (n,γ), (n,p), (n,α), (p,γ), (p,n), (p,α), (α,γ), (α,n), (α,p), (γ,n), (γ,p), and (γ,α) reactions on some 8500 targets with 8 ≤ Z ≤ 110
- NACRE II surveys 34 two-body exothermic reactions (15 particle-transfer and 19 radiative capture reactions) on targets with A ≤ 16
- Nuclear level densities are provided for 8508 nuclides for excitation energies U up to 200 MeV and spins up to J = 49 for even-A nuclei or 99/2 for odd-A nuclei
- Partition functions are provided for 8508 nuclides for temperatures in the 0.001 ≤ T₉ ≤ 10 range (where T₉ denotes temperature in 10⁹ K)
- E1 strength functions are provided for all 8508 nuclides on an energy grid of 0.1 MeV between 0 and 30 MeV
- Fission properties are provided for nuclides with 90 ≤ Z ≤ 110 lying between the valley of β-stability and the neutron drip lines
- NETGEN v10.0 contains data for about 8600 nuclides located between the neutron and proton drip lines in the 1 ≤ Z ≤ 110 range

## Context

This work extends the earlier BRUSLIB package described by Arnould and Goriely (2005) and supersedes the NACRE compilation from 1999 with the new NACRE II compilation. The BRUSLIB database provides a unique microscopic framework for nuclear data that competes with other databases such as the Karlsruhe Astrophysical Database of Nucleosynthesis in Stars (KADoNiS) and the JINA REACLIB database. The NETGEN tool enables astrophysicists to build custom nuclear reaction networks using these data, and the full package is available electronically at http://www.astro.ulb.ac.be/NuclearData.
