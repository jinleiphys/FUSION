# Static quark anti-quark free energy and the running coupling at finite temperature

> We analyze the free energy of a static quark anti-quark pair in quenched QCD at short and large distances. From this we deduce running couplings, g^2(r,T), and determine the length scale that separates at high temperature the short distance perturbative regime from the large distance non-perturbative regime in the QCD plasma phase. Ambiguities in the definition of a coupling beyond the perturbative regime are discussed in their relation to phenomenological considerations on heavy quark bound states in the quark gluon plasma. Our analysis suggests that it is more appropriate to characterize the non-perturbative properties of the QCD plasma phase close to T_c in terms remnants of the confinement part of the QCD force rather than a strong Coulombic force.

# Static quark anti-quark free energy and the running coupling at finite temperature

## Key claim

The short-distance behavior of the singlet free energy in quenched QCD above the deconfinement temperature is controlled by a temperature-independent running coupling that agrees with the zero-temperature perturbative result for distances up to about 0.1 fm, contradicting scenarios that invoke a strongly enhanced Coulombic coupling in the quark-gluon plasma near T_c. The non-perturbative properties of the plasma phase close to T_c are better characterized by remnants of the confining force rather than a strong Coulombic force.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of SU(3) gauge theory (quenched QCD) on 32^3 x N_tau lattices with N_tau = 4, 8, and 16 using the tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action, covering temperatures from 1.03 T_c to 12 T_c. They computed the color singlet free energy from Polyakov loop correlation functions in Coulomb gauge, renormalized by matching to the zero-temperature heavy quark potential, and extracted running couplings in both the qq-scheme (from the force) and V-scheme (from the potential) at short distances, while fitting the large-distance behavior to a Debye-screened Coulomb form.

## Key numbers

- The screening length scale separating short-distance from large-distance regimes follows r_screen = (0.48 +/- 0.01) fm * T_c / T over the entire temperature range analyzed.
- At temperatures below 3 T_c, the running coupling alpha_qq(r,T) agrees with the zero-temperature perturbative result for distances r less than or approximately equal to 0.1 fm.
- At high temperatures T >= 6 T_c, the large-distance coupling follows a rescaled 2-loop perturbative running with alpha(T) = 2.095(82) * alpha_pert(T), using T_c / Lambda_MSbar = 1.14(4).
- The ratio alpha(T)/tilde_alpha(T) from large-distance fits reaches approximately 5 close to T_c and approaches unity for T/T_c approximately 6.
- The short-distance regime reduces from r approximately 0.5 fm at T approximately T_c to r approximately 0.03 fm at T approximately 12 T_c.

## Context

This work extends zero-temperature lattice studies of the heavy quark potential and running coupling by Bali (2000) and Necco and Sommer (2001, 2002) to finite temperature, and provides a quantitative lattice test of the strongly-coupled plasma scenario proposed by Shuryak and Brown (2003). The analysis enables a systematic separation of perturbative and non-perturbative regimes in the quark-gluon plasma, with implications for understanding heavy quark bound states, thermal dilepton rates, and jet production in heavy ion collisions. The results contradict the interpretation that the plasma near T_c supports a large number of Coulombic bound states, instead emphasizing the role of surviving confining forces.
