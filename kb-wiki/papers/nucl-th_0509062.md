# Semiclassical treatment of fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We describe a semiclassical treatment of nuclear fusion reactions involving weakly bound nuclei. In this treatment, the complete fusion probabilities are approximated by products of two factors: a tunneling probability and the probability that the system is in its ground state at the strong absorption radius. We investigate the validity of the method in a schematic two-channel application, where the channels in the continuum are represented by a single resonant state. Comparisons with full coupled-channels calculations are performed. The agreement between semiclassical and quantal calculations isquite good, suggesting that the procedure may be extended to more sophisticated discretizations of the continuum.

# Semiclassical treatment of fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

## Key claim
The semiclassical Alder-Winther method, when extended by analytical continuation of the time variable into the classically forbidden region and by treating the breakup channel as a resonant state with finite width, reproduces full quantum coupled-channels calculations of complete fusion cross sections both above and below the Coulomb barrier, provided the effective channel has a nonzero width.

## Method
The authors approximate complete fusion probabilities as the product of a tunneling probability through the potential barrier and the probability that the system remains in its ground state at the strong absorption radius (the "survival probability"). They solve the Alder-Winther time-dependent coupled equations along classical trajectories, using an energy-symmetrized Rutherford trajectory for the relative motion. To handle sub-barrier energies, they analytically continue the time variable into the complex plane, allowing the classical trajectory to penetrate the classically forbidden region. The breakup continuum is represented by a single effective resonant state with a finite width, implemented by adding a constant imaginary potential \(-i\Gamma/2\) to the system Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers
- Breakup threshold energy for \(^{6}\)He: \(B = 0.975\) MeV.
- Wood-Saxon potential parameters used: \(V_0 = -50\) MeV, \(r_{0r} = 1.25\) fm, \(a_r = 0.65\) fm, \(W_0 = -50\) MeV, \(r_{0i} = 1.0\) fm, \(a_i = 0.65\) fm.
- Coulomb barrier height: \(V_B \simeq 20\) MeV.
- Typical resonance width used in the improved model: \(\Gamma = 2\) MeV.
- Cut-off radius for the imaginary potential required to be \(R_\infty \gtrsim 20\) fm for stable results at \(E_{c.m.} = 20\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the semiclassical Alder-Winther method (Alder and Winther, 1975) from bound-state problems to fusion reactions with weakly bound projectiles, following an earlier application to breakup cross sections (Alder and Winther, 1975). It addresses the failure of the standard semiclassical approach at sub-barrier energies, previously noted by Hussein et al. (2004) for a two-bound-channel problem. The method is compared to full quantum coupled-channels calculations and to the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) approach of Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002). The improved semiclassical treatment enables future calculations of incomplete fusion and sequential fusion contributions, which are not accessible in standard CDCC descriptions of fusion.
