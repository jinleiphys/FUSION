# Reconstructing the equation of state for cold nuclear matter from the relationship of any two properties of neutron stars

> A direct method is developed to reconstruct the equation of state for high-density nuclear matter from the relationship between any two properties of neutron stars, such as masses, radii, moments of inertia, baryonic masses, binding energies, gravitational redshifts, and their combinations.

## Key claim

A direct method is developed to reconstruct the equation of state for high-density nuclear matter from the relationship between any two properties of neutron stars, such as masses, radii, moments of inertia, baryonic masses, binding energies, gravitational redshifts, and their combinations. The method is pointwise exact at the grid points for finite N and converges as 1/N as the number of grid points is increased.

## Method

The reconstruction proceeds by discretizing the pressure domain into N grid points and assuming the equation of state is known up to a pressure P_i. For each step, a logarithmically linear interpolation (polytropic approximation) is used between P_i and P_{i+1}, parameterized by the unknown density ρ_{i+1}. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are integrated with central pressure P_{i+1} to compute the two observables Q^{(1)}_{i+1} and Q^{(2)}_{i+1} as functions of ρ_{i+1}, and the root ρ_{i+1} satisfying the observed relationship f(Q^{(1)}, Q^{(2)})=0 is found numerically by bisection or Newton-Raphson methods. For a finite data set, both P_{i+1} and ρ_{i+1} are determined simultaneously by a two-dimensional Newton-Raphson method.

## Key numbers

- For mass-radius pairs, desired observational accuracy is ΔM ≲ 0.1 M⊙ and ΔR ≲ 1 km.
- For I/MR² and M/R pairs, desired accuracy is Δ(I/MR²) ≲ 0.01 and Δ(M/R) ≲ 0.01.
- For B/M and M/R pairs, desired accuracy is Δ(B/M) ≲ 0.01 and Δ(M/R) ≲ 0.01.
- Current mass measurement accuracy from radio binary pulsars is ΔM ≃ 0.0007 M⊙; from X-ray binaries it is ΔM ≃ 0.4 M⊙.
- Current radius measurement accuracy is ΔR ≃ 5.2 km when interstellar absorption and distance are fixed.
- The convergence rate of the reconstruction scheme is as fast as 1/N, with the accumulated error bounded by (1/N)(log P_N − log P_0) times the supremum of the second derivative of log ρ with respect to log P.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lindblom (1992), who developed a method to reconstruct the equation of state from neutron-star masses and radii alone, by generalizing the reconstruction to any pair of observable properties. The method builds on systematic studies of neutron-star structure for various equations of state by Arnett and Bowers (1977) and Lattimer and Prakash (2000). The technique enables direct use of future multi-messenger observations, including gravitational wave measurements from coalescing neutron star/black hole binaries and X-ray observations of kHz quasiperiodic oscillations, to constrain the high-density nuclear matter equation of state.
