# The Contribution of the Light Quark Condensate to the Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term

> There has been a discrepancy between values of the pion-nucleon sigma term extracted by two different methods for many years. Analysis of recent high precision pion-nucleon data has widened the gap between the two determinations. It is argued that the two extractions correspond to different quantities and that the difference between them can be understood and calculated.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the sigma term extracted from pion-nucleon scattering (Σ_S) and from baryon mass differences (Σ_M) arises because the two methods measure different quantities; the difference equals the contribution from the light quark condensate, which is absent inside the nucleon and must be subtracted from the scattering extraction but does not appear in the mass-difference extraction.

## Method

The author defines Σ_S by subtracting the vacuum expectation value of the light quark condensate from the nucleonic matrix element, using the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation to compute the vacuum energy density. A simple "bubble" model of the nucleon as a sphere of radius R is constructed, where the pion’s effective mass is set to zero inside to represent the vanishing condensate, and the s-wave scattering amplitude is solved from the Klein-Gordon equation. The mass-difference extraction Σ_M is obtained from Gasser’s lowest-order baryon mass formulas using the quark mass ratio m_s/m_q = 25, with a higher-order correction of about 10 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum energy density from the GOR relation: 2 m_q ⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ = −μ² f_π² = −21.7 MeV/fm³, where f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- Σ_M from baryon mass differences: (m_q/(m_s − m_q)) (M_Ξ⁰ + M_Σ⁰ − 2 M_n) = 26.3 MeV, using m_s/m_q = 25; Cheng and Li obtain 25.9 MeV from a similar combination.
- Corrected Σ_M = 35 ± 5 MeV after including higher-order quark mass corrections.
- Σ_S from scattering: 79 ± 7 MeV (Pavan, using VPI/GWU analysis), with a 15 MeV correction for the finite t at the Cheng-Dashen point, giving ≈ 70 ± 7 MeV.
- Vacuum contribution δ = V μ² f_π²: estimated as 22.7 to 43.2 MeV from monopole form-factor radii (800 MeV to 1 GeV mass), 25.0 MeV from a lattice valence-quark distribution (equivalent square radius 0.65 fm), and 38 MeV from the bubble model (R ≈ 0.75 fm).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discrepancy between Σ_S and Σ_M, previously attributed to nucleon strangeness content (Donoghue, Sainio; estimates of 6–10% from strange meson clouds and neutrino reactions), which required an implausible 20% strangeness. The author argues that the difference is instead explained by the light quark condensate’s contribution to the nucleon mass, independent of internal structure. This enables a consistent interpretation of both extractions and suggests that the condensate’s existence is evidenced by the sigma-term gap; it also connects to bag model calculations (Birse, Jaffe, Lyubovitskij) where the bag constant B includes a chiral-symmetry-breaking part from the condensate.
