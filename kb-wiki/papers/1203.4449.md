# Third minima in actinides - do they exist?

> We study the existence of third, hyperdeformed minima in a number of even-even Th, U and Pu nuclei using the Woods-Saxon microscopic-macroscopic model that very well reproduces first and second minima and fission barriers in actinides. Deep ($3 ÷4$ MeV) minima found previously by Ćwiok et al. are found spurious after sufficiently general shapes are included. Shallow third wells may exist in $^{230,232}$Th, with IIIrd barriers $\le$ 200 and 330 keV (respectively). Thus, a problem of qualitative discrepancy between microscopic-macroscopic and selfconsistent predictions is resolved. Now, an understanding of experimental results on the apparent third minima in uranium becomes an issue.

## Key claim

Deep (3–4 MeV) third minima in actinides previously reported by Ćwiok et al. are spurious once sufficiently general nuclear shapes, including the dipole deformation β₁, are considered; only shallow third wells may exist in ²³⁰Th and ²³²Th, with third barriers ≤ 200 keV and 330 keV respectively.

## Method

The authors used the Woods-Saxon microscopic-macroscopic model with a deformed Woods-Saxon potential, calculating energy surfaces for even-even Th, U, and Pu nuclei. They performed two sets of axially symmetric calculations: (A) 7D maps in (β₂, β₃) with minimization over β₄–β₈, and (B) 6D hypercube calculations in β₁–β₆, followed by continuous 8D path searches in β₁–β₈ to determine upper limits on third barriers. Two macroscopic energy models were used: Yukawa-plus-exponential and the Liquid Drop with mean-curvature term (LSD).

## Key numbers

- Third barrier in ²³⁰Th: ≤ 200 keV
- Third barrier in ²³²Th: ≤ 330 keV (with an estimated upper limit of ≈ 450 keV from model tests)
- Quadrupole moments of apparent third minima: Q ≈ 120 b and 170 b (for smaller and larger octupole deformations, respectively)
- Grid step in all deformation directions: 0.05
- Range of β₁ explored: −0.35 to 0
- Range of β₂ explored: 0.55 to 1.5
- Range of β₃ explored: 0 to 0.35

## Context

This work resolves a qualitative discrepancy between microscopic-macroscopic predictions (Ćwiok et al., 2005) and self-consistent mean-field results (Bender, 2008; Gogny force calculations; SkM* Hartree-Fock plus BCS) regarding the existence of deep third minima in actinides. The findings align with self-consistent predictions that third minima are shallow or absent in U and Pu, and only possibly present in Th isotopes. The results challenge the interpretation of experimental transmission resonances from the Debrecen-Munich group (Thirolf, 2010; Csige, 2011) that suggested deep third minima in uranium isotopes, and call for beyond-mean-field collective corrections and higher-spin studies to understand the observed fission resonances.
