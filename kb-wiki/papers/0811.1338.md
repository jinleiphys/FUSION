# Modern Theory of Nuclear Forces

> Effective field theory allows for a systematic and model-independent derivation of the forces between nucleons in harmony with the symmetries of Quantum Chromodynamics. We review the foundations of this approach and discuss its application for light nuclei at various resolution scales. The extension of this approach to many-body systems is briefly sketched.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory provides a systematic and model-independent derivation of nuclear forces based on QCD's symmetries, but the approach requires a non-perturbative resummation to handle the unnaturally large S-wave scattering lengths (23.8 fm in the singlet channel and 5.4 fm in the triplet channel) that would otherwise restrict the EFT to momenta below about 8 MeV.

## Method

The authors review the construction of chiral nuclear effective field theory by exploiting the spontaneous and explicit breaking of chiral symmetry in QCD, which generates pions as Goldstone bosons and determines the longest-range parts of the nuclear force through Goldstone boson exchange. They discuss the power counting scheme that organizes contributions by powers of a low-momentum scale over a high-momentum scale (around 770 MeV, the rho meson mass), and describe how short-distance physics is encoded in low-energy constants of multi-fermion contact interactions. The review covers applications to light nuclei at various resolution scales and briefly sketches the extension to many-body systems.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton scattering length in the singlet channel: |a(^1S_0)| = 23.8 fm, much larger than the pion Compton wavelength of 1.5 fm
- Neutron-proton scattering length in the triplet channel: a(^3S_1) = 5.4 fm, much larger than 1/M_pi
- Deuteron binding momentum: gamma = sqrt(m B_D) approximately 45 MeV, with deuteron binding energy B_D = 2.224 MeV and nucleon mass m = 938.2 MeV
- Charged pion mass: M_pi = 139.57 MeV, setting the natural scale lambda_pi = 1/M_pi approximately 1.5 fm
- Physical pion decay constant: F_pi = 92.4 MeV, determined from pion decay pi to nu mu
- Hard scale related to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking: Lambda_chi approximately M_rho = 770 MeV
- Fermi momentum at nuclear matter saturation density: k_F approximately 2 M_pi

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral perturbation theory developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and the power divergence scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for handling unnaturally large scattering lengths. It contrasts with conventional phenomenological approaches such as the CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt, 2000), Nijmegen potentials (Stoks, 1994), and Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa, 1994), which lack a systematic connection to QCD and cannot assign trustworthy theoretical errors. The framework enables consistent treatment of two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces with controlled uncertainties, and connects to lattice QCD simulations such as those by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane, 2006) and the work of Aoki, Hatsuda, and Ishii (2006) on extracting nuclear potentials from lattice calculations.
