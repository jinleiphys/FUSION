# Low-lying states in $^{30}$Mg: a beyond relativistic mean-field investigation

> The recently developed model of three-dimensional angular momentum projection plus generator coordinate method on top of triaxial relativistic mean-field states has been applied to study the low-lying states of $^{30}$Mg. The effects of triaxiality on the low-energy spectra and E0 and E2 transitions are examined.

## Key claim

The inclusion of triaxial degrees of freedom in the beyond-mean-field calculations (3DAMP+GCM) brings the predicted excitation energies and E0 transition strength for \(^{30}\)Mg closer to experimental data than the axially-symmetric (1DAMP+GCM) treatment, but the agreement is not perfect; the model still overestimates the \(2^+_1\) and \(0^+_2\) energies.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations with the PC-F1 functional to generate a set of intrinsic deformed states. They then applied three-dimensional angular momentum projection (3DAMP) to restore rotational symmetry and used the generator coordinate method (GCM) to mix configurations over the quadrupole deformation parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\). The resulting wave functions were used to compute excitation energies and E0/E2 transition strengths, and the results were compared with both axially-symmetric (1DAMP+GCM) calculations and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- \(E_x(2^+_1)\) = 1.721 MeV from 3DAMP+GCM (PC-F1), compared to 1.482 MeV experimentally.
- \(E_x(0^+_2)\) = 2.864 MeV from 3DAMP+GCM (PC-F1), compared to 1.789 MeV experimentally.
- \(\rho^2_{21}(E0) \times 10^3\) = 24.72 from 3DAMP+GCM (PC-F1), compared to 26.2 ± 7.5 experimentally.
- \(B(E2;0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) = 277 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) from 3DAMP+GCM (PC-F1), compared to 241(31) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) experimentally.
- \(B(E2;0^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) = 68 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) from 3DAMP+GCM (PC-F1), compared to 53(6) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) experimentally.
- The dynamic correlation energy from rotational symmetry restoration is about 2.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the beyond-mean-field framework for relativistic density functionals by including triaxial shapes, following earlier developments of 1DAMP+GCM models with Skyrme forces (Valor 2000), Gogny forces (Guzman 2002), and relativistic functionals (Niksic 2006). It also builds on recent 3DAMP+GCM implementations with Skyrme (Bender 2008) and Gogny (Rodriguez 2010) forces. The results for \(^{30}\)Mg show that triaxiality is essential to reproduce the low-energy spectra and E0 strength, and the approach can be applied to other neutron-rich nuclei where shell erosion (e.g., the vanishing of the \(N=20\) magic number) is observed.
