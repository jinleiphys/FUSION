# Spacetime profile of electromagnetic fields in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> I numerically estimate the spacetime profile of the electromagnetic fields produced in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions at $\sqsNN=2.4\,\mathchar`-\,7.7\;{\rm GeV}$ for a wide range of impact parameters $b=0\,\mathchar`-\,9\;{\rm fm}$, using a hadronic cascade model, JAM (Jet AA Microscopic transport model). I demonstrate that (1) the produced electromagnetic fields are as strong as $eE, eB = {\mathcal O}((50\;{\rm MeV})^2)$; (2) that the produced fields extend in spacetime about $V_4 = {\mathcal O}((10\;{\rm fm})^4)$; (3) that the magnetic field dominates around the collision point, while the other broad regions of spacetime are dominated by the electric field; and (4) that a topological electromagnetic-field configuration such that ${\bm E}\cdot {\bm B} \neq 0$ is realized.

## Key claim
The electromagnetic fields produced in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions at center-of-mass energies of 2.4 to 7.7 GeV per nucleon pair reach strengths of approximately (50 MeV)^2 and extend over spacetime volumes of approximately (10 fm)^4, with the magnetic field dominating only near the collision point while the electric field dominates broader spacetime regions, and a topological configuration with non-vanishing E·B is realized.

## Method
The author used the hadronic cascade model JAM (Jet AA Microscopic transport model) with default settings to simulate gold-gold collisions at intermediate energies. The electric current at each spacetime point was calculated from the phase-space distributions of charged hadrons using relativistic Gaussian smearing with a width of 1 fm, and the electromagnetic fields were obtained from the retarded potential via numerical integration using Gauss-Legendre quadrature and center differentiation. Event averaging was performed over 100 events to obtain smooth field distributions.

## Key numbers
- Peak electromagnetic field strengths: eE, eB = O((50 MeV)^2), exceeding the QED critical field strength m_e^2 ≈ (0.5 MeV)^2
- Spacetime volume of the electromagnetic field: V_4 = O((10 fm)^4)
- For central collisions (b=0 fm), max e^2F ≈ (23 MeV)^4 × (sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV))^2
- For b=6 fm, min e^2F ≈ (29 MeV)^4 - (18 MeV)^4 × (sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV))^2
- For b=6 fm, max e^2G ≈ -min e^2G ≈ (-25 MeV)^4 + (19 MeV)^4 × (sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV))^2
- Spacetime volume of strong electric field region at b=0 fm: V_4[F > max F/4] ≈ (2.7 fm) + (27 fm) × (sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV))^{-1}
- Spacetime volume of strong magnetic field region at b=6 fm: V_4[F < min F/4] ≈ (2.9 fm) + (2.5 fm) × (sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV))^{-1}
- Spacetime volume of E·B region at b=6 fm: V_4[G > max G/4] ≈ (2.3 fm) + (15 fm) × (sqrt(s_NN)/(1 GeV))^{-1}
- Lifetime of the electromagnetic field: O(1-10 fm/c), much longer than in high-energy collisions

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Taya 2024) which focused on central collisions and the resulting electric field, by introducing finite impact parameters to study the interplay between electric and magnetic fields. It contrasts with high-energy studies at RHIC and LHC energies (Hattori 2016, Voronyuk 2011, Bzdak 2011, Deng 2012) where magnetic fields reach eB = O((0.1-1 GeV)^2) but are extremely short-lived (O(0.01-0.1 fm/c)). The results enable quantitative modeling of strong-field QED effects and electromagnetic signatures in intermediate-energy experiments such as the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, NA61/SHINE at SPS, NICA, FAIR, HIAF, and J-PARC-HI, and provide a baseline for future studies incorporating mean fields or hydrodynamic phases.
