# Work distribution of an expanding gas and transverse energy production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The work distribution of an expanding extreme relativistic gas is shown to be a gamma distribution with a different shape parameter as compared with its non-relativistic counterpart. This implies that the shape of the transverse energy distribution in relativistic heavy ion collisions depends on the particle contents during the evolution of the hot and dense matter. Therefore, transverse energy fluctuations provide additional insights into the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in these collisions.

## Key claim

The work distribution for an extreme relativistic gas undergoing adiabatic expansion or compression is a gamma distribution, but with a shape parameter of \(k=3N\) (where \(N\) is the number of particles) that is twice as large as the non-relativistic shape parameter \(k=3N/2\). This implies that the shape of the transverse energy distribution in relativistic heavy ion collisions depends on the particle contents (mass) during the evolution of the hot and dense matter, not just on the final temperature.

## Method

The authors derived the work distribution for an extreme relativistic classical gas by starting from the density of states \(\Phi(E;V)\) for a gas of massless particles in a box, using the asymptotic formula \(\Phi(E;V) = V^N E^{3N} / (\pi^{2N} N! (3N)!)\). They then computed the canonical energy distribution, used the adiabatic invariant \(\Phi(E;V)\) to relate initial and final energies via \(E_1 = (V_0/V_1)^{1/3} E_0\), and obtained the work distribution \(\rho(W)\) as a gamma distribution. The transverse energy distribution was then derived by relating the final total energy \(E_1\) to the final transverse energy \(E_{1\perp}\) via an angular integral, giving \(E_{1\perp} = (\pi/4) E_1\) for the extreme relativistic case.

## Key numbers

*   For an extreme relativistic gas, the shape parameter of the work and transverse energy gamma distribution is \(k = 3N\), where \(N\) is the number of particles.
*   For a non-relativistic gas, the shape parameter is \(k = 3N/2\).
*   For an extreme relativistic gas, the scale parameter of the work magnitude distribution is \(s = |\alpha|/\beta = |\Delta T|\), where \(\Delta T\) is the change in temperature.
*   For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV, the 0-5% centrality bin has a relative width \(\sigma/\mu = 0.116\) and \(\sigma^2/\mu = 1.60\) GeV, giving a shape parameter \(k = 74\) and a scale parameter \(s = 1.60\) GeV.
*   The relation between final total energy and final transverse energy for the extreme relativistic case is \(E_{1\perp} = (\pi/4) E_1\).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic work distribution calculation of Crooks and Jarzynski (2007) to the extreme relativistic case, which is relevant for the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in heavy ion collisions. The result that the transverse energy distribution is a gamma distribution with a shape parameter sensitive to particle mass provides a theoretical basis for interpreting experimental data from the PHENIX collaboration (Armendariz, 2007) and earlier work by Chaudhuri (1993) on gamma distributions in proton-proton collisions. The findings enable future studies of how particle production, changing particle contents, transverse expansion, and differential freeze-out modify the transverse energy distribution, and they suggest that deviations from a simple gamma distribution could signal non-equilibrium effects or the formation of a Bose-Einstein condensate.
