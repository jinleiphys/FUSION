# Relativistic mean-field study of neutron-rich nuclei

> A relativistic mean-field model is used to study the ground-state properties of neutron-rich nuclei. Nonlinear isoscalar-isovector terms, unconstrained by present day phenomenology, are added to the model Lagrangian in order to modify the poorly known density dependence of the symmetry energy. These new terms soften the symmetry energy and reshape the theoretical neutron drip line without compromising the agreement with existing ground-state information. A strong correlation between the neutron radius of 208Pb and the binding energy of valence orbitals is found: the smaller the neutron radius of 208Pb, the weaker the binding energy of the last occupied neutron orbital. Thus, models with the softest symmetry energy are the first ones to drip neutrons. Further, in anticipation of the upcoming one-percent measurement of the neutron radius of 208Pb at the Thomas Jefferson Laboratory, a close relationship between the neutron radius of 208Pb and neutron radii of elements of relevance to atomic parity-violating experiments is established.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that adding nonlinear isoscalar-isovector terms to a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian can soften the symmetry energy and reshape the theoretical neutron drip line without compromising agreement with existing ground-state data, but only for models that are already constrained to reproduce symmetric nuclear matter properties at saturation.

## Method

The authors solved coupled, nonlinear Klein-Gordon equations for the meson fields and Dirac equations for the single-particle states in a self-consistent mean-field approximation for spherically symmetric nuclei. They introduced mixed isoscalar-isovector couplings (Λ_v) into the effective Lagrangian to modify the density dependence of the symmetry energy, while keeping the symmetry energy fixed at a_sym = 25.67 MeV at k_F = 1.15 fm⁻¹. Three parameter sets (NL3, S271, Z271) were used, all constrained to the same saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 37.285 MeV (NL3), 36.637 MeV (S271), 36.298 MeV (Z271)
- Binding energy per nucleon of ²⁰⁸Pb: 7.854 MeV (NL3), 7.939 MeV (S271), 7.775 MeV (Z271); experimental value 7.885 MeV
- Charge radius of ²⁰⁸Pb: 5.509 fm (NL3), 5.509 fm (S271), 5.508 fm (Z271); experimental value 5.504 fm
- Neutron radius of ²⁰⁸Pb: 5.740 fm (NL3), 5.714 fm (S271), 5.700 fm (Z271)
- Neutron skin of ⁶⁰Ca for NL3 model: 0.608 fm (Λ_v=0), 0.567 fm (Λ_v=0.015), 0.523 fm (Λ_v=0.030)
- Binding energy of 1f⁵/² neutron in ⁶⁰Ca for NL3 model: 4.907 MeV (Λ_v=0), 4.705 MeV (Λ_v=0.015), 4.443 MeV (Λ_v=0.030)
- Projected 1% measurement of neutron radius in ²⁰⁸Pb yields ΔR_n ≈ 0.056 fm
- This translates to neutron radius uncertainties of ΔR_n(¹³⁸Ba) ≈ 0.045 fm, ΔR_n(¹⁵⁸Dy) ≈ 0.034 fm, ΔR_n(¹⁷⁶Yb) ≈ 0.052 fm

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field studies of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) that first introduced the isoscalar-isovector couplings to modify the symmetry energy, and builds on the NL3 parameter set of Lalazissis et al. (1997). The paper establishes a direct correlation between the neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb and the binding energies of valence neutrons in neutron-rich nuclei, enabling predictions for the neutron drip line that can be tested by the upcoming Parity Radius Experiment at Jefferson Laboratory. The results also connect to atomic parity-violation experiments in barium, dysprosium, and ytterbium by providing model-independent skin-skin correlations.
