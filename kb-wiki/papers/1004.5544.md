# Microscopic description of large-amplitude shape-mixing dynamics with inertial functions derived in local quasiparticle random-phase approximation

> On the basis of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method, we develop an efficient microscopic method of deriving the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian and illustrate its usefulness by applying it to the oblate-prolate shape coexistence/mixing phenomena in proton-rich 68,70,72Se. In this method, the vibrational and rotational collective masses (inertial functions) are determined by local normal modes built on constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states. Numerical calculations are carried out using the pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian including the quadrupole-pairing interaction. It is shown that the time-odd components of the moving mean-field significantly increase the vibrational and rotational collective masses in comparison with the Inglis-Belyaev cranking masses. Solving the collective Schroedinger equation, we evaluate excitation spectra, quadrupole transitions and moments. Results of the numerical calculation are in excellent agreement with recent experimental data and indicate that the low-lying states of these nuclei are characterized as an intermediate situation between the oblate-prolate shape coexistence and the so-called gamma unstable situation where large-amplitude triaxial-shape fluctuations play a dominant role.

## Key claim

The paper develops a practical microscopic method for deriving the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian using local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (LQRPA) built on constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) states, and demonstrates that the time-odd components of the moving mean field significantly increase both vibrational and rotational collective masses compared to the Inglis-Belyaev cranking masses, with the method successfully describing oblate-prolate shape coexistence and mixing in proton-rich 68,70,72Se.

## Method

The authors formulate the CHFB+LQRPA method as an approximation of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, solving local QRPA equations at each point of the (beta, gamma) deformation plane to determine vibrational and rotational inertial functions. Numerical calculations use the pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian including quadrupole-pairing interaction within two major-shell active model spaces for both neutrons and protons, with the collective Schrodinger equation solved to obtain excitation spectra, quadrupole transitions, and moments.

## Key numbers

- The two-dimensional mesh for solving CHFB+LQRPA equations consists of 3600 points in the region 0 < beta < 0.6 and 0 degrees < gamma < 60 degrees, with beta steps of 0.01 and gamma steps of 1 degree
- Effective charges of (e_n, e_p) = (0.4, 1.4) are used for calculating quadrupole transitions and moments
- The monopole-pairing interaction strengths G_0^(tau) and quadrupole-particle-hole interaction strength chi are determined such that quadrupole deformation beta and monopole-pairing gaps at oblate and prolate local minima in 68Se approximately reproduce Skyrme-HFB calculations, with mass-number dependence G_0^(tau) ~ A^{-1} and chi' ~ A^{-5/3}

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method (PTP.103.959, 2000) to a practical two-dimensional implementation for quadrupole collective dynamics, addressing the long-standing insufficiency of the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula for vibrational masses that has been noted by Baranger (1978), Dobaczewski (1981), and others. The method enables microscopic calculation of collective masses with time-odd mean-field effects included, which previous realistic calculations using the five-dimensional quadrupole Hamiltonian by Niksic (2009), Li (2009), and Girod (2009) still lacked, and it successfully describes the transitional region between oblate-prolate shape coexistence and gamma-unstable situations in proton-rich selenium isotopes.
