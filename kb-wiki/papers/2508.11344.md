# Studying the in-medium $ϕ$ meson spectrum through kaons in proton-nucleus reactions

> Exploring the mass modifications of $ϕ$ mesons in nuclei provides insights into the nature of strongly interacting matter. Specifically, $ϕ$ meson mass shifts can be related to the in-medium modification of the strange quark condensate. Therefore, the partial restoration of chiral symmetry can be studied by observing the mass shifts through the decay channels $ϕ\rightarrow e^+e^-$, and $ϕ\rightarrow K^+K^-$. In this paper, we examine the possibility of observing the $ϕ$ meson mass modifications of the $ϕ$ mesons in 30 GeV proton-nucleus (C, Cu, Pb) collisions, to be studied at the J-PARC E88 experiment, through the kaonic decay channel, with the off-shell Budapest Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BuBUU) transport model. By applying different mean fields to the kaons, we examine their effects on the invariant mass spectra. Our simulations suggest that, although different mean fields for the kaons do affect the spectrum, there is a common observable effect primarily driven by the $ϕ$ mass shift. However, due to the threshold associated with the two kaons, the signal we observe is quite different from the one expected in dilepton spectra. Therefore, to meaningfully constrain the mass shift, it will be useful to include both kaon and dilepton channels in the analysis of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper examines whether the in-medium mass shift of the phi meson can be observed through the kaonic decay channel in 30 GeV proton-nucleus collisions at J-PARC E88, finding that while different kaon mean fields affect the invariant mass spectrum, a common observable effect driven by the phi mass shift exists, but the signal differs significantly from dilepton spectra due to the two-kaon threshold, so both kaon and dilepton channels should be used together to meaningfully constrain the mass shift.

## Method

The authors used the off-shell Budapest Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BuBUU) transport model to simulate 30 GeV proton collisions on carbon, copper, and lead targets, treating the phi mesons as off-shell particles with a density-dependent mass shift parameter. Four different mean field models for the kaons (M1 through M4) were applied, including a zero-potential reference, symmetric potentials, a momentum-dependent fit from Sibirtsev (1998), and a self-consistent chiral unitary approach from Tolos (2006, 2008), with elastic and inelastic scatterings and K- absorption included in the realistic simulations.

## Key numbers

- The phi meson mass shift parameter was set to Delta m0 = -34 MeV at normal nuclear density, based on the KEK-E325 experiment (KEK-PS-E325:2005wbm)
- Normal nuclear density used in the simulations is rho0 = 0.168 fm^-3
- The phi to K+K- branching ratio is 0.491, the phi to K0_L K0_S branching ratio is 0.339, and the phi to 3pi branching ratio is 0.154
- The phi N elastic cross section parametrization uses a = 10 mb and b = 10 mb*GeV/c, while the absorption cross section uses a = 5 mb and b = 4.5 mb*GeV/c
- The M3 kaon mean field parameters include a2 = 0.167 GeV*fm^3 for K+, and for K-: b3 = 0.341 GeV*fm^3, c3 = 0.823 GeV*fm^3, d3 = 2.5 GeV^-1

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phi meson spectral modification at 12 GeV bombarding energies from the KEK-E325 experiment (KEK-PS-E325:2005wbm), where dilepton data suggested a small negative mass shift but with insufficient statistical significance, and where the K+K- data interpretation was not clear (E325:2006ioe). The simulations use the BuBUU transport code previously applied to charmonium mass shifts in antiproton-induced reactions (Wolf 2018), and complement studies using the Parton Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) code for phi meson dilepton decays (Gubler 2024, PSE325 2025). The results enable planning for the J-PARC E88 experiment by showing that both kaon and dilepton channels are needed to constrain the phi mass shift, and that target size significantly affects the invariant mass spectrum shape.
