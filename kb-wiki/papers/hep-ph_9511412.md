# Low-energy QCD

> After a brief introduction to chiral perturbation theory, the effective field theory of the standard model at low energies, two recent applications are reviewed: elastic pion-pion scattering to two-loop accuracy and the complete renormalized pion-nucleon Lagrangian to $O(p^3)$ in the chiral expansion.

# Low-Energy QCD

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) provides a systematic quantum field theory framework for low-energy QCD, but its predictive power at higher orders is limited by a rapidly growing number of low-energy constants (LECs) that are not constrained by symmetries; additional phenomenological or theoretical input is required to make predictions, especially beyond leading order.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of the effective chiral Lagrangian of the standard model, organized by chiral dimension and loop order, and presents two recent applications: elastic pion-pion scattering calculated to two-loop accuracy (O(p^6)) using standard CHPT, and the complete renormalized pion-nucleon Lagrangian to O(p^3) derived via Heavy Baryon CHPT (HBCHPT), where the nucleon mass is transferred from propagators to vertices using velocity-dependent fields.

## Key numbers

- The I=0 S-wave pion-pion scattering length a_0^0 is predicted to be 0.156 at O(p^2), 0.201 at O(p^4), and 0.217 at O(p^6) (using F_pi = 93.2 MeV and the charged pion mass).
- The natural expansion parameter for SU(2) chiral perturbation theory near threshold is 4 M_pi^2 / (16 pi^2 F_pi^2) = 0.06.
- The O(p^4) prediction for a_0^0 is 0.20 +/- 0.01, compared to the experimental value 0.26 +/- 0.05 from Nagels et al. (1979).
- The O(p^6) prediction for a_0^0 increases from 0.217 to 0.222 when using the radiatively corrected pion decay constant F_pi = 92.4 MeV instead of 93.2 MeV.
- The lowest-order expression for a_0^0 using the charged pion mass gives 0.156, while using the neutral pion mass gives 0.146.
- The complete renormalized pion-nucleon Lagrangian at O(p^3) contains 24 new LECs b_i, in addition to the 7 LECs a_i at O(p^2).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on chiral perturbation theory by Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984, 1985) and the relativistic pion-nucleon framework by Gasser et al. (1988). The two-loop pion-pion scattering calculation was performed independently by Knecht et al. (1995c) using dispersion relations and by Bijnens et al. (1995) using standard field theory, with both groups agreeing on the absorptive parts. The O(p^3) pion-nucleon Lagrangian enables systematic calculations of single-nucleon processes including Compton scattering, pion photoproduction, and nucleon form factors, and provides the foundation for testing the standard mechanism of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking through the quark condensate against forthcoming precision data from DAΦNE.
