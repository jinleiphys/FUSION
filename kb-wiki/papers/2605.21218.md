# The quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A$ in $β$-decay: joint effects from chiral two-body currents and many-body correlations

> In nuclear $β$-decay calculations, the axial-vector coupling constant $g_A \approx 1.27$ usually needs to be quenched phenomenologically by a factor $q~\approx$ 0.75 to reproduce {the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strengths}. We propose a novel approach to quench the GT {strength} of $β$-decay within the microscopic random phase approximation (RPA) plus particle-vibration coupling (PVC) approach, incorporating the contributions of two-body currents (TBC) derived from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). Self-consistent RPA+PVC calculations are performed in three doubly magic nuclei, $^{56}$Ni, $^{100}$Sn, and $^{132}$Sn, with various Skyrme energy density functionals, and the effect of TBC is evaluated by using the obtained many-body wavefunctions. A combined effects of the many-body correlations introduced by PVC and chiral TBC quench the GT strength and reproduce quantitatively experimental data without any additional adjustments. The extracted quenching factors $q$ by the present microscopic model lie in the range $\approx$ 0.73--0.80, which is quite close to the commonly adopted empirical value of $q \approx 0.75$.

## Key claim
The phenomenological quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) in Gamow-Teller \(\beta\)-decay can be explained microscopically by combining particle-vibration coupling (PVC) many-body correlations with two-body currents (TBC) from chiral effective field theory, without any additional adjustable parameters. The extracted quenching factors \(q\) lie in the range \(\approx 0.73\)–\(0.80\), close to the commonly adopted empirical value of \(q \approx 0.75\), but the authors note this range is obtained from calculations in only three doubly magic nuclei.

## Method
Self-consistent random phase approximation plus particle-vibration coupling (RPA+PVC) calculations were performed for the Gamow-Teller strength in the doubly magic nuclei \(^{56}\)Ni, \(^{100}\)Sn, and \(^{132}\)Sn using various Skyrme energy density functionals. The two-body current contributions derived from chiral effective field theory were evaluated using the many-body wavefunctions obtained from the RPA+PVC approach, with the transition matrix elements expanded in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis and transformed to Jacobi coordinates via Moshinsky brackets.

## Key numbers
- Quenching factors \(q\) extracted from the microscopic model lie in the range \(\approx 0.73\)–\(0.80\), compared to the empirical value \(q \approx 0.75\).
- For \(^{100}\)Sn, the dominant particle-hole configuration \([\pi1g_{9/2}^{-1},\nu1g_{7/2}]\) contributes a one-body current matrix element (Term 1) of \(2.340 \times 10^{0}\) and a two-body current matrix element (Term 3) of \(-5.229 \times 10^{-1}\) for the \(1^+\) state at 4.451 MeV.
- When only the short-range \(\bar{c}_D\) term of the two-body current is included, the adjusted \(\bar{c}_D\) parameters needed to reproduce experimental B(GT) values are \(-6.4\) for \(^{56}\)Ni, \(-9.3\) for \(^{100}\)Sn, and \(-23.1\) for \(^{132}\)Sn.
- Convergence of the calculated B(GT) values requires 6 to 8 oscillator shells in the harmonic oscillator basis expansion for these doubly magic nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the microscopic understanding of the long-standing \(g_A\) quenching problem in nuclear \(\beta\)-decay, which previously required phenomenological reduction factors of about 0.75 to reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller strengths. The approach combines the RPA+PVC framework (which incorporates many-body correlations beyond simple RPA) with chiral two-body currents, building on earlier work that considered these effects separately. The results enable future calculations that may eliminate the need for phenomenological quenching factors in nuclear structure and weak interaction studies, though the authors note the current calculations are limited to doubly magic nuclei and further work is needed for open-shell systems.
