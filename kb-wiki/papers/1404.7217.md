# Normal or abnormal isospin-fractionation as a qualitative probe of nuclear symmetry energy at supradensities

> Within two different frameworks of isospin-dependent transport model, effect of nuclear symmetry energy at supradensities on the isospin-fractionation (IsoF) was investigated. With positive/negative symmetry potential at supradensities (i.e., values of symmetry energy increase/decrease with density above saturation density), for energetic nucleons, the value of neutron to proton ratio of free nucleons is larger/smaller than that of bound nucleon fragments. Compared with extensively studied quantitative observables of nuclear symmetry energy, the normal or abnormal isospin-fractionation of energetic nucleons can be a qualitative probe of nuclear symmetry energy at supradensities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the normal or abnormal isospin-fractionation (IsoF) of energetic nucleons can serve as a qualitative probe of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, specifically whether the symmetry energy is stiff (increasing with density) or soft (decreasing with density). The hedge is that this is a qualitative, not quantitative, observable, and it is intended as a first-step diagnostic to distinguish between stiff and soft behavior before more precise constraints are applied.

## Method

The authors used two different isospin-dependent transport models, IBUU04 and UrQMD, to simulate central \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. They implemented positive (stiff) and negative (soft) symmetry potentials at supra-saturation densities by varying parameters (x = -1.2 and x = 1 in IBUU04; γ = 1.5 and a = 1.6 in UrQMD) and then compared the neutron-to-proton (n/p) ratio of free nucleons (A=1, gas phase) versus bound nucleons in fragments (A>1, liquid phase) as a function of nucleon kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 400 MeV/nucleon for central \({}^{197}\)Au+\({}^{197}\)Au collisions.
- In IBUU04, parameters x = -1.2 (positive/stiff symmetry potential) and x = 1 (negative/soft symmetry potential) were used.
- In UrQMD, parameters γ = 1.5 (positive/stiff) and a = 1.6 (negative/soft) were used.
- The local density threshold for gas phase was set at ρ ≤ ρ₀/10, with ρ₀ the normal nuclear density.
- With the positive (stiff) symmetry potential, the n/p ratio of free nucleons was larger than that of bound nucleons across the entire kinetic energy range.
- With the negative (soft) symmetry potential, the n/p ratio of free nucleons became smaller than that of bound nucleons at higher kinetic energies, indicating abnormal IsoF.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isospin-fractionation during nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions in dilute asymmetric nuclear matter (e.g., Muller (1995), Li (2000), Baran (2005)). It directly addresses the divergent constraints on the high-density symmetry energy obtained from FOPI and FOPI-LAND data by different transport models, such as those by Xiao (2009), Feng (2010), Russotto (2011), Xie (2013), and Cozma (2013). The proposed qualitative probe is intended to bypass the model-dependent uncertainties of quantitative observables like the π⁻/π⁺ ratio, and it enables future experimental measurements (e.g., by the FOPI-LAND Collaboration) to qualitatively determine whether the symmetry energy is stiff or soft at supra-saturation densities.
