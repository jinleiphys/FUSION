# $^{35,37,39}$S isotopes in $sd-pf$ space : Shell-model interpretation

> The structure of $^{35,37,39}$S isotopes is described by performing comprehensive shell model calculations with SDPF-U and SDPFMW interactions. Protons and neutrons are restricted to the $sd$-shell for $N < 20$, neutrons start to fill the $pf$-shell for $N > 20$. Natural parity states are described by only in-shell mixing, unnatural parity states with 1p-1h inter-shell neutron excitations. With SDPF-U interaction, reported are the results for natural parity states only because this interaction is not suitable for cross shell excitations. Calculated energy levels, electromagnetic properties and spectroscopic factors are in good agreement with the recently available experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that comprehensive shell-model calculations using the SDPF-U and SDPFMW interactions can successfully describe the low-energy structure of odd sulfur isotopes $^{35,37,39}$S, but this success is conditional: the SDPF-U interaction is only suitable for natural parity states (0p-0h configurations) and cannot describe cross-shell excitations, so unnatural parity states require the SDPFMW interaction with 1p-1h neutron excitations and removal of spurious center-of-mass components.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the $sd$-$pf$ valence space (orbitals $0d_{5/2}$, $1s_{1/2}$, $0d_{3/2}$, $0f_{7/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$) using the SDPF-U and SDPFMW effective interactions with the NuShell and NuShellX codes. For $N<20$, protons and neutrons were restricted to the $sd$-shell; for $N>20$, neutrons filled the $pf$-shell. Natural parity states were described by 0p-0h in-shell mixing, while unnatural parity states required 1p-1h inter-shell neutron excitations, with spurious center-of-mass components removed via the Gloeckner and Lawson method using $\beta_{\rm cm}=10$.

## Key numbers

- For $^{35}$S, the SDPFMW interaction predicts the $1/2_1^+$ state at 1558 keV, compared to the PSDPF interaction prediction of 1739 keV and the experimental value.
- For $^{35}$S, the $B(M2; 7/2_1^- \rightarrow 3/2_1^+)$ transition is 3.4 $\mu_N^2fm^2$ with free $g_s$, and reduces to 1.7 $\mu_N^2fm^2$ using effective $g$ factors ($g_{\nu s}^{\rm eff} = -2.869$, $g_{\nu l}^{\rm eff} = -0.1$, $g_{\pi s}^{\rm eff} = 4.189$, $g_{\pi l}^{\rm eff} = 1.1$), compared to the experimental value of 1.6(5) $\mu_N^2fm^2$.
- For $^{35}$S, the $B(E3; 7/2_1^- \rightarrow 3/2_1^+)$ is 26.64 $e^2fm^6$ with effective charges $e_{\rm eff}^\pi=1.5e$, $e_{\rm eff}^\nu=0.5e$, compared to the experimental value of 115(86) $e^2fm^6$.
- For $^{37}$S, the SDPFMW interaction predicts the $11/2^-$ state at 2888 keV, with a wave function 87.6% dominated by the $\pi(d_{5/2}^6s_{1/2}^1d_{3/2}^1) \otimes \nu(d_{5/2}^6s_{1/2}^2d_{3/2}^4f_{7/2}^1)$ configuration.
- For $^{39}$S, the SDPFMW interaction predicts the $3/2_1^+$ level at 1036 keV, while the experimental value is 864 keV.
- For $^{39}$S, the $11/2^-$ state is predicted at 1698 keV, with the largest wave function component (53%) being $I_p=0^+ \otimes I_n=11/2^-$.
- For $^{35}$S, the quadrupole moment of the $3/2_1^+$ state is +0.053 eb (SDPFMW) compared to the experimental value +0.0471(9) eb.
- For $^{37}$S, the spectroscopic factor for the $7/2_1^-$ state is 0.91 (SDPFMW) compared to the experimental value 0.77.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model studies of sulfur isotopes by Aydin (2011), Chapman (2011), and Nowacki (2009), which used the SDPF-U interaction but were limited to natural parity states. The present paper provides the first shell-model results for positive-parity states in $^{37}$S and $^{39}$S using the SDPFMW interaction, which allows simultaneous description of both parities. The calculations enable future experimental planning by predicting quadrupole and magnetic moments for low-lying states in $^{37,39}$S that are not yet measured, and they contribute to understanding the erosion of the $N=20$ and $N=28$ shell gaps and the development of collectivity in neutron-rich sulfur isotopes.
