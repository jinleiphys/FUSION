# Neutrinos at FPF

> The GiBUU model is used to obtain information on possible neutrino-nucleus events at the proposed Forward Physics Facility (FPF) at CERN. An FPF neutrino program could contribute to fundamental questions such as formation times, color transparency and the EMC effect for neutrinos.

## Key claim

The GiBUU model predicts that for 1 TeV muon neutrinos on a tungsten target, final-state interactions (FSI) dramatically alter baryon multiplicity and energy distributions via an "avalanche effect," while pion multiplicities remain largely unchanged; this opens a new kinematic regime to study formation times, color transparency, and the EMC effect for neutrinos.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport-theory framework, which incorporates quantum-kinetic transport for final-state interactions and PYTHIA for deep inelastic scattering (DIS), to simulate 1 TeV muon neutrinos incident on a tungsten target. They compared inclusive invariant mass, four-momentum transfer, energy-transfer distributions, and final-state baryon and pion multiplicities both with and without FSI.

## Key numbers

- Invariant mass distribution peaks at about W = 30 GeV for 1 TeV neutrinos on tungsten.
- Energy-transfer distribution per nucleon decreases from about 0.8 × 10⁻³⁸ cm²/GeV at low ω to about 0.6 × 10⁻³⁸ cm²/GeV at 1 TeV.
- Baryon multiplicity before FSI peaks at multiplicity = 1 with a tail up to about 3–4; after FSI, the peak height decreases by about a factor of 5 and a tail reaches up to about 25.
- Proton kinetic energy distribution before FSI peaks at about 0.3 GeV; after FSI, cross sections decrease at T_p > 2 GeV and increase at T_p < 1.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model's application to the proposed Forward Physics Facility (FPF) at CERN, building on earlier analyses of HERMES and EMC electron data by Gallmeister (2007) that showed hadronic FSI cross sections increase linearly with time. The results enable future FPF experiments to validate formation-time models, test color transparency in DIS-dominated events (contrasting with the unexpected JLAB result from Hall C (2020) that CT for baryons does not set in up to Q² = 14 GeV²), and resolve the tension between electron and neutrino EMC effects reported by Sajjad Athar (2020).
