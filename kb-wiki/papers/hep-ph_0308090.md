# Pairing of Parafermions of Order 2: Seniority Model

> As generalizations of the fermion seniority model, four multi-mode Hamiltonians are considered to investigate some of the consequences of the pairing of parafermions of order two. 2-particle and 4-particle states are explicitly constructed for H_A = - G A^+ A with A^+}= 1/2 Sum c_{m}^+ c_{-m}^+ and the distinct H_C = - G C^+ C with C^+}= 1/2 Sum c_{-m}^+ c_{m}^+, and for the time-reversal invariant H_(-)= -G (A^+ - C^+)(A-C) and H_(+) = -G (A^+dagger + C^+)(A+C), which has no analogue in the fermion case. The spectra and degeneracies are compared with those of the usual fermion seniority model.

## Key claim

The paper constructs and analyzes four multi-mode Hamiltonians for parafermions of order 2 as generalizations of the fermion seniority model, finding that the spectra for $H_A$, $H_C$, and $H_{(-)}$ match the form of the usual $p=1$ seniority model spectrum, but with the key qualifier that for $H_{(-)}$ the parameter $\Omega_{p=1}$ is replaced by $2\Omega$ and many additional degeneracies appear beyond those of the fermion case.

## Method

The author explicitly constructs 2-particle and 4-particle states for four Hamiltonians: $H_A = -G A^\dagger A$, $H_C = -G C^\dagger C$, $H_{(-)} = -G (A^\dagger - C^\dagger)(A-C)$, and $H_{(+)} = -G (A^\dagger + C^\dagger)(A+C)$, using parafermi operators of order 2 with the tri-linear commutation relations. The analysis employs quasi-spin algebraic structures for the two-body pairing operators $A$, $C$, $B_i$, and $D_i$, and constructs orthonormal N-particle states with various seniorities $s=0,2,4$ for $\Omega$ modes, with explicit calculations for $\Omega=4$ in the 4-particle case.

## Key numbers

- For $H_A$ and $H_C$, the $s=0$ N-particle energy eigenvalue is $E_0^{(A)}(N) = -\frac{1}{4} G N (2\Omega - N + 2)$.
- For $H_A$ and $H_C$, the $s=2$ N-particle energy eigenvalue is $E_2^{(A)}(N) = -\frac{1}{4} G (N-2) (2\Omega - N)$.
- For $H_A$ and $H_C$, the $s=4$ N-particle energy eigenvalue is $E_4^{(A)}(N) = -\frac{1}{4} G (N-4) (2\Omega - N - 2)$.
- For $H_{(-)}$, the $s=0$ state $(A^\dagger - C^\dagger)^{N/2}|0\rangle$ has energy $E_0^{(-)}(N) = -\frac{1}{4} G N (4\Omega - N + 2)$.
- For $H_{(-)}$, the 2-particle $s=0$ state $|2^-\rangle_0 = (A^\dagger - C^\dagger)|0\rangle$ has energy $E_0^{(-)}(2) = -2G\Omega$ with normalization ${N}_0(2) = 2\Omega$.
- For $H_{(-)}$, the 2-particle $s=0$ state $|\breve{2}^-\rangle_0 = (A^\dagger + C^\dagger)|0\rangle$ has energy $E_0^{(-)}(\breve{2}) = 0$ with normalization ${N}_0(\breve{2}) = 2\Omega$.
- For $H_{(-)}$, the 4-particle $s=0$ state $|4^-\rangle_0 = (A^\dagger - C^\dagger)^2|0\rangle$ has energy $E_0^{(-)}(4) = -2G(2\Omega - 1)$ with normalization ${N}_0(4) = 2\Omega(4\Omega - 1)$.
- For $H_{(-)}$, the 4-particle $s=0$ state $|\breve{4}^-\rangle_0 = (A^\dagger - C^\dagger)(A^\dagger + C^\dagger)|0\rangle$ has energy $E_0^{(-)}(\breve{4}) = -2G(\Omega - 1)$ with normalization ${N}_0(\breve{4}) = 2(\Omega)^2$.
- For $H_{(-)}$, the 4-particle $s=2$ states $|4_i^-\rangle_2$ and $|\breve{4}_i^-\rangle_2$ have energy $E_2^{(-)}(4) = -2G(2\Omega - 2)$ with normalization ${N}_2(4) = 4(\Omega - 1)i(i+1)$.
- For $H_{(-)}$ with $\Omega = 4$, the $s=4$ 4-particle states have zero energy eigenvalues and normalization constant ${N}_4(4) = 144$.

## Context

This work extends the fermion seniority model (Kerman, 1961; known as the quasi-spin formalism) to parafermions of order 2, where the maximum number of identical particles in a symmetric state is 2 rather than 1. The paper generalizes the pairing Hamiltonians studied in nuclear physics and condensed matter contexts, and introduces time-reversal-invariant Hamiltonians $H_{(-)}$ and $H_{(+)}$ that have no analogue in the fermion case. The results enable further investigation of parafermion pairing phenomena and spontaneous symmetry breaking in multi-mode systems, though the author notes that inclusion of kinetic energy terms and perturbations remains an open challenge.
