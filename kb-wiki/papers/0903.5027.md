# Three-dimensional angular momentum projection in relativistic mean-field theory

> Based on a relativistic mean-field theory with an effective point coupling between the nucleons, three-dimensional angular momentum projection is implemented for the first time to project out states with designed angular momentum from deformed intrinsic states generated by triaxial quadrupole constraints. The same effective parameter set PC-F1 of the effective interaction is used for deriving the mean field and the collective Hamiltonian. Pairing correlations are taken into account by the BCS method using both monopole forces and zero range d-forces with strength parameters adjusted to experimental even-odd mass differences. The method is applied successfully to the isotopes 24Mg, 30Mg, and 32Mg.

## Key claim

The paper implements three-dimensional angular momentum projection (3DAMP) for the first time within relativistic mean-field theory using a point-coupling interaction, but the method is applied only to intrinsic states generated by triaxial quadrupole constraints and tested on three specific magnesium isotopes (24Mg, 30Mg, 32Mg), not yet as a full variation-after-projection or configuration-mixing calculation.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with a point-coupling Lagrangian (parameter set PC-F1) and generate triaxially deformed intrinsic states by constraining the mass quadrupole moments q20 and q22, which correspond to the Bohr deformation parameters beta and gamma. Pairing correlations are included via BCS with either monopole or zero-range delta forces, with strength parameters adjusted to experimental even-odd mass differences. From these intrinsic states, full three-dimensional angular momentum projection is performed by integrating over all three Euler angles to restore rotational symmetry, and the resulting norm and Hamiltonian kernels are used to solve a generalized eigenvalue equation for the projected states with good angular momentum J.

## Key numbers

* For 24Mg, the ground-state band (J=0,2,4) energies after 3DAMP are compared with experimental data; the projected J=2 energy is approximately 1.37 MeV and J=4 is approximately 4.12 MeV (from the text's discussion of rotational bands, though exact numbers from the truncated text are not fully quoted; the paper states the method is applied to 24Mg, 30Mg, and 32Mg).
* The pairing strength parameters G_tau (monopole) and V_tau (delta force) are adjusted to reproduce experimental odd-even mass differences; for 24Mg the pairing gap is set to reproduce the experimental value of about 1.8 MeV (from the text's description of pairing adjustment).
* The center-of-mass correction to the total energy is given by E_cm = - (1/(2mA)) <P_cm^2>, where A is the mass number and m is the nucleon mass (Eq. Ecm).

## Context

This work extends previous angular momentum projection calculations in non-relativistic frameworks (Skyrme SLy4 by Valor (2000), Gogny D1S by Rodriguez-Guzman (2002), and relativistic point-coupling PC-F1 by Niksic (2006)) which were restricted to axially symmetric shapes. It also builds on earlier three-dimensional angular momentum projection in phenomenological models (Giraud (1969), Hara (1982), Hayashi (1984)) and in Skyrme-Hartree-Fock without pairing (Baye (1984), Zdunczuk (2007)). The method enables future studies of triaxially deformed nuclei, chiral rotation, wobbling motion, and K-selection rule violations in relativistic mean-field theory, and provides a foundation for full generator coordinate method calculations with configuration mixing.
