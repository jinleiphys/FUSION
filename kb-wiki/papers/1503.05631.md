# Different mechanism of two-proton emission from proton-rich nuclei $^{23}$Al and $^{22}$Mg

> Two-proton relative momentum ($q_{pp}$) and opening angle ($θ_{pp}$) distributions from the three-body decay of two excited proton-rich nuclei, namely $^{23}$Al $\rightarrow$ p + p + $^{21}$Na and $^{22}$Mg $\rightarrow$ p + p + $^{20}$Ne, have been measured with the projectile fragment separator (RIPS) at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory. An evident peak at $q_{pp}\sim20$ MeV/c as well as a peak in $θ_{pp}$ around 30$^\circ$ are seen in the two-proton break-up channel from a highly-excited $^{22}$Mg. In contrast, such peaks are absent for the $^{23}$Al case. It is concluded that the two-proton emission mechanism of excited $^{22}$Mg is quite different from the $^{23}$Al case, with the former having a favorable diproton emission component at a highly excited state and the latter dominated by the sequential decay process.

## Key claim

The two-proton emission mechanism differs between the odd-Z nucleus 23Al and the even-Z nucleus 22Mg, with 22Mg showing a clear diproton emission component in a specific excitation energy window (12.5 < E* < 18 MeV) while 23Al is dominated by sequential decay in the window 10.5 < E* < 15 MeV; the diproton component is only observed for 22Mg and not for 23Al under the selected conditions.

## Method

The experiment used the RIPS beamline at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory with secondary beams of 23Al (57.4A MeV) and 22Mg (53.5A MeV) produced by projectile fragmentation of a 135A MeV 28Si primary beam on a 9Be target, then directed onto a 12C reaction target. The three-body decay channels p + p + 21Na and p + p + 20Ne were identified using a combination of the DALI2 gamma detector array, five layers of silicon detectors (including Si-strip detectors for angle measurement), and three layers of plastic hodoscopes for proton detection. Excitation energies were reconstructed via the invariant mass method, and relative momentum (q_pp) and opening angle (theta_pp) distributions were analyzed in selected excitation energy windows.

## Key numbers

- The diproton emission component in 22Mg contributes approximately 30% of the decay events in the excitation energy window 12.5 < E* < 18 MeV.
- A clear peak in the two-proton relative momentum distribution is observed at q_pp ~ 20 MeV/c for 22Mg in the 12.5 < E* < 18 MeV window.
- A peak in the opening angle distribution is observed at theta_pp ~ 30 degrees for 22Mg in the same excitation energy window.
- The resolution for reconstructed excitation energy is estimated to be ~1 MeV.
- A resolution better than 5 MeV/c for the relative momentum of protons at typical energy of 65 MeV was achieved.
- For the 23Al -> p + p + 20Ne channel, a 10% fraction of diproton emission reproduces the data well.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-proton emission from proton-rich nuclei, particularly the diproton emission observed in 17Ne (Zerguerras et al., 2004) with ~70% diproton contribution and in 18Ne (Raciti et al., 2008) with ~30% diproton contribution from the 6.15 MeV state. The results confirm the existence of diproton emission from the 14.044 MeV T=2 analog state in 22Mg, which was previously suggested by Cable et al. (1984) but without distinguishing between diproton and sequential emission. The finding that odd-Z 23Al shows no diproton component while even-Z 22Mg does supports the theoretical expectation that diproton emission is favored in even-Z nuclei due to pairing correlations. This work enables further studies of nucleon-nucleon pair correlations in exotic nuclei and has implications for astrophysical reaction rates involving 21Na(p,gamma)22Mg and 18Ne(alpha,p)21Na in explosive stellar scenarios.
