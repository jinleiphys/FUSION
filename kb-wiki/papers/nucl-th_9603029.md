# Time development of a density perturbation in the unstable nuclear matter

> We present the solution of the time development of an unstable initial density perturbation in the linearized Vlasov equation, completing the previous analysis in the literature. The additional contributions found are usually damped and can be neglected at large times in the unstable region. The work clarifies also the problem of the normalization of the solution with respect to the initial perturbation of the density.

## Key claim

The paper completes the previous analysis of the time development of an unstable initial density perturbation in the linearized Vlasov equation by showing that, in addition to the exponentially growing and damped pole contributions, a cut contribution exists on the real frequency axis; this cut contribution is usually damped and can be neglected at large times in the unstable region, but is required to recover the correct initial density perturbation at zero time.

## Method

The author solves the linearized Vlasov equation for nuclear matter in the spinodal (unstable) region using a one-sided Fourier transform in time. The inverse Fourier transform integral is evaluated by closing the contour in the lower half-plane, yielding contributions from the poles of the inverse susceptibility (giving the growing and damped modes) and from the cut on the real frequency axis arising from the logarithmic branch cut in the susceptibility function. The cut contribution is computed explicitly for two types of initial momentum-space perturbations: a delta-function in momentum and a deformation of the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- The overlap of the initial density perturbation with the eigenfunctions of the Vlasov equation is greater than 1, with limits of 1 and 2 in the cases of strong and weak instability respectively (Fig. 2).
- For the delta-function momentum perturbation at zero temperature, the cut contribution to the density is oscillating and undamped; at temperature T = epsilon_f/2, the cut contribution becomes smooth and damped (Fig. 1).
- For the Fermi-surface deformation initial condition, the cut contribution is rapidly damped in time (Fig. 3).
- The time scales of the cut contribution scale as 1/kv_f, the same scaling as the growth rate Gamma(k) approximately kv_f for constant F_0.
- The cut contribution is important at small times and can be neglected at large times where the unstable mode dominates (Fig. 4).

## Context

This work extends the linear response analysis of spinodal decomposition in nuclear matter, which was first proposed by Heiselberg et al. as a mechanism for multifragmentation in intermediate energy nuclear collisions. The paper clarifies an incompleteness in prior analyses by P1 and by references C1 and C2, which either discussed only the large-time limit or used discrete momentum bases that required large complete bases to capture the damping. The results enable a more accurate treatment of the initial condition in stochastic transport equations (Boltzmann-Langevin equation) and show that the overlap of the initial perturbation with unstable eigenfunctions modifies the simple prediction that the most unstable modes are preferentially excited, weakening that prediction for modes with large growth rates.
