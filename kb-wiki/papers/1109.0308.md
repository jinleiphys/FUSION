# Excited-State Hadron Masses from Lattice QCD

> Progress in computing the spectrum of excited baryons and mesons in lattice QCD is described. Large sets of spatially-extended hadron operators are used. The need for multi-hadron operators in addition to single-hadron operators is emphasized, necessitating the use of a new stochastic method of treating the low-lying modes of quark propagation which exploits Laplacian Heaviside quark-field smearing. A new glueball operator is tested, and computing the mixing of this glueball operator with a quark-antiquark operator and multiple two-pion operators is shown to be feasible.

# Excited-State Hadron Masses from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
The stochastic LapH method enables accurate estimation of temporal correlations involving both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators needed for extracting excited-state hadron masses from lattice QCD, but the results presented for the isovector mass spectrum are preliminary because only single-hadron operators were used and extractions in regions where multi-hadron thresholds lie could be complicated by "false plateaux."

## Method
The authors computed finite-volume stationary-state energies of QCD using Markov-chain Monte Carlo integration of QCD path integrals on a space-time lattice. They estimated matrices of temporal correlations of sets of single-hadron and multi-hadron operators, then diagonalized these correlation matrices to extract energy eigenvalues. A new stochastic method called stochastic LapH (Laplacian Heaviside quark-field smearing) was introduced to handle the computationally demanding quark propagation from all spatial sites on one time slice to all spatial sites on another, as well as sink-to-sink propagation for many sink times.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass of approximately 390 MeV used for the isovector mass spectrum results on a 24^3 x 128 anisotropic lattice with 170 gauge configurations
- Pion mass of approximately 240 MeV used for the rho-pi-pi mixing study on 584 configurations of the 24^3 x 128 lattice
- Pion mass of approximately 390 MeV used for the scalar isoscalar 4x4 correlator study on 100 configurations of a 16^3 x 128 lattice
- A 2x2 correlation matrix was evaluated involving a single-site rho-meson operator and a total isospin I=1 pi-pi operator in a P-wave
- A 4x4 correlator matrix was evaluated involving a scalar isoscalar single-site quark-antiquark meson operator, the new glueball operator G_Delta, and two I=0 pi-pi operators in an S wave

## Context
This work extends a series of papers by the same collaboration (Morningstar et al., 2005-2010) on computing baryon and meson spectra using lattice QCD. The new stochastic LapH method addresses the challenge that as the pion mass is lowered in simulations, more multi-hadron states lie below the masses of excited resonances, making multi-hadron operators essential. The results demonstrate feasibility for future full calculations of the QCD stationary-state energy spectrum including mixing between glueball, quark-antiquark, and multi-pion operators, which is necessary for reliable extraction of resonance masses and widths from finite-volume spectra.
