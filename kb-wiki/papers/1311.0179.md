# Inhomogeneous phases in one-dimensional mass- and spin-imbalanced Fermi gases

> We compute the phase diagram of strongly interacting fermions in one dimension at finite temperature, with mass and spin imbalance. By including the possibility of the existence of a spatially inhomogeneous ground state, we find regions where spatially varying superfluid phases are favored over homogeneous phases. We obtain estimates for critical values of the temperature, mass and spin imbalance, above which these phases disappear. Finally, we show that an intriguing relation exists between the general structure of the phase diagram and the binding energies of the underlying two-body bound-state problem.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spatially inhomogeneous (FFLO-type) superfluid phases occupy a considerable region of the phase diagram of one-dimensional mass- and spin-imbalanced Fermi gases at finite temperature, but the authors acknowledge that their mean-field approach is at best qualitative for strict one-dimensional systems and is justified only by assuming weak but finite inter-tube coupling in quasi-one-dimensional experimental realizations.

## Method

The authors employed a mean-field approximation to the path-integral representation of the Hamiltonian for two fermion species interacting via a zero-range two-body interaction. They introduced an auxiliary bosonic field via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, integrated out the fermions, and minimized the resulting effective action numerically by expanding the order-parameter field in plane waves (up to l_max=5) and the effective action in n-point functions (up to n_max=2). The phase diagram was computed in the (T, m-bar, h) space for a fixed coupling strength k_F a_s = 1/(sqrt(2) pi).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the BCS-to-normal phase transition: T_cr/mu = 0.55 for m-bar = h = 0.
- The numerical approach reproduced the phase boundary of the inhomogeneous phase in the one-dimensional massless Gross-Neveu model at the level of 0.1%.
- The phase diagram was computed in the range 0 ≤ T/mu ≤ 0.6, 0 ≤ m-bar ≤ 0.95, and -1 ≤ h/mu ≤ 1.
- The coupling strength used was k_F a_s = sqrt(mu/(2 g^2)) = 1/(sqrt(2) pi).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of FFLO-type phases in one-dimensional Fermi gases by Yang, Orso, Liu, Parish, and Casula (various years) to include both mass imbalance and finite temperature simultaneously. It also connects to analytic studies of inhomogeneous phases in one-dimensional relativistic fermion models by Thies (2003). The results enable future investigations of the robustness of the back-bending phase structure when finite transverse coupling between atomic tubes is included, and may contribute to understanding strong fermion dynamics beyond ultracold quantum gases.
