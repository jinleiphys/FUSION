# Lowest Eigenvalues of Random Hamiltonians

> In this paper we present results of the lowest eigenvalues of random Hamiltonians for both fermion and boson systems. We show that an empirical formula of evaluating the lowest eigenvalues of random Hamiltonians in terms of energy centroids and widths of eigenvalues are applicable to many different systems (except for $d$ boson systems). We improve the accuracy of the formula by adding moments higher than two. We suggest another new formula to evaluate the lowest eigenvalues for random matrices with large dimensions (20-5000). These empirical formulas are shown to be applicable not only to the evaluation of the lowest energy but also to the evaluation of excited energies of systems under random two-body interactions.

# Lowest Eigenvalues of Random Hamiltonians

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple empirical formula based on energy centroids, spectral widths, and state dimensions can statistically predict the lowest eigenvalues of random Hamiltonians for both fermion and boson systems, but this formula fails systematically for d-boson systems due to their non-Gaussian eigenvalue distributions. The authors improve prediction accuracy by adding the third-order central moment, though they cannot provide a simple closed-form expression for the corresponding coefficient.

## Method

The authors generate random two-body Hamiltonians using the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) for fermions in single-j and two-j shells, and for sd-boson and d-boson systems. For each spin I state, they compute the lowest eigenvalue by exact diagonalization over 1000 random realizations, then compare it to the empirical formula E_min = mean(E) - Phi(d) * sigma, where Phi(d) = sqrt(a * ln(d) + b), d is the state dimension, and sigma is the spectral width. They also test an improved formula incorporating the cubic root of the third central moment (sigma_3) to account for non-Gaussian deviations.

- For sd-boson systems with n=5 to n=12, a values range from 1.05 +/- 0.02 to 1.13 +/- 0.05, and b values from 0.17 +/- 0.08 to 0.30 +/- 0.07 (Table II).
- For excited states of fermions and sd-bosons, a increases from 1.04 (ground state) to 1.25 (fourth excited state), while b decreases from 0.30 to -3.03 (Table IV).
- The formula applies to dimensions ranging from 2 to 5000 for most systems studied.

## Context

This work extends the earlier formula of Yoshinaga (2006) for predicting ground state energies under random interactions, which itself improved upon less accurate approaches by Ratcliff (1971), Vary (1977), and Zuker (1984). The paper also builds on the work of Papenbrock and Weidenmueller (2004, 2005) who used correlations between J-dependent spectral widths to evaluate spin ground state probabilities. The results enable statistical prediction of not only ground states but also excited state energies for random Hamiltonians, though the failure for d-boson systems highlights the need for alternative approaches when eigenvalue distributions are non-Gaussian (triangular or trapezoidal rather than Gaussian).
