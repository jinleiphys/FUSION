# Global analysis of Skyrme forces with higher-order density dependence

> The density dependent term in Skyrme forces is essential, which simulates three-body and many-body correlations beyond the low-momentum two-body interaction. We speculate that a single density term may be insufficient and a higher-order density dependent term is added. The present work investigates the influences of higher-order density dependencies based on extended UNEDF0 and SkM* forces. The global descriptions of nuclear masses and charge radii have been presented. Consequently the extended UNEDF0 force gives a global rms error on binding energies of 1.29 MeV. The influences on fission barriers and equation of state have also been investigated. The perspectives to improve Skyrme forces have also been discussed, including global center-of-mass corrections and Lipkin-Nogami pairing corrections.

## Key claim

The authors speculate that a single density-dependent term in Skyrme forces may be insufficient to describe the wide range of densities encountered in nuclear physics, and they add a higher-order density-dependent term. The extended UNEDF0 force gives a global rms error on binding energies of 1.29 MeV, but the authors note that there is a competition between simultaneously optimizing for nuclear masses and fission barriers.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the SKYAX code in axial-symmetric coordinate-spaces. They refitted only the momentum-independent Skyrme parameters (t0, t3, t3E, x0, x3, x3E) using the Simulated Annealing Method, keeping other parameters unchanged, and fitted to binding energies of 50 nuclei and charge radii of 8 spherical nuclei. They extended two base Skyrme forces (UNEDF0 and SkM*) by adding a higher-order density-dependent term with power factor γ+1/3.

## Key numbers

- Extended UNEDF0 force (UNEDF0_ext1) gives a global rms error on binding energies of 1.29 MeV for 603 even-even nuclei (compared to 1.503 MeV for original UNEDF0 in the same calculations)
- Extended SkM* force (SkM*_ext1) gives a global rms error on binding energies of 2.358 MeV (compared to 6.305 MeV for original SkM*)
- Charge radii rms errors are 0.027 fm for UNEDF0_ext1 and 0.023 fm for SkM*_ext1 for 339 even-even nuclei
- Fission isomer energy of 240Pu: UNEDF0 gives 4.95 MeV, UNEDF0_ext1 gives 4.99 MeV, UNEDF0_ext2 gives 4.23 MeV (experimental value is 2.8 MeV)
- Fission isomer energy of 240Pu: SkM* gives 2.65 MeV, SkM*_ext1 gives 4.2 MeV, SkM*_ext2 gives 3.44 MeV
- Slope of symmetry energy L: UNEDF0 = 45 MeV, UNEDF0_ext1 = 45.4 MeV, UNEDF0_ext2 = 51.8 MeV; SkM* = 45.8 MeV, SkM*_ext1 = 27.5 MeV, SkM*_ext2 = 42.5 MeV
- Total center-of-mass correction fitted as -18.33A^(-0.208) for UNEDF0_ext1 and -18.61A^(-0.213) for SkM*_ext1

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Xiong et al. (2016) on the SLy4 force, where the higher-order density dependency reduced the rms error of global binding energies from 2.9 MeV to 2.3 MeV. The authors build on the UNEDF0 force optimized by Kortelainen et al. (2010) for nuclear masses (rms 1.455 MeV) and the SkM* force by Bartel et al. (1982) for fission barriers. The work enables future development of high-precision nuclear energy density functionals by demonstrating the need to include beyond-mean-field corrections such as center-of-mass corrections and Lipkin-Nogami pairing corrections in the optimization process.
