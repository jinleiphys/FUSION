# Sensitivity of the Polyakov loop to chiral symmetry restoration

> In the heavy, static quark mass regime of QCD, the Polyakov loop is well known to be an order parameter of the deconfinement phase transition; however, the sensitivity of the Polyakov loop to the deconfinement of light, dynamical quarks is less clear. On the other hand, from the perspective of an effective Lagrangian written in the vicinity of the chiral transition, the Polyakov loop is an energy-like operator and should hence scale as any energy-like operator would. We show here that the Polyakov loop and heavy-quark free energy are sensitive to the chiral transition, i.e. their scaling is consistent with energy-like observables in 3-$d$ $O(N)$ universality classes.

## Key claim

The Polyakov loop and heavy-quark free energy are sensitive to the chiral transition, with their scaling consistent with energy-like observables in 3-d O(N) universality classes, but this sensitivity is easier to detect in the quark-mass dependence than in the temperature dependence because the singular contribution to the temperature slope is nearly constant over a wide temperature range due to the small negative critical exponent alpha.

## Method

The authors analyzed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD configurations generated with highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) and the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on N_tau=8 lattices, with the strange quark mass fixed to its physical value and the light-to-strange quark mass ratio H=m_l/m_s varied from 1/160 to 1/20. They fitted the H-derivative of the heavy-quark free energy F_q/T to the singular part of the O(2) scaling function to extract the non-universal parameters A, T_c, and z_0, then used these to fit F_q/T itself with only constant and linear H-independent regular terms, and finally obtained the Polyakov loop via exponentiation.

## Key numbers

* The critical exponents used for the 3-d O(2) universality class are beta=0.349, delta=4.780, and alpha=-0.0172.
* The universal ratio A+/A- = c_0+/c_0- = 1.115(30), agreeing with the literature value 1.12(5) from Cucchieri (2002).
* The asymptotic coefficients of the scaling function f_f(z) are c_0+ = 2.728(30) and c_0- = 2.447(40).
* The sub-leading correction coefficient c_1+ = -R_chi/2 = -0.678(2) from Cucchieri (2002).
* The chiral pseudo-critical temperature at N_tau=8 is T_c^{N_tau=8}=144(2) MeV from Ding (2019).
* The derivative partial(F_q/T)/partial H shows maxima at T ~ 145-150 MeV for the smallest quark masses, approaching T_c^{N_tau=8} as H decreases.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Polyakov loop beyond the quenched limit studied by Kogut (1983) and the larger-than-physical quark mass studies by Cheng (2008), where inflection points of the Polyakov loop and chiral condensate appeared coincident. The authors challenge the interpretation that the rapid change in the Polyakov loop is a remnant of infinite quark mass physics, instead showing it is sensitive to the chiral transition as an energy-like operator. This enables future continuum-extrapolated studies of Polyakov loop scaling near the chiral limit and provides a framework for understanding the relationship between deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration in QCD.
