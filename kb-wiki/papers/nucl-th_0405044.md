# The Vector Analyzing Power in Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> We compute the vector analyzing power (VAP) for the elastic scattering of transversely polarized electrons from protons at low energies using an effective theory of electrons, protons, and photons. We study all contributions through second order in $E/M$, where $E$ and $M$ are the electron energy and nucleon mass, respectively. The leading order VAP arises from the imaginary part of the interference of one- and two-photon exchange amplitudes. Sub-leading contributions are generated by the nucleon magnetic moment and charge radius as well as recoil corrections to the leading-order amplitude. Working to ${\cal O}(E/M)^2$, we obtain a prediction for $A_n$ that is free of unknown parameters and that agrees with the recent measurement of the VAP in backward angle $ep$ scattering.

## Key claim

The authors compute the vector analyzing power (VAP) for elastic electron-proton scattering through second order in \(E/M\) using an effective theory with pions integrated out, obtaining a parameter-free prediction that agrees with the SAMPLE measurement at backward angles. The agreement suggests that dynamical pions and nucleon resonances play a less important role in this kinematic regime than one might naively expect, though the authors caution they have no _a priori_ reason to expect this agreement given that the electron energy is of the same order as the pion mass.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with pions integrated out, keeping only electrons, protons, and photons as dynamical degrees of freedom. They compute the one-loop two-photon exchange amplitude and the tree-level one-photon exchange amplitude, expanding all contributions in powers of \(p/M\) through second order, and evaluate the imaginary part of the interference that generates the VAP. The calculation includes recoil corrections, the nucleon isovector magnetic moment (\(\mu = 2.793\)), and the proton charge radius (\(\langle r^2\rangle_E = 0.743\) fm\(^2\)), while contributions from nucleon polarizability and dimension-seven T-odd, P-even operators arise at higher order and are neglected.

## Key numbers

- The proton magnetic moment used is \(\mu = 2.793\).
- The proton charge radius squared is \(\langle r^2\rangle_E = 0.743\) fm\(^2\), corresponding to \(C_r = 2.81\).
- The SAMPLE measurement was performed at electron energy \(E = 192\) MeV and scattering angle \(\theta = 146.1^\circ\).
- The leading-order calculation overestimates the magnitude of \(A_n\) by a factor of roughly four at the SAMPLE kinematics.
- The coefficient of the dimension-seven T-odd, P-even operator constrained by the SAMPLE result is \(C_{7a} = 3.07 \pm 6.64\).
- For the Mainz kinematics at \(E = 570\) MeV and \(\theta = 30^\circ\), the estimated contribution from the dimension-seven operator is \(-2.0 \leq A_n^{(7)} \leq 0.7\) ppm, while the loop contribution is \(A_n^{\rm loop} = -0.64\) ppm.

## Context

This work extends the simplest theoretical estimate of the VAP from Mott scattering (Mott, 1929) by including recoil, magnetic moment, and charge radius effects that resolve a factor-of-four discrepancy with the SAMPLE measurement (SAMPLE Collaboration, 2004). The calculation provides a test of two-photon exchange amplitudes needed for interpreting other precision observables, such as the ratio of proton electromagnetic form factors from Rosenbluth separation (Blunden, 2003) and QED corrections to weak interaction observables (McKeown, 2002). Future low-energy VAP measurements over a broader range in \(q^2\) and scattering angle would test the conclusion that pions play a minor role, while the Mainz A4 data at higher energies (Maas, 2003) require non-perturbative methods such as dispersion relations or inclusion of explicit \(\pi\) and \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom.
