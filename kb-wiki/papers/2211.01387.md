# The persistent nonperturbative charm enigma

> The question of the existence and possible magnitude of nonperturbative (often called "intrinsic") charm in the proton has long confounded attempts to cleanly isolate such a contribution in global analyses of high-energy experiments. In this letter, we show that the available (non)perturbative QCD theory and hadronic data have still not developed to a sufficient level to clearly resolve this problem. We highlight a number of challenging aspects that must be confronted in extracting nonperturbative charm in PDF fits, and in so doing, present an updated next-to-next-to-leading order CT analysis of fitted charm, CT18 FC, which we also compare to recent studies. We outline the theory developments and future data needed to make progress on this subject.

# The persistent nonperturbative charm enigma

## Key claim
The available nonperturbative QCD theory and hadronic data have not developed to a sufficient level to clearly resolve the question of whether nonperturbative ("intrinsic") charm exists in the proton. The authors present an updated next-to-next-to-leading order CT analysis (CT18 FC) and conclude that evidence for nonperturbative charm remains elusive, counter to the recent finding by NNPDF (Ball 2022) which claimed 3-sigma evidence.

## Method
The authors performed a global QCD analysis within the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) CTEQ-TEA (CT) framework, repeating the CT18 NNLO and CT18X NNLO analyses using three parametrizations of the charm and anticharm parton distribution functions (PDFs) at the initial scale Q0 = 1.265 GeV. The parametrizations included the BHPS3 model and two meson-baryon models (MBMC and MBME), producing 12 PDF sets across 4 variations of the analysis and underlying intrinsic charm model. The authors compared their results against the NNPDF4.0 analysis and assessed the statistical significance of claimed evidence for intrinsic charm.

## Key numbers
- The charm momentum fraction at the input scale is found to be ⟨x⟩_FC ≡ ⟨x⟩_{c+}(Q0) ∼ 0.5% across all considered CT18 FC fits, with a Δχ² ≈ 10-unit difference relative to the "no FC" scenario
- Under the approximate CT tolerance of Δχ² = 30, the authors obtain ⟨x⟩_FC ≲ 0.013 in all scenarios, representing a reduction from the CT14 IC study upper limit of ⟨x⟩_FC ≲ 0.02
- The NNPDF4.0 analysis determined the first moment to be ⟨x⟩_FC = 0.62 ± 0.28% based on PDF uncertainty only, and 0.62 ± 0.61% after adding missing higher-order uncertainty, making it consistent with zero at 1-sigma
- The improvement of χ² by no more than 25 units for ⟨x⟩_FC ≈ 0.5% in all considered FC scenarios is milder than in the CT14 IC analysis, where ⟨x⟩_FC ≈ 0.8-1% corresponded to a reduction in χ² of up to approximately 40 units for the BHPS models
- The CT18 theory predictions overshoot the EMC F2^c data points over the whole x range, with χ²/N_pt approximately 2.3-3.5 for the EMC data regardless of the IC model

## Context
This work extends the earlier CT14 IC study (Hou 2017) and the CT18 global analysis (Hou 2019), updating the investigation of nonperturbative charm within the CTEQ-TEA framework. The authors contrast their findings with the NNPDF4.0 analysis (Ball 2022), which claimed 3-sigma evidence for intrinsic charm, and with the MSHT observation (McGowan 2022) that partial N3LO fits can enhance perturbatively-generated charm at high x, reducing parameter space for nonperturbative charm. The paper enables future work by outlining four specific developments needed: theoretical progress relating nonperturbative correlation functions to wave functions; clean experimental data at hadron-hadron and lepton-hadron colliders; NNLO calculations including parton showering for processes like Z+c production; and faithful estimates of PDF errors in global analyses. The authors recommend that future data from the Electron-Ion Collider and the proposed CERN Forward Physics Facility could be invaluable for resolving the charm enigma.
