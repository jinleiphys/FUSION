# Upper limit on the mass of RX J1856.5--3754 as a possible quark star

> Recent deep Chandra LETG+HRC-S observations suggest the possibility that RX J1856.5--3754 is a compact star whose radiation radius is 3.8--8.2 km. In this paper, we systematically calculate the mass-radius relations of quark stars within the bag model. Assuming that RX J1856.5--3754 is a pure quark star, we derive an upper limit on its mass for various sets of the bag-model parameters.

# Upper limit on the mass of RX J1856.5--3754 as a possible quark star

## Key claim
Assuming RX J1856.5--3754 is a pure quark star described by the bag model, the upper limit on its mass is of order 0.5--1 solar mass, but this result depends on the bag-model parameters and holds only if the star is a pure quark star rather than a hybrid star or a member of a third family of compact stars.

## Method
The authors constructed equations of state for zero-temperature uds quark matter using the bag model with first-order corrections in the QCD fine structure constant, imposing beta equilibrium and charge neutrality. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain mass-radius relations for pure quark stars across a grid of bag-model parameters: bag constant B^(1/4) from 130 to 250 MeV, strange quark mass m_s from 0 to 300 MeV, and alpha_c from 0 to 0.9. They then compared these relations to the radiation radius R_infinity = 3.8--8.2 km inferred from Chandra observations of RX J1856.5--3754 by Drake et al. (2002).

## Key numbers
- Radiation radius of RX J1856.5--3754 inferred from Chandra LETG+HRC-S observations: R_infinity = 3.8--8.2 km (Drake et al. 2002)
- Blackbody temperature from X-ray spectrum: T = 61.2 +/- 1.0 eV (Drake et al. 2002)
- Distance to RX J1856.5--3754: D = 111--170 pc (from HST parallax analyses)
- Interstellar neutral hydrogen column density: N_H = (0.8--1.1) x 10^20 cm^-2
- Mass upper limit M_up increases from ~0.4 solar mass to ~1 solar mass when B^(1/4) increases from 145 MeV to 200 MeV, while being almost independent of m_s
- For a star of M ~ 1 solar mass, the central energy density would be ~10^16 g cm^-3
- For m_s = 0, the maximum mass of a stable quark star is M = 2.00 solar mass (B_0/B)^(1/2) with B_0 corresponding to B^(1/4) = 145 MeV
- X-ray luminosity: L_X ~ 6 x 10^31 (D/140 pc)^2 erg s^-1

## Context
This work extends previous analyses by Prasanna (2002), Nakamura (2002), and Rosinska (2002) that calculated mass-radius relations for pure quark stars and compared them with constraints from RX J1856.5--3754. The authors systematically explore the full parameter space of the bag model, including the QCD coupling constant and strange quark mass, which earlier studies did not cover. The results enable future observational constraints on the mass of RX J1856.5--3754 to distinguish between quark star, hybrid star, and neutron star interpretations. The paper also discusses how a pressure-dependent bag constant, as considered by Peshier (2002) and Sinha (2002), could modify the allowed parameter region.
