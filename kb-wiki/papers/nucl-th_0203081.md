# Effect of recent R_p and R_n measurements on extended Gari-Krumpelmann model fits to nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> The Gari-Krumpelmann (GK) models of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, in which the rho, omega, and phi vector meson pole contributions evolve at high momentum transfer to conform to the predictions of perturbative QCD (pQCD), was recently extended to include the width of the rho meson by substituting the result of dispersion relations for the pole and the addition of rho' (1450) isovector vector meson pole. This extended model was shown to produce a good overall fit to all the available nucleon electromagnetic form factor (emff) data. Since then new polarization data shows that the electric to magnetic ratios R_p and R_n obtained are not consistent with the older G_{Ep} and G_{En} data in their range of momentum transfer. The model is further extended to include the omega' (1419) isoscalar vector meson pole. It is found that while this GKex cannot simultaneously fit the new R_p and the old G_{En} data, it can fit the new R_p and R_n well simultaneously. An excellent fit to all the remaining data is obtained when the inconsistent G_{Ep} and G_{En} is omitted. The model predictions are shown up to momentum transfer squared, Q^2, of 8 GeV^2/c^2.

# Effect of recent \(R_p\) and \(R_n\) measurements on extended Gari-Krumpelmann model fits to nucleon electromagnetic form factors

## Key claim

The extended Gari-Krumpelmann model (GKex) cannot simultaneously fit the new polarization data for the proton electric-to-magnetic ratio \(R_p\) and the older \(G_{En}\) data, but it can fit the new \(R_p\) and \(R_n\) data well together; an excellent fit to all remaining data is obtained only when the inconsistent older \(G_{Ep}\) and \(G_{En}\) data are omitted from the fit.

## Method

The author extended the DR-GK'(1) model from Lomon (2001) by adding an \(\omega'\)(1419) isoscalar vector meson pole term, while retaining the \(\rho\) meson dispersion relation treatment and the \(\rho'\)(1450) isovector pole. Four variants of the model were fitted to different data subsets using a Levenberg-Marquardt optimization: GKex(01) used the full older data set; GKex(01-) omitted the high \(G_{Ep}\) data of Rock et al.; GKex(02L) added new \(R_p\) data from Jones et al. (2002) and \(R_n\) data from Madey et al. (2002) while omitting conflicting \(G_{En}\) data above \(Q^2 = 0.779\) GeV\(^2/c^2\); GKex(02S) further omitted the high-\(Q^2\) \(G_{Ep}\) data of Littauer et al. above \(Q^2 = 1.75\) GeV\(^2/c^2\).

## Key numbers

- The GKex(02S) fit achieved a total \(\chi^2\) of 237.7 for 178 data points, compared to 326.7 for 191 points in GKex(01).
- The \(\chi^2\) contribution from all 21 \(R_p\) data points was 11.8 for GKex(02S), down from 29.0 for the 17 points in GKex(01) and 114.0 when the 4 higher-\(Q^2\) points of Jones et al. (2002) were added to the GKex(01) fit.
- The \(\chi^2\) contribution from the 3 \(R_n\) data points at \(Q^2 = 0.45\), 1.15, and 1.47 GeV\(^2/c^2\) was 0.6 for both GKex(02L) and GKex(02S).
- The GKex(02S) model predicts \(R_p = 0.19\) at \(Q^2 = 8\) GeV\(^2/c^2\) and a zero crossing near \(Q^2 = 14\) GeV\(^2/c^2\).
- The model predicts \(R_n\) reaches a maximum of 0.426 at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2/c^2\), compared to the Galster parameterization value of 0.309 at the same \(Q^2\).
- The \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) parameter was fixed at 0.15 GeV/\(c\) for the GKex(02L) and GKex(02S) fits.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Gari-Krumpelmann model fits by Lomon (2001), which had used the full data set available before September 2001 including older Rosenbluth separation measurements. The new polarization data from Jones et al. (2002) for \(R_p\) up to \(Q^2 = 5.6\) GeV\(^2/c^2\) and from Madey et al. (2002) for \(R_n\) at three \(Q^2\) values revealed systematic discrepancies with the older \(G_{Ep}\) and \(G_{En}\) data. The resulting GKex(02S) model provides a consistent set of nucleon electromagnetic form factors suitable for predicting nuclear electromagnetic properties and for planning future experiments, particularly at higher \(Q^2\) where the polarization measurements may be extended.
