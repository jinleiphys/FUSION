# Nuclear Structure Aspects of the Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> In this article, we analyze some nuclear structure aspects of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NME), in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model. We give results for the decays of 48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 124Sn, 128Te, 130Te, and 136Xe, using improved effective interactions and valence spaces. We examine the dependence of the NME's on the effective interaction and the valence space, and analyze the effects of the short range correlations and the finite size of the nucleon. Finally we study the influence of the deformation on the values of the NME's.

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double beta decay computed with the Interacting Shell Model are systematically smaller than the central values from Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations, except for 136Xe, and the ISM results are robust against inclusion of small wave-function components, though a large mismatch of deformation between parent and grand-daughter nuclei can reduce the matrix elements by factors as large as 2-3.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale Interacting Shell Model (ISM) calculations using improved effective interactions and valence spaces for seven candidate nuclei (48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 124Sn, 128Te, 130Te, 136Xe). They computed the 0νββ nuclear matrix elements in the closure approximation, modeling short-range correlations with a Jastrow ansatz and finite nucleon size with a standard dipole form factor, and examined the dependence of the NMEs on the effective interaction, valence space, short-range correlations, and deformation.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca, the Gamow-Teller NME MGT(0ν) = 0.67, with an effective neutrino mass limit of 0.85 eV for a half-life of 10^25 years
- For 76Ge, MGT(0ν) = 2.35, with an effective neutrino mass limit of 0.90 eV for a half-life of 10^25 years
- For 82Se, MGT(0ν) = 2.26, with an effective neutrino mass limit of 0.42 eV for a half-life of 10^25 years
- For 130Te, MGT(0ν) = 2.13, with an effective neutrino mass limit of 0.35 eV for a half-life of 10^25 years
- For 136Xe, MGT(0ν) = 1.77, with an effective neutrino mass limit of 0.41 eV for a half-life of 10^25 years
- The ISM NME for 76Ge is 2.58, compared to QRPA ranges of 3.81-4.96 (Rodin et al.) and 4.06-5.26 (Kosu et al.)
- For 48Ca, the three effective interactions KB3, FPD6, and GXPF1 give MGT(0ν) values of 0.67, 0.73, and 0.62 respectively
- Including 2p-2h proton excitations from the 0f7/2 orbit for 82Se gives a 20% increase of M(0ν)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous ISM calculations (Caurier et al., 2005) and provides a systematic comparison with QRPA calculations by Rodin et al. (2006) and Kosu et al. (2006). The paper addresses the pessimistic assessment by Bahcall (2004) regarding the spread of nuclear matrix element calculations, and demonstrates that with improved effective interactions and valence spaces, the ISM results cluster around M(0ν) ≈ 2.5 for most emitters. The findings enable more reliable extraction of the effective neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits and call for benchmarking between ISM and QRPA approaches.
