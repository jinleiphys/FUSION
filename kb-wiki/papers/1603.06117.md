# Vorticity in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the event-by-event generation of flow vorticity in RHIC Au + Au collisions and LHC Pb + Pb collisions by using the HIJING model. Different definitions of the vorticity field and velocity field are considered. A variety of properties of the vorticity are explored, including the impact parameter dependence, the collision energy dependence, the spatial distribution, the event-by-event fluctuation of the magnitude and azimuthal direction, and the time evolution. In addition, the spatial distribution of the flow helicity is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that heavy-ion collisions generate large flow vorticity (on the order of 10^21 s^-1) which is competitive with the chiral magnetic effect in strength, but the magnitude and direction of this vorticity fluctuate significantly event-by-event, meaning that the average vorticity alone does not capture the full picture for phenomenological studies of the chiral vortical effect.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING model to simulate Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV) on an event-by-event basis, generating particle momenta and positions for each collision. They defined the velocity field using two different prescriptions: the particle flow velocity (Eckart frame) and the energy flow velocity (Landau frame), with a Gaussian smearing function to convert discrete particle information into continuous fields. The vorticity was then computed as the curl of the velocity field, and both the non-relativistic definition (omega_1 = curl v) and the relativistic spatial component (omega_2 = gamma^2 curl v) were evaluated at the initial proper time tau_0 (0.4 fm for RHIC, 0.2 fm for LHC) and zero spacetime rapidity.

## Key numbers

* For RHIC Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with impact parameter b=10 fm, the double-averaged vorticity of the energy flow (omega_2) is about 10^21 s^-1 or 20 MeV.
* The initial angular momentum of the two nuclei for Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with b=10 fm is roughly J_0 ~ 10^6, and for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV with b=10 fm is J_0 ~ 10^7.
* About 10% of the angular momentum of the total participants is retained by the quark-gluon plasma at mid-centrality region.
* The vorticity reaches its maximum value at impact parameter b approximately equal to 2R_A (twice the nuclear radius), after which it drops as the nuclei no longer collide.
* The strength of the axial chiral vortical effect is roughly proportional to T^2 * omega_y ~ 10^6 MeV^3, assuming T ~ 300 MeV, which is competitive with the chiral separation effect strength (mu * eB_y ~ 5 m_pi^2) at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions by Betz (2007), Becattini (2007), Huang (2011), Csernai (2013), and others, but provides a systematic event-by-event study within a unified framework using the HIJING model, parallel to the authors' previous work on electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (Deng 2012). The results enable quantitative predictions for the chiral vortical effect and chiral vortical wave in heavy-ion collisions, and provide the necessary input for phenomenological studies of baryon charge separation and Lambda baryon elliptic flow splitting that have been reported by the STAR Collaboration.
