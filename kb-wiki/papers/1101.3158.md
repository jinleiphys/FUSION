# Neutron halo in deformed nuclei from a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model in a Woods-Saxon basis

> Halo phenomenon in deformed nuclei is studied by using a fully self-consistent deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model in a spherical Woods-Saxon basis with the proper asymptotic behavior at large distance from the nuclear center. Taking a deformed neutron-rich and weakly bound nucleus $^{44}$Mg as an example and by examining contributions of the halo, deformation effects, and large spatial extensions, we show a decoupling of the halo orbitals from the deformation of the core.

## Key claim

Using a fully self-consistent deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (DRHB) model in a spherical Woods-Saxon basis, the authors show that in the deformed neutron-rich nucleus ⁴⁴Mg the neutron halo orbitals decouple from the deformation of the core, with the core being prolately deformed while the halo has a slightly oblate shape.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations for axially deformed nuclei with spatial reflection symmetry, expanding potentials and densities in Legendre polynomials and quasiparticle wave functions in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis that has proper asymptotic behavior at large distances. The calculation uses the NL3 density functional, a zero-range density-dependent pairing force with parameters fixed by reproducing the proton pairing energy of ²⁰Mg from a spherical calculation with the Gogny force D1S, and a spherical box of radius 20 fm with mesh size 0.1 fm.

## Key numbers

- ⁴⁴Mg has quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.32 and a two-neutron separation energy S₂n = 0.44 MeV.
- The neutron chemical potential λₙ = −230 keV.
- The 5th orbital (Ω^π = 7/2⁻) has occupation probability 27.2% and the 6th orbital (Ω^π = 1/2⁻) has occupation probability 14.3%.
- The quadrupole moment of the neutron core is 160 fm² and that of the neutron halo is −27 fm².
- The pairing force parameters are ρ_sat = 0.152 fm⁻³ and V₀ = 380 MeV·fm³, with a quasiparticle energy cutoff E^q.p._cut = 60 MeV and a positive-energy state cutoff E⁺_cut = 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field calculations of halos in deformed nuclei (Li 1996, Pei 2006, Nakada 2008) and addresses doubts raised by Hamamoto (2004) about whether low-Ω orbitals contribute to deformation at near-zero binding, and by Nunes (2005) who suggested that correlations inhibit halo formation in deformed drip-line nuclei. The DRHB model in a Woods-Saxon basis, previously developed by the same group (Zhou 2003a, Zhou 2006, Zhou 2008a), provides a self-consistent treatment of continuum, deformation, and large spatial extensions, enabling the study of shape decoupling between core and halo. The results enable further investigation of halo conditions in other isotopic chains such as Ne isotopes.
