# Quasi-universal relations for generalized Skyrme stars

> First proposed in 2013 by Yagi and Yunes, the quasi-universal \emph{I-Love-Q relations} consist of a set of relations between the moment of inertia, the spin-induced quadrupole moment and the electric quadrupolar tidal deformability of neutron stars which are independent of the Equation of State (EoS) within an accuracy of $\sim1\%$. In this work, we show that these relations hold for different Skyrme-based nuclear matter EoS and also for the star-like solutions of different Einstein-BPS-Skyrme-models, some of which do not even present a barotropic equation of state. Further, other quasi-universal relations are analyzed, and together with recent GW observations, we use them to select the generalized Skyrme model that better reproduces observations. Our results reaffirm both the universality of the \emph{I-Love-Q} relations and the suitability of generalized Skyrme models to describe nuclear matter inside neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quasi-universal I-Love-Q relations, previously established for barotropic equations of state, also hold for Skyrme-based nuclear matter models including non-barotropic fluids (where pressure and energy density are not algebraically related), with deviations within the typical ~1% accuracy.

## Method

The authors construct static, spherically symmetric neutron star solutions by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for several Skyrme-type models: the standard Skyrme crystal, the BPS Skyrme submodel (both in mean-field and exact non-barotropic formulations), a generalized interpolating EoS, and a hybrid EoS with a crust. They then apply the Hartle-Thorne formalism to compute slow-rotation and tidal perturbations up to second order in spin and linear order in tidal field, extracting the moment of inertia, quadrupole moment, and tidal deformability (Love numbers) for each model.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_0 = 0.16 \, {\rm fm}^{-3}\); energy per baryon at saturation: \(E_0 = 923.3 \, {\rm MeV}\)
- Maximal mass for the standard Skyrme crystal (cubic, face-centered lattice of \(\mathcal{B}=4\) Skyrmions): \(M_{\rm max} \simeq 1.49 M_\odot\)
- Maximal mass for the generalized EoS with phase transition pressure \(p_{PT}=25 \, {\rm MeV/fm}^3\): \(M_{\rm max} \simeq 2.55 M_\odot\)
- BPS Skyrme model yields maximal masses exceeding \(3 M_\odot\) for the four potentials studied (theta, pion-mass, pion-mass squared, partially flat)
- Phase transition pressure range for the generalized EoS: \(p_{PT} \in (25, 50) \, {\rm MeV/fm}^3\); transition parameter \(\beta = 0.9\)
- Hybrid EoS transition pressure range: \(p_* \in [0.5, 2] \, {\rm MeV/fm}^3\) with \(\beta = 2\)

## Context

This work extends the I-Love-Q relations first proposed by Yagi and Yunes (2013) to Skyrme-based nuclear matter models, including non-barotropic fluids not previously tested. The results reaffirm the universality of these relations and support the suitability of generalized Skyrme models for describing neutron star interiors. The hybrid EoS constructed here passes all current observational constraints, enabling future comparisons with gravitational wave data from binary neutron star mergers to further constrain the Skyrme model parameters.
