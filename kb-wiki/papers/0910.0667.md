# Estiamte of the two-photon exchange effect on deuteron electromagnetic form factors

> The corrections of two-photon exchange on deuteron electromagnetic form factors are estimated based on an effective Lagrangian approach. Numerical results for the form factors $G_{C,M,Q}$ of the deuteron with the corrections are compared to its empirical ones. Moreover, the two new form factors, due to the two-photon exchange, are analyzed. Possible way to test the two-photon exchange corrections to the deuteron form factors is discussed.

## Key claim
The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to deuteron electromagnetic form factors is estimated to be small (less than 1% for the conventional form factors \(G_C, G_M, G_Q\)), but the effect may be detectable through polarization observables in the small-angle limit, particularly near the crossing points of the form factors at \(Q^2 \sim 0.5\) and \(2.0\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method
The author uses an effective Lagrangian approach where the deuteron is treated as a weakly bound state of a proton and a neutron, with a Gaussian correlation function characterized by a size parameter \(\Lambda_D = 0.30\) GeV. Three classes of TPE diagrams are computed simultaneously to preserve gauge invariance: photons coupling to the contact point, one photon to a contact point and one to a nucleon, and both photons to the two nucleons. The loop integrations are simplified using the assumption of Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003) that each photon carries approximately half the transferred momentum, with a step-function cutoff \(\theta(M_N^2 \tau_N - |\kappa^2|)\).

## Key numbers
- The TPE corrections to the conventional deuteron form factors \(G_C, G_M, G_Q\) are less than 1% across the studied \(Q^2\) range.
- The deuteron charge and magnetic form factors have crossing points at \(Q^2_{\text{crossing}} \sim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2_{\text{crossing}} \sim 2.0\) GeV\(^2\).
- The size parameter of the deuteron correlation function is set to \(\Lambda_D = 0.30\) GeV, consistent with the condition \(\langle r^{-2} \rangle \leq 0.02\) GeV\(^2\).
- The new form factors \(G_5'\) and \(G_6'\) are shown to be of order \(10^8\) in the plotted scale (dimensionless, from figures).
- The deuteron magnetic moment normalization is \(G_M(0) = 1.714\) and the quadrupole form factor normalization is \(G_Q(0) = M_D^2 Q_D = 25.83\).

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculation (Dong 2008) that considered only part of the TPE corrections (the third type of diagrams) by now including all three sources of TPE contributions simultaneously, ensuring gauge invariance. It builds on earlier qualitative discussions of TPE in deuteron form factors (Dong 2007, Gakh 2007, Gakh 2008) and on the effective Lagrangian approach for deuteron electromagnetic form factors (Ivanov 2005, Dong 2008). The results enable future experimental tests of TPE effects through precise measurements of deuteron polarization observables \(P_{xz}\) and \(P_z\) at small scattering angles, particularly near the form factor crossing points, and suggest that a full calculation without the Blunden-Melnitchouk-Tjon approximation is needed.
