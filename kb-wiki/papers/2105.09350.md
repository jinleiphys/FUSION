# Jet quenching from heavy to light ion collisions

> We perform an analysis of jet quenching in heavy and light ion collisions for scenarios without and with quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions. We find that the results for these scenarios are very similar, and both of them are in reasonable agreement with data for heavy ion collisions. However, their results become differ significantly for light nuclei. Using the parameters fitted to heavy ion data on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$, we make predictions for 0.2 and 7 TeV O+O collisions that can be verified by future experiments at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper finds that scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in proton-proton collisions produce very similar predictions for jet quenching in heavy ion collisions, but their predictions diverge significantly for light ion collisions such as oxygen-oxygen, enabling future experimental discrimination at RHIC and the LHC.

## Method

The author performs calculations of the nuclear modification factor R_AA using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, with a temperature-dependent running strong coupling constant alpha_s fitted to heavy ion data. The QGP fireball is modeled with Bjorken 1+1D longitudinal expansion and a uniform transverse density distribution, using Glauber model initial conditions. Free parameters are determined via chi-squared fitting to experimental R_AA data for Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions, and then predictions are made for O+O collisions at 0.2 and 7 TeV for scenarios with and without mini-QGP formation in pp collisions.

## Key numbers

* For pp collisions at LHC energies, the medium suppression factor R_pp is approximately 0.7-0.8 (20-30% suppression) at p_T ~ 10-20 GeV.
* The charged multiplicity density of the underlying event in pp jet events, dN_ch^ue/d_eta, is larger than the minimum bias multiplicity density by a factor K_ue of approximately 2-2.5.
* The critical pseudorapidity density for onset of QGP formation in pp collisions is dN_ch/d_eta ~ 6 (from Camp1) to dN_ch/d_eta ~ 9 (from Csanad).
* For pp collisions at RHIC energy sqrt(s)=0.2 TeV, dN_ch^ue/d_eta ~ 6.
* The ALICE preliminary data on the jet fragmentation function modification factor I_pp at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV show a monotonic decrease of about 20% over the UE multiplicity density range ~5-20.
* The thermalization time tau_0 is set to 0.5 fm for both AA and pp collisions, with additional calculations for tau_0=0.8 fm.
* The Debye mass is obtained from lattice analysis, with light quark quasiparticle mass m_q=300 MeV and gluon mass m_g=400 MeV.
* The QCD scale Lambda_QCD=200 MeV is used in the running coupling parametrization.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analyses of jet quenching in pp collisions (Zakharov 2013, 2016) by using a temperature-dependent running coupling that resolves ambiguities in choosing alpha_s for different system sizes, as demonstrated in Zakharov (2020). It contrasts with the BDMPS formalism approach of W_mQGP1 (2020) which did not consider mini-QGP formation in pp collisions. The predictions for O+O collisions at 0.2 and 7 TeV can be tested by future experiments at RHIC (CERN_STAR) and the LHC (CERN_LHC, CERN_OOsum), and the results enable discrimination between scenarios with and without QGP formation in small collision systems.
