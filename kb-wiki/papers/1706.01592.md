# Compact stars with sequential QCD phase transitions

> Compact stars may contain quark matter in their interiors at densities exceeding several times the nuclear saturation density. We explore models of such compact stars where there are two first-order phase transitions: the first from nuclear matter to a quark-matter phase, followed at higher density by another first-order transition to a different quark matter phase [e.g., from the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) to the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase). We show that this can give rise to two separate branches of hybrid stars, separated from each other and from the nuclear branch by instability regions and, therefore, to a new family of compact stars, denser than the ordinary hybrid stars. In a range of parameters, one may obtain twin hybrid stars (hybrid stars with the same masses but different radii) and even triplets where three stars, with inner cores of nuclear matter, 2SC matter, and CFL matter, respectively, all have the same mass but different radii.

## Key claim

Compact stars with two sequential first-order phase transitions (nuclear to 2SC quark matter, then 2SC to CFL quark matter) can produce a new "fourth family" of compact stars and, for certain parameter ranges, twin hybrid stars and even triplet configurations where three stars with different internal compositions have the same mass but different radii, provided the quark-matter equation of state is sufficiently stiff.

## Method

The authors construct a synthetic equation of state using a constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parameterization for two sequential quark-matter phases, with the nuclear matter described by the DDME2 relativistic density functional. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for spherically symmetric, nonrotating, nonmagnetized stars and apply the Bardeen-Thorne-Meltzer stability criterion, identifying stable branches where mass rises with central pressure. The parameter space explored includes the transition pressures, energy-density jumps at each transition, and squared sound speeds in the 2SC and CFL phases.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear-to-2SC transition pressure: P_1 = 1.7 x 10^{35} dyn cm^{-2}, corresponding to nucleonic energy density epsilon_1 = 8.34 x 10^{14} g cm^{-3} and baryon density n_1 = 3.0 n_sat (for the stiff 2SC case)
- Maximum mass of nucleonic stars from DDME2 EOS: M = 2.3 M_sun
- Observational lower bound on maximum neutron star mass: 1.93(2) M_sun to 2.01(4) M_sun
- For the stiff case (s_1=0.7, s_2=1), the maximum separation between nucleonic and CFL branches is about 2 km
- For the triplet configuration with Delta_ep2/Delta_ep1=0.23: radii of the three stars with mass 1.975 M_sun are 11.5 km (CFL core), 12.5 km (2SC core), and 13.5 km (purely nucleonic)
- For the softer 2SC case (s_1=0.5): nuclear-to-2SC transition at P_1=1.14 x 10^{35} dyn cm^{-2}, epsilon_1=7.28 x 10^{14} g cm^{-3}, n_1=2.6 n_sat; nucleonic branch ends at about 1.74 M_sun
- Forbidden radius ranges cover hundreds of meters

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars with a single phase transition (reviewed by Brambilla (2014) and Baym (2014)) to include sequential transitions between different color-superconducting quark phases, specifically the 2SC and CFL phases. It builds on the CSS parameterization developed by Alford (2013) and earlier work on third families of compact stars by Gerlach (1968), Brecher (1977), and others. The results are relevant for interpreting upcoming measurements from the NICER experiment (expected to achieve better than 10% uncertainty on radii) and for understanding possible signatures of QCD phase transitions in gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers.
