# Precise Measurement of Short-Range Correlations in Nuclei from Bremsstrahlung Gamma Ray Emission in Low-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Atomic nuclei and dense nucleonic matter in neutron stars exhibit short-range correlations (SRCs), where nucleons form temporally correlated pairs in proximity beyond mean-field approximation. It is essential to make precision measurement of the fraction of SRC since it carries the signature of underlying quark dynamics in nuclear medium. In this letter, we present the first high-precision measurement of neutron-proton bremsstrahlung $γ$-ray emission from the symmetric $\rm ^{124}Sn$+$\rm ^{124}Sn$ reactions at 25 MeV/u. From the observed spectral hardening, the precise SRC fraction in the $\rm ^{124}Sn$ nucleus is extracted to be $(20 \pm 3)\%$. This result provides a novel, direct and unambiguous evidence of SRCs, and demonstrates that low-energy heavy-ion collisions offers a new approach to studying nuclear structure in connection with quark-level dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the short-range correlation (SRC) fraction in the 124Sn nucleus is (20 ± 3)%, extracted from the observed spectral hardening of neutron-proton bremsstrahlung gamma rays in symmetric 124Sn+124Sn collisions at 25 MeV/u, but this result depends on comparing the measured gamma-ray spectrum shape (not absolute yield) to predictions from the IBUU-MDI transport model, with the analysis restricted to the 35-100 MeV center-of-mass energy range to avoid contamination from collective resonances and statistical emissions.

## Method

The experiment used the Compact Spectrometer for Heavy Ion Experiment (CSHINE) at the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL) to measure bremsstrahlung gamma rays from 124Sn+124Sn collisions at 25 MeV/u. A 4x4 CsI(Tl) hodoscope with surrounding plastic scintillators detected gamma rays up to 100 MeV, with cosmic muon background rejected using topological event reconstruction. The measured gamma-ray spectrum was compared to predictions from the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with Momentum-Dependent Interaction (MDI), which incorporates a parameterized high-momentum tail (HMT) fraction R_HMT in the initial nucleon momentum distribution and calculates gamma production via the one boson exchange (OBE) model for each np collision.

## Key numbers

- SRC fraction (HMT fraction) in 124Sn: R_HMT = (20 ± 3)%, extracted from maximum likelihood analysis of the gamma-ray spectrum in the range 35 < E_gamma_cm < 100 MeV
- Optimal value from quadratic fit: R_HMT = (20.1 ± 2.1)%
- Beam energy: 25 MeV/u for the symmetric 124Sn+124Sn reaction
- Gamma-ray energy range measured: up to 100 MeV energy deposit in the hodoscope
- Detector calibration uncertainty: within 4% for the linear response assumption
- From the extracted R_HMT = 20% in 124Sn (A=124, isospin asymmetry δ=0.194), the derived fractions of neutrons and protons in SRC pairs are C_n^124 = 16% and C_p^124 = 24%, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous bremsstrahlung gamma-ray measurements from 86Kr+124Sn collisions by Qin (2023) and Xu (2024), which first demonstrated the potential of this probe, but with significantly enhanced statistics, extended high-energy spectral coverage, and a symmetric reaction system that allows unambiguous extraction of the SRC fraction in 124Sn. The result resolves a longstanding puzzle from the 1980s regarding the origin of high-energy gamma rays in heavy-ion collisions (Njock 1986), and establishes low-energy heavy-ion collisions as a new approach to studying nuclear structure in connection with quark-level dynamics, complementing traditional electron-nucleus scattering experiments (Jefferson Lab Hall A, 2007; Subedi, 2008; CLAS, 2018) and proton-nucleus knockout measurements (Tang, 2002; Piasetzky, 2006; Patsyuk, 2021).
