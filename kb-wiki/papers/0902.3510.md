# The ab initio no-core shell model

> This contribution reviews a number of applications of the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) within nuclear physics and beyond. We will highlight a nuclear-structure study of the A = 12 isobar using a chiral NN + 3NF interaction. In the spirit of this workshop we will also mention the new development of the NCSM formalism to describe open channels and to approach the problem of nuclear reactions. Finally, we will illustrate the universality of the many-body problem by presenting the recent adaptation of the NCSM effective-interaction approach to study the many-boson problem in an external trapping potential with short-range interactions.

## Key claim

The ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) can successfully describe nuclear structure for systems like the A=12 isobar using chiral NN+3NF interactions, but it has a limitation: states that exhibit a high degree of clusterization are missing from the low-energy spectrum and typically end up at much higher excitation energy, far from converged, regardless of the interaction used.

## Method

The NCSM solves the translational invariant A-body problem by matrix diagonalization in a finite harmonic oscillator basis, combined with a cluster-approximated Lee-Suzuki transformation to generate effective interactions. For open channels, the NCSM formalism is combined with resonating group methods (RGM), where the many-body wave function is decomposed into antisymmetrized cluster basis functions and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized to obtain a non-local, coupled-channels Schrodinger equation for the relative motion of clusters. The method was also adapted to describe a two-dimensional system of strongly interacting bosons with a repulsive Gaussian potential, requiring a complete rewrite of the NCSM code suite for bosonic statistics.

## Key numbers

* The triple-alpha threshold in 12C is at 7.3 MeV.
* For n+4He scattering, there is a sharp low-energy resonance in the 3/2- channel and a broader high-energy resonance in the 1/2- channel.
* For the many-boson problem, calculations were performed for a system of nine bosons with total angular momentum L=0, using a repulsive Gaussian interaction with range sigma=0.1 in oscillator units.

## Context

This review extends the NCSM approach to include chiral three-nucleon forces (3NF) as implemented by Navratil et al. (2007), which gave the correct ordering of T=1 states in the A=12 isobar but over-corrected the spin-orbit strength. The NCSM/RGM approach for open channels, tested on A=4-5 low-energy single-nucleon scattering problems by Quaglioni et al. (2008), enables a unified picture of bound and continuum states grounded in fundamental interactions. The adaptation of the NCSM effective-interaction approach to the many-boson problem, reported by Christensson et al. (2008), demonstrates the universality of the method and opens the door to studying three-dimensional systems and higher particle numbers in cold-atom physics.
