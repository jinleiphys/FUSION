# Strongly Interacting Dark Matter admixed Neutron Stars

> Dark matter may accumulate in neutron stars given its gravitational interaction and abundance. We investigate the influence of strongly-interacting dark matter, described by a QCD-like one-flavor $G_2$ gauge theory, on neutron stars. This choice allows to test, for the first time, a first-principles-determined non-Abelian dark matter equation of state, which supports composite fermionic dark matter and thus a Fermi-pressure-stabilized dark matter component. The ordinary matter part of the mixed star is described by available model-agnostic equations of state that interpolate between the low-density regime and high-density regime. We find that strongly-interacting dark matter has a similar impact on neutron stars as other model equation of states and confirm that strongly-interacting dark matter can be accommodated by constraints from neutron star observations within our uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that strongly-interacting dark matter described by a first-principles lattice QCD-like G₂ gauge theory can be accommodated within current neutron star observational constraints, but only within the uncertainties of the ordinary matter equation of state and for dark matter candidate masses in a specific range; the authors emphasize this is an exploratory study and that the results are not quantitatively definitive due to limited lattice data precision.

## Method

The authors solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for a static, spherically symmetric neutron star composed of ordinary nuclear matter and dark matter, assuming negligible interactions between the two components. The ordinary matter is described by three model-agnostic piecewise polytrope equations of state (EoS I, II, III) from Kurkela (2014) that interpolate between nuclear chiral perturbation theory at low densities and perturbative QCD at high densities. The dark matter equation of state is taken from lattice simulations of one-flavor G₂-QCD at two different bare quark masses (light and heavy ensembles), with the dark matter candidate identified as the lightest fermionic three-dark-quark state, and the equation of state is interpolated using polytropes between lattice data points.

## Key numbers

- Dark matter candidate mass range investigated: 500 MeV to 4 GeV, with four benchmark values shown in figures
- Dark pion mass is 0.25 m_C (light ensemble) and 0.33 m_C (heavy ensemble), where m_C is the dark matter candidate mass
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817: Lambda_tilde < 800 for total mass M_tot = 1.38 (+0.24, -0.19) solar masses (low-spin prior)
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW190425: Lambda_tilde < 600 for total mass M_tot = 1.6 (+0.27, -0.14) solar masses
- For a single-fluid compact object with trivially scalable equation of state, mass and radius scale as m^(-2) where m is the constituent mass scale

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars by using, for the first time, a first-principles non-perturbative equation of state for strongly-interacting dark matter from lattice simulations, specifically G₂-QCD, which supports fermionic dark baryons and thus Fermi-pressure stabilization. Prior work on mixed neutron stars used either weakly interacting massive particle models (Quddus 2019, Quddus 2020) or various simplified dark matter equations of state (Guver 2012, Tolos 2015, Ellis 2018, and many others). The results confirm that strongly-interacting dark matter can be consistent with NICER mass-radius observations and LIGO tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 and GW190425, and the framework enables future searches for dark matter signatures in neutron star observations when more precise lattice data become available.
