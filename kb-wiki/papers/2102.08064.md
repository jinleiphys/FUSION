# Rapidity dependence of heavy-flavour production in heavy-ion collisions within a full 3+1 transport approach: quenching, elliptic and directed flow

> We extend our POWLANG transport setup for the modelling of heavy-flavour production in heavy-ion collisions to the case of full 3+1 simulations, dropping the approximation of longitudinal boost-invariance of the background medium. This enables us to provide predictions for observables for which the rapidity dependence is essential in order to obtain a non-vanishing signal, like the directed flow $v_1$, and to get reliable results also for kinematic distributions of heavy-flavour particles at forward rapidity. We compare our predictions with experimental data obtained in Au-Au and Pb-Pb collisions at RHIC and at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper extends the POWLANG transport setup for heavy-flavour production in heavy-ion collisions from a (2+1)D boost-invariant approximation to a full (3+1)D simulation, enabling predictions for observables like directed flow that require rapidity dependence and for kinematic distributions at forward rapidity, while noting that the hydrodynamic background is validated against soft-particle data but does not constitute a global precision fit.

## Method

The authors interface a relativistic Langevin simulation of heavy-quark (charm and beauty) propagation through the quark-gluon plasma with the full (3+1)D output of the ECHO-QGP hydrodynamic code, using initial conditions from the optical Glauber model with a tilted fireball parametrization that breaks longitudinal boost-invariance. Heavy quarks are produced from binary nucleon-nucleon collisions with no initial spatial tilting, and hadronization is modelled by recombining heavy quarks with light thermal partons from the same fluid cell at T_H = 155 MeV, followed by Lund string fragmentation via PYTHIA 6.4. Two sets of transport coefficients are employed: one from a hard-thermal-loop (HTL) weak-coupling calculation and one from lattice-QCD (l-QCD) simulations.

## Key numbers

- Initial entropy density s_0 = 84 fm^{-3} for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and s_0 = 400 fm^{-3} for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 1 fm/c for RHIC and tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c for LHC
- Kinetic decoupling temperature for soft hadrons T_FO = 140 MeV, common to all centrality classes
- Entropy per unit rapidity dS/deta_s approximately 14600 for central (0-5%) Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC
- Hadronization temperature T_H = 155 MeV
- Slope |dv_1/dy| for D mesons is about one order of magnitude larger than for primary pions at both RHIC and LHC energies
- Parameter eta_m = 3.36 (= y_beam - 2) for RHIC and eta_m = 8.58 (= y_beam) for LHC to describe fireball tilting

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous POWLANG transport setup (Beraudo 2014) from (2+1)D boost-invariant simulations to full (3+1)D, following the approach of Bozek (2010) for tilted initial conditions in hydrodynamic modelling. It enables comparison with directed flow measurements from STAR (Adam 2019) and ALICE (Acharya 2019) and provides a framework for future inclusion of electromagnetic fields via relativistic magnetohydrodynamics, as suggested by Das (2016) and Chatterjee (2018). The results show that combined measurements of R_AA, v_2, and v_1 can constrain heavy-quark transport coefficients, with the directed flow providing a particularly sensitive probe due to the mismatch between initial heavy-quark and bulk matter distributions.
