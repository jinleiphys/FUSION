# Wake in anisotropic Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We calculate the wake in charge density and the wake potential due to the passage of a fast parton in an anisotropic quark gluon plasma(AQGP). For the sake of simplicity small $ξ$(anisotropic parameter) limit has been considered. When the velocity($v$) of the jet is parallel to the anisotropy direction(${\hat n}$) and remains below the phase velocity($v_p$), the wake in induced charge density shows a little oscillatory behavior in AQGP, contrary to the isotropic case. With the jet velocity greater than the phase velocity, the oscillatory behavior increases with $ξ$. Also for $v>v_p$ one observes a clear modification of the cone-like structure in presence of anisotropy. For the parallel direction in the backward region, the depth of the wake potential decreases with the increase of $ξ$ for $v<v_p$ and the potential becomes modified Coulomb-like for higher values of $ξ$. In the forward region, the potential remains modified Coulomb-like with the change in magnitude for nonzero $ξ$, for both $v>v_p$ and $v<v_p$. In the perpendicular direction, the wake potential is symmetric in the forward and backward regions. With the increase of $ξ$, the depth of negative minimum is moving away from the origin irrespective of the jet velocity. On the other hand when the jet velocity is perpendicular to the anisotropy direction, we find significant changes in the case of both wake charge density and potential in comparison to the isotropic case.

## Key claim

In an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (AQGP), the wake in charge density and wake potential induced by a fast parton are modified compared to the isotropic case, but these modifications depend critically on the direction of the parton velocity relative to the anisotropy direction and on whether the parton speed is below or above the phase velocity. The analysis is restricted to the small anisotropy parameter limit (ξ ≪ 1) for analytical tractability.

## Method

The authors use linear response theory within the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation to compute the dielectric function of an AQGP, employing a small-ξ expansion of the gluon self-energy to first order in the anisotropy parameter ξ. The induced color charge density and wake potential are then obtained by Fourier transforming the response to a point-like test parton moving with constant velocity v, with the dielectric function evaluated at ω = k·v (the Cerenkov condition). Numerical contour plots and line profiles are generated for two parton speeds (v = 0.55 and v = 0.99, in units of c) and for two orientations: v parallel to the anisotropy direction n̂ and v perpendicular to n̂.

## Key numbers

- Anisotropy parameter values used: ξ = 0 (isotropic), 0.4, 0.5, and 0.8
- Parton velocities considered: v = 0.55c (below average phase velocity v_p) and v = 0.99c (above v_p)
- For v parallel to n̂ at v = 0.55: at ξ = 0.8, the wake potential in the backward direction shows no negative minimum and becomes "modified Coulomb-like" (contrary to the Lennard-Jones type at ξ = 0)
- For v parallel to n̂ at v = 0.99: the oscillatory behavior of the induced charge density increases with ξ; the depth of the negative minimum in the wake potential shows "no appreciable change" between ξ = 0.5 and ξ = 0.8
- For v perpendicular to n̂ at v = 0.99: at ξ = 0.5, the oscillatory structure of the wake potential in the backward direction is "smeared out" (absent) compared to the isotropic case
- For v perpendicular to n̂ at v = 0.55 and ξ = 0.5: the screening potential along the perpendicular direction becomes a modified Coulomb potential instead of a Lennard-Jones potential

## Context

This work extends the study of wakes in isotropic QGP by Ruppert and Muller (2005) and Chakraborty et al. (2006) to the case of momentum-space anisotropy, which is expected in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions due to rapid longitudinal expansion. The anisotropic distribution function follows the ansatz of Romatschke and Strickland (2003, 2004). The results enable future studies of how anisotropy affects jet-induced Mach cones and two-particle correlations, and the authors note that including collisions (as done by Jiang et al. for viscous QGP) and extending to arbitrary ξ are natural next steps.
