# Meson-Nucleus Bound States with Neural-Network Quantum States

> We present the first systematic calculations of $ϕ$-, $η_c$-, and $J/ψ$-nucleus ground states up to mass number $A{=}12$ based on the HAL QCD meson-nucleon potentials at near-physical point. The $(A{+}1)$-body Schrödinger equation is solved with a neural-network variational Monte Carlo framework, generalized to incorporate mesonic degrees of freedom. Benchmarking on light nuclei from $^2$H to $^{12}$C yields ground-state energies consistent with experiment. Meson-nucleus bound states emerge at $A\ge2$ for $ϕ$, $A\ge4$ for $J/ψ$, and $A\ge6$ for $η_c$. The $ϕ$-nucleus systems exhibit the strongest binding, with binding energies reaching tens of MeV. The $J/ψ$-nucleus and $η_c$-nucleus systems are weakly bound at the few-MeV and sub-MeV scale, respectively. The binding energy per nucleon deepens nearly linearly with $A$ for charmonium systems, whereas the $ϕ$-nucleus system exhibits a non-monotonic behavior peaking at $^4$He -- a distinctive hallmark of the short-range and strongly attractive $ϕN$ interaction. The meson compresses the nucleon distribution relative to the parent nucleus, and evolves from a halo configuration to one embedded inside the nucleus with increasing $A$. Our results provide predictions for future experimental searches, and establish a quantitative bridge between lattice QCD meson-nucleon interactions and the emergent many-body phenomena in meson-nucleus bound states.

## Key claim

The authors present the first systematic calculations of meson-nucleus bound states for phi, eta_c, and J/psi up to mass number A=12, using HAL QCD potentials at a near-physical pion mass of 146 MeV. The claim of binding is conditional on the specific LO pionless-EFT nuclear interaction and the model-dependent construction of the phi-N spin-doublet potential, with the eta_c-4He bound state remaining inconclusive under potential uncertainties.

## Method

The (A+1)-body Schrodinger equation is solved using a neural-network variational Monte Carlo (NN-VMC) framework with a Slater-Jastrow trial wave function generalized to include mesonic degrees of freedom. The nuclear interaction uses a leading-order pionless EFT with two- and three-nucleon forces fitted to np effective-range expansion and light-nucleus binding energies, while the meson-nucleon interactions are taken from HAL QCD lattice potentials at m_pi=146 MeV, with the phi-N spin-doublet potential constructed by scaling the J/psi-N spin-spin component by the meson mass ratio m_J/psi/m_phi.

## Key numbers

- phi-2H binding energy: -1.400(5) +/- 0.640 MeV
- phi-4He binding energy: -17.010(8) +/- 2.737 MeV
- phi-12C binding energy: -34.00(5) +/- 4.30 MeV
- J/psi-4He binding energy: -1.059(3) +/- 0.294 MeV
- J/psi-12C binding energy: -4.49(3) +/- 0.93 MeV
- eta_c-12C binding energy: -2.06(3) +/- 0.46 MeV
- eta_c-4He binding energy: -0.041(2) +/- 0.077 MeV (inconclusive under potential uncertainty)
- phi-4He meson-nucleon rms radius: 1.97 fm
- eta_c-4He meson-nucleon rms radius: 7.04 fm
- phi-2H spin splitting: S=0 state at -1.400(5) MeV versus S=2 state at -0.062(2) MeV
- J/psi-6Li spin splitting: S=2 state lies approximately 77 keV above S=0 state

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used the same HAL QCD potentials but were restricted to three-body systems (Wen 2025) or relied on alpha-cluster approximations (Zhou 2025), which introduced uncontrolled model uncertainty. The calculations establish a quantitative bridge between lattice QCD meson-nucleon interactions and emergent many-body phenomena in meson-nucleus bound states, providing predictions for future experimental searches at J-PARC, Jefferson Lab, and FAIR.
