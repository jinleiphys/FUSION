# Symmetry potentials and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in relativistic impulse approximation

> In the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA), we study symmetry potentials and in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that features chiral symmetry. The chiral symmetry that plays a fundamental role in the nonperturbative physics in the strong interaction is anticipated to add restrictive effects on the symmetry potentials and in-medium NN cross sections. For comparison, we also perform the study with the usual relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. The numerical results with the NJL and RMF models are similar at saturation density and below, since a priori fit was made to saturation properties. With the increase of nuclear density, the chiral symmetry starts to be restored partially in the NJL model, resulting in the explicit fall of the scalar density. In a large energy span, the symmetry potential acquires a significant rise for the partial restoration of the chiral symmetry, compared to the one with the RMF model. It is found that the in-medium NN cross sections in the RIA with the NJL and RMF models both increase with the density in the energy region interested in this study, whereas those with the NJL model increase sharply as long as a clear chiral symmetry restoration takes place. The different tendency of observables in density can be transmitted to the different energy dependence in the RIA. The NJL model is shown to have characteristic energy-dependent symmetry potentials and NN cross sections beyond saturation point, apart from the RMF models.

## Key claim

The NJL model, which incorporates chiral symmetry, predicts that symmetry potentials and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections deviate significantly from those obtained with the usual RMF model at densities above saturation, but only when partial chiral symmetry restoration occurs; at saturation density and below, the results are similar because both models are fitted to saturation properties.

## Method

The authors computed scalar and vector densities in asymmetric nuclear matter using the NJL model (with two momentum cutoffs, 350 and 400 MeV) and the FSUGold RMF parametrization. They then applied the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) using forward NN elastic scattering amplitudes from experimental phase shifts to obtain Dirac optical potentials, from which Schrödinger equivalent potentials, symmetry potentials, nucleon mean free paths, and in-medium NN cross sections were derived.

## Key numbers

- Critical densities for chiral symmetry restoration: 2.37ρ₀ for NJL350 and 3.53ρ₀ for NJL400, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Incompressibility values: 262 MeV for NJL350 and 296 MeV for NJL400
- Symmetry energy and slope set to 31.6 MeV and 50.0 MeV respectively for both NJL parametrizations
- Scalar density with NJL350 begins to decrease at a density lower than with NJL400, and at ρ_B = 0.48 fm⁻³ the NJL350 scalar density is very small while NJL400 shows partial restoration
- At ρ_B = 0.32 fm⁻³ (close to NJL350 critical density), the scalar Dirac optical potential with NJL350 shallows clearly compared to FSUGold and NJL400
- In-medium NN cross sections with NJL350 and NJL400 show dramatic rise at large densities close to the critical density, while FSUGold cross sections rise only moderately

## Context

This work extends the RIA studies of symmetry potentials by Chen and Li (2006) and in-medium NN cross sections by Ji (2007) by incorporating the NJL model that respects chiral symmetry, in contrast to the usual RMF models like FSUGold by Todd and Piekarewicz (2003). The results enable improved constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy extracted from heavy-ion reactions, particularly at supra-saturation densities where chiral symmetry restoration effects become important. The approach also provides a unified framework for deriving consistent symmetry potentials and in-medium cross sections, addressing the uncertainty noted by Tsang (2012) and others that the isospin-dependent in-medium NN cross sections are poorly known.
