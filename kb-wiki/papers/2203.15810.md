# Prospects for precise predictions of $a_μ$ in the Standard Model

> We discuss the prospects for improving the precision on the hadronic corrections to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, and the plans of the Muon $g-2$ Theory Initiative to update the Standard Model prediction.

## Key claim

The paper discusses the prospects for improving the precision of the Standard Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment, noting that while the current 4.2 sigma tension with experiment motivates urgent theory improvements, the feasibility of reaching the target precision depends on resolving tensions between data-driven and lattice QCD evaluations of hadronic vacuum polarization, particularly the 2.1 sigma discrepancy between the BMW20 lattice result and the White Paper data-driven reference value.

## Method

The paper reviews the current status of hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) and hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contributions to the muon g-2, comparing data-driven evaluations using dispersion relations with experimental e+e- to hadrons cross section data against first-principles lattice QCD calculations. For HVP, the data-driven approach uses a master formula relating the leading-order contribution to the total cross section for e+e- to hadrons, while lattice QCD computes HVP through weighted integration of Euclidean correlation functions. For HLbL, the phenomenological estimate considers lowest-lying singularities of the HLbL tensor in a dispersive approach supplemented by short-distance constraints.

## Key numbers

- Combined experimental value (E821 + E989 Run-1): 116,592,061(41) x 10^-11
- Total Standard Model value: 116,591,810(43) x 10^-11
- Difference (experiment minus SM): 251(59) x 10^-11, corresponding to 4.2 sigma tension
- Data-driven HVP LO (e+e-): 6931(40) x 10^-11
- BMW20 lattice HVP LO (udsc): 7116(184) x 10^-11, giving 2.1 sigma tension with data-driven result
- HLbL combined (phenomenology + lattice): 90(17) x 10^-11
- Projected final experimental uncertainty for Fermilab E989: 16 x 10^-11
- Feasible data-driven HVP precision by 2025: 0.3%
- Feasible lattice HVP average error by 2025: less than or equal to 0.5%

## Context

This paper extends the work of the Muon g-2 Theory Initiative White Paper (Aoyama 2020) and addresses the tension between the data-driven HVP evaluation and the BMW20 lattice result (Borsanyi 2021). The paper discusses plans for resolving this tension through independent lattice calculations from collaborations including Aubin, ETM, FNAL/HPQCD/MILC, Mainz, and RBC/UKQCD, as well as new experimental data for the 2pi channel from BaBar, CMD-3, BESIII, and Belle II. The work enables future updates to the Standard Model prediction that will be needed to match the precision of upcoming experimental results from Fermilab, J-PARC, and potentially PSI.
