# First Order Phase Transition in Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> We model the disassembly of an excited nuclear system formed as a result of a heavy ion collision. We find that, as the beam energy in central collisions in varied, the dissociating system crosses a liquid-gas coexistence curve, resulting in a first-order phase transition. Accessible experimental signatures are identified: a peak in specific heat, a power-law yield for composites, and a maximum in the second moment of the yield distribution.

# First Order Phase Transition in Intermediate Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The disassembly of an excited nuclear system formed in central heavy ion collisions crosses a liquid-gas coexistence curve as beam energy is varied, resulting in a first-order phase transition, but only because the freeze-out density (between 0.3ρ₀ and 0.4ρ₀) lies on the low-density side of the coexistence curve, below the critical point, rather than at the second-order critical density of ρ = 0.5ρ₀.

## Method

The authors used a lattice gas model on a 7³ cubic lattice, placing nucleons by Monte-Carlo sampling with the Metropolis algorithm and assigning momenta from a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution. Clusters were identified using a Coniglio-Klein bonding probability based on relative kinetic energy and nearest-neighbor attraction. The freeze-out density was varied by changing the number of nucleons placed in the fixed lattice volume, and observables including specific heat at constant volume C_v, the effective power-law exponent τ from cluster yield distributions, and the second moment S₂ of the mass distribution were calculated.

## Key numbers

- The thermal critical point occurs at T ≈ 1.1275|ε| and density ρ_f/ρ₀ = 1/2.
- The freeze-out density that best fits experimental data lies between 0.3ρ₀ and 0.4ρ₀.
- The lattice size used was N = 7³ (343 sites).
- For mass number A = 85, Coulomb interaction is a small perturbation; for A = 394, Coulomb forces totally alter the picture with no minimum in τ.
- A minimum in τ continues to be obtained for mass numbers as large as A = 200.
- At the first-order transition in an infinite system, the effective exponent τ = 2.
- C_v is reported in units of k_B, with kinetic energy contributing 1.5 to it at all temperatures.

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Curtin, Toki, and Scott (1983) and Finn et al. (1982) that intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions could probe the liquid-gas phase transition region of nuclear matter. The lattice gas model had previously been used by the authors to fit data on central collisions (Pan and Das Gupta, 1995), peripheral collisions (Beaulieu et al., 1996), and central Au on Au collisions (Das Gupta and Pan, 1996). The paper identifies experimental signatures, a peak in specific heat, a power-law yield for composites, and a maximum in the second moment of the yield distribution, that can distinguish this first-order transition from the second-order critical behavior that would occur at the critical density. The results enable experimental searches for these signatures in intermediate-energy heavy ion collision data, though the authors note that very large Coulomb forces (as in A=394 systems) can obscure the signal.
