# Nuclear Matrix Elements for Tests of Local Lorentz Invariance Violation

> The nuclear matrix elements for the momentum quadrupole operator are important for the interpretation of precision atomic physics experiments that search for violations of local Lorentz and CPT symmetry and for new spin-dependent forces. We use the configuration-interaction nuclear shell model and self-consistent mean field theory to calculate these matrix elements in $^{21}$Ne, $^{23}$Na, $^{131}$Xe, $^{173}$Yb and $^{201}$Hg. These are the first microscopic calculations of the nuclear matrix elements for the momentum quadrupole tensor that go beyond the single-particle estimate. We show that they are strongly suppressed by the many-body correlations, in contrast to the well known enhancement of the spatial quadrupole nuclear matrix elements.

# Nuclear Matrix Elements for Tests of Local Lorentz Invariance Violation

## Key claim
The momentum quadrupole matrix elements for nuclei used in precision tests of local Lorentz invariance violation are strongly suppressed by many-body correlations, in contrast to the well-known enhancement of spatial quadrupole matrix elements. The suppression arises from a variational principle that forces the isoscalar momentum quadrupole to vanish at the energy minimum, leaving only small isovector contributions.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the Gogny D1S interaction for five nuclei: 21Ne, 23Na, 131Xe, 173Yb, and 201Hg. For the lighter nuclei 21Ne and 23Na, they also carried out configuration-interaction (CI) shell-model calculations in the sd valence space using the USDB Hamiltonian, including core-polarization corrections with effective charges of opposite sign for the momentum operator compared to the spatial quadrupole operator.

## Key numbers
- For 21Ne: CI with core polarization gives Mp = 2.7 m MeV and Mn = 7.0 m MeV; SCMF gives Mp = 2.8 m MeV and Mn = 4.2 m MeV; the simple single-particle 0d3/2 model gives Mn = -18.2 m MeV
- For 23Na: CI with core polarization gives Mp = 3.6 m MeV and Mn = 5.9 m MeV; SCMF gives Mp = 3.2 m MeV and Mn = 3.6 m MeV
- For 21Ne, the experimental magnetic moment is -0.662, compared to the CI result of -0.720 and the single-particle model result of 1.148
- For heavy nuclei (133Cs, 173Yb, 201Hg), SCMF gives upper limits of |Mp| < 10 m MeV and |Mn| < 10 m MeV, a factor of five smaller than previous estimates by Flambaum et al.
- The experimental charge quadrupole moment for 21Ne is 10.3(8) e fm2; for 23Na it is 10.45(10) e fm2

## Context
This work extends the single-particle estimate used for 21Ne in Smiciklas et al. (2011) and contradicts the model of Flambaum et al. (2004, 2015) that related momentum quadrupole moments to experimental spatial quadrupole moments. The results provide the first microscopic calculations beyond single-particle estimates for the momentum quadrupole tensor, enabling more accurate interpretation of precision atomic physics experiments searching for local Lorentz and CPT violation. The authors suggest that for heavy nuclei a nominal value of 1 m MeV should be used to interpret LLIV experiments, and that ab-initio approaches should be applied to light nuclei to check these phenomenological results.
