# Observable measures of critical behavior in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Critical behaviors of quark-hadron phase transition in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are investigated with the aim of identifying hadronic observables. The surface of the plasma cylinder is mapped onto a 2D lattice. The Ising model is used to simulate configurations corresponding to cross-over transitions in accordance to the findings of QCD lattice gauge theory. Hadrons are formed in clusters of all sizes. Various measures are examined to quantify the fluctuations of the cluster sizes and of the voids among the clusters. The canonical power-law behaviors near the critical temperature are found for appropriately chosen measures. Since the temperature is not directly observable, attention is given to the problem of finding observable measures. It is demonstrated that for the measures considered the dependence on the final-state randomization is weak. Thus the critical behavior of the measures proposed is likely to survive the scattering effect of the hadron gas in the final state.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that critical behavior of the quark-hadron phase transition can be observed through measures of fluctuations in hadronic clusters and voids, even after final-state randomization and configuration mixing, provided that the temperature dependence is replaced by dependence on the average hadron density \(\bar{\rho}\), which is directly measurable.

## Method

The surface of the expanding plasma cylinder is mapped onto a 2D lattice, and the Ising model is used to simulate configurations corresponding to a cross-over transition near the second-order phase transition. Hadron density per cell is defined from the Ising spins using a prescription that selects only cells aligned with the total magnetization, producing either positive hadron density or zero (void). Two families of measures are studied: the entropy-like quantity \(J(M,T)\) for cluster fluctuations, and the \(G_q\) moments and their derivatives \(S_q\) for void patterns, with scaling exponents extracted as functions of temperature and bin size.

## Key numbers

- The exponent \(\zeta\) for the power-law behavior of \(\alpha(T) \propto (T_c - T)^{-\zeta}\) is \(\zeta = 1.88\).
- When using the measurable average density \(\bar{\rho}\), the exponent \(\bar{\zeta}\) in \(\alpha(\bar{\rho}) \propto (\bar{\rho} - \bar{\rho}_0)^{-\bar{\zeta}}\) is \(\bar{\zeta} = 0.97\).
- For void analysis at \(T = T_c\) and \(\rho_0 = 20\), the scaling exponent \(\gamma_q\) in \(\langle G_q \rangle \propto M^{\gamma_q}\) depends linearly on \(q\) with slope \(c = 0.8\).
- For the same conditions, the exponent \(\sigma_q\) in \(S_q \propto M^{\sigma_q}\) depends linearly on \(q\) with slope \(s = 0.76\).
- For \(\rho_0 > 20\), the values of \(c\) and \(s\) vary by as much as 20% for \(T\) varying within \(\pm 2\%\) of \(T_c\), but converge to the quoted values as \(\rho_0 \rightarrow 20\).

## Context

This work extends the application of Ising model universality to quark-hadron phase transitions, building on lattice QCD findings that the chiral phase transition belongs to the same universality class as the Ising system (Karsch, 1994). It introduces new observable measures, \(J(M,T)\) and void scaling exponents, that are designed to survive the final-state scattering effects of the hadron gas, unlike earlier proposals that relied on unobservable temperature. The void analysis method is also suggested as a tool for studying multifragmentation in intermediate-energy nuclear collisions, where the proposed erraticity measure \(E_q\) could challenge theoretical models that lack sufficient dynamical fluctuations.
