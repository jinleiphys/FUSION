# Dipole Oscillations in Bose - Fermi Mixture in the Time-Dependent Grosspitaevskii and Vlasov equations

> We study the dipole collective oscillations in the bose-fermi mixture using a dynamical time-dependent approach, which are formulated with the time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii equation and the Vlasov equation. We find big difference in behaviors of fermion oscillation between the time-dependent approach and usual approaches such as the random-phase approximation and the sum-rule approach. While the bose gas oscillates monotonously, the fermion oscillation shows a beat and a damping. When the amplitude is not minimal, the dipole oscillation of the fermi gas cannot be described with a simple center-of-mass motion.

## Key claim

The time-dependent approach (TDGP + Vlasov) reveals that fermion dipole oscillations in a Bose-Fermi mixture exhibit beat and damping due to multimode dephasing, in contrast to the single-mode behavior predicted by the random-phase approximation (RPA) and sum-rule approaches, which are limited to minimal amplitudes and cannot capture the large-amplitude dynamics where the fermion density distribution changes significantly.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Gross-Pitaevskii equation for condensed bosons and the Vlasov equation (using the test-particle method with 100 test particles per fermion) for fermions in a spherical trap with axial symmetry, for a system of 10000 87Rb bosons and 1000 40K fermions at zero temperature. They boosted the boson wave function and fermion test particles at tau=0 with parameters lambda_B=0.4 and lambda_F=-0.4 (out-of-phase initial condition) and evolved the system using the second-order predictor-corrector method, varying the boson-fermion coupling strength h_BF relative to the boson-boson coupling g_BB.

## Key numbers

- Boson number N_b = 10000, fermion number N_f = 1000 for the 87Rb-40K system
- Fermion mass normalized by boson mass m_f = 40/87 ≈ 0.46
- Boson-boson interaction parameter g_BB = 1.34 × 10^(-2), corresponding to a_BB = 4.22 nm
- Fermion trapped frequency omega_f = 1/sqrt(m_f) ≈ 1.48
- Boson oscillation frequency omega_D^b = 1.0 (in units of Omega_B)
- Fermion intrinsic mode frequencies: mode-2 at omega = omega_f ≈ 1.47 and mode-3 at omega = omega_D^f (coupling-dependent)
- Initial amplitude of fermion oscillation about 0.4, decreasing to about 0.1-0.2 after damping (tau ≳ 60) for h_BF = ±g_BB
- RPA excitation energy for h_BF = g_BB with boson motion frozen is about 80 hbar Omega_B

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of monopole oscillations in Bose-Fermi mixtures (Maruyama et al., 2005) using the same time-dependent approach, which showed rapid damping at zero temperature not seen in RPA calculations (Sogo, 2005). The results contradict the sum-rule approach (Miyakawa, 2004), which predicts a single fermion frequency sensitive to density distributions but fails when transition strength distributes to multiple modes. The RPA (Sogo, 2005) can describe only minimal vibrations (radial variation about 0.05%) while actual experiments (ColEx) can have amplitudes as large as 10%; the time-dependent approach enables study of experimentally relevant large-amplitude oscillations where fermions change density distribution and move to regions of low resistance.
