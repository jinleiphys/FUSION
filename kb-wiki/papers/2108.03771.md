# A brief account of Steven Weinberg's legacy in ab initio many-body theory

> In this contribution to the special issue "Celebrating 30 years of Steven Weinberg's papers on Nuclear Forces from Chiral Lagrangians," we emphasize the important role chiral effective field theory has played in leading nuclear physics into a precision era. To this end, we share our perspective on a few of the recent advances made in ab initio calculations of nuclear structure and nuclear matter observables, as well as Bayesian uncertainty quantification of effective field theory truncation errors.

# A brief account of Steven Weinberg's legacy in ab initio many-body theory

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory (χEFT) has been essential in moving nuclear physics into a precision era, but the authors present this as a perspective on recent advances rather than a definitive proof; they emphasize that the combination of χEFT with ab initio many-body methods and Bayesian uncertainty quantification is what enables controlled calculations of nuclear structure and nuclear matter observables.

## Method
The paper reviews the Weinberg power-counting scheme for nuclear forces derived from chiral Lagrangians and its implementation in ab initio many-body calculations. It surveys recent progress in applying these interactions to finite nuclei (e.g., oxygen isotopes) and infinite nuclear matter, and discusses the use of Bayesian statistical methods to quantify truncation errors from the χEFT expansion. The authors also describe the development of reduced-order emulators to accelerate many-body calculations.

## Key numbers
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is given as n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³, with the energy per particle at saturation around -16 MeV.
- The paper references calculations of oxygen isotopes up to ²⁴O and ²⁸O using coupled-cluster theory with chiral interactions at N²LO and N³LO.
- Bayesian uncertainty quantification yields 68% credible intervals for χEFT truncation errors that are typically 1-2 MeV for binding energies of light-to-medium mass nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the foundational papers of Weinberg (1990-1992) that first applied chiral Lagrangians to nuclear forces, and it reviews subsequent developments by Epelbaum, Machleidt, and Entem (2000s) who implemented these interactions at higher orders. The paper enables future work by showing how Bayesian methods developed by Furnstahl, Phillips, and collaborators (2015-2020) can be combined with ab initio methods to provide statistically rigorous uncertainty estimates for nuclear observables, which is critical for applications in nuclear astrophysics and fundamental symmetry tests.
