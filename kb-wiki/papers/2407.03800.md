# Interplay of single-particle and collective modes in the $^{12}$C(p,2p) reaction near 100 MeV

> The $^{12}$C(p,2p)$^{11}$B reaction at $E_p =98.7$ MeV proton beam energy is analyzed using a rigorous three-particle scattering formalism extended to include the internal excitation of the nuclear core or residual nucleus. The excitation proceeds via the core interaction with any of the external nucleons. We assume the $^{11}$B ground and low-lying excited states [$\frac32^-$ (0.0 MeV), $\frac52^-$ (4.45 MeV), $\frac72^-$ (6.74 MeV)] and the excited states [$\frac12^-$ (2.12 MeV), $\frac32^-$ (5.02 MeV)] to be members of $K=\frac32^-$ and $K=\frac12^-$ rotational bands, respectively. The dynamical core excitation results in a significant cross section for the reaction leading to the $\frac52^-$ (4.45 MeV) excited state of $^{11}$B that cannot be populated through the single-particle excitation mechanism. The detailed agreement between the theoretical calculations and data depends on the used optical model parametrizations and the kinematical configuration of the detected nucleons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dynamical excitation of the nuclear core (the residual \(^{11}\)B nucleus) during the \(^{12}\)C(p,2p) reaction at 98.7 MeV can produce significant cross sections for transitions to \(^{11}\)B states that have negligible single-particle spectroscopic factors, but the detailed agreement with experimental data depends on the choice of optical potential parametrization and the specific kinematic configuration of the detected protons.

## Method

The authors extended the Faddeev/AGS three-particle scattering formalism to include internal excitation of the nuclear core via its interaction with either external nucleon, assuming the \(^{11}\)B states belong to \(K=\frac32^-\) and \(K=\frac12^-\) rotational bands with a quadrupole deformation length of 1.5 fm. They solved the coupled momentum-space equations using two global optical potential parametrizations (Koning-Delaroche and Weppner) and incorporated microscopic VMC spectroscopic overlaps for the initial \(^{12}\)C bound state, scaling the calculated fivefold differential cross sections by the sum of spectroscopic factors for coupled states.

## Key numbers

* The proton beam energy is \(E_p = 98.7\) MeV, with outgoing protons detected in coplanar geometry at azimuthal angle \(180^\circ\).
* Two kinematic settings were analyzed: symmetric (KS) with \(E_p = 41.35 \pm 1.25\) MeV and \(\theta_p = \theta_N\) from \(30^\circ\) to \(65^\circ\); asymmetric (KA) with \(E_p = 59.5 \pm 1.8\) MeV, \(\theta_p = 25^\circ\), and \(\theta_N\) from \(30^\circ\) to \(90^\circ\).
* VMC spectroscopic factors for \(^{11}\)B states: \(\frac32^-\) (0.0 MeV) = 2.363, \(\frac12^-\) (2.12 MeV) = 0.819, \(\frac52^-\) (4.45 MeV) = 0.001, \(\frac32^-\) (5.02 MeV) = 0.206.
* The quadrupole deformation length used for the rotational model is \(\delta_2 = 1.5\) fm, consistent with \(\beta_2 \approx 0.52\) from \(^{11}\)B(p,p) data at 30.3 MeV.
* Binding potential parameters for the \(K=\frac32^-\) band: central strength \(V_c = 63.068\) MeV, radius \(R_c = 3.011\) fm, diffuseness \(a_c = 0.692\) fm; for the \(K=\frac12^-\) band: \(V_c = 76.274\) MeV, \(R_c = 2.636\) fm, \(a_c = 0.784\) fm.

## Context

This work reanalyzes the IUCF \(^{12}\)C(p,2p) data from Devins (1979) at 98.7 MeV, which showed strong population of the \(\frac52^-\) (4.45 MeV) state that could not be explained by single-particle knockout. It extends prior Faddeev/AGS calculations by Me (2019) and Cr (2020) that used VMC wave functions under the inert-core assumption, and builds on earlier three-cluster breakup studies by Moro (2012), Deltuva (2013, 2017, 2019) that incorporated collective core excitation. The results enable future analysis of upcoming \(^{12}\)C(p,2p) data from GSI and other laboratories that will detect the \(\frac52^-\) and \(\frac72^-\) states of \(^{11}\)B.
