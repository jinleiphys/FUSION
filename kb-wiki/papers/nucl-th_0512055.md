# Aspects of phi-meson production in proton-proton collisions

> We analyze near-threshold cross section data for the reaction pp->pp phi published by the DISTO collaboration and recent, still preliminary results presented by the ANKE Collaboration. We formulate a procedure to evaluate the OZI ratio at low energies by taking into account corrections from the kinematics and the final-state interaction. Combining the new data with the few measurements available at higher energies we give a limit for the OZI rule violation and estimate the possible contribution from a five-quark baryonic resonance coupled to the phi-p system.

## Key claim

The near-threshold cross section data for \(pp\to pp\phi\) from DISTO and preliminary ANKE results, when corrected for kinematic differences and final-state interactions, yield an OZI ratio \(R_{\phi/\omega}\) that is somewhat larger than, but still compatible with, the value extracted from \(\pi N\) and \(NN\) reactions; the strong energy dependence of the \(pp\to pp\phi\) amplitude for excess energies between 0.1 and 1 GeV might be indicative of a resonance coupling to the \(\phi p\) system, but the data do not allow a definitive claim of OZI rule violation beyond that seen in vector meson decays.

## Method

The authors extract the average reaction amplitude \(|{\cal M}|^2\) for \(pp\to pp\omega\) and \(pp\to pp\phi\) from measured cross sections using a non-relativistic three-body phase space formula that incorporates final-state interactions (FSI) between the two protons via a Jost function parameterization with scattering parameters \(\alpha = -20.5\) MeV/c and \(\beta = 166.7\) MeV/c. For the \(\omega\) production at low excess energies, they also account for the finite \(\omega\) width (\(\Gamma = 8.49\) MeV) by integrating over the \(\omega\) spectral distribution. The OZI ratio is then evaluated as the ratio of the squared amplitudes \(|{\cal M}_\phi|^2 / |{\cal M}_\omega|^2\), using an average \(\omega\) amplitude of \(|{\cal M}| = (40 \pm 3)\) fm interpolated from TOF and SPES-III data. To explore a possible resonance contribution, they introduce a Breit-Wigner term for a cryptoexotic baryon \(B_\phi\) in the \(\phi p\) subsystem, adjusting its mass, width, and coupling to reproduce the cross section data.

## Key numbers

* The OZI limit from SU(3) symmetry and ideal mixing is \(R_{\phi/\omega} = 4.2 \times 10^{-3}\) (Eq. 1).
* The ratio from vector meson decays is \(R_{\phi/\omega} = (12.5 \pm 3.4) \times 10^{-3}\).
* The ratio from a combined analysis of \(\pi N\) and \(NN\) data is \(R_{\phi/\omega} = (13.4 \pm 3.2) \times 10^{-3}\).
* The DISTO measurement yields \(R_{\phi/\omega} = (36 \pm 16) \times 10^{-3}\) at an excess energy \(\epsilon \simeq 82\) MeV (proton beam energy \(T_{lab} \simeq 2.85\) GeV).
* The average \(pp\to pp\omega\) reaction amplitude used for the OZI analysis is \(|{\cal M}| = (40 \pm 3)\) fm.
* For the \(pp\to pp\phi\) reaction, a constant amplitude \(|{\cal M}| = 2.3\) fm normalized to high-energy data is used to illustrate the enhancement at low energies.
* Two parameter sets for a hypothetical \(B_\phi\) resonance are given: Set A: \(M = 1956\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 120\) MeV, \(c_0 = 8\) fm; Set B: \(M = 2000\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 40\) MeV, \(c_0 = 8\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sibirtsev et al. (2005) on the OZI ratio from \(\pi N\) and \(NN\) reactions, and addresses the earlier DISTO result (Balestra, 1998) which suggested a large OZI violation but did not fully implement kinematic and FSI corrections. The authors use high-statistics \(\omega\) data from the TOF Collaboration (TOF1, TOF2) to extract the \(\omega\) production amplitude, enabling a more reliable comparison with the \(\phi\) data from DISTO and the preliminary ANKE results (Hartmann). The analysis enables future searches for cryptoexotic pentaquark baryons with hidden strangeness (\(B_\phi = udds\bar s\)) at facilities like COSY, by predicting the \(\phi p\) and \(pp\) invariant mass spectra that would reveal such a resonance.
