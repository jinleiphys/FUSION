# Relation of $a^\dagger a$ terms to higher-order terms in the adiabatic expansion for large-amplitude collective motion

> We investigate the relation of $a^\dagger a$ terms in the collective operator to the higher-order terms in the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method. In the ASCC method, a state vector is written as $e^{i\hat G(q,p,n)}|ϕ(q)\rangle$ with $\hat G(q,p,n)$ which is a function of collective coordinate $q$, its conjugate momentum $p$ and the particle number $n$. According to the generalized Thouless theorem, $\hat G$ can be written as a linear combination of two-quasiparticle creation and annihilation operators $a^\dagger_μa^\dagger_ν$ and $a_νa_μ$. We show that, if $a^\dagger a$ terms are included in $\hat G(q,p,n)$, it corresponds to the higher-order terms in the adiabatic expansion of $\hat G$. This relation serves as a prescription to determine the higher-order collective operators from the $a^\dagger a$ part of the collective operator, once it is given without solving the higher-order equations of motion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including $a^\dagger a$ terms (B-terms) in the collective operator $\hat G$ of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method is equivalent to including certain higher-order terms in the adiabatic expansion of $\hat G$, providing a prescription to determine higher-order collective operators from the B-part without solving higher-order equations of motion.

## Method

The author compares two approaches to preserving gauge symmetry in the ASCC method: Approach A, which includes higher-order collective operators (up to third order in $p$ and $n$) consisting only of A-terms ($a^\dagger a^\dagger$ and $aa$), and Approach B, which includes only first-order operators but allows B-terms ($a^\dagger a$) in the collective coordinate operator $\hat Q$. By comparing the moving-frame HFB and QRPA equations and directly rewriting the state vector using Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff and Zassenhaus formulas, the author derives explicit correspondences between the B-part of the first-order operator and the higher-order operators.

## Key numbers

* The moving-frame QRPA equation of $O(p)$ yields the correspondence $\hat Q^{(2)} \Leftrightarrow i[\hat Q_B,\hat Q_A]$, where $\hat Q^{(2)}$ is the second-order collective operator and $\hat Q_B$ is the B-part of the first-order operator.
* The moving-frame QRPA equation of $O(p^2)$ yields the correspondence $\hat Q^{(3)} \Leftrightarrow [[\hat Q_B , \hat Q_A],\hat Q_B] = -[\hat Q_B , [\hat Q_B,\hat Q_A]]$, where $\hat Q^{(3)}$ is the third-order collective operator.
* The expansion of the state vector is carried out up to $O(p^4)$ using the Zassenhaus and BCH formulas to derive the correspondence.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gauge symmetry in the ASCC method previously studied by Sato (2017) and Hinohara et al. (2007, 2008, 2009). The ASCC method, formulated by Matsuo (2000), is an advanced version of adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) theory. The paper clarifies the relationship between two approaches to preserving gauge invariance: one using higher-order operators (Approach A) and one using B-terms in first-order operators (Approach B), which were previously treated as separate prescriptions. This enables practitioners to determine higher-order collective operators from the B-part of the first-order collective coordinate operator without solving the higher-order equations of motion.
