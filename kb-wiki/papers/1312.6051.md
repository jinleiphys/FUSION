# Isovector dipole-resonance structure within the effective surface approximation

> The nuclear isovector-dipole strength structure is analyzed in terms of the main and satellite (pygmy) peaks within the Fermi-liquid droplet model. Such a structure is sensitive to the value of the surface symmetry-energy constant obtained analytically for different Skyrme forces in the leptodermous effective surface approximation. Energies, sum rules and transition densities of the main and satellite peaks for specific Skyrme forces are qualitatively in agreement with the experimental data and other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The isovector dipole resonance (IVDR) strength function splits into a main peak and a satellite (pygmy) peak due to coupling between isovector and isoscalar density-volume vibrations, and this two-peak structure is sensitive to the surface symmetry-energy constant \(k_S\) derived from Skyrme force parameters within the effective surface approximation.

## Method

The authors solved linearized Landau-Vlasov equations for isoscalar and isovector distribution functions in the rare quasiparticle-collision (zero sound) regime, with boundary conditions at the effective surface (ES) defined by the maximum density gradient. They used the leptodermous expansion (small parameter \(a/R \sim A^{-1/3}\)) to derive analytical expressions for the surface symmetry-energy constant \(k_S\) from Skyrme force parameters, then computed the response function, energies, energy-weighted sum rules (EWSR), and transition densities for the main (\(n=1\)) and satellite (\(n=2\)) IVDR peaks for three Skyrme forces (SLy5*, SVmas08, SVsym32) in the nuclei \({}^{208}\)Pb, \({}^{132}\)Sn, and \({}^{68}\)Ni.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{132}\)Sn with SLy5*: main peak energy \(E_1 = 17.0\) MeV (68% EWSR), satellite peak energy \(E_2 = 19.8\) MeV (32% EWSR); surface symmetry-energy constant \(k_S = -3.94\) MeV.
- For \({}^{132}\)Sn with SVmas08: \(E_1 = 16.1\) MeV (83% EWSR), \(E_2 = 17.1\) MeV (17% EWSR); \(k_S = 12.4\) MeV.
- For \({}^{132}\)Sn with SVsym32: \(E_1 = 18.1\) MeV (64% EWSR), \(E_2 = 14.8\) MeV (36% EWSR); \(k_S = 3.41\) MeV.
- For \({}^{208}\)Pb with SLy5*: \(E_1 = 14.5\) MeV (74% EWSR), \(E_2 = 17.1\) MeV (26% EWSR); mean FLDM energy constant \(D_{\text{FLD}} = 89\) MeV, hydrodynamic (SJ) constant \(D_{\text{HD}} = 84\) MeV.
- For \({}^{208}\)Pb with SVmas08: \(E_1 = 13.9\) MeV (89% EWSR), \(E_2 = 14.8\) MeV (11% EWSR); \(D_{\text{FLD}} = 83\) MeV, \(D_{\text{HD}} = 101\) MeV.
- For \({}^{208}\)Pb with SVsym32: \(E_1 = 15.6\) MeV (53% EWSR), \(E_2 = 12.8\) MeV (47% EWSR); \(D_{\text{FLD}} = 84\) MeV, \(D_{\text{HD}} = 97\) MeV.
- Relaxation times at the IVGDR peak: SLy5* \(\tau = 6.0 \times 10^{-21}\) s (\({}^{208}\)Pb), SVmas08 \(\tau = 7.4 \times 10^{-21}\) s (\({}^{208}\)Pb), SVsym32 \(\tau = 9.9 \times 10^{-21}\) s (\({}^{208}\)Pb).

## Context

This work extends the Fermi-liquid droplet model (FLDM) of Kolomietz, Magner, and Shlomo (1990s) and Belyaev (1990s) by incorporating the effective surface approximation for the surface symmetry-energy constant derived in BMRV (2010). It provides analytical calculations of the pygmy dipole resonance structure that are qualitatively consistent with experimental data for \({}^{208}\)Pb, \({}^{132}\)Sn, and \({}^{68}\)Ni from Adrich et al. (2005) and Wieland et al. (2009), and with time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations by Nesterenko et al. (2006). The results enable systematic evaluation of the surface symmetry-energy constant \(k_S\) by comparing predicted IVDR satellite energies and sum rules with future experimental data on neutron-rich nuclei.
