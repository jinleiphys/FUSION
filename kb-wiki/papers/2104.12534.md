# Hydrodynamic Attractor in a Hubble Expansion

> We analytically investigate hydrodynamic attractor solutions in both Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) and kinetic theories in a viscous fluid system undergoing a Hubble expansion with a fixed expansion rate. We show that the gradient expansion for the MIS theory and the Chapman-Enskog expansion for the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation are factorially divergent and obtain hydrodynamic attractor solutions by applying the Borel resummation technique to those asymptotic divergent series. In both theories, we find that the hydrodynamic attractor solutions are globally attractive and only the first-order non-hydrodynamic mode exists. We also find that the hydrodynamic attractor solutions in the two theories disagree with each other when gradients become large, and that the speed of the attraction is different. Similarities and differences from hydrodynamic attractors in the Bjorken and Gubser flows are also discussed. Our results push the idea of far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamics in systems undergoing a Hubble expansion.

## Key claim

The paper analytically demonstrates that hydrodynamic attractor solutions exist in a viscous fluid system undergoing a Hubble expansion with a fixed expansion rate, but only when the gradient expansion is resummed via Borel techniques; the attractor solutions in Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) and kinetic theories disagree with each other when gradients become large, and the speed of attraction differs between the two theories.

## Method

The authors analytically solve the Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) equations for a Hubble expansion with a power-law scale factor \(a(t) = (t/t_{\rm in})^\alpha\) (taking \(\alpha = 2/3\)) and constant transport coefficients. They also solve the Boltzmann equation within the relaxation time approximation using the Chapman-Enskog expansion. In both cases, they show the gradient expansions are factorially divergent and apply Borel resummation to obtain hydrodynamic attractor solutions, then analyze the non-hydrodynamic modes via trans-series ansatz.

## Key numbers

- The scale factor exponent is set to \(\alpha = 2/3\), inspired by a matter-dominated universe.
- The effective bulk viscosity \(\zeta_B/\zeta\) peaks at \(t/\tau_\Pi \sim 3\) and becomes negative for \(t/\tau_\Pi < 0.373\).
- The first-order non-hydrodynamic mode decays with instanton action \(A = t/\tau_\Pi\); no higher-order non-hydrodynamic modes (\(n \geq 2\)) exist.
- The deviation in bulk stress \(\delta \Pi\) decays exponentially as \(e^{-(t-t_{\rm in})/\tau_\Pi}\), while the deviation in energy density \(\delta E\) decays as a power law \((t_{\rm in}/t)^Q\) with \(Q = 3\alpha(c_s^2+1)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors, previously investigated in Bjorken flow (Heller 2013, Heller 2015) and Gubser flow (Behtash 2018), to the Hubble expansion relevant for cosmology and the primordial quark-gluon plasma. The authors note that Ref. Buchel (2016) studied entropy production in primordial QGP via holography and found that simple gradient expansion cannot describe it, motivating the resurgence approach. The results enable application of far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamics to cosmological fluid systems and cold atomic systems where expansion rates can be controlled.
