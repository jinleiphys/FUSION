# Properties of neutron stars and strangeness-mixed stars from a pion mean-field approach

> We investigate the properties of the static neutron stars and strangeness-mixed stars, based on the equations of state derived from a pion mean-field approach. Using the empirical data on the pion-nucleus scattering and bulk properties of nuclear matter, we have already fixed all the parameters in a previous work, where the nucleons and hyperons were shown to be modified in various nuclear medium. In the current work, we first examine the energy and pressure inside a neutron star. We show that the central densities in various neutron stars vary within the range of $(3-4)ρ_0$, where $ρ_0$ is the normal nuclear matter density. The mass-radius relations are obtained and discussed. As the slope parameter for neutron matter increases, the radii of the neutron stars increase with their masses fixed. We also study the strangeness-mixed stars or the hyperon stars using the same sets of the parameters. As the strangeness content of strange matter increases, the binding energy per nucleon is saturated and the corresponding equation of state becomes softened. Consequently, the central densities of the strangeness-mixed stars increase. Assuming that recently observed neutron stars are the strangeness-mixed ones, we find that the central densities increase.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the central densities of neutron stars vary within the range of (3-4)ρ₀, where ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density, but this result is restricted to densities below 4ρ₀ because the linear density approximation used in the framework would break causality at higher densities, and quark matter contributions are not included.

## Method

The authors use the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for static, spherically symmetric non-rotating neutron stars, with equations of state derived from a pion mean-field approach (chiral quark-soliton model). All dynamical parameters were previously fixed by reproducing bulk properties of nuclear matter and low-energy pion-nucleus scattering data, with four different parameter sets for the symmetry energy a_sym (30 or 32 MeV) and its slope parameter L_sym (50 or 60 MeV). Leptonic contributions from electrons and protons are included using a relativistic Fermi gas model under beta-stable and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Central densities of neutron stars vary within the range of (3-4)ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 nucleons per fm³ (approximately 2.7 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³)
- The symmetry energy at saturation a_sym is taken as 30 MeV or 32 MeV, and the slope parameter L_sym as 50 MeV or 60 MeV, based on empirical values of a_sym = (31.7 ± 3.2) MeV and L_sym = (58.7 ± 28.1) MeV from Oertel (2016)
- The incompressibility is fixed at K₀ = 240 MeV
- The electron and proton fractions are fixed at n_e = n_p = 0.1
- The pressure vanishes at approximately 11-12 km, corresponding to the neutron star radius
- The central density for observed neutron stars PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J1614+2330 is approximately λ ≈ 4 or ρ ≈ 0.64 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the pion mean-field approach previously developed by Ghim (2021, 2022) for medium modifications of baryons in nuclear matter to the study of neutron star properties. The results are compared with the APR predictions from Akmal (1998), which gave maximum masses of 2.2 M⊙ at λ ≈ 6, and with recent work by Brandes (2023) incorporating the black-widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 data, which estimated λ ≈ 4 for M = 2.1 M⊙. The approach enables testing the equation of state against observational data from pulsars such as PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432, and PSR J1614+2330, and can be extended to strangeness-mixed (hyperon) stars using the same parameter sets.
