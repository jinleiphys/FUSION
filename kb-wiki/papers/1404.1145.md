# Quasi-particle and collective magnetism: Rotation, pairing and blocking in high-K isomers

> For the first time, a wide range of collective magnetic g-factors g$_{\rm R}$, obtained from a novel analysis of experimental data for multi-quasiparticle configurations in high-K isomers, is shown to exhibit a striking systematic variation with the relative number of proton and neutron quasiparticles, N$_{\rm p}$ - N$_{\rm n}$. Using the principle of additivity, the quasi-particle contribution to magnetism in high-K isomers of Lu - Re, Z = 71 - 75, has been estimated. Based on these estimates, band-structure branching ratio data are used to explore the behaviour of the collective contribution as the number and proton/neutron nature (N$_{\rm p}$, N$_{\rm n}$), of the quasi-particle excitations, change. Basic ideas of pairing, its quenching by quasi-particle excitation and the consequent changes to moment of inertia and collective magnetism are discussed. Existing model calculations do not reproduce the observed g$_{\rm R}$ variation adequately. The paired superfluid system of nucleons in these nuclei, and their excitations, present properties of general physics interest. The new-found systematic behaviour of g$_{\rm R}$ in multi-quasi-particle excitations of this unique system, showing variation from close to zero for multi-neutron states to above 0.5 for multi-proton states, opens a fresh window on these effects and raises the important question of just which nucleons contribute to the `collective' properties of these nuclei.

## Key claim

For the first time, a wide range of collective magnetic g-factors g_R, extracted from experimental data for multi-quasiparticle configurations in high-K isomers, is shown to exhibit a systematic variation with the relative number of proton and neutron quasiparticles (N_p - N_n), though the authors note that existing model calculations do not reproduce the observed g_R variation adequately.

## Method

The authors used the principle of additivity to estimate the intrinsic quasiparticle g-factor g_K for multi-quasiparticle states in high-K isomers of nuclei from Z=71 (Lu) to Z=75 (Re), based on adopted single-quasiparticle g_K values derived from measured magnetic moments. They then extracted g_R values from published branching ratio data and the parameter |(g_K - g_R)/Q_0|, using a fixed intrinsic quadrupole moment Q_0 = 7.2 eb for most cases. The extracted g_R values were plotted against N_p - N_n, the difference between the numbers of proton and neutron quasiparticles in each isomer.

## Key numbers

* g_R values extracted from multi-quasiparticle bands vary from close to zero for multi-neutron states to above 0.5 for multi-proton states, with the higher values clearly above Z/A (of order 0.41).
* For the 7/2+[404] odd-proton excitation, the change in g_R relative to the even-even neighbor is +0.09(1) from band analysis, compared to +0.07 from moment-of-inertia estimates.
* For the 5/2-[512] odd-neutron excitation, the change in g_R is -0.16(3) from band analysis, compared to -0.04 from moment-of-inertia estimates.
* In 177Hf, specific g_R values include: 0.24(2) for the 7/2- (0 keV) state, 0.21(2) for the 9/2+ (321 keV) state, 0.30(3) for the 23/2+ (1335 keV) state, and 0.21(4) for the 37/2- (2790 keV) state.
* In 178Hf, the 2+_1 qp-vacuum state has g_R = 0.280(7), the 6+ state (1554 keV) has g_R = 0.43(6), and the 16+ state (2446 keV) has g_R = 0.27(2).

## Context

This work extends the earlier recognition by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) that g_R values for single-quasiparticle states should differ from their even-even neighbors, and provides the first systematic investigation of g_R variation across multi-quasiparticle configurations in high-K isomers. The findings open a new window on how pairing and superfluidity are modified by the blocking effects of specific multiple proton and neutron excitations, and the authors hope the results will stimulate new theoretical work to improve descriptions of the collective aspects of these nuclei, as well as more precise experimental spectroscopic studies and direct magnetic moment measurements.
