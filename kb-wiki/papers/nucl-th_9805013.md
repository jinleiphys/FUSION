# Deduction of the quantum numbers of low-lying states of 6-nucleon systems based on symmetry

> The inherent nodal structures of the wavefunctions of 6-nucleon systems have been investigated. The existence of a group of six low-lying states dominated by L=0 has been deduced. The spatial symmetries of these six states are found to be mainly {4,2} and {2,2,2}.

## Key claim

Based solely on symmetry considerations (not dynamics), the authors deduce that exactly six low-lying states of 6-nucleon systems should be dominated by L=0 components that are inherently nodeless in the two most important spatial domains (the regular octahedron and the regular centered-pentagon), and they predict the quantum numbers of these states; the claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis identifies only the dominant L=0 components, and states with L=1 or other origins may also appear in the low-lying spectrum.

## Method

The authors analyze the inherent nodal surfaces (INS) imposed on 6-nucleon wavefunctions by the combined constraints of rotation, inversion, and permutation symmetry at highly symmetric geometric configurations (regular octahedron and regular centered-pentagon). For each combination of total orbital angular momentum L, parity Pi, and spatial permutation symmetry lambda, they solve the homogeneous linear equations arising from the invariance of these configurations under specific combined operations (rotations plus cyclic permutations, space inversion plus particle interchanges). The existence or absence of nonzero solutions determines whether the wavefunction can access those configurations; only components that are nodeless at both the octahedron and the centered-pentagon are identified as the dominant building blocks of low-lying states.

- Six low-lying states dominated by L=0 components are predicted (Table 3).
- For the T=0 states of ^6Li: a J^Pi = 1^+ ground state at 0 MeV, a second J^Pi = 1^+ state at 5.65 MeV, a J^Pi = 3^+ state at 2.19 MeV, and a J^Pi = 2^+ state at 4.31 MeV (the latter not predicted by the symmetry analysis).
- For the T=1 states of ^6Li: a J^Pi = 0^+ state at 3.56 MeV (one partner identified, the other not yet found), and a J^Pi = 2^+ state at 5.37 MeV.
- The energy gap between the two J^Pi = 1^+ partner-states (T=0) is 5.65 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-based approach previously applied to 4-nucleon systems by Bao (1997) to the more complex 6-nucleon case, where the 15 spatial degrees of freedom make direct solution of the Schrodinger equation difficult. The analysis challenges the conventional shell-model interpretation of low-lying states in light nuclei: for example, the J^Pi = 3^+ state at 2.19 MeV in ^6Li is assigned S=3, L=0 here, whereas the shell model (Mayer and Jensen, 1955; deShalit and Talmi, 1963) would require S <= 1 and L >= 2 for that state. The results enable a qualitative understanding of the low-lying spectrum based purely on symmetry, and the authors suggest the method can be generalized to other few-body systems.
