# Time-Like Shock Hadronization of a Supercooled Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study the energy-momentum and baryonic number conservation laws for quark-gluon plasma discontinuity transitions into hadron matter states. We find that the time-like shock hadronization of a supercooled quark-gluon plasma (when the normal vector to the discontinuity hypersurface is time-like) should take place. We consider some properties of this process, which is different from the standard space-like shock hadronization.

# Time-Like Shock Hadronization of a Supercooled Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that when a supercooled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) undergoes a shock-like hadronization transition, the discontinuity hypersurface most probably has a time-like normal 4-vector (a "time-like shock"), rather than the standard space-like normal vector assumed in previous studies; this conclusion depends on the specific hydrodynamical expansion scenario, such as the Bjorken scaling model in 1+1 dimensions.

## Method

The authors apply the relativistic conservation laws for energy-momentum and baryonic number across a discontinuity hypersurface, using the Taub adiabat equation, to both space-like and time-like shock cases. They adopt a "cut-off" model for the QGP equation of state (with a momentum cutoff k_c = 900 MeV) and an excluded-volume model for hadronic matter (with pion and nucleon proper volumes of 1.63 fm^3), then compute the Taub adiabats for supercooled QGP initial states at fixed baryochemical potential μ_o = 1000 MeV and various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The minimal supercooling temperature for thermodynamically stable time-like shock hadronization at μ = 1000 MeV is 125.78 MeV.
- For zero baryonic density, the minimal supercooling temperature is only 6% smaller than the phase transition temperature.
- The proper volumes used for both pions and nucleons in the hadronic excluded-volume model are V*_π = V*_N = 1.63 fm^3 (corresponding to a radius of 0.73 fm).
- The momentum cutoff in the QGP cut-off model is k_c = 900 MeV, applied to both u,d-quarks and gluons.
- Example Taub adiabats are computed for supercooled QGP initial states with μ_o = 1000 MeV and temperatures T_o = 127.5 MeV, 124.0 MeV, and 122.25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of shock-like QGP hadronization previously considered in Refs. [4] and [5], which only examined space-like shock transitions (detonations and deflagrations). The authors show that in expanding systems such as the Bjorken scaling model (Bjorken, 1983), the hadronization hypersurface naturally has a time-like normal vector, leading to a qualitatively different shock solution. This enables future hydrodynamical calculations of QGP hadronization to correctly identify the shock type based on the space-time geometry of the expansion, rather than assuming the standard space-like shock framework.
