# Multiplicity, mean $p_T$, $p_T$-spectra and elliptic flow of identified particles in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC

> Israel-Stewart's causal theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, with the ADS/CFT lower limit of shear viscosity to entropy ratio ($η/s$=0.08), give consistent description of a number of experimental observables in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (c.m. energy $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV) \cite{Chaudhuri:2008sj}. Assuming that in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (c.m. energy $\sqrt{s}$=5.5 TeV), except for the initial temperature, other parameters of the fluid remain unchanged, we have predicted for the centrality dependence of multiplicity, mean $p_T$, $p_T$-spectra, elliptic flow. The central temperature of the fluid is adjusted to $T_i$=421 MeV such that in a Pb+Pb collision, with participant number $N_{part}$=350, average charge particle multiplicity is $\sim$ 900 and is consistent with the experimental trend observed at lower energies. Compare to Au+Au collisions at RHIC, in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, on the average, particle multiplicity increases by a factor of $\sim$1.6, the mean $p_T$ is increased by $\sim$10% only. The elliptic flow on the other hand decreases by $\sim$15%.

## Key claim

Assuming that in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (c.m. energy √s=5.5 TeV), except for the initial temperature, all other fluid parameters remain unchanged from those that successfully described Au+Au collisions at RHIC (√s=200 GeV), the author predicts that particle multiplicity increases by a factor of ~1.6, mean pT increases by ~10%, and elliptic flow decreases by ~15% compared to RHIC.

## Method

The author solves Israel-Stewart's 2nd-order causal theory of dissipative hydrodynamics in 2+1 dimensions (code "AZHYDRO-KOLKATA") for a baryon-free fluid with shear viscosity only, using the ADS/CFT lower limit η/s=0.08. The QGP fluid is initialized at τi=0.6 fm with Glauber model initial conditions (75% soft, 25% hard collisions), zero transverse fluid velocity, and boost-invariant shear stress tensor; the central entropy density is adjusted to Sini=180 fm⁻³ to reproduce an extrapolated charged particle multiplicity of ~900 for Npart=350. The freeze-out temperature is TF=130 MeV, and the equation of state (EOS-Q) has a first-order phase transition at Tc=164 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Central temperature of the fluid in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC: Ti=421 MeV (compared to Ti=357 MeV at RHIC, a ~20% increase)
- Average charged particle multiplicity for Npart=350 in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC: ~900 (extrapolated value ~927±70)
- Particle multiplicity increase from RHIC to LHC: factor of ~1.6
- Mean pT increase from RHIC to LHC: ~10% (e.g., in central collisions with Npart=350, for all species)
- Elliptic flow decrease from RHIC to LHC: ~15%
- Central entropy density in Pb+Pb collisions: Sini=180 fm⁻³ (compared to Sini=110 fm⁻³ at RHIC)
- Initial time: τi=0.6 fm; freeze-out temperature: TF=130 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio: η/s=0.08

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis of Au+Au collisions at RHIC using minimally viscous hydrodynamics (Chaudhuri, 2008) to predict observables at LHC energies. The predictions of reduced elliptic flow at LHC are consistent with results from a parton recombination model by Krieg and Bleicher (2007) and parton transport models by Molnar (2007). The results are intended to guide planning for future LHC experiments, with the caveat that predictions are valid only up to pT ~3 GeV, beyond which jet contributions become significant.
