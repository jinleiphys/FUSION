# Orbital and spin scissors modes in superfluid nuclei

> Nuclear scissors modes are considered in the frame of Wigner function moments method generalized to take into account spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations simultaneously. A new source of nuclear magnetism, connected with counter-rotation of spins up and down around the symmetry axis (hidden angular momenta), is discovered. Its inclusion into the theory allows one to improve substantially the agreement with experimental data in the description of energies and transition probabilities of scissors modes in rare earth nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that simultaneously including spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations in the Wigner Function Moments method substantially improves agreement with experimental data for energies and transition probabilities of scissors modes in rare earth nuclei, but this improvement depends on the newly discovered "hidden angular momenta" arising from counter-rotation of spin-up and spin-down nucleons around the symmetry axis.

## Method

The authors generalized the Wigner Function Moments method by solving the Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations including spin dynamics, using a model Hamiltonian with harmonic oscillator, spin-orbit potential, separable quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin residual interactions, and monopole pairing. They derived dynamical equations for collective variables by integrating the Wigner-transformed TDHFB equations over phase space with weights of position-momentum, position-position, and momentum-momentum tensors, then linearized the equations in small amplitude approximation to extract energies and B(M1) transition probabilities for both orbital and spin scissors modes.

## Key numbers

- Energies of scissors modes in rare earth nuclei were reproduced with approximately 10% accuracy when including both spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations.
- B(M1) values were reduced by about a factor of two with respect to non-superfluid values when pair correlations alone were included, but remained about two times too high compared to experiment.
- The experimentally observed group of peaks in the energy interval 2-4 MeV corresponds to two different types of motion: the orbital scissors mode and the spin scissors mode.
- Calculations were performed for 26 nuclei in the rare earth region.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner Function Moments method from earlier treatments that considered only orbital motion (Balbutsev and collaborators, 2010s) or only spin dynamics without pairing (Balbutsev and Molodtsova, 2013), by simultaneously incorporating spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations. The approach builds on previous TDHF-based calculations that reproduced scissors mode energies with approximately 10% accuracy but overestimated B(M1) values by a factor of two (Malov and Urban, 2000s). The discovery of hidden angular momenta from spin-up/spin-down counter-rotation provides a new mechanism to resolve this discrepancy and enables improved microscopic description of weakly collective magnetic dipole excitations in deformed nuclei.
