# Isospin Violations in the Pion-Nucleon System

> We examine the effect of isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants on low-energy pion-nucleon scattering. We compute the couplings in the context of a nonrelativistic quark model. The difference between the up and down constituent masses induces a coupling of the neutral pion to the proton that is slightly larger than the corresponding one for the neutron. This difference generates a large isospin-violating correction---proportional to the isospin-even contribution arising from the nucleon Born terms---to the charge-exchange ($π^{-}p \rightarrow π^{0}n$) amplitude. In contrast to the isospin-conserving case, this correction is not cancelled by $σ$-meson exchange; in our model there is no isospin-violating $NNσ$ coupling at $q^2=0$. As a result, we find a violation of the triangle identity consistent with the one reported by Gibbs, Ai, and Kaufmann from a recent analysis of pion-nucleon data.

## Key claim

Isospin-violating corrections to the neutral-pion–nucleon coupling constant, driven by the up–down constituent quark mass difference, produce a violation of the triangle identity in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering that is consistent with the value reported by Gibbs, Ai, and Kaufmann (1995), but only because the large isospin-even Born-term contribution is not cancelled by sigma-meson exchange (since the model yields no isospin-violating \(NN\sigma\) coupling at \(q^2=0\)).

## Method

The author computed the isospin-violating \(NN\pi^0\) coupling constant at \(q^2=0\) using a nonrelativistic quark model, evaluating matrix elements of a flavor-odd pseudoscalar quark current between nucleon states. The resulting coupling was inserted into the tree-level amplitudes of the linear sigma model (including nucleon Born terms and sigma-meson exchange) to modify the charge-exchange (\(\pi^- p \rightarrow \pi^0 n\)) amplitude. The triangle discrepancy \(D\) was then evaluated at threshold and at \(T_{\rm lab}=40\) MeV.

## Key numbers

*   Isospin-violating component of the \(NN\pi^0\) coupling constant: \(g^{\pi}_0 = 0.004\), derived from constituent quark masses \(m = 313\) MeV and \(\Delta m = 4.1\) MeV.
*   Triangle discrepancy at threshold: \(D = -0.0145\) fm.
*   Triangle discrepancy at \(T_{\rm lab}=40\) MeV (s-wave only): \(D = -0.014\) fm; p-wave contributions were much smaller (\(1.3\times 10^{-4}\) fm for \(1^{+}\) and \(-2.0\times 10^{-4}\) fm for \(1^{-}\)).
*   The result at 40 MeV agrees with the value reported by Gibbs, Ai, and Kaufmann (1995): \(D = -0.012 \pm 0.003\) fm (s-wave alone) or \(D = -0.011 \pm 0.003\) fm (s + p waves).
*   Predicted difference between mirror charge-exchange reactions: \(\widetilde{D} \approx -0.029\) fm, representing a 15% isospin violation relative to the charge-exchange scattering length \(a_0 = -0.19\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the author’s earlier study of isospin-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants in the nucleon-nucleon system (Gardner, Horowitz, and Piekarewicz, 1995) to the pion-nucleon system. It directly addresses the large triangle discrepancy observed by Gibbs, Ai, and Kaufmann (1995) in their analysis of low-energy \(\pi N\) data. The paper argues that, contrary to some expectations, \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing and other vector-meson mechanisms cannot explain the violation at \(q^2=0\) (citing Piekarewicz and Williams (1993) and O’Connell et al. (1994)), and instead identifies the \(NN\pi^0\) coupling as the dominant source. The result enables future experimental tests via the mirror reactions \(\pi^- p \rightarrow \pi^0 n\) and \(\pi^+ n \rightarrow \pi^0 p\).
