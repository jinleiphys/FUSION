# Particle emission following Coulomb excitation in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study nuclear reactions induced by virtual photons associated with Lorentz-boosted Coulomb fields of ultrarelativistic heavy ions. Evaporation, fission and multifragmentation mechanisms are included in a new RELDIS code, which describes the deexcitation of residual nuclei formed after single and double photon absorption in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. Partial cross sections for different dissociation channels, including the multiple neutron emission ones, are calculated and compared with data when available. Rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of nucleons, nuclear fragments and pions, produced electromagnetically, are also calculated. These results provide important information for designing large-rapidity detectors and zero-degree calorimeters at RHIC and LHC. The electromagnetic dissociation of nuclei imposes some constrains on the investigation of exotic particle production in gamma-gamma fusion reactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the RELDIS code can reliably describe electromagnetic dissociation (ED) of ultrarelativistic heavy ions across a wide energy range, but the authors acknowledge that the model's agreement with existing one- and two-neutron removal data is "not as good" as for three-neutron emission, and they consider this a "low limit for the accuracy" that should improve at higher collision energies.

## Method

The authors developed the RELDIS Monte Carlo code, which simulates single and double virtual photon absorption via the Weizsacker-Williams method, followed by an intranuclear cascade (INC) model for fast particle emission, and finally statistical deexcitation of the residual nucleus through evaporation, binary fission, or multifragmentation. Photoabsorption cross sections for Au and Pb nuclei were taken from experimental data sets, with branching ratios for partial channels calculated from the model rather than from experimental input, except for the non-statistical contribution in the one-neutron channel.

## Key numbers

- Total electromagnetic dissociation cross section for Pb+Pb at LHC: approximately 200 b
- Nuclear interaction cross section at LHC: approximately 7 b (ED rate expected to be 30 times higher)
- Mean neutron multiplicities: 4.1 at SPS, 7.2 at RHIC, and 8.8 at LHC energies
- Probability to emit more than 20 neutrons at RHIC: approximately 5%
- Double-photon absorption cross section for Au+Au: 226 mb, compared to single-photon cross section of 3546 mb
- Fraction of non-statistical contribution in the (gamma,n) channel: approximately 35% for Au and 20% for Pb
- Average excitation energy for photoabsorption on lead at E_gamma=70 MeV: 43.4 +/- 5 MeV
- Cross section for D+_s meson production at LHC: 0.144 mb, peaked at mid-rapidity
- Predicted dsigma/dy_lab for pi+ from gamma A process at |y_lab|=2: approximately 10 mb

## Context

This work extends the model of electromagnetic dissociation first introduced in Pshenichnov (1998) and builds upon the intranuclear cascade model of Iljinov (1996) for photonuclear reactions. The RELDIS code provides a "ready-to-use" event generator for simulating soft gamma A processes, which were previously unavailable, and addresses discrepancies found in earlier calculations by Aumann (1995) and Aumann (1996) regarding two-neutron removal cross sections. The results enable better design of zero-degree calorimeters and large-rapidity detectors at RHIC and LHC, and quantify the background from gamma A processes that will complicate studies of gamma-gamma fusion reactions for exotic particle production.
