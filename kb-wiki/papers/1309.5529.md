# SU(3) chiral dynamics and baryon-baryon interactions

> We calculate hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions at next-to-leading order in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory extending earlier work by the Bonn-Juelich group. The constructed potentials in momentum space include all one- and two-meson exchange terms generated by the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian. Effects from intermediate decuplet baryons are considered as well. These chiral baryon-baryon potentials, together with appropriate contact terms, provide a new basis for systematic studies of hyperon-nucleon scattering and light hypernuclei.

## Key claim
This paper constructs hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions at next-to-leading order in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory, extending earlier work by the Bonn-Juelich group. The abstract glosses over the fact that these potentials are to be supplemented by appropriate counter terms encoding unresolved short-distance dynamics, and that the full potentials must be inserted into a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation to achieve a good description of available scattering data.

## Method
The author calculated baryon-baryon potentials in momentum space up to next-to-leading order (chiral dimension nu=2) in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory, using the heavy-baryon formalism with a 1/M0 expansion before loop integrations. The potentials include all one- and two-meson exchange diagrams generated by the SU(3) chiral Lagrangian, with effects from intermediate decuplet baryons considered as well. The Feynman diagrams evaluated include planar box, crossed box, triangle, and football diagrams, with the power counting scheme of Weinberg used to order contributions.

## Key numbers
- The leading order (nu=0) potential is given by one-meson-exchange diagrams and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms; next-to-leading order (nu=2) includes higher order contact terms and two-meson-exchange diagrams.
- For the planar box diagram, the irreducible central potential V^box_irr,C(q) contains terms proportional to 5/3 q^2, 16(m1^2+m2^2), and logarithmic terms with ln(m1/m2) and ln(sqrt(m1 m2)/lambda).
- The triangle diagram with intermediate decuplet baryon contributes to the central potential with terms including 12 Delta^2, -(m1^2+m2^2), -13/6 q^2, and terms proportional to ln(sqrt(m1 m2)/lambda).
- For the hyperon-nucleon interaction with total isospin 1/2 and strangeness -1, the diagonal part Lambda N -> Lambda N requires evaluating 93 contributing Feynman diagrams.

## Context
This work extends earlier leading order (tree level) calculations of hyperon-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory by Polinder (2006) and the Bonn-Juelich group's prior work. The constructed potentials provide a new basis for systematic studies of hyperon-nucleon scattering and light hypernuclei, with applications to hypernuclei reviewed by Nogga and detailed scattering results presented by Haidenbauer. The nucleon-nucleon limits of the potentials reduce to results from Kaiser (1997) and Kaiser (1998).
