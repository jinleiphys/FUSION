# Kaon and Pion Ratio Probes of Jet Quenching in Nuclear Collisions

> Non-abelian energy loss in quark gluon plasmas is shown to lead to novel hadron ratio suppression patterns in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions. We apply GLV estimates for the gluon radiative energy loss. The K^-/K^+ and K^+/π^+ ratios are found to be most sensitive to the initial density of the plasma.

# Kaon and Pion Ratio Probes of Jet Quenching in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Non-abelian radiative energy loss in quark-gluon plasmas, calculated using the GLV opacity expansion with finite kinematic constraints, produces an approximately constant fractional energy loss over the RHIC-accessible energy range, leading to a weak pT-dependent suppression of single inclusive hadron spectra but strong, measurable suppression patterns in the K-/K+ and K+/pi+ ratios. The authors hedge that the fragmentation-dominated pQCD region will be reached only at higher pT (above approximately 6 GeV/c), and that in an intermediate pT region other particle production mechanisms (such as quark coalescence) dominate the K/pi ratios.

## Method

The authors performed leading-order perturbative QCD calculations of pion and kaon production in pp and Au+Au collisions, incorporating parton distribution functions (MRST98 LO), fragmentation functions (BKK95 LO), and intrinsic transverse momentum via a Gaussian distribution. For Au+Au collisions, they applied the GLV first-order opacity expression for radiative energy loss, shifting the jet energy before fragmentation by Delta E, and included nuclear shadowing and isospin asymmetry in the nuclear PDFs, using a slab geometry with average opacity values nbar = L/lambda_g = 1-4.

## Key numbers

- GLV fractional energy loss for gluon jets: Delta E_GLV/E approximately constant, scaling as (rho/10 fm^-3) * (L/6 fm)^2, for jet energies E = 2-10 GeV
- BDMS fractional energy loss for comparison: Delta E_BDMS/E approximately (40 GeV/E) * (L/6 fm)^2 for v-tilde = 2
- Transport coefficient used: mu^2/lambda_g = 0.25 GeV^2/fm, with screening scale mu = 0.5 GeV, alpha_s = 0.3, gluon mean free path lambda_g = 1 fm
- Opacity values at RHIC: L/lambda = 3-4, corresponding to gluon rapidity densities dN_g/dy = 1000-1200
- Measured K+/pi+ ratio in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV and pT = 2 GeV/c: 0.75 +/- 0.05
- Measured K-/pi- ratio in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV and pT = 2 GeV/c: 0.65 +/- 0.05
- Measured K-/K+ ratio in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV and low pT: approximately 1
- Antiproton/proton ratio at RHIC: constant at approximately 0.75 up to pT <= 6 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the GLV opacity series formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2001) for non-abelian energy loss to hadron ratio observables, contrasting with the BDMS approach (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1997-1998) which predicts a stronger energy dependence of fractional energy loss. The calculations show that the approximately constant fractional energy loss from GLV produces a structureless downward shift of single inclusive yields, but the K-/K+ and K+/pi+ ratios are predicted to drop dramatically in the few-GeV domain, providing a test of the nonlinearity of energy loss as a function of plasma thickness L. The authors note that experimental data at RHIC show anomalously high kaon-to-pion ratios at pT = 2 GeV/c that exceed their fragmentation-based calculations, indicating that quark coalescence/recombination models (Hwa, Yang; Fries, Muller, Bass, Nonaka, 2003-2004) are needed for the intermediate pT region, while their fragmentation approach should become valid for pT > 6 GeV/c to extract the opacity of the hot dense parton matter.
