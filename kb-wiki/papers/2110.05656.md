# The mean square radius of the neutron distribution in the relativistic and non-relativistic mean field models

> It is investigated why the root mean square radius of the point neutron distribution is smaller by about 0.1 fm in non-relativistic mean field models than in relativistic ones. The difference is shown to stem from the different values of the product of the effective mass and the strength of the one-body potential in the two frameworks. The values of those quantities are constrained by the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem. The neutron skin is not a simple function of the symmetry potential, but depends on the nucleon effective mass.

## Key claim

The 0.1 fm difference in the root-mean-square radius of the point neutron distribution between relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field models is shown to stem from different values of the product of the effective mass and the strength of the one-body potential in the two frameworks, with these quantities constrained by the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem.

## Method

The authors derive an analytical expression for the nuclear radius in terms of the effective mass and one-body potential strength using Woods-Saxon and harmonic oscillator potentials. They then compare the equations of motion of relativistic nonlinear σ-ω-ρ models with non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock models, showing they have the same structure. The Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem is extended to asymmetric nuclear matter in both frameworks to constrain the relationship between the effective mass and one-body potential.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square radius of the point neutron distribution in non-relativistic models is smaller by about 0.1 fm than in relativistic models for both ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the neutron skin δR = R_n - R_p is 0.275 fm in relativistic models and 0.162 fm in non-relativistic models
- Using NL3 for ⁴⁸Ca: R_n = 3.6050 fm, R_p = 3.3789 fm
- Using NL3 for ²⁰⁸Pb: R_n = 5.7405 fm, R_p = 5.4600 fm
- The effective mass in relativistic models is dominated by V_σ ≈ -380 MeV and V_ω ≈ 306 MeV around the center of ²⁰⁸Pb
- The harmonic oscillator fit to Woods-Saxon parameters yields ω = 8.49 MeV and V_H(0) = -54.80 MeV for V_ws = -50 MeV, R_ws = 5.8 fm, a_ws = 0.65 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous least squares analysis (Kurasawa and Suzuki, 2021) which showed that relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field models yield different neutron radii in ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb. The paper provides a theoretical explanation for this difference using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, which was originally proved for symmetric nuclear matter by Hugenholtz and Van Hove (1958). The results are important for neutron star physics and nuclear fission/fusion studies, where the 0.1 fm difference in neutron skin is significant.
