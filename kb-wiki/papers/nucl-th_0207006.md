# D mesons in matter and the in-medium properties of charmonium

> We study the changes in the partial decay widths of excited charmonium states into $D \bar{D}$, when the D meson mass decreases in nuclear matter, taking the internal structure of the hadrons into account. Calculations within the 3P0 model for $ψ(3686)$ and $ψ(3770)$ imply that naive estimates of the in-medium widths based only on phase space are grossly exaggerated. Due to nodes in the wave functions, these states may even become narrow at high densities, if the D meson mass is decreased by about 200 MeV. For the $χ$ states, we generally expect stronger modifications of the widths. The relevance of the $χ$ widths for $J/ψ$ suppression in heavy ion collision is discussed. These phenomena could be explored in experiments at the future accelerator facility at GSI.

# D mesons in matter and the in-medium properties of charmonium

## Key claim

The partial decay widths of excited charmonium states into $D\bar{D}$ do not increase monotonically with dropping D meson mass as naive phase-space estimates would suggest; instead, due to nodes in the quark wave functions, the widths of $\psi(3686)$ and $\psi(3770)$ can vanish at D meson mass shifts of about 200–250 MeV, and the states may even become narrow at high densities.

## Method

The authors model charmonium and D meson wave functions using a harmonic oscillator potential and compute partial decay widths into $D\bar{D}$ within the 3P0 model, which describes the creation of a quark–antiquark pair in a $^3P_0$ state. The overlap integral in the decay matrix element is evaluated as a function of the D meson momentum in the charmonium rest frame, allowing the oscillator parameters $\beta$ (for D mesons) and $r = \delta/\beta$ (ratio of charmonium to D meson oscillator strength) to be fixed by fitting the branching ratios of $\psi(4040)$ decays into $DD$, $DD^*$, and $D^*D^*$; the 3P0 coupling $\gamma$ is fixed by the vacuum width of $\psi(3770) \to D\bar{D}$.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator parameter for D mesons is $\beta = 0.3$ GeV; the ratio $r = \delta/\beta = 1.04$; the 3P0 coupling is $\gamma = 0.281$.
- For $\psi(3770)$ and $\psi(3686)$, the partial width into $D\bar{D}$ first grows to about 90 MeV for a D meson mass shift of about 50 MeV, then vanishes for a mass shift of about 200 MeV ($\psi(3770)$) and 250 MeV ($\psi(3686)$).
- For a D meson mass shift of 200–250 MeV, the naive constant-coupling estimate gives $\Gamma_{D\bar{D}}^\star(\psi(3770)) \sim 1.2$ GeV, compared to the 3P0 result which drops to zero.
- The additional width from $\psi(3686) + N \to \Lambda_c + \bar{D}$ scattering is $\Gamma_N \sim 4$ MeV at nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$ and $\sim 25$ MeV at $4\rho_0$ (for $p_\psi = 0$); for $p_\psi = 1$ GeV/$c$, $\Gamma_N \sim 6.9$ MeV at $\rho_0$ and $\sim 34$ MeV at $4\rho_0$.
- The expected mass shift of $J/\psi$ at nuclear matter density is $\delta m_{J/\psi} \sim -7$ MeV, corresponding to $\delta\beta = -5.6$ MeV (less than 2% relative shift).

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Le Yaouanc et al. (1977) that the effective $\psi D\bar{D}$ coupling varies rapidly with relative momentum and can vanish at nodes in the wave function, which explained the anomalous branching ratios of $\psi(4040)$. The authors apply this mechanism to the in-medium environment, where QCD sum rule calculations by Hayashigaki (2000) and Morath (2001) and the Quark Meson coupling model by Tsushima (1999) predict a D meson mass reduction of about 50 MeV at nuclear matter density. The results contradict the naive expectation that excited charmonium states would immediately dissolve when the $D\bar{D}$ threshold drops below their mass, as assumed in earlier discussions of stepwise $J/\psi$ suppression by Hayashigaki (2000), Digal (2001), and Wong (2001). The findings enable a more nuanced interpretation of $J/\psi$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions, particularly regarding the role of $\chi_{c2}(3556)$ whose monotonically increasing width could eliminate more than 10% of the $J/\psi$ yield from radiative decays, and suggest that measurements of dilepton spectra at the future GSI facility could probe these in-medium modifications.
