# Diquark bound states with a completely crossed ladder truncation

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation in the diquark channel is investigated by employing the Dyson-Schwinger method together with the Munczek-Nemirovsky model. The novelty of our study is a resummation of completely crossed ladder diagrams in the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. These diagrams are enhanced due to their color factors in the diquark channel, but not in the meson channel. In our analysis, diquark bound-state solutions exist in the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The authors show that, contrary to previous studies which found that beyond-rainbow-ladder contributions remove diquark bound-state solutions in the Bethe-Salpeter equation, a resummation of completely crossed ladder diagrams (which are enhanced by color factors in the diquark channel) can restore bound-state solutions, at least within the Munczek-Nemirovsky model.

## Method

The authors employ the Munczek-Nemirovsky model, a contact-interaction model where the gluon propagator is proportional to a delta function in momentum space, and solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar diquarks in the color-antisymmetric (anti-triplet) channel. They perform a coupling expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter kernel up to order \(G^6\) and then resum all completely crossed ladder diagrams using large-\(N\) counting as a truncation criterion, treating the recursion relation between successive orders via a 16-dimensional basis of Dirac matrices.

## Key numbers

* The pion mass squared is \(0.136\,\eta^2\) at order \(G\) (rainbow-ladder) and stabilizes to \(0.142\,\eta^2\) at orders \(G^3\) through \(G^6\), where \(\eta \simeq 1\) GeV sets the model scale.
* The rho meson mass squared is \(0.724\,\eta^2\) at order \(G\) and \(0.842\,\eta^2\) at order \(G^6\).
* The scalar diquark mass squared is \(1.14\,\eta^2\) at order \(G\) (rainbow-ladder), disappears at order \(G^2\), reappears at order \(G^3\) with mass squared \(0.88\,\eta^2\) (Scheme A), and shows multiple solutions at higher orders (e.g., mass squared values of \(0.86\,\eta^2\), \(1.09\,\eta^2\), and \(1.16\,\eta^2\) at order \(G^6\) in Scheme A).
* The current quark mass is set to \(m_0 = 0.012 \times \eta\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bender (1996), Hellstern (1997), Alkofer (2000), and Bender (2002), who found that a certain crossed ladder diagram gives a large repulsive contribution in the diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation, removing bound-state solutions that exist in the rainbow-ladder truncation of Cahill (1987) and Maris (2002). The authors point out that this repulsive effect is not limited to that single diagram but is common to all completely crossed ladder diagrams, and they show that resumming these diagrams can restore bound-state solutions. This result provides a counterexample to the previous claim that the Bethe-Salpeter equation has no diquark bound-state solutions, and it may inform future studies of diquark correlations in baryon models and exotic hadron phenomenology.
