# Vector meson masses in hot nuclear matter : the effect of quantum corrections

> The medium modification of vector meson masses is studied taking into account the quantum correction effects for the hot and dense hadronic matter. In the framework of Quantum Hadrodynamics, the quantum corrections from the baryon and scalar meson sectors were earlier computed using a nonperturbative variational approach through a realignment of the ground state with baryon-antibaryon and sigma meson condensates. The effect of such corrections was seen to lead to a softer equation of state giving rise to a lower value for the compressibility and, an increase in the in-medium baryonic masses than would be reached when such quantum effects are not taken into account. These quantum corrections arising from the scalar meson sector result in an increase in the masses of the vector mesons in the hot and dense matter, as compared to the situation when only the vacuum polarisation effects from the baryonic sector are taken into account.

# Vector meson masses in hot nuclear matter: the effect of quantum corrections

## Key claim

Quantum corrections from the scalar meson sector, computed via a nonperturbative variational approach with sigma meson condensates, increase the in-medium vector meson masses relative to the case where only vacuum polarization effects from the baryonic sector (relativistic Hartree approximation) are included. The effect is conditional on the inclusion of multi-loop, self-consistent quantum corrections from the sigma field beyond the one-loop approximation.

## Method

The authors use the Walecka model within a nonperturbative variational framework that realigns the ground state with baryon-antibaryon and sigma meson condensates, extended to finite temperature and density via thermofield dynamics. The vector meson self-energies are computed in the relativistic random phase approximation using the in-medium nucleon propagator modified by quantum corrections, with the effective nucleon mass and meson fields determined self-consistently through minimization of the thermodynamic potential. For the rho meson, a phenomenological subtraction procedure is employed due to non-renormalizability from tensor coupling, while the omega meson tensor coupling is neglected.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density at T=0, the omega meson mass drop from its vacuum value is approximately 150 MeV in relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA), reducing to about 117 MeV when sigma quantum corrections are included (with lambda_R=1.8 and C_v^2=96.45).
- For RHA, C_v^2 = g_omega^2 M_N^2 / m_omega^2 = 114.7; with quantum corrections from scalar mesons (lambda_R=1.8), C_v^2 = 96.45.
- The nucleon-rho vector coupling determined from symmetry energy a_sym=32.5 MeV gives g_rho = 6.82 for RHA and g_rho = 7.0 for lambda_R=1.8.
- At baryon density rho/rho_0=0.5, with tensor coupling approximately 6, the rho meson mass ratio M_rho*/M_rho is about 0.33 when g_rho is fixed from symmetry energy, compared to about 0.65 when g_rho is fixed from N-N forward scattering.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quantum corrections in nuclear matter using a nonperturbative variational approach (Mishra, 1999) and their generalization to hot nuclear matter (Mishra, 2000) and hyperon-rich dense matter (Mishra, 2000). It contrasts with the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991) which predicts dropping vector meson masses, and with QCD sum rule approaches (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Jin, 1995) that support such scaling. The results show that including sigma meson quantum corrections raises vector meson masses compared to the relativistic Hartree approximation (Chin, 1977; Hatsuda, 1993; Jean, 1994), and that the phi meson has smaller medium modification than the omega meson in hyperonic matter, consistent with earlier observations (Hatsuda, 1993; Pal, 1996). The findings are relevant for understanding dilepton production in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
