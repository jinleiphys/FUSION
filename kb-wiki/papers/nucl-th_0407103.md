# Effective field theory of the deuteron with dibaryon field

> Pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields is reexamined for observables involving the deuteron. The electromagnetic form factors of the deuteron and the total cross sections of radiative neutron capture on the proton, $np \to dγ$, are calculated. The low energy constants of vector(photon)-dibaryon-dibaryon vertices in the effective lagrangian are fixed primarily by the one-body vector(photon)-nucleon-nucleon interactions. This scheme for fixing the values of the low energy constants satisfactorily reproduces the results of the effective range theory. We also show that, by including higher order corrections, one can obtain results that are close to those of Argonne v18 potential model.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT) can reproduce deuteron electromagnetic observables as accurately as effective range theory and the Argonne v18 potential model, but only if the low-energy constants of the vector-dibaryon-dibaryon vertices are enhanced by an order of \(Q^{-1}\) relative to naive counting, because their naive contributions are anomalously large (40–50% of leading-order terms) and would otherwise imply slow convergence.

## Method

The authors constructed a pionless effective Lagrangian including dibaryon fields in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels, with strong-interaction low-energy constants fixed by effective-range parameters. They calculated the deuteron electromagnetic form factors (electric charge, magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole) and the total cross section for radiative neutron capture \(np \to d\gamma\) from Feynman diagrams up to next-to-next-to-leading order, and fixed the vector-dibaryon-dibaryon low-energy constants primarily from one-body vector-nucleon-nucleon interactions rather than from experimental data alone.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding momentum \(\gamma \simeq 45.7\) MeV and effective range \(\rho_d \simeq 1.764\) fm, giving expansion parameter \(\gamma \rho_d \simeq 0.4\).
* Charge radius from effective range theory: \(\sqrt{\langle r_{ch}^2\rangle^{ER}} = 1.985\) fm; empirical value is \(\sqrt{\langle r_{ch}^2\rangle} = 2.1303(10)\) fm.
* Leading-order electric form factor at \(q=0\): the \(Vdd\) vertex contribution is \(-40\%\) of the \(VNN\) vertex contribution.
* Magnetic moment of the deuteron: \(\mu_d = 0.8574382284(94)\); electric quadrupole moment: \(\mu_Q = 0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\).
* Low-energy constant \(L_2\) fixed from magnetic moment: \(L_2 = -0.4033\) fm; the leading-order part \(L_2^0 = -0.3880\) fm (3.7% difference).
* Low-energy constant \(L_2'\) fixed from magnetic moment: \(L_2' = -0.07096\) fm\(^{5/2}\), giving a 2.5% correction to the leading term.
* Low-energy constant \(C_Q\) fixed from quadrupole moment: \(C_Q = -1.709\) (units from context: fm\(^2\) or dimensionless, as given in the text).

## Context

This work extends the dibaryon effective field theory (dEFT) approach of Beane and Savage (2001) for the deuteron to electromagnetic observables, and contrasts with earlier pionless EFT calculations by Chen, Rupak, and Savage (1999) and with the KSW scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998). The proposed reordering of the low-energy constants makes dEFT converge as efficiently as other EFT formulations, and enables systematic inclusion of higher-order corrections that bring results close to those of the Argonne v18 potential model of Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995).
