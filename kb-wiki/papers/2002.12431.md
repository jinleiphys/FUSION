# Dalitz plots and lineshape of $a_1(1260)$ from a relativistic three-body unitary approach

> We formulate the final state interaction of the $a_1(1260)$ resonance decay in a manifestly three-body unitary parameterization and fit it to the $a_1(1260)$ lineshape measured by the ALEPH experiment. Dalitz plots calculated from this fit are presented. The work demonstrates the feasibility to numerically solve a previously derived amplitude and its generalization to isobars with spin and coupled channels. The model can also be applied to other meson decays and modified for the finite-volume problem as it arises in lattice QCD due to its manifest unitarity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates the feasibility of numerically solving a previously derived relativistic three-body unitary amplitude, generalized to isobars with spin and coupled channels, by fitting it to the $a_1(1260)$ lineshape measured by the ALEPH experiment; the approach is restricted to the $\rho\pi$ $S$- and $D$-wave channels, and the authors note that a detailed description of Dalitz plots would require additional channels beyond this truncation.

## Method

The authors formulate the final-state interaction of the $a_1(1260)$ resonance decay using a manifestly three-body unitary parameterization based on the isobar-spectator formalism of Mai (2017). They solve the Bethe-Salpeter type integral equation for the isobar-spectator amplitude numerically by discretizing momenta, projecting onto the $J=1$ partial wave in the $JLS$ basis (channels $101$ and $121$ for $\rho\pi$), and modeling the three-body force $C$ as a propagating bare $a_1(1260)$ with couplings $g_{fL}$. The production amplitude is then constructed including direct production and resonant terms, and fitted to the ALEPH $\tau^- \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\pi^- \nu_\tau$ lineshape data.

## Key numbers

* The $a_1(1260)$ lineshape data from the ALEPH experiment (Schael 2005) are used as the main experimental input for the fit.
* The isobar propagator $\tau$ is parametrized with a real-valued free parameter $m_\rho$ and coupling $g_1$, fitted together with the form-factor to two-body $\pi\pi$ phase-shift data from Protopopescu (1973) and Estabrooks (1974).
* The contact term $C_{L'L}$ includes a bare mass parameter $m_{\rm fit}$ and bare couplings $g_{fL}$, $g_{fL'}$ that are fixed to reproduce the $a_1(1260)$ lineshape.
* The production vertex $D_{L'}(p)$ contains free parameters $D_{fL'}$ for $L'=0,2$ and $D_{\tilde{f}}$ that are fit to the lineshape data.

## Context

This work extends the three-body unitary approach of Mai (2017), inspired by the work of Amado et al. (1969), to include isobars with spin and coupled channels, specifically for the $a_1(1260)$ meson decaying to $\rho\pi$. It builds on earlier non-perturbative treatments of the three-pion problem by Janssen (1993, 1994) but improves upon them by using an unstable $\rho$ isobar with proper imaginary part to maintain three-body unitarity, and by providing data fits to ALEPH lineshape data. The approach is closely related to the EBAC/ANL-Osaka framework of Kamano (2011) but with manifest unitarity proven in Mai (2017). The work enables future extensions to finite-volume calculations for lattice QCD, as demonstrated in Mai (2017) for a simpler spinless version, and can be applied to other meson decays.
