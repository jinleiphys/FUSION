# Large scale evaluation of beta-decay rates of r-process nuclei with the inclusion of first-forbidden transitions

> R-process nucleosynthesis models rely, by necessity, on nuclear structure models for input. Particularly important are beta-decay half-lives of neutron rich nuclei. At present only a single systematic calculation exists that provides values for all relevant nuclei making it difficult to test the sensitivity of nucleosynthesis models to this input. Additionally, even though there are indications that their contribution may be significant, the impact of first-forbidden transitions on decay rates has not been systematically studied within a consistent model. We use a fully self-consistent covariant density functional theory (CDFT) framework to provide a table of $β$-decay half-lives and $β$-delayed neutron emission probabilities, including first-forbidden transitions. We observe a significant contribution of the first-forbidden transitions to the total decay rate in nuclei far from the valley of stability. The experimental half-lives are in general well reproduced, both for even-even, odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, in particular for short-lived nuclei.

# Large scale evaluation of beta-decay rates of r-process nuclei with the inclusion of first-forbidden transitions

## Key claim

The authors provide a fully self-consistent covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculation of beta-decay half-lives and beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities for 5409 neutron-rich nuclei, including first-forbidden transitions on an equal footing with Gamow-Teller transitions. The results show that first-forbidden transitions contribute significantly to total decay rates in nuclei far from stability, but the model is only strictly applicable to even-even nuclei and is extended to odd-A and odd-odd nuclei with an approximation that cannot account for angular momentum mismatches between ground states.

## Method

The ground states of all nuclei are calculated using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the D3C* parametrization, and excited states are obtained within the proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) using the same density functional for consistency. The residual interaction in the particle-particle channel uses the Gogny D1S force for T=1 pairing and a Gaussian-based interaction for T=0 proton-neutron pairing, with the overall strength parameterized as a function of N-Z. For odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, the ground state is computed by constraining the particle number operator to an odd number, producing an "even" RHB state whose energy differs from the true odd ground state by the energy of the odd quasiparticle.

## Key numbers

- Half-lives, beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities, and average number of emitted neutrons calculated for **5409** neutron-rich nuclei with atomic numbers **8 ≤ Z ≤ 124**
- The weak axial coupling constant is quenched to an effective value of **g_A = -1.0** for both Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions
- The constant K for superallowed beta-decay is taken as **K = 6144 ± 2 s**
- The D3C* parametrization is used for the density-dependent couplings and meson masses
- The T=0 pairing strength parameterization uses V_L = **160.0 MeV**, V_D = **15.0 MeV**, a = **7.2**, and b = **-0.3**
- For nuclei in the range **36 ≤ Z ≤ 43**, the calculated half-lives agree with experimental data from Nishimura (2011) within a factor of **2**
- For recently measured 110 isotopes from rubidium to tin (N = 65 to N = 89), almost all values are within a factor of **2** from data, except for tin where half-lives are underestimated by a factor of **5**
- The mass difference between neutron and hydrogen atom is taken as ΔM_nH = **0.782 MeV**

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent RHB+pn-RQRPA framework previously applied to Gamow-Teller resonances (Paar, 2004; Marketin, 2012) and beta-decay half-lives (Niksic, 2005; Marketin, 2007) to include first-forbidden transitions systematically across the nuclear chart. It provides an alternative to the only existing large-scale calculation of beta-decay properties for r-process nuclei, which is based on the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) with a schematic QRPA interaction (Moeller, 2003). The results enable sensitivity studies of r-process nucleosynthesis models to beta-decay rates, which was previously impossible due to the existence of only a single data table. The interacting shell model (Caurier, 2005) has been extended to include first-forbidden transitions (Zhi, 2013) but is restricted to nuclei near closed shells due to computational cost.
