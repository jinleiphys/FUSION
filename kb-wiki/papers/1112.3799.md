# Differential cross section and analysing power of the pp -> {pp\}_s pi0 reaction at 353 MeV

> In order to establish links between p-wave pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions and low energy three-nucleon scattering, an extensive programme of experiments on pion production is currently underway at COSY-ANKE. The final proton pair is detected at very low excitation energy, leading to an S-wave diproton, denoted here as {pp}_s. We now report on measurements of the differential cross section and analysing power of the pol{p}p->{pp}_s pi^0$ reaction at 353 MeV. Both observables can be described in terms of s- and d-wave pion production and, by using the phase information from elastic pp scattering, unique solutions can be obtained for the corresponding amplitudes. This information is vital for the partial wave decomposition of the corresponding pn->{pp}_s pi^- reaction and hence for the extraction of the p-wave terms.

## Key claim

The differential cross section and analysing power of the polarized proton-proton to diproton-plus-neutral-pion reaction at 353 MeV can be described using only s- and d-wave pion production amplitudes, with no evidence for higher partial waves, provided that phase information from elastic pp scattering is used to resolve ambiguities in the partial wave decomposition.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the COSY-ANKE facility using a transversely polarized proton beam incident on a hydrogen cluster-jet target. Proton pairs from the reaction were detected in the forward detector system, and events with diproton excitation energy below 3 MeV were selected to isolate the S-wave diproton state. The differential cross section and analyzing power were extracted after correcting for detector acceptance, dead time, and relative luminosity, with the beam polarization determined from parallel measurements of elastic pp scattering and the pp to deuteron-plus-pion reaction using SAID parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 353 MeV; corresponding center-of-mass momentum p = 407 MeV/c; average pion momentum k ≈ 94 MeV/c over the 3 MeV diproton excitation energy range
- Differential cross section fit coefficients: a0 = 4.05 ± 0.08 μb/sr, a2 = −2.31 ± 0.14 μb/sr (no evidence for a4 term)
- Analyzing power fit coefficient: b2 = 1.82 ± 0.10 μb/sr (no evidence for b4 term)
- Extracted partial wave amplitudes: M_s^P = (55.3 ± 0.4) − (14.7 ± 0.1)i √(nb/sr); M_d^P = −(26.6 ± 1.1) − (8.6 ± 0.4)i √(nb/sr); M_d^F = (5.3 ± 2.3) √(nb/sr)
- Total luminosity: 544 ± 22 nb⁻¹; beam polarization: P = 0.68 ± 0.03
- Resolution in diproton excitation energy: σ(E_pp) better than 0.6 MeV; center-of-mass angular resolution better than 5°

## Context

This work extends the program of pion production measurements at COSY-ANKE that aims to link p-wave pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions to low-energy three-nucleon scattering within chiral perturbation theory. The results agree with earlier differential cross section measurements from the PROMICE-WASA collaboration at CELSIUS (BIL2001) at 360 MeV, but the ANKE data show no flattening near 90 degrees that would indicate significant F-wave contributions. The extracted s- and d-wave amplitudes are essential for the partial wave decomposition of the corresponding neutron-proton to diproton-plus-charged-pion reaction reported in a companion letter (DYM2011), and together with planned measurements of the spin correlation parameter A_x,z will constrain chiral effective field theory parameters linking pion production to the three-nucleon interaction (HAN2000, EPE2002).
