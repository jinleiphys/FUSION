# Decoherence in Sub-Systems of an Isolated System and the Disappearance of Quantum Multiverse

> This study was started to know mysterious classicality of nuclei. Using three particles model without external environments, it is found that decisions of respective state of three particles by decoherence are not simultaneous. Furthermore, in this model, wave function of total three body system collapses spontaneously without any external environments. Therefore we may able to insist that a wavefunction of our universe has already collapsed spontaneously without any external observer, because of the same mechanism with this model.

## Key claim

Using a three-particle model with no external environment, the author finds that decoherence decisions for each particle are not simultaneous and that the total three-body wavefunction collapses spontaneously, suggesting that the wavefunction of the universe may have already collapsed without any external observer.

## Method

The author models three particles coupled by springs with different angular frequencies (ω₁₂=0.305, ω₁₃=0.1, ω₂₃=0.202) and no external environment. Each particle starts in a Schrödinger cat state (superposition of two Gaussian wavepackets). The reduced density matrix for each particle is obtained by tracing out the other two particles, following a Caldeira-Leggett-type influence functional approach. Classical trajectories corresponding to the eight possible combinations of initial packet centers are computed and compared with the quantum decoherence times.

## Key numbers

- Particle-2 decoherence occurs between t=2.0 and t=5.0 (arbitrary time units), with interference starting to damp at t=3.505
- Particle-1 decoherence occurs between t=3.0 and t=6.0, with interference starting to damp at t=3.505
- Particle-3 decoherence occurs between t=4.0 and t=10.0, with interference starting to damp at t=5.505
- Spring frequencies: ω₁₂=0.305, ω₁₃=0.1, ω₂₃=0.202 (all particles have mass m=1.0, ℏ=1.0)
- Initial packet separations: d₁=-5.0, d₂=6.0, d₃=7.5

## Context

This work extends the author's previous paper (Ishikawa, 2015) which showed decoherence in a subsystem of an isolated three-particle system, and applies the Caldeira-Leggett technique (Caldeira and Leggett, 1981, 1983) to a finite system without an infinite reservoir. The result challenges the Everett many-worlds interpretation (Everett, 1957) by suggesting that a closed system with ergodic properties can undergo spontaneous wavefunction collapse, eliminating the need for external observers or environments to resolve quantum superpositions. This could enable a new understanding of classicality in finite quantum systems such as atomic nuclei, where semi-classical models like the liquid drop model and TDHF have been empirically successful.
