# Shear Viscosity and Electrical Conductivity of Rotating Nuclear Medium in Hadron Resonance Gas and Nambu-Jona Lasinio Models

> Motivated by recent observations of spin polarization and alignment in heavy-ion collisions, we study the impact of rotation on the transport properties of strongly interacting matter within kinetic theory in the relaxation time approximation. Our analysis focuses on the anisotropic shear viscosity--parallel ($╬Ě_{\parallel}$), perpendicular ($╬Ě_{\perp}$), and Hall ($╬Ě_{\times}$)--and electrical conductivity--$¤â_{\parallel}$, $¤â_{\perp}$, and $¤â_{\times}$--induced by the Coriolis force in a rotating medium. We employ two approaches: a combined quark-gluon plasma--hadron resonance gas (QGP--HRG) framework and a two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. In the QGP--HRG description, noninteracting HRG (massless partonic) degrees of freedom are used below (above) the transition temperature. In the NJL model, rotation enters through spinorial connections in the Lagrangian, and the constituent quark masses are obtained over the full temperature range. Rotation suppresses the chiral condensate and slightly enhances the transport coefficients for phenomenologically relevant angular velocities. Assuming a temperature-dependent angular velocity consistent with standard cooling, we find that $╬Ě_{||,\perp,\times}/s$ and $¤â_{\perp,\times}/T$ exhibit a valley-like temperature dependence, with reduced magnitudes compared to the isotropic $╬Ě/s$ and $¤â/T$ obtained without rotation. At zero net baryon density, rotation generates a sizable nondissipative Hall-like conductivity, unlike the case with magnetic fields where baryon and antibaryon contributions cancel.

## Key claim

Rotation induces anisotropic shear viscosity and electrical conductivity in strongly interacting matter, but the effects remain small for phenomenologically relevant angular velocities; the results depend on assuming a temperature-dependent angular velocity consistent with standard cooling, and the NJL model uses a fixed coupling constant rather than a rotation-dependent one that would reverse the chiral condensate trend seen in recent lattice QCD studies.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation in the relaxation time approximation within a rotating frame, using the rotating frame metric and connection coefficients to incorporate the Coriolis force while ignoring centrifugal effects (keeping only terms linear in angular velocity). They employ two frameworks: a combined QGP-HRG model using noninteracting HRG degrees of freedom below the transition temperature and massless partonic degrees of freedom above it, and a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model where rotation enters through spinorial connections in the Lagrangian and constituent quark masses are obtained over the full temperature range. ## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic studies of anisotropic shear viscosity (Aung 2023) and electrical conductivity (Dwibedi 2023) in rotating gases to a relativistic framework, and generalizes the relativistic rotating hadron gas conductivity calculation (Padhan 2024) to include both shear viscosity and the quark phase. The results contradict recent lattice QCD studies (Yang 2023, Braguta 2024) which found chiral condensate enhancement and confinement under real rotation, unlike the suppression seen in effective models; the authors note that a rotation-dependent running coupling in the NJL model (Jiang 2022) can reverse this trend, but they use a fixed coupling in the present work. The findings enable future studies of anisotropic transport in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions with finite vorticity.
