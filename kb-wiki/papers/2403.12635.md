# Compositeness of near-threshold $s$-wave resonances

> The near-threshold clustering phenomenon is well understood by the low-energy universality, for shallow bound states below the threshold. Nevertheless, the characteristics of resonances slightly above the threshold still lack thorough elucidation. We introduce a novel probabilistic interpretation scheme for complex compositeness of resonances, in which resonances with unphysically large decay widths are inherently excluded. Employing this scheme to analyze resonances via the effective range expansion, we demonstrate that near-threshold resonances have small composite fraction, in sharp contrast to shallow bound states below the threshold.

## Key claim

Near-threshold $s$-wave resonances are not composite dominant but rather elementary dominant, in sharp contrast to shallow bound states below threshold; this conclusion holds only for resonances with narrow decay widths that satisfy the interpretability condition $\Gamma \leq \text{Re }E$, while resonances with larger widths are classified as non-interpretable.

## Method

The authors analyze single-channel $s$-wave scattering using the effective range expansion (ERE) truncated at the $k^2$ term, expressing the scattering amplitude in terms of the scattering length $a_0$ and effective range $r_e$. They derive the compositeness $X$ of resonances from the weak-binding relation expressed in terms of the pole momentum $k^-$, obtaining $X = -i\tan\theta_k = -i\tan(\theta_E/2)$, where $\theta_E$ is the argument of the complex eigenenergy. To interpret this complex compositeness, they introduce a three-category probabilistic scheme with probabilities $\mathcal{X}$ (certainly composite), $\mathcal{Y}$ (uncertain), and $\mathcal{Z}$ (certainly elementary), related to $|X|$ and $|Z|$ via a parameter $\alpha$ chosen so that the boundary $\Gamma = \text{Re }E$ corresponds to $\mathcal{X}=0$.

## Key numbers

- The boundary between interpretable and non-interpretable resonances is set at $\Gamma = \text{Re }E$, corresponding to $\theta_E = \arctan(-1/2)$ in the complex energy plane.
- The parameter $\alpha_0$ that makes this boundary coincide with $\mathcal{X}=0$ is $\alpha_0 = (\sqrt{5}-1+\sqrt{10-4\sqrt{5}})/2 \approx 1.1318$.
- In the interpretable region, the elementary probability $\mathcal{Z}$ is $\gtrsim 0.8$ for near-threshold resonances, indicating elementary dominance.
- For the $f_0(980)$ meson, using the reported compositeness $X_{\bar{K}K} = 0.87 - 0.04i$, the scheme yields $\mathcal{X}=0.86$, $\mathcal{Y}=0.01$, and $\mathcal{Z}=0.13$, indicating $\bar{K}K$ molecule dominance.
- For $\Xi(1620)$ above the $K^-\Lambda$ threshold, the small compositeness $|g_{K^-\Lambda}^2 dG/dE| = 0.162$ from prior work is consistent with the elementary dominance found in this study.

## Context

This work extends the compositeness framework developed by Weinberg (1965) and later authors (Hyodo 2013, Kamiya 2015, Sekihara 2015, Matuschek 2020) for bound states to the case of resonances above threshold. It contradicts the expectation from low-energy universality that near-threshold states are composite dominant, showing instead that near-threshold resonances are elementary dominant. The probabilistic interpretation scheme is inspired by Berggren (1970) on classifying resonance contributions in nuclear reactions. The results enable quantitative analysis of exotic hadron structure, such as the $\Xi(1620)$ resonance studied via $K^-\Lambda$ correlation functions by Sarti (2023), and can be applied to any states with complex compositeness like $f_0(980)$.
