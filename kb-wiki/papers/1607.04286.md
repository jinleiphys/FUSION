# Radial vibrations of BPS skyrmions

> We study radial vibrations of spherically symmetric skyrmions in the BPS Skyrme model. Concretely, we numerically solve the linearised field equations for small fluctuations in a skyrmion background, both for linearly stable oscillations and for (unstable) resonances. This is complemented by numerical solutions of the full nonlinear system, which confirm all the results of the linear analysis. In all cases, the resulting fundamental excitation provides a rather accurate value for the Roper resonance, supporting the hypothesis that the BPS Skyrme model already gives a reasonable approximate description of this resonance. Further, for many potentials additional higher resonances appear, again in agreement with known experimental results.

## Key claim
The fundamental radial excitation frequency of BPS skyrmions provides a rather accurate value for the Roper resonance, supporting the hypothesis that the BPS Skyrme model already gives a reasonable approximate description of this resonance, but only for certain potentials where additional higher resonances also appear in agreement with experimental results.

## Method
The authors numerically solved the linearised field equations for small fluctuations in a spherically symmetric skyrmion background within the BPS Skyrme model, studying both linearly stable oscillations and unstable resonances. They complemented this linear analysis with numerical solutions of the full nonlinear system to confirm the linear results. For the BPS Skyrme model, they used an axially symmetric ansatz for general baryon number B that leads to spherically symmetric energy density, allowing study of radial vibrations for arbitrary B.

## Key numbers
- For the phi^4 model in 1+1 dimensions, the collective Derrick mode frequency squared is omega_c^2 = 12/(pi^2 - 6) approximately 3.101, compared to the true oscillational mode frequency squared omega_d^2 = 3
- For the sine-Gordon model in 1+1 dimensions, the collective excitation frequency squared is omega_c^2 = 12/pi^2 approximately 1.21585, which lies above the mass threshold m^2 = 1
- For the double sine-Gordon model with epsilon = 0.2, there exists a single oscillational mode with frequency omega = 1.2681
- For the massless case of the double sine-Gordon model (epsilon = -1/4), the Schrodinger potential has a resonance mode with frequency omega = 1.2664 + 0.4441 i

## Context
This work extends previous studies of vibrational modes in Skyrme models, particularly the BPS Skyrme model introduced by Adam et al. (2010) which has the BPS property of zero classical binding energies. The paper builds on prior analyses of radial vibrations in Skyrme models by Campbell et al. and Bizon, and complements numerical studies of resonance modes within the BPS Skyrme model for certain potentials by Arpad. The results enable improved description of nuclear excitation spectra, including the Roper resonance for B=1 skyrmions and potentially the giant monopole resonance for higher baryon numbers, while also allowing determination of the compression modulus of nuclear matter.
