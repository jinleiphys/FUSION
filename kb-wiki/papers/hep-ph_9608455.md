# Ghost poles and chiral symmetry in $πN$ scattering

> We solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in the vacuum using $π$, $σ$, and $ω$ mesons. For bare interaction vertices (Hartree-Fock approximation) we obtain a pair of complex-conjugated poles (ghost poles) and for vertices dressed by phenomenological form-factors these ghosts disappeared. We use these two different approaches to evaluate the scattering lengths and the phase shifts for the $πN$ scattering at threshold. Our results show that only when the form factors are present is possible to obtain a good agreement with the low-energy observables.

# Ghost poles and chiral symmetry in πN scattering

## Key claim

The authors show that solving the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator with bare interaction vertices (Hartree-Fock approximation) using π, σ, and ω mesons produces ghost poles that spoil agreement with low-energy πN observables; only when the vertices are dressed with phenomenological form factors that eliminate these ghost poles is it possible to obtain good agreement with experimental scattering lengths and phase shifts at threshold.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in vacuum using π, σ, and ω mesons within a linear σ-model augmented with the ω meson. They consider two cases: bare interaction vertices (Hartree-Fock approximation) and vertices dressed with phenomenological factorized form factors of the Pearce-Jennings type, using meson form factors from Gross, Van Orden and Holinde (1992) and nucleon form factors from Gross and Surya (1993). The dressed propagator is then used to evaluate the πN scattering amplitudes A± and B±, from which scattering lengths and phase shifts are computed and compared to experimental data and low-energy theorem predictions.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g²/4π = 14.6
- Omega-nucleon coupling constant: g_ω²/4π = 6.36
- Pion mass: m_π = 138.03 MeV
- Omega meson mass: m_ω = 783 MeV
- Sigma meson masses studied: 550, 770, 980 MeV and m_σ → ∞
- Nucleon cutoff mass for form factors: Λ_B = 1330 MeV
- Experimental scattering lengths (Olsson and Osypowski, 1975): a⁺ = -(0.021 ± 0.021) fm, a⁻ = 0.139 (+0.004/-0.010) fm
- With form factors and m_σ → ∞: a⁺ = -0.01686 fm, a⁻ = 0.6802 fm
- With form factors and m_σ = 550 MeV: a⁺ = -0.09516 fm, a⁻ = 0.6931 fm
- Without form factors and m_σ = 550 MeV: a⁺ = -2.0520 fm, a⁻ = 2.1790 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator by Krein, Nielsen, Puff and Wilets (1993) and Bracco, Eiras, Krein and Wilets (1994), which showed that ghost poles disappear when form factors are used. The authors incorporate chiral symmetry via the linear σ-model, building on the work of Lee (1972) and the nuclear matter calculations of Serot and Walecka (1986). The results demonstrate that while the linear σ-model with bare vertices gives good results for the bare nucleon propagator, the dressed propagator with ghost poles spoils this agreement; using form factors to eliminate ghosts restores qualitative agreement with low-energy observables. The work identifies the need for future inclusion of ρ and Δ resonances and σππ and ρππ vertices in the Schwinger-Dyson equation to improve the description of the isospin-antisymmetric scattering length a⁻.
