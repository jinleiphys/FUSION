# SU(3) centre vortex geometry at finite temperature

> The importance of examining the structure of centre-vortex matter in the ground-state fields of nonabelian gauge-field theory has been demonstrated in the recent centre-vortex based discovery of a second finite-temperature transition in QCD associated with quark deconfinement. This signals the presence of a new phase of ground-state field structure between the well separated chiral and deconfinement transitions. In this short presentation, we re-examine pure SU(3) gauge theory which provides a foundation for the development of techniques for the examination of full QCD. This time, we reconsider visualisations of the centre-vortex structure in light of the quantitative analysis that demonstrates the first order nature of the deconfinement phase transition in the pure-gauge theory. Here we consider a detailed side-by-side comparison of the field structure slightly below and slightly above the critical temperature. The abrupt changes of the field structure in the first order phase transition are easy to observe in the representative visualisations.

## Key claim

The paper presents side-by-side visualisations of centre-vortex structure in pure SU(3) gauge theory slightly below (T/T_c = 0.91) and slightly above (T/T_c = 1.22) the critical temperature, demonstrating that the abrupt changes in vortex matter structure observed in these visualisations correspond to the first-order nature of the deconfinement phase transition previously established through quantitative analysis in Mickley (2024).

## Method

The authors generate pure SU(3) gauge field configurations on a 32^3 × N_τ lattice with fixed isotropic lattice spacing a = 0.1 fm using the Iwasaki renormalisation-group improved action. Centre vortices are identified via Maximal Centre Gauge fixing followed by centre projection onto Z_3, with vortex locations identified by nontrivial plaquettes in the centre-projected field. Three-dimensional visualisations are constructed by taking temporal or spatial slices of the lattice, with the percolating cluster rendered in blue and secondary vortex clusters in contrasting colours.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1 fm, spatial volume: 32^3
- Below T_c: temporal extent N_τ = 8 gives T/T_c = 0.91
- Above T_c: temporal extent N_τ = 6 gives T/T_c = 1.22
- Vortex density in temporal slices drops from 1.8 fm^{-2} to 1.2 fm^{-2} across the transition (approximately 2/3 of the original density)
- Vortex density in spatial slices drops from 1.9 fm^{-2} to 0.6 fm^{-2} across the transition (approximately 2/3 drop)
- Branching point density drops from values approaching 2.9 fm^{-3} to 0.5 fm^{-3} across the transition (more than five-fold reduction)
- Above T_c, a single pair of branching points observed in a 6 × 32^2 volume slice corresponds to a density of 0.3 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the quantitative analysis of Mickley (2024) which demonstrated the first-order nature of the deconfinement phase transition in pure SU(3) gauge theory and discovered a second finite-temperature transition in full QCD associated with quark deconfinement. The visualisations build on centre-vortex visualisation techniques established by Biddle (2019) and the SU(3) cluster identification algorithm developed by Biddle (2023). The paper enables future studies to connect qualitative visual features of vortex matter (such as branching point clustering and secondary cluster suppression) to quantitative measures, and provides a foundation for developing techniques to examine centre-vortex structure in full QCD.
