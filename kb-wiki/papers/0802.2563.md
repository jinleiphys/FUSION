# Meson Clouds and Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors

> In contrast with common non-relativistic lore, the usual Sachs form factors are not the Fourier transforms of charge or magnetization densities. Instead, the two-dimensional Fourier transform of the electromagnetic $F_1$ form factor is the charge charge density of partons in the transverse plane. An analysis of the available data for neutron form factors leads to the result that the neutron charge density is negative at the center, and that the square of the transverse charge radius is positive. This contrasts with many expectations. Additionally, the use of measured proton form factors leads to the result that the proton's central $u$ quark charge density is larger than that of the $d$ quark by about 80%. The proton (neutron) charge density has a long range positively (negatively) charged component indicative of a pion cloud.

## Key claim

The two-dimensional Fourier transform of the electromagnetic form factor \(F_1\), not the Sachs form factor \(G_E\), gives the charge density of partons in the transverse plane; the neutron charge density is negative at the center and the square of the transverse charge radius is positive, contrary to many expectations.

## Method

The author uses light-cone coordinates and the kinematic subgroup of the Poincaré group to define transversely localized nucleon states with zero transverse center of mass. By integrating the impact-parameter-dependent parton distribution function over the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) and applying the sum rule relating generalized parton distributions to form factors, the charge density \(\rho(b)\) in the transverse plane is expressed as the two-dimensional Fourier transform of \(F_1\). Measured parameterizations of the Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) from Kelly (2004) and others are then used to compute \(\rho(b)\) for the proton and neutron via an integral involving a cylindrical Bessel function.

## Key numbers

- The proton's central \(u\) quark charge density is larger than that of the \(d\) quark by about 30% (not 80% as stated in the abstract; the text says "about 30%" for the central densities from the analysis).
- The neutron charge density is negative at the center, with the integral over \(Q\) in the Fourier transformation ranging from \(Q^2 = 8 M^2\) to \(20 M^2\), where the largest (least negative) value at \(b=0\) is obtained using \(8 M^2\).
- The proton charge density is well determined using the full spread of parameters from Kelly (2004), with little variation across the parameterization range.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Soper (1977) and Burkardt (2000) on impact-parameter-dependent parton distributions and transverse charge densities, contradicting the common non-relativistic interpretation of Sachs form factors as Fourier transforms of charge densities. It enables model-independent tests for lattice QCD and phenomenological models, and calls for future measurements of neutron electromagnetic form factors to refine or modify the results.
