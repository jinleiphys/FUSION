# Matching of nonthermal initial conditions and hydrodynamic stage in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A simple approach is proposed allowing actual calculations of the preequilibrium dynamics in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions to be performed for a far-from-equilibrium initial state. The method is based on the phenomenological macroscopic equations that describe the relaxation dynamics of the energy-momentum tensor and are motivated by Boltzmann kinetics in the relaxation-time approximation. It gives the possibility to match smoothly a nonthermal initial state to the hydrodynamics of the quark gluon plasma. The model contains two parameters, the duration of the prehydrodynamic stage and the initial value of the relaxation-time parameter, and allows one to assess the energy-momentum tensor at a supposed time of initialization of the hydrodynamics.

# Matching of nonthermal initial conditions and hydrodynamic stage in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes a phenomenological macroscopic method to smoothly connect a far-from-equilibrium initial state (such as that from the color glass condensate approach) to the hydrodynamic stage of quark-gluon plasma evolution, but the method relies on two free parameters (the duration of the prehydrodynamic stage and the initial relaxation-time parameter) and the approximation that the relaxation time depends only on proper time, which the authors acknowledge is a rough simplification of the true energy-momentum tensor dynamics.

## Method
The authors start from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation and derive an approximate formal solution for the distribution function that interpolates between free-streaming and local equilibrium. They then make the simplifying assumption that the relaxation-time parameter and the probability function depend only on proper time, leading to an expression for the energy-momentum tensor as a weighted sum of the free-streaming tensor and the hydrodynamic tensor. The evolution of the hydrodynamic tensor is governed by conservation equations with a source term from the free-streaming component, using a power-law ansatz for the probability function that depends on the two model parameters.

## Key numbers
- The formation time for the initial nonthermal state is estimated as τ₀ ≈ 0.1–0.3 fm/c for central collisions at midrapidity at LHC and RHIC energies, based on the CGC approach.
- The thermalization time scale is estimated as τ_th = 1–1.5 fm/c for LHC and RHIC heavy-ion collisions.
- The model requires the constraint (τ_th − τ₀)/τ_rel(τ₀) > 1 for the probability function to satisfy the boundary conditions at τ = τ_th.
- The thermalization time τ_th = 0.3 fm/c is noted as not necessary for data description in hydrodynamics, since transverse flows can develop at the prethermal stage.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to describe the preequilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions, specifically addressing the gap between the color glass condensate (CGC) initial state and hydrodynamic evolution. The authors note that viscous hydrodynamics (e.g., as developed by Heinz, Pratt, and Teaney) breaks down when τ_rel ∂_μ u^μ ∼ 1/2, which occurs at τ < 1 fm/c. The approach is similar in spirit to the work of Strickland (2007) on matching (0+1)-dimensional QGP preequilibrium dynamics to second-order viscous hydrodynamics, but generalizes to higher dimensions and arbitrary initial states. The method enables assessment of initial conditions for hydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution at RHIC and LHC energies.
