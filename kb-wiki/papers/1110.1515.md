# Effective mass splitting of neutron and proton and isospin emission in heavy-ion collisions

> Within the framework of an isospin and momentum dependent transport model, the emissions of isospin particles (nucleons and light clusters) squeezed out in heavy-ion collisions are investigated as probes of the poorly known symmetry energy at high baryon density. Two different mass splittings of neutrons and protons in nuclear medium as $m_{n}^{\ast}>m_{p}^{\ast}$ and $m_{n}^{\ast}<m_{p}^{\ast}$ are used in the model and their influence on the isospin emission in heavy-ion collisions is discussed thoroughly. The competition between the stiffness and the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential on reaction dynamics are compared and systematically analyzed. It is found that the difference of the neutron and proton directed flows and the transverse momentum distribution of the neutron/proton ratio are sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy, which can not be changed with the controversial effective mass splitting. The elliptic flows of free nucleons at high transverse momentum within mid-rapidity emission are a promising observable as distinguishing the nucleon effective mass splitting.

# Effective mass splitting of neutron and proton and isospin emission in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The elliptic flows of free nucleons at high transverse momentum within mid-rapidity emission are a promising observable for distinguishing the nucleon effective mass splitting (whether \(m_n^* > m_p^*\) or \(m_n^* < m_p^*\)), while the difference of neutron and proton directed flows and the transverse momentum distribution of the neutron/proton ratio are sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy and cannot be changed by the controversial effective mass splitting.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, an isospin- and momentum-dependent model, was used to simulate \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions at 400\(A\) MeV for near-central (b=1 fm) and semi-central (b=6 fm) impact parameters. Two different nucleon effective mass splittings (\(m_n^* > m_p^*\) and \(m_n^* < m_p^*\)) were implemented via a Skyrme-type momentum-dependent potential with parameter \(x = -0.65\) and \(x = 0.65\), respectively, and the local symmetry energy was varied with stiffness parameters \(\gamma_s = 0.5\) (soft), 1.0 (linear), 2.0 (hard), and a supersoft form. The emissions of free nucleons and light clusters (e.g., \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He) were analyzed via rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and Fourier flow coefficients (directed flow \(V_1\) and elliptic flow \(V_2\)).

## Key numbers
- The compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter was set to K = 230 MeV, with bulk parameters \(\alpha = -296.6\) MeV, \(\beta = 197\) MeV, and \(\gamma = 1.143\).
- The local symmetry energy at saturation density was fixed to 31.5 MeV for all stiffness cases.
- The momentum-dependent potential parameters were \(C_{mom} = 1.76\) MeV and \(\epsilon = 500\) c\(^2\)/GeV\(^2\), yielding an effective mass \(m^*/m = 0.75\) in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density.
- The parameter \(x\) was set to \(-0.65\) for \(m_n^* > m_p^*\) and \(+0.65\) for \(m_n^* < m_p^*\).
- The symmetry energy density dependence was parameterized as \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho) \approx 31.6 (\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma\) MeV with \(\gamma \approx 0.69\)–1.05 for densities between 0.1\(\rho_0\) and 1.2\(\rho_0\), citing Li (2008).

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies of the symmetry energy at high baryon density, particularly addressing the conflicting predictions of nuclear many-body theories: Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations predict \(m_n^* > m_p^*\) (e.g., Danielewicz 2005, Zuo 1999, Frick 2002), while relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with isovector mesons gives \(m_n^* < m_p^*\) (Li 2002, Baran 2005). The results are consistent with stochastic mean-field (SMF) calculations by Giordano (2010). The findings enable future experimental measurements of neutron/proton ratios and elliptic flow differences in heavy-ion collisions to constrain both the effective mass splitting and the high-density symmetry energy.
