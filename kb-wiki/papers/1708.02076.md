# Importance of initial and final state effects for azimuthal correlations in p+Pb collisions

> We investigate the relative importance of initial and final state effects on azimuthal correlations of gluons in low and high multiplicity p+Pb collisions. To achieve this, we couple Yang-Mills dynamics of pre-equilibrium gluon fields (IP-GLASMA) to a perturbative QCD based parton cascade for the final state evolution (BAMPS) on an event-by-event basis. We find that signatures of both the initial state correlations and final state interactions are seen in azimuthal correlation observables, such as $v_2\left\lbrace2PC\right\rbrace(p_T)$, their strength depending on the event multiplicity and transverse momentum. Initial state correlations dominate $v_2\left\lbrace2PC\right\rbrace(p_T)$ in low multiplicity events for transverse momenta $p_T>2~{\rm GeV}$. While final state interactions are dominant in high multiplicity events, initial state correlations affect $v_2\left\lbrace2PC\right\rbrace(p_T)$ for $p_T>2~{\rm GeV}$ as well as the pT integrated $v_2\left\lbrace2PC\right\rbrace$.

## Key claim

Initial state momentum correlations and final state interactions both contribute to azimuthal correlations in p+Pb collisions, with their relative importance depending on event multiplicity and transverse momentum; initial state correlations dominate at low multiplicity for pT > 2 GeV, while final state interactions dominate at high multiplicity but initial state correlations still affect v2 for pT > 2 GeV and the pT-integrated v2.

## Method

The authors coupled the IP-Glasma model (classical Yang-Mills dynamics of pre-equilibrium gluon fields) to the BAMPS parton cascade (perturbative QCD based Boltzmann transport) on an event-by-event basis for p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_pA) = 5.02 TeV. Initial gluon Wigner distributions were extracted from IP-Glasma at tau0 = 0.2 fm/c, then Gaussian-smeared to obtain positive semi-definite phase-space distributions for sampling individual gluons. The BAMPS simulation evolved the system from 0.2 fm/c to 2.0 fm/c, including 2-to-2 and 2-to-3 pQCD interactions with fixed alpha_s = 0.3 and dynamically computed screening masses, with the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect treated via a dynamically determined mean free path.

## Key numbers

- Initial state correlations dominate v2{2PC}(pT) in low multiplicity events for transverse momenta pT > 2 GeV
- In high multiplicity events (average dNg/dy = 26), final state interactions reduce the initial anisotropy at intermediate momenta (pT ~ 2-5 GeV) within the first 0.2 fm/c, then increase azimuthal anisotropy for all pT up to maximally 5%
- In low multiplicity events (average dNg/dy = 6), final state effects increase azimuthal anisotropy at low pT < 2 GeV to 2-3%
- Average number of scatterings: 4.5 +/- 1.1 in low multiplicity events, 5.6 +/- 1.1 in high multiplicity events
- Average number of large angle scatterings: 0.53 +/- 0.14 in low multiplicity events, 1.0 +/- 0.18 in high multiplicity events
- Relative effect of initial state correlations on final v2{2PC} is 25-50%, larger for low multiplicity events
- In the rand. azimuth scenario (no initial state correlations), final state interactions build up v2(pT) of ~4% at pT ~ 2 GeV in high multiplicity events and < 3% at pT ~ 1 GeV in low multiplicity events by t = 2.0 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether azimuthal correlations in small collision systems (p+p, p+A) arise from intrinsic momentum correlations or final state hydrodynamic/transport effects, as discussed by Dusling (2015) and Schlichting (2016). Previous calculations studied either initial state correlations (e.g., Schenke 2015, 2016 using IP-Glasma) or final state effects separately; this is the first study combining both in a single framework. The results enable differential studies across multiplicities and transverse momenta to disentangle initial state properties from early-time non-equilibrium dynamics, and suggest that multi-particle correlations beyond two particles could provide further insight, as explored in an Abelian model by Dusling (2017).
