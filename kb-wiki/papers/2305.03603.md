# Elasticity of neutron star mantle: improved compressible liquid drop model for cylindrical phases

> Neutron stars are the densest objects in the Universe. They have microscopically homogeneous core and heterogeneous crust. In particular, there may be a specific layer inside neutron stars, the mantle, which consists of substantially non-spherical nuclei immersed in a background of relativistic degenerate electrons and quasi-free neutrons. In this paper we reconsider transverse shear modulus for cylindrical phases of the mantle within the framework of compressible liquid drop model. We demonstrate that transverse shear affects the shape of nuclear clusters: their cross-section becomes elliptical. This effect reduces respective elastic constant. Using a simple model we perform all derivations analytically and obtain the expression for the transverse shear modulus, which can be useful for astrophysical applications.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that transverse shear in the cylindrical phases of a neutron star mantle causes the cross-section of nuclear clusters to become elliptical, which reduces the elastic constant compared to previous estimates. The authors derive an analytical expression for the transverse shear modulus within the compressible liquid drop model, but note that the result is based on a simple model and may require refinement for realistic astrophysical applications.

## Method

The authors reconsider the transverse shear modulus for cylindrical phases (rods and tubes) in the neutron star mantle using the compressible liquid drop model. They treat the nuclear clusters as elastic cylinders embedded in a background of relativistic degenerate electrons and quasi-free neutrons, and allow the cluster cross-section to deform from circular to elliptical under applied shear. All derivations are performed analytically by minimizing the total energy (surface, Coulomb, and elastic contributions) with respect to the deformation parameter.

## Key numbers

* The transverse shear modulus for cylindrical phases is reduced by a factor that depends on the ellipticity of the cluster cross-section; the reduction factor is derived analytically but no specific numerical value for the modulus is given in the truncated text.
* The paper does not provide explicit numerical results for the shear modulus in units such as erg/cm^3 or dyn/cm^2; the key result is the analytical expression for the shear modulus.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the elasticity of neutron star crusts, specifically the compressible liquid drop model applied to non-spherical nuclei in the mantle. Prior work by Pethick and Potekhin (1998) and by Chugunov and Horowitz (2010) calculated shear moduli for spherical and cylindrical phases but assumed fixed cylindrical shapes; the present paper shows that shape deformation under shear reduces the elastic constant. The results enable more accurate modeling of elastic deformations and oscillations in neutron stars, which are relevant for interpreting quasi-periodic oscillations in magnetar flares and gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers.
