# Transverse momentum fluctuations in ultrarelativistic Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions with wounded quarks

> We analyze the phenomenon of size-flow transmutation in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions in a model where the initial size fluctuations are driven by the wounded quarks and the collectivity is provided by viscous hydrodynamics. It is found that the model properly reproduces the data for the transverse momentum fluctuations measured for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76TeV by the ALICE Collaboration. The agreement holds for a remarkably wide range of centralities, from 0-5% up to 70-80%, and displays a departure from a simple scaling with $(dN_{\rm ch}/dη)^{1/2}$ in the form seen in the data. The overall agreement in the model with wounded quarks is significantly better than with nucleon participants. This feature joins the previously-found wounded quark multiplicity scaling in the argumentation in favor of subnucleonic degrees of freedom in the early dynamics. We also examine in detail the correlations between measures of the initial size and final average transverse momentum of hadrons. Predictions are made for the transverse momentum fluctuations in p+Pb collisions at 5.02TeV.

## Key claim

The wounded quark model combined with viscous hydrodynamics reproduces the ALICE data for transverse momentum fluctuations in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV across a wide centrality range (0-5% to 70-80%), with significantly better agreement than the nucleon participant model, which overshoots the data by about 50% at the highest multiplicities.

## Method

The authors generate initial-state entropy profiles using the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo implementation of the wounded quark model, then evolve them with 3+1-dimensional event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics (shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s=0.08, bulk viscosity to entropy ratio ζ/s=0.04) starting at τ0=0.6 fm/c. Hadronization is performed via the statistical Cooper-Frye formalism at freeze-out temperature Tf=150 MeV using THERMINATOR, with kinematic cuts matching the ALICE analysis (|η|<0.8, 0.15 < pT < 2 GeV). The transverse momentum fluctuation measure ⟨ΔpTΔpT⟩ is computed using the STAR Collaboration definition, which removes trivial fluctuations from finite multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN=2.76 TeV: the wounded quark model reproduces data from 0-5% to 70-80% centrality
- The nucleon participant model overshoots the data by about 50% at the highest multiplicities (most central collisions)
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at τ0=0.6 fm/c
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s=0.08, bulk viscosity to entropy ratio ζ/s=0.04
- Freeze-out temperature Tf=150 MeV
- The rescaled radius estimator uses α≃1/3 for maximum correlation with final transverse momentum
- In the wounded quark model, the anticorrelation coefficient between size estimator and ⟨pT⟩ is close to -1 for most central collisions, increasing to about -0.6 at 80% centrality

## Context

This work extends the size-flow transmutation analysis of Bozek (2012) from wounded nucleons to subnucleonic degrees of freedom (wounded quarks), motivated by the wounded quark multiplicity scaling found by Lacey (2016) and Bozek (2016). The results contradict the independent superposition model prediction of scaling with (dNch/dη)−1/2, as noted by the ALICE Collaboration (2014). The paper confirms the principal component analysis approach of Mazeliauskas and Teaney (2015) and provides predictions for p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV that can be tested in future data analyses.
