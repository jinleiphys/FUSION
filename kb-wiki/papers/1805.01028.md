# Eta Decay and Muonic Puzzles

> New physics motivated by muonic puzzles (proton radius and muon $g-2$ discrepancies) is studied. Using a light scalar boson $ϕ$, assuming Yukawa interactions, accounts for these muonic puzzles simultaneously. Our previous work limits the existence of such a scalar boson's mass $m_ϕ$ from about 160 keV to 60 MeV. We improve this result by including the influence of all of the possible particles that couple to the $ϕ$ in computing the decay rate. Doing this involves including the strong interaction physics, involving quarks, necessary to compute the $ηπϕ$ vertex function. The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, which accounts for the spontaneous symmetry breaking that yields the constituent mass is employed to represent the relevant strong-interaction physics. We use the $ηπϕ$ vertex function to reanalyze the electron beam dump experiments. The result is that the allowed range of $m_ϕ$ lies between about 160 keV and 3.5 MeV. This narrow range represents an inviting target for ruling out or discovering this scalar boson. A possible UV completion of our phenomenological model is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors improve their previous constraint on the mass of a hypothetical light scalar boson \(\phi\) (which could simultaneously explain the muonic proton radius and muon \(g-2\) discrepancies) by including strong-interaction physics via the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the \(\eta\pi\phi\) vertex function, thereby narrowing the allowed mass range from 160 keV–60 MeV down to 160 keV–3.5 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute the \(\eta\pi\phi\) vertex form factor using the NJL model, a chiral effective theory of QCD that incorporates dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, to account for the strong-interaction physics of quarks inside the mesons. They then use this vertex to reanalyze the production and decay of the scalar boson \(\phi\) in electron beam dump experiments and in \(\eta\) meson decays, specifically \(\eta\to\pi^0 e^+e^-\) and \(\eta\to\pi^0\gamma\gamma\), including contributions from \(\phi\) couplings to muons, up quarks, and down quarks in addition to electrons.

## Key numbers

* The allowed mass range for the scalar boson \(\phi\) is narrowed to between about 160 keV and 3.5 MeV (from a previous range of 160 keV to 60 MeV).
* The \(\eta\pi\phi\) form factor \(f(q^2)\) is computed to be \(f = 825^{+450}_{-275}\ \text{MeV}\).
* The total decay width of the \(\eta\) meson is \(\Gamma_{\eta,\text{total}} = 1.31 \pm 0.05\ \text{keV}\).
* The experimental constraint on \(\eta\to\pi^0 \mu^+\mu^-\) is \(\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^0 \mu^+\mu^-)/\Gamma_{\eta,\text{total}} < 5\times 10^{-6}\) at 90% CL.
* The experimental constraint on \(\eta\to\pi^0 e^+e^-\) is \(\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^0 e^+e^-)/\Gamma_{\eta,\text{total}} < 4\times 10^{-5}\) at 90% CL.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Liu et al., 2017) which considered a scalar boson coupling only to electrons and nucleons, by now including couplings to quarks and muons and using the NJL model to compute the necessary strong-interaction vertex. The new, narrower mass range provides a specific target for experimental searches, and the paper discusses a possible UV completion based on the "Dark Higgs" model of Chen et al. (2015). The results also address the possibility that the proton radius puzzle and muon \(g-2\) discrepancy could coexist with recent electronic hydrogen measurements (Beyer, 2017) that agree with the muonic hydrogen value.
