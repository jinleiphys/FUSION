# Quarkonium production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions: suppression vs. enhancement

> After a brief review of the various scenarios for quarkonium production in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions we focus on the ingredients and assumptions underlying the statistical hadronization model. We then confront model predictions for J/$ψ$ phase space distributions with the most recent data from the RHIC accelerator. Analysis of the rapidity dependence of the J/$ψ$ nuclear modification factor yields first evidence for the production of J/$ψ$ mesons at the phase boundary. We conclude with predictions for charmonium production at the LHC.

# Quarkonium production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions: suppression vs. enhancement

## Key claim
The rapidity dependence of the J/psi nuclear modification factor R_AA at RHIC energy provides first unambiguous evidence for statistical production of J/psi mesons at the phase boundary (chemical freeze-out), rather than from initial hard collisions followed by melting in the quark-gluon plasma. This conclusion depends sensitively on the magnitude and rapidity dependence of the open charm cross section, which has not yet been directly measured.

## Method
The author compares predictions of the statistical hadronization model to PHENIX data on J/psi yields and nuclear modification factors in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV. The model assumes all charm quarks are produced in initial hard collisions, all early-produced charmonia are completely destroyed in the QGP, and charmonia are formed only at chemical freeze-out (T_c) via statistical hadronization. The rate equation for charm quark annihilation in the plasma is evaluated to justify the assumption that charm quark number stays constant during plasma evolution.

## Key numbers
- In-medium width of J/psi in QGP: Gamma(T=300 MeV) = 320 MeV and Gamma(T=400 MeV) = 760 MeV, based on parton density n_p = 4.25 T^3 and J/psi-parton cross section sigma approx 2 mb.
- Rapidity density of initially produced charm quark pairs at top RHIC energy: dN_{c\bar{c}}/dy = 1.6.
- Gaussian width of J/psi rapidity distribution in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: sigma_y = 1.63 +/- 0.05 (chi^2/N_df = 4.5/8).
- Minimum volume for QGP creation in the model: 400 fm^3, corresponding to N_part approx 50.
- At LHC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=5.5 TeV), the charm production cross section is expected to be about an order of magnitude larger than at RHIC.
- For central LHC collisions, R_AA is predicted to exceed 1, with enhancement up to a factor of 2 if the charm cross section is a factor of 2 larger than current pQCD calculations.

## Context
This work extends the statistical hadronization model developed by Braun-Munzinger and collaborators (2003, 2004) and contrasts it with the original Debye screening/melting scenario proposed by Satz (1988) and with the kinetic recombination model of Thews et al. (2001) and Yan et al. (2005). The analysis of PHENIX data shows that the observed larger suppression away from midrapidity is opposite to predictions from both the melting model (Satz, 1988; Satz, 2000) and the comoving hadron destruction model (Gavin and Vogt, 1990; Capella, 1998), which would produce maximum suppression at midrapidity. The results enable predictions for J/psi production at the LHC that will be tested by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS in the first heavy ion run.
