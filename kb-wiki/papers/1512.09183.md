# The Constant-Sound-Speed parameterization for NJL models of quark matter in hybrid stars

> The discovery of pulsars as heavy as 2 solar masses has led astrophysicists to rethink the core compositions of neutron stars, ruling out many models for the nuclear equations of state (EoS). We explore the hybrid stars that occur when hadronic matter is treated in a relativistic mean-field approximation and quark matter is modeled by three-flavor local and non-local Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models with repulsive vector interactions. The NJL models typically yield equations of state that feature a first order transition to quark matter. Assuming that the quark-hadron surface tension is high enough to disfavour mixed phases, and restricting to EoSes that allow stars to reach 2 solar masses, we find that the appearance of the quark matter core either destabilizes the star immediately (this is typical for non-local NJL models) or leads to a very short hybrid star branch in the mass-radius relation (this is typical for local NJL models). Using the Constant-Sound-Speed parametrization we can see that the reason for the near-absence of hybrid stars is that the transition pressure is fairly high and the transition is strongly first order.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for the specific NJL models studied, the hadron-quark transition pressure is fairly high and the transition is strongly first-order, which causes hybrid stars with quark matter cores to either be immediately destabilized or to have only a very short hybrid star branch in the mass-radius relation, contrary to the expectation that such hybrid stars might naturally exist.

## Method

The authors combined a relativistic mean-field description of hadronic matter (using GM1 and NL3 parameter sets) with both local and non-local three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models of quark matter that include repulsive vector interactions. They assumed a sharp interface (Maxwell construction) between hadronic and quark matter, corresponding to a high surface tension, and used the Constant-Sound-Speed (CSS) parametrization to characterize the quark matter equation of state in terms of the transition pressure, the energy density discontinuity at the transition, and the squared speed of sound in quark matter.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of hybrid stars using NJL models (Klahn 2006, Bonanno 2011, Benic 2014) and the CSS parametrization (Alford 2013) to understand why NJL-based quark matter models produce very short or absent hybrid star branches. The results show that the NJL models studied are of the "high transition pressure" type, which previous CSS analysis (Alford 2015) had identified as tending to produce very small hybrid branches. The paper enables future work to focus on NJL parameter sets that might yield lower transition pressures or weaker first-order transitions if observable hybrid star signatures are to be found.
