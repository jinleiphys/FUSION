# Violation of the Spin Statistics Theorem and the Bose-Einstein Condensation of Particles with Half Integer Spin

> We consider the Bose condensation of particles with spin 1/2. The condensation is driven by an external magnetic field. Our work is motivated by ideas of quantum critical deconfinement and bosonic spinons in spin liquid states. We show that both the nature of the novel Bose condensate and the excitation spectrum are fundamentally different from that in the usual integer spin case. We predict two massive ("Higgs") excitations and two massless Goldstone excitations. One of the Goldstone excitations has a linear excitation spectrum and another has quadratic spectrum. This implies that the Bose condensate does not support superfluidity, the Landau criterion is essentially violated. We formulate a "smoking gun" criterion for searches of the novel Bose condensation.

# Violation of the Spin Statistics Theorem and the Bose-Einstein Condensation of Particles with Half Integer Spin

## Key claim

Bose condensation of spin-1/2 particles (spinons) driven by an external magnetic field produces a condensate with two gapless Goldstone modes, one with quadratic dispersion and one with linear dispersion, which fundamentally differs from conventional integer-spin Bose condensation and implies the condensate does not support superfluidity. The authors emphasize that their results are generic and decoupled from specific motivations, applying to bosonic spinons in solids or particles in vacuum that violate the spin-statistics theorem.

## Method

The authors construct an effective CP¹ Lagrangian for spin-1/2 bosonic spinons without a dynamic gauge field, motivated by quantum Monte Carlo results on the J-Q model that show no gauge field contribution. They analyze the classical condensate by minimizing the energy density in an external magnetic field, then compute the four excitation modes (two Goldstone, two Higgs) by solving the linearized Euler-Lagrange equation for fluctuations around the condensate. The spin polarization vectors of each mode are calculated to provide geometric interpretation of the excitations.

## Key numbers

- Condensate density: $z_0^\dagger z_0 = (\mathcal{B}^2 - m^2)/\alpha$, where $\mathcal{B} = 2S\mu_B B$ with $S=1/2$
- Ground state energy: $E_0 = -(\mathcal{B}^2 - m^2)^2/\alpha$
- Quadratic Goldstone mode dispersion at small $k$: $\omega_{1k} \approx k^2/(2\mathcal{B})$
- Linear Goldstone mode velocity: $c = \sqrt{(\mathcal{B}^2 - m^2)/(3\mathcal{B}^2 - m^2)}$
- Specific heat in 2D: $C = (1/\pi)\zeta(2)\mathcal{B} T$
- Specific heat in 3D: $C = (15/(8\sqrt{2}\pi^{3/2}))\zeta(5/2)\mathcal{B}^{3/2}T^{3/2}$
- In zero magnetic field quantum phase transition: three gapless Goldstone modes with $\omega = k$ and one gapped Higgs mode with $\omega_4 = \sqrt{2|m^2| + k^2}$

## Context

This work extends the deconfined quantum criticality (DQC) scenario proposed by Senthil et al. (2004) and the bosonic spinon concept from spin liquid theories by Wen (2002). It is directly motivated by quantum Monte Carlo simulations of the J-Q model by Sandvik (2007, 2011) and Melko et al., which demonstrated that bosonic quasiparticles in that model have spin S=1/2 and show precursor signs of Bose condensation. The results enable experimental or numerical identification of unconventional spinon Bose condensation via the predicted quadratic Goldstone mode and the associated temperature dependence of specific heat, which serves as a "smoking gun" criterion distinct from conventional magnon condensation (as studied in TlCuCl₃ by Tanaka, Ruegg, and collaborators).
