# Relative production rates of $^{6}$He, $^{9}$Be, $^{12}$C in astrophysical environments

> We assume an environment of neutrons and $α$-particles of given density and temperature where nuclear syntheses into $^{6}$He, $^{9}$Be and $^{12}$C are possible. We investigate the resulting relative abundance as a function of density and temperature. When the relative abundance of $α$-particles $Y_α$ is between 0.2 and 0.9, or larger than 0.9, the largest production is $^{9}$Be or $^{12}$C, respectively. When $Y_α<0.2$ $^{6}$He is mostly frequently produced for temperatures above about 2 GK whereas the $^{9}$Be production dominates at smaller temperatures.

# Relative production rates of $^{6}$He, $^{9}$Be, $^{12}$C in astrophysical environments

## Key claim

The relative production rates of $^{6}$He, $^{9}$Be, and $^{12}$C from neutrons and $\alpha$-particles depend on both the relative abundance of $\alpha$-particles ($Y_{\alpha}$) and temperature, but the dominance of $^{12}$C only occurs when $Y_{\alpha}$ exceeds about 0.99 (i.e., when $\alpha$-particles outnumber neutrons by roughly 100:1), not simply when $Y_{\alpha} > 0.9$ as the abstract states. The abstract's threshold of $Y_{\alpha} > 0.9$ for $^{12}$C dominance is a simplification; the full calculation shows $^{9}$Be still dominates up to $Y_{\alpha} \approx 0.99$.

## Method

The authors performed genuine three-body calculations of radiative capture reactions ($\alpha + n + n \rightarrow {}^{6}\text{He} + \gamma$, $\alpha + \alpha + n \rightarrow {}^{9}\text{Be} + \gamma$, and $\alpha + \alpha + \alpha \rightarrow {}^{12}\text{C} + \gamma$) using the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method with box boundary conditions to discretize the continuum. The photodissociation cross sections for the inverse processes were computed from electric multipole strength functions, then converted to production rates via detailed balance and Maxwell-Boltzmann averaging. The two-body interactions for $n$-$n$, $\alpha$-$n$, and $\alpha$-$\alpha$ were taken from Alv07 and Gar02.

## Key numbers

- The $^{9}$Be photodissociation cross section shows a narrow $5/2^-$ resonance at 0.9 MeV (populated only by M1 transitions, neglected in this calculation) and a $3/2^+$ resonance that is twice as wide as the experimental value, causing overestimation above 2 MeV.
- For $^{6}$He, the dipole strength function shows a sharp enhancement at low energies that is not seen in experiment; all calculations (Cob97, Dan98, Myo01) disagree with experimental data despite large error bars.
- The $^{9}$Be production rate is largest at low temperatures; the $^{6}$He rate matches $^{9}$Be at $T \approx 8$ GK; $^{12}$C production is much smaller in the 0.1–10 GK range.
- The transition from $^{9}$Be to $^{6}$He dominance occurs at $T \approx 2.5$ GK when $Y_{\alpha} = 0.1$; for $Y_{\alpha} > 0.3$, $^{9}$Be dominates at all temperatures.
- $^{12}$C production exceeds the others only when $Y_{\alpha} > 0.99$ (fewer than 1 neutron per 100 $\alpha$-particles).
- The $^{6}$He rate from the present three-body calculation is about three orders of magnitude larger than the sequential capture calculation of Gor95, and about one order of magnitude smaller than the dineutron-capture calculation of Bar06.

## Context

This work extends previous sequential-capture approaches (Efr96, Sum02, Bar06) by performing full three-body calculations without assuming a two-step mechanism, which is important because the lifetimes of intermediate resonances ($^{5}$He, $^{8}$Be) are comparable to or shorter than the reaction time. The results agree well with the triple-$\alpha$ rate of Ang99 for $^{12}$C production but show no indication of the huge (20-order-of-magnitude) enhancement at very low temperatures reported by Oga09 for the triple-$\alpha$ process. The computed relative production rates provide initial conditions for future four-body calculations of heavier-nucleus formation sequences.
