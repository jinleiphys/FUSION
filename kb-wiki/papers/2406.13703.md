# Working group 1 summary: $V_{ud}$, $V_{us}$, $V_{cd}$, $V_{cs}$ and semileptonic/leptonic $D$ decays

> We summarize the program of working group 1 at the 12th Workshop on the CKM Unitarity Triangle, whose main subjects covered $V_{ud}$, $V_{us}$, and first-row unitarity as well as $V_{cd}$, $V_{cs}$, and (semi-)leptonic $D$ decays.

## Key claim

The first-row CKM unitarity test shows a persistent tension of about 2.8 sigma (global fit deficit of -0.0018(6)), but this tension arises not only between data and unitarity but also among different kaon decay constraints themselves, with the K_ell2-K_ell3 combination alone showing a 2.6 sigma deficit.

## Method

The working group reviewed experimental and theoretical inputs for extracting V_ud from superallowed beta decays, neutron decay, and pion beta decay, and V_us from kaon semileptonic (K_ell3) and leptonic (K_ell2) decays, including updated radiative corrections in a dispersive framework and chiral perturbation theory. For V_cd and V_cs, the group examined new measurements from BESIII of leptonic D_s decays and semileptonic D decays, combined with new lattice QCD calculations of decay constants and form factors from Fermilab-MILC and HPQCD, as well as progress on inclusive semileptonic charm decays using lattice QCD and heavy quark effective theory.

## Key numbers

- First-row unitarity global fit deficit: Delta_CKM = -0.0018(6), corresponding to 2.8 sigma tension
- K_ell2-K_ell3 combination deficit: Delta_CKM = -0.016(6), corresponding to 2.6 sigma tension
- K_ell2-beta combination deficit: Delta_CKM = -0.0010(6), corresponding to 1.7 sigma tension
- K_ell3-beta combination deficit: Delta_CKM = -0.0018(6), corresponding to 3.1 sigma tension
- BESIII measurement of Br[K_S -> pi e nu] = 7.153(37)_stat(44)_syst x 10^{-4}
- Updated BESIII determination of |V_cs| = 0.9774(56)(72) from leptonic D_s decays
- For D_s inclusive semileptonic decays, difference between inclusive branching fraction and sum of exclusive modes: -0.04(13)(20)%

## Context

This working group summary from the 12th Workshop on the CKM Unitarity Triangle (Santiago de Compostela, September 2023) updates the status reported by the Particle Data Group (2022) and extends the analysis framework of Cirigliano et al. (2022). The tensions in first-row unitarity motivate new measurements such as a K_mu3/K_mu2 ratio at NA62 (Anzivino et al., 2023) and the PIONEER experiment's pion beta decay program (PIONEER collaboration, 2022). For charm decays, the new Fermilab-MILC (2022) and HPQCD (2021) lattice calculations enable sub-percent precision on |V_cs|, while inclusive charm decay studies on the lattice (Kellermann et al., 2022) open a new route complementary to the exclusive approach used in the beauty sector.
