# Rapidity scan in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=72$~GeV using a viscous hydro + cascade model

> In this note we discuss the rapidity dependence of the initial and final conditions for hydrodynamic evolution as well as the resulting basic hadronic observables in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=72$ GeV in the framework of a viscous hydro+cascade model vHLLE+UrQMD. The resulting rapidity dependences are driven to a big extent by the initial state, which is simulated with the UrQMD cascade. The results can serve as a prediction for future experiments such as the AFTER@LHC or the BES-II program at STAR.

# Rapidity scan in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=72$ GeV using a viscous hydro + cascade model

## Key claim
The rapidity dependence of hadronic observables in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=72$ GeV is driven largely by the initial state from the UrQMD cascade, and different rapidity slices of the system cover different regions of the $T-\mu_{\rm B}$ plane during their evolution, offering a "rapidity scan" as a complementary tool to the collision energy scan for studying the QCD phase diagram. The results are presented as predictions for future experiments such as AFTER@LHC or the BES-II program at STAR, with the caveat that the net proton distribution's dip at midrapidity is not reproduced well due to the baryon charge distribution from the UrQMD initial state.

## Method
The author uses a viscous hydrodynamics + cascade model (vHLLE+UrQMD) to simulate PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=72$ GeV. The initial stage is generated with the hadron/string cascade UrQMD, and at a constant proper time $\tau_0 = 0.64$ fm/c the system is fluidized onto a 3D hydrodynamic grid with nonzero baryon, electric charge, and strangeness densities. The hydrodynamic evolution is performed with the vHLLE code, and when the energy density reaches $\epsilon_{\rm sw} = 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$, particlization is performed via the Cooper-Frye prescription, followed by hadronic rescatterings and resonance decays in UrQMD.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 72$ GeV for PbPb collisions.
- Initial fluidization time: $\tau_0 = 0.64$ fm/c.
- Switching energy density for particlization: $\epsilon_{\rm sw} = 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$.
- Centrality classes studied: 0-5% central for rapidity distributions of identified hadrons and net protons; 20-30% central for elliptic flow and phase diagram trajectories.
- The elliptic flow of all charged hadrons exhibits peaks around pseudorapidity $\eta = \pm 2$.
- The peak value of the net proton distribution is close to the experimental value from BRAHMS at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 62.4$ GeV, but the dip at midrapidity is not reproduced.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling approach previously applied to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) program, using the same "universal" particlization criterion ($\epsilon_{\rm sw}=0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$) from Karpenko (2015) to perform a rapidity scan at a fixed collision energy rather than a beam energy scan. The initial baryon density profile differs from the dynamical initial state model of Shen (2017). The results serve as predictions for the AFTER@LHC fixed-target program (Brodsky 2012, Trzeciak 2017, Kikola 2017, Massacrier 2017) and the STAR BES Phase II, and the finite angular momentum in the initial state is noted as relevant for global $\Lambda$ polarization studies (Karpenko 2016).
