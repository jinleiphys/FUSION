# Freeze-out of Strange Hadron in pp, pPb and PbPb Collisions at LHC Energies

> In this article, we will present a systematic analysis of transverse momentum spectra of the strange hadron in different multiplicity events produced in pp collision at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV, pPb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV and PbPb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. The differential freeze out scenario of strange hadron $K^{0}_{s}$ assumed while analyzing the data using a Tsallis distribution which is modified to include transverse flow. The $p_{T}$ distributions of strange hadron in different systems are characterized in terms of the parameters namely, Tsallis temperature ($T$), power ($n$) and average transverse flow velocity ($β$).

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified Tsallis distribution incorporating transverse flow can describe the transverse momentum spectra of the strange hadron \(K^{0}_{s}\) across pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at LHC energies, but the abstract glosses over the finding that the Tsallis temperature \(T\) does not show a noticeable change with event multiplicity for the PbPb system, unlike the pp and pPb systems.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic analysis of the transverse momentum (\(p_{T}\)) spectra of \(K^{0}_{s}\) mesons measured by the CMS experiment in pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}\) = 7 TeV, pPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 5.02 TeV, and PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 TeV, for different multiplicity classes in the mid-rapidity region \(|y_{\rm{CM}}| < 1\). They fitted the spectra using a modified Tsallis distribution function that includes parameters for Tsallis temperature (\(T\)), power (\(n\)), and average transverse flow velocity (\(\beta\)), assuming a differential freeze-out scenario.

## Key numbers

*   The analysis covers pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}\) = 7 TeV, pPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 5.02 TeV, and PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 2.76 TeV.
*   The Tsallis parameter \(n\) decreases with multiplicity in pp collisions but remains similar in pPb and PbPb collisions.
*   The Tsallis temperature \(T\) increases with multiplicity in pp and pPb collisions but varies little with multiplicity in PbPb collisions.
*   The transverse flow \(\beta\) increases with multiplicity in all three systems (pp, pPb, and PbPb).
*   The PbPb system has smaller temperature and transverse flow compared to the pp and pPb systems.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological study of strange hadron production using a Tsallis distribution with flow, as previously applied by Khandai et al. (2013), to a systematic comparison across different collision systems and multiplicities at LHC energies. The analysis uses data from the CMS experiment (Khachatryan et al., 2016) and enables a deeper understanding of thermalization and collective effects in small (pp, pPb) and large (PbPb) systems. The results suggest that the degree of thermalization, as indicated by the parameter \(n\), remains similar across multiplicity classes in all three systems.
