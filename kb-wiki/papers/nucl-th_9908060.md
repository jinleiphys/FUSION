# High-spin yrast structure of 32S suggested by symmetry-unrestricted, cranked Hartree-Fock calculations

> High-spin yrast structure of 32S is investigated by means of the cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method in the three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation without imposing restrictions on spatial symmetries. The result suggests that 1) a crossover from the superdeformed to the hyperdeformed-like configurations takes place on the yrast line at angular momentum $I \simeq 24$, which corresponds to the ``band termination'' point in the cranked harmonic-oscillator model, and 2) non-axial octupole deformations of the $Y_{31}$ type play an important role in the yrast states in the range $5\leq I \leq 13$.

# High-spin yrast structure of 32S suggested by symmetry-unrestricted, cranked Hartree-Fock calculations

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a crossover from superdeformed to hyperdeformed-like configurations occurs on the yrast line of 32S at angular momentum I ≈ 24, and that non-axial octupole deformations of the Y31 type play an important role in yrast states between I=5 and I=13. These results are obtained from cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations that remove restrictions on spatial symmetries, but the authors note that the hyperdeformed-like configuration is unstable against fission for I ≥ 34 and that the Y31 potential is shallow enough that zero-point vibrational motion may exceed the equilibrium deformation.

## Method

The cranked Hartree-Fock equation was solved using the Skyrme SIII and SkM* forces in a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation (mesh size 1 fm, sphere radius 8 fm) without imposing parity or signature symmetries on the individual wave functions. The imaginary-time evolution technique was used to solve the cranked HF equation, with quadratic constraints applied to enforce center-of-mass and principal-axis conditions. Calculations were performed in steps of Δω = 0.2 MeV/ℏ, and pairing correlations were neglected.

## Key numbers

- Angular momentum I ≈ 24: the crossover point from superdeformed to hyperdeformed-like configurations on the yrast line, corresponding to the "band termination" point in the cranked harmonic-oscillator model.
- Rotational frequency ω ≈ 2.9 MeV/ℏ: the point at which a jump occurs in both angular momentum (from about 22 to 26) and quadrupole deformation β2 (from about 0.6 to 0.7).
- Quadrupole deformation β2 ≈ 0.6 for the superdeformed band below I ≈ 24, increasing to β2 ≈ 0.7 for the hyperdeformed-like configuration above I ≈ 24.
- Y31 deformation region: yrast states with 5 ≤ I ≤ 13 possess non-axial octupole deformations of the Y31 type, with α31 values rising quickly when ω exceeds 1 MeV/ℏ.
- Excitation energy of the superdeformed state at I=0: about 12 MeV.
- Maximum angular momentum for the SD configuration from j-j coupling shell model: 24ℏ, corresponding to the 4p-12h configuration π[(f7/2)²(sd)⁻⁶] ⊗ ν[(f7/2)²(sd)⁻⁶] relative to ⁴⁰Ca.
- Critical ratio αc = (√27 + √2)/(√27 - √2) ≈ 1.75 for the CHO model, below which configurations reach the oblate limit; the SD configuration has Σ₃/Σ₂ = 2 > αc.
- Dynamical moment of inertia J⁽²⁾ shows a singular behavior near the band crossing point at ω ≈ 2.9 MeV/ℏ.
- Time-odd component contributions to the energy at ω = 1.0 MeV/ℏ: total -1.04 MeV (SIII) and -0.95 MeV (SkM*), with current-density terms (B₃+B₄) dominating at -1.15 MeV (SIII) and -0.93 MeV (SkM*).
- Effective mass m*/m: 0.76 for SIII, 0.79 for SkM*.

## Context

This work extends previous cranked HF calculations on 32S by Flocard et al. (1984) using the BKN interaction, which assumed parity and signature symmetries, by removing those restrictions to explore exotic shapes. The results are compared with the cranked harmonic-oscillator model of Troudet and Arvieu (1979, 1981), showing similar behavior near the critical angular momentum. The paper also relates to Nilsson-Strutinsky approaches by Sheline et al. (1972), Leander and Larsson (1975), Ragnarsson et al. (1978), and Bengtsson et al. (1981), as well as to recent cranked SHF calculations by Molique, Dobaczewski and Dudek (1999) using the SLy4 force, which did not discuss yrast states above I ≈ 24 or non-axial octupole deformations. The findings suggest that experimental yrast γ-spectroscopy with higher resolving power is needed to explore the predicted high-spin region, which lies about 10 MeV below the observed molecular resonance region associated with ¹⁶O-¹⁶O configurations.
