# Effect of space-momentum correlations on the constituent quark number scaling of hadron elliptic flows

> Using models ranging from schematic one with a simple quark distribution to more realistic blast wave, we study the elliptic flow of hadrons produced from coalescence of quarks and antiquarks in the quark-gluon plasma that is formed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. In particular, we study effects due to azimuthal anisotropy in the local transverse momentum distribution of quarks, as generated by their position-momentum correlations as a result of radial flow and/or jet quenching. We find that even if quarks have large local non-elliptic anisotropic flow, the elliptic flow of produced hadrons can still scale with their constituent quark numbers. This scaling is, however, broken if the radial flow of coalescing quarks is anisotropic and/or if the momentum dispersion of quarks inside hadrons is included.

## Key claim

Even if quarks have large local non-elliptic anisotropic flow (such as directed flow) due to position-momentum correlations from radial flow or jet quenching, the elliptic flow of hadrons produced via naive coalescence can still scale with constituent quark number; this scaling is broken only if the radial flow of coalescing quarks is anisotropic or if the momentum dispersion of quarks inside hadrons is included.

## Method

The authors study the relation between quark and meson elliptic flows using three models: a schematic model with quarks having momenta pointing radially outward plus a cos(2φ) anisotropy; a blast wave model with isotropic radial flow and a relativistic Boltzmann distribution; and a blast wave model with anisotropic radial flow (ρ₂ ≠ 0). They also incorporate a finite-width hadron Wigner function to account for momentum dispersion of quarks inside hadrons, evaluating the coalescence integral via Monte Carlo.

## Key numbers

- In the schematic model with anisotropy parameter a₂ = 0.1, quarks in the first quadrant have local directed flow v₁ = 0.68 and u₁ = 0.59, while the global quark elliptic flow remains v̄₂,q = 0.1.
- In the blast wave model with isotropic radial flow (T = 170 MeV, m_q = 300 MeV, ρ₀ = 0.4, a₂(p_T) = 0.1 tanh(1.2 p_T)), the quark number scaling of meson elliptic flow holds exactly.
- In the blast wave model with anisotropic radial flow (ρ₂ = 0.25/{1+exp[(p_T-0.5)/0.75]} + 0.014), the quark number scaling is violated by about 20% at p_T/n ≈ 1.5 GeV.
- With a hadron Wigner function width Δ_p = 0.24 GeV, the quark number scaling is violated by about 10% in the scaled momentum region p_T/n ≈ 1-2 GeV; with Δ_p = 0.48 GeV, the violation is about 20% in the same region.

## Context

This work extends the study of quark number scaling of hadron elliptic flow beyond the naive coalescence model of Molnar and Voloshin (2003), Fries et al. (2003), and Kolb et al. (2004). It directly addresses the concern raised by Molnar (2004) that position-momentum correlations could generate large local directed flow and thus strongly violate the scaling. The authors show that such local anisotropies do not affect the global scaling, but that anisotropic radial flow (similar to the blast wave model of Huovinen et al., 2001) and finite hadron wave functions (as in Greco et al., 2003) do cause violations. The results enable experimental tests of whether observed scaling violations at RHIC arise from these specific mechanisms, supporting the quark coalescence picture of hadronization from the quark-gluon plasma.
