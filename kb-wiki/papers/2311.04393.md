# Quark confinement in an equiparticle quark model: application to stellar matter

> We perform an improvement in a thermodynamical consistent model with density dependent quark masses ($m'_{u,d,s}$) by introducing effects of quark confinement/deconfinement phase transition, at high density regime and zero temperature, by means of the traced Polyakov loop ($Φ$). We use realistic values for the current quark masses, provided by the Particle Data Group, and replace the constants of the interacting part of $m'_{u,d,s}$ by functions of $Φ$, leading to a first order phase transition structure, for symmetric and stellar quark matter, with $Φ$ being the order parameter. We show that the improved model points out the direction of the chiral symmetry restoration due to the emergence of a deconfined phase. In another application, we construct quark stars mass-radius profiles, obtained from this new model, and show to be possible to satisfy recent astrophysical observational data coming from the LIGO and Virgo Collaboration, and the NICER mission concerning the millisecond pulsars PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0740+6620.

## Key claim

The authors improve the thermodynamically consistent equiparticle (EQP) quark model by introducing confinement/deconfinement phase transition effects at zero temperature through the traced Polyakov loop (Φ), with the key condition that the free parameters C and D of the density-dependent quark masses are replaced by functions of Φ as C(1-Φ²) and D(1-Φ²), which produces a first-order phase transition structure for both symmetric and stellar quark matter.

## Method

The authors modify the density-dependent quark mass formula m_i = m_i0 + D/ρ_b^(1/3) + Cρ_b^(1/3) by replacing C and D with C(1-Φ²) and D(1-Φ²), where Φ is the traced Polyakov loop order parameter. They then solve the grand-canonical thermodynamical potential Ω_PEQP = -P_PEQP as a function of Φ for various chemical potentials, identifying first-order phase transitions by the appearance of two degenerate minima in Ω_PEQP versus Φ curves. For stellar matter, they impose charge neutrality and beta equilibrium conditions including electrons, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to generate mass-radius profiles for quark stars.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric matter with parameters C=0.2, √D=150 MeV and a₃=-0.14, the confinement chemical potential μ_conf = 316.629 MeV, with Ω_PEQP ≈ -5.7 MeV/fm³ at the transition point
- For stellar matter with C=0.81, √D=127 MeV and a₃=-0.30, μ_conf = 419.485 MeV; for a₃=-0.50, μ_conf = 488.596 MeV
- For stellar matter with a₃=-0.30, the baryonic density at the phase transition start is 2.94ρ₀ (with ρ₀=0.15 fm⁻³), and 4.19ρ₀ at the coexistence phase end; for a₃=-0.50, these values are 5.24ρ₀ and 6.84ρ₀ respectively
- The minimum energy per baryon for stellar matter in the PEQP model is 929.92 MeV, satisfying the stability criterion of ≤930 MeV

## Context

This work extends the equiparticle (EQP) model of Xia (2014) by incorporating confinement/deconfinement phenomenology previously applied to the PNJL model by Mattos (2019, 2021), where coupling constants were made functions of (1-Φ²). The approach enables the description of deconfinement at zero temperature, which standard PNJL models cannot achieve. The resulting quark star mass-radius profiles can be tested against observational data from LIGO/Virgo and NICER for pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the model shows chiral symmetry restoration through reduced quark masses, similar to effects seen in the PNJL model of Mattos (2021).
