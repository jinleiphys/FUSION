# $S$-factor and scattering parameters from ${}^3$He + ${}^4$He $\rightarrow {}^7$Be + $γ$ data

> We use the next-to-leading-order (NLO) amplitude in an effective field theory (EFT) for ${}^3$He + ${}^4$He $\rightarrow {}^7$Be + $γ$ to perform the extrapolation of higher-energy data to solar energies. At this order the EFT describes the capture process using an s-wave scattering length and effective range, the asymptotic behavior of $^7$Be and its excited state, and short-distance contributions to the E1 capture amplitude. We use a Bayesian analysis to infer the multi-dimensional posterior of these parameters from capture data below 2 MeV. The total $S$-factor $S(0)= 0.578^{+0.015}_{-0.016}$ keV b at 68% degree of belief. We also find significant constraints on $^3$He-$^4$He scattering parameters.

## Key claim

The paper uses a next-to-leading-order (NLO) effective field theory (EFT) amplitude to extrapolate higher-energy capture data to solar energies, finding a total S-factor at zero energy of S(0) = 0.578^{+0.015}_{-0.016} keV b at 68% degree of belief, with the caveat that the analysis only uses data below 2 MeV and does not include existing scattering phase shift analyses because their systematic errors are poorly quantified.

## Method

The authors employ a Halo EFT treating 3He and 4He as fundamental degrees of freedom and 7Be ground and excited states as shallow p-wave bound states, with an EFT breakdown scale of about 200 MeV. They perform a Bayesian analysis via Markov-Chain-Monte-Carlo sampling to infer the six-dimensional posterior of EFT parameters (two asymptotic normalization coefficients, scattering length, effective range, and two short-distance parameters) from 59 S-factor data points and 32 branching-ratio data points from six experiments, all at energies below 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Total S-factor at zero energy: S(0) = 0.578^{+0.015}_{-0.016} keV b at 68% degree of belief
* Branching ratio for excited- to ground-state capture at zero energy: Br(0) = 0.406^{+0.013}_{-0.011} at 68% degree of belief
* EFT breakdown scale: about 200 MeV, with low-momentum scale Q of 70-80 MeV, giving Q/Lambda approximately 0.4
* The recommended value from Adelberger (2010) of 0.56 plus or minus 0.02 (experimental) plus or minus 0.02 (theory) is consistent with the result but has an uncertainty almost a factor of two larger

## Context

This work extends the Halo EFT approach previously applied to the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction by Zhang (2015) and Zhang (2017) to the 3He(4He,gamma)7Be solar fusion reaction. It differs from a recent EFT evaluation by Higa (2016) by using Bayesian methods and by not including scattering phase shift analyses due to poorly quantified systematic errors. The results enable future tests of the EFT approach through better measurements of 3He-4He scattering cross sections and may help reduce uncertainties in solar model inputs.
