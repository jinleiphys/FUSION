# Role of density dependent symmetry energy in nuclear stopping

> Information about the nuclear matter under the extreme conditions of temperature and density and the role of symmetry energy under these conditions is still a topic of crucial importance in the present day nuclear physics research. The multifragmentation, collective flow and the nuclear stopping is among the various rare phenomenon which can be observed in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies. The nuclear stopping, which is sensitive towards the symmetry energy has gained a lot of interest because it provides the possibility to examine the degree of thermalization or equilibration in the matter. Aim of the present study is to pin down the nuclear stopping for the different forms of density dependent symmetry energy

## Key claim

The study finds that the density-dependent symmetry energy has a significant effect on nuclear stopping only for asymmetric nuclear systems (with N/Z > 1), while symmetric systems show no sensitivity to different forms of the symmetry energy. The abstract's general claim of sensitivity is qualified by this system-dependence.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model for several thousand events at 100 MeV/nucleon incident energy. They calculated the anisotropy ratio R, defined as (2/π) times the sum of transverse momenta divided by the sum of longitudinal momenta, as a measure of global nuclear stopping. The time evolution of R was compared for systems with different isotopic compositions (Ca-40 + Ca-40 with N/Z=1 and Ca-60 + Ca-60 with N/Z=1.5) using a constant symmetry energy of 32 MeV and a density-dependent form 32(ρ/ρ₀)^γ with γ=0.66.

## Key numbers

* Anisotropy ratio increases by 10 percent for the system with large isotopic asymmetry (N/Z=1.5) compared to the symmetric system (N/Z=1) at 100 MeV/nucleon.
* The constant symmetry energy value used is 32 MeV, corresponding to normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
* The density-dependent symmetry energy parametrization used is E(ρ) = 32(ρ/ρ₀)^γ MeV with γ = 0.66.

## Context

This work extends studies of nuclear stopping as a probe of thermalization in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, building on the parametrization of density-dependent symmetry energy by Bao (2005). The results indicate that nuclear stopping measurements in asymmetric systems can help constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, while symmetric systems are insensitive to this effect. The findings enable future experimental comparisons to distinguish between stiff and soft forms of the symmetry energy using isotopic chains.
