# Quarkonium dissociation in a far-from-equilibrium holographic setup

> The real-time dissociation of the heavy quarkonium in a strongly coupled boost-invariant non-Abelian plasma relaxing towards equilibrium is analyzed in a holographic framework. The effects driving the plasma out of equilibrium are described by boundary quenching, impulsive variations of the boundary metric. Quarkonium is represented by a classical string with endpoints kept close to the boundary. The evolution of the string profile is computed in the time-dependent geometry, and the dissociation time is evaluated for different configurations with respect to the direction of the plasma expansion. Dissociation occurs fastly for the quarkonium placed in the transverse plane.

## Key claim

The real-time dissociation of heavy quarkonium in a strongly coupled, boost-invariant non-Abelian plasma is analyzed holographically, with the plasma driven far from equilibrium by boundary quenching (impulsive variations of the boundary metric). The dissociation time depends on the orientation of the quarkonium relative to the plasma expansion direction, and the effect is fast for quarkonium placed in the transverse plane.

## Method

The authors model the far-from-equilibrium plasma using a 5D holographic dual with a time-dependent metric obtained by solving Einstein's vacuum equations with a negative cosmological constant, subject to boundary quench profiles (models A(2) and B). Quarkonium is represented as a classical open string in the dual space, with endpoints fixed near the boundary; the string's evolution is computed from the Nambu-Goto action in the time-dependent geometry. The dissociation time is extracted as the time when the string reaches the black hole horizon, for both transverse (w=x) and longitudinal (w=y) string configurations relative to the plasma expansion axis.

## Key numbers

* For quench model B, transverse configuration (w=x) with separation L=10 and v=0: dissociation time intercept a^{B,x}=0.0333366 and slope b^{B,x}=0.000287307.
* For quench model B, longitudinal configuration (w=y) with y_L=10 and v=0: a^{B,y}=0.0335199 and b^{B,y}=0.000287263.
* For quench model A(2), transverse configuration (w=x) with L=10 and v=0: a^{A(2),x}=0.0332289 and b^{A(2),x}=0.000287869.
* For quench model A(2), longitudinal configuration (w=y) with y_L=10 and v=0: a^{A(2),y}=0.0334651 and b^{A(2),y}=0.00028788.
* Asymptotic dissociation time for r_0=12: t_infinity=0.0334108 (from Eq. tas).
* Assuming a temperature T ~ 500 MeV after the quenches, the dissociation time is of order 10^{-2} fm.

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of quarkonium dissociation in static thermal plasmas (Lin 2006, Iatrakis 2015) to far-from-equilibrium conditions, using the boundary sourcing approach of Chesler (2008, 2009). The results are consistent with earlier findings that the quarkonium screening length depends on orientation relative to the plasma flow (Liu 2006), with faster dissociation for strings oriented perpendicular to the expansion direction. The analysis enables future comparisons with holographic models that include confinement effects via scalar fields (Gubser 2008, Janik 2015, 2016).
