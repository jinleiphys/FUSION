# \textit{Ab initio} calculations of first-forbidden $β$ transitions in the reactor antineutrino anomaly

> We present the first \textit{ab initio} calculations of first-forbidden $β$ transitions which are important for understanding the reactor antineutrino anomaly. Starting from chiral two- plus three-nucleon forces, we derive both valence-space effective Hamiltonians and forbidden transition operators self-consistently. Focusing on the dominant first-forbidden transitions relevant to reactor antineutrino spectra, we compute 20 key transitions and obtain $\log ft$ values that show reasonable agreement with experimental data. Obtained shape factors exhibit significant deviations from the values approximated with forbidden transitions treated as allowed transitions, indicating the importance of explicit treatments of forbidden transitions in reactor antineutrino studies. By incorporating these microscopic shape factors into the calculation of the ${}^{235}$U antineutrino spectrum, we observe a pronounced spectrum enhancement around 5 MeV of the antineutrion energy, which may partially explain the well-known ``5 MeV bump'' observed in reactor experiments.

# *Ab initio* calculations of first-forbidden β transitions in the reactor antineutrino anomaly

## Key claim

The authors present the first *ab initio* calculations of first-forbidden β transitions relevant to the reactor antineutrino anomaly, finding that incorporating microscopic shape factors from 20 key transitions into the ²³⁵U antineutrino spectrum produces a pronounced enhancement around 5 MeV that may partially explain the "5 MeV bump" observed in reactor experiments. The calculated log ft values show reasonable agreement with experimental data without introducing any quenching factor, though some systematic overestimates remain, particularly for ΔJ=2 unique transitions.

## Method

Starting from chiral two- plus three-nucleon forces (2NF truncated at N⁴LO and 3NF at N²LO, evolved via similarity renormalization group at λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹), the authors used many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) to derive valence-space effective Hamiltonians and forbidden transition operators self-consistently in a Hartree-Fock basis built from 15 harmonic-oscillator major shells at ħω = 16 MeV. The valence space consisted of π{0f₅/₂, 1p₃/₂, 1p₁/₂, 0g₉/₂} and ν{0g₇/₂, 1d₅/₂, 1d₃/₂, 2s₁/₂}, with diagonalization performed using the large-scale shell-model code KSHELL. The authors computed 20 dominant first-forbidden transitions and their shape factors, then incorporated these into the ²³⁵U antineutrino spectrum using experimental Q values, branching ratios from ENSDF, and cumulative fission yields from the ENDF database.

## Key numbers

- 20 key first-forbidden transitions were computed, accounting for more than half of the total forbidden branching ratios and about 50% of the total cumulative flux in the 4–7 MeV energy region
- For ⁹⁰Rb(0₁⁻) → ⁹⁰Sr(0₁⁺): calculated log ft = 7.85 vs. experimental 7.81 (ΔJ=0)
- For ⁹²Rb(0₁⁻) → ⁹²Sr(0₁⁺): calculated log ft = 6.20 vs. experimental 5.75 (ΔJ=0)
- For ⁹⁶Y(0₁⁻) → ⁹⁶Zr(0₁⁺): calculated log ft = 6.32 vs. experimental 5.59 (ΔJ=0)
- For ⁸⁶Br(1₁⁻) → ⁸⁶Kr(0₁⁺): calculated log ft = 7.16 vs. experimental 7.45 (ΔJ=1)
- For ⁸⁷Se(3/2₁⁺) → ⁸⁷Br(5/2₁⁻): calculated log ft = 7.35 vs. experimental 6.10 (ΔJ=1)
- For ⁸⁸Rb(2₁⁻) → ⁸⁸Sr(0₁⁺): calculated log ft = 7.13 vs. experimental 9.25 (ΔJ=2)
- For ⁹⁴Y(2₁⁻) → ⁹⁴Zr(0₁⁺): calculated log ft = 7.42 vs. experimental 9.35 (ΔJ=2)
- For ⁹⁵Rb(5/2₁⁻) → ⁹⁵Sr(1/2₁⁺): calculated log ft = 10.56 vs. experimental ≥ 10.2 (ΔJ=2)
- The calculated spectrum shows an increase of about 6% around 5 MeV antineutrino energy relative to the allowed approximation
- Many-body calculation uncertainty estimated at about 10%, with truncation uncertainty less than 1% at e_max = 14 and E₃max = 24, and ħω variation from 12 to 20 MeV affecting the spectrum by less than 5%

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* MBPT framework previously developed for allowed β transitions (PhysRevC.110.024308) to first-forbidden transitions for the first time, addressing a key limitation of the Huber-Mueller (HM) model (PhysRevC.83.054615, PhysRevC.84.024617) which treated forbidden transitions using highly simplified approximations. The HM model predicts a reactor antineutrino flux approximately 6% higher than experimental data from short-baseline experiments (Daya Bay, RENO, Double Chooz), creating the reactor antineutrino anomaly, and also fails to reproduce the "5 MeV bump" spectral distortion. The present results demonstrate that explicit treatment of first-forbidden transitions partially resolves both the rate and shape discrepancies, though the authors note that two-body currents in forbidden decays remain unexplored and will be addressed in future work. The chiral interaction used (NN+3N(lnl)) was previously developed by Gysbers et al. (2019) for allowed transitions.
