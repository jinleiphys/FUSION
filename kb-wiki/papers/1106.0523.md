# Nuclear matter and chiral phase transition at large-$N_{c}$

> Two aspects of the QCD phase diagrams are studied in the limit of a large number of colors: at zero temperature and nonzero density the (non)existence of nuclear matter, and at zero density and nonzero temperature the chiral phase transition.

# Nuclear matter and chiral phase transition at large-$N_{c}$

## Key claim
Nuclear matter does not bind for any number of colors greater than three, because the scalar attraction between nucleons (mediated by a light scalar field of about 0.6 GeV) scales unfavorably relative to the vector repulsion when $N_{c}$ is increased, unless the scalar field is a pure quark-antiquark state, a scenario disfavored by spectroscopy. For the chiral phase transition, the critical temperature $T_{c}$ in the sigma-model scales as $\sqrt{N_{c}}$ in contradiction with the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, but this mismatch can be resolved by introducing a temperature-dependent mass term that restores $T_{c} \propto N_{c}^{0}$.

## Method
The existence of nuclear matter at large $N_{c}$ is studied using a Walecka-type effective Lagrangian with a scalar field $S$ (mass ~0.6 GeV) and an isoscalar vector meson $\omega$, examining four possible interpretations of $S$ (quark-antiquark, tetraquark, two-pion-exchange effect, low-mass glueball) and their $N_{c}$ scaling laws. The chiral phase transition at zero density is analyzed by computing the critical temperature $T_{c}$ in the linear sigma-model using the CJT (Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis) formalism in the Hartree and double-bubble approximations, and then modifying the model with a temperature-dependent mass term to recover the expected $N_{c}$-independent scaling.

## Key numbers
- The scalar field mass is about 0.6 GeV for the light scalar resonance $f_{0}(600)$, while the quark-antiquark scalar partner $\sigma$ corresponds to $f_{0}(1370)$ with a mass of about 1.3 GeV.
- The pion decay constant $f_{\pi} = 92.4$ MeV is used in the sigma-model expression for $T_{c}$.
- In the unmodified sigma-model, $T_{c}(N_{c}) = f_{\pi} \sqrt{2 N_{c} / 3} \propto \sqrt{N_{c}}$.
- With the temperature-dependent mass modification and $T_{0} = \Lambda_{QCD} \simeq 225$ MeV, the critical temperature for $N_{c}=3$ is $T_{c} \simeq 113$ MeV.
- Including (axial-)vector mesons via a factor $Z = 1.67 \pm 0.2$ raises the critical temperature to $T_{c} \simeq 157$ MeV, close to lattice results.

## Context
This work extends the large-$N_{c}$ analysis of the QCD phase diagram initiated by Witten (1979) and McLerran (2009) by addressing two specific axes: nuclear matter at zero temperature and nonzero density, and the chiral phase transition at zero density and nonzero temperature. The nuclear matter result contradicts the possibility of binding for $N_{c}>3$ and supports the strong anthropic principle that $N_{c}=3$ is special; it builds on the spectroscopy review of Amsler (2008) and the tetraquark interpretation of Jaffe (1977). The chiral transition analysis resolves a discrepancy between the NJL model (where $T_{c} \propto N_{c}^{0}$) and the sigma-model (where $T_{c} \propto \sqrt{N_{c}}$), and the temperature-dependent mass modification is consistent with the hadronization approach of Leutwyler (1994). This enables future work coupling sigma-models to the Polyakov loop to achieve confinement-deconfinement and chiral restoration simultaneously.
