# Self-consistent Green's functions with three-body forces

> The present thesis aims at studying the properties of symmetric nuclear and pure neutron matter from a Green's functions point of view, including two-body and three-body chiral forces. An extended self-consistent Green's function formalism is defined to consistently incorporate three-body forces in the many-body calculations. The effect of three-nucleon interactions is included via the construction of a dressed two-body density dependent force. This is obtained performing an average of the leading order three-body terms in the chiral effective field theory expansion. The dressed force corresponds to the use of an in-medium propagator in the average which takes into account the correlations characterizing the system at each stage of the many-body calculation. The total energy of the system is obtained by means of a modified Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sumrule to correctly account for the effect of three-body forces. Microscopic as well as macroscopic properties of symmetric nuclear and pure neutron matter are analyzed in detailed.

# Self-consistent Green's functions with three-body forces

## Key claim
The thesis demonstrates that three-body forces (3BFs) can be consistently incorporated into self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter by constructing a dressed, density-dependent two-body force from the leading-order chiral three-nucleon interaction, with the crucial condition that the averaging procedure must use a correlated (dressed) momentum distribution rather than a free Fermi gas distribution to correctly account for many-body correlations.

## Method
The author extends the SCGF formalism to include three-body forces by defining a new class of "interaction-irreducible" self-energy diagrams alongside the standard one-particle-irreducible and skeleton diagrams. The leading-order three-nucleon force at N2LO in chiral effective field theory (two-pion-exchange, one-pion-exchange, and contact terms) is averaged over the third nucleon using a dressed momentum occupation number n(p) obtained from the correlated single-particle propagator, producing a density-dependent two-body force. The total energy is then computed using a modified Galitskii-Migdal-Koltun sumrule that correctly accounts for three-body contributions, and the resulting in-medium interaction is applied within the ladder approximation for infinite nuclear systems.

## Key numbers
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.29
- Pion mass: M_pi = 138.04 MeV
- Weak pion decay constant: F_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_chi = 700 MeV
- The three-body force cutoff Lambda_3NF is applied via a regulator function f(p,q) = exp[-(p^2 + 3q^2/4)^2 / Lambda_3NF^4]
- The density-dependent contact term from the 3NF contraction yields: V_cont^3NF = -3 c_E rho_f / (2 F_pi^4 Lambda_chi), where rho_f is the integral of the correlated momentum distribution weighted by the regulator

## Context
This work extends the SCGF approach of previous nuclear matter calculations (Bogner 2010, Hebeler 2010) by providing a formal, first-principles derivation of how three-body forces enter the self-energy and propagator equations, rather than using purely phenomenological effective interactions. The formalism enables consistent treatment of three-body forces in infinite nuclear matter calculations, with the density-dependent force construction following the method of Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2010) but using correlated momentum distributions instead of step functions. The approach opens the door to studying how three-body forces affect single-particle properties such as spectral functions and momentum distributions, and consequently bulk properties like the symmetry energy (Vidana 2011).
