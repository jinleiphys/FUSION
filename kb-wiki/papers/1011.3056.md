# Non-boost-invariant anisotropic dynamics

> We study the non-boost-invariant evolution of a quark-gluon plasma subject to large early-time momentum-space anisotropies. Rather than using the canonical hydrodynamical expansion of the distribution function around an isotropic equilibrium state, we expand around a state which is anisotropic in momentum space and parameterize this state in terms of three proper-time and spatial-rapidity dependent parameters. Deviations from the Bjorken scaling solutions are naturally taken into account by the time evolution of the spatial-rapidity dependence of the anisotropic ansatz. As a result, we obtain three coupled partial differential equations for the momentum-space anisotropy, the typical momentum of the degrees of freedom, and the longitudinal flow. Within this framework (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken expansion is obtained as an asymptotic limit. Finally, we make quantitative comparisons of the temporal and spatial-rapidity evolution of the dynamical parameters and resulting pressure anisotropy in both the strong and weak coupling limits.

## Key claim
The paper extends previous work on highly anisotropic quark-gluon plasma dynamics (Martinez and Strickland, 2010) by removing the assumption of boost invariance, allowing both temporal and spatial-rapidity dependence of the system. The authors claim that their framework smoothly connects early-time anisotropic expansion and late-time hydrodynamical behavior, with the (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken expansion obtained as an asymptotic limit.

## Method
The authors derive three coupled partial differential equations for the momentum-space anisotropy parameter ξ, the hard momentum scale phard, and the longitudinal flow rapidity ϑ by taking moments of the Boltzmann equation with a Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution function ansatz. The collisional kernel is modeled using the relaxation time approximation, with the relaxation rate Γ related to the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/S through matching to second-order viscous hydrodynamics. Numerical solutions are obtained for LHC-like initial conditions with Gaussian rapidity profiles, comparing both isotropic and anisotropic initial conditions under strong and weak coupling limits.

## Key numbers
- Initial proper time τ0 = 0.1 fm/c for all simulations
- Initial hard momentum scale at central rapidity p0 = 845 MeV for isotropic initial conditions (Case I)
- Initial anisotropy parameter ξ0 = 100 for anisotropic initial conditions (Case II), with central phard increased by factor (1+ξ)^(1/6) ≈ 2.16 to 1.82 GeV
- Strong coupling viscosity: η/S = 1/(4π)
- Weak coupling viscosity: η/S = 10/(4π)
- At τ = 6.1 fm/c with strong coupling and isotropic initial conditions, PL/PT ≈ 0.82 at spatial rapidity ς = 5
- LHC center-of-mass energy √sNN = 5.5 TeV used for initial condition parametrization
- Sound velocity cs = 1/√3 (ideal equation of state)

## Context
This work extends the boost-invariant anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Martinez and Strickland (2010) to include non-boost-invariant dynamics, building on the Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution function ansatz (Romatschke and Strickland, 2003). The approach provides an alternative to standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart theory) which can break down at early times when momentum-space anisotropies become large. The method enables quantitative study of the transition between early-time anisotropic expansion and late-time hydrodynamical behavior in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, and can be generalized to include transverse expansion in future work.
