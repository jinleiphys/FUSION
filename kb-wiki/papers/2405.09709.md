# Spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions induced by thermal vorticity and thermal shear

> The vorticity is a quantity defined in a relativistic fluid that describes how much a fluid element is rotating and accelerating. By measuring the spin polarization of hadrons, it was found that the quark gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions is the most "vorticous" fluid ever observed. More generally, this opens the possibility to study the physics of QCD matter using spin. Here I use statistical quantum field theory applied to a fluid in local thermodynamic equilibrium to show how to connect the average spin of a fermion with hydrodynamic quantities, and in particular with the thermal vorticity and the thermal shear. I show that the spin polarization of a Dirac particle induced by thermal vorticity is related to the gravitational (in medium) form factor related to spin-rotation coupling. For these reasons, as we are understanding the role of spin in hydrodynamics and in heavy-ion collisions, spin is becoming a promising tool to investigate the properties of QCD and whose applications are just begun to be explored.

# Spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions induced by thermal vorticity and thermal shear

## Key claim
The spin polarization of hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions can be connected to both thermal vorticity and thermal shear of the quark-gluon plasma, with the shear contribution being essential to resolve the disagreement between earlier theoretical predictions and experimental data on local (momentum-dependent) spin polarization. The vorticity-induced polarization is related to the gravitational (in-medium) form factor for spin-rotation coupling, which can receive radiative corrections at finite temperature, unlike the vacuum case where it must equal unity.

## Method
The author uses statistical quantum field theory applied to a fluid in local thermodynamic equilibrium, deriving the density matrix operator from entropy maximization with a gradient expansion of the hydrodynamic fields. Linear response theory is then employed to compute the Wigner functions and the average spin vector (Pauli-Lubanski operator) for Dirac fermions, expressed in terms of thermal vorticity and thermal shear. The in-medium matrix elements of the energy-momentum tensor are decomposed into form factors including the gravitomagnetic moment, which is calculated explicitly at one-loop order in finite-temperature QED.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic picture of spin polarization that began with the prediction that off-central heavy-ion collisions generate large angular momentum (Liang and Voloshin, 2004) and the connection of spin polarization to thermal vorticity (Becattini et al., 2007, 2013). The approach resolves the discrepancy between the first measurement of local spin polarization (STAR, 2019) and predictions from thermal vorticity alone, by including the thermal shear contribution (Liu et al., 2021; Becattini et al., 2021). The formalism enables using spin polarization measurements to constrain initial conditions of hydrodynamic models and probe QGP properties such as shear and bulk viscosities, as well as to investigate local parity violations, the critical point, and jet energy loss in the medium.
