# Magnetar QPOs and neutron star crust elasticity

> The crust region is a tiny fraction of neutron stars, but it has a variety of physical properties and plays an important role in astronomical observations. One of the properties characterizing the crust is the elasticity. In this review, with the approach of asteroseismology, we systematically examine neutron star oscillations excited by crust elasticity, adopting the Cowling approximation. In particular, by identifying the quasi-periodic oscillations observed in magnetar flares with the torsional oscillations, we make a constraint on the nuclear saturation parameters. In addition, we also discuss how the shear and interface modes depend on the neutron star properties. Once one detects an additional signal associated with neutron star oscillations, one can get a more severe constraint on the saturation parameters and/or neutron star properties, which must be a qualitatively different constraint obtained from terrestrial experiments, and help us to complementarily understand astrophysics and nuclear physics.

## Key claim
By systematically identifying quasi-periodic oscillations (QPOs) observed in magnetar flares with torsional oscillations of neutron stars, the nuclear saturation parameters can be constrained, but only within the Cowling approximation (which neglects metric perturbations) and assuming the crust elasticity is the dominant restoring force.

## Method
The author performed a systematic asteroseismology study of neutron star torsional oscillations, adopting the Cowling approximation to decouple the fluid and spacetime perturbations. The shear modulus of the crust was calculated from the nuclear equation of state, and the resulting eigenfrequencies were compared to observed QPO frequencies from magnetar flares to place constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence.

## Context
This work extends the approach of asteroseismology, previously applied to other compact objects, to systematically link magnetar QPO observations to the nuclear equation of state. It builds on prior studies of torsional oscillations (e.g., by Sotani and collaborators) and aims to provide constraints on nuclear saturation parameters that are complementary to those from terrestrial experiments. The review enables future work where the detection of additional oscillation signals could yield more severe constraints on neutron star properties and nuclear physics.
