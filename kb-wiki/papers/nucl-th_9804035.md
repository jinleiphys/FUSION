# Evidence of early multi-strange hadron freeze-out in high energy nuclear collisions

> Recently reported transverse momentum distributions of strange hadrons produced in Pb(158AGeV) on Pb collisions and corresponding results from the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) approach are examined. We argue that the experimental observations favor a scenario in which multi-strange hadrons are formed and decouple from the system rather early at large energy densities (around 1 GeV/fm$^3$). The systematics of the strange and non-strange particle spectra indicate that the observed transverse flow develops mainly in the late hadronic stages of these reactions.

# Evidence of early multi-strange hadron freeze-out in high energy nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The experimental transverse momentum spectra of multi-strange hadrons (Xi, Omega) in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV show much softer slopes than expected from the mass-dependent trend observed for non-strange and singly-strange hadrons, which the authors argue favors a scenario where multi-strange hadrons decouple early (around 1 GeV/fm^3 energy density) before significant transverse flow develops, rather than at a common freeze-out state.

## Method

The authors compared published transverse momentum distributions of strange and non-strange hadrons from the NA44, NA49, and WA97 collaborations with predictions from the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD) model (version 2.3), for which they generated 1600 events of central Pb(158A GeV) on Pb collisions. They analyzed the slope parameters from exponential fits to the transverse mass distributions and examined the RQMD time evolution of transverse source sizes and freeze-out distributions for different hadron species.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter for Omega baryons: 237 +/- 24 MeV, consistent with the "temperature" extracted from particle ratios
- Parameterization of non-strange data: T_fo approximately 145 MeV, with average transverse flow velocity <beta_t> = 0.4 c
- RQMD predicts freeze-out of Omega baryons occurs mostly between 2 and 8 fm/c, while nucleon freeze-out distribution is centered around 20 fm/c
- Local energy densities at which multi-strange hadrons decouple in RQMD cluster around 1 GeV/fm^3
- Decay width ratios for baryon resonances as function of flavor content: approximately 0.45:0.62:0.85:1 for Omega*:Xi*:Y*:N*
- RQMD predicts the pre-equilibrium stage with ultrasoft transverse pressure lasts about 5 fm/c in central Pb on Pb reactions

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation of strong radial transverse flow in Pb+Pb collisions (Sorge, Nagle, and Kumar 1995; Esumi, Chapman, van Hecke, and Xu 1997; NA44 Collaboration 1997) by showing that multi-strange hadrons do not participate in this flow. The results contradict the one-fluid flow model with common freeze-out (Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, Wessels, and Xu 1996) and instead support sequential freeze-out scenarios (Bravina et al. 1995; Sorge 1996). The finding that Xi slope parameters in Pb+Pb are comparable to those in p+W and S+W reactions (WA94 Collaboration 1997) provides strong constraints on reaction dynamics, ruling out scenarios with sizable flow before multi-strange hadron freeze-out. The RQMD model provides an explanation for the systematics from p+A and S+A to Pb+Pb (Sorge 1997), whereas hydrodynamical approaches invoking the "softest point" of the equation of state (Hung and Shuryak 1995) fail to explain the mass dependence of transverse flow for S projectiles.
