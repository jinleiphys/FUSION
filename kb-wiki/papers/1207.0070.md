# Hypernclear in the improved quark mass density- dependent model

> The improved quark mass density- dependent model, which has been successfully used to describe the properties of both finite nuclei and bulk nuclear matter, is extended to include the strange quark. The parameters of the model are determined by the saturation properties of bulk matter. Then the given parameter set is employed to investigate both the properties of strange hadronic matter and those of $Λ$ hypernuclei. Bulk strange hadronic matter consisting of nucleons, $Λ$- hyperons and $Ξ$- hyperons is studied under mean-field approximation. Among others, density dependence of the effective baryon mass, saturation properties and stability of the physical system are discussed. For single-$Λ$ hypernuclei, single particle energies of $Λ$ hyperon is evaluated. In particular, it is found that the present model produces a small spin-orbit interaction, which is in agreement with the experimental observations. The above results show that the present model can consistently describe the properties of strange hadronic matter, as well as those of single $Λ$ hypernuclei within an uniform parameterization.

# Hypernuclei in the improved quark mass density-dependent model

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model, extended to include strange quarks, can consistently describe both bulk strange hadronic matter and single-Λ hypernuclei within a uniform parameterization, but the stability of strange hadronic matter depends critically on whether the hyperon-hyperon (Y-Y) interaction is taken as strong or weak, with weak Y-Y interaction, strange hadronic matter becomes unstable for strangeness fractions above about 1.2.

## Method

The IQMDD model was extended by including strange quarks and hyperons (Λ and Ξ) with additional strange mesons σ* and φ mediating hyperon-hyperon interactions. Parameters were fixed by reproducing saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, E/A = -15 MeV, compression modulus K = 210 MeV) and the experimental single-Λ potential depth in nuclear matter (-28 MeV). Bulk strange hadronic matter was studied under mean-field approximation with chemical equilibrium conditions, while single-Λ hypernuclei were treated as closed-shell cores plus one Λ hyperon, solved self-consistently via coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per baryon of symmetric nuclear matter: E/A = -15 MeV
- Compression modulus: K(ρ₀) = 210 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: 33.2 MeV
- Bag constant: B = 174 MeV fm⁻³
- Coupling constants: g_ω^q = 2.44, g_σ^q = 4.67, g_ρ^q = 9.07
- Strange quark current mass: m_s0 = 162 MeV (fits free Λ mass M_Λ = 1116 MeV)
- Calculated Ξ mass: M_Ξ = 1306 MeV (close to theoretical value 1318 MeV)
- Single-Λ potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter: E_Λ^(N) = -28 MeV
- Coupling constant g_σ^s = 0.2 (from fitting E_Λ^(N))
- Calculated single-Ξ potential depth: E_Ξ^(N) = -13 MeV (experimental: -18 MeV)
- Strong Y-Y interaction coupling: g_σ*^s = 7.12 (from U_Λ^(Λ) ≈ 20 MeV)
- Weak Y-Y interaction coupling: g_σ*^s = 2.83 (from ΔB_ΛΛ ≈ 1 MeV)
- For strong Y-Y interaction, minimum binding energy per baryon: (E/B, f_s) ≈ (-21.5 MeV, 1.5) at ρ_B ≈ 0.40 fm⁻³
- For weak Y-Y interaction, no negative minimum exists for f_s > 1.2
- Λ 1s₁/₂ single-particle energies: -12.4 MeV (¹⁷_ΛO), -15.8 MeV (⁴¹_ΛCa), -21.4 MeV (²⁰⁹_ΛPb)
- Asymptotic 1s₁/₂ Λ energy for A→∞: 25 MeV (close to infinite matter value 28 MeV)
- Spin-orbit splittings for Λ are very small (e.g., 1p₃/₂-1p₁/₂ splitting in ¹⁷_ΛO: 0.6 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the IQMDD model previously developed by Wu et al. (2010-2012) for finite nuclei and nuclear matter to include strange quarks, building on the quark mass density-dependent model of Fowler, Raha and Weiner and the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon. It addresses incomplete treatments in earlier studies of strange hadronic matter noted by Schaffner and Gal (2000), which either discussed only Λ matter, ignored Ξ hyperons, or arbitrarily constrained strangeness fraction. The results enable future calculations of multi-strange systems and neutron star properties, and the model's uniform parameterization allows testing against experimental Λ hypernuclear spectroscopy data from (π⁺, K⁺) reactions.
