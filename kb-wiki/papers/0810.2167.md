# First-Forbidden Continuum- and Bound-State beta^- -Decay Rates of Bare 205Hg80+ and 207Tl81+ Ions

> We calculate the decay rates lambda_(beta^-_c) and lambda_(beta^-_b) of the continuum- and bound-state beta^- decays for bare 205Hg80+ and 207Tl81+ ions. For the ratio of the decay rates R_(b/c) = lambda_(beta^-_b)/lambda_(beta^-_c) we obtain the values R_(b/c) = 0.161 and R_(b/c) = 0.190 for bare 205Hg80+ and 207Tl81+ ions, respectively. The theoretical value of the ratio R_(b/c) = 0.190 for the decays of 207Tl81+ agrees within 1% of accuracy with the experimental data R^exp_(b/c) = 0.188(18), obtained at GSI. The theoretical ratio R_(b/c) = 0.161 for 205Hg80+ is about 20% smaller than the experimental value R^exp_(b/c) = 0.20(2), measured recently at GSI.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the ratio of bound-state to continuum-state beta-decay rates for bare 205Hg80+ ions is 0.161, which is about 20% smaller than the experimental value of 0.20(2) measured at GSI; they argue this discrepancy suggests the experimental value may be too high, based on the weak dependence of the ratio on electric charge and the different Q-values.

## Method

The authors apply the standard theory of weak interactions of heavy ions to compute first-forbidden (ΔI^P = 0^-) continuum- and bound-state beta-decay rates for bare 205Hg80+ and 207Tl81+ ions. The continuum-state decay rate is calculated using a Fermi integral with Wood-Saxon nuclear matrix elements, while the bound-state decay rate sums contributions from electron capture into all ns states (including hyperfine-split states) using Dirac wave functions averaged over the nuclear density. The ratio R_{b/c} = λ_{β⁻_b}/λ_{β⁻_c} is then evaluated from these rates.

## Key numbers

- For bare 205Hg80+ ions: theoretical ratio R^{th}_{b/c} = 0.161, compared to experimental value R^{exp}_{b/c} = 0.20(2) (about 20% smaller)
- For bare 207Tl81+ ions: theoretical ratio R^{th}_{b/c} = 0.190, compared to experimental value R^{exp}_{b/c} = 0.188(18) (agreement within 1%)
- Q-values for continuum-state decays: Q_{β⁻_c} = 1515.734 keV for 205Hg80+ and Q_{β⁻_c} = 1407.471 keV for 207Tl81+
- Fermi integrals: f(Q_{β⁻_c}, Z+1) = 22.119 MeV^5 for 205Hg80+ and 17.747 MeV^5 for 207Tl81+
- Q-values for bound-state 1s decays: Q_{1s} = 1614.557 keV for 205Hg80+ and Q_{1s} = 1509.053 keV for 207Tl81+
- Previous theoretical value from Takahashi and Yokoi for 207Tl81+: R^{th}_{b/c} = 0.171(1)

## Context

This work extends the standard theory of weak interactions of heavy ions, previously applied successfully to K-shell electron capture and β⁺ decays of H-like and He-like 140Pr ions (Ivanov, 2008), to first-forbidden β⁻ decays of bare heavy ions. The calculation improves upon the earlier theoretical value of 0.171(1) for 207Tl81+ from Takahashi and Yokoi (1987), achieving 1% agreement with GSI experimental data. The results enable more precise tests of weak interaction theory in highly charged ions and suggest the need for remeasurement of the 205Hg80+ decay ratio.
