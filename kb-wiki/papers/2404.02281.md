# Avenues for a number density interpretation of dihadron fragmentation functions

> In this comment, we reassess the underlying physics of the number sum rule for dihadron fragmentation functions. We will argue that, currently, there are no settled constraints on what constitutes a valid number density interpretation for multihadron fragmentation functions. Imposing overly restrictive criteria might lead to misinterpretating the data. Most importantly, and on the basis of phenomenological analyses, the slightly varying definitions used in previous work are not excluded from possessing legitimate number density interpretations (up to the usual issues with ultraviolet divergences and renormalization), so long as they are paired with appropriate factorization theorems. We advocate for further theoretical analyses to be challenged with experimental data, available at JLab or at the future EIC.

## Key claim

The authors argue that previously used definitions of dihadron fragmentation functions are not excluded from having legitimate number density interpretations, contrary to claims in Pitonyak (2023), provided they are paired with appropriate factorization theorems; however, they acknowledge that the number density interpretation cannot be taken literally due to ultraviolet divergences and renormalization.

## Method

The authors reassess the number sum rule for dihadron fragmentation functions by examining the formal operator derivation in Pitonyak (2023) and comparing it with the critique in Collins (2023). They analyze the orthogonality between fragmenting quark states and true asymptotic hadronic final states, showing that the total multiplicity sum rule interpretation fails because the fragmenting quark state has zero overlap with physical hadronic states. They then demonstrate that a differential number density interpretation remains valid, with different variable choices related by Jacobian factors that can be absorbed into hard factors in factorization formulas.

## Key numbers

- Typical hadron multiplicities in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering at Jefferson Lab are around 5, while at a future Electron-Ion Collider they are expected to be about 12 to 13
- The ambiguity in the total multiplicity sum rule for ordinary fragmentation functions is at least order 1 hadron, as shown in Eq. (16) of Collins (2023)

## Context

This comment responds to Pitonyak (2023), which criticized certain dihadron fragmentation function definitions for lacking a number density interpretation based on a total multiplicity sum rule. The authors extend the critique by Collins (2023) of multiplicity sum rules for single hadron fragmentation functions to the multihadron case, arguing that the breakdown of the total multiplicity interpretation does not invalidate number density interpretations used in pioneering dihadron work by Bianconi (1999) and Bacchetta (2002), and subsequent phenomenological applications by Courtoy (2012) and Radici (2015). The paper advocates for guiding future refinements of multihadron fragmentation function definitions by requirements for useful factorization theorems and experimental tests at Jefferson Lab or the future Electron-Ion Collider.
