# Light Sea Fermions in Electron-Proton and Muon-Proton Interactions

> The proton radius conundrum [R. Pohl et al., Nature vol.466, p.213 (2010) and A. Antognini et al., Science vol.339, p.417 (2013)] highlights the need to revisit any conceivable sources of electron-muon nonuniversality in lepton-proton interactions within the Standard Model. Superficially, a number of perturbative processes could appear to lead to such a nonunversality. One of these is a coupling of the scattered electron into an electronic as opposed to a muonic vacuum polarization loop in the photon exchange of two valence quarks, which is present only for electron projectiles as opposed to muon projectiles. However, we can show that this effect actually is part of the radiative correction to the proton's polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift, equivalent to a radiative correction to double scattering. We conclude that any conceivable genuine nonuniversality must be connected with a nonperturbative feature of the proton's structure, e.g., with the possible presence of light sea fermions as constituent components of the proton. If we assume an average of roughly 0.7*10^(-7) light sea positrons per valence quark, then we can show that virtual electron-positron annihiliation processes lead to an extra term in the electron-proton versus muon-proton interaction, which has the right sign and magnitude to explain the proton radius discrepancy.

## Key claim

Any genuine nonuniversality between electron-proton and muon-proton interactions must arise from a nonperturbative feature of the proton's structure, such as the presence of light sea fermions, rather than from perturbative QED processes which are absorbed into known radiative corrections. Assuming an average of roughly 0.7 × 10⁻⁷ light sea positrons per valence quark, virtual electron-positron annihilation produces an extra term in the electron-proton interaction with the right sign and magnitude to explain the proton radius discrepancy.

## Method

The author examines two candidate mechanisms for electron-muon nonuniversality in lepton-proton scattering. First, the coupling of a projectile electron into an electronic vacuum-polarization loop inserted into a photon exchanged between two valence quarks is shown, via Feynman propagator analysis, to be equivalent to a radiative correction to double scattering and is absorbed into the proton's polarizability contribution to the Lamb shift. Second, the author proposes that nonperturbative light sea electron-positron pairs inside the proton produce an annihilation-channel interaction for electronic hydrogen (but not muonic hydrogen), modeled as a Dirac-δ potential whose strength is parameterized by the sea fermion density per valence quark.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the vacuum-polarization-induced effective potential to the finite-size Hamiltonian is estimated as R ∼ 2.2 × 10⁻⁶, too small to explain the proton radius puzzle.
- The electronic and muonic hydrogen proton charge radius values are roughly 0.88 fm and 0.84 fm, respectively.
- To explain the discrepancy, the ratio ⟨H_ann⟩/⟨H_fs⟩ must equal 0.089, yielding ε_p = 2.1 × 10⁻⁷ for the electron-positron pair density within the proton.
- Per valence quark, this corresponds to ε_p/3 = 0.7 × 10⁻⁷ sea fermion pairs.

## Context

This work addresses the proton radius conundrum reported by Pohl et al. (2010) and Antognini et al. (2013), where muonic hydrogen spectroscopy gives a significantly smaller proton charge radius than electronic hydrogen measurements. The author shows that perturbative mechanisms such as those considered by Jentschura (2011) are absorbed into standard radiative corrections, and instead proposes a nonperturbative light sea fermion mechanism. The paper extends earlier work on the proton's internal electromagnetic structure by Chodos et al. (1974) and others, and suggests that the old electron-muon scattering experiment by Camilleri et al. (1969), which found an 8% cross-section difference, urgently needs independent verification.
