# Two Particles in a Trap

> The Busch-formula relates the energy-spectrum of two point-like particles interacting in a 3-D isotropic Harmonic Oscillator trap to the free scattering phase-shifts of the particles. This formula is used to find an expression for the \it shift \rm in the spectrum from the unperturbed (non-interacting) spectrum rather than the spectrum itself. This shift is shown to be approximately $Δ=-δ(k)/π\times dE$, where $dE$ is the spacing between unperturbed energy levels. The resulting difference from the Busch-formula is typically 1/2% except for the lowest energy-state and small scattering length when it is 3%. It goes to zero when the scattering length $\rightarrow \pm \infty$. The energy shift $Δ$ is familiar from a relatedproblem, that of two particles in a spherical infinite square-well trap of radius $R$ in the limit $R\rightarrow \infty$. The approximation ishowever as large as 30% for finite values of $R$, a situation quite different from the Harmonic Oscillator case. The square-well results for $R\rightarrow \infty$ led to the use ofin-medium (effective) interactions in nuclear matter calculations that were $\propto Δ$ and known as the \it phase shift approximation \rm.Our results indicate that the validity of this approximation depends on the trapitself, a problem already discussed by DeWitt more than 50 years ago for acubical vs spherical trap.

# Two Particles in a Trap

## Key claim
The phase-shift approximation, which states that the energy shift of two interacting particles in a trap is approximately Δ = −δ(k)/π × dE (where dE is the unperturbed level spacing), is highly accurate for a 3-D isotropic Harmonic Oscillator trap (typically within 1/2% error) but can be as inaccurate as 30% for a spherical infinite square-well trap of finite radius, showing that the validity of the approximation depends critically on the trap geometry.

## Method
The author used the Busch-formula, which relates the energy spectrum of two point-like particles in a 3-D isotropic Harmonic Oscillator trap to free scattering phase-shifts, and rewrote it in terms of energy shifts rather than absolute energies. For the spherical infinite square-well trap, the author solved the boundary condition equations for s-wave radial wavefunctions, requiring the wavefunctions to vanish at the sphere radius R, and compared the exact energy shifts to the phase-shift approximation for various quantum numbers n and scattering lengths.

## Key numbers
- The difference between the Busch-formula result and the phase-shift approximation is typically less than 1/2% for the Harmonic Oscillator trap, except for the lowest energy state (n=0) and small scattering length where it reaches 3%.
- The phase-shift approximation becomes exact (difference goes to zero) when the scattering length a → ±∞ in the Harmonic Oscillator case.
- For the spherical infinite square-well trap, the deviation from the phase-shift approximation can be as large as 30% for finite values of the radius R.
- The level spacing in the uncorrelated Harmonic Oscillator system is dE = 2 (in units of ℏω).

## Context
This work extends the classic result by Reifman and DeWitt (1956) and others (Fukuda and Newton, 1956; DeWitt, 1956; Riesenfeld and Watson, 1956) who showed that for two particles in a large spherical box, the energy shift approaches Δ = −δ(k)/π × dE, known as the phase-shift approximation used in early nuclear matter calculations by Brueckner (1960) and others. The paper shows that this approximation is far more accurate for the Harmonic Oscillator trap than for the spherical square-well trap, echoing a problem discussed by DeWitt more than 50 years ago regarding cubical versus spherical traps. The results enable better understanding of when the phase-shift approximation can be reliably applied in trapped atomic and nuclear systems, and raise questions about its validity for other trap geometries such as the cubical box studied by Lüscher (1991).
