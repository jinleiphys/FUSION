# Quark Number Susceptibility in Hard Thermal Loop Approximation

> We calculate the quark number susceptibility in the deconfined phase of QCD using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the quark propagator. This improved perturbation theory takes into account important medium effects such as thermal quark masses and Landau damping in the quark-gluon plasma. We explicitly show that the Landau damping part in the quark propagator for spacelike quark momenta does not contribute to the quark number susceptibility due to the quark number conservation. We find that the quark number susceptibility only due to the collective quark modes deviates from that of free one around the critical temperature but approaches free results at infinite temperature limit. The results are in conformity with recent lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the quark number susceptibility in the deconfined phase of QCD using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the quark propagator and quark-meson vertices, finding that the result deviates from the free susceptibility around the critical temperature but approaches the free result in the infinite temperature limit, in conformity with recent lattice calculations. The key hedge is that the calculation does not aim at a complete leading order perturbative calculation but rather studies the influence of medium effects incorporated in the HTL resummed quark propagator, using this propagator for the entire momentum range instead of consistently distinguishing between soft and hard momenta.

## Method

The authors compute the quark number susceptibility from the time-time component of the vector meson self-energy using the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, evaluating the imaginary part of the polarization tensor at zero momentum. They use HTL-resummed quark propagators and HTL quark-meson vertices for the self-energy and tadpole diagrams, which incorporate thermal quark masses and Landau damping, and show that the tadpole diagram partially compensates the cut contribution from Landau damping. The final expression for the susceptibility contains only pole-pole contributions from the two collective quark modes (quark and plasmino modes), with the cut contributions dropping out due to quark number conservation.

## Key numbers

- The quark number susceptibility at T=5T_c lies 25-40% below the free result, depending on the choice of coupling constant parameters.
- The critical temperature T_c is about 170 MeV, as suggested by numerical solutions of QCD.
- The thermal quark mass is given by m_q = g(T) T / sqrt(6), with the strong coupling constant alpha_s(T) = 12 pi / ((33-2N_f) ln(Q^2/Lambda_0^2)) where Lambda_0 = 200-300 MeV.
- For T_c=170 MeV and Lambda_0=300 MeV, the susceptibility decreases by about 5% compared to the dashed curves shown in the paper.
- The free field theory susceptibility is given by chi_q^f(T) = N_f T^2 in the units used.

## Context

This work extends the study of quark number susceptibility beyond lowest order perturbation theory (McLerran 1987, Hatsuda 1994) by incorporating non-perturbative medium effects through the HTL approximation. The results agree with recent lattice calculations by Gavai (2001) and are similar to findings from an approximately self-consistent resummation of HTLs by Blaizot, Iancu, and Rebhan (2001), though the present work emphasizes the importance of effective HTL resummed vertices in compensating the Landau damping contribution. The calculation enables the use of quark number susceptibility as an observable related to charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions.
