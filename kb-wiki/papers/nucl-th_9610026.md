# Elliptical flow -- a signature for early pressure in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Elliptical energy flow patterns in non-central Au(11.7AGeV) on Au reactions have been studied employing the RQMD model. The strength of these azimuthal asymmetries is calculated comparing the results in two different modes of RQMD (mean field and cascade). It is found that the elliptical flow which is readily observable with current experimental detectors may help to distinguish different reasonable expansion scenarios for baryon-dense matter. The final asymmetries are very sensitive to the pressure at maximum compression, because they involve a partial cancelation between early squeeze-out and subsequent flow in the reaction plane. This cancelation can be expected to occur in a broad energy region covered by the current heavy ion fixed-target programs at BNL and at CERN.

# Elliptical flow -- a signature for early pressure in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
Elliptical flow in non-central Au+Au collisions at 11.7 AGeV involves a partial cancellation between early squeeze-out (out-of-plane) and later in-plane flow, making the final azimuthal asymmetry highly sensitive to the pressure at maximum compression. The RQMD model predicts that a factor of 2 difference in early pressure is not washed out in later evolution, allowing experimental distinction between different expansion scenarios for baryon-dense matter.

## Method
The author employed the RQMD (Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model to simulate Au(11.7 AGeV) on Au collisions at impact parameter 6 fm, comparing two modes: a cascade mode (ideal gas of hadrons and resonances) and a mean-field mode with repulsive quasi-potentials that generate additional pressure at high baryon density. The azimuthal asymmetry in energy flow was quantified using a variable E_dir that sums hadron energies weighted by sign(phi) within a rapidity cut of +/-0.7 around center-of-mass rapidity, and the time evolution of pressure, energy density, and baryon density was computed using the virial theorem.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 11.7 AGeV for Au on Au collisions
- Impact parameter: 6 fm
- Maximum compression values: energy density approximately 1.3 GeV/fm^3, baryon density approximately 3.5 times ground state density (rho_0)
- Passage time of spectators: approximately 5.4 fm/c at 12 AGeV and 1.4 fm/c at 160 AGeV for the heaviest systems
- The mean field effect on average nucleon transverse momentum (p_tr) is 10 percent, while the effect on azimuthal asymmetry is approximately 60 percent larger in the cascade mode compared to the mean-field mode
- Rapidity cut for analysis: +/-0.7 around center-of-mass rapidity

## Context
This work extends studies of directed transverse flow and squeeze-out at energies around 1 AGeV (Partlan 1995) to higher energies at the AGS and CERN fixed-target programs. It builds on predictions by Ollitrault (1993) that at sufficiently high energies the main flow direction becomes parallel to the impact parameter, and on preliminary data from E877 and NA49 collaborations showing elliptical flow patterns. The paper enables a quantitative program to extract the early pressure in baryon-dense matter by combining elliptical flow measurements with radial flow measurements, addressing a gap left by directed flow studies which are concentrated in fragmentation regions and receive strong preequilibrium contributions.
