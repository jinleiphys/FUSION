# On the possible decoherence observed at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV

> We briefly discuss the possible decoherence observed at the LHC emphasizing that the value of the average transverse momentum at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV can be interpreted as a manifestation of the deviation from the mechanism of the transient state of rotating matter.

# On the possible decoherence observed at √s = 7 TeV

## Key claim
The observed deviation of the average transverse momentum at √s = 7 TeV from the power-law energy dependence that described data up to 2.36 TeV may indicate the onset of decoherence in proton interactions, possibly signaling a gradual phase transition from a strongly interacting transient state (quark-pion liquid) into a weakly interacting gas of quarks and gluons (quark-gluon plasma). The authors explicitly note this interpretation remains speculative pending further experimental studies at higher energies.

## Method
The authors fit a power-law energy dependence ⟨p_T⟩(s) = a + c s^{δ_C} to existing pp and p̅p data up to √s = 2.36 TeV, with parameters a = 0.337 GeV/c, c = 6.52×10⁻³ GeV/c, and δ_C = 0.207. They then compare the recent CMS measurement at √s = 7 TeV to this fitted curve and observe a deviation, which they interpret as a possible loss of the collective rotation mechanism that had previously explained the energy rise of average transverse momentum, anisotropic flows, and ridge correlations.

## Key numbers
- The fitted power-law parameters for ⟨p_T⟩(s) = a + c s^{δ_C} using data up to √s = 2.36 TeV are: a = 0.337 GeV/c, c = 6.52×10⁻³ GeV/c, and δ_C = 0.207.
- The exponent δ_C = 0.207 for average transverse momentum numerically coincides with the exponent for mean multiplicity energy dependence.
- The deviation from the fitted energy dependence is observed at √s = 7 TeV, in the region √s > 3 TeV where the energy dependence is claimed to have started changing.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier mechanism (Troshin and Tyurin, prior papers) where hadron collisions at non-zero impact parameter generate rotating transient matter (quark-pion liquid) that explains the ridge, anisotropic flows v₁ and v₂, and the energy rise of ⟨p_T⟩ in pp collisions up to 2.36 TeV. The observed deviation at 7 TeV contradicts the continued validity of that collective rotation picture and is contrasted with an alternative analysis (M. K. L., 2011) that used lower exponent values based on non-universality of pp and p̅p data. The result suggests that LHC data may now be probing the transition from strongly interacting collective matter toward a weakly interacting quark-gluon plasma, a possibility previously predicted in the authors' 2010 work. Further measurements at higher LHC energies are needed to confirm or refute this interpretation.
