# Modeling of Deuteron Induced Reactions on Molybdenum at Low Energies

> The activities of the EUROfusion consortium on the development of high quality nuclear data for fusion applications include evaluations of deuteron induced reactions and related data libraries for needs of the DEMO fusion power plant and IFMIF-DONES neutron--source nuclear analyses. Molybdenum is one of the major constituents of the reference stainless steels used in critical components of these projects. While the TENDL deuteron data library was the current reference used by EUROfusion, need of its further improvement has already been pointed out. The weak binding energy of the deuteron is responsible for the high complexity of its interaction with nuclei, involving also a variety of reactions initiated by the nucleons following the deuteron breakup. Their analysis completed that of the deuteron interactions with Mo and its stable isotopes, from elastic scattering to pre-equilibrium and compound--nucleus reactions, up to 50 MeV. A particular attention has been paid to the breakup, stripping, and pick-up direct interactions which amount to around half of the deuteron total--reaction cross section. The due account of most experimental data has validated the present approach, highlighted some prevalent features, and emphasized weak points and consequently the need for modeling/evaluation upgrade.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent nuclear-model analysis of deuteron-induced reactions on molybdenum, explicitly accounting for breakup, stripping, and pick-up direct interactions alongside statistical processes, can validate most experimental data up to 50 MeV, but the authors emphasize that weak points remain and an upgrade of the modeling and evaluation is still needed.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous analysis of deuteron elastic scattering and activation cross sections on natural molybdenum and its stable isotopes (⁹²Mo, ⁹⁴Mo, ⁹⁵Mo, ⁹⁶Mo, ⁹⁷Mo, ⁹⁸Mo, ¹⁰⁰Mo) up to 50 MeV. They used the Daehnick et al. optical model potential for deuterons, the code FRESCO for distorted-wave Born approximation calculations of stripping and pick-up direct reactions, and TALYS-1.96 for breakup (elastic and inelastic breakup), pre-equilibrium emission, and compound-nucleus evaporation contributions.

## Key numbers

- Breakup, stripping, and pick-up direct interactions amount to around half of the deuteron total-reaction cross section.
- The maximum of stripping excitation functions occurs around 8-12 MeV.
- The pre-equilibrium plus compound-nucleus fraction reaches its maximum at deuteron energies of 15-20 MeV.
- The analysis covers deuteron energies up to 50 MeV.
- The spin distribution cut-off parameter was amended by a factor of 0.25 to improve agreement for ground and isomeric state excitation functions of ⁹¹Nb, ⁹³Mo, and ⁹³,⁹⁴,⁹⁶,⁹⁷Tc residual nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of deuteron activation on structural materials (Al, V, Cr, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zr, Nb) and addresses the need for improvement of the TENDL deuteron data library identified by the EUROfusion consortium. The analysis uses the Daehnick et al. (1980) optical model potential and compares results with the TENDL-2021 library, while the direct reaction calculations rely on spectroscopic factors from earlier experimental studies such as Freeman et al. and Sharp et al. The validated approach enables more reliable evaluated nuclear data for the DEMO fusion power plant and IFMIF-DONES neutron-source design.
