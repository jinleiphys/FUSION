# Dyson-Schwinger Equation Approach to the QCD Deconfinement Transition and J/Psi Dissociation

> We consider an extension of the finite-temperature Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to heavy mesons and quarkonia and apply it to calculate the cross section for the J/Psi breakup reaction J/Psi + pi --> D + D. We study the effects of chiral symmetry restoration in the light quark sector on this process and obtain a critical enhancement of the reaction rate at the chiral/ deconfinement transition. Implications for the kinetics of charmonium production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions are discussed with particular emphasis on the recently observed anomalous J/Psi suppression as a possible signal for quark matter formation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the anomalous (quark-exchange) contribution to the J/ψ dissociation cross section via the reaction J/ψ + π → D + D̅ shows a critical enhancement at the chiral/deconfinement transition temperature, which could explain the threshold effect in the transverse-energy dependence of J/ψ suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration. The hedge is that this is a qualitative demonstration using a simplified separable model with a Gaussian formfactor, and the absolute cross section without in-medium effects is much smaller than nonrelativistic potential model results.

## Method

The authors extend a finite-temperature Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach with a confining, separable rank-1 model gluon propagator to heavy mesons and quarkonia. They solve the quark DSE gap equation at finite temperature to obtain the temperature-dependent dynamical quark mass, then use the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation to compute meson masses and the triangle diagram for the D* → Dπ decay as a test. The J/ψ dissociation cross section is calculated from the box diagram (quark exchange process) as a function of center-of-mass energy and temperature, with the temperature dependence entering through the D-meson mass modification due to chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key numbers

- Chiral restoration transition temperature: T_c = 150 MeV
- Model parameters: D_0 = 227.8 GeV^{-2}, Λ = 0.60 GeV, m_0 = 7.2 MeV
- Heavy quark mass: m_c = 1.844 GeV
- Calculated meson masses (experimental values in brackets): M_D = 1.869 GeV (1.8693 ± 0.0005 GeV), M_{D*} = 2.006 GeV (2.0067 ± 0.0005 GeV), M_{J/ψ} = 3.459 GeV (3.09688 ± 0.00004 GeV)
- D*Dπ coupling constant: g_{D*Dπ} = 10.7, compared to experimental value 10.0 ± 1.3
- Onset of comover absorption cross section in NA50 data at transverse energy E_T ∼ 40 GeV
- Nonrelativistic potential model peak cross section for J/ψ dissociation: about 6 mb

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature DSE approach previously applied to light mesons (π, ρ) by Blaschke et al. (2000) and others to heavy mesons and charmonium, going beyond nonrelativistic potential model calculations by Matinyan and Müller (1998) that inadequately treat pions as Goldstone bosons. The results enable a microscopic interpretation of the anomalous J/ψ suppression observed by the NA50 collaboration at CERN SPS as a signal for quark-gluon plasma formation, and provide a framework for future quantitative calculations of charmonium production at RHIC and LHC colliders, including possible J/ψ enhancement from D D̅ annihilation (gain process) as discussed by Ko et al. (1998) and others.
