# Dispersion $γZ$-box correction to the weak charge of the proton

> We consider elastic scattering of electrons off a proton target. The parity violating (PV) asymmetry arises at leading order in $α$ due to interference of $γ$ and $Z$ exchange. The radiative corrections to this leading mechanism were calculated in the literature and included in experimental analyses, except for $γZ$ box and cross-box contributions. We present here a dispersion calculation of these corrections in forward kinematics. We demonstrate that at the GeV energies of current PV experiments, such corrections are not suppressed by the small vector weak charge of the electron, as occurs in the atomic PV. Our results suggest that the current theoretical uncertainty in the analysis of the QWEAK experiment might be substantially underestimated, and more accurate account of the dispersion corrections are needed in order to interprete the PV data.

## Key claim
The dispersion γZ-box correction to the proton weak charge, previously assumed to be suppressed (≤0.65%) based on atomic parity-violation arguments, is calculated to be about 5.7% at the 1.16 GeV energy of the QWEAK experiment, suggesting the current theoretical uncertainty of 2.2% may be substantially underestimated.

## Method
The authors calculate the real part of the γZ direct and crossed box diagrams using a dispersion relation formalism, starting from the imaginary part of the forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering amplitude (γ*p → Z*p). The imaginary part is expressed in terms of parity-violating deep-inelastic scattering (PVDIS) structure functions F̃₁,₂,₃, which are modeled using a combination of nucleon resonance contributions (in Breit-Wigner form with dipole transition form factors) and a high-energy Regge part described by a color dipole picture/vector meson dominance (CDP/VMD) approach. The dispersion integral is evaluated up to 500 GeV, with the axial-vector part (odd in ν) and vector part (even in ν) treated separately.

## Key numbers
- The predicted weak charge of the proton in the Standard Model is Q_W^p = 1 − 4 sin²θ_W ≈ 0.05, based on sin²θ_W(0) = 0.23807 ± 0.00017.
- The current theoretical uncertainty in the QWEAK analysis is 2.2%.
- At the QWEAK experiment energy of 1.16 GeV, the calculated Re δ_{γZ_A} correction is 5.7%.
- The previous estimate assumed the γZ-box correction was ≤0.65%.
- The QWEAK experiment aims for a 4% measurement of Q_W^p and a 2% measurement of the parity-violating asymmetry A^PV.
- The upper limit of the dispersion integral was set at 500 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the treatment of radiative corrections to parity-violating electron-proton scattering, which previously included γγ, ZZ, and WW box contributions calculated by Erler and Sirlin (2000s). The authors challenge the assumption from atomic parity violation (Sirlin, 1980) that γZ-box corrections are suppressed by the small vector weak charge of the electron, showing that at GeV energies the axial-vector part is not suppressed. The results indicate that the QWEAK experiment at Jefferson Lab may require a 30% fractional accuracy on the ~6% dispersion correction to match its proposed 2% experimental error, and that experimental PVDIS data would be needed to reduce model dependence.
