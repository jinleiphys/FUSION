# Single Quark Transition Model Analysis of Electromagnetic Nucleon Resonance Transitions in the [70,1-] Supermultiplet

> We apply the single quark transition model to resonance transition amplitudes extracted from photo-and electroproduction data. We use experimental data on the S11(1535) and D13(1520) nucleon resonances to extract the amplitudes for the electromagnetic transition from the nucleon ground state to the [70,1-] SU(6)xO(3) supermultiplet, and make predictions for the transition amplitudes of all other states associated with the [70,1-]. We find suprisingly good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The single quark transition model (SQTM), using only the experimentally determined amplitudes for the S11(1535) and D13(1520) resonances together with mixing angles from hadronic decay analyses, can predict the electromagnetic transition amplitudes for all other states in the [70,1-] supermultiplet with surprisingly good agreement, though the comparison is hampered by poor data quality for many states, especially in the electroproduction sector.

## Method

The authors applied the single quark transition model (SQTM) to resonance helicity amplitudes extracted from photo- and electroproduction data, assuming factorization of spin and spatial transition matrix elements and using previously determined mixing angles from hadronic decay properties for states with the same spin, parity, and flavor. They used experimental data on the S11(1535) and D13(1520) nucleon resonances to extract the three quark multipole moments (e11, m11, m12) at fixed Q2, then parametrized their Q2 dependence using a dipole form factor and polynomial fits to predict amplitudes for all other states in the [70,1-] supermultiplet. The predictions were compared with photoproduction data from the Particle Data Group compilation and electroproduction data from Bonn, DESY, NINA, and JLab.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle for the 1/2- states: approximately 31 degrees, estimated from hadronic decay properties, leading to the physical states |S11(1535)> = 0.85|N2,1/2-> - 0.53|N4,1/2-> and |S11(1650)> = 0.53|N2,1/2-> + 0.85|N4,1/2->
- Mixing angle for the 3/2- states: approximately 6 degrees, leading to |D13(1520)> = 0.99|N2,3/2-> - 0.11|N4,3/2-> and |D13(1700)> = 0.11|N2,3/2-> + 0.99|N4,3/2->
- Reduced quark multipole parametrizations valid for q_EVF^2 less than 4 GeV^2: e11 = 2.67 + 1.10 q_EVF^2 - 0.21 q_EVF^4; m11 = 1.32 - 4.51 q_EVF^2 - 0.10 q_EVF^4; m12 = -1.34 + 9.20 q_EVF^2 - 3.39 q_EVF^4 + 0.34 q_EVF^6
- The SQTM predictions show good agreement with data for Q2 less than or equal to 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the SQTM approach of Hey, Cottingham, and Close (1970s) to analyze electromagnetic transitions from the nucleon ground state [56,0+] to the [70,1-] supermultiplet using modern experimental data, including new JLab measurements. It tests the consistency of mixing angles derived from hadronic decay analyses (Isgur and Karl; Koniuk and Isgur) against electromagnetic data, and enables predictions for states such as S11(1650), D13(1700), and D33(1700) that can be tested with future high-precision data from MAMI, GRAAL, and JLab. The analysis is limited to the [70,1-] supermultiplet because insufficient data exist for other supermultiplets, though new JLab two-pion electroproduction data may allow future determination of states in the [56,2+] multiplet.
