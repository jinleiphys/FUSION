# Tracking energy fluctuations from fragment partitions in the Lattice Gas model

> Partial energy fluctuations are known tools to reconstruct microcanonical heat capacities. For experimental applications, approximations have been developed to infer fluctuations at freeze out from the observed fragment partitions. The accuracy of this procedure as well as the underlying independent fragment approximation is under debate already at the level of equilibrated systems. Using a well controlled computer experiment, the Lattice Gas model, we critically discuss the thermodynamic conditions under which fragment partitions can be used to reconstruct the thermodynamics of an equilibrated system.

## Key claim

The independent fragment approximation, combined with a liquid-drop parametrization of fragment binding energies fixed at low temperature, can reconstruct the average kinetic energy and its fluctuations from fragment partitions in the Lattice Gas model with good accuracy (within a few percent) only at subcritical pressures \(p/p_c \lesssim 1/3\); at higher pressures the approximation tends to break down, though the fluctuation estimation stays at the 30% accuracy level even in the supercritical regime.

## Method

The authors performed microcanonical "isobar" ensemble simulations of the 3D Lattice Gas model, imposing the average volume via a Lagrange multiplier \(\lambda\) (interpreted as a constraining pressure). They compared the exact interaction energy \(V\) and its independent-fragment approximation \(Q_{LD} = \sum_f (a_V A_f + a_S A_f^{2/3})\) using liquid-drop parameters fitted to low-temperature simulations (\(a_V = -2.86\epsilon\), \(a_S = 2.73\epsilon\)). They then computed the average kinetic energy and its normalized fluctuation as functions of total energy for several pressures, and also tested the effect of using the same \(Q_{LD}\) to estimate the total energy (calorimetric reconstruction).

## Key numbers

- Liquid-drop parameters fitted at \(T/T_c \approx 0.29\) and density \(\rho/\rho_0 = 0.0135\): \(a_V = -2.86\epsilon\), \(a_S = 2.73\epsilon\), where \(\epsilon\) is the lattice coupling.
- At subcritical pressures up to \(\lambda/\lambda_c = 0.33\), the percentage error on the kinetic energy fluctuation \(|\sigma_Q^2 - \sigma_K^2|/\sigma_K^2\) is below 10% for all energies.
- At the critical pressure \(\lambda/\lambda_c = 1.00\), the percentage error on the fluctuation remains below 20%.
- The \(Q_{LD}/V\) correlation coefficient \(c\) exceeds 0.4 at subcritical pressures, dropping to about 0.2 in the supercritical regime (\(\lambda/\lambda_c = 2.46\)).
- When calorimetric reconstruction is included, the systematic error on the fluctuation stays below the 20% level at subcritical pressures using fixed low-temperature liquid-drop parameters.
- The validity condition for the simple approximation corresponds to an average volume \(V \gtrsim 1.5 V_c\).

## Context

This work extends the debate on the independent fragment hypothesis raised by Campi et al. (2001) using molecular dynamics with a Lennard-Jones potential, who criticized the zero-temperature approximation for fragment binding and the neglect of interfragment nuclear forces. The authors also build on the fluctuation-based heat capacity reconstruction method proposed by Chomaz et al. (2000) and tested on statistical models by D'Agostino et al. (2002). The results enable a quantitative assessment of when fragment partitions from equilibrated systems can reliably reconstruct thermodynamic observables, and they highlight that dynamical effects and quantum corrections (absent in classical models like Lattice Gas or Lennard-Jones) must be addressed before applying these methods to nuclear data.
