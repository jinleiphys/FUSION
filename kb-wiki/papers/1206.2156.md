# High-lying Gamow-Teller excited states in the deformed nuclei, $^{76}\textrm{Ge}$ and $^{82}\textrm{Se}$, by the smearing of the Fermi surface in Deformed Quasi-particle RPA (DQRPA)

> With the advent of high analysis technology in detecting the Gamow-Teller (GT) excited states beyond one nucleon emission threshold, the quenching of the GT strength to the Ikeda sum rule seems to be recovered by the high-lying GT states. Moreover, in some nuclei, the stronger GT peaks than any other peaks appear explicitly in the high-lying excited states. We address that these high-lying GT excited states stems from the smearing of the Fermi surface by the increase of the chemical potential owing to the deformation within a framework of the deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation (DQRPA). Detailed mechanism leading to the smearing is discussed, and comparisons to the available experimental data are shown to explain the strong peaks on the high-lying GT excited states in a satisfactory manner.

## Key claim

The paper claims that high-lying Gamow-Teller excited states in deformed nuclei arise from the smearing of the Fermi surface caused by an increase in chemical potential due to deformation, as calculated within the deformed quasi-particle random phase approximation (DQRPA). The authors qualify that this mechanism explains the strong peaks observed in high-lying GT states, but note that contributions from delta excitation and two-body currents may still be effective for the quenching problem.

## Method

The authors used a deformed axially symmetric Woods-Saxon potential to describe single-particle states in a deformed basis, then performed deformed BCS and deformed QRPA calculations with a realistic two-body interaction derived from an ab initio Brueckner G-matrix based on the Bonn CD potential. The single-particle states were expanded from the Nilsson basis into the spherical basis using overlap integrals, and the GT strength distributions were calculated for the deformed nuclei 76Ge and 82Se as a function of the deformation parameter β2.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge with β2 = 0.35, the Ikeda sum rule ratios ISR I and ISR II are 96.48% and 99.54%, respectively, with theoretical pairing gaps Δp_th = 1.562 MeV and Δn_th = 1.545 MeV.
- For 82Se with β2 = 0.2, the ISR I and ISR II are 95.62% and 107.55%, respectively, with theoretical pairing gaps Δp_th = 1.419 MeV and Δn_th = 1.547 MeV.
- The experimental GT data for both 76Ge and 82Se were deduced from (p,n) reactions at 134.4 MeV, showing a strong GT state peak around 12 MeV excitation energy.
- For 76Ge, the deformation parameter β2 = 0.35 reproduces the high-lying peak, while the RMF value is 0.157; for 82Se, β2 = 0.2 reproduces the peak, while the RMF value is 0.133.
- The single-particle model space was truncated to 4 ℏω for both nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous spherical QRPA calculations by the same group (Ch93) to include deformation, and builds on the deformed QRPA formalism of Simkovic (using Nilsson basis with separable forces) and Saleh (using realistic interactions but only T=1, J=0 pairing). The results enable better understanding of high-lying GT states relevant to neutrino-induced reactions in supernovae and the nucleosynthesis of neutron-deficient nuclei such as 138La and 180Ta, and suggest that the GT quenching problem may be partially resolved by including deformation effects.
