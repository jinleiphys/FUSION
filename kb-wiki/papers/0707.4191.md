# Neutron Form Factor from Neutrino-Nucleus Coherent Elastic Scattering

> We analyze the prospect of measuring the neutron form factor of a nucleus through the detection of neutrino-nucleus coherent elastic scattering. We predict numbers of events in a liquid noble nuclear recoil detector at a stopped pion neutrino source. We discuss the precision required to distinguish between different theoretical models for the form factor.

## Key claim

Measuring the neutron form factor of a nucleus via neutrino-nucleus coherent elastic scattering is feasible with a stopped-pion neutrino source and a liquid noble nuclear recoil detector, but distinguishing between different theoretical models for the form factor requires systematic uncertainties below 5% to extract the neutron radius to better than 10% for argon-40.

## Method

The authors calculate expected event rates for neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering in a CLEAN detector filled with liquid argon-40, placed 20 meters from the stopped-pion neutrino source at the Oak Ridge Spallation Neutron Source (SNS). They fold the known energy spectra of electron neutrinos and muon antineutrinos from muon decay at rest with the differential cross section for coherent elastic scattering, using an analytic form factor that models the neutron and proton density distributions with a constant interior density and surface thickness. The neutron radius is varied by ±10%, ±15%, and ±20% relative to the proton radius to simulate different nuclear structure predictions, and the resulting event rates are binned in 10 keV intervals of nuclear recoil energy.

## Key numbers

- The expected neutrino flux at 20 meters from the SNS target is 10⁷ neutrinos per second per cm² for each flavor (ν_e, ν_μ, and ν̅_μ).
- For the case R_n = R_p, the predicted event rate in the 60–70 keV bin is 778 events per (keV year tonne) from ν_e and ν̅_μ combined.
- For R_n = R_p + 20%, the event rate in the 80–90 keV bin is 301 events per (keV year tonne), a 13% difference from the R_n = R_p case (347 events).
- For R_n = R_p + 10%, the event rate in the 90–100 keV bin is 194 events per (keV year tonne), a 10% difference from the R_n = R_p case (215 events).
- The low-energy threshold of the detector is expected to be about 10 keV nuclear recoil energy.
- The proton RMS radius for argon-40 is taken as R_p = 3.43 fm, with a surface thickness s = 0.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the idea of using neutrino-nucleus elastic scattering, originally proposed by Freedman (1974), to measure neutron distributions, complementing the parity-violating electron scattering approach planned for lead-208 at JLAB (the PREX experiment, tentatively scheduled for 2008). The specific experimental configuration (CLEAN detector at the SNS) was previously proposed by Scholberg (2006) for detecting coherent elastic scattering and searching for beyond-standard-model physics. The authors note that using a nuclear recoil detector at a low-energy beta beam was also considered by Bueno et al. (2003), Auerbach et al. (2005), and Barranco et al. (2005). This work enables future experiments to calibrate nuclear structure calculations by providing a new probe of neutron densities at different energy ranges and for different nuclei than electron scattering.
