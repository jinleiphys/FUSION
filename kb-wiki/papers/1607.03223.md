# Understanding spin parity of $P_c(4450)$ and $Y(4274)$ in a hadronic molecular state picture

> The hidden-charmed pentaquark $P_c(4450)$ and the charmonium-like state $Y(4274)$ are investigated as a $\bar{D}^*Σ_c$ and a $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ molecular state, respectively. The spin parities of these two states cannot be well understood if only S-wave $\bar{D}^*Σ_c$ and $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ interactions are considered. In this work, the interactions are studied in a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach with a partial wave decomposition on spin parity $J^P$, and the contributions of different partial waves are studied in a two-channel scattering model including a generating channel and an observation channel. Two poles at $4447\pm4i$ and $4392\pm46i$ MeV are produced from the $\bar{D}^*Σ_c$ interaction coupled with the $J/ψp$ channel in $3/2^-$ wave and $5/2^+$ wave, respectively. The peak for the $5/2^+$ state has a comparable height as that of the $3/2^-$ state in the $J/ψp$ invariant mass spectrum. The $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ interaction coupled with the $J/ψϕ$ channel is studied and a pole at $4275\pm11i$ MeV is produced in $J^{P}=1^{+}$ wave, which corresponds to P-wave $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ interaction. The pole from S-wave $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ interaction is far below that from P-wave interaction even the $J/ψϕ$ threshold, so cannot be observed in the $J/ψϕ$ channel. The result suggests that in these cases a state carrying a spin parity corresponding to P-wave interaction should be taken as seriously as these carrying a spin parity corresponding to S-wave interaction in the hadronic molecular state picture.

## Key claim

The spin parities of $P_c(4450)$ and $Y(4274)$ cannot be explained by S-wave hadronic molecular interactions alone; the author shows that P-wave interactions, though weaker than S-wave, can still produce observable bound states that should be taken as seriously as S-wave states in the hadronic molecular state picture.

## Method

The interactions were studied using a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation approach with partial wave decomposition on spin parity $J^P$, employing a two-channel scattering model with a generating channel (higher threshold) and an observation channel (lower threshold). For $P_c(4450)$, the $\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$ interaction was coupled to the $J/\psi p$ channel via $D$ and $D^*$ exchanges; for $Y(4274)$, the $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ interaction was coupled to the $J/\psi\phi$ channel via $D^*_s$ exchange. Poles were found by analytic continuation into the complex plane to satisfy $|1-V(z)G(z)|=0$.

## Key numbers

- Pole at $4447\pm4i$ MeV from $\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$ interaction coupled with $J/\psi p$ channel in $5/2^+$ wave (cutoff $\Lambda=1.7$ GeV), identified as $P_c(4450)$
- Pole at $4392\pm46i$ MeV from $\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$ interaction coupled with $J/\psi p$ channel in $3/2^-$ wave (cutoff $\Lambda=1.7$ GeV), identified as $P_c(4380)$
- Pole at $4275\pm11i$ MeV from $D_s\bar{D}_{s0}(2317)$ interaction coupled with $J/\psi\phi$ channel in $J^P=1^{+}$ wave (cutoff $\Lambda=1.8$ GeV, $\eta_{\beta\beta'}=1.8$), identified as $Y(4274)$
- With cutoff varying from 1.6 to 1.8 GeV, the $5/2^+$ pole mass decreases from 4456 to 4438 MeV; the $3/2^-$ pole mass decreases from 4441 to 4350 MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (He 2015) of $\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*$, $\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$, and $\bar{D}^*\Sigma^*_c$ interactions for LHCb pentaquarks, and earlier S-wave molecular interpretations of $Y(4274)$ by Liu (2010) and He (2013). The results challenge the common assumption that only S-wave interactions produce molecular states, showing that P-wave bound states can be narrower and closer to threshold than S-wave states, which matches the experimental pattern of $P_c(4450)$ (narrow) versus $P_c(4380)$ (broad). The work enables future experimental searches for P-wave molecular states in other hadronic systems and suggests that spin parity assignments from partial wave analysis should be taken as seriously as S-wave predictions.
