# Anisotropic hydrodynamics

> The recently formulated framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics is used in 3+1 dimensions to study behavior of matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The model predictions for various hadronic observables show that the effects of the initial anisotropy of pressure may be compensated by appropriate adjustment of the initial energy density. In this way, the final hadronic observables become insensitive to the early stage dynamics and the early thermalization/isotropization puzzle may be circumvented.

## Key claim
The effects of initial pressure anisotropy on final hadronic observables can be compensated by adjusting the initial energy density, making the observables insensitive to the early-stage dynamics and thereby circumventing the early thermalization/isotropization puzzle, but only if the initial energy density is separately tuned for each assumed initial anisotropy value.

## Method
The authors solved the 3+1 dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics equations, which conserve energy-momentum and govern entropy production, using a generalized equation of state based on a realistic QCD equation of state from Chojnacki (2007). They initialized the system at proper time 0.25 fm with either strongly oblate (x=100) or prolate (x=0.032) momentum-space anisotropy, evolved until the entropy density dropped to 1.79 fm^{-3} (corresponding to T=150 MeV for isotropic case), and then used the THERMINATOR Monte Carlo code with the Cooper-Frye formalism to generate hadrons on the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- Initial starting time: tau_0 = 0.25 fm
- Initial anisotropy parameter values: x = 100 (oblate) and x = 0.032 (prolate)
- Initial energy density at center for most central collisions: 48.8 GeV/fm^3 for x_0 = 100, and 80.1 GeV/fm^3 for x_0 = 0.032
- Freeze-out entropy density: sigma_f = 1.79 fm^{-3}, corresponding to T_f = 150 MeV for x=1
- Equilibration time-scale parameter: tau_eq = 1.0 fm/c (constant)
- Beam energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (Au+Au collisions at RHIC)

## Context
This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework introduced by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), Ryblewski (2010), and others, which was developed to handle large momentum-space anisotropies at early times in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with standard perfect-fluid and viscous hydrodynamics approaches (Chaudhuri 2006, Song 2007, Luzum 2008, Schenke 2010) that assume near-isotropic conditions or small shear corrections. The results enable future studies to focus on later-stage dynamics without needing to resolve the early thermalization puzzle, though the entropy production formula for large anisotropies remains an assumption that could be refined by AdS/CFT correspondence insights (Heller 2011).
