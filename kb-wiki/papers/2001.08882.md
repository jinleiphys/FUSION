# Single $Λ_c^+$ hypernuclei within quark mean-field model

> The quark mean-field (QMF) model is applied to study the single $Λ^+_c$ hypernuclei. The charm baryon, $Λ^+_c$, is constructed by three constituent quarks, $u, ~d$, and $c$, confined by central harmonic oscillator potentials. The confinement potential strength of charm quark is determined by fitting the experimental masses of charm baryons, $Λ^+_c,~Σ^+_c$, and $Ξ^{++}_{cc}$. The effects of pions and gluons are also considered to describe the baryons at the quark level. The baryons in $Λ^+_c$ hypernuclei interact with each other through exchanging the $σ,~ω$, and $ρ$ mesons between the quarks confined in different baryons. The $Λ^+_c N$ potential in the QMF model is strongly dependent on the coupling constant between $ω$ meson and $Λ^+_c$, $g_{ωΛ^+_c}$. When the conventional quark counting rule is used, i. e., $g_{ωΛ^+_c}=2/3g_{ωN}$, the massive $Λ^+_c$ hypernucleus can exist, whose single $Λ^+_c$ binding energy is smaller with the mass number increasing due to the strong Coulomb repulsion between $Λ^+_c$ and protons. When $g_{ωΛ^+_c}$ is fixed by the latest lattice $Λ^+_c N$ potential, the $Λ^+_c$ hypernuclei only can exist up to $A\sim 50$.

## Key claim

The quark mean-field (QMF) model predicts that single $\Lambda_c^+$ hypernuclei can exist, but their stability and maximum mass number depend critically on the coupling constant $g_{\omega\Lambda_c^+}$: using the conventional quark counting rule ($g_{\omega\Lambda_c^+}=2/3g_{\omega N}$) allows massive $\Lambda_c^+$ hypernuclei to exist, while using the coupling fixed by lattice QCD $\Lambda_c^+ N$ potentials limits existence to $A\sim 50$.

## Method

The authors constructed $\Lambda_c^+$ baryons from three constituent quarks ($u$, $d$, $c$) confined by central harmonic oscillator potentials, with the charm quark confinement strength fitted to experimental masses of $\Lambda_c^+$, $\Sigma_c^+$, and $\Xi_{cc}^{++}$. They applied the quark mean-field (QMF) model where baryons in $\Lambda_c^+$ hypernuclei interact through exchanging $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ mesons between quarks confined in different baryons, including pion and gluon corrections at the quark level. The coupling constants between $\Lambda_c^+$ and mesons were determined by two schemes: naive quark counting rules and lattice QCD simulations from the HAL QCD Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark masses used: 1300, 1350, and 1400 MeV, corresponding to up/down quark masses of 250, 300, and 350 MeV respectively
- The confinement potential parameters for charm quark: $a_c$ values range from 0.116036 to 0.118172 fm$^{-3}$ across the three parameter sets
- Charmed baryon masses from the model reproduce experimental data with errors less than 5%
- When $g_{\omega\Lambda_c^+}$ is fixed by lattice QCD potentials, $\Lambda_c^+$ hypernuclei can only exist up to mass number $A\sim 50$
- Pion mass used in calculations: $m_\pi=140$ MeV; pion decay constant: $f_\pi=93$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark mean-field model, previously applied successfully to normal nuclei and $\Lambda$, $\Xi$ hypernuclei (Hu et al., 2014; Xing et al., 2016, 2017), to the study of charmed hypernuclei. It builds on earlier theoretical work by Dover and Kahana (1977) who first predicted charmed hypernuclei using $SU(4)$ symmetry, and more recent lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Miyamoto et al., 2016, 2017, 2018) which found attractive $\Lambda_c^+ N$ potentials. The results are compatible with calculations using the quark meson-coupling model (Tsushima et al., 2003, 2004, 2018) and relativistic mean-field model (Tan et al., 2004), and with the recent microscopic many-body approach by Vidaña et al. (2019). This work enables future experimental searches for charmed hypernuclei at facilities such as FAIR and J-PARC.
