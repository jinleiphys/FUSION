# Nuclear configurational entropy and high energy hadron-hadron scattering reactions

> In this work, the high energy hadron-hadron scattering is studied in the framework of holographic AdS/QCD models, using the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan Pomeron exchange kernel and gravitational form factors. We apply the configurational entropy techniques to estimate the slope of the total cross section for the total hadron-hadron cross sections at high energies. A good agreement is derived between our approach and the total cross section for combinations that include the pion-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon, and pion-pion, as well as for any high energy data with inclusion of data from the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC and approximated by the Pomeron exchange. In the case of pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering, the agreement for the critical points to the differential configurational entropy can be reached within 1.1% even without the involvement of any extra parameters.

## Key claim
The slope of the total hadron-hadron cross section at high energies, derived from the global minimum of the nuclear configurational entropy, agrees with the experimentally fitted value from Watanabe (2018) within 1.1%, but the DCE minimum occurs at ρ = 0.814 rather than the reference value of ρ = 0.824, indicating the most stable nuclear configuration is slightly offset from the best-fit experimental parameter.

## Method
The author computed total hadron-hadron cross sections for pion-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon, and pion-pion scattering using the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron exchange kernel in holographic AdS/QCD, with density distributions derived from gravitational form factors for nucleons and pions. The nuclear configurational entropy (DCE) was then calculated via Fourier transform of the cross section as a function of the slope parameter ρ, and the global minimum of the DCE was identified as the critical point corresponding to the most stable nuclear configuration.

## Key numbers
- Global minimum of nuclear DCE occurs at ρ = 0.814, with DCE = 164.72 nat (natural units of information).
- The reference value of ρ from Watanabe (2018) is 0.824; the DCE critical point agrees within 1.1%.
- The fixed cut-off parameter for the nucleon density distribution is z₀^N = 4.081 keV.
- The cut-off parameter for the pion is z₀^π = 3.105 keV, with σ = (332 MeV)³ and α = (2π/3)σ.
- The two adjustable parameters from experimental data are g₀² = 6.27 × 10² and ρ = 0.824 (from Watanabe (2018)).
- The energy range considered is 10² < √s < 10⁵ GeV, using data from the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC and Particle Data Group.

## Context
This work extends the application of configurational entropy techniques, previously used for inelastic hadron cross sections and nuclear systems at extreme conditions (Karapetyan, 2018; Karapetyan, 2019), to total hadron-hadron cross sections within the BPST Pomeron exchange framework of Watanabe (2018). The approach enables a parameter-free prediction of the most stable configuration for high-energy hadron scattering, and the DCE critical point method can be applied to other scattering processes to determine natural energy-dependence parameters without additional adjustable parameters.
