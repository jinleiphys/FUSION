# Radiative resonance couplings in $γπ\toππ$

> Studies of the reaction $γπ\toππ$, in the context of the ongoing Primakoff program of the COMPASS experiment at CERN, give access to the radiative couplings of the $ρ(770)$ and $ρ_3(1690)$ resonances. We provide a vector-meson-dominance estimate of the respective radiative width of the $ρ_3$, $Γ_{ρ_3\toπγ}=48(18)$ keV, as well as its impact on the $F$-wave in $γπ\toππ$. For the $ρ(770)$, we establish the formalism necessary to extract its radiative coupling directly from the residue of the resonance pole by analytic continuation of the $γπ\toππ$ amplitude to the second Riemann sheet, without any reference to the vector-meson-dominance hypothesis.

# Radiative resonance couplings in γπ → ππ

## Key claim

The paper provides a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) estimate of the radiative width of the ρ₃(1690) resonance, Γ_{ρ₃→πγ} = 48(18) keV, and establishes the formalism to extract the radiative coupling of the ρ(770) directly from the residue of the resonance pole by analytic continuation of the γπ → ππ amplitude to the second Riemann sheet, without relying on the VMD hypothesis. The authors note that for the ρ(770) a simplified VMD approach is not adequate due to the statistics available at the ρ peak, which should allow a significantly sharper test of the chiral low-energy theorem.

## Method

The authors construct a dispersive representation of the γπ → ππ amplitude using twice-subtracted dispersion relations with basis functions calculated from ππ phase shifts (from GKPY equations), with two free subtraction constants C₂⁽¹⁾ and C₂⁽²⁾ that encode the chiral anomaly and the ρ radiative coupling. For the ρ₃(1690), they use a VMD model coupling the ρ₃ to ππ and πω via Lagrangian interactions, with the photon coupling through the ω meson, and determine the coupling constants from the known ρ₃ branching ratios and mass. The analytic continuation of the P-wave amplitude to the ρ pole on the second Riemann sheet is performed using the unitarity relation, yielding an expression for the ρπγ coupling g_{ρπγ} in terms of the amplitude evaluated at the pole position.

## Key numbers

- Γ_{ρ₃→πγ} = 48(18) keV (VMD estimate for the ρ₃(1690) radiative width)
- |g_{ρππ}| = 6.01⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀·₀₇ (GKPY determination of the ρ → ππ coupling from pole residue)
- |g_{ρ₃ππ}| = 0.056(2), |g_{ρ₃πω}| = 1.2(2) GeV⁻² (couplings extracted from ρ₃ branching ratios)
- |g_{ωγ}| = 16.7(2) (extracted from Γ_{ω→e⁺e⁻})
- σ_F(m_{ρ₃}²) = 1.7(6) μb (peak cross section of the F-wave at the ρ₃ resonance, roughly 6% of the dominant ρ(770) peak)
- M_ρ = 763.7⁺¹·⁷₋₁·₅ MeV, Γ_ρ = 146.4⁺²·⁰₋₂·₂ MeV (GKPY pole parameters for the ρ(770))
- m_{ρ₃} = 1688.8(2.1) MeV, Γ_{ρ₃} = 161(10) MeV (ρ₃ mass and width)
- BR(ρ₃ → ππ) = 23.6(1.3)%, BR(ρ₃ → πω) = 16(6)% (ρ₃ branching ratios)
- C₂⁽¹⁾ = 9.9(1.0) GeV⁻³, C₂⁽²⁾ = 24.1(2.5) GeV⁻⁵ (central values of subtraction constants from current constraints)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive formalism for γπ → ππ developed by Hoferichter (2012) to enable model-independent extraction of the ρ(770) radiative coupling, and provides the first VMD estimate of the ρ₃(1690) radiative width for the COMPASS Primakoff program. The formalism allows simultaneous determination of the chiral anomaly F_{3π} (currently tested at the 10% level, far behind the 1.5% accuracy in π⁰ → γγ) and the ρ radiative couplings from the same data set. The results are directly relevant to the hadronic-light-by-light contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, where γπ → ππ appears as a crucial input for a data-driven determination of the π⁰ → γ*γ* transition form factor, and complement lattice calculations of γ*π → ππ reported by Briceno (2015, 2016).
