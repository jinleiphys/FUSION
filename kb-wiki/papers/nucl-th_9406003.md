# Neutrino Oscillations in Noisy Media

> We develop the Redfield equation for delta-correlated gaussian noise and apply it to the case of two neutrino flavor or spin precession in the presence of a noisy matter density or magnetic field, respectively. The criteria under which physical fluctuations can be well approximated by the delta-correlated gaussian noise for the above cases are examined. Current limits on the possible neutrino magnetic moment and solar magnetic field suggest that a reasonably noisy solar magnetic field would not appreciably affect the solar electron neutrino flux. However, if the solar electron density has fluctuations of a few percent of the local density and a small enough correlation length, the MSW effect is suppressed for a range of parameters.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Redfield equation for delta-correlated Gaussian noise and applies it to two-neutrino flavor or spin precession in a noisy matter density or magnetic field, showing that the MSW effect can be suppressed for a range of parameters if the solar electron density has fluctuations of a few percent of the local density and a small enough correlation length, but that current limits on the neutrino magnetic moment and solar magnetic field suggest a reasonably noisy solar magnetic field would not appreciably affect the solar electron neutrino flux.

## Method

The authors derive a Redfield equation (Eq. 16) for the ensemble-averaged density matrix of an N-level system subject to a delta-correlated Gaussian random field, starting from the Schrodinger-like evolution equation and iterating the time-ordered expansion. They then apply this formalism to two specific two-level neutrino problems: spin-flavor precession in a noisy solar magnetic field (with Hamiltonian given by Eq. 27) and flavor precession with a noisy matter density (Eq. 33), solving the resulting coupled differential equations both analytically for special cases and numerically for the solar case using the Bahcall solar electron density profile.

## Key numbers

- For spin-flavor precession in the sun, with a neutrino transition magnetic moment of mu = 3 x 10^{-12} mu_B and an assumed rms magnetic field of 100 kG from 0.7 R_sun to 0.85 R_sun, the maximum average muon antineutrino production is about 3% for Delta m^2 / E near 1 x 10^{-8} eV^2/MeV.
- For flavor precession with noisy matter density, with rms density fluctuations of 2% of the local density (sqrt(<beta^2>) = 0.02), the MSW transition probability is suppressed by as much as 20% in the region around Delta m^2 / E = 1 x 10^{-5} eV^2/MeV.
- When the rms density fluctuation is doubled to 4% of the average density, the suppression saturates at a probability of one half for values near Delta m^2 / E = 1 x 10^{-5} eV^2/MeV.
- The correlation length required for significant suppression of the MSW effect is about 40 km (corresponding to tau_c of about 10 km for Delta m^2 / E = 1 x 10^{-5} eV^2/MeV and sin^2 2theta = 0.01).

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino oscillations in inhomogeneous matter, building on the general approach of Sawyer (1990) and the specific treatment of random magnetic fields by Nicolaidis (1991) and Enqvist and Semikoz (1993). The authors find a different result from Enqvist and Semikoz: rather than a noisy magnetic field acting like a larger constant matter density, they find that both noisy magnetic fields and noisy densities act to depolarize neutrinos, with the transition probability eventually relaxing to one half. The results enable future numerical studies of density fluctuations in stellar collapse and supernova dynamics, and suggest that solar density fluctuations of a few percent with correlation lengths around 40 km could significantly suppress the MSW effect for neutrinos whose resonant transition occurs deep in the sun.
