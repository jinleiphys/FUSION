# Energy Dependence of exotic nuclei production cross sections by photofission reaction in GDR range

> Photofission of actinides is studied in the region of nuclear excitation energies that covers the entire giant dipole resonance (GDR) region. The mass distributions of $^{238}$U photofission fragments have been explored theoretically for eight different endpoint bremsstrahlung energies from 11.5 MeV to 67.7 MeV which correspond to average photon energy of 9.09 MeV to 15.90 MeV. Among these energies, the 29.1 MeV corresponds to the average photon energy of 13.7$\pm$0.3 MeV which coincides with GDR peak for $^{238}$U photofission. The integrated yield of $^{238}$U photofission as well as charge distribution of photofission products are calculated and its role in producing nuclei and their neutron-richness is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for photofission of $^{238}$U, the maximum production cross sections for neutron-rich exotic nuclei occur at an average photon energy of 13.7$\pm$0.3 MeV, which coincides with the giant dipole resonance (GDR) peak, but this advantage is limited to nuclei with neutron richness of $8 \pm 3$ (cross sections in the mb range) while production drops to the $\mu$b range for neutron richness of $14 \pm 2$.

## Method

The authors used a multimode-fission model with five Gaussian functions (one symmetric mode and two asymmetric double-Gaussian modes) to fit mass yield distributions of $^{238}$U photofission fragments at eight bremsstrahlung endpoint energies from 11.5 MeV to 67.7 MeV. Photofission cross sections were calculated using Lorentz line shapes for the GDR with a ratio method relating photoabsorption and photofission parameters, scaled by fissility $f=Z^2/A$. Charge distributions were modeled as Gaussians with parameters $C_p=0.8$ and shift $\Delta=3.8$, assumed independent of photon energy over the range studied.

## Key numbers

- Eight endpoint bremsstrahlung energies from 11.5 MeV to 67.7 MeV were studied, corresponding to average photon energies from 9.09 MeV to 15.90 MeV.
- The 29.1 MeV endpoint energy corresponds to average photon energy 13.7$\pm$0.3 MeV, which coincides with the GDR peak for $^{238}$U photofission.
- At average photon energy 13.7 MeV, the number of neutron-rich species produced with cross sections $>$100 fb is 777, compared to 743 at 9.09 MeV and 765 at 15.9 MeV.
- For $^{138}$Xe at 13.7 MeV: cross section = $3.893\times 10^{0}$ mb; at 9.09 MeV: $8.182\times 10^{-1}$ mb; at 15.9 MeV: $2.184\times 10^{0}$ mb.
- For $^{101}$Zr at 13.7 MeV: cross section = $4.813\times 10^{0}$ mb; at 9.09 MeV: $1.185\times 10^{0}$ mb; at 15.9 MeV: $2.429\times 10^{0}$ mb.
- For $^{80}$Zn (waiting point nucleus) at 13.7 MeV: cross section = $2.6\times 10^{-3}$ mb (2.6 $\mu$b).
- For $^{134}$Sn (waiting point nucleus) at 13.7 MeV: cross section = $0.18$ $\mu$b.
- For $^{162}$Ce: production cross section = $3.2\times 10^{-15}$ mb, with last neutron separation energy 2.2 MeV.
- Gaussian parameters for 13.7$\pm$0.3 MeV: $C_{SM}=0.49\pm0.25$, $\sigma_{SM}=4.47\pm3.33$, $C_{ASMI}=5.90\pm0.28$, $\sigma_{ASMI}=5.96\pm0.28$, $C_{ASMII}=2.29\pm0.27$, $\sigma_{ASMII}=1.62\pm0.39$.

## Context

This work extends the multimode-fission model analysis of De08, which calculated fission mode contributions at two accelerator energies, and the qualitative discussion of Po93. The results are compared with experimental data from Na13, Is14, and Be15. The study is directly relevant to the design of e-LINAC-based radioactive ion beam facilities such as ARIEL at TRIUMF (under construction), ALTO at IPN Orsay, JINR Dubna, and the planned ANURIB facility at VECC Kolkata, providing guidance that electron energies within 50 MeV are optimal for producing neutron-rich nuclei via photofission.
