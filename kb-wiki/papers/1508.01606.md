# New Photodisintegration Model of GEANT4 for the $dγ\to np$ Reaction with a Dibaryon Effective Field Theory

> We develop a new hadronic model for GEANT4 that is specialized for the disintegration of the deuteron by photons, $dγ\to n p$. For the description of two-nucleon interactions, we employ a pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT). We apply the new model of GEANT4 (G4dEFT) to the calculations of the total and the differential cross sections in $dγ\to n p$ and compare the results with empirical data. As an application of the new model, we calculate the neutron yield from the $γ+ {\rm CD}_2$ process. G4dEFT predicts peaks for the neutron yield, but the existing model of GEANT4 does not show such behavior.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new GEANT4 hadronic model (G4dEFT) for the deuteron photodisintegration reaction \(d\gamma \to np\) using a pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT), and show that it reproduces experimental cross sections where the existing GEANT4 model (G4EmExtraPhysics) gives a null result for energies below the pion threshold (about 70 MeV).

## Method

The authors incorporate analytic formulae from dEFT up to next-to-leading order (NLO) into the GEANT4 simulation toolkit, replacing the default Bertini-style cascade model for photonuclear interactions on deuterons. They calculate total and differential cross sections for \(d\gamma \to np\) and compare them with experimental data from the EXFOR database and evaluated nuclear data libraries (ENDF/B-VII.1, JENDL/PD-2004). As an application, they simulate neutron yields from a \(\gamma + \text{CD}_2\) target using a cylindrical geometry with a spherical scoring shell.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section of \(d\gamma \to np\) from G4dEFT is zero for \(E_\gamma \leq 70\) MeV in the existing G4 model, but G4dEFT gives non-zero results that agree with data within experimental errors for \(E_\gamma < 15\) MeV.
* For \(15 < E_\gamma < 30\) MeV, ratios of G4dEFT total cross sections to experimental data are largely in the range 1.0 to 1.2, with discrepancies of factors of about 2 at \(E_\gamma = 25.4\) MeV and \(E_\gamma = 25.7\) MeV where experimental errors are large.
* For \(E_\gamma > 30\) MeV, G4dEFT agrees with four experimental data points within about 20% error.
* Differential cross sections at \(E_\gamma = 19.8\) MeV agree with experimental data within error bars; at \(E_\gamma = 38.6\) MeV there are systematic underestimates at backward angles.
* Ratio of neutron yields from \(\gamma + \text{CD}_2\) for \(E_\gamma = 9.8\) MeV to \(E_\gamma = 19.8\) MeV is \(2.293 \pm 0.046\), close to the ratio of total cross sections (2.287) for those energies (1.44 mb at 9.8 MeV, 0.63 mb at 19.8 MeV).
* Peaks in neutron energy spectra appear at \(E_n \sim 3.7\) MeV for \(E_\gamma = 9.8\) MeV and at \(E_n \sim 8.7\) MeV for \(E_\gamma = 19.8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of GEANT4 to low-energy few-nucleon systems, motivated by the failure of built-in hadronic models for the \(^9\text{Be}(p,n)^9\text{B}\) reaction reported by Shin (2014). The authors use a pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields (dEFT) developed by Hyun and collaborators (2010-2015), which has been successfully applied to two-nucleon systems at low energies. The new model enables simulation of neutron yields from photon-induced reactions on deuterated targets, which could be tested experimentally to validate the approach and improve low-energy photonuclear simulations in GEANT4.
