# Small x parton distributions and initial conditions in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> At the colliders RHIC and LHC, nuclei at the ultrarelativistic energies of $100$ GeV/A and $2.7$ TeV/A will be smashed together with the hope of creating an elusive and short-lived state of matter called the quark gluon plasma. The initial conditions which determine the dynamical evolution of the quark gluon matter formed in the central region after the collision depend crucially on the small $x$ component of the nuclear wavefunction before the collision. In this comment, we discuss recent work which argues that, for large nuclei, weak coupling techniques in QCD can be used to calculate the distribution of these small $x$, or wee, partons. The ramifications of this approach for the dynamics of heavy ion collisions and the various signatures of a quark gluon phase of matter are discussed.

## Key claim

Weak coupling techniques in QCD can be used to calculate the distribution of small \(x\) partons in large nuclei, but only when the parton density is so large that it sets the scale for the running coupling constant (\(\alpha_S(\rho) \ll 1\)); the approach specifically applies for \(x \ll A^{-1/3}\) and relies on the valence quark color charge per unit area \(\mu^2 \sim A^{1/3}\) fm\(^{-2}\) as the intrinsic scale.

## Method

The author formulates the problem in light cone gauge \(A^+=0\) by treating valence quarks as static external sources \(\rho_a(x^+,\vec{x}_\perp)\delta(x^-)\) and averaging over their color configurations with a Gaussian weight \(\exp\left(-\frac{1}{2\mu^2}\int d^2x_t\,\rho^2(x)\right)\). The classical background field is obtained by solving the non-linear Yang-Mills equations \(D_\mu F^{\mu\nu}=gJ^\nu\), yielding a pure gauge field \(\alpha_i\) satisfying \(\vec{\nabla}\cdot U\vec{\nabla}U^\dagger = -ig^2\rho(x_t)\). Quantum corrections are computed via a Dyson-Schwinger expansion around this background, and nuclear collisions are treated as the collision of two such Weizsäcker-Williams fields, with the post-collision classical gluon field obtained by solving the non-linear Yang-Mills equations with boundary conditions set by each nucleus's field.

## Key numbers

* RHIC center-of-mass energy: 100 GeV/A; LHC center-of-mass energy: 2.7 TeV/A.
* The valence quark color charge squared per unit area: \(\mu^2 \approx 1.1\,A^{1/3}\) fm\(^{-2}\).
* The classical gluon distribution for a single nucleus in the weak coupling limit (\(k_t \gg g^2\mu\)): \(\frac{1}{\pi R^2}\frac{dN}{dx d^2q_t} = \frac{\alpha_S \mu^2 (N_c^2-1)}{\pi^2}\frac{1}{x q_t^2}\).
* The one-loop corrected gluon distribution for a single nucleus: \(\frac{1}{\pi R^2}\frac{dN}{dx d^2k_t} = \frac{\alpha_s \mu^2 (N_c^2-1)}{\pi^2}\frac{1}{x k^2_t}\left\{1 + \frac{2\alpha_s N_c}{\pi}\ln\left(\frac{k_t}{\alpha_s\mu}\right)\ln\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)\right\}\).
* The gluon distribution at late times after a nuclear collision: \(\frac{1}{\pi R^2}\frac{dN}{dy d^2k_\perp} = \frac{16\alpha_s^3}{\pi^2} N_c (N_c^2-1)\frac{\mu^4}{k_t^4}\ln\left(\frac{k_t}{\alpha_s\mu}\right)\).

## Context

This comment extends the Weizsäcker-Williams approach to QCD at small \(x\), building on earlier work by the author and collaborators (LV1-LV5) and by Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (KLW1, KLW2). It contrasts with the parton cascade model of Geiger and Muller and the HIJING model of Wang and Gyulassy, which assume factorization and incoherent multiple scattering, arguing instead that small \(x\) partons have highly coherent wave-like interactions. The approach enables a first-principles QCD description of initial conditions for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and suggests that deep inelastic scattering experiments on nuclei at HERA would complement the heavy ion program by probing the same Bjorken \(x\) range.
