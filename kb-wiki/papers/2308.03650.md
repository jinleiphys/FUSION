# Hexadecapole axial collectivity in the rare earth region, a beyond mean field study

> Hexadecapole collectivity and its interplay with quadrupole degrees of freedom is studied in an axial symmetry preserving framework based on the Hartree Fock Bogoliubov (HFB) plus generator coordinate method (GCM). Results are obtained for several even-even isotopes of Sm and Gd with various parametrizations of the Gogny force. The analysis of the results indicates the strong coupling between the quadrupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom. The first two excited states are vibrational in character in most of the cases. The impact of prolate-oblate shape mixing in the properties of hexadecapole states is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quadrupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom are strongly coupled in the rare-earth region, meaning they cannot be treated independently in generator coordinate method calculations; however, the authors note that the predicted excitation energies for hexadecapole vibrational states are likely overestimated because the formalism omits collective momentum degrees of freedom and triaxial effects.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with constraints on both the quadrupole moment Q20 and hexadecapole moment Q40, using the Gogny D1S and D1M* parametrizations. They then solved the Griffin-Hill-Wheeler equation in the generator coordinate method (GCM) framework, using a two-dimensional mesh of β2 and β4 deformation parameters with step sizes of 0.02, for several even-even isotopes of Sm (Z=62) and Gd (Z=64). The calculations used 17 major harmonic oscillator shells and were checked against 19-shell results.

## Key numbers

- For 154Sm with D1S, the 2D GCM correlation energy is 1.239 MeV, compared to 0.694 MeV for the 1D β2-only calculation and 0.663 MeV for the 1D β4-only calculation
- The ground state hexadecapole deformation for 154Sm is β4 = 0.21 (using the R0 definition) or β4 = 0.17 (using the ⟨r4⟩ definition)
- The first excited 0+ state in 154Sm from the 2D GCM calculation lies at 2.635 MeV excitation energy with D1S
- The second excited 0+ state in 154Sm from the 2D GCM calculation lies at 3.059 MeV excitation energy with D1S
- The 1D GCM calculation along the B direction (perpendicular to the valley bottom) for 154Sm yields a correlation energy of 0.555 MeV and a first excited state at 3.075 MeV
- For 154Sm, the HFB minimum shows an approximate linear relation β4 = 1.8 β2 − 0.4 along the valley bottom

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field studies of quadrupole collectivity (such as the β-vibration analyses of Garrett (2001, 2018)) by incorporating the hexadecapole degree of freedom, which has been predicted to be significant in the rare-earth region by systematic HFB calculations with Gogny D1S. The results are compared to the macroscopic-microscopic calculations of Moller (2016), which give β4 = 0.11 for 154Sm, and to Skyrme interaction results from Scamps (2021). The paper enables future studies of hexadecapole effects in neutrino-less double beta decay matrix elements (Engel, 2017) and in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Ryssens, 2023), and the authors note that extending these calculations to the Kr region could help resolve discrepancies with experimental data from Spieker (2023).
