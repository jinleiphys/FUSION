# New results for the fully renormalized proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation

> A many-body Hamiltonian describing a system of Z protons and N neutrons moving in spherical shell model mean field and interacting among themselves through proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing and a dipole-dipole proton-neutron interaction of both particle-hole and particle-particle type, is treated within a fully renormalized (FR) pnQRPA approach. Two decoupling schemes are formulated. One of them decouples the equations of motion of particle total number conserving and non-conserving operators. One ends up with two very simple dispersion equations for phonon operators which are formally of Tamm-Dancoff types. For excitations in the (N-1,Z+1) system, Ikeda sum rule is fully satisfied provided the BCS equations are renormalized as well and therefore solved at a time with the FRpnQRPA equations. Next, one constructs two operators ${\cal R}^{\dagger}_{1μ}$, ${\cal R}_{1,-μ}(-)^{1-μ}$ which commutes with the particle total number conserving operators, ${\cal A}^{\dagger}_{1μ}$ and ${\cal A}_{1,-μ}(-)^{1-μ}$, and moreover could be renormalized so that they become bosons. Then, a phonon operator is built up as a linear combination of these four operators. The FRpnQRPA equations are written down for this complex phonon operator and the ISR is calculated analytically. This formalism allows for an unified description of the dipole excitations in four neighboring nuclei (N-1,Z+1),(N+1,Z-1),(N-1,Z-1),(N+1,Z+1). The phonon vacuum describes the (N,Z) system ground state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully renormalized proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (FRpnQRPA) can be formulated such that the Ikeda sum rule (ISR) is fully satisfied for excitations in the (N-1,Z+1) system, but only if the BCS equations are also renormalized and solved simultaneously with the FRpnQRPA equations; if standard BCS equations are kept unmodified, the ISR is violated.

## Method

The authors treat a many-body Hamiltonian with proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing plus a dipole-dipole proton-neutron interaction in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels, using a spherical shell model mean field. They construct two decoupling schemes: one separates particle total number conserving and non-conserving operators, yielding two independent Tamm-Dancoff-type dispersion equations for phonon operators; the other builds a complex phonon operator from four renormalized boson-like operators (two conserving and two non-conserving particle number) and writes the FRpnQRPA equations for this operator. The renormalization factors depend on the average quasiparticle numbers in the ground state, requiring the BCS equations for chemical potentials to be renormalized and solved together with the FRpnQRPA equations.

## Key numbers

- The Ikeda sum rule states that the difference of total beta-minus and beta-plus strengths for a single beta-minus emitter equals 3(N-Z).
- The number of FRpnQRPA solutions is twice the number of standard pnQRPA solutions, specifically 2N_s where N_s is the number of proton-neutron states coupling to angular momentum one.
- The dispersion equations for the phonon operators are linear in energy (not quadratic as in standard pnQRPA), meaning they have no vanishing solutions.
- The renormalization factors D_1(pn) and D_2(pn) are defined in terms of BCS occupation probabilities U and V and the average quasiparticle numbers ⟨N_p⟩ and ⟨N_n⟩, with D_1(pn) = U_p^2 - U_n^2 + (U_n^2 - V_n^2)⟨N_n⟩/(2j_n+1) - (U_p^2 - V_p^2)⟨N_p⟩/(2j_p+1).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FRpnQRPA formalism (Raduta and Raduta, 2003) which included scattering terms but violated the Ikeda sum rule. It contrasts with the standard pnQRPA approach that fails at a critical value of the particle-particle interaction strength g_pp, and with the renormalized pnQRPA (pnRQRPA) of Suhonen (1994) which renormalizes only two-quasiparticle dipole operators. The formalism enables a unified description of dipole excitations in four neighboring nuclei (N-1,Z+1), (N+1,Z-1), (N-1,Z-1), and (N+1,Z+1), and provides a framework for calculating nuclear matrix elements relevant to double beta decay calculations.
