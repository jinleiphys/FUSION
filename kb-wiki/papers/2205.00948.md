# Theoretical study of the $γd \to π^0ηd$ reaction

> We have done a theoretical study of the $γd \to π^0 ηd$ reaction starting with a realistic model for the $γN \to π^0 ηN$ reaction that reproduces cross sections and polarization observables at low energies and involves the $γN \to Δ(1700)\to ηΔ(1232) \to ηπ^0 N$ process. For the coherent reaction in the deuteron we considered the impulse approximation together with the rescattering of the pions and the $η$ on a different nucleon than the one where they are produced. We found this second mechanism very important since it helps share between two nucleons the otherwise large momentum transfer of the reaction. Other contributions to the $γd\toπ^0ηd$ reaction, involving the $γN\to π^\pmπ^0 N^\prime$ process, followed by the rescattering of the $π^\pm$ with another nucleon to give $η$ and a nucleon, have also been included. We find a natural explanation, tied to the dynamics of our model, for the shift of the $η-d$ mass distribution to lower invariant masses, and of the $π^0-d$ mass distribution to larger invariant masses, compared to a phase space calculation. We also study theoretical uncertainties related to the large momenta of the deuteron wave function involved in the process as well as to the couplings present in the model. Striking differences are found with the experimental angular distribution and further theoretical investigations might be necessary.

## Key claim

The observed shifts of the η-d mass distribution to lower invariant masses and the π⁰-d mass distribution to larger invariant masses, compared to phase space, arise naturally from the dynamics of the γN → π⁰ηN reaction model rather than requiring an ηd bound state, though the model shows striking differences with experimental angular distributions that require further theoretical investigation.

## Method

The authors constructed amplitudes for γd → π⁰ηd using a model where γN → Δ(1700) → ηΔ(1232) → ηπ⁰N is the dominant mechanism, with the Δ(1700) coupling taken from chiral unitary theory. They evaluated tree-level (impulse approximation) diagrams and added rescattering contributions where the produced π or η interacts with the spectator nucleon via s-wave πN interactions (using two different models) or p-wave πN interactions exciting the spectator to Δ(1232). The deuteron wave function was incorporated using realistic parametrizations from several sources, and the amplitudes were integrated numerically over the loop momentum.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant g_{γpΔ*} = 0.188, determined from the radiative decay width of Δ(1700) from the Particle Data Group (2020)
- Coupling constant g_{ηΔΔ*} = 1.7 − i 1.4, from the chiral unitary approach of Sarkar (2004)
- f* = 2.13 for the Δ → πN vertex
- λ₁ = 0.0075 and λ₂ = 0.053 for the s-wave πN interaction Lagrangian
- g_d = (2π)^{3/2} × 2.68 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹/² for the d ↔ pn coupling
- The rescattering mechanisms of Egorov (2020) produce an increase of about 20–30% in the cross section relative to impulse approximation

## Context

This work extends the γp → π⁰ηp model of Doring (2005), which successfully reproduces cross sections and polarization observables at low energies, to the coherent deuteron reaction γd → π⁰ηd. It complements the calculations of Egorov (2013) and Egorov (2020), which reproduce integrated cross sections but show difficulties with invariant mass and angular distributions. The study addresses the claim by Ishikawa (2021, 2022) that the data demand a bound ηd state, finding instead that the observed mass distribution shifts are explained by the reaction dynamics, and it enables further theoretical work to resolve the discrepancy with experimental angular distributions.
