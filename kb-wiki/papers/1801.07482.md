# Transport Dynamics of Parton Interactions in pp Collisions at LHC Energies

> We investigate the transport dynamics of partons in proton-proton collisions at the Large Hadron Collider using a Boltzmann transport approach, the parton cascade model. The calculations include semi-hard pQCD interaction of partons populating the nucleons and provide a space-time description of the collision in terms of cascading partons undergoing scatterings and fragmentations. Parton production and number of collisions rise rapidly with increase in center of mass energy of the collision. For a given center of mass energy, the number of parton interactions is seen to rise stronger than linear with decreasing impact parameter before saturating for very central collisions. The strangeness enhance factor $γ_s$ for the semi-hard processes is found to rise rapidly and saturate towards the highest collision energies. Overall, our study indicates a significant amount of partonic interactions in proton-proton collisions, which supports the observation of fluid-like behavior for high multiplicity proton-proton collisions observed in the experiments.

## Key claim

The authors use a parton cascade model to show that semi-hard partonic interactions in proton-proton collisions at LHC energies produce enough partons and multiple scatterings to potentially form an interacting medium that could lead to collective behavior, but they explicitly note that their approach cannot describe the dynamics of thermalized degrees of freedom and can only assess whether conditions for QGP formation are met.

## Method

The authors employ the VNI/BMS implementation of the parton cascade model, a Boltzmann transport approach that treats parton scatterings with momentum transfer above a cut-off of 2 GeV using lowest-order pQCD matrix elements. They perform three sets of calculations: (1) only primary-primary parton collisions, (2) multiple scatterings among primary and secondary partons without fragmentation, and (3) multiple scatterings with final-state parton fragmentation included via time-like branchings in the leading-logarithmic approximation. The initial nucleon state uses GRV-HO parton distribution functions initialized at Q² = 4 GeV², with partons distributed spatially according to an exponential distribution giving a root mean square radius of 0.81 fm.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 200 GeV, more than 100 interacting partons are deposited into the system from semi-hard processes with pT > 2 GeV; this number grows to about 500 at 2.76 TeV
- The strangeness enhancement factor γ_s for semi-hard processes saturates at a value of about 0.9 at the highest collision energies
- For the most central collisions at √s = 7 TeV with fragmentation included, on average in excess of 170 parton-parton scatterings and more than 300 fragmentations occur
- The pT cut-off for regularizing infrared divergences is set at 2 GeV, with the virtuality cut-off for terminating time-like branchings set at μ₀ = 1 GeV
- Calculations are performed at center-of-mass energies of 0.2, 2.76, 5.02, 7.00, and 14 TeV for minimum bias collisions
- Impact parameter values studied: 0.0, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm at √s = 7 TeV

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade model studies previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Geiger 1991, Bass 2002) to proton-proton collisions at LHC energies, addressing the recent experimental observations from ALICE (2017) of flow and enhanced strangeness in high-multiplicity pp events. The calculations support the notion that sufficient partonic interactions occur in pp collisions to potentially form a medium exhibiting collective behavior, though the authors note that non-QGP based interpretations remain viable as argued by Blok (2017), Greif (2017), and Aichelin (2016). The results enable future work connecting parton-level dynamics to hydrodynamic descriptions and provide a framework for interpreting strangeness enhancement measurements as a function of charged particle multiplicity.
