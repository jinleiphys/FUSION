# The shape of the $Δ$ baryon in a covariant spectator quark model

> Using a covariant spectator quark model that describes the recent lattice QCD data for the $Δ$ electromagnetic form factors and all available experimental data on $γN \to Δ$ transitions, we analyze the charge and magnetic dipole distributions of the $Δ$ baryon and discuss its shape. We conclude that the quadrupole moment of the $Δ$ is a good indicator of the deformation and that the $Δ^+$ charge distribution has an oblate shape. We also calculate transverse moments and find that they do not lead to unambiguous conclusions about the underlying shape.

## Key claim

Using a covariant spectator quark model that reproduces lattice QCD data and experimental $\gamma N \to \Delta$ transitions, the authors conclude that the $\Delta^+$ charge distribution has an oblate shape based on the spectroscopic quadrupole moment, but they find that transverse moments do not provide unambiguous information about the intrinsic three-dimensional deformation.

## Method

The authors constructed relativistic quark-diquark model wave functions for the $\Delta$ baryon within the covariant spectator theory, using two parameterizations: one with only $S$-wave components (spherically symmetric) and another including $D$-wave components with admixture coefficients $a=0.08556$ and $b=0.08572$ (each about 0.89% probability). They calculated charge and magnetic dipole density distributions in both momentum and coordinate space directly from the wave functions, and compared the resulting spectroscopic quadrupole and octupole moments with transverse moments derived from the electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- $D$-state admixture coefficients: $a=0.08556$ and $b=0.08572$, each corresponding to about 0.89% probability
- For the deformed model (Spectator-SD): $G_{E2}(0) = -1.70$ and $G_{M3}(0) = -1.72$ for the $\Delta^+$
- For the spherical model (Spectator-S): $G_{M1}(0) = 3.29$ for the $\Delta^+$; for the deformed model: $G_{M1}(0) = 3.27$
- Transverse electric quadrupole moment for Spectator-SD: ${\cal Q}_\Delta^\perp(+\sfrac{3}{2}) = 0.92$ in units of $e/M_\Delta^2$
- Transverse electric quadrupole moment for Spectator-S (spherical): ${\cal Q}_\Delta^\perp(+\sfrac{3}{2}) = 0.29$ in units of $e/M_\Delta^2$
- Transverse magnetic octupole moment for Spectator-SD: ${\cal O}_\Delta^\perp(+\sfrac{3}{2}) = -3.38$ in units of $e/(2 M_\Delta^3)$
- Transverse magnetic octupole moment for Spectator-S: ${\cal O}_\Delta^\perp(+\sfrac{3}{2}) = -3.44$ in units of $e/(2 M_\Delta^3)$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\Delta$ deformation by Alexandrou (2009) and others, who found a negative electric quadrupole moment implying oblate intrinsic deformation but a prolate transverse charge density pattern. The authors show that while spectroscopic quadrupole and octupole moments clearly distinguish spherical from deformed $\Delta$ states, transverse moments do not provide the same unambiguous information, as even the spherical $S$-wave model yields nonzero transverse moments. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of the transverse magnetic octupole moment to test the model predictions.
