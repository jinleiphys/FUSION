# Measurements of charged-particle multiplicity dependence of higher-order net-proton cumulants in $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} =$ 200 GeV from STAR at RHIC

> We report on the charged-particle multiplicity dependence of net-proton cumulant ratios up to sixth order from $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV $p$+$p$ collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The measured ratios $C_{4}/C_{2}$, $C_{5}/C_{1}$, and $C_{6}/C_{2}$ decrease with increased charged-particle multiplicity and rapidity acceptance. Neither the Skellam baselines nor PYTHIA8 calculations account for the observed multiplicity dependence. In addition, the ratios $C_{5}/C_{1}$ and $C_{6}/C_{2}$ approach negative values in the highest-multiplicity events, which implies that thermalized QCD matter may be formed in $p$+$p$ collisions.

## Key claim

The measured net-proton cumulant ratios \(C_4/C_2\), \(C_5/C_1\), and \(C_6/C_2\) in 200 GeV \(p\)+\(p\) collisions decrease with increasing charged-particle multiplicity and rapidity acceptance, with \(C_5/C_1\) and \(C_6/C_2\) approaching negative values in the highest-multiplicity events; this is interpreted as implying that thermalized QCD matter may be formed in such collisions, but the authors note that baryon number conservation could also contribute to the decrease and that the PYTHIA8 model, which includes baryon number conservation, does not produce negative values.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed 220 million minimum-bias \(p\)+\(p\) collision events at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, using the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Time of Flight (TOF) detector to identify protons and antiprotons in the transverse momentum range \(0.4 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\) and midrapidity \(|y| < 0.5\). Cumulants up to sixth order of the net-proton multiplicity distribution were calculated as a function of charged-particle multiplicity (defined by tracks with TPC and TOF hits, excluding protons and antiprotons to suppress self-correlations), and efficiency corrections were applied assuming binomial distributions. Statistical uncertainties were estimated via the bootstrap method, and systematic uncertainties were evaluated by varying track quality, particle identification, luminosity, and detector efficiency selections.

## Key numbers

* The multiplicity-averaged ratios show the hierarchy \(C_4/C_2 > C_5/C_1 > C_6/C_2\) with a significance of 8.7\(\sigma\) for the first inequality and 0.9\(\sigma\) for the second.
* Total systematic uncertainties for the multiplicity-averaged results are 0.90% for \(C_4/C_2\), 7.8% for \(C_5/C_1\), and 8.4% for \(C_6/C_2\).
* At high charged-particle multiplicity bins \(m_{\rm ch}^{\rm TPC} > 12\), both \(C_5/C_1\) and \(C_6/C_2\) are consistent with zero within uncertainties.
* The luminosity contribution constitutes 50% of the total systematic uncertainty for \(C_4/C_2\) at \(m_{\rm ch}^{\rm TPC}=4\), increasing to above 90% at \(m_{\rm ch}^{\rm TPC}>18\).

## Context

This work extends the study of higher-order net-proton cumulants, previously measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV (STAR 2021), to the smaller \(p\)+\(p\) collision system at the same energy. The observed decreasing trend of \(C_4/C_2\), \(C_5/C_1\), and \(C_6/C_2\) with multiplicity, and the approach to negative values for \(C_5/C_1\) and \(C_6/C_2\) in the highest-multiplicity events, are consistent with thermodynamic model expectations from lattice QCD calculations (Bazavov et al. 2020) and functional renormalization group (Fu et al. 2021) for thermalized QCD matter, and the results are not reproduced by the PYTHIA8 event generator. These findings enable systematic comparisons with larger collision systems (e.g., \(p\)+Au, \(d\)+Au, Zr+Zr, Ru+Ru) and at higher collision energies to further probe the dynamics of thermalization in small systems.
