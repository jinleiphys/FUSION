# The Dropping of In-Medium Hadron Mass in Holographic QCD

> We study the baryon density dependence of the vector meson spectrum using the D4/D6 system together with the compact D4 baryon vertex. We find that the vector meson mass decreases almost linearly in density at low density for small quark mass, but saturates to a finite non-zero value for large density. We also compute the density dependence of the $η\prime$ mass and the $η\prime$ velocity. We find that in medium, our model is consistent with the GMOR relation up to a few times the normal nuclear density. We compare our hQCD predictions with predictions made based on hidden local gauge theory that is constructed to model QCD.

## Key claim
The vector meson mass decreases almost linearly in baryon density at low density for small quark mass, but saturates to a finite non-zero value at large density, contradicting the vector manifestation (VM) prediction that the mass should vanish at the chiral transition.

## Method
The authors use the D4/D6 holographic system with a compact D4 baryon vertex in a confining background, modeling dense baryonic matter by introducing fundamental strings connecting the baryonic D4 and probe D6 branes. They solve the DBI actions for both branes, impose a force balance condition at the cusp where the strings attach, and compute the meson spectrum from linearized fluctuations of the flavor brane fields (scalar, pseudoscalar, and transverse vector) as a function of baryon density.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3} = 1.28 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^3\)
- Kaluza-Klein scale: \(M_{KK} = 1.039\ \text{GeV}\) (from meson spectra for light quark systems)
- Vector meson mass scaling at low density: \(\Phi(n) \approx 1 - 0.18 \cdot \frac{6}{\lambda} \frac{n}{n_0}\)
- Pseudoscalar (\(\eta'\)) mass scaling at low density: \(m_{\eta'}(n) \approx m_{\eta'}(0) \left(1 - 0.20 \cdot \frac{6}{\lambda} \frac{n}{n_0}\right)\)
- Validity condition for the solution: 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\) must satisfy \(1 < \lambda < N_c\) (with \(N_c = 3\) giving \(\lambda < 2.5\))

## Context
This work extends holographic QCD methods (specifically the D4/D6 system from Kruczenski et al. (2003) and Sakai-Sugimoto) to dense baryonic matter, a regime inaccessible to lattice QCD due to the sign problem. It tests the Brown-Rho scaling (BR91) and the vector manifestation (VM) prediction from hidden local gauge theory (Harada-Yamawaki). The results support a linear mass drop at moderate densities (consistent with BR scaling if \(\lambda \approx 6\)) but predict saturation at high density, contradicting the VM prediction that the vector meson mass vanishes at chiral restoration. The model also finds the \(\eta'\) mass drops faster than the vector meson mass, which disagrees with QCD expectations where the pion mass is protected by chiral symmetry.
