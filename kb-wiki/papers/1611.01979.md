# Nuclear structure calculations in $^{20}$Ne with No-Core Configuration-Interaction model

> Negative parity states in $^{20}$Ne and Gamow-Teller strength distribution for the ground-state beta-decay of $^{20}$Na are calculated for the very first time using recently developed No-Core Configuration-Interaction model. The approach is based on multi-reference density functional theory involving isospin and angular-momentum projections. Advantages and shortcomings of the method are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The No-Core Configuration-Interaction (NCCI) model, rooted in multi-reference density functional theory, can calculate negative parity states in \(^{20}\)Ne and Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the beta-decay of \(^{20}\)Na for the first time, but the results are preliminary and systematically overestimate excitation energies of negative-parity states by approximately 3 MeV while shifting the Gamow-Teller strength distribution toward lower energies relative to experiment.

## Method

The authors used a multi-reference density functional theory (MR-DFT) approach with isospin and angular-momentum projections applied to self-consistent Hartree-Fock Slater determinants built from the SV\(_{\text{so}}\) Skyrme force (a variant of the SV parametrization with spin-orbit strength increased by 20%). For \(^{20}\)Ne, they constructed a configuration space including the ground state and ten positive-parity particle-hole excitations within the \(sd\) shell, plus two negative-parity cross-shell particle-hole configurations; these projected states were then mixed by solving the Hill-Wheeler equation. For the Gamow-Teller strength calculation, the \(I=2^+\) ground state of \(^{20}\)Na was computed using four lowest-lying particle-hole configurations, while the daughter states in \(^{20}\)Ne used the ground state and ten particle-hole configurations.

## Key numbers

- The calculated negative-parity states in \(^{20}\)Ne systematically overestimate excitation energies by approximately 3 MeV.
- The energy splitting between the [101]3/2 and [211]3/2 Nilsson levels in the ground-state configuration is approximately 10 MeV; polarization from particle-hole excitation reduces this by about 2 MeV.
- The Gamow-Teller strength distribution resonant peak is shifted by approximately 3 MeV toward lower energies relative to both shell-model calculations and experimental data.
- The Gamow-Teller strength functions were smoothed using a Lorentzian distribution with half-width \(\Gamma = 0.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DFT-rooted NCCI approach previously applied to rotational spectra and electromagnetic transitions in \(^{25}\)Mg (Bal14b) and to beta-decay matrix elements (Sat14, Kon16). The method addresses the limitations of single-reference DFT, which breaks fundamental symmetries and cannot rigorously describe quantum transitions, by restoring isospin and angular-momentum symmetries through projection techniques. The results enable future optimization of the spin-orbit interaction strength to improve agreement with experimental data, and demonstrate that the Gamow-Teller strength distribution is sensitive enough to serve as a tool for identifying the correct order and spacing of single-particle orbitals.
