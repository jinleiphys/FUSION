# Thermodynamical description of heavy ion collisions

> We analyze the thermodynamical state of nuclear matter in transport descriptions of heavy ion reactions. We determine thermodynamical variables from an analysis of local momentum space distributions and compare to blast model parameters from an analysis of fragment energy spectra. These descriptions are applied to spectator and fireball matter in semi-central and central Au+Au collisions at SIS-energies, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper argues that spectator matter in semi-central Au+Au collisions at SIS energies reaches a well-defined, equilibrated, and unstable freeze-out configuration, while participant matter in central collisions does not form a common equilibrated source, meaning slope temperatures from fragment spectra must be interpreted with caution in that region.

## Method

The authors performed transport calculations for Au+Au collisions and extracted local thermodynamic temperatures (\(T_{loc}\)) by fitting local momentum space distributions. They also generated fragments via phase-space coalescence and analyzed their kinetic energy spectra using a blast model to obtain slope temperatures (\(T_{slope}\)) and radial flow velocities (\(\beta_f\)). These theoretical results were compared for spectator matter (semi-central collisions, impact parameter \(b=4.5\) fm) and participant matter (central collisions) at SIS energies.

## Key numbers

- Local temperature in spectator matter reaches \(T_{loc} \sim 5-6\) MeV at breakup, with density \(\rho \sim (\frac{1}{3}-\frac{1}{2}) \rho_{sat}\).
- Spectator matter enters an instability region (effective compressibility \(K<0\)) after \(t \stackrel{>}{\sim} 40-45\) fm/c.
- For central Au+Au reactions at \(0.6\) AGeV, the nucleon freeze-out time in participant matter is \(t_{freeze-out} \sim 35\) fm/c.
- Slope temperatures for participant fragments rise with fragment mass, while radial flow velocities fall, in contrast to the mass-independent behavior seen for spectator fragments.

## Context

This work extends transport-based studies of the nuclear equation of state at sub-saturation densities and finite temperature, building on prior multifragmentation models (e.g., statistical models cited in the paper) and experimental data from ALADIN (2000) and the EOS collaboration (at 1 AGeV). The finding that spectator matter reaches a common freeze-out configuration supports the use of blast-model analyses for spectators, while the participant-region result contradicts the assumption of a single equilibrated source, as also discussed theoretically by Daffin and Hombach. This enables more careful interpretation of fragment energy spectra in heavy-ion collisions and raises open questions about the formation time and survival of heavier fragments in the fireball.
