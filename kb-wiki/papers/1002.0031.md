# Fission of super-heavy nuclei explored with Skyrme forces

> We present a large scale survey of life-times for spontaneous fission in the regime of super-heavy elements (SHE), i.e. nuclei with Z=104-122. This is done on the basis of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model. The axially symmetric fission path is computed using a quadrupole constraint. Self-consistent cranking is used for the collective masses and associated quantum corrections. The actual tunneling probability is estimated by the WKB approximation. Three typical Skyrme forces are used to explore the sensitivity of the results. Benchmarks in the regime Z=104-108 show an acceptable agreement. The general systematics reflects nicely the islands of shell stabilization and the crossover from $α$-decay to fission for the decay chains from the region of Z/N=118/176.

## Key claim

The paper presents a large-scale survey of spontaneous fission lifetimes for super-heavy elements (Z=104-122) using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model, finding that while the general systematics of shell stabilization and the crossover from alpha-decay to fission are reproduced, the computed fission lifetimes are dramatically underestimated for the decay chain from Z/N=118/176, especially toward the lower end at Z=112/N=170.

## Method

The axially symmetric fission path was computed using a quadrupole constraint on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock energy functional. Self-consistent cranking was used to compute the collective masses and quantum corrections (zero-point energies for angular momentum projection and quadrupole motion). The tunneling probability was estimated using the WKB approximation, with the ground state energy (entrance energy for fission) computed from a fully quantum-mechanical treatment of collective quadrupole oscillations in all five quadrupole degrees of freedom. Three Skyrme parameterizations (SkP, SkI3, and SLy6) were used to explore sensitivity to the force.

## Key numbers

- Fission lifetimes for super-heavy elements range from almost immediate decay to 10^16 s, corresponding to barrier heights from 0 to 12 MeV.
- For the benchmark region Z=104-108, theoretical results deviate from experimental values by ±4 orders of magnitude (the text states "deviations of ±4 orders of magnitude look huge" and "deviations of ±2 orders of magnitude" in the conclusions).
- The crossover from alpha-decay to spontaneous fission occurs at about Z=112 or Z=110 along decay chains from the upper island of stability.
- The quantum corrections on the potential reduce the barrier by an amount that makes lifetimes 3 to 6 orders lower than with the raw potential.
- Long-lived super-heavy elements are found in two islands of stability: one around Z/N=104/152 (deformed) and one around Z/N=120/184 (spherical).

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field calculations of fission barriers (Bur04) to include full fission lifetimes with collective masses and quantum corrections, which are much more involved than barrier calculations alone. The results are compared with experimental data from the lower SHE region (Z=104-108) and with the decay chain from Z/N=118/176 (exp118). The paper enables future investigations into the sensitivity of fission lifetimes to the Skyrme parameterization and the need for improved descriptions in the very soft transitional region around Z/N=112/166, where all three forces grossly underestimate known fission lifetimes.
