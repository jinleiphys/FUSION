# Some aspects of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> In this talk, I discuss some recent results obtained in Heavy Ion Collisions and what they tell us -- or what questions they raise -- about the physics of the system of quarks and gluons formed in these collisions.

## Key claim

The talk discusses recent heavy ion collision results and the questions they raise about quark-gluon plasma physics, but the abstract glosses over that the interpretation of these results depends on whether one assumes weak or strong coupling, and that the early-time dynamics remain an open theoretical question.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results showing the deconfinement phase transition, then examines two key experimental observations from RHIC: jet quenching (disappearance of back-to-back azimuthal correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions) and elliptic flow (anisotropy of transverse momenta in peripheral collisions). The talk compares weak-coupling perturbative QCD predictions with strong-coupling results obtained via the AdS/CFT correspondence applied to N=4 super-symmetric Yang-Mills theory, and discusses the Color Glass Condensate effective theory for early-time dynamics.

## Key numbers

- 99% of the multiplicity in a RHIC collision consists of particles with transverse momentum p_T <= 2 GeV
- The saturation momentum Q_sat(x) grows like x^{-0.3} at small x and depends on nuclear atomic number as A^{1/3}
- The viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio eta/s in the strong coupling limit of N=4 SUSY Yang-Mills theory is 1/(4 pi)
- In weak-coupling perturbative QCD, eta/s scales as (g^4 ln(1/g))^{-1}, making it large in the weak coupling limit

## Context

This talk extends the discussion of heavy ion collisions from lattice QCD results on the deconfinement transition (referenced as "latt") and the Color Glass Condensate effective theory (referenced as "cgc") to the experimental findings from RHIC (referenced as "exp"). The AdS/CFT calculation of eta/s (referenced as "ads") provides a strong-coupling benchmark that contrasts with perturbative QCD calculations (referenced as "eta"), enabling the interpretation that the small viscosity implied by RHIC elliptic flow data may require strong coupling, though this remains an educated guess for QCD.
