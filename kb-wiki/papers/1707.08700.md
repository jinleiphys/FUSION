# Density Functional approach for multi-strange hypernuclei: competition between $Λ$ and $Ξ^{0,-}$ hyperons

> The question of the competition between $Λ$ and $Ξ^{0,-}$ in the ground-state of multi-strange hypernuclei is addressed within a non-relativistic density functional approach, partially constrained by ab-initio calculations and experimental data. The exploration of the nuclear chart for $10<Z<120$ as a function of the strangeness number is performed by adding hyperons to a nuclear core imposing either conserved total charge $Q$ or conserved proton number $Z$. We find that almost all $Λ$ hypernuclei present an instability with respect to the strong interaction decay of $Λ$ towards $Ξ^{0,-}$ and that most of the instabilities generates $Ξ^-$ (resp. $Ξ^0$) in the case of conserved total charge $Q$ (resp. proton number $Z$). The strangeness number at which the first $Ξ^{0,-}$ appear is generally lower for configurations explored in the case of conserved $Q$ compared to the case of conserved $Z$, and corresponds to the crossing between the $Λ$ and the neutron or proton chemical potentials. About two to three hundred thousands pure $Λ$ hypernuclei may exist before the onset of $Ξ^{0,-}$. The largest uncertainty comes from the unknown $ΛΞ$ interaction, since the $NΛ$ and the $NΞ$ ones can be constrained by a few experimental data. The uncertainty on the $ΛΞ$ interaction can still modify the previous estimation by 30-40\%, while the impact of the unknown $ΞΞ$ interaction is very weak.

## Key claim

Almost all pure-Λ hypernuclei are actually unstable against strong-interaction decay into Ξ0 or Ξ−, and the strangeness number at which the first Ξ hyperons appear is generally lower when total charge Q is conserved than when proton number Z is conserved; the onset corresponds to the crossing of the Λ chemical potential with the neutron or proton chemical potential.

## Method

The authors used a non-relativistic density functional approach (Skyrme SLy4 for nucleons, plus Nijmegen-based functionals DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a, and DF-NSC97f for the hyperon sectors) to compute the ground-state energy of multi-strange hypernuclei across the nuclear chart (10 < Z < 120) as a function of strangeness number S. They added hyperons to a nuclear core under two separate constraints: conserved total charge Q or conserved proton number Z, and determined the threshold at which converting Λ into Ξ0 or Ξ− becomes energetically favorable. The ΛΛ interaction was simplified to a quadratic density dependence (EmpC prescription) calibrated to the bond energy of 6ΛΛHe, and the unknown ΛΞ and ΞΞ interactions were varied to assess uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- About 200,000 to 300,000 pure-Λ hypernuclei may exist before the onset of Ξ0,−.
- The uncertainty from the unknown ΛΞ interaction can modify the previous estimate by 30–40%.
- The impact of the unknown ΞΞ interaction on the threshold is very weak.
- For the EmpC prescription, the fitted α1ΛΛ parameter is 22.81 MeV·fm3 (DF-NSC89), 21.12 MeV·fm3 (DF-NSC97a), and 33.25 MeV·fm3 (DF-NSC97f), yielding a bond energy ΔBΛΛ(6)HF of 1.00, 0.99, and 1.01 MeV respectively.
- The average Λ density in 6ΛΛHe relative to saturation density ρ0 is 0.137 (DF-NSC89), 0.148 (DF-NSC97a), and 0.094 (DF-NSC97f).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the Λ hypernuclear chart (Khan 2015) by including the possibility of Λ → Ξ0,− conversion, which was neglected in earlier multi-strange hypernuclei calculations (e.g., Schaffner 1992, Dover 1993, Balberg 1994). It uses updated constraints from double-Λ hypernuclei (Ahn 2013) and single-Ξ data (Nak 2015) to replace the overly attractive hyperon-hyperon interactions assumed in 1980s–1990s work (Rufa 1987, Mares 1989). The results provide a baseline for future experimental searches at J-PARC and FAIR, and highlight that the ΛΞ interaction is the dominant unknown limiting the precision of predictions for multi-strange hypernuclei.
