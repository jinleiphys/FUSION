# From p+p to Pb+Pb Collisions: Wounded Nucleon versus Statistical Models

> System size dependence of hadron production properties is discussed within the Wounded Nucleon Model and the Statistical Model in the grand canonical, canonical and micro-canonical formulations. Similarities and differences between predictions of the models related to the treatment of conservation laws are exposed. A need for models which would combine a hydrodynamical-like expansion with conservation laws obeyed in individual collisions is stressed.

# From p+p to Pb+Pb Collisions: Wounded Nucleon versus Statistical Models

## Key claim
The Wounded Nucleon Model and the Statistical Model in the grand canonical formulation produce almost identical predictions for the system size dependence of hadron production properties, including mean multiplicity ratios and fluctuation measures such as Delta, Sigma, and phi. However, when conservation laws are enforced via canonical or micro-canonical ensembles in the Statistical Model, the predictions diverge significantly, particularly for scaled variances in large systems and mean multiplicities in small systems.

## Method
The author compares the Wounded Nucleon Model (WNM), which assumes particle production from independent wounded nucleons calculated via the Glauber approach, against the Statistical Model in its grand canonical (SM-GCE), canonical (SM-CE), and micro-canonical (SM-MCE) formulations. The comparison focuses on mean multiplicities, scaled variances, and strongly intensive quantities (Delta and Sigma) as functions of system size, using analytic expressions and calculations from an ideal gas model of charged particles and the hadron-resonance gas model.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of mean pion multiplicity to mean number of wounded nucleons in p+p and central Pb+Pb collisions differs by about 30% from low SPS to top RHIC energies.
- In the ideal gas model of classical positively and negatively charged particles, the scaled variance in SM(CE) approaches 0.5 for sufficiently large volume, while SM(GCE) gives a constant value of 1 independent of volume.
- At 158A GeV, transverse mass spectra of hadrons in p+p and central Pb+Pb collisions are approximately exponential for m_T ≤ 2 GeV.
- The WNM prediction that mean multiplicity per wounded nucleon equals the nucleon-nucleon value divided by 2 is strictly valid at about 40A GeV, where the p+p and Pb+Pb curves cross.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing comparison between the Wounded Nucleon Model, proposed by Bialas, Bleszynski and Czyz (1976), and the Statistical Model initiated by Fermi (1950). It is motivated by the NA61/SHINE ion program studying hadron production as a function of colliding nuclei size and energy, aiming to understand the onset of deconfinement and search for the critical point of strongly interacting matter. The paper demonstrates that the similarity of predictions explains why string-hadronic and hydrodynamical models (successors of WNM and SM-GCE respectively) can co-exist, while emphasizing the need for new models that combine hydrodynamical-like expansion with conservation laws obeyed in individual collisions, an effort that has already started.
