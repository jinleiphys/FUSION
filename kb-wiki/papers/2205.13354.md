# Double-charm tetraquark under the complex scaling method

> The LHCb Collaboration discovered a double-charm tetraquark $T_{cc}^{+}$ with a very small width. We investigate the $T_{cc}^{+}$ as a $DD^{*}$ molecule with $J^{P}=1^{+}$ in the framework of the one-boson-exchange potential model. The isospin breaking effect and $S-D$ wave coupling are taken into account carefully. We adopt the complex scaling method (CSM) to study the $DD^{*}$ system and obtain a quasibound state corresponding to the $T_{cc}^{+}$. Its binding energy relative to the $D^{0}D^{*+}$ and width are $-354$ keV and $61$ keV respectively. The isospin breaking effect is found to be enormous, and the $S-$wave $D^{0}D^{*+}$ and $D^{+}D^{*0}$ components give dominant contributions with the probabilities of $72.1\%$ and $27.1\%$ respectively. In addition, we do not find any resonances in the $DD^{*}$ system. As a by-product, we study the $X(3872)$ as a $(D\bar{D}^*-D^*\bar{D})/\sqrt{2}$ molecule with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$. We also find a quasibound state corresponding to the $X(3872)$. Its binding energy relative to the $D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold and width are $-111$ keV and $26$ keV respectively. The $S-$wave $(D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}-D^{*0}\bar{D}^{0})/\sqrt{2}$ component dominates this state with the probability of $92.7\%$.

## Key claim

The paper models the double-charm tetraquark \(T_{cc}^{+}\) as a \(DD^{*}\) molecule using the complex scaling method (CSM) to handle the imaginary part of the one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential arising from the \(D^{*}\to D\pi\) decay, obtaining a quasibound state with binding energy and width that agree with the LHCb pole extraction, but only after taking the binding energy as input to fix the cutoff parameter.

## Method

The authors solved the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation in coordinate space using the Gaussian expansion method, with a one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential including \(\pi\), \(\sigma\), \(\eta\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) exchange. They explicitly included isospin breaking by coupling the \(D^{0}D^{*+}\) and \(D^{+}D^{*0}\) channels in both \(^{3}S_{1}\) and \(^{3}D_{1}\) partial waves, and retained the energy-dependent \(q_{0}\) in the OPE propagator to account for the three-body \(DD\pi\) intermediate state. The cutoff \(\Lambda\) was adjusted so that the calculated binding energy matched the experimental mass of the \(T_{cc}^{+}\).

## Key numbers

* Binding energy of the \(T_{cc}^{+}\) quasibound state relative to the \(D^{0}D^{*+}\) threshold: \(-354\) keV (OBE potential) and \(-364\) keV (OPE-only potential).
* Width of the \(T_{cc}^{+}\) quasibound state: \(61\) keV (OBE potential) and \(62\) keV (OPE-only potential), where width = \(-2 \times \text{Im}(E)\).
* Probabilities of the \(S\)-wave components in the OBE case: \(72.1\%\) for \(D^{0}D^{*+}\) and \(27.1\%\) for \(D^{+}D^{*0}\).
* Binding energy of the \(X(3872)\) quasibound state relative to the \(D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}\) threshold: \(-111\) keV (OBE potential).
* Width of the \(X(3872)\) quasibound state: \(26\) keV.
* Probability of the \(S\)-wave \([D^{0}\bar{D}^{*0}]\) component in the \(X(3872)\): \(92.7\%\).
* Cutoff values: \(\Lambda = 1170\) MeV for the \(DD^{*}\) OBE case, \(\Lambda = 1155\) MeV for the \([D\bar{D}^{*}]\) OBE case.
* Root-mean-square radius of the \(T_{cc}^{+}\) in the OBE case: \(5.0 - 0.1i\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the \(T_{cc}^{+}\) discovered by LHCb (2021), following earlier predictions by Li et al. (2013) who obtained a binding energy of \(-0.47\) MeV using a coupled-channel approach with a principal-value prescription for the OPE potential. The authors introduce the complex scaling method to treat the OPE pole on the real momentum axis, which differs from the Cauchy principal-value scheme used by Chen et al. (2021) and Li et al. (2012); this method naturally yields an imaginary potential from the \(D^{*}\) decay. The study also finds no resonances in the \(DD^{*}\) system, in contrast to the prediction of a \(T_{cc}^{'+}\) resonance by Chen et al. (2021). The \(X(3872)\) is treated as a by-product with the same formalism, and the results enable future studies of the correlation between binding energy and width in near-threshold molecular states.
