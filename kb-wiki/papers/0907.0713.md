# Strangeness Physics with CLAS at JLab

> A brief overview of strangeness physics with the CLAS detector at JLab is given, mainly covering the domain of nucleon resonances. Several excited states predicted by the symmetric constituent quark model may have significant couplings to the K-Lambda or K-Sigma channels. I will discuss data that are relevant in the search for such states in the strangeness channel, and give an outlook on the future prospects of the N* program at JLab with electromagnetic probes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the symmetric constituent quark model predicts many excited nucleon states that may have significant couplings to the K-Lambda or K-Sigma channels, and that strangeness production with electromagnetic probes can help discriminate between the symmetric quark model and the quark-diquark model, but the identification of specific s-channel resonances requires complete measurements including polarization observables, not just differential cross sections.

## Method

The CLAS detector at JLab was used to measure photoproduction and electroproduction of hyperons (K-Lambda, K-Sigma, and cascade states) with photon energies covering the nucleon resonance region. The program employed linearly and circularly polarized photon beams, polarized proton targets (FROST), and planned polarized neutron targets (HD-Ice) to measure differential cross sections, recoil polarization, and double polarization observables for complete amplitude extraction. Data were analyzed by the Bonn-Gatchina group using coupled-channel fits that simultaneously included cross section and spin transfer measurements.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy tagger resolution: sigma(E)/E = 10^{-3}
- Linearly polarized photons achieved polarizations up to 90%
- Cross section data collected in fine steps of about 10 MeV in hadronic mass W
- The spin transfer from circularly polarized photon to Lambda hyperon is complete, with R^2 = P^2 + C_x^2 + C_z^2 consistent with 1 throughout the measured region
- A P_{13}(1900) resonance candidate is required to fit the combined CLAS differential cross section and spin transfer data; this state is listed as a 2-star candidate in the 2008 Review of Particle Physics
- The peak in K+Lambda data near 1900 MeV was originally attributed to a D_{13}(1900) resonance before spin transfer data became available
- Cascade data taken at 3.6 GeV beam energy show the Xi(1530) state and indications of structure near 1620 MeV
- New electroproduction data taken at 5.7 GeV electron energy are being analyzed
- Electroproduction response functions sigma_u and sigma_TT show strong resonance-like structure near W=1.9 GeV

## Context

This work extends the search for "missing resonances" predicted by the symmetric constituent quark model but not observed in pion-induced reactions, complementing earlier studies using hadronic probes. The finding that a P_{13}(1900) state is required to fit the CLAS data would be strong evidence against the quark-diquark model of Santo (2005), which has no place for such a state in this mass range. The paper enables future complete measurements using the FROST and HD-Ice polarized targets, which will provide the double and triple polarization data needed for unambiguous resonance identification, and opens the search for new cascade baryons with strangeness S=-2, including a dynamically generated Xi state predicted by Oset and collaborators.
