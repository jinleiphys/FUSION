# Two- and three-body structure of the Y(4660)

> We study general features of three-meson bound states using the Y(4660) as an example. Here the Y(4660) is assumed to be either a two-body bound state of the f_0(980), itself a bound state of K and Kbar, and the psi'= psi(2s), or a three-body bound state of psi', K, and Kbar. In particular, we investigate in detail the interplay of the various scales inherent in the problem, namely the f_0 binding energy, the Y binding energy, and the K-psi' scattering length. This allows us to understand under which circumstances the substructure of the f_0(980) can be neglected in the description of the Y(4660).

# Two- and three-body structure of the Y(4660)

## Key claim

The Y(4660) can be interpreted as either a two-body bound state of psi' and f0(980) or a three-body bound state of psi', K, and anti-K, depending on the relative binding energies and the unknown psi'-K scattering length; the Weinberg method for distinguishing two- from three-body molecules is applicable only when the f0(980) binding energy is close to the Y(4660) binding energy (b0 near 1) and the psi'-K scattering length is sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors set up a non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) for three distinguishable particles with different masses, introducing dimer fields for convenience. They solve the resulting integral equations numerically for psi'-f0(980) scattering within a three-body model, using the f0(980) as a bound state of K and anti-K. The Weinberg Z-factor is then computed from the effective range parameters to determine whether the Y(4660) can be treated as a two-body molecule or requires a full three-body description.

## Key numbers

- Masses used: m_psi' = 3686.1 MeV, m_K = m_anti-K = 495.6 MeV (average of K+ and K0)
- Experimental masses with errors: m_Y = 4664 +/- 11 +/- 5 MeV, m_f0 = 980 +/- 10 MeV
- Binding energies varied in the region 0 < B_f0 < B_Y less than or approximately 20 MeV
- psi'-K scattering length constrained to -0.1 fm less than or approximately a_psi'K = a_psi'anti-K < 0 fm
- For the Y(4660) physical region, the dimensionless parameter b1 = 1/(2 mu_psi'K a_psi'K^2 B_Y) is greater than approximately 225
- The scattering length a_psi'f0 diverges to +infinity as b0 = B_f0/B_Y approaches 1
- Z_a approaches 1 as b0 approaches 0 (three-body system) and approaches 0 as b0 approaches 1 (two-body system)

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg method (Weinberg, 1965) for quantifying two-body molecular components to distinguish between two- and three-body molecules, using the Y(4660) as a test case. The Y(4660) was previously proposed as a psi'-f0(980) molecule by Guo et al. (2008) using the Weinberg method, while the f0(980) itself was proposed as a K anti-K molecule by earlier work on light scalar mesons. The study enables future investigations of other hadronic molecules such as the X(3872) as a D D pi system, and provides a framework for including unstable constituents and decay channels in line-shape analyses.
