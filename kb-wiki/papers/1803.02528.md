# Applied Holography of the AdS$_5$-Kerr Spacetime

> Asymptotically Anti-de Sitter Kerr black holes (we focus here on the five-dimensional case) are associated holographically with matter at conformal infinity which has a non-zero angular momentum density. It is natural to attempt to associate this angular momentum with the recently discovered vorticity of the plasmas produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. We assume that an AdS$_5$-Kerr black hole with angular momentum to mass ratio $\mathcal{A}$ is dual to boundary matter with an angular momentum density to energy density ratio also equal to $\mathcal{A}$. With this assumption, we find that, for collisions corresponding to a given value of $\mathcal{A}$, there is a maximal possible angular velocity (well below the maximal value permitted by causality) for such matter at infinity, and that this value is in approximate agreement with the experimentally reported value of the average plasma vorticity produced in typical peripheral collisions of heavy ions.

## Key claim

The paper assumes that an AdS$_5$-Kerr black hole with angular momentum to mass ratio $\mathcal{A}$ is dual to boundary matter with an angular momentum density to energy density ratio also equal to $\mathcal{A}$, and finds that this yields a maximal possible angular velocity for such matter that is well below the causality limit and in approximate agreement with experimentally reported average plasma vorticity from peripheral heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author computes the angular velocity of massive particles on the conformal boundary of the AdS$_5$-Kerr spacetime (with one rotation parameter set to zero) that have an angular momentum per unit mass equal to the black hole's specific angular momentum $\mathcal{A}$. By solving the geodesic equations for such particles on the boundary metric and subtracting the angular velocity of zero-angular-momentum particles, the vorticity $\omega$ is expressed as a function of $\mathcal{A}$ and the AdS curvature scale $L$, and then maximized with respect to $L$ to obtain an upper bound independent of the unknown $L$.

## Key numbers

- The maximal possible angular velocity for a given specific angular momentum $\mathcal{A}$ is $\omega_{\text{max}} = \varkappa / \mathcal{A} \approx 0.2782 / \mathcal{A}$.
- At this maximal angular velocity, the linear velocity of objects on the equator is $v_{\omega}(\omega_{\text{max}}) \approx 0.750$ (in natural units, well below light speed).
- The experimentally reported average vorticity from the STAR collaboration is $\omega \approx 9 \pm 1 \times 10^{21} \text{ s}^{-1}$.
- The predicted average vorticity over impact energies from the holographic model is $5.3 \times 10^{21} \text{ s}^{-1}$.
- For gold ion collisions at 200 GeV impact energy per pair and 20% centrality, the estimated ratio of plasma angular momentum density to energy density is approximately 72 femtometres, giving a lower bound on the boundary spatial volume of $V(L, \mathcal{A}) \geq \approx 7.4 \times 10^6 \text{ fm}^3$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to rotating AdS black holes previously studied by Sonner (2009) and Schalm (2009) for four-dimensional bulk geometries, adapting it to the five-dimensional AdS$_5$-Kerr spacetime relevant for modeling the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in peripheral heavy-ion collisions. The paper responds to the STAR collaboration's observations of vorticity in such plasmas, and provides a holographic bound on vorticity that can be tested against experimental data, while noting that future work must incorporate hydrodynamic aspects as in the work of Kovtun, Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2005) and the explosive expansion of the plasma as studied by Chesler (2015).
