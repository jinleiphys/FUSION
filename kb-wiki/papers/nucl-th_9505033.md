# New Scaling Law for Deuteron Production in Ultra-Relativistic Nucleus Nucleus Collisions

> Deuteron production in S and Pb induced collisions at beam energies of 200 and 160 AGeV is studied in the framework of the transport theoretical approach RQMD. Strong transverse flow invalidates the differential coalescence formula in momentum space. The transverse momentum integrated $d$ yields scale in a broad rapidity interval with the squared proton densities and inversely with the produced particle rapidity densities. This kind of scaling can be linked to constant relative sizes of nucleon and pion sources at freeze-out. With increasing projectile mass the nucleon source blows up stronger than the pion source. As a result, the scaled deuteron densities drop in central Pb+Pb collisions by 15 percent as compared to S induced reactions.

# New Scaling Law for Deuteron Production in Ultra-Relativistic Nucleus Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim
Deuteron yields in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions scale with the squared proton densities and inversely with the produced particle rapidity density, but this scaling breaks down by about 15% in central Pb+Pb collisions compared to S-induced reactions because the nucleon source expands more strongly than the pion source at freeze-out.

## Method
The authors used the RQMD transport model (version 1.09) to simulate S+S, S+W, and Pb+Pb collisions at beam energies of 200 and 160 AGeV, then calculated deuteron yields via the Wigner function method for cluster coalescence applied to the freeze-out phase-space distributions of protons and neutrons. They tested the scaling relation by comparing the transverse-momentum-integrated deuteron rapidity densities against the squared proton rapidity densities divided by the negative particle rapidity density.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of proton to pion transverse RMS radius at midrapidity is 1.05 in S+S reactions (5.4 fm for protons, 5.1 fm for pions), increasing to 1.19 in central Pb+Pb collisions (10.3 fm for protons, 8.6 fm for pions).
- The scaled deuteron densities drop in central Pb+Pb collisions by 15 percent as compared to S induced reactions.
- The initial transverse RMS sizes of S and Pb are 2.4 fm and 4.5 fm respectively.
- The primordial proton spectra are described by thermal distributions with slope parameter 180 MeV for S+S and 232 MeV for Pb+Pb collisions; the deuteron tail in Pb+Pb requires a slope parameter of 300 MeV.
- The calculated d/p ratios are less than one percent in the central rapidity region, smaller by an order of magnitude than data at 10-15 AGeV.

## Context
This work extends the traditional coalescence scaling law of Butler and Pearson (1963), which was successful at Bevalac and SIS energies around 1 AGeV but violated by factors up to 40 at 10-15 AGeV as reported by Barrette et al. (1993). The new scaling incorporates the effect of produced particles on freeze-out volume, which was neglected in earlier formulations. The results enable experimental groups such as NA44 and NA52 to extract information on relative source sizes of protons and pions and on transverse flow strength in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN-SPS energies.
