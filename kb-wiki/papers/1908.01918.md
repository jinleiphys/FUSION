# Properties of strange quark stars with isovector interactions

> We study the properties of strange quark stars by employing a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with both scalar-isovector and vector-isovector interactions. Using the constraint on the vector-isoscalar interaction strength obtained from the elliptic flow splitting between particles and their antiparticles in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we investigate the dependence of the properties of strange quark stars on the vector-isovector and the scalar-isovector interactions, and compare the results with the state-of-art astrophysical constraints on the compact star radius and mass as well as its tidal deformability from the GW170817 event. Results from our study reinforce the prospect of using both heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical observations to provide constraints on the isovector coupling strength in quark matter and thus the quark matter equation of state as well as the QCD phase structure at finite isospin chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The study shows that the vector-isovector and scalar-isovector interactions in the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model affect the mass, radius, and tidal deformability of strange quark stars, but the tidal deformabilities for a canonical 1.4 solar mass star from all considered scenarios remain within the 90% confidence uncertainty range (70 to 580) extracted from the GW170817 event, meaning that current tidal deformability data cannot provide additional constraints on quark interactions beyond those from mass and radius measurements.

## Method

The authors employed a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with scalar-isoscalar, vector-isoscalar, scalar-isovector, and vector-isovector interactions in the mean-field approximation, using the Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft six-point interaction. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the mass-radius relation and the first-order differential equation for the tidal Love number to obtain the tidal deformability, imposing charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions for strange quark matter composed of u, d, and s quarks plus electrons and muons.

## Key numbers

- The vector-isoscalar coupling strength was constrained to the range 0.5 < R_V < 1.1 from the relative elliptic flow (v2) splitting between protons and antiprotons as well as between K+ and K- in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
- The maximum mass of strange quark stars without isovector couplings reaches about 1.8 solar masses for R_V = 0.5 and about 2.2 solar masses for R_V = 1.1.
- The radius of compact stars with the canonical mass M = 1.4 solar masses is constrained to the range 10.62 km < R_1.4 < 12.83 km from analyzing quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries.
- The tidal polarizability for a 1.4 solar mass star is within the 90% confidence range 70 < Lambda_1.4 < 580 from the GW170817 event.
- The maximum baryon density for strange quark stars is less than 6 times the nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3.
- The sound velocity squared exceeds the conformal limit of 1/3 at higher densities for strong repulsive vector-isoscalar interactions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange quark stars using the NJL model (Han01, Wan19) by including both scalar-isovector and vector-isovector interactions, and connects heavy-ion collision constraints on the vector-isoscalar coupling from Jxu14 and the vector-isovector coupling from Liu19 with astrophysical constraints from the GW170817 event (Abb18) and mass measurements of J1614-2230 (Dem10) and J0348+0432 (Ant13). The results enable future constraints from the second gravitational wave event with total mass about 3.4 solar masses (LIGO20) and from the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) to further narrow the allowed region for the isovector coupling strengths in quark matter.
