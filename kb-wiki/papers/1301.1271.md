# Fluctuations and correlations in Polyakov loop extended chiral fluid dynamics

> We study nonequilibrium effects at the QCD phase transition within the framework of Polyakov loop extended chiral fluid dynamics. The quark degrees of freedom act as a locally equilibrated heat bath for the sigma field and a dynamical Polyakov loop. Their evolution is described by a Langevin equation with dissipation and noise. At a critical point we observe the formation of long-range correlations after equilibration. During a hydrodynamical expansion nonequilibrium fluctuations are enhanced at the first order phase transition compared to the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nonequilibrium fluctuations of the sigma field and the Polyakov loop are enhanced during a first-order phase transition compared to a critical point, but this result is conditional on the use of unphysical quark-meson coupling values (g=4.7 for first order, g=3.52 for critical point) chosen for qualitative study rather than physical reproduction of constituent quark masses.

## Method

The authors couple a Langevin equation for the sigma field and a phenomenological Langevin equation for the Polyakov loop to an ideal relativistic fluid dynamic description of the quark heat bath, using the Polyakov loop extended quark meson model. They perform two types of simulations: temperature quenches in a box with periodic boundary conditions, and 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamical expansion of an ellipsoidal hot plasma droplet initialized at T=200 MeV. The effective potential is computed in mean-field approximation at zero baryochemical potential.

## Key numbers

- For the first-order transition scenario, the coupling is set to g=4.7 with a critical temperature T_c=172.9 MeV
- For the critical point scenario, the coupling is set to g=3.52 with a critical temperature T_c=180.5 MeV
- The damping coefficient for the Polyakov loop is given as eta_l ~ 1/fm
- The temperature quench for the first-order scenario goes from T=180 MeV to T=140 MeV
- The temperature quench for the critical point scenario goes from T=186 MeV to T=166 MeV
- The expansion simulation initializes an ellipsoidal region at T=200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral fluid dynamics framework of Nahrgang (2011) by adding a dynamical Polyakov loop to simultaneously treat the chiral and deconfinement transitions, building on the Polyakov loop extended quark meson model from the literature. The results enable future studies of how nonequilibrium effects like supercooling and reheating modify experimental signatures of the QCD critical end point in heavy-ion collisions, addressing concerns raised by Berdnikov (1999) about critical slowing down and finite system size limiting correlation length divergence.
