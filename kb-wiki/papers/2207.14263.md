# Sensitivity of the $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^{0}γγ$ and $η^{\prime}\toηγγ$ decays to a sub-GeV leptophobic $U(1)_{B}$ boson

> The sensitivity of the rare decays $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^{0}γγ$ and $η^{\prime}\toηγγ$ to signatures of a leptophobic $B$ boson in the MeV-GeV mass range is analyzed in this work. By adding an explicit $B$-boson resonance exchange, $η\to Bγ\toπ^{0}γγ$, to the Standard Model contributions from vector and scalar meson exchanges, and employing experimental data for the associated branching ratios, it allows us to improve the current constraints on the $B$-boson mass $m_{B}$ and coupling to Standard Model particles $α_{B}$. From these constraints and the analysis of the available experimental $γγ$ invariant mass distribution, we show that a $B$-boson signature in the resonant mass range $m_{π^{0}}\lesssim m_{B}\lesssim m_η$ is strongly suppressed and would be very difficult to experimentally identify, assuming that the leptophobic $B$ boson only decays to Standard Model particles. In contrast, the limits outside this mass window are less stringent and the corresponding $t$- and $u$-channel signatures may still be observable in the data, as it occurs with the nonresonant Standard Model $ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$ meson exchanges. In addition, we make use of experimental data from the $η^{\prime}\toπ^{0}γγ$ and $η^{\prime}\toηγγ$ decays to explore larger $B$-boson masses. Our results are relevant for the $B$-boson search programs at existing and forthcoming light-meson facilities, such as KLOE(-II) and Jefferson Lab Eta Factory experiments.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of rare η(′)→π0γγ and η′→ηγγ decays to a leptophobic B boson is analyzed, but the authors show that a B-boson signature in the resonant mass range mπ0 ≲ mB ≲ mη is strongly suppressed and would be very difficult to experimentally identify, assuming the leptophobic B boson only decays to Standard Model particles; in contrast, limits outside this mass window are less stringent and t- and u-channel signatures may still be observable.

## Method

The authors add an explicit B-boson resonance exchange (η → Bγ → π0γγ) to the Standard Model contributions from vector meson exchanges (ρ0, ω, φ) and scalar meson exchanges, using the vector meson dominance (VMD) framework and linear sigma model (LσM) amplitudes from Escribano (2018). They employ experimental branching ratio data and available γγ invariant mass distributions to set constraints on the B-boson mass mB and coupling αB, and extend the analysis to η′ → π0γγ and η′ → ηγγ decays to explore larger B-boson masses.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio for η → π0γγ used from KLOE is (1.23 ± 0.14) × 10−4 (preliminary) and from PDG is (2.56 ± 0.22) × 10−4
- The branching ratio for η′ → π0γγ from BESIII is (3.20 ± 0.07 ± 0.23) × 10−3
- The branching ratio for η′ → ηγγ from BESIII is (8.25 ± 3.41 ± 0.72) × 10−5
- The ρ0 → π0γ branching ratio is (4.7 ± 0.8) × 10−4, yielding |gρ0π0γ| = 0.22(2) GeV−1
- The ω → π0γ branching ratio is (8.34 ± 0.26)%, yielding |gωπ0γ| = 0.71(1) GeV−1
- The φ → π0γ branching ratio is (1.32 ± 0.06) × 10−3, yielding |gφπ0γ| = 0.041(1) GeV−1

## Context

This work extends the conservative constraints on B-boson parameters from Tulin (2014), which set the Standard Model contribution to zero, by incorporating realistic Standard Model amplitudes from Escribano (2018) for η(′) → π0γγ and η′ → ηγγ decays. The analysis is relevant for B-boson search programs at KLOE(-II) and the Jefferson Lab Eta Factory (JEF) experiment, and enables improved exclusion limits in the MeV–GeV mass range that has been less explored due to nonperturbative QCD challenges.
