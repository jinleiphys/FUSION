# A comparative study of super- and highly-deformed bands in the A ~ 60 mass region

> Super- and highly-deformed rotational bands in the A ~ 60 mass region are studied within cranked relativistic mean field theory and the configuration-dependent shell-correction approach based on the cranked Nilsson potential. Both approaches describe the experimental data well. Low values of the dynamic moments of inertia J^(2) compared with the kinematic moments of inertia J^(1) seen both in experiment and in calculations at high rotational frequencies indicate the high energy cost to build the states at high spin and reflect the limited angular momentum content in these configurations.

## Key claim

Both cranked relativistic mean field theory and the configuration-dependent shell-correction approach describe the experimental data well, but the best description of excitation energies within configurations at high spin is obtained with the CRMF theory using NLSH and NL3 forces for 60,62Zn while the band in 58Cu is somewhat better described in the CN model; however, none of the approaches give the configuration assigned to the SD band in 62Zn as yrast.

## Method

The authors applied cranked relativistic mean field theory (CRMF) with three parameterizations (NL1, NL3, NLSH) and the configuration-dependent shell-correction approach based on the cranked Nilsson potential (CN) to study super- and highly-deformed rotational bands in 58Cu, 60Zn, and 62Zn. Pairing correlations were neglected, making results realistic only for spins I ≥ 15ℏ. Configurations were labeled by the shorthand notation [p1p2, n1n2] indicating numbers of proton and neutron f7/2 holes and g9/2 particles, and effective alignments were compared between band pairs to assign configurations.

## Key numbers

- The SD band in 60Zn extends to rotational frequencies of ∼1.8 MeV, the highest observed in SD bands.
- The lowest transition in the highly-deformed band of 58Cu has energy 830 keV, corresponding to a spin change of 11⁺ → 9⁺.
- The lowest transition in the SD band of 62Zn has energy 1993 keV, corresponding to a spin change of 20⁺ → 18⁺.
- The bands in 58Cu, 60Zn, and 62Zn are observed up to 23⁺, 36⁺, and 30⁺ respectively, with 'maximum' spins of 29⁺, 36⁺, and 40⁺.
- Transition energies reach 3.2 MeV or more at the top of all three bands studied.
- Full alignment of two highest f7/2 holes gives 6ℏ, while full alignment of two lowest g9/2 particles gives 8ℏ in angular momentum.

## Context

This work extends the study of superdeformed bands from the A ∼ 140–150 mass region into the lighter A ∼ 60 region, where the doubly-magic SD nucleus 60Zn (predicted by Ragnarsson (1990)) provides a linked band enabling absolute spin assignments for the first time in unlinked SD bands. The results show that the smooth drop of J⁽²⁾ relative to J⁽¹⁾ at high spin is a general feature of SD and highly-deformed bands in this mass region, similar to smooth terminating bands in the A ∼ 110 region (as studied by Afanasjev et al.) and in 62,64Zn, and contradicts the rigid-rotor assumption (J⁽¹⁾ ≈ J⁽²⁾) sometimes used in SD band analysis. Future observation of other SD structures in 62Zn and neighboring nuclei will be essential to establish single-particle level ordering around the N=30 SD shell gap and improve theoretical descriptions.
