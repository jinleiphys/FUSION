# Hard exclusive processes in the backward region

> We review the potentialities offered by the study of backward exclusive processes in a new scaling regime, i.e. involving a large -timelike or spacelike- Q2 photon and a baryonic exchange in the t-channel. We recall the concept of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) containing unique information on the hadron structure, then discuss how they enter the description of processes such as backward electroproduction of a pion, antiproton-proton annihilations into a dilepton + meson as well as into J/psi + meson. We then discuss first phenomenological studies for processes that are being analysed at JLAB and HERMES or that will be measured by Panda at GSI-FAIR. Finally we present outlooks for their theoretical studies based on approaches such as the pion-cloud model.

## Key claim

Backward hard-exclusive processes involving a large \(Q^2\) photon and a baryonic exchange in the \(t\)-channel can be described via factorised amplitudes involving baryonic Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), but the scaling regime is not yet guaranteed at currently accessible \(Q^2\) values (a few GeV\(^2\)), and quantitative comparisons between TDA models and experiments require further theoretical and experimental work.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for backward exclusive reactions, defining baryonic Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) as non-perturbative matrix elements of three-quark light-cone operators between a baryon and a meson state. They apply this formalism to three processes: backward electroproduction of a pion (\(\gamma^\star N \to N' \pi\)), antiproton-proton annihilation into a dilepton plus a meson (\(\bar p p \to \gamma^\star \pi\)), and \(\bar p p \to J/\psi \pi\). The hard part is computed at leading order in perturbative QCD, while the TDAs are constrained via soft-pion theorems and modelled using the pion-cloud approach.

## Key numbers

- The soft-pion theorem relates proton-to-pion TDAs to proton distribution amplitudes in the limit \(|\vec{p}_\pi| \to 0\), with the relation \(\langle \pi^a(p_\pi) |{\cal O}| P(p_1,s_1)\rangle \to -\frac{i}{f_\pi} \langle 0 | [Q^a_5, {\cal O}] | P(p,s) \rangle\).
- The backward electroproduction kinematics satisfy \(|u| \ll s\) and \(t \simeq -(s+Q^2)\), in contrast to the fixed-angle regime where \(u \simeq t \simeq -(s+Q^2)/2\).
- JLab data in the resonance region cover \(\sqrt{s_{\gamma^\star p}} = W < 1.8\) GeV for backward pion and eta electroproduction.
- The TDA description is expected to apply for \(|\Delta^2_T| < 1\) GeV\(^2\) in backward pion electroproduction.
- Present experimental studies are limited to \(Q^2\) of order a few GeV\(^2\), which does not guarantee reaching the TDA scaling regime.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Generalised Parton Distributions (GPDs), introduced by Muller and others for forward exclusive reactions, to the backward region via baryonic Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), as pioneered by Pire, Szymanowski, and Lansberg (2005). It enables the study of hard exclusive processes at JLab (12 GeV upgrade), HERMES, COMPASS, and the PANDA experiment at GSI-FAIR, providing a new window into hadron structure through collinear factorisation in QCD. The paper also discusses model-independent tests such as target transverse-spin asymmetries to distinguish hard parton-induced scattering from soft baryon exchange.
