# Heavy Quarkonium-nuclear bound states within a generalized linear sigma model

> We estimate the binding energies of charmonium ($J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, $ψ(1D)$, $χ_{c0}$, $χ_{c1}$, $χ_{c2}$) and bottomonium ($Υ(1S)$, $Υ(2S)$, $Υ_2(1D)$, $χ_{b0}$, $χ_{b1}$, $χ_{b2}$) states bound in various nuclei (${\rm{^{4}He}}$, ${\rm{^{12}C}}$, ${\rm{^{16}O}}$, ${\rm{^{40}Ca}}$, ${\rm{^{90}Zr}}$, and ${\rm{^{208}Pb}}$) using the quarkonia-nuclei potentials obtained from their mass shifts in nuclear matter within the generalized linear sigma model. In the absence of light partons in heavy quarkonia, at the tree level, the medium modifications are driven by the gluon condensate, which is simulated within this model through a scalar dilaton field, $χ$, by introducing broken scale invariance of QCD. Our study shows that charmonium states bind more deeply with the atomic nuclei as compared to bottomonium states, providing a better probe for nuclear medium effects. Such bound states' investigations are particularly interesting for the upcoming J-PARC-E29, $\rm{\bar{P}ANDA}$@FAIR, and CEBAF@JLab experiments. The mass shifts of the heavy quarkonium states in hot isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated and are observed to receive an appreciable medium modification. These medium effects are anticipated at FAIR@GSI, where such neutron-rich hot nuclear matter is expected to be produced.

## Key claim

The paper estimates binding energies of charmonium and bottomonium states in various nuclei using quarkonia-nuclei potentials derived from mass shifts in nuclear matter, but the mass shifts and binding energies depend on the specific quarkonium state and nucleus, with charmonium states binding more deeply than bottomonium states, providing a better probe for nuclear medium effects.

## Method

The authors used a generalized linear sigma model incorporating chiral SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry, scale invariance breaking via a scalar dilaton field χ that simulates the gluon condensate, and mean-field approximation to compute in-medium modifications of the scalar dilaton field in hot, isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The mass shifts of heavy quarkonium states were then calculated from the leading-order operator product expansion formula relating the mass shift to the change in the fourth power of the dilaton field, using harmonic oscillator wave functions for the quarkonium states. Finally, the Klein-Gordon equation was solved with the position-dependent scalar potential Δm(r) to obtain binding energies and eigenfunctions for quarkonium-nucleus bound states.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quarkonium-nucleus bound states first proposed by Brodsky (1990) and later calculated by Luke et al. (who estimated J/ψ binding around 10 MeV and Υ(1S) at a few MeV), Lee and Ko (who calculated J/ψ, ψ(2S), and ψ(1D) binding energies as 8 MeV, 100 MeV, and 140 MeV respectively), and QMC model calculations (giving 18-24 MeV for J/ψ and 16-22 MeV for Υ(1S) at saturation density). The results are relevant for upcoming experiments at J-PARC-E29, PANDA@FAIR, and CEBAF@JLab, which aim to produce low-momenta heavy quarkonia in atomic nuclei.
