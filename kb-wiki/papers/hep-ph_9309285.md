# Evidence for Color Fluctuations in Hadrons from Coherent Nuclear Diffraction}

> A QCD-based treatment of projectile size fluctuations is used to compute inelastic diffractive cross sections $σ_{diff}$ for coherent hadron-nuclear processes. We find that fluctuations near the average size give the major contribution to the cross section with $ \le few \%$ contribution from small size configurations. The computed values of $σ_{diff}$ are consistent with the limited available data. The importance of coherent diffraction studies for a wide range of projectiles for high energy Fermilab fixed target experiments is emphasized. The implications of these significant color fluctuations for relativistic heavy ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that color fluctuations in the projectile wave function, rather than small-size configurations, give the dominant contribution to inelastic coherent nuclear diffraction cross sections, with small-size configurations contributing at most a few percent for realistic nuclei.

## Method

The authors use the scattering-eigenstates formalism (Good-Walker approach) combined with a QCD-based probability distribution \(P(\sigma)\) for the hadron-nucleon cross section, whose moments are constrained by diffractive dissociation data on nucleons and deuterons. They compute the coherent nuclear diffractive cross section \(\sigma_{diff}(A)\) by integrating over impact parameter and cross section using an optical potential, and compare the results to semi-inclusive diffraction data for pion and nucleon projectiles on nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- For pion projectiles, \(\sigma_{diff}(A)\) varies as \(A^{1.05}\) for \(A \approx 16\) and as \(A^{0.65}\) for large \(A \approx 200\).
- For proton projectiles, \(\sigma_{diff}(A)\) varies as \(A^{0.80}\) for \(A \sim 16\) and as \(A^{0.4}\) for \(A \sim 200\).
- The dispersion parameter \(\omega_\sigma \equiv (\langle \sigma^2\rangle - \langle \sigma\rangle^2)/\langle \sigma\rangle^2\) is \(0.4\)–\(0.5\) for pions and approximately \(0.25\) for protons at 400 GeV (since \(\langle \sigma^2\rangle \approx 1.25 \langle \sigma\rangle^2\)).
- The contribution from small-size configurations (cross sections below 5 mb) ranges from 2% to 5% as A increases from about 12 to 200.
- The forward differential cross section \(d\sigma_{diff}/dt(0^\circ)\) varies approximately as \(A^{1.24}\) for pions and as \(A\) for protons.

## Context

This work extends the scattering-eigenstates formalism of Feinberg and Pomeranchuk (1956) and Good and Walker (1960) by using a realistic QCD-based \(P(\sigma)\) determined from moments extracted from nucleon and deuteron diffraction data (Miettinen and Pumplin, 1978; Blattel et al., 1992). It contradicts earlier preQCD models that included a delta-function piece in \(P(\sigma)\) (Miettinen and Pumplin, 1978; Boreskov and Shchepkin, 1983) which QCD does not allow, and shows that small-size configurations do not dominate as suggested by Bertsch (1981) and Zakharov et al. (1981). The results enable new fixed-target experiments at Fermilab to constrain \(P(\sigma)\) more precisely, with implications for fluctuations of transverse energy and percolation phase transitions in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
