# Ultrarelativistic quark-nucleus scattering in a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> We investigate the scattering of a quark on a heavy nucleus at high energies using the time-dependent basis light-front quantization (tBLFQ) formalism, which is the first application of the tBLFQ formalism in QCD. We present the real-time evolution of the quark wave function in a strong classical color field of the relativistic nucleus, described as the Color Glass Condensate. The quark and the nucleus color field are simulated in the QCD SU(3) color space. We calculate the total and the differential cross sections, and the quark distribution in coordinate and color spaces using the tBLFQ approach. We recover the eikonal cross sections in the eikonal limit. We find that the differential cross section from the tBLFQ simulation is in agreement with a perturbative calculation at large $p_\perp$, and it deviates from the perturbative calculation at small $p_\perp$ due to higher-order contributions. In particular, we relax the eikonal limit by letting the quark carry realistic finite longitudinal momenta. We study the sub-eikonal effect on the quark through the transverse coordinate distribution of the quark with different longitudinal momentum, and we find the sub-eikonal effect to be sizable. Our results can significantly reduce the theoretical uncertainties in small $p_\perp$ region which has important implications to the phenomenology of the hadron-nucleus and deep inelastic scattering at high energies.

## Key claim

The authors present the first application of the time-dependent basis light-front quantization (tBLFQ) formalism to QCD, showing that it recovers eikonal cross sections in the eikonal limit and that the differential cross section agrees with perturbative calculations at large transverse momentum but deviates at small transverse momentum due to higher-order contributions; the sub-eikonal effect from finite longitudinal momentum is found to be sizable.

## Method

The authors solve the time evolution of a single quark state in a strong classical SU(3) color field representing a heavy nucleus, described by the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory with the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. The quark wave function is expanded in a basis of transverse momentum and color eigenstates, and the time evolution operator is decomposed into small steps of light-front time \(x^+\) to handle the non-perturbative interaction with the background field. The gluon field is generated from stochastic color charges with a local Gaussian distribution, regularized by a gluon mass \(m_g\) and discretized on a transverse lattice with periodic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

* Total and elastic cross sections per unit impact parameter are computed for \(L=50\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) (9.87 fm) and \(L_\eta=50\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) (approximately 10 fm), with the eikonal analytical expressions given by \(\frac{d\sigma_{\text{tot}}}{d^2b} = 2\left\{1 - \exp\left[-\frac{(N_c^2-1)(g^2\mu)^2 L_\eta}{16\pi N_c}\left(\frac{1}{\Lambda_{IR}^2} - \frac{1}{\Lambda_{UV}^2}\right)\right]\right\}\) and similarly for the elastic cross section.
* The screening mass (IR regulator) is taken as \(m_g = 0.1\ \text{GeV}\).
* The quark mass is set to \(m_q = 0.15\ \text{GeV}\), and the authors checked that varying it in the range \(0.05-4.50\ \text{GeV}\) does not noticeably change the results.
* The transverse lattice spacing is \(a = L/N\) with \(L=50\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(N\) varied (e.g., \(N=8\) gives \(a=6.25\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\)), and the number of longitudinal layers \(N_\eta\) is varied with convergence observed for \(N_\eta \ge 4\).

## Context

This work extends the tBLFQ formalism, previously applied to QED bound states and nonlinear Compton scattering (Zhao 2013), to QCD scattering for the first time. It complements perturbative QCD studies of sub-eikonal effects (Jalilian-Marian 2017, Kovchegov 2018) by providing a non-perturbative treatment of the quark-nucleus interaction. The results can be used to calculate particle production in proton-nucleus collisions in the proton fragmentation region and provide a foundation for future studies of deep inelastic scattering and exclusive vector meson production using light-front wavefunctions from BLFQ.
