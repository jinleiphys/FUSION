# From jet quenching to wave turbulence

> We discuss average properties of the gluon cascade generated by an energetic parton propagating through a dense QCD medium. The cascade is mostly made with relatively soft gluons, whose production is not suppressed by the LPM effect. Unlike for usual QCD cascades in the vacuum, where the typical splittings are very asymmetric (soft and collinear), the medium-induced branchings are quasi-democratic and lead to wave turbulence. This results in a very efficient mechanism for the transport of energy at large angles with respect to the jet axis, which might explain the di-jet asymmetry observed in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

# From jet quenching to wave turbulence

## Key claim

Medium-induced gluon cascades from an energetic parton traversing a dense QCD medium produce quasi-democratic branchings that lead to wave turbulence, providing a mechanism for energy transport to large angles that may explain the di-jet asymmetry observed in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. The claim is hedged by the condition that this mechanism applies specifically to relatively soft gluons whose production is not suppressed by the LPM effect, and that the analytic solution involves a simplified 1+1 dimensional branching process.

## Method

The author reviews the BDMPSZ formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation and its recent generalization to multiple branchings, treating successive emissions as independent due to negligible interference effects (suppressed by a factor τ_f(ω)/L ≪ 1). A probabilistic branching process is formulated as a Markovian evolution equation for the gluon spectrum D(x,τ) in energy fraction x and dimensionless medium size τ, with the BDMPSZ spectrum serving as the elementary branching rate. An exact analytic solution is obtained for a simplified 1+1 dimensional process describing the energy distribution alone.

## Key numbers

- The jet quenching parameter is estimated as \hat q = 1 GeV²/fm for a QGP with temperature T = 250 MeV, with medium length L = 4 fm
- The characteristic energy scale ω_c ≡ \hat q L²/2 ≈ 40 GeV
- The characteristic emission angle θ_c ≡ 2/(\hat q L³)^{1/2} ≈ 0.05
- The single-emission BDMPSZ spectrum for soft gluons: ω dN/dω ≈ \bar α √(ω_c/ω) where \bar α ≡ α_s N_c/π
- The energy fraction transported via single gluon emission at angles larger than θ_0 is \mathcal{E}^{(1)}(θ > θ_0, τ) ≈ 2τ √x_0
- The exact solution for the gluon spectrum at x ≪ 1: D(x≪1, τ) ≈ (τ/√x) exp(-πτ²)
- The energy sum rule: ∫₀¹ dx D(x,τ) = exp(-πτ²)
- The energy carried by the turbulent flow: \mathcal{E}_{flow}(τ) = 1 - exp(-πτ²)
- For a jet with E = 100 GeV ≈ 2ω_c, the dimensionless evolution time τ ≈ \bar α ≈ 0.3
- The energy inside a jet with angular opening R₀ represents less than 80% of total energy for L ≥ 4 fm, with more than 20% associated with the flow component independent of R₀

## Context

This work extends the BDMPSZ formalism (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, Schiff 1996; Zakharov 1996) from single, rare hard emissions controlling leading-particle energy loss to multiple soft branchings relevant for the di-jet asymmetry observed by ATLAS (Aad 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan 2011, 2012). The generalization to multiple branchings was recently developed by Casalderrey-Solana (2011), Mehtar-Tani (2011, 2012), and Blaizot (2012, 2013). The finding that medium-induced branchings are quasi-democratic (unlike the highly asymmetric splittings in DGLAP or BFKL cascades) and produce wave turbulence is a new result that enables quantitative predictions for energy flow out of jet cones, consistent with CMS data showing that about 10% of total jet energy (∼20 GeV) is transported by soft particles to large angles.
