# About disposition of energy levels

> The unique properties of central potential of the form $-βe^{-r}r^γ$ were studied using the recently developed critical parameter technique. The particular cases of $γ=0$ and $γ=-1$ yield, respectively, the exponential and Yukawa potentials widely used in the atomic, molecular and nuclear physics. We found different behavior of the energy levels of this potential for three different ranges of the value of $γ$. For $γ\geq0$ it was found that the energy of bound states with the same principal quantum number $N$ decreases with increasing angular momentum $\ell$. The Gaussian and Woods-Saxon potentials also show this behavior. On the contrary, for $-2\leqγ\leq-1$ increasing $\ell$ gives a higher energy, resembling the Hulthen potential. However, a potential with $-1<γ<0$ possesses mixed properties, which give rise to several interesting results. For one, the order of energy levels with different quantum numbers is not preserved when varying the parameter $β$. This leads to a quantum degeneracy of the states, and in fact, for a given value of $γ$ we can find the values $β_{thr}$ for which two energy levels with different quantum numbers coincide. Another interesting phenomena is the possibility, for some values of $γ$ in this range, for two new energy levels with different quantum numbers to appear simultaneously when $β$ reaches their common critical value.

## Key claim

For the exponential-power (EP) potential \(V_{\beta\gamma}(r)=-\beta e^{-r}r^{\gamma}\) with \(-1<\gamma<0\), the order of energy levels with the same principal quantum number \(N\) is not preserved when varying the parameter \(\beta\), contradicting the earlier conjecture that the order in which levels appear as \(\beta\) increases is maintained for all larger \(\beta\).

## Method

The authors applied the critical parameter technique (CPT) to the non-relativistic Schrodinger equation for the EP potential, computing critical parameters \(\beta_{n,\ell}\) at which each eigenstate becomes a zero-energy transition state. They developed a new double-series expansion for the reduced radial wave function (Eq. 10) to handle potentials with non-integer \(\gamma\). Numerical calculations were performed using Mathematica 8, and binding energies were verified by a second independent method from Ref. [MK].

## Key numbers

* For \(\gamma=-0.238825\), the critical parameters for simultaneous appearance of two levels are \(\beta_{2,0}=\beta_{1,1}\simeq 7.9797\).
* For \(\gamma=-0.42046\), the critical parameters for simultaneous appearance are \(\beta_{3,0}=\beta_{2,1}\simeq 19.1036\).
* For \(\gamma=-1/2\) and \(\beta>\beta_{1,8}\equiv 194.393\), bound states exist only for \(\ell\leq 8\) and \(n\leq (9-\ell)\).
* For \(\gamma=-1/2\) at \(\beta=200\), only the four energy levels with \(n+\ell=9\) and \(5\leq\ell\leq 8\) satisfy \(E_{n,\ell}<E_{n-1,\ell+1}\), while levels with \(0\leq\ell\leq 5\) (same \(N=10\)) satisfy the opposite inequality \(E_{n,\ell}>E_{n-1,\ell+1}\).

## Context

This work extends the critical parameter technique developed by Liverts (2011) and the general energy-level ordering theorems of Baumgartner, Grosse, and Martin (1984) to the EP potential family, which includes the exponential (\(\gamma=0\)) and Yukawa (\(\gamma=-1\)) potentials widely used in atomic, molecular, and nuclear physics. The results disprove the conjecture from Liverts (2011) that the order of level appearance is preserved for all \(\beta\), and demonstrate that for \(-1<\gamma<0\) the potential exhibits mixed properties including level degeneracy and simultaneous appearance of bound states. This enables systematic study of level ordering for short-range potentials that do not satisfy the strict Laplacian sign conditions of Baumgartner, Grosse, and Martin (1984).
