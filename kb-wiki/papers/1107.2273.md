# Probing deconfinement in a chiral effective model with Polyakov loop at imaginary chemical potential

> The phase structure of the two-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lashinio model is explored at finite temperature and imaginary chemical potential with a particular emphasis on the confinement-deconfinement transition. We point out that the confined phase is characterized by a $\cos3μ_I/T$ dependence of the chiral condensate on the imaginary chemical potential while in the deconfined phase this dependence is given by $\cosμ_I/T$ and accompanied by a cusp structure induced by the Z(3) transition. We demonstrate that the phase structure of the model strongly depends on the choice of the Polyakov loop potential $\mathcal{U}$. Furthermore, we find that by changing the four fermion coupling constant $G_s$, the location of the critical endpoint of the deconfinement transition can be moved into the real chemical potential region. We propose a new parameter characterizing the confinement-deconfinement transition.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the confinement-deconfinement transition in the two-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can be characterized by the dependence of the chiral condensate on imaginary chemical potential: a $\cos 3\mu_I/T$ dependence signals the confined phase, while a $\cos \mu_I/T$ dependence with a cusp from the Z(3) transition signals the deconfined phase. However, the phase structure strongly depends on the choice of the Polyakov loop potential $\mathcal{U}$, and the location of the critical endpoint of the deconfinement transition can be moved into the real chemical potential region by changing the four-fermion coupling constant $G_s$.

## Method
The authors employed the two-flavor PNJL model in the mean-field approximation, solving the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate, the modulus of the Polyakov loop, and its phase as functions of temperature $T$ and imaginary chemical potential $\theta = \mu_I/T$. They compared two widely used parametrizations of the Polyakov loop effective potential: a polynomial potential and a logarithmic potential, using parameters fitted to pure SU(3) lattice data. The analysis included both the chiral limit ($m_0 = 0$) and finite quark mass ($m_0 = 5.5$ MeV) cases, with the fermion sector parameters fixed to reproduce vacuum pion mass and decay constant.

## Key numbers
- The polynomial potential parameters include $T_0 = 270$ MeV, $a_0 = 6.75$, $a_1 = -1.95$, $a_2 = 2.625$, $a_3 = -7.44$, $b_3 = 0.75$, $b_4 = 7.5$.
- The logarithmic potential parameters include $T_0 = 270$ MeV, $a_0 = 3.51$, $a_1 = -2.47$, $a_2 = 15.22$, $b_3 = -1.75$.
- The fermion sector parameters are $G_s = 5.498$ GeV$^{-2}$, $\Lambda = 0.6315$ GeV, and $m_0 = 5.5$ MeV for finite quark mass.
- The Roberge-Weiss endpoint temperature $T_E$ is approximately 255 MeV for the logarithmic potential and approximately 275 MeV for the polynomial potential.
- At $T = 280$ MeV, the phase of the Polyakov loop jumps from $\phi = 0$ to $-2\pi/3$ at $\theta = \pi/3$, exhibiting the first-order Roberge-Weiss transition.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the PNJL model at imaginary chemical potential by Sakai et al. (2008) and others, who demonstrated that the model reproduces the Roberge-Weiss transition. The authors systematically compare two different Polyakov loop potentials (polynomial and logarithmic) to show that qualitative features of the phase diagram depend on this choice, unlike previous studies that focused on a single parametrization. The proposed new parameter for characterizing the confinement-deconfinement transition, based on the $\cos 3\theta$ versus $\cos \theta$ dependence of the chiral condensate, is related to the dual order parameter introduced by Bilgici et al. (2008). This work enables future lattice QCD calculations to test these qualitative predictions and refine effective models of the QCD phase diagram.
