# Competition in rotation-alignment between high-j neutrons and protons in transfermium nuclei

> The study of rotation-alignment of quasiparticles probes sensitively the properties of high-j intruder orbits. The distribution of very high-j orbits, which are consequences of the fundamental spin-orbit interaction, links with the important question of single-particle levels in superheavy nuclei. With the deformed single-particle states generated by the standard Nilsson potential, we perform Projected-Shell-Model calculations for transfermium nuclei where detailed spectroscopy experiment is currently possible. Specifically, we study the systematical behavior of rotation-alignment and associated band-crossing phenomenon in Cf, Fm, and No isotopes. Neutrons and protons from the high-j orbits are found to compete strongly in rotation-alignment, which gives rise to testable effects. Observation of these effects will provide direct information on the single-particle states in the heaviest nuclear mass region.

## Key claim
The paper claims that in transfermium nuclei (Cf, Fm, No isotopes), rotation-alignment at high spins involves strong competition between high-j neutron pairs from the j15/2 orbital and high-j proton pairs from the i13/2 orbital, with the dominant aligning species depending sensitively on the specific isotope. The abstract's claim of "testable effects" is hedged by the authors' explicit statement that no definite data on rotation-alignment in this mass region currently exist, and that experimental confirmation or repudiation of their results would serve as a test of the standard Nilsson model.

## Method
The authors performed Projected Shell Model (PSM) calculations using deformed Nilsson single-particle states with quadrupole deformation parameters ε2 ranging from 0.222 to 0.265, incorporating pairing correlations via BCS and using a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian with monopole and quadrupole pairing terms. They constructed multi-quasiparticle basis states (0-qp, 2-qp neutron, 2-qp proton, and 4-qp configurations) from three major harmonic oscillator shells (N=5,6,7 for neutrons; N=4,5,6 for protons), projected these states onto good angular momentum, and diagonalized the Hamiltonian in the projected basis to obtain yrast states up to spin I=30ħ for 12 even-even isotopes across Cf, Fm, and No isotopic chains.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters ε2 used: 0.255 (246Cf), 0.260 (248Cf), 0.265 (250Cf), 0.245 (252Cf), 0.240 (250Fm), 0.255 (252Fm), 0.250 (254Fm), 0.222 (256Fm), 0.224 (252No), 0.260 (254No), 0.250 (256No), 0.230 (258No)
- Monopole-pairing strength formula: G_M = (21.24 ∓ 13.86(N-Z)/A)/A, with minus for neutrons and plus for protons
- Quadrupole-pairing strength G_Q set to 0.12 × G_M
- Effective charges: e^π = 1.5e for protons, e^ν = 0.5e for neutrons
- g-factor damping factor: 0.75 applied to free-nucleon g_s values (g_s^π = 5.586 × 0.75, g_s^ν = -3.826 × 0.75)
- Band-crossing occurs around spin I=24 in the N=154 isotones (252Cf, 254Fm, 256No)
- In Cf isotopes, g factor nearly doubles from low-spin values at high spins where rotation-alignment occurs
- For 256Fm, a sudden rise in g factor is predicted at high spins; for 250,252,254Fm, g factor remains nearly constant across the entire spin region
- In 252No, g factor increase starts from I=22; in 254No, constant g factor is predicted; in 256,258No, a big drop in g factor is predicted at about I=24
- Dynamic moment of inertia J^(2) for 254No shows down-turning at I=24; for 252No, down-turning at I=22 followed by up-turning at I=26

## Context
This work extends earlier cranking-approximation studies of transfermium nuclei, including the cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Egido and Robledo (2000) for 254No and the cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Afanasjev et al. (2003) for 252,254No, which had suggested either proton alignment or simultaneous proton-neutron alignment. The PSM approach differs from these cranking methods by working in the laboratory frame with angular momentum projection, avoiding the well-known problem at band-crossing regions caused by the cranking approximation noted by Hamamoto (1976). The results enable future experimental tests of single-particle states in the transfermium region through g-factor measurements at high spins, which the authors argue are more sensitive than energy levels or B(E2) values for distinguishing proton versus neutron alignment contributions.
