# Heavy Quark diffusion from lattice QCD spectral functions

> We analyze the low frequency part of charmonium spectral functions on large lattices close to the continuum limit in the temperature region $1.5\lesssim T/T_c\lesssim 3$ as well as for $T \simeq 0.75T_c$. We present evidence for the existence of a transport peak above $T_c$ and its absence below $T_c$. The heavy quark diffusion constant is then estimated using the Kubo formula. As part of the calculation we also determine the temperature dependence of the signature for the charmonium bound state in the spectral function and discuss the fate of charmonium states in the hot medium.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence for the existence of a transport peak in charmonium spectral functions above the critical temperature \(T_c\) and its absence below \(T_c\), but the transport peaks have relatively strong default model dependences, making the quantitative extraction of the heavy quark diffusion constant uncertain.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on quenched lattices with a large volume of \(128^3 \times N_\tau\) and a fine lattice spacing of \(a = 0.01\) fm. They computed charmonium correlation functions in the vector (\(V_{ii}\)) and pseudo-scalar (\(PS\)) channels at temperatures \(T/T_c = 0.73, 1.46, 2.20, 2.93\) and used the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) to extract spectral functions from these correlators via the Kubo formula.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size of \(128^3 \times 96\), \(128^3 \times 48\), \(128^3 \times 32\), and \(128^3 \times 24\) at \(0.73~T_c\), \(1.46~T_c\), \(2.20~T_c\), and \(2.93~T_c\), respectively, with a lattice spacing \(a = 0.01\) fm.
- Temperature range studied: \(1.5 \lesssim T/T_c \lesssim 3\) for above \(T_c\) and \(T \simeq 0.75 T_c\) for below \(T_c\).
- At \(1.46~T_c\), the dimensionless combination \(2\pi T D\) is found to be close to unity and slightly increasing with temperature.
- The ground state peak location at \(0.73~T_c\) is very close to the physical \(J/\psi\) mass.

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative QCD calculations of heavy quark diffusion, which gave \(2\pi TD \approx 71.2\) at leading order (Moore, 2004) and \(2\pi TD \approx 8.4\) at next-to-leading order (Caron-Huot, 2007), as well as the AdS/CFT result of \(2\pi TD = 1\) in the strong coupling limit (Kovtun, 2003). The lattice results provide a non-perturbative estimate of the diffusion constant and suggest the dissociation of both \(J/\psi\) and \(\eta_c\) charmonium states at \(T \geq 1.46~T_c\), enabling further studies of heavy quark transport in the quark-gluon plasma.
