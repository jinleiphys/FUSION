# Skyrme-Rpa Description of Spin-Flip M1 Giant Resonance

> The spin-flip M1 giant resonance is explored in the framework of Random Phase Approximation on the basis of the Skyrme energy functional. A representative set of eight Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, SG2, SkO, SkO', SkI4, and SV-bas) is used. Light and heavy, spherical and deformed nuclei ($^{48}$Ca, $^{158}$Gd, $^{208}$Pb, and $^{238}$U) are considered. The calculations show that spin densities play a crucial role in forming the collective shift in the spectrum. The interplay of the collective shift and spin-orbit splitting determines the quality of the description. None of the considered Skyrme parameterizations is able to describe simultaneously the M1 strength distribution in closed-shell and open-shell nuclei. It is found that the problem lies in the relative positions of proton and neutron spin-orbit splitting. Necessity to involve the tensor and isovector spin-orbit interaction is called for.

## Key claim

None of the eight Skyrme parameterizations tested can simultaneously describe the spin-flip M1 strength distribution in both closed-shell and open-shell nuclei, because the problem lies in the relative positions of proton and neutron spin-orbit splitting, and the tensor and isovector spin-orbit interactions must be involved to improve the description.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) calculations based on the Skyrme energy functional, including all spin-density-dependent terms that are usually omitted for electric modes. They used eight Skyrme parameterizations (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, SG2, SkO, SkO', SkI4, and SV-bas) to compute the spin-flip M1 strength function for four nuclei: the spherical doubly-magic nuclei ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, and the axially deformed nuclei ¹⁵⁸Gd and ²³⁸U.

## Key numbers

- The total collective shifts of the main M1 peak vary from 1-2 MeV in ⁴⁸Ca to 0.5-2 MeV in ²⁰⁸Pb and 0.5-1.5 MeV in ¹⁵⁸Gd and ²³⁸U.
- In the interval 0-45 MeV, the unperturbed summed B(M1) strength is 3.2 μ_N² in ⁴⁸Ca and 18.4-18.6 μ_N² in ²⁰⁸Pb; with residual interaction, the values become 2.5-4.8 μ_N² in ⁴⁸Ca and 14.8-17.3 μ_N² in ²⁰⁸Pb, compared to experimental values of about 5.3 μ_N² and about 17.9 μ_N², respectively.
- The experimental proton-neutron splitting in the M1 gross structure is about 2 MeV in ¹⁵⁸Gd and ²³⁸U, and zero in ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The proton-neutron spin-orbit splitting difference E^{np}_{so} is about 1-2 MeV for most parameterizations, but SkI4, SkO, and SkO' give very close splittings with even E^p_{so} > E^n_{so} for SkI4 and SkO.

## Context

This work extends earlier DFT treatments of magnetic modes, which were limited to a few publications using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) such as Hilton (1998) and Sarriguren (2001), where the former omitted the crucial spin density and the latter used a hybrid model with partial inclusion of SHF. The study is the first systematic and self-consistent SHF exploration of the spin-flip M1 resonance, and it enables future work to fine-tune tensor and isovector spin-orbit interactions to improve both the description of the M1 resonance and the treatment of spin-orbit interaction in nuclei.
