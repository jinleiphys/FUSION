# On the structure observed in the in-flight ${}^{3}\text{He} ( K^{-} , \, Λp ) n$ reaction at J-PARC

> A theoretical investigation is done to clarify the origin of the peak structure observed near the $K^{-} p p$ threshold in the in-flight ${}^{3}\text{He} ( K^{-}, \, Λp ) n$ reaction of the J-PARC E15 experiment, which could be a signal of the lightest kaonic nuclei, that is, the $\bar{K} N N (I=1/2)$ state. For the investigation, we evaluate the $Λp$ invariant mass spectrum assuming two possible scenarios to interpret the experimental peak. One assumes that the $Λ(1405)$ resonance is generated after the emission of an energetic neutron from the absorption of the initial $K^-$, not forming a bound state with the remaining proton. This uncorrelated $Λ(1405) p$ system subsequently decays into the final $Λp$. The other scenario implies that, after the emission of the energetic neutron, a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is formed, decaying eventually into a $Λp$ pair. Our results show that the experimental signal observed in the in-flight ${}^{3}\text{He} ( K^{-} , \, Λp ) n$ reaction at J-PARC is qualitatively well reproduced by the assumption that a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is generated in the reaction, definitely discarding the interpretation in terms of an uncorrelated $Λ(1405) p$ state.

## Key claim

The peak structure observed near the $K^{-} p p$ threshold in the J-PARC E15 experiment is qualitatively well reproduced by assuming the formation of a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state, while the interpretation in terms of an uncorrelated $\Lambda(1405) p$ system is definitely discarded, though the theoretical calculation reproduces the experimental signal only qualitatively, not with exact quantitative agreement.

## Method

The authors evaluate the $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum for the in-flight ${}^{3}\text{He} (K^{-}, \Lambda p) n$ reaction using two theoretical scenarios: one where an uncorrelated $\Lambda(1405) p$ system is generated after emission of a fast neutron, and another where a $\bar{K} N N$ quasi-bound state is formed via multiple kaon scattering between two nucleons. The $\bar{K} N \to \bar{K} N$ scattering and $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance are described by the chiral unitary approach, while the $\bar{K} N N$ bound state is described using the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equation. The cross section is computed with the $\bar{K} N \to \bar{K} N$ scattering amplitudes and the $\bar{K} N N$ multiple scattering amplitudes, incorporating the kaon absorption width by two nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The experimental peak is described by a Breit-Wigner with mass $M_X = 2355 \, ^{+6}_{-4} \text{(stat.)} \pm 12 \text{(sys.)} \text{ MeV}$ and width $\Gamma_X = 110 \, ^{+19}_{-17} \text{(stat.)} \pm 27 \text{(sys.)} \text{ MeV}$ from the J-PARC E15 experiment.
- The $\bar{K} N N$ bound state has a binding energy of approximately $15 \text{ MeV}$ from the $K^{-} p p$ threshold.
- The kaon absorption width by two nucleons in the $\bar{K} N N$ system is fixed to $\Gamma_K = 15 \text{ MeV}$.
- The initial kaon momentum in the laboratory frame is $k_{\text{lab}} = 1 \text{ GeV}/c$ as employed in the J-PARC E15 experiment.
- The ${}^{3}\text{He}$ binding energy is $B_{\HeT} = 7.7 \text{ MeV}$.
- The meson decay constant is taken as $f = 93 \text{ MeV}$, with $D = 0.795$ and $F = 0.465$ for the weak decay of baryons.
- The cutoff for the form factor at the $K^{-} p \Lambda$ vertex is $\Lambda = 0.8 \text{ GeV}$.
- The invariant mass range for the $\Lambda p$ spectrum is restricted to $M_{\text{min}} = 2.2 \text{ GeV}$ and $M_{\text{max}} = 2.6 \text{ GeV}$.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of kaonic nuclei, particularly the $\bar{K} N N$ system, which have been addressed by Akaishi (2002), Shevchenko (2006, 2007), Ikeda (2007, 2008, 2010), Dote (2008), Wycech (2008), Bayar (2011, 2012), Barnea (2012), and others, with binding energies ranging from a few MeV to 100 MeV and widths from 10 to 90 MeV. The study specifically builds on the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equation developed by Bayar (2011, 2012), which found a binding of 15-30 MeV and a width of about 80 MeV. The results enable a definitive interpretation of the J-PARC E15 experimental signal as a $\bar{K} N N$ bound state, resolving previous contradictory claims from experiments such as those by Agnello (2005), Yamazaki (2010), Tokiyasu (2013), Ichikawa (2014), and Hashimoto (2014).
