# Ab initio optical potentials for magnesium isotopes: from stability to the island of inversion

> We present the first calculations of ab initio nonlocal optical potentials for $^{24,26,28}$Mg and $^{32}$Mg isotopes using the leading-order term of the spectator expansion of multiple-scattering theory. We use the structure input from the ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM), which provides translationally invariant, off-shell scalar and spin-projected densities so that structure and reaction inputs are treated on equal footing with no adjustable parameters. This leading-order potential reproduces $^{24}$Mg neutron total, reaction, and elastic-scattering data at energies between 65 and 250 MeV and provides predictions for $^{26,28}$Mg and $^{32}$Mg. We compare our prediction with those from uncertainty-quantified Koning-Delaroche (KDUQ) and Weppner-Penney global optical potentials, and with the ENDF nuclear data evaluations. These comparisons highlight some of the limitations of the global models, while also validating their use in reaction modeling near the N=20 island of inversion.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first ab initio nonlocal optical potentials for magnesium isotopes using leading-order multiple-scattering theory with structure input from the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model, but the agreement with experimental data is best at energies above 100 MeV and deteriorates at lower energies (65-80 MeV) where rescattering effects not included at leading order become important.

## Method

The authors compute ground-state wavefunctions for 24,26,28,32Mg using the NNLO_opt chiral nucleon-nucleon potential within the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) framework. These wavefunctions provide translationally invariant scalar and spin-projected one-body densities, which are folded with off-shell nucleon-nucleon amplitudes in the leading-order spectator expansion to construct nonlocal optical potentials. The resulting Watson optical potentials are then used to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for elastic scattering observables, with Coulomb interactions included via exact momentum-space formulation for proton projectiles.

## Key numbers

- Neutron total cross sections for 24Mg are reproduced between 100 and 250 MeV laboratory kinetic energy with excellent agreement to experimental data from Abfalterer (2001)
- For 24Mg, calculations use Nmax=4, 6, and 8 model spaces with hw=13, 15, and 17 MeV; for 32Mg only Nmax=6 with hw=15 MeV is presented
- Proton elastic scattering data for 24Mg are compared at 65, 80, 135, and 250 MeV laboratory energies, with data from Kato (1985), Sakaguchi (1979), Hatanaka (1984), Schwandt (1982), and Hicks (1988)
- The KDUQ phenomenological potential shows approximately 10-15% lower proton reaction cross sections compared to the ab initio calculations across all magnesium isotopes
- For 24Mg, the maximum contribution to the reaction cross section at 65 MeV comes from angular momentum L approximately 5 for both the ab initio calculation and KDUQ

## Context

This work extends ab initio optical potential calculations from lighter systems studied by Burrows (2018) and Baker (2024) to the magnesium isotopic chain, including the neutron-rich 32Mg in the N=20 island of inversion. The results are compared with the uncertainty-quantified Koning-Delaroche (KDUQ) global optical potential from Pruitt (2023), the Weppner-Penney phenomenological potential from Weppner (2009), and ENDF nuclear data evaluations. The comparisons validate the use of global phenomenological models near stability while revealing their limitations when extrapolated to neutron-rich isotopes, particularly for reaction cross sections and at energies above 200 MeV where KDUQ is not recommended for use.
