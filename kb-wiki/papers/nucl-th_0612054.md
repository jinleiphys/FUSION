# Pair production from nuclear collisions and cosmic ray transport

> Modern cosmic ray transport codes, that are capable of use for a variety of applications, need to include all significant atomic, nuclear and particle reactions at a variety of energies. Lepton pair production from nucleus-nucleus collisions has not been included in transport codes to date. Using the methods of Baur, Bertulani and Baron, the present paper provides estimates of electron-positron pair production cross sections for nuclei and energies relevant to cosmic ray transport. It is shown that the cross sections are large compared to other typical processes such as single neutron removal due to strong or electromagnetic interactions. Therefore lepton pair production may need to be included in some transport code applications involving MeV electrons.

## Key claim

Electron-positron pair production cross sections from nucleus-nucleus collisions are large (typically in the barn range) compared to single neutron removal cross sections (typically in the millibarn range) for nuclei and energies found in the cosmic ray spectrum, and therefore may need to be included in cosmic ray transport codes for applications involving MeV electrons, depending on the particular application, particles of interest, and incident spectrum.

## Method

The author used the theoretical methods of Baur, Bertulani, and Baron to calculate electron-positron pair production cross sections for various projectile-target combinations and kinetic energies relevant to cosmic ray transport. The pair production cross sections were computed using the formula σ = (28/27π) (Z_P Z_T α² / m_l)² log³(γ/2), where Z_P and Z_T are projectile and target charges, α is the fine structure constant, m_l is the lepton mass, and γ is the relativistic gamma factor. For comparison, single neutron removal cross sections via strong and electromagnetic interactions were calculated according to the methods of References bcv and norbury respectively.

- For ¹²C projectile on ¹²C target at 3 AGeV: σ_pair = 0.7 mb, σ_Strong = 64 mb, σ_EM = 0.6 mb
- For ¹²C projectile on ⁵⁶Fe target at 50 AGeV: σ_pair = 1220 mb, σ_Strong = 92 mb, σ_EM = 25 mb
- For ²⁸Si projectile on ⁵⁶Fe target at 50 AGeV: σ_pair = 6643 mb, σ_Strong = 100 mb, σ_EM = 52 mb
- For ⁵⁶Fe projectile on ⁵⁶Fe target at 50 AGeV: σ_pair = 22910 mb, σ_Strong = 116 mb, σ_EM = 298 mb
- For ¹⁹⁷Au projectile on ⁵⁶Fe target at 50 AGeV: σ_pair = 211516 mb, σ_Strong = 156 mb, σ_EM = 2108 mb
- For ¹⁹⁷Au projectile on ¹²C target at 50 AGeV: σ_pair = 11264 mb, σ_Strong = 128 mb, σ_EM = 118 mb
- Pair production cross sections scale as Z_P² Z_T² and are inversely proportional to lepton mass squared (1/m_l²)

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Baur, Bertulani, and Baron (1990s) for lepton pair production from very high energies and very heavy nuclei to the specific nuclei and energies found in the cosmic ray spectrum. The results show that electron-positron pair production cross sections are large enough that this reaction may need to be added to existing cosmic ray transport codes such as HZETRN, GEANT, and FLUKA, which currently do not include lepton pair production from nucleus-nucleus collisions. This enables more accurate radiation environment predictions for applications involving MeV electrons, such as calculating doses for extra-vehicular activity (EVA) where a 0.5 MeV electron can penetrate a spacesuit.
