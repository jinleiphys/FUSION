# Statistical Hauser-Feshbach model description of $(n,α)$ reaction cross sections for the weak s-process

> The $(n,α)$ reaction contributes in many processes of energy generation and nucleosynthesis in stellar environment. Since experimental data are available for a limited number of nuclei and in restricted energy ranges, at present only theoretical studies can provide predictions for all astrophysically relevant $(n,α)$ reaction cross sections. The purpose of this work is to study $(n,α)$ reaction cross sections for a set of nuclei contributing in the weak s-process nucleosynthesis. Theory framework is based on the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model implemented in TALYS code with nuclear masses and level densities based on Skyrme energy density functional. In addition to the analysis of the properties of calculated $(n,α)$ cross sections, the Maxwellian averaged cross sections are described and analyzed for the range of temperatures in stellar environment. Model calculations determined astrophysically relevant energy windows in which $(n,α)$ reactions occur in stars. In order to reduce the uncertainties in modeling $(n,α)$ reaction cross sections for the s-process, novel experimental studies are called for. Presented results on the effective energy windows for $(n,α)$ reaction in weak s-process provide a guidance for the priority energy ranges in the future experimental studies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that statistical Hauser-Feshbach model calculations using the TALYS code, with nuclear masses and level densities based on the Skyrme energy density functional (HFB-17), can provide reasonable predictions for $(n,\alpha)$ reaction cross sections for twelve nuclei relevant to the weak s-process, but the results show considerable model dependence (up to an order of magnitude difference between different theoretical approaches) and the authors call for novel experimental studies to reduce uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used the statistical Hauser-Feshbach model implemented in the nuclear reaction program TALYS-1.95 to calculate $(n,\alpha)$ reaction cross sections for twelve nuclei contributing to the weak s-process nucleosynthesis. Two approaches were employed: TALYS-a using experimental masses with Fermi gas model level densities, and TALYS-b using nuclear masses and level densities from the Skyrme energy density functional (HFB-17) with the $\alpha$ optical model potential from Avrigeanu (2014) and width fluctuation corrections using the Moldauer model. The cross sections were then averaged over the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution to obtain Maxwellian averaged cross sections (MACS) for stellar temperatures, and the astrophysically relevant energy windows (Gamow windows) were determined from the subintegral function $f_I(E) = \sigma(E)Ee^{-E/(kT)}$.

## Key numbers

- The $(n,\alpha)$ reaction Q-values (in MeV) for the twelve studied nuclei, calculated using Skyrme HFB-17 except for $^{17}$O and $^{22}$Na where experimental values were adopted: $^{17}$O (1.817), $^{18}$F (3.442), $^{22}$Na (1.9523), $^{26}$Al (2.462), $^{33}$S (3.870), $^{37}$Ar (3.264), $^{39}$Ar (3.060), $^{40}$K (2.973), $^{41}$Ca (6.101), $^{59}$Ni (5.711), $^{65}$Zn (6.223), $^{71}$Ge (5.704)
- Maximal cross section values at intermediate energies (E $\approx$ 1-20 MeV) vary from approximately 30 mb for $^{71}$Ge to more than 300 mb for $^{41}$Ca and $^{18}$F
- For $^{59}$Ni, a single resonance peak structure was obtained at 203 eV excitation energy, where the Hauser-Feshbach model is not applicable
- For $^{40}$K, only a single experimental data point is available at 0.025 eV
- The Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions were displayed for the temperature range $kT =$ 30-210 keV
- For $kT =$ 30 keV, the maximal reaction peaks for all nuclei are located below energy of 0.1 MeV
- For $^{33}$S and $^{37}$Ar, more than an order of magnitude difference was obtained between the present MACS results and those from NON-SMOKER
- For $^{33}$S, the MACS values in this work and from NON-SMOKER are considerably larger than ENDF, JEFF, and JENDL evaluation data for $kT <$ 10 keV

## Context

This work extends the Hauser-Feshbach model calculations of $(n,\alpha)$ reaction cross sections for the weak s-process network introduced by Pignatari (2010), focusing on twelve nuclei from convective He burning core and shell C-burning in massive stars. The study provides systematic theoretical predictions where experimental data are limited or nonexistent, particularly for $^{18}$F, $^{22}$Na, $^{39}$Ar, $^{40}$K, $^{65}$Zn, and $^{71}$Ge. The results are compared with existing experimental data for $^{17}$O, $^{26}$Al, $^{33}$S, $^{37}$Ar, and $^{41}$Ca from Smet (2007), Koehler (1995), Wagemans (1987), Vermote (2012), and Goeminne (2000), as well as with evaluated data sets including NON-SMOKER from Rauscher (2000), TENDL-2019, ENDF-B-VIII, JEFF-3.3, and BROND-3.1. The determination of astrophysically relevant energy windows provides guidance for priority energy ranges in future experimental studies at novel facilities, which are needed to reduce the systematic uncertainties in modeling $(n,\alpha)$ reaction cross sections for the s-process.
