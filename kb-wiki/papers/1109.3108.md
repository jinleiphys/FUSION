# Probing flavor-dependent EMC effect with W boson production

> A recent theoretical model predicts that the modification of quark distributions in the nuclear medium (EMC effect) depends on the flavor of the quarks. We investigate $W$-boson production in proton-nucleus collision as a possible tool to test this theoretical prediction. Several experimental observables in $W$ production sensitive to the flavor-dependent EMC effect are identified. Calculations for these experimental observables at the RHIC and LHC energies are presented using the recent flavor-dependent EMC model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that flavor-dependent modifications of quark distributions in nuclei (the EMC effect), as predicted by the Cloët-Bentz-Thomas (CBT) model, can be tested via \(W\)-boson production ratios in proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC, but the effect is relatively small (maximally about 6.5% in one ratio) and requires high-statistics measurements at negative rapidity or negative \(x_F\).

## Method

The authors calculate leading-order (LO) differential cross sections for \(W^+\) and \(W^-\) production in \(p+A\) and \(p+D\) collisions using the CBT model’s flavor-dependent nuclear parton distribution functions (PDFs), evolved via DGLAP to the \(W\) mass scale. They define three ratios (\(R^+_{A/D}\), \(R^-_{A/D}\), and \(R^{\pm}_A\)) that isolate the nuclear modification of \(u\) and \(d\) valence quarks at large negative \(x_F\) (or negative lepton rapidity \(y\)), and compare predictions with and without the \(\rho^0\) mean field that drives the flavor dependence. The calculations are performed for \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (RHIC, \(p+\)Au) and \(\sqrt{s}=5.520\) TeV (LHC, \(p+\)Pb), and the lepton-level observables are obtained by convolving with the \(W\to l\nu\) decay angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- The maximal effect of the \(\rho^0\) mean field on \(R^+_{A/D}\) is 3.6% at \(y=-3.5\) for \(p+\)Pb at \(\sqrt{s}=5.520\) TeV.
- The maximal effect on \(R^-_{A/D}\) is 3.6% at \(y=-4.5\) for the same system.
- The maximal effect on \(R^{\pm}_A\) is 6.5% at \(y=-3.8\) for the same system.
- The \(K\) factor for first-order QCD corrections at the \(W\) mass scale is \(K \simeq 1.323\) (with \(\alpha_s \simeq 0.1158\)).
- The expected statistical uncertainties for LHCb with an integrated luminosity of 1 fb\(^{-1}\) are sufficient to distinguish the flavor-dependent from flavor-independent predictions for \(R^+_{A/D}\) and \(R^-_{A/D}\).
- The CBT model uses a \(\rho^0\) coupling strength \(G_\rho = 14.2\) GeV\(^{-2}\), determined from the empirical symmetry energy of nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the study of the EMC effect beyond inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) by proposing \(W\)-boson production as a probe of flavor-dependent nuclear modifications, following the theoretical model of Cloët, Bentz, and Thomas (CBT) (2010). It builds on earlier constraints from Drell-Yan measurements (which ruled out nuclear enhancement of \(\bar u\) distributions) and complements other proposed probes such as parity-violating DIS at JLab 12 GeV, semi-inclusive DIS, and pion-induced Drell-Yan. The results enable future experimental tests at LHCb (with coverage up to \(|y|=4.5\)) and at RHIC, and call for next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations once the CBT model’s NLO PDFs become available.
