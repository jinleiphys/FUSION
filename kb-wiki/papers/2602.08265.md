# Stochastic many-body perturbation theory for high-order calculations

> High-order perturbative $\textit{ab initio}$ calculations are challenging due to the rapidly growing configuration space and the difficulty of assessing convergence. In this letter, we introduce perturbation theory quantum Monte Carlo (PTQMC), a stochastic approach designed to compute high-order many-body perturbative corrections. By representing the perturbative wave function with random walkers in configuration space, PTQMC avoids the exponential scaling inherent to conventional constructions of high-rank excitation operators. Benchmark calculations for the Richardson pairing model demonstrate that PTQMC accurately reproduces exact many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) coefficients up to 16th order, even in strongly divergent regimes. We further show that combining PTQMC with series resummation techniques yields stable and precise energy estimates in cases where the straightforward perturbative series fails. Finally, we propose the effective number of configurations, $e^{S}$, as a global measure of perturbative wave-function complexity that can be directly extracted within PTQMC. We demonstrate that the saturation behavior of $e^{S}$ provides a more reliable indicator of the validity of perturbative expansions than energy convergence alone.

## Key claim

Perturbation theory quantum Monte Carlo (PTQMC) can compute many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) coefficients up to 16th order in the Richardson pairing model, including in strongly divergent regimes, but the method's reliability depends on the saturation behavior of the effective number of configurations \(e^{S}\) rather than on energy convergence alone.

## Method

PTQMC reformulates Rayleigh-Schrodinger MBPT as a stochastic sampling problem in configuration space, representing the perturbative wave function by signed random walkers distributed over many-body configurations. Walkers are propagated order by order via spawning events to connected configurations, with probabilities proportional to Hamiltonian matrix elements and energy denominators, following the exact MBPT recursion relation. The method avoids explicit construction of high-order diagrams and the exponential scaling of conventional deterministic implementations, and it provides direct access to order-resolved wave-function structure.

- The criterion for poor convergence of \(e^{S}\) is defined as \(|e^{S^{(10)}} - e^{S^{(9)}}| / e^{S^{(9)}} \ge 0.2\%\), with threshold variation between \(0.1\%\) and \(0.5\%\) yielding the same qualitative identification.

## Context

This work extends the FCIQMC algorithm of Booth (2009) and Hu (2024) to a perturbative variant that samples perturbative orders rather than evolving toward the exact ground state. It addresses the long-standing challenge that high-order MBPT calculations exceeding fourth order have never been achieved in large systems, as noted by Machleidt (2024) and Tichai (2016). The method enables systematic assessment of perturbative uncertainties in ab initio nuclear theory and, when combined with Pade resummation, can outperform nonperturbative methods such as ADC(2) and IMSRG(2) in strong-correlation regimes, as demonstrated by comparison with Heinz (2020) and Brolli (2025).
