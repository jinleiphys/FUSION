# Connecting Polyakov Loops to the Thermodynamics of $SU(N_c)$ Gauge Theories Using the Gauge-String Duality

> We show that in four-dimensional gauge theories dual to five-dimensional Einstein gravity coupled to a single scalar field in the bulk the derivative of the single heavy quark free energy in the deconfined phase is $dF_{Q}(T)/dT \sim -1/c_s^2(T)$, where $c_s(T)$ is the speed of sound. This general result provides a direct link between the softest point in the equation of state of strongly-coupled plasmas and the deconfinement phase transition described by the expectation value of the Polyakov loop. We give an explicit example of a gravity dual with black hole solutions that can reproduce the lattice results for the expectation value of the Polyakov loop and the thermodynamics of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the (non-perturbative) temperature range between $T_c$ and $3T_c$.

## Key claim
The derivative of the single heavy quark free energy in the deconfined phase of four-dimensional gauge theories dual to five-dimensional Einstein gravity coupled to a single scalar field is shown to be proportional to the inverse square of the speed of sound, specifically \(dF_{Q}(T)/dT \sim -1/c_s^2(T)\). This result is derived under the assumption that the gravity dual's action comes from a non-critical string theory in five dimensions and that the scalar potential has a specific exponential form in the infrared.

## Method
The author considers a five-dimensional Einstein gravity action coupled to a single scalar field, with the metric ansatz including a blackening factor \(f(u)\) and a scalar field \(\phi = u\) as the radial coordinate. The equations of motion are solved using a generating function \(G(u) = A'(u)\), following the method of Gubser and Nellore (2008), which reduces the system to a nonlinear differential equation for \(G\). The Polyakov loop expectation value is computed from the regularized free energy of a single static heavy quark, obtained via the Nambu-Goto action for a classical string hanging from a probe D-brane at the boundary to the black hole horizon. The derivative \(dF_Q/dT\) is then expressed in terms of the speed of sound using thermodynamic identities and horizon conditions.

## Key numbers
- The scalar potential used is \(V(\phi) = (-12 \cosh \gamma \phi + b_1 \phi^2)/R^2\) with \(\gamma = \sqrt{1/2}\) and \(b_1 = 1.95\), giving a UV scaling dimension \(\Delta = 3.3784\).
- The gravitational constant is set to \(k_5^2 = 0.150376\) to match lattice entropy density data.
- The ratio \(\alpha'/R^2 = 3.5\) is chosen to fit the renormalized Polyakov loop to lattice data from Gupta et al. (2007) in the temperature range \(T/T_c = 1\) to \(3\).
- The high-temperature limit yields \(F_Q = -(R^2/(2\alpha')) T\) for any potential satisfying the UV boundary condition.

## Context
This work extends the bottom-up holographic approach of Gubser and Nellore (2008) and Gursoy et al. (2007, 2008) by directly linking the Polyakov loop to the speed of sound, a key thermodynamic quantity. It provides a general relation that connects the deconfinement phase transition (characterized by the Polyakov loop expectation value) to the softest point in the equation of state of strongly-coupled plasmas. The explicit example reproduces lattice results for SU(3) Yang-Mills thermodynamics from Boyd et al. (1996) and Polyakov loop data from Gupta et al. (2007) and Kaczmarek et al. (2002) in the non-perturbative temperature range between \(T_c\) and \(3T_c\), enabling future studies of higher-order derivative corrections to the gravity action.
