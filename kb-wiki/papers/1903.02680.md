# Photoproduction of charged final states in ultra-peripheral collisions and electroproduction at an electron-ion collider

> Ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) of relativistic ions are an important tool for studying photoproduction at high energies. Vector meson photoproduction is an important tool for nuclear structure measurements and other applications. A future electron-ion collider (EIC) will allow additional studies, using virtual photons with a wide range of $Q^2$. We propose a significant expansion of the UPC and EIC photoproduction physics programs to include charged final states which may be produced via Reggeon exchange. We consider two examples: $a_2^+(1320)$, which is a conventional $q\overline q$ meson, and the exotic $Z_c^+(4430)$ state (modeled here as a tetraquark). The $Z_c^+(4430)$ cross-section depends on its internal structure, so photoproduction can test whether the $Z_c^+(4430)$ is a tetraquark or other exotic object. We calculate the rates and kinematic distributions for $γp\rightarrow X^+n$ in $pA$ UPCs and $ep$ collisions at an EIC and in UPCs. The rates are large enough for detailed studies of these final states. Because the cross-section for Reggeon exchange is largest near threshold, the final state rapidity distribution depends on the beam energies. At high-energy colliders like the proposed LHeC or $pA$ collisions at the LHC, the final states are produced at far forward rapidities. For lower energy colliders, the systems are produced closer to mid-rapidity, within reach of central detectors.

# Photoproduction of charged final states in ultra-peripheral collisions and electroproduction at an electron-ion collider

## Key claim

Photoproduction of charged final states via Reggeon exchange in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) and at a future electron-ion collider (EIC) can produce measurable rates of both conventional mesons like the $a_2^+(1320)$ and exotic states like the $Z_c^+(4430)$, provided that detectors have sufficient forward acceptance or beam energies are tuned to bring production to mid-rapidity. The cross-section for the $Z_c^+(4430)$ depends on its internal structure, so photoproduction can test whether it is a tetraquark or other exotic object, but the calculations assume a specific tetraquark model with $J^P=1^+$ and the actual cross-section could differ by up to a factor of three depending on the spin and parity.

## Method

The authors extended two existing Monte Carlo codes, STARlight for UPCs and eSTARlight for $ep$ collisions, to model photoproduction of $a_2^+(1320)$ and $Z_c^+(4430)$ via Reggeon exchange in the reaction $\gamma p \rightarrow X^+ n$. For the $a_2^+(1320)$, they used a Regge-inspired parameterization fitted to fixed-target photoproduction data from five experiments; for the $Z_c^+(4430)$, they used a four-parameter fit to a theoretical Reggeon-exchange calculation assuming a tetraquark structure. They calculated cross-sections and event rates for six accelerator configurations: $pAu$ UPCs at RHIC, $pPb$ UPCs at the LHC, and $ep$ collisions at eRHIC, JLEIC, LHeC, and EicC, considering both photoproduction ($Q^2<1$ GeV$^2$) and electroproduction ($1<Q^2<5$ GeV$^2$) regimes.

## Key numbers

- $a_2^+(1320)$ photoproduction cross-section at $W=2.9$ GeV is about 7% of the $\rho^0$ photoproduction cross-section
- $a_2^+(1320)$ mass $M_X=1318$ MeV with width $\Gamma=105$ MeV; $Z_c^+(4430)$ mass $M_X=4478$ MeV with width $\Gamma=181$ MeV
- $a_2^+(1320)$ photoproduction cross-section in $ep$ collisions: 79 nb at eRHIC, 69 nb at JLEIC, 106 nb at LHeC, 47 nb at EicC (for $Q^2<1$ GeV$^2$)
- $Z_c^+(4430)$ photoproduction cross-section in $ep$ collisions: 0.26 nb at eRHIC, 0.22 nb at JLEIC, 0.36 nb at LHeC, 0.094 nb at EicC (for $Q^2<1$ GeV$^2$)
- $a_2^+(1320)$ photoproduction cross-section in UPCs: 0.17 mb at RHIC ($pAu$), 0.56 mb at LHC ($pPb$)
- $Z_c^+(4430)$ photoproduction cross-section in UPCs: 0.32 $\mu$b at RHIC ($pAu$), 1.76 $\mu$b at LHC ($pPb$)
- Event rates for $a_2^+(1320)$ at EICs: 0.47–1.06 billion events per year (photoproduction); at RHIC: 0.78 billion events; at LHC: 1.12 billion events
- Event rates for $Z_c^+(4430)$ at EICs: 0.94–3.6 million events per year (photoproduction); at RHIC: 1.4 million events; at LHC: 3.52 million events
- For $Z_c^+(4430)$ with $J^P=1^-$, the cross-section is 40% larger; with $J^P=0^-$, the cross-section is about three times larger at the peak

## Context

This work extends the established program of vector meson photoproduction in UPCs at RHIC and the LHC (studied for $\rho$, $\omega$, $J/\psi$, $\psi'$, and $\Upsilon$) to charged final states produced via Reggeon exchange, which has previously been observed only for the $\rho$ at RHIC. The authors build on the STARlight code (Klein 2016) and eSTARlight code (Lomnitz 2018) used for vector meson photoproduction, and on fixed-target $a_2^+$ photoproduction data from Eisenberg (1969), Ballam (1971), Struczinski (1975), Condo (1993), and Nozar (2008). The $Z_c^+(4430)$ was discovered by Belle (Choi 2007) and its photoproduction cross-section is calculated following the Reggeon-exchange model of Galata (2011). This work enables future experimental tests of exotic hadron structure at RHIC, the LHC, and proposed EICs, particularly for heavy exotic states beyond the reach of fixed-target photon beams.
