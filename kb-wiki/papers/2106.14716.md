# String breaking in a cold wind as seen by string models

> Using the gauge/string duality, we model a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a color singlet state moving through a cold medium and explore the consequences of temperature and velocity on string breaking. We show that the string breaking distance slowly varies with temperature and velocity away from the critical line but could fall near it.

## Key claim

Using gauge/string duality, the author models a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a color singlet state moving through a cold medium and shows that the string breaking distance slowly varies with temperature and velocity away from the critical line but could fall near it, with the analysis restricted to the case of two dynamical flavors.

## Method

The author employs the AdS/QCD framework with a five-dimensional metric deformed by a soft wall and a blackening factor, then Wick rotates to Minkowski form and boosts to the rest frame of the quark-antiquark pair to model a cold wind. The Nambu-Goto action for the string is combined with boundary terms representing light quarks via a constant background scalar field, and the string breaking distance is computed by equating the energy of the connected string configuration (heavy quark-antiquark pair) with twice the energy of a disconnected configuration representing two heavy-light mesons. Two cases are considered: wind velocity perpendicular and parallel to the quark-antiquark axis.

## Key numbers

- The soft wall parameter is fixed at s = 0.450 GeV^2 from the Regge trajectory slope for rho(n) mesons.
- The string tension parameter g = 0.176 is fixed by fitting the physical string tension at T=0 to the value from lattice data.
- The parameter n = 3.057 is adjusted to reproduce the lattice result for the string breaking distance at T=0, which is 1.22 fm.
- The critical temperature T_pc = 132 MeV at s = 0.45 GeV^2.
- In the transverse case, the string breaking distance deviation from the constant value (1.22 fm) reaches a maximum of about 2% on the domain boundary.
- In the longitudinal case, the deviation from the constant value does not exceed 2%.
- The low-temperature asymptotic expansion coefficients are a_perp = 0.026 and b_perp = 0 for the transverse case, and a_parallel = -0.022 and b_parallel = 0.048 for the longitudinal case.
- The maximal discrepancy between the full calculation and the low-temperature approximation is less than 0.2%.
- The light quark mass estimate gives m_u/d = 46.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of string breaking using gauge/string duality, specifically the zero-temperature analysis by KKW and the finite-temperature and non-zero chemical potential study by Andreev (a-strb1). It addresses the previously unexamined case of a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving through a cold medium, where no lattice results exist at finite temperature and velocity. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD studies of string breaking in moving systems and provide a framework for understanding the behavior near the critical line where thermal fluctuations dominate over light quark pair production.
