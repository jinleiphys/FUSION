# Heavy meson production in hot dense matter

> The properties of charmed mesons in dense matter are studied using a unitary coupled-channel approach in the nuclear medium which takes into account Pauli-blocking effects and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner. We obtain the open-charm meson spectral functions in this dense nuclear environment, and discuss their implications on hidden charm and charm scalar resonances and on the formation of $D$-mesic nuclei at FAIR energies.

## Key claim
The properties of open-charm mesons in dense nuclear matter are studied using a self-consistent unitary coupled-channel approach, but the results depend strongly on which effective Lagrangian (SU(4) versus SU(8) with heavy-quark symmetry) is used, and the formation of D-mesic nuclei is only feasible in the SU(8) scheme where widths are sufficiently small relative to mass shifts.

## Method
The authors compute the self-energies and spectral functions of D and D* mesons in symmetric nuclear matter by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, using effective Lagrangians derived from SU(4) and SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry groups. The in-medium solution incorporates Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings, and meson self-energies in a self-consistent manner, with the transition amplitudes summed over the nucleon Fermi distribution at finite temperature. The resulting self-energies are then used to study the modification of dynamically-generated scalar resonances (D_s0(2317) and X(3700)) and to solve the Schrödinger equation for D^0-nucleus bound states.

## Key numbers
- The D_s0(2317) resonance, which has zero width in vacuum, develops a width of order 100 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- The X(3700) hidden charm scalar resonance, with a small vacuum width, develops a width of order 200 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- The D^0-nucleus bound states are weakly bound with significant widths, in contrast to previous results using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model by Tsushima et al. (1999).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of in-medium modification of open-charm mesons by Tolos et al. (2007) and Mizutani et al. (2006), using both SU(4) and SU(8) symmetry schemes. It builds on the generation of dynamically-generated resonances such as the D_s0(2317) from Gamermann et al. (2006) and the predicted X(3700) from the same group. The results are relevant for the PANDA and CBM experiments at the FAIR facility at GSI, and the finding of weakly bound D^0-nucleus states (in contrast to the stronger binding predicted by the QMC model of Tsushima et al. (1999)) provides guidance for experimental searches with antiproton or proton beams.
