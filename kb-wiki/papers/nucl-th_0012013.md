# Chiral symmetry and quantum hadro-dynamics

> Using the linear sigma model, we study the evolutions of the quark condensate and of the nucleon mass in the nuclear medium. Our formulation of the model allows the inclusion of both pion and scalar-isoscalar degrees of freedom. It guarantees that the low energy theorems and the constrains of chiral perturbation theory are respected. We show how this formalism incorporates quantum hadro-dynamics improved by the pion loops effects.

## Key claim

Using a reformulation of the linear sigma model that keeps the chiral-invariant scalar-isoscalar field (theta) as an explicit degree of freedom, the authors show that the in-medium nucleon mass evolution is driven exclusively by this scalar field and is independent of the pion cloud contribution to the quark condensate, thereby avoiding a conflict with chiral perturbation theory constraints that would forbid a pion-loop-induced mass change.

## Method

The authors reformulate the linear sigma model by making a point transformation from the usual (sigma, pion) fields to a chiral-invariant scalar field (theta) and a pion field (phi), where theta represents fluctuations along the chiral radius. They then compute the in-medium quark condensate, nucleon mass, theta mass, and axial coupling constant using a mean-field approximation for the scalar field combined with a pion-gas treatment for the pion loops, expanding results to leading order in density and pion field expectation values.

## Key numbers

* At normal nuclear density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}), the pion cloud produces a relative decrease of the quark condensate of about 20%.
* For a theta mass of 1 GeV (resp. 0.8 GeV), the scalar mean field at normal density is 17 MeV (resp. 30 MeV), giving a nucleon mass reduction of -170 MeV (resp. -300 MeV).
* For a theta mass of 1 GeV (resp. 0.8 GeV), the theta mass drops by 22% (resp. 34%) at normal density.
* The scalar-field-driven part of the condensate evolution gives a relative decrease of 14% (resp. 25%) at normal density for m_theta = 1 GeV (resp. 0.8 GeV).
* At normal density, the scalar contribution yields a quenching of g_A of about 6%, while the pionic contribution gives a quenching of about 15%.

## Context

This work extends the study of in-medium chiral symmetry restoration by clarifying the role of the scalar-isoscalar meson, which was treated in the mean-field approximation of quantum hadro-dynamics (QHD) by Serot (1986) but without a chiral-invariant formulation. It addresses the conflict pointed out by Birse (1996, 1998) between the Brown-Rho scaling law and chiral perturbation theory, showing that the nucleon mass and condensate evolutions are not universally linked. The reformulation incorporates both the pion-loop effects studied by Chanfray et al. (1998) and the scalar meson exchange, enabling a consistent treatment of the relation between mass and condensate evolutions that was missing in the work of Birse and McGovern (1993) (which used the linear sigma model without pion loops) and Delorme et al. (1996) (which used the non-linear sigma model without scalar mesons).
