# Mitigating Green's function Monte Carlo signal-to-noise problems using contour deformations

> The Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) method provides accurate solutions to the nuclear many-body problem and predicts properties of light nuclei starting from realistic two- and three-body interactions. Controlling the GFMC fermion-sign problem is crucial, as the signal-to-noise ratio decreases exponentially with Euclidean time, requiring significant computing resources. Inspired by similar scenarios in lattice quantum field theory and spin systems, in this work, we employ integration contour deformations to improve the GFMC signal-to-noise ratio. Machine learning techniques are used to select optimal contours with minimal variance from parameterized families of deformations. As a proof of principle, we consider the deuteron binding energies and Euclidean density response functions. We only observe mild signal-to-noise improvement for the binding energy case. On the other hand, we achieve an order of magnitude reduction of the variance for Euclidean density response functions, paving the way for computing electron- and neutrino-nucleus cross-sections of larger nuclei.

## Key claim
Contour deformations of the coordinate integration in Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) can reduce variance for certain observables, but the improvement is observable-dependent: only mild signal-to-noise improvement is seen for the deuteron binding energy, while an order-of-magnitude variance reduction is achieved for Euclidean density response functions.

## Method
The authors analytically continue the Argonne v6' (AV6P) and v4' (AV4P) potentials and the deuteron variational wavefunction to holomorphic functions using Chebyshev polynomial fits with degree 250 and a cutoff of (20 fm)^2. They then parameterize families of contour deformations in spherical coordinates and use machine learning techniques to optimize the deformation parameters by minimizing the variance of the observable of interest, treating the variance as a loss function.

## Key numbers
- Chebyshev polynomial degree: \(n_{\mathrm{max}} = 250\), with \(\varrho_{\mathrm{max}} = (20\ \mathrm{fm})^2\) for the holomorphic approximation of potentials and wavefunctions
- Uniform grid spacing for Chebyshev fitting: \(0.001\ \mathrm{fm}^2\)
- An order of magnitude reduction of the variance is achieved for Euclidean density response functions of the deuteron
- Only mild signal-to-noise improvement is observed for the deuteron binding energy

## Context
This work extends contour deformation techniques previously applied to lattice quantum field theory (Detmold 2020, Detmold 2021) and spin systems to the nuclear GFMC framework. The method addresses the fermion sign problem that causes exponential signal-to-noise degradation in GFMC calculations of nuclei up to \(A \lesssim 12\) (Carlson 2014, Gandolfi 2020). The variance reduction for Euclidean density response functions enables more precise computations of electron- and neutrino-nucleus cross-sections, which are needed for next-generation neutrino experiments (DUNE 2015, NuSTEC 2017).
