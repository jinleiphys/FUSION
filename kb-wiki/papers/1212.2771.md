# Results from the spin programme at COSY-ANKE

> Some of the important results from the COSY-Juelich spin programme are summarised. These include the measurement of the deuteron beam momentum through the excitation of a depolarising resonance, which allowed the mass of the eta-meson to be determined to high precision. The charge exchange of polarised deuterons on hydrogen gave rise to a detailed study of the spin dependence of large angle neutron-proton elastic scattering amplitudes. The measurements of the cross section and analysing powers for pion production in both pp and pn collisions at 353 MeV could be described very successfully in terms of a partial wave decomposition.

## Key claim

The COSY-ANKE spin programme achieved high-precision results by exploiting polarised beams and targets, but the abstract's claim of "very successful" partial-wave decomposition of pion production data relies on theoretical assumptions (neglecting coupling between initial \(^{3}P_2\) and \(^{3}F_2\) waves and using Watson theorem to determine phases) that reduce the number of free parameters from ten to seven.

## Method

The beam momentum was determined by measuring the frequency of artificially induced depolarising resonances in a vector polarised deuteron beam, using a solenoid to match the spin precession frequency and Schottky noise to measure the revolution frequency. The charge exchange of tensor polarised deuterons on hydrogen was used to study spin-dependent neutron-proton elastic scattering amplitudes via the \(dp\to \{pp\}_s n\) reaction with low excitation energy \(E_{pp}<3\) MeV. Cross sections and analysing powers for pion production in both \(pp\) and \(pn\) collisions at 353 MeV were measured and subjected to a partial-wave decomposition retaining amplitudes up to pion \(d\)-waves.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam momentum at \(\eta\) threshold: \(p_d = 3141.686 \pm 0.021\) MeV/\(c\)
- \(\eta\)-meson mass: \(m_\eta = (547.873 \pm 0.005_{\text{stat}} \pm 0.026_{\text{syst}})\) MeV/\(c^2\)
- Depolarising resonance frequency measured with precision \(\approx 10^{-5}\)
- Full width at half maximum of depolarising resonance: 80–100 Hz, corresponding to \((\delta p/p)_{\text{rms}} \approx 2 \times 10^{-4}\)
- Schottky frequency measurement statistical uncertainty: below 0.2 Hz compared to typical 1.4 MHz
- Pion production measurements at \(T = 353\) MeV (with \(\pm 20\) MeV interval for quasi-free \(pn\) data)
- Systematic uncertainty on \(\eta\) mass: 26 keV/\(c^2\), dominated by threshold beam momentum determination
- Systematic uncertainty on \(\vec{p}p\to \{pp\}_s\pi^0\) analysing power: \(\approx 5\%\)
- Systematic uncertainty on \(pn\to \{pp\}_s\pi^-\) cross section: \(\approx 6\%\)

## Context

This work extends the COSY-EDDA collaboration's (2005) proton-proton elastic scattering data to include spin-dependent neutron-proton amplitudes at higher energies, where SAID partial-wave analysis predictions (SAID) become inadequate above about 1 GeV. The \(\eta\) mass determination improves upon earlier missing-mass measurements that differed by \(\sim 0.5\) keV/\(c^2\) and is compatible with Particle Data Group (2012) results from \(\eta\) decay. The pion production partial-wave analysis provides input for Chiral Perturbation Theory by establishing consistency of the short-ranged \(NN\to NN\pi\) vertex with other intermediate energy phenomena, and enables future measurements of spin correlations using a Siberian snake (planned for 2013) and studies of \(\Delta\) isobar production in \(\vec{p}\,\vec{d}\to \{pp\}_s\Delta^0\).
