# $J/ψ$ reaction mechanisms and suppression in the nuclear medium

> Recent studies of the interaction of vector mesons with nuclei make possible and opportune the study of the interaction of the $J/ψ$ with nuclei and the investigation of the origin of the $J/ψ$ suppression in its propagation thorough a nuclear medium. We observe that the transition of $J/ψN$ to $VN$ with $V$ being a light vector, $ρ, ω,ϕ$, together with the inelastic channels, $J/ψN \to \bar D Λ_c$ and $J/ψN \to \bar D Σ_c$ leads to a particular shape of the inelastic cross section. Analogously, we consider the mechanisms where the exchanged $D$ collides with a nucleon and gives $πΛ_c$ or $πΣ_c$. The cross section has a peak around $\sqrt s=4415$ MeV, where the $J/ψN$ couples to a resonance predicted recently. We study the transparency ratio for electron induced $J/ψ$ production in nuclei at about 10 GeV and find that 30 - 35% of the $J/ψ$ produced in heavy nuclei are absorbed inside the nucleus. This ratio is in line with depletions of $J/ψ$ though matter observed in other reactions.

# J/ψ reaction mechanisms and suppression in the nuclear medium

## Key claim
The observed 30-35% depletion of J/ψ production in heavy nuclei arises from inelastic J/ψN reactions, primarily J/ψN → D̄Λc via D-exchange and Kroll-Ruderman contact terms, with a smaller contribution from a dynamically generated hidden-charm resonance at √s = 4415 MeV; however, the resonance peak in the inelastic cross section is washed out by Fermi motion of nucleons in the nucleus, making the dip in transparency ratio unobservable in practice.

## Method
The authors use a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter approach with potentials from SU(4) extrapolation of local hidden gauge Lagrangians to compute J/ψN scattering amplitudes. They include transitions to D̄*Λc and D̄*Σc channels, light vector-baryon channels (ρN, ωN, φN, K*Λ, K*Σ) via box diagrams, and direct J/ψN → D̄Λc(Σc) reactions through D-exchange and Kroll-Ruderman contact terms. The inelastic cross section is obtained from the imaginary part of the J/ψN → J/ψN amplitude via the optical theorem, and the transparency ratio for J/ψ photoproduction in nuclei is calculated using a uniform-density sphere model with Fermi motion averaging.

## Key numbers
- Resonance pole position: √s = 4415 − 9.5i MeV for the (I,S) = (1/2,0) sector, with coupling g_a(J/ψN) = −0.85 + 0.02i
- Total width of the resonance: approximately 50 MeV (28 MeV from light vector-baryon channels plus 19.2 MeV from J/ψN decay)
- Thresholds: J/ψN → D̄Λc at 4160 MeV (p_cm = 405 MeV for J/ψ); J/ψN → D̄Σc at 4290 MeV
- D-exchange cross section for J/ψN → D̄Λc: σ ∼ 1.2 mb around √s = 4400 MeV
- Total inelastic J/ψN cross section at √s = 4600 MeV: σ_total(in) ≃ 6.8 mb
- Transparency ratio T_A for heavy nuclei: 0.60–0.70, corresponding to 30–35% J/ψ absorption
- Normalized transparency ratio T_A/T_{12C} for heavy nuclei: down to 0.75
- Photon shadowing correction factor for 208Pb relative to 12C at E_γ = 10 GeV: approximately 0.8

## Context
This work extends the vector-baryon coupled-channels approach developed for light vector mesons (Angelsvec, Souravvec) and the hidden-charm sector (Wu 2010, Wu 2010) to J/ψ absorption in cold nuclear matter. It builds on prior studies of J/ψ suppression in p-nucleus collisions (NA3, E772, NA38, E866, NA50, NA60) and provides a microscopic mechanism for the depletion observed in those experiments. The transparency ratio calculation follows the method used for η' photoproduction in nuclei (Mariana). The results enable quantitative predictions for J/ψ electroproduction experiments at the Jefferson Lab upgrade (∼10 GeV electron beam) and complement studies of J/ψ suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signature in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (Matsui 1986).
