# A relation among the effective nucleon mass, the incompressibility and the effective $σ$-meson mass in nuclear matter

> A relation among the effective nucleon mass $M^*$, the incompressibility $K$ and the effective $σ$-meson mass $m_{\rm s}^*$ in nuclear matter is studied by using the relativistic nuclear model. We found that there is a strong correlation between $M^*$ and $m_{\rm s}^*$, while there is only a weak correlation between $K$ and $m_{\rm s}^*$. At the normal density, $m_{\rm s}^*$ is smaller than the one at zero density, if $M^*$ is smaller than 0.8 times of the nucleon mass at zero density. It is also found that the off-shell effective mass $μ_{\rm s}^*$ is related directly to $K$ and $M^*$ at the normal density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a strong correlation between the effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) and the effective \(\sigma\)-meson mass \(m_{\rm s}^*\) in nuclear matter, while finding only a weak correlation between the incompressibility \(K\) and \(m_{\rm s}^*\). The key condition is that at normal density, \(m_{\rm s}^*\) is smaller than its vacuum value only if \(M^*\) is smaller than 0.8 times the free nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) based on the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model of quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), including one-loop vacuum fluctuation effects and an additional higher-order \(\sigma\)-meson self-interaction potential \(U^{\rm H}\) to adjust the incompressibility. They define two effective \(\sigma\)-meson masses: an off-shell mass \(\mu_{\rm s}^*\) (related to the second derivative of the energy density with respect to the \(\sigma\) field) and an on-shell mass \(m_{\rm s}^*\) (defined by the pole of the \(\sigma\) propagator). Numerical calculations are performed at and around normal nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(a_1 = 15.75\ \text{MeV}\) and free nucleon mass \(M = 939\ \text{MeV}\).

## Key numbers

- The ratio \(m_{\rm s}^*/m_{\rm s}\) is smaller than 1 for \(M^*_0 < 0.815M\) when \(K = 200\ \text{MeV}\), for \(M^*_0 < 0.802M\) when \(K = 300\ \text{MeV}\), and for \(M^*_0 < 0.793M\) when \(K = 400\ \text{MeV}\).
- The ratio \(\mu_{\rm s}^*/\mu_{\rm s}\) is smaller than 1 for \(M^*_0 < 0.560M\) when \(K = 200\ \text{MeV}\), for \(M^*_0 < 0.542M\) when \(K = 300\ \text{MeV}\), and for \(M^*_0 < 0.526M\) when \(K = 400\ \text{MeV}\).
- The condition \(C_{\rm v}^2 > 0\) from the saturation condition yields \(M^*_0/M < 0.944\).
- The physical \(\sigma\)-meson mass in vacuum is set to \(m_{\rm s} = 550\ \text{MeV}\).
- The ordinary RHA without higher-order terms gives \(K = 473\ \text{MeV}\), which is larger than the empirical range of 150–350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quantum hadrodynamics framework of Walecka (1974) and the relativistic Hartree approximation of Chin (1977) by systematically relating the effective \(\sigma\)-meson mass to the effective nucleon mass and incompressibility. The authors build on the approach of Boguta (1981) who introduced \(\sigma\)-meson self-interactions to adjust nuclear matter properties. The results enable phenomenological determination of the off-shell \(\sigma\)-meson mass \(\mu_{\rm s}^*\) directly from the empirically accessible quantities \(M^*_0\) and \(K\), and provide constraints for models of nuclear matter and the \(\sigma\)-meson in medium.
