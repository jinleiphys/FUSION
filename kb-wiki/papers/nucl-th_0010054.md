# Chiral unitary approach to the K^- deuteron scattering length

> Starting from a recent model where the Kbar N amplitudes are evaluated from the chiral Lagrangians using a coupled channel unitary method, we evaluate here the scattering length for K^- deuteron scattering. We find that the double scattering contribution is very large compared to the impulse approximation and that the charge exchange contribution of this rescattering is as large as the sequential K^- scattering on the two nucleons. Higher order rescattering corrections are evaluated using coupled channels with K^- and Kbar^0 within the integral form of the fixed centre approximation to the Faddeev equations. The higher order corrections involving intermediate pions and hyperons are found negligible.

## Key claim

The $K^-$ deuteron scattering length is evaluated using elementary $\bar{K} N$ amplitudes from a chiral unitary coupled-channel model, finding that the double scattering contribution is very large compared to the impulse approximation and that charge exchange rescattering is as large as sequential $K^-$ scattering on the two nucleons. Higher order rescattering corrections involving intermediate pions and hyperons are found negligible, but the multiple scattering series converges poorly, requiring summation via the fixed centre approximation to the Faddeev equations.

## Method

The authors start from elementary $\bar{K} N$ amplitudes obtained from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian using a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with a regularization cut-off of 630 MeV and a weak decay constant $f = 1.15 f_\pi$. They then evaluate the $K^-$ deuteron scattering length by solving Faddeev-like equations in the fixed centre approximation (FCA) for the nucleons, including coupled channels for $K^-$ and $\bar{K}^0$ but omitting intermediate $\pi \Sigma$ and other inelastic channels, which are shown to contribute negligibly. The calculation uses the physical particle basis rather than assuming isospin symmetry, and the final scattering length is obtained as an expectation value weighted by the deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- $K^-d$ scattering length from the full FCA calculation: $A_{Kd} = -1.61 + i\,1.91$ fm (using physical-basis elementary amplitudes at $W = 1432.6$ MeV)
- Impulse approximation result: $A_{Kd}^{IA} = (-0.26 + i\,1.87)$ fm
- Double scattering contributions: $A_{Kd}^{(2,a)} = (-1.70 + i\,0.07)$ fm (sequential $K^-$ scattering) and $A_{Kd}^{(2,b)} = (-0.77 + i\,0.95)$ fm (charge exchange)
- Triple scattering contribution: $A_{Kd}^{(3)} = (-1.12 + i\,0.07)$ fm
- Contribution from $\Sigma \pi$ intermediate channels: $A_{Kd}^{(\Sigma)} = (-0.015 + i\,0.000)$ fm, only about 1% of the triple scattering term
- Expectation value $\langle 1/r \rangle = 0.449$ fm$^{-1}$ and $\langle 1/r^2 \rangle = 0.289$ fm$^{-2}$ for the deuteron wave function
- Isospin violation effects can be as large as 30% in the imaginary part of the scattering length

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach to $\bar{K} N$ scattering developed by the same group (Oset, Ramos, 1998) to the $K^-$ deuteron system, improving on earlier FCA calculations by Chand and Dalitz (1962) and Barrett, Deloff (1999) that omitted charge exchange channels or used isospin symmetry. The results differ significantly from those of Torres, Deloff (1999) and Gal (1971), primarily due to the different elementary amplitudes used, with the chiral approach giving larger real and imaginary parts. The predicted scattering length is intended for comparison with upcoming measurements from the DEAR experiment at Frascati, which will provide constraints on chiral symmetry and isospin breaking in the $\bar{K} N$ interaction.
