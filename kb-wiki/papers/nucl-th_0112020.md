# p-$^3$He Effective Potentials based on the Pauli Corrected Resonating Group Method

> Effective interactions that fit the low energy p-$^3$He experimental data have been constructed. They are based on the Resonating Group Method and a modified Orthogonality Condition Model in which Pauli and partly Pauli forbidden states are removed from the spectrum. Partial waves up to L=3 have been considered. The LS force acting between the proton and $^3$He has been included phenomenologically, while the Coulomb interaction has been incorporated using a renormalization technique for a screened Coulomb interaction. The potentials are also given in a separable momentum space form, obtained using the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) method. In all cases the potentials generate phase shifts that fit well the low energy experimental data.

## Key claim
The authors construct effective p-³He potentials that fit low-energy experimental phase shifts by combining the Resonating Group Method (RGM) with a modified Orthogonality Condition Model (MOCM) and a phenomenological LS force, but the potentials are real and omit tensor forces because the experimental mixing parameters were found to be negligibly small.

## Method
The authors start from the RGM formalism and remove Pauli forbidden states using the Orthogonality Condition Model, then introduce a phenomenological LS force via a polynomial in L·S to lift degeneracies in spin-triplet channels. The Coulomb interaction is incorporated using a screened Yukawa potential with a renormalization technique to recover the pure Coulomb phase shift in the long-range limit. Finally, the potentials are converted into separable momentum-space form using the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) method at fixed energies of 1, 10, and 15 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the RGM calculations of Reichstein et al. (1970s) and the Generator Coordinate Method calculations of Furutani et al. by adding phenomenological LS forces to reproduce experimental phase shifts from Yoshino et al. (1990s) and earlier analyses. The resulting separable potentials are intended for use in three-body Faddeev calculations of the ³He(d,p)⁴He reaction, which is relevant for primordial nucleosynthesis studies and aneutronic fusion.
