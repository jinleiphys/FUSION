# Relevance of baseline hard proton-proton spectra for high-energy nucleus-nucleus physics

> We discuss three different cases of hard inclusive spectra in proton-proton collisions: high $p_T$ single hadron production at $\sqrt{s}\approx$ 20 GeV and at $\sqrt{s}$ = 62.4 GeV, and direct photon production at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV; with regard to their relevance for the search of Quark Gluon Plasma signals in A+A collisions at SPS and RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The apparent strong enhancement (Cronin effect) of high-$p_T$ pion production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN-SPS energies is not actually supported by the data but arises from the use of inexact proton-proton baseline references that undershoot the true cross-sections by factors of 2–3.

## Method

The author compiled all existing single inclusive pion spectra measured at high $p_T$ ($p_T \gtrsim$ 2 GeV/$c$) in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 16.9–19.4 GeV and at $\sqrt{s}$ = 62–63 GeV, corrected for direct-photon and eta contaminations and for center-of-mass energy differences where required. For the SPS energy regime, the author compared the WA98 and Wang&Wang parametrizations to the full set of experimental data and proposed an alternative empirical parametrization from Blattnig et al. (2001) derived from a global analysis of $\pi^0$ spectra at $\sqrt{s}\approx$ 7–63 GeV. For the RHIC intermediate energy, the author collected all ISR $\pi^0$, $\pi^\pm$, and $h^\pm$ measurements at $\sqrt{s}$ = 62–63 GeV, applied corrections, and fitted them to a common functional form.

## Key numbers

* At CERN-SPS energies ($\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}\approx$ 20 GeV), the WA98 and Wang&Wang p+p parametrizations undershoot the experimental p+p pion cross-sections by factors of 2–3, comparable to the reported Cronin enhancements.
* Using the Blattnig et al. (2001) parametrization, the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for central Pb+Pb and Pb+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ = 17.3 GeV is consistent with $R_{AA}\approx$ 1 within uncertainties, not enhanced.
* In peripheral Pb+Pb reactions at SPS, high $p_T$ pion production is enhanced by up to a factor of $\sim$2 compared to $N_{coll}$ scaling.
* For $\sqrt{s}$ = 62.4 GeV, the PHENIX charged hadron parametrization uses parameters $A$ = 196.4 [mb GeV$^{-2}c^{3}$], $a$ = 0.0226 [GeV$^{-1}c$], $p_0$ = 2.301 [GeV/$c$], and $n$ = 14.86, with an assigned overall uncertainty of $\pm$25%.
* The direct-$\gamma$/$\pi^0$ ratio at $\sqrt{s}\approx$ 62.4 GeV reaches $\sim$1/3 at $p_T\sim$10 GeV/$c$ and equals unity at $\sim$14 GeV/$c$.
* Theoretical predictions for central Pb+Pb at SPS including Cronin broadening, shadowing, and partonic energy loss with gluon densities $dN^g/dy=$ 400–600 reproduce the observed $R_{AA}$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of high-$p_T$ hadron suppression in heavy-ion collisions by revisiting the baseline p+p references used at SPS and RHIC energies. It corrects earlier conclusions by the WA98 collaboration (1999) and Wang&Wang (2002) that claimed strong Cronin enhancement at SPS, showing instead that central A+A collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}\approx$ 20 GeV are consistent with binary collision scaling. The revised SPS results, combined with theoretical work by Vitev and Gyulassy (2002), suggest moderate jet quenching may already be present at SPS energies. The compilation of ISR $\pi^0$ data at $\sqrt{s}$ = 62–63 GeV provides a baseline for interpreting Au+Au results at RHIC intermediate energy ($\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ = 62.4 GeV), enabling more quantitative constraints on the excitation function of parton energy loss. A direct p+p measurement at $\sqrt{s}$ = 62.4 GeV at RHIC is identified as necessary to reduce uncertainties.
