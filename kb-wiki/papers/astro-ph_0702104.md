# Baryon number conservation and enforced electric charge neutrality for bulk viscosity in quark matter

> General constraints on fluid velocity divergences for particles in quark matter are derived from baryon number conservation and enforced electric charge neutrality. A new oscillation pattern in three-flavor normal quark matter satisfying these conditions is found and its bulk viscosity is calculated. The result may have astrophysical implication for maximum rotation frequencies of compact stars.

# Baryon number conservation and enforced electric charge neutrality for bulk viscosity in quark matter

## Key claim
The paper derives general constraints from baryon number conservation and enforced electric charge neutrality on fluid velocity divergences in quark matter, and finds a new solution where strange quarks respond more reluctantly to density oscillations than light quarks, leading to bulk viscosities that can be up to one order of magnitude larger than those from the standard homogeneous-fluid assumption.

## Method
The authors start from continuity equations for quark flavors, baryon number, and electrons, imposing baryon number conservation and charge neutrality as constraints on number densities and velocity divergences. They solve for a new set of velocity divergence relations under the assumption that strange quarks have zero velocity divergence while up and down quarks share the same divergence, then compute the bulk viscosity from the pressure deviation using linear response theory for three cases: the d↔s transition alone, the Urca II process alone, and all three coupled processes (d↔s, Urca I, Urca II) together.

## Key numbers
- The oscillation frequency ω is taken in the range 10³–10⁴ s⁻¹.
- For the d↔s transition with parameters m_s = 100 MeV, μ_s = μ_d = 500 MeV, μ_u = 495 MeV, μ_e = 5 MeV, the bulk viscosity ζ₁ increases with decreasing frequency until saturating below a critical frequency ω₁c ∼ λ₁|∂μ₁/∂n_s|.
- For Urca II with the same parameters, the critical frequency ω₃c ∼ λ₃|∂μ₃/∂n_s| is much smaller than ω₁c because λ₃ ≪ λ₁, and the saturation value of ζ₃ is larger than that of ζ₁.
- For the three coupled processes at T = 0.1 MeV with m_s = 100 MeV, the new solution (solid curve) gives a bulk viscosity about one order of magnitude larger than the standard homogeneous-fluid solution (dashed curve) with the same parameters.
- With m_s = 300 MeV, μ_s = μ_d = 500 MeV, μ_u = 456 MeV, μ_e = 44 MeV, the standard solution (dash-dotted curve) is comparable to the new solution at high frequencies but differs substantially at low frequencies.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of bulk viscosity in normal quark matter by Wang (1985), Sawyer (1989), Madsen (1992), Zheng (2002), Sa'd (2006), and Alford (2006), which all assumed a single fluid velocity field for all particle species. The new solution explicitly accounts for the different dynamical response of massive strange quarks versus light quarks, and the authors note that similar constraints on fluid velocity divergences can be obtained for nuclear matter. The result may have astrophysical implications for larger maximum rotation frequencies of compact stars, particularly regarding r-mode instability damping.
