# Hadronic Observables: Theoretical Highlights

> I present highlights from the parallel sessions on the theory of hadronic observables in e^+ e^-, hadronic and nuclear reactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observation of a universal chemical freeze-out temperature of about 170 MeV in both elementary (e+e-, pp) and heavy-ion collisions suggests that hadronization proceeds via the same statistical phase-space occupation mechanism in all these systems, but the factor-of-two larger strangeness fraction in AA collisions (λs ≈ 0.4–0.45) compared to pp (λs ≈ 0.2–0.25) reflects a genuine difference in the prehadronic state, possibly indicating a longer-lived, thermally and chemically equilibrated quark-gluon plasma before hadronization.

## Method

The author synthesizes results from parallel theory sessions at the Quark Matter '97 conference, covering thermal model fits to hadron yields and m⊥-spectra, hydrodynamic analyses of collective flow, HBT interferometry for source imaging, and transport model simulations (URQMD, HIJING, VNI). The analysis compares chemical freeze-out parameters (Tchem, μB, λs) extracted from hadron abundances with thermal freeze-out parameters (Tf, ⟨v⊥⟩) from momentum spectra and two-particle correlations across SIS, AGS, and SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature Tchem ≈ 170 MeV, numerically equal to the Hagedorn temperature TH, observed universally in e+e-, pp, and AA collisions.
- Strangeness fraction λs ≈ 0.2–0.25 in e+e- and pp collisions, rising to λs ≈ 0.4–0.45 in AA collisions at SPS.
- Strangeness saturation coefficient γs ≈ 0.7 in AA collisions, corresponding to λs = 0.45.
- Thermal freeze-out temperature Tf ≈ 120–130 MeV in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, with average transverse flow velocity ⟨v⊥⟩ ≈ 0.5c.
- Thermal freeze-out temperature Tf ≈ 140–150 MeV in S+S collisions at SPS.
- Pion source at freeze-out in central Pb+Pb is more than twice as large as the colliding Pb nuclei.
- Longitudinal expansion time before pion decoupling ≈ 9 fm/c, with emission duration ≈ 2–3 fm/c.
- Estimated initial energy density ε0 ≈ 2 GeV/fm³ ≥ 2 εcrit for central Pb+Pb at SPS.
- DCC destruction time scale by hadronic collisions ≈ 1–2 fm/c in URQMD simulations.
- Up to factor 100 enhancement in dielectron spectrum at low pT from DCC mean-field simulations.

## Context

This conference summary extends the statistical hadronization approach of Becattini (1996) and Braun-Munzinger & Heppe (1997) from elementary to nuclear collisions, while contradicting the initial-state scattering model of Wong (1996) which claimed nuclear broadening of p⊥-spectra could be explained without collective flow. The HBT analyses of Wiedemann, Pratt, and Schmidt-Sørensen enable direct reconstruction of freeze-out geometry and dynamics, allowing backward extrapolation to initial energy densities. The work enables quantitative tests of transport models (HIJING, VENUS, URQMD) against multi-observable constraints, and opens the field of event-by-event fluctuation analysis for QGP signatures.
