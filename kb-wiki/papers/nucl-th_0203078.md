# Virtual-pion and two-photon production in pp scattering

> Two-photon production in pp scattering is proposed as a means of studying virtual-pion emission. Such a process is complementary to real-pion emission in pp scattering. The virtual-pion signal is embedded in a background of double-photon bremsstrahlung. We have developed a model to describe this background process and show that in certain parts of phase space the virtual-pion signal gives significant contribution. In addition, through interference with the two-photon bremsstrahlung background, one can determine the relative phase of the virtual-pion process.

## Key claim

Two-photon production in pp scattering can serve as a probe of virtual-pion emission, but the virtual-pion signal is embedded in a background of double-photon bremsstrahlung; the authors show that in certain parts of phase space the virtual-pion contribution is significant, and that interference with the bremsstrahlung background can determine the relative phase of the virtual-pion process.

## Method

The authors developed a Soft-Photon Model (SPM) for two-photon bremsstrahlung, based on a power-series expansion of the T-matrix around an appropriately chosen on-shell kinematic point, which avoids introducing explicit contact terms and satisfies gauge invariance. For the virtual-pion process, they used a covariant model with a mixed pseudo-scalar/pseudo-vector pion-nucleon vertex, where the mixing parameter chi is adjusted to reproduce real-pion production data from Meyer (1992). The total amplitude for pp to pp gamma gamma is the sum of the two-photon bremsstrahlung amplitude and the virtual-pion amplitude, and cross sections are calculated for an incoming proton energy of 280 MeV in the lab system, just below the real-pion production threshold.

## Key numbers

* The beam energy used for the two-photon calculations is 280 MeV in the lab system, which is just below the pion-production threshold (given as 2m_pi + m_pi^2 / 2 m_p).
* The pi0 to gamma gamma decay constant used is g_pi_gamma_gamma approximately 0.0375.
* For the real-pion production model, the mixing parameter chi = 1.05 reproduces the experimentally measured cross section; for chi = 0 (pure pseudo-vector coupling), the cross section is about a factor 10 larger than experiment.
* The off-energy-shell CM momentum for the dominant diagrams in pion production is approximately (m_pi m_p)^(1/2) approximately 370 MeV.
* The single-photon bremsstrahlung data used for comparison were taken at KVI at 190 MeV incident energy, with photon energies up to about 80 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of near-threshold neutral-pion production in pp scattering, which have been investigated experimentally at IUCF by Meyer (1992) and others, and theoretically by many groups including Lee (1993), Horowitz (1994), and Engel (1996). The authors propose virtual-pion production via two-photon decay as a complementary probe that can access an extended kinematical regime below the real-pion threshold, where inelasticities from pion-nucleon scattering are absent. The approach enables future investigation of off-shell form factors and the relative sign of matrix elements, which is relevant to the debate about constructive versus destructive interference of higher-order diagrams in field-theoretical treatments.
