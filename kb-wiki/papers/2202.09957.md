# Scale symmetry and composition of compact star matter

> The dense compact star matter is studied by using the skyrmion crystal approach. The chiral effective theory used includes the lightest scalar meson, the lowest-lying vector mesons as well as pions. Consistency with the vector manifestation and the dilaton limit fixed point at high density constrains the anomalous dimension of the gluon field $1.0 \lesssim |γ_{G^2}| \lesssim 2.0$ and leads to the significance of the scale symmetry breaking in the intrinsic parity-odd part of the effective theory. The speed of sound $v_s^2 \simeq 1/3$ and the polytropic index $γ\simeq 1$ -- both satisfy the conformal limits -- after the dilaton limit fixed point at high density but the matter is still in the hadronic phase. This means that neither the conformal speed of sound nor the smallness of the polytropic index can be used as a criterion of the onset of quark matter. These conclusions are significant for constructing the equation of state of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in dense compact star matter described by a skyrmion crystal approach, the speed of sound approaches \(v_s^2 \simeq 1/3\) and the polytropic index approaches \(\gamma \simeq 1\) after the dilaton limit fixed point, but the matter remains in the hadronic phase rather than transitioning to quark matter. This means that neither the conformal speed of sound nor the smallness of the polytropic index can be used as a sufficient criterion for the onset of deconfined quark matter, contrary to claims in prior literature such as Annala (2019).

## Method

The authors use a skyrmion crystal approach on a face-centered cubic (FCC) lattice, based on a hidden scale and hidden local symmetric Lagrangian (\(s\)HLS) that includes pions, the lightest scalar meson (dilaton \(\sigma\)), and the lowest-lying vector mesons (\(\rho\) and \(\omega\)). The dilaton potential includes a nonperturbative anomalous dimension of the gluon field \(\beta' = |\gamma_{G^2}|\), and the intrinsic parity-odd (homogeneous Wess-Zumino) term is coupled to the dilaton with a free parameter \(c_h\). The parameters \(\beta'\) and \(c_h\) are constrained by requiring consistency with the vector manifestation fixed point and the dilaton limit fixed point at high density, with the onset density \(n_{\rm DLFP}\) between \(2\) and \(10\) times the saturation density \(n_0\).

## Key numbers

* The anomalous dimension of the gluon field is constrained to \(1.0 \lesssim |\gamma_{G^2}| \lesssim 2.0\) by requiring the dilaton limit fixed point onset density \(n_{\rm DLFP}\) to lie in the range \(2 \lesssim n_{\rm DLFP}/n_0 \lesssim 10\).
* The free parameter \(c_h\) in the scale symmetry breaking of the intrinsic parity-odd term is constrained to \(0 \lesssim c_h \lesssim 0.2\).
* After the dilaton limit fixed point, the speed of sound approaches \(v_s^2 \to 1/3\) and the polytropic index approaches \(\gamma \to 1\).
* The dilaton decay constant \(f_\sigma\) is taken as 240 MeV and the dilaton mass \(m_\sigma\) as 640 MeV.
* The equation of state from the crystal approach is compared to astrophysical constraints for densities \(5.7 n_0 \lesssim n \lesssim 10 n_0\), and at \(n \gtrsim 7.5 n_0\) the EoS falls into the constrained region from Annala (2017) with an upper bound of neutron star mass \(\gtrsim 2.0 M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach to dense nuclear matter by including scale symmetry breaking effects via the dilaton, building on prior work by Park (2008) and Ma (2016) which used a logarithmic dilaton potential. The results contradict the common interpretation in the literature, such as that of Annala (2019), that a conformal speed of sound and polytropic index near unity signal the onset of deconfined quark matter. Instead, the paper supports the concept of pseudoconformal nuclear matter introduced by Paeng (2017) and Ma (2018, 2019), where the trace of the energy-momentum tensor is a nonzero constant after the dilaton limit fixed point, indicating the matter remains hadronic. This enables a new perspective for constructing the equation of state of nuclear matter at densities relevant to the cores of massive neutron stars.
