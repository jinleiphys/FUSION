# Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Nucleon in an Improved Quark Model

> Nucleon electromagnetic form factors are studied in the cloudy bag model (CBM) with center-of-mass and recoil corrections. This is the first presentation of a full set of nucleon form factors using the CBM. The center of mass motion is eliminated via several different momentum projection techniques and the results are compared. It is found that the shapes of these form factors are significantly improved with respect to the experimental data if the Lorentz contraction of the internal structure of the baryon is also appropriately taken into account.

## Key claim

The cloudy bag model (CBM), when combined with both Galilean momentum projection (Peierls-Yoccoz or Peierls-Thouless) and a semiclassical Lorentz contraction prescription, yields nucleon electromagnetic form factors in "rather good agreement" with experimental data up to momentum transfers of about 1 GeV², despite the model's simplicity and the absence of explicit vector meson contributions.

## Method

The authors constructed momentum eigenstates from static CBM bag states using two projection techniques: the Peierls-Yoccoz (PY) method, which superposes bag states at different locations, and the Peierls-Thouless (PT) method, which additionally restores translational invariance. They then applied a Lorentz contraction prescription by Licht and Pagnamenta, rescaling quark coordinates along the direction of motion in the Breit frame. Pionic corrections from the perturbative pion cloud were included via two classes of diagrams: direct γππ coupling and γqq coupling inside a pion loop, evaluated in the heavy baryon approximation.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge form factor at Q² = 0.5 GeV²: relativistic normalization raises the form factor roughly 5% compared to nonrelativistic normalization; at Q² = 1.0 GeV² the increase is about 10%.
- Bare charge form factor with PY projection at Q² ≈ 0.5 GeV² increases nearly 100% relative to the static bag result.
- Neutron charge r.m.s. radius: ⟨r²⟩_En = -0.14 fm² (with bag radius R = 1 fm), compared to the experimental value of -0.12 fm².
- Nucleon magnetic moments: center-of-mass correction reduces static values by 5–10%; bag radius dependence is significantly reduced by the pion cloud over the range R = 0.8–1.0 fm.
- Renormalized πNN coupling constant used: f^NN ≈ 3.03, corresponding to f²_πNN ≈ 0.081.
- Bare baryon probability Z₂^N ≈ 0.73 for R = 1 fm; using renormalized coupling constants increases the Fig. 1(b) contribution by roughly 30%.

## Context

This work extends earlier CBM calculations by Theberge and Thomas (1983) and MIT bag model studies, being the first full presentation of all nucleon electromagnetic form factors using the CBM. It compares two momentum projection methods (Peierls-Yoccoz and Peierls-Thouless) and incorporates Lorentz contraction following Licht and Pagnamenta (1970), which was previously applied in Skyrme model calculations by Ji (1991) but with a different prefactor. The results enable future inclusion of vector meson contributions and ππ interactions, and serve as a foundation for electromagnetic interaction studies in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model.
