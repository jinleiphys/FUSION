# Soft Hadron Ratios at the LHC

> High precision soft hadron abundance data produced in relativistic nuclear collisions at LHC at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\le 5500$ GeV will become available beginning in 2007/8. We explore, within the statistical hadronization model, how these results can help us understand the properties of the deconfined quark--gluon phase at its breakup. We make assumptions about the physical properties of the fireball and obtain particle production predictions. Then, we develop a strategy to measure parameters of interest, such as strangeness occupancy $γ_s$, chemical potentials $μ_{\rm B}$ and $μ_{\rm S}$.

## Key claim

The authors predict soft hadron production ratios at LHC Pb–Pb collisions within the statistical hadronization model, but emphasize that these predictions depend on specific assumptions about fireball properties (such as baryon stopping and entropy content) and on the unknown value of the strangeness phase-space occupancy parameter γ_s, which may range from 0.5 to 10.

## Method

The authors use the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) implemented in the SHARE software package, imposing seven constraints: strangeness conservation, electric charge-to-baryon ratio Q/b = 0.39 ± 0.01, a fixed energy-to-entropy ratio E/(TS) = 0.78 (non-equilibrium) or 0.845 (equilibrium), and an assumed thermal energy per net baryon at midrapidity of 412 ± 20 GeV derived from extrapolating SPS and RHIC trends. They solve for the model parameters (temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, strange chemical potential μ_S, light-quark occupancy γ_q, and strangeness occupancy γ_s) as functions of γ_s, then compute particle yield ratios.

## Key numbers

- Top LHC Pb–Pb collision energy: √s_NN = 5500 GeV, a 27.5-fold increase over top RHIC energy.
- Baryochemical potential at RHIC: μ_B ≃ 25 MeV; predicted at LHC: μ_B ≃ 2.6 MeV (for γ_s = 5, non-equilibrium) and μ_B = 2.6 MeV (equilibrium at T = 156 MeV).
- Hadronization temperature: T = 140 MeV near γ_s = 2.4 (γ_q = 1.6), dropping to T = 110 MeV at high γ_s; at γ_s = 5, T = 125 MeV (non-equilibrium).
- Predicted η/π^0 ratio range: 0.07 < η/π^0 < 0.12.
- Predicted (K⁺ + K⁻)/h ratio range: 0.12 < (K⁺ + K⁻)/(h⁺ + h⁻) < 0.23.
- Predicted 2φ/h ratio range: 0.015 < 2φ/(h⁺ + h⁻) < 0.06.
- Predicted baryon ratios (after weak decays): (p + p̅)/h between 0.07 and 0.04; (Λ + Λ̅)/h between 0.02 and 0.04; (Ξ⁻ + Ξ̅⁺)/h between 0.004 and 0.015; (Ω⁻ + Ω̅⁺)/h between 0.0006 and 0.004.
- Maximum allowed γ_s from η(548) Bose singularity: γ_s < 10.4 at T ≃ 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization analysis previously applied to SPS and RHIC data (Letessier and Rafelski, 2005) to predict soft hadron ratios at LHC energies, where the baryochemical potential is expected to be much smaller than at RHIC (μ_B ≃ 25 MeV) and approach the early-Universe value (μ_B^U ≃ 1 eV, Fromerth and Rafelski, 2002). The predictions enable experimental strategies to measure the strangeness occupancy γ_s and the baryochemical potential μ_B from particle ratios such as φ/h, η/π^0, and baryon-to-meson ratios, and to distinguish between chemical equilibrium and non-equilibrium hadronization scenarios. The authors note that their predicted chemical parameters differ from those of Andronic et al. (2003), who proposed μ_B = 1 MeV and μ_S = 0.3 MeV.
