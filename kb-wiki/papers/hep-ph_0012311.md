# Higgs description of two-flavor QCD vacuum

> A Higgs description for the QCD vacuum in the limit of two quark flavors is proposed. Complete ``spontaneous breaking'' of color symmetry is realized by condensation of an adjoint quark-antiquark and a quark-quark pair. The vacuum is invariant under isospin symmetry applying color and flavor transformations simultaneously. All elementary excitations acquire integral charges. The dressed microscopic quark and gluon degrees of freedom can be identified with the macroscopic baryon and vector meson degrees of freedom. In addition to the nucleons we find soliton--type fermions with integer isospin. Their presence provides an important test accessible to lattice QCD simulations.

# Higgs description of two-flavor QCD vacuum

## Key claim

The authors propose that the QCD vacuum in the two-flavor limit can be described by a Higgs mechanism in which complete spontaneous breaking of color symmetry occurs through simultaneous condensation of an adjoint quark-antiquark pair and a color-antitriplet diquark pair. This description is conditional on the existence of both condensates together, as neither condensate alone breaks color completely.

## Method

The authors construct a Higgs description of the QCD vacuum by introducing a color-octet quark-antiquark condensate (χ) and a color-antitriplet diquark condensate (Δ), along with a color-singlet quark-antiquark condensate (φ). They analyze the symmetry breaking pattern, showing that the condensates leave a global vector-like SU(2)_{color+V} subgroup invariant, which corresponds to physical isospin. The quantum numbers of quarks and gluons are then recomputed in the presence of these condensates, and the resulting excitations are identified with hadronic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon masses are given by M_p^2 = M_n^2 = (m_q + φ)^2, independent of χ and Δ
- The S-state fermion masses are M_S^2 = (m_q + φ − χ)^2 + Δ^2
- The L^0 fermion mass is M_L^2 = (m_q + φ + 3χ)^2 + Δ^2
- The ρ-meson mass is M_ρ^2 = c_χ g^2 χ^2
- The ω-meson mass is M_ω^2 = c_Δ g^2 Δ^2
- The κ-meson masses are M_κ = (3/8) c_χ g^2 χ^2 + (3/4) c_Δ g^2 Δ^2
- The pion mass squared is m_π^2 ∼ m_q (proportional to the current quark mass)

## Context

This work extends the concept of color-flavor locking, previously applied to high-density QCD by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and to three-flavor QCD vacuum by Wetterich (1999), to the two-flavor case. The authors propose that the Higgs description provides a simple relation between microscopic quark-gluon degrees of freedom and macroscopic hadron degrees of freedom, overcoming qualitative problems of analytic approaches to low-energy QCD. The prediction of soliton-type fermions with integer isospin and bosons with half-integer isospin provides a test accessible to lattice QCD simulations, and the framework opens the possibility that nuclear matter in the non-superfluid phase may be continuously connected to the vacuum.
