# Hadrons in the Nuclear Medium

> In this talk I first discuss predictions based on QCD sum rules and on hadronic models for the properties of vector mesons in the nuclear medium. I then describe possible experimental signatures and show detailed predictions for dilepton invariant mass spectra with a special emphasis on nuclear reactions involving elementary incoming beams and nuclear targets. I will in particular illustrate that the sensitivity of pion and photon induced reactions to in-medium effects is nearly as large as that of heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

QCD sum rule predictions of a lowered rho meson mass in nuclear matter are too naive because they assumed a sharp resonance state; recent analyses show that QCD sum rules are compatible with a wide range of masses and widths, and that a significant broadening of the meson at nearly constant pole position is also compatible with the sum rules.

## Method

The paper reviews two approaches to determining in-medium properties of vector mesons: QCD sum rules, which evaluate the current-current correlator using the Operator Product Expansion and a hadronic spectral function; and hadronic models, which calculate the vector meson selfenergy by dressing its propagators with hadronic loops, particularly including coupling to nucleon resonances such as the N(1520) D13 resonance. For experimental observables, the author presents transport-theoretical calculations using the Coupled Channel BUU method for pion-induced and photon-induced reactions on nuclei, comparing the resulting dilepton invariant mass spectra to those from heavy-ion reactions.

## Key numbers

- The N(1520) resonance has a rho decay branch of about 20 percent
- In ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, baryon densities of up to 10 times normal nuclear density are predicted
- In relativistic heavy-ion collisions (about 1 to 4 A GeV), nuclear densities of about 2 to 3 times normal nuclear density can be reached
- For 200 A GeV S + Au and S + W reactions, agreement with measured dilepton mass spectra in the range between about 300 and 700 MeV requires rho-meson strength shifted downwards
- In pion-induced reactions at 1.3 GeV on Pb, in-medium changes in the dilepton mass spectrum are of the order of a factor 2 in the mass range between 300 MeV and 700 MeV
- In photon-induced reactions at 2.2 GeV on Pb-208, the sensitivity in the region between about 300 and 700 MeV amounts to a factor of about 3
- The in-medium broadening of the rho leads to a mean free path of about 2 fm in nuclear matter

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule predictions of Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and the scaling arguments of Brown and Rho, which predicted dropping vector meson masses with density. It contradicts the simplified interpretation of those predictions by showing that hadronic models (Friman and Pirner, Peters et al.) yield very broad spectral functions rather than simple mass shifts, and that recent QCD sum rule analyses (Leupold et al.) only give wide constraints on mass and width. The paper enables experimental searches for in-medium effects using elementary beams (pions and photons) rather than only heavy-ion collisions, and suggests that the observed disappearance of the second resonance region in nuclear photoabsorption (Bianchi et al.) may be explained by the broadening of the N(1520) resonance due to rho meson in-medium effects.
