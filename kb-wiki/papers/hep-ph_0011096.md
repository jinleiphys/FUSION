# Pion and Kaon Vector Form Factors

> We develop a unitarity approach to consider the final state interaction corrections to the tree level graphs calculated from Chiral Perturbation Theory ($χPT$) allowing the inclusion of explicit resonance fields. The method is discussed considering the coupled channel pion and kaon vector form factors. These form factors are then matched with the one loop $χPT$ results. A very good description of experimental data is accomplished for the vector form factors and for the $ππ$ P-wave phase shifts up to $\sqrt{s}\lesssim 1.2$ GeV, beyond which multiparticle states play a non negligible role. In particular the low and resonance energy regions are discussed in detail and for the former a comparison with one and two loop $χPT$ is made showing a remarkable coincidence with the two loop $χPT$ results.

## Key claim

The authors develop a unitarity-based approach that incorporates final-state interaction corrections to tree-level Chiral Perturbation Theory (χPT) amplitudes with explicit resonance fields, achieving a good description of experimental data for pion and kaon vector form factors up to √s ≲ 1.2 GeV, beyond which multiparticle states become non-negligible.

## Method

The method starts from tree-level form factors and scattering amplitudes calculated from the lowest-order χPT Lagrangian plus chiral resonance Lagrangians (including vector mesons ρ, ω, φ). These tree-level amplitudes are then unitarized using a coupled-channel formalism that solves the Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem, producing a T-matrix via the expression T(s) = [I + K(s)·g(s)]⁻¹·K(s), where K(s) contains the tree-level amplitudes and g(s) is a diagonal loop function. The final form factors are obtained by matching the unitarized expressions to the one-loop χPT results, fixing the subtraction constants d^I_i and the polynomial corrections α_I.

## Key numbers

- The bare ρ mass is fixed to M_ρ = 829.8 MeV to reproduce the physical ρ mass of 770 MeV in the ππ P-wave amplitude.
- The bare φ mass is fixed to M_φ = 1026.581 MeV to reproduce the physical φ mass of 1019.413 MeV.
- The vector coupling constants are taken from experiment: F_V = 154 MeV (from Γ(ρ⁰ → e⁺e⁻)) and G_V = 53 MeV (from pion electromagnetic radii).
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is taken as f = 87.4 MeV.
- The approach describes the ππ P-wave phase shifts up to √s ≤ 1.5 GeV and the vector form factors up to √s ≤ 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier nonperturbative approaches that match with one-loop χPT, such as the Omnès representation used by Törnqvist (1999) and the Padé approximant method by Hannah (1999), by incorporating coupled channels (ππ and K̄K) and explicit vector resonance fields. The method enables a unified description of both the low-energy region (where it shows remarkable coincidence with two-loop χPT results) and the resonance region, and it allows the estimation of isospin-breaking effects from ω-ρ mixing as a perturbation.
