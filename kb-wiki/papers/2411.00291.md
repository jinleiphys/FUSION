# The linearized Israel-Stewart equations with a physical vacuum boundary

> In this article, we consider the Israel-Stewart equations of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics with bulk viscosity. We investigate the evolution of the equations linearized about solutions that satisfy the physical vacuum boundary condition and establish local well-posedness of the corresponding Cauchy problem.

## Key claim
The paper establishes local well-posedness for the linearized Israel-Stewart equations with bulk viscosity under the physical vacuum boundary condition, but only for the specific case where the bulk viscosity decays as $\eta = \frac{1}{\kappa}+2$ near the vacuum boundary, with the claim that other decay rates either lead to singular coefficients or can be handled similarly.

## Method
The authors linearize the Israel-Stewart equations (with bulk viscosity only) about a background solution satisfying the physical vacuum boundary condition, where both density and bulk viscosity vanish on the free boundary. They introduce new variables $r = \varrho^\kappa$ and $\pi = \Pi^{\frac{\kappa}{2\kappa+1}}$ to achieve linear decay rates near the boundary, then derive weighted Sobolev spaces $H^{j,\sigma}$ with weights determined by $r$ to handle the degeneracy. The basic energy estimate is obtained using a material derivative formulation and a differential formula for moving domains, with the energy functional serving as an equivalent norm in the base space $\mathcal{H}$.

## Key numbers
- The physical vacuum boundary condition requires $\sigma = \frac{1}{\kappa}$ for density decay and $\eta \geq \sigma + 1$ for bulk viscosity decay, where $\varrho \simeq d^\sigma$ and $\Pi \simeq d^\eta$ with $d$ the distance to the boundary.
- The specific case solved is $\eta = \frac{1}{\kappa}+2$, with technical assumptions $\tau_{\Pi}=\frac{1}{2+\frac{1}{\kappa}}$ and $\zeta(\varrho) \simeq \varrho^{2\kappa+1}$.
- The base space for solutions is $\mathcal{H} = L^2(r^{\frac{1-\kappa}{\kappa}}) \times L^2(r^{\frac{1}{\kappa}}) \times L^2(r^{\frac{1}{\kappa}})$.
- High-order spaces are defined as $\mathcal{H}^{2l} = H^{2l,\frac{1}{2\kappa}+l-\frac{1}{2}} \times H^{2l,\frac{1}{2\kappa}+l} \times H^{2l,\frac{1}{2\kappa}+l}$.

## Context
This work extends the study of free-boundary relativistic fluids from ideal fluids (described by the relativistic Euler equations) to viscous fluids, specifically the Israel-Stewart (DNMR) model with bulk viscosity. Prior work on free-boundary relativistic ideal fluids includes the seminal TOV equations from Tolman (1939), Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939), and more recent mathematical treatments by Jang and Masmoudi (2014), Disconzi (2022), and Ifrim, Tataru, and collaborators (2023). The paper initiates the mathematical study of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics in a free-boundary setting, with the linearized results serving as the foundation for a forthcoming nonlinear solution by the same author.
