# Variational Monte Carlo method for shell-model calculations in odd-mass nuclei and restoration of symmetry

> We investigate two kinds of extensions for the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method with the Pfaffian in the nuclear shell-model calculations. One is the extension to odd-mass nuclei, for which we find a new Pfaffian expression of the VMC matrix elements. We can, thereby, give a unified VMC framework both for even and odd mass nuclei. The other is the extension of the variation after angular-momentum projection. We successfully implement the full angular-momentum projected trial state into the VMC method, which can provide us with the precise yrast energies. We also find a unique characteristic, namely that this angular-momentum projection in the VMC can be even "approximately" performed. This characteristic is useful not only for efficient computation but also for precise estimation of the yrast energies through the energy-variance extrapolation.

## Key claim

The authors extend the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method with Pfaffian to handle odd-mass nuclei and implement full angular-momentum projection after variation, but they also find that the angular-momentum projection can be performed "approximately" with a small number of integration mesh points, which still yields energies close to the exact full-projection results and enables efficient energy-variance extrapolation.

## Method

The authors use a trial wave function consisting of a Gutzwiller-like factor multiplied by a pair-correlated wave function, which for odd-mass nuclei takes the form of a one-quasiparticle operator acting on a pair condensate. The overlap between this trial state and the m-scheme basis is expressed as a single Pfaffian, enabling unified treatment of even and odd systems. The variational parameters are optimized using the stochastic reconfiguration method, and the angular-momentum projection integrals over Euler angles are approximated by Gauss-Legendre quadrature for β and trapezoidal rule for γ, with the number of mesh points (N_z, N_y) varied from (1,1) to (21,11) to study the effect of approximate projection.

## Key numbers

- The J-VAP VMC energies for ⁴⁸Cr converge within 160 keV of exact shell-model energies for states from 0⁺ to 12⁺.
- The Monte Carlo error of the energy is typically 2 keV and small enough that error bars are omitted.
- For ⁴⁹Cr odd-mass nuclei, the difference between converged J-VAP VMC energy and exact energy is similar to the even case.
- For full angular-momentum projection to achieve J² expectation equal to I(I+1) to 6 decimal digits, the required mesh points are (N_z,N_y) = (28,14) for 0⁺, (28,14) for 2⁺, (31,16) for 4⁺, and (35,18) for 6⁺ states of ⁴⁸Cr.
- With approximate projection using (N_z,N_y) = (6,3), the energies agree with full J-VAP VMC within 70-keV difference.
- The VMC calculations cost a few hours typically on a PC server with 56 CPU cores.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous VMC method for even-mass nuclei (Shimizu and Mizusaki, 2015) to odd-mass nuclei by deriving a new Pfaffian expression, and implements variation after angular-momentum projection (J-VAP) which improves upon the earlier variation-before-projection (VBP) approach. The approximate projection scheme is novel compared to prior angular-momentum projection methods such as the Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM), VAMPIR approach, and hybrid multideterminant method, which require high-precision numerical integration. The energy-variance extrapolation using the sequence of approximate projection wave functions provides a way to estimate exact shell-model energies beyond the variational limit, similar in spirit to the particle-hole truncation scheme used by Mizusaki et al. (2008) but independent of the underlying shell structure.
