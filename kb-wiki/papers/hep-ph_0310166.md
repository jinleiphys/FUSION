# Neutrino Scattering Physics at Superbeams and Neutrino Factories

> Neutrino scattering physics is discussed for investigating internal structure of the nucleon and nuclei at future neutrino facilities. We explain structure functions in neutrino scattering. In particular, there are new polarized functions g_3, g_4, and g_5, and they should provide us important information for determining internal nucleon spin structure. Next, nuclear structure functions are discussed. From F_3 structure function measurements, valence-quark shadowing should be clarified. Nuclear effects on the NuTeV sin^2θ_W anomaly are explained. We also comment on low-energy neutrino scattering, which is relevant to current long-baseline neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

Future neutrino superbeams and neutrino factories could uniquely determine the nucleon spin structure via the polarized structure functions \(g_3\), \(g_4\), and \(g_5\), and clarify valence-quark shadowing in nuclei via \(F_3\) measurements, but the feasibility of these measurements depends on achieving sufficient luminosity and on resolving nuclear corrections that are not yet fully understood.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism for unpolarized and polarized neutrino deep inelastic scattering (DIS) in the parton model, expressing charged-current cross sections in terms of structure functions \(F_1, F_2, F_3\) and polarized functions \(g_1, g_4, g_5\). It then discusses nuclear modifications of parton distribution functions (PDFs) using existing parametrizations (EKRS and HKM) and the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation to analyze the NuTeV \(\sin^2\theta_W\) anomaly. Finally, it examines low-energy nuclear corrections (Pauli exclusion, nucleon-nucleon correlation) and a scaling-variable prescription to connect DIS and resonance regions for neutrino-oxygen scattering.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV measured \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2277 \pm 0.0013\,(\text{stat}) \pm 0.0009\,(\text{syst})\) for an iron target, compared to a global fit value of \(0.2227 \pm 0.0004\) without neutrino-nucleus data.
- The Pauli exclusion effect modifies the low-energy quasi-elastic cross section by typically 8% as a function of neutrino energy (Figure 5, based on Sakuda).
- The Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule for \(F_3\) has perturbative QCD corrections known up to \(\alpha_s^4\) level.
- The Adler sum rule for \(F_2\) gives \(S_A = 2\).
- The unpolarized Bjorken sum rule for \(F_1\) gives \(S_{Bj} = 1 - \frac{2}{3}\frac{\alpha_s(Q^2)}{\pi} + \cdots + O(1/Q^2)\).
- The Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule gives \(S_{GRS} = 3\left[1 - \frac{\alpha_s(Q^2)}{\pi} + \cdots\right] + O(1/Q^2)\).

## Context

This plenary talk from the 5th International Workshop on Neutrino Factories & Superbeams (2003) extends the line of work on nucleon structure via neutrino DIS, building on prior parametrizations of nuclear PDFs by EKRS and HKM. It directly addresses the NuTeV \(\sin^2\theta_W\) anomaly (NuTeV, 2002) and proposes that future neutrino facilities could resolve it through nuclear corrections. The paper also connects to low-energy neutrino scattering studies needed for long-baseline oscillation experiments (e.g., the NuMI project and the NuInt01 workshop), and references feasibility studies for a European neutrino factory (FMR) and polarized PDF analyses by Blumlein and Bottcher.
