# Axions from cooling compact stars: pair-breaking processes

> Once formed in a supernova explosion, a neutron star cools rapidly via neutrino emission during the first 10^4-10^5 yr of its life-time. Here we compute the axion emission rate from baryonic components of a star at temperatures below their respective critical temperatures T_c for normal-superfluid phase transition. The axion production is driven by a charge neutral weak process, associated with Cooper pair breaking and recombination. The requirement that the axion cooling does not overshadow the neutrino cooling puts a lower bound on the axion decay constant f_a > 6 10^9 T_{c9}^{-1} GeV, with T_{c9} = T_c/10^9 K. This translates into a upper bound on the axion mass m_a < 10^{-3} T_{c9} eV.

## Key claim

The axion emission rate from Cooper pair-breaking processes in superfluid neutron stars scales as \(T^5\), which is faster than the \(T^7\) scaling of the competing neutrino emission; requiring that axion cooling does not dominate over neutrino cooling yields a lower bound on the axion decay constant \(f_a > 5.92 \times 10^{9} \, T_{c9}^{-1}\) GeV, which depends on the critical temperature \(T_c\) of the superfluid and is somewhat larger than previous bounds from axion bremsstrahlung.

## Method

The authors compute the axion emissivity from baryonic superfluid phases in neutron stars using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism for the polarization tensor of superfluid matter, considering only \(S\)-wave (spin-0, isospin-triplet) Cooper pairs. They evaluate the contraction of the baryonic polarization tensor with the axion current to leading order in the small parameter \(\delta = |\mathbf{q}| v_F / \omega\), and then integrate over the axion phase space to obtain the emissivity. The resulting axion emissivity is compared analytically to the known neutrino emissivity from Cooper pair-breaking processes to derive bounds on the axion decay constant and mass.

## Key numbers

- Axion emissivity in cgs units: \(\epsilon_a = 1.06 \times 10^{21} \left(\frac{10^{10}\,\text{GeV}}{f_a}\right)^2 \left(\frac{m^*}{m}\right)^2 \left(\frac{v_F}{c}\right)^3 \left(\frac{T}{10^9\,\text{K}}\right)^5 I_a\) erg cm\(^{-3}\) s\(^{-1}\).
- Lower bound on axion decay constant: \(f_a > 5.92 \times 10^{9}\,\text{GeV} \left[\frac{0.1\,\text{MeV}}{\Delta(T)}\right]\), which translates to \(f_a > 6 \times 10^{9} \, T_{c9}^{-1}\) GeV with \(T_{c9} = T_c / 10^9\) K.
- Upper bound on axion mass: \(m_a \le 1.05 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{eV} \left[\frac{\Delta(T)}{0.1\,\text{MeV}}\right]\), which translates to \(m_a < 10^{-3} \, T_{c9}\) eV.
- Numerical values of the ratio \(r(z) = z^2 (I_a / I_\nu)\): \(r(0.5) = 0.07\), \(r(1) = 0.26\), \(r(2) = 0.6\), and asymptotically \(r(z) \to 1\) for \(z \gg 1\), where \(z = \Delta(T)/T\).

## Context

This work extends the study of axion emission from neutron stars beyond the earlier nucleon bremsstrahlung process considered by Iwamoto (1984) and the cooling simulations by Umeda et al. (1997), which gave bounds \(f/10^{10}\,\text{GeV} > 0.3\) and \(f/10^{10}\,\text{GeV} > 0.1-0.2\), respectively. The new bound is somewhat larger than those from supernova energy-loss arguments (Raffelt, 2006; Brinkmann, 1988; Burrows, 1988; Raffelt, 1993; Janka, 1995; Hanhart, 2000). The results enable more precise constraints on axion parameters through targeted numerical simulations of cooling neutron stars, such as those applied to the neutron star in Cas A by Page (2010) and Blaschke (2011), though uncertainties remain in the pairing gap magnitude, surface composition, magnetic field effects, and age estimates.
