# Multiple giant resonances in nuclei: their excitation and decay

> The excitation of multiphonon giant resonances with heavy ions is discussed. The conventional theory, based on the use of the virtual photon number method in conjunction with the harmonic model is presented and its shortcomings are discussed. The recently developed model that invoke the Brink-Axel mechanism as an important contribution to the cross-section is discussed and compared to the conventional, harmonic model. The decay properties of these multiple giant resonances are also discussed within the same coherent + fluctuation model in conjunction with the hybrid decay model. It is demonstrated that the Brink-Axel mechanism enhances the direct decay of the states, as data seem to require. Comparison of our model with other recent theoretical works is presented.

# Multiple giant resonances in nuclei: their excitation and decay

## Key claim

The conventional harmonic (folding) model fails to fully account for double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) excitation data, with discrepancies reaching over 80% in the case of Xe+Pb at 640 MeV/A; the authors argue that incorporating the Brink-Axel mechanism, incoherent excitation of collective phonons on top of the statistical background from previously decayed phonons, provides a consistent description of both excitation cross sections and enhanced direct decay of multiphonon states.

## Method

The authors develop a semiclassical reaction theory based on a density-matrix evolution equation for a damped harmonic oscillator representing the giant resonance spectrum, separating the cross section into a coherent (average) component and a fluctuation (statistical) component arising from the Brink-Axel mechanism. They solve the coupled equations of motion as a function of impact parameter for a three-dimensional generalization that accounts for transverse and longitudinal Coulomb excitation modes, then extract asymptotic occupation probabilities and integrate over impact parameter to obtain cross sections. Decay probabilities are calculated using the hybrid direct+statistical decay model of Dias-Hussein-Adhikari (DHA), which partitions decay into direct emission from the giant resonance and statistical emission from the compound-nucleus-like background.

## Context

This work extends the coherent+fluctuation model developed by Carlson et al. (1999) and applies it to multiple giant resonance excitation and decay, addressing discrepancies between the conventional folding model (Llope and Braun-Munzinger, 1990) and experimental data reviewed by Aumann, Bortignon, and Emling (1998). The approach is supported by random matrix theory calculations of Gu and Weidenmüller (2001) and contrasts with the microscopic structure model of Ponomarev et al. (1994) and the Weisäcker-Williams approximation of Baur and Bertulani (1986). The results enable predictions for triple giant dipole resonance (TGDR) cross sections and decay branching ratios at bombarding energies around 100 MeV/A, where the Brink-Axel mechanism dominates, and suggest that similar phenomena may occur in other systems with multiply excited collective modes coupled to a statistical background.
