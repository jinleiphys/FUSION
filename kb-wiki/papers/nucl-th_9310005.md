# Nuclear Shadowing at Small x and Q^2

> Shadowing corrections to structure functions of heavy nuclei are calculated at very low values of Bjorken-$x$ and at values of the momentum transfer relevant to recent experiments. Good agreement is obtained with data from the E665 Collaboration for Xe/D and Pb/D, and with the NMC data on Ca/D and C/D structure function ratios. Corrections to the deuteron structure function are also estimated down to $x \sim 10^{-5}$, and found to be less than about $3\%$ over the range of $x$ covered by the E665 data.

# Nuclear Shadowing at Small x and Q²

## Key claim

Shadowing corrections to nuclear structure functions, calculated using a two-phase model combining vector meson dominance (VMD) and Pomeron-exchange mechanisms, reproduce the observed saturation of shadowing at very low x and the A-dependence seen in E665 and NMC data, but the deuteron shadowing corrections are less than about 3% over the experimental range and cannot be distinguished by current data.

## Method

The authors combine a VMD model for low-Q² virtual photon interactions with a partonic Pomeron-exchange description for higher Q², using Glauber multiple scattering theory to compute shadowing corrections to nuclear structure functions. For heavy nuclei (A > 16) they use Woods-Saxon densities, for light nuclei harmonic oscillator densities, and for the deuteron they use realistic NN potential model wavefunctions. The VMD contribution includes only the lowest mass vector mesons (ρ⁰, ω, φ), while the Pomeron-exchange contribution uses a structure function for the Pomeron that includes both a qq̅ component and a triple Pomeron term, with a cutoff to avoid double counting with the VMD contribution.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron shadowing corrections to F₂D/F₂p are less than ~3% over the experimental range of x and Q² covered by E665 data
- As a fraction of total F₂D, deuteron shadowing amounts to ≤ 2-3%
- The free neutron structure function is ~5% larger at x ~ 10⁻⁴ and ~2% larger at x ~ 10⁻² than the bound neutron structure function extracted without accounting for shadowing
- The VMD contribution peaks at Q² ~ 1 GeV² and becomes almost negligible for Q² > 10 GeV²
- The Pomeron-exchange contribution starts to dominate above Q² ~ 5 GeV²
- The parameter Q₀² = 0.485 GeV² is used for the low-Q² extrapolation of structure functions
- The triple Pomeron coupling constant g₃𝒫 = 0.364 mb¹/²
- The quark-Pomeron coupling constant β₀² = 3.4 GeV⁻²
- The correlation function parameter κ = 3.6 fm⁻¹ reproduces a hole in the two-body density approximately 0.5 fm wide at half-maximum
- Using an independent particle approximation differs from the full calculation by only 5-10%
- Projectile attenuation reduces the amount of shadowing by up to 30-40%
- The meson-exchange contribution to deuteron shadowing is ~6% of the total shadowing correction at x ~ 10⁻³ and ~13% at x ~ 10⁻²

## Context

This work extends the VMD/Pomeron-exchange approach of Kwiecinski and Badelek (1988) and the description of real photon interactions by Schuler and Sjöstrand (1993) to the very low x and Q² region accessed by the E665 Collaboration at Fermilab. The model successfully reproduces E665 data on Xe/D and Pb/D ratios, NMC data on Ca/D and C/D, and predicts deuteron shadowing corrections that are too small to be resolved by current E665 data but could become testable with improved statistics. The results enable more accurate extraction of the free neutron structure function from deuteron measurements and suggest that neutrino-proton DIS data with improved statistics could provide an independent check via charge symmetry.
