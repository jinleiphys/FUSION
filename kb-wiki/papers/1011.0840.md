# New ways to access the transverse spin content of the nucleon

> We first describe a new way to access the chiral odd transversity parton distribution in the proton through the photoproduction of lepton pairs. The basic ingredient is the interference of the usual Bethe-Heitler or Drell-Yan amplitudes with the amplitude of a process, where the photon couples to quarks through its chiral-odd distribution amplitude, which is normalized to the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum. We also show how the chiral-odd transversity generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of the nucleon can be accessed experimentally through the exclusive electro - or photoproduction process of a meson pair with a large invariant mass and when the final nucleon has a small transverse momentum. We calculate perturbatively the scattering amplitude at leading order, both in the high energy domain which may be accessed in electron-ion colliders and in the medium energy range. Estimated rates are encouraging.

## Key claim

The paper proposes two new ways to access chiral-odd transversity parton distributions and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the nucleon, but the feasibility depends on the interference of amplitudes involving the photon's chiral-odd distribution amplitude, which is normalized to the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum, and on the assumption that collinear factorization holds at least in the Born approximation for the exclusive processes considered.

## Method

The authors calculate perturbatively at leading order in the strong coupling constant the scattering amplitude for photoproduction of lepton pairs, where the interference between the Bethe-Heitler amplitude and the amplitude involving the photon's chiral-odd twist-2 distribution amplitude yields a transversity-dependent cross section proportional to the transversity parton distribution \(h_1(x)\). They also compute the exclusive electro- or photoproduction of a meson pair (either two \(\rho\) mesons or a \(\pi\rho_T\) pair) with large invariant mass and small transverse momentum transfer to the nucleon, factorizing the amplitude into a perturbatively calculable hard part and chiral-odd transversity GPDs, which they model using double distributions based on parametrized transversity PDFs.

## Key numbers

* The product of the quark condensate and the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum, \(\chi \langle \bar q q \rangle\), is estimated to be of the order of 50 MeV.
* The transverse decay constant of the \(\rho\) meson, \(f_\rho^\bot\), is 160 MeV.
* The \(t\)-dependence of the chiral-odd GPDs is modeled with a dipole form using \(C = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\).
* Differential cross sections for \(\pi\rho_T\) photoproduction are shown at \(S_{\gamma N} = 20\) GeV\(^2\), 100 GeV\(^2\), and 200 GeV\(^2\), with values on the order of nb GeV\(^{-2}\).
* Differential cross sections for \(\rho_L^0 \rho_T^+\) photoproduction are shown as a function of \(\xi\) for \(p_T^2 = 2, 4, 6\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering studies on transversity distributions (Barone, 2000s) by proposing new hard processes that avoid the decoupling of chiral-odd quantities in leading-twist amplitudes, which has plagued earlier attempts. The lepton-pair photoproduction method builds on the observation by Braun (2000) that the photon twist-2 distribution amplitude is chiral-odd, while the exclusive meson-pair production extends the strategy of Ivanov, Pire, Szymanowski, and Teryaev (2000s) for accessing chiral-odd GPDs, similar to the approach advocated by Kumano (2010) for chiral-even GPDs. The results enable experimental searches at JLab@12GeV and COMPASS, which could provide complementary information on the transversity GPD \(H_T(x,\xi,t)\) in different \(\xi\) regions.
