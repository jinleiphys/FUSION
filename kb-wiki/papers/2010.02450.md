# Neutron skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb determined from reaction cross section for proton scattering

> The reaction cross section $σ_R$ is useful to determine the neutron radius $R_n$ as well as the matter radius $R_m$. The chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model for $^{12}$C scattering on $^{9}$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al targets was tested in the incident energy range of $30 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 400 $ MeV, and it is found that the model reliably reproduces the $σ_R$ in $30 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 100 $ MeV and $250 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 400$ MeV. \item[Aim] We determine $R_n$ and the neutron skin thickness $R_{\rm skin}$ of ${}^{208}{\rm Pb}$ by using high-quality $σ_R$ data for the $p+{}^{208}{\rm Pb}$ scattering in $30 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 100$ MeV. The theoretical model is the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with the densities calculated with Gongny-D1S HFB (GHFB) with the angular momentum projection (AMP). \item[Results] The Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with the GHFB+AMP densities underestimates $σ_{\rm R}$ in $30 \leq E_{\rm in} \leq 100$~MeV only by a factor of 0.97. Since the proton radius $R_p$ calculated with GHFB+AMP agrees with the precise experimental data of 5.444 fm, the small deviation of the theoretical result from the data on $σ_R$ allows us to scale the GHFB+AMP neutron density so as to reproduce the $σ_R$ data. In $E_{\rm in}$ = 30--100 MeV, the experimental $σ_R$ data can be reproduced by assuming the neutron radius of ${}^{208}{\rm Pb}$ as $R_n$ = $5.722 \pm 0.035$ fm. \item[Conclusion] The present result $R_{\rm skin}$ = $0.278 \pm 0.035$ fm is in good agreement with the recent PREX-II result of $r_{\rm skin}$ = $0.283\pm 0.071$ fm.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of 208Pb is determined to be 0.278 ± 0.035 fm from proton reaction cross section data, consistent with the PREX-II result of 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, but with a smaller uncertainty; this result depends on the assumption that the Kyushu g-matrix folding model with GHFB+AMP densities is reliable in the 30–100 MeV energy range and that the small 0.97 scaling factor applied to the theoretical cross section can be attributed entirely to an underestimation of the neutron density.

## Method

The authors used the Kyushu g-matrix folding model, which is a localized version of a chiral g-matrix interaction, to calculate reaction cross sections for proton scattering on 208Pb. The proton and neutron densities of 208Pb were computed using the Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (GHFB) method with angular momentum projection (AMP). The theoretical reaction cross sections were compared to experimental data in the incident energy range 30–100 MeV, and the neutron density was scaled to reproduce the measured cross sections, yielding the neutron radius and skin thickness.

## Key numbers

- Proton radius of 208Pb from GHFB+AMP: Rp = 5.444 fm, which agrees with the experimental value of 5.444 fm
- Neutron radius of 208Pb determined from reaction cross section scaling: Rn = 5.722 ± 0.035 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: Rskin = 0.278 ± 0.035 fm
- PREX-II result for comparison: rskin = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- The Kyushu g-matrix folding model with GHFB+AMP densities underestimates the reaction cross section by a factor of 0.97 in the 30–100 MeV range
- An alternative combination of eight data points yields Rn = 5.735 ± 0.035 fm and Rskin = 0.291 ± 0.035 fm

## Context

This work extends the application of the Kyushu g-matrix folding model, previously tested for 12C scattering on light targets (Tagami et al., 2020) and for calcium isotopes (Tagami et al., 2020), to determine the neutron skin of 208Pb from proton scattering data. The result is consistent with the parity-violating measurement from the PREX-II collaboration (2021) and with a dispersive-optical-model analysis (DOM, 2020) that found rskin = 0.25 ± 0.05 fm, but is significantly larger than other model-dependent experimental values. This determination enables tests of the nuclear matter equation of state and the symmetry energy parameter L, with implications for neutron star properties.
