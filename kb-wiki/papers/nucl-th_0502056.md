# Charm elliptic flow at RHIC

> Charm elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) is studied in a multiphase transport model. Assuming that the cross section for charm quark scattering with other light quarks is the same as that between light quarks, we find that both charm and light quark elliptic flows are sensitive to the value of the cross section. Compared to that of light quarks, the elliptic flow of charm quarks is smaller at low transverse momentum but approaches comparable values at high transverse momentum. Similar features are seen in the elliptic flow of charmed mesons as well as that of the electrons from their semileptonic decays when the charmed mesons are produced from quark coalescence during hadronization of the partonic matter. To describe the large electron elliptic flow observed in available experimental data requires a charm quark scattering cross section that is much larger than that given by the perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

Using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model with the assumption that charm quark scattering cross sections with light quarks equal those between light quarks, the authors find that describing the measured electron elliptic flow from charmed meson decays at RHIC requires a charm quark scattering cross section much larger than the perturbative QCD estimate of 3 mb, and even an isotropic 10 mb cross section is insufficient to fully reproduce the experimental data.

## Method

The authors extended the AMPT model (with string melting) to include charm quarks, using initial charm quark distributions derived from STAR d+Au collision data parameterized as a power law. Charm quarks were propagated through partonic matter using Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) model with elastic scatterings, using current quark masses (1.35 GeV for charm), and were hadronized via a coordinate-space quark coalescence model into D mesons, whose semileptonic decay electrons were then compared to PHENIX and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- Charm production cross section in nucleon-nucleon collisions extracted from STAR d+Au data: 1.3 mb, about a factor of 30 smaller than the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section (~40 mb)
- Two parton scattering cross sections used: σ_p = 3 mb (perturbative QCD estimate) and σ_p = 10 mb (strongly interacting QCD)
- Current quark masses used: d quark 9.9 MeV, u quark 5.6 MeV, s quark 199 MeV, c quark 1.35 GeV
- Maximum quark v₂ values: about 6% for σ_p = 3 mb and about 9% for σ_p = 10 mb
- Integrated electron elliptic flow: v₂e = 0.55 ± 0.01% (σ_p = 3 mb) and v₂e = 0.90 ± 0.01% (σ_p = 10 mb)
- Integrated D meson elliptic flow: v₂D = 2.73 ± 0.04% (σ_p = 3 mb) and v₂D = 3.91 ± 0.04% (σ_p = 10 mb)
- Charm production cross section used for PHENIX comparison: 600 μb

## Context

This work extends the schematic study of Greco (2003) by using a dynamical AMPT model rather than assuming charm quark elliptic flow equals fitted light quark flow, and it finds similar results to the Parton Cascade (MPC) model of Molnar (2004) but much larger charmed meson elliptic flow than found in the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) model of Bratkovskaya (2004). The study enables quantitative constraints on heavy quark interactions in partonic matter, showing that electron elliptic flow data require charm scattering cross sections beyond perturbative QCD, potentially consistent with resonant heavy-light quark interactions as suggested by vanHees (2004).
