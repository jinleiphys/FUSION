# Effects of relativistic kinematics in heavy ion elastic scattering

> Relativistic corrections to the reaction kinematic parameters were made for elastic scattering of \nuc{6}{Li}, \nuc{12}{C} and \nuc{40}{Ar} from \nuc{40}{Ca}, \nuc{90}{Zr}, and \nuc{208}{Pb} targets at incident energies between 20 and 100 MeV/nucleon. Results of optical model calculations show that the effects of such corrections are important in describing the angular distributions of elastic scattering cross sections for heavy ion scattering at incident energies as low as around 40 MeV/nucleon. The effects on the total reaction cross sections on the other hand, were found to be small within the energy range studied when the optical model potential is fixed.

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections to reaction kinematic parameters are important for describing the angular distributions of heavy ion elastic scattering cross sections at incident energies as low as around 40 MeV/nucleon, but the effects on total reaction cross sections are small (within 0.5%) when the optical model potential is fixed.

## Method

Optical model calculations were performed for elastic scattering of 6Li, 12C, and 40Ar projectiles from 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb targets at incident energies between 20 and 100 MeV/nucleon. The relativistic corrections were applied by transforming the projectile and target masses and the effective bombarding energy according to the prescription of Satchler (1992), using the Lorentz factors gamma_p and gamma_t. A systematic single-folding nucleus-nucleus potential based on the Bruyeres JLMB model nucleon-nucleus potential was used, with potential parameters previously established for heavy targets (A >= 40) at 5 to 40 MeV/nucleon and extended up to 100 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ar elastic scattering from 208Pb at 20, 40, 60, 80, and 100 MeV/nucleon, the changes in the quarter-point angle theta_1/4 were 1%, 1.5%, 2.2%, 2.9%, and 3.6%, respectively.
- For most cases studied, the changes in total reaction cross sections sigma_R were within 0.5% when the optical model potential was fixed.
- The ratio k/k^rel (wave number without/with relativistic corrections) was plotted as a function of incident energy for all projectile-target combinations, showing that the change in k decreases with increasing projectile mass and increases with increasing target mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic kinematic corrections by Farid and Satchler (1984), who found changes in optical model potential strength larger than 20% at 44 MeV/nucleon, by systematically examining the effects on angular distributions and total reaction cross sections across multiple projectile-target combinations. The results enable improved description of heavy ion elastic scattering angular distributions at energies previously thought to be in the non-relativistic regime, and suggest that similar relativistic corrections may be important in decay problems such as alpha decay studied by Xu and Chang (2006).
