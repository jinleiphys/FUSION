# Finite momentum at string endpoints

> We argue that classical strings, both bosonic and supersymmetric, can have finite energy and momentum at their endpoints. We show that in a general curved background, string endpoints must propagate along null geodesics as long as their energy remains finite. Finite endpoint momentum allows strings with a fixed energy to travel a greater distance in an AdS5-Schwarzschild background than has been possible for classical solutions considered previously. We review the relevance to heavy ion phenomenology of the dependence of this distance on energy, and we propose a scheme for determining the instantaneous rate of energy loss.

## Key claim

Classical strings, both bosonic and supersymmetric, can have finite energy and momentum at their endpoints, contrary to standard boundary conditions that require zero endpoint momentum; the authors argue that string endpoints with finite momentum must propagate along null geodesics as long as their energy remains finite, and that such configurations allow strings with fixed energy to travel a greater distance in an AdS5-Schwarzschild background than previously considered classical solutions.

## Method

The authors augment the classical bosonic string action with explicit boundary terms whose form is the classical massless particle action, deriving equations of motion showing that string endpoints with finite momentum follow spacetime geodesics. They extend this construction to the light-cone Green-Schwarz superstring action, demonstrating that finite endpoint momentum can be incorporated while preserving sixteen real supercharges in ten dimensions. For the AdS5-Schwarzschild geometry, they analyze falling string configurations with finite endpoint momentum, computing the stopping distance by tracing null geodesics and assuming endpoint energy vanishes at the horizon.

## Key numbers

- The stopping distance for falling strings with zero endpoint momentum is Δx_stop ≈ 0.526 × (1/T) × (E_*/(√λ T))^(1/3), where the numerical factor 0.526 comes from [2^(1/3)/√π] × Γ(5/4)/Γ(3/4)
- For strings with finite endpoint momentum, the stopping distance becomes Δx_stop = [2^(1/3)/π^(2/3)] × [Γ(5/4) Γ(1/4)^(1/3) / Γ(3/4)] × (1/T) × (E_*/(√λ T))^(1/3), which is approximately 19% larger than the zero-momentum case
- The initial energy of a falling string with zero endpoint momentum is E_* = (√λ/(2π)) × (z_H^2/z_*^3) in the limit z_* ≪ z_H
- For the AdS5 yo-yo solution, the radius at which snapback occurs is ρ_* = sinh^(-1)((πα'/(2L)) E), and the total energy is E = (2L/(πα')) sinh ρ_*

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of falling strings as dual descriptions of energy loss for gluons and light quarks in heavy ion phenomenology, particularly the numerical work of Chesler (2008) which found the stopping distance scaling Δx ∝ E^(1/3)/T^(4/3). The authors show that finite endpoint momentum allows surpassing the upper bound on Δx obtained numerically in that work by about 19%. The yo-yo solutions studied generalize earlier work by Bardeen (1975) and Artru (1979), and the AdS5 yo-yo is related to the pulsating string solutions of Gubser (2002). The results enable a scheme for determining the instantaneous rate of energy loss for energetic quarks and gluons traversing a thermal medium.
