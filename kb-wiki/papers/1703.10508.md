# Heavy Quark Potential in a static and strong homogeneous magnetic field

> We have investigated the properties of quarkonia in a thermal QCD medium in the background of strong magnetic field. For that purpose, we employ the Schwinger proper-time quark propagator in the lowest Landau level to calculate the one-loop gluon self-energy, which in the sequel gives the the effective gluon propagator. As an artifact of strong magnetic field approximation ($eB>>T^2$ and $eB>>m^2$), the Debye mass for massless flavors is found to depend only on the magnetic field which is the dominant scale in comparison to the scales prevalent in the thermal medium. However, for physical quark masses, it depends on both magnetic field and temperature in a low temperature and high magnetic field but the temperature dependence is very meagre and becomes independent of temperature beyond a certain temperature and magnetic field. With the above mentioned ingredients, the potential between heavy quark ($Q$) and anti-quark ($\bar Q$) is obtained in a hot QCD medium in the presence of strong magnetic field by correcting both short and long range components of the potential in real-time formalism. It is found that the long range part of the quarkonium potential is affected much more by magnetic field as compared to the short range part. This observation facilitates us to estimate the magnetic field beyond which the potential will be too weak to bind $Q\bar Q$ together. For example, the $J/ψ$ is dissociated at $eB \sim$ 10 $m_π^2$ and $Υ$ is dissociated at $eB \sim$ 100 $m_π^2$ whereas its excited states, $ψ^\prime$ and $Υ^\prime$ are dissociated at smaller magnetic field $eB= m_π^2$, $13 m_π^2$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the strong magnetic field approximation (SMFA), where \(eB \gg T^2\) and \(eB \gg m^2\), the Debye screening mass for massless quarks depends only on the magnetic field and is independent of temperature; for physical quark masses, the Debye mass depends on both magnetic field and temperature, but the temperature dependence is very weak and becomes negligible beyond a certain temperature and magnetic field. The authors hedge that their work is valid only under the SMFA conditions and that the magnetic fields expected at RHIC may not satisfy these conditions, making the results primarily applicable to LHC events.

## Method

The authors employed the Schwinger proper-time quark propagator in the lowest Landau level (LLL) to compute the one-loop gluon self-energy at finite temperature in a strong homogeneous magnetic field. They then obtained the effective gluon propagator and derived the heavy quark potential by correcting both the short-range (Coulomb) and long-range (string) components of the Cornell potential using the dielectric function in real-time formalism. The Debye screening mass was extracted from the static limit of the longitudinal (00-component) gluon self-energy, and the dissociation conditions for quarkonium states were estimated from the magnetic field strength at which the potential becomes too weak to bind the quark-antiquark pair.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass squared for massless quarks: \(m_D^2 = \frac{g^2}{8\pi^2} \sum_f |q_f| B\), depending only on the magnetic field.
- The \(J/\psi\) meson dissociates at \(eB \sim 10\, m_\pi^2\).
- The \(\Upsilon\) meson dissociates at \(eB \sim 100\, m_\pi^2\).
- The excited state \(\psi^\prime\) dissociates at \(eB = m_\pi^2\).
- The excited state \(\Upsilon^\prime\) dissociates at \(eB = 13\, m_\pi^2\).
- The upper bound on magnetic field for charmonium states is taken as \(eB = 25\, m_\pi^2\), giving \(\sqrt{eB} \sim 0.7\) GeV, to satisfy the condition \(m_Q \gg \sqrt{eB}\).
- The starting magnetic field for the analysis is \(eB = 10\, m_\pi^2 \sim 0.2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The string tension used is \(\sigma = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends perturbative thermal QCD studies of heavy quarkonia in a hot medium, previously done by Thakur et al. (2013, 2014) using the dielectric function in real-time formalism for isotropic and anisotropic media, by incorporating the effects of a strong homogeneous magnetic field. The results qualitatively agree with lattice estimates by Bonati et al. (2017) for the electric screening mass in a magnetic background, though the lattice values are larger by about an order of magnitude, which the authors attribute to nonperturbative effects beyond their scope. The findings enable estimates of the magnetic field strengths required to dissociate specific quarkonium states in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at LHC energies where the SMFA conditions are satisfied.
