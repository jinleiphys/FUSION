# Entanglement minimization in hadronic scattering with pions

> Recent work arXiv:1812.03138 [nucl-th] conjectured that entanglement is minimized in low-energy hadronic scattering processes. It was shown that the minimization of the entanglement power (EP) of the low-energy baryon-baryon $S$-matrix implies novel spin-flavor symmetries that are distinct from large-$N_c$ QCD predictions and are confirmed by high-precision lattice QCD simulations. Here the conjecture of minimal entanglement is investigated for scattering processes involving pions and nucleons. The EP of the $S$-matrix is constructed for the $ππ$ and $πN$ systems, and the consequences of minimization of entanglement are discussed and compared with large-$N_c$ QCD expectations.

## Key claim

The paper tests the conjecture that entanglement is minimized in low-energy hadronic scattering, specifically for systems involving pions, and finds that for pion-pion scattering the implications of entanglement minimization are indistinguishable from large-\(N_c\) QCD expectations, while for pion-nucleon scattering the implications are distinct but yield only simple scaling laws rather than smoking-gun predictions.

## Method

The entanglement power (EP) of the \(S\)-matrix was constructed for both \(\pi\pi\) and \(\pi N\) systems by expressing the \(S\)-matrix in a direct-product basis of isospin states and averaging the linear entropy over the initial product-state manifolds (\(\mathbf{CP}^2\) for pions, \(\mathbf{CP}^2 \times \mathbf{S}^2\) for pion-nucleon). The EP was evaluated using Roy-equation determinations of phase shifts for \(\pi\pi\) scattering and Roy-Steiner equation determinations for \(\pi N\) scattering, and compared with leading-order chiral perturbation theory expressions near threshold.

## Key numbers

* For \(\pi\pi\) s-wave EP near threshold: \(\mathcal{E}(\hat{\mathbf{S}}_0) = \frac{1}{9 M_\pi^2}\big[4(a^0_0)^2 -5 (a^0_0 a^2_0)+10 (a^2_0)^2\big] q^2 + \mathcal{O}(q^4)\), with scattering lengths \(a_0^0 = 7M_\pi^2/(32\pi F_\pi^2)\) and \(a_0^2 = -M_\pi^2/(16\pi F_\pi^2)\) at leading order in chiral perturbation theory, where \(F_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(\pi\pi\) p-wave EP: \(\mathcal{E}(\hat{\mathbf{S}}_1) = \frac{1}{4}\sin^2(2\delta_1^1)\), with non-entangling solutions at \(\delta_1^1 = 0\) or \(\pi/2\).
* For \(\pi N\) EP: \(\mathcal{E}(\hat{\mathbf{S}}_{\ell \pm}) = \frac{8}{243}\big[17 + 10\cos(2(\delta_{\ell \pm}^{3/2} - \delta_{\ell \pm}^{1/2}))\big] \sin^2(\delta_{\ell \pm}^{3/2} - \delta_{\ell \pm}^{1/2})\), which vanishes only when \(\delta_{\ell \pm}^{3/2} = \delta_{\ell \pm}^{1/2}\).
* For \(\pi N\) EP near threshold: \(\mathcal{E}(\hat{\mathbf{S}}_{\ell \pm}) = \frac{8}{9}(a^{1/2}_{\ell \pm} - a^{3/2}_{\ell \pm})^2 \vec{q}^{\,2+4\ell}\), with s-wave scattering lengths \(a^{1/2}_{0+} = 2 M_\pi m / [8\pi (m+M_\pi) F_\pi^2]\) and \(a^{3/2}_{0+} = -M_\pi m / [8\pi (m+M_\pi) F_\pi^2]\) at leading order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement-minimization conjecture of Beane (2018), which was previously tested for baryon-baryon scattering and found to imply an enhanced \(SU(16)\) symmetry in the three-flavor case that is distinct from large-\(N_c\) QCD predictions. For \(\pi\pi\) scattering, the results are indistinguishable from large-\(N_c\) expectations, while for \(\pi N\) scattering the absence of an enhanced symmetry limits predictive power to simple scaling laws. The paper uses Roy-equation solutions from Ananthanarayan (2000) and Colangelo (2001) for \(\pi\pi\) phase shifts, and Roy-Steiner equation determinations from Hoferichter (2016) for \(\pi N\) phase shifts.
