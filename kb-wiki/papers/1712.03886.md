# Sum rules across the unpolarized Compton processes involving generalized polarizabilities and moments of nucleon structure functions

> We derive two new sum rules for the unpolarized doubly virtual Compton scattering process on a nucleon, which establish novel low-$Q^2$ relations involving the nucleon's generalized polarizabilities and moments of the nucleon's unpolarized structure functions $F_1(x,Q^2)$ and $F_2(x,Q^2)$. These relations facilitate the determination of some structure constants which can only be accessed in off-forward doubly virtual Compton scattering, not experimentally accessible at present. We perform an empirical determination for the proton and compare our results with a next-to-leading-order chiral perturbation theory prediction. We also show how these relations may be useful for a model-independent determination of the low-$Q^2$ subtraction function in the Compton amplitude, which enters the two-photon-exchange contribution to the Lamb shift of (muonic) hydrogen. An explicit calculation of the $Δ(1232)$-resonance contribution to the muonic-hydrogen $2P-2S$ Lamb shift yields $-1 \pm 1$ $\mathsfμ$eV, confirming the previously conjectured smallness of this effect.

## Key claim

The paper derives two new sum rules for the unpolarized doubly virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) process on a nucleon, which establish novel low-\(Q^2\) relations connecting generalized polarizabilities (GPs) to moments of the unpolarized nucleon structure functions \(F_1(x,Q^2)\) and \(F_2(x,Q^2)\), with the caveat that one of the low-energy coefficients (\(b_{3,0}\)) remains inaccessible to current experiments and requires a measurement of doubly virtual Compton scattering with a spacelike initial and timelike final photon.

## Method

The authors use the tensor decomposition of the spin-independent VVCS amplitude into five invariant amplitudes \(B_i\) and perform low-energy expansions (LEXs) in the photon virtualities \(q^2, q^{\prime 2}\) and energy \(\nu\). They connect these expansions to known observables from real Compton scattering (RCS), virtual Compton scattering (VCS), and forward VVCS, then derive dispersion relations for the forward amplitudes \(T_1\) and \(T_2\) that relate the LEX coefficients to moments of the structure functions \(F_1\) and \(F_2\). The sum rules are verified using a next-to-leading-order (NLO) baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) calculation, and the \(\Delta(1232)\)-resonance contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift is computed explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta(1232)\)-resonance contribution to the muonic-hydrogen \(2P-2S\) Lamb shift is \(-1 \pm 1\) \(\mu\)eV, confirming the previously conjectured smallness of this effect.
- The Baldin sum rule at \(Q^2 = 0\) gives \(\alpha_{E1} + \beta_{M1} = M^{(2)}_1(0) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_{\nu_0}^{\infty} d\nu' \, \sigma_T(\nu') / \nu^{\prime 2}\), where \(\sigma_T\) is the total photoabsorption cross section on a proton.
- The higher-order generalization of the Baldin sum rule at \(Q^2 = 0\) gives \(\alpha_{E1,\nu} + \beta_{M1,\nu} + \frac{1}{12}(\alpha_{E2} + \beta_{M2}) = M^{(4)}_1(0) = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int_{\nu_0}^{\infty} d\nu' \, \sigma_T(\nu') / \nu^{\prime 4}\).
- The low-energy coefficient \(b_{4,1}\) is expressed via Eq. (b4) in terms of \(M^{(2)\prime}_1(0)\), \(\beta_{M1,\nu}\), \(\beta_{M2}\), \(\alpha_{E2}\), \(\alpha_{E1}^\prime\), \(\beta_{M1}^\prime\), spin polarizabilities (\(\delta_{LT}\), \(\gamma_{M1M1}\), \(\gamma_{E1E1}\), \(\gamma_{M1E2}\), \(\gamma_{E1M2}\)), and \(\alpha_{E1} + \beta_{M1}\).
- The low-energy coefficient \(b_{3,0}\) is expressed via Eq. (b30Formula) in terms of \(\frac{1}{2}\bar{T}_1^{\prime\prime}(0)\), \(\beta_{M2}\), \(\beta_{M1}^\prime\), and \(\beta_{M1}\).

## Context

This work extends the family of sum rules previously derived for spin-dependent structure functions by Pascalutsa et al. (2014) and Lensky et al. (2017) to the spin-independent case, connecting low-\(Q^2\) slopes of moments of \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) to generalized polarizabilities accessible in RCS and VCS experiments. The new relations enable a model-independent determination of the low-\(Q^2\) subtraction function in the forward VVCS amplitude \(T_1\), which enters the two-photon-exchange contribution to the Lamb shift of muonic hydrogen, relevant to the proton radius puzzle. The results also provide empirical and NLO BChPT predictions for the low-energy coefficients \(b_{3,0}\), \(b_{4,1}\), and \(b_{19,0}\), which can be tested in future doubly virtual Compton scattering measurements.
