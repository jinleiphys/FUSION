# Spectral asymmetries in nucleon sum rules at finite density

> Apparent inconsistencies between different formulations of nucleon sum rules at finite density are resolved through a proper accounting of asymmetries in the spectral functions between positive- and negative-energy states.

## Key claim

Apparent inconsistencies between different formulations of nucleon QCD sum rules at finite density, which had led some authors to conclude that the vector self-energy of a nucleon in nuclear matter is attractive rather than repulsive, are resolved by properly accounting for asymmetries in the spectral functions between positive- and negative-energy states and by allowing the effective continuum thresholds for positive and negative energies to differ.

## Method

The paper derives finite-energy sum rules (FESR) for the nucleon correlator in nuclear matter using two alternative Lorentz decompositions of the correlator into invariant functions. The phenomenological spectral density is modeled with a quasiparticle pole ansatz, while the theoretical spectral density is approximated using the leading terms of the operator product expansion (OPE). Sum rules are written for each invariant function with monomial weighting functions, and the consequences of allowing asymmetric duality intervals (different continuum thresholds for positive and negative energies) are examined.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density, relativistic phenomenology expects the effective nucleon mass Mstar approximately 0.65 MN and the vector self-energy sigmav approximately 0.35 MN, where MN is the nucleon mass.
- The difference between positive and negative continuum thresholds is found to scale as wzero - wzerobar approximately sigmav, which is of order the vector self-energy.
- Numerical estimates using wzero approximately 1.6 to 1.7 GeV and wzero - wzerobar = sigmav from the derived relations show that the positive-energy pole energy Eq(q=0) is essentially constant with density, rather than decreasing precipitously as found in the work of Kondo and Morimatsu (1992-1993).
- The sum rules imply that wzero + wzerobar is roughly constant with density.

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between the finite-density nucleon sum rule formulations of Furnstahl, Griegel, and Cohen (1992) and those of Kondo and Morimatsu (1992-1993), which had yielded opposite signs for the vector self-energy. The resolution enables consistent QCD sum rule support for the relativistic phenomenology of nucleons in nuclear matter, where large attractive scalar and repulsive vector self-energies arise. The paper shows that the decomposition of the correlator used by Furnstahl, Griegel, and Cohen (1992) is more robust against uncertainties in the continuum treatment than the alternative decomposition.
