# Lowest-order electron-electron and electron-muon scattering in a strong magnetic field

> In this work we have investigated how the much studied scattering processes in vacuum at the lowest-order, {\em viz.} electron-muon ($e$-$μ$) scattering in both $s$- and $t$-channel, Bhabha scattering, and Møller scattering, have been modified in the presence of a strong magnetic field ($|eB|>>m^2$, $m$ is the mass of electron or muon). For that purpose, we have first calculated the square of the matrix element by summing over the spin states, using the spinors in the presence of a strong magnetic field and then obtain the crosssection by integrating over the available phase space for the final states and averaging over the initial states. The first noticeable observation in the spin-summed of the matrix element squared is that the interference term between $s$- and $t$-channel and $t$- and $u$-channel in Bhabha and Møller scattering, respectively are missing in the presence of strong magnetic field. We have found that in the presence of strong magnetic field, the crosssection of $e^- e^+ \rightarrow μ^- μ^+$ annihilation process in the lowest order decreases inversely proportional to the fourth power of the center-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s}$), compared to the inversely proportional to the square of center-of-mass energy in vacuum alone. Like in vacuum, the crosssection for $e$-$μ$ scattering in ${\rm t}$ channel, {\em i.e.} for $e^- μ^- \rightarrow e^- μ^-$ process, even in a strong magnetic field too diverges but the finite part decreases with $\sqrt{s}$ much faster than in vacuum alone. Similarly the crosssections for both Bhabha and Møller scattering at the lowest-order diverges in the infrared limit. However, the finite term decreases with $\sqrt{s}$ much faster than the vacuum alone. In addition there is finite negative contribution, which is independent of $\sqrt{s}$ and decreases with the magnetic field.

## Key claim

In the presence of a strong magnetic field (|eB| >> m²), the cross-sections for lowest-order electron-muon annihilation, electron-muon scattering, Bhabha scattering, and Møller scattering all decrease with center-of-mass energy much faster than in vacuum, but only under the simplifying assumption that only the lowest Landau level (n=0) is populated and masses are neglected in the extreme relativistic limit.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation in a constant, uniform magnetic field along the z-direction using Ritus eigenfunctions and the Schwinger proper-time formalism, then constructed spin-summed matrix elements squared for each scattering process using the lowest-Landau-level spinors. They integrated the matrix elements over the available phase space, averaging over initial states and summing over final states, to obtain cross-sections expressed in terms of Mandelstam variables and newly defined "magnetic Mandelstam variables" that account for the longitudinal-only momentum structure in the strong-field limit.

## Key numbers

- The cross-section for e⁻ e⁺ → μ⁻ μ⁺ annihilation decreases inversely proportional to the fourth power of the center-of-mass energy (√s) in a strong magnetic field, compared to inversely proportional to the square of √s in vacuum.
- The magnetic field strength considered satisfies |eB| >> m², where m is the mass of the electron or muon; at RHIC the field may reach m_π² (≈ 10¹⁸ Gauss) and at LHC up to 15 m_π², with extreme cases up to 50 m_π² at LHC.
- The finite part of the e⁻ μ⁻ → e⁻ μ⁻ t-channel cross-section decreases with √s much faster than in vacuum alone, while the full cross-section still diverges as in vacuum.
- For both Bhabha and Møller scattering, the cross-sections diverge in the infrared limit, but the finite term decreases with √s much faster than the vacuum case, with an additional finite negative contribution independent of √s that decreases with the magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the well-studied lowest-order QED scattering processes in vacuum, previously calculated by Halzen and Martin and others, to the regime of strong magnetic fields relevant to non-central heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as to magnetar cores and the early universe during the electroweak phase transition. The results enable future calculations of higher-order corrections and more realistic scattering processes in magnetized environments, building on the spinor formalism developed by Furry, Bhattacharya (2007), and others for charged fermions in external magnetic fields.
