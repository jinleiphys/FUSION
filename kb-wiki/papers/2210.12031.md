# Neutron Lifetime Anomaly and Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> We calculate the Big Bang Nucleosynthesis abundances for helium-4 and deuterium for a range of neutron lifetimes, $τ_n = 840 - 1050$ s, using the state-of-the-art Python package \textsc{PRyMordial}. We show the results for two different nuclear reaction rates, calculated by NACRE II [1] and the PRIMAT [2] collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that current Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) predictions for helium-4 and deuterium abundances, calculated with the PRyMordial code using two different nuclear reaction rate sets (NACRE II and PRIMAT), are consistent with the bottle-method neutron lifetime of 878.4 s but slightly disfavor the beam-method value of 888.45 s, though this conclusion depends on which nuclear reaction rates and which primordial abundance measurements are adopted.

## Method

The authors used the Python package PRyMordial to compute BBN abundances of helium-4 (mass fraction Y_p) and deuterium (relative abundance D/H) across a neutron lifetime range of 840–1050 s. They performed calculations with two different nuclear reaction rate compilations: NACRE II and PRIMAT, while fixing the baryon-to-photon ratio to the CMB value of η = (6.105 ± 0.057) × 10⁻¹⁰ and the effective number of relativistic neutrino species to N_eff = 3.044. The resulting abundance curves were compared to observed primordial abundances from the Particle Data Group (PDG) and a recent Subaru Telescope measurement.

## Key numbers

- Neutron lifetime from bottle experiments: τ_n = 878.4 ± 0.5 s
- Neutron lifetime from beam experiments: τ_n = 888.45 ± 1.65 s
- Neutron lifetime from space-based measurements: τ_n = 887 ± 14 s
- PDG average primordial helium-4 mass fraction: Y_p^PDG = 0.245 ± 0.003
- Subaru Telescope primordial helium-4 measurement: Y_p^Subaru = 0.2379^{+0.0031}_{-0.0030}
- PDG average primordial deuterium abundance: D/H^PDG = (25.47 ± 0.25) × 10⁻⁶
- CMB baryon-to-photon ratio: η_CMB = (6.105 ± 0.057) × 10⁻¹⁰
- Neutron freeze-out temperature: T_n ~ 0.75 MeV
- Time at nucleosynthesis start: t_NS ~ 200 s

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Mathews (2004) and Salvati (2015) that used BBN to extract the neutron lifetime, now incorporating the newest neutron lifetime measurements, updated BBN observations, and the latest nuclear reaction rate compilations from NACRE II (Xu, 2013) and PRIMAT (Pitrou, 2020). The results highlight that the discrepancy between bottle and beam neutron lifetime measurements could be probed through BBN, and that the recent lower helium-4 measurement from the Subaru Telescope (Matsumoto, 2022) would require a much shorter neutron lifetime if standard BBN parameters are assumed, motivating further study of both nuclear rates and possible new physics.
