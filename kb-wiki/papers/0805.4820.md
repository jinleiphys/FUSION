# Viscosity and thermodynamic properties of QGP in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the viscosity and thermodynamic properties of QGP at RHIC by employing the recently extracted equilibrium distribution functions from two hot QCD equations of state of $O(g^5)$ and $O(g^6\ln(1/g))$ respectively. After obtaining the temperature dependence of energy density, and entropy density, we focus our attention on the determination of shear viscosity for a rapidly expanding interacting plasma, as a function of temperature. We find that interactions significantly decrease the shear viscosity. They decrease the viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/{\mathcal S}$ as well.

# Viscosity and thermodynamic properties of QGP in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Interactions incorporated via hot QCD equations of state of O(g⁵) and O(g⁶ln(1/g)) reduce the shear viscosity and the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio η/S compared to ideal-gas expectations, but the reduction is modest (approximately 7% for EOS1 and 4% for EOS2 near 2T_c) rather than dramatic; the ratio η/S can fall below the AdS/CFT bound of 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08 only if the jet quenching parameter q̂_R exceeds about 1 GeV²/fm, and such violations are marginal near 2T_c.

## Method

The authors employed equilibrium distribution functions for quasi-gluons and quasi-quarks with effective fugacities z_g and z_q, previously extracted from two hot QCD equations of state (EOS1 of O(g⁵) and EOS2 of O(g⁶ln(1/g))), to compute the temperature dependence of energy density and entropy density. They then generalized the transport-theory approach of Asakawa, Müller, and Bass (2006) by replacing ideal parton distributions with these interacting distributions, and determined the anomalous (Vlasov) and collisional contributions to shear viscosity via a variational minimization of the linearized transport equation including both diffusive Vlasov dynamics and a collision term from Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000).

## Key numbers

- The energy density ε/T⁴ for pure gauge theory approaches the ideal value only asymptotically; for EOS2 the agreement with lattice results is good beyond 2.5 T_c.
- The relative gluon viscosity R_g ≡ η_A^g / η_A^{I,g} can reduce to about 70% of the ideal value (i.e., a reduction of up to ~30%) as a function of temperature.
- The relative quark viscosity R_q can fall by as much as 80% compared to the ideal value.
- For pure gauge theory with q̂_R = 1 GeV²/fm, η/S near 2T_c is close to the AdS/CFT lower bound of 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08 but does not violate it.
- For q̂_R = 10 GeV²/fm, η/S can fall significantly below 0.08 even at 3T_c, but the authors note this large q̂_R may not be accommodated within the weak-coupling framework.
- The reduction in η/S near 2T_c is approximately 7% for EOS1 and 4% for EOS2 relative to the ideal-gas values.
- The QCD transition temperature T_c was taken as 270 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the transport-theory approach of Asakawa, Müller, and Bass (2006) by incorporating realistic hot QCD equations of state (EOS1 from Zhai (1995) and EOS2 from Kajantie (2003)) rather than ideal parton distributions, building on the quasiparticle mapping developed by Chandra et al. (2007, 2008). The results are compared with lattice QCD data from Karsch (2002) and with the AdS/CFT lower bound on η/S from Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001). The findings support the near-perfect-fluid picture of the quark-gluon plasma created at RHIC and enable future studies using lattice-based equations of state rather than perturbative ones.
