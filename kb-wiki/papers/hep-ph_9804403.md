# Color-Flavor Locking and Chiral Symmetry Breaking in High Density QCD

> We propose a symmetry breaking scheme for QCD with three massless quarks at high baryon density wherein the color and flavor SU(3)_color times SU(3)_L times SU(3)_R symmetries are broken down to the diagonal subgroup SU(3)_{color+L+R} by the formation of a condensate of quark Cooper pairs. We discuss general properties that follow from this hypothesis, including the existence of gaps for quark and gluon excitations, the existence of Nambu-Goldstone bosons which are excitations of the diquark condensate, and the existence of a modified electromagnetic gauge interaction which is unbroken and which assigns integral charge to the elementary excitations. We present mean-field results for a Hamiltonian in which the interaction between quarks is modelled by that induced by single-gluon exchange. We find gaps of order 10-100 MeV for plausible values of the coupling. We discuss the effects of nonzero temperature, nonzero quark masses and instanton-induced interactions on our results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that in QCD with three massless quarks at high baryon density, the color and flavor symmetries SU(3)_color × SU(3)_L × SU(3)_R are broken to the diagonal subgroup SU(3)_{color+L+R} by a condensate of quark Cooper pairs, with the hedge that this symmetry breaking pattern is energetically favorable only at densities high enough that the ordinary quark-antiquark condensate vanishes, and that the strange quark mass being zero is a reasonable starting point for estimating the gap magnitude.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a four-fermion interaction having the color, flavor, and spinor structure of single-gluon exchange, including a schematic form factor with either smoothed-step or power-law profiles. They solve the mean-field gap equations via the Bogoliubov-Valatin approach, rewriting the Hamiltonian in terms of Weyl spinors and diagonalizing the 9×9 color-flavor matrices to find two gap parameters Δ_1 and Δ_8. The coupling constant K is calibrated by calculating the zero-density chiral gap in an appendix.

## Key numbers

* Gaps of order 10-100 MeV for plausible values of the coupling, found in mean-field results for a Hamiltonian modelling single-gluon exchange.
* The strange quark mass m_s is of order 100 MeV, which is small compared to chemical potentials of interest.
* The condensate involves two gap parameters Δ_1 and Δ_8, where eight of the nine quarks have a gap parameter given by Δ_8 and the remaining linear combination has gap parameter Δ_1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier two-flavor analysis (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek, 1998) where color symmetry was broken to SU(2) with a condensate antisymmetric in color and flavor, and contrasts with the single-flavor model studied by Iwasaki and Iwado. The proposed color-flavor locked phase enables predictions for neutron star phenomenology including exponentially suppressed neutrino emission, and provides a framework for studying the phase diagram of dense QCD with three flavors, including the effects of nonzero temperature, quark masses, and instanton-induced interactions.
