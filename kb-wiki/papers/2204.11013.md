# The relationship of the neutron-skin thickness to the symmetry energy and its slope

> The neutron-skin thickness of asymmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter is shown to be a function of Coulomb energy, the asymmetry-energy coefficient($J$), the slope($L$) of the asymmetry energy, and the incompressibility coefficient, in addition to he Fermi momentum and the asymmetry parameter. The relational formula is derived on the basis of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem in the mean-field approximation for nuclear matter. Using the formula as a guide, the neutron-skin thickness($δR$) in $^{208}$Pb is examined. The linear correlation between $L$ and $δR$ appears as a kind of spurious ones through the model-dependent correlation of $L$ with $J$ which is included in the main components of the formula.

## Key claim

The neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb is shown to depend primarily on the asymmetry-energy coefficient J rather than on its slope L, and the widely reported linear correlation between L and δR is argued to be a spurious one arising from the model-dependent correlation between L and J.

## Method

The author derives an analytic formula for the neutron-skin thickness of asymmetric semi-infinite nuclear matter (δR_M) using the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem in the mean-field approximation, expanding the nucleon densities to second order in the Coulomb energy V_c. The resulting expression is expressed in terms of the Fermi momentum k_F, the asymmetry parameter I, the Coulomb energy V_c, the asymmetry-energy coefficient J, the slope L of the asymmetry energy, and the incompressibility coefficient K. The formula is then applied to analyze δR_0 (the flat-part contribution to the neutron-skin thickness) in 208Pb for 11 relativistic and 9 non-relativistic mean-field models, using Fermi-type fits to the calculated density distributions from Ref. [ks2].

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the Coulomb energy used is V_c = 22.144 MeV (with r_c = 1.350 fm).
- In the relativistic mean-field models, δR_0 ranges from 0.11804 fm (FSU model) to 0.21358 fm (NL1 model); the L-dependent term δR_L contributes between 2.15% and 7.17% of δR_0.
- In the non-relativistic mean-field models, δR_0 ranges from 0.04955 fm (SKI model) to 0.13833 fm (Ska model); the L-dependent term δR_L contributes between 1.49% and 7.44% of δR_0.
- The diffuseness contribution δR_a is about 0.090 ± 0.015 fm in the RMF models and 0.070 ± 0.010 fm in the SMF models.
- The experimental fourth moment of the charge distribution Q^4_c in 208Pb is 1171.981(17.627) fm^4; from the least-squares analysis, the extracted R^2_n is 32.943(2.934) fm^2.

## Context

This work extends the semi-infinite nuclear matter approach of Brink (1970s) and the recent numerical studies by Roca-Maza et al. (2011, 2015) and others that found a linear correlation between L and δR in 208Pb using many nuclear models. The author challenges the interpretation of that correlation by showing analytically that the dominant contribution to δR comes from J and the Coulomb term, with the L-term contributing less than 10%. This implies that even if δR is measured experimentally (e.g., from parity-violating electron scattering at JLab), the value of L remains model-dependent and cannot be uniquely determined without additional constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
