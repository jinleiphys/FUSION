# Microscopic Description of Super Heavy Nuclei

> The results of extensive microscopic Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations for the nuclei appearing in the alpha - decay chains of recently discovered superheavy elements with Z = 109 to 118 are presented and discussed. The calculated ground state properties like total binding energies, Q values, deformations, radii and densities closely agree with the corresponding experimental data, where available. The double folding (t-rho-rho) approximation is used to calculate the interaction potential between the daughter and the alpha, using RMF densities along with the density dependent nucleon - nucleon interaction (M3Y). This in turn, is employed within the WKB approximation to estimate the half lives without any additional parameter for alpha - decay. The half lives are highly sensitive to the Q values used and qualitatively agree with the corresponding experimental values. The use of experimental Q values in the WKB approximation improves the agreement with the experiment, indicating that the resulting interaction potential is reliable and can be used with confidence as the real part of the optical potential in other scattering and reaction processes.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations, using the NL3 parameter set and a constant-gap BCS treatment of pairing, reproduce ground-state binding energies, Q-values, and deformations for superheavy nuclei in the alpha-decay chains of Z=109-118, but the resulting alpha-decay half-lives are highly sensitive to the Q-values used and only qualitatively agree with experiment; using experimental Q-values in the WKB approximation significantly improves the half-life agreement.

## Method

The authors solved the RMF equations in an axially symmetric deformed oscillator basis using the NL3 Lagrangian parameter set, with spherical pairing gaps tuned to reproduce pairing energies from RHB calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction. They then used the calculated RMF densities in a double-folding model with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain the alpha-daughter interaction potential, and employed this potential within the WKB approximation to estimate alpha-decay half-lives without any additional free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for 294 118: -7.11 MeV (NL3 calculation)
- Binding energy per nucleon for 277 112: -7.24 MeV (NL3) vs. -7.20 MeV (Audi2003)
- Quadrupole deformation parameter beta for 294 118: 0.01 (NL3) vs. -0.09 (Moller-Nix)
- Q_alpha for 294 118: 11.18 MeV (NL3) vs. 11.81 MeV (experimental)
- log10(T_alpha) for 294 118: -1.67 s (NL3), -3.21 s (Ex+WKB using experimental Q), vs. -2.74 s (experimental)
- log10(T_alpha) for 277 112: -3.02 s (NL3), -4.66 s (Ex+WKB), vs. -3.55 s (experimental)
- Root-mean-square radius constant r_0 = 0.9639 +/- 0.0005 fm from the A^(1/3) law fit

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of superheavy nuclei by Lalazissis et al. (1996) and others, who predicted shell closures at N=162 and stability around Z=112, N=166. The authors go beyond previous ground-state property calculations by using the RMF densities to compute alpha-decay half-lives via the double-folding and WKB method, enabling comparison with experimental decay chains from GSI, Dubna, RIKEN, and LBNL. The demonstrated reliability of the resulting interaction potential opens the possibility of using it as the real part of the optical potential in other scattering and reaction processes involving superheavy nuclei.
