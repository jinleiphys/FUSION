# The omega rho pi coupling in the VMD model revisited

> We determine the value of the ω-ρ- πmesons coupling (g_{ωρπ}), in the context of the vector meson dominance model, from radiative decays, the ω\rightarrow 3πdecay width and the e^+e^- \rightarrow 3πcross section. For the last two observables we consider the effect of either a heavier resonance (ρ'(1450)) or a contact term. A weighted average of the results from the set of observables yields g_{ωρπ} =14.7 \pm 0.1 GeV^{-1} in absence of those contributions, and g_{ωρπ} =11.9 \pm 0.2 GeV^{-1} or g_{ωρπ} =11.7 \pm 0.1 GeV^{-1} when including the ρ' or contact term respectively. The inclusion of these additional terms makes the estimates from the different observables to lay in a more reduced range. Improved measurements of these observables and the ρ'(1450) meson parameters are needed to give a definite answer on the pertinence of the inclusion of this last one in the considered processes.

## Key claim

The value of the omega-rho-pi coupling depends on whether additional contributions (a heavier rho'(1450) resonance or a contact term) are included in the analysis of the omega to 3pi decay and the e+e- to 3pi cross section; the weighted average yields g_{omega rho pi} = 14.7 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1} without those contributions, but drops to 11.9 +/- 0.2 GeV^{-1} or 11.7 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1} when including the rho' or contact term respectively.

## Method

The authors determined the omega-rho-pi coupling in the vector meson dominance (VMD) model from three sets of observables: radiative decays (rho- to pi- gamma, rho0 to pi0 gamma, omega to pi0 gamma, and pi0 to gamma gamma), the omega to 3pi decay width, and the e+e- to 3pi cross section. For the omega to 3pi decay and the e+e- to 3pi cross section, they considered two scenarios: including a contact term fixed by the Wess-Zumino-Witten anomaly and the KSFR relation, or including a rho'(1450) resonance with its propagator approximated as an effective contact term. They performed weighted averages of the coupling values extracted from each observable under each scenario.

## Key numbers

- Weighted average g_{omega rho pi} = 14.7 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1} when only the rho channel is considered (no contact term or rho').
- Weighted average g_{omega rho pi} = 11.9 +/- 0.2 GeV^{-1} when including the rho'(1450) contribution.
- Weighted average g_{omega rho pi} = 11.7 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1} when including the contact term.
- From radiative decays alone, weighted average g_{omega rho pi} = 11.9 +/- 0.2 GeV^{-1}, with individual values: rho- to pi- gamma = 11.3 +/- 0.9 GeV^{-1}, rho0 to pi0 gamma = 13.1 +/- 0.9 GeV^{-1}, omega to pi0 gamma = 11.4 +/- 0.2 GeV^{-1}, pi0 to gamma gamma = 12.8 +/- 0.3 GeV^{-1}.
- From the omega to 3pi decay width (experimental value 7.56 +/- 0.13 MeV), the coupling required to reproduce the width with only the rho channel is g_{omega rho pi} = 15.7 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1}; with the contact term it becomes 12.8 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1}; with the rho' it becomes 12.6 +/- 1.3 GeV^{-1}.
- From fits to the e+e- to 3pi cross section (CMD2 and SND data), the coupling with only the rho channel is 13.3 +/- 0.2 GeV^{-1}; with the contact term it is 10.5 +/- 0.1 GeV^{-1}; with the rho' it is 10.3 +/- 1.6 GeV^{-1}.
- The contact term coupling g_{3pi} from the WZW anomaly and KSFR relation is -47 GeV^{-3}; the value obtained blindly from the omega to 3pi width is -62 +/- 7 GeV^{-3} or +409 +/- 10 GeV^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous determinations of the omega-rho-pi coupling within the VMD model by systematically comparing results from radiative decays, the omega to 3pi decay, and the e+e- to 3pi cross section, while exploring the role of additional contributions. The authors compare their results with estimates from other approaches: QCD sum rules (Khathimovsky 1985, Margvelashvili 1988) giving values around 9 GeV^{-1} or 15-17 GeV^{-1}; a quark-level linear sigma model (Lucio) giving 10.3-14.7 GeV^{-1}; and a Dyson-Schwinger framework (Maris 2003) giving 10.3 GeV^{-1}. The analysis shows that including a contact term or the rho'(1450) resonance reduces the spread of coupling values extracted from different observables, but improved measurements of the observables and the rho'(1450) parameters are needed to settle whether these additional contributions are required.
