# Spectral distribution Method for neutrinoless double beta decay: Results for $^{82}$Se and $^{76}$Ge

> Statistical spectral distribution method based on shell model and random matrix theory is developed for calculating neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear transition matrix elements. First results obtained for $^{82}$Se and $^{76}$Ge using the spectral method are close to the available shell model results.

## Key claim

The spectral distribution method (SDM), based on shell model and random matrix theory, yields neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear transition matrix elements for 82Se and 76Ge that are close to available large-scale shell model results, but the agreement depends on the chosen values of the correlation coefficient (zeta between 0.6 and 0.8) and the spin cut-off factor (sigma_J between 3 and 6), which are not uniquely determined by the theory.

## Method

The authors developed a statistical spectral distribution method for calculating the nuclear transition matrix element (NTME) for neutrinoless double beta decay, treating the decay operator as a two-body transition operator and applying embedded Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (EGOE) theory to obtain bivariate Gaussian transition strength densities. They computed configuration centroids, variances, and configuration-averaged squared matrix elements of the transition operator using the JUN45 effective interaction in the f5/2pg9/2 shell model space with a 56Ni core, then applied angular momentum decomposition via spin cut-off factors to extract the ground-state-to-ground-state matrix elements for 82Se decaying to 82Kr and 76Ge decaying to 76Se.

## Key numbers

- For 82Se to 82Kr, the SDM NTME values for zeta=0.6 and sigma_J=3, 4, 5, 6 are 1, 1.54, 2.15, and 2.83 respectively; for zeta=0.7 and sigma_J=3, 4, 5, 6 they are 1.38, 2.12, 2.97, and 3.9 respectively; for zeta=0.8 and sigma_J=3, 4, 5, 6 they are 1.78, 2.74, 3.82, and 5.03 respectively.
- The shell model value for 82Se from Horoi et al. using JUN45 interaction is 2.59; the shell model value from Poves et al. is approximately 2.18.
- For 76Ge to 76Se, the SDM NTME values for zeta=0.65 and sigma_J=3, 4, 5, 6 are 1.02, 1.56, 2.19, and 2.87 respectively; for zeta=0.7 and sigma_J=3, 4, 5, 6 they are 1.29, 1.98, 2.77, and 3.63 respectively; for zeta=0.8 and sigma_J=3, 4, 5, 6 they are 1.96, 3.01, 4.21, and 5.54 respectively.
- The shell model result for 76Ge from Horoi et al. using JUN45 interaction is 2.72; the shell model value from Poves et al. is 2.3.
- The average width for 82Se configurations is 3.34 MeV with 9% fluctuation; for 82Kr it is 4.7 MeV with 5% fluctuation.
- The average width for 76Ge configurations is 4.4 MeV with 6% fluctuation; for 76Se it is 5.51 MeV with 4% fluctuation.
- The total transition strength sum is 31239 for 82Se and 54178 for 76Ge.

## Context

This work extends the spectral distribution method developed by French and collaborators, previously applied to nuclear structure and transition strengths, to the calculation of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements. The results are compared with large-scale shell model calculations by Horoi et al. (using JUN45 interaction) and Poves et al. for 82Se and 76Ge, and the SDM values with zeta approximately 0.7-0.8 and sigma_J approximately 4 are close to those shell model results. The method enables rapid calculation of NTME for candidate nuclei where full shell model diagonalization is computationally prohibitive, with planned applications to 124Sn, 130Te, and 136Xe, and can provide total strength sums and energy-weighted sum rules to constrain other nuclear models.
