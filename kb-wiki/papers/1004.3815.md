# Thermodynamics and Phase Structure of the Two-Flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model Beyond Large-N_c

> The optimized perturbation theory (OPT) method is applied to the $SU(2)$ version of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model both at zero and at finite temperature and/or density. At the first nontrivial order the OPT exhibits a class of 1/N_c corrections which produce nonperturbative results that go beyond the standard large-N_c, or mean-field approximation. The consistency of the OPT method with the Goldstone theorem at this order is established, and appropriate OPT values of the basic NJL (vacuum) parameters are obtained by matching the pion mass and decay constant consistently. Deviations from standard large-N_c relations induced by OPT at this order are derived, for example, for the Gell--Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. Next, the results for the critical quantities and the phase diagram of the model, as well as a number of other thermodynamical quantities of interest, are obtained with OPT and then contrasted with the corresponding results at large N_c.

## Key claim

The optimized perturbation theory (OPT) method, applied at first nontrivial order to the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, captures a class of 1/N_c corrections beyond the standard large-N_c (mean-field) approximation without introducing new effective parameters beyond those already present in the mean-field picture, though the method is a different approximation scheme than the 1/N expansion and also partly includes corrections belonging formally to higher 1/N order.

## Method

The authors deformed the NJL Lagrangian density by introducing a fictitious expansion parameter δ and an arbitrary mass parameter η, then evaluated the Landau free energy density to first order in δ using OPT-dressed fermion propagators. The optimization (fixing η) was performed via the principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS), and the resulting gap equation and pion mass/decay constant were derived consistently with the Goldstone theorem at this order. Numerical results for the phase diagram and thermodynamic quantities were obtained by solving the coupled PMS and gap equations at finite temperature and/or chemical potential, using a sharp three-momentum cutoff Λ for regularization.

## Key numbers

- The OPT relation between the variational mass η and the scalar condensate σ_c at μ=0 is η̄ = σ̄_c × (1 + 1/(2 N_f N_c)), where N_f=2 and N_c=3, giving a 1/N_c correction factor of 1 + 1/12 ≈ 1.0833 relative to the large-N_c result η̄ = σ̄_c.
- The OPT vacuum mass gap equation is M_q^OPT = m_c + 4 G N_f N_c × M I_2(0,0)|_{η̄+m_c=M}, where M ≡ η̄+m_c = M_q^OPT + (M_q^OPT - m_c)/(2 N_f N_c), modifying the large-N_c relation M_q^LN = m_c + 4 G N_f N_c M_q^LN I_2(0,0).
- The inverse pion propagator at q²=0 at first OPT order is 1 - 8 i G N_f N_c I_G(m) + 16 N_f N_c (n_π-1) G² I_G²(m), with n_π=3, which vanishes upon using the perturbatively expanded OPT gap equation 1 - 8 i G N_f N_c I_G(η̄) + 32 N_f N_c G² I_G²(η̄) = 0 in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the NJL model beyond the large-N_c (mean-field) approximation, which were technically involved and often required extra parameters (e.g., NLO 1/N_c calculations by Oertel and others). The OPT method had been successfully applied to Gross-Neveu models (Kneur, Pinto, Ramos, 2007) and scalar theories, and here it is adapted to the SU(2) NJL model to provide a more complete thermodynamic description including the trace anomaly, specific heat, and quark susceptibility. The results enable quantitative comparison of 1/N_c corrections to the phase diagram and critical quantities, and may help locate critical points relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments.
