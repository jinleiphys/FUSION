# Nuclear Saturation with Low Momentum Interactions

> Relativistic effects are investigated in nuclear matter calculations employing renormalized low-momentum nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions. It is demonstrated that the relativistic effects cure a problem of non-relativistic low-momentum interactions, which fail to reproduce saturation of nuclear matter. Including relativistic effects, one already obtains saturation in a Hartree-Fock calculation. Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations lead to a further improvement of the saturation properties. The results are rather insensitive to the realistic $NN$ interaction on which they are based.

## Key claim

Including relativistic effects by dressing the Dirac spinors of the underlying realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction cures the failure of non-relativistic low-momentum interactions to reproduce nuclear matter saturation, and this result is rather insensitive to the specific realistic interaction used, though some quantitative model dependence remains.

## Method

The authors construct a density-dependent low-momentum interaction \(V_{lowk}(\rho)\) by first evaluating the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction using Dirac spinors dressed with medium properties obtained from a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state, then applying the unitary-model-operator approach to separate low- and high-momentum components with a cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\). They perform Hartree-Fock (HF) and Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using this density-dependent interaction, comparing results across the Bonn A, B, and C potentials.

## Key numbers

* HF calculation with density-dependent \(V_{lowk}(\rho)\) based on Bonn A yields a saturation energy of about -11 MeV per nucleon.
* BHF calculation with density-dependent \(V_{lowk}(\rho)\) yields about 2 MeV more binding in the saturation region than the corresponding HF calculation.
* HF calculation using Bonn A as underlying interaction gives about 2.2 MeV stronger binding at saturation than the one using Bonn C.
* The model dependence of the relativistic \(V_{lowk}\) interactions is much weaker than the corresponding difference of 8 MeV per nucleon obtained in non-relativistic BHF calculations using directly Bonn A and C potentials.
* All calculations use a cutoff \(\Lambda\) of 2 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the low-momentum interaction approach of Bogner (2001, 2005) and Bozek (2006), which fails to produce nuclear matter saturation without three-body forces, by incorporating relativistic effects from the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework of Brockmann and Müther. It contrasts with the approach of Siu (2009), who increased the cutoff and added ring diagrams and Brown-Rho scaling, and instead shows that relativistic dressing of Dirac spinors alone can restore saturation while preserving the perturbative nature of low-momentum interactions. This opens the door for studying finite nuclei using realistic interactions with perturbative correlation treatments.
