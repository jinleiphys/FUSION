# Low lying S=-1 excited baryons and chiral symmetry

> The s-wave meson-baryon interaction in the $S = -1$ sector is studied by means of coupled-channels, using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian and the N/D method to implement unitarity. The loops are regularized using dimensional renormalization. In addition to the previously studied $Λ(1405)$, employing this chiral approach leads to the dynamical generation of two more s-wave hyperon resonances, the $Λ(1670)$ and $Σ(1620)$ states. We make comparisons with experimental data and look for poles in the complex plane obtaining the couplings of the resonances to the different final states. This allows us to identify the $Λ(1405)$ and the $Λ(1670)$ resonances with $\bar{K}N$ and $KΞ$ quasibound states, respectively.

## Key claim

Using the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian with the N/D method and dimensional regularization, the authors dynamically generate the Λ(1405), Λ(1670), and Σ(1620) resonances as s-wave meson-baryon states, identifying the Λ(1405) and Λ(1670) as quasibound \(\bar{K}N\) and \(K\Xi\) states respectively, though the agreement with experimental branching ratios for the Λ(1670) is only qualitative without parameter tuning.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for ten coupled channels (\(\bar{K}N\), \(\pi\Sigma\), \(\pi\Lambda\), \(\eta\Lambda\), \(\eta\Sigma\), \(K\Xi\)) using the on-shell factorization of the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian amplitudes, with the loop function \(G_l\) regularized via dimensional renormalization. The subtraction constants \(a_l\) are initially fixed to reproduce the cut-off regularization results of Oset and Ramos (1998) at the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold, and then the \(a_{K\Xi}\) parameter is varied to fine-tune the Λ(1670) position. Poles are searched for in the complex plane on the second Riemann sheet to extract resonance masses, widths, and couplings.

## Key numbers

- Λ(1405) pole: \(M_R = (1426 + i16)\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 32\) MeV, with couplings \(|g_{\bar{K}N}|^2 = 7.4\), \(|g_{\pi\Sigma}|^2 = 2.3\), \(|g_{\eta\Lambda}|^2 = 2.0\), \(|g_{K\Xi}|^2 = 0.12\) (using \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.52\)).
- Λ(1670) pole with \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.52\): \(M_R = 1708 + i21\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 42\) MeV, branching ratios \(B_{\bar{K}N} = 47\%\), \(B_{\eta\Lambda} = 47\%\), \(B_{\pi\Sigma} = 6\%\).
- Λ(1670) pole with \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.67\): \(M_R = 1680 + i20\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 40\) MeV, branching ratios \(B_{\bar{K}N} = 54\%\), \(B_{\eta\Lambda} = 38\%\), \(B_{\pi\Sigma} = 8\%\).
- Λ(1670) couplings with \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.52\): \(|g_{\bar{K}N}|^2 = 0.51\), \(|g_{\pi\Sigma}|^2 = 0.052\), \(|g_{\eta\Lambda}|^2 = 1.0\), \(|g_{K\Xi}|^2 = 11\).
- Λ(1670) couplings with \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.67\): \(|g_{\bar{K}N}|^2 = 0.61\), \(|g_{\pi\Sigma}|^2 = 0.073\), \(|g_{\eta\Lambda}|^2 = 1.1\), \(|g_{K\Xi}|^2 = 12\).
- Σ(1620) pole with \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.67\): \(M_R = (1579 + i264)\) MeV, \(\Gamma \sim 528\) MeV.
- Speed plot analysis of Λ(1670) with \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.67\): \(M_R = 1679\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 40\) MeV, \(B_{\bar{K}N} = 61\%\), \(B_{\eta\Lambda} = 30\%\), \(B_{\pi\Sigma} = 9\%\).
- Predicted cross section for \(K^- p \to K^+ \Xi^-\) at 1.6 GeV/c: 0.17 mb (experimental: 0.16–0.18 mb).
- Predicted cross section for \(K^- p \to K^0 \Xi^0\) at 1.6 GeV/c: 0.24 mb (experimental: 0.08–0.1 mb).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Oset and Ramos (1998), which used a cut-off regularization and only studied the Λ(1405), by applying the N/D method with dimensional regularization (following Oller and Meissner, 2001) to search for higher-energy resonances. The dynamical generation of the Λ(1670) and Σ(1620) as meson-baryon quasibound states challenges quark-model classifications that treat these as SU(3) octet members, and the identification of the Λ(1670) as a \(K\Xi\) quasibound state provides a new interpretation that can be tested in future experiments on \(K\Xi\) production and decays.
