# Spectroscopic factors as a probe of nuclear shape in $^{44}$S via one-neutron knockout reaction

> Background: Neutron-rich nucleus $^{44}$S lies in the region where traditional $N=28$ shell closure weakens, leading to the emergence of shape coexistence and large-amplitude collective motion (LACM). Understanding the nature and degree of shape mixing in this nucleus remains an important and fascinating problem. Purpose: We investigate the manifestation of shape fluctuations in $^{44}$S and examine how the electric transitions and the spectroscopic factors from one-neutron knockout reactions can serve as probes of shapes mixing. Method: The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with the generator coordinate method (AMD+GCM) is used to study the structure of $^{44}$S and $^{43}$S. Calculations are performed by using Gogny effective interactions with two different parameter sets, D1S and D1M, to explore the interaction dependence of shape mixing. Monopole and quadrupole transition strengths and spectroscopic factors are evaluated. The cross sections for the $^{44}$S$(p,pn)^{43}$S reaction are calculated within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA). Results: The calculations reveal a strong interaction dependence of shape fluctuation in $^{44}$S. The structural differences obtained from D1S and D1M interactions produce distinct patterns of the electric transitions, the spectroscopic factors, and the cross sections for $^{44}$S$(p,pn)^{43}$S knockout reaction. Conclusion: The population of $3/2^-$ and $7/2^-$ states of $^{43}$S is particularly sensitive to the underlying shape fluctuation in $^{44}$S. Thus, the measurement of $^{44}$S$(p,pn)^{43}$S reaction can provide a direct experimental probe.

## Key claim
The paper argues that spectroscopic factors from the one-neutron knockout reaction \(^{44}\mathrm{S}(p,pn)^{43}\mathrm{S}\) can serve as a direct experimental probe of shape mixing in \(^{44}\mathrm{S}\), but only if the effective interaction (Gogny D1S versus D1M) is correctly chosen, because the two interactions predict qualitatively different patterns of shape fluctuation (large-amplitude collective motion versus rigid prolate deformation) in the ground state of \(^{44}\mathrm{S}\).

## Method
The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with the generator coordinate method (AMD+GCM) to calculate the structure of \(^{44}\mathrm{S}\) and \(^{43}\mathrm{S}\) with Gogny D1S and D1M effective interactions. They evaluated electric monopole and quadrupole transition strengths and spectroscopic factors for one-neutron knockout, and computed cross sections for the \(^{44}\mathrm{S}(p,pn)^{43}\mathrm{S}\) reaction within the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) at 250 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- \(\rho^2(E0, 0_1^+ \rightarrow 0_2^+)\): \(13.3 \times 10^{-3}\) (D1S) vs \(4.4 \times 10^{-3}\) (D1M); experimental value \(8.7(7) \times 10^{-3}\) from Force (2010).
- \(\rho^2(E0, 2_1^+ \rightarrow 2_2^+)\): \(3.2 \times 10^{-3}\) (D1S) vs \(10.7 \times 10^{-3}\) (D1M).
- \(B(E2, 0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)\): 318 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (D1S) vs 405 e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) (D1M); experimental values 314(88) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) from Glasmacher (1997) and 221(28) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) from Longfellow (2021).
- Spectroscopic factor \(C^2S\) for the \(7/2_1^-\) (triaxial) state of \(^{43}\mathrm{S}\): 1.45 (D1S) vs 0.25 (D1M).
- Spectroscopic factor \(C^2S\) for the \(3/2_2^-\) (oblate) state of \(^{43}\mathrm{S}\): 0.32 (D1S) vs 0.06 (D1M).
- Integrated cross section \(\sigma^{\text{AMD}}\) for the \(7/2_1^-\) state: 6.37 mb (D1S) vs 1.06 mb (D1M).

## Context
This work extends previous AMD+GCM studies by Suzuki and Kimura (2021, 2022) that identified large-amplitude collective motion (LACM) in \(^{44}\mathrm{S}\), and earlier experimental work by Force (2010) and Santiago-Gonzalez (2011) that established shape coexistence in \(^{44}\mathrm{S}\). The paper shows that the choice of effective interaction (D1S vs D1M) leads to qualitatively different predictions for shape mixing, and that the \(^{44}\mathrm{S}(p,pn)^{43}\mathrm{S}\) reaction can discriminate between these scenarios. The results motivate a direct measurement of this reaction to clarify the role of shape mixing, complementing existing knockout data from a \(^{9}\mathrm{Be}\) target reported by Momiyama (2023).
