# Rotational stability of magnetic field in rotating quark-gluon plasma

> Relaxation of the magnetic field in rigidly rotating quark-gluon plasma is studied. It is shown that the infrared modes satisfying $k < |m|Ω$ and $k< |m\pm 1|Ω$, where integer $m$ is the projection of the orbital angular momentum along the rotating axis and $Ω$ is the angular velocity, are unstable. The instability onset time and the magnetic field growth rate are computed for a standard initial profile of the magnetic field. Given the present phenomenological values of $Ω$ and electrical conductivity $σ$ the instability is not expected to be a significant factor in the field's time evolution.

# Rotational stability of magnetic field in rotating quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Infrared magnetic field modes with wavenumber \(k < |m+\kappa|\Omega\) (where \(m\) is the orbital angular momentum projection and \(\kappa = 0,\pm1\) distinguishes field components) are unstable in rigidly rotating quark-gluon plasma, but given the phenomenological values of angular velocity \(\Omega\) and electrical conductivity \(\sigma\) in heavy-ion collisions, the instability is not expected to be a significant factor in the field's time evolution.

## Method

The authors derived the evolution equation for the magnetic field in a rigidly rotating plasma by combining Maxwell's equations with Ohm's law \(\mathbf{J} = \sigma(\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{v} \times \mathbf{B})\) and the velocity field \(\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{\Omega} \times \mathbf{r}\). They separated variables in cylindrical coordinates, expanded the field in Bessel-Fourier modes, and obtained the dispersion relation \(\omega^2 + i\sigma\omega - k^2 - i\sigma(m+\kappa)\Omega = 0\). The instability condition was derived from the sign of the imaginary part of the eigenfrequencies, and the time evolution was computed numerically for a Gaussian initial magnetic field profile representing the field from two colliding ultrarelativistic heavy ions.

## Key numbers

- Instability condition: modes with \(k < |m+\kappa|\,\Omega\) are unstable, independent of electrical conductivity \(\sigma\).
- For realistic quark-gluon plasma parameters, the instability onset time scales as \(\tau (\text{fm}) \approx 20 / \Omega(\text{MeV})\).
- Electrical conductivity used in numerical calculations: \(\sigma = 5.8\) MeV.
- Realistic angular velocity in heavy-ion collisions does not exceed about 20 MeV; the instability would require \(\Omega\) on the order of a few GeV to become phenomenologically relevant within the plasma lifetime of about 10 fm/c.
- Gaussian initial field parameters: \(\Delta_x = \Delta_z = 10\) fm, \(\Delta_y = \Delta_x / \gamma\) with Lorentz factor \(\gamma = 10\).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field evolution in quark-gluon plasma (Tuchin 2013, Tuchin 2015, Stewart 2017) by incorporating the effect of rigid rotation, which is known to destabilize electromagnetic fields in other contexts (Velikhov 1959, Chandrasekhar 1960, Balbus 1991). The instability is related to superradiance (Zel'dovich 1971, 1972). The authors find that for the modest angular velocities and short lifetimes of heavy-ion collision fireballs, the rotational instability does not significantly alter the magnetic field decay, but the formalism enables future studies of rapidly rotating plasmas where the light-cylinder boundary condition becomes important (Buzzegoli 2023).
