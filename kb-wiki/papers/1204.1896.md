# (Oscillating) non-exponential decays of unstable states

> We discuss deviations from the exponential decay law which originate when going beyond the Breit-Wigner distribution for an unstable state. In particular, we concentrate on an oscillating behavior, reminiscent of the Rabi-oscillations, in the short-time region. We propose that these oscillations can explain the GSI experiment, which measured superimposed oscillations on top of the exponential law for hydrogen-like nuclides decaying via electron-capture (the so-called GSI anomaly). Moreover, we discuss the possibility, that the deviations from the Breit-Wigner distribution in the case of the GSI anomaly are (predominantly) caused by the interaction of the unstable state with the measurement apparatus. The consequences of this scenario, such as the non-existence of oscillations in an analogous experiment performed at the Berkeley Lab, are investigated.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the oscillatory deviations from exponential decay observed in the GSI experiment on hydrogen-like ions decaying via electron capture can be explained by a cutoff in the energy distribution of the unstable state, where this cutoff may originate from the interaction of the unstable system with the measurement apparatus rather than from fundamental microphysics.

## Method
The authors construct a phenomenological model for the survival probability by taking a Breit-Wigner spectral function and introducing a low-energy cutoff Λ₁ and a high-energy cutoff Λ₂, then computing the survival amplitude as the Fourier transform of this truncated distribution. They connect the cutoff Λ to the experimental time resolution via the time-energy uncertainty relation, Λ ∼ 1/Δt_resolution, and compare the resulting oscillatory decay rate h(t) = -dp(t)/dt to the GSI data. The model is applied to the two-channel case of electron-capture and β⁺ decay, and to the Berkeley experiment where no oscillations were observed.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁴²Pm, the cutoff Λ ≃ 32Γ ≃ 0.5 × 10⁻¹⁵ eV, where Γ = 0.0224 s⁻¹ is the decay width of the state.
- The experimental time resolution at GSI is Δt_resolution ≃ 200 ms, giving Λ ∼ 1/Δt_resolution ∼ 10⁻¹⁵ eV.
- The ions take ∼0.5 μs to complete a turn in the storage ring.
- The time interval between disappearance of the mother ion frequency and appearance of the daughter ion frequency is 900 ms for ¹⁴⁰Pr and 1200 ms for ¹⁴²Pm.
- For ¹⁴⁰Pr and ¹⁴²Pm, the ratio Λ/Γ is ∼32 and ∼470 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of non-exponential decays from Quantum Mechanics (Ghirardi) and Quantum Field Theory (Giacosa 2010, Pagliara 2011) to explain the GSI anomaly reported by Litvinov (2008). The authors build on the concept of apparatus-induced cutoffs introduced by Degasperis (1974) in the paper "Does the Lifetime of an Unstable System Depend on the Measuring Apparatus," and contrast their explanation with alternative proposals involving neutrino oscillations, neutrino spin precession, and quantum beats (Cohen 2008, Gal, Merle 2010, Wu 2010). The work enables future detailed modeling of the measurement procedure and two-channel decay problems, and predicts that improved time resolution at GSI would suppress the oscillations.
