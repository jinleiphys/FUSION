# Employing ternary fission of $^{242}$Pu as a probe of very neutron rich matter

> Detailed assessments of the ability of recent theoretical approaches to modeling existing experimental data for ternary fission confirm earlier indications that the dominant mode of cluster formation in ternary fission is clusterization in very neutron rich, very low density, essentially chemically equilibrated, nucleonic matter. An extended study and comparison of these approaches applied to ternary fission yields in the thermal neutron induced reaction $^{241}$Pu($n_{\rm th}$,f) has been undertaken to refine the characterization of the source matter. The resonance gas approximation has been improved taking in-medium effects on the binding energies into account. A temperature of 1.29 MeV, density of $6.7 \times 10^{-5}$ nucleons/fm$^3$ and proton fraction $Y_p$ = 0.035 are found to provide a good representation of yields of the ternary emitted light particles and clusters. In particular, results for $Z= 1$ and 2 isotopes are presented. Isotopes with larger $Z$ are discussed, and the roles of medium and continuum effects, even at very low density are illustrated.

## Key claim

The dominant mode of cluster formation in ternary fission is clusterization in very neutron rich, very low density, essentially chemically equilibrated nucleonic matter, with the resonance gas approximation improved by taking in-medium effects on binding energies into account providing a good representation of yields for light particles and clusters.

## Method

The authors applied three successive approximations to model ternary fission yields from the thermal neutron induced reaction ²⁴¹Pu(n_th,f): (i) the resonance gas approximation (nuclear statistical equilibrium) treating the system as an ideal mixture of nuclei in ground and excited states; (ii) the virial approximation accounting for binary interactions via scattering phase shifts; and (iii) a full treatment including in-medium corrections such as self-energy shifts and Pauli blocking effects. They extracted Lagrange parameters (temperature, neutron chemical potential, proton chemical potential) by fitting calculated final yields to observed experimental yields using the Lestone fit metric, focusing on isotopes with Z ≤ 2 to avoid suppression effects for heavier isotopes.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: 1.29 MeV (specifically 1.2897 MeV in the resonance gas fit)
- Baryon density: 6.4 × 10⁻⁵ nucleons/fm³ (resonance gas approximation); 6.741 × 10⁻⁵ nucleons/fm³ (with medium effects)
- Proton fraction Y_p: 0.03486 (resonance gas); 0.03479 (with medium effects)
- Scission neutron yield: 0.107 per fission, approximately one thirtieth of the total neutron yield
- Scission proton yield upper limit: 2.9 to 4.0 × 10⁻⁵ per fission
- Total α particle emission yield per fission: 2.015 × 10⁻³
- Fit metric (Lestone) for resonance gas: 0.005485; for virial: 0.009384; for medium: 0.009574

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations by Natowitz et al. (2020) and Röpke et al. (2020, 2021) that used information entropy approaches and nuclear statistical equilibrium to characterize ternary fission sources, and builds on the time-dependent nucleation approach of Wuenschel et al. (Sara) and Lestone (2008). The results provide a laboratory test of the low-density nuclear equation of state for conditions similar to those encountered in core-collapse supernova events where a neo-neutron star forms and cools, and in binary neutron star mergers, where temperatures of about 1 MeV and densities of 10¹¹ g/cm³ are typical.
