# Partial Dynamical Symmetry in the f7/2 and g9/2 shell

> We discuss partial dynamical symmetries which occur in single j shell calculations mostly for high spin states for systems of three or four particles (holes). The relevant nuclei are 43Ti,43Sc, 44Ti, 52Fe,53Fe, 53Co,96Cd,97Cd, and 97In.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) occur in single-j shell calculations when all T=0 two-body matrix elements are set to zero, leading to selected states where proton and neutron angular momenta Jp and Jn are separately good quantum numbers and degenerate in energy; this condition applies specifically to states with angular momenta that cannot occur for a system of identical particles.

## Method

The author performs single-j shell model calculations for systems of three and four particles (or holes) in the f7/2 and g9/2 shells, setting all T=0 two-body matrix elements to zero. The conditions for PDS are derived from the vanishing of specific 6j and 9j symbols, which enforce off-diagonal decoupling (ensuring Jp and Jn are good quantum numbers) and diagonal degeneracy (ensuring states with the same Jp, Jn are energy-degenerate). The analysis is extended from the f7/2 shell to the g9/2 shell for nuclei with Z=50, N=50 cores.

## Key numbers

- For three nucleons in the g9/2 shell: degenerate set with Jp=8, Jn=9/2 gives J=19/2, 23/2, and 25/2 (all T=1/2)
- For four nucleons in the g9/2 shell: degenerate set with Jp=8, Jn=8 gives J=14, 16 (T=0)
- For four nucleons in the g9/2 shell: degenerate set with Jp=8, Jn=6 gives J=11, 13, 14 (T=0)
- In 96Ag single-j shell calculation with Q.Q interaction: energies relative to J=1+ ground state are J=15+ at 2.48 MeV, J=14+ at 3.09 MeV, J=13+ at 2.53 MeV, J=12+ at 2.59 MeV, and J=11+ at 1.96 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous PDS studies in the f7/2 shell by Robinson and Zamick (2001) and Escuderos, Bayman, and Zamick (2005) to the g9/2 shell, with applications to nuclei such as 96Cd, 97Cd, and 97In. The mathematical conditions for PDS are shown to be related to companion problems including Talmi's (1993) work on coefficients of fractional parentage for states that do not exist in identical-particle systems, and Zhao and Arima's (2005) work on J-pairing Hamiltonians. The results enable predictions of spin-gap isomers in the g9/2 region, including the J=16+ isomer in 96Cd reported by the York group (Nara Singh, 2011), and suggest that reintroducing T=0 interactions is required to remove degeneracies and create the observed isomerism.
