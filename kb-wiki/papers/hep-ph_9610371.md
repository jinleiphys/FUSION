# Electroproduction of vector mesons off nuclei at ELFE: exact solution for color transparency

> A multichannel evolution equation is developed for the density matrix describing a hadronic wave packet produced by a virtual photon and propagation through a nuclear matter. This approach is dual to the quark-gluon representation, incorporates the effects of coherence and formation times and gives an exact solution for color transparency problem at any photon energy. We propose a procedure of data analysis, which provides an unambiguous way of detection of a color transparency signal even at medium energies.

# Electroproduction of vector mesons off nuclei at ELFE: exact solution for color transparency

## Key claim

The authors develop a multichannel evolution equation for the density matrix that provides an exact solution to the color transparency problem at any photon energy, incorporating both coherence and formation time effects; however, they caution that variation of the coherence time with photon energy and virtuality can mimic the onset of color transparency, making unambiguous detection difficult without a special data analysis procedure that fixes the coherence time constant.

## Method

A multichannel evolution equation for the density matrix describing a hadronic wave packet (components including γ*, V, V', etc.) is derived and solved for exclusive incoherent electroproduction of vector mesons off nuclei. The approach uses a hadronic basis dual to the quark-gluon representation, incorporating Gribov's inelastic corrections and treating both coherence time (longitudinal momentum transfer q_c = (Q² + m_V²)/2ν) and formation time effects. The authors calculate the Q²-dependence of nuclear transparency for ρ-meson electroproduction on lead in a two-channel approximation, comparing results with the Glauber approximation.

## Key numbers

- Coherence time: t_c ≈ 2ν/(Q² + m_V²)
- Formation time bounds: 2ν/Q² < t_f < 2ν/(m_V'² - m_V²)
- Nuclear transparency calculations performed for ρ-meson electroproduction on lead at photon energies ν = 5, 10, 20, and 30 GeV
- Longitudinal momentum transfer: q_c = (Q² + m_V²)/2ν
- In the limit q_c ≫ 1/R_A, Tr_inc = σ_in^{VA}/A σ_in^{VN}; in the limit q_c ≪ 1/R_A, Tr_inc = σ_qel^{VA}/A σ_el^{VN}

## Context

This work extends the Glauber approximation for incoherent electroproduction (Hüfner, Kopeliovich 1995) by including formation time effects and Gribov's inelastic corrections (Gribov 1970). The approach is dual to the quark-gluon path integral technique (Kopeliovich, Zakharov 1991), which becomes unreasonably complicated when coherence time effects are included. The results enable a proposed procedure for unambiguous detection of color transparency at medium energies (HERMES, TJNAF, ELFE) by fixing the coherence time constant through special selection of events with different Q² and ν, building on earlier predictions from the E665 experiment (1995).
