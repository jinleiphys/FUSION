# Implications of a DK Molecule at 2.32 GeV

> We discuss the implications of a possible quasinuclear DK bound state at 2.32 GeV. Evidence for such a state was recently reported in D_s^+pi^o by the BaBar Collaboration. We first note that a conventional quark model c-sbar assignment is implausible, and then consider other options involving multiquark systems. An I=0 c sbar n nbar baryonium assignment is one possibility. We instead favor a DK meson molecule assignment, which can account for the mass and quantum numbers of this state. The higher-mass scalar c-sbar state expected at 2.48 GeV is predicted to have a very large DK coupling, which would encourage formation of an I=0 DK molecule. Isospin mixing is expected in hadron molecules, and a dominantly I=0 DK state with some I=1 admixture could explain both the narrow total width of the 2.32 GeV state as well as the observed decay to D_s^+ pi^o. Additional measurements that can be used to test this and related scenarios are discussed.

# Implications of a DK Molecule at 2.32 GeV

## Key claim

The narrow state near 2.32 GeV reported by BaBar in D_s^+π^0 is unlikely to be a conventional c\bar{s} quark-model state (the predicted mass is 2.48 GeV, 160 MeV higher, and the predicted width is 270–990 MeV), and is instead best explained as an I=0 DK meson molecule with a binding energy of approximately 40 MeV, where isospin mixing with an I=1 component can account for both the narrow total width and the observed decay to D_s^+π^0.

## Method

The authors compare the observed state's mass, width, and decay properties against conventional quark-model predictions for the ^3P_0 c\bar{s} scalar meson (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985; Godfrey and Kokoski, 1991), then evaluate two multiquark alternatives: I=0 c\bar{s}n\bar{n} baryonium and a DK molecule. They apply the Weinstein-Isgur (1990) binding mechanism, level repulsion between the two-meson continuum and a higher-mass scalar q\bar{q} state, to the DK system, and construct a two-state mixing model for the D^+K^0 and D^0K^+ channels to quantify isospin mixing from the 9.3 MeV mass splitting between these thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The BaBar state has mass near 2.32 GeV and total width ≤ 10 MeV (experimental resolution limit).
- The Godfrey-Isgur prediction for the ^3P_0 c\bar{s} scalar is 2.48 GeV, giving a 160 MeV mass discrepancy.
- The predicted width for the ^3P_0 c\bar{s} state at 2.48 GeV is 270–990 MeV (Godfrey and Kokoski, 1991).
- DK thresholds: m(D^0K^+) = 2358 MeV, m(D^0K^0) = 2362 MeV, m(D^+K^+) = 2363 MeV, m(D^+K^0) = 2367 MeV; a DK molecule at 2.32 GeV has binding energy ≈ 40 MeV.
- The mass splitting between D^+K^0 and D^0K^+ is δm = 9.3 ± 1.1 MeV.
- The predicted E1 transition rate for a ^3P_0 c\bar{s} state at 2.32 GeV to D_s^{*+}γ is ≈ 2 keV (Godfrey, 2003).
- The D^*K threshold is 2.50 GeV; a second BaBar signal at 2.46 GeV is a candidate D^*K molecule with mass difference 2.46 − 2.32 = 0.14 GeV, approximately equal to M(D^*) − M(D).
- For a BD molecule: M(B) + M(D) ≈ 7145 MeV, M(B_c) ≈ 6400 ± 400 MeV, giving a pion energy of ≈ 750 ± 400 MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the Weinstein-Isgur (1990) mechanism for K\bar{K} molecule formation to the DK system, and builds on earlier suggestions by Lipkin (1977, 1986) and Isgur and Lipkin (1981) that four-quark states of type c\bar{s}n\bar{n} and DK molecules might exist. The work contradicts a conventional c\bar{s} assignment for the D_{sJ}^*(2317)^+ and instead favors a molecular interpretation, which predicts additional heavy-quark molecules such as D^*K and BD. The analysis enables experimental tests including searches for charged partners in D_s^+π^±, measurement of the E1 transition to D_s^{*+}γ, and Q^2-dependence studies at CLEO-c to distinguish multiquark from conventional charmed meson production.
