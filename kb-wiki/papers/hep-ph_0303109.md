# Flavor-Mixing Effects on the QCD Phase Diagram at non-vanishing Isospin Chemical Potential: One or Two Phase Transitions?

> We investigate effects of a fixed nonzero isospin chemical potential on the mu_B-T phase diagram of strongly interacting matter using a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio-type four fermion interaction. We focus on the influence of a flavor-mixing interaction induced by instantons. We find that already for rather moderate values of the coupling strength in the flavor-mixing channel the recent findings of two seperate phase transitions do not persist.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the previously reported finding of two separate first-order phase transitions in the QCD phase diagram at non-zero isospin chemical potential is unlikely to persist once flavor-mixing effects (induced by instantons) are taken into account, even for rather moderate values of the flavor-mixing coupling strength.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a four-fermion interaction split into two parts: a flavor-symmetric term (coupling \(G_1\)) and a flavor-mixing term (coupling \(G_2\)) structured as a 't Hooft determinant. They introduce a mixing parameter \(\alpha\) such that \(G_1 = (1-\alpha)G_0\) and \(G_2 = \alpha G_0\), then compute the mean-field thermodynamic potential and solve the gap equations for the up and down quark condensates at fixed isospin chemical potential \(\delta\mu = 30\) MeV, varying \(\alpha\) to study the phase diagram in the \(\mu\)-\(T\) plane.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum constituent quark mass \(M_{vac} = 400\) MeV, with current quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 6\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 590\) MeV, and \(G_0\Lambda^2 = 2.435\).
- At \(T=0\) and \(\delta\mu = 30\) MeV with \(\alpha = 0\) (no flavor mixing), two distinct phase transitions occur at \(\mu = 353\) MeV (up quarks) and \(\mu = 413\) MeV (down quarks).
- The critical mixing parameter for a single phase transition at \(T=0\) is \(\alpha^c(T=0) = 0.104\); at \(\alpha = 0.11\) only one first-order transition remains.
- At \(\alpha = 0.11\) and finite temperature, the single phase boundary splits into two lines at \(T = 25\) MeV, ending at two second-order endpoints; at \(\alpha = 0.12\) only one boundary with a single endpoint exists.
- Estimated upper bound for the critical mixing parameter: \(\alpha^c(T=0) \lesssim 2\delta\mu / M_{vac} = 0.15\) for \(\delta\mu = 30\) MeV.
- Realistic \(\alpha\) values from 3-flavor NJL fits: \(\alpha \simeq 0.21\) (Rehberg parameters) and \(\alpha \simeq 0.16\) (Kunihiro parameters).

## Context

This work extends the studies of the \(\mu_B\)-\(T\) phase diagram at non-zero isospin chemical potential performed by Toublan and Kogut (2003) using an NJL model and by Klein, Toublan, and Verbaarschot (2003) using a random matrix model, both of which found two separate phase transitions. The authors show that including a flavor-mixing interaction (the 't Hooft term) removes the second transition for realistic coupling strengths, and note that their results are consistent with the single-transition scenario found by Bedaque, Rupak, and Savage (2002) at high baryon density. The work enables future studies to incorporate flavor mixing when modeling the QCD phase diagram for heavy-ion collisions, where isospin chemical potential is non-zero.
