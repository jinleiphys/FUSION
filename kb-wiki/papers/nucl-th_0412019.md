# Relativistic Generalization of the Gamow Factor for Fermion Pair Production or Annihilation

> In the production or annihilation of a pair of fermions, the initial-state or final-state interactions often lead to significant effects on the reaction cross sections. For Coulomb-type interactions, the Gamow factor has been traditionally used to take into account these effects. However the Gamow factor needs to be modified when the magnitude of the coupling constant or the relative velocity of two particles increases. We obtain the relativistic generalization of the Gamow factor in terms of the overlap of the Feynman amplitude with the relativistic wave function of two fermions with an attractive Coulomb-type interaction. An explicit form of the corrective factor is presented for the spin-singlet S-wave state. While the corrective factor approaches the Gamow factor in the non-relativistic limit, we found that the Gamow factor significantly over-estimates the effects when the coupling constant or the velocity is large.

## Key claim

The paper derives a relativistic generalization of the Gamow factor for fermion pair production or annihilation with an attractive Coulomb-type interaction, showing that the traditional Gamow factor significantly over-estimates the corrective effect when the coupling constant or the relative velocity is large, due to an effective screening of the long-range interaction.

## Method

The authors used Dirac's constraint dynamics to obtain the relativistic two-body wave function for a fermion pair with an attractive Coulomb-type interaction, then computed the corrective K-factor as the ratio of the overlap of the Feynman amplitude with the interacting wave function to the overlap with the free wave function. The calculation was carried out explicitly for the spin-singlet S-wave state, with the K-factor expressed in terms of the Sommerfeld parameter eta and the coupling constant alpha.

## Key numbers

* For alpha = 0.01, the K-factor approaches the Gamow factor (ratio K/G(eta) near 1); for alpha = 0.32, the ratio K/G(eta) decreases significantly, showing the Gamow factor over-estimates the correction.
* The K-factor goes to unity as the relative velocity v approaches 1 (the speed of light) for all values of alpha.
* The critical value of the coupling constant alpha for the singlet S-state is 1/2.
* The K-factor for bosons is slightly smaller than for fermions, with the difference greater for larger alpha (e.g., alpha = 0.32).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic treatment of Coulomb final-state interactions for bosons (Yoon and Wong, 2000) to the more practically relevant case of fermions, which couple to electromagnetic and chromodynamic gauge fields. The results can be applied to processes such as open charm pair production near threshold, where the color-Coulomb coupling constant is about 0.3 and relative velocities can be small. The paper also relates to earlier work by Chatterjee and Wong (1995) and Wong and Chatterjee (1997) on non-relativistic Gamow factor applications, and to Baym and Braun-Munzinger (1996) who modified the Gamow factor for Coulomb interactions in intensity interferometry.
