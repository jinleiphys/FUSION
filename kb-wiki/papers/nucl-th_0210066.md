# Generalized Partial Dynamical Symmetries in Nuclear Spectroscopy

> Explicit forms of IBM Hamiltonians with a generalized partial dynamical O(6) symmetry are presented and compared with empirical data in $^{162}$Dy.

## Key claim

The paper presents an IBM-1 Hamiltonian with a generalized partial dynamical O(6) symmetry, where only a subset of states (the ground band) have good O(6) symmetry and are exactly solvable, while all states break O(5) symmetry; this is a more restrictive condition than previous partial symmetries where either all states preserved part of the symmetry or part of the states preserved all of it.

## Method

The author constructs two IBM-1 Hamiltonians: H1 (Eq. h1) with two- and three-body interactions that preserves O(6) but breaks O(5), and H2 (Eq. h2) with a different two-body term that breaks both O(6) and O(5) yet has an exactly solvable ground band with good O(6) symmetry. Both Hamiltonians are fitted to the experimental spectrum of 162Dy and their predictions for B(E2) transition rates are compared with empirical data, using a band-mixing calculation in the large-N limit to analyze the intrinsic structure of the excited bands.

## Key numbers

- For 162Dy, the H2 Hamiltonian parameters are h0=28.5, h2=6.3, λ2=13.45 keV with N=15 bosons
- The K=0_2 band of H2 has O(6) decomposition: σ=N (85.50%), σ=N-2 (14.45%), σ=N-4 (0.05%)
- The K=2_3 band of H2 has O(6) decomposition: σ=N (50.36%), σ=N-2 (49.25%), σ=N-4 (0.38%), σ=N-6 (0.01%)
- The K=2_1 band of H2 is 99.85% pure γ-phonon and 0.15% βγ mode
- The K=0_2 band of H2 is 36.29% β, 63.68% γ^2_{K=0}, and 0.03% β^2 modes
- Measured B(E2) ratio R1 = B(E2; 0+_{K=0_2}→2+_{K=2_1}) / B(E2; 0+_{K=0_2}→2+_{K=0_1}) = 10(5); H1 predicts 0.86, H2 predicts 77.59
- Measured B(E2) ratio R2 = B(E2; 2+_{K=0_2}→4+_{K=0_1}) / B(E2; 2+_{K=0_2}→0+_{K=0_1}) = 65(28); H1 predicts 4.00, H2 predicts 3.25

## Context

This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetries previously developed for SU(3) in the IBM-1 (Leviatan 1996, Leviatan and Sinai 1999) and for F-spin in the IBM-2 (Leviatan and Ginocchio 2000), and for O(6) partial symmetry of the second kind (Isacker 1999). The generalized partial dynamical symmetry provides a new framework for describing axially deformed nuclei where ΔK=2 interband transitions from the K=2_1 and K=0_2 bands are stronger than ΔK=0 transitions from the K=0_2 band to the ground band, a feature that distinguishes it from the simpler O(6) partial symmetry of H1.
