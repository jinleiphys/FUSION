# The optimized point-coupling interaction for the relativistic energy density functional of Hartree-Bogoliubov approach quantifying the nuclear bulk properties

> We propose a newly optimized nonlinear point-coupling parameterized interaction, PC-L3R, for the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov framework with a further optimized separable pairing force by fitting to observables, i.e., the binding energies of 91 spherical nuclei, charge radii of 63 nuclei, and 12 sets of mean pairing gaps consisting of 54 nuclei in total. The separable pairing force strengths of proton and neutron are optimized together with the point-coupling constants, and are justified in satisfactory reproducing the empirical pairing gaps. The comparison of experimental binding energies compiled in AME2020 for 91 nuclei with the ones generated from the present and other commonly used point-coupling interactions indicates that the implementation of PC-L3R in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov yields the lowest root-mean-square deviation. The charge radii satisfactory agree with experiment. Meanwhile, PC-L3R is capable of estimating the saturation properties of the symmetric nuclear matter and of appropriately predicting the isospin and mass dependence of binding energy. The experimental odd-even staggering of single nucleon separation energies is well reproduced. The comparison of the estimated binding energies for 7,373 nuclei based on the PC-L3R and other point-coupling interactions is also presented.

## Key claim
The newly optimized nonlinear point-coupling interaction PC-L3R, fitted within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) framework with a separable pairing force whose proton and neutron strengths are optimized separately, yields the lowest root-mean-square deviation for binding energies among currently used point-coupling interactions, but only for the 91 spherical nuclei selected in the fitting procedure.

## Method
The authors performed a grid search of approximately 10^9 calculations with cross validation for 91 spherical nuclei to optimize nine point-coupling constants together with the neutron pairing strength G_n, proton pairing strength G_p, and range parameter a of the separable Gaussian pairing force. The chi-squared minimization used binding energies of 91 nuclei, charge radii of 63 nuclei, and 12 sets of mean pairing gaps comprising 54 nuclei, with theoretical weights of 1 MeV, 0.02 fm, and 0.1 MeV respectively. The RHB equations were solved in a harmonic oscillator basis with N_f = 26 fermionic shells.

## Key numbers
- Root-mean-square deviation of binding energies for 91 spherical nuclei: 1.339 MeV (constrained to within approximately 20 keV of this value for the uncertainty estimates of the coupling constants)
- Root-mean-square deviation of charge radii for 63 nuclei: maintained in the range 0.0185 to 0.0187 fm
- Neutron pairing strength G_n: -749.552 ± 3.5 MeV fm^3
- Proton pairing strength G_p: -680.080 ± 20 MeV fm^3
- Range parameter a: 0.62948 ± 0.0015 fm
- The chi-squared change was limited to Δχ^2(P) less than approximately 5 for the uncertainty estimates

## Context
This work extends the line of point-coupling interactions for relativistic energy density functionals, following PC-PK1 (Zhao 2010), PC-F1 (Burvenich 2002), PC-LA (Nikolaus 1992), and DD-PC1 (Niksic 2008), which were fitted within RMF+BCS frameworks with delta pairing forces. It also builds on the more recent PC-X (Taninah 2020) and DD-PCX (Yuksel 2019) interactions proposed for the RHB model with separable pairing, but improves upon them by optimizing the proton and neutron pairing strengths separately rather than treating them equally. The PC-L3R interaction enables improved predictions of binding energies for 7,373 nuclei and provides a benchmark for future relativistic density functional studies of nuclear bulk properties across the nuclear chart.
