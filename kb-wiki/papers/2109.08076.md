# Heavy-meson chiral Lagrangians, effective flavored couplings, SU(4) flavor breaking and their consequences

> We review heavy quark flavor and spin symmetries, their exploitation in heavy meson effective theories and the flavored couplings of charmed and light mesons in the definition of their effective Lagrangians. We point out how nonperturbative continuum QCD approaches based on Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations can be used to calculate strong and leptonic decays of open-charm mesons and heavy quarkonia. The strong decay $D^*\to Dπ$ serves as a benchmark, as it is the only physical open-charm observable that can be related to the effective Lagrangian's couplings. Nonetheless, a quantitative comparison of $D^*Dπ$, $ρDD$, $ρD^*D$ and $ρD^* D^*$ couplings for a range of off-shell momenta of the $ρ$-meson invalidates SU(4)$_F$ symmetry relations between these couplings. Thus, besides the breaking of flavor symmetry by mass terms in the Lagrangians, the flavor-symmetry breaching in couplings and their dependence on the $ρ$-meson virtuality cannot be ignored. We also take the opportunity to present new results for the effective $J/ψDD$ and $J/ψD^*D$ couplings. We conclude this contribution with a discussion on how the description of pseudoscalar and vector $D$, $D_s$, $B$ and $B_s$ meson properties can be drastically improved with a modest modification of the flavor-dependence in the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the assumption of a universal SU(4) flavor symmetry coupling constant for interactions between heavy charmed mesons and light mesons is invalidated by quantitative comparisons of the \(D^*D\pi\), \(\rho DD\), \(\rho D^*D\), and \(\rho D^* D^*\) couplings, showing that flavor-symmetry breaking in the couplings themselves and their dependence on the \(\rho\)-meson virtuality cannot be ignored, even beyond the breaking introduced by mass terms in the Lagrangians.

## Method

The authors review heavy quark effective theory (HQET) and Heavy Meson Chiral Perturbation Theory (HMChPT) to define effective Lagrangians for heavy-light meson interactions. They then compute the strong couplings \(g_{D^*D\pi}\), \(g_{B^*B\pi}\), and couplings involving \(\rho\) and \(J/\psi\) mesons using an impulse approximation, where dressed quark propagators (algebraic approximations to Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions) and modeled Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are employed. The calculations are performed for both constituent and confining heavy-quark propagators, and the results are compared with experimental data, QCD sum rules, and lattice QCD to quantify SU(4) flavor symmetry breaking.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of effective SU(4) flavor Lagrangians (e.g., Matinyan (1998), Lin (1999), Oh (2000), Bracco (2011)) used to describe interactions between open-charm mesons and nuclei, charmonium production, and exotic states like the \(X(3872)\). It contradicts the assumption of a universal coupling \(\hat g\) derived from heavy-quark symmetry, showing that the extracted \(\hat g\) differs materially between charm and bottom sectors (\(\hat g_c\) vs. \(\hat g_b\)). The results enable more reliable calculations of \(DN\) scattering cross sections, charmed nuclear bound states, and \(J/\psi\) regeneration in heavy-ion collisions by providing flavor-dependent couplings that account for symmetry breaking without resorting to additional terms in the \(1/M_H\) expansion.
