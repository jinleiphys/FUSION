# Production of cascade hypernuclei via the (K-,K+) reaction within a quark-meson coupling model

> We study the production of bound cascade hypernuclei via the (K-,K+) reaction on 12C and 28Si targets within a covariant effective Lagrangian model, employing the cascade bound state spinors derived from the latest quark-meson coupling model as well as Dirac single particle wave functions. The K+-cascade production vertex is described by excitation, propagation and decay of Lambda and Sigma resonance states in the initial collision of a K- meson with a target proton in the incident channel. The parameters of the resonance vertices are fixed by describing the available data on total and differential cross sections for the cascade production in elementary (K-,K+) reaction. We find that both the elementary and hypernuclear production cross sections are dominated by the contributions from the Lambda(1520) intermediate resonant state. The 0 degree differential cross sections for the formation of simple s-state cascade particle-hole states peak at a beam momentum around 1.0 GeV/c, with a value in excess of 1 mub.

# Production of Cascade Hypernuclei via the (K⁻,K⁺) Reaction within a Quark-Meson Coupling Model

## Key claim

The 0° differential cross sections for producing bound cascade hypernuclei via the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction on ¹²C and ²⁸Si targets peak at a beam momentum around 1.0 GeV/c with values exceeding 1 μb, but only when considering the six most significant intermediate Λ and Σ resonant states, with the Λ(1520) resonance dominating the production mechanism. The authors caution that the experimental zero-degree differential cross section for the elementary p(K⁻,K⁺)Ξ⁻ reaction remains uncertain, with existing data from different experiments differing considerably from each other.

## Method

The authors employed a covariant effective Lagrangian model to describe the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction on ¹²C and ²⁸Si targets, where the incoming K⁻ collides with a bound target proton to excite intermediate Λ and Σ resonance states (Λ, Λ(1405), Λ(1520), Λ(1810), Σ, and Σ(1385)), which then decay into a Ξ⁻ hyperon captured into a nuclear orbit and an outgoing K⁺. The Ξ⁻ bound state spinors were calculated using the latest quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and also using a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential, with the resonance vertex parameters fixed by fitting total and differential cross section data for the elementary p(K⁻,K⁺)Ξ⁻ reaction. The nuclear structure was treated using pure single-particle-single-hole (Ξp⁻¹) wave functions with plane waves for the kaons, and distortion effects were included via reduction factors of 2.8 for ¹²C and 5.0 for ²⁸Si targets.

## Key numbers

- The 0° differential cross section for the ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺)¹²_Ξ⁻Be reaction forming the 1⁻ state peaks at a beam momentum around 1.0 GeV/c with a value in excess of 1 μb.
- The 0° differential cross section for the ²⁸Si(K⁻,K⁺)²⁸_Ξ⁻Mg reaction forming the 2⁺ state also peaks near 1.0 GeV/c with a value in excess of 1 μb.
- The QMC model predicts a binding energy of 5.681 MeV for the 1s₁/₂ Ξ⁻ state in ¹²_Ξ⁻Be, and binding energies of 11.376 MeV (1s₁/₂), 5.490 MeV (1p₃/₂), and 5.836 MeV (1p₁/₂) for ²⁸_Ξ⁻Mg.
- The threshold beam momentum for the ¹²C target reaction is approximately 0.761 GeV/c, and for the ²⁸Si target reaction approximately 0.750 GeV/c.
- The momentum transfers involved at 0° vary between 1.8 and 2.9 fm⁻¹.
- The QMC model cross sections are larger than those from the phenomenological model by about 10-15% in all cases.
- The elementary p(K⁻,K⁺)Ξ⁻ reaction total cross section peaks in the beam momentum region of 1.35-1.4 GeV/c.
- The Λ(1520) resonance coupling constants are g_KRN = 27.46 and g_KRΞ = -16.610.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to the elementary p(K⁻,K⁺)Ξ⁻ reaction by Shyam (2011) to the production of bound cascade hypernuclei on nuclear targets, going beyond earlier impulse approximation models by Gal (1983), Yamada (1994), Ikeda (1994), Tadokoro (1995), and Harada (2010) that did not calculate elementary cross sections but extracted them from sparse experimental data. The use of QMC model bound state spinors, which incorporate quark degrees of freedom through self-consistent treatment of the internal structure of nucleons and hyperons, represents a new element not present in previous studies of this system. The calculations are directly relevant to the upcoming "day one" experiments at the JPARC facility in Japan, which aim to observe bound Ξ hypernuclear states with high-resolution spectrometers, and the results suggest that the 0° differential cross sections are large enough to be measured, particularly for the ¹²C target.
