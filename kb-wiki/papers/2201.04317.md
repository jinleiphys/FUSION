# Quarkonium in a bulk viscous QGP medium

> The non-equilibrium properties of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) have been a topic of intensive research. In this contribution, we explore the nature of heavy quarkonia immersed in a QGP with bulk viscosity. We incorporate the bulk viscous effect through the deformation of the distribution functions of thermal quarks and gluons, with which the color dielectric permittivity can be computed. We use the color dielectric permittivity to compute the heavy quark potential inside a bulk viscous plasma and solve the Schrödinger equation using the potential to obtain the physical properties such as binding energies and decay widths. We discuss the effect of the bulk viscous correction on the quarkonium properties and the melting temperatures.

## Key claim

Heavy quarkonium binding energies and decay widths are sensitive to the bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), with the effect parameterized by a dimensionless strength parameter Phi; the melting temperatures of quarkonium states decrease slightly as Phi increases, suggesting that heavy quarkonia could serve as a probe of bulk viscous effects near the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors incorporated bulk viscous corrections into the thermal distribution functions of quarks and gluons using a parametrization with strength Phi and exponent a, then computed the color dielectric permittivity of the medium within the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation at one loop. Using this permittivity, they derived a modified heavy quark potential (real and imaginary parts) and solved the Schrodinger equation numerically to obtain binding energies and decay widths for charmonium and bottomonium states as functions of temperature and Phi.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium reduced mass taken as m_q = 1.25/2 GeV; bottomonium reduced mass taken as m_q = 4.66/2 GeV.
- String tension sigma = (0.44 GeV)^2 used in the Cornell potential.
- For Phi = 0, the J/psi melting temperature is 0.254^{+0.027}_{-0.032} GeV, compared to 0.267^{+0.033}_{-0.036} GeV from Lafferty (2019).
- For Phi = 0, the Upsilon melting temperature is 0.464^{+0.026}_{-0.022} GeV, compared to 0.440^{+0.080}_{-0.055} GeV from Lafferty (2019).
- For Phi = 0, the Upsilon' melting temperature is 0.235^{+0.025}_{-0.022} GeV, compared to 0.250^{+0.050}_{-0.053} GeV from Lafferty (2019).
- The one-loop coupling parameter Lambda was varied between 1/2 x 0.176 GeV and 2 x 0.176 GeV to estimate systematic uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quarkonia as probes of QGP properties by incorporating bulk viscous corrections, building on the earlier work of Thakur (2020) and Du (2016). The results are compared with melting temperatures extracted from lattice QCD by Lafferty (2019), showing qualitative agreement for the Phi = 0 case. The authors suggest that if bulk viscosity is enhanced near the QCD critical point, as discussed by Kharzeev (2007), Karsch (2007), and Moore (2008), then observables such as R_AA for quarkonia might show anomalous behavior as a function of collision energy in beam energy scan programs.
