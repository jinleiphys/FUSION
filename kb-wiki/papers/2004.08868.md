# Magnetic field-induced anisotropic interaction in heavy quark bound states

> We have investigated how a strong magnetic field (B) could decipher the anisotropic interaction in heavy quark ($Q$) and antiquark ($\bar Q$) bound states through the perturbative thermal QCD in real-time formalism. So we thermalize Schwinger propagator for quarks in LLL and the Feynman propagator for gluons to calculate the gluon self-energy. For the quark-loop contribution to the self-energy, the medium does not have any temperature correction and the vacuum term gives rise an anisotropic term whereas the gluon-loop yields temperature correction. This finding in quark-loop contribution corroborates the equivalence of a massless QED in (1+1)-dimension with the massless thermal QCD in strong B, which (quark sector) is reduced to (1+1)-dimension (longitudinal). Thus the permittivity of the medium behaves like as a tensor. Thus the permittivity of medium makes the $Q \bar Q$ potential anisotropic, which resembles with a contemporary results found in lattice studies. As a result, potential for $Q \bar Q$-pairs aligned transverse to B is more attractive than parallel alignment. However, potential is always more attractive compared to B=0 due to softening of screening mass. However, the imaginary-part of potential becomes smaller compared to B=0. We have next investigated the effects of strong ${\bf B}$ on binding energies (B.E.) and thermal widths ($Γ$) of ground states of $c \bar c$ and $b \bar b$ in a time-independent perturbation theory, where binding energies gets increased and widths gets decreased, compared to $B =0$. Finally we have studied the quasi-free dissociation of bound states in a strong B. The dissociation temperatures estimated for $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ states are obtained as $1.59 \rm{T_c} $ and $2.22 \rm{T_c}$, respectively, which are higher than the estimate in B=0 , thus preventing early dissolution of $Q \bar Q$ bound states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strong magnetic field induces an anisotropic interaction in heavy quark-antiquark bound states, where the potential for pairs aligned transverse to the magnetic field is more attractive than for parallel alignment, and the overall potential is more attractive compared to the B=0 case due to softening of the electric screening mass.

## Method

The authors calculated the gluon self-energy up to one loop using the Schwinger propagator for quarks in the lowest Landau level and the Feynman propagator for gluons within perturbative thermal QCD in real-time formalism. The dielectric permittivity was obtained as a tensor from the static limit of the resummed gluon propagator, which was then used to derive the anisotropic heavy quark potential in coordinate space. Binding energies and thermal widths of charmonium and bottomonium ground states were computed using first-order time-independent perturbation theory, and dissociation temperatures were estimated from the criterion binding energy equals half the thermal width.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperature for J/ψ: 1.59 Tc
- Dissociation temperature for Υ: 2.22 Tc
- Magnetic field strength at RHIC: mπ² (10¹⁸ Gauss)
- Magnetic field strength at LHC: 15 mπ²
- QCD scale Λ_QCD: approximately 0.200 GeV
- Parameters Λ_V and μ₀: 0.385 GeV and 1.1 GeV respectively
- k_z value for coupling running: 0.1√(eB)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonium properties in thermal QCD medium by Thakur (2014) and Singh (2018), and addresses the absence of anisotropic behavior in those earlier perturbative approaches. The results corroborate lattice findings by Bonati (2014, 2016) that showed anisotropic screening masses between transverse and longitudinal directions relative to the magnetic field. The study enables more realistic modeling of heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where strong magnetic fields are present.
