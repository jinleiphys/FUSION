# Charge density distributions and related form factors in neutron-rich light exotic nuclei

> Charge form factors corresponding to proton density distributions in exotic nuclei, such as $^{6,8}$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{17,19}$B and $^{14}$Be are calculated and compared. The results can be used as tests of various theoretical models for the exotic nuclei structure in possible future experiments using a colliding electron-exotic nucleus storage ring. The result of such a comparison would show the effect of the neutron halo or skin on the proton distributions in exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The charge form factors of neutron-rich light exotic nuclei (⁶,⁸He, ¹¹Li, ¹⁷,¹⁹B, ¹⁴Be) are calculated from existing theoretical proton density distributions; the results show that different models predict opposite trends for the effect of added neutrons on the charge distribution, meaning that future electron-scattering experiments could distinguish between these models.

## Method

The charge form factor \(F_{ch}(q)\) is obtained as the Fourier transform of the charge density, which is the point-proton density folded with the Gaussian charge distribution of the proton (rms radius 0.80 fm). Point-proton densities are taken from three sources: Tanihata et al. (1992) and COSMA (cluster-orbital shell model approximation) densities for He and Li isotopes; large-space shell-model (LSSM) densities for He and Li; and Suzuki et al. (1999) densities for B and Be isotopes. For harmonic-oscillator-based densities, a centre-of-mass correction factor \(\exp[(qR)^2/6A]\) is applied.

## Key numbers

- Proton rms radii from Tanihata densities: 1.62 fm (⁴He), 1.72 fm (⁶He), 1.76 fm (⁸He).
- Proton rms radii from COSMA densities: 1.89 fm (⁶He), 1.69 fm (⁸He); 2.44 fm (⁶Li), 2.31 fm (¹¹Li).
- Proton rms radii from Suzuki densities: 2.722 fm (¹⁷B), 2.872 fm (¹⁹B), 2.755 fm (¹⁴Be).
- Experimental charge rms radius of ⁴He: 1.696(14) fm (DeVries 1987).
- Experimental charge rms radius of ⁶Li: 2.57 fm (Patter 2003, Fried 1994).
- Proton form factor parameter \(\alpha = 0.6532\) fm, corresponding to proton rms radius \(R_p = 0.80\) fm.
- Harmonic-oscillator width parameters for Tanihata densities: \(a = 1.40\) fm, \(b = 2.04\) fm (⁶He); \(a = 1.43\) fm, \(b = 1.93\) fm (⁸He); \(a = 1.325\) fm (⁴He).
- COSMA parameters for ⁶He: \(a = 1.55\) fm, \(b = 2.24\) fm; for ⁸He: \(a = 1.38\) fm, \(b = 1.99\) fm.
- COSMA parameters for ⁶Li: \(a = 1.55\) fm, \(b = 2.07\) fm; for ¹¹Li: \(a = 1.89\) fm, \(b = 3.68\) fm.
- Suzuki width parameters \(\lambda\): 1.9366 fm (¹⁷B), 2.0365 fm (¹⁹B), 2.0218 fm (¹⁴Be).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of exotic-nuclei structure (reviewed by Cas 2000) by calculating charge form factors that could be measured in a future electron-exotic nucleus storage ring, complementing existing proton elastic-scattering data (e.g., Korsh 1997, Chul 1995, Cort 1997) and total reaction cross-section analyses (Tanihata 1992, Suzuki 1999). The results enable a direct test of competing theoretical models (Tanihata, COSMA, LSSM) for the proton density in neutron-rich light nuclei, particularly to resolve whether the neutron halo or skin expands or contracts the proton distribution.
