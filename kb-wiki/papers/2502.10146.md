# Modeling of acceleration in heavy-ion collisions: occurrence of temperature below the Unruh temperature

> It has recently been shown that extremely strong electric fields can be created in central collisions of heavy ions, due to which the Schwinger effect can be significant. A direct analogue of the electric field in hydrodynamics is the acceleration of the medium. Using the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) framework we model the Au-Au collisions at intermediate collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.5-11.5\,$GeV and obtain the spatial distribution of acceleration at different time moments. The present study demonstrates that extremely high acceleration of the order of 1 GeV may be generated in both central and non-central collisions, and that the distribution exhibits a core-corona structure. Consequently, in contrast to the case with an electric field, the Unruh effect is expected to be significant. It is demonstrated that for the confined phase the temperature is less than the Unruh temperature. Conversely, in the deconfined phase, the relationship is inverse. The obtained results thus support the prediction about the existence of states with $T<T_U$ at the early stages of the collision and the associated complementary description of thermalization in terms of the novel phase transition at the Unruh temperature.

## Key claim

Using the PHSD transport model for Au-Au collisions at intermediate energies (√sNN = 4.5–11.5 GeV), the authors demonstrate that states where the thermodynamic temperature T is less than the Unruh temperature TU exist at early times after the collision, but only in the hadronic (confined) phase; in the partonic (deconfined) phase the relationship is inverted, with T exceeding TU.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model to simulate Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 4.5, 7.7, and 11.5 GeV for both central (b = 0 fm) and non-central (b = 7.5 fm) impact parameters. From the test-particle distributions, they constructed the energy-momentum tensor on a spatial grid, extracted the flow velocity via the Landau eigenvector condition, and computed the acceleration aμ = uν ∂ν uμ. The thermodynamic temperature T was obtained from the equation of state of Satarov, Dmitriev, and Mishustin (2009) using the local energy density and baryon density, while the Unruh temperature was calculated as TU = a/(2π).

## Key numbers

- Maximum Unruh temperature at √sNN = 11.5 GeV reaches approximately 250 MeV, corresponding to acceleration a ≈ 1.6 GeV, exceeding the maximum medium temperature of about 170 MeV at the moment of maximum nuclear overlap.
- For √sNN = 7.7 GeV, the maximum thermodynamic temperature at maximum overlap is T ≈ 160–170 MeV, close to the confinement-deconfinement phase transition temperature of 150–170 MeV.
- For √sNN = 4.5 GeV, the Unruh temperature is significantly less than the thermodynamic temperature and decreases nearly to zero after maximum overlap at t − tcoll ≈ 5 fm/c.
- The spatial grid used for fluidization had steps (Δx, Δy, Δz) = (1, 1, 1/γ) fm, where γ is the Lorentz factor of the colliding ions.
- Total statistics comprised (5–10) × 10^4 events for each impact parameter and collision energy, with N = 200 parallel ensembles and M ≈ 500 independent model runs for 7.7 and 11.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions by Prokhorov, Teryaev, and Zakharov (2023) that states with T < TU should appear at early stages of heavy-ion collisions and that the line T = TU corresponds to a second-order phase transition. It complements earlier studies of acceleration in heavy-ion collisions by Palermo, Grossi, Karpenko, and Becattini (2024) and Karpenko (2018), and connects to the program of thermal hadron production via the Unruh effect proposed by Cleymans and Satz (1993), Kharzeev and Tuchin (2005), and Castorina, Kharzeev, and Satz (2007). The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted phase transition at the Unruh temperature, particularly at the NICA facility, and motivate deeper analysis of the acceleration field structure including spectator effects at lower collision energies.
