# Nonperturbative aspects of the quark-photon vertex

> The electromagnetic interaction with quarks is investigated through a relativistic, electromagnetic gauge-invariant treatment. Gluon dressing of the quark-photon vertex and the quark self-energy functions is described by the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation and the Schwinger-Dyson equation in the rainbow approximation respectively. Results for the calculation of the quark-photon vertex are presented in both the time-like and space-like regions of photon momentum squared, however emphasis is placed on the space-like region relevant to electron scattering. The treatment presented here simultaneously addresses the role of dynamically generated $q\bar{q}$ vector bound states and the approach to asymptotic behavior. The resulting description is therefore applicable over the entire range of momentum transfers available in electron scattering experiments. Input parameters are limited to the model gluon two-point function, which is chosen to reflect confinement and asymptotic freedom, and are largely constrained by the obtained bound-state spectrum.

## Key claim

The quark-photon vertex can be described nonperturbatively over the entire range of momentum transfers relevant to electron scattering by solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation, provided the gluon two-point function is chosen to reflect both confinement and asymptotic freedom; however, the specific model used here is not sufficient to reproduce the empirical excitation spectrum of vector mesons beyond the ground state.

## Method

The gluon dressing of the quark-photon vertex and quark self-energy functions was described by the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation and the Schwinger-Dyson equation in the rainbow approximation, respectively, with zero current-quark masses. The gluon two-point function was parametrized with an infrared term characterized by an effective strength eta and range R0, plus a known ultraviolet form, and the quark self-energy functions were modeled analytically based on solutions from a simpler delta-function gluon model. The resulting coupled integral equations for five transverse coefficient functions were discretized using Gauss quadrature and solved as a matrix equation in the direct-product space of momentum magnitude and direction cosine.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state vector meson mass: 880 MeV, obtained with infrared scale R0 = 1 fm and eta = 5 fm^-1 (approximately 1 GeV)
- Dynamical quark mass m_D approximately 0.29 eta, yielding m_D approximately 290 MeV for eta approximately 1 GeV
- Spatial extent of the ground-state vector meson: r_V approximately 0.57 fm, estimated from exponential fit with Delta approximately 3.5 fm^-1
- QCD scale Lambda fixed at 1 fm^-1 throughout the investigation

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states (CRP 1987, PCR 1989, Aoki 1991, Munczek and Jain 1992) by simultaneously addressing the quark-photon vertex in both time-like and space-like regions, including the role of dynamically generated q\bar{q} vector bound states and the approach to asymptotic behavior. The treatment enables a quark-based description of electromagnetic interactions with hadrons applicable over the full range of momentum transfers available in electron scattering experiments such as those proposed at CEBAF, though the author notes that the ladder approximation and the specific gluon model used here are insufficient to reproduce the empirical excitation spectrum beyond the ground state.
