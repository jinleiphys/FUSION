# Constraining the QCD phase diagram by tricritical lines at imaginary chemical potential

> We present unambiguous evidence from lattice simulations of QCD with three degenerate quark species for two tricritical points in the (T,m) phase diagram at fixed imaginary μ/T=iπ/3 mod 2π/3, one in the light and one in the heavy mass regime. These represent the boundaries of the chiral and deconfinement critical lines continued to imaginary chemical potential, respectively. It is demonstrated that the shape of the deconfinement critical line for real chemical potentials is dictated by tricritical scaling and implies the weakening of the deconfinement transition with real chemical potential. The generalization to non-degenerate and light quark masses is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents unambiguous lattice evidence for two tricritical points in the (T, m) phase diagram of three-flavor QCD at fixed imaginary chemical potential μ/T = iπ/3, one in the light and one in the heavy mass regime, and demonstrates that the shape of the deconfinement critical line for real chemical potentials is dictated by tricritical scaling.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of QCD with three degenerate quark flavors using standard staggered fermions on Nt = 4 lattices at fixed μ/T = iπ, employing the RHMC algorithm. For each bare quark mass am, they investigated the nature of the transition as a function of the lattice gauge coupling β by analyzing the finite-size scaling of the Binder cumulant B4 of the imaginary part of the Polyakov loop on volumes L = 8, 10, 12 (and 20 in a few cases), with additional points filled in by Ferrenberg-Swendsen reweighting. The critical exponent ν was extracted by fitting all curves simultaneously to the scaling form B4(β, L) = B4(βc, ∞) + a x + b x² + ... with x = (β - βc) L^(1/ν).

## Key numbers
- Two tricritical quark masses on Nt = 4 lattices are bounded by 0.07 < am_tric1 < 0.3 (light regime) and 0.5 < am_tric2 < 1.5 (heavy regime).
- The Binder cumulant takes universal values: B4 = 3 for crossover, B4 = 1.5 for first order triple point, B4 = 1.604 for 3d Ising, and B4 = 2 for tricritical transitions.
- The critical exponent ν takes values ν = 1/3 for first order, ν = 0.63 for 3d Ising, and ν = 1/2 for tricritical transitions.
- For the light quark mass am = 0.04, the finite-size scaling analysis yields ν consistent with 1/3, corresponding to a first order transition.
- The critical line for heavy quarks follows the tricritical scaling form m_c/T(μ²) = m_c/T(0) + K [(π/3)² + (μ/T)²]^(2/5).

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of QCD at imaginary chemical potential by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2002) and by D'Elia and Lombardo (2003), which provided evidence that the chiral/deconfinement transition line meets the Z(3) transition at its endpoint. The results confirm and generalize the phase structure previously investigated for Nf = 4 by D'Elia and Lombardo (2003) and for Nf = 2 by Masser et al. (2009). The tricritical scaling found at imaginary chemical potential provides a constraint on the critical surfaces at real chemical potential and serves as a benchmark for effective models such as PNJL, sigma models, and quark-hadron models.
