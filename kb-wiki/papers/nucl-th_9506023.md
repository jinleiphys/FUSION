# Rho-Omega Mixing Off Shell and Charge Symmetry Breaking In the N-N Potential

> The role of the off-shell dependence of $ρ-ω$ mixing in the charge symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potential is discussed. It is shown that models describing the off-shell dependence of $ρ-ω$ mixing are not sufficient to determine the charge symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key claim

The authors argue that models calculating the off-shell momentum dependence of the ρ-ω mixing amplitude are not sufficient to determine the charge symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potential, because knowledge of the off-shell propagator alone provides no physical information without a consistent simultaneous description of the vertex functions from the same theory.

## Method

The paper uses field-theoretic arguments, including the Haag-Ruelle-Borchers theorem on field redefinition freedom, to show that off-shell propagators depend on the arbitrary choice of interpolating fields while observables do not. The authors then apply the philosophy of meson-exchange potentials, which separates long-range effects (explicit meson exchange) from short-range effects (phenomenological vertex functions), to demonstrate that any realistic momentum dependence of the ρ-ω mixing amplitude can be absorbed into charge-symmetry-breaking nucleon-vector-meson coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell ρ-ω mixing matrix element extracted from e+e- → π+π- data is approximately -5200 MeV².
- The experimental charge symmetry breaking scattering length difference is Δa_CSB = a_pp - a_nn = 1.5 ± 0.5 fm.
- Using the QCD sum-rule model of Hatsuda et al. (1994), the required charge-symmetry-breaking coupling constants are 0.8% of the standard coupling constants to reproduce the potential obtained without momentum dependence in the ρ-ω mixing matrix element.

## Context

This work extends the debate on ρ-ω mixing initiated by the successful phenomenology of Miller et al. (1990, 1994) and challenges the conclusions of models by Goldman et al. (1992), Piekarewicz and Williams (1992), and others who claimed that the momentum dependence of the mixing amplitude drastically changes the charge symmetry breaking potential. The paper enables future work to construct consistent charge symmetry breaking potentials by treating vertex functions and propagators together, rather than relying on off-shell propagator calculations alone.
