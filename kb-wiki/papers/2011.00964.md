# Lattice continuum-limit study of nucleon quasi-PDFs

> The quasi-PDF approach provides a path to computing parton distribution functions (PDFs) using lattice QCD. This approach requires matrix elements of a power-divergent operator in a nucleon at high momentum and one generically expects discretization effects starting at first order in the lattice spacing $a$. Therefore, it is important to demonstrate that the continuum limit can be reliably taken and to understand the size and shape of lattice artifacts. In this work, we report a calculation of isovector unpolarized and helicity PDFs using lattice ensembles with $N_f=2+1+1$ Wilson twisted mass fermions, a pion mass of approximately 370 MeV, and three different lattice spacings. Our results show a significant dependence on $a$, and the continuum extrapolation produces a better agreement with phenomenology. The latter is particularly true for the antiquark distribution at small momentum fraction $x$, where the extrapolation changes its sign.

## Key claim

The continuum limit of nucleon quasi-PDFs can be reliably taken using three lattice spacings, and the continuum extrapolation produces better agreement with phenomenological PDFs, particularly for the antiquark distribution at small momentum fraction \(x\), where the extrapolation changes its sign, but discretization effects start at first order in the lattice spacing \(a\) and are significant.

## Method

The authors compute isovector unpolarized and helicity quasi-PDFs using three \(N_f=2+1+1\) Wilson twisted mass fermion ensembles with a pion mass of approximately 370 MeV and lattice spacings \(a = 0.0644\), 0.0820, and 0.0934 fm. Bare nucleon matrix elements of a nonlocal operator containing a Wilson line are renormalized using two nonperturbative methods (the whole-operator RI\('\)-MOM approach and the auxiliary-field RI-xMOM approach), converted to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme and then to a modified \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme, and Fourier-transformed to obtain quasi-PDFs. Continuum extrapolations are performed using both linear-in-\(a\) and quadratic-in-\(a\) fits, and a ratio of matrix elements at nonzero and zero momentum is also studied to cancel renormalization.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: \(a = 0.0644(07)(25)\) fm (ensemble D45), \(0.0820(10)(36)\) fm (B55), \(0.0934(13)(35)\) fm (A60).
- Pion mass: approximately 370 MeV for all three ensembles (365, 373, 371 MeV respectively).
- Nucleon momenta: \(p_z = 1.66\) GeV (A60), 1.89 GeV (B55), 1.80 GeV (D45).
- Source-sink separations: \(t_s = 0.934\) fm (A60), 0.984 fm (B55), 0.966 fm (D45).
- Auxiliary-field mass parameters: \(am = -0.392(1)(57)\) (\(\beta=1.90\)), \(-0.373(1)(50)\) (\(\beta=1.95\)), \(-0.305(1)(37)\) (\(\beta=2.10\)).
- Renormalization factor \(Z_\phi^2(1-r_\text{mix}^2)\): 0.986(25) (\(\beta=1.90\)), 0.908(20) (\(\beta=1.95\)), 0.907(11) (\(\beta=2.10\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier quasi-PDF calculations by the same collaboration (Alexandrou et al., 2015–2020) and others (Lin et al., 2014; Chen et al., 2016; Orginos et al., 2017; Izubuchi et al., 2019) by systematically studying the continuum limit with three lattice spacings, which was previously attempted with only two spacings (Green et al., 2017; Izubuchi et al., 2019; Gao et al., 2020). The results demonstrate that \(O(a)\) discretization effects are present and must be controlled, enabling future lattice calculations to reliably extract PDFs from quasi-PDFs with controlled systematic uncertainties.
