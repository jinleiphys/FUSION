# Quark-Model Baryon-Baryon Interaction and its Applications to Hypernuclei

> The quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2, proposed by the Kyoto-Niigata group, is a unified model for the complete baryon octet (B_8=N, Lambda, Sigma and Xi), which is formulated in a framework of the (3q)-(3q) resonating-group method (RGM) using the spin-flavor SU_6 quark-model wave functions and effective meson-exchange potentials at the quark level. Model parameters are determined to reproduce properties of the nucleon-nucleon system and the low-energy cross section data for the hyperon-nucleon scattering. Due to the several improvements including the introduction of vector-meson exchange potentials, fss2 has achieved very accurate description of the NN and YN interactions, comparable to various one-boson exchange potentials. We review the essential features of fss2 and our previous model FSS, and their predictions to few-body systems in confrontation with the available experimental data. Some characteristic features of the B_8 B_8 interactions with the higher strangeness, S=-2, -3, -4, predicted by fss2 are discussed. These quark-model interactions are now applied to realistic calculations of few-body systems in a new three-cluster Faddeev formalism which uses two-cluster RGM kernels. As for the few-body systems, we discuss the three-nucleon bound states, the Lambda NN-Sigma NN system for the hypertriton, the alpha alpha Lambda system for 9Be Lambda, and the Lambda Lambda alpha system for 6He Lambda Lambda.

## Key claim

The quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2 achieves an accurate description of nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions comparable to one-boson exchange potentials, but the paper's claim that it reproduces the triton binding energy without reducing the deuteron D-state probability is qualified by the fact that the fss2 prediction of -8.52 MeV for the triton is still about 150 keV less bound than experiment after charge-dependence corrections are applied, and this difference is attributed to three-nucleon force effects that are smaller than generally accepted values of 0.5-1 MeV.

## Method

The authors formulate the baryon-baryon interaction using the (3q)-(3q) resonating-group method (RGM) with spin-flavor SU6 quark-model wave functions, incorporating a colored version of the one-gluon exchange Fermi-Breit interaction and effective meson-exchange potentials (scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector) acting between quarks. Model parameters are determined to reproduce NN system properties and low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering cross section data, and the resulting RGM kernels are used in a new three-cluster Faddeev formalism that eliminates three-cluster redundant components by orthogonality to pairwise two-cluster Pauli-forbidden states.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy predicted by fss2: -8.52 MeV (full 50-channel calculation up to maximum angular momentum J=6), compared to experimental value of -8.48 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy from fss2: 2.2247 MeV (experimental: 2.224644 ± 0.000046 MeV)
- Deuteron D-state probability from fss2: 5.49% (FSS: 5.86%, Bonn-B: 4.99%)
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio from fss2: 0.0253 (experimental: 0.0256 ± 0.0004)
- Deuteron rms radius from fss2: 1.960 fm (experimental: 1.9635 ± 0.0046 fm)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment from fss2: 0.270 fm² (experimental: 0.2860 ± 0.0015 fm²)
- Deuteron magnetic moment from fss2: 0.8485 μ_N (experimental: 0.857406 ± 0.000001 μ_N)
- ΔB_ΛΛ value from the Nagara event for ⁶_ΛΛHe: 1.01 ± 0.20 MeV, with fss2 G-matrix calculation yielding 1.12-1.24 MeV
- Hypertriton Λ separation energy experimental value: 130 ± 50 keV
- Self-consistent two-cluster RGM kernel energy ε_NN for fss2: 4.492 MeV
- Kinetic energy expectation value for triton from fss2: 43.99 MeV; potential energy: -52.51 MeV
- In-medium Ξ⁻N total cross section around p_lab ~ 550 MeV/c: 30 ± 6.7^{+3.7}_{-3.6} mb (Eikonal analysis: 20.9 ± 4.5^{+2.5}_{-2.4} mb)

## Context

This work extends the Kyoto-Niigata group's previous quark-model interaction FSS (Fujiwara et al., 1996-1998) by introducing vector-meson exchange potentials and momentum-dependent Bryan-Scott terms, achieving accuracy comparable to one-boson exchange potentials like the Bonn-B potential (Machleidt, 1989) and the Paris potential. The fss2 predictions for the ΛΛ interaction are in good agreement with the Nagara event (Takahashi et al., 2001) for the double Λ hypernucleus ⁶_ΛΛHe, and the Faddeev calculations of the three-nucleon bound state show that the quark-model approach can reproduce the triton binding energy with a three-nucleon force contribution smaller than that required by modern meson-theoretical NN interactions such as CD-Bonn and AV18 (Nogga et al., 2000). The work enables systematic studies of few-body hypernuclear systems including the hypertriton, ⁹_ΛBe, and ⁶_ΛΛHe using a unified quark-model framework.
