# Probing new bosons and nuclear structure with ytterbium isotope shifts

> In this Letter, we present mass-ratio measurements on highly charged Yb$^{42+}$ ions with a precision of $4\times 10^{-12}$ and isotope-shift measurements on Yb$^{+}$ on the $^{2}$S$_{1/2}$ $\to$ $^{2}$D$_{5/2}$ and $^{2}$S$_{1/2}$ $\to$ $^{2}$F$_{7/2}$ transitions with a precision of $4\times 10^{-9}$ for the isotopes $^{168,170,172,174,176}$Yb. We present a new method that allows us to extract higher-order changes in the nuclear charge distribution along the Yb isotope chain, benchmarking ab-initio nuclear structure calculations. Additionally, we perform a King plot analysis to set bounds on a fifth force in the keV$/c^2$ to MeV$/c^2$ range coupling to electrons and neutrons.

## Key claim

The paper presents mass-ratio and isotope-shift measurements that are up to two orders of magnitude more precise than previous work, enabling the extraction of higher-order changes in nuclear charge distributions and competitive bounds on a fifth force. However, the King plot analysis reveals a 23-sigma preference for more than one source of nonlinearity, meaning the new boson interpretation is not uniquely favored and nuclear deformation (specifically the quartic charge radius) is identified as the leading source of nonlinearity.

## Method

The authors performed cyclotron frequency ratio measurements of highly charged Yb42+ ions using the Pentatrap Penning-trap mass spectrometer to determine nuclear mass ratios with 4e-12 precision. They also conducted absolute frequency measurements of the 2S1/2 to 2D5/2 (alpha) and 2S1/2 to 2F7/2 (gamma) transitions in singly charged Yb+ ions using laser spectroscopy with a cryogenic silicon cavity reference. A generalized King plot analysis was constructed from seven mass-normalized isotope-shift vectors, and the nonlinearity decomposition was compared to predictions from ab initio nuclear structure calculations using valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) and quasi-particle vacua shell model (QVSM) with chiral effective field theory interactions.

## Key numbers

* Mass ratios of ytterbium isotopes relative to 172Yb determined with relative precision of 4e-12, corresponding to uncertainties of 0.3 Hz on isotope shifts.
* Isotope shifts for the alpha transition measured with uncertainties below 6 Hz (e.g., 2179098868.0(5.3) Hz for the 168-170 pair).
* Isotope shifts for the gamma transition measured with uncertainties below 16 Hz (e.g., -4438159671.1(15.7) Hz for the 168-170 pair).
* Deviations from King plot linearity averaging to 20.17(2) kHz.
* Electron binding energy for 172Yb42+ calculated as 350773(5) eV and for neutral 172Yb as 382301(16) eV.
* Reference value of drfour176,174 = 7 fm4 used for extracting quartic charge radius changes.

## Context

This work extends previous isotope-shift measurements in ytterbium by Counts (2020) and Hur (2022) with significantly improved precision, and corrects earlier mass-ratio measurements by Rana (2012) and Nesterenko (2020). The generalized King plot method follows the approach of Berengut (2020) and enables competitive spectroscopic bounds on new bosons coupling to electrons and neutrons, superseding bounds from Ca+ King plots by Solaro (2020) and Chang (2023). The ab initio nuclear structure calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions provide a first microscopic description of Yb nuclei, benchmarking predictions against experimentally extracted quartic charge radii. Future work can extend this approach to neutron-rich calcium isotopes to investigate the puzzling charge radius increase toward 52Ca observed by Garcia Ruiz (2016).
