# Isospin-symmetry breaking in masses of $N\simeq Z$ nuclei

> Effects of the isospin-symmetry breaking (ISB) beyond mean-field Coulomb terms are systematically studied in nuclear masses near the $N=Z$ line. The Coulomb exchange contributions are calculated exactly. We use extended Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) with proton-neutron-mixed densities, to which we add new terms breaking the isospin symmetry. Two parameters associated with the new terms are determined by fitting mirror and triplet displacement energies (MDEs and TDEs) of isospin multiplets. The new EDFs reproduce MDEs for the $T=\frac12$ doublets and $T=1$ triplets, and TDEs for the $T=1$ triplets. Relative strengths of the obtained isospin-symmetry-breaking terms {\em are not} consistent with the differences in the $NN$ scattering lengths, $a_{nn}$, $a_{pp}$, and $a_{np}$. Based on low-energy experimental data, it seems thus impossible to delineate the strong-force ISB effects from beyond-mean-field Coulomb-energy corrections.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding two isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) terms to Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) can systematically reproduce mirror and triplet displacement energies (MDEs and TDEs) for nuclei near the N=Z line, but the extracted strengths of these terms are not consistent with differences in NN scattering lengths (a_nn, a_pp, a_np), implying that low-energy experimental data cannot disentangle strong-force ISB effects from beyond-mean-field Coulomb corrections.

## Method

The authors extended Skyrme EDFs with zero-range class-II and class-III ISB forces, implemented in the HFODD code using the isocranking method to access T=1 states. They calculated Coulomb exchange contributions exactly and fitted two coupling constants (t0^II and t0^III) to experimental MDEs and TDEs of T=1/2 doublets and T=1 triplets for nuclei with 10 ≤ A ≤ 75, using linear regression. The TDEs were obtained from energies of three Slater determinants via an isospin projection procedure that removes dispersion in T_z.

## Key numbers

- MDEs calculated with mean-field Coulomb alone are systematically about 10% lower than experimental values.
- For the SV_T^ISB functional, the overall rms deviation of TDEs decreases from 190 keV to 69 keV after including the class-II force; for A=4n+2 triplets, rms decreases from 250 keV to 75 keV, while for A=4n triplets it changes from 58 keV to 60 keV.
- Fitted coupling constants for SV_T^ISB: t0^II = 4.6 ± 1.6 MeV fm^3, t0^III = -7.4 ± 1.9 MeV fm^3; for SkM*^ISB: t0^II = 7 ± 4 MeV fm^3, t0^III = -5.6 ± 1.4 MeV fm^3; for SLy4^ISB: t0^II = 6 ± 4 MeV fm^3, t0^III = -5.6 ± 1.1 MeV fm^3.
- Ratio t0^II/t0^III from experiment-based estimate using NN scattering lengths is -2.5 ± 0.5, but the fitted ratios are -0.6 ± 0.3 (SV_T^ISB), -1.3 ± 0.8 (SkM*^ISB), and -1.1 ± 0.7 (SLy4^ISB), all 2–4 times smaller.
- Predicted mass excesses: ^52Co = -34370(40) keV, ^56Cu = -38650(40) keV, ^73Rb = -46100(80) keV, ^44V = -23710(40) keV.
- For the same set of nuclei as analyzed in Ormand (1989), the two-parameter SV_T^ISB fit yields rms deviations of 220 keV for MDEs and 66 keV for TDEs, compared to 44 keV and 23 keV from the 29-parameter shell-model fit.

## Context

This work extends the earlier DFT studies of Suzuki (1993) and Brown (2000), who used class-III ISB terms to describe MDEs, by adding class-II terms within a proton-neutron mixed DFT framework (Sato (2013), Sheikh (2014)) to also reproduce TDEs. It contradicts the expectation that ISB strengths should match those derived from NN scattering lengths (Miller (1990)), and it enables predictions of mass excesses for neutron-deficient nuclei such as ^52Co, ^56Cu, ^73Rb, and ^44V, which can be tested by future experiments. The results also disprove the motivation for a phenomenological (-1)^Z term in Coulomb energies introduced by Nolen and Schiffer (1969) and Shlomo (1978).
