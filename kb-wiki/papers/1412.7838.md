# Topics in Low-Energy QCD with Strange Quarks

> Recent developments and pending issues in low-energy strong interaction physics with strangeness are summarized. Chiral SU(3) effective field theory has progressed as the appropriate theoretical framework applied to antikaon- and hyperon-nuclear systems. Topics include antikaon-nucleon interactions and the Lambda(1405), KbarNN systems, recent developments in hyperon-nucleon interactions and strangeness in dense baryonic matter with emphasis on new constraints from neutron star observations.

## Key claim

Chiral SU(3) effective field theory, applied non-perturbatively via coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equations, provides the appropriate framework for low-energy QCD with strange quarks, but the existence of the Λ(1405) just 27 MeV below the K̄N threshold rules out a perturbative chiral perturbation theory treatment as an expansion in powers of small momenta.

## Method

The paper reviews chiral SU(3) coupled-channels dynamics applied to antikaon-nucleon interactions, using the chiral effective Lagrangian at next-to-leading order as input while solving coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equations to all orders. For hyperon-nucleon interactions, the chiral SU(3) effective field theory framework is developed at next-to-leading order incorporating one- and two-meson exchange mechanisms involving the complete pseudoscalar meson octet. The resulting interactions are applied to three-body K̄NN systems via variational and Faddeev calculations, and to dense baryonic matter through Brueckner G-matrix computations.

## Key numbers

- K⁻p scattering length: Re a(K⁻p) = (-0.65 ± 0.10) fm, Im a(K⁻p) = (0.81 ± 0.15) fm, constrained by SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurement
- K⁻n scattering length: Re a(K⁻n) = (0.57⁺⁰·⁰⁴₋₀·₂₁) fm, Im a(K⁻n) = (0.72⁺⁰·²⁶₋₀·⁴¹) fm from NLO calculations
- Λ(1405) two-pole structure: first pole at (E₁, Γ₁/2) = (1424 ± 15, 26 ± 5) MeV with dominant K̄N component; second pole at (E₂, Γ₂/2) = (1381 ± 15, 81 ± 8) MeV with dominant πΣ component
- K⁻d scattering length: a(K⁻d) = (-1.55 + i 1.66) fm from three-body multiple scattering calculation
- Predicted kaonic deuterium 1s strong-interaction shift and width: ΔE = 0.87 keV, Γ = 1.19 keV
- K⁻pp binding energies from chiral SU(3) dynamics: 9-23 MeV across three calculations; widths: 34-70 MeV
- Λ single particle potential in nuclear matter stabilizes at values consistent with hypernuclear properties
- Two neutron stars observed with M ≈ 2M⊙ set constraints on equation of state stiffness

## Context

This review extends the chiral SU(3) effective field theory framework previously applied to ππ, πN and NN scattering (HKW 2013, HRW 2014) into the strangeness sector, addressing the non-perturbative nature of antikaon-nucleon interactions that was recognized in earlier work (KSW 1995). The two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) from chiral dynamics (HJ 2012) is contrasted with earlier phenomenological single-pole models (AY). The paper connects to lattice QCD studies (Hall 2014) showing the Λ(1405) transitions from a three-quark state at heavy quark masses to a quasi-molecular K̄N structure at physical light-quark masses. The neutron star constraints from two-solar-mass observations are compared with quantum Monte Carlo computations including hyperons (LLGP 2014) and with ChEFT results using nucleon and pion degrees of freedom (HW 2014). The review enables focused experimental programs including SIDDHARTA-2 kaonic deuterium measurements and J-PARC experiments E15, E27, and E31.
