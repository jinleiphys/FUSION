# On the Equation of State of Nuclear Matter in 158A GeV Pb+Pb Collisions

> Within a hydrodynamical approach we investigate the sensitivity of single inclusive momentum spectra of hadrons in 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions to three different equations of state of nuclear matter. Two of the equations of state are based on lattice QCD results and include a phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma. The third equation of state has been extracted from the microscopic transport code RQMD under the assumption of complete local thermalization. All three equations of state provide reasonable fits to data taken by the NA44 and NA49 Collaborations. The initial conditions before the evolution of the fireballs and the space-time evolution pictures differ dramatically for the three equations of state when the same freeze-out temperature is used in all calculations. However, the softest of the equations of state results in transverse mass spectra that are too steep in the central rapidity region. We conclude that the transverse particle momenta are determined by the effective softness of the equation of state during the fireball expansion.

## Key claim

The transverse particle momenta in 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions are determined by the effective softness of the equation of state during fireball expansion, but the softest equation of state tested (EOS-II, with a critical temperature of 160 MeV) produces transverse mass spectra that are too steep in the central rapidity region compared to experimental data, whereas the other two equations of state provide reasonable fits.

## Method

The authors used the one-fluid hydrodynamical simulation code HYLANDER-C to solve the relativistic Euler equations with three different equations of state (EOS-I and EOS-II based on lattice QCD results with phase transitions to quark-gluon plasma, and EOS-III extracted from the RQMD transport model assuming complete local thermalization with no QGP transition). They compared the calculated single inclusive momentum spectra of hadrons (negative hadrons, protons, positive kaons, and positive pions) to experimental data from the NA44 and NA49 Collaborations at 158A GeV Pb+Pb collisions, using a fixed freeze-out temperature of 139 MeV for all calculations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for EOS-I: T_c = 200 MeV with critical energy density epsilon_c = 3.2 GeV/fm^3
- Critical temperature for EOS-II: T_c = 160 MeV with critical energy density epsilon_c approximately 1.5 GeV/fm^3
- Maximum initial energy density: epsilon_Delta = 15.3 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-I), 6.55 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-II), 8.66 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-III)
- Maximum initial baryon density: n_B^max = 3.93 fm^{-3} (EOS-I), 1.24 fm^{-3} (EOS-II), 1.92 fm^{-3} (EOS-III)
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon_f = 0.292 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-I), 0.126 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-II), 0.130 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-III)
- Freeze-out temperature fixed at T_f = 139 MeV for all calculations
- Fireball lifetime: t_max = 13.1 fm/c (EOS-I), 29.3 fm/c (EOS-II), 27.3 fm/c (EOS-III)
- Maximum transverse velocity at freeze-out: v_perp^max = 0.46 c (EOS-I), 0.30 c (EOS-II), 0.42 c (EOS-III)
- Lifetime of QGP phase: t_QGP = 2.4 fm/c (EOS-I), 3.0 fm/c (EOS-II), none for EOS-III
- 71% of baryonic matter initially in central fireball for EOS-I, 30% for EOS-II, 36% for EOS-III

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamical approach of Schlei (1997) and Ornik, Plumer, Schlei, Strottman, and Weiner (1996) by testing three different equations of state against the same experimental data from NA44 and NA49 Collaborations. The study contradicts the expectation that a softer equation of state with a phase transition would necessarily provide the best description of data, as the softest EOS (EOS-II from Hung and Shuryak, 1995) produced transverse mass spectra inconsistent with measurements. The results enable future combined analyses of single inclusive momentum spectra and Bose-Einstein correlation functions to further constrain possible equations of state, as suggested by Ferenc (1996) and Gyulassy, Rischke, and Zhang (1997).
