# Gamma strength function and level density of $^{208}$Pb from forward-angle proton scattering at 295 MeV

> Gamma strength functions (GSFs) and level densities (LDs) are essential ingredients of statistical nuclear reaction theory with many applications in astrophysics, reactor design, and waste transmutation. The aim of the present work is a test of systematic parametrizations of the GSF recommended by the RIPL-3 data base for the case of $^{208}$Pb. The upward GSF and LD in $^{208}$Pb are compared to gamma decay data from an Oslo-type experiment to examine the validity of the Brink-Axel (BA) hypothesis. The E1 and M1 parts of the total GSF are determined from high-resolution forward angle inelastic proton scattering data taken at 295 MeV at RCNP, Osaka, Japan. The total LD in $^{208}$Pb is derived from the $1^-$ LD extracted with a fluctuation analysis in the energy region of the isovector giant dipole resonance. The E1 GSF is compared to parametrizations recommended by the RIPL-3 data base showing systematic deficiencies of all models in the energy region around neutron threshold. The new data for the poorly known spinflip M1 resonance call for a substantial revision of the model suggested in RIPL-3. The total GSF derived from the present data is larger in the PDR energy region than the Oslo data but the strong fluctuations due to the low LD resulting from the double shell closure of $^{208}$Pb prevent a conclusion on a possible violation of the BA hypothesis. Using the parameters suggested by RIPL-3 for a description of the LD in $^{208}$Pb with the back-shifted Fermi gas model, remarkable agreement between the two experiments spanning a wide excitation energy range is obtained. Systematic parametrizations of the E1 and M1 GSF parts need to be reconsidered at low excitation energies. The good agreement of LD provides an independent confirmation of the approach underlying the decomposition of GSF and LDs in Oslo-type experiments.

# Gamma strength function and level density of $^{208}$Pb from forward-angle proton scattering at 295 MeV

## Key claim

The E1 and M1 gamma strength functions (GSFs) and level density (LD) of $^{208}$Pb extracted from proton scattering data show systematic deficiencies in the RIPL-3 database parametrizations, particularly around neutron threshold and for the spinflip M1 resonance; however, the strong fluctuations due to the low level density in doubly magic $^{208}$Pb prevent a definitive conclusion on whether the Brink-Axel hypothesis is violated in the pygmy dipole resonance energy region.

## Method

The E1 and M1 parts of the total GSF were determined from high-resolution forward-angle inelastic proton scattering data at 295 MeV taken at RCNP, Osaka, using multipole decomposition analysis and the virtual photon method. The total level density in $^{208}$Pb was derived from the $1^-$ level density extracted via fluctuation analysis in the isovector giant dipole resonance energy region (9–12.5 MeV), then converted to total LD using three different back-shifted Fermi gas model parametrizations. The resulting GSFs and LDs were compared to reanalyzed Oslo-type ($^3$He,$^3$He$^\prime\gamma$) decay data and to the SLO, MLO, and EGLO model predictions recommended by RIPL-3.

## Key numbers

- E1 GSF at neutron threshold ($S_n = 7.37$ MeV) from experimental systematics is almost an order of magnitude smaller than experimental strengths in the PDR region.
- Total spinflip M1 resonance strength: $\sum B({\rm M1}) = 20.5(13) \, \mu_N^2$; empirical models overpredict this by factors of 2 (EGLO) to 5 (SLO).
- E2 strength distribution from ($\alpha,\alpha'$) data exhausts $100 \pm 15\%$ of the energy-weighted sum rule.
- Level densities from the three BSFG models normalized to the data point at $S_n$ with factors 1.15 (Ref. Sye09), 2.18 (RIPL-3), and 0.52 (Ref. Rauscher97).
- Level counting from Ref. Heusler (2016) shows approximately a factor of two missing levels at 6 MeV compared to complete spectroscopy up to 6.2 MeV.

## Context

This work tests the systematic parametrizations of GSFs and LDs recommended in the RIPL-3 database (Capote 2009) for the specific case of $^{208}$Pb, extending prior measurements of E1 strength from the same ($p,p'$) experiment (Tamii 2011, Poltoratska 2012) and M1 extraction using isospin symmetry (Birkhan 2016). The comparison with reanalyzed Oslo-method data (Siem 2009, Guttormsen 2016) provides an independent test of the Brink-Axel hypothesis and validates the decomposition of GSF and LD in Oslo-type experiments. The results enable improved parametrizations of the spinflip M1 resonance and motivate similar studies in open-shell nuclei with higher level densities to test the BA hypothesis more conclusively.
