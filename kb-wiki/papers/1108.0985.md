# Renormalizing Chiral Nuclear Forces: A Case Study of 3P0

> We discuss in this paper the subleading contact interactions, or counterterms, in the 3P0 channel of nucleon-nucleon scattering up to O(Q^3), where, already at leading order, Weinberg's original power counting (WPC) scheme fails to fulfill renormalization group invariance due to the singular attraction of one-pion exchange. Treating the subleading interactions as perturbations and using renormalization group invariance as the criterion, we investigate whether WPC, although missing the leading order, could prescribe correct subleading counterterms. We find that the answer is negative and, instead, that the structure of counterterms agrees with a modified version of naive dimensional analysis. Using 3P0 as an example, we also study the cutoffs where the subleading potential can be iterated together with the leading one.

# Renormalizing Chiral Nuclear Forces: A Case Study of 3P0

## Key claim

Weinberg's original power counting (WPC) fails to prescribe correct subleading counterterms for the 3P0 channel of nucleon-nucleon scattering when subleading interactions are treated as perturbations and renormalization group invariance is used as the criterion; instead, the counterterm structure agrees with a modified version of naive dimensional analysis (mNDA), though the authors note that nonperturbative treatment of WPC can still fulfill renormalization group invariance in a limited cutoff window.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a sharp momentum cutoff for the 3P0 partial wave, treating one-pion exchange (OPE) as the leading-order long-range potential and two-pion exchanges (TPEs) as subleading corrections up to O(Q^3). They compare two counterterm prescriptions: the WPC prescription with one counterterm per order (C p' p) and the mNDA prescription with additional momentum-dependent counterterms (D p' p (p'^2 + p^2)), fitting phase shifts to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis at selected laboratory energies and examining cutoff dependence as a test of renormalization group invariance.

## Key numbers

- The leading 3P0 counterterm is enhanced by O(M_hi^2 / M_lo^2) relative to WPC, where M_hi ~ m_sigma and M_lo ~ 100 MeV; the leading S-wave counterterms scale generically as C_S ~ 4 pi / (m_N M_lo)
- The residual cutoff dependence of the renormalized LO 3P0 amplitude is O(Lambda^{-5/2}), which vanishes faster than O(Lambda^{-2}) and thus does not require a nonvanishing O(Q) correction
- The superficial divergence of T^(2) from TPE scales as Lambda^{5/2} plus a k^2 term, while T^(3) scales as Lambda^{7/2} plus a Lambda k^2 term
- Low-energy constants for pi pi NN seagull couplings used: c1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c3 = -4.7 GeV^{-1}, c4 = 3.4 GeV^{-1}
- Phase shifts are fit to PWA at T_lab = 20 and 50 MeV for the mNDA prescription, and at T_lab = 50 MeV for the WPC prescription; improved fits use T_lab = 25, 50, 75, and 100 MeV
- Cutoff values examined include Lambda = 400 MeV and Lambda = 1200 MeV for comparison of perturbative and nonperturbative schemes at O(Q^3)

## Context

This work extends the critique of Weinberg's original power counting (WPC) for nuclear forces, which was shown by Nogga (2005) and others to fail renormalization group invariance in channels with singular OPE attraction such as 3P0, 3S1-3D1, and 3P2-3F2. The authors build on a model study by Long (2007) that proposed modified naive dimensional analysis (mNDA) for singular attractive potentials, and test whether mNDA applies to realistic chiral nuclear forces. The results enable a systematic renormalization-group-invariant power counting for triplet partial waves in chiral effective field theory, though the authors note that a simultaneous coordinate-space calculation by Valderrama (2011) found a proliferation of six counterterms in coupled channels (3S1-3D1 and 3P2-3F2) whereas mNDA suggests three, leaving open questions for future momentum-space calculations of those channels.
