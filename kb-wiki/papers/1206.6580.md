# Velocity-dependent energy gaps and dynamics of superfluid neutron stars

> We show that suppression of the baryon energy gaps, caused by the relative motion of superfluid and normal liquid components, can substantially influence dynamical properties and evolution of neutron stars. This effect has been previously ignored in the neutron-star literature.

# Velocity-dependent energy gaps and dynamics of superfluid neutron stars

## Key claim
The suppression of baryon energy gaps due to the relative motion between superfluid and normal liquid components (the "Delta-V effect") can substantially influence neutron star dynamics, but this effect had been previously ignored in the neutron-star literature. The effect operates when the relative velocity Delta-V is comparable to a critical value Delta-V_cr, which depends on temperature and density.

## Method
The authors solve the BCS gap equation modified for nonzero superfluid current, where fermions pair with momenta (-p+Q, p+Q) rather than (-p, p), and the total Cooper pair momentum is 2Q = 2m V_s. They obtain numerical solutions for the energy gap Delta as a function of temperature T and momentum Q = m V_s, then apply these results to model radial oscillations of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star using finite-temperature superfluid hydrodynamics with the APR equation of state and specific nucleon superfluidity models.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for superfluidity at Q=0: T_c ≈ 0.567 Delta_0 (BCS result)
- Critical momentum at T=0: Q_cr0 = (e/2) * (Delta_0 * m / p_F)
- Estimated critical relative velocity: Delta V_cr(T) ~ 10^7 * [Delta(T,0)/(10^9 K)] * (n_0/n)^(1/3) cm/s, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Neutron star model: gravitational mass M = 1.4 M_sun, circumferential radius R = 12.2 km, central density rho_c = 9.26 × 10^14 g cm^{-3}
- Redshifted proton critical temperature: T_cp^infinity = 5 × 10^9 K (constant)
- Maximum redshifted neutron critical temperature: T_cn_max^infinity = 6 × 10^8 K (at stellar centre)
- Oscillation frequencies of first radial mode: omega/(10^4 s^{-1}) ≈ 1.702, 1.702, 1.064, and 0.516 for T^infinity = 3.0×10^7 K, 8.0×10^7 K, 2.0×10^8 K, and 5.0×10^8 K respectively
- For oscillation energy E_mech = 10^47 erg, the oscillation amplitude epsilon ≈ 4.4 × 10^{-4} (for a nonsuperfluid NS)
- For E_mech = 10^48 erg, epsilon ≈ 1.4 × 10^{-3}; for E_mech = 5×10^48 erg, epsilon ≈ 3.1 × 10^{-3}

## Context
This work extends the BCS theory of superfluidity in neutron stars by incorporating the velocity dependence of energy gaps, an effect known in superconductor physics (Bardeen 1962) but previously ignored in neutron-star literature. It builds on prior work on superfluid hydrodynamics by Andersson and Comer (2007), Gusakov (2007), and Kantor and Gusakov (2011), as well as entrainment calculations by Gusakov and Haensel (2005). The effect is complementary to the bulk viscosity enhancement mechanism identified by Alford, Reddy, and Schwenzer (2012), but has wider applicability as it directly influences baryon energy gaps and thus all aspects of neutron star dynamics. The results enable future studies of nonlinear oscillation equations, mutual friction reduction in rotating stars, and revised kinetic coefficients for superfluid neutron star matter.
