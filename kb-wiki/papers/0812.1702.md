# Hadron properties in the nuclear medium

> The QCD vacuum shows the dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry. In the hot/dense QCD medium, the chiral order parameter such as $ < \bar q q > $ is expected to change as function of temperature $T$ and density $ρ$ of the medium, and its experimental detection is one of the main challenges in modern hadron physics. In this article, we discuss theoretical expectations for the in-medium hadron spectra associated with partial restoration of chiral symmetry and the current status of experiments with an emphasis on the measurements of properties of mesons produced in near-ground-state nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical expectations and experimental status of in-medium hadron spectra as probes of partial chiral symmetry restoration, but emphasizes that experimental results on vector meson mass shifts and widths have not yet converged, with inconsistencies between experiments such as E325 and CLAS g7 on the ρ(ω) mass shift and between E325 and CBELSA/TAPS on the ω width.

## Method

The authors compile and compare experimental results from hadron-nucleus and photon-nucleus reactions, heavy-ion collisions, and deeply bound mesic atoms, focusing on measurements of meson properties in near-ground-state nuclei. They analyze theoretical frameworks including in-medium chiral perturbation theory, the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation, and the Tomozawa-Weinberg relation to connect chiral condensate changes to observable hadron spectral modifications.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate reduction at nuclear matter density: ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ/⟨q̄q⟩_0 ≃ 1 − (0.35 ± 0.09)(ρ/ρ_0) from linear density approximation
- Pion decay constant at finite density: f_π^t(ρ)/f_π = 1 − (0.26 ± 0.04)(ρ/ρ_0) and f_π^s(ρ)/f_π = 1 − (1.23 ± 0.07)(ρ/ρ_0)
- E325 experiment: ρ and ω masses reduced at ρ_0 by 9% (mass shift parameter k = 0.092 ± 0.002)
- CLAS g7 experiment: 95% confidence upper limit on mass shift parameter at k = 0.053
- CBELSA/TAPS: ω width at ρ_0 of 130−150 MeV/c², corresponding to σ_ωN ≈ 70 mb
- LEPS: φN cross section σ_φN = 35 mb, leading to Γ_φ(ρ_0) ≈ 80 MeV/c²
- Lattice QCD chiral condensate: ⟨S⁰⟩_0 = −(242 (04)(⁺¹⁹_₋₁₈) MeV)³ at κ = 2 GeV

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework connecting chiral symmetry breaking to hadron spectra, building on the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner, 1968) and in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculations by Kaiser (2008) and others. The paper highlights unresolved discrepancies between experiments such as E325 at KEK, CLAS g7 at JLab, and CBELSA/TAPS, and identifies the need for new high-statistics data from the Crystal Ball/TAPS detector (Metag, 2008, private communication) to resolve the interpretation of ππ spectra and vector meson in-medium properties.
