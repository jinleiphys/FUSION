# Self-consistent band calculation of slab phase in neutron-star crust

> Fully self-consistent band calculation has been performed for slab phase in neutron-star inner crust, using the BCPM energy density functional. Optimized slab structure is calculated at given baryon density either with the fixed proton ratio or with the beta-equilibrium condition. Numerical results indicate the band gap of in order of keV to tens of keV, and the mobility of dripped neutrons are enhanced by the Bragg scattering, which leads to the macroscopic effective mass, $\bar{m}^*_z/m_n=0.65\sim 0.75$ near the bottom of the inner crust in neutron stars. We also compare the results of the band calculation with those of the Thomas-Fermi approximation. The Thomas-Fermi approximation becomes invalid at low density with high proton ratio.

## Key claim

Fully self-consistent band calculations for the slab phase in neutron-star inner crust, using the BCPM energy density functional, show that the band gap is on the order of keV to tens of keV and that the macroscopic effective mass near the bottom of the inner crust is \(\bar{m}^*_z/m_n = 0.65\sim 0.75\), but these results are obtained only for the slab geometry and neglect spin-orbit and pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Kohn-Sham calculations for periodic slab nuclei using the BCPM energy density functional, solving the Bloch equations in one dimension with the Bloch wave number discretized into \(N_k=30\) points. They optimized the lattice constant (slab interval) \(a\) by minimizing the total energy per baryon at fixed baryon density, both for fixed proton ratio and under beta-equilibrium conditions, and compared results with Thomas-Fermi calculations using the same functional.

## Key numbers

- Band gap is on the order of keV to tens of keV for the slab phase in neutron-star inner crust.
- Macroscopic effective mass \(\bar{m}^*_z/m_n = 0.65\sim 0.75\) near the bottom of the inner crust.
- Optimum lattice constant \(a = 39.2\) fm for the case of proton fraction \(Y_p=0.05\) and baryon density \(n_B=0.04\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Energy convergence criterion used: \(|(E_B - E_B^{(i)})/E_B| < 10^{-10}\).
- Mesh spacing \(\Delta z = 0.2\) fm used for discretization of the unit cell.

## Context

This work extends the band-theory approach to neutron-star crusts pioneered by Chamel (2012), who performed non-self-consistent band calculations using an extended Thomas-Fermi potential and predicted large effective masses (\(m^*/m_n\) up to 10 or larger). The present self-consistent calculation using the BCPM functional yields much smaller effective masses (\(\bar{m}^*_z/m_n \sim 0.65-0.75\)), which has significant implications for the interpretation of pulsar glitches and the required moment of inertia of superfluid neutrons. The authors also show that the Thomas-Fermi approximation becomes invalid at low density with high proton ratio, and that the periodic boundary condition (equivalent to \(k_z=0\) only) produces spurious oscillations in the energy as a function of lattice constant, demonstrating the necessity of full band calculations for the inner crust.
