# Calculation of cluster decays half-lives for nuclei between 56 < Zp < 120 by using temperature dependent proximity model

> Cluster decays half-lives of elements with proton numbers between 56 < Zp < 120 are calculated by applying temperature dependent proximity potential approach. for showing the influence of temperature on cluster decays, we compared the results among temperature dependent and inde-pendent case with experimental values. The obtained results of the present investigation reveal that we have more accurate results for temperature dependent proximity potential in comparison to ignoring one. In the present work, we find that results provided with temperature dependent prox-imity model are reasonable estimates for cluster decays half-lives and provide reliable predictions for other super heavies cluster decays.

# Calculation of cluster decays half-lives for nuclei between 56 < Zp < 120 by using temperature dependent proximity model

## Key claim

Including a temperature dependence in the proximity potential improves the agreement between calculated and experimental cluster-decay half-lives compared to the temperature-independent version, but the improvement is modest and the model still provides only "reasonable estimates" rather than precise predictions, especially for superheavy nuclei where experimental data are absent.

## Method

The author calculates cluster-decay half-lives using the WKB approximation for the penetration probability through a potential barrier composed of nuclear, Coulomb, and centrifugal terms. The nuclear potential is obtained from the proximity-2010 model, modified to include temperature dependence in the surface energy coefficient, nuclear radii, and surface width via the relations γ(T), R_i(T), and b(T). The temperature of the parent nucleus is derived from the excitation energy using the relation E*_CN = (1/9)AT² − T. Half-lives are computed for 28 parent nuclei with known experimental data (emitting clusters including ¹⁴C, ¹⁸,²⁰O, ²³F, ²²,²⁴,²⁵,²⁶Ne, ²⁸,³⁰Mg, and ³⁴Si) and then extended to parent nuclei with proton numbers Z_p between 56 and 120.

## Key numbers

- For the 28 parent nuclei with experimental data, the relative error χ²_R (defined as the average squared deviation of log₁₀(T₁/₂)) is smaller for the temperature-dependent (TD) case than for the temperature-independent (IND) case; the exact numerical values of χ²_R are not given in the truncated text.
- The temperature dependence of the nuclear radius is given by R_i(T) = R_i(T=0)[1 + 0.0005T²] fm, and the surface width by b(T) = b(T=0)[1 + 0.009T²].
- The surface energy coefficient at temperature T is γ(T) = γ(T=0)[1 − (T − T_B)/T_B]^(3/2), with γ₀ = 1.460734 MeV/fm² and K_s = 4 for the proximity-2010 version used.
- The effective sharp radius formula used is R_i = 1.28A_i^(1/3) − 0.76 + 0.8A_i^(−1/3) fm.
- The zero-point vibration energy is given by E_v = Q[0.056 + 0.039 exp((4 − A_e)/2.5)], where A_e is the mass number of the emitted cluster.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study on temperature-dependent proximity potentials for alpha decay (Zanganeh 2014) to the case of cluster decays heavier than alpha particles. It builds on the proximity potential formalism originally developed by Blocki (1977) and later refined in the proximity-2010 version by Dutt (2010), as well as on the temperature-dependent modification introduced by Salehi (2013) for fusion reactions. The calculated half-lives are compared with the ASAF model predictions by Poenaru for superheavy nuclei, and the results suggest that the temperature-dependent proximity model can provide reasonable estimates for cluster-decay half-lives where experimental data are not yet available, particularly in the region of the island of stability.
