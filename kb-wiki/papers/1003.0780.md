# Consistency of field-theoretical and kinetic calculations of viscous transport coefficients for a relativistic fluid

> The transport coefficients of causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics are calculated both in a field-theoretical and a kinetic approach. We find that the results from the traditional kinetic calculation by Israel and Stewart are modified. The new expressions for the viscous transport coefficients agree with the results obtained in the field-theoretical approach when the contributions from pair creation and annihilation are neglected.

# Consistency of field-theoretical and kinetic calculations of viscous transport coefficients for a relativistic fluid

## Key claim
The apparent inconsistency between field-theoretical and kinetic calculations of viscous transport coefficients for causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics is resolved by showing that the traditional 14-moment approximation used by Israel and Stewart is not unique; a new kinetic calculation yields expressions for the shear and bulk viscosity coefficients that agree with the field-theoretical results, provided that contributions from pair creation and annihilation are neglected.

## Method
The authors performed two parallel calculations of the transport coefficients βη (shear viscosity/relaxation time) and βζ (bulk viscosity/relaxation time) for causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. In the kinetic approach, they derived evolution equations for the shear stress tensor and bulk viscous pressure directly from the comoving time derivative of the Boltzmann equation using a modified 14-moment ansatz for the distribution function. In the field-theoretical approach, they used the projection operator method to compute Kubo-type correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor in the free gas approximation, separating the results into a collision part and a pair annihilation-creation (PAC) part.

## Key numbers
- For bosons, the field-theoretical result including the PAC part gives βη,ft = P (the pressure), while the new kinetic calculation (neglecting PAC) yields βη = (1/15)[9P + ε - m⁴ ∫ d³k/(2π)³ E_k³ f₀(k)].
- For bosons, the field-theoretical result including the PAC part gives βζ,ft = (1/3 - c²_s)(ε + P) - (2/9)(ε - 3P), while the new kinetic calculation (neglecting PAC) yields βζ = (1/3 - c²_s)(ε + P) - (2/9)(ε - 3P) - (m⁴/9)∫ d³k/(2π)³ E_k³ f₀(k).
- For fermions, the full field-theoretical calculation gives βη,ft = 0 and βζ,ft = (1/3 - c²_s)(ε + P) - (1/3)(ε - 3P).
- The unified expressions for the field-theoretical results are βη,ft = |3-α|P and βζ,ft = [(s/3)(d/ds) - α/9](ε - 3P), where α=2 for bosons and α=3 for fermions, and s is the entropy density.
- The temperature dependence of βη/(ε+P) and βζ/(ε+P) was computed for pions with mass m=140 MeV, showing that the new kinetic calculation gives values above the original Israel-Stewart result, and the field-theoretical result including PAC gives even larger values.

## Context
This work resolves a discrepancy between the field-theoretical approach developed by Koide (2007) and the kinetic calculation of Israel and Stewart (1979) for transport coefficients of causal relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. The new kinetic calculation produces results consistent with the field-theoretical formula when pair creation and annihilation are neglected, establishing the correspondence that exists for Navier-Stokes fluids. The inclusion of PAC contributions, which are absent in the Boltzmann equation, yields transport coefficients expressible solely in terms of thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, and speed of sound), and for bulk viscosity gives a monotonically decreasing function of temperature similar to predictions from string theory calculations. This work enables improved modeling of relativistic fluid dynamics in heavy-ion collisions and astrophysics, and raises the open question of how to incorporate PAC effects into kinetic theory by extending the Boltzmann equation itself.
