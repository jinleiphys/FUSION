# Heavy quark dynamics for charm and bottom flavor on Fermi surface at zero temperature

> We discuss the dynamics in finite density medium including a heavy impurity particle (hadron or quark) with a heavy flavor, charm and bottom, at zero temperature. As a system, we consider a $\bar{D}$ ($B$) meson embedded in nuclear matter as a heavy impurity boson with SU(2) isospin symmetry. As another system, we also consider a charm (bottom) quark embedded in quark matter as a heavy impurity fermion with SU(3) color symmetry. We suppose a vector current interaction with SU($n$) symmetry ($n \ge 2$) for the fermion composing the Fermi surface and the embedded heavy impurity particle, and calculate the scattering amplitude perturbatively for the small coupling constant up to one-loop level. We obtain that the scattering amplitude has a logarithmic enhancement in the large mass limit of the heavy impurity particle, and show that the perturbative calculation breaks down for any small coupling constant in this limit.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a heavy impurity particle (meson or quark) embedded in a Fermi gas at zero temperature, the one-loop scattering amplitude acquires a logarithmic enhancement proportional to log(M) in the large mass limit, causing perturbative calculations to break down for any arbitrarily small coupling constant, a result that extends the Kondo effect to systems where the impurity mass is not effectively infinite.

## Method

The authors consider two systems: a \(\bar{D}\) (B) meson as a heavy impurity boson in nuclear matter with SU(2) isospin symmetry, and a charm (bottom) quark as a heavy impurity fermion in quark matter with SU(3) color symmetry. They assume a vector current interaction with SU(n) symmetry (n≥2) between the fermions composing the Fermi surface and the heavy impurity particle, and calculate the scattering amplitude perturbatively up to one-loop level using the in-medium fermion propagator with Pauli blocking effects. The analysis is performed separately for nonrelativistic and relativistic fermions in the limit where the heavy impurity mass goes to infinity.

## Key numbers

* For nonrelativistic fermions with a heavy impurity boson, the critical mass above which perturbation breaks down is \( M_{\rm B}^{\rm{cr,nonrel}} = m \exp\left( \frac{1}{n |G_{\rm B}|} \frac{4\pi^{2}}{2mk_{\rm F}} \right) \), where m is the fermion mass, k_F is the Fermi momentum, and G_B is the coupling constant.
* For relativistic (massless) fermions with a heavy impurity boson, the critical mass is \( M_{\rm B}^{\rm{cr,rel}} = k_{\rm F} \exp\left( \frac{1}{n |G_{\rm B}|} \frac{4\pi^{2}}{k_{\rm F}^{2}} \right) \).
* The second-order scattering amplitude for nonrelativistic fermions contains a term proportional to \( G_{\rm B}^{2} M_{\rm B} \log M_{\rm B} \) in the large mass limit, while the first-order term is proportional to \( G_{\rm B} M_{\rm B} \).
* For the case where the heavy impurity boson mass equals the fermion mass (M_B = m), no logarithmic term appears in the scattering amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the Kondo problem (Kondo, 1964) from condensed matter physics to heavy quark systems in nuclear and quark matter, where the impurity mass is not effectively infinite as in the electron-atom case. The analysis shows that the logarithmic enhancement found by Kondo for low-temperature scattering also appears in the heavy mass limit of charm and bottom hadrons embedded in Fermi gases, with the SU(n) symmetry structure (isospin for mesons, color for quarks) playing a crucial role. The results suggest that heavy mesons such as \(\bar{D}\) and B mesons in nuclear matter may exhibit non-perturbative behavior distinct from light mesons like K and \(\bar{K}\) mesons (Akaishi, 2002; Hyodo, 2011), and that similar effects should occur for charm and bottom quarks in quark matter where color superconductivity may arise (Alford, 2007; Fukushima, 2010).
