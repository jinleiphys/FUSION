# Random matrix ensembles with random interactions: Results for EGUE(2)-SU(4)

> We introduce in this paper embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble of random matrices, for $m$ fermions in $Ω$ number of single particle orbits, generated by random two-body interactions that are SU(4) scalar, called EGUE(2)-SU(4). Here the SU(4) algebra corresponds to Wigner's supermultiplet SU(4) symmetry in nuclei. Formulation based on Wigner-Racah algebra of the embedding algebra $U(4Ω) \supset U(Ω) \otimes SU(4)$ allows for analytical treatment of this ensemble and using this analytical formulas are derived for the covariances in energy centroids and spectral variances. It is found that these covariances increase in magnitude as we go from EGUE(2) to EGUE(2)-$\cs$ to EGUE(2)-SU(4) implying that symmetries may be responsible for chaos in finite interacting quantum systems.

## Key claim

The paper introduces EGUE(2)-SU(4), an embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble for m fermions in Ω single-particle orbits generated by random two-body interactions that are SU(4) scalar, and finds that the covariances in energy centroids and spectral variances increase in magnitude as one goes from EGUE(2) to EGUE(2)-s to EGUE(2)-SU(4), implying that symmetries may be responsible for chaos in finite interacting quantum systems.

## Method

The authors define the EGUE(2)-SU(4) ensemble using the embedding algebra U(4Ω) ⊃ U(Ω) ⊗ SU(4), where SU(4) corresponds to Wigner's supermultiplet symmetry in nuclei. They employ Wigner-Racah algebra to derive analytical formulas for the covariances in energy centroids (Σ₁₁) and spectral variances (Σ₂₂) for m-fermion U(Ω) irreps of the type {4ʳ,p} with p=0,1,2,3. Numerical calculations are carried out for Ω=6 (2s1d-shell) and Ω=10 (2p1f-shell) examples with λ²_{1²}=λ²_{2}=1.

## Key numbers

- For Ω=6 with m=m′, [Σ₁₁]^{1/2} ranges from approximately 10% to 28% and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2} ranges from approximately 6% to 16% as m changes from 4 to 12.
- For Ω=6 with m≠m′, [Σ₁₁]^{1/2} ranges from approximately 10% to 24% and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2} ranges from approximately 6% to 12%.
- For N=24 (Ω=12 for EGUE(2)-s and Ω=6 for EGUE(2)-SU(4)) with m=m′=6: [Σ₁₁]^{1/2}=0.017 and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2}=0.006 for EGUE(2); for EGUE(2)-s with S=S′=0, [Σ₁₁]^{1/2}=0.043 and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2}=0.017; for EGUE(2)-SU(4), [Σ₁₁]^{1/2}=0.124 and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2}=0.069.
- For N=24 with m=m′=8: [Σ₁₁]^{1/2}=0.026 and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2}=0.006 for EGUE(2); for EGUE(2)-s with S=S′=0, [Σ₁₁]^{1/2}=0.066 and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2}=0.021; for EGUE(2)-SU(4), [Σ₁₁]^{1/2}=0.16 and [Σ₂₂]^{1/2}=0.082.

## Context

This work extends the study of embedded Gaussian ensembles with symmetries, following prior work on EGUE(2) for spinless fermions by Kota (2005) and EGUE(2)-s for fermions with spin by Kota (2007). The paper introduces the SU(4) supermultiplet symmetry into the embedded ensemble framework, motivated by evidence for SU(4) symmetry in some parts of the periodic table and renewed interest in heavy N≈Z nuclei. The results enable future investigation of EGUE(2)-SU(4) for general U(Ω) irreps, comparison with EGOE(2)-SU(4), and extension to ensembles with full SU(4)-ST symmetry, as well as studies of rotational collectivity with random interactions via SU(3) subalgebras.
