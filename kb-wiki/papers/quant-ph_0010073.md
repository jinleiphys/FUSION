# Singular Potentials and Limit Cycles

> We show that a central $1/r^n$ singular potential (with $n\geq 2$) is renormalized by a one-parameter square-well counterterm; low-energy observables are made independent of the square-well width by adjusting the square-well strength. We find a closed form expression for the renormalization-group evolution of the square-well counterterm.

# Singular Potentials and Limit Cycles

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that attractive singular potentials of the form $1/r^n$ (with $n \geq 2$) can be renormalized by introducing a square-well counterterm at short distances, but only if the square-well strength is adjusted as a function of the cutoff radius to keep low-energy observables invariant; the renormalization-group flow of this counterterm exhibits periodic, limit-cycle behavior rather than the usual fixed-point behavior seen in standard effective field theories.

## Method

The authors consider two particles of reduced mass $M$ interacting via an $S$-wave potential consisting of a long-range singular $1/r^n$ tail (with characteristic scale $r_0$ and strength $\lambda_L/2Mr_0^2$) and a short-range square well of radius $R = \mathcal{R} r_0$ and depth $\lambda_S/2Mr_0^2$. They solve the radial Schrödinger equation analytically for zero energy (using Bessel functions for $n \geq 3$ and trigonometric functions for $n=2$) and match logarithmic derivatives at the square-well boundary to derive the renormalization-group evolution of the running coupling $H_n(\mathcal{R}) \equiv \sqrt{\lambda_S(\mathcal{R})}\,\mathcal{R}$. The WKB approximation is used to extend the zero-energy renormalization to finite energies and to estimate the residual cutoff dependence in phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- For the marginal case $n=2$ with $\lambda_L > 1/4$, adjacent bound-state energies are related by $\kappa_{m+1}/\kappa_m = \exp(-\pi/\nu)$, where $\nu = \sqrt{\lambda_L - 1/4}$.
- For $n=4$, the scattering length is given by $a_4 = r_0 \sqrt{\lambda_L} \tan \phi_4$, where $\phi_4$ is the zero-energy phase.
- Numerical phase-shift calculations for $n=4$ with $\lambda_L = 1$ show that errors $|\Delta\delta(k)|$ between different cutoffs scale as $k^2$, consistent with the WKB error estimate $\delta_\mathcal{R} - \delta_{\mathcal{R}'} \sim \eta^2 \mathcal{E}(\mathcal{R};0)$.
- The weak-coupling expansion of the $n=4$ scattering length yields $a_4/R = (1 - \tan(\sqrt{\lambda_S}\mathcal{R})/(\sqrt{\lambda_S}\mathcal{R})) - (1/3)(1 + \tan(\sqrt{\lambda_S}\mathcal{R})/(\sqrt{\lambda_S}\mathcal{R}) + (\tan(\sqrt{\lambda_S}\mathcal{R})/(\sqrt{\lambda_S}\mathcal{R}))^2)(\lambda_L/\mathcal{R}^2) + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_L^2/\mathcal{R}^4)$.

## Context

This work extends the modern renormalization-group perspective on singular potentials, building on foundational studies by Case (1950) and Landau and Lifshitz. The $n=2$ case is directly relevant to the Efimov effect in the three-body problem with short-range interactions (Efimov 1970), where the running of the three-body force was previously found to follow similar periodic behavior using momentum-cutoff regularization (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck 1999). The $n=3$ case corresponds to the tensor force between nucleons, central to ongoing efforts to develop a perturbative theory of nuclear interactions. The paper enables a systematic treatment of singular potentials in effective field theory, showing that low-energy observables can be predicted with controlled errors despite the nonperturbative short-distance behavior, and clarifies that apparent nonanalytic dependence on the coupling constant at weak coupling is an artifact removed by proper short-distance regularization.
