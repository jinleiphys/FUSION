# General predictions of neutron star properties using unified relativistic mean-field equations of state

> In this work we present general predictions for the static observables of neutron stars (NSs) under the hypothesis of a purely nucleonic composition of the ultra-dense baryonic matter, using Bayesian inference on a very large parameter space conditioned by both astrophysical and nuclear physics constraints. The equation of states are obtained using a unified approach of the NS core and inner crust within a fully covariant treatment based on a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian density with density dependent couplings. The posterior distributions are well compatible with the ones obtained by semi-agnostic meta-modelling techniques based on non-relativistic functionals, that span a similar portion of the parameter space in terms of nuclear matter parameters, and we confirm that the hypothesis of a purely nucleonic composition is compatible with all the present observations. We additionally show that present observations do not exclude the existence of very massive neutron stars with mass compatible with the lighter partner of the gravitational event GW190814 measured by the LIGO-Virgo collaboration. Some selected representative models, that respect well all the constraints taken into account in this study, and approximately cover the residual uncertainty in our posterior distributions, will be uploaded in the CompOSE database for use by the community.

## Key claim
The authors show that a purely nucleonic composition of neutron star matter is compatible with all current astrophysical and nuclear physics observations, and that present data do not exclude the existence of very massive neutron stars with masses compatible with the lighter partner of the gravitational event GW190814 (approximately 2.6 solar masses), provided the equation of state is modeled with sufficient flexibility using a relativistic mean-field approach with density-dependent couplings.

## Method
The authors construct unified equations of state for neutron star cores and inner crusts using a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian with density-dependent couplings following the GDFM parametrization (Gogelein et al., 2007). They perform Bayesian inference on a 12-dimensional parameter space, sampling nuclear matter parameters (saturation density, energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy and its curvature) with uniform priors, then re-weighting to ensure a flat prior on the symmetry energy curvature K_sym. The inner crust is built using a compressible liquid drop approximation with surface tension optimized on experimental nuclear masses from the AME2016 atomic mass evaluation (Wang et al., 2017). Posterior distributions are conditioned by three categories of constraints: theoretical chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron matter, experimental nuclear matter properties near saturation, and astrophysical observations including NICER radius measurements and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint.

## Key numbers
- Prior distribution contains approximately 4 × 10^5 models after filtering for nuclear mass fit quality, crust solution existence, and thermodynamic stability
- Prior mean values: saturation density ρ_sat = 0.154 fm⁻³, saturation energy E_sat = -15.4 MeV, incompressibility K_sat = 260.3 MeV, symmetry energy J_sym = 34.3 MeV, symmetry energy slope L_sym = 71.7 MeV
- Prior 90% confidence interval for maximum neutron star mass: 2.32 to 2.85 solar masses (mean 2.57 M_⊙)
- Prior 90% confidence interval for radius of 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: 12.6 to 14.9 km (mean 13.7 km)
- Prior 90% confidence interval for tidal deformability of 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: 744 to 2007 (mean 1294)
- Prior 90% confidence interval for central density of maximum mass star: 0.62 to 0.89 fm⁻³ (mean 0.73 fm⁻³)

## Context
This work extends the relativistic meta-modelling approach of Char et al. (2023) by including a unified treatment of both core and inner crust, and by systematically studying the effects of different constraint categories (chiral EFT, nuclear experiments, astrophysical observations) on posterior distributions. The results are compatible with those obtained by semi-agnostic meta-modelling techniques based on non-relativistic functionals such as those by Margueron et al. (2018), which span a similar portion of the nuclear matter parameter space. The selected representative models will be uploaded to the CompOSE database for community use, enabling future comparisons with observations from the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA O4 and O5 runs, as well as next-generation detectors like the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer.
