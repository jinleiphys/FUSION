# The Weak Neutral Current

> This is a review of electroweak precision physics with particular emphasis on low-energy precision measurements in the neutral current sector of the electroweak theory and includes future experimental prospects and the theoretical challenges one faces to interpret these observables. Within the minimal Standard Model they serve as determinations of the weak mixing angle which are competitive with and complementary to those obtained near the Z-resonance. In the context of new physics beyond the Standard Model these measurements are crucial to discriminate between models and to reduce the allowed parameter space within a given model. We illustrate this for the minimal supersymmetric Standard Model with or without R-parity.

## Key claim

This review argues that low-energy precision measurements of the weak neutral current provide determinations of the weak mixing angle that are competitive with and complementary to those obtained near the Z-resonance, and that in the context of new physics beyond the Standard Model these measurements are crucial to discriminate between models and to reduce the allowed parameter space within a given model, illustrated for the minimal supersymmetric Standard Model with or without R-parity.

## Method

The paper reviews electroweak precision physics by summarizing the Standard Model structure of the weak neutral current, including the gauge sector, Higgs sector, and fermion currents. It then surveys experimental results from Z-pole asymmetries at LEP and SLC, W and Z boson mass measurements from LEP-2 and the Tevatron, and low-energy measurements from neutrino scattering, polarized lepton scattering, and atomic parity violation, comparing each to Standard Model predictions and discussing implications for the Higgs boson mass and new physics scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Z boson mass: M_Z = 91.1876 ± 0.0021 GeV (from LEP-1 lineshape scan)
- W boson mass world average: M_W = 80.385 ± 0.015 GeV (from LEP-2 and Tevatron)
- Effective leptonic weak mixing angle from all Z-pole data: sin^2θ_eff^ℓ = 0.23155 ± 0.00016
- SLD left-right asymmetry into hadrons: A_LR^q = 0.1514 ± 0.0022, giving sin^2θ_eff^ℓ = 0.23097 ∓ 0.00027
- LEP forward-backward asymmetry for b quarks: A_FB^b = 0.0992 ± 0.0016, giving sin^2θ_eff^ℓ = 0.23221 ∓ 0.00029
- Fermi constant: G_F = 1.1663787(6) × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}
- Muon lifetime: τ_μ = 2.1969803(22) μs (MuLan Collaboration)
- Higgs vacuum expectation value: v = 246.22 GeV
- On-shell weak mixing angle from combined W and Z masses: s^2_W = 0.22290 ∓ 0.00028

## Context

This review extends and updates earlier reviews of low-energy neutral current measurements by Erler (2004) and of the weak mixing angle at low momentum transfer by Kumar (2013). It provides a comprehensive survey of electroweak precision observables from both the energy frontier (LEP, SLC, Tevatron, LHC) and the intensity frontier (neutrino scattering, polarized electron scattering, atomic parity violation), enabling comparisons between direct and indirect determinations of the weak mixing angle and constraining models of new physics such as the minimal supersymmetric Standard Model.
