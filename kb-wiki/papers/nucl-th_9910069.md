# The Pion Electroproduction in the $Δ$(1232) region region

> The amplitudes of the pion electroproduction from the nucleon are derived up to third order in the chiral effective theory including explicit $Δ$(1232) >. The Q^2 evolutions of weighted integrals of amplitudes are presented.

## Key claim

The paper derives the amplitudes for pion electroproduction from the nucleon up to third order in chiral effective theory that explicitly includes the Δ(1232) isobar, but the author states that direct comparison with experimental data is not possible because the Δ(1232) pole makes the power counting scheme unreliable; instead, only weighted integrals of the amplitudes through the Δ(1232) region can be predicted.

## Method

The author extended Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) to include the Δ(1232) isobar as an explicit degree of freedom, treating the mass difference Δ = mΔ − mN as a light scale comparable to the pion mass. The amplitudes for pion electroproduction were calculated up to O(ϵ³) using the standard CGLN decomposition into electric (El±), magnetic (Ml±), and longitudinal (Ll±) multipoles, with divergences absorbed by counterterms in the chiral Lagrangian. Because the s-channel Δ(1232) pole prevents direct calculation of physical observables, the author computed weighted integrals of the multipoles defined as M̄(n)l± = (1/mp) ∫ M_{l±}(E) (E/mp)^n dE over the Δ(1232) region.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) mass splitting is set to Δ = 294 MeV, the pion decay constant to Fπ = 92.4 MeV, the nucleon mass to MN = 938.7 MeV, and the upper integration limit to Emax = 340 MeV, with renormalization scale μ = 500 MeV.
- For neutral pion electroproduction at Q² = 0.01 (GeV/c)², the tree-level coefficient ζA₁ for n=1 is 24.80, ζB₁;R is 29.26, and ζE₁;R is −12.97, all in units of 10⁻⁴/mπ.
- At Q² = 0.04 (GeV/c)², ζA₁ for n=1 becomes 25.27, ζB₁;R becomes 35.73, and ζE₁;R becomes −16.07.
- At Q² = 0.06 (GeV/c)², ζA₁ for n=1 becomes 23.67, ζB₁;R becomes 39.89, and ζE₁;R becomes −18.35.
- The empirical value of the pion mean square charge radius is ⟨r²⟩π = 0.439 fm², which fixes the counterterm l6(μ = 1 GeV) = 6.6 × 10⁻³.
- The counterterm b23 is related to the axial mean square radius via (4πFπ)² b23 = 3.08 ± 0.27 from (anti)neutrino-proton scattering data.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of pion photoproduction (Kao, 1999) to the electroproduction case, where virtual photons introduce additional longitudinal multipoles and new counterterms such as b7 and b23. The approach is motivated by the need to describe both resonant and nonresonant amplitudes simultaneously, as earlier model calculations showed deviations from new data from MAMI, MIT/Bates, and Jefferson Lab. The method enables future comparison with experimental data through weighted integrals, but the author notes the approach is limited to Q² ≤ 0.1–0.2 (GeV/c)² because the expansion of nucleon propagators breaks down at higher Q².
