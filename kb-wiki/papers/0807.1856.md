# Kaon Condensation with Lattice QCD

> Kaon condensation may play an important role in the structure of hadronic matter at densities greater than that of nuclear matter, as exist in the interior of neutron stars. We present the results of the first lattice QCD investigation of kaon condensation obtained by studying systems containing up to twelve negatively charged kaons. Surprisingly, the properties of the condensate that we calculate are remarkably well reproduced by leading order chiral perturbation theory. In our analysis, we also determine the three-kaon interaction from the multi-kaon systems and update our results for pion condensates.

## Key claim
The first lattice QCD investigation of kaon condensation finds that the chemical potential of negatively charged kaons as a function of density is remarkably well reproduced by leading order chiral perturbation theory, despite expectations of corrections at the 25% level from higher-order terms.

## Method
The authors compute correlation functions for systems containing up to twelve negatively charged kaons on MILC gauge configurations with mixed-action lattice QCD (domain-wall valence quarks on asqtad-improved staggered sea quarks). They extract interaction energies from ratios of multi-kaon to single-kaon correlation functions and determine the kaon chemical potential from the density dependence of the ground-state energy using finite-difference approximations.

## Key numbers
- Kaon number density for the 12-kaon system on L ~ 2.5 fm ensembles: 0.77 fm^{-3}
- Lattice spacings used: b ~ 0.125 fm (coarse) and b ~ 0.090 fm (fine)
- Spatial extents: L ~ 2.5 fm and L ~ 3.5 fm
- Two-kaon scattering length values (m_K * a_KK^(I=1)) range from 0.402(6)(18) to 0.584(12)(18) across ensembles
- Three-kaon interaction (m_K f_K^4 * eta_3K^L) consistent with zero, ranging from -0.1(2)(4) to 0.3(1)(3) on the 2.5 fm coarse ensembles
- Pion mass values explored include m_pi ~ 291 MeV and m_pi ~ 352 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD studies of charged-pion condensates (Beane 2007, Detmold 2008) to kaon systems for the first time. The results enable future lattice calculations of multi-nucleon-multi-kaon systems needed to explore kaon condensation in a baryonic background relevant to neutron star interiors, where condensates with <K>/f_K less than 0.3 are possible at densities above nuclear matter density (Kaplan and Nelson).
