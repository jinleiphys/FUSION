# Effects of the quark anomalous magnetic moment in the chiral symmetry restoration: magnetic catalysis and inverse magnetic catalysis

> In this work, we consider the effect of a constant anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in the SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in the mean field approximation. To this end, we use the Schwinger {\it ansatz}, which represents a linear magnetic field term in the Lagrangian. A regularization method inspired in the vacuum magnetic regularization (VMR) is adopted to avoid ultraviolet divergences. Our results indicate a smooth decrease of the pseudocritical temperature and quark condensates for magnetic fields $B \leq 0.1$ GeV$^2$ when a sizable AMM is considered. We found only a small window for Inverse Magnetic Catalysis (IMC), in contradiction with NJL predictions made in the literature. For a low value of AMM, we observe for all ranges of magnetic fields considered that the pseudocritical temperature increases with the magnetic field, indicating only Magnetic Catalysis (MC). In our approach, for nonvanishing quark AMM, the chiral symmetry restoration happens always as a smooth crossover and never turns into a first order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including a sizable quark anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) via the Schwinger ansatz in the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model produces only a small window of inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) for magnetic fields \(B \leq 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), contradicting earlier NJL predictions of IMC; for low AMM values, only magnetic catalysis (MC) is observed across all magnetic field ranges considered.

## Method

The authors incorporate a constant quark AMM through a Pauli term in the SU(2) NJL model Lagrangian, using the Schwinger ansatz for a linear magnetic field term. They derive the thermodynamic potential and gap equation in the mean-field approximation, regularizing ultraviolet divergences with a vacuum magnetic regularization (VMR) scheme that separates pure magnetic contributions from the vacuum. The effective quark mass and pseudocritical temperature are computed as functions of temperature and magnetic field strength for two fixed sets of AMM parameters taken from Fayazbakhsh (2014).

## Key numbers

- For the sizable AMM set \(\kappa^{[1]}\) (\(k_u = 0.29016\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(k_d = 0.35986\) GeV\(^{-1}\)), the pseudocritical temperature decreases smoothly for \(eB \leq 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) and increases for stronger fields.
- For the low AMM set \(\kappa^{[2]}\) (\(k_u = 0.00995\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(k_d = 0.07975\) GeV\(^{-1}\)), the pseudocritical temperature increases for all magnetic fields considered.
- The lower bound on the effective quark mass for \(\kappa^{[1]}\) at \(eB = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) is approximately 125.7 MeV; for \(\kappa^{[2]}\) at the same field, the bound is approximately 19.8 MeV.
- The model parameters are \(\Lambda = 591.6\) MeV, \(m = 5.7233\) MeV, and \(G = 2.404/\Lambda^2\), yielding vacuum values \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and \(\langle \overline{u}u \rangle = (-241\ \text{MeV})^3\).

## Context

This work extends studies of the AMM in the NJL model by Fayazbakhsh (2014), Mei (2020), and Chaudhuri (2019, 2020), but uses a VMR regularization scheme that avoids unphysical oscillations in the quark mass and pseudocritical temperature seen in earlier works. The results contradict claims of IMC from those prior NJL studies and show that chiral symmetry restoration remains a smooth crossover with nonvanishing AMM, never turning first order. The findings enable more reliable calculations of thermodynamic properties of magnetized quark matter with AMM effects, to be reported in a future publication.
