# Diffractive dissociation of gluons into heavy quark-antiquark pairs in proton-proton collisions

> We discuss diffractive dissociation of gluons into heavy quark pairs. The particular mechanism is similar to the diffractive dissociation of virtual photons into quarks, which drives diffractive deep inelastic production of charm in the low-mass diffraction, or large $β$-region. There, it can be understood, with some reservations, in terms of a valence heavy quark content of the Pomeron. The amplitude for the $g p \to Q \bar Q p$ is derived in the impact parameter and momentum space. The cross section for single diffractive $p p \to Q \bar Q p X$ is calculated as a convolution of the elementary cross section and gluon distribution in the proton. Integrated cross section and the differential distributions in e.g. transverse momentum and rapidity of the charm and bottom quark and antiquark, as well as the quark-antiquark invariant mass are calculated for the nominal LHC energy for different unintegrated gluon distributions from the literature. The ratio of the bottom-to-charm cross sections are shown and discussed as a function of several kinematical variables.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that diffractive dissociation of gluons into heavy quark-antiquark pairs in proton-proton collisions can be calculated via a microscopic approach using unintegrated gluon distributions, without assuming Regge factorization, and that this mechanism is complementary to the standard Ingelman-Schlein approach where heavy quarks are generated from gluons inside the Pomeron rather than from a valence-like heavy quark contribution.

## Method

The authors derive the amplitude for the partonic subprocess $g p \to Q \bar Q p$ in impact parameter and momentum space, using a two-gluon exchange model where the QCD Pomeron is represented by a gluon ladder related to the unintegrated gluon distribution in the proton. The hadron-level cross section for single diffractive $p p \to Q \bar Q p X$ is obtained by convoluting the elementary cross section with the collinear gluon distribution in the proton, using three different unintegrated gluon distribution functions from the literature (Ivanov-Nikolaev, linear Kutak-Stasto, and nonlinear Kutak-Stasto). The calculations are performed for the nominal LHC energy of $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV.

## Key numbers

* Integrated cross section for single diffractive $c \bar c$ production at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV ranges from $0.8322 \times 10^{-2}$ mb (nonlinear Kutak-Stasto UGDF with GJR08 PDF) to $1.9719 \times 10^{-2}$ mb (linear Kutak-Stasto UGDF with CTEQ6 PDF), without gap survival factor.
* Integrated cross section for single diffractive $b \bar b$ production at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV ranges from $0.9005 \times 10^{-4}$ mb to $1.4201 \times 10^{-4}$ mb, without gap survival factor.
* The ratio $\sigma(b \bar b)/\sigma(c \bar c)$ is approximately $0.5\%$ to $1\%$, consistent with the expected $m_Q^{-4}$ scaling: $(m_c/m_b)^4 \sim 1\%$ with $m_c = 1.5$ GeV and $m_b = 4.75$ GeV.
* The diffractive slope parameters used are $B_0 = 4.0$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $\alpha'_\Pom = 0.164$.
* A gap survival factor of $S_G = 0.05$ is applied to differential distributions.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nikolaev (1991) on diffractive dissociation of virtual photons into quarks via two-gluon exchange, which was applied to diffractive deep inelastic production of open charm by Genovese (1995). The mechanism differs from earlier calculations by Alves (1996) and Yuan (1998), who used an approximation where gluon transverse momenta in the Pomeron are integrated out; the authors argue those works contain errors in the treatment of diagrams and color algebra. The results enable future experimental searches at the LHC for heavy quark production in the Pomeron fragmentation region, close to the rapidity gap, where the standard gluon-fusion mechanism gives a tiny contribution.
