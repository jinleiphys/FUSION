# On the properties of the $^{229}$Th isotope

> Electromagnetic properties of the deformed neutron-odd nucleus $^{229}$Th are investigated in the framework of the unified model, with primary emphasis upon the properties of the low-lying isomeric state.

# On the properties of the $^{229}$Th isotope

## Key claim
The low-lying isomeric state in $^{229}$Th at approximately 7.6 eV can be populated via Coulomb excitation, not only through $\alpha$ and $\beta$-decay, and its half-life in practice depends only on the transition matrix element, not on the exact energy, because the conversion coefficients scale steeply with energy. However, the excitation energy of this state has not yet been measured in a direct experiment.

## Method
The author applied the unified model (Bohr-Mottelson framework) for axially-symmetric deformed odd nuclei, using Nilsson orbitals and Bogoliubov transformation coefficients to account for superfluid correlations. Reduced transition matrix elements for $E2$ and $M1$ transitions were calculated using formulas that include collective and single-particle contributions, with effective gyromagnetic ratios and a tensor term for $l$-forbidden $M1$ transitions. The deformation parameter $\beta \approx 0.22$ was extracted from experimental rotational band data, and Coulomb excitation cross sections were computed for protons and $\alpha$-particles using the semiclassical theory of Alder et al. (1956).

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy of the low-lying isomeric state: $\sim 7.6$ eV (Beck 2007), above the neutral Th ionization threshold of $\sim 6.3$ eV
- Half-life of the isomeric state: $7(\pm 1)\ \mu$s (Seif 2017)
- Deformation parameter: $\overline\beta \approx 0.22$
- $B(E2;9/2,5/2 \to 5/2,3/2) = 6.2(8)$ W.u. (experimental normalization for interband transitions)
- $B(M1;3/2,3/2 \to 5/2,5/2) = 0.0108\ \mu_N^2$ (calculated)
- $B(E2;3/2,3/2 \to 5/2,5/2) = 8.0$ W.u. (calculated)
- Total conversion coefficient for the 0.0076 keV $\gamma$-transition: $\alpha^{M1}_{tot} \approx 1.4 \times 10^9$, $\alpha^{E2}_{tot} \approx 1.2 \times 10^{16}$
- Calculated half-lives for the isomeric transition: $T_{1/2}(M1) = 5.9 \times 10^{-6}$ s, $T_{1/2}(E2) = 2.7 \times 10^{-3}$ s (including electron conversion)
- Ground-state quadrupole moment: $Q_2(5/2,5/2) = +4.3(9)$ barn (experimental), $+2.9$ barn (calculated)
- Ground-state magnetic moment: $\mu(5/2,5/2) = +0.46(4)\ \mu_N$ (experimental), $+0.47\ \mu_N$ (calculated)
- Effective cross section for Coulomb excitation of the 0.0076 keV level by 10 MeV protons: $\sigma_{\rm eff} = 1.679 \times 10^{-3}$ barn
- Estimated counting rate for $\gamma$-quanta from the isomeric decay with 10 MeV protons on a 10 $\mu$m Th foil: $\sim 20$ per day

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of $^{229}$Th, the nucleus with the lowest known nuclear excited state, following experimental evidence from Beck (2007), Larsen (2016) who detected conversion electrons, and Seif (2017) who measured the half-life. The calculations use the unified model originally developed by Bohr (1952-1955) and Nilsson (1955), and the Alaga rules (1955) for interband transitions. The author proposes Coulomb excitation as an alternative population method to the synchrotron radiation approach of Jeet (2015) and the hollow-cathode discharge method of Inamura (2005). The results enable future experiments to populate the isomeric state via particle beams and to compare calculated transition rates with more precise measurements, though the author notes that the calculated $B(M1;9/2,5/2 \to 7/2,3/2)$ is about four times smaller than the average experimental value.
