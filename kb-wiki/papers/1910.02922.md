# Global sensitivity analysis of bulk properties of an atomic nucleus

> We perform a global sensitivity analysis of the binding energy and the charge radius of the nucleus $^{16}$O to identify the most influential low-energy constants in the next-to-next-to-leading order chiral Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon forces. For this purpose we develop a subspace-projected coupled-cluster method using eigenvector continuation [Frame D. et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 121, 032501 (2018)]. With this method we compute the binding energy and charge radius of $^{16}$O at more than one million different values of the 16 low-energy constants in one hour on a standard laptop. For relatively small subspace projections, the root-mean-square error is about 1% compared to full space coupled-cluster results. We find that 58(1)% of the variance in the energy can be apportioned to a single contact-term in the $^3S_1$-wave, whereas the radius depends sensitively on several low-energy constants and their higher-order correlations. The results identify the most important parameters for describing nuclear saturation, and help prioritize efforts for uncertainty reduction of theoretical predictions. The achieved acceleration opens up for an array of computational statistics analyses of the underlying description of the strong nuclear interaction in nuclei across the Segré chart.

## Key claim

The variance in the ground-state energy of ¹⁶O is dominated (58(1)%) by a single contact-term low-energy constant in the ³S₁-wave, while the charge radius depends on several low-energy constants and their higher-order correlations; these conclusions are drawn from a subspace-projected coupled-cluster method that achieves 1% root-mean-square error relative to full-space coupled-cluster results.

## Method

The authors developed a subspace-projected coupled-cluster (SP-CC) method using eigenvector continuation, generalized to non-Hermitian problems, to approximate full-space coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles (CCSD) calculations. They constructed SP-CC Hamiltonians from 64 or 128 exact CCSD ground-state vectors obtained at different values of the 16 low-energy constants (LECs) in a next-to-next-to-leading order chiral Hamiltonian, then performed global sensitivity analysis via quasi Monte Carlo sampling with 1,114,112 evaluations of the SP-CC(64) Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- 58(1)% of the variance in the ¹⁶O ground-state energy is attributed to the leading-order LEC \(\tilde{C}_{^3S_1}\) (the contact-term in the ³S₁-wave)
- SP-CC(64) root-mean-square error: 4 MeV for ground-state energy and 0.04 fm for charge radius, relative to exact CCSD calculations
- SP-CC(128) root-mean-square error: 3 MeV for ground-state energy and 0.02 fm for charge radius
- 1,114,112 quasi Monte Carlo samples of the SP-CC(64) Hamiltonian computed in about 1 hour on a standard laptop; equivalent exact CCSD computations would require 20 years
- CCSD result for ¹⁶O with NNLO\(_{\rm sat}\) in the chosen model-space: -118.76 MeV, within 1 MeV of converged CCSD value using a Hartree-Fock basis
- Second-order correlations between LECs account for almost 14% of the variance in the charge radius

## Context

This work extends eigenvector continuation (Frame et al., 2018) to non-Hermitian coupled-cluster problems, enabling global sensitivity analysis that was previously computationally prohibitive for medium-mass nuclei. The results identify why certain chiral Hamiltonians like NNLO\(_{\rm sat}\) (Ekstrom et al., 2015) reproduce binding energies and charge radii up to mass A~50 while the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction (Nogga et al., 2004; Hebeler et al., 2011) reproduces binding energies up to A~100 but underestimates radii. The SP-CC method enables future Bayesian calibration, uncertainty quantification, and correlation studies across the Segre chart.
