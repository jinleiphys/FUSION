# Production of $α$-particle condensate states in heavy-ion collisions

> The fragmentation of quasi-projectiles from the nuclear reaction $^{40}Ca$ + $^{12}C$ at 25 MeV/nucleon was used to produce excited states candidates to $α$-particle condensation. The experiment was performed at LNS-Catania using the CHIMERA multidetector. Accepting the emission simultaneity and equality among the $α$-particle kinetic energies as experimental criteria for deciding in favor of the condensate nature of an excited state, we analyze the $0_2^+$ and $2_2^+$ states of $^{12}$C and the $0_6^+$ state of $^{16}$O. A sub-class of events corresponding to the direct 3-$α$ decay of the Hoyle state is isolated.

## Key claim
The authors report the first experimental evidence that a sub-class of Hoyle state decays in $^{12}$C is compatible with alpha-particle condensation, but only 2% of the populated Hoyle states satisfy the criteria of simultaneous emission and equal kinetic energies among the three alpha particles.

## Method
Quasi-projectiles from the reaction $^{40}$Ca + $^{12}$C at 25 MeV/nucleon were detected with the CHIMERA 4π multidetector at LNS-Catania. The authors constructed intra-event correlation functions in the two-dimensional space of average alpha kinetic energy and its RMS, together with Dalitz plots, to distinguish direct 3-alpha decay (equal energy sharing) from sequential decay through $^8$Be for states in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O.

## Key numbers
- 39 events out of 1072 total events with $m_{\alpha}=3$ and $7.375 \leq E_{ex} \leq 7.975$ MeV were localized at ($\left<E_{\alpha} \right>=110$ keV, $\sigma_{E_{\alpha}} \leq 25$ keV), corresponding to 2% of populated Hoyle states compatible with alpha-particle condensation.
- The sequential decay peak was centered at ($\left<E_{\alpha} \right>=130$ keV, $\sigma_{E_{\alpha}} \approx 90$ keV), corresponding to energy sharing between the two alphas of $^8$Be and the remaining alpha of 191 keV.
- For the $2_2^+$ state region, 1.5 $\cdot 10^4$ events with $m_{\alpha}=3$ and $8.9 \leq E_{ex} \leq 10.3$ MeV showed no evidence of condensation-compatible states.
- The maximum energy calibration deviation was 20 keV across the apparatus.

## Context
This work extends theoretical predictions by Tohsaki (2001), Suzuki (2002), and Yamada (2004) that alpha-particle condensed states should exist in self-conjugate 4N nuclei near the N-alpha decay threshold, specifically the Hoyle state and $2_2^+$ state of $^{12}$C (Funaki 2005, Yamada 2005) and the $0_6^+$ state of $^{16}$O (Funaki 2008). The competition between direct and sequential decay of the Hoyle state was previously reported by Freer. The intra-event correlation method developed here can be applied to 4-alpha events in future dedicated experiments planned to overcome the poor statistics that prevented definite conclusions for $^{16}$O.
