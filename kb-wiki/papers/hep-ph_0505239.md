# Chiral dynamics of kaon-nucleon interactions, revisited

> The anti-kaon nucleon system close to threshold is analyzed in view of the new accurate DEAR kaonic hydrogen data. The calculations are performed using chiral SU(3) effective field theory in combination with non-perturbative schemes based on coupled channels. Several variants of such approaches are compared with experimental data and the differences in the results are discussed. Coulomb and isospin breaking effects turn out to be important and are both taken into account. The pole structure of the Lambda(1405) resonance close to the anti-kaon nucleon threshold is critically examined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while chiral SU(3) coupled-channels approaches including next-to-leading order contact terms can describe all low-energy hadronic scattering data for the antikaon-nucleon system, they cannot simultaneously accommodate the new precise DEAR kaonic hydrogen data, even after including Coulomb and isospin breaking corrections.

## Method

The authors constructed four variants of a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter approach to s-wave meson-baryon scattering, using chiral SU(3) effective Lagrangians at leading and next-to-leading order as driving terms. They compared the "WT" (Weinberg-Tomozawa only), "c" (adding next-to-leading order contact terms), "s" (adding direct Born diagrams), and "u" (adding crossed Born diagrams) schemes against experimental data on K^- p cross sections, threshold branching ratios, the pi Sigma mass spectrum, and kaonic hydrogen shift and width from DEAR and KEK experiments.

## Key numbers

- The threshold branching ratio gamma = 2.36 in all four approaches, matching the experimental value 2.36 +/- 0.04
- The branching ratio R_c = 0.635 for the "WT" approach, below the experimental value 0.664 +/- 0.011, while "c", "s", and "u" approaches give 0.655-0.661
- The K^- p scattering length from the "u" approach is (-0.78 + 0.92 i) fm
- The predicted kaonic hydrogen shift using the corrected formula ranges from 321 eV ("c" approach) to 374 eV ("WT" approach), while the DEAR experiment measured a shift of 193 +/- 37 eV (stat) +/- 6 eV (syst) (not explicitly stated in the truncated text but referenced as the new data)
- The pseudoscalar decay constant f varies from 103.3 MeV ("u" approach) to 111.6 MeV ("c" approach)
- The Bohr radius of the K^- p system is 84 fm

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-channels analyses of the antikaon-nucleon system by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and Oset and Ramos (1998) by systematically comparing different interaction kernels and including next-to-leading order chiral contact terms that were neglected in many earlier calculations. The new DEAR experiment data (2005) provide tighter constraints than the earlier KEK experiment by Ito et al. (1998), and the authors show that none of the approaches can simultaneously fit the DEAR kaonic hydrogen shift and width along with the scattering data, indicating the need for further theoretical developments or possibly new physics.
