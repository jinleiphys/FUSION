# Quantum simulation of baryon scattering in SU(2) lattice gauge theory

> We present a first real-time study of hadronic scattering in a $(1+1)$-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory with fundamental fermions using tensor-network techniques. Working in the gaugeless Hamiltonian formulation, we investigate scattering processes across sectors of fixed global baryon number $B = 0, 1, 2$, corresponding respectively to meson--meson, meson--baryon, and baryon--baryon collisions. At strong coupling, the $B = 0$ and $B = 2$ channels exhibit predominantly elastic dynamics closely resembling the U(1) Schwinger model. The mixed $B = 1$ sector displays qualitatively new behavior: meson and baryon wavepackets become entangled during the collision, with the slower state becoming spatially delocalized while the faster one propagates ballistically. We characterize these processes through local observables, entanglement entropy, and the information lattice.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first real-time simulation of hadronic scattering in a (1+1)-dimensional SU(2) lattice gauge theory using tensor-network methods, but only in the gaugeless Hamiltonian formulation at strong coupling (ga=5, ma=0.2), where the B=0 and B=2 channels are predominantly elastic while the B=1 meson-baryon sector exhibits genuinely new entangled collective behavior.

## Method

The authors map the SU(2) lattice gauge theory with staggered fermions onto a spin-1/2 chain via Jordan-Wigner transformation in the gaugeless formulation, then prepare Gaussian-modulated wavepackets for mesons (B=0) and baryons (B=1,2) acting on the DMRG-computed vacuum. They evolve the system under the spin Hamiltonian using the time-dependent variational principle (TDVP) algorithm with maximum bond dimension chi_max=80 and time step a*Delta t=0.1 on N=60 qubits, studying scattering across sectors of fixed global baryon number B=0,1,2.

## Key numbers

- Strong coupling parameters: ga=5, ma=0.2 (dimensionless ratios mu=2m/(ag^2)=0.016, x=1/(ag)^2=0.04)
- Lattice size: N=60 qubits
- DMRG parameters: N_sweep=2000 sweeps, maximum bond dimension chi_max=80 (convergence checks up to chi_max=200), cutoff epsilon=10^{-12}
- TDVP time evolution: chi_max=80, time step a*Delta t=0.1
- Information lattice analysis shows characteristic correlation length scales of ell approximately 3-4 for B=0 wavepackets

## Context

This work extends tensor-network simulations of real-time scattering from Abelian U(1) gauge theories (the Schwinger model, studied by Papaefstathiou (2024) and others) to the non-Abelian SU(2) case, which features a conserved baryon number enabling diquark baryon states. The results demonstrate that QIS-inspired diagnostics like the information lattice (introduced by Kvorning (2021)) can probe hadronic correlation structures, and open the door to simulating more QCD-like phenomena such as confinement and flavor dynamics in higher dimensions.
