# Isospin-dependent pion in-medium effects on charged pion ratio in heavy ion collisions

> Using results from the chiral perturbation theory for the s-wave interaction and the delta-resonance model for the p-wave interaction of pions with nucleons, we have evaluated the spectral functions of pions in asymmetric nuclear matter with unequal proton and neutron densities. We find that in hot dense neutron-rich matter the strength of the spectral function of positively charged pion at low energies is somewhat larger than that of negatively charged pion. In a thermal model, this isospin-dependent effect slightly reduces the ratio of negatively charged to positively charged pions that are produced in heavy ion collisions induced by radioactive beams. Relevance of our results to the determination of the nuclear symmetry energy from measured ratio of negatively to positively charged pions produced in heavy ion collisions is discussed.

# Isospin-dependent pion in-medium effects on charged pion ratio in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The isospin-dependent modification of pion spectral functions in asymmetric nuclear matter, where the s-wave interaction increases the π⁻ mass and decreases the π⁺ mass in neutron-rich matter, while the p-wave interaction softens the π⁻ dispersion more than that of π⁺, slightly reduces the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in a thermal model of heavy ion collisions, but the effect is modest enough that it does not qualitatively alter the extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy from measured pion ratios.

## Method

The authors computed pion spectral functions in hot dense asymmetric nuclear matter by combining the s-wave pion self-energy from two-loop chiral perturbation theory (Kaiser 2001) with the p-wave self-energy from a Δ-hole model including the Migdal short-range repulsion parameter g′. These spectral functions were then used in a thermal model that assumes thermal but not chemical equilibrium among nucleons, pions, and Δ resonances, with fugacity parameters adjusted to maintain the measured pion-to-nucleon ratio. The π⁻/π⁺ ratio was evaluated for three different stiffnesses of the nuclear symmetry energy (parameterized by x = 0, 0.5, 1 in the momentum-independent interaction) and for Migdal parameters g′ = 1/3, 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear density ρ = 0.165 fm⁻³ and isospin asymmetry δ = 0.2, the s-wave interaction changes pion masses by Δm(π⁻) = +13.8 MeV, Δm(π⁺) = −1.2 MeV, and Δm(π⁰) = +6.1 MeV relative to free space.
- For Au+Au collisions at beam energy 0.4 AGeV, the thermal model uses density ρ_B = 2ρ₀ (with ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), temperature T ≈ 43.6 MeV, and baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 942 MeV with charge chemical potential μ_Q ≈ −18.5 MeV.
- With g′ = 1/3, the π⁻/π⁺ ratios are 2.12, 2.30, and 2.47 for symmetry energy parameters x = 0, 0.5, and 1 respectively, compared to values of 2.20, 2.40, and 2.60 without pion in-medium effects (g′ = ∞).
- The measured π⁻/π⁺ ratio from the FOPI Collaboration is approximately 3.
- Fugacity parameters required to maintain the pion-to-nucleon ratio with medium effects for x = 1 are z_π ≈ 0.0855 and z_Δ ≈ 0.459.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion in-medium effects in symmetric nuclear matter (Weise 1975, Friedman 1981, Oset 1982) and asymmetric matter (Korpa 1999) by combining both s-wave and p-wave interactions self-consistently. It directly addresses the transport model calculations of Bali (2002), Ferini (2006), and Xiao (2009) that extracted the nuclear symmetry energy from the FOPI π⁻/π⁺ data but neglected pion medium effects. The results show that including isospin-dependent pion in-medium effects slightly reduces the extracted π⁻/π⁺ ratio, requiring an even softer symmetry energy than the x = 1 case in the IBUU model, but the authors note that different transport models (e.g., the relativistic mean-field model in Ferini 2005 and the ImIQMD model in Zhao 2010) give opposite correlations between symmetry energy stiffness and pion ratio, indicating that further theoretical work is needed to resolve the relation.
