# Open bottom mesons in hot asymmetric hadronic medium

> The in-medium masses and optical potentials of $B$ and ${\bar B}$ mesons are studied in an isospin asymmetric, strange, hot and dense hadronic environment using a chiral effective model. The chiral $SU(3)$ model originally designed for the light quark sector, is generalized to include the heavy quark sector ($c$ and $b$) to derive the interactions of the $B$ and ${\bar B}$ mesons with the light hadrons. Due to large mass of bottom quark, we use only the empirical form of these interactions for the desired purpose, while treating the bottom degrees of freedom to be frozen in the medium. Hence, all medium effects are due to the in-medium interaction of the light quark content of these open-bottom mesons. Both $B$ and $\bar B$ mesons are found to experience net attractive interactions in the medium, leading to lowering of their masses in the medium. The mass degeneracy of particles and antiparticles, ($B^+$, $B^-$) as well as ($B^0$, $\bar {B^0}$), is observed to be broken in the medium, due to equal and opposite contributions from a vectorial Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction term. Addition of hyperons to the medium lowers further the in-medium mass for each of these four mesons, while a non-zero isospin asymmetry is observed to break the approximate mass degeneracy of each pair of isospin doublets. These medium effects are found to be strongly density dependent, and bear a considerably weaker temperature dependence. The results obtained in the present investigation are compared to predictions from the quark-meson coupling model, heavy meson effective theory, and the QCD Sum Rule approach.

## Key claim

Both B and anti-B mesons experience net attractive interactions in a hot, dense, isospin-asymmetric hadronic medium, leading to lowered in-medium masses, with the mass degeneracy between particle-antiparticle pairs (B+, B-) and (B0, anti-B0) broken by equal and opposite contributions from the vectorial Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction term, while the approximate mass degeneracy of isospin doublets is broken by non-zero isospin asymmetry.

## Method

The authors generalized the chiral SU(3) effective model, originally designed for light hadrons, to include the heavy bottom quark sector by treating the bottom degrees of freedom as frozen in the medium and using only the empirical form of the interactions derived from SU(5) symmetry. They derived the in-medium dispersion relations for B and anti-B mesons from the interaction Lagrangian density, which includes the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, scalar meson exchange term, and range terms, and solved these within the mean field approximation for scalar and vector meson fields at finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- The in-medium mass shift for B mesons is strongly density dependent and shows considerably weaker temperature dependence, with the magnitude of the shift increasing with baryon density up to and beyond nuclear saturation density (rho_0)
- Addition of hyperons to the medium lowers further the in-medium mass for each of the four meson species (B+, B-, B0, anti-B0)
- The Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction term gives equal and opposite contributions to particle and antiparticle masses, breaking the mass degeneracy of (B+, B-) and (B0, anti-B0) pairs
- Non-zero isospin asymmetry breaks the approximate mass degeneracy of each pair of isospin doublets

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective model approach previously applied to kaons and antikaons (Mishra et al., 2004-2008) and to D mesons (Mishra et al., 2004; Arvind et al., various) by generalizing to the bottom sector. The results are compared to predictions from the quark-meson coupling model (Tsushima et al.), heavy meson effective theory (Yasui and Sudoh, 2014), and the QCD Sum Rule approach (Hilger et al.). This enables further studies of B meson diffusion, drag, and propagation in hot and dense hadronic matter, relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC.
