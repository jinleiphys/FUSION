# Electron and Photon Interactions in the Regime of Strong LPM Suppression

> Most searches for ultra-high energy (UHE) astrophysical neutrinos look for radio emission from the electromagnetic and hadronic showers produced in their interactions. The radio frequency spectrum and angular distribution depend on the shower development, so are sensitive to the interaction cross sections. At energies above about 10^{16} eV (in ice), the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect significantly reduces the cross sections for the two dominant electromagnetic interactions: bremsstrahlung and pair production. At higher energies, above about 10^{20} eV, the photonuclear cross section becomes larger than that for pair production, and direct pair production and electronuclear interactions become dominant over bremsstrahlung. The electron interaction length reaches a maximum around 10^{21} eV, and then decreases slowly as the electron energy increases further. In this regime, the growth in the photon cross section and electron energy loss moderates the rise in nu_e shower length, which rises from ~10 m at 10^{16} eV to ~50 m at 10^{19} eV and ~100 m at 10^{20} eV, but only to ~1 km at 10^{24} eV. In contrast, without photonuclear and electronuclear interactions, the shower length would be over 10 km at 10^{24} eV.

# Electron and Photon Interactions in the Regime of Strong LPM Suppression

## Key claim

The Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect significantly suppresses bremsstrahlung and pair production cross sections above about 10^16 eV in ice, but at energies above about 10^20 eV, photonuclear and electronuclear interactions become dominant, moderating the otherwise dramatic elongation of electromagnetic showers from ultra-high energy neutrino interactions; without these additional interactions, the shower length at 10^24 eV would exceed 10 km, but with them it reaches only about 1 km.

## Method

The authors computed electron and photon interaction cross sections in ice using Migdal's quantum mechanical formulation for LPM and dielectric suppression, then added semi-classical corrections for suppression when the bremsstrahlung formation length exceeds the photon interaction length. They incorporated photonuclear cross sections using the Engel parameterization (generalized vector dominance plus direct photon-quark interactions), direct pair production cross sections from Baier and Katkov including LPM suppression, and electronuclear interactions via the Weizsäcker-Williams method, then calculated total electron interaction lengths and electromagnetic shower lengths using an integral over the inverse energy loss.

## Key numbers

- LPM onset energy in ice: E_LPM = 303 TeV (material-dependent constant)
- Photon interaction length in ice reaches a maximum at a few × 10^21 eV where falling pair-production meets rising photonuclear cross section
- Electron interaction length reaches a maximum around 10^21 eV, then decreases slowly with further energy increase
- ν_e shower length rises from ~10 m at 10^16 eV to ~50 m at 10^19 eV and ~100 m at 10^20 eV, but only to ~1 km at 10^24 eV
- Without photonuclear and electronuclear interactions, shower length would be over 10 km at 10^24 eV
- Critical energy in ice: E_c = 79 MeV (where Compton scattering dominates)
- Hadronic interaction length in ice: 90.7 cm
- Photonuclear cross section scales as k^0.08 at high energies and with atomic number A as A^0.887
- For ice, dielectric suppression becomes important when y < 1.3 × 10^-6 × (E_LPM/E)^(1/3)
- At 10^20 eV, direct pair production energy loss is a factor of about 10 lower than unscreened calculations due to LPM suppression

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Klein (2004) and Ralston (2002) on LPM suppression in ultra-high energy neutrino interactions, providing more complete cross sections that include photonuclear, electronuclear, and direct pair production processes which become dominant above 10^20 eV. The results are directly relevant to experiments searching for ultra-high energy astrophysical neutrinos via radio Cherenkov radiation, including ANITA (sensitive above 10^19 eV), RICE, ARIANNA, and ARA (thresholds around 10^17 eV), as well as acoustic and lunar-based experiments with thresholds above 10^20 eV. The calculations enable improved modeling of electromagnetic shower development and radio emission from ν_e interactions, which most previous high-threshold experiments had ignored, calculating sensitivity solely from hadronic showers.
