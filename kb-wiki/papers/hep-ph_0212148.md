# Cronin effect in proton-nucleus collisions: a survey of theoretical models

> I compare the available theoretical models that describe the Cronin effect on hadron and minijet production in proton-nucleus collisions, pointing out similarities and differences among them. The effect may be summarized by the value of two variables. Their values computed in the different models are compared in the energy range 27.4 GeV - 5500 GeV. Finally, I propose to use the pseudorapidity systematics as a further handle to distinguish among the models.

# Cronin effect in proton-nucleus collisions: a survey of theoretical models

## Key claim
The Cronin effect in proton-nucleus collisions can be characterized by the transverse momentum at which the Cronin ratio crosses unity (p_x) and the momentum at which it reaches its maximum (p_M, R_M), but the extrapolation from lower energies to LHC energy (5500 GeV) is haunted by large theoretical uncertainties that may make unreliable any interpretation of signals such as jet quenching in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The pseudorapidity systematics is proposed as a further handle to distinguish among the models, but only one reference (Accardi and Treleani, 2001) has considered this in the literature.

## Method
The paper compares five theoretical models for the Cronin effect in proton-nucleus collisions: soft hadronic rescattering (Wang, 2000), soft-saturated hadronic rescattering (Zhang, Fai, Papp, Barnafoldi, Levai, 2002), color dipole model (Kopeliovich, Nemchik, Schmidt, Tarasov, 2002), hard partonic rescattering (Accardi and Treleani, 2001), and hard partonic rescattering (Gyulassy and Vitev, 2002). The models are classified according to whether the projectile hadron or its partons undergo rescatterings and according to the hardness of the rescattering processes. The Cronin parameters p_M and R_M are computed for charged pions and partons at center-of-mass energies of 27.4 GeV, 200 GeV, and 5500 GeV, with targets varying from pW/pBe to pAu/pp to pPb/pp.

## Key numbers
- At sqrt(s)=27.4 GeV (pW/pBe): Soft model gives p_M=4.0 GeV, R_M=1.55 for charged pions; Soft-saturated model gives p_M=4.5 GeV, R_M=1.46 with HIJING shadowing; Color dipole gives p_M=4.5 GeV, R_M=1.43 +/- 0.08; Hard GV gives p_M=4 GeV, R_M=1.4.
- At sqrt(s)=200 GeV (pAu/pp): Soft model gives p_M=3.5 GeV, R_M=1.35 +/- 0.2; Soft-saturated gives p_M=2.9 GeV, R_M=1.15 with HIJING shadowing; Color dipole gives p_M=2.7 GeV, R_M=1.1; Hard GV gives p_M=3.0 GeV, R_M=1.3.
- At sqrt(s)=5500 GeV (pPb/pp): Soft model gives p_M=3.5 GeV, R_M=1.08 +/- 0.02; Soft-saturated gives p_M=2.4 GeV, R_M=0.78 with HIJING shadowing; Color dipole gives p_M=2.5 GeV, R_M=1.06; Hard AT for partons gives p_M=11 +/- 1.3 GeV, R_M=2.1 (with mu=1.5 GeV) or 1.2 (with mu energy-dependent); Hard GV gives p_M approximately 40 GeV, R_M=1.05 (dominated by EKS98 antishadowing, considered not reliable).
- The regulator mass used in pQCD cross-sections is m=0.8 GeV for soft models; the hard GV model uses mu=0.42 GeV as the medium screening mass.
- The color dipole model uses dipole cross-section parameters sigma_0=23 mb, lambda=0.288, x_0=3x10^{-4}.

## Context
This survey extends the analysis of the Cronin effect originally discovered by Cronin (1975) and later explained in terms of multiple interactions by hard model and Levai and Papp (1983). The paper compares models that have been applied to pA collisions, including the soft hadronic rescattering model of Wang (2000), the soft-saturated model of Zhang, Fai, Papp, Barnafoldi, and Levai (2002), the color dipole model of Kopeliovich, Nemchik, Schmidt, and Tarasov (2002), and the hard partonic rescattering models of Accardi and Treleani (2001) and Gyulassy and Vitev (2002). The work enables future pA runs at LHC to test these models and provide reliable baselines for disentangling the Cronin effect from jet quenching in AA collisions, where an unexpectedly small R<1 was observed at RHIC at sqrt(s)=130 GeV. The paper also notes that the pseudorapidity systematics, considered only by Accardi and Treleani (2001), could be a powerful handle given the large pseudorapidity coverage of CMS.
