# Chiral pasta

> Interiors of neutron stars are ultra-dense and may contain a core of deconfined quark matter. Such a core connects to the outer layers smoothly or through a sharp microscopic interface or through an intermediate macroscopic layer of inhomogeneous mixed phases, which is globally neutral but locally contains electrically charged domains. Here I employ a nucleon-meson model under neutron star conditions that shows a first-order chiral phase transition at large densities. In the vicinity of this chiral transition I calculate the free energies of various mixed phases - different 'pasta structures' - in the Wigner-Seitz approximation. Crucially, chirally broken nuclear matter and the approximately chirally symmetric phase (loosely interpreted as quark matter) are treated on the same footing. This allows me to compute the interface profiles of bubbles, rods, and slabs fully consistently, taking into account electromagnetic screening effects and without needing the surface tension as an input. I find that the full numerical results tend to disfavor mixed phases compared to a simple step-like approximation used frequently in the literature and that the predominantly favored pasta structure consists of slabs with a surface tension $Σ\simeq 6\, {\rm MeV}/{\rm fm}^2$.

## Key claim

The paper finds that when the interface profiles of mixed phases at the chiral transition are computed fully consistently (rather than approximated as step-like), the mixed phases are generally disfavored, and the predominantly favored pasta structure consists of slabs with a surface tension of approximately 6 MeV/fm².

## Method

The author employs a nucleon-meson model under neutron star conditions (zero temperature, beta-equilibrated, electrically neutral matter) that exhibits a first-order chiral phase transition at high densities. Using the Thomas-Fermi and Wigner-Seitz approximations, the Euler-Lagrange equations for the mesonic condensates (sigma, omega, rho) are solved simultaneously with the Poisson equation for the electrostatic potential, computing the interface profiles of bubbles, rods, and slabs without requiring surface tension as an input parameter.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of surface tension at the chiral phase transition by Fraga (2018) from isospin-symmetric matter to isospin-asymmetric neutron star conditions, using the same nucleon-meson model developed by Drews (2014). It improves upon the step-like approximation used extensively in the literature (Glendenning, 1992) by computing fully consistent interface profiles, and is the first such calculation for the quark-hadron transition context. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors and their observable properties, including gravitational wave emission from rotating neutron stars.
