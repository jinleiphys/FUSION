# Inverse magnetic catalysis effect and current quark mass effect on mass spectra and Mott transitions of pions under external magnetic field

> Mass spectra and Mott transition of pions $(π^0,\ π^\pm)$ at finite temperature and magnetic field are investigated in a two-flavor NJL model, and we focus on the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect and current quark mass (CQM) effect. Due to the dimension reduction of the constituent quarks, the pion masses jump at their Mott transitions, which is independent of the IMC effect and CQM effect. We consider the IMC effect by using a magnetic dependent coupling constant, which is a monotonic decreasing function of magnetic field. With IMC effect, the Mott transition temperature of $π^0$ meson $T_m^0$ is a monotonic decreasing function of magnetic field. For charged pions $π^{\pm}$, the Mott transition temperature $T_m^+$ fast increases in weak magnetic field region and then decreases with magnetic field, which are accompanied with some oscillations. Comparing with the case without IMC effect, $T_m^0$ and $T_m^+$ are lower when including IMC effect. CQM effect are considered by varying parameter $m_0$ in non-chiral limit. For $π^0$ meson, $T_m^0$ is not a monotonic function of magnetic field with low $m_0$, but it is a monotonic decreasing function with larger $m_0$. In the weak magnetic field region, $T_m^0$ is higher for larger $m_0$, but in the strong magnetic field region, it is lower for larger $m_0$. For $π^+$ meson, $T^+_m$ is only quantitatively modifies by current quark mass effect, and it becomes higher with larger $m_0$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect, modeled via a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant, lowers the Mott transition temperatures of both neutral and charged pions compared to the case without IMC, and that the current quark mass qualitatively changes the magnetic-field dependence of the neutral pion Mott temperature but only quantitatively modifies that of charged pions.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with Pauli-Villars regularization, solving the gap equation for the dynamical quark mass and the pole equations for the neutral and charged pion propagators at finite temperature and external magnetic field. The IMC effect is introduced by fitting a magnetic-field-dependent coupling constant \(G(eB)\) to lattice QCD results for the decreasing pseudo-critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration. The current quark mass effect is studied by varying the bare quark mass parameter \(m_0\) from 2 to 50 MeV while keeping other parameters fixed.

## Key numbers

*   The magnetic-field-dependent coupling \(G(eB)/G(eB=0)\) is a monotonic decreasing function of magnetic field, reducing by 16% at \(eB/m_\pi^2 = 30\) (with \(m_\pi = 134\) MeV).
*   At fixed magnetic field \(eB = 20 m_\pi^2\) and with IMC effect, the charged pion \(\pi^+\) Mott transition temperature is \(T_m^+ = 124\) MeV, with additional mass jumps at \(T_1^+ = 136\) MeV and \(T_2^+ = 157\) MeV; without IMC effect, these values are \(T_m^+ = 160\) MeV, \(T_1^+ = 172\) MeV, and \(T_2^+ = 187\) MeV.
*   For the neutral pion \(\pi^0\) at vanishing temperature, the mass \(m_{\pi^0}\) is a decreasing function of magnetic field, and including IMC effect gives a lower value than without IMC effect.
*   For the charged pion \(\pi^+\) at vanishing temperature, the mass \(m_{\pi^+}\) is an increasing function of magnetic field, and including IMC effect gives a lower value than without IMC effect.
*   The normalized neutral pion mass \(m_{\pi^0}(eB)/m_{\pi^0}(eB=0)\) increases with current quark mass \(m_0\) at fixed magnetic field, while the normalized charged pion mass \(m_{\pi^+}(eB)/m_{\pi^+}(eB=0)\) decreases with \(m_0\) at fixed magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion properties under magnetic fields in NJL models (e.g., Mao (2020), Mao (2021)) by incorporating the inverse magnetic catalysis effect via a magnetic-field-dependent coupling fitted to lattice QCD results (Lattice Collaboration (2016)). It also systematically investigates the role of the current quark mass, which had not been studied in effective models for pions at finite temperature and magnetic field, and compares its findings to lattice QCD results (Lattice Collaboration (2018)). The results enable predictions for pion production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions where strong magnetic fields are generated, suggesting possible sudden enhancement or suppression of pions during fireball cooling due to mass jumps at Mott transitions.
