# Leading-order Corrections of a Dynamical Model for Electromagnetic Pion Production Reactions

> By applying a unitary transformation method, we have derived the leading-order corrections on the effective Hamiltonian of a dynamical model developed in Phys. Rev. C54, 2660 (1996) for electromagnetic pion production reactions. The resulting energy-independent one-loop corrections on the baryon masses and the gamma N -> Delta vertex interaction are associated with the structure of the nucleon and Delta and have been calculated within a constituent quark model. We find that the one-loop corrections on the magnetic M1 transition of the gamma N -> Delta are very small, while their contributions to the electric E2 and Coulomb C2 transitions are found to be in opposite signs of that due to pion cloud effects associated with the scattering states. Our results further indicate that the determination of the nonspherical L=2 components of the constituent quark wavefunctions of N and Delta from the extracted empirical E2 and C2 form factors requires a rigorous and complete calculation of meson cloud effects. We also find that the one-loop corrections on the non-resonant pion production operator can resolve the difficulty in describing the near threshold gamma p -> pi^0 p reaction. Possible future developments are discussed.

## Key claim

The leading-order one-loop corrections derived via unitary transformation produce energy-independent contributions to the baryon masses and the gamma N -> Delta vertex that are associated with nucleon and Delta structure; these corrections to the magnetic M1 transition are very small, while their contributions to the electric E2 and Coulomb C2 transitions have opposite signs compared to pion cloud effects from scattering states, and the one-loop corrections on the non-resonant pion production operator can resolve the difficulty in describing the near-threshold gamma p -> pi^0 p reaction.

## Method

The authors applied a unitary transformation method to a field-theoretical Hamiltonian describing pseudo-vector coupling between pi, N, and Delta fields, deriving an effective Hamiltonian within the N, Delta, pi N, and gamma N Hilbert space. They evaluated the one-loop corrections to baryon masses and the gamma N -> Delta vertex using a constituent quark model with nonrelativistic quarks in L=0 s-wave configurations, employing a dipole form factor with cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV/c for all pi B B' vertices and setting all electromagnetic form factors equal to F_V(q^2) = 1/(1 - q^2/M_V^2)^2 with M_V = 0.76 GeV. The quark mass was fixed at m_q = 360 MeV by fitting nucleon magnetic moments including the one-loop corrections.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass correction: Sigma^0_N = -139 MeV (composed of -73.5 MeV from pi N intermediate state and -65.4 MeV from pi Delta intermediate state)
- Delta mass correction (excluding pi N rescattering): Sigma^0_Delta = -76.6 MeV from pi Delta intermediate state
- Rescattering contribution to Delta mass shift: Sigma^{res}_Delta = -46.2 MeV from principal-value integration
- Bare nucleon mass: m_N^0 = 1077 MeV
- Bare Delta mass: m_Delta^0 = 1355 MeV
- Bare mass difference: delta^0 = m_Delta^0 - m_N^0 = 278 MeV
- Quark mass determined from fit: m_q = 360 MeV
- Proton magnetic moment: tree value 2.61 mu_N, total with loop corrections 2.75 mu_N
- Neutron magnetic moment: tree value -1.74 mu_N, total with loop corrections -1.95 mu_N

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee (SL) dynamical model from Phys. Rev. C54, 2660 (1996) by systematically including leading-order one-loop corrections that were previously neglected; the SL model had not treated mass correction terms explicitly and had not calculated the one-loop corrections to the gamma N -> Delta vertex or the non-resonant gamma N -> pi N amplitude. The results indicate that extracting the nonspherical L=2 components of constituent quark wavefunctions from empirical E2 and C2 form factors requires a complete calculation of meson cloud effects, and the improved non-resonant operator addresses the near-threshold gamma p -> pi^0 p reaction that earlier models could not describe.
