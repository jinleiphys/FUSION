# On the Role of Differentiation Parameter in a Bound State Solution of the Klein-Gordon Equation

> Recently, the bound state solutions of a confined Klein-Gordon particle under the mixed scalar-vector generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential in one spatial dimension have been investigated. The obtained results reveal that in the spin symmetric limit discrete spectrum exists, while in the pseudo-spin symmetric limit it does not. In this manuscript, new insights and information are given by employing an analogy of the variational principle. The role of the difference of the magnitudes of the vector and scalar potential energies, namely the differentiation parameter, on the energy spectrum is examined. It is observed that the differentiation parameter determines the measure of the energy spectrum density by modifying the confined particle's mass-energy in addition to narrowing the spectrum interval length.

## Key claim

The differentiation parameter (the difference between vector and scalar potential magnitudes, denoted ε⁺) modifies the confined particle's effective mass-energy and narrows the spectrum interval length, thereby controlling the density of eigenvalues in the bound-state energy spectrum of the Klein-Gordon equation under the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential in the spin symmetry limit.

## Method

The author starts from the one-dimensional Klein-Gordon equation in the spin symmetry limit and substitutes the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential (GSWSP). By applying a transformation to a hypergeometric differential equation, the general bound-state wave function is expressed in terms of hypergeometric functions. Continuity conditions at x=0 are imposed, leading to transcendental quantization equations for even and odd parity subsets, which are solved numerically using the Newton-Raphson method for a neutral Kaon confined in a GSWSP well with repulsive and attractive surface interactions.

## Key numbers

- Rest mass energy of the neutral Kaon: m₀c² = 497.648 MeV.
- Potential well depth parameter: V₀ = m₀c²/2.
- Well slope parameter: α = 1 fm⁻¹.
- Effective well length: L = 6 fm.
- Surface interaction parameters: W = 2 m₀c² (repulsive) and W = -2 m₀c² (attractive).
- For repulsive surface effects with ε⁺ = 0, the allowed energy interval length is m₀c²; the spectrum contains only positive eigenvalues.
- For repulsive surface effects with ε⁺ = 50 MeV, the ground state energy eigenvalue approaches zero and becomes negative when ε⁺ = 40 MeV.
- For repulsive surface effects with ε⁺ = m₀c²/2, the number of eigenvalues decreases from nine to seven compared to the ε⁺ = 0 case.
- For attractive surface effects with ε⁺ = 0, sixteen eigenvalues are obtained (lowest node number = 6); with ε⁺ = 50 MeV, seventeen eigenvalues are obtained (lowest node number = 4).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous bound-state study of the Klein-Gordon equation with the generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential (Lütfüoğlu, 2018) by explicitly investigating the role of the differentiation parameter ε⁺, which was previously set to zero in finite-range potential wells (Ginocchio, 1999). The variational principle analogy provides a new perspective on how the difference between vector and scalar potentials controls the spectrum density and interval. The results enable future studies of eigenvalue density tuning in relativistic quantum systems and may inform nuclear structure models where spin symmetry and pseudo-spin symmetry are relevant, such as those by Wei et al. (2008–2010) and Hassanabadi et al. (2012–2013).
