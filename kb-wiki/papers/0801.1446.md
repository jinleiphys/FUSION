# The study of dense matter through jet modification

> The use of jet modification to study the properties of dense matter is reviewed. Different sets of jet correlations measurements which may be used to obtain both the space-time and momentum space structure of the produced matter are outlined.

## Key claim

Jet modification observables can be used to extract both the space-time profile and the momentum-space structure of the dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions, but the magnitude of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ extracted from data ranges from 1 GeV$^2$/fm to 20 GeV$^2$/fm depending on the theoretical scheme and the assumed medium model.

## Method

The paper reviews four major perturbative QCD schemes for calculating in-medium jet energy loss: the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) reaction-operator approach in opacity, the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) path-integral approach, the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) finite-temperature field theory approach, and the higher-twist (HT) scheme. Each scheme factorizes the cross section into nuclear structure functions, a hard partonic cross section, and a medium-modified fragmentation function, with the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ (mean transverse momentum squared per unit length transferred to the jet) controlling the magnitude of modification. The paper then outlines how single-inclusive hadron suppression constrains the maximum $\hat{q}$, while jet correlation measurements (e.g., $R_{AA}$ versus reaction-plane angle, and near-side ridge structures) can probe the space-time profile and momentum-space anisotropy of the medium.

## Key numbers

- The number of hadrons with $p_T \geq 7$ GeV is reduced by almost a factor of 5 in central $Au$-$Au$ collisions compared to binary-scaled nucleon-nucleon expectations.
- Single inclusive observables have been extended to $p_T \leq 20$ GeV.
- Values of the maximum $\hat{q}$ near the center of a central collision at a time of $\sim 1$ fm/c range from 1 GeV$^2$/fm up to 20 GeV$^2$/fm, depending on the scheme and medium model.
- In the quantitative estimate of ridge-like structure, a trigger quark with $E = 10$ GeV radiates a 4 GeV gluon, which propagates through 3 fm of turbulent plasma.

## Context

This review extends the established finding of jet quenching at RHIC (Adcox 2001) by surveying how different theoretical schemes (GLV, ASW, AMY, HT) relate the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ to medium properties. It enables future work to discriminate among these schemes by comparing predictions for multi-particle correlations (e.g., $R_{AA}$ versus reaction-plane angle, near-side ridge distributions) against experimental data, potentially revealing whether the medium has anisotropic momentum-space structure from turbulent colour fields (Asakawa 2006).
