# Living on the edge: radius effects in the angular substructure of heavy-ion jets

> Jet substructure observables serve as essential tools for probing the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Their interpretation, however, is often complicated by edge effects, which arise when correlated particles fall outside the reconstructed jet radius, introducing distortions that obscure the underlying QCD dynamics. In this work, we present a comprehensive phenomenological study of edge effects in soft-insensitive angular observables, taking the two-point energy correlator (EEC) as a representative example. We argue that these distortions scale linearly with the average angular separation between the winner-take-all and $E$-scheme axes $\langle ϕ\rangle$, and validate this behavior across proton-proton (p-p) simulations with Pythia8 and Herwig7, as well as lead-lead (Pb-Pb) simulations using JEWEL and CoLBT. In p-p collisions, edge effects are strongly suppressed, scaling as $(R_L/R)^4$, whereas medium-modified jets can exhibit larger distortions, with contributions scaling as $(R_L/R)^2$ and $(R_L/R)^4$. Taking Pb-Pb/p-p ratios of the EEC substantially reduces, but does not completely eliminate, these distortions, highlighting the need of accounting for edge effects in the interpretation of heavy-ion jet substructure measurements. Since edge effects are largely governed by the $\langle ϕ\rangle$ distribution, studying this distribution provides a new handle for benchmarking and constraining the modeling of edge effects in heavy-ion event generators.

## Key claim

Edge effects in the two-point energy correlator (EEC) for heavy-ion jets scale linearly with the average angular separation between the winner-take-all and E-scheme axes ⟨ϕ⟩, and while taking Pb-Pb/p-p ratios substantially reduces these distortions, it does not completely eliminate them, meaning that genuine QGP-induced modifications cannot be fully disentangled from reconstruction artifacts without explicitly accounting for edge effects.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive phenomenological study using Monte Carlo event generators, simulating proton-proton collisions with Pythia8 and Herwig7, and lead-lead collisions with JEWEL and CoLBT. They developed a simplified analytical model for the combined jet function G that describes both jet clustering and the EEC measurement, deriving a multiplicative correction factor E(⟨ϕ⟩, R_L/R) = (1 − ⟨ϕ⟩(p_T) K(R_L/R)) that accounts for edge effects in the angular range R_L < R. The model was validated by comparing EEC spectra across different jet radii (R = 0.2, 0.4, 0.8) and testing the scaling behavior of edge-effect distortions.

## Key numbers

- In p-p collisions, edge effects scale as (R_L/R)^4, providing strong suppression of distortions
- In medium-modified jets (Pb-Pb), edge effects scale as (R_L/R)^2 and (R_L/R)^4, leading to larger distortions
- Edge effects become pronounced for angular separations R_L ≳ 0.1, as shown in simulations with jet radii R = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.8 at √s = 5.02 TeV
- The study used jets with transverse momentum 120 < p_T < 140 GeV for the p-p comparison across different jet radii

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework for energy correlators developed by Dixon (2019), Lee (2024), and others, which previously neglected edge effects arising from practical jet clustering. The study is motivated by recent CMS measurements (2025) showing EEC deviations in Pb-Pb collisions for R_L ≳ 0.1, precisely the region where edge effects become significant. The findings enable more robust theory-data comparisons by providing a data-driven method to quantify and mitigate edge effects through measurements of the ⟨ϕ⟩ distribution (sometimes called Δj in experimental literature from ALICE 2022, 2023 and CMS 2025, 2024), and establish a framework for benchmarking heavy-ion event generators like JEWEL and CoLBT against these effects.
