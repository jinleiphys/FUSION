# Comment on "Tidal Love numbers of neutron and self-bound quark stars"

> We comment on the paper of S. Postnikov et al. in Phys. Rev. D 82, 024016 (2010) and give a modified formula that needs to be taken into account when calculating the tidal Love number of neutron stars in case a first order phase-transition occurs at non-zero pressure. We show that the error made when using the original formula tends to zero as $p \rightarrow 0$ and we estimate the maximum relative error to be $\sim 5\%$ if the density discontinuity is at larger densities.

## Key claim

The jump condition for the tidal Love number at a first-order phase transition must include an extra pressure term in the denominator; the original formula of Postnikov et al. (2010) is only valid when the phase transition occurs at very low pressure compared to the central energy density, and the maximum relative error from using the uncorrected formula is about 5% for transitions at higher densities.

## Method

The authors derived the correct jump condition for the logarithmic derivative \(y(r)\) of the metric perturbation at a density discontinuity by integrating the differential equation for \(y(r)\) across the discontinuity, using a delta-function representation of the divergent \(1/c_s^2\) term. They then computed tidal Love numbers for hybrid stars using a constant-sound-speed construction (Alford et al., 2013; Han et al., 2019) with the SFHo and DD2 nuclear matter equations of state, varying the transition density between \(n_0\) and \(3.5 n_0\) and the energy density jump \(\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_\mathrm{trans}\) between 0 and 3, and compared results from the corrected and original formulas.

## Key numbers

- The maximum relative error in the tidal Love number from using the original formula is \(\sim 4.2\%\) for the SFHo equation of state and \(\sim 4.3\%\) for the DD2 equation of state.
- The maximum relative error occurs at \(n_\mathrm{trans}/n_0 \approx 2.5\) and \(\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_\mathrm{trans} \approx 1.5\) for SFHo, and at \(n_\mathrm{trans}/n_0 \approx 2.0\) and \(\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_\mathrm{trans} \approx 1.5\) for DD2.
- The error does not exceed \(5\%\) over the whole parameter range studied.
- The nuclear saturation density is \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This comment corrects the jump condition for the tidal Love number at a first-order phase transition originally derived by Postnikov et al. (2010), which has been applied in subsequent work by Zhao et al. (2018) and Han et al. (2019). The corrected formula is already used in Zhang et al. (2020) and was verified to have been used in the actual calculations of Han et al. (2019) and later papers by that group; the results of Zhao et al. (2018) are also mainly unaffected because their approximate analytic fits inherently contain few-percent uncertainties. The corrected formula enables more accurate calculations of tidal deformabilities for hybrid stars with phase transitions at non-negligible pressures, which is relevant for interpreting gravitational-wave signals from binary neutron star mergers.
