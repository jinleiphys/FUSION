# Comment on “Exact three-dimensional wave function and the on-shell t matrix for the sharply cut-off Coulomb potential: Failure of the standard renormalization factor”

## Key claim

The analytically derived three-dimensional wave function and on-shell t matrix for scattering on a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential presented by Gloeckle et al. (2009) are fallacious for finite cut-off radius R and mathematically ungrounded in the limit R → ∞; the renormalization factor they propose lacks mathematical grounds because it derives from an incorrect wave function ansatz and neglects a second term in the scattering amplitude that may provide a nonvanishing contribution.

## Method

The authors demonstrate that the interior-region wave function assumed by Gloeckle et al. (2009), a single product of a plane wave and a confluent hypergeometric function multiplied by a constant A, is incorrect. They show that the correct interior solution requires an angular-dependent amplitude A(k̂) expanded in Legendre polynomials, with partial-wave coefficients A_l obtained by matching interior and exterior solutions at r = R. They then analyze the asymptotic limit R → ∞ using partial-wave expansions, showing that the convergence of A_l is not uniform in angular momentum l, and that the scattering amplitude derived by Gloeckle et al. (2009) omits a second term involving an auxiliary function ψ_R that does not vanish for r ≲ R.

## Key numbers

- Sommerfeld parameter η = me²/(2p) appears in the confluent hypergeometric function and phase shifts.
- The incorrect constant A = 1/₁F₁(−iη, 1, 2ipR) from Gloeckle et al. (2009) matches only the l = 0 partial-wave coefficient A₀, while A_{l≥1} ≠ A.
- The asymptotic limit for partial-wave coefficients is A_l ≃ e^{−πη/2} Γ(1 + iη) e^{−iη ln(2pR)} + O(1/(pR)) for l ≪ pR.
- The scattering amplitude takes the asymptotic form f_R = e^{−2iη ln(2pR)} f_c + f_osc, where f_osc oscillates rapidly like cos(qR) with q the momentum transfer.

## Context

This Comment refutes the analytical results of Gloeckle et al. (2009) on scattering from a sharply cut-off Coulomb potential, showing that their wave function ansatz and renormalization factor are invalid. The work extends the standard partial-wave treatment of cut-off Coulomb potentials found in textbooks such as Taylor (1972) and earlier work by Ford (1964) and Taylor (1974). The authors point to alternative renormalization methods based on regularization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in the on-shell limit, as developed by Schwinger, Shastry, and Popov (1981), which avoid the uncertainties of cut-off renormalization and can be generalized to many-body Coulomb scattering as in Shablov et al.
