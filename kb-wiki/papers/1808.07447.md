# Nuclear shape phase transitions within a correlation between two quantum concepts

> We present a correlation that we have revealed, for the first time, between both quantum concepts, namely: the Minimal Length (ML) and the Deformation Dependent Mass (DDM) in transitional nuclei near the critical points symmetries (CPS) X(3) and Z(4). Such a correlation could be considered as a new signature for these CPS. This new signature allowed us to predict new candidate nuclei for these critical points.

# Nuclear shape phase transitions within a correlation between two quantum concepts

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate a strong correlation between the Minimal Length (ML) and Deformation Dependent Mass (DDM) concepts in transitional nuclei near the X(3) and Z(4) critical point symmetries, with the correlation holding independently of the form of the potential used (infinite square well or Davidson potential). This correlation is presented as a new signature for identifying candidate nuclei for these critical point symmetries, though the authors note that the existence of gamma-rigid triaxial nuclei in nature remains hypothetical.

## Method
The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian separately within the DDM framework (using a deforming function f(beta) = beta^{-a}) and within the ML framework (using a generalized uncertainty principle with parameter alpha) for the X(3) and Z(4) critical point symmetries. They fit the energy ratios R_{Lg/2g}, R_{Lbeta/2g}, and R_{Lgamma/2g} from the theoretical formulas to experimental data for 36 even-even nuclei (for X(3)) and additional isotopes (for Z(4)), extracting the best-fit values of the DDM parameter a and the ML parameter alpha. The correlation between these two parameters is then examined for both the infinite square well potential and the Davidson potential.

## Key numbers
- Cross-correlation coefficient between a (DDM) and alpha (ML) for X(3) with infinite square well potential: 0.96
- Cross-correlation coefficient between a (DDM) and alpha (ML) for Z(4) with infinite square well potential: 0.97
- Cross-correlation coefficient between a (DDM) and alpha (ML) for X(3) with Davidson potential: 0.97
- Cross-correlation coefficient between a (DDM) and alpha (ML) for Z(4) with Davidson potential: 0.99
- For the infinite square well potential, the best candidate nuclei for X(3) and Z(4) have parameters a and alpha near 0.001
- For the Davidson potential, the best candidate nuclei have parameters a and alpha spread between 0.07 and 0.2
- Cross-correlation coefficient between Davidson potential minima beta_0^{ML} and beta_0^{DDM}: less than 0.3
- Reference ratio for X(3) model: R_{4g/2g} = 2.44
- Reference ratio for Z(4) model: R_{4g/2g} = 2.23

## Context
This work extends previous studies of critical point symmetries in nuclear shape phase transitions, building on the X(3) model introduced by Bonatsos et al. and the Z(4) model for prolate-to-triaxial transitions. The authors had previously introduced the Minimal Length concept into nuclear structure through the Bohr-Mottelson model, while DDM had been applied in earlier work by various groups. The correlation revealed here provides a new method for identifying candidate nuclei for X(3) symmetry (including new candidates 124Xe and 190Pt) and Z(4) symmetry (including new candidate 114Pd), and opens the possibility of investigating such correlations in other critical point symmetries.
