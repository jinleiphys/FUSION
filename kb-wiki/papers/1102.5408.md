# An extension to the Luscher's finite volume method above inelastic threashold (formalism)

> An extension of the Luscher's finite volume method above inelastic thresholds is proposed. It is fulfilled by extendind the procedure recently proposed by HAL-QCD Collaboration for a single channel system. Focusing on the asymptotic behaviors of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions (equal-time) near spatial infinity, a coupled channel extension of effective Schrodinger equation is constructed by introducing an energy-independent interaction kernel. Because the NBS wave functions contain the information of T-matrix at long distance, S-matrix can be obtained by solving the coupled channel effective Schrodinger equation in the infinite volume.

# An extension to the Luscher's finite volume method above inelastic threshold (formalism)

## Key claim

The paper proposes an extension of Luscher's finite volume method to energies above inelastic thresholds by constructing a coupled-channel effective Schrodinger equation with an energy-independent interaction kernel, using the asymptotic behavior of equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions. The method is formalized for the NLambda-NSigma coupled system and is claimed to work provided the volume is sufficiently large compared to the range of the interaction and the NBS wave functions are linearly independent.

## Method

The authors focus on the asymptotic behavior of equal-time NBS wave functions near spatial infinity for the NLambda-NSigma coupled system, showing that these wave functions contain the on-shell T-matrix in their long-distance part. They construct K functions by applying Helmholtz operators to the NBS wave functions, which filter out propagating degrees of freedom and yield localized objects, then factorize these K functions into an energy-independent interaction kernel U multiplied by the NBS wave functions. This factorization leads to a coupled-channel effective Schrodinger equation that can generate all NBS wave functions simultaneously, and once U is determined in a finite volume, the S-matrix is obtained by solving the equation in infinite volume.

## Key numbers

- Masses used: m_N ~ 940 MeV, m_Lambda ~ 1115 MeV, m_Sigma ~ 1190 MeV
- Energy region considered: m_N + m_Lambda <= E <= m_N + m_Lambda + m_pi, which covers the elastic NLambda region (m_N + m_Lambda <= E <= m_N + m_Sigma) and the NLambda-NSigma coupling region (m_N + m_Sigma <= E <= m_N + m_Lambda + m_pi)
- The asymptotic forms of NBS wave functions contain factors of 2/(E_N(p) + E_Lambda(p)) and 2/(E_N(q) + E_Sigma(q)) multiplying the on-shell T-matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the single-channel procedure previously proposed by the HAL-QCD Collaboration (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2009) to coupled-channel systems, addressing a limitation of the standard Luscher finite volume method (Luscher 1991) which is restricted to elastic scattering. An earlier attempt to extend Luscher's method above inelastic thresholds (Liu 2006) could only impose constraints on S-matrix elements but could not extract them separately. The method enables lattice QCD calculations of scattering in hyperon systems such as LambdaLambda-NXi-SigmaSigma, where experimental information is limited, with a first application reported by Sasaki (2010).
