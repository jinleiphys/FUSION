# Comparing matching prescriptions between pre-equilibrium and hydrodynamic models in high-energy nuclear collisions

> State-of-the-art simulations of high-energy nuclear collisions rely on hybrid setups, involving in particular a pre-equilibrium stage to let the system evolve from a far-from-equilibrium initial condition towards a near-equilibrated state after which fluid dynamics can be applied meaningfully. A known issue is the mismatch between the equation of state in the fluid-dynamical evolution and the effective one in the previous stage, which leads to discontinuities at the interface between the two models. Here we introduce a new matching prescription at this interface, based on the entropy, and we compare it with the standard one relying on local energy conservation. We study the behavior of various quantities at the switching time between the models and investigate a number of final-state hadronic observables. For the latter, we show that they are not modified significantly by the choice of matching prescription, provided an appropriate normalization is chosen for the initial state. In turn, our approach reduces sizeably the ratio of bulk over thermodynamic pressure at the beginning of the fluid-dynamical stage.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new matching prescription between pre-equilibrium and hydrodynamic models based on entropy continuity rather than the standard local energy conservation, and shows that final-state hadronic observables are not significantly modified by the choice of matching prescription provided an appropriate normalization is chosen for the initial state, while the new approach reduces the ratio of bulk over thermodynamic pressure at the beginning of the fluid-dynamical stage.

## Method

The authors simulate Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV using a hybrid setup: Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium model (with shear viscosity eta/s = 0.16 and effective degrees of freedom nu_eff = 40), the MUSIC relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics code with lattice QCD equation of state, and the SMASH hadronic afterburner. They compare two matching prescriptions at the switching time tau_hydro = 1 fm: the standard "energy matching" where the energy density from KøMPøST becomes the energy density in MUSIC, and a proposed "entropy matching" where the equivalent equilibrium entropy density from KøMPøST determines the energy density in MUSIC via the lattice QCD equation of state, with the bulk pressure initialized to ensure continuity of total pressure.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid simulations in high-energy nuclear collisions, specifically addressing the known mismatch between the conformal equation of state in pre-equilibrium models (e = 3P) and the non-conformal lattice QCD equation of state used in fluid dynamics, which causes discontinuities at the interface. The standard energy-matching approach (used in Liu (2015) and others) ensures local energy conservation but leads to large bulk-to-thermodynamic pressure ratios, while the new entropy-matching prescription reduces this ratio significantly. The approach enables more reliable fluid-dynamical evolution, particularly for smaller systems studied by Grosse-Oetringhaus (2024) where edge effects are more important, and can be tested with other pre-equilibrium or fluid-dynamical models beyond KøMPøST and MUSIC.
