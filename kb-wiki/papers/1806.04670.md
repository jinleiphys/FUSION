# Reply to Comment on "Sensitivity of $(d,p)$ reactions to high $n$-$p$ momenta and the consequences for nuclear spectroscopy studies

> We point out that after presenting our results on high $n$-$p$ momentum sensitivity of the $(d,p)$ cross sections in [Phys. Rev. Lett. 117 162502 (2016)] the last paragraph of our Letter refers to a need of going beyond the leading order of Weinberg state treatment. This task could be achieved by using any method that can provide exact solution of the three-body problem. Deltuva [arXiv:1806.00298] uses Faddeev equations to study the NN-model dependence of the $(d,p)$ cross sections. His results are consistent with a new study performed at Surrey which is undergoing a reviewing process at Physical Review C. Both studies discuss the $n$-$p$ sensitivity within three-body $n+p+A$ models with $NN$-independent $N$-$A$ optical potentials. The sensitivity may reappear in many-body treatment of $(d,p)$ reactions, for example, due to the threshold position dependence.

## Key claim

The original claim of sensitivity of $(d,p)$ cross sections to high $n$-$p$ momenta may be reduced when using exact three-body solutions rather than leading-order Weinberg state treatments, but the sensitivity could reappear in many-body treatments due to effects such as threshold position dependence.

## Method

The authors respond to a Comment by Deltuva, who used Faddeev equations to provide an exact solution of the three-body $n+p+A$ problem with $NN$-independent $N$-$A$ optical potentials. The Surrey group performed a new study (under review at Physical Review C) that is consistent with Deltuva's results. Both studies examine the $n$-$p$ sensitivity within three-body models where the nucleon-nucleus optical potential is independent of the nucleon-nucleon interaction model.

## Key numbers

- No new numerical results are presented in this Reply; the paper discusses consistency between Deltuva's Faddeev calculations and the Surrey group's new study, but no explicit cross-section values, energies, or precisions are given.

## Context

This Reply responds to a Comment by Deltuva (2018) on the authors' earlier work Bailey et al. (2016) in Physical Review Letters. The original Letter claimed sensitivity of $(d,p)$ cross sections to high $n$-$p$ momenta, but the last paragraph already noted the need to go beyond leading-order Weinberg state treatment. The present Reply clarifies that exact three-body solutions (such as Faddeev equations) reduce that sensitivity in three-body models, but leaves open the possibility that the sensitivity may reappear in full many-body treatments. The next step is to investigate many-body effects, particularly threshold position dependence, which could restore the sensitivity.
