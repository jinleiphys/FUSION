# Beyond-mean-field approach to low-lying spectra of $Λ$ hypernuclei

> Taking the hypernucleus $^{13}_{~Λ}$C as an example, we illustrate the miscroscopic particle-rotor model for low-lying spectra of hypernuclei. This approach is based on the beyond-mean-field method, with the particle number and angular momentum projections. The quantum fluctuation of the mean-field is also taken into account for the core nucleus using the generator coordinate method. We show that the impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperon, such as a change in $B(E2)$, is well described with this model. Our calculation indicates that the most important impurity effect in $sd$-shell hypernuclei is a change in a deformation parameter rather than in a nuclear size.

## Key claim

The microscopic particle-rotor model, based on a beyond-mean-field approach with particle number and angular momentum projections plus generator coordinate method for the core nucleus, reproduces the low-lying spectrum of \(^{13}_{\Lambda}\)C and shows that the dominant impurity effect of the \(\Lambda\) hyperon in \(sd\)-shell hypernuclei is a change in the deformation parameter rather than a change in nuclear size.

## Method

The authors construct wave functions for the hypernucleus by coupling a \(\Lambda\) hyperon to low-lying states of the core nucleus (\(^{12}\)C), where the core states are obtained via a beyond-mean-field approach including angular momentum projection, particle number projection, and generator coordinate method over the quadrupole deformation parameter \(\beta\). Coupled-channels equations for the radial wave functions of the \(\Lambda\) hyperon are solved using a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with 18 major shells, employing a relativistic point coupling model (PC-F1 parameter set) for nucleon-nucleon interactions and a relativistic zero-range interaction for nucleon-hyperon interactions, with parameters fitted to reproduce the empirical \(\Lambda\) binding energy of \(^{13}_{\Lambda}\)C.

## Key numbers

- The ground state rotational band of \(^{13}_{\Lambda}\)C consists of a 1/2\(^+\) ground state and doublets at (5/2\(^+\), 3/2\(^+\)) and (9/2\(^+\), 7/2\(^+\)), with energy splitting within each doublet of only 10 keV.
- The energy splitting between the first 3/2\(^-\) and 1/2\(^-\) states (negative parity, \(\Lambda\) in \(p\) orbitals) is 199 keV, in good agreement with the empirical value of 152\(\pm\)54\(\pm\)36 keV.
- The \(E2\) transition strength for \(2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1\) in \(^{12}\)C is reduced by approximately 14% due to the addition of a \(\Lambda\) particle.
- The proton radius \(r_p\) is reduced from 2.44 fm to 2.39 fm by adding a \(\Lambda\) particle, leading to about 7.9% reduction in \(r_p^4\).
- The deformation parameter \(\beta\) changes from \(-0.29\) to \(-0.23\), leading to a 37.1% reduction in \(\beta^2\).

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field methods previously developed for nuclear structure (Bender 2003, Yao 2014) to hypernuclei, providing a microscopic particle-rotor model that yields spectra in the laboratory frame, overcoming a limitation of self-consistent mean-field approaches (Zhou 2007, Win 2008, Win 2011, Lu 2011). The method complements other beyond-mean-field applications to hypernuclei (Mei 2015, Yao 2011) and enables systematic studies of impurity effects in light to heavy hypernuclei, with future extensions to triaxial deformation planned for systems such as \(^{25}_{\Lambda}\)Mg.
