# Finite-size effects on the QCD phase diagram

> We discuss the finite-size effects on the chiral phase transition in QCD. We employ the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and calculate the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation. Finite-size effects on the thermodynamic potential are taken into account by employing the multiple reflection expansion. The critical temperature is lowered and the order of the phase transition is changed form first to second as the size of the system of interest is reduced.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, treated via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE), lower the critical temperature and can change the order of the chiral phase transition from first to second, but only if the mass parameter in the MRE density of states is treated as an independent boundary-condition parameter rather than as the dynamical quark mass; a naive identification of that parameter with the dynamical mass yields unphysical results where the second-order transition disappears entirely.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor NJL model in the mean-field approximation, with an ultraviolet cutoff of 0.65 GeV and coupling constant 5.01 GeV\(^{-2}\) chosen to reproduce the pion decay constant and chiral condensate in the chiral limit. Finite-size effects are incorporated by replacing the usual density of states with the MRE density of states for a spherical volume, which includes surface and curvature terms and introduces an infrared cutoff defined by the largest zero of the MRE density. The thermodynamic potential is minimized with respect to the dynamical quark mass to map the phase diagram for various sphere radii, and two distinct treatments of the mass parameter in the MRE density are compared: one where it equals the dynamical quark mass, and one where it is set to a fixed boundary-condition parameter (Dirichlet \(\kappa=\infty\) or Neumann \(\kappa=0\)).

## Key numbers

- Ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda_{\text{UV}} = 0.65\) GeV; coupling constant \(G = 5.01\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV; chiral condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = (-250\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- For the Dirichlet boundary condition (\(\kappa=\infty\)), the infrared cutoff is \(\Lambda_{\text{IR}} \simeq 1.8 / R\) (with \(R\) in fm).
- For the Neumann boundary condition (\(\kappa=0\)), the infrared cutoff is \(\Lambda_{\text{IR}} = 1/(\sqrt{2}R)\).
- For Dirichlet boundary condition, the tricritical point disappears at \(R = 12\) fm; chiral symmetry breaking ceases below \(R = 5.7\) fm.
- For Neumann boundary condition, the tricritical point disappears at \(R = 1.2\) fm; chiral symmetry breaking ceases below \(R = 0.9\) fm.
- At zero chemical potential, the critical temperature for Dirichlet boundary condition changes from \(T_c \sim 130\) MeV at \(R = 10\) fm to \(T_c \sim 180\) MeV at \(R = 50\) fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of finite-size effects on the QCD phase diagram, which were limited to (2+1)-dimensional systems (He) or addressed phase transitions not directly related to chiral symmetry (Kogut). It uses the MRE method previously applied to finite lumps of quark matter (Mad4) and contrasts its results with finite-size scaling analyses and lattice QCD calculations (Fuku1, Fuku2, Bail, Gopie, QCDSF, Koma, Orht, SS). The paper shows that the choice of boundary condition in the MRE framework dramatically affects the predicted size dependence of the critical temperature and the existence of a tricritical point, and it suggests that for the Neumann boundary condition the thermodynamic limit is effectively reached for radii above about 20 fm, while for the Dirichlet boundary condition finite-size effects remain significant up to much larger radii.
