# Nuclear dependence asymmetries in direct photon production

> We study the nuclear dependences of high-$p_T$ jet cross sections in one photon and one jet production in proton-nucleus collisions. We find that there exist asymmetries between the outgoing jets and photons. A convincing reason responsible for those asymmetries are demonstrated in perturbative QCD. Significant nuclear enhancements are also found in the inclusive jet cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that in high-\(p_T\) jet and direct photon production in proton-nucleus collisions, the inclusive cross section for the final jet receives contributions from both initial-state and final-state multiple scatterings, while the direct photon receives contributions only from initial-state multiple scatterings, resulting in an asymmetry between the two distributions that is not present in the single-scattering baseline.

## Method

The authors calculate the inclusive cross sections for the final jet and the direct photon in the reaction \(P+A \rightarrow \gamma+jet+X\) using a higher-twist perturbative QCD formalism. They factorize the double-scattering contributions into short-distance partonic hard parts multiplied by twist-4 multiparton correlation functions (Eqs. 15-16), adopting the same approximation for these matrix elements as Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1994) and Guo and Qiu (1996): \(T_i(x,A)=\lambda^2 A^{4/3} f_{i/N}(x)\). The jet double-scattering cross section is separated into initial-state and final-state terms (Eq. 8), while the photon double-scattering cross section contains only the initial-state term (Eq. 9).

## Key numbers

* The nuclear dependence parameter \(\alpha_i\) (defined by \(A^{\alpha_i-1} E_l d\sigma^{(S)}_i/d^3l = E_l d\sigma^{(S)}_i/d^3l + E_l d\sigma^{(D)}_i/d^3l\)) is compared for jet and photon at a proton beam energy of 515 GeV on a copper target.
* At fixed transverse momentum \(l_T = 6\) GeV, the asymmetry between \(\alpha_{jet}\) and \(\alpha_{\gamma}\) increases as \(x_F\) decreases.
* The \(l_T\) dependence of \(\alpha_i\) is shown at \(x_F = 0\).
* The twist-4 matrix elements are parameterized as \(T_i(x,A)=\lambda^2 A^{4/3} f_{i/N}(x)\), where the nuclear enhancement scales as \(A^{4/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the higher-twist perturbative QCD treatment of multiple scatterings in nuclei, previously applied to deeply inelastic scattering and photoproduction by Luo, Qiu, and Sterman (1994) and to direct photon production by Guo and Qiu (1996). While Guo and Qiu (1996) calculated only initial-state multiple scatterings for direct photons, this paper adds the final-state multiple scatterings that contribute to the jet cross section, revealing an asymmetry between jet and photon distributions. The results enable experimental tests of the relative roles of initial-state and final-state multiple scatterings in proton-nucleus collisions, particularly at Fermilab E706 kinematics.
