# Trajectory analysis for fusion path in superheavy-mass region

> We propose an effective method for the precise investigation of the fusion-fission mechanism in the superheavy-mass region, using the fluctuation-dissipation model. The trajectory calculation with friction is performed in the nuclear deformation space using the Langevin equation. In the reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu, the trajectories are classified into the fusion-fission process, the quasi-fission process and the deep quasi-fission process. By analyzing the time evolution of each trajectory, the mechanism of each process is clearly revealed, i.e., it is explained why a trajectory takes a characteristic path in this model. We discuss, in particular, the condition under which the fusion path is followed, which is crucial in the discussion of the possibility of synthesizing superheavy elements.

# Trajectory analysis for fusion path in superheavy-mass region

## Key claim

The necessary condition for a trajectory to enter the fusion area in the superheavy-mass region is overcoming the second ridge on the $z-\delta$ plane, not the first ridge near the point of contact; even if a trajectory overcomes the first ridge, it does not mean it enters the fusion area.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional Langevin trajectory calculations in the nuclear deformation space using two-center parameterization (coordinates $z_0$, $\delta$, $\alpha$) with a fluctuation-dissipation model, incorporating hydrodynamical inertia in the Werner-Wheeler approximation, wall-and-window one-body dissipation, and temperature-dependent potential energy including shell correction. Trajectories were started at the point of contact for the reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu and classified into fusion-fission (FF), quasi-fission (QF), and deep quasi-fission (DQF) processes based on their time evolution in deformation space.

## Key numbers

- In the reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb at $E^{*}=50$ MeV, the fusion-fission process makes up 96.2% of all trajectories.
- For the FF trajectory in $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, the trajectory is trapped in the subpocket for a time interval of $1.7 \times 10^{-20}$ sec before moving to the main pocket.
- In $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu at $E^{*}=50$ MeV, kinetic energy dissipates by $t=3.0 \times 10^{-22}$ sec after contact.
- The turning stage of the DQF process in $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu takes $2.0$ to $3.7 \times 10^{-21}$ sec.
- The fusion box is defined as $\{z < 0.6, \delta < 0.2, |\alpha| < 0.25\}$.
- The injection point for $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu is at $z=1.54$, $\delta=0.0$, $\alpha=0.67$.

## Context

This work extends the fluctuation-dissipation approach of Swiatecki (1981) and Blocki (1986), who used mean trajectories and liquid drop model potentials, by incorporating shell correction energy and three-dimensional deformation coordinates including fragment deformation. The authors previously showed (Aritomo, 2004) that their model reproduces experimental mass distributions and cross sections for $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu. The present analysis reveals that the effective fusion barrier is the second ridge on the $z-\delta$ plane, not the first ridge near contact, which is crucial for estimating fusion probabilities in superheavy element synthesis.
