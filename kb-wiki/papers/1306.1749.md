# Parity Odd Domain Structure with Generalized $θ$ Vacuum

> Recent experiments in heavy ion collisions have shown the possibility of creating parity-odd domains resulting from the $θ$ term in strong interaction Lagrangian. The $θ$ term originates from the nontrivial solution of QCD vacuum known as the $θ$ vacuum, and the value of $θ$ is taken to be a function of spacetime coordinates in the parity-odd domains. This means that we have to consider different theories at each point so that we need to devise a new approach to define the QCD vacuum. In this Letter, we suggest a method to generalize the $θ$ vacuum by exploiting the dimension 2 condensates and to calculate the parity-odd domain structure as the union of gauge slices defined by the constant value of dimension 2 condensate.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the spacetime-dependent $\theta$ parameter observed in parity-odd domains from heavy ion collisions can be reinterpreted as a set of gauge slices defined by constant values of the dimension 2 gluon condensate $\langle A_{\mu}^{2} \rangle$, rather than requiring a different QCD vacuum theory at each spacetime point.

## Method

The authors construct topological spaces of gluonic domains classified by the numbers of quarks and antiquarks inside each domain, using union and intersection operations on open sets assigned to the domains. They derive a functional form for the dimension 2 condensate $\langle A_{\mu}^{2} \rangle$ by introducing a measure $\mathfrak{M}(Q)$ on the nonlocal quark condensate amplitude $Q(x^2)$, imposing that the measure decreases as $Q$ increases and satisfies additivity for independent $Q_1$ and $Q_2$, yielding $\mathfrak{M}(Q) = -k \ln(Q/Q_0)$. This leads to an explicit expression for $\langle A_{\mu}^{2} \rangle$ as a function of quark positions, which they evaluate numerically for a 6-quark domain.

## Key numbers

* The parity violating parameter $\theta$ measured in heavy ion collisions is of order $10^{-2}$, in contrast to the stringent limit $\theta < 3 \times 10^{-10}$ from neutron electric dipole moment measurements.
* The variation of $\theta$ between the hadronic phase and quark-gluon plasma phase can be up to a factor of $10^{8}$.
* The average size of an instanton is taken to be about $1/3$ fermi, requiring at least 2 or 3 fermi size domains for instanton liquid formation.

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid picture of the QCD vacuum, where instantons generate different $\theta$-worlds via tunnelling (Author R3), and builds on lattice calculations confirming the relation between dimension 2 condensate and instanton contributions (Author R5, R6). The paper enables a new topological description of parity-odd domains observed by the STAR and ALICE Collaborations in relativistic heavy ion collisions, providing a framework where the large difference between the neutron EDM limit and the heavy-ion $\theta$ value can be explained by localized, metastable vacuum domains with spacetime-dependent $\theta$.
