# Phase Transitions in Non-extensive Spin Systems

> The spherical spin model with infinite-range ferromagnetic interactions is investigated analytically in the framework of non-extensive thermostatics generalizing the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistical mechanics. We show that for repulsive correlations, a new weak-ferromagnetic phase develops. There is a tricritical point separating para, weak-ferro and ferro regimes. The transition from paramagnetic to weak-ferromagnetic phase is an unusual first order phase transition in which a discontinuity of the averaged order parameter appears, even for finite number of spins. This result puts in a new way the question of the stability of critical phenomena with respect to the long-ranged correlations.

# Phase Transitions in Non-extensive Spin Systems

## Key claim

The spherical spin model with infinite-range ferromagnetic interactions, when analyzed using Tsallis generalized statistical mechanics with a subadditive entropy regime (q>1, corresponding to repulsive long-range correlations), develops a new weak-ferromagnetic phase separated from the paramagnetic phase by a first-order phase transition that exhibits a discontinuity of the averaged order parameter even for finite numbers of spins. This result suggests that second-order critical phenomena may be unstable in the presence of long-range repulsive correlations, putting the interpretation of critical behavior in nuclear collisions into a different perspective.

## Method

The authors analytically solved the Berlin-Kac Model (BKM) of spherical spins with infinite-range ferromagnetic interactions using Tsallis generalized statistical mechanics, characterized by the entropic index q. They introduced the Out-of-Extensivity (OE) parameter κ = N(q-1), which remains constant in the thermodynamic limit, and derived the free energy per spin and the averaged order parameter m_q from the equilibrium energy distribution. The phase diagram was constructed by analyzing the behavior of the order parameter as a function of temperature (βJ) and the OE parameter κ, identifying the nature of phase transitions through the analytic properties of the free energy.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point separating para, weak-ferro, and ferro regimes is located at (βJ)_TC = 4 and κ_TC = 2.
- The critical temperature for the non-extensive BKM is (βJ)_c,q = 8/κ.
- For κ > 2/(1-2/N), the order parameter m_q exhibits a discontinuous jump at the critical temperature between a small value of order 1/√N and 1/2.
- For κ < 2/(1-2/N), the order parameter m_q is continuous but its first temperature derivative becomes discontinuous at (βJ)_c,q = 8/κ.
- In the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs limit (κ=0), the critical temperature is (βJ)_c = 4.
- For finite N=50, the pseudo-critical point of the standard second-order phase transition is (βJ)_c,N = 3.84.

## Context

This work extends the Berlin-Kac spherical spin model (Berlin and Kac, 1952) into the framework of Tsallis non-extensive statistical mechanics (Tsallis, 1988), building on earlier studies of non-extensive thermodynamics dating back to Renyi (1970) and Landsberg (1970). The results are motivated by experimental observations of critical-like signals in nuclear collisions (Pochodzalla, 1997; Ma, 1997) and charged cluster fragmentation (Chandezon, 1995), where long-range repulsive Coulomb interactions are essential. The finding that the fundamental scaling parameter is N(q-1) rather than q alone is a new result in non-extensive physics, and the demonstration that first-order transitions can persist in finite systems enables reinterpretation of critical behavior signatures observed in small fragmenting systems.
