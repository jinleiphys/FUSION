# Threshold $pp\to ppπ^0$ up to one-loop accuracy

> The $pp\to ppπ^0$ cross section near threshold is computed up to one-loop order including the initial and final state interactions using the hybrid heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and the counting rule a la Weinberg. With the counter terms whose coefficients are fixed by the resonance-saturation assumption, we find that the one-loop contributions are as important as the tree-order contribution and bring the present theoretical estimation of the total cross section close to the experimental data. The short-ranged contributions are controlled by means of a cutoff, and a mild cutoff dependence is observed when all diagrams of the given chiral order are summed. To the order treated, however, the expansion is found to converge rather slowly, calling for further studies of the process.

## Key claim

The one-loop contributions to the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ cross section near threshold are as important as the tree-order contribution, bringing the theoretical estimate close to experimental data; however, the chiral expansion converges rather slowly, calling for further studies of the process.

## Method

The authors used hybrid heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) with Weinberg's counting rule to compute the total cross section up to one-loop order, including initial and final state interactions (ISI and FSI) via phenomenological wavefunctions from the Reid soft core (RSC) and Hamada-Johnston (HJ) potentials. Short-ranged counter terms were fixed by the resonance-saturation assumption using omega and sigma meson exchanges, and a cutoff regularization was applied to control short-distance contributions.

## Key numbers

- The momentum scale at threshold is $Q \sim \sqrt{m_\pi m_N}$, with $Q/\Lambda_\chi \sim \sqrt{m_\pi/m_N} \simeq 0.4$, where $\Lambda_\chi \sim m_N \sim 4\pi f_\pi$ and $f_\pi \simeq 93$ MeV.
- The low-energy constants used are $c_1 = -0.93 \pm 0.10$, $c_2 = 3.34 \pm 0.20$, $c_3 = -5.29 \pm 0.25$ [GeV$^{-1}$].
- The resonance-saturation values for counter terms give $d_1^{(2)}-d_2^{(2)}+d_3^{(2)}-d_4^{(2)} = -7.70$ and $d_9^{(2)R} = -4.78$, using $g_\sigma = 10.5$, $m_\sigma = 508$ MeV, $g_\omega = 10.1$, $m_\omega = 783$ MeV.
- Relative to the impulse approximation (IA) amplitude at $|\vec{q}|_{\rm max} = 0.1\ m_\pi$ with RSC wavefunctions: leading meson-exchange contribution (OPE) is $-0.8$; one-loop OPE plus TPE contributions sum to $(-1.0) + (-1.2) = (-2.2)$; total (including IA) is $-2.0$, i.e., twice the IA contribution with opposite sign.
- The $1/m_N$ correction (diagram b2) is about 20% (RSC) and 40% (HJ) of the leading MEC contribution (b1) after including ISI and FSI, compared to about 260% without them.

## Context

This work extends tree-order calculations by Park et al. (1996), Cohen et al. (1996), Sato et al. (1997), and van Kolck (1997), and partial one-loop calculations by Gedalin et al. (1998) and Dmitrasinovic et al. (1999). The process is identified as chiral-filter-unprotected, similar to the polarized $np$-capture process and the solar "hep" process discussed by Park et al. (1996) and Park et al. (1998). The results enable future studies of higher-order terms, alternative counting rules, and verification of the fixed kinematics approximation.
