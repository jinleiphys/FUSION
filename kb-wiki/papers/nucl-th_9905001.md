# The inclusive 56Fe(nu_e,e-)56Co cross section

> We study the 56Fe(nu_e,e^-)56Co cross section for the KARMEN neutrino spectrum. The Gamow-Teller contribution to the cross section is calculated within the shell model, while the forbidden transitions are evaluated within the continuum random phase approximation. We find a total cross section of 2.73 x 10^-40 cm^2, in agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The total calculated cross section of 2.73 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm² agrees with the KARMEN experimental value of 2.56 ± 1.08(stat.) ± 0.43(syst.) × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm², but the authors note the large experimental uncertainty and present their result as a demonstration that the hybrid shell-model-plus-continuum-RPA approach can reliably evaluate neutrino-induced cross sections on iron-mass nuclei.

## Method

The Gamow-Teller (1⁺) contribution was calculated using large-scale shell model calculations in the complete pf shell with the modified KB3 interaction, applying a universal scaling factor of (0.74)² to account for overestimation of GT strength. The forbidden transitions (including 1⁻, 2⁻, and other multipoles) were evaluated using the continuum random phase approximation (RPA) with partial occupancies in the parent ground state derived from the shell model. The Fermi (0⁺) contribution to the isobaric analog state was calculated within the RPA using isospin symmetry. All partial cross sections were folded with the Michel energy spectrum of neutrinos from muon decay at rest.

## Key numbers

- Total ⁵⁶Fe(νₑ,e⁻)⁵⁶Co cross section for the KARMEN neutrino spectrum: 2.73 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²
- Experimental KARMEN result: 2.56 ± 1.08(stat.) ± 0.43(syst.) × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²
- Partial Gamow-Teller (1⁺) cross section (shell model, corrected): 110 × 10⁻⁴² cm²
- Partial Fermi (0⁺) cross section: 53 × 10⁻⁴² cm²
- Summed forbidden transition cross section: 111 × 10⁻⁴² cm²
- Total GT₋ strength from shell model: 8.85 units (experimental value: 9.9 ± 2.4 from Rapaport)
- Truncated shell model total GT₋ strength: 9.3 units
- Occupation numbers for pf shell: protons n₇/₂=5.30, n₃/₂=0.32, n₁/₂=0.07, n₅/₂=0.30; neutrons n₇/₂=7.29, n₃/₂=1.57, n₁/₂=0.44, n₅/₂=0.70

## Context

This work extends earlier shell model estimates by Bugaev (1979) and provides the first calculation combining shell model for allowed transitions with continuum RPA for forbidden transitions on ⁵⁶Fe. The authors build on the continuum RPA formalism developed by Kolbe, Langanke, and Vogel (1999) and the large-scale shell model codes ANTOINE and NATHAN by Caurier. The results are relevant for interpreting neutrino signals from supernovae (Woosley 1990) and for future long-baseline oscillation experiments using steel detectors such as MINOS and OMNIS.
