# BEC-BCS Crossover and the EoS of Strongly Interacting Systems

> We show that at sufficiently strong coupling, quarks form a BEC system that does not collapse into a pressureless gas at zero temperature only if the diquark-diquark repulsion is self-consistently taken into account. It is found that there is a critical value of the coupling constant of the diquark-diquark interaction beyond which the tendency at zero temperature of the strongly interacting diquark gas to condense into the system ground state with zero momentum is compensated by the repulsion between diquarks so keeping a positive pressure with no significant variation along the whole strongly interacting region. We discuss possible implications of the diquark-diquark interaction for the astrophysics of compact stars. Also we analyze the effect of a strong magnetic field for the BEC-BCS crossover.

## Key claim

At sufficiently strong coupling, quarks form a BEC system that does not collapse into a pressureless gas at zero temperature only if the diquark-diquark repulsion is self-consistently taken into account; however, once the diquark-diquark repulsion is included, the diquark system loses its BEC nature, meaning a gravitational-bound compact star cannot be formed by BEC quark molecules.

## Method

The author uses a simplified four-fermion interaction model with a gap parameter in the mean-field approximation to study the BCS-BEC crossover in quark matter. The system of gap equation and number density equation is solved numerically to find the gap and chemical potential as functions of the coupling constant. The equation of state is then obtained from the quantum-statistical average of the energy-momentum tensor, with a bag constant B^(1/4)=145 MeV included to model the energy difference between perturbative and true vacuum. The effect of diquark-diquark repulsion is incorporated by adding a λΔ^4 term to the free energy, and the magnetic field effect is studied using a Yukawa model with charged fermions coupled to an external magnetic field in the Landau gauge.

## Key numbers

- The critical value for the coupling constant for the BCS-BEC crossover is found at g̃_cr ~ 1.1, where g̃ = gΛ²/4π² and Λ = 602.3 MeV.
- The model parameters are scaled with P_F/Λ = 0.3 and m/Λ = 0.2 to guarantee a relativistic regime.
- The bag constant used in the equation of state calculations is B^(1/4) = 145 MeV.
- The diquark-diquark repulsion coupling constant is taken as λ = 27.8, estimated from the modified P-matrix formalism of Jaffe and Low.
- For the magnetic field study, the fermion mass is m = 0.2 GeV and the Yukawa coupling is g = 1, with a smooth ultraviolet cutoff Λ = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of BCS-BEC crossover in quark matter previously investigated by the author and collaborators (Ferrer and collaborators, 2013) to include the equation of state and magnetic field effects. The analysis addresses the chromomagnetic instabilities that render known color-superconducting phases unphysical at intermediate densities, as discussed in the context of the gCFL phase. The results are relevant for neutron star astrophysics, where magnetic fields range from 10^12 to 10^15 G on the surface (pulsars and magnetars) with upper limit estimates reaching 10^18-10^20 G in the core, and for heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, NICA, and JPARK where magnetic fields of order eB ~ 2m²_p to 15m²_p can be generated.
