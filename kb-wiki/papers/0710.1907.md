# Cross sections for meson-meson nonresonant reactions

> Meson-meson nonresonant reactions governed by the quark-interchange mechanism are studied in a potential that is derived from QCD. S-wave elastic phase shifts for I=2 ππand I=3/2 K πscattering are obtained with wave functions determined by the central spin-independent term of the potential. The reactions include inelastic scatterings of two mesons in the ground-state pseudoscalar octet and the ground-state vector nonet. Cross sections for reactions involving pion, rho, K and K^* indicate that mesonic interactions in matter consisting of only K and K^* can be stronger than mesonic interactions in matter consisting of only pions and rhos and the reaction of I=3/2 πK^* \to ρK is most important among the endothermic nonresonant reactions. By the quark-interchange mechanism we can offer \sqrt s-dependences of phi absorption cross sections in collisions with pion and rho and relevant average cross sections what are very small for the reaction of I=1 πϕ\to K^* K^* and remarkably large for the reaction of I=1 ρϕ\to K^* K^*. It is found from the \sqrt s-dependences of cross sections that rho and K^* creation cross sections can be larger than their absorption cross sections, respectively.

## Key claim

The quark-interchange mechanism, applied with a QCD-derived potential and identical spatial wave functions for all ground-state pseudoscalar and vector mesons, yields cross sections for meson-meson nonresonant reactions that indicate mesonic interactions in matter consisting only of K and K* can be stronger than those in matter consisting only of pions and rhos, and that rho and K* creation cross sections can be larger than their absorption cross sections, though the results depend on the assumption that all mesons share the same spatial wave function and on averaging over the post-prior discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic quark-interchange model with the Buchmuller-Tye potential extended by Foldy-Wouthuysen transformations to include spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor terms. S-wave elastic phase shifts for I=2 pi pi and I=3/2 K pi scattering were computed from the central spin-independent term alone, and cross sections for inelastic nonresonant reactions among ground-state pseudoscalar octet and vector nonet mesons were calculated by averaging transition amplitudes from prior-form and post-form diagrams. The same spatial wave function for quark-antiquark relative motion, obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the central spin-independent potential, was used for all mesons.

## Key numbers

- Mass splitting between pi and rho: 0.6308 GeV (theoretical) versus 0.6304 GeV (experimental)
- Mass splitting m_{K*} - m_K: 0.3928 GeV (theoretical) versus 0.3963 GeV (experimental)
- Maximum cross section for I=2 pi pi -> rho rho: 0.47 mb at sqrt(s) = 1.79 GeV
- Maximum cross section for I=2 pi rho -> rho rho: 0.73 mb at sqrt(s) = 1.86 GeV
- Maximum cross section for I=1 K K -> K* K*: 0.60 mb at sqrt(s) = 1.94 GeV
- Maximum cross section for I=1 K K* -> K* K*: 0.84 mb at sqrt(s) = 2.09 GeV
- Maximum cross section for I=3/2 pi K* -> rho K: 1.41 mb at sqrt(s) = 1.42 GeV (largest among endothermic nonresonant reactions studied)
- Maximum cross section for I=1 pi phi -> K K* (or K* K): 0.99 mb at sqrt(s) = 1.51 GeV
- Maximum cross section for I=1 pi phi -> K* K*: 0.65 mb at sqrt(s) = 2.11 GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-interchange model previously applied to hadron-J/psi dissociation (Barnes, Swanson, and collaborators, 1990s) and to elastic pi pi and K pi scattering (Barnes et al., 1992) by calculating a comprehensive set of inelastic cross sections for meson-meson reactions relevant to hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions. The results provide cross sections for reactions involving pi, rho, K, K*, and phi that were previously unknown in both experiment and theory, enabling transport model calculations for chemical equilibration and thermalization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding phi meson behavior in hadronic matter where earlier effective Lagrangian approaches (Haglin, 1990s; Ko and collaborators, 1990s) gave conflicting results.
