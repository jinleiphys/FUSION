# R-mode instability of strange stars and observations of neutron stars in LMXBs

> Using a realistic equation of state (EOS) of strange quark matter, namely, the modified bag model, and considering the constraints to the parameters of EOS by the observational mass limit of neutron stars, we study the r-mode instability window of strange stars, and find the same result as the brief study of Haskell, Degenaar and Ho in 2012 that these instability windows are not consistent with the spin frequency and temperature observations of neutron stars in LMXBs.

# R-mode instability of strange stars and observations of neutron stars in LMXBs

## Key claim
The r-mode instability windows of strange stars, calculated using a realistic modified bag model equation of state with parameters constrained by observed neutron star masses, are not consistent with the spin frequency and temperature observations of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs). However, if the bulk viscosity of strange quark matter is artificially increased by a factor of 10 to 100 (as might occur from non-Fermi liquid effects), the instability windows could become consistent with the observations.

## Method
The authors used the modified bag model equation of state for strange quark matter, treating up and down quarks as massless and the strange quark mass as a free parameter (100 MeV or 200 MeV), with first-order perturbative corrections from the strong coupling constant α_S. They first constrained the parameter space (bag constant B^{1/4} and α_S) by requiring absolute stability of strange quark matter, stability of atomic nuclei, and maximum masses exceeding the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 M_⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 M_⊙). They then solved the r-mode instability inequality (1/τ_GW + 1/τ_η + 1/τ_ζ < 0) numerically for the dominant l=2, m=2 r-mode, computing gravitational-wave growth timescale, shear viscosity damping timescale from quark scattering, and bulk viscosity damping timescale from the non-leptonic weak interaction u+d ↔ s+u.

## Key numbers
- PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙ (Demorest 2010)
- PSR J0348+0432 mass: 2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙ (Antoniadis 2013)
- Shear viscosity of strange quark matter: η ≈ 1.7 × 10^18 (0.1/α_S)^(5/3) ρ_15^(14/9) T_9^(-5/3) g cm^(-1) s^(-1), where T_9 = T/10^9 K and ρ_15 = ρ/10^15 g cm^(-3)
- Bulk viscosity in low-T limit: ζ ≈ 3.2 × 10^28 m_100^4 ρ_15 T_9^2 ω^(-2) g cm^(-1) s^(-1), where m_100 is strange quark mass in units of 100 MeV
- For m_s = 200 MeV and B^(1/4) = 135 MeV with M = 1.0 M_⊙, the instability windows for α_S = 0.4 and α_S = 0.6 can explain all observational data except for SAX J1808.4-3658
- Artificially increasing bulk viscosity by a factor of 100 (left panel) or 10 (right panel) can make instability windows almost consistent with observations

## Context
This work extends the brief study by Haskell, Degenaar and Ho (2012) by using a more detailed equation of state (the modified bag model) and explicitly constraining the parameter space with the observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432. The conclusion that strange star r-mode instability windows are inconsistent with LMXB observations confirms the earlier result of Haskell et al. (2012). The paper suggests that including non-Fermi liquid effects in the bulk viscosity calculation, as studied by Zheng et al. (2002, 2003, 2005) and Schwenzer (2012), could resolve this discrepancy, and the authors indicate this will be explored in future work. The study does not consider strange stars with a crystalline superconducting quark crust (Rupak and Jaikumar 2013) or solid strange quark stars composed of quark clusters (Xu 2003; Yu and Xu 2011; Zhou et al. 2014), where r-mode instability would not occur.
