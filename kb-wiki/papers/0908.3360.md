# Coordinate space proton-deuteron scattering calculations including Coulomb force effects

> We present a practical method to solve the proton-deuteron scattering problem at energies above the three-body breakup threshold, in which we treat three-body integral equations in coordinate space accommodating long-range proton-proton Coulomb interactions. The method is examined for phase shift parameters, and then applied to calculations of differential cross sections in elastic and breakup reactions, analyzing powers, etc. with a realistic nucleon-nucleon force and three-nucleon forces. Effects of the Coulomb force and the three-nucleon forces on these observables are discussed in comparing with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a practical method for solving proton-deuteron scattering above the three-body breakup threshold using coordinate-space integral equations with Coulomb interactions, but the method requires a cutoff factor to partially include long-range Coulomb effects in higher-order iterations, and the cancellation between the proton-proton Coulomb potential and auxiliary Coulomb potentials is not guaranteed once three-body breakup channels open.

## Method

The author solves the Sasakawa-Sawada-Faddeev (SSF) integral equations in coordinate space using the Method of Continued Fractions (MCF) iterative scheme. The method accommodates the long-range proton-proton Coulomb interaction by introducing auxiliary Coulomb potentials that act between charged spectator particles and the center of mass of the remaining pair, with the Coulomb source term in the integral kernel multiplied by a cutoff factor \(e^{-(x_3/R_C)^N}\) for higher-order iterations. The Argonne V\(_{18}\) (AV18) nucleon-nucleon potential and a Brazil two-pion-exchange three-nucleon force with cutoff mass \(\Lambda = 660\) MeV are used as input interactions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^3\)H with AV18 alone: 7.626 MeV (underbound by about 1 MeV compared to empirical 8.482 MeV)
- Binding energy of \(^3\)He with AV18 alone: 6.928 MeV (underbound by about 1 MeV compared to empirical 7.718 MeV)
- Binding energy of \(^3\)H with AV18+BR\(_{660}\): 8.492 MeV
- Binding energy of \(^3\)He with AV18+BR\(_{660}\): 7.763 MeV
- For \(E_p = 5.0\) MeV, \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\) state with AV18: \(^4\)S\(_{1/2}\) eigenphase shift real part \(-41.8^\circ\) and imaginary part \(2.15^\circ\) (with cutoff \(N=4\), \(R_C=8\) fm), compared to Kohn variational principle values of \(-41.8^\circ\) and \(1.74^\circ\)
- For \(E_p = 10.0\) MeV, \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\) state with AV18: \(^4\)S\(_{1/2}\) eigenphase shift real part \(-60.8^\circ\) and imaginary part \(11.9^\circ\) (with cutoff \(N=4\), \(R_C=8\) fm), compared to Kohn variational principle values of \(-60.6^\circ\) and \(11.7^\circ\)
- Cutoff parameters chosen for production calculations: \((N, R_C) = (4, 8\) fm)

## Context

This work extends the Sasakawa-Sawada modification of the Faddeev equation, previously applied to \(^3\)He bound states and proton-deuteron scattering below the three-body breakup threshold by Sasakawa and Sawada (1979) and Ishikawa (2003), to energies above the breakup threshold. The method enables calculations of differential cross sections, analyzing powers, and breakup observables with realistic nucleon-nucleon forces and three-nucleon forces, allowing direct comparison with experimental data. Prior momentum-space approaches using the screening and renormalization method were developed by Alt et al. (2002) and Deltuva et al. (2005, 2006), while the Kohn variational principle calculations were performed by Kievsky (1999, 2001) and Viviani et al. (2001).
