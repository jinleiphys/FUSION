# Structure functions and parton distributions

> In this talk, I review the status of theoretical understanding of nuclear structure functions and parton distributions and discuss the constraints on nuclear parton distributions from existing data and the global QCD analysis.

## Key claim

The pQCD factorization theorems can be applied to hard scattering involving nuclei only if the partonic system is dilute enough that partons are "long-lived" relative to the hard collision time scale; when the parton density becomes so high that power corrections are comparable to leading-power contributions, conventional pQCD factorization breaks down and the concept of universal parton distributions no longer applies.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical derivation of pQCD factorization for nuclear structure functions and parton distributions, showing how the forward scattering amplitude in deep inelastic scattering can be expanded in Feynman diagrams and factorized into a convolution of perturbatively calculable coefficient functions with nonperturbative parton distribution functions. The talk then examines the conditions under which this factorization holds for nuclear targets, including the role of multi-gluon interactions, nuclear shadowing, and the modified DGLAP evolution equations that include gluon recombination terms. Finally, the author compares the two existing global QCD analyses of nuclear parton distributions: the EKS98 package from Eskola, Kolhinen, Ruuskanen, and Salgado (which uses both DIS and Drell-Yan data) and the HKM sets from Hirai, Kumano, and Miyama (which use only DIS data).

## Key numbers

- The modified evolution equation for the gluon distribution in a nucleus includes a nonlinear term proportional to \(1/(R^2 \mu^2)\) that slows the growth of the gluon distribution at small \(x\); this term is suppressed by a power of \(1/\mu^2\) but its effect does not vanish as \(\mu^2 \rightarrow \infty\) because the integral \(\int_{\mu_0^2}^{\mu^2} d\bar{\mu}^2/(\bar{\mu}^2)^2\) approaches \(1/\mu_0^2\).
- The nonlinear evolution is sufficient to keep nuclear shadowing from disappearing as one increases \(\mu^2\) up to values as large as 100 GeV\(^2\).
- The saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) is estimated from the condition \(xG(x,Q_s^2) \propto R^2 Q_s^2 / \alpha_s(Q_s)\).
- The input scale for the EKS98 and HKM global analyses is \(\mu_0^2 = 2.25\) GeV\(^2\).
- Third-type multi-gluon interactions (between two ions) are suppressed by a power of \(1/Q^4\).

## Context

This talk reviews the theoretical status of nuclear parton distributions following the discovery of the EMC effect by the European Muon Collaboration (1983), which showed that the structure function \(F_2\) of iron differs significantly from that of deuterium. The work extends the pQCD factorization framework of Collins, Soper, and Sterman to nuclear targets and discusses the modified evolution equations first derived by Mueller and Qiu. The two existing global analyses discussed are the EKS98 package (Eskola, Kolhinen, Ruuskanen, Salgado) and the HKM sets (Hirai, Kumano, Miyama); the talk identifies the need for next-to-leading-order nuclear parton distributions with better precision on the A-dependence to match the level of precision tests achieved in collisions with free hadrons.
