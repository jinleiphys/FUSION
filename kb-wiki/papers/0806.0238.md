# Surface energy and magneto-capacitance of superconductors under electric field bias

> A superconducting layer exposed to a perpendicular electric field and a parallel magnetic field is considered within the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) approach. The GL equation is solved near the surface and the surface energy is calculated. The nucleation critical field of superconducting state at the surface depends on the magnetic and electric fields. Special consideration is paid to the induced magnetic-field effect cause d by diamagnetic surface currents. The latter effect is strongly dependent on the thickness of the sample. The effective inverse capacitance determines the effective penetration depth. It is found that the capacitance exhibits a jump at the surface critical field. An experiment is suggested for determining the change in the effective capacitance of the layer.

## Key claim

The surface critical field \(B_{\rm c3}\) and the surface energy of a superconducting layer can be tuned by an external electric field via the DeGennes boundary condition, with the effect being asymmetric with respect to the sign of the bias; the bulk upper critical field \(B_{\rm c2}\) remains unchanged by the electric field.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) equation near the surface of a superconducting slab of thickness \(d\), using the DeGennes surface condition with an extrapolation length \(b\) that depends linearly on the applied electric field \(E\). The wave function is expressed in terms of parabolic cylinder functions, and the eigenvalue problem yields the surface critical field \(B_{\rm c3}\). The surface energy is then calculated variationally using the linearized wave function as an ansatz, including the effect of diamagnetic surface currents and finite sample thickness. The effective inverse capacitance is derived from the second derivative of the surface energy with respect to the electric field.

## Key numbers

* The ratio \(B_{\rm c3}/B_{\rm c2}\) for zero external bias is 1.69461, reproducing the known GL result (Saint-James and de Gennes, 1963).
* The variational expansion for \(B_{\rm c3}/B_{\rm c2}\) to first order in \(1/b\) gives \(\sqrt{\pi/(\pi-2)} \approx 1.66\) at zero bias.
* The transition GL parameter between type-I and type-II superconductivity for the superconductor-vacuum boundary is \(\kappa_0 = 0.41727\) (from Eq. (del26a)), compared to \(\kappa_0 = 1.257\) for the superconductor-normal boundary.
* The characteristic potential \(\varphi_{\rm fe}\) in the linear relation \(1/b = 1/b_0 + E/\varphi_{\rm fe}\) is of the order of a few MeV for conventional superconductors.

## Context

This work extends the classic GL treatment of surface superconductivity (Saint-James and de Gennes, 1963; Tinkham) by incorporating an electric field bias through the DeGennes boundary condition, building on earlier studies of field effects on critical parameters (Shanenko, 1985, 1993; Sham and Kogan, 1994). The predicted electric-field dependence of the surface critical field and the jump in capacitance at \(B_{\rm c3}\) suggest a new experimental route to probe surface superconductivity via magneto-capacitance measurements, complementing earlier work on field-effect control of critical temperature (Glover and Sherrill, 1960; Mannhart et al., 1994).
