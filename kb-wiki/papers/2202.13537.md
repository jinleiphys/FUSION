# Casimir effect in kinetic theory

> We study Casimir effect in equilibrium and non-equilibrium photon gas in the frame of quantum kinetic theory for $U(1)$ gauge field. We derive first the transport, constraint and gauge fixing equations for the photon number distribution from Maxwell's equations, and then calculate the energy variation and Casimir force for a finite system by considering boundary condition on the surface of the system. The Casimir force in vacuum is suppressed by the thermal motion of photons in equilibrium state, when considering two adiabatic plates. In non-equilibrium state, the photon induced Casimir force oscillates and decays with time and finally disappears.

# Casimir effect in kinetic theory

## Key claim

The Casimir force in an equilibrium photon gas is *suppressed* by thermal motion when the plates are adiabatic (using internal energy as the potential), contrary to previous works that found enhancement using free energy; in non-equilibrium states, the photon-induced Casimir force oscillates and decays with time, eventually disappearing.

## Method

The authors derive transport, constraint, and gauge-fixing equations for the photon Wigner function from Maxwell's equations under the Lorenz gauge, then factorize the Wigner function into a tensor structure and scalar distribution functions. They impose boundary conditions for perfectly conducting parallel plates at z=0 and z=a, replace the continuous p_z integration with a discrete sum over nπ/a, and compute the energy variation and Casimir force from the internal energy expressed via the photon number distribution f_+.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum Casimir force per unit area: F_0 = -π²/(240 a⁴)
- Equilibrium Casimir force at finite temperature T: F_T = (π²/15)T⁴ - (π²/a⁴) Σ_{n=0}^{∞} n³/(e^{nπ/(aT)}-1)
- Ratio R(aT) = F_m/F_0 at low temperature (aT ≪ 1) using free energy: R = 1 + (16/3)(aT)⁴
- Ratio R(aT) at high temperature (aT ≫ 1) using free energy: R = (60/π³)ζ(3) aT
- Non-equilibrium ratio at t=0: R(t/a=0) = 2.276
- Energy variation at t=0: Δ\bar{ℰ}(t/a=0) = -0.017

## Context

This work extends the Casimir effect from vacuum (Casimir 1948) and thermal equilibrium treatments (Bostrom 2000, Brevik 2006, Geyer 2008, Sushkov 2010) to non-equilibrium photon gases using quantum kinetic theory. It contrasts with prior equilibrium calculations that used free energy ℱ (which gives enhanced force at finite temperature) by instead using internal energy ℰ for adiabatic plates, and it provides the first kinetic-theory framework for the Casimir effect in non-equilibrium systems, enabling future studies of time-dependent Casimir forces in systems such as pulsed light sources between plates.
