# RG-Consistent (P)NJL Model: Impact of Thermal Cutoff Modifications on Thermodynamics and Net-Baryon Number Fluctuations

> In this paper, we investigate the impact of renormalization group (RG) consistency on the chiral phase transition and thermodynamic properties of QCD matter using the RGNJL and RGPNJL models. By implementing a temperature-dependent thermal cutoff $Λ_T = kΛ_0$, we ensure that thermodynamic quantities converge toward the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high temperatures, effectively extending the applicability of these effective theories. Our analysis shows that while the RG-consistency condition ($k \rightarrow \infty$) resolves causality violations in the RGNJL model by binding the speed of sound to the conformal limit, the RGPNJL model exhibits a more complex, non-monotonic sensitivity to the parameter $k$. Furthermore, we demonstrate that the RG-improved PNJL framework significantly enhances the description of net-baryon number fluctuations ($κσ^2$) relative to lattice QCD data at vanishing chemical potential, though the intensification of these fluctuations at high baryon density highlights a critical sensitivity to the model's parametric constraints. This study provides a rigorous evaluation of the RG-consistency framework's predictive power in mapping the QCD phase diagram and interpreting experimental observables.

## Key claim

The RG-consistent thermal cutoff modification (Λ_T = kΛ_0) resolves causality violations in the RGNJL model by binding the speed of sound to the conformal limit, but the RGPNJL model exhibits a more complex, non-monotonic sensitivity to the parameter k, and while the RG-improved PNJL framework significantly enhances the description of net-baryon number fluctuations relative to lattice QCD data at vanishing chemical potential, these fluctuations intensify dramatically at high baryon density, highlighting critical sensitivity to parametric constraints.

## Method

The authors implement a temperature-dependent thermal cutoff Λ_T = kΛ_0 in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models, separating the cutoff scales for vacuum and thermal contributions to the thermodynamic potential. They solve the gap equations for the constituent quark mass M, Polyakov loop variables Φ and Φ̄, and compute thermodynamic quantities (pressure, energy density, specific heat, speed of sound) and net-baryon number fluctuations (κσ²) as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential for various values of the truncation factor k. The model parameters are fixed to reproduce the pion mass m_π = 139.3 MeV and pion decay constant f_π = 92 MeV in vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum cutoff Λ_0 = 651 MeV, coupling G_S = 5.04 GeV⁻², current quark mass m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV (two-flavor parameter set)
- At μ_B = 0, the RGPNJL model with k → ∞ yields κσ² approaching the free quark gas (FQG) limit of 0.068, matching lattice QCD trends better than the RGNJL model
- For μ_B = 600 MeV in the RGPNJL model with k = 50.0, κσ² oscillates from a positive peak near +4 to a negative dip below -6 near T/T_c ≈ 1.0
- The hadron resonance gas (HRG) limit for κσ² is 1; the ideal free quark gas (FQG) limit is 0.068
- In the RGNJL model, the baseline (k = 1.0) shows v_s² > 1 (causality violation) at high temperatures, while k → ∞ binds v_s² to the conformal limit of 1/3
- In the RGPNJL model, intermediate k ≈ 3.0 shows v_s² > 1/3 for T/T_c > 1.5

## Context

This work extends the RG-consistency framework recently applied to the NJL model by Gholami (2024) for color superconductivity, and addresses inconsistencies in cutoff prescriptions previously identified by Xue (2021). The study enables systematic comparison with lattice QCD data from Bazavov (2017) on baryon number fluctuations, and provides a framework for interpreting experimental fluctuation observables from the STAR collaboration (2020, 2025) in the search for the QCD critical point. The RGPNJL model's enhanced sensitivity to critical fluctuations at high baryon density suggests both predictive power and the need for further parametric refinement to map the QCD phase diagram reliably.
