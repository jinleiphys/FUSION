# Neutron-transfer induced breakup of the Borromean nucleus $^9$Be

> We address the problem of evaluating neutron-transfer induced breakup cross sections caused by the Borromean nucleus $^{9}$Be, using the reaction $^{197}$Au($^{9}$Be,$^{8}$Be)$^{198}$Au as a test case. This reaction was recently measured over a wide range of incident energies around the Coulomb barrier. To deal with the high density of $^{198}$Au states that can be potentially populated in this reaction, we employ the Ichimura, Austern, Vicent model, in which the spectrum of physical states for this system is replaced by the solutions of a complex n+$^{197}$Au potential, accounting effectively for the fragmentation of single-particle states into physical states. Furthermore, to account for the unbound nature of the emitted $^{8}$Be system, we employ a three-body model of $^{9}$Be. The calculated stripping cross sections are found to be in good agreement with existing data over a wide range of incident energies. The importance of taking into account the energy spread of the single-particle strength of the outgoing $^{8}$Be and the target-like residual nucleus is discussed.

# Neutron-transfer induced breakup of the Borromean nucleus \(^{9}\)Be

## Key claim

The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model, combined with a three-body description of \(^{9}\)Be, can reproduce measured inclusive neutron-stripping cross sections for the reaction \(^{197}\)Au(\(^{9}\)Be,\(^{8}\)Be)\(^{198}\)Au over a wide range of incident energies around the Coulomb barrier, provided the energy spread of both the \(^{8}\)Be and \(^{198}\)Au single-particle strengths is properly accounted for; standard DWBA or CRC calculations that concentrate the full spectroscopic strength at discrete energies systematically overestimate the data.

## Method

The authors applied the IAV inclusive breakup formalism, which replaces the dense spectrum of \(^{198}\)Au states with a complex neutron+\(^{197}\)Au optical potential whose imaginary part accounts for fragmentation of single-particle strength into physical states. The unbound nature of the emitted \(^{8}\)Be was treated using a three-body (\(\alpha+\alpha+n\)) model of \(^{9}\)Be built within the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method, with \(^{8}\)Be continuum states discretized via a transformed harmonic oscillator pseudostate basis. The double differential cross section for nonelastic breakup was computed for each \(^{8}\)Be pseudostate and integrated over the bound-state region of \(^{198}\)Au (excitation energy below the neutron separation energy \(S_n = 6.5\) MeV), using the Sao Paulo potential for the heavy-ion channels and the Koning-Delaroche global potential for the neutron-target interaction.

## Key numbers

* The \(^{8}\)Be ground-state resonance (\(0^+\)) lies at approximately 0.10 MeV above the \(\alpha+\alpha\) threshold and exhausts about 38% of the total \(^{9}\)Be norm in the three-body model.
* The \(2^+\) resonance in \(^{8}\)Be is concentrated around 3 MeV relative energy, carrying 44% of the norm; the \(4^+\) states contribute only 1% and were neglected.
* The neutron separation energy of \(^{198}\)Au is \(S_n = 6.5\) MeV; the cross section was integrated from \(E_x = 0\) to \(E_x = S_n\).
* Calculations were performed for incident energies from \(E_\mathrm{cm} = 20\) to 50 MeV, covering the Coulomb barrier region.
* The \(0^+\) contribution dominates the cross section at all energies; the \(2^+\) contribution becomes significant only above \(E_\mathrm{cm} = 40\) MeV.
* Rescaling the three-body overlaps by variational Monte Carlo spectroscopic factors (0.595 for \(0^+\), 0.603 for \(2^+\)) improved agreement at the highest energies.

## Context

This work extends the IAV inclusive breakup formalism, previously applied to reactions with stable projectiles (Jin 2015, Potel 2015, 2017), to the case of a Borromean projectile where the ejected core is unbound. It addresses limitations of earlier coupled-reaction-channel (CRC) calculations by Gollan (2021) for the same reaction, which truncated the \(^{198}\)Au spectrum below 700 keV and could not reproduce the inclusive data, and by Kaushik (2021), who assumed unit spectroscopic factors for states up to 1.56 MeV and obtained a misleading dominance of the \(^{8}\)Be(\(2^+\)) state. The method enables quantitative evaluation of neutron-transfer induced breakup as a competing channel potentially responsible for the 20-30% suppression of complete fusion observed in reactions with \(^{9}\)Be (Das 1999, 2002, 2004; Tripathi 2002; Mukherjee 2006; Rath 2009; Rafiei 2010). Future work could extend the approach to other Borromean nuclei and investigate the role of additional \(^{9}\)Be configurations not included in the present three-body model.
