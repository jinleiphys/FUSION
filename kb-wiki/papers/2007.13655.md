# Turbulence Fingerprint on Collective Oscillations of Supernova Neutrinos

> We bring to light a novel mechanism through which turbulent matter density fluctuations can induce collective neutrino flavor conversions in core-collapse supernovae, i.e., the leakage of flavor instabilities between different Fourier modes. The leakage mechanism leaves its notable fingerprint on the flavor stability of a dense neutrino gas by coupling flavor conversion modes on different scales which in turn, makes the flavor instabilities almost ubiquitous in the Fourier space. The most remarkable consequence of this effect is in that it allows for the presence of significant flavor conversions in the deepest supernova regions even in the absence of the so-called fast modes. This is yet another crucial impact of turbulence on the physics of core-collapse supernovae which can profoundly change our understanding of neutrino flavor conversions in the supernova environment.

## Key claim

Turbulent matter density fluctuations can induce collective neutrino flavor conversions in core-collapse supernovae through a "leakage" of flavor instabilities between different Fourier modes, making flavor instabilities nearly ubiquitous in Fourier space even for tiny turbulence amplitudes and allowing significant flavor conversions in the deepest supernova regions without requiring the presence of fast modes.

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of neutrino flavor evolution in a two-flavor scenario using the Liouville-von Neumann equation, then introduce turbulent matter density fluctuations with a Kolmogorov-like spectrum. They solve the resulting coupled-mode equations for a stationary two-beam, monochromatic neutrino gas model to find unstable modes and the corresponding eigenvector distributions as functions of the Fourier mode in the transverse direction.

## Key numbers

- For a neutrino gas with low neutrino number density (μ = 50 km⁻¹), only very low Fourier modes are unstable in a homogeneous gas, but turbulence with amplitude C = 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻⁶ allows instability to leak to much higher Fourier modes
- The leakage amplitude scales as |Q^(k₀±k)|/|Q^(k₀)| ~ λ_k/k, where λ_k is the Fourier component of the matter potential
- For the accretion phase profile, the neutrinosphere radius R = 15 km, with μ_R = 2×10⁵ km⁻¹ and λ_R = 6×10⁷ km⁻¹ (corresponding to matter density ρ ≈ 3×10¹¹ g cm⁻³)
- The growth rate for slow modes is approximately 10 km⁻¹, meaning unstable modes with amplitude |Q^k| ~ 10⁻⁹ can become activated within only ~2 km
- The Kolmogorov turbulence cutoff scale is set to k_cut = 0.01 km⁻¹ with spectral index α = 5/3

## Context

This work extends studies of collective neutrino oscillations in core-collapse supernovae beyond the symmetric "neutrino bulb" models of Duan (2006-2015) and the fast flavor conversion studies of Sawyer (2005), Chakraborty (2016), and Izaguirre (2016). Previous work by Raffelt (2013), Duan (2013-2014), and Abbar (2015) showed that multidimensional effects could remove suppression of collective oscillations, but Chakraborty (2016) found that rapidly changing physical conditions still prevented significant conversions. This paper demonstrates that turbulence provides a mechanism to overcome that limitation. The effect differs fundamentally from turbulence-induced parametric resonances studied by Lund (2013) and others, which require considerably larger turbulence amplitudes to produce noticeable effects. This work enables the possibility of significant collective neutrino flavor conversions in deep supernova regions regardless of whether fast modes exist.
