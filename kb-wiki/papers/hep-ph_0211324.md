# Proton breakup in high-energy pA collisions from perturbative QCD

> We argue that the distribution of hadrons near the longitudinal light-cone in central high-energy pA collisions is computable in weak coupling QCD. This is because the density of gluons per unit transverse area in the dense target at saturation provides an intrinsic semi-hard momentum scale, Qs. We predict that the longitudinal distribution of (anti-)baryons and mesons steepens with increasing energy and atomic number of the target, and that the transverse momentum distribution broadens. We show that the evolution of high moments of the longitudinal net baryon distribution with Qs is determined by the anomalous dimension gamma_qq.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the inclusive distribution of leading hadrons in high-energy proton-nucleus collisions can be computed in weak coupling QCD when the target approaches the "black body" limit, because the saturation momentum Qs provides an intrinsic semi-hard scale; they predict that forward hadron production is strongly suppressed and that the longitudinal distribution steepens with increasing energy and atomic number, while the transverse momentum distribution broadens.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive hadron production in the forward region of central pA collisions by convoluting the quark distribution function of the proton (CTEQ5L parameterization) with the quark-nucleus scattering cross section derived from the Color Glass Condensate model and with leading-twist fragmentation functions (KKP parameterizations). The quark-nucleus cross section is obtained from the correlation function of two Wilson lines in the background field of the dense target, which yields a transverse momentum distribution that is flat up to momenta of order Qs. The factorization scale is set to Qs^2, and the calculation includes scattering of quarks and gluons, though gluon contributions are small for z > 0.1.

## Key numbers

- For A ~ 200 central nuclear targets at BNL-RHIC energy, Qs^2 ~ 10 GeV^2 at x ~ 10^-4
- For A ~ 200 central nuclear targets at CERN-LHC energy, Qs^2 ~ 50 GeV^2 at x ~ 10^-6
- At asymptotically large q_t >> Qs, the correlator C(q_t) matches leading-twist perturbation theory: C(q_t) -> Qs^2 / q_t^4
- The inelastic qA cross section equals pi R_A^2 for a perfectly "black disc" target
- The evolution of high moments of the longitudinal net baryon distribution with Qs^2 is given by d/d(log Qs^2) <z^n>_(B-Bbar) = (alpha_s(Qs)/pi) gamma^(0)_qq(n+1) <z^n>_(B-Bbar), with applicability for n >= 5

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and the saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) to compute leading hadron production in pA collisions, contrasting with nonperturbative models based on "gluon junction" and "diquark breaking" mechanisms. The predictions for a "limiting fragmentation curve" that evolves with Qs due to DGLAP scaling violations connect to the experimental observation of limiting fragmentation by the PHOBOS collaboration at RHIC (2002). The results enable experimental tests at RHIC (deuteron-gold collisions) and LHC, where forward neutron calorimeters and detectors like STAR and BRAHMS can measure the predicted suppression and broadening of hadron distributions.
