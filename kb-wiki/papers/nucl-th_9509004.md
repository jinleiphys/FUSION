# Radiative $πρ$ and $πω$ transition form factors in a light-front constituent quark model

> The form factors of the $πρ$ and $πω$ radiative transitions are evaluated within a light-front constituent quark model, using for the first time the eigenfunctions of a light-front mass operator reproducing the meson mass spectrum and including phenomenological Dirac and Pauli quark form factors in the one-body electromagnetic current operator. The sensitivity of the transition form factors both to the meson wave functions and to the constituent quark form factors is illustrated. It is shown that the measurement of the $πρ$ and $πω$ radiative transitions could help in discriminating among various models of the meson structure.

## Key claim

The radiative transition form factors for $\pi\rho$ and $\pi\omega$ are evaluated using light-front constituent quark model wave functions that reproduce the meson mass spectrum, but the authors note that a non-vanishing constituent quark anomalous magnetic moment is required to match experimental decay widths when configuration mixing from the one-gluon-exchange interaction is included, and that the ratio of the two form factors is sensitive to differences in the electromagnetic structure of $u$ and $d$ constituent quarks rather than to the choice of meson wave function.

## Method

The authors construct meson state vectors using the Hamiltonian light-front formalism, with radial wave functions obtained as eigenfunctions of the Godfrey-Isgur effective $q\bar{q}$ Hamiltonian (including one-gluon-exchange and linear-confining terms). They evaluate the transition form factors $F_{\pi\rho}(Q^2)$ and $F_{\pi\omega}(Q^2)$ using a one-body electromagnetic current operator that includes both Dirac and Pauli quark form factors, parametrized as monopole and dipole functions respectively. The calculations are performed for three different radial wave functions: one with only confinement, one with the spin-independent part, and one with the full Godfrey-Isgur interaction.

## Key numbers

- Experimental transition magnetic moments: $\mu_{\pi^+ \rho^+}^{exp} = 0.741 \pm 0.038$ (c/GeV) and $\mu_{\pi^0 \omega}^{exp} = 2.33 \pm 0.06$ (c/GeV)
- Calculated $\mu_{\pi^+ \rho^+}$ values (assuming zero quark anomalous magnetic moments): 0.833, 0.637, and 0.561 (c/GeV) for the confinement-only, spin-independent, and full Godfrey-Isgur wave functions respectively
- Extracted quark anomalous magnetic moments (using the full Godfrey-Isgur wave function): $\kappa_u = 0.133 \pm 0.016$ and $\kappa_d = -0.075 \pm 0.016$
- Extracted charge radius parameter for the Dirac quark form factor: $r_1^V = 0.41$ fm
- Extracted size parameter for the Pauli quark form factor: $r_2^u = r_2^d = 0.52$ fm
- Constituent quark mass adopted: $m_q = 0.220$ GeV
- The $D$-wave component of the $\rho$ ($\omega$) meson is extremely small: $p_D \simeq 0.16\%$
- Pauli quark form factor contributions to the form factors are at most $\sim 30\%$

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model approach previously applied to elastic pion and rho meson form factors by Cardarelli et al. (1994, 1995) to the radiative $\pi\rho$ and $\pi\omega$ transitions. The authors compare their predictions with results from a Bethe-Salpeter approach by Ivanov et al. (1993), QCD sum rule techniques by Belyaev and Ioffe (1994), and a simple Vector Meson Dominance model. The paper shows that unlike the elastic pion form factor, the $\pi\rho$ transition form factor exhibits sizeable model-dependent differences at $Q^2 > 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$, which could be tested by experiments planned at CEBAF. The results also have implications for meson exchange current contributions to deuteron elastic form factors.
