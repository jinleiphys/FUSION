# Realistic and effective interactions in the study of nuclear matter

> The equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter is addressed starting both from a realistic interaction derived from nucleon-nucleon scattering processes and from a low-momentum effective potential. The approach is based on finite temperature Green's functions. The internal energy per particle is estimated from the summation of diagrams and through the Galitskii-Koltun's sum rule.

# Realistic and effective interactions in the study of nuclear matter

## Key claim

The equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter can be studied using both a bare realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction (CD-Bonn) and a low-momentum effective potential ($V_{low-k}$) within a self-consistent finite-temperature Green's functions framework, but the effective potential alone fails to produce a saturation minimum at high densities when a sufficiently low cutoff is used.

## Method

The author employed a self-consistent ladder approximation based on finite-temperature Green's functions, solving the in-medium $T$-matrix equation with dressed single-particle propagators. The internal energy per particle was computed in two ways: directly from the expectation value of the Hamiltonian (summing kinetic and potential contributions) and via the Galitskii-Koltun sum rule. For the CD-Bonn potential the full ladder expansion was used, while for $V_{low-k}$ only diagrams up to second order (Born approximation) were included, as higher orders were expected to be negligible.

## Key numbers

- The empirical saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is taken as $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Calculations with the CD-Bonn potential and with $V_{low-k}$ agree up to $0.8 \, \rho_0$ in the internal energy per particle.
- At densities above $0.8 \, \rho_0$, the $V_{low-k}$ calculations give too attractive energies and exhibit no minimum, while the CD-Bonn calculation shows a saturation point (though not at the experimental value).

## Context

This work extends the use of finite-temperature Green's functions and ladder resummation techniques, previously applied to nuclear matter by Bozek (2002) and others, to compare a bare realistic potential (CD-Bonn) with a renormalized low-momentum potential $V_{low-k}$ introduced by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators. The results show that $V_{low-k}$ alone at low cutoff does not capture all correlations at high density, motivating future inclusion of three-body forces or adjustment of the cutoff to test the reliability of different bare nucleon-nucleon interaction models.
