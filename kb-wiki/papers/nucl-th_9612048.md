# Microscopic Study of Superdeformed Rotational Bands in 151Tb

> Structure of eight superdeformed bands in the nucleus 151Tb is analyzed using the results of the Hartree-Fock and Woods-Saxon cranking approaches. It is demonstrated that far going similarities between the two approaches exist and predictions related to the structure of rotational bands calculated within the two models are nearly parallel. An interpretation scenario for the structure of the superdeformed bands is presented and predictions related to the exit spins are made. Small but systematic discrepancies between experiment and theory, analyzed in terms of the dynamical moments, J(2), are shown to exist. The pairing correlations taken into account by using the particle-number-projection technique are shown to increase the disagreement. Sources of these systematic discrepancies are discussed -- they are most likely related to the yet not optimal parametrization of the nuclear interactions used.

# Microscopic Study of Superdeformed Rotational Bands in 151Tb

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock and Woods-Saxon cranking approaches produce nearly parallel predictions for the structure of superdeformed rotational bands in 151Tb, but small systematic discrepancies with experiment persist in the dynamical moments J(2), and these discrepancies increase when pairing correlations are included via particle-number projection. The authors suggest that the source of these discrepancies is most likely related to non-optimal parametrization of the nuclear interactions used, rather than to the omission of pairing.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme effective interaction with a new numerical code (HFODD) employing a three-dimensional Cartesian deformed harmonic oscillator basis, and compared these to deformed Woods-Saxon cranking calculations using the Strutinsky method for total-Routhian surfaces. Both approaches were applied to eight superdeformed bands in 151Tb, with the dynamical moments J(2) calculated by numerical differentiation of either the average angular momentum or total energy with respect to rotational frequency, and the results compared to experimental gamma-ray data from the EUROGAM spectrometer.

## Key numbers

- The Hartree-Fock basis used M=306 harmonic oscillator states, with frequencies hbar*omega_perp = 11.200 MeV and hbar*omega_parallel = 6.246 MeV, corresponding to deformations beta_2 = 0.61 and beta_4 = 0.10
- Total energy of the yrast configuration at rotational frequency hbar*omega = 0.6 MeV was -1199.322 MeV for M=306 basis and -1201.889 MeV for M=604 basis
- The dynamical moment J(2) for the yrast configuration at hbar*omega = 0.6 MeV changed from 84.71 to 84.84 hbar^2 MeV^{-1} when increasing basis from M=306 to M=604
- The HF calculations achieved total energy accuracy of 0.5 keV after 50 iterations at hbar*omega = 0.6 MeV
- The Woods-Saxon calculations used a deformation mesh with alpha_20 from 0.400 to 0.800 (17 points), alpha_40 from 0.020 to 0.180 (9 points), and gamma from -10 to +10 degrees (5 points), with cranking frequencies from 0.00 to 1.50 MeV (31 points)
- Relative intensities of the eight bands: Band 2 at 50(5)%, Band 3 at 35(5)%, Band 4 at 6(2)%, Band 5 at 10(2)%, Band 6 at 9(2)%, Band 7 at 11(2)%, Band 8 at 7(3)% relative to the yrast band

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of superdeformed nuclei in the A~150 region that used Nilsson or Woods-Saxon cranking approaches without pairing (Braun 1988) or with dynamic pairing correlations (YRS 1987, JDH 1988, Nazarewicz 1989), as well as more recent Woods-Saxon (SWM 1994) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (PBF 1996) studies. The paper is distinguished by simultaneously analyzing all eight known superdeformed bands in a single nucleus (151Tb) using both self-consistent (HF) and non-self-consistent (WS) approaches, and by demonstrating that the systematic discrepancies between theory and experiment cannot be attributed to pairing correlations, pointing instead to the need for modified nuclear interactions. The results enable future investigations of band crossing frequencies and alignments in this nucleus.
