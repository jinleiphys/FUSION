# Heavy vector and axial-vector $D$ mesons in hot magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter

> We observed the impact of finite magnetic field on the in-medium mass and decay constant of isospin averaged vector $D^*(D^{*^+},D^{*^0})$ and axial-vector $D_1(D^+_1, D^0_1)$ mesons. The quark and gluon condensates of the nuclear medium at finite magnetic field, temperature, isospin asymmetry, and density have been obtained by the meson exchange scalar fields within the chiral SU(3) model. The medium attributes modify the scalar and vector density of nuclear medium and this variation reflects in the in-medium mass and decay constant of spin 1 $D$ mesons. We calculate these observables by comparing the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) and the phenomenological side in the QCD Sum Rules. In the results, we observed a positive mass shift for charged vector and axial-vector $D$ mesons with respect to magnetic field. For neutral vector (axial-vector) $D$ mesons we observed negative (positive) mass shift as a function of magnetic field. In the application part, we calculate the in-medium partial decay width of the process $D^*_s$(2715/2860) $\rightarrow$ $D^* K$ by using $^3P_0$ model. The in-medium effects are incorporated through the in-medium masses of $D^*$ and $K$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in hot magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter, charged vector and axial-vector D mesons experience a positive mass shift with increasing magnetic field, while neutral vector D mesons show a negative mass shift and neutral axial-vector D mesons show a positive mass shift; these results are conditional on the combined application of the chiral SU(3) model and QCD sum rules, and the specific values depend on the chosen nuclear density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry parameters.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled equations of motion of the chiral SU(3) model in mean-field approximation to obtain the magnetic-field-induced light quark condensates and gluon condensates in hot, dense, asymmetric nuclear matter. These condensates are then used as input in Borel-transformed QCD sum rules to calculate the in-medium mass and decay constant of isospin-averaged vector D* and axial-vector D1 mesons. As an application, the in-medium partial decay width of the process D*_s(2715/2860) → D* K is computed using the 3P0 model, where the in-medium masses of D* and K mesons (the latter also from the chiral SU(3) model) are used.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength considered is eB ∼ 2–15 m_π^2, where 1 m_π^2 = 2.818 × 10^18 gauss.
* The parameter d in the gluon condensate expression is taken as 0.1818 from the QCD beta function at one-loop level.
* The parameters d1 and d2 in the kaon interaction Lagrangian are taken as 2.56/m_K and 0.73/m_K respectively, fitted to kaon-nucleon scattering length data.
* The excited D*_s mesons studied, D*_s(2860) and D*_s(2715), have vacuum decay widths approximately 48 MeV and 115 MeV respectively, as reported by the Babar and Belle collaborations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of D meson properties in nuclear matter (Kumar 2015, Chhabra 2017, Chhabra 2018) by adding the effect of a finite magnetic field, which had not been applied to vector D* and axial-vector D1 mesons before. It builds on the combined chiral SU(3) model and QCD sum rules approach used for pseudoscalar and scalar D mesons (Kumar 2020) and on magnetic field studies of charmonia and bottomonia (Kumar 2019, Kumar 2019a). The results enable future calculations of in-medium decay widths of excited charmonium states and help identify the spectroscopic states of D*_s mesons by comparing theoretical decay widths with experimental data from facilities such as CBM, PANDA, and NICA.
