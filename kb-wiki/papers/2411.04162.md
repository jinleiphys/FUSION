# Parametrization of GPDs from t-channel string exchange in AdS spaces

> We introduce a string-based parametrization for nucleon quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that is valid for all skewness. Our approach leverages conformal moments, representing them as the sum of spin-j nucleon A-form factor and skewness-dependent spin-j nucleon D-form factor, derived from t-channel string exchange in AdS spaces consistent with Lorentz invariance and unitarity. This model-independent framework, satisfying the polynomiality condition due to Lorentz invariance, uses Mellin moments from empirical data to estimate these form factors. With just five Regge slope parameters, our method accurately produces various nucleon quark GPD types and symmetric nucleon gluon GPDs through pertinent Mellin-Barnes integrals. Our isovector nucleon quark GPD is in agreement with existing lattice data, promising to improve the empirical extraction and global analysis of nucleon GPDs in exclusive processes, by avoiding the deconvolution problem at any skewness, for the first time.

# Parametrization of GPDs from t-channel string exchange in AdS spaces

## Key claim

The authors introduce a string-based parametrization for nucleon quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that is valid for all skewness, using conformal moments represented as the sum of spin-j nucleon A-form factor and skewness-dependent spin-j nucleon D-form factor derived from t-channel string exchange in AdS spaces. The framework claims to avoid the deconvolution problem in GPD extraction from exclusive processes for the first time, but this is contingent on the model-independent, universal nature of the string-based parametrization satisfying the polynomiality condition at any skewness.

## Method

The conformal moments of non-singlet (valence) and singlet (sea) quark GPDs, and gluon GPDs, are parametrized at a low resolution scale (mu_0 = 1 GeV) using empirical parton distribution functions (PDFs) from the MSTW 2008 leading-order set, combined with five Regge slope parameters fixed by experimental electromagnetic and gravitational form factors of the nucleon. The moments are expressed as integrals over PDFs with Reggeized t-dependence, plus skewness-dependent D-form factors constructed from a universal hypergeometric coefficient function that enforces the polynomiality condition. The full GPDs in x-space are then obtained via Mellin-Barnes integrals (Sommerfeld-Watson transformations) of the conformal moments, which are evolved to mu = 2 GeV for comparison with lattice QCD data.

## Key numbers

- Five Regge slope parameters are used: alpha'_T = 0.627 GeV^{-2}, alpha'_S = 4.277 GeV^{-2}, alpha'_(+) = alpha'_(-) = alpha'_(u+d) = 0.891 GeV^{-2}, alpha'_(u-d) = 1.069 GeV^{-2}, and alpha'_(+),s = 1.828 GeV^{-2}
- The low resolution scale is mu_0 = 1 GeV, with results evolved and compared at mu = 2 GeV
- The isovector quark GPD H^{(-)}_{u-d} is compared to lattice data at multiple kinematic points including eta = 0, -t = 0; eta = 1/3, -t = 0.69 GeV^2; eta = 0.1, -t = 0.23 GeV^2; and eta = 0, -t = 0.39 GeV^2
- The spin-2 A-form factor of the gluonic gravitational form factor of the proton is A_g(t; mu_0) = A_g(2, t; mu_0), and the D-form factor is eta^2 D_g(t; mu_0) = D_{g eta}(2, eta, t; mu_0)
- The valence combination F_{u+d}^{(-)}(1, t; mu_0) = 3(F_{1p}(t) + F_{1n}(t)) and isovector combination F_{u-d}^{(-)}(1, t; mu_0) = F_{1p}(t) - F_{1n}(t) are fixed using experimental Dirac electromagnetic form factors from Mamo (2021)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic approach to nucleon GPDs in the ERBL regime at low-x (Mamo and Zahed, 2022) to the full DGLAP regime at high-x, using a string-based parametrization that enforces Lorentz invariance, unitarity, and the polynomiality condition. The framework is contrasted with direct x-space parametrizations using double distributions (Radyushkin, 1998) which suffer from a deconvolution problem in extracting GPDs from Compton form factors (Guidal, 2013; Kumericki, 2016; Bertone, 2021; Moffat, 2023). The results for isovector quark GPDs agree with lattice QCD data from Alexandrou (2020), Holligan (2023), and Lin (2020), and the gluon GPD parametrization builds on the successful extraction of the gluonic spin-2 moment by the J/Psi-007 collaboration at JLab (Duran, 2022) using the authors' holographic prediction. The approach promises to improve global data analysis of GPDs at JLab and the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider by providing a model-independent parametrization that bypasses the deconvolution problem.
