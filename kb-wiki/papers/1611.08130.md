# Influence of broken flavor and C and P symmetry on the quark propagator

> Embedding QCD into the standard model breaks various symmetries of QCD explicitly, especially C and P. While these effects are usually perturbatively small, they can be amplified in extreme environments like merging neutron stars or by the interplay with new physics. To correctly treat these cases requires fully backcoupled calculations. To pave the way for later investigations of hadronic physics, we study the QCD quark propagator coupled to an explicit breaking. This substantially increases the tensor structure even for this simplest correlation function. To cope with the symmetry structure, and covering all possible quark masses, from the top quark mass to the chiral limit, we employ Dyson-Schwinger equations. While at weak breaking the qualitative effects have similar trends as in perturbation theory, even moderately strong breakings lead to qualitatively different effects, non-linearly amplified by the strong interactions.

# Influence of broken flavor and C and P symmetry on the quark propagator

## Key claim

The authors show that while weak explicit C and P symmetry breaking in QCD produces qualitatively similar effects to perturbation theory, even moderately strong breaking leads to qualitatively different effects that are non-linearly amplified by strong interactions, particularly for the axial channel dressing functions of the quark propagator.

## Method

The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in the rainbow-ladder truncation with a Maris-Tandy effective coupling to study the quark propagator coupled to an explicit symmetry-breaking term that mimics weak interactions. The breaking is implemented via an effective Lagrangian term that breaks C, P, and flavor within generations, and the quark propagator is solved for all possible quark masses from the chiral limit to the top quark mass, with the weak coupling strength varied from small values up to approximately 0.4.

## Key numbers

- The Maris-Tandy coupling parameters used are: Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV, m_t = 1.0 GeV, ω = 0.4 GeV, and D = 0.93 GeV
- Bare quark masses used: m_up = 2.3 MeV, m_down = 4.8 MeV, m_strange = 95 MeV, m_charm = 1.275 GeV, m_bottom = 4.18 GeV, m_top = 160 GeV, all at renormalization point μ = 10^6 GeV
- The tree-level denominator N(p²) = (1-4g_w²)(p²+M_l²)(p²+M_h²) shows that M_l and M_h diverge at g_w = 0.5, limiting solutions to g_w ≲ 0.4
- For g_w ≤ 0.1, the infrared scalar channel of the mixed propagator is approximately proportional to g_w
- The mass function M(p²) starts to change appreciably for g_w ≳ 0.01 in the chiral limit
- For g_w ≲ 0.1, the absolute value |r̃| in the UV and IR increases by two orders of magnitude when g_w is increased by one order of magnitude
- The transition scale where the axial channel changes sign occurs at approximately 1 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the study of quark propagators in QCD using Dyson-Schwinger equations, which have been successfully applied by Alkofer (2000), Fischer (2006), and Roberts (2015) to determine the quark propagator in various levels of sophistication. The study is motivated by neutron star mergers where weak interactions become prevalent enough to require fully backcoupled non-perturbative calculations, as discussed by Rosswog (2003), Sekiguchi (2011), and Faber (2012). The results enable future investigations of hadronic physics with explicit C and P symmetry breaking, though the authors note that only future studies of observable quantities will reveal the physical implications.
