# Centrality, rapidity, and transverse-momentum dependence of gluon shadowing and antishadowing on $J/ψ$ production in $d$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV

> We have carried out a wide study of shadowing and antishadowing effects on \jpsi\ production in \dAu\ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. We have studied the effects of three different gluon nPDF sets, using the exact kinematics for a $2\to 2$ process, namely $g+g\to J/ψ+g$ as expected from LO pQCD. We have computed the rapidity dependence of \RCP\ and $R_{d\rm Au}$ for the different centrality classes of the PHENIX data. For mid rapidities, we have also computed the transverse-momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor, which cannot be predicted with the usual $2\to 1$ simplified kinematics. All these observables have been compared to the PHENIX data in \dAu\ collisions.

## Key claim

Using a $2\to 2$ partonic process ($g+g\to J/\psi+g$) rather than the usual $2\to 1$ simplified kinematics, the authors find that the EPS08 gluon nuclear PDF with a spatially-dependent shadowing proportional to local nuclear density reproduces the most forward $R_{\rm CP}$ data fairly well, but the precision of the data does not allow distinguishing between different shadowing parametrisations.

## Method

The authors implemented a probabilistic Glauber Monte-Carlo framework (JIN) that encodes the $2\to 2$ partonic mechanism $g+g\to J/\psi+g$ at leading order in the Colour-Singlet Model, using the exact kinematics to compute the allowed $(x_1,x_2)$ pairs weighted by the partonic differential cross section times gluon PDFs. They applied three different gluon nuclear PDF sets (EKS98, EPS08, nDSg) with a spatial dependence proportional to the local nuclear density, and considered four values of the effective absorption cross section ($\sigma_{\rm abs}=0, 2.8, 4.2, 6$ mb) to account for nuclear break-up of the $c\bar{c}$ pair.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV taken as $\sigma_{NN}=42$ mb
- Maximum nucleon density taken as $\rho_0=0.17$ nucleons/fm$^3$
- Effective absorption cross sections considered: $\sigma_{\rm abs}=0, 2.8, 4.2, 6$ mb
- Best match to $R_{d\rm Au}$ data suggests $\sigma_{\rm abs}\approx 3-4$ mb from comparison with $R_{d\rm Au}$ and $\sigma_{\rm abs}\approx 2-3$ mb from comparison with $R_{\rm CP}$
- Global systematic uncertainty on PHENIX data: $\pm 10\%$ for peripheral data, $\pm 8.5\%$ for central data, $\pm 8.2\%$ for $R_{\rm CP}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ferreiro et al. (2008, 2009) that used a $2\to 1$ partonic mechanism, by implementing the more realistic $2\to 2$ kinematics which allows predicting the transverse-momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor. The results are compared to the latest PHENIX data from Adare et al. (2010) and show that the EPS08 gluon nPDF set (Eskola et al., 2008) with spatially-dependent shadowing combined with a $2\to 2$ process reproduces the most forward $R_{\rm CP}$ data, though the difference between the $\sigma_{\rm abs}$ values extracted from $R_{d\rm Au}$ versus $R_{\rm CP}$ may have physical meaning but uncertainties preclude strong conclusions. This enables future comparisons with forthcoming data at backward and forward rapidities, and provides a framework for predictions at LHC energies.
