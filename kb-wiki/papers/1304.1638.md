# Alternative Scenarios of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions: II. Particle Production

> Particle production in relativistic collisions of heavy nuclei is analyzed in a wide range of incident energies 2.7 GeV $\le \sqrt{s_{NN}}\le$ 62.4 GeV. The analysis is performed within the three-fluid model employing three different equations of state (EoS): a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with the first-order phase transition and that with a smooth crossover transition. It is found that the hadronic scenario fails to reproduce experimental yields of antibaryons (strange and nonstrange), starting already from lower SPS energies, i.e. $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>$ 5 GeV. Moreover, at energies above the top SPS one, i.e. $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>$ 17.4 GeV, the mid-rapidity densities predicted by the hadronic scenario considerably exceed the available RHIC data on all species. At the same time the deconfinement-transition scenarios reasonably agree (to a various extent) with all the data. The present analysis demonstrates certain advantage of the deconfinement-transition EoS's. However, all scenarios fail to reproduce the strangeness enhancement in the incident energy range near 30A GeV (i.e. a horn anomaly in the $K^+/π^+$ ratio) and yields of $ϕ$-mesons at 20A--40A GeV.

## Key claim

The three-fluid model with deconfinement-transition equations of state (first-order phase transition and smooth crossover) shows certain advantage over a purely hadronic equation of state in reproducing particle production data across a wide energy range, but all scenarios fail to reproduce the horn anomaly in the K+/π+ ratio near 30A GeV and φ-meson yields at 20A–40A GeV.

## Method

The analysis was performed within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats projectile, target, and fireball fluids with finite stopping power, using three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with first-order phase transition, and an EoS with smooth crossover transition. Particle rapidity distributions and mid-rapidity densities were calculated for central Au+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at fixed impact parameters (b=2 fm for Au+Au, b=2.4 fm for Pb+Pb) across incident energies from 2.7 GeV to 62.4 GeV in center-of-mass energy. Freeze-out was performed using a modified Milekhin procedure with a freeze-out energy density of 0.4 fm/fm³, and strangeness suppression factors γ_S were applied to strange particle yields where needed.

## Key numbers

- Energy range analyzed: 2.7 GeV ≤ √s_NN ≤ 62.4 GeV
- Hadronic scenario fails to reproduce experimental antibaryon yields starting from √s_NN > 5 GeV (lower SPS energies)
- At √s_NN > 17.4 GeV (above top SPS energy), hadronic scenario mid-rapidity densities considerably exceed available RHIC data on all species
- Pion rapidity distributions from all three EoS's agree with experimental data within 10% at SPS energies
- Freeze-out energy density ε_frz = 0.4 fm/fm³ was chosen for best reproduction of secondary particle yields
- Strangeness suppression factor γ_S is approximately unity (no additional suppression needed) at √s_NN > 5 GeV
- At √s_NN ≥ 17 GeV, the hadronic scenario again requires additional strangeness suppression, though this is physically unjustified at high energies

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid dynamics simulations reported in Ivanov (2012) and the first paper of this series on baryon stopping (3FD-2012-stopping), applying them to particle production across the energy range of the RHIC beam-energy scan, SPS low-energy scan, and future FAIR and NICA facilities. The analysis contradicts the purely hadronic EoS scenario at energies above √s_NN ≈ 5 GeV, while supporting deconfinement-transition scenarios, though it fails to resolve the horn anomaly in K+/π+ ratios previously identified by Gazdzicki (1998) and Alt (2007) that remains unexplained by any dynamical model including Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (Bratkovskaya, 2009) and UrQMD (Bass, 1998). The results enable further investigation of freeze-out conditions and isotopic effects that might resolve remaining discrepancies in φ-meson yields and strangeness enhancement.
