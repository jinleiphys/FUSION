# Skyrmions at Finite Density

> In this paper, we will describe recent advances in analytical methods to construct exact solutions of the Skyrme model (and its generalizations) representing inhomogeneous Hadronic condensates living at finite Baryon density. Such novel analytical tools are based on the idea to generalize the well known spherical hedgehog ansatz to situations (relevant for the analysis of finite density effects) in which there is no spherical symmetry anymore. Besides the intrinsic mathematical interest to find exact solutions with non-vanishing Baryonic charge confined to a finite volume, this framework opens the possibility to compute important physical quantities which would be difficult to compute otherwise.

## Key claim

This paper reviews recent analytical methods for constructing exact solutions of the Skyrme model (and its generalizations) that represent inhomogeneous hadronic condensates at finite baryon density, achieved by generalizing the spherical hedgehog ansatz to situations without spherical symmetry, which the abstract presents as a mathematical advance but the full text clarifies is enabled precisely by the *absence* of spherical symmetry.

## Method

The authors use the Euler angle and exponential parametrizations of the SU(2)-valued Skyrme field to construct ansatze that reduce the full set of coupled nonlinear Skyrme field equations to simpler equations, such as the Sine-Gordon equation or linear equations. They impose boundary conditions corresponding to confinement in a flat box of finite spatial volume with metric \(ds^{2}=-dt^{2}+L_{r}^{2}dr^{2}+L_{\theta}^{2}d\theta^{2}+L_{\phi}^{2}d\phi^{2}\), where the adimensional coordinates have ranges \(0\leq r\leq 2\pi\), \(0\leq \theta \leq \pi\), and \(0\leq \phi \leq 2\pi\). The ansatze incorporate light-like functions of the coordinate \(u = t/L_{\phi} - \phi\) to represent chiral degrees of freedom that modulate the hadronic layers and tubes.

## Key numbers

* The volume available for solitons in the box metric is \(V = 4\pi^{3} L_{r} L_{\theta} L_{\phi}\), where \(L_{i}\) are constants representing the box side lengths.
* The topological charge (baryon number) for the hadronic layer solution is \(B = pq\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are integers determined by boundary conditions on the profile \(H(r)\) and the light-like function \(G(u)\).
* The profile function for the hadronic layer satisfying boundary conditions \(H(0)=0\) and \(H(2\pi)=\pi/2\) is \(H(r) = r/4\).
* The Sine-Gordon layer solution reduces the Skyrme field equations to \(\Box H - (\lambda / (8 L^{2} (\lambda + 2L^{2}))) \sin(4H) = 0\), with topological charge density \(\rho_{B} = 3 \sin(2H) dH d\gamma d\phi\) and boundary conditions \(H(t,0)=0\), \(H(t,2\pi)=\pm \pi/2\) giving \(B = \pm 1\).

## Context

This work extends the classic variational and rational map approximation approaches to Skyrmions at finite density from references such as SkCr2-SkCr5 and WITTEN1981513, and the numerical studies of nuclear pasta phases (pasta1-pasta10). It provides the first analytic constructions of inhomogeneous baryonic condensates (hadronic layers and tubes) in the low-energy limit of QCD, enabling explicit computation of transport coefficients (thermal conductivity, viscosity) and shear moduli, with the shear modulus of lasagna configurations computed in gaugsksu(n) showing good agreement with pasta5 and pasta9. The approach also reveals emergent chiral conformal degrees of freedom living on the hadronic layers, which can be interpreted as chiral fields propagating within a baryonic medium.
