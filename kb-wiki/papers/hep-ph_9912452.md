# Anomalous gamma -> 3 pi amplitude in a bound-state approach

> The form factor for the anomalous process gamma pi^+ -> pi^+ pi^0, which is presently being measured at CEBAF, is calculated in the Schwinger-Dyson approach in conjunction with an impulse approximation. The form factors obtained by us are compared with the ones predicted by the simple constituent quark loop model, vector meson dominance and chiral perturbation theory, as well as the scarce already available data.

## Key claim

The Schwinger-Dyson (SD) approach, when extended beyond the soft and chiral limits via an expansion in pion momenta, reproduces the anomalous $\gamma \to 3\pi$ amplitude at the soft point exactly, but the momentum-dependent form factor away from that point depends on the internal structure of the pion and yields predictions that are lower than those of vector meson dominance (VMD) and chiral perturbation theory ($\chi$PT) in the experimentally accessible $s$-interval.

## Method

The authors computed the $\gamma \pi^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^0$ form factor using the SD approach with a generalized impulse approximation (GIA), employing the Ball–Chiu vertex to preserve the Ward–Takahashi identity and the Alkofer–Roberts (AR) quark propagator *Ansätze* with parameters fixed by fitting pion observables. They expanded the loop integrals around the soft limit $p_i=0$ to order $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$, summed all six contributing box diagrams to ensure symmetry under pion-momentum interchange, and expressed the normalized form factor in terms of Mandelstam variables for on-shell and off-shell kinematics relevant to the CEBAF experiment.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic mass scale of the model is $\Lambda = \sqrt{2D} = 565.69\ \mathrm{MeV}$, with $D = 0.16\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$.
- For massive quarks ($\bar{m}=0.00897$), the normalized form factor at the soft point is $0.96274$, slightly less than 1 due to explicit chiral symmetry breaking.
- For on-shell pions with $m_\pi = 138.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$ and the third pion on shell ($t=m_\pi^2$), the form factor is $\widetilde{F}^{3\pi}_\gamma(s,t') = 1.0319 - 0.00065( \bar{s} + \bar{t'}) + 0.00022 ( \bar{s}^2 + \bar{s}\bar{t'} + \bar{t'}^2)$.
- For the CEBAF kinematics with the third pion off shell ($t = -m_\pi^2$), the form factor is $\widetilde{F}^{3\pi}_\gamma(s,t') = 0.98524 - 0.00015 \bar{s} - 0.00022 \bar{t'} + 0.00022 ( \bar{s}^2 + \bar{s}\bar{t'} + \bar{t'}^2)$.
- In the chiral limit with on-shell pions, the form factor is $\widetilde{F}^{3\pi}_\gamma(s,t')_0 = 1 + 0.000190 (\bar{s}^2 + \bar{s}\bar{t'} + \bar{t'}^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier SD approach of Alkofer and Roberts (1996), who reproduced the anomalous $\gamma \to 3\pi$ amplitude only at the soft point, by computing the form factor away from that limit using an expansion method similar to that in the authors' prior free quark loop calculation (Bistrovic and Klabucar, 1999). The results are compared with predictions from the constituent quark loop model, VMD (Rudaz, 1984), and $\chi$PT (Bijnens, 1990; Holstein, 1996), and are directly relevant to the ongoing CEBAF measurement (Miskimen et al., 1994) and planned Primakoff experiments at CERN (Moinester et al., 1999). The paper enables experimental discrimination between competing theoretical models and suggests that if the measured form factor is higher than the SD prediction, the SD modeling should be refitted with a smaller mass scale.
