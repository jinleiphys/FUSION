# Convergence of density-matrix expansions for nuclear interactions

> We extend density-matrix expansions in nuclei to higher orders in derivatives of densities and test their convergence properties. The expansions allow for converting the interaction energies characteristic to finite- and short-range nuclear effective forces into quasi-local density functionals. We also propose a new type of expansion that has excellent convergence properties when benchmarked against the binding energies obtained for the Gogny interaction.

# Convergence of density-matrix expansions for nuclear interactions

## Key claim

The authors extend density-matrix expansions (DME) to sixth order in derivatives of densities and show that a new "damped Taylor" (DT) expansion converges rapidly for the exchange energy, achieving total RMS deviations of 1.54 MeV at fourth order and 0.36 MeV at sixth order when benchmarked against exact Gogny D1S interaction energies, but only for the nine doubly magic nuclei tested and with the caveat that precision deteriorates slightly in heavier isospin-unsaturated nuclei.

## Method

The authors start from the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction and expand the nonlocal one-body density matrix in spherical-tensor derivative operators around the average coordinate, converting the direct and exchange interaction energies into quasi-local density functionals. For the exchange term, they propose a new damped Taylor (DT) expansion that combines a model density (symmetric nuclear matter nonlocal dependence) with a Gaussian damping factor of width a = 4/k_F, and they evaluate the expansion up to sixth order using realistic densities generated from self-consistent SLy4 calculations for nine doubly magic nuclei. The exact energies are computed with the HFODD code for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Direct interaction energy in 208Pb for the scalar-isoscalar channel: -12294.769 MeV (exact, Gogny D1S)
- Exchange interaction energy in 208Pb for the scalar-isoscalar channel: -594.992 MeV (exact, Gogny D1S)
- Exchange interaction energy in 208Pb for the vector-isoscalar channel: 0.629 MeV (exact, Gogny D1S)
- Exchange interaction energy in 208Pb for the scalar-isovector channel: -32.337 MeV (exact, Gogny D1S)
- Exchange interaction energy in 208Pb for the vector-isovector channel: 0.215 MeV (exact, Gogny D1S)
- Total RMS deviation for DT exchange energy at fourth order: 1.54 MeV (over nine nuclei)
- Total RMS deviation for DT exchange energy at sixth order: 0.36 MeV (over nine nuclei)
- Total RMS deviation for PSA exchange energy at second order: 14.09 MeV (over nine nuclei)
- Damping width estimate: a = 4/k_F, with k_F^0 = 1.35 fm^{-1} for equilibrium symmetric nuclear matter
- Precision of sixth-order direct expansion: 0.1-0.01% or 0.1 MeV in light nuclei
- RMS deviation of best tuned Gogny forces for experimental masses: 0.798 MeV (Gor09a)

## Context

This work extends the original Negele-Vautherin (NV) density-matrix expansion (1972) and the more recent phase-space averaging (PSA) method of Geb09 to higher orders (beyond second order) for the first time, testing convergence properties systematically. The authors compare their new DT expansion against the NV expansion and the PSA method (INM-DME version), finding that the NV expansion works well in scalar channels but fails to converge in vector channels beyond second order, while the DT expansion converges in all channels. The results provide a baseline for constructing quasi-local higher-order nuclear density functionals from first-principles effective forces, building on the systematic higher-order derivative approach proposed by Carlsson (2008) and the ab initio nuclear DFT program of Geb09 and Dru10.
