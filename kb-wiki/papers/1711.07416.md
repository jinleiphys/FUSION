# Quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In this proceedings contribution, we will review the basics of quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics. Then, we will present some recent phenomenological results of 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics and Pb-Pb $2.76$ TeV collisions from the ALICE collaboration. We show that 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics model is able to describe the experimental results for Pb-Pb collisions at $2.76$ TeV quite well for many observables such as the spectra, the elliptic flow, and HBT radii in many centrality classes.

## Key claim

The 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model can describe ALICE Pb-Pb collision data at 2.76 TeV for many observables, but only with a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.159 and smooth Glauber initial conditions, and only up to transverse momentum of about 1.5 GeV for elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors derived dynamical equations by taking moments (zeroth, first, and second) of the Boltzmann equation for massive quasiparticles with a temperature-dependent mass, using an anisotropic distribution function with three anisotropy parameters and a scale parameter. The quasiparticle mass was determined from lattice QCD equation of state via a thermodynamic identity, and a background field was added to ensure thermodynamic consistency. The resulting 3+1d equations were solved with smooth Glauber initial conditions and isotropic initial anisotropy parameters, using THERMINATOR 2 for hadronic freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature at proper time 0.25 fm/c: T0 = 600 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s = 0.159 (constant)
- Freeze-out temperature: T_FO = 130 MeV
- Good agreement with identified elliptic flow data up to p_T ~ 1.5 GeV
- Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV

## Context

This work extends anisotropic hydrodynamics from conformal to non-conformal systems by introducing a temperature-dependent quasiparticle mass, building on prior developments by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010, 2012), and the authors' own earlier work (Alqahtani 2015, 2016, 2017). The model provides a framework for describing bulk viscosity from kinetic theory and enables systematic comparison with LHC heavy-ion data across multiple observables (spectra, elliptic flow, HBT radii) using only a few fit parameters.
