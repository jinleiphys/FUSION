# Suppression of high-pT particle production in AA collisions: the role of in-medium color-flow

> The suppression of high-pT single-hadron spectra in heavy-ion collisions is usually interpreted as due to parton energy-loss of high-momentum quarks and gluons propagating in the plasma. Here, we discuss to what extent this partonic picture must be complemented by a picture of medium-modified hadronization. In particular, we show how color-exchange with the medium modifies the properties of color singlet-clusters arising from the parton branchings, producing a softening of the hadron spectra.

## Key claim

The standard picture of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, which attributes high-pT hadron suppression solely to parton energy loss followed by vacuum fragmentation, must be complemented by medium-modified hadronization effects: color exchange between the hard parton and the quark-gluon plasma creates color-singlet clusters with different kinematic properties than those in vacuum, producing additional softening of hadron spectra beyond what energy-loss models predict.

## Method

The authors analyze medium-induced gluon radiation at first order in opacity (single scattering with the medium) using the large-Nc limit to track color flow, decomposing the radiation spectrum into two independent color channels (aa₁ and a₁a). They compare the invariant mass and momentum of the resulting color-singlet clusters in each channel, using the HERWIG cluster-hadronization model as an illustration, and examine both the totally incoherent regime (formation times much shorter than medium length) and the totally coherent regime (formation times much longer than medium length).

## Key numbers

- In the aa₁ channel, the cluster C₁ has invariant mass M² ∼ k²/2x (for x ≪ 1), matching vacuum kinematics; in the a₁a channel, the cluster C₂ has invariant mass M² ∼ ET, so for large parton energy M_C₂ ≫ M_C₁
- The HERWIG cluster-hadronization model uses a critical mass M_c = 4 GeV to separate light clusters (two-body decay) from heavy clusters (fission into sub-clusters), with Q₀ = 0.6 GeV as a parameter for the momentum of leading sub-clusters
- In the totally coherent regime, the induced radiation spectrum dσ_ind = 0 exactly, and the a₁a channel contribution vanishes (dσ_{a₁a}^{ind} = 0)
- In the totally incoherent regime, more than 50% of the induced radiation spectrum comes from the a₁a channel, which produces softer hadrons

## Context

This work extends the standard energy-loss framework for jet quenching established at RHIC and the LHC, where suppression of high-pT hadrons is typically modeled via Eq. (1) using vacuum fragmentation functions. The authors show that the factorization assumption of Eq. (1) is insufficient because color exchange with the medium creates clusters with larger invariant mass (M² ∼ ET) than vacuum clusters, leading to softer hadron spectra. This enables future work to incorporate medium-modified hadronization into phenomenological calculations of the nuclear modification factor R_AA, as referenced in the companion paper Beraudo, Milhano, and Wiedemann (2011).
