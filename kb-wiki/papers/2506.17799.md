# Relations between spin observables of the reactions $dd\to npd$ and $pd\to pd$ in impulse approximation

> It is shown that the vector and tensor analyzing powers $A_y$, $A_{yy}$ and also double spin correlation coefficients $C_{y,y}$, $C_{y y,y}$ of the reaction $dd\to n+p+d$ in impulse approximation are linearly connected to corresponding observables of the $pd$- elastic scattering. Obtained relations are necessary for motivation of the polarization experiments for the first phase of the SPD NICA project and for analysis of expected data.

# Relations between spin observables of the reactions $dd\to npd$ and $pd\to pd$ in impulse approximation

## Key claim

The vector analyzing power $A_y$, tensor analyzing power $A_{yy}$, and double spin correlation coefficients $C_{y,y}$ and $C_{yy,y}$ of the reaction $dd\to n+p+d$ are linearly connected to corresponding observables of $pd$ elastic scattering, but only within the impulse approximation (pole mechanism) and the S-wave approximation for the deuteron wave function, which restricts validity to small internal relative momenta $q<0.15$ GeV/c between nucleons in the deuteron.

## Method

The author assumes the pole mechanism (impulse approximation) for the reaction $dd\to npd$, where the transition amplitude factorizes into the amplitude of virtual deuteron decay $d_1\to np$ and the $pd$ elastic scattering amplitude $M(p d_2\to p'd')$, with only the S-wave component of the deuteron wave function $u(q)$ retained. Using this factorized amplitude, the author derives expressions for the differential cross sections with definite spin projections of the initial deuterons, then constructs the vector and tensor analyzing powers and double spin correlation coefficients for the $dd\to npd$ reaction and compares them to the analogous observables in $pd\to pd$ scattering.

## Key numbers

- The vector analyzing power for the polarized deuteron $d_2$ in $dd\to npd$ equals the vector analyzing power of $pd\to pd$: $A_y^{d_2}(d_1 d^\uparrow_2\to npd) = A_y^d(p d^\uparrow\to pd)$.
- The vector analyzing power for the polarized deuteron $d_1$ in $dd\to npd$ is related to the vector analyzing power of $pd\to pd$ with respect to the polarized proton by a factor of $2/3$: $A_y^{d_1}(d^\uparrow_1 d_2\to npd) = \frac{2}{3} A_y^p(p^\uparrow d\to pd)$.
- The tensor analyzing power for the polarized deuteron $d_2$ in $dd\to npd$ equals the tensor analyzing power of $pd\to pd$: $A_{yy}(d_1 d^\uparrow_2\to npd) = A_{yy}(p d^\uparrow\to pd)$.
- The tensor analyzing power for the polarized deuteron $d_1$ is zero in the S-wave approximation for the deuteron.
- The double spin vector correlation coefficient satisfies $C_{y,y}(dd\to npd) = \frac{2}{3} C_{y,y}(pd\to pd)$.
- The double tensor-vector correlation coefficient satisfies $C_{yy,y}(dd\to npd) = C_{yy,y}(pd\to pd)$.
- The approximations are expected to be valid at small internal relative momenta $q<0.15$ GeV/c; at momenta $q>0.2$ GeV/c the D-wave contribution and rescattering effects become significant.

## Context

This work extends the impulse approximation formalism previously applied to the $dd\to npnp$ reaction by Uzikov (2023) to the $dd\to npd$ reaction, establishing linear relations between spin observables of $dd\to npd$ and $pd$ elastic scattering. The results are motivated by the need to motivate polarization experiments for the first phase of the SPD NICA project at center-of-mass energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5-20$ GeV, where data on spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon amplitudes are incomplete and existing phenomenological parametrizations by Sibirtsev (2010), Ford (2012), and Selyugin (2024) require verification. The spin-dependent Glauber theory developed by Platonova (2010, 2019) and Temerbayev (2015) describes $pd$ elastic scattering observables well using known $pp$ and $pn$ spin amplitudes, and the derived relations enable using $dd\to npd$ reaction data as a test of these spin amplitudes at NICA energies.
