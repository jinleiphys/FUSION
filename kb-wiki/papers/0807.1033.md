# Isovector energy-weighted sums for hot nuclei in presence of relaxation processes

> We investigate the collective response function and the energy-weighted sums m_k for isovector mode in hot nuclei. The approach is based on the collisional kinetic theory and takes into consideration the temperature and the relaxation effects. Taking into account a connection between the isovector sound mode and the corresponding surface vibrations we have established the A-dependence of the enhancement factor for the isovector sum rule in a good agreement with experimental data. We have shown that the enhancement factor for the "model independent" sum m_1 is only slightly sensitive to the temperature change.

## Key claim

The enhancement factor for the "model independent" isovector energy-weighted sum \(m_1\) is only slightly sensitive to temperature changes, remaining practically constant in the interval \(T = 0 \div 5\) MeV, even when relaxation processes are included.

## Method

The authors used the linearized collisional Landau-Vlasov equation with a relaxation-time approximation to derive the density-density response function for isovector excitations in hot nuclear matter. They then applied a macroscopic boundary condition representing force balance on the free nuclear surface to connect the infinite-matter response to finite nuclei, and evaluated the energy-weighted sums \(m_k(q)\) from the strength function \(S(\omega,q) = \text{Im}\,\chi(\omega,q)/\pi\).

## Key numbers

* Enhancement factor \(\kappa(A)\) for the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) sum rule is about 10% for light nuclei and increases to 20% for heavy nuclei, for the adopted Landau parameters \(F_1 = -0.3\) and \(F_1^\prime = 1.1\).
* The isovector interaction parameter \(F_0^\prime = 1.41\) was used to keep the isospin symmetry energy \(C_{sym}\) near 60 MeV.
* The relaxation-time parameter \(\alpha_2 = 5.4\) MeV was adopted, corresponding to in-medium \(NN\)-scattering cross sections.
* The effective nucleon mass was taken as \(m^* = 0.9m\), corresponding to the Landau parameter \(F_1 = -0.3\).
* The "model independent" EWS \(m_1(q)\) normalized to the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule is practically constant over the temperature interval \(T = 0 \div 5\) MeV (shown for nucleus \(A=208\)).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic-theory treatment of isovector response from cold nuclei (as in the zero-sound regime of Landau's Fermi-liquid theory) to hot nuclei with relaxation, building on prior approaches by Kolomietz and collaborators (2007) and by Di Toro et al. (1999). The results enable predictions for the temperature dependence of the IVGDR in heated nuclei, relevant for understanding nuclear collective dynamics in hot compound systems, and provide a fit-derived value of the Landau parameter \(F_1^\prime \approx 1.1\) from comparison with experimental photoabsorption data from the Livermore group (Berman and Fultz, 1975; Bergere, 1977).
