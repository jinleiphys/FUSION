# Covariant derivative expansion of fermionic effective action at high temperatures

> We derive the fermionic contribution to the 1-loop effective action for A_4 and A_i fields at high temperatures, assuming that gluon fields are slowly varying but allowing for an arbitrary amplitude of A_4.

## Key claim

The paper derives the fermionic contribution to the 1-loop effective action for static $A_4$ and $A_i$ fields at high temperatures, assuming gluon fields are slowly varying but allowing for an arbitrary amplitude of $A_4$, for the $SU(2)$ gauge group with massless fermions in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors use a background field method for gluons and evaluate the 1-loop fermionic action through a functional determinant formalism, employing a covariant derivative expansion in spatial derivatives while summing all powers of $A_4$. They work in a static gauge for $A_4$, restrict background fields to their zero Matsubara frequency, and regularize UV divergences using a Pauli-Villars cutoff, performing the Matsubara frequency sums and momentum integrals analytically.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass from curvature of the fermion potential around $\phi=0$: $m_D^2 = \frac{1}{3}T^2\left(N_c+\frac{N_f}{2}\right)$, with the fermionic contribution giving $-(N_f T)/6$ for the $SU(2)$ case
* Fermion potential: $V^{{\rm F}} = - N_f\,\frac{(2\pi)^2}{24}\left[(1-\nu^2)^2 -1\right]_{{\rm mod}\,\,2}$ where $\nu=\phi/(2\pi T)$
* Pure Yang-Mills potential for comparison: $V^{{\rm YM}}=\frac{(2\pi)^2}{3}\nu^2 (1-\nu)^2|_{{\tiny{\rm mod}}\,\,1}$
* Fermion potential periodicity: $4\pi T$ (double the gluon potential period of $2\pi T$)
* The function $\Phi(\nu) = 2\left[\psi\left(\frac{1+\nu}{2}\right)+\psi\left(\frac{1-\nu}{2}\right)\right]$ appears in the electric sector coefficients, where $\psi$ is the digamma function

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the 1-loop kinetic energy for eigenvalues of the Polyakov line from gluon and ghost fluctuations (Diakonov and Oswald, 2003) to include quark contributions. It uses the covariant derivative expansion method originally developed for zero-temperature QCD by Diakonov, Petrov, and Yung (1988). The results are suitable for studying correlation functions of the Polyakov line near the deconfinement phase transition and for computing quantum weights of semiclassical objects such as dyons and calorons.
