# Six-quark structure of $d^*(2380)$ in chiral constituent quark model

> The structure of $d^*(2380)$ is re-studied with the single cluster structure in the chiral SU(3) quark model which has successfully been employed to explain the $N-N$ scattering data and the binding energy of deuteron. The binding behavior of such a six quark system is solved by using a variational method. The trial wave function is chosen to be a combination of a basic spherical symmetric component of $[(0s)^6]_{orb}$ in the orbital space with $0\hbarω$ excitation and an inner structural deformation component of $[(0s)^5(1s)]_{orb}$ and $[(0s)^4(0p)^2]_{orb}$ in the orbital space with $2\hbarω$ excitation, both of which are in the spatial [6] symmetry. It is shown that the mass of the system is about $2356$ MeV, which is qualitative consistent with the result both from the two-cluster configuration calculation and from the data measured by the WASA Collaborations. This result tells us that as long as the medium-range interaction due to the chiral symmetry consideration is properly introduced, the mass of system will be reduced in a rather large extent. It also implies that the observed $d^*$ is a six-quark bound state with respect to the $ΔΔ$ threshold, which again supports the conclusion that $d^*$ is a hexaquark dominant state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the six-quark system with quantum numbers \(I(J^P)=0(3^+)\) can form a bound state with a mass of about 2356 MeV in a single-cluster configuration calculation, provided the medium-range interaction from chiral symmetry (Goldstone boson exchange) is included. This result is qualitatively consistent with the observed \(d^*(2380)\) and contradicts the conclusion of Park (2015) that such a system is not bound when only one-gluon-exchange and confinement are used.

## Method

The authors use the chiral SU(3) constituent quark model with a linear confining potential, solving the six-quark system via a variational method. The trial wave function is a linear combination of a spherical \((0s)^6\) component with \(0\hbar\omega\) excitation and a deformation component composed of \((0s)^5(1s)\) and \((0s)^4(0p)^2\) configurations with \(2\hbar\omega\) excitation, both in spatial [6] symmetry. A two-parameter variation over the size parameters \(b_1\) and \(b_2\) is performed to find the minimum mass.

## Key numbers

- The minimum mass of the \(d^*(2380)\) system is 2356 MeV, obtained at variational parameters \(b_1 = 0.5\) fm and \(b_2 = 0.61\) fm.
- Without the chiral-field-induced interaction \(V^{ch}_{ij}\), the mass of the system is about 2481 MeV.
- The mass is about 108 MeV below the \(\Delta\Delta\) threshold and about 46 MeV above the \(\Delta N\pi\) threshold.
- The mixing coefficients of the wave function are \(c_1 = 0.849\) and \(c_2 = -0.727\).
- The mirror state with \(IS=30\) has a mass of 2412 MeV at the same variational parameters.

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-cluster Resonating Group Method (RGM) calculations by Huang (2014) and Dong (2015), which predicted a bound \(d^*\) with a large hidden-color component, by showing that a single-cluster configuration can reproduce a similar binding energy. The result contradicts the conclusion of Park (2015), who used a naive constituent quark model with only one-gluon-exchange and confinement and found no binding. The paper enables further study of the \(d^*\) as a hexaquark-dominated state, though the simple trial wave function used here underestimates the decay width, indicating that a more sophisticated treatment of the surface region is needed.
