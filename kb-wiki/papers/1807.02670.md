# Going from Asymmetric Nuclei to Neutron Stars to Tidal Polarizability in Gravitational Waves

> Among the five-year government-funded World Class University Projects in Korea, the category-3 program approved at Hanyang University in Seoul led to an exploratory effort to go from neutron-rich nuclei to dense matter in neutron stars. The principal results in what transpired in the effort -- and what followed afterwards -- are described with the focus on the possibly important, hitherto unexplored, role played in nuclear dynamics of topology and hidden symmetries of QCD. The potential link to the proton mass problem is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that topology change (skyrmion to half-skyrmion transition) and hidden symmetries of QCD (scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry) play a crucial, previously unexplored role in nuclear dynamics from asymmetric nuclei to neutron stars, with the claim that these effects can be "translated" into density-dependent parameters of an effective field theory without explicitly introducing quarks and gluons.

## Method

The authors employ an effective field theory approach (denoted EFT-\(bs\)HLS) that includes nucleons, pions, a dilaton scalar (identified with \(f_0(500)\)), and the lightest vector mesons \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) as hidden gauge fields, matched to QCD at a scale \(\Lambda_M \sim 1\) GeV. They incorporate topology change via skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition at a density \(n_{1/2}\), which is translated into intrinsic density dependence (IDD) of the Lagrangian parameters, and apply Wilsonian renormalization group decimations (\(V_{\text{lowk}}\)RG) to compute nuclear matter properties and compact-star equations of state.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Topology change density \(n_{1/2}\) estimated at \(\gsim 2n_0\) (rough guess)
- Vector manifestation density \(n_{vm} \gsim 20 n_0\) at which chiral symmetry is restored
- Effective baryon mass in half-skyrmion phase (parity doubling): \(m_0 \sim (0.6-0.9) m_N\)
- Pion decay constant in vacuum: \(f_\pi \simeq 93\) MeV
- Chiral scale: \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi f_\pi \sim 1\) GeV
- Observed \(\sim 2\)-solar mass star mentioned as constraint on hadron-quark continuity

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior program (Rho and Ma, 2018) under the WCU/Hanyang project, which aimed to connect RAON accelerator physics on neutron-rich nuclei to neutron-star observations and gravitational-wave tidal polarizability. It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT) by explicitly incorporating hidden local and scale symmetries, and builds on earlier skyrmion approaches (Cheshire-Cat phenomenon, 1980s) and the vector manifestation fixed point (Harada and Yamawaki). The results enable predictions for the equation of state of neutron-rich matter, including a cusp in the symmetry energy from topology change, and link to the proton mass problem.
