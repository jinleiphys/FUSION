# Assisted Tunneling of a metastable state between barriers

> The assisted tunneling of a metastable state between barriers is investigated analytically by means of a simplified one dimensional model. A time dependent perturbation changes the pole spectrum of the wave function introducing a larger decay constant. New insights about the decay of a metastable state are found. The scheme is exemplified for parameters corresponding to the nuclear process of $α$ decay.

## Key claim

A time-dependent perturbation (such as a low-frequency oscillating electric field) applied to a metastable state trapped between two barriers introduces a new line of poles in the wave function's momentum-space expansion, producing a larger decay constant than the unperturbed case; the effect is not specific to time-varying fields and can arise even from a static spatially inhomogeneous potential, though the paper treats only the time-harmonic case that vanishes in the zero-frequency limit.

## Method

The author models a one-dimensional quantum system with two delta-function barriers at positions ±x₀, initially prepared as a Gaussian wave packet between them. A time-harmonic perturbation V(x,t) = μ x sin(ω t) is applied, and the wave function is expanded in the complete orthonormal set of even and odd stationary states of the unperturbed system. The amplitudes are solved perturbatively in the coupling μ, and the decay behavior is extracted from the pole structure of the normalization factors n_e(k) and n_o(k) in the complex momentum plane, with contour integration selecting poles in the lower half-plane.

## Key numbers

* For nuclear α-decay parameters, the first even pole appears at q_{e,n=1} ≈ 0.024 ± i 3 × 10⁻⁷ fm⁻² (real and imaginary parts of the squared momentum).
* The unperturbed decay constant from the first even pole is Λ_e = π³ / (8 m x₀⁴ m² λ²).
* The first odd pole produces a decay constant Λ_o = 8 Λ_e (a factor of 8 larger than the even-pole decay constant, specific to the delta-barrier model).
* For the δ-barrier model with mλ x₀ = 400, x₀ = 10 fm, and m ≈ 20 fm⁻¹, the product mλ x₀ = 400 >> 1.
* The perturbative regime requires ω_min³ > μ² m; for α decay with μ ≈ 1 eV² (corresponding to E₀ ≈ 5 × 10⁶ V/m), ω_min > 10¹² sec⁻¹.
* If the initial wave packet width Δ ≈ x₀, the first odd pole contributes about 10% to the wave function, while the second even pole contributes much less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the standard Gamow (1928) and Gurney-Condon (1928) theory of α-decay tunneling by introducing a perturbative mechanism for assisted tunneling, building on the complex-time method of Ivlev (2007) and the "elevator effect" model of resonance-assisted activation by Azbel (1994). The Floquet formalism (Shirley, 1965) is the main theoretical tool for assisted tunneling in solid-state systems, but the present model provides an exact analytical perturbative solution that avoids the large-matrix computations of that approach. The results enable assessment of experimental feasibility for accelerating nuclear decay processes, with potential implications for radioactive waste treatment and alternative fuel sources, though the author notes that electronic screening of static fields in a nuclear setting would be almost complete.
