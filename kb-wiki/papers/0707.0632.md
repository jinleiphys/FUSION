# Pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor locked phase at moderate densities

> The properties of the pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor locked phase at moderate densities are studied within a model of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio type. The Goldstone bosons are constructed explicitly by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-quark scattering in random phase approximation. Main focus of our investigations are (i) the weak decay constant in the chiral limit, (ii) the masses of the flavored (pseudo-) Goldstone bosons for non-zero but equal quark masses, (iii) their masses and effective chemical potentials for non-equal quark masses, and (iv) the onset of kaon condensation. We compare our results with the predictions of the low-energy effective field theory. The deviations from results obtained in the weak-coupling limit are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The authors study pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase at moderate densities using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model, finding that deviations from weak-coupling results become significant at intermediate densities relevant for compact star phenomenology, with the Goldberger-Treiman relation \(g_\pi f_\pi = \Delta\) holding in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors construct pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons explicitly by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-quark scattering in the random phase approximation (RPA) within an NJL-type model. They determine the CFL ground state by solving the gap equations for the diquark condensates and then compute the T-matrix in Nambu-Gorkov space to extract meson masses, decay constants, and effective chemical potentials. The calculations are performed at zero temperature with a 3-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) to regularize divergent integrals.

## Key numbers

- In the chiral limit, the weak decay constant \(f_\pi\) is related to the gap parameter \(\Delta\) by the Goldberger-Treiman relation \(g_\pi f_\pi = \Delta\), where \(g_\pi\) is the meson-quark coupling constant.
- For equal non-zero quark masses \(m_u = m_d = m_s \equiv m\), the flavored pseudoscalar mesons acquire masses \(m_\pi\) that are calculated from the pole of the T-matrix at \(\vec q = 0\).
- For non-equal quark masses, the mesons acquire effective chemical potentials \(\mu_i\) that appear in the pole approximation \(T^{(i)}(q) \approx -g_i^2 / [(q_0 + \mu_i)^2 - c_i^2 \vec q^2 - m_i^2]\).
- The onset of kaon condensation is determined by the condition that the effective chemical potential for kaons exceeds their mass, leading to a zero-energy mode.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the CFL phase that were restricted to the chiral limit (Fukushima 2005, Ebert) or focused only on ground state properties without explicit Goldstone boson construction (Buballa 2004, Forbes 2004, Warringa 2006). The results are compared with predictions from low-energy effective field theory (LEET) developed by Casalbuoni (1999), Schafer (2000), and others, as well as with weak-coupling calculations using High Density Effective Theory (HDET) by Beane (2000) and Nardulli (2002). The findings enable improved modeling of transport and thermodynamic properties of quark matter in compact stars at intermediate densities where perturbative QCD is not applicable.
