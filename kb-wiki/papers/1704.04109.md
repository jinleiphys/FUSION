# A hybrid approach to relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC BES energies

> Using a hybrid (viscous hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade) framework, we model the bulk dynamical evolution of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) Beam Energy Scan (BES) collision energies, including the effects from non-zero net baryon current and its dissipative diffusion. This framework is in full (3+1)D, which allows us to study the non-trivial longitudinal structure and dynamics of the collision systems, for example baryon stopping and transport, as well as longitudinal fluctuations. For the first time, the quantitative effect of net-baryon diffusion on hadronic observables is addressed. Finally, we propose a dynamical initialization scheme to study the importance of the pre-equilibrium stage at the RHIC BES energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including net-baryon diffusion in a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade hybrid framework quantitatively affects hadronic observables at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, but the extraction of the net-baryon diffusion constant from net-proton rapidity distributions is entangled with the unknown initial shape of the net-baryon density distribution.

## Method

The authors model heavy-ion collisions using a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic evolution with shear viscosity and net-baryon diffusion, initialized with event-averaged Monte-Carlo Glauber density profiles for entropy and net-baryon density. The hydrodynamic evolution includes second-order Israel-Stewart equations for the shear stress tensor and net-baryon diffusion current, with the net-baryon diffusion constant \(\kappa_B\) parameterized by a free coefficient \(C_B\). When the local energy density drops to 0.3 GeV/fm\(^3\), fluid cells are converted to particles via the Cooper-Frye prescription with out-of-equilibrium corrections for shear and diffusion, and the particles are subsequently evolved using the UrQMD hadronic cascade model.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity is set to \(\eta T/(e + P) = 0.08\) with \(\tau_\pi = 0.4/T\).
- The switching energy density for converting fluid to particles is \(e_\mathrm{sw} = 0.3\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The pre-equilibrium strings are assumed to free-stream for \(\tau_\mathrm{th} = 0.5\) fm/c before thermalization.
- At 19.6 GeV, the thermalization times of strings have a finite spread from \(\tau = 0.5\) to 1.5 fm/c.
- The time for two nuclei to pass through each other at RHIC BES energies is 1–3 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends hybrid (viscous hydrodynamics + hadronic cascade) frameworks previously applied at top RHIC and LHC energies (e.g., Heinz 2013, Gale 2013) to lower RHIC BES collision energies by incorporating a non-zero net-baryon current and its dissipative diffusion. It builds on earlier dynamical initialization models (Monnai 2015, Denicol 2015) that studied longitudinal fluctuations, now generalized to account for finite nuclear thickness and collision-time spread. The framework provides fireball trajectories in the QCD phase diagram needed to search for the critical point, and enables future extraction of the QGP heat conductivity from baryon-rich environments.
