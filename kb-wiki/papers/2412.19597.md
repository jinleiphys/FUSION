# Composite nature of the $T_{cc}$ state

> In 2021, LHCb collaboration reported a very narrow state in the $D^0D^0π^+$ mass spectrum just below the $D^{*+}D^0$ mass threshold. We consider the influence of the Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole in the scattering amplitude to derive a general treatment for the two-body final state interaction near its threshold. The line shape (or the energy dependent event distribution) are then obtained, where the parameters can be fixed by fitting to the experimental data on the $D^0D^0π^+$ mass spectrum. Within our method the data are quite well reproduced. The pole structure in the complex energy plane indicates that the $T_{cc}$ state has a large portion of elementary degree of freedom (e.g., the compact tetraquark component) inside its hadron wave function. The compositeness as a measure of molecule component in its wave function is predicted to be $0.23_{-0.09}^{+0.40}$. Clearly, the non-molecular component takes a non-negligible or even dominant portion.

## Key claim

The $T_{cc}$ state, while often interpreted as a molecular state, has a large or even dominant elementary (compact tetraquark) component in its wave function, with the molecular component's compositeness predicted to be only $0.23_{-0.09}^{+0.40}$.

## Method

The authors incorporate a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole into the non-relativistic $D^{*+}D^0$ scattering amplitude in $S$-wave, deriving a general treatment for the two-body final state interaction near threshold. They fit the resulting line shape, including experimental mass resolution and background parameterization, to the LHCb $D^0D^0\pi^+$ mass spectrum data using MINUIT. The pole structure in the complex energy plane is then analyzed to extract the compositeness via the residue of the scattering amplitude and the two-point Green function.

## Key numbers

*   Compositeness (molecular component fraction) of $T_{cc}$: $X = 0.23_{-0.09}^{+0.40}$.
*   Pole parameters from LHCb (input): $\delta m_{\rm pole} = -360 \pm 40^{+4}_{-0}$ keV, $\Gamma_{\rm pole} = 48 \pm 2^{+0}_{-14}$ keV.
*   Fitted CDD pole position relative to threshold: $M_{\text{CDD}} - m_{\text{th}} = 0.47 \pm 0.38$ MeV.
*   Scattering length from central fit parameters: $a = 1.8$ fm; effective range: $r = -77.2$ fm.
*   Bound state pole found at 3874.72 MeV (physical sheet) and resonance pole at 3874.48 - i 1.74 MeV (unphysical sheet), for the central fit with $\Gamma_{D^{*+}}\to 0$.

## Context

This work extends the CDD-pole method previously applied to $X(3872)$ (Kang, 2016), $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ (Kang, 2016; Zhang, 2022), and other near-threshold resonances, to the $T_{cc}$ state discovered by LHCb (2021). The result contradicts a purely molecular interpretation and instead supports a dominant compact tetraquark component, overlapping with the LHCb's own Weinberg compositeness analysis which found $X > 0.46$ at 90% confidence level. The approach enables a more general treatment of final state interactions than effective range expansion, which fails here due to the extremely large effective range ($r = -77.2$ fm).
