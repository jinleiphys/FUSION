# Analyses of k_t distributions at RHIC by means of some selected statistical and stochastic models

> The new data on k_t distributions obtained at RHIC are analysed by means of selected models of statistical and stochastic origin in order to estimate their importance in providing new information on hadronization process, in particular on the value of the temperature at freeze-out to hadronic phase.

# Analyses of k_t distributions at RHIC by means of some selected statistical and stochastic models

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that RHIC transverse momentum (k_t) distributions can be reasonably described by either a non-extensive Tsallis-type statistical model or a stochastic diffusion (Ornstein-Uhlenbeck) process in transverse rapidity space, but the classical Hagedorn model fails at high k_t. The authors hedge that the stochastic approach is "more dynamical" than the q-statistical one, where the true dynamical origin of the nonextensivity parameter is "not yet firmly established."

## Method

The authors fit three models to k_t distributions from BRAHMS (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV), STAR (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV), and PHENIX (sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV) data at various centralities: (1) a Hagedorn statistical model with a mass spectrum; (2) a non-extensive generalization using Tsallis statistics with two formulations of the parameter Q (Q = q/(1-q) and Q = 1/(1-q)); and (3) a Fokker-Planck equation solution for an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process in transverse rapidity space y_t = sinh^{-1}(k_t/m), treating the mass m as a free parameter. The fits are evaluated via chi-squared per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- For BRAHMS data (0-10% centrality), the Hagedorn model yields chi^2/n.d.f. = 19.3/23 with T_0 = 0.169 +/- 0.006 GeV and T_h = 0.180 +/- 0.007 GeV (m_0 = 0.5 GeV fixed)
- For BRAHMS data (0-10% centrality), the Tsallis model (Q = q/(1-q)) yields chi^2/n.d.f. = 11.2/23 with T_0 = 0.232 GeV and q = 1.043
- For BRAHMS data (0-10% centrality), the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck model yields chi^2/n.d.f. = 39.9/23 with T_0 = 0.201 GeV and m = 0.784 GeV (free mass parameter)
- For STAR data (0-5% centrality), the Tsallis model (Q = q/(1-q)) yields chi^2/n.d.f. = 170/32 with T_0 = 0.171 GeV and q = 1.071
- For STAR data (0-5% centrality), the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck model yields chi^2/n.d.f. = 221/32 with T_0 = 0.169 GeV and m = 0.533 GeV
- For PHENIX data (0-5% centrality), the Tsallis model (Q = q/(1-q)) yields chi^2/n.d.f. = 5.13/29 with T_0 = 0.201 GeV and q = 1.049
- Temperature fluctuations Delta T_0 extracted from the Q = 1/(1-q) formulation range from 0.032 to 0.047 GeV across all datasets
- The simple Boltzmann gas model (Hagedorn with rho(m)=1) gives chi^2/n.d.f. = 177/23 for BRAHMS 0-10% centrality and 567/22 for BRAHMS 60-80% centrality

## Context

This work extends earlier applications of Tsallis statistics to k_t distributions in e+e- annihilations (Bediaga 1999) and p-pbar collisions (Wibig), and applies for the first time a systematic comparison of two formulations of the Tsallis parameter Q. The stochastic Ornstein-Uhlenbeck approach builds on prior work by Biyajima (2002) and collaborators. The paper enables future simple estimations of new RHIC data using either the non-extensive or stochastic framework, and suggests that the q parameter can be interpreted via the fluctuation-dissipation relation as a measure of the total heat capacity (and thus volume) of the hadronizing system, with q decreasing from e+e- (q ~ 1.2) through p-p to AA collisions.
