# Magnetic polarizability of a charged pion from four-point functions in lattice QCD

> Electromagnetic dipole polarizabilities are fundamental properties of a hadron that represent its resistance to deformation under external fields. For a charged hadron, the presence of acceleration and Landau levels complicates the isolation of its deformation energy in the conventional background field method. In this work, we explore a general method based on four-point functions in lattice QCD that takes into account all photon, quark and gluon interactions. The electric polarizability ($α_E$) has been determined from the method in a previous proof-of-principle simulation. Here we focus on the magnetic polarizability ($β_M$) using the same quenched Wilson action on a $24^3\times 48$ lattice at $β=6.0$ with pion mass from 1100 to 370 MeV. The results from the connected diagrams show a large cancellation between the elastic and inelastic contributions, leading to a relatively small and negative value for $β_M$ consistent with chiral perturbation theory. We also discuss the mechanism for $α_E+β_M$ from combining the two studies.

## Key claim
The magnetic polarizability of the charged pion is extracted from four-point correlation functions in lattice QCD, yielding a relatively small and negative value consistent with chiral perturbation theory, but only after a large cancellation between elastic and inelastic contributions; the result is obtained from connected diagrams only, with disconnected contributions left for future work.

## Method
The method uses four-point functions in lattice QCD with two electromagnetic current insertions, avoiding the background field method complications of Landau levels for charged hadrons. The calculation employs quenched Wilson action on a 24^3 x 48 lattice at beta=6.0 with pion masses from 1100 to 370 MeV, using conserved vector currents and zero-momentum Breit frame kinematics. The magnetic polarizability is extracted from the time integral of subtracted four-point functions at finite momentum, extrapolated to zero momentum, combined with the elastic charge radius term.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses studied: 1104.7 ± 1.2 MeV, 795.0 ± 1.1 MeV, 596.8 ± 1.4 MeV, 367.7 ± 2.2 MeV
- Lattice spacing: 1/a = 2.312 GeV (inverse)
- Lattice volume: 24^3 x 48
- Momentum values in physical units: q^2 = 0, 0.366, 0.733, 1.465 GeV^2
- Total beta_M at physical point: -3.2 ± 0.9 to -1.4 ± 0.5 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Elastic beta_M at physical point: -13.9 ± 1.8 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Inelastic beta_M at physical point: 10.7 ± 2.0 to 12.4 ± 1.9 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Known ChPT value: -2.77 ± 0.11 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)
- Known PDG value: -2.0 ± 0.6 ± 0.7 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)

## Context
This work extends the four-point function approach previously applied to electric polarizability (Lee et al., 2023) to the magnetic polarizability of the charged pion, providing an alternative to the background field method which faces challenges from Landau levels for charged hadrons (Detmold, 2006; Primer, 2014; Luschevskaya, 2015). The results enable future calculations including disconnected diagrams and extension to other hadrons such as the proton, and provide lattice QCD input for comparison with chiral perturbation theory predictions (Gasser, 2006) and experimental PDG values.
