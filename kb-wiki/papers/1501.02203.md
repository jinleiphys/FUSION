# Direct observation of quark-hadron duality in the free neutron F_2 structure function

> Using data from the recent BONuS experiment at Jefferson Lab, which utilized a novel spectator tagging technique to extract the inclusive electron-free neutron scattering cross section, we obtain the first direct observation of quark-hadron duality in the neutron F_2 structure function. The data are used to reconstruct the lowest few (N=2, 4 and 6) moments of F_2 in the three prominent nucleon resonance regions, as well as the moments integrated over the entire resonance region. Comparison with moments computed from global parametrizations of parton distribution functions suggest that quark--hadron duality holds locally for the neutron in the second and third resonance regions down to Q^2 ~ 1 GeV^2, with violations possibly up to 20% observed in the first resonance region.

## Key claim
Using spectator-tagged electron-deuteron scattering data from the BONuS experiment, the authors report the first direct, model-independent observation of quark-hadron duality in the free neutron F_2 structure function. Duality holds locally in the second and third resonance regions down to Q^2 ~ 1 GeV^2, with violations possibly up to 20% observed in the first (Delta) resonance region.

## Method
The BONuS experiment at Jefferson Lab detected low-momentum (70-100 MeV) spectator protons at backward angles in electron-deuteron scattering using a Radial Time Projection Chamber, isolating the free neutron cross section with minimal nuclear smearing corrections. The authors computed the N=2, 4, and 6 truncated moments of the neutron F_2 structure function over four invariant mass intervals (first, second, third, and total resonance regions) and compared them to moments from the ABKM global PDF parametrization including target mass and higher twist corrections.

## Key numbers
- Neutron F_2 truncated moments (N=2) at Q^2 = 1.0 GeV^2: first region = (31.5 ± 1.1) × 10^{-3}, second region = (16.4 ± 0.4) × 10^{-3}, third region = (14.1 ± 0.3) × 10^{-3}, total = (76.7 ± 1.2) × 10^{-3}
- Neutron F_2 truncated moments (N=2) at Q^2 = 4.1 GeV^2: first region = (1.3 ± 0.1) × 10^{-3}, second region = (1.7 ± 0.1) × 10^{-3}, third region = (2.8 ± 0.1) × 10^{-3}
- Neutron F_2 truncated moments (N=4) at Q^2 = 1.0 GeV^2: first region = (11.58 ± 0.43) × 10^{-3}, total = (17.49 ± 0.44) × 10^{-3}
- Neutron F_2 truncated moments (N=6) at Q^2 = 1.0 GeV^2: first region = (4.39 ± 0.18) × 10^{-3}, total = (5.28 ± 0.18) × 10^{-3}
- Total systematic uncertainty in the extracted neutron structure function: 8.7%, with an overall 10% scale uncertainty
- Duality holds to within ~10% for the second, third, and total resonance regions over Q^2 = 1-4 GeV^2
- First resonance region moments are ~20%-30% larger than the PDF-based fit

## Context
This work extends the study of quark-hadron duality from the proton (Niculescu 2000, Malace 2009) and from model-dependent deuterium analyses (Malace 2010) to the free neutron using a direct experimental technique. The results disfavor the model by Brodsky (2000) that predicted strong duality violations in the neutron due to accidental charge cancellations in the proton. The BONuS data enable future tests at higher Q^2 and x with the 11 GeV Jefferson Lab beam (BONuS12).
