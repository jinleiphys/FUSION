# $Z_b(10610)^\pm$ and $Z_b(10650)^\pm$ as the $B^*\bar{B}$ and $B^*\bar{B}^{*}$ molecular states

> In the framework of the one-boson-exchange model, we have studied the interaction of the $B^*\bar{B}$ and $B^*\bar{B}^{*}$ system. After considering the S-wave and D-wave mixing, we notice that both $Z_b(10610)^\pm$ and $Z_b(10650)^\pm$ can be interpreted as the $B^*\bar{B}$ and $B^*\bar{B}^{*}$ molecular states quite naturally. Within the same framework, there also exist several molecular charmonia including X(3872) and several other molecular bottomonia, which are the partners of $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$. The long-range one-pion-exchange force alone is strong enough to form these loosely bound molecular states, which ensures the numerical results quite model-independent and robust.

## Key claim

Both $Z_b(10610)^\pm$ and $Z_b(10650)^\pm$ can be interpreted as $B^*\bar{B}$ and $B^*\bar{B}^{*}$ molecular states, respectively, within the one-boson-exchange model when S-wave and D-wave mixing is included; the long-range one-pion-exchange force alone is sufficient to form these loosely bound states, making the numerical results "quite model-independent and robust."

## Method

The authors employed the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model to derive effective potentials for the $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$ systems, including exchanges of pseudoscalar ($\pi$, $\eta$), scalar ($\sigma$), and vector ($\rho$, $\omega$) mesons. They constructed flavor wave functions for isovector and isoscalar states, then solved the coupled-channel SchrÃ¶dinger equation incorporating both S-wave and D-wave interactions between the heavy mesons. A monopole form factor with a cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ was introduced to regularize the potential and account for the finite size of the vertices.

## Key numbers

* $Z_b(10610)$ mass: $M = 10608.4 \pm 2.0$ MeV/$c^2$, width: $\Gamma = 15.6 \pm 2.5$ MeV/$c^2$ (Belle average over five hidden-bottom decay channels).
* $Z_b(10650)$ mass: $M = 10653.2 \pm 1.5$ MeV/$c^2$, width: $\Gamma = 14.4 \pm 3.2$ MeV/$c^2$ (Belle average over five hidden-bottom decay channels).
* The pion decay constant used: $f_\pi = 132$ MeV.
* Coupling constant $g = 0.59$ extracted from the experimental width of $D^{*+}$.
* Vector meson coupling parameters: $\beta = 0.9$ (from vector meson dominance), $\lambda = 0.56$ GeV$^{-1}$ (from light cone sum rule and lattice QCD).
* Scalar meson coupling: $g_s = g_\pi/(2\sqrt{6})$ with $g_\pi = 3.73$.
* Vector meson coupling constant: $g_V = m_\rho/f_\pi = 5.8$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation previously applied to charmonium-like states such as $X(3872)$ (studied by Liu et al. (2008) and others) to the bottomonium sector, following the Belle Collaboration's discovery of the charged $Z_b$ states in 2011. The authors note that earlier QCD sum rule calculations by Chen et al. predicted tetraquark masses around $10.1$-$10.2$ GeV, significantly lower than the observed $Z_b$ masses, making the molecular interpretation more plausible. The study enables future dynamical calculations of decay patterns for these molecular bottomonia and predicts several partner states, including molecular charmonia analogous to $X(3872)$ and additional bottomonium molecular candidates.
