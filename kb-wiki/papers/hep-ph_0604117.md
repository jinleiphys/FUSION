# Quenching of Leading Jets and Particles: the p_t Dependent Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect from Nonlinear k_t Factorization

> We report the first derivation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect for leading jets at fixed values of the transverse momentum p_t in the beam fragmentation region of hadron-nucleus collisions from RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) to LHC (Large Hadron Collider). The major novelty of this work is a derivation of the missing virtual radiative pQCD correction to these processes - the real-emission radiative corrections are already available in the literature. We manifestly implement the unitarity relation, which in the simplest form requires that upon summing over the virtual and real-emission corrections the total number of scattered quarks must exactly equal unity. For the free-nucleon target, the leading jet spectrum is shown to satisfy the familiar linear Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov leading log(1/x) (LL-1/x) evolution. For nuclear targets, the nonlinear k_t-factorization for the LL-1/x evolution of the leading jet sepctrum is shown to exactly match the equally nonlinear LL-1/x evolution of the collective nuclear glue - there emerges a unique linear k_t-factorization relation between the two nonlinear evolving nuclear observables. We argue that within the standard dilute uncorrelated nucleonic gas treatment of heavy nuclei, in the finite energy range from RHIC to LHC, the leading jet spectrum can be evolved in the LL-1/x Balitsky-Kovchegov approximation. We comment on the extension of these results to, and their possible reggeon field theory interpretation for, mid-rapidity jets at LHC.

## Key claim

The authors present the first derivation of the missing virtual radiative pQCD correction to the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect for leading jets at fixed transverse momentum in hadron-nucleus collisions, claiming that the leading jet spectrum provides a unique linear mapping of the collective nuclear glue despite both quantities undergoing nonlinear evolution.

## Method

The authors derive the virtual radiative corrections (VRC) to quasielastic quark-nucleus scattering using color dipole formalism and s-channel unitarity, complementing previously available real-emission corrections (REC). They implement the unitarity relation requiring that the sum of virtual and real-emission corrections yields exactly one scattered quark, and develop a nonlinear k_t-factorization framework for the leading jet spectrum in the beam fragmentation region. The analysis covers both color-triplet (quark) and color-octet (gluon) projectiles, and extends to the leading-log(1/x) evolution properties of the spectra.

## Key numbers

* The coherence condition for coherent nuclear effects is x less than or approximately equal to x_A = 1/(2R_A m_N), where R_A is the nuclear radius and m_N the nucleon mass
* The nuclear optical thickness T(b) is normalized such that the integral over impact parameter yields the nuclear mass number A
* The dipole cross section for a color-singlet a-bar-a dipole is given by sigma_{a-bar-a}(x,r) = (C_a/C_F) sigma(x,r), where C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c) is the quark Casimir
* For the free-nucleon target, the quasielastic scattering cross section satisfies d(sigma_{Qel})/d^2p = (C_a/(2C_F)) f(x,p), where f(x,p) is the unintegrated gluon density

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on nonlinear k_t-factorization for dijet spectra (Nikolaev and Schafer, 2004-2006) and single-jet spectra (Schafer, 2005), where conventional linear k_t-factorization was shown to break down in nuclear environments. The paper provides the missing virtual corrections needed for a complete description of the LPM effect, enabling quantitative treatment of leading jet quenching at RHIC and LHC energies. The results connect to the Color Glass Condensate approach and the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) approximation (Balitsky, 1996; Kovchegov, 1999), and the authors argue that for heavy nuclei the BK approximation provides a useful working framework from RHIC to LHC energies.
