# Renormalization group flow equations with full momentum dependence

> After a short elementary introduction to the exact renormalization group for the effective action, I discuss a particular truncation of the hierarchy of flow equations that allows for the determination of the full momentum of the $n$-point functions. Applications are then briefly presented, to critical O(N) models, to Bose-Einstein condensation and to finite temperature field theory.

## Key claim
The paper presents a truncation of the exact renormalization group hierarchy, the BMW scheme, that captures the full momentum dependence of n-point functions, going beyond the derivative expansion. The key hedge is that the leading-order truncation (at the level of the two-point function) is accurate only when the internal loop momentum in the flow equations can be set to zero in the vertices, which relies on the regulator ensuring smooth vertex functions.

## Method
The author derives the exact flow equation for the effective action and its hierarchy for n-point functions in a scalar O(N) field theory. The BMW approximation truncates the hierarchy at the level of the two-point function by setting the loop momentum to zero in the three- and four-point vertices that appear in its flow equation, then using relations that express those vertices as derivatives of the two-point function with respect to a constant background field. This yields a closed system of integro-differential equations for the two-point function and the effective potential, which is solved numerically.

## Key numbers
- For the O(N) models, critical exponents are calculated; the paper references results from Benitez et al. (2009) but does not list specific exponent values in the provided text.
- For Bose-Einstein condensation, the shift of the transition temperature in a dilute Bose gas is computed; the paper references Blaizot et al. (2008) but does not give a numerical value in the provided text.
- For finite temperature field theory, the method is applied to the thermodynamics of quantum fields, but no specific numerical results are given in the provided text.

## Context
This work extends the exact renormalization group formalism beyond the local potential approximation and derivative expansion, which only yield polynomial momentum dependence and cannot capture anomalous scaling at small momenta. The BMW scheme, originally proposed by Blaizot, Mendez-Galain, and Wschebor (2005), enables calculation of the full momentum dependence of correlation functions, which is essential for problems like the critical O(N) models, Bose-Einstein condensation, and finite temperature field theory. The paper also connects the BMW truncation to the two-particle irreducible (2PI) formalism, noting that the 2PI truncation yields flow equations that are exact derivatives and thus regulator-independent, unlike the BMW scheme.
