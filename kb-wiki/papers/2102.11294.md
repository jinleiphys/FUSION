# On systems of maximal quantum chaos

> A remarkable feature of chaos in many-body quantum systems is the existence of a bound on the quantum Lyapunov exponent. An important question is to understand what is special about maximally chaotic systems which saturate this bound. Here we provide further evidence for the `hydrodynamic' origin of chaos in such systems, and discuss hallmarks of maximally chaotic systems. We first provide evidence that a hydrodynamic effective field theory of chaos we previously proposed should be understood as a theory of maximally chaotic systems. We then emphasize and make explicit a signature of maximal chaos which was only implicit in prior literature, namely the suppression of exponential growth in commutator squares of generic few-body operators. We provide a general argument for this suppression within our chaos effective field theory, and illustrate it using SYK models and holographic systems. We speculate that this suppression indicates that the nature of operator scrambling in maximally chaotic systems is fundamentally different to scrambling in non-maximally chaotic systems. We also discuss a simplest scenario for the existence of a maximally chaotic regime at sufficiently large distances even for non-maximally chaotic systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hydrodynamic effective field theory (EFT) of chaos proposed by the authors in prior work is only consistent for systems that saturate the quantum Lyapunov bound, meaning the EFT describes exclusively maximally chaotic systems; the authors further claim that in such maximally chaotic systems, the exponential growth of commutator squares of generic few-body operators is suppressed at leading order in the large-N expansion.

## Method

The authors analyze the consistency conditions of their previously proposed hydrodynamic EFT for chaos, which describes operator growth via a "chaos mode" sigma coupled to energy conservation. They impose fluctuation-dissipation relations on generic operator two-point functions and show these are compatible with the EFT's shift symmetry only when the Lyapunov exponent takes the maximal value lambda = 2 pi / beta_0. They then compute the double commutator (commutator square) within the EFT at tree level and demonstrate exact cancellation of exponentially growing contributions, verifying this suppression explicitly in the SYK model and in holographic theories.

## Key numbers

- The quantum Lyapunov bound: lambda_max = 2 pi / (hbar beta_0), where beta_0 is the inverse temperature (equation (2)).
- In the SYK model at low temperature, the Lyapunov exponent is lambda = (2 pi / beta_0) * (1 - c/(beta_0 J)), with alpha_1 = 1 in the notation of the paper.
- In the large-q SYK model, the commutator square prefactor is suppressed relative to the OTOC prefactor by a factor 2 cos(pi nu/2), where nu parametrizes the coupling via (pi nu)/cos(pi nu/2) = beta_0 J.
- The OTOC prefactor in the large-q SYK model scales as (beta_0 J)/N, while the commutator square prefactor scales as 1/N (finite in the strong coupling limit beta_0 J to infinity).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior hydrodynamic EFT for chaos (Blake and Liu, 2017) by establishing that it applies exclusively to maximally chaotic systems, and clarifies a signature of maximal chaos implicit in earlier work by Kitaev, Shenker, Stanford, and others. The suppression of commutator squares in maximally chaotic systems was previously observed in SYK models (Kitaev; Gu et al., 2018) and holographic theories (Shenker and Stanford, 2014), but the paper provides a general EFT argument for this phenomenon. The results enable a sharper distinction between maximally and non-maximally chaotic systems and suggest that operator scrambling in maximally chaotic systems is fundamentally different in nature.
