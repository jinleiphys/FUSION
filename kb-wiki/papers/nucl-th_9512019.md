# Second Order Phase Transitions : From Infinite to Finite Systems

> We investigate the Equation of State (EOS) of classical systems having 300 and 512 particles confined in a box with periodic boundary conditions. We show that such a system, independently on the number of particles investigated, has a critical density of about 1/3 the ground state density and a critical temperature of about $2.5~ MeV$. The mass distribution at the critical point exhibits a power law with $τ= 2.23$. Making use of the grand partition function of Fisher's droplet model, we obtain an analytical EOS around the critical point in good agreement with the one extracted from the numerical simulations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a classical system of 300 or 512 particles confined in a box with periodic boundary conditions exhibits a liquid-gas phase transition with a critical point at a density of about 1/3 the ground state density and a temperature of about 2.5 MeV, and that this equation of state is independent of the number of particles used, contradicting earlier expectations that the critical temperature should decrease with system size.

## Method

Classical Molecular Dynamics simulations were performed for systems of 300 particles (150 protons + 150 neutrons) and 512 particles (256 protons + 256 neutrons) confined in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using a two-body potential with an attractive neutron-proton term and a purely repulsive identical-nucleon term. The equation of state was calculated via the virial theorem after thermalization, using two different initialization and thermalization procedures. Fragment mass distributions at the critical point were obtained by removing the box and allowing the system to expand, then applying a percolation-type cluster recognition algorithm with a distance cutoff of 5.4 fm.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c = 2.53 MeV
- Critical density: ρ_c = 0.04 fm^{-3} (about 1/3 the ground state density)
- Critical pressure: P_c = 0.029 MeV fm^{-3}
- Ratio P_c/(T_c ρ_c) = 0.32, compared to 0.287 for CO_2, 0.290 for Xe, and 0.305 for ^4He
- Power-law exponent for mass distribution at critical point: τ = 2.23
- For the Fisher droplet model analysis with τ = 2.33, the ratio M_0/M_1 at the critical point gives 0.299 for A_tot = 100 and 0.298 for A_tot = 10^8, while the numerical simulation gives 0.317

## Context

This work extends previous Classical Molecular Dynamics studies by Belkacem et al. (1995) that found evidence for critical behavior in expanding finite systems, by now calculating the equation of state for an infinite system with periodic boundary conditions. The results contradict earlier investigations using Skyrme-type interactions by Mekjian (1991) which concluded that the critical temperature decreases with decreasing particle number. The paper enables the use of Fisher's droplet model to describe nuclear fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions, where only a few hundred nucleons are involved, and provides an analytical equation of state around the critical point that agrees with the numerical simulations.
