# Resonance Electroproduction and the Origin of Mass

> One of the greatest challenges within the Standard Model is to discover the source of visible mass. Indeed, this is the focus of a "Millennium Problem", posed by the Clay Mathematics Institute. The answer is hidden within quantum chromodynamics (QCD); and it is probable that revealing the origin of mass will also explain the nature of confinement. In connection with these issues, this perspective describes insights that have recently been drawn using contemporary methods for solving the continuum bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory and how they have been informed and enabled by modern experiments on nucleon-resonance electroproduction.

# Resonance Electroproduction and the Origin of Mass

## Key claim

The paper argues that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB), driven by gluon self-interactions, is the source of more than 98% of visible mass in the Universe, and that this mechanism can be probed through nucleon-resonance electroproduction experiments; however, the claim is hedged by the recognition that the trace anomaly in the pion state vanishes identically in the chiral limit, requiring a precise cancellation between one-body dressing and two-body interaction effects that must be explained simultaneously with the proton's mass.

## Method

The paper employs Poincaré-covariant Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and the Faddeev equation for baryons, solved in a quark-diquark approximation, to compute baryon spectra and transition form factors. Continuum nonperturbative methods are combined with lattice QCD results for the gluon propagator and dressed-quark mass function, and predictions for the $\gamma^\ast p \to \Delta(1232)$ and $\gamma^\ast p \to \Delta(1600)$ transitions are compared with experimental data from Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

- Proton mass $m_p \approx 1$ GeV $\approx 2000 m_e$, where $m_e$ is the electron mass
- Renormalization-group-invariant gluon mass-scale $m_0 \approx 0.5$ GeV $\approx m_p/2$
- Gluon decoupling wavelength $\sigma := 1/m_0 \approx 0.5$ fm
- Dressed-quark mass in the infrared approximately $m_p/3$
- Mean-absolute-relative-difference between calculation and experiment for 39 baryon states: $3.6\,(2.7)\%$
- $\gamma^\ast p \to \Delta(1600)$ electric quadrupole form factor $G_E^\ast$ shows a zero at $Q^2 \approx 4 m_p^2$ in the complete result
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment of $\Delta(1600)$ is 45% of that of $\Delta(1232)$
- Data available for $\gamma^\ast p \to \Delta^+$ for $0 \leq Q^2 \lesssim 8$ GeV$^2$
- Roper resonance core mass reduced by approximately 20% by meson cloud

## Context

This perspective extends the continuum bound-state approach developed by Roberts and collaborators over decades, building on the pioneering work of Cornwall (1981) on gluon mass generation and Faddeev (1960) on three-body scattering. It directly addresses the "Millennium Problem" posed by the Clay Mathematics Institute regarding the origin of visible mass. The work enables future experimental programs at Jefferson Lab to chart the origin and distribution of mass through resonance electroproduction, and provides predictions for the $\gamma^\ast p \to \Delta(1600)$ transition form factors that can be tested against existing data from Trivedi (2018) and Burkert (2019).
