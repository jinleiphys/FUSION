# Local alpha-removal strength in the mean-field approximation

> The local alpha strength is proposed to quantify the possibility to form an alpha particle at a specific location inside the nucleus. It also provides the strength of ground and excited states in the residual nuclei after the removal of the alpha particle. We use the Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS (HF+BCS) method in the calculation of the local alpha strengths for Sn isotopes. The local alpha strengths are easily calculable and the results are consistent with recent experimental data for Sn isotopes.

## Key claim

The local alpha-removal strength, calculated with the mean-field approximation and neglecting rearrangement, produces a universal surface-region value for Sn isotopes that is consistent with experimental alpha-knockout cross sections only when the increase in nuclear radius with neutron number is taken into account, not through the raw strength at a fixed radial coordinate.

## Method

The authors define a local alpha-removal strength operator using a point-alpha approximation (delta-function wavefunction for the alpha particle) and compute its expectation value in the Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS (HF+BCS) framework for even-even Sn isotopes. They neglect rearrangement of the mean fields upon alpha removal, approximate the residual nucleus states using the same mean-field Hamiltonian as the parent nucleus, and use the SkM* Skyrme energy density functional on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with 1.0 fm spacing inside a sphere of radius 12 fm.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing gaps determined from experimental mass differences: 1.4 MeV for A=112, 1.2 MeV for A=116, 1.4 MeV for A=120, and 1.3 MeV for A=124.
- The peak of the local alpha-removal strength to the ground state is located at r = 4.7–4.8 fm for all Sn isotopes studied.
- The peak height of the ground-state local alpha-removal strength is similar for A=112, 116, and 120, but is "apparently smaller" for A=124.
- Doubling the neutron pairing gap increases the peak height of the ground-state local alpha-removal strength by about 50 percent.
- The alpha density from Typel (2014) peaks at 6.5–7.5 fm, while the present calculation's peak is at significantly smaller radius.

## Context

This work extends the localization function approach of Reinhard et al. (2011) by proposing a direct measure of four-nucleon proximity rather than the same-spin-isospin pair correlation that the localization function quantifies. The authors note that the localization function does not check whether four nucleons exist next to each other, as shown by Khoa et al. (2022). The calculation is motivated by the quasifree alpha-knockout experiment on Sn isotopes by Tanaka et al. (2021), which showed a monotonic decrease in cross section with increasing neutron number. The present results enable a simple mean-field interpretation of those data without explicit alpha clustering correlations.
