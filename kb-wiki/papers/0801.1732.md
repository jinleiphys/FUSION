# Three-Body Recombination of Identical Bosons with a Large Positive Scattering Length at Nonzero Temperature

> For identical bosons with a large scattering length, the dependence of the 3-body recombination rate on the collision energy is determined in the zero-range limit by universal functions of a single scaling variable. There are six scaling functions for angular momentum zero and one scaling function for each higher partial wave. We calculate these universal functions by solving the Skorniakov--Ter-Martirosian equation. The results for the 3-body recombination as a function of the collision energy are in good agreement with previous results from solving the 3-body Schroedinger equation for 4He atoms. The universal scaling functions can be used to calculate the 3-body recombination rate at nonzero temperature. We obtain an excellent fit to the data from the Innsbruck group for 133Cs atoms with a large positive scattering length.

## Key claim

The paper provides definitive zero-range predictions for the three-body recombination rate of identical bosons with large positive scattering length at nonzero temperature, calculated by solving the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation. The authors claim their universal scaling functions give an excellent fit to the Innsbruck group's data for 133Cs atoms, but note that the zero-range results are exact only in the limit of zero range and corrections from nonzero range are suppressed by powers of ℓ/|a|.

## Method

The authors calculated universal scaling functions for three-body recombination by solving the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian equation in the zero-range limit, expressing the hyperangular average of the recombination rate in terms of atom-dimer elastic scattering phase shifts. For angular momentum J=0, they used Efimov's Radial Law to express S-matrix elements in terms of universal functions of the scaling variable x = (m a^2 E/ℏ^2)^(1/2) and the Efimov parameter κ*, while for J≥1 the scattering rates depend only on x. The nonzero temperature rate constant was obtained by performing a Boltzmann average over collision energy.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is e^(π/s₀) ≈ 22.7, where s₀ ≈ 1.00624
- The maximum three-body recombination rate at threshold is K_max = 6 C_max ℏ a^4/m, where C_max ≈ 67.1
- The analytic threshold result for K^(0)(E=0) = (768 π^2 (4π - 3√3) sin^2[s₀ ln(a/a_*0)])/(sinh^2(π s₀) + cos^2[s₀ ln(a/a_*0)]) × ℏ a^4/m, with sinh^2(π s₀) ≈ 139
- The Efimov parameter relation a_*0 ≈ 0.32 κ_*^(-1)
- The Innsbruck group observed a resonant enhancement at a ≈ -850 a₀ and a local minimum near a ≈ 210 a₀ at 200 nK
- The analytic result for A(x) as x→0 is A(x) → (8(4π-3√3))/(3√3 sinh(π s₀)) x^4

## Context

This work extends the universal approach to three-body recombination pioneered by Efimov (1970, 1979) and provides definitive zero-range predictions that improve upon earlier calculations by Jonsell (2006), Yamashita, Frederico, and Tomio (2006), and others that involved uncontrolled approximations. The results enable calculation of three-body recombination rate constants at nonzero temperature for any system of identical bosons with large scattering length, and the authors validate their approach by comparing with previous calculations for 4He atoms and fitting experimental data from the Innsbruck group (Grimm, 2006) for 133Cs atoms.
