# Kaon Condensation in High Density Quark Matter

> We point out that the problem of kaon condensation in dense hadronic matter can be addressed in perturbative QCD. Indeed, perturbative calculations suggest that negative kaons are condensed in high density quark matter if the electroweak interaction is taken into account. This observation sheds new light on the proposal that the low density hyperon and high density quark phase of QCD are continuously connected.

# Kaon Condensation in High Density Quark Matter

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD calculations suggest that negative kaons are condensed in high density quark matter when electroweak interactions are included, but the conclusion carries significant uncertainty because the numerical values of the kaon mass and the electron chemical potential are very close, and the result depends sensitively on the value of the superconducting gap, the running coupling scale, and several other corrections that are not fully determined.

## Method

The author constructs the low-energy effective Lagrangian for pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of three-flavor quark matter, with coefficients determined from perturbative QCD. The thermodynamic potential including a kaon condensate is minimized with respect to quark fractions, electron chemical potential, and condensate amplitude, enforcing beta equilibrium and charge neutrality. The electron chemical potential and kaon mass are compared as functions of the light quark Fermi momentum, using one-loop running coupling at two different scales and varying quark masses within their experimental ranges.

## Key numbers

- Kaon mass in CFL phase: \(m_{K^{-}} \simeq 5\) MeV at \(\mu = 500\) MeV and \(m_{K^{-}} \simeq 2.5\) MeV at \(\mu = 1000\) MeV
- Pion decay constant in CFL phase: \(f_\pi^2 = \frac{21-8\log(2)}{18} \frac{\mu^2}{2\pi^2}\)
- Coefficient in Goldstone boson mass term: \(c = \frac{3\Delta^2}{2\pi^2}\)
- Goldstone boson velocity: \(v_\pi^2 = 1/3\)
- Gap formula for \(N_f=3\): \(\Delta = 512\pi^4 2^{-1/3}(2/3)^{5/2}\mu g^{-5} \exp\left(-\frac{3\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}g}\right)\)
- Electron chemical potential without kaon condensation: \(\mu_e \simeq \frac{m_s^2}{4p_F} = \frac{m_s^2}{4\pi^{2/3}\rho_B^{1/3}}\)
- Quark mass ranges used: \(m_u = (3-5)\) MeV, \(m_d = (6-8)\) MeV, \(m_s = (120-150)\) MeV
- QCD scale parameter: \(\Lambda_{QCD} = 238\) MeV, corresponding to \(\alpha_s(m_\tau) = 0.35\)
- Condition for CFL stability: \(m_s < \sqrt{2p_F\Delta}\), satisfied for \(p_F > 500\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the color-flavor-locking proposal of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the effective Lagrangian approach of Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999) and Son and Stephanov (1999). It contrasts with the original kaon condensation mechanism of Kaplan and Nelson (1986), which was driven by the attractive kaon-nucleon interaction, whereas here condensation occurs because CFL inverts the Goldstone boson spectrum and Fermi surface kinematics suppress the kaon mass. The result supports the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schaefer and Wilczek (1999) and suggests that the density where it becomes more useful to describe matter in terms of quarks rather than hadrons coincides with the point where kaon and pion masses become degenerate.
