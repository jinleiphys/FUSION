# Low-energy constants and relativity in peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering

> In our recent works we derived a chiral $O(q^4)$ two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential (TPEP) formulated in a relativistic baryon (RB) framework, expressed in terms of the so called low energy constants (LECs) and functions representing covariant loop integrations. We showed that the expansion of these functions in powers of the inverse of the nucleon mass reproduces most of the terms of the TPEP derived from the heavy baryon (HB) formalism, but such an expansion is ill defined and does not converge at large distances. In the present work we perform a study of the phase shifts in nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) scattering for peripheric waves ($L\geq 3$), which are sensitive to the tail of the potential. We assess quantitatively the differences between the RB and HB results, as well as variations due to different values of the LECs. By demanding consistency between the LECs used in $πN$ and $NN$ scattering we show how $NN$ peripheral phase shifts could constrain these values. We demonstrate that this idea, first proposed by the Nijmegen group, favors a smaller value for the LEC $c_3$ than the existing ones, when considering the TPEP up to order $q^4$ in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that peripheral nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts (L ≥ 3) can constrain the low-energy constants (LECs) of chiral perturbation theory, and that this constraint favors a smaller value for the LEC c₃ than existing determinations, when using a relativistic baryon (RB) chiral O(q⁴) two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) rather than the heavy baryon (HB) expansion.

## Method

The author computed phase shifts for peripheral NN partial waves (L ≥ 3) using the relativistic baryon (RB) chiral O(q⁴) TPEP derived in prior work, and compared these to phase shifts from the heavy baryon (HB) expansion. The study assessed quantitative differences between RB and HB results and varied the LECs (c₁, c₂, c₃, c₄, and d_i) to test consistency with πN scattering constraints, following the approach first proposed by the Nijmegen group. The phase function (variable phase) method was used to isolate contributions from different radial ranges of the potential.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar tensor potential V_T at r = 1.0 fm is -3.9311 × 10¹ MeV (total), with the two-loop term V_T^(b) contributing -4.5479 × 10⁰ MeV (RB) versus -5.4716 × 10⁰ MeV (HB), a difference of about 20% at that distance.
- The isoscalar tensor potential V_T at r = 3.0 fm is -1.5170 × 10⁻² MeV (total), with V_T^(b) contributing -6.6798 × 10⁻⁴ MeV (RB) versus -7.0387 × 10⁻⁴ MeV (HB), a difference of about 5%.
- The isovector central potential W_C at r = 1.0 fm is -5.3305 × 10¹ MeV (total), with the two-loop term W_C^(b) contributing -5.8826 × 10⁰ MeV (RB) versus -5.5372 × 10⁰ MeV (HB).
- The isovector central potential W_C at r = 4.0 fm is -3.0848 × 10⁻³ MeV (total), with W_C^(b) contributing -9.0177 × 10⁻⁵ MeV (RB) versus -5.5440 × 10⁻⁵ MeV (HB), a difference of nearly a factor of two at large distances.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior derivation of a relativistic baryon chiral O(q⁴) TPEP (Higa, 2004) and compares it to the heavy baryon formalism of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Kaiser et al. (1997-1998). The study addresses the proposal by the Nijmegen group (1999, 2003) to extract LECs from NN partial wave analyses, which gave much smaller statistical uncertainties than πN determinations. The results show that remaining discrepancies between RB and HB expanded potentials are numerically small for peripheral phase shifts, and that consistency between πN and NN scattering favors a smaller c₃ value than existing determinations from πN data alone.
