# Wave functions of multiquark hadrons from representations of the symmetry groups $S_n$

> Construction of the wave functions of multiquark hadrons by traditional method based on tensor products of colors, flavors, spins (and orbital) parts becomes quite complex when quark numbers grow $n=5,6...12$, as it gets difficult to satisfy requirements of Fermi statistics. Our novel approach is focused directly on representations of the permutation symmetry generators. After showing how $C_3$ is manifested in the wave functions of (excited) baryons, we use it to construct the wave functions for a set of pentaquarks and hexaquarks (n=5,6). We also have some partial results for larger systems, with $n=9$ and 12, and even beyond that as far as $n=24$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel method for constructing fully antisymmetric wave functions of multiquark hadrons by directly building representations of the permutation symmetry group generators \(P_{12}\) and \(P_{cycle}\), rather than the traditional approach of combining tensor products of color, flavor, and spin sectors; the method is demonstrated for pentaquarks and hexaquarks (n=5,6) with partial results for n=9, 12, and up to n=24, but the authors note that for larger systems the monom basis dimensions become too large for explicit Mathematica computation.

## Method

The authors first construct a basis of all possible "monoms" (tensor products of color, flavor, and spin indices for each quark) and then generate all permutations of these monoms under the symmetric group \(S_n\). They orthogonalize this set using Gram-Schmidt to obtain a reduced "good basis" of linearly independent vectors, then compute the matrix representations of the two group generators \(P_{12}\) and \(P_{cycle}\) acting on this basis for each sector (color, flavor, spin) separately. Finally, they take the Kronecker product of these sector matrices, diagonalize both generators simultaneously, and identify common eigenvectors with the required eigenvalue (-1 for fermion antisymmetry) as the desired wave functions.

## Key numbers

* For the \(uuuddd\) hexaquark with maximal spin \(S=3\), the color monom space of \(3^6 = 729\) dimensions reduces to a "good basis" of only 5 linearly independent vectors after orthogonalization; the flavor sector similarly reduces to 5 independent vectors.
* The total Hilbert space for the \(S=3\) hexaquark color-flavor system is \(5 \times 5 = 25\) dimensional, and exactly one fully antisymmetric wave function is found.
* For hexaquarks with \(S=1\) and \(S_z=0\), the spin sector reduces to 9 independent vectors after orthogonalization, yielding \(5 \times 5 \times 9 = 225\) dimensional total space for color-flavor-spin.
* For the \(uuuddd\) hexaquark with \(S=0\) and \(S=2\), the number of antisymmetric wave functions found is zero.
* The total number of monoms for n=6 \(u,d\) quarks is \(3^6 \cdot 2^6 \cdot 2^6 = 2,985,984\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for constructing wave functions of excited baryons with \(L=1\) and \(L=2\) (Miesch and Shuryak, 2023) to multiquark systems with n=5,6 and beyond. The approach directly addresses the complexity that arises when using traditional tensor product methods for n=5,6...12 quarks, as noted in earlier work by Isgur and Karl (1978) who did not explicitly construct wave functions enforcing Fermi statistics for excited baryons. The method reproduces the explicit hexaquark wave function previously derived by Kim, Kim, and Oka (2020) for the \(uuuddd\) \(S=3\) state, and enables systematic construction of wave functions for pentaquarks, hexaquarks with arbitrary spin and orbital momentum, and potentially larger systems such as tribaryons (n=9) and tetrabaryons (n=12).
