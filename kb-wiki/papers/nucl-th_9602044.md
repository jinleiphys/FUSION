# Theoretical Predictions for Pionium Searches

> Characteristic properties of pionium $A_{2π}$ and associated low energy s--wave cross sections $σ(π^0π^0\to π^0π^0)$, $σ(π^+π^-\toπ^0π^0)$ and $σ(π^0π^0\to π^+π^-)$ are investigated with a coupled channels potential model. Some experimental results and conclusions are to be reconsidered.

# Theoretical Predictions for Pionium Searches

## Key claim
The coupled channels potential model predicts that the experimental pionium signal claimed by Afanasyev et al. (1993) is not necessarily due to pionium formation; instead, the observed π⁺π⁻ surplus can be explained by initial state interactions of the π⁺π⁻ pair with the Coulomb field of target nuclei combined with the strongly energy-dependent π⁺π⁻ → π⁰π⁰ cross section.

## Method
The authors constructed a coupled channels potential model for the π⁺π⁻ and π⁰π⁰ systems, using quantum inversion of experimental and theoretical hadronic s-wave phase shifts for isospin T=0 and T=2 from Froggatt (1977) and Lohse (1990) in the elastic domain up to M_{ππ} ≤ 1 GeV. The potential matrix was built from isospin projectors and the Coulomb interaction, and the Schrödinger equation was solved numerically with asymptotic matching to Coulomb and Bessel functions to determine the hadronic S-matrix and resonance properties for the ground state (n=1) and excited states (n=2–5) of pionium.

## Key numbers
- Pionium ground state (n=1) binding energy: E_B = 2.445 keV (Froggatt input) and 2.407 keV (Lohse input), compared to 1.858 keV for the pure Coulomb 1S ground state.
- Pionium ground state resonance width: FWHM = 14 eV (Froggatt input) and 20 eV (Lohse input).
- Pionium ground state lifetime: τ = 4.7 × 10⁻¹⁷ sec (Froggatt input) and 3.3 × 10⁻¹⁷ sec (Lohse input).
- Pionium resonance cross sections are large, approximately 4 barns, and equal in magnitude for all resonances n=1–5.
- Resonance widths follow the rule Γ₁/n² eV.
- The threshold reaction cross section peak for π⁺π⁻ → π⁰π⁰ has a cross section of approximately 1 barn and a width of approximately 1 MeV.
- The inversion potentials reproduce input phase functions within 0.1 degrees for all energies.

## Context
This work extends the coupled channels treatment of dimesonic systems to pionium, using quantum inversion of phase shifts from Froggatt (1977) and Lohse (1990) to construct the hadronic interaction. The authors challenge the interpretation of Afanasyev et al. (1993), who claimed experimental observation of pionium, by showing that their calculated lifetimes are two orders of magnitude smaller than those quoted in that experiment and that the lifetime scaling follows τ_n = τ₁ n² rather than the n³ dependence assumed by Afanasyev et al. (1993). The results enable refined experimental searches for pionium by clarifying the expected resonance parameters and identifying the threshold reaction peak as a potential background mechanism.
