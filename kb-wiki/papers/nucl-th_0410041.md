# Charm elliptic flow from quark coalescence dynamics

> From covariant transport theory, a significant ~10% light quark elliptic flow at RHIC implies an elliptic flow of similar magnitude for charm quarks, at moderately large pT > 2.5 - 3 GeV. At lower transverse momenta, charm quark elliptic flow reduces progressively, reminiscent of the mass ordering pattern in ideal hydrodynamics. From the quark flows we predict the elliptic flow of D mesons at RHIC via quark coalescence. The large parton opacities needed to generate the light quark flow also lead to substantial ~40-50% secondary charm production.

# Charm elliptic flow from quark coalescence dynamics

## Key claim

From covariant transport theory, a significant ~10% light quark elliptic flow at RHIC implies an elliptic flow of similar magnitude for charm quarks at moderately large pT > 2.5-3 GeV, but only under the assumption of large parton opacities (sigma_gg->gg = 10 mb, about three times the perturbative QCD estimate) that also lead to substantial ~40-50% secondary charm production.

## Method

The study uses Molnar's Parton Cascade algorithm (MPC) to solve a Lorentz-covariant Boltzmann equation with elastic 2->2 and inelastic 2->2 QCD processes for a system of massless gluons, massless light (u,d) and strange quarks/antiquarks, and charm quarks/antiquarks with mass Mc = 1.2 GeV, applied to Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm (~30% central). The parton initial conditions use leading-order pQCD minijet distributions with a K-factor of 2, GRV98LO PDFs, and Q^2 = pT^2 (Q^2 = s-hat for charm), with a total parton dN(b=0)/dy = 2000 at midrapidity, and the transverse density distribution proportional to the binary collision distribution for two Woods-Saxon distributions.

## Key numbers

- Light quark elliptic flow reaches approximately 10% at freezeout, in quantitative agreement with prior transport calculations
- Charm quark elliptic flow at high pT > 3 GeV is about the same magnitude as light quark v2 (~10%), with the heavy-light difference disappearing by pT ~ 2.5-3 GeV
- At low pT < 2 GeV, charm quark elliptic flow is suppressed relative to light parton v2, consistent with mass ordering in ideal hydrodynamics
- Secondary charm production is 40-50% from inelastic processes
- The total elastic cross section for gg->gg is taken as 10 mb, about three times the perturbative QCD estimate
- D meson elliptic flow predicted to saturate at approximately 20% at pT ~ 3 GeV, which is 50% higher than the pion/kaon saturation pT of 2 GeV
- Above pT ~ 3-4 GeV, fragmentation contributions reduce D and D_s v2 to approximately 10%
- Strangeness is enhanced about five-fold relative to initial conditions
- Light antiquark (u-bar, d-bar) and strange quark (s, s-bar) distributions become nearly equal in the final state

## Context

This work extends the covariant parton transport theory of Molnar (2004) and earlier studies of light quark elliptic flow (Binv2, v2) to charm quarks for the first time at RHIC energies. It builds on schematic scenarios for charm elliptic flow explored by charmcoal and Texascharm, and uses the quark coalescence formulas from charmcoal to predict D and D_s meson elliptic flow. The results contradict the assumption of zero charm rescattering (scenario i) and also show that the extreme assumption v2_charm = v2_light (scenario ii) only applies at high pT > 2.5-3 GeV. The paper enables experimental tests of charm thermalization at RHIC through D meson elliptic flow measurements, and highlights that dynamical coalescence approaches (dyncoal) may reduce the predicted D meson v2 by almost half above pT ~ 3.5 GeV due to the dominance of fragmentation over coalescence.
