# Transverse momentum fluctuations as a probe of thermalization and collective dynamics of QGP

> We study fluctuations of mean transverse momentum per particle ($[p_T]$) in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We show that the steep fall in the variance of transverse momentum fluctuation in ultracentral Pb+Pb collision serves as a natural consequence of the thermalization of the QGP medium. We study the correlation between the spectra and $[p_T]$ which maps these fluctuations differentially and dubbed as $v_0(p_T)$. We highlight the importance of $v_0(p_T)$ showing that it plays similar role as anisotropic flow when probing the collective nature of QGP.

# Transverse momentum fluctuations as a probe of thermalization and collective dynamics of QGP

## Key claim
The steep fall in the variance of mean transverse momentum fluctuations in ultracentral Pb+Pb collisions serves as a natural consequence of thermalization of the QGP medium, specifically arising from the rapid decline of impact parameter fluctuations at fixed multiplicity. The new observable v0(pT) plays a similar role to anisotropic flow when probing the collective nature of QGP, but depends only on particle momenta magnitude rather than their direction.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of 1000 Pb+Pb collisions at fixed impact parameter b=0, modeling the joint distribution of [pT] and Nch as a 2D correlated Gaussian characterized by five parameters. They decomposed the variance of [pT] at fixed Nch into two contributions: intrinsic fluctuations at fixed b and impact parameter fluctuations at fixed Nch, with the latter calculated using Bayes' theorem to obtain P(b|Nch). They then constructed the correlation between particle spectra and [pT] as v0(pT) and compared hydrodynamic results with smooth and fluctuating initial conditions, including shear and bulk viscosity effects.

## Key numbers
- Relative dynamical fluctuations of [pT] are around 1%
- The model was fitted to ATLAS data for the 20% most central Pb+Pb events
- Results for v0(pT)/v0 were shown at b=0 and b=7 fm
- The acceptance correction factor CA was used to reproduce data across three pT windows: 0.5<pT<5 GeV (input), 0.5<pT<2 GeV, and 1<pT<2 GeV
- The Pearson correlation coefficient r between Nch and [pT] returned a very large value from the model fit

## Context
This work extends the study of mean transverse momentum fluctuations as a probe of QGP thermalization, building on prior work by Samanta (2023) on the Gaussian modeling of [pT]-Nch correlations and by Teaney and Schenke (2020) on the proposal of spectra-[pT] correlations. The observable v0(pT) shows similar mass hierarchy to elliptic flow v2(pT) when studied for identified particles, as shown in prior work by Parida (2024), and can be measured experimentally through methods similar to those used by ALICE (2025) and ATLAS (2025). The results enable future extension to small systems such as p+Pb and p+p collisions to further understand QGP dynamics.
