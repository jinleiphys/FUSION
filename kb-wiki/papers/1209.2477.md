# The EMC Effect and Short-Range Correlations

> We overview the progress made in studies of EMC and short range correlation (SRC) effects with the special emphasis given to the recent observation of the correlation between the slope of the EMC ratio at Bjorken x<1 and the scale factor of the same ratio at x>1 that measures the strength of the SRCs in nuclei. This correlation may indicate the larger modification of nucleons with higher momentum thus making the nucleon virtuality as the most relevant parameter of medium modifications. To check this conjecture we study the implication of several properties of high momentum component of the nuclear wave function on the characteristics of EMC effect. We observe two main reasons for the EMC-SRC correlation: first, the decrease of the contribution from the nuclear mean field due to the increase, with A, the fraction of the high momentum component of nuclear wave function. Second, the increase of the medium modification of nucleons in SRC. Our main prediction however is the increase of the proton contribution to the EMC effect for large A asymmetric nuclei. This prediction is based on the recent observation of the strong dominance of pn SRCs in the high momentum component of nuclear wave function. Our preliminary calculation based on this prediction of the excess of energetic and modified protons in large A nuclei describes reasonably well the main features of the observed EMC-SRC correlation.

# The EMC Effect and Short-Range Correlations

## Key claim
The observed correlation between the slope of the EMC ratio at Bjorken x<1 and the scale factor a₂ at x>1 (measuring short-range correlation strength) may arise from two mechanisms: the decrease of nuclear mean-field contribution as the high-momentum component increases with mass number A, and the increased medium modification of nucleons in SRCs. The main prediction is an increase of the proton contribution to the EMC effect for large A asymmetric nuclei, based on the strong dominance of proton-neutron SRCs.

## Method
The author develops a light-cone (LC) approximation model of deep inelastic eA scattering in which the nuclear DIS structure function is expressed as an integral over the LC momentum fraction α of the bound nucleon, with the high-momentum component constructed according to a 2N SRC model that includes asymmetry for protons and neutrons. The bound nucleon DIS structure function incorporates nuclear modification via a color screening model, where the suppression of point-like configurations (PLCs) in bound nucleons is calculated perturbatively using the parameter κ = |⟨U_A⟩/ΔE_A|, with ⟨U_A⟩ the average nuclear potential energy per nucleon and ΔE_A ≈ 0.6–1 GeV the typical nucleon excitation energy. The suppression factor δ_A(p²) = 1/(1+κ)² depends on the bound nucleon momentum p and separation energy ε_A.

## Key numbers
- The EMC ratio R_EMC is measured for 0.3 < x < 0.8, with substantial depletion below unity observed in first experiments.
- No Q² dependence of R_EMC depletion was observed for 4 < Q² < 6 GeV² at Jefferson Lab.
- The typical energy for nucleon excitations within the nucleus is ΔE_A ≈ 0.6–1 GeV.
- For ¹²C, the fraction of high-momentum (>k_F) protons is 20% and neutrons 20%; for ⁵⁶Fe, protons 27% and neutrons 23%; for ¹⁹⁷Au, protons 31% and neutrons 20%.
- The suppression factor for PLCs is δ_A(p²) = 1/[1 + (p²/M + 2ε_A)/ΔE_A]².
- The correlation between the EMC slope (−dR_EMC/dx) and the SRC strength a₂ is observed, with recent measurements indicating possible non-linearity.

## Context
This work extends the line of EMC effect studies following the original discovery by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC1, 1983) and subsequent experiments at SLAC and Jefferson Lab (EMC5, EMC7). It builds on the observation by Larry1 and Larry2 of a correlation between EMC slope and SRC strength, and on the finding of strong pn SRC dominance (by a factor of 20) over pp and nn SRCs in the momentum range k_F < p < 600 MeV/c (isosrc, EIP4). The model incorporates the color screening approach of FS85 and the light-cone formalism of FS8188. The prediction of enhanced proton contribution to the EMC effect in asymmetric nuclei can be tested in semi-inclusive A(e,e'N)X reactions with backward spectator detection, as discussed in tagged, polext, and Cosyn.
