# Topological charge fluctuations in the Glasma

> The early-time evolution of the system generated in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions is dominated by the presence of strong color fields known as Glasma fields. These can be described following the classical approach embodied in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, which approximates QCD in the high gluon density regime. In this framework we perform an analytical first-principles calculation of the two-point correlator of the divergence of the Chern-Simons current at proper time $τ\!=\!0^+$, which characterizes the early fluctuations of axial charge density in the plane transverse to the collision axis. This object plays a crucial role in the description of anomalous transport phenomena such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect. We compare our results to those obtained under the Glasma Graph approximation, which assumes gluon field correlators to obey Gaussian statistics. While this approach proves to be equivalent to the exact calculation in the limit of short transverse separations, important differences arise at larger distances, where our expression displays a remarkably slower fall-off than the Glasma Graph result ($1/r^4$ vs.\ $1/r^8$ power-law decay). This discrepancy emerges from the non-linear dynamics mapping the Gaussianly-distributed color source densities onto the Glasma fields, encoded in the classical Yang-Mills equations. Our results support the conclusions reached in a previous work, where we found indications that the color screening of correlations in the transverse plane occurs at relatively large distances.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical first-principles calculation of the two-point correlator of the divergence of the Chern-Simons current at proper time τ = 0⁺ in the Glasma, showing that the exact result decays as 1/r⁴ at large transverse separations, which is slower than the 1/r⁸ decay predicted by the Glasma Graph approximation that assumes Gaussian statistics for gluon field correlators.

## Method

The calculation is performed within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, using a generalized McLerran-Venugopalan model with explicit impact parameter dependence and relaxed transverse locality. The classical Yang-Mills equations are solved at proper time τ = 0⁺ with two independent color source distributions representing the colliding nuclei, and the two-point correlator of the divergence of the Chern-Simons current is computed analytically by evaluating correlators of four gluon fields at two transverse positions, involving non-trivial projections of correlators of four Wilson lines in the adjoint representation.

## Key numbers

- The exact calculation yields a power-law decay of the two-point correlator as 1/r⁴ at large transverse separations, compared to the Glasma Graph approximation result of 1/r⁸.
- The expectation value of the divergence of the Chern-Simons current at a single point is zero, confirming global CP symmetry of the Glasma state.
- The building block for the two-point correlator involves the dipole function in the adjoint representation, C^(2)_adj(x_⊥, y_⊥) = exp{ -g² (N_c/2) Γ(r_⊥) λ̄(x⁻, b_⊥) }, where Γ(r_⊥) = 2(L(0_⊥) - L(r_⊥)).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous first-principles calculation of the energy-momentum tensor correlator in the Glasma (Albacete 2018) to the topological charge density, and directly compares with the Glasma Graph approximation of Lappi (2017) which assumed Gaussian statistics for gluon fields. The results support the conclusion that color screening of correlations in the transverse plane occurs at relatively large distances, and provide a theoretical foundation for initial conditions in hydrodynamic simulations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect and other anomalous transport phenomena in heavy ion collisions.
