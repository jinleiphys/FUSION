# A new Microscopic Model for J/ψ Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The model can simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of J/ψ at low transverse momentum in PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV, but only when the QGP temperature has fallen below a dissociation temperature \(T_{diss}\); at the highest \(p_T\) values, deviations between experimental values and predictions remain due to several identified shortcomings.

## Method

The authors follow individual charm and anticharm quarks from their creation in hard collisions through the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) described by the EPOS2 event generator, using a potential between \(c\bar{c}\) pairs adjusted to lattice gauge data. The annihilation and creation of J/ψ is described by a density matrix approach whose time evolution is studied in the expanding system, employing a temperature-dependent width in the Gaussian parametrization of the J/ψ Wigner density that reproduces the in-medium radius from a potential model based on lattice data. Collisions of heavy quarks with QGP constituents are treated using the MC@sHQ model previously applied to open charm mesons.

## Key numbers

- PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV are used for comparison with experimental data
- The dissociation temperature \(T_{diss}\) is the threshold above which J/ψ are not stable according to lattice gauge calculations
- The model simultaneously reproduces \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) at low \(p_T\) where enhancement has been observed

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system approach to quarkonia dynamics, similar in spirit to recent treatments that study the time evolution of the quarkonia density matrix. For pp collisions, the production follows the Wigner-coalescence approximation of Song (2017), which describes J/ψ, ψ', and χ_c. The MC@sHQ model for heavy quark interactions with QGP constituents was previously applied to open charm mesons by Gossiaux (2008, 2009). The new model enables microscopic tracking of individual heavy quarks through the QGP and can be improved by including gluon-dissociation mechanisms, contributions from excited charmonium states and B meson decays, corona contributions, and dipole interactions between \(c\bar{c}\) pairs and QGP partons.
