# Neutrino emission due to Cooper-pair recombination in neutron stars revisited

> Neutrino emission in processes of breaking and formation of neutron and proton Cooper pairs is calculated within the Larkin-Migdal-Leggett approach for a superfluid Fermi liquid. We demonstrate explicitly that the Fermi-liquid renormalization respects the Ward identity and assures the weak vector current conservation. The systematic expansion of the emissivities for small temperatures and nucleon Fermi velocity, v_{F,i}, i=n,p, is performed. Both neutron and proton processes are mainly controlled by the axial-vector current contributions, which are not strongly changed in the superfluid matter. Thus, compared to earlier calculations the total emissivity of processes on neutrons paired in the 1S_0 state is suppressed by a factor ~(0.9-1.2) v_{F,n}^2. A similar suppression factor (~v_{F,p}^2) arises for processes on protons.

## Key claim

The total emissivity of neutrino emission from Cooper-pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes on neutrons paired in the 1S0 state is suppressed by a factor of approximately (0.9–1.2) vF,n² compared to earlier calculations, and a similar suppression factor of about vF,p² arises for protons, because the leading contribution comes from the axial-vector current rather than the vector current, which is cancelled by vertex corrections required by the Ward identity.

## Method

The authors used the Larkin-Migdal-Leggett formalism for superfluid Fermi liquids, which properly distinguishes interactions in the particle-particle and particle-hole channels, unlike the Nambu-Gorkov matrix formalism used in prior work. They solved the Larkin-Migdal equations for the full vertices (normal, hole, and anomalous) including both vector and axial-vector weak currents, and calculated the imaginary part of the current-current correlator to obtain the neutrino emissivity. The calculation was performed for 1S0 neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing at low temperatures (T ≪ Δ), with systematic expansion in small temperature and nucleon Fermi velocity vF,i.

## Key numbers

- Neutron PBF emissivity suppression factor: ~(0.9–1.2) vF,n², where vF,n is the neutron Fermi velocity
- Proton PBF emissivity suppression factor: ~vF,p², where vF,p is the proton Fermi velocity
- For neutrons at n ~ n0, the suppression factor is approximately vF,n² (order 10⁻²), not vF,n⁴/20 ~ 10⁻³ as claimed by Leinson and Perez (2006)
- The axial-vector coupling constant used: gA = 1.26
- The vector coupling constants: gV⁽ⁿ⁾ = -1 for neutrons, gV⁽ᵖ⁾ = cV = 1 - 4 sin²θW ≈ 0.04 for protons
- The local charge for the axial-vector current eA ≈ 0.8–0.95, varying in different parameterizations

## Context

This work revisits the calculation of neutrino emission from Cooper-pair breaking and formation (PBF) processes in neutron stars, which were first evaluated by Flowers, Ruderman, and Sutherland (1976) and later by Voskresensky and Senatorov (1987). The paper directly addresses the observation by Kundu and Reddy (2004) and Leinson and Perez (2006) that previous calculations violated the Ward identity and vector current conservation. The authors show that while Leinson and Perez (2006) correctly identified the cancellation of vector current contributions, their claimed suppression factor of vF,n⁴/20 ~ 10⁻³ for neutrons is too severe; the actual suppression is only vF,n². Similarly, the result differs from Sedrakian and collaborators (2007), who claimed suppression by T/m* ~ 5×10⁻³. The correct treatment using the Larkin-Migdal-Leggett formalism yields a less dramatic suppression, which has significant implications for neutron star cooling models that include PBF processes alongside modified Urca processes.
