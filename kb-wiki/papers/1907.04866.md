# Deciphering the $z_g$ distribution in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> Within perturbative QCD, we develop a new picture for the parton shower generated by a jet propagating through a dense quark-gluon plasma. This picture combines in a simple, factorised, way multiple medium-induced parton branchings and standard vacuum-like emissions, with the phase-space for the latter constrained by the presence of the medium. We implement this picture as a Monte Carlo generator that we use to study two phenomenologically important observables: the jet nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and the $z_g$ distribution reflecting the jet substructure. In both cases, the outcome of our Monte Carlo simulations is in good agreement with the LHC measurements. We provide basic analytic calculations that help explaining the main features observed in the data. We find that the energy loss by the jet is increasing with the jet transverse momentum, due to a rise in the number of partonic sources via vacuum-like emissions. This is a key element in our description of both $R_{AA}$ and the $z_g$ distribution. For the latter, we identify two main nuclear effects: incoherent jet energy loss and hard medium-induced emissions. As the jet transverse momentum increases, we predict a qualitative change in the ratio between the $z_g$ distributions in PbPb and pp collisions: from increasing at small $z_g$, this ratio becomes essentially flat, or even slightly decreasing.

## Key claim

Within perturbative QCD, the authors develop a factorized picture of the parton shower in a quark-gluon plasma that combines vacuum-like emissions (VLEs) obeying angular ordering with medium-induced emissions (MIEs) that violate it, and they show that this picture reproduces LHC data for both the jet nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and the \(z_g\) distribution, with the caveat that the medium is modeled as a uniform "brick" of fixed size \(L\) and constant jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\).

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo generator that factorizes the in-medium parton shower into three sequential steps: (1) an angular-ordered vacuum-like cascade inside the medium using full DGLAP splitting functions, with the medium constraining the phase-space via the condition that the formation time of a VLE must be much shorter than the formation time of a MIE; (2) medium-induced emissions and transverse momentum broadening for each parton from the VLE cascade, treated as independent Markovian processes with the BDMPS-Z spectrum; (3) a final vacuum-like cascade outside the medium. The medium parameters \(\hat q\) and \(L\) are calibrated by comparing the predicted jet \(R_{AA}\) to ATLAS measurements.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic scale for multiple medium-induced branchings is \(\omega_\text{br} \equiv \alpha_{s,\text{med}}^2 \hat q L^2 / 2\).
* The upper limit on the energy of medium-induced emissions is \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2 / 2\).
* The saturation momentum from elastic collisions is \(Q_s^2 \equiv \hat q L\).
* The transition between "low energy" and "high energy" regimes for the \(z_g\) distribution occurs around \(p_T = \hat q L^2 / (2 z_c)\), where the Soft Drop cut is set to \(z_c = 0.1\) in compliance with experimental measurements by CMS (2017) and ALICE (2019).
* The formation time of a vacuum-like emission is \(t_f \simeq 2 / (\omega \theta^2)\) for a soft gluon of energy \(\omega\) and emission angle \(\theta\).
* The decoherence time for a pair of partons separated by angle \(\theta\) is \(t_\text{coh}(\theta) = (4 / (\hat q \theta^2))^{1/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the factorized picture for in-medium parton showers originally developed by Caucal, Iancu, and Soyez (2018) in the double-logarithmic approximation, now incorporating full DGLAP splitting functions, medium-induced radiation, and transverse momentum broadening. It builds on the effective theory of multiple medium-induced branchings by Blaizot, Iancu, and collaborators (2012-2014) and on the demonstration that vacuum-like emissions inside the medium preserve angular ordering (Caucal 2018). The results enable a unified description of jet quenching observables and predict a qualitative change in the ratio of \(z_g\) distributions between PbPb and pp collisions as jet transverse momentum increases, from rising at small \(z_g\) to becoming flat or slightly decreasing.
