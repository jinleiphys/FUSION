# Determining the $Θ^+$ quantum numbers through a Kaon induced reaction

> We study the $K^+p\to π^+KN$ reaction with kinematical condition suited to the production of the $Θ^+$ resonance. It is shown that in this reaction with the polarization experiment, a combined consideration of the strength at the peak and the angular dependence of cross section can help determine the $Θ^+$ quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The paper shows that by combining the strength of the resonance peak in the invariant mass distribution with the angular dependence of the cross section in a polarized $K^+p \to \pi^+KN$ reaction, one can determine the spin and parity of the $\Theta^+$ resonance, but only if the background contribution is properly accounted for, as the signal-to-background ratio and interference effects depend on the quantum numbers.

## Method

The authors calculated the $K^+p \to \pi^+KN$ reaction using a model with background terms (meson pole and contact term from effective chiral Lagrangians) plus resonance contributions for the $\Theta^+$ in the $K^+n$ final state. They considered three possible quantum number assignments for the $\Theta^+$: $s$-wave ($J^P = 1/2^-$), $p$-wave with $J^P = 1/2^+$, and $p$-wave with $J^P = 3/2^+$, with couplings fixed to a width of 20 MeV. They computed double differential cross sections at forward angle and at 90 degrees with polarized initial protons to identify distinctive signatures for each assignment.

## Key numbers

- The initial kaon three-momentum in the laboratory frame was set to $k_{in}(Lab) = 850$ MeV/$c$, corresponding to $\sqrt{s} = 1722$ MeV.
- The $\Theta^+$ width was taken as $\Gamma = 20$ MeV, used to fix the coupling constants $g_{K^+n}^2$, $\bar{g}_{K^+n}^2$, and $\tilde{g}_{K^+n}^2$ for the $s$-wave, $p$-wave $1/2^+$, and $p$-wave $3/2^+$ cases respectively.
- For the $I, J^P = 0, 1/2^+$ case, a clear resonance peak appears in the polarized cross section at 90 degrees, while for $s$-wave ($1/2^-$) the resonance term vanishes entirely in that observable.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of the $K^+p \to \pi^+KN$ reaction by Liu (2003) and Oh (2003) by including background contributions, which are important for signal-to-background ratio and interference effects. The study was motivated by the 2003 LEPS collaboration observation of a narrow $S=+1$ resonance around 1540 MeV at SPring-8 (Nakano, 2003). The results enable experimental determination of the $\Theta^+$ quantum numbers through polarization measurements, and the authors note that a combined study with $(\pi^-, K^-)$ reactions based on two-meson coupling is in progress (Hosaka, 2004).
