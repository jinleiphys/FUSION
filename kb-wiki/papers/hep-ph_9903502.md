# Unlocking Color and Flavor in Superconducting Strange Quark Matter

> We explore the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter with massless u and d quarks as a function of the strange quark mass m_s and the chemical potential mu for baryon number. Neglecting electromagnetism, we describe the different baryonic and quark matter phases at zero temperature. For quark matter, we support our model-independent arguments with a quantitative analysis of a model which uses a four-fermion interaction abstracted from single-gluon exchange. For any finite m_s, at sufficiently large mu we find quark matter in a color-flavor locked state which leaves a global vector-like SU(2)_{color+L+R} symmetry unbroken. As a consequence, chiral symmetry is always broken in sufficiently dense quark matter. As the density is reduced, for sufficiently large m_s we observe a first order transition from the color-flavor locked phase to a color superconducting phase analogous to that in two flavor QCD. At this unlocking transition chiral symmetry is restored. For realistic values of m_s our analysis indicates that chiral symmetry breaking may be present for all densities down to those characteristic of baryonic matter. This supports the idea that quark matter and baryonic matter may be continuously connected in nature. We map the gaps at the quark Fermi surfaces in the high density color-flavor locked phase onto gaps at the baryon Fermi surfaces at low densities.

## Key claim

For any finite strange quark mass, at sufficiently high chemical potential quark matter enters a color-flavor locked (CFL) state that breaks chiral symmetry; the transition from this CFL phase to a two-flavor color superconducting phase as density is reduced is necessarily first order because the mismatch between strange and light quark Fermi momenta prevents arbitrarily small mixed-flavor pairing gaps.

## Method

The authors analyze the zero-temperature phase diagram of QCD with massless up and down quarks as a function of strange quark mass and baryon chemical potential, using model-independent symmetry arguments supported by a quantitative mean-field treatment of a four-fermion interaction with quantum numbers abstracted from single-gluon exchange. They introduce a sharp momentum cutoff at Λ = 800 MeV and fix the coupling G = 7.5 GeV⁻² to reproduce a vacuum constituent light quark mass of 400 MeV, then solve gap equations for diquark condensates using an ansatz with five independent superconducting gap parameters that interpolates between the two-flavor and three-flavor limits.

## Key numbers

* The onset chemical potential for nuclear matter is μ₀ ∼ 300 MeV.
* The momentum cutoff used in the model is Λ = 800 MeV.
* The coupling constant is set to G = 7.5 GeV⁻², yielding a vacuum constituent light quark mass of 400 MeV.
* On the CFL side of the unlocking transition, the mixed-flavor gap Δ_us is of order 50–100 MeV when the coupling is calibrated to give a reasonable vacuum chiral condensate.
* The criterion for a nonzero u–s condensate is that the Fermi momentum mismatch (M_s² − M_u²)/(2μ) must be less than or of order Δ_us.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity in QCD from the two-flavor case studied by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and the three-massless-flavor case analyzed by the same authors, to the realistic situation with a nonzero strange quark mass. The paper supports the conjecture of Schäfer and Wilczek that quark matter and baryonic matter may be continuously connected, by showing that for realistic strange quark masses chiral symmetry breaking may persist down to densities characteristic of baryonic matter. The results enable mapping of quark Fermi surface gaps in the CFL phase onto baryon Fermi surface gaps in superfluid baryonic matter.
