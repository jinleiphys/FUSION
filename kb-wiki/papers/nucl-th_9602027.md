# Can Gluons Trace Baryon Number?

> QCD as a gauge non-Abelian theory imposes severe constraints on the structure of the baryon wave function. We point out that, contrary to a widely accepted belief, the traces of baryon number in a high-energy process can reside in a non-perturbative configuration of gluon fields, rather than in the valence quarks. We argue that this conjecture can be tested experimentally, since it can lead to substantial baryon asymmetry in the central rapidity region of ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions.

# Can Gluons Trace Baryon Number?

## Key claim

Contrary to the widely accepted belief that baryon number in high-energy processes is carried by valence quarks, the trace of baryon number may instead reside in a non-perturbative configuration of gluon fields (the "string junction"), a consequence of local gauge invariance in QCD. This conjecture can be tested experimentally through baryon asymmetry in the central rapidity region of ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the author notes that the dynamics of the stopping process is genuinely non-perturbative and a reliable way of computing the coupling constant entering the cross section expressions has not yet been found.

## Method

The author uses the gauge-invariant baryon wave function of QCD, which necessarily includes gluonic "string operators" connecting quark fields to a central point, to argue that baryon number is associated with the string junction rather than valence quarks. Quantitative estimates are derived using the topological expansion of QCD and Regge phenomenology, where the quarkless junction-antijunction state lies on a Regge trajectory with intercept α₀ᴶ(0) ≃ 1/2, and cross sections for single and double baryon stopping in pp collisions are computed via Mueller-Kancheli techniques.

## Key numbers

- The string junction intercept is estimated as α₀ᴶ(0) ≃ 1/2, derived from α_B(0) = 0 and α_R(0) = 1/2.
- The annihilation cross section for pp̄ is predicted to scale as (s/s₀)^(-1/2), compared to the s⁻¹ dependence from conventional baryon exchange.
- At √s = 53 GeV, the measured cross sections at y* = 0 and p_t = 0.6 GeV/c are: d³σ^p/d³p = 0.700 ± 0.162 mb GeV⁻² and d³σ^p̄/d³p = 0.430 ± 0.033 mb GeV⁻².
- The stopping fraction at √s = 53 GeV is f_st ≃ 25%.
- The ratio of multiplicities associated with proton versus antiproton production is estimated as R ≃ 1.05, compared to the measured value of R ≃ 1.1.
- Extrapolation to √s = 6 TeV (LHC Pb-Pb per nucleon-nucleon) with Δ = 0.08 yields a stopping fraction f_st ∼ 5%.
- The average inter-baryon distance in a lead nucleus is r ≃ 0.4 fm.
- The associated multiplicities for single and double baryon stopping are predicted as n^(1) ≃ (5/4) n^inel and n^(2) ≃ (3/2) n^inel.

## Context

This work extends the gauge-invariant formulation of baryon structure in QCD, following the topological expansion scheme of Veneziano (1979), and applies it to baryon number transport in high-energy collisions. The author notes that phenomenological approaches based on topological expansion but not explicitly including the string junction (Capella et al., 1994; Kaidalov, 1985; Cohen, 1993; CREV, 1993) appear to underestimate the pronounced baryon stopping observed experimentally in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies. The paper enables experimental tests at RHIC and LHC, and connects to the "Centauro" and "Chiron" cosmic-ray events interpreted by Bjorken and McLerran (1979) as evidence for dense quark matter with non-vanishing baryon number density.
