# Universal properties of bulk viscosity near the QCD phase transition

> We extract the bulk viscosity of hot quark-gluon matter in the presence of light quarks from the recent lattice data on the QCD equation of state. For that purpose we extend the sum rule analysis by including the contribution of light quarks. We also discuss the universal properties of bulk viscosity in the vicinity of a second order phase transition, as it might occur in the chiral limit of QCD at fixed strange quark mass and most likely does occur in two-flavor QCD. We point out that a chiral transition in the O(4) universality class at zero baryon density as well as the transition at the chiral critical point which belongs to the Z(2) universality class both lead to the critical behavior of bulk viscosity. In particular, the latter universality class implies the divergence of the bulk viscosity, which may be used as a signature of the critical point. We discuss the physical picture behind the dramatic increase of bulk viscosity seen in our analysis, and devise possible experimental tests of related phenomena.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of hot quark-gluon matter rises dramatically near the QCD phase transition, and this behavior is a general phenomenon not specific to the first-order transition in SU(3) gluodynamics. At zero baryon density, the bulk viscosity does not diverge but has a cusp belonging to the O(4) universality class, while at the chiral critical point at finite baryon density it diverges in the Z(2) universality class.

## Method

The authors extend a sum rule analysis based on low-energy theorems of broken scale invariance to QCD with 2+1 flavors (two light quarks and a strange quark), including the contribution of light quarks. They combine this sum rule with recent lattice data on the QCD equation of state from the RIKEN-BNL-Columbia-Bielefeld Collaboration, using an ansatz for the spectral density of the correlation function of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. The bulk viscosity is extracted by relating the sum rule to thermodynamic quantities such as the trace anomaly, entropy density, speed of sound, and the quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum contribution to the sum rule is given numerically as \(16 |\epsilon_v| (1 + \frac{3}{8} \cdot 1.6) \simeq (560\ {\rm MeV})^4 \simeq (3 \,T_c)^4\).
- The bulk viscosity is extracted using a scale parameter \(\omega_0\) estimated in the range \(500~{\rm MeV} \le \omega_0 \le 1500~{\rm MeV}\).
- The fermionic contribution to the trace anomaly is at least a factor 3 smaller than the gluonic contribution.
- For the O(4) universality class, the specific heat exponent is \(\alpha = -0.21\); for the Z(2) universality class, \(\alpha = 0.11\).
- At the chiral critical point, the specific heat diverges as \(c_v \sim h^{-\gamma/\beta\delta}\) with \(\gamma/\beta\delta \simeq 0.8\), where \(h\) parametrizes the distance to the critical point.

## Context

This work extends the sum rule analysis of Kharzeev, Kharzeev and Tuchin (2007) from SU(3) gluodynamics to full QCD with light quarks, using lattice data from the RIKEN-BNL-Columbia-Bielefeld Collaboration (Cheng et al., 2007). The results agree qualitatively with the direct lattice measurement of bulk viscosity in SU(3) gluodynamics by Meyer (2007). The paper classifies the critical behavior of bulk viscosity in terms of the O(4) universality class at zero baryon density and the Z(2) universality class at the chiral critical point, enabling experimental searches for the critical point in heavy ion collisions through signatures such as decreased average transverse momentum and increased multiplicity.
