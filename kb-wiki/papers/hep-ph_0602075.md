# New Members in the $0^+$ ($0^{++})$ Family

> Recent BES data on $J/ψ\to ϕππ$ indicate that there is a possible new spin-0 state ($f_0(1790)$) with a mass of $m=1790^{+40}_{-30} \mathrm{MeV}/\mathrm{c}^2$. Assuming it to be an iso-singlet $0^+(0^{++})$, we propose a new mixing scheme to describe this and the other three known iso-singlet $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$, $f_0(1710)$ states by adding iso-singlet hybrid states to the usual basis of two iso-singlet quarkonia and one glueball. Since there are two iso-singlet hybrid states, $(u\bar u+d\bar d)g/\sqrt 2$ and $s\bar sg$, this new basis implies existence of another iso-singlet state $X$. Using known data, we estimate the ranges of the mixing parameters. We find two sets of solutions with X mass predicted to be about 1820 MeV and 1760 MeV, respectively. We also study implications on the decay properties of these new states.

# New Members in the $0^+$ ($0^{++})$ Family

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the newly observed $f_0(1790)$ state, together with the three known $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1710)$ states, can be described by a five-state mixing scheme that includes two iso-singlet hybrid states $(u\bar u+d\bar d)g/\sqrt{2}$ and $s\bar s g$ in addition to the usual basis of two quarkonia and one glueball. This scheme predicts a fifth physical state $X$ with mass approximately 1820 MeV or 1760 MeV depending on the solution, but the authors note that the isospin and G-parity of $f_0(1790)$ have not been well determined yet.

## Method
The authors construct a $5\times5$ mass matrix in the basis of two hybrid states ($\xi_S = s\bar s g$, $\xi_N = (u\bar u+d\bar d)g/\sqrt{2}$), a glueball $G$, and two quarkonia ($S = s\bar s$, $N = (u\bar u+d\bar d)/\sqrt{2}$), assuming only glueball-hybrid and glueball-quarkonia couplings are substantial. They diagonalize this matrix using the four known eigenmasses of $f_0(1370,1500,1710,1790)$ and eight ratios of branching ratios to two pseudoscalar mesons as inputs, with the scalar glueball mass constrained to the lattice range of 1.5 to 1.7 GeV. The decay amplitudes are computed using an effective Hamiltonian with four coupling parameters $\xi_i = f_{1+i}/f_1$ relative to the leading term.

## Key numbers
- Mass of $f_0(1790)$: $m = 1790^{+40}_{-30}$ MeV/$c^2$ with width $\Gamma = 270^{+60}_{-30}$ MeV/$c^2$, from BES data on $J/\psi \to \phi \pi\pi$
- Observed branching ratio product: $\mathcal{B}(J/\psi \to \phi f_0(1790)) \cdot \mathcal{B}(f_0(1790) \to \pi\pi) = (6.2 \pm 1.4) \times 10^{-4}$
- Predicted mass of new state $X$: approximately 1820 MeV (case a) or 1760 MeV (case b)
- Ratio $B(f_0(1790) \to \pi\pi)/B(f_0(1790) \to K\bar K)$: approximately 2.3 for case (a) (from ratio 23:10) and approximately 0.42 for case (b) (from ratio 13:31), compared to the data central value of 3.88
- Two-photon decay ratios for case (a): $\Gamma(X\to\gamma\gamma):\Gamma(f_0(1790)\to\gamma\gamma):\Gamma(f_0(1710)\to\gamma\gamma):\Gamma(f_0(1500)\to\gamma\gamma):\Gamma(f_0(1370)\to\gamma\gamma) = 0.06:0.16:3.42:10.39:12.98$
- Two-photon decay ratios for case (b): $0.36:0.11:3.17:10.41:12.94$

## Context
This work extends the three-state mixing scheme of Close et al. (2000s) for $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1710)$ as mixtures of a scalar glueball, $N=(u\bar u+d\bar d)/\sqrt{2}$, and $S=s\bar s$, by adding two hybrid states to accommodate the new $f_0(1790)$ from BES. The authors note that a state X(1810) observed by BES in $J/\psi \to \gamma \omega\phi$ (Li, 2006) fits their case (a) prediction, while alternative interpretations exist such as the four-quark state proposed by B. A. Li (2006) or the chiral nonet tetraquark mixing scheme of Vijande et al. (2006). The scheme enables predictions for decays of $f_0(1790)$ and the predicted $X$ into two pseudoscalar mesons, two vector mesons, and two photons, which can be tested with improved BES data.
