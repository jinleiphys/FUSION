# Second-order (2+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We present a complete formulation of second-order (2+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics. The resulting framework generalizes leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics by allowing for deviations of the one-particle distribution function from the spheroidal form assumed at leading order. We derive complete second-order equations of motion for the additional terms in the macroscopic currents generated by these deviations from their kinetic definition using a Grad-Israel-Stewart 14-moment ansatz. The result is a set of coupled partial differential equations for the momentum-space anisotropy parameter, effective temperature, the transverse components of the fluid four-velocity, and the viscous tensor components generated by deviations of the distribution from spheroidal form. We then perform a quantitative test of our approach by applying it to the case of one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) in which case it is possible to numerically solve the Boltzmann equation exactly. We demonstrate that the second-order anisotropic hydrodynamics approach provides an excellent approximation to the exact (0+1)-dimensional RTA solution for both small and large values of the shear viscosity.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete second-order (2+1)-dimensional anisotropic hydrodynamics framework that generalizes leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics by adding perturbative corrections from deviations of the distribution function away from spheroidal form, and demonstrates that this approach provides an excellent approximation to the exact (0+1)-dimensional RTA Boltzmann solution for both small and large shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors derive second-order equations of motion for the additional dissipative currents (bulk viscous pressure and shear stress tensor) generated by deviations of the one-particle distribution function from the spheroidal Romatschke-Strickland form assumed in leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics. They use a Grad-Israel-Stewart 14-moment ansatz to expand the residual deviation around the anisotropic background, obtaining coupled partial differential equations for the anisotropy parameter, effective temperature, transverse fluid velocity components, and the viscous tensor components. The framework is tested by comparing numerical solutions for (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant expansion in the relaxation time approximation against the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation.

## Context

This work extends leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) developed by Martinez, Florkowski, Ryblewski, and collaborators (2010-2013), which expanded around a spheroidal momentum distribution to handle large early-time momentum anisotropies in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with standard Israel-Stewart theory (Israel 1976, 1979) and second-order treatments by Denicol et al. (2012) that expand around isotropic local equilibrium, which can fail when shear stress becomes comparable to isotropic pressure. The framework enables more accurate modeling of the early pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where longitudinal and transverse pressures differ dramatically due to rapid longitudinal expansion.
