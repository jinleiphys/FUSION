# Gluon shadowing effects on J/psi and Upsilon production in p+Pb collisions at sqrt s_NN = 115 GeV and Pb+p collisions at sqrt s_NN = 72 GeV at AFTER@LHC

> We explore the effects of shadowing on inclusive J/psi and Upsilon(1S) production at AFTER@LHC. We also present the rates as a function of p_T and rapidity for p+Pb and Pb+p collisions in the proposed AFTER@LHC rapidity acceptance.

## Key claim

Gluon shadowing effects on J/psi and Upsilon(1S) production at AFTER@LHC are calculated using the EPS09 NLO parameterization, but the paper explicitly notes that other cold nuclear matter effects (absorption, comovers, energy loss) are not included, so the results represent only the shadowing contribution, not the full nuclear modification.

## Method

The author calculates inclusive J/psi and Upsilon(1S) production cross sections in the next-to-leading order (NLO) color evaporation model (CEM), using the EPS09 NLO parameterization for nuclear parton distribution functions with the CT10 proton PDFs. Intrinsic transverse momentum smearing is included via a Gaussian function with ⟨k_T^2⟩ = 1 + (1/12)ln(√s/20) GeV^2. The nuclear suppression factors R_pPb and R_Pbp are computed as ratios of per-nucleon cross sections in p+Pb or Pb+p collisions relative to p+p collisions at the same center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers

- For p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 115 GeV, the J/psi rate in the backward rapidity bin -2.5 < y_cms < -2.0 is B dN/dy ~ 2.7 × 10^8 per year
- For p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 115 GeV, the Upsilon(1S) rate in the same backward bin is 8.68 × 10^3 per year
- For p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 115 GeV, more than 100 J/psi events can be collected at p_T ~ 9.5 GeV
- For Pb+p collisions at √s_NN = 72 GeV, the J/psi rate in the bin -1.9 < y_cms < -1.4 is on the order of 10^6 per year
- For Pb+p collisions at √s_NN = 72 GeV, the Upsilon(1S) rate in the AFTER@LHC acceptance is under 100 per year
- The R_pPb(y_cms) central value changes by ~30% over the rapidity range -2.5 to -2.0 for J/psi at √s_NN = 115 GeV
- The intrinsic k_T smearing parameter is ⟨k_T^2⟩ = 1.19 GeV^2 at √s = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear shadowing effects on quarkonium production to the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiment, bridging the gap between previous fixed-target experiments at CERN SPS (NA38, NA50) and Fermilab Tevatron (E866) in the range √s_NN = 17.2-41 GeV and collider experiments at RHIC. The large-x region probed in p+Pb collisions (x_2 > 0.1) has been virtually unexplored by gluon-dominated processes like quarkonium production, with only HERA-B having measured part of this backward region. The results enable future AFTER@LHC measurements to constrain the large-x gluon shadowing uncertainties in the EPS09 parameterization (Eskola, 2009) and distinguish shadowing from other cold nuclear matter effects such as absorption and energy loss.
