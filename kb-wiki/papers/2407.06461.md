# Electromagnetic and two-photon transition form factors of the pseudoscalar mesons: An algebraic model computation

> We compute electromagnetic and two-photon transition form factors of ground-state pseudoscalar mesons: $π,\,K,\,η_c,\,η_b$. To this end, we employ an algebraic model based upon the coupled formalism of Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations. Within this approach, the dressed quark propagator and the relevant Bethe-Salpeter amplitude encode the internal structure of the corresponding meson. Electromagnetic properties of the meson are probed via the quark-photon interaction. The algebraic model employed by us unifies the treatment of all ground-state pseudoscalar mesons. Its parameters are carefully fitted performing a global analysis of existing experimental data including the knowledge of the charge radii of the mesons studied. We then compute and predict electromagnetic and two-photon transition form factors for a wide range of probing photon momentum-squared which is of direct relevance to the experimental observations carried out thus far or planned at different hadron physics facilities such as the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab) and the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider. We also present comparisons with other theoretical models and approaches and lattice quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The algebraic model, fitted to a global analysis of existing experimental data including charge radii, can simultaneously describe the electromagnetic and two-photon transition form factors of ground-state pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η_c, η_b) across a wide range of photon momentum-squared, but the transverse piece of the quark-photon vertex (parameter α_q) is essential to satisfy the Abelian anomaly at Q²=0 and is independent of the electromagnetic form factor.

## Method

The authors employ an algebraic model based on the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using simple ansätze for the dressed quark propagator and the pseudoscalar meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. The quark-photon vertex is constructed via a gauge-technique ansatz that satisfies the Ward-Green-Takahashi identity, with a transverse term proportional to α_q that is exponentially damped at large Q². The model parameters (dressed quark masses, anomalous dimension ν, and α_q⁽⁰⁾) are fixed by performing a χ² minimization against experimental data for the pion electromagnetic form factor (up to 2.5 GeV²), pion transition form factor, kaon electromagnetic form factor at low Q², and η_c transition form factor, supplemented by theoretical charge radii from prior Schwinger-Dyson equation calculations.

## Key numbers

* Pion charge radius: r_π = 0.672(8) fm (from the fit, consistent with experimental data)
* Pion decay constant in chiral limit: f_π⁰ = 0.092 GeV (used in the Abelian anomaly relation 2f_π⁰ G_π(0) = 1)
* Asymptotic limit for the pion transition form factor: Q² G_π(Q²) → 2f_π (approximately 0.184 GeV at large Q²)
* The pion electromagnetic form factor data from JLab collaborations cover up to Q² = 2.5 GeV², with planned extensions to 8.5 GeV² (12 GeV upgrade), 15 GeV² (potential 22 GeV upgrade), and 35 GeV² (EIC)
* For η_c, BaBar data for the γγ* → η_c transition extend up to Q² > 35 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the algebraic model approach previously applied to pseudoscalar mesons by Albino (2022) and others, unifying the treatment of light mesons (π, K) where emergent hadronic mass dominates with heavy mesons (η_c, η_b) where Higgs-generated mass is dominant. The results address the ongoing controversy between BaBar and Belle collaborations regarding the pion transition form factor above Q² ~ 10 GeV², where BaBar data disagree with perturbative QCD predictions and Belle data. The model enables predictions for the η_b transition form factor (no experimental data yet exist) and provides form factors relevant for planned experiments at JLab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
