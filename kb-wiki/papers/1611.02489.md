# Calculation of three-body nuclear reactions with angular-momentum and parity-dependent optical potentials

> Angular-momentum or parity-dependent nonlocal optical potentials for nucleon-${}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ scattering able to fit differential cross section data over the whole angular regime are developed and applied to the description of deuteron-${}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ scattering in the framework of three-body Faddeev-type equations for transition operators. Differential cross sections and deuteron analyzing powers for elastic scattering and ${}^{16}\mathrm{O}(d,p){}^{17}\mathrm{O}$ transfer reactions are calculated using a number of local and nonlocal optical potentials and compared with experimental data. Angular-momentum or parity-dependence of the optical potential turns out to be quite irrelevant in the considered three-body reactions while nonlocality is essential for a successful description of the differential cross section data, especially in transfer reactions.

## Key claim

Angular-momentum or parity dependence in the optical potential is "quite irrelevant" for three-body deuteron-16O scattering and 16O(d,p)17O transfer reactions, while nonlocality of the potential is essential for a successful description of differential cross section data, especially in transfer reactions.

## Method

The authors developed nonlocal, angular-momentum-dependent and parity-dependent optical potentials for nucleon-16O scattering, fitted to experimental differential cross section and analyzing power data at fixed proton lab energies. These potentials were then used as inputs in exact Faddeev-type Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-body equations, solved in momentum-space partial-wave representation, to calculate deuteron-16O elastic scattering and 16O(d,p)17O transfer reaction observables. The results were compared with those obtained using local angular-momentum-dependent potentials from Kobos (1979) and nonlocal angular-momentum-independent potentials from Giannini and Ricco.

## Key numbers

- Proton lab energy Ep = 27.3 MeV used to fit the nonlocal L-dependent and π-dependent optical potentials to p+16O elastic scattering data
- At Ep = 34.1 MeV, nonlocal OPs fitted at 27.3 MeV successfully predict data, while local L-dependent OP fails at backward angles
- For d+16O elastic scattering at Ed = 56 MeV, local L-dependent OP from Kobos (1979) strongly overpredicts differential cross section at large angles, while nonlocal models reasonably follow the shape
- For 16O(d,p)17O transfer at Ed = 36 MeV, local L-dependent OP fails over the whole angular regime, while all nonlocal OPs provide good description of experimental data
- For 16O(d,p)17O transfer to ground state at Ed = 63.2 MeV, all nonlocal potentials reproduce data well
- Calculations included total angular momentum states J ≤ 30, with orbital angular momenta Lα up to 3 for n+p, 8 for n+16O, and 14 for p+16O pairs
- Coulomb screening radius of 10 or 12 fm used for convergence

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nonlocal optical potentials in three-body reactions by Deltuva (2009) and Deltuva (2015), who found that Perey-Buck-type exchange nonlocality is important for transfer reactions. It directly tests the angular-momentum-dependent local potentials of Kobos (1979) and parity-dependent local potentials of Cooper (1997) by creating nonlocal versions and applying them in exact Faddeev calculations. The results contradict the expectation that parity or angular-momentum dependence, which improves fits to two-body nucleon-nucleus scattering at large angles, would significantly affect three-body observables. The work enables future studies to focus on nonlocality rather than L- or π-dependence when modeling three-body reactions, and suggests that deuteron tensor analyzing powers are not well constrained by nucleon-nucleus analyzing power data.
