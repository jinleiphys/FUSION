# Energy Dependent Separable Optical Potentials for (d,p) Reactions

> An important ingredient for applications of nuclear physics to e.g. astrophysics or nuclear energy are the cross sections for reactions of neutrons with rare isotopes. Since direct measurements are often not possible, indirect methods like $(d,p)$ reactions must be used instead. Those $(d,p)$ reactions may be viewed as effective three-body reactions and described with Faddeev techniques. An additional challenge posed by $(d,p)$ reactions involving heavier nuclei is the treatment of the Coulomb force. To avoid numerical complications in dealing with the screening of the Coulomb force, recently a new approach using the Coulomb distorted basis in momentum space was suggested. In order to implement this suggestion separable representations of neutron- and proton-nucleus optical potentials, which are not only complex but also energy dependent, need to be introduced. Including excitations of the nucleus in the calculation requires a multichannel optical potential, and thus separable representations thereof.

# Energy Dependent Separable Optical Potentials for (d,p) Reactions

## Key claim
The authors develop separable representations of complex, energy-dependent optical potentials for neutron-nucleus and proton-nucleus systems, extending the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) scheme, which enable Faddeev-AGS three-body calculations of (d,p) reactions without relying on Coulomb screening procedures. The separable representation reproduces standard coordinate space calculations of elastic and inelastic scattering cross sections, but the approach requires that the separable potential be treated as energy-independent in the EST framework, with explicit energy dependence introduced through the coupling matrix elements.

## Method
The authors generalize the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler (EST) separable representation scheme, originally developed for Hermitian potentials, to handle complex, energy-dependent optical potentials by allowing explicit energy dependence of the coupling matrix elements λ_{ij}(E). For proton-nucleus systems, the scheme is further extended to incorporate the Coulomb interaction by replacing free wavefunctions with Coulomb-distorted nuclear wavefunctions and the free resolvent with the Coulomb Green's function. For multichannel optical potentials, the single-channel scattering states are replaced by multichannel scattering states, and the formalism is applied to the n+¹²C system using the Olsson (1989) deformed optical potential model (DOMP) with support points at 6 and 40 MeV laboratory kinetic energy.

## Key numbers
- The s-wave off-shell t-matrix elements for the n+⁴⁸Ca system are calculated at 20 MeV incident neutron laboratory kinetic energy, with on-shell momentum k = 0.978 fm⁻¹.
- The unpolarized differential cross section for elastic scattering of protons from ⁴⁸Ca is calculated at a laboratory kinetic energy of 38 MeV and scaled by a factor of 4.
- The unpolarized differential cross section for elastic scattering of protons from ²⁰⁸Pb is calculated at E_lab = 45 MeV.
- The form factors for the n+⁴⁸Ca and n+²⁰⁸Pb systems use EST support points at 5, 21, and 47 MeV.
- For the multichannel n+¹²C system, the support points are at E_lab = 6 and 40 MeV, and the first excited state of ¹²C (2⁺) is located at 4.43 MeV above the 0⁺ ground state, with the second excited state (4⁺) at 14.08 MeV.
- The Olsson (1989) DOMP is fitted to elastic and inelastic scattering data between 16 and 22 MeV laboratory kinetic energy.

## Context
This work extends the EST separable representation scheme (Ernst, Shakin, Thaler, 1973) from Hermitian potentials to complex, energy-dependent optical potentials, building on prior extensions to complex potentials (Hlophe, 2013) and to proton-nucleus scattering with Coulomb forces (Hlophe, 2014). The approach enables the implementation of the Faddeev-AGS equations in the Coulomb-distorted basis as formulated by Mukhamedzhanov (2012), which avoids the numerical difficulties of Coulomb screening procedures used in earlier work (Deltuva, 2005). The multichannel extension follows the approach of Pieper (1974) and allows inclusion of nuclear excitations, with the n+¹²C test case using the Olsson (1989) deformed optical potential model. The separable representations are intended as input for three-body Faddeev-AGS calculations of (d,p) transfer reactions, which are currently in progress.
