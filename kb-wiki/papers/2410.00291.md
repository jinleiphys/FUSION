# Magnetic Field Effects on Hadron Yields and Fluctuations

> We explore the impact of an external magnetic field on hadron yields and fluctuations in a thermalized system within the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model using an expanded Thermal-FIST package. The magnetic field sizably enhances the final proton-to-pion (p/$π$) ratio due to $Δ(1232)^{++}$ decay feeddown indicating that this ratio may serve as a potential magnetometer for freeze-out conditions. At the same time, magnetic field does not generate additional dynamical fluctuations of hadron numbers. This suggests that fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions provide limited additional information about the magnetic field beyond what is already inferred from mean multiplicities.

# Magnetic Field Effects on Hadron Yields and Fluctuations

## Key claim
The magnetic field sizably enhances the final proton-to-pion ratio via Δ(1232)++ decay feeddown, potentially serving as a magnetometer for freeze-out conditions, but magnetic fields do not generate additional dynamical fluctuations of hadron numbers beyond what is already captured by mean multiplicities. The claim is conditional on the assumption that magnetic field leaves decay branching ratios unaffected and that the constant-field approximation at freeze-out may not be entirely accurate.

## Method
The hadron resonance gas (HRG) model in an external magnetic field was implemented in the Thermal-FIST package (version 1.5) following the formulation of Endrodi (2013). Partial pressures of charged hadron species were computed including Landau level discretization of transverse momenta, and hadron number densities were obtained as chemical potential derivatives of the pressure. Final yields included feeddown from strong and electromagnetic decays, computed with and without finite resonance widths through an energy-dependent Breit-Wigner prescription.

## Key numbers
- Maximum magnetic field in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions extracted from ALICE data: eB ≃ (0.13 ± 0.01) GeV² ≃ (6.6 ± 0.6) mπ²
- Temperature used for LHC conditions: T = 155 MeV with vanishing chemical potentials
- Temperature used for lattice QCD comparison: T = 145 MeV
- HRG model shows qualitative agreement with lattice QCD for susceptibilities up to eB ≲ 0.08 GeV², with quantitative improvement needed beyond that

## Context
This work extends the HRG model with magnetic fields (Endrodi, 2013) to hadron yield ratios and fluctuations, complementing lattice QCD studies of magnetic field effects on conserved charge susceptibilities (Ding, 2021; Ding, 2023). The proton-to-pion ratio enhancement is attributed to Δ(1232)++ feeddown, and the extracted magnetic field values are compared to ALICE centrality-dependent data (ALICE Collaboration, 2019). The results suggest that yield ratios, particularly the neutron-to-proton ratio, may serve as better magnetic field probes than fluctuations, though baryon annihilation effects (Vovchenko, 2022) must be disentangled. The work enables future experimental measurements of neutron yields and precision comparisons of the normalized proton-charge correlator with the p/π ratio.
