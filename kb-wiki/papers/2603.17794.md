# Hydrodynamics of dilation and spin currents

> We formulate a relativistic hydrodynamic theory for fluids with spin and intrinsic dilation charges. Using an entropy-current analysis, we derive constitutive relations featuring a bulk viscosity and a dilation conductivity governing the relaxation and diffusion of dilation charge. Linear mode analysis reveals a gapped dilation excitation and the freeze-out of long-wavelength sound modes, similar to the superhorizon modes in cosmology. In the nonrelativistic limit, the theory reduces to that of microstretch fluids. Upon coupling to electromagnetic field, we show that the scale anomaly permits additional contributions in the electric current, dilation current, and energy-momentum tensor. Our theory naturally applies to nearly conformal fluids undergoing rapid expansion or contraction.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a relativistic hydrodynamic theory for fluids that carry both spin and intrinsic dilation (scale) charges, claiming that a finite bulk viscosity can exist even in a scale-invariant fluid where it measures the relaxation rate of intrinsic dilation rather than signaling broken scale invariance. The key qualifier is that this framework applies specifically to nearly conformal fluids undergoing rapid expansion or contraction, and the linear mode analysis reveals a gapped dilation excitation and freeze-out of long-wavelength sound modes analogous to superhorizon modes in cosmology.

## Method

The authors use an entropy-current analysis to derive constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor, spin tensor, and dilation current, employing a power-counting scheme where the scalar expansion is treated as zeroth-order while spin potential scales as first-order in derivatives. They linearize the hydrodynamic equations around a global equilibrium configuration described by a conformal Killing vector, perform a linear mode analysis in momentum space, and obtain the dispersion relations for all collective modes. The nonrelativistic limit is taken by expanding the four-velocity and treating the dilation potential and spin potential as small quantities, and the coupling to electromagnetic fields is incorporated via the scale anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The critical momentum for sound-mode freeze-out is given by \( k_c = \frac{2 |\lambda_0|}{c_s} \left[ 1 - \lambda_0 \gamma_c + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_0^2) \right] \), where \( c_s^2 = 1/3 \) is the speed of sound squared and \( \gamma_c = \frac{3}{4}\zeta' + \frac{13}{2}\eta' + \frac{1}{2}\eta_s' \)
- The gapped dilation mode has dispersion \( \omega = -3i D_d (1+3\kappa_d'\lambda_0) -3i\lambda_0 - i(\zeta'+\kappa_d'+a)k^2 + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_0^2,k^3) \), with damping rate approximately \( 1/(3D_d) \)
- The shear modes exhibit a small gap at order \( \lambda_0^2 \), with dispersion \( \omega = -i\eta'k^2 [1 + \gamma_+ \lambda_0] + \mathcal{O}(\lambda_0^2,k^3) \)
- The longitudinal spin mode is purely relaxational with \( \omega = -2i D_s \)
- The sound modes in the zero-wavelength limit behave as \( \omega = -i\lambda_0^2 \gamma_- \) and \( \omega = -4i\lambda_0 \) to leading order in \( \lambda_0 \)

## Context

This work extends the framework of relativistic spin hydrodynamics, developed by Florkowski (2017), Montenegro (2018), Hattori (2019), Hongo (2021), and others, by incorporating the dilation (scale) symmetry of the Weyl-Poincare group. The nonrelativistic limit reduces to the theory of microstretch fluids developed by Eringen (1969), where intrinsic dilation plays the role of microstretch and spin corresponds to microrotation. The scale anomaly treatment follows the strategy of Son and Surowka (2009) for chiral anomalies, and the freeze-out of long-wavelength sound modes is analogous to superhorizon mode freeze-out in cosmological inflation as described by Mukhanov (1990) and Dodelson (2003). The framework enables applications to rapidly expanding nearly conformal systems such as the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and the radiation-dominated early universe.
