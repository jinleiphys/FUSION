# Glauber theory of final-state interactions in $(e,e'p)$ scattering

> We develop the Glauber theory description of final-state interaction (FSI) in quasielastic $A(e,e'p)$ scattering. The important new effect is an interaction between the two trajectories which enter the calculation of FSI-distorted one-body density matrix and are connected with incoherent elastic rescatterings of the struck proton on spectator nucleons. We demonstrate that FSI distortions of the missing momentum distribution are large over the whole range of missing momenta. Important finding is that incoherent elastic rescatterings of the ejected proton lead to a strong quantum mechanical distortions of both the longitudinal and transverse missing momentum distributions. It is shown that allowance for finite longitudinal size of the interaction region for proton-nucleon collision neglected in the standard Glauber model drastically affects the theoretical predictions at high longitudinal missing momentum. We also find very large corrections to the missing momentum distribution calculated within the local-density approximation.

## Key claim

The authors develop a Glauber theory description of final-state interactions (FSI) in quasielastic A(e,e'p) scattering, finding that FSI distortions of the missing momentum distribution are large over the whole range of missing momenta, and that the standard Glauber model's neglect of the finite longitudinal size of the proton-nucleon interaction region drastically affects predictions at high longitudinal missing momentum.

## Method

The authors derived the missing momentum distribution w(p_m) using the Glauber approximation for the FSI factor S(r_1,...,r_A), expressed as a product over spectator nucleons of profile functions with step-function theta(z_j - z_1) for the struck proton's straight-path trajectory. They evaluated the FSI-distorted one-body density matrix by performing the sum over final states of the residual nucleus via closure, then approximated the many-body nuclear density in factored form and exponentiated the resulting expression for the FSI factor Phi(r_1, r_1') at large mass number A. The analysis focused on the region of missing momenta p_m less than or similar to the Fermi momentum k_F ~ 250 MeV/c, using an independent particle shell model for the target nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi momentum is k_F ~ 250 MeV/c, used as the boundary for the region of missing momenta studied.
- The analysis targets the region Q^2 ~ 2-10 GeV^2, where the typical kinetic energy of the struck proton T_kin ~ Q^2/2m_p is greater than or similar to 1 GeV.
- The standard Glauber model predicts an anomalously slow decrease proportional to |p_m,z|^{-2} of the missing momentum distribution at high longitudinal missing momenta.
- Corrections to the numerical predictions for the missing momentum distribution and T_A from neglected higher-order terms in the ratio B_pN/R_A^2 do not exceed 1-3% for nuclear mass number A greater than or similar to 10.
- The finite-proton-size effects cause large uncertainties at |p_m,z| greater than or similar to 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model treatment of FSI in quasielastic (e,e'p) scattering, which had previously been applied to light nuclei like deuterium and helium-4 (He4, D2, 1994), to heavier nuclei where FSI effects are strong even at p_m less than or similar to 300 MeV/c. The analysis is motivated by the need to disentangle conventional FSI effects from color transparency (CT) effects predicted by QCD (Nikolaev, Zakharov, 1994) in experiments at CEBAF, particularly for the interpretation of nuclear transparency measurements from the NE18 experiment (1994) and future studies of forward-backward asymmetry as a CT signal (Jensen, 1994; Boffi, 1994). The results also establish applicability limits for the Glauber formalism in (e,e'p) reactions due to finite-proton-size effects, which must be accounted for when extracting short-range NN correlation information from high-missing-momentum data.
