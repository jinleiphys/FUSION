# Nuclear modification of the J/Psi transverse momentum distributions in high energy pA and AA collisions

> We evaluate the transverse momentum spectrum of J/Psi (up to semi-hard momenta) in pA and AA collisions taking into account only the initial state effects, but resumming them to all orders in α_s^2 A^{1/3}. In our previous papers we noticed that cold nuclear matter effects alone could not explain the experimental data on rapidity and centrality dependencies of the J/Psi yield in AA collisions indicating the existence of an additional suppression mechanism. Our present calculations indicate that the discrepancy persists and even increases at semi-hard transverse momenta, implying a significant final state effect on J/Psi production in this kinematical domain. The QCD dipole model we employ is only marginally applicable for J/Psi production at mid-rapidity at RHIC energies but its use is justified in the forward rapidity region. At LHC energies we can quantitatively evaluate the magnitude of cold nuclear matter effects in the entire kinematical region of interest. We present our calculations of J/Psi transverse momentum spectra in pA and AA collisions at LHC and RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects alone cannot explain the observed J/Psi suppression in AA collisions, and that the discrepancy between their CNM-only calculation and experimental data grows at semi-hard transverse momenta, implying significant final-state effects in this kinematic domain. The QCD dipole model they employ is only marginally applicable for J/Psi production at mid-rapidity at RHIC energies but is justified in the forward rapidity region.

## Method

The authors evaluate the J/Psi transverse momentum spectrum in pA and AA collisions by resumming initial-state cold nuclear matter effects to all orders in α_s^2 A^{1/3} using the QCD dipole model. They derive the scattering amplitude by summing multiple gluon exchanges of the c-cbar dipole with the nucleus, separating contributions from elastic and inelastic scatterings before and after the last inelastic interaction that converts the color-octet c-cbar pair into a color-singlet J/Psi. The low-x evolution is incorporated by replacing the exponential saturation factor with the adjoint dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude, and the nuclear modification factor is computed using the DHJ model for the forward dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale parameterization: Q_s^2 = 0.13 GeV^2 e^{λ y} N_coll, with λ = 0.3 and Λ = 0.6 GeV
- Anomalous dimension γ_s = 0.628, with parameter d = 1.2 fixed by fitting to dA collision data at RHIC
- The pp J/Psi transverse momentum spectrum is parameterized as dσ/(σ_pp d^2 p_T) = N (1 + p_T^2/p_0^2)^{-6}, with average transverse momentum ⟨p_T^2⟩ = p_0^2/4
- At RHIC energy √s = 200 GeV and rapidity y = 1.7, the dipole model provides a fair estimate of the nuclear modification factor for p_T ≲ 2 GeV
- The longitudinal form factor calculation shows the dipole model is applicable at LHC energies even at higher p_T

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kharzeev, Levin, Tuchin, 2005-2011) where they calculated total J/Psi cross sections in pA and AA collisions using the QCD dipole model. Their earlier finding that CNM effects could not fully explain the experimental data on rapidity and centrality dependencies of J/Psi yield in AA collisions is now confirmed and strengthened at semi-hard transverse momenta. The results enable quantitative evaluation of CNM effects at LHC energies, allowing future experimental tests of predicted final-state suppression mechanisms such as absorption in quark-gluon plasma or suppression by strong magnetic fields (Marasinghe, 2011; Tuchin, 2011).
