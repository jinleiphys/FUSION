# Classical Strongly Coupled QGP II: Screening and Equation of State

> We analyze the screening and bulk energy of a classical and strongly interacting plasma of color charges, a model we recently introduced for the description of a quark-gluon plasma at T=(1-3)Tc. The partition function is organized around the Debye-Huckel limit. The linear Debye-Huckel limit is corrected by a virial expansion. For the pressure, the expansion is badly convergent even in the dilute limit. The non-linear Debye-Huckel theory is studied numerically as an alternative for moderately strong plasmas. We use Debye theory of solid to extend the analysis to the crystal phase at very strong coupling. The analytical results for the bulk energy per particle compare well with the numerical results from molecular dynamics simulation for all couplings.

# Classical Strongly Coupled QGP II: Screening and Equation of State

## Key claim

The classical colored plasma model (cQGP) can describe the screening and bulk energy of a quark-gluon plasma at temperatures T = (1–3)Tc, but the virial expansion around the linear Debye-Hückel limit breaks down at very low concentrations (c* ≈ 10⁻⁴ T³ for a 2-color system), and the spherically symmetric mean-field treatment becomes unstable for coupling Γ > 2, indicating that textbook methods fail in the liquid regime studied by molecular dynamics.

## Method

The authors analyze a classical nonrelativistic model of massive quarks, antiquarks, and gluons interacting via color Coulomb forces plus a short-range repulsive core, using the partition function organized around the linear Debye-Hückel limit. They perform a virial expansion to order c^(5/2) in particle concentration, solve the nonlinear Poisson-Boltzmann (mean-field) equation numerically for spherically symmetric screening, and extend the analysis to the crystal phase using Debye theory of solids with a momentum cutoff. Analytical results for the excess free energy and excess energy per particle are compared to molecular dynamics simulations from the companion paper (Gelman, Shuryak, Zahed, 2006, referred to as I).

## Key numbers

- The virial expansion around linear Debye-Hückel theory breaks down for concentrations as low as c* ≈ 10⁻⁴ T³ for a 2-color sQGP with core size a ≈ 1/(2T) and C₂α_s ≈ 1.
- For the one-species cQGP with N_c = 2, the excess energy per particle in the crystal regime (Γ ≈ 16–131) follows U_ex/N ≈ −0.5 + 2.25 T (in simulation units).
- The Debye interpolation for the crystal phase yields U_ex/N ≈ −0.65 + 2.25 T, with the Madelung constant c_MD = 2.64 for N_c = 2.
- In the liquid regime (Γ ≈ 0.8–3), the excess energy per particle follows U_ex/N ≈ −0.3 − 0.03/√T.
- The linear Debye-Hückel excess free energy per particle is F_DH/(N T) = −(N_c² − 1) Γ^(3/2)/√3.
- The nonlinear screening solution shows rapid growth and oscillations for Γ > 2, indicating breakdown of the mean-field approach.

## Context

This work extends the classical QGP model introduced by Gelman, Shuryak, and Zahed (2006, paper I) to provide analytical understanding of screening and thermodynamics, complementing the molecular dynamics simulations reported there. The analysis shows that standard Debye-Hückel theory and virial expansions, well-established for weakly coupled plasmas, fail for the strongly coupled QGP regime relevant to RHIC experiments (T ≈ 2 Tc), where the plasma behaves as a near-perfect liquid as suggested by hydrodynamic analyses (Shuryak, 2004). The results enable future studies of collective excitations (sound and plasma-color waves) and jet energy loss in the sQGP.
