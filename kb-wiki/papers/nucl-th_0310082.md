# Coupled cluster calculations of ground and excited states of nuclei

> The standard and renormalized coupled cluster methods with singles, doubles, and noniterative triples and their generalizations to excited states, based on the equation of motion coupled cluster approach, are applied to the He-4 and O-16 nuclei. A comparison of coupled cluster results with the results of the exact diagonalization of the Hamiltonian in the same model space shows that the quantum chemistry inspired coupled cluster approximations provide an excellent description of ground and excited states of nuclei. The bulk of the correlation effects is obtained at the coupled cluster singles and doubles level. Triples, treated noniteratively, provide the virtually exact description.

# Coupled cluster calculations of ground and excited states of nuclei

## Key claim
Quantum chemistry inspired coupled cluster methods with singles, doubles, and noniterative triples provide an excellent description of ground and excited states of nuclei when compared to exact diagonalization in the same model space, but the accuracy depends on the choice of basis (oscillator versus Hartree-Fock) and the specific variant of the triples correction used. The best methods reproduce the exact ground-state energy of ⁴He to within 40 keV, but only when using the oscillator basis and the CR-CCSD(T),a/Δ₀=1 approximation.

## Method
The authors applied standard and renormalized coupled cluster methods (CCSD, CR-CCSD(T) variants) and equation-of-motion coupled cluster (EOMCCSD) for excited states to ⁴He and ¹⁶O nuclei in a model space of four major oscillator shells. The Idaho-A nucleon-nucleon potential from chiral effective field theory was renormalized via a G-matrix procedure to remove the hard core, and calculations were performed in both oscillator and Hartree-Fock basis states. Results were benchmarked against exact full configuration interaction diagonalization for ⁴He and against truncated shell-model diagonalizations (CISD, CISDT, CISDTQ) for ¹⁶O.

## Key numbers
- CCSD ground-state energy of ⁴He in oscillator basis: -21.978 MeV (exact: -23.484 MeV), recovering the bulk of correlation from the reference energy of -7.211 MeV
- Best ⁴He ground-state result: CR-CCSD(T),a/Δ₀=1 at -23.524 MeV, within 40 keV of the exact -23.484 MeV
- CR-CCSD(T),c/Δ₀=1 for ⁴He ground state: -23.149 MeV, within 300 keV of exact
- EOMCCSD excitation energy for the J=0 state in ⁴He: 21.203 MeV (exact: 21.569 MeV); CR-CCSD(T) gives 21.489 MeV, within 0.1 MeV
- EOMCCSD excitation energy for the J=2 state in ⁴He: 22.435 MeV (exact: 22.697 MeV); CR-CCSD(T) gives 22.650 MeV
- CCSD ground-state energy of ¹⁶O in oscillator basis: -139.310 MeV (reference: -109.452 MeV); CISDTQ gives -138.387 MeV
- CR-CCSD(T),a/Δ₀=1 for ¹⁶O ground state: -139.621 MeV

## Context
This work extends coupled cluster theory, originally developed in nuclear physics by Coester (1958, 1960) and later advanced in quantum chemistry, to finite nuclei including excited states for the first time. It builds on earlier nuclear coupled cluster studies by Guardiola et al. (1996) and Mihaila and Heisenberg (1999) but incorporates modern quantum chemistry developments such as completely renormalized CCSD(T) methods. The results demonstrate that coupled cluster methods can achieve accuracy comparable to or better than truncated shell-model diagonalizations (CISD, CISDT, CISDTQ) at lower computational cost, enabling future ab initio calculations for medium-mass nuclei that are inaccessible to Green's Function Monte Carlo or no-core shell model approaches.
