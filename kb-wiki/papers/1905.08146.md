# In-medium effect on the thermodynamics and transport coefficients in van der Waals hadron resonance gas

> An extension of the van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model which includes in-medium thermal modification of hadron masses, the TVDWHRG model, is considered in this paper. Based on the 2+1 flavor Polyakov Linear Sigma Model(PLSM) and the scaling mass rule for hadrons we obtain the temperature behavior of all hadron masses for different fixed baryon chemical potentials $μ_{B}$. We calculate various thermodynamic observables at $μ_{B}=0$ GeV in TVDWHRG model. An improved agreement with the lattice data by TVDWHRG model in the crossover region ($T\sim 0.16-0.19$ GeV) is observed as compared to those by VDWHRG and Ideal HRG (IHRG) models. We further discuss the effects of in-medium modification of hadron masses and VDW interactions on the transport coefficients such as shear viscosity ($η$), scaled thermal ($λ/T^{2}$) and electrical ($σ_{el}/T$ conductivities in IHRG model at different $μ_{B}$, by utilizing quasi-particle kinetic theory with relaxation time approximation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including in-medium thermal modification of hadron masses (via the TVDWHRG model) improves agreement with lattice QCD thermodynamic data in the crossover region (T ~ 0.16-0.19 GeV) compared to the standard VDWHRG and IHRG models, but this improvement is conditional on the baryon chemical potential being zero or small (0.1-0.2 GeV), while at higher μB (0.3 GeV) the effect becomes more significant and qualitatively different.

## Method

The authors extended the van der Waals hadron resonance gas (VDWHRG) model by incorporating temperature- and baryon-chemical-potential-dependent hadron masses obtained from the 2+1 flavor Polyakov Linear Sigma Model (PLSM) combined with a generalized scaling rule that assumes hadron masses are linear in constituent quark masses. They calculated thermodynamic observables at μB = 0 GeV and transport coefficients (shear viscosity η, scaled thermal conductivity λ/T², and scaled electrical conductivity σel/T) at various μB using quasi-particle kinetic theory with relaxation time approximation, treating all hadrons as hard spheres with constant cross section σij = 4πr²h.

## Key numbers

- The crossover region where improved agreement with lattice data is observed: T ~ 0.16-0.19 GeV at μB = 0 GeV
- Light constituent quark vacuum mass: M⁰u,d = 300 MeV; strange constituent quark vacuum mass: M⁰s = 433 MeV (from PLSM)
- Light constituent quark masses begin to melt at T ~ 160 MeV for μB = 0 GeV; strange constituent quark masses begin to melt at T ~ 180 MeV for μB = 0 GeV
- For μB = 0.2 GeV, meson states degenerate at T ~ 130 MeV; for μB = 0.3 GeV, meson states degenerate at T < 100 MeV
- VDW parameters: a ≈ 239 MeV·fm³, b = 3.42 fm³ (from fitting nuclear matter ground state properties)
- Hadron list includes all hadrons and resonances up to 2.0 GeV from the Particle Data Group (2014)

## Context

This work extends the VDWHRG model of Vovchenko (2017) by incorporating in-medium hadron mass modifications, which were previously neglected in transport coefficient calculations for hadronic matter. The approach uses PLSM (similar to the work of Tawfik (2014, 2015) and Schaefer (2008)) to obtain thermal hadron masses, and applies the scaling mass rule previously used by Kadam (2015) and Blaschke (2011, 2015). The results enable more realistic modeling of the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding how chiral symmetry restoration affects transport properties like shear viscosity and conductivities in the crossover region.
