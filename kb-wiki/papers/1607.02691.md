# Charmonium dissociation in collision with phi meson in hadronic matter

> The phi-charmonium dissociation reactions in hadronic matter are studied. Unpolarised cross sections for 12 reactions are calculated in the Born approximation, in the quark-interchange mechanism and with a temperature-dependent quark potential. The potential leads to remarkable temperature dependence of the cross sections. With the cross sections and the phi distribution function we calculate the dissociation rates of the charmonia in the interactions with the phi meson in hadronic matter. The dependence of the rates on temperature and charmonium momentum is meaningful to the influence of phi mesons on charmonium suppression.

## Key claim

The paper calculates unpolarised cross sections for 12 phi-charmonium dissociation reactions using a temperature-dependent quark potential, finding that the dissociation rates of J/psi, psi', and chi_c with the phi meson are all less than 0.002, indicating that charmonium suppression caused by phi-charmonium dissociation is weak.

## Method

Unpolarised cross sections for phi-charmonium dissociation reactions (phi + J/psi, phi + psi', phi + chi_c producing D_s and D*_s mesons) were calculated in the Born approximation using the quark-interchange mechanism with a temperature-dependent quark potential derived from perturbative QCD at short distances and lattice gauge results at intermediate and large distances. The potential includes a central spin-independent term and a spin-spin interaction, with meson masses and quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation. Dissociation rates were then computed by thermally averaging the cross sections over the phi meson Bose-Einstein distribution in hadronic matter.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation rate of J/psi with phi: less than 0.00066
- Dissociation rate of psi' with phi: less than 0.002
- Dissociation rate of chi_c with phi: less than 0.0008
- Critical temperature T_c = 0.175 GeV used in the temperature-dependent potential
- Charm quark mass: 1.51 GeV; strange quark mass: 0.5 GeV; up quark mass: 0.32 GeV
- Potential parameter D = 0.7 GeV; E = 0.6 GeV; A = 1.5[0.75+0.25(T/T_c)^10]^6 GeV
- Cross sections calculated at six temperatures: 0, 0.65T_c, 0.75T_c, 0.85T_c, 0.9T_c, and 0.95T_c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium dissociation in collisions with pi, rho, and K mesons in vacuum (Martins et al., 1995) and in hadronic matter (Zhou and Xu, 2012; Ji et al., 2015) by calculating the previously unstudied phi-charmonium dissociation cross sections in the quark-interchange approach. The results enable assessment of the contribution of phi mesons to charmonium suppression in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly relevant given the enhanced phi-meson production observed in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies.
