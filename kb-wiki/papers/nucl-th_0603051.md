# Microscopic description of the pygmy and giant electric dipole resonances in stable Ca isotopes

> The properties of the pygmy (PDR) and giant dipole resonance (GDR)in the stable $^{40}Ca$,$^{44}Ca$ and $^{48}Ca$ isotopes have been calculated within the \emph{Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems}(ETFFS). This approach is based on the random phase approximation (RPA) and includes the single particle continuum as well as the coupling to low-lying collectives states which are considered in a consistent microscopic way. For $^{44}Ca$ we also include pairing correlations. We obtain good agreement with the experimental data for the gross properties of both resonances. It is demonstrated that the recently measured A-dependence of the strength of the PDR below 10 MeV is well understood in our model:due to the phonon coupling some of the strength in $^{48}Ca$ is simply shifted beyond 10 MeV. The predicted fragmentation of the PDR can be investigated in $(e,e')$ and $(γ,γ')$ experiments. Whereas the isovector dipole strength of the PDR is small in all Ca isotopes, we find in this region surprisingly strong isoscalar dipole states, in agreement with an $(α,α'γ)$ experiment. We conclude that for the detailed understanding of the structure of excited nuclei e.g. the PDR and GDR an approach like the present one is absolutely necessary.

## Key claim

The Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (ETFFS), which includes phonon coupling and the single-particle continuum, reproduces the experimentally observed A-dependence of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) strength in stable Ca isotopes, where the strength does not simply scale with neutron excess because phonon coupling shifts some strength in \(^{48}\)Ca above 10 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied the Extended Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (ETFFS) based on the random phase approximation (RPA) with the single-particle continuum included via a Green function technique in coordinate representation, and with coupling to low-lying collective phonons (3\(^{-}_{1}\), 5\(^{-}_{1}\) for \(^{40}\)Ca; 2\(^{+}_{1}\), 3\(^{-}_{1}\), 5\(^{-}_{1}\) for \(^{44}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca). For the open-shell nucleus \(^{44}\)Ca, pairing correlations were included using the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA) with BCS equations and particle-particle Landau-Migdal forces. The effective particle-hole interaction used the standard Landau-Migdal parameters with a "forced consistency" procedure to eliminate the spurious E1 state.

## Key numbers

- Summed B(E1)\(\uparrow\) for PDR (5-10 MeV) in \(^{40}\)Ca: ETFFS(QTBA) = 3.67 \(\times 10^{3}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{2}\), experiment = 5.1 \(\pm\) 0.8 \(\times 10^{3}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{2}\)
- Summed B(E1)\(\uparrow\) for PDR (5-10 MeV) in \(^{44}\)Ca: ETFFS(QTBA) = 81.0 \(\times 10^{3}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{2}\), experiment = 92.2 \(\pm\) 15.6 \(\times 10^{3}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{2}\)
- Summed B(E1)\(\uparrow\) for PDR (5-10 MeV) in \(^{48}\)Ca: ETFFS(QTBA) = 66.2 \(\times 10^{3}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{2}\), experiment = 68.7 \(\pm\) 7.5 \(\times 10^{3}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{2}\)
- PDR energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) fraction in \(^{44}\)Ca (5-10 MeV): ETFFS(QTBA) = 0.38%, experiment = 0.39 \(\pm\) 0.07%
- PDR EWSR fraction in \(^{48}\)Ca (5-10 MeV): ETFFS(QTBA) = 0.30%, experiment = 0.33 \(\pm\) 0.04%
- PDR mean energy \(\bar{E}\) in \(^{40}\)Ca: ETFFS(QTBA) = 8.68 MeV, experiment = 6.80 MeV
- GDR width \(\Gamma\) in \(^{40}\)Ca: ETFFS(QTBA) = 6.2 MeV, experiment = 5.0 MeV

- GDR maximum cross section \(\sigma_{\text{max}}\) in \(^{40}\)Ca: ETFFS(QTBA) = 75.6 mb, experiment = 95.0 mb

## Context

This work extends previous ETFFS calculations for Ca isotopes (We 2004) by removing approximations and applying the generalized QTBA version with pairing for \(^{44}\)Ca, achieving quantitative rather than qualitative agreement with experimental data from Hartmann (2002) and We (2004). It contradicts the phenomenological neutron-skin model and earlier self-consistent RPA calculations (Chamber 1994) which predicted PDR strength scaling with neutron excess; the phonon coupling mechanism explains why \(^{44}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca have similar strength below 10 MeV. The results enable detailed predictions for \((e,e')\) and \((\gamma,\gamma')\) experiments on PDR fragmentation and for \((\alpha,\alpha'\gamma)\) experiments on the surprisingly strong isoscalar dipole states found in all three isotopes.
