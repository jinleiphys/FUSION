# Constraining the High-Density Behavior of Nuclear Symmetry Energy with the Tidal Polarizability of Neutron Stars

> Using a set of model equations of state satisfying the latest constraints from both terrestrial nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations as well as state-of-the-art nuclear many-body calculations of the pure neutron matter equation of state, the tidal polarizability of canonical neutron stars in coalescing binaries is found to be a very sensitive probe of the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy which is among the most uncertain properties of dense neutron-rich nucleonic matter. Moreover, it changes less than $\pm 10%$ by varying various properties of symmetric nuclear matter and symmetry energy around the saturation density within their respective ranges of remaining uncertainty.

## Key claim

The tidal polarizability of canonical (1.4 solar mass) neutron stars in coalescing binaries is a very sensitive probe of the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, while being largely insensitive to variations of symmetric nuclear matter properties and symmetry energy around saturation density; the tidal polarizability changes less than ±10% when varying properties of symmetric nuclear matter and symmetry energy around saturation density within their remaining uncertainty ranges.

## Method

The authors constructed 17 relativistic mean field (RMF) parameterizations and one Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) model (SkIU-FSU) that simultaneously satisfy constraints from terrestrial nuclear experiments, astrophysical observations (including a maximum neutron star mass of about 2 solar masses), and state-of-the-art microscopic many-body calculations of pure neutron matter at sub-saturation densities. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the tidal Love number differential equation to compute the tidal polarizability λ = 2k₂R⁵/(3G) for neutron stars of various masses, systematically varying the nuclear incompressibility K₀, nucleon effective mass M*₀, symmetry energy slope L, and the ω-meson self-interaction parameter ζ.

## Key numbers

- The IU-FSU and SkIU-FSU models, which differ only in their high-density symmetry energy predictions, produce tidal polarizabilities of 2.828×10³⁶ and 1.657×10³⁶ respectively for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, a 41% change.
- The symmetry energy S(ρ) with IU-FSU is 40-60% higher than with SkIU-FSU in the density range ρ/ρ₀ = 3-4 expected in the core of canonical neutron stars.
- All RMF models with symmetry energy slope L between 42 and 58 MeV satisfy the pure neutron matter equation of state constraint at sub-saturation densities.
- The RMF models give maximum neutron star masses between 1.94 and 2.07 solar masses and radii between 12.33 km and 13.22 km for canonical neutron stars.
- The base RMF model uses K₀ = 230 MeV, M*₀ = 0.61 M, L = 50 MeV, and ζ = 0.025, predicting ρ₀ = 0.1524 fm⁻³, binding energy B = -16.33 MeV, and symmetry energy J = 31.64 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tidal polarizability using polytropic and popular nuclear equations of state (Flanagan 2007, Hinderer 2007, Damour 2009, Postnikov 2010) by using models that simultaneously satisfy the latest constraints from terrestrial nuclear experiments, the 2 solar mass neutron star discovery (Demorest 2010), and microscopic pure neutron matter calculations (Hebeler 2009, Gandolfi 2008). The only previously proposed astrophysical probe for high-density symmetry energy was the late-time neutrino signal from core-collapse supernovae (Roberts 2011). The results show that the proposed Einstein Telescope could tightly constrain the high-density symmetry energy, while Advanced LIGO-Virgo measurements at 100 Mpc distance would likely be insufficient for this purpose.
