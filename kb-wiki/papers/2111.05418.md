# Infinite volume, three-body scattering formalisms in the presence of bound states

> Strong interactions produce a rich spectrum of resonances that decay into three or more hadrons. Understanding their phenomenology requires a theoretical framework to extract parameters fromexperimental data and Lattice QCD simulations of hadron scattering. Two classes of relativistic three-body approaches are currently being pursued: the EFT-based and unitarity-based one. We consider a model of relativistic three-body scattering with an S-wave bound state in the two-body sub-channel using both formalisms. We present and discuss numerical solutions for the multi-hadron scattering amplitudes in different kinematical regions, obtained from integral equationsof the EFT-based approach. The connection of our work to the ongoing program of computingthe three-body spectrum from the lattice is highlighted. Finally, we show how to generalizethe unitarity-based framework to include all relevant open channels, discuss the nonphysicalsingularities near the physical region, and show how to eliminate them in a simple case.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the EFT-based and unitarity-based (B-matrix) relativistic three-body scattering formalisms can be numerically solved for systems with a two-body bound state, but the "minimal" B-matrix approach introduces nonphysical singularities near the physical region that must be removed via dispersion relations and explicit inclusion of coupled bound-stateŌĆōparticle channels to obtain physically meaningful amplitudes.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic three-body ladder integral equation for scalar particles with an S-wave two-body bound state (a toy model for nucleon-deuteron scattering) using uniform discretization with an iŽĄ prescription, extrapolating to the infinite-N and zero-ŽĄ limit. They then analyze the same system in the "minimal" B-matrix formalism, where the integration cutoff Žā_min = 4m┬▓ excludes the bound-state pole, and generalize the formalism by explicitly incorporating coupled channels for bound-stateŌĆōparticle (2ŌåÆ2, 2ŌåÆ3, 3ŌåÆ2) scattering. For a contact-interaction model, they derive exact solutions and apply Chew-Mandelstam-like dispersion relations to remove nonphysical left-hand cuts and restore unitarity between the bound-stateŌĆōparticle and three-body thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the numerical methodology of Jackura (2020) for solving relativistic three-body integral equations and the analytic studies of Dawid (2020) on the B-matrix formalism. The numerical results agree with finite-volume calculations from Romero-Lopez (2019). The paper connects to the ongoing lattice QCD program for computing three-particle spectra, where the three-body K-matrix (K_df,3 or R-matrix) has been determined for three-pion systems by Hansen (2020), Brett (2021), and Mai (2021). The generalized B-matrix formalism with dispersion relations enables future coupled-channel studies of resonances decaying into three or more hadrons, though a more general dispersion procedure is needed beyond contact interactions.
