# Study of a possible S=+1 dynamically generated baryonic resonance

> Starting from the lowest order chiral Lagrangian for the interaction of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons we find an attractive interaction in the $ΔK$ channel with L=0 and I=1, while the interaction is repulsive for I=2. The attractive interaction leads to a pole in the second Riemann sheet of the complex plane and manifests itself in a large strength of the $ΔK$ scattering amplitude close to the $ΔK$ threshold, which is not the case for I=2. However, we also make a study of uncertainties in the model and conclude that the existence of this pole depends sensitively upon the input used and can disappear within reasonable variations of the input parameters. We take advantage to study the stability of the other poles obtained for the ${3/2}^-$ dynamically generated resonances of the model and conclude that they are stable and not contingent to reasonable changes in the input of the theory.

# Study of a possible S=+1 dynamically generated baryonic resonance

## Key claim

The interaction of the baryon decuplet with pseudoscalar mesons produces an attractive potential in the ΔK channel with I=1, which can generate a pole in the second Riemann sheet of the complex plane corresponding to an exotic S=+1 baryonic resonance with J^P=3/2^-; however, the existence of this pole depends sensitively upon the input parameters and can disappear within reasonable variations, such as using f_K instead of f_π.

## Method

The authors start from the lowest order chiral Lagrangian for the interaction of the baryon decuplet with the octet of pseudoscalar mesons and derive the s-wave transition amplitudes for ΔK scattering. They solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (or equivalently use the N/D unitary approach) with the on-shell factorized potential to obtain the scattering amplitude t = (1 - VG)^{-1} V, where G is the loop function regularized either by a cut-off or by dimensional regularization with a subtraction constant. The analytical properties of the amplitude are explored in the first and second Riemann sheets to search for poles, and the stability of the results is tested by varying the meson decay constant (f_π vs f_K) and the regularization parameters.

## Key numbers

- The interaction strength in the I=1 ΔK channel is V(S=1,I=1) = -(1/(4f^2))(k^0 + k'^0), which is attractive, while the I=2 channel has V(S=1,I=2) = (3/(4f^2))(k^0 + k'^0), which is repulsive.
- Using f = f_π = 93 MeV and a subtraction constant a_l = -2 (or cut-off q_max = 700 MeV), a pole is found in the second Riemann sheet around √s = 1600 MeV.
- Using f_K = 1.22 f_π with q_max = 800 MeV, the cusp effect is diminished and the cross section decreases by about a factor of three; no pole is found in the second Riemann sheet in this case.
- If f is decreased by about 25% with respect to f_π, a pole appears in the first Riemann sheet just below the ΔK threshold, indicating a bound state.
- The ΔK(I=1) cross section is estimated to be of the order of 30-80 mb, while the K^+p → ΔK cross section is of the order of 1 mb (Giacomelli, 1976).
- For the other dynamically generated 3/2^- resonances, changing f by f_K or 1.15 f_π shifts the real parts of the poles by about 50 MeV on average, and the couplings change by less than 10% on average.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the interaction of the baryon octet with pseudoscalar mesons (which generated the Λ(1405) and other 1/2^- resonances, as in Kaiser (1995), Oset (1997), and Oller (2000)) to the interaction of the baryon decuplet with pseudoscalar mesons, following the work of Lutz (2003) which identified several 3/2^- dynamically generated resonances. The specific S=+1 channel belongs to the 27 representation of SU(3), where the interaction is attractive but weaker than in the octet (factor 6) or decuplet (factor 3) representations. The results enable experimental searches for this exotic state through reactions such as pp → Λ Δ^+ K^+, pp → Σ^- Δ^{++} K^+, and pp → Σ^0 Δ^{++} K^0, where invariant mass distributions of ΔK could reveal the underlying dynamics. The stability analysis confirms that the other 3/2^- resonances claimed in Lutz (2003) and in the companion paper (decu_ss) stand on firm ground.
