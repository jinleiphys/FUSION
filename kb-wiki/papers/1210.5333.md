# Tensor and tensor-isospin terms in the effective Gogny interaction

> We discuss the need of including tensor terms in the effective Gogny interaction used in mean-field calculations. We show in one illustrative case that, with the usual tensor term that is employed in the Skyrme interaction (and that allows us to separate the like-nucleon and the neutron-proton tensor contributions), we can describe the evolution of the N=28 neutron gap in calcium isotopes. We propose to include a tensor and a tensor-isospin term in finite-range interactions of Gogny type. The parameters of the two tensor terms allow us to treat separately the like-nucleon and the neutron-proton contributions. Two parameterizations of the tensor terms have been chosen to reproduce different neutron single-particle properties in the 48Ca nucleus and the energy of the first 0- state in the 16O nucleus. By employing these two parameterizations we analyze the evolution of the N=14, 28, and 90 neutron energy gaps in oxygen, calcium and tin isotopes, respectively. We show that the combination of the parameters governing the like-nucleon contribution is crucial to correctly reproduce the experimental (where available) or shell-model trends for the evolution of the three neutron gaps under study.

## Key claim

The authors show that including only a single tensor-isospin term in the Gogny interaction (as done in previous D1ST and D1MT forces) cannot simultaneously reproduce both the neutron single-particle splitting in 48Ca and the first 0- state in 16O; a second independent tensor parameter is required to separately control the like-nucleon and neutron-proton tensor contributions.

## Method

The authors added two finite-range tensor terms (pure tensor and tensor-isospin) of Gaussian form with range μT = 1.2 fm to the existing D1S Gogny force without changing other parameters. They fixed the like-nucleon strength (VT1+VT2) by reproducing either the 1f5/2-1f7/2 neutron splitting in 48Ca (D1ST2a) or the N=28 neutron gap increase from 40Ca to 48Ca as given by the Skyrme SLy5T interaction (D1ST2b). The neutron-proton strength VT2 was then chosen in both cases to reproduce the first 0- state energy in 16O via HF plus RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- The experimental 1f5/2-1f7/2 neutron splitting in 48Ca is 8.8 MeV; the authors chose VT1+VT2 = -20 MeV for D1ST2a to reproduce this value.
- For D1ST2a, VT2 = 115 MeV gives a 0- state energy of 10.72 MeV in 16O (experimental value 10.96 MeV), implying VT1 = -135 MeV.
- For D1ST2b, VT1+VT2 = -80 MeV and VT2 = 102 MeV.
- The experimental N=28 neutron gap increases from about 2.2 MeV in 40Ca to 4.8 MeV in 48Ca.
- The experimental N=14 neutron gap in 16O is 0.87 MeV and in 22O is 4.11 MeV (deduced from gamma-decay studies by Stanoiu et al. (2004)).
- Changing the sign of the D1ST tensor strength moves the 0- state in 16O from 10.94 MeV to 14.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to include tensor terms in finite-range Gogny interactions, specifically the D1ST and D1MT forces of Co' et al. (2011) which used only a single tensor-isospin term, and the GT2 force of Otsuka et al. (2005). The authors show that a single tensor-isospin parameter cannot simultaneously describe like-nucleon and neutron-proton tensor effects, unlike the Skyrme interaction where separate αT and βT parameters exist (as in the SLy5T force of Colò et al. (2007)). The new two-parameter tensor Gogny forces enable future studies of unnatural parity states in neutron-rich nuclei with closed proton shells, and the authors note that a full refit including the spin-orbit strength will be needed for quantitative accuracy.
