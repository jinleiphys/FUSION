# Constrained relativistic mean field approach with fixed configurations

> A diabatic (configuration-fixed) constrained approach to calculate the potential energy surface (PES) of the nucleus is developed in the relativistic mean field model. {As an example}, the potential energy surfaces of $^{208}$Pb obtained from both adiabatic and diabatic constrained approaches are investigated and compared. {It is shown that} the diabatic constrained approach enables one to decompose the segmented PES obtained in usual adiabatic approaches into separate parts uniquely characterized by different configurations, {to follow the evolution of single-particle orbits till very deformed region}, and to obtain several well defined deformed excited states which can hardly be expected from the adiabatic PES's.

## Key claim

The diabatic (configuration-fixed) constrained relativistic mean field approach can decompose the segmented potential energy surface obtained in usual adiabatic approaches into separate parts uniquely characterized by different configurations, but the resulting excited states cannot always be identified as physical states because the solutions violate the variational principle and serve only as a basis for qualitative understanding.

## Method

The authors implemented a diabatic constrained approach within the relativistic mean field model by using "parallel transport" to track single-particle configurations as a function of quadrupole deformation. For each deformation step, the wavefunction and occupation number of every state at the initial configuration were recorded, and for each state at configuration q, the state at configuration q+Δq with the largest overlap and same quantum numbers Ω^π was identified to transfer the occupation number. The method was applied to ²⁰⁸Pb using the PK1 parameter set with 20 oscillator shells and without pairing correlations, constraining the quadrupole moment ⟨Q̂₂⟩ to obtain the potential energy surface as a function of β₂.

## Key numbers

- The first proton level crossing in ²⁰⁸Pb occurs between 3s₁/₂ and one 1h₉/₂ state at approximately β₂ ≈ -0.1
- The first neutron level crossing occurs between 3p₁/₂ and one 1i₁₁/₂ state at a |β₂| slightly larger than where the first proton level crossing occurs
- State B has excitation energy E_x = 7.63 MeV at deformation β₂ = -0.11, with configuration (3s₁/₂)⁻¹(1h₉/₂)¹ for protons and (3p₁/₂)⁻¹(1i₁₁/₂)¹ for neutrons
- State C has excitation energy E_x = 15.63 MeV at β₂ = -0.28
- State D has excitation energy E_x = 21.19 MeV at β₂ = -0.39
- State E has excitation energy E_x = 21.13 MeV at β₂ = 0.21
- State G has excitation energy E_x = 19.19 MeV at β₂ = 0.69

## Context

This work extends the diabatic constrained approach, previously combined with non-relativistic methods such as Skyrme Hartree-Fock (Bengtsson and Nazarewicz, 1989; Meng, Zeng, and Zhao, 1994; Xu, Walker, Sheikh, and Wyss, 1998), to the relativistic mean field framework where it had not received sufficient attention. The method enables study of level crossings, shape coexistence (Cwiok, Heenen, and Nazarewicz, 2005), and provides a basis for Generator-Coordinate-Method calculations including excited configurations, though quantitative description requires going beyond the mean-field level.
