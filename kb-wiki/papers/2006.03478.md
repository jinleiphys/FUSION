# Strange mesons in strong magnetic fields

> The masses of the strange mesons ($K$, $K^*$ and $ϕ$) are investigated in the presence of strong magnetic fields. The changes in the masses of these mesons arise from the mixing of the pseusdoscalar and vector mesons in the presence of a magnetic field. For the charged mesons, these mass modifications are in addition to the contributions from the lowest Landau energy levels to their masses. The decay widths, $ϕ\rightarrow K\bar K$ and $K^* \rightarrow Kπ$, in the presence of the magnetic field are studied using a field theoretic model of composite hadrons with constituent quarks/antiquarks. The model uses the free Dirac Hamiltonian in terms of the constituent quark fields as the light quark antiquark pair creation term and explicit constructions for the meson states in terms of the constituent quarks and anitiquarks to study the decay processes. The study of the masses and decay widths of the strange mesons in strong magnetic fields can have observable consequences on the production of the open and hidden strange mesons in the peripheral ultra high energy collisions at LHC, where the created magnetic field can be huge.

## Key claim

The masses of strange mesons (K, K*, phi) are modified in strong magnetic fields primarily through pseudoscalar-vector meson mixing (K-K* and phi-eta' mixing), with additional Landau level contributions for charged mesons; the mixing effect dominates at large magnetic field values, and the resulting mass modifications cause the decay widths of phi to K anti-K and K* to K pi to differ significantly between charged and neutral channels.

## Method

The authors compute mass modifications of strange mesons using a phenomenological Lagrangian for pseudoscalar-vector mixing in a magnetic field, with coupling strengths determined from observed radiative decay widths of vector to pseudoscalar mesons in vacuum. For charged mesons, Landau level contributions are added as m_P(B) = (m_P^2 + eB)^(1/2) and m_V(B) = (m_V^2 - eB)^(1/2) with gyromagnetic ratio 2 for vector mesons. The decay widths for K* to K pi and phi to K anti-K are calculated using a field theoretical model of composite hadrons with constituent quarks/antiquarks, where the free Dirac Hamiltonian provides the light quark-antiquark pair creation term and meson states are constructed explicitly with harmonic oscillator wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: eB up to approximately 15 m_pi^2 (corresponding to LHC conditions), where m_pi is the pion mass
- For charged K mesons, Landau level contribution gives m_P(B) = (m_P^2 + eB)^(1/2); for charged K* mesons, m_V(B) = (m_V^2 - eB)^(1/2)
- The mixing parameter c_PV = g_PV * eB, where g_PV is determined from observed V to P gamma decay widths in vacuum
- The decay width expression for K* to K pi includes a strength parameter gamma_K*^2 fitted to the observed vacuum decay width
- The decay width expression for phi to K anti-K includes a strength parameter gamma_phi^2 fitted to the observed vacuum decay width

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pseudoscalar-vector mixing effects in magnetic fields for charmonium states (Mishra and Misra, 2020) and D mesons (Mishra and Misra, 2020) to the strange meson sector. It builds on prior investigations of light mesons in magnetic fields by Aguirre (neutral K and phi) and Chernodub (rho meson condensation), and on the field theoretical model of composite hadrons developed by S.P. Misra and collaborators. The results enable predictions for strange meson production in peripheral ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at LHC, where the created magnetic field can be huge, and may have implications for kaon condensation in neutron star interiors.
