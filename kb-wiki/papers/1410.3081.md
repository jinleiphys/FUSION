# Exact canonically conjugate momenta approach to a one-dimensional neutron-proton system, I

> Introducing collective variables, a collective description of nuclear surface oscillations has been developed with the first quantized language, contrary to the second quantized one in Sunakawa's approach for a Bose system. It overcomes difficulties remaining in the traditional theories of nuclear collective motions: Collective momenta are not exact canonically conjugate to collective coordinates and are not independent. On the contrary to such a description, Tomonaga first gave the basic idea to approach elementary excitations in a one-dimensional Fermi system. The Sunakawa's approach for a Fermi system is also expected to work well for such a problem. In this paper, on the $isospin$ space, we define a density operator and further following Tomonaga, introduce a collective momentum. We propose an $exact$ canonically momenta approach to a one-dimensional neutron-proton (N-P) system under the use of the Grassmann variables.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an exact canonically conjugate momenta approach for a one-dimensional neutron-proton system using Grassmann variables, overcoming the difficulty that collective momenta in Tomonaga's approach are not exact canonically conjugate to collective coordinates and are not independent. However, the exact canonical commutation relations are only fully achieved for the isospin T=0, Tz=0 case, while for the T=1, Tz=0 case the commutators do not vanish and the variables are not exact canonical variables.

## Method

The authors define a density operator on the isospin space (T,Tz) using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and introduce a collective momentum following Tomonaga. They then construct exact canonically conjugate momenta through a discrete integral equation of the Sunakawa type, expressing the kinetic part of the Hamiltonian in terms of these exact canonical variables without approximation up to a certain stage, and using Grassmann variables to approximate the constant term in the collective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The density operator for the zero-momentum state is defined as ρ⁰·⁰₀ = √A, where A is the total number of the neutron-proton system
- The commutation relation [Π⁰·⁰ₖ, ρ⁰·⁰ₖ'] = -iℏδₖₖ' is established for the exact canonically conjugate variables
- The kinetic energy expansion includes a term (1/2m) Σₖ k² Π⁰·⁰ₖ Π⁰·⁰₋ₖ and a correction term -(1/2m√A) Σ_{p≠0,q≠0,p+q≠0} pq ρ⁰·⁰_{p+q} Π⁰·⁰ₚ Π⁰·⁰_q
- The condition θθ̄ = 1 is imposed on the Grassmann variables, though the authors note this is problematic for a realistic Fermi system

## Context

This work extends the collective field formalism developed by Jevicki and Sakita for SU(N) quantum systems and the Sunakawa discrete integral equation method (Sunakawa, 1962) to a one-dimensional neutron-proton system. It revisits Tomonaga's approach (Tomonaga, 1955) for elementary excitations in Fermi systems and the authors' previous work on nuclear surface oscillations (Nishiyama and da Providencia, 2014). The paper enables a microscopic foundation of nuclear collective motions related to the Bohr-Mottelson model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1974) and provides a framework for constructing collective Hamiltonians for isospin-dependent systems.
