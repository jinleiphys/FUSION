# Early thermalization of quark-gluon matter initially created in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Elastic parton-parton-parton scattering is briefly reviewed and is included in transport equations of quark-gluon matter. We solve the transport equations and get thermal states from initially produced quark-gluon matter. Both gluon matter and quark matter take early thermalization, but gloun matter has a shorter thermalization time than quark matter.

## Key claim

Elastic parton-parton-parton scattering (3-to-3 processes) in addition to elastic 2-to-2 scattering is necessary to achieve early thermalization of quark-gluon matter; gluon matter thermalizes faster than quark matter, with the thermalization time of gluon matter being 0.32 fm/c and that of quark matter being 0.66 fm/c.

## Method

The author solves transport equations for gluon matter and quark matter that include both elastic 2-to-2 and elastic 3-to-3 parton scattering amplitudes, covering all types of parton-parton-parton scattering (e.g., gluon-gluon-gluon, gluon-quark-quark, quark-quark-quark, etc.) with up and down flavors. Initial parton distributions are generated from HIJING for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, with 1500 gluons, 250 up-quarks, 250 down-quarks, 250 up-antiquarks, and 250 down-antiquarks within a longitudinal range of -0.3<z<0.3 fm and transverse radius r<6.4 fm. The transport equations are solved using a Monte Carlo method after replacing quantum statistical factors with classical Boltzmann-like factors, and thermalization is identified when the momentum distribution matches the Juttner distribution.

## Key numbers

- Gluon matter thermalization time: 0.32 fm/c, with temperature T=0.52 GeV and gluon fugacity lambda_g=0.328 at t=0.52 fm/c for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.
- Quark matter thermalization time: 0.66 fm/c, with temperature T=0.46 GeV and quark fugacity lambda_q=0.143 at t=0.86 fm/c for the same system.
- Initial parton numbers: 1500 gluons, 250 up-quarks, 250 down-quarks, 250 up-antiquarks, and 250 down-antiquarks generated within -0.3<z<0.3 fm and r<6.4 fm.
- Elastic gluon-gluon-gluon scattering involves 220 Feynman diagrams at tree level.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that found elastic 2-to-2 parton scattering alone was insufficient to explain early thermalization (Shuryak, Wong, Shin, Blaizot). The author previously proposed elastic gluon-gluon-gluon scattering for gluon matter (Xu 2012) and found that elastic quark-quark and quark-quark-quark scattering gave long thermalization times for quark matter (Xu 2013). The present paper includes all types of elastic parton-parton-parton scattering involving quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, showing that both gluon and quark matter achieve early thermalization, with gluon matter thermalizing faster. This enables more realistic hydrodynamic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies.
