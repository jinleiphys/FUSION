# How the HESS J1731-347 event could be explained using $\bf{K^{-}}$ condensation

> The recent observation of a compact star with a mass of $M=0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}~{\rm M_{\odot}}$ and a radius of $R=10.4^{+0.86}_{-0.78}$ km, located within the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347, has substantially reinforced the evidence for the presence of exotic matter in neutron stars core. This finding has markedly enhanced our comprehension of the equation of state for dense nuclear matter. In the present work, we investigate the possible existence of a kaon condensation in hadronic neutron stars by employing and comparing two theoretical frameworks: the Relativistic Mean Field model with first order kaon condensate and the Momentum-Dependent Interaction model complemented by chiral effective theory. To the best of our knowledge, this represents a first alternative attempt aimed to explain the bulk properties of the specific object with the inclusion of a kaon condensation in dense nuclear matter. The application of two different models enriches the research, providing insights from the aspect of different theoretical frameworks that accurately predict the existence of HESS J1731-347. In both cases significant insights are extracted for the parameter space of models, emphasizing to those concerning the nucleon-kaon potential, the threshold density for the appearance of a kaon condensation, as well as the parameter $a_{3}m_{s}$ which is related to the strangeness content of the proton. Concluding, the present research indicates that a more systematic investigation of similar objects could offer valuable constraints on the properties of dense nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ultralight compact object HESS J1731-347 (mass 0.77 solar masses, radius 10.4 km) can be explained as a neutron star with a kaon condensate in its core, using two different theoretical frameworks. The authors hedge that this represents a first alternative attempt to explain this specific object via kaon condensation, and that the results depend on specific parameter choices such as the kaon potential and the strangeness content of the proton.

## Method

The authors employed two theoretical frameworks: the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with a first order kaon condensate (FOKC) following Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1998, 1999), and the Momentum-Dependent Interaction (MDI) model complemented by chiral effective theory following Kaplan and Nelson (1988) and Thorsson et al. (1994). For each model, they solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, varying the kaon potential in the RMF case and the parameter a3ms (related to strangeness content of the proton) in the MDI case, then compared the resulting curves to the observed mass and radius of HESS J1731-347.

## Key numbers

- HESS J1731-347 mass: M = 0.77 +0.20 -0.17 solar masses
- HESS J1731-347 radius: R = 10.4 +0.86 -0.78 km
- In the RMF+FOKC model, kaon potentials U_K0 = -180, -170, -160, -140, -120, -100 MeV were tested; the three strongest potentials (-180, -170, -160 MeV) produced curves crossing the HESS J1731-347 region
- In the MDI+APR1 model, a3ms values from -238 MeV to -280 MeV were used; the onset of kaon condensation reduced the maximum mass by about 16%
- The pion decay constant used in the MDI model: f = 93 MeV
- The constants a1ms and a2ms were set to -67 MeV and 134 MeV respectively
- The allowed range for a3ms is [-134, -310] MeV, corresponding to strangeness content of 0% to 20%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of HESS J1731-347 that interpreted it as a normal neutron star or hybrid star, including work by Kubis et al. (2023) using the RMF model, Gao et al. (2024) using a mean-field model with parity doublet structure, and Sagun et al. (2023) who investigated various scenarios including neutron stars, hybrid stars, strange stars, and dark matter admixtures. The paper also builds on the long history of kaon condensation theory in neutron stars initiated by Kaplan and Nelson (1986), Brown et al. (1992), and Thorsson et al. (1994). The results enable future systematic investigation of similar light compact objects (such as XTE J1814-338 and PSR J1231-1411) to constrain dense nuclear matter properties, and suggest that kaon condensation provides an additional mechanism for the appearance of twin stars in the mass-radius diagram.
