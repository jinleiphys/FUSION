# Topological transition in a parallel electromagnetic field

> In this work, we attack the problem of "chiral phase instability" ($χ$PI) in a quantum chromodynamics (QCD) system under a parallel and constant electromagnetic field. The $χ$PI refers to that: When $I_2\equiv{\bf E\cdot B}$ is larger than the threshold $I_2^c$, no homogeneous solution can be found for $σ$ or $π^0$ condensate, and the chiral phase (or angle) $θ$ becomes unstable. Within the two-flavor chiral perturbation theory, we obtain an effective Lagrangian density for $θ(x)$ where the chiral anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten term is found to play a role of "source" to the "potential field" $θ(x)$. The Euler-Lagrangian equation is applied to derive the equation of motion for $θ(x)$, and physical solutions are worked out for several shapes of system. In the case $I_2>I_2^c$, it is found that the $χ$PI actually implies an inhomogeneous QCD phase with $θ(x)$ spatially dependent. By its very nature, the homogeneous-inhomogeneous phase transition is of pure topological and second order at $I_2^c$. Finally, the work is extended to the three-flavor case, where an inhomogeneous $η$ condensation is also found to be developed for $I_2>I_2^c$. Correspondingly, there is a second critical point, $I_2^{c'}=24.3I_2^c$, across which the transition is also of topological and second order by its very nature.

## Key claim

The "chiral phase instability" (χPI) in QCD under a parallel electromagnetic field, previously interpreted as a chaotic regime where the chiral angle keeps changing with time, actually implies an inhomogeneous QCD phase with a spatially dependent chiral angle θ(x); the homogeneous-inhomogeneous phase transition at the critical value I₂^c is of pure topological and second order, not of Landau type.

## Method

Within two-flavor chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), the author derives an effective Lagrangian density for the chiral angle θ(x) including the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term, which acts as a source term. The Euler-Lagrange equation yields a static equation of motion for θ(x) that is solved analytically and numerically for spherical and cylindrical geometries, both in the chiral limit and with finite pion mass. The analysis is extended to three-flavor ChPT for an infinitely long cylindrical system.

## Key numbers

- Critical value of the second Lorentz invariant: e²I₂^c = (12π²/N_c) f_π² m_π², where N_c=3, f_π is the pion decay constant, and m_π is the pion mass.
- At the critical point I₂^c, the homogeneous solution gives θ₁ = θ₂ = π/2 and energy density H^c = 0.
- For the three-flavor case, a second critical point exists at I₂^{c'} = 24.3 I₂^c, across which the transition is also topological and second order.
- For a spherical system with I₂ = 2 I₂^c, the asymptotic solution for large radius r is θ(r) = -r²/6 × ρ̃₅, where ρ̃₅ = N_c e² I₂ / (12π² f_π²).
- For a cylindrical system (infinitely long), the approximate solution near the center is θ(r) = -r²/4 (ρ̃₅ - m_π²) + π/2.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Cao 2015, Cao 2019, Cao 2020) which identified the chiral phase instability regime where homogeneous solutions cease to exist. It contrasts with the chiral soliton lattice approach of Son (2007) and Brauner (2016), which addressed similar phenomena under different conditions. The finding of a topological phase transition in QCD is novel and enables future investigation of inhomogeneous QCD phases in heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly in relation to the chiral magnetic effect studied by Kharzeev (2022) and the STAR collaboration (2021, 2023).
