# Special relativity with two invariant scales: Motivation, Fermions, Bosons, Locality, and Critique

> We present a Master equation for description of fermions and bosons for special relativities with two invariant scales, SR2, (c and lambda_P). We introduce canonically-conjugate variables (chi^0, chi) to (epsilon, pi) of Judes-Visser. Together, they bring in a formal element of linearity and locality in an otherwise non-linear and non-local theory. Special relativities with two invariant scales provide all corrections, say, to the standard model of the high energy physics, in terms of one fundamental constant, lambda_P. It is emphasized that spacetime of special relativities with two invariant scales carries an intrinsic quantum-gravitational character. In an addenda, we also comment on the physical importance of a phase factor that the whole literature on the subject has missed and present a brief critique of SR2. In addition, we remark that the most natural and physically viable SR2 shall require momentum-space and spacetime to be non-commutative with the non-commutativity determined by the spin content and C, P, and T properties of the examined representation space. Therefore, in a physically successful SR2, the notion of spacetime is expected to be deeply intertwined with specific properties of the test particle.

# Special relativity with two invariant scales: Motivation, Fermions, Bosons, Locality, and Critique

## Key claim

The paper presents a Master equation for fermions and bosons in special relativities with two invariant scales (SR2, with invariants c and the Planck length λ_P), but the author emphasizes that the most natural and physically viable SR2 requires momentum-space and spacetime to be non-commutative, with the non-commutativity determined by spin content and C, P, and T properties of the examined representation space. The author also critiques existing SR2 proposals, noting serious questions about whether SR2 theories are physically distinguishable from standard special relativity (SR1).

## Method

The author constructs a Master equation for (j,0)⊕(0,j) representation spaces by introducing canonically-conjugate variables (χ^0, χ) to the Judes-Visser variables (ε, π). The boost generators remain unchanged from the Lorentz group, but the boost parameter ξ takes a modified, non-linear form specific to each SR2 proposal (Amelino-Camelia type or Magueijo-Smolin type). The Master equation is derived from the transformation properties of spinors from rest frame to a frame with momentum p, using the relation φ_(0,1/2)(0) = ζ φ_(1/2,0)(0) where ζ is an undetermined phase factor encoding C, P, and T properties. For arbitrary spin j, the Master equation takes the form with exp(2J·ξ) and exp(-2J·ξ) in the off-diagonal blocks.

## Key numbers

- The gravitationally-modified de Broglie wavelength saturates to 4 λ_P in the Planck realm, where λ_P = √(ħG/c³) is the Planck length
- For Amelino-Camelia's SR2, the Casimir invariant μ = (2/λ_P) sinh(λ_P m/2)
- For Magueijo-Smolin's SR2, the Casimir invariant μ = m/(1 - λ_P m)
- To lowest order in λ_P, the spin-1/2 Master equation yields corrections δ₁ that are either γ⁰((E²-m²)/2)+γⁱ p_i E (Amelino-Camelia) or γ^μ p_μ (E-m) (Magueijo-Smolin)
- The SR2 framework provides all corrections to the standard model in terms of one fundamental constant λ_P, not forty-six constants

## Context

This work extends the program of doubly special relativity initiated by Amelino-Camelia (2000) and Magueijo and Smolin (2001), which modifies special relativity to include a second invariant scale (the Planck length) alongside the speed of light. The author introduces Judes-Visser variables (2002) as a more natural description for Planck-scale physics and constructs explicit fermionic and bosonic representations. The paper enables future study of SR2 effects on gauge fields, weak-field gravity, and the equivalence principle, while also raising critical questions about physical distinguishability from SR1 raised by Grumiller (2003), Schutzhold (2003), and others. The author notes that the phase factor ζ (which distinguishes particles from antiparticles) has been missed in prior work by Arzano and collaborators (2002).
