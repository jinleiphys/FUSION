# Spectroscopy From The Lattice: The Scalar Glueball

> Lattice calculations allow us to probe the low-lying, non-perturbative spectrum of QCD using first principles numerical methods. Here we present the low-lying spectrum in the scalar sector with vacuum quantum numbers including, in fully dynamical QCD for the first time, the mixing between glueball, q-qbar, and meson-meson operators.

## Key claim

For the first time in fully dynamical lattice QCD, the low-lying scalar spectrum with vacuum quantum numbers is studied including mixing between glueball, quark-antiquark, and meson-meson operators; however, no finite-volume energy eigenstate predominantly created by a scalar glueball operator is found below approximately 1.9 times the reference mass (2m_K), and the extracted spectrum is largely insensitive to the inclusion of the glueball operator aside from increased statistical noise.

## Method

The authors used a single anisotropic ensemble of N_f=2+1 clover-improved Wilson fermions with m_pi approximately 390 MeV, generated by the Hadron Spectrum collaboration, on a 24^3 x 128 lattice with spatial spacing a_s=0.12 fm and anisotropy xi=3.4464(71). Finite-volume stationary state energies were extracted from matrices of temporal correlation functions using the stochastic LapH method, solving a generalized eigenvalue problem with a single-pivot diagonalization approach. The operator basis included scalar glueball operators constructed from eigenvalues of the covariant Laplacian, isoscalar quark-antiquark single-hadron operators, and pi pi, eta eta, and K anti-K two-hadron operators with various back-to-back momenta.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi approximately 390 MeV
- Lattice spatial extent: (L/a_s)^3 = 24^3, with m_pi L = 5.7
- Spatial lattice spacing: a_s = 0.12 fm
- Anisotropy: xi = 3.4464(71)
- Number of configurations: N_cfgs = 551
- Reference mass: m_ref = 2 m_K, with a_t m_K = 0.09689(15)
- No finite-volume energy eigenstate predominantly created by a scalar glueball operator is found below approximately 1.9 m_ref
- Only two quark-antiquark dominated states are found below 2 m_ref
- GEVP metric and diagonalization times used: (tau_0, tau_D) = (3,6)
- Condition numbers of correlation matrices remained less than 10 at tau_0 and tau_D

## Context

This work extends previous quenched QCD calculations of the scalar glueball, which yielded a glueball mass in the range 1.5-1.7 GeV (Bali 1993, Morningstar 1999, Chen 2006), and more recent unquenched studies (Bali 2000, Hart 2002, Hart 2006, Richards 2010, Gregory 2012, Sun 2018) that did not include meson-meson operators. The results suggest that the experimental candidates f_0(1370), f_0(1500), and f_0(1710) are likely molecular in nature rather than conventional quark-antiquark or pure glueball states. This work enables future determination of coupled-channel infinite-volume scattering amplitudes above the 4 pi threshold, requiring three- and four-hadron interpolating operators and the formalism for extracting infinite-volume scattering information from finite-volume energies, with work in this direction already underway (Horz 2019, Hansen 2019).
