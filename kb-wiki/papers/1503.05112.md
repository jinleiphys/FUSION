# $η_c$ production in photon - induced interactions at the AFTER@LHC experiment as a probe of the Odderon

> One of the open questions of the strong interaction theory is the existence of the Odderon, which is an unambiguous prediction of Quantum Chromodynamics, but still not confirmed experimentally. An alternative to probe the Odderon is the exclusive $η_c$ photoproduction in hadronic collisions. As the Pomeron exchange cannot contribute to this process, its observation would indicate the existence of the Odderon. In this paper we estimate the $η_c$ production in photon - induced interactions in hadronic collisions at the AFTER@LHC experiment. We demonstrate that the experimental analysis of this process is feasible in the AFTER@LHC experiment and that the observation of the $η_c$ production in nuclear collisions is a unambiguous signature of the Odderon.

## Key claim
The exclusive photoproduction of the $\eta_c$ meson in hadronic collisions at the AFTER@LHC experiment can serve as an unambiguous signature of the Odderon, provided that the background from two-photon fusion is absent; this condition is satisfied in nuclear (pPb and PbPb) collisions but not in pp collisions, where the two-photon background dominates.

## Method
The authors calculate the cross sections for exclusive $\eta_c$ production in photon-hadron (γh) and photon-photon (γγ) interactions for pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions at AFTER@LHC energies. For γh processes, the cross section is obtained by convoluting the equivalent photon flux of one hadron with the γh → ηc h cross section, which is computed using the impact-factor representation with the Odderon Green function from the Bartels–Kwiecinski–Praszalowicz (BKP) equation; two Odderon models are compared: the Bartels–Lipatov–Vacca (BLV) solution with intercept exactly one and the CKMS simplified three-gluon exchange model. For γγ processes, the cross section is computed from the two-photon fusion formula using the ηc → γγ partial decay width and the folded photon spectra with a no-overlap condition.

## Key numbers
- For pp collisions at √sNN = 115 GeV, the exclusive ηc photoproduction cross section is 0.05 pb (CKMS model) or 0.30 pb (BBCV model), corresponding to event rates of 1000/yr and 6000/yr respectively.
- For pPb collisions at √sNN = 72 GeV, the cross section is 28.1 pb (CKMS) or 356.6 pb (BBCV), with event rates of 31/yr and 393/yr.
- For PbPb collisions at √sNN = 72 GeV, the cross section is 5870 pb (CKMS) or 74366 pb (BBCV), with event rates of 41/yr and 520/yr.
- The γγ background cross section for pp collisions is 2.2 pb, a factor ≥8 larger than the γh signal in pp.
- The ηc mass is taken as 2.983 GeV and its two-photon partial decay width as 5.0 keV.
- The maximum γh center-of-mass energies are ≤44 GeV (pp), ≤12 GeV (pPb), and ≤9 GeV (PbPb); the maximum γγ energies are ≤17 GeV (pp), ≤2.0 GeV (pPb), and ≤1.0 GeV (PbPb).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Goncalves and Sauter (2015) for LHC energies to the lower-energy regime of the proposed AFTER@LHC experiment (√sNN = 115/72/72 GeV for pp/pPb/PbPb). It builds on the perturbative QCD Odderon formalism developed by Bartels, Lipatov, and Vacca (2000) and by Cudell, Kwiecinski, and collaborators (1999), and on the impact-factor approach of Cheng and Wu. The key advance is demonstrating that in nuclear collisions the γγ background is kinematically forbidden (maximum γγ energy below the ηc mass), so that observation of exclusive ηc production in pPb or PbPb would provide a clean Odderon signal, whereas in pp collisions the γγ background must be separated via transverse-momentum cuts. The predicted event rates (≥30/yr for nuclear collisions, ≥1000/yr for pp) suggest the measurement is feasible at AFTER@LHC.
