# Simple relativistic model of a finite-size particle

> Soluble model of a relativistic particle describing a bag of matter with fixed radius held together in perfect balance by a self-consistent combination of three forces generated by electromagnetic and massive scalar and vector fields is presented. For realistic values of parameters the bag radius becomes that of a proton.

## Key claim

The paper presents a soluble classical model of a relativistic charged particle (a "bag" of matter with a fixed, sharply defined radius) held together by a self-consistent combination of electromagnetic, massive scalar, and massive vector fields, where the equilibrium condition is imposed only inside the bag rather than everywhere in space. For realistic parameter values taken from the QHD-I mean-field theory of nuclear matter, the bag radius becomes comparable to that of a proton, but the author explicitly states the model is not advocated as a realistic nuclear structure model in its present rudimentary form.

## Method

The author constructs a static, spherically symmetric solution to a set of coupled relativistic Vlasov-Maxwell-type equations describing a dust of particles (or a pressureless fluid) interacting with three fields: an electromagnetic vector field, a massive scalar field, and a massive vector field. The equilibrium condition (net force vanishing on each particle) is enforced only where matter is present, leading to a sharp bag boundary; the field equations reduce to Helmholtz equations inside the bag and Yukawa-type equations outside, with continuity conditions at the boundary producing a quantization condition for the bag radius.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius R = 1.05 fm, obtained from the quantization condition using sigma-meson mass m_S = 550 MeV/hbar c and omega-meson mass m_V = 783 MeV/hbar c, with coupling constants g_S^2 = 91.64 hbar c and g_V^2 = 136.2 hbar c.
- Depth of the binding potential V_0 = 31.42 MeV.
- Proton mean radius r_p = 0.714 fm, compared to the experimentally measured radius of 0.862 fm.
- Binding energy E_B = 15.71 MeV, compared to the bulk binding energy per nucleon in nuclear matter of 15.75 MeV.
- Density at the bag surface rho(R) = (e^2 m_S m_V)/(4 pi Q^2 R), where Q^2 = e^2 - g_S^2 + g_V^2 must be positive for a solution to exist.

## Context

This work extends the classical program of modeling extended charged particles with Poincare stresses, previously pursued by Abraham (1904), Poincare (1906), Lorentz (1909), and von Laue (1911), and more recently by Dirac (1962), Schwinger (1983), and Bialynicki-Birula himself (1983). Unlike earlier models where Poincare stresses were introduced ad hoc, this model incorporates realistic elements from the relativistic mean-field theory of nuclear matter (QHD-I) developed by Serot and Walecka (1986). The model enables a zeroth-order semi-classical description of nucleons and nuclei, and the potential well it produces could serve as an initial step toward a relativistic version of the nuclear shell model using the Dirac equation.
