# Calculated corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decay: New evaluation of the nuclear-structure-dependent terms

> The measured $ft$-values for superallowed $0^{+} \to 0^{+}$ nuclear $β$-decay can be used to obtain the value of the vector coupling constant and thus to test the unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix. An essential requirement for this test is accurate calculations for the radiative and isospin symmetry-breaking corrections that must be applied to the experimental data. We present a new and consistent set of calculations for the nuclear-structure-dependent components of these corrections. These new results do not alter the current status of the unitarity test -- it still fails by more than two standard deviations -- but they provide calculated corrections for eleven new superallowed transitions that are likely to become accessible to precise measurements in the future. The reliability of all calculated corrections is explored and an experimental method indicated by which the structure-dependent corrections can be tested and, if necessary, improved.

## Key claim

The paper presents new, consistent shell-model calculations for the nuclear-structure-dependent corrections (\(\delta_{NS}\) and \(\delta_C\)) applied to superallowed Fermi beta decay, but these updated calculations do not alter the existing failure of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix unitarity test, which still fails by more than two standard deviations. The authors argue that restoring unitarity would require implausibly large shifts in the calculated corrections, for example, shifting \(\delta_R'\) downward by 0.3% for all nine well-measured transitions, or shifting \((\delta_C - \delta_{NS})\) upward by 0.3% for all nine cases.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for \(\delta_{NS}\) (the nuclear-structure-dependent part of the radiative correction) and \(\delta_C\) (the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction) for a wide range of superallowed \(0^+ \to 0^+\) beta decays, using consistent model spaces and approximations for both correction terms. For \(\delta_{NS}\), they evaluated axial-vector and electromagnetic interactions on different nucleons, applying quenching factors to the coupling constants and including a non-universal component from the Born term. For \(\delta_C\), they separated the correction into \(\delta_{C1}\) (from configuration mixing due to Coulomb and charge-dependent nuclear interactions) and \(\delta_{C2}\) (from differences in neutron and proton radial wave functions), constraining the charge-dependent interactions to reproduce the coefficients of the Isobaric Multiplet Mass Equation (IMME) and scaling results to match experimental excitation energies of the first excited \(0^+\) state.

## Key numbers

- The corrected \(ft\)-value \(\mathcal{F}t\) is defined as \(\mathcal{F}t \equiv ft (1 + \delta_R')(1 + \delta_{NS} - \delta_C)\), where \(\delta_R'\) values range from 1.39(4)% for \(^{34}\)Ar and \(^{38}\)Ca to 1.65(1)% for \(^{10}\)C (Table radct).
- The transition-independent radiative correction \(\Delta_R^V\) is \((2.40 \pm 0.08)\%\) (Eq. DRV).
- The derived value of \(V_{ud}\) is \(0.9740 \pm 0.0005\) (Eq. Vud), with contributions to the uncertainty of 0.0001 from experiment, 0.0001 from \(\delta_R'\), 0.0003 from \((\delta_C - \delta_{NS})\), and 0.0004 from \(\Delta_R^V\).
- The unitarity test yields \(V_{ud}^2 + V_{us}^2 + V_{ub}^2 = 0.9968 \pm 0.0014\) (Eq. unitarity), failing by more than two standard deviations.
- Adopted \(\delta_{NS}\) values range from \(-0.360(35)\%\) for \(^{10}\)C to \(0.009(20)\%\) for \(^{26m}\)Al (Table t:dnsnew).
- Adopted \(\delta_{C1}\) values range from \(0.010(10)\%\) for \(^{10}\)C and \(^{22}\)Mg to \(0.350(40)\%\) for \(^{70}\)Br (Table t:dc1new).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Towner and Hardy, 1998) by providing a consistent set of nuclear-structure-dependent corrections for both the nine well-known superallowed transitions and eleven new transitions that may become accessible to precise measurements. The calculations are linked to other observables such as neutron and proton binding energies, IMME coefficients (Brown, 1998), and non-analog \(0^+ \to 0^+\) transition rates (Hagberg et al., 1994; Daehnick and Rosa, 1985), following the approach of Ormand and Brown (1985, 1989). The results enable future experimental tests of the structure-dependent corrections, potentially reducing the uncertainty in the CKM unitarity test.
