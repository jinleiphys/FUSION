# Linking Electromagnetic Moments to Nuclear Interactions with a Global Physics-Driven Machine-Learning Emulator

> Understanding how specific components of the nuclear interaction shape observable properties of atomic nuclei remains a central challenge in nuclear structure research. While previous studies have focused on bulk observables such as nuclear energies and charge radii, it is unclear how distinct operator components of nuclear interactions impact complementary observables such as nuclear electromagnetic moments. Here, we develop a global, physics-constrained emulator to establish a quantitative link between electromagnetic moments and components of chiral nuclear forces. Unlike traditional sensitivity analyses that vary low-energy constants independently, we quantify parameter contributions while accounting for correlations within the physically supported parameter manifold. We show that, unlike bulk observables, electromagnetic moments probe complementary spin and isospin sectors of the interaction and exhibit a pronounced isotope-dependent sensitivity. These developments enable a quantitative assessment of the importance of prospective measurements, providing predictions with quantified uncertainties for observables that may be beyond the current experimental reach.

# Linking Electromagnetic Moments to Nuclear Interactions with a Global Physics-Driven Machine-Learning Emulator

## Key claim

Electromagnetic moments (magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole) probe complementary spin and isospin sectors of chiral nuclear forces that are largely invisible to bulk observables such as binding energies and charge radii, but this sensitivity is highly isotope-dependent and only emerges when accounting for correlations within the physically supported parameter manifold (the posterior), rather than varying low-energy constants independently as in traditional sensitivity analyses.

## Method

The authors construct a Fidelity-Resolved Affine Matrix Emulator (FRAME) that maps low-energy constants (LECs) of chiral effective field theory, nuclear identifiers (Z, N), and model-space fidelity (e_max truncation) to binding energies, charge radii, magnetic dipole moments, and electric quadrupole moments across the calcium isotopic chain. The emulator is trained on valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations at multiple model-space truncations (e_max = 4, 6, 8, 10) for a representative subset of interactions from a history-matched ensemble of ~10^4 non-implausible LEC vectors. A posterior-integrated Shapley value analysis then quantifies the importance of individual LECs and physical sectors while respecting correlations within the posterior distribution.

## Key numbers

- The LEC ensemble contains approximately 10^4 non-implausible interaction parameterizations, each specified by a 17-dimensional vector of LEC values.
- The chiral interaction is truncated at next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO) with a regulator cutoff of 394 MeV.
- Model-space truncations span e_max values of 4, 6, 8, and 10 for the single-particle basis.
- The posterior update using magnetic moments of four calcium isotopes (39, 47, 49, 51) yields a reduction in the log-determinant of the covariance matrix of Delta log|Sigma| = 1.2.
- The calcium isotopic chain studied spans from 37Ca to 55Ca (Z=20, N=17 to 35).
- Shell closures and subshell closures occur at N = 20, 28, 32, and 34.

## Context

This work extends prior sensitivity analyses of chiral nuclear forces (Ekstrom 2019, Belley 2024, Belley 2025) that focused on bulk observables such as binding energies and charge radii in oxygen, germanium, and nickel isotopes using Sobol variance decomposition. Unlike those earlier studies, which varied LECs independently over hyper-rectangular prior volumes, the present approach performs Shapley analysis on the posterior distribution, respecting correlations among LECs and the nontrivial geometry of the history-matched manifold. The FRAME emulator generalizes Parametric Matrix Models (Cook 2024, Yu 2025) to multi-isotope predictions and builds on the BANNANE architecture (Belley 2025) for global emulation. The results enable targeted experimental design for electromagnetic moment measurements and provide a pathway toward incorporating such data into global chiral EFT calibrations.
