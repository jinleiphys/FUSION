# Enhanced non-quark-antiquark and non-glueball Nc behavior of light scalar mesons

> We show that the latest and very precise dispersive data analyses require a large and very unnat- ural fine-tuning of the 1/Nc expansion at Nc = 3 if the f_0(600) and K(800) light scalar mesons are to be considered predominantly quark-antiquark states, which is not needed for light vector mesons. For this, we use scattering observables whose 1/Nc corrections are suppressed further than one power of 1/Nc for quark-antiquark or glueball states, thus enhancing contributions of other nature. This is achieved without using unitarized ChPT, but if it is used we can also show that it is not just that the coefficients of the 1/Nc expansion are unnatural, but that the expansion itself does not even follow the expected 1/Nc scaling of a glueball or a quark-antiquark meson.

## Key claim

The latest dispersive data analyses require a very unnatural fine-tuning of the 1/Nc expansion at Nc=3 if the f0(600) and K(800) light scalar mesons are to be considered predominantly quark-antiquark states, with coefficients two orders of magnitude larger than expected; this conclusion is reached without extrapolating to Nc≠3 using unitarized ChPT, but unitarized ChPT further shows the expansion itself does not follow the expected 1/Nc scaling of a glueball or a quark-antiquark meson.

## Method

The authors define two dimensionless observables, Δ1 and Δ2, constructed from the phase shift δ and its derivative δ' evaluated at the resonance mass mR², whose 1/Nc corrections are suppressed by Nc⁻³ and Nc⁻² respectively for quark-antiquark states (and by higher powers for glueballs). They evaluate these observables using recent model-independent dispersive data analyses: for ππ scattering, the output of GKPY and Roy equations from GarciaMartin (2011) constrained by NA48/2 Kℓ4 data; for πK scattering, the Roy-Steiner dispersive analysis of Descotes-Genon (2006) for the K(800) and a single-channel Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) for the K*(892). They then compare the resulting coefficients a and b (defined by Δ1=1+a/Nc³ and Δ2=1+b/Nc²) against the natural O(1) expectation for quark-antiquark states.

## Key numbers

- For the ρ(770) vector meson: a = -0.06 ± 0.01, b = 0.37 (+0.04/-0.05), both of order one or less, as expected for a quark-antiquark state.
- For the K*(892) vector meson: a = 0.02, b = 0.16, both of order one or less.
- For the f0(600) scalar meson: a = -252 (+119/-156), b = 77 (+28/-24), two orders of magnitude larger than the natural O(1) expectation for a quark-antiquark state.
- For the K(800) scalar meson: a = -2527, b = 162, also two orders of magnitude larger than natural.
- For the glueball interpretation of the f0(600): a' = -6800 (+3200/-4200), b' = 2080 (+760/-650), three to four orders of magnitude larger than the natural O(1) expectation for a glueball.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Pelaez (2003) and Pelaez (2006) which used unitarized ChPT to study the Nc scaling of light scalar meson poles, and the work of Nieves (2009) which studied the π/2 phase-shift criterion for the f0(600) with inconclusive results. The present paper provides more conclusive results by using recent precise dispersive data analyses (GarciaMartin 2011, Descotes-Genon 2006) and by defining observables with enhanced suppression of 1/Nc corrections, enabling a test of the quark-antiquark or glueball nature of light scalars directly from data at Nc=3 without extrapolation. The results enable future work on disentangling the composition of light scalars as mixtures of tetraquark, molecule, or other components that would dominate the 1/Nc expansion with a different Nc behavior.
