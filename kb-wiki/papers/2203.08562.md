# Correlation between nuclear temperatures and symmetry energy in sub-saturation

> A study of the correlation between nuclear temperatures and symmetry energy is presented for heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies via the isospin-dependent quantum molecular-dynamics model. It is found that different symmetry energy parameters will change the density and kinetic energy distribution of the hot nuclei. More importantly, nuclear temperatures that are based on kinetic energy properties can be used to study symmetry energy information.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "classical" momentum quadrupole temperature of hot nuclei formed in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions is correlated with the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities, but only if the Coulomb effect on the probe particles is minimized; neutrons show a clearer temperature difference between soft and hard symmetry energy than protons do.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model coupled with the statistical decay model GEMINI to simulate central collisions of 36Ar + 197Au at beam energies of 70, 75, and 80 MeV/nucleon. They selected equilibrated projectile spectators with mass 185 ≤ A ≤ 195 and neutron-proton ratio 1.3 ≤ N/Z ≤ 1.4, then calculated nuclear temperatures from the momentum quadrupole of emitted neutrons and protons using the classical Maxwell distribution.

## Key numbers

- The average density of the hot nuclei candidates is approximately 0.7ρ₀ (sub-saturation density), with a range of 0.7ρ₀ < ρ < 0.9ρ₀ during formation (50 fm/c < t).
- The difference in potential energy per nucleon among the different symmetry energies is approximately 2 MeV/nucleon.
- The difference in collective kinetic energy among different symmetry energies is approximately 0.2-0.6 MeV/nucleon for different excitation energies.
- The difference in intrinsic kinetic energy among different symmetry energies is approximately 2 MeV/nucleon, with higher values for soft symmetry energy.
- The stopping criterion for the dynamical simulation was E_stop = 2 MeV/nucleon, corresponding to the threshold energy for intermediate-mass-fragment emission.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on using isospin-dependent observables to constrain the symmetry energy at sub-saturation densities, following prior studies on isotopic scaling, isospin fractionation, and neutron-proton ratios (referenced as fus2-fus10 in the paper). It builds on the finding by McIntosh et al. (referenced as fus20) of a linear dependence of temperature on Coulomb energy and symmetry energy based on Landau theory. The results suggest that momentum quadrupole temperatures from neutrons, rather than protons, can serve as a probe for symmetry energy in low-density regions, enabling future experimental constraints using neutron-sensitive detectors.
