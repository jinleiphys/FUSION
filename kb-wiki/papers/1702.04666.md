# Third-order perturbative lattice and complex Langevin analyses of the finite-temperature equation of state of non-relativistic fermions in one dimension

> We analyze the pressure and density equations of state of unpolarized non-relativistic fermions at finite temperature in one spatial dimension. For attractively interacting regimes, we perform a third-order lattice perturbation theory calculation, assess its convergence properties by comparing with hybrid Monte Carlo results (there is no sign problem in this regime), and demonstrate agreement with real Langevin calculations. For repulsive interactions, we present lattice perturbation theory results as well as complex Langevin calculations, with a modified action to prevent uncontrolled excursions in the complex plane. Although perturbation theory is a common tool, our implementation of it is unconventional; we use a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to decouple the system and automate the application of Wick's theorem, thus generating the diagrammatic expansion, including symmetry factors, at any desired order. We also present an efficient technique to tackle nested Matsubara frequency sums without relying on contour integration, which is independent of dimension and applies to both relativistic and non-relativistic systems, as well as all energy-independent interactions. We find exceptional agreement between perturbative and non-perturbative results at weak couplings, and furnish predictions based on complex Langevin at strong couplings. We additionally present perturbative calculations of up to the fifth-order virial coefficient for repulsive and attractive couplings. Both the lattice perturbation theory and complex Langevin formalisms can easily be extended to a variety of situations including polarized systems, bosons, and higher dimension.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that third-order lattice perturbation theory and complex Langevin dynamics with a modified action can reliably compute the finite-temperature equation of state of non-relativistic fermions in one dimension, but the complex Langevin method requires a regulating term with parameter xi to prevent uncontrolled excursions in the complex plane and converge to the correct result.

## Method

The authors performed a third-order lattice perturbation theory calculation using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to decouple the system and automate the application of Wick's theorem, generating the diagrammatic expansion including symmetry factors at each order. They evaluated nested Matsubara frequency sums analytically using a technique based on geometric series expansions and Kronecker delta representations rather than contour integration. For non-perturbative calculations, they implemented complex Langevin dynamics with a modified action that adds a damping term xi*sigma^2 to prevent the auxiliary field from diverging in the complex plane, and compared results with hybrid Monte Carlo for attractive interactions where no sign problem exists.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid Monte Carlo calculations of the equation of state for 1D fermions from Ref. EoS1D to repulsive interactions using complex Langevin techniques, which have been applied primarily to finite-density lattice QCD (FiniteDQCD0 through FiniteDQCD8) but rarely to non-relativistic matter (see CLYamamoto1 and CLYamamoto2). The perturbative approach using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to automate diagram generation is unconventional compared to standard continuum perturbation theory, and the method for computing Matsubara frequency sums without contour integration applies to both relativistic and non-relativistic systems. The results enable future calculations for polarized systems, bosons, and higher-dimensional systems using the same formalisms.
