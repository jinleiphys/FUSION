# Is there a crystalline state of nuclear matter?

> A possibility of the crystalline state of nuclear matter is discussed in a medium--modified Skyrme model. The interaction energy per nucleon in nuclear matter is evaluated by taking into account the medium influence on single nucleon--skyrmion properties and the tensor part of the nucleon--nucleon potential, and by using a variational method of Hartree--Fock type including zero--point quantum fluctuations. It is shown that in this approach the ground state of nuclear matter has no crystalline structure due to quantum fluctuations as well as medium modifications of hadron properties.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when medium modifications of hadron properties (such as the nucleon mass, pion-nucleon coupling, and tensor potential) are fully included, the ground state of nuclear matter does **not** form a crystalline (FCC) structure, contrary to earlier classical Skyrme-model results; this conclusion holds only when both quantum fluctuations and medium effects are simultaneously considered.

## Method

The authors use a medium-modified Skyrme model to compute the interaction energy per nucleon in nuclear matter, incorporating density-dependent hadron properties (nucleon mass, pion mass, pion-nucleon coupling, cutoff, Delta-nucleon mass splitting) from Table 1. They adopt a variational Hartree-Fock-type method with a trial wave function localized at FCC lattice sites, including zero-point kinetic and iso-rotational fluctuations and an admixture of the Delta resonance. The tensor part of the nucleon-nucleon potential is taken from one-pion exchange (OPEP) with medium-modified parameters, and the energy functional is minimized with respect to variational parameters (spatial extent, Delta admixture, and an additional shape parameter eta).

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}, corresponding to nearest-neighbor distance R ≈ 2.03 fm in the FCC structure.
- Free-space nucleon mass: M_N = 868 MeV; at rho = rho_0 with g' = 0.6, M_N^* = 675 MeV.
- Free-space pion-nucleon coupling: g_piNN = 12.49; at rho = rho_0 with g' = 0.6, g_piNN^* = 7.75.
- Free-space pion mass: m_pi = 140 MeV; at rho = rho_0 with g' = 0.6, m_pi^* = 152 MeV.
- Free-space cutoff: Lambda = 528 MeV; at rho = rho_0 with g' = 0.6, Lambda^* = 462 MeV.
- Free-space Delta-nucleon mass splitting: M_DeltaN = 243 MeV; at rho = rho_0 with g' = 0.6, M_DeltaN^* = 196 MeV.
- A medium-modification parameter beta_{S,P} = 0.1 is sufficient to break the FCC crystalline structure; full medium effects (beta_{S,P} = 1) eliminate the crystalline minimum entirely.
- When quantum fluctuations are artificially suppressed (beta_kin ≈ 0.3), a condensed FCC state reappears even with full medium modifications.

## Context

This work extends the variational Hartree-Fock approach of Diakonov and Mirlin (1988), who found a crystalline FCC ground state at several times normal density using free-space OPEP parameters. It also builds on earlier Skyrme-model studies of crystalline matter by Klebanov (1985), Glendenning (1986), and others, which assumed classical field configurations. The key novelty is the inclusion of medium modifications of hadron properties (nucleon swelling, mass decrease, tensor-potential suppression) as developed by the authors' previous work (prc001, npa001, npa002). The results contradict the classical crystalline picture and suggest that a full treatment of quantum fluctuations and medium effects is essential; the authors note that including scalar-isoscalar channels (as in Serot 1986) could alter the conclusion and is left for future work.
