# Neutral Pion Photoproduction on Nuclei in Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Threshold neutral pion photoproduction on light nuclei is studied in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation theory. We obtain a general formula for the electric dipole amplitude in the special case of neutral pion photoproduction on a nucleus. To third order in small momenta, the amplitude is a sum of 2- and 3-body interactions with no undetermined parameters. With reasonable input from the single nucleon sector, our result for neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron is in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that to third order in small momenta, the electric dipole amplitude for neutral pion photoproduction on light nuclei is a sum of two- and three-body interactions with no undetermined parameters, and that for the deuteron this result agrees with experiment when using phenomenological input from the single-nucleon sector. The hedge is that the single-nucleon sector itself is not well understood theoretically, so the single-scattering contribution must be treated as phenomenological input rather than a pure chiral prediction.

## Method

The authors applied baryon chiral perturbation theory to threshold neutral pion photoproduction on nuclei, using heavy-fermion formalism to maintain consistent power counting. They performed a power counting analysis of irreducible diagrams, identified which graphs contribute at orders ν = 3-3A through ν = 5-3A, and derived a general formula for the threshold amplitude. For the deuteron, they evaluated the single-scattering contribution using phenomenological estimates for the nucleon electric dipole amplitudes and computed the three-body contributions using the Bonn potential wave function and a chiral wave function.

## Key numbers

- Single-scattering contribution to deuteron electric dipole amplitude: E_d^ss = -1.34 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (Bonn wave function) or -1.38 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (chiral wave function)
- Three-body contribution from figure 4a: E_d^{(a)} = -2.20 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (Bonn) or -2.18 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (chiral)
- Three-body contribution from figure 4b: E_d^{(b)} = -0.43 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (Bonn) or -0.39 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (chiral)
- Total deuteron electric dipole amplitude: E_d = -3.97 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (Bonn) or -3.95 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+} (chiral)
- Experimental value: E_d^{exp} = (-3.74 ± 0.25) × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+}
- Deuteron form factor: S_d(k/2) = 0.722 (Bonn) or 0.742 (chiral wave function)
- Phenomenological proton amplitude: E_{0+}^{π^0 p} = -2.24 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+}
- Phenomenological neutron amplitude: E_{0+}^{π^0 n} = 0.5 × 10^{-3} / M_{π^+}

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory to nuclear processes initiated by Weinberg (1990-1992), who studied pion-nucleus scattering lengths, and builds on the framework for two- and few-body forces developed by Weinberg and others. The results are similar to earlier calculations by Koch, Laget, and Faldt based on photoproduction low-energy theorems in the impulse approximation with estimates of three-body corrections, but the chiral power counting places these results on a more systematic theoretical footing. The approach enables future calculations for charged pion photoproduction, where the single-nucleon sector is better understood, and for heavier nuclei such as tritium or helium-3.
