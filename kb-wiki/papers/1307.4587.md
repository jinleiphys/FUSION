# A Divergence-Free Method to Extract Observables from Meson Correlation Functions

> Correlation functions provide information on the properties of mesons in vacuum and of hot nuclear matter. In this Letter, we present a new method to derive a well-defined spectral representation for correlation functions. Combining this method with the quark gap equation and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation, we calculate in-vacuum masses of light mesons and the electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma. The analysis can be extended to other observables of strong-interaction systems.

# A Divergence-Free Method to Extract Observables from Meson Correlation Functions

## Key claim

The paper presents a new spectral-representation method based on a polynomial transform that eliminates both ultraviolet and infrared divergences in meson correlation functions, enabling Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations of observables at both zero and nonzero temperature. The method is claimed to be well-defined where the standard twice-subtracted dispersion relation fails at nonzero temperature because the analytical continuation between Euclidean and retarded correlators breaks down.

## Method

The author introduces a third-order transform \(\hat{\mathcal{O}}_3\) that annihilates polynomial subtractions, yielding a convergent spectral representation for meson correlation functions. This transform is combined with the rainbow-ladder truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations: the quark gap equation and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, using a one-loop renormalization-group-improved interaction model with a Debye mass for the quark-gluon plasma region. At zero temperature, spectral functions are extracted via the maximum entropy method; at nonzero temperature, the vector spectral function is parameterized as a Breit-Wigner-like distribution plus a perturbative continuum tail, and fitted to the transformed correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass in vacuum: 0.135 GeV (parameter set I), 0.152 GeV (parameter set II)
- Rho meson mass in vacuum: 0.748 GeV (parameter set I), 1.043 GeV (parameter set II)
- First radially excited pion mass: \(1.065^{+0.021}_{-0.025}\) GeV (parameter set I)
- First radially excited rho mass: \(1.185^{-0.003}_{+0.045}\) GeV (parameter set I)
- Critical temperature for hadron-to-QGP transition: \(T_c = 144\) MeV
- Thermal quark mass for massless quarks at high temperature: \(m_T = 0.8T\) for \(T \gtrsim 3T_c\)
- Ground-state mass uncertainties from MEM default model variation: \(<1\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of Roberts (2007) and Maris (2003) to a wider range of applications by solving the long-standing divergence problem in meson correlation functions at nonzero temperature. The method addresses limitations of lattice QCD (Fodor, 2012; Philipsen, 2007) and the failure of the standard twice-subtracted dispersion relation at nonzero temperature. The calculated electrical conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma is consistent with recent lattice QCD results (Ding, 2010; Aarts, 2007), and the approach can be extended to baryon correlation functions, radially excited states, and other transport coefficients such as the heavy quark diffusion constant and thermal dilepton rate.
