# Coulomb exchange and pairing contributions in nuclear Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny force

> We present exact Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the finite range density dependent Gogny force using a triaxial basis. For the first time, all contributions to the Pairing and Fock Fields arising from the Gogny and Coulomb interactions as well as the two-body correction of the kinetic energy have been calculated in this basis. We analyze the relevance of these terms in different regions of the periodic table at zero and high angular momentum. The validity of commonly used approximations that neglect different terms in the variational equations is also checked. We find a decrease of the proton pairing energies mainly due to a Coulomb antipairing effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neglecting Coulomb exchange (Fock) and pairing terms in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny force produces only small errors in total binding energies (approximately 0.1% or less), but causes significant errors in proton pairing energies, which can be reduced by up to 4 MeV when all Coulomb terms are treated self-consistently. The authors qualify that the commonly used Slater approximation for the Coulomb Fock term gives results close to the simpler Hfp approximation (Hartree self-consistent, Fock and pairing perturbative), not to the full exact calculation.

## Method

The authors performed exact HFB calculations using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny force (primarily the D1S parameterization, with some D1 calculations) in a triaxial harmonic oscillator basis, including for the first time all contributions to the pairing and Fock fields from the Gogny interaction, Coulomb interaction, and two-body kinetic energy correction. They compared the exact self-consistent treatment (HFB\(_e\)) against the standard approximate approach (HFB\(_s\)) that neglects the Coulomb Fock term, Coulomb pairing term, and the pairing contributions from spin-orbit, two-body kinetic energy, and center-of-mass correction terms, as well as against perturbative corrections (HFB\(_{s+}\)) where neglected terms are added after convergence. The study covered spherical nuclei in the N=50, N=82, and N=126 regions, deformed rare-earth nuclei (Er and Yb isotopes), the actinide nucleus \(^{240}\)Pu, and the superdeformed A≈190 region.

## Key numbers

- For spherical N=50 nuclei, the exact HFB calculation (HFP) gives proton pairing energies ranging from -6.964 MeV (\(^{86}\)Kr) to -2.293 MeV (\(^{90}\)Zr), compared to the reference calculation without Coulomb terms giving -10.332 MeV to -6.967 MeV, a reduction of up to ~4 MeV for \(^{90}\)Zr.
- For \(^{90}\)Zr, the Coulomb pairing term itself contributes only about 650 keV, but when treated self-consistently it produces a 4 MeV reduction in pairing energy.
- For N=82 isotones, exact proton pairing energies range from -4.7615 MeV (\(^{134}\)Te) to -8.8442 MeV (\(^{140}\)Ce), while the standard approximation (HFB\(_s\)) gives -7.4700 MeV to -15.7718 MeV, a difference of 2.7 to 6.9 MeV.
- For N=126 isotones, exact proton pairing energies range from -3.9841 MeV (\(^{210}\)Po) to -8.2135 MeV (\(^{214}\)Ra), compared to HFB\(_s\) values of -6.1489 MeV to -13.7754 MeV.
- For deformed rare-earth nuclei, exact proton pairing energies are -1.723 MeV (\(^{164}\)Er) to -4.167 MeV (\(^{162}\)Yb), while HFB\(_s\) gives -5.642 MeV to -10.081 MeV; for \(^{164}\)Yb and \(^{166}\)Yb, proton pairing vanishes completely in the exact calculation.
- The lowest two-quasiproton excitation energies for N=50 nuclei decrease from ~3.5 MeV (Hfp approximation) to ~2.1-2.5 MeV (exact HFB\(_e\)).
- Binding energies differ by less than 1 MeV between approximate and exact calculations for all nuclei studied (e.g., for \(^{164}\)Er: -1328.665 MeV vs -1329.176 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier test by Tittle (1974) of Coulomb exchange approximations in Hartree-Fock calculations to the more complex HFB framework with the Gogny force, where such a systematic test had never been performed. The authors directly challenge the common assumption that findings from the HF case apply to HFB, showing that while total energies are robust, pairing properties are strongly affected. The results enable more accurate HFB calculations for phenomena sensitive to pairing correlations, such as two-quasiparticle excitation energies, rotational bands, and Random Phase Approximation calculations, and they validate the use of the D1S parameterization (Berger 1991) while cautioning against the standard approximations used in prior triaxial HFB work by Girod (1983), Egido (1993), and others.
