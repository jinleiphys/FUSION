# Flow harmonics from self-consistent particlization of a viscous fluid

> The quantitative extraction of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) properties from heavy-ion data, such as its specific shear viscosity $η/s$, typically requires comparison to viscous hydrodynamic or "hybrid" hydrodynamics+transport simulations. In either case, one has to convert the fluid to hadrons, yet without additional theory input the conversion is ambiguous for dissipative fluids. Here, shear viscous phase-space corrections calculated using linearized transport theory are applied in Cooper-Frye freezeout to quantify the effects on anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n(p_T)$ at both RHIC and LHC energies. Expanding upon our previous flow harmonics studies [1,2], we calculate pion and proton $v_2(p_T)$, $v_4(p_T)$, and $v_6(p_T)$. Unlike in Ref. [1], we incorporate a hadron gas that is chemically frozen below a temperature of 175 MeV, and use hypersurfaces from realistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations. With additive quark model cross sections and relative phase-space corrections with $p^{3/2}$ momentum dependence, rather than the quadratic Grad form, we find at moderately high transverse momentum noticeably higher $v_4(p_T)$ and $v_6(p_T)$ for protons than for pions. In addition, the value of $η/s$ deduced from elliptic flow data differs by nearly 50\% from the value extracted using the naive "democratic Grad" form of freeze-out distributions. To facilitate the use of the self-consistent viscous corrections calculated here in hydrodynamic and hybrid calculations, we also present convenient parameterizations of the corrections for the various hadron species (cf. Table I).

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity η/s of the quark-gluon plasma deduced from elliptic flow data differs by nearly 50% when using self-consistent, species-dependent shear viscous phase-space corrections (with p^(3/2) momentum dependence) compared to the value extracted using the naive "democratic Grad" form of freeze-out distributions, but only for the particular hadron gas model with additive quark model cross sections and chemical freezeout below 175 MeV.

## Method

Shear viscous phase-space corrections were calculated using linearized transport theory (solving the linearized Boltzmann equation via a variational method) for a 49-species effective hadron gas with additive quark model cross sections (σ_MM:σ_MB:σ_BB = 4:6:9) and chemical freezeout below T_ch = 175 MeV. These corrections, with either quadratic (Grad) or p^(3/2) momentum dependence, were applied in Cooper-Frye freezeout on hypersurfaces from 2+1D boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic simulations (with η/s = 0.12 in the deconfined phase) to compute differential anisotropic flow coefficients v_2(p_T), v_4(p_T), and v_6(p_T) for pions and protons at both RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV) and LHC (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV) energies, including resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV (20-30% centrality) with T_conv = 100 MeV: self-consistent p^(3/2) corrections give a pion-proton elliptic flow crossing at p_T ≈ 1.6 GeV, with roughly 10% splitting at p_T ≈ 2.5 GeV, compared to negligible splitting with democratic Grad.
- For Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (20-30% centrality) with T_conv = 100 MeV: self-consistent p^(3/2) corrections give a crossing at p_T ≈ 2.2 GeV; at T_conv = 160 MeV the crossing shifts to p_T ≈ 1.6 GeV.
- The value of η/s deduced from elliptic flow data using self-consistent freezeout is 50% larger (κ = 1.5) than the value extracted using democratic Grad freezeout (original simulation had η/s = 0.12 in the plasma phase).
- At T_conv = 100 MeV with chemical freezeout, the viscous correction coefficients are c_π = 1.46 and c_N = 0.75 for the Grad case, compared to c_π = 1.08 and c_N = 0.56 in full chemical equilibrium.
- The hadron gas specific shear viscosity η/s drops from about 1.3 at T = 100 MeV to 0.2 at T = 165 MeV in the 49-species calculation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Molnar and Wolff, 2014; Wolff, 2014) by incorporating chemical freezeout below 175 MeV and using hypersurfaces from realistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations (Paatelainen and Niemi, 2014) rather than simplified estimates. The self-consistent approach resolves the ambiguity in viscous phase-space corrections that is ignored by the commonly used "democratic Grad" ansatz (quadratic in momentum, species-independent). The results enable more reliable extraction of QGP transport coefficients from heavy-ion data, and the parameterized correction coefficients (Table I) facilitate implementation in other hydrodynamic and hybrid calculations. The hadron gas shear viscosity calculation is compared to results from Demir et al. (2008) using UrQMD, Wiranata et al. (2013) using K-matrix cross sections, and Prakash et al. (1993).
