# QCD factorization for hadronic quarkonium production at high $p_T$

> Heavy quarkonium production at high transverse momentum ($p_T$) in hadronic collisions is explored in the QCD factorization approach. We find that the leading power in the $1/p_T$ expansion is responsible for high $p_T$ regime, while the next-to-leading power contribution is necessary for the low $p_T$ region. We present the first numerical analysis of the scale evolution of coupled twist-2 and twist-4 fragmentation functions (FFs) for heavy quarkonium production and demonstrate that the QCD factorization approach is capable of describing the $p_T$ spectrum of hadronic $J/ψ$ production at the LHC.

## Key claim
The QCD factorization approach, combining leading-power (LP) and next-to-leading-power (NLP) contributions with coupled scale evolution of twist-2 and twist-4 fragmentation functions, can describe the transverse momentum spectrum of prompt \(J/\psi\) production at the LHC, but only if the NLP contribution is included with an approximate \(K\)-factor of 2 to mimic missing higher-order corrections.

## Method
The authors factorize the inclusive hadroproduction cross section for \(J/\psi\) at high \(p_T\) into a leading-power (LP) term involving twist-2 parton-to-\(J/\psi\) fragmentation functions and a next-to-leading-power (NLP) term involving twist-4 \(Q\bar{Q}\)-pair-to-\(J/\psi\) fragmentation functions. They model the non-perturbative input fragmentation functions at scale \(\mu_0 = 4m_c\) using NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) with a parameterized \(z\)-dependence of the form \(N z^\alpha (1-z)^\beta / B[1+\alpha,1+\beta]\), setting \(\alpha=30\) and \(\beta=0.5\) to peak near \(z=1\). They then solve the coupled DGLAP-type evolution equations for the twist-2 and twist-4 fragmentation functions and convolve the evolved functions with perturbative hard parts (NLO for LP, LO for NLP) and CT18NLO parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers
- The LDME \(\langle \mathcal{O}_{^1S_0^{[8]}}^{J/\psi}\rangle\) is extracted by fitting the LP contribution (without nonlinear corrections) to CMS data at \(p_T \geq 60\) GeV, yielding \(\langle \mathcal{O}_{^1S_0^{[8]}}^{J/\psi}\rangle = 0.129 \pm 5.18 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^3\).
- The input scale for fragmentation functions is set to \(\mu_0 = 4m\) with \(m = m_{J/\psi}/2 \approx 1.5\) GeV.
- The twist-2 fragmentation functions at large \(z\) are enhanced by about 10–30% due to nonlinear evolution from the coupling to twist-4 fragmentation functions.
- The NLP contribution becomes significant around \(p_T = 30\) GeV and below; a \(K\)-factor \(K_{\rm NLP}=2\) is needed to achieve agreement with CMS data.

## Context
This work extends the QCD factorization formalism for heavy quarkonium production previously developed by Nayak, Qiu, and Sterman (2005) and by Kang, Qiu, and collaborators (2014), who derived the factorization theorems and evolution kernels for twist-2 and twist-4 fragmentation functions. It provides the first numerical implementation of the coupled evolution equations and demonstrates that the approach can describe LHC data on prompt \(J/\psi\) production, complementing fixed-order NRQCD calculations (e.g., Chao et al., 2012) that do not fully resum logarithmic enhancements. The results enable systematic improvements by including higher-order perturbative corrections and more precise non-perturbative input.
