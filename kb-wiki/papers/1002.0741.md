# Astrophysical S-factors for fusion reactions involving C, O, Ne and Mg isotopes

> Using the Sao Paulo potential and the barrier penetration formalism we have calculated the astrophysical factor S(E) for 946 fusion reactions involving stable and neutron-rich isotopes of C, O, Ne, and Mg for center-of-mass energies E varying from 2 MeV to 18-30 MeV (covering the range below and above the Coulomb barrier). We have parameterized the energy dependence S(E) by an accurate universal 9-parameter analytic expression and present tables of fit parameters for all the reactions. We also discuss the reduced 3-parameter version of our fit which is highly accurate at energies below the Coulomb barrier, and outline the procedure for calculating the reaction rates. The results can be easily converted to thermonuclear or pycnonuclear reaction rates to simulate various nuclear burning phenomena, in particular, stellar burning at high temperatures and nucleosynthesis in high density environments.

## Key claim

Using the Sao Paulo potential and barrier penetration formalism, the authors calculated the astrophysical S-factor S(E) for 946 fusion reactions involving stable and neutron-rich isotopes of C, O, Ne, and Mg, covering center-of-mass energies from 2 MeV to 18-30 MeV. The abstract's claim of an "accurate universal 9-parameter analytic expression" is hedged by the fact that the fit quality is quantified by a parameter delta (ranging from 3 to 13 in the tables), indicating varying precision across different reaction systems.

## Method

The astrophysical S-factor S(E) was calculated using the Sao Paulo potential combined with the barrier penetration formalism for 946 fusion reactions. The energy dependence of S(E) was parameterized using a universal 9-parameter analytic expression, with fit parameters tabulated for each reaction. A reduced 3-parameter version of the fit was also provided, which is highly accurate at energies below the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers

* 946 fusion reactions were calculated, involving stable and neutron-rich isotopes of C, O, Ne, and Mg.
* Center-of-mass energies E varied from 2 MeV to 18-30 MeV, covering the range below and above the Coulomb barrier.
* For C+C reactions, the Coulomb barrier parameter E_C ranges from 4.651 MeV (for 24C+24C) to 7.134 MeV (for 12C+12C).
* For C+Mg reactions, E_C ranges from 8.394 MeV (for 24C+46Mg) to 12.871 MeV (for 10C+20Mg).
* For C+Ne reactions, E_C ranges from 6.983 MeV (for 24C+40Ne) to 10.947 MeV (for 12C+18Ne).
* For C+O reactions, E_C ranges from 6.967 MeV (for 16C+28O) to 9.120 MeV (for 12C+14O).
* The fit quality parameter delta ranges from 3 to 13 across all tabulated reactions.

## Context

This work extends the systematic calculation of fusion cross sections for light and intermediate-mass nuclei using the Sao Paulo potential, a method previously applied by Gasques et al. (2005) and others. The parameterized S-factors provide a comprehensive database that can be directly converted to thermonuclear or pycnonuclear reaction rates, enabling simulations of stellar burning at high temperatures and nucleosynthesis in high-density environments. The results enable future studies of nuclear burning phenomena in astrophysical contexts such as superbursts and neutron star crusts.
