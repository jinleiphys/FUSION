# Unitarity constraint for threshold coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron and chiral perturbation theory

> The contribution of the two-step process gamma + d -> p + n -> pi0 + d to the imaginary part of the amplitude for coherent pion production on the deuteron is calculated exploiting unitarity constraints. The result shows that this absorptive process is not negligible and has to be considered in an extraction of the elementary neutron production amplitude from the gamma + d -> pi0 + d cross section at threshold. In addition, it is argued that a consistent calculation of gamma + d -> pi0 + d in baryon chiral perturbation theory beyond next-to-leading order requires the inclusion of this absorptive process.

## Key claim

The two-step absorptive process gamma + d -> p + n -> pi0 + d contributes a non-negligible imaginary part to the coherent pion photoproduction amplitude at threshold, and a consistent chiral perturbation theory calculation beyond next-to-leading order must include this process; the magnitude of the imaginary part alone is comparable to the three-body contribution of order nu=0 calculated by Beane et al. (1997).

## Method

The author applied the unitarity constraint for coupled two-particle channels to the three channels pi0 d, pn, and gamma d at the pi0 production threshold, restricting to partial waves of angular momentum and parity J^pi = 1^-. The imaginary part of the electric dipole amplitude E_pi0d was expressed in terms of the E1 matrix element for deuteron photodisintegration and the amplitude for pn -> pi0 d, using experimental data for the hadronic cross section from Hutcheon et al. (1991) and a theoretical value for the photodisintegration cross section from an updated model of Wilhelm and Arenhoevel (1993). The power counting within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory was then analyzed to determine where the absorptive contribution fits in the chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

* Imaginary part of the electric dipole amplitude: |Im E_d| = 0.22 x 10^(-3)/m_pi+, where E_d = E_pi0d/4, obtained using sigma(gamma d -> ^3P_1(pn); E1) = 10.5 microbarn and the experimental limit lim_{p_pi0d -> 0} 2 (m_pi / p_pi0d) sigma(pn -> pi0 d) = 184 +/- 5 +/- 13 microbarn.
* Imaginary part of the magnetic quadrupole amplitude: |Im M_d| = 0.018 x 10^(-3)/m_pi+, where M_d = M_pi0d/4, more than two orders of magnitude smaller than the E1 contribution.
* The chiral perturbation theory prediction for the real electric dipole amplitude from Beane et al. (1997): E_d^chiPT = -1.8 x 10^(-3)/m_pi+, using the neutron electric dipole amplitude E_0+^(pi0 n) = 2.13 x 10^(-3)/m_pi+ from Bernard et al. (1996).
* The three-body contribution of order nu=0 calculated by Beane et al. (1997): E_d^(tb,4) = -0.25 x 10^(-3)/m_pi+.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations of coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron by Beane et al. (1995, 1997), which predicted a real threshold amplitude and did not include the absorptive two-step process. The author notes an analogy to pi d elastic scattering, where the absorptive process pi d -> NN -> pi d contributes about 10% of the total amplitude (Ericson and Weise, 1988). The result enables a more complete extraction of the elementary neutron amplitude gamma n -> pi0 n from the gamma d -> pi0 d cross section, which is experimentally not directly accessible; a first measurement of the ed -> e pi0 d reaction near q^2_mu = -0.075 GeV^2/c^2 was planned at MAMI.
