# Estimate magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions via the direct photon elliptic flow

> There must be electromagnetic fields created during high-energy heavy-ion collisions. As the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) starts to evolve hydrodynamically, although these fields may become weak comparing to the energy scales of the strong interaction, they are potentially important to some electromagnetic probes. In this work, we focus on the dissipative corrections in QGP due to the presence of a weak external magnetic field, and calculate accordingly the induced photon radiation in the framework of viscous hydrodynamics. By event-by-event hydrodynamical simulations, the experimentally measured direct photon elliptic flow can be well reproduced. Correspondingly, the direct photon elliptic flow implies a magnetic field strength around 0.1$m_π^2 \sim 10^{16}$ G. This is indeed a weak field in heavy-ion physics that is compatible to the theoretical predictions, however, it is still an ultra-strong magnetic field in nature.

## Key claim

The experimentally measured direct photon elliptic flow can be well reproduced by event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations that include dissipative corrections from a weak external magnetic field, implying a field strength around 0.1 m_pi^2 ~ 10^16 G during the QGP evolution, a weak field in heavy-ion physics but still ultra-strong in nature.

## Method

The authors calculated photon radiation from QGP by incorporating dissipative corrections to the quark distribution function due to a weak external magnetic field, using the Chapman-Enskog method to solve the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation in the relaxation time approximation. They implemented this formulation in event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at top RHIC energy, and compared the resulting direct photon elliptic flow to experimental data from PHENIX and ALICE.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength implied by direct photon elliptic flow: 0.1 m_pi^2 ~ 10^16 G
- Initial magnetic field at top RHIC energy: |eB|/m_pi^2 ~ 10
- Initial magnetic field at LHC: |eB|/m_pi^2 ~ 10^2
- Magnetic field decays as 1/t^3, dropping to 10^-2 m_pi^2 ~ 10^15 G in less than 1 fm/c
- Strongest magnetic field observed in neutron stars: 1.6 x 10^13 G
- QGP temperature squared ranges from ~1.2 m_pi^2 to ~10 m_pi^2 in heavy-ion experiments

## Context

This work extends the formulation developed by Sun and Yan (2023) for photon production in QGP with weak magnetic fields, applying it to realistic event-by-event simulations. It addresses the long-standing disagreement between theoretical predictions and experimental measurements of direct photon elliptic flow from PHENIX (2015) and ALICE (2018). The results enable using direct photon elliptic flow as a probe to estimate magnetic field strength in QGP, complementing earlier theoretical predictions by McLerran (2013), Skokov (2009), Bzdak (2011), and Deng (2012) for initial magnetic fields, and later work by Yan (2021), Stewart (2021), and Huang (2022) on magnetic field decay in conducting QGP.
