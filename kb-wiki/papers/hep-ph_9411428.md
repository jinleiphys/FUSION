# QCD sum rules and chiral logarithms

> Standard QCD sum-rule analyses of the nucleon mass give results that are inconsistent with chiral perturbation theory due to an overly simple continuum ansatz on the phenomenological side of the sum rule. We show that a careful treatment of the continuum, including $π$-$N$ states and other states with virtual pions, resolves the inconsistency associated with chiral logs.

## Key claim

Standard QCD sum-rule analyses of the nucleon mass produce a spurious chiral logarithm term proportional to \(m_q \ln m_q\) that is inconsistent with chiral perturbation theory, but this inconsistency is resolved when the continuum contribution to the sum rule includes \(\pi\)-\(N\) states and other states with virtual pions, provided the weighting function covers a region well above the \(\pi\)-\(N\) threshold.

## Method

The authors used the Ioffe interpolating fields for the nucleon to construct the time-ordered correlation function \(\Pi_N(q)\), decomposed it into scalar functions \(\Pi_1\) and \(\Pi_q\), and applied a Borel-transform-like sum rule with a weighting function \(W(s)\). They evaluated the leading nonanalytic chiral behavior of the OPE side and the phenomenological side separately, using soft-pion theorems and spectral representations to compute the contributions from nucleon-pole, antinucleon-pole, and \(\pi\)-\(N\) continuum states. The chiral expansions of both sides were then compared to determine whether the spurious \(m_q \ln m_q\) term cancels in the ratio that gives the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate is \(\langle\overline{q}q\rangle \simeq -(225\ \text{MeV})^3\) and the gluon condensate is \(\langle(gG)^2\rangle \simeq 0.5\ \text{GeV}^4\).
- The uncertainty in the nucleon mass from the inconsistent chiral-log behavior was estimated at \(\sim 100\ \text{MeV}\), with a comparable uncertainty for the \(\sigma\) term.
- The lack of the \(m_q^{3/2}\) term (from subleading nonanalytic contributions) leads to uncertainties of \(\sim 15\ \text{MeV}\) for the nucleon mass and \(\sim 20\ \text{MeV}\) for the \(\sigma\) term.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Griegel and Cohen (1994), who first pointed out the inconsistency between the standard QCD sum-rule treatment of the nucleon mass and chiral perturbation theory. The resolution presented here is reminiscent of the treatment of octet baryons at finite temperature by Koike (1993). The results enable future QCD sum-rule analyses to incorporate low-lying \(\pi\)-\(N\) continuum states explicitly, removing one source of uncertainty, though the \(m_q^{3/2}\) term from subleading nonanalytic contributions remains a challenge that may require a model-independent treatment of soft-to-hard pion corrections.
