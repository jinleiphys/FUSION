# Isospin effect on nuclear stopping in intermediate energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> By using the Isospin Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model (IQMD), we study the dependence of nuclear stopping Q_{ZZ}/A and R in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions on system size, initial N/Z, isospin symmetry potential and the medium correction of two-body cross sections. We find the effect of initial N/Z ratio, isospin symmetry potential on stopping is weak. The excitation function of Q_{ZZ}/A and R depends on the form of medium correction of two-body cross sections, the equation of state of nuclear matter (EOS). Our results show the behavior of the excitation function of Q_{ZZ}/A and R can provide clearer information of the isospin dependence of the medium correction of two-body cross sections.

# Isospin effect on nuclear stopping in intermediate energy Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Using the IQMD model, the authors find that nuclear stopping (measured by \(Q_{ZZ}/A\) and \(R\)) depends only weakly on the initial \(N/Z\) ratio and the isospin symmetry potential, but the excitation function of stopping is strongly affected by the form of medium correction of two-body cross sections. The key hedge is that this conclusion applies to the energy range studied (50–400 AMeV) and that the influence of the equation of state (EOS) on stopping becomes comparable to that of cross-section medium corrections for the heavier system \(^{120}\text{Sn}+^{120}\text{Sn}\), whereas for the medium-mass system \(^{58}\text{Ni}+^{58}\text{Ni}\) the EOS effect is much weaker.

## Method
The authors performed IQMD simulations for two groups of symmetric colliding systems: \(A=60\) nuclei with \(Z=24\)–\(32\) and \(A=120\) nuclei with \(Z=47\)–\(55\), at beam energies of 50, 150, and 400 AMeV. They compared four forms of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections: free cross section \(\sigma_0\), a density-dependent form \(\sigma_0^*\) with \(\alpha=-0.2\), a Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock based form \(\sigma_1\) from Li and Machleidt (1993–1994), and an isospin-dependent medium-corrected form \(\sigma_2\) derived from QHD-II by Li et al. (2000). The mean field was varied using a soft EOS (\(K=200\) MeV) and a hard EOS (\(K=380\) MeV), with and without the isospin symmetry potential.

## Key numbers
- The soft EOS uses incompressibility \(K=200\) MeV and the hard EOS uses \(K=380\) MeV, both at normal density \(\rho_0 = 0.168\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the medium correction \(\sigma_0^*\), the formula is \(\sigma^* = [1 + \alpha(\rho/\rho_0)]\sigma^{\text{free}}\) with \(\alpha = -0.2\).
- The excitation function of \(Q_{ZZ}/A\) saturates at about 150–300 AMeV, depending on the in-medium cross section used.
- For \(R\), the slope of the excitation function changes sign at \(E \sim 100\) AMeV for impact parameters \(b=0\) fm to \(b=3\) fm in \(^{120}\text{Sn}+^{120}\text{Sn}\).
- The dependence of nuclear stopping on impact parameter is weak at low energy and becomes stronger above \(\sim 50\)–\(100\) AMeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nuclear stopping and equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions (referenced as Cu81 through Li94) by incorporating isospin asymmetry, which became accessible with radioactive beam facilities. It builds on the study by Liu et al. (2001), which tested sensitivity of stopping to isospin dependence of cross sections but did not examine the isospin dependence of medium corrections or initial system asymmetry. The results suggest that measuring the excitation function of stopping for medium-mass systems like \(^{58}\text{Ni}+^{58}\text{Ni}\) can provide clearer information on the isospin dependence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, disentangling it from EOS effects that become comparable in heavier systems.
