# Inhomogeneous Chiral Phase in Quark Matter

> Recent development of inhomogeneous chiral phase in Kyoto group is briefly reviewed. First, the nesting effect of the Fermi surface is emphasized as a key mechanism leading to inhomogeneous chiral phase. After introduction of inhomogeneous chiral phase, some topological aspect is discussed in 1+1 dimensions and in the presence of the magnetic field in 1+3 dimensions. Spectral asymmetry gives rise to anomalous quark number and is closely related to chiral anomaly. It may induce spontaneous magnetization and a novel Lifshitz point may appear on the line $μ=0$. Some astronomical implications are briefly discussed. Finally, the effects of the fluctuations around the order parameter are discussed; the propagator of the fluctuations exhibits a singularity at finite momentum and changes the properties of the phase transition through loop diagrams.

# Inhomogeneous Chiral Phase in Quark Matter

## Key claim
The inhomogeneous chiral phase (iCP) may appear near the chiral transition in the QCD phase diagram due to the nesting effect of the Fermi surface, but nesting is complete only in 1+1 dimensions and incomplete in 1+3 dimensions, so the DCDW phase in 1+3 dimensions is described as a "reminiscence of nesting" rather than a fully realized nesting effect.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model within the mean-field approximation to study the dual chiral density wave (DCDW) configuration, where the order parameter rotates in chiral space as a plane wave along one spatial direction. They analyze the energy spectrum by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian, examine the nesting mechanism for the Fermi surface, and extend the analysis to include magnetic fields and the effects of chiral pair fluctuations (CPF) beyond mean-field through ring diagrams and renormalized vertex functions.

## Key numbers
- The DCDW phase develops in the intermediate density region (3-5)ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≃ 0.17 fm⁻³ is the nuclear saturation density
- The neutrino emissivity in the DCDW phase is estimated as ε_DCDW ≃ 6.1 × 10²⁶ (ρ_B/ρ₀)^{2/3} Y_e^{2/3} T_9⁶ erg·cm⁻³·s⁻¹ near the onset density, where T_9 ≡ T/10⁹ K
- The magnetic field produced on the surface of a magnetar from spontaneous magnetization in the DCDW phase is estimated to be 10¹⁶ G
- The anomalous number density from the lowest Landau level in the presence of magnetic field is ρ_an = (eB/2π)(q/2π) for q/2 < m, μ < q/2 + m
- The wave number in the chiral spiral phase in 1+1 dimensions satisfies q = 2μ everywhere due to chiral anomaly

## Context
This work reviews recent developments by the Kyoto group on inhomogeneous chiral phases, extending earlier studies of the DCDW configuration (Nakano and Tatsumi, 2005) and real kink crystal (Nickel, 2009). The nesting mechanism is analogous to that in spin-density waves and charge-density waves, and shares features with the FFLO state of superconductivity. The fluctuation analysis applies the Brazovskii-Dyugaev effect to show that chiral pair fluctuations change the phase transition from second-order to fluctuation-induced first-order. The results enable further studies of compact star cooling, magnetar magnetic field origins, and possible "chiral crystal" solidification in high-density cores.
