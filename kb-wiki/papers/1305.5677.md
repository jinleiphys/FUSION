# Parton densities from LHC vector boson production at small and large transverse momenta

> The parton densities of the proton are of fundamental importance not only for our description of hadronic and nuclear structure, but also for reliable predictions for new heavy particle searches at colliders. At the large partonic momentum fractions required for the production of these particles, the parton distribution functions, in particular the one of the gluon, are unfortunately still badly constrained. In this paper, we investigate the possibility to improve on their determination with new data coming from electroweak vector boson production at large transverse momenta at the LHC with center-of-mass energies of 7, 8 or 14 TeV. We demonstrate that this process is dominated by quark-gluon scattering, that theoretical predictions can be reliably made on the basis of next-to-leading order perturbation theory and its resummation, and that these data should thus be used in global fits. We also point out that the non-perturbative parameters determined from Tevatron run-1 Z-boson data at low p_T describe very well the new LHC data at \sqrt{s}=7 TeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that electroweak vector boson (W and Z) production at large transverse momenta at the LHC is dominated by quark-gluon scattering (the QCD "Compton" process) and can therefore be used to constrain the poorly-known gluon parton distribution function at large partonic momentum fractions x, provided that theoretical predictions combine next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbation theory with soft-gluon resummation at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) level.

## Method

The authors computed transverse-momentum (p_T) spectra for W and Z boson production at the LHC using a full NLO calculation matched to soft-gluon resummation at NLL level based on the Collins-Soper-Sterman formalism in impact-parameter (b) space. They decomposed the cross section into partonic subprocesses to identify the relative contribution of quark-gluon versus quark-antiquark scattering, and compared their theoretical predictions to published CMS and ATLAS data at sqrt(s)=7 TeV. They then used the CT10, MSTW08, and NNPDF2.1 parton distribution function (PDF) sets to evaluate the sensitivity of the cross sections to the gluon PDF at large x, estimating uncertainties from both PDF variations and scale variations.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s)=7 TeV, the quark-gluon subprocess dominates Z-boson production for p_T > 20 GeV and W-boson production for p_T > 15 GeV, contributing 75-80% of the cross section up to 3 TeV.
- At sqrt(s)=14 TeV, the quark-gluon contribution peaks above 85% at p_T ~ 100 GeV, with a local minimum of about 70% at p_T ~ 1.5 TeV.
- The gluon PDF uncertainty from CT10 at scale Q=1 TeV is +22/-17% at x=0.3 and +43/-28% at x=0.4.
- The transition from the resummation-dominated to the perturbative regime occurs at p_T ~ 75 GeV for Z bosons and p_T ~ 65 GeV for W bosons at sqrt(s)=7 TeV; at sqrt(s)=14 TeV this transition shifts to p_T ~ 90 GeV for Z bosons and p_T ~ 80 GeV for W bosons.
- The scale uncertainty from varying factorization and renormalization scales by a factor of two is ±10% to ±15% at large p_T.
- The non-perturbative BLNY parameters (g1=0.21, g2=0.68, g3=-0.60) fitted to Tevatron run-1 Z-boson data at starting scale Q0=1.6 GeV describe LHC data at sqrt(s)=7 TeV very well.
- CMS data for Z bosons at sqrt(s)=7 TeV extend to p_T = 600 GeV with 35.9 pb^-1; ATLAS data for W bosons extend to p_T = 300 GeV with 31 pb^-1.
- At sqrt(s)=14 TeV with 100 fb^-1, the reach in p_T should increase to at least 2 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Berger and Klasen, 1998, 1999) to use low-mass lepton pairs as a surrogate for prompt photons to constrain the gluon density, now applied to massive electroweak boson production at the much higher LHC energies. It contrasts with current practice in global PDF analyses (CT10 by Lai et al., 2010; MSTW08 by Martin et al., 2009; NNPDF2.1 by Ball et al., 2011), which use W and Z production only in the Drell-Yan mode at low p_T to constrain quark flavour decomposition. The paper enables future global fits to include LHC vector boson data at high p_T to improve the gluon PDF at large x, and shows that the non-perturbative resummation parameters from Tevatron data (Landry et al., 2002) remain valid for LHC data.
