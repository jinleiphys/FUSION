# Stable gapless superconductivity at strong coupling

> We study cross-flavor Cooper pairing in a relativistic system of two fermion species with mismatched Fermi surfaces. We find that there exist gapless phases which are characterized by either one or two gapless nodes in the energy spectra of their quasiparticles. An analysis of the current-current correlator reveals that, at strong coupling, both of these gapless phases can be free of magnetic instabilities and thus are stable. This is in contrast to the weak-coupling case where there are always two gapless nodes and the phase becomes magnetically unstable.

## Key claim

At strong coupling, gapless superconducting phases with either one or two gapless nodes in the quasiparticle spectrum can be free of magnetic instabilities and thus stable, in contrast to the weak-coupling case where the phase always has two gapless nodes and becomes magnetically unstable.

## Method

The authors study a relativistic model of two fermion species with equal masses but mismatched chemical potentials, using a local interaction term that drives cross-flavor Cooper pairing. They compute the zero-temperature phase diagram in the mean-field approximation and calculate the fermion-number current-current correlator in the static, long-wavelength limit to determine the magnetic screening mass, which signals instability when imaginary.

## Key numbers

- For fixed mismatch $\delta\mu/m = 0.5$ and mass $m/\Lambda = 0.2$, the magnetic screening mass squared $m_M^2$ is positive definite in the whole gSC(1) region and in part of the gSC(2) region, but becomes negative for $\bar\mu/m \gtrsim 1.8$ in the entire range $\Delta/\delta\mu < 1$.
- The boundary of the gSC(1) region satisfies $(\Delta/\delta\mu)^2 + ((\bar\mu - m)/\delta\mu)^2 \leq 1$.
- The coupling constant is normalized by $G_0 = 4\pi / \Lambda^2$; for $m=0$ and $G \geq G_0$, the vacuum is unstable with respect to Bose-Einstein condensation of diquarks.
- The phase diagrams are shown for $m/\Lambda = 0.1$, $0.2$, and $0.3$ with $\delta\mu/m = 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends studies of gapless color-superconducting phases in dense quark matter, such as the gapless 2-flavor color-superconducting (g2SC) phase proposed by Shovkovy and Huang (2003) and the gapless color-flavor-locked (gCFL) phase. It directly addresses the chromomagnetic instability found in those phases by Huang and Shovkovy (2004) and others, showing that at strong coupling the instability can be absent. The results connect to studies of Bose-Einstein condensation of diquarks and the crossover from BCS to BEC pairing, and are expected to remain qualitatively similar in non-relativistic models as argued by Sheehy and Radzihovsky (2005) and others.
