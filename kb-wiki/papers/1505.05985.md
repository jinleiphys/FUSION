# Stable Yang-Lee zeros in truncated fugacity series from net-baryon number multiplicity distribution

> We investigate Yang-Lee zeros of grand partition functions as truncated fugacity polynomials of which coefficients are given by the canonical partition functions $Z(T,V,N)$ up to $N \leq N_{\text{max}}$. Such a partition function can be inevitably obtained from the net-baryon number multiplicity distribution in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where the number of the event beyond $N_{\text{max}}$ has insufficient statistics, as well as canonical approaches in lattice QCD. We use a chiral random matrix model as a solvable model for chiral phase transition in QCD and show that the closest edge of the distribution to real chemical potential axis is stable against cutting the tail of the multiplicity distribution. The similar behavior is also found in lattice QCD at finite temperature for Roberge-Weiss transition. In contrast, such a stability is found to be absent in the Skellam distribution which does not have phase transition. We compare the number of $N_{\text{max}}$ to obtain the stable Yang-Lee zeros with those of critical higher order cumulants.

## Key claim

The closest edge of the Yang-Lee zero distribution to the real chemical potential axis is stable against truncation of the fugacity polynomial (i.e., cutting off the tail of the net-baryon multiplicity distribution) in systems that exhibit a phase transition, but this stability is absent in the Skellam distribution which has no phase transition.

## Method

The authors used a chiral random matrix model extended to have the correct periodicity in imaginary chemical potential as a solvable model for the chiral phase transition in QCD. They computed the exact grand partition function and then reconstructed truncated fugacity series from the canonical partition functions up to a maximum net-baryon number N_max, solving for the Yang-Lee zeros in the complex chemical potential plane using high-precision arithmetic (50-300 digits). They compared the zero distributions from the truncated series to the exact ones for the chiral random matrix model, lattice QCD at finite temperature for the Roberge-Weiss transition, and the Skellam distribution.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point in the chiral limit is located at T_3 ≈ 0.731 T_c and μ_3 ≈ 4.504 (in units of σ ≈ 100 MeV).
- The critical point at finite quark mass m=0.05 is located at T_CP = 0.675 T_c and μ_CP = 4.72.
- The chiral transition temperature at μ=0 is T_c = 160 MeV, corresponding to a = 0.2 in the model.
- The first order phase transition at T=0 occurs at μ_c ≈ 1200 MeV with b=0.13.
- The model parameters are a=0.2, b=0.13, and σ ≈ 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of Yang-Lee zeros in QCD phase transitions, building on the chiral random matrix model approach of Stephanov (2006) and the canonical partition function method of Nakamura (2013). It addresses a practical limitation in both heavy-ion experiments and lattice QCD calculations: the truncation of the fugacity series due to finite statistics or computational resources. The results enable the use of Yang-Lee zeros from experimentally measured net-baryon multiplicity distributions to probe phase transitions, and provide a criterion for how many moments of the distribution must be measured to obtain stable information about the phase structure.
