# How perturbative QCD constrains the Equation of State at Neutron-Star densities

> We demonstrate in a general and analytic way how high-density information about the equation of state (EoS) of strongly interacting matter obtained using perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (pQCD) constrains the same EoS at densities reachable in physical neutron stars. Our approach is based on utilizing the full information of the thermodynamic potentials at the high-density limit together with thermodynamic stability and causality. This requires considering the pressure as a function of chemical potential $p(μ)$ instead of the commonly used pressure as a function of energy density $p(ε)$. The results can be used to propagate the pQCD calculations reliable around 40$n_s$ to lower densities in the most conservative way possible. We constrain the EoS starting from only few times the nuclear saturation density $n \gtrsim 2.2 n_s$ and at $n = 5 n_s$ we exclude at least 65% of otherwise allowed area in the $(ε- p)$-plane. This provides information complementary to astrophysical observations that should be taken into account in any complete statistical inference study of the EoS. These purely theoretical results are independent of astrophysical neutron-star input, and hence, they can also be used to test theories of modified gravity and BSM physics in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that using the full thermodynamic potential \(p(\mu)\) (pressure as a function of baryon chemical potential) rather than the reduced form \(p(\epsilon)\) (pressure as a function of energy density) yields stricter constraints on the neutron-star equation of state at intermediate densities, and that these constraints are independent of any specific interpolation function between the low-density and high-density limits.

## Method

The authors consider all possible interpolations of the thermodynamic grand canonical potential \(\Omega(\mu) = -p(\mu)\) at zero temperature between a low-density limit (from chiral effective theory, CET) and a high-density limit (from perturbative QCD, pQCD), imposing thermodynamic stability (monotonic density) and causality (speed of sound \(c_s^2 \leq 1\)). They derive analytic bounds on the allowed density as a function of chemical potential by requiring that the integrated area under the \(n(\mu)\) curve matches the known pressure difference \(\Delta p = p(\mu_Q) - p(\mu_C)\), and then map these bounds onto the energy-density–pressure plane using the Euler relation \(\epsilon = -p + \mu n\).

## Key numbers

- At \(n = 5 n_s\) (where \(n_s = 0.16/\text{fm}^3\)), at least 65% of the otherwise allowed area in the \((\epsilon, p)\)-plane is excluded by the pQCD input (for renormalization scale parameter \(X=1\)); for \(X=2\), only 25% of the \(\epsilon-p\) values remain allowed.
- At \(n = 10 n_s\), the allowed range is reduced to approximately 6.5% of the total area (for \(X=2\)).
- The lowest density at which the high-density input limits the EoS from above is \(n_{\text{min}}(\mu_C) = [2.2, 2.5]\, n_s\) for \(X=[1,4]\); from below it is \(n_{\text{max}}(\mu_C) = [4.8, 9.0]\, n_s\).
- The minimum limiting speed of sound for which the low- and high-density limits can be connected consistently is \(\csminlim = [0.30, 0.32]\) for \(X=[1,4]\), just below the asymptotic conformal value \(c_s^2 = 1/3\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of earlier studies that used large ensembles of parameterized EoSs to interpolate between low- and high-density limits (Kurkela 2014, Annala 2017, Most 2018) by deriving analytic, interpolation-independent constraints. It goes beyond previous bounds that used only the reduced form \(p(\epsilon)\) (Rhoades 1974, Koranda 1996, Lope-Oter 2019) by exploiting the full information of the grand canonical potential. The results enable purely theoretical constraints that are complementary to astrophysical neutron-star observations and can be used in statistical inference studies of the EoS, as well as in tests of modified gravity and beyond-standard-model physics in neutron stars.
