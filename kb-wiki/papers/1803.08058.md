# Short-lived modes from hydrodynamic dispersion relations

> We consider the dispersion relation of the shear-diffusion mode in relativistic hydrodynamics, which we generate to high order as a series in spatial momentum q for a holographic model. We demonstrate that the hydrodynamic series can be summed in a way that extends through branch cuts present in the complex q plane, resulting in the accurate description of multiple sheets. Each additional sheet corresponds to the dispersion relation of a different non-hydrodynamic mode. As an example we extract the frequencies of a pair of oscillatory non-hydrodynamic black hole quasinormal modes from the hydrodynamic series. The analytic structure of this model points to the possibility that the complete spectrum of gravitational quasinormal modes may be accessible from the hydrodynamic derivative expansion.

# Wiki Page: Short-lived modes from hydrodynamic dispersion relations

## Key claim

The hydrodynamic derivative expansion of the shear-diffusion mode dispersion relation can be summed through branch cuts in the complex momentum plane to accurately describe multiple sheets, each corresponding to a different non-hydrodynamic mode. The claim is conditional on the specific holographic model (Einstein-Maxwell theory in AdS₄) and on the particular values of T/μ studied, where the branch points at q = ±i q_* are the closest non-analyticities to q=0 and set a finite radius of convergence.

## Method

The author computes the Taylor expansion of the shear-diffusion mode dispersion relation ω(q) to high order (up to N=40 in q², i.e. hydrodynamic order 79) by solving linear gravitational perturbation equations on a Reissner-Nordstrom black brane background in AdS₄, using a shooting method. The series is then analytically continued past square-root branch points by introducing quadratic conformal maps (first z, then u) that unfold the multi-sheeted structure, and the resulting series is summed as a diagonal Pade approximant to extract the dispersion relations of non-hydrodynamic quasinormal modes.

## Key numbers

* The shear-diffusion mode dispersion relation coefficients at T/μ = 1/(4π) are tabulated for n=1 to 40; the leading coefficient is -i ω₁ q_* = -0.250 (dimensionless), where q_* ≡ (ε+p)/(2μ√η).
* The branch point location is q = ±i q_*, with q_* = (ε+p)/(2μ√η); the closest pole of the Pade approximant to q=0 is at q ≃ 0.753i, compared to the exact branch point at iq_* ≃ 0.750i.
* A second branch point is located at q = i q_⊲ ≃ 0.69iμ from hydrodynamic data, compared to the exact holographic value iq_⊲ ≃ 0.70iμ.
* The extracted non-hydrodynamic mode frequency at q=0 on the second sheet is ω(0)/μ = -0.7493 - 0.5182i (and its complex conjugate on the third sheet), agreeing with exact numerics to four significant digits at hydrodynamic order 79.

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic series convergence initiated by Heller (2013) and others, who showed that the late proper-time expansion of the stress tensor has zero radius of convergence and that non-hydrodynamic modes appear as non-perturbative contributions in a transseries. Unlike those works which used Borel-Pade methods on one-point functions, Withers directly accesses non-hydrodynamic mode dispersion relations by analytically continuing the hydrodynamic series in complex momentum, exploiting the linearized hydrodynamic framework to reach high orders. The method enables extraction of black hole quasinormal mode frequencies from purely hydrodynamic data, and suggests that the complete quasinormal mode spectrum may be accessible from the derivative expansion, with potential applications to steady flows over obstacles as studied by Sonner (2017).
