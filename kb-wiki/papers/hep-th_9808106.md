# The Relation of Constraints on Particle Statistics for Different Species of Particles

> Quons are particles characterized by the parameter $q$, which permits smooth interpolation between Bose and Fermi statistics; $q=1$ gives bosons, $q=-1$ gives fermions. In this paper we give a heuristic argument for an extension of conservation of statistics to quons with trilinear couplings of the form $\bar{f}fb$, where $f$ is fermion-like and $b$ is boson-like. We show that $q_f^2=q_b$. In particular, we relate the bound on $q_γ$ for photons to the bound on $q_e$ for electrons, allowing the very precise bound for electrons to be carried over to photons. An extension of this argument suggests that all particles are fermions or bosons to high precision.

## Key claim
The paper argues that for quons (particles with continuous statistics parameter q) coupled via trilinear interactions of the form $\bar{f}fb$, the relation $q_f^2 = q_b$ holds approximately, allowing the extremely precise experimental bound on violations of Fermi statistics for electrons to be carried over to photons. The argument relies on approximations that drop terms of order $1-q_e^2$ and is explicitly described as heuristic.

## Method
The authors model photon emission as a quon field coupled to an external quonic Grassmann source, then replace that source with a bilinear product of electron creation and annihilation operators. Using the quon algebra relations and working to leading order in $1-q_e^2$, they rearrange operator products to derive the relation $q_e^2 \approx q_\gamma$ between the electron and photon q-parameters.

## Key numbers
- Ramberg-Snow bound on electron statistics violation: $v_e \leq 1.7 \times 10^{-26}$, corresponding to $q_e \leq -1 + 3.4 \times 10^{-26}$
- Derived bound on photon statistics violation: $q_\gamma \geq 1 - 6.8 \times 10^{-26}$, corresponding to $v_\gamma \leq 3.4 \times 10^{-26}$

## Context
This work extends the concept of conservation of statistics, previously established for bosons and fermions by Streater and Wightman (1964) and for parabosons and parafermions by Greenberg and Messiah (1965), to the quon formalism that allows continuous small violations of Bose and Fermi statistics. The result enables the extremely precise electron bound from the Ramberg and Snow (1990) experiment to constrain photon statistics far more tightly than any direct experiment could, though the authors note that Budker and DeMille are performing a direct test of photon statistics. The argument also suggests that all particles coupled bilinearly to photons must obey Bose or Fermi statistics to comparable precision.
