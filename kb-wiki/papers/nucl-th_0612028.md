# Decay Properties Of The Dipole Isobaric Analog Resonances

> A continuum-RPA-based approach is applied to describe the decay properties of isolated dipole isobaric analog resonances in nuclei having not-too-large neutron excess. Calculated for a few resonances in 90Zr the elastic E1-radiative width and partial proton widths for decay into one-hole states of 89Y are compared with available experimental data.

# Decay Properties Of The Dipole Isobaric Analog Resonances

## Key claim
The continuum-RPA-based approach can describe the gross and direct decay properties of isolated dipole isobaric analog resonances (DIAR) in nuclei with not-too-large neutron excess, but the description relies on treating the isobaric analog state as an "ideal" one that exhausts 100% of the Fermi strength (whereas real IAR exhaust about 95%), and the approximations used lead to uncertainties in DIAR energies of presumably a few hundred keV.

## Method
The authors apply a continuum-RPA (CRPA) approach based on a phenomenological version of Migdal's finite Fermi-system theory, using a realistic isoscalar mean field with self-consistently calculated isovector and Coulomb parts. They calculate the GDR$_>$ strength function from the parent-nucleus GDR$^{(+)}$ strength function via an isospin transformation, then extract DIAR properties including elastic E1-radiative widths, form factors, and partial proton widths from pole representations of the effective field and strength functions. Proton pairing is approximately included by substituting occupation numbers with Bogolyubov coefficients.

## Key numbers
- The DIAR in $^{90}$Zr at $E_x = 16.28$ MeV has been experimentally identified in reactions with protons, electrons, and photons.
- The strength parameters used are $U_0 = 54.9$ MeV, $U_{S0} = 13.85$ MeV fm$^2$, and $f' = 1.03$ (where $F' = f' \times 300$ MeV fm$^3$).
- The parameter $\widetilde{k}' = 0.1$ describes the relative contribution of momentum-dependent forces to the energy-weighted sum rule for $^{90}$Zr.
- The energy gap parameter for proton pairing is taken as 1.0 MeV.
- The excitation energy interval studied is 14-18 MeV, corresponding to the low-energy part of the GDR$^{(+)}$ strength function.
- The calculated DIAR at 16.1 MeV is related to the experimentally studied resonance; its elastic E1-radiative width and partial proton widths for decay into $2p_{1/2}$, $1g_{9/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, and $1f_{5/2}$ one-hole states in $^{89}$Y are compared with experimental data from Cosel (2002).
- Apparatus averaging of the $(\gamma p_{tot})$-reaction cross section is performed with $I = 50$ and 100 keV.

## Context
This work extends the CRPA-based approach previously applied to charge-exchange giant resonances by Moukhai (2002) and Gorelik (2005) to the specific case of dipole isobaric analog resonances, which are the isobaric analogs of the charge-exchange giant dipole resonance (GDR$^{(+)}$). The study is motivated by experimental results from Cosel (2002) on the DIAR in $^{90}$Zr using $(e,e'p)$ reactions, which had remained unexplained. The approach enables prediction of additional DIAR (at calculated energies 16.7 and 17.2 MeV) that could be found experimentally via the $(e,e'p)$-reaction, and provides a framework for describing the decay properties of these doubly-collective $1^-$-IAR states.
