# Structural and decay properties of $Z=132,138$ superheavy nuclei

> In this paper, we analyze the structural properties of $Z=132$ and $Z=138$ superheavy nuclei within the ambit of axially deformed relativistic mean-field framework with NL$3^{*}$ parametrization and calculate the total binding energies, radii, quadrupole deformation parameter, separation energies, density distributions. We also investigate the phenomenon of shape coexistence by performing the calculations for prolate, oblate and spherical configurations. For clear presentation of nucleon distributions, the two-dimensional contour representation of individual nucleon density and total matter density has been made. Further, a competition between possible decay modes such as $α$-decay, $β$-decay and spontaneous fission of the isotopic chain of superheavy nuclei with $Z=132$ within the range 312 $\le$ A $\le$ 392 and 318 $\le$ A $\le$ 398 for $Z=138$ is systematically analyzed within self-consistent relativistic mean field model. From our analysis, we inferred that the $α$-decay and spontaneous fission are the principal modes of decay in majority of the isotopes of superheavy nuclei under investigation apart from $β$ decay as dominant mode of decay in $^{318-322}138$ isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the majority of the studied superheavy isotopes with Z=132 and Z=138, alpha-decay and spontaneous fission are the principal decay modes, but with the explicit qualification that beta-decay is the dominant mode for the specific neutron-rich isotopes $^{318-322}138$ (i.e., Z=138 with mass numbers 318 to 322).

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* parametrization with a constant-gap BCS pairing approximation, solving the equations in a harmonic oscillator basis of N_F = N_B = 20. They calculated binding energies, charge and matter radii, and quadrupole deformation parameters for prolate, oblate, and spherical configurations of isotopic chains with Z=132 (A = 312 to 392) and Z=138 (A = 318 to 398). They then analyzed the competition between alpha-decay, beta-decay, and spontaneous fission for these nuclei using the self-consistent RMF results.

## Key numbers

* Binding energy for $^{312}132$ (prolate configuration): 2103.0 MeV; for $^{392}132$ (prolate configuration): 2502.9 MeV.
* Binding energy for $^{318}138$ (prolate configuration): 2091.4 MeV; for $^{395}138$ (oblate configuration): 2531.8 MeV (truncated value).
* Quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$ for $^{312}132$ (spherical): 0.073; for $^{320}132$ (prolate): 0.496; for $^{318}138$ (prolate): 0.542.
* Charge radius $r_c$ for $^{312}132$ (spherical): 6.457 fm; for $^{318}138$ (prolate): 6.809 fm.
* The neutron range studied was 180 $\le$ N $\le$ 260, covering the suggested neutron magic numbers N=198, 228, 238, and 258.
* The doubly magic systems identified include $^{330}132$, $^{360}132$, $^{370}132$, $^{366}138$, $^{376}138$, and $^{396}138$.

## Context

This work extends the study of superheavy nuclei beyond the currently synthesized elements (Z up to 118) to the unexplored region of Z=132 and 138, which were suggested as proton magic numbers by Zhang (2005) and Bhuyan (reference in text). The calculations use the NL3* parameter set, a recent refinement of the NL3 force, and compare binding energies with the finite range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (reference in text) where available, finding close agreement. The results enable predictions for the dominant decay modes of these yet-unsynthesized nuclei, guiding future experimental searches for superheavy elements in this region.
