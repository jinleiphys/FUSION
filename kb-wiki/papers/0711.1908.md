# Scaling functions applied to three-body recombination of Cesium-133 atoms

> We demonstrate the implications of Efimov physics in the recently measured recombination rate of Cesium-133 atoms. By employing previously calculated results for the energy dependence of the recombination rate of Helium-4 atoms, we obtain three independent scaling functions that are capable of describing the recombination rates over a large energy range for identical bosons with large scattering length. We benchmark these and previously obtained scaling functions by successfully comparing their predictions with full atom-dimer phase shift calculations with artificial Helium-4 potentials yielding large scattering lengths. Exploiting universality, we finally use these functions to determine the 3-body recombination rate of Cesium-133 atoms with large positive scattering length, compare our results to experimental data obtained by the Innsbruck group and find excellent agreement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three-body recombination rates for Cesium-133 atoms can be accurately predicted using universal scaling functions extracted from Helium-4 atom-atom potentials, provided one assumes the s11 function in the S-matrix remains negligible for all energies. The authors relax all but this one simplifying assumption made in prior work by Braaten (2006) and extract three independent universal functions rather than two.

## Method

The authors extracted three universal functions (r12, r22, and Phi) by fitting the S-matrix parameterization of Eq. (4) to recombination rates calculated from four different Helium-4 potentials (HDFB, TTY, LM2M2, and HFDB3FCII). They benchmarked these functions against full atom-dimer phase shift calculations using three artificial potentials with different three-body parameters but similar range-to-scattering-length ratios as the real Helium potentials. Finally, they applied both the new three-function set and previously obtained two-function scaling functions (h1 and h2) to compute the temperature-dependent recombination length for Cesium-133 atoms at 200 nK, incorporating deep dimer effects through the parameter eta_star.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is e^(pi/s0) ≈ 22.7, with s0 ≈ 1.00624
- The threshold value of s11(0) ≈ 0.002, justifying the assumption that s11 remains small
- The polynomial fits for h1 and h2 are valid over the energy range 0 < x < 1.2, where x = (m a^2 E / hbar^2)^(1/2)
- The Cesium-133 recombination length rho_3 was computed for a_star0 = 210 a0 (where a0 is the Bohr radius) at T = 200 nK, with eta_star values of 0, 0.01, and 0.06
- The three artificial potentials had a/a_star0 ratios of 1.384, 1.188, and 1.780 respectively

## Context

This work extends the universal scaling function approach of Braaten (2006) and Shepard (2007) by relaxing all but one simplifying assumption to obtain three independent universal functions capable of describing recombination rates over a larger energy range than previously possible. The paper demonstrates universality by successfully predicting Cesium-133 recombination rates measured by the Innsbruck group (Grimm, 2006) using functions extracted from Helium-4 potentials, showing that few-body systems with large scattering length exhibit universal features across completely different atomic species. The results enable future calculations that could include range corrections systematically through effective field theory, as noted by Bedaque (2002), Hammer (2001), and Platter (2006).
