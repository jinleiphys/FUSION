# An Alternative View of the Dynamical Origin of the P11 Nucleon Resonances: Results from the Excited Baryon Analysis Center

> We present an alternative interpretation for the dynamical origin of the P11 nucleon resonances, which results from the dynamical coupled-channels analysis at Excited Baryon Analysis Center of Jefferson Lab. The results indicate the crucial role of the multichannel reaction dynamics in determining the N^* spectrum.

# An Alternative View of the Dynamical Origin of the P11 Nucleon Resonances: Results from the Excited Baryon Analysis Center

## Key claim
The three P11 resonance poles below 2 GeV (including the two poles comprising the Roper resonance and the N*(1710) pole) all originate from a single bare N* state at 1763 MeV through its coupling to multichannel meson-baryon continuum states, rather than from multiple distinct bare states. The two-pole structure of the Roper resonance specifically requires coupling to the pi-Delta channel to emerge.

## Method
The EBAC dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model was used to analyze world data of gamma N, pi N to pi N, eta N, pi pi N, KY reactions up to 2 GeV. The partial wave amplitudes were calculated from coupled-channels integral equations incorporating meson-exchange potentials and bare N* propagators, with parameters fixed by fitting pi N scattering up to 2 GeV and gamma p to pi N and ep to e' pi N up to 1.6 GeV. The pole positions were obtained as zeros of the determinant of the inverse dressed N* propagator, and their dynamical origin was traced by continuously varying channel coupling strengths y_MB from 0 to 1.

## Key numbers
- Three P11 resonance poles found below Re(E) = 2 GeV: 1357 - i76 MeV (Pole A), 1364 - i105 MeV (Pole B), and 1820 - i248 MeV (Pole C)
- The bare N* state mass from which all three poles originate is 1763 MeV
- Two Roper resonance poles appear with Re(E) approximately 1350 MeV, stable against large variations of model parameters
- The analysis covers pi N scattering up to E = 2 GeV and gamma p to pi N and ep to e' pi N up to E = 1.6 GeV

## Context
This work extends the EBAC-DCC analysis reported in Sjklms (2010) and provides an alternative interpretation to static hadron structure calculations (quark models) that predict a much higher Roper mass. The finding that multichannel dynamics can generate multiple resonance poles from a single bare state was theoretically predicted by Eden and Taylor over four decades ago, with few prior experimental evidences (Morgan). This result enables a reinterpretation of the Roper resonance as a two-pole structure, consistent with findings from other groups (VPI 1985, Said 2006, CMB 1990, Julich 2009), and demonstrates that reaction dynamics cannot be neglected in understanding the hadron spectrum.
