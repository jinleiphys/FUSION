# Prompt double $J/ψ$ production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> We provide a detailed study of prompt double $J/ψ$ production within the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. We confront the recent LHC data with the results obtained at leading-order (LO) in the NRQCD framework within two approaches of the collinear factorization and the $k_T$-factorization. We show that the LHCb data are consistent with the $k_T$-factorized LO NRQCD results. We show that the full LO NRQCD formalism cannot describe the recent CMS data, with about one order of magnitude discrepancy. If the CMS data are confirmed, this indicates rather large higher-order corrections for prompt double $J/ψ$ production. We provide various predictions which can further test the NRQCD-based approach at the LHC in a kinematic region that LO contributions dominate. We also investigate long-range in rapidity double $J/ψ$ correlations. We found no evidence of a ridge-like structure for double $J/ψ$ production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC up to subleading $α_s^6$ accuracy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that leading-order (LO) non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) calculations, in both collinear factorization and kT-factorization approaches, are consistent with LHCb data for prompt double J/ψ production but cannot describe CMS data, with about one order of magnitude discrepancy; if the CMS data are confirmed, this indicates rather large higher-order corrections are needed.

## Method

The authors computed the cross-section for prompt double J/ψ production in proton-proton collisions at the LHC using the NRQCD framework at leading order (O(αs⁴)), including both color-singlet (CS) and color-octet (CO) contributions. They employed two approaches: standard collinear factorization with parton distribution functions (PDFs) and kT-factorization with unintegrated gluon densities (UGDs) from CCFM evolution, which incorporate small-x dynamics via initial-state gluon radiations. Theoretical uncertainties were quantified by varying the factorization/renormalization scale and using different UGD sets (A⁺, A⁰, A⁻), and the results were compared to LHCb and CMS data at √s = 7 TeV under the respective experimental kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- The leptonic decay width fit yields |R²_J/ψ(0)| = 0.8 GeV³ for the J/ψ radial wavefunction at the origin.
- The color-octet long-distance matrix elements used are ⟨O¹_J/ψ (³S₁[⁸])⟩ = 1.2 × 10⁻² GeV³ and ⟨O¹_J/ψ (³P₀[⁸])⟩ = 1.6 × 10⁻² GeV³.
- The discrepancy between full LO NRQCD results and CMS data can be as large as about two orders of magnitude at large invariant mass M (and/or large pair transverse momentum p_T^{J/ψ J/ψ}).
- The LHCb data are sampled at √s = 7 TeV with individual J/ψ transverse momentum 0 < p_T^{J/ψ} < 10 GeV and rapidity 2 < y^{J/ψ} < 4.5.
- The CMS data are sampled at √s = 7 TeV with individual J/ψ transverse momentum p_T^{J/ψ} > 6.5 GeV for |y^{J/ψ}| < 1.2, and p_T^{J/ψ} > 4.5 GeV for 1.43 < |y^{J/ψ}| < 2.2.
- The D0 collaboration found that (46 ± 22)% of double J/ψ production cross-section at √s = 1.96 TeV is due to double parton scattering.

## Context

This work extends the LO NRQCD analysis of prompt double J/ψ production by He and Kniehl (2015) in collinear factorization, by additionally employing the kT-factorization approach to incorporate small-x dynamics via unintegrated gluon densities. The paper confronts both formalisms with recent LHCb and CMS data, finding that the CMS data cannot be described at LO, suggesting large NLO corrections are needed, similar to the factor-of-10 enhancement seen for single inclusive J/ψ production at NLO. The study also investigates long-range rapidity correlations and finds no ridge-like structure for double J/ψ production up to subleading αs⁶ accuracy, contrasting with di-hadron correlations that exhibit such a ridge in high-multiplicity proton-proton events at the LHC.
