# Many Phases of Generalized 3D Instanton Crystals

> Nuclear matter at large number of colors is necessarily in a solid phase. In particular holographic nuclear matter takes the form of a crystal of instantons of the flavor group. In this article we initiate the analysis of the three-dimensional crystal structures and the orientation patterns for the two-body potential that follows from holographic duality. The outcome of the analysis includes several unexpected results. We perform simulations of ensembles of O(10000) instantons whereby we identify the lattice structure and orientations for the different values of the weight factors of the non-Abelian orientation terms in the two-body potential. The resulting phase diagram is surprisingly complex, including a variety of ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic crystals with various global orientation patterns, and various "non-Abelian" crystals where orientations have no preferred direction. The latter include variants of face-centered-cubic, hexagonal, and simple cubic crystals which may have remarkably large or small aspect ratios. The simulation results are augmented by analytic analysis of the long-distance divergences, and numerical computation of the (divergence free) energy differences between the non-Abelian crystals, which allows us to precisely determine the structure of the phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper claims that holographic nuclear matter at large $N_c$ forms a three-dimensional crystal of instantons, and that the phase diagram of such crystals is surprisingly complex, including ferromagnetic, antiferromagnetic, and "non-Abelian" phases; however, this result depends on the approximation of using only two-body interactions between instantons, which is adequate only for low-density crystals, and the analysis is performed for a generalized two-body potential with variable weight factors $\alpha$ and $\beta$ rather than the specific values derived from the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of ensembles of approximately 10000 instantons in a spherical cavity, using a relaxation algorithm with added drag forces and a compensating external force that sets the local instanton density constant, to find the lowest-energy crystal structures and orientation patterns. They augmented these simulations with analytic analysis of the long-distance divergences in the continuum limit and numerical computation of divergence-free energy differences between candidate non-Abelian crystals to precisely determine phase boundaries. The two-body potential used was a generalization of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model result, depending on two parameters $\alpha$ and $\beta$ that control the weights of different orientation-dependent terms.

## Key numbers

- The two-body potential scales as $1/|X_m - X_n|^2$ with a prefactor $2N_c/((1+2\alpha+2\beta)\lambda M)$
- For the oriented case $\alpha=\beta=1$, the crystal is face-centered tetragonal with aspect ratio $c \approx 2.467$, forming clearly separated layers with two-dimensional square lattice structure
- The phase boundary between oriented non-Abelian and anti-ferromagnetic phases occurs at $\alpha = \gamma_1 \beta$ with $\gamma_1 \approx 0.0575$
- The boundary between anti-ferromagnetic and spherical ferromagnetic phases occurs at $\beta = \Gamma \alpha$ with $\Gamma \approx 2.14$
- The boundary between global ferromagnetic and ferromagnetic egg phases occurs at $\alpha = \gamma_2 \beta$ with $\gamma_2 \approx 0.543$
- The interaction becomes attractive (leading to collapse) when $\alpha < -1/8$ or $\beta < -1/8$, restricting the phase diagram to $\alpha > -1/8$ and $\beta > -1/8$
- Two instantons with similar orientations repel each other 9 times stronger than those with orientations differing by a $180^\circ$ rotation around a suitable axis

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of one-dimensional and two-dimensional instanton crystals (Kaplunovsky 2012, Kaplunovsky 2013) to three dimensions, using the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model framework. The two-body potential was derived in earlier work by Kaplunovsky (2013) and Kim (2008), and the paper notes that related studies of dense instanton configurations using homogeneous non-Abelian fields were done by Rozali (2007), Li (2015), and Elliot-Ripley (2016). The results enable future analysis of transitions between three-dimensional and four-dimensional crystals with increasing density, and the methods developed could be applied to other non-Abelian lattices.
