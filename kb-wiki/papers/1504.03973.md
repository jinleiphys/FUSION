# Quark-hadron duality constraints on γZ box corrections to parity-violating elastic scattering

> We examine the interference γZ box corrections to parity-violating elastic electron--proton scattering in the light of the recent observation of quark-hadron duality in parity-violating deep-inelastic scattering from the deuteron, and the approximate isospin independence of duality in the electromagnetic nucleon structure functions down to Q^2 \approx 1 GeV^2. Assuming that a similar behavior also holds for the γZ proton structure functions, we find that duality constrains the γZ box correction to the proton's weak charge to be \Re e\, \square_{γZ}^V = (5.4 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-3} at the kinematics of the Q_{\text{weak}} experiment. Within the same model we also provide estimates of the γZ corrections for future parity-violating experiments, such as MOLLER at Jefferson Lab and MESA at Mainz.

## Key claim

Assuming that quark-hadron duality, which holds for electromagnetic nucleon structure functions down to Q² ≈ 1 GeV², also holds for the γZ proton structure functions, the γZ box correction to the proton's weak charge is constrained to Re e □_{γZ}^V = (5.4 ± 0.4) × 10⁻³ at the kinematics of the Q_weak experiment, with the uncertainty well within the range needed for an unambiguous extraction of the weak charge.

## Method

The authors compute the imaginary part of the γZ box correction using a dispersion integral over the γZ interference structure functions F₁^{γZ} and F₂^{γZ}, split into three kinematic regions: Region I (low Q², low W²) using a resonance-based parametrization rotated from electromagnetic to γZ; Region II (low Q², high W²) using a vector meson dominance plus Regge model; and Region III (high Q², high W²) using leading-twist parton distribution functions from the ABM global QCD fit. The real part is obtained via an unsubtracted dispersion relation. The key new element is extending the leading-twist description (Region III) down to Q² = 1 GeV², motivated by duality, and refitting the continuum rotation parameters κ_C^T and κ_C^L by matching the γZ to γγ cross section ratios with the leading-twist structure function ratios at Q² = 1 GeV².

## Key numbers

- Re e □_{γZ}^V = (5.4 ± 0.4) × 10⁻³ at the Q_weak experiment beam energy E = 1.165 GeV
- Re e □_{γZ}^V = (11.2 ± 0.7) × 10⁻³ at the MOLLER experiment beam energy E = 11 GeV
- Re e □_{γZ}^V = (1.2 ± 0.1) × 10⁻³ at the MESA experiment beam energy E = 0.18 GeV
- Continuum rotation parameters from matching at Q² = 1 GeV²: κ_C^T = 0.36 ± 0.15, κ_C^L = 1.5 ± 3.1
- At Q_weak energy, approximately 2/3 of the integral comes from the resonance region W < 2 GeV and Q² < 1 GeV²; the DIS region (W > 2 GeV, Q² > 1 GeV²) contributes ≈ 13%
- Average duality violation in the proton electromagnetic structure functions over 1 ≤ Q² ≤ 2.5 GeV² is 3% for F₁^{γγ} and 4% for F₂^{γγ}; maximum violation ≈ 5% and ≈ 10% at the lower end of the Q² range
- For the neutron, average duality violation is 5% for F₁^{γγ} and 8% for F₂^{γγ} over the same Q² range

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hall et al. (2013) on γZ box corrections to parity-violating elastic scattering, which used the Adelaide-Jefferson Lab-Manitoba (AJM) parametrization with a higher Q² boundary (2.5 GeV²) for the DIS region. The new constraint comes from the first direct observation of quark-hadron duality in parity-violating deep-inelastic scattering from the deuteron by the E08-011 experiment at Jefferson Lab (Wang et al., 2013, 2014), and from the demonstration of approximate isospin independence of duality in electromagnetic structure functions (Niculescu et al., 2015). The result enables more precise extraction of the proton's weak charge from the Q_weak experiment and provides predictions for future experiments such as MOLLER at Jefferson Lab and MESA at Mainz, with the uncertainty well within the 4% accuracy goal of Q_weak.
