# Unpolarized proton PDF at NNLO from lattice QCD with physical quark masses

> We present a lattice QCD calculation of the unpolarized isovector quark parton distribution function (PDF) of the proton utilizing a perturbative matching at next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO). The calculations are carried out using a single ensemble of gauge configurations generated with $N_f = 2 + 1$ highly-improved staggered quarks with physical masses and a lattice spacing of $a = 0.076$ fm. We use one iteration of hypercubic smearing on these gauge configurations, and the resulting smeared configurations are then used for all aspects of the subsequent calculation. For the valence quarks, we use the Wilson-clover action with physical quark masses. We consider several methods for extracting information on the PDF. We first extract the lowest four Mellin moments using the leading-twist operator product expansion approximation. Then, we determine the $x$ dependence of the PDF through a deep neural network within the pseudo-PDF approach and additionally through the framework of large-momentum effective theory utilizing a hybrid renormalization scheme. This is the first application of the NNLO matching coefficients for the nucleon directly at the physical point.

# Unpolarized proton PDF at NNLO from lattice QCD with physical quark masses

## Key claim
This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the unpolarized isovector quark PDF of the proton using NNLO perturbative matching directly at the physical point (physical pion mass), but the results have significantly larger errors than global fits, and the authors note that statistical errors dominate over any potential systematic error.

## Method
The calculation uses a single ensemble of gauge configurations with N_f = 2+1 highly-improved staggered quarks at physical masses and lattice spacing a = 0.076 fm, with Wilson-clover valence quarks. The PDF is extracted through three complementary approaches: (1) leading-twist operator product expansion to extract the lowest four Mellin moments, (2) a deep neural network within the pseudo-PDF approach to determine the x-dependence, and (3) large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) with a hybrid renormalization scheme. Excited-state contamination in three-point functions is controlled using two-state fits and summation methods with n_exc = 3.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.076 fm
- Ground-state energy for n_z = 0: E_0 = 0.9345(57) GeV
- First excited-state energy for n_z = 0: E_1 = 1.533(71) GeV
- Lowest moment from DNN approach: ⟨x⟩ = 0.200(17) for q^+(x) with z_max = 0.92 fm
- Second moment from DNN approach: ⟨x^2⟩ = 0.069(12) for q^-(x) with z_max = 0.92 fm
- Third moment from DNN approach: ⟨x^3⟩ = 0.031(10) for q^+(x) with z_max = 0.92 fm
- Fourth moment from DNN approach: ⟨x^4⟩ = 0.014(9) for q^-(x) with z_max = 0.92 fm
- χ^2/N_data for q^-(x) DNN fit with z_max = 0.76 fm: 0.52 (27 data points)
- χ^2/N_data for q^+(x) DNN fit with z_max = 0.76 fm: 0.78 (27 data points)

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD approach to PDFs by applying NNLO matching coefficients for the first time at the physical point, building on the pseudo-distribution framework developed by Radyushkin (2017) and Orginos et al. (2017), as well as the authors' previous work Gao et al. (2022). The results are compared to the global analysis from NNPDF4.0 (2021), finding agreement within the large statistical errors. The lowest moment ⟨x⟩ lies above the NNPDF4.0 result, which the authors note is typical of lattice calculations and likely due to unaccounted systematics such as discretization effects and higher-twist contributions. The LaMET results show some tension with both the DNN results and NNPDF4.0, suggesting a need for larger momenta and multiple ensembles in future work.
