# Fluctuations in Charged Particle Multiplicities in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Multiplicity distributions of charged particles and their event-by-event fluctuations have been compiled for relativistic heavy-ion collisions from the available experimental data at Brookhaven National Laboratory and CERN and also by the use of an event generator. Multiplicity fluctuations are sensitive to QCD phase transition and to the presence of critical point in the QCD phase diagram. In addition, multiplicity fluctuations provide baselines for other event-by-event measurements. Multiplicity fluctuation expressed in terms of the scaled variance of the multiplicity distribution is an intensive quantity, but is sensitive to the volume fluctuation of the system. The importance of the choice of narrow centrality bins and the corrections of centrality bin width effect for controlling volume fluctuations have been discussed. It is observed that the mean and width of the multiplicity distributions monotonically increase as a function of increasing centrality at all collision energies, whereas the multiplicity fluctuations show minimal variations with centrality. The beam energy dependence shows that the multiplicity fluctuations have a slow rise at lower collision energies and remain constant at higher energies.

## Key claim

The paper compiles and analyzes charged-particle multiplicity fluctuations from experimental data and AMPT model calculations, finding that the scaled variance shows minimal variation with centrality and a slow rise at lower collision energies that plateaus at higher energies, with no non-monotonic behavior that would signal a QCD phase transition or critical point.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental data from AGS (E802), SPS (WA98, NA49, NA61, CERES), and RHIC (PHENIX) experiments, and generated events using the AMPT model in both Default and String Melting modes for Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. Centrality was selected using charged particle multiplicity in the forward pseudorapidity range 2.0 < |η| < 3.0, while fluctuations were calculated in the central region |η| < 0.5 with 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c, applying centrality bin width corrections to minimize volume fluctuations. The scaled variance ω = σ²/μ was used as the fluctuation measure.

## Key numbers

- Scaled variance ωch for central collisions at higher energies is found to be approximately 2 within statistical precision
- Centrality bin width correction reduces fluctuations by close to 23% for central collisions and 8% for peripheral collisions when using 5% bins compared to 1% bins (shown for Au+Au at √sNN = 62.4 GeV)
- The fluctuation in number of particles per participant (ωn) varies from 0.98 at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 2.0 at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- AMPT model parameters used: fragmentation parameters a = 2.2, b = 0.5, strong coupling αs = 0.47, Debye screening mass μ = 1.8 fm⁻¹, giving total parton elastic cross section σgg = 10 mb

## Context

This work extends prior measurements of multiplicity fluctuations from WA98 (1999), NA49 (2004), and PHENIX (2007) experiments by compiling them into a systematic study across beam energies and comparing with AMPT model calculations. The results provide baselines for future fluctuation studies at the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and LHC energies, and the absence of non-monotonic behavior in the data suggests no critical phenomenon for the systems studied at SPS and RHIC, consistent with the expectation that AMPT contains no phase transition physics. The paper also compares with theoretical predictions from the hadron resonance gas model in grand canonical, canonical, and micro canonical ensembles as calculated by Begun (2004).
