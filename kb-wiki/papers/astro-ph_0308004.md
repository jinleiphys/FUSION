# Neutrinos and Nucleosynthesis in Gamma-Ray Burst Accretion Disks

> We calculate the nuclear composition of matter in accretion disks surrounding stellar mass black holes as are thought to accompany gamma-ray bursts (GRBs). We follow a mass element in the accretion disk starting at the point of nuclear dissociation and calculate the evolution of the electron fraction due to electron, positron, electron neutrino and electron antineutrino captures. We find that the neutronization of the disk material by electron capture can be reversed by neutrino interactions in the inner regions of disks with accretion rates of 1 M_solar/s and higher. For these cases the inner disk regions are optically thick to neutrinos, and so to estimate the emitted neutrino fluxes we find the surface of last scattering for the neutrinos (the equivalent of the proto-neutron star neutrinosphere) for each optically thick disk model. We also estimate the influence of neutrino interactions on the neutron-to-proton ratio in outflows from GRB accretion disks, and find it can be significant even when the disk is optically thin to neutrinos.

# Neutrinos and Nucleosynthesis in Gamma-Ray Burst Accretion Disks

## Key claim

The neutronization of accretion disk material by electron capture can be reversed by neutrino interactions in the inner regions of disks with accretion rates of 1 solar mass per second and higher, but this reversal is limited to the optically thick regions; outside these regions, geometric effects and competition between neutrino and antineutrino captures severely limit the influence of neutrinos on the electron fraction, even when the disk is optically thin to neutrinos.

## Method

The authors follow a mass element radially inward from the point of nuclear dissociation (T=10^10 K) to the inner disk edge, calculating the dynamical evolution of the electron fraction via electron, positron, electron neutrino, and electron antineutrino capture rates. For optically thin regions, they use an iterative scheme to compute effective neutrino fluxes including geometric solid-angle factors, height corrections from vertical disk structure, and extinction corrections from scattering; for optically thick regions, they assume weak equilibrium with lepton number conservation and define neutrino decoupling surfaces analogous to proto-neutron star neutrinospheres. They apply this method to the disk models of PWF (1999) for lower accretion rates and DPN (2002) for higher accretion rates where neutrino opacity is included self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- For the DPN model with accretion rate dot-m = 1, the electron fraction in the inner disk reaches approximately 0.24 after neutrino trapping, compared to values closer to 0.1 without neutrino interactions.
- Neutrino decoupling temperatures range from T_nu = 2.5 to 4.5 MeV and T_antinu = 3.6 to 5.1 MeV depending on the disk model.
- The boundary between optically thin and optically thick regions is defined at tau_nu, tau_antinu = 2/3.
- In the PWF model with dot-m = 0.1, alpha = 0.1, and spin parameter a = 0.95, neutrino trapping occurs only within a narrow region between r = 10 and 14 km, where the crossing time is comparable to the neutrino capture time of about half a millisecond.
- The optical depths calculated by the authors agree with DPN to within a maximum of 20% for the dot-m = 10 case.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Pruet et al. (2002), who considered only forward electron and positron capture reactions and were therefore limited to lower accretion rate disk models where neutrinos are less important. The authors include both forward and reverse reactions (neutrino and antineutrino capture) and find that for accretion rates of 1 solar mass per second and higher, neutrino interactions significantly alter the electron fraction in the inner disk, whereas for lower rates the results do not differ significantly from Pruet et al. (2002). The study uses disk models from PWF (1999) for optically thin cases and DPN (2002) for optically thick cases, and the results enable future self-consistent calculations that simultaneously include both the electron fraction evolution and the disk structure.
