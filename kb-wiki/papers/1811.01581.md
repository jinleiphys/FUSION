# Determining $α_s$ from hadronic $τ$ decay: the pitfalls of truncating the OPE

> We discuss sum-rule determinations of $α_s$ from non-strange hadronic $τ$-decay data. We investigate, in particular, the reliability of the assumptions underlying the "truncated OPE strategy," which specifies a certain treatment of non-perturbative contributions, and which was employed in Refs. [1-3]. Here, we test this strategy by applying the strategy to the $R$-ratio obtained from $e^+e^-$ data, which extend beyond the $τ$ mass, and, based on the outcome of these tests, we demonstrate the failure of this strategy.We then present a brief overview of new results on the form of duality-violating non-perturbative contributions, which are conspicuously present in the experimentally determined spectral functions. As we show, with the current precision claimed for the extraction of $α_s$, including a representation of duality violations is unavoidable if one wishes to avoid uncontrolled theoretical errors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "truncated OPE strategy" used in prior determinations of $\alpha_s$ from hadronic $\tau$ decay (e.g., Pich, ALEPH, OPAL) fails when tested against $e^+e^-$ data at energies above $m_\tau^2$, and that including a representation of duality violations is unavoidable at the current level of precision claimed for $\alpha_s$.

## Method

The authors test the truncated OPE strategy by applying it to electromagnetic $R(s)$ data from $e^+e^-$ annihilation, which extends above the $\tau$ mass. They perform fits to $s_0=m_\tau^2$ versions of weighted spectral integrals using the same assumptions as in $\tau$-decay analyses (truncating the OPE at finite dimension and neglecting duality violations), then compare the resulting OPE predictions to actual spectral integrals at $s_0>m_\tau^2$. They also review recent progress deriving the form of duality-violating contributions from Regge behavior of the meson spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The $ud$ $V+A$ spectral function shows the non-parton-model part is approximately zero at $s\simeq 2\ {\rm GeV}^2$, indicating DV and $\alpha_s$-dependent perturbative contributions cancel each other in that region.
- The EM FESR tests use data up to $s=4\ {\rm GeV}^2$ for the $I=1$ component of the EM spectral function, obtained from a $G$-parity based isovector/isoscalar separation.
- The optimal weight set includes weights $w^{(2n)}(y)$ with $n=1,\cdots,5$, involving OPE contributions up to $D=16$ in principle, but fits assume contributions from $C_{10}$, $C_{12}$, $C_{14}$, and $C_{16}$ are negligible.
- The $(kl)$ spectral weight set includes weights up to degree 7, involving OPE contributions up to $D=16$, with fits assuming $C_{10}$, $C_{12}$, $C_{14}$, and $C_{16}$ can be neglected.

## Context

This work extends and contradicts the analyses of Pich (2008), ALEPH (1998), and OPAL (1999), which employed the truncated OPE strategy assuming integrated duality violations are negligible for doubly and triply pinched weights at $s_0=m_\tau^2$ and that the OPE behaves as if rapidly convergent. The paper builds on earlier work by Boito et al. (2015) showing that self-consistency tests in Pich (2008) fail to detect numerically relevant DVs in a mock-data model. The results enable future $\tau$-decay analyses that must include explicit DV parametrizations, such as those developed in Cata et al. (2005, 2008) and confirmed by the Regge-based derivation in this paper.
