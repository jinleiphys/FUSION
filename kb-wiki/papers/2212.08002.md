# Coexistence of quartets and pairs in even-even $N>Z$ nuclei

> We analyse the structure of the ground states of even-even $N>Z$ nuclei with nucleons moving in the same major shell and interacting via realistic two-body forces of shell-model type. We express the ground states of these nuclei as a product of a quartet term, which represents the $N = Z$ subsystem, and a pair condensate built with the excess neutrons. The accuracy of this approximation is discussed for nuclei with valence nucleons in the $sd$ and $pf$ major shells.

## Key claim

The ground states of even-even \(N>Z\) nuclei with valence nucleons in the same major shell can be approximated as a product of a \(T=0\) quartet core (representing the \(N=Z\) subsystem) and a neutron pair condensate, with errors in ground-state energy remaining below 5% for all tested nuclei in the \(sd\) and \(pf\) shells.

## Method

The authors construct the ground state as \(|\Omega(m,n)\rangle = (P^+)^m |\Psi_0(n)\rangle\), where \(|\Psi_0(n)\rangle\) is a \(J=0, T=0\) state built from a configuration-interaction calculation over \(T=0\) quartets \(q^+_{JM}\) (with angular momenta \(J=0,2,4\)), and \(P^+\) creates a collective \(J=0, T=1\) neutron pair. The quartet core and the neutron pair amplitudes are determined iteratively by minimizing the energy of the product state, alternating between fixing the quartet core and the neutron condensate. The approximation is tested against exact shell-model diagonalizations using the USDB interaction for \(sd\)-shell nuclei and the KB3G interaction for \(pf\)-shell nuclei, with the code BIGSTICK.

## Key numbers

- Relative errors in ground-state energies for self-conjugate nuclei with 2 and 3 quartets are under 0.7%.
- For all analyzed nuclei, the relative error in ground-state energy remains below 5%.
- Errors increase significantly when the first neutron pair is added to self-conjugate nuclei, especially for Ne and Ti isotopes.
- Errors decrease rapidly when passing from one to two extra neutron pairs in Ne and Ti isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the quartet-pair description previously developed for proton-neutron pairing Hamiltonians (Dobes 2008, Sandulescu 2012) to realistic shell-model interactions with general two-body forces. It builds on the earlier approach of Arima and Gillet (1971) for quartet-pair bases in shell-model calculations, but proposes a simpler variational product form. The results enable future applications of quartet-pair structures to heavier nuclei and suggest that the simple product ansatz, previously validated only for schematic pairing forces, remains reasonably accurate for realistic interactions in the \(sd\) and \(pf\) shells.
