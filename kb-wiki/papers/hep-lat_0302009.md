# Low-Dimensional Long-Range Topological Charge Structure in the QCD Vacuum

> While sign-coherent 4-dimensional structures cannot dominate topological charge fluctuations in the QCD vacuum at all scales due to reflection positivity, it is possible that enhanced coherence exists over extended space-time regions of lower dimension. Using the overlap Dirac operator to calculate topological charge density, we present evidence for such structure in pure-glue SU(3) lattice gauge theory. It is found that a typical equilibrium configuration is dominated by two oppositely-charged sign-coherent connected structures (``sheets'') covering about 80% of space-time. Each sheet is built from elementary 3-d cubes connected through 2-d faces, and approximates a low-dimensional curved manifold (or possibly a fractal structure) embedded in the 4-d space. At the heart of the sheet is a ``skeleton'' formed by about 18% of the most intense space-time points organized into a global long-range structure, involving connected parts spreading over maximal possible distances. We find that the skeleton is locally 1-dimensional and propose that its geometrical properties might be relevant for understanding the possible role of topological charge fluctuations in the physics of chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

While sign-coherent 4-dimensional structures cannot dominate topological charge fluctuations in the QCD vacuum at all scales due to reflection positivity, the authors present evidence that enhanced sign-coherence exists over extended space-time regions of lower dimension, specifically that a typical equilibrium configuration is dominated by two oppositely-charged connected structures covering about 80% of space-time.

## Method

The authors used the overlap Dirac operator to calculate the topological charge density operator \(q_x\) at every space-time point on pure-glue SU(3) Wilson gauge configurations at two lattice spacings (ensembles \(\mathcal{E}_1\) at \(\beta=5.91\) with \(12^4\) volume and \(\mathcal{E}_2\) at \(\beta=6.07\) with \(16^4\) volume, both with physical volume 3.0 fm\(^4\)). They identified maximal connected coherent regions built from elementary 4-d cubes (connected through 3-d faces) and from elementary 3-d cubes (connected through 2-d faces), and studied the fraction of space-time points occupied by the largest such structures. They also defined subsets \(\mathcal{S}^f\) containing the \(fV\) most intense sites ranked by \(|q_x|\) and measured the average maximal Euclidean distance reachable within a single coherent structure from points in the top 1% most intense sites, as a function of \(f\).

## Key numbers

* Two oppositely-charged sign-coherent connected structures (sheets) cover about 80% of space-time in a typical equilibrium configuration.
* The skeleton, formed by about 18% of the most intense space-time points, is organized into a global long-range structure with connected parts spreading over maximal possible distances.
* For 4-d cubes, the average number of cubes per large structure was about 35.3 at lattice spacing \(a=0.110\) fm and about 29.4 at \(a=0.082\) fm, indicating that 4-d structures shrink in lattice units and likely do not survive the continuum limit.
* For the skeleton at fraction \(f=0.18\), the average lattice coherence dimension \(\langle d^L(\mathcal{S}^{f\pm})\rangle\) extrapolates to approximately 1.5 in the continuum limit, leading to the conclusion that the skeleton is locally 1-dimensional (\(1 \le d \lesssim 1.5\)).
* The super-long-distance state (average maximal distance \(\approx 0.9\) of the maximum possible) is reached at \(f \approx 0.8\) for 3-d cubes, \(f \approx 0.4\) for 2-d cubes, and \(f \approx 0.2\) for 1-d cubes.

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction by Witten (1979) that topological charge is not concentrated in unit-quantized instanton lumps, and the lattice evidence for this presented by Horvath et al. (2002). It uses the overlap Dirac operator and the associated topological charge density operator of Hasenfratz et al. (1998), which satisfies the Ginsparg-Wilson relation and provides a local operator with chiral smoothing that suppresses ultraviolet noise. The finding of a low-dimensional skeleton structure suggests a possible mechanism for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking via the propagation of massless quarks on a 1-dimensional manifold, and the super-long-distance property of the skeleton may be relevant for understanding Goldstone boson propagation.
