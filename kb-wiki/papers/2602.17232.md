# Quarkonium in non-zero isospin chemical potential environment at $T \simeq 0$

> We study how the isospin asymmetry affects quarkonium states in QCD at near zero temperature. Using lattice Non-Relativistic QCD formalism, we calculate bottom quark correlators in the gauge field ensembles generated with $N_f = 2 + 1$ flavors of dynamical staggered quarks whose dynamics include the isospin chemical potential effect and then construct $S-$ and $P-$ wave quarkonium state correlators. From these quarkonium correlators, we consider the ratios of quarkonium correlators at non-zero isospin chemical potential to that at $μ_I a = 0.000$. Here, the gauge field ensemble with $μ_I a = 0.000, 0.048, 0.053, 0.059, 0.066, 0.080, 0.092$ and $0.106$ on a $32^3 \times 48$ lattice with non-zero isospin current strength $λa = 0.0010, 0.0018,$ and $0.0036$, where $m_π= 135$ MeV and $a = 0.1535$ fm from \cite{Brandt:2022hwy}, are used. Preliminary results suggest that for $μ_I a = 0.106$, the Upsilon mass gets heavier than the Upsilon mass in the vacuum and that below $μ_I a = 0.106$ the isospin asymmetry effect on the Upsilon mass is not monotonic.

## Key claim

The isospin chemical potential effect on the Upsilon mass is small (less than 3% at the maximum Euclidean time) and non-monotonic as a function of isospin chemical potential; only at the largest value studied (μ_I a = 0.106) does the Upsilon mass become clearly heavier than in vacuum, while below that value the effect is not monotonic and may even produce a slightly lighter mass.

## Method

The authors use lattice Non-Relativistic QCD (NRQCD) at O(v^4) to compute bottom quark propagators on N_f = 2+1 dynamical staggered quark gauge field ensembles that include the isospin chemical potential effect. The ensembles have a 32^3 × 48 lattice volume with lattice spacing a = 0.1535 fm and pion mass m_π = 135 MeV, and include eight values of μ_I a (0.000, 0.048, 0.053, 0.059, 0.066, 0.080, 0.092, 0.106) each with three values of the (u,d) current source λa (0.0010, 0.0018, 0.0036). S-wave and P-wave quarkonium correlators are constructed from the NRQCD propagators, and the effect of isospin asymmetry is studied via ratios of correlators at non-zero μ_I to those at μ_I a = 0.000.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.1535 fm, pion mass m_π = 135 MeV, lattice volume 32^3 × 48.
- Eight values of isospin chemical potential used: μ_I a = 0.000, 0.048, 0.053, 0.059, 0.066, 0.080, 0.092, 0.106.
- Three values of (u,d) current source: λa = 0.0010, 0.0018, 0.0036.
- Tuned bottom quark mass parameter M_b a = 3.3177; the lattice spin-averaged 1S mass is a M_2^{lattice} = 7.386(32), compared to the experimental value a M_2(1S)^{exp} = 7.3480.
- The isospin chemical potential effect on the Upsilon correlator is less than 3% at τ/a = 48.
- Number of gauge configurations per ensemble ranges from 102 to 200.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quarkonium in isospin-asymmetric QCD by Detmold (2012) and of quarkonium in SU(2) gauge theory at non-zero baryon chemical potential by Hands (2012). The results are consistent with Detmold (2012) in finding a small, non-monotonic mass shift for the Upsilon, but differ qualitatively from the SU(2) case where quarkonium masses become significantly lighter at high baryon density. The study enables future quantitative investigation of how pion condensation affects heavy quarkonium and whether quarkonium states can serve as a "densimeter" for QCD at high baryon densities.
