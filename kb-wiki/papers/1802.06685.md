# New class of compact stars: Pion stars

> We investigate the viability of a new type of compact star whose main constituent is a Bose-Einstein condensate of charged pions. Several different setups are considered, where a gas of charged leptons and neutrinos is also present. The pionic equation of state is obtained from lattice QCD simulations in the presence of an isospin chemical potential, and requires no modeling of the nuclear force. The gravitationally bound configurations of these systems are found by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations. We discuss weak decays within the pion condensed phase and elaborate on the generation mechanism of such objects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that gravitationally bound configurations of a Bose-Einstein condensate of charged pions, termed "pion stars", can exist as a new class of compact stellar objects, provided that the isospin chemical potential exceeds the pion mass and that local charge neutrality is maintained by a lepton gas, with the equation of state derived from first-principle lattice QCD simulations rather than nuclear force modeling.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD simulations with physical quark masses and a staggered fermion discretization on a 24³×32 ensemble at near-zero temperature to compute the isospin density and axial vector condensate as functions of the isospin chemical potential, extrapolating an auxiliary pion source parameter to zero via a Banks-Casher relation. The resulting equation of state was combined with free relativistic lepton and neutrino contributions under local charge neutrality and weak equilibrium conditions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations, with stability assessed via the Sturm-Liouville equation for radial oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Pure pion stars reach a maximum mass of 10.5(5) solar masses with a radius of 55(3) km and central energy density 57(5) MeV/fm³.
- Pion-electron stars reach a maximum mass of 250(10) solar masses with a radius of 3.3(2)×10⁴ km and central energy density 4.5(4)×10⁻⁵ MeV/fm³.
- Pion-muon stars reach a maximum mass of 18.9(4) solar masses with a radius of 267(8) km and central energy density 2.7(3) MeV/fm³.
- Pion-lepton-neutrino stars (with muon and electron families in chemical equilibrium) reach a maximum mass of 20.8(9) solar masses with a radius of 137(6) km and central energy density 7.5(7) MeV/fm³.
- The pion decay constant extracted from the lattice data fit is f_π = 133(4) MeV, in agreement with the physical value.
- The threshold for pion condensation is at μ_I = m_π ≈ 135 MeV, with the isospin density rising sharply beyond this point.

## Context

This work extends the concept of boson stars, previously studied with hypothetical elementary scalars (Kaup 1968, Colpi 1986) or Q-balls (Coleman 1985, Alford 1987), to a Standard-Model realization using charged pions, whose equation of state is obtained from first-principle lattice QCD rather than nuclear force modeling. It differs from earlier studies of neutron stars with pion condensate cores (Migdal 1979, 1990) by considering objects with zero baryon density and high isospin charge. The results enable future investigations of primordial pion star formation from lepton asymmetries in the early universe (Schwarz 2009, Abuki 2009, Wygas 2018) and predictions for neutrino and photon signatures from their evaporation.
