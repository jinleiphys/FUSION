# Nuclear PDFs from the LHeC perspective

> We study the prospects for constraining the nuclear parton distribution functions by small-x deep inelastic scattering. Performing a global fit of nuclear parton distribution functions including a sample of pseudodata representing expected measurements at the planned LHeC collider, we demonstrate that the accuracy of the present nuclear parton distributions could be be improved substantially. We also discuss the impact of flavour-tagged data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including simulated high-precision deep inelastic scattering pseudodata from the planned LHeC collider in a global fit of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) could substantially reduce the small-\(x\) uncertainties of nPDFs, particularly for gluons and sea quarks, but caution that these results represent a lower limit for the expected impact because the study only considered the small-\(x\) part of the LHeC kinematic reach.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading order (NLO) global fit of nuclear PDFs (similar to the EPS09 analysis) that included pseudodata for inclusive reduced cross-section ratios \(\sigma_{\rm reduced}^{\rm Ca}/\sigma_{\rm reduced}^{\rm p}\) and \(\sigma_{\rm reduced}^{\rm Pb}/\sigma_{\rm reduced}^{\rm p}\) in the kinematic region \(x<0.01\) and \(2\,{\rm GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 1000\,{\rm GeV}^2\), assuming a 50 GeV lepton beam, 7000 GeV proton beam, 3500 GeV/nucleon Calcium ions, and 2750 GeV/nucleon Lead ions. A second fit (Fit 2) additionally included pseudodata for charm-tagged and bottom-tagged reduced cross-section ratios to estimate the improvement from flavour-tagged data.

## Key numbers

- The pseudodata cover the kinematic region \(x<0.01\) and \(2\,{\rm GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 1000\,{\rm GeV}^2\).
- The assumed lepton beam energy is 50 GeV, proton beam 7000 GeV, Calcium ions 3500 GeV/nucleon, and Lead ions 2750 GeV/nucleon.
- The error bands for the nuclear modifications are calculated by restricting \(\chi^2\) to remain within \(\Delta \chi^2=50\) from its minimum.
- The initial scale for the nuclear PDFs is \(Q^2_0=1.69\,{\rm GeV}^2\), with results also shown at \(Q^2=100\,{\rm GeV}^2\).
- The full LHeC kinematic reach would extend up to \(x\sim 0.6\) and \(Q^2 \sim 10^6\,{\rm GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the EPS09 analysis (Eskola 2009) by demonstrating how LHeC data could dramatically reduce the small-\(x\) uncertainties that currently plague nuclear PDFs, where DIS measurements for nuclear targets are limited to \(x \gtrsim 10^{-2}\) compared to the \(x \sim 10^{-5}\) reach of HERA data for free protons. The study enables future global fits with realistic LHeC data and shows that inclusive DIS provides a cleaner environment for nPDF extraction than processes like \(\pi^0\) production in d+Au collisions (Adler 2006), which are complicated by fragmentation function uncertainties (Sassot 2009).
