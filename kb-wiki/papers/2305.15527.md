# Imprints of the nuclear symmetry energy slope in gravitational wave signals emanating from neutron stars

> We investigate possible traces of the nuclear symmetry energy slope ($L$) in the gravitational wave emission of neutron stars. For fixed stellar mass values, we examine how the slope influences the stellar radius, compactness, the tidal deformability, the frequency of the quadrupole fundamental fluid mode, and the damping time of the mode due to the gravitational wave emission. We demonstrate that all these physical quantities are sensitive to the slope and could potentially impose significant constraints on it.

# Imprints of the nuclear symmetry energy slope in gravitational wave signals emanating from neutron stars

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy slope ($L$) leaves detectable imprints on neutron star properties including radius, compactness, tidal deformability, and the frequency and damping time of the quadrupole fundamental fluid mode, but the sensitivity depends on stellar mass and the specific coupling mechanism ($\omega-\rho$ nonlinear coupling versus scalar-isovector $\delta$ meson) used to vary $L$ while keeping the symmetry energy fixed at $S_0 = 31.7$ MeV.

## Method

The authors employed quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) with $\sigma-\omega-\rho$ mesons, using two extensions to vary the slope $L$: the nonlinear $\omega-\rho$ coupling (as in the IUFSU model) to reduce $L$, and the scalar-isovector $\delta$ meson to increase $L$, while fixing the symmetry energy at saturation to $S_0 = 31.7$ MeV. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for stellar structure, computed the tidal deformability parameter $\Lambda$ via the second Love number $k_2$, and solved the perturbed Einstein equations for the quadrupolar ($\ell=2$) fundamental fluid mode frequency and damping time, using the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland and Baym-Bethe-Pethick equations of state for the crust.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation fixed at $S_0 = 31.7$ MeV; slope $L$ varied from 44 MeV to 116 MeV across seven parametrizations.
- Maximum neutron star mass ranges from $2.30 M_\odot$ (for $L = 60$ MeV) to $2.39 M_\odot$ (for $L = 116$ MeV).
- Radius of the canonical $1.4 M_\odot$ star ranges from 12.58 km ($L = 44$ MeV) to 14.30 km ($L = 116$ MeV).
- Tidal deformability $\Lambda_{(1.4)}$ ranges from 513 ($L = 60$ MeV) to 812 ($L = 116$ MeV); for $L = 44$ MeV, $\Lambda_{(1.4)} = 515$.
- For the $2.01 M_\odot$ star, radius ranges from 12.40 km ($L = 44$ MeV) to 13.82 km ($L = 116$ MeV), and $\Lambda_{(2.01)}$ ranges from 40 ($L = 60$ and 76 MeV) to 70 ($L = 116$ MeV).
- Symmetry energy at twice saturation density ranges from 45 MeV ($L = 44$ MeV) to 85 MeV ($L = 116$ MeV); slope at twice saturation density ranges from $L(2n_0) = 83$ MeV ($L = 44$ MeV) to $L(2n_0) = 412$ MeV ($L = 116$ MeV).
- For $L = 116$ MeV, the canonical star radius exceeds 14.26 km, which is the upper limit from Miller (2019).

## Context

This work extends studies of the symmetry energy slope constraint problem highlighted by the tension between PREX2 (suggesting $76 < L < 165$ MeV) and CREX (suggesting $0 < L < 51$ MeV) results, as discussed by Tagami (2022). It builds on prior QHD-based investigations of the $\omega-\rho$ coupling by Rafa (2011) and Bao (2020), and on studies of the $\delta$ meson by Kubis (1997) and Liu (2002). The results enable future gravitational wave observations of neutron star mergers and post-merger oscillations to constrain $L$ independently of terrestrial experiments, particularly through combined measurements of the $f$-mode frequency and tidal deformability.
