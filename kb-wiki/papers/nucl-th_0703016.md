# Spin distribution of nuclear levels using static path approximation with random-phase approximation

> We present a thermal and quantum-mechanical treatment of nuclear rotation using the formalism of static path approximation (SPA) plus random-phase approximation (RPA). Naive perturbation theory fails because of the presence of zero-frequency modes due to dynamical symmetry breaking. Such modes lead to infrared divergences. We show that composite zero-frequency excitations are properly treated within the collective coordinate method. The resulting perturbation theory is free from infrared divergences. Without the assumption of individual random spin vectors, we derive microscopically the spin distribution of the level density. The moment of inertia is thereby related to the spin-cutoff parameter in the usual way. Explicit calculations are performed for 56^Fe; various thermal properties are discussed. In particular, we demonstrate that the increase of the moment of inertia with increasing temperature is correlated with the suppression of pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a microscopic spin distribution for nuclear level densities without assuming individual random spin vectors, by properly treating zero-frequency rotational modes in the static path approximation plus random-phase approximation (SPA+RPA) framework, thereby avoiding infrared divergences that plague naive perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors apply the collective coordinate method of Gervais and Sakita to separate rotational zero-frequency modes from intrinsic vibrations in the SPA+RPA formalism for a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole (P+QQ) Hamiltonian. They perform number-parity projected SPA+RPA calculations using an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential with 15 doubly-degenerate single-particle levels for neutrons and protons outside a ⁴⁰Ca core, with pairing strengths G_n=25/A MeV and G_p=29/A MeV and QQ-force strength χ=240/A^(5/3) b⁴. The level density is obtained via saddle-point approximation of the partition function.

## Key numbers

- Pairing-force strengths: G_n = 25/A MeV for neutrons, G_p = 29/A MeV for protons
- QQ-force strength: χ = 240/A^(5/3) b⁴
- Level-density parameter a = A/10 = 5.6 MeV⁻¹ and back-shift parameter E₁ = 1.0 MeV used in back-shifted Fermi-gas model comparison
- Critical temperature T_c ~ 0.9 MeV for the S-shaped heat capacity in ⁵⁶Fe, compared to SMMC result ~0.7 MeV and experimental ~1.3 MeV
- S-shaped heat capacities with T_c ~ 0.5 MeV observed in ¹⁶²Dy, ¹⁶⁶Er, and ¹⁷²Yb

## Context

This work extends the SPA+RPA method for nuclear level densities by properly treating rotational zero-frequency modes, building on prior work by Alhassid et al. using shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) and SPA methods that found suppression of the moment of inertia and odd-even staggering of the spin-cutoff parameter due to pairing correlations. The approach enables microscopic calculation of spin-dependent level densities for Hauser-Feshbach reaction rate calculations in astrophysics, and the results for ⁵⁶Fe reproduce experimental level densities and show correlation between increasing moment of inertia and suppression of pairing correlations with temperature.
