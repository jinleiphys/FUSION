# Bjorken Flow, Plasma Instabilities, and Thermalization

> At asymptotically high energies, thermalization in heavy ion collisions can be described via weak-coupling QCD. We present a complete treatment of how thermalization proceeds, at the parametric weak-coupling level. We show that plasma instabilities dominate the dynamics, from immediately after the collision until well after the plasma becomes nearly in equilibrium. Initially they drive the system close to isotropy, but Bjorken expansion and increasing diluteness makes the system again become more anisotropic. At time τ~ α^(-12/5) Q^(-1) the dynamics become dominated by a nearly-thermal bath; and at time τ~ α^(-5/2) Q^(-1)$ the bath comes to dominate the energy density, completing thermalization. After this time there is a nearly isotropic and thermal Quark-Gluon Plasma.

## Key claim

Plasma instabilities dominate the dynamics of thermalization in heavy ion collisions at asymptotically high energies from immediately after the collision until well after the plasma becomes nearly in equilibrium, but the system follows one of two attractor solutions; the authors argue that the more anisotropic attractor is realized, leading to thermalization at time τ ∼ α^(-5/2) Q_s^(-1), which is faster than the BMSS estimate of τ ∼ α^(-13/5) Q_s^(-1) but slower than the absolute lower bound of α^(-7/3) Q_s^(-1) derived by Arnold and Lenaghan.

## Method

The authors combine their previously developed parametric framework (KM1) for weakly coupled gauge theories out of equilibrium with Bjorken expansion, using logarithmic variables a (time), b (typical momentum), c (mean occupancy), and d (anisotropy) to track the system's evolution. They identify two attractor solutions in the occupancy-anisotropy plane by analyzing how Bjorken expansion and plasma instability dynamics (broadening and merging timescales) compete, then follow the detailed evolution along the preferred anisotropic attractor through three stages until thermalization.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density at t ∼ Q_s^(-1): ε ∼ α^(-1) Q_s^4
- First attractor solution: d = a/8, c = 1 - 7a/8, b = 0, where t = α^(-a) Q_s^(-1)
- Second attractor solution: d = -8a/135, c = 1 - 56a/135, b = 31a/135
- Time when soft bath forms and takes over dynamics: τ ∼ α^(-12/5) Q_s^(-1) (a = 12/5)
- Time when thermalization completes: τ ∼ α^(-5/2) Q_s^(-1) (a = 5/2)
- Final temperature: T_final ∼ α^(-11/24) Q_s^(2/3) t^(-1/3)
- Time when fluid becomes Newtonian: t ∼ α^(-45/16) Q_s^(-1)
- Time when attractor 1 crosses c-axis (loss of Bose stimulation): a = 8/7
- Time when new plasma instabilities dominate q̂ at large angles: a = 96/55
- Time when soft bath becomes nearly thermal: a = 56/25

## Context

This work extends the earlier bottom-up thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff and Son (BMSS) (2001) by incorporating plasma instabilities that were pointed out as missing by Arnold, Lenaghan and Moore (2003). It builds on analytic studies by Bödeker (2005) and Arnold and Moore (2007) of plasma instabilities in early-stage dynamics, and on the authors' own KM1 framework (2011) for nonequilibrium gauge theories. The results contradict the scenario of Blaizot, Gelis, Liao, McLerran, and Venugopalan (2011) by finding that no Bose-Einstein condensate forms and that plasma instabilities play a key role throughout. The paper provides a complete parametric description of thermalization from glasma initial conditions through to a nearly isotropic and thermal quark-gluon plasma, enabling future work on the precise numerical coefficients and non-parametric corrections.
