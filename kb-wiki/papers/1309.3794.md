# Nucleon and nucleon-pair momentum distributions in A <= 12 nuclei

> We report variational Monte Carlo calculations of single-nucleon momentum distributions for A <= 12 nuclei and nucleon-pair and nucleon-cluster momentum distributions for A <= 8. The wave functions have been generated for a Hamiltonian containing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana X three-nucleon potentials. The single-nucleon and nucleon-pair momentum distributions exhibit universal features attributable to the one-pion-exchange tensor interaction. The single-nucleon distributions are broken down into proton and neutron components and spin-up and spin-down components where appropriate. The nucleon-pair momentum distributions are given separately for pp and pn pairs. The nucleon-cluster momentum distributions include dp in 3He, tp and dd in 4He, alpha-d in 6Li, alpha-t in 7Li, and alpha-alpha in 8Be. Detailed tables are provided on-line for download.

## Key claim

The single-nucleon and nucleon-pair momentum distributions in light nuclei (A ≤ 12) exhibit universal features attributable to the one-pion-exchange tensor interaction, with the broad shoulder in single-nucleon distributions above 2 fm⁻¹ present in all nuclei studied. The three-nucleon potential (Urbana X) boosts the tensor-induced effects by approximately 15% compared to using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential alone.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations were performed using wave functions generated for a Hamiltonian containing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana X three-nucleon potentials. The Fourier transform of the density was computed by Monte Carlo integration using a Metropolis walk guided by the squared wave function, with symmetric displacement of particle positions and Gauss-Legendre integration to reduce statistical errors. Nucleon-pair momentum distributions were evaluated as functions of relative pair momentum q, center-of-mass momentum Q, or both, projected into spin-isospin ST combinations or into pp, pn, and nn pairs.

## Key numbers

- Proton momentum distribution in ⁴He: VMC energy of −27.6 MeV with AV18+UX Hamiltonian (GFMC gives −28.3 MeV, experimental −28.3 MeV); point proton radius 1.44 fm (experimental 1.46 fm)
- ⁴He D-state increases from 13% with AV18 alone to 15% with AV18+UX; ρp(k) beyond 2 fm⁻¹ increases by 10–20% when three-nucleon potential is added
- ⁴He tp cluster distribution integrates to Ntp = 1.62 (compared to Np = 2); dd cluster distribution integrates to Ndd = 1.005 (0.98 from S-wave, 0.025 from D-wave)
- ⁶He: for q ≥ 2 fm⁻¹, ratio ρn/ρp = 1.1; ⁸He: for q ≥ 2 fm⁻¹, ratio ρn/ρp = 1.2
- ⁶Li αd cluster distribution: Nαd = 0.86 in ground state (S-wave 0.846, D-wave 0.017); in 3⁺ excited state Nαd = 0.94
- ⁷Li αt cluster distribution: Nαt = 1.00 in ground state
- ⁸Be αα cluster distribution: Nαα = 0.84 in 0⁺ ground state, 0.83 in 2⁺ excited state, 0.75 in 4⁺ rotational state at 11 MeV excitation
- ⁴He pair transmutation: AV18+UX converts 0.46 (01) pairs to (11) pairs; AV18 alone converts 0.40; AV18(4) without tensor converts only 0.01
- ⁸He: 15 nn pairs, 12 np pairs, 1 pp pair; at q ≥ 2 fm⁻¹, 70% more np than nn pairs
- ³He consistency check: ρpp(Q) = ρn(k) for k = Q satisfied to better than 1%

## Context

This work extends earlier VMC calculations of momentum distributions in A=3,4 nuclei by Schiavilla et al. (1986) and subsequent studies of nucleon-pair distributions by Wiringa et al. (2007, 2008). It is complementary to ab initio calculations by Alvioli et al. (2013) for A=2,3,4 s-shell nuclei and approximate calculations for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca, with substantial quantitative agreement where calculations overlap. The results fill in light p-shell nuclei up to ¹²C and provide detailed tabulations online for use in interpreting (e,e′p) and (e,e′pp/pn) electrodisintegration experiments and neutrino-nucleus interaction experiments. The finding that in neutron-rich systems the fraction of protons at high momenta is substantially larger than the fraction of neutrons supports a recent suggestion by Sargsian (2013) with implications for the EMC effect in asymmetric nuclei.
