# Quantification of spin alignment in fission by simultaneous treatment of gamma and conversion electron angular distributions

> The study of the angular momentum properties of fission fragments can shed light about the complex mechanisms that characterize the fission process. One quantity that is of significant interest, and has not yet been studied adequately, is the alignment of the fragments, which is the cause of anisotropy of the γ rays along the fission axis and has been observed in various past and recent experiments. In this work, we have performed calculations using the FIFRELIN code, in an attempt to quantify the alignment of the nuclear spins after neutron-emission. Under the statistical tensor formalism of angular distributions, the conversion-electron and the γ-ray angular distributions can be treated simultaneously in an event-by-event calculation. This enables a first prediction of the conversion-electron angular distribution with respect to the fission axis. An average value for the alignment of fission fragments is deduced for 252Cf, with the use of recent experimental data. The method used for the present work can serve as a starting point for future theoretical and experimental studies in terms of γ and conversion-electron spectroscopy in view of studying the spin alignment of individual fission fragments, which could further improve our understanding on the process of fission.

## Method

Calculations were performed using the FIFRELIN code in an event-by-event approach, applying the statistical tensor formalism of angular distributions to treat both gamma and conversion-electron emissions simultaneously after neutron emission from fission fragments. The angular distributions were computed with respect to the fission axis, and the results were compared to recent experimental data for 252Cf to extract an average alignment value.
