# Quasinormal modes of massive charged flavor branes

> We present an analysis and classification of vector and scalar fluctuations in a D3/D7 brane setup at finite termperature and baryon density. The system is dual to an N=2 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with SU(N_c) gauge group and N_f hypermultiplets in the fundamental representation in the quenched approximation. We improve significantly over previous results on the quasinormal mode spectrum of D7 branes and stress their novel physical interpretation. Amongst our findings is a new purely imaginary scalar mode that becomes tachyonic at sufficiently low temperature and baryon density. We establish the existence of a critical density above which the scalar mode stays in the stable regime for all temperatures. In the vector sector we study the crossover from the hydrodynamic to the quasiparticle regime and find that it moves to shorter wavelengths for lower temperatures. At zero baryon density the quasinormal modes move toward distinct discrete attractor frequencies that depend on the momentum as we increase the temperature. At finite baryon density, however, the trajectories show a turning behavior such that for low temperature the quasinormal mode spectrum approaches the spectrum of the supersymmetric zero temperature normal modes. We interpret this as resolution of the singular quasinormal mode spectrum that appears at the limiting D7 brane embedding at vanishing baryon density.

## Key claim

The D3/D7 brane system at finite temperature and baryon density is completely stable above a critical density, meaning the scalar excitation spectrum contains no tachyonic mode; below this critical density, a purely imaginary scalar mode becomes tachyonic at sufficiently low temperature and baryon density, establishing a connection between a black hole phase transition and the tachyonic scalar.

## Method

The authors study vector and scalar fluctuations in a D3/D7 brane setup dual to N=2 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with SU(N_c) gauge group and N_f hypermultiplets in the quenched approximation. They compute quasinormal modes by solving linearized fluctuation equations of motion derived from the DBI action, using both a relaxation method and an improved shooting method that shifts the numerical integration starting point away from the horizon to avoid round-off errors. The fluctuation equations are rewritten in Schrodinger form to analyze effective potentials, and the quasinormal frequencies are extracted as poles of the retarded correlator in the complex frequency plane.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for scalar stability: \(\tilde d_c = 0.00315\); above this value the system contains no tachyonic scalar mode
- Critical density for transverse vector turning behavior: \(\tilde d_c \approx 0.04\); above this value the turning point in the quasinormal mode trajectory disappears
- For vanishing density and massless quarks, the analytical quasinormal mode spectrum is \(\omega = n(1-i)\) for \(n \in \mathbb{N}_0\)
- The relaxation method convergence criterion was set to \(\mathtt{conv} = 10^{-6}\) with a typical grid of 5000 points
- The baryon number density parameter \(\tilde d\) relates to physical density by \(\tilde d = 2^{5/2} n_B / (N_f \sqrt{\lambda} T^3)\)
- The supersymmetric meson mass formula for vanishing angular momentum: \(M_{meson} = (2 M_q / (\sqrt{\lambda} T R^2)) \sqrt{2(n+1)(n+2)}\) for \(n \in \mathbb{N}\)

## Context

This work significantly improves over previous results on the D7 brane quasinormal mode spectrum from Erdmenger (2007) and Myers (2007), providing a novel physical interpretation including the discovery of a tachyonic scalar mode. The analysis extends the study of mesonic excitations in holographic QCD-like models and connects to thermodynamic instabilities. The Schrodinger potential analysis reveals that at finite charge density a barrier develops near the black hole horizon, hiding the horizon from the boundary and causing quasinormal modes to asymptote to normal mode behavior, similar to observations by Horowitz (2009) in a setup where a black hole in AdS_4 develops scalar hair. The results enable further study of phase transitions in holographic systems with finite isospin density or spontaneously broken symmetry, as in the work of Ammon (2009).
