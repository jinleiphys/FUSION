# Generalized Universality for TMD Distribution Functions

> Azimuthal asymmetries in high-energy processes, most pronounced showing up in combination with single or double (transverse) spin asymmetries, can be understood with the help of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution and fragmentation functions. These appear in correlators containing expectation values of quark and gluon operators. TMDs allow access to new operators as compared to collinear (transverse momentum integrated) correlators. These operators include nontrivial process dependent Wilson lines breaking universality for TMDs. Making an angular decomposition in the azimuthal angle, we define a set of universal TMDs of definite rank, which appear with process dependent gluonic pole factors in a way similar to the sign of T-odd parton distribution functions in deep inelastic scattering or the Drell-Yan process. In particular, we show that for a spin 1/2 quark target there are three pretzelocity functions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions can be made universal by decomposing the quark correlator into tensors of definite rank, where process dependence is isolated into calculable gluonic pole factors; for a spin-1/2 quark target, this decomposition reveals three distinct universal pretzelocity functions rather than one.

## Method

The authors perform an angular decomposition of the quark-quark correlator into irreducible tensors of rank m (constructed from symmetric traceless combinations of transverse momentum), separating contributions from gluonic pole matrix elements, partial derivatives, and mixed terms. They then identify universal TMD PDFs by matching the transverse moments of these correlators to the parametrization of the correlator in terms of standard TMD functions, with the gauge link dependence absorbed into process-dependent gluonic pole factors \(C_G^{[U]}\) and \(C_{GG,c}^{[U]}\).

## Key numbers

* For the Sivers function, the first transverse moment obeys \(f_{1T}^{\perp(1)[U]}(x) = C_G^{[U]} f_{1T}^{\perp(1)}(x)\), where \(C_G^{[\pm]} = \pm 1\) for the simplest gauge links (future- or past-pointing).
* For the pretzelocity function on a spin-1/2 target, the second transverse moment decomposes as \(h_{1T}^{\perp(2)[\pm]}(x) = h_{1T}^{\perp(2)(A)}(x) + h_{1T}^{\perp(2)(B1)}(x)\) for the simplest gauge links, with \(C_{GG,1}^{[\pm]} = 1\) and \(C_{GG,2}^{[\pm]} = 0\).
* For more complex gauge links, the pretzelocity function involves three universal functions: \(h_{1T}^{\perp [U]}(x,p_\st^2) = h_{1T}^{\perp (A)}(x,p_\st^2) + C_{GG,1}^{[U]}\,h_{1T}^{\perp (B1)}(x,p_\st^2) + C_{GG,2}^{[U]}\,h_{1T}^{\perp (B2)}(x,p_\st^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the framework of process-dependent TMDs established by Collins (2002) for the Sivers function sign change between SIDIS and Drell-Yan, and by Bomhof, Mulders, and Pijlman (2006) for more complex gauge link structures. It generalizes the universality concept to all TMDs by introducing correlators of definite rank, enabling a systematic classification of process dependence for higher-twist effects such as the pretzelocity distribution; the authors note that for fragmentation functions, gluonic pole matrix elements vanish (Meissner, Metz, and Goeke, 2008), so TMD PFFs are already universal.
