# Monte Carlo Glauber wounded nucleon model with meson cloud

> We study the effect of the nucleon meson cloud on predictions of the Monte Carlo Glauber wounded nucleon model for $AA$, $pA$, and $pp$ collisions. From the analysis of the data on the charged multiplicity density in $AA$ collisions we find that the meson-baryon Fock component reduces the required fraction of binary collisions by a factor of $\sim 2$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV and $\sim 1.5$ for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV. For central $AA$ collisions the meson cloud can increase the multiplicity density by $\sim 16-18$\%. We give predictions for the midrapidity charged multiplicity density in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV for the future LHC run 2. We find that the meson cloud has a weak effect on the centrality dependence of the ellipticity $ε_2$ in $AA$ collisions. For collisions of the deformed uranium nuclei at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV we find that the meson cloud may improve somewhat agreement with the data on the dependence of the elliptic flow on the charged multiplicity for very small centralities defined via the ZDCs signals. We find that the meson cloud may lead to a noticeable reduction of $ε_2$ and the size of the fireball in $pA$ and $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim
The meson-baryon Fock component of the nucleon reduces the required fraction of binary collisions ($\alpha$) needed to fit charged multiplicity data in heavy-ion collisions, by a factor of $\sim 2$ for Au+Au at 0.2 TeV and $\sim 1.5$ for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, compared to the standard two-component wounded nucleon model without this cloud.

## Method
The author developed a Monte Carlo Glauber (MCG) model where the physical nucleon is treated as a superposition of a bare nucleon and meson-baryon Fock states (primarily $\pi N$, $\pi\Delta$, $\rho N$, $\rho\Delta$), with a total meson cloud weight of $n_{MB}=0.4$. Inelastic interactions are modeled as independent collisions of these bare constituents, using constituent quark counting rules for cross sections and a Gaussian impact parameter profile. The model was fit to $pp$ multiplicity data to determine source parameters, and then the binary collision fraction $\alpha$ was fit to reproduce the centrality dependence of charged multiplicity density in Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV and Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV.

## Key numbers
- The meson cloud reduces the required fraction of binary collisions ($\alpha$) from $\sim 0.135$ to $\sim 0.06$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV.
- The meson cloud reduces the required $\alpha$ from $\sim 0.14$ to $\sim 0.09$ for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV.
- For central $AA$ collisions, the meson cloud increases the charged multiplicity density by $\sim 16-18\%$.
- The total weight of the meson-baryon Fock components in the physical nucleon is taken as $n_{MB}=0.4$.
- The root mean square transverse radius of the $\pi N$ component is $\langle \rho^2_{\pi N}\rangle^{1/2}\approx0.87$ fm.

## Context
This work extends the standard wounded nucleon Glauber model (WNG) and its two-component version by Kharzeev and Nardi (2001) to include sub-nucleon degrees of freedom, specifically the meson cloud. It contrasts with earlier sub-nucleon models that only considered constituent quarks or quark-diquark systems, such as those by Bozek (2016) and the PHENIX collaboration (2013). The reduction in the required binary collision fraction helps resolve a tension with the absence of a predicted "knee" structure in the elliptic flow of U+U collisions at RHIC, as discussed by Lednicky and Voloshin (2009). The model provides predictions for the charged multiplicity density in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV for future LHC runs.
