# Density matrix renormalization group description of the island of inversion isotopes $^{28-33}$F

> Recent experiments have confirmed that the neutron-rich isotopes $^{28,29}$F belong to the so-called island of inversion (IOI), a region of the nuclear chart around $Z=10$ and $N=20$ where nuclear structure deviates from the standard shell model predictions due to deformation and continuum effects. However, while the general principles leading to the IOI are relatively well understood, the details of the low-lying structure of the exotic fluorine isotopes $^{28-33}$F are basically unknown. In this work, we perform large-scale shell model calculations including continuum states to investigate the properties of the neutron-rich isotopes $^{25-33}$F, using a core of $^{24}$O and an effective two-body interaction with only three adjustable parameters. We adjust the core potential and interaction on experimentally confirmed states in $^{25,26}$O and $^{25-27}$F and solve the many-body problem using the density matrix renormalization group method for open quantum systems in a $sd$-$fp$ model space. We obtain the first detailed spectroscopy of $^{25-33}$F in the continuum and show how the interplay between continuum effects and deformation explains the recent data on $^{28,29}$F, and produces an inversion of the ${5/2}^+$ and ${1/2}^+$ states in $^{29,31,33}$F. Several deformed one- and two-neutron halo states are predicted in $^{29,31}$F, and we predict the ground state of $^{30}$F to have a structure similar to that of the first ${5/2}^+$ state of $^{29}$F. We also suggest several experimental studies to constraint models and test the present predictions. The complex structure of neutron-rich fluorine isotopes offers a trove of information about the formation of the southern shore of the IOI through a subtle interplay of deformation and continuum couplings driven by the occupation of the quasi-degenerate neutron shells $0d_{3/2}$ and $1p_{3/2}$.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first detailed spectroscopy of neutron-rich fluorine isotopes \(^{25-33}\)F including continuum effects, showing that the interplay between deformation and continuum couplings explains the recent experimental data on \(^{28,29}\)F, but the model requires fine tuning in isotopes with \(A>29\) and the ground state of \(^{28}\)F gained about 700 keV between the fit truncation and the almost exact result, making it an outlier.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations using a core of \(^{24}\)O with an effective two-body interaction reduced to only three adjustable parameters in the central \((S,T) = (1,0)\), \((0,1)\), and \((1,1)\) channels. They solved the many-body problem using the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) method for open quantum systems in an \(sd\)-\(fp\) model space, representing neutron states in the Berggren basis to include resonant and scattering states, and proton states in a harmonic oscillator basis. The core potential and interaction were adjusted on experimentally confirmed states in \(^{25,26}\)O and \(^{25-27}\)F.

## Key numbers

* The \(\nu 0d_{3/2}\) shell in \(^{25}\)O was placed at \(E = 0.767\) MeV with a width of \(\Gamma = 77\) keV, and the \(\nu 1p_{3/2}\) shell was lowered to \(E = 0.298\) MeV with a width of \(\Gamma = 343\) keV after readjustment.
* The three adjusted interaction parameters were \(V_c^{01} = -1.360\) MeV, \(V_c^{10} = -1.244\) MeV, and \(V_c^{11} = -68.00\) MeV.
* The theoretical ground state energy of \(^{25}\)F was \(-14.29\) MeV relative to \(^{24}\)O (experimental: \(-14.43\) MeV), and for \(^{27}\)F it was \(-17.46\) MeV (experimental: \(-17.32\) MeV).
* The ground state of \(^{28}\)F was calculated at \(-17.64\) MeV relative to \(^{24}\)O with \(J^\pi = 2^-\) (experimental: \(-17.10\) MeV with \(J^\pi = 4^-\) originally assigned).
* The energy difference \(S_n(^{27}\text{F}) - S_n(^{26}\text{F})\) was about 0.09 MeV in the model, compared to 1.33 MeV experimentally.
* The single-particle basis consisted of 22 proton shells and 67 neutron shells, with the Hamiltonian dimension in \(^{33}\)F estimated at approximately \(10^{11}\) Slater determinants.

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of the island of inversion around \(Z=10\) and \(N=20\), notably the Gamow shell model calculations in \(^{31}\)F by Michel et al. (2020) which used significant truncations and seven adjustable parameters, whereas the present work uses only three parameters. The calculations predict deformed one- and two-neutron halo states in \(^{29,31}\)F and an inversion of the \(5/2^+\) and \(1/2^+\) states in \(^{29,31,33}\)F, enabling future experimental studies to constrain models of the southern shore of the island of inversion through the interplay of deformation and continuum couplings driven by occupation of the quasi-degenerate neutron shells \(0d_{3/2}\) and \(1p_{3/2}\).
