# The Doubly-heavy Tetraquarks ($qq'\bar{Q}\bar{Q'}$) in a Constituent Quark Model with a Complete Set of Harmonic Oscillator Bases

> We have improved our previous variational method based constituent quark model by introducing a complete set of 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator bases as the spatial part of the total wave function. To assess the validity of our approach, we compared the binding energy, thus calculated with the exact value for the hydrogen model. After fitting to the masses of the ground state hadrons, we apply our new method to analyze the doubly-heavy tetraquark states $qq'\bar{Q}\bar{Q'}$ and compared the result for the binding energies with that from other works. We also calculated the ground state masses of $T_{sc} (ud\bar{s}\bar{c})$ and $T_{sb} (ud\bar{s}\bar{b})$ with $(I,S) = (0,1), (0,2)$. We found that $T_{bb} (ud\bar{b}\bar{b})$ and $us\bar{b}\bar{b}$, both with $(I,S) = (0,1)$, are stable against the two lowest threshold meson states with binding energies $-145$ MeV and $-42$ MeV, respectively. We further found that $T_{cb} (ud\bar{c}\bar{b})$ is near the lowest threshold. The spatial sizes for the tetraquarks are also discussed.

## Key claim

Using a complete set of 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator bases in a constituent quark model, the authors find that the doubly-bottom tetraquarks \(T_{bb} (ud\bar{b}\bar{b})\) and \(us\bar{b}\bar{b}\) are stable against strong decay, while the \(T_{cb} (ud\bar{c}\bar{b})\) state is near the lowest threshold; the abstract's claim of stability for \(T_{bb}\) and \(us\bar{b}\bar{b}\) is conditional on the specific choice of the \((I,S) = (0,1)\) channel and on the use of the improved basis set.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian containing a confining potential, a Coulomb term, and a hyperfine spin-spin interaction. They expanded the spatial part of the tetraquark wave function in a complete set of 3-dimensional harmonic oscillator bases organized by quanta \(Q = 2n_1 + 2n_2 + 2n_3 + l_1 + l_2 + l_3\), including bases up to the 5th quanta (\(Q=8\)) and internal angular momenta up to \(l_i=3\). A variational method was then applied to determine the ground state masses for the tetraquark systems, after fitting the model parameters to the experimental masses of 16 mesons and 18 baryons.

## Key numbers

*   \(T_{bb} (ud\bar{b}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S) = (0,1)\): mass = 10517 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = -145\) MeV relative to the two lowest threshold meson states.
*   \(us\bar{b}\bar{b}\) with \((I,S) = (0,1)\): mass = 10694 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = -42\) MeV.
*   \(T_{cb} (ud\bar{c}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S) = (0,1)\): mass = 7212 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = -3\) MeV (near the lowest threshold).
*   \(T_{cc} (ud\bar{c}\bar{c})\) with \((I,S) = (0,1)\): mass = 3873 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = +13\) MeV (unbound).
*   \(T_{sc} (ud\bar{s}\bar{c})\) with \((I,S) = (0,1)\): mass = 2596 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = +91\) MeV.
*   \(T_{sb} (ud\bar{s}\bar{b})\) with \((I,S) = (0,1)\): mass = 5949 MeV, binding energy \(B_T = +90\) MeV.
*   The model parameters were fit to hadron masses with errors mostly within 0.7%, including the doubly-charmed baryon \(\Xi_{cc}\) at 3621.1 MeV (experimental 3621.4 MeV, error 0.01%).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous variational constituent quark model (Woosung and Lee, 2019) by introducing a complete set of harmonic oscillator bases, which allows for a more accurate description of the spatial wave function including excited orbital states. The results for \(T_{bb}\) stability confirm earlier findings by Brink and Stancu (1998) and Janc and Rosina (2004), while the improved basis yields a larger binding energy (\(-145\) MeV vs. \(-121\) MeV in the previous work). The method enables more reliable predictions for doubly-heavy tetraquark candidates, particularly for the near-threshold \(T_{cb}\) state, and can be applied to other exotic hadron configurations.
