# Lattice QCD at High Temperature and Density

> After a brief introduction into basic aspects of the formulation of lattice regularized QCD at finite temperature and density we discuss our current understanding of the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature. We present results from lattice calculations that emphasize the deconfining as well as chiral symmetry restoring features of the QCD transition, and discuss the thermodynamics of the high temperature phase.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations show that deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occur at the same temperature in QCD with dynamical quarks, contrary to model expectations that two distinct transitions could exist; however, for the physically realized quark mass spectrum the transition may be only a rapid crossover rather than a true phase transition.

## Method

The author reviews lattice-regularized QCD thermodynamics using the Euclidean path integral on a four-dimensional space-time lattice of size \(N_\sigma^3 \times N_\tau\), with staggered fermions for the quark sector and the Wilson gauge action. Thermodynamic observables such as the Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, and their corresponding susceptibilities are computed as functions of the gauge coupling \(\beta = 6/g^2\) and quark mass, with the temperature scale set by the string tension or hadron masses.

## Key numbers

- The string tension in the vacuum is \(\sigma \simeq (425\ \text{MeV})^2\).
- The chiral condensate in the vacuum is \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle \simeq (250\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- The heavy quark potential rises linearly at large distances with slope \(\sigma\) up to \(r \simeq 1\ \text{fm}\), beyond which string breaking occurs.
- The critical temperature in the pure \(SU(3)\) gauge theory (infinite quark mass limit) has been calculated with an accuracy of a few percent.

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD thermodynamics program initiated by McLerran, Kuti, and the Bielefeld group in the 1980s, and builds on universality arguments for the phase diagram from Yaffe (heavy quark limit) and Pisarski & Wilczek (1984) and Gavai (1994) (light quark limit). The results enable quantitative determination of the QCD equation of state and critical temperature with realistic light quark masses, and set the stage for future studies of QCD at non-zero baryon density, where the sign problem currently prevents direct numerical simulations.
