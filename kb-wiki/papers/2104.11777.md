# Viscous control of minimum uncertainty state in hydrodynamics

> A minimum uncertainty state for position and momentum of a fluid element is obtained. We consider a general fluid described by the Navier-Stokes-Korteweg (NSK) equation, which reproduces the behaviors of a standard viscous fluid, a fluid with the capillary action and a quantum fluid, with the proper choice of parameters. When the parameters of the NSK equation is adjusted to reproduce Madelung's hydrodynamic representation of the Schreodinger equation, the uncertainty relation of a fluid element reproduces the Kennard and the Robertson-Schreodinger inequalities in quantum mechanics. The derived minimum uncertainty state is the generalization of the coherent state and its uncertainty is given by a function of the shear viscosity. The viscous uncertainty can be smaller than the inviscid minimum value when the shear viscosity is smaller than a critical value which is similar in magnitude to the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound. This uncertainty reflects the information of the fluctuating microscopic degrees of freedom in the fluid and will modify the standard hydrodynamic scenario, for example, in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The minimum uncertainty product for a fluid element's position and momentum can be smaller than the inviscid (quantum-mechanical) minimum when the shear viscosity is below a critical value comparable to the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound, but only for specific ratios of the Korteweg parameter \(\kappa\) to the noise intensity \(\nu\).

## Method

The Navier-Stokes-Korteweg (NSK) equation is formulated via the stochastic variational method (SVM), where viscous and capillary terms arise from stochastic trajectories of constituent particles described by forward and backward stochastic differential equations. The uncertainty relation for position and momentum of a fluid element is derived using the two momenta \(\mathbf{p}_\pm\) that emerge from the non-differentiability of the stochastic trajectory, and the minimum uncertainty state is obtained by assuming a Gaussian density profile and a velocity field linear in position, then imposing the condition that saturates the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.

## Key numbers

* For liquid water at room temperature: \(\uM = 3 \times 10^{-26}\) kg, kinematic viscosity \(\xi \sim 10^{-6}\) m\(^2\)/s, diffusion coefficient \(\nu \sim 10^{-9}\) m\(^2\)/s, giving a minimum uncertainty product \(\sim 600 \times (\hbar/2)\).
* For water vapor at room temperature: \(\xi \sim 0.3 \times 10^{-6}\) m\(^2\)/s, \(\nu \sim 10^{-4}\) m\(^2\)/s, giving a minimum uncertainty product \(\sim 60 \times (\hbar/2)\).
* The inviscid minimum uncertainty is \(\uM \kappa / \nu\); the viscous minimum uncertainty is \(\uM \nu \, |(\kappa/\nu^2) - (\xi/\nu)^2| / \sqrt{1 + (\xi/\nu)^2}\).
* The condition for viscous uncertainty to be smaller than the inviscid minimum is \(\xi^*_{min} < \xi < \xi^*_{max}\), where \(\xi^*_{min}\) and \(\xi^*_{max}\) are functions of \(\kappa/\nu^2\) given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic uncertainty relations previously derived by Koide (2018, 2020) for the Navier-Stokes-Fourier equation to the more general Navier-Stokes-Korteweg equation, which includes capillary effects and reproduces Madelung's hydrodynamic representation of the Schrodinger equation. The minimum uncertainty state generalizes the quantum coherent state to viscous fluids, and the result suggests that the uncertainty could modify standard hydrodynamic scenarios in heavy-ion collisions, where short time evolutions in small inhomogeneous systems are relevant.
