# Systematic study on proton radioactivity of spherical proton emitters within two--potential approach

> In the present work we systematically study the half--lives of proton radioactivity for spherical proton emitters with ${Z\ge 69}$ based on two--potential approach. While the nuclear potential of the emitted proton--daughter nucleus is adopted by a parameterized cosh type, the parameters of the depth and diffuseness for nuclear potential are determined by fitting experimental data of 32 spherical proton emitters. In order to reduce the deviations between experimental half-lives and calculated ones, we propose a simple analytic expression for formation probability of proton radioactivity with the same orbital angular momentum $l$. The results indicate that the formation probability can be simply described by a formula of $A_d^{1/3}$. Moreover, the linear relationship between the formation probability and the fragmentation potential also exists. The calculated half-lives can well reproduce the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the formation probability of proton radioactivity for spherical emitters with Z ≥ 69 can be simply described by a linear function of the cube root of the daughter mass number (A_d^{1/3}) when the orbital angular momentum l is held fixed, and that including this formation probability significantly improves the agreement between calculated and experimental half-lives.

## Method

The authors used the two-potential approach (TPA) with a parameterized cosh-type nuclear potential to calculate proton radioactivity half-lives for 32 spherical proton emitters. They determined the depth (V_0) and diffuseness (a) parameters of the nuclear potential by fitting experimental half-lives to minimize the standard deviation. They then extracted experimental formation probabilities S_p from the ratio of calculated to experimental half-lives and proposed a simple linear formula log10(S_p) = a_l * A_d^{1/3} + b_l, with separate parameters for l = 0, 2, and 5.

## Key numbers

- Depth of cosh-type nuclear potential: V_0 = 58.405 MeV; diffuseness: a = 0.537 fm, determined by fitting 32 spherical proton emitters.
- Standard deviation σ between experimental and calculated half-lives without formation probability: σ_calc1 = 0.297 (TPA with cosh potential), σ_UDLP1 = 0.385 (universal decay law), σ_Gamow-like = 0.304.
- Standard deviation σ with formation probability included: σ_calc2 = 0.163 (TPA with cosh potential), σ_UDLP2 = 0.253 (universal decay law).
- Improvement in standard deviation when including formation probability: 45.1% for the present method (from 0.297 to 0.163) and 34.3% for UDLP (from 0.385 to 0.253).
- Adjustable parameters for the formation probability formula: for l=0, a_l = 0.301, b_l = -1.877; for l=2, a_l = -2.665, b_l = 13.912; for l=5, a_l = -2.537, b_l = 13.405.
- Renormalization factor λ varied from 0.98 to 1.05 across all calculations.

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approach with cosh-type nuclear potential, previously applied successfully to alpha decay by the same group (Chen et al., 2016-2018), to the study of proton radioactivity. It builds on the universal decay law for proton emission (UDLP) proposed by Qi et al. (2012) and the linear relationship between formation probability and A^{1/3} noted by Delion et al. (2021). The results enable more accurate predictions of proton radioactivity half-lives for spherical emitters and suggest a simple phenomenological description of the formation probability that can be incorporated into other models.
