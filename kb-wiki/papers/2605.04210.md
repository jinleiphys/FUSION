# The nonlocal magic of a holographic Schwinger pair

> We analyze the emergence of nonlocal magic in Schwinger pair creation in strong non-Abelian (chromo)electric fields using holography. The produced quark--antiquark pair is entangled into a color singlet, yet accelerates into causally disconnected Rindler wedges. Using the Casini--Huerta--Myers conformal mapping and the probe-brane framework, we compute the refined Rényi entropy and its derivative, which captures the antiflatness of the entanglement spectrum for a spherical bipartition. We find that for boundary spacetime dimension $d>2$, the entanglement spectrum is non-flat, implying the dynamical generation of nonlocal magic in the pair creation process. Interestingly, the nonlocal magic in the holographic dual can be obtained from the free energy of the probe action.

## Key claim

Nonlocal magic (a measure of non-stabilizerness across a bipartition that cannot be removed by local unitary transformations) is dynamically generated in Schwinger pair creation at strong coupling for boundary spacetime dimension \(d>2\), as diagnosed by a strictly positive capacity of entanglement \(C_E\). This result is independent of the acceleration \(a = E/M\) and hence of the Unruh temperature, indicating the nonlocal magic reflects the intrinsic structure of the pair's quantum state rather than being a thermal artifact.

## Method

The author uses holography to study a quark-antiquark pair produced by a constant non-Abelian chromoelectric field in a large-\(N_c\), large-\(\lambda\) gauge theory with a holographic dual, where fundamental matter is introduced via a probe Dirichlet-brane. The Casini-Huerta-Myers conformal mapping is employed to compute the refined Rényi entropy and its derivative (the capacity of entanglement) for a spherical spatial region containing one member of the pair, using the on-shell Nambu-Goto action of the probe string in the \(n\)-th topological black hole background. The capacity of entanglement \(C_E\) serves as a diagnostic for nonlocal magic because a nonvanishing \(C_E\) is both necessary and sufficient for a non-flat entanglement spectrum, which implies nonlocal magic by Lemma 1 of Cao (2024).

## Key numbers

- For \(d=4\) (e.g., \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills), the entanglement entropy of the probe string is \(S_{EE} = \sqrt{\lambda}/3\).
- For \(d=4\), the capacity of entanglement is \(C_E = 2\sqrt{\lambda}/27\).
- The ratio \(C_E/S_{EE} = (d-2)/(d-1)^2\), which for \(d=4\) equals \(2/9\).
- The capacity of entanglement in general is \(C_E = \sqrt{\lambda}\,(d-2)/(d-1)^3\), strictly positive for \(d>2\).
- For \(d=2\), the capacity of entanglement vanishes, implying no nonlocal magic at leading order in the probe approximation.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum complexity measures beyond entanglement entropy in holographic systems, following the framework of Cao (2024) which established that gravitational backreaction encodes nonlocal magic. It applies the probe-brane approach of Karch (2014) and Chalabi (2020) to the holographic Schwinger pair setup previously analyzed by Jensen (2013, 2014) and Sonner (2013), where the produced pair is entangled into a color singlet but accelerates into causally disconnected Rindler wedges. The result enables future studies of nonlocal magic in other probe-brane systems (defects, impurities, flavor sectors), in time-dependent backgrounds such as Sauter pulses, and in the context of black hole evaporation and the Page curve.
