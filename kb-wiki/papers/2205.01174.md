# Bayesian Inference of Phenomenological EoS of Neutron Stars with Recent Observations

> The description of stellar interior remains as a big challenge for the nuclear astrophysics community. The consolidated knowledge is restricted to density regions around the saturation of hadronic matter $ρ_{0} = 2.8\times 10^{14} {\rm\ g\ cm^{-3}}$, regimes where our nuclear models are successfully applied. As one moves towards higher densities and extreme conditions up to five to twenty times $ρ_{0}$, little can be said about the microphysics of such objects. Here, we employ a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) strategy to access the variability of polytropic three-pircewised models for neutron star equation of state. With a fixed description of the hadronic matter, we explore a variety of models for the high density regimes leading to stellar masses up to $2.5\ M_{\odot}$. In addition, we also discuss the use of a Bayesian power regression model with heteroscedastic error. The set of EoS from the Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory (LIGO) was used as inputs and treated as data set for testing case.

## Key claim

The authors use a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) strategy to explore the variability of three-piece polytropic models for the neutron star equation of state, but only for a fixed description of hadronic matter at low densities; the analysis is limited to five specific parameter combinations and does not claim to cover the full space of possible EoS.

## Method

The authors construct a three-piece polytropic equation of state with the first segment fixed to the SLy4 EoS (Γ₀ = 1.475) up to nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 2.8×10¹⁴ g cm⁻³. They define five models (MD1–MD5) with different choices for the transition density ρ₁→₂ (either 4.8ρ₀ or 7.2ρ₀) and different polytropic indices Γ₁ and Γ₂ for the intermediate and high-density regions. They then apply MCMC sampling with 10000 iterations to obtain posterior distributions for Γ₁ and Γ₂, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to generate mass-radius relations, and compare these to observational constraints from LIGO-VIRGO, NICER, and massive pulsar measurements.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 2.8×10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Transition densities used: ρ₁→₂ = 4.8ρ₀ and 7.2ρ₀
- Polytropic index ranges: Γ₁ = {2.5, 2.6, 2.8, 3.0} and Γ₂ = {1.8, 1.9, 3.0, 3.3, 3.7}
- MCMC uncertainty assumed: σ = 0.01 for both Γ₁ and Γ₂ in each model
- Number of MCMC iterations: N = 10000
- Radius for a 1.4 M⊙ star from model MD4: R̄_MD4 = 11.96 km
- Radius for a 1.4 M⊙ star from model MD5: R̄_MD5 = 12.44 km
- Maximum mass for model MD4: M̄_max MD4 = 2.35 M⊙ with radius R̄_MD4 = 9.63 km
- Maximum mass for model MD5: M̄_max MD5 = 2.57 M⊙ with radius R̄_MD5 = 10.21 km
- Maximum mass for model MD2: M̄_max MD2 = 1.97 M⊙ with radius R̄_MD2 = 9.49 km
- Number of EoS from LIGO used as data set for the Bayesian power regression model: N = 65

## Context

This work extends the piecewise polytropic approach of Read et al. (2009) by applying Bayesian MCMC methods to explore parameter variability, rather than direct cost function minimization. The results are compared to constraints from the GW170817 event (LIGO-VIRGO), NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 (Miller 2019; Riley 2019), and massive pulsars including PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2020) and PSR J2215+5135 (Linares 2018). The authors also introduce a Bayesian power regression model with heteroscedastic errors as a potential tool for capturing the increasing variance of pressure with density across different EoS models, which could enable future physics-informed machine learning approaches to constrain the neutron star equation of state.
