# $ΛΛ$ interaction and hypernuclei

> Data on $ΛΛ$ hypernuclei provide a unique method to learn details about the strangeness S =- 2 sector of the baryon-baryon interaction. From the free space Bonn-Jülich potentials, determined from data on baryon-baryon scattering in the S = 0, -1 channels, we construct an interaction in the S =-2 sector to describe the experimentally known LL hypernuclei. After including short-range (Jastrow) and RPA correlations, we find masses for these LL hypernuclei in a reasonable agreement with data, taking into account theoretical and experimental uncertainties. Thus, we provide a natural extension, at low energies, of the Bonn-Jülich one-boson exchange potentials to the S =-2 channel.

## Key claim

The Bonn-Julich one-boson exchange potentials, originally fitted to baryon-baryon scattering data in the strangeness S=0 and S=-1 channels, can be extended to the S=-2 sector to describe double-Lambda hypernuclei, but only after including short-range Jastrow correlations and random-phase approximation (RPA) medium effects does the model achieve reasonable agreement with experimental binding energies, given the theoretical and experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors model a double-Lambda hypernucleus as two Lambda hyperons interacting with each other and with a nuclear core, using a variational approach with a Jastrow-correlated wavefunction. The free-space Lambda-Lambda interaction is constructed from sigma, omega, and phi meson exchanges with monopolar form factors, using coupling constants from the Bonn-Julich model A via SU(6) symmetry. In-medium modifications are included by summing RPA diagrams in nuclear matter via a Dyson equation for the sigma-omega propagator, then applying a local density approximation to finite nuclei by averaging over straight-line trajectories of the exchanged mesons.

## Key numbers

- For $^{6}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, the experimental binding energy is $B_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{\rm exp} = 6.91 \pm 0.13$ MeV; the model with RPA and $\Lambda_{\phi\Lambda\Lambda}=2.0$ GeV gives 6.82 MeV, and with 2.5 GeV gives 7.33 MeV.
- For $^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be, two experimental values exist: $17.7 \pm 0.4$ MeV (Danysz) and $11.9 \pm 0.13$ MeV (Nakazawa 2010); the model with RPA and $\Lambda_{\phi\Lambda\Lambda}=2.0$ GeV gives 15.6 MeV.
- For $^{13}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$B, the experimental binding energy is $23.3 \pm 0.7$ MeV; the model with RPA and $\Lambda_{\phi\Lambda\Lambda}=2.0$ GeV gives 25.4 MeV.
- The nuclear core dynamical reordering effect is estimated to be around 0.5 MeV for light double-Lambda hypernuclei.
- The phi-meson cutoff $\Lambda_{\phi\Lambda\Lambda}$ is varied among three values: 1.5, 2.0, and 2.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn-Julich one-boson exchange potentials (model A of Reuber 1994) from the S=0 and S=-1 channels to the S=-2 sector, providing a natural low-energy extension for double-Lambda hypernuclei. The results agree with earlier calculations by Saha showing that in-medium corrections are important and that RPA effects from particle-hole excitations provide larger binding energies. The findings are relevant for neutron star physics, where hyperon appearance at lower densities affects the equation of state, cooling via direct URCA processes, and r-mode instability in rotating neutron stars (Jones).
