# Relativistic descriptions of final-state interactions in charged-current quasielastic antineutrino-nucleus scattering at MiniBooNE kinematics

> The analysis of the recent charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections measured by the MiniBooNE Collaboration requires relativistic theoretical descriptions also accounting for the role of final-state interactions. In this work, we evaluate differential antineutrino-nucleus cross sections with the relativistic Green's function model, where the final-state interactions are described in the inclusive scattering consistently with the exclusive scattering using a complex optical potential. The sensitivity to the parameterization adopted for the phenomenological optical potential is discussed. The predictions of the relativistic Green's function model are compared with the results of different descriptions of final-state interactions.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (RGF) model can describe charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) antineutrino-nucleus cross sections at MiniBooNE kinematics without increasing the axial mass $M_A$ above the world average value, but the predictions are sensitive to the parameterization chosen for the phenomenological optical potential, with differences between the EDAI and EDAD1 potentials reaching up to 25% in some kinematic bins.

## Method

The authors evaluated flux-averaged double-differential CCQE cross sections for antineutrinos on $^{12}$C using the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, where final-state interactions (FSI) are treated consistently in inclusive and exclusive scattering via a complex optical potential. Bound nucleon states were described as self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions from a relativistic mean field approach. The RGF results were compared with the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), where FSI are neglected, and with the rROP approach, which uses only the real part of the optical potential.

## Key numbers

- RGF cross sections with the EDAI and EDAD1 optical potentials differ by about 15%–20% in the peak region for forward angle bins $\cos\vartheta_{\mu}=0.85$ and $0.75$ for the $^{12}\text{C}(\bar\nu_{\mu},\mu^{+})$ reaction.
- For the backward angular bin $\cos\vartheta_{\mu}=-0.15$, differences between the two optical potentials are enhanced up to 25%, though the absolute cross section magnitude is significantly reduced.
- The rROP results are typically 15% lower than the RPWIA ones for antineutrino scattering.
- The MiniBooNE $\bar\nu_{\mu}$ flux has an average energy of approximately 0.66 GeV, compared to approximately 0.79 GeV for the $\nu_{\mu}$ flux.
- The MiniBooNE collaboration reported $M_A = 1.35 \pm 0.17$ GeV/$c^2$ for CCQE measurements and $M_A = 1.39 \pm 0.11$ GeV/$c^2$ for neutral-current elastic data, while the world average from deuterium is $M_A = 1.03$ GeV/$c^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RGF calculations for neutrino scattering (Meucci and Giusti, 2011) to the antineutrino channel, where the destructive vector-axial interference term reduces the pure quasielastic contribution and makes multinucleon effects relatively more important. The results are compared with the relativistic Fermi gas model used by the MiniBooNE collaboration (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2008) and with models incorporating two-particle-two-hole excitations via meson-exchange currents (Amaro et al., 2011; Martini et al., 2011; Nieves et al., 2011), which also reproduce MiniBooNE data without increasing $M_A$. The analysis enables future comparison with MiniBooNE antineutrino data, which were still being analyzed at the time of publication, and may help disentangle the role of different nuclear effects in neutrino and antineutrino scattering.
