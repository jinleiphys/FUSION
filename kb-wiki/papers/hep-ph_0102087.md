# Deeply inelastic scattering off nuclei at RHIC

> 36 pages LaTex, 10 figures, Plenary talk at EPIC meeting, MIT, September 14th-16th, 2000

## Key claim

An electron-nucleus collider at RHIC with center-of-mass energy √s = 60–100 GeV could probe a regime of QCD where parton densities become so high that the theory enters a novel, non-perturbative "colored glass condensate" phase, even though the coupling remains weak; this regime would otherwise require an electron-proton collider with an order of magnitude higher energy than HERA to access.

## Method

The paper reviews the physics case for an eA collider at RHIC (eRHIC) by analyzing deeply inelastic scattering kinematics in the small x_Bj region (x_Bj < 0.01), comparing the accessible x–Q² range to that of HERA and fixed-target experiments. It discusses how the saturation scale Q_s(x) grows with nuclear size A^{1/3} and decreasing x, and examines inclusive observables (F₂, F_L, nuclear shadowing) and semi-inclusive observables (hard diffraction, vector meson production) as signatures of the colored glass condensate.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy range for the proposed eA collider: √s = 60–100 GeV
- Small x_Bj region defined as x_Bj < 1/(2 m_N R_A) ≈ 0.01 for a large nucleus
- At HERA, √s ≈ 300 GeV (27.5 GeV electrons on 920 GeV protons)
- At HERA, for Q² = 20 GeV², one finds 25–30 gluons per unit rapidity at x_Bj = 10⁻⁴ in the proton
- The saturation scale defined as Q_s²(x) = (1/πR²) dN/dη
- For a nucleus, Q_s² ∝ A^{1/3} / x^δ with δ ≈ 0.3 at Q² of a few GeV²
- The effective A^{1/3} = 4 (conservative, without impact parameter tagging) gives x_proton ≈ x_nucleus / 100 for the same parton density
- Hard diffraction at HERA: roughly 10% of the cross-section corresponds to hard diffractive events with M_X > 3 GeV
- For eA collisions at eRHIC, saturation models predict the diffractive ratio R_D^A can be on the order of 30% for the largest nuclei

## Context

This paper extends the physics program of the HERA ep collider (which operated at √s ≈ 300 GeV) to nuclear targets, arguing that an eA collider can access parton densities in nuclei that would require an order-of-magnitude higher energy in ep collisions. It builds on the BFKL Pomeron framework developed by Lipatov and colleagues, the saturation models of Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (GLR), and the color glass condensate formalism of McLerran and Venugopalan. The work enables future experimental searches for saturation effects and the colored glass condensate at eRHIC, complementing heavy-ion collision studies at RHIC and LHC.
