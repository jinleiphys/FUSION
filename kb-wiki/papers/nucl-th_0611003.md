# Landau parameters for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter based on a relativistic model of composite and finite extension nucleons

> We study the properties of cold asymmetric nuclear matter at high density, applying the quark meson coupling model with excluded volume corrections in the framework of the Landau theory of relativistic Fermi liquids. We discuss the role of the finite spatial extension of composite baryons on dynamical and statistical properties such as the Landau parameters, the compressibility, and the symmetry energy. We have also calculated the low lying collective eigenfrequencies arising from the collisionless quasiparticle transport equation, considering both unstable and stable modes. An overall analysis of the excluded volume correlations on the collective properties is performed.

# Landau parameters for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter based on a relativistic model of composite and finite extension nucleons

## Key claim

The quark meson coupling model with excluded volume corrections (representing finite nucleon size) produces a stiffer equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter at high density than point-like nucleon models, with the symmetry energy lying within empirical constraints from isospin diffusion data for densities up to about 2.5 times saturation density, but only for a narrow range of the isovector meson coupling constants.

## Method

The authors apply the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, which treats nucleons as MIT bags containing three valence quarks interacting with sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons, and incorporate excluded volume corrections via a Van der Waals-like normalization factor that reduces the available volume for baryon motion. They compute the Landau parameters from second variations of the energy density with respect to quasiparticle distribution functions, then solve the collisionless Landau kinetic equation to obtain collective eigenfrequencies for both stable and unstable modes in cold asymmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: n0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: E_bind = -16 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_s(n0) = 31.6 MeV
- Nuclear compressibility at saturation for symmetric matter: kappa = 340 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameter: L = 95.8 MeV (CC case)
- Symmetry energy curvature parameter: K_s = 19 MeV (CC case)
- Asymmetry energy combination: K_asy = -555 +/- 5 MeV (CC case)
- For the NC (no excluded volume) case: L = 91.4 +/- 0.6 MeV and K_asy = -553 +/- 2 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass from prior work with this model: M_max = 1.89 solar masses
- Bag radius at zero density: R_b = 0.6 fm
- Bag constant: B_0^(1/4) = 210.89 MeV
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_delta = 984 MeV, m_rho = 770 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark meson coupling model with excluded volume corrections previously developed by the same authors (Aguirre and De Paoli, 2005) to the calculation of Landau parameters and collective modes in asymmetric nuclear matter. It builds on the relativistic Fermi liquid theory framework of Matsui (1981) and Caillon (1999), and the symmetry energy constraints from isospin diffusion data analyzed by Chen et al. (2005). The results enable improved modeling of neutron star structure, particularly addressing the unexpectedly high mass (2.1 +/- 0.2 solar masses) measured for pulsar PSR J0751+18007 by Nice et al. (2005), which requires a stiffer equation of state than conventional models with compressibility around 250 MeV provide.
