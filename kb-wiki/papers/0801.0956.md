# Field Theoretic Description of Ultrarelativistic Electron-Positron Plasmas

> Ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas can be produced in high-intensity laser fields and play a role in various astrophysical situations. Their properties can be calculated using QED at finite temperature. Here we will use perturbative QED at finite temperature for calculating various important properties, such as the equation of state, dispersion relations of collective plasma modes of photons and electrons, Debye screening, damping rates, mean free paths, collision times, transport coefficients, and particle production rates, of ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas. In particular, we will focus on electron-positron plasmas produced with ultra-strong lasers.

## Key claim

Perturbative QED at finite temperature can reliably predict the properties of ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas (EPPs), and these predictions are more trustworthy than analogous QCD calculations for quark-gluon plasmas because the QED coupling is small (e=0.3), making the EPP a weakly coupled plasma (Coulomb coupling parameter Λ = 5.3×10⁻³ at T=10 MeV) in contrast to the strongly coupled QGP.

## Method

The author applies perturbative QED at finite temperature using the hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation technique, which is necessary for obtaining gauge-invariant, infrared-finite results complete to leading order in the coupling constant. Results for collective phenomena (dielectric functions, dispersion relations, Debye screening) are derived from the one-loop polarization tensor and electron self-energy in the HTL approximation, while transport properties (damping rates, mean free paths, shear viscosity, energy loss) are computed from scattering diagrams using HTL-resummed propagators. The formalism is extended to non-equilibrium situations by introducing a fugacity factor and to finite chemical potential by modifying the Fermi-Dirac distribution.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium electron/positron number density: ρ_e^eq = 0.37 T³, giving 4.9×10⁴⁰ m⁻³ at T=10 MeV
- Energy density of electron-positron-photon gas: ε^eq = 1.81 T⁴, giving 3.8×10²⁹ J m⁻³ at T=10 MeV
- Effective photon mass: m_ph = eT/3 = 1 MeV at T=10 MeV
- Plasma frequency: ω_pl = m_ph = 1.5×10²¹ s⁻¹ at T=10 MeV
- Debye screening length: λ_D = 1/(√3 m_ph) = 1.1×10⁻¹³ m at T=10 MeV
- Effective electron mass: m_F = eT/√8 = 1.1 MeV at T=10 MeV
- Electron damping rate: γ_e = e²T/(4π) ln(1/e) = 86 keV at T=10 MeV
- Electron transport rate: Γ_e = 0.54 keV at T=10 MeV
- Electron mean free path: λ_e^mfp = 0.37 nm at T=10 MeV
- Electron collision time: τ_e = 1.2×10⁻¹⁸ s at T=10 MeV
- Shear viscosity (elementary kinetic theory): η_e = 7.9×10¹⁰ Pa s at T=10 MeV
- Photon mean free path: λ_ph^mfp = 0.28 nm at T=10 MeV
- Photon collision time: τ_ph = 9.4×10⁻¹⁹ s at T=10 MeV
- Collisional energy loss of 100 MeV electron: 200 MeV/nm at T=10 MeV
- For non-equilibrium laser-produced EPP (fugacity λ=10⁻¹²): m_ph^noneq = 1 eV, ω_pl^noneq = 1.5×10¹⁵ Hz, λ_D = 0.1 μm

## Context

This work extends the perturbative thermal field theory methods developed for the quark-gluon plasma (Thoma, 1995a; Braaten and Pisarski, 1990) to the case of ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas, which can now be studied in laboratory laser experiments (Liang et al., 1998; Shen and Meyer-ter-Vehn, 2001). The paper provides a comprehensive reference for experimentalists by collecting and adapting QGP results to QED plasmas, including predictions for a specific laser-induced EPP at T=10 MeV. It enables future experimental searches for collective phenomena such as the plasmino branch in the electron dispersion relation, which could be observed through van Hove singularities in the electron spectrum, and provides the theoretical foundation for interpreting upcoming laser-plasma experiments.
