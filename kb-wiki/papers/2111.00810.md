# Forward coherent $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons production in the photon induced reaction on nuclei

> The differential cross sections of the forward coherent $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons photoproduction from nuclei have been calculated using the Glauber model for the nuclear reaction. The measured cross section of the elementary reaction, i.e., $γN \to ω(ϕ) N$ reaction, are used as input in the Glauber model. The experimentally determined free-space scattering parameters of the quoted mesons have been used to evaluate the meson nucleus interactions. The sensitivity of the cross section to the Fermi-motion of the nucleon and the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation in the nucleus are studied. The calculated results are compared with the measured cross sections.

# Forward coherent ω and φ mesons production in the photon induced reaction on nuclei

## Key claim

The Glauber model calculations using measured elementary cross sections and free-space scattering parameters can reproduce the measured forward coherent photoproduction cross sections for ω and φ mesons from nuclei, but only when nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation is included; the Fermi-motion effect is significant for ω mesons at 3.9 GeV but negligible for φ mesons at 6.4 and 8.3 GeV.

## Method

The differential cross sections for forward coherent ω and φ meson photoproduction from nuclei were calculated using the Glauber model, with the measured forward four-momentum transfer cross sections of the elementary γN → ωN and γN → φN reactions as input. The meson-nucleus distortion was evaluated using experimentally determined free-space scattering parameters (αVN and σtVN) for each meson. The sensitivity of the cross sections to Fermi-motion of nucleons and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation was investigated by incorporating the nuclear spectral function and a correlation function C(l) based on nuclear matter estimates.

## Key numbers

- For ω meson photoproduction at 3.9 GeV, the cross section decreases due to Fermi-motion and increases due to short-range correlation; for nuclei with A > 25, the combined effect (GM+FS) yields an increase relative to the plain Glauber model.
- For φ meson photoproduction at 6.4 and 8.3 GeV, the Fermi-motion contribution was found to be insignificant, while short-range correlation considerably enhances the cross section.
- The correlation function parameters used are α = 1.1 fm⁻² and β = 0.68 fm⁻², with Fermi momentum kF = 1.36 fm⁻¹.
- The φ meson coupling constant γφ = 6.72 was extracted from the measured φ → e⁺e⁻ decay width using Γ(mφ)φ→e⁺e⁻ = (π/3)(αem/γφ)² mφ, with αem = 1/137.036.
- The ratio of real to imaginary part of the forward φN scattering amplitude αφN = -0.3 was used.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sibirtsev et al. (using VMD and Glauber model) of the same forward coherent cross section data from Brodsky et al. and McClellan et al., but uses measured elementary γN → VN cross sections and free-space scattering parameters rather than VMD-based production. The study investigates two nuclear effects, Fermi-motion and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlation, that were not considered in the earlier Sibirtsev analysis. The results enable improved extraction of in-medium vector meson scattering parameters and inform future studies of vector meson properties in nuclear matter, particularly for heavy nuclei where short-range correlation effects are most pronounced.
