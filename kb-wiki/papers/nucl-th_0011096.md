# Present Status of Realistic Shell-Model Calculations

> The present paper is comprised of two parts. First, we give a brief survey of the theoretical framework for microscopic nuclear structure calculations starting from a free nucleon-nucleon potential. Then, we present some selected results of a comprehensive study of nuclei near doubly closed shells. In all these shell-model calculations we have made use of realistic effective interactions derived from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential by means of a G-matrix folded-diagram method. We show that this kind of calculation yields a very good agreement with experiment for all the nuclei considered. This leads to the conclusion that realistic effective interactions are now able to describe with quantitative accuracy the properties of complex nuclei.

## Key claim

Realistic shell-model calculations using effective interactions derived from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential can reproduce experimental nuclear structure properties with quantitative accuracy, but this claim is currently validated only for nuclei with identical valence nucleons (isospin T=1 matrix elements), while a stringent test of the T=0 matrix elements remains to be completed.

## Method

The authors derive a model-space effective interaction from the free Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential using a G-matrix folded-diagram method. The G-matrix is calculated via the Tsai-Kuo method, and the effective interaction is obtained by summing the Q-box folded-diagram series to all orders using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method, including G-diagrams through second order. The resulting two-body effective interaction is then used in shell-model calculations for nuclei near doubly magic cores, with experimental single-particle energies taken from the spectra of the corresponding single-particle or single-hole valence nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Bonn-A potential predicts a deuteron D-state probability P_D = 4.4%, compared to 5.8% for the Paris potential
- The Bonn-A potential predicts the triton binding energy as 8.35 MeV, close to the experimental value of 8.48 MeV
- The CD-Bonn potential predicts the triton binding energy as 8.0 MeV, while other high-precision potentials underbind the triton by about 800 keV
- Root mean square deviation sigma for energy levels across 15 nuclei ranges from 59 keV (for I-135) to 165 keV (for Sn-104), with 7 out of 15 values below 100 keV
- For Po-210, the calculated B(E2;4_1+ -> 2_1+) is 4.46 W.u. versus experimental 4.53 +/- 0.15 W.u.; the calculated B(E2;8_1+ -> 6_1+) is 1.25 W.u. versus experimental 1.10 +/- 0.05 W.u.
- For Po-210, the calculated magnetic moment of the 8_1+ state is +7.06 nm versus experimental +7.35 +/- 0.05 nm; the calculated quadrupole moment of the 8_1+ state is -0.588 e b versus experimental -0.552 +/- 0.020 e b
- Ground-state binding energies relative to the closest doubly closed core agree within error bars for all six nuclei reported, e.g., for Pb-206: calculated -14.12 +/- 0.01 MeV versus experimental -14.12 +/- 0.00 MeV

## Context

This work extends the pioneering approach of Kuo and Brown (1966) and Kuo and Herling (1972), who first derived effective interactions from free nucleon-nucleon potentials, but addresses the skepticism that arose in subsequent decades (Elliott, 1988) about the reliability of such "realistic" shell-model calculations. The authors present a comprehensive study of nuclei near doubly magic Sn-100, Sn-132, and Pb-208, showing that modern G-matrix folded-diagram methods combined with the Bonn-A potential yield quantitative agreement with experiment. This enables future work to extend these calculations to nuclei with both neutrons and protons in valence shells and to systematically test the dependence of nuclear structure results on the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential, including comparisons with the newer CD-Bonn potential.
