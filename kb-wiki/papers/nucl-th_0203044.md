# Coherent $ω$ photoproduction from nuclei and $ω$ properties in nuclear matter

> The coherent $ω$-meson photoproduction from nuclei is proposed as a phenomenological method to evaluate the momentum dependence of $ω$-meson mass shift and width in nuclear matter. We analyze available data on un-separated coherent and incoherent $ω$-meson photoproduction from nuclei and extract the imaginary part of the the complex forward $ω{N}$ scattering amplitude, which is proportional to the in-medium $ω$-meson width. The accuracy of the currently available data is not sufficient to evaluate the real part of forward $ω{N}$ scattering amplitude and reconstruct the momentum dependence of the mass shift of the $ω$-meson.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that coherent ω-meson photoproduction from nuclei can be used to extract the momentum dependence of the ω-meson mass shift and width in nuclear matter, but the accuracy of currently available data is insufficient to evaluate the real part of the forward ωN scattering amplitude and thus cannot reconstruct the mass shift.

## Method

The authors analyze existing data on unseparated coherent and incoherent ω-meson photoproduction from nuclear targets (Be, C, Al, Cu) at photon beam energies between 3.9 and 8.2 GeV. They fit the differential cross sections using an eikonal model that incorporates the total ωN cross section σ_ωN and the ratio α_ω of real to imaginary parts of the forward ωN scattering amplitude, extracting the imaginary part (proportional to the in-medium ω width) from the data.

## Key numbers

- σ_ωN = 36.1 ± 2.2 mb extracted from forward ω photoproduction data at E_γ = 3.9 GeV (Brodbeck data)
- σ_ωN = 36.8 ± 2.0 mb extracted from forward ω photoproduction data at E_γ = 6.8 GeV (Behrend1 data)
- σ_ωN = 45.9 ± 1.3 mb extracted from Be target data at E_γ = 6.8 GeV (Behrend data)
- σ_ωN = 44.8 ± 0.1 mb extracted from Cu target data at E_γ = 6.8 GeV (Behrend data)
- σ_ωN = 48 ± 4 mb extracted from C target data at E_γ = 5.7 GeV (Braccini data)
- σ_ωN = 44 ± 2 mb extracted from Al target data at E_γ = 5.7 GeV (Braccini data)
- Lower limit for Im f_ωN(0) at threshold (E_ω = m_ω) = 0.164 fm, evaluated from π⁻ + p → ω + n measurements (Hanhart)
- Photon-ω coupling constant γ_ω = 8.24 ± 0.24

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions for ω-meson in-medium modification by Tsushima, Friman, Klingl, and Saito, which were given only for ω-mesons at rest (k_ω = 0). The method enables future experiments (such as those planned with HADES at GSI, TAPS and Crystal Barrel at ELSA, and ANKE at COSY) to measure the momentum dependence of the ω-meson mass shift and width in nuclear matter, provided that coherent and incoherent photoproduction can be experimentally separated. The analysis also compares with predictions from the additive quark model and Regge theory (Donnachie and Landshoff) and with coupled channel analyses by Friman and effective Lagrangian calculations by Klingl.
