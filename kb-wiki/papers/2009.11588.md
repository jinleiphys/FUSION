# Structure of the quartetting ground state of $N=Z$ nuclei

> The formal equivalence between the quartetting picture and the symmetry restored BCS picture is established for the ground state correlations induced by the general isovector-isoscalar pairing interaction. Multiple ground state structures compatible with the particle number and isospin symmetries are evaluated. The competition of isovector and isoscalar correlations is discussed for the $N=Z$ nuclei above $^{100}$Sn.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the formal equivalence between the quartetting picture and the symmetry-restored BCS picture for ground state correlations induced by the general isovector-isoscalar pairing interaction, but notes that the maximally correlated collective quartet state (with a specific mixing coefficient) is slightly different from both the PQCM and projected BCS states, though the energy variation is less than 0.01 MeV.

## Method

The authors compare several symmetry-preserving ground state ansatze for the isovector-isoscalar pairing Hamiltonian in axially deformed N=Z nuclei: the pair-quartet condensation model (PQCM) state, the particle-number and isospin projected BCS (P_NT BCS) state, pure isovector and pure isoscalar quartet condensates, and a simple superposition of the two. They perform realistic calculations using Skyrme-HF single-particle states from the code ev8 with the Sly4 force, a zero-range pairing force, and a model space of 10 single-particle levels above closed cores of O-16, Ca-40, and Sn-100, minimizing the energy with respect to mixing amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energy for Ne-20: 11.38 MeV for both PQCM and P_NT BCS states
- Correlation energy for Ce-116: 12.40 MeV for both PQCM and P_NT BCS states
- Overlap between PQCM and P_NT BCS states: 100% for Ne-20, Mg-24, Si-32, Ti-44, Ni-56, Te-104, Xe-108, Ba-112, Ce-116; 99.99% for Fe-52
- Relative error of pure isoscalar solution compared to the iv+is state for Sm-124: 31.88%
- Correlation energy variation range for the maximally correlated collective quartet state in Xe-108: less than 0.01 MeV
- Pairing force strengths used: V0(T=1) = 465 MeV fm^3, V0(T=0) = 1.5 times V0(T=1)

## Context

This work extends the formal correspondence between quartetting models and symmetry-restored BCS approaches previously established for pure isovector pairing by Sandulescu et al. (2012) and the bridging paper, now generalized to combined isovector and isoscalar pairing. It contrasts with mean-field studies by Frauendorf (2014) and Gezerlis et al. (2011) which predict that isovector and isoscalar correlations do not coexist in N=Z nuclei, and specifically contradicts the spherical mean-field prediction of Gezerlis et al. (2011) of dominating isoscalar correlations above N=Z=60, finding instead dominating isovector correlations in the deformed quartetting approach. The work enables future inclusion of angular momentum projection for improved description of the isovector-isoscalar interplay.
