# Modeling neutrino-induced charged pion production on water at T2K kinematics

> Pion production is a significant component of the signal in accelerator-based neutrino experiments. Over the last years, the MiniBooNE, T2K and MINERvA collaborations have reported a substantial amount of data on (anti)neutrino-induced pion production on the nucleus. However, a comprehensive and consistent description of the whole data set is still missing. We aim at improving the current understanding of neutrino-induced pion production on the nucleus. To this end, the comparison of experimental data with theoretical predictions, preferably based on microscopic models, is essential to disentangle the different reaction mechanisms involved in the process. To describe single-pion production (SPP) we use a hybrid model that combines a low- and a high-energy approach. The low-energy model (LEM) contains resonances and background terms. At high invariant masses, a high-energy model based on a Regge approach is employed. The model is implemented in the nucleus using the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA). We present a comparison of the hybrid-RPWIA and LEM with the recent neutrino-induced charged current $1π^+$ production cross section on water reported by T2K. In order to judge the impact of final-state interactions (FSI) we confront our results with those of the NuWro Monte Carlo generator. The hybrid-RPWIA model and NuWro compare favorably to the data, albeit that FSI are not included in the former. These results complement our previous work [Phys. Rev. D 97, 013004 (2018)] where we compared the models to the MINERvA and MiniBooNE $1π^+$ data. The hybrid-RPWIA model tends to overpredict both the T2K and MINERvA data in kinematic regions where the largest suppression due to FSI is expected, and agrees remarkably well with the data in other kinematic regions. On the contrary, the MiniBooNE data is underpredicted over the whole kinematic range.

## Key claim

The hybrid-RPWIA model, which combines a low-energy resonance-plus-background description with a high-energy Regge approach, reproduces the shape and strength of T2K charged-current single-pion production data on water, but tends to overpredict the data in kinematic regions where final-state interactions are expected to cause the largest suppression, while agreeing well in other regions.

## Method

The authors model single-pion production using a hybrid approach: at low invariant masses (W less than about 1.5 GeV), they use a low-energy model containing the delta resonance, higher-mass isospin-1/2 resonances (P11(1440), S11(1535), D13(1520)), and background terms from chiral perturbation theory; at high invariant masses, they employ a Regge-based description for the non-resonant amplitudes. The elementary model is embedded in the nucleus using the relativistic plane wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), where bound nucleons are described by relativistic mean-field wavefunctions and final-state pion and nucleon are treated as plane waves. The model predictions are compared to T2K data and to results from the NuWro Monte Carlo generator, which includes final-state interactions via an intranuclear cascade.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section reported by T2K: 4.25 ± 0.48 (stat) ± 1.56 (syst) × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/nucleon, compatible with the hybrid-RPWIA model prediction of 4.82 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/nucleon (average of predictions with and without Oset-Salcedo medium modification of the delta width).
* The uncertainty due to medium modification of the delta width in the hybrid-RPWIA model is around 9%.
* NuWro Monte Carlo predicts a total cross section of 6.97 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/nucleon before final-state interactions and 5.44 × 10⁻⁴⁰ cm²/nucleon after final-state interactions.
* The T2K neutrino flux peaks at approximately 600 MeV.
* The phase space is restricted to muon momentum Pμ > 200 MeV, pion momentum Pπ > 200 MeV, cosθπ > 0.3, and cosθμ > 0.3.
* Higher-mass isospin-1/2 resonances contribute up to 20% of the cross section for pion momenta between 0.5 GeV and 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Phys. Rev. D 97, 013004, 2018) where the hybrid-RPWIA model was compared to MINERvA and MiniBooNE charged-current single-pion data. The present paper focuses on T2K kinematics, where the higher-energy tail of the neutrino flux makes the high-energy Regge component essential, unlike in the MiniBooNE and MINERvA comparisons. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments such as T2K and DUNE, and highlight the need for consistent treatment of final-state interactions and medium modifications of the delta resonance.
