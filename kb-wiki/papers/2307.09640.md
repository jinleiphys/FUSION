# Multiscale evolution of heavy flavor in the QGP

> Shower development dynamics for a jet traveling through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is a multiscale process, where the heavy flavor mass is an important scale. During the high virtuality portion of the jet evolution in the QGP, emission of gluons from a heavy flavor is modified owing to heavy quark mass. Medium-induced radiation of heavy flavor is sensitive to microscopic processes (e.g. diffusion), whose virtuality dependence is phenomenologically explored in this study. In the lower virtuality part of shower evolution, i.e. when the mass is comparable to the virtuality of the parton, scattering and radiation processes of heavy quarks differ from light quarks. The effects of these mechanisms on shower development in heavy flavor tagged showers in the QGP is explored here. Furthermore, this multiscale study examines dynamical pair production of heavy flavor (via virtual gluon splittings) and their subsequent evolution in the QGP, which is not possible otherwise. A realistic event-by-event simulation is performed using the JETSCAPE framework. Energy-momentum exchange with the medium proceeds using a weak coupling recoil approach. Using leading hadron and open heavy flavor observables, differences in heavy versus light quark energy-loss mechanisms are explored, while the importance of heavy flavor pair production is highlighted along with future directions to study.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multiscale simulation (MATTER+LBT) with a virtuality-dependent transport coefficient \(\hat{q}(t)\) is necessary to simultaneously describe both D-meson and charged hadron nuclear modification factors \(R_{AA}\) in Pb-Pb collisions, and that dynamical in-medium heavy flavor production via \(g\to Q+\bar{Q}\) contributes significantly (at the 20% level) to heavy quark multiplicity and cannot be neglected.

## Method

The JETSCAPE framework was used to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV in the 0-10% centrality class, employing a two-stage parton energy loss model: the high-virtuality portion was described by the MATTER module using higher twist formalism with a virtuality-dependent \(\hat{q}(t)\), and the low-virtuality portion was modeled by the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) module. The medium-modified splitting function for \(Q\to Q+g\) was used, and dynamical heavy flavor production via \(g\to Q+\bar{Q}\) was included in MATTER. Energy-momentum exchange with the medium used a weak coupling recoil approach, and the QGP medium was generated using Bayesian maximum a posteriori parameters from Bernhard (2019).

## Key numbers

- D-meson \(R_{AA}\) is significantly affected by the \(g\to Q+\bar{Q}\) process, with an effect on the order of 20% (based on ratio plots in Figure 3).
- The switching virtuality between MATTER and LBT was set to \(t_s = 4\) GeV\(^2\), found to best describe the \(R_{AA}\) data.
- The \(\hat{q}(t)\) parametrization used \(c_1 = 10\) and \(c_2 = 100\) for the MATTER alone and MATTER+LBT curves.
- The study used Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV in the 0-10% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework's multiscale modeling of jet quenching (JETSCAPE Collaboration, 2020-2023) by specifically investigating heavy flavor dynamics, including the virtuality-dependent \(\hat{q}(t)\) proposed by Kumar (2019) and dynamical heavy flavor pair production. It contrasts with calculations by Attems (2022) that did not rely on light-flavor \(\hat{q}\) effects when correcting the \(P_{Q\leftarrow g}\) splitting function. The results enable future Bayesian analyses to constrain the virtuality and mass dependence of the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}(t,M)\), and motivate improved medium-induced splitting functions for \(P_{Q\leftarrow g}\) using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) formalism from Abir (2015) and Kumar (2019).
