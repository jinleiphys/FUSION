# Effects of bulk viscosity and hadronic rescattering in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC

> We describe ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC with a hybrid model using the IP-Glasma model for the earliest stage and viscous hydrodynamics and microscopic transport for the later stages of the collision. We demonstrate that within this framework the bulk viscosity of the plasma plays an important role in describing the experimentally observed radial flow and azimuthal anisotropy simultaneously. We further investigate the dependence of observables on the temperature below which we employ the microscopic transport description.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a finite bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma is essential for simultaneously describing the observed radial flow (mean transverse momentum) and azimuthal anisotropy (flow coefficients) in heavy ion collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, but only within the specific framework of IP-Glasma initial conditions combined with viscous hydrodynamics and hadronic transport.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining IP-Glasma initial conditions for the pre-equilibrium stage, second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with both shear and bulk viscosity for the QGP evolution, and the UrQMD microscopic transport model for the hadronic afterburner stage. They performed systematic scans of the switching temperature between hydrodynamics and transport (T_sw from 135 to 165 MeV) and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (eta/s) to compare with experimental data on identified particle multiplicities, mean transverse momentum, and anisotropic flow coefficients at RHIC (Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and LHC (Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV).

## Key numbers

- At LHC, eta/s = 0.095 when bulk viscosity is included, versus eta/s = 0.16 when bulk viscosity is set to zero, to reproduce the measured charged hadron v_n
- The bulk viscosity peak temperature is set to T_peak = 180 MeV based on the equation of state transition temperature
- Switching temperature at LHC: T_sw = 145 MeV provides best agreement with ALICE proton multiplicity data
- Switching temperature at RHIC: T_sw = 165 MeV provides best agreement with STAR proton multiplicity data
- At RHIC, eta/s = 0.06 was adjusted to describe the momentum anisotropies
- The IP-Glasma initial conditions are evolved up to tau_0 = 0.4 fm before matching to hydrodynamics
- Quantum effects from using Poisson distributions at switching are less than 10% for pions and less than 1% for heavier hadrons in the T_sw range 135-165 MeV

## Context

This work extends the findings of Ryu (2015) by providing a more detailed analysis of bulk viscosity effects on additional observables and different collision energies, and by systematically studying the hadronic rescattering stage. It builds on prior work showing that bulk viscosity can become large near the QCD crossover (Meyer 2007, Karsch 2007, Buchel 2007) and that shear viscosity alone was previously considered the dominant dissipation source (Romatschke 2007, Song 2010, Song 2011). The results enable more precise extraction of QGP transport properties through Bayesian analysis approaches such as that of Bernhard (2016).
