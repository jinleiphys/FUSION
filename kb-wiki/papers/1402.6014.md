# Constraining Majorana CP Phase in Precision Era of Cosmology and Double Beta Decay Experiment

> We show that precision measurement of (1) sum of neutrino masses by cosmological observation and (2) lifetime of neutrinoless double beta decay in ton-scale experiments, with supplementary use of (3) effective mass measured in single beta decay experiment, would allow us to obtain information on the Majorana phase of neutrinos. To quantify the sensitivity to the phase we use the CP exclusion fraction, a fraction of the CP phase parameter space that can be excluded for a given set of assumed input parameters, a global measure for CP violation. We illustrate the sensitivity under varying assumptions, from modest to optimistic ones, on experimental errors and theoretical uncertainty of nuclear matrix elements. Assuming that the latter can be reduced to a factor of ~1.5 we find that one of the two Majorana phases (denoted as alpha_(21)) can be constrained by excluding ~10-40% of the phase space at 2sigma CL even with the modest choice of experimental error for the lowest neutrino mass of 0.1 eV. The characteristic features of the sensitivity to alpha_(21), such as dependences on the true values of alpha_(21), are addressed.

## Key claim
Precision measurements of the sum of neutrino masses from cosmology and the neutrinoless double beta decay lifetime from ton-scale experiments, supplemented by single beta decay data, can constrain one of the two Majorana CP phases (α₂₁) by excluding approximately 10–40% of the phase space at 2σ confidence level, but only if the nuclear matrix element uncertainty is reduced to a factor of about 1.5 and the lightest neutrino mass is at least 0.1 eV.

## Method
The authors performed a χ²-based statistical analysis combining three observables: the effective Majorana mass m₀νββ from neutrinoless double beta decay, the sum of neutrino masses Σ from cosmology, and the effective electron neutrino mass m_β from single beta decay. They assumed standard three-flavor mixing with Majorana neutrinos and used the CP exclusion fraction f_CPX, defined as the fraction of the Majorana CP phase parameter space that can be excluded at a given confidence level, as a global sensitivity measure. The analysis varied experimental errors and nuclear matrix element uncertainties from modest to optimistic assumptions, with the lightest neutrino mass m₀ as the key parameter.

## Key numbers
- With the lightest neutrino mass m₀ = 0.1 eV, a nuclear matrix element uncertainty factor of ~1.5, and modest experimental errors (σ_{0νββ} = 0.01 eV, σ_Σ = 0.05 eV, σ_β = 0.06 eV), the Majorana phase α₂₁ can be constrained by excluding approximately 10–40% of the phase space at 2σ CL.
- The ratio of maximum to minimum effective Majorana mass in the almost degenerate regime (m₀ ≳ 0.05 eV) is m_{0νββ}^{max} / m_{0νββ}^{min} ≃ 2.6, arising from the dependence on α₂₁.
- For the normal mass ordering with small m₀ (~10⁻⁴ eV), m_{0νββ} varies between about 9.12 × 10⁻⁴ eV and 3.58 × 10⁻³ eV, a factor of ~4 variation due to the phase α₃₁.
- The reference experimental uncertainties used are: σ_{0νββ} = 0.01 eV, σ_Σ = 0.05 eV, and σ_β = 0.06 eV.

## Context
This work extends earlier efforts to probe Majorana CP phases using double beta decay, cosmology, and beta decay, as previously examined by Minakata (1996), Bilenky (2001), Pascoli (2001, 2002, 2005), Barger (2002), Nunokawa (2002), and others. It builds on the improved precision of mixing angles from reactor experiments (KamLAND, Daya Bay, RENO) and cosmological constraints from Planck (2013), which now allow the sum of neutrino masses to be bounded to Σ < 0.23 eV. The CP exclusion fraction f_CPX, previously applied to the Dirac CP phase in long-baseline experiments by Machado (2013), is adapted here for Majorana phases. The results enable future experimental strategies: if the lightest neutrino mass is near 0.1 eV (as hinted by some cosmological analyses favoring Σ ~ 0.32 eV), combined ton-scale double beta decay and precision cosmology could provide the first partial constraints on Majorana CP violation, despite the dominant pessimism in earlier decades.
