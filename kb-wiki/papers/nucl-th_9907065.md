# Symmetry-Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Equations

> Symmetry-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations are derived using the variational ansatz for the generalized one-body density-matrix in the Valatin form. It is shown that the projected-energy functional can be completely expressed in terms of the HFB density-matrix and the pairing-tensor. The variation of this projected-energy is shown to result in HFB equations with modified expressions for the pairing-potential and the Hartree-Fock field. The expressions for these quantities are explicitly derived for the case of particle number-projection. The numerical applicability of this projection method is studied in an exactly soluble model of a deformed single-j shell.

## Key claim

The paper derives, for the first time, a set of symmetry-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations that have the same form as the unprojected case, with the only difference being that the expressions for the Hartree-Fock potential and the pairing field are more involved; this allows modification of existing unprojected HFB codes without much difficulty.

## Method

The authors express the projected-energy functional entirely in terms of the HFB density-matrix ρ and the pairing-tensor κ, using the generalized Wick theorem to write norm- and Hamiltonian-overlaps in terms of these quantities. They then perform a variation of this projected-energy functional with respect to ρ and κ under the constraint R² = R (where R is the generalized density matrix in Valatin form), which yields HFB equations of the conventional structure but with modified fields h and Δ defined as derivatives of the projected energy. Explicit expressions for these fields are derived for the case of particle-number projection, and the numerical applicability is tested in an exactly soluble model of a deformed single-j shell.

## Key numbers

* The norm-overlap for particle-number projection is given by x(φ) = (1/2π) e^(−iφN) √det(e^(2iφ)ρ + (1−ρ)) = (1/2π) e^(iφ(M−N)) / √det C(φ), where M is the dimension of the single-particle space.
* The projected HF field h^N is decomposed into three parts: h^N = ε^N + Γ^N + Λ^N, each involving integrals over the gauge angle φ with the weight function y(φ).
* The matrix C(φ) for number projection is C(φ) = (ρ + e^(−2iφ)(1−ρ))^(−1) = e^(2iφ)(1 + ρ(e^(2iφ)−1))^(−1).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing method of variation after projection (VAP), which has been known for over thirty years but whose numerical solution has been complicated; previous fully self-consistent VAP calculations were restricted to light nuclei (Schmid) or to BCS theory with particle-number violation (DMP 1964). The paper provides a diagonalization form for the projected variational problem for the first time, enabling the use of fast iterative numerical techniques already developed for unprojected HFB solutions. This enables future applications of exact number projection within full HFB theory without the need for gradient methods (ER 1982a) or the approximate Lipkin-Nogami method (fo 1996).
