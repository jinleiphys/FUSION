# Constraining the initial conditions and temperature dependent transport with three-particle correlations in Au+Au collisions

> We present three-particle mixed-harmonic correlations $\la \cos (mϕ_a + nϕ_b - (m+n) ϕ_c)\ra$ for harmonics $m,n=1-3$ for charged particles in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. These measurements provide information on the three-dimensional structure of the initial collision zone and are important for constraining models of a subsequent low-viscosity quark-gluon plasma expansion phase. We investigate correlations between the first, second and third harmonics predicted as a consequence of fluctuations in the initial state. The dependence of the correlations on the pseudorapidity separation between particles show hints of a breaking of longitudinal invariance. We compare our results to a number of state-of-the art hydrodynamic calculations with different initial states and temperature dependent viscosities. These measurements provide important steps towards constraining the temperature dependent transport and the longitudinal structure of the initial state at RHIC.

## Key claim

Three-particle mixed-harmonic correlations in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions provide constraints on the three-dimensional structure of the initial collision zone and the temperature-dependent shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, but the pseudorapidity dependence of these correlations shows only "hints" of longitudinal invariance breaking rather than a definitive observation.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured three-particle mixed-harmonic correlations of the form ⟨cos(mφ_a + nφ_b − (m+n)φ_c)⟩ for harmonics m,n = 1–3 using charged particles produced in √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The correlations were analyzed as functions of centrality and pseudorapidity separation between particles, and the results were compared to several state-of-the-art hydrodynamic calculations that employ different initial-state models and temperature-dependent shear viscosities.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √s_NN = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at RHIC
- Harmonic orders studied: m,n = 1–3 for the three-particle mixed-harmonic correlations
- The correlations were measured as a function of pseudorapidity separation between particles, with the dependence showing hints of longitudinal invariance breaking

## Context

This work extends previous two-particle azimuthal correlation measurements (v_n²{2}) at RHIC and LHC by using three-particle mixed-harmonic correlations to access the three-dimensional structure of the initial collision zone. The results are compared to hydrodynamic calculations with different initial-state models and temperature-dependent viscosities, building on theoretical work by Prakash (1993), Arnold (2003), and Csernai (2006) on the temperature dependence of QGP transport properties. These measurements enable further constraints on the temperature-dependent shear viscosity and longitudinal structure of the initial state in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
