# IMSRG with flowing 3 body operators, and approximations thereof

> We explore the impact of retaining three-body operators within the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), as well as various approximations schemes. After studying two toy problems, idential fermions with a contact interaction and the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model, we employ the valence-space formulation of the IMSRG to investigate the even-$A$ carbon isotopes with a chiral two-body potential. We find that retaining only those commutators expressions that scale as $N^7$ provides an excellent approximation of the full three-body treatment.

## Key claim

Retaining only those commutator expressions that scale as $N^7$ provides an excellent approximation of the full three-body treatment in the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), though the approximation is validated primarily through toy models and a single realistic application to carbon isotopes with a chiral two-body potential.

## Method

The authors implemented the Magnus formulation of the IMSRG, evaluating commutators of Fock-space operators truncated at various levels. They studied two toy problems (identical fermions with a contact interaction and the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model) to benchmark approximations, then applied the valence-space IMSRG to even-A carbon isotopes using a chiral two-body potential. The approximations tested include IMSRG(2), IMSRG(2*) (which restores missing fourth-order quadruple diagrams via an auxiliary one-body operator), perturbative triples, and truncation to N^7 scaling terms (IMSRG(3N7)).

## Key numbers

- For the two-fermion contact interaction problem, IMSRG(2) diverges for coupling strength g less than approximately -1.8, while IMSRG(2*) and higher approximations converge.
- The IMSRG(2*) approximation captures the full fourth-order quadruple energy diagrams exactly, while IMSRG(2) undercounts these diagrams by a factor of 1/2.
- The [3,3]_3 commutator topology scales as N^9, making it too expensive for realistic applications.
- The perturbative triples correction involves a single commutator evaluation scaling as N_h^3 N_p^4 (better than the worst IMSRG(2) commutators).

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG method developed by Tsukiyama (2011, 2012), Hergert (2016), and Stroberg (2019) by systematically investigating the impact of retaining three-body operators. It builds on the perturbative analysis of Morris (2016) and Hergert (2016) showing that IMSRG(2) ground state energy is exact through third order in many-body perturbation theory but misses certain fourth-order diagrams. The IMSRG(2*) approximation was previously identified as important by Evangelista and collaborators (2012, 2014) in quantum chemistry. The work enables more reliable uncertainty quantification for ab initio nuclear structure calculations, particularly for open-shell nuclei where three-body effects may be significant.
