# $J/ψ$-kaon cross section in meson exchange model

> We calculate the cross section for the dissociation of $J/ψ$ by kaons within the framework of a meson exchange model including anomalous parity interactions. Off-shell effects at the vertices were handled with QCD sum rule estimates for the running coupling constants. The total $J/ψ$-kaon cross section was found to be $1.0 \sim1.6$ mb for $4.1\leq\sqrt{s}\leq5 \GeV$.

## Key claim

The total J/ψ-kaon dissociation cross section is 1.0–1.6 mb for center-of-mass energies between 4.1 and 5 GeV, but only when QCD sum rule estimates for the coupling constants and form factors are used; without form factors the cross section grows much more strongly with energy and can be an order of magnitude larger.

## Method

The authors computed the cross section for J/ψ dissociation by kaons using a meson-exchange model with SU(4) effective Lagrangians that include both normal parity and anomalous parity (pseudo-scalar-vector-vector) interactions. Off-shell effects at the vertices were handled by inserting momentum-dependent form factors derived from QCD sum rule calculations for the J/ψDD, J/ψD*D, and πD*D vertices, with the remaining couplings obtained from SU(4) relations. The total cross section was obtained by summing the contributions from four classes of diagrams: Kψ → D_sD̅*, Kψ → D_s*D̅, Kψ → D_sD̅, and Kψ → D_s*D̅* (and their charge-conjugate processes).

## Key numbers

- Total J/ψ-kaon cross section: 1.0–1.6 mb for 4.1 ≤ √s ≤ 5 GeV (with QCD sum rule form factors and couplings).
- For comparison, the J/ψ-pion total dissociation cross section using the same form factors and the experimental value g_{πD*D} = 12.6 is 9.0–10.0 mb in the same energy range.
- Coupling constants used (from QCD sum rules plus SU(4) relations): g_{ψDD} = g_{ψD_sD_s} = g_{ψD*D*} = g_{ψD_s*D_s*} = 5.8; g_{KD_sD*} = g_{KDD_s*} = 5.0; g_{ψKD_sD*} = g_{ψKDD_s*} = 28.8; g_{ψD*D} = g_{ψD_s*D_s} = 4.0 GeV^{-1}; g_{KD_s*D*} = 7.0 GeV^{-1}.
- Form factor cut-offs are of order ~4 GeV (from the Gaussian and monopole parameterizations in Eqs. (h1)–(h4)).

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange approach to J/ψ absorption by kaons, which had previously been applied mainly to J/ψ-N, J/ψ-π, and J/ψ-ρ cross sections. It corrects inconsistencies in the earlier kaon calculation by Haglin (2000) and incorporates anomalous parity interactions following Oh, Song, and Lee (2001). The use of QCD sum rule form factors (from Navarra, Nielsen, Bracco, and collaborators, 2000–2003) is a key improvement over earlier calculations that used simple monopole form factors with much smaller cut-offs (~1 GeV). The results enable more realistic modeling of J/ψ suppression in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where both pion and kaon absorption channels must be considered.
