# Relativistic effects and three-body interactions in atomic nuclei

> Based on the leading-order covariant pionless effective field theory, a relativistic nuclear Hamiltonian is derived and solved using the variational Monte Carlo approach for $A\le 4$ nuclei by representing the nuclear many-body wave functions with a symmetry-based artificial neural network. It is found that the relativistic effects rescue the renormalizability of the theory, and overcome the energy collapse problem for $^3$H and $^4$He without promoting a repulsive three-nucleon interaction to leading order as in nonrelativistic calculations. Nevertheless, to exactly reproduce the experimental ground-state energies, a three-nucleon interaction is needed and its interplay with the relativistic effects plays a crucial role. The strongly repulsive relativistic effects suppress the energy contribution given by the three-nucleon interactions, so a strong strength for the three-nucleon interaction could be required to reproduce the experimental energies. These results shed light on a consistent understanding of relativistic effects and three-body interactions in atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects alone can rescue the renormalizability of leading-order pionless effective field theory and prevent the Thomas collapse for \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He without requiring a repulsive three-nucleon interaction at leading order, but a three-nucleon interaction is still needed to exactly reproduce experimental ground-state energies, and its required strength is strongly enhanced by the repulsive relativistic effects.

## Method

The authors derived a relativistic nuclear Hamiltonian from the leading-order covariant pionless effective field theory, starting from a manifestly covariant four-fermion contact Lagrangian and expanding the nucleon field in powers of \(\bm p/m_N\). They solved this Hamiltonian using the variational Monte Carlo approach for \(A \le 4\) nuclei, representing the nuclear many-body wave functions with a symmetry-based artificial neural network that depends only on distances from nucleons to the center of mass and inter-nucleon distances, and optimized the wave function using a newly introduced Gradient-Adaptive algorithm.

## Key numbers

* The low-energy constants \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) (in GeV) for the relativistic Hamiltonian with transfer interactions at cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) are \(C_1 = -0.141\) and \(C_2 = -0.010\); at \(\Lambda = 8\) fm\(^{-1}\) they are \(C_1 = -2.88\) and \(C_2 = -0.086\).
* The nonrelativistic Hamiltonian without three-nucleon interactions produces ground-state energies of \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He that collapse as the cutoff \(\Lambda \rightarrow \infty\), while the relativistic Hamiltonian (with both boost and transfer interactions) produces convergent ground-state energies as the cutoff increases.
* At cutoff \(\Lambda = 6\) fm\(^{-1}\), the dimensionless three-nucleon interaction strength \(D_1 m_N \Lambda^{-2}\) required to reproduce the experimental \(^3\)H ground-state energy is significantly larger for the relativistic Hamiltonian with transfer interactions than for the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian or the relativistic Hamiltonian with only boost interactions.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic leading-order pionless effective field theory calculations for \(A \le 4\) nuclei (Adams 2021) by incorporating relativistic effects derived from a covariant Lagrangian. It addresses the long-standing Thomas collapse problem (Thomas 1935) that in nonrelativistic formulations requires promoting a repulsive three-nucleon interaction to leading order (Bedaque 1999). The results enable future studies of how higher-order relativistic effects and pion degrees of freedom influence the interplay between relativity and three-body interactions, and suggest that similar relativistic effects might rescue renormalizability at next-to-leading order without requiring four-body interactions (Bazak 2019).
