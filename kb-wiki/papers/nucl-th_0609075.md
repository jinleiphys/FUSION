# Effective Theories of Dense and Very Dense Matter

> In these lectures we discuss the uses of effective field theory in studying dense hadronic matter. We focus on two regimes in the phase diagram. At low baryon density nucleons can be described as non-relativistic point particles interacting via short range forces. Interesting effects arise because of the large nucleon-nucleon scattering length. At very large density QCD matter can be described in terms of quarks and gluons. Unusual many body effects arise because of long range gluons fields, and because of the way color and flavor get entangled in the superconducting state.

# Effective Theories of Dense and Very Dense Matter

## Key claim

Effective field theory provides a unified description of dense hadronic matter across vastly different density regimes, from dilute neutron matter to superconducting quark matter, but the treatment is limited to two specific regimes in the phase diagram: low baryon density where nucleons are non-relativistic point particles, and very high density where quarks and gluons become the relevant degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author develops effective field theories for two density regimes of QCD matter. At low density, neutrons are treated as non-relativistic fermions with short-range interactions, using an effective Lagrangian with coupling constants determined by the neutron-neutron scattering amplitude and the effective range expansion. At very high density, the system is described in terms of quarks and gluons, with many-body effects arising from long-range gluon fields and color-flavor entanglement in the superconducting state.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -17 fm (much larger than typical strong interaction length scales)
- Dilute Fermi gas energy density expansion: E = rho * (p_F^2/(2m)) * (3/5 + (2/(3pi))*(p_F*a) + ...), showing the first correction term in the expansion parameter p_F*a
- Effective mass correction: m*/m = 1 - (8/(15*pi^2))*(1 - 7*log(2))*(p_F*a)^2 + ...
- Wave function renormalization: Z^{-1} = 1 - (4/pi^2)*log(2)*(p_F*a)^2 + ...
- Debye screening mass: m_D^2 = e^2 * (p_F*m/(2*pi^2))
- BCS gap for dilute Fermi gas: Delta = (8*E_f/e^2)*exp(-pi/(2*p_F*|a|))
- Neutron gap estimate at nuclear matter density: approximately 1 MeV

## Context

This lecture extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear systems developed by Kaplan (2005), Beane (2000), Bedaque (2002), and Epelbaum (2005), applying it specifically to many-body physics at finite density. The work connects the dilute Fermi gas treatment of Lee, Huang, and Yang (1957) with the BCS theory of superconductivity, and discusses screening effects analogous to those in condensed matter systems. The formalism enables systematic calculations of ground state properties, quasiparticle parameters, and superfluid gaps in neutron matter, with the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov (1961) correction providing next-to-leading order screening of the s-wave gap.
