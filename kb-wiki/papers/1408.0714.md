# Measuring and interpreting charge dependent anisotropic flow

> The Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) [1] predicts a dependence of the positive and negative particle elliptic flow on the event charge asymmetry. Such a dependence has been observed by the STAR Collaboration [2]. However, it is rather difficult to interpret the results of this measurement, as well as to perform cross-experiment comparisons, due to the dependence of the observable on experimental inefficiencies and the kinematic acceptance used to determine the net asymmetry. We propose another observable that is free from these deficiencies. It also provides possibilities for differential measurements clarifying the interpretation of the results. We use this new observable to study the effect of the local charge conservation that can mimic the effect of the CMW in charge dependent flow measurements.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new three-particle correlator observable for studying charge-dependent anisotropic flow that is free from experimental inefficiencies and acceptance biases affecting previous measurements, while noting that local charge conservation (LCC) can mimic the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) signal in such measurements.

## Method

The authors introduce a three-particle cumulant correlator \(\langle \cos[n(\phi_1-\Psi_n)]\,c_3 \rangle\) that measures the anisotropic flow of a particle as a function of the mean charge of another particle in a given momentum window, with subtraction of reducible correlations. They implement a Blast Wave model with azimuthally modulated radial boost and local charge conservation to simulate the effect, assuming balancing charges are produced at the same spatial point and become correlated due to radial flow. The model parameters are selected to roughly correspond to Pb--Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and 30% centrality, with temperature \(T = 100\) MeV and charged particle density \(dN_{ch}/d\eta = 420\).

## Key numbers

- Blast Wave model parameters: \(T = 100\) MeV, \(\rho_{t,max} = 1.1\), \(s_2 = 0.1\), \(s_3 = 0.06\), \(\rho_2 = 0.06\), \(\rho_3 = 0.03\), chosen to roughly correspond to Pb--Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and 30% centrality
- Charged particle density used in model: \(dN_{ch}/d\eta = 420\)
- The correlator becomes negative at \(\Delta\eta \gtrsim 0.6\)--0.7
- The third harmonic correlator amplitude is about three times smaller than the second harmonic in the Blast Wave calculations

## Context

This work extends the STAR Collaboration (2012) observation of charge-dependent elliptic flow as a function of event charge asymmetry, which was interpreted as evidence for the Chiral Magnetic Wave predicted by Burnier et al. (2011). The paper addresses the difficulty in cross-experiment comparisons due to acceptance and efficiency dependencies, and directly responds to the LCC mechanism proposed by Bzdak and Bozek (2013) as a background that can mimic the CMW signal. The new differential observable enables more detailed study of underlying physics mechanisms, including the role of magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions as discussed by Pratt (2012, 2013) and Gursoy et al. (2014).
