# New Class of Solvable and Integrable Many-Body Models

> Integrability conditions for systems of bosons or fermions with seniority conserving hamiltonians are derived. The conditions are shown to be invariant under a large class of transformations of the interaction matrix elements. Previously published integrable models are shown to satisfy these conditions and the existence of a new class of integrable models is demonstrated. The number of free parameters in the interaction in these models equals the number of single particle levels plus 3 . Equations for the energy eigenstates and eigenvalues are derived and the eigenvalues of the complete set of two-body integrals of the motion are given for the new class. Some two-body correlations in these eigenstates are derived from the integrals of the motion.

## Key claim

Integrability conditions for seniority-conserving Hamiltonians are derived and shown to be invariant under a large class of transformations of the interaction matrix elements, with the existence of a new class of integrable models demonstrated where the number of free parameters in the interaction equals the number of single-particle levels plus 3.

## Method

The author derives integrability conditions by requiring that a set of two-body operators \(K\) (with the same structure as the Hamiltonian) commute with the Hamiltonian \(H\). The commutator \([H, K]\) is computed explicitly, yielding equations (2.8) and (2.9) that relate the matrix elements of the interaction terms. A symmetry transformation (2.18) is identified that generates families of solutions from any particular solution of the integrability condition (2.16). For the new class of solutions, the eigenstates are constructed as products of correlated pair creation operators, and the eigenvalues are obtained by solving a set of coupled algebraic equations (3.11) for the pair energies \(\omega_p\).

## Key numbers

- The number of free parameters in the interaction for the new class of models equals the number of single-particle levels plus 3.
- The new particular solution (2.26) has two free parameters \(\gamma_1\) and \(\gamma_2\) in the functions \(u(x,y) = 1 + \gamma_1 (x+y) + \gamma_2 x y\) and \(\gamma(x) = (1 + 2\gamma_1 x + \gamma_2 x^2)^{1/2}\).
- The energy of a seniority-zero eigenstate of \(2N\) particles is given by equation (3.13): \(E = E^{(0)} + 2\sum_{p=1}^N E_p\), where \(E_p\) is defined in (3.12).
- For the case \(f_k = e_k\), the energy simplifies to \(E = E^{(0)} + 2\sum_{p=1}^N \omega_p - gs\sum_{p\neq q} u(\omega_p,\omega_q) - g\sum_{p=1}^N \sum_k \Omega_k u(\omega_p, e_k)\) (equation 3.14).

## Context

This work extends the exact analytical solution of the pairing Hamiltonian with constant interaction matrix elements (Richardson, 1963; Gaudin, 1976) and the demonstration of its integrability (Ref. 7). It also generalizes the class of solvable and integrable models involving pairing forces plus an interaction term quadratic in the particle number operators (Refs. 8, 9). The results enable the construction of new exactly solvable models for finite fermion and boson systems, with applications to metallic grains, nuclear pairing, and condensed matter systems.
