# Quarkonium Formation at High Energy

> The production of quarkonium in heavy ion collisions is studied at RHIC and LHC energies. General arguments indicate that, due to initial production of multiple quark pairs in each central collision, the final population of quarkonium may exhibit significant enhancements over straightforward extrapolation of behavior at SPS energy. Explicit calculations based on both a statistical hadronization picture and a kinetic formation mechanism in a deconfined state verify these general expectations. Such enhancements will alter the nature of how quarkonium yields may be used as a signature of deconfinement.

## Key claim
Due to the initial production of multiple charm quark pairs in each central heavy ion collision at RHIC and LHC, the final J/ψ population may exhibit significant enhancements over straightforward extrapolation of behavior at SPS energy, with the quadratic dependence on total charm production expected to become dominant at high energy.

## Method
The paper presents two distinct models: a statistical hadronization model that distributes initially-produced charm quarks into hadrons according to chemical equilibrium with an oversaturation factor γ_c, and a kinetic formation model where J/ψ form directly in a deconfined medium via the inverse of gluon-induced breakup reactions, described by a rate equation with formation and dissociation terms. Both models are applied to central heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, with the kinetic model also exploring variations in charm quark momentum distributions and centrality dependence.

## Key numbers
- At SPS (√s = 18 GeV), average number of charm pairs per event <N_ccbar> = 0.2, leading to <N_J> = 0.00018 from the quadratic formation mechanism
- At RHIC (√s = 200 GeV), <N_ccbar> = 10, N_ch = 3250, giving <N_J> = 0.034 from quadratic formation compared to N_J^initial = 0.06
- At LHC (√s = 5500 GeV), <N_ccbar> = 200, N_ch = 16500, giving <N_J> = 2.4 from quadratic formation compared to N_J^initial = 1.2
- The kinetic model uses a final deconfinement temperature T_f = 150 MeV to determine hadronization time

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of J/ψ suppression in deconfined matter (Kharzeev, 1996) by demonstrating that the inverse formation process must also occur, potentially overwhelming suppression effects. The statistical hadronization model builds on thermal equilibrium fits for light hadrons (thermalfit) and the oversaturation approach of Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000). The paper enables experimental searches at RHIC and LHC for J/ψ enhancement that grows with collision centrality, opposite to suppression scenarios, and emphasizes the need for simultaneous open charm measurements as a baseline.
