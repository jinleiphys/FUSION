# Kaon Freeze-out Dynamics in $\sqrt s_{NN}$=200 GeV Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> Measurements of three-dimensional correlation functions of like-sign low transverse momentum kaon pairs from Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy $\sqrt s_{NN}$=200 GeV are presented. The extracted kaon source function is narrower than the pion one and does not have the long tail along the pair transverse momentum direction. This indicates a much smaller role of long-lived resonance decays and/or of the emission duration on kaon emission. Three-dimensional Gaussian shape of the kaon source function can be adequately reproduced by Therminator simulations with resonance contributions taken into account. Comparison to pion data at the same energy reveals that the kaon Gaussian radii in the outward and sideward directions scale with the transverse mass $m_T$. In the longitudinal direction, unlike at lower SPS energies, the Gaussian radii do not seem to follow the exact $m_T$ scaling between kaons and pions.

## Key claim
The three-dimensional kaon source function extracted from low-transverse-momentum like-sign kaon pairs in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is largely Gaussian and lacks the long non-Gaussian tail seen in pions, indicating that long-lived resonance decays and/or emission duration contribute much less to kaon emission than to pion emission; however, the Gaussian radii in the longitudinal direction do not follow exact transverse-mass scaling between kaons and pions, contrary to what perfect hydrodynamic models predict and what was observed at lower SPS energies.

## Method
The STAR Collaboration analyzed Bose-Einstein correlations of like-sign charged kaon pairs with low transverse momentum (0.1 < pT < 1.0 GeV/c) from 4.6 million (2004) and 16 million (2007) 0-20% central Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using the Cartesian surface-spherical harmonic decomposition technique. Because limited statistics prevented full source imaging, a three-dimensional Gaussian trial function was fitted simultaneously to the even independent moments up to l=4 of the correlation function, yielding chi^2/ndf values of 1.7 for the shape analysis and 1.1 and 1.3 for the two kT bins (0.2-0.36 and 0.36-0.48 GeV/c) in the kT-dependent analysis. The extracted source function was compared to Therminator blast-wave simulations and hydrokinetic model (HKM) calculations.

## Key numbers
- Kaon source shape analysis used 4.6 million 0-20% central events from 2004 and 16 million 0-20% central events from 2007; kT-dependent analysis used 6.6 million 0-30% central events from 2004.
- Kaon tracks were selected with transverse momentum 0.1 < pT < 1.0 GeV/c; kaon pairs were accepted in two kT bins: 0.2 < kT < 0.36 GeV/c and 0.36 < kT < 0.48 GeV/c.
- The three-dimensional Gaussian fit to the source shape yielded chi^2/ndf = 1.7; the kT-dependent fits gave chi^2/ndf = 1.1 (0.2-0.36 GeV/c bin) and chi^2/ndf = 1.3 (0.36-0.48 GeV/c bin).
- The kaon source function profiles are narrower than the corresponding pion source functions from PHENIX (Afanasiev et al., 2008), especially in the outward direction, and show no significant non-Gaussian tail.

## Context
This work extends previous one-dimensional kaon source imaging by PHENIX (Afanasiev et al., 2009) and three-dimensional pion femtoscopy by PHENIX (Afanasiev et al., 2008) and STAR (Chung, 2011) at the same collision energy, providing the first model-independent three-dimensional kaon source extraction at RHIC. The results contradict the exact transverse-mass scaling predicted by perfect hydrodynamic models (e.g., Buda-Lund model calculations by Csanad and Csorgo, 2008) and instead favor the hydrokinetic model (Karpenko and Sinyukov, 2010), which better describes the longitudinal direction. This enables future studies of freeze-out dynamics using kaons as a cleaner probe than pions, and motivates further investigation of the breaking of transverse-mass scaling in the longitudinal direction at RHIC energies.
