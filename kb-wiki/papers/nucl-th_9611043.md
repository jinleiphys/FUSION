# Low Energy Pion-Nucleus Interactions: Nuclear Deep Inelastic Scattering, Drell-Yan and Missing Pions

> The experimental discovery that the nucleus is approximately transparent to low energy pions is reviewed. The consequences of this for nuclear deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan interactions are discussed. I argue that low energy nucleus data imply that there is little nuclear enhancement of the pion cloud of a nucleon, and try to interpret this in terms of nucleon-nucleon correlations.

## Key claim

Low energy pion-nucleus scattering data (around 50 MeV) show that the nucleus is approximately transparent to pions, implying that there is little nuclear enhancement of the pion cloud of a nucleon, contrary to earlier pion enhancement models that successfully reproduced the EMC effect but failed to account for the Drell-Yan data.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental evidence from four types of low energy pion-nucleus measurements: elastic angular distributions (showing s-p interference minima independent of nuclear target), single charge exchange reactions (where the forward scattering amplitude zero near 50 MeV is maintained across the periodic table), inelastic scattering (showing backward-peaked angular distributions), and total cross sections (proportional to nucleon number A). These results are compared with nuclear deep inelastic scattering (EMC effect) data and Fermilab Experiment 772 Drell-Yan measurements of pA → μ⁺μ⁻ + X cross section ratios on nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- Average π-nucleon cross section at 50 MeV is about 25 mb, but effective cross section in nuclear matter is 15 mb due to Fermi sea blocking, giving a mean free path of 4 fm
- Total cross section ratio σ_tot(πA)/A ≈ σ(πN) holds precisely for π⁺ data, with π⁻ data showing only a slight rise with A attributed to Coulomb effects
- EMC data showed Fe/D structure function ratio of about 1.15 at x = 0.05, dropping approximately linearly with x until x ≈ 0.65
- Drell-Yan experiment E772 measured cross section ratios C/²H, Ca/²H, Fe/²H, W/²H and found very little A dependence, with no evidence for pion excess
- Improved pion enhancement calculation (with g' = 0.7) gave a ratio of 1.1 for Fe/²H, in disagreement with E772 data

## Context

This work extends the experimental discovery from LAMPF and TRIUMF (1970s-1990s) that low energy pions have essentially no multiple scattering in nuclei, contradicting the earlier Ericson-Thomas (1983) pion enhancement model that successfully reproduced early EMC effect data using virtual pion multiple scattering with Landau-Migdal parameter g'. The paper connects these low energy results to the "missing pions" problem highlighted by Bertsch, Frankfurt and Strikman, and suggests that nucleon-nucleon correlations (as in the EELL potential) suppress multiple scattering, explaining why Fermilab Experiment 772 found no nuclear enhancement in Drell-Yan production. The work enables future calculations that simultaneously explain both nuclear deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data.
