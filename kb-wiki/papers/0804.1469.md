# Near threshold ppbar enhancement in the J/psi -> omega ppbar decay

> The near-threshold behavior of the ppbar invariant mass spectrum from the J/psi -> omega ppbar decay reported recently by the BES Collaboration is analyzed. Contrary to the statement made by the BES Collaboration itself our study demonstrates that there is indeed a noticeable enhancement in the ppbar invariant mass spectrum near threshold. Moreover, this enhancement is nicely reproduced by the final state interaction in the relevant 11S0 ppbar partial wave as given by the Julich nucleon--antinucleon model. Therefore, and again contrary to the statement by the BES Collaboration, their new data on J/psi -> omega ppbar decay in fact strongly support the FSI interpretation of the ppbar enhancement, seen also in other decay reactions.

# Near threshold ppbar enhancement in the J/psi -> omega ppbar decay

## Key claim

Contrary to the BES Collaboration’s own statement that no obvious near-threshold ppbar mass enhancement is observed in J/psi -> omega ppbar, the authors demonstrate that a noticeable enhancement is indeed present and is well reproduced by the final state interaction (FSI) in the 11S0 ppbar partial wave as given by the Jülich nucleon–antinucleon model, thus supporting the FSI interpretation.

## Method

The authors extracted the squared reaction amplitude |A|^2 from the BES Collaboration’s measured ppbar invariant mass spectrum for J/psi -> omega ppbar using the differential decay rate formula with phase-space factors. They compared the extracted amplitude to the squared scattering amplitude |T|^2 predicted by the Jülich NbarN model A(OBE) for the 11S0 partial wave, assuming the Watson-Migdal factorization approach where the production amplitude is proportional to the final-state scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The normalization of the phase-space curve was performed in the region M(ppbar) – 2m_p ≈ 100–140 MeV, where the data follow the phase-space distribution.
- The Jülich NbarN model scattering amplitude for the 11S0 partial wave reproduces the experimental |A|^2 dependence on invariant mass “almost perfectly” in the near-threshold region (below M(ppbar) – 2m_p ≈ 100 MeV).
- Beyond M(ppbar) – 2m_p ≈ 100 MeV, the model results disagree with experiment, attributed to contributions from higher partial waves not included in the calculation.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous FSI-based analyses of the ppbar enhancement in J/psi -> gamma ppbar (Haidenbauer, Meißner, Sibirtsev, 2006) and B+ -> K+ ppbar decays (Haidenbauer, Meißner, Sibirtsev, 2007), and contradicts the BES Collaboration’s (2007) claim that the lack of enhancement in J/psi -> omega ppbar disfavors the FSI interpretation. The result enables further tests of the FSI mechanism across different decay channels, noting that quantitative differences are expected due to different quantum numbers, conservation laws, and partial waves involved.
