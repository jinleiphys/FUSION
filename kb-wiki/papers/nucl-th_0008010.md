# Tilted Rotation and Wobbling Motion in Nuclei

> The self-consistent harmonic oscillator model including the three-dimensional cranking term is extended to describe collective excitations in the random phase approximation. It is found that quadrupole collective excitations associated with wobbling motion in rotating nuclei lead to the appearance of two- or three-dimensional rotation.

## Key claim

The self-consistent harmonic oscillator model with three-dimensional cranking, extended to include random phase approximation (RPA) collective excitations, shows that tilted rotation (two- or three-dimensional rotation) emerges naturally beyond a critical rotational frequency as an instability of wobbling motion, but only for nuclei that are near-oblate when non-rotating; for prolate nuclei the tilted solution occurs only at rotational frequencies so large that the global minimum becomes unphysical.

## Method

The authors extended the three-dimensional cranking harmonic oscillator model by including a residual quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and solving the RPA equations of motion for collective excitations. They performed numerical minimization of the total energy in the rotating frame with respect to the three oscillator frequencies (subject to volume conservation) and the tilt angles (θ, φ) of the rotational vector Ω, and derived analytical expressions for the critical rotational frequencies where RPA phonon modes go soft. The analysis focused on the positive signature quadrupole phonon (λ = -2) and the negative signature wobbling phonon (λ = -1) to identify the transitions from principal-axis rotation to oblate rotation and then to tilted rotation.

## Key numbers

- The critical ratio r_cr = (√27 + √2)/(√27 - √2) determines whether a prolate system can become oblate with the rotational axis coinciding with the symmetry axis; this case is not a global energy minimum.
- The first critical rotational frequency Ω_cr^(1) = (ω_⊥/2)√(2/3)(cos((χ+π)/3) - √3 sin((χ+π)/3)) marks the transition from non-collective rotation to non-axial collective rotation (tri-axial to oblate shape transition).
- The second critical rotational frequency Ω_cr^(2) = √((ω_x² + ω_⊥²)/3)(cos(ψ/3) - √3 sin(ψ/3)) marks the onset of stable tilted rotation, where the wobbling phonon frequency goes to zero.
- For tri-axial near-oblate nuclei, the tilted rotation occurs at θ = 75° and θ = 105° (φ = 0°) at the bifurcation point, shifting to different φ values as Ω increases further.

## Context

This work extends the cranking random phase approximation (CRPA) formalism of Marshalek (1977) and Janssen (1977) from one-dimensional rotation to three-dimensional rotation, and builds on the Tilted Axis Cranking Model of Frauendorf (1993). It provides a microscopic mechanism for the formation of high-K isomer states and their decay via quadrupole transitions to ground states, explaining experimental observations in Hf, W, and Os nuclei (A ~ 180) reported by Walker (1990), Crowell (1994), and Purry (1998). The results enable future calculations including pairing vibrations, which the authors note are needed for quantitative comparison with experiment.
