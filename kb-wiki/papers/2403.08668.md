# BHAC-QGP: three-dimensional MHD simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, I. Methods and tests

> We present BHAC-QGP, a new numerical code to simulate the evolution of matter created in heavy-ion collisions in the presence of electromagnetic fields. It is derived from the Black Hole Accretion Code (BHAC), which has been designed to model astrophysical processes in a general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamical description. As the original Black Hole Accretion Code, BHAC-QGP benefits from the use of Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR), which allows us to dynamically adjust the resolution where necessary, and makes use of time-dependent Milne coordinates and the ultrarelativistic equation of state, $P = e/3$. We demonstrate that BHAC-QGP accurately passes a number of systematic and rigorous tests.

## Key claim

BHAC-QGP successfully adapts the general-relativistic astrophysical code BHAC to simulate relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the presence of electromagnetic fields, passing a series of systematic tests, but the paper restricts itself to ideal magnetohydrodynamics (infinite conductivity) and an ultrarelativistic equation of state (P = e/3) with g = 37 degrees of freedom, with the stated intention to later extend to second-order dissipative RMHD.

## Method

The code solves the (3+1)-dimensional equations of ideal relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (RMHD) using second-order finite-volume methods on a grid employing Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR), implemented via the MPI-AMRVAC toolkit. The equations are formulated in time-dependent Milne coordinates (proper time τ and spacetime rapidity η_S) with the metric diag(-1, 1, 1, 1/τ²), and the code uses the ultrarelativistic equation of state P = e/3 for classical massless particles with g = 37 internal degrees of freedom. The conserved variables evolved are D (density in Eulerian frame), S_j (covariant three-momentum density), ε (rescaled energy density), and B^j (magnetic field components), with an additional source term -U/τ accounting for longitudinal expansion.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths of order B ~ 10¹⁸ G are estimated for a typical gold-gold collision at RHIC energies using Liénard-Wiechert potentials
- The equation of state uses g = 37 internal degrees of freedom for classical massless particles
- The speed of sound is c_s² = 1/3 for the ultrarelativistic equation of state
- The extrinsic curvature in Milne coordinates has the only non-zero component K₃₃ = -τ
- The determinant of the spatial metric in Milne coordinates is √g_s = τ
- The fine-structure constant is taken as α ≈ 1/137, giving dimensionless electric charge e ≈ 0.303

## Context

This work extends the astrophysical Black Hole Accretion Code (BHAC) by Porth et al. (2016) to the domain of heavy-ion collisions, joining existing codes such as ECHO-QGP by Del Zanna et al. (2013) and Inghirami et al. (2016) for RMHD simulations, and MUSIC by Schenke et al. (2010) and VHLLE by Karpenko et al. (2013) for viscous hydrodynamics. BHAC-QGP is distinguished by its use of Adaptive Mesh Refinement for heavy-ion collision simulations and is intended as a foundation for future extensions to second-order dissipative RMHD, following the initial work by Dash et al. (2022).
