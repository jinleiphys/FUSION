# Diffractive rho + lepton pair production at an electron-ion collider

> In high energy electron-ion colliders, a new way to probe nucleon structure becomes available through diffractive reactions, where the incident particle produces a very energetic almost forward particle. QCD describes these reactions as due to the exchange of a Pomeron which may be perturbatively described as a dressed two-gluon state, provided a hard scale allows the factorization of the amplitude in terms of two impact factors convoluted with a Pomeron propagator. We consider here a process where such a description allows to access hadronic structure in terms of the generalized parton distributions, namely the electroproduction of a forward $ρ$ meson and a timelike deeply virtual photon, separated by a large rapidity gap. We explore the dependence of the cross section on the kinematic variables and study the dependence on the non-perturbative inputs (generalized parton distributions, distribution amplitude). Our leading order studies show the cross section is mainly sensitive to the GPD model input, but the small size of the cross sections could prohibit straightforward analysis of this process at planned facilities.

## Key claim

The cross sections for diffractive rho + lepton pair production at leading order are quite small, which could make straightforward analysis of this process at planned electron-ion collider facilities very difficult, despite the process being mainly sensitive to the GPD model input.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude using a k_T-factorization approach, writing it as a two-dimensional integral over transverse components of exchanged gluon momenta, with the amplitude expressed as two impact factors convoluted with a Pomeron propagator. The upper impact factor involves the rho meson distribution amplitude (DA), while the lower impact factor involves quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the ERBL region. Cross sections are computed for photoproduction and electroproduction kinematics expected at future electron-ion colliders, comparing three different GPD parametrizations (GK16, VGG, MMS) and two DA parametrizations (asymptotic and holographic).

## Key numbers

- The rho meson decay constant used is f_rho0 = 216 MeV
- Cross sections are shown for t_N = -0.1 GeV^2 and t_N = -0.2 GeV^2
- Minimum Q'^2 considered is 2 GeV^2 to ensure a sufficient hard scale
- The cross section drops by more than 2 decades over 1 decade of Q^2 at fixed Q'^2 = 3 GeV^2
- The imaginary part of the Compton form factors contributes 80% or more of the total cross section strength
- Differences between asymptotic and holographic DA parametrizations are 20% or smaller
- Variations in magnitude between GPD parametrizations are up to a factor of about 2
- The t_rho-dependence parameter K is approximately 6 GeV^{-2} for a rho N final state and approximately 2 GeV^{-2} for diffractive dissociation

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Pire (2019) to study diffractive processes at electron-ion colliders, where the incoming photon in timelike Compton scattering (TCS) is replaced by a Pomeron. The process is related to diffractive electroproduction studies at HERA (Aaron 2009, Chekanov 2007) and is contrasted with the diffractive rho pi N' process studied by Ivanov (2002) and Cosyn (2020), which had a single large scale. The small cross sections are more reminiscent of deep electroproduction of large invariant mass dileptons (Guidal 2002, Belitsky 2002) or diphotons (Pedrak 2020). The work enables future studies of NLO QCD effects using the BFKL framework and improved understanding of the transition region where the Pomeron becomes soft.
