# Computing Jet Transport Coefficients On The Lattice

> The leading jet transport coefficients $\hat{q}$ or $\hat{e}_{2}$ encode transverse or longitudinal momentum broadening of a hard parton traversing a hot medium. Understanding their temperature dependence is key to appreciating the observed suppression of high-transverse momentum probes at RHIC or LHC collision energies. We present a first continuum extrapolated result of $\hat{q}$ computed on pure SU(3) lattices with non-trivial temperature dependence different from the weak-coupling expectation. We discuss our formalism and its challenges and status in view of obtaining $\hat{e}_{2}$ or of unquenching the calculation. We consider a hard quark subject to a single scattering on the plasma. The transport coefficients are factorized in terms of matrix elements given as integrals of non-local gauge-covariant gluon field-strength field-strength correlators. After the analytic continuation to the deep-Euclidean region, the hard scale permits to recast these as a series of local, gauge-invariant operators. The renormalized leading-twist term in this expansion is closely related to static quantities, and is computed on pure SU(3) lattices ($N_τ=4,~6,~8,$ and $10$) for a wide range of temperatures, ranging from 200MeV < T < 1GeV. Our estimate for the unquenched result in $2+1$-flavor QCD has very similar features.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first continuum-extrapolated lattice result for the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ in pure SU(3) gauge theory, finding a non-trivial temperature dependence that differs from the weak-coupling expectation; the result is scheme-dependent because the squared coupling is taken at an infrared scale $\mu_{\text{IR}} = (2\ldots4)\pi T$, and the unquenched estimate for $2+1$-flavor QCD carries a 30% systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compute $\hat{q}(\infty)$ for a hard quark in the limit $q^{-} \to \infty$ by evaluating the leading-twist operator in an operator product expansion of non-local gauge-covariant gluon field-strength correlators, after analytic continuation to the deep-Euclidean region. In pure SU(3) theory, this operator coincides with the renormalized energy-momentum tensor (EMT) in triplet representation, so $\hat{q}/T^3$ is proportional to the entropy density $s/T^3$ times a squared coupling. Lattice simulations use plaquette action with $N_\tau = 4, 6, 8, 10$ and aspect ratio $N_\sigma/N_\tau = 4$, with clover discretization of the field strength and known multiplicative renormalization constant $Z_T^{(3)}$. For the unquenched estimate, the authors use Luscher-Weisz gauge action with (2+1)-flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) at physical strange quark mass and $m_\pi = 161$ MeV, with $N_\tau = 4, 6, 8$, applying tadpole improvement and estimating missing contributions with two complementary arguments.

## Key numbers

- In pure SU(3) theory, $\hat{q}(\infty)/T^3$ is computed for temperatures ranging from 200 MeV < T < 1 GeV, using $N_\tau = 4, 6, 8,$ and $10$ lattices.
- The continuum-extrapolated result for $\hat{q}(\infty)/T^3$ in pure SU(3) rises similarly to $s/T^3$ during the QCD crossover/transition and exhibits a flat temperature dependence above $T \gtrsim 300$ MeV.
- The LO HTL result at $q^- = 100$ GeV (assuming constant $g^2$) is compatible with the lattice result only at $T \gtrsim 2$ GeV.
- For the unquenched estimate in $2+1$-flavor QCD, the systematic uncertainty is 30%, and results are shown at $N_\tau = 6$.
- The relation for $\hat{e}_2(\infty)/T^3$ in pure SU(3) is given by $\frac{1}{4}\hat{q}(\infty)/T^3 + \frac{2\pi^2}{3N_C b_0} T_G^{(1)}/T^4$.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Kumar, Majumder, and collaborators (2020) and Majumder (2012) for computing jet transport coefficients from lattice QCD, providing the first continuum extrapolation of $\hat{q}$ in pure SU(3) theory. The results contradict the logarithmic rise of leading-order hard-thermal-loop (HTL) perturbation theory (Arnold, 2008) at temperatures below about 2 GeV, and instead show a flattening that is quantitatively close to phenomenological determinations from the JETSCAPE framework (Soltz, 2019) and the JET collaboration (2013). The calculation enables future lattice determinations of $\hat{e}_2$ and unquenched results with controlled systematics, and provides non-perturbative input for modeling jet energy loss in heavy-ion collisions.
