# Influence of finite volume and magnetic field effects on the QCD phase diagram

> The Polyakov linear sigma model (PLSM) is used to investigate the respective influence of a finite volume and a magnetic field on the quark-hadron phase boundary in the plane of baryon chemical potential ($μ_{B}$) vs. temperature ($T$) of the QCD phase diagram. The calculated results indicate sizable shifts of the quark-hadron phase boundary to lower values of $(μ_{B}~\text{and}~T)$ for increasing magnetic field strength, and an opposite shift to higher values of $(μ_{B}~\text{and}~T)$ for decreasing system volume. Such shifts could have important implications for extraction of the thermodynamic properties of the QCD phase diagram from heavy ion data.

## Key claim

The Polyakov linear sigma model (PLSM) calculations indicate that the quark-hadron phase boundary shifts to higher values of baryon chemical potential and temperature with decreasing system volume, and to lower values with increasing magnetic field strength, with the caveat that the Polyakov loop effective potential choice does not influence the main conclusions.

## Method

The 2+1 flavor SU(3) Polyakov linear sigma model (PLSM) was used with the Fukushima Polyakov loop effective potential to compute the grand potential in the mean field approximation. Finite volume effects were introduced via a lower momentum cut-off p_min = pi/R, where R is the length of a cubic volume. The quark and anti-quark contributions at non-vanishing magnetic field strength were included using Landau quantization and magnetic catalysis concepts.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters include m_sigma = 800 MeV, c = 4807.84 MeV, lambda_1 = 13.49, m^2 = -(306.26)^2 MeV^2, lambda_2 = 46.48, h_x = (120.73)^3 MeV^3, h_y = (336.41)^3 MeV^3
- Polyakov loop potential parameters: a = 0.664 GeV, b = 0.0075 GeV^3
- Results shown for mu_B = 0 GeV and mu_B = 0.2 GeV
- Phase boundary shifts studied for eB values including 0 GeV^2 and for volumes including R = infinity fm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of magnetic field effects using lattice QCD (lattice QCD studies), the MIT bag model (Fraga and Mizher), the Nambu-Jona Lasinio model (boom1, boom2), and the Linear Sigma Model (rg1, rg2, rg3), as well as PNJL and PLSM models (Gatto, gattopnjl). It contrasts with earlier results that indicated an increase of transition temperature with increasing magnetic field, instead supporting recent lattice QCD calculations showing T_c decreases with increasing magnetic field. The finite volume dependence reported here is similar to that found by Fraga (2011). The work enables future studies of the combined influence of finite volume and magnetic field on the location of the critical end point.
