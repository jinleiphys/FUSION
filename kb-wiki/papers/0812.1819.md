# Recent progress in Hamiltonian light-front QCD

> Hamiltonian light-front quantum field theory constitutes a framework for the non-perturbative solution of invariant masses and correlated parton amplitudes of self-bound systems. By choosing light-front gauge and adopting a basis function representation, we obtain a large, sparse, Hamiltonian matrix for mass eigenstates of gauge theories that is solvable by adapting the ab initio no-core methods of nuclear many-body theory. Full covariance is recovered in the continuum limit, the infinite matrix limit. We outline our approach and discuss the computational challenges.

# Recent progress in Hamiltonian light-front QCD

## Key claim

The authors present a basis-function approach to Hamiltonian light-front QCD (termed "Basis Light Front Quantized" or BLFQ) that adapts *ab initio* no-core methods from nuclear many-body theory, but full covariance is only recovered in the continuum limit, the infinite matrix limit, which has not yet been achieved for QCD.

## Method

The authors adopt a light-front basis consisting of 2-D harmonic oscillator states for transverse modes and discretized momentum modes with antiperiodic boundary conditions for longitudinal modes, working in light-front gauge. They generate a large, sparse Hamiltonian matrix for mass eigenstates and apply renormalization/regularization procedures analogous to those used in the No Core Shell Model (NCSM) and No Core Full Configuration (NCFC) methods for nuclear physics. The approach is first illustrated with cavity-mode QED for massless leptons confined in a transverse harmonic trap, then extended to QCD by implementing SU(3) color degrees of freedom with a global color singlet constraint.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of $^{12}$C calculated with JISP16 NN interaction: extrapolated value agrees with experiment within an assessed uncertainty of about 2 MeV, using matrices up to dimension near 600 million at $N_{max}=8$.
- Largest NCSM/NCFC calculation to date: matrices of dimension 2 billion solved on 30,628 processors with a 3-hour run; $N_{max}=10$ for $^{12}$C produces a matrix near 8 billion.
- For the BLFQ cavity-mode QED demonstration with 3 identical massless fermions: total charge Z=3, total magnetic projection M=0, total spin projection S=1/2, maximum total 2-D oscillator quanta $N_{max}=8-19$, and total longitudinal momentum in dimensionless units K=6 (allowing up to 6 partons).
- The Hamiltonian for the non-interacting QED system is defined as $H = 2M_0 P^-_c = \frac{2M_0\hbar\Omega}{K}\sum{\frac{2n_i+|m_i| +1}{x_i}}$, where $\Lambda^2 = 2M_0\hbar\Omega$ sets the scale.
- For QCD color space: restricting to global color singlets reduces basis multiplicity by a factor of 30-40 compared to constraining all color components to zero color projection.

## Context

This work extends the basis-function approach developed in the No Core Shell Model (NCSM) and No Core Full Configuration (NCFC) methods for nuclear many-body theory (Navratil et al., 2000; Maris et al., 2008) to light-front quantum field theory, bridging particle physics and nuclear physics. The approach builds on earlier discretized momentum basis methods (Brodsky et al., 1998) and transverse lattice approaches (Vary et al., 2002; Grange et al., 2008), and is consistent with developments in AdS/CFT correspondence with QCD (Brodsky, 2004). The authors identify a log divergence in the fermion-boson vertex matrix element that falls only as the inverse square root of the principal quantum number, and indicate that sector-dependent coupling constant renormalization (Karmanov, 2008) is being investigated to manage this divergence.
