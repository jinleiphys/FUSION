# A Consistent Way of Analysis of Hadrons Measured in a Finite Rapidity Interval in Relativistic Heavy-ion Collisions

> A new iteration method is proposed for analyzing both the multiplicities and the transverse momentum spectra measured only within a small rapidity interval and with low momentum limit without any inconsistency or ad hoc assumptions and applied to the hadron data by the ALICE collaboration in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV/A. In order to correctly consider the resonance contribution only to the small rapidity interval, ratios involving only those hadrons whose transverse momentum spectrum is available are considered. In spite of the small number of ratios considered, the fittings of both the ratios the transverse momentum spectra are excellent. Also the calculated ratios involving strange baryons with parameters obtained agree with data surprisingly well.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an iterative method to consistently analyze both hadron multiplicities and transverse momentum spectra measured within a finite rapidity interval, removing the inconsistency that arises when resonance numbers are assumed to be chemically equilibrated at the thermal freeze-out temperature rather than at the higher chemical freeze-out temperature. The method is applied to ALICE data and claims excellent fits without needing strangeness fugacity or special treatment for protons, but the key hedge is that only four ratios (involving pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons) were actually fitted, while the agreement for strange baryon ratios is a postdiction from the fitted parameters.

## Method

The authors use a blast-wave model with two distinct freeze-out temperatures: chemical freeze-out at higher temperature T_ch where hadron species abundances are fixed, and thermal freeze-out at lower temperature T_th where momentum spectra are determined. They iterate between chemical analysis (fitting hadron ratios to extract T_ch, baryon chemical potential mu_B, and strangeness chemical potential mu_S) and thermal analysis (fitting transverse momentum spectra to extract T_th and transverse expansion rapidity rho_0), using the resonance numbers from the chemical stage to correctly compute the resonance decay contribution to the thermal spectra. The iteration continues until parameters converge, and the method is applied to ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV in the rapidity window -0.5 < y < 0.5.

## Key numbers

* Chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch = 155.7 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B = 1.7 MeV, strangeness chemical potential mu_S = 0.14 MeV
* Thermal freeze-out temperature T_th = 112.0 MeV, transverse expansion rapidity rho_0 = 1.3, longitudinal surface rapidity eta_max = 6.8
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom chi^2/N = 0.9 for all ratios including calculated strange baryon ratios, and chi^2/N = 3.8 for the thermal freeze-out fit
* The analysis uses ALICE data from Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV in the rapidity window -0.5 < y < 0.5

## Context

This work extends the two-freeze-out blast-wave model previously developed by Choi and Lee (2011) for RHIC data, now applied to LHC energies using ALICE data. It addresses the inconsistency in standard blast-wave models where resonance numbers are calculated at T_th rather than at the correct chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch, and it differs from the usual statistical model by not assuming Lorentz-boost invariance of the rapidity distribution. The method enables simultaneous fitting of both hadron ratios and transverse momentum spectra within a single framework, and the authors state that calculations for RHIC data using the same method are underway.
