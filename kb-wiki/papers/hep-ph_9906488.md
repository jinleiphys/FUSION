# Flavor Asymmetries in the Proton and Semi-Inclusive Processes

> Semi-inclusive electron scattering provides a powerful tool with which to study the spin and flavor distributions in the proton. Greater kinematic coverage at the proposed Electron-Polarized Ion Collider facility will enable the valence d/u ratio to be determined at large x through pion production. At small x, pion production can be used to extract the d-bar/u-bar ratio, complementing existing semi-inclusive measurements by HERMES, and Drell-Yan data from Fermilab. Asymmetries in heavier quark flavors can also be probed by tagging strange and charm hadrons in the final state.

# Flavor Asymmetries in the Proton and Semi-Inclusive Processes

## Key claim

Semi-inclusive electron scattering can determine the valence d/u ratio at large x and the d-bar/u-bar ratio at small x through pion production, but the extraction of d/u from neutron targets requires careful treatment of nuclear effects in the deuteron that become dramatic beyond x ~ 0.7, and the d/u ratio obtained without such corrections could give misleading results.

## Method

The paper analyzes semi-inclusive pion production in the current fragmentation region using the quark-parton model, where cross sections factorize into quark distribution functions and fragmentation functions. For valence quarks, the ratio of pi- to pi+ yields on protons alone is constructed to avoid nuclear corrections, with the leading fragmentation function dominating as z approaches 1. For sea quarks, isovector combinations of pion yields from proton and neutron targets are used to extract d-bar/u-bar, and the results are compared with Drell-Yan data from Fermilab E866. For strange and charm quarks, chiral cloud model predictions for s-s-bar and c-c-bar asymmetries are compared with neutrino deep-inelastic data and charm structure function measurements.

## Key numbers

- For the Pauli blocking contribution to d-bar - u-bar, a fit gives alpha approximately 14 and normalization tau^Pauli approximately 7%, with tau^pi approximately 0.05 from pions, giving a combined tau = tau^pi + tau^Pauli approximately 0.12, compared with the experimental result 0.100 +/- 0.018 from E866.
- For the pion cloud model of Delta++ production, the form factor cut-off is Lambda = 0.8 GeV, giving an average number density of the pi-Delta component <n>_piDelta approximately 2%.
- For the kaon cloud model of strange form factors, the cut-off mass is Lambda = 1 GeV for the KNY vertex functions.
- For intrinsic charm, EMC data at large x appear to rule out intrinsic charm at the 1% level, while non-perturbative contributions of order 0.4% are still allowed.
- For the target fragmentation asymmetry calculations, typical kinematics are x = 0.075, Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, and sqrt(s) approximately 8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the program of flavor decomposition in the proton beyond inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, which sums over all charge-squared-weighted flavors. It builds on semi-inclusive measurements by HERMES (1998) and Drell-Yan data from Fermilab E866 (1998), and addresses the problem of nuclear corrections in deuteron targets identified by Melnitchouk and Thomas (1996). The proposed Electron-Polarized Ion Collider (EPIC) would enable measurements in both current and target fragmentation regions, allowing tests of the pion cloud model originally proposed by Thomas (1983) and the perturbative QCD predictions of Farrar and Jackson (1975) for the d/u ratio at large x. The target fragmentation analysis offers a new way to distinguish between meson-exchange and diquark fragmentation mechanisms for baryon production.
