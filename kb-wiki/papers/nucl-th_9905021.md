# Generalizing Phi-measure of event-by-event fluctuations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The Phi-measure of event-by-event fluctuations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions corresponds to the second moment of the fluctuating quantity distribution of interest. It is shown that the measure based on the third moment preserves the properties of Phi but those related to the higher moments do not. In particular, only the second and third moment measures are intensive as thermodynamic quantities. The Phi_2- and Phi_3-measure of p_T-fluctuations are computed for the hadron gas in equilibrium and the results are analyzed in context of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the generalization of the Phi-measure to higher moments preserves the key property of independence from source-number distribution only for the second and third moments; measures based on the fourth and higher moments do not retain this property and are not intensive thermodynamic quantities.

## Method

The author analytically derives the dependence of the generalized Phi_n measures on the number of independent sources (e.g., nucleon-nucleon collisions) by computing the moments of the event variable Z for a superposition of k identical sources. The formalism is then applied to compute Phi_2 and Phi_3 for energy and transverse-momentum fluctuations in an ideal quantum gas using the grand canonical partition function, with explicit formulas derived for bosons and fermions. The calculations are extended to include pions from resonance decays by treating the fireball as a mixture of quantum pions and classical pions from decays of rho(770) and omega(782) mesons.

## Key numbers

- For massless particles with zero chemical potential: Phi_2 ≈ 0.40 T for bosons and Phi_2 ≈ -0.07 T for fermions; Phi_3 ≈ -∞ (logarithmically divergent) for bosons and Phi_3 ≈ 0.11 T for fermions.
- For the ideal pion gas at T = 180 MeV and mu = 0, Phi_2 = 15 MeV without resonances and Phi_2 = 8.7 MeV with resonances included.
- For the ideal pion gas at T = 120 MeV and mu = 0, Phi_2 = 6.5 MeV without resonances and Phi_2 = 5.6 MeV with resonances included.
- For the ideal pion gas at T = 120 MeV and mu = 0, Phi_3 = -12.3 MeV with resonances included.
- Experimental value from NA49 for central Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon: Phi_2 = 4.6 ± 1.5 MeV after two-track resolution correction (App99); earlier result was Phi_2 = 0.7 ± 0.5 MeV (Rol98).

## Context

This work extends the Phi-measure formalism introduced by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992) for event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which was designed to disentangle dynamical fluctuations from trivial geometrical ones due to impact parameter variation. The paper responds to a suggestion by Belkacem et al. (1999) to use higher-moment measures, showing that only the third-moment measure preserves the useful properties of the original Phi_2. The calculations of Phi_2 and Phi_3 for the equilibrium hadron gas build on earlier work by Mrowczynski (1998) and are compared to NA49 experimental data (Rol98, App99). The results support the claim that short-range correlations from Bose-Einstein statistics of pions dominate the observed fluctuations, and the paper predicts a specific negative value of Phi_3 that could be tested experimentally to confirm this interpretation.
