# The structural evolution in transitional nuclei of mass 80 $\leq$ A $\leq$ 132

> In this theoretical study, we report an investigation on the behavior of two neutron separation energy, differential variation of the separation energy and the abnormality in nuclear charge radius along the isotopic and isotonic chains of transition nuclei. We have used relativistic mean field formalism with NL3 and NL3$^*$ forces for this present analysis. The study refers to {\it even-even} nuclei such as Zr, Mo, Ru and Pd with $N$ = 40$-$ 86, where a rich collective phenomena such as proton radioactivity, cluster or nucleus radioactivity, exotic shapes, {\it Island of Inversion} and etc. are observed. These non-monotonic aspects over the isotopic chain are mainly correlated with the structural properties like shell/sub-shell closures, shape transition, clustering and magicity etc. In addition to these, we have shown the internal configuration of these nuclei to get a further insight into the reason for these discrepancies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with NL3 and NL3* force parameters can reproduce the observed non-monotonic behavior of two-neutron separation energies, charge radii, and deformation parameters across isotopic chains of Zr, Mo, Ru, and Pd, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that shape coexistence and near-degenerate minima mean the ground state assignment can change with small variations in input parameters like pairing strength.

## Method

The author performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using NL3 and NL3* effective interactions, with BCS pairing treated via constant gap approximations. The Dirac equations were solved by expanding spinors and boson fields in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 18 fermion shells and 20 boson shells, and the potential energy surface was mapped by constrained RMF calculations as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂. Binding energies, two-neutron separation energies S₂n, differential variation dS₂n, charge radii rch, and single-particle levels were computed for even-even isotopes of Zr (N=42-86), Mo, Ru, and Pd.

## Key numbers

- Mean deviation of calculated binding energy from experimental data over the isotopic chains is approximately 0.01 MeV.
- Mean deviation of calculated charge radius rch from experimental data over the isotopic chains is approximately 0.004 fm.
- For ⁸²Zr, two near-degenerate solutions exist: β₂ = -0.197 with binding energy 691.3 MeV and β₂ = 0.488 with binding energy 691.0 MeV (NL3 force).
- For ⁹⁰Zr, the ground state β₂ = 0.001 with binding energy 783.9 MeV (NL3) compared to experimental binding energy 783.9 MeV and experimental rch = 4.2696 fm.
- For ¹¹⁰Zr, the ground state β₂ = 0.445 with binding energy 897.2 MeV (NL3).
- For ¹²⁰Zr, the ground state β₂ = 0.003 with binding energy 929.6 MeV (NL3).
- The relative binding energy difference (ΔE)B = EB(NF=18) - EB(NF=10-22) is ≤ 0.02% on binding energy and ≤ 0.01% on nuclear radii for NF from 10 to 14, reducing to ≤ 0.002% on binding energy and ≤ 0.001% on nuclear radii for NF from 14 to 22.

## Context

This work extends the application of RMF theory with NL3 and NL3* forces to transitional nuclei in the mass region A = 82-132, following earlier RMF calculations by Lalazissis et al. (1999) and others. The study focuses on the neutron-rich Zr, Mo, Ru, and Pd isotopes where shape coexistence and the island of inversion have been observed experimentally. The results enable comparison with experimental data from Audi et al. (2012) and Angeli et al. (2013), and provide a theoretical basis for understanding shell closures at N=50 and N=82, shape transitions, and the onset of deformation in this mass region relevant to the astrophysical r-process.
