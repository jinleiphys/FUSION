# Complex Langevin dynamics in the SU(3) spin model at nonzero chemical potential revisited

> The three-dimensional SU(3) spin model is an effective Polyakov loop model for QCD at nonzero temperature and density. It suffers from a sign problem at nonzero chemical potential. We revisit this model using complex Langevin dynamics and assess in particular the justification of this approach, using analyticity at small mu^2 and the criteria for correctness developed recently. Finite-stepsize effects are discussed in some detail and a higher-order algorithm is employed to eliminate leading stepsize corrections. Our results strongly indicate that complex Langevin dynamics is reliable in this theory in both phases, including the critical region. This is in sharp contrast to the case of the XY model, where correct results were obtained in only part of the phase diagram.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that complex Langevin dynamics yields reliable results for the SU(3) spin model at nonzero chemical potential in both the disordered and ordered phases, including the critical region, based on tests using analyticity in \(\mu^2\) and recently developed criteria for correctness. This contrasts with the XY model, where correct results were obtained in only part of the phase diagram.

## Method
The authors discretize the SU(3) spin model on a three-dimensional lattice by diagonalizing the SU(3) matrices in terms of two angles per site, then complexify those angles to handle the complex action. They solve the complex Langevin equations using both a standard lowest-order discretization and a higher-order Runge–Kutta-type algorithm that eliminates leading stepsize corrections, and assess reliability by checking analyticity in \(\mu^2\) (comparing real-\(\mu\) results with imaginary-\(\mu\) results) and by evaluating the expectation value of the Langevin operator \(\langle \tilde{L} O \rangle\) on holomorphic observables, which should vanish for a correct process.

## Key numbers
- Fermion coupling \(h = 0.02\) is used throughout; the critical \(\beta\) at \(\mu=0\) is around 0.1324 on a \(10^3\) lattice.
- At \(\beta=0.125\) and \(h=0.02\), the coefficients from a Taylor expansion around \(\mu=0\) are \(c_1 = 0.1446(21)\) and \(c_2 = -3.534(72)\), used to verify the linear dependence of density and Polyakov-loop expectations at small \(\mu\).
- Simulations use Langevin trajectories of length 10,000 (disordered phase) and 5,000 (ordered phase), with stepsizes from \(\eps = 0.001\) down to \(0.00005\).
- The histogram of the imaginary parts of the angles shows an exponential drop \(P(\phi^I) \sim e^{-b|\phi^I|}\) with \(b = 35, 45\), much faster than in the U(1) model where \(b \sim 2\) and complex Langevin failed.

## Context
This work revisits the SU(3) spin model originally studied with complex Langevin dynamics by Karsch and Wyld (1988) and by Bilic, Gausterer, and Sanielevici (1990), and extends it by applying recently developed justification criteria from Aarts et al. (2009–2011) to assess reliability. The model is an effective Polyakov-loop theory for QCD at nonzero temperature and density, and the results contrast with the XY model studied by Aarts et al. (2010), where complex Langevin gave correct results only in part of the phase diagram. The paper also notes that Gattringer (2011) reformulated the same model in terms of fluxes to eliminate the sign problem, providing an independent check.
