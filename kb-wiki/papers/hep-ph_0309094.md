# Resummed QCD Power Corrections to Nuclear Shadowing

> We calculate and resum a perturbative expansion of nuclear enhanced power corrections to the structure functions measured in deeply inelastic scattering of leptons on a nuclear target. Our results for the Bjorken $x$-, $Q^2$- and $A$-dependence of nuclear shadowing in $F_2^A(x,Q^2)$ and the nuclear modifications to $F_L^A(x,Q^2)$, obtained in terms of the QCD factorization approach, are consistent with the existing data. We demonstrate that the low-$Q^2$ behavior of these data and the measured large longitudinal structure function point to a critical role for the power corrections when compared to other theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear shadowing in deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) can be explained entirely by resummed QCD power corrections (higher twist effects) without invoking any leading-twist shadowing in the nuclear parton distributions, provided the characteristic scale ξ² is in the range 0.09–0.12 GeV². This gives an upper limit for the power correction scale in cold nuclear matter and implies that less leading-twist shadowing (and antishadowing) is needed than currently assumed.

## Method

The authors calculate and resum nuclear-enhanced power corrections to the DIS structure functions F₂^A and F_L^A using collinear QCD factorization. They model multiple final-state scattering of the struck quark in a nucleus by summing an infinite series of twist-2+2n contributions, where each pair of gluon interactions is replaced by an effective scalar vertex. The multi-field matrix elements are evaluated in a constant-density nuclear model, and the resummation yields a simple shift of the Bjorken x variable by Δx = x ξ²(A^{1/3}–1)/Q².

## Key numbers

* Characteristic scale of power corrections: ξ² = 0.09–0.12 GeV², extracted from comparison to CERN-NA37 and FNAL-E665 data on F₂(A)/F₂(D) for x < 0.1.
* Nucleon mass used as lower cutoff: Q² = m_N² = (0.94 GeV)² is imposed for virtualities below which the approach may not be valid.
* Nuclear density parameters: r₀ ≈ 1.2 fm, m_N = 0.94 GeV, with normalization N_p = 3/(8π r₀³ m_N).
* Comparison to prior analyses: ξ² ~ 0.04 GeV² from Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening (Guo, 1998) and ξ² ~ 0.2 GeV² from dijet photoproduction.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Qiu (2002) on nuclear-enhanced power corrections and provides a systematic resummation to all twists, in contrast to the leading-twist EKS98 parametrization (Eskola, 1998) which attributes shadowing to modified input parton distributions at μ₀ = 1.5 GeV. The results are also compared to the Gribov-Regge model by Armesto (2003). The formalism enables predictions for RHIC, the future Electron Ion Collider, and the LHC, and is further applied to other QCD processes in Qiu (2004).
