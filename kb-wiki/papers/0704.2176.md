# Theoretical treatments of fusion processes in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We review the theoretical methods to evaluate fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. We piont out that in such collisions the coupling to the breakup channel leads to the appearance of different fusion processes. The extentsion of the coupled-channel method to coupling with the continuum is the most successful treatment for these collisions. However, evaluating separate cross section for each fusion process remains a very hard task.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical methods for fusion of weakly bound nuclei, arguing that continuum-continuum couplings play an essential role in reducing the complete fusion cross section, but that evaluating separate cross sections for each fusion process (complete vs. incomplete) remains a very hard task.

## Method

The authors survey the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method, which replaces the continuous spectrum of the projectile with a finite number of discrete states built from wave packets in momentum bins. They also describe alternative approaches including classical trajectory methods, time-dependent wave-packet evolution, and semiclassical calculations, comparing their ability to separate complete fusion (CF) from incomplete fusion (ICF) and sequential capture processes.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb collision without continuum-continuum couplings, the CF cross section is strongly enhanced at low energies and slightly hindered at high energies, with the transition energy \(E_{tr} > V_B\) (the Coulomb barrier).
- When continuum-continuum couplings are included for the same system, both CF and TF cross sections are much smaller, and \(E_{tr}\) falls below the Coulomb barrier.
- For \(^{6,7}\)Li projectiles on \(^{209}\)Bi and \(^{59}\)Co targets, the presence of the breakup channel enhances the total fusion cross section around the Coulomb barrier, with a more marked effect for the \(^{209}\)Bi target than for \(^{59}\)Co.
- For \(^{6}\)He on \(^{208}\)Pb, the CDCC cross section including breakup couplings is consistent with data for \(^{6}\)He + \(^{209}\)Bi, and the static halo effect (which would make \(^{6}\)He fusion much larger than \(^{6}\)Li fusion) is cancelled by repulsive polarization potentials.

## Context

This review extends earlier coupled-channel treatments of fusion for stable nuclei (Dasso and Vitturi, 1994; Hagino et al., 2004) by focusing on the additional complexity of continuum coupling in weakly bound systems. The CDCC method builds on prior work by Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) and Keeley, Rusek and collaborators (2003), while the authors note that classical trajectory methods (Hagino et al., 2004) and time-dependent approaches (Yabana et al., 1992) offer alternative routes to separate complete and incomplete fusion. The paper identifies the need for a full three-body tunneling model as the next major challenge.
