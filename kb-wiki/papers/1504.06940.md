# Nonperturbative numerical calculation of the fine and hyperfine structure of muonic hydrogen by Breit potential including the effects from the proton size

> By solving the two-body Schordinger equation in a very high precise nonperturbative numerical (NPnum) way, we reexamine the contributions of fine, hyperfine structure splittings of muonic hydrogen based on the Breit potential. The comparison of our results with those by the first order perturbative theory ($^{1st}$PT) in the literature shows, when the structure of proton is considered, the differences between the results by the $^{1st}$PT and NPnum methods are small for the fine and hyperfine splitting of $2P$ state, while are about $-0.009$ meV and $0.08$ meV for the $F=1$ and total hyperfine splitting of $2S$ state of muonic hydrogen, respectively. These differences are larger than the current experimental precision and would be significant to be considered in the theoretical calculation.

## Key claim

When the proton's finite size is included via dipole form factors in the Breit potential, the nonperturbative numerical (NPnum) calculation yields differences from first-order perturbative theory (1stPT) of about -0.009 meV for the F=1 hyperfine splitting and 0.08 meV for the total hyperfine splitting of the 2S state in muonic hydrogen, which exceed current experimental precision and are significant for theoretical calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Schrödinger equation using a shooting method implemented in Mathematica with 200-digit precision and a PrecisionGoal of 15 for NDSolve, simulating boundary conditions at r=10^{-50} fm and r=10^6 fm. The Breit potential was constructed from the one-photon exchange diagram using the dipole form of the proton electromagnetic form factors G_E(Q^2)=G_M(Q^2)/μ_p=1/(1+Q^2/m^2)^2, with the proton size parameter m related to the charge radius as r_p^2=12/m^2. Energy shifts for the 2S and 2P states were computed nonperturbatively and compared with first-order perturbative results for several proton radii.

## Key numbers

- For the 2S state hyperfine splitting ΔE_HFS^{2S}(F=1) at r_p=0.84184 fm: 1stPT gives 5.67884 meV, NPnum gives 5.66918 meV (difference ~ -0.009 meV)
- For the total 2S state hyperfine splitting ΔE_HFS^{2S} at r_p=0.84184 fm: 1stPT gives 22.71535 meV, NPnum gives 22.79324 meV (difference ~ 0.08 meV)
- For the 2P state fine structure ΔE_FS^{2P} at r_p=0.84184 fm: 1stPT gives 8.34672 meV, NPnum gives 8.34702 meV (difference ~ 3×10^{-4} meV)
- For the 2P_{3/2} hyperfine splitting ΔE_HFS^{2P_{3/2}} at r_p=0.84184 fm: 1stPT gives 3.39121 meV, NPnum gives 3.39123 meV (difference ~ 2×10^{-5} meV)
- The ratio ΔE_HFS^{2S}(F=0)/ΔE_HFS^{2S}(F=1) from NPnum is about 3.0206 at r_p=0.84184 fm, not exactly 3 as predicted by 1stPT
- The fitted polynomial for ΔE_HFS^{2S}(F=1, NPnum) = 5.68008 - 0.00261343 r_p - 0.0122699 r_p^2 meV in the region r_p ∈ [0.82, 0.90] fm
- For the hydrogen (ep) system at r_p=0.84184 fm: 1S hyperfine splitting from 1stPT is 1420439.6 kHz, from NPnum is 1425700.8 kHz

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative numerical approach previously applied to muonic hydrogen by Indelicato (2013) using the multiconfiguration Dirac-Fock method and by Carroll (2011) using a shooting method with quad-precision Fortran. The authors provide a higher-precision calculation using Mathematica that reveals significant deviations from first-order perturbative results for the 2S hyperfine splitting, which are relevant to the proton radius puzzle arising from the discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement (Pohl et al., 2010, Nature) giving r_p=0.84184 fm and the CODATA-2010 value of r_p≈0.878 fm. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for the 2S-2P transition frequencies and suggest that the relation ΔE_HFS^{2S}(F=1)=¼ΔE_HFS^{2S} should not be used, potentially affecting the extracted proton radius by about 3%.
