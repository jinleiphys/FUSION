# Heavy quark mass near the phase transition

> Assuming that the number densities of heavy flavor in hadron gas and in QGP are same at $T_c$, we obtain the effective mass of heavy quark at $T_c$ from the comparison with the hadron resonance gas model which well describes particle yield in heavy-ion collisions. We find that charm quark mass at vanishing baryon chemical potential is around 1.8 GeV which is much heavier than QCD bare mass and close to $D$ meson mass. The mass slightly increases with increasing baryon chemical potential and then decreases. On the other hand, anticharm quark mass constantly decreases with increasing baryon chemical potential. Bottom quark mass has a similar pattern. Extending the hadron resonance gas model to a bit higher temperature beyond $T_c$, the effective masses of charm and bottom quarks decrease with increasing temperature.

## Key claim
Assuming that the number densities of heavy flavor in hadron gas and in quark-gluon plasma are equal at the critical temperature \(T_c\), the effective mass of charm quark at vanishing baryon chemical potential is around 1.8 GeV, which is much heavier than the QCD bare mass and close to the \(D\) meson mass; this result depends on the validity of the hadron resonance gas model and the crossover nature of the phase transition.

## Method
The authors matched the number density of charm (or bottom) quarks in the QGP phase, described by a Boltzmann distribution with an effective mass, to the number density of all charm (or bottom) hadrons in the hadron gas phase, computed using the hadron resonance gas model including all known hadron states up to high excitations. The matching was performed at the critical temperature \(T_c(\mu_B)\) parameterized as a function of baryon chemical potential, and the effective masses were extracted by numerically solving the equality equations using Newton's method. The same procedure was repeated for anticharm and for bottom and antibottom quarks, both in grand canonical and canonical ensembles.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark effective mass at \(\mu_B = 0\): approximately 1.82 GeV.
- Bottom quark effective mass at \(\mu_B = 0\): approximately 5.17 GeV.
- Critical temperature at zero baryon chemical potential: \(T_c(0) = 0.158\) GeV.
- Parameter \(\alpha\) in the critical temperature formula \(T_c(\mu_B) = T_c(0) \sqrt{1 - \alpha \mu_B^2}\): \(\alpha = 0.974\), valid up to \(\mu_B = 0.5\) GeV.
- Charm fugacity near \(T_c\) in hadron gas: around 30, based on \(dN_{c\bar{c}}/dy = 12.95\) and volume 4997 fm\(^3\).
- Asymmetric charm chemical potential for vanishing net charm density saturates at large \(\mu_B\) where the mass difference between charm and anticharm quarks is about 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the hadron resonance gas model (Cleymans 1999, Becattini 2000, Braun-Munzinger 2001) and the dynamical quasiparticle model (Cassing 2007, Moreau 2019) to extract heavy quark masses near the phase transition, a quantity not accessible from the equation of state provided by lattice QCD (Borsanyi 2010, 2012). The results are consistent with heavy quark masses derived from lattice heavy quark potential (Kaczmarek 2003) when combining 15-20% free energy and 80-85% internal energy, as used in quarkonium studies (Andronic 2024). The extracted masses enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium binding energies and dissociation in heavy-ion collisions across a range of baryon chemical potentials.
