# Precision calculation of $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be for solar physics

> We calculate the cross section for radiative capture $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). At this order of perturbation, momentum dependent two-body currents make their first appearance. We provide a model-independent construction of these currents from gauge and Galilean invariance, where the general framework for constructing higher-order two-body currents in low-energy effective field theories becomes evident. The $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be astrophysical S-factor $S_{34}(0)= 0.564^{+0.17}_{-0.015}$ keV b is obtained from a Bayesian analysis at NNLO, with an additional nominal theoretical uncertainty $\pm0.017$ keV b of 3%.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the $^3$He$(α,γ)^7$Be astrophysical S-factor at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in halo effective field theory, obtaining $S_{34}(0)=0.564^{+0.17}_{-0.015}$ keV b from a Bayesian analysis, with an additional nominal theoretical uncertainty of $\pm0.017$ keV b (3%). The NNLO result is nearly identical to the NLO result, confirming that the NLO calculation already describes the capture data accurately in the fitted region.

## Method

The authors treat $^3$He and $α$ as point-like particles in halo EFT, with the ground and excited states of $^7$Be as $p$-wave bound states. They construct model-independent two-body currents from gauge and Galilean invariance at NNLO, where momentum-dependent two-body currents first appear. The EFT parameters are determined through Bayesian fits to capture data (S-factor and branching ratio) from six experimental datasets (ATOMKI, ERNA, LUNA, Madrid, Seattle, Weizmann) with energies up to 2000 keV, combined with elastic scattering phase shift data from Boykin et al. (1972) up to 3000 keV.

## Key numbers

- $S_{34}(0) = 0.564^{+0.17}_{-0.015}$ keV b from Bayesian analysis at NNLO, with additional nominal theoretical uncertainty $\pm0.017$ keV b (3%)
- Binding energies: ground state $B_0 = 1.5866$ MeV, excited state $B_1 = 1.1575$ MeV
- Masses: $m_\psi = 2808.4$ MeV ($^3$He), $m_\phi = 3727.4$ MeV ($α$)
- Gamow energy peak at approximately 23 keV
- Relative evidence for NNLO vs NLO fits: $\ln[P_\text{NNLO}(D)/P_\text{NLO}(D)] = -2.52 \pm 0.52$, slightly favoring NLO
- Analytic fit for $E \leq 1600$ keV: $S_{34}(E) = [0.564 \pm 0.006\text{ keV b}] \times \exp[-0.544 \pm 0.167 \times E/\text{keV}] \times [1 - 0.528 \pm 0.464 \times E^2/\text{keV}^2 + 0.696 \pm 0.481 \times E^3/\text{keV}^3 - 0.176 \pm 0.172 \times E^4/\text{keV}^4]$

## Context

This work extends the NLO halo EFT calculation of Higa et al. (2016) and Premarathna et al. (2019) to NNLO, providing a self-consistent theoretical uncertainty estimate of 3% rather than the conventional 10% estimate used in the Solar Fusion III (SF3) recommendation of $S_{34}(0) = 0.561 \pm 0.018 \pm 0.022$ keV b (Acharya et al., 2024). The improved precision in $S_{34}$ is needed to reduce uncertainties in predicted solar neutrino fluxes from $^7$Be and $^8$B decays for next-generation neutrino detectors including Hyper-K, SNO+, JUNO, and DUNE.
