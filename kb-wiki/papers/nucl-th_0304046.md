# Higher-order calculations of electron-deuteron scattering in nuclear effective theory

> Motivated by recent advances in the application of effective field theory techniques to light nuclei we revisit the problem of electron-deuteron scattering in these approaches. By sidestepping problems with the description of electron-nucleon scattering data in effective field theories, we show that the effective theory expansion for deuteron physics converges well over a wide range of momentum transfers. The resultant description of the physics of the two-nucleon system is good up to virtual photon momenta of order 700 MeV.

## Key claim

By applying the chiral expansion to the ratio of deuteron and nucleon form factors rather than to the deuteron form factors directly, the effective theory description of deuteron structure converges well and reproduces experimental data up to momentum transfers of order 700 MeV, whereas a strict chiral expansion of the deuteron current alone fails above Q ~ 300 MeV due to difficulties in describing isoscalar nucleon structure.

## Method

The deuteron electromagnetic current J_mu was expanded in operators ordered by chiral dimension up to O(eP^3) for the charge and quadrupole form factors and O(eP^2) for the magnetic form factor, using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The matrix elements were evaluated with several deuteron wave functions: a strict chiral perturbation theory NLO wave function (Lambda=600 MeV and 540 MeV), an N^2LO wave function from Epelbaum and Meissner (2001), a simple one-pion exchange potential plus short-distance wave function from Phillips and Cohen (1999), and the Nijm93 meson-theoretic potential wave function. Nucleon structure effects were included via factorization, multiplying the computed ratios G_C/G_E^N and G_Q/G_E^N by the experimental parameterization of G_E^N from Mergell et al. (1995).

## Key numbers

- The effective theory description of G_C and G_Q agrees with data up to virtual photon momenta of order 700 MeV
- The deuteron charge radius r_d computed at NNLO with the NLO chiPT wave function (Lambda=600 MeV) is 1.987 fm, compared to the experimental value of 1.971(5) fm
- The deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d computed at NNLO with the same wave function is 0.2764 fm^2, compared to the experimental value of 0.2859(3) fm^2
- The deuteron magnetic moment mu_d computed at NLO with the same wave function is 0.8531 nuclear magnetons, compared to the experimental value of 0.857406(1) n.m.
- The isoscalar charge radius of the nucleon used in the calculation is (0.777 fm)^2
- The deuteron quadrupole moment varies by about 5% between models with different short-distance physics

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation of Walzl and Meissner (2001), which computed all three deuteron form factors only up to O(eP^2) and found poor agreement with data above Q^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2. By factorizing the nucleon structure effects, the present work shows that the failure lies in chiral perturbation theory's description of isoscalar nucleon structure rather than deuteron structure. The results enable extraction of nucleon structure information from deuteron data and demonstrate that the charge and quadrupole form factors are relatively insensitive to short-distance physics, while the magnetic form factor shows greater sensitivity as anticipated by the effective theory counting.
