# Strange quark matter with effective quark masses

> The properties of strange quark matter at zero temperature are investigated including medium effects. The quarks are considered as quasiparticles which acquire an effective mass generated by the interaction with the other quarks of the dense system. Within this approach we find that these medium effects reduce the binding energy of strange quark matter with respect to $^{56}Fe$.

# Strange quark matter with effective quark masses

## Key claim

The authors find that including medium effects via effective quark masses reduces the binding energy of strange quark matter relative to $^{56}Fe$, suggesting that strange quark matter is **not absolutely stable** when these effects are taken into account, although it may remain metastable.

## Method

The quarks are treated as quasiparticles with effective masses derived from the hard dense loop (HDL) approximation to the quark self energy at zero temperature and finite chemical potential. The equation of state is then computed using ideal Fermi gas formulas for these quasiparticles, with a $\mu$-dependent bag function $B^*$ introduced to maintain thermodynamic self-consistency. The energy per baryon of strange quark matter is minimized over the strangeness fraction $f_s$ for various values of the coupling constant $g$.

## Key numbers

- For bag constant $B_0^{1/4}=145$ MeV and current strange quark mass $m_s=150$ MeV, the absolute minimum energy per baryon increases from 874 MeV (at $g=0$) to 943 MeV (at $g=3$).
- The baryon density decreases with increasing coupling constant $g$, while the energy density stays approximately constant.
- The strangeness fraction $f_s$ decreases with increasing $g$.

## Context

This work extends the Fermi gas model for strange quark matter, previously treated as a non-interacting gas with a bag constant (Witten 1984; Farhi and Jaffe 1984), by incorporating medium effects through effective quark masses. The approach is analogous to successful descriptions of hot gluon gases using effective masses (Golosov et al. 1992; Peshier et al. 1994). The authors find that the influence of effective masses cannot be reproduced by simply altering the bag constant or by including $\alpha_s$-corrections from one-gluon exchange (Farhi and Jaffe 1984; Greiner et al. 1987), since the strangeness fraction behaves oppositely. The results suggest that strange quark matter may be only metastable, with implications for strangelet formation in heavy ion collisions and for the structure of strange quark stars.
