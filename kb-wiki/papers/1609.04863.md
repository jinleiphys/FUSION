# Vortex structure in superfluid color-flavor locked quark matter

> The core region of a neutron star may feature quark matter in the color-flavor- locked (CFL) phase. The CFL condensate breaks the baryon number symmetry, such that the phenomenon of superfluidity arises. If the core of the star is rotating, vortices will form in the superfluid, carrying the quanta of angular momentum. In a previous study we have solved the question of stability of these vortices, where we found numerical proof of a conjectured instability, according to which superfluid vortices will decay into an arrangement of so-called semi-superfluid fluxtubes. Here we report first results of an extension of our framework that allows us to study multi-vortex dynamics. This will in turn enable us to investigate the structure of semi-superfluid string lattices, which could be relevant to study pinning phenomena at the boundary of the core.

## Key claim

Superfluid vortices in color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter are unstable and decay into a triplet of well-separated semi-superfluid flux tubes, but this instability is conditional on the values of the gauge coupling and condensate self-couplings; there exist regions of parameter space where the global vortex is metastable rather than immediately unstable.

## Method

The authors used a Ginzburg-Landau effective theory for the CFL condensate, described by a complex 3x3 matrix field coupled to gluon gauge fields. They solved the classical field equations for single-vortex and multi-vortex configurations on a lattice, using relaxation methods for initial profiles and time evolution with damping to study vortex dynamics. For the multi-vortex case, they constructed initial conditions by superposing single-vortex profiles and introduced a modified fixed boundary condition with "ghost" vortices placed outside the simulation region to reduce excess winding.

## Key numbers

- The energy density of a global superfluid vortex far from its core is \(\epsilon_{\rm sf} = 3 \bar\phi^2 / r^2\), while that of a single semi-superfluid flux tube is \(\epsilon_{\rm ssft} = \frac{1}{3} \bar\phi^2 / r^2\), making the flux tube energetically favored by a factor of 9.
- The metastability region in parameter space can be characterized by \(\lambda_1 \lesssim -0.16 g\).
- At ultra-high density, the physically relevant regime gives \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_2 = \lambda/4 = \frac{36}{7} \frac{\pi^4}{\zeta(3)} (T_c / \mu)^2\).
- The multi-vortex simulation used 90 vortices on a \(448 \times 448\) lattice with a minimal initial vortex separation of 15 lattice units, evolved to times up to \(t = 3000\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Alford et al., 2016) that provided numerical proof of the conjectured instability of superfluid vortices in CFL quark matter, originally proposed by Nakano (2007). The results connect to weak-coupling mean-field calculations by Iida (2000) and Giannakis (2001) that predict \(\lambda_1 = \lambda_2\) at ultra-high density. The multi-vortex framework developed here enables future studies of semi-superfluid string lattices, which are needed to understand pinning phenomena at the boundary of a neutron star core and their possible role in neutron star glitches.
