# Probing proton fluctuations with asymmetric rapidity correlations

> Intrinsic fluctuations of the proton saturation momentum generate asymmetric rapidity distributions on an event-by-event basis. We argue that the asymmetric component, $\left<a_1^2\right>$, of the orthogonal polynomial decomposition of the two-particle rapidity correlation function is a sensitive probe to this distribution of fluctuations. We present a simple model connecting the experimentally measured $\left<a_1^2\right>$ to the variance, $σ$, of the distribution of the logarithm of the proton saturation scale. We find that $σ\approx 0. 5-1$ describes the asymmetric component of the rapidity correlations recently measured by the ATLAS collaboration.

## Key claim

Intrinsic fluctuations of the proton saturation momentum generate asymmetric rapidity distributions on an event-by-event basis, and the asymmetric component ⟨a₁²⟩ of the orthogonal polynomial decomposition of the two-particle rapidity correlation function is a sensitive probe of the variance σ of the distribution of the logarithm of the proton saturation scale, with σ ≈ 0.5–1 describing the asymmetric component of the rapidity correlations recently measured by the ATLAS collaboration.

## Method

The authors model proton-proton collisions using the KLN (Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi) formula for the single inclusive rapidity distribution, where the saturation scales of the two protons evolve with rapidity as Q₁² = Qₒ,₁² e^{+λy} and Q₂² = Qₒ,₂² e^{-λy}. The initial saturation scales Qₒ,₁ and Qₒ,₂ fluctuate event-by-event according to a Gaussian distribution in the logarithm of the saturation scale, P[ρ] = (1/√(2π)σ) exp[−ρ²/(2σ²)] with ρ ≡ ln(Q²/⟨Q²⟩). The authors then compute the two-particle correlation function C₂(y₁,y₂) and extract the coefficient ⟨a₁²⟩ from its expansion in orthogonal polynomials, deriving an analytic expression relating ⟨a₁²⟩ to σ and the evolution parameter λ.

## Key numbers

- The variance σ of the logarithm of the proton saturation scale is found to be in the range 0.5–1 when compared to ATLAS data.
- The ATLAS measurement of √⟨a₁²⟩ at √s = 13 TeV for minimum bias p+p collisions at Nch = 17.6 is centered at ⟨a₁²⟩ = 0.098 with a two-standard-deviation band thickness of ±0.012.
- The evolution parameter λ is constrained by phenomenological fits to Deep Inelastic Scattering data to be within the range 0.25–0.35.
- The analytic expression for ⟨a₁²⟩ near mid-rapidity is given by ⟨a₁²⟩ ≃ (λ²σ²/2) × [4π(1+2σ²)exp(σ²)Erfc(σ) − 8√π σ] / [√π(σ²−2)Erfc(σ/2) − 2σ exp(−σ²/4)]².

## Context

This work extends the treatment of McLerran (2015) to include fluctuations in both colliding nucleons, where previous work had considered fluctuations in only one nucleon for p+Pb collisions. The results are in qualitative agreement with the values σ = 0.5 obtained by McLerran (2015) from fitting p+p multiplicity distributions and σ = 1.55 obtained by McLerran (2015) from fitting p+Pb rapidity distributions. The paper enables experimental extraction of the width of saturation scale fluctuations in the proton from two-particle rapidity correlation measurements, providing a cleaner observable than multiplicity distributions because many other fluctuation sources (impact parameter fluctuations, color charge fluctuations) produce symmetric rapidity distributions and therefore do not contribute to ⟨a₁²⟩.
