# Relativistic effects in exclusive neutron-deuteron breakup

> We extended the study of relativistic effects in neutron-deuteron scattering to the exclusive breakup. To this aim we solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation including such relativistic features as relativistic kinematics and boost effects at incoming neutron lab. energies E_n^{lab}=65 MeV, 156 MeV and 200 MeV. As dynamical input a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction exactly on-shell equivalent to the CD Bonn potential has been used. We found that the magnitude of relativistic effects increases with the incoming neutron energy and, depending on the phase-space region, relativity can increase as well as decrease the nonrelativistic breakup cross section. In some regions of the breakup phase-space dynamical boost effects are important. For a number of measured exclusive cross sections relativity seems to improve the description of data.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in exclusive neutron-deuteron breakup can increase or decrease the nonrelativistic cross section depending on the phase-space region, with the magnitude growing with incoming neutron energy; the boost effects are important in some regions and relativity appears to improve the description of data for a number of measured exclusive cross sections.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation including relativistic kinematics and boost effects at incoming neutron laboratory energies of 65 MeV, 156 MeV, and 200 MeV. They used a relativistic nucleon-nucleon interaction exactly on-shell equivalent to the nonrelativistic CD Bonn potential, with the boosted potential approximated by a leading-order expansion in q/ω and v/ω. The breakup cross sections were computed in the three-nucleon center-of-mass system and then transformed to the laboratory system via Lorentz transformations.

## Key numbers

- At 65 MeV incoming neutron energy, relativistic effects increase the nonrelativistic cross section by up to approximately 14% and decrease it by up to approximately 25%.
- At 200 MeV incoming neutron energy, relativistic effects increase the nonrelativistic cross section by up to approximately 55% and decrease it by up to approximately 60%.
- For quasi-free-scattering configurations at 65 MeV, inclusion of relativity reduces the cross section by approximately 9%.
- For the symmetrical space star configuration at 65 MeV, the relativistic effect amounts to approximately 7% reduction.
- The calculations included all partial wave states with total angular momenta of the two-nucleon subsystem up to j_max=5 and total angular momenta of the three-nucleon system up to J=25/2, leading to up to 143 coupled integral equations for a given J and parity.

## Context

This work extends the study of relativistic effects in neutron-deuteron scattering from elastic scattering (Skibinski et al., 2005) to the exclusive breakup reaction. It builds on the Hamiltonian scheme in equal-time formulation developed by Kamada and Gloeckle (2002) and uses the analytical scale transformation of momenta relating nonrelativistic and relativistic potentials (Kamada and Gloeckle, 1998). The results enable comparison with existing breakup data at 65 MeV (Allet et al., 1994; Zejma et al., 1997), 156 MeV, and 200 MeV, and suggest that relativity may resolve the long-standing overestimation of the 65 MeV symmetrical space star cross section by modern nuclear forces.
