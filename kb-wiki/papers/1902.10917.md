# Dressed Quark Tensor Vertex and Nucleon Tensor Charge

> We construct the quark-antiquark scattering kernels of Bethe-Salpeter equation from the quark self-energy directly under two specific forms of quark-gluon vertices. The quark dressed tensor vertex is then calculated within this consistent framework and rainbow-ladder(RL) approximation. After employing a simplified nucleon model, the nucleon tensor charge can be defined with the tensor vertex. We then compute the tensor charge with the bare tensor vertex and the dressed vertices obtained in this framework and in RL approximation. The obtained results are consistent with the lattice QCD calculations. We also find that typically the gluon dressing effects suppress the nucleon tensor charge compared to the bare tensor vertex, by about $23\%$ for RL approximation, and turn to be about $13\%$ in this framework.

## Key claim

The gluon dressing of the quark tensor vertex suppresses the nucleon tensor charge compared to using a bare vertex, with the suppression being about 23% in rainbow-ladder (RL) approximation and about 13% when using the Munczek ansatz for the quark-gluon vertex that goes beyond RL; the results are consistent with lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors construct the two-particle irreducible scattering kernel of the Bethe-Salpeter equation directly from the functional derivative of the quark self-energy, using two forms of the quark-gluon vertex: the rainbow-ladder truncation and the Munczek ansatz which satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. They solve the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations for the quark propagator and the dressed tensor vertex, then insert the tensor vertex into a simplified nucleon model consisting of a quark and a scalar diquark to compute the u-quark tensor charge.

## Key numbers

- The u-quark tensor charge with the bare tensor vertex is 0.898.
- The u-quark tensor charge in rainbow-ladder approximation is 0.687 (+0.007 / -0.006) at a renormalization point of μ = 2 GeV.
- The u-quark tensor charge with the Munczek ansatz is 0.782 (+0.016 / -0.013) at μ = 2 GeV.
- The gluon dressing suppresses the tensor charge by about 23% for RL approximation and about 13% for the Munczek ansatz, relative to the bare vertex.
- The quark condensate is set to ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ = -(0.225 GeV)³ at μ = 2 GeV for both truncation schemes.
- The gluon parameters are mg = 0.82 GeV, ω = 0.5 GeV for RL; mg = 0.436 GeV, ω = 0.355 GeV for Munczek ansatz.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Hobbs (2016) who studied dressing effects on the axial charge using a similar quark-diquark nucleon model, and it builds on the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework developed by Chang (2009) and Munczek (1994). The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou (2017) and Gupta (2018), as well as experimental extractions from global analyses; the computed tensor charges are consistent with lattice QCD but differ from current experimental data, indicating the need for further theoretical and experimental refinement.
