# Isentropic thermodynamics across the hadron-quark mixed phase in a two-phase model with a PNJL quark description

> We study the hadron-quark mixed phase within a two-phase model for symmetric and asymmetric matter. For the quark sector we employ the (2+1) Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (PNJL) with vector interactions. We investigate how the hadronic equation of state affects the phase diagram and the thermodynamic properties inside the mixed phase. The behavior of isentropic trajectories in the mixed phase depends on the fixed entropy per baryon ($s/ρ_B$), with trajectories near the critical end point (CEP) exhibiting a pronounced cooling pattern, while isentropic trajectories with low entropy per baryon undergo pronounced heating as the baryonic density increases. The adiabatic squared speed of sound displays characteristic peak and dip structures that depend on $s/ρ_B$. The polytropic index along isentropic and isothermal trajectories, including in the vicinity of the CEP are also investigated. The effects of vector interactions and isospin asymmetry on thermodynamic observables likewise depend on the chosen $s/ρ_B$ value. Finally, we discuss the population of hyperons along isentropic trajectories and their influence on the phase diagram. The main effect of hyperons is to shift the onset of deconfinement to larger densities and decrease the density extension of the mixed phase.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic behavior of the hadron-quark mixed phase along isentropic trajectories depends strongly on the fixed entropy per baryon (s/ρ_B), with trajectories near the critical end point exhibiting pronounced cooling while low-entropy trajectories undergo pronounced heating as baryonic density increases; these effects are modulated by the choice of hadronic equation of state, the strength of vector interactions in the quark sector, and the presence of hyperons.

## Method

The authors constructed a two-phase model coupling a hadronic phase described by relativistic mean-field models (NL3ωρ for nucleonic matter and FSU2H including the full baryon octet) with a quark phase described by the (2+1)-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model including vector interactions. The mixed phase was determined by imposing Gibbs conditions (thermal, chemical, and mechanical equilibrium) under conservation of baryon number, isospin, and zero net strangeness, with the global isospin asymmetry parameter α held constant. Thermodynamic quantities including the squared speed of sound, polytropic index, and baryon fractions were computed along isentropic trajectories at fixed s/ρ_B values of 0.5, 2, and 5, for symmetric (α=0) and asymmetric (α=0.2) matter, with vector coupling ratios ζ = G_V/G_S of 0, 0.1, and 0.5.

## Key numbers

- Isentropic trajectories were computed at fixed entropy per baryon ratios s/ρ_B = 0.5, 2, and 5.
- Isotherms were calculated at fixed temperatures T = 10, 80, 100, and 150 MeV.
- The global isospin asymmetry parameter α = 0.2 was chosen to study asymmetric matter, based on the value α = 0.227 for 238U + 238U collisions from Cavagnoli (2010).
- The Polyakov loop effective potential used T_0 = 210 MeV with parameters a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, and b_3 = -1.75 from Roessner (2006).
- The PNJL model used a sharp cutoff Λ = 630 MeV, with current quark masses m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 135.7 MeV, and coupling constants G_S Λ^2 = 1.781 and G_D Λ^5 = 9.29 from Camara Pereira (2016).
- The horizontal dotted line γ = 1.75 serves as an approximate reference value to distinguish hadronic from quark degrees of freedom in the polytropic index plots.
- The horizontal dashed line c_s^2 = 1/3 indicates the high-density conformal limit for the squared speed of sound.

## Context

This work extends the two-phase model approach previously applied to neutron-star matter by Glendenning (1992) and to heavy-ion collision scenarios by Di Toro (2006, 2008, 2009) and Cavagnoli (2010), incorporating both hyperonic degrees of freedom and vector interactions in the quark sector. The study of isentropic trajectories connects to experimental efforts at RHIC Beam Energy Scan II (BES-II) to locate the critical end point, and to the work of Sorensen (2021) on the speed of sound and its connection to baryon-number cumulants. The results enable more realistic modeling of the hydrodynamic evolution of fireballs in heavy-ion collisions and provide complementary observables (speed of sound peaks, polytropic index behavior) that may serve as signatures of the hadron-quark phase transition beyond net-baryon number susceptibilities, which Ferreira (2018) showed can exhibit nonmonotonic behavior even without a critical end point.
