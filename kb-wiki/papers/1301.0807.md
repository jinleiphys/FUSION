# Density Functional Theory with Spatial-Symmetry Breaking and Configuration Mixing

> This article generalizes the notion of the local density of a many-body system to introduce collective coordinates as explicit degrees of freedom. It is shown that the energy of the system can be expressed as a functional of this object. The latter can in turn be factorized as the product of the square of a collective wave function and a normalized collective-coordinate-dependent density. Energy minimization translates into a set of coupled equations, i.e. a local Schrödinger equation for the collective wave function and a set of Kohn-Sham equations for optimizing the normalized density at each point in the collective space. These equations reformulate the many-body problem exactly provided one is able to determine density- and collective-wave-function-dependent terms of the collective mass and potential which play a similar role to the exchange-correlation term in electronic Kohn-Sham density functional theory.

## Key claim

This paper generalizes density functional theory (DFT) to include collective coordinates as explicit degrees of freedom by defining a generalized density that factorizes into a collective wave function and a coordinate-dependent normalized density, but the resulting coupled equations reformulate the many-body problem exactly only *provided* one can determine density- and collective-wave-function-dependent terms analogous to the exchange-correlation functional in standard Kohn-Sham DFT.

## Method

The author introduces a projector operator \(\hat{P}(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{R})\) that selects configurations of the \(N\)-particle system for which a chosen set of collective coordinates \(\mathbf{q}\) (expectation values of one-body operators \(\hat{Q}_\mu\)) take specific values, then defines a generalized density \(D(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{r})\) as the joint probability density of finding one particle at \(\mathbf{r}\) and the collective configuration at \(\mathbf{q}\). This generalized density is factorized into a collective wave function \(f(\mathbf{q})\) and a normalized \(\mathbf{q}\)-dependent density \(d(\mathbf{q},\mathbf{r})\), and the energy is expressed as a functional \(E[f,d]\) from which a collective Schrödinger equation for \(f\) and Kohn-Sham-like equations for \(d\) are derived via minimization.

## Key numbers

- The collective mass term \(\mathcal{A}_{\mu\nu}(\mathbf{q}) = F_{\mu\nu}(\mathbf{q}) / \langle \hat{P}(\mathbf{q}) \rangle\) is defined, where \(F_{\mu\nu}(\mathbf{q})\) involves the gradient of the collective-coordinate operators \(\hat{Q}_\mu\) and \(\hat{Q}_\nu\) integrated over the many-body wave function.
- The collective potential \(\mathcal{U}(\mathbf{q})\) contains terms proportional to \(F_{\mu\nu}(\mathbf{q})\) and \(J_\mu(\mathbf{q})\) (the collective current), plus the expectation value of the kinetic and interaction operators projected onto each slice.
- The rearrangement potential \(\mathcal{U}_\text{ra}(\mathbf{q})\) arises from the functional derivative of \(\mathcal{A}_{\mu\nu}\) and \(\mathcal{U}\) with respect to \(|f|^2\) when deriving the collective Schrödinger equation.

## Context

This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn (1964) and Kohn-Sham (1965) framework to accommodate spatial-symmetry breaking (translation, rotation) and configuration mixing, addressing a known limitation of nuclear DFT that relies on symmetry-breaking density configurations. It provides a formal foundation for methods like the Generator Coordinate Method (Hill and Wheeler, 1953; Griffin and Wheeler, 1957) and collective Bohr Hamiltonians (Bohr, 1953; Bohr and Mottelson), which previously lacked a clear connection to first-principles DFT, and recovers the internal-density functional of Messud et al. (2009) as a limit case for translational motion. The formalism enables exact treatment of collective motion in nuclei while keeping the DFT structure, though pairing (particle-number violation) is deferred to future work.
