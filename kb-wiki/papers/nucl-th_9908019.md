# Renormalization Group Flow Equation at Finite Density

> For the linear sigma model with quarks we derive renormalization group flow equations for finite temperature and finite baryon density using the heat kernel cutoff. At zero temperature we evolve the effective potential to the Fermi momentum and compare the solutions of the full evolution equation with those in the mean field approximation. We find a first order phase transition either from a massive constituent quark phase to a mixed phase, where both massive and massless quarks are present, or from a metastable constituent quark phase at low density to a stable massless quark phase at high density. In the latter solution, the formation of droplets of massless quarks is realized even at low density.

## Key claim

For the linear sigma model with quarks at zero temperature, the renormalization group flow equation yields either a first-order phase transition from a massive constituent quark phase to a mixed phase containing both massive and massless quarks, or alternatively a transition from a metastable constituent quark phase at low density to a stable massless quark phase at high density, with the latter solution allowing droplet formation of massless quarks even at low density.

## Method

The authors derived renormalization group (RG) flow equations for the effective potential of the linear sigma model with quarks at finite temperature and finite baryon density, using a heat kernel cutoff. At zero temperature, they evolved the effective potential down to the Fermi momentum and compared the full evolution equation solutions with those obtained in the mean field approximation. The analysis was performed for the case of two quark flavors with N_c = 3 colors.

## Key numbers

* The effective potential was evolved to the Fermi momentum scale k = k_F, with the dimensionless variable z = k/k_F used to parameterize the RG flow.
* The constituent quark mass m_Q appears in the energy per baryon expression (epsilon(rho_B) - epsilon_0)/rho_B, plotted in GeV units for baryon densities rho_B up to 5 times nuclear saturation density rho_0.
* The binding energy per baryon, defined as (epsilon(rho_B) - epsilon_0)/rho_B - 3 m_Q, is shown in GeV for baryon densities rho_B/rho_0 from 0 to 5.
* The dimensionless density variable 3 pi^2 rho / (2 N_c k^3) is used to characterize the RG flow, with N_c = 3.

## Context

This work extends the application of renormalization group methods to finite-density QCD using the linear sigma model as an effective theory, building on earlier RG flow equation approaches at zero density and finite temperature. The results enable comparison between full RG evolution and simpler mean-field treatments of the chiral phase transition at finite baryon density, and suggest the possibility of inhomogeneous phases (droplet formation) in dense nuclear matter. The approach contrasts with standard mean-field calculations that do not capture the full momentum-mode integration of the RG flow.
