# Reexamining the perturbative renormalizability of the coupled triplets

> I reexamine the perturbative renormalizability of chiral two-pion exchange in two-nucleon scattering for coupled triplets when one-pion exchange has been fully iterated at leading order. Improving over previous works, it is shown that only two counterterms are required to obtain cutoff independent results, which is one less than in naive dimensional analysis. The explanation for this reduction is the existence of an attractive and repulsive eigenchannel in the one-pion exchange potential for the coupled triplets: the attractive eigenchannel can be renormalized like a regular attractive uncoupled triplet, while the repulsive eigenchannel is always finite regardless of whether there are counterterms or not. I discuss the implications of this finding for the power counting of the $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ and $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ partial waves.

## Key claim

The paper shows that chiral two-pion exchange in coupled triplet channels can be renormalized with only two counterterms when one-pion exchange is fully iterated at leading order, which is one less than naive dimensional analysis would suggest and fewer than the six counterterms previously thought necessary; this reduction works because the attractive eigenchannel of the one-pion exchange potential requires renormalization like an uncoupled attractive triplet while the repulsive eigenchannel is automatically finite.

## Method

The author uses distorted wave perturbation theory in coordinate space with a sharp cutoff radius \(R_c\), expanding the reduced wave functions in powers of \(k^2\) after removing an energy-dependent normalization factor. The coupled-channel Schrodinger equation is diagonalized into attractive and repulsive eigenchannels of the one-pion exchange tensor force, and the perturbative integrals for subleading two-pion exchange are analyzed for divergences as \(R_c \to 0\). The phase shifts for the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) and \(^3P_2\)-\(^3F_2\) partial waves are then computed with two subtraction constants (or equivalently two contact-range counterterms) to demonstrate cutoff independence.

## Key numbers

- The one-pion exchange potential at short distances behaves as \(\pm a_3 / r^3\), where \(a_3\) is a length scale characterizing the tensor force strength in triplet channels.
- The perturbative integral for attractive eigenchannels diverges when \(\frac{3}{2} + \nu - \frac{5n}{2} \geq 1\), requiring two subtractions (a constant and a \(k^2\) term) for leading and subleading two-pion exchange (\(\nu = 2, 3\)).
- The repulsive eigenchannel integrals converge as \(\int_{R_c} dr \, e^{-4\sqrt{a_{3R}/r}} / r^{3/2+\nu-5n/2}\) for \(R_c \to 0\), requiring no counterterms.
- The number of required counterterms is reduced from six (as in Valderrama (2009) and Valderrama (2011)) to two, which is one less than the three expected from naive dimensional analysis.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative renormalization program for chiral nuclear forces initiated by Valderrama (2009, 2011), who originally found six counterterms needed for coupled triplets, and by Long and Yang (2011), who reduced that number to three. It addresses the regulator issues raised by Gasparyan and Epelbaum (2022), who showed that some momentum-space regulators do not produce cutoff-independent amplitudes, while coordinate-space sharp-cutoff methods (as used here) are proven to have a well-defined \(R_c \to 0\) limit. The result enables a consistent power counting for the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) and \(^3P_2\)-\(^3F_2\) partial waves with fewer free parameters than previously thought necessary.
