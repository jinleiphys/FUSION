# Excited decuplet baryons from QCD sum rules

> A calculation of the mass spectrum in the baryon decuplet sector is presented using the method of QCD sum rules. Sum rules are derived for states of spin-parity 3/2+- and 1/2+ using both the conventional method and a parity-projection method. The predictive ability of the sum rules is explored by a Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure in which the three phenomenological parameters (mass, coupling, threshold) are treated as free parameters and fitted simultaneously. Taken together, the results give an improved determination of the mass spectrum in this sector from the perspective of non-perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

The parity-projected QCD sum rule method, applied to the decuplet baryon sector, can separate positive- and negative-parity states exactly, but for the spin-3/2 positive-parity states a three-parameter fit (mass, coupling, threshold) could not be found, so the continuum threshold had to be fixed to values suggested by the Particle Data Group, meaning the sum rules do not have enough information to resolve all three parameters simultaneously.

## Method

The author derived QCD sum rules for spin-3/2 and spin-1/2 decuplet baryons using both the conventional two-point correlation function and a parity-projection method that introduces a step function to separate positive- and negative-parity contributions. For spin-3/2 states, the sum rules were constructed from the Dirac structures gμνp̂ and gμν, which couple only to spin-3/2, and parity projection was applied exactly. For spin-1/2 states, sum rules were derived from the γμpν+γνpμ and γμγν+1/3 gμν structures, but parity projection could not be applied, so conventional mixed-parity sum rules were used. The analysis employed a Monte-Carlo based fitting procedure where the QCD input parameters (condensates, strange quark mass, etc.) were randomly sampled with Gaussian distributions, and the phenomenological parameters were extracted by χ² minimization over a Borel window, using 500 Monte-Carlo sets.

## Context

This work extends the parity-projection method of Jido (1997) from the octet baryon sector to the decuplet sector, and builds on earlier QCD sum rule studies of decuplet baryons by Belyaev (1982, 1983), Reinders (1982, 1985), Chung (1984), and Derek (1990). It also applies the Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure of Derek (1996) to systematically estimate uncertainties from QCD input parameters. The results provide an improved determination of the decuplet mass spectrum from non-perturbative QCD, and the parity-projected sum rules offer insight that parity splittings originate from chiral-odd vacuum condensates. The findings enable future identification of spin-parity assignments for excited decuplet states, such as suggesting that the 1690 MeV state in the Ξ channel is likely not 3/2+, and casting doubt on the existence of the Δ(1/2+) state at 1.75 GeV.
