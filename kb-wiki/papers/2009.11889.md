# Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of fully-heavy (multiquark) bound states

> We use a diffusion Monte Carlo method to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation describing fully-heavy tetraquark systems. This approach allows to reduce the uncertainty of the numerical calculation at the percent level, accounts for multi-particle correlations in the physical observables, and avoids the usual quark-clustering assumed in other theoretical techniques applied to the same problem. The interaction between particles was modeled by the most general and accepted potential, i.e. a pairwise interaction including Coulomb, linear-confining and hyperfine spin-spin terms. This means that, in principle, our analysis should provide some rigorous statements about the mass location of the all-heavy tetraquark ground states, which is particularly timely due to the very recent observation made by the LHCb collaboration of some enhancements in the invariant mass spectra of $J/ψ$-pairs. Our main results are: (i) the $cc\bar c\bar c$, $cc\bar b\bar b$ ($bb\bar c\bar c$) and $bb\bar b \bar b$ lowest-lying states are located well above their corresponding meson-meson thresholds; (ii) the $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ $cc\bar c\bar c$ ground state with preferred quark-antiquark pair configurations is compatible with the enhancement(s) observed by the LHCb collaboration; (iii) our results for the $cc\bar c\bar b$ and $bb\bar c\bar b$ sectors seem to indicate that the $0^+$ and $1^+$ ground states are almost degenerate with the $2^+$ located around $100\,\text{MeV}$ above them; (iv) smaller mass splittings for the $cb\bar c\bar b$ system are predicted, with absolute mass values in reasonable agreement with other theoretical works; (v) the $1^{++}$ $cb\bar c\bar b$ tetraquark ground state lies at its lowest $S$-wave meson-meson threshold and it is compatible with a molecular configuration.

## Key claim

The diffusion Monte Carlo method, which avoids assuming any particular quark clustering and accounts for multi-particle correlations non-perturbatively, predicts that the lowest-lying states of the $cc\bar c\bar c$, $cc\bar b\bar b$ ($bb\bar c\bar c$) and $bb\bar b \bar b$ systems are located well above their corresponding meson-meson thresholds, while the $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ $cc\bar c\bar c$ ground state with preferred quark-antiquark pair configurations is compatible with the enhancement(s) observed by the LHCb collaboration.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body Schrödinger equation for fully-heavy tetraquark systems using a diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) technique, which eliminates the systematic uncertainty associated with the trial wave function. The interaction between quarks was modeled by a pairwise potential including Coulomb, linear-confining, and hyperfine spin-spin terms (the AL1 potential from Semay (1994) and Silvestre-Brac (1996)), with parameters fixed by a simultaneous fit of 36 mesons and 53 baryons. The DMC algorithm propagated walkers in imaginary time using importance sampling with a trial function of the form $\phi(\vec{r}_{ij}) = e^{-a_{ij}r_{ij}}$, and the Green's function was approximated to order $(\Delta t)^2$ for small time steps.

## Key numbers

- Charmonium $J/\psi$ ($1^{3}S_1$, $1^{--}$) mass: 3101 MeV from DMC, compared to experimental value 3096.900 ± 0.006 MeV.
- Bottomonium $\Upsilon(1S)$ ($1^{3}S_1$, $1^{--}$) mass: 9462 MeV from DMC, compared to experimental value 9460.30 ± 0.26 MeV.
- $B_c$ meson ($1^{1}S_0$, $0^{-+}$) mass: 6292 MeV from DMC, compared to experimental value 6274.9 ± 0.8 MeV.
- For the $cc\bar c\bar b$ and $bb\bar c\bar b$ sectors, the $0^+$ and $1^+$ ground states are almost degenerate with the $2^+$ located around 100 MeV above them.
- Statistical error of the DMC algorithm is of the order of 1 MeV, negligible compared to the systematic model uncertainty estimated at 10-20%.

## Context

This work extends prior potential model studies of fully-heavy tetraquarks (e.g., Ader (1981), Lloyd (2003), Richard (2018), and others) by using a diffusion Monte Carlo method that avoids the usual quark-clustering assumptions (meson-meson or diquark-antidiquark) employed in variational approaches. The calculation is similar to that of Bai (2016) but differs by not assuming specific clusters, including the sextet-antisextet diquark-antidiquark configuration, and treating the hyperfine spin-spin interaction non-perturbatively. The results are timely due to the LHCb collaboration's observation of enhancements in the $J/\psi$-pair invariant mass spectrum, and they enable future experimental searches for fully-heavy tetraquark states by providing mass predictions for ground states across multiple flavor sectors.
