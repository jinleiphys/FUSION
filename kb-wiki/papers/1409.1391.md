# Diffusion on the Distorted Fermi Surface

> The diffusion approximation to the relaxation on the distorted Fermi surface in a Fermi liquid is considered. The dependence of the relaxation time on the multipolarity of a Fermi surface deformation is established. The time evolution of the non-equilibrium particle-hole excitations is studied.

## Key claim
The relaxation time for a distorted Fermi surface depends on the multipolarity \(l\) of the deformation as \(\tau_{r,l}^{-1} = (D_p(p_F)/p_F^2)\, l(l+1)\), but this diffusion-based result holds only for \(l \geq 2\) because the \(l=0\) and \(l=1\) terms are excluded by particle-number and momentum conservation in collisions.

## Method
The authors start from the collisional kinetic equation for the Wigner distribution function and apply the diffusion approximation, keeping terms up to second order in the transferred momentum. They linearize the collision integral around the equilibrium Fermi distribution, expand the deviation in spherical harmonics, and derive the relaxation time for each multipole. For the particle-hole case, they solve the resulting nonlinear diffusion equation numerically for a homogeneous nuclear matter system with an initial non-equilibrium distribution.

## Key numbers
- Diffusion coefficient used: \(D_p(p_F) = 2.5 \times 10^{-21}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{2}\,\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\,\mathrm{s}\).
- Drift coefficient used: \(K_p = -6.25 \times 10^{-22}\ \mathrm{MeV}\,\mathrm{fm}^{-2}\,\mathrm{s}\).
- Relaxation time for quadrupole (\(l=2\)) distortion: \(\tau_{r,l=2} = p_F^2 / (6 D_p(p_F))\).
- For the GQR width calculation: Fermi energy \(\epsilon_F = 40\ \mathrm{MeV}\), mean nucleon-nucleon distance \(r_0 = 1.2\ \mathrm{fm}\), GQR energy \(\hbar\omega_R = 63 A^{-1/3}\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the particle-hole excitation in \(A=16\): initial excitation energy \(E_{\mathrm{ex}} = 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\), equilibrium temperature \(T \approx 4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), level density parameter \(a \approx A/8.5\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\).

## Context
This work extends the diffusion approximation of Lipi (1981) to the relaxation of Fermi-surface distortions in nuclear Fermi liquids. It provides a microscopic justification for the fluid-dynamic approximation (FLDA) used by Kosh (2004) to describe giant resonances, showing that higher multipolarities (\(l>2\)) damp quickly. The results enable the adjustment of the diffusion coefficient to experimental GQR width data from Bert (1981) and reproduce the empirical level density parameter \(a \approx A/8\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) from Shko (2005).
