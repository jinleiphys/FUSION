# Single-channel, single-energy partial-wave analysis with continuity improved through minimal phase constraints

> Single-energy partial-wave analysis has often been applied as a way to fit data with minimal model dependence. However, remaining unconstrained, partial waves at neighboring energies will vary discontinuously because the overall amplitude phase cannot be determined through single-channel measurements. This problem can be mitigated through the use of a constraining penalty function based on an associated energy-dependent fit. However, the weight given to this constraint results in a biased fit to the data. In this paper, for the first time, we explore a constraining function which does not influence the fit to data. The constraint comes from the overall phase found in multi-channel fits which, in the present study, are the Bonn-Gatchina and Jülich-Bonn multi-channel analyses. The data are well reproduced and weighting of the penalty function does not influence the result. The method is applied to $K Λ$ photoproduction data and all observables can be maximally well reproduced. While the employed multi-channel analyses display very different multipole amplitudes, we show that the major difference between two sets of multipoles can be related to the different overall phases.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that single-channel, single-energy partial-wave analyses can be constrained using only the overall phase from multi-channel fits, without biasing the fit to experimental data. The key advance is that the penalty function weighting does not influence the result, unlike previous constraining methods that directly affected observables.

## Method
The authors applied a penalty function technique to fix the overall phase of the transversity amplitude \(b_1\) to values taken from two multi-channel analyses (Bonn-Gatchina and Julich-Bonn). They performed single-energy fits to \(K \Lambda\) photoproduction data using eight measured observables plus two pseudo-observables (E and F) generated from the multi-channel models, over the center-of-mass energy range 1625 to 2179.83 MeV. The penalty function constrained the normalized \(b_1\) amplitude (its sine and cosine) rather than the phase directly, and the weight \(\lambda_{ph}\) was varied from 25 to 1000 to test independence of the constraint.

## Key numbers
- Data set: 3615 differential cross section points from CLAS (2007, 2010) over \(E_{c.m.} = 1625-2295\) MeV; 400 \(\Sigma\) points from GRAAL (2007) and CLAS (2016) over 1649-2179 MeV; 408 \(T\) points over 1645-2179 MeV; 1597 \(P\) points over 1625-2295 MeV; 415 \(O_{x'}\) and 415 \(O_{z'}\) points over 1645-2179 MeV; 138 \(C_x\) and 138 \(C_z\) points over 1678-2296 MeV.
- Energy range of analysis: \(1625 \le W \le 2179.83\) MeV.
- Penalty function weight tested: \(\lambda_{ph} = 25\) and \(\lambda_{ph} = 1000\), with "almost invisible differences" in results.
- Angular range extended from experimentally measured \(-0.7 < \cos\theta < 0.8\) to \(-0.9 < \cos\theta < 0.9\) using kinematical constraints.
- Pseudo-data E and F were randomized by 10% with 10% error assigned.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Svarc (2018), which demonstrated the phase-fixing method using infinite-precision numeric data for \(\eta\) photoproduction, to real experimental data with uncertainties. It contrasts with earlier constraining methods such as the Karlsruhe-Helsinki fixed-t analyticity approach (Hohler) and the Zagreb group's s-variable analyticity method (Svarc 2020, 2022), which used full amplitudes as constraints and thus influenced observables. The method enables direct comparison of multipoles from different multi-channel analyses (Bonn-Gatchina and Julich-Bonn) by attributing their differences to the overall phase, and provides a path toward more theory-independent single-energy partial-wave analyses.
