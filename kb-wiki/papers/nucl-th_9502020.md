# The Photodissociation of $^8B$ and the Solar Neutrino Problem

> The extraction of the photodissociation cross sections of $^8B$ from Coulomb dissociation experiments is investigated. A careful study is done on the contributions of the E1, E2 and M1 multipolarities to the breakup. A comparison with the data of a recent experiment is performed. It is shown that the extraction of the radiative capture cross sections $^7Be(p,\ γ)^8B$ which are relevant for the solar neutrino problem is not affected appreciably by Coulomb reacceleration. A non-perturbative model is used for the purpose. Emphasis is put on the perspectives for future experiments which are planned at the University of Notre Dame, RIKEN (Japan), and GSI (Germany). An analysis of the total yields of ``photon-point" processes in inelastic electron scattering is also done.

## Key claim

The extraction of the $^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B$ radiative capture cross section from Coulomb dissociation experiments is not appreciably affected by Coulomb reacceleration effects, but the relative contributions of E1, E2, and M1 multipolarities to the breakup must be carefully disentangled through angular selection and experiments at multiple bombarding energies.

## Method

The author used a non-perturbative approach by numerically solving the three-dimensional time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the p+$^7Be$ system in a Woods-Saxon potential, including the time-dependent E1 Coulomb interaction from the target. First-order perturbation theory calculations were also performed for comparison, using the equivalent photon method with semiclassical Coulomb excitation theory including retardation and Coulomb recoil effects. The model was applied to the Coulomb breakup of $^8B$ on lead targets at bombarding energies of 5, 50, and 250 MeV/nucleon, corresponding to planned experiments at Notre Dame University, RIKEN, and GSI.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the p+$^7Be$ system is $Q=137$ MeV
- The $^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B$ reaction is dominated by a $1^+$ resonance at $E=633$ keV in the relative energy
- At 5 MeV/nucleon on lead, the E1 Coulomb breakup cross section below the grazing angle ($75^\circ$) is 159 mb and the E2 component is 73 mb; at 50 MeV/nucleon ($9.4^\circ$ grazing angle) E1 is 172 mb and E2 is 23 mb; at 250 MeV/nucleon ($2.5^\circ$ grazing angle) E1 is 38 mb and E2 is 0.95 mb
- The effective $S_{E1}$-factor used to reproduce the Motobayashi et al. (1994) data was 18 eV b, compared to their preliminary best-fit value of $16.7 \pm 3.2$ eV b
- The M1 multipolarity is about a factor 100, 10, and 3 smaller than E1 for bombarding energies of 5, 50, and 250 MeV/nucleon, respectively
- The E2 cross section from the Kim et al. (1987) model is a factor 500-1000 smaller than the E1 cross section over the relevant energy domain
- The reacceleration energy shift from the non-perturbative calculation is smaller than that from the semiclassical model of Bertulani et al. (1992) and the Monte-Carlo method of Bertulani (1994)

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb dissociation method proposed by Baur et al. (1986) for measuring radiative capture cross sections relevant to astrophysics, specifically addressing the $^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B$ reaction that produces the high-energy neutrinos detected by the Homestake and Kamiokande experiments. The non-perturbative treatment follows the approach of Esbensen et al. (1994) for $^{11}Li$ and $^{11}Be$ breakup, and the results contradict the suggestion by Langanke and Shoppa (1994) that E2 contributions could significantly alter the extracted S-factor from the Motobayashi et al. (1994) experiment. The analysis enables future experiments at multiple bombarding energies to separate multipolarity contributions and provides a framework for applying Coulomb dissociation to other astrophysical reactions such as $^{12}C(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}O$.
