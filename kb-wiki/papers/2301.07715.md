# Target mass corrections in lepton--nucleus DIS: theory and applications to nuclear PDFs

> Motivated by the wide range of kinematics covered by current and planned deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) facilities, we revisit the formalism, practical implementation, and numerical impact of target mass corrections (TMCs) for DIS on unpolarized nuclear targets. An important aspect is that we only use nuclear and later partonic degrees of freedom, carefully avoiding a picture of the nucleus in terms of nucleons. After establishing that formulae used for individual nucleon targets $(p,n)$, derived in the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) formalism, are indeed applicable to nuclear targets, we rewrite expressions for nuclear TMCs in terms of \mbox{re-scaled} (or averaged) kinematic variables. As a consequence, we find a representation for nuclear TMCs that is approximately independent of the nuclear target. We go on to construct a single-parameter fit for all nuclear targets that is in good numerical agreement with full computations of TMCs. We discuss in detail qualitative and quantitative differences between nuclear TMCs built in the OPE and the parton model formalisms, as well as give numerical predictions for current and future facilities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that target mass corrections (TMCs) derived for nucleon targets in the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) formalism are valid for nuclear targets, but only when expressed in terms of nuclear kinematic variables and without invoking a nucleon-based picture of the nucleus. The authors find a representation for nuclear TMCs that is approximately independent of the nuclear target, enabling a single-parameter fit applicable to all nuclei.

## Method

The authors first establish that OPE-based TMC formulae for nucleons apply to nuclear targets by working exclusively with nuclear degrees of freedom, avoiding any nucleon substructure. They then rewrite nuclear TMC expressions in terms of rescaled (averaged) kinematic variables, specifically using the averaged nucleon quantities x_N = A x_A and M_N = M_A/A. Finally, they construct a single-parameter fit for all nuclear targets and compare OPE-based TMCs with parton model TMCs derived in the ACOT formalism, providing numerical predictions for current and future facilities including the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and JLab.

## Context

This work extends the OPE-based TMC master formulae previously developed for nucleons by Schienbein (2007) and Kretzer and Reno (2003) to the nuclear case, addressing the subtle distinctions between nucleon and nuclear targets that earlier discussions did not resolve. The paper enables proper theoretical definitions of nuclear structure functions and nuclear PDFs (nPDFs) for use in analyses of data from current facilities (JLab, Fermilab) and future experiments (EIC, DUNE, Forward Physics Facility at CERN), where precise control over nuclear TMCs is essential for extracting parton distributions and understanding neutrino-nucleus interactions.
