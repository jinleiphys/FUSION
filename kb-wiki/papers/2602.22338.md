# Fluctuation-Dissipation Relation for Hard Partons in a Gluonic Plasma

> We derive a fluctuation dissipation relation connecting the drag and diffusion jet transport coefficients for an energetic light quark traversing a non-perturbative thermalized gluon plasma. The hard quark is taken to be close to on-shell, with an energy scale parametrically larger than the medium temperature. We introduce a general complex-valued function for each transport coefficient. Evaluating these in the deep Euclidean momentum region enables their expression in terms of local operators. Using contour-integration techniques, we relate these local operators, after vacuum subtraction, to the physical transport coefficients that arise along a branch cut, close to light-like dispersion. The derived relation relates the longitudinal drag coefficient to the longitudinal and transverse diffusion coefficients, and the thermal gluon condensate.

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-perturbative fluctuation-dissipation relation connecting the longitudinal drag coefficient (hat{e}) to the longitudinal diffusion coefficient (hat{e}_2), the transverse momentum diffusion coefficient (hat{q}), and the thermal gluon condensate, under the assumption that the hard quark has a single interaction with the medium and that the quark energy is parametrically larger than the medium temperature.

## Method

The authors consider a forward-scattering process where an energetic on-shell quark exchanges a single Glauber gluon with a thermalized, isotropic SU(3) gluon plasma. They introduce a complex-valued function C(q^+) for each transport coefficient, evaluate it in the deep Euclidean region (q^+ → -∞) to express it in terms of local operators, and then use contour-integration techniques to relate these local operators, after vacuum subtraction, to the physical transport coefficients obtained from the discontinuity across a branch cut near q^+ ≈ 0. The resulting local operator expressions are simplified using isotropy, parity, and time-reversal invariance to obtain the final relation.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal drag coefficient hat{e} is suppressed by the inverse light-cone momentum q^- of the hard quark relative to the other coefficients, as shown in Eq. (13): hat{e} = -[hat{e}_2 - hat{q}/2 + (d_0/T) ⟨M| α_s F^{μν} F_{μν} |M⟩] / q^-
- The coefficient d_0 is defined as d_0 = 4π^2 / (δ_T √2 N_c), where δ_T T represents the width of the thermal discontinuity of C(q^+) along the q^+ real axis
- The transverse momentum diffusion coefficient hat{q} is expressed as hat{q} = tilde{c}_0 ⟨M| [F^{03}F^{03} + F^{31}F^{31}] |M⟩, with tilde{c}_0 = 2π c_0/(δ_T T) and c_0 = 8π α_s/(√2 N_c)
- In the high energy limit (q^- ≫ T), the drag coefficient hat{e} is determined by the vacuum-subtracted thermal gluon condensate alone

## Context

This work extends the non-perturbative operator product approach for jet transport coefficients developed by Majumder (2012) and Kumar (2020) to derive a fluctuation-dissipation relation without assuming a specific model for the scattering kernel or medium, unlike the perturbative relation derived by Ghiglieri (2015) for a hard parton about to thermalize. The relation enables the calculation of the longitudinal drag coefficient hat{e} from lattice QCD computations of hat{q}, hat{e}_2, and the thermal gluon condensate, which is otherwise inaccessible via lattice methods since hat{e} represents a time-dependent process. The authors note that at temperatures close to T_C, the gluon condensate contribution indicates an enhancement of drag loss and longitudinal fluctuation near the transition temperature.
