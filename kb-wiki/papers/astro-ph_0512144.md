# Gaps below strange star crusts

> The gap caused by a strong electric field between the quark surface and nuclear crust of a strange star is studied in an improved model including gravity and pressure as well as electrostatic forces. The transition from gap to crust is followed in detail. The properties of the gap are investigated for a wide range of parameters assuming both color-flavor locked and non color-flavor locked strange star cores. The maximally allowed crust density is generally lower than that of neutron drip. Finite temperature is shown to increase the gap width, but the effect is significant only at extreme temperatures. Analytical approximations are derived and shown to provide useful fits to the numerical results.

# Gaps below strange star crusts

## Key claim

The gap between the quark surface and nuclear crust of a strange star is narrower than previously found when gravity and pressure are included alongside electrostatic forces, which constrains the maximum density at the base of the crust to below the neutron drip density for most parameter choices; the effect of finite temperature on gap width is significant only at extreme temperatures above approximately 10^8–10^10 K depending on crust density.

## Method

The authors solve coupled differential equations for the electron and nucleon chemical potentials derived from Poisson's equation for electrostatics and Newtonian gravity, assuming a Fermi gas description for electrons and nuclei with the effective chemical potentials held constant to enforce hydrostatic equilibrium. They use a shooting method with Runge-Kutta integration from the quark core surface (for color-flavor locked cores) or from deep within the core (for non color-flavor locked cores) to the point of charge neutrality in the crust, varying the derivative of the electron chemical potential at the starting point. Analytical approximations are derived for the pure electron atmosphere, thin crusts treated as test particles, and cold charge-neutral crusts, and these are compared to the full numerical solutions.

## Key numbers

- The electric field at the quark surface can reach up to 10^18 V/cm.
- The maximum crust mass for a strange star is approximately 2 × 10^−5 M_⊙ when the inner crust reaches neutron drip density (4 × 10^11 g/cm^3).
- For a cold crust with eφ_e(R_S) = 30 MeV and a crust composed of ^56Fe, the gap width is given by z_gap ≈ 170.5 (ρ/ρ_D)^(−1/3) fm − 167 (eφ_e(R_S)/30 MeV)^(−1) fm, where ρ_D = 4 × 10^11 g/cm^3.
- At zero temperature, the gap width for a crust at neutron drip density with eφ_e(R_S) = 30 MeV is approximately 3.5 fm (from Fig. 5, reading the BPS curve at ρ/ρ_D = 1).
- For eφ_e(R_S) = 20 MeV, the gap width goes to zero before reaching neutron drip density, limiting the maximum crust density to approximately 0.1 ρ_D if a minimum gap of 200 fm is required to prevent strong interactions.
- The transmission coefficient for ions through the gap at low density follows log(τ) ≈ −2.5 × 10^4 [ (π/2)(ρ/ρ_D)^(−1/6) − 1.84 ] for the HZ2003 composition.
- Temperature effects on gap width become noticeable only above approximately 1 MeV (≈ 10^10 K), with negligible changes below 10^7 K.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the electron atmosphere and crust structure of strange stars by Alcock et al. (1986), Kettner et al. (1995), Huang (1997), Xu (1999), Cheng (2003), Usov (2004), and Jaikumar (2005) by including Newtonian gravity and pressure in the force balance, which previous treatments had neglected or only approximately included. The results show that the gap is narrower than found in those earlier works, implying that the maximum crust density is generally below neutron drip rather than reaching it. The analytical approximations derived here enable quick estimates of gap properties for future models of strange star crusts, including studies of crust mass, moment of inertia, and core-crust coupling such as those by Zdunik (2002).
