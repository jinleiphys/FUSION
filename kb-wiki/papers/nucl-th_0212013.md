# Separable Kernel of Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach for J=0,1

> The solution for the nucleon-nucleon T matrix in the framework of the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach for a two spin-one-half particle system with a separable kernel of interaction is analyzed. The explicit analytical connection between parameters of the separable kernel and low energy scattering parameters, deuteron binding energy and phase shifts is established.Covariant separable kernels for positive-energy partial channels with total angular momentum J=0 (1S0+, 3P0+) and J=1 (3S1+-3D1+, 1P1+, 3P1+) are constructed by using obtained relations.

# Separable Kernel of Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter Approach for J=0,1

## Key claim
The paper establishes an explicit analytical connection between the parameters of a rank-I separable kernel for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction in the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach and low-energy scattering parameters, deuteron binding energy, and phase shifts, but only for positive-energy partial channels with total angular momentum J=0 and J=1, and only for the specific covariant generalization of Yamaguchi-type form factors used.

## Method
The authors start from the partial-wave decomposed Bethe-Salpeter equation for the NN T matrix in the two-nucleon rest frame and assume a separable (rank I) form for the kernel. They use covariant generalizations of Yamaguchi functions for the S-wave, P-wave, and D-wave form factors, then derive analytic expressions for the function h(s) that appears in the T matrix. By imposing on-mass-shell normalization conditions that relate the T matrix to phase shifts and mixing parameters, and by using the effective range expansion for low-energy parameters, they solve systems of nonlinear equations to extract the kernel parameters (lambda and beta) that reproduce experimental data for each partial channel.

## Context
This work extends the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach with separable interactions, building on the Yamaguchi (1954) form factor method, to include P-wave and coupled S-D wave channels for NN scattering. The constructed separable kernels enable practical calculations of NN observables within a fully covariant framework, and the explicit parameter sets allow direct comparison with phase shift data from the SAID program and low-energy parameters from standard references (data compilation cited as "data"). The results provide a foundation for extending such separable kernel constructions to higher partial waves and for use in few-body and nuclear matter calculations within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism.
