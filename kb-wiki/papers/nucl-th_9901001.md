# Gauging of equations method. I. Electromagnetic currents of three distinguishable particles

> We present a general method for incorporating an external electromagnetic field into descriptions of few-body systems whose strong interactions are described by integral equations. In particular, we address the case where the integral equations are those of quantum field theory and effectively sum up an infinite number of Feynman diagrams. The method involves the idea of gauging the integral equations themselves, and results in electromagnetic amplitudes where an external photon is effectively coupled to every part of every strong interaction diagram in the model. Current conservation is therefore implemented in the way prescribed by quantum field theory. We apply our gauging procedure to the four-dimensional integral equations describing a system of three distinguishable relativistic particles. In this way we obtain the expressions needed to calculate all possible electromagnetic processes of the three-body system. An interesting aspect of our results is the natural appearance of a subtraction term needed to avoid the overcounting of diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method for constructing gauge-invariant electromagnetic currents for few-body systems whose strong interactions are described by integral equations, by "gauging" the equations themselves rather than attaching photons to individual Feynman diagrams. The method is claimed to work for any number of particles, but the paper explicitly restricts the detailed derivation to three distinguishable particles, with the case of identical particles deferred to a companion paper.

## Method

The authors start from the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two-body Green function and its kernel, then apply a "product rule" to obtain a linear integral equation for the gauged Green function \(G^\mu\) in terms of the gauged one-particle propagator \(d^\mu\) and the gauged two-body potential \(V^\mu\). This procedure is then extended to the four-dimensional integral equations for three distinguishable relativistic particles, yielding expressions for the electromagnetic vertex function \(\Gamma^\mu\) and the gauged three-body bound-state wave function \(\psi^\mu\) that automatically satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

* The two-body Ward-Takahashi identity is given as \(q_\mu G^\mu(k_1k_2;p_1p_2) = i\sum_{i=1}^2[e_i G(k_i-q;p_1p_2) - G(k_1k_2;p_i+q)e_i]\) (Eq. WT2b), where \(e_i\) is the charge of particle \(i\) and \(q\) is the photon momentum.
* For nucleons, the one-particle electromagnetic vertex function to lowest order in the strong interaction is \(\ggama_i^\mu = e_i\gamma^\mu\), where \(\gamma^\mu\) is a Dirac matrix and \(e_i = \frac{1}{2}[1+\tau_3^{(i)}]e_p\) with \(e_p\) the proton charge.
* The two-body bound-state pole behavior is given by \(G(p_1'p_2',p_1p_2) \sim i\, \psi_P(p_1'p_2')\bar{\psi}_P(p_1p_2)/(P^2-m^2)\) as \(P^2\rightarrow m^2\), where \(m\) is the mass of the bound two-body system.

## Context

This work extends the result of Gross and Riska (1988) for two-nucleon conserved currents to systems of three or more particles, where no rigorous derivation previously existed. The method is applied to the four-dimensional three-body equations previously pursued numerically by Rupp and Tjon (1990), and to the four-dimensional version of the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations (1967). The paper provides the theoretical foundation for subsequent three-dimensional reductions in the spectator formalism (Gross, 1982) and for applications to the \(\pi N\) and \(\pi NN\) systems, and enables gauge-invariant calculations of electromagnetic form factors of the three-nucleon bound state (\(^3\)H) and processes such as \(Nd\rightarrow Nd\gamma\) and \(\gamma ^3\)H\(\rightarrow NNN\).
