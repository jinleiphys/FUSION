# Bounding the ground-state energy of a many-body system with the differential method

> This paper promotes the differential method as a new fruitful strategy for estimating a ground-state energy of a many-body system. The case of an arbitrary number of attractive Coulombian particles is specifically studied and we make some favorable comparison of the differential method to the existing approaches that rely on variational principles. A bird's-eye view of the treatment of more general interactions is also given.

## Key claim

The differential method provides both upper and lower bounds on the ground-state energy of a many-body system using a single trial wavefunction, without requiring any integrals, but it is restricted to systems whose ground-state wavefunction is node-free (non-negative) in configuration space, which excludes fermionic ground-states with antisymmetric spatial wavefunctions.

## Method

The method constructs a trial wavefunction as a product of two-body functions φ(q_N) = ∏_{i<j} φ_{ij}(r_{ij}), where each φ_{ij} solves the two-body Schrödinger equation with the corresponding pairwise potential v_{ij}. The local energy E_loc^{[φ]}(q_N) = Hφ/φ is then computed analytically; its infimum and supremum over configuration space provide rigorous lower and upper bounds on the exact ground-state energy E_0, respectively. For Coulombian systems, the local energy reduces to a sum of two-body energies plus angular terms involving cosines of interparticle angles, whose extrema can be found by standard optimization.

## Key numbers

- For N identical attractive Coulombian particles in D=3, the differential method gives E_0 ≤ −(ε_0/6(D−1)²) N(N−1)(N+1) with ε_0 = mg², compared to the variational upper bound E_0 ≤ −ε_0 (25/512) N(N−1)² from Levy-Leblond (1969)
- For N→∞ identical particles in D=3, the differential method yields −1/18 ≃ −0.0556 ≲ E_0/N³ ≲ −0.0542
- For three particles with masses (m,1,1) and unit attractive Coulomb couplings in D=3: when m=0.05, the differential method gives −0.34922 ≤ E_0 ≤ −0.29989; when m=1, it gives −1.1250 ≤ E_0 ≤ −1.0625; when m=20, it gives −2.1094 ≤ E_0 ≤ −1.6786
- For three identical particles (m=1), the differential method yields sup_{Q_3}F_3 = 3/2 (equilateral triangle) and inf_{Q_3}F_3 = 1 (aligned configuration), where F_N is the sum of cosines of interparticle angles

## Context

This work extends the differential method, previously applied to few-body systems, to arbitrary N-body systems with pairwise interactions, specifically Coulombian potentials. It provides a simpler alternative to the optimized variational methods of Basdevant (1990, 1993) and Benslama (1998), which require integral computations and are limited to N≤4. The method enables systematic lower bounds for large N that improve upon the Hall-Post inequality of Basdevant (1990), and can be extended to non-Coulombian interactions such as power-law potentials relevant for quark models (Nussinov 1983, Richard 2001) or short-range atomic interactions.
