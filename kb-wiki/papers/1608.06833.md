# On Friedrichs Model with Two Continuum States

> The Friedrichs model with one discrete state coupled to more than one continuum is studied. The exact eigenstates for the full Hamiltonian can be solved explicitly. The discrete state is found to generate more than one virtual state pole or more than one pair of resonance poles in different Riemann sheets in different situations. The form factors could also generate new states on different sheets. All these states can appear in the generalized completeness relation.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Friedrichs model to one discrete state coupled to two continuum states with different thresholds, showing that the discrete state can generate more than one virtual state pole or more than one pair of resonance poles on different Riemann sheets, depending on the relative positions of the bare energy and thresholds; the form factors can also generate additional poles on various sheets.

## Method

The authors solve the Friedrichs model Hamiltonian exactly for one discrete state coupled to two continua with thresholds \(a_1\) and \(a_2\) (\(a_2 > a_1\)), using an ansatz for the eigenstate and deriving the function \(\eta(x)\) whose zeros give the discrete eigenvalues. They analyze the pole positions of \(1/\eta(x)\) on the four-sheeted Riemann surface for weak couplings, using specific form factors \(G_1(\omega) = \sqrt{\omega - a_1}/(\omega + \zeta_1)\) and \(G_2(\omega) = \sqrt{\omega - a_2}/(\omega + \zeta_2)\) to illustrate the general behavior. They then generalize the completeness relation of Petrosky, Prigogine, and Tasaki (1991) to include all discrete states on unphysical sheets.

## Key numbers

- Thresholds are set with \(a_1 \ge 0\) and \(a_2 > 0\), with \(a_2 > a_1\).
- For \(\omega_0 < a_1\) and small couplings, the discrete state generates one bound state on the first sheet and one virtual state pole on each of the second, third, and fourth sheets.
- For \(a_1 < \omega_0 < a_2\), the discrete state generates a pair of resonance poles on the second sheet and a virtual state pole on the third sheet (for the example form factors).
- For \(\omega_0 > a_2\), turning on \(\lambda_2\) first then \(\lambda_1\) yields resonance poles on the third and fourth sheets; increasing \(\lambda_1\) can cause the fourth-sheet pole to cross the cut to the second sheet.

## Context

This work extends the original Friedrichs model (Friedrichs, 1948) from one continuum to two continua, building on prior studies of the single-continuum case (Xiao, 2016) where a discrete state generates a bound state and a virtual state or a pair of resonance poles. It is motivated by hadron spectroscopy problems such as the coupled-channel model (Pennington, 2007; Coito, 2012; Zhou, 2013) and the interpretation of \(\sigma\) and \(\kappa\) resonances (Xiao, 2000; Zheng, 2003), where discrete states couple strongly to multiple thresholds. The results enable a systematic classification of poles on multi-sheeted Riemann surfaces and provide a framework for including all virtual and resonance states in a generalized completeness relation, applicable to phenomenological studies of hadron-loop effects.
