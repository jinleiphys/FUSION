# The interplay of single-particle and collective motions in the low-lying states of $^{21}_Λ$Ne with quadrupole-octupole correlations

> The beyond mean-field approach for low-lying hypernuclear states is extended by mixing the configurations associated with both single-particle and quadrupole-octupole collective excitations within the generator coordinate method based on a covariant density functional theory. The method is demonstrated in the application to the low-lying states of $^{21}_Λ$Ne, where the configurations with the $Λ$ hyperon occupying the first ($Λ_s$) and second ($Λ_p$) lowest-energy states are considered. The results indicate that the positive-parity states are dominated by the $α+^{12}$C+$α+Λ_s$ structure. In contrast, the low-lying negative-parity states are dominated by a strong admixture of $α+^{16}$O+$Λ_s$ structure and $α+^{12}$C+$α+Λ_p$ structure due to the inclusion of octupole correlations. As a result, the low-lying negative-parity states become much lower than what is expected from the previous studies without the mixing, and the electric multipole transition strengths are significantly quenched.

## Key claim

The mixing of configurations where the Λ hyperon occupies either the first (Λ_s) or second (Λ_p) lowest-energy state, combined with quadrupole-octupole collective excitations, significantly lowers the negative-parity states of ²¹_ΛNe compared to previous studies without such mixing, and quenches the electric multipole transition strengths.

## Method

The authors extended the generator coordinate method (GCM) for hypernuclei by mixing configurations associated with both single-particle excitations of the Λ hyperon and quadrupole-octupole collective excitations of the nuclear core, based on a covariant density functional theory with the PC-F1 nucleon-nucleon interaction and the PCY-S2 ΛN interaction. Intrinsic states with the Λ hyperon in the Λ_s or Λ_p orbital were generated under constraints on quadrupole (β₂₀) and octupole (β₃₀) deformation parameters, and broken symmetries (parity, particle number, angular momentum) were restored via projection operators. The weight functions for the final hypernuclear states were determined by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation, and electric multipole transition strengths were computed from the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energies of the positive-parity doublets (3/2⁺, 5/2⁺) are shifted down by about 0.4 MeV when mixing both Λ_s and Λ_p configurations, compared to the calculation with only Λ_s configurations.
- The excitation energies of the negative-parity doublets (1/2⁻, 3/2⁻) are reduced by about 3.2 MeV when mixing both configurations.
- The energy splitting between the (1/2⁻, 3/2⁻) doublet is 88 keV.
- The energy splitting between the positive-parity doublets (3/2⁺, 5/2⁺) is 18 keV, and between (7/2⁺, 9/2⁺) is 38 keV.
- The B(E2; 3/2⁺₁ → 1/2⁺₁) strength is 58.7 e²fm⁴ from the full HyperGCM calculation, compared to 59.0 e²fm⁴ from HyperGCM(Λ_s) and 108.7 e²fm⁴ from HyperGCM(Λ_p).
- The B(E2; 5/2⁻₁ → 1/2⁻₁) strength is 102.3 e²fm⁴ from the full HyperGCM calculation, compared to 140.2 e²fm⁴ from HyperGCM(Λ_s) and 65.9 e²fm⁴ from HyperGCM(Λ_p).
- The B(E3; 5/2⁻₁ → 1/2⁺₁) strength is 69.7 e²fm⁶ from the full HyperGCM calculation, compared to 205.3 e²fm⁶ from HyperGCM(Λ_s).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HyperGCM framework (Xia 2019) by mixing configurations with the Λ hyperon in both the Λ_s and Λ_p orbitals, which was not done in earlier studies that considered only one type of configuration (Mei 2016, Cui 2017). The approach goes beyond the axially-deformed GCM calculations for ²¹_ΛNe (Mei 2016) and the HyperAMD model (Isaka 2011, Isaka 2012) by including octupole correlations and the mixing of single-particle excitations. The results enable future comparisons with high-precision γ-ray spectroscopy data from facilities like J-PARC, and provide a framework for studying the interplay of single-particle and collective motions in other hypernuclei.
