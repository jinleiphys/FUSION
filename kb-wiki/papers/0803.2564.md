# Towards an Extended Microscopic Theory for the Upper fp-shell nuclei

> An extended SU(3) shell model that for the first time explicitly includes unique-parity levels is introduced. Shell-model calculations for the isotopes of $^{64}$Ge and $^{68}$Se are performed where valence nucleons beyond the N=28=Z core occupy levels of the normal parity upper-$fp$ shell ($f_{5/2},p_{3/2},p_{1/2}$) and the unique parity $g_{9/2}$ intruder configuration. The levels of the upper-$fp$ shell are handled within the framework of an m-scheme basis as well as its pseudo-SU(3) counterpart, and respectively, the $g_{9/2}$ as a single level and as a member for the complete $gds$ shell. It is demonstrated that the extended SU(3) approach allows one to better probe the effects of deformation and to account for many key properties of the system by using a highly truncated model space.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an extended SU(3) shell model that for the first time explicitly includes unique-parity intruder levels (the \(g_{9/2}\) orbital) alongside the normal-parity upper-\(fp\) shell, but the model's ability to reproduce the intruder-level occupancy is limited: the absolute occupancy values are underestimated by at least a factor of 3 in \(^{64}\)Ge and by even more in \(^{68}\)Se, suggesting the model space may need further expansion or that the pairing strengths require adjustment.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for \(^{64}\)Ge and \(^{68}\)Se using valence nucleons beyond a \(^{56}\)Ni core (\(N=28=Z\)) occupying the normal-parity upper-\(fp\) shell (\(f_{5/2}, p_{3/2}, p_{1/2}\)) and the unique-parity \(g_{9/2}\) intruder level. The normal-parity space was treated with an m-scheme basis and its pseudo-SU(3) counterpart, while the \(g_{9/2}\) was handled both as a single level and as part of the full \(gds\) shell. The Hamiltonian included spherical Nilsson single-particle energies, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, pairing interactions (identical-particle and proton-neutron, within and between shells), and two rotor-like terms (\(J^2\) and \(K_J^2\)) for fine-tuning, with parameters fitted for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

*   The occupancy probability for the \(g_{9/2}\) intruder level in the low-lying states of \(^{64}\)Ge is approximately 0.3 particles.
*   For \(^{64}\)Ge, the leading pseudo-SU(3) irrep contributes 50-60% of the wavefunction in the ground-state band; using only five irreps with the highest \(C_2\) value accounts for at least 70% and up to about 95% of the wavefunction for states in these bands.
*   For \(^{68}\)Se, the first eleven irreps with distinct \(\lambda\) and \(\mu\) account for 88-93% of the wavefunction in the ground-state band; the \(0_3^+\) state at 2.51 MeV is dominated (64%) by irreps with the biggest \(C_2\) value (180).
*   The extended SU(3) model for \(^{64}\)Ge uses 492 coupled irreps; for \(^{68}\)Se it uses 580 coupled irreps.
*   For \(^{64}\)Ge, the B(E2) transition strength for \(2_{g.s.}^+ \rightarrow 0_{g.s.}^+\) is 292.80 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) in the extended SU(3) model, compared to 253.91 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) from the realistic G-matrix in the full \(pf_{5/2}g_{9/2}\) space.
*   For \(^{68}\)Se, the B(E2) transition strength for \(2_{g.s.}^+ \rightarrow 0_{g.s.}^+\) is 354.17 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) in the extended SU(3) model, compared to 322.71 \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) from the realistic G-matrix in the full \(pf_{5/2}\) space.
*   The Hamiltonian parameters for \(^{64}\)Ge are \(G = 0.280\) MeV, \(\chi = 0.0176\) MeV, \(a = -0.002\) MeV, \(b = 0.020\) MeV; for \(^{68}\)Se they are \(G = 0.263\) MeV, \(\chi = 0.0152\) MeV, \(a = -0.002\) MeV, \(b = 0.000\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous SU(3) shell-model calculations (Elliott for light nuclei; Draayer and collaborators for pseudo-SU(3) in heavy nuclei) to the intermediate mass region (\(A=56\) to \(A=100\)), where the \(g_{9/2}\) intruder level is spectroscopically relevant and cannot be ignored or mimicked through renormalization. The calculations for the waiting-point \(N=Z\) nuclei \(^{64}\)Ge and \(^{68}\)Se, which are important in astrophysics (Schatz), demonstrate that a symmetry-based truncation scheme can reproduce many features of full-space realistic calculations (using a G-matrix interaction from Caurier and Abzouzi) while using a highly truncated model space. The results enable future applications of the extended SU(3) model to other upper-\(fp\) shell nuclei and open questions about the proper treatment of the unique-parity space, including whether the quasi-SU(3) concept (Vargas, 2001; Vargas, 2002) may also be applicable.
