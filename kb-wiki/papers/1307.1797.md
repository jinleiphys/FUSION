# Modification of the B Meson Mass in a Magnetic Field from QCD Sum Rules

> In this paper we extend the well known QCD sum rules used in the calculation of the mass of heavy mesons to estimate the modification of the charged B meson mass, m_B, in the presence of an external Abelian magnetic field, eB. Two simplifying limits were considered: the weak field limit in which the external field satisfies eB << m^2 (with m being any of the masses involved) and the strong field limit in which the field strength is small in comparison to the bottom quark mass (or the B meson mass) squared but it is large compared to the mass of the light quarks, i.e., m^2_{u,d} << eB << m^2_{b,B}. We found that m_B decreases with the magnetic field in the both of these limits.

## Key claim

The charged B meson mass decreases in the presence of an external Abelian magnetic field in both the weak field limit (eB much smaller than any mass squared involved) and the strong field limit (eB large compared to light quark masses but small compared to the bottom quark or B meson mass squared), with the effect being surprisingly sizable even for very weak fields.

## Method

The authors extended the QCD Spectral Sum Rules (QCDSR) method for two-point correlators to include a constant, homogeneous external magnetic field via two modifications: (1) replacing free quark propagators with the Schwinger proper-time propagator for fermions in a magnetic field, and (2) replacing the quark condensate with its magnetic-field-dependent value. In the weak field limit, the propagators were expanded in powers of eB/m² up to (eB/m²)², while in the strong field limit the light quark propagator was truncated to the lowest Landau level and the heavy quark propagator was expanded similarly to the weak field case.

## Key numbers

- In the weak field limit, for eB = 2 × 10⁻⁶ m_π² (approximately 4 × 10⁻⁸ GeV²) and q_⟂² = 1 GeV², the B meson mass decreases by about 10% relative to its vacuum value.
- In the strong field limit, for eB ≈ 75 m_π² (approximately 1.5 GeV²) and q_⟂² = 0.5 GeV², the B meson mass decrease is in the 10% to 20% range depending on field strength, perpendicular momentum, and QCDSR parameters.
- The vacuum B meson mass obtained from QCDSR was m_B = 5.25 GeV, with coupling f_B = 0.29 GeV, using quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-0.23)³ GeV, m_b = 4.24 GeV, and m_u,d ≈ 0.
- The Borel window for the vacuum case was M̄²_min ≈ 4 GeV² and M̄²_max ≈ 8 GeV² for √s₀ = 6.0 GeV.
- In the strong field limit, the valid range for eB was approximately 50 m_π² (≈ 1 GeV²) to 200 m_π² (≈ 4 GeV²).

## Context

This work extends the QCDSR method, originally developed by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and applied to heavy mesons by Reinders, Yazaki, and Rubinstein (1981) and Shuryak (1982), to include external magnetic fields. The results agree qualitatively with potential model calculations by Simonov, Kerbikov, and Andreichikov (2012) and with the expectation of Zeeman-like splitting observed for ρ mesons in magnetic fields by Hidaka and Yamamoto (2012) and Luschevskaya and Larina (2012). The findings enable future calculations for arbitrary magnetic field strengths and for other mesons without the same separation of scales present in B mesons.
