# Higher-order corrections to jet quenching

> We compute the inclusive jet spectrum in the presence of a dense QCD medium by going beyond the single parton energy loss approximation. We show that higher-order corrections are important yielding large logarithmic contributions that must be resummed to all orders. This reflects the fact that jet quenching is sensitive to fluctuations of the jet substructure.

## Key claim

Higher-order corrections to the inclusive jet spectrum in a dense QCD medium yield large logarithmic contributions that must be resummed to all orders, because jet quenching is sensitive to fluctuations of the jet substructure; the effect is controlled by the condition that the formation time of a splitting be smaller than the decoherence time, which itself must be smaller than the medium length.

## Method

The authors compute the inclusive jet spectrum in the presence of a dense QCD medium by going beyond the single parton energy loss approximation. They evaluate the phase-space for vacuum-like splittings that occur inside the medium and are resolved by it, imposing the constraint that the formation time be less than the decoherence time and that the decoherence time be less than the medium length. They then derive a non-linear evolution equation for the ratio of the full quenching factor to the single-parton quenching factor, using the coherent branching algorithm with strict angular ordering, and solve it numerically in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Key numbers

* Phase-space for higher-order corrections: \(\text{PS} = 2\bar\alpha \ln(R/\theta_c) [\ln(p_T/\omega_c) + (2/3)\ln(R/\theta_c)]\), where \(\bar\alpha = \alpha_s C_F/\pi\), \(R\) is the jet cone size, \(\theta_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}\) is the minimum characteristic angle, and \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2\) is the maximum medium-induced frequency.
* Naive phase-space estimate: \(\text{PS} = (\bar\alpha/2) \ln^2(p_T R^2 L)\); for \(L=5\) fm, \(R=0.3\) rad, \(p_T=500\) GeV, and \(\bar\alpha=0.2\), this gives \(\text{PS} \simeq 2.5\).
* Numerical solution parameters: \(\hat q = 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and \(L=3\) fm for the quark jet suppression factor shown in Fig. 1 (right).
* The quenching factor at leading order for a quark: \(\Q_q(p_T) \approx \exp[-2\bar\alpha L \sqrt{\pi n \hat q/p_T}]\), where \(n\) is the power index of the steeply falling vacuum spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the standard single-parton radiative energy loss approach to jet quenching, which is reviewed in Mehtar-Tani (2013) and Blaizot (2015), by including higher-order corrections from jet substructure fluctuations. It builds on earlier Monte Carlo studies by Milhano (2015) and Casalderrey-Solana (2016) that indicated the importance of substructure fluctuations for experimental data, while also accounting for color coherence and interference effects that are neglected in most Monte Carlo implementations. The results enable future quantitative estimates that include in-cone medium radiation, momentum broadening, and total quenching of jet constituents.
