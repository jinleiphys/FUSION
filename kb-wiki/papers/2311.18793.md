# Finite-volume scattering on the left-hand cut

> The two-particle finite-volume scattering formalism derived by LΟΦscher and generalized in many subsequent works does not hold for energies far enough below the two-particle threshold to reach the nearest left-hand cut. The breakdown of the formalism is signaled by the fact that a real scattering amplitude is predicted in a regime where it should be complex. In this work, we address this limitation by deriving an extended formalism that includes the nearest branch cut, arising from single particle exchange. We focus on two-nucleon ($NN \to NN$) scattering, for which the cut arises from pion exchange, but give expressions for any system with a single channel of identical particles. The new result takes the form of a modified quantization condition that can be used to constrain an intermediate K-matrix in which the cut is removed. In a second step, integral equations, also derived in this work, must be used to convert the K-matrix to the physical scattering amplitude. We also show how the new formalism reduces to the standard approach when the $N \to N œÄ$ coupling is set to zero.

## Key claim

The standard LΟΦscher finite-volume scattering formalism breaks down for energies below the two-particle threshold that reach the nearest left-hand cut (e.g., at \(s = (2M_N)^2 - M_\pi^2\) for nucleon-nucleon scattering), because it predicts a real scattering amplitude where it should be complex; this work derives an extended formalism that includes the nearest branch cut from single-particle exchange, valid for \((2M_N)^2 - (2M_\pi)^2 < s < (2M_N + M_\pi)^2\), but does not treat two-pion exchange cuts.

## Method

The authors derive a modified quantization condition by separating out pion-exchange contributions in all diagrams and avoiding the problematic on-shell replacement of internal momenta that fails near the left-hand cut. This yields an intermediate K-matrix (denoted \(\overline {\mathcal K}^{\sf os}\)) from which the cut is removed, and integral equations are derived to convert this K-matrix to the physical scattering amplitude. The formalism is developed for any single-channel system of identical particles, with explicit focus on two-nucleon (\(NN \to NN\)) scattering where the cut arises from single-pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- The standard formalism's range of validity for \(NN\) systems is \((2 M_N)^2 - M_\pi^2 < s < (2 M_N + M_\pi)^2\); the extended formalism applies for \((2 M_N)^2 - (2 M_\pi)^2 < s < (2 M_N + M_\pi)^2\).
- The left-hand cut branch point for \(NN \to NN\) from single-pion exchange is at \(s = (2 M_N)^2 - M_\pi^2\).
- The two-pion exchange left-hand cut (not treated) starts at \(s = 4 M_N^2 - 4 M_\pi^2\).
- The phase-space volume factor is \(\rho(s) = k^\star_{\sf os} / (16 \pi \sqrt{s})\) with \(k^\star_{\sf os} = \sqrt{s/4 - M_N^2}\).
- The coupling constant \(g\) is defined as \(g \equiv \lim_{q^2 \to M_\pi^2} \mathcal G(q^2)\) for the \(N \to N \pi\) vertex.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume scattering formalism of LΟΦscher (1986) and its generalizations (Rummukainen 1995, Kim 2005, Christ 2005) to energies below the two-particle threshold where the left-hand cut invalidates the standard approach, a problem highlighted by Green (2021). It complements recent three-particle finite-volume formalisms (Hansen 2014, Blanton 2020) and relates to the work of Dawid (2023) on analytically continuing three-particle relations below threshold. The results enable lattice QCD calculations of systems such as \(D D^* \to D D^*\) (relevant to the \(T_{cc}(3875)^+\) tetraquark) to use finite-volume energies that overlap the left-hand cut, addressing concerns raised by Du (2023).
