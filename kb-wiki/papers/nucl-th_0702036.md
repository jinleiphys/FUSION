# Thermalization of quark-gluon matter by 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 elastic scatterings

> Thermalization of quark-gluon matter is studied with a transport equation that includes contributions of 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 elastic scatterings. Thermalization time is related to the squared amplitudes for the elastic scatterings that are calculated in perturbative QCD.

# Thermalization of quark-gluon matter by 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 elastic scatterings

## Key claim
Thermalization of quark-gluon matter proceeds faster when more types of 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 elastic scatterings are included, with the thermalization time depending on the squared amplitudes of these scatterings calculated in perturbative QCD. The claim is conditional on the specific set of parton scatterings considered, and the author notes that contributions from mixed quark-gluon scatterings (e.g., qg → qg, qqg → qqg) have not yet been included.

## Method
The author solves a transport equation that includes collision terms for both 2-to-2 and 3-to-3 elastic scatterings among quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, with squared amplitudes calculated in perturbative QCD at tree level. Initial anisotropic parton distributions for central Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV are obtained from HIJING Monte Carlo simulations, and the transport equation is evolved numerically until local momentum isotropy is achieved. Quark and gluon propagators are regularized with a screening mass to remove Coulomb exchange divergence.

## Key numbers
- Gluon number density of 19.4 fm⁻³ from HIJING simulation for central Au-Au at √sNN = 200 GeV
- For quark matter governed by qq → qq and qqq → qqq elastic scatterings: thermalization time ≈ 1.8 fm/c
- For quark matter including antiquark interactions via qq̄ → qq̄, qqq̄ → qqq̄, and qq̄q̄ → qq̄q̄: thermalization time ≈ 1.55 fm/c
- For gluon matter with gg → gg and ggg → ggg elastic scatterings: thermalization time ≈ 0.45 fm/c
- Initial gluon distribution function given by f(p) = [17.1 (2π)^(1.5)] / [g_G π R_A² Y(|p|/cosh(y) + 0.3)] × exp(-|p|/(0.9 cosh(y)) - (|p| tanh(y))²/8), with R_A = 6.4 fm, g_G = 16, and rapidity range -5 ≤ y ≤ 5

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies (Xu, 2006a,b,c) that first included 3-to-3 elastic scatterings for quark matter (qqq → qqq) and gluon matter (ggg → ggg), and now adds quark-quark-antiquark and quark-antiquark-antiquark scatterings. The study demonstrates that multi-parton elastic scatterings are important for thermalization beyond the 2-body scatterings considered in earlier work by Shuryak and Muller (1990s), Wong (1990s), and others. The results enable future inclusion of mixed quark-gluon scatterings (qg → qg, qqg → qqg, qq̄g → qq̄g, qgg → qgg), which the author expects to further shorten thermalization times.
