# The role of electron polarization on nuclear spin diffusion

> Dynamic nuclear polarization (DNP) is capable of boosting signals in nuclear magnetic resonance by orders of magnitude by creating out-of-equilibrium nuclear spin polarization. The diffusion of nuclear spin polarization in the vicinity of paramagnetic dopants is a crucial step for DNP and remains yet not well understood. In this Letter, we show that the polarization of the electron spin controls the rate of proton spin diffusion in a DNP sample at 1.2 K and 7 T; at increasingly high electron polarization, spin diffusion vanishes. We rationalize our results using a 2 nucleus - 1 electron model and Lindblad s Master equation, which generalizes preexisting models in the literature and qualitatively accounts for the experimental observed spin diffusion dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the rate of proton spin diffusion in a DNP sample at 1.2 K and 7 T is controlled by the electron spin polarization, with spin diffusion vanishing at increasingly high electron polarization; this effect is rationalized using a 2-nucleus-1-electron model and Lindblad's master equation that generalizes preexisting models and qualitatively accounts for the observed dynamics.

## Method

The authors performed an extension of the hyperpolarization resurgence (HypRes) experiment on a sample of 50 mM TEMPOL in H2O:D2O:D8-glycerol at 1.2 K and 7.05 T, where the electron polarization was lowered to a stable level by microwave irradiation to compare spin diffusion efficiency at various electron polarizations. They constructed a theoretical model treating the electron as a random fluctuating field and describing nuclear spin dynamics with Lindblad's master equation, calculating the transition rate probability between the |αβ⟩ and |βα⟩ states of a pair of coupled nuclei subject to the dipolar field of an electron spin.

## Key numbers

- Electron polarization at Boltzmann equilibrium: 99.93% at 1.2 K and 7.05 T; at 4.2 K it is 83%
- Boltzmann equilibrium proton polarization: 0.60% under the experimental conditions
- DNP polarization during preparation: on the order of +70% and -70%
- Spin diffusion at the interface between hidden and visible spins is approximately 7 times faster when electron polarization is about 50% compared to Boltzmann equilibrium (99.93%)
- The radius of the hidden spin reservoir (interface between hidden and visible spins): 0.9 nm, defined where proton spins have a coupling of 100 kHz with the electron
- Nuclear interdistance a: 0.66 nm; nuclear broadening due to nucleus-nucleus interactions: 18 kHz; electron correlation time 1/Γc: 0.5 μs
- Sample heating by microwave irradiation: less than 10 mK

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HypRes experiment (Stern 2021) which revealed strong temperature dependence of proton spin diffusion between 1.2 and 4.2 K, and confirms the hypothesis that electron polarization is the key factor. The model generalizes the perturbative approach of Horvitz (1971) and, when electron polarization approaches unity, becomes equivalent to static electron models such as those of Karabanov (2014, 2018) and Hovav (2012). The findings could contribute to understanding spin diffusion in dissolution DNP and other DNP experiments, including MAS-DNP and 13C hyperpolarization with color centers in diamonds.
