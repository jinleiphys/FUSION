# Relativistic baryon-baryon interactions in chiral perturbation theory

> We report on the recent studies of leading order baryon-baryon interactions in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. In the strangeness $S=0$ sector, one can achieve a rather good description of the Nijmegen $np$ phase shifts with angular momenta $J\leq 1$, particularly the $^1S_0$ and $^3P_0$ partial waves, comparable with the next-to-leading order (NLO) heavy baryon approach. In the strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon sector, the best fit of the 36 scattering data is similar to the sophisticated phenomenological models and the NLO heavy baryon approach.

## Key claim

The authors report that leading-order covariant (relativistic) baryon chiral perturbation theory can describe Nijmegen neutron-proton phase shifts for angular momenta \(J \leq 1\) with quality comparable to the next-to-leading-order heavy baryon approach, but only for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_0\) partial waves is the description "rather good"; for other \(J=1\) waves the LO relativistic and non-relativistic results are similar.

## Method

The authors construct leading-order baryon-baryon interactions from Lorentz-invariant chiral Lagrangians, including four-baryon contact terms and one-meson-exchange contributions. They keep the full Dirac spinor structure and iterate the potentials using the (generalized) Kadyshevsky equation, a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with a separable exponential regulator. The low-energy constants are fitted to Nijmegen \(np\) phase shifts (for \(S=0\)) and to 36 hyperon-nucleon scattering data (for \(S=-1\)).

## Key numbers

- For the \(np\) channel, fitting to \(J=0,1\) Nijmegen phase shifts with laboratory kinetic energy \(E_\mathrm{lab} \leq 100\) MeV yields a minimum \(\tilde{\chi}^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} \sim 2.0\) at cutoff \(\Lambda_F = 750\) MeV.
- The cutoff \(\Lambda_F\) is varied from 500 MeV to 1000 MeV without qualitatively changing the overall picture.
- For the hyperon-nucleon sector, the best fit to 36 scattering data gives \(\chi^2 \approx 16\) at cutoff \(\Lambda_F = 600\) MeV, with constraints \(a^{\Lambda p}_{1S0} = -2.44\) fm, \(a^{\Lambda p}_{3S1} = -1.32\) fm, and \(a^{\Sigma^+ p}_{3S1} > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the covariant (relativistic) chiral perturbation theory, previously successful in the one-baryon sector, to the two-baryon sector, where most existing chiral potentials are based on the non-relativistic heavy baryon approach (e.g., Epelbaum (1999), Machleidt (2011)). The results are compared with the phenomenological NSC97f (Rijken, 1998) and Jülich 04 (Haidenbauer, 2005) potentials. The relativistic potentials can be directly used in relativistic many-body calculations such as Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, and the authors note that higher-order calculations are needed to test convergence.
