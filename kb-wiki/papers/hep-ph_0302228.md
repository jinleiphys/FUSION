# Equivalence of Minkowski and Euclidean Field Theory Solutions

> We consider the correspondence between solutions of non-gravitational field theories formulated in Euclidean space-time and Minkowski space-time. Infinitely many "Euclidean" spaces can be obtained from M4 via a group of transformations in which the Wick rotation is a special case. We then discuss how the solutions of gauge field theories formulated in these "Euclidean" spaces have a one-to-one correspondence with the solutions of field theories formulated in Minkowski space-time, provided we avoid the one-point compactification into S4 = E4 U infinity.

# Equivalence of Minkowski and Euclidean Field Theory Solutions

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that solutions of gauge field theories formulated in Euclidean space-time have a one-to-one correspondence with solutions in Minkowski space-time, provided that the asymptotic conditions imposed in Euclidean space do not amount to a one-point compactification into \(S^4 = E^4 \cup \infty\). When such compactification is performed, the homeomorphism between base spaces fails and the correspondence is lost.

## Method
The authors use principal fiber bundle theory to describe gauge theories on both Minkowski base space \(M^4\) and Euclidean base space \(E^4\). They show that a Wick rotation (a special case of a more general conformal transformation \(\tau = \alpha_1 t + \alpha_2\) with \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2 \in \mathbb{C}\)) provides a homeomorphic mapping \(f\) between \(M^4\) and \(E^4\), which induces a pullback bundle over \(E^4\) with the same typical fiber as the original Minkowski bundle. The cross-sections of the two bundles are homeomorphic, establishing the one-to-one correspondence of solutions, provided the base spaces remain contractable and the one-point compactification into \(S^4\) is avoided.

## Key numbers
- The generalized Wick rotation is given by \(\tau = \alpha_1 t + \alpha_2\) with \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2 \in \mathbb{C}\); the standard Wick rotation corresponds to \(\alpha_1 = e^{i\pi/2}\) and \(\alpha_2 = 0\).
- The one-point compactification that breaks the correspondence is \(S^4 = E^4 \cup \infty\).
- Compactification of \(M^4\) yields a topologically different space \(S^1 \times S^3\) with a flat Lorentz metric.

## Context
This work extends the standard Wick rotation technique by showing it is a special case of a larger group of conformal transformations, and clarifies the topological conditions under which Euclidean and Minkowski solutions are equivalent. It directly addresses the Gribov ambiguity (Gribov, 1978) and Singer's theorem (Singer, 1978) on the non-existence of global sections for principal bundles over \(S^4\) with compact nonabelian gauge groups. The paper enables a rigorous understanding of when Euclidean techniques (such as instanton calculations) can be reliably mapped back to Minkowski space-time, and warns against the loss of correspondence when one-point compactification is imposed.
