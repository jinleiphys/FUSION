# Interaction Matrix Element Fluctuations in Ballistic Quantum Dots: Random Wave Model

> We study matrix element fluctuations of the two-body screened Coulomb interaction and of the one-body surface charge potential in ballistic quantum dots. For chaotic dots, we use a normalized random wave model to obtain analytic expansions for matrix element variances and covariances in the limit of large kL (where k is the Fermi wave number and L the linear size of the dot). These leading-order analytical results are compared with exact numerical results. Both two-body and one-body matrix elements are shown to follow strongly non-Gaussian distributions, despite the Gaussian random nature of the single-electron wave functions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for ballistic quantum dots with finite Thouless conductance (in the experimentally relevant range \(30 \le kL \le 70\)), the variances of two-body interaction matrix elements converge only logarithmically slowly to their asymptotic \(kL \to \infty\) limits, and the matrix element distributions remain strongly non-Gaussian despite the Gaussian random nature of the single-electron wave functions.

## Method

The authors use a normalized random wave model (Berry's conjecture) for chaotic ballistic quantum dots, where each random wave function is explicitly normalized to unity within the dot area. They derive analytic expansions for the normalized intensity correlator \(\tilde C(\vec r,\vec r')\) to leading order in \(1/kL\), and use this correlator to compute variances and covariances of two-body screened Coulomb interaction matrix elements (diagonal \(v_{\alpha\beta}\), self-overlap \(v_{\alpha\alpha}\), and off-diagonal \(v_{\alpha\beta\gamma\delta}\)) and one-body surface charge potential matrix elements. The analytical results are compared with exact numerical simulations of normalized random waves in disk and elliptical geometries.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order variance of diagonal two-body matrix elements \(v_{\alpha\beta}\) scales as \(\overline{\delta v_{\alpha\beta}^2} = \Delta^2 \frac{3}{\pi} \left(\frac{2}{\beta}\right)^2 \frac{\ln kL + b_g}{(kL)^2}\), where \(b_g = -0.1\) for a disk geometry and \(\beta=1\) (real wave functions, time-reversal symmetry present).
- For the self-overlap \(v_{\alpha\alpha}\) variance, the combinatorial factor is \(c_1 = 24\) for \(\beta=1\) and \(c_2 = 4\) for \(\beta=2\), with \(b'_g = -2.25\) for a disk.
- The ratio \(\overline{\delta v^2_{\alpha\alpha}} / \overline{\delta v^2_{\alpha\beta}}\) converges to the asymptotic value of 6 only logarithmically slowly, remaining near 4.5 at \(kL = 30\) and near 5 at \(kL = 70\) for \(\beta=1\).
- The effective number of basis states is \(N_{\rm eff} \approx 1.04 kL\) for a disk geometry.
- Shape dependence of the \(v_{\alpha\beta}\) variance is weak: changing the aspect ratio of an elliptical dot by a factor of 16 changes the variance by only about \(5-6\%\) for the experimental range of \(kL\).

## Context

This work extends the study of interaction matrix element fluctuations beyond the diffusive dot regime, where Blanter et al. (1997) and Mirlin (2000) derived variances scaling as \(\Delta^2/g_T^2\) for two-body matrix elements and \(\Delta^2/g_T\) for one-body matrix elements. The paper provides the first systematic analytic treatment for ballistic dots using a normalized random wave model, correcting the unnormalized Berry (1977) random wave model which fails to satisfy wave function normalization. The results enable quantitative predictions for peak spacing fluctuations in Coulomb blockade experiments (where discrepancies with the constant interaction plus random matrix theory model have been observed by Sivan et al. (1996), Simmel et al. (1997), Patel et al. (1998), and Luscher et al. (2001)) and for spectral scrambling as electrons are added to a dot.
