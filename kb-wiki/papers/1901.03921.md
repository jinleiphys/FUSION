# Pion Valence Quark Distribution from Matrix Element Calculated in Lattice QCD

> We present the first exploratory lattice QCD calculation of the pion valence quark distribution extracted from spatially separated current-current correlations in coordinate space. We show that an antisymmetric combination of vector and axial-vector currents provides direct information on the pion valence quark distribution. Using the collinear factorization approach, we calculate the perturbative tree-level kernel for this current combination and extract the pion valence distribution. The main goal of this article is to demonstrate the efficacy of this general lattice QCD approach in the reliable extraction of parton distributions. With controllable power corrections and a good understanding of the lattice systematics, this method has the potential to serve as a complementary to the many efforts to extract parton distributions in global analyses from experimentally measured cross sections. We perform our calculation on an ensemble of 2+1 flavor QCD using the isotropic-clover fermion action, with lattice dimensions $32^3\times 96$ at a lattice spacing \mbox{$a=0.127$ fm} and the quark mass equivalent to a pion mass $m_π\simeq 416$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an antisymmetric combination of vector and axial-vector current-current correlators computed in lattice QCD can be factorized to directly yield the pion valence quark distribution, but only as an exploratory calculation at a single lattice spacing and unphysical pion mass of 416 MeV, with the method requiring validation at the physical point and with controlled systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compute four-point correlation functions of spatially separated vector and axial-vector currents on a 2+1 flavor isotropic-clover fermion ensemble with lattice dimensions 32^3 × 96. They extract the Lorentz-invariant form factor T₁ from the antisymmetric current combination, perform a Fourier transform in Ioffe time ω = p·ξ, and use the leading-order perturbative kernel C₁^(q(0)) = (2x/π²)cos(xω) to relate the Fourier-transformed form factor directly to the valence quark distribution q_v(ẋ) = π² T̃₁(ẋ, ξ²). The calculation uses Jacobi-smearing combined with momentum-smearing techniques to improve overlap with boosted pion states at momenta up to 1.525 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.127 fm
- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 416 MeV
- Lattice volume: 32³ × 96
- Number of gauge configurations: 490
- Pion momenta used: 0.610 GeV, 0.915 GeV, 1.220 GeV, and 1.525 GeV
- Jacobi smearing parameters: σ = 4.0, n_σ = 50
- Auxiliary quark mass set to the strange quark mass

## Context

This work extends the "good lattice cross sections" approach proposed by Ma and Qiu (2014, 2017) to the specific case of the pion valence distribution, providing a first exploratory calculation that complements global fitting efforts by collaborations such as MMHT, CT, NNPDF, and JAM. The method offers an alternative to quasi-PDFs (Ji 2013) and pseudo-PDFs (Radyushkin 2017) for extracting x-dependent parton distributions from lattice QCD, with the advantage of controllable power corrections of order ξ²Λ²_QCD. Future work requires calculations at the physical pion mass, multiple lattice spacings, and inclusion of next-to-leading order perturbative matching to achieve precision comparable to experimental extractions from Drell-Yan data.
