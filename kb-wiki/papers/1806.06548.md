# Chiral geometry and rotational structure for $^{130}$Cs in the projected shell model

> The projected shell model with configuration mixing for nuclear chirality is developed and applied to the observed rotational bands in the chiral nucleus $^{130}$Cs. For the chiral bands, the energy spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities are well reproduced. The chiral geometry illustrated in the $K~plot$ and the $azithumal~plot$ is confirmed to be stable against the configuration mixing. The other rotational bands are also described in the same framework.

## Key claim

The projected shell model with configuration mixing for nuclear chirality reproduces the observed chiral doublet bands in $^{130}$Cs, and the chiral geometry illustrated by the $K$ plot and azimuthal plot is confirmed to be stable against the inclusion of configuration mixing that was neglected in the earlier single-configuration calculation of Chen et al. (2017).

## Method

The authors develop a projected shell model that includes configuration mixing by constructing a basis of two-quasiparticle states projected onto good particle number and angular momentum, then diagonalizing a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian. The chiral geometry is examined using the $K$ plot (distributions of angular momentum components on the three intrinsic axes) and the azimuthal plot (distributions of the polar and azimuthal angles of the total angular momentum in the intrinsic frame), obtained from the collective wave functions in the generator coordinate method. The calculation uses 60 positive-parity and 68 negative-parity two-quasiparticle configurations built from orbitals in the $N=4$ and $N=5$ major shells, with a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 3.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters fixed at ($\beta = 0.20$, $\gamma = 30^\circ$), consistent with the deformation ($0.19, 39^\circ$) used in a prior projected shell model calculation by Gao et al. (2006).
- Quasiparticle energy cutoff $E_\nu + E_\pi \le 3.5$ MeV for selecting two-quasiparticle configurations.
- Configuration space contains 60 positive-parity and 68 negative-parity two-quasiparticle configurations; the projected basis dimension for spin $I$ is $30(2I+1)$ for positive parity and $34(2I+1)$ for negative parity.
- For the chiral doublet bands A and B at spins $I=14$ and $15~\hbar$, the most probable value for $K_i$ appears at $K_i \sim 13~\hbar$ for band A, indicating development of collective rotation around the intermediate axis.
- For the azimuthal plot at $I=14,15~\hbar$, band A shows peaks at $(\theta \sim 75^\circ, \phi \sim 35^\circ)$ and $(\theta \sim 75^\circ, \phi \sim 145^\circ)$; band B shows peaks at $(\theta \sim 70^\circ, \phi \sim 40^\circ)$ and $(\theta \sim 70^\circ, \phi \sim 140^\circ)$.

## Context

This work extends the angular-momentum-projected approach of Chen et al. (2017), which studied $^{128}$Cs with a single fixed configuration, by incorporating configuration mixing within the projected shell model framework. It applies the method to $^{130}$Cs, a nucleus where chiral doublet bands were first identified by Starosta et al. (2001). The results confirm that the chiral geometry persists when configuration mixing is included, and the same framework simultaneously describes the other rotational bands (bands C and D) in the nucleus. This enables future microscopic studies of chirality in other mass regions using the projected shell model with configuration mixing.
