# Transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic limit

> The calculation of the transport coefficients of the strong quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), i.e. sheer viscosity $η$, heat conductivity $\gk$ and bulk viscosity $ζ$, in the first Chapman-Enskog approximation is presented. Their formulation in terms of two-fold integrals depending on the particle interaction, known as relativistic omega integrals, is derived and their evaluation in the ultrarelativistic limit is worked out assuming a cross section independent of the relative and total momentum of two colliding particles. We find a suppression of the bulk viscosity and a pronounced temperature dependence for the sheer viscosity and heat conductivity. However, at high temperatures, they scale with the third and second power of the temperature respectively as expected. Furthermore, we find that all results in this ultrarelativistic expansion are dominated by the leading order contribution.

# Transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic limit

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for shear viscosity, heat conductivity, and bulk viscosity of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma in the ultrarelativistic limit using the first Chapman-Enskog approximation, under the assumption that the differential cross section is independent of the relative and total momentum of two colliding particles. The authors find that all results in this ultrarelativistic expansion are dominated by the leading order contribution, with the bulk viscosity suppressed relative to the other coefficients.

## Method

The transport coefficients are formulated in terms of relativistic omega integrals, which are two-fold integrals over the relative momentum and scattering angle that encode the particle interaction through the differential cross section. The omega integrals are evaluated analytically in the ultrarelativistic limit (z = m/T approaching zero) by expanding the modified Bessel functions and performing the momentum integration using gamma functions. The transport cross section is obtained from an effective quark-quark potential motivated by non-perturbative dimension two gluon condensates, with the temperature-dependent coupling extracted from the potential in the qq-scheme.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity leading term: η = 4T/(5σ_t) (1 + z²/20 + O(z⁴ ln z)), where z = m/T and σ_t is the transport cross section
- Heat conductivity leading term: κ = 4/(3σ_t) (1 - z²/4 + O(z⁴ ln z))
- Bulk viscosity leading term: ζ = m z³/(108 σ_t) (1 + O(z² ln z))
- At high temperatures, η scales as T³ and κ scales as T²
- The coupling parameter g̃ = 1.30 is used to describe three-flavor QCD lattice data up to 5 T_c
- The non-perturbative dimension two condensate is C₂ = (0.9 GeV)²
- The constant c_EM ≈ 1.731863 appears in the expansion of K₂(z)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigation of shear viscosity in the sQGP (Mattiello, 2009) using the virial expansion approach to also include bulk viscosity and heat conductivity. The calculation uses a relativistic extension of the Chapman-Enskog method developed by van Leeuwen (1973) and reviewed by Wiranata (2012) for shear viscosity only. The results enable systematic inclusion of temperature-dependent transport coefficients in dissipative hydrodynamical calculations of heavy-ion collisions, following the hybrid model VISHNU that couples viscous hydrodynamics to hadron cascade models.
