# Power Counting in Dimensionally Regularized NRQCD

> We present a scheme for calculating in NRQCD with consistent power counting in the heavy quark velocity v. As an example, we perform the systematic matching of an external current onto NRQCD at subleading order in v, a calculation relevant for the process e^+e^- -> hadrons near threshold. Consistent velocity power counting in dimensional regularization is achieved by including two distinct gluon fields, one corresponding to gluon radiation and one corresponding to an instantaneous potential. In this scheme power counting is manifest in any gauge, and also holds for non-gauge interactions. The matching conditions for an external vector current in NRQCD are calculated to O(g^2 v^2) and the cancellation of infrared divergences in the matching conditions is shown to require both gluon fields. Some subtleties arising in the matching conditions at subleading order are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a scheme for consistent velocity power counting in dimensionally regularized NRQCD by introducing two distinct gluon fields (potential and radiation), which is necessary because no single rescaling of fields can correctly describe both the instantaneous potential scattering and on-shell gluon radiation that contribute to nonanalytic infrared physics in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Method

The authors introduce separate "potential" and "radiation" gluon fields in the NRQCD Lagrangian, where potential gluons couple with standard three-momentum conservation and radiation gluons couple via a multipole expansion that breaks translational invariance. They perform one-loop matching of an external vector current onto NRQCD at subleading order in the heavy quark velocity v, first in a simpler nonrelativistic Yukawa theory and then in NRQCD, using dimensional regularization to regulate both infrared and ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers

- Matching conditions for an external vector current in NRQCD are calculated to O(g²v²)
- The infrared divergent part of the full theory amplitude for Yukawa theory contains a term -i g²/(8πβ) J_i u_h† σ^i v_h ln m_φ, where β is the fermion three-velocity
- The radiation scalar vertex correction in NRY gives g²/(4π²) J_i u_h† σ^i v_h ln m_φ
- The QCD amplitude for quark-antiquark production contains a 1/v singularity from Coulomb scattering with coefficient g²/(12π²) u_h† σ^i v_h [m/|p|] π²
- The tree-level matching coefficients for the vector current operators O₁ and O₂ are c₁ = c₂ = 1 + O(g²), while c₃ = O(g²)

## Context

This work extends the NRQCD power counting framework of Luke and Manohar (1996) and Grinstein and Rothstein (1997), resolving the problem that no single field rescaling can simultaneously describe both potential scattering and on-shell gluon radiation. The scheme enables systematic matching calculations for processes such as e⁺e⁻ → hadrons near threshold, and the authors demonstrate that cancellation of infrared divergences in the matching conditions requires both gluon fields. The approach also applies to other nonrelativistic systems including positronium and low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering.
