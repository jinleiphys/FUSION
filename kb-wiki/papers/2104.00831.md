# Dynamical evolution of magnetic field in the pre-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma

> High-energy heavy-ion collisions generate extremely strong magnetic field which plays a key role in a number of novel quantum phenomena in quark-gluon plasma (QGP), such as the chiral magnetic effect (CME). However, due to the complexity in theoretical modellings of the coupled electromagnetic fields and the QGP system, especially in the pre-equilibrium stages, the lifetime of the magnetic field in the QGP medium remains undetermined. We establish, for the first time, a kinetic framework to study the dynamical decay of the magnetic field in the early stages of a weakly coupled QGP by solving the coupled Boltzmann and Maxwell equations. We find that at late times a magnetohydrodynamical description of the coupled system emerges. With respect to realistic collisions at RHIC and the LHC, we estimate the residual strength of the magnetic field in the QGP when the system start to evolve hydrodynamically.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a kinetic framework for the first time to study the dynamical decay of magnetic fields in the pre-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma, finding that a magnetohydrodynamical description emerges at late times. However, the analysis is restricted to a weakly coupled QGP (small strong coupling constant) and the condition $|eB| \ll \Lambda_c^2$ must hold for the perturbative treatment of quark distribution functions to remain valid.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Boltzmann and Maxwell equations for a weakly coupled QGP with Bjorken symmetry, treating the background quark and gluon distributions as determined by QCD 2-to-2 elastic scatterings at small angle approximation. The splitting distribution between quarks and anti-quarks is described by a collisionless Boltzmann-Vlasov equation, and the induced electromagnetic fields are computed from the resulting charge current. Initial conditions are taken from saturation physics with saturation scale $Q_s = 1$ GeV, and parameters are fixed to reproduce multiplicity yields in AuAu collisions at RHIC and PbPb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Initial magnetic field peak strength: $|eB| \sim 10^{19}$ Gauss at RHIC and $\sim 10^{20}$ Gauss at the LHC
- Saturation scale: $Q_s = 1$ GeV, with initial time $t_0 = 1\, Q_s^{-1} \sim 0.2$ fm/c
- Strong coupling constant: $\alpha_s \sim 0.2$
- Initial distribution parameter: $\xi = 1.4$ for the out-of-equilibrium QGP
- Magnetic field decays as $\sim t^{-3}$ in vacuum and as $\sim t^{-1}$ in ideal MHD for Bjorken expansion
- Two limiting cases for initial quark occupation: $f_{q/\bar q}^{(0)} = 0$ (no initial quarks) and $f_{q/\bar q}^{(0)} = 1$ (fully populated quarks)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic field decay in heavy-ion collisions that assumed a fully thermalized QGP with charge conductivity as input, such as those by McLerran (2013), Tuchin (2013), and Gursoy (2014). It provides the first consistent treatment of the pre-equilibrium stage where the magnetic field decays most violently and the QGP is highly out-of-equilibrium. The framework enables future quantitative studies of the chiral magnetic effect and other electromagnetic-field-induced phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, and demonstrates that the $t^{-1}$ decay characteristic of ideal MHD emerges earlier than the system reaches local equilibrium.
