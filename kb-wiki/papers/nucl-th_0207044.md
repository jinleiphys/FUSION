# Cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation for superdeformed and hyperdeformed rotational bands in N=Z nuclei from 32S to 48Cr

> With the use of the symmetry-unrestricted cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method in the three-dimensional coordinate-mesh representation, we have carried out a systematic theoretical search for the superdeformed and hyperdeformed rotational bands in the mass A=30-50 region. Along the N=Z line, we have found superdeformed solutions in 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, and hyperdeformed solutions in 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, 48Cr. The superdeformed band in 40Ca is found to be extremely soft against both the axially symmteric (Y30) and asymmetric (Y31) octupole deformations. An interesting role of symmetry breaking in the mean field is pointed out.

# Cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation for superdeformed and hyperdeformed rotational bands in N=Z nuclei from 32S to 48Cr

## Key claim

The authors report a systematic theoretical search for superdeformed (SD) and hyperdeformed (HD) rotational bands in N=Z nuclei from 32S to 48Cr using a symmetry-unrestricted cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method, finding that the SD band in 40Ca is extremely soft against both axially symmetric (Y30) and asymmetric (Y31) octupole deformations. The abstract's claim of "an interesting role of symmetry breaking" is hedged: the symmetry breaking is shown to be important specifically in the crossing region between configurations with different numbers of particles in the fp shell, where reflection-symmetry breaking paths enable smooth configuration rearrangements that are impossible under parity-restricted calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the cranked Hartree-Fock equation for a Skyrme-type Hamiltonian using the imaginary-time evolution technique in a three-dimensional Cartesian-mesh representation with a sphere radius of 10 fm and mesh size of 1 fm, completely removing restrictions on parity and signature symmetries. They employed three versions of the Skyrme interaction (SIII, SkM*, and SLy4) and used constrained HF procedures with quadratic constraints to enforce center-of-mass and principal-axis conditions. Deformation energy curves were obtained by constraining the mass-quadrupole moment, and rotational bands were generated by cranking each SD and HD local minimum solution with increasing rotational frequency until the configuration could no longer be clearly identified.

## Key numbers

* SD solutions found in 32S, 36Ar, 40Ca, and 44Ti in the quadrupole deformation region 0.4 ≤ β2 ≤ 0.8 at I=0
* HD solutions found in 36Ar, 40Ca, 44Ti, and 48Cr in the region β2 ≥ 0.8 at I=0
* The SD band in 40Ca terminates at I ≈ 24 with the SIII interaction and I ≈ 24 with the SkM* interaction, with triaxial shapes of γ = 6°-9° (SIII) and 8°-9° (SkM*)
* The SD band in 44Ti terminates at I ≈ 12 (SIII) and I ≈ 16 (SkM*), with triaxial shapes of γ = 18°-25° (SIII) and 13°-19° (SkM*)
* The HD band in 36Ar with the f6g2 configuration emerges at I ≈ 16 due to rotation alignment of the g9/2 orbit
* In 44Ti, two HD bands (f8g4 and (fp)12g4 configurations) cross at I = 30-34
* The center-of-mass and principal-axis constraints were fulfilled to order O(10^-15) with parameters μk ~ O(10^2) and μk,k' ~ O(1)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cranked SHF calculation on 32S (Yamagami 2000) and provides a systematic theoretical survey of SD and HD bands in the A=30-50 region, complementing experimental observations of SD bands in 36Ar (Svensson 2000, 2001), 40Ca (Ideguchi 2001), and 44Ti (Lennarz 2000). The HD solutions agree with earlier SHF calculations by Zheng, Zamick and Berdichevsky (1990) and with macroscopic-microscopic model calculations by Leander and Larsson (1975). The octupole softness found in 40Ca has also been suggested by Kanada-En'yo (2001). The results enable future searches for negative-parity rotational bands associated with octupole shape vibrational excitations built on the SD yrast band in 40Ca, and motivate inclusion of pairing correlations via cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations.
