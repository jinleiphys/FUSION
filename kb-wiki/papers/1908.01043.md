# Hadron and Nuclear Physics on the Light Front

> The QCD light-front (LF) Hamiltonian equation H_{LF}|Ψ> = M^2 |Ψ> derived from quantization at fixed LF time τ= t+z/c provides a causal, frame-independent, method for computing hadron spectroscopy as well as dynamical observables such as structure functions, transverse momentum distributions, and distribution amplitudes. The LF formalism also leads to novel nuclear phenomena, such as "hidden color", "color transparency", "nuclear-bound quarkonium" and the shadowing and antishadowing of nuclear structure functions. For example, there are five distinct color-singlet Fock state representations of the six color-triplet quarks of the deuteron. The hidden color Fock states become manifest when the deuteron is probed when it has small transverse size, as in measurements of the deuteron form factor at large momentum transfer. The QCD Lagrangian with zero quark mass has no explicit mass scale. However, as shown by de Alfaro, Fubini, and Furlan (dAFF), a mass scale can appear in the equations of motion without affecting the conformal invariance of the Action. When one applies the dAFF procedure to the QCD LF Hamiltonian, it leads to a color confining potential κ^4 ζ^2 for mesons, where ζ^2 is the LF radial variable conjugate to the qbar-q invariant mass squared. The same result, including spin terms, is obtained using LF holography (AdS/QCD) -- the duality between LF dynamics and AdS_5 -- if one modifies the AdS_5 Action by the dilaton exp{+κ^2 z^2} in the fifth dimension z. If this procedure is generalized using superconformal algebra, the resulting LF eigensolutions provide unified Regge spectroscopy of mesons, baryons, and tetraquarks, including remarkable supersymmetric relations between the masses of mesons, baryons and tetraquarks with a universal Regge slope...

## Key claim

The paper argues that light-front (LF) quantization, combined with holographic mapping to AdS₅ space and the de Alfaro-Fubini-Furlan (dAFF) mechanism, provides a frame-independent, causal method for computing hadron spectroscopy and dynamics, including novel nuclear phenomena such as hidden color Fock states in the deuteron. The authors claim that the resulting formalism yields a universal mass scale κ that determines hadron masses and Regge slopes, and that this scale can be connected to the perturbative QCD parameter Λₘₛ₋bar via matching at a scale Q₀.

## Method

The authors derive the QCD light-front Hamiltonian equation H_LF|Ψ⟩ = M²|Ψ⟩ from quantization at fixed LF time τ = t+z/c, obtaining boost-invariant LF wavefunctions (LFWFs) as eigensolutions. They apply the dAFF procedure to introduce a mass scale κ while preserving conformal symmetry of the action, yielding a confining potential κ⁴ζ² for mesons, where ζ² is the LF radial variable conjugate to the qq̄ invariant mass squared. They then generalize this using superconformal algebra to obtain unified Regge spectroscopy of mesons, baryons, and tetraquarks, and match the nonperturbative coupling from AdS/QCD to perturbative QCD at a scale Q₀ to connect κ to Λₘₛ₋bar.

## Key numbers

- The mesonic qq̄ bound-state eigenvalues for massless quarks are M²(n, L, S) = 4κ²(n + L + S/2), predicting a massless pion for n=L=S=0.
- The best fit to measurements of α_s^{g₁}(Q²) gives κ = 0.513 ± 0.007 GeV.
- The matching scale Q₀ in the MSbar scheme is Q₀ = 0.87 ± 0.08 GeV.
- The predicted value of Λₘₛ₋bar = 0.339 ± 0.019 GeV agrees with the measured value 0.332 ± 0.017 GeV.
- Hadron masses expressed in terms of Λₘₛ₋bar: m_p = √2 κ = 3.21 Λₘₛ₋bar, m_ρ = κ = 2.2 Λₘₛ₋bar, and m_p = √2 m_ρ.
- The nuclear shadowing exponent α ≈ 0.8 for the cross-section σ(γA → X) = A^α σ(γN → X).

## Context

This work extends the light-front quantization approach pioneered by Dirac (1949) and applies the dAFF mechanism (de Alfaro, Fubini, and Furlan, 1976) to QCD, building on earlier AdS/QCD holographic mappings by de Teramond and Brodsky (2013). The formalism enables unified predictions for hadron spectroscopy, form factors, structure functions, and the QCD running coupling at all scales, connecting nonperturbative dynamics to perturbative QCD. It also predicts novel nuclear phenomena such as hidden color Fock states in the deuteron (Brodsky, 1983) and antishadowing of nuclear structure functions (Brodsky, 1989), which can be tested at future electron-ion colliders.
