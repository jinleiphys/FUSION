# Recent Theoretical Developments in Strongly Coupled QCD

> Heavy-ion collisions involve strongly coupled dynamics of QCD in the entire history of time evolution. We review recent theoretical efforts to meet this challenge, focusing on the two approaches that the speaker has contributed to: 1) Holography or AdS/CFT correspondence, and 2) Symmetry protected phenomena such as those originating from triangle anomaly. The presentation is oriented to non-experts on these fields, and hence relies on intuitive pictures of the methods and the results, without going into specific details.

# Recent Theoretical Developments in Strongly Coupled QCD

## Key claim
Holographic (AdS/CFT) methods and symmetry-protected phenomena from the triangle anomaly provide complementary approaches to understanding strongly coupled QCD dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, but the high-energy limit may not commute with the large-\(N_c\) limit, raising questions about the applicability of leading-order holographic results for realistic \(N_c=3\).

## Method
The paper reviews two theoretical approaches applied to heavy-ion collisions: (1) holography/AdS/CFT correspondence, which maps strongly coupled large-\(N_c\) gauge theory to weakly coupled classical gravity in a 5-dimensional anti-de Sitter space, where a finite-temperature deconfined phase corresponds to a black-brane solution; and (2) triangle-anomaly-induced transport phenomena, specifically the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW), derived from the axial-vector-vector three-point function. The holographic analysis covers high-energy small-momentum-transfer scattering via stringy Pomerons, thermalization dynamics from colliding shock waves or falling mass shells, and jet quenching modeled by a 5D string endpoint moving through a black-brane background. The anomaly analysis derives the CME relation \(\vec j_{V,A} = (e N_c / 2\pi^2) \mu_{A,V} \vec B\) and the CMW dispersion relation \(\omega = \pm v_\chi k - i D_L k^2 + \cdots\).

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field in off-central heavy-ion collisions can be as large as \(eB \sim m_\pi^2\) (approximately \(10^{19}\) Gauss), with a lifetime of \(\lesssim 1\) fm.
- For jet quenching in the geodesic regime (\(Q \ll Q_s \equiv (E T^2)^{1/3}\)), the penetration length scales as \(\Delta x \sim (1/T) (E/T)^{1/3}\).
- For jet quenching in the diffusion regime (\(Q \gg Q_s\)), the penetration length scales as \(\Delta x \sim (1/T) (E/Q)^{1/2}\).
- The subleading \(1/N_c^2\) correction to jet energy loss gives \({dE}/{dt} \sim (1/N_c^2) E^2\).
- The QCD sphaleron rate in QGP is \(\Gamma \approx 30 \alpha_s^5 T^4\).
- The net vector charge chemical potential at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV is approximately 20 MeV.
- The thermalization time from holographic studies is an order-one number in units of the final temperature, with \(\tau \le 0.5\) fm inferred from hydrodynamic fits.

## Context
This review extends the application of AdS/CFT to heavy-ion phenomenology, building on the foundational holographic duality of Maldacena (1997) and earlier holographic QCD models by Sakai and Sugimoto (2004) and Erlich et al. (2005). The high-energy scattering analysis connects to the BFKL approach and the Regge behavior observed by Donnachie and Landshoff (1992), while the thermalization studies follow work by Janik and Peschanski (2005), Chesler and Yaffe (2010), and others. The anomaly-related phenomena extend the original CME proposal by Kharzeev et al. (2007) and Fukushima et al. (2008), with the CMW developed by Kharzeev and Yee (2010) and Burnier et al. (2011). The experimental signatures discussed have been tested by STAR at RHIC (Abelev et al., 2009) and at LHC (Selyuzhenkov, 2011), with recent Uranium-Uranium collision data (Wang, 2012) supporting the magnetic-field origin of the observed correlations. The open problem of unitarizing the holographic Pomeron and the non-commutativity of the high-energy and large-\(N_c\) limits point toward needed developments in multi-Pomeron exchange and subleading corrections in jet quenching.
