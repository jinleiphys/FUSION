# Antikaons and higher order couplings in relativistic-mean field study of neutron stars

> We investigate the role of higher order couplings, along with the condensation of antikaons ($K^-$ and $\bar K^0$), on the properties of neutron star (NS). We employ extended versions of the relativistic mean-field model, in which kaon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions are taken on the same footing. We find that the onset of condensation of $K^-$ and $\bar K^0$ highly depends not only on the strength of optical potential but also on the new couplings. The presence of antikaons leads to a softer equation of state and makes the neutron star core symmetric and lepton-deficient. We show that these effects strongly influence the mass-radius relation as well as the composition of neutron star. We also show that the recently observed 1.97$\pm$.04 solar mass NS can be explained in three ways: (i) a stiffer EoS with both antikaons, (ii) a relatively soft EoS with $K^-$ and (iii) a softer EoS without antikaons.

# Antikaons and higher order couplings in relativistic-mean field study of neutron stars

## Key claim

The onset of antikaon condensation (both K⁻ and anti-K⁰) in neutron stars depends not only on the kaon optical potential but also strongly on higher-order couplings in the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian; the recently observed 1.97 ± 0.04 solar mass neutron star can be explained by three different compositions depending on the stiffness of the equation of state and which antikaons are present.

## Method

The authors employ extended versions of the relativistic mean-field model (E-RMF) with parameter sets G1, G2, and FSU2.1, treating kaon-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions on the same footing. They solve the field equations for nucleons, leptons, and antikaons (K⁻ and anti-K⁰) under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, then obtain the mass-radius relation by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The onset conditions are ω_K⁻ = μ_e for K⁻ and ω_anti-K⁰ = 0 for anti-K⁰.

## Key numbers

- The recently observed pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has mass 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses (M⊙)
- With the G2 parameter set, onset densities for K⁻ and anti-K⁰ in neutron matter are 0.36 fm⁻³ and 0.71 fm⁻³ respectively; for neutron star matter they are 0.59 fm⁻³ and 0.98 fm⁻³
- For a 2 M⊙ maximum mass neutron star: without antikaons (G2) radius R₂ = 11.03 km; with K⁻ only (FSU2.1) R₂ = 11.42 km; with both K⁻ and anti-K⁰ (G1) R₂ = 12.55 km
- Kaon optical potentials considered: U_K = -120 MeV and U_K = -160 MeV
- Antikaon masses: m_K⁻ = m_anti-K⁰ = 495 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Gupta and Arumugam (2012) that considered only K⁻ condensation, and builds on the framework of Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich (1999) who first proposed detailed antikaon studies in neutron stars. The authors use more recent RMF parametrizations (G1, G2, FSU2.1) that have been tested on finite nuclei by Del Estal et al. (2001) and on symmetric nuclear matter, unlike earlier works that used less rigorously tested parametrizations such as those by Banik and Bandyopadhyay (2001). The results enable future studies of the interplay between hyperons and antikaons within extended RMF models with higher order couplings.
