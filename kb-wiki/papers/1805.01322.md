# Benchmarking the variational reduced density matrix theory in the doubly-occupied configuration interaction space with integrable pairing models

> The variational reduced density matrix theory has been recently applied with great success to models within the truncated doubly-occupied configuration interaction space, which corresponds to the seniority zero subspace. Conservation of the seniority quantum number restricts the Hamiltonians to be based on the SU(2) algebra. Among them there is a whole family of exactly solvable Richardson-Gaudin pairing Hamiltonians. We benchmark the variational theory against two different exactly solvable models, the Richardson-Gaudin-Kitaev and the reduced BCS Hamiltonians. We obtain exact numerical results for the so-called PQGT N-representability conditions in both cases for systems that go from 10 to 100 particles. However, when random single-particle energies as appropriate for small superconducting grains are considered, the exactness is lost but still a high accuracy is obtained.

## Key claim

The variational reduced density matrix (v2RDM) method with the PQGT (P, Q, G, T1, T2) N-representability conditions yields numerically exact ground-state energies and canonical gaps for the Richardson-Gaudin-Kitaev and reduced BCS Hamiltonians within the seniority-zero subspace, but this exactness is lost when random single-particle energies appropriate for small superconducting grains are considered, although high accuracy is retained.

## Method

The authors formulated the variational optimization of the second-order reduced density matrix (2RDM) as a semidefinite program (SDP) within the doubly-occupied configuration interaction (DOCI) space, which corresponds to the seniority-zero subspace. They implemented the P, Q, G, T1, and T2 N-representability conditions in terms of the seniority blocks of the 2RDM (the P and D matrices) and solved the SDP using the SDPA code with a Mehrotra-type predictor-corrector primal-dual interior-point method, setting a summation error of delta = 10^-7. The method was benchmarked against exact solutions of two integrable Richardson-Gaudin pairing models: the Richardson-Gaudin-Kitaev (RGK) Hamiltonian and the reduced BCS (constant pairing) Hamiltonian, for systems ranging from 10 to 100 doubly degenerate single-particle levels at half filling.

## Key numbers

- For the RGK Hamiltonian with L=50 doubly degenerate levels at half filling (M=25 fermion pairs, Hilbert space dimension 1.26 x 10^14), the PQGT v2RDM approximation produced numerically exact ground-state energies and canonical gaps within the accuracy limit of the SDPA code, while the PQG approximation and PBCS were exact only at the Moore-Read point G_MR = 1/(L-M+1) = 1/26.
- For the RGK Hamiltonian, the PQG approximation showed energy errors comparable to PBCS in the weak coupling region but was two orders of magnitude worse than PBCS in the superconducting region; the PQGT conditions removed this discrepancy entirely.
- For the reduced BCS Hamiltonian with L=50 equidistant single-particle levels (spacing d=1/L) and M=25 fermion pairs, the PQGT conditions reproduced exact results within numerical error, while PQG and PBCS showed complementary behavior with PQG more accurate at weak pairing and PBCS more accurate in the strong superconducting region.
- For both models, the PQGT v2RDM method was tested for system sizes from L=10 to L=100 levels, and the relative energy errors remained of the same order of magnitude across all sizes, with the summation error relaxed to delta = 3 x 10^-7 for larger systems.

## Context

This work extends the variational reduced density matrix method within the DOCI space, previously applied to seniority-nonconserving Hamiltonians by Poelmans (2015), Head-Marsden (2017), and Alcoba (2018), by benchmarking it against exactly solvable Richardson-Gaudin pairing models. The RGK model (Ortiz, 2014) and the reduced BCS Hamiltonian (Richardson, 1963) serve as ideal testbeds because their exact solutions allow assessment of the v2RDM accuracy for systems far beyond exact diagonalization limits. The results demonstrate that the PQGT conditions are sufficient for exactness in these integrable models, enabling future applications to more realistic pairing Hamiltonians with random single-particle energies, where the method remains highly accurate even though exactness is lost.
