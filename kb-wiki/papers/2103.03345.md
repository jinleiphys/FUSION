# $^{16}\mathrm{O}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ at RHIC and the LHC comparing $α$ clustering vs substructure

> Collisions of light and heavy nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions have been shown to be sensitive to nuclear structure. With a proposed $^{16}\mathrm{O}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ run at the LHC and RHIC we study the potential for finding $α$ clustering in $^{16}$O. Here we use the state-of-the-art iEBE-VISHNU package with $^{16}$O nucleonic configurations from {\rm ab initio} nuclear lattice simulations. This setup was tuned using a Bayesian analysis on pPb and PbPb systems. We find that the $^{16}\mathrm{O}^{16}\mathrm{O}$ system always begins far from equilibrium and that at LHC and RHIC it approaches the regime of hydrodynamic applicability only at very late times. Finally, by taking ratios of flow harmonics we are able to find measurable differences between $α$-clustering, nucleonic, and subnucleonic degrees of freedom in the initial state.

## Key claim

The paper finds that alpha-clustering in oxygen-16 produces measurable differences in flow harmonic ratios at LHC energies, but the hydrodynamic description of oxygen-oxygen collisions is pushed to the limits of its validity because the system begins far from equilibrium and only approaches the regime of hydrodynamic applicability at very late times.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic package with initial nucleonic configurations for oxygen-16 from ab initio nuclear lattice simulations using the pinhole algorithm. They generated initial entropy distributions using the TRENTo model with a Woods-Saxon parameterization and compared three initial-state models: spherical Woods-Saxon, Woods-Saxon with subnucleonic (quark) fluctuations, and alpha-clustering configurations. The hydrodynamic evolution was run for 30,000 events per configuration at both RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (6.5 TeV) energies, with final-state particles produced via UrQMD at a freeze-out temperature of 151 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon cross-sections: 42.5 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC) and 72.5 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 6.5 TeV (LHC)
- Woods-Saxon parameters for oxygen-16: R = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fo = 151 MeV
- Free-streaming duration: tau_s = 0.37 fm/c
- Entropy normalization constants: 5.3 (RHIC) and 17 (LHC)
- Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers for shear are problematically large (greater than or equal to 0.5) for the majority of the hydrodynamic phase, predominantly at early times tau less than 2 fm/c
- Reasonable Knudsen and inverse Reynolds numbers are reached after tau approximately 3 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis of Bernhard (2019) for pPb and PbPb collisions to the proposed oxygen-oxygen runs at RHIC and LHC, building on prior studies of alpha-clustering in nuclear collisions by Rybczynski (2019) and others. The paper enables experimental searches for alpha-clustering signatures in oxygen-oxygen collisions, particularly through the ratio v_4{2}/v_2{2} and the cumulant v_4{4}^4, where alpha-clustering and subnucleonic fluctuations produce opposite effects at LHC energies. The finding that the system is far from equilibrium for most of its evolution raises questions about the applicability of standard viscous hydrodynamics to intermediate-size systems and motivates further work on anisotropic hydrodynamics or alternative frameworks.
