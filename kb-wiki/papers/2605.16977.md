# Two-nucleon systems at $m_π\approx292$ MeV from lattice QCD

> Nucleon-nucleon systems in the $^3S_1$ and the $^1S_0$ channels are studied in lattice quantum chromodynamics at a pion mass of approximately $m_π\approx292$ MeV, employing three $N_f = 2+1$ ensembles with the same pion mass and lattice spacing $a=0.10530(18)$ fm but different spatial volumes. Finite-volume energies of the nucleon-nucleon systems are determined in both the rest frame and a moving frame. The distillation quark smearing method is applied to improve the precision and to ensure the symmetric correlators by using the same interpolating operators at sink and source. The scattering amplitudes are extracted from the finite-volume spectra using the Lüscher's finite-volume method. At the studied pion mass, both the $^3S_1$ (deuteron) and $^1S_0$(di-neutron) channels exhibit a virtual state pole, with binding energies of $6^{+5}_{-3}$ MeV and $11^{+6}_{-5}$ MeV, respectively. To investigate the effects of the left-hand cut, an alternative method -- the Non-Perturbative Hamiltonian framework (NPHF) -- is used for the scattering analysis and yields consistent results with those from the Lüscher method.

## Key claim

At a pion mass of approximately 292 MeV, both the $^3S_1$ (deuteron) and $^1S_0$ (di-neutron) channels exhibit virtual state poles rather than bound states, with the abstract's claim of "binding energies" actually referring to the pole positions on the unphysical Riemann sheet below threshold, not to stable bound states.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using three $N_f = 2+1$ ensembles with identical pion mass $m_\pi\approx292$ MeV and lattice spacing $a=0.10530(18)$ fm but different spatial volumes ($24^3$, $32^3$, and $48^3$). Finite-volume energies of two-nucleon systems were computed in both the rest frame and a moving frame using the distillation quark smearing method with momentum-space interpolating operators at both source and sink. Scattering amplitudes were extracted using Luscher's finite-volume method, and as a cross-check, the Non-Perturbative Hamiltonian framework (NPHF) was employed to account for left-hand cut effects.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: $m_\pi\approx292$ MeV across all three ensembles
- Lattice spacing: $a=0.10530(18)$ fm
- Nucleon masses: $m_N = 1026(4)$ MeV (C24P29), $1005(3)$ MeV (C32P29), $1007(1)$ MeV (C48P29)
- $m_\pi L$ values: 3.75 (C24P29), 5.01 (C32P29), 7.52 (C48P29)
- $^3S_1$ channel virtual state pole position: $6^{+5}_{-3}$ MeV below threshold
- $^1S_0$ channel virtual state pole position: $11^{+6}_{-5}$ MeV below threshold
- Best-fit K-matrix parameters for $^3S_1$: $c_0 = -0.17^{+0.04}_{-0.07}$, $c_1 = 0.15^{+0.06}_{-0.03}$ with $\chi^2/{\rm dof}=0.44$
- Best-fit K-matrix parameters for $^1S_0$: $c_0 = -0.526277$, $c_1 = 0.462596$ with $\chi^2/{\rm dof}=0.33$ (central values)

## Context

This work bridges the gap between heavy-pion-mass lattice QCD studies (e.g., NPLQCD at $m_\pi\approx800$ MeV, which found deeply bound deuteron and di-neutron) and the physical point, at a pion mass of 292 MeV. It extends the approach of Francis (2018) and Horz (2020) using distillation and boosted frames, and addresses tensions raised by those studies with earlier asymmetric-correlator calculations. The finding of virtual states rather than bound states at this pion mass is consistent with the recent conclusion of BaSc (2025) at $m_\pi=714$ MeV that previous bound-state identifications may have arisen from misidentification of the spectrum, and enables future studies to determine at what pion mass the transition from virtual to bound states occurs.
