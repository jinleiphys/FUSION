# Beyond the known baryon resonances

> eta photoproduction data on the proton up to E=2 GeV are interpreted within a chiral constituent quark formalism, which embodies all known three and four star resonances. This study confirms the need for a new S11 resonance, with M=1.780 GeV and Gamma=280 MeV, already introduced in investigating lower energy data.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model, which includes all known three- and four-star baryon resonances, fails to reproduce eta photoproduction data above 1 GeV; adding a new S11 resonance with mass 1.780 GeV and width 280 MeV improves the reduced chi-squared by more than a factor of two over the complete dataset.

## Method

The authors fitted approximately 650 data points from recent measurements of differential cross sections and single polarization asymmetries for the reaction γp → ηp, using the MINUIT minimization code. The chiral constituent quark formalism includes s-channel, u-channel, and seagull terms, with configuration mixing angles for the two S11 and two D13 states around 1.5–1.7 GeV. The model treats all eleven known resonances below 2 GeV explicitly, with contributions from higher-mass resonances treated as degenerate, and introduces a new S11 resonance with three free parameters (mass, width, and strength).

## Key numbers

- New S11 resonance: mass M = 1.780 GeV, width Γ = 280 MeV
- Configuration mixing angles: θ_S = 12°, θ_D = −35°
- The model with the new resonance improves the reduced χ² on the complete database by more than a factor of 2 compared to the model with only known resonances
- Data fitted: approximately 650 data points from CLAS, GRAAL, and Mainz experiments, up to laboratory photon energy E_γ^lab ≈ 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by the same authors (Li and Saghai, 2001, 2002) that investigated lower-energy eta photoproduction data. The extracted mass and width are close to predictions by Li and Workman (1996) (M = 1.712 GeV, Γ = 184 MeV) and consistent with results from the Zagreb group coupled-channel analysis (M = 1.792 ± 0.023 GeV, Γ = 360 ± 49 MeV), the BES Collaboration (M = 1.800 ± 0.040 GeV, Γ = 165^{+165}_{-85} MeV), and a hypercentral constituent quark model prediction (M = 1.861 GeV). The findings enable future searches for this resonance in double polarization asymmetry measurements at GRAAL, JLAB, and Spring-8, and motivate extending coupled-channel analyses to the eta photoproduction sector.
