# Hydrodynamical response of plane correlation in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}$=2.76 TeV

> In high energy heavy-ion collisions, the final anisotropic flow coefficients and their corresponding event-plane correlations are considered as the medium evolutional response to the initial geometrical eccentricities and their corresponding participant-plane correlations. We formulate a systematic theoretical analysis to study the hydrodynamical responses concerning higher order effects in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=2.76$ TeV by using Monte Carlo Glauber (MC-Glauber) model. To further understand the transformations of the initial participant-plane correlation, we construct a new set of events which randomize the directions of the initial participant planes of the original events. Our results indicate that the final strong event-plane correlations are mainly transformed from the large initial eccentricities, rather than the strong participant-plane correlations. However, the large flow coefficients and the discrepancies between the flow coefficients calculated by the single-shot and event-by-event simulations in peripheral collisions are relevant to those strong initial participant-plane correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong final-state event-plane correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV are primarily transformed from large initial eccentricities rather than from strong initial participant-plane correlations, but the large flow coefficients and discrepancies between single-shot and event-by-event simulations in peripheral collisions are relevant to those strong initial participant-plane correlations.

## Method

The authors used the MC-Glauber model to generate fluctuating initial entropy density profiles for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, then evolved them with (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNEW) using shear viscosity η/s = 0.08 and the s95p-PCE equation of state. They compared single-shot simulations (where initial profiles are rotated and averaged by participant-plane orientation before evolution) with event-by-event simulations for centralities 0-10%, 40-50%, and 70-80% and Gaussian widths σ = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, and 0.9 fm. To isolate the role of participant-plane correlations, they constructed a new set of events by randomizing the directions of the first fifty participant-plane angles while preserving the eccentricity magnitudes.

## Key numbers

- Average similarity between single-shot and event-by-event eccentricities: 4.0% for 0-10%, 3.9% for 40-50%, 7.4% for 70-80% centrality (summed over σ = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9 fm)
- Average similarity between single-shot and event-by-event flow coefficients: 12.1% for 0-10%, 31.2% for 40-50%, 37.1% for 70-80% centrality
- Hydrodynamical evolution started at proper time 0.6 fm/c with chemical freeze-out at 165 MeV and decoupling at 120 MeV
- Shear viscosity coefficient η/s = 0.08 for both QGP and hadron gas phases
- Pion transverse momentum cut pT > 0.5 GeV and pseudorapidity range 0.5 < |η| < 2.5, matching ATLAS experimental analysis

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the transformation from initial eccentricities to final flow coefficients (AnnuRevNuclPartSci.57.205, PhysRevC.82.064903, PhysRevC.85.024908) and from participant-plane correlations to event-plane correlations (PhysLettB.717.261, EurPhysJC.73.2510). The authors build on the method of randomizing participant-plane angles from JPhysG.41.015103 to disentangle the roles of eccentricity magnitude versus angular correlations. The results enable a clearer interpretation of experimental event-plane correlation measurements from the LHC (PhysRevLett.107.032301, PhysLettB.708.249) by showing that strong final correlations do not necessarily imply strong initial angular correlations.
