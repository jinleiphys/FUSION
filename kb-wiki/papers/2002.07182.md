# Sterile neutrinos and neutrinoless double beta decay in effective field theory

> We investigate neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) in the presence of sterile neutrinos with Majorana mass terms. These gauge-singlet fields are allowed to interact with Standard-Model (SM) fields via renormalizable Yukawa couplings as well as higher-dimensional gauge-invariant operators up to dimension seven in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory extended with sterile neutrinos. At the GeV scale, we use Chiral effective field theory involving sterile neutrinos to connect the operators at the level of quarks and gluons to hadronic interactions involving pions and nucleons. This allows us to derive an expression for $0νββ$ rates for various isotopes in terms of phase-space factors, hadronic low-energy constants, nuclear matrix elements, the neutrino masses, and the Wilson coefficients of higher-dimensional operators. The needed hadronic low-energy constants and nuclear matrix elements depend on the neutrino masses, for which we obtain interpolation formulae grounded in QCD and chiral perturbation theory that improve existing formulae that are only valid in a small regime of neutrino masses. The resulting framework can be used directly to assess the impact of $0νββ$ experiments on scenarios with light sterile neutrinos and should prove useful in global analyses of sterile-neutrino searches. We perform several phenomenological studies of $0νββ$ in the presence of sterile neutrinos with and without higher-dimensional operators. We find that non-standard interactions involving sterile neutrinos have a dramatic impact on $0νββ$ phenomenology, and next-generation experiments can probe such interactions up to scales of $\mathcal O(100)$ TeV.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic effective-field-theory framework for neutrinoless double beta decay that includes light sterile neutrinos (masses below about 1 GeV) and non-standard interactions from higher-dimensional operators up to dimension seven, but the resulting master formula and interpolation formulae for hadronic matrix elements are only valid when the sterile neutrino mass is below the chiral symmetry breaking scale (approximately 1 GeV), and the phenomenological reach of next-generation experiments to probe non-standard interactions up to scales of order 100 TeV depends on the specific operator and isotope considered.

## Method

The authors construct the sterile-neutrino-extended Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) including renormalizable Yukawa couplings and gauge-invariant operators up to dimension seven, then evolve these operators via renormalization group equations to the electroweak scale where heavy SM particles are integrated out. At the GeV scale, they match the resulting operators to chiral effective field theory involving sterile neutrinos, pions, and nucleons, deriving the neutrinoless double beta decay transition operators from both soft and hard neutrino exchange between nucleons. The framework produces a master formula that expresses decay rates in terms of phase-space factors, hadronic low-energy constants, nuclear matrix elements, neutrino masses, and Wilson coefficients, with interpolation formulae for the neutrino-mass dependence of the hadronic inputs that are grounded in QCD and chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* Next-generation neutrinoless double beta decay experiments can probe non-standard sterile neutrino interactions up to scales of order 100 TeV.
* Current experimental limits on neutrinoless double beta decay half-lives are at the level of 10^26 years.
* The electroweak scale is taken as v = 246 GeV, the chiral symmetry breaking scale as Lambda_chi approximately 1 GeV, and the pion mass m_pi is used as a reference scale in the chiral expansion.
* The Higgs vacuum expectation value is v = 246 GeV, and the QCD scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the SMEFT-to-neutrinoless-double-beta-decay framework developed by Cirigliano et al. (2017, 2018) to include light sterile neutrinos as propagating degrees of freedom below the GeV scale, whereas previous work had integrated out all sterile neutrinos above the chiral scale. The framework enables direct connection of any beyond-the-Standard-Model scenario with sterile neutrinos (such as low-scale leptogenesis, dark matter models, or left-right symmetric models) to neutrinoless double beta decay rates, and can be combined with other probes including LHC searches, oscillation experiments, and meson decays as studied by Alcaide et al. (2019) and Butterworth et al. (2019).
