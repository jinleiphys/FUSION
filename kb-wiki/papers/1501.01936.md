# Quasi-power law ensembles

> Quasi-power law ensembles are discussed from the perspective of nonextensive Tsallis distributions characterized by a nonextensive parameter $q$. A number of possible sources of such distributions are presented in more detail. It is further demonstrated that data suggest that nonextensive parameters deduced from Tsallis distributions functions $f\left(p_T\right)$, $q_1$, and from multiplicity distributions (connected with Tsallis entropy), $q_2$, are not identical and that they are connected via $q_1 + q_2 = 2$. It is also shown that Tsallis distributions can be obtained directly from Shannon information entropy, provided some special constraints are imposed. They are connected with the type of dynamical processes under consideration (additive or multiplicative). Finally, it is shown how a Tsallis distribution can accommodate the log-oscillating behavior apparently seen in some multiparticle data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that quasi-power law (Tsallis) distributions in high-energy multiparticle production can arise from a variety of physical mechanisms beyond nonextensive Tsallis statistics, and that the nonextensivity parameter extracted from transverse momentum distributions ($q_1$) differs from that extracted from multiplicity distributions ($q_2$), with the data suggesting they satisfy $q_1 + q_2 = 2$.

## Method

The authors review and derive multiple mechanisms that produce Tsallis distributions: superstatistics (gamma fluctuations of the scale parameter), preferential attachment in stochastic networks, multiplicative noise in Langevin equations, constrained systems with fixed total energy, and fluctuations of multiplicity. They also show that Tsallis distributions can be obtained from Shannon entropy under specific constraints, and demonstrate how log-periodic oscillations observed in high-energy data can be accommodated either by a complex nonextensivity parameter or by a log-periodically oscillating scale parameter.

## Key numbers

- The relation $q_1 + q_2 = 2$ connects the nonextensivity parameter from Tsallis distributions ($q_1$) with that from Tsallis entropy ($q_2$)
- For particles inside jets, $q = 0.885$ for $\vec{p}_{jet} \in (4-6)$ GeV and $q = 1.094$ for $\vec{p}_{jet} \in (24-40)$ GeV
- In the multiplicative noise derivation, $q = 1 + 2Var(\gamma)/\langle \gamma\rangle$ for the $p^2$ distribution, with $T = 2Var(\xi)/\langle \xi\rangle$
- For the conditional probability case with $N$ degrees of freedom, $q = (N-3)/(N-2) < 1$ and $\lambda = \alpha N/(N-1)$
- The relation $q - 1 = Var(N)/\langle N\rangle^2 - 1/\langle N\rangle$ connects $q$ to multiplicity fluctuations
- For the log-periodically oscillating $T$ fit to CMS data at 7 TeV, the parameters are $\bar{a} = 0.143$, $\bar{b} = 0.0045$, $\bar{c} = 2.0$, $\bar{d} = 2.0$, $\bar{f} = -0.4$
- The dressing factor approach requires $\kappa \sim 22$ sequential cascades to match data

## Context

This review extends the authors' previous work on Tsallis distributions in high-energy physics (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2009, 2012) and connects to the broader literature on nonextensive statistics initiated by Tsallis (1988). The paper synthesizes multiple known mechanisms, superstatistics from Beck and Cohen (2003), preferential attachment from network theory, and multiplicative noise from stochastic processes, into a unified discussion of quasi-power law ensembles. It enables future work on understanding the physical meaning of the nonextensivity parameter $q$ and provides a framework for interpreting log-periodic oscillations observed in LHC data from CMS (2010), ATLAS (2010), and ALICE (2010).
