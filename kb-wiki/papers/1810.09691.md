# Skyrme functional with tensor terms from \textit{ab initio} calculations of neutron-proton drops

> A new Skyrme functional devised to account well for standard nuclear properties as well as for spin and spin-isospin properties is presented. The main novelty of this work relies on the introduction of tensor terms guided by \textit{ab initio} relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of neutron-proton drops. The inclusion of tensor term does not decrease the accuracy in describing bulk properties of nuclei, experimental data of some selected spherical nuclei such as binding energies, charge radii, and spin-orbit splittings can be well fitted. The new functional is applied to the investigation of various collective excitations such as the Giant Monopole Resonance (GMR), the Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance (IVGDR), the Gamow-Teller Resonance (GTR), and the Spin-Dipole Resonance (SDR). The overall description with the new functional is satisfactory and the tensor terms are shown to be important particularly for the improvement of the Spin-Dipole Resonance results. Predictions for the neutron skin thickness based on the non-energy weighted sum rule of the Spin-Dipole Resonance are also given.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new Skyrme energy density functional (SAMi-T) where the tensor terms are constrained by *ab initio* relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations of neutron-proton drops, rather than fitted solely to experimental data; the tensor terms are shown to be important for improving the description of the Spin-Dipole Resonance, while the functional maintains accuracy for bulk nuclear properties.

## Method

The authors fit a Skyrme functional with self-consistent tensor terms using a protocol based on the SAMi functional, replacing the neutron matter pseudo-data with RBHF results for neutron drops and adding new constraints from RBHF calculations of neutron-proton drops. The fit includes experimental binding energies and charge radii of five spherical nuclei, experimental spin-orbit splittings in three nuclei, the relative change of spin-orbit splittings between neutron-proton drops with (N=20, Z=20) and (N=28, Z=20) from RBHF, and total energies of neutron drops with N=8, 20, 40, 50 from RBHF. The Hartree-Fock equations are solved in coordinate space with a spherical box of radius 15 fm and radial mesh 0.1 fm.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies of five spherical nuclei (40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, 132Sn, 208Pb) are fitted with an error of 1.00 MeV per nucleus, yielding a partial chi-squared of 9.71.
* Charge radii of the same five nuclei are fitted with an error of 0.01 fm, yielding a partial chi-squared of 20.09.
* The relative change of spin-orbit splittings from RBHF neutron-proton drops (from 40,20 to 48,20) is fitted with an error of 0.01 times the observable, yielding a partial chi-squared of 3.25 for 4 data points.
* The total energy of neutron drops (N=8,20,40,50) from RBHF, scaled as E/(hbar-omega * N^(4/3)), is fitted with an error of 0.01, yielding a partial chi-squared of 3.02 for 4 data points.
* The total chi-squared is 47.5 for 21 data points, giving chi-squared per data point of 2.26.
* Saturation density rho_0 = 0.164(1) fm^{-3}, binding energy per nucleon e_0 = -16.15(3) MeV, incompressibility K_0 = 244(5) MeV, symmetry energy J = 29.7(6) MeV, and slope parameter L = 46(12) MeV.
* The tensor parameters are alpha_T = -39.8(39.9) MeV fm^5 and beta_T = 66.7(39.9) MeV fm^5.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using *ab initio* calculations to constrain nuclear energy density functionals, following studies of neutron drops by Pudliner (1996), Gandolfi (2011), and Maris (2013), and the SAMi functional of Roca-Maza (2012). It differs from earlier tensor-inclusive Skyrme functionals like the TIJ family of Lesinski (2007) by fitting tensor terms to RBHF pseudo-data rather than solely to experimental single-particle properties, which are complicated by particle-vibration coupling as noted by Colo (1994) and Niu (2012). The new functional enables predictions for neutron skin thickness via the non-energy weighted sum rule of the Spin-Dipole Resonance and provides a benchmark for future studies of spin and spin-isospin excitations in exotic nuclei.
