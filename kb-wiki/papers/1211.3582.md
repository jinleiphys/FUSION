# Can parameters of f_0-mesons be determined correctly analyzing only pion-pion scattering?

> The coupled processes -- the pion-pion scattering and pi pi --> K antiK in the I^GJ^{PC}=0^+0^{++} channel -- are analyzed (both separately and combined) in a model-independent approach based on analyticity and unitarity and using a uniformization procedure. It is shown: 1) a structure of the Riemann surface of the S-matrix for considered coupled processes must be allowed for calculating both amplitudes and resonance parameters, such as the mass and width; 2) the combined analysis of coupled processes is needed as the analysis of only pi-pi channel does not give correct values of resonance parameters even if the Riemann surface structure is included.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that analyzing only the pion-pion scattering channel, even when the full Riemann surface structure of the S-matrix is properly included, does not yield correct resonance parameters for scalar mesons; a combined analysis of the coupled processes (pion-pion scattering and pion-pion to kaon-antikaon) is necessary, especially for wide resonances.

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent analysis based on analyticity and unitarity, using a uniformization procedure that maps the four-sheeted Riemann surface of the two-channel S-matrix onto a single complex plane. They first analyzed only the pion-pion scattering data in the I^G J^PC = 0^+ 0^++ channel, then performed a combined analysis of both pion-pion scattering and pion-pion to kaon-antikaon data, using the Le Couteur-Newton relations to express the S-matrix elements in terms of the Jost matrix determinant. The resonance contributions were represented by pole clusters on the Riemann surface, with the background parameterized by a polynomial expansion.

- In the analysis of only pion-pion scattering: f0(600) pole on sheet II at (447.5 ± 5.9) - i(267 ± 6.5) MeV; total chi-squared per degree of freedom approximately 1.07
- In the combined analysis (A-solution): f0(600) pole on sheet II at (517 ± 7.8) - i(393.9 ± 6) MeV; total chi-squared per degree of freedom 1.53
- In the combined analysis (B-solution): f0(600) pole on sheet II at (550.6 ± 9) - i(502.1 ± 7.2) MeV; total chi-squared per degree of freedom 1.44
- Pion-pion scattering length a0^0 from the analysis of only pion-pion scattering: 0.222 ± 0.008 m_pi^+^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-channel analyses (Surovtsev et al., 2010; Surovtsev et al., 2011) by performing a full two-channel analysis without the approximations used earlier, to verify the plausibility of their assumptions. The results differ considerably from analyses using dispersion relations and Breit-Wigner approaches cited by the Particle Data Group (2012), and the combined analysis resolves two flaws found in the single-channel analysis: the appearance of a negative background phase shift at the pion-pion threshold and the failure to describe the modulus of the pion-pion to kaon-antikaon transition above 1.15 GeV. The A-solution reproduces chiral perturbation theory results (Colangelo et al., 2001; Gasser and Leutwyler, 2000) and is preferred based on precise NA48/2 and DIRAC experiments, while the B-solution resembles predictions from Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type models (Volkov, 1986; Ivanov and Troitskaya, 1995).
