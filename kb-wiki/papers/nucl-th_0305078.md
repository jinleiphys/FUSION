# The nonrelativistic limit of the relativistic point coupling model

> We relate the relativistic finite range mean-field model (RMF-FR) to the point-coupling variant and compare the nonlinear density dependence. From this, the effective Hamiltonian of the nonlinear point-coupling model in the nonrelativistic limit is derived. Different from the nonrelativistic models, the nonlinearity in the relativistic models automatically yields contributions in the form of a weak density dependence not only in the central potential but also in the spin-orbit potential. The central potential affects the bulk and surface properties while the spin-orbit potential is crucial for the shell structure of finite nuclei. A modification in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with a density-dependent spin-orbit potential inspired by the point-coupling model is suggested.

## Key claim

The nonrelativistic limit of the relativistic point-coupling (RMF-PC) model recovers the basic structure of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) energy functional, but with a crucial difference: the RMF-PC model automatically generates a density dependence not only in the central potential but also in the spin-orbit potential, whereas standard SHF lacks explicit density dependence in the spin-orbit term. The authors suggest that modifying the SHF model with a density-dependent spin-orbit potential inspired by the RMF-PC model could improve shell structure predictions.

## Method

The authors first relate the relativistic finite-range mean-field (RMF-FR) model to the point-coupling variant (RMF-PC) by expanding the meson propagators in powers of the Laplacian, eliminating the meson fields, and comparing the resulting effective point-coupling parameters. They then perform a nonrelativistic reduction of the RMF-PC Hamiltonian density by expanding the Dirac equation in powers of \(v/c\) up to \((p/m)^2\), expressing the scalar density \(\rho_S\) and tensor density \(\rho_T\) in terms of the nonrelativistic densities \(\rho\), \(\tau\), and \(\vec{J}\). The resulting energy density is cast into a general form with coefficients \(\mathcal{C}_i\) and compared term-by-term with the standard SHF energy functional.

## Key numbers

- The effective point-coupling parameters from RMF-FR forces (NL3, NL1, NL-Z2) and genuine RMF-PC forces (PC-F1, PC-LA) are tabulated; for example, \(\alpha_S = -15.74\) (NL3), \(-16.52\) (NL1), \(-16.45\) (NL-Z2), \(-14.94\) (PC-F1), and \(-17.55\) (PC-LA) in units of \(\text{fm}^2\).
- The scalar density in bulk is taken as \(\rho_S \approx 0.14\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) to test convergence of the nonlinear expansion.
- For the PC-F1 parameter set at equilibrium density \(\rho_{\text{eq}}\), the derived spin-orbit coefficients are: \(b_4 = 122.93\,\text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^5\), \(b'_4 = 7.01\,\text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^5\), \(W_1\rho_{\text{eq}}/2 = 36.04\,\text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^5\), and \(W_2(\rho_{\text{eq}}/2)^2 = -10.26\,\text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^5\).

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between relativistic mean-field models and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock by authors such as Reinhard (1995) and Aboussir (1995), who connected RMF-FR to SHF without tensor contributions, and by Rusnak and Furnstahl (2001), who applied effective field theory concepts to point-coupling models. The paper enables future numerical studies of a modified Skyrme energy functional with density-dependent spin-orbit terms, which could improve shell structure predictions for superheavy nuclei where existing Skyrme forces (e.g., SkI3, SkI4) show discrepancies compared to relativistic models.
