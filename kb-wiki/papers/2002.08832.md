# Correlation coefficient between harmonic and transverse flow in heavy-ion collisions

> The correlation between the harmonic flow and the transverse flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions is calculated in the hydrodynamic model. The partial correlation coefficient, corrected for fluctuations of multiplicity, is compared to experimental data. Estimators of the final transverse and harmonic flow are used to predict the value of the correlation coefficient from the moments of the initial distribution. A good description of the hydrodynamic simulation results is obtained if the estimator for the final transverse flow, besides the most important transverse size and entropy, includes also the eccentricities.

## Key claim

The correlation coefficient between harmonic flow and transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions can be reasonably well described by linear hydrodynamic response estimators, but only if the estimator for the final transverse momentum includes not just the transverse size and entropy but also the eccentricities; the partial correlation coefficient corrected for multiplicity fluctuations is larger than the standard correlation coefficient and reproduces ATLAS data fairly well.

## Method

The authors performed viscous hydrodynamic simulations (with shear viscosity eta/s=0.08 and temperature-dependent bulk viscosity) for Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, using a nucleon Glauber model for initial entropy density. They calculated the correlation coefficient rho(v_n{2}^2,[p_T]) and the partial correlation coefficient rho(v_n{2}^2,[p_T] bullet N) that corrects for multiplicity fluctuations within each centrality bin, and compared these to ATLAS Collaboration data. They also constructed linear estimators for the final observables from initial-state moments (transverse size, entropy, eccentricities) and tested how well these estimators reproduce the hydrodynamic covariances.

## Key numbers

- The correlation coefficient rho(v_2{2}^2,[p_T]) for charged particles in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV is calculated and compared to ATLAS data; the partial correlation coefficient is larger than the standard correlation coefficient.
- For p+Pb collisions, two initial-state scenarios give different rms transverse sizes: R_RMS approx 1.5 fm (standard Glauber) and R_RMS approx 0.9 fm (compact source); the compact source scenario reproduces the sign of the experimental correlation coefficient while the standard Glauber gives the wrong sign.
- The covariance Cov(v_n{2}^2,[p_T]) from hydrodynamic simulations is well described by the improved estimator that includes eccentricities in the transverse momentum predictor, while the simpler estimator (transverse size and entropy only) shows significant deviations for semiperipheral collisions.
- The partial variance of the average transverse momentum with respect to multiplicity is significantly smaller than the standard variance in Pb+Pb collisions.
- The correlation coefficient between elliptic and triangular flow rho(v_2{2}^2,v_3{2}^2) shows negligible corrections from control variables (multiplicity and transverse momentum).

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic calculations of flow-transverse momentum correlations by Bozek (2016) from Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV, and provides the first detailed comparison to ATLAS data (Aad, 2019). It applies the partial correlation method of Olszewski (2017) to correct for multiplicity fluctuations in broad centrality bins. The study enables future comparisons of initial-state models in p+Pb collisions and tests of linear hydrodynamic response frameworks, building on the eccentricity-based estimators of Gardim (2011), Qiu (2011), and Niemi (2012), and the transverse momentum predictors of Broniowski (2009) and Mazeliauskas (2015).
