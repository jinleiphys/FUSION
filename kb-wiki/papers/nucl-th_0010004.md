# Kinetic equation with exact charge conservation

> We formulate the kinetic master equation describing the production of charged particles which are created or destroyed only in pairs due to the conservation of their Abelian charge.Our equation applies to arbitrary particle multiplicities and reproduces the equilibrium results for both canonical (rare particles) and grand canonical (abundant particles) systems. For canonical systems, the equilibrium multiplicity is much lower and the relaxation time is much shorter than the naive extrapolation from the grand canonical ensemble results. Implications for particle chemical equilibration in heavy-ion collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a kinetic master equation for the production of charged particles that are created or destroyed only in pairs, which is valid for arbitrary particle multiplicities and reproduces both canonical (rare particles) and grand canonical (abundant particles) equilibrium results. The key hedge is that for canonical systems, the equilibrium multiplicity is much lower and the relaxation time is much shorter than what would be obtained from a naive extrapolation of grand canonical ensemble results.

## Method

The authors derive a master equation for the probability \(P_n(\tau)\) of finding \(n\) pairs of oppositely charged particles at time \(\tau\), where the transition probability for pair creation is \(G\langle N_{a_1}\rangle\langle N_{a_2}\rangle/V\) and for pair annihilation is \(n^2 L/V\). They then convert this iterative equation into a partial differential equation for a generating function \(g(x,\tau)\), which is solved analytically at equilibrium using Bessel functions. The general rate equation for the average number of pairs is obtained by multiplying the master equation by \(n\) and summing over \(n\), yielding \(d\langle N\rangle/d\tau = (G/V)\langle N_{a_1}\rangle\langle N_{a_2}\rangle - (L/V)\langle N^2\rangle\).

## Key numbers

- For the grand canonical (abundant) limit (\(\langle N\rangle \gg 1\)): equilibrium pair number \(N_{\rm eq}^{\rm GC} = \sqrt{\epsilon}\) and relaxation time \(\tau_0^{\rm GC} = V/(L\sqrt{\epsilon})\), where \(\epsilon \equiv G\langle N_{a_1}\rangle\langle N_{a_2}\rangle/L\).
- For the canonical (rare) limit (\(\langle N\rangle \ll 1\)): equilibrium pair number \(N_{\rm eq}^{\rm C} = \epsilon\) and relaxation time \(\tau_0^{\rm C} = V/L\).
- The canonical equilibrium multiplicity is related to the grand canonical result by \(N_{\rm eq}^{\rm C} = (N_{\rm eq}^{\rm GC})^2 \ll N_{\rm eq}^{\rm GC}\).
- The canonical relaxation time is related to the grand canonical result by \(\tau_0^{\rm C} = \tau_0^{\rm GC} N_{\rm eq}^{\rm GC} \ll \tau_0^{\rm GC}\).
- For example, in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/A, the total number of produced kaons is of order 0.02, making the canonical relaxation time a factor of 50 shorter than the grand canonical estimate.

## Context

This work extends the standard kinetic rate equation approach, which uses the grand canonical ensemble and assumes abundant particle production, to correctly handle rare particle production where exact charge conservation is crucial. The authors build on prior statistical model work by Hagedorn (1971), Rafelski (1980), and Cleymans (1991) that showed canonical conservation laws severely reduce phase space for particle production, and on earlier kinetic studies by Koch and by Turko that did not obtain a complete solution. The results enable more accurate modeling of chemical equilibration for rare particles in heavy-ion collisions, such as strange particles at SIS energies or open charm and charmonium at SPS and higher energies, and provide a framework for transport model studies where local charge conservation requires assigning production probability to the pair rather than to each particle separately.
