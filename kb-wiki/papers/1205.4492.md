# Quarkyonic Chiral Spirals in a Magnetic Field

> We discuss the formation of quarkyonic chiral spirals in the presence of a magnetic field. The explicit breaking of the rotational symmetry by the external magnetic field gives rise to an additional chiral spiral that varies along the field direction and rotates in the chiral space between pion and magnetic moment components.

# Quarkyonic Chiral Spirals in a Magnetic Field

## Key claim
In the presence of a magnetic field of strength comparable to the QCD scale, quarkyonic matter at large $N_c$ develops two independent chiral spirals rather than one, because the lowest Landau level breaks the spin degeneracy and allows a second condensate involving magnetic moment and pion components that is absent at zero field.

## Method
The authors start from the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark self-energy in a constant magnetic field, using Ritus's method to transform to momentum space and the Gribov-Zwanziger gluon propagator to capture confining interactions near the Fermi surface. They show that for patches perpendicular to the magnetic field, the (3+1)-dimensional theory maps onto a (1+1)-dimensional QCD theory with $2L+1$ flavors, where $L$ is the number of Landau levels within a shell of width $\Lambda_{QCD}$ from the Fermi surface. By applying a field transformation to remove the chemical potential and analyzing the resulting chiral condensates for each flavor, they identify two independent inhomogeneous condensates that oscillate along the field direction.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be $\sim 10^{19} G$ for Au-Au collisions at RHIC.
- The condition for the quarkyonic phase to exist is $m_D \ll \Lambda_{QCD} \ll \mu$, with $m_D$ the screening mass and $\mu$ the quark chemical potential.
- The number of Landau levels contributing to the patch is $L = l_{max} = [\Lambda_{QCD}^2 / (2|e_f B|)]$, where $[\dots]$ denotes the integer part.
- The external magnetic field satisfies $e_f B \lesssim \mathcal{O}(\Lambda_{QCD}^2)$.
- The patch size is $\sim \Lambda_{QCD} \ll \mu$, with momentum component parallel to the field satisfying $p_3 \approx \mu + \delta p_3$ where $\delta p_3 \leq \Lambda_{QCD}$.
- The transverse momentum satisfies $p_\perp = \sqrt{2|e_f B| l} \leq \Lambda_{QCD}$.

## Context
This work extends the concept of quarkyonic chiral spirals introduced by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) for quarkyonic matter at zero magnetic field, and the subsequent demonstration by Kojo et al. (2010) that chiral symmetry is broken through translational non-invariant condensates in quarkyonic matter. The paper shows that an external magnetic field, which is naturally present in heavy-ion collisions, introduces a second chiral spiral involving a magnetic moment condensate $\langle \overline{\psi}\gamma^1\gamma^2\psi \rangle$ and a pion condensate $\langle \overline{\psi}\gamma^5\psi \rangle$, in addition to the previously known spiral combining the chiral condensate $\langle \overline{\psi}\psi \rangle$ and the electric dipole condensate $\langle \overline{\psi}\gamma^0\gamma^3\psi \rangle$. This result enables future studies of observational implications in neutron star cores and planned high-density heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR, NICA, and JPARC.
