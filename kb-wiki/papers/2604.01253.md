# Mechanical Equilibrium in the Magnetized Quark--Hadron Mixed Phase: A Covariant Generalization of the Gibbs Condition

> We formulate a covariant mechanical equilibrium condition for the quark-hadron mixed phase boundary in the presence of a magnetic-field-induced pressure anisotropy. Using the \emph{relativistic thin-shell} formalism to describe the quark-hadron boundary, we interpret conservation of stress-energy across the interface as a set of generalized Young--Laplace conditions which characterize the geometry of the interface. In a comoving stationary frame, this provides a covariant description of mechanical equilibrium at the interface, which serves as a replacement for the scalar pressure-balance condition used in the isotropic Gibbs construction.

## Key claim
The paper replaces the scalar pressure-balance condition of the standard Gibbs construction for the quark-hadron mixed phase with a covariant set of generalized Young-Laplace conditions derived from the relativistic thin-shell formalism, but only for interfaces where the magnetic-field-induced pressure anisotropy is present; the formalism also shows that even in the isotropic (zero-field) limit, nonzero constant surface tension on curved interfaces is incompatible with the equal-pressure assumption.

## Method
The author models the quark-hadron phase boundary as a timelike thin shell with surface stress-energy, imposes stress-energy conservation across the interface using the Israel junction formalism, and derives normal and tangential jump conditions that couple the bulk stress-energy discontinuity to the interface geometry. For magnetized fermion matter, the bulk stress-energy tensor is taken in its anisotropic form with distinct parallel and perpendicular pressures relative to the magnetic field direction, and the surface stress-energy is modeled both as a constant isotropic tension and as a minimal anisotropic phenomenological tensor that depends on the tangential projection of the magnetic field onto the interface.

## Key numbers
- The bulk stress-energy tensor for magnetized fermions yields perpendicular pressure \(P_\perp = -\Omega_f - M_f B + B^2/2\) and parallel pressure \(P_\parallel = -\Omega_f - B^2/2\) in the \(T\to0\) limit (Eqs. EOS).
- For constant surface tension, the tangential balance condition \(\Delta\Pi(\hat{\mathbf n}\cdot\hat{\mathbf b})[b^j-(\hat{\mathbf n}\cdot\hat{\mathbf b})n^j]=0\) restricts admissible interfaces to those where the surface normal is either parallel/antiparallel or orthogonal to the magnetic field direction at every point.
- In the isotropic limit (\(B\to0\)), the Young-Laplace condition reduces to \(\Delta P = -(D-1)\sigma/R\) for a sphere (\(D=3\)), cylinder (\(D=2\)), or slab (\(D=1\)), matching the energy-minimization result from the compressible liquid-drop model.

## Context
This work extends the standard Gibbs construction for the quark-hadron mixed phase in neutron stars (Glendenning 1992, Schertler 1999, Burgio 2002) to include magnetic-field-induced pressure anisotropies first identified by Ferrer (2010) and Strickland (2012). It generalizes the isotropic Gibbs condition used in prior studies of magnetized mixed phases (Bandyopadhyay 1997, Rabhi 2009, Rather 2023) by replacing scalar pressure balance with a tensorial interface condition. The relativistic thin-shell formalism, previously applied to neutron-star core-crust interfaces (Pereira 2014, 2015), is here adapted to mixed-phase pasta structures. The framework provides the geometric and mechanical basis for future self-consistent constructions of the magnetized mixed-phase equation of state, which will require supplementing the modified Gibbs conditions with a size constraint from Coulomb or charge-screening effects.
