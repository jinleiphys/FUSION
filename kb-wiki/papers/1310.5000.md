# Isospin effects and symmetry energy studies with INDRA

> The equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter is still controversial, as predictions at subsaturation as well as above normal density widely diverge. We discuss several experimental results measured in heavy-ion collisions with the INDRA array in the incident energy range 5-80 MeV/nucleon. In particular an estimate of the density dependence of the symmetry energy is derived from isospin diffusion results compared with a transport code: the potential part of the symmetry energy linearly increases with the density. We demonstrate that isospin equilibrium is reached in mid-central collisions for the two reactions Ni+Au at 52 MeV/nucleon and Xe+Sn at 32 MeV/nucleon. New possible variables and an improved modelization to investigate symmetry energy are discussed.

## Key claim

The potential part of the symmetry energy linearly increases with density (asystiff behavior), with a constraint of γ = 1 ± 0.2 or L = 75 ± 25 MeV, but the authors acknowledge that extracting the stiffness from isospin diffusion remains an issue and that other studies (e.g., Tsa09) favor an asy-softer EOS.

## Method

The INDRA 4π multidetector (90% geometrical efficiency, 4% energy accuracy) measured charged products from heavy-ion collisions at 5–80 MeV/nucleon. Central quasi-fusion reactions (Xe+Sn at 32 and 45 AMeV) were compared to Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport calculations with two symmetry energy parametrizations (asystiff with γ ≥ 1, asysoft from SKM*). Semi-peripheral collisions (Ni+Ni, Ni+Au at 52 and 74 AMeV; Xe+Sn at 32 AMeV) were analyzed via isospin transport ratios and the (N/Z) of complex particles forward-emitted in the quasi-projectile frame, compared to SMF after secondary decay treatment with the SIMON code.

## Key numbers

- Incident energy range: 5–80 MeV/nucleon
- For Ni+Au at 52 AMeV and Xe+Sn at 32 AMeV, isospin equilibrium is reached in mid-central collisions; equilibration time for Xe+Sn at 32 AMeV estimated between 5 and 7 × 10⁻²² s
- For Ni+Au at 52 AMeV, isospin equilibrium predicted by SMF for impact parameter b ≤ 4 fm, corresponding to reaction time 4 × 10⁻²² s
- Difference in Z_bound in fragments (Z_b5) between neutron-richest and neutron-poorest Xe+Sn systems at 45 AMeV: 5.5 units (9.8%) experimentally; 2.6 units (5.3%) in asystiff SMF; 3.2 units (6.5%) in asysoft SMF
- Fragment multiplicity difference ΔM_{Z>2} between ¹³⁶Xe+¹¹²Sn and ¹²⁴Xe+¹²⁴Sn at 32 AMeV levels off above E_tr12^FWcm = 110 MeV; asystiff SMF matches data for b < 6 fm
- For Ni+Au at 52 AMeV, (N/Z)_CP varies by at most 1.5% with dissipation for the symmetric Ni+Ni system
- Constraint on symmetry energy: γ = 1 ± 0.2 or L = 75 ± 25 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier INDRA studies of isospin effects (Bar05, Riz08, Nap10) and compares with SMF transport calculations (Colon98, Bar02). The preference for asystiff symmetry energy is consistent with results from neck fragment isospin evolution (Def12) and Ca-induced reactions (Amo09), but contradicts Sn+Sn results at 35 and 50 AMeV (Tsa09) which favor an asy-softer EOS when compared with the ImQMD model. The finding that isospin equilibrium is reached for Xe+Sn at 32 AMeV disagrees with claims for Sn+Sn at 35 AMeV (Sun10). The results enable tighter constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy in the subsaturation domain, though the authors note that all results fit within the large limits of the L–S₀ plane.
