# A new tension in the cosmological model from primordial deuterium?

> Recent measurements of the D(p,$γ)^3$He, nuclear reaction cross-section and of the neutron lifetime, along with the reevaluation of the cosmological baryon abundance from cosmic microwave background (CMB) analysis, call for an update of abundance predictions for light elements produced during the big-bang nucleosynthesis (BBN). While considered as a pillar of the hot big-bang model in its early days, BBN constraining power mostly rests on deuterium abundance. We point out a new $\simeq1.8σ$-tension on the baryonic density, or equivalently on the D/H abundance, between the value inferred on one hand from the analysis of the primordial abundances of light elements and, on the other hand, from the combination of CMB and baryonic oscillation data. This draws the attention on this sector of the theory and gives us the opportunity to reevaluate the status of BBN in the context of precision cosmology. Finally, this paper presents an upgrade of the BBN code PRIMAT.

## Key claim

The authors report a new tension of approximately 1.8 sigma between the baryonic density (or equivalently the deuterium-to-hydrogen ratio) inferred from big-bang nucleosynthesis and the value derived from cosmic microwave background plus baryon acoustic oscillation data, but note that this tension depends on the specific choice of nuclear reaction rates and the inclusion of the latest LUNA measurement of the D(p,gamma)3He cross-section.

## Method

The authors updated the BBN code PRIMAT with new nuclear reaction rates, most importantly the LUNA measurement of the D(p,gamma)3He cross-section, and incorporated updated neutron lifetime (879.4 +/- 0.6 s), Planck 2018 CMB+BAO baryon density, and refined neutrino decoupling treatment. They then computed light element abundances and compared the inferred baryon density from BBN (using observed deuterium and helium-4 abundances) with the CMB+BAO determination, using Monte Carlo methods to propagate nuclear rate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- BBN-only baryon density: Omega_b h^2 = 0.02195 +/- 0.00022, which is a 1.6 sigma tension with CMB alone (Omega_b h^2 = 0.02237 +/- 0.00015) and a 1.84 sigma tension with CMB+BAO (Omega_b h^2 = 0.02242 +/- 0.00014)
- Predicted D/H assuming CMB+BAO baryon density: (2.439 +/- 0.037) x 10^{-5}, compared to the observed value of (2.527 +/- 0.030) x 10^{-5} from Cooke (2018), giving a 1.84 sigma tension
- Neutron lifetime used: tau_n = 879.4 +/- 0.6 s, consistent with the value inferred from V_ud = 0.97420 +/- 0.00028 and g_A = 1.2756 +/- 0.0013 giving tau_n = 879.4 +/- 0.5 s
- Predicted helium-4 abundance: Y_p = 0.24721 +/- 0.00014, compared to observed Y_p = 0.2453 +/- 0.0034 from Aver (2020)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BBN analysis in Pitrou (2018) by incorporating the new LUNA measurement of the D(p,gamma)3He cross-section from NatureDPG (2020), which confirms the theoretical S-factor from Marcucci (2005) and resolves a long-standing controversy about this key reaction rate. The tension contrasts with the earlier consensus that deuterium was a success of the standard cosmological model, and the authors note that other BBN codes using different reaction rate choices (such as Fields (2020) using NACRE-II rates) would predict higher D/H values. The result enables further scrutiny of nuclear data and cosmological assumptions, though it does not resolve the lithium-7 problem which remains a factor of approximately 3 discrepancy.
