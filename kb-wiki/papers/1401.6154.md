# Constraints on muon-specific dark forces

> The recent measurement of the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen allows for the most precise extraction of the charge radius of the proton which is currently in conflict with other determinations based on $e-p$ scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy. This discrepancy could be the result of some new muon-specific force with O(1-100) MeV force carrier---in this paper we concentrate on vector mediators. Such an explanation faces challenges from the constraints imposed by the $g-2$ of the muon and electron as well as precision spectroscopy of muonic atoms. In this work we complement the family of constraints by calculating the contribution of hypothetical forces to the muonium hyperfine structure. We also compute the two-loop contribution to the electron parity violating amplitude due to a muon loop, which is sensitive to the muon axial-vector coupling. Overall, we find that the combination of low-energy constraints favors the mass of the mediator to be below 10 MeV, and that a certain degree of tuning is required between vector and axial-vector couplings of new vector particles to muons in order to satisfy constraints from muon $g-2$. However, we also observe that in the absence of a consistent standard model embedding, high energy weak-charged processes accompanied by the emission of new vector particles are strongly enhanced by $(E/m_V)^2$, with $E$ a characteristic energy scale and $m_V$ the mass of the mediator. In particular, leptonic $W$ decays impose the strongest constraints on such models completely disfavoring the remainder of the parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a new muon-specific vector force with a mediator mass in the O(1-100) MeV range could explain the proton charge radius discrepancy between muonic hydrogen and other determinations, but finds that while low-energy constraints favor mediator masses below 10 MeV, high-energy constraints from leptonic W decays completely disfavor the remaining parameter space unless a consistent Standard Model embedding is found.

## Method

The authors introduce a phenomenological model with a new vector boson V that couples to the electromagnetic current via kinetic mixing parameter κ and has additional vector (g_V) and axial-vector (g_A) couplings to muons. They calculate the one-loop contribution of this new force to the muonium hyperfine structure, compute the two-loop contribution to the electron parity-violating amplitude from a muon loop, and evaluate the enhancement of W boson decay to muon-neutrino-V due to the absence of gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The proton charge radius discrepancy corresponds to a relative shift of the squared radius of 0.06 fm^2
- The muonium hyperfine structure constraint at 2σ: |ΔE_hfs/E_hfs| < 1.24 × 10^-7
- The bound on new physics contribution to the weak charge of cesium at 2σ: |ΔQ_W| < 0.86
- The maximum allowed κ from electron g-2: |κ^max| = 1.8 × 10^-3 × (m_V/20 MeV) for m_V ≫ m_e
- The W decay constraint: g_V < 2.2 × 10^-3 × (m_V/10 MeV) at 2σ
- The required g_V to explain the r_p discrepancy normalized to κ/(3×10^-6)^(1/2) × (g_V/e)/0.06 × (20 MeV/m_V)^2 gives -0.06 fm^2
- The cancellation condition for muon g-2: g_A^tuned = ± g_V × m_V/(√2 m_μ)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of muon-specific dark forces motivated by the proton radius puzzle first highlighted by Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013), and builds on earlier attempts such as the gauged μ_R model of Batell (2011). The paper complements existing constraints from muon and electron g-2 and muonic atom spectroscopy by calculating new constraints from muonium hyperfine structure and two-loop parity violation transfer to electrons. The high-energy constraints from W decays, following the approach of Barger (2011) and Carlson (2012), demonstrate that without a consistent Standard Model embedding, such models are strongly disfavored, motivating future searches for enhanced parity violation involving muons in polarized electron scattering experiments.
