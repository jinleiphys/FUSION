# The Pion-Cloud Contribution to the Electromagnetic Nucleon Form Factors

> We study the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon within a hybrid constituent-quark model that comprises, in addition to the $3q$ valence component, also a $3q$+$π$ non-valence component. To this aim we employ a Poincaré-invariant multichannel formulation based on the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics. With a simple 3-quark wave function for the bare nucleon, i.e. the $3q$-component, we obtain reasonable results for the nucleon form factors and predict the meson-cloud contribution to be significant only below $Q^2\lesssim 0.5$\,GeV$^2$ amounting to about 10\% for $Q^2\rightarrow 0$, in accordance with the findings of other authors.

## Key claim

The pion-cloud contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors is significant only below momentum transfers \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), amounting to about 10% for \(Q^2 \rightarrow 0\), in accordance with findings of other authors; this result is obtained within a hybrid constituent-quark model using a simple 3-quark wave function for the bare nucleon, without solving the full bound-state problem for a particular confinement potential.

## Method

The authors employ a Poincare-invariant multichannel formulation based on the point-form of relativistic quantum mechanics, using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to separate overall motion from internal motion. They model the physical nucleon as a superposition of a \(3q\) valence component and a \(3q+\pi\) non-valence component, with pions coupling directly to constituent quarks subject to an instantaneous confining force. The 1\(\gamma\)-exchange electron-nucleon scattering amplitude is calculated via a Feshbach reduction of a coupled-channel mass operator, and the electromagnetic and strong vertex form factors of the bare nucleon are both derived from the same microscopic 3-quark bound-state wave function, unlike prior approaches that used a phenomenological \(\pi \tilde{N} \tilde{N}\) form factor.

## Key numbers

- The pion-cloud contribution to nucleon form factors is significant only for momentum transfers \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The meson-cloud contribution amounts to about 10% for \(Q^2 \rightarrow 0\).
- The 3-quark bound-state wave function of the bare nucleon is parameterized as \(\Phi_{\tilde N}\left({\tilde {\bf k}}_i\right)=\frac{\mathcal{N}}{\left((\sum \tilde{\omega}_i)^2 + \beta^2\right)^\gamma}\), with parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\) taken from Pasquini (2007).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid constituent-quark model approach of Miller (2002) and Pasquini (2007), who used the light-front formalism, by instead adopting the point-form approach previously applied to mesonic form-factor calculations by Biernat (2009, 2010, 2014) and Gomez Rocha (2012). The key advance is that both the electromagnetic form factors of the bare nucleon and the strong \(\pi \tilde{N} \tilde{N}\) vertex form factor are calculated from the same microscopic 3-quark wave function, rather than using a phenomenological form factor for the pion-nucleon vertex. The results enable future refinements including a more sophisticated 3-quark wave function with mixed \(SU(6)\) spin-flavor symmetry, replacement of pseudoscalar by pseudovector \(\pi \tilde{N} \tilde{N}\) coupling, and inclusion of \(\Delta\) baryons in the pion loop.
