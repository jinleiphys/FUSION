# Uncertainty quantification of effective nuclear interactions

> We give a brief review on the development of phenomenological NN interactions and the corresponding quantification of statistical uncertainties. We look into the uncertainty of effective interactions broadly used in mean field calculations through the Skyrme parameters and effective field theory counter-terms by estimating both statistical and systematic uncertainties stemming from the NN interaction. We also comment on the role played by different fitting strategies on the light of recent developments.

# Uncertainty quantification of effective nuclear interactions

## Key claim

The paper argues that while phenomenological NN interactions can achieve statistically equivalent fits to scattering data (with normally distributed residuals and χ²/d.o.f. ~ 1), the systematic uncertainties arising from different theoretical representations of the NN interaction are at least an order of magnitude larger than the statistical uncertainties from any single potential, particularly for Skyrme parameters and EFT counter-terms.

## Method

The authors compute Moshinsky-Skyrme parameters and chiral EFT counter-terms from six statistically equivalent phenomenological NN potentials (DS-OPE, DS-χTPE, DS-Born, Gauss-OPE, Gauss-χTPE, Gauss-Born) by evaluating radial integrals of the coordinate-space potentials in specific partial waves. Statistical uncertainties are propagated from each potential's covariance matrix, while systematic uncertainties are estimated as the sample standard deviation across the six potentials at a renormalization scale Λ = 400 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The six potentials describe a self-consistent database of ~6700 np and pp scattering data below laboratory energies of 350 MeV with χ²/(N-P) ~ 1
- For the Skyrme parameter t₀, statistical uncertainties range from 53 to 289 MeV fm³ across individual potentials, while the systematic uncertainty (compilation) is 751 MeV fm³
- For the counter-term C_{¹S₀}, statistical uncertainties range from 0.01 to 0.02 × 10⁴ GeV⁻⁴ across potentials, while the systematic uncertainty is 0.06 × 10⁴ GeV⁻⁴
- The SAID analysis yields χ²/N ~ 1.7 for data below 350 MeV, which is 25 σ away from any reasonable confidence level
- The Optimized Chiral NN Interaction at N²LO (Ekstrom 2013) fits N = 1945 scattering data below 125 MeV with χ²/d.o.f. = 1.15, already 5 σ excluded as a consistent fit

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Granada analysis (Perez 2013, 2014) which produced statistically consistent NN potentials with normally distributed residuals, contrasting with the SAID analysis and the chiral potential of Ekstrom (2013) which fail normality tests. The paper enables uncertainty quantification for nuclear structure calculations using Skyrme forces and chiral EFT, showing that systematic uncertainties from different NN interaction representations dominate over statistical ones, as previously observed for phase-shifts and scattering amplitudes (Perez 2014). The authors recommend fitting to the average and standard deviation of their six potentials rather than to single phase-shift analyses.
