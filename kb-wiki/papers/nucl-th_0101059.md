# Relativistic Contributions to the Photon Asymmetry in Deuteron Photodisintegration

> The cross section asymmetry $Σ$ in deuteron disintegration with linearly polarized light is considered in the framework of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. Relativistic contributions to the asymmetry are investigated within the approximation, which takes into account the one-body part of the electromagnetic current operator and the dominant positive-energy partial amplitudes of the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The photon asymmetry Σ in deuteron photodisintegration shows strong sensitivity to relativistic effects, but the analysis is limited to the one-body part of the electromagnetic current operator and the dominant positive-energy partial amplitudes of the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function, meaning a fully quantitative description requires inclusion of two-body currents and negative-energy states.

## Method

The authors compute the cross-section asymmetry Σ for deuteron photodisintegration with linearly polarized photons using the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) formalism, employing the one-body plane-wave approximation for the electromagnetic current operator and the dominant positive-energy components of the BS vertex function for the deuteron. The vertex function is obtained as a solution of the homogeneous BS equation with the Graz-II separable interaction kernel, and the matrix elements are calculated in the transverse gauge. The analysis compares the exact positive-energy BS calculation with approximations that neglect the Lorentz boost on internal and spin variables (static approximation) and with the nonrelativistic impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry Σ at Θ = 90° is calculated as a function of laboratory photon energy Eγ, with results shown for the exact positive-energy BS calculation, the static approximation, and the nonrelativistic impulse approximation (Fig. 1).
- The D-state strength of the Graz-II interaction kernel is 5%.
- The minima in the S-waves of the one-boson-exchange and Graz-II vertex functions are shifted by approximately 100 MeV.
- The D-wave of the realistic vertex function is much softer than that of the separable one, which becomes crucial for the asymmetry behavior at photon energies higher than 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to the differential cross section (Kazakov and Shulga, 2000) to the polarization observable Σ in deuteron photodisintegration. It contrasts with nonrelativistic treatments that include meson-exchange currents and delta-isobar configurations (Arenhövel, 1990s) and with relativistic corrections based on the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction carried out to order O(1/m²) (Jaus, 1990). The results show that relativistic effects significantly influence the asymmetry, motivating a more complete analysis including two-body currents and negative-energy states to achieve quantitative description.
