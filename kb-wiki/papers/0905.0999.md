# Photo- and Electroproduction of Kaons

> Isobar models for the electromagnetic production of K+ and K0 are discussed with emphasis on the K0 photoproduction off neutron. Predictions of the models for the K0 photoproduction on deuteron are compared with the recent data and some properties of the elementary amplitudes are shown.

## Key claim

Existing isobar models for K⁺ photoproduction need revision at forward kaon angles before they can give reliable predictions for hypernuclear calculations, and data on d(γ,K⁰)YN' reactions near threshold can discriminate between models that otherwise fit p(γ,K⁺)Λ data equally well.

## Method

The author compares two isobar models (Saclay-Lyon (SLA) and Kaon-MAID (KM)) for electromagnetic kaon production, using effective Lagrangian approaches with Born terms and resonance exchanges. Calculations for K⁰ photoproduction on the deuteron are performed in the impulse approximation with a nonrelativistic Bonn deuteron wave function, assuming final-state interactions are small in the studied kinematical region. The models are tested against recent inclusive momentum spectra data from Tohoku University for the d(γ,K⁰)Λp process.

## Key numbers

- At forward angle θ_K^lab = 3° and photon lab energy 1.42 GeV, SLA predicts 2.24 μb/sr and KM predicts 1.60 μb/sr for the K⁺ lab cross section (30% discrepancy).
- For photoproduction of ¹²B_Λ at 1.42 GeV, excitation cross sections for the two main multiplets are 169 and 173 nb/sr for SLA, and 119 and 127 nb/sr for KM.
- For the SLA model fitted to lower-energy-region data, χ²_n.d.f. = 0.88 with r_K₁ = -2.09; for KM fitted to the same data, χ²_n.d.f. = 3.64 with r_K₁ = -2.34.
- The ratio r_K* = -√(Γ_K*⁰→K⁰γ / Γ_K*⁺→K⁺γ) = -1.53, with sign from quark model prediction.
- In KM, the fitted ratio r_K₁ = -0.45; in SLA, r_K₁ is a free parameter.

## Context

This work extends isobar model comparisons for kaon photoproduction (SLA from Saclay-Lyon, KM from Kaon-MAID by Bennhold et al. (1999), and Janssen et al. (2001)) to the K⁰ channel on neutrons and deuterons, using new data from Tohoku University (Tsubota et al., 2008). The results enable better constraints on elementary amplitudes needed for hypernucleus electroproduction calculations, as previously discussed by Bydzovsky (2004) for hypernuclear production. The study contradicts the assumption that models fitting p(γ,K⁺)Λ data equally well are interchangeable, showing that K⁰ data can discriminate between them.
