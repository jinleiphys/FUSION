# Nonequilibrium pion distribution within the Zubarev approach

> We discuss how the nonequilibrium process of pion production within the Zubarev approach of the nonequilibrium statistical operator leads to a theoretical foundation for the appearance of a nonequilibrium pion chemical potential for the pion distribution function for which there is experimental evidence in experiments at the CERN LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Zubarev nonequilibrium statistical operator (NSO) formalism provides a theoretical foundation for the appearance of a nonequilibrium pion chemical potential in the pion distribution function, which is needed to explain experimental observations at LHC; the key condition is that the pion number becomes effectively conserved on the relevant timescale because elastic collisions dominate over inelastic (absorptive) processes, with the relaxation time for absorptive processes satisfying τπ,abs > tfo.

## Method

The authors apply the Zubarev NSO method to a fireball model of pions, nucleons, Δ resonances, and other hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions, splitting the interaction Hamiltonian into an elastic part Hcol that conserves pion number and an inelastic part Hreac that changes it. They construct relevant statistical operators with progressively larger sets of relevant observables: the Kubo case (only conserved quantities), inclusion of pion number as a relevant observable, single-particle occupation numbers leading to kinetic equations, and finally a coherent-state formulation with non-diagonal operators to describe Bose-Einstein condensate formation. The self-consistency conditions determine the Lagrange multipliers, and the evolution equations for the relevant observables are derived from the NSO.

## Key numbers

- For LHC at √s = 2.76 TeV, measured proton abundances disagree with the standard thermal model based on the grand canonical ensemble (Stachel 2013, Becattini 2012).
- The condition for the relevant timescales is given as τπ,el ≪ tfo < τπ,abs, where τπ,el is the elastic collision time, tfo is the thermal freeze-out time, and τπ,abs is the absorptive process time.
- The freeze-out condition is τcol(tfo) = τexp(tfo), where τcol = Σd ⟨vcd σcd⟩ nd is the momentum-averaged collision time and τexp = q/·q is the expansion timescale.
- The critical temperature for Bose-Einstein condensation is determined by the condition E0,π − μπ(t) = 0 for the condensate state.

## Context

This work extends the thermal statistical model approach of Braun-Munzinger (2001), Andronic (2017), and Stachel (2013) by addressing the failure of the standard grand canonical ensemble to describe proton abundances at LHC energies, as noted by Petran (2013) and Begun (2013). The paper provides a theoretical foundation for the phenomenological pion chemical potential used by Kataja (1990) and Gavin (1991) to describe enhanced low-transverse momentum pion spectra, and it enables a systematic treatment of nonequilibrium effects beyond the single-particle distribution functions used in transport codes based on kinetic equations (Welke and Bertsch, Semikoz 1994, Voskresensky 1996).
