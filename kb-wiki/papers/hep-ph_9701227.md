# Study Medium-induced Parton Energy Loss in Gamma+jet Events of High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The effect of medium-induced parton energy loss on jet fragmentation is studied in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. It is shown that an effective jet fragmentation function can be extracted from the inclusive $p_T$ spectrum of charged particles in the opposite direction of a tagged direct photon with a fixed transverse energy. We study the modification of the effective jet fragmentation function due to parton energy loss in AA as compared to pp collisions, including $E_T$ smearing from initial state radiations for the photon-tagged jets. The effective fragmentation function at $z=p_T/E_T^γ\sim 1$ in pA collisions is shown to be sensitive to the additional $E_T$ smearing due to initial multiple parton scatterings whose effect must be subtracted out in AA collisions in order to extract the effective parton energy loss. Jet quenching in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scatterings as a measure of the parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter is also discussed. We also comment on the experimental feasibilities of the proposed study at the RHIC and LHC energies and some alternative measurements such as using $Z^0$ as a tag at the LHC energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the modification of the jet fragmentation function due to medium-induced parton energy loss can be extracted from the inclusive charged-particle transverse momentum spectrum in the direction opposite a tagged direct photon in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, but the extraction requires careful subtraction of the transverse energy smearing from initial-state multiple parton scatterings, particularly at fractional momentum values near unity.

## Method

The authors model the modification of jet fragmentation functions using a phenomenological approach where a fast parton traveling through a dense medium undergoes multiple inelastic scatterings with a Poisson-distributed probability, each scattering causing an average energy loss epsilon and transverse momentum broadening. They compute the inclusive charged-particle transverse momentum spectrum from photon-tagged jets by convolving the parton-level cross sections for direct photon production with the modified fragmentation functions, including transverse energy smearing effects obtained from HIJING Monte Carlo simulations of initial-state parton showers. The analysis is performed for central gold-gold collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energy of 200 GeV per nucleon pair and for lead-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energy.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the minimum tagged photon transverse energy required to extract the fragmentation function is E_T^gamma_min = 6 GeV for A=200 (gold).
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, a tagged photon with E_T^gamma = 60 GeV produces a fragmentation hadron spectrum that is clearly above background; for E_T^gamma = 40 GeV the fragmentation spectrum is roughly as large as the background.
- The average transverse energy of the jet parton from HIJING simulations for a tagged photon with E_T^gamma = 10 GeV at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is ⟨E_T⟩ = 8.08 GeV; for E_T^gamma = 15 GeV it is ⟨E_T⟩ = 12.57 GeV.
- The acceptance factor C(Δy,Δφ) for the kinematic region |y|≤0.5 and |φ-φ̅_γ|≤1.0 is approximately 0.5, independent of collision energy and photon energy.

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic (1996) to use photon-tagged jets for measuring parton energy loss, by incorporating the transverse energy smearing from initial-state radiations that was neglected in that prior study. The paper builds on the theoretical framework of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff (BDMPS, 1996) who showed that the radiative energy loss per unit distance grows linearly with the medium length and is related to the parton density of the medium. The proposed measurement enables experimental determination of the parton energy loss and mean-free-path in dense QCD matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, and the authors also discuss how similar measurements in deeply inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering could probe parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter.
