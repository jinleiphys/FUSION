# Effects of Phi and $σ^{*}$-meson on properties of hyperon stars including $Δ$ resonance

> In this work, we study the properties of neutron stars using the linear Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) theory and consider multiple degrees of freedom inside neutron stars, including hyperons and $Δ$ resonances. We investigate different coupling parameters $x_{σΔ}$ between $Δ$ resonances and nucleons and compare the differences between neutron stars with and without strange mesons $σ^*$ and $ϕ$. These effects include particle number distributions, equations of state (EOS), mass-radius relations, and tidal deformabilities. To overcome the "hyperon puzzle," we employ the $σ-cut$ scheme to obtain neutron stars with masses up to $2M_{\odot}$. We find that strange mesons appear at around 3$ρ_0$ and reduce the critical density of baryons in the high-density region. With increasing coupling parameter $x_{σΔ}$, the $Δ$ resonances suppress hyperons, leading to a shift of the critical density towards lower values. The early appearance of $Δ$ resonances may play a crucial role in the stability of neutron stars. Strange mesons soften the EOS slightly, while $Δ$ resonances predominantly soften the EOS in the low-density region. By calculating tidal deformabilities and comparing with astronomical observation GW170817, we find that the inclusion of $Δ$ resonances decreases the radius of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The inclusion of strange mesons ($\sigma^*$ and $\phi$) and $\Delta$ resonances in neutron star models, combined with the $\sigma$-cut scheme, can produce neutron stars with masses exceeding $2M_{\odot}$ while satisfying tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 only when the $\sigma$-cut scheme is not applied; the $\sigma$-cut scheme stiffens the equation of state enough to reach $2M_{\odot}$ but produces tidal deformabilities that fall outside the GW170817 constraint.

## Method

The authors used the IUFSU relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with baryon octet (n, p, $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, $\Xi$) and $\Delta$ resonances ($\Delta^{++}$, $\Delta^{+}$, $\Delta^{0}$, $\Delta^{-}$), including strange mesons $\sigma^*$ and $\phi$ that couple only to hyperons. They varied the scalar coupling parameter $x_{\sigma\Delta}$ between $\Delta$ resonances and nucleons (values 1.05, 1.1, 1.15) while fixing $x_{\omega\Delta}=1.1$ and $x_{\rho\Delta}=1.0$, and applied the $\sigma$-cut scheme with parameter $c_{\sigma}=0.15$ to stiffen the equation of state at high density. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass without $\sigma$-cut scheme: 1.46 $M_{\odot}$ (n,p,Y only), decreasing to 1.40 $M_{\odot}$ for $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.15$ (n,p,Y,D)
- Maximum neutron star mass with $\sigma$-cut scheme ($c_{\sigma}=0.15$): 2.15 $M_{\odot}$ (n,p,Y only), decreasing to 2.02 $M_{\odot}$ for $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.15$ (n,p,Y,D)
- Strange mesons $\sigma^*$ and $\phi$ appear at approximately 3$\rho_0$ (three times nuclear saturation density)
- Threshold density for $\Delta^{-}$ resonance without $\sigma$-cut: 2.13$\rho_0$ ($x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.05$), 1.90$\rho_0$ ($x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.1$), 1.74$\rho_0$ ($x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.15$)
- Radius of 1.4 $M_{\odot}$ neutron star: tidal deformability $\Lambda_{1.4}=190^{+390}_{-120}$ from GW170817 constraint
- Neutron star radius with $\sigma$-cut and $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.15$: 12.91 km (maximum mass 2.02 $M_{\odot}$)
- Neutron star radius without $\sigma$-cut and $x_{\sigma\Delta}=1.15$: 10.1 km (maximum mass 1.40 $M_{\odot}$)

## Context

This work extends the IUFSU RMF model studies of neutron star matter by Fattoyev (2010) and Grill (2014) to include both hyperons and $\Delta$ resonances simultaneously, addressing the "hyperon puzzle" identified by Burgio (2011) and Bombaci (2016) where hyperons soften the equation of state too much. The $\sigma$-cut scheme follows the approach of Maslov (2015) to stiffen the equation of state at high density. The results enable comparisons with NICER observations of PSR J0030-0451 and MSP J0740+6620 (Riley 2021, Fonseca 2021) and gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 (LIGO Scientific Collaboration 2018), showing that $\Delta$ resonances reduce neutron star radii and that future binary neutron star merger events may constrain tidal deformability at $2.0 M_{\odot}$.
