# Neutron stars in a class of non-linear relativistic models

> We introduce in this work a class of relativistic models for nuclear matter and neutron stars which exhibits a parameterization, through mathematical constants, of the non-linear meson-baryon couplings. For appropriate choices of the parameters, it recovers current QHD models found in the literature: Walecka and the Zimanyi and Moszkowski models (ZM and ZM3). For other choices of the parameters, the new models give very interesting and new physical results.We have obtained numerical values for the maximum mass and radius of a neutron star sequence, red-shift, hyperon populations, radial distribution of particles, among other relevant static properties of these stellar objects. The phenomenology of neutron stars in ZM models is presented and compared to the phenomenology obtained in other versions of the Walecka model. We have found that the ZM3 model is too soft and predicts a very small maximum neutron star mass, $\sim0.72 M_\odot$. A strong similarity between the results of ZM-like models and those with exponential couplings is noted. Sensibility of the results to the specific choice of the values for the binding energy and saturation density is pointed out. Finally, we discuss the very intense scalar condensates found in the interior of neutron stars which may lead to negative effective masses.

# Neutron Stars in a Class of Non-Linear Relativistic Models

## Key claim

The paper introduces a class of relativistic models for nuclear matter and neutron stars that uses mathematical parameters (λ, β, γ) to control the non-linear meson-baryon couplings, recovering the Walecka, Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM), and ZM3 models as special cases. The authors claim that for intermediate parameter values, the new models produce "very interesting and new physical results," but note that the ZM3 model is "too soft" and predicts a very small maximum neutron star mass of approximately 0.72 solar masses, and that results are sensitive to the specific choices of binding energy and saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed a phenomenological QHD Lagrangian with parameterized meson-baryon couplings of the form m*_nB = (1 + g_σ σ / (n M_B))^{-n} for n = λ, β, γ, applied the mean-field approximation, and solved the resulting field equations together with the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain neutron star properties. They considered two cases: scalar-only variations (λ varied, β = γ = 0) which recovers Walecka and ZM models, and scalar-vector variations (λ = β = γ) which recovers Walecka and ZM3 models, and extended the formalism to include the full baryon octet and leptons (electrons and muons) under chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- ZM3 model maximum neutron star mass: ~0.72 M_⊙ (too soft, very small)
- Saturation density used: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Saturation binding energy: B = -16 MeV
- Compression modulus for BB model: K = 250 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass for BB model: M*/M = 0.75
- Symmetry energy coefficient: a₄ = 32.5 MeV
- BB model coupling constants: (g_σ/m_σ)² = 9.86 fm², (g_ω/m_ω)² = 5.85 fm², (g_ρ/m_ρ)² = 4.80 fm², b = 0.00103, c = 0.0100
- Relativistic coefficients: R_W = 0.931, R_BB = 0.963, R_ZM = 0.970, R_ZM3 = 0.959
- Electron chemical potential saturates at ~200 MeV in BB model with hyperons
- Neutron star radius with hyperons: ~11 km; without hyperons: ~12 km (BB model)
- Energy density in heavier stars approximately constant around 10¹⁴ g/cm³

## Context

This work extends the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) framework of Walecka (1986) and the derivative coupling models of Zimanyi and Moszkowski (1990) by introducing a parameterized class of non-linear couplings that interpolates between existing models. It builds on the Boguta-Bodmer model (with cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions) and the ZM/ZM3 models, applying them for the first time to neutron star structure with hyperon degrees of freedom. The results enable systematic comparison of how different coupling schemes affect neutron star maximum masses, radii, and particle populations, and point toward the need for models that avoid the very soft EOS of ZM3 while maintaining reasonable effective masses.
