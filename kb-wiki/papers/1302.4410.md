# Nucleon Excited States in N$_f$=2 lattice QCD

> We investigate the excited states of the nucleon using $N_f=2$ twisted mass gauge configurations with pion masses in the range of about 270 MeV to 450 MeV and one ensemble of $N_f=2$ Clover fermions at almost physical pion mass. We use two different sets of variational bases and study the resulting generalized eigenvalue problem. We present results for the two lowest positive and negative parity states.

## Key claim

The variational method for extracting nucleon excited states from lattice QCD, when applied with the ALPHA collaboration's prescription of varying the reference time \(t_0\), does not show improvement over the standard fixed-\(t_0\) approach within the statistical accuracy typical of baryon calculations (about \(10^2\) configurations); the key condition is that the energy gap between states is not particularly small, so the systematic error reduction predicted by Luscher and Wolff (1990) is not observed at this precision.

## Method

The authors use \(N_f=2\) twisted mass fermion gauge configurations at five pion masses between 270 MeV and 450 MeV (lattice spacing \(a=0.089\) fm) and one \(N_f=2\) Clover fermion ensemble at near-physical pion mass (160 MeV, \(a\simeq0.073\) fm). They construct variational bases from different Gaussian smearing levels of the nucleon interpolating field \(\chi_1 = (u^T C\gamma_5 d)u\) (basis A with five smearing levels, basis B with three heavily smeared levels) and from combinations of \(\chi_1\) and \(\chi_2 = (u^T C d)\gamma_5 u\) with two smearing levels, then solve the generalized eigenvalue problem (GEVP) to extract the two lowest positive- and negative-parity states.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses for the twisted mass ensembles: 0.2696(9) GeV, 0.3082(6) GeV, 0.3131(16) GeV, 0.3903(9) GeV, and 0.4470(12) GeV (from Table 1).
* Lattice spacing for twisted mass ensembles: \(a = 0.089(1)(5)\) fm from the nucleon mass.
* Lattice spacing for the Clover ensemble: \(a = 0.0728(5)(19)\) fm.
* Clover ensemble pion mass: \(m_\pi \simeq 160\) MeV.
* Gaussian smearing parameters for basis A: \(\alpha=0.1\), \(n_s = 0, 22, 45, 67, 135\), producing root-mean-square radii in lattice units of 0, 1.96, 2.72, 3.25, and 4.48.
* Gaussian smearing parameters for basis B: \(\alpha=4.0\), \(n_s = 10, 50, 180\), producing rms radii in lattice units of 2.36, 4.87, and 8.60.
* Condition number of the \(3\times3\) GEVP ranges from \(10^4\) to \(10^6\) for basis A and is of order \(10^6\) for basis B; using \(n_s=300\) increases the condition number to \(\mathcal{O}(10^9)\).
* For the positive parity ground state, a plateau is reached from time-slice \(t/a=5\) using basis A and from \(t/a=4\) for the first excited state; with basis B, faster convergence is observed.

## Context

This work extends the variational approach to nucleon excited states pioneered by Luscher and Wolff (1990) and refined by the ALPHA collaboration (Blossier 2009), applying it to \(N_f=2\) lattice QCD with both twisted mass and Clover fermions. The finding that heavily smeared interpolating fields are crucial for lowering the first excited positive-parity state (the Roper candidate) confirms the strategy advocated by Mahbub (2010) and Roberts (2012) for producing a nodal wavefunction. The results enable future studies of the Roper resonance at physical pion mass and provide guidance for choosing variational bases in baryon spectroscopy calculations.
