# Alpha Clustering and the stellar nucleosynthesis of carbon

## Key claim
The paper shows that stellar carbon production via the triple-alpha process is extremely sensitive to small variations in the effective nucleon-nucleon (N-N) interaction, with a 0.1% change in interaction strength altering the total reaction rate by a factor of 250 at 10^8 K. This sensitivity is driven by shifts of 10-30 keV in the resonance energies of the 8Be ground state and the 0_2^+ state in 12C, which enter exponentially in the Boltzmann factors.

## Method
The astrophysical S-factor and reaction rates for the triple-alpha process were calculated using the direct-capture (DC) model with renormalized folding potentials, implemented in the computer code TEDCA. The resonant contribution of the 0_2^+ state in 12C at 0.2875 MeV and the nonresonant background, including their interference, were obtained simultaneously. Small variations in the strength of the effective N-N interaction were introduced without changing the renormalization factors in the folding potentials, and the resulting changes in resonance energies and widths were used to compute modified reaction rates for the first step (8Be ground state formation) and second step (8Be(alpha,gamma)12C) separately.

## Key numbers
- Resonance energy of the 0_2^+ state in 12C: 0.2875 MeV (287.5 keV) for the unmodified N-N interaction.
- Total width of the 0_2^+ state: Gamma(0_2^+) = 7.5 eV (calculated), compared to experimental value (8.3 ± 1.0) eV.
- Gamma width of the 0_2^+ state: Gamma_gamma(0_2^+) = 4.1 meV (calculated), compared to experimental value (3.7 ± 0.5) meV.
- For a +0.1% variation in N-N interaction strength: 8Be ground state resonance energy shifts to 79.2 keV, 0_2^+ resonance energy shifts to 251.7 keV, total width becomes 1.5 eV, and the total reaction rate factor F_T = 250 (relative to 1 at 0% variation).
- For a -0.1% variation: 8Be ground state resonance energy shifts to 103.7 keV, 0_2^+ resonance energy shifts to 323.0 keV, total width becomes 29 eV, and F_T = 0.0038.
- For a +0.2% variation: F_T = 6.7 × 10^4; for -0.2% variation: F_T = 1.5 × 10^-5.
- Depth of folding potentials: V_0 ≈ 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the direct-capture model calculations of Krauss (1993) and the alpha-alpha scattering and 12C state analysis of Staudt (1993) to quantify the sensitivity of the triple-alpha process to fundamental interactions. The results are directly relevant to the anthropic principle, following the prediction by Hoyle (1954) that carbon creation requires a resonant reaction, later confirmed experimentally by Dunbar (1953) and Hoyle (1953). Prior work by Pochet (1991) examined variations in coupling constants for deuteron unbinding and diproton binding, finding limits of about 16% and 12% respectively; the present paper shows that the triple-alpha process is far more sensitive, with 0.1% variations already significant for carbon production. This enables quantitative constraints on fundamental constant variations from stellar nucleosynthesis.
