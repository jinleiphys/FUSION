# Perturbative Vacuum Wavefunctional for Gauge Theories in the Milne Space

> The spectrum of vacuum fluctuations in the Milne space (i.e. the tau-eta coordinate system) is an important ingredient in the thermalization studies in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this paper, the Schrodinger functional for the gauge theory perturbative vacuum is derived for the Milne space. The Wigner-transform of the corresponding vacuum density functional is also found together with the propagators. We finally identify the fluctuation spectrum in vacuum, and show the equivalence between the present approach and the symplectic product based method.

## Context

This work extends the initial attempt by Fukushima (2006) to derive the vacuum wavefunctional in Milne space, and provides the formal foundation for the classical-statistical approximation used in thermalization studies of relativistic heavy ion collisions. The results enable numerical implementations of the initial value problem for gauge theories in the Glasma picture, where the vacuum fluctuations serve as initial conditions for the classical Yang-Mills field evolution. The authors note that non-Abelian gauge theory vacuum can be obtained by having N_cВІ в€’ 1 copies of the Abelian vacuum within perturbation theory, and that the problem of vacuum fluctuations in a classical background field (as in Dusling (2011) and Epelbaum (2013)) remains an open question for future work.
