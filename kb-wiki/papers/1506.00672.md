# Perturbative Vacuum Wavefunctional for Gauge Theories in the Milne Space

> The spectrum of vacuum fluctuations in the Milne space (i.e. the tau-eta coordinate system) is an important ingredient in the thermalization studies in relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this paper, the Schrodinger functional for the gauge theory perturbative vacuum is derived for the Milne space. The Wigner-transform of the corresponding vacuum density functional is also found together with the propagators. We finally identify the fluctuation spectrum in vacuum, and show the equivalence between the present approach and the symplectic product based method.

## Key claim

The paper derives the perturbative vacuum wavefunctional for Abelian gauge theories in Milne coordinates, showing that the resulting propagators coincide with those obtained from the symplectic product based method of Dusling (2011) and Epelbaum (2013), but only for the free-field (quadratic) part of the Lagrangian and with the restriction that the initial proper time τ₀ must be kept finite since the kernel functions have no well-defined limit as τ₀ → 0.

## Method

The authors solve the functional Schrödinger equation for the vacuum state in the Aτ = 0 gauge, decomposing the spatial gauge field into a transverse component AT and longitudinal components Aη and φ (where φ is defined via ∇⊥·A⊥ = ∇⊥²φ). They employ a Gaussian ansatz for the vacuum wavefunctional and derive the kernel functions by requiring consistency with the Minkowski space vacuum, selecting the Hankel function of the first kind H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) to ensure positive definiteness of the kernel's real part. The Wigner transform of the vacuum density operator is then computed in the retarded-advanced (Keldysh) basis, and the r-a and symmetric propagators are obtained from the generating functional.

## Key numbers

- The transverse vacuum wavefunctional kernel is τ²G_T(τ,k̃) = −iΠ⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) / H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ), where Π⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(x) = x ∂_x H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(x)
- The real part of the transverse kernel is R_T(τ,k̃) = 2 / [π H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) H⁽²⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ)] > 0
- The longitudinal kernel is τ²G_η(τ,k̃) = k⊥² H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) / [−iΠ⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ)]
- The real part of the longitudinal kernel is R_η(τ,k̃) = 2k⊥² / [π Π⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) Π⁽²⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ)] > 0
- The retarded propagator for the transverse field is G_R(τ,τ';k̃) = (π/4) θ(τ−τ') [H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) H⁽²⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ') − H⁽²⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ')]
- The symmetric propagator for the transverse field is ⟨A_{rT}(τ,−k̃) A_{rT}(τ',k̃)⟩ = (π/8) [H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) H⁽²⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ') + H⁽²⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ) H⁽¹⁾_{ikη}(k⊥τ')]

## Context

This work extends the initial attempt by Fukushima (2006) to derive the vacuum wavefunctional in Milne space, and provides the formal foundation for the classical-statistical approximation used in thermalization studies of relativistic heavy ion collisions. The results enable numerical implementations of the initial value problem for gauge theories in the Glasma picture, where the vacuum fluctuations serve as initial conditions for the classical Yang-Mills field evolution. The authors note that non-Abelian gauge theory vacuum can be obtained by having N_c² − 1 copies of the Abelian vacuum within perturbation theory, and that the problem of vacuum fluctuations in a classical background field (as in Dusling (2011) and Epelbaum (2013)) remains an open question for future work.
