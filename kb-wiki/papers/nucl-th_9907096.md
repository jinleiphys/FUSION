# Reconstructing the Freeze-out State in Pb+Pb Collisions at 158 AGeV/c

> For a class of analytical parametrizations of the freeze-out state of relativistic heavy ion collisions, we perform a simultaneous analysis of the single-particle m_t-spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations measured in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS. The analysis includes a full model parameter scan with chi^2 confidence levels. A comparison of different transverse density profiles for the particle emission region allows for a quantitative discussion of possible model dependencies of the results. Our fit results suggest a low thermal freeze-out temperature T approximately 95 +- 15 MeV and a large average transverse flow velocity of about 0.55c +- 0.07c. Moreover, the fit favours a box-shaped transverse density profile over a Gaussian one. We discuss the origins and the consequences of these results in detail. In order to reproduce the measured pion multiplicity our model requires a positive pion chemical potential. A study of the pion phase-space density indicates μ_πapproximately 60 MeV for T = 100 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that simultaneous fits to single-particle spectra and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV/c favor a low freeze-out temperature and strong transverse flow, but this conclusion depends on the assumed transverse density profile and is subject to significant systematic uncertainties from the choice of profile shape and from unresolved experimental inconsistencies between different detector components.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous chi-squared analysis of single-particle transverse mass spectra and two-particle HBT correlation radii (in the Cartesian parametrization) using an analytical fireball model with a Boltzmann-type emission function that includes longitudinal and transverse collective flow, resonance decay contributions, and either a box-shaped or Gaussian transverse density profile. They scanned the full temperature-transverse flow parameter space, fitting the remaining geometric parameters (source radius, freeze-out proper time, and duration) at each point, and compared results for the two transverse profiles to assess model dependence.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T = 95 ± 15 MeV (from combined fit to spectra and correlations)
- Average transverse flow velocity v̄⊥ = 0.55c ± 0.07c (from combined fit)
- Pion chemical potential μπ ≈ 60 MeV at T = 100 MeV (from phase-space density analysis)
- Box-shaped transverse profile favored over Gaussian; for box model, best-fit temperature range 80–110 MeV and average transverse velocity 0.47c–0.62c
- Measured negative hadron multiplicity 715 ± 30 (used for yield normalization)

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Wiedemann, Tomasik, and Heinz (1997) and others that attempted to disentangle temperature and flow effects in heavy-ion collisions, but provides the first full model parameter scan with chi-squared confidence levels for two-particle spectra and a quantitative assessment of model dependence from different transverse density profiles. The results enable more reliable dynamical back-extrapolation to the early hot and dense stage of the collision and provide an experimentally determined endpoint for microscopic simulations; the finding of a positive pion chemical potential suggests possible multiparticle effects such as stimulated emission or a pion laser, as previously discussed by Bertsch (1994).
