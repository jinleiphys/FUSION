# Surface effects on hydrodynamic evolution

> We study the effect of surface tension of the phase boundary in the dynamics of an expanding fluid. A fluid at local thermal equilibrium, but has a slowly varying temperature profile, like the plasma formed in heavy ion collisions, will have rapidly varying order parameter field at the edge of the plasma where the temperature falls below the transition temperature. In the case where the free energy admits a first order transition, the gradient energy of this field will act as surface tension. We couple hydrodynamics and order parameter field evolutions to study the effect of this surface in the expansion of the plasma. We see that the surface slows down the expansion which reflects in the development of radial flow and momentum anisotropy.

# Surface effects on hydrodynamic evolution

## Key claim
Surface tension at the phase boundary of an expanding quark-gluon plasma, modeled via gradient energy of the Polyakov loop order parameter field, slows the development of radial flow and momentum anisotropy; this effect is strongest for small systems and low initial energy densities, with up to 40% suppression of radial flow at 15 fm/c for a 2 fm initial radius and 2 GeV/fm³ initial energy density.

## Method
The authors couple relativistic hydrodynamics to the evolution of the Polyakov loop order parameter field using the Pisarski effective potential, which yields a weak first-order phase transition for pure gauge theory. The fluid energy-momentum tensor and the field gradient energy are evolved together in 2+1 dimensions with cylindrical symmetry, using the Kurganov-Tadmor scheme for the fluid conservation equations and a Lax-Friedrichs scheme for the field equation of motion, with initial Gaussian energy density profiles for both central (spherical) and non-central (ellipsoidal) geometries.

## Key numbers
- Radial flow suppression reaches 40% at 15 fm/c for an initial radius of 2 fm and initial energy density 2 GeV/fm³.
- At 4 fm initial radius and 4 GeV/fm³ initial energy density, radial flow suppression is still 5%.
- Momentum anisotropy suppression decreases from 25% to 20% as the major axis of the ellipsoid increases from 3 fm to 5 fm (minor axis fixed at 2 fm, maximum energy density 2 GeV/fm³).
- The Polyakov loop model parameters used: b₃ = 2.0, b₄ = 0.6016, α = 1.6 (for g/4π = 0.3, N = 3 colors).

## Context
This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions by incorporating surface tension effects that are absent in standard hydrodynamics even with a first-order equation of state. Prior approaches either used hydrodynamics alone (reviewed by flowrev, flowrev2) or order parameter field evolution without collective flow (rkmams1, rkmams2, rkmamsfo; Mishra (2016)). The coupling method introduced here enables study of how the phase boundary at the plasma edge, present whenever the temperature profile falls below T_c, affects flow observables. The results suggest that elliptic flow measurements at lower energies (FAIR, NICA) and in small systems may carry signatures of first-order phase transition dynamics.
