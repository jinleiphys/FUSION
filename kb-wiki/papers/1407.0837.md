# Relaxation-time approximation and relativistic third-order viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory

> Using the iterative solution of Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation, the derivation of a third-order evolution equation for shear stress tensor is presented. To this end we first derive the expression for viscous corrections to the phase-space distribution function, $f(x,p)$, up to second-order in derivative expansion. The expression for $δf(x,p)$ obtained in this method does not lead to violation of the experimentally observed $1/\sqrt{m_T}$ scaling of the femtoscopic radii, as opposed to the widely used Grad's 14-moment approximation. Subsequently, we present the derivation of a third-order viscous evolution equation and demonstrate the significance of this derivation within one-dimensional scaling expansion. We show that results obtained using third-order evolution equations are in excellent accordance with the exact solution of Boltzmann equation as well as with transport results.

## Key claim

The paper derives a third-order evolution equation for the shear stress tensor from the iterative solution of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation, claiming that this third-order equation yields results in excellent accordance with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation and with transport results, whereas second-order Israel-Stewart theory shows disagreement for shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s > 0.5\).

## Method

The author starts from the relativistic Boltzmann equation with the relaxation-time approximation for the collision term and expands the distribution function iteratively in powers of space-time gradients, obtaining expressions for the viscous correction \(\delta f\) up to second order in derivatives. Using this \(\delta f\) in the definition of the shear stress tensor and its time derivative, the author derives a third-order evolution equation for \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) by keeping terms up to cubic order in gradients. The resulting equation is then tested within a one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion of a massless Boltzmann gas.

## Key numbers

* For initial conditions corresponding to RHIC: initial temperature \(T_0 = 300\) MeV at initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/c.
* For initial conditions corresponding to LHC: initial temperature \(T_0 = 500\) MeV at initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.4\) fm/c.
* The second-order Israel-Stewart theory shows disagreement with transport results for \(\eta/s > 0.5\).
* The third-order results from the entropy method overestimate pressure anisotropy for \(\eta/s > 0.2\) compared to BAMPS results.

## Context

This work extends the iterative solution method for the Boltzmann equation previously developed by Jaiswal (2013) and others, which avoids the Grad 14-moment approximation that leads to violation of the experimentally observed \(1/\sqrt{m_T}\) scaling of femtoscopic radii. The derived third-order equation contains fourteen third-order terms, compared to only two third-order terms in the equation obtained by invoking the second law of thermodynamics (El, 2009), confirming that the entropy-based derivation is incomplete. This work enables more accurate hydrodynamic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly for systems with larger shear viscosity.
