# Magic nuclei in superheavy valley

> An extensive theoretical search for the proton magic number in the superheavy valley beyond $Z=$82 and corresponding neutron magic number after $N=$126 is carried out. For this we scanned a wide range of elements $Z=112-130$ and their isotopes. The well established non-relativistic Skryme-Hartree-Fock and Relativistic Mean Field formalisms with various force parameters are used. Based on the calculated systematics of pairing gap, two neutron separation energy and the shell correction energy for these nuclei, we find $Z=$120 as the next proton magic and N=172, 182/184, 208 and 258 the subsequent neutron magic numbers.

## Key claim

Based on systematic calculations of pairing gaps, two-neutron separation energies, and shell correction energies across a wide range of superheavy nuclei (Z=112-130), the authors identify Z=120 as the next proton magic number beyond Z=82, with candidate neutron magic numbers at N=172, 182/184, 208, and 258, though the exact neutron magic numbers depend on the specific force parameterization used.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations with FITZ, SIII, SkMP, and SLy4 interactions, and Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations with NL3, G1, G2, and NL-Z2 parameter sets across all elements Z=112-130 and their isotopic chains. They evaluated the average pairing gaps for protons and neutrons using constant-strength pairing with BCS equations, the two-neutron separation energy, and the shell correction energy via the Strutinski method, then analyzed single-particle energy gaps as a confirmatory test.

## Key numbers

*   Binding energy per particle for $^{302}$120 with SHF (FITZ set) is 7.048 MeV, compared to 7.046 MeV for $^{300}$120 and 7.044 MeV for $^{304}$120.
*   Binding energy per particle for $^{302}$120 with SHF (SLy4 set) is 6.952 MeV, compared to 6.950 MeV for $^{300}$120 and 6.933 MeV for $^{304}$120.
*   The neutron single-particle energy gap at N=182 in $^{302}$120 (FITZ) is 1.977 MeV, comparable to 1.898 MeV for the known magic gap at N=50.
*   The proton single-particle energy gap at Z=120 in $^{302}$120 (FITZ) is 1.340 MeV, comparable to 1.862 MeV for the known magic gap at N=50.

## Context

This work extends previous macroscopic-microscopic predictions of superheavy magic numbers, such as Z=114 and N=184 from Patyk and Sobiczewski (1991) and Moller and Nix (1994), by using fully microscopic SHF and RMF formalisms with multiple force parameter sets. The results provide specific candidate nuclei for experimental searches, particularly $^{302}$120 and $^{304}$120, and suggest that the previously discussed Z=114 is only feebly magic in nature, redirecting experimental efforts toward Z=120 isotopes.
