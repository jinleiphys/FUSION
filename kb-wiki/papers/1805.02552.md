# Can pion spectra in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies be described by those in p+p reactions and energy-momentum conservation ? Rapidity distributions of pions in p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies

> The centrality dependence of rapidity distributions of pions in Pb+Pb reactions can be understood by imposing local energy-momentum conservation in the longitudinal "fire-streaks" of excited matter. With no tuning nor adjustment to the experimental data, the rapidity distribution of pions produced by the fire-streak which we obtained from Pb+Pb collisions reproduces the shape of the experimental pion rapidity distribution in p+p interactions, measured by the NA49 Collaboration at the same energy. The observed difference in the absolute normalization of this distribution can be explained by the difference in the overall energy balance, induced by baryon stopping and strangeness enhancement phenomena occurring in heavy ion collisions. We estimate the latter effects using a collection of SPS experimental data on $π^\pm$, $K^\pm$, net $p$, and $n$ production in p+p and Pb+Pb reactions. Implications of the above findings are discussed.

## Key claim

The shape of the pion rapidity distribution in p+p collisions can be reproduced by the single fire-streak fragmentation function inherited from Pb+Pb collisions without any tuning, but only after applying a normalization factor of 0.812 (after isospin correction) which the authors attribute to differences in energy balance from baryon stopping and strangeness enhancement in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors apply a fire-streak model previously developed for Pb+Pb collisions, which assumes local energy-momentum conservation in longitudinal "fire-streaks" of excited matter with a three-parameter fragmentation function. They take the single fire-streak fragmentation function parameters (A=0.05598, σ_y=1.475, r=2.55) obtained from fitting NA49 Pb+Pb data at √s_NN=17.3 GeV and directly compare the resulting function to NA49 p+p data at the same energy, after correcting for isospin differences between the p+p and Pb+Pb systems using the approach of weighting π⁺ and π⁻ distributions by the proton/neutron fractions in lead nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The fire-streak fragmentation function parameters obtained from Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN=17.3 GeV are A=0.05598, σ_y=1.475, and r=2.55
- The normalization factor needed to match the single fire-streak function to p+p→π⁻X data is 0.748; after isospin correction to N+N→π⁻X, the factor becomes 0.812
- The collision energy is √s_NN=17.3 GeV for both p+p and Pb+Pb data from the NA49 experiment
- The lead nucleus has Z/A=39.4% protons and (1-Z/A)=60.6% neutrons

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fire-streak model for Pb+Pb collisions (Rybicki et al., 2017) to p+p reactions, finding that the same fragmentation function shape applies. The results address the long-standing question of whether non-perturbative dynamics in p+p and heavy ion collisions are qualitatively similar, which has been debated in the context of strangeness enhancement as a signature of deconfinement (Rafelski, 1982; Smeś, 2012). The work enables further investigation of how baryon stopping and strangeness enhancement affect the energy balance between nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies.
