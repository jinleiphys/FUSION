# Nuclear rho transparencies in a relativistic Glauber model

> [Background] The recent Jefferson Lab data for the nuclear transparency in $ρ^ {0}$ electroproduction have the potential to settle the scale for the onset of color transparency (CT) in vector meson production. [Purpose] To compare the data to calculations in a relativistic and quantum-mechanical Glauber model and to investigate whether they are in accordance with results including color transparency given that the computation of $ρ$-nucleus attenuations is subject to some uncertainties. [Method] We compute the nuclear transparencies in a multiple-scattering Glauber model and account for effects stemming from color transparency, from $ρ$-meson decay, and from short-range correlations (SRC) in the final-state interactions (FSI). [Results] The robustness of the model is tested by comparing the mass dependence and the hard-scale dependence of the $A(e,e'p)$ nuclear transparencies with the data. The hard-scale dependence of the $(e,e' ρ^ {0})$ nuclear transparencies for $^ {12}$C and $^ {56}$Fe are only moderately affected by SRC and by $ρ^ {0}$-decay. [Conclusions] The RMSGA calculations confirm the onset of CT at four-momentum transfers of a few (GeV/c)$^2$ in $ρ$ meson electroproduction data. A more precise determination of the scale for the onset of CT is hampered by the lack of precise input in the FSI and $ρ$-meson decay calculations.

## Key claim

The RMSGA calculations confirm the onset of color transparency (CT) at four-momentum transfers of a few (GeV/c)^2 in rho meson electroproduction data, but a more precise determination of the scale for the onset of CT is hampered by the lack of precise input in the final-state interactions and rho-meson decay calculations.

## Method

The authors computed nuclear transparencies for rho0 electroproduction using a relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA) model, incorporating effects from color transparency via the quantum diffusion model, short-range correlations (SRC) in the final-state interactions, and rho-meson decay into pion pairs. The calculations were performed for 12C and 56Fe targets under the kinematic conditions of the JLab E02-110 experiment, with the transparency defined as the ratio of the RMSGA cross section to the plane-wave approximation (PWA) cross section for the same target nucleus.

## Key numbers

- For 12C with sigma_rhoN_tot = 25 mb and Delta M^2 = 0.5 (GeV/c^2)^2, the computed slope of the hard-scale dependence of the rho transparency is 0.040 (with SRC, CT, and decay), compared to the JLab data slope of 0.044 +/- 0.015 +/- 0.019.
- For 56Fe with sigma_rhoN_tot = 25 mb and Delta M^2 = 0.5 (GeV/c^2)^2, the computed slope is 0.029, compared to the JLab data slope of 0.053 +/- 0.008 +/- 0.013.
- The rho decay lowers the transparency by about 7% at the lowest Q^2 data point and 6% at the highest Q^2 data point.
- The SRC component in the FSI adds about 0.5% to the transparency, independent of the hard scale parameter.
- The CT effects increase the transparency by 3 to 6% from low to high Q^2.
- The formation length parameter Delta M^2 = 0.7 (GeV/c^2)^2 was used, with the value 0.7 (GeV/c^2)^2 reproducing measured nuclear pion transparencies.
- The rho0 decay width Gamma_rho = 149 MeV was used in the laboratory frame.

## Context

This work extends the RMSGA model previously applied to A(e,e'p) reactions (Ryckebusch 2003), A(p,2p) reactions (VanOvermeire 2006), and A(e,e'pi+) reactions (Cosyn 2006, 2007) to the case of rho0 electroproduction. The calculations are compared to recent JLab Hall-B experiment E02-110 data (El Fassi 2012) and to two other model calculations: the semi-classical Glauber model of Frankfurt, Miller, and Strikman (2008) and the semi-classical GiBUU transport model from the Giessen group (Gallmeister 2010). The results strengthen the case for an onset of CT at four-momentum transfers of a few (GeV/c)^2 in exclusive meson production reactions, following similar conclusions from pion transparency measurements (Clasie 2007).
