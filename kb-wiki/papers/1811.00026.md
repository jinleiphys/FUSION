# Determining the Nonperturbative Collins-Soper Kernel From Lattice QCD

> At small transverse momentum $q_T$, transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) arise as genuinely nonperturbative objects that describe Drell-Yan like processes in hadron collisions as well as semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering. TMDPDFs naturally depend on the hadron momentum, and the associated evolution is determined by the Collins-Soper equation. For $q_T \sim Λ_\mathrm{QCD}$ the corresponding evolution kernel (or anomalous dimension) is nonperturbative and must be determined as an independent ingredient in order to relate TMDPDFs at different scales. We propose a method to extract this kernel using lattice QCD and the Large-Momentum Effective Theory, where the physical TMD correlation involving light-like paths is approximated by a quasi TMDPDF, defined using equal-time correlation functions with a large-momentum hadron state. The kernel is determined from a ratio of quasi TMDPDFs extracted at different hadron momenta.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a first-principle lattice QCD method to extract the nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel \(\gamma_\zeta^q(\mu,b_T)\) for transverse momentum \(q_T \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), using a ratio of quasi-TMDPDFs computed at different hadron momenta \(P^z_1 \ne P^z_2\), with the caveat that the relation between quasi-TMDPDF and TMDPDF involves a nonperturbative soft mismatch factor \(g^S_q(b_T,\mu)\) that cancels in the ratio.

## Method

The authors define a quasi beam function \(\tilde B_q(b^z, \bt, a, L, P^z)\) using equal-time correlation functions with spatial Wilson lines of length \(L\) in a boosted hadron state, and combine it with a quasi soft factor \(\tilde\Delta_S^q(b_T, a, L)\) to form the quasi-TMDPDF \(\tilde f_q^\TMD\). The Collins-Soper kernel is extracted from the ratio of quasi-TMDPDFs at two different hadron momenta \(P^z_1\) and \(P^z_2\), using the perturbative matching coefficient \(C_\ns^\TMD(\mu, x P^z)\) computed to one-loop order, with the soft factors and TMDPDFs canceling in the ratio.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop matching coefficient is \(C^{(1)}(\mu, x P^z) = -\ln^2\frac{(2 x P^z)^2}{\mu^2} + 2 \ln\frac{(2 x P^z)^2}{\mu^2} - 4 + \frac{\pi^2}{6}\).
- The one-loop soft mismatch factor is \(g^S_q(b_T,\mu) = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{2\pi} \ln\frac{b_T^2 \mu^2}{4 e^{-2\gamma_E}} + \mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\).
- The Collins-Soper kernel is extracted via \(\gamma^q_\zeta(\mu, b_T) = \frac{1}{\ln(P^z_1/P^z_2)} \ln \frac{C^\TMD_\ns(\mu,x P_2^z)\, \tilde f^\TMD_\ns(x, \bt, \mu, P^z_1)}{C^\TMD_\ns(\mu,x P_1^z)\, \tilde f^\TMD_\ns(x, \bt, \mu, P^z_2)}\).
- The lattice calculations are restricted to \(\mu_0^2 \sim \zeta_0 \sim \mathcal{O}(4\ {\rm GeV}^2)\) due to finite lattice spacing, while Drell-Yan applications use \(\mu^2 \sim \zeta \sim m_Z^2 \approx (91\ {\rm GeV})^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Large-Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET) approach of Ji (2013, 2014) from collinear PDFs to TMDPDFs, building on earlier lattice studies of TMD correlators by Musch et al. (2010, 2011) and Engelhardt et al. (2015). The method enables first-principle determination of the nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel, which is needed to evolve TMDPDFs from lattice scales to phenomenologically relevant scales for Drell-Yan and SIDIS processes, complementing experimental extractions from global fits such as those by Bacchetta et al. (2017) and Scimemi and Vladimirov (2017).
