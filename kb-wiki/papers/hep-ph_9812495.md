# gamma^* gamma -> pi^0 transition and asymptotics of gamma^* gamma and gamma^* gamma^* transitions of other unflavored pseudoscalar mesons

> For the spacelike momenta k of the virtual photon gamma^*, the pi^0(p) gamma^*(k) gamma(k') transition form factor is considered in the coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach in conjunction with the generalized impulse approximation using the dressed quark-photon-quark vertices of the Ball-Chiu and Curtis-Pennington type. These form factors are compared with the ones predicted by the vector meson dominance, operator product expansion, QCD sum rules, and the perturbative QCD for the large spacelike transferred momenta k. The most important qualitative feature of the asymptotic behavior, namely the 1/k^2 dependence, is in our approach obtained in the model-independent way. Again model-independently, our approach reproduces also the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly result for the limit of both photons being real. For the case of one highly virtual photon, we find in the closed form the asymptotic expression which can be easily generalized both to the case of other unflavored pseudoscalar mesons P^0 = pi^0, eta_8, eta_0, eta_c, eta_b, and to the case of arbitrary virtuality of the other photon. Implications thereof for certain important theoretical and experimental applications are pointed out.

## Key claim
The coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach, using dressed quark-photon-quark vertices that satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity, reproduces both the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly at zero virtuality and the leading 1/k² asymptotic behavior for large spacelike momenta in a model-independent way, though the numerical value of the transition form factor at finite momenta depends on the choice of dressed vertex (Ball-Chiu versus Curtis-Pennington type).

## Method
The authors solved the coupled Schwinger-Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion bound-state vertex in the improved ladder approximation, using an effective gluon propagator with perturbative ultraviolet and modeled infrared parts. The π⁰γ*γ transition amplitude was computed via the generalized impulse approximation using dressed quark-photon-quark vertices of the Ball-Chiu and modified Curtis-Pennington types, which satisfy the vector Ward-Takahashi identity. Numerical evaluations were performed for spacelike photon momenta up to 8 GeV², and analytical asymptotic expressions were derived for the large-Q² limit.

## Key numbers
- The ABJ anomaly amplitude at Q²=0: T_π⁰(0,0) = 1/(4π² f_π)
- For the Ball-Chiu vertex at large Q²: Q² T_π⁰(-Q²,0) ≈ (4/3) f_π × [1+A(q̃²)]²/4, yielding J ≈ 1.78 with the model parameters (where A(0)=1.25)
- For the modified Curtis-Pennington vertex at Q²→∞: Q² T_π⁰(-Q²,0) = (4/3) f_π (J=4/3)
- The asymptotic pQCD prediction (Lepage-Brodsky) gives J=2; the Chernyak-Zhitnitsky distribution gives J=10/3; the OPE result of Manohar gives J=4/3
- The VMD and QCD sum rule results give J≈1.6, corresponding to a narrow distribution with ζ=2.5 in the parametrization φ_π(x) ∝ x^ζ (1-x)^ζ

## Context
This work extends the coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied by the authors to π⁰, η, η' → γγ decays (Kekez and Klabucar, 1998) and to η_c, η_b → γγ (Kekez, Klabucar, and Bistrović, 1998). It provides a unified description from Q²=0 to asymptotic Q² that is consistent with the axial anomaly, the operator product expansion result of Manohar (1990), and the QCD sum rule approach of Radyushkin and Ruskov, while also agreeing with the new CLEO data up to 8 GeV². The results enable recalculation of the π⁰γ*γ* form factor needed for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon g-2, replacing the double-VMD Ansatz used by Kinoshita and collaborators (1998) which gives incorrect asymptotic behavior.
