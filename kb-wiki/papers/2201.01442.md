# Determination of neutron-skin thickness using configurational information entropy

> Configurational information entropy (CIE) theory was employed to determine the neutron skin thickness of neutron-rich calcium isotopes. The nuclear density distributions and fragment cross-sections in 350 MeV/u $^{40-60}$Ca + $^{9}$Be projectile fragmentation reactions were calculated using a modified statistical abrasion-ablation model. CIE quantities were determined from the nuclear density, isotopic, mass, and charge distributions. The linear correlations between the CIE determined using the isotopic, mass, and charge distributions and the neutron skin thickness of the projectile nucleus show that CIE provides new methods to extract the neutron skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

Configurational information entropy (CIE) derived from isotopic, mass, and charge distributions in projectile fragmentation reactions shows a linear correlation with the neutron-skin thickness of the projectile nucleus, but the correlation for mass-yield CIE has a bend point at the neutron-skin thickness of $^{42}$Ca due to the transition from proton-skin to neutron-skin, and the correlation for $\Delta\rho$ CIE is linear except for $^{40}$Ca.

## Method

The authors calculated 350 MeV/u $^{40-60}$Ca + $^{9}$Be projectile fragmentation reactions using a modified statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model, which treats the reaction as a two-step process of abrasion followed by de-excitation. Fermi-type density distributions were adopted for neutrons and protons in the projectile nuclei. The CIE of the nuclear density distributions (proton, neutron, and their difference) and of the fragment cross-section distributions (isotopic, mass, and charge) were determined by applying fast Fourier transforms to the distributions and computing the Shannon information entropy of the resulting Fourier spectra.

## Key numbers

*   The projectile fragmentation reactions were calculated at 350 MeV/u for $^{40-60}$Ca projectiles on a $^{9}$Be target.
*   The excitation energy of the prefragment after abrasion was calculated as $E^* = 13.3 \langle \Delta A(b) \rangle$ MeV, where $\langle \Delta A(b) \rangle$ is the number of abraded nucleons.
*   The CIE of isotopic distributions ($S_\text{C}^{\sigma_Z}[f]$) for fragments with $Z_\text{fr}$ from 10 to 18 showed a linear decrease with increasing neutron-skin thickness of the projectile nucleus.
*   The correlation between mass-yield CIE ($S_\text{C}^{\sigma_A}[f]$) and neutron-skin thickness was linear for reactions with projectile mass number $A_\text{p} \ge 44$, with a bend point at the $\delta_\text{np}$ of $^{42}$Ca.
*   The CIE of the nuclear density difference $\Delta\rho = \rho_\text{n} - \rho_\text{p}$ ($S_\text{C}^{\Delta\rho}[f]$) decreased linearly with increasing $\delta_\text{np}$ for all calcium isotopes except $^{40}$Ca.

## Context

This work extends the application of configurational information entropy, previously used in studies of nuclear liquid-gas transitions and fragment scaling (Ma 1999, Ma 2015), to the problem of neutron-skin determination. It builds on prior methods for extracting neutron-skin thickness from reaction cross sections, charge-changing cross sections, and isoscaling parameters (Ma 2008, Ma 2015), and contrasts with parity-violating electron scattering (PVS), which the authors note is the only model-independent method. The linear correlations found suggest that measuring fragment distributions in projectile fragmentation reactions at radioactive beam facilities could provide a new experimental probe for the neutron-skin thickness of neutron-rich nuclei, particularly those near the neutron drip line.
