# Elastic and Inelastic Neutrino-Deuteron Scattering in Effective Field Theory

> The differential cross-sections for elastic and inelastic neutrino-deuteron scattering are calculated analytically using nucleon-nucleon effective field theory. For elastic scattering, the deuteron axial form factor and the deuteron strange magnetic moment form factor are computed to next-to-leading order, including two-body currents. For inelastic scattering, two neutral current processes nu+d->nu+n+p, nubar+d->nubar+n+p and one charged current process nubar+d->(e^+)+n+n are computed to next-to-leading order. These depend on an isovector axial two-body matrix element whose value is yet to be fixed by experiment. Potential model calculations by Kubodera et al. and Ying et al. are reproduced for different values of the two-body matrix element. This implies that the differences between the two potential model calculations lie in their treatment of short distance physics. The charged current to neutral current nubar-d cross-section ratio is confirmed to be insensitive to short distance physics, and the same ratio is obtained by potential models and the calculation presented here, within 5%, for incident incident neutrino energies up to 20 MeV. The two-body matrix element could be fixed using the parity violating process epol+d->e+n+p.

## Key claim

The differential cross-sections for elastic and inelastic neutrino-deuteron scattering are calculated analytically to next-to-leading order using nucleon-nucleon effective field theory, but the inelastic results depend on an isovector axial two-body matrix element whose value is not yet fixed by experiment; the charged-current to neutral-current antineutrino-deuteron cross-section ratio is confirmed to be insensitive to short-distance physics and agrees with potential model calculations within 5% for incident neutrino energies up to 20 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the effective field theory power-counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise to compute the hadronic tensors and differential cross-sections for elastic neutrino-deuteron scattering and for three inelastic processes: two neutral-current reactions (nu+d -> nu+n+p and nubar+d -> nubar+n+p) and one charged-current reaction (nubar+d -> e+ + n + n). The calculations were performed to next-to-leading order, including two-body currents parameterized by low-energy constants that are fit to nucleon-nucleon scattering data and, for the axial two-body matrix element, left to be determined by future experiments.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: B = 2.2245 MeV, used as input for the effective field theory calculations
- Nucleon axial coupling: g_A = 1.26, used in the single-nucleon axial current
- Strange axial moment of the nucleon: Delta s = -0.17 +/- 0.17, from analysis of lepton scattering data including SU(3) symmetry breaking effects
- Strange magnetic moment of the proton: mu_s has a large uncertainty, with the SAMPLE experiment measuring G_M^s(-0.1 GeV^2) = 0.23 +/- 0.37 +/- 0.15 +/- 0.19 nuclear magnetons, while theoretical predictions range from -0.8 to 0.18 nuclear magnetons
- The charged-current to neutral-current nubar-d cross-section ratio agrees between potential models and the effective field theory calculation within 5% for incident neutrino energies up to 20 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f = 132 MeV, used in the chiral Lagrangian
- Nucleon magnetic moments: kappa_p = 2.79285 and kappa_n = -1.91304 nuclear magnetons

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to deuteron electromagnetic form factors and moments by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and to the np -> d gamma process by Savage and others. The calculations reproduce the potential model results of Kubodera et al. and Ying et al. for different values of the two-body axial matrix element, showing that the differences between those two potential model calculations arise from their treatment of short-distance physics. The results are directly relevant to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO), which relies on theoretical neutrino-deuteron cross-sections for solar neutrino flux measurements, and the authors suggest that the undetermined two-body axial matrix element could be fixed using the parity-violating process e-polarized + d -> e + n + p.
