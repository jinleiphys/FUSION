# Proposed Search for $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ Mixing in Polarization Phenomena

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the existence of $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing, induced by the $K^+$-$K^0$ mass difference, can be unambiguously established through a strong jump in the azimuthal asymmetry of the $\eta\pi^0$ $S$-wave production cross section in polarized-target $\pi^-p\to\eta\pi^0n$ experiments, with the estimates being largely model independent.

## Method

The authors calculate the interference between the $G$-parity conserving $\rho_2$ Regge pole exchange amplitude and the $G$-parity violating $\pi$ Regge pole exchange amplitude in the reaction $\pi^-p\to(\eta\pi^0)_S n$ on a polarized proton target. The $\pi$ exchange amplitude arises from the process $\pi^-p\to f_0(980)n\to a^0_0(980)n\to(\eta\pi^0)_S n$, which is enabled by the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing. The mixing amplitude $\Pi_{a_0f_0}(m)$ is computed from one-loop diagrams involving $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar K^0$ intermediate states, and its sharply varying phase near the $K\bar K$ thresholds produces a distinctive interference pattern with the $\rho_2$ exchange amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing amplitude between the $K^+K^-$ and $K^0\bar K^0$ thresholds is of order $m_K\sqrt{m^2_{K^0}-m^2_{K^+}}\approx\sqrt\alpha\,m^2_K$, with a numerical factor of approximately $0.1265\,|g_{a_0K^+K^-}g_{f_0K^+K^-}/16\pi|$ in the 8-MeV-wide region between thresholds.
* The $\pi$ exchange contribution to the total $\pi^-p\to a^0_0(980)n\to(\eta\pi^0)_S n$ cross section at $P^{\pi^-}_{lab}=18.3$ GeV is $\sigma^\pi\approx10.9$ nb, which is about 5.5% of the total cross section of approximately 200 nb.
* At its maximum near $t\approx-0.0149$ GeV$^2$, $d\sigma^\pi/dt\approx139$ nb/GeV$^2$, accounting for approximately 14.7% of $(d\sigma/dt)|_{t\approx0}$.
* The smoothed asymmetry $A(0\leq-t\leq0.025\text{ GeV}^2,\,m)$ shows a jump of approximately 0.95 between its maximal and minimal values in the mass region from 0.965 to 1.01 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discovery of $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing by Achasov, Devyanin, and Shestakov (1979) and addresses limitations of a prior proposal by Close and Kirk (2000) to observe mixing in centrally produced $a^0_0(980)$ in $pp$ collisions, which suffered from model uncertainties and secondary Regge trajectory contamination. The proposed polarized-target method enables a clean signature of the mixing effect without requiring high $\eta\pi^0$ mass resolution, and is applicable at any high energy from 8 to 100 GeV at facilities including KEK, BNL, IHEP, CERN (COMPASS), FNAL, ITEP, and Julich.
