# The microcanonical ensemble of the ideal relativistic quantum gas with angular momentum conservation

> We derive the microcanonical partition function of the ideal relativistic quantum gas with fixed intrinsic angular momentum as an expansion over fixed multiplicities. We developed a group theoretical approach by generalizing known projection techniques to the Poincare' group. Our calculation is carried out in a quantum field framework and applies to particles with any spin. It extends known results in literature in that it does not introduce any large volume approximation and it takes particle spin fully into account. We provide expressions of the microcanonical partition function at fixed multiplicities in the limiting classical case of large volumes and large angular momenta and in the grand-canonical ensemble. We also derive the microcanonical partition function of the ideal relativistic quantum gas with fixed parity.

## Key claim

The paper derives the microcanonical partition function of an ideal relativistic quantum gas with fixed intrinsic angular momentum, using a group theoretical projection technique generalized to the Poincaré group, but the derivation is strictly valid only in the system rest frame where the Pauli-Lubanski vector is proportional to the intrinsic angular momentum, and the projector expressions are not proper projectors because the Poincaré group is non-compact.

## Method

The authors generalize known projection operator techniques from internal symmetries to the orthochronous Poincaré group, writing the projector onto irreducible states with fixed four-momentum, spin, and parity as an integral over the Poincaré group. They work in the rest frame of the system where Lorentz transformations reduce to pure rotations, factorizing the projector into energy-momentum, spin-helicity, and parity projections. The microcanonical partition function is then expressed as an expansion over fixed multiplicities (channels), and the single-particle channel weight is calculated using free quantum fields for particles with arbitrary spin, with a conjecture for the matrix element of the localization projector.

## Key numbers

- The projector onto irreducible states is written with a factor (2J+1) where J is the spin quantum number, and the SU(2) rotation measure dR is normalized to 1.
- The invariant measure of the Lorentz group is written as dΛ = sinh²ξ dξ (dΩ_ñ/4π) dR, with ξ ∈ [0,+∞).
- The single-particle state normalization is ⟨[p],σ|[q],τ⟩ = δ³(p−q)δ_{στ}.
- The Fourier integral over the system volume V is F_V(p−p') = (1/(2π)³) ∫_V d³x e^{ix·(p−p')}, which for p=p' and σ=σ' reduces to V/(2π)³ ⟨0|Pro_V|0⟩.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of the microcanonical partition function for spinless bosons (Becattini and Ferroni, earlier work) to particles with arbitrary spin, overcoming limitations of prior approaches that either used non-relativistic quantum mechanics (Cerang), treated only spinless particles (Satz), or omitted momentum conservation (Koba). The classical limit had been worked out by Ericson. The result enables consistent treatment of angular momentum conservation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and other systems where spin degrees of freedom are important, without introducing large-volume approximations.
