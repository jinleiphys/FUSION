# Pionic susceptibility for charged pions in asymmetric nuclei

> At low energies the particle-hole (ph) part of the pionic susceptibility in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is known to behave very differently from the susceptibility in finite nuclei due to the presence of an energy gap in the ph excitation spectrum. In this note we show that for charged pions in N > Z nuclei the changes due to the gap are very similar to those in the symmetric case, except at very low momenta, where a qualitatively different behavior is found.

# Pionic susceptibility for charged pions in asymmetric nuclei

## Key claim

For charged pions in neutron-rich (N > Z) nuclei, the particle-hole part of the pionic susceptibility at low energies is modified by the energy gap in the excitation spectrum in a manner very similar to the symmetric case, except at very low momenta (below approximately the difference between neutron and proton Fermi momenta), where the susceptibility remains nearly constant rather than going to zero, because Pauli blocking can never be complete in asymmetric matter.

## Method

The authors compute the non-interacting (Fermi-gas) pionic susceptibility for charged pions by splitting the Lindhard function into direct and crossed contributions, distinguishing neutron and proton Fermi momenta. They incorporate two modifications relevant to finite nuclei: an energy gap Δ added to the particle-hole excitation energy to account for the discrete excitation spectrum, and a Coulomb potential shift VC applied to proton energies to equalize neutron and proton Fermi energies as occurs in β-stable nuclei. The resulting susceptibility is evaluated at nuclear matter density (0.17 fm⁻³) for fixed ω = 0 as a function of momentum transfer k, comparing symmetric matter (equal neutron and proton Fermi momenta) with asymmetric matter (neutron density 1.5 times proton density).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter density: ρn + ρp = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Symmetric matter Fermi momenta: pF(n) = pF(p) = 1.36 fm⁻¹
- Asymmetric matter (ρn = 1.5 ρp): pF(n) = 1.45 fm⁻¹, pF(p) = 1.26 fm⁻¹
- For symmetric matter at ω = 0 and k → 0, the Lindhard function gives φ(ω = 0, k → 0) = −m pF/(2π²)
- The qualitative difference in asymmetric matter occurs at momenta below approximately pF(n) − pF(p) = 0.19 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Oset (1981) on the energy-gap modification of the pionic susceptibility from isospin-symmetric to isospin-asymmetric nuclei. The standard Lindhard function (Lindhard, 1954; Fetter and Walecka, 1971) is inadequate for finite nuclei because it allows vanishing denominator at ω = 0, whereas the discrete particle-hole excitation spectrum in finite nuclei introduces a gap Δ. The results are relevant for calculating polarization functions at very low energy transfer (ω ≈ 0) in applications such as those discussed by Oset (1981) and Kohl (1999), and for understanding pion properties in neutron-rich environments including finite nuclei and neutron star interiors.
