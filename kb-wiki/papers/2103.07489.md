# Novel Relaxation Time Approximation to the Relativistic Boltzmann Equation

> We show that the widely used relaxation time approximation to the relativistic Boltzmann equation contains basic flaws, being incompatible with microscopic and macroscopic conservation laws. We propose a new approximation that fixes such fundamental issues and maintains the basic properties of the linearized Boltzmann collision operator. We show how this correction affects transport coefficients, such as the bulk viscosity and particle diffusion.

## Key claim
The widely used Anderson-Witting (AW) relaxation time approximation (RTA) to the relativistic Boltzmann equation is fundamentally flawed because it is incompatible with microscopic and macroscopic conservation laws when energy-dependent relaxation times or general matching conditions are used. The authors propose a new RTA that fixes these issues by adding counterterms that project onto the subspace orthogonal to the five zero-eigenvalue eigenvectors of the linearized collision operator, thereby preserving the conservation laws regardless of the relaxation time's energy dependence.

## Method
The authors first demonstrate that the standard AW approximation violates particle number and energy-momentum conservation unless the relaxation time is momentum-independent and Landau matching conditions are imposed. They then construct a new linearized collision operator in Hilbert space by subtracting projections onto the five conserved quantities (1 and \(k^\mu\)), ensuring the operator annihilates these eigenfunctions. Using this new operator, they perform a first-order Chapman-Enskog expansion to derive expressions for the bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) and particle diffusion coefficient \(\kappa_n\), expanding the nonequilibrium correction \(\phi_\mathbf{k}\) in orthogonal polynomials and solving for the expansion coefficients. They compute these transport coefficients numerically for a classical gas with a power-law relaxation time \(\tau_R = (\beta E_\mathbf{k})^\gamma t_R\) and Landau matching conditions, comparing results for \(\gamma = 0, 0.5, 1\).

## Key numbers
- For \(\gamma = 0.5\) (a value argued to approximate effective kinetic descriptions of QCD), the traditional RTA yields a negative bulk viscosity coefficient, violating the second law of thermodynamics.
- The dimensionless bulk viscosity \(\zeta / [t_R (\varepsilon + P)]\) is plotted as a function of \(m\beta\) for \(\gamma = 0\) (solid curve), \(\gamma = 0.5\) (dashed curve), and \(\gamma = 1\) (dashed-dotted curve) under Landau matching conditions.
- The dimensionless particle diffusion coefficient \(\kappa_n / (t_R n)\) is plotted as a function of \(m\beta\) for the same three \(\gamma\) values under Landau matching conditions.
- The shear viscosity \(\eta\) is unaffected by the new RTA because it depends only on the tensor coefficient \(\mathcal{S}_\mathbf{k}^{(2)}\), which is unchanged.

## Context
This work extends the non-relativistic projector method for the RTA (known from textbooks such as Reichl (1999) and Cercignani (1990)) to the relativistic regime, correcting the Anderson-Witting approximation (Anderson and Witting, 1974) that has been widely used in studies of hydrodynamization in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Denicol (2014), Heller (2016), Strickland (2017)). The new RTA enables consistent investigation of different hydrodynamic frames (matching conditions) and their effects on causality and stability, building on recent work by Bemfica (2017), Kovtun (2019), and Bemfica (2020). It also provides a framework to revisit analytic solutions for rapidly expanding gases derived by Denicol (2014), Noronha (2015), and Florkowski (2013) under arbitrary matching conditions.
