# Effective radii of deuteron induced reactions

> The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for exclusive reactions and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) as an extension of CDCC to inclusive reactions are applied to deuteron induced reactions. The CDCC result reproduces experimental data on the reaction cross section for $d+^{58}$Ni scattering at 200 MeV/nucleon and ERT does data on the neutron-stripping cross section for inclusive $^7$Li$(d,n)$ reaction at 40 MeV. For deuteron induced reactions at 200 MeV/nucleon, target-dependence of the reaction, elastic-breakup, nucleon-stripping, nucleon-removal, complete- and incomplete-fusion cross sections is clearly explained by simple formulae. Accuracy of the Glauber model is also investigated.

## Key claim

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) reproduces experimental data on the reaction cross section for deuteron-induced reactions, and the eikonal reaction theory (ERT) reproduces neutron-stripping cross section data, but the Glauber model fails for elastic scattering at angles larger than about 10 degrees due to its eikonal and adiabatic approximations, particularly for heavy targets where the Coulomb interaction is significant.

## Method

The authors applied CDCC to calculate exclusive reaction cross sections and ERT (an extension of CDCC to inclusive reactions) to calculate inclusive reaction cross sections for deuteron-induced reactions. They used the Koning-Delaroche global optical potential for the proton and neutron optical potentials and the Ohmura potential for the deuteron intrinsic interaction, with s-, p-, and d-wave breakup states up to k = 1.0 fm⁻¹ discretized into bins of width Δk = 0.1 fm⁻¹. The S-matrix was separated into neutron and proton parts, allowing calculation of individual stripping, removal, and fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross section for d+⁵⁸Ni at 200 MeV/nucleon: CDCC gives 1056 mb, experimental value is 1083 mb
- Elastic-breakup cross section for d+⁵⁸Ni at 200 MeV/nucleon: 59 mb (with spin-orbit and Coulomb breakup), 69 mb (without spin-orbit), 31 mb (without Coulomb breakup), 25 mb (without both)
- Total-fusion cross section for d+⁵⁸Ni at 200 MeV/nucleon: 997 mb (all cases)
- Proton-stripping cross section for inclusive ⁷Li(d,n) at 40 MeV: ERT gives 253 mb, Glauber model gives 214 mb, experimental extraction gives 244 ± 34(theor.) ± 37(exp.) mb
- Non-eikonal correction for d+⁹Be at 200 MeV/nucleon: less than 1% for σ_R, σ_EB, σ_n:STR, and σ_p:STR
- Non-eikonal correction for d+²⁰⁸Pb at 200 MeV/nucleon: 1.5% for σ_R, σ_EB, and σ_n:STR; 16% for σ_p:STR
- Adiabatic approximation error for d+⁹Be at 200 MeV/nucleon: 0.3% for σ_R, 2% for σ_EB
- Adiabatic approximation error for d+²⁰⁸Pb at 200 MeV/nucleon: 0.4% for σ_R, 6% for σ_EB
- Effective radius formula for elastic-breakup: R_EB = 0.33 + 1.46 A^(1/3) (fm)
- Effective radius formula for total-fusion: R_TF = 0.18 + 1.41 A^(1/3) (fm)

## Context

This work extends the CDCC framework, previously successful for exclusive reactions of stable and unstable projectiles (Rusek, 2005; Tostevin, 2001; Davids, 2001), to inclusive reactions via ERT. It addresses limitations of the Glauber model (Glauber, 1959), which diverges for Coulomb-induced breakup (Capel, 2008) and fails for heavy targets. The results enable accurate evaluation of nucleon-stripping cross sections needed for the international fusion materials irradiation facility (IFMIF) (Matsui, 2007) and provide simple formulae for target-mass-number dependence of various reaction cross sections at intermediate energies (100-300 MeV/nucleon) relevant to experiments at MSU, RIKEN, and GSI.
