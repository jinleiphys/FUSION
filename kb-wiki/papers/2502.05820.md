# Robust linear correlations related to neutron skin thickness

> We observe various robust linear correlations related to neutron skin thickness ($ΔR_{\rm np}$) within different interaction ensembles, including newly proposed random Skyrme ensemble. The robust linear correlation between $ΔR_{\rm np}$, or charge radius difference of mirror nuclei ($ΔR_{\rm mirr}$), and the isospin asymmetry ($I=\frac{N-Z}{A}$) becomes apparent as the model space is enlarged. Shape coexistence, or shape effect on charge radius, is considered to explain the experimental deviation of ${}^{18}$O/Ne and some odd-$A$ $ΔR_{\rm mirr}$s from the $ΔR_{\rm mirr}-I$ linearity. The slopes of the linear $ΔR_{\rm mirr}-I$ and $ΔR_{\rm np}-I$ correlations ($C_{\rm np}$ and $C_{\rm mirr}$, respectively) are also robustly and linearly correlated to the slope of the symmetry energy ($L$). These linear correlations are further understood with the similar formulation between between $L$ and the symmetry energy coefficient ($J$). The linear correlations between $C_{\rm np}-L$ and $C_{\rm mirr}-L$ are also adopted to constrain $L$ to $20\sim36$ MeV with 1$σ$ confidence. Considering the deviation of ${}^{18}$O/Ne $ΔR_{mirr}$ due to shape coexistence, the 1$σ$ range for $L$ is further narrowed to $28\sim36$ MeV, suggesting a relatively soft equation of state for nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the slopes of the linear correlations between neutron skin thickness (or charge radius difference of mirror nuclei) and isospin asymmetry are themselves robustly and linearly correlated to the slope of the symmetry energy (L), and that this chain of correlations can constrain L to 20-36 MeV at 1 sigma, or 28-36 MeV when accounting for shape coexistence in the 18O/Ne mirror pair.

## Method

The authors performed random quasi-particle ensemble (RQE) shell-model calculations in two-major-shell model spaces and random Skyrme ensemble (RSE) Hartree-Fock calculations with 160 existing Skyrme parametrizations plus approximately 4000 randomly generated parametrizations per 5 MeV interval of L from 0 to 200 MeV. For each interaction, they computed neutron skin thickness and charge radius differences of mirror nuclei for a set of nuclei, then extracted Pearson correlation coefficients and linear fit slopes (C_np and C_mirr) against isospin asymmetry I = (N-Z)/A, and examined the correlations of these slopes with L.

## Key numbers

- Experimental linear fit for recent Delta R_np data: Delta R_np = 0.9(1)*I - 0.04(2) fm, with Pearson's r = 0.85
- Experimental proportional fit for Delta R_mirr (excluding 18O/Ne): Delta R_mirr = 1.31(4)*I fm, with Pearson's r = 0.99
- In the RSE, 50.6(9)% of parametrizations produced strong linearity (r > 0.95) for Delta R_np-I correlation, and 89(1)% for Delta R_mirr-I correlation
- From the RSE, L is constrained to 28 +8/-8 MeV at 1 sigma using experimental C_np and C_mirr values
- When additionally considering the 18O/Ne mirror pair deviation, the 1 sigma range for L is narrowed to 28-36 MeV (asymmetric Gaussian fit gives L = 35 +7/-4 MeV without 18O/Ne, and L = 26 +9/-7 MeV with 18O/Ne)

## Context

This work extends the known linear correlation between neutron skin thickness and isospin asymmetry (previously noted in mean-field and ab initio calculations by Myers (1969, 1974, 1980), Pethick (1996), and others) by demonstrating its robustness in random interaction ensembles. It also builds on the proposal by Wang, Li, and Brown that charge radius differences of mirror nuclei correlate with L, and on prior L constraints from neutron skin measurements (e.g., 106 +/- 37 MeV from 208Pb elastic scattering) and mirror nuclei charge radii (e.g., 5-70 MeV from 36Ca/S, 21-88 MeV from 54Ni/Fe). The new approach uses the slopes of the global linear correlations rather than individual nuclei, yielding a tighter constraint that favors a relatively soft equation of state for nuclear matter.
