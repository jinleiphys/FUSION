# Nuclear Chiral EFT in the Precision Era

> Chiral effective field theory has established itself as the method of choice to study nuclear forces and low-energy nuclear dynamics. I review the status and prospects of this approach and discuss ongoing efforts to advance the precision frontier for ab initio description of few-nucleon systems. Special emphasis is put on the precise determination of the two-nucleon force at fifth order in the chiral expansion, role of the chiral symmetry, the convergence pattern of the chiral expansion and the quantification of the theoretical uncertainties. The discussed topics are essential for ongoing studies towards elucidating the structure of the three-nucleon force which will be briefly addressed as well.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory is entering a precision era where the two-nucleon force at fifth order (N⁴LO) in the chiral expansion can be determined with quantified theoretical uncertainties, but the approach still requires a finite ultraviolet regulator because the truncated chiral potential cannot be fully renormalized without introducing infinitely many counterterms.

## Method

The authors derived nuclear forces using the method of unitary transformation applied to the heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian, then solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a finite ultraviolet cutoff. The long-range pion-exchange potentials were regularized in coordinate space with a cutoff function f(r/R) = (1 - exp(-r²/R²))⁶ for R values between 0.8 fm and 1.2 fm, while short-range contact interactions used a non-local Gaussian regulator in momentum space with cutoff Λ = 2/R. Low-energy constants for the long-range part were taken from fits to πN scattering data, and the 24 unknown contact-term LECs were fitted to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis of np and pp S-, P- and D-waves and mixing angles ε₁ and ε₂.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff distance R chosen in the range R = 0.8…1.2 fm, consistent with the expected breakdown distance of ~0.8 fm for the chiral expansion of the pion-exchange potential
- At N²LO and N³LO, πN LEC values used: c₁ = -0.81, c₂ = 3.28, c₃ = -4.69, c₄ = 3.40 (in units of GeV⁻ⁿ)
- At N⁴LO, πN LEC values used: c₁ = -0.75, c₂ = 3.49, c₃ = -4.77, c₄ = 3.34 (in units of GeV⁻ⁿ)
- χ̃² per datum for np phase shifts (0-200 MeV lab energy, R=0.9 fm): LO = 480, NLO = 63, N²LO = 21, N³LO = 0.7, N⁴LO = 0.3
- χ̃² per datum for pp phase shifts (0-200 MeV lab energy, R=0.9 fm): LO = 9150, NLO = 560, N²LO = 130, N³LO = 0.7, N⁴LO = 0.6
- 2 order-Q⁰ contact terms at LO, 7 additional order-Q² contact interactions at NLO and N²LO, and 15 additional order-Q⁴ contact terms at N³LO and N⁴LO, totaling 24 unknown LECs

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT program initiated by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and builds on the first-generation N³LO potentials of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2004). The new coordinate-space regulator avoids distorting the long-range part of the interaction, unlike the non-local momentum-space regulator used in those earlier potentials, and eliminates the need for spectral-function regularization employed by Kaiser (1997) and Epelbaum et al. (2003). The results enable precision ab initio calculations of few-nucleon systems and provide the foundation for ongoing studies of the three-nucleon force, which requires the 10 unknown LECs at N⁴LO identified by Girlanda et al. (2011).
