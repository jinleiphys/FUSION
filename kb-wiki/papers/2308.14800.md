# Basic characteristics of neutrino flavor conversions in the post-shock regions of core-collapse supernova

> One of the active debates in core-collapse supernova (CCSN) theory is how significantly neutrino flavor conversions induced by neutrino-neutrino self-interactions change the conventional picture of CCSN dynamics. Recent studies have indicated that strong flavor conversions can occur inside neutrino spheres where neutrinos are tightly coupled to matter. These flavor conversions are associated with either collisional instability or fast neutrino-flavor conversion (FFC) or both. The impact of these flavor conversions on CCSN dynamics is, however, still highly uncertain due to the lack of global simulations of quantum kinetic neutrino transport with appropriate microphysical inputs. Given fluid profiles from a recent CCSN model at three different time snapshots in the early post-bounce phase, we perform global quantum kinetic simulations in spherical symmetry with an essential set of microphysics. We find that strong flavor conversions occur in optically thick regions, resulting in a substantial change of neutrino radiation field. The neutrino heating in the gain region is smaller than the case with no flavor conversions, whereas the neutrino cooling in the optically thick region is commonly enhanced. Based on the neutrino data obtained from our multi-angle neutrino transport simulations, we also assess some representative classical closure relations by applying them to diagonal components of density matrix of neutrinos. We find that Eddington tensors can be well approximated by these closure relations except for the region where flavor conversions occur vividly. We also analyze the neutrino signal by carrying out detector simulations for Super-Kamiokande, DUNE, and JUNO. We propose a useful strategy to identify the sign of flavor conversions in neutrino signal, that can be easily implemented in real data analyses of CCSN neutrinos.

## Key claim
Strong neutrino flavor conversions driven by neutrino-neutrino self-interactions occur in optically thick regions of core-collapse supernovae, but the impact on dynamics remains uncertain because the simulations use an artificially reduced oscillation Hamiltonian (attenuation parameter xi = 10^-4) and simplified microphysics, and the results are limited to three early post-bounce time snapshots (100, 200, and 300 ms) from a single spherically symmetric model.

## Method
The authors performed global quantum kinetic neutrino transport simulations in spherical symmetry using fluid profiles from a 15 solar mass progenitor CCSN model at three time snapshots (T_b = 100, 200, and 300 ms). They solved the mean-field quantum kinetic equation with neutrino emission, absorption, and scattering reactions, but with the oscillation Hamiltonian attenuated by a factor of xi = 10^-4 to make the simulations tractable. They also conducted linear stability analysis of the initial classical neutrino radiation fields and performed detector simulations for Super-Kamiokande, DUNE, and JUNO.

## Key numbers
- The attenuation parameter used in the quantum kinetic simulations is xi = 10^-4, with a convergence check using xi = 2 x 10^-4 showing qualitatively the same result.
- The simulations used 12,288 radial grid points with innermost resolution of 30 cm, 12 neutrino energy grid points from 0 to 80 MeV, and 96 angular grid points covering cos(theta_nu) from -1 to 1.
- The computational domains were 20-180 km (T_b = 100 ms), 25-150 km (T_b = 200 ms), and 20-100 km (T_b = 300 ms), with inner boundaries at matter density rho ~ 10^13 g/cm^3.
- The simulations were run for 1 ms total, with time-averaged quantities computed over the interval 1 ms <= t <= 1.05 ms.
- The vacuum oscillation parameters used were squared mass differences Delta m^2_21 = 7.42 x 10^-5 eV^2 and Delta m^2_31 = 2.51 x 10^-3 eV^2, with all mixing angles set to 10^-6.
- The electron fraction Y_e was reduced by 10% from the original CCSN model, with a check at 7% reduction for the T_b = 300 ms snapshot showing no qualitative change.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Nagakura and Zaizen, 2023) which was limited to a single time snapshot at 300 ms, by adding two earlier post-bounce snapshots (100 and 200 ms) to investigate time-dependent effects of flavor conversions. It also provides the first global assessment of classical closure relations for Eddington tensors in the presence of flavor conversions, and proposes a strategy for identifying flavor conversion signatures in neutrino detectors. The study is motivated by earlier local-box simulations of fast neutrino-flavor conversion (FFC) and collisional instability (e.g., Johns et al., 2020; Bhatt et al., 2021; Shalgar and Tamborra, 2021; Martin et al., 2021) and by parametric studies (Ehring et al., 2023) that showed strong sensitivity to model parameters.
