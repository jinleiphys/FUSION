# Lattice QCD Study of Pion Electroproduction and Weak Production from a Nucleon

> Quantum fluctuations in QCD influence nucleon structure and interactions, with pion production serving as a key probe of chiral dynamics. In this study, we present a lattice QCD calculation of multipole amplitudes at threshold, related to both pion electroproduction and weak production from a nucleon, using two gauge ensembles near the physical pion mass. We develop a technique for spin projection and construct multiple operators for analyzing the generalized eigenvalue problem in both the nucleon-pion system in the center-of-mass frame and the nucleon system with nonzero momentum. The numerical lattice results are then compared with those extracted from experimental data and predicted by low-energy theorems incorporating one-loop corrections.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of multipole amplitudes for pion electroproduction and weak production from a nucleon at threshold, using two gauge ensembles near the physical pion mass. The lattice results align more closely with experimental extractions than with low-energy theorem predictions, but the authors note that deviations between the two lattice ensembles could arise from either lattice artifacts or statistical fluctuations, requiring further investigation with larger statistics and additional lattice spacings.

## Method

The authors computed four-point correlation functions for the processes gamma* N -> N pi, W* N -> N pi, and Z* N -> N pi on two RBC-UKQCD domain wall fermion ensembles (24D and 32Df) with similar pion masses (142.6 and 142.9 MeV) and spatial volumes (4.6 fm) but different lattice spacings. They developed spin projection operators to extract the S-wave multipole amplitudes E0+, L0+, L0+^(W), M0+, and H0+ from the correlation functions, and constructed optimized nucleon and nucleon-pion operators by solving generalized eigenvalue problems (GEVP) using the four lowest momentum modes to reduce excited-state contamination.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses: 142.6(3) MeV (24D ensemble) and 142.9(7) MeV (32Df ensemble)
* Lattice spacings: a^{-1} = 1.023(2) GeV (24D) and 1.378(5) GeV (32Df)
* Spatial volume: L = 4.6 fm for both ensembles
* The factor k0/|k| takes small values of 0.376, 0.170, and 0.063 for the momentum modes used
* The GEVP correction on the N side for L0+ can reach 6 sigma in the more precise 24D data
* The overlap with excited states for the unoptimized operators is about 5% for O_{Npi}^{(1)} and about 10% for O_N^{(1)}

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon electric polarizabilities (Wang 2023) and subtraction functions in forward Compton scattering (Fu 2024) to the more challenging case of pion production, which involves baryonic multi-hadron states. The results are compared with experimental extractions from coupled-channel partial-wave analyses (Mai 2023) and with low-energy theorem predictions incorporating one-loop chiral perturbation theory corrections (Bernard 1993). The techniques developed for spin projection and multiple operator construction hold promise for future calculations of shallow inelastic neutrino-nucleon scattering, which is crucial for reducing systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, and JUNO.
