# Coulomb suppression of the stellar enhancement factor

> It is commonly assumed that reaction measurements for astrophysics should be preferably performed in the direction of positive Q value to minimize the impact of the stellar enhancement factor, i.e. the difference between the laboratory rate and the actual stellar rate. We show that the stellar effects can be minimized in the charged particle channel, even when the reaction Q value is negative. As a demonstration, the cross section of the astrophysically relevant 85Rb(p,n)85Sr reaction has been measured by activation between 2.16 < Ec.m. < 3.96 MeV and the astrophysical reaction rate for (p,n) as well as (n,p) is directly inferred from the data. The presented arguments are also relevant for other alpha and proton-induced reactions in the p and rp processes. Additionally, our results confirm a previously derived modification of a global optical proton potential.

## Key claim

The authors show that the stellar enhancement factor can be minimized in the charged-particle channel even when the reaction Q value is negative, contradicting the common assumption that measurements should be performed in the direction of positive Q value. This Coulomb suppression effect occurs when the absolute Q value is low compared to the Coulomb barrier, and it allows direct determination of astrophysically relevant rates from measurements in the direction with suppressed enhancement.

## Method

The cross section of the 85Rb(p,n)85Sr reaction (Q = -1.847 MeV) was measured using the activation method with proton beams from Van de Graaff and cyclotron accelerators at ATOMKI, covering center-of-mass energies from 2.16 to 3.96 MeV in 200 keV steps. Rubidium targets were produced by evaporating natural RbCl onto thin Al foil, with target thickness determined by Rutherford Backscattering Spectrometry using a nuclear microbeam facility. The induced gamma activity from the ground state and isomeric state of 85Sr was measured with a calibrated HPGe detector, and the astrophysical S factor and reaction rates for both (p,n) and (n,p) directions were inferred from the experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Reaction Q value for 85Rb(p,n)85Sr: -1.847 MeV
- Measured total cross sections range from 0.058 ± 0.006 mb at Ec.m. = 2158 ± 8 keV to 19.65 ± 1.82 mb at Ec.m. = 3952 ± 40 keV
- S factor values range from (7.13 ± 0.67) × 10^6 MeV barn at Ec.m. = 2158 keV to (6.93 ± 0.64) × 10^6 MeV barn at Ec.m. = 3952 keV
- Stellar enhancement factor for (p,n) direction: 1.03 ≤ f_pn ≤ 1.08 in the plasma temperature range 2 ≤ T ≤ 4 GK
- Stellar enhancement factor for (n,p) direction: 2.6 ≤ f_np ≤ 3.9 in the same temperature range
- Astrophysical reaction rate for 85Rb(p,n)85Sr at 2.00 GK: (8.30 ± 0.83) cm^3 s^-1 mole^-1
- Astrophysical reaction rate for 85Rb(p,n)85Sr at 4.00 GK: (1.15 ± 0.12) × 10^4 cm^3 s^-1 mole^-1
- Astrophysical reaction rate for 85Sr(n,p)85Rb at 2.00 GK: (2.56 ± 0.26) × 10^5 cm^3 s^-1 mole^-1
- Astrophysical reaction rate for 85Sr(n,p)85Rb at 4.00 GK: (2.04 ± 0.20) × 10^6 cm^3 s^-1 mole^-1

## Context

This work extends the understanding of stellar enhancement factors beyond the conventional assumption that measurements should always be performed in the direction of positive Q value, as previously assumed in astrophysical reaction rate determinations. The experimental results confirm a modification of the global optical proton potential previously derived by Kiss (2007), where the strength of the imaginary part of the microscopic potential should be increased by 70%. The Coulomb suppression effect identified here applies to more than 1200 reactions involving nucleons, alpha particles, and gamma rays, including alpha captures relevant in the p process and proton captures near the proton dripline in the rp process and nu-p process, enabling direct experimental determination of astrophysical rates for reactions with negative Q values that were previously considered problematic.
