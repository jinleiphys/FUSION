# Mixed Kaon Condensation in CFL Matter

> Previous work on high-density QCD suggests that the ground state configuration in the presence of a non-zero neutrino chemical potential consists of CFL matter plus a homogenous condensate of neutral and positive kaons. We consider here the stability of this homogenous configuration toward the production of a charge-separated mixed phase, and find that the system is indeed stable against the production of such a heterogenous configuration. We identify the critical value of the fine structure constant which would allow for phase separation. We also derive dispersion relations for the low-lying excitations above the kaon-condensed ground state and discuss their possible phenomenological implications.

## Key claim

The homogeneous mixed phase of neutral and positive kaon condensates in Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) matter is stable against the formation of a charge-separated heterogeneous mixed phase for the physical value of the fine structure constant (alpha ~ 1/137), but would become unstable if alpha were reduced by roughly six orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors use the chiral Lagrangian formalism to describe mesonic excitations above the symmetric CFL ground state, parametrizing the condensate by an SU(3) matrix. They perform a small-fluctuation stability analysis of the homogeneous K0/K+ mixed phase by expanding the Lagrangian to quadratic order in fields, coupling the meson fields to the gauge field of the unbroken U(1)tilde{Q} symmetry, and including the electron Debye screening contribution. The dispersion relations for the low-lying excitations are derived by solving the coupled linearized equations of motion for plane-wave ansatze.

## Key numbers

* Surface tension for the boundary between pure K0 and pure K+ regions at quark chemical potential mu = 400 MeV: sigma = 0.082 MeV/fm^2
* Height of the free energy barrier between the two minima: of order 10^3 MeV^4
* Critical value of the fine structure constant for phase separation: alpha less than or approximately 10^(-8) (compared to physical value alpha_0 = 1/137)
* For alpha less than or approximately 10^(-6) * alpha_0, the longitudinal photon mode becomes unstable (pure imaginary energy for a range of wavelengths)
* Most unstable wave vectors for the static plasma wave: |k| ~ 1 MeV, corresponding to charged structures of size D ~ 200 fm
* In-medium masses of charged pions: approximately 15 MeV (pi-minus-like state) and 40 MeV (pi-plus-like state) at mu = 400 MeV and mu_nu = 100 MeV
* In-medium masses of kaonic modes: two massive kaons at about 32 MeV and 48 MeV, and a longitudinal photon mode with mass about 8 MeV at mu = 400 MeV and mu_nu = 100 MeV
* Electron Debye screening length squared: k_D^2 = q^2 * mu_e^2 / (2 * pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kaplan and Reddy (2002) who mapped the phase diagram for CFL matter with meson condensates and identified the homogeneous K0/K+ mixed phase as the ground state at intermediate neutrino chemical potential. The authors rule out the possibility of a heterogeneous mixed phase of the type considered by Glendenning (1992) for neutron star matter, and show that the homogeneous phase is stable against charge separation. The derived excitation spectra, particularly the relatively light photon mode (mass less than or approximately 10 MeV), may be relevant for calculating neutrino scattering rates in cooling neutron stars, similar to the coherent neutrino scattering from kaonic droplets discussed in prior work. The results also inform the properties of kaonic vortices and vortons that may arise from the breaking of U(1) symmetries by the mixed kaon condensate.
