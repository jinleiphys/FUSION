# Signatures of Jet Drift in QGP Hard Probe Observables

> Hard probe tomography of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy ion collisions has long been a preeminent goal of the high-energy nuclear physics program. In service of this goal, the isotropic modification of jets and high-energy hadrons has been studied in great detail at the leading-power (eikonal) level, with effects originating from sub-eikonal $\mathcal{O}(μ/E)$ anisotropic interactions presumed to be small. We present the first investigation of sub-eikonal, collective-flow-induced asymmetric jet broadening (jet drift) in event-by-event $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV PbPb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider using the new Anisotropic Parton Evolution (APE) computational framework. We show that jet drift imparts a sizeable enhancement of elliptic flow ($v_2$) and increases the mean acoplanarity for low and intermediate energy particles ($p_T < 10$ GeV). Importantly, these novel modifications to hard probe observables are shown to survive averaging over events and collision geometry. They couple to the collective flow of the medium seen by the jet and encode information about the QGP dynamics inaccessible to studies considering only isotropic, eikonal level effects.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that sub-eikonal, collective-flow-induced asymmetric jet broadening (jet drift) produces sizeable enhancements of elliptic flow and acoplanarity for low and intermediate energy particles (p_T < 10 GeV) that survive averaging over fluctuating events and collision geometry, contrary to the presumption that such O(mu/E) anisotropic effects are small.

## Method
The authors developed the Anisotropic Parton Evolution (APE) Monte Carlo framework, which tracks step-by-step average deflections of hard partons as they propagate through a realistic event-by-event QGP medium generated from T_RENTo initial conditions, 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner. They computed the first odd moment of flow-mediated drift from Sadofyev et al. (2021) and implemented radiative energy loss via the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism at first order in opacity, collisional energy loss, and fragmentation functions from JAM20-SIDIS fits, using separate simulations with and without drift effects to extract the enhancement.

## Key numbers
- Collision energy: sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV PbPb collisions at the LHC
- Coupling constant: g = sqrt(4 pi alpha_s) ≈ 2.1 with radiative energy loss alone; g ≈ 1.8 with radiative and collisional energy loss, tuned via reduced chi-squared fit to CMS R_AA data at 30-50% centrality for p_T in [8 GeV, 35 GeV]
- Debye screening mass: mu ≈ gT sqrt(1 + N_f/6) with N_f = 2
- Hadronization temperature: T_had = 155 MeV
- Thermalization time: tau_0 = 0.37 fm
- Timestep: Delta t = 0.1 fm in the plasma center of mass rest frame
- Elliptic flow enhancement: several times the experimental uncertainty (e.g., < ±0.005 for charged pion v_2 at 3.00-3.25 GeV in 30-40% centrality from ALICE (2018))
- Acoplanarity enhancement: greater than 5 degrees below 6 GeV hadronic p_T
- Coalescence momentum width: Delta_p = 0.24 GeV

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of sub-eikonal QCD dynamics in matter developed by Sadofyev et al. (2021) and Antiporda et al. (2021), who showed that momentum broadening has an O(mu/E) anisotropic component coupling to collective flow. It addresses the long-standing R_AA ⊗ v_2 puzzle where perturbative calculations underpredict elliptic flow below 10 GeV, as noted by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2016), Molnar et al. (2013), and others. The APE framework differs substantially from existing simulators like HIJING, HYDJET++, JEWEL, JETSCAPE, DREENA-A, and HYBRID by tracking sub-eikonal deflections rather than simply boosting eikonal scattering amplitudes to a moving frame, enabling future studies of jet substructure sensitivity to flow.
