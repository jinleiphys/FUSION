# The role of surface energy coefficients and nuclear surface diffuseness in the fusion of heavy-ions

> We discuss the effect of surface energy coefficients as well as nuclear surface diffuseness in the proximity potential and ultimately in the fusion of heavy-ions. Here we employ different versions of surface energy coefficients. Our analysis reveals that these technical parameters can influence the fusion barriers by a significant amount. A best set of these parameters is also given that explains the experimental data nicely.

## Key claim

The choice of surface energy coefficient and nuclear surface diffuseness in the proximity potential can alter fusion barrier heights by 10-15%, and using the Möller-Nix 1976 or 1995 surface energy coefficients (γ-MN1976 or γ-MN1995) instead of the original Myers-Świątecki coefficient yields a modified proximity potential ("Proximity 2010") that agrees much better with experimental fusion data.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear potentials and fusion barriers for 390 reactions (both symmetric N=Z and asymmetric N≠Z) using the original proximity potential (Prox 77) but substituted four different versions of the surface energy coefficient γ: γ-MS (Myers-Świątecki), γ-MN1976 (Möller-Nix 1976), γ-MN1995 (Möller-Nix 1995), and γ-MSNew (Myers-Świątecki droplet model). They also tested different radius formulas for nuclear surface diffuseness, including the Prox 77 radius, the Aage Winther radius (AW95), and the newer Myers-Świątecki radius (C-00 and C-00N). Fusion cross sections were calculated using the Wong formula for selected reactions and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Average deviation in fusion barrier heights over 390 reactions: 3.99% for γ-MS, 0.77% for γ-MN1976, 1.77% for γ-MN1995, and 2.37% for γ-MSNew
- Average deviation in fusion barrier positions over 272 reactions: -1.74% for γ-MS, 1.95% for γ-MN1976, 0.73% for γ-MN1995, and 0.0% for γ-MSNew
- Surface energy constant γ0 = 1.460734 MeV/fm² and surface-asymmetry constant ks = 4.0 for γ-MN1976
- Surface energy constant γ0 = 1.25284 MeV/fm² and ks = 2.345 for γ-MN1995 (fitted to ground-state masses of 1654 nuclei from ¹⁶O to ²⁶³106 and fission-barrier heights)
- Original γ-MS parameters: γ0 = 1.01734 MeV/fm² and ks = 1.79 (later revised to γ0 = 0.9517 MeV/fm² and ks = 1.7826)
- The effect of surface energy coefficient and surface diffuseness on fusion barriers is of the order of 10%-15%

## Context

This work extends the long line of proximity potential studies initiated by Blocki et al. (1977) and later refined by Myers and Świątecki (2000) and Winther (1995). The authors show that the choice of surface energy coefficient, often treated casually in the literature, can affect fusion barriers as much as the choice of nuclear potential itself. The paper enables future fusion studies to use the "Proximity 2010" potential with γ-MN1976 or γ-MN1995 coefficients, which reduces average barrier height deviations from ±10% (with the original Prox 77) to below 2%.
