# Recent progress in pion photo- and electroproduction analysis

> Pion photo- and electroproduction has been studied at threshold and in the resonance region below W=2GeV. At threshold pi^0 production can be very well explained within a dynamical model derived from an effective chiral Lagrangian. The final state interaction is nearly saturated by single charge exchange rescattering. In the resonance region new electroproduction data at Q^2=1GeV^2 has been analyzed with MAID and longitudinal and transverse photon helicity amplitudes have been determined for different resonances. A detailed study of the E/M and S/M ratios of the N->Delta transition shows a zero crossing of REM near Q^2=4GeV^2, whereas the RSM becomes increasingly negative at large Q^2.

## Key claim

Pion photo- and electroproduction data in the resonance region below W=2 GeV can be described by the unitary isobar model MAID and the dynamical model DMT, but the claim that the E/M ratio of the N to Delta transition crosses zero near Q^2=4 GeV^2 is presented as a finding from the authors' own analysis, in contrast to an earlier analysis of the same data by Frolov (1999) which concluded that R_EM would stay negative and become more negative with increasing Q^2.

## Method

The authors used two models: the unitary isobar model MAID, which combines Born terms, vector meson exchange, and Breit-Wigner resonance excitations with K-matrix unitarization, and the dynamical model DMT, which includes pion loop effects via principal value integrals over the pi-N reaction matrix. For the threshold region, they solved coupled channels equations for pi0 photoproduction and electroproduction, including charge exchange rescattering and cusp effects from the pi+ n channel. For the resonance region, they performed a fit of MAID to 363 data points from JLab Hall A at Q^2=1.0 GeV^2, varying 18 resonance parameters, and extracted helicity amplitudes from the imaginary parts of multipoles at resonance positions.

## Key numbers

- For the Delta(1232) at Q^2=1.0 GeV^2: R_EM = -2.0 +/- 0.9 percent, R_SM = -8.1 +/- 0.2 percent
- For the Delta(1232) at Q^2=1.0 GeV^2: helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} = -72 +/- 3 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), A_{3/2} = -135 +/- 3 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), S_{1/2} = 18 +/- 1 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2})
- For the D13(1520) at Q^2=1.0 GeV^2: A_{1/2} = -47 +/- 6 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), A_{3/2} = 26 +/- 4 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2})
- For the S11(1535) at Q^2=1.0 GeV^2: A_{1/2} = 61 +/- 14 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}), S_{1/2} = -15 +/- 2 (10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2})
- The phenomenological correction parameter for low-energy pi0 photoproduction: A = 2.01 x 10^{-3}/m_{pi+}, B = 0.71 fm
- The PV-PS mixing parameter Lambda_m = 350 +/- 35 MeV from the fit at Q^2=1.0 GeV^2
- The zero crossing of R_EM is found near Q^2=4 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the MAID unitary isobar model (Drechsel, 1999) and the dynamical model DMT (Kamalov and Yang, 1999) for analyzing pion photo- and electroproduction. The threshold analysis builds on chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard, 1991; Bernard, 1996) and the cusp effect treatment (Bernstein, 1998). The resonance analysis provides new fits to JLab Hall A data (Laveissiere, 2001) and reanalyzes JLab Hall C data (Frolov, 1999), finding a different Q^2 evolution of the Delta transition ratios. This enables improved extraction of resonance parameters and longitudinal couplings where previous information was lacking, and sets the stage for tests of perturbative QCD predictions at Q^2 above 5 GeV^2.
