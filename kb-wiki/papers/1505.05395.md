# Understanding the structure of $d^*(2380)$ in chiral quark model

> The structure and decay properties of $d^*$ have been detailedly investigated in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model that describe the energies of baryon ground states and the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering data satisfactorily. By performing a dynamical coupled-channels study of the system of $ΔΔ$ and hidden-color channel (CC) with quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(3^+)$ in the framework of the resonating group method (RGM), we find that the $d^*$ has a mass of about $2.38-2.42$ GeV and a root-mean-square radius (RMS) of about $0.76-0.88$ fm. The channel wave function is extracted by a projection of the RGM wave function onto the physical basis, and the fraction of CC component in the $d^*$ is found to be about $66\%-68\%$, which indicates that the $d^*$ is a hexaquark-dominated exotic state. Based on this scenario the partial decay widths of $d^*\to d π^0 π^0$ and $d^*\to d π^+π^-$ are further explicitly evaluated and the total width is then obtained by use of the branching ratios extracted from the measured cross sections of other possible decay channels. Both the mass and the decay width of $d^*$ calculated in this work are compatible with the data ($M\approx 2380$ MeV, $Γ\approx 70$ MeV) reported by WASA-at-COSY Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $d^*(2380)$ resonance is a hexaquark-dominated exotic state with a hidden-color channel (CC) component of about 66%-68%, based on dynamical coupled-channels calculations in chiral quark models, but this conclusion depends on the specific model parameters and the types of confinement potential used, with the results tested to be "rather stable" against variations.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical coupled-channels study of the $\Delta\Delta$ and hidden-color channel (CC) system with quantum numbers $I(J^P)=0(3^+)$ using the resonating group method (RGM) within both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model. They solved the RGM bound-state equation to obtain the binding energy and wave function, then extracted the channel wave functions by projecting the RGM wave function onto the physical basis to determine the $\Delta\Delta$ and CC components. The partial decay widths for $d^*\to d\pi^0\pi^0$ and $d^*\to d\pi^+\pi^-$ were explicitly evaluated, and the total width was obtained using branching ratios extracted from measured cross sections of other decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $d^*$: about 2.38-2.42 GeV, compatible with the experimental value of about 2380 MeV reported by WASA-at-COSY Collaboration
- Root-mean-square radius (RMS) of $d^*$: about 0.76-0.88 fm
- Fraction of hidden-color channel (CC) component in $d^*$: about 66%-68%
- Binding energy of $d^*$ in the $\Delta\Delta$-CC coupled calculation: 47.27 MeV (chiral SU(3) model), 83.95 MeV (extended SU(3) with f/g=0), 70.25 MeV (extended SU(3) with f/g=2/3)
- Binding energy of deuteron: 2.09-2.24 MeV, with RMS of 1.34-1.38 fm
- Total decay width of $d^*$: compatible with the experimental value of about 70 MeV reported by WASA-at-COSY Collaboration

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions from 1999 (YZYS99) of a $\Delta\Delta$-CC bound state with binding energy of about 40-80 MeV, which was compatible with the later observed $d^*(2380)$ mass. It contrasts with the $\pi N\Delta$ three-body calculation by Gal and Garcilazo (2013, 2014) that found a dynamically generated pole at the right mass with about 100 MeV width, and with the chiral SU(2) quark model calculation by Huang, Ping and Wang (2014) that obtained a width of about 150 MeV, which the authors consider too large. The results enable future experimental measurements of the $d^*$ form factor through electromagnetic transitions to distinguish between different theoretical pictures of the resonance structure.
