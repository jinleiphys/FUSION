# $K^{*0}$ and $Σ^*$ production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV and 62.4 GeV

> Applying a quark combination model for the hadronization of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) and A Relativistic Transport (ART) model for the subsequent hadronic rescattering process, we investigate the production of $K^{*0}$ and $Σ^*$ resonances in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV and 62.4 GeV. The initial $K^{*0}$ produced via hadronization is higher than the experimental data in the low $p_T$ region and is close to the data at 2-3 GeV/c. We take into account the hadronic rescattering effects which lead to a strong suppression of $K^{*0}$ with low $p_T$, and find that the $p_T$ spectrum of $K^{*0}$ can be well described. According to the suppressed magnitude of $K^{*0}$ yield, the time span of hadronic rescattering stage is estimated to be about 13 fm/c at 200 GeV and 5 fm/c at 62.4 GeV. The $p_T$ spectrum of $Σ^*$ directly obtained by quark combination hadronization in central Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV is in well agreement with the experimental data, which shows a weak hadronic rescattering effects. The elliptic flow v2 of $Σ^*$ in minimum bias Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and $p_T$ spectrum of $Σ^*$ at lower 62.4 GeV are predicted.

## Key claim

The quark combination model for hadronization of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) alone overpredicts the $K^{*0}$ yield in the low $p_T$ region, but when combined with hadronic rescattering effects from the ART model, the final $p_T$ spectrum of $K^{*0}$ can be well described; the time span of the hadronic rescattering stage is estimated to be about 13 fm/c at 200 GeV and 5 fm/c at 62.4 GeV, assuming a vector-to-pseudoscalar (V/P) ratio of 0.45. For $\Sigma^*$, the hadronization results alone agree with experimental data, indicating weak hadronic rescattering effects.

## Method

The authors used the Shandong Quark Combination Model (SDQCM) to simulate the hadronization of QGP into initial hadrons, including $K^{*0}$ and $\Sigma^*$ resonances, in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV and 62.4 GeV. They then applied the A Relativistic Transport (ART) model, as implemented in the AMPT event generator V2.25t3, to simulate the subsequent hadronic rescattering stage, treating the rescattering time as an adjustable parameter. The input quark $p_T$ spectra were fixed by fitting the experimental data of $\pi^-$ and $K^-$ mesons, and the vector-to-pseudoscalar (V/P) ratio for meson formation was varied (values of 3, 1, and 0.45) to test its influence on the $K^{*0}$ yield and the extracted rescattering time.

## Key numbers

- The time span of the hadronic rescattering stage needed to reproduce the $K^{*0}$ yield in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV is about 13 fm/c for V/P = 0.45, about 30 fm/c for V/P = 1, and about 60 fm/c for V/P = 3.
- The time span of the hadronic rescattering stage needed to reproduce the $K^{*0}$ yield in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 62.4 GeV is about 5 fm/c for V/P = 0.45, about 14 fm/c for V/P = 1, and about 20 fm/c for V/P = 3.
- The yield density $dN/dy$ of $\Sigma^* + \overline{\Sigma}^*$ at midrapidity just after hadronization in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 200 GeV is 9.0, compared to the STAR experimental data of $9.3 \pm 1.4 \pm 1.2$.
- The yield density of $\Sigma^*$ at midrapidity just after hadronization in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 62.4 GeV is predicted to be 4.3.
- The $K^{*0}$ $p_T$ spectrum from hadronization is close to experimental data in the range 2-3 GeV/c but overpredicts data in the low $p_T$ region.
- The slope parameter $T_s$ for the thermal exponential fit of quark $p_T$ spectra at 62.4 GeV is 0.31 GeV for strange quarks and 0.29 GeV for light quarks.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Shandong Quark Combination Model (SDQCM), previously used to describe stable hadron production at RHIC (Shao 2005, Shao 2007, Shao 2009), to short-lived resonances $K^{*0}$ and $\Sigma^*$. It directly tests the quark combination mechanism against resonance data from STAR (KstarRHIC62_200GeV, StrangeBresonance200Gev) and uses the ART transport model (Li and Bao 1995, 2001) to quantify hadronic rescattering effects. The extracted hadronic rescattering time of about 13 fm/c at 200 GeV is consistent with the typical lifespan of $13 \pm 3$ fm/c from UrQMD simulations (Bleicher 2002), and the results enable predictions for $\Sigma^*$ elliptic flow v2 and $p_T$ spectra at lower energies for future STAR data comparison.
