# Future Directions in Parity Violation: From Quarks to the Cosmos

> I discuss the prospects for future studies of parity-violating (PV) interactions at low energies and the insights they might provide about open questions in the Standard Model as well as physics that lies beyond it. I cover four types of parity-violating observables: PV electron scattering; PV hadronic interactions; PV correlations in weak decays; and searches for the permanent electric dipole moments of quantum systems.

# Future Directions in Parity Violation: From Quarks to the Cosmos

## Key claim
Parity-violating observables at low energies can provide insights into open questions in the Standard Model and physics beyond it, but the interpretation of hadronic parity violation remains complicated by the interplay of weak and strong interactions, and the success of extracting meaningful constraints depends on systematic effective field theory frameworks and few-body measurements that avoid nuclear many-body uncertainties.

## Method
The paper surveys four categories of parity-violating observables: parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) to probe nucleon structure and strange quark contributions; parity-violating deep inelastic scattering (PV DIS) to investigate higher-twist effects beyond the parton model; hadronic parity-violating interactions analyzed via effective field theory with seven low-energy constants; and parity-violating correlations in weak decays (muon and neutron) as probes of non-Standard Model interactions. The author also discusses searches for permanent electric dipole moments as tests of time-reversal violation.

## Key numbers
- Strange quarks contribute at most (20 ± 15)% to the isoscalar magnetic moment of the nucleon, based on PVES experiments (SAMPLE, PV A4, HAPPEX, G0).
- The nominal scale of hadronic parity-violating effects is ≤ 10⁻⁷, with enhancements possible in nuclei due to accidental near degeneracies of opposite parity states.
- In the Standard Model, the Michel parameters for muon decay are ρ = δ = 3/4, P_μξ = 1, and η = 0; deviations would indicate non-(V−A)×(V−A) interactions.
- Neutrino mass naturalness considerations imply bounds on |g^V_{LR,LR}| ≤ 10⁻⁴ for certain couplings in muon decay.
- The PV N→Δ asymmetry in the real photon limit is given by A_PV^{N→Δ} = −2 (d_Δ / C^V_3) (m_N / Λ_had), with expected magnitude of order 10⁻⁶ if dynamics similar to hyperon radiative decays apply.

## Context
This talk extends the long history of parity violation studies following the discovery by Wu (1957) and Garwin (1957), confirming the Lee-Yang prediction (1956). The PVES program built on suggestions by Kaplan and Manohar (1988), McKeown (1989), and Beck (1989) to probe strange quark contributions, and the results now rule out many early theoretical expectations from Jaffe (1989) and others. The hadronic parity violation program reformulates the older meson-exchange framework of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) using effective field theory, following work by Zhu (2004) and Ramsey-Musolf (2006). The weak decay correlation studies connect to ongoing TWIST collaboration results and future neutron decay experiments at the Spallation Neutron Source, and the neutrino mass naturalness arguments follow from work by Prezeau (2004) and Erwin (2006). The paper enables a systematic program of few-body parity-violation measurements at SNS and NIST, and motivates future PV DIS experiments to resolve the puzzle of missing higher-twist effects in Jefferson Lab inclusive electroproduction data.
