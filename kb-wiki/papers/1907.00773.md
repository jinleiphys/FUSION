# Fluid Dynamics Study of the $Λ$ Polarization for Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> With a Yang-Mills field, stratified shear flow initial state and a high resolution (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic (PICR) hydrodynamic model, we calculate the $Λ$ polarization for peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy of $\sqrt{S_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The obtained longitudinal polarization in our model agrees with the experimental signature and the quadrupole structure on transverse momentum plane. It is found that the relativistic correction (2nd term), arising from expansion and from the time component of the thermal vorticity, plays a crucial role in our results. This term is changing the signature and exceeds the first term, arising from the classical vorticity. Finally, the global polarization in our model shows no significant dependence on rapidity, which agrees with the experimental data. It is also found that the second term flattens the sharp peak arising from the classical vorticity (1st term).

## Key claim

Using a Yang-Mills field stratified shear flow initial state combined with a (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic hydrodynamic model, the authors calculate that the relativistic correction (second term) from the temporal component of thermal vorticity is crucial for obtaining the correct sign distribution of longitudinal \(\Lambda\) polarization, and that this second term exceeds the first term arising from classical vorticity.

## Method

The authors simulated peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV using a Yang-Mills flux-tube initial state divided into slab-slab collisions, which naturally generates longitudinal velocity shear flow. This initial state was evolved with a high resolution (3+1)D Particle-in-Cell Relativistic (PICR) hydrodynamic model with cell size \(0.343^3\) fm\(^3\) and time increment 0.0423 fm/c. The \(\Lambda\) polarization was calculated from the relativistic thermal vorticity at freeze-out using the formula from Becattini et al. (2013), with the polarization vector separated into a spatial vorticity term and a temporal relativistic correction term.

## Key numbers

* Global polarization for Au+Au 200 GeV at centrality 25%: approximately 2.8% (from figure description, averaged value for \(b_0=0.5\))
* Global polarization for Au+Au 200 GeV at centrality 36%: approximately 3.8% (from figure description, averaged value for \(b_0=0.6\))
* Global polarization for Au+Au 200 GeV at centrality 46%: approximately 6% (from figure description, averaged value for \(b_0=0.68\))
* Peak value of longitudinal polarization at \(p_T = 1.4\) GeV: about 0.5%, similar magnitude to the global polarization at \(b=0.5\) fm/c
* Freeze-out time: 4+4.91 fm/c for 25% and 36% centralities; 3.5+4.75 fm/c for 46% centrality
* Cell size: \(0.343^3\) fm\(^3\); time increment: 0.0423 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic simulations of \(\Lambda\) polarization by the same group for FAIR's U+U 8 GeV collisions (Xie 2016) and earlier calculations (Becattini et al. 2013). It directly addresses discrepancies between theory and STAR collaboration experimental data at RHIC, particularly the sign problem of longitudinal polarization and the in-plane versus out-of-plane behavior of global polarization. The results enable comparison with alternative approaches that use projected thermal vorticity (Ivanov et al. 2019; Florkowski et al. 2019) and provide a comprehensive calculation that can be directly compared to experimental differential measurements.
