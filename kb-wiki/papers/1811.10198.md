# Sensitivity of nuclear statistical equilibrium to nuclear uncertainties during stellar core collapse

> I have systematically investigated the equations of state (EOSs) in nuclear statistical equilibrium under thermodynamic conditions relevant for core collapse of massive stars by varying the bulk properties of nuclear matter, the mass data for neutron-rich nuclei, and the finite-temperature modifications of the nuclear model. It is found that the temperature dependence of the nuclear free energies has a significant impact on the entropy and nuclear composition, which affect the dynamics of core-collapse supernovae. There is a little influence from the bulk properties and the mass data. For all models, common nuclei that are likely to contribute to core-deleptonization are those near $Z\approx30$ and $N\approx50$. A model with a semi-empirical expression for internal degrees of freedom, however, overestimates the number densities of magic nuclei with $N\approx50$ and $82$, while a model, in which nuclear shell effects are not considered, underestimates the number densities of heavy nuclei, and especially of the magic nuclei. Other models, which include the temperature dependence of shell effects in the internal degrees of freedom and/or in the nuclear internal free energy, indicate that the difference in population between magic nuclei and non-magic nuclei disappears as the temperature increases. The construction of complete statistical EOS will require further theoretical and experimental studies of medium-mass, neutron-rich nuclei with proton numbers 25-45 and neutron numbers 40-85.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of nuclear free energies has a significant impact on entropy and nuclear composition during core collapse, while the bulk properties of nuclear matter and mass data for neutron-rich nuclei have little influence; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific finite-temperature modeling approach used, as models that omit shell effects or use semi-empirical partition functions produce substantially different nuclear compositions.

## Method

The author systematically varied three nuclear inputs in statistical equilibrium equations of state (EOSs): bulk nuclear matter properties (using parameter sets B, D, and E with different incompressibility and symmetry energy values), theoretical nuclear mass data (FRDM, HFB24, KTUY, or WS4), and finite-temperature treatments of nuclear excitations (temperature-dependent internal free energy in models 1 and 2, or temperature-dependent internal degrees of freedom in models 3 and 4). The free-energy density was minimized with respect to number densities of all particles under baryon and charge conservation constraints, and the resulting nuclear compositions were compared along core-collapse trajectories for 15 solar mass and 25 solar mass stars from Juodagalvis et al. (2010).

## Key numbers

- For the 25 solar mass star at density 1.9 × 10^11 g/cm^3, temperature 1.3 MeV, and charge fraction 0.36, the mass distributions of heavy nuclei were compared across models 1E, 2FE, 3FE, and 4FE
- For the 15 solar mass star at density 2.0 × 10^12 g/cm^3, temperature 1.8 MeV, and charge fraction 0.28, the same model comparison was performed
- The critical temperature for nuclear vaporization was set to T_cAZ = 18 MeV in model 1
- The shell washout parameter tau_AZ = 2 pi^2 T / (41 A^{-1/3}) was used in model 2
- The semi-empirical internal partition function in model 3 used parameters c_1 = 0.2 MeV^{-1} and c_2 = 0.8, with level density parameter a(A) = (A/8)(1 - 0.8 A^{-1/3}) MeV^{-1}
- At T = 3 MeV, nuclear shell effects were significantly reduced, especially for nuclei with large mass numbers
- Common nuclei contributing to core-deleptonization across all models were those near Z approximately 30 and N approximately 50
- The average mass numbers for models 1B, 1D, and 1E were smaller and grew more gradually than other models
- At density approximately 10^12 g/cm^3, model 3FE showed abrupt change from nuclei with N approximately 50 to those with N approximately 82

## Context

This work systematically compares multiple statistical EOS approaches (SMSM, FYSS, HS, SRO) that have been developed by different groups, including Hempel (2010), Typel (2010), Furusawa (2011, 2013, 2017), Schneider (2017), and Botvina (2004, 2010). Previous comparisons by Buyukcizmeci (2013), Hempel (2015), and Oertel (2017) were not systematic enough for quantitative conclusions. The study identifies that medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei with proton numbers 25-45 and neutron numbers 40-85 require further theoretical and experimental study to construct complete statistical EOSs, and that the temperature dependence of shell effects determines whether magic and non-magic nuclei populations differ at high temperatures.
