# Lattice QCD Results on Strangeness and Quasi-Quarks in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Fluctuations of conserved quantities in heavy-ion collisions have been argued to be diagnostic tools for the nature of the produced phase. These can be related to the predictions of quark number susceptibilities (QNS) from lattice QCD. Using the diagonal QNS, we extracted the Wroblewski parameter in a dynamical QCD computation. Our results on the cross correlations χ_{BQ}, χ_{BY}, χ_{BS} and χ_{QY} allow us to explore the charge and baryon number of objects that carry flavour. We present evidence that in the high temperature phase of QCD the different flavour quantum numbers are excited in linkages which are exactly the same as one expects from quarks.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD evidence that in the high-temperature phase of QCD, different flavour quantum numbers are excited in linkages exactly matching those of quarks, but the authors caution that this agreement near T_c occurs in a regime where the coupling is not weak, so the excitations are termed "quasi-quarks" rather than free quarks.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of light dynamical quarks (u and d) using staggered fermions and the standard Wilson gluonic action, computing diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities (QNS) from the QCD partition function via stochastic trace estimation. They extracted the Wroblewski parameter λ_s from the ratio of diagonal susceptibilities χ_s/(χ_u+χ_d) and studied flavour correlations through ratios of off-diagonal susceptibilities such as C_BS and C_QS, examining their behaviour as functions of temperature across the QCD transition.

## Key numbers

- The Wroblewski parameter at T_c is λ_s ≈ 0.4, in agreement with experimental extraction from RHIC and SPS data when the freeze-out temperature is close to T_c.
- The ratio C_B/Q and C_(QY)/Q at T=2T_c show very good scaling behaviour with lattice spacing, with r(a)=r+O(a^n) where n>2.
- Quenched and partially quenched results on N_t=4 lattices differ by only a few per cent, although T_c in the two cases differs by a factor of 1.6.
- The simulations used bare sea quark mass m=0.1T_c, giving m_π=0.3m_ρ, with light valence quark mass m_v=0.03T_c and strange valence quark mass m_s=T_c.
- Temperature values studied include T/T_c = 0.75±0.02, 0.8±0.02, 0.85±0.01, 0.9±0.01, 0.95±0.01, 1.00±0.01, 1.045±0.01, 1.25±0.02, 1.65±0.06, and 2.15±0.10, with additional sets at 0.975±0.010 and 1.15±0.01.

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched QCD results on the Wroblewski parameter (Gavai and Gupta, 2005) to full QCD with two dynamical light quarks, and provides the first lattice QCD computation of the correlation variables C_BS and C_QS proposed by Koch, Majumder, and Randrup (2005) to distinguish between bound-state QGP and ordinary quark-gluon plasma. The results enable direct comparison with experimental fluctuation measurements from RHIC and SPS, and support the interpretation that the high-temperature phase of QCD consists of quasi-quarks rather than free quarks or hadronic bound states.
