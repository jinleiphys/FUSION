# Masses and magnetic moments of pentaquarks

> We discuss the spectroscopy of pentaquarks. The quantum numbers of the ground state depend on the interplay between spin-flavor and orbital contributions to the energy. The magnetic moments of the lowest pentaquark state with negative and positive parity are found to be 0.382 μ_N and 0.089 μ_n, respectively.

## Key claim

The parity of the ground-state pentaquark depends on the relative size of the orbital excitation energy and the spin-flavor hyperfine splitting: if the spin-flavor term is large compared to the orbital spacing the ground state has positive parity, whereas for a relatively small spin-flavor splitting the parity becomes negative. The magnetic moments reported in the abstract (0.382 μ_N and 0.089 μ_N) are therefore conditional on which parity state is actually the lowest.

## Method

The authors construct a complete classification of q⁴q̄ pentaquark states using spin-flavor SU(6) symmetry, imposing color-singlet and Pauli antisymmetry for the four-quark subsystem. They compute magnetic moments for the lowest exotic antidecuplet states (Θ⁺, Ξ₃/₂⁺, Ξ₃/₂⁻⁻) in a harmonic oscillator quark model, separating the cases of negative parity (orbital S-wave, L=0⁺) and positive parity (orbital P-wave, L=1⁻). They then repeat the magnetic moment calculation for a stringlike algebraic model using a generalized Gürsey-Radicati mass-squared operator with coefficients taken from previous work on q³ baryons.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic moment of the Θ⁺ pentaquark with negative parity: 0.382 μ_N (from Eq. 13, using quark moments μ_u=1.852 μ_N, μ_d=-0.972 μ_N, μ_s=-0.613 μ_N)
* Magnetic moment of the Θ⁺ pentaquark with positive parity: 0.089 μ_N (from Eq. 14)
* Magnetic moment of the Ξ₃/₂⁻⁻ cascade with negative parity: -0.439 μ_N
* Magnetic moment of the Ξ₃/₂⁺ cascade with negative parity: 0.502 μ_N
* In the stringlike algebraic model, the ground state Θ⁺(1540) has Jᵖ=1/2⁻, with the first excited state (isospin triplet, S=+1, 27-plet) at 1599 MeV and the lowest positive-parity pentaquark at 1668 MeV
* The antidecuplet state with strangeness S=-2 and isospin I=3/2 is calculated at 1956 MeV (compared to the observed Ξ⁻⁻(1862) from the NA49 Collaboration)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model treatment of exotic baryons following the experimental discovery of the Θ⁺(1540) by the LEPS Collaboration and the Ξ⁻⁻(1862) by the NA49 Collaboration. It provides the first uncorrelated (constituent) quark model calculation of pentaquark magnetic moments, complementing prior results from chiral soliton models (Kim), diquark models (Nam, Zhao, Liu), the MIT bag model (Zhao, Liu), and light-cone QCD sum rules (Huang). The stringlike algebraic model uses interaction strengths from a previous study of nonstrange and strange baryon resonances (Bijker, Iachello, Leviatan) and predicts a negative-parity ground state, in agreement with QCD sum rules (sumrule), lattice QCD (lattice), and a quark model calculation (cqmneg). The results enable calculations of photo- and electroproduction cross sections for pentaquarks, which are needed to guide experimental parity determination.
