# 3He structure from coherent hard exclusive processes

> Hard exclusive processes, such as deep electroproduction of photons and mesons off nuclear targets, could give access, in the coherent channel, to nuclear generalized parton distributions (GPDs). Here, a realistic microscopic calculation of the unpolarized quark GPD H_q of the 3He nucleus is reviewed. In Impulse Approximation, H_q is obtained as a convolution between the GPD of the internal nucleon and the non-diagonal spectral function, describing properly Fermi motion and binding effects. The obtained formula has the correct limits. Nuclear effects, evaluated by a modern realistic potential, are found to be larger than in the forward case. In particular, they increase with increasing the momentum transfer and the asymmetry of the process. Another feature of the obtained results is that the nuclear GPD cannot be factorized into a Delta^2-dependent and a Delta^2-independent term, as suggested in prescriptions proposed for finite nuclei. The dependence of the obtained GPDs on different realistic potentials used in the calculation shows that these quantities are sensitive to the details of nuclear structure at short distances.

## Key claim

The paper presents a realistic microscopic calculation of the unpolarized quark generalized parton distribution (GPD) H_q for the 3He nucleus, finding that nuclear effects are larger than in the forward case and increase with momentum transfer and process asymmetry, but the author explicitly states that "the main result of this investigation is not the size and shape of the obtained H_q^3 for 3He, but the size and nature of nuclear effects on it."

## Method

The unpolarized quark GPD H_q^3 of 3He was calculated in Impulse Approximation (IA) as a convolution between the GPD of the internal nucleon and the non-diagonal spectral function, which describes Fermi motion and binding effects. The non-diagonal spectral function was evaluated using overlap integrals between the 3He ground state wave function and the eigenfunctions of the two-nucleon recoiling system, with realistic wave functions calculated using the AV18 interaction including Coulomb repulsion of protons. The nucleon GPD H_q^N was modeled using the Double Distribution representation, with the Delta^2 dependence given by the quark contribution to the nucleon form factor obtained from experimental values of the proton and neutron Dirac form factors.

## Key numbers

- The removal energy E_min = |E_3He| - |E_2H| = 5.5 MeV, defining the minimum energy in the delta function of the spectral function
- Nuclear effects for x_3 <= 0.7 are as large as 15% at most
- The calculation is valid for Delta^2 << Q^2, M^2, with numerical results shown at Delta^2 = -0.15 GeV^2 and Delta^2 = -0.25 GeV^2
- The x integral of the nuclear GPD gives a good description of form factor data of 3He in the kinematical region -Delta^2 <= 0.25 GeV^2
- The GPDs are shown for the allowed xi_3 range and in the x_3 >= 0 region, with xi_3 values of 0, 0.1, and 0.2 presented

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear GPDs beyond the deuteron case (Cano and Pire, 2002) and finite nuclei (Guichon and Vanderhaeghen, 2003) to the specific case of 3He, which is well known and allows realistic studies of conventional nuclear effects. The calculation demonstrates that the nuclear GPD cannot be factorized into a Delta^2-dependent and a Delta^2-independent term as suggested in prescriptions proposed for finite nuclei, and that nuclear GPDs are sensitive to the details of nuclear structure at short distances, as shown by the dependence on different realistic NN potentials (AV18 versus AV14). The results enable future calculations of DVCS cross sections off 3He and studies of polarized GPDs for the free neutron.
