# Properties of neutral mesons in a hot and magnetized quark matter

> The properties of non-interacting $σ$ and $π^{0}$ mesons are studied at finite temperature, chemical potential and in the presence of a constant magnetic field. To do this, the energy dispersion relations of these particles, including nontrivial form factors, are derived using a derivative expansion of the effective action of a two-flavor, hot and magnetized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model up to second order. The temperature dependence of the pole and screening masses as well as the directional refraction indices of magnetized neutral mesons are explored for fixed magnetic fields and chemical potentials. It is shown that, because of the explicit breaking of the Lorentz invariance by the magnetic field, the refraction index and the screening mass of neutral mesons exhibit a certain anisotropy in the transverse and longitudinal directions with respect to the direction of the external magnetic field. In contrast to their longitudinal refraction indices, the transverse indices of the neutral mesons are larger than unity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a hot and magnetized quark matter, the refraction indices and screening masses of neutral sigma and pi0 mesons exhibit anisotropy between transverse and longitudinal directions relative to the magnetic field, with transverse refraction indices larger than unity, but this result is derived for non-interacting mesons using a derivative expansion of the effective action of a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model up to second order, and the mesons are massive so the transverse index exceeding unity does not imply superluminal propagation.

## Method

The authors used a derivative expansion of the one-loop effective action of a two-flavor NJL model up to second order to derive energy dispersion relations for sigma and pi0 mesons in the presence of a constant magnetic field at finite temperature and chemical potential. They computed the squared mass matrices and kinetic coefficients (form factors) analytically, performing numerical integration over p3-momentum and summation over Landau levels to determine the temperature dependence of pole masses, directional refraction indices, and screening masses for fixed magnetic field strengths eB = 0.03, 0.2, and 0.3 GeV^2 at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* For eB = 0.03 GeV^2 (corresponding to B ~ 1.5 m_pi^2, with m_pi = 138 MeV), the transverse refraction index of neutral mesons is larger than unity while the longitudinal refraction index equals unity.
* For eB = 0.2 GeV^2 and eB = 0.3 GeV^2, the same anisotropy pattern is observed: transverse refraction indices exceed unity, longitudinal indices equal unity.
* The magnetic fields studied correspond to eB = 0.03 GeV^2 (RHIC scale) and eB = 0.3 GeV^2 (LHC scale), where eB = 1 GeV^2 corresponds to B ~ 1.7 x 10^20 Gauss.
* Stronger magnetic fields eB = 0.5 and 0.7 GeV^2 lead to instabilities at low temperature.

## Context

This work extends studies of pion properties in hot and dense matter without magnetic fields, such as Shuryak (1990) and Pisarski (1996), to include constant magnetic fields, and contrasts with studies by Andersen (2011) and Anderson (2012) that used chiral perturbation theory to study charged pions interacting directly with the magnetic field. The results enable future investigation of charged magnetized pions and provide a theoretical framework for understanding how magnetic fields produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC affect neutral meson properties, despite the mesons lacking electric charge.
