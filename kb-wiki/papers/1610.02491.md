# Deformed shell model study of heavy N=Z nuclei and dark matter detection

> Deformed shell model (DSM) based on Hartree-Fock intrinsic states is applied to address two current problems of interest. Firstly, in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space with jj44b effective interaction along with isospin projection, DSM is used to describe the structure of the recently observed low-lying $T=0$ and $T=1$ bands in the heavy odd-odd N=Z nucleus $^{66}$As. DSM results are close to the data and also to the shell model results. For the $T=1$ band, DSM predicts structural change at $8^+$ just as in the shell model. In addition, the lowest two $T=0$ bands are found to have quasi-deuteron structure above a $^{64}$Ge core and the $5^+$ and $9^+$ levels of the third $T=0$ band are found to be isomeric states. Secondly, in a first application of DSM to dark matter, detection rates for the lightest supersymmetric particle (a dark matter candidate) are calculated with $^{73}$Ge as the detector.

## Key claim

The deformed shell model (DSM), using Hartree-Fock intrinsic states with angular momentum and isospin projection, reproduces the observed low-lying T=0 and T=1 bands in the heavy odd-odd N=Z nucleus ⁶⁶As and, in a first application of DSM to dark matter, calculates detection rates for the lightest supersymmetric particle (LSP) with ⁷³Ge as the detector; the agreement for ⁶⁶As is close to both experimental data and shell model results, but the DSM prediction for the 9⁺ level in band 4 is higher than experiment by about 1 MeV.

## Method

For ⁶⁶As, DSM calculations were performed in the f₅/₂pg₉/₂ model space with a ⁵⁶Ni inert core using the jj44b effective interaction, employing 114 intrinsic configurations (78 prolate, 36 oblate) generated by particle-hole excitations, with isospin projection and band mixing. For ⁷³Ge, DSM was used to compute the spin structure functions and static spin matrix elements needed for the LSP-nucleus elastic scattering cross section, using three positive-parity and three negative-parity intrinsic states with a modified Kuo interaction.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁶As, the prolate HF intrinsic state is more bound than the oblate state by more than 1 MeV.
- For the T=1 band in ⁶⁶As, DSM B(E2) ratios B(E2,I→I-2)/B(E2,I-2→I-4) are 1.22 (I=4), 0.97 (I=6), and 0.001 (I=8); corresponding shell model ratios are 1.29, 1.09, and 0.001.
- For the T=1, 8₁⁺ level in ⁶⁶As, the shell model occupancy of the 1g₉/₂ orbit is 1.05, compared to about 0.64 for lower T=1 levels.
- For ⁷³Ge, the DSM magnetic moment is −0.811 μ_N, close to the experimental value −0.879 μ_N.
- For ⁷³Ge, DSM gives Ω₀ = 0.798 and Ω₁ = −0.803, which are 20–30% smaller than values from a quasi-particle-phonon model.
- The LSP mass m_χ is taken as 110 GeV and the escape velocity v_esc as 625 km/s.

## Context

This work extends previous DSM studies of N=Z nuclei in the A=60–100 region (Sahu and Kota, 2005; Kota and Sahu, 2009) by including six-particle configurations and mixing of prolate and oblate intrinsic states, showing considerable improvement over earlier DSM results that used only limited two-particle configurations. The ⁶⁶As results confirm the spin-aligned np isoscalar paired phase seen in ⁹²Pd (Cederwall, 2011) and agree with cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations and shell model predictions (Srivastava, 2015). The dark matter application provides DSM-based spin structure functions for ⁷³Ge that are similar to those from the quasi-particle-phonon model (Holmlund, 2004) and shell model (Ressell, 1993), enabling future studies of inelastic WIMP-nucleus scattering in ⁸³Kr.
