# Universal scaling of non-equilibrium critical fluctuations from Langevin dynamics of model A

> Within the framework of the Kibble-Zurek Mechanism, we investigate the universal behavior of the non-equilibrium critical fluctuations, using the Langevin dynamics of model A. With properly located typical time, length and angle scales, $τ_{\mbox{KZ}}$, $l_{\mbox{KZ}}$, and $θ_{\mbox{KZ}}$, the constructed functions $\bar{f}_n((τ-τ_c)/τ_{\mbox{KZ}},θ_{\mbox{KZ}})$ (n=1...4) for the cumulants of the sigma field show universal behavior near the critical point, which are independent from some non-universal factors, such as the relaxation time or the evolution trajectory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-equilibrium cumulants of the sigma field near the QCD critical point, computed via Langevin dynamics of model A, can be rescaled into universal functions that are independent of non-universal factors such as the relaxation time or the evolution trajectory, provided that the Kibble-Zurek scales (time, length, and angle) are properly identified.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Langevin equation for a non-conserved order parameter field (model A) in 3+1 dimensions, using an effective potential whose parameters are mapped from the 3d Ising model via a linear map between QCD variables (T, μ) and Ising variables (r, h). The system was evolved along two types of trajectories in the r-h plane: type A with fixed chemical potential (a_h=0, r_c=0.02Δr) and type B with approximately equal correlation length near the crossover line (r_c=0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9 with tuned a_h). The temperature of the heat bath followed a Hubble-like expansion T=T_I(τ/τ_I)^{-0.45}, and the effective relaxation time scaled as τ_eff = τ_rel (ξ_eq/ξ_min)^3 with dynamical critical exponent z=3. Cumulants C_1 through C_4 of the spatially averaged sigma field were computed event-by-event.

## Key numbers

* The temperature of the heat bath drops as T = T_I (τ/τ_I)^{-0.45}, with initial time τ_I and initial temperature T_I.
* The critical temperature is set to T_c = 0.16 GeV and the critical chemical potential to μ_c = 0.395 GeV.
* The boundary of the critical regime is defined by ξ_eq = 1 fm.
* For type A trajectories, the relaxation time parameter τ_rel/τ_c was varied over values 0.02, 0.06, 0.1, and 0.14.
* For type B trajectories, the parameter r_c was set to 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, and 0.9, with a_h tuned to yield approximately equal ξ_eq near the crossover.
* The mapping parameters are ΔT = T_c/8, Δμ = 0.1 GeV, Δr = (5/3)^{3/4}, Δh = 1.

## Context

This work extends the Kibble-Zurek scaling approach previously applied to the Fokker-Planck equation for the zero-mode sigma field by Mukherjee (2016) to the full Langevin dynamics of model A, which preserves spatial information. The results enable the construction of parameter-independent universal observables for the search of the QCD critical point in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, complementing earlier equilibrium studies by Stephanov (2008) and non-equilibrium studies by Mukherjee (2015) and Jiang (2017). The paper notes that the realistic QGP fireball evolution belongs to model H according to Son (2004) and the classification by Rev (1977), but model A is used as a simpler starting point.
