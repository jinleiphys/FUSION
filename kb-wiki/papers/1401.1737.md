# Nature of $γ$-deformation in Ge and Se nuclei and the triaxial projected shell model description

> Recent experimental data have demonstrated that $^{76}$Ge may be a rare example of a nucleus exhibiting rigid $γ$-deformation in the low-spin regime. In the present work, the experimental analysis is supported by microscopic calculations using the multi-quasiparticle triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach. It is shown that to best describe the data of both yrast and $γ$-vibrational bands in $^{76}$Ge, a rigid-triaxial deformation parameter $γ\approx 30^\circ$ is required. TPSM calculations are discussed in conjunction with the experimental observations and also with the published results from the spherical shell model. The occurrence of a $γγ$-band in $^{76}$Ge is predicted with the bandhead at an excitation energy of $ \sim$ 2.5 MeV. We have also performed TPSM study for the neighboring Ge- and Se-isotopes and the distinct $γ$-soft feature in these nuclei is shown to result from configuration mixing of the ground-state with multi-quasiparticle states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that $^{76}$Ge exhibits rigid $\gamma$-deformation in low-spin states, but this conclusion depends on the specific triaxial deformation parameter $\gamma \approx 30^\circ$ and only holds after full configuration mixing of multi-quasiparticle states; without such mixing, all studied Ge and Se isotopes show the same staggering phase.

## Method

The authors performed multi-quasiparticle triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) calculations using triaxially-deformed Nilsson states as a starting basis, with three-dimensional angular-momentum projection applied to 0-quasiparticle, 2-quasiparticle, and 4-quasiparticle configurations. The Hamiltonian included pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, with deformation parameters $\epsilon$ and $\epsilon'$ chosen to reproduce observed $\gamma$-band head energies. The calculated band energies, staggering parameters $S(I)$, and $B(E2)$ transition probabilities were compared with experimental data and with spherical shell model (SSM) results from Nyberg (2008).

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge, the rigid-triaxial deformation parameter required is $\gamma \approx 30^\circ$, corresponding to $\epsilon = 0.200$ and $\epsilon' = 0.160$
- The $\gamma\gamma$-band in $^{76}$Ge is predicted with bandhead at excitation energy $\sim 2.5$ MeV; calculated band-head energies of the $\gamma$- and $\gamma\gamma$-bands are about 1.21 MeV and 3.03 MeV above ground state
- The calculated $B(E2)$ ratio $R = B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+)/B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)$ for $^{76}$Ge is 0.05 (experimental: 0.027(3)); for $4_2^+ \rightarrow 4_1^+$ vs $4_2^+ \rightarrow 2_2^+$, TPSM gives 1.62 (experimental: 1.34(4))
- For $^{76}$Ge, the inter-band $B(E2)$ from $(2,2) \rightarrow (0,0)$ is 5.39 W.u.; for $^{78}$Se the same transition is 3.59 W.u.
- The axial deformation parameters $\epsilon$ range from 0.180 ($^{82}$Se) to 0.260 ($^{76}$Se); $\gamma$ values range from 30.37$^\circ$ ($^{78}$Se) to 38.66$^\circ$ ($^{76}$Ge)

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Y. Toh (2013) who identified $^{76}$Ge as a rare example of rigid $\gamma$-deformation based on Coulomb excitation and inelastic scattering data. The TPSM calculations complement the spherical shell model (SSM) study by Nyberg (2008), which focused on low-lying states, by providing a microscopic description of high-spin band structures up to $I \sim 20\hbar$. The results show that configuration mixing of ground-state with multi-quasiparticle states determines the staggering phase, explaining why neighboring nuclei appear $\gamma$-soft while $^{76}$Ge appears rigid; this enables future experimental searches for the predicted $\gamma\gamma$-band and high-spin lifetime measurements to distinguish between TPSM and SSM predictions.
