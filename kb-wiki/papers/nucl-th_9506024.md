# Two Model-Independent Results for the Momentum Dependence of Rho-Omega Mixing

> Two model-independent results on the momentum-dependence of $ρ$-$ω$ mixing are described. First, an explicit choice of interpolating fields for the vector mesons is displayed for which both the mixing in the propagator and the isospin-breaking at the nucleon-vector meson vertices (and hence also the one-vector-meson-exchange contribution to NN charge symmetry breaking) vanish identically at $q^2=0$. Second, it is shown, using the constraints of unitarity and analyticity on the spectral function of the vector meson propagator, that there is no possible choice of interpolating fields for the $ρ^0$, $ω^0$ mesons such that, with the $ρω$ element of the propagator defined by $Δ^{ρω}_{μν}(q^2)= (g_{μν}-q_μq_ν/q^2)$$θ(q^2)/(q^2-m^2_ρ)(q^2- m^2_ω)$, $θ(q^2)$ is independent of momentum. It follows that the standard treatment of charge symmetry breaking in few-body systems cannot be interpreted as arising from any realizable effective meson-baryon Lagrangian and must, therefore, be considered purely phenomenological in content.

# Two Model-Independent Results for the Momentum Dependence of Rho-Omega Mixing

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that there exists an explicit choice of interpolating fields for the rho and omega mesons such that the rho-omega mixing in the propagator and the isospin-breaking at nucleon-vector meson vertices both vanish identically at momentum transfer q^2=0; however, using unitarity and analyticity constraints, it is also shown that no choice of interpolating fields can make the mixing amplitude momentum-independent, meaning the standard treatment of charge symmetry breaking in few-body systems cannot be derived from any realizable effective meson-baryon Lagrangian.

## Method

The author constructs an explicit field redefinition for the rho^0 and omega^0 interpolating fields that eliminates the mixing at q^2=0. The momentum dependence of the rho-omega mixing amplitude theta(q^2) is then analyzed using the constraints of unitarity and analyticity applied to the spectral function of the vector meson propagator, demonstrating that theta(q^2) cannot be momentum-independent for any choice of interpolating fields.

## Context

This work provides rigorous model-independent constraints on the momentum dependence of rho-omega mixing, which is central to understanding charge symmetry breaking in nuclear systems. The results contradict the common phenomenological assumption that the mixing amplitude can be treated as momentum-independent, as done in standard treatments of charge symmetry breaking in few-body systems. The paper shows that such treatments must be considered purely phenomenological rather than derivable from any effective Lagrangian, extending prior work on the field-dependence of mixing amplitudes. This enables future work to focus on the actual momentum-dependent structure of the mixing rather than relying on the simplified constant-mixing approximation.
