# The angular correlation between the fission fragment intrinsic spins

> The generation of fission fragments (FF) spins and of their relative orbital angular momentum has been debated for more than six decades and no consensus has been yet achieved so far. (The published abstract, being too long, can be found in the manuscript.)

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strong angular correlation between primordial fission fragment intrinsic spins, with a preferred relative angle close to $2\pi/3$, arises primarily from phase-space constraints imposed by total angular momentum conservation rather than from detailed dynamical mechanisms, and that this conclusion is robust across different assumed spin distributions.

## Method

The author constructs probability distributions for the triplet of angular momenta (light fragment spin, heavy fragment spin, and relative orbital angular momentum) subject to the triangle constraint from total angular momentum conservation, using both uniform distributions and Fermi-gas statistical distributions with parameters reproducing microscopic TDDFT results from Bulgac et al. (2022). The angle between the two fragment spins is computed from the magnitudes of the three angular momenta via the law of cosines, and the resulting probability distribution p(φ^LH) is obtained by histogramming over all allowed triplets weighted by the assumed distributions.

## Key numbers

- The fraction of configurations with φ^LH > π/2 is approximately 0.65 in the classical limit (S_max → ∞), compared to less than 0.25 for φ^LH < π/2
- The number of allowed triplet configurations is N_0 ≈ (2/3)S_max^3 for large S_max, with the factor 2/3 reflecting the phase-space suppression of configurations with φ^LH < π/2
- The average angle between fragment spins from the statistical distribution (using σ^L = 108.2, σ^H = 44.8, σ^Λ = 161.3 for ²⁴⁰Pu) gives ⟨φ^LH⟩/π = 0.64, close to the uniform distribution result of 0.65
- Prompt neutrons and statistical gammas carry approximately 3.5–5 ℏ of angular momentum according to Stetcu et al. (2021)

## Context

This work extends the microscopic TDDFT results of Bulgac et al. (2022) by showing that the predicted strong antiparallel correlation of fragment spins follows from general phase-space arguments independent of the detailed dynamics. It contradicts the interpretation of Wilson et al. (2021) that fragment spins are uncorrelated and emerge after scission, and also disagrees with the FREYA model results of Randrup and Vogt (2021) which predict nearly uniform angular distributions. The analysis enables future experimental tests of the predicted angular correlation, as noted by Sobotka at the 2022 Workshop on Fission Fragment Angular Momenta.
