# Are nuclear matter properties correlated to neutron star observables ?

> We investigate properties of nuclear matter and examine possible correlations with neutron star observables for a set of microscopic nuclear equations of state derived within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism employing compatible three-body forces. We find good candidates for a realistic nuclear EOS up to high density and confirm strong correlations between neutron star radius, tidal deformability, and the pressure of betastable matter. No correlations are found with the saturation properties of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper confirms strong correlations between neutron star radius, tidal deformability, and the pressure of beta-stable matter at around twice saturation density, but finds no correlations between these neutron star observables and the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (such as binding energy, incompressibility, or symmetry energy at saturation density).

## Method

The authors computed nuclear equations of state (EOS) using the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory with realistic two-body nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bonn B, Nijmegen 93, Argonne V18) combined with compatible three-body forces, plus additional EOSs from the fss2 quark-model potential, Dirac-BHF, variational APR, and phenomenological relativistic mean-field models (LS220, SFHo). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, then compared these predictions against experimental constraints from heavy-ion collisions (KaoS and FOPI flow data), the GW170817 gravitational wave event, and nuclear saturation properties.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density values range from 0.155 fm^{-3} (LS220) to 0.185 fm^{-3} (N93), with experimental range ~0.14-0.17 fm^{-3}
* Binding energy per nucleon at saturation ranges from -13.9 MeV (V18) to -16.3 MeV (FSS2CC), with experimental range ~15-16 MeV
* Incompressibility K0 ranges from 171 MeV (UIX) to 244 MeV (SFHO), with experimental range 220-260 MeV
* Symmetry energy S0 ranges from 27.8 MeV (LS220) to 36.5 MeV (N93), with experimental range 28.5-34.9 MeV
* Slope parameter L ranges from 51 MeV (FSS2GC, APR) to 77 MeV (N93), with experimental range 30-87 MeV
* Maximum neutron star mass ranges from 1.94 Msun (FSS2CC) to 2.50 Msun (BOB), with observational lower limit >2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} Msun
* Tidal deformability for 1.4 Msun neutron star ranges from 262 (FSS2GC) to 681 (DBHF), with GW170817 constraint 70 < lambda_{1.4} < 580
* Radius for 1.4 Msun neutron star ranges from 11.5 km (FSS2GC) to 13.1 km (DBHF), with GW170817 constraint 10.5 < R_{1.4} < 13.3 km
* Correlation coefficient between pressure at 2 rho0 and lambda_{1.4} is 0.982; between pressure and R_{1.4} is 0.971
* Correlation coefficient between incompressibility K at 2 rho0 and lambda_{1.4} is 0.885; between K and R_{1.4} is 0.846
* Correlation coefficients between saturation properties and lambda_{1.4}: r(S0, lambda_{1.4}) = 0.128, r(K0, lambda_{1.4}) = 0.300, r(L, lambda_{1.4}) = 0.808

## Context

This work extends the microscopic BHF calculations of Burgio et al. (various years) and Li et al. (2008) by systematically comparing multiple BHF-based EOSs against the full set of modern constraints from heavy-ion collisions and the GW170817 event. The strong correlation between neutron star radius and pressure at twice saturation density was originally found by Lattimer and Prakash (2000), and the paper confirms this for the BHF family of EOSs. The absence of correlation with saturation properties was also noted in RMF-based studies by PhysRevC.87.015806, Malik et al. (2018), and Tsang et al. (2019). The results enable future work to use neutron star radius and tidal deformability measurements (e.g., from NICER and eXTP missions) to directly constrain the high-density pressure of beta-stable matter, rather than relying on extrapolations from saturation properties.
