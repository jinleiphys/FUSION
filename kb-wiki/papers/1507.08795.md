# Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in finite volumes

> We discuss the 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in a finite volume. The main objective is to check the volume scaling of thermodynamic observables for various temperatures and chemical potentials. We observe the possible violation of the scaling with system size in a considerable window along the whole transition region in the $T-μ_q$ plane.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the volume scaling of thermodynamic observables in the 2+1 flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model is violated in a finite window of temperature and chemical potential along the transition region, but only for system sizes with lateral dimension R less than approximately 5 fm.

## Method

The authors study the 2+1 flavor PNJL model with up to six-quark interactions in a finite cubic volume V = R^3, implementing finite-size effects by introducing a lower momentum cutoff p_min = π/R. They compute the thermodynamic potential as a function of temperature and chemical potentials, then extract second and fourth order susceptibilities for quark number, electric charge, and strangeness via Taylor expansion around zero chemical potential, and also compute correlations and net quark number density at finite chemical potential using numerical differentiation.

## Key numbers

- Cross-over temperatures at zero chemical potential: T_c = 181 MeV for infinite volume, 178 MeV for R = 4 fm, 174 MeV for R = 3 fm, and 160 MeV for R = 2 fm.
- Finite volume effects are significant for second order susceptibilities in the range 0.8 T_c < T < 2 T_c and for fourth order susceptibilities in the range 0.8 T_c < T < 1.2 T_c.
- Finite volume effects become important when the system freezes out with a size less than R ~ 5 fm.
- The freeze-out volume in heavy-ion collisions is estimated to range from 2000 to 3000 fm^3 from HBT radii measurements, while homogeneity volumes from UrQMD simulations range from 50 to 250 fm^3.
- Model parameters include: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 134.76 MeV, Λ = 631 MeV, g_s Λ^2 = 3.67, g_D Λ^5 = 9.33, T_0 = 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of finite volume effects in the 2-flavor PNJL model (Bhattacharyya et al., 2014) and the hadron resonance gas model (Samanta et al., 2014) to the more realistic 2+1 flavor case. It also connects to lattice QCD calculations of susceptibilities (Gottlieb et al., HOTQCD Collaboration, Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration) and to the finite-size scaling arguments for locating the critical end point proposed by Fraga et al. (2011). The results enable experimental programs at CERN and BNL to use fluctuation measurements as a probe of system size in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that freeze-out volume should be treated as a fitting parameter alongside temperature and chemical potentials.
