# How large is "large $N_c$" for Nuclear matter?

> We argue that a so far neglected dimensionless scale, the number of neighbors in a closely packed system, is relevant for the convergence of the large $N_c$ expansion at high chemical potential. It is only when the number of colors is large w.r.t. this new scale ($\sim \order{10}$) that a convergent large $N_c$ limit is reached. This provides an explanation as to why the large $N_c$ expansion, qualitatively successful in in vacuum QCD, fails to describe high baryo-chemical potential systems, such as nuclear matter. It also means that phenomenological claims about high density matter based on large $N_c$ extrapolations should be treated with caution.

## Key claim

The convergence of the large $N_c$ expansion for nuclear matter requires $N_c$ to be large not just compared to 1, but compared to the number of neighbors in a closely packed system (approximately 10 in three dimensions); when $N_c$ is smaller than this scale, the expansion fails to describe high baryo-chemical potential systems, explaining why large $N_c$ arguments work in vacuum but not for dense nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors introduce a dimensionless scale $N_N$, the number of neighbors a nucleon has in tightly packed nuclear material (the "kissing number" $k(d)$, which is 12 for $d=3$), and argue that Pauli blocking of valence quarks from neighboring nucleons disturbs the configuration-space wavefunction when $N_c < N_N$. They implement this conjecture in a Van der Waals gas model for nuclear matter, where the excluded volume parameter $\alpha$ scales as $\alpha \sim \order{k(d)/N_c} + 1$, and examine how the phase diagram changes as $N_c$ is varied relative to $N_N$.

## Key numbers

- The kissing number $k(d)$ for $d=3$ is 12; for $d=1$ it is 2, for $d=2$ it is 6.
- The confinement scale $\Lambda_{QCD}$ is approximately 200-300 MeV, independent of $N_c$.
- The critical temperature $T_c$ for the liquid-gas transition is $\ll \Lambda_{QCD}$ when $N_c \ll N_N$, but becomes $\sim \order{\Lambda_{QCD}}$ when $N_c \gg N_N$ (provided $\beta,\gamma \sim N_c^0$ or $\sim \ln N_c$).
- The baryon mass scales as $\sim N_c \Lambda_{QCD}$.

## Context

This work extends the large $N_c$ analysis of 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979) to dense nuclear matter, addressing the failure of the large $N_c$ expansion to reproduce the realistic binding energy of nuclear matter found in skyrmion crystal models (e.g., crystal1, crystal2). It directly challenges the "quarkyonic matter" hypothesis of McLerran and Pisarski (2007) by arguing that the quarkyonic phase, where entropy density scales as $N_c$, is only realized when $N_c \gg 10$, not in our $N_c=3$ world. The paper enables a systematic criterion for when large $N_c$ extrapolations are reliable in dense QCD, and motivates further work (e.g., Lottini, 2011) to test the conjecture in more rigorous frameworks.
