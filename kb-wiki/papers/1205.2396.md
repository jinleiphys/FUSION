# Model and parameter dependence of heavy quark energy loss in a hot and dense medium

> Within the framework of the Langevin equation, we study the energy loss of heavy quark due to quasi-elastic multiple scatterings in a quark-gluon plasma created by relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We investigate how the initial configuration of the quark-gluon plasma as well as its properties affect the final state spectra and elliptic flow of D meson and non-photonic electron. We find that both the geometric anisotropy of the initial quark-gluon plasma and the flow profiles of the hydrodynamic medium play important roles in the heavy quark energy loss process and the development of elliptic flow. The relative contribution from charm and bottom quarks is found to affect the transverse momentum dependence of the quenching and flow patterns of heavy flavor decay electron; such influence depends on the interaction strength between heavy quark and the medium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the geometric anisotropy of the initial quark-gluon plasma and the collective flow profiles of the hydrodynamic medium both play important roles in heavy quark energy loss and elliptic flow, but the relative contribution of these two factors depends on the diffusion coefficient (coupling strength) between heavy quarks and the medium.

## Method

The authors simulate heavy quark propagation through a (3+1)-D relativistic ideal hydrodynamic medium using the Langevin equation with quasi-elastic multiple scatterings, considering only collisional energy loss. They compare two initial condition models (Glauber and KLN-CGC) for the hydrodynamic evolution, vary the diffusion coefficient D between 1.5/(2πT) and 6/(2πT), and treat the charm-to-bottom quark ratio as a free parameter. Heavy quark fragmentation into D mesons and subsequent decay into electrons are simulated using Pythia 6.4 with the Lund symmetric fragmentation function modified by the Bowler space-time picture.

## Key numbers

* Diffusion coefficient values studied: D = 1.5/(2πT), D = 3/(2πT), and D = 6/(2πT) for charm quarks in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 6.5 fm
* Initial charm quark transverse momentum distribution parameters: α = 3.9, Λ = 2.1; bottom quark parameters: α = 4.9, Λ = 7.5
* The difference in charm quark elliptic flow v2 between KLN-CGC and Glauber initial conditions can be as large as 20% for D = 6/(2πT) and 40% for D = 1.5/(2πT)
* A 0.7% difference in the charm-to-bottom mixing ratio leads to approximately 25% variation in non-photonic electron v2 for D = 6/(2πT) and over 30% for D = 1.5/(2πT)
* The bottom quark contribution to electron spectra may start dominating over charm at transverse momentum as low as 3 GeV or as high as 9 GeV, based on Armesto (2005)

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based heavy quark energy loss framework from Cao (2011) by systematically investigating how medium geometry, collective flow, and charm-to-bottom ratio affect observables. It builds on prior studies by Gossiaux (2011) showing that different expansion scenarios cause factor-of-two variations in suppression and flow, and by He (2011) on medium flow effects on D-meson spectra. The results enable future high-precision measurements of non-photonic electron v2 to distinguish between strong and weak coupling scenarios and help determine the heavy quark diffusion coefficient, which varies from D < 2/(2πT) from lattice QCD calculations by Ding (2011) to D > 7/(2πT) from T-matrix methods by He (2011).
