# Twist-4 contributions to the azimuthal asymmetry in SIDIS

> We calculate the differential cross section for the unpolarized semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scattering (SIDIS) process $e^-+N \to e^-+q+X$ in leading order (LO) of perturbative QCD and up to twist-4 in power corrections and study in particular the azimuthal asymmetry $<cos2ϕ>$. The final results are expressed in terms of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton matrix elements of the target nucleon up to twist-4. %Under the maximal two-gluon correlation approximation, these TMD parton matrix elements in a nucleus %can be expressed terms of a Gaussian convolution of that in a nucleon with the width given by the jet transport %parameter inside cold nuclei. We also apply it to $e^-+A \to e^-+q+X$ and illustrate numerically the nuclear dependence of the azimuthal asymmetry $<cos2ϕ>$ by using a Gaussian ansatz for the TMD parton matrix elements.

## Key claim

The azimuthal asymmetry ⟨cos2φ⟩ in unpolarized semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scattering (SIDIS) arises from twist-4 power corrections and is expressed in terms of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton correlation functions of the target nucleon; the result reduces to the naive parton model result of Cahn (1978) only when the strong coupling constant g is set to zero, meaning that multiple soft gluon interactions are neglected.

## Method

The authors calculate the hadronic tensor for the process e⁻ + N → e⁻ + q + X up to twist-4 using the generalized collinear expansion technique, which resums multiple soft gluon interactions to ensure gauge invariance of the TMD parton matrix elements. The differential cross section is obtained by contracting the hadronic tensor with the leptonic tensor, and the azimuthal asymmetry ⟨cos2φ⟩ is extracted from the cross section at fixed transverse momentum k⊥. For nuclear targets, the TMD parton matrix elements in a nucleus are approximated by a Gaussian convolution of the corresponding nucleon matrix elements with a broadening width Δ₂F determined by the jet transport parameter in cold nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The azimuthal asymmetry ⟨cos2φ⟩ at fixed k⊥ is given by ⟨cos2φ⟩_{eN} = −[2(1−y)/(1+(1−y)²)] × (|k⊥|²/Q²) × {x_B[φ_{⊥2}^{(1)N}(x_B,k⊥) − φ̃_{⊥2}^{(1)N}(x_B,k⊥)] / f_q^N(x_B,k⊥)}, where y = p·q/p·l and Q² = −q².
- In the free parton limit (g=0), the asymmetry reduces to ⟨cos2φ⟩_{eN}|_{g=0} = −[2(1−y)/(1+(1−y)²)] × (|k⊥|²/Q²), reproducing the result of Cahn (1978).
- The nuclear broadening width is Δ₂F = ∫ dξ⁻_N q̂_F(ξ_N) = (2π²α_s/N_c) ∫ dξ⁻_N ρ_N^A(ξ_N) [x f_g^N(x)]_{x=0}, where ρ_N^A is the spatial nucleon number density and f_g^N is the gluon distribution in a nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the collinear expansion method previously applied to twist-3 contributions in SIDIS by Liang et al. (2006) to the twist-4 level, enabling the study of the ⟨cos2φ⟩ asymmetry. The results provide a framework to extract new TMD quark correlation functions from experimental measurements, and the nuclear dependence calculation connects to the transverse momentum broadening and jet quenching parameter in cold nuclear matter studied by Gao et al. (2010) and Liang et al. (2008). Measurements of ⟨cos2φ⟩ and its deviation from the Cahn (1978) result can probe multiple parton scattering and the gluon saturation scale in nuclei.
