# Recombination of Shower Partons at High $p_T$ in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> A formalism for hadron production at high \pt in heavy-ion collisions has been developed such that all partons hadronize by recombination. The fragmentation of a hard parton is accounted for by the recombination of shower partons that it creates. Such shower partons can also recombine with the thermal partons to form particles that dominate over all other possible modes of hadronization in the $3<p_T<8$ GeV range. The results for the high \pt spectra of pion, kaon, and proton agree well with experiments. Energy loss of partons in the dense medium is taken into account on the average by an effective parameter by fitting data, and is found to be universal independent of the type of particles produced, as it should. Due to the recombination of thermal and shower partons, the structure of jets produced in nuclear collisions is different from that in $pp$ collisions. The consequence on same-side correlations is discussed.

## Key claim

Hadron production at high $p_T$ in heavy-ion collisions can be described entirely by recombination of partons, with the fragmentation of hard partons accounted for by recombination of shower partons they create; the thermal-shower (TS) recombination component dominates over all other hadronization modes in the $3 < p_T < 8$ GeV range, but this dominance depends on the broadness of the recombination function and would be suppressed for mesons with more sharply peaked valon distributions.

## Method

The authors formulate hadron production in 1D momentum space using the recombination model, where the invariant inclusive distribution for a meson is given by an integral over the joint distribution of quark and antiquark and the recombination function. The joint distribution is decomposed into four components: thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), shower-shower from one hard parton (SS1), and shower-shower from two overlapping jets (SS2). Shower parton distributions (SPDs) are determined from known fragmentation functions in the recombination framework, and an effective parameter $\xi$ describes the average fraction of hard partons that escape the dense medium.

## Key numbers

- Thermal parameters: $C = 23.2$ GeV$^{-1}$, $T = 0.317$ GeV, determined by fitting low-$p_T$ pion data
- Effective suppression factor: $\xi = 0.07$, determined by fitting $\pi^0$ data from PHENIX on central Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- Thermal-shower recombination dominates over shower-shower (1-jet) recombination for $p_T < 8$ GeV; conventional jet fragmentation becomes more important only for $p_T > 9$ GeV
- Minimum hard parton momentum cutoff: $k_0 = 3$ GeV; upper integration limit at 20 GeV
- For kaon production, the recombination function uses parameters $a=1$ and $b=2$ from the valon model

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2003) by applying shower parton distributions (SPDs) derived from fragmentation functions to heavy-ion collisions, creating a unified framework where all hadrons are produced by recombination at any $p_T$. It differs from the conventional fragmentation approach and from other recombination models by Fries et al. (2003) and Greco et al. (2003) in the treatment of hadronization in the $4 < p_T < 8$ GeV range. The finding that thermal-shower recombination dominates in this intermediate range enables predictions for same-side correlations in heavy-ion collisions that differ from those in $pp$ collisions, and provides a framework for understanding the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and jet quenching phenomena.
