# Nucleon-antinucleon Interaction from the Modified Skyrme Model

> We calculate the static nucleon-antinucleon interaction potential from the modified Skyrme model with additional $B^μB_μ$ term using the product ansatz. The static properties of single baryon are improved in the modified Skyrme model. State mixing is taken into account by perturbation theory, which substantially increases the strength of the central attraction. We obtain a long and mid range potential which is in qualitative agreement with some phenomenological potentials.

## Key claim
The modified Skyrme model (Mod-SKM) with an additional $B^\mu B_\mu$ term yields a static nucleon-antinucleon ($N\overline{N}$) interaction potential that is in qualitative agreement with phenomenological potentials (Bryan-Phillips and Nijmegen), but only after including state mixing via perturbation theory, which substantially increases the central attraction; the agreement is best at long and mid range, while the product ansatz is not valid at short distance.

## Method
The authors use the product ansatz to describe the Skyrmion-antiSkyrmion ($S\overline{S}$) system, with the $S\overline{S}$ interaction energy computed numerically from the Mod-SKM Lagrangian. They map the $S\overline{S}$ interaction to the $N\overline{N}$ interaction using the algebraic method of Amado et al. (1984), projecting onto nucleon degrees of freedom. State mixing effects from intermediate $N\overline{\Delta}$, $\Delta\overline{N}$, and $\Delta\overline{\Delta}$ states are included via second-order perturbation theory, with the $N-\Delta$ energy difference taken as about 300 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The Mod-SKM parameters are chosen as $e = 19.48$, $F_\pi = 129.11\ \text{MeV}$, and $m = 420\ \text{MeV}$; this yields $g_{\pi NN} \approx 13.5$.
- The nucleon mass $M_N = 938.9\ \text{MeV}$ and $\Delta$ mass $M_\Delta = 1232\ \text{MeV}$ are used as inputs.
- The isoscalar mean square electric radius $\langle r^2 \rangle_{E,I=0}^{1/2} = 0.71\ \text{fm}$ (experiment: $0.72\ \text{fm}$); the isovector radius $\langle r^2 \rangle_{E,I=1}^{1/2} = 1.04\ \text{fm}$ (experiment: $0.88\ \text{fm}$).
- The proton magnetic moment $\mu_p = 2.01$ (experiment: $2.79$); the neutron magnetic moment $\mu_n = -1.20$ (experiment: $-1.91$).
- The axial coupling constant $g_A = 0.82$ (experiment: $1.24$).
- The $N-\Delta$ energy difference $\delta$ is about $300\ \text{MeV}$.
- The tensor potential difference between theoretical and phenomenological results is of order $10\ \text{MeV}$, compared to the static soliton mass or nucleon mass of about $1\ \text{GeV}$.

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme model approach to $N\overline{N}$ interactions previously applied with the minimal Skyrme model (Min-SKM) by Amado et al. (1984, 1985) and by Vinh Mau et al. (1985). The Mod-SKM, which adds a $B^\mu B_\mu$ term simulating $\omega$ meson exchange, improves the static properties of single baryons over the conventional Skyrme model of Adkins et al. (1983). The results are compared to the phenomenological $N\overline{N}$ potentials of Bryan-Phillips (1970) and the Nijmegen group (1977). The work enables future studies of $N\overline{N}$ scattering cross sections, polarization, and possible baryonium bound states, though non-adiabatic effects and inclusion of vector mesons remain open challenges.
