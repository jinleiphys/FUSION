# Complex Plasmas as a Model for the Quark-Gluon-Plasma Liquid

> The quark-gluon plasma, possibly created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, is a strongly interacting many-body parton system. By comparison with strongly coupled electromagnetic plasmas (classical and non-relativistic) it is concluded that the quark-gluon plasma could be in the liquid phase. As an example for a strongly coupled plasma, complex plasmas, which show liquid and even solid phases, are discussed briefly. Furthermore, methods based on correlation functions for confirming and investigating the quark-gluon-plasma liquid are presented. Finally, consequences of the strong coupling, in particular a cross section enhancement in accordance with experimental observations at RHIC, are discussed.

# Complex Plasmas as a Model for the Quark-Gluon-Plasma Liquid

## Key claim
The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is likely a strongly coupled plasma in the liquid phase rather than a gas, based on an estimated Coulomb coupling parameter of Γ = 1.5–6 at temperatures around 200 MeV, which places it in the strongly coupled regime where Γ ≥ O(1).

## Method
The author estimates the QCD coupling parameter Γ using the Casimir invariants for quarks (C=4/3) and gluons (C=3), assuming a temperature of T=200 MeV, a coupling constant α_s = 0.3–0.5, and an interparton distance of about 0.5 fm. The static structure function and pair correlation function are defined for the QGP and computed within the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximation as a reference for lattice QCD simulations. The author draws analogies to complex (dusty) plasmas, which are classical, non-relativistic Yukawa systems that exhibit liquid and solid phases and can be studied experimentally via laser scattering and CCD imaging.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb coupling parameter for the QGP: Γ = 1.5–6, assuming T = 200 MeV, α_s = 0.3–0.5, and interparton distance d ≈ 0.5 fm.
- In complex plasmas, microparticles carry negative charges between 10^3 and 10^5 e, with electron temperatures T_e = 1–10 eV and interparticle distances of about 200 μm, yielding Γ up to 10^5.
- In complex plasma experiments, reducing pressure from about 60 Pa (crystal phase) to 30 Pa produces liquid behavior; at 10 Pa, particle velocities increase by a factor of about 200 compared to the solid phase (where thermal lattice oscillations correspond to velocities of about 0.2 mm/s).
- The ratio r_c/λ_D in the QGP at T ≈ 200 MeV is 1–5, leading to a parton cross section enhancement by a factor of 2–9 compared to perturbative results.

## Context
This work extends the analogy between strongly coupled electromagnetic plasmas and the QGP, building on prior studies of Coulomb crystals (Ref. 3) and Yukawa system phase diagrams (Ref. 4). The author references experimental observations at RHIC (elliptic flow, particle spectra) that are described well by hydrodynamics with negligible viscosity, indicating fast thermalization and liquid-like behavior (Refs. 11a, 12–15). The paper proposes using the static structure function, a standard tool in liquid physics (Ref. 18), as a diagnostic for the QGP liquid phase, and suggests that lattice QCD simulations could confirm the liquid behavior. The cross section enhancement mechanism is connected to the ion drag force enhancement observed in complex plasmas (Ref. 20) and to the order-of-magnitude cross section enhancement postulated from RHIC elliptic flow data (Ref. 20a).
