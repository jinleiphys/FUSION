# Most Strange Dibaryon from Lattice QCD

> The $ΩΩ$ system in the $^1S_0$ channel (the most strange dibaryon) is studied on the basis of the (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with a large volume (8.1 fm)$^3$ and nearly physical pion mass $m_π\simeq 146$ MeV at a lattice spacing $a\simeq 0.0846$ fm. We show that lattice QCD data analysis by the HAL QCD method leads to the scattering length $a_0 = 4.6 (6)(^{+1.2}_{-0.5}) {\rm fm}$, the effective range $r_{\rm eff} = 1.27 (3)(^{+0.06}_{-0.03}) {\rm fm}$ and the binding energy $B_{ΩΩ} = 1.6 (6) (^{+0.7}_{-0.6}) {\rm MeV}$. These results indicate that the $ΩΩ$ system has an overall attraction and is located near the unitary regime. Such a system can be best searched experimentally by the pair-momentum correlation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The $\Omega\Omega$ system in the $^1S_0$ channel is found to have overall attraction and is located near the unitary regime, with a binding energy of about 1.6 MeV, but this result depends on the HAL QCD method and the specific lattice setup with a pion mass of 146 MeV and a lattice spacing of 0.0846 fm, and the binding energy is reduced to about 0.7 MeV when Coulomb repulsion is included.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations on a 96^4 lattice with volume (8.1 fm)^3, using the Iwasaki gauge action and nonperturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quark action. They employed the time-dependent HAL QCD method to extract the leading-order central potential V(r) from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function, then fitted V(r) with three Gaussians to obtain scattering phase shifts via the Schrödinger equation, and finally extracted the scattering length, effective range, and binding energy from the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length: a_0 = 4.6 (6)(^{+1.2}_{-0.5}) fm for the ΩΩ system in the ^1S_0 channel at t/a=17
- Effective range: r_eff = 1.27 (3)(^{+0.06}_{-0.03}) fm for the ΩΩ system in the ^1S_0 channel at t/a=17
- Binding energy (QCD only): B_{ΩΩ} = 1.6 (6)(^{+0.7}_{-0.6}) MeV for the ΩΩ system at t/a=17
- Binding energy (QCD + Coulomb): B_{ΩΩ}^{(QCD+Coulomb)} = 0.7 (5)(5) MeV
- Ω baryon mass from lattice: m_Ω = 1712 ± 1 MeV (about 2% higher than the physical value of 1672 MeV)
- Pion mass: m_π ≃ 146 MeV; kaon mass: m_K ≃ 525 MeV; nucleon mass: m_N ≃ 964 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a ≃ 0.0846 fm (a^{-1} ≃ 2.333 GeV)
- Lattice volume: (8.1 fm)^3
- Using the physical Ω mass (1672 MeV) instead gives: a_0 = 4.9(8) fm, r_eff = 1.29(3) fm, B_{ΩΩ}^{(QCD)} = 1.3(5) MeV, B_{ΩΩ}^{(QCD+Coulomb)} = 0.5(5) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the ΩΩ system that gave conflicting results: a finite volume method study with m_π ≃ 390 MeV showed weak repulsion (Buchoff 2012), while a HAL QCD study with m_π ≃ 700 MeV showed moderate attraction (Yamada 2015). The present calculation uses nearly physical quark masses and a large volume, resolving the controversy in favor of attraction. The results enable experimental searches for the ΩΩ dibaryon via pair-momentum correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as proposed by Cho (2017) and Morita (2016).
