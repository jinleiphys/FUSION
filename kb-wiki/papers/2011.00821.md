# Low-energy dipole excitation mode in $^{18}$O with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> Low-energy dipole (LED) excitations in $^{18}$O were investigated using a combination of the variation after $K$-projection in the framework of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with $β$-constraint with the generator coordinate method. We obtained two LED states, namely, the $1_1^-$ state with a dominant shell-model structure and the $1_2^-$ state with a large $^{14}\textrm{C}+α$ cluster component. Both these states had significant toroidal dipole (TD) and compressive dipole (CD) strengths, indicating that the TD and CD modes are not separated but mixed in the LED excitations of $^{18}$O. This is unlike the CD and TD modes for well-deformed nuclei such as $^{10}$Be, where the CD and TD modes are generated as $K^π=0^-$ and $K^π=1^-$ excitations, respectively, in a largely deformed state.

## Key claim

The low-energy dipole (LED) excitations in \(^{18}\)O show mixing of toroidal dipole (TD) and compressive dipole (CD) modes in the \(1_1^-\) and \(1_2^-\) states, unlike the clear separation of these modes by \(K\)-quantum number previously found in the well-deformed nucleus \(^{10}\)Be.

## Method

The authors applied antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after \(K\)-projection (K-VAP) under a \(\beta\)-constraint, combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to obtain the wave functions of \(^{18}\)O. They performed \(K\)-projected energy variation for \(K^\pi=0^+\), \(K^\pi=0^-\), and \(K^\pi=1^-\) bases at various \(\beta\) values, then superposed these bases with \(K\)-mixing and angular-momentum projection to describe the ground and excited states. Dipole transition strengths were calculated using the \(E1\), toroidal dipole (TD), and compressive dipole (CD) operators.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the ground state: 128.8 MeV (calculated), compared to the experimental value of 139.8 MeV.
- The \(0_2^+\) state (lowest \(\Cf+\alpha\) cluster band head) appears at 7.40 MeV excitation energy.
- The \(1_1^-\) state has a dominant shell-model component; the \(1_2^-\) state appears at 8.68 MeV with a large \(\Cf+\alpha\) cluster component.
- Calculated \(B(E2; 2_3^+ \to 0_2^+)\) = 78.6 \(e^2\)fm\(^4\) for the \(\Cf+\alpha\) cluster band, in good agreement with the experimental value of \(70\pm42\) \(e^2\)fm\(^4\).
- The \(0_1^+\) state has 93% overlap with the \(K^\pi=0^+\) base at \(\beta=0.28\).
- The experimental \(B(E1:0_1^+\to1_1^-) < 1.5\times10^{-6}\) W.u. is overestimated by the calculation by a few orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the AMD with K-VAP method previously applied to \(^{10}\)Be and \(^{16}\)O by Shikata and Kanada-En'yo (2020) to the neutron-rich nucleus \(^{18}\)O. It addresses the role of cluster structures in LED excitations, which had not been discussed in association with LED modes in prior mean-field studies by Sagawa (2001), Vretenar (2001), and Paar (2002). The results show that, unlike the clear separation of TD and CD modes by \(K\)-quantum number in the well-deformed \(^{10}\)Be, the two modes are mixed in \(^{18}\)O due to configuration mixing along \(\beta\) and \(K\)-mixing, similar to the case of \(^{16}\)O. This enables future studies of LED mode mixing in other neutron-rich oxygen isotopes.
