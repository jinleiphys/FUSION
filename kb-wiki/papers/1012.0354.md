# Bulk viscosity in the nonlinear and anharmonic regime of strange quark matter

> The bulk viscosity of cold, dense three-flavor quark matter is studied as a function of temperature and the amplitude of density oscillations. The study is also extended to the case of two different types of anharmonic oscillations of density. We point several qualitative effects due to the anharmonicity, although quantitatively they appear to be relatively small. We also find that, in most regions of the parameter space, with the exception of the case of a very large amplitude of density oscillations (i.e. 10% and above), nonlinear effects and anharmonicity have a small effect on the interplay of the nonleptonic and semileptonic processes in the bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of cold, dense three-flavor quark matter is studied as a function of temperature and the amplitude of density oscillations, including two types of anharmonic oscillations. The authors find that nonlinear effects and anharmonicity have a small effect on the interplay of nonleptonic and semileptonic processes in most regions of parameter space, except for very large amplitude density oscillations (10% and above).

## Method

The bulk viscosity is calculated by comparing the hydrodynamic relation for energy dissipation with the thermodynamic relation for mechanical work, averaged over one oscillation period. The instantaneous composition of quark matter is determined by solving nonlinear differential equations for the electron and strangeness fractions, which include both nonleptonic and semileptonic weak processes with higher-order corrections in powers of δμᵢ/T. Two types of anharmonic density oscillations are modeled using classical anharmonic oscillators with cubic (Type I) and quartic (Type II) potentials, with solutions given in terms of Jacobi elliptic functions.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity saturates in the linear regime at small values of δn₀/n₀ (amplitude of density oscillations relative to equilibrium density)
- For temperature T ∼ 1 MeV, a "semileptonic hump" in bulk viscosity appears from the interplay of semileptonic and nonleptonic processes
- Nonlinear effects become substantial only when δn₀/n₀ is 10% and above
- Calculations use model parameters at n = 5ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≃ 0.15 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density
- Results shown for two representative oscillation periods: τ = 0.1 s and τ = 10⁻³ s
- For Type I anharmonicity, periodic solutions exist for −1 < α* < 1/2, where α* ≡ α δn₀
- For Type II anharmonicity, periodic solutions exist for β* > −1, where β* ≡ β (δn₀)²
- The period of Type I oscillations is τ_α ≃ (2π/ω₀)(1 + (5/12)(α x₀)² + (5/18)(α x₀)³ + ...) for small α x₀
- The period of Type II oscillations is τ_β ≃ (2π/ω₀)(1 − (3/8)β x₀² + (57/256)β² x₀⁴ + ...) for small β x₀²

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of bulk viscosity in strange quark matter by Madsen (1992), Alford (2010), and others, which considered only nonleptonic processes in the harmonic regime. The authors incorporate semileptonic processes and nonlinear corrections to the weak rates, building on the formalism of Sa'd (2007) and Wang (2010). The study makes a first attempt at exploring how anharmonicity of density oscillations (due to nonlinear mode coupling) affects bulk viscosity, which is relevant for understanding r-mode damping in compact stars. Future work could extend this analysis to color superconducting quark matter and more realistic density oscillations from stellar simulations.
