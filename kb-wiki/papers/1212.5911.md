# Interplay between the symmetry energy and the strangeness content of neutron stars

> The effect of the density dependence of the nucleonic equation of state and the hyperon meson couplings on the star properties, including strangeness content, mass and radius, are studied within a relativistic mean field formalism. It is shown that there is still lacking information on the nucleonic equation of state at supra-saturation densities and on the hyperon interactions in nuclear matter that will allow a clear answer to the question whether the mass of the pulsar J1614-2230 could rule out exotic degrees of freedom from the interior of compact stars. We show that some star properties are affected in a similar way by the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the hyperon content of the star. To disentangle these two effects it is essential to have a good knowledge of the equation of state at supra-saturation densities. A linear correlation between the radius and the strangeness content of a star with a fixed mass is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the hyperon content of neutron stars affect some star properties in a similar way, making it difficult to disentangle these two effects without a good knowledge of the equation of state at supra-saturation densities; the authors do not claim that the mass of pulsar J1614-2230 definitively rules out exotic degrees of freedom, as there is still lacking information on both the nucleonic equation of state and hyperon interactions.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism using the TM1 parametrization and its variations, including a modified version (TM1-2) with a smaller quartic omega term and models with a non-linear omega-rho term to vary the symmetry energy slope L from 55 to 110 MeV. Hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) were included with coupling constants determined by SU(6) symmetry and adjusted to hyperon potential depths in symmetric nuclear matter, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain neutron star profiles.

## Key numbers

- The TM1 nucleonic EOS without hyperons gives a maximum star mass of 2.18 solar masses for L=110 MeV and 2.13 solar masses for L=55 MeV.
- For hyperonic stars with U^N_Xi = -18 MeV and without strange mesons, the maximum mass is 1.68 solar masses (L=110 MeV) and 1.69 solar masses (L=55 MeV).
- For hyperonic stars with U^N_Xi = 18 MeV and without strange mesons, the maximum mass is 1.75 solar masses (L=110 MeV) and 1.76 solar masses (L=55 MeV).
- The radius of a 1.67 solar mass star decreases by approximately 1 km when L decreases from 110 MeV to 55 MeV or when the total strangeness fraction increases from zero to 10 percent.
- The linear correlation between radius and strangeness content for a 1.67 solar mass star has a slope of -11.27 km (plus or minus 4 percent) for L=110 MeV and -10.62 km (plus or minus 1 percent) for L=55 MeV.
- The maximum strangeness fraction for stars with strange mesons included is of order 0.04-0.05 for L=55 MeV and can reach 0.07-0.08 for L=110 MeV.
- The onset of hyperons occurs only in stars with gravitational masses above 1.5 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends studies of hyperonic neutron stars within relativistic mean-field models, following the approach of Schaffner et al. (2012) who showed that including the strange phi meson is essential for a stiff enough equation of state to describe a 2 solar mass star. The paper builds on earlier work by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001, 2003) on the effect of the symmetry energy slope on star properties, and by Providencia et al. (2012) on the correlation between radius and strangeness content. The results enable future studies to disentangle the effects of symmetry energy and hyperon content by analyzing stars in different mass ranges, since hyperons only appear in stars more massive than about 1.5 solar masses.
