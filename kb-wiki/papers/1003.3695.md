# Transition density and pressure in hot neutron stars

> Using the momentum-dependent MDI effective interaction for nucleons, we have studied the transition density and pressure at the boundary between the inner crust and liquid core of hot neutron stars. We find that their values are larger in neutrino-trapped neutron stars than in neutrino-free neutron stars. Furthermore, both are found to decrease with increasing temperature of a neutron star as well as increasing slope parameter of the nuclear symmetry energy, except that the transition pressure in neutrino-trapped neutron stars for the case of small symmetry energy slope parameter first increases and then decreases with increasing temperature. We have also studied the effect of the nuclear symmetry energy on the critical temperature above which the inner crust in a hot neutron star disappears and found that with increasing value of the symmetry energy slope parameter, the critical temperature decreases slightly in neutrino-trapped neutron stars but first decreases and then increases in neutrino-free neutron stars.

## Key claim

Using the momentum-dependent MDI interaction, the transition density and pressure at the inner crust-core boundary of hot neutron stars are larger in neutrino-trapped matter than in neutrino-free matter, and both generally decrease with increasing temperature and with increasing slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy, except that the transition pressure in neutrino-trapped matter with small L first increases then decreases with temperature.

## Method

The authors used the momentum-dependent MDI effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in a mean-field approximation to model hot neutron star matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, considering both neutrino-trapped (lepton fraction Y_l = 0.4) and neutrino-free (zero neutrino chemical potential) conditions at temperatures up to 10 MeV. The inner edge of the crust was located using the dynamical approach, which evaluates the stability of homogeneous matter against small periodic density perturbations by including bulk nuclear, density-gradient, and Coulomb contributions. The proton fraction and total pressure were computed self-consistently from the nucleon phase-space distribution functions at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Transition density ρ_t for cold neutrino-free matter with the MDI interaction lies in the range 0.040 fm^{-3} < ρ_t < 0.065 fm^{-3}, and transition pressure P_t in the range 0.01 MeV/fm^3 < P_t < 0.26 MeV/fm^3, as previously constrained from cold neutron star studies.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy was varied between about 15 MeV (x=1), 60 MeV (x=0), and 106 MeV (x=-1), corresponding to the range 15 < L < 106 MeV.
- For neutrino-trapped matter at T=5 MeV, the transition density decreases with increasing L; for neutrino-free matter at T=1 MeV, P_t becomes very small and even negative for L larger than about 100 MeV.
- The critical temperature T_c above which the inner crust disappears is about 12 MeV for neutrino-trapped neutron stars and about 1.5 MeV for neutrino-free neutron stars when L is 70-80 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at normal density is fixed to 30.5 MeV; the incompressibility K_0 of symmetric nuclear matter is 212 MeV; the binding energy per nucleon at saturation is -16 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the cold neutron star crust-core transition study of Xu et al. (2009) to finite temperatures relevant for proto-neutron stars, using the same MDI interaction. The results are consistent with similar temperature-dependent studies using Skyrme interactions and relativistic mean field models, as reported by Ducoin et al. (2008) and Parra et al. (2009). The findings enable predictions for the structural evolution and cooling of newly-born neutron stars, and the negative transition pressure obtained for stiff symmetry energies at high temperature can rule out certain density dependences of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities.
