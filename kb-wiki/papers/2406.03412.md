# Nonlocal chiral contributions to generalized parton distributions of the proton at nonzero skewness

> We compute the one-loop contributions to spin-averaged generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the proton from pseudoscalar mesons with intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states at nonzero skewness. Our framework is based on nonlocal covariant chiral effective theory, with ultraviolet divergences regularized by introducing a relativistic regulator derived consistently from the nonlocal Lagrangian. Using the splitting functions calculated from the nonlocal Lagrangian, we find the nonzero skewness GPDs from meson loops by convoluting with the phenomenological pion GPD and the generalized distribution amplitude, and verify that these satisfy the correct polynomiality properties. We also compute the lowest two moments of GPDs to quantify the meson loop effects on the Dirac, Pauli and gravitational form factors of the proton.

## Key claim

The one-loop contributions to spin-averaged generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from pseudoscalar mesons with intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states at nonzero skewness are computed within nonlocal covariant chiral effective theory, and the authors verify that the resulting GPDs satisfy the correct polynomiality properties, a condition the abstract mentions but does not elaborate on as a central consistency check.

## Method

The authors used nonlocal covariant chiral effective theory with a relativistic regulator derived from the nonlocal Lagrangian to compute one-loop splitting functions for nucleon-to-meson-plus-baryon transitions at nonzero skewness. They then obtained the nonzero skewness GPDs by convoluting these splitting functions with a phenomenological pion GPD and a generalized distribution amplitude (GDA), separating contributions into DGLAP and ERBL kinematic regions. The lowest two moments of the GPDs were computed to quantify meson loop effects on the Dirac, Pauli, and gravitational form factors of the proton.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \( f_\pi = 92.4 \) MeV, used as the scale in the chiral Lagrangian.
- Axial vector coupling constant for the octet baryon: \( g_A = 1.26 \), used in the meson-baryon interaction.
- Octet-decuplet transition coupling: \( \mathcal{C} = -\frac65 g_A \), chosen from SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry.
- Coefficients for the nucleon-pion contact interaction: \( b_{10} = 1.24 \) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \( b_{11} = 0.46 \) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \( \Lambda_\chi \sim 1 \) GeV, the scale associated with the chiral effective theory.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of zero-skewness GPDs in the proton within nonlocal chiral effective theory (He, 2022) to the nonzero skewness case. It builds on prior applications of nonlocal chiral effective theory to nucleon electromagnetic and strange form factors (He, 2017; He, 2018), the strange-antistrange PDF asymmetry (Salamu, 2018; Salamu, 2019), and sea quark TMD Sivers functions (He, 2019). The results enable future calculations of complete one-loop GPDs at nonzero skewness and provide chiral constraints that can complement lattice QCD calculations and phenomenological extractions from deeply-virtual Compton scattering data.
