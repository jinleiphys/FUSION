# Return of the EMC Effect

> The relationship between the properties of nuclear matter and structure functions measured in lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering is investigated using light front dynamics. We find that relativistic mean field models such as the Walecka, Zimanyi-Moszkowski (and point-coupling versions of the same) and Rusnak-Furnstahl models contain essentially no binding effect, in accord with an earlier calculation by Birse. These models are found to obey the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, which is applicable if nucleons are the only degrees of freedom. Any model in which the entire Fock space wave function can be represented in terms of free nucleons must obey this theorem, which implies that all of the plus momentum is carried by nucleons, and therefore that there will be essentially no binding effect. The explicit presence of nuclear mesons allows one to obtain a modified form of the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, which is equivalent to the often-used momentum sum rule. These results argue in favor of a conclusion that the depletion of the deep inelastic structure function observed in the valence quark regime is due to some interesting effect involving dynamics beyond the conventional nucleon-meson treatment of nuclear physics.

## Key claim

Relativistic mean field models of nuclear matter (Walecka, Zimanyi-Moszkowski, point-coupling versions, and Rusnak-Furnstahl) contain essentially no binding effect that could explain the EMC effect depletion of the deep inelastic structure function, because the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem forces the nucleon distribution function to peak at y=1, meaning nucleons carry all of the nuclear plus-momentum as seen in deep inelastic scattering.

## Method

The authors used light front dynamics to derive the nucleon distribution function f_N(y) from the manifestly covariant nucleon Green's function in infinite nuclear matter, computed in the mean field approximation for five relativistic models. They then applied the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, which states that the energy of a nucleon at the Fermi surface equals the average nuclear mass per nucleon (E_F = M_A/A), to show that f_N(y) peaks at y=1 independent of the specific model parameters. The nuclear structure function F_2A was computed via the convolution formula F_2A(x_A)/A = ∫ dy f_N(y) F_2N(x_A/y) using a parameterization of the free nucleon structure function.

## Key numbers

- The average binding energy per nucleon for infinite nuclear matter is 16 MeV, which is far too small to explain the observed EMC depletion (the separation energy of ~70 MeV is not the relevant quantity).
- For the Walecka model, the nucleonic contribution to the nuclear plus-momentum is only 65% (P_N^+/P^+ = 0.65), yet the correct distribution function f_N(y) still peaks at y=1, giving no binding effect.
- The Fermi momentum for the Walecka, NVW, ZM, and NVZM models is k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}; for the Rusnak-Furnstahl model it is k_F = 1.31 fm^{-1}.
- The effective mass ratio M*/M varies widely across models: 0.56 (Walecka, NVW), 0.85 (ZM, NVZM), and 0.73 (RF), yet all produce essentially the same ratio R(x) = F_2A(x_A)/[A F_2N(x)].
- The vector potential g_v V^+/M-bar is 0.355 for Walecka and NVW, 0.079 for ZM and NVZM, and 0.194 for RF.

## Context

This work extends an earlier calculation by Birse (1990s) showing that relativistic mean field models do not produce a binding effect for the EMC effect, and it contradicts the conventional lore that nuclear binding (using separation energy ~70 MeV) explains the depletion observed by the European Muon Collaboration. The authors show that any model obeying the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem (which applies when nucleons are the only degrees of freedom contributing to the nuclear energy) cannot produce a significant binding effect, and that the observed EMC effect must arise from dynamics beyond conventional nucleon-meson treatments of nuclear physics. This enables future work to focus on exotic effects such as nucleon modification in the nuclear medium rather than conventional binding explanations.
