# Higgs-Confinement Transitions in QCD from Symmetry Protected Topological Phases

> In gauge theories with fundamental matter there is typically no sharp way to distinguish confining and Higgs regimes, e.g. using generalized global symmetries acting on loop order parameters. It is standard lore that these two regimes are continuously connected, as has been explicitly demonstrated in certain lattice and continuum models. We point out that Higgsing and confinement sometimes lead to distinct symmetry protected topological (SPT) phases -- necessarily separated by a phase transition -- for ordinary global symmetries. We present explicit examples in 3+1 dimensions, obtained by adding elementary Higgs fields and Yukawa couplings to QCD while preserving parity P and time reversal T. In a suitable scheme, the confining phases of these theories are trivial SPTs, while their Higgs phases are characterized by non-trivial P- and T-invariant theta-angles $θ_f, θ_g = π$ for flavor or gravity background gauge fields, i.e. they are topological insulators or superconductors. Finally, we consider conventional three-flavor QCD (without elementary Higgs fields) at finite $U(1)_B$ baryon-number chemical potential $μ_B$, which preserves P and T. At very large $μ_B$, three-flavor QCD is known to be a completely Higgsed color superconductor that also spontaneously breaks $U(1)_B$. We argue that this high-density phase is in fact a gapless SPT, with a gravitational theta-angle $θ_g = π$ that safely co-exists with the $U(1)_B$ Nambu-Goldstone boson. We explain why this SPT motivates unexpected transitions in the QCD phase diagram, as well as anomalous surface modes at the boundary of quark-matter cores inside neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors argue that Higgs and confining phases in certain QCD-like gauge theories with fundamental matter can be distinct symmetry protected topological (SPT) phases for ordinary global symmetries, necessarily separated by a phase transition, contradicting the standard lore that these regimes are continuously connected.

## Method

The authors construct Higgs-Yukawa-QCD theories in 3+1 dimensions by adding elementary Higgs fields and Yukawa couplings to QCD while preserving parity P and time reversal T. They analyze the SPT classification of the confining and Higgs phases by computing the gravitational theta-angle theta_g and flavor theta-angle theta_f that arise from integrating out massive fermions, using positivity of the path integral measure in the confining phase as the trivial reference SPT. They also apply this framework to conventional three-flavor QCD at finite baryon-number chemical potential mu_B, where the high-density color-superconducting phase is argued to be a gapless SPT.

## Key numbers

* The Higgs phases are characterized by non-trivial P- and T-invariant theta-angles theta_f = pi and theta_g = pi for flavor or gravity background gauge fields.
* For a single 2-component Weyl fermion with negative mass m < 0, integrating out the fermion generates a gravitational theta-angle theta_g = pi in the effective action.
* For a single 4-component Dirac fermion with negative mass m < 0 and unit U(1) charge, the background theta-angle for the U(1) flavor symmetry is theta_f = pi, while theta_g = 2pi = 0 modulo 2pi.
* The gravitational theta-angle theta_g = pi gives a partition function phase of (-1)^{sigma/16} on a spin four-manifold with signature sigma in 16Z, e.g. non-trivial on a K3 surface where sigma = 16.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of T-invariant SPTs in QCD by Tachikawa (2016) to theories with elementary Higgs fields and Yukawa couplings, and generalizes the SPT interpretation of Higgs phases recently explored by Verresen (2022) and Thorngren (2023) from Abelian gauge theories with one-form symmetries to non-Abelian SU(N) gauge theories with only zero-form symmetries. The results motivate unexpected transitions in the QCD phase diagram and predict anomalous surface modes at the boundary of quark-matter cores inside neutron stars.
