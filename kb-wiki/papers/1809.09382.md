# A possible probe of the new fifth force

> The proposed protophobic fifth force in \emph{Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf \emph{117}}, 071803 (2016)} recently attracts much attention. To confirm or refute the existence of this new interaction, here I propose a method to probe the protophobic fifth force in dense matter that formed in nucleus-nucleus collisions. As expected, the protophobic fifth force has negligible effects on the usual observables in nucleus-nucleus collisions. While the protophobic fifth force evidently affects the value of final positively and negatively charged pions ratio at very high kinetic energies. The signal thus could be used to probe the protophobic fifth force in dense matter by nucleus-nucleus collisions' experiments at current experimental equipments worldwide.

# A possible probe of the new fifth force

## Key claim
The protophobic fifth force, mediated by a 17 MeV boson, can be probed via the ratio of positively to negatively charged pions at very high kinetic energies in heavy-ion collisions, where the effect on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio can exceed 25%, even though the force has negligible effects on most standard observables.

## Method
The study uses the semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate the dynamical evolution of nucleus-nucleus collisions. A Yukawa-type fifth force with range 12 fm is implemented, with coupling strengths for u and d quarks taken from Feng et al. (2016), and the force is applied to all particles including neutrons, pions, and Δ resonances with charge-dependent strength parameters. Central Au + Au collisions at 0.4 GeV/nucleon incident beam energy are simulated, accumulating 5,000,000 events per case.

## Key numbers
- The fifth force is mediated by a boson with mass 17 MeV and range λ = 12 fm.
- The coupling strength for u quarks is −3.5 × 10⁻³ e, for d quarks is 7 × 10⁻³ e, where e = 0.303 is the electromagnetic coupling constant.
- The strength parameter for neutrons is 1.05 × 10⁻² e; for π⁻ it is 1.05 × 10⁻² e; for π⁺ it is −1.05 × 10⁻² e; for Δ⁻ it is 2.1 × 10⁻² e; for Δ⁺⁺ it is −1.05 × 10⁻² e; protons, π⁰, and Δ⁺ have zero coupling.
- The maximal effect of the fifth force on the free neutron/proton ratio in central Au + Au at 0.4 GeV/nucleon is about 1.5%.
- The maximal effect on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in the same reaction is about 3.5% when considering all particles, but exceeds 25% at very high kinetic energies (deduced from the lower band of π⁻/π⁺ with fifth force = 3.5 and upper band without = 2.8).
- Each event produces about 3 × 10⁻⁵ pions with kinetic energy of about 0.8 GeV in Au + Au at 400 MeV/nucleon.

## Context
This work extends the proposal of a protophobic fifth force by Feng et al. (2016), motivated by the anomalous 8Be decay observed by Krasznahorkay et al. (2016). It provides a nuclear-reaction-based test of this force, complementing particle-physics searches. The method enables experimental tests at existing heavy-ion facilities such as RIBF-RIKEN in Japan, CSR in China, and GSI in Germany, and distinguishes the fifth-force signal from effects of nuclear symmetry energy and pion potentials, which are argued to be negligible at very high kinetic energies.
