# Thermal shifts, fluctuations, and missing states

> Thermal shifts and fluctuations at finite temperature below the deconfinement crossover from hadronic matter to the quark-gluon plasma provide a viable way to search for missing states with given quantum numbers in the hadronic spectrum. We analyze three realizations of the hadron resonance gas model in the light quark (uds) sector: the states from the Particle Data Group tables with or without width and from the Relativized Quark Model. We elaborate on the meaning of hadronic completeness and thermodynamical equivalence on the light of lattice QCD trace anomaly, heavy quark entropy shift and baryon,charge and strangeness susceptibilities.

## Key claim

Thermal shifts and fluctuations at finite temperature below the deconfinement crossover provide a viable way to search for missing states with given quantum numbers in the hadronic spectrum, but the thermodynamic equivalence between different hadron resonance gas realizations (PDG, PDG with width, and Relativized Quark Model) depends on the quantum numbers, meaning no single spectrum universally reproduces lattice QCD results for all observables.

## Method

The authors analyze three realizations of the hadron resonance gas model in the light quark (uds) sector: states from the Particle Data Group tables with or without width smearing and states from the Relativized Quark Model. They compute the trace anomaly, heavy quark entropy shift, and baryon, charge, and strangeness susceptibilities, comparing these to lattice QCD results from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations. The effect of finite resonance width is incorporated via a Gaussian profile smearing of the mass distribution.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass recorded by PDG for light (uds) mesons and baryons is around 2.5 GeV, with the cumulative number of states N(M_max) approximately 2.5 x 10^3.
- The average width-to-mass ratio for PDG resonances is ⟨Γ_R/M_R⟩ = 0.12(8) for both mesons and baryons.
- The deconfinement crossover temperature is T_c ∼ 150 MeV.
- The highest temperature of agreement for the trace anomaly between HRG and lattice QCD is T ≲ 150 MeV.
- The asymptotic high-temperature limits for susceptibilities are: χ_BB → 1/N_c, χ_QQ → Σ q_i^2, and χ_SS → 1.
- The entropy shift for a heavy quark at zero distance is ΔS_Q(0) = log(2 N_f) with N_f = 2, and at infinite distance ΔS_Q(∞) = log(N_c).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hadronic completeness and thermodynamic equivalence previously studied by Broniowski (2000, 2004) and Arriola (2014), who showed remarkable coincidence between PDG and RQM cumulative state counts. The authors build on the Hagedorn hypothesis and earlier work by Majumder (2010) and Broniowski (2016) regarding exponential mass distributions near T_c. The fluctuation analysis follows the approach of Jeon and Koch (1999, 2000) and compares with lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi, 2011, 2013) and HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov, 2012, 2014). The results enable refined HRG models incorporating width effects and volume effects, and suggest that separate analysis by quantum numbers reveals a less obvious pattern for quark-hadron duality than the trace anomaly alone.
