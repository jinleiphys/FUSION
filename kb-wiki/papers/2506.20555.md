# DeepQuark: A Deep-Neural-Network Approach to Multiquark Bound States

> For the first time, we implement the deep-neural-network-based variational Monte Carlo approach for the multiquark bound states, whose complexity surpasses that of electron or nucleon systems due to strong SU(3) color interactions. We design a novel and high-efficiency architecture, DeepQuark, to address the unique challenges in multiquark systems such as stronger correlations, extra discrete quantum numbers, and intractable confinement interaction. Our method demonstrates competitive performance with state-of-the-art approaches, including diffusion Monte Carlo and Gaussian expansion method, in the nucleon, doubly heavy tetraquark, and fully heavy tetraquark systems. Notably, it outperforms existing calculations for pentaquarks, exemplified by the triply heavy pentaquark. For the nucleon, we successfully incorporate three-body flux-tube confinement interactions without additional computational costs. In tetraquark systems, we consistently describe hadronic molecule $T_{cc}$ and compact tetraquark $T_{bb}$ with an unbiased form of wave function ansatz. In the pentaquark sector, we obtain weakly bound $\bar D^*Ξ_{cc}^*$ molecule $P_{cc\bar c}(5715)$ with $S=\frac{5}{2}$ and its bottom partner $P_{bb\bar b}(15569)$. They can be viewed as the analogs of the molecular $T_{cc}$. We recommend experimental search of $P_{cc\bar c}(5715)$ in the D-wave $J/ψΛ_c$ channel. DeepQuark holds great promise for extension to larger multiquark systems, overcoming the computational barriers in conventional methods. It also serves as a powerful framework for exploring confining mechanism beyond two-body interactions in multiquark states, which may offer valuable insights into nonperturbative QCD and general many-body physics.

## Key claim

For the first time, a deep-neural-network-based variational Monte Carlo approach (DeepQuark) is applied to multiquark bound states, achieving competitive performance with diffusion Monte Carlo and Gaussian expansion method for nucleon and tetraquark systems while outperforming existing calculations for pentaquarks; however, the method's success is demonstrated only within a specific nonrelativistic quark model (the AL1 model) and for systems with at most three to four identical fermions where the factorial complexity of explicit antisymmetrization remains manageable.

## Method

The authors employ the nonrelativistic AL1 quark model Hamiltonian with one-gluon-exchange and linear confinement interactions, plus a three-body flux-tube confinement term for the nucleon. They construct a neural-network wave function in coupled color-spin-isospin bases, encoding symmetry information as standard basis vectors fed into a deep neural network with four fully connected hidden layers and tanh activation functions, then enforce fermionic antisymmetry and parity projection through explicit summation over permutations. The network is trained unsupervisedly using the variational principle, with Monte Carlo evaluation of the energy.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon ground-state energy: DeepQuark converges to benchmark results from GEM and DMC for both two-body AL1 and flux-tube confinement interactions, with statistical uncertainties below 0.1 MeV
- Doubly charmed tetraquark T_cc (cc\bar q\bar q): binding energy ΔE = -15 MeV relative to D*D threshold, with DeepQuark lowering the GEM result by approximately 1 MeV; rms radii r_cc = 1.24 fm, r_c\bar q = 1.06 fm, r_\bar q\bar q = 1.41 fm
- Doubly bottom tetraquark T_bb (bb\bar q\bar q): binding energy ΔE = -153 MeV relative to \bar B*\bar B threshold; rms radii r_bb = 0.33 fm, r_b\bar q = 0.69 fm, r_\bar q\bar q = 0.78 fm
- Fully heavy tetraquarks (cc\bar c\bar c and bb\bar b\bar b): no bound state solutions found; ground-state energies lie above lowest meson-meson thresholds
- Triply heavy pentaquark P_cc\bar c(5715) (ccqq\bar c) with S=5/2: binding energy ΔE = -3 MeV relative to \bar D*Ξ_cc* threshold; rms radii r_cc = 0.50 fm, r_c\bar c = 1.73 fm
- Triply heavy pentaquark P_bb\bar b(15569) (bbqq\bar b) with S=5/2: binding energy ΔE = -14 MeV relative to B*Ξ_bb* threshold
- No bound states found for S=1/2 or S=3/2 triply heavy pentaquarks

## Context

This work extends the deep-neural-network variational Monte Carlo approach, previously successful in quantum spin systems (Carleo 2017), atomic and molecular physics (FermiNet, Hermann 2020), and nuclear physics (Adams 2020, Yang 2022), to the multiquark sector where strong SU(3) color interactions create stronger correlations than in electron or nucleon systems. It overcomes limitations of the Gaussian expansion method (GEM, Hiyama 2003), which suffers exponential growth of basis states, and diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC), which faces the sign problem (Troyer 2004). The method enables unbiased description of both molecular states (like T_cc) and compact tetraquarks (like T_bb) within a single framework, and can incorporate complex interactions beyond two-body forces such as flux-tube confinement, paving the way for investigating lattice-inspired potentials and confining mechanisms in multiquark states.
