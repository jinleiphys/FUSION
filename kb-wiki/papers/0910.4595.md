# Meson-Baryon Scattering Parameters from Lattice QCD with an Isospin Chemical Potential

> The extraction of meson-baryon scattering parameters from lattice QCD is complicated by the necessity, in some channels, of including annihilation diagrams. We consider a strategy to avoid the need for these extremely costly and noisy contributions. The strategy is based on simulations with an isospin chemical potential which, contrary to a baryon chemical potential, has no sign problem. When the isospin chemical potential is larger than a critical value, a charged pion condensate forms. Baryons propagating in the pion condensate will have their masses modified by pion-baryon scattering parameters. Consequently these parameters can be extracted from lattice QCD simulations in an isospin chemical potential, and we detail precisely which low-energy constants using baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that meson-baryon scattering parameters (low-energy constants) can be extracted from lattice QCD simulations by using an isospin chemical potential to create a pion condensate, thereby avoiding the need for computationally expensive annihilation diagrams that plague direct scattering calculations in channels like pion-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to compute the mass shifts of nucleons, hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ), and cascade baryons when they propagate through a charged pion condensate formed at isospin chemical potentials above the critical value (μ_I > m_π). The mass shifts are expressed in terms of low-energy constants (LECs) from the second-order chiral Lagrangian, and the authors detail which linear combinations of LECs can be determined by measuring baryon masses at several values of the isospin chemical potential and quark mass. The analysis is performed for both SU(2) and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory, with matching conditions relating the two frameworks.

## Key numbers

- The critical isospin chemical potential for pion condensate formation is approximately m_π (the pion mass)
- The pion mass satisfies the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation: m_π² = 2λ m
- The nucleon mass shift at leading order is M_N = M_N^(0) - μ_I cos α (τ³/2), where α is the condensate angle
- The Λ baryon mass is M_Λ = M_Λ^(0) + 4c₁^Λ (m_π² cos α + λε sin α) + (c₂^Λ + c₃^Λ) μ_I² sin² α
- The Σ⁰ baryon mass is M_Σ⁰ = M_Σ^(0) + 4c₁^Σ (m_π² cos α + λε sin α) + (c₂^Σ + c₃^Σ) μ_I² sin² α
- The charged Σ baryon mass eigenvalues are M_± = M_Σ⁰ + (c₆^Σ + c₇^Σ) μ_I² sin² α ± μ_I √[cos² α + (c₆^Σ + c₇^Σ)² μ_I² sin⁴ α]
- The cascade baryon mass is M_Ξ = M_Ξ^(0) - μ_I cos α (τ³/2) + 4c₁^Ξ (m_π² cos α + λε sin α) + (c₂^Ξ - g_ΞΞ²/(8M_Ξ^(0)) + c₃^Ξ) μ_I² sin² α

## Context

This work extends the use of isospin chemical potential in lattice QCD, previously applied to meson systems by Kogut et al. (2002-2004) and de Forcrand et al. (2007), to the baryon sector for extracting scattering parameters. The method directly addresses the difficulty of computing annihilation diagrams in channels like pion-nucleon and antikaon-nucleon scattering, which have remained elusive to first-principles lattice calculations. The approach enables determination of the nucleon σ-term (related to the c₁ coefficient), which is important for dark matter interactions with nuclei and has been subject to controversy between extractions by Gasser (1990) and Buettiker (1999), and can also help resolve uncertainties in antikaon-nucleon couplings from analyses by Oller (2000, 2005) and Meissner (2004).
