# Analysis of recent eta photoproduction data

> Recent data on eta-meson photoproduction off a proton target in the energy range 2 < sqrt{s} < 3 GeV are analyzed with regard to their overall consistency. Results from the ELSA and CLAS measurements are compared with predictions of a Regge model whose reaction amplitude was fixed via a global fit to pre-2000 measurements of differential cross sections and polarization observables for gamma p -> eta p at higher energies. We find that all recent experimental results on differential cross sections for eta-meson photoproduction are in good agreement with each other, except for the CLAS data from 2009. However, the latter can be made consistent with the other data at the expense of introducing an energy-dependent renormalization factor. We point out that there indications in the data for a possible excitation of baryon resonances with masses around 2.1 and 2.4 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors find that all recent experimental results on differential cross sections for eta-meson photoproduction are in good agreement with each other, except for the CLAS data from 2009, which can be made consistent with the other data only by introducing an energy-dependent renormalization factor. They point out that there are indications in the data for a possible excitation of baryon resonances with masses around 2.1 and 2.4 GeV, though firm conclusions require further analyses including polarization observables.

## Method

The authors compare recent ELSA and CLAS measurements of gamma p -> eta p differential cross sections in the energy range 2 < sqrt(s) < 3 GeV with predictions from a gauge-invariant Regge model that includes rho, omega, and b1 exchanges with Regge pole and cut amplitudes. The model parameters were fixed by a global fit to pre-2000 measurements of differential cross sections and polarization observables at photon energies above 3 GeV (sqrt(s) > 2.55 GeV). The consistency between data sets is quantified using a deviation function D(sqrt(s)) that averages the relative difference between experimental and theoretical differential cross sections over the t-distribution at fixed energy.

## Key numbers

- The ELSA measurement covers invariant collision energies up to sqrt(s) = 2.37 GeV, while the highest energy in the CLAS experiment is sqrt(s) = 2.795 GeV
- The isotropic part of the differential cross section at energies 2.03 <= sqrt(s) <= 2.14 GeV amounts to dsigma/dt = 0.5 to 0.18 microbarn/GeV^2, decreasing slowly with energy
- The deviation of the Regge model from pre-2000 experimental results amounts to about 20% in average
- For ELSA data at energies sqrt(s) > 2.15 GeV, the relative deviation averages to about 7%, while for the more recent ELSA measurements at 2.15 < sqrt(s) < 2.4 GeV the average deviation is about 5%
- At lower energies the deviation reaches maximum values of about 45% around invariant energies of 2.1 GeV and about 40% for the more recent ELSA data
- The CLAS 2009 data have an additional error of 11% in average included in quadrature following the published results

## Context

This work extends the approach used by Adelseck (1990) and Mart (2007) for examining consistency of K Lambda photoproduction data, applying it to eta photoproduction. The analysis compares three ELSA experiments (Crede 2005, Jaegle 2008, Crede 2009) and two CLAS experiments (Dugger 2002, Williams 2009) with a Regge model previously used by the authors for pion photoproduction (Sibirtsev 2008, Sibirtsev 2009). The findings enable future searches for high-mass baryon resonances, particularly the S11(2090), P11(2110), and G17(2190) listed by the Particle Data Group, by establishing which data sets are mutually consistent for combined analyses.
