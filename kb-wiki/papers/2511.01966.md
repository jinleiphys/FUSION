# Entanglement asymmetry in gauge theories: chiral anomaly in the finite temperature massless Schwinger model

> The entanglement asymmetry has emerged in recent years as a practical quantity to study phases of matter. We present the first study of entanglement asymmetry in gauge theories by considering the chiral anomaly of the analytically solvable massless Schwinger model at both zero and finite temperatures. At zero temperature, we find the asymmetry exhibits logarithmic growth with system size. At finite temperature, we show that it is parametrically more sensitive to chiral symmetry-breaking than the corresponding local order parameter: while the chiral condensate decays exponentially, the asymmetry decreases only logarithmically. This establishes the entanglement asymmetry as a promising tool to probe (finite-temperature) phase transitions in gauge theories.

## Key claim

The entanglement asymmetry detects chiral symmetry breaking in the massless Schwinger model even though the symmetry breaking arises intrinsically from the quantum anomaly rather than from explicit symmetry-breaking terms in the Lagrangian; at finite temperature, the asymmetry decreases only logarithmically with temperature while the chiral condensate decays exponentially, making the asymmetry parametrically more sensitive to symmetry breaking than the local order parameter.

## Method

The authors bosonize the massless Schwinger model to a massive free boson of mass \(m = g/\sqrt{\pi}\), then compute the Rényi entanglement asymmetry for the chiral charge \(Q_5\) in both the ground state and the finite-temperature Gibbs state. At zero temperature, they evaluate Gaussian integrals of charged moments using the momentum-space representation of the ground state wavefunction. At finite temperature, they compute the charged moments by inserting resolutions of the identity in the momentum basis and using the harmonic oscillator propagator, then perform the replica limit \(n \to 1\) to obtain the von Neumann entanglement asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* Zero-temperature entanglement asymmetry: \(\Delta S \simeq \frac{1}{2}\log(mL\pi) + \frac{1}{2}\), showing logarithmic growth with system size \(L\).
* Variance of chiral charge at zero temperature: \(\langle Q_5^2\rangle = mL/2\).
* Variance of chiral charge at finite temperature: \(\langle Q_5^2\rangle_{\beta} = \frac{mL}{2}\coth(\beta m/2)\).
* High-temperature asymptotic entanglement asymmetry (for \(1/(mL) < m\beta \ll mL\)): \(\Delta S \approx \frac{1}{2}(-1 + \log(2\pi m^2 \beta L))\).
* Chiral condensate at high temperature decays as \(\langle\bar{\psi}\psi\rangle \sim e^{-\pi/(m\beta)}\).
* Second Rényi asymmetry at very small \(\beta\) (finite size): \(\Delta S^{(2)} \sim (\pi^2/12) L m^2 \beta\).

## Context

This work extends the entanglement asymmetry framework, introduced by Ares (2022) for many-body quantum systems, to gauge theories for the first time. It uses the analytically solvable massless Schwinger model, originally solved by Schwinger (1962) and bosonized by Coleman (1976). The results contrast with the exponential decay of the chiral condensate found by Sachs (1991) at finite temperature. The logarithmic scaling of the asymmetry matches patterns seen in Gaussian states by Murciano (2024), Haar random states by Ares (2024), and generic translation-invariant matrix product states by Capizzi (2024), despite the different origin of symmetry breaking. This work enables future studies of entanglement asymmetry in higher-dimensional gauge theories and non-invertible symmetries, as initiated by Benini (2025) and Ahmad (2025).
