# Jet-medium interaction and the Gubser flow

> We study the effects of expansion and viscous corrections on the hydrodynamical medium response to a high energy jet parton. More specifically, using a semi-analytical Gubser solution to relativistic fluid dynamics, modifications to the formed Mach cone, diffusive wake, and the momentum flow of the medium response along and perpendicular to the jet particle, are analyzed mode-by-mode. This should provide intuition and guidance for analyses of the experimentally measured jet sub-structure in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that viscous suppression of the jet-induced medium response arises from two distinct mechanisms: a "dynamical" suppression due to the viscosity dependence of the jet energy loss rate, and a "hydrodynamical" suppression due to viscous damping of the fluid response modes; the dynamical suppression is the dominant effect.

## Method

The authors treat the jet-medium interaction as a linear perturbation on top of an analytically solved Gubser flow background, which provides a conformal fluid with both radial and longitudinal expansion. The perturbations are decomposed into spherical harmonic modes in the Gubser coordinate system, and the linearized hydrodynamic equations are solved mode-by-mode with a source term derived from a kinetic description of jet parton energy loss. The resulting conical flow structures are converted into pion spectra via a Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription at proper time τ_f = 6 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- The Mach cone angle in a static medium is θ^M_0 ≈ 70°, determined by the ratio of parton velocity to speed of sound.
- The background Gubser flow is set to approximate ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.
- The initial proper time for jet parton propagation is τ_0 = 1 fm/c.
- Two values of specific shear viscosity are compared: η/s = 1/4π and η/s = 2/4π.
- A lower transverse momentum cut of p_T ≥ 1 GeV is applied to the computed pion spectra.
- The freeze-out proper time is τ_f = 6 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of jet-induced Mach cones and diffusive wakes from static medium calculations (Casalderrey-Solana, 2004; Chesler, 2007) to an expanding conformal fluid using the Gubser flow solution (Gubser, 2010). The mode-by-mode decomposition approach enables separation of viscous effects into dynamical suppression (related to the jet energy loss parameter κ and its relation to η/s via κ ≈ s/3η from Majumder, 2007) and hydrodynamical suppression (mode damping proportional to exp(-l² η/s)). The results provide guidance for interpreting jet sub-structure measurements from LHC experiments (CMS, 2014) and can be extended to quadratic order hydrodynamics for more realistic descriptions.
