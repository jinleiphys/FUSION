# Incorporation of three-nucleon force in the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic approach

> It is shown how a bare three-nucleon force is incorporated into the formalism of the effective interaction approach for hyperspherical harmonics. As a practical example we calculate the ground state properties of 3H and 3He using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. A very good convergence of binding energies and matter radii is obtained. We also find a very good agreement of our results compared to other high precision calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates how a bare three-nucleon force can be incorporated into the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic (EIHH) formalism, achieving a very good convergence of binding energies and matter radii for 3H and 3He that agrees well with other high-precision calculations, though the method is presented as a proof-of-principle for systems with A=3 and the extension to A>3 is described as an alternative that remains to be fully tested.

## Method

The authors start from the A-body Hamiltonian in hyperspherical coordinates, including both the Argonne V18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. They construct an effective Hamiltonian by first solving a quasi two-body adiabatic Hamiltonian to obtain a two-body effective interaction via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation, then adding the bare three-nucleon force directly to the effective Hamiltonian for the three-nucleon calculations. For the three-nucleon force matrix elements, they perform a multipole expansion of the Urbana IX operator and evaluate the spatial integrals using a six-dimensional integration technique with a special parametrization of the Jacobi vectors and hyperspherical harmonics.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy: convergence to within about 50 keV at K=8, about 20 keV at K=10, and essentially the final result at K=12, compared to the K=20 value
- 3He binding energy: similar convergence pattern to triton, with excellent convergence shown in Figure 2
- Comparison with results from ELOT2: triton binding energy difference of about 40 keV between the two calculations
- Comparison with results from BoPi: maximal difference for binding energy is 6 keV for both 3H and 3He

## Context

This work extends the EIHH method, previously developed for two-nucleon forces (Barnea et al., 2001; Barnea et al., 2003), to include bare three-nucleon forces, which are known to be necessary for accurate binding energies of three-body nuclei since two-nucleon forces alone are insufficient. The results agree well with other high-precision calculations using Green Function Monte Carlo (GFMC), Faddeev techniques, and hyperspherical harmonics expansions (ELOT, ELOT2, BoPi, Nogga), and the method opens the way to include three-nucleon forces for systems with A>3 in the EIHH approach, which is particularly suitable for larger systems where exact methods become computationally prohibitive.
