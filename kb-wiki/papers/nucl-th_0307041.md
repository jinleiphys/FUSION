# Radiative capture cross sections: challenges and solutions

> Radiative capture reactions are one of the main inputs in stellar modelling. In numerous situations, the low energies at which these reactions occur in stars are not accessible with present experimental techniques. Electron screening is one of the major causes of problems in separating the bare cross sections from the screened ones. Indirect experimental techniques have been proposed and are now one of the main tools to obtain these cross sections. I discuss the electron screening problem and the latest progresses obtained with indirect methods.

# Radiative capture cross sections: challenges and solutions

## Key claim
Indirect experimental techniques (Trojan-Horse method, asymptotic normalization coefficients, Coulomb dissociation) can provide radiative capture cross sections free from the electron screening problem that plagues direct measurements, but the fundamental discrepancy between theory and experiment for laboratory electron screening remains unresolved and may even challenge basic principles such as the expected presence of nuclear recoil.

## Method
The paper reviews the electron screening problem in low-energy nuclear reactions, comparing the Debye-Hückel (Salpeter) plasma screening model with laboratory atomic screening measurements. It then surveys three indirect methods: the Trojan-Horse method (using breakup reactions to extract low-energy S-factors), asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) techniques from peripheral transfer reactions, and Coulomb dissociation (where breakup cross sections are related to photodisintegration and, by time reversal, to radiative capture). The author also presents new coupled-channels calculations of stopping power in He⁺+He collisions using a two-center molecular orbital basis with Landau-Zener level-crossing treatment.

## Key numbers
- The reaction ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B in the Sun occurs at approximately 20 keV; the S-factor was historically uncertain by as much as 30% according to Barker (1980).
- The reaction ¹²C(α,γ)¹⁶O occurs at approximately 300 keV in stars and should be known to better than 20% for good stellar modelling.
- Plasma screening (Debye-Hückel/Salpeter approach) enhances reaction rates such as ³He(³He,2p)⁴He and ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B by as much as 20%.
- For proton+hydrogen collisions, the calculated stopping power is proportional to v^α with α=1.35; Andersen-Ziegler table extrapolations predict a larger value of α.
- Reference (GS91) found stopping power S ~ v_p^3.34 for protons in the energy range of 4 keV incident on helium targets.
- The Coulomb dissociation method for ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B yielded an S₁₇(0) value of 19.0 eV·b, compatible with solar model calculations from Bahcall (1989).

## Context
This review extends the long-standing discussion of the laboratory electron screening puzzle, where experimental findings disagree systematically by a factor of two with adiabatic theory (Assenbaum 1987, Engstler 1988, Blüge 1990, Rolfs 1995, Rolfs 2001). The paper contrasts direct measurements with indirect methods: the Trojan-Horse method (Baur 1986, Typel 2003), asymptotic normalization coefficients (Mukhamedzhanov 1990), and Coulomb dissociation (Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel 1986). The work enables future applications of radioactive beam facilities to measure cross sections for reactions such as ¹³N(p,γ)¹⁵O and others relevant to stellar modelling and the r-process, while noting that the unexplained "disappearance" of nuclear recoil in low-energy stopping power data remains an open problem.
