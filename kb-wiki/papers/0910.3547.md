# Multiplicity fluctuations and temperature fluctuations

> We argue that specific fluctuations observed in high-energy nuclear collisions can be attributed to intrinsic fluctuations of temperature of the hadronizing system formed in such processes and therefore can be described by the same nonextensivity parameter $q$ characterizing Tsallis statistics describing such systems (for $q \to 1$ one recovers the usual Boltzmann-Gibbs approach). It means that $|q-1|$, which is a direct measure of temperature fluctuations, can also be expressed by the observed mean multiplicity, $<N>$, and by its variance, $Var(N)$. This allows to deduce from the experimental data the system size dependence of parameter $q$ with $q = 1$ corresponding to an infinite, thermalized source with a fixed temperature, and with the observed $q > 1$ corresponding to a finite source in which both the temperature and energy fluctuate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that multiplicity fluctuations observed in high-energy nuclear collisions can be attributed to intrinsic temperature fluctuations of the hadronizing system, quantified by the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) from Tsallis statistics, with the specific condition that this relationship holds when the system has finite size and both temperature and energy fluctuate simultaneously.

## Method

The authors derive a relation between the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) and the variance of multiplicity \(Var(N)\) and mean multiplicity \(\langle N\rangle\) using the Lindhard uncertainty relation between energy and temperature fluctuations. They connect \(q-1 = Var(T)/\langle T\rangle^2\) to the heat capacity \(C_V\) and the system size parameter \(\xi\), which is assumed proportional to the fraction of participating nucleons \(N_P/A\). The resulting expression \(q-1 = (1/a N_P)(1 - N_P/A)\) is then compared to experimental data on multiplicity fluctuations and transverse momentum correlations from heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The relation \(q-1 = Var(T)/\langle T\rangle^2 = 1/C_V\) connects the nonextensivity parameter to the heat capacity of the hadronizing system.
- For the intermediate case, \(Var(U) = \langle T\rangle^2 C_V \xi\) with \(\xi \in (0,1)\), leading to \(q-1 = (1-\xi)/C_V\).
- The system size dependence is given by \(q-1 = (1/a N_P)(1 - N_P/A)\), where \(N_P\) is the number of participating nucleons and \(A\) is the total number of nucleons.
- For transverse momentum correlations, the parameters used are: \(\Phi_{N_P=A} = 1.44\) MeV, \(\langle p_T\rangle = 400\) MeV, \(b = 4.8\) MeV, \(n_0 = 0.642\), \(c = 4.1\), \(a'=1\), and \(Var(p_T)/\langle p_T\rangle = 0.43\).
- The observed \(Var(N)/\langle N\rangle\) decreases with \(N_P\) and is proportional to \(1 - N_P/A\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of nonextensive Tsallis statistics to high-energy collisions, summarized in their review (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2009) and earlier papers (EPJA, OurPaper). It builds on the Lindhard uncertainty relation (Lindhard, 1974) and the interpretation of \(q\) as a measure of thermal bath heat capacity (limq, 2004). The results enable a unified description of multiplicity fluctuations and transverse momentum correlations, showing they convey the same information about the hadronizing system, and provide a framework for extracting system size dependence from experimental data.
