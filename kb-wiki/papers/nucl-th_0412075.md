# Nucleons and nuclei in the context of low-energy QCD

> This presentation reports on recent developments concerning basic aspects of low-energy QCD as they relate to the understanding of the nucleon mass and the nuclear many-body problem.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low-energy QCD, through spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and its effective field theory description, provides a unifying framework for understanding both the nucleon mass and the nuclear many-body problem, with the caveat that the approach works within a specific window around nuclear saturation density where the Fermi momentum and pion mass are comparable scales.

## Method

The author develops chiral effective field theory (ChPT) for the pion-nucleon system, expanding the effective Lagrangian in powers of derivatives and quark masses. In-medium chiral perturbation theory is then applied to nuclear matter by introducing the in-medium nucleon propagator with a Fermi sea insertion, organizing diagrams systematically in the number of medium insertions and performing an expansion in inverse powers of the nucleon mass. The energy density is computed to three-loop order, incorporating one- and two-pion exchange processes with a single adjustable contact term representing short-distance dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Chiral condensate: \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle \simeq -1.5\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.4 \pm 0.3\) MeV
- Nucleon axial vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.270 \pm 0.003\)
- Nucleon sigma term: \(\sigma_N \simeq 45-55\) MeV from empirical determination, and \(\sigma_N = (47 \pm 3)\) MeV from chiral interpolation
- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit: \(M_0 \simeq 0.89\) GeV
- Mean squared scalar radius of the nucleon: \(\langle r_S\rangle^{1/2} \simeq 1.3\) fm
- Nuclear matter saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) with Fermi momentum \(k_F^{(0)} \simeq 0.26\) GeV \(\sim 2m_\pi\)
- Binding energy per nucleon from one-parameter in-medium ChPT: \(E(\rho_0)/A = -15.3\) MeV at equilibrium density \(\rho_0 = 0.178\) fm\(^3\)
- Compressibility: \(K = 255\) MeV (empirical: \(K = 220 \pm 50\) MeV)
- Asymmetry energy per nucleon: \({\cal A} = 33.8\) MeV (empirical: \({\cal A} \simeq 33\) MeV)
- Liquid-gas transition temperature: \(T_c \simeq 15\) MeV (empirical: \(T_c = 16.6 \pm 0.9\) MeV)
- Scalar mean field at saturation: \(\Sigma_S \simeq -350\) MeV (using \(\sigma_N \simeq 50\) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) for pion-pion scattering and by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner for pion-nucleon interactions, applying it to the nuclear many-body problem. The approach builds on earlier phenomenological understanding of the nuclear force from two-pion exchange (Kotthoff and Brown; Ericson and Figureau) and connects to lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon mass (CP-PACS, JLQCD, QCDSF collaborations). The in-medium chiral perturbation theory results are compared with sophisticated many-body calculations (Friedman and Pandharipande) and with the universal low-momentum NN interaction \(V\)(low-k) from Bogner, Kuo, and Schwenk. The work enables a systematic connection between QCD condensates and relativistic mean field models (Serot and Walecka; Ring and Furnstahl), and provides a foundation for developing improved nuclear energy density functionals.
