# Updated Analysis of the Mass of the H Dibaryon from Lattice QCD

> Recent lattice QCD calculations from the HAL and NPLQCD Collaborations have reported evidence for the existence of a bound state with strangeness -2 and baryon number 2 at quark masses somewhat higher than the physical values. A controlled chiral extrapolation of these lattice results to the physical point suggested that the state, identified with the famed H dibaryon, is most likely slightly unbound (by 13 $\pm$ 14 MeV) with respect to the $Λ--Λ$ threshold. We report the results of an updated analysis which finds the H unbound by 26 $\pm$ 11 MeV. Apart from the insight it would give us into how QCD is realized in Nature, the H is of great interest because of its potential implications for the equation of state of dense matter and studies of neutron stars. It may also explain the enhancement above the $Λ--Λ$ threshold already reported experimentally. It is clearly of great importance that the latter be pursued in experiments at the new J-PARC facility.

## Key claim

The updated chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results from the HAL and NPLQCD Collaborations finds that the H dibaryon is unbound by 26 ± 11 MeV with respect to the Λ–Λ threshold at physical quark masses, revising the earlier estimate of 13 ± 14 MeV unbound.

## Method

The authors performed a chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD results for the H dibaryon binding energy, assuming the H is a compact SU(3)-singlet six-quark state (not a molecular state like the deuteron). They simultaneously fit updated lattice data from the HAL QCD Collaboration (using hadron-hadron potentials from time-dependent Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions) and the NPLQCD Collaboration (using Luscher's method with finite volume scattering phase shifts), together with PACS-CS Collaboration lattice results for the octet baryon masses, using finite range regularization for the chiral loops.

## Key numbers

- The H dibaryon is unbound by 26 ± 11 MeV with respect to the Λ–Λ threshold at physical quark masses (updated analysis).
- The earlier analysis found the H unbound by 13 ± 14 MeV (Shanahan et al. 2011).
- The χ²/d.o.f. for the updated fit is 0.64.
- The NAGARA event (sequential decay of ⁶_ΛΛHe) gives the most stringent experimental constraint on H binding: B_H < 6.91 MeV.
- The J-PARC experiment will achieve 1 MeV mass resolution, compared to 5 MeV resolution in the earlier KEK experiment.

## Context

This work extends the chiral extrapolation analysis of Shanahan et al. (2011) and Thomas et al. (2011) by incorporating revised and additional lattice results from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Inoue et al. 2011) and NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane et al. 2011). The result contradicts the original bag-model prediction of Jaffe (1977) that the H should be deeply bound, and is quantitatively similar to the analysis of Haidenbauer and Meissner (2011), who assumed the H is a loosely bound baryon-baryon molecule and found a resonance at 21 MeV based on NPLQCD results. The finding enables experimental searches at J-PARC and FAIR to focus on the near-threshold region, and has implications for neutron star equations of state and the possible conversion of neutron stars into strange quark stars.
