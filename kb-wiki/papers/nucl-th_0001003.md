# Angular momentum projected analysis of Quadrupole Collectivity in \protect(^{30,32,34}Mg\protect) and \protect(^{32,34,36,38}Si\protect) with the Gogny interaction

> A microscopic angular momentum projection after variation is used to describe quadrupole collectivity in (^{30,32,34}Mg) and (^{32,34,36,38}Si). The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states obtained in the quadrupole constrained mean field approach are taken as intrinsic states for the projection. Excitation energies of the first (2^{+}) states and the (B(E2,0^{+}\to 2^{+})) transition probabilities are given. A reasonable agreement with available experimental data is obtained. It is also shown that the mean field picture of those nuclei is strongly modified by the projection.

## Key claim

A microscopic angular momentum projection after variation, using the Gogny D1S interaction, provides a reasonable but not perfect description of quadrupole collectivity in neutron-rich Mg and Si isotopes; the mean-field picture is strongly modified by the projection, and configuration mixing (beyond the present work) is needed for a fully quantitative description.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed with the D1S parameterization of the Gogny force, using the mass quadrupole operator as the constraining operator and imposing axial symmetry, parity, and time reversal. From the resulting intrinsic wave functions, angular momentum projected energy curves for I=0 and I=2 were computed, along with projected B(E2,0+→2+) transition probabilities. A harmonic oscillator basis of 10 major shells, closed under rotations, was used to expand the quasiparticle operators.

## Key numbers

- For ³²Mg, the projected B(E2,0+→2+) from the prolate 0+ to prolate 2+ configuration is 593.24 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 454 ± 78 e²fm⁴.
- For ³²Mg, the projected 0+–2+ excitation energy (prolate 0+ to prolate 2+) is 0.873 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.885 MeV.
- For ³⁴Mg, the projected 0+–2+ excitation energy (prolate 0+ to prolate 2+) is 0.753 MeV.
- For ³²Si, the projected B(E2,0+→2+) from the oblate 0+ to oblate 2+ configuration is 50.71 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 113 ± 33 e²fm⁴.
- For ³⁴Si, the projected B(E2,0+→2+) from the oblate 0+ to oblate 2+ configuration is 39.16 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 85 ± 33 e²fm⁴.
- For ³⁸Si, the projected B(E2,0+→2+) from the oblate 0+ to prolate 2+ configuration is 35.18 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 193 ± 71 e²fm⁴.
- The projected I=0 ground state energy for ³²Mg is -245.62 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -249.68 MeV (including a Coulomb exchange correction of -4.22 MeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of shape coexistence in the N≈20 region (e.g., Reinhard (1999) using Skyrme parameterizations) by performing, for the first time with the Gogny force, exact angular momentum projection rather than approximate rotational energy corrections. The results show that the commonly used approximate rotational correction (based on the Yoccoz moment of inertia) fails quantitatively for these light nuclei, and that configuration mixing (Generator Coordinate Method) is required as a next step to improve agreement with experiment, particularly for ³⁶Si and ³⁸Si.
