# Properties of inclusive hadron production in Deep Inelastic Scattering on heavy nuclei at low x

> In this paper we present a comprehensive study of inclusive hadron production in DIS at low $x$. Properties of the hadron spectrum are different in different kinematic regions formed by three relevant momentum scales: photon virtuality $Q^2$, hadron transverse momentum $k_T$ and the saturation momentum $Q_s(x)$. We investigate each kinematic region and derive the corresponding asymptotic formulas for the cross section at the leading logarithmic order. We also analyze the next-leading-order (NLO) corrections to the BFKL kernel that are responsible for the momentum conservation. In particular, we establish the asymptotic behavior of the forward elastic dipole--nucleus scattering amplitude at high energies deeply in the saturation regime and a modification of the pomeron intercept. We study the nuclear effect on the inclusive cross section using the nuclear modification factor and its logarithmic derivative. We argue that the later is proportional to the difference between the anomalous dimension of the gluon distribution in nucleus and in proton and thus is a direct measure of the coherence effects. To augment our arguments and present quantitative results we performed numerical calculations in the kinematic region that may be accessible by the future DIS experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear modification factor's logarithmic derivative with respect to rapidity is proportional to the difference between the anomalous dimensions of the gluon distribution in a nucleus and in a proton, and thus serves as a direct probe of coherence effects at low x; this claim is hedged by the condition that it holds in the kinematic region where the hadron transverse momentum is much larger than the saturation momentum.

## Method

The authors used the dipole model to relate the deep inelastic scattering cross section on a heavy nucleus to the cross section for a color dipole scattering on the nucleus, then derived asymptotic formulas for gluon production in various kinematic regions defined by the relative sizes of photon virtuality, hadron transverse momentum, and saturation momentum. They analyzed next-to-leading-order corrections to the BFKL kernel responsible for momentum conservation by employing a modified Balitsky-Kovchegov equation that satisfies energy conservation, and performed numerical calculations using the bCGC model for the dipole-nucleus forward scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order pomeron intercept is given by $\alpha_P^{(0)}-1 = 4\bar{\alpha}_s \ln 2$, where $\bar{\alpha}_s = \alpha_s N_c / \pi$.
- The saturation momentum at leading double-logarithmic accuracy evolves as $Q_s(y) = Q_{s0} e^{2\bar{\alpha}_s y}$.
- The NLO correction to the BFKL eigenvalue function yields the relation $\omega = 2\bar{\alpha}_s \chi(\nu) / (1 + 2\bar{\alpha}_s \chi(\nu))$ for the Mellin variable $\omega$.
- The anomalous dimension in the NLO energy-conserving model is given by $\gamma(\omega) = \bar{\alpha}_s (1/\omega - 1)$ in the limit $\gamma \to 0$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of gluon saturation and the color glass condensate developed by Gribov (1984), Mueller (1985-1994), McLerran and Venugopalan (1993-1994), and Kovchegov (1996-1999), as well as the BFKL pomeron formalism of Kuraev, Lipatov, and Balitsky (1977-1978). It builds on prior studies of inclusive hadron production in deep inelastic scattering by Kovchegov (1998-2001), Dumitru (2001), and Kharzeev (2002-2004), and on the modified BK equation with energy conservation proposed by Kormilitzin (2010) and Gotsman (2004). The results enable quantitative predictions for future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider and the Large Hadron-electron Collider, particularly for measuring the nuclear modification factor and its logarithmic derivative as probes of low-x gluon dynamics.
