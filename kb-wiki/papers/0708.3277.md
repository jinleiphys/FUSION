# Hadron-quark mixed phase in hyperon stars

> We analyze the different possibilities for the hadron-quark phase transition occurring in beta-stable matter including hyperons in neutron stars. We use a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach including hyperons for the hadronic equation of state and a generalized MIT bag model for the quark part. We then point out in detail the differences between Maxwell and Gibbs phase transition constructions including the effects of surface tension and electromagnetic screening. We find only a small influence on the maximum neutron star mass, whereas the radius of the star and in particular its internal structure are more affected.

## Key claim

The paper finds that while the hadron-quark mixed phase in hyperon stars has very different internal structure and composition compared to the simpler Maxwell construction, the influence on global neutron star properties such as maximum mass and mass-radius relation is small, with the main caveat being that this conclusion depends on the specific choice of surface tension and bag model parameters.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potentials, Urbana UIX three-body forces, and Nijmegen NSC89 hyperon-nucleon potentials to compute the hadronic equation of state including hyperons (n, p, Lambda, Sigma-). For the quark phase, they employ a generalized MIT bag model with both constant and density-dependent bag parameters, solving the Gibbs conditions for the mixed phase within a Wigner-Seitz approximation that includes finite-size effects from surface tension and Coulomb screening, using the Thomas-Fermi approximation for density profiles.

## Key numbers

- Hyperons appear at around 2-3 times normal nuclear density (rho_0 approximately 0.17 fm^{-3}) in beta-stable nuclear matter
- The mixed phase occurs in the density interval rho_B = 0.298 to 0.708 fm^{-3} for the constant bag model with B = 100 MeV/fm^3
- The mixed phase occurs in the density interval rho_B = 0.236 to 0.670 fm^{-3} for the density-dependent bag model
- The critical surface tension above which mixed phase structures become mechanically unstable is sigma_crit approximately 70 MeV/fm^2
- The surface tension used in the main results is sigma = 40 MeV/fm^2
- The equilibrium densities in the Maxwell construction are rho_H approximately 0.34 fm^{-3} and rho_Q approximately 0.50 fm^{-3}
- The Wigner-Seitz cell radius at rho_B = 0.4 fm^{-3} is R_W = 21.6 fm with droplet radius R = 13.8 fm for the constant bag case

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadron-quark phase transitions in neutron stars by Glendenning (1992) and Voskresensky et al. (2003) by including hyperons in the hadronic phase and performing a detailed finite-size calculation with Coulomb screening effects. The authors show that the charge screening effect can destabilize the geometrical structures of the mixed phase above a critical surface tension, effectively recovering the Maxwell construction result. The paper enables future studies of how the different internal core structures might affect neutrino transport and emission processes in neutron stars, as noted by Reddy et al. (2003).
