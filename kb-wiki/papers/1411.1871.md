# Thermodynamics and higher order moments in SU(3) linear $σ$-model with gluonic quasi-particles

> In framework of linear $σ$-model (LSM) with three quark flavors, the chiral phase-diagram at finite temperature and density is investigated. At temperatures higher than the critical temperature ($ T_c $), we added to LSM the gluonic sector from the quasi-particle model (QPM), which assumes that the interacting gluons in the strongly interacting matter, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), are phenomenologically the same as non-interacting massive quasi-particles. The dependence of the chiral condensates of strange and non-strange quarks on temperature and chemical potential is analysed. Then, we have calculated the thermodynamics in the new approach (combination of LSM and QPM). Confronting the results with recent lattice QCD simulations shows an excellent agreement in almost all thermodynamic quantities. The first and second order moments of particle multiplicity are studied in dependence on the chemical potential but at fixed temperature and on the chemical potential but at fixed temperature. These are implemented in characterizing the large fluctuations accompanying the chiral phase-transition. The results of first and second order moments are compared with the SU(3) Polyakov linear $σ$-model (PLSM). Also, the resulting phase-diagrams deduced in PLSM and LSM$+$QPM are compared with each other.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a gluonic quasi-particle sector to the SU(3) linear sigma model (LSM) above the critical temperature allows the combined model to reproduce recent lattice QCD thermodynamic results, but this agreement is conditional on excluding the purely mesonic potential contribution at high temperatures and on fixing the Yukawa coupling and quasi-particle parameters to specific values (g=8.3, lambda=2.0, T_s=0.0 MeV).

## Method

The authors combined the SU(3) linear sigma model (LSM) with three quark flavors and the gluonic sector from the quasi-particle model (QPM), where interacting gluons are treated as non-interacting massive quasi-particles with a temperature-dependent effective mass. They minimized the total thermodynamic potential (quark, mesonic, and quasigluonic parts) in mean-field approximation to obtain the chiral condensates sigma_q and sigma_s for light and strange quarks, then calculated thermodynamic quantities from the resulting pressure. The mesonic potential was found to vanish at high temperatures and was excluded from the effective potential above T_c.

## Key numbers

- Yukawa coupling fixed at g = 8.3
- Quark masses at zero temperature: m_q = 500 MeV, m_s = 1075 MeV
- Quasi-particle parameters: lambda = 2.0, T_s = 0.0 MeV
- Critical temperature T_c approximately 150 MeV (from lattice QCD reference [QCDL])
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit for pressure: 5.2 (in units of T^4, shown as arrow in Fig. 1)
- At high T, lattice QCD pressure from [QCDL] is approximately 30% lower than Stefan-Boltzmann limit
- At high T, lattice QCD pressure from [HotQCD] is approximately 14% lower than Stefan-Boltzmann limit

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov linear sigma model (PLSM) approach from Tawfik (2014) by replacing the Polyakov loop with a quasi-particle gluonic sector, motivated by the disagreement between PLSM and the most recent lattice QCD simulations from [QCDL] (which use physical quark masses and continuum extrapolation). The new approach reduces the number of free parameters in QPM from three to one (only lambda remains free). The results enable mapping the chiral phase diagram in the chemical potential direction, which the authors note cannot be directly compared with lattice QCD higher-moment calculations that are temperature-dependent at fixed chemical potential.
