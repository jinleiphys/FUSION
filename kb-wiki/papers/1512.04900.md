# Oscillations of superfluid hyperon stars: decoupling scheme and g-modes

> We analyse the oscillations of general relativistic superfluid hyperon stars, following the approach suggested by Gusakov & Kantor and Gusakov et al. and generalizing it to the nucleon-hyperon matter. We show that the equations governing the oscillations can be split into two weakly coupled systems with the coupling parameters $s_{\rm e}$, $s_{\rm μ}$, and $s_{\rm str}$. The approximation $s_{\rm e} = s_{\rm μ} = s_{\rm str} = 0$ (decoupling approximation) allows one to drastically simplify the calculations of stellar oscillation spectra. An efficiency of the presented scheme is illustrated by the calculation of sound speeds in the nucleon-hyperon matter composed of neutrons (n), protons (p), electrons (e), muons ($μ$), as well as $\rm Λ$, ${\rm Ξ}^-$, and ${\rm Ξ}^0$-hyperons. However, the gravity oscillation modes (g-modes) cannot be treated within this approach, and we discuss them separately. For the first time we study the composition g-modes in superfluid hyperon stars with the $\rm npeμΛ$ core and show that there are two types of g-modes (`muonic' and `$Λ$--hyperonic') in such stars. We also calculate the g-mode spectrum and find out that the eigenfrequencies $ν$ of the superfluid g-modes can be exceptionally large (up to $ν\approx 742~{\rm Hz}$ for a considered stellar model).

## Key claim

The equations governing oscillations of general relativistic superfluid hyperon stars can be split into two weakly coupled systems using the coupling parameters \(s_{\rm e}\), \(s_{\mu}\), and \(s_{\rm str}\); setting these parameters to zero (the decoupling approximation) drastically simplifies calculations of stellar oscillation spectra, but this approximation cannot treat gravity oscillation modes (g-modes), which must be studied separately.

## Method

The authors generalized the decoupling scheme previously applied to superfluid neutron stars with npe cores (Gusakov & Kantor 2011; Gusakov et al. 2013) to nucleon-hyperon matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi^{-}\), and \(\Xi^{0}\) hyperons. They derived the coupling parameters from thermodynamic derivatives of the pressure with respect to number densities, then tested the decoupling approximation by calculating sound speeds in the nucleon-hyperon mixture. For g-modes, they solved the full superfluid equations separately for a stellar model with an npe\(\mu\Lambda\) core, adopting the hyperonic equation of state from Gusakov et al. (2014).

## Key numbers

* The coupling parameter \(s_{\rm str}\) generally does not exceed 0.2 for the realistic hyperonic equations of state GM1A, GM1'B, and TM1C from Gusakov et al. (2014).
* The eigenfrequencies \(\nu\) of superfluid g-modes can be exceptionally large, up to \(\nu \approx 742~{\rm Hz}\) for the considered stellar model.
* Hyperons appear at densities around \(\rho \sim (2 \div 3) \rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 \approx 2.8 \times 10^{14}~{\rm g~cm}^{-3}\) is the density in atomic nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the decoupling approach of Gusakov & Kantor (2011) and Gusakov et al. (2013), previously applied to superfluid neutron stars with npe cores, to the more complex case of hyperon stars. It also provides the first calculation of composition g-modes in superfluid hyperon stars with an npe\(\mu\Lambda\) core, following the method of Kantor & Gusakov (2014) who studied g-modes in neutron stars with superfluid npe cores containing muons. The results enable simplified calculations of f-, p-, and r-modes in hyperon stars using the decoupling approximation, while the g-mode spectrum provides predictions for future observations of oscillating compact stars.
