# Low-Energy Direct Capture in the 16O(alpha,gamma)20Ne Reaction

> The cross section of the 16O(a,g)20Ne capture reaction is analyzed at low energies where the direct capture mechanism is dominant. For temperatures below T9 = 0.2 the resulting astrophysical reaction rate is about a factor of two higher than in a recent compilation whereas the energy dependence of the astrophysical S-factor and the branching ratios to the 20Ne bound states are very similar to previous calculations. The validity of the widely used detailed balance theorem for the inverse 20Ne(g,a)16O photodisintegration rate is confirmed for the special case of high-lying first excited states.

# Low-Energy Direct Capture in the 16O(α,γ)20Ne Reaction

## Key claim
The direct capture cross section for 16O(α,γ)20Ne at low energies (below T₉ = 0.2) produces an astrophysical reaction rate about a factor of two higher than the NACRE compilation, although the energy dependence of the S-factor and branching ratios to 20Ne bound states are very similar to previous calculations. The validity of the detailed balance theorem for the inverse 20Ne(γ,α)16O photodisintegration rate is confirmed for the special case of high-lying first excited states.

## Method
The direct capture cross section was calculated using a systematic double-folding potential that simultaneously reproduces elastic 16O(α,α)16O scattering data and bound state properties of 20Ne = 16O ⊗ α. The potential strength parameter λ was adjusted to reproduce binding energies of bound and quasi-bound states in 20Ne, and the absolute capture cross section was normalized using the ratio of experimental to calculated B(Eℒ) values for electromagnetic transitions, particularly to account for the suppressed E1 transitions in this N=Z system.

## Key numbers
- Total S-factor at 300 keV: S(300 keV) = 3.7 ± 1.2 MeV·b
- S-factor polynomial fit up to 0.5 MeV: S(E) = 3808 keV·b − 864 keV·b × E/MeV + 1527 keV·b × (E/MeV)²
- Slope of S-factor at zero energy: a = −0.23 MeV⁻¹ for the total S-factor
- Branching ratio b = (S₀+S₂)/S₀ = 4.5, with 78% contribution to the 2⁺ state and 22% to the 0⁺ ground state below 800 keV
- At T₉ = 0.2, the Gamow window is centered at E₀ = 390 keV with width Δ = 190 keV
- Ratio of 16O(α,γ)20Ne to 12C(α,γ)16O rates at T₉ = 0.2 is about 5 × 10⁻⁵
- Calculated S-factor to 0⁺ ground state: S → 0⁺ = 802 keV·b at E = 300 keV
- Calculated S-factor to 2⁺ state: S → 2⁺ = 2877 keV·b at E = 300 keV
- Potential strength parameter λ constant within ±2% for the positive parity band and ±5% for the negative parity band
- Calculated widths overestimate experimental widths by factor 6.6 for the 6⁺ state and 14.5 for the 8⁺ state in the ground state band

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical calculations of direct capture in 16O(α,γ)20Ne by Langanke (1983), Descouvemont and Baye (1985, 1986, 1988), and Dufour (1994), using a systematic folding potential from Abe (1993) that simultaneously describes elastic scattering and bound state properties. The calculated S-factor of 3.7 ± 1.2 MeV·b at 300 keV is slightly higher than but overlaps within uncertainties with the range of 0.7 to 2.5 MeV·b from prior calculations, and is about a factor of two above the value adopted in the NACRE compilation (2000). The result enables improved reaction rate calculations for helium burning in stars at temperatures below T₉ = 0.2, where direct capture dominates, and confirms that the detailed balance theorem remains valid for deriving the inverse photodisintegration rate despite the high-lying first excited states in 20Ne.
