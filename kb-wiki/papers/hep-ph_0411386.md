# Near threshold enhancement of the ppbar mass spectrum in J/Psi decay

> We investigate the nature of the near-threshold enhancement in the ppbar invariant mass spectrum of the reaction J/Psi -> gamma ppbar reported recently by the BES Collaboration. Using the Juelich NNbar model we show that the mass dependence of the ppbar spectrum close to the threshold can be reproduced by the S-wave ppbar final state interaction in the isospin I=1 state within the Watson-Migdal approach. However, because of our poor knowledge of the NNbar interaction near threshold and of the J/Psi -> gamma ppbar reaction mechanism and in view of the controversal situation in the decay J/Psi -> pi0 ppbar, where no obvious signs of a ppbar final state interaction are seen, explanations other than final state interactions cannot be ruled out at the present stage.

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the \(p\bar{p}\) invariant mass spectrum of \(J/\Psi \to \gamma p\bar{p}\) can be reproduced by the \(S\)-wave \(p\bar{p}\) final state interaction in the isospin \(I=1\) state using the Juelich \(N\bar{N}\) model within the Watson-Migdal approach, but explanations other than final state interactions cannot be ruled out due to poor knowledge of the \(N\bar{N}\) interaction near threshold and the reaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors used the Juelich \(N\bar{N}\) model A(OBE), which consists of the \(G\)-parity transform of the Bonn one-boson-exchange \(NN\) potential supplemented by a phenomenological complex Gaussian potential for \(N\bar{N}\) annihilation. They applied the Watson-Migdal factorization approach, where the production amplitude is proportional to the \(p\bar{p}\) scattering amplitude, and also considered a more refined distorted-wave Born approximation expression. The calculated \(p\bar{p}\) scattering amplitudes for different partial waves and isospin channels were compared to the BES data for \(J/\Psi \to \gamma p\bar{p}\) and \(J/\Psi \to \pi^0 p\bar{p}\) after normalizing to the data at an excess energy of 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The BES Collaboration fitted the \(p\bar{p}\) invariant mass spectrum below 1.95 GeV with an \(S\)-wave Breit-Wigner resonance giving mass \(M = 1859^{+3+5}_{-10-25}\) MeV and total width \(\Gamma < 30\) MeV; a \(P\)-wave fit gave \(M = 1876 \pm 0.9\) MeV and \(\Gamma = 4.6 \pm 1.8\) MeV.
- The scattering lengths predicted by the Juelich model for the \(^{11}S_0\) partial wave are \(a_0 = (-0.18 - i1.18)\) fm (isospin \(I=0\)) and \(a_1 = (1.13 - i0.61)\) fm (isospin \(I=1\)).
- The spin-averaged \(p\bar{p}\) scattering length from model A(OBE) is \(a_S = 0.84 - i0.85\) fm.
- The partial \(J/\Psi \to \gamma p\bar{p}\) decay width is \(\Gamma = (3.3 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-2}\) keV.
- The branching ratio for the near-threshold contribution in the \(^{31}S_0\) partial wave is estimated as \(BR(J/\Psi \to \gamma ^1\tilde S_0) \times BR(^1\tilde S_0 \to p\bar{p}) \simeq 9.5 \times 10^{-5}\), compared to the total \(J/\Psi \to \gamma p\bar{p}\) branching rate of \((3.8 \pm 1.0) \times 10^{-4}\).
- The Juelich model A(OBE) yields two bound states within 100 MeV of threshold: at \(E = -104 - i413\) MeV in the \(^{11}S_0\) partial wave and at \(E = -24.2 - i107\) MeV in the \(^{13}P_0\) partial wave.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the near-threshold \(p\bar{p}\) enhancement reported by the BES Collaboration (Bai et al., 2003) and earlier seen by Belle in \(B^+ \to K^+ p\bar{p}\) (Abe et al., 2002). It contradicts the scattering length approximation used by Kerbikov et al. (2004) and Bugg (2004), showing that approximation fails to reproduce the energy dependence of the full \(N\bar{N}\) amplitude over the relevant mass range. The study enables more quantitative tests of final state interaction explanations versus resonance interpretations, and highlights the need for experimental constraints on the \(^{11}S_0\) scattering length, which could be obtained at the future FLAIR facility with a polarized antiproton beam.
