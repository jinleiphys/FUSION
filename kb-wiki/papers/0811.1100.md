# Manifestation of three-body forces in three-body Bethe-Salpeter and light-front equations

> Bethe-Salpeter and light-front bound state equations for three scalar particles interacting by scalar exchange-bosons are solved in ladder truncation. In contrast to two-body systems, the three-body binding energies obtained in these two approaches differ significantly from each other: the ladder kernel in light-front dynamics underbinds by approximately a factor of two compared to the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation. By taking into account three-body forces in the light-front approach, generated by two exchange-bosons in flight, we find that most of this difference disappears; for small exchange masses, the obtained binding energies coincide with each other.

## Key claim

In contrast to two-body systems, the ladder kernel in light-front dynamics (LFD) underbinds three-body bound states by approximately a factor of two compared to the ladder Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation; however, including three-body forces generated by two exchange-bosons in flight in the LFD approach eliminates most of this difference, and for small exchange masses the binding energies coincide.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation and the three-body light-front bound state equation for scalar particles interacting via scalar exchange-boson exchange in ladder truncation, using bare propagators and no self-energies. They then calculated perturbatively the contribution to the LFD binding energy from the three-body forces depicted in nine time-ordered graphs (Fig. 3bf), which are implicitly included in the BS equation but not generated by iteration of the LF ladder kernel. The bound state equations were solved numerically by discretization of all five independent scalar variables and direct integration, without partial wave expansions.

## Key numbers

- For the three-body system, the ladder kernel in LFD underbinds by approximately a factor of two compared to the ladder BS equation (abstract).
- For small exchange masses, the binding energies from LFD (with three-body forces included) coincide with those from the BS equation (abstract).
- The constituent mass m is set to unity, so the maximal binding energy for a three-body system is three (Section 2.2).
- Results are presented for three exchange-boson masses: μ = 0.01, μ = 0.5, and μ = 1.5 (in units where constituent mass m = 1) (Fig. 3 caption).
- The dimensionless coupling constant is defined as α = g²/(16π m²) (Section 2.2).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of Bethe-Salpeter and light-front approaches from two-body systems (where binding energies are very close, as shown by Maris and collaborators (2000) and Karmanov, Carbonell, and Maris (2006)) to three-body bound states. The three-body forces studied here are of relativistic origin, analogous to diagrams first considered by Brueckner (1950s) and calculated for the triton by Yang (1970s); the authors note that Glöckle and collaborators showed that the first-order relativistic correction cancels in the Schrödinger equation, but the full relativistic treatment is required here. This work enables future extension to fermionic systems such as three-nucleon bound states (triton, ³He) and baryons as bound states of three dressed quarks.
