# Microscopic multiphonon approach to nuclei with a valence hole in the oxygen region

> An equation of motion phonon method, developed for even nuclei and recently extended to odd systems with a valence particle, is formulated in the hole-phonon coupling scheme and applied to A=15 and A=21 isobars with a valence hole. The method derives a set of equations which yield an orthonormal basis of states composed of a hole coupled to an orthonormal basis of correlated n-phonon states (n = 0, 1, 2, . . .), built of constituent Tamm-Dancoff phonons, describing the excitations of a doubly magic core. The basis is then adopted to solve the full eigenvalue problem. The method is formally exact but lends itself naturally to simplifying approximations. Self-consistent calculations using a chiral Hamiltonian in a space encompassing up to two-phonon and three-phonon basis states in A=21 A=15 nuclei, respectively, yield full spectra, moments, electromagnetic and beta-decay transition strengths, and electric dipole cross sections. The analysis of the hole-phonon composition of the eigenfunctions contributes to clarify the mechanism of excitation of levels and resonances and to understand the reasons of the deviations of the theory from the experiments. Prescriptions for reducing these discrepancies are suggested.

## Key claim

The equation of motion phonon method (EMPM), previously developed for even nuclei and odd systems with a valence particle, is reformulated in the hole-phonon coupling scheme and applied to A=15 and A=21 isobars with a valence hole; the method is formally exact but the calculations reveal a strong violation of mirror symmetry between $^{15}$O and $^{15}$N, attributed to the combined effect of charge symmetry breaking in the two-body potential and the enforcement of the Pauli principle through the Cholesky decomposition method.

## Method

The method generates an orthonormal basis of states composed of a valence hole coupled to correlated n-phonon states (n=0,1,2,...) built from Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) phonons describing the excitations of a doubly magic core ($^{16}$O or $^{22}$O). A Hartree-Fock (HF) basis derived from the chiral NN potential NNLO$_{opt}$ is used, and the generalized eigenvalue equations are solved iteratively using the Cholesky decomposition method to extract linearly independent states from the over-complete basis. Self-consistent calculations are performed in a space encompassing up to two-phonon basis states for A=21 and up to three-phonon basis states for A=15 nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The $3/2^-_1$ level in $^{15}$O is computed at 8.066 MeV (experimental ground state is 0.000 MeV), while in $^{15}$N it is at 7.057 MeV (experimental ground state is 0.000 MeV)
- The $7/2^+_1$ state in $^{15}$O is at 9.529 MeV and in $^{15}$N at 13.153 MeV, showing a mirror symmetry breaking of approximately 3.6 MeV
- Ground state magnetic moment of $^{15}$O: theoretical +0.5986 $\mu_N$ versus experimental $\pm 0.7189(8)$ $\mu_N$
- Ground state magnetic moment of $^{15}$N: theoretical -0.249919471 $\mu_N$ versus experimental -0.283188842(45) $\mu_N$
- $B(E3; 7/2^+_1 \rightarrow 1/2^-_1)$ in $^{15}$O: theoretical 7.37 W.u. versus experimental $6.4 \pm 2.5$ W.u.
- $B(E3; 7/2^+_1 \rightarrow 1/2^-_1)$ in $^{15}$N: theoretical 0.06 W.u. versus experimental $2.50 \pm 0.22$ W.u.
- $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ in $^{16}$O: theoretical 0.379 W.u. versus experimental $3.1 \pm 0.1$ W.u.
- $B(E3; 3^-_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ in $^{16}$O: theoretical 3.19 W.u. versus experimental $13.5 \pm 0.7$ W.u.

## Context

This work extends the EMPM approach previously applied to even-even nuclei (AndLo, AndLo1, Bianco) and to odd systems with a valence particle (DeGreg16a, DeGreg17a, DeGreg17b, DeGreg18) to the hole-phonon coupling scheme for nuclei around $^{16}$O. The method contrasts with earlier approaches such as the particle-vibration coupling model using RPA (Mizu12, Cao14, Tarpa14), the quasiparticle phonon model (MishVor08), and shell model calculations in the (p,sd) configuration space using the WBM interaction (Utsuno2011, Mertin2015). The results reveal a significant mirror symmetry violation between $^{15}$O and $^{15}$N that is not present in experiments or shell model analyses (Alburger79, Raman94), and the authors suggest that neglecting charge symmetry breaking terms in generating the HF and TDA basis states while reintroducing them directly in the eigenvalue equations for odd nuclei could resolve this discrepancy.
