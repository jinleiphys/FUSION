# Nonperturbative effects in neutrino magnetic moments

> In this paper, we calculate the QCD nonperturbative contributions of the neutrino-quark tensor operators to the neutrino magnetic moments by matching onto the chiral perturbation theory at low energies. These nonperturbative contributions can be compared to the perturbative ones, which are induced from one-loop mixing when performing the renormalization group evolutions from $μ=m_W$ down to $μ=2~\mathrm{GeV}$. We then constrain the dipole and tensor Wilson coefficients of the low-energy neutrino effective field theory (LNEFT) separately from the neutrino-electron scattering with Borexino data and coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) with COHERENT data to show the competition between these two contributions, at the renormalization scales $μ=2~\mathrm{GeV}$ and $μ=m_W$ in the $\bar{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme. In the neutrino-electron scattering, it is found that the nonperturbative contributions dominate for the coefficients involving up and down quarks, while they are expected to be of the same order of magnitude as the perturbative contributions for the coefficients involving strange quark. As for constraints in the CE$ν$NS, the tensor operators can contribute to the process through either direct or indirect way. As a result, the indirect contributions including nonperturbative and perturbative parts for all couplings become negligible in comparison with the direct ones. As the nonperturbative contributions crucially depend on the value of $c_T$, its inputs will affect the extraction of limits on the tensor LNEFT Wilson coefficients. We compute the upper bounds on these coefficients with $c_T$ quoting from the model and lattice estimates.

## Key claim

The QCD nonperturbative contributions from neutrino-quark tensor operators to neutrino magnetic moments, computed by matching onto chiral perturbation theory, dominate over perturbative contributions for Wilson coefficients involving up and down quarks in neutrino-electron scattering, while they are of the same order of magnitude for coefficients involving the strange quark; in coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, the indirect contributions (including both nonperturbative and perturbative parts) become negligible compared to direct tensor contributions.

## Method

The authors matched dimension-6 LNEFT tensor operators onto chiral perturbation theory operators with tensor external sources at the renormalization scale μ = 2 GeV, using the low-energy constant Λ₁ ∼ c_T F_π²/Λ_χ obtained from naive dimensional analysis. They then constrained the dipole and tensor Wilson coefficients separately using Borexino data for neutrino-electron scattering and COHERENT data for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, at both μ = 2 GeV and μ = m_W in the MSbar scheme, accounting for renormalization group running effects.

## Key numbers

- The nonperturbative contributions to dipole operators are of order L_{iγ} ∼ e (F_π²/Λ_χ) L_{iq}^{T,AA}, where F_π = 92.3(1) MeV and Λ_χ ∼ 4π F_π.
- The perturbative contributions are of order L_{iγ} ∼ e m_q/(16π²) L_{iq}^{T,AA}, obtained from one-loop mixing during RG running from μ = m_W down to μ = 2 GeV.
- The model estimate of the low-energy constant gives c_T ≈ −3.2, while the lattice input combined with χPT and RχT yields c_T ≈ −1.0(2), a disagreement of 11σ.
- The dipole coefficients at 0.5 MeV are related to those at 2 GeV by a factor of approximately 0.994, due to small QED running effects.

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Dekens (2018) for computing nonperturbative effects in charged lepton flavor violation (μ → eγ) to the neutrino sector, specifically for neutrino magnetic moments. The results enable improved constraints on tensor LNEFT Wilson coefficients from current experiments (Borexino, COHERENT) and highlight the sensitivity of these constraints to the value of the low-energy constant c_T, which currently has significant disagreement between model and lattice estimates.
