# Strong Isospin Breaking in the Nucleon and Delta Masses on the Lattice

> Strong isospin breaking in the spectrum of the nucleons and deltas can be studied in lattice QCD with the help of chiral perturbation theory. At leading order in the chiral expansion, the mass splittings between the proton and neutron and between the deltas are linear in the quark mass difference. The next-to-leading order contributions to these splittings vanish even away from the strong-isospin limit. Therefore, any non-linear quark mass dependence of these mass splittings is a signal of the next-to-next-to-leading order mass contributions, thus providing access to LECs at this order. We determine the mass splittings of the nucleons and deltas in two-flavor, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading order. We also derive expressions for the nucleon and delta masses in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory to the same order. The resulting mass expressions will be useful both for the extrapolation of lattice data on baryon masses, and for the study of strong isospin breaking.

# Strong Isospin Breaking in the Nucleon and Delta Masses on the Lattice

## Key claim
The next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions to the nucleon and delta mass splittings vanish even away from the strong-isospin limit, meaning any non-linear quark mass dependence in these splittings is a signal of next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) contributions, providing access to low-energy constants (LECs) at that order. The authors determine these mass splittings to NNLO in two-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), but note that the expressions are functions of quark masses rather than meson masses because in SU(2) away from the isospin limit, one cannot independently associate m_u and m_d to the pion masses.

## Method
The authors calculate the nucleon and delta masses to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in non-degenerate two-flavor heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT), using dimensional regularization with a modified minimal subtraction (MS-bar) scheme. They include contributions from pion loops, the delta-nucleon mass splitting Δ (treated as order q in the power counting), and higher-dimensional operators constrained by reparameterization invariance and chiral symmetry. They also derive expressions in partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQχPT) for two non-degenerate valence and sea quarks, presented in an appendix.

## Key numbers
- The pion decay constant f is taken as 132 MeV in the conventions used.
- The leading-order nucleon mass splitting is given by M_n - M_p = -2 α_M (m_d - m_u), where α_M is a LEC.
- The leading-order delta mass splitting is given by δM_T = (2/3) γ_M (m_d - m_u), where γ_M is a LEC.
- The NLO contribution to the nucleon mass includes a term (3/(16 π f^2)) g_A^2 m_π^3 from pion loops.
- The NLO contribution to the delta mass includes a term -(25 g_{ΔΔ}^2)/(432 π f^2) m_π^3 from pion loops.
- The NNLO nucleon mass expression contains coefficients C_π^B, F_π^B, and G_π^B tabulated for proton and neutron; for example, C_π^p = 2 α_M (2 m_u + m_d) and C_π^n = 2 α_M (m_u + 2 m_d).
- The NNLO delta mass expression contains coefficients C_π^T, F_π^T, and G_π^T tabulated for each delta state; for example, C_π^{Δ++} = 2 m_u + m_d and C_π^{Δ-} = m_u + 2 m_d.
- The delta equal spacing rule is first violated at NNLO, and only by the operator with coefficient t_2^M, where the violation coefficient δt^M is 2 m_d for M_{Δ-} - M_{Δ0}, m_u + m_d for M_{Δ0} - M_{Δ+}, and 2 m_u for M_{Δ+} - M_{Δ++}.

## Context
This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of baryon masses by Gasser (1974), Jenkins (1992), Bernard (1993), and others, providing the first complete NNLO calculation of nucleon and delta masses in non-degenerate two-flavor HBχPT. The vanishing of NLO contributions to the mass splittings was noted earlier by Gasser (1974) for nucleons. The results enable lattice QCD practitioners to extrapolate baryon mass data from unphysically large quark masses (where m_π ≳ 500 MeV has been typical) down to physical quark masses, and to determine LECs that are otherwise difficult to extract. The authors note that electromagnetic contributions to isospin breaking in baryon masses are of the same size as NNLO chiral effects, but lattice QCD can turn off quark electric charges, making these purely strong-isospin-breaking expressions applicable.
