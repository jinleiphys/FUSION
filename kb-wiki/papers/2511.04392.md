# Emergence of kaonium as a sharp resonance in photon-photon to meson-meson cross-sections

> We calculate the binding energies of the hypothetical mesonic atom, $K^+ K^-$ (kaonium), using the $K^+ K^- \to K^+ K^-$ elastic scattering amplitude. Our findings are in line with previously reported results, which involve solving an eigenvalue equation of the Kudryavtsev-Popov type. Using chiral perturbation theory, we show that kaonium manifests itself as a sharp resonance around 992 MeV accompanying $f_0 (980)$ or $a_0 (980)$ in cross-sections for processes $γγ\to π^0 π^0$ or $γγ\to π^0 η$. The latter process is particularly striking: the peak at the kaonium resonance energy is highly pronounced, with the ratio of the cross-sections $σ(γγ\to π^0 η) / σ(γγ\to π^0 π^0) \approx 9$. Due to the short lifetime of kaonium ($\sim 10^{-18}$ s) and its small decay width ($\sim 0.4$ keV), direct detection of this exotic atom poses a significant challenge and requires high experimental resolution. However, we show that once the formation of kaonium is considered in the cross-section, a better fit to the available experimental data is obtained.

# Emergence of kaonium as a sharp resonance in photon-photon to meson-meson cross-sections

## Key claim
Kaonium, the hypothetical K⁺K⁻ mesonic atom, manifests as a sharp resonance near 992 MeV in γγ → π⁰π⁰ and γγ → π⁰η cross-sections when isospin breaking due to the K⁰–K± mass difference and Coulomb interactions are included in chiral perturbation theory amplitudes; the effect improves the fit to existing experimental data, though direct detection remains challenging due to the atom's short lifetime (~10⁻¹⁸ s) and small decay width (~0.4 keV).

## Method
The authors compute K⁺K⁻ elastic scattering amplitudes using leading-order chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with on-shell potentials and coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations, then incorporate isospin breaking from the K⁰–K± mass difference and attractive Coulomb interactions via a matching procedure at a joining radius between strong-interaction and Coulomb-dominated regions. The resulting modified amplitudes are inserted into the γγ → π⁰π⁰ and γγ → π⁰η cross-section formulas, with the kaonium resonance appearing as a Breit-Wigner peak in the elastic T-matrix.

## Key numbers
- Kaonium 1s state mass: M_Ka. = 991.994 MeV (from 2m_K − 0.006252 MeV, with m_K± ≈ 493.677 MeV)
- Kaonium 1s state total width: Γ_Ka. = 0.000448 MeV (≈ 0.4 keV)
- Kaonium lifetime: ~10⁻¹⁸ s
- Ratio of cross-sections at kaonium resonance: σ(γγ → π⁰η) / σ(γγ → π⁰π⁰) ≈ 9
- Strong K⁺K⁻ scattering length: a_p = (2.688 − 1.896 i) m_K⁻¹
- Joining radius: d ≈ 2.2 m_K⁻¹
- Cutoff parameter: Λ = 1351 MeV (fixed to reproduce f₀(980) mass)
- Coulombic 1s binding energy (without strong interactions): −α²μ/2 ≈ −6.576 keV
- Kaonium-to-K⁺K⁻ coupling constant: g_Ka. = 62.598 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of kaonium by Klevansky and Lemmer (2004) and by Klevansky and collaborators (2011), which calculated binding energies and decay widths using the Kudryavtsev-Popov eigenvalue equation and local or nonlocal potentials from vector-meson exchange or ChPT. The present paper goes beyond those studies by incorporating kaonium formation directly into γγ → π⁰π⁰ and γγ → π⁰η cross-sections, building on the authors' earlier amplitude calculations (2025). The results enable indirect experimental searches for kaonium via photon-photon fusion processes, complementing recent indications of the 2p state from CMD-3 e⁺e⁻ → K⁺K⁻ data (2020) and production yield estimates for ultraperipheral LHC collisions (2025).
