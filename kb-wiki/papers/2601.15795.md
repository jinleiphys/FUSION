# Decay Effect on Near-Threshold Mass Scaling with Complex and Coupled-Channel Potentials

> We investigate the effect of decay channels on the near-threshold mass scaling by employing potential models. By varying the attractive strength of a square-well potential, we examine the pole trajectory associated with the transition of an $s$-wave bound state into a resonance state, incorporating decay-channel effects through both a single-channel complex potential model and a coupled-channel real potential model. As a result, we show that the pole of a quasibound state below the threshold is not continuously connected to that of a resonance state above the threshold. Furthermore, by comparing the results obtained from the single-channel and coupled-channel models, we clarify the correspondence between the pole trajectories in the two approaches.

# Decay Effect on Near-Threshold Mass Scaling with Complex and Coupled-Channel Potentials

## Key claim

The pole of a quasibound state below threshold is **not** continuously connected to the pole of a resonance state above threshold when decay channels are included; instead, the resonance pole originates from the quasivirtual state, and a kind of interchange of the relevant poles takes place as the attractive interaction is varied.

## Method

The authors solve the s-wave radial Schrodinger equation with a square-well potential of range b, using two approaches: a single-channel complex potential model (with strength V0 + iW0) and a two-channel real potential model (with channel coupling V12). Pole trajectories in the complex momentum plane are obtained by finding zeros of the Jost function (single-channel) or the determinant of the Jost matrix (coupled-channel) as the attractive strength is varied from -15 to -0.1 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}].

## Key numbers

- The critical strength for the n=2 bound state to appear is V0^{(2)} approximately -11.1 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}], while the n=1 bound state emerges at V0^{(1)} approximately -1.23 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}].
- For the single-channel model with W0 = -1 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}], pole trajectories are computed over the range -15 <= V0 <= -0.1 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}].
- For the coupled-channel model with V12 = 2 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}] and Delta1 = -15 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}], pole trajectories are computed over the same V22 range (-15 to -0.1 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}]).
- At V0 = -15 [b^{-2} mu^{-1}] (real potential), the n=2 bound state pole sits at p approximately 2i [b^{-1}] and its accompanying virtual-state pole at p approximately -4i [b^{-1}].

## Context

This work extends the established near-threshold mass scaling for s-wave states (Hyodo 2014, Hanhart 2014) by incorporating decay-channel effects through both complex and coupled-channel potentials. It builds on the Feshbach resonance formalism (Feshbach 1958, 1962) and recent chiral effective model analyses (Nishibuchi 2025) that discussed decay-channel influence on pole trajectories. The results clarify the correspondence between single-channel complex potential models and coupled-channel real potential models, showing that the sign of the imaginary part in the complex potential corresponds to the boundary condition of the eliminated decay channel (Kamiya 2022). This enables a unified understanding of pole trajectories in near-threshold exotic hadron systems.
