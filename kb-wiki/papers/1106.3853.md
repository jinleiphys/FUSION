# Spectra of helium clusters with up to six atoms using soft core potentials

> In this work we investigate small clusters of helium atoms using the hyperspherical harmonic basis. We consider systems with $A=2,3,4,5,6$ atoms with an inter-particle potential which does not present a strong repulsion at short distances. We use an attractive gaussian potential that reproduces the values of the dimer binding energy, the atom-atom scattering length, and the effective range obtained with one of the widely used He-He interactions, the LM2M2 potential. In systems with more than two atoms we consider a repulsive three-body force that, by construction, reproduces the trimer binding energy of the LM2M2 potential. With this model, consisting in the sum of a two- and three-body potential, we have calculated the spectrum of clusters formed by four, five and six helium atoms. We have found that these systems present two bound states, one deep and one shallow close to the threshold fixed by the energy of the $(A-1)$-atom system. Universal relations between the energies of the excited state of the $A$-atom system and the ground state energy of the $(A-1)$-atom system are extracted as well as the ratio between the ground state of the $A$-atom system and the ground state energy of the trimer.

## Key claim

Using a soft-core Gaussian two-body potential plus a repulsive three-body force tuned to reproduce the LM2M2 trimer ground state, the authors find that helium clusters with A=4,5,6 atoms each support exactly two bound states (one deep and one shallow near the (A-1)-atom threshold), but only when the three-body force range is sufficiently large; for smaller ranges the shallow state becomes unbound.

## Method

The authors model He-He interactions with an attractive Gaussian two-body potential fitted to the dimer binding energy, scattering length, and effective range of the LM2M2 potential, and add a repulsive Gaussian three-body force whose strength is fixed to reproduce the LM2M2 trimer ground state energy of 126.4 mK. They solve the A-body Schrodinger equation for A=2 through 6 using an unsymmetrized hyperspherical harmonic (HH) basis expansion up to grand angular momentum K=40 (A=4), K=24 (A=5), and K=22 (A=6), diagonalizing the Hamiltonian to extract L=0 eigenvalues and classify states by permutation symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Dimer binding energy from the Gaussian potential: E2b = -1.296 mK (LM2M2 value: -1.302 mK)
- Atom-atom scattering length from the Gaussian potential: a0 = 189.95 a.u. (LM2M2: 189.05 a.u.)
- Effective range from the Gaussian potential: r0 = 13.85 a.u. (LM2M2: 13.84 a.u.)
- Trimer ground state energy (with three-body force fixed to LM2M2): E3b(0) = 126.4 mK
- With two-body force only, trimer ground state: E3b(0) = 150.4 mK
- With three-body force (rho0=14 a.u.), ground state ratios: E4b(0)/E3b(0) = 4.5, E5b(0)/E3b(0) = 10.5, E6b(0)/E3b(0) = 18.5
- With three-body force (rho0=14 a.u.), shallow-state ratios: E4b(1)/E3b(0) = 1.020, E5b(1)/E4b(0) = 1.011, E6b(1)/E5b(0) = 1.018
- Without three-body force, ground state ratios: E4b(0)/E3b(0) = 5.01, E5b(0)/E3b(0) = 12.97, E6b(0)/E3b(0) = 25.4

## Context

This work extends the soft-core potential approach of Kievsky (2011) for the helium trimer to larger clusters, and provides a systematic comparison to LM2M2-potential calculations by Lewerenz (1997) and Blume (2000). The finding of exactly two bound states in the A=4,5,6 systems confirms the universal Efimov-inspired picture proposed by Hammer (2007) for the four-body case, and the ground-state energy ratios agree with the universal predictions of von Stecher (2009, 2010) and Deltuva (2010). The results enable future studies of universal ratios as the two-body scattering length approaches infinity, and suggest that a four-body force is not required at leading-order effective field theory for these systems.
