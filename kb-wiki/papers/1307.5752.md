# On the isospin 0 and 1 resonances from $蟺危$ photoproduction data

> Recently we presented a successful strategy to extract the position of the two $螞(1405)$ poles from experimental photoproduction data on the $纬p \to K^+ 蟺^0 危^0 $ reaction at Jefferson Lab. Following a similar strategy, we extend the previous method to incorporate also the isospin 1 component which allows us to consider in addition the experimental data on $纬p \to K^+ 蟺^\pm 危^\mp$. The idea is based on considering a production mechanism as model independent as possible and implementing the final state interaction of the final meson-baryon pair based on small modifications of the unitary chiral perturbation theory amplitudes. Good fits to the data are obtained with this procedure, by means of which we can also predict the cross sections for the $K^- p \to \bar K N$, $蟺危$, $蟺螞$ reactions for the different charge channels. Besides the two poles found for the $螞(1405)$ resonance, we discuss the possible existence of an isospin 1 resonance in the vicinity of the $\bar K N$ threshold.

## Key claim

The photoproduction data alone contain enough information to extract both the isospin 0 and isospin 1 meson-baryon amplitudes, but while two poles are found for the 螞(1405) resonance in the usual unphysical Riemann sheet, no clear pole is found for an isospin 1 state in that sheet; instead the isospin 1 amplitude shows a strong enhancement near the \(\bar K N\) threshold that behaves as a pronounced cusp, analogous to the \(a_0(980)\) resonance.

## Method

The authors fit photoproduction data from CLAS for the reactions \(\gamma p \to K^+ \pi^0 \Sigma^0\), \(\gamma p \to K^+ \pi^+ \Sigma^-\), and \(\gamma p \to K^+ \pi^- \Sigma^+\) using a model where the final state interaction of the meson-baryon pair is described by unitarized chiral perturbation theory amplitudes. The production mechanism is kept as model independent as possible by allowing energy-dependent complex coefficients for the initial production vertices, while the chiral unitary amplitudes are allowed to vary slightly through scaling parameters \(\alpha_i\) on the potential coefficients and \(\beta_i\) on the subtraction constants, iteratively fitted to the data.

## Key numbers

- The two \(\Lambda(1405)\) poles in the isospin 0 channel are found at \(1352-48i\) MeV and \(1419-29i\) MeV in the complex energy plane.
- The modulus of the couplings for the lower pole are \(|g_{\bar K N}| = 2.71\) and \(|g_{\pi\Sigma}| = 2.96\); for the higher pole \(|g_{\bar K N}| = 3.06\) and \(|g_{\pi\Sigma}| = 1.96\).
- The fit achieves \(\chi^2/dof = 2.1\) after the iterative procedure.
- The predicted kaonic hydrogen shift and width from the fitted parameters are \(\Delta E - i\Gamma/2 = (194 \pm 4) - i(301 \pm 9)\) eV, compared to the SIDDHARTA experimental value of \((283 \pm 42) - i(271 \pm 55)\) eV.

## Context

This work extends the method of Roca and Oset (2013) that previously extracted the two \(\Lambda(1405)\) poles from only the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \pi^0 \Sigma^0\) data, which filters isospin 0, by now incorporating the isospin 1 component from the charged \(\pi\Sigma\) channels. The analysis contradicts the assumption that Breit-Wigner shapes are suitable for these resonances, as done in the fit by Schumacher and the CLAS collaboration (2013), and instead shows that the unitarized coupled-channel approach is necessary. The results enable predictions for \(K^- p \to \bar K N\), \(\pi\Sigma\), and \(\pi\Lambda\) cross sections without fitting those data, and the isospin 1 amplitude's cusp-like structure near the \(\bar K N\) threshold suggests effects similar to the \(a_0(980)\) resonance, with implications for future experiments searching for a possible isospin 1 state.
