# Gravitational form factors of the nucleon and one pion graviproduction in chiral EFT

> In the framework of chiral effective field theory of delta resonances, nucleons and pions interacting with background gravitational field we calculate the gravitational form factors of the nucleon up to fourth order in the small scale expansion and obtain the long-range behavior of the corresponding contributions to the energy, spin, pressure and shear force distributions. By comparing nucleon gravitational form factors with and without delta contributions we conclude that explicit inclusion of deltas plays an important role. Next we explore the Lorentz structure of the $N \mapsto N π$ transition matrix element of the conserved symmetric energy-momentum tensor and introduce its parametrization in terms of twelve transition form factors. We use the chiral effective field theory to calculate the tree-order contributions to the gravitational transition form factors of the pion graviproduction off the nucleon up to third order.

## Key claim
The paper calculates nucleon gravitational form factors (GFFs) up to fourth order in the small-scale expansion within chiral effective field theory that explicitly includes delta resonances, and finds that the inclusion of deltas is important, particularly for the D-term, where differences between theories with and without deltas reach 62% at momentum transfer \(Q^2 = M_\pi^2\), well beyond the estimated 0.02% truncation error.

## Method
The author extends the chiral effective Lagrangian for pions, nucleons, and delta resonances to curved spacetime, including terms up to fourth chiral order, and derives the energy-momentum tensor via variation with respect to the metric or vielbein fields. One-loop corrections to the nucleon GFFs are computed using the small-scale expansion (epsilon counting) and renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme, with the renormalization scale set to the nucleon mass. The Lorentz structure of the \(N \to N\pi\) transition matrix element of the conserved symmetric energy-momentum tensor is parametrized in terms of twelve independent transition form factors, and tree-order contributions to these gravitational transition form factors are calculated up to third chiral order.

## Key numbers
- D-term at zero momentum transfer: \(D(0) = -0.2\) (taken from Gegelia (2021) as a model-independent bound, used to fix the low-energy constant \(c_8\)).
- At \(Q^2 = M_\pi^2 = 0.019\ \text{GeV}^2\), the full GFFs with deltas are: \(A^\Delta = 1.0086\), \(J^\Delta = 0.5001\), \(D^\Delta = 0.45\); without deltas: \(A = 1.0085\), \(J = 0.5005\), \(D = 0.17\).
- Non-analytic parts at \(Q^2 = M_\pi^2\): \(A^\Delta = 1.8 \times 10^{-3}\), \(A = 2.5 \times 10^{-3}\); \(J^\Delta = -27 \times 10^{-3}\), \(J = -26 \times 10^{-3}\); \(D^\Delta = -0.28\), \(D = -0.24\).
- Fitted low-energy constants (with deltas): \(c_8^\Delta = -0.97\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(c_9^\Delta = -1.29\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\), \(\tilde d_{g4}^\Delta = 0.44\ \text{GeV}^{-3}\), \(x_1^\Delta = 1\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\), \(y_1^\Delta = -7.2\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), \(y_2^\Delta = 12.2\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- Input parameters: \(g_{\pi N\Delta} = 1.35\), \(g_A = 1.289\), \(m_N = 0.93827\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_\Delta = 1.232\ \text{GeV}\), \(M_\pi = 0.13957\ \text{GeV}\), \(F_\pi = 0.0922\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT calculation of nucleon GFFs by Alharazin (2020), which included only pions and nucleons, by explicitly incorporating delta resonances as dynamical degrees of freedom. The delta contributions are shown to be significant, especially for the D-term and for the non-analytic parts of all GFFs, which affect the long-range behavior of energy, spin, pressure, and shear force spatial densities. The parametrization of the one-pion graviproduction (OPGP) matrix element in terms of twelve transition form factors extends the threshold analysis of Polyakov (2020) and Alharazin (2020) to general kinematics, providing a framework for extracting the new low-energy constants from planned CLAS12 measurements at JLab.
