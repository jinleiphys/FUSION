# Covariant kinetic theory for effective fugacity quasi particle model and first order transport coefficients for hot QCD matter

> An effective relativistic kinetic theory has been constructed for an interacting system of quarks, anti-quarks and gluons within a quasi-particle description of hot QCD medium at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, where the interactions are encoded in the gluon and quark effective fugacities with non-trivial energy dispersions. The local conservations of stress-energy tensor and number current require the introduction of a mean field term in the transport equation which produces non-vanishing contribution to the first order transport coefficients. Such contribution has been observed to be significant for the temperatures which are closer to the QCD transition tem- perature, however, induces negligible contributions beyond a few times the transition temperature. As an implication, impact of the mean field contribution on the the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity, bulk viscosity and thermal conductivity of a hot QCD medium in the presence of binary, elastic collisions among the constituents, has been investigated. Visible effects have been observed for the temperature regime closer to the QCD transition temperature.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a covariant kinetic theory for hot QCD matter using the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM), where a mean field term must be introduced in the transport equation to satisfy local conservation of stress-energy tensor and number current. This mean field contribution significantly affects first-order transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, thermal conductivity) for temperatures near the QCD transition temperature, but becomes negligible beyond a few times that temperature.

## Method

The authors map hot QCD equation-of-state effects onto a system of quasi-gluons and quasi-quarks/antiquarks with temperature-dependent effective fugacities and modified energy dispersions, where the quasi-particle four-momentum is \(\tilde{p}^{\mu}=p^{\mu}+\delta\omega\,u^{\mu}\) with \(\delta\omega=T^2\partial_T\ln z_{g,q}\). They derive the relativistic transport equation including a mean field force term \(F^{i}=-\partial_{\mu}\{\delta\omega\,u^{\mu}u^{i}\}\) and solve it using the Chapman-Enskog method with a relaxation-time approximation for binary, elastic \(2\rightarrow2\) collisions among the partonic constituents. The first-order transport coefficients are then obtained by decomposing the viscous pressure tensor and heat flow into shear, bulk, and thermal conductivity contributions, with additional terms proportional to \(\delta\omega\) arising from the quasi-particle excitations.

## Context

This work extends the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) developed by Chandra and collaborators (2010-2012) by constructing a fully covariant kinetic theory with proper conservation laws, addressing a gap in previous quasi-particle approaches. The mean field treatment builds on earlier work by Jeon, Dusling, and Gorenstein (2008-2010) who identified the need for such terms in effective mass quasi-particle models for hot QCD. The formalism enables consistent calculation of first-order transport coefficients for quark-gluon plasma near the QCD transition temperature, and provides a foundation for extending dissipative hydrodynamics to second and third order within the EQPM framework.
