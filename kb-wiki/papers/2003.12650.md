# Lensing Mechanism Meets Small-$x$ Physics: Single Transverse Spin Asymmetry in $p^{\uparrow}+p$ and $p^{\uparrow}+A$ Collisions

> We calculate the single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) in polarized proton-proton ($p^{\uparrow}+p$) and polarized proton-nucleus ($p^{\uparrow}+A$) collisions ($A_N$) generated by a partonic lensing mechanism. The polarized proton is considered in the quark-diquark model while its interaction with the unpolarized target is calculated using the small-$x$/saturation approach, which includes multiple rescatterings and small-$x$ evolution. The phase required for the asymmetry is caused by a final-state gluon exchange between the quark and diquark, as is standard in the lensing mechanism of Brodsky, Hwang and Schmidt. Our calculation combines the lensing mechanism with small-$x$ physics in the saturation framework. The expression we obtain for the asymmetry $A_N$ of the produced quarks has the following properties: (i) The asymmetry is generated by the dominant elastic scattering contribution and $1/N_c^2$ suppressed inelastic contribution (with $N_c$ the number of quark colors); (ii) The asymmetry grows or oscillates with the produced quark's transverse momentum $p_T$ until the momentum reaches the saturation scale $Q_s$, and then only falls off as $1/p_T$ for larger momenta; (iii) The asymmetry decreases with increasing atomic number $A$ of the target for $p_T$ below or near $Q_s$, but is independent of $A$ for $p_T$ significantly above $Q_s$. We discuss how these properties may be qualitatively consistent with the data on $A_N$ published by the PHENIX collaboration and with the preliminary data on $A_N$ reported by the STAR collaboration.

## Key claim

The single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) in polarized proton-proton and polarized proton-nucleus collisions can be generated by combining the partonic lensing mechanism (final-state gluon exchange between the quark and diquark in the polarized proton) with the small-x/saturation framework for the target, yielding an asymmetry that grows or oscillates with transverse momentum \(p_T\) up to the saturation scale \(Q_s\) and then falls off as \(1/p_T\) at higher momenta, with the elastic scattering contribution dominating over the inelastic contribution.

## Method

The authors model the polarized proton using a quark-diquark wave function and describe its interaction with the unpolarized target (proton or nucleus) via eikonal Wilson lines in the saturation framework, which resums multiple rescatterings and small-x evolution. The STSA phase is generated by a final-state gluon exchange between the quark and diquark after the target interaction, following the lensing mechanism of Brodsky, Hwang and Schmidt (2002). The calculation is performed in light-cone perturbation theory, keeping only the imaginary part of the energy denominator for the intermediate quark-diquark state, and the resulting cross section is expressed in terms of dipole S-matrices and modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

* The asymmetry \(A_N\) falls off as \(1/p_T\) for transverse momenta \(p_T \gg N_c \, Q_s\), where \(N_c\) is the number of quark colors and \(Q_s\) is the saturation scale.
* The inelastic contribution to the asymmetry is suppressed by a factor of \(1/N_c^2\) relative to the dominant elastic contribution.
* For \(p_T \lesssim Q_s\), the asymmetry decreases with increasing atomic number \(A\) of the target; for \(p_T \gg N_c \, Q_s\), the asymmetry becomes approximately independent of \(A\).
* The asymmetry grows or oscillates with \(p_T\) for \(p_T \ll Q_s\) before the \(1/p_T\) falloff sets in at higher momenta.

## Context

This work extends the earlier odderon-based STSA calculation by Kovchegov (2012) to include the lensing mechanism of Brodsky, Hwang and Schmidt (2002), completing the order-\(\alpha_s^2\) calculation in the saturation power counting. The results are qualitatively compared to data from the PHENIX collaboration (Aidala 2019) and preliminary STAR collaboration data (Dilks 2016), suggesting that the lensing mechanism combined with small-x dynamics may describe the observed \(A_N\) trends, including the dominance of elastic events and the \(A\)-dependence. Future work should incorporate fragmentation effects and perform detailed phenomenological comparisons.
