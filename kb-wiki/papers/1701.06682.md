# Medium effects on pion production in heavy ion collisions

> Within the framework of the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model based on the relativistic nonlinear NL$ρ$ interaction, we study pion in-medium effects on the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in Au+Au collisions at the energy of $E/A=400~\mathrm{MeV}$. These effects include the isospin-dependent pion s-wave and p-wave potentials, which are taken from calculations based on the chiral perturbation theory and the $Δ$-hole model, respectively. We find that the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in this collision is suppressed by the pion s-wave potential but enhanced by the p-wave potential, with a net effect of a significantly suppressed $π^-/π^+$ ratio. Including also the in-medium threshold effects on $Δ$ resonance production and decay and using a nuclear symmetry energy with a slope parameter $L=59~\mathrm{MeV}$ by reducing the coupling of isovector-vector $ρ$ meson to nucleon, our result is in good agreement with measured $π^-/π^+$ ratio from the FOPI Collaboration. We further investigate the pion in-medium effects on the ratio of charged pions as a function of their kinetic energies.

# Medium effects on pion production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The pion in-medium effects (s-wave and p-wave potentials) produce a net suppression of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in Au+Au collisions at E/A=400 MeV, with the s-wave potential reducing and the p-wave potential enhancing the ratio; when combined with the threshold effect and a softer symmetry energy (slope parameter L=59 MeV), the model agrees with FOPI experimental data, but this agreement requires reducing the ρ meson coupling constant from f_ρ=0.95 fm² to f_ρ=0.43 fm².

## Method

The authors extended the relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model based on the nonlinear NLρ interaction to include isospin-dependent pion s-wave potentials from chiral perturbation theory and p-wave potentials from the Δ-hole model. They incorporated these potentials into the pion dispersion relation, Δ resonance decay widths, pion absorption cross sections, and the production/absorption thresholds of Δ resonances in Au+Au collisions at E/A=400 MeV, then compared the resulting π⁻/π⁺ ratios with FOPI Collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio is suppressed by approximately 10% when both s-wave and p-wave pion potentials are included together (net effect).
- The threshold effect alone increases the π⁻/π⁺ ratio by about 20%.
- The slope parameter of nuclear symmetry energy was reduced from L=84 MeV to L=59 MeV by reducing the ρ meson coupling from f_ρ=0.95 fm² to f_ρ=0.43 fm².
- At a pion kinetic energy of E_kin=250 MeV in the center-of-mass frame, including both threshold effect and pion potentials increases the π⁻/π⁺ ratio by a factor of 2.
- The free Δ resonance decay width is Γ_0=0.12 GeV for a Δ mass of m_0=1.232 GeV.
- The minimum mass for Δ production is evaluated as m_Δ,min* = m_N* + m_π* + Σ⁰_N − Σ⁰_Δ, with the s-wave potential effect included via m_π*.

## Context

This work extends the isospin-dependent RVUU model of Son (2015), which included threshold effects but not pion in-medium effects, to incorporate both s-wave and p-wave pion potentials. The results are qualitatively consistent with thermal model predictions by Xu (2010, 2013) showing that pion in-medium effects on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio are comparable to symmetry energy effects. The finding that a softer symmetry energy (L=59 MeV) reduces the π⁻/π⁺ ratio when threshold effects are included contradicts earlier transport model predictions by Xiao (2009), Feng (2010), and Xie (2013), but this reversal is attributed to the threshold effect identified by Son (2015). The work enables more reliable extraction of the high-density symmetry energy from FOPI pion ratio data by accounting for previously neglected medium modifications.
