# A New Model of Intranuclear Neutron-Antineutron Transformations in ${}^{16}_{8}$O

> There has been much work in recent years pertaining to viability studies for the intranuclear observation of neutron-antineutron transformations. These studies begin firstly with the design and implementation of an event generator for the simulation of this rare process, where one hopes to retain as much of the underlying nuclear physics as possible in the initial state, and then studying how these effects may perturb the final state observable particles for detector efficiency studies following simulated reconstruction. There have been several searches for intranuclear neutron-antineutron transformations, primarily utilizing the ${}^{16}_{8}$O nucleus, and completed within large underground water Cherenkov detectors such as Super-Kamiokande. The latest iteration of a generator is presented here for use in such an experiment. This generator includes several new features, including a new radial (position) annihilation probability distribution and related intranuclear suppression factor for ${}^{16}_{8}$O, as well as a highly general, modern nuclear multifragmentation model with photonic de-excitations. The latter of these may allow for improved identification of the signal using large underground detectors such as Super-Kamiokande and the future Hyper-Kamiokande, potentially increasing the overall signal efficiencies of these rare searches. However, it should be noted that certain fast photonic de-excitations may be washed out by $π^0$ decays to photons. These new features implemented in these $\bar{n}$\isotope[15][8]{O} simulations increase the overall physical realism of the model, and are easily portable to other future searches such as to extranuclear $\bar{n}{}^{12}_{6}$C for the ESS NNBAR experiment, as well as intranuclear $\bar{n}{}^{39}_{18}$Ar used in DUNE.

## Key claim

This paper presents an updated Monte Carlo event generator for intranuclear neutron-antineutron transformations in oxygen-16 that includes a new radial annihilation probability distribution and a statistical multifragmentation model with photonic de-excitations; however, certain fast photonic de-excitations may be washed out by neutral pion decays to photons, potentially limiting their utility for signal identification in large water Cherenkov detectors.

## Method

The authors compute the antineutron radial wave function for each neutron shell in oxygen-16 by solving an inhomogeneous Schrodinger equation with a complex optical potential derived from antiproton-nucleus scattering data, using an effective scattering length of b = 1.3 + i 1.9 fm. The resulting annihilation position distribution feeds into an intranuclear cascade model that propagates annihilation mesons through a local Fermi gas description of the nucleus divided into seven concentric density zones. The residual excited nucleus is then evolved using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model, which handles multifragmentation at high excitation energies, compound nucleus evaporation and fission at low energies, and Fermi break-up for light fragments, with subsequent gamma de-excitations of the fragments.

## Key numbers

- Suppression factor for oxygen-16: T_R = 0.65 x 10^23 s^-1, comparable to the estimate of 0.65 x 10^23 s^-1 from Friedman (2008) and 0.8 x 10^23 s^-1 from Dover (1982)
- Simulated average pion multiplicity for antiproton-proton annihilation: M(pi) = 4.95, compared to experimental values of 4.98 +/- 0.35 and 4.94 +/- 0.14
- Simulated average pion multiplicity for antiproton-carbon-12 annihilation: M(pi) = 4.60, compared to experimental value of 4.57 +/- 0.15
- Simulated total energy carried by pions and photons in antiproton-carbon-12 annihilation: 1762 MeV, compared to experimental value of 1758 +/- 59 MeV
- Optical potential parameters used in the second Dover model: U_R = 107 MeV, U_I = 222 MeV, R = 2.388 fm, a = 0.523 fm
- SMM parameters: W_0 = 16 MeV (binding energy of infinite nuclear matter), B_0 = 18 MeV (surface coefficient), T_c = 18 MeV (critical temperature), gamma = 25 MeV (symmetry energy parameter), r_0 = 1.17 fm

## Context

This work extends the intranuclear neutron-antineutron simulation framework previously developed by Golubeva (2018) and Barrow (2019) for other nuclei, and directly contrasts with the Super-Kamiokande Monte Carlo generator described by Abe (2020), which assumes uniform annihilation probability over the nuclear volume rather than the peripheral distribution found here. The new generator is designed for use in Super-Kamiokande and the future Hyper-Kamiokande experiments, and the authors note it is portable to other searches including extranuclear antineutron-carbon-12 for the ESS NNBAR experiment and intranuclear antineutron-argon-39 for DUNE.
