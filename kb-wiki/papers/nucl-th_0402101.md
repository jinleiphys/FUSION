# Cronin effect and geometrical shadowing in d+Au collisions: pQCD vs. CGC

> Multiple initial state parton interactions in p(d)+A collisions are calculated in a Glauber-Eikonal formalism, which incorporates the competing pattern of low-pT suppression due geometrical shadowing, and a moderate-pT Cronin enhancement of hadron spectra. Dynamical shadowing effects, which are not included in the computation, may be extracted by comparing experimental data to the baseline provided by the Glauber-Eikonal model. Data for neutral pion production at midrapidity show absence of dynamical shadowing in the RHIC energy range, sqrt(s)~20-200 GeV. Recent preliminary data at forward rapidity are addressed, and their interpretation discussed.

# Cronin effect and geometrical shadowing in d+Au collisions: pQCD vs. CGC

## Key claim
Geometrical shadowing from unitarity constraints in a Glauber-Eikonal formalism can explain the observed low-pT suppression and moderate-pT Cronin enhancement in d+Au collisions at RHIC midrapidity, without requiring dynamical shadowing from Color Glass Condensate effects. However, at forward rapidity the Glauber-Eikonal model overestimates the Cronin ratio, suggesting that dynamical shadowing may be needed in the x ~ 10^-3 to 10^-2 regime, though the evidence is not yet conclusive.

## Method
The authors compute multiple initial-state parton interactions in p(d)+A collisions using a Glauber-Eikonal formalism, where sequential multiple partonic collisions are calculated in pQCD with unitarity preserved through an exponential factor representing the probability of no further semihard scatterings. The model is first fit to p+p collision data at the relevant energy and rapidity by adjusting two free parameters (p0, a gluon mass regulator, and the K-factor), then applied to p+A collisions without introducing additional parameters. The Cronin ratio R_pA is computed as the ratio of the d+Au hadron spectrum to the binary-scaled p+p spectrum at the average impact parameter of the centrality bin.

## Key numbers
- At Fermilab energies (midrapidity): p0 = 0.7 ± 0.1 GeV, K = 1.07 ± 0.02
- At RHIC energies (midrapidity): p0 = 1.0 ± 0.1 GeV, K = 0.99 ± 0.03
- Fixed intrinsic transverse momentum <kT^2> = 0.52 GeV^2 used to improve p+p spectrum fits in the 1-5 GeV range
- The opacity parameter at forward rapidity: χ0 = 0.95, with a test using triple that value still overestimating the BRAHMS data
- The Glauber-Eikonal model reproduces PHENIX midrapidity data and the 0-20%/60-88% centrality ratio at RHIC within theoretical errors

## Context
This work extends the Glauber-Eikonal approach of Accardi and Gyulassy (2003) and earlier multiple-scattering models to provide a baseline for distinguishing geometrical shadowing from dynamical shadowing predicted by Color Glass Condensate models (CGC). The authors show that at RHIC midrapidity (sqrt(s) ~ 20-200 GeV), the data are consistent with purely geometrical effects, contradicting CGC expectations of additional suppression in central collisions. At forward rapidity, the model overestimates preliminary BRAHMS data, leaving open the possibility of dynamical shadowing; the authors identify the pT = 3-6 GeV region at forward rapidity as optimal for future study, and note that current CGC scenarios overestimate absolute cross sections by an order of magnitude compared to data.
