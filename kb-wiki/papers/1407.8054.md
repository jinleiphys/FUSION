# Constraining the density slope of nuclear symmetry energy at subsaturation densities using electric dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb

> Nuclear structure observables usually most effectively probe the properties of nuclear matter at subsaturation densities rather than at saturation density. We demonstrate that the electric dipole polarizibility $α_ {\text{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb is sensitive to both the magnitude $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ_{\text{c}})$ and density slope $L(ρ_{\text{c}})$ of the symmetry energy at a subsaturation cross density $ρ_{\text{c}} = 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$. Using the experimental data of $α_ {\text{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb from RCNP and the recent accurate constraint of $E_{\text{sym}}(ρ_{\text{c}})$ from the binding energy difference of heavy isotope pairs, we extract a value of $L(ρ_{\text{c}}) = 47.3 \pm 7.8$ MeV. The implication of the present constraint of $L(ρ_{\text{c}})$ to the symmetry energy at saturation density, the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb and the core-crust transition density in neutron stars is discussed.

# Constraining the density slope of nuclear symmetry energy at subsaturation densities using electric dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb

## Key claim
The electric dipole polarizability $\alpha_{\text{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb is sensitive to both the magnitude $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{c}})$ and density slope $L(\rho_{\text{c}})$ of the symmetry energy at the subsaturation cross density $\rho_{\text{c}} = 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$, and using the experimental $\alpha_{\text{D}}$ from RCNP together with a previously constrained $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{c}})$ from binding energy differences of heavy isotope pairs, the authors extract $L(\rho_{\text{c}}) = 47.3 \pm 7.8$ MeV. This result is conditional on the use of the standard Skyrme-Hartree-Fock energy density functional and the assumption that correlations with other macroscopic quantities are weak.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) plus random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations of the electric dipole polarizability $\alpha_{\text{D}}$ in $^{208}$Pb, using the MSL0 Skyrme interaction as a baseline. They varied individual macroscopic nuclear matter parameters (including $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_r)$ and $L(\rho_r)$) within their empirical ranges to isolate correlations, then fixed $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{c}})$ to the precise value $26.65 \pm 0.20$ MeV from Zhang (2013) and optimized the remaining 8 macroscopic parameters against experimental binding energies, charge radii, and breathing mode energies to obtain a refined constraint on $L(\rho_{\text{c}})$.

## Key numbers
- $L(\rho_{\text{c}}) = 47.3 \pm 7.8$ MeV at $\rho_{\text{c}} = 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$, extracted from the experimental $\alpha_{\text{D}} = 20.1 \pm 0.6$ fm$^3$ in $^{208}$Pb from RCNP and the constraint $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{c}}) = 26.65 \pm 0.20$ MeV.
- $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0) = 32.7 \pm 1.7$ MeV and $L(\rho_0) = 47.1 \pm 17.7$ MeV at saturation density $\rho_0 \sim 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, derived from the subsaturation constraint.
- Neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.176 \pm 0.027$ fm.
- Core-crust transition density in neutron stars: $\rho_t = 0.084 \pm 0.009$ fm$^{-3}$.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Roca-Maza (2013), who correlated $\alpha_{\text{D}}$ with $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0)$ and $L(\rho_0)$ but suffered from large uncertainties in $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0)$. By instead using the precisely constrained $E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_{\text{c}})$ from Zhang (2013), the authors obtain a tighter constraint on $L(\rho_{\text{c}})$ that agrees remarkably well with the independent constraint $L(\rho_{\text{c}}) = 46.0 \pm 4.5$ MeV from neutron skin data of Sn isotopes (Zhang 2013). The results enable improved predictions for the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb and the core-crust transition density in neutron stars, and are consistent with constraints from neutron star mass-radius observations (Steiner 2012) and pygmy dipole resonance analyses (Klimkiewicz 2007, Carbone 2010).
