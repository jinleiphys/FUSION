# Non-Extensive Behaviour of the QCD Strong Coupling Constant

> The compatibility of theoretically calculated values for the strong coupling constant,through the renormalization group approach with experimental data is studied. There exist considerable divergence in-between theoretical and experimental results at low energies, which can not be explained by thermal field theory and considering chemical potential. Such great deviation can be treated successfully by considering the q-generalized statistical effects through adding a q-nonextensive parameter in the fitting of theoretical results on experimental data.

# Non-Extensive Behaviour of the QCD Strong Coupling Constant

## Key claim
Standard perturbative QCD and thermal field theory both fail to reproduce the experimental running of the strong coupling at low energies (below about 3 GeV), but introducing a single q-nonextensive parameter from Tsallis statistics into the thermal-field-theory calculation yields a fit that successfully describes the low-energy data, with the caveat that the temperature is treated as a constant (51 MeV) and the chemical potential is absorbed into the renormalization scale.

## Method
The authors computed the running strong coupling α_s(Q) at one-, three-, and five-loop order in zero-temperature perturbative QCD, and at two- and three-hard-thermal-loop order in finite-temperature QCD, using the renormalization group equation with β-functions up to five loops. They then modified the finite-temperature two- and three-loop expressions by multiplying them with a q-dependent factor derived from Tsallis non-extensive statistics (Eq. 4 in the paper), and performed a χ² minimization against experimental data from CMS, D0, ATLAS, H1, and the Particle Data Group to determine the optimal value of the nonextensive parameter q.

## Key numbers
- World-average reference value: α_s(M_Z) = 0.1181 ± 0.0011 (from Particle Data Group, 2019).
- Best-fit α_s(M_Z) from global fit: 0.11800 (three-loop and five-loop zero-temperature), 0.11805 (three-loop thermal).
- χ² for zero-temperature calculations: one-loop χ² = 54.337, three-loop χ² = 24.299, five-loop χ² = 23.782.
- χ² for three-loop thermal QCD (with α_s(1.5 GeV)=0.326 as initial condition): χ² = 45.147.
- χ² for three-loop thermal QCD with best-fit α_s(M_Z)=0.11805: χ² = 27.903.
- Best-fit nonextensive parameter for two-loop thermal QCD: q = 1.2412, with χ² = 12.24.
- Best-fit nonextensive parameter for three-loop thermal QCD: q = 1.3181, with χ² = 11.93.
- Λ_MSbar extracted from three-loop thermal fit: 337 ± 8 MeV (compared to 316 MeV reported in Andersen (2015) for three-loop).
- Temperature values used (from fits to ABCLOS and CDHS parton distribution data): T = 55 MeV at Q = 2.5 GeV, 58 MeV at 4 GeV, 54 MeV at 7 GeV, 51 MeV at 20–60 GeV, 47.6 MeV at 80 GeV; the authors fixed T = 51 MeV for the q-generalized calculation.

## Context
This work extends the application of Tsallis non-extensive statistics (Tsallis, 1988, 1998) to the QCD running coupling, following earlier successes of the q-generalized approach in describing transverse momentum distributions in e⁺e⁻ → hadrons (ee reaction) and p–p collisions at the LHC (pp1, pp2). It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between perturbative QCD predictions and experimental α_s data at low energies, which persists even when thermal field theory (Kapusta, 1989) and chemical potential are included. The results enable future work on non-extensive effects in parton distribution functions, jet evolution, and spin statistics in QCD, as the authors note that the nonextensivity reflected in α_s likely affects many statistical aspects of hadronic systems.
