# Quark Matter in the NJL Model with a Vector Interaction and the Structure of Hybrid Stars

> The properties of hadron-quark hybrid stars are studied when the quark phase is described in terms of a local SU(3) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model taking into account the contribution of the vector and axial-vector interaction between the quarks, and the hadronic phase, in the relativistic mean field (RMF) model. For different values of the vector coupling constant $G_V$, the equations of state of the quark matter are calculated and the parameters of the hadron-quark phase transition are determined under the assumption that the phase transition takes place in accordance with Maxwell's construction. It is shown that for a larger vector coupling constant, the equation of state of the quark matter will be "stiffer" and the coexistence pressure $P_0$ of the phases will be greater. Using the resulting hybrid equations of state, the TOV equations are integrated numerically and the mass and radius of the compact star are determined for different values of the central pressure $P_c$. It is shown that when $G_V$ is larger, the maximum mass of the compact star will be larger and thereby, the radius of the configuration with maximum mass will be smaller. Questions of the stability of hybrid stars are also discussed. It is shown that in terms of the model examined here, for all values of the vector coupling constant, a hybrid star with an infinitely small quark core is stable. These results are compared with recent measurements of the mass and radius of the pulsars PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, carried out at the International Space Station with the NASA's Neutron star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) X-ray telescope. A comparison of the theoretical results with observational data does not exclude the possibility of quark deconfinement in the interiors of compact stars.

# Quark Matter in the NJL Model with a Vector Interaction and the Structure of Hybrid Stars

## Key claim
The paper shows that including a vector interaction channel in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter produces stiffer equations of state and larger maximum masses for hybrid stars, but only for central pressures very close to the phase coexistence pressure does the hybrid star satisfy the stability condition dM/d epsilon_c > 0; for larger quark cores the configurations are statistically unstable, though they may be dynamically stable if phase conversion is slow.

## Method
The hadronic phase was described using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model including sigma, omega, rho, and delta meson fields, while the quark phase was described by a local SU(3) NJL model with scalar, pseudoscalar, Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft six-quark, and vector/axial-vector interaction terms. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated via Maxwell construction (constant pressure, discontinuous baryon density), and the resulting hybrid equations of state were used to integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations numerically for different values of the vector coupling constant G_V relative to the scalar coupling G_S.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- NJL model parameters: m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV, Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G_S = 1.835/Lambda^2, K = 12.36/Lambda^5
- For G_V/G_S = 0: coexistence pressure P_0 = 150.2 MeV/fm^3, hadronic energy density epsilon_H0 = 646.88 MeV/fm^3, quark energy density epsilon_Q0 = 958.56 MeV/fm^3, baryon densities n_H0 = 0.5841 fm^{-3} and n_Q0 = 0.8128 fm^{-3}, baryon chemical potential mu_B0 = 1364.4 MeV
- For G_V/G_S = 0.6: P_0 = 659.5 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_H0 = 1582.41 MeV/fm^3, epsilon_Q0 = 1680.02 MeV/fm^3, n_H0 = 1.1495 fm^{-3}, n_Q0 = 1.5420 fm^{-3}, mu_B0 = 1950.8 MeV
- Jump parameter lambda = epsilon_Q0/(epsilon_H0 + P_0) values: 1.20 (G_V=0), 1.18 (G_V=0.2 G_S), 0.98 (G_V=0.4 G_S), 0.75 (G_V=0.6 G_S); all satisfy lambda < 3/2
- Observational comparison: PSR J0030+0451 mass M = 1.44^{+0.15}_{-0.14} M_sun, radius R = 13.02^{+1.24}_{-1.06} km; PSR J0740+6620 mass M = 2.08^{+0.07}_{-0.07} M_sun, radius R = 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5} km

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Alaverdyan, 2021) of hybrid stars using the NJL model without vector interactions, and now includes vector and axial-vector channels. It contrasts with earlier studies using the MIT bag model (referenced as Refs. 6-17 in the paper) and builds on the RMF model with delta mesons from Ref. 11. The results enable comparison with NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the finding that all hybrid stars with infinitely small quark cores are stable (lambda < 3/2) supports the possibility of quark deconfinement in compact star interiors, though the stability of larger quark cores requires further analysis of slow phase conversion dynamics as discussed in Refs. 35, 47, 48, and 49.
