# Light Nuclei from Lattice QCD: Spectrum, Structure and Reactions

> Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (LQCD) studies of light nuclei have entered an era when first results on structure and reaction properties of light nuclei have emerged in recent years, complementing existing results on their lowest-lying spectra. Although in these preliminary studies the quark masses are still set to larger than the physical values, a few results at the physical point can still be deduced from simple extrapolations in the quark masses. The progress paves the road towards obtaining several important quantities in nuclear physics, such as nuclear forces and nuclear matrix elements relevant for pp fusion, single and double-beta decay processes, neutrino-nucleus scattering, searches for CP violation, nuclear response in direct dark-matter detection experiments, as well as gluonic structure of nuclei for an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) program. Some of the recent developments, the results obtained, and the outlook of the field will be briefly reviewed in this talk, with a focus on results obtained by the Nuclear Physics From LQCD (NPLQCD) collaboration.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD studies of light nuclei have produced first results on structure and reaction properties, but nearly all calculations are performed at larger-than-physical values of the quark masses; a few results at the physical point can be deduced from simple extrapolations in the quark masses.

## Method

The NPLQCD collaboration performed Monte Carlo sampling of quantum fields in QCD on a finite discretized Euclidean spacetime to obtain n-point correlation functions for few-nucleon systems. They applied external magnetic fields to induce transitions between two-nucleon channels and used Luscher's method to convert finite-volume spectra into scattering amplitudes. The calculations were matched to effective field theory descriptions to constrain low-energy coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic moments of the proton, neutron, deuteron, He-3 and H-3 were extracted from energy shifts in an external magnetic field at larger-than-physical quark masses; when expressed in nuclear magnetons defined with the mass of the nucleon or nuclei at the corresponding quark masses, the values were surprisingly close to their natural values.
- The two-nucleon short-distance coupling of the pionless EFT for the radiative capture process np -> d gamma, L1, was constrained at two unphysically large values of the quark masses and extrapolated to the physical point, giving a cross section consistent with the experimental value.
- The short-distance coupling L1,A for the pp fusion process was calculated at a large value of the quark masses and extrapolated to the physical point under the assumption of mild quark-mass dependence; the obtained value was consistent with the current phenomenological value.
- The matrix element for the nn -> pp transition was calculated at a large value of the quark masses, and a new short-distance two-nucleon doubly-weak coupling of the pionless EFT was identified and constrained; its contribution to the matrix element was comparable to that from the L1,A coupling.

## Context

This work extends the milestone demonstration by Beane et al. (2012) that light nuclei and hypernuclei emerge from QCD at larger-than-physical quark masses, and builds on algorithmic advances by Detmold and collaborators for forming nuclear correlation functions. The results enable a systematic program where LQCD input in the few-body sector constrains nuclear effective field theories, which can then be applied to grand-challenge problems such as neutrinoless double-beta decay, neutrino-nucleus scattering, and dark matter detection, as well as providing theoretical support for an Electron-Ion Collider program.
