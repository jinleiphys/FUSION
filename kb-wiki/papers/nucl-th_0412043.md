# Hadron production from quark coalescence and jet fragmentation in intermediate energy collisions at RHIC

> Transverse momentum spectra of pions, protons and antiprotons in Au+Au collisions at intermediate RHIC energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62$ GeV are studied in a model that includes both quark coalescence from the dense partonic matter and fragmentation of the quenched perturbative minijet partons. The resulting baryon to meson ratio at intermediate transverse momenta is predicted to be larger than that seen in experiments at higher center of mass energies.

## Key claim

The baryon-to-meson ratio at intermediate transverse momenta in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62\) GeV is predicted to be larger than that seen at higher RHIC energies, specifically reaching a \(p/\pi\) ratio of about 1.5 at \(p_T \sim 4\) GeV, compared to the value of about 1 measured at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. This prediction depends on extrapolations of the fireball parameters and minijet quenching from available data at other energies.

## Method

The authors model hadron production by combining two mechanisms: quark coalescence from a thermally equilibrated, collectively flowing partonic matter, and independent fragmentation of quenched perturbative minijet partons. The thermal parton distributions are determined from the total transverse energy and baryon chemical potential, extrapolated from data at other collision energies, while the minijet distributions are computed using pQCD with Cronin effect and radiative energy loss via the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev approach. The coalescence integral is evaluated via Monte Carlo, including formation of unstable resonances (\(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\Delta\), \(K^*\)) whose decays modify the stable hadron spectra.

## Key numbers

- Predicted \(p/\pi\) ratio reaches about 1.5 at \(p_T \sim 4\) GeV in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62\) GeV.
- Predicted \(\bar p/\pi\) ratio reaches a maximum value of about 0.7 at slightly larger \(p_T\) than at 200 GeV.
- Quark chemical potential \(\mu_q = 23\) MeV at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62\) GeV, leading to \(\bar p/p = 0.44\).
- Total transverse energy \(dE_T/dy \simeq 460\) GeV for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62\) GeV, with charged particle multiplicity \(dN_{ch}/d\eta = 480\).
- Partonic matter at hadronization has \(N_{u+d}=285\), \(N_{\bar u+\bar d}=218\), and \(N_s = N_{\bar s}=79\) quarks and antiquarks in a volume \(V=555\) fm\(^3\), giving critical energy density about 0.8 GeV/fm\(^3\).
- Quark coalescence dominates proton production up to \(p_T < 6.5\) GeV, while for pions it dominates only up to \(p_T < 4\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the coalescence-plus-fragmentation model previously applied to Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV (Greco, Ko, and Vitev, 2003) to a lower RHIC energy of 62 GeV, testing the onset of the coalescence mechanism. The prediction of a larger \(p/\pi\) ratio at intermediate \(p_T\) contrasts with the expectation from pure minijet fragmentation and with the ratio of about 1 observed at 200 GeV by the PHENIX collaboration. Experimental confirmation would strengthen evidence for quark coalescence as a non-perturbative hadronization mechanism and help determine the energy threshold for quark-gluon plasma formation in heavy-ion collisions.
