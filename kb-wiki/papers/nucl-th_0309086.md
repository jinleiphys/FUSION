# Realistic calculations of correlations and final state interaction effects in the A(e,e'p)X process off complex nuclei

> A linked cluster expansion for the calculation of ground state observables of complex nuclei with realistic interactions has been used to calculate the ground state energy, density and momentum distribution of 16O and 40Ca. Using the same cluster expansion and the wave function and correlation parameters obtained from the energy calculation, we have evaluated the semi inclusive reaction A(e,e'p)X taking final state interaction (FSI) into account by a Glauber type approach; the comparison between the distorted and undistorted momentum distributions provides an estimate of the transparency of the nuclear medium to the propagation of the hit proton. The effect of color transparency is also included by considering the Finite Formation Time (FFT) that the hit hadron needs to reach its asymptotic physical state.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a linked-cluster expansion using realistic two-nucleon interactions (Argonne V8') can produce correlated wave functions for complex nuclei (16O and 40Ca) that reproduce ground-state observables, and that these same wave functions, when combined with a Glauber-type treatment of final-state interactions, yield a consistent description of the semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X reaction. The authors note that the charge densities corresponding to the minimum of the energy appreciably disagree with experimental data, requiring adjustment of mean-field parameters to achieve agreement.

## Method
The authors used a linked-cluster expansion to evaluate the expectation value of the nuclear Hamiltonian with correlated wave functions of the form Ψ_A = F Φ_A, where Φ_A is a Slater determinant and F is a symmetrized correlation operator. They calculated the ground-state energy, charge density, and momentum distribution of 16O and 40Ca using the Argonne V8' potential in the f6 approximation (six operator components). Using the same cluster expansion and the wave function and correlation parameters from the energy calculation, they evaluated the semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X reaction by computing the distorted momentum distribution, with final-state interactions treated via a Glauber S-matrix and color transparency effects included through a finite formation time approach.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state kinetic energy for 16O with Harmonic Oscillator single-particle wave functions: 230.28 MeV (mean field), 287.23 MeV (f1 approximation, central correlations only), 435.94 MeV (f3 approximation), 458.29 MeV (f6 approximation)
- Ground-state kinetic energy for 16O with Saxon-Woods single-particle wave functions: 244.94 MeV (mean field), 306.98 MeV (f1 approximation), 469.83 MeV (f3 approximation), 494.47 MeV (f6 approximation)
- Integrated nuclear transparency for 16O: 0.5

## Context
This work extends the cluster expansion approach of Fabrocini (2001) for ground-state calculations to the calculation of electro-disintegration processes, providing a consistent treatment of initial-state correlations and final-state interactions. The momentum distributions agree well with the Variational Monte Carlo results of Pandharipande (2001) using the AV18 interaction. The approach enables systematic calculations of semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X reactions for complex nuclei with realistic interactions, with comparison to experimental data reported as in progress.
