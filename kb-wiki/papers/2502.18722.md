# Ab Initio Calculations of the Carbon and Oxygen Isotopes: Energies, Correlations, and Superfluid Pairing

> We perform \textit{ab initio} nuclear lattice calculations of the neutron-rich carbon and oxygen isotopes using high-fidelity chiral interactions. We find good agreement with the observed binding energies and compute correlations associated with each two-nucleon interaction channel. For the isospin $T=1$ channels, we show that the dependence on $T_z$ provides a measure of the correlations among the extra neutrons in the neutron-rich nuclei. For the spin-singlet S-wave channel, we observe that any paired neutron interacts with the nuclear core as well as its neutron pair partner, while any unpaired neutron interacts primarily with only the nuclear core. For the other partial waves, the correlations among the extra neutrons grow more slowly and smoothly with the number of neutrons. These general patterns are observed in both the carbon and oxygen isotopes and may be universal features that appear in many neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed "staircase" pattern in spin-singlet S-wave neutron-neutron correlations, where paired neutrons interact with both the nuclear core and their partner while unpaired neutrons interact primarily with only the core, may be a universal feature appearing in many neutron-rich nuclei, but this is presented as a possibility rather than a proven general law.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) on a three-dimensional lattice with spatial lattice spacing 1.32 fm. They used wavefunction matching to accelerate perturbation theory convergence and applied quantum Monte Carlo simulations with auxiliary fields to compute ground state energies and two-nucleon correlation measures for neutron-rich carbon and oxygen isotopes. For each partial-wave channel, they introduced a short-range two-nucleon operator that produces a 1% reduction in scattering phase shift at relative momentum 150 MeV and computed the expectation value of that operator in the many-body ground state.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies for carbon isotopes from 12C to 23C and oxygen isotopes from 16O to 26O are computed and shown to agree well with experimental data, with error bars including statistical errors and uncertainties from extrapolation to infinite Euclidean time and infinite volume.
- Proton-proton correlations in the 1S0 channel decrease by only 14% from 16O to 26O and by only 15% from 12C to 23C, indicating the proton structure of the nuclear core remains largely intact.
- Proton-proton correlations in the 3P0 channel decrease by 25% from 16O to 26O and by 49% from 12C to 23C, suggesting alpha clusters become less correlated with each other as extra neutrons are added.
- Total T=0 S-wave correlations are larger than total T=1 S-wave correlations by only 26% for 16O and only 25% for 12C.

## Context

This work extends previous NLEFT calculations reported in Elhatisari (2022) for 12-14C and 16-18O to a wider range of neutron-rich carbon and oxygen isotopes. It builds on prior ab initio calculations of neutron-rich oxygen isotopes by Hagen (2009), Otsuka (2009), and others, as well as neutron-rich carbon isotopes by Forssen (2011), Jansen (2014), and Kanungo (2016). The correlation measurement technique introduced here provides a model-independent framework that can be adopted by other nuclear many-body methods, and the authors plan future calculations of charge radii, quadrupole moments, and electromagnetic transitions using the pinhole algorithm.
