# Critical line of the deconfinement phase transition

> Phase diagram of strongly interacting matter is discussed within the exactly solvable statistical model of the quark-gluon bags. The model predicts two phases of matter: the hadron gas at a low temperature T and baryonic chemical potential muB, and the quark-gluon gas at a high T and/or muB. The nature of the phase transition depends on a form of the bag mass-volume spectrum (its pre-exponential factor), which is expected to change with the muB/T ratio. It is therefore likely that the line of the 1st} order transition at a high muB/T ratio is followed by the line of the 2nd order phase transition at an intermediate muB/T, and then by the lines of "higher order transitions" at a low muB/T.

## Key claim

The model predicts that the line of first-order deconfinement phase transition at high baryochemical-potential-to-temperature ratio is followed by a line of second-order transition at intermediate ratios, and then by lines of "higher order transitions" at low ratios, but this sequence depends critically on the pre-exponential power-law parameters \(\gamma\) and \(\delta\) of the bag mass-volume spectrum, which are treated as free parameters because the physical constraints that determine them are not well defined.

## Method

The authors construct the grand canonical partition function for a gas of quark-gluon bags, where each bag has a mass \(m\) and volume \(v\), and the bag mass-volume spectrum \(\rho(m,v)\) includes both a discrete sum over known low-lying hadron states and a continuous asymptotic form derived from the bag model for large masses and volumes. The pressure is obtained from the farthest-right singularity of the Laplace-transformed partition function, and a phase transition occurs when the pole singularity \(s_H(T)\) (hadron phase) collides with the singularity \(s_Q(T)\) (quark-gluon plasma phase) of the function \(f(T,s)\); the order of the transition is determined by the behavior of the derivatives of these singularities at the collision temperature \(T_C\), which in turn depends on the exponent \(\alpha \equiv -(\gamma+\delta+2)\) in the pre-exponential factor of the spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant is taken as \(B \approx 400\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- The Stefan-Boltzmann constant for the quark-gluon phase is \(\sigma_Q = \frac{\pi^2}{30} \cdot \frac{95}{2}\).
- The asymptotic mass-volume spectrum is assumed valid for bag volumes \(v > V_0 \approx 1\) fm\(^3\) and masses \(m > Bv + M_0\) with \(M_0 \approx 2\) GeV.
- The condition for any phase transition to exist is \(\gamma < -5/4\) and \(\delta < -7/4\).
- For massless non-interacting quarks and gluons with zero total three-momentum, \(\gamma = -5/4\) and \(\delta = -7/4\), yielding no phase transition.
- For colorless bags only, \(\gamma = -9/4\) and \(\delta = -19/4\), which satisfies the conditions for a phase transition.
- A first-order transition occurs when \(\alpha > 2\) (i.e., \(\gamma+\delta < -4\)).
- A second-order or higher-order transition occurs when \(1 < \alpha \le 2\) (i.e., \(-4 \le \gamma+\delta < -3\)).
- For \(3/2 < \alpha \le 2\) the transition is second order with infinite specific heat at \(T_C\); for \(\alpha = 3/2\) it is second order with finite specific heat.
- For \(4/3 \le \alpha < 3/2\) the transition is third order.

## Context

This work extends the statistical bootstrap model of Hagedorn (1965) and the van der Waals excluded-volume approach of the authors' earlier papers (e.g., Gorenstein, Petrov, and Zinovjev, 1982) by incorporating a continuous bag mass-volume spectrum that unifies the hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma phases within a single partition function. The model provides a framework for interpreting the rapid changes in matter properties observed at low SPS energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 6\)–12 GeV) and for guiding the upcoming experimental program at CERN SPS (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 5\)–20 GeV). The predicted sequence of transition orders at different \(\mu_B/T\) ratios offers a theoretical basis for searching for the critical endpoint, complementing lattice QCD calculations (e.g., Fodor and Katz, 2004) and the qualitative QCD-based phase diagram conjectures reviewed by Stephanov (2004).
