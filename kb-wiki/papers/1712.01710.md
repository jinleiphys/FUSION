# A modification of Faddeev-Popov approach free from Gribov ambiguity

> We propose a modified version of the Faddeev-Popov quantization approach for non-Abelian gauge field theory to avoid the Gribov ambiguity. We show that by means of introducing a new method to insert the correct identity into the Yang-Mills generating functional and considering the identity generated by an integral through a subgroup of the gauge group, the problem of the Gribov ambiguity can be removed naturally. Meanwhile by handling the absolute value of the Faddeev-Popov determinant with the method introduced by Williams and collaborators, we lift the Jacobian determinant together with the absolute value and obtain a local Lagrangian. The new Lagrangian have a nilpotent symmetry which can be viewed as an analogue of the BRST symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors propose a modification of the Faddeev-Popov quantization procedure that avoids the Gribov ambiguity by inserting the identity using an integral over a subgroup of the gauge group (the maximal Abelian subgroup) rather than the full gauge group, rather than by constraining the functional integration domain to the Gribov region as in the Gribov-Zwanziger approach. The claim is conditional on working in Landau gauge with the infinity vanishing boundary condition, which ensures that the gauge-fixing equation has only the trivial solution for the subgroup transformation.

## Method

The authors replace the standard Faddeev-Popov identity, which integrates over the full gauge group, with an identity that integrates only over the maximal Abelian subgroup of the gauge group (for SU(2), this is U(1)). They choose a quadratic gauge condition \((\partial^\mu A_\mu^a)^2 = \omega\) that reduces to the Landau gauge in the limit \(\xi \to 0\), and show that within this subgroup the gauge-fixing equation \(\partial^\mu A_\mu^U = 0\) has only the trivial solution \(\theta = 0\) under the infinity vanishing boundary condition, eliminating Gribov copies. The absolute value of the resulting Faddeev-Popov determinant is handled using the method of Ghiotti and collaborators (2005), introducing auxiliary fields \(\varphi, B, d, \bar{d}\) to obtain a local Lagrangian with a nilpotent symmetry analogous to BRST symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The free gluon propagator in 4-dimensional Minkowski space is given as \(\langle A_\mu^a(x) A_\nu^b(y) \rangle = \int \frac{d^4k}{(2\pi)^4} \frac{-i}{k^2 + i\epsilon} \left( g_{\mu\nu} - \frac{k_\mu k_\nu}{k^2} \right) \delta^{ab} e^{-ik\cdot(x-y)}\), identical to the standard Faddeev-Popov result.
- The gauge-fixing parameter \(\xi\) is taken to zero (\(\xi \rightarrow 0\)) to obtain the Landau gauge, which is required for the factor \(N(G[A])\) to become well-defined and equal to 1.

## Context

This work extends the line of attempts to resolve the Gribov ambiguity in non-Abelian gauge theory, originally identified by Gribov (1978). It contrasts with the Gribov-Zwanziger approach (Gribov 1978, Zwanziger 1989, 1993), which constrains the functional integral to the Gribov region but still contains Gribov copies, and with the refined Gribov-Zwanziger action (Dudal 2008) that introduces a soft breaking of BRST symmetry. The method also differs from approaches using the fundamental modular region, which has no analytic expression (Vandersickel 2012), and from gauge conditions like the space-like planar gauge (Bassetto 1983) or modified axial gauge (Zhou 2017) that break Lorentz invariance. The new approach enables a local Lagrangian free from Gribov ambiguity while preserving a nilpotent symmetry, and can be generalized to SU(N) gauge groups, including SU(3) for QCD.
