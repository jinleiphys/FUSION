# Violation of energy conditions and entropy production in holographic Bjorken flow

> We demonstrate that a Bjorken expanding strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma can display dynamically-driven violations of the dominant and also the weak energy condition during hydrodynamization. In addition, we find that a period of vanishing entropy production in far-from-equilibrium stages induces later violations of the dominant energy condition in the strongly coupled plasma. Such violations cannot occur in a classical description of hydrodynamization and suggest that the inclusion of quantum effects in transport can lead to new phenomena in these regimes, even for systems without anomalies or spin.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Bjorken-expanding strongly coupled N=4 Supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma can display dynamically-driven violations of the dominant and weak energy conditions during hydrodynamization, even when the initial conditions satisfy these constraints; a period of vanishing entropy production in far-from-equilibrium stages acts as a precursor to later violations of the dominant energy condition.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the 5d Einstein equations in asymptotically AdS5 spacetime using a characteristic formulation in infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, with the metric Ansatz compatible with Bjorken flow symmetries. They evolve 25 different initial conditions for the metric anisotropy and extract the time-dependent energy-momentum tensor components from the UV coefficient a2(tau), while computing the non-equilibrium entropy density from the area of the apparent horizon.

## Key numbers

- Violations of the dominant energy condition occur when the pressure anisotropy satisfies Delta p/epsilon < -1; violations of the weak energy condition occur when Delta p/epsilon < -4 or Delta p/epsilon > 2
- The region where energy condition violations occur and entropy may display transient plateaus corresponds to omega_Lambda less than or approximately 0.6, which maps to times tau less than or approximately 0.5 fm/c assuming Lambda ~ 0.2 GeV
- The initial time for numerical simulations is tau_0 = 0.2
- The initial energy density parameter a_2(tau_0) is set to -20/3 for most initial conditions, with two exceptions: IC#24 uses -7.75 and IC#25 uses -7.1
- The second-order hydrodynamic approximation for pressure anisotropy is given by [Delta p/epsilon]_2nd = 2/(3 pi omega_Lambda) + 2(1-ln 2)/(9 pi^2 omega_Lambda^2)

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of Bjorken flow in N=4 SYM plasma (Chesler 2009, Heller 2011, Heller 2012, Jankowski 2014) by establishing a direct numerical connection between transiently vanishing entropy production and energy condition violations. It contrasts with classical kinetic theory approaches based on the Boltzmann equation (Kurkela 2014, Kurkela 2018), where energy conditions are always satisfied because the single-particle distribution function is non-negative. The findings suggest that comparisons between holography and kinetic theory can only be meaningfully performed after energy condition violations have ceased, and motivate investigating quantum effects in the Kadanoff-Baym equations (Baym 1961) for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
