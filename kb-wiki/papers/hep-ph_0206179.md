# Nonlinear Behavior of Quarkonium Formation and Deconfinement Signals

> We anticipate new features of quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies which differ from a straightforward extrapolation of results at CERN SPS energy. General arguments indicate that one may expect quarkonium formation rates to increase more rapidly with energy and centrality than the production rate of the heavy quarks which they contain. This is due to new formation mechanisms in which independently-produced quarks and antiquarks form a bound quarkonium state. This mechanism will depend quadratically on the total number of initially-produced heavy quark pairs, and becomes numerically significant only at RHIC and LHC energy. When viewed as a signal of color deconfinement, a transition from suppression to enhancement may be observed. Explicit model calculations are presented, in which one can follow striking variations of final quarkonium production within a range of parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at RHIC and LHC energies, quarkonium formation rates may increase more rapidly with energy and centrality than the production rate of the heavy quarks they contain, due to a new formation mechanism where independently-produced quarks and antiquarks form bound states. This mechanism depends quadratically on the total number of initially-produced heavy quark pairs and could lead to a transition from suppression to enhancement in deconfinement signals, but the author notes that the exact result will depend on the details of the physics controlling formation.

## Method

The author calculates heavy quark production in heavy ion collisions using perturbative QCD parton-level cross sections at next-to-leading order, with multiple structure function sets and parameter choices for charm mass and scale. These cross sections are combined with nuclear geometry via the nuclear overlap function to predict the number of charm quark pairs per collision at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. The author then develops generic arguments for quarkonium formation from uncorrelated charm pairs and applies them to two specific models: a generic quadratic formation model and a statistical hadronization model with canonical corrections.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energy (sqrt(s) = 18 GeV), the average number of charm quark pairs per central collision is 0.2
- At RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV), the average number of charm quark pairs per central collision is 10
- At LHC energy (sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV), the average number of charm quark pairs per central collision is 200
- The nuclear overlap function for central heavy ion collisions is T_AA(b=0) approximately 30 mb^{-1}
- The number of charged hadrons per event is estimated as 1350 at SPS, 3250 at RHIC, and 16500 at LHC
- The generic J/psi yield per event (with lambda = 1) is 0.00018 at SPS, 0.034 at RHIC, and 2.4 at LHC
- The initial J/psi yield per event is 0.0012 at SPS, 0.06 at RHIC, and 1.2 at LHC
- The PHENIX Collaboration measured sigma(pp -> c cbar) = 380 +/- 200 microbarns from the 130 GeV run
- The centrality dependence exponent for J/psi production at SPS from NA50 data is beta approximately 0.77
- The measured centrality dependence exponent for charged hadrons is alpha = 1.07 +/- 0.04 at SPS and alpha = 1.13 +/- 0.05 at RHIC-130

## Context

This work extends the Matsui-Satz (1986) proposal that J/psi suppression signals color deconfinement in heavy ion collisions, and addresses predictions by Vogt (1999) that J/psi suppression would be virtually complete at higher energies. The author introduces a quadratic formation mechanism that becomes significant when multiple charm pairs are produced, contrasting with the linear dependence assumed in earlier work. The paper enables experimental tests at RHIC and LHC where the predicted transition from suppression to enhancement could be observed, and provides a framework for interpreting centrality dependence of J/psi production that differs from the NA50 results at SPS.
