# Nuclear Schiff moment in nuclei with soft octupole and quadrupole vibrations

> Nuclear forces violating parity and time reversal invariance (${\cal P},{\cal T}$-odd) produce ${\cal P},{\cal T}$-odd nuclear moments, for example, the nuclear Schiff moment. In turn, this moment can induce the electric dipole moment in the atom. The nuclear Schiff moment is predicted to be enhanced in nuclei with static quadrupole and octupole deformation. The analogous suggestion of the enhanced contribution to the Schiff moment from the soft collective quadrupole and octupole vibrations in spherical nuclei is tested in this article in the framework of the quasiparticle random phase approximation with separable quadrupole and octupole forces applied to the odd $^{217-221}$Ra and $^{217-221}$Rn isotopes. We confirm the existence of the enhancement effect due to the soft modes. However, in the standard approximation the enhancement is strongly reduced by a small weight of the corresponding "particle + phonon" component in a complicated wave function of a soft nucleus. The perspectives of a better description of the structure of heavy soft nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that an enhancement of the nuclear Schiff moment due to soft collective quadrupole and octupole vibrations does exist in spherical nuclei, but in the standard quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) the enhancement is strongly reduced by the small weight of the corresponding "particle + phonon" component in the complicated wave function of a soft nucleus.

## Method

The authors used the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with separable quadrupole and octupole forces to calculate phonon states in even-even cores of \(^{216-220}\)Ra and \(^{216-220}\)Rn isotopes. They then solved for odd-neutron nuclei \(^{217-221}\)Ra and \(^{217-221}\)Rn by coupling a quasiparticle to the quadrupole (\(2^+\)) and octupole (\(3^-\)) phonons, constructing wave functions as a single-quasiparticle component plus one-phonon admixtures. The Schiff moment was computed from the mixing of opposite-parity states via a \(\mathcal{P},\mathcal{T}\)-odd weak interaction, using the matrix element of the Schiff operator between the quadrupole and octupole phonon states.

## Key numbers

- Phonon energies in even-even nuclei: \(\omega_2\) ranges from 0.18 MeV (\(^{220}\)Ra) to 0.69 MeV (\(^{216}\)Ra); \(\omega_3\) ranges from 0.47 MeV (\(^{220}\)Ra) to 1.40 MeV (\(^{216}\)Ra).
- In odd-neutron nuclei, the probability of the \(2^+ \otimes g_{9/2}\) component in the ground state is of order 10–30% (e.g., 0.14 in \(^{217}\)Ra, 0.11 in \(^{217}\)Rn, 0.27 in \(^{219}\)Rn).
- The reduced matrix element \(\langle 2^+ || S || 3^- \rangle\) in even nuclei is of order 1–2 \(e\cdot\text{fm}^3\).
- The Schiff moment in the odd-neutron nuclei is within the limits (0.04–0.6) \(\eta \cdot 10^{-8} \, e\cdot\text{fm}^3\) (e.g., \(^{219}\)Ra: 0.30 \(\eta \cdot 10^{-8} \, e\cdot\text{fm}^3\); \(^{221}\)Ra: \(-0.07\) \(\eta \cdot 10^{-8} \, e\cdot\text{fm}^3\)).
- Under a scaling factor \(y = 0.01\) (frequencies reduced to 1% of their values), the Schiff moment in \(^{221}\)Ra reaches 560 \(\eta \cdot 10^{-8} \, e\cdot\text{fm}^3\).

## Context

This work tests the suggestion by Hayes (2000) that soft octupole vibrations could produce a similar enhancement of the Schiff moment as static octupole deformation, which was previously calculated by Auerbach et al. (1996) and Spevak et al. (1997) to yield moments 2–3 orders of magnitude larger than in spherical nuclei. The authors find that in the QRPA framework the enhancement is suppressed by the spreading of single-particle strength, but they show that near the collapse of the RPA solution (signaling a transition to static deformation) the enhancement can become large, as demonstrated for \(^{221}\)Ra. This points to the need for a treatment beyond the RPA, such as including many-phonon components, to fully capture the effect in soft nuclei.
