# The Impact of Isospin Breaking on the Distribution of Transition Probabilities

> In the present paper we investigate the effect of symmetry breaking in the statistical distributions of reduced transition amplitudes and reduced transition probabilities. These quantities are easier to access experimentally than the components of the eigenvectors and were measured by Adams et al. for the electromagnetic transitions in ^{26}Al. We focus on isospin symmetry breaking described by a matrix model where both, the Hamiltonian and the electromagnetic operator, break the symmetry. The results show that for partial isospin conservation, the statistical distribution of the reduced transition probability can considerably deviate from the Porter-Thomas distribution.

## Key claim

The distribution of reduced transition probabilities can deviate considerably from the Porter-Thomas distribution when isospin symmetry is only partially conserved, but this deviation depends on the degree of symmetry breaking in both the Hamiltonian and the transition operator; the effect is not captured by a naive application of the central limit theorem because the wave function components have an N-dependent parametric dependence on the symmetry-breaking parameter that competes with the large-N expansion.

## Method

The authors constructed random Hamiltonians of dimension N=200 with block-diagonal GOE matrices for T=0 and T=1 subspaces plus a coupling matrix representing the Coulomb interaction, controlled by a mixing parameter λ. They modeled the electromagnetic transition operator as a random matrix with separate isoscalar and isovector components controlled by a second mixing parameter β. For each pair of parameters (λ, β), they diagonalized ten Hamiltonians, computed approximately 2×10⁵ transition matrix elements W_if between eigenstates, and compared the resulting distributions to Gaussians using the generalized entropy S.

## Key numbers

- The Hamiltonian dimension was N=200, with N₀=N₁=100 for the T=0 and T=1 subspaces.
- The mean level spacing D of the full Hamiltonian was 0.157 (in the units of the random matrix model), while Dⱼ = 0.314 for each subspace.
- The mixing parameter λ = α/D was varied over the range 0 ≤ λ ≤ 1.0, and β was varied over 0 ≤ β ≤ 1.0.
- For λ=0 and β=0 (no symmetry breaking), the entropy S was -1.05, indicating strong deviation from a Gaussian distribution.
- For λ=1.0 and β=1.0 (maximal symmetry breaking), the entropy S was -0.0004, consistent with a Gaussian distribution.
- The experimental data from Adams et al. comprised 873 values of the normalized transition probability y, with the histogram maximum occurring at z = log₁₀(y) ≈ -0.5, while the Porter-Thomas maximum occurs at z = 0.

## Context

This work extends the random matrix model of isospin breaking previously applied to spectral fluctuations in ²⁶Al by Mitchell et al. (late 1980s) and analyzed theoretically by Weidenmüller and coworkers. The authors address the conjecture by Adams, Mitchell, and Shriner (1990s) that the observed shift in the distribution of electromagnetic transition probabilities in ²⁶Al relative to the Porter-Thomas distribution may be due to isospin breaking. The model simultaneously treats symmetry breaking in both the Hamiltonian and the transition operator, and the results enable quantitative extraction of the root mean square symmetry-breaking matrix element from transition probability data, analogous to what was previously done for spectral fluctuations.
