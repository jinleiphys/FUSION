# Studying parton correlations via double parton scatterings in associated quarkonium production at the LHC and the Tevatron

> Quarkonium production in proton-proton (pp) collision provides interesting means to study the parton content and their correlations in the proton. Recent LHC and Tevatron data of J/psi + Z, J/psi + W and J/psi +J/psi production suggest the relevance of double parton scatterings (DPSs) as opposed to single parton scatterings (SPSs). In this proceedings contribution, we review the corresponding SPS contributions and discuss their upper limits set up by quark-hadron duality. These allow us to perform an improved extraction of the DPS yields and of their implications.

## Key claim

The Color Evaporation Model (CEM), based on quark-hadron duality, provides a conservative upper limit on the single parton scattering (SPS) contribution to associated quarkonium production; the difference between this upper limit and the ATLAS data is attributed to double parton scatterings (DPS), yielding effective cross sections compatible with other extractions.

## Method

The authors computed the SPS cross sections for \(J/\psi+Z\), \(J/\psi+W\), and \(J/\psi+J/\psi\) production in the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) at NLO (for the first two) and LO (for di-\(J/\psi\)), using MadGraph5\_aMC@NLO. They compared these CEM results, which overshoot data at high \(p_T\) and thus serve as an upper bound on SPS, to ATLAS, CMS, and D0 experimental data; the residual cross section after subtracting the CEM upper limit was fitted to the DPS formula \(\sigma_{\rm DPS} = \frac{1}{1+\delta_{AB}} \frac{\sigma(A)\sigma(B)}{\sigma_{\rm eff}}\) to extract \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\).

## Key numbers

- For \(J/\psi+Z\) production: ATLAS data = \(1.6 \pm 0.4\) pb; NLO CEM upper limit = \(0.19^{+0.05}_{-0.04}\) pb; extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (4.7 ^{+2.4}_{-1.5})\) mb.
- For \(J/\psi+W\) production: ATLAS data = \(4.5 ^{+1.9}_{-1.5}\) pb; NLO CEM upper limit = \(0.28 \pm 0.07\) pb; extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (6.1 ^{+3.3}_{-1.9})\) mb.
- For di-\(J/\psi\) production at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV: CMS extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (8.2 \pm 2.0_{\rm stat} \pm 2.9_{\rm sys})\) mb; D0 extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (4.8 \pm 0.5_{\rm stat} \pm 2.5_{\rm sys})\) mb; ATLAS extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff} = (6.3 \pm 1.6_{\rm stat} \pm 1.0_{\rm sys})\) mb.
- The CEM universal parameter for prompt \(J/\psi\): \({\cal P}^{\rm NLO, prompt}_{J/\psi} = 0.009\) (NLO) and 0.014 (LO).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical SPS calculations in NRQCD by Li (2010), Mao (2011), Gong (2012), Lansberg (2013, 2016), and Shao (2016) to the CEM framework, providing a conservative upper bound on SPS. The extracted \(\sigma_{\rm eff}\) values are compatible with those from di-\(J/\psi\) production by the CMS, D0, and ATLAS Collaborations, and with other central-rapidity quarkonium data. This enables improved isolation of DPS signals in multi-particle final states at the LHC and Tevatron, which is important for reducing backgrounds in searches for new particles.
