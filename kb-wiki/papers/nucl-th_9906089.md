# The H-Dibaryon and the Hard Core

> The H dibaryon, a single, triply magic bag containing two up, two down and two strange quarks, has long been sought after in a variety of experiments. Its creation has been attempted in $K^-$, proton and most recently in relativistic heavy ion induced reactions. We concentrate on the latter, but our conclusions are more generally applicable. The two baryons coalescing to form the single dibaryon, likely $ΛΛ$ in the case of heavy ions, must penetrate the short range repulsive barrier which is expected to exist between them. We find that this barrier can profoundly affect the probability of producing the H state, should it actually exist.

# The H-Dibaryon and the Hard Core

## Key claim

The short-range repulsive barrier (hard core) expected between two Lambda baryons can profoundly suppress the probability of forming the H dibaryon in relativistic heavy ion collisions, even if the H state actually exists; the suppression is so severe that for reasonable values of the bag formation radius (0.2–0.3 fm), the H yield per central Au+Au event drops by factors of 10⁻⁴ to 10⁻² relative to earlier estimates that ignored the barrier.

## Method

The authors embed a WKB barrier penetration factor into a previously developed quantum-mechanical coalescence model for heavy ion collisions. The Lambda-Lambda potential is constructed from the short-range omega and sigma exchange components of the Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, assumed to be approximately flavor-independent for the strange sector. The coalescence probability for forming a broad di-Lambda doorway state is multiplied by the transmission coefficient through the repulsive barrier to obtain the final H production rate, with the critical separation radius a (where the two baryons dissolve into a six-quark bag) treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The Bonn-inspired Lambda-Lambda potential is given numerically as V(r) = 3.94 exp(-3.97r)/r - 1.44 exp(-2.97r)/r GeV, with r in fermis.
- The earlier calculation without a hard core predicted approximately 0.07 H per central Au+Au collision.
- For a bag formation radius a = 0.2 fm, the suppression factor relative to the earlier estimate is approximately 10⁻⁴, leaving perhaps a few thousand dibaryons in the E896 sample of 100 million central Au+Au events.
- For a = 0.35 fm, the suppression factor is approximately 10⁻².
- Reducing the collision momentum from 14.6 to 10.6 GeV/c produces close to a 30% reduction in H yield per central event.
- The H binding energy is assumed to be less than 20 MeV, and probably considerably less, based on evidence from doubly strange hypernuclei.

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence calculations for the H dibaryon by Baltz et al. (1997), which predicted a high formation probability of 0.07 per central Au+Au collision but omitted the hard core effect. The authors had previously developed a dynamic coalescence model (Kahana et al., 1996) that successfully reproduced deuteron yields from Au+Au collisions at 11.6 GeV/c, as confirmed by E866 data. The present calculation introduces the barrier penetration factor into that framework, finding that the hard core can dramatically reduce H production. The paper suggests that an alternative route to observing the H may be through doubly strange hypernuclei such as ⁵_ΛΛH, where the nuclear environment keeps the Lambda pair together long enough (approximately 100 picoseconds) to penetrate the barrier.
