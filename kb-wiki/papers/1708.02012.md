# Excitation energy spectra of the Lambda_c and Lambda_b baryons in a finite-size diquark model

> The excitation energies of the $Λ_{c}$ and $Λ_{b}$ baryons are investigated in a finite-size diquark potential model, in which the heavy baryons are treated as bound states of a charm quark and a scalar-isoscalar diquark. The diquark is considered as a sizable object. The quark-diquark interaction is calculated as a sum of the quark-quark interaction which is assumed to be half of the quark-antiquark interaction for the color singlet. The potential parameters in the quark-antiquark interaction are fixed so as to reproduce the charmonium spectrum. We find the diquark size to be 1.1 fm for the diquark mass 0.5 GeV/c$^{2}$ to reproduce the $1p$ excitation energy of $Λ_{c}$. In this model, the $Λ_{c}$ and $Λ_{b}$ excitation spectra are reproduced well, while this model does not explain $Λ_{c}(2765)$, whose isospin nor spin-parity are unknown yet. Thus, the detailed properties of $Λ_{c}(2765)$ is very important to the presence of the diquark in heavy baryons as a effective constituent. We also discuss the $Ξ_{c}$ spectrum with the scalar strange diquark.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a finite-size diquark model, treating the diquark as a rigid rotor with size 1.1 fm, reproduces the observed excitation spectra of Λc and Λb baryons well, but explicitly fails to explain the Λc(2765) state, whose isospin and spin-parity are unknown; the authors state that the detailed properties of Λc(2765) are crucial to determining whether the diquark picture is valid for heavy baryons.

## Method

The authors treat heavy baryons (Λc, Λb) as bound states of a heavy quark (charm or bottom) and a scalar-isoscalar diquark with finite size, modeled as a rigid rotor with fixed separation ρ between the two light quarks. The quark-diquark interaction is computed as the sum of two quark-quark interactions, each taken as half of the quark-antiquark interaction for the color singlet, using a Coulomb-plus-linear potential with parameters α = 0.4 and k = 0.9 GeV/fm fixed to reproduce the charmonium spectrum. The Schrödinger equation is solved numerically for each angular momentum channel, with the effective potential obtained by integrating the interaction over the diquark orientation and relative coordinate angles.

## Key numbers

- Diquark size ρ = 1.1 fm for diquark mass md = 0.5 GeV/c² reproduces the 1p excitation energy of Λc.
- The spin-weighted average excitation energy of the 1p Λc states (Λc(2595) with 1/2⁻ and Λc(2625) with 3/2⁻) is 0.330 GeV.
- For the strange diquark in Ξc, the mass mds = 0.94 GeV/c² and size ρs = 0.68 fm reproduce both the 1s and 1p masses.
- The observed mass difference between the ground states of Ξc and Λc is 0.18 GeV/c²; this is reproduced with strange diquark mass mds = 0.75 GeV/c² when assuming the same size as the ud diquark (1.1 fm).
- The 1p excitation energy of Ξc calculated with mds = 0.75 GeV/c² and ρ = 1.1 fm is 0.30 GeV, smaller than the experimental spin-weighted average of 0.34 GeV.
- Charm quark mass fixed at 1.5 GeV/c²; bottom quark mass fixed at 4.0 GeV/c².
- Potential parameters: α = 0.4, k = 0.9 GeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous point-like diquark model (Jido 2016) which found that reproducing the Λc 1p excitation required halving the string tension; the finite-size diquark resolves this discrepancy. The diquark size of 1.1 fm is consistent with calculations in a Schwinger-Dyson formalism (Imai 2014) and lattice QCD studies (Alexandrou, Babich, Orginos). The model enables testing the diquark picture against future experimental determination of the quantum numbers of Λc(2765), and provides a framework for studying strange diquarks in Ξc baryons.
