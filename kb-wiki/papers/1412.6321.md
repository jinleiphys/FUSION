# Classicalization of Quantum Variables and Quantum-Classical Hybrids

> The extraction of classical degrees of freedom in quantum mechanics is studied in the stochastic variational method. By using this classicalization, a hybrid model constructed from quantum and classical variables (quantum-classical hybrids) is derived. In this procedure, conservation laws such as energy are maintained, and Ehrenfest's theorem is still satisfied with modification. The criterion for the applicability of quantum-classical hybrids is also discussed.

# Classicalization of Quantum Variables and Quantum-Classical Hybrids

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantum-classical hybrid (QCH) model from the stochastic variational method (SVM) by replacing the stochastic trajectory of one particle with a deterministic classical trajectory, but this approximation is only valid when the classical and quantum particles are well-separated and the overlap of their wave functions is small; the model fails when the distance between particles is shorter than the width of the quantum wave function.

## Method

The author starts with a non-relativistic two-particle system and quantizes it using the stochastic variational method (SVM), where particle trajectories are replaced by stochastic differential equations with Wiener processes. To classicalize one degree of freedom, the stochastic differential equation for particle 1 is replaced with a deterministic classical velocity equation, while particle 2 retains its stochastic description. The resulting coupled equations consist of a classical equation of motion for particle 1 (via a quasi trajectory function) and a Schrodinger-like equation for particle 2, with the interaction potential coupling both.

## Key numbers

- The noise intensity for each particle is set to ν_i = ℏ/(2m_i), linking quantum fluctuation to the inverse particle mass.
- In the numerical example, the mass ratio is m₁/m₂ = 5, with a harmonic potential K = ℏ²/(2m₂λ⁴) where λ is a spatial parameter.
- Three initial wave function widths for particle 2 are tested: α = 0.5, 1, and 2 (dimensionless, from Eq. for Ψ₂).
- The dimensionless time is defined as T ≡ ℏt/(2m₂λ²).
- Initial conditions: r̂₁(0) = f₁(x₂,0) = 0.5, v₁(f₁(x₂,0),x₂,0) = -ℏ/(2m₂λ).

## Context

This work extends the stochastic variational method of Yasue (1979) and Nelson's stochastic quantization to construct a hybrid quantum-classical dynamics, differing from prior QCH models by Hall (2008), Elze (2012), and Buric (2013) which combine Hilbert space and phase space descriptions. The key novelty is the introduction of a quasi trajectory function that allows conserved quantities to be defined via a stochastic Noether theorem, and the model enables future construction of QCH for field systems as noted by Koide (2014).
