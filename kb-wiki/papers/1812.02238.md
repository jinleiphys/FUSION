# Quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions: coupled Boltzmann transport equations

> By coupling the Boltzmann transport equations of both quarkonium and open heavy quarks, we investigate their dynamical evolution inside the quark-gluon plasma and study quarkonium production in heavy ion collisions. The Boltzmann transport equation of quarkonium is derived from the open quantum system formalism and effective field theory of QCD by assuming quarkonium interacts weakly with the plasma. The dissociation and recombination terms in the Boltzmann equation are calculated in potential nonrelativistic QCD. It is shown that the combination of quarkonium dissociation, recombination, open heavy quark diffusion and energy loss can drive the system of quarkonium and open heavy quarks to detailed balance and kinetic thermalization. By solving the transport equations with initial momenta of quarkonia and heavy quarks sampled from PYTHIA and a hydrodynamic medium, we can calculate the nuclear modification factors of bottomonium and describe the data at both RHIC and LHC energies. The azimuthal angular anisotropy coefficient $v_2$ of $Υ$(1S) in 5.02 TeV peripheral Pb-Pb collisions is also predicted.

## Key claim
By coupling Boltzmann transport equations for both quarkonium and open heavy quarks, the authors show that dissociation, recombination, open heavy quark diffusion, and energy loss together drive the system to detailed balance and kinetic thermalization, enabling description of bottomonium nuclear modification factors at both RHIC and LHC energies. The key hedge is that the Boltzmann equation for quarkonium is derived under the assumption that quarkonium interacts weakly with the plasma, and the calculations use a Coulomb potential for bound state wavefunctions rather than lattice-motivated potentials.

## Method
The authors derived the Boltzmann transport equation for quarkonium from the open quantum system formalism and potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) under the Markovian approximation, then coupled it with Boltzmann equations for open heavy quarks. Dissociation and recombination terms were calculated from pNRQCD scattering amplitudes for gluon absorption/emission and inelastic scattering with medium constituents, with the finite binding energy of quarkonium serving as a soft divergence regulator. The coupled equations were solved via Monte Carlo simulations using initial momenta sampled from PYTHIA with EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions, initial positions from the TRENTo model, and a boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic medium background.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant chosen as α_s = 0.3 for the calculation.
- Singlet potential used: V_s = -C_F * (0.42 / r).
- Melting temperature of Υ(2S) set to 210 MeV.
- Calculations performed for 200 GeV Au-Au collisions (STAR data), 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions (CMS data), and 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions (CMS data).
- Υ(1S) v_2 predicted for 5.02 TeV peripheral Pb-Pb collisions in the centrality range 10%–60%.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical treatment of quarkonium dissociation and recombination by placing both processes in a unified framework derived from pNRQCD, going beyond earlier phenomenological models that assumed detailed balance at equilibrium without tracking the real-time evolution of open heavy quark distributions. The approach builds on prior work by Matsui (1986) on static screening and by Brambilla (1999) on pNRQCD, and enables future studies of doubly charm baryon production in heavy ion collisions as well as extensions using lattice-motivated potentials and event-by-event hydrodynamics.
