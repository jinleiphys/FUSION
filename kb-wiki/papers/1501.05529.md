# Proton Structure and PHENIX Experiment

> We briefly summarize the important and critical roles that PHENIX Experiment has played in determining the proton's internal structure in terms of quarks and gluons, and their dynamics. Some pioneering measurements by PHENIX Experiment on the motion and polarization of quarks and gluons, as well as their correlations inside a fast moving proton are presented. Some future opportunities and potentials of PHENIX Experiment are also discussed.

## Key claim

The PHENIX experiment at RHIC has provided the first clear experimental evidence that gluons inside a longitudinally polarized fast-moving proton have a net positive polarization, contributing about 20% of the proton's helicity, but this result is limited to the momentum fraction region accessible by current RHIC measurements (0.05 less than x less than 0.2) and carries large uncertainty from unmeasured regions.

## Method

The PHENIX experiment measured double-helicity asymmetries (A_LL) for inclusive pi0 and eta meson production at mid-rapidity in polarized proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, using data collected during the 2005, 2006, and 2009 RHIC runs. These asymmetries were compared with next-to-leading order QCD global analyses (DSSV, DSSV++) to extract constraints on the gluon helicity distribution Delta g(x). For sea quark helicity structure, PHENIX measured parity-violating single-spin asymmetries (A_L) from W-boson production and decay to leptons in polarized p-p collisions at sqrt(s)=500 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Gluon helicity contribution to the proton's spin: about 20% from the momentum region 0.05 less than x less than 0.2, based on the DSSV++ global analysis of RHIC data (deFlorian 2014)
* W+ boson production cross section times branching ratio at sqrt(s)=500 GeV: 144.1 plus or minus 21.2 (stat) plus 3.4 minus 10.3 (syst) plus or minus 15% (norm) pb
* W- boson production cross section times branching ratio at sqrt(s)=500 GeV: 31.7 plus or minus 12.1 (stat) plus 10.1 minus 8.2 (syst) plus or minus 15% (norm) pb
* Combined quark and antiquark helicity contributes about 30% of the proton's spin from the region accessible by existing measurements

## Context

This paper reviews the PHENIX experiment's contributions to the RHIC spin program, which was designed to address the proton spin puzzle discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (Ashman 1987, 1989). The measurements extend and complement earlier DIS-only global analyses by Bluemlein and Boettcher (2010), Ball et al. (NNPDF, 2013), and Leader et al. (LSS10, 2010), as well as the earlier DSSV analysis (deFlorian 2008, 2009). The new PHENIX and STAR data enabled the DSSV++ global analysis (deFlorian 2014) that provided the first evidence for nonzero gluon polarization. Future measurements at sqrt(s)=500 GeV with PHENIX's forward calorimeter can access gluon helicity at x as low as 0.002, and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider (Accardi 2012) will provide the final word on the gluon spin contribution.
