# Parton distribution functions in the pion from lattice QCD

> We analyze the moments of parton distribution functions in the pion calculated in lattice QCD, paying particular attention to their chiral extrapolation. Using the lowest three non-trivial moments calculated on the lattice, we assess the accuracy with which the x dependence of both the valence and sea quark distributions in the pion can be extracted. The resulting valence quark distributions at the physical pion mass are in fair agreement with existing Drell-Yan data, but the statistical errors are such that one cannot yet confirm (or rule out) the large-x behavior expected from hadron helicity conservation in perturbative QCD. However, one can expect that the next generation of calculations in lattice QCD will allow one to extract parton distributions with a level of accuracy comparable with current experiments.

# Parton distribution functions in the pion from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The valence quark distributions in the pion reconstructed from the lowest three non-trivial lattice QCD moments, after chiral extrapolation, show fair agreement with existing Drell-Yan data at the physical pion mass, but the statistical errors are too large to confirm or rule out the large-x behavior expected from hadron helicity conservation in perturbative QCD (a (1-x)^2 behavior as x approaches 1).

## Method

The authors analyze the n=1, 2, and 3 moments of pion parton distribution functions calculated by the QCDSF collaboration using quenched lattice QCD on a 16^3 x 32 lattice at beta=6.0 with Wilson fermions at three quark masses (approximately 70, 130, and 190 MeV). They perform chiral extrapolations of these moments to the physical pion mass using chiral perturbation theory, incorporating the leading non-analytic behavior for non-singlet moments and a modified functional form that accounts for the suppression of disconnected diagrams at large quark masses. The x-dependence of the valence and sea quark distributions is then reconstructed by fitting parameterized forms (such as v_pi(x) = A x^b (1-x)^c) to the extrapolated moments via Mellin transformation.

## Context

This work extends the methodology previously applied to nucleon parton distribution functions by Detmold et al. (2001) and Thomas et al. (2000) to the case of the pion, using lattice data from the QCDSF collaboration (Best et al., 1997). The analysis builds on the chiral perturbation theory calculations of Arndt and Savage (2001) for the leading chiral corrections to pion moments, and compares reconstructed distributions with phenomenological parameterizations from SMRS (Sutton et al., 1991) and GRS (Gluck et al., 1999). The results enable future lattice calculations at smaller quark masses and with unquenched configurations to extract pion PDFs with accuracy comparable to current experiments, potentially resolving the long-standing discrepancy between the (1-x)^2 behavior predicted by hadron helicity conservation (Farrar, 1979; Lepage, 1980) and the approximately linear (1-x) behavior seen in Drell-Yan data.
