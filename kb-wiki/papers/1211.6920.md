# Resonant states of deformed nuclei in complex scaling method

> We develop a complex scaling method for describing the resonances of deformed nuclei and present a theoretical formalism for the bound and resonant states on the same footing. With $^{31}$Ne as an illustrated example, we have demonstrated the utility and applicability of the extended method and have calculated the energies and widths of low-lying neutron resonances in $^{31}$Ne. The bound and resonant levels in the deformed potential are in full agreement with those from the multichannel scattering approach. The width of the two lowest-lying resonant states shows a novel evolution with deformation and supports an explanation of the deformed halo for $^{31}$Ne.

## Key claim

The complex scaling method (CSM) is extended to deformed nuclei for the first time, enabling bound and resonant states to be treated on the same footing. The method reproduces the bound and resonant levels from the multichannel scattering approach of Hamamoto (2005) and supports the interpretation of a deformed halo in \({}^{31}\)Ne, but only for a specific deformation interval near \(\beta_2 \sim 0.3\).

## Method

The authors solve the complex-scaled Schrodinger equation for an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential (including central, quadrupole-deformed, and spin-orbit terms) by expanding the wave functions in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with 60 oscillator shells. The complex eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian are obtained by matrix diagonalization for various rotation angles \(\theta\), and the optimal resonance parameters (energy and width) are determined from the \(\theta\)-trajectory where the rate of change with respect to \(\theta\) is minimal. The method is applied to \({}^{31}\)Ne, with potential parameters taken from Hamamoto (2010).

## Key numbers

- The oscillator frequency of the HO basis is fixed at \(\hbar\omega_0 = 41 A^{-1/3}\) MeV, with \(A=31\) for \({}^{31}\)Ne.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are: diffuseness \(a = 0.67\) fm, depth \(V_0 = -39\) MeV, spin-orbit strength \(v = 32\), and radius \(R = r_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(r_0 = 1.27\) fm.
- The rotation angle used for the main calculation is \(\theta = 18^\circ\), with the continuum rotated by \(2\theta = 36^\circ\).
- For the spherical case (\(\beta_2 = 0.0\)), three resonances are observed: \(1f_{7/2}\), \(1f_{5/2}\), and \(1g_{9/2}\); for \(\beta_2 \neq 0.0\), these split into 12 resonances labeled by asymptotic quantum numbers \(\Omega[Nn_z\Lambda]\).
- The width of the 1/2[330] and 3/2[321] resonant states shows a minimum near \(\beta_2 = 0.0\) and a maximum at larger deformation; the most likely deformation for halo formation in \({}^{31}\)Ne is near \(\beta_2 \sim 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method, previously successful for spherical nuclei (Guo 2010), to deformed systems, where prior approaches include the multichannel scattering method of Hamamoto (2005, 2012), the ACCC method and real stabilization method applied to deformed Woods-Saxon potentials (Cattapan 2000, Hagino 2004), and the contour-deformation method in momentum space (Hagen 2006). The results enable systematic studies of single-particle resonances in deformed exotic nuclei near the drip line and provide a framework for interpreting deformed halo phenomena, as suggested by the Coulomb breakup data on \({}^{31}\)Ne (Nakamura 2009).
