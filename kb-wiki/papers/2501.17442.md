# Particle number diffusion in second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics with momentum-dependent relaxation time

> This article explores particle number diffusion in relativistic hydrodynamics using kinetic theory with a modified collision kernel that incorporates the momentum dependence of the particle relaxation time. Starting from the Boltzmann equation within the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA), we derive second-order evolution equations for the dissipative number current and calculate the associated transport coefficients. The sensitivity of transport coefficients to the particle momentum dependence of the collision time scale of the microscopic interactions in the hot QCD medium is analyzed. For a conformal, number-conserving system, we compare the ERTA-modified transport coefficients for particle diffusion with exact results derived from scalar field theory. With an appropriate parameterization of the relaxation time, we demonstrate the consistency of our analysis and assess the degree of agreement of the results with the exact solutions from scalar field theory. The relaxation times for the shear and number diffusion evolution equations are seen to be distinct in general when the momentum dependence of the relaxation time is taken into consideration.

## Key claim

The paper derives second-order evolution equations for the dissipative particle number current using the Extended Relaxation Time Approximation (ERTA) with a momentum-dependent relaxation time, and shows that the relaxation times for shear and number diffusion are distinct in general when momentum dependence of the relaxation time is included, contrary to the conventional constant-relaxation-time approximation.

## Method

Starting from the Boltzmann equation within the ERTA framework, the authors performed a Chapman-Enskog-like gradient expansion to obtain the non-equilibrium distribution function up to second order in gradients for a conformal, number-conserving system of massless particles with finite chemical potential following Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics. They then applied Landau matching conditions and the Landau frame condition to determine the counter-terms, and derived the second-order evolution equation for the number diffusion current, computing the associated transport coefficients in terms of thermodynamic integrals that incorporate the momentum-dependent relaxation time.

## Key numbers

- First-order shear viscosity coefficient: \(\eta = \frac{2 K_{32}}{T}\), where \(K_{32}\) is a relaxation-time-dependent thermodynamic integral
- First-order diffusion coefficient: \(\kappa = -C\beta^2 I_{21} + \frac{n}{\epsilon+P} K_{21} - K_{11}\), where \(C = \frac{1}{\beta^2 I_{31}}\left( \frac{n}{\epsilon+P}K_{31} - K_{21} \right)\)
- Equilibrium number density: \(n_0 = \frac{d_g e^\alpha}{\pi^2 \beta^3}\) for massless Maxwell-Boltzmann particles
- Equilibrium energy density: \(\epsilon_0 = \frac{3 d_g e^\alpha}{\pi^2 \beta^4}\)
- Equilibrium pressure: \(P = \frac{d_g e^\alpha}{\pi^2 \beta^4}\), with equation of state \(P = \epsilon/3\)

## Context

This work extends prior studies on second-order relativistic hydrodynamics with momentum-dependent relaxation time, specifically building on the ERTA framework developed by Dash et al. (2021, 2023) which previously derived the shear stress tensor evolution. The paper addresses the relative lack of attention to dissipative charge currents compared to shear and bulk viscosity, which becomes important for low-energy heavy-ion collisions such as those in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and upcoming FAIR experiments where net-baryon density is significant. The results enable comparison with exact transport coefficients from scalar field theory (Denicol 2022, Rocha 2023) and provide a framework for incorporating momentum-dependent relaxation times into hydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma at finite chemical potential.
