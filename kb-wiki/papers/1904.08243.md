# Systematic study of proton radioactivity based on Gamow--like model with a screened electrostatic barrier

> In the present work we systematically study the half--lives of proton radioactivity for $51 \leq Z \leq 83$ nuclei based on the Gamow--like model with a screened electrostatic barrier. In this model there are two parameters while considering the screened electrostatic effect of Coulomb potential with the Hulthen potential i.e. the effective nuclear radius parameter r_0 and the screening parameter a. The calculated results can well reproduce the experimental data. In addition, we extend this model to predict the proton radioactivity half--lives of 16 nuclei in the same region within a factor of 2.94, whose proton radioactivity are energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified. Meanwhile, studying on the proton radioactivity half-life by a type of universal decay law has been done. The results indicate that the calculated half--lives are linearly dependent on Coulomb parameter with the same orbital angular momentum.

# Systematic study of proton radioactivity based on Gamow-like model with a screened electrostatic barrier

## Key claim

The Gamow-like model modified with a Hulthen-type screened electrostatic potential reproduces experimental proton radioactivity half-lives for 41 nuclei with Z between 51 and 83 within a factor of 2.94, and the calculated half-lives show a linear dependence on the Coulomb parameter Z_d/sqrt(Q_p) for a given orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors modified the Gamow-like model of Zdeb (2016) by replacing the pure Coulomb electrostatic potential with a Hulthen potential to account for screening of the proton-daughter nucleus Coulomb interaction. The model contains two adjustable parameters: the effective nuclear radius parameter r_0 and the screening parameter a, which were determined by fitting to 41 experimental proton emitters. The penetration probability was calculated using the WKB approximation, and the assault frequency was derived from the oscillation frequency of the decaying system.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation sigma = 0.468 between calculated and experimental log10 half-lives for 41 proton emitters with Z between 51 and 83
- Effective nuclear radius parameter r_0 = 1.14 fm determined from fitting
- Screening parameter a = 5.6 x 10^{-4} fm^{-1} determined from fitting
- Calculated half-lives reproduce experimental data within a factor of 2.94
- Predicted half-lives for 16 nuclei whose proton radioactivity is energetically allowed but not yet quantified
- Standard deviation for the Gamow-like model of Zdeb (2016) is sigma = 0.559 for the same 41 nuclei
- Standard deviation for the universal decay law for proton emission (UDLP) from Qi (2012) is sigma = 0.424

## Context

This work extends the Gamow-like model developed by Zdeb (2016) by incorporating the screened electrostatic effect using the Hulthen potential, following the approach of Budaca (2017) who studied screening effects in proton radioactivity. The model improves upon the standard Gamow-like model (sigma = 0.559 versus 0.468) and provides predictions comparable to the universal decay law for proton emission (UDLP) developed by Qi (2012). The results enable predictions for 16 unquantified proton emitters and confirm the linear Geiger-Nuttall-type relationship between log half-life and Coulomb parameter for fixed orbital angular momentum.
