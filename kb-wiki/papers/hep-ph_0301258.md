# Estimating the inelasticity with the information theory approach

> Using the information theory approach, in both its extensive and nonextensive versions, we estimate the inelasticity parameter $K$ of hadronic reactions together with its distribution and energy dependence from $p\bar{p}$ and $pp$ data. We find that the inelasticity remains essentially constant in energy except for a variation around $K\sim 0.5$, as was originally expected.

## Key claim

Using information theory in both its extensive (Shannon) and nonextensive (Tsallis) forms, the authors estimate that the inelasticity parameter \(K\) of hadronic reactions remains essentially constant with energy, with a value around \(K\sim 0.5\), but this conclusion depends on neglecting one data point (P238 at 630 GeV) which does not follow the overall trend.

## Method

The authors applied the maximum entropy principle to rapidity distributions of charged secondaries from \(p\bar{p}\) and \(pp\) collisions, using only the mean multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, and the assumption that only a fraction \(K\sqrt{s}\) of the total energy is available for particle production. They solved the constraint equations for the Lagrange multiplier \(\beta\) (extensive case) or for \(\beta_q\) and the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) (Tsallis case) to obtain the most probable rapidity distribution, from which the inelasticity was extracted by fitting to experimental data at energies from 20 to 1800 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Extensive inelasticity \(K = 0.45\) at \(\sqrt{s}=20\) GeV for \(pp\) data.
* Extensive inelasticity \(K = 0.51\) at \(\sqrt{s}=53\) GeV for \(p\bar{p}\) data.
* Extensive inelasticity \(K = 0.55\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV for \(p\bar{p}\) data.
* Extensive inelasticity \(K = 0.45\) at \(\sqrt{s}=540\) GeV for \(p\bar{p}\) data.
* Extensive inelasticity \(K = 0.50\) at \(\sqrt{s}=1800\) GeV for \(p\bar{p}\) data.
* Nonextensive inelasticity \(K_q = 0.44\) at \(\sqrt{s}=20\) GeV, with \(q = 1.05\).
* Nonextensive inelasticity \(K_q = 0.51\) at \(\sqrt{s}=1800\) GeV, with \(q = 1.33\).
* The nonextensivity parameter \(q\) increases from 1.05 at 20 GeV to 1.33 at 1800 GeV, following the relation \(q = 1 + 1/k\) where \(k\) is the Negative Binomial distribution parameter.

## Context

This work extends the information theory approach previously applied to multiparticle production data by Chao (1970s), which showed that only limited transverse momentum and a finite inelasticity \(K\) are needed to describe the data, making other model assumptions spurious. The results agree with early estimates by Cocconi and with experimental leading-particle analyses by Basile, and they contradict the decreasing trend seen in some earlier cosmic-ray analyses. The approach enables a parameter-economical description of high-energy collisions for cosmic-ray cascade modeling, and the connection between \(q\) and the Negative Binomial parameter \(k\) suggests that multiplicity fluctuations are the main source of nonextensivity in these data.
