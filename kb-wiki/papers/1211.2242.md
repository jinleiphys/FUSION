# Atomic Quantum Simulation of U(N) and SU(N) Non-Abelian Lattice Gauge Theories

> Using ultracold alkaline-earth atoms in optical lattices, we construct a quantum simulator for U(N) and SU(N) lattice gauge theories with fermionic matter based on quantum link models. These systems share qualitative features with QCD, including chiral symmetry breaking and restoration at non-zero temperature or baryon density. Unlike classical simulations, a quantum simulator does not suffer from sign problems and can address the corresponding chiral dynamics in real time.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a quantum simulator for U(N) and SU(N) non-Abelian lattice gauge theories using ultracold alkaline-earth atoms, but only for quantum link models (QLMs) with finite-dimensional Hilbert spaces per link, not for full QCD; the authors explicitly state that the system is "still far from a quantum simulator for full QCD."

## Method

The authors map the rishon representation of quantum link models onto a Fermi-Hubbard model, where quarks occupy lattice sites and rishons occupy link-sites in an optical lattice. The color degrees of freedom are encoded in the Zeeman sublevels of fermionic alkaline-earth isotopes such as 87Sr or 173Yb, exploiting the nearly exact SU(2I+1) symmetry of their interactions. The gauge-invariant hopping of quarks and rishons is realized through tunnel-coupled triple-wells in (1+1)D or cross-shaped vertices in (2+1)D, with the constraint of fixed rishon number per link enforced by strong repulsive interactions on overlapping rishon sites.

## Key numbers

- For the (1+1)D U(2) model with N = 1 rishon per link, exact diagonalization shows the energy splitting between the two almost degenerate vacuum states decreases exponentially with system size L, indicating Z(2) chiral symmetry breaking.
- For the (2+1)D U(2) model with N = 2 rishons per link, the model has charge conjugation symmetry C and exhibits chiral symmetry breaking at a critical temperature Tc > 0.
- For the (2+1)D SU(2) model with N = 2 rishons per link, the model exhibits chiral symmetry breaking at Tc > 0 and chiral symmetry restoration at non-zero baryon density nB > 0.
- For the (3+1)D SU(3) model with N = 3 rishons per link, the model exhibits chiral symmetry breaking at Tc > 0 and chiral symmetry restoration at nB > 0.
- The coefficient of the determinant term for the SU(2) case is gamma = 2 Omega^2 Delta U / delta^2, where Omega is the Raman Rabi frequency, delta is the detuning, and Delta U is the difference in repulsive interaction energy between rishons on the same versus different link-sites.
- Numerical verification on small systems with up to 12 fermions (4 quark-sites and 8 rishon-sites) shows that gauge invariance is well preserved even for a color-dependent hopping rate imperfection z approximately 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of quantum simulators for Abelian U(1) gauge theories (Banerjee 2012) to the non-Abelian U(N) and SU(N) case, using quantum link models originally developed by Horn (1981) and Chandrasekharan (1997). The approach enables the study of chiral symmetry breaking and its real-time dynamics, which are inaccessible to classical simulations due to sign problems; the authors note that two related works on SU(2) gauge theories in cold atomic systems appeared concurrently (Tagliacozzo 2012, Zohar 2012).
