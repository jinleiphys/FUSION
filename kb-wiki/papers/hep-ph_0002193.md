# Chiral Unitary approach to meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions and nuclear applications

> We report on recent nonperturbative techniques that combine the information of chiral Lagrangians (with and without resonances) with unitarity in coupled channels and other requirements of the S-matrix theory of the strong interactions. As a result, the region of applicability of such techniques is much larger than the one of Chiral Perturbation Theory allowing one to study also resonance physics. Applications to meson-meson and meson-baryon scattering, as well as to problems where pairs of mesons or a meson and a baryon appear in the initial or final state are shown. Implications in several nuclear problems are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports on nonperturbative techniques that combine chiral Lagrangians with unitarity in coupled channels, extending the applicability of chiral approaches well beyond the typical 500 MeV limit of Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) into the resonance region, though the methods are still restricted to energies where the underlying chiral expansion remains meaningful.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral unitary approach that uses the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian as a kernel for coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations, imposing unitarity and analyticity constraints from S-matrix theory. The method is applied to meson-meson scattering (ππ, πK, K anti-K, etc.) and meson-baryon scattering (K⁻p, πΣ, etc.), with explicit inclusion of resonance fields (vector, axial, scalar, pseudoscalar octets and singlets) where needed. The approach is then extended to nuclear applications involving meson pairs or meson-baryon pairs in initial or final states.

## Key numbers

- The convergence limit of ChPT is estimated as Λ_χPT ≤ 4π f_π ≈ 1.2 GeV, with f_π the pion decay constant in the chiral limit.
- The lowest-order chiral Lagrangian (O(p²)) yields the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation: 3 m_η₈² = 4 m_K² - m_π².
- The ρ(770) resonance mass of 770 MeV is cited as the lightest well-established resonance, setting an upper limit for the ChPT series.
- The next-to-leading order (O(p⁴)) Lagrangian requires ten additional coupling constants L_i to determine low-energy behavior.

## Context

This work extends the standard ChPT framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and the resonance saturation approach of Ecker, Gasser, Pich, and de Rafael (1989) by incorporating unitarity nonperturbatively, enabling the study of resonance physics beyond the 500 MeV range. The chiral unitary method builds on earlier work by Truong (1988) and Dobado, Herrero, and Truong (1990) on the inverse amplitude method, and by Oller and Oset (1997) on coupled-channel unitarization. The results enable applications to nuclear problems such as kaon-nucleus interactions, meson production in nuclear reactions, and the behavior of mesons in nuclear medium, connecting to studies by Weise and Tauscher (1976), Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995), and Oset and Ramos (1998).
