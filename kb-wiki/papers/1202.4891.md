# The QGP phase in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The dynamics of partons, hadrons and strings in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed within the novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons (DQPM) matched to reproduce recent lattice-QCD results - including the partonic equation of state - in thermodynamic equilibrium. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks), respectively, obeying flavor current-conservation, color neutrality as well as energy-momentum conservation. The PHSD approach is applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions from low SIS to RHIC energies. The traces of partonic interactions are found in particular in the elliptic flow of hadrons as well as in their transverse mass spectra.

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons matched to lattice QCD results, shows that partonic interactions leave observable traces in hadron elliptic flow and transverse mass spectra, but the authors note that the quasiparticle properties fixed in thermal equilibrium are adopted for out-of-equilibrium configurations as an approximation that is "far from being arbitrary, however, not fully equivalent to the exact solution."

## Method

The authors apply the PHSD transport approach, which is based on Kadanoff-Baym equations with self-energies from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), to nucleus-nucleus collisions from low SIS to RHIC energies. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks) obeying flavor current-conservation, color neutrality, and energy-momentum conservation. The PHSD results for transverse mass spectra and elliptic flow are compared to experimental data from the NA49, BRAHMS, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations and to the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) model which lacks explicit partonic degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c approximately 160 MeV from lattice QCD results of Ref. aori10, corresponding to a critical energy density epsilon_c approximately 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- PHSD applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions from sqrt(s_NN) approximately 5 to 200 GeV
- Central Pb+Pb collisions at 40, 80, and 158 A GeV and central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for transverse mass spectra of pi-, K+, and K- mesons
- The elliptic flow v_2 from PHSD shows growth with bombarding energy and is fairly in line with data from STAR and PHENIX collaborations, while HSD predicts approximately energy-independent v_2 at larger energies above sqrt(s_NN) approximately 10 GeV
- PHSD approximately reproduces the universal scaling of v_2/epsilon_2 versus (1/S) dN_ch/dy, a feature not reproduced by hadronic transport models such as HSD and UrQMD

## Context

This work extends the PHSD approach previously presented in Refs. PHSD and BCKL11, applying it to transverse mass spectra and elliptic flow at relativistic energies. The authors note that HSD and UrQMD hadronic models underestimate the K+- slope (BratPRL) and fail to reproduce the rise of v_2 with bombarding energy, which the present PHSD calculations attribute to partonic degrees of freedom and repulsive scalar mean-field potentials. The universal scaling of v_2/epsilon_2 versus (1/S) dN_ch/dy, proposed by Voloshin (2000), puts strong constraints on initial microscopic properties and global longitudinal structure, and is approximately reproduced by PHSD but not by hadronic transport models or various hydrodynamic descriptions.
