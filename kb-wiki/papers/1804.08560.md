# Covariant quark model for the electromagnetic structure of light nucleon resonances

> We present estimates from the covariant spectator quark model for the electromagnetic transition form factors for the resonances $N(1440)\frac{1}{2}^+$, $N(1535)\frac{1}{2}^-$, $N(1520)\frac{3}{2}^-$ and $Δ(1232)\frac{3}{2}^+$, at intermediate and large square momentum transfer ($Q^2$). The calculations associated to the $N(1440)\frac{1}{2}^+$, $N(1535)\frac{1}{2}^-$ and $N(1520)\frac{3}{2}^-$ states are based exclusively on the parametrizations derived for the nucleon. In the case of the $Δ(1232)\frac{3}{2}^+$ (isospin 3/2), we use lattice QCD data to estimate the radial structure, and take into account the well known effects associated with the pion cloud dressing of the baryons. Our estimates are based mainly on the valence quark degrees of freedom and are in good agreement with the data for $Q^2 > 2$ GeV$^2$, with a few exceptions. The present predictions can be tested in a near future for large $Q^2$ at the Jefferson Lab -- 12 GeV upgrade.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, based on valence quark degrees of freedom and calibrated using nucleon elastic form factor data, provides good agreement with experimental transition form factor data for the resonances N(1440)1/2+, N(1535)1/2-, N(1520)3/2-, and Δ(1232)3/2+ specifically for momentum transfers Q² > 2 GeV², with a few exceptions that may be explained by meson cloud effects not included in the valence quark framework.

## Method

The author uses the covariant spectator quark model, which reduces the three-quark system to a quark-diquark system with a radial wave function ψ_B, and parametrizes the electromagnetic structure of the constituent quarks using vector meson dominance. For the N(1440)1/2+, N(1535)1/2-, and N(1520)3/2- resonances, the transition form factors are calculated using the same radial wave function and quark form factor parametrizations derived from the nucleon, with the N(1440) radial wave function modified by a function g(ϰ) that includes one adjustable parameter determined by the orthogonality condition between the nucleon and the resonance. For the Δ(1232)3/2+, lattice QCD data are used to determine the radial wave functions for the S, D1, and D3 angular momentum states, and the pion cloud contributions are added using a phenomenological parametrization for the magnetic dipole form factor and large N_c parameter-free relations for the quadrupole form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the N(1535)1/2- and N(1520)3/2- resonances, the model describes data well for Q² > 2 GeV², with exceptions for the Pauli form factor F₂* in N(1535) and the electric form factor G_E in N(1520) at low Q²
- For the N(1440)1/2+, the model describes data well for Q² > 2 GeV², corroborating the interpretation of N(1440) as the radial excitation of the nucleon
- For the Δ(1232)3/2+ magnetic dipole form factor G_M, the valence quark contribution underestimates data below Q² = 2 GeV², with convergence to the full result only at larger Q²
- The quadrupole form factors G_E and G_C for Δ(1232)3/2+ show valence quark contributions of approximately 10%, with the remainder from pion cloud contributions
- The magnetic dipole form factor normalization uses the dipole form factor G_D = 1/(1 + Q²/Λ_D²)² with Λ_D² = 0.71 GeV²
- The Coulomb quadrupole form factor G_C is multiplied by the factor κ = (M_Δ - M_N)/(2 M_Δ) where M_Δ is the Δ(1232) mass

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously successful in describing nucleon elastic form factors measured at Jefferson Lab (Jones 2000), to the calculation of transition form factors for light nucleon resonances. The results for N(1535) and N(1520) build on earlier work by the author (Ramalho, various years) and are compared with data from the CLAS collaboration at JLab (MokeevDatabase) and the MAID analysis. The N(1440) results are compared with a holographic model (Roper-AdS), and the Δ(1232) results are compared with the EBAC/JLab dynamical coupled-channel model (JDiaz07) and recent experimental data (Blomberg16a). The predictions can be tested at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade for large Q².
