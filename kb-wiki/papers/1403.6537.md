# Determination of the strange nucleon form factors

> The strange contribution to the electric and magnetic form factors of the nucleon is determined at a range of discrete values of $Q^2$ up to $1.4$ GeV$^2$. This is done by combining recent lattice QCD results for the electromagnetic form factors of the octet baryons with experimental determinations of those quantities. The most precise result is a small negative value for the strange magnetic moment: $G_M^s(Q^2=0) = -0.07\pm0.03\,μ_N$. At larger values of $Q^2$ both the electric and magnetic form factors are consistent with zero to within $2$-sigma.

## Key claim

The strange contribution to the nucleon electric and magnetic form factors is determined indirectly by combining lattice QCD results for connected quark contributions with experimental total form factors, under the assumption of charge symmetry. The most precise result is a small negative strange magnetic moment, but at larger momentum transfers both form factors are consistent with zero to within two standard deviations.

## Method

The authors combine lattice QCD results for the connected (valence) u- and d-quark contributions to the octet baryon electromagnetic form factors (from 2+1-flavor dynamical simulations at two lattice spacings) with experimental parameterizations of the total proton and neutron form factors. Using charge symmetry relations, the disconnected (sea) strange quark loop contribution is isolated via Eq. (8), which requires an estimate of the ratio R_sd = disconnected strange to disconnected down quark loops, obtained from chiral effective field theory. The analysis is performed independently for two lattice ensembles with different lattice spacings and volumes, and systematic uncertainties are estimated by comparing the lattice isovector combination with experiment and by using two different experimental form factor parameterizations.

## Key numbers

* Strange magnetic moment at Q²=0: G_M^s(0) = -0.07 ± 0.03 μ_N (final combined result, non-zero to 2-sigma)
* For the a=0.074(2) fm ensemble at Q²=0.26 GeV²: G_M^s = -0.069(91) μ_N, G_E^s = -0.096(84)
* For the a=0.062(2) fm ensemble at Q²=0.17 GeV²: G_M^s = -0.080(80) μ_N, G_E^s = 0.0081(63)
* At Q²=1.33 GeV² (a=0.074 fm): G_M^s = -0.12(17) μ_N, G_E^s = 0.14(17)
* At Q²=1.13 GeV² (a=0.062 fm): G_M^s = -0.089(188) μ_N, G_E^s = 0.22(18)
* Strange electric charge radius estimates: ⟨r²_E⟩^s = 0.0086(79) fm² (a=0.074 fm) and -0.0114(88) fm² (a=0.062 fm)

## Context

This work extends the indirect method previously applied by Leinweber et al. (2004, 2006) using quenched lattice QCD to determine the strange magnetic form factor at Q²={0,0.23} GeV² and the strange electric form factor at Q²=0.1 GeV². By using dynamical 2+1-flavor lattice simulations, the authors determine both strange form factors at six to seven discrete Q² values up to 1.4 GeV², providing the first determinations above Q²=0.6 GeV². The results are consistent with experimental determinations from JLab (G0, HAPPEX), MIT-Bates (SAMPLE), and Mainz (A4) collaborations, and the strange magnetic moment is an order of magnitude more precise than the closest experimental results.
