# Band crossings in $^{168}$Ta: a particle-number conserving analysis

> The structures of two observed high-spin rotational bands in the doubly-odd nucleus ${}^{168}$Ta are investigated using the cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method, in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The experimental moments of inertia and alignments are reproduced very well by the calculations, which confirms the configuration assignments for these two bands in previous works. The backbending and upbending mechanisms in these two bands are analyzed in detail by calculating the occupation probabilities of each orbital close to the Fermi surface and the contributions of each orbital to the total angular momentum alignments. The investigation shows that the level crossings in the first backbending is the neutron $i_{13/2}$ crossing and the in second upbending is the proton $h_{11/2}$ crossing.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the cranked shell model with a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, which treats blocking effects exactly, can reproduce the experimental moments of inertia and alignments for two high-spin rotational bands in \({}^{168}\)Ta, including the second upbending that conventional cranked shell model calculations could not reproduce. However, the gradual upbending in the \(\alpha=0\) sequence of band 2 at \(\hbar\omega\sim 0.5\) MeV is not reproduced by the PNC-CSM, which the authors note may require further investigation.

## Method

The authors used the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, solving the Hamiltonian directly in a truncated Fock-space of cranked many-particle configurations (CMPCs). The CMPC space was constructed in the proton \(N=4,5\) major shell and neutron \(N=5,6\) major shell with truncation energies about \(0.7\hbar\omega_0\), using dimensions of 1000 for both protons and neutrons. The effective pairing strengths were \(G_{\rm p}=0.34\) MeV for protons and \(G_{\rm n}=0.46\) MeV for neutrons, and the deformation parameters \(\varepsilon_2=0.217\) and \(\varepsilon_4=0\) were taken as an average of neighboring even-even Hf and W isotopes.

## Key numbers

* Kinematic moments of inertia and alignments for two high-spin rotational bands in \({}^{168}\)Ta were calculated and compared with experimental data up to spin \(\sim 40\hbar\).
* The first backbending occurs at \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.3\) MeV and is caused by neutron \(i_{13/2}\) crossing.
* The second upbending occurs at \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.5\) MeV and is caused by proton \(h_{11/2}\) crossing.
* Harris parameters used for alignment definition: \(J_0 = 28\ \hbar^2\)MeV\(^{-1}\) and \(J_1 = 58\ \hbar^4\)MeV\(^{-3}\).
* Nilsson parameters for neutrons were slightly modified: \(\kappa_6\) from 0.062 to 0.068 and \(\mu_6\) from 0.34 to 0.32.
* The proton orbital \(\pi 1/2^{-}[541]\) was shifted upward by about \(0.2\hbar\omega_0\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of high-spin rotational bands in the doubly-odd nucleus \({}^{168}\)Ta, which were first observed by Theine (1992) and later extended up to spin \(\sim 40\hbar\) by Wang (2010). The conventional cranked shell model could only reproduce the first neutron crossing but not the second proton crossing observed by Wang (2010). The PNC-CSM approach used here has been successfully applied to various nuclear phenomena including identical bands, super-deformed bands, and high-\(K\) isomers in rare-earth and actinide nuclei. The results confirm the configuration assignments of \(\pi 9/2^{-}[514] \otimes \nu 5/2^{+}[642]\) for band 1 and \(\pi 5/2^{+}[402] \otimes \nu 5/2^{+}[642]\) for band 2, and the authors suggest that the recently developed shell-model-like approach based on cranking covariant density functional theory may provide more detailed information for the upbending not reproduced in this work.
