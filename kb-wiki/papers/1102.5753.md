# Effect of three-pion unitarity on resonance poles from heavy meson decays

> We study the final state interaction in 3-pion decay of meson resonances at the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) of JLab. We apply the dynamical coupled-channels formulation which has been extensively used by EBAC to extract N* information. The formulation satisfies the 3-pion unitarity condition which has been missed in the existing works with the isobar models. We report the effect of the 3-pion unitarity on the meson resonance pole positions and Dalitz plot.

## Key claim

The paper reports that including the three-pion unitarity condition (via Z-graphs) in a dynamical coupled-channels model changes meson resonance pole positions by approximately 10% for the higher-mass state and 2-3% for the lower-mass state, and also modifies the Dalitz plot distributions for heavy meson decays.

## Method

The authors first develop a pi-pi scattering model using an isobar-type parametrization with resonances (f0, rho, f2) and solve the coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation. They then construct a pi-isobar scattering equation that includes Z-graphs and self-energy terms to satisfy three-pion unitarity, and fit the bare masses and couplings of the pi2(1670) and pi2(2100) resonances to experimental pole positions and branching ratios from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

* pi2(1670) pole position with Z-graphs: 1672-130i MeV; without Z-graphs: 1689-133i MeV (shift of approximately 2-3%)
* pi2(2100) pole position with Z-graphs: 2090-313i MeV; without Z-graphs: 2084-346i MeV (shift of approximately 10%)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels model previously used by the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) at Jefferson Lab for extracting N* resonance information to the three-meson scattering system. It addresses the missing three-body unitarity in conventional isobar models used for Dalitz plot analyses in experiments searching for exotic mesons (such as those by the E852 and GlueX collaborations) and in CP violation studies in B- and D-decays (by the BaBar and Belle collaborations). The results enable more reliable extraction of resonance properties from three-pion final states in future experiments.
