# Robustness of chiral symmetry in atomic nuclei with reflection-asymmetric shapes

> The present paper is a comment regarding the robustness of the chirality in presence of the space-reflection asymmetry, which leads to pairs of interleaved positive- and negative-parity bands. The recent results reported in Ref. \cite{2006.12062} which introduced the $chiture$ and $chiplex$ quantum numbers to describe an ideal nuclear system with simultaneous chiral and reflection symmetry breaking, are commented.

# Robustness of chiral symmetry in atomic nuclei with reflection-asymmetric shapes

## Key claim
The paper argues that chiral symmetry in atomic nuclei is robust and not destroyed by octupole (reflection-asymmetric) correlations, but this robustness is demonstrated only for an idealised case of maximum triaxial deformation (γ=90°) and static octupole deformation with the lowest negative-parity state at exactly zero energy.

## Method
The author comments on the theoretical framework developed in Ref. (2006.12062), which introduced the reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) applied to an ideal odd-odd nucleus. The model uses two-j shells (h11/2 and d5/2) with orbital and total angular momenta differing by 3ħ, maximum triaxial deformation γ=90°, and static octupole deformation. New quantum numbers called "chiture" (A) and "chiplex" (B = A × P) are derived to classify states and establish selection rules for E2, M1, and E3 electromagnetic transitions.

## Key numbers
- The orbital and total angular momenta of the two-j shells differ by 3ħ (Δl = Δj = 3ħ)
- Maximum triaxial deformation is set to γ = 90°
- The energy of the lowest negative-parity state is set to E(0⁻) = 0 MeV
- More than 60 chiral doublet bands have been reported in A ∼ 80, 100, 130, and 190 mass regions
- The model uses energy spacing typical for A ≈ 130 nuclei

## Context
This comment extends the work of Frauendorf and Meng (2005) who first pointed out that rotation of a triaxial nucleus can attain chiral character, and the work of Butler and Nazarewicz (1996) on reflection asymmetry. It comments directly on the results reported in Ref. (2006.12062) which introduced the RAT-PRM model. The paper notes that multiple chiral doublet bands (MχD) have been predicted by Meng et al. (2006) and triggered experimental studies in nuclei such as 133Ce, 103Rh, 78Br, 136Nd, 135Nd, and 131Ba. Future extensions of the RAT-PRM model are needed for three- and four-quasiparticle configurations in odd-even and even-even nuclei, and for inclusion of multi-j shells to describe bands built on pseudo-spin partners.
