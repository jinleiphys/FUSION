# Transport properties of the Fermi hard-sphere system

> The transport properties of neutron star matter play an important role in many astrophysical processes. We report the results of a calculation of the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity coefficients of the hard-sphere fermion system of degeneracy $ν$=2, that can be regarded as a model of pure neutron matter. Our approach is based on the effective interaction obtained from the formalism of correlated basis functions and the cluster expansion technique. The resulting transport coefficients show a strong sensitivity to the quasiparticle effective mass, reflecting the effect of second order contributions to the self-energy that are not taken into account in nuclear matter studies available in the literature.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity and thermal conductivity coefficients of the hard-sphere fermion system (a model of pure neutron matter) show a strong sensitivity to the quasiparticle effective mass, reflecting the effect of second-order contributions to the self-energy that are not taken into account in nuclear matter studies available in the literature; the authors emphasize that the inclusion of these second-order contributions dramatically affects both the magnitude and density dependence of the effective mass, which in turn modifies the transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity and thermal conductivity of a hard-sphere fermion system with degeneracy ν=2 using the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov formalism within Landau's theory of normal Fermi liquids. The input quantities, quasiparticle effective masses and in-medium scattering probabilities, were obtained from the correlated basis functions (CBF) effective interaction, including both first-order (Hartree-Fock) and second-order contributions to the proper self-energy. The ground-state energies needed to determine the CBF effective interaction were obtained from Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) calculations, and the scattering probabilities were computed in Born approximation using the CBF effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity coefficient η and thermal conductivity coefficient κ are given by the Brooker-Sykes corrections to the Abrikosov-Khalatnikov results; the ratio η/η_AK lies between 0.750 and 0.925, while κ/κ_AK lies between 0.417 and 0.561.
- The difference between the effective mass m*(k_F)/m computed using the CBF effective interaction and that from the low-density expansion (Eq. 17) is ≤ 2% for c ≤ 0.6, growing to 5.5% at c = 1.0.
- The DMC results for the ground-state energy parameter ζ (deviation from non-interacting energy) for ν=2 show differences from VMC/FHNC results ranging between ~2% and ~9% over 0.2 ≤ c ≤ 1, corresponding to less than 3% difference in the ground-state energy E_0.
- The low-density expansion for ν=2 (Eq. 13) is within 5% of DMC results at c < 0.5.
- Solidification for ν=2 occurs at density ρ_s such that ρ_s a^3 = 0.23.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Mecca et al., 2015, referred to as "I") of the fermion hard-sphere system with degeneracy ν=4 to the ν=2 case relevant to neutron star matter. It improves upon earlier calculations by Flowers and Itoh (1979) and Benhar and Valli (2007) by including second-order contributions to the self-energy that strongly affect the effective mass, which previous Hartree-Fock-based studies (e.g., G-matrix perturbation theory by Wambach et al., Lombardo et al., and Baldo et al.) had neglected. The results enable more accurate predictions of transport properties for neutron star matter, relevant to neutron star cooling and the Chandrasekhar-Friedman-Schutz instability of rapidly rotating stars.
