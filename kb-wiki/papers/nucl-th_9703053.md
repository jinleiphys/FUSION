# Recoil effects on nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> The electromagnetic form factors are computed using eigenstates of linear momentum for the nucleon. The latter is described in the framework of the chiral color-dielectric model, projecting the hedgehog ansatz on eigenstates of angular momentum and isospin. Form factors are well reproduced, with the exception of the magnetic one for the proton, up to $q^2 \simeq 0.5$ GeV$^2$. The effect of the removal of the spurious center-of-mass contributions shows up mainly in the electric form factor of the proton. A noticeable improvement is obtained with respect to the calculation without linear momentum projection.

## Key claim

The authors compute nucleon electromagnetic form factors using eigenstates of linear momentum (via Peierls-Yoccoz projection) rather than the static approximation, and find that form factors are well reproduced up to \(q^2 \simeq 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) with the exception of the proton magnetic form factor, which is less satisfactory due to the model's weak spin-spin interaction.

## Method

The nucleon is described in the chiral color-dielectric model (CDM) with a quadratic ("single minimum") potential for the confining scalar field, using three valence quarks in a hedgehog state surrounded by coherent meson fields. The hedgehog ansatz is projected onto eigenstates of angular momentum and isospin, and then onto eigenstates of linear momentum via a Peierls-Yoccoz projection, approximating boosted states. The electric and magnetic Sachs form factors are computed from matrix elements of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame, with the direction of the momentum transfer integrated over to remove spurious angular dependence.

## Key numbers

- Form factors are computed in the space-like region \(0 \le q^2 \le 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The model parameter \(G = \sqrt{g M}\) is fixed to \(G = 0.2\) GeV to reproduce the isoscalar nucleon radius.
- For \(G = 0.2\) GeV, the nucleon-delta average mass is around 1.13 GeV (experimental value 1.085 GeV).
- The pion mass is fixed at \(m_\pi = 0.139\) GeV, the sigma mass at \(m_\sigma = 1.2\) GeV, and the pion decay constant at \(f_\pi = 0.093\) GeV.
- Changing the \(\chi\) field mass in the range 0.8–2.0 GeV affects results by less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends previous static-approximation calculations of nucleon form factors in chiral soliton models, such as the linear sigma model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the Skyrme model, and the CDM (Fiolhais and Neuber 1994), by incorporating recoil effects via linear momentum projection. The formalism generalizes the approach of Neuber et al. (1993) for static properties. The results enable improved comparisons with experimental form factor data and suggest that the CDM, while promising for spin-independent observables and quark matter studies, requires additional tensor force (e.g., from color-magnetic interactions) to reproduce spin-dependent quantities like magnetic form factors and the \(\Delta\)-\(N\) mass splitting.
