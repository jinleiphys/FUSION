# Simultaneous description of wobbling and chiral properties in even-odd triaxial nuclei

> A particle-triaxial rigid core Hamiltonian is semi-classically treated. The coupling term corresponds to a particle rigidly coupled to the triaxial core, along a direction that does not belong to any principal plane of the inertia ellipsoid.The equations of motion for the angular momentum components provide a sixth-order algebraic equation for one component and subsequently equations for the other two. Linearizing the equations of motion, a dispersion equation for the wobbling frequency is obtained. The equations of motion are also considered in the reduced space of generalized phase space coordinates. Choosing successively the three axes as quantization axis will lead to analytical solutions for the wobbling frequency, respectively. The same analysis is performed for the chirally transformed Hamiltonian. With an illustrative example one identified wobbling states whose frequencies are mirror image to one another. Changing the total angular momentum I, a pair of twin bands emerged. Note that the present formalism conciliates between the two signatures of triaxial nuclei, i.e., they could coexist for a single nucleus.

# Simultaneous description of wobbling and chiral properties in even-odd triaxial nuclei

## Key claim
The paper claims that wobbling motion and chiral doublets, traditionally considered as separate signatures of triaxial nuclei observed in different nuclei, can coexist in a single even-odd nucleus when the odd particle is rigidly coupled to the triaxial core along a direction that does not lie in any principal plane of the inertia ellipsoid. The claim is hedged by the illustrative example being a single parameter set, and the authors state that new experimental data is necessary for testing.

## Method
A particle-triaxial rigid core Hamiltonian was treated semi-classically by replacing quantum operators with classical angular momentum components and commutators with Poisson brackets. The equations of motion for the angular momentum components were derived, yielding a sixth-order algebraic equation for one component. The equations were linearized around the deepest minimum to obtain a dispersion equation for the wobbling frequency, and the same analysis was performed for the chirally transformed Hamiltonian. The equations of motion were also considered in a reduced two-dimensional phase space using polar coordinates with each of the three axes successively chosen as the quantization axis.

## Key numbers
- Moments of inertia of the triaxial core: (J1, J2, J3) = (60, 20, 30) hbar^2 MeV^{-1}
- Total angular momentum of the composite system: I = 35/2 hbar
- Odd particle angular momentum: j = 13/2 hbar, with polar angles theta_0 = pi/4 and phi_0 = pi/4
- Four real stationary solutions for x1: -13.062, -8.811, -1.81, 16.185 hbar
- Wobbling frequency from linearized equations: omega = 0.362 MeV; chiral partner frequency: 3.651 MeV
- Wobbling frequencies from reduced space analysis (axis-1 quantization): 0.245 MeV; (axis-2): 0.205 MeV; (axis-3): 0.065 MeV
- Wobbling frequencies for chirally transformed Hamiltonian (deepest minima): axis-1: 0.245 MeV; axis-2: 0.209 MeV; axis-3: 0.210 MeV
- Minimum energy for aligned I parallel to j: 1.765 MeV

## Context
This work extends the particle-triaxial rotor model previously used by Davydov and Filippov (1958) and later authors such as Frauendorf (1997, 2016) and Petrache (1996, 2006), who studied wobbling and chiral doublets separately. Prior treatments by Buda (2019) fixed the odd nucleon to an axis, and by Raduta (2020) to a principal plane; here the coupling direction is outside any principal plane. The formalism enables the simultaneous description of both signatures in a single nucleus, potentially unifying the two phenomena, and provides a framework for interpreting future experimental data on triaxial even-odd nuclei.
