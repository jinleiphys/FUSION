# High Resolution Nonperturbative Light-Front Simulations of the True Muonium Atom

> Through the development of a parallel code called TMSWIFT, an extensive light-front quantization study of the nonperturbative spectrum of the bound state $(μ^+μ^-)$, true muonium, has been performed. Using Padé approximants, it has been possible to extract continuum and infinite-cutoff limits for the singlet and triplet states for a range of values of the coupling constant $α$. This data set allows for an investigation of the $α$ dependence of the light-front spectra, the results of which are compared to standard calculations. Decay constants have also been obtained. Improved calculations have been undertaken for the energy shifts due to the presence of a second, lighter flavor ($e$). Finally, initial results for three-flavor ($e$, $μ$, $τ$) calculations are presented.

## Key claim
Through nonperturbative light-front simulations using the parallel code TMSWIFT, the authors extract continuum and infinite-cutoff limits for the singlet and triplet states of true muonium, but the hyperfine splitting results are systematically larger than instant-form predictions, particularly due to difficulties in regularizing the annihilation channel.

## Method
The authors use Discretized Light Cone Quantization (DLCQ) with a truncated Fock space of $|\ell_i\bar{\ell}_i\rangle$, $|\ell_i\bar{\ell}_i\gamma\rangle$, and $|\gamma\rangle$ states, folding the photon components into an effective potential via iterated resolvents. They employ polar coordinates in momentum space and a regularization scheme that subtracts the asymptotic large-momentum limit of the $G_2$ matrix element. Padé approximants are used to extrapolate results to the continuum ($N\rightarrow\infty$) and infinite-cutoff ($\Lambda\rightarrow\infty$) limits for a range of coupling constant $\alpha$ values.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state singlet $1^1S_0$ extrapolated squared mass $M_0 = 2.00000(1)$ in units of $m_\mu^2$ for $\alpha$ in [0.01,0.1]
- Ground-state triplet $1^3S_1$ extrapolated squared mass $M_0 = 1.99999(1)$ in units of $m_\mu^2$ for $\alpha$ in [0.01,0.1]
- Hyperfine coefficient $C_{\rm HFS}$ values range from approximately 0.7 to 0.8 across the $\alpha$ range, compared to the leading-order instant-form prediction of 7/12 (approximately 0.58)
- Large-momentum power-law exponent $\kappa$ for the singlet $\uparrow\downarrow$ component: approximately 4.0 for $\alpha=0.01$, 3.59 for $\alpha=0.3$ with regularization, compared to 2.5 without regularization at $\alpha=0.3$

## Context
This work extends prior DLCQ studies of QED bound states by Krautgärtner (1992) and Trittmann (1997), who found logarithmic singularities in the hyperfine splitting at large cutoff values. The regularization scheme developed by Lamm and Lebed (2013) is applied here to lower $\alpha$ values approaching the physical QED coupling. The results enable future nonperturbative studies of new physics contributions to true muonium bound states, which could provide constraints beyond conventional perturbative calculations, and the TMSWIFT code allows investigation of multiple-flavor effects including electron and tau leptons.
