# New Atomic probes for Dark Matter detection: Axions, Axion-like particles and Topological Defects

> We present a brief overview of recently proposed detection schemes for axion, axion-like pseudoscalar particle and topological defect dark matter. We focus mainly on the possibility of using atomic and molecular systems for dark matter detection. For axions and axion-like particles, these methods are complementary probes to ongoing photon-axion interconversion experiments and astrophysical observations. For topological defects, these methods are complementary to conventional astrophysical search schemes based on gravitational signatures.

## Key claim

This paper reviews recently proposed detection schemes for axion, axion-like particle, and topological defect dark matter using atomic and molecular systems, arguing that these methods are complementary to ongoing photon-axion interconversion experiments and astrophysical observations, but the abstract glosses over the fact that these are proposals and not yet realized experimental detections.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical mechanisms by which axions and axion-like particles induce oscillating electric dipole moments (EDMs), nuclear Schiff moments, nuclear magnetic quadrupole moments, and spin-dependent couplings (axion-wind and spin-gravity effects) in atoms and molecules. For topological defects, they describe how scalar field defects can alter fermion masses and how pseudoscalar defects can produce transient EDMs and effective magnetic fields, detectable via synchronized global networks of magnetometers (GNOME) and atomic clocks.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM induced by axion: \( d_n = 2.4 \times 10^{-16} \, \theta \, e \cdot \text{cm} = 2.4 \times 10^{-16} \, (a/f_a) \, e \cdot \text{cm} \)
- Current limit on QCD \(\theta\) parameter from neutron EDM measurements: \( |\theta| < 10^{-10} \)
- Best current fractional inaccuracy of atomic clocks: \(\sim 10^{-18}\) (Hinkley 2013, Bloom 2014)
- Atomic Hg provides the most precise limits on proton EDM, quark chromo-EDM, \(\mathcal{P},\mathcal{T}\)-odd nuclear forces, neutron EDM, and QCD \(\theta\) term from atomic or molecular experiments (Griffith 2009, Swallows 2013)
- Molecular ThO provides the most precise limit on the electron EDM (Baron 2014)

## Context

This review extends the line of work using atomic systems as precision probes of fundamental physics, building on prior proposals by Graham (2011, 2013) for axion-induced oscillating nucleon EDMs and by Derevianko (2013) for topological defect detection via atomic clocks. It complements existing axion search methods such as haloscopes (Sikivie 1983, ADMX 2014), helioscopes (CAST 2007, IAXO 2014), and photon regeneration experiments (PVLAS 2008, OSQAR 2013), as well as gravitational searches for topological defects (Planck 2013, BICEP2 2014). The paper enables future experimental searches using the CASPEr experiment, the GNOME magnetometer network, and atomic clock networks to probe dark matter candidates in parameter space regions inaccessible to conventional methods.
