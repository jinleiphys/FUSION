# A mixed-mode shell-model theory for nuclear structure studies

> We introduce a shell-model theory that combines traditional spherical states, which yield a diagonal representation of the usual single-particle interaction, with collective configurations that track deformations, and test the validity of this mixed-mode, oblique basis shell-model scheme on $^{24}$Mg. The correct binding energy (within 2% of the full-space result) as well as low-energy configurations that have greater than 90% overlap with full-space results are obtained in a space that spans less than 10% of the full space. The results suggest that a mixed-mode shell-model theory may be useful in situations where competing degrees of freedom dominate the dynamics and full-space calculations are not feasible.

# A mixed-mode shell-model theory for nuclear structure studies

## Key claim

The authors propose that combining spherical shell-model states with SU(3) collective basis states in a non-orthogonal (oblique) basis can reproduce full-space results for $^{24}$Mg using less than 10% of the full model space, but the method's success depends on the competing single-particle and collective degrees of freedom having similar excitation energies and the specific choice of which SU(3) irreps are included.

## Method

The authors construct a double mixed-mode basis consisting of spherical shell-model (ssm) states in spherical single-particle coordinates and SU(3) basis states in cylindrical single-particle coordinates, both with good total angular momentum projection $M_J$. They solve the generalized eigenvalue problem $Hv = \lambda \Theta v$ using the Cholesky algorithm, where the overlap matrix $\Theta$ contains off-diagonal blocks $\Omega_{\alpha i} = e_\alpha \cdot E_i$ between the two basis sets. For $^{24}$Mg in the $0\hbar\omega$ $sd$-shell, they test various truncations: SM(n) spaces allowing n particles excited from $d_{5/2}$ into $s_{1/2}$ and $d_{3/2}$, combined with the leading SU(3) irrep (8,4) and the next most important (9,2) irreps, using the Wildenthal interaction.

## Key numbers

* The full $M_J=0$ $sd$-shell space for $^{24}$Mg has dimensionality 28503; the SM(2)+(8,4)&(9,2) oblique basis uses 10.4% of this space (2829 + 128 = 2957 basis states).
* The SM(2)+(8,4)&(9,2) calculation gives a ground-state energy 3.3 MeV below the SM(2) result, achieved by adding only 0.5% more basis states (from 9.9% to 10.4% of full space).
* The SM(4) calculation (64.2% of full space) gives a ground-state energy 4.2 MeV below SM(2), requiring a 54% increase in dimensionality compared to SM(2).
* Low-energy eigenstates from SM(2)+(8,4)&(9,2) have greater than 90% overlap with full-space results.
* The SM(4)+(8,4)&(9,2) calculation yields eigenstate overlaps of approximately 98% with exact results, using 64.6% of the full space.
* The SM(0)+(8,4) oblique basis uses only 0.2% of the full space (29 + 23 = 52 basis states).

## Context

This work extends the Elliott SU(3) symmetry approach (Elliott, 1958) and the spherical shell-model $m$-scheme (Brown and Wildenthal, 1988) by combining both representations in a non-orthogonal basis, addressing the long-standing problem of competing single-particle and collective quadrupole degrees of freedom in nuclei (Bohr and Mottelson, 1969). The method enables calculations for nuclei where full-space diagonalization is not feasible, and the authors suggest extensions to $pf$-shell nuclei, no-core calculations using the symplectic group (Navratil, Vary, and Barrett, 2000), and a general iterative procedure for identifying dominant modes from any one- and two-body Hamiltonian.
