# Chiral-scale effective field theory for dense and thermal systems

> We established a new power counting scheme, chiral-scale density counting (CSDC) rules, for the application of the chiral-scale effective field theory to nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures. Within this framework, the free fermion gas is at the leading order, while one-boson-exchange interactions appear at the next-to-leading order, and the multi-meson couplings are at higher orders. Then, we applied the CSDC rules to study the nuclear matter properties, and estimated the valid regions of the CSDC rules. It was found that the zero temperature symmetric nuclear matter properties around saturation density and the critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition can be captured by an appropriate choice of CSDC orders, and the results beyond these regions are align with the chiral nuclear force. Moreover, the evolution of scale symmetry was found to be consistent with previous studies. The results of this work indicate that the quantum corrections may be crucial in the studies of nuclear matter in a wide density region.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a new power-counting scheme, chiral-scale density counting (CSDC), for applying chiral-scale effective field theory to nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures, and finds that zero-temperature symmetric nuclear matter properties around saturation density and the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition can be captured by an appropriate choice of CSDC orders, with results beyond these regions aligning with the chiral nuclear force.

## Method

The authors extended the two-flavor \(bs\)HLS (baryon-scalar-hidden local symmetry) model, which incorporates vector mesons \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) via hidden local symmetry and the scalar meson \(\sigma\) as a dilaton of scale symmetry, to finite temperature and density. They developed the CSDC rules, where the characteristic momentum \(k_{\rm{c}}\) (up to \(\sim 700\) MeV) is used to organize the Lagrangian, with the free fermion gas at leading order \(\mathcal{O}(k_{\rm{c}}^4)\), one-boson-exchange interactions at next-to-leading order \(\mathcal{O}(k_{\rm{c}}^6)\), and multi-meson couplings at higher orders. The nuclear matter equation of state was then calculated using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation, neglecting exchange (Fock) terms, to study properties at zero and finite temperature.

## Key numbers

*   The characteristic momentum for the CSDC rules is up to \(k_{\rm{c}} \sim 700\) MeV, corresponding to densities \(n \lesssim 10 n_0\).
*   The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), which corresponds to a Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.68\) fm\(^{-1} \sim 330\) MeV.
*   The cutoff for standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT) is typically set at \(\Lambda \sim (400-500)\) MeV.
*   The CSDC order \(\mathcal{O}(k_{\rm{c}}^{12})\) is stated to be good enough to capture the nuclear matter properties around saturation density and the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition.
*   The framework is estimated to be valid for temperatures roughly \(T \lesssim 100\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the \(bs\)HLS model, previously applied to zero-temperature dense nuclear matter in studies by Zhang et al. (2024) and Ma et al. (2018, 2019), to finite temperatures. It provides a systematic power-counting scheme (CSDC) that contrasts with the standard chiral effective field theory (S\(\chi\)EFT) of Epelbaum et al. (2008), which is limited to densities around \(n_0\) due to its cutoff, and with Walecka-type models, which lack the chiral symmetry pattern of QCD. The results enable systematic studies of the equation of state for nuclear matter in conditions relevant to supernova explosions, neutron star mergers, and heavy-ion collisions, and indicate that quantum corrections may be crucial for a wide density region.
