# The nuclear component of the EMC effect beyond the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation

> We study the response of a nucleus composed of nucleons and confined quarks. Assuming dynamics implied by a non-relativistic cluster model, we prove that the total response is a convolution of the responses of, respectively, a nucleus with interacting point-nucleons and an isolated nucleon with confined quarks. Defining as an intermediary structure functions in terms of the non-relativistic limit of light-cone variables, we subsequently conjecture a generalization to the relativistic regime. That result contains a nuclear part with full inter-nucleon dynamics and allows the study of approximations, as are the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation and a modification of it. The framework also permits a clean treatment of the response of a moving composite nucleon in the nucleus which may be off-shell.

## Key claim

The paper proves that, under the assumptions of a non-relativistic cluster model with separable quark and nucleon degrees of freedom, the total nuclear structure function is an exact convolution of the structure function for a nucleus of point-nucleons and that for an isolated nucleon; the authors then conjecture a generalization of this convolution to the relativistic regime, which contains the full inter-nucleon dynamics and allows study of approximations such as the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) and modifications thereof.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic cluster model Hamiltonian where quarks move in a confining potential and nucleons interact via a nuclear potential that does not depend on internal quark coordinates, and vice versa. They prove that the total response factorizes into a convolution of the nuclear response (for point-nucleons) and the nucleon response (for confined quarks). Using the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) representation, they analyze the high-momentum-transfer behavior and then introduce light-cone variables to conjecture a relativistic extension of the convolution formula, which they apply to the PWIA and a modified West-type approximation for the nuclear part.

## Key numbers

- The GRS-West scaling variable is defined as \(y = -q/2 + m\nu/q\), where \(m\) is the mass of the struck particle (quark or nucleon).
- The asymptotic reduced response for a nucleus of point-nucleons is \(\lim_{q\to\infty} \phi_{N/A}(q,Y) = \int \mathcal{N}(P) \delta(Y - P_z) d\mathbf{P}\), where \(\mathcal{N}(P)\) is the single-nucleon momentum distribution.
- The asymptotic total reduced response for the composite system is \(\lim_{q\to\infty} \phi_{\alpha/A}(q,y) = \int \tilde{n}(p) \delta(y - p_z) d\mathbf{p}\), with \(\tilde{n}(p) = \int \mathcal{N}(P) n(\mathbf{p} - (m/M)\mathbf{P}) d\mathbf{P}\).
- The relativistic convolution conjecture is \(f_{\alpha/A}(Q^2, x_{\alpha/A}) = \int_{x_{\alpha/A}}^{M_A/M} dx_{N/A} f_{N/A}(Q^2, x_{N/A}) f_{\alpha/N}(Q^2, x_{\alpha/A}/x_{N/A})\), where \(x\) are light-cone momentum fractions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the EMC effect by providing a clean separation of nuclear and nucleonic contributions beyond the standard Plane Wave Impulse Approximation used in prior treatments (e.g., Bickerstaff and Thomas (1989), Dunne and Thomas (1985), and others cited in the paper). The authors build on the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) formalism (1973) and the West approximation (1975) to derive exact convolution results in a non-relativistic cluster model, then conjecture a relativistic generalization. This framework enables future calculations of the nuclear component of the EMC effect using realistic nuclear spectral functions and momentum distributions, and allows systematic study of final-state interaction corrections beyond the PWIA.
