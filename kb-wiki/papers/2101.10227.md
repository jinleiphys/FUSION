# A Trailhead for Quantum Simulation of SU(3) Yang-Mills Lattice Gauge Theory in the Local Multiplet Basis

> Maintaining local interactions in the quantum simulation of gauge field theories relegates most states in the Hilbert space to be unphysical -- theoretically benign, but experimentally difficult to avoid. Reformulations of the gauge fields can modify the ratio of physical to gauge-variant states often through classically preprocessing the Hilbert space and modifying the representation of the field on qubit degrees of freedom. This paper considers the implications of representing SU(3) Yang-Mills gauge theory on a lattice of irreducible representations in both a global basis of projected global quantum numbers and a local basis in which controlled-plaquette operators support efficient time evolution. Classically integrating over the internal gauge space at each vertex (e.g., color isospin and color hypercharge) significantly reduces both the qubit requirements and the dimensionality of the unphysical Hilbert space. Initiating tuning procedures that may inform future calculations at scale, the time evolution of one- and two-plaquettes are implemented on one of IBM's superconducting quantum devices, and early benchmark quantities are identified. The potential advantages of qudit environments, with either constrained 2D hexagonal or 1D nearest-neighbor internal state connectivity, are discussed for future large-scale calculations.

## Key claim

Representing SU(3) Yang-Mills lattice gauge theory in a local multiplet basis, where the internal gauge space at each vertex is classically integrated over, significantly reduces both the qubit requirements and the dimensionality of the unphysical Hilbert space, but the authors note that the comparison between SU(2) and SU(3) is complicated by the non-linear behavior in color space of SU(3), and the expectation that SU(3) color fields are also localized in irrep space remains to be verified by direct simulation.

## Method

The authors reformulate SU(3) Yang-Mills theory on a lattice using irreducible representations in both a global basis of projected quantum numbers and a local basis where controlled-plaquette operators support efficient time evolution. They implement Trotterized time evolution of one- and two-plaquette systems on IBM's superconducting quantum device (the "Athens" processor), and provide explicit enumerations of the 81 operators in the {1, 3, 3-bar} local basis contributing to magnetic time evolution. They also analyze the SU(2) single-plaquette case analytically, deriving the Hamiltonian matrix in the basis of Casimir eigenstates and showing that low-lying wavefunctions satisfy a Weber type-A differential equation with Parabolic Cylinder function solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(2) single plaquette, the asymptotic wavefunction scales as exp(-g^2/(2*sqrt(2)) * (j+1/2)^2), demonstrating Gaussian convergence in field space, with rapid convergence expected for j greater than or approximately equal to 4/g.
- For the SU(3) single plaquette with g=1, the exact first maximum of the electric energy expectation value is 0.9389 in the infinite global basis, while the color parity truncation at the 3 representation gives 0.7967 and at the 6 representation gives 0.9296.
- For the SU(3) single plaquette with g=1, the exact first minimum of the electric energy expectation value is 0.0234 in the infinite global basis, while the color parity truncation at the 3 representation gives 0.0000 and at the 6 representation gives 0.0206.
- On the IBM Athens quantum processor, the first maximum of the electric energy for the color parity truncated at 3 representation was measured as 0.829 (+0.039/-0.045), and for the global basis with p,q less than or equal to 1 using 1 Trotter step of order 2 with 3 CNOTs, the measured value was 1.06 plus or minus 0.08 (compared to the Trotterized classical value of 1.1602 and exact value of 0.8699).

## Context

This work extends the program of quantum simulation of gauge theories by addressing the specific challenges of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, building on earlier studies of SU(2) Hamiltonian gauge theory by Chin (1985) and Piekarewicz (1988). The paper provides a concrete path toward large-scale quantum simulations by demonstrating that classically preprocessing the Hilbert space through integration over internal gauge degrees of freedom reduces qubit requirements, and by identifying benchmark quantities from one- and two-plaquette systems on current quantum hardware. The results enable future calculations at scale by establishing tuning procedures and discussing the potential advantages of qudit environments with constrained 2D hexagonal or 1D nearest-neighbor internal state connectivity.
