# Simplified P$_N$ Equations for Nonclassical Transport with Isotropic Scattering

> An asymptotic analysis is used to derive a set of diffusion approximations to the nonclassical transport equation with isotropic scattering. These approximations are shown to reduce to the simplified P$_N$ equations under the assumption of classical transport, and therefore are labeled nonclassical SP$_N$ equations. In addition, the nonclassical SP$_N$ equations can be manipulated into a classical form with modified parameters, which can be implemented in existing SP$_N$ codes. Numerical results are presented for an one-dimensional random periodic system, validating the theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

Using an asymptotic expansion, the authors derive a hierarchy of diffusion approximations (nonclassical SP$_N$ equations) to the nonclassical transport equation with isotropic scattering; these reduce to the classical simplified P$_N$ equations when the free-path distribution is exponential, but the derivation requires the first $2M$ raw moments of the free-path distribution to be finite for the SP$_N$ equations of order $N=M$.

## Method

The authors perform an asymptotic analysis of the one-speed nonclassical transport equation with isotropic scattering, scaling the total cross section as $\Sigma_t(s) = \Sigma_t(s/\epsilon)/\epsilon$, the scattering ratio as $c = 1 - \epsilon^2 \kappa$, and the source as $Q = \epsilon q$, where $\epsilon \ll 1$. They expand the angular flux in a power series in $\epsilon$, integrate over angle and free-path $s$, and truncate the resulting operator equation at successive orders to obtain explicit nonclassical SP$_1$, SP$_2$, and SP$_3$ equations. They then manipulate these equations into a classical form with modified cross sections and sources, enabling the use of standard Marshak vacuum boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The nonclassical SP$_1$ (diffusion) equation is $-\frac{1}{6} \frac{\langle s^2 \rangle}{\langle s \rangle} \nabla^2 \Phi(\mathbf{x}) + \frac{1-c}{\langle s \rangle} \Phi(\mathbf{x}) = Q(\mathbf{x})$, where $\langle s \rangle$ and $\langle s^2 \rangle$ are the first and second raw moments of the free-path distribution $p(s)$.
- For the classical case ($p(s) = \Sigma_t e^{-\Sigma_t s}$), the constants in the nonclassical SP$_2$ and SP$_3$ equations reduce to $\lambda_1 = 4/5$, $\lambda_2 = 11/7$, and $\beta_1 = \beta_2 = 0$.
- In the 1-D random periodic system with layer width $\ell = 0.5$, total width $2X = 4\ell M$, and $M = 1/\epsilon$, the percent relative error of the nonclassical SP$_N$ estimates at the center ($x=0$) decreases as $M$ increases and as $N$ increases, confirming the asymptotic predictions.

## Context

This work extends the nonclassical transport theory developed by Larsen and Vasques (2011) and the nonclassical diffusion equation derived by Larsen (2007) and rigorously analyzed by Fragoulis (2010). It generalizes the classical simplified P$_N$ (SP$_N$) equations first derived by Gelbard (1960, 1961, 1962) and shown to be a high-order asymptotic approximation of the transport equation by Larsen, Morel, and McGhee (1993). The results enable the implementation of nonclassical SP$_N$ approximations in existing SP$_N$ codes by using modified parameters and Marshak boundary conditions, and pave the way for future work on multi-dimensional nonclassical systems, angular-dependent free-path distributions, and anisotropic scattering.
