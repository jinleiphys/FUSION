# Spin-flip doublets of $^9$Be spectrum within a cluster model

> The structure of the $^9$Be low-lying spectrum is studied within the cluster model $α+α+n$. In the model the total orbital momentum is fixed for each energy level. Thus each level is determined as a member of the spin-flip doublet corresponding to the total orbital momentum ($L^π=0^+, 2^+,4^+, 1^-, 2^-,3^-, 4^-$) of the system. The Ali-Bodmer potential (model E) is applied for the $αα$ interaction. We employ a local $αn$ potential which was constructed to reproduce the $α-n$ scattering data. The Pauli blocking is simulated by the repulsive core of the $s$-wave components of these potentials. Configuration space Faddeev equations are used to calculate the energy of the bound state ($E_{cal.}$=-1.493 MeV v.s. $E_{exp.}$=-1.5735 MeV) and resonances. A variant of the method of analytical continuation in the coupling constant is applied to calculate the energies of low-lying levels. Available $^9$Be spectral data are satisfactorily reproduced by the proposed model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-lying spectrum of \(^9\)Be can be satisfactorily reproduced by a cluster model \(\alpha+\alpha+n\) where each energy level is classified as a member of a spin-flip doublet corresponding to a fixed total orbital momentum \(L^\pi\). The hedge is that the model's agreement with experiment is good but not perfect: the calculated spin-doublet spacing for the ground-state doublet is about 2.4 MeV, whereas the experimental value is about 2.68\(\pm\)0.12 MeV, and some states (e.g., \(J^\pi=3/2^-\) with \(L^\pi=2^-\) and \(J^\pi=5/2^+\) with \(L^\pi=2^+\)) show relatively large disagreement.

## Method

The authors solved configuration-space Faddeev equations for the three-body \(\alpha+\alpha+n\) system using a finite-difference approximation with spline collocation. The \(\alpha\alpha\) interaction was described by the Ali-Bodmer potential (model E), and a local \(\alpha n\) potential with central and spin-orbit parts was constructed to reproduce R-matrix fits to \(\alpha-n\) scattering data. Pauli blocking was simulated by repulsive cores in the \(s\)-wave components of both potentials. Resonance energies and widths were obtained via a variant of the method of analytical continuation in the coupling constant, using a non-physical three-body potential as a variational parameter and Padé approximants to continue bound-state energies into the complex plane.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of \(^9\)Be: calculated \(E_{cal.} = -1.493\) MeV versus experimental \(E_{exp.} = -1.5735\) MeV.
- Spin-doublet spacing for the \(L^\pi=1^-\) doublet (ground state \(J^\pi=3/2^-\) and excited \(J^\pi=1/2^-\)): calculated about 2.4 MeV, experimental about 2.68\(\pm\)0.12 MeV.
- Energy of the upper member of the \(L^\pi=1^-\) doublet (\(J^\pi=1/2^-\)): experimental value 1.21\(\pm\)0.12 MeV; calculated value approximately 0.9 MeV.
- Energy of the wide \(J^\pi=3/2^-\) resonance (\(L^\pi=2^-\)): experimental evidence gives 3.4\(\pm\)0.5 MeV, close to the calculated values.
- Predicted excitation energies: \(J^\pi=7/2^+\) resonance about 10 MeV, \(J^\pi=9/2^-\) resonance about 8 MeV, \(J^\pi=7/2^-\) resonance (upper member of \(L^\pi=4^-\)) about 12 MeV.
- The \(\alpha n\) potential was fitted to reproduce \(\alpha-n\) scattering phase shifts for neutron energies up to 4–5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior cluster-model calculations of the \(^9\)Be spectrum by Arai (2000), Tursunov (2006, 2007), and others, using the \(\alpha+\alpha+n\) framework with local potentials that simulate Pauli blocking via repulsive cores. The authors introduce a new \(\alpha n\) potential adjusted to R-matrix scattering data from Nollett (2007) and classify levels as spin-flip doublets with fixed total orbital momentum, a scheme previously applied to the \(\alpha\alpha\Lambda\) system by Filikhin et al. (2004). The results enable predictions for higher-lying resonances (e.g., \(4^+\) and \(4^-\) doublets) and provide a basis for future calculations of the mirror nucleus \(^9\)B, though the authors note that the \(\alpha p\) potential must differ from the \(\alpha n\) potential to avoid systematic overbinding.
