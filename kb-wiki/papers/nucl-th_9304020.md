# No-Lose `Theorem' for Parity Violating Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering Experiments

> A purely left-chiral model of the weak interactions is used to show that the total parity-violating asymmetry in quark-quark scattering must grow with increasing energy. In the absence of other new physics, non-observation of a large asymmetry can therefore be used to infer an upper bound on the mass scale for new right-chiral weak vector bosons. Applying this idea to actual nucleon-nucleon scattering requires more involved calculations, as the dominant contribution appears to come from a component of diquark-quark scattering related to, but not identical to, wavefunction-mixing. Earlier criticism of this model by Simonius and Unger is refuted and a new calculation is proposed as an additional check on the result. Finally, we argue that the so-called `spin crisis' does not affect our conclusions. (Talk given at the BNL workshop on future directions in particle and nuclear physics at multi-GeV hadron beam facilities, March 4--6, 1993.)

# No-Lose 'Theorem' for Parity Violating Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering Experiments

## Key claim
In a purely left-chiral weak interaction model, the parity-violating asymmetry (PVA) in quark-quark scattering must grow with increasing energy below the weak vector boson production threshold; if no large asymmetry is observed at higher energies, this implies an upper bound on the mass scale for new right-chiral weak vector bosons, but applying this to actual nucleon-nucleon scattering requires more involved calculations where the dominant contribution appears to come from a diquark-quark scattering component related to wavefunction-mixing.

## Method
The author calculates one-loop weak corrections to the left-chiral quark-gluon vertex function in a pure SU(2)L model, finding that the left-chiral form-factor falls more rapidly with increasing spacelike four-momentum transfer than the right-chiral one. For nucleon-nucleon scattering, the calculation is extended to include all terms in the non-strange part of the weak Hamiltonian to all orders in perturbation theory using the leading logarithm approximation of the renormalization group, with a dominant contribution from quark-diquark scattering where the diquark undergoes a weak scattering and one of its quarks exchanges a gluon with an intruding quark from the other nucleon.

## Key numbers
- The vertex function decrements are only a tiny fraction of a percent of the dimensionless weak coupling constant at four-momentum transfers of order 1 GeV, but grow by more than an order of magnitude by 10 GeV, and grow to 30% by the TeV region.
- The ANL experiment at 6 GeV/c found a PVA of 2 x 10^{-6}.
- Low energy experiments at 15 and 45 MeV found negative PVA's of order 10^{-7}.
- The author predicts a PVA of approximately 10^{-5} at Brookhaven and almost 10^{-4} at Fermilab.
- Simonius and Unger found 8 barns for the nucleon-nucleon total cross section at a total center-of-mass energy squared (S) of 13 GeV^2, which the author argues is inconsistent with QCD.

## Context
This work extends the meson-exchange approach to parity-violating nucleon-nucleon scattering, where earlier theoretical efforts (e.g., DDH) produced the correct sign and order of magnitude at low energies but failed to explain the large ANL result at 6 GeV/c. The author refutes criticism by Simonius and Unger (1993) and proposes a new calculation using a diquark wavefunction to provide a qualitative check. The results are consistent with data between 6 and 1.5 GeV/c, and the approach enables future experiments to either observe growing PVA or set an upper bound on the mass scale for new right-chiral weak vector bosons. The author argues that the QCD spin crisis does not invalidate the calculation because the PVA contribution comes dominantly from harder scattering of quarks at higher Bjorken x, where valence quark spin distributions are not significantly different from expectation (Kunz, Mulders and Pollock).
