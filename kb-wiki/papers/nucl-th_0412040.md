# The NN final-state interaction in the helicity structure of $\vec d(\vecγ,π^-)pp$ reaction

> The influence of final-state $NN$-rescattering on the helicity structure of the $\vecγ\vec d\toπ^-pp$ reaction in the energy range from $π$-threshold up to 550 MeV has been investigated. The differential polarized cross-section difference for the parallel and antiparallel helicity states is predicted and compared with recent experimental data. It is shown that the effect of $NN$-rescattering is much less important in the polarized differential cross-section difference than in the previously studied unpolarized differential cross section. Furthermore, the contribution of $\vecγ\vec d\toπ^-pp$ to the spin asymmetry of the deuteron is explicitly evaluated over the region of the $Δ$(1232)-resonance with inclusion of $NN$-rescattering. The effect of $NN$ final-state interaction is found to be much larger in the asymmetry than in the total cross section and leads to an appreciable reduction of the spin asymmetry in the $Δ$-region.

## Key claim

The influence of final-state NN-rescattering on the polarized differential cross-section difference for parallel and antiparallel helicity states in the $\vec\gamma\vec d\to\pi^-pp$ reaction is much less important than in the previously studied unpolarized differential cross section, but the effect of NN final-state interaction is much larger in the spin asymmetry than in the total cross section and leads to an appreciable reduction of the spin asymmetry in the Delta(1232)-resonance region.

## Method

The author calculated the transition matrix for $\vec\gamma\vec d\to\pi^-pp$ using the impulse approximation (IA) plus an NN-rescattering term, where the elementary $\gamma N\to\pi N$ operator was taken from an effective Lagrangian model (Schmidt et al., 1996) and the NN-scattering matrix was obtained from a separable representation of the Paris potential (Graz group, 1984-1985). The differential polarized cross-section difference for parallel and antiparallel helicity states and the polarized total cross sections were evaluated by integrating over the final-state phase space, and the results were compared with experimental data from the GDH collaboration and earlier bubble-chamber measurements.

## Key numbers

* The NN-rescattering reduces the unpolarized differential cross section by about 15% at $\theta_{\pi}=0^{\circ}$ (from Dar03).
* The reduction of the spin asymmetry ($\sigma^P-\sigma^A$) due to final-state interaction becomes about 35 $\mu$b at the maximum in the Delta(1232)-resonance region.
* For the unpolarized total cross section, NN-rescattering reduces the cross section in the Delta(1232)-resonance region by about 5 percent.
* The energy range studied is from pion threshold up to 550 MeV photon lab-energy.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Darwish (2003) on unpolarized cross sections for $\gamma d\to\pi^-pp$ to the helicity-dependent observables, and it builds on the diagrammatic approach of Loginov (2000) and the plane-wave impulse approximation studies of Darwish (2004, 2005). The results are compared with experimental data from the GDH collaboration (Pedroni) and earlier bubble-chamber measurements by ABHHM (1973), Frascati (1975), and Asai (1990). The work enables tests of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the deuteron and provides a baseline for future three-body treatments of the final $\pi NN$ system and more sophisticated NN potentials.
