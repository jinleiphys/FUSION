# Leading Order $k_Fa$ Corrections to the Free Energy and Phase Separation in Two-component Fermion Systems

> We study phase separation in a dilute two-component Fermi system with attractive interactions as a function of the coupling strength and the polarization or number density asymmetry between the two components. In weak and strong couplings with a finite number density asymmetry, phase separation is energetically more favorable. A heterogeneous phase containing a symmetric superfluid component and an asymmetric normal phase has lower energy than a homogeneous normal phase. We show that for a small number density asymmetry, taking into consideration the leading order corrections at order $k_Fa$ of the interaction parameter, phase separation is stable against the normal phase in the whole BCS range. We investigate the consequences of the consideration of the leading order $k_Fa$ corrections to the thermodynamic potentials of the normal and BCS phase on the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit. We have also investigated the stability of a Bose-Fermi mixture in the far-BEC limit. We find that the molecular BEC is locally stable against an external magnetic field $h$, provided $|h|$ is smaller than the pairing gap $Δ_{gap}$.

## Key claim

Phase separation between a symmetric superfluid component and an asymmetric normal phase is energetically favorable over a homogeneous normal phase in the whole BCS range ($-\infty < 1/k_F|a| < 0$) when leading order $k_Fa$ corrections are included, but only for small number density asymmetry.

## Method

The author calculates the grand thermodynamic potentials for both the normal and BCS phases of a dilute two-component Fermi gas with attractive interactions, including the first beyond-mean-field corrections proportional to $k_Fa$. Using these corrected potentials, the conditions for phase equilibrium between the normal and superfluid phases are derived via Gibbs conditions, and the energy of the phase-separated state is minimized with respect to the volume fraction of the normal phase. The Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit is then re-evaluated with these corrections, and the stability of a Bose-Fermi mixture in the far-BEC limit is also examined.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential imbalance to pairing gap ratio is $\delta \mu_c/\Delta = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{\sqrt{1+\frac{8 k_F|a|}{3\pi}}}{\sqrt{1+\frac{4 k_F|a|}{3\pi}}}$, which reduces to the mean-field value $1/\sqrt{2}$ when $k_F|a|=0$.
- The critical polarization is $p_c = \frac{3}{\sqrt{2}} \left(\frac{2}{e}\right)^2 e^{-\frac{\pi}{2 k_F |a|}} \sqrt{1+\frac{8 k_F|a|}{3\pi}} \sqrt{1+\frac{4 k_F|a|}{3\pi}}$.
- The spin susceptibility of the normal phase is $\chi_N = \frac{k_F^3}{3\pi^2} \frac{1}{\mu} \left[1 + \frac{2 k_F |a|}{\pi}\right]$.
- The zero-temperature BCS gap in the weak-coupling limit is $\Delta(k_F a) \approx 4 \bar\mu e^{-2 - \frac{\pi}{2 k_F |a|}}$.
- The molecular BEC is locally stable against an external magnetic field $h$ provided $|h| < \Delta_{gap}$.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field phase separation studies of Bedaque (2003) and Caldas (2004) by incorporating the leading order $k_Fa$ corrections to the thermodynamic potentials, following the approach of Sanjay (2005) for the equation of state. The results improve upon the standard Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit and provide analytical expressions that show better agreement with quantum Monte Carlo simulations by Pilati and Giorgini (2008) than pure mean-field theory. The findings enable more accurate predictions for phase separation in trapped cold atom experiments, particularly those by Zwierlein (2006) and Shin (2006) in the strongly interacting regime.
