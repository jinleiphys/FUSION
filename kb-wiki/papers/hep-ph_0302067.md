# Static potential in baryon

> The baryon static potential is calculated in the framework of field correlator method and is shown to match the recent lattice results. The effects of the nonzero value of the gluon correlation length are emphasized.

## Key claim

The baryon static potential calculated in the field correlator method (FCM) completely describes recent lattice QCD results, with the slope of the nonperturbative potential growing from zero to the string tension sigma when the total string length L changes from zero to about 1.5 fm; the nonzero gluon correlation length T_g is the key parameter inducing this change of slope.

## Method

The static potential is extracted from the baryon Wilson loop using the nonabelian Stokes theorem and a bilocal (two-point) approximation for the field strength correlators. The correlators are expressed via two scalar formfactors D(z) and D1(z), where D(z) decays exponentially with the gluon correlation length T_g, and D1(z) gives the perturbative color-Coulomb contribution. The surface integrations over the three quark trajectories and the string junction path are performed, yielding the potential as a function of the distances from each quark to the string junction.

## Key numbers

- String tension sigma = 0.18 GeV^2 (phenomenological value from meson Regge trajectories) and also sigma = 0.22 GeV^2 used in one lattice comparison
- Gluon correlation length T_g = 0.12 fm
- Perturbative potential for equilateral triangle baryon: V_pert(r) = -(3/2) * (C_F * alpha_s / r) with C_F = 4/3 and alpha_s = 0.18
- Energy of gluon excitation in hadron spectra: approximately 1.5 GeV
- Difference between potentials for one-line and three-line string configurations at L=1.8 fm: Delta V_1 = 150 MeV
- Difference between potentials at gamma=2pi/3 and three-line configuration: Delta V_2 = 55 MeV
- Difference between potentials at gamma=pi and gamma=2pi/3: Delta V_3 = 30 MeV
- Diquark radii from relativistic quark model: r_bb = 0.37 fm, r_cc = 0.56 fm

## Context

This work extends the field correlator method (MFC) developed by Simonov and collaborators to the baryon case, matching lattice results from Bali (2000) and Takahashi et al. (2001) for quenched QCD. The calculation shows that the baryon potential has a Y-type structure depending only on distances from quarks to the string junction, with the slope varying from zero to sigma over about 1.5 fm; this contrasts with the meson case where the slope changes little at small distances due to perturbative interference. The results enable refined potential model calculations of baryon spectra and wave functions, and suggest that quark-diquark configurations are not strongly favored by nonperturbative effects.
