# Centrality fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> Volume or centrality fluctuations (CF) is one of the main uncertainties for interpreting the centrality dependence of many experimental observables. The CF is constrained by centrality selection based on particle multiplicity in a reference subevent, and contributes to observables measured in another subevent. Using a Glauber-based independent source model, we study the influence of CF on several distributions of multiplicity $N$ and eccentricities $ε_n$: $p(N)$, $p(ε_n)$, $p(ε_n,ε_m)$ and $p(N,ε_n)$, where the effects of CF is quantified using multi-particle cumulants of these distributions. In mid-central collisions, a general relation is established between the multiplicity fluctuation and resulting CF in the reference subevent. In ultra-central collisions, where distribution of particle production sources is strongly distorted, we find these cumulants exhibit rich sign-change patterns, due to observable-dependent non-Gaussianity in the underlying distributions. The details of sign-change pattern change with the size of the collision systems. Simultaneous comparison of these different types cumulants between model prediction and experimental data can be used to constrain the CF and particle production mechanism in heavy-ion collisions. Since the concept of centrality and CF are expected to fluctuate in the longitudinal direction within a single event, we propose to use pseudorapidity-separated subevent cumulant method to explore the nature of intra-event fluctuations of centrality and collective dynamics. The subevent method can be applied for any bulk observable that is sensitive to centrality, and has the potential to separate different mechanisms for multiplicity and flow fluctuations happening at different time scales. The forward detector upgrades at RHIC and LHC will greatly enhance such studies in the future.

## Key claim

Centrality fluctuations (CF) arising from centrality selection based on particle multiplicity in a reference subevent contribute to observables measured in another subevent, and in ultra-central collisions these cumulants exhibit rich sign-change patterns due to observable-dependent non-Gaussianity in the underlying distributions, with the details of the sign-change pattern changing with the size of the collision system.

## Method

The authors used a Glauber-based independent source model where particle production from each source follows a negative binomial distribution (NBD), with sources generated via a standard Glauber model for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. Events were divided into two subevents: subevent A for centrality selection and subevent B for calculating multiplicity and eccentricity cumulants, with particle multiplicities generated independently from the same number of sources in each event. The centrality fluctuations were induced by placing narrow selections on reference multiplicity in subevent A, and their influences on multi-particle cumulants of p(N), p(epsilon_n), p(epsilon_n,epsilon_m), and p(N,epsilon_n) were calculated.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section of sigma=68 mb used to simulate collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- Two-component model parameter x=0.09, close to values used at top RHIC energy
- NBD parameter sets: Par0 (p=0.688, m=3.45, mean n_bar=7.6, RMS/mean hat_sigma=0.65), Par1 (p=0.831, m=1.55, mean n_bar=7.6, RMS/mean hat_sigma=0.88), Par2 (p=0.928, m=0.593, mean n_bar=7.6, RMS/mean hat_sigma=1.35) for wounded nucleon model
- For two-component model: Par0 (p=0.391, m=13.7, mean n_bar=8.7, RMS/mean hat_sigma=0.43), Par1 (p=0.738, m=3.10, mean n_bar=8.7, RMS/mean hat_sigma=0.66), Par2 (p=0.909, m=0.878, mean n_bar=8.7, RMS/mean hat_sigma=1.12)
- Second-order cumulant ratio r_2 = (n_bar * k_2^v)/k_2 = 1 in mid-central collisions
- Third-order cumulant ratio r_3 = (n_bar^2 * k_3^v)/k_3 = (2-p)/(1+p), with range 1/2 <= r_3 <= 2
- Fourth-order cumulant ratio r_4 = (n_bar^3 * k_4^v)/k_4 = (p^2+6(1-p))/((1-p)^2+6p), with range 1/6 <= r_4 <= 6

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Skokov et al. (2012) who first pointed out the importance of CF for multiplicity fluctuation measurements, and by Xu et al. (2016) who derived relations between multiplicity cumulants and CF. The paper builds on the ATLAS Collaboration's observation of a characteristic sign-change of four-particle cumulant for v_2 in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions (2017). The proposed subevent cumulant method, recently applied for flow correlations by Jia et al. (2017), Aaboud et al. (2017), Huo et al. (2017), and Nie et al. (2018), can be applied to any bulk observable sensitive to centrality and has the potential to separate different mechanisms for multiplicity and flow fluctuations happening at different time scales.
