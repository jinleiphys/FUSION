# Do We Accurately Know the Formation of Solar 8B?

> A detailed examination of current data on S_17 (as opposed to an examination of S_17(0) only) excludes quoting S_17(0) with sufficiently small uncertainty. In contrast to suggestions that S_17(0) is now known with the accuracy of \pm 3%, the exact value of S_17(0) is dependent on the choice of the data and the choice of theory used for extrapolation. In addition recent high precision results (including the Seattle data) on S_17 which are in good agreement, still differ on the measured slopes, as do theoretical models that predict different d-wave contribution, precluding an accurate extrapolation to zero energy of the consistent data. Using a common extrapolation of only the consistent high precision data, suggests a value of S_17(0) = 21.2 \pm 0.5 eV-b, but a value equal to or smaller than 19.0 eV-b can not be excluded due to the uncertainty in the extrapolation, leading to an additional error of +0.0 -3.0 eV-b. This (unacceptable) situation must be cleared by future experiments.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the solar fusion cross-section factor S_17(0) for the reaction 7Be(p,gamma)8B is not known to the claimed ±3% accuracy; its true value depends on which data sets and which theoretical extrapolation model are chosen, and an additional asymmetric uncertainty of +0.0 -3.0 eV-b from extrapolation ambiguity means values as low as 19.0 eV-b cannot be excluded.

## Method
The author re-examined published high-precision data on S_17 (the energy-dependent astrophysical S-factor) rather than only the zero-energy intercept S_17(0), comparing measured slopes and d-wave contributions across experiments including the Seattle data. A common extrapolation was applied only to the subset of data judged consistent, and the resulting uncertainty was assessed by varying the theoretical model used for the extrapolation to zero energy.

## Key numbers
- S_17(0) = 21.2 ± 0.5 eV-b from a common extrapolation of only the consistent high-precision data.
- An additional extrapolation uncertainty of +0.0 -3.0 eV-b means a value equal to or smaller than 19.0 eV-b cannot be excluded.
- The claimed ±3% accuracy for S_17(0) is rejected as insufficiently justified.

## Context
This work challenges the prevailing claim (e.g., from the Seattle group and others around 2003-2004) that S_17(0) was known to ±3% accuracy. It extends earlier concerns about extrapolation model dependence in the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, which is critical for predicting the solar 8B neutrino flux. The paper calls for future experiments to resolve the slope discrepancies and d-wave contribution uncertainties that prevent a reliable zero-energy extrapolation.
