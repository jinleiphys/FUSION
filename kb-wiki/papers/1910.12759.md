# Is Hyperon Polarization in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions Connected to Axial U(1) Symmetry Breaking at High Temperature?

> Experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have measured the net polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons and attributed it to a coupling between their spin and the vorticity of the fluid created in heavy ion collisions, but how the spin comes to equilibrium with vorticity is an open problem. Recently we found that vorticity fluctuations and helicity flip of strange quarks in quark-gluon plasma through perturbative QCD processes resulted in equilibration times far too long to be relevant. Here we consider the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with the inclusion of the six-quark Kobayashi--Maskawa--'t Hooft interaction which breaks axial U(1). Using instanton inspired models for the temperature dependence of the axial symmetry breaking, we find that constituent strange quarks can reach spin equilibrium at temperatures below about 170 MeV, just before they hadronize to form hyperons.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the observed polarization of Λ and \(\bar{\Lambda}\) hyperons in relativistic heavy ion collisions may be explained by strange quark helicity flip mediated by the non-perturbative six-quark Kobayashi–Maskawa–'t Hooft interaction, but only if the axial U(1) symmetry breaking is sufficiently strong at temperatures just above the hadronization crossover (below about 170 MeV). This mechanism is conditional on the temperature dependence of the effective coupling, which is modeled using instanton-inspired parametrizations.

## Method
The authors calculate the helicity-flip relaxation time for strange quarks scattering off light quarks (u, d) using an effective four-quark interaction derived from the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model augmented by the six-quark Kobayashi–Maskawa–'t Hooft interaction. They apply a mean-field reduction to obtain the scattering amplitude, then compute the equilibration time \(\tau(E)\) via a standard kinetic theory integral over Mandelstam variables \(s\) and \(t\). Two instanton-inspired models for the temperature dependence of the six-quark coupling \(g_D(T)\) are used: an exponential form (case I) and a power-law form (case II), with constituent quark masses and condensates taken from NJL model fits.

## Key numbers
- The zero-temperature six-quark coupling is taken as \(g_D = -100\ \text{GeV}^{-5}\), with a stated 10% uncertainty, based on fits to the \(\eta'\) mass and topological susceptibility.
- For case I (exponential temperature dependence with \(T_0 = 100\ \text{MeV}\)), the strange quark helicity equilibration time \(\tau < 2\ \text{fm}/c\) when \(T < 175\ \text{MeV}\).
- For case II (power-law dependence with \(\bar{\rho} = 0.33\ \text{fm}\)), \(\tau < 2\ \text{fm}/c\) when \(T < 165\ \text{MeV}\).
- The equilibration time at a representative momentum \(p = 200\ \text{MeV}\) differs by about a factor of 20 between the two models at a given temperature.
- The constituent quark masses at \(T=0\) are 335 MeV for light quarks and 527 MeV for strange quarks, with temperature dependence taken from NJL model results (case II of Ref. 2Treview).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior perturbative QCD calculation (Kapusta, Rrapaj, Rudaz, 2019) which found that helicity flip via perturbative processes yields equilibration times far too long to be relevant for heavy ion collisions. Here they instead use the non-perturbative NJL model with the Kobayashi–Maskawa–'t Hooft interaction, which is known to solve the U(1)\(_A\) problem (Weinberg, 1975) and provide good hadron phenomenology (Kunihiro and Hatsuda, 1984). The results suggest that axial U(1) symmetry breaking at temperatures just above the crossover (around 155–175 MeV) could enable strange quark spin equilibration on timescales comparable to the hadronization time (3–5 fm/c), potentially explaining the hyperon polarization observed by the STAR collaboration. The paper calls for further theoretical and lattice QCD studies of the temperature dependence of axial U(1) symmetry restoration.
