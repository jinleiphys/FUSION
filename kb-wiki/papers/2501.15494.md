# Three flavor QCD phase transition with Möbius domain wall fermions

> We present an updated study of the $N_f=3$ QCD phase transition using Möbius domain wall fermions. Simulations were performed on $N_t=12$ lattices with aspect ratios ranging from 2 to 4 for various quark masses, at a lattice spacing of $a=0.1361(20)$ fm, corresponding to a temperature of 121(2) MeV. To clarify the nature of the phase transition, a large-volume lattice, $48^3 \times 12\times 16$, was added to analyze the volume dependence of disconnected chiral susceptibility. By examining the chiral condensate, disconnected chiral susceptibility, and Binder cumulant, and incorporating results from $24^3 \times 12 \times 16$ and $36^3 \times 12 \times 16$ lattices reported in earlier studies, we observe that the transition is consistent with a crossover at a quark mass of approximately $m_f^{\mathrm{\overline {MS}}}(2\, \mathrm{GeV}) \sim 4$ MeV at this temperature. Furthermore, we discuss the effects of residual chiral symmetry breaking on the chiral condensate and disconnected chiral susceptibility for different sizes in the 5th direction.

# Three flavor QCD phase transition with Möbius domain wall fermions

## Key claim
The Nf=3 QCD phase transition at a temperature of 121(2) MeV is consistent with a smooth crossover at a quark mass of approximately m_f^MSbar(2 GeV) ~ 4 MeV, rather than a first-order or second-order phase transition. The authors hedge that while finite-volume effects are observed in the disconnected chiral susceptibility, the volume dependence is not as large as anticipated from a genuine first-order or Z(2) second-order phase transition, leaving open the possibility that a first-order region could exist at smaller quark masses.

## Method
The study uses Nf=3 lattice QCD simulations with tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action and Möbius domain wall fermions on Nt=12 lattices at fixed gauge coupling beta=4.0, corresponding to lattice spacing a=0.1361(20) fm. Simulations were performed on 24^3 x 12 x 16, 36^3 x 12 x 16, and 48^3 x 12 x 16 lattices with various quark masses, plus additional 24^3 x 12 x 32 lattices to study residual chiral symmetry breaking effects. The chiral condensate, disconnected chiral susceptibility, and Binder cumulant were analyzed to determine the nature of the phase transition, with zero-temperature ensembles used to determine lattice spacing, residual mass, and remove ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1361(20) fm, corresponding to temperature T = 121(2) MeV
- Residual mass for Ls=16: am_res(am_f=0) = 0.00613(9); for Ls=32: am_res(am_f=0) = 0.00324(3)
- Coefficient of UV divergence term in chiral condensate: C^D = 1.12(6)
- Coefficient x in UV divergence term C^D (m_f + x m_res)/a^2: x = -0.6(1)
- Transition mass point from peak of disconnected chiral susceptibility: (m_f + m_res)^MSbar(2 GeV) ~ 3.6 MeV
- Quark mass at transition: m_f^MSbar(2 GeV) ~ 4 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies using staggered fermions (Bazavov 2017, Dini 2021) and O(a)-improved Wilson fermions (Kuramashi 2020) that found no direct evidence of a first-order transition for pion masses above 50-110 MeV, and supports the scenario where the Nf=3 chiral phase transition is second-order in the chiral limit as suggested by functional renormalization group analyses (Fejos 2022, 2024) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Bernhardt 2023). The use of Möbius domain wall fermions provides exact chiral symmetry in the Ls to infinity limit, offering a complementary approach to staggered and Wilson fermion formulations that partially or completely break chiral symmetry at finite lattice spacing. This work enables future studies to determine whether a first-order region exists at quark masses below 4 MeV and to quantify the critical pion mass if such a region exists.
