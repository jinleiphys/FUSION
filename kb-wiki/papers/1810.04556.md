# The s-process nucleosynthesis in low mass stars: impact of the uncertainties in the nuclear physics determined by Monte Carlo variations

> We investigated the impact of uncertainties in neutron-capture and weak reactions (on heavy elements) on the s-process nucleosynthesis in low-mass stars using a Monte-Carlo based approach. We performed extensive nuclear reaction network calculations that include newly evaluated temperature-dependent upper and lower limits for the individual reaction rates. Our sophisticated approach is able to evaluate the reactions that impact more significantly the final abundances. We found that beta-decay rate uncertainties affect typically nuclides near s-process branchings, whereas most of the uncertainty in the final abundances is caused by uncertainties in neutron capture rates, either directly producing or destroying the nuclide of interest. Combined total nuclear uncertainties due to reactions on heavy elements are approximately 50%.

## Key claim

The combined total nuclear uncertainties due to reactions on heavy elements in the s-process nucleosynthesis of low-mass stars are approximately 50%, but this overall figure masks that most individual nuclide abundances have uncertainties smaller than 50% while a few nuclides around branching points show uncertainties larger than a factor of two.

## Method

The authors performed extensive nuclear reaction network calculations using a Monte Carlo framework that simultaneously varied all neutron-capture and weak reaction rates on heavy elements, incorporating newly evaluated temperature-dependent upper and lower limits for each reaction rate. They approximated the thermodynamic conditions inside a low-mass AGB star with a single trajectory following the key phase of a 13C pocket in a 3 solar mass star of solar metallicity, repeating the simulation 10000 times to complete the sensitivity study.

## Key numbers

- Combined total nuclear uncertainties due to reactions on heavy elements are approximately 50%.
- Most individual nuclide abundance uncertainties are smaller than 50%.
- Several nuclides have uncertainties larger than a factor of two, particularly those around branching points such as Kr-86.
- The relevant temperature range for the 13C pocket is approximately 8 keV.
- The stellar model used is a 3 solar mass star of solar metallicity.
- Three neutron-capture rates play a significant role in the uncertainty for many nuclides: those on Fe-56, Ni-64, and Ba-138.

## Context

This work extends the Monte Carlo sensitivity approach previously applied by Rauscher (2016) to the s-process in massive stars and by Nishimura (2017, 2018) to the gamma-process in core collapse supernovae and supernovae type Ia. The study uses the same temperature-dependent uncertainty methodology as those prior works but now focuses on the main s-process in low-mass AGB stars, where the complex TP-AGB phase evolution (Cristallo 2015) makes full stellar simulations impractical for the 10000 runs required. The results identify the specific neutron-capture rates that dominate the nuclear uncertainties, enabling future experimental efforts to target those key reactions to reduce the overall uncertainty in s-process abundance predictions.
