# Superfluid density in disordered pasta phases in neutron star crusts

> We calculate the superfluid density of nucleons in disordered pasta phases in the inner crust of neutron stars using an effective medium approach which parallels that previously used for calculating the electrical conductivity of terrestrial matter. We allow for the effect of entrainment, the fact that the current density of one species of nucleon depends on the gradient of the phase not only of the same species but also of the other species. The superfluid density tensors for the perfectly ordered pasta phases are very anisotropic, and we derive expression for the effective superfluid densities of the disordered phases.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the effective superfluid density of neutrons and protons in disordered pasta phases (lasagna and spaghetti) in neutron star crusts, using an effective medium approach that assumes the system consists of randomly oriented domains; the key hedge is that detailed predictions require better calculations of the superfluid density tensor for perfectly ordered phases, which are not yet available.

## Method

The authors adopt an effective medium approach analogous to that used for electrical conductivity of polycrystals and binary metallic mixtures (Bruggeman 1935, Landauer 1952, Stroud 1975). They consider a spherical inclusion with anisotropic superfluid density embedded in a homogeneous isotropic medium, then demand that on averaging over all possible orientations of the inclusion, the response of the system matches that of the surrounding medium. For the two-component case with entrainment (the coupling between neutron and proton currents), the effective superfluid density is obtained by solving a matrix equation derived from the condition that the inclusion gives no net scattering.

## Key numbers

- For the lasagna phase with no entrainment, when the superfluid density for flow perpendicular to the sheets is zero, the effective superfluid density drops to only one half of the value for flow in the plane of the sheets.
- For the spaghetti phase with no entrainment, when the superfluid density for flow perpendicular to the strands vanishes, the effective superfluid density is zero; for small perpendicular component, \(n_{\alpha\alpha}^{\rs\rme} \simeq \sqrt{n_{\alpha\alpha}^{\rs\perp} n_{\alpha\alpha}^{\rs\para}/2}\).
- The London length for a charged superfluid is of order 110 fm for a proton fraction of 5% and a density of half nuclear saturation density.
- The lattice spacing for the lasagna phase is estimated to be about 44 fm (Zhang and Pethick 2021).

## Context

This work extends the effective medium approach previously applied to elastic properties of polycrystals (Berryman 1980, Kobyakov and Pethick 2014, Pethick, Zhang, and Kobyakov 2017) to the superfluid density of nucleons in disordered pasta phases. The results enable improved modeling of glitches in neutron star rotation rates (which depend on neutron superfluid density) and magnetic properties (which depend on proton superfluid density). The paper notes that detailed calculations of the superfluid density tensor for perfectly ordered pasta phases are needed, and suggests extending the calculations of Watanabe and Pethick (2017) for fermions in sinusoidal potentials to more realistic pasta structures.
