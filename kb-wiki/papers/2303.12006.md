# Shedding light on shadow generalized parton distributions

> The feasibility of extracting generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from deeply-virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) data has recently been questioned because of the existence of an infinite set of so-called ''shadow GPDs'' (SGPDs). These SGPDs depend on the process and manifest as multiple solutions (at a fixed scale $Q^2$) to the inverse problem that needs to be solved to infer GPDs from DVCS data. SGPDs therefore pose a significant challenge for extracting GPDs from DVCS data. With this motivation we study the extent to which QCD evolution can provide constraints on SGPDs. This is possible because the known classes of SGPDs begin to contribute to observables after evolution, and can then be constrained (at the input scale $Q^2_0$) by data that has a finite $Q^2$ range. The impact that SGPDs could have on determining the total angular momentum, pressure and sheer force distributions, and tomography is also discussed. Our key finding is that scale evolution, coupled with data over a wide range of skewness $ξ$ and $Q^2$, can constrain the class of SGPDs that we studied and potentially make possible the extraction of GPDs from DVCS data over a limited range in the GPD variables.

## Key claim

QCD evolution, combined with deeply-virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) data over a wide range of skewness ξ and Q², can constrain the class of shadow generalized parton distributions (SGPDs) studied in this work, potentially making possible the extraction of GPDs from DVCS data over a limited range in the GPD variables, contrary to earlier suggestions that the impact of evolution would be very small.

## Method

The authors constructed a proxy model for the "true" GPDs using the Vanderhaeghen-Guichon-Guidal (VGG) phenomenological model with JAM20-SIDIS parton distribution functions at an input scale μ₀ = 1.27 GeV. They generated simulated DVCS data for the imaginary parts of the Compton form factors ℋ and ℰ with 1% uncertainties across Q² values from (1.27 GeV)² to 100 GeV², then introduced three example SGPDs of order N=27 with arbitrary scaling parameters λᵢ. By randomly sampling λᵢ values between -10⁴ and 10⁴ and checking which combinations produced CFFs within the 1% error bands, they determined the range of SGPDs that cannot be ruled out by the simulated data.

## Key numbers

- Input scale: μ₀ = 1.27 GeV, taken from the JAM20-SIDIS global analysis
- Evolution scale: μ² = 100 GeV² (LO evolution)
- Simulated data: 50 data points for Q² values between Q₀² = (1.27 GeV)² and Q² = 100 GeV²
- Assigned error on simulated CFF data: 1%
- t value used in VGG model: t = -1.2 GeV²
- Skewness values studied: ξ = 0.01, 0.1, 0.5
- SGPD order: N = 27, with (N+1)(N+3)/8 = 105 unknown coefficients cₘₙ
- Random sampling range for λᵢ: -10⁴ ≤ λᵢ ≤ 10⁴
- Number of replica SGPDs generated: 10000

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bertone (2021) and Bertone (2021), who first demonstrated that an infinite set of SGPDs makes GPD extraction from DVCS data non-unique at a fixed scale Q². The authors explicitly solve the QCD evolution equations for GPDs to test whether evolution can constrain SGPDs, a possibility first suggested by Freund (1999). The results enable future GPD extractions from DVCS data at facilities including Jefferson Lab, COMPASS at CERN, and the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider, provided data spans a sufficient range in Q² and skewness.
