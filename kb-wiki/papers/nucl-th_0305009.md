# Critical-Point Symmetry in a Finite System

> At a critical point of a second order phase transition the intrinsic energy surface is flat and there is no stable minimum value of the deformation. However, for a finite system, we show that there is an effective deformation which can describe the dynamics at the critical point. This effective deformation is determined by minimizing the energy surface after projection onto the appropriate symmetries. We derive analytic expressions for energies and quadrupole rates which provide good estimates for these observables at the critical point.

## Key claim

For a finite system at the critical point of a second-order shape phase transition, an effective deformation can describe the dynamics even though the intrinsic energy surface is flat and has no stable minimum; this effective deformation is determined by minimizing the energy surface after projection onto the appropriate symmetries, not by the usual global minimum of the unprojected surface.

## Method

The authors work within the interacting boson model (IBM) for a system of \(N\) bosons and consider the Hamiltonian at the critical point of the \(U(5)\)–\(O(6)\) phase transition, where the parameters satisfy \(\epsilon = (N-1)A\). They construct analytic wave functions with fixed \(N\) and good \(O(5)\) symmetry by projecting from intrinsic states containing a parameter \(y = \beta^2\), where \(\beta\) is the quadrupole deformation. The effective deformation \(y\) is determined by minimizing the \(O(5)\)-projected energy surface \(E_{\xi=1,\tau=0}(y)\) of the critical Hamiltonian, rather than the unprojected intrinsic energy surface. Using this \(y\), they derive closed-form expressions for energies and \(B(E2)\) transition rates for the ground band (\(\xi=1\)) and first excited band (\(\xi=2\)).

## Key numbers

* For \(N=5\) (relevant to \(^{134}\)Ba), the effective deformation parameter is \(y = 0.314\), obtained as the global minimum of the projected energy surface \(E_{1,0}(y)\).
* The band-mixing parameters \(\eta_{\tau}\) between the \(\xi=1\) and \(\xi=2\) bands for \(N=5\) and \(y=0.314\) are: \(\eta_0 = 0.12\%\), \(\eta_1 = 3.53\%\), \(\eta_2 = 4.14\%\), \(\eta_3 = 3.05\%\).
* The critical Hamiltonian parameters satisfy \(\epsilon = (N-1)A\), with the intrinsic energy surface given by \(E(\beta) = E_0 + A\,N(N-1)\,\beta^4(1+\beta^2)^{-2}\) and \(E_0 = \frac{1}{4}AN(N-1)\).

## Context

This work extends the geometric E(5) critical-point symmetry of Iachello (2000) to finite boson systems using the algebraic framework of the interacting boson model (IBM). It provides analytic estimates for energies and quadrupole rates at the critical point without invoking large-\(N\) approximations, complementing numerical IBM calculations for finite \(N\) (e.g., \(N=5\) for \(^{134}\)Ba) that had previously replicated E(5) predictions. The approach is analogous to the method of Otsuka (1987), where a \(\gamma\)-unstable \(O(6)\) state was generated from a rigid triaxial intrinsic state with an effective \(\gamma\)-deformation; here the \(\gamma\)-instability is treated exactly via \(O(5)\) symmetry while the \(\beta\)-instability is handled by an effective deformation. The results enable analytic parameter-free predictions for energy ratios and \(B(E2)\) ratios at the critical point that lie between the spherical vibrator [\(U(5)\)] and deformed \(\gamma\)-unstable rotor [\(O(6)\)] limits.
