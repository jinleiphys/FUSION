# Time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for multinucleon transfer and quasifission processes in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction

> Background: Multinucleon transfer (MNT) and quasifission (QF) processes are dominant processes in low-energy collisions of two heavy nuclei. They are expected to be useful to produce neutron-rich unstable nuclei. Nuclear dynamics leading to these processes depends sensitively on nuclear properties such as deformation and shell structure. Purpose: We elucidate reaction mechanisms of MNT and QF processes involving heavy deformed nuclei, making detailed comparisons between microscopic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations and measurements for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction. Methods: Three-dimensional Skyrme-TDHF calculations are performed. Particle-number projection method is used to evaluate MNT cross sections from the TDHF wave function after collision. Results: Fragment masses, total kinetic energy (TKE), scattering angle, contact time, and MNT cross sections are investigated for the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction. They show reasonable agreements with measurements. At small impact parameters, collision dynamics depends sensitively on the orientation of deformed $^{238}$U. In tip (side) collisions, we find a larger (smaller) TKE and a shorter (longer) contact time. In tip collisions, we find a strong influence of quantum shells around $^{208}$Pb. Conclusions: It is confirmed that the TDHF calculations reasonably describe both MNT and QF processes in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction. Analyses of this system indicates the significance of the nuclear structure effects such as deformation and quantum shells in nuclear reaction dynamics at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations reasonably describe both multinucleon transfer (MNT) and quasifission (QF) processes in the $^{64}$Ni+$^{238}$U reaction, but this agreement is conditional on the initial orientation of the deformed $^{238}$U target and is limited by the neglect of pairing correlations, which the authors state may be important for open-shell nuclei but are omitted due to computational cost.

## Method

Three-dimensional Skyrme-TDHF calculations were performed using the SLy5 parameter set on a uniform grid with 0.8 fm mesh spacing, with the time-evolution operator treated by fourth-order Taylor expansion. The particle-number projection method was applied to the TDHF wave function after collision to evaluate MNT cross sections for each neutron and proton number channel. Calculations were carried out for three initial orientations of the prolate $^{238}$U target (symmetry axis parallel to the collision axis, parallel to the impact parameter vector, and perpendicular to the reaction plane) over an impact parameter range of 0 to 12 fm at center-of-mass energies near 307.35 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Barrier height for $x$-direction (tip) collisions: 242.93 MeV; for $y$-direction (side) collisions: 263.97 MeV
- Contact time in head-on collisions: 10-11 zs for $y$- and $z$-direction cases; approximately 4-5 zs for $x$-direction case
- TKEL at small impact parameters ($b \lesssim 4$ fm): approximately 50-60 MeV for $x$-direction case; approximately 70-80 MeV for $y$- and $z$-direction cases
- In $x$-direction tip collisions at $b=2$ fm, produced fragments roughly correspond to $^{100}_{40}$Zr$_{60}$ and $^{202}_{80}$Hg$_{122}$
- In $y$-direction side collisions at $b=2$ fm, produced fragments roughly correspond to $^{116}_{47}$Ag$_{69}$ and $^{185}_{73}$Ta$_{112}$
- Fully equilibrated $N/Z$ ratio of the system: 1.52
- Deformation parameters: $^{64}$Ni oblate with $\beta \approx 0.12$; $^{238}$U prolate with $\beta \approx 0.27$

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of MNT processes in lighter systems such as $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb by Sekizawa and Yabana (2013), and QF studies in $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{232}$Th+$^{250}$Cf by other groups. The results confirm and generalize the orientation-dependent shell effects previously reported for $^{40}$Ca+$^{238}$U by Wakhle et al. (2014), showing that quantum shells around $^{208}$Pb strongly influence tip collisions while side collisions produce more mass-symmetric fragments. The findings enable future microscopic predictions for MNT and QF cross sections in heavy deformed systems, though the authors note that inclusion of pairing correlations would be needed for a complete description.
