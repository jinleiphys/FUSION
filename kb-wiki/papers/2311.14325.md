# Universal characterization of Efimovian $D^0 nn$ System via Faddeev Techniques

> We demonstrate remnant structural universality in a putative S-wave $2n$-halo-bound $D^0nn$ system in the $J=0, T=3/2$ channel by invoking the zero-coupling limit (ZCL), which eliminates sub-threshold decay channels. Within this framework, we evaluate the one- and two-body matter density form factors, their associated root mean-square radii, and the $n$-$D^0$-$n$ opening angle. Our analysis is carried out at leading order using a quantum mechanical Faddeev technique in the momentum representation. Employing Jacobi momenta, we construct a complete partial-wave basis to expand the full three-body $D^0nn$ wave function across distinct rearrangement channels. Projection onto this basis yields a coupled set of Faddeev integral equations that govern the multiple-scattering dynamics of the constituent coupled spin-isospin subsystems. By introducing short-range separable interactions and expressing the two-body scattering amplitudes via spectator functions, we establish a direct correspondence with the familiar Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosyan equations from halo-EFT approach at leading order. A regulator-dependent analysis highlights the Efimov-like character of the three-body observables, with ground state properties exhibiting marked sensitivity to cutoff variations. However, the inclusion of a three-body force suppresses this dependence, as expected from renormalization-group invariance. We thereby conclude that, for sufficiently shallow three-body binding, the $D^0nn$ system in the ZCL exhibits a universal halo-bound structure. The subtle implications of range-like corrections at LO are addressed at a qualitative level in this analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $D^0nn$ system in the $J=0, T=3/2$ channel exhibits remnant structural universality characteristic of an Efimov-like halo-bound state, but only under the zero-coupling limit (ZCL) that eliminates sub-threshold decay channels and for sufficiently shallow three-body binding.

## Method

The authors employed a leading-order quantum mechanical Faddeev technique in momentum representation, using Jacobi momenta and partial-wave decomposition to construct the full three-body $D^0nn$ wave function across distinct rearrangement channels. Short-range separable interactions were introduced, and the two-body scattering amplitudes were expressed via spectator functions, establishing a direct correspondence with the Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosyan equations from halo-EFT at leading order. A regulator-dependent analysis was performed to assess the Efimov-like character of the three-body observables, with a three-body force included to suppress cutoff sensitivity as expected from renormalization-group invariance.

## Key numbers

* The critical cutoff for the first Efimov-bound state appearing above the $n+(D^0n)$ particle-dimer break-up threshold is $\Lambda^{(0)}_{\rm crit}\approx 48$ MeV, compared to the pionless EFT hard/breakdown scale $\lambda_H\sim m_\pi$.
* The S-wave $n$-$D^0$ scattering length in the ZCL is $a_{nD}=4.141$ fm, extracted from a dynamical coupled-channel model analysis.
* The S-wave $n$-$n$ scattering length is $a_{nn}=-18.63$ fm, corresponding to a virtual (anti-bound) di-neutron sub-system.
* The $D^0$-meson mass is expressed as $M_D = y M_n$, where $M_n$ is the neutron mass, with the mass ratio $y$ used throughout the formalism.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Raha (2017) and Raha (2020) that demonstrated the feasibility of Efimov-like states in the $D^0nn$ system using pionless EFT and the ZCL idealization. The paper applies a halo-EFT-inspired Faddeev framework, previously developed for $2n$-halo nuclei such as ${}^{6}$He and ${}^{20}$C by Canham (2008) and DL-Canham (2009), to investigate the geometrical features of a plausible S-wave $D^0nn$ Efimov-bound system. The structural predictions provide input for other approaches including lattice QCD simulations and help elucidate hadronic clustering mechanisms in the charm sector.
