# Neutron-proton scattering and singular potentials

> We consider a Bargmann-type rational parametrization of the nucleon scattering phase shifts. Applying Marchenko's method of quantum inverse scattering we show that the scattering data suggest a singular repulsive core of the potential of the form $2/r^2$ and $6/r^2$ in natural units, for the ${}^3S_1$ and ${}^1S_0$ channels respectively. The simplest solution in the ${}^3S_1$ channel contains three parameters only but reproduces all features of the potential and bound state wave function within one percent error. We also consider the ${}^3S_1$-${}^3D_1$ coupled channel problem with the coupled channel Marchenko inversion method.

## Key claim

Using Marchenko's inverse scattering method with a Bargmann-type rational parametrization of nucleon scattering phase shifts, the scattering data imply a singular repulsive core of the form \(2/r^2\) for the \({}^{3}S_1\) channel and \(6/r^2\) for the \({}^{1}S_0\) channel, but only when the correct singularity degree (determined by Levinson's theorem) is enforced; simpler fits with fewer parameters can reproduce the long-range behavior but fail to match the short-range repulsive core.

## Method

The authors applied the Marchenko quantum inverse scattering method to rational (Bargmann-type) parametrizations of the neutron-proton S-matrix for the \({}^{1}S_0\) single channel and the \({}^{3}S_1\)-\({}^{3}D_1\) coupled channel. They constructed S-matrices with varying numbers of poles (3, 4, 5, and 6 parameters) and solved the Marchenko integral equation algebraically to derive the corresponding potentials, comparing them to the phenomenological Reid93 and AV18 potentials.

## Key numbers

* For the \({}^{1}S_0\) channel, a 3-parameter S-matrix with poles at \(a=-0.04007\), \(b=1.1061\), \(c=2.9254\) yields a singularity degree \(\nu=1\) and a potential with incorrect short-range behavior.
* For the \({}^{1}S_0\) channel, a 4-parameter S-matrix with poles at \(a=-0.04034\), \(b=1.4561\), \(c=5.2856\), \(d=4.9680\) yields the correct singularity degree \(\nu=2\) and a potential matching the repulsive core.
* For the \({}^{1}S_0\) channel, a 6-parameter S-matrix from prior work (Stancu) with poles at \(a=-0.0401\), \(b=-0.7540\), \(c=0.6152\), \(d=2.0424\), \(e=4.1650\), \(f=4.6000\) also gives \(\nu=2\) and a good match to phenomenological potentials.
* For the \({}^{3}S_1\) channel, the simplest solution contains three parameters and reproduces all features of the potential and bound state wave function within one percent error.

## Context

This work extends prior Bargmann-type parametrizations of the nucleon potential (Bargmann) and earlier studies using supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SparBay, Stancu) to analyze the short-range singularity of the nuclear force. The authors show that the Marchenko method provides compact analytic expressions for the potential and wave function, and that the correct singularity strength is essential for reproducing the repulsive core, enabling systematic construction of nucleon-nucleon potentials from scattering data.
