# Mach cones in Heavy Ion Collisions

> MACE, a model for the propagation of mach cones in the velocity field of heavy ion collisions (as created by hydrodynamical models) is explained. It is tested on an analytical test case (static medium) and applied to velocity fields created by the Particle-in-Cell model from LHC and RHIC initial conditions.

# Mach cones in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The MACE model can propagate mach cones through velocity fields from hydrodynamical simulations of heavy ion collisions, but its validation is limited to an analytical test case in a static medium before being applied to velocity fields from the Particle-in-Cell model.

## Method
The author developed MACE, a model that propagates mach cone perturbations through pre-computed velocity fields from hydrodynamical simulations. The model was first tested against an analytical solution in a static medium, then applied to velocity fields generated by the Particle-in-Cell (PIC) model using initial conditions for both LHC and RHIC energies.

## Key numbers
- The PIC model assumes a constant speed of sound of c_S = 1/sqrt(3) for the equation of state
- The PIC model uses a freeze-out condition where massless QGP transitions to massive quarks with a rest mass of m_q approximately 300 MeV
- The hydrodynamical initial state has a formation time of tau_0 approximately 1 fm

## Context
This work extends the study of mach cones in heavy ion collisions, building on prior work by Casalderrey-Solana, Shuryak and Teaney (2005) on conical flow induced by quenched QCD jets, and by Satarov, Stoecker and Mishustin (2005) on mach shocks induced by partonic jets in expanding quark-gluon plasma. The MACE model enables future comparisons between mach cone predictions and experimental three-particle correlation measurements from STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC, as well as predictions for the ALICE experiment at LHC.
