# Sensitivity of HBT interferometry to the microscopic dynamics of freeze-out

> We study the HBT interferometry of ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions using a freezeout model in which free pions emerge in the course of the last binary collisions in the hadron gas. We show that the HBT correlators of both identical and non-identical pions change with respect to the case of independent pion production. Practical consequences for the design of the event generator with the built in Bose-Einstein correlations are discussed. We argue that the scheme of inclusive measurement of the HBT correlation function does not require the symmetrization of the multi-pion transition amplitudes (wave-functions).

## Key claim

The paper argues that HBT interferometry can detect differences between freezeout mechanisms in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, specifically that the normalized two-particle correlator of non-identical pions must differ from unity at any momentum difference, and the correlator of identical pions does not approach unity at large momentum differences and slightly exceeds 2 at zero momentum difference, though in the idealized model used to demonstrate these effects, they are small.

## Method

The authors formulate HBT interferometry as a quantum transition problem using a freezeout model where free pions emerge from the last binary collisions in a hadron gas, rather than from independent production. They derive the two-pion inclusive spectrum from field-theoretic operators, incorporating the dynamics of binary collisions that involve four interfering amplitudes (two detected and two undetected pions), and compute the correlator for a boost-invariant Bjorken geometry using saddle-point approximations for pions with large transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- The critical freezeout temperature is taken as \(T_c \sim m_\pi\) (approximately 140 MeV).
- Pions with large transverse momenta satisfying \(p_t \agt 3T \sim 3m_\pi\) are used to achieve localization in rapidity.
- The one-particle distribution for boost-invariant geometry yields approximately \(\tau S_\bot m_1 \sqrt{2\pi T_c/m_1} e^{-m_1/T_c}\).
- The correlator function \(f(|\vec{q}_\bot|R_\bot)\) for a uniform transverse distribution gives \([2 J_1(|\Delta \vec{k}|R_\bot)/|\Delta \vec{k}|R_\bot]^2\), where \(J_1\) is a Bessel function.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of HBT interferometry from Makhlin and Surdutovich (1998) and Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988) by incorporating a dynamical freezeout model with binary collisions, contrasting with the naive independent-source picture used at Bevalac energies by Gyulassy, Kauffmann, and Wilson (1979). The results enable the design of event generators with built-in Bose-Einstein correlations and provide a tool to distinguish between scenarios where pions emerge from a hadron gas versus direct hadronization, with implications for constraining the full self-consistent scenario of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies.
