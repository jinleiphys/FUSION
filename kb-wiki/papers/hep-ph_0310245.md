# On Unique Predictions for Single Spin Azimuthal Asymmetry

> Theoretically there are two approaches to predict single spin azimuthal asymmetries. One is to take transverse momenta of partons into account by using transverse momentum dependent parton distributions, while another is to take asymmetries as a twist-3 effect. The nonperturbative effects in these approaches are parameterized with different matrix elements and predictions can be different. Recently, gauge invariant definitions of transverse momentum dependent parton distributions were derived. With these definitions it can be shown that there are relations between nonperturbative matrix elements in two approaches. These relations may enable us to unify two approaches and to have unique predictions for single spin azimuthal asymmetries.In this letter we derive these relations by using time-reversal symmetry and show that even with these relations the single spin azimuthal asymmetry in Drell-Yan process is predicted differently in different approaches.

# On Unique Predictions for Single Spin Azimuthal Asymmetry

## Key claim

Even after deriving relations between the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) Sivers function and the twist-3 quark-gluon correlator using time-reversal symmetry, the predicted single spin azimuthal asymmetry in the Drell-Yan process remains different between the TMD approach and the twist-3 (Qiu-Sterman) approach, because the perturbative coefficients are calculated differently in each framework.

## Method

The authors derive relations between the second transverse-momentum moment of the Sivers function \(f_{1T,DY}^\perp\) and the twist-3 quark-gluon correlation function \(T_F(x,x)\) using time-reversal symmetry and gauge-invariant definitions of TMD parton distributions. They then compute the single spin azimuthal asymmetry \(A_N\) for the Drell-Yan process \(p^\uparrow + p \to l^+ l^- + X\) at leading order in two ways: first using the twist-3 (Qiu-Sterman) mechanism, and second using \(k_T\)-factorization with the Sivers function. The two results are compared analytically.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry from the twist-3 approach (Qiu-Sterman mechanism) is given by \(A_N = -\frac{1}{Q} \cdot \frac{\sin 2\theta \sin\phi}{1+\cos^2\theta} \cdot \left( \frac{\sum_q e_q^2 \int dx_A dx_B \delta(Q^2 - x_A x_B S) T_{F,q/A}(x_A,x_A) f_{\bar q/B}(x_B)}{\sum_q e_q^2 \int dx_A dx_B f_{q/A}(x_A) f_{\bar q/B}(x_B)} + \cdots \right)\).
- The asymmetry from the TMD approach with Sivers function, after using the relation \(T_F(x,x) = \int d^2 k_T |\mathbf{k}_T|^2 f_{1T,DY}^\perp(x,k_T^2)\), yields an extra factor \(\frac{x_A - x_B}{2(\sqrt{x_A} + \sqrt{x_B})^2}\) compared to the twist-3 result.
- After restoring electromagnetic gauge invariance by replacing \(\gamma^-\) with \(\gamma \cdot k / (k \cdot n)\) in the quark density matrix, the factor changes to \(\frac{\sqrt{x_B}}{\sqrt{x_A} + \sqrt{x_B}}\), still different from unity.

## Context

This work extends the study of relations between TMD and twist-3 approaches for single spin asymmetries, following the gauge-invariant definitions of TMD distributions by Collins (2002) and Ji (2003), and the relation between the second moment of the Sivers function and the twist-3 matrix element found by Boer (2003). The paper contradicts the expectation that these relations would unify the two approaches and produce unique predictions. It shows that the Drell-Yan process yields different asymmetries depending on the approach used, and suggests that further study is needed to resolve the discrepancy, which may also appear in other processes.
