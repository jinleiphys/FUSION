# $^4{\rm He}$+$n$+$n$ continuum within an ab initio framework

> The low-lying continuum spectrum of the $^6{\rm He}$ nucleus is investigated for the first time within an ab initio framework that encompasses the $^4{\rm He}$+$n$+$n$ three-cluster dynamics characterizing its lowest decay channel. This is achieved through an extension of the no-core-shell model combined with the resonating-group method, in which energy-independent non-local interactions among three nuclear fragments can be calculated microscopically starting from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and consistent ab initio many-body wave functions of the clusters. The three-cluster Schrödinger equation is solved with three-body scattering boundary conditions by means of the hyperspherical-harmonic method on a Lagrange mesh. Using a soft similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, we find the known $J^π= 2^+$ resonance as well as a result consistent with a new low-lying second $2^+$ resonance recently observed at GANIL at $2.6$ MeV above the $^6$He ground state. We also find resonances in the $2^-$, $1^+$ and $0^-$ channels, while no low-lying resonances are present in the $0^+$ and $1^-$ channels.

## Key claim

Using a soft similarity-renormalization-group evolved chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, the authors find the known $J^\pi = 2^+$ resonance of $^6$He as well as a result consistent with a new low-lying second $2^+$ resonance recently observed at GANIL at 2.6 MeV above the $^6$He ground state, but note that the omission of three-nucleon forces and core polarization effects limits predictive capability.

## Method

The authors extend the no-core shell model combined with the resonating-group method (NCSM/RGM) to treat three-cluster ($^4$He+$n$+$n$) configurations, computing energy-independent non-local interactions among the three nuclear fragments from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions and ab initio many-body wave functions of the clusters. They solve the three-cluster Schrödinger equation with three-body scattering boundary conditions using the hyperspherical-harmonic method on a Lagrange mesh, expanding the relative motion wave functions in hyperspherical coordinates and applying the microscopic $R$-matrix method.

## Key numbers

- The $^6$He ground state lies 0.975 MeV below the $^4$He+$n$+$n$ threshold (the lowest decay channel).
- The first $2^+$ resonance appears around 1.25 MeV above the $^4$He+$n$+$n$ threshold.
- A second $2^+$ resonance emerges at approximately 2.6 MeV above the $^4$He+$n$+$n$ threshold.
- A $1^+$ resonance is found at 2.77 MeV in the present calculations.
- The $2^+$ resonance observed at GANIL has a width $\Gamma = 1.6$ MeV; a $J=1$ resonance of unassigned parity was measured at 5.3 MeV with $\Gamma \sim 2$ MeV.
- Calculations use a chiral N$^3$LO $NN$ interaction softened via SRG to $\Lambda = 1.5$ fm$^{-1}$, with convergence in HO expansions within $N_{\rm max} \sim 13$ quanta.
- The $0^+$ channel requires $K_{\rm max} = 28$ for convergence; other channels converge at $K_{\rm max} = 19$ (negative parity) or $K_{\rm max} = 20$ (positive parity).
- Changing the SRG parameter to $\Lambda = 1.8$ fm$^{-1}$ shifts resonance energies by less than 1 MeV.

## Context

This work presents the first ab initio calculation of the $^4$He+$n$+$n$ continuum, extending prior three-body models (e.g., Danilin (1998), Descouvemont (2005)) and microscopic three-cluster models (e.g., Korennov (2004)) that used schematic interactions and simplified cluster descriptions. It also goes beyond six-body bound-state calculations (e.g., PRL 87.172502, PhysRevC 68.034305) that could not provide resonance widths. The results enable future studies incorporating three-nucleon forces and core polarization effects through the no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) coupling, and the method is expected to be applicable to systems such as $^{11}$Li within a $^9$Li+$n$+$n$ basis.
