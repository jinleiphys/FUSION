# Elastic scattering of 17F, 17O and 19F on a heavy target in microscopic continuum discretized coupled-channels method

> Background: Microscopic description of the projectile, based on an effective NN interaction, in a microscopic CDCC (MCDCC) model [PRL 111, 082701 (2013)] has been successfully applied to the 7Li+208Pb scattering. Purpose: The MCDCC method is applied to the low energy elastic scattering of 17F, 17O and 19F on 58Ni and 208Pb targets. The goal of the calculations is twofold - to test the adequacy and the accuracy of the MCDCC model for the heavier projectiles, and to study the contribution of various channels to the elastic scattering cross sections. Methods: The elastic scattering cross sections are calculated using the MCDCC method. The nucleon-target optical potential is folded with the projectile densities resulting from an effective NN interaction, which includes central nuclear, spin-orbit and Coulomb terms. Discretization of the continuum is achieved via the pseudo state method. Coupled equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. Results: For the test case of 17F at 10 MeV/nucleon, the cross sections are weakly sensitive to the choice of the effective NN interaction, three different energy dependent optical nucleon-target potentials provide a similar reasonable agreement with data. Just below the Coulomb barrier, the MCDCC significantly underestimates the cross sections at larger angles. The coupling to continuum is not significant in most of the assessed cases. Conclusions: The MCDCC is very satisfactory in the sense, that it includes the microscopic properties of the projectile in a reaction model. Well above the Coulomb barrier, the cross sections are in a good agreement with data. The reasons for the discrepancy between the data and the calculated cross sections at the lower energies, which is also observed in a traditional CDCC, are unclear.

## Key claim

The microscopic continuum discretized coupled-channels (MCDCC) method, which includes a fully antisymmetric microscopic description of the projectile, provides satisfactory elastic scattering cross sections for 17F, 17O, and 19F on heavy targets well above the Coulomb barrier, but systematically underestimates cross sections at larger angles just below the Coulomb barrier for reasons that remain unclear and are also observed in traditional CDCC.

## Method

The MCDCC method describes the projectile using an effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction (Minnesota or Volkov force) within the resonating group method, with the continuum discretized via the pseudostate method. The nucleon-target optical potential (Koning-Delaroche, CH89, or Bechetti-Greenlees) is folded with the projectile's microscopic transition densities, and the coupled-channel equations are solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. The 17F and 17O projectiles are treated as 16O + nucleon subsystems, while 19F is treated as 15N + alpha.

## Key numbers

- For 17F + 208Pb at 10 MeV/nucleon (Elab = 170 MeV), the single-channel and multichannel calculations are indistinguishable at the scale of the figure, and the real part of the Koning-Delaroche potential must be renormalized by a factor of 0.65 to better reproduce the quasielastic data from Li (2009).
- For 17F + 58Ni at 10 MeV/nucleon (Elab = 170 MeV), the real part of the Koning-Delaroche potential is renormalized by a factor of 0.8 to better match the quasielastic data from Li (2009).
- The experimental transition probability B(E2, 5/2+ to 1/2+) from the first excited state to the ground state of 17F is 64.9 e2fm4, while the generator coordinate method calculation gives 50.1 e2fm4.
- For 17F + 208Pb, the Coulomb barrier is VBlab = 91.7 MeV; for 17F + 58Ni, VBlab is approximately 46 MeV; for 17O + 208Pb, VBlab is approximately 83 MeV; for 19F + 208Pb, VBlab is approximately 94 MeV.
- For 17O + 58Ni at Elab = 55 MeV (just above the Coulomb barrier), the MCDCC calculation underestimates the cross sections at larger angles compared to quasielastic data from Singh (2013).
- For 19F + 208Pb at Elab = 102 MeV (just above the Coulomb barrier), the cross sections are in reasonable agreement with data from Liang (2001) without any renormalization factor; at Elab = 91 MeV (just below the Coulomb barrier), the calculations underestimate the cross sections at large angles.

## Context

This work extends the MCDCC method, previously applied successfully to 7Li + 208Pb scattering by Descouvemont and Hussein (2013), to heavier projectiles (A=17 mirror nuclei and 19F) on 58Ni and 208Pb targets. The results confirm the findings of traditional CDCC calculations by Kubota and Moro (2012) and Moro (unpublished) that coupling to continuum is not significant in most cases, and that discrepancies between theory and experiment near the Coulomb barrier remain unresolved. The method enables future studies of exotic nuclei where experimental data are scarce, and could be improved by including target excitations or additional core configurations.
