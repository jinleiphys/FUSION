# Gauge and Lorentz invariant one-pion exchange currents in electron scattering from a relativistic Fermi gas

> A consistent analysis of relativistic pionic correlations and meson-exchange currents for electroweak quasielastic electron scattering from nuclei is carried out. Fully-relativistic one-pion-exchange electromagnetic operators are developed for use in one-particle emission electronuclear reactions within the context of the relativistic Fermi gas model. Then the exchange and pionic correlation currents are set up fully respecting the gauge invariance of the theory. Emphasis is placed on the self-energy current which, being infinite, needs to be renormalized. This is achieved starting in the Hartree-Fock framework and then expanding the Hartree-Fock current to first order in the square of the pion coupling constant to obtain a truly, gauge invariant, one-pion-exchange current. The model is applied to the calculation of the parity-conserving (PC) and parity-violating (PV) inclusive responses of nuclei. Interestingly, in the pionic correlations terms exist which arise uniquely from relativity, although their impact on the responses is found to be modest.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a fully relativistic, gauge-invariant one-pion-exchange current operator for electron scattering from a relativistic Fermi gas, including both meson-exchange currents and pionic correlation currents, with the self-energy current renormalized via a Hartree-Fock expansion to first order in the square of the pion-nucleon coupling constant; the resulting current yields response functions that agree with those obtained from the polarization propagator method, though the impact of purely relativistic pionic correlation terms on the responses is found to be modest.

## Method

The authors developed fully relativistic one-pion-exchange electromagnetic operators for one-particle emission within the relativistic Fermi gas model, including seagull, pion-in-flight, vertex correlation, and self-energy currents. The self-energy current, which diverges, was renormalized by starting from the Hartree-Fock framework and expanding the Hartree-Fock current to first order in the square of the pion coupling constant, yielding a finite, gauge-invariant one-pion-exchange current. The model was then applied to calculate the parity-conserving and parity-violating inclusive response functions of nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi momentum \(k_F\) is used as the cutoff for the momentum integration over occupied states, with the sum \(\frac{1}{V}\sum_{\mathbf{k} \leq k_F}\) becoming an integral \(\int \frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\) over \(0 \leq k \leq k_F\).
- The pion mass \(m_\pi\) appears in the pseudovector \(\pi NN\) coupling Hamiltonian: \(\mathcal{H}_{\pi NN} = \frac{f}{m_\pi}\overline{\psi}\gamma_5\gamma^{\mu}(\partial_{\mu}\phi_a)\tau_a \psi\).
- The nucleon mass \(m\) is used in the on-shell energy \(E_{\mathbf{p}} = \sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m^2}\) and in the normalization of spinors and the nucleon propagator \(S_F(P) = \frac{\not{P} + m}{P^2 - m^2}\).
- The electromagnetic form factors \(F_1^V\) (isovector nucleon) and \(F_\pi\) (pion) are set equal (\(F_\pi = F_1^V\)) to fulfill gauge invariance, with the Galster parameterization used for \(F_1\) and \(F_2\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Amaro et al. (2002) and Amaro et al. (1998, 1999) that systematically investigated relativistic effects in nuclear electromagnetic responses, including meson-exchange and isobar currents, within the relativistic Fermi gas model. It provides a consistent, gauge-invariant treatment of one-pion-exchange currents that includes both correlation and self-energy contributions, enabling future studies of quasielastic electron scattering at high momentum transfers where relativistic effects are important, and can be compared with non-relativistic approaches such as those by Gil et al. (1997) and Carlson et al. (2002).
