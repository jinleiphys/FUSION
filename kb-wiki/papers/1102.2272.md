# Prerainbow Oscillations in $^3$He Scattering from the Hoyle State of $^{12}$c and Alpha Particle Condensation

> $^3$He+$^{12}$C scattering is studied in a coupled channel method by using a double folding model with microscopic wave functions of $^{12}$C. Experimental angular distributions in elastic and inelastic scattering to the $2^+$ (4.44 MeV), 0$^+_2$ (7.65 MeV) and 3$^-$ (9.63 MeV) states of $^{12}$C are well reproduced. It is found that the Airy minimum of the prerainbow oscillations for the Hoyle state is considerably shifted to a larger angle due to its dilute density distribution compared with that of the normal ground state in agreement with the idea of $α$ particle condensation.

# Prerainbow Oscillations in $^3$He Scattering from the Hoyle State of $^{12}$C and Alpha Particle Condensation

## Key claim

The Airy minimum of the prerainbow oscillations for the Hoyle state (0$^+_2$, 7.65 MeV) in $^3$He+$^{12}$C scattering is shifted to a larger angle compared with the ground state, consistent with the dilute density distribution expected from $\alpha$ particle condensation; this shift is more clearly visible in the prerainbow regime at 34.7 MeV than in the rainbow regime at 72 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel calculations using a double folding model with the DDM3Y effective interaction and microscopic wave functions of $^{12}$C from the resonating group method by Kamimura et al. (1981). They analyzed elastic and inelastic $^3$He scattering to the 0$^+_1$ (ground state), 2$^+$ (4.44 MeV), 0$^+_2$ (7.65 MeV, Hoyle state), and 3$^-$ (9.63 MeV) states of $^{12}$C at laboratory energies of 34.7 and 72 MeV, decomposing the scattering amplitudes into farside and nearside components to identify Airy minima.

## Key numbers

- At $E_L$ = 72 MeV, the first Airy minimum (A1) for the 0$^+_2$ (Hoyle) state appears at 35$^\circ$.
- At $E_L$ = 34.7 MeV, the Airy minimum A1 appears at 60$^\circ$ for elastic scattering and at 75$^\circ$ for the 0$^+_2$ state.
- The experimental angular distributions were measured at $E_L$ = 34.7 MeV by Fujisawa et al. (1973) and at 72 MeV by Demyanova et al. (1992).

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear rainbow scattering to inelastic scattering from the Hoyle state, building on prior studies of $\alpha$+$^{12}$C scattering by Ohkubo (2004) that suggested the Hoyle state has a large lens composed of three $\alpha$ particles in a dilute density distribution. The study uses wave functions from Uegaki et al. (1979) and Kamimura and Fukushima (1981), which have been shown to be equivalent to an $\alpha$ particle condensate wave function where three $\alpha$ particles occupy the lowest 0s state in a dilute gas. The approach enables future experimental tests using heavy ion rainbow scattering, such as $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C, and suggests that states in $^{13}$C, $^{14}$C, and $^{16}$C with additional neutrons may also exhibit shifted prerainbow oscillations if they possess dilute density distributions.
