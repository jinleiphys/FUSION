# Bound States in Galilean-Invariant Quantum Field Theory

> We consider the nonrelativistic quantum mechanics of a model of two spinless fermions interacting via a two-body potential. We introduce quantum fields associated with the two particles as well as the expansion of these fields in asymptotic ``in'' and ``out'' fields, including such fields for bound states, in principle. We limit our explicit discussion to a two-body bound state. In this context we discuss the implications of the Galilean invariance of the model and, in particular, show how to include bound states in a strictly Galilean-invariant quantum field theory.

# Bound States in Galilean-Invariant Quantum Field Theory

## Key claim

The authors show that bound states can be consistently included in a strictly Galilean-invariant quantum field theory, provided the Bargmann mass superselection rule is obeyed, the bound state mass must be exactly the sum of its constituent masses, not the relativistic expectation of mass minus binding energy. The Haag amplitude for a two-body bound state is shown to be precisely the Schrödinger wavefunction, but only when the bound-state asymptotic field is constructed from constituent fields at *separated* points weighted by the bound-state amplitude, rather than at the same point as proposed by Nishijima and Zimmermann.

## Method

The authors consider a nonrelativistic model of two spinless fermions (A and B) interacting via a two-body potential V_AB, with no AA or BB interactions. They expand the interacting Heisenberg-picture fields in terms of asymptotic "in" and "out" fields using the Haag expansion, then impose Galilean transformation properties including the Bargmann phase factors. By inserting the Haag expansion into the equation of motion and equating coefficients of the bound-state operator terms, they derive the Schrödinger equation for the bound-state amplitude. The asymptotic field for the composite bound state is constructed as the weak limit of a product of constituent fields at separated points, integrated with the bound-state wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- The bound state energy is given by E = m_AB - ε_i + k²/(2 m_AB), where m_AB = m_A + m_B and ε_i is the binding energy; the kinetic term uses the sum mass m_AB, not the reduced mass or (m_AB - ε)
- Under Galilean boosts, the bound state momentum transforms as k → k + m_AB v, not k + (m_AB - ε) v
- The Schrödinger equation for the bound state wavefunction F_B;i contains the reduced mass μ defined by 1/μ = 1/m_A + 1/m_B
- The elastic unitarity relation (Eq. 4.18) involves a factor (2π)^(5/2) in the phase space integral

## Context

This work extends the Haag expansion method (Haag, 1958) to nonrelativistic Galilean-invariant theories, taking into account the Bargmann phase (Bargmann, 1954) that forces the mass superselection rule. It differs from the approaches of Nishijima (1958) and Zimmermann (1958), who constructed bound states as products of constituent fields at the same point; the authors instead use separated points weighted by the bound-state amplitude. The formalism enables future treatment of three-body scattering, rearrangement collisions, and relativistic bound-state problems as an alternative to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with applications already explored in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model (Corley, Greenberg, and collaborators).
