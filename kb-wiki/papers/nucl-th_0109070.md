# Few-Nucleon Calculations and Correlations

> Some present day results for few-nucleon bound state, two-nucleon correlation functions and scattering observables are briefly reviewed. The old idea of the Coulomb sum rule as a way to extract the pp correlation function is reconsidered. It appears to be an ideal case to probe properties of the density operator and the ground state wave functions without disturbances of final state interactions (FSI) in exclusive electron scattering on 3He. Also exclusive photodisintegration of 3He is regarded which appears to be promising to identify 3N force effects.

# Few-Nucleon Calculations and Correlations

## Key claim

Modern high-precision NN forces alone underbind three- and four-nucleon systems, and while three-nucleon (3N) forces are mandatory for binding energies and several scattering observables, their effect on two-nucleon correlation functions in the ground state is very small for 3He and 4He. The Coulomb sum rule is reconsidered as a way to extract the pp correlation function, but relativistic corrections in the density operator and two-body pieces play an important role and cannot be treated as a small perturbation.

## Method

The authors solve the nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation rigorously using the Faddeev scheme for three-nucleon bound and scattering states and the Yakubovsky equations for the four-nucleon bound state. They employ five high-precision NN potentials (CD-Bonn, AV18, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, Nijmegen 93) and two 3N force models (Tucson-Melbourne and Urbana IX), with the 3N force strength parameters adjusted to reproduce the 3H binding energy. For electromagnetic processes, they compute response functions for exclusive electron scattering and photodisintegration on 3He, including final state interactions (FSI) via a Faddeev-like integral equation.

## Key numbers

* 3H binding energy with NN forces only: ranges from -7.628 MeV (AV18) to -8.013 MeV (CD-Bonn); experimental value is -8.482 MeV.
* 4He binding energy with NN forces only: ranges from -24.28 MeV (AV18) to -26.26 MeV (CD-Bonn); experimental value is -28.30 MeV.
* With NN+3N forces, 4He binding energy ranges from -28.36 MeV (AV18+TM') to -29.15 MeV (CD-Bonn+TM); experimental value is -28.30 MeV.
* Average attraction due to NN forces in 4He is 24.92 MeV (88% of 28.30 MeV); average additional attraction from 3N forces is 3.9 MeV (14%); average overbinding is 0.5 MeV (2%).
* For exclusive electron scattering on 3He at Q = 400, 500, and 600 MeV/c, FSI reduces the longitudinal response RL by a factor of 10-100 compared to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA).
* In photodisintegration of 3He, regions of phase space are identified where 3N force effects exceed 40% for relative azimuthal angle phi_12 <= 50 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous rigorous Faddeev and Yakubovsky calculations (Glockle et al., 1996; Nogga et al., 2000) by examining two-nucleon correlation functions and their connection to observables via the Coulomb sum rule, originally proposed by Czyz and Gottfried (1963) and later studied by Schiavilla et al. (1993). The paper demonstrates that the Tjon line correlation between 3H and 4He binding energies holds across multiple NN and 3N force combinations, consistent with expectations from chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg, 1991; van Kolck, 1994). The results enable future experimental tests of correlated wave functions and electromagnetic current operators in 3He, particularly through exclusive (e,e'NN) and photodisintegration measurements, where the 3N system provides the only case where FSI is fully under control below the pion threshold.
