# Matching pre-equilibrium dynamics and viscous hydrodynamics

> We demonstrate how to match pre-equilibrium dynamics of a 0+1 dimensional quark gluon plasma to 2nd-order viscous hydrodynamical evolution. The matching allows us to specify the initial values of the energy density and shear tensor at the initial time of hydrodynamical evolution as a function of the lifetime of the pre-equilibrium period. We compare two models for the pre-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma, longitudinal free streaming and collisionally-broadened longitudinal expansion, and present analytic formulas which can be used to fix the necessary components of the energy-momentum tensor. The resulting dynamical models can be used to assess the effect of pre-equilibrium dynamics on quark-gluon plasma observables. Additionally, we investigate the dependence of entropy production on pre-equilibrium dynamics and discuss the limitations of the standard definitions of the non-equilibrium entropy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates how to match pre-equilibrium dynamics of a 0+1 dimensional quark-gluon plasma to second-order viscous hydrodynamical evolution, but the matching is restricted to a 0+1-dimensional boost-invariant system with uniform energy density in the transverse plane and assumes an ideal equation of state, limiting direct applicability to realistic heavy-ion collision simulations.

## Method

The authors introduce a pre-equilibrium period from formation time τ₀ to hydrodynamical starting time τ_hydro, during which the plasma develops momentum-space anisotropy due to longitudinal expansion. They parametrize the microscopic anisotropy using ξ = (1+ξ₀)(τ/τ₀)^δ - 1, where δ = 2 for longitudinal free streaming and δ = 2/3 for collisionally-broadened longitudinal expansion. At τ_hydro, they compute the pressure anisotropy Δ and energy density from ξ, then use the relation Π_hydro = (2Δ_hydro)/(3+2Δ_hydro) × ε_hydro to set the initial shear tensor for solving the second-order viscous hydrodynamical equations (∂_τ ε = -(ε+P)/τ + Π/τ and ∂_τ Π = -Π/τ_π + 4η/(3τ_π τ) - (4/3τ)Π - λ₁/(2τ_π η²) Π²).

## Key numbers

- For collisionally-broadened expansion: δ = 2/3 in the relation ξ = (1+ξ₀)(τ/τ₀)^δ - 1
- For free streaming expansion: δ = 2 in the same relation
- Weakly coupled QGP transport coefficients: η̄ = 10/(4π), c_π = 6, c_λ₁ = 9/2
- Strongly coupled N=4 SYM transport coefficients: η̄ = 1/(4π), c_π = 2(2-log 2), c_λ₁ = 2
- Number of degrees of freedom for QCD with N_c=3 and N_f=2: N_dof = 37
- For small ξ: Δ ≈ (4/5)ξ + O(ξ²)
- For large ξ: Δ ≈ (1/2)ξ + O(√ξ)
- The Navier-Stokes limit gives ξ_NS = (10/(Tτ))(η/S) + O(Π²_NS)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Martinez and Strickland (2007-2008) that matched pre-equilibrium dynamics to ideal hydrodynamics for calculating dilepton and photon production, and by Baier et al. (2000) who established the bottom-up thermalization scenario. The matching procedure enables assessment of how pre-equilibrium dynamics affects QGP observables such as dilepton and photon production and heavy quark screening, and allows investigation of entropy production dependence on the pre-equilibrium period. The authors note that the transport coefficients in both weak and strong coupling limits do not satisfy universal relations, so their values can only be taken as estimates.
