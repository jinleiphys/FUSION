# Hypothesis about semi-weak interaction and experiments with solar neutrinos. II. Deuteron disintegration by neutral currents

> The present work provides one more evidence of that the solar neutrino problem has an elegant solution based on the hypothesis about the existence of a new, semi-weak, interaction. The analysis of the deuteron disintegration by neutral currents of solar neutrinos, generated by both the electroweak and semi-weak interactions, is fulfilled. A good agreement between the theoretical and experimental results for this process is obtained, which is in harmony with the conclusions of the first part of the work on the other four observed processes with solar neutrinos.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the solar neutrino problem can be resolved by introducing a new "semi-weak" interaction mediated by a massless pseudoscalar boson, and that the deuteron disintegration by neutral currents of solar neutrinos shows good agreement between the theoretical predictions of this hypothesis and experimental results from SNO, conditional on the effective number of neutrino-nucleon collisions in the Sun being set to n0 = 10 or 11.

## Method
The author calculates the cross sections for two non-interfering sub-processes of deuteron disintegration by neutral currents: a standard electroweak sub-process via Z-boson exchange (using tabulated cross sections from prior work) and a new sub-process via exchange of a massless pseudoscalar boson described by a Yukawa Lagrangian. The cross section for the pseudoscalar sub-process is derived using a spherically symmetric np-potential of the delta-function type, expanding the current in a Taylor series in powers of the neutron-proton mass difference (Mn - Mp), and integrating over the phase space of the 2→3 process. The total effective solar neutrino flux for the combined sub-processes is then computed for the ⁸B neutrino spectrum and compared to SNO experimental results.

## Key numbers
- The product of Yukawa coupling constants: g_{ν_e ps} g_{N ps} / 4π = (3.2 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁵, derived from the best-fit effective number of collisions n0 = 11.
- Effective solar neutrino flux for the Z-boson sub-process (Φ_eff^{nc(Z)}(⁸B)): 2.10 × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ for n0 = 11; 2.16 × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ for n0 = 10.
- Effective solar neutrino flux for the pseudoscalar boson sub-process (Φ_eff^{nc(ps)}(⁸B)): (2.87 ± 0.36) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ for n0 = 11; (2.90 ± 0.36) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ for n0 = 10.
- Total effective solar neutrino flux for neutral-current deuteron disintegration (Φ_eff^{nc}(⁸B)): (4.97 ± 0.36) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ for n0 = 11; (5.06 ± 0.36) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ for n0 = 10.
- SNO experimental results for Φ_eff^{nc}(⁸B) (in units of 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹): 5.09⁺⁰·⁴⁴₋₀·₄₃⁺⁰·⁴⁶₋₀·₄₃, 5.21 ± 0.27 ± 0.38, 4.94⁺⁰·²¹₋₀·²¹⁺⁰·³⁸₋₀·³⁴, and 5.54⁺⁰·³³₋₀·³¹⁺⁰·³⁶₋₀·³⁴.
- Cross sections for the pseudoscalar sub-process at selected neutrino energies: e.g., 0.047 × 10⁻⁴² cm² at 3.0 MeV, 0.382 × 10⁻⁴² cm² at 10.0 MeV, and 0.467 × 10⁻⁴² cm² at 16.0 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior paper (Slad, 2018, Part I) which applied the semi-weak interaction hypothesis to four other solar neutrino processes (³⁷Cl→³⁷Ar, ⁷¹Ga→⁷¹Ge, ν_e e⁻→ν_e e⁻, and ν_e D→e⁻ pp). The present analysis addresses the fifth observed process (deuteron disintegration by neutral currents) that was not covered in Part I. The results are compared to SNO experimental data (references 2-5 in the paper) and to standard model calculations of the Z-boson exchange cross section (reference 6). The author suggests that future experiments on deuteron disintegration by reactor antineutrinos could test the hypothesis, predicting a higher event rate than the standard electroweak model predicts.
