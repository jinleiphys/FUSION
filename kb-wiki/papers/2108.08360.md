# Energy dependence of underlying-event observables from RHIC to LHC energies

> A study of the charged-particle density (number density) in the transverse region of the di-hadron correlations exploiting the existing pp and p$\bar{\rm p}$ data from RHIC to LHC energies is reported. This region has contributions from the Underlying Event (UE) as well as from Initial- and Final-State Radiation (ISR-FSR). Based on the data, a two-component model is built. This has the functional form $\propto s^α+β\log(s)$, where the logarithmic (($β= 0.140 \pm 0.007$)) and the power-law ($α= 0.270 \pm 0.005$) terms describe the components more sensitive to the ISR-FSR and UE contributions, respectively. The model describes the data from RHIC to LHC energies, the extrapolation to higher energies indicates that at around $\sqrt{s} \approx 100$\,TeV the number density associated to UE will match that from ISR-FSR. Although this behaviour is not predicted by PYTHIA~8.244, the power-law behaviour of the UE contribution is consistent with the energy dependence of the parameter that regulates Multiparton Interactions. Using simulations, KNO-like scaling properties of the multiplicity distributions in the regions sensitive to either UE or ISR-FSR are also discussed. The results presented here can be helpful to constrain QCD-inspired Monte Carlo models at the Future Circular Collider energies, as well as to characterize the UE-based event classifiers which are currently used at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper presents a data-driven two-component model for the charged-particle density in the transverse region of di-hadron correlations, where the power-law term (exponent α = 0.270 ± 0.005) describes the component sensitive to Multiparton Interactions (UE) and the logarithmic term (β = 0.140 ± 0.007) describes the component sensitive to Initial- and Final-State Radiation; the abstract does not emphasize that the model is built specifically from published data at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC energies and that the extrapolation to 100 TeV predicts the UE contribution will match the ISR-FSR contribution, a behavior not reproduced by PYTHIA 8.244.

## Method

The analysis uses published charged-particle density measurements in the transverse region (π/3 < |Δφ| < 2π/3) of di-hadron correlations from pp and p\(\bar{\rm p}\) collisions at center-of-mass energies from 0.2 TeV (RHIC) to 13 TeV (LHC), selecting events with a trigger particle of pT ≥ 5 GeV/c and charged particles with pT ≥ 0.5 GeV/c and |η| < 0.8. The transverse region is subdivided into trans-min and trans-max to isolate contributions more sensitive to Multiparton Interactions (UE) and ISR-FSR, respectively. A two-component function of the form sα + β log(s) is fitted simultaneously to the transverse, trans-min, and trans-max data, with the power-law term constrained by trans-min data and the logarithmic term by trans-max data; systematic uncertainties are propagated by shifting data points within one sigma and repeating the fit 500 times.

## Key numbers

- Power-law exponent for the MPI-sensitive component: α = 0.270 ± 0.005
- Logarithmic coefficient for the ISR-FSR-sensitive component: β = 0.140 ± 0.007
- Extrapolation predicts that at √s ≈ 100 TeV, the number density from UE will match that from ISR-FSR
- At √s = 0.9 TeV, trans-max activity ≈ 0.78 and trans-min activity ≈ 0.20; at √s = 13 TeV, trans-max ≈ 1.53 and trans-min ≈ 0.81 (MPI contribution increases by factor ≈ 4, ISR-FSR by factor ≈ 2)
- Systematic uncertainty on the parametrization: ≈ 7.2% (constant with energy)
- In PYTHIA 8.244 (Monash tune), the exponent for the MPI-sensitive region is ≈ 0.23, close to the b = 0.215 value used in the tune's MPI energy evolution

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by the CDF Collaboration (Aaltonen, 2015) that trans-dif (trans-max minus trans-min) increases logarithmically with √s while trans-min increases as a power law, building a data-driven model that unifies measurements from RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2019), Tevatron (CDF), and LHC (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS). The model enables predictions for the Future Circular Collider (FCC) at √s up to 100 TeV, where the MPI contribution is expected to dominate the transverse region, contrary to PYTHIA 8.244 predictions, and can help constrain QCD-inspired Monte Carlo models in unexplored kinematic regimes (e.g., x ≲ 10⁻⁵). The KNO-like scaling analysis extends the work of Ortiz (2017) by examining trans-min, trans-max, and trans-dif regions separately, showing that the MPI-sensitive region exhibits approximate scaling for z up to 2.5 while the ISR-FSR-sensitive region shows scaling for z from 0 to 5.
