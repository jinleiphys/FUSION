# Nucleon form factors and a nonpointlike diquark

> Nucleon form factors are calculated on q^2 in [0,3] GeV^2 using an Ansatz for the nucleon's Fadde'ev amplitude motivated by quark-diquark solutions of the relativistic Fadde'ev equation. Only the scalar diquark is retained, and it and the quark are confined. A good description of the data requires a nonpointlike diquark correlation with an electromagnetic radius of 0.8 r_pi. The composite, nonpointlike nature of the diquark is crucial. It provides for diquark-breakup terms that are of greater importance than the diquark photon absorption contribution.

## Key claim

A good description of nucleon electromagnetic form factors on $q^2 \in [0,3]$ GeV$^2$ requires a nonpointlike scalar diquark correlation with an electromagnetic radius of $0.8\,r_\pi$, and the diquark-breakup terms arising from this composite nature are of greater importance than the diquark photon absorption contribution.

## Method

The authors represent the nucleon as a three-quark bound state involving a scalar diquark correlation, using a Fadde'ev amplitude motivated by solutions of the relativistic Fadde'ev equation. They calculate an impulse approximation to the nucleon-photon vertex that includes five contributions: direct quark-photon scattering with a spectator diquark, photon-diquark scattering with a spectator quark, and three distinct diquark breakup diagrams that effect the antisymmetrisation of the nucleon's Fadde'ev amplitude. The dressed-quark propagator and quark-photon vertex are taken from earlier Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of meson properties, while the diquark correlation is described by a one-parameter model for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and propagator.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: $r_p = 0.79$ fm (empirical: $0.87$ fm)
- Neutron charge radius: $r_n = -0.43$ fm (empirical: $-0.34$ fm)
- Proton magnetic moment: $\mu_p = 2.88\,\mu_N$ (empirical: $2.79\,\mu_N$)
- Neutron magnetic moment: $\mu_n = -1.58\,\mu_N$ (empirical: $-1.91\,\mu_N$)
- Neutron-to-proton magnetic moment ratio: $\mu_n/\mu_p = -0.55$ (empirical: $-0.68$)
- Diquark electromagnetic radius: $r_{0^+} = 0.45$ fm $= 0.80\,r_\pi$
- Scalar diquark correlation length: $\lambda_{0^+} = 0.27$ fm
- Model parameters (all in GeV): $\omega_\psi = 0.20$, $\omega_\Gamma = 1.0$, $m_\Delta = 0.63$
- Statistical errors on calculated quantities are $\leq 1\%$

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of meson electromagnetic properties (Mark 1996, Maris and Roberts 1998) to the nucleon three-body problem, using a quark-diquark truncation of the relativistic Fadde'ev equation. The calculation contradicts earlier pointlike diquark models that required the axial-vector diquark alone to remedy the neutron magnetic moment; here the scalar diquark breakup diagrams provide approximately 50% of both $\mu_p$ and $\mu_n$. The model enables future improvements including the addition of a nonpointlike axial-vector diquark correlation and direct solution of the Fadde'ev equation, which would allow correlating meson and baryon observables with very few parameters.
