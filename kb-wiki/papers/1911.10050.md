# An approximation to the Woods-Saxon potential based on a contact interaction

> We study a non-relativistic particle subject to a three-dimensional spherical potential consisting of a finite well and a radial $δ$-$δ'$ contact interaction at the well edge. This contact potential is defined by appropriate matching conditions for the radial functions, thereby fixing a self adjoint extension of the non-singular Hamiltonian. Since this model admits exact solutions for the wave function, we are able to characterize and calculate the number of bound states. We also extend some well-known properties of certain spherically symmetric potentials and describe the resonances, defined as unstable quantum states. Based on the Woods-Saxon potential, this configuration is implemented as a first approximation for a mean-field nuclear model. The results derived are tested with experimental and numerical data in the double magic nuclei $^{132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb with an extra neutron.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a finite spherical well combined with a radial δ-δ' contact interaction at the well edge can serve as a first approximation to the Woods-Saxon potential for mean-field nuclear models, with the caveat that this approximation holds in the limit where the surface thickness parameter a approaches zero, and the results are tested only for the specific cases of ¹³²Sn and ²⁰⁸Pb with an extra neutron.

## Method

The authors start from the three-dimensional single-particle Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon potential, spin-orbit coupling, and a second-derivative term, then take the limit a → 0⁺ to replace the smooth potential by a finite well plus δ and δ' contact interactions at the nuclear radius R. The radial Schrödinger equation is solved exactly in terms of Bessel functions inside the well and modified Bessel functions outside, with matching conditions at r = R that define a self-adjoint Hamiltonian via a 2×2 SL(2,ℝ) matrix. The secular equation for bound states is derived and analyzed to determine the number and ordering of bound states, and the model is tested against experimental and numerical data for neutron energy levels in ¹³³Sn and ²⁰⁹Pb.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear radius parametrization: R = r₀ A¹/³ with r₀ = 1.27 fm, surface thickness a = 0.7 fm, and typical well depth V₀ = 51 ± 33(N−Z)/A MeV for neutrons.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb core with a = 0.7 fm, r₀ = 1.27 fm, V₀ = 44.4 MeV, and V_so = 16.5 MeV·fm, the 0s₁/₂ state energy is −40.231 MeV using either form of the spin-orbit interaction.
- The 2p₁/₂ state in ²⁰⁸Pb shows the largest difference between the two spin-orbit form factors: −7.413 MeV with the 1/r factor versus −3.370 MeV without it.
- The dimensionless parameter v₀ = √(2μR²V₀/ħ²) is defined as positive, and the relative energy ε = |E|/V₀ lies in (0,1) for bound states within the well.

## Context

This work extends the use of contact interactions in nuclear physics, building on prior studies that employed Dirac delta interactions for calculating resonant parameters and energy spectra (dela Madrid, 2017) and for studying the spectral function of ²⁵O (Id Betan, 2018). The δ-δ' model provides exact analytic solutions for the wave function in terms of Bessel functions, enabling analytic characterization of bound state properties that are normally only accessible numerically with the full Woods-Saxon potential. The results enable future application to other nuclei and further study of resonances as unstable quantum states in nuclear mean-field models.
