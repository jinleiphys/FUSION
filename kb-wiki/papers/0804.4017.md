# Exploring Early Parton Momentum Distribution with the Ridge from the Near-Side Jet

> In a central nucleus-nucleus collision at high-energies, medium partons kicked by a near-side jet acquire a momentum along the jet direction and subsequently materialize as the observed ridge particles. They carry direct information on the early parton momentum distribution which can be extracted by using the ridge data for central AuAu collisions at \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV. The extracted parton momentum distribution has a thermal-like transverse momentum distribution but a non-Gaussian, relatively flat rapidity distribution at mid-rapidity with sharp kinematic boundaries at large rapidities that depend on the transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The early parton momentum distribution in central AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV can be extracted from ridge particle data using a momentum kick model, but the extracted distribution is not a simple product of independent transverse momentum and rapidity distributions; it has a thermal-like transverse shape but a non-Gaussian, relatively flat rapidity distribution at mid-rapidity with sharp kinematic boundaries at large rapidities that depend on transverse momentum.

## Method

The author applied a momentum kick model in which a near-side jet near the surface kicks medium partons, each acquiring a momentum kick q, and these kicked partons materialize as ridge particles via parton-hadron duality. The ridge particle momentum distribution is related to the initial parton momentum distribution by a momentum shift, allowing extraction of the initial distribution by fitting the model to STAR ridge data for central AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The initial parton momentum distribution was parametrized with a thermal factor exp(-√(m²+p_t²)/T) and a rapidity fall-off factor (1-x)^a, where x contains kinematic boundary constraints, and the parameters were determined by self-consistent comparison with experimental data from p_t = 0.15 GeV to 4 GeV and |η| from 0 to 3.9.

## Key numbers

- Average momentum kick per jet-parton collision |q| = 1 GeV
- Average effective number of kicked partons per jet f_R ⟨N⟩ = 4
- Rapidity fall-off parameter a = 0.5
- Temperature parameter T = 0.50 GeV
- Mass parameter m_d = 1 GeV
- Jet attenuation factor f_J = 0.63
- Jet temperature T_jet = 0.55 GeV
- Jet normalization N_jet = 0.75
- Jet width parameter σ_φ0 = 0.5 with mass scale m_a = 1.1 GeV
- For y = 3, the maximum p_t is 1.54 GeV; for y = 4, the maximum p_t is 0.55 GeV

## Context

This work extends the momentum kick model previously proposed by Wong (2007) to describe the ridge phenomenon observed by the STAR Collaboration in central AuAu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The extracted parton momentum distribution provides initial conditions for studying the evolution toward quark-gluon plasma and the dynamics of parton production, and the results were found to agree with independent PHOBOS measurements (Wen, 2008). The approach enables future investigations of jet momentum loss and parton evolution dynamics.
