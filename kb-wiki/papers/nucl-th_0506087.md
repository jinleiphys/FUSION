# Hadronic Matter is Soft

> The stiffness of the hadronic equation of state has been extracted from the production rate of $K^+$ mesons in heavy ion collisions around 1 $A$ GeV incident energy. The data are best described with a compressibility coefficient $κ$ around 200 MeV, a value which is usually called ``soft''. This is concluded from a detailed comparison of the results of transport theories with the experimental data using two different procedures: (i) the energy dependence of the ratio of $K^+$ from Au+Au and C+C collisions and (ii) the centrality dependence of the $K^+$ multiplicities. It is demonstrated that input quantities of these transport theories which are not precisely known, like the kaon-nucleon potential, the $ΔN \to N K^+ Λ$ cross section or the life time of the $Δ$ in matter do not modify this conclusion.

# Hadronic Matter is Soft

## Key claim

The hadronic equation of state is best described by a compressibility coefficient κ around 200 MeV (a "soft" EoS), extracted from K⁺ meson production in heavy-ion collisions around 1 A GeV, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that cross sections are not explicitly density-dependent in a way that compensates the increasing density with decreasing cross section, a form not proposed in the literature.

## Method

The authors employed the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) transport approach, which evolves Gaussian wave functions via classical Hamilton equations where the two-body potential is replaced by the expectation value of the real part of the Brückner G-matrix. They compared two independent experimental observables, the energy dependence of the K⁺ yield ratio for Au+Au versus C+C collisions, and the centrality dependence (participant-number scaling) of K⁺ multiplicities, against IQMD simulations using both soft (κ = 200 MeV) and hard (κ = 380 MeV) equations of state, while varying uncertain inputs such as the kaon-nucleon potential, the ΔN → N K⁺ Λ cross section, and the Δ lifetime in matter.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility coefficient κ ≈ 200 MeV (soft EoS) best describes the data, compared to κ = 380 MeV (hard EoS) which is ruled out.
- The ratio of K⁺ multiplicities per mass number A for Au+Au versus C+C collisions as a function of beam energy clearly distinguishes soft from hard EoS, with experimental uncertainties smaller than the difference between the two predictions.
- For Au+Au collisions at 1.5 A GeV, the measured centrality dependence gives α = 1.34 ± 0.16 in the scaling relation M_{K⁺} = A_{part}^α, compatible only with a soft EoS.
- The compressibility κ ≤ 200 MeV is extracted for hadronic matter at densities around 2.5 times normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀).
- The kaon mass in medium is taken as m^K(ρ) = m^K_0 (1 − 0.075 ρ/ρ₀).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to determine the nuclear equation of state from giant monopole resonances (which gave κ ≈ 240 MeV) and from in-plane nucleon flow (which had yielded premature conclusions for a hard EoS before momentum-dependent interactions were included). The authors specifically reference the KaoS collaboration data (Sturm et al., Fuchs et al.) and show that their IQMD calculations converge with those of Fuchs et al. The results supersede earlier flow-based conclusions (Pawlak et al.) that favored a hard EoS, and the finding that κ ≤ 200 MeV is even smaller than the monopole-resonance value, placing it near the lower bound of previously discussed intervals. This enables future work to use K⁺ production as a robust probe of the EoS at supra-normal densities, and the participant-number scaling exponent α as a model-independent observable.
