# Toward Universality in Similarity Renormalization Group Evolved Few-body Potential Matrix Elements

> We first examine how T-matrix equivalence drives the flow of similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved potential matrix elements to a universal form, with the ultimate goal of gaining insight into universality for three-nucleon forces. In agreement with observations made previously for Lee-Suzuki transformations, regions of universal potential matrix elements are restricted to where half-on-shell T-matrix equivalence holds, but the potentials must also reproduce binding energies. We find universality in local energy regions, reflecting a local decoupling by the SRG. To continue the study in the 3-body sector, we create a simple 1-D spinless boson "theoretical laboratory" for a dramatic improvement in computational efficiency. We introduce a basis-transformation, harmonic oscillator (HO) basis, which is used for current many-body calculations and discuss the imposed truncations. When SRG evolving in a HO-basis, we show that the evolved matrix elements, once transformed back into momentum-representation, differ from those when evolving with momentum representation. This is because the generator in each basis is not exactly the same due to the truncation, which creates evolution artifacts in the 3-body potential matrix elements. In the 2- body sector, this can be avoided by increasing the basis size, but it remains unclear whether this is possible in the 3-body sector, as truncation errors in the 3-body sector are more difficult to avoid, and the computational power required is greatly increased for three-body evolution.

## Key claim

The paper argues that similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved potential matrix elements flow to a universal form only where half-on-shell T-matrix equivalence holds and the potentials reproduce correct binding energies, not merely phase-shift equivalence; universality in matrix elements is restricted to local energy regions reflecting local decoupling by the SRG.

## Method

The author examines SRG evolution of several realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne v18, N3LO chiral potentials from Entem-Machleidt with 500 MeV cutoff, and five N3LO chiral potentials from Epelbaum et al.) and compares them to inverse scattering separable potentials (ISSPs) constructed directly from phase shifts with independently tunable binding energy. The ISSP formalism uses a rank-1 separable potential solved via the inverse scattering problem, with Blatt-Beidenharn convention for coupled channels, allowing tests of whether phase-shift equivalence alone suffices for universality. The author also introduces a delta-shell plus one-pion exchange (OPE) potential to test whether explicit long-range pion physics is required.

## Key numbers

- SRG evolution to lambda = 1.5 fm^-1 causes diagonal matrix elements of various realistic potentials to collapse to a universal form at low momentum
- The region of phase-shift equivalence among realistic potentials ends near the inelastic threshold around 350 MeV (approximately 4.1 fm^-1 in momentum)
- ISSP with binding energy 0 MeV (instead of physical 2.224 MeV) shows substantial deviation in low-energy diagonal matrix elements after SRG evolution
- ISSP with binding energy 5 MeV reproduces this energy to better than 100 eV and shows overbinding effects in low-momentum matrix elements
- Off-diagonal potential matrix elements V(k,k0) with k0 = 0.1 fm^-1 show that ISSP does not collapse to universal form at lambda = 1.5 fm^-1, unlike realistic potentials
- Local decoupling scale Lambda can be set to SRG lambda, with lambda = 1.5 fm^-1 and lambda = 4.0 fm^-1 tested for truncated phase shift reproduction

## Context

This work extends observations made for Lee-Suzuki transformations (vlowk potentials) by Bogner et al. (2001, 2003) that low-momentum matrix elements become model-independent, and applies similar analysis to SRG evolution. The paper tests the hypothesis from vlowk studies that half-on-shell T-matrix equivalence is required for off-diagonal universality, and demonstrates that correct binding energy is an additional prerequisite. The findings enable future work on universality in three-nucleon forces, for which the author proposes a one-dimensional spinless boson "theoretical laboratory" to improve computational efficiency, and warns that SRG evolution in a harmonic oscillator basis introduces truncation artifacts not present in momentum-space evolution.
