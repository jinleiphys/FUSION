# Kaon differential flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Using a relativistic transport model, we study the azimuthal momentum asymmetry of kaons with fixed transverse momentum, i.e., the differential flow, in heavy-ion collisions at beam momentum of 6 GeV/c per nucleon, available from the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) at the Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL). We find that in the absence of kaon potential the kaon differential flow is positive and increases with transverse momentum as that of nucleons. The repulsive kaon potential as predicted by theoretical models, however, reduces the kaon differetnial flow, changing it to negative for kaons with low momenta. Cancellation between the negative differential flow at low mementa and the positive one at high momenta is then responsible for the experimentally observed nearly vanishing in-plane transverse flow of kaons in heavy ion experiments.

# Kaon differential flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The experimentally observed nearly vanishing in-plane transverse flow of kaons in heavy-ion collisions is not due to isotropic production or cancellation from colliding hadrons, but rather results from a cancellation between negative differential flow at low transverse momenta (caused by a repulsive in-medium kaon potential) and positive differential flow at high transverse momenta (reflecting baryonic flow).

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model (ART1.0) for heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies, treating the imaginary part of the kaon self-energy via scatterings with other hadrons and the real part via a mean-field potential derived from the kaon-nucleon scattering length using the impulse approximation. They simulated Au+Au collisions at a beam momentum of 6 GeV/c per nucleon and an impact parameter of 4 fm, comparing results with and without the repulsive kaon potential. The differential flow was analyzed by measuring the azimuthal momentum asymmetry \(<\cos\phi>(y,p_t)\) as a function of transverse momentum for kaons in the rapidity range \(|y/y_{\rm cm}|\leq 0.5\).

## Key numbers

- The isospin-averaged kaon-nucleon scattering length used is \(a_{KN}\approx -0.255\) fm, which gives a repulsive kaon potential of about 30 MeV for a kaon at rest in normal nuclear matter.
- The average \(p_x/m\) of protons at \(y/y_{\rm c.m.}=0.5\) is around 0.1c, about twice as large as that of kaons when the kaon potential is not turned on.
- The differential flow of kaons becomes negative for transverse momenta less than about 0.8 GeV/c when the repulsive kaon potential is included, while remaining positive at higher transverse momenta.

## Context

This work extends the differential flow analysis previously applied to nucleons at the balance energy by Li and Sustich (1998) to the case of kaons, building on earlier studies by Li et al. (1995) that first demonstrated the possibility of extracting kaon in-medium properties from collective flow. The results contradict suggestions by Ogi (1998) and collaborators that vanishing kaon flow arises from isotropic production or by David (1998) that it results from colliding hadrons with opposite transverse flows. The findings enable a more detailed probe of the kaon in-medium potential using differential flow measurements, which have already been applied by the E877 collaboration at AGS and could be extended to SIS/GSI experiments.
