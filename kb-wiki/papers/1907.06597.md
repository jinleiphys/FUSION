# Hot quark matter and (proto-) neutron stars

> In part one of this paper, we use a non-local extension of the 3-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, which takes into account flavor-mixing, momentum dependent quark masses, and vector interactions among quarks, to investigate the possible existence of a spinodal region (determined by the vanishing of the speed of sound) in the QCD phase diagram and determine the temperature and chemical potential of the critical end point. In part two of the paper, we investigate the quark-hadron composition of baryonic matter at zero as well as non-zero temperature. This is of great topical interest for the analysis and interpretation of neutron star merger events such as GW170817. With this in mind, we determine the composition of proto-neutron star matter for entropies and lepton fractions that are typical of such matter. These compositions are used to delineate the evolution of proto-neutron stars to neutron stars in the baryon-mass versus gravitational-mass diagram. The hot stellar models turn out to contain significant fractions of hyperons and $Δ$-isobars but no deconfined quarks. The latter, are found to exist only in cold neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper uses a non-local extension of the 3-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (3nPNJL) model to investigate the QCD phase diagram and finds that hot proto-neutron star matter contains significant fractions of hyperons and Delta-isobars but no deconfined quarks, while deconfined quarks exist only in cold neutron stars, contingent on the specific choice of vector interaction coupling strength (zeta_v = 0.5) and the use of density-dependent relativistic mean-field models for hadronic matter.

## Method

The authors model quark matter using a non-local SU(3) PNJL model with Gaussian form factors, including flavor-mixing via the 't Hooft term and vector interactions parameterized by zeta_v = G_V/G_S. Hadronic matter is described using two density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) parametrizations (GM1L and DD2) that include neutrons, protons, hyperons, and Delta-isobars, with meson-hyperon couplings determined from the Nijmegen ESC08 model. The quark-hadron phase transition is treated as a sharp transition, and the composition of proto-neutron star matter is computed for entropies and lepton fractions typical of such matter, with stellar models evolved in the baryon-mass versus gravitational-mass diagram.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature from LQCD results: T_c(0) approximately 155 MeV (cited from Bazavov et al. and Aoki et al.)
- Gaussian form factor cutoff: Lambda = 1071.38 MeV
- Current quark masses: m_u = m_d = 3.63 MeV, m_s = 95.00 MeV (with m_s/m_u approximately 26)
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV; meson masses: m_pi = 139.0 MeV, m_K = 495 MeV, m_eta' = 958 MeV
- Polyakov loop potential parameter: T_0 = 195 MeV
- Nuclear saturation densities: n_0 = 0.153 fm^-3 (GM1L) and n_0 = 0.149 fm^-3 (DD2)
- Nuclear incompressibilities: K_0 = 300.0 MeV (GM1L) and K_0 = 242.7 MeV (DD2)
- Asymmetry energies: J = 32.5 MeV (GM1L) and J = 32.8 MeV (DD2)
- Slope of asymmetry energy: L_0 = 55.0 MeV (GM1L) and L_0 = 55.3 MeV (DD2)
- Effective nucleon masses: m*/m_N = 0.70 (GM1L) and m*/m_N = 0.56 (DD2)
- Vector interaction coupling values studied: zeta_v = 0.0 and zeta_v = 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram using local NJL models (Sasaki 2007, Sasaki 2008, PhysRevC.95.055203) by employing a non-local 3nPNJL model that avoids artificial momentum cutoffs and better reproduces LQCD results for momentum-dependent quark masses (Parapilly 2006). The results are relevant for interpreting neutron star merger events such as GW170817 and enable future studies of proto-neutron star evolution, building on earlier work by Most (2018) and Bauswein (2018) on first-order phase transitions in mergers. The hadronic model parameters are constrained by the 2 solar mass constraints from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) and radius constraints from GW170817 (Bauswein 2017, Annala 2017).
