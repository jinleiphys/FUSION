# Heavy-flavour transport: from large to small systems

> Predictions for heavy-flavour production in relativistic heavy-ion experiments provided by the POWLANG transport setup, including now also an in-medium hadronization model, are displayed, After showing some representative findings for the Au-Au and Pb-Pb cases, a special focus will be devoted to the results obtained in the small systems formed in proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions, where recent experimental data suggest the possible formation of a medium featuring a collective behaviour.

## Key claim

The POWLANG transport setup, now including an in-medium hadronization model based on recombination of heavy quarks with light thermal partons, can reproduce the bump in D-meson nuclear modification factor around pT ≈ 1.5 GeV/c in Au-Au collisions and also yields predictions for small systems (d-Au and p-Pb) where the observed overshoot above unity in the nuclear modification factor is interpreted as due to radial flow of parent D and B mesons, though experimental D-meson data in p-Pb do not yet allow firm conclusions about final-state hot-medium effects.

## Method

The authors interfaced the POWHEG-BOX pQCD event generator (with EPS09 nuclear PDF corrections) to a relativistic Langevin transport code for heavy-quark propagation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, whose evolution was provided by relativistic hydrodynamic calculations (ECHO-QGP code). The initial state for small systems was modeled through a Glauber Monte-Carlo approach generating thousands of initial conditions, with each nucleon-nucleon collision depositing entropy smeared by a Gaussian distribution. Heavy-quark hadronization was treated via recombination with light thermal partons to form colour-singlet strings that subsequently fragment.

## Key numbers

- D-meson nuclear modification factor in Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV shows a bump around pT ≈ 1.5 GeV/c, qualitatively reproduced by the model for most scenarios explored
- Initial temperature of the deconfined medium in p-Pb and d-Au collisions reaches around 400 MeV, with the medium living in the deconfined phase for up to 2-3 fm/c
- In p-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the theory curves for D-meson RpPb display a bump around pT ≈ 3 GeV/c
- Central (0-20%) d-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV show heavy-flavour decay electron data overshooting unity over an extended pT-range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous POWLANG transport studies (Beraudo 2014) by adding in-medium hadronization and applying the framework to small systems (d-Au at RHIC and p-Pb at LHC), where recent experimental data suggest collective behavior. The results are compared to STAR data for Au-Au, PHENIX data for d-Au, and ALICE data for p-Pb, and the interpretation of the bump in D-meson RAA as a signature of radial flow is consistent with independent studies (He 2011, Cao 2013, Gossiaux 2009). The work enables future tests of whether small deconfined systems leave signatures on heavy-flavour observables, with the authors noting that current experimental D-meson data do not contradict their results but do not yet allow firm conclusions.
