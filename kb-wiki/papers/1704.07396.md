# Hydrodynamics and critical slowing down

> 4 pages, 1 figure, Contribution to the Proceedings of the XXVI International Conference on Ultrarelativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions (Quark Matter 2017), Feb 5-11, Chicago, U.S.A

## Key claim
The authors introduce an effective theory called "Hydro+" that extends standard hydrodynamics into regimes where critical slowing down would otherwise make hydrodynamics inapplicable, specifically near the QCD critical point where the correlation length diverges and the bulk viscosity becomes anomalously large.

## Method
The authors augment conventional relativistic hydrodynamics with an additional non-hydrodynamic mode \(\phi\) (or a family of modes \(f_{\mathcal N}\)) whose relaxation rate is parametrically slow, governed by the correlation length \(\xi\). They derive the constitutive equations for the stress-energy tensor, current, and the additional mode from a quasi-equilibrium entropy functional, imposing constraints from the second law of thermodynamics. The linearized spectrum of the theory is analyzed to identify three regimes: conventional hydrodynamics (Regime I), Hydro+ (Regime II), and mode-coupling dynamics (Regime III).

## Key numbers
- The relaxation rate of the slow mode near the critical point scales as \(\xi^{-z}\) with \(z \approx 3\).
- The bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) diverges as \(\xi^{z-\alpha/\nu}\), approximated as \(\xi^3\).
- The domain of applicability of ordinary hydrodynamics is \(k \ll c_s w / \zeta \sim \xi^{-3}\), which is much smaller than the inverse correlation length \(\xi^{-1}\) near the critical point.
- The sound attenuation coefficient in Regime I is \(\gamma_L \sim \zeta / w \sim c_s^2 / \Gamma\), diverging as \(\Gamma \to 0\).
- The relation between regimes I and II is given by \(\Gamma c_s^2 = \Gamma^* {c^*_s}^2\) and \(\Delta \zeta \equiv \zeta - \zeta^* = w \Delta c_s^2 / \Gamma\) (relativistic generalization of the Landau-Khalatnikov formula).
- The characteristic scale for the mode distribution function \(f_{\mathcal N}\) is \(Q \sim \xi^{-1}\).

## Context
This work addresses the breakdown of conventional hydrodynamics near the QCD critical point, where the bulk viscosity diverges and the gradient expansion fails, as previously discussed by Stephanov (1998). The approach extends the Israel-Stewart theory, which adds non-hydrodynamic modes but with relaxation rates on the microscopic scale rather than parametrically small rates. The Hydro+ framework enables modeling of heavy-ion collision fireballs including critical slowing down effects, and connects to the mode-coupling dynamics of model H (RevModPhys.49.435) in the regime where the slow mode relaxation rate and diffusion rate become comparable.
