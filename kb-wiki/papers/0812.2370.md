# A simple and efficient numerical scheme to integrate non-local potentials

> As nuclear wave functions have to obey the Pauli principle, potentials issued from reaction theory or Hartree-Fock formalism using finite-range interactions contain a non-local part. Written in coordinate space representation, the Schrodinger equation becomes integro-differential, which is difficult to solve, contrary to the case of local potentials, where it is an ordinary differential equation. A simple and powerful method has been proposed several years ago, with the trivially equivalent potential method, where non-local potential is replaced by an equivalent local potential, which is state-dependent and has to be determined iteratively. Its main disadvantage, however, is the appearance of divergences in potentials if the wave functions have nodes, which is generally the case. We will show that divergences can be removed by a slight modification of the trivially equivalent potential method, leading to a very simple, stable and precise numerical technique to deal with non-local potentials. Examples will be provided with the calculation of the Hartree-Fock potential and associated wave functions of 16O using the finite-range N3LO realistic interaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the divergences in the trivially equivalent local potential (TELP) method, which arise at nodes of wave functions, can be removed by a slight modification that combines the TELP method with a source term, yielding a simple, stable, and precise numerical technique for non-local potentials.

## Method

The author modifies the TELP method by introducing a smoothing function \(F(r)\) that is virtually zero except near nodes of the wave function, where it cancels the divergences by switching the treatment from a local potential to a source term. The resulting scheme solves an integro-differential Schrödinger equation iteratively using a local potential \(v_{loc}(r)\) and a small source term \(s(r)\), with the smoothing function designed to keep both quantities finite. The method is applied to the Hartree-Fock (HF) potential of \(^{16}\)O using the finite-range N\(^3\)LO realistic interaction renormalized with a low-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.9\) fm\(^{-1}\), with HF equations solved iteratively using linear mixing.

## Key numbers

- HF single-particle energies for \(^{16}\)O: proton \(0s_{1/2}\) at -64.657 MeV, neutron \(0s_{1/2}\) at -69.444 MeV; proton \(0p_{3/2}\) at -29.221 MeV, neutron \(0p_{3/2}\) at -33.821 MeV; proton \(0p_{1/2}\) at -19.753 MeV, neutron \(0p_{1/2}\) at -24.065 MeV; unoccupied proton \(1s_{1/2}\) at 0.519 MeV with width 22.928 keV, neutron \(1s_{1/2}\) at -2.826 MeV.
- HF iterative process converged in 32 iterations using a potential mixing of 80%.
- Overlaps between calculated \(s_{1/2}\) states (bound, resonant, and scattering) showed average norms of \(2.207 \times 10^{-9}\) for protons and \(1.307 \times 10^{-9}\) for neutrons, and maximal norms of \(1.526 \times 10^{-8}\) for protons and \(1.287 \times 10^{-8}\) for neutrons, demonstrating numerical orthogonality.
- The N\(^3\)LO interaction was decomposed using 9 harmonic oscillator basis states per partial wave with oscillator parameter \(b = 2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the TELP method introduced by Vautherin and Veneroni (several years prior), which suffered from divergences at wave function nodes, and improves upon the source method used in the FRESCO reaction code, which converges slowly and can be unstable. The proposed technique enables efficient and precise calculation of both bound and scattering states for non-local potentials, as demonstrated for the HF potential of \(^{16}\)O, and is applicable to more complex scenarios such as coupled-channel equations and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations.
