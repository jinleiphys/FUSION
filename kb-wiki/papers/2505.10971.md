# Collective excited states at small amplitude in neutron elastic scattering at low-energies

> We investigate the contributions of isoscalar and isovector collective excitations in the neutron elastic scattering of $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb nuclei by using a microscopic optical potential (MOP) derived from nuclear structure models based on self-consistent mean-field approaches. Particular attention is given to the role of these collective modes in shaping the imaginary part of the MOP and the resulting angular distributions. Our analysis indicates that both isoscalar and isovector contributions are significant for all considered targets, especially for light and medium targets. Furthermore, the Coulomb interaction is found to play an important role in describing absorption mechanisms and reproducing the experimental angular distributions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both isoscalar and isovector collective excitations contribute significantly to the imaginary part of the microscopic optical potential and to neutron elastic scattering angular distributions, but this significance is conditional on the inclusion of the Coulomb interaction in the mean-field and RPA residual interaction; without Coulomb interaction, isovector contributions are small and the isoscalar contribution alone can reasonably reproduce experimental data for heavier targets like ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Method

The authors perform fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the Skyrme SLy5 effective interaction, followed by Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to obtain collective excited states with natural parity multipolarities L = 0⁺, 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 5⁻ and excitation energies below 50 MeV. The nonlocal, complex, energy-dependent microscopic optical potential (MOP) is constructed from particle-vibration coupling (PVC) on top of these RPA states, and the Schrödinger equation is solved using the DWBA98 code to obtain angular distributions for neutron elastic scattering off ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ²⁰⁸Pb at incident energies below 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The radial Hartree-Fock equations are solved using a radial mesh size of 0.1 fm, with the radial coordinate extending up to a maximum of 15 fm.
- The parameter η = 1.5 is introduced to perform energy averaging on the dynamical potential ΔΣ(ω).
- All natural parity RPA excited states with multipolarity L = 0⁺, 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 5⁻ and excitation energies below 50 MeV are included.
- Incident neutron energies are below 50 MeV for all four targets (¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb).

## Context

This work extends the fully self-consistent microscopic optical potential approach previously developed by T. V. Nhan Hao et al. (2015, 2018) and Nguyen Hoang Tung et al. (2020), which used Skyrme interactions to study neutron elastic scattering. It goes beyond the seminal but non-self-consistent calculation by V. Bernard et al. (1979), who found isovector modes contributed very little only for ²⁰⁸Pb, by employing a fully self-consistent approach in a significantly broader configuration space (including both isoscalar and isovector states with L = 0⁺, 2⁺, 3⁻, 4⁺, 5⁻). The results enable future extension to unstable nuclei via deformed Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) frameworks, as pursued by Mizuyama et al. (2023, 2024).
