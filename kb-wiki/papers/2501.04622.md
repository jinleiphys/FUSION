# Pressure inside hadrons: criticism, conjectures, and all that

> The interpretation of the energy-momentum tensor form factor $D(t)$ of hadrons in terms of pressure and shear force distributions is discussed, concerns raised in the literature are reviewed, and ways to reconcile the concerns with the interpretation are indicated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the interpretation of the energy-momentum tensor form factor D(t) in terms of pressure and shear force distributions inside hadrons is physically sound, despite concerns raised in the literature, provided one recognizes that the isotropic pressure function p(r) can and must become negative in some region to satisfy the von Laue condition, and that the continuum description is justified in the large-Nc limit or by adopting a phase-space perspective.

## Method

The authors review the standard derivation of the static energy-momentum tensor in the Breit frame via Fourier transform of the gravitational form factor matrix elements, leading to a stress tensor of the form T^{ij}(r) = delta^{ij} p(r) + (r^i r^j/r^2 - delta^{ij}/3) s(r). They then systematically examine each criticism raised in the literature: relativistic recoil corrections in the Breit frame, the sign of pressure, applicability of continuum mechanics to hadrons, the sign of the D-term, QED long-range effects, and the positive D-term of the hydrogen atom. For each concern, they present counterarguments and resolutions drawing on the von Laue condition, the liquid drop model, the bag model, and the large-Nc limit.

## Key numbers

- The D-term is given by D = -(4/15) M integral d^3r r^2 s(r) = M integral d^3r r^2 p(r), where M is the nucleon mass
- The von Laue condition requires integral d^3r T^{ii}(r) = 0, equivalently integral_0^infinity dr r^2 p(r) = 0
- In the Bogoliubov model, the nucleon mass is M_Bogo = N_c omega_0/R with omega_0 approximately 2.04, and p(r)_Bogo > 0 (unstable system)
- In the bag model, the mass is M_bag = N_c omega_0/R + (4/3)pi R^3 B, and minimization yields N_c omega_0 = 4pi R^4 B, giving M_bag = (4/3) M_Bogo
- For the liquid drop, the Kelvin relation gives p_0 = 2gamma/R for the internal pressure in terms of surface tension gamma and radius R
- Maxim predicted nuclear D-terms scale as D proportional to -A^{7/3} for mass number A

## Context

This work extends and defends the interpretation originally proposed by Polyakov (2002) that the D-term form factor encodes mechanical properties of hadrons, specifically pressure and shear force distributions. It directly addresses criticisms raised by Jaffe (2020), Freese (2021, 2024), Ji (2021, 2022), Fujita (2022), and Czarnecki (2023) regarding Breit frame recoil corrections, the sign of pressure, and the applicability of continuum descriptions. The paper reconciles these concerns by appealing to the large-Nc limit for justifying 3D densities, the von Laue condition from von Laue (1911) as a necessary stability condition, and the analogy with the liquid drop model and bag model where negative pressure regions provide the confining Poincare stresses. This enables future work on the mechanical properties of hadrons, including experimental tests of the A^(7/3) scaling for nuclear D-terms.
