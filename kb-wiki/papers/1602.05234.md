# Di-neutrons in neutron matter within Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach

> We investigate the appearance of di-neutron bound states in pure neutron matter within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach at zero temperature. We consider Argonne $v_{18}$ and Paris bare interactions as well as chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. Self-consistent single-particle potentials are calculated controlling explicitly singularities in the $g$ matrix associated with bound states. Di-neutrons are loosely bound, with binding energies below $1$ MeV, but are unambiguously present for Fermi momenta below $1$ fm$^{-1}$ for all interactions. Within the same framework we are able to calculate and characterize di-neutron bound states, obtaining mean radii as high as $\sim 110$ fm. The resulting equations of state and mass-radius relations for pure neutron stars are analyzed including di-neutron contributions.

## Key claim

Di-neutron bound states are unambiguously present in pure neutron matter for Fermi momenta below 1 fm⁻¹ for all interactions considered (Argonne v₁₈, Paris, and chiral two- and three-nucleon forces), but only when self-consistent single-particle potentials are calculated with explicit control of singularities in the g matrix associated with bound states.

## Method

The authors solve the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) equations self-consistently at zero temperature for pure neutron matter, using the continuous choice for the single-particle spectrum. They explicitly treat singularities in the G-matrix arising from bound states in the ¹S₀ and ³PF₂ channels, and extract di-neutron binding energies and wave functions from the pole structure of the in-medium interaction. Calculations include partial waves up to total angular momentum J=7, and use Argonne v₁₈, Paris, chiral N³LO two-nucleon, and chiral N³LO+N²LO three-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers

- Di-neutron binding energies are below 700 keV for all interactions, with the deepest binding at k_F ≈ 0.6 fm⁻¹ ranging between 550 and 700 keV
- Argonne v₁₈ yields the largest binding energy of -0.7 MeV, while Paris and chiral interactions produce binding energies of approximately -0.6 MeV
- Di-neutron bound states exist for Fermi momenta in the range 0.05-1.05 fm⁻¹ for all four interactions
- Mean radii of di-neutrons exceed 60 fm in all cases, reaching values as high as approximately 110 fm
- Effective-to-bare mass ratio m*/m reaches a maximum of approximately 1.2 at Fermi momenta in the range 0.3-0.4 fm⁻¹
- The radial probability density shows oscillatory nodes with periodicity near 5.2 fm at k_F = 0.6 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the BHF treatment of in-medium di-nucleon bound states from symmetric nuclear matter (Arellano, 2015) to pure neutron matter, where the isoscalar ³SD₁ channel is suppressed and no coexisting single-particle spectra are found. The results provide microscopic input for cluster models used in astrophysical simulations (Typel, 2010; Hempel, 2011; Shen, 2011) and connect to studies of the BCS-BEC crossover in neutron matter (Alm, 1993; Margueron, 2007; Jin, 2010; Stein, 2014). The findings enable future investigations of di-neutron contributions to neutron star equations of state and cooling evolution, and establish a link between di-neutron bound states and Cooper pairs in superfluid neutron matter.
