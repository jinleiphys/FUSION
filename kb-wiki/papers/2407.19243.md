# Cold nuclear matter effects on azimuthal decorrelation in heavy-ion collisions

> The assumption of factorization lies at the core of calculations of medium effects on observables computable in perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics. In this work we examine this assumption, for which we propose a setup to study hard processes and bulk nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions on the same footing using the Glauber modelling of heavy nuclei. To exemplify this approach, we calculate the leading-order corrections to azimuthal decorrelation in Drell-Yan and boson-jet processes due to cold nuclear matter effects, not considering radiation. At leading order in both the hard momentum scale and the nuclear size, the impact-parameter dependent cross section is found to factorize for both processes. The factorization formula involves a convolution of the hard cross section with the medium-modified parton distributions, and, for boson-jet production, the medium-modified jet function.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at leading order in both the hard momentum scale and the nuclear size, the impact-parameter dependent cross section for Drell-Yan and boson-jet processes factorizes even when cold nuclear matter effects are included, provided the factorization formula involves a convolution of the hard cross section with medium-modified parton distributions and, for boson-jet production, the medium-modified jet function.

## Method

The authors propose a setup that treats hard processes and bulk nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions on the same footing using Glauber modelling of heavy nuclei. They calculate leading-order corrections to azimuthal decorrelation in Drell-Yan and boson-jet processes due to cold nuclear matter effects, without considering radiation. The calculation employs soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) with rapidity renormalization group evolution to resum large logarithms, and derives the one-loop beam functions, jet functions, and soft functions needed for the factorization theorem.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop quark beam function anomalous dimension: $\gamma_\mu^{B_q} = \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{\pi}\left( 2\ln\frac{\nu}{p^-} +\frac{3}{2}\right)$
- The one-loop gluon beam function anomalous dimension: $\gamma_\mu^{B_g} = \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}\left( 2C_A\ln\frac{\nu}{p^-} +\frac{\beta_0}{2}\right)$ with $\beta_0 = \frac{11}{3}C_A - \frac{4}{3}T_F n_f$
- The one-loop quark jet function finite piece: $J_q = 1 + \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{\pi}{\left(L_\perp\ln\frac{\nu}{p_J^-}+\frac{3}{4} L_\perp-\frac{\pi ^2}{6}+\frac{7}{4}-\frac{3}{2}\ln2\right)}$
- The one-loop gluon jet function $J_{g,2}$: $J_{g,2} = \frac{\alpha}{4\pi}\frac{1}{3}(2T_F n_f - C_A)$
- The cusp anomalous dimension up to three loops: $\gamma_0^{\text{cusp}} = 4$, $\gamma_1^{\text{cusp}} = \left( \frac{268}{9} - \frac{4\pi^2}{3} \right) C_A - \frac{80}{9}\,T_F n_f$

## Context

This work extends the study of factorization in perturbative QCD for heavy-ion collisions, specifically examining the assumption of factorization for medium effects on observables. The approach builds on the Glauber modelling of heavy nuclei and SCET techniques for resummation, as developed in prior work by Stewart (2009) on beam function matching to PDFs and by Moch (2004) on cusp anomalous dimensions. The results enable future calculations of azimuthal decorrelation observables in heavy-ion collisions that consistently include both hard scattering and cold nuclear matter effects.
