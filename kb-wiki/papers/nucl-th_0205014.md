# Strange particle production at RHIC in the Dual Parton Model

> We compute the mid-rapidity densities of pions, kaons, baryons and antibaryons in $Au$-$Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s} =$ 130 GeV in the Dual Parton Model supplemented with final state interaction (comovers interaction). The ratios $B/n_{part}$ ($\bar{B}/n_{part}$) increase between peripheral ($n_{part} = 18$) and central ($n_{part} = 350)$ collisions by a factor 2.4 (2.0) for $Λ$'s, 4.8 (4.1) for $Ξ$'s and 16.5 (13.5) for $Ω$'s. The ratio $K^-/π^-$ increases by a factor 1.3 in the same centrality range. A comparison with available data is presented.

## Key claim

The Dual Parton Model (DPM) supplemented with final-state interactions (comover interactions) can reproduce the centrality-dependent enhancement of strange baryon and antibaryon yields per participant in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, provided that the net baryon production includes a diquark-breaking (string-junction) mechanism and that the strangeness suppression factor is set to S = 0.1. The enhancement pattern is a firm prediction of the approach, but the absolute yields depend on a single free parameter that must be re-determined after feed-down corrections from weak decays are applied to the data.

## Method

The authors compute mid-rapidity densities of pions, kaons, baryons, and antibaryons in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV using the Dual Parton Model with shadowing corrections from Capella and Sousa (2001). The initial DPM yields are then evolved using gain-and-loss differential equations (comover interaction model) that include strangeness-exchange and production channels such as pi N <-> K Lambda, pi Lambda <-> K Xi, and pi Xi <-> K Omega, all with a common cross-section of 0.2 mb. Net baryon production is treated via a diquark-breaking mechanism based on baryon-junction transfer in rapidity, with relative weights for baryon species determined by quark-counting rules using either I2 or I3 expansions depending on whether one or three sea quarks form the net baryon.

## Key numbers

- The ratios B/n_part and anti-B/n_part increase between peripheral (n_part = 18) and central (n_part = 350) collisions by a factor 2.4 (2.0) for Lambdas, 4.8 (4.1) for Xis, and 16.5 (13.5) for Omegas.
- The ratio K-/pi- increases by a factor 1.3 (30%) over the same centrality range, from about 1.1 to 1.4.
- At N_ch/N_max_ch = 1/2, the predicted antibaryon-to-baryon ratios are: anti-p/p = 0.69, anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.72, anti-Xi/Xi = 0.79, anti-Omega/Omega = 0.83.
- The freeze-out density used is rho_f = 2 fm^-2, corresponding to the charged density per unit rapidity in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV.
- The strangeness suppression factor is S = 0.1 (S/L = 0.22).
- The common cross-section for all final-state interaction channels is sigma = 0.2 mb.

## Context

This work extends the DPM-plus-comovers approach previously applied to SPS data by Capella and Salgado (1999, 2000) to RHIC energies, using the same formalism for baryon stopping via junction transfer developed by Rossi and Veneziano (1977) and implemented by Capella and Kopeliovich (1996). The results are compared to preliminary PHENIX and STAR data from 2001-2002; the model reproduces the observed mild increase of K-/pi- and the centrality dependence of hyperon yields, but the predicted Xi yields are 60-70% lower than preliminary STAR results for the 14% most central events. The work enables future tests of whether "anomalous" stopping exists in AA collisions once feed-down corrected data become available.
