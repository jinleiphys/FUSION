# Addendum to: Combined Constraints on First Generation Leptoquarks

> In this addendum to arXiv:2101.07811 we discuss the implications of the recent CMS analysis of lepton flavour universality violation in non-resonant di-lepton pairs for first generation leptoquarks. As CMS finds more electron events than expected from background, this analysis prefers the LQ representations $\tilde{S}_1, S_2, S_3, \tilde{V}_1, V_2\,(κ_2^{RL}\ne 0)$ and $V_3$ which lead to constructive interference with the SM. In principle the excess could also be (partially) explained by the representations $\tilde{S}_2, V_1\,(κ_1^R\ne 0), V_2\,(κ_2^{LR}\ne 0), \tilde{V}_2$ which are interfering destructively, as this would still lead to the right effect in bins with high invariant mass where the new physics contribution dominates. However, in these cases large couplings would be required which are excluded by other observables. The representations $S_1, V_1\, (κ_1^{L} \ne 0)$ cannot improve the fit to the CMS data compared to the SM.

## Key claim

The CMS analysis of lepton flavour universality in non-resonant di-lepton pairs, which finds more electron events than expected from background, prefers first-generation leptoquark representations that interfere constructively with the Standard Model; representations that interfere destructively could in principle explain the excess in high-invariant-mass bins but require large couplings that are excluded by other observables.

## Method

The authors calculated the ratio of differential cross sections Rμμ/ee for Drell-Yan production of muon and electron pairs at leading order in perturbation theory, using the NNPDF23LO parton distribution functions. They integrated this ratio over the nine invariant mass bins between 200 and 3500 GeV reported by CMS, replaced the effective interaction by the full LQ propagator when the LQ mass was not much higher than the dilepton energy, and performed a χ² fit with two degrees of freedom to find the best-fit mass and coupling for each of the ten possible scalar and vector LQ representations under the SM gauge group.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit masses for scalar LQs range from 1.5 TeV (for the coupling λ̃₂) to 3.1 TeV (for λ̃₁), with corresponding couplings from 0.55 (λ₃) to 0.91 (λ̃₁).
- The best-fit masses for vector LQs range from 0.6 TeV (for κ̃₂) to 4.3 TeV (for κ̃₁), with corresponding couplings from 0.35 (κ₂^LR, κ̃₂) to 0.61 (κ̃₁).
- The improvement in χ² relative to the Standard Model, Δχ², is approximately -11 for the representations that provide better fits, with values ranging from -10.0 (κ̃₂) to -11.2 (λ₃, λ̃₂).
- The CMS data points cover an invariant mass range from 200 to 3500 GeV across nine bins.

## Context

This addendum updates the combined low- and high-energy constraints on first-generation leptoquarks presented in Crivellin (2021) by incorporating the recent CMS measurement of lepton flavour universality violation in non-resonant di-leptons from Sirunyan (2021). The analysis extends previous work on leptoquark explanations of flavor anomalies including b→sℓ⁺ℓ⁻ data, R(D^(*)), and the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and identifies which LQ representations can simultaneously explain the CMS electron excess while remaining consistent with constraints from parity violation, kaon decays, D⁰-D̄⁰ mixing, and ATLAS Drell-Yan measurements.
