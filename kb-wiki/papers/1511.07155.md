# Effect of correlations on cumulants in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the effects of correlations on cumulants and their ratios of net-proton multiplicity distributions which have been measured for central (0\%-5\%) Au + Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). This effect has been studied assuming individual proton and anti-proton distributions as a Poisson or Negative Binomial Distribution (NBD). In-spite of significantly correlated production due to baryon number, electric charge conservation and kinematical correlations of protons and anti-protons, the measured cumulants of the net-proton distribution follow the independent production model. In the present work we demonstrate how the introduction of the correlations will affect the cumulants and their ratios for the difference distributions. We have also demonstrated this study using the proton and anti-proton distributions obtained from the HIJING event generator.

## Key claim

The measured cumulants of net-proton distributions in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC follow the independent production model even though protons and anti-protons are significantly correlated due to baryon number conservation, electric charge conservation, and kinematic correlations; this agreement does not rule out the existence of a critical endpoint, because the ratios \(C_4/C_2\) and \(C_3/C_1\) are independent of the correlation coefficient for any degree of correlation.

## Method

The authors introduced correlations between proton and anti-proton multiplicity distributions by adding a common third Poisson or negative binomial distribution (NBD) to independently generated proton and anti-proton distributions, controlling the correlation coefficient \(\rho\) via the mean of the mixing distribution. They then compared cumulants and their ratios calculated from the event-by-event net-proton distribution with those obtained from the independent production formula \(C_n = C_n^+ + (-1)^n C_n^-\), using mean multiplicities from STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) and 200 GeV. The same procedure was repeated using proton and anti-proton distributions from the HIJING event generator for central (0%–5%) Au+Au collisions at multiple energies.

## Key numbers

- Mean multiplicities used for protons: \(5.664 \pm 0.0006\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and \(11.375 \pm 0.003\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV; for anti-protons: \(4.116 \pm 0.0005\) at 200 GeV and \(1.15 \pm 0.001\) at 19.6 GeV (from STAR data).
- For Poisson and NBD cases, the \(C_2\) and \(C_4\) values from the independent production model deviate from the uncorrelated baseline when the correlation coefficient exceeds approximately 20%.
- The \(C_4/C_2\) and \(C_3/C_1\) ratios are independent of the correlation coefficient for all values of \(\rho\) in both Poisson and NBD cases.
- The independent production formula is valid for net-proton cumulants only when the correlation coefficient is less than about 30%–35%.
- In HIJING, the correlation coefficient between protons and anti-protons is less than about 20% across energies, leading to agreement between the two calculation methods.

## Context

This work extends studies by the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk 2014) which found that net-proton cumulant ratios follow the independent production model, and by Tarnowsky (2012) and Westfall (2014) who used HIJING and UrQMD event generators. The paper demonstrates that the agreement with the independent production model does not require the absence of correlations; rather, the ratios \(C_4/C_2\) and \(C_3/C_1\) are insensitive to correlations, while \(C_1/C_2\) and \(C_3/C_2\) are sensitive probes. This enables experimental searches for the critical endpoint by focusing on the correlation-sensitive ratios as a function of collision energy.
