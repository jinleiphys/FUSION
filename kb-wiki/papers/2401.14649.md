# Role of Coulomb interaction in elastic pion-proton scattering from holography

> Differential cross sections of the elastic pion-proton scattering are investigated at very small momentum transfer in a holographic QCD model, considering both the strong and Coulomb interaction in the Regge regime. The strong interaction is described by the Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, and the Coulomb interaction is characterized by the one photon exchange. The two interactions are linked through an interference term and we only need to determine a single adjustable parameter involved in this term. As to the parameters for the strong interaction, we can utilize the values determined in the previous studies. The differential cross sections can be predicted without any additional parameters, and it is shown that our predictions are consistent with the experimental data. We explicitly show the momentum transfer dependence for the interference effect. The energy dependence of the contribution ratios for each component is also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper extends a previous holographic QCD model for elastic pion-proton scattering by including the Coulomb interaction and its interference with the strong interaction, claiming that differential cross sections can be predicted without additional free parameters beyond a single adjustable parameter in the interference term, and that predictions are consistent with experimental data in the very low momentum transfer region \(0 < |t| < 0.01\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method
The strong interaction amplitude is constructed from Pomeron (glueball) and Reggeon (vector meson) exchange in the Regge regime, using Reggeized propagators and gravitational form factors for the proton and pion obtained from the hard-wall bottom-up AdS/QCD model. The Coulomb amplitude is described by one-photon exchange in QED with electromagnetic form factors from the same AdS/QCD model, and the total amplitude combines both via an eikonal-model-based Coulomb-nuclear interference phase. The nine parameters for the strong interaction are taken from previous fits to \(pp\), \(p\bar{p}\), and \(\pi p\) scattering data, leaving only the interference phase parameter \(\Lambda^2\) to be determined by matching the dipole form factor to the product \(G_p(Q^2) \times G_\pi(Q^2)\).

## Key numbers
- The strong interaction parameters include: \(\alpha_g(0) = 1.086\), \(\alpha_g' = 0.377\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\lambda_{gpp} = 9.699\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (from high-energy \(pp\)/\(p\bar{p}\) fits); \(\alpha_v(0) = 0.444\), \(\alpha_v' = 0.925\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\lambda_{vpp} = 7.742\) (from medium-energy \(pp\)/\(p\bar{p}\) fits); \(\lambda_{g\pi\pi} = 3.361\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(\lambda_{v\pi^-\pi^-} = 4.528\), \(\lambda_{v\pi^+\pi^+} = 6.049\) (from \(\pi p\) fits in Liu (2023)).
- The IR boundary parameters for the hard-wall model are \(z_0^p = 1/(245\ \mathrm{MeV})\) for the proton and \(z_0^\pi = 1/(323\ \mathrm{MeV})\) for the pion.
- The pion decay constant is set to \(f_\pi = 131\ \mathrm{MeV}\), yielding \(\beta^{1/3} = 424\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The Coulomb and interference contributions are studied in the range \(0 < |t| < 0.01\) GeV\(^2\), extending the previous strong-interaction-only study which covered \(0.01 < |t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\sqrt{s} > 10\) GeV.
- The Coulomb-nuclear interference nearly vanishes at \(|t| = 0.01\) GeV\(^2\), as shown in earlier works by Amos (1985) and the UA4 Collaboration (1987).

## Context
This work extends the holographic QCD model of Liu (2023), which described \(\pi p\) scattering via Pomeron and Reggeon exchange in the range \(0.01 < |t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\), by adding the Coulomb interaction and interference term for the even smaller momentum transfer region \(0 < |t| < 0.01\) GeV\(^2\). It builds on prior holographic studies of \(pp\) and \(p\bar{p}\) scattering by Xie (2019) and Liu (2022), and on the theoretical treatment of Coulomb-nuclear interference by Bethe (1958), West and Yennie (1968), Cahn (1982), and Kundrat and Lokajicek (2004, 2007). The results enable future extraction of the \(\rho\) parameter and detailed amplitude structure for \(\pi p\) scattering at very forward angles.
