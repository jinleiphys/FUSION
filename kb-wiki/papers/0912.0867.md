# Synthesis of transactinide nuclei in cold fusion reactions using radioative beams

> Chances of synthesis of transactinide nuclei in cold fusion reactions (one-neutron-out) reactions using radioactive beams are evaluated. Because intensities of radioactive beams are in most of the cases significantly lower than the ones of the stable beams, reactions with the highest radioactive beam intensities for the particular elements are considered. The results are compared with the recent ones obtained by Loveland who investigated the same nuclei.

# Synthesis of transactinide nuclei in cold fusion reactions using radioactive beams

## Key claim
The paper evaluates production rates for neutron-rich transactinide nuclei using cold fusion (one-neutron-out) reactions with radioactive-ion beams, finding that synthesis of lower-Z elements (106-107, 109-111) is most likely with production rates of days per atom, while higher-Z synthesis is practically excluded with present-day technology. The author notes that their cross section for 208Pb(51Ti,1n)258Rf appears overestimated by two orders of magnitude compared to systematics, possibly due to overestimation of the fission barrier and underestimation of the neutron separation energy in the macroscopic-microscopic model.

## Method
The author calculates formation cross sections at the optimal bombarding energy using a model formula (from Smolanczuk 2008) that multiplies the classical capture cross section, the compound nucleus formation probability (fusion hindrance), and the survival probability (neutron-to-fission width ratio at zero angular momentum). The fusion barrier is computed using a folding potential with dependence on nuclear surface thickness and the least-bound nucleon separation energy, while the inner barrier position is inversely proportional to the fissility parameter. Beam intensities are taken from the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) webpage as listed in Loveland (2007), and the cross section times beam intensity factor is compared to the reference reaction 208Pb(70Zn,1n)277112 with a stable beam intensity of 3 × 10^12.

## Key numbers
- Reference reaction 208Pb(70Zn,1n)277112: measured cross section of 0.5 pb with stable beam intensity 3 × 10^12 gives a production rate of 23 days/atom.
- Best candidate reaction 208Pb(54V,1n)261Db: production rate of 0.13 days/atom.
- For element Rg (Z=111): production rate of almost 23 days/atom.
- For 208Pb(91Kr,1n)298118: predicted beam intensity is high enough to produce 23 atoms/day, yielding an isotope of element 118 with neutron number 180 (close to the neutron magic number 184).
- The constant a = 5.03 fm^-1 was adjusted to measured excitation functions for five reactions: 208Pb(50Ti,1n)257Rf, 209Bi(50Ti,1n)258Db, 208Pb(54Cr,1n)261Sg, 208Pb(58Fe,1n)265Hs, and 208Pb(64Ni,1n)271Ds.
- The constant b_inner = 427.69 fm was determined assuming 208Pb(48Ca,1n)255No is unhindered (P_CN = 1).
- Temperatures used: T_sd = 1 MeV; T_eq = 0.55 MeV for even-even and odd-N compound nuclei; T_eq = 0.5 MeV for odd-Z and odd-odd compound nuclei.

## Context
This work extends the cold fusion reaction approach (originally proposed by Oganessian and collaborators in 1975) that has been used to discover elements from Bh (Z=107) to 112 and recently element 113 (Morita 2004), all using stable projectiles. The paper compares its results directly with Loveland (2007), who investigated the same nuclei using the same beam intensities from the RIA webpage; the author finds generally smaller production rates than Loveland except for 208Pb(54V,1n)261Db. The results suggest that synthesis of lower-Z transactinides (106-107, 109-111) with neutron-rich radioactive beams is feasible with production rates of days per atom, while higher-Z synthesis remains impractical with current technology, with production rates up to two to three orders of magnitude smaller than the reference reaction.
