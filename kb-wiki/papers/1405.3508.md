# Light vector meson masses in strange hadronic matter -- a QCD sum rule approach

> We study the properties of the light vector mesons ($ρ$, $ω$ and $ϕ$) in strange hadronic matter using the QCD sum rule approach. The in-medium masses of the vector mesons are calculated from the modifications of the light quark condensates and the gluon condensates in the hadronic medium. The light quark condensates in the hadronic matter are obtained from the values of the non strange and strange scalar fields of the explicit chiral symmetry breaking term in a chiral SU(3) model. The in-medium gluon condensate is calculated through the medium modification of a scalar dilaton field, which is introduced into the chiral SU(3) model to simulate the scale symmetry breaking of QCD. The mass of the $ω$ meson is observed to have initially a drop with increase in density and then a rise due to the scattering with the baryons. The mass of the $ρ$ meson is seen to drop with density due to decrease of the light quark condensates in the medium. The effects of isospin asymmetry and strangeness of the medium on the masses of the vector mesons are also studied in the present work. The $ϕ$ meson is observed to have marginal drop in its mass in the nuclear medium. However, the strangeness of the medium is seen to lead to an appreciable increase in its mass arising due to scattering with the hyperons.

## Key claim

The masses of light vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ) in strange hadronic matter are calculated using QCD sum rules with in-medium quark and gluon condensates derived from a chiral SU(3) model; the ω meson mass initially drops with density but then rises due to baryon scattering, while the φ meson shows only a marginal drop in nuclear matter but an appreciable increase in strange matter due to hyperon scattering.

## Method

The in-medium light quark condensates (⟨ūu⟩, ⟨dd̄⟩, ⟨s̄s⟩) were obtained from the expectation values of the non-strange and strange scalar fields (σ, δ, ζ) of the explicit chiral symmetry breaking term in a chiral SU(3) model, solved in the mean field approximation. The in-medium gluon condensate was calculated from the medium modification of a scalar dilaton field χ introduced to simulate scale symmetry breaking via the QCD trace anomaly. These condensates were then used as inputs in finite energy QCD sum rules (FESR) to solve for the medium-dependent vector meson masses, continuum thresholds, and residue parameters, including baryon scattering contributions for ω, ρ, and φ mesons in hyperonic matter.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum value of (−m_q⟨q̄q⟩)^{1/4} for u,d quarks is 95.8 MeV; at nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ it drops to 85.7 MeV (about 36% drop in (−m_q⟨q̄q⟩)), and at 4ρ₀ it reaches (72 MeV)⁴ (about 69% drop).
- The vacuum value of (−m_s⟨s̄s⟩)^{1/4} is 258 MeV; at ρ₀ it changes to 252 MeV (about 9% drop in 8π²⟨m_s s̄s⟩), and at 4ρ₀ to 248.7 MeV (about 13.7% drop).
- The scalar gluon condensate ⟨(α_s/π) G^a_{μν} G^{a μν}⟩ has vacuum value (373 MeV)⁴; at ρ₀ it becomes (371.3 MeV)⁴ (about 1.8% drop), and at 4ρ₀ (361.9 MeV)⁴ (about 11.4% drop).
- The vacuum continuum threshold s₀^V is 1.3 GeV² for ω, 1.27 GeV² for ρ, and 1.6 GeV² for φ; the residue F_V is 0.242 GeV² for ω, 0.258 GeV² for ρ, and 0.55 GeV² for φ.
- The 4-quark condensate coefficient κ is 7.788 for ω, 7.236 for ρ, and −1.21 for φ.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to vector meson masses in dense matter (Hatsuda and Lee (1992), Klingl et al. (1997), Kwon et al. (2008)) by incorporating strange hadronic matter effects through a chiral SU(3) model previously used for nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and neutron stars (Papazoglou et al. (1999), Schramm et al. (1992)). The results enable predictions for low-mass dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions with strangeness-rich environments and complement studies of kaon and antikaon modifications in hyperonic matter (Mishra et al. (2009, 2010)).
