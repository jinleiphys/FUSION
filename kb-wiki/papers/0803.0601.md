# Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule and the Discrepancy between the New CLAS and SAPHIR Data

> Contribution of the K^+Λchannel to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule has been calculated by using the models that fit the recent SAPHIR or CLAS differential cross section data. It is shown that the two data sets yield quite different contributions. Contribution of this channel to the forward spin polarizability of the proton has been also calculated. It is also shown that the inclusion of the recent CLAS C_x and C_z data in the fitting data base does not significantly change the result of the present calculation. Results of the fit, however, reveal the role of the S_{11}(1650), P_{11}(1710), P_{13}(1720), and P_{13}(1900) resonances for the description of the C_x and C_z data. A brief discussion on the importance of these resonances is given. Measurements of the polarized total cross section σ_{TT'} by the CLAS, LEPS, and MAMI collaborations are expected to verify this finding.

## Key claim

The contribution of the \(\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda\) channel to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule depends strongly on which experimental data set (SAPHIR or CLAS) is used to fit the model, with SAPHIR-based fits yielding positive contributions near +1.3 \(\mu\)b and CLAS-based fits yielding negative contributions near -0.8 \(\mu\)b, a discrepancy that the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The author used a multipole model with tree-level background terms and Breit-Wigner resonance terms, fitting it separately to SAPHIR differential cross section data (Fit 1, 750 data points) and CLAS differential cross section data (Fit 2, 1377 data points), then to combinations including LEPS data (Fit 1a, Fit 2a). The models were subsequently refitted after including 320 CLAS beam-recoil polarization \(C_x\) and \(C_z\) data points (Fits 1x, 1ax, 2x, 2ax). The GDH integral \(I_{\rm GDH}\) and forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0\) were computed from the predicted spin-dependent cross section \(\sigma_{\rm TT'} = \frac{1}{2}(\sigma_{3/2} - \sigma_{1/2})\).

## Key numbers

- \(I_{\rm GDH}\) for the \(\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda\) channel: Fit 1 (SAPHIR only) = +1.309 \(\mu\)b; Fit 2 (CLAS only) = -0.845 \(\mu\)b; Fit 1a (SAPHIR+LEPS) = +1.274 \(\mu\)b; Fit 2a (CLAS+LEPS) = -0.333 \(\mu\)b.
- After including \(C_x\) and \(C_z\) data: Fit 1x = +1.140 \(\mu\)b; Fit 2x = -0.642 \(\mu\)b; Fit 1ax = +1.380 \(\mu\)b; Fit 2ax = -1.181 \(\mu\)b.
- Forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0\) (in \(10^{-7}\) fm\(^4\)): Fit 1 = 0.807; Fit 2 = -0.208; Fit 1a = 0.753; Fit 2a = 0.060; after \(C_x\)/\(C_z\) inclusion: Fit 1x = 0.752; Fit 2x = -0.033; Fit 1ax = 0.785; Fit 2ax = -0.466.
- The GDH sum rule for the proton gives \(I_{\rm GDH} = -204.5\) \(\mu\)b; Ref. kamalov00 estimated +4 \(\mu\)b from all kaon channels.
- The relative-global-scaling factor between SAPHIR and CLAS data was found to be 1.13 by Bydzovsky (2006).
- The CLAS \(C_x\)/\(C_z\) data yield \(R_\Lambda = 1.01 \pm 0.01\) when integrated over all angles and energies.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Mart (2006) and Bydzovsky (2006) on the discrepancy between SAPHIR (Glander 2003) and CLAS (Bradford 2005) differential cross section data for \(\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda\). It contradicts the expectation from Kamalov (2000) that kaon-hyperon final states contribute about +4 \(\mu\)b to the GDH sum rule, showing that CLAS-based fits give negative contributions. The inclusion of CLAS \(C_x\) and \(C_z\) data (Bradford 2006) corroborates the finding of Anisovich (2007) on the importance of the \(P_{13}(1900)\) resonance and also highlights the role of \(S_{11}(1650)\), \(P_{11}(1710)\), and \(P_{13}(1720)\). Future measurements of \(\sigma_{\rm TT'}\) by CLAS, LEPS, and MAMI are expected to resolve the discrepancy.
