# Connecting far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamics to resummed transport coefficients and attractors

> We investigate whether hydrodynamic attractors are present in simulations of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions. We argue that Lagrangian schemes to solve the relativistic viscous fluid equations can be particularly useful to characterize the properties of attractors in heavy ion collisions. Preliminary results are shown in Israel-Stewart theory to support such a claim. We also discuss how to perform the slow-roll expansion in Israel-Stewart theory undergoing a general flow, which naturally leads to the definition of a resummed shear viscosity coefficient that depends on the gradients of the hydrodynamic fields.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hydrodynamic attractors, universal far-from-equilibrium behavior of dissipative currents independent of initial conditions, can persist in realistic, inhomogeneous heavy-ion collision simulations, not only in highly symmetric flows like Bjorken flow. The claim is hedged: the results are described as "preliminary" and the authors state that "a detailed analysis still needs to be performed."

## Method

The authors solved conformal Israel-Stewart (IS) theory at zero chemical potential using a Lagrangian Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) algorithm, assuming longitudinal boost invariance and radial symmetry in the transverse plane. Initial conditions corresponded to central smooth Glauber Au+Au collisions with a maximum central temperature of 400 MeV. They tracked the shear stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) for individual fluid elements and compared its evolution from different initial conditions to identify attractor behavior.

## Key numbers

- Maximum central temperature: 400 MeV.
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s = 0.1\) (left panel) and \(\eta/s = 0.2\) (right panel) were used.
- The evolution was followed for approximately 10 fm.
- The resummed shear viscosity coefficient \(\eta^R(K_N) = \eta \, \mathcal{S}(K_N)\) is defined with \(\mathcal{S}(K_N) = \frac{15}{16K_N^2}\left(\sqrt{1+\frac{32}{15}K_N^2}-1\right)\), where \(K_N = \tau_\pi \sqrt{\sigma_{\mu\nu}\sigma^{\mu\nu}}\); \(\eta^R \leq \eta\) for all gradients and \(\eta^R \sim 1/K_N\) when \(K_N \gg 1\).

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors, previously established in Bjorken flow by Heller and Spalinski (2015) and analytically characterized by Denicol and Noronha (2017, 2019), to the inhomogeneous, expanding fireball of heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the numerical investigation of attractors in spatially inhomogeneous systems by Romatschke (2017). The paper introduces a slow-roll expansion for general flow in Israel-Stewart theory, which yields a resummed shear viscosity coefficient that depends on the local Knudsen number, enabling a systematic far-from-equilibrium description beyond the Navier-Stokes regime.
