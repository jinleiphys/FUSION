# In-medium properties of the low-lying strange, charm, and bottom baryons in the quark-meson coupling model

> In-medium properties of the low-lying strange, charm, and bottom baryons in symmetric nuclear matter are studied in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. Results for the Lorentz-scalar effective masses, mean field potentials felt by the light quarks in the baryons, in-medium bag radii, and the lowest mode bag eigenvalues are presented for those calculated using the updated data. This study completes the in-medium properties of the low-lying baryons in symmetric nuclear matter in the QMC model, for the strange, charm and bottom baryons which contain one or two strange, one charm or one bottom quarks, as well as at least one light quark. Highlight is the prediction of the bottom baryon Lorentz-scalar effective masses, namely, the Lorentz-scalar effective mass of $Œ£_b$ becomes smaller than that of $Œû_b$ at moderate nuclear matter density, $m^*_{Œ£_b} < m^*_{Œû_b}$, although in vacuum $m_{Œ£_b} > m_{Œû_b}$. We study further the effects of the repulsive Lorentz-vector potentials on the excitation (total) energies of these bottom baryons.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model predicts that the Lorentz-scalar effective mass of the bottom baryon Œ£_b becomes smaller than that of Œû_b at moderate nuclear matter density (m*_Œ£b < m*_Œûb), even though in vacuum the opposite ordering holds (m_Œ£b > m_Œûb). This prediction is conditional on the specific treatment of repulsive vector potentials, with the paper studying two possibilities: one including effective Pauli potentials extracted from Œõ and Œ£ hypernuclear data, and one without.

## Method

The author uses the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which the Lorentz-scalar-isoscalar œÉ, Lorentz-vector-isoscalar œâ, and Lorentz-vector-isovector œÅ mean fields couple directly only to the light u and d quarks in baryons, not to strange, charm, or bottom quarks. The MIT bag model describes the baryons, and the quark-meson coupling constants are fixed by nuclear matter saturation properties (binding energy 15.7 MeV at œÅ‚ÇÄ = 0.15 fm‚Åª¬≥). The self-consistent equations for the mean fields are solved in symmetric nuclear matter within the Hartree approximation, and the baryon effective masses, bag radii, and lowest mode bag eigenvalues are computed as functions of nuclear density.

## Context

This work completes the study of in-medium properties for all low-lying baryons (strange, charm, and bottom) in symmetric nuclear matter within the QMC model, extending earlier studies by Saito (2005) and Krein (2017). The QMC model, originally invented by Guichon (1987), has been successfully applied to finite (hyper)nuclei and hadron properties in medium; this paper provides the first explicit predictions for bottom baryon effective masses in nuclear matter. The results enable future studies of heavy baryon production in nuclear reactions and provide input for understanding partial restoration of chiral symmetry in dense matter, as the light-quark condensates decrease faster with density than those of heavier quarks.
