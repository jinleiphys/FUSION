# High-p_t in heavy ion collisions: an abridged theoretical overview

> This overview focusses on recent developments, in the most part triggered by LHC data, aimed at the development of a reliable and complete theoretical description of high-p$_t$ physics in heavy ion collisions. Particular emphasis is placed on the understanding of the underlying in-medium dynamics as a prior to the use of high-p$_t$ observables as detailed probes of the QCD matter created in the collisions.

# High-p_t in heavy ion collisions: an abridged theoretical overview

## Key claim
A complete theoretical description of high-p_t physics in heavy ion collisions requires moving beyond pre-LHC energy-loss models for leading partons toward a full in-medium parton shower formalism, but this goal remains incomplete because current approaches still lack rigorous field-theoretical treatment of parton-medium interactions and have not yet addressed all phenomenologically relevant antenna configurations.

## Method
The paper reviews recent theoretical developments in jet quenching triggered by LHC data, focusing on medium-induced parton energy loss calculations based on the transport parameter $\hat{q} = \mu^2/\lambda$ (where $\mu^2$ is the squared average momentum transfer per scattering and $\lambda$ the parton mean free path). It examines multiple gluon emission through antenna interference patterns, mass effects for heavy quarks via the dead cone effect, jet observables including dijet asymmetry and fragmentation functions, and colour flow modifications between propagating partons and the medium using leading-order opacity calculations.

## Key numbers
- LHC centre-of-mass energy: 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair, compared to 200 GeV at RHIC
- Characteristic gluon energy scale $\omega_c$ sets the radiated energy distribution, with average energy loss $\Delta E \sim \alpha_s \hat{q} L^2$
- For jet collimation, soft fragments with energy $\omega \leq \sqrt{\hat{q} L}$ become completely decorrelated from the initial jet direction
- Dijet asymmetry analysis performed for leading jet transverse momentum $p_{T,leading} > 100$ GeV and associated jet momentum $p_{T,associated} > 40$ GeV for lead-lead collisions with centrality class 0-30%
- Colour flow calculation performed to leading order in opacity $\zeta \equiv L^+/\lambda^+_{\rm el}$ (in-medium path length $L^+$ and elastic mean free path $\lambda^+_{\rm el}$)

## Context
This overview extends pre-LHC jet quenching theory (reviewed by Wiedemann (2009), d'Enterria (2009), Casalderrey-Solana (2007), Majumder (2010), Jacobs (2004), Gyulassy (2003), and systematically compared by Armesto (2011)) by addressing challenges raised by LHC measurements from ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE. It enables the development of Monte Carlo implementations of in-medium parton showers and first-principles simulations of QCD matter, with ongoing work by Mehtar-Tani (2010-2011) and Casalderrey-Solana (2011) on antenna interference patterns, and by Beraudo (2011) on colour flow effects.
