# Neutrino-nucleus interactions as a probe to constrain double-beta decay predictions

> We propose to use charged-current neutrino-nucleus interactions as a probe of the many virtual transitions involved in neutrinoless double-beta decay. By performing neutrino and anti-neutrino interaction studies on the initial and final nucleus respectively, one can get information on the two branches involved in the double-beta decay process. The measurement of such reactions could help to further constrain double-beta decay predictions on the half-lives.

## Key claim

Charged-current neutrino-nucleus interactions can serve as a supplementary probe to constrain theoretical predictions of neutrinoless double-beta decay half-lives, because the forbidden transitions that contribute to the decay are the same as those involved in neutrino-nucleus interactions, and their contribution to the cross section increases with neutrino energy.

## Method

The author uses the proton-neutron Quasi-Particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with Skyrme forces (SGII and SIII) to calculate charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for the specific case of \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ti. The contribution of states of different multipolarity (Fermi, Gamow-Teller, spin-dipole, and higher multipoles) to the total cross section is computed at neutrino energies of 30 and 60 MeV to show how the relative importance of forbidden transitions changes with energy.

## Key numbers

- For \(\nu_e + ^{48}\)Ca at \(E_{\nu}=30\) MeV, the total cross section is mainly dominated by allowed Fermi and Gamow-Teller states, though other multipoles can give a significant contribution.
- For \(\nu_e + ^{48}\)Ca at \(E_{\nu}=60\) MeV, the contribution of spin-dipole states (\(J^{\pi}=0^-,1^-,2^-\)) and higher multipoles (mainly \(J^{\pi}=2^+\) and \(3^+\)) becomes as important as or dominates the total cross section.
- The typical momentum in neutrinoless double-beta decay from a massive Majorana neutrino exchange is expected to be of the order of 100 MeV, based on the uncertainty principle.

## Context

This work extends the set of probes used to constrain double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, which previously included charge-exchange reactions (probing mainly allowed Gamow-Teller transitions), beta-decay (probing only low-energy states), and muon capture (probing only one branch of the decay). The proposal specifically addresses the need to constrain the forbidden transitions that contribute to neutrinoless double-beta decay, as highlighted by calculations for \(^{48}\)Ca and \(^{76}\)Ge in prior work by various authors. The approach could be enabled by future low-energy neutrino facilities such as the SNS source or a low-energy beta-beam facility.
