# Multi-peak structure of meson spectral function in magnetic field

> We investigate the spectral functions of neutral and charged mesons in a hot dense medium under a external magnetic field using the two-flavor quark-meson model within the functional renormalization group (FRG) framework. Our results show that the spectral functions of σ and π0 mesons develop new structures due to decay channels into quarks occupying different Landau levels. By consistently incorporating the momentum relations at vertices for charged particles in a magnetic field, we further show that the π+ spectral function develops a multi-peak structure at finite temperatures, resulting from the various annihilation and decay channels available to π+ in the magnetic environment. This multi-peak structure is further enhanced in a finite-density medium, causing the π+ meson to become a broad resonance at lower temperatures and densities compared to neutral mesons. Such a multi-peak pattern is expected to be universal for charged mesons under magnetic fields and carries significant implications for understanding transport properties in magnetized strongly interacting fluids

## Key claim

The spectral function of the charged pion (\(\pi^+\)) develops a multi-peak structure at finite temperature and density under an external magnetic field, originating from the various annihilation and decay channels available to \(\pi^+\) that involve quarks occupying different Landau levels; this multi-peak pattern is expected to be universal for charged mesons in magnetic fields, though the authors note that within the Local Potential Approximation (LPA) used, the results provide a qualitative rather than quantitative description of the spectral structure.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor quark-meson model within the functional renormalization group (FRG) framework, using an anisotropic \(p_3\)-regulator suited for finite temperature and magnetic field backgrounds. They derived flow equations for the effective potential and meson two-point functions, incorporating Landau-level representations for charged pion and quark propagators, and evaluated loops in coordinate space to correctly handle the modified momentum relations at vertices for charged particles in a magnetic field. The flow equations were solved using both Taylor expansion and grid methods, with analytic continuation to Minkowski space performed to extract the meson spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- For \(eB = 10\,m_\pi^2\) at zero chemical potential, the pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral crossover is \(T_c = 196\) MeV.
- Critical endpoint (CEP) locations: \((T,\mu) = (25,274)\,\text{MeV}\) for \(eB = 10\,m_\pi^2\); \((56,272)\,\text{MeV}\) for \(eB = 20\,m_\pi^2\); \((79,278)\,\text{MeV}\) for \(eB = 30\,m_\pi^2\).
- Vacuum observables used to fix parameters: \(m_q = 300\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 137\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- The transverse momentum integration was performed up to \(p_T^{\max} = 5\) GeV, and Landau-level summation truncated at an energy cutoff of 5 GeV.
- In the analytic continuation, a small imaginary part \(\epsilon = 1\) MeV was introduced and kept fixed throughout.

## Context

This work extends studies of meson properties in magnetic fields using continuum non-perturbative approaches such as Dyson–Schwinger equations (DSE) and functional renormalization group (FRG), as previously applied by Fischer (2011), Mueller (2014), Gao (2021), Fu (2020), and Braun (2009). The results are consistent with the magnetic catalysis effect reported by Kamikado (2013), showing increased chiral restoration temperature with growing magnetic field strength. The multi-peak structure identified for charged pions, arising from Landau-level-resolved decay and annihilation channels, provides a foundation for understanding transport properties in magnetized strongly interacting matter, such as that produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions or present in magnetars.
