# A light-fronts approach to electron-positron pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We perform a gauge-transformation on the time-dependent Dirac equation describing the evolution of an electron in a heavy-ion collision to remove the explicit dependence on the long-range part of the interaction. We solve, in an ultra-relativistic limit, the gauged-transformed Dirac equation using light-front variables and a light-fronts representation, obtaining non-perturbative results for the free pair-creation amplitudes in the collider frame. Our result reproduces the result of second-order perturbation theory in the small charge limit while non-perturbative effects arise for realistic charges of the ions.

## Key claim

The authors show that, after a gauge transformation removes the long-range Coulomb tails, the ultra-relativistic two-center Dirac equation reduces to a form where the interactions are confined to the light fronts, yielding non-perturbative pair-production amplitudes that reproduce second-order perturbation theory in the small-charge limit but exhibit non-perturbative effects for realistic ion charges.

## Method

The time-dependent Dirac equation for an electron in the field of two colliding heavy ions is first subjected to a gauge transformation that subtracts the long-range part of each ion's potential. In the ultra-relativistic limit (β→1, γ≫b,r⊥), the gauge-transformed interactions become delta-function singularities on the light fronts (t±z=0). The resulting "sharp" Dirac equation is solved piecewise in four space-time regions separated by the light fronts, using light-front variables and a light-fronts representation of the Dirac matrices; the discontinuity conditions across each light front give phase shifts that mix transverse momenta, and the transition amplitudes are obtained via Gauss' theorem applied to the conserved transition current.

## Key numbers

- The gauge-transformed interactions in the ultra-relativistic limit take the form (I₄∓α̂_z) Zα δ(t∓z) ln[(r⊥±b)²/b²] (Eqs. W_A, W_B).
- The discontinuity across the τ₊=0 light front is |G₊(τ₊=0⁺)⟩ = exp[-i Z_B α ln((r⊥+b)²/b²)] |G₊(τ₊=0⁻)⟩ (Eq. dGB); across τ₋=0, |G₋(τ₋=0⁺)⟩ = exp[-i Z_A α ln((r⊥-b)²/b²)] |G₋(τ₋=0⁻)⟩ (Eq. dGA).
- The transverse-momentum mixing kernel is Q_Z(κ,b) = (1/(2π)²) ∫ d r⊥ e^{i r⊥·κ} [(r⊥-b)²/b²]^{-iαZ} (Eq. F).
- In the limit Z→0, Q_Z(κ,b) → δ(κ), recovering continuity.

## Context

This work extends the classical-field approach of Baltz (1997) and the eikonal treatment of Jackiw (1992) by providing a non-perturbative solution for free pair production in the collider frame using light-front variables. It addresses the open question of whether non-perturbative effects will enhance or reduce cross sections at RHIC and LHC energies, where deviations from second-order perturbation theory (e.g., two-photon exchange diagrams of Bertulani and Baur, 1988) are expected. The method enables future calculations of free-pair amplitudes without relying on perturbation theory, though it does not treat bound-free production or the large-r⊥ region.
