# Examination of nucleon distribution with Bayesian imaging for isobar collisions

> Relativistic collision of isobaric systems is found to be valuable in differentiating the nucleon distributions for nuclei with the same mass number. In recent contrast experiment of $^{96}_{44}\text{Ru}+^{96}_{44}\text{Ru}$ versus $^{96}_{40}\text{Zr}+^{96}_{40}\text{Zr}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 200~\text{GeV}$, the ratios of multiplicity distribution, elliptic flow, triangular flow, and radial flow are precisely measured and found to be significantly different from unity, indicating the difference in the shapes of the isobar pair. In this work, we investigate the feasibility of nuclear structure reconstruction from heavy-ion collision observables. We perform Bayesian Inference with employing the Monte-Carlo Glauber model as an estimator of the mapping from nuclear structure to the final state observables and to provide the mock data for reconstruction. By varying combination of observables included in the mock data, we find it plausible to infer Woods--Saxon parameters from the observables. We also observe that single-system multiplicity distribution for the isobar system, rather than their ratio, is crucial to simultaneously determine the nuclear structure for the isobar system.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Bayesian inference can reconstruct Woods-Saxon nuclear structure parameters (radius, diffuseness, quadrupole and octupole deformations) from heavy-ion collision observables, but only when single-system multiplicity distributions are included alongside isobar ratios; the ratios alone are insufficient to simultaneously determine the structure of both isobar nuclei.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model to generate mock data for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV, computing charged multiplicity, elliptic flow, triangular flow, and radial flow estimators from the initial energy density via linear response approximations. They performed Bayesian inference with Gaussian process emulators (using fourth-order polynomial kernels) trained on 1001 design points in the four-dimensional Woods-Saxon parameter space, then applied Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling to reconstruct posterior distributions of the parameters from the mock observables.

## Key numbers

- Parameter ranges sampled: R ∈ [4.9, 5.2] fm, a ∈ [0.3, 0.6] fm, β₂ ∈ [0, 0.4], β₃ ∈ [0, 0.4]
- 10⁶ events per parameter set for training data; 10⁸ events per system for mock "experiment" data
- First four principal components explained 96% of variance in the 160-dimensional observable space; first ten PCs covered 98%
- Ground truth parameters used for mock data (from Jia (2021)): R_Ru = 5.09 fm, a_Ru = 0.46 fm, β₂_Ru = 0.162, β₃_Ru = 0; R_Zr = 5.02 fm, a_Zr = 0.52 fm, β₂_Zr = 0.06, β₃_Zr = 0.20
- Reconstructed mean values for Ru: R = 5.09 fm, a = 0.4609 fm, β₂ = 0.1599, β₃ = 0.02304; for Zr: R = 5.018 fm, a = 0.5217 fm, β₂ = 0.05786, β₃ = 0.1996

## Context

This work extends the approach of using isobar collision ratios proposed by Zhang (2021) and Jia (2021) to reduce medium-evolution uncertainties, moving from empirical parameter fitting to principled Bayesian inference. It builds on Bayesian methods previously applied to extract transport parameters (Bernhard 2016, JETSCAPE 2020) and the empirical linear response relation between observables and deformation parameters found by Jia (2021). The results enable future work incorporating full hydrodynamic or transport evolution models (such as AMPT or hydrodynamics) to extract nuclear structure from actual STAR isobar data, and suggest that single-system multiplicity distributions are essential for simultaneous reconstruction of both isobar nuclei.
