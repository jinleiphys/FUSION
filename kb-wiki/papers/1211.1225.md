# Pairing fluctuation effects in a strongly coupled color superfluid/superconductor

> We investigate the effects of pairing fluctuations in fermionic superfluids/superconductors where pairing occurs among three species (colors) of fermions. Such color superfluids/superconductors can be realized in three-component atomic Fermi gases and in dense quark matter. The superfluidity/superconductivity is characterized by a three-component order parameter which denotes the pairing among the three colors of fermions. Due to the SU$(3)$ symmetry of the Hamiltonian, one color does not participate pairing. This branch of fermionic excitation is gapless in the naive BCS mean-field description. In this paper, we adopt a pairing-fluctuation theory to investigate the pairing fluctuation effects on the unpaired color in strongly coupled atomic color superfluids and quark color superconductors. At low temperature, a large pairing gap of the paired colors suppresses the pairing fluctuation effects for the unpaired color, and the spectral density of the unpaired color shows a single Fermi-liquid peak, which indicates the naive mean-field picture remains valid. As the temperature is increased, the spectral density of the unpaired color generally exhibits a three-peak structure: the Fermi-liquid peak remains but get suppressed, and two pseudogap-like peaks appears. At and above the superfluid transition temperature, the Fermi-liquid peak disappears completely and the all three colors exhibits pseudogap-like spectral density. The coexistence of Fermi liquid and pseudogap behavior is generic for both atomic color superfluids and quark color superconductors.

## Key claim

The naive BCS mean-field description of color superfluids/superconductors predicts a gapless, free-fermion dispersion for the unpaired color, but this picture is only valid at low temperature where the large pairing gap of the paired colors suppresses pairing fluctuation effects; as temperature increases, the unpaired color develops a three-peak spectral structure combining a Fermi-liquid peak and two pseudogap-like peaks, and at or above the superfluid transition temperature the Fermi-liquid peak vanishes entirely.

## Method

The authors generalize the T-matrix (pairing fluctuation) theory, previously developed for two-component fermionic systems and for heated quark matter, to three-component (three-color) systems with SU(3) symmetry. They compute the fermion self-energy beyond BCS by constructing the particle-particle ladder (pair propagator) for the red-green, red-blue, and green-blue pairing channels, using the BCS mean-field propagator as input to preserve Goldstone's theorem, and then evaluate the spectral density of the unpaired blue fermions from the dressed Green's function.

## Key numbers

* For the resonant interaction case (infinite scattering length, \(1/a_s = 0\)), the order parameter \(\Delta\) in units of \(\mu\) as a function of \(T/\mu\) is solved from the BCS gap equation; the spectral density is computed for fermion momentum \(k = k_\mu = \sqrt{2m\mu}\).
* At very low temperature \(T \rightarrow 0\), the spectral density of the unpaired color shows a single sharp Fermi-liquid peak around \(\omega = 0\) with very small continuum spectral weight.
* For quark matter at moderate density, the ratio \(\kappa = T_c/E_F\) is approximately \(10^{-1}\), close to that for resonant superfluidity in strongly interacting atomic Fermi gases, compared to \(\kappa \sim 10^{-5}\) for ordinary BCS superconductors and \(\kappa \sim 10^{-2}\) for high-temperature superconductors.
* The pairing gap in dense quark matter at moderate density (\(\mu \sim 400\) MeV) was estimated to be about 2 orders of magnitude larger than the early prediction of \(\Delta \sim 1\) MeV from screened electric interactions.

## Context

This work extends the T-matrix pairing fluctuation theory, previously applied to two-component (spin-1/2) fermionic superfluids by Pieri and collaborators and to heated quark matter by Kitazawa, Koide, Kunihiro, and Nemoto (2005), to the superfluid/superconducting phase of three-component systems. It addresses the strongly coupled regime where the superconducting coherence length becomes comparable to the interparticle distance, a situation analogous to the BCS-BEC crossover in atomic Fermi gases studied experimentally by the groups of Jin, Ketterle, and others. The results enable future investigation of single-particle properties in three-component atomic Fermi gases and in two-flavor dense quark matter at chemical potentials around 400 MeV.
