# Improved Fixed Center Approximation of the Faddeev equations for the $\bar{K}NN$ system with S=0

> We extend the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev Equations for the $\bar{K} NN$ system with S=0, including the charge exchange mechanisms in the $\bar{K}$ rescattering which have been ignored in former works within the FCA. We obtain similar results to those found before, but the binding is reduced by 6 MeV. At the same time we also evaluate the explicit contribution the $πN Σ$ intermediate state in the three body system and find that it produces and additional small decrease in the binding of about 3 MeV. The system appears bound by about 35 MeV and the width omitting two body absorption, is about 50 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations for the $\bar{K}NN$ system with total spin S=0, including charge exchange mechanisms in $\bar{K}$ rescattering that were ignored in prior FCA works. The authors find that including these mechanisms reduces the binding by about 6 MeV compared to earlier results, and that explicitly including the $\pi N \Sigma$ intermediate state produces an additional small decrease in binding of about 3 MeV.

## Method

The authors derive a new FCA formula for the $\bar{K}NN$ system with S=0 by solving coupled equations for partition functions that describe $K^- p p \rightarrow K^- p p$ scattering, including charge exchange processes ($K^- p \rightarrow \bar{K}^0 n$). They express all elementary amplitudes in terms of isospin I=0 and I=1 $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes from chiral unitary theory, then isolate the total isospin I=1/2 amplitude. In a second step, they add the explicit contribution of the $\pi \Sigma N$ intermediate state in the three-body system, using the isospin combination $t_{\bar{K}N,\pi \Sigma} = \frac{1}{4}t^{I=1}_{\bar{K}N,\pi \Sigma} + \frac{3}{4}t^{I=0}_{\bar{K}N,\pi \Sigma}$ for S=0.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $\bar{K}NN$ system with S=0: about 35 MeV (final result including charge exchange and explicit $\pi \Sigma N$ channel, using reduced $NN$ radius from Dote (2008))
- Width of the $\bar{K}NN$ system with S=0 (omitting two-body absorption): about 50 MeV
- Charge exchange processes reduce the binding by 4 MeV (from 44 MeV to 38 MeV) when using the reduced $NN$ radius from Dote (2008)
- Explicit $\pi \Sigma N$ channel reduces the binding by an additional 1 MeV (from 38 MeV to 35 MeV) with the reduced $NN$ radius
- The peak of $|T|^2$ appears at about 3334 MeV total energy
- For comparison, the S=1 case from Trento (2011) gave 9 MeV binding with 30 MeV width, with a 12 MeV reduction from charge exchange

## Context

This work extends the FCA formalism for $\bar{K}NN$ systems, complementing the S=1 case studied by Kamalov (2001) and Trento (2011). It improves upon the earlier FCA work by Bayar (2011) which ignored charge exchange processes in multiple scattering. The results are compared to variational calculations by Dote (2008) and Dote (2008) which found about 20 MeV binding, and to Faddeev calculations by Ikeda (2007) and Shevchenko (2006) which found 50-70 MeV binding. The paper enables a simpler analytical approach to estimate binding and width of the $\bar{K}NN$ system, with results that qualitatively agree with more technically involved methods.
