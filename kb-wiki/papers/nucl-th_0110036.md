# The nuclear pions and quark distributions in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei

> We propose simple Monte Carlo method for calculating parton distribution in nuclei. The EMC effect is automatically included by means of two parameters, which characterize the change of nuclear pion field. Good agreement with experimental data in the broad range of variable x is obtained.

# The nuclear pions and quark distributions in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple Monte Carlo method for calculating parton distributions in nuclei that reproduces the EMC effect using only two physically motivated parameters, but the good agreement with experimental data is obtained specifically for iron-56 and only after adjusting the nucleon size in the medium and the effective number of nuclear pions contributing to the sea quark structure function.

## Method

The authors generate initial parton momenta in nucleons from spherically symmetric Gaussian distributions, with valence quark widths derived from the Heisenberg uncertainty relation and sea parton widths dictated by virtual pions. Events are accepted only if they satisfy exact kinematic constraints of deep inelastic scattering, and the nuclear parton density distributions are obtained by calculating the corresponding light-cone longitudinal momentum fractions (Bjorken x). The nuclear pion field is modified by two parameters: one controlling the width of the valence quark momentum distribution and one controlling the fraction of pions that contribute to the nuclear structure function at small x.

## Key numbers

- Valence quark primordial Gaussian distribution width: 0.172 GeV (in the nucleon rest frame)
- Sea parton primordial Gaussian distribution width: 0.052 GeV (derived from pion distribution in the nucleon)
- Up to 12% of pions are excluded from interaction at small x due to the shadowing mechanism
- The nucleon chemical potential is about 8 MeV smaller than the nucleon mass, which is too weak to reproduce the EMC minimum at x=0.7 without additional modifications
- Scalar and vector mean field values: U_V = 300 MeV, U_S = -400 MeV (at nuclear saturation density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3)
- The valence quark width is adjusted from 0.18 GeV to 0.172 GeV to produce the minimum at x ~ 0.7 and the maximum at x ~ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the approach of Rozynek and Wilk (2001) for calculating parton distributions using Gaussian momentum distributions with strict energy-momentum conservation, and addresses the EMC effect observed in deep inelastic scattering on nuclei. The method contrasts with the relativistic mean field approach of Walecka (1974) and the Dirac phenomenology applied to nuclear structure, which alone cannot reproduce the EMC minimum at x=0.7 without including higher-order nucleon-nucleon correlations as discussed by Benhar (1990s). The results enable a physically motivated description of nuclear parton distributions using only two parameters, with potential extension to a broad range of nuclear masses.
