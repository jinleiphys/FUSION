# Solution of the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations in the Cartesian deformed harmonic oscillator basis. (I) The method

> We describe a method of solving the nuclear Skyrme-Hartree-Fock problem by using a deformed Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis. The complete list of expressions required to calculate local densities, total energy, and self-consistent fields is presented, and an implementation of the self-consistent symmetries is discussed. Formulas to calculate matrix elements in the Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis are derived for the nuclear and Coulomb interactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete method for solving the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations using a deformed Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis, claiming that this approach combines the robustness of the basis expansion technique with the simplicity of the zero-range Skyrme interaction, but the method is primarily motivated by the need for rapid solutions for superdeformed or hyperdeformed rotating states where the deformation is known a priori.

## Method

The authors expand single-particle wave functions in a truncated Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis and iteratively diagonalize the mean-field Hamiltonian to obtain self-consistent solutions. Local densities (particle, kinetic, spin, spin-current, momentum, and vector kinetic densities) are computed from the wave functions and their derivatives at each spatial point, then used to construct the Skyrme energy density functional and the mean-field potentials. The direct Coulomb potential is calculated using matrix elements in the harmonic oscillator basis, while the exchange Coulomb energy is treated in the Slater approximation.

## Key numbers

- A typical basis of about 300 harmonic oscillator wave functions is sufficient for most applications.
- A typical spatial lattice of 12×12×12 points is used in the finite-difference approach for comparison.
- The total energy expression includes a center-of-mass correction factor of (1 − 1/A) where A is the number of nucleons.
- The Coulomb exchange energy is calculated using the Slater approximation: −(3e²/4)(3/π)^(1/3) ∫ d³r ρ_p^(4/3)(r).

## Context

This work extends the basis-expansion technique previously used for the finite-range Gogny interaction (Gogny, 1975; Girod and Grammaticos, 1983) and for relativistic mean field theory (Afanasjev et al., 1996) to the Skyrme interaction, which had previously been solved primarily using spatial coordinate methods such as finite-difference (Bonche et al., 1985), Fourier (Baye and Heenen, 1986), or spline-collocation (Umar et al., 1991; Chinn et al., 1995) approaches. The method enables rapid calculations for superdeformed and hyperdeformed rotating nuclear states and is implemented in the computer code HFODD, described in a companion paper.
