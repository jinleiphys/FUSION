# Renormalization of the gluon distribution function in the background field formalism

> We derive the Leading Order DGLAP evolution of gluon distribution function in the target light cone gauge starting from its standard operator definition. The derivation is performed using the background field formalism also employed in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory of small $x$ QCD. We adopt Mandelstam-Leibbrandt prescription to regulate in an unambiguous way the spurious singularity appearing in the light-cone gauge Feynman propagator. UV divergences are regulated via dimensional regularization. The methods introduced in this paper represent the first steps in the construction of a unified framework for QCD evolution, which could address collinear physics as well as small $x$ physics and gluon saturation.

## Key claim

The authors derive the leading-order DGLAP evolution of the gluon distribution function in the target light-cone gauge using the background field formalism, showing that no infrared divergences appear at any step due to the combined use of the background field method and the Mandelstam-Leibbrandt prescription for the light-cone gauge propagator singularity. This represents a first step toward a unified framework that could address both collinear physics and small-x physics with gluon saturation, though the derivation is currently restricted to the target light-cone gauge and the gluon distribution only.

## Method

The authors start from the standard operator definition of the gluon distribution function in the target light-cone gauge, where the gauge link reduces to the identity. They split the gluon field into a background field (encoding non-perturbative target modes) and a fluctuation field (representing perturbative harder modes), then expand the gluon distribution to one-loop order in renormalized perturbation theory. Ultraviolet divergences are regulated using dimensional regularization in D=4-2ϵ dimensions, and the spurious light-cone gauge singularity is treated with the Mandelstam-Leibbrandt prescription. The one-loop counterterms are determined by requiring cancellation of UV divergences, from which the splitting functions and DGLAP evolution are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Dimensional regularization is performed in D=4-2ϵ dimensions, with ϵ controlling the UV divergence.
- The gluon distribution g(x) has support only for 0≤x≤1, with g(x)=0 for x>1.
- The field renormalization constants are Z3=1+O(g²) for gluons and Z2=1+O(g²) for quarks.
- The renormalization matrix elements are Zgg=δ(z-1)+O(g²), Zgq=O(g²), and Zgq̄=O(g²) at leading order.

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Jalilian-Marian (2017, 2018, 2019) to go beyond the standard eikonal approximation in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, where scattering from both small-x and large-x gluon fields is considered. It differs from the earlier derivation of DGLAP evolution by Curci (1980) and Bassetto (1998) in the target light-cone gauge, which used on-shell partons and produced unphysical infrared divergences as artifacts; the background field method used here avoids those IR divergences entirely. The results enable future derivation of the quark distribution evolution using the same method, and ultimately the construction of a unified evolution framework that interpolates between DGLAP and JIMWLK equations in their respective limits.
