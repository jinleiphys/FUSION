# Roy-Steiner equations for $γγ\toππ$

> Starting from hyperbolic dispersion relations, we present a system of Roy--Steiner equations for pion Compton scattering that respects analyticity and unitarity requirements, gauge invariance, as well as crossing symmetry, and thus all symmetries of the underlying quantum field theory. To suppress the dependence on the high-energy region, we also consider once- and twice-subtracted versions of the equations, where the subtraction constants are identified with dipole and quadrupole pion polarizabilities. We consider the resolution of the $γγ\toππ$ partial waves by a Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation with finite matching point, and discuss the consequences for the two-photon coupling of the $σ$ resonance as well as its relation to pion polarizabilities.

## Key claim

The paper presents a system of Roy-Steiner equations for pion Compton scattering that respects all symmetries of quantum field theory, and uses this formalism to constrain the two-photon coupling of the sigma resonance, finding a correlation between the sigma's two-photon width and the I=0 pion polarizabilities that depends on the input for pi-pi phases and the subtraction scheme chosen.

## Method

The authors construct Roy-Steiner equations starting from hyperbolic dispersion relations, coupling the partial waves of gamma-gamma to pi-pi (h^I_{J,pm}(t)) to those of pion Compton scattering gamma-pi to gamma-pi (f^I_{J,pm}(s)). They consider once- and twice-subtracted versions where subtraction constants are identified with dipole and quadrupole pion polarizabilities, then solve the resulting Muskhelishvili-Omnes problem with a finite matching point at (0.98 GeV)^2, truncating the partial wave system at J=2 and approximating the high-energy input with a Breit-Wigner description of the f2(1270) resonance.

## Key numbers

- Sigma resonance pole parameters from pi-pi Roy equations: M_sigma = 441^{+16}_{-8} MeV, Gamma_sigma = 544^{+18}_{-25} MeV
- Charged-pion quadrupole polarizability from sum rule combined with ChPT: (alpha_2 - beta_2)^{pi^pm} = (15.3 +/- 3.7) x 10^{-4} fm^5
- Two-photon width of the sigma resonance from Roy-Steiner equations plus ChPT-plus-sum-rule input: Gamma_{sigma gamma gamma} = (1.7 +/- 0.4) keV
- Matching point for the Muskhelishvili-Omnes representation: t_m = (0.98 GeV)^2

## Context

This work extends the Roy-equation techniques previously applied to pi-pi scattering (ACGL, Madrid group) and pi-K scattering to the gamma-gamma to pi-pi reaction, providing an alternative channel to study the sigma resonance. The formalism enables a reliable analytic continuation to the sigma pole and establishes a correlation between Gamma_{sigma gamma gamma} and I=0 pion polarizabilities, building on earlier dispersive treatments by GM (2010) and using pi-pi phase input from two recent state-of-the-art analyses (Madrid group, Bern group).
