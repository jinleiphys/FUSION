# Unveiling the nuclear matter EoS from neutron star properties: a supervised machine learning approach

> We explore supervised machine learning methods in extracting the non-linear maps between neutron stars (NS) observables and the equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter. Using a Taylor expansion around saturation density, we have generated a set of model independent EoS describing stellar matter constrained by nuclear matter parameters that are thermodynamically consistent, causal, and consistent with astrophysical observations. From this set, the full non-linear dependencies of the NS tidal deformability and radius on the nuclear matter parameters were learned using two distinct machine learning methods. Due to the high accuracy of the learned non-linear maps, we were able to analyze the impact of each nuclear matter parameter on the NS observables, identify dependencies on the EoS properties beyond linear correlations and predict which stars allow us to draw strong constraints.

## Key claim

Supervised machine learning methods (Deep Neural Networks and Support Vector Machines Regression) can learn the non-linear maps between nuclear matter empirical parameters and neutron star observables (tidal deformability and radius) with high accuracy, but only for nucleonic equations of state described by a third-order Taylor expansion around saturation density and restricted to low-mass neutron star properties due to limitations of the parametrization at high density.

## Method

The authors generated 10^8 equations of state by sampling a 6-dimensional multivariate Gaussian distribution of nuclear matter empirical parameters (K_sat, Q_sat, E_sym, L_sym, K_sym, Q_sym) with diagonal covariance matrix, then applied physical constraints (thermodynamic stability, causality, maximum mass ≥ 1.97 M_sun, tidal deformability 70 < Λ_1.4M_sun < 580, positive symmetry energy) to obtain 13038 valid EoS. They trained Deep Neural Networks and Support Vector Machines Regression models on 80% of the dataset to predict neutron star radius and tidal deformability from the six empirical parameters, using the remaining 20% for testing.

## Key numbers

- Final dataset contained 13038 valid equations of state from 10^8 initial samples after applying physical constraints
- For a 1.4 M_sun neutron star, the Deep Neural Network predicted tidal deformability with root mean square error of 1.932 (0.46% relative error) and radius with root mean square error of 0.006 km (0.05% relative error)
- For a 1.4 M_sun neutron star, the Support Vector Machines Regression predicted tidal deformability with root mean square error of 2.236 (0.54% relative error) and radius with root mean square error of 0.010 km (0.08% relative error)
- The mean tidal deformability for 1.4 M_sun in the dataset was 415.723 with standard deviation 46.179
- The mean radius for 1.4 M_sun in the dataset was 12.055 km with standard deviation 0.194 km
- K_sat was the most important parameter: shuffling it increased prediction error by 14.102% for Λ_1.4M_sun and 1.866% for R_1.4M_sun

## Context

This work extends correlation analyses between nuclear matter parameters and neutron star properties that were previously limited to linear dependencies and showed considerable model dependence (Vidana 2009, Xu 2009, Ducoin 2010, Alam 2016, Malik 2018). The supervised machine learning approach builds on earlier applications of Deep Neural Networks to neutron star physics by Fujimoto (2017) for mapping mass-radius observations onto the equation of state plane, and on non-parametric equation of state representations by Landry (2018) and Essick (2019). The results enable systematic study of how each nuclear matter parameter independently affects neutron star observables beyond linear correlations, and identify which neutron star masses provide the strongest constraints on specific empirical parameters.
