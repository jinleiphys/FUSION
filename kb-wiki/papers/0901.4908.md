# Does interferometry probe thermalization?

> We carry out a systematic study of interferometry radii in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions within a two-dimensional transport model. We compute the transverse radii R_o and R_s as a function of p_t for various values of the Knudsen number, which measures the degree of thermalization in the system. They converge to the hydrodynamical limit much more slowly (by a factor 3) than elliptic flow. This solves most of the HBT puzzle for central collisions: R_o/R_s is in the range 1.1-1.2 for realistic values of the Knudsen number, much closer to experimental data ($\simeq 1$) than the value 1.5 from hydrodynamical calculations. The p_t dependence of R_o and R_s, which is usually said to reflect collective flow, also has a very limited sensitivity to the degree of thermalization. We then study the azimuthal oscillations of R_o, R_s, and R_{os} for non central collisions. Their amplitudes depend little on the Knudsen number, and reflect the eccentricity of the overlap area between the two nuclei.

## Key claim

Interferometry radii converge to the hydrodynamic limit much more slowly (by a factor of about 3) than elliptic flow, meaning that the HBT puzzle for central collisions is largely resolved by partial thermalization rather than requiring full local equilibrium.

## Method

The authors solved a two-dimensional relativistic Boltzmann equation for massless particles undergoing 2-to-2 elastic collisions, using a Monte-Carlo algorithm to track particle trajectories. They varied the Knudsen number (ratio of mean free path to system size) to control the degree of thermalization, and computed the transverse HBT radii R_o and R_s from the distribution of last-scattering points. The study used both thermal (Maxwell-Boltzmann) and Color Glass Condensate initial conditions, with results extrapolated to the limit of zero dilution parameter.

## Key numbers

- R_o/R_s is in the range 1.1-1.2 for realistic Knudsen numbers (K approximately 0.3), compared to the hydrodynamic limit value of 1.5 and experimental data near 1.0
- The fit parameter K_0 (Knudsen number at which R_o is halfway between free-streaming and hydro) is 0.167 +/- 0.007 for R_o and 0.215 +/- 0.006 for R_s, while K_0 for elliptic flow v_2 is 0.7
- In the hydrodynamic limit, R_o = 3.071 +/- 0.006 fm and R_s = 2.088 +/- 0.006 fm
- For non-central collisions with K=0.32, Delta R_o^2/Delta R_s^2 = 1.08 +/- 0.02 and Delta R_os/Delta R_o^2 = 0.97 +/- 0.03
- The product n_coll * K remains very close to 1.6 for all values of K

## Context

This work extends the transport model approach of Gombeaud (2007) and directly addresses the HBT puzzle, where ideal hydrodynamic models (Kolb 2003, Hirano 2002) overpredict R_o/R_s near 1.5 while experimental data from STAR (Adams 2004) show values near 1. The study shows that partial thermalization, which explains the centrality dependence of elliptic flow (Drescher 2007), also resolves most of the HBT puzzle for R_o/R_s. The results enable future work incorporating the QCD equation of state and longitudinal expansion to fully reproduce experimental HBT radii magnitudes.
