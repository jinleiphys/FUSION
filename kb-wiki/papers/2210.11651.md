# Neutron star mass formula with nuclear saturation parameters for asymmetric nuclear matter

> Low-mass neutron stars are directly associated with the nuclear saturation parameters because their central density is definitely low. We have already found a suitable combination of nuclear saturation parameters for expressing the neutron star mass and gravitational redshift, i.e., $η\equiv (K_0L^2)^{1/3}$ with the incompressibility for symmetric nuclear matter, $K_0$, and the density-dependent nuclear symmetry energy, $L$. In this study, we newly find another suitable combination given by $η_τ\equiv (-K_τL^5)^{1/6}$ with the isospin dependence of incompressibility for asymmetric nuclear matter, $K_τ$, and derive the empirical relations for the neutron star mass and gravitational redshift as a function of $η_τ$ and the normalized central number density. With these empirical relations, one can evaluate the mass and gravitational redshift of the neutron star, whose central number density is less than threefold the saturation density, within $\sim 10\%$ accuracy, and the radius within a few \% accuracies. In addition, we discuss the neutron star mass and radius constraints from the terrestrial experiments, using the empirical relations, together with those from the astronomical observations. Furthermore, we find a tight correlation between $η_τ$ and $η$. With this correlation, we derive the constraint on $K_τ$ as $-348\le K_τ\le -237$ MeV, assuming that $L=60\pm 20$ and $K_0=240\pm 20$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper derives new empirical relations for low-mass neutron star mass and gravitational redshift using a newly identified combination of nuclear saturation parameters, $\eta_\tau \equiv (-K_\tau L^5)^{1/6}$, which work for central densities up to three times the saturation density; the relations achieve approximately 10% accuracy for mass and gravitational redshift and a few percent accuracy for radius, but only for neutron stars whose central number density is less than threefold the saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed neutron star models by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation using a set of unified equations of state (EOSs), specifically the phenomenological Oyamatsu-Iida (OI-EOS) family and several Skyrme-type interactions (KDE0v, KDE0v1, SLy2, SLy4, SLy9, SKa, SkI3, SkMp). By trial and error, they identified $\eta_\tau$ as a suitable combination of the isospin dependence of incompressibility $K_\tau$ and the density-dependent symmetry energy $L$, then fitted polynomial expressions for the mass and gravitational redshift as functions of $\eta_\tau$ and the normalized central number density $n_c/n_0$, with coefficients themselves fitted as fourth-order polynomials in $n_c/n_0$.

## Key numbers

* The newly identified combination is $\eta_\tau \equiv (-K_\tau L^5)^{1/6}$, where $K_\tau$ is the isospin dependence of incompressibility and $L$ is the density-dependent nuclear symmetry energy.
* Using the correlation between $\eta$ and $\eta_\tau$, the authors derive a constraint on $K_\tau$ of $-348 \le K_\tau \le -237$ MeV, assuming $L = 60 \pm 20$ MeV and $K_0 = 240 \pm 20$ MeV.
* With the conservative ranges $L = 20-145$ MeV and $K_0 = 200-315$ MeV, the constraint on $K_\tau$ broadens to $-718 \le K_\tau \le -245$ MeV.
* The empirical relations evaluate neutron star mass and gravitational redshift within approximately 10% accuracy, and radius within a few percent accuracy, for central densities $n_c/n_0 \ge 1.7$.
* The fiducial value of $K_\tau$ adopted from the literature is $K_\tau = -550 \pm 100$ MeV, from analysis of ISGMR in Sn isotopes at RCNP (Li 2010).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous empirical relation using $\eta \equiv (K_0 L^2)^{1/3}$ (Sotani et al. 2014) to a new combination $\eta_\tau$ that incorporates the isospin dependence of incompressibility, enabling direct connection between terrestrial nuclear experiments and neutron star properties. The derived constraint on $K_\tau$ ($-348$ to $-237$ MeV) is significantly narrower than existing experimental constraints such as $K_\tau = -550 \pm 100$ MeV from Li (2010) or $K_\tau = -555 \pm 75$ MeV from Patel (2012), and the empirical relations allow future terrestrial constraints on $K_\tau$ and $L$ to be mapped onto neutron star mass-radius predictions for comparison with astronomical observations from NICER (Riley 2019, 2021; Miller 2019, 2021) and gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 (Annala 2018).
