# Towards a theoretical description of dense QCD

> The properties of matter at finite baryon densities play an important role for the astrophysics of compact stars as well as for heavy ion collisions or the description of nuclear matter. Because of the sign problem of the quark determinant, lattice QCD cannot be simulated by standard Monte Carlo at finite baryon densities. I review alternative attempts to treat dense QCD with an effective lattice theory derived by analytic strong coupling and hopping expansions, which close to the continuum is valid for heavy quarks only, but shows all qualitative features of nuclear physics emerging from QCD. In particular, the nuclear liquid gas transition and an equation of state for baryons can be calculated directly from QCD. A second effective theory based on strong coupling methods permits studies of the phase diagram in the chiral limit on coarse lattices.

# Towards a theoretical description of dense QCD

## Key claim

Standard lattice QCD cannot simulate finite baryon densities via Monte Carlo due to the sign problem of the quark determinant, but effective lattice theories derived from analytic strong coupling and hopping expansions can reproduce all qualitative features of nuclear physics emerging from QCD, though close to the continuum this approach is valid only for heavy quarks.

## Method

The author reviews two complementary effective lattice theory approaches. The first integrates over spatial link variables in Wilson's lattice QCD to produce a three-dimensional theory of Polyakov loops, expanded in combined strong coupling and hopping series ordered by powers of the lattice gauge coupling beta and the hopping parameter kappa. The second approach starts from the staggered lattice formulation, integrates out all link variables in the strong coupling limit to obtain a four-dimensional theory in terms of fermionic fields only, then integrates out quarks to express the partition function via integer variables amenable to worm algorithm simulations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature predictions from the 3d effective theory for SU(3) Yang-Mills match full lattice results within 10% after continuum extrapolation
- For N_f=1 at zero density and N_tau=4, the critical mass-over-temperature ratio M_c/T = 7.22(5) from the effective theory, compared to kappa_c = 0.0822(11) versus 0.0783(4) from full QCD simulations by Saito (2014)
- For N_f=2 at zero density and N_tau=4, M_c/T = 7.91(5) with kappa_c = 0.0691(9) versus 0.0685(3) from full QCD
- For N_f=3 at zero density and N_tau=4, M_c/T = 8.32(5) with kappa_c = 0.0625(9) versus 0.0595(3) from full QCD
- In the static strong coupling limit at zero temperature, the quark number density exhibits a discontinuity when the quark chemical potential equals the constituent mass m, jumping from 0 to 2N_c quarks per flavour per lattice site
- The cold and dense regime simulation used beta=5.7, kappa=0.0000887, N_tau=116 corresponding to meson mass m_M=20 GeV, temperature T=10 MeV, lattice spacing a=0.17 fm
- The effective theory for heavy quarks has been computed through order u^5 kappa^8 in the cold and dense regime

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work on effective lattice theories for QCD at finite density, including collaborations with Bergner, de Forcrand, Fromm, Glesaaen, Langelage, Neuman, Lottini, and Unger. The heavy-quark effective theory can be benchmarked against complex Langevin simulations by Sexty (2014) and the density of states method by Lang (2015), and tested against full two-colour QCD simulations by Scior (2015). The approach enables calculation of the nuclear liquid gas transition and equation of state for baryons directly from QCD, with the continuum-extrapolated pressure showing consistency with a polytropic equation of state for non-relativistic fermions. The second effective theory for coarse lattices extends earlier work by Wolff (1985) and Karsch (1988), and with leading gauge corrections to O(beta) evaluated by Unger (2015), permits studies of the chiral phase diagram on coarse lattices.
