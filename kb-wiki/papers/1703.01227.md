# Tree-level correlations in the strong field regime

> We consider the correlation function of an arbitrary number of local observables in quantum field theory. We show that, at tree level in the strong field regime, these correlations arise solely from fluctuations in the initial state. We obtain the general expression of these correlation functions in terms of the classical solution of the field equation of motion and its derivatives with respect to its initial conditions, that can be arranged graphically as the sum of labeled trees where the nodes are the individual observables, and the links are pairs of derivatives acting on them. For 3-point (and higher) correlation functions, there are additional tree-level terms beyond from the strong field approximation, generated throughout the evolution of the system.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at tree level in the strong field regime, correlation functions of an arbitrary number of local observables arise solely from fluctuations in the initial state, but for 3-point and higher correlation functions there are additional tree-level terms beyond the strong field approximation that are generated throughout the evolution of the system.

## Method

The author considers a real scalar field theory with a quartic self-interaction term and an external source, using the Schwinger-Keldysh (in-in) formalism expressed in the retarded-advanced basis. A generating functional is defined by coupling the observable to a fictitious source, and the fields are expanded in powers of this source to obtain the correlation functions in terms of the classical solution of the field equation of motion and its functional derivatives with respect to initial conditions.

## Key numbers

* For a theory with quartic interaction \(V(\phi)\sim g^2\phi^4\), the strong field regime occurs when \(\phi\sim Q/g\) where \(Q\) is a typical momentum scale, and the external source required to create such large fields scales as \(J\sim Q^3/g\)
* The order of magnitude of a connected graph \(\mathcal{G}\) with \(n_E\) external legs, \(n_L\) loops and \(n_J\) external sources is \(\mathcal{G}\sim g^{-2} g^{-n_E} g^{-2n_L} (gJ)^{n_J}\)
* To fully connect \(n\) observables, \(n-1\) propagators are necessary, suppressing the correlation by a factor \(g^{2(n-1)}\) with respect to the uncorrelated part

## Context

This work extends earlier results on 1-point and 2-point functions in the strong field regime from Gelis (2006, 2008) and applies techniques previously developed for heavy ion collisions and cosmology. The approach builds on the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism as developed by Schwinger (1961), Bakshi (1962, 1963), and Keldysh (1964), and relates to work on cosmological perturbations by Mukhanov (1990) and Weinberg (2005, 2008). The result enables calculation of higher-point correlation functions in systems with strong fields, such as those encountered in heavy ion collisions and early universe cosmology.
