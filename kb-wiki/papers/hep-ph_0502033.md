# Study the exotic $Θ^+$ in polarized photoproduction reactions

> We present an analysis of a Beam-Target double polarization asymmetry in $γn\to Θ^+ K^-$. We show that this quantity can serve as a filter for the determination of the $Θ^+$'s spin-parity assignment near threshold. It is highly selective between $1/2^+$ and $1/2^-$ configurations due to dynamical reasons.

## Key claim

The Beam-Target double polarization asymmetry $D_{xz}$ in $\gamma n\to \Theta^+ K^-$ can serve as a filter for determining the $\Theta^+$'s spin-parity assignment near threshold, with the asymmetry exhibiting a similar $\sin\theta_{c.m.}$ behavior but opposite sign for $1/2^+$ versus $1/2^-$ configurations due to dynamical reasons, though the prediction is robust only near threshold and may be affected by a possible spin-3/2 partner.

## Method

The author constructed effective Lagrangians for the $\Theta N K$ and $\Theta N K^*$ couplings for both $1/2^+$ and $1/2^-$ spin-parity assignments, with the coupling constant $g_{\Theta NK}$ determined by assuming $\Gamma_{\Theta^+\to NK}\simeq 10$ MeV. The transition amplitudes were expressed in terms of CGLN amplitudes, and the Beam-Target double polarization asymmetry $D_{xz}$ was derived for circularly polarized photons along the $z$-axis and transversely polarized neutrons along the $x$-axis in the reaction plane, with numerical calculations performed at center-of-mass energies $W=2.1$ GeV and $W=2.5$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The $\Theta^+$ mass is approximately 1.54 GeV, with a width less than 25 MeV.
- The coupling $g_{K^{*0}K^0\gamma}=1.13$ is determined by $\Gamma_{K^{*0}\to K^0\gamma}=117$ keV.
- The neutron anomalous magnetic moment $\kappa_n=-1.91$ drives the sign difference between $1/2^+$ and $1/2^-$ asymmetries near threshold.
- The overall center-of-mass energy near threshold is $W \sim 2.1$ GeV.
- The decay width $\Gamma_{\Theta^+\to NK}\simeq 10$ MeV is assumed, though a narrower width of 1 MeV is adopted in other analyses based on $N$-$K$ scattering data.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Zhao (2004) and Zhao and Al-Khalili (2004) for $\Theta^+$ photoproduction by focusing on polarization observables rather than cross sections, which are strongly model-dependent due to unknown form factors and the role of $K^*$ exchange. The analysis is motivated by experimental signals of the $\Theta^+$ reported by the LEPS, SAPHIR, and NA49 collaborations, and the theoretical predictions of the $\bar{\bf 10}$ pentaquark multiplet from the chiral soliton model of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and the quark model of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003). The results enable experimental facilities at Spring-8, JLab, ELSA, and ESRF to determine the $\Theta^+$ parity through measurement of the $D_{xz}$ asymmetry, though caution is noted regarding possible contributions from a spin-3/2 partner as studied by Dudek and Close (2004).
