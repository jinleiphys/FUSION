# How Disoriented Chiral Condensates Form: Quenching vs. Annealing

> We demonstrate that semiclassical fluctuations, their relaxation, and the chiral phase transition are automatically incorporated in the numerical simulations of the classical equations of motion in the linear $σ$-model when longitudinal and transverse expansions are included. We find that domains of disoriented chiral condensate with 4--5 fm in size can form through a quench while an annealing leads to domains of smaller sizes. We also demonstrate that quenching cannot be achieved by relaxing a chirally symmetric system through expansion.

# How Disoriented Chiral Condensates Form: Quenching vs. Annealing

## Key claim

Disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains of 4–5 fm in size can form through a quench, but only if the initial fluctuation is below the critical value and the mean fields start near the central lump of the effective potential; a quenching condition cannot be achieved by relaxing a chirally symmetric system through expansion, and annealing scenarios produce smaller domains.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of the classical equations of motion in the linear sigma-model, including both longitudinal expansion (via boost invariance) and transverse expansion (via cylindrical boundary conditions). They separated the fields into mean fields and semiclassical fluctuations, using an ensemble averaging technique to evolve the coupled system. Three initial conditions were compared: a quenching scenario with subcritical fluctuations and mean fields near zero, a cold annealing scenario with the system initially at the equilibrium point of a spontaneously broken effective potential, and a hot annealing scenario with fluctuations much larger than the critical value.

## Key numbers

- DCC domain sizes of 4–5 fm were observed in the quenching scenario.
- The linear sigma-model parameters used were lambda = 19.97, v = 87.4 MeV, and H = (119 MeV)^3, giving m_pi = 135 MeV and m_sigma = 600 MeV.
- The critical fluctuation for the chiral phase transition (neglecting explicit symmetry breaking) is delta^2_c = v^2/6.
- The initial correlation length was set to l_corr = 0.5 fm, with lattice spacing a = 0.25 fm and initial proper time tau_0 = 1 fm.
- The excited region radius was R_0 = 5 fm with a transient thickness Gamma = 0.5 fm.
- For the hot annealing case, the initial fluctuation was delta^2_0 = v^2/4 and sigma_0 = 20 MeV; for cold annealing, sigma_0 = 44 MeV.
- The decoupling of sigma and pion fluctuations occurred at approximately delta = 35 MeV, corresponding to the critical fluctuation value.
- At tau = 7 fm in the quenching case, the correlation length extended to r ~ 2.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the quenching proposal of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) by demonstrating that their numerical simulations already included semiclassical fluctuations that render the effective potential temperature-dependent, and by explicitly including longitudinal and transverse expansions which were absent in earlier simulations by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and by Gavin, Gocksch, and Pisarski (1994). The paper also contrasts quenching with annealing scenarios proposed by Gavin and Muller (1994) and Boyanovsky et al. (1994), showing that annealing leads to smaller DCC domains. The results enable future work on whether and how a quenching initial state can be realized in hadronic or nuclear collisions.
