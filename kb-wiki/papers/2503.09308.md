# Fourier shape parametrization in covariant density functional theory for nuclear fission

> We implement the Fourier shape parametrization within the point-coupling covariant density functional theory to construct the collective space, potential energy surface (PES), and mass tensor, which serve as inputs for the time-dependent generator coordinate method to simulate the fission dynamics. Taking \(^{226}\)Th as a benchmark, we demonstrate the superiority of Fourier shape parametrization over conventional spherical harmonic parametrization: it significantly enhances the convergence of higher-order collective shape parameters by efficiently characterizing extreme nuclear deformations. Consequently, the new framework generates more reasonable elongated configurations, particularly for the scission configurations, and significantly improves the description of charge distribution near the symmetric fission peak. Moreover, the Fourier shape parametrization provides a smooth and well-defined three-dimensional (3D) PES with minimal correlations between degrees of freedom, enabling high-precision 3D dynamical simulations of fission.

## Key claim

The Fourier shape parametrization, implemented within point-coupling covariant density functional theory, provides a more efficient and accurate description of extreme nuclear deformations along the fission path than the conventional spherical harmonic parametrization, specifically for the benchmark nucleus \(^{226}\)Th. The improvement is conditional on the Fourier series' rapid convergence, which allows the first three shape parameters to dominate the description of highly elongated configurations, whereas the spherical harmonic expansion shows no convergence up to the 7th order.

## Method

The authors derived Fourier shape operators and implemented the Fourier shape parametrization within point-coupling covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using the PC-PK1 energy density functional and a delta pairing force. They performed large-scale deformation-constrained CDFT calculations to generate potential energy surfaces, scission lines, and mass tensors in a three-dimensional collective space defined by the Fourier shape parameters \(q_2\) (elongation), \(q_3\) (mass asymmetry), and \(q_4\) (neck thickness). The fission dynamics were then simulated using the time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) in the Gaussian overlap approximation, solving the collective Schrödinger equation in the two-dimensional \((q_2, q_3)\) plane with the FELIX code package.

## Key numbers

* The Fourier shape parameters at the third fission barrier along the optimal asymmetric fission path of \(^{226}\)Th are \(a_2 = 0.4865\), \(a_3 = 0.1050\), \(a_4 = -0.2607\), \(a_5 = -0.0165\), \(a_6 = -0.0096\), and \(a_7 = -0.0131\), demonstrating that higher-order terms decrease by one to two orders of magnitude.
* In contrast, the spherical harmonic shape parameters at the same configuration are \(\beta_2 = 2.2329\), \(\beta_3 = -1.0275\), \(\beta_4 = 3.5623\), \(\beta_5 = -3.1828\), \(\beta_6 = 6.2295\), and \(\beta_7 = -7.2577\), showing no convergence and divergent behavior.
* The first (inner) fission barrier height for \(^{226}\)Th is 6.59 MeV with Fourier parametrization and 6.66 MeV with spherical harmonic parametrization; the second asymmetric barrier is 8.57 MeV (Fourier) versus 8.38 MeV (SH); the third asymmetric barrier is 7.20 MeV (Fourier) versus 7.10 MeV (SH).
* The scission configuration energies at the asymmetric fission valley minima are identical for both parametrizations at approximately \(-1726\) MeV, with heavy fragment charge \(Z_H \sim 56\).
* The TDGCM simulation used a time step of \(\delta t = 5 \times 10^{-4}\) zs, an absorption rate of \(r = 30 \times 10^{22}\) s\(^{-1}\), and an absorption band width of \(w = 3.0\).

## Context

This work extends the Fourier shape parametrization, previously used in macroscopic-microscopic approaches such as the Lublin-Strasbourg drop model by Schmitt (2017) and Pomorski (2023), into the fully microscopic covariant density functional theory framework. It directly addresses the poor convergence of spherical harmonic expansion in DFT-based fission studies noted by Schunck (2016, 2022), and improves upon prior CDFT+TDGCM calculations that used spherical harmonic parametrization, such as those by Regnier (2016) and Zhao (2019), which showed artifacts like plateaus and jumps in fragment charge numbers. The new framework enables high-precision three-dimensional dynamical simulations of fission, which the authors state is currently underway for \(^{236}\)U, overcoming limitations of the neck nucleon number \(q_N\) as a collective variable used in previous three-dimensional work by Zhou (2023).
