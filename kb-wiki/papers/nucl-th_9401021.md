# Nuclear Anapole Moments in Single Particle Approximation

> Nuclear anapole moments of $\;^{133}$Cs, $\;^{203,205}$Tl, $\;^{207}$Pb, $\;^{209}$Bi are treated in the single-particle approximation. Analytical results are obtained for the oscillator potential without spin-orbit interaction. Then the anapole moments are calculated numerically in a Woods-Saxon potential which includes spin-orbit interaction. The results obtained demonstrate a remarkable stability of nuclear anapole moment calculations in the single-particle approximation.

# Nuclear Anapole Moments in Single Particle Approximation

## Key claim

Nuclear anapole moment calculations in the single-particle approximation show remarkable stability (variation does not exceed 5%) across different choices of Woods-Saxon potential parameters, but the authors restrict to the single-particle approximation throughout and note that many-body effects, which are certainly important, will be considered in future publications.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear anapole moments for 133Cs, 203,205Tl, 207Pb, and 209Bi using the single-particle approximation with a valence nucleon above a spherically-symmetric core. They first obtain analytical results using an oscillator potential without spin-orbit interaction and a step-like core density profile, then perform numerical calculations using a Woods-Saxon potential that includes spin-orbit interaction, with nucleon wave functions and Green's functions obtained from solving the radial Schrodinger equation. The spin-orbit current contribution, omitted in prior work, is included, and the spin-orbit interaction itself is treated beyond perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- For 133Cs, the leading approximation gives κ = 0.33 at g_p = 4; numerical calculations with Woods-Saxon potential at the same g_p give κ = 0.25
- The experimental result for 133Cs is κ = 0.72(39)
- The effective PNC coupling constants used are: g_pp = g_nn = 1.5, g_pn = 6.5, g_np = -2.2, giving g_p = 4.5 for valence protons in Cs, Tl, Bi
- The spin-orbit interaction constants are: U^{pp}_{ls} = U^{nn}_{ls} = 36.6 MeV·fm^5, U^{pn}_{ls} = U^{np}_{ls} = 134.3 MeV·fm^5
- The nuclear density parameters used: R = 1.11 A^{1/3} fm, a = 0.54 fm, ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- The spin-orbit current contribution constitutes about 30% of the spin current contribution
- The correction to the spin current contribution from the oscillator model is about -4% in Cs and Bi, -7% in Tl

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of nuclear anapole moments by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1984) and the numerical calculations by those same authors using Woods-Saxon potentials, while addressing discrepancies with results from Bouchiat and Piketty (1991) for 209Bi and 203,205Tl. The paper provides the first consistent treatment of the spin-orbit current contribution and treats spin-orbit interaction beyond perturbation theory, unlike prior work. The results enable interpretation of ongoing experiments detecting nuclear-spin-dependent parity violation in cesium, thallium, lead, and bismuth, with the first evidence already seen in cesium by Wieman et al. (1993).
