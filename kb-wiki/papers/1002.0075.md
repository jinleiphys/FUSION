# Time Modulation of K-electron Capture Decay of Hydrogen-Like Ions with Multiphoton Resonance Transitions

> The multiphoton resonance transitions between ground hyperfine states are used for the time modulation of the electron capture decay of hydrogen like ions with the Gamow-Teller transition $1^+\to 0^+$. The proposed mechanism offers a time oscillating decay with the frequency up to 0.1 Hz. The experiment to observe the modulation is proposed for ions stored in a Penning trap. An attempt to understand the GSI anomaly with multiple photon transitions is made.

## Key claim

Multiphoton resonance transitions between ground hyperfine states of hydrogen-like ions with a Gamow-Teller transition \(1^+\to 0^+\) can produce time-modulated electron capture decay with oscillation frequencies up to 0.1 Hz, but the mechanism proposed to explain the GSI anomaly requires magnetic fields many times stronger than those present in the Experimental Storage Ring, casting doubt on the involvement of hyperfine structure in the observed oscillations.

## Method

The author derives the time evolution of a two-level system consisting of the hyperfine states \(F=1/2\) and \(F=3/2\) of a hydrogen-like ion in a Penning trap or storage ring, subject to a static magnetic field and a periodic driving field. The system is described by a Hamiltonian with diagonal and off-diagonal coupling terms, and the equations are solved in the quasienergy representation using Sambe's perturbation approach for weak driving fields. For multiphoton resonances where \(K\) photons are involved, the Rabi frequency and resonance width are expressed in terms of Bessel functions and their derivatives, and the probability of finding the ion in the decaying state is given by a damped oscillation formula.

## Key numbers

- The proposed mechanism offers a time oscillating decay with frequency up to 0.1 Hz.
- For the ion \(^{68}\text{Ga}^{30+}\) in a Penning trap with \(B_0 = 1.0\) T, the linearly polarized field strength required for a 1-photon resonance with half width \(\Gamma_K/2\pi = 0.1\) Hz is \(b_l = 1.54 \times 10^{-11}\) T; for a 5-photon resonance it is \(b_l = 9.07 \times 10^{-2}\) T.
- For the same ion and conditions, the circularly polarized field strength required for a 1-photon resonance with \(\Gamma_K/2\pi = 0.1\) Hz is \(b_c = 1.00 \times 10^{-12}\) T; for a 5-photon resonance it is \(b_c = 1.69 \times 10^{-2}\) T.
- For the ion \(^{140}\text{Pr}^{58+}\) or \(^{142}\text{Pm}^{60+}\) in the ESR, the half width for the \(n=999\) harmonic with \(K=1\) is \(\Gamma_K = 2420\) Hz with detuning \(\Delta = 0.989\) MHz; for the \(n=500\) harmonic with \(K=2\) it is \(\Gamma_K = 854\) Hz with detuning \(\Delta = -2.79\) MHz.
- The hyperfine splitting for \(^{68}\text{Ga}\) is \(\Delta E = 0.596\) meV, with a mean lifetime of the upper hyperfine state \(\tau = 7.95\) years and nuclear half-life \(T_{1/2} = 67.6\) minutes.
- The Zeeman splitting for \(^{140}\text{Pr}^{58+}\) in the ESR is \(\varpi/2\pi = 5.65\) GHz.

## Context

This work extends the Rabi resonance method, previously used in nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy, to the modulation of electron capture decay in hydrogen-like ions, proposing a Penning trap experiment for intermediate-mass ions such as \(^{68}\text{Ga}\). It attempts to explain the oscillatory modulation of EC decay observed at GSI for \(^{140}\text{Pr}^{58+}\) and \(^{142}\text{Pm}^{60+}\) by Litvinov et al. (2008), which had been attributed to neutrino mixing by Ivanov et al. (2008). The author finds that the periodic magnetic fields of the ESR can generate oscillations with the observed frequency, but the required field strength to explain the oscillation amplitude is many times larger than the ESR fields, suggesting that experiments with ions lacking hyperfine structure (e.g., \(^{123,5}\text{Cs}^{54+}\) or He-like ions) are needed to resolve competing hypotheses.
