# Elliptic Flow and Jet Quenching of a Parton System after Relativistic Heavy Ion Collision

> We obtain the initial phase space distribution after relativistic heavy ion collision by the CGC shattering method incorporating the uncertainty principle and solve the semi-classical Boltzmann equation which includes the gluon radiation processes. We present as a function of time the attenuation rate of high $p_T$ partons, which have transverse momenta over 6 $GeV/c$, in the medium which is formed after relativistic heavy ion collision. We calculate the elliptic flow as a function of an impact parameter, time and transverse momentum and also present the polar anisotropy, which gives the initial condition for color filamentation.

# Elliptic Flow and Jet Quenching of a Parton System after Relativistic Heavy Ion Collision

## Key claim
The elliptic flow observed at RHIC can be reproduced from kinetic theory (semi-classical Boltzmann equation with gluon radiation) without invoking hydrodynamics, but the system never achieves polar isotropy even at central rapidity, meaning the conditions for color filamentation are met while those for hydrodynamic applicability are not. The results are qualified by large fluctuations due to averaging over only 10 Monte Carlo sets and by acknowledged flaws including the neglect of formation time and the assumption that particles are born at the collision point.

## Method
The author uses a parton cascade code (PCC) solving the semi-classical Boltzmann equation with leading-order QCD cross sections for 2-to-2 processes and an approximate cross section for the 2-to-3 gluon radiation channel (gg → ggg). The initial phase-space distribution is obtained via the CGC shattering method of Krasnitz et al. (2003), with the saturation momentum set to Q_s = 1.7 GeV, and the transverse positions are sampled from the nuclear overlap function using an acceptance-rejection method. The simulation includes only partons with transverse momentum above 1 GeV/c from primary collisions, with a minimum momentum transfer of 500 MeV/c and a K-factor of 2 or 4 to account for higher-order diagrams.

## Key numbers
- Saturation momentum Q_s = 1.7 GeV (set for RHIC energy)
- Total number of initial partons for head-on (b=0 fm) collision: 5400
- Number of partons at b=1 fm: 5180; b=3 fm: 4150; b=5 fm: 2800; b=7 fm: 1570; b=9 fm: 630
- Total energy liberated from CGC shattering: 34.5 TeV out of approximately 40 TeV total available energy
- Average energy of partons in rapidity |y| < 1: 1.03 GeV; in 1 < |y| < 2: 2.14 GeV; in 2 < |y| < 3: 5.53 GeV
- Formation time for low-energy partons: 0.1–0.2 fm/c
- Number of jets (p_T > 6 GeV/c) for head-on collision: approximately 45 using CGC shattering, compared to about 10 using the factorization method
- Total cross section for gg → gg with the momentum cutoff: about 10/GeV^2 (approximately 4 mb)
- Coupling constant α_s = 0.3
- Minimum momentum transfer for collisions: 500 MeV/c
- Total attenuation rate of high p_T jets: estimated at 1/3 or 1/4

## Context
This work extends the parton cascade approach of Shin (2002, 2003) by incorporating the CGC shattering method of Krasnitz et al. (2003) for initial conditions, rather than the factorization method which gives only relatively high p_T partons and performs poorly at RHIC energy. The elliptic flow results are compared to the STAR experimental data (2005) and to hydrodynamic calculations, while the polar anisotropy analysis addresses the color filamentation mechanism proposed by Mrowczynski (1996) and the conditions for hydrodynamic applicability discussed by Arnold et al. (2004). The study enables future work on hadronization via recombination for low-energy partons and fragmentation for high-energy partons, as well as improved statistics with more Monte Carlo sets.
