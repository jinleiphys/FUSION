# General structure of the neutral $Žü$ meson self-energy and its spectral properties in a hot and dense magnetized medium

> The one loop self energy of the neutral $Žü$ meson is obtained for the effective $ŽüŽĆŽĆ$ and $ŽüNN$ interaction at finite temperature and density in the presence of a constant background magnetic field of arbitrary strength. In our approach, the eB-dependent vacuum part of the self energy is extracted by means of dimensional regularization where the ultraviolet divergences corresponding to the pure vacuum self energy manifest as the pole singularities of gamma as well as Hurwitz zeta functions. This improved regularization procedure consistently reproduces the expected results in the vanishing magnetic field limit and can be used quite generally in other self energy calculations dealing with arbitrary magnetic field strength. In presence of the external magnetic field, the general Lorentz structure for the in-medium vector boson self energy is derived which can also be implemented in case of the gauge bosons such as photons and gluons. It has been shown that with vanishing perpendicular momentum of the external particle, essentially two form factors are sufficient to describe the self energy completely. Consequently, two distinct modes are observed in the study of the effective mass, dispersion relations and the spectral function of $Žü^0$ where one of the modes possesses two fold degeneracy. For large baryonic chemical potential, it is observed that the critical magnetic field required to block the $Žü^0\rightarrowŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ decay channel increases significantly with temperature. However, in case of smaller values reaching down to vanishing chemical potential, the critical field follows the opposite trend.

## Key claim

The paper derives the one-loop self-energy of the neutral rho meson in a hot and dense magnetized medium using a dimensional regularization scheme that extracts the eB-dependent vacuum part as pole singularities of gamma and Hurwitz zeta functions, and shows that for vanishing perpendicular momentum of the external particle, two form factors are sufficient to describe the self energy completely, leading to two distinct modes in the effective mass and spectral function.

## Method

The one-loop self-energy of the neutral rho meson was calculated using effective rho-pi-pi and rho-NN interactions in the real-time formalism of finite-temperature field theory, with charged pion and proton propagators expressed as sums over Landau levels in a constant background magnetic field of arbitrary strength. Dimensional regularization was employed to separate the eB-dependent vacuum contributions from the pure vacuum divergences, and the general Lorentz structure of the in-medium vector boson self-energy was derived. For the numerical analysis, the perpendicular momentum of the external rho meson was set to zero, reducing the self-energy to two independent form factors.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_rho-pi-pi = 20.72 GeV^{-2}, g_rho-NN = 3.25, kappa_rho = 6.1, with nucleon mass m_N = 939 MeV.
- The critical magnetic field required to block the rho^0 -> pi+ pi- decay channel increases significantly with temperature for large baryonic chemical potential, but follows the opposite trend for smaller values reaching down to vanishing chemical potential.
- The effective masses of the charged pion and proton in the magnetized medium are given by m_l = sqrt(m_pi^2 + (2l+1)eB) and M_l = sqrt(m_N^2 + 2leB), where l is the Landau level index.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rho meson properties in magnetic fields at zero temperature (Ghosh 2016, Kawaguchi strong field) to finite temperature and density, and improves upon the authors' earlier work (Ghosh 2017) by using dimensional regularization and including the full Lorentz structure rather than spin-averaged thermal contributions. The results enable more realistic modeling of rho^0 -> pi+ pi- decay as a probe of magnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and in dense hadronic matter within compact stars, and the derived Lorentz structure can be applied to gauge bosons such as photons and gluons.
