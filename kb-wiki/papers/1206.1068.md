# Structures in high-energy fusion data

> Structures observed in heavy-ion fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier are interpreted as caused by the penetration of centrifugal barriers that are well-separated in energy. The structures are most pronounced in the fusion of lighter, symmetric systems, where the separation in energy between successive angular momentum barriers is relatively large. It is shown that the structures or peaks can be revealed by plotting the first derivative of the energy weighted cross section. It is also shown how an orbital angular momentum can be assign to the observed peaks by comparing to coupled-channels calculations. This is illustrated by analyzing high-energy fusion data for $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O and $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O, and the possibility of observing similar structures in the fusion of heavier systems is discussed.

## Key claim

Structures observed in heavy-ion fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier are caused by the penetration of centrifugal barriers that are well-separated in energy, but the extracted barriers are effective barriers influenced by coupled-channels effects rather than the real barriers of the centrifugal potential in the entrance channel.

## Method

The author applies the Hill-Wheeler barrier penetration formula to model fusion cross sections and shows that plotting the first derivative of the energy-weighted cross section reveals peaks corresponding to individual centrifugal barriers. Coupled-channels calculations in the rotating frame approximation with ingoing-wave boundary conditions, using an M3Y+repulsion potential and a short-ranged imaginary potential, are compared to experimental fusion data for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O, $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O, and $^{28}$Si+$^{28}$Si.

## Key numbers

- For $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O, the Hill-Wheeler parametrization uses $V_{CB}$ = 9.9 MeV, $\epsilon_0$ = 0.4 MeV, and $R_{CB}$ = 8.4 fm, providing a fair representation of data above 8 MeV.
- For $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, the Hill-Wheeler parametrization uses $V_{CB}$ = 6.23 MeV, $\epsilon_0$ = 0.4 MeV, and $R_{CB}$ = 7.667 fm.
- For $^{28}$Si+$^{28}$Si, the critical angular momentum for fusion from Vineyard et al. is about $L_c$ = 38$\hbar$, and the M3Y+repulsion potential uses a repulsion diffuseness $a_r$ = 0.378 fm.
- For $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O, coupled-channels calculations with 6 channels reproduce peak structures in the first derivative of the energy-weighted cross section, with the $L$=14 peak shifted by about 2 MeV compared to the no-coupling limit.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of oscillations in light-ion fusion data, previously associated with resonances, as arising from centrifugal barrier penetration, building on earlier suggestions by Vandenbosch and Esbensen (2008). The analysis of $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O fusion data from Tserruya et al. (1978) and Thomas et al. (1986) using coupled-channels calculations with a shallow M3Y+repulsion potential connects to the fusion hindrance phenomenon observed at deep subbarrier energies by Jiang et al. (2002) and others. The results enable future high-precision measurements with smaller energy steps for heavier systems such as $^{28}$Si+$^{28}$Si, where Gary and Volant (1982) observed modest oscillations, to better resolve individual centrifugal barriers and constrain the ion-ion potential.
