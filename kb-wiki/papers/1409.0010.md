# Extracting the shear viscosity of a high temperature hadron gas

> Quark-Gluon plasmas produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions quickly expand and cool, entering a phase consisting of multiple interacting hadronic resonances just below the QCD deconfinement temperature. The transport properties of this hot hadron gas are poorly understood, yet they play an important role in our ability to infer transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma, because experimental measurements integrate over the whole system evolution. Assuming that the hot hadron gas can be modeled by a hadron cascade code based on kinetic theory assuming binary interactions, the shear viscosity over entropy ratio of a hot hadron gas for temperatures in between 120 MeV and 170 MeV is extracted. Furthermore, we present estimates for a second order transport coefficient, the shear viscous relaxation time at a temperature of 165 MeV.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity over entropy ratio of a hot hadron gas can be extracted from a hadron cascade code based on kinetic theory assuming binary interactions, yielding a temperature-dependent value between 120 MeV and 170 MeV; the authors caution that the extraction assumes the cascade model provides a perfect description of the hot hadron gas, and that previous studies using the same type of code found inconsistent values.

## Method

The authors used the B3D hadron cascade code initialized over a large transverse area with particles in thermal and chemical equilibrium, then modified the initial momentum distribution to adjust the initial anisotropy of the stress-energy tensor. They simulated a one-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) expansion and extracted the shear stress component Phi from the stress-energy tensor at mid-rapidity as a function of proper time. Two methods were employed: Method I directly measured the estimator 3*Phi*tau/(4s) as a function of temperature, while Method II initialized the system with different values of Phi and found the value where the time derivative of Phi/(epsilon+P) vanishes, corresponding to the Navier-Stokes regime.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity over entropy ratio for temperatures between 120 MeV and 170 MeV: eta/s = 1.45 - 1.3 * (T / 170 MeV) +/- 0.02
- Shear viscous relaxation time at T = 165 MeV: tau_pi * T is consistent with a value of two
- Temperature range of the study: 120 MeV to 170 MeV
- QCD deconfinement temperature: T_c approximately 170 MeV
- Initial proper time for the cascade: tau_0 = 10 fm/c (with verification at tau_0 = 40 fm/c)
- Hadron resonance masses included in the simulation: up to 2.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior attempts to extract shear viscosity from hadron cascade codes, specifically addressing inconsistent results between Demir (2008) and Song (2010) who both used the URQMD code. The authors find much smaller eta/s values than Demir (2008) and somewhat larger values than Song (2010), attributing the latter discrepancy to sensitivity to transients when initializing at the switching temperature of 170 MeV. The methodology enables future precision studies of quark-gluon plasma properties by providing quantitatively reliable hadron gas transport coefficients, and the authors note that bulk viscosity extraction was not possible due to partial chemical equilibrium effects on the equation of state.
