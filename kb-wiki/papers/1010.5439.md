# Strong longitudinal color field effects in pp collisions at energies available at the Large Hadron Collider

> We study the effect of strong longitudinal color fields (SCF) in p+p reactions up to Large Hadron Collider energies in the framework of the HIJING/BBbar v2.0 model that combines (collinear factorized) pQCD multiple minijet production with soft longitudinal string excitation and hadronization. The default vacuum string tension, kappa0 = 1 GeV/fm, is replaced by an effective power law energy dependent string tension, that increases monotonically with center-of-mass energy. The exponent 0.06 is found sufficient to reproduce well the energy dependence of multiparticle observables in RHIC, Tevatron, as well as recent LHC data. This exponent is found to be only half of that predicted by the Color Glass Saturation model, lambda(CGC)=0.115, where gluon fusion multiparticle production mechanisms are assumed. In HIJING/BBbar v2.0, the rapid growth of central-rapidity density with energy is due to the interplay of copious minijet production and increasing SCF contributions. The large (strange)baryon-to-meson ratios measured at Tevatron energies are well described. A significant enhancement of these ratios is predicted up to the highest LHC energy (14 TeV). The effect of JJbar loops and SCF on baryon-anti-baryon asymmetry and its relation to baryon number transport is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy dependence of multiparticle observables in pp collisions from RHIC to LHC energies can be reproduced by replacing the vacuum string tension (1 GeV/fm) with an effective energy-dependent string tension that grows as (s/s0)^0.06, but this exponent is only half of the 0.115 predicted by the Color Glass Condensate model, indicating that both minijet production and strong color field contributions are needed to explain the rapid growth of central-rapidity density.

## Method

The authors modified the HIJING/BBbar v2.0 event generator, which combines collinear-factorized pQCD multiple minijet production with soft longitudinal string excitation and hadronization, by replacing the default vacuum string tension kappa0 = 1 GeV/fm with an energy-dependent string tension kappa(s) = kappa0 (s/s0)^0.06. They also implemented junction anti-junction (JJbar) loops as novel color flux topologies and enhanced the intrinsic diquark-quark transverse momentum kick by a factor of 3 for strings containing JJbar loops. The model was then compared to experimental data on charged particle pseudorapidity densities, transverse momentum spectra, and baryon-to-meson ratios across multiple energies.

## Key numbers

- The effective string tension increases from kappa = 1.40 GeV/fm at sqrt(s) = 0.017 TeV to kappa = 3.14 GeV/fm at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV, with kappa = 1.88 GeV/fm at RHIC energy sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV
- The exponent lambda = 0.06 in the string tension energy dependence is half of the CGC model prediction lambda_CGC = 0.115
- At 7 TeV, changing kappa(s) from 2.89 GeV/fm to 2.0 GeV/fm results in an 11% increased multiplicity
- At 7 TeV, changing the cut-off parameter p0 by +/- 0.5 GeV results in a change of only +/- 1.8% for the central pseudorapidity density
- The model predicts an increase of the (Lambda0 + anti-Lambda0)/KS0 ratio by a factor of approximately 10 at Tevatron energy (1.8 TeV) due to SCF effects
- The model underestimates kaon production by 15-25% at 1.8 TeV and by approximately a factor of two at 7 TeV
- For events with ten mini-jets at 1.8 TeV, the central charged particle pseudorapidity density could increase to approximately 20 and total multiplicity could exceed 150

## Context

This work extends the HIJING/BBbar v2.0 model previously applied to RHIC energies (Topor Pop et al., 2005, 2007) to LHC energies, and contrasts with the Color Glass Condensate model (McLerran, 2010) which predicts a stronger energy dependence of the saturation scale. The model provides a pp baseline for future extrapolations to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies, and addresses the failure of default HIJING to describe the dependence of mean transverse momentum on charged particle multiplicity noted by Wang (1991, 1992). The results show that PYTHIA (Sjostrand, 2007) and EPOS (Werner, 2009, 2010) cannot reproduce the observed high baryon-to-meson ratios at LHC energies.
