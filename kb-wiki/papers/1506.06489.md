# Mapping the Generator Coordinate Method to the Coupled Cluster Approach

> The generator coordinate method (GCM) casts the wavefunction as an integral over a weighted set of non-orthogonal single determinantal states. In principle this representation can be used like the configuration interaction (CI) or shell model to systematically improve the approximate wavefunction towards an exact solution. In practice applications have generally been limited to systems with less than three degrees of freedom. This bottleneck is directly linked to the exponential computational expense associated with the numerical projection of broken symmetry Hartree-Fock (HF) or Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wavefunctions and to the use of a variational rather than a bi-variational expression for the energy. We circumvent these issues by choosing a hole-particle representation for the generator and applying algebraic symmetry projection, via the use of tensor operators and the invariant mean (operator average). The resulting GCM formulation can be mapped directly to the coupled cluster (CC) approach, leading to a significantly more efficient approach than the conventional GCM route.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the exponential computational bottleneck of the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) can be circumvented by choosing a hole-particle representation for the generator and applying algebraic symmetry projection via tensor operators and the invariant mean, which allows a direct mapping to the Coupled Cluster (CC) approach; this mapping is claimed to be significantly more efficient than the conventional GCM route, but the paper presents only the formal mapping and does not provide an explicit solution procedure or numerical demonstration.

## Method

The author reformulates the GCM wavefunction, which is an integral over a weighted set of non-orthogonal Slater determinants parameterized by generator coordinates, by restricting the generators to a hole-particle form relative to a reference determinant. Algebraic symmetry projection is then performed using the invariant mean (operator average) over the symmetry group, which replaces the computationally expensive numerical projection of broken-symmetry states. The resulting symmetry-adapted operators are expanded order-by-order and compared term-by-term with the coupled cluster exponential expansion, establishing that the GCM weights can be interpreted as a pseudo-distribution whose moments (cumulants) correspond to the coupled cluster amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The conventional GCM discretization scales exponentially as \(K = N^D = 10^D\) for \(N \approx 10\) points per dimension and \(D\) generator coordinates, becoming intractable for \(D > 3\).
* The proposed GCM-to-CC mapping, when symmetry breaking is not present, yields a computational scaling of \(D^6\) once intermediates have been calculated.
* Applications of conventional GCM have generally been limited to systems with less than three degrees of freedom (\(D \leq 3\)).

## Context

This work extends the Generator Coordinate Method, originally developed by Hill and Wheeler (1953) and Griffin and Wheeler (1957) for nuclear collective motion, and addresses the scaling bottleneck that has limited GCM applications for sixty years. The mapping to Coupled Cluster theory, originating with Cizek (1966, 1969), provides an alternative route to solve the GCM wavefunction via the CC equations. The author's previous work (Stuber 2009) on analytical symmetry projection for broken-symmetry wavefunctions is the direct precursor. The formalism enables systematic improvement beyond projected Hartree-Fock and projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wavefunctions toward exact solutions, and provides a framework to discuss sparsity structure of cluster amplitudes and to address deficiencies of conventional CC truncation schemes such as CCSD.
