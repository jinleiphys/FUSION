# Challenges in Solving Chiral Hydrodynamics

> We prove that ideal chiral hydrodynamics, as derived from chiral kinetic theory, is acausal and its initial-value problem is ill-posed both in the linearized case around a local equilibrium solution and also in the full nonlinear regime. Therefore, such theory cannot be used to determine how the chiral anomaly affects the hydrodynamic evolution. We show that these fundamental issues can be fixed by using different definitions (frames) for the hydrodynamic fields. This leads to a causal theory of ideal chiral hydrodynamics where the vorticity strength is constrained by the coefficient that encodes the anomaly.

## Key claim

The paper proves that ideal chiral hydrodynamics derived from chiral kinetic theory is acausal and its initial-value problem is ill-posed, both in the linearized and fully nonlinear regimes; however, these issues can be fixed by redefining the hydrodynamic fields (changing to the Landau frame), which yields a causal theory where the vorticity strength is bounded by the anomaly coefficient.

## Method

The authors analyze the initial-value problem of the system of partial differential equations (PDEs) describing ideal chiral hydrodynamics, using standard techniques from PDE theory including the characteristic determinant. They first study the linearized regime around a general solution of conventional ideal hydrodynamics, then extend to the full nonlinear regime, computing the characteristic determinant to test for well-posedness and causality. They then repeat this analysis after performing a frame transformation to the Landau hydrodynamic frame, deriving conditions for causality and local well-posedness in Gevrey spaces.

## Key numbers

* The characteristic determinant for ideal chiral hydrodynamics in the original frame vanishes identically, implying the initial-value problem is ill-posed for any hypersurface.
* In the Landau frame with vanishing vector chemical potential, the condition for causality is \(|\xi^{(0)}_{AL,n_A}|\, \omega^{(0)}_L\le 1\), where \(\omega^{(0)}_L=\sqrt{\omega_L^{(0)\mu}\omega^{(0)}_{L\mu}}\) is the magnitude of the background vorticity.
* In the Landau frame with both vector and axial currents present, the causality conditions are \((\xi^{(0)}_{AL,n_A}-\xi^{(0)}_{VL,n_V})^2+4\xi^{(0)}_{VL,n_V}\xi^{(0)}_{VL,n_A}\ge0\) and \(|\alpha^{(0)}_\pm|\omega^{(0)}_L\le1\), where \(\alpha^{(0)}_\pm\) are defined in terms of the derivatives of the transport coefficients.
* For the specific case of massless fermions, the causality condition becomes \(6\omega^{(0)}_L[5(\mu_A^{(0)})^3 + \pi^2 \mu_A^{(0)} (T^{(0)})^2]/[15(\mu^{(0)}_A)^4+6\pi^2 (\mu^{(0)}_A)^2 (T^{(0)})^2 + 7\pi^4 (T^{(0)})^4]\leq 1\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of causality and well-posedness in relativistic hydrodynamics, previously studied for ideal fluids by Choquet-Bruhat and for first-order viscous theories by Bemfica, Disconzi, and Noronha (2020). The paper directly addresses the chiral hydrodynamic equations derived from chiral kinetic theory by Chen et al. (2015), showing that the original formulation is unusable for numerical simulations of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable causal simulations of chiral hydrodynamics in the Landau frame, with the anomaly coefficient constraining the maximum allowed vorticity.
