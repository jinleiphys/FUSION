# On a Missed Mechanism of Dielectron Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions

> We examine a new mechanism of $e^+e^-$ pair production in $NN$ collisions associated with the $NN$-decoupled dibaryon $d^\star_1$(1956) formation in the process $NN \to γ^\star d^\star_1$, where $γ^\star$ is the virtual photon which converts into a $e^+e^-$ pair. It is shown that a substantial excess of dielectron yields from $Ca+Ca$ and $C+C$ collisions at 1 GeV/A in the dielectron mass spectra in the region from 0.2 to 0.5 GeV/$c^2$ measured by the \textit{DLS Collaboration} as compared with calculated ones can be attributed to the contribution of this mechanism. A simple means for verification of the existence of such a mechanism is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the long-standing "DLS puzzle", an excess of dielectron yields in the mass region 0.2 to 0.5 GeV/c² from Ca+Ca and C+C collisions at 1 GeV/A compared to theoretical predictions, can be explained by a previously overlooked mechanism: virtual photon emission during the formation of the NN-decoupled dibaryon resonance d*₁(1956) in the process NN → γ* d*₁, where the virtual photon converts into an e⁺e⁻ pair.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section dσ/dM for dielectron production via the dibaryon mechanism NN → e⁺e⁻ d*₁ using a matrix element for a magnetic dipole (M1) transition from an initial pp state to a final d*₁ state with assumed spin zero and isospin two. The calculation employed a vector dominance model form factor and Monte Carlo integration over phase space limited by the DLS apparatus acceptance, with the total cross section normalized to the experimentally measured total cross section of the process pp → γ d*₁ at 1.36 GeV from CELSIUS-WASA data. For C+C collisions, the quasi-free NN collision approximation was used with a nucleon momentum distribution for ¹²C.

## Key numbers

- The dibaryon resonance d*₁ has a mass of 1956 MeV; its decay into two nucleons and a real photon has a kinematic limit for the virtual photon mass of M_max = M_R - M_NN ∼ 0.08 GeV/c².
- For pp collisions at a kinetic energy of 1.04 GeV, the upper limit for the dielectron mass from the proposed mechanism is M_max ≃ 0.38 GeV.
- The total cross section for pp → γ d*₁ at a kinetic energy of 1.36 GeV was determined from CELSIUS-WASA data to be σ_γ^tot(s₀) = 11 μb, with the background cross section in the mass region of the observed structure estimated at 1 μb.
- The DLS spectrometer had a finite mass resolution of σ_M/M = 0.1.

## Context

This work addresses the "DLS puzzle," a discrepancy between measured dielectron spectra from Ca+Ca and C+C collisions at 1 GeV/A by the DLS Collaboration (DLS, 2000s) and theoretical models that included only conventional processes such as Dalitz decays of mesons (π⁰, η, ω) and baryon resonances (Δ(1232), N(1520)), direct decays of vector mesons (ρ⁰, ω), and NN virtual photon bremsstrahlung. The puzzle was later confirmed by the HADES Collaboration (2008). The proposed mechanism extends earlier work on the d*₁(1956) dibaryon, which had been shown to manifest in photon production processes (Khrykin, 2002; 2007), and offers a simple experimental verification via missing mass spectroscopy in the reaction pp → e⁺e⁻ X, where a narrow peak at around 1956 MeV would confirm the mechanism.
