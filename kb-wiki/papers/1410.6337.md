# Nucleon self-energies for supernova equations of state

> Nucleon self-energies and interaction potentials in supernova (SN) matter, which are known to have an important effect on nucleosynthesis conditions in SN ejecta are investigated. Corresponding weak charged-current interaction rates with unbound nucleons that are consistent with existing SN equations of state (EOSs) are specified. The nucleon self-energies are made available online as electronic tables. The discussion is mostly restricted to relativistic mean-field models. In the first part of the article, the generic properties of this class of models at finite temperature and asymmetry are studied. It is found that the quadratic expansion of the EOS in terms of asymmetry works reasonably well at finite temperatures and deviations originate mostly from the kinetic part. The interaction part of the symmetry energy is found to be almost temperature independent. At low densities, the account of realistic nucleon masses requires the introduction of a linear term in the expansion. Finally, it is shown that the important neutron-to-proton potential difference is given approximately by the asymmetry of the system and the interaction part of the zero-temperature symmetry energy. The results of different interactions are then compared with constraints from nuclear experiments and thereby the possible range of the potential difference is limited. In the second part, for a certain class of SN EOS models, the formation of nuclei is considered. Only moderate modifications are found for the self-energies of unbound nucleons that enter the weak charged-current interaction rates. This is because in the present approach the binding energies of bound states do not contribute to the single-particle energies of unbound nucleons.

## Key claim

The neutron-to-proton potential difference ΔU in supernova matter is approximately given by the asymmetry of the system multiplied by the interaction part of the zero-temperature symmetry energy, with the quadratic expansion of the equation of state in terms of asymmetry working reasonably well at finite temperatures and deviations originating mostly from the kinetic part.

## Method

The author investigates nucleon self-energies and interaction potentials in supernova matter using relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, deriving the connection between the neutron-proton potential difference ΔU and the interaction part of the symmetry energy. The study examines eight different RMF models at finite temperature and asymmetry, comparing their predictions with experimental constraints, and then considers how the formation of nuclei modifies the self-energies of unbound nucleons that enter weak charged-current interaction rates.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton mass difference used is Q = m_n - m_p ≈ 1.29 MeV, which is the threshold for achieving neutron-rich conditions in the neutrino-driven wind
- The neutron mass is set to m_n = 939.565346 MeV and the proton mass to m_p = 938.272013 MeV, taken from Mohr (2008)
- The potential difference ΔU is shown to be approximately ΔΣ_V = 4(1-2Y_p) E^int_sym, where Y_p is the proton fraction and E^int_sym is the interaction part of the symmetry energy
- For the DD2 model, the vector self-energies have a strictly linear dependence on asymmetry and are temperature independent, with no notable differences between the exact calculation and the linear approximation
- For the SFHo model, deviations from linearity in ΔU are visible at n_B = n_B^0 and n_B = 5n_B^0, but the linear approximation still works reasonably well
- The temperature dependence of the interaction symmetry energy E^int_sym is so small that no differences are visible between using E^int_sym(T,n_B) and E^int,0_sym(n_B) in the approximations

## Context

This work extends the studies of Martinez (2012) and Roberts (2012), who showed that nucleon interaction potentials modify neutrino spectra in core-collapse supernovae and influence deleptonization of proto-neutron stars, but did not provide details on the connection between ΔU and the symmetry energy. The paper provides the formal derivation linking ΔU to the interaction part of the symmetry energy, enabling consistent inclusion of nucleon self-energies in weak interaction rates for supernova equations of state. The results are compared with constraints from nuclear experiments to limit the possible range of ΔU values, and the author provides online tables of nucleon self-energies for use in future simulations.
