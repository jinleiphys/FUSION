# The Three-Body Continuum Coulomb Problem and the 3$α$ Structure of $^{12}$C

> We introduce an approach, based on the coordinate space Faddeev equations, to solve the quantum mechanical three-body Coulomb problem in the continuum. We apply the approach to compute measured properties of the first two $0^+$ levels in $^{12}$C, including the width of the excited level, in a 3$α$-particle model. We use the two-body interaction which reproduces the low energy $α$-$α$ scattering data and add a three-body force to account for the compound state admixture.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method to solve the three-body Coulomb problem in the continuum using coordinate space Faddeev equations with adiabatic hyperspherical expansion, and applies it to compute the width of the second 0⁺ state in ¹²C within a 3α model, finding a width of 20 eV that agrees with experiment only after including a phenomenological three-body force to adjust the resonance energy to the correct position.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations in coordinate space using the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method, where the hyperradius ρ and five generalized angles serve as coordinates. For fixed ρ, they solve the eigenvalue equation for the angular part of the Faddeev operator, then expand the total three-body wave function in terms of these eigenfunctions. The resulting coupled hyperradial equations are solved with complex-energy boundary conditions to find the resonance energy and width, using the Ali-Bodmer potential version "a" for the α-α interaction plus a Gaussian three-body force whose strength and range are adjusted to reproduce the 0⁺₂ level at 0.38 MeV and the ground state energy near −7.27 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The 0⁺₂ level in ¹²C is located at 0.38 MeV above the three-α threshold.
- The ground state energy is −7.27 MeV (observed value).
- The width of the 0⁺₂ state computed from the complex-energy solution is Γ = 20 eV, with an uncertainty of about 10 eV from the asymptotic extrapolation.
- The quasiclassical approximation gives a width of about 100 eV, an order of magnitude larger than the experimental value.
- The ground state maximum probability configuration is an equal-side triangle with side length 2.98 fm.
- The excited state maximum probability configuration is an elongated triangle with side lengths 3.9, 4.2, and 6.1 fm.
- The root mean square radius of the α-particle used is R_α = 1.47 fm.
- The hyperradius definition uses A_α = m_α / m = 3.97.
- The Ali-Bodmer potential parameters: repulsive terms 125 MeV (l=0) and 20 MeV (l=2) with range 1.53 fm, attractive term −30.18 MeV with range 2.85 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous three-body calculations of ¹²C structure (Vislay 1971, Vallieres 1976, Marsh 1982) by providing the first consistent treatment of the continuum Coulomb problem for the 0⁺₂ state, which lies above the three-α threshold. The approach generalizes the adiabatic hyperspherical method used in atomic physics (Christensen-Dalsgaard 1984) into the Faddeev equations framework, and improves upon earlier two-body ⁸Be+α approximations (Mohr) by allowing direct decay into the three-body continuum. The method enables future studies of other three-body Coulomb problems in nuclear, atomic, and molecular physics where none of the two-body subsystems are bound.
