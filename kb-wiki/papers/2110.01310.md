# Investigating the low moments of the nucleon structure functions in lattice QCD

> We highlight QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration's recent developments on computing the Compton amplitude directly via an implementation of the second order Feynman-Hellmann theorem. As an application, we compute the nucleon Compton tensor across a range of photon virtuality at an unphysical quark mass. This enables us to study the $Q^2$ dependence of the low moments of the nucleon structure functions in a lattice calculation for the first time. We present some selected results for the moments of the $F_1$, $F_2$ and $F_L$ structure functions and discuss their implications.

## Key claim

The QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration demonstrates that the forward Compton amplitude can be computed directly on the lattice via a second-order Feynman-Hellmann method, enabling the first lattice QCD extraction of the low moments of the nucleon structure functions \(F_1\), \(F_2\), and \(F_L\) as a function of photon virtuality \(Q^2\), albeit at an unphysical quark mass corresponding to \(m_\pi \approx 420\)–\(470\) MeV.

## Method

The authors modify the fermion action with a perturbing term that couples an external electromagnetic current to the quarks at momentum \(\mathbf{q}\), then compute the second-order energy shift of the nucleon two-point correlation function with respect to the coupling strength \(\lambda\). By matching this shift to the Compton amplitude via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, they extract the Compton structure functions \(\mathcal{F}_1\) and \(\mathcal{F}_2\) for several values of nucleon momentum \(\mathbf{p}\) and current momentum \(\mathbf{q}\). The longitudinal structure function \(\mathcal{F}_L\) is constructed from \(\mathcal{F}_1\) and \(\mathcal{F}_2\), and a Bayesian simultaneous fit of the dispersion-relation expansions (truncated at \(n=4\)) yields the first few Mellin moments of \(F_1\), \(F_2\), and \(F_L\) at each \(Q^2\).

## Key numbers

- Simulations use two ensembles: \(32^3 \times 64\) with \(\beta = 5.50\) (\(a = 0.074\) fm, \(a^{-1} = 2.667\) GeV) and \(48^3 \times 96\) with \(\beta = 5.65\) (\(a = 0.068\) fm, \(a^{-1} = 2.902\) GeV).
- Pion masses are \(m_\pi \approx 470\) MeV (smaller volume) and \(m_\pi \approx 420\) MeV (larger volume), set at the \(SU(3)\) symmetric point.
- Photon virtualities are accessed in the range \(1.5 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 7\) GeV\(^2\).
- The perturbing coupling strength is \(|\lambda| \leq 0.025\); the second-order energy shift is extracted from polynomial fits of the form \(\Delta E^e_{N_\lambda}(\mathbf{p}) = \frac{\lambda^2}{2} \left. \frac{\partial^2 E_{N_\lambda}(\mathbf{p})}{\partial \lambda^2} \right|_{\lambda = 0} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^4)\).
- Moments are sampled from uniform distributions with bounds \(M_2(Q^2) \in [0,1]\) and \(M_{2n}(Q^2) \in [0, M_{2n-2}(Q^2)]\) for \(n > 1\).
- At \(Q^2 \lesssim 4\) GeV\(^2\), moments of \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) differ significantly; for \(Q^2 > 4\) GeV\(^2\), they approach similar asymptotic values, and the moments of \(F_L\) become nearly vanishing, indicating recovery of the Callan-Gross relation.

## Context

This work extends the QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration’s earlier Feynman-Hellmann approach to hadron structure (Chambers 2017, Bouchard 2016) and their previous application to the \(F_1\) structure function (PhysRevLett.118.242001, PhysRevD.102.114505). It provides a complementary method to quasi- and pseudo-PDF approaches (reviewed by Lin 2017, Cichy 2018) for accessing nucleon structure functions from lattice QCD. The results enable future studies of the \(Q^2\) evolution of structure function moments at physical quark masses and comparisons with experimental Nachtmann moments, such as those from E06-009 (2018) at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).
