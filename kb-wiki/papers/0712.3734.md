# Thermalization of a color glass condensate and review of the "Bottom-Up" scenario

> Thermalization of a longitudinally expanding color glass condensate with Bjorken boost invariant geometry is investigated within microscopical parton cascade BAMPS. Our main focus lies on the detailed comparison of thermalization, observed in BAMPS with that suggested in the \BUp scenario. We demonstrate that the tremendous production of soft gluons via $gg \to ggg$, which is shown in the \BUp picture as the dominant process during the early preequilibration, will not occur in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, because the back reaction $ggg\to gg$ hinders the absolute particle multiplication. Moreover, different from the \BUp scenario, soft and hard gluons thermalize at the same time. The time scale of thermal equilibration obtained from BAMPS calculations is of order $\as^{-2} (\ln \as)^{-2} Q_s^{-1}$. After this time the gluon system exhibits nearly hydrodynamical behavior. The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio has weak dependence on $Q_s$ and lies close to the lower bound from the AdS/CFT conjecture.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the "Bottom-Up" thermalization scenario, which predicts a strong parametric enhancement of soft gluon production via $gg \to ggg$ bremsstrahlung during the early preequilibration stage, does not occur in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies because the back reaction $ggg\to gg$ hinders absolute particle multiplication. Contrary to the Bottom-Up scenario, soft and hard gluons thermalize at the same time rather than sequentially.

## Method
The authors used the microscopical parton cascade BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings) to solve the Boltzmann equations for on-shell gluons using Monte Carlo techniques, implementing both elastic $gg\to gg$ scatterings and inelastic $gg\leftrightarrow ggg$ bremsstrahlung processes with consistent back reactions. The simulations employed a Bjorken boost-invariant geometry with a cylindrical tube boundary condition to mimic one-dimensional longitudinal expansion, using color glass condensate initial conditions with saturation scales $Q_s$ of 2, 3, and 4 GeV and coupling constants $\alpha_s$ of 0.1, 0.2, and 0.3.

## Key numbers
- Thermalization times extracted from simulations: $t_{\rm th}(Q_s=2\ {\rm GeV})=1.2\ {\rm fm/c}$, $t_{\rm th}(Q_s=3\ {\rm GeV})=0.75\ {\rm fm/c}$, $t_{\rm th}(Q_s=4\ {\rm GeV})=0.55\ {\rm fm/c}$ for $\alpha_s=0.3$
- Thermalization time scaling: $t_{\rm th}\alpha_s^{2}(\ln \alpha_s)^2=0.09\ {\rm fm/c}$ for $\alpha_s=0.1$, $0.1\ {\rm fm/c}$ for $\alpha_s=0.2$, $0.1\ {\rm fm/c}$ for $\alpha_s=0.3$ (all with $Q_s=3$ GeV)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: $\eta/s \approx 0.15$ for $\alpha_s=0.3$ with $Q_s=2$, 3, and 4 GeV
- Final gluon multiplicities: $dN/d\eta \approx 700-1700$ for $Q_s=2-3$ GeV, equivalent to $dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta \approx 470-1100$ for charged mesons
- Momentum isotropization relaxation times: $\theta_{\rm rel}=0.85$, $0.52$, and $0.42$ fm/c for $Q_s=2$, 3, and 4 GeV respectively (with $\alpha_s=0.3$)
- Effective temperature at $t=0.5$ fm/c: $T\simeq 0.67$ GeV for $\alpha_s=0.3$ and $Q_s=3$ GeV

## Context
This work extends the study of thermalization of a color glass condensate (CGC) initially investigated by Bjoraker and Venugopalan (2001) using the Landau transport equation and by Serreau and Schiff (2001) using relaxation time approximation, who found that elastic $gg\to gg$ collisions alone are insufficient for thermalization. The paper directly challenges the "Bottom-Up" scenario proposed by Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001), which predicted a parametric thermalization time of $\tau_{\rm th} \sim \alpha_s^{-13/5} Q_s^{-1}$ and sequential thermalization of soft then hard gluons. The results enable more accurate modeling of the pre-equilibrium stage in heavy ion collisions and support the use of ideal hydrodynamics with small viscosity at RHIC and LHC energies.
