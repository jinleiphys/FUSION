# Stringent Constraint on Galactic Positron Production

> The intense 0.511 MeV gamma-ray line emission from the Galactic Center observed by INTEGRAL requires a large annihilation rate of nonrelativistic positrons. If these positrons are injected at even mildly relativistic energies, higher-energy gamma rays will also be produced. We calculate the gamma-ray spectrum due to inflight annihilation and compare to the observed diffuse Galactic gamma-ray data. Even in a simplified but conservative treatment, we find that the positron injection energies must be $\lesssim 3$ MeV, which strongly constrains models for Galactic positron production.

## Key claim

The positron injection energies in the Galactic Center must be less than or approximately 3 MeV, which is a much stronger constraint than the previous limit of less than or approximately 20 MeV from internal bremsstrahlung alone, and this limit applies to any positron production mechanism.

## Method

The authors calculated the gamma-ray spectrum from inflight annihilation of relativistic positrons with electrons in the interstellar medium, using the survival probability of positrons as they lose energy through ionization. They normalized the predicted inflight annihilation flux to the observed 0.511 MeV line flux and angular distribution from INTEGRAL, then compared the combined spectrum to the diffuse Galactic gamma-ray data from COMPTEL and INTEGRAL in a 5-degree diameter region at the Galactic Center.

## Key numbers

- The 0.511 MeV gamma-ray line flux from the Galactic Center measured by INTEGRAL is (1.07 plus or minus 0.03) times 10 to the minus 3 photons per square centimeter per second
- The angular distribution of the 0.511 MeV emission is consistent with a two-dimensional Gaussian of approximately 8 degrees full width at half maximum
- For injection energies of 10, 3, and 1 MeV, the survival probability differs from unity by approximately 11, 5.5, and 1.4 percent respectively
- The positronium fraction in the Galaxy is 0.967 plus or minus 0.022
- The diffuse gamma-ray background is modeled as 0.013 times (E per MeV) to the minus 1.8 photons per square centimeter per second per steradian per MeV
- The COMPTEL measurement gives 0.0096 photons per square centimeter per second per steradian between 1 and 3 MeV in the inner Galactic Plane

## Context

This work extends the earlier constraint from internal bremsstrahlung by Beacom (2004) which found injection energies must be less than approximately 20 MeV, and improves upon earlier inflight annihilation constraints by Aharonian using older data. The new high-quality INTEGRAL data allow a model-independent test that strongly constrains both astrophysical models and exotic models involving new particle physics for positron production, and the authors note that INTEGRAL data already show a hint of excess in the 0.5 to 1.0 MeV range that could be the first detection of the inflight annihilation signal.
