# Impulse approximation in nuclear pion production reactions: absence of a one-body operator

> The impulse approximation of pion production reactions is studied by developing a relativistic formalism, consistent with that used to define the nucleon-nucleon potential. For plane wave initial states we find that the usual one-body (1B) expression O_1B is replaced by O_2B=-iK(m_pi/2)O_1B/m_pi, where K(m_pi/2) is the sum of all irreducible contributions to nucleon-nucleon scattering with energy transfer of m_pi/2. We show that O_2B is approximately O_1B for plane wave initial states. For distorted waves, we find that the usual operator is replaced with a sum of two-body operators that are well approximated by the operator O_2B. Our new formalism solves the (previously ignored) problem of energy transfer forbidding a one-body impulse operator. Using a purely one pion exchange deuteron, the net result is that the impulse amplitude for np --> d pi^0 at threshold is enhanced by a factor of approximately two. This amplitude is added to the larger "rescattering" amplitude and, although experimental data remain in disagreement, the theoretical prediction of the threshold cross section is brought closer to (and in agreement with) the data.

## Key claim

The traditional one-body impulse approximation for pion production is forbidden by energy-momentum conservation; the correct formalism replaces it with a two-body operator that includes non-static one-pion exchange, and this new operator is approximately equal to the old one only for plane wave initial states, while for distorted waves the two-body operator enhances the impulse amplitude by a factor of about two.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic Bethe-Salpeter formalism for the \(NN\to d\pi\) reaction, consistent with the formalism used to define the nucleon-nucleon potential. They derive the full distorted-wave impulse amplitude as a loop integral, reduce it to time-ordered perturbation theory terms using partial fraction decomposition, and then algebraically recombine those terms into a sum of two-body operators. The new two-body operators are evaluated numerically at threshold using a purely one-pion-exchange deuteron wave function and three different phenomenological potentials (Argonne v18, Nijmegen II, Reid '93) for the initial-state distorted waves.

## Key numbers

* For plane wave initial states, the reduced matrix element of the traditional one-body operator is \(A_\text{1B}^\text{PW} = -24.0\) and that of the new two-body operator is \(A_\text{2B}^\text{PW} = -25.6\), showing approximate agreement.
* Using a purely one-pion-exchange deuteron, the impulse amplitude for \(np\to d\pi^0\) at threshold is enhanced by a factor of approximately two relative to the traditional one-body result.
* The two-body operator \({\cal O}_\text{2B}^a\) (Fig. 5a) is assigned power counting order \(\mathcal{O}\left(\sqrt{m_\pi/m_N}\right)\) while \({\cal O}_\text{2B}^b\) (Fig. 5b) is \(\mathcal{O}\left(m_\pi/m_N\right)\), indicating the latter is parametrically suppressed.

## Context

This work resolves a debate raised by Bolton and Miller (2010) regarding the inclusion of one-pion exchange in the impulse kernel and the need for "wave function corrections" to avoid double counting. It extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Partovi and Lomon (1969) and the chiral perturbation theory framework of Cohen et al. (1995) to the pion production problem. The new formalism enables more precise calculations of the total cross section for \(NN\to d\pi\) and prepares for future application to other observables such as \(p\)-wave pion production, where the impulse approximation contributes more significantly.
