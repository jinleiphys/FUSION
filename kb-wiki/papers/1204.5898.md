# Approximate Spin and Pseudospin Solutions of the Dirac equation with Rosen-Morse Potential including a Coulomb Tensor Interaction

> By applying the Pekeris-type approximation to deal with the (pseudo or) centrifugal term, the spin and pseudospin symmetry solutions of the Dirac equation for the Rosen-Morse potential including a Coulomb-like tensor potential with arbitrary spin-orbit coupling quantum number $κ$ are obtained by standard method. It has been shown from the numerical results that the degeneracies between spin and pseudospin state doublets are removed by the tensor interaction. Special case of this potential, that is, the spin and pseudospin solutions of the Dirac equation with the modified Pöschl-Teller potential including a tensor interaction is also considered. The results obtained in this case show that the tensor interaction removes the degeneracies between the members of doublets states in spin and pseudospin symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper claims that approximate spin and pseudospin symmetry solutions of the Dirac equation for the Rosen-Morse potential, including a Coulomb-like tensor potential, can be obtained using a Pekeris-type approximation for the centrifugal term; the key qualifier is that the solutions are approximate (not exact) for arbitrary spin-orbit coupling quantum number κ, and the degeneracies between spin and pseudospin state doublets are removed by the tensor interaction.

## Method

The Dirac equation with equal scalar and vector Rosen-Morse potentials and a Coulomb-like tensor potential (U(r) = -H/r) was solved under spin symmetry (Σ(r) = constant) and pseudospin symmetry (Δ(r) = constant) limits. A Pekeris-type approximation was applied to the centrifugal term (1/r²) to handle the spin-orbit coupling term for arbitrary κ, and the resulting differential equations were transformed using z = -e^{-2αr} and solved via the standard method (hypergeometric functions). Numerical bound-state energy eigenvalues were computed for various values of the tensor coupling constant H (0.0, 0.5, 1.0) and for both the Rosen-Morse potential and its special case, the modified Pöschl-Teller potential (V₂ = 0).

## Key numbers

- Bound state energy eigenvalues (in fm⁻¹) for the spin symmetry Rosen-Morse potential were computed with parameters: r₀ = 2.40873 fm, V₁ = 0.001 fm⁻¹, V₂ = 0.01 fm⁻¹, α = 0.988879, M = 10 fm⁻¹, Cₛ = 10 fm⁻¹ and Cₛ = 0, for H = 0.0, 0.5, 1.0.
- Bound state energy eigenvalues (in fm⁻¹) for the pseudospin symmetry Rosen-Morse potential were computed with the same parameters for H = 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, with Cₚₛ = 10 fm⁻¹ and Cₚₛ = 0.
- For the spin symmetry modified Pöschl-Teller potential (V₂ = 0), bound state energy eigenvalues (in fm⁻¹) were computed for H = 0.0, 0.5, 1.0 with Cₛ = 10 fm⁻¹.
- For the pseudospin symmetry modified Pöschl-Teller potential (V₂ = 0), bound state energy eigenvalues (in fm⁻¹) were computed for H = 0.0, 0.5, 1.0 with Cₚₛ = 10 fm⁻¹.
- The Coulomb radius used for the tensor potential was R_c = 7.78 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of spin and pseudospin symmetry in the Dirac equation for various potentials, including the Eckart potential (Hamzavi et al., 2011), the Woods-Saxon potential (Oyewumi et al., 2010), and the Hulthén potential (Ikhdair and Sever, 2010), all including Coulomb-like tensor interactions. The results are consistent with earlier findings by Oyewumi (2012) for the Rosen-Morse potential without tensor interaction, and by Agboola (2011) for the modified Pöschl-Teller potential. The work enables further application of these approximate solutions to nuclear structure problems, particularly in describing deformed nuclei and Λ-hypernuclei, where tensor interactions are known to remove degeneracies between spin and pseudospin doublets.
