# Nuclear effects in the deuteron in the resonance and deep-inelastic scattering region

> We discuss a hybrid model of the proton and neutron inelastic structure functions which incorporates both the partonic and the nucleon resonance structures and applicable in a wide region of the invariant mass $W$ of produced hadronic states. Focusing at the typical kinematics of JLab experiments we compute the deuteron structure functions and demonstrate good performance of the model against data. We perform systematic study of the ratios $F_2^n/F_2^d$ and $(F_2^p+F_2^n)/F_2^d$ for both, the deep-inelastic and the resonance, region in the context of recent measurements of BoNuS experiment at Jefferson Lab.

# Nuclear effects in the deuteron in the resonance and deep-inelastic scattering region

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid model combining partonic (deep-inelastic scattering) and nucleon resonance descriptions of the proton and neutron structure functions can successfully describe the deuteron structure function and its ratios in the kinematic region of Jefferson Lab experiments, but only when the model includes proper treatment of the inelastic threshold and averaging over kinematic bins to smooth out resonance oscillations.

## Method

The author constructs a hybrid model of nucleon structure functions that uses a next-to-next-to-leading-order QCD parton distribution function fit with target mass corrections and twist-4 contributions for the deep-inelastic region (W > 2 GeV), and an empirical resonance fit for W < 1.8 GeV, with linear interpolation in the overlap region. The deuteron structure function is computed using the convolution approach with the deuteron wave function, incorporating off-shell nucleon effects through an expansion in nucleon virtuality, and the model is compared to data from the BoNuS experiment at Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

- The BoNuS data cover 4-momentum transfer squared Q^2 between 0.7 and 4.5 GeV^2
- The interpolation region between DIS and resonance models is set between W_1 = 1.8 GeV and W_2 = 2.0 GeV
- The threshold suppression function uses parameter d = 0.15 GeV with W_th = M + m_pi (where m_pi is the pion mass)
- The deuteron binding energy is approximately -2.2 MeV
- The maximum difference between DIS and resonance integrals in the duality relation is about 5% in the region 1 < Q^2 < 9 GeV^2
- At Q^2 = 2 GeV^2, nuclear corrections reach about 60% in the Delta(1232) resonance region, about 30% in the second resonance region, and about 15% in the third resonance region
- The slope of r_d = (F_2^p + F_2^n)/F_2^d in the linear region 0.25 < x < 0.55 is approximately 0.1

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of nuclear deep-inelastic scattering and the EMC effect (Kulagin 2004, Kulagin 2010, Kulagin 2014) by incorporating resonance structure to address the kinematic region of the BoNuS experiment at Jefferson Lab (Tkachenko 2014, Griffioen 2015). The model builds on the parton distribution function fits of Alekhin (2006, 2007) and the resonance fit of Christy (2007), and demonstrates that the quark-hadron duality phenomenon (Bloom 1970) holds at the 5% level in the overlap region. The results enable more precise extraction of neutron structure functions from deuteron data and improved understanding of nuclear effects in the resonance region, though the author notes that further analysis is needed for very large Bjorken x.
