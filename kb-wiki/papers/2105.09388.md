# Interpolating 't Hooft model between instant and front forms

> The 't Hooft model, i.e. the two-dimensional quantum chromodynamics in the limit of infinite number of colors, is interpolated by an angle parameter $δ$ between $δ=0$ for the instant form dynamics (IFD) and $δ=π/4$ for the light-front dynamics (LFD). With this parameter $δ$, we formulate the interpolating mass gap equation which takes into account the non-trivial vacuum effect on the bare fermion mass to find the dressed fermion mass. Our interpolating mass gap solutions link the IFD and LFD results with the $δ$ parameter. We find the interpolation angle independent characteristic energy function which satisfies the energy-momentum dispersion relation of the dressed fermion, identifying the renormalized fermion mass function and the wave function renormalization factor. Using the dressed fermion propagator interpolating between IFD and LFD, we derive the corresponding quark-antiquark bound-state equation in the interpolating formulation. The mass spectra of mesons bearing the feature of the Regge trajectories are found independent of the $δ$-parameter for the equal mass quark and antiquark bound-states. The Gell-Mann - Oakes - Renner relation for the pionic ground-state in the zero fermion mass limit is confirmed indicating that the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry occurs regardless of the quantization for $0 \le δ\le π/4$. We obtain the corresponding bound-state wave functions and discuss their reference frame dependence. Applying them for the computation of the so-called quasi parton distribution functions, we note a possibility of utilizing not only the reference frame dependence but also the interpolation angle dependence to get an alternative effective approach to the LFD-like results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the 't Hooft model can be interpolated continuously between instant form dynamics (IFD, δ=0) and light-front dynamics (LFD, δ=π/4) using an angle parameter δ, and that physical observables such as meson mass spectra and the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation are independent of δ, while the bound-state wave functions and quasi-parton distribution functions depend on δ and the reference frame.

## Method

The authors formulate an interpolating mass gap equation for the dressed fermion mass in two-dimensional QCD with N_c → ∞, using both a Hamiltonian method (Bogoliubov transformation and normal-ordering) and a Feynman-diagram method (self-energy equation in the large-N_c approximation). They solve this mass gap equation numerically for interpolation angles δ between 0 and π/4, then derive the corresponding quark-antiquark bound-state equation and solve for meson mass spectra and wave functions. The resulting wave functions are used to compute quasi-parton distribution functions in the interpolating formulation.

## Key numbers

- The interpolation angle δ ranges from δ=0 (IFD) to δ=π/4 (LFD), with the metric components given by ℂ = cos(2δ) and 𝕊 = sin(2δ).
- The 't Hooft coupling is defined as λ = g²(N_c − 1/N_c)/(4π), which has dimension of mass squared in 1+1 dimensions.
- The mass gap equation for the Bogoliubov angle θ(p_−) is: (p_−/ℂ) cos θ(p_−) − (m/√ℂ) sin θ(p_−) = (λ/2) ∫ dk_−/(p_− − k_−)² sin(θ(p_−) − θ(k_−)), where the integral uses the principal-value prescription.
- The energy function E(p_−) = (ℂ/2)[E_u(p_−) − E_v(p_−)] satisfies: E(p_−) = p_− sin θ(p_−) + √ℂ m cos θ(p_−) + (ℂλ/2) ∫ dk_−/(p_− − k_−)² cos(θ(p_−) − θ(k_−)).
- The meson mass spectra are found independent of the δ-parameter for equal mass quark and antiquark bound-states, reproducing previous LFD and IFD results.
- The Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation for the pionic ground-state in the zero fermion mass limit is confirmed for all 0 ≤ δ ≤ π/4.

## Context

This work extends the interpolation method previously applied to scalar particles (Ma and Ji, 2019), electromagnetic gauge fields (Ma and Ji, 2020), and helicity spinors (Ma and Ji, 2020) to the 't Hooft model, which was originally solved in LFD by 't Hooft (1974) and later re-derived in IFD by Bars and Green (1978). The paper resolves an earlier inconsistency noted by Ji and Rey (1996) regarding the principal-value prescription in interpolated axial gauges. The results enable the computation of quasi-parton distribution functions using the interpolation angle as an additional parameter alongside reference frame dependence, providing an alternative approach to obtaining LFD-like results.
