# String Inspired QCD and $E_6$ Models

> The work in this thesis consists of two distinct parts: A class of models called, ``String-flip potential models,'' (SFP's) are studied as a possible candidate for modeling nuclear matter in terms of constituent quarks. These models are inspired from lattice quantum-chromodynamics (QCD) and are nonperturbative in nature. It is shown that they are viable candidates for modeling nuclear matter since they reproduce most of the bulk properties except for nuclear binding. Their properties are studied in nuclear and mesonic matter. A new class of models is developed, called ``flux-bubble potential models,'' which allows for the SFP's to be extended to include perturbative QCD interactions. Attempts to obtain nuclear binding is not successful, but valuable insight was gained towards possible future directions to pursue. The possibility of studying Superstring inspired $E_6$ phenomenology at high energy hadron colliders is investigated. The production of heavy lepton pairs {\it via} a gluon-gluon fusion mechanism is discussed. An enhancement in the parton level cross-section is expected due to the heavy (s)fermion loops which couple to the gluons.

# String Inspired QCD and $E_6$ Models

## Key claim

String-flip potential models (SFP's) are viable candidates for modeling nuclear matter in terms of constituent quarks because they reproduce most bulk properties, but they fail to produce nuclear binding; a new class of "flux-bubble potential models" extends SFP's to include perturbative QCD interactions but also does not achieve nuclear binding, though the work provides insight into future directions.

## Method

The author studied string-flip potential models as nonperturbative, lattice-QCD-inspired approaches to describe nuclear matter using constituent quarks, evaluating their properties in both nuclear and mesonic matter. A new class of flux-bubble potential models was developed to incorporate perturbative QCD interactions into the SFP framework. Separately, the production of heavy lepton pairs via gluon-gluon fusion at high energy hadron colliders was investigated within Superstring-inspired $E_6$ phenomenology, with an enhancement in the parton-level cross-section expected from heavy (s)fermion loops coupling to gluons.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy correlation piece for three-quark systems is given by $T_{\mathrm{C}} = \frac{\alpha\beta^\alpha}{2m_q} \sum_{\{\mathrm{rgb}\}} \xi_{\mathrm{rgb}}^{\alpha-2} [\alpha(1-(\beta\xi_{\mathrm{rgb}})^\alpha)+4]$, where $m_q$ is the constituent quark mass and $\alpha$, $\beta$ are variational parameters.
- The Fermi kinetic energy contribution is $T_{\mathrm{F}} = \frac{2\pi^2}{m_q L^2} \sum_q \vn^2_q$, where $L$ is the lattice size and $\vn_q$ are lattice vectors.
- For generating Slater wave functions, the total number of states is $(2k+1)^3$, with $k = \frac{1}{2} n_F^{1/3} - 1$ for $n_F$ fermions.
- The distributed minimization algorithm requires $p = \frac{1}{2}d(d-1)+2d+1$ points for fitting a $d$-dimensional paraboloid, with ${\cal O}(2p)$ points needed for good statistics.
- The distributed minimization method reduced computation time by approximately $1/2$ when using weighted variance.

## Context

This work extends the string-flip potential model approach to nuclear matter, which was inspired by lattice QCD and is nonperturbative in nature. The failure to obtain nuclear binding in both the SFP and flux-bubble models indicates that additional physics is needed beyond these constituent quark approaches. The $E_6$ phenomenology part investigates heavy lepton pair production via gluon-gluon fusion at hadron colliders, building on the work of Haber and Kane (1985) for the gauge and Yukawa coupling structure, and using Mathematica (Wolfram) for extracting couplings from the scalar potential.
