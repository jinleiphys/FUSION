# Exotic Hybrid Quark Potentials

> We use gauge/string duality to model some hybrid heavy-quark potentials. The potentials under consideration can't be described by a single Nambu-Goto string. This is why we call them "exotic". For $Σ_u^-$, the result is in quite good agreement with lattice simulations.

## Key claim

Using gauge/string duality with a point-like "defect" inserted on the string, the author models hybrid heavy-quark potentials that cannot be described by a single Nambu-Goto string; for the specific channel Σ_u^-, the result shows quite good agreement with lattice simulations, but the agreement depends on the choice of model (Model A vs Model B) and the value of the free parameter κ.

## Method

The author uses a five-dimensional deformed AdS metric with a deformation parameter s and places a quark-antiquark pair at the boundary, with the two fundamental strings joining at a point-like "defect" in the interior. The total action consists of two Nambu-Goto string actions plus a defect action, which is taken either as a point particle in 5D (Model A) or as a fivebrane wrapping an internal space in 10D (Model B). The configuration is extremized with respect to the worldsheet fields and the defect location, yielding parametric expressions for the interquark distance R(λ) and the hybrid potential Ṽ(λ), with λ = s r₀² where r₀ is the defect depth.

## Key numbers

- String tension σ = e g s, with fitted parameters g = 0.176, s = 0.44 GeV², and C = 0.71 GeV from fitting the Σ_g⁺ potential of Kuti et al.
- For Model A fitting Σ_u⁻, the free parameter κ is set to 2.3; for Model B fitting Σ_u⁻, κ is set to 2000.
- The finite energy gap between the hybrid and ground state at large R is Δ = 2κ √(g σ) for Model A.
- For Model B, the short-distance coefficient A at the critical point is A(λ_*) = g s^(3/2) (√π erf(√λ_*) - 2√λ_* e^(-λ_*))⁻¹, with an estimated value A ≈ 0.03 GeV³ for Σ_u⁻.
- The constant term for Σ_u⁻ in Model B at short distances is Ṽ₀ ≈ 1.90 GeV.
- From lattice data fits, the energy gaps above ground state are Δ₃ ≈ 0.77 GeV for Σ_u⁻ and Δ₄ ≈ 0.90 GeV for Σ_g⁻.
- The fitted mass from closed flux tube data (Teper) is m = 1.85 √σ, giving m = 0.78 GeV using σ = 0.18 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of quark-antiquark potentials (Andreev, 2009) and three-quark potentials (Andreev, 2011) using gauge/string duality, where the deformed AdS model successfully reproduced lattice data for ground-state potentials. The hybrid potentials studied here are classified by the D₄h symmetry group (Σ_g⁺ for ground state, Σ_u⁻ and Σ_g⁻ for excited states) following the lattice work of Kuti et al. (2006). The results challenge effective string theories in flat space (Arvis, 1983; Lüscher and Weisz, 2004) which predict vanishing energy gaps at large R, instead finding finite gaps consistent with lattice data up to 2.5 fm. The model enables future comparison with the GlueX experiment at Jefferson Lab and suggests that the same gluonic object may appear in closed flux tube spectra (Teper, 2012).
