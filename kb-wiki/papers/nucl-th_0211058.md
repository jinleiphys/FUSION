# Mott Effect and J/Psi Dissociation at the Quark-Hadron Phase Transition

> We investigate the in-medium modification of pseudoscalar and vector mesons in a QCD motivated chiral quark model by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equations for quarks and mesons at finite temperature for a wide mass range of meson masses, from light (pi, rho) to open-charm (D, D*) states. At the chiral / deconfinement phase transition, the quark-antiquark bound states enter the continuum of unbound states and become broad resonances (the hadronic Mott effect). We calculate the in-medium cross sections for charmonium dissociation due to collisions with light hadrons in a chiral Lagrangian approach, and show that the D and D* meson spectral broadening lowers the threshold for charmonium dissociation by pi and rho meson impact. This leads to a step-like enhancement in the reaction rate. We suggest that this mechanism for enhanced charmonium dissociation may be the physical mechanism underlying the anomalous J/Psi suppression observed by NA50.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the hadronic Mott effect, the transition of quark-antiquark bound states into broad resonances at the chiral/deconfinement phase transition, causes a step-like enhancement in the J/psi dissociation rate by light hadrons, and that this mechanism may be the physical explanation for the anomalous J/psi suppression observed by NA50, but only if the dramatic changes in mesonic spectral functions from the modified NJL model turn out to be realistic.

## Method

The authors solve Dyson-Schwinger equations for quarks and mesons at finite temperature in a QCD-motivated chiral quark model to obtain in-medium modifications of pseudoscalar and vector mesons from light (pi, rho) to open-charm (D, D*) states. They then calculate in-medium cross sections for charmonium dissociation by pi and rho meson impact using a chiral Lagrangian approach, where the spectral functions of D and D* mesons are modeled with a modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that includes an infrared cutoff to exclude unphysical quark production thresholds below the Mott temperature.

## Key numbers

* Mott transition temperature for D and D* mesons: T_Mott approximately 172 MeV, at which the spectral broadening effectively lowers the threshold for charmonium dissociation reactions.
* Above T_Mott, the J/psi dissociation rate shows a step-like enhancement by an order of magnitude compared to below T_Mott, dominated by the J/psi + rho subprocess.
* The vacuum cross sections for J/psi + pi -> D* + anti-D and J/psi + rho -> D* + anti-D* are taken from the chiral Lagrangian approach of Ref. [ikbb].

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the Mott transition for mesons in the NJL model (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1996) and prior calculations of charmonium dissociation by comoving hadrons (Vogt, 1988) and nucleons (Gerschel and Hufner, 1988). It builds on the finding that the Mott temperature for D mesons is close to that of pi and K mesons (Goeke and Klevansky, 1992). The mechanism offers a possible explanation for the anomalous J/psi suppression observed by NA50 in 158A GeV Pb-Pb collisions at CERN SPS, and the authors plan to improve the modeling using QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations and compare with lattice QCD results (Karsch, 2002).
