# Nonconservation of the Quantum Number K and Phase Transitions in Rapidly Rotating Nuclei

> Three different effects observed in experiments with rotating nuclei--backbending, noncollective quadrupole transitions between different levels of the same band, and transitions that occur, in rapidly rotating nuclei, from large-$K$ isomeric states immediately to the levels of a rotational band despite their strong forbiddenness in $K$--are explained in terms of nonconservation of the quantum number $K$ in such nuclei.

# Nonconservation of the Quantum Number K and Phase Transitions in Rapidly Rotating Nuclei

## Key claim
Backbending, noncollective quadrupole transitions within a rotational band, and transitions from large-K isomeric states to rotational-band levels in rapidly rotating nuclei can all be explained by the nonconservation of the quantum number K, which becomes fully mixed above a critical spin I_c. The authors propose that this represents a collective phase transition in the coupling scheme of angular momenta, rather than band crossings or other mechanisms.

## Method
The authors analyze experimental rotational frequency versus angular momentum I for rotational bands in 160Yb, comparing the frequency to the rigid-body line. They develop a Landau-type phase-transition formalism using an order parameter η = 1 − 3⟨K²⟩/[I(I+1)], which vanishes when all K values become equally probable (the "n" phase). They derive expressions for electric quadrupole transition probabilities that depend on K² averaging, and compare these predictions to experimental lifetime data and to observed transitions from large-K isomers.

## Key numbers
- The rigid-body moment of inertia is given by 𝒥₀ = (2/5)MR², where M and R are the nuclear mass and radius.
- In the n phase (I ≥ I_c), the mean values become ⟨K⟩ = 0, ⟨K²⟩ = I(I+1)/3, and ⟨cos²θ⟩ = 1/3.
- At the onset of the n phase, the rotational frequency satisfies ℏΩ_{nc} = (ℏ²/𝒥₀)I_c.
- In the n phase, the effective quadrupole moment Q_I for radiative transitions equals two-thirds of the quadrupole moment Q₀ for the lowest I in the yrast band of an even-even nucleus.
- The ratio ζ = Q_I²/Q₀² is given by ζ = (2/15)[(2I−1)(2I+1)/I(I−1)](ω_{2→0}/ω_{I→I−2})⁵(w_{I→I−2}/w_{2→0}).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier hypothesis (Kamchatnov and Nosov, 2000) that backbending results from K nonconservation rather than band intersections, as had been commonly assumed since the alignment model of Stephens and Simon (1972). The approach treats the phenomenon as a collective phase transition in the Landau formalism, distinguishing first backbendings from secondary backbends and low-frequency anomalies (described by Frauendorf and others, 1990s). The predictions for reduced quadrupole transition probabilities and for transitions from large-K isomers (observed in 182Os, 174Hf, and 179W by various experimental groups in the 1990s) provide testable consequences that could confirm or disprove the K-mixing hypothesis.
