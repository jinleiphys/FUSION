# Exploring core excitation in halo nuclei using halo effective field theory: an application to the bound states of $^{11}$Be

> Halo effective field theory (Halo-EFT) has proved to be very efficient for describing halo nuclei within models of nuclear reactions. Its order-by-order expansion scheme enables us to single out the structure observables that are probed in reactions, and therefore improve the accuracy of their values inferred from experiment. This formalism is however limited by its breakdown scale. Structure effects beyond that scale cannot be considered explicitly in reaction models. To extend the usual Halo-EFT, we include core excitation considering a particle-rotor model. We apply it to the case of $^{11}$Be, the archetypical one-neutron halo nucleus. The corresponding set of coupled equations is solved using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh. As a first application, we analyze in detail the structure of both bound states of $^{11}$Be and the $^{10}$Be-n phaseshifts at low energy in the corresponding partial wave as a function of the core deformation. We also compute the electric dipole transition between these bound states. The comparison of our results with existing \textit{ab initio} calculations, show that including core excitation within Halo-EFT can significantly improve the description of the $\frac{1}{2}^+$ ground state of $^{11}$Be over single-particle models. On the contrary, core excitation has a negative effect on the description of the $\frac{1}{2}^-$ bound excited state. This is probably related to the presence of Pauli-forbidden states in our two-body model of $^{11}$Be.

## Key claim

Including core excitation within Halo-EFT via a particle-rotor model significantly improves the description of the $\frac{1}{2}^+$ ground state of $^{11}$Be over single-particle models, but has a negative effect on the description of the $\frac{1}{2}^-$ bound excited state, likely due to Pauli-forbidden states in the two-body model.

## Method

The authors extend standard Halo-EFT by incorporating core excitation through a particle-rotor model, treating the $^{10}$Be core as an axially symmetric rigid rotor with its $0^+$ ground state and $2^+$ excited state as members of a rotational band. They solve the coupled-channel equations using the R-matrix method on a Lagrange mesh, fitting the low-energy constants of a Gaussian-regularized NLO contact interaction to reproduce the experimental one-neutron separation energy and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) predicted by the ab initio NCSMC calculation of Calci et al. (2016).

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Be ground state: $E_{1/2^+} = -0.503$ MeV (from Ajzen (1990))
* ANC of $^{11}$Be ground state from ab initio: ${\cal C}_{1/2^+} = 0.786$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (from Calci et al. 2016)
* One-neutron separation energy of $^{11}$Be excited state: $E_{1/2^-} = -0.184$ MeV (from Ajzen (1990))
* ANC of $^{11}$Be excited state from ab initio: ${\cal C}_{1/2^-} = 0.129$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (from Calci et al. 2016)
* Core excitation energy of $^{10}$Be $2^+$ state: $\epsilon_{2^+} = 3.368$ MeV (from Ajzen (1990))
* Small expansion parameter for $^{11}$Be: $\eta \approx 0.4$ (from Hammer et al. 2017 review)
* At optimal deformation $\beta=0.5$ with $\sigma=1.5$ fm, the spectroscopic factor for the ground state dominant channel is ${\cal S}_{1s_{1/2} \otimes 0^+} = 0.92$, matching the ab initio value of 0.90
* At $\beta=0.5$ with $\sigma=1.5$ fm, the rotor-EFT reproduces the ab initio phaseshift up to at least 4 MeV, compared to 1.5 MeV for standard Halo-EFT

## Context

This work extends standard Halo-EFT (Hammer et al. 2017 review) by including core excitation, following earlier collective-model approaches by Esbensen (1995), Nunes (1996), and Thompson et al. The particle-rotor model builds on the Bohr and Mottelson framework and is justified by AMD calculations (KHD 1999) and NCSM calculations (McCoy 2024) showing that $^{10}$Be states belong to the same rotational band. The approach enables bridging ab initio NCSMC predictions (Calci et al. 2016) to reaction models, shifting the breakdown scale to higher energies than the single-channel Halo-EFT used in Capel et al. (2018). This work expands the panorama of effective field theories for collective excitations reviewed by Coello Perez and Papenbrock (2025) to light deformed halo nuclei.
