# Lower bound on the value of the fine-structure constant

> Recently, we have proposed the existence of a universal relation between the maximal electric charge and total mass of any weakly self-gravitating object: $Z\leq Z^*=α^{-1/3}A^{2/3}$, where $Z$ is the number of protons, $A$ is the total baryon (mass) number, and $α=e^2/\hbar c$ is the fine-structure constant. Motivated by this novel bound, we explore the $(Z,A)$-relation of atomic nuclei as deduced from the Weizsäcker semi-empirical mass formula. It is shown that {\it all} nuclei, including the meta-stable maximally charged ones, conform to the upper bound. Moreover, we suggest that the new charge-mass bound places an interesting constraint on the value of the fine-structure constant: $α\gtrsim 1/323$.

# Lower bound on the value of the fine-structure constant

## Key claim
The paper derives a lower bound on the fine-structure constant, α ≳ 1/323, from the requirement that all atomic nuclei (including maximally charged meta-stable ones) respect a previously proposed universal charge-mass bound Z ≤ α⁻¹/³ A²/³, which itself follows from the weak cosmic censorship conjecture.

## Method
The author uses the Weizsäcker semi-empirical mass formula with experimentally fitted coefficients (a_V, a_S, a_C, a_A) to model nuclear binding energies. By demanding that any collection of nucleons with Z > α⁻¹/³ A²/³ has negative binding energy (and is therefore unstable), the author derives a quadratic inequality in α¹/³ that must hold for all mass numbers A. Solving this inequality with the measured coefficients yields the lower bound on α.

## Key numbers
- Lower bound on the fine-structure constant: α ≳ 1/323.6 (from the maximally charged nuclei stability condition)
- A weaker bound from stable nuclei alone gives α ≳ 1/5161.7
- The experimentally measured value of α is approximately 1/137.036
- The Coulomb coefficient in the mass formula is a_C = 0.691(2) (empirically fitted), and the relation a_C ≃ cα with c ≃ 97.38 gives a_C ≃ 0.711 (within 3% of the empirical value)
- The volume coefficient a_V = 15.36(3), surface coefficient a_S = 16.42(8), asymmetry coefficient a_A = 22.53(7)
- The ratio Z_max/Z* for all nuclei has a maximal value of approximately 0.85
- The ratio Z_stable/Z* has a maximal value of approximately 0.56
- Lead-208 satisfies Z/A²/³ ≃ 2.33 < α⁻¹/³; the artificial nucleus with Z=118, A=294 satisfies Z/A²/³ ≃ 2.67 < α⁻¹/³

## Context
This work extends the author's previous derivation (Hod, 2010) of a universal charge-mass bound Z ≤ α⁻¹/³ A²/³ from the weak cosmic censorship conjecture of Penrose (1969). The bound is tested against the Weizsäcker semi-empirical mass formula, a standard nuclear physics tool developed by von Weizsäcker (1935) and refined by subsequent authors (Rohlf, Segre, Cook, Goodman, Kirson). The result suggests that the measured value of the fine-structure constant is not arbitrary but is constrained by the requirement that atomic nuclei do not violate cosmic censorship. This opens the possibility of deriving fundamental constants from consistency conditions linking quantum mechanics, electromagnetism, and general relativity.
