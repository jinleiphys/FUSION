# Estimate for the bulk viscosity of strongly coupled quark matter using perturbative QCD and holography

> Modern hydrodynamic simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers require knowledge not only of the equilibrium properties of strongly interacting matter, but also of the system's response to perturbations, encoded in various transport coefficients. Using perturbative and holographic tools, we derive here an improved weak-coupling and a new strong-coupling result for the most important transport coefficient of unpaired quark matter, its bulk viscosity. These results are combined in a simple analytic pocket formula for the quantity that is rooted in perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics at high densities but takes into account nonperturbative holographic input at neutron-star densities, where the system is strongly coupled. This expression can be used in the modeling of unpaired quark matter at astrophysically relevant temperatures and densities.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first strong-coupling predictions for the bulk viscosity of unpaired quark matter, combined with improved weak-coupling results, but the results are limited to unpaired quark matter and do not account for quark pairing, which may substantially alter the viscosity at low temperatures.

## Method

The bulk viscosity was computed using three approaches: perturbative QCD (pQCD) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) for the thermodynamic susceptibilities, and two holographic models (D3-D7 and V-QCD) for strong-coupling input. The dominant contribution arises from the electroweak process u+d ↔ u+s, with the rate λ₁ depending on temperature and chemical potentials, and the thermodynamic coefficients A₁ and C₁ derived from susceptibilities of the quark matter pressure. The strange quark mass was set to m_s = 93.4 MeV in all calculations, and charge neutrality and beta equilibrium conditions were enforced to determine the chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The bulk viscosity ζ was evaluated at baryon density n_B ≈ 5 n_sat (with n_sat ≈ 0.16/fm³) and rotation frequency ω = 2π × 1 kHz, shown as a function of temperature T.
- The D3-D7 model constituent quark masses, fixed by matching to pQCD at μ_d = 1 GeV with renormalization scale parameter X ∈ [1/2, 2], are: M_u ∈ (522.5, 434.6) MeV, M_d ∈ (526.4, 435.9) MeV, M_s ∈ (541.8, 450.1) MeV.
- The electroweak rate λ₁ at leading order contains a factor μ_d⁵ T², with the Fermi constant G_F and Cabibbo angle θ_c, and an O(α_s) correction factor (1 + σ log(Λ/T))⁴ where σ = 4α_s/(9π) and Λ ≈ 0.158 √α_s √(μ_u² + μ_d² + μ_s²).
- The D3-D7 model yields an analytic formula for ζ involving μ_d⁶ (M_s² - M_d²)² in the numerator, with K_i ≡ 3μ_d² - M_i² in the denominator.

## Context

This work extends prior perturbative results for quark matter bulk viscosity from Madsen (1992) and Heiselberg (1993) to next-to-leading order, and provides the first strong-coupling predictions using holography, where previously only the purely QCD contribution had been considered (Czajka 2018, Hoyos 2020). The results enable improved hydrodynamic simulations of neutron-star mergers and core-collapse supernovae, and can be compared to nuclear and hyperonic matter results from Alford (2022) and Jyothilakshmi (2022). The D3-D7 analytic formula is recommended for phenomenological modeling of unpaired quark matter, while the V-QCD result provides a lower bound.
