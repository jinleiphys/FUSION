# A New Parameter Set for the Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> Subtracting the Strutinsky shell corrections from the selfconsistent energies obtained within the Relativistic Mean Field Theory (RMFT) we have got estimates for the macroscopic part of the binding energies of 142 spherical even-even nuclei. By minimizing their root mean square deviations from the values obtained with the Lublin-Srasbourg Drop (LSD) model with respect to the nine RMFT parameters we have found the optimal set (NL4). The new parameters reproduce also the radii of these nuclei with an accuracy comparable with that obtained with the NL1 and NL3 sets.

## Key claim

By subtracting Strutinsky shell corrections from self-consistent Relativistic Mean Field Theory (RMFT) energies and minimizing the root mean square deviation against the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) model, the authors obtain a new parameter set (NL4) that reduces the rms deviation for macroscopic binding energies by more than a factor of two compared to the NL3 set, though the NL4 masses still do not reach the quality of the Gogny D1S force.

## Method

The macroscopic part of the binding energy was extracted for 142 spherical even-even nuclei by subtracting Strutinsky shell corrections from RMFT binding energies computed without pairing forces. The nine RMFT parameters (nucleon mass, meson masses for rho, omega, and sigma, and their coupling constants) were then optimized by minimizing the root mean square deviation of these macroscopic energies from the LSD model values. The resulting NL4 set was tested against experimental data for masses and charge, neutron, and proton radii, and compared with the NL1, NL2, NL3, and Gogny D1S results.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation of macroscopic energies from the LSD model is 7.17 MeV for the NL3 set and 3.29 MeV for the new NL4 set, an improvement by more than a factor of two.
- The NL4 parameters include: nucleon mass m = 938 MeV; meson masses m_omega = 782.474 MeV, m_rho = 763.9 MeV, m_sigma = 508.194 MeV; coupling constants g_omega = 12.867, g_rho = -4.360, g_sigma = 10.216, rho_2 = -10.432, rho_3 = -28.882.
- The fitted radius formula parameters for charge radii are r_0 = 1.2328 fm, alpha = -0.15, kappa = 2.1253; for neutron radii r_0 = 1.1761 fm, alpha = 0.2625, kappa = 3.085; for the ratio r_p/r_n, r_0 = 1.0378, alpha = -0.3702, kappa = -1.6249.

## Context

This work extends the line of RMFT parameter fitting initiated by Reinhard (1986) with NL1, Rufa (1988) with NL2, and Lalazissis (1997) with NL3, and compares results against the Gogny D1S force of Berger (1984) and the LSD model of Pomorski (2003). The new NL4 set provides improved reproduction of masses and radii for spherical even-even nuclei, though the approximate BCS treatment of pairing in RMFT prevents it from reaching the quality of the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the Gogny D1S force. The fitted isospin-dependent radius formulas enable systematic predictions for nuclear structure studies.
