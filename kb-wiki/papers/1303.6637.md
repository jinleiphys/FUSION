# Calculating Track-Based Observables for the LHC

> By using observables that only depend on charged particles (tracks), one can efficiently suppress pile-up contamination at the LHC. Such measurements are not infrared safe in perturbation theory, so any calculation of track-based observables must account for hadronization effects. We develop a formalism to perform these calculations in QCD, by matching partonic cross sections onto new non-perturbative objects called track functions which absorb infrared divergences. The track function T_i(x) describes the energy fraction x of a hard parton i which is converted into charged hadrons. We give a field-theoretic definition of the track function and derive its renormalization group evolution, which is in excellent agreement with the Pythia parton shower. We then perform a next-to-leading order calculation of the total energy fraction of charged particles in e+ e- -> hadrons. To demonstrate the implications of our framework for the LHC, we match the Pythia parton shower onto a set of track functions to describe the track mass distribution in Higgs plus one jet events. We also show how to reduce smearing due to hadronization fluctuations by measuring dimensionless track-based ratios.

# Calculating Track-Based Observables for the LHC

## Key claim
Track-based observables that depend only on charged particles can be calculated in QCD by matching partonic cross sections onto non-perturbative track functions, but only if the track functions absorb the infrared divergences that would otherwise make such calculations ill-defined in perturbation theory.

## Method
The authors introduce track functions T_i(x) that describe the energy fraction x of a hard parton i converted into charged hadrons, defined analogously to fragmentation functions. They derive the renormalization group evolution equation for track functions using DGLAP-type splitting kernels, and perform a next-to-leading order calculation of the total energy fraction of charged particles in e+ e- → hadrons. For LHC applications, they match the Pythia parton shower onto track functions evolved to low scales to describe the track mass distribution in Higgs plus one jet events.

## Key numbers
- On average 60% of the energy of an initial quark is contained in charged hadrons, based on Pythia extraction at LO with a peak at x = 0.6
- The NLO track function evolution from μ = 100 GeV down to μ = 10 GeV and up to μ = 1000 GeV shows excellent agreement with the Pythia parton shower
- The e+ e- → hadrons calculation at Q = 91 GeV shows good convergence from LO to NLO, with uncertainty bands obtained by varying μ between Q/2 and 2Q
- The jet scale for narrow well-separated jets is μ_J ≃ p_T R, where p_T is the jet transverse momentum and R is the azimuthal-rapidity jet size

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for jet substructure calculations by providing a field-theoretic definition of track functions, analogous to how fragmentation functions and parton distribution functions absorb infrared divergences. The formalism builds on prior work on jet charge distributions (Krohn 2012, Waalewijn 2012) and jet functions (Ellis 2010, Procura 2011), and enables precision predictions for track-based observables that suppress pile-up contamination at the LHC. The authors demonstrate that independent fragmentation is an excellent approximation for these observables, and show that dimensionless ratios like jet mass to jet p_T reduce smearing from hadronization fluctuations.
