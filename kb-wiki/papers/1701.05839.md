# Inclusive production of small radius jets in heavy-ion collisions

> We develop a new formalism to describe the inclusive production of small radius jets in heavy-ion collisions, which is consistent with jet calculations in the simpler proton-proton system. Only at next-to-leading order (NLO) and beyond, the jet radius parameter $R$ and the jet algorithm dependence of the jet cross section can be studied and a meaningful comparison to experimental measurements is possible. We are able to consistently achieve NLO accuracy by making use of the recently developed semi-inclusive jet functions within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET). In addition, single logarithms of the jet size parameter $α_s^n\ln^n R$ are resummed to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL$_R$) accuracy. The medium modified semi-inclusive jet functions are obtained within the framework of SCET with Glauber gluons that describe the interaction of jets with the medium. We present numerical results for the suppression of inclusive jet cross sections in heavy ion collisions at the LHC and the formalism developed here can be extended directly to corresponding jet substructure observables.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new formalism for inclusive small-radius jet production in heavy-ion collisions that achieves next-to-leading-order (NLO) accuracy combined with resummation of logarithms of the jet radius parameter \( \alpha_s^n \ln^n R \) to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL\(_R\)) accuracy, but the medium contribution is included only at NLO without resummation, and the opacity expansion is truncated at first order.

## Method

The authors extend the semi-inclusive jet function (siJF) formalism from Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), previously applied to proton-proton collisions, to heavy-ion collisions by adding in-medium siJFs derived from SCET with Glauber gluons (SCET\(_{\rm G}\)). The in-medium siJFs are obtained by replacing vacuum splitting functions with collinear in-medium splitting functions at first order in opacity, and the cross section is written as the sum of a vacuum NLO+NLL\(_R\) term and a medium term added at NLO. The vacuum siJFs are evolved via DGLAP equations to resum \(\ln R\) terms, while the medium siJFs are evaluated numerically using a cut-off scheme.

## Key numbers

- The jet radius parameter values studied are \(R = 0.2, 0.3, 0.4\) for both proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 2.76\) TeV.
- The coupling strength between the jet and the QCD medium is chosen as \(g = 2.1 \pm 0.1\).
- The cold nuclear matter (CNM) energy loss corresponds to a momentum exchange scale of \(\mu_{\mathrm{CNM}} = 0.35\) GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is computed for \(0-10\%\) and \(30-50\%\) centrality bins, with \(|\eta| < 2\) and anti-\(k_T\) jets.

## Context

This work extends the semi-inclusive jet function formalism of Kang, Ringer, and Vitev (2016) for proton-proton collisions to heavy-ion collisions, using the SCET\(_{\rm G}\) framework for medium interactions developed by Idilbi and Ovanesyan (2008) and Ovanesyan and Vitev (2011). The in-medium splitting functions are derived to first order in opacity, analogous to the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) approach to parton energy loss. The formalism enables consistent NLO+NLL\(_R\) calculations for small-radius jets in heavy-ion collisions and can be extended to jet substructure observables.
