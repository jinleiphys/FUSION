# Viscous Hydrodynamic Predictions for Nuclear Collisions at the LHC

> Hydrodynamic simulations are used to make predictions for the integrated elliptic flow coefficient v_2 in sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV lead-lead and sqrt(s)=14 TeV proton-proton collisions at the LHC. We predict a 10% increase in v_2 from RHIC to Pb+Pb at LHC, and v_2 ~ 0 in p+p collisions unless eta/s < 0.08.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the integrated elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC will be about 10% larger than at RHIC, but this prediction depends on the assumption that the average shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) is similar at both energies. For p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV, they predict \(v_2 \sim 0\) unless \(\eta/s < 0.08\).

## Method

The authors used a second-order viscous hydrodynamic model with boost-invariant initial conditions in Milne coordinates, solving the conservation of the energy-momentum tensor numerically. Initial energy density profiles were generated using both Glauber and Color-Glass-Condensate (CGC) models, with normalization adjusted to match predicted multiplicities at LHC energies. The elliptic flow was approximated as half the momentum anisotropy \(e_p/2\) rather than computed from full freeze-out, to avoid uncertainties from hadronic modeling.

## Key numbers

- Predicted 10% increase in integrated \(v_2\) from RHIC Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV to LHC Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV
- For p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV, \(v_2 \sim 0.035\) for ideal hydrodynamics, but \(v_2\) consistent with zero if \(\eta/s \ge 0.08\)
- Threshold for non-zero elliptic flow in p+p: \(\eta/s < 0.08\) would be required for integrated \(|v_2| > 0.02\) in minimum bias collisions
- Charged hadron multiplicity assumed for central Pb+Pb: \(dN_{\rm ch}/dY \simeq 1800\)
- Initial temperature at center for Pb+Pb Glauber model: \(T_i = 0.42\) GeV; for CGC model: \(T_i = 0.39\) GeV (both at \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm/c)
- Upper limit on \(\eta/s\) from RHIC data: \(\eta/s < 0.5\) for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV
- Best-fit \(\eta/s\) values from RHIC: \(\eta/s \simeq 0.08\) for Glauber initial conditions, \(\eta/s \simeq 0.16\) for CGC initial conditions

## Context

This work extends viscous hydrodynamic simulations from RHIC Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (Luzum 2008, Song 2008, Huovinen 2008) to predict elliptic flow at LHC energies. The authors note that their predicted 10% increase in \(v_2\) is smaller than the ideal hydrodynamics prediction by Niemi (2008) but agrees with extrapolations by Drescher (2007). The results enable experimental tests of whether the successful hydrodynamic description of RHIC data was coincidental, and provide a method to constrain \(\eta/s\) from LHC data once initial eccentricity is known.
