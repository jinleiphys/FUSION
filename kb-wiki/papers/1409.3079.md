# Non-extensive Statistics and Understanding Particle Production and Kinetic Freeze-out Process from $p_T$-spectra at 2.76 TeV

> An approach, based on Tsallis non-extensive statistics, has been employed, here, to analyse, systematically, the $p_T$-spectra of various identified secondary hadrons like pions, kaons, protons and antiprotons, produced in different central $Pb+Pb$ interactions at LHC energy 2.76 TeV in terms of multiplicity and temperature fluctuations. The results, thus obtained, have been utilized to understand the various stages of different types of hadron production during evolution of the fireball produced in such collisions.

# Non-extensive Statistics and Understanding Particle Production and Kinetic Freeze-out Process from \(p_T\)-spectra at 2.76 TeV

## Key claim

Tsallis non-extensive statistics, modified to include transverse collective flow, can simultaneously describe the transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and protons/antiprotons in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the extracted kinetic freeze-out temperatures differ by particle species, contrary to the assumption of a single freeze-out temperature, and the interpretation of the parameter \(c\) remains ambiguous.

## Method

The author fitted the invariant yield formula derived from Tsallis non-extensive statistics (equation 13) to published \(p_T\)-spectra of pions, kaons, and protons/antiprotons from Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV for several centrality classes. The fit included a transverse flow velocity \(v_T\) and imposed phenomenological correlations (equations 14 and 16) linking the effective temperature \(T_{eff}\), the non-extensivity parameter \(q\), and the average multiplicity per unit rapidity \(dN/dy\). The same formula (without flow) was first applied to \(p+p\) data at the same energy to obtain reference yields \(n_0\).

## Key numbers

* Kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{kin}\) for kaons is approximately constant at 167 MeV across centralities.
* Kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{kin}\) for protons/antiprotons is approximately constant at 236 MeV across centralities.
* Kinetic freeze-out temperature \(T_{kin}\) for pions increases from 95 MeV in the most central (0-5%) collisions to about 160 MeV in the most peripheral (80-90%) collisions.
* The average transverse flow velocity \(v_T\) increases with particle mass for a given centrality, being smallest for pions and largest for protons.
* The integrated yield per unit rapidity \(dN/dy\) shows a nearly linear dependence on the number of participant quarks \(N_{q-part}\) rather than on the number of participant nucleons \(N_{part}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier analysis (De, 2014) which treated only pions and kaons without transverse flow, and now incorporates flow to handle heavier secondaries like protons. The results are compared to a blast-wave analysis by the ALICE collaboration (Abelev et al., 2013) which found similar centrality dependence of \(v_T\) and compatible final freeze-out temperatures. The finding that heavier hadrons freeze out at higher temperatures supports a similar conclusion from a study at RHIC energies (Khandai et al., 2013) that used a related Tsallis-based approach. The analysis is limited by the need for both \(p+p\) and \(A+A\) spectra at the same energy for each particle species, preventing extension to heavier baryons like \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi\) until such data become available.
