# Universal pion freeze-out phase-space density

> Results on the pion phase-space density at freeze-out in sulphur-nucleus, Pb-Pb and pi-p collisions at the CERN SPS are presented. All heavy-ion reactions are consistent with the thermal Bose-Einstein distribution f=1/[exp(E/T)-1] at T ~ 120 MeV, modified for radial expansion. pi-p data are also consistent with f, but at T ~ 180 MeV and without radial flow.

## Key claim

All heavy-ion reactions at the CERN SPS (sulphur-nucleus and Pb-Pb collisions) are consistent with a thermal Bose-Einstein distribution at a freeze-out temperature of about 120 MeV, modified for radial expansion, while π-p collisions are consistent with the same distribution but at about 180 MeV and without radial flow; however, the analysis relies on a pragmatic estimate of the intercept parameter λ = 0.7 ± 0.1, with systematic uncertainties from Coulomb corrections, contamination, and weak decays that could shift the results.

## Method

The authors extracted the spatially averaged pion phase-space density at freeze-out by combining measured single-particle pT-spectra (parameterized as exponentials with inverse slope Teff) with two-pion Bose-Einstein correlation radii (Rs, Ro, Rl, Rol) and the intercept parameter λ, using Eq. (5) from the paper. They applied this to S-nucleus (NA35, NA44), Pb-Pb (NA49, NA44), and π-p (NA22) data at the CERN SPS, all analyzed in the longitudinally comoving system (LCMS). For data sets lacking the out-longitudinal cross term Rol, the analysis was restricted to near mid-rapidity where Rol is small, limiting the systematic error to less than 5%.

## Key numbers

- For all heavy-ion data at mid-rapidity, the average phase-space density ⟨f⟩ is consistent with a thermal Bose-Einstein distribution at T ~ 120 MeV, with a range of 100-140 MeV.
- The most likely value for the intercept parameter λ across all data sets is 0.7, with a wide error of ±0.1.
- Multiplicity densities dn-/dy for S-nucleus collisions: S-S = 22, S-Cu = 24, S-Ag = 28, S-Au = 37; for Pb-Pb collisions: 40-185, depending on rapidity.
- For NA49 Pb-Pb data at mid-rapidity (2.9 < y < 3.4), the data can be fitted with the function exp[-1.1(1) - pT/0.31(6) GeV] (χ²/ndf = 0.2), showing a flatter slope than the static Bose-Einstein distribution.
- For π-p collisions, the data are consistent with a thermal Bose-Einstein distribution at T ~ 180 MeV.
- All measured phase-space densities are smaller than 0.5, indicating multiparticle symmetrization effects are weak.

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Bertsch (1980s) for estimating the pion phase-space density from combined single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations, applying it systematically to multiple collision systems at the CERN SPS (NA35, NA44, NA49, NA22). The results confirm the two-stage freeze-out scenario in heavy-ion collisions (chemical freeze-out at Tchem ~ 170-180 MeV, thermal freeze-out at Ttherm ~ 120 MeV) as seen in analyses by Becattini (1990s) and NA49, while showing that π-p collisions freeze out immediately at the higher temperature. The finding that ⟨f⟩ < 0.5 for all systems enables the current practice of calculating two-particle correlation functions from two-particle symmetrization only, and rules out a pion laser or condensate. The data also show consistency with AGS Au-Au results from E877 at 10.8 GeV/c per nucleon, and with models including radial expansion (Tomasik et al., 1999).
