# $Z_{cs}(3985)$ in next-to-leading-order chiral effective field theory -- the first truncation uncertainty analysis

> We revisit the $D_s^-D^{*0}$/$D_s^{*-}D^0$ interaction and the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ state in chiral effective field theory(EFT) up to the next-to-leading order. We examine the relative importance of the leading-order contact, one-eta exchange, next-to-leading-order contact, and two-kaon-exchange contributions. We show that the leading-order and next-to-leading-order contact contributions play the most important role such that the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ state qualifies as a $D_s^-D^{*0}$/$D_s^{*-}D^0$ resonance. On the other hand, the weak one-eta-exchange and weakly energy-dependent two-kaon-exchange contributions are less important in dynamically generating the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ state, indicating that chiral EFT is less predictive in the present situation. Furthermore, we apply the Bayesian method to estimate chiral truncation uncertainties and find that they are of similar magnitude as their statistical counterparts. Our study shows that if $Z_c(3900)$ exists, then SU(3)-flavor symmetry also predicts $Z_{cs}(3985)$ with a certain robustness, i.e., both can be accommodated in the chiral EFT with dominant contact contributions.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ state can be described as a $D_s^-D^{*0}/D_s^{*-}D^0$ resonance in next-to-leading-order chiral effective field theory, but only because the leading-order and next-to-leading-order contact terms dominate; the one-eta-exchange and two-kaon-exchange contributions are too weak to generate the state on their own, meaning chiral EFT is not very predictive for this system without external experimental input.

## Method
The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for $D_s^-D^{*0}/D_s^{*-}D^0$ scattering using a chiral potential up to next-to-leading order, comprising leading-order contact, one-eta-exchange, next-to-leading-order contact, and two-kaon-exchange terms. They fitted the low-energy constants and a Gaussian cutoff to the BESIII $K^+$ recoil-mass spectrum data for $e^+e^-\to K^+(D_s^-D^{*0}+D_s^{*-}D^0)$, then applied a Bayesian model to estimate chiral truncation uncertainties for both the potential and the spectrum.

## Key numbers
- The $Z_{cs}(3985)$ mass from BESIII is $3982.5^{+1.8}_{-2.6}\pm 2.1$ MeV and width $12.8^{+5.3}_{-4.4}\pm 3.0$ MeV.
- With the NLO chiral potential and both statistical and systematic uncertainties, the pole position is $(m,\Gamma)=(3982.4_{-5.2}^{+6.7}, 11.8_{-7.4}^{+8.9})$ MeV.
- After refitting the BESIII data, the NLO pole position is $(M,\Gamma)=(3983.5_{-6.5}^{+9.8}, 21.3_{-12.5}^{+18.5})$ MeV.
- The optimal cutoff from the NLO fit is $\Lambda=0.36_{-0.14}^{+0.14}$ GeV, implying the EFT can be trusted up to about 4.041 GeV.
- The LO and NLO contact LECs from the refit are $\tilde{C}_S=2.33_{-1.45-0.93}^{+1.45+0.93}\times10^2$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $C_S=-46.80_{-11.56-7.04}^{+11.56+7.04}\times10^2$ GeV$^{-4}$.

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT study of Wang et al. (2020) that used $Z_c(3900)$ data and SU(3)-flavor symmetry to predict $Z_{cs}(3985)$, by adding the first Bayesian truncation uncertainty analysis in the heavy-quark exotic-hadron sector. It contradicts earlier one-boson-exchange model studies (Chen et al., 2020) that found no $D_s^-D^{*0}$ molecule, and shows that the OEE and TKE contributions are too weak for a predictive chiral EFT, unlike in nucleon-nucleon systems where pion exchanges dominate. The results enable statistically meaningful uncertainty quantification for future chiral EFT studies of exotic hadrons and confirm that $Z_{cs}(3985)$ remains a robust resonance even when truncation uncertainties are included.
