# Global quark polarization in non-central $A+A$ collisions

> Partons produced in the early stage of non-central heavy-ion collisions can develop a longitudinal fluid shear because of unequal local number densities of participant target and projectile nucleons. Under such fluid shear, local parton pairs with non-vanishing impact parameter have finite local relative orbital angular momentum along the direction opposite to the reaction plane. Such finite relative orbital angular momentum among locally interacting quark pairs can lead to global quark polarization along the same direction due to spin-orbital coupling. Local longitudinal fluid shear is estimated within both Landau fireball and Bjorken scaling model of initial parton production. Quark polarization through quark-quark scatterings with the exchange of a thermal gluon is calculated beyond small-angle scattering approximation in a quark-gluon plasma. The polarization is shown to have a non-monotonic dependence on the local relative orbital angular momentum dictated by the interplay between electric and magnetic interaction. It peaks at a value of relative orbital angular momentum which scales with the magnetic mass of the exchanged gluons. With the estimated small longitudinal fluid shear in semi-peripheral $Au+Au$ collisions at the RHIC energy, the final quark polarization is found to be small $|P_q|<0.04$ in the weak coupling limit. Possible behavior of the quark polarization in the strong coupling limit and implications on the experimental detection of such global quark polarization at RHIC and LHC are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that global quark polarization in non-central heavy-ion collisions arises from spin-orbital coupling during quark-quark scatterings with a finite relative orbital angular momentum, but the final polarization is small ($|P_q| < 0.04$) in the weak coupling limit for semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, contrary to earlier estimates that could exceed unity.

## Method

The authors estimated the longitudinal fluid shear in two models of parton production: a Landau fireball model using wounded nucleon distributions with both hard-sphere and Woods-Saxon nuclear geometries, and a Bjorken scaling scenario using HIJING Monte Carlo simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. They then calculated quark polarization via quark-quark scatterings with exchange of a Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagator, going beyond the small-angle scattering approximation used in earlier work (Liang 2004). The calculation was performed in impact parameter space by Fourier transforming the momentum-space scattering amplitudes, with the relative orbital angular momentum fixed by the longitudinal fluid shear.

## Key numbers

* Total orbital angular momentum $L_y$ in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy is of order $10^4$ at most impact parameters, with significant differences between Woods-Saxon and hard-sphere nuclear distributions.
* For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, the number of charged hadrons per participating nucleon is about 15, giving $c(s) \simeq 45$ (including neutral hadrons), leading to $dp_0/dx \simeq 0.34$ GeV/fm and $l_0 \equiv -(\Delta x)^2 dp_0/dx \simeq -1.7$ for $\Delta x=1$ fm.
* In the Bjorken scaling scenario from HIJING simulations, the average longitudinal momentum shear in the central rapidity region is $\partial \langle p_z\rangle/\partial x \sim 0.003$ GeV/fm, much smaller than the Landau fireball model estimate.
* The final quark polarization in the weak coupling limit is $|P_q| < 0.04$ for semi-peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy.

## Context

This work extends the initial proposal by Liang (2004) that global orbital angular momentum in non-central heavy-ion collisions could lead to quark polarization via spin-orbital coupling, but corrects the earlier small-angle approximation which could give unphysical polarization values exceeding 1. The calculation uses HTL resummed gluon propagators (Weldon 1982) and introduces a non-perturbative magnetic screening mass $\mu_m \approx 0.255\sqrt{N_c/2} g^2 T$ (TBBM 1993) to regularize infrared divergences in the magnetic sector. The results enable experimental searches for global hyperon polarization and vector meson spin alignment at RHIC and LHC, with preliminary measurements already reported by the STAR collaboration (Abelev 2007).
