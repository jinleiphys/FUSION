# Shell effects on the drift and fluctuation in multinucleon transfer reactions

> This study employs the dinuclear system model (DNS-sysu) to investigate drift and fluctuation mechanisms in 136Xe + 209Bi collisions above the Coulomb barrier. The DNS-sysu model demonstrated its effectiveness in providing reasonable descriptions of drift and fluctuation dynamics for the multinucleon transfer reaction at low energies. We observe temperature-induced changes in the shell effect, impacting nucleon transfer. At higher energies, the weakening constraint of the potential energy surface leads to a reversal in the evolution direction. Additionally, the consideration of shell corrections notably affects fragment distribution at low energies but diminishes for high-energy conditions. This research provides valuable insights into understanding the macroscopic manifestation of nucleon transfer in the multinucleon transfer reaction.

## Key claim
The DNS-sysu model provides reasonable descriptions of drift and fluctuation dynamics for the 136Xe + 209Bi multinucleon transfer reaction at low energies, but the shell effect on fragment distribution diminishes at high incident energies and the constraint of the potential energy surface weakens, causing a reversal in the evolution direction of the average proton number.

## Method
The authors employed the dinuclear system model (DNS-sysu) with a master equation framework to solve the time-dependent joint probability distribution of projectile-like fragment proton number, neutron number, and deformation. The potential energy surface included temperature-dependent shell and pairing corrections, and the de-excitation of primary fragments was treated using the Gemini++ code. Calculations were performed for 136Xe + 209Bi collisions at three center-of-mass energies above the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers
- Three collision energies studied: Ec.m. = 569, 684, and 861 MeV for the 136Xe + 209Bi system
- For initial angular momentum J = 50 ħ, the maximal average proton number Z̅ of primary PLF increased from 57.3 at Ec.m. = 500 MeV to 59.9 at Ec.m. = 684 MeV, then decreased at Ec.m. = 861 MeV
- Experimental detection conditions: 40° < θc.m. < 100° and 23 MeV < TKEL < 309 MeV for Ec.m. = 569 MeV; 25° < θc.m. < 75° and 34 MeV < TKEL < 384 MeV for Ec.m. = 684 MeV; 18° < θc.m. < 128° and 51 MeV < TKEL < 601 MeV for Ec.m. = 861 MeV
- The contact radius Rcont was calculated as R1 + R2 + 0.7 fm, where R1,2 = 1.16 A1,2^(1/3)

## Context
This work extends the DNS-sysu model previously described by Zhu (2021) to study drift and fluctuation in multinucleon transfer reactions, building on the Fokker-Planck approach introduced by Nörenberg (1974) for the 40Ar + 232Th reaction. The study uses experimental data from Schröder (1978) and subsequent works on 136Xe + 209Bi to test the model. The results enable future investigation of whether shell effects persist in inverse quasi-fission reactions aimed at reaching the superheavy stable island, where dozens of nucleons are exchanged to form highly excited systems.
