# Charm quark evolution in the early stages of heavy-ion collisions

> Heavy quarks are predominantly generated at the initial stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions such that heavy flavor observables have the potential to provide information on the pre-equilibrium medium dynamics. In this study, we investigate the sensitivity of D-meson $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ to early-time charm quark dynamics in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. We employ the IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD framework to model the evolution of the bulk medium. Charm quarks are generated using PYTHIA with nuclear parton distribution functions and evolved using Langevin dynamics within MARTINI. We observe that even though there is significant momentum broadening in the earliest stage, D-meson $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ are only weakly sensitive to pre-equilibrium interactions.

## Key claim

Even though charm quarks experience significant momentum broadening during the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase (with the ratio of non-equilibrium to equilibrium transverse momentum broadening approximately 0.8-1 depending on centrality), the final D-meson nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) are only weakly sensitive to these pre-equilibrium interactions, with the effect on \(R_{AA}\) being smaller than typical model systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors employed the IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD multi-stage framework to model the bulk medium evolution in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV. Charm quarks were generated using PYTHIA with EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions at binary collision positions from IP-Glasma, then evolved via Langevin dynamics within MARTINI through both the Glasma phase (from formation time \(\tau_F = 1/m_c\) until \(\tau = 0.4\) fm) and the subsequent hydrodynamically expanding QGP phase, using a spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_s\) parametrized from \(N_f=2+1\) lattice QCD results. Hadronization combined a modified color evaporation model for charmonium with coalescence and Peterson fragmentation for D-mesons.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of non-equilibrium to equilibrium transverse momentum broadening: \(\Delta p_T^2|^{\text{non-eq}} / \Delta p_T^2|^{\text{eq}} \approx 0.8-1\), depending on centrality
- Hydrodynamic switching time from IP-Glasma to MUSIC: \(\tau = 0.4\) fm
- Hadronization temperature: \(T_h = 165\) MeV
- Charm quark formation time in rest frame: \(\tau_F = 1/m_c\)
- Coalescence normalization parameter: \(N_{coal} = 0.2\) (tuned to experimental data)
- Peterson fragmentation parameter: \(\bar{\varepsilon} = 0.15\)
- Lattice QCD temperature range for \(D_s\) extraction: \(195\) MeV \(< T < 352\) MeV with pion mass \(320\) MeV
- Thermal mass of light quarks for coalescence: \(m_{u/d} = 300\) MeV
- Angular frequency of oscillator in coalescence Wigner function: \(\omega = 0.106\) GeV

## Context

This study extends the IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD framework (Schenke 2020) to include charm quark dynamics during the pre-equilibrium Glasma phase, building on recent work by Boguslavski (2023), Du (2023), and Carrington (2022) that suggested pre-equilibrium effects could affect heavy flavor observables despite the short duration of this phase. The finding that D-meson \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) are only weakly sensitive to pre-equilibrium evolution, despite significant momentum broadening, contradicts expectations from those earlier transport coefficient studies and indicates that experimental discrimination of pre-equilibrium effects on charm quarks will be challenging. This work enables future studies of two-particle azimuthal correlations as potentially more sensitive observables to pre-equilibrium dynamics.
