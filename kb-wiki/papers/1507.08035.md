# Nuclear shape coexistence in Po isotopes: An interacting boson model study

> Background: The lead region, Po, Pb, Hg, and Pt, shows up the presence of coexisting structures having different deformation and corresponding to different particle-hole configurations in the Shell Model language. Purpose: We intend to study the importance of configuration mixing in the understanding of the nuclear structure of even-even Po isotopes, where the shape coexistence phenomena are not clear enough. Method: We study in detail a long chain of polonium isotopes, 190-208Po, using the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM). We fix the parameters of the Hamiltonians through a least-squares fit to the known energies and absolute B(E2) transition rates of states up to 3 MeV. Results: We obtained the IBM-CM Hamiltonians and we calculate excitation energies, B(E2)'s, electric quadrupole moments, nuclear radii and isotopic shifts, quadrupole shape invariants, wave functions, and deformations. Conclusions: We obtain a good agreement with the experimental data for all the studied observables and we conclude that shape coexistence phenomenon is hidden in Po isotopes, very much as in the case of the Pt isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that shape coexistence is "hidden" in Po isotopes, meaning that unlike in Pb and Hg where distinct intruder bands are easily identified, in Po the regular and intruder configurations mix so strongly that the ground state itself has a mixed character rather than being purely intruder, and the parabolic energy systematics characteristic of shape coexistence are not immediately visible.

## Method

The authors performed an interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) study of even-even polonium isotopes from 190Po to 208Po, including both the regular [N] boson space (proton 2p configurations) and the intruder [N+2] boson space (proton 2h-4p configurations) with a mixing interaction between them. They fixed the 8 free parameters of the Hamiltonian and the E2 transition operator for each isotope through a least-squares fit to known excitation energies (up to 3 MeV) and absolute B(E2) transition rates, using the MINUIT package to minimize a chi-squared function with assigned sigma weights for each state.

## Key numbers

- The energy needed to create a 2p-2h excitation across the Z=82 shell gap (the offset parameter Delta^{N+2}) was fixed to 2400 keV for all Po isotopes, compared to 2800 keV for Pt and 3480 keV for Hg.
- The mixing strengths between regular and intruder configurations were fixed to w0^{N,N+2} = w2^{N,N+2} = 30 keV for all Po isotopes, compared to 50 keV for Pt, 18 keV for Pb, and 20 keV for Hg.
- The experimental 2+_1 energy drops from approximately 600-650 keV in heavier Po isotopes to about 300 keV at mass A=194, and further to about 250 keV for the lightest known isotopes down to A=190.
- The first excited 0+ state drops from 1758 keV at mass A=202 to 558 keV at mass A=196.
- The experimental B(E2; 2+_1 -> 0+_1) for 194Po is 90(20) W.u., reproduced by the IBM-CM calculation as 90 W.u.

## Context

This work extends previous IBM-CM studies of shape coexistence in the lead region, specifically the detailed studies of Pt isotopes by Garcia-Ramos et al. (2009, 2011, 2014) and Hg isotopes by Garcia-Ramos et al. (2014), as well as earlier particle-core coupling calculations by Bernthal et al. (1995) and Young et al. (1995, 1997) and mean-field studies by Oros et al. (1999) and Smirnova et al. (2003). The results enable a unified description of shape coexistence across the entire Pb region (Po, Pb, Hg, Pt) and show that the Po isotopes behave similarly to Pt, where shape coexistence is hidden by strong mixing, in contrast to Pb and Hg where intruder bands are clearly separated. The authors note that near the shell closure (N=126), the IBM-CM calculations produce intruder state energies lower than experimentally observed, indicating a deficiency of the model in that region.
