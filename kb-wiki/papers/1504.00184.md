# Medium effects on the relaxation of dissipative flows in a hot pion gas

> The relaxation times over which dissipative fluxes restore their steady state values have been evaluated for a pion gas using the 14-moment method. The effect of the medium has been implemented through a temperature dependent pi-pi cross-section in the collision integral which is obtained by including one-loop self-energies in the propagators of the exchanged rho and sigma mesons. To account for chemical freeze out in heavy ion collisions, a temperature dependent pion chemical potential has been introduced in the distribution function. The temperature dependence of the relaxation times for shear and bulk viscous flows as well as the heat flow is significantly affected.

## Key claim

The relaxation times for shear and bulk viscous flows and heat flow in a hot pion gas are significantly modified when temperature-dependent in-medium pi-pi cross-sections are used in the collision integral, and these relaxation times are found to be of comparable magnitude to each other, contrary to the large separation seen in the first-order transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors used Grad's 14-moment method to solve the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport equation for a pion gas, obtaining equations of motion for the dissipative fluxes that include time derivatives and cross-couplings. The collision integral employed a temperature-dependent pi-pi cross-section derived from an effective Lagrangian with rho and sigma meson exchange, where the meson propagators were modified by one-loop self-energy corrections evaluated at finite temperature using thermal field theory in the real-time formalism. A temperature-dependent pion chemical potential was introduced in the Bose-Einstein distribution function to account for chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constants used in the effective Lagrangian are g_rho = 6.05 and g_sigma = 2.5.
- The temperature-dependent pion chemical potential is parameterized as mu(T) = a + bT + cT^2 + dT^3 with a = 0.824, b = 3.04, c = -0.028, d = 6.05 x 10^-5, where T and mu are in MeV.
- The constant pion chemical potential used for comparison is mu = 85 MeV, representative of kinetic freeze-out.
- The temperature-dependent chemical potential decreases from 85 MeV at kinetic freeze-out (T = 100 MeV) to zero at chemical freeze-out (T = 170 MeV).
- The relaxation times for bulk viscous flow, heat flow, and shear viscous flow are plotted over a temperature range and are found to be of similar magnitude on the same scale.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in a pion gas by Prakash (1993), Davesne (1996), and others, which used vacuum pi-pi cross-sections, to include medium effects on the relaxation times. The authors had previously studied medium effects on viscosities and thermal conductivity using the Chapman-Enskog approach (Mitra, 2012; Mitra, 2013). The results provide input parameters for second-order viscous hydrodynamic equations used in heavy ion collision simulations, where the comparable magnitude of all three relaxation times suggests that bulk viscosity and heat flow should not be ignored in dissipative hydrodynamic simulations.
