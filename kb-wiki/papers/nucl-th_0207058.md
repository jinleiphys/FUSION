# The Lambda-Lambda Interaction and ^{6}_{Lambda Lambda}He

> An OBE potential model for the ^{1}S_0 S = -2 interaction is analyzed with emphasis on the role of coupling between the Lambda Lambda, N Xi, and Sigma Sigma channels. Singlet scalar exchange, an approximation to two-pion exchange, is significant in all channels; surprisingly, the one-pion exchange component is almost negligible. The size of the channel coupling as a function of the overall strength of the OBE model potential is examined. Implications of the analysis for the binding energy of ^{6}_{Lambda Lambda}He are considered; the new experimental datum may suggest a consistency between the extracted Lambda Lambda matrix element and the relation implied by SU(3) among OBE baryon-baryon interactions. \\

## Key claim

The new measurement of the Lambda-Lambda separation energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe (1.01 ± 0.20 +0.18/-0.11 MeV) suggests the effective Lambda-Lambda interaction is much weaker than earlier measurements indicated (≈ 4.7 MeV), and if confirmed, would be consistent with the SU(3) prediction that the Lambda-Lambda interaction is weaker than both the nucleon-nucleon and Lambda-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential for the ¹S₀ S = -2 interaction based on the Nijmegen Model D, using SU(3) symmetry to determine coupling constants from the S=0 and S=-1 sectors. They varied a single short-range repulsion parameter C while keeping the long-range OBE tail fixed, and examined the role of channel coupling between the ΛΛ, NΞ, and ΣΣ channels by computing scattering lengths and binding energies of ⁶_ΛΛHe.

## Key numbers

- The new experimental ΛΛ separation energy in ⁶_ΛΛHe is ΔB_ΛΛ = 1.01 ± 0.20 +0.18/-0.11 MeV, compared to the earlier measurement by Prowse (1966) of ≈ 4.7 MeV.
- For a ΛΛ scattering length a_ΛΛ = -1.91 fm, the ⁶_ΛΛHe binding energy is 10.007 MeV with full coupling and 9.738 MeV with coupling only to NΞ; the ΔB matrix element is 3.60 MeV.
- For a_ΛΛ = -0.5 fm, the potential is 30% shallower than for a_ΛΛ = -1.91 fm, and the role of channel coupling is reduced.
- The new experimental binding energy of ⁶_ΛΛHe (7.25 ± 0.2 +0.18/-0.11 MeV) implies a ΛΛ scattering length of approximately -0.5 fm.
- The ΣΣ threshold lies about 160 MeV above the ΛΛ threshold, yet coupling to ΣΣ remains important for larger scattering lengths.

## Context

This work extends the OBE approach of Carr et al. (1997) and the Nijmegen Model D (Nagels et al., 1977) to analyze the S = -2 baryon-baryon interaction. It contradicts the earlier interpretation from Prowse (1966) and other measurements of ΛΛ hypernuclei (D63, D89, A90, A91, D91) that implied a strong ΛΛ matrix element of 4-5 MeV. The analysis enables future calculations of light double-Lambda hypernuclei without needing to include NΞ and ΣΣ channel coupling if the weak interaction is confirmed, and suggests that explicit two-pion exchange should be included in future OBE models.
