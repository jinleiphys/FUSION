# Operator interpretation of resonances arising in spectral problems for 2x2 matrix Hamiltonians

> We consider the analytic continuation of the transfer function for a 2x2 matrix Hamiltonian into the unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface. We construct non-selfadjoint operators representing operator roots of the transfer function which reproduce certain parts of its spectrum including resonances situated in the unphysical sheets neighboring the physical sheet. On this basis, completeness and basis properties for the root vectors of the transfer function (including those for the resonances) are proved.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for a 2x2 matrix Hamiltonian whose off-diagonal couplings satisfy certain analyticity and bounded-variation conditions, the resonances (discrete spectrum of the transfer function on unphysical sheets) can be represented as eigenvalues of non-selfadjoint operator roots of the transfer function, and that the root vectors of these operators (including those for resonances) form a complete and even a basis in the Hilbert space.

## Method

The authors analytically continue the transfer function \(M_1(z)=A_1-z+V_1(z)\) through the absolutely continuous spectrum of the entry \(A_0\) into the adjacent unphysical sheets of the energy Riemann surface, using a contour integral representation of the self-energy term \(V_1(z)\). They then construct non-selfadjoint operators \(H_1^{(l)}=A_1+X^{(l)}\) (for \(l=\pm1\)) that satisfy the nonlinear equation \(X=V_1(A_1+X,\Gamma)\) for a suitable contour \(\Gamma\), and prove that these operators are operator roots of the continued transfer function, meaning that \(M_1(z,\Gamma^l)=W_1(z,\Gamma^l)(H_1^{(l)}-z)\). Completeness and basis properties of the root vectors are established using the theory of compact resolvents and spectral projections.

## Key numbers

* The solvability condition for the basic equation is \(\cV_0(B,\Gamma) < d_0^2(\Gamma)/4\), where \(\cV_0(B,\Gamma)\) is the variation of the function \(K_B(\mu)\) along the contour \(\Gamma\) and \(d_0(\Gamma)=\dist\{\sigma(A_1),\Gamma\}\).
* The solution \(X\) lies in a ball of radius \(r_{\rm min}(\Gamma)=d_0(\Gamma)/2 - \sqrt{d_0^2(\Gamma)/4 - \cV_0(B,\Gamma)}\).
* The spectrum of \(H_1^{(l)}\) lies in the closed \(r_0(B)\)-vicinity of the spectrum of \(A_1\), where \(r_0(B)=\Inf\limits_{\Gamma^l:\,\omega(B,\Gamma^l)>0} r_{\rm min}(\Gamma^l)\) and \(\omega(B,\Gamma^l)=d_0^2(\Gamma^l)-4\cV_0(B,\Gamma^l)\).
* For the multichannel Schrodinger example with Laplacian \(A_0=-\Delta\) in \(L_2(\mathbb{R}^n)\), the derivative of the spectral function is \(K'_B(\mu)=\frac{1}{2}\mu^{(n-2)/2}\int_{S^{n-1}}d\widehat{p}\,b(\mu^{1/2}\widehat{p})\langle\cdot,b(\mu^{1/2}\widehat{p})\rangle_{\cH_1}\) for \(\mu>0\).

## Context

This work extends the operator Riccati equation approach of Motovilov and Mennicken (1998) and the earlier results of Adamyan, Langer, Mennicken, and Shkalikov (1990s) on invariant subspaces of matrix Hamiltonians to the case where the spectra of the diagonal entries overlap. It provides a rigorous operator-theoretic framework for treating resonances in multichannel Schrodinger equations, complementing the standard resonance theory presented in Reed and Simon (1978). The results enable the application of operator theory methods (Kato, 1966; Gohberg and Krein, 1969) to study completeness and basis properties of resonance states, with immediate applications to scattering problems.
