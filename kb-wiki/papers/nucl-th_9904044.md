# (Hybrid) Baryons: Symmetries and Masses

## Key claim

The lowest mass hybrid baryon is estimated at approximately 2 GeV, specifically 1976 MeV, which is substantially higher than the bag model estimate of about 1.5 GeV and QCD sum rule estimate of 1.5 ± 10% GeV; this result depends on the adiabatic approximation for quark motion and the small oscillation approximation for flux motion, both of which are partially or fully lifted in this work.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid baryons in the non-relativistic flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton, where the flux settles in a Mercedes-Benz configuration and the junction acquires an effective mass from the motion of the remainder of the flux-tube and the quarks. They build proper eigenstates of orbital angular momentum and determine the flavour, spin, and J^P of hybrid baryons from the exchange symmetry and parity symmetries of the system. The hybrid baryon mass is obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with a 95 × 95 dimensional matrix of spin-space basis states incorporating L_q = 0, 1, 2 harmonic oscillator wave functions, adding the energy difference to the spin-averaged mass of the N and Delta baryons (1085 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Lowest hybrid baryon mass: 1976 MeV, obtained from M_H1 - M_B = 891 MeV added to the spin-averaged N and Delta mass of 1085 MeV
- First three quark orbital excitations (L_q = 0, 1, 2) of hybrid baryons composed of up and down quarks: 1976, 2341, and 2619 MeV respectively
- Root-mean-square size of the low-lying baryon: sqrt(⟨ρ^2⟩) = sqrt(⟨λ^2⟩) = 2.12 GeV^{-1}
- Root-mean-square size of the H_1 hybrid baryon: sqrt(⟨ρ^2⟩) = sqrt(⟨λ^2⟩) = 2.52 GeV^{-1}, making the hybrid baryon 20% larger than the conventional baryon

## Context

This work extends the flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton (1985) from hybrid mesons to hybrid baryons, and differs from the bag model predictions of Barnes and Close (1983) in the flavour and spin assignments of the low-lying hybrid baryons. The mass estimate of 1976 MeV is considerably higher than the bag model estimate of about 1.5 GeV and the QCD sum rule estimate of 1.5 ± 10% GeV from Zhu, P. and Li (1998). The results enable experimental searches for hybrid baryons in Nρ and Nω decay channels at TJNAF and in πN scattering at Brookhaven, as well as in ψ decays at BEPC and future tau-charm factories.
