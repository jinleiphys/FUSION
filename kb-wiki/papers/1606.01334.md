# Charmonium dissociation cross sections and charmonium dissociation rates in hadronic matter

> K*-charmonium dissociation reactions in hadronic matter are studied in the Born approximation, in the quark-interchange mechanism, and with a temperature-dependent quark potential. We obtain the temperature dependence of unpolarized cross sections for K*-charmonium dissociation reactions which produce charmed mesons and charmed strange mesons. We use the cross sections for charmonium dissociation in collisions with pion, rho meson, kaon, vector kaon, and eta meson to calculate dissociation rates of charmonium with the five types of mesons. Because of the temperature dependence of the meson masses, dissociation cross sections, and meson distribution functions, the charmonium dissociation rates generally increase with the increase of temperature and decrease with the increase of charmonium momentum from 2.2 GeV/c. We find that the first derivative of the dissociation rate with respect to the charmonium momentum is zero when the charmonium is at rest. While the eta + psi' and eta + chi_c dissociation reactions can be neglected, the J/psi, psi', and chi_c dissociation are caused by the collisions with pion, rho meson, kaon, vector kaon, and eta meson.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of temperature-dependent unpolarized cross sections for twelve $K^*$-charmonium dissociation reactions in hadronic matter using the quark-interchange mechanism, but the results are limited to the Born approximation and a specific temperature-dependent quark potential, with the caveat that the $\eta + \psi'$ and $\eta + \chi_c$ dissociation reactions can be neglected.

## Method

The authors compute unpolarized cross sections for $K^*J/\psi$, $K^*\psi'$, and $K^*\chi_c$ dissociation into charmed mesons ($\bar{D}$, $\bar{D}^*$, $D_s^+$, $D_s^{*+}$) using the Born approximation with the quark-interchange mechanism and a temperature-dependent central spin-independent potential plus spin-spin interaction. They solve the Schrodinger equation with this potential to obtain mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions, then calculate transition amplitudes in both prior and post forms, averaging them to obtain unpolarized cross sections at six temperatures ($T/T_c = 0$, 0.65, 0.75, 0.85, 0.9, 0.95). These cross sections are then used together with previously calculated cross sections for pion, rho meson, kaon, and eta meson dissociation of charmonia to compute charmonium dissociation rates in hadronic matter.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: up/down quark 0.32 GeV, strange quark 0.5 GeV, charm quark 1.51 GeV
- Critical temperature $T_c = 0.175$ GeV
- Confining potential parameter $D = 0.7$ GeV
- Charmonium dissociation rates generally decrease with increasing charmonium momentum from 2.2 GeV/c
- The first derivative of the dissociation rate with respect to charmonium momentum is zero when the charmonium is at rest
- Experimental mass splitting $m_{K^*} - m_K = 0.3963$ GeV is large, leading to larger cross sections for exothermic $K^*$ + charmonium reactions compared to $K$ + charmonium reactions with the same final states at $T=0$
- At $T/T_c = 0.95$, all $K^*$ + charmonium reactions become endothermic

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium dissociation in hadronic matter by the same group (Xu and collaborators, 2015-2016) that provided cross sections for $\pi$, $\rho$, $K$, and $\eta$ meson dissociation of charmonia, now adding $K^*$ meson dissociation. It contrasts with the meson-exchange approach of Haglin (2000) which considered $K^*+J/\psi$ dissociation in vacuum but presented no cross sections, and with earlier quark-interchange studies (Barnes and Swanson, 1992; Wong, 2000) that focused on vacuum dissociation. The results enable more complete modeling of charmonium suppression in hadronic matter produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions, helping to separate suppression due to hadronic matter from that due to quark-gluon plasma.
