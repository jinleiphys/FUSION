# Volume fluctuation and multiplicity correlation on higher-order cumulants

> Initial volume fluctuation (VF) arising from the participant fluctuation would be the background which should be subtracted experimentally from the measured higher-order cumulants. We study the validity of the Volume Fluctuation Correction (VFC) on higher-order net-proton cumulants by using simple toy model and UrQMD model in Au+Au collisions at \sqrt s_{NN} = 200 GeV for various centrality definitions. The results are compared to the conventional data driven method called Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC). We find VFC works well in toy model assuming independent particle production (IPP), but does not seem to work well in UrQMD model. It is also found that cumulants are strongly affected by the multiplicity correlation effect as well as the centrality resolution effect. These results show that neither VFC nor CBWC are perfect method. Thus, both methods should be compared in the real experiment.

## Key claim

The Volume Fluctuation Correction (VFC) works well only under the assumption of independent particle production (IPP), which holds in a simple toy model but is broken in the more realistic UrQMD model; neither VFC nor the conventional Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) is a perfect method for removing volume fluctuation backgrounds from higher-order cumulant measurements.

## Method

The authors studied net-proton cumulants up to fourth order in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV using a toy model with Glauber geometry and independent Poisson particle production, and the UrQMD microscopic transport model with 45 million events. They applied both VFC (which subtracts participant-number cumulant terms derived from IPP) and CBWC (which averages cumulants over multiplicity bins) for various centrality definitions using different pseudorapidity windows, and compared results to "CBWC-N" baselines where cumulants are binned directly by the true number of participants N_W.

## Key numbers

- UrQMD model: 45 million events of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Net-proton cumulants measured in kinematic range |y| < 0.5 and 0.4 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c
- Centrality definitions tested in pseudorapidity windows: |eta| < 1, 1 < |eta| < 2, 2 < |eta| < 3, 3 < |eta| < 4, and 4 < |eta| < 5
- EPD-like centrality definition tested: 2 < |eta| < 5 using pi+/- and K+/- multiplicities
- Toy model: 100 million events with Glauber parameters from STAR net-proton analysis at 200 GeV
- Centrality bin widths tested: 10%, 5%, and 2.5% steps

## Context

This work extends the study of volume fluctuation corrections proposed by the VFC method (2018) and the conventional CBWC method used by the STAR experiment (Xiaofeng Luo, 2015) for higher-order cumulant analysis in heavy-ion collisions. The authors show that while VFC works perfectly in toy models assuming IPP, it fails in UrQMD where IPP is broken, and that multiplicity correlation effects (distinct from auto-correlation) significantly affect measured cumulants depending on the pseudorapidity window used for centrality determination. This study enables future experimental analyses at RHIC BES-II to properly compare both correction methods and account for centrality definition dependence when using the Event Plane Detector (EPD) with acceptance 2.1 < |eta| < 5.1.
