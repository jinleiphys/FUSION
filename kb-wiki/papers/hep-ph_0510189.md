# Leading twist moments of the neutron structure function F2n

> We perform a global analysis of neutron F2n structure function data, obtained by combining proton and deuteron measurements over a large range of kinematics. From these data the lowest moments (n <= 10) of the leading twist neutron F2n structure function are extracted. Particular attention is paid to nuclear effects in the deuteron, which become increasingly important for the higher moments. Our results for the nonsinglet, isovector (p - n) combination of the leading twist moments are used to test recent lattice simulations. We also determine the lowest few moments of the higher twist contributions, and find these to be approximately isospin independent, suggesting the possible dominance of ud correlations over uu and dd in the nucleon.

# Leading twist moments of the neutron structure function F2n

## Key claim
The authors extract the lowest moments (n ≤ 10) of the leading twist neutron F2n structure function from a global analysis of proton and deuteron data, but the extraction depends critically on the treatment of nuclear effects in the deuteron, particularly off-shell corrections, which become increasingly important for higher moments and introduce systematic uncertainties that the abstract does not quantify.

## Method
The analysis combines world data on proton and deuteron F2 structure functions over a wide kinematic range, including high-precision Jefferson Lab data covering the resonance region up to invariant masses of about 3.5 GeV/c². The neutron moments are extracted by solving the convolution equation M_n^D = F_n^D M_n^N + δC_n δM_n^N, where F_n^D are moments of the nucleon light-cone momentum distribution in the deuteron, and off-shell corrections are modeled using both nonrelativistic (Kulagin-Petti, 2004) and relativistic (Melnitchouk-Sargsian-Terry, 2004) approaches. Nuclear shadowing and meson exchange current corrections are also included for the lowest moments.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy ε_D = -2.22 MeV, used in the nonrelativistic convolution formalism
- Off-shell correction parameterization δf₂ = C_N (x - x₁)(x - x₀)(1 + x₀ - x) with C_N = 8.1 ± 0.3_stat ± 0.5_syst, x₁ = 0.05, and x₀ = 0.448 ± 0.005_stat ± 0.007_syst (Kulagin-Petti, 2004)
- Off-shell correction Δ^(off)_n for n=2: -5.67×10⁻³ (relativistic model) and +5.08×10⁻³ (nonrelativistic model), showing opposite signs for the lowest moment
- Off-shell correction Δ^(off)_n for n=10: -1.53×10⁻² (relativistic model) and -3.97×10⁻² (nonrelativistic model), showing factor ~2.6 difference
- Shadowing correction Δ^(shad)_n for n=2: -1.0×10⁻⁴, negligible for n≥4 (≤ 4.1×10⁻⁷)
- Meson exchange current correction Δ^(MEC)_n for n=2: +2.6×10⁻⁴, negligible for n≥4 (≤ 1.6×10⁻⁷)
- Momentum integration cutoff at p_max = 0.5 GeV affects the n=10 moment by 6%, while p_max = 1 GeV cutoff affects it by 1%

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of neutron structure functions that ignored or underestimated nuclear corrections in the deuteron, particularly the analyses of Osipenko et al. (2003, 2004) for proton and deuteron F2 moments. The extracted nonsinglet isovector (p-n) leading twist moments are used to test recent lattice QCD simulations. The finding that higher twist contributions are approximately isospin independent suggests possible dominance of ud diquark correlations over uu and dd correlations in the nucleon, which can be tested by future lattice calculations and by higher-precision Jefferson Lab experiments at large x.
