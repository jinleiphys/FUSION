# Thermal Model Description of Collisions of Small Nuclei

> The dependence of particle production on the size of the colliding nuclei is analysed in terms of the thermal model using the canonical ensemble. The concept of strangeness correlation in clusters of sub-volume $V_c$ is used to account for the suppression of strangeness. A systematic analysis is presented of the predictions of the thermal model for particle production in collisions of small nuclei. The pattern of the maxima of strange-particles-to-pion ratios as a function of beam energy is quite special, as they do not occur at the same beam energy and are sensitive to system size. In particular, the $Λ/π^+$ ratio shows a clear maximum even for small systems while the maximum in the K$^+/π^+$ ratio is less pronounced

## Key claim

The thermal model, using a canonical ensemble with a strangeness correlation volume \(V_c\), can describe the system-size dependence of strange-particle-to-pion ratios, predicting that the \(\Lambda/\pi^+\) ratio retains a clear maximum even for small systems while the maximum in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio gradually disappears as the correlation radius decreases. The authors emphasize that these results are qualitative, as variations of temperature and baryon chemical potential with system size were not taken into account.

## Method

The authors applied the thermal model in the canonical ensemble to describe particle production in collisions of small nuclei, using the concept of a strangeness correlation sub-volume \(V_c\) (parameterized by a radius \(R_c\)) to enforce exact strangeness conservation and account for strangeness suppression. They calculated particle ratios along the chemical freeze-out curve (using the freeze-out parameterization from Cleymans (2005) and Vovchenko (2015)) for various values of \(R_c\), keeping the overall fireball radius fixed at \(R = 10\) fm and \(\gamma_S = 1\), to illustrate the qualitative trend of how maxima in strange-particle-to-pion ratios evolve with system size.

## Key numbers

- The maxima in the \(K^+/\pi^+\) and \(\Lambda/\pi^\pm\) ratios occur at a center-of-mass energy of \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \approx 10\) GeV for large systems (e.g., Pb-Pb or Au-Au).
- For pp collisions, the strangeness correlation radius is about \(R_c \approx 1.5\) fm (citing Kraus (2007)).
- The overall fireball volume radius was fixed at \(R = 10\) fm for central Pb-Pb collisions.
- The Wroblewski factor \(\lambda_s\) shows a maximum as a function of beam energy along the chemical freeze-out curve, calculated using the THERMUS code.

## Context

This work extends the thermal model description of particle production, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Pb-Pb and Au-Au) by Cleymans (2005) and Andronic (2005), to collisions of smaller nuclei such as p-p and light-ion systems. It directly addresses the puzzling non-monotonic behavior of the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio observed in heavy-ion collisions, which has been conjectured as a signature of a phase change to deconfined matter by Gazdzicki (1998). The predictions are relevant for interpreting data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (Mustafa, 2015) and NA61 (Abgrall, 2014) experiments, and will be testable at future facilities like NICA and FAIR.
