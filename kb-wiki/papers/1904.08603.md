# Two-particle angular correlations in heavy ion collisions from a multiphase transport model

> We extend our earlier study on two-particle angular correlations in $pp$ collisions at low transverse momentum ($p_T$) to $p$-Pb, Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. We mainly use the string melting version of a multiphase transport model with improved quark coalescence for this study. We start from the analysis of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$ and $p$($\bar{p}$) $p_T$ and rapidity distributions at different centralities. We then focus on two-particle angular correlations in $p$-Pb collisions at $\mathrm{\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02}$ TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at $\mathrm{\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76}$ TeV. For $p$-Pb collisions, a near side depression in the angular correlation is observed for low $p_T$ proton pairs and $Λ$ pairs but not for pion pairs or kaon pairs, similar to our earlier finding for $pp$ collisions at $\mathrm{\sqrt{s}= 7}$ TeV. This is also the case for very low multiplicity Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions. We also find that parton interactions and the improved quark coalescence are mainly responsible for the depression feature in baryon pair angular correlations. However, no such baryon-baryon anti-correlations are observed in Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions at higher multiplicities. Therefore our results suggest that low $p_T$ baryon-baryon angular anti-correlations have a strong multiplicity dependence.

## Key claim

The paper extends earlier findings of low-\(p_T\) baryon-baryon angular anti-correlations from \(pp\) collisions to \(p\)-Pb, Pb-Pb, and Au-Au collisions using the AMPT model, but the depression feature is only present in very low multiplicity events; the authors hedge that the effect has a strong multiplicity dependence and is not observed in higher-multiplicity heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model (v2.31t1) with an improved quark coalescence scheme (newCoal) to simulate \(p\)-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, and Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) and 200 GeV. They first validated the model by comparing \(\pi^\pm\), \(K^\pm\), and \(p(\bar{p})\) transverse momentum and rapidity distributions against experimental data, then constructed two-particle angular correlation functions \(C(\Delta\phi)\) for same-charge and opposite-charge pairs of pions, kaons, protons, and \(\Lambda\) baryons, varying the parton cross section (0 or 3 mb) and hadronic cascade termination time (0, 30, or 60 fm/c) to isolate the contributions of parton interactions and quark coalescence.

## Key numbers

- In \(p\)-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, a near-side depression is observed for low-\(p_T\) proton-proton and \(\Lambda\)-\(\Lambda\) pairs, but not for pion pairs or kaon pairs.
- In Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the near-side depression in baryon-baryon correlations is absent for events with multiplicity > 5000 (charged tracks with \(|y| < 0.8\) and \(p_T < 2.5\) GeV/c).
- In very peripheral Pb-Pb and Au-Au collisions with multiplicity in the range (0, 200), the near-side depression reappears, and the correlation functions are similar across \(pp\), \(p\)-Pb, Pb-Pb, and Au-Au systems at the same low multiplicity.
- The AMPT model with new quark coalescence reduces the overestimation of proton yield in central Pb-Pb collisions from 50% (old coalescence) to 10% at midrapidity.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of two-particle angular correlations in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV by Zhang et al. (2018), which first showed that the AMPT model with new quark coalescence could qualitatively describe the baryon-baryon anti-correlation observed by the ALICE experiment. The present results contradict the expectation that such anti-correlations would persist in larger systems; instead, they show a strong multiplicity dependence, with the depression disappearing in central and semi-central heavy-ion collisions. This enables future experimental analyses at LHC and RHIC to test the multiplicity-driven origin of the effect and to further constrain the role of parton interactions and coalescence in particle production.
