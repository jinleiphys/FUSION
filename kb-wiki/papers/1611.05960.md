# Two Photon Transition Form Factor of $\bar{c}c $ Quarkonia

> The two photon transition of $\bar{c}c$ quarkonia are studied within a covariant approach based on the consistent truncation scheme of the quantum chromodynamics Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe--Salpeter equation for the mesons. We find the decay widths of $η_{c}^{} \to γγ$ and $χ_{c0,2}^{} \to γγ$ in good agreement with experimental data. The obtained transition form factor of $η_{c}^{} \to γγ^{\ast}$ for a wide range of space-like photon momentum transfer squared is also in agreement with the experimental findings of the BABAR experiment. As a by-product, the decay widths of $η_{b}^{},χ_{b0,2}^{} \to γγ$ and the transition form factor of $η_{b}^{}, χ_{c0,b0}^{} \toγγ^{\ast}$ are predicted, which await for experimental test.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the two-photon decay widths of ηc → γγ and χc0,2 → γγ calculated within a covariant Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach agree well with experimental data, but the ηc decay width is systematically larger than the world average value (5.1 ± 0.4 keV) and cannot accommodate the recent Lattice QCD result of 1.122(14) keV within a large parameter window.

## Method
The authors employ a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, together with the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex. The transition amplitude is computed via the impulse approximation using a momentum-space integration, with the effective gluon interaction modeled by an infrared constant Ansatz plus a perturbative ultraviolet term. The calculation is performed in Euclidean metric with the meson at rest, and the photon momenta are parameterized to handle one on-shell and one space-like virtual photon.

## Key numbers
- ηc → γγ decay width: 6.32–6.39 keV (for ω = 0.5–0.8 GeV), compared to experimental world average 5.1 ± 0.4 keV
- χc0 → γγ decay width: 2.06–2.39 keV (for ω = 0.5–0.8 GeV), compared to experimental 2.33 ± 0.42 keV
- χc2 → γγ decay width: 0.401–0.655 keV (for ω = 0.5–0.8 GeV), compared to experimental 0.63 ± 0.10 keV
- Ratio Γ(χc2 → γγ)/Γ(χc0 → γγ): 0.191–0.274 (for ω = 0.5–0.8 GeV), compared to experimental 0.27 ± 0.06
- Helicity ratio σ0/2 for χc2 → γγ: 0.00413 (0.413%) at ω = 0.8 GeV, consistent with prediction of about 0.5%
- ηc interaction radius: rηc = 0.154 fm, compared to experimental 0.17 ± 0.01 fm
- Charm quark mass at μ = 2 GeV: mc = 1.835 GeV
- ηc mass: 2.98 GeV (for all ω values tested)
- J/ψ mass: 3.13 GeV (for all ω values tested)
- ηc decay constant: fηc = 0.279 GeV (for ω = 0.8 GeV)
- J/ψ decay constant: fJ/ψ = 0.303 GeV (for ω = 0.8 GeV)
- χc0 mass: 3.33 GeV (for ω = 0.8 GeV)
- χc2 mass: 3.56 GeV (for ω = 0.8 GeV)
- π0 transition form factor at zero momentum: Fπ0(0) = 0.507
- π0 mass: 0.138 GeV, decay constant: fπ = 0.093 GeV (for ω = 0.5 GeV, Dω = (0.80 GeV)³)
- Parameter δ in the ηc decay width relation: δ = 0.03

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach, previously applied to light meson electromagnetic form factors by Maris and Tandy (2000) and to pion transition form factors by Chang et al. (2013), to heavy charmonium and bottomonium two-photon decays. The calculation uses a consistent rainbow-ladder truncation that preserves gauge invariance for the three-point function, unlike earlier DSE studies of heavy quarkonia from nearly 20 years ago that used model expressions for the quark-photon vertex. The results contradict the recent Lattice QCD calculation (2016) giving Γ(ηc → γγ) = 1.122(14) keV, and the authors call for more precise experimental measurements to resolve the discrepancy. The work also provides predictions for bottomonium decays (ηb, χb0, χb2 → γγ) that await experimental verification.
