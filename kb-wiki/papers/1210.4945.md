# Medium-induced splitting kernels from SCET_G

> Using the framework of soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons ($\SCETG$), we evaluate medium-induced splitting kernels. Because of the power counting of the effective theory, our results are valid for arbitrary, not necessarily small values of the energy fraction $x$ taken by the emitted parton. In this framework we prove the factorization from the hard process and gauge invariance of the splitting kernels, we also show how nuclear recoil and the phase space cuts can be implemented into the phenomenology.

## Key claim
The paper derives medium-induced splitting kernels using soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluons (SCET_G) that are valid for arbitrary values of the energy fraction \(x\) taken by the emitted parton, not only the small-\(x\) approximation used in prior energy loss calculations. The results are proven to be gauge invariant and factorized from the hard scattering process.

## Method
The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian for SCET_G that includes terms for elastic scattering of collinear quark and gluon fields off of medium sources via Glauber gluons, using a hybrid gauge (covariant gauge for Glauber gluons, light-cone gauge for collinear gluons). They then evaluated single and double Born Feynman diagrams at first order in opacity for four splitting channels (\(q\rightarrow qg\), \(g\rightarrow gg\), \(g\rightarrow q\overline{q}\), \(q\rightarrow gq\)), expressing the results through five phase factors \(\Phi_k\) and splitting-dependent coefficients \(\alpha_k^{(i)}\).

## Key numbers
- The medium-induced splitting kernel for any splitting is given by \(\frac{{\rm{d}}N^{(i)}}{{\rm{d}}x \,{\rm{d}}^2\vc{k}_{\perp}}=\frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi^2}\,{P^{(i)}_{{\rm{vac}}}(x)}\int \frac{{{\rm{d}}\Delta z}}{\lambda_{i}(z)}\,{\rm{d}}^2\vc{q}_{\perp}\frac{1}{\sigma_{{\rm{el}}}}\frac{{\rm{d}}\sigma_{{\rm{el}}}}{{\rm{d}}^2\vc{q}_{\perp}}\,\sum_{k=1}^5\,\alpha_k^{(i)}(1-\cos\Phi_k)\), where \(\Psi=\frac{\Delta z}{x(1-x)\,\overline{n}\mcdot p_0}\).
- The scattering length \(\lambda_i\) equals the gluon scattering length \(\lambda_g\) for \(q\rightarrow qg\) and \(g\rightarrow gg\) splittings, and the quark scattering length \(\lambda_q\) for \(g\rightarrow q\overline{q}\) and \(q\rightarrow gq\) splittings.
- For the \(q\rightarrow qg\) splitting, the five coefficients \(\alpha_k^{(1)}\) are given explicitly in terms of transverse momentum vectors \(\vc{A}_{\perp}=\vc{k}_{\perp}\), \(\vc{B}_{\perp}=\vc{k}_{\perp}+x\,\vc{q}_{\perp}\), \(\vc{C}_{\perp}=\vc{k}_{\perp}-(1-x)\,\vc{q}_{\perp}\), and \(\vc{D}_{\perp}=\vc{k}_{\perp}-\vc{q}_{\perp}\).
- Numerical results for \(q\rightarrow qg\) splitting intensity \(xdN/dx\) are shown for initial quark energy \(E_0\) and effective gluon mass \(\mu=1\,\)GeV, with phase-space cuts such as \(q_{\perp {\rm{max}}}=\sqrt{E_0\mu/2}\) and \(k_{\perp {\rm{max}}}=E_0\sqrt{x(1-x)}\).

## Context
This work extends the SCET_G framework developed by Idilbi (2008), D'Eramo (2010), and Ovanesyan (2011) for jet propagation in dense QCD matter, going beyond the soft-gluon approximation used in traditional energy loss calculations by Gyulassy (2003) and others. The results provide all four splitting kernels (\(q\rightarrow qg\), \(g\rightarrow gg\), \(g\rightarrow q\overline{q}\), \(q\rightarrow gq\)) with full \(x\) dependence, enabling improved theoretical predictions for jet production at RHIC and LHC, as noted by He (2011) and Neufeld (2012).
