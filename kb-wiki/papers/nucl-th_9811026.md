# Final-State Interactions in (e,e'p) Reactions with Polarized Nuclei

> The cross section for coincidence, quasielastic proton knock-out by electrons from a polarized K39 nucleus is computed in DWIA using an optical potential in describing the wave function of the ejected nucleon. The dependence of the FSI on the initial polarization angles of the nucleus is analyzed and explained in a new, semi-classical picture of the reaction in which the nuclear transparency decreases as a function of the amount of nuclear matter that the proton has to cross, thus providing a method for obtaining detailed information on its mean free path in finite nuclei. We propose a procedure to find the best initial kinematical conditions for minimizing the FSI which will be useful as a guide for future experiments with polarized nuclei.

## Key claim

The nuclear transparency in quasielastic (e,e'p) reactions with polarized nuclei can be maximized or minimized by selecting specific polarization directions, because the final-state interaction (FSI) depends on the amount of nuclear matter the ejected proton must traverse, which is determined by the orientation of the initial-state nucleon's orbit relative to the ejection direction.

## Method

The authors computed the coincidence cross section for proton knockout from polarized 39K (with the proton ejected from the d3/2 shell, leaving 38Ar in its ground state) using the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with a complex optical potential for the ejected nucleon wave function. They performed a multipole expansion of the nuclear current operator, truncating at J=32 for convergence, and calculated the cross section for 26 different polarization angle combinations at fixed kinematics corresponding to the quasielastic peak (q=500 MeV/c, ω=133.5 MeV, in-plane emission). The nuclear transparency was defined as the ratio of the DWIA to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) cross section.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer: q = 500 MeV/c
- Energy transfer: ω = 133.5 MeV
- Electron scattering angle: θ_e = 30°
- For θ* = 90°, Δφ = 45°: transparency is small, approximately 0.4
- For θ* = 90°, Δφ = 225°: transparency is large, approximately 0.9
- Missing momentum at the maximum of the momentum distribution: p ≈ 140 MeV/c
- Multipole expansion truncated at J = 32 for convergence

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Amaro and Donnelly, 1998) of multipole reduced responses in polarized (e,e'p) reactions, where different sensitivities to FSI were found. It goes beyond earlier preliminary DWIA studies of polarized medium and heavy nuclei (Garrido et al., 1995; Boffi et al., 1988) by providing a systematic semi-classical interpretation of the FSI dependence on polarization angles. The results enable experimentalists to select optimal polarization orientations for minimizing FSI effects (to study initial-state nuclear structure) or maximizing them (to study the absorptive part of the optical potential and extract the mean free path in finite nuclei).
