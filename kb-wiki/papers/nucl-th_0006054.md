# NN-->NNpi from the effective field theory point of view: short comings and gains

> Based on a counting scheme for the application of chiral perturbation theory to pion production in NN collisions p-wave production is studied. It is demonstrated that, contrary to the s-wave production where loops enter at too low an order to allow the scheme predictive power, there are certain p-wave observables where parameter free predictions are possible. These predictions are consistent with experimental data. We show that the investigation of charged pion production yields a constraint on a contribution to the three-nucleon force with a spin-isospin structure that could resolve the $A_y$ puzzle in $nd$ scattering.

# NN→NNπ from the effective field theory point of view: short comings and gains

## Key claim

Contrary to s-wave pion production, where loop contributions enter at too low an order to allow predictive power, certain p-wave observables in NN→NNπ allow parameter-free predictions that are consistent with experimental data. The investigation of charged pion production yields a constraint on a contribution to the three-nucleon force with a spin-isospin structure that could resolve the A_y puzzle in nd scattering, but this constraint depends on the as-yet-unknown low-energy constants d₁ and d₂.

## Method

The author applies chiral perturbation theory (χPT) with a counting scheme that orders contributions in powers of √(m_π/M_N). For p-wave pion production, the expansion starts at O(1) and the next non-vanishing order at O(m_π/M_N) is free of loops, unlike the s-wave case where loops enter at O(√(m_π/M_N)²). The calculation includes diagrams up to next-to-leading order for neutral-pion production and extracts the amplitude a₀ for the ¹S₀ → ³S₁p transition from pp → pnπ⁺ data to constrain the linear combination d = d₁ + 4d₂ of short-range operators.

## Key numbers

- The scale of typical internal momenta is set by the initial nucleon momentum p_i ≥ √(M_N m_π).
- The leading order for s-wave production is O(√(m_π/M_N)), while for p-wave production the expansion starts at O(1).
- The next non-vanishing order for p-wave production is O(m_π/M_N), which is free of loops because loop orders are enhanced by a factor of q_ex/p_typ = m_π/√(m_π M_N) compared to the s-wave case.
- The short-range parameter d is rewritten as d = δ/(f_π² M_N), where δ is expected to be a number of order 1.
- The observable a₀ from pp → pnπ⁺ shows sensitivity to δ for values δ = 1, 0, -0.2, and -1 (shown in Fig. 3 of the paper).

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory to pion production in NN collisions, following earlier tree-level χPT calculations by CP2, CP1, CP3, HHH, and CP4. It addresses the failure of s-wave predictions due to loop contributions entering at too low an order, as emphasized in CP2 and ulfrel. The paper establishes a direct connection between pion production at energies around 350 MeV (IUCF) and nucleon-deuteron scattering at energies around 10 MeV (Madison, TUNL), enabling future extraction of three-nucleon force parameters from pion production data.
