# Linear stability of Israel-Stewart theory in the presence of net-charge diffusion

> In this paper, we perform a linear stability analysis of Israel-Stewart theory around a global equilibrium state, including the effects of shear-stress tensor, net-baryon diffusion current and diffusion-viscous coupling. We find all the relevant modes of this theory and derive necessary conditions that these modes must satisfy in order to be stable and subluminal. With these conditions, we then derive constraints for the shear and diffusion relaxation times and the transport coefficients related to diffusion-viscous coupling.

## Key claim

The paper shows that Israel-Stewart theory including net-baryon diffusion and diffusion-viscous coupling is linearly stable and subluminal only if the shear and diffusion relaxation times satisfy specific inequalities and if the product of the diffusion-viscous coupling coefficients is negative, with the coupling coefficients themselves bounded by a condition derived from the stability analysis.

## Method

The authors linearize the Israel-Stewart equations around a global equilibrium state with vanishing net-baryon density and zero dissipative currents, then decompose the perturbations into longitudinal and transverse modes in Fourier space. They derive dispersion relations for all modes, impose stability (non-growing perturbations) and subluminality (asymptotic group velocity ≤ 1) conditions, and obtain constraints on the relaxation times τ_π and τ_n and on the coupling coefficients ℒ_nπ and ℒ_πn.

## Key numbers

- Shear relaxation time must satisfy τ_π ≥ τ_η (i.e., τ̂_π ≥ 1) for stability and causality; this is derived from the homogeneous limit of the transverse shear mode in a moving background.
- Diffusion relaxation time must satisfy τ_n ≥ 0 for stability of the transverse diffusion mode.
- For an ultrarelativistic hard-sphere gas, the kinetic-theory values are τ̂_π = 5, τ̂_n = 27/4, τ̂_κ = 9/16.
- The coupling coefficients satisfy ℒ_nπ ℒ_πn < 0, as supported by kinetic theory and the second law of thermodynamics.

## Context

This work extends previous linear stability analyses of Israel-Stewart theory that included only shear and bulk viscosity (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1985; Olson, 1990; Pu et al., 2010; Denicol et al., 2008) by adding net-baryon diffusion and diffusion-viscous coupling. It also complements nonlinear causality studies (Bemfica et al., 2018; Disconzi et al., 2017) that did not include net-charge diffusion. The derived constraints on relaxation times and coupling coefficients are directly applicable to fluid-dynamical simulations of the quark-gluon plasma at finite net-baryon density, such as those planned at RHIC, NICA, and FAIR.
