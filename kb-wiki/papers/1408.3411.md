# A scaling relation between proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> It is recently discovered that at high multiplicy, the proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions give rise to two particle correlations that are strikingly similar to those of nucleus-nucleus ($AA$) collisions at the same multiplicity, although the system size is smaller in $pA$. Using an independent cluster model and a simple conformal scaling argument, where the ratio of the mean free path to the system size stays constant at fixed multiplicity, we argue that flow in $pA$ emerges as a collective response to the fluctuations in the position of clusters, just like in $AA$ collisions. With several physically motivated and parameter free rescalings of the recent LHC data, we show that this simple model captures the essential physics of elliptic and triangular flow in $pA$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the similarity of two-particle correlations in high-multiplicity proton-nucleus (pA) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions arises from a collective response to initial geometry, provided that the ratio of the mean free path to the system size stays constant at fixed multiplicity. This "conformal scaling" framework is presented as capturing the essential physics without fitting parameters, but the authors acknowledge that the agreement is best at low transverse momentum and that non-flow effects may become significant at higher pT.

## Method

The authors implement an independent cluster model where the initial state is described by N_clust independently distributed clusters, with multiplicity proportional to N_clust. They assume linear response of the final flow harmonics v_n to the initial eccentricities ε_n, with response coefficients k_n that depend only on the ratio of mean free path to system size (and thus on multiplicity). For AA collisions, the average eccentricity from the collision geometry is subtracted to isolate the fluctuation-driven part, and the pT axes of AA data are rescaled by the ratio of average transverse momenta ⟨pT⟩ between pA and AA systems.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of average transverse momenta between pPb and PbPb systems is κ ≡ ⟨pT⟩_pPb / ⟨pT⟩_PbPb ≈ 1.25.
- The ratio of system sizes inferred from conformal scaling is L_AA / L_pA = ⟨pT⟩_pA / ⟨pT⟩_AA ≈ 1.25, while the Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) measurement gives R_AA / R_pA ≈ 1.4.
- The integrated v_3 in pA and AA are equal "up to a few percent" at the same multiplicity.
- The rescaling factor for v_2 in AA to isolate fluctuation-driven part is computed via Monte Carlo Glauber simulation and is a nontrivial function of centrality (multiplicity).

## Context

This work extends the observation by the LHC collaborations (CMS, ALICE, ATLAS) and RHIC (PHENIX) that high-multiplicity pA collisions show long-range two-particle correlations similar to AA collisions. It contrasts with Color Glass Condensate (CGC) explanations that do not invoke fluctuating geometry (Dusling 2012, Dusling 2013, McLerran 2014, Gyulassy 2014), and instead supports hydrodynamic simulations (Bozek 2011, Bozek 2012, Bzdak 2013) that predicted collective flow in pA. The conformal scaling framework (Basar 2013) enables parameter-free comparisons that, if confirmed, would establish collective flow as the common origin of anisotropy in both systems.
