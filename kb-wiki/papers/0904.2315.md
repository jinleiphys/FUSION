# Effect of flow fluctuations and nonflow on elliptic flow methods

> We discuss how the different estimates of elliptic flow are influenced by flow fluctuations and nonflow effects. It is explained why the event-plane method yields estimates between the two-particle correlation methods and the multiparticle correlation methods. It is argued that nonflow effects and fluctuations cannot be disentangled without other assumptions. However, we provide equations where, with reasonable assumptions about fluctuations and nonflow, all measured values of elliptic flow converge to a unique mean v_{2,PP} elliptic flow in the participant plane and, with a Gaussian assumption on eccentricity fluctuations, can be converted to the mean v_{2,RP} in the reaction plane. Thus, the 20% spread in observed elliptic flow measurements from different analysis methods is no longer mysterious.

## Key claim

The 20% spread in elliptic flow measurements from different analysis methods can be resolved into a unique mean elliptic flow in the participant plane, but only under specific assumptions about flow fluctuations and nonflow effects; fluctuations and nonflow cannot be disentangled without additional assumptions, and the conversion to reaction-plane flow requires a Gaussian assumption on eccentricity fluctuations.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for how flow fluctuations (characterized by σ_v) and nonflow correlations (characterized by δ) affect the measured elliptic flow from two-particle correlations v{2}, multiparticle correlations v{4}, the event-plane method v{EP}, and the subevent-plane method v{subEP}. They expanded these expressions to leading order in σ_v² and δ, assuming additivity of the two contributions, and applied the resulting equations to STAR Au+Au data at √s_NN = 200 GeV using Monte Carlo Glauber and Color Glass Condensate models for eccentricity fluctuations, with nonflow estimated from p+p collisions scaled by participant number.

## Key numbers

- v{2}² = ⟨v⟩² + δ + σ_v² (Eq. 1 in summary)
- v{4}² = ⟨v⟩² − σ_v² (Eq. 2 in summary)
- v{2}² − v{4}² = δ + 2σ_v² (Eq. 3 in differences)
- δ_pp = 0.0145 from minimum bias p+p collisions (from Ref. Adams:2004wz)
- Nonflow scaling: δ = δ_pp × 2 / N_part (Eq. 4 in delta)
- For CGC fluctuations, σ_ε/⟨ε⟩ is roughly 30% smaller than Glauber
- v{2}² − v{subEP}² = [2 i₁²/(i₀+i₁)²] × (δ + 2σ_v²) (Eq. 3 in differences)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Voloshin (2008) and Bhalerao (2006) on eccentricity fluctuations and their relation to reaction-plane flow, and directly addresses the spread in elliptic flow measurements observed by STAR (Adams 2004, Abelev 2008). The paper provides the analytic machinery to correct different flow estimates to a common participant-plane mean v₂, enabling systematic comparison of results from two-particle, event-plane, and multiparticle methods. It also shows that the PHOBOS simulations of Alver (2008) are quantitatively explained by fluctuations without adjustable parameters.
