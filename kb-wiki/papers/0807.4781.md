# Determination of Landau Fermi-liquid parameters of strongly interacting fermions by means of a nonlinear scaling transformation

> A nonlinear transformation approach is formulated for the correlated fermions' thermodynamics through a medium-scaling effective action. An auxiliary implicit variable-effective chemical potential is introduced to characterize the non-Gaussian fluctuations physics. By incorporating the nonlocal correlation effects, the achieved grand partition function is made of coupled highly nonlinear parametric equations. Analytically, the low temperature expansions for the strongly interacting unitary Fermi gas are performed for the adiabatic compressibility-sound speed and specific heat with the Sommerfeld lemma. The expressions for the Landau Fermi-Liquid parameters $F_0^s$ and $F_1^s$ of the strongly interacting fermion system are obtained. As a universal constant, the effective fermion mass ratio is $m^*/m={10/9}$ at unitarity.

## Key claim

The paper derives Landau Fermi-liquid parameters for strongly interacting fermions at unitarity using a nonlinear scaling transformation that introduces an auxiliary effective chemical potential to capture non-Gaussian fluctuation effects; the key result is a universal effective fermion mass ratio of \(m^*/m = 10/9\) at unitarity, which the abstract presents as a fixed constant but the full text qualifies as arising from the lowest-order low-temperature expansion of the specific heat.

## Method

The author formulates a medium-scaling Hamiltonian in the grand canonical ensemble, where the bare coupling constant is replaced by a medium-dependent effective interaction \(U_{\text{eff}}^*\) that incorporates a minus sign in the denominator to enforce thermodynamic stability. The grand partition function is obtained as coupled parametric equations for pressure and chemical potential, with an implicit effective chemical potential \(\mu^*\) introduced as an auxiliary variable. Low-temperature expansions are performed using the Sommerfeld lemma, expanding in terms of \(T/\mu^*\) rather than \(T/\mu\), and the Landau parameters \(F_0^s\) and \(F_1^s\) are extracted by comparing the expansions of sound speed squared and specific heat with standard Landau Fermi-liquid theory.

## Key numbers

- Effective fermion mass ratio at unitarity: \(m^*/m = 10/9\) (derived from the lowest-order coefficient of \(C_V/N\)).
- Universal coefficient \(\xi = 4/9\) for the ground state energy (from the lowest-order pressure expansion at unitarity).
- The specific heat ratio \(C_V/C_V^0\) at unitarity approaches \(10/9\) as \(T \rightarrow 0\).
- A knee peak in the \(C_V/C_V^0\) ratio appears at \(T^* \approx 0.21 T_f\).
- The effective mass expansion: \(m^*/m = 1 + \frac{4(ak_f)^2}{9(\pi - 2 a k_f)^2}\), yielding \(10/9\) when \(|a| = \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the Landau Fermi-liquid framework (Landau, 1956) to the unitary regime of ultracold atomic Fermi gases, where the scattering length diverges and universal properties emerge (Heiselberg, 2000; Ho, 2004; Giorgini, 2007). The calculated effective mass ratio \(m^*/m = 10/9\) falls within the range \(1.04-1.17\) from previous Monte Carlo and analytic attempts (Lobo, 2006; Pilati, 2008; Combescot, 2007), and the parametric-equation formalism provides a non-perturbative method to handle infrared singularities in three-dimensional contact-interaction fermion systems, enabling future calculations of transport properties and phase separation in asymmetric trapped systems.
