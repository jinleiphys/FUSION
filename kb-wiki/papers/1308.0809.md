# Improved Nucleon Properties in the Extended Quark Sigma Model

> The quark sigma model describes the quarks interacting via exchange the pions and sigma meson fields. A new version of mesonic potential is suggested in the frame of some aspects of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The field equations have been solved in the mean-field approximation for the hedgehog baryon state. The obtained results are compared with previous works and other models. We conclude that the suggested mesonic potential successfully calculates nucleon properties.

# Improved Nucleon Properties in the Extended Quark Sigma Model

## Key claim
The paper claims that adding a higher-order mesonic potential term (quartic in the squared chiral field) to the standard linear sigma model improves nucleon properties, particularly the hedgehog mass and the sigma commutator, but the axial-vector coupling constant g_A(0) remains too large compared to experiment and would require vector mesons to be corrected.

## Method
The author solves the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations for quarks and meson fields in the mean-field approximation using the hedgehog ansatz, iterating the implicit integral equations for the sigma and pion fields to self-consistency. The mesonic potential includes both the standard quartic term and an additional term proportional to (sigma^2 + pi^2)^4, with parameters fixed by the pion decay constant f_pi and the sigma and pion masses. The Dirac equations are solved using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method, and nucleon properties are computed from the self-consistent fields.

## Key numbers
- Hedgehog mass M_B = 1090 MeV at m_sigma = 600 MeV, m_q = 500 MeV, compared to experimental value 1086 MeV
- Proton magnetic moment mu_p = 2.84 (nuclear magnetons) at m_q = 500 MeV, m_sigma = 600 MeV
- Neutron magnetic moment mu_n = -2.20 (nuclear magnetons) at m_q = 500 MeV, m_sigma = 600 MeV
- Sigma commutator sigma(pi N) = 77 MeV at m_q = 500 MeV, m_sigma = 600 MeV, reduced from 126.99 MeV in the original sigma model
- Axial-vector coupling constant g_A(0) = 1.78, compared to experimental value 1.25
- Hedgehog mass decreases from 1230 MeV to 1089 MeV as quark mass increases from 400 MeV to 500 MeV
- Sigma commutator sigma(pi N) increases from 49.19 MeV to 77.02 MeV as quark mass increases from 400 MeV to 500 MeV

## Context
This work extends the linear sigma model of Birse and Banerjee (1985) and the higher-order mesonic potential studies of Rashdan, Abu-Shady, and Ali (2006, 2007) by introducing a different form of the higher-order term that satisfies the chiral limit at m_pi = 0, which was a weakness in the earlier Rashdan et al. (2006) formulation. The results are compared with the perturbative chiral quark model of Lyubovitskij, Gutsche, and Faessler (2001) and the extended Skyrme model of Braghin and Cavalcante (2003). The improved sigma commutator value (77 MeV) brings the model closer to the experimental range of 50 +/- 20 MeV, but the author notes that vector mesons would need to be added to fix the g_A(0) value.
