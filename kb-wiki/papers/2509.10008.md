# SU(3) rigid triaxiality in $^{154}$Sm

> The $^{154}$Sm nucleus, which has long been regarded as a typical quantum system exhibiting axial symmetry, has been recently suggested to exhibit a small degree of triaxiality by Otsuka \textit{et al.}. This small triaxiality was recently observed using the $γ$ decay of the $^{154}$Sm isovector giant dipole resonance. Thus further studying this small triaxiality is very necessary for understanding the nature of the collectivity in nuclei. In this paper, the rigid triaxiality in $^{154}$Sm is investigated using the newly proposed SU3-IBM theory. If the irreducible representation (irrep) of the ground state is (18,2) in the SU(3) symmetry limit, the new model can effectively reproduce the experimental data of $^{154}$Sm, showing good agreements with realistic energy spectra, B(E2) values, and quadrupole moments. This result further confirms the vality of the SU3-IBM for the description of the rigid triaxiality in realistic nuclei, and reveals the SU(3) dominance of the collectivity motions in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the SU3-IBM (interacting boson model with SU(3) higher-order interactions) can reproduce the experimental data of $^{154}$Sm if the ground-state SU(3) irreducible representation is (18,2), but this yields a triaxiality angle $\gamma \approx 7.2^{\circ}$, which is larger than the $\gamma \approx 3.7^{\circ}$ predicted by Otsuka et al. (2025) and lies between the experimental value of $5.0(15)^{\circ}$ and the Monte Carlo shell model prediction.

## Method

The authors apply the SU3-IBM Hamiltonian, which combines the U(5) symmetry limit (spherical) with SU(3) symmetry (deformed) through a rigid rotor Hamiltonian containing static and dynamic parts built from SU(3) Casimir operators up to fourth order. They fix the ground-state SU(3) irrep to (18,2) and fit seven parameters ($\alpha$, $a_1$, $a_2$, $a_3$, $t_1$, $t_2$, $t_3$) to reproduce the experimental energy spectra, B(E2) transition strengths, and quadrupole moments of $^{154}$Sm.

## Key numbers

* The ground-state SU(3) irrep is (18,2), yielding a triaxiality angle $\gamma \approx 7.2^{\circ}$ for the $0_1^+$ state, compared to the experimental value of $5.0(15)^{\circ}$ and the MCSM value of $\approx 3.7^{\circ}$.
* The $0_2^+$ state has $\gamma \approx 13.9^{\circ}$ in SU3-IBM, close to the MCSM value of $\approx 12.6^{\circ}$.
* The calculated quadrupole moment $Q(2_1^+) = -1.88$ eb agrees well with the experimental value $-1.87(4)$ eb, while IBM-CM gives $-2.34$ eb and CQ gives $-2.44$ eb.
* The calculated $B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 176$ W.u. matches the experimental value $176(3)$ W.u. exactly (by construction).
* The $B(E2; 2_2^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = 0.37$ W.u. agrees with the experimental $0.32(4)$ W.u., compared to X(5) which gives $3.5$ W.u.

## Context

This work extends the recent finding by Otsuka et al. (2019, 2023, 2025) that $^{154}$Sm exhibits small triaxiality, which was experimentally confirmed by Kleeman (2025) via $\gamma$ decay of the isovector giant dipole resonance. The SU3-IBM approach, previously applied to the B(E2) anomaly and the Cd puzzle by Wang et al. (2020, 2022, 2025), provides a framework where rigid triaxial shapes emerge from SU(3) higher-order interactions, in contrast to the standard IBM-1 which cannot describe rigid triaxiality with only two-body interactions (Van Isacker, 1981). The results enable future comparisons with more detailed configuration interaction calculations and further experimental measurements of higher-lying states and quadrupole moments.
