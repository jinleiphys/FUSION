# Effect of isovector-scalar meson on neutron star matter in strong magnetic fields

> We study the effects of isovector-scalar meson $δ$ on the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star matter in strong magnetic fields. The EOS of neutron-star matter and nucleon effective masses are calculated in the framework of Lagrangian field theory, which is solved within the mean-field approximation. From the numerical results one can find that the $δ$-field leads to a remarkable splitting of proton and neutron effective masses. The strength of $δ$-field decreases with the increasing of the magnetic field and is little at ultrastrong field. The proton effective mass is highly influenced by magnetic fields, while the effect of magnetic fields on the neutron effective mass is negligible. The EOS turns out to be stiffer at $B < 10^{15}$G but becomes softer at stronger magnetic field after including the $δ$-field. The AMM terms can affect the system merely at ultrastrong magnetic field($B > 10^{19}$G). In the range of $10^{15}$ G -- $10^{18}$ G the properties of neutron-star matter are found to be similar with those without magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector-scalar (δ) meson in the relativistic mean-field description of neutron-star matter makes the equation of state stiffer at magnetic fields below 10^15 G but softer at stronger fields, with the δ-field effect diminishing at ultrastrong fields above 10^18 G; the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) terms affect the system only at fields above 10^19 G.

## Method

The authors solved a Lagrangian field theory for n-p-e-μ matter in a uniform magnetic field using the mean-field approximation, including σ, ω, ρ, and δ meson exchanges. They imposed charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium (β-equilibrium) and iteratively solved the meson field equations for the scalar and vector fields to obtain nucleon effective masses, energy density, and pressure. Calculations were performed for magnetic field strengths from 10^12 G to 3×10^19 G, both with and without the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons, muons, and electrons.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field for electrons is B_c^e = 4.414 × 10^13 G; for protons it is B_c^p = 1.487 × 10^20 G.
- The anomalous magnetic moment coefficients used: κ_p = 3.5856, κ_n = -3.8263, κ_e = 1159652188(4) × 10^12 (4 ppb), κ_μ = 11659203(8) × 10^−10 (0.7 ppm).
- At B = 10^12 G and B = 10^13 G, the proton effective mass is significantly enhanced compared to the B = 0 case; at B = 10^15 G and above, the proton effective mass becomes indistinguishable from the field-free case.
- The δ-field strength becomes near zero at B > 10^15 G and higher densities; at B = 3 × 10^19 G and ρ > 2ρ_0, the δ-field strength turns negative.
- The equation of state is stiffer at B ~ 10^12 G compared to B = 0, indistinguishable from B = 0 in the range 10^15 G to 10^19 G, and softer at B > 10^19 G.
- Including AMM terms, the EOS becomes much stiffer at B = 10^19 G, with nucleon effective masses increasing at high densities (ρ > 5ρ_0).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron-star matter in strong magnetic fields by Broderick et al. (2000) and others, which did not include the δ meson, and builds on calculations by Liu et al. (2002) and Kubis et al. (1997) showing that the δ-field causes splitting of proton and neutron effective masses in asymmetric matter. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron-star structure and composition, particularly for stars with interior magnetic fields in the 10^12–10^18 G range, and motivate future inclusion of hyperons and quark degrees of freedom as noted by Schaffner et al. (1996) and Glendenning (2000).
