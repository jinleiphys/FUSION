# Colored Chaos

## Key claim

The paper argues that the rapid thermalization observed in high-energy hadronic interactions, which justifies Hagedorn's statistical model, is caused by the intrinsic chaotic dynamics of nonabelian gauge theories, specifically that classical SU(2) and SU(3) gauge fields exhibit positive Lyapunov exponents that lead to ergodic behavior on time scales much shorter than 1 fm/c. The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn from classical lattice gauge theory simulations, and the quantum effects from shorter distances must be incorporated separately via transport coefficients.

## Method

The author and collaborators numerically integrated the classical Hamiltonian equations of motion for SU(2) and SU(3) lattice gauge theory on cubic lattices of sizes ranging from 2^3 to 28^3, using the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian formulation. They computed the maximal Lyapunov exponent by tracking the exponential divergence of initially neighboring gauge field configurations, and for SU(2) on smaller lattices (1^3, 2^3, 3^3) they computed the full Lyapunov spectrum. The distance between field configurations was measured using a gauge-invariant metric based on differences in electric and magnetic energy densities.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) gauge theory, the maximal Lyapunov exponent scales as h = b_2 g^2 E_p, with b_2 ≈ 1/6, where E_p is the average energy per plaquette.
- For SU(3) gauge theory, the maximal Lyapunov exponent scales as h = b_3 g^2 E_p, with b_3 ≈ 1/10.
- In thermal equilibrium, the maximal Lyapunov exponent for SU(3) becomes h ≈ (8/15) g^2 T ≈ 0.53 g^2 T.
- For SU(2) in thermal equilibrium, h ≈ (1/3) g^2 T ≈ 0.33 g^2 T.
- The Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy density for SU(2) is given by dot_sigma_KS = (1/3) b_2 c_2 g^2 epsilon, where c_2 ≈ 2 and epsilon = 3E_p/a^3 is the average energy density.
- The thermalization time scale in SU(3) gauge theory, defined as the inverse of the maximal Lyapunov exponent, is much smaller than 0.5 fm/c for all relevant temperatures (using one-loop running coupling with Lambda = 200 MeV).
- The maximal Lyapunov exponent for SU(3) (0.53 g^2 T) agrees within numerical errors with the analytically calculated damping rate of a nonabelian plasmon at rest, Gamma = 2 gamma(0) = 6.635 (n/24 pi) g^2 T ≈ 0.53 g^2 T for SU(3).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of chaotic dynamics in nonabelian gauge theories, building on earlier studies of the simple Hamiltonian H = (1/2)(p_x^2 + p_y^2 + x^2 y^2) which arises in the extreme infrared limit of Yang-Mills fields and was shown by Matinyan and others to have a positive Lyapunov exponent of approximately 0.4. The paper directly addresses the conceptual puzzle left by Hagedorn (1964) regarding why statistical occupation of hadronic states is justified, drawing an analogy to Bohr's statistical model of compound nucleus reactions where chaotic quantum behavior (Wigner distribution of level spacings) resolved similar questions. The results enable the calculation of thermalization times in heavy-ion collisions and suggest that classical Yang-Mills theory can be used to compute other quantities of order g^2 T, such as the thermal magnetic screening mass, provided an effective quasi-classical theory incorporating quantum effects from shorter distances is developed.
