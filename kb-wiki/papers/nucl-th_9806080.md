# The Polarizability of the Deuteron

> The scalar and tensor polarizabilities of the deuteron are calculated using the recently developed effective field theory that describes nucleon-nucleon interactions. Leading and next-to-leading order contributions in the perturbative expansion predict a scalar electric polarizability of 0.595 fm^3. The tensor electric polarizability receives contributions starting at next-to-leading order from the exchange of a single potential pion and is found to be -0.062 fm^3. We compute the leading contributions to the scalar and tensor magnetic polarizabilities, finding 0.067 fm^3 and 0.195 fm^3, respectively.

## Key claim

The scalar and tensor electric polarizabilities of the deuteron are computed to next-to-leading order (NLO) and the magnetic polarizabilities to leading order in an effective field theory (EFT) of nucleon-nucleon interactions, with the scalar electric polarizability predicted to be 0.595 fm^3; however, the authors note that the tensor electric polarizability has a much larger fractional uncertainty because it vanishes at leading order, and that the individual nucleon polarizabilities are unlikely to be extracted from the deuteron polarizabilities.

## Method

The authors use the effective field theory developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) that describes nucleon-nucleon interactions with consistent power counting, employing dimensional regularization with power divergence subtraction (PDS). They compute the scalar and tensor electric polarizabilities of the deuteron by matching loop diagrams (including minimal coupling, potential pion exchange, and the C_2 operator) onto a deuteron effective Lagrange density, expanding in powers of Q = m_pi / Lambda_NN. The magnetic polarizabilities are computed from leading-order graphs involving nucleon magnetic moments and minimal coupling.

## Key numbers

- Scalar electric polarizability of the deuteron: alpha_E0 = 0.595 fm^3, with an estimated uncertainty of Delta alpha_E0 ≈ 0.1 fm^3 from higher-order contributions
- Tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron: alpha_E2 = -0.062 fm^3, with an estimated uncertainty of Delta alpha_E2 ≈ 0.03 fm^3
- Scalar magnetic polarizability of the deuteron: beta_M0 = 0.067 fm^3
- Tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron: beta_M2 = 0.195 fm^3
- Electric polarizability of the |m|=1 deuteron state: 0.637 fm^3
- Electric polarizability of the m=0 deuteron state: 0.511 fm^3
- Magnetic polarizability of the |m|=1 deuteron state: -0.063 fm^3
- Magnetic polarizability of the m=0 deuteron state: 0.327 fm^3
- Leading-order contribution to alpha_E0: 0.386 fm^3; NLO contributions from the C_2 operator: 0.153 fm^3; from single potential pion exchange: 0.057 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of the deuteron, previously applied to electromagnetic moments and form factors by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), to the polarizabilities. The results agree within uncertainties with potential model calculations, such as the scalar electric polarizability of 0.6328 ± 0.0017 fm^3 from Friar and Payne (1997) and the zero-range limiting value of 0.632 ± 0.003 fm^3 from Friar and Fallieros (1984), as well as the state-dependent values from Karchenko (1997). The model-independent analytic expressions enable systematic inclusion of higher-order chiral dynamics and relativistic effects, and the authors plan to compute the photon-deuteron scattering cross section over a range of photon energies in subsequent work.
