# Effective field theory of Bose-Einstein condensation of $α$ clusters and Nambu-Goldstone-Higgs states in $^{12}$C

> An effective field theory of $α$ cluster condensation is formulated as a spontaneously broken symmetry in quantum field theory to understand the raison d'etre and nature of the Hoyle and $α$ cluster states in $^{12}$C. The Nambu--Goldstone and Higgs mode operators in infinite systems are replaced with a pair of canonical operators whose Hamiltonian gives rise to discrete energy states in addition to the Bogoliubov--de Gennes excited states. The calculations reproduce well the experimental spectrum of the $α$ cluster states. The existence of the Nambu--Goldstone--Higgs states is demonstrated and crucial. The $γ$ decay transitions are also obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Hoyle state and the excited alpha cluster states in $^{12}$C can be understood as manifestations of spontaneously broken global phase symmetry (Bose-Einstein condensation of alpha clusters) in quantum field theory, with the crucial hedge being that the Nambu-Goldstone and Higgs modes, which would be gapless in infinite systems, become discrete energy states (NGH states) in this finite system, and that the existence of these NGH states is essential to reproduce the observed spectrum.

## Method

The authors formulate an effective field theory for alpha cluster condensation using the interacting zero mode formulation (IZMF), where the field operator is expanded into a condensate component, Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) excitation modes, and a pair of canonical operators (Q and P) that replace the Nambu-Goldstone and Higgs mode operators of infinite systems. They introduce a phenomenological external harmonic potential to mimic three-body forces and use the Ali-Bodmer potential for the alpha-alpha interaction, then solve the coupled Gross-Pitaevskii equation, BdG equation, and the NGH (zero mode) eigenvalue equation to obtain energy levels and wave functions, fitting the two key parameters (harmonic oscillator strength Omega and repulsive potential strength V_r) to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The Hoyle state (0_2^+) is at an excitation energy E_x = 7.654 MeV, and the alpha particle threshold is at 7.367 MeV.
* For parameter set A (Omega = 2.14 MeV, V_r = 422 MeV), the calculated rms radius of the Hoyle state is 4.21 fm.
* The calculated 0_3^+ state (first NGH state, nu=1) and 0_4^+ state (second NGH state, nu=2) correspond well to the experimental states at 9.04 MeV and 10.56 MeV, respectively.
* For parameter set B (Omega = 2.58 MeV, V_r = 400 MeV), the rms radius is fixed to 3.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative discussion of Nambu-Goldstone-Higgs states in alpha cluster condensates by Ohkubo (2013) and provides a quantum field theory foundation for the alpha condensate model of Tohsaki (2001) and Yamada (2004, 2005). It offers an alternative to traditional alpha cluster models (Uegaki, 1977; Kurokawa, 2005, 2007) and ab initio calculations (Epelbaum, 2012; Dreyfuss, 2013) that explain the Hoyle state and excited states as collective modes in configuration space (rotational bands or vibrational states), resolving the long-standing question of which state is the bandhead for the 2_2^+ and 4_1^+ states. The approach enables the prediction of NGH states as a new class of excitations and provides a framework for calculating gamma decay transitions that can be tested experimentally.
