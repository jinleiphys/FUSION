# Tensor effects on gap evolution of N=40 from non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field theory

> Tensor effects on the N=40 gap evolution of N=40 isotones are studied by employing the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) and relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theories. The results with and without the inclusion of the tensor component are compared with the experimental data. When the tensor force is included, both of the two different approaches are found to give the same trend and agree with the experimental one, which indicates the necessity of introducing the tensor force in the evolution of N=40 subshell and on the other hand the reliability of the methods. Furthermore, it is shown that the gap evolution is primarily determined by the corresponding tensor contributions from $π$ and $ρ$-tensor coupling in the relativistic framework.

## Key claim

When the tensor force is included, both non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SHFB) and relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculations yield the same trend for the N=40 gap evolution and qualitatively agree with the experimental trend from beta-decay studies, whereas calculations without tensor components fail to reproduce this trend.

## Method

The authors performed spherical SHFB calculations using the SLy5 and T31 effective interactions, with tensor components added perturbatively (SLy5+T and T31+T), and RHFB calculations using the PKO2, PKO3, and PKA1 effective interactions, comparing to RHB calculations with DD-ME2 which omit Fock terms. The neutron gap at N=40 was extracted for six neutron-rich isotones from Z=20 to Z=30, and the contributions from pion and rho-tensor couplings were decomposed in the relativistic framework.

## Key numbers

- The N=40 gap evolution was studied for six isotones from Z=20 (60Ca) to Z=30 (68Ni).
- The SLy5+T and T31+T calculations reproduced the experimental trend of decreasing gap when removing protons from 68Ni, while SLy5 and T31 without tensor components showed opposite behavior.
- In the RHFB calculations with PKA1, the pion and rho-tensor couplings provided the dominant contributions to the gap evolution, while other channels (kinetic energy, sigma-scalar, omega-vector, rho-vector couplings, and rearrangement term) showed fairly remarkable cancellation.

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor force effects on shell evolution, following the suggestion by Otsuka (2005) that the tensor force is crucial for modeling shell evolution and magic numbers in exotic regions. It addresses the failure of mean-field calculations without tensor components to describe the rapidly weakening N=40 gap when removing protons from 68Ni, as observed in beta-decay experiments by Mueller (1999) and Sorlin (2001). The results are consistent with semi-realistic mean-field calculations by Nakada (2010) and enable future extension to deformed mean-field calculations for a more comprehensive study of the N=40 island of inversion.
