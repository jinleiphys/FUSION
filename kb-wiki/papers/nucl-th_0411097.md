# Isoscaling behavior in the Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model

> The isoscaling behavior is investigated in the frame of Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) models. The isotopic yields ratio $Y_2$/$Y_1$ for reactions $^{48}Ca$+$^{48}Ca$ and $^{40}Ca+^{40}Ca$ at different entrance channels are simulated and presented, the relationship between the isoscaling parameter and the entrance channel is analyzed, the results show that $őĪ$ and $ő≤$ reduce with the rise of incident energies and increase with the impact parameter b, which can be attributed to the temperature varying of the pre-fragments in different entrance channels. The relation of $őĪ$ and symmetry-term coefficient $C_{sym}$ reveals that the chemical potential difference $\triangleőľ$ is sensitive to the symmetry-term coefficient $C_{sym}$.

## Key claim
The isoscaling parameters őĪ and ő≤ in IQMD simulations of Ca+Ca collisions decrease with increasing incident energy and increase with impact parameter, which the authors attribute primarily to changes in the temperature of the emitting pre-fragments rather than to changes in chemical potential differences.

## Context
This work extends the isoscaling studies of Tsang (2001) and the IQMD model development of Zhang, Wei, and Liu to systematically investigate how entrance channel conditions affect the isoscaling parameters. The results confirm the linear relationship between őĪ and (Z/A)¬≤ predicted by the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Botvina and the lattice gas model (LGM) of Ma (2004), while showing that őĪ is sensitive to the symmetry-term coefficient Csym. This enables future experimental extraction of the symmetry energy coefficient by comparing isoscaling parameters from reactions with different isospin asymmetries.
