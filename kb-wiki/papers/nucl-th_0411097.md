# Isoscaling behavior in the Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model

> The isoscaling behavior is investigated in the frame of Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) models. The isotopic yields ratio $Y_2$/$Y_1$ for reactions $^{48}Ca$+$^{48}Ca$ and $^{40}Ca+^{40}Ca$ at different entrance channels are simulated and presented, the relationship between the isoscaling parameter and the entrance channel is analyzed, the results show that $α$ and $β$ reduce with the rise of incident energies and increase with the impact parameter b, which can be attributed to the temperature varying of the pre-fragments in different entrance channels. The relation of $α$ and symmetry-term coefficient $C_{sym}$ reveals that the chemical potential difference $\triangleμ$ is sensitive to the symmetry-term coefficient $C_{sym}$.

## Key claim
The isoscaling parameters α and β in IQMD simulations of Ca+Ca collisions decrease with increasing incident energy and increase with impact parameter, which the authors attribute primarily to changes in the temperature of the emitting pre-fragments rather than to changes in chemical potential differences.

## Method
The authors performed IQMD simulations of central collisions (b=0) for ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca at incident energies of 25, 35, 50, and 70 MeV/A, selecting fragments from the neck region using a parallel velocity cut of |v∥/vin| < 0.2. They extracted isoscaling parameters α and β from the exponential fits R₂₁(N) = C'exp(αN) and R₂₁(Z) = C"exp(βZ) to the yield ratios. Additional simulations with ⁶⁰Ca+⁶⁰Ca and varying symmetry-term coefficient Csym were performed at 35 MeV/A to study the relationship between α, (Z/A)², and Csym.

## Key numbers
- At E/A=35 MeV for central collisions (b=0), α = 0.236 and β = -0.326 for the yield ratio Y(⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca)/Y(⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca)
- Incident energies studied: 25, 35, 50, and 70 MeV/A for central collisions
- Normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ used in the mean field
- Skyrme potential parameters: a = -356 MeV, b = 303 MeV, γ = 1.17 (Soft EOS)
- Gaussian wave-packet width L = 2.0 fm² used in the IQMD model
- Yukawa potential parameters: Vy = -0.0024 GeV, m = 0.83
- Fragment selection for (Z/A)² averaging used fragments with 5 ≤ Z ≤ 8

## Context
This work extends the isoscaling studies of Tsang (2001) and the IQMD model development of Zhang, Wei, and Liu to systematically investigate how entrance channel conditions affect the isoscaling parameters. The results confirm the linear relationship between α and (Z/A)² predicted by the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Botvina and the lattice gas model (LGM) of Ma (2004), while showing that α is sensitive to the symmetry-term coefficient Csym. This enables future experimental extraction of the symmetry energy coefficient by comparing isoscaling parameters from reactions with different isospin asymmetries.
