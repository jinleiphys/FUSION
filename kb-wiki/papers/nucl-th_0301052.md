# An alternative model of jet suppression at RHIC energies

> We propose a simple Glauber-type mechanism for suppression of jet production up to transverse momenta of about 10 GeV/c at RHIC. For processes in this kinematic region, the formation time is smaller than the interval between two successive hard partonic collisions and the subsequent collision influences the jet production. Number of jets then roughly scales with the number of participants. Proportionality to the number of binary collisions is recovered for very high transverse momenta. The model predicts suppression of jet production in d+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that jet suppression at RHIC for transverse momenta up to about 10 GeV/c arises from a simple Glauber-type nuclear absorption mechanism, rather than from energy loss in quark-gluon plasma; the suppression occurs because the formation time of medium-pT jets is smaller than the time interval between successive hard partonic collisions, allowing subsequent collisions to interfere with jet production.

## Method

The authors construct a Glauber model in which the cross-section for destruction of jets during their formation by an incident nucleon is parametrized as a function of pT, with an absorptive cross-section that vanishes for high pT. They compute the ratio of jet yields in nucleus-nucleus collisions to those in proton-proton collisions, integrating over the nuclear overlap geometry with constant nuclear densities, and evaluate the results via Monte Carlo simulation for Au+Au and d+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The mean free path of a nucleon in a nucleus at rest is lambda ≈ 2.5 fm; in the RHIC center-of-mass frame this is Lorentz contracted to about 0.025 fm, giving a time interval between successive nucleon-nucleon collisions of Δt ≈ 0.025 fm/c.
- The limiting longitudinal momentum transfer for processes to be completed before the next nucleon arrives is ΔpL ≈ 8 GeV/c at RHIC (compared to ≈ 0.8 GeV/c at SPS energies).
- The absorptive cross-section is parametrized with pT0 ≈ 8 GeV/c and σ0 of order a few mb; the authors use σ0 = 8 mb for their calculations.
- The non-diffractive nucleon-nucleon cross-section is taken as σnn = 4 fm².
- The standard nuclear density used is 0.138 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-type absorption formalism previously applied to J/ψ suppression in heavy-ion collisions (Gerschel and Hüfner, Review) to the case of jet production. It contrasts with the dominant interpretation at the time that jet suppression at RHIC is due to parton energy loss in quark-gluon plasma (Vitev and Gyulassy 2002; Wang 1995; Wiedemann 2000; Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev 2000; Hirano and Nara 2002; Baier and Mueller 2002). The model predicts suppression in d+Au collisions, which the authors note was also predicted by Kharzeev, Levin, and McLerran (2002) via parton saturation; the authors suggest the two approaches may describe similar effects. The work enables future inclusion of fragmentation functions and parton energy loss effects to produce hadronic spectra for direct comparison with data.
