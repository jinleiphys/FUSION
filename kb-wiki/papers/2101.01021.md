# A survey of heavy-antiheavy hadronic molecules

> Many efforts have been made to reveal the nature of the overabundant resonant structures observed by the worldwide experiments in the last two decades. Hadronic molecules attract special attention because many of these seemingly unconventional resonances are located close to the threshold of a pair of hadrons. To give an overall feature of the spectrum of hadronic molecules composed of a pair of heavy-antiheavy hadrons, namely, which pairs are possible to form molecular states, we take charmed hadrons for example to investigate the interaction between them and search for poles by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation. We consider all possible combinations of hadron pairs of the $S$-wave singly-charmed mesons and baryons as well as the narrow $P$-wave charmed mesons. The interactions, which are assumed to be meson-exchange saturated, are described by constant contact terms which are resummed to generate poles. It turns out that if a system is attractive near threshold by the light meson exchange, there is a pole close to threshold corresponding to a bound state or a virtual state, depending on the strength of interaction and the cutoff. In total, 229 molecular states are predicted. The observed near-threshold structures with hidden-charm, like the famous $X(3872)$ and $P_c$ states, fit into the spectrum we obtain. We also highlight a $Λ_c\bar Λ_c$ bound state that has a pole consistent with the cross section of the $e^+e^-\toΛ_c\bar Λ_c$ precisely measured by the BESIII Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic survey of possible hadronic molecules composed of a pair of heavy-antiheavy hadrons, predicting 229 molecular states in total, but the existence of each state depends on the strength of the interaction and the cutoff used in the Bethe-Salpeter equation, meaning that not all predicted poles necessarily correspond to observable bound states.

## Method

The authors considered all possible combinations of S-wave singly-charmed mesons and baryons as well as narrow P-wave charmed mesons, assuming the interactions between hadron pairs are saturated by light meson exchange and described by constant contact terms. They solved the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation to search for poles near threshold, using potentials derived from vector meson exchange with coupling constants constrained by heavy quark spin symmetry, chiral symmetry, and SU(3) flavor symmetry. Coupled-channel effects were not included in the analysis.

## Key numbers

- 229 molecular states are predicted in total across all considered heavy-antiheavy hadron combinations
- The coupling constant for S-wave charmed mesons to vector mesons is \(\beta = 0.9\), and for P-wave charmed mesons \(\beta_2 \approx -0.9\)
- The coupling constant for sextet baryons is \(\beta_S = -2\beta_B\) with \(\beta_B\) taking values 1.44, 2.06, or 1.74 depending on the estimation method
- The vector meson coupling constant is \(g_V = 5.8\) and the pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 92\) MeV
- The \(\Lambda_c \bar \Lambda_c\) bound state has a pole consistent with the cross section of \(e^+e^- \to \Lambda_c \bar \Lambda_c\) precisely measured by the BESIII Collaboration

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadronic molecules such as those by Peng (2020) focusing on \(\bar D^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}\) systems, and by various authors including Liu (2007), Ding (2009), Wu (2010), Yang (2011), and Valderrama (2019) who used different models and conventions with sometimes inconsistent results. The systematic spectrum enables future studies of near-threshold line shapes in invariant mass distributions, as discussed by Dong (2020), and provides a unified framework for interpreting observed exotic states like the \(X(3872)\) and \(P_c\) states as hadronic molecules.
