# Effects of chiral symmetry restoration on dilepton production in heavy ion collisions

> Because of their weak interactions with the strongly interacting matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, dileptons provide an ideal probe of the early dynamics of these collisions. Here, we study dilepton production using a partonic transport model that is based on an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. In this model, the in-medium quark masses decrease with increasing temperature as a result of the restoration of chiral symmetry. We find that the extracted temperature from dileptons of intermediate masses agrees well with the temperature of the partonic matter, suggesting that dilepton production can be used as a thermometer for the produced partonic matter. Our results also indicate that the extracted in-medium quark masses decrease with increasing dilepton temperature, implying that dilepton production can further serve as a probe of chiral symmetry restoration in high energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The study claims that the temperature extracted from the intermediate-mass region of dilepton spectra agrees well with the actual temperature of the partonic matter, and that the extracted in-medium quark masses decrease with increasing dilepton temperature, suggesting dilepton production can serve as both a thermometer and a probe of chiral symmetry restoration. The hedge is that these conclusions are drawn from a partonic transport model based on an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, not from experimental data, and the agreement is best for the higher invariant mass sub-region (IMR-II: 2-3 GeV).

## Method

The authors used a partonic transport model with interactions described by a three-flavor NJL model including scalar, vector, and Kobayashi-Maskawa-t'Hooft (KMT) interactions, solved in the mean-field approximation. Dilepton production from quark-antiquark annihilation was calculated perturbatively using the stochastic collision criterion, with quarks and antiquarks propagated via Hamilton's equations of motion that include effective vector fields and in-medium quark masses. The temperature of the partonic matter was extracted by fitting the dilepton invariant mass spectrum using an analytic formula derived from the Maxwell-Boltzmann approximation, and compared to the weighted mean temperature of the medium cells.

## Key numbers

* The extracted dilepton temperature from the IMR (1-3 GeV) for a box calculation at medium temperature 200 MeV was 202 MeV; at 300 MeV it was 307 MeV; at 400 MeV it was 420 MeV (Table I).
* The extracted dilepton temperature from the IMR-II sub-region (2-3 GeV) for a box calculation at medium temperature 200 MeV was 201 MeV; at 300 MeV it was 297 MeV; at 400 MeV it was 401 MeV (Table I).
* The STAR Collaboration extracted dilepton temperatures of 280 +/- 64 (stat.) +/- 10 (syst.) MeV for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV and 303 +/- 59 (stat.) +/- 28 (syst.) MeV at 54.4 GeV (from the paper's introduction).
* The expanding fireball simulations used initial central net-baryon density rho_0 = 0.5 fm^{-3}, Woods-Saxon radius R_WS = 6 fm, surface thickness a_WS = 0.6 fm, and initial temperatures T_0 from 200 to 400 MeV.
* The effective quark mass extracted from dilepton spectra increased as the quark matter expanded and cooled, consistent with the gap equation predictions at constant baryon density rho_B = 0.3 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production as a probe of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly the temperature extraction method suggested by Rapp (2014) and Churchill (2023). It differs from effective many-body theory models (Rapp, 2013) and transport models like PHSD (Linnyk, 2011; Bratkovskaya, 2012) and UrQMD (Endres, 2014) by using a partonic transport model based on the NJL model that explicitly includes the temperature dependence of quark masses from chiral symmetry restoration. The results enable future studies using more realistic models to quantify the temperature dependence of chiral symmetry restoration from dilepton spectra at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies.
