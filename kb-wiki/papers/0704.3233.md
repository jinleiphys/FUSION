# Slowly rotating pulsars

> In the present work we investigate one possible variation on the usual static pulsars: the inclusion of rotation. We use a formalism proposed by Hartle and Thorne to calculate the properties of rotating pulsars with all possible compositions. All calculations were performed for zero temperature and also for fixed entropy equations of state.

## Key claim

The paper applies the Hartle-Thorne perturbative formalism to compute the properties of slowly rotating pulsars (hadronic, hybrid, and quark stars) at zero and finite temperature, finding that rotation produces a small increase in maximum mass relative to non-rotating configurations and that only the conversion of a hadronic star into a hybrid star at fixed baryonic mass releases energy compatible with a short gamma ray burst.

## Method

The authors use the Hartle-Thorne perturbative approximation to treat rotation as a perturbation on non-rotating stellar configurations, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for the background and then integrating the perturbation equations for the metric functions. Equations of state are computed for hadronic matter (non-linear Walecka model), hybrid matter (hadronic phase with Walecka model plus quark phase with MIT bag model), and quark matter (MIT bag model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) at fixed entropies S=0, 1, and 2 (in units of Boltzmann's constant) to account for zero-temperature and finite-temperature protoneutron star conditions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of non-rotating hadronic star at S=0: 2.04 solar masses; rotating counterpart at Kepler frequency: 2.07 solar masses
- Maximum mass of non-rotating hybrid star at S=0: 1.63 solar masses; rotating counterpart: 1.65 solar masses
- Maximum mass of non-rotating quark star (MIT model) at S=0: 1.22 solar masses; rotating counterpart: 1.25 solar masses
- Maximum mass of non-rotating quark star (NJL model) at S=0: 1.20 solar masses; rotating counterpart: 1.23 solar masses
- Kepler angular velocity for hadronic star at S=0: 1661.37 Hz; for quark star (MIT) at S=0: 2938.89 Hz; for quark star (NJL) at S=0: 2316.71 Hz
- Eccentricities for all models range from 0.43 to 0.49
- Moment of inertia for hadronic star at S=0: 2.39 x 10^45 g cm^2; for quark star (MIT) at S=0: 0.53 x 10^45 g cm^2
- Energy released in conversion of hadronic (metastable) to hybrid (stable) star at fixed baryonic mass of 1.56 solar masses: 1.14 x 10^51 erg

## Context

This work extends previous studies of rotating neutron stars by Berti et al. (2005) and earlier works that used simpler equations of state at zero temperature, by including finite-temperature effects (fixed entropies S=1 and S=2) relevant for protoneutron stars and by considering all possible compositions (hadronic, hybrid, and quark stars). The results enable future investigation of phase transitions in rotating stellar matter and their connection to gamma ray bursts, as the authors find that only the hadronic-to-hybrid conversion at fixed baryonic mass releases energy compatible with short gamma ray bursts.
