# Probing vorticity through femtoscopic correlations

> In heavy-ion collisions, as the two nuclei pass through one another and create hot and dense matter, part of their initial angular momentum is transferred to the fireball, generating a nonzero average vorticity. Understanding heavy-ion collision dynamics and its influence on key observables, including those used to probe the initial state or assess thermodynamics of nuclear matter, requires understanding the magnitude of effects tied to vorticity. In this work, we use simulations of non-central Au+Au collisions at $E_{\rm{kin}}=1.23~A\rm{GeV}$ to show that the rotation of the system impacts the space-time picture of particle emission and, in particular, leaves imprints on proton-pion femtoscopic correlations. Next, we use coarse-graining of the simulation outputs to extract the collective velocity as a function of position and time, shedding light on the dynamical origin of this effect. Moreover, we demonstrate that the displacement between the proton and pion emission centers quantifies the strength of the rotation and propose it as a new signal of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that vorticity in heavy-ion collisions leaves an imprint on proton-pion femtoscopic correlations, specifically through a displacement between the average emission points of protons and pions, and proposes the angle between the pair velocity and the average pair separation as a new observable for quantifying vorticity. The claim is conditional on using an appropriate non-Gaussian ansatz for the source function when extracting source sizes from experimental correlation functions.

## Method

The authors simulated midcentral (b=6.6 fm, 15-25% centrality) Au+Au collisions at E_kin=1.23 AGeV using the UrQMD transport model with a Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field model (CMF) equation of state. They extracted proton-pion source functions from 4x10^6 collision events and computed femtoscopic correlation functions using the Coral package, which incorporates experimentally measured phase shifts and Coulomb forces. The collective flow velocity was extracted via coarse-graining of the simulation outputs at t=20 fm/c, considering only warm (T > 50 MeV) and dense (n > 0.16 fm^{-3}) regions.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: E_kin = 1.23 AGeV for Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameter: b = 6.6 fm (15-25% centrality bin)
- Nuclear incompressibility of the CMF model: K_0 = 267 MeV
- Source functions calculated at t = 50 fm/c
- Seven pair velocities studied: v^x_{p pi}/c in {0.55, 0.60, 0.65, 0.69, 0.73, 0.76, 0.78}
- Selection cuts: |v^y_{p pi}/c| < 0.05 and |v^z_{p pi}/c| < 0.05
- The region with flow parallel to the x-axis is located approximately 1 fm from the z = 0 fm line
- Coulomb suppression dominates for |q| less than approximately 50 MeV/c
- Delta++ resonance attraction dominates for |q| greater than approximately 100 MeV/c
- Pion detection threshold: p_T < 50 MeV/c typically undetectable; proton statistics limited for p_T > 1.0 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends studies of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions beyond the traditional approach of Lambda hyperon polarization measurements (STAR Collaboration, 2017; HADES Collaboration, 2022), which suffer from systematic uncertainties due to nuclear spin alignment and freeze-out hypersurface effects. The method is complementary to polarization measurements and is particularly useful at low collision energies (e.g., at FRIB) where Lambda baryons are not produced, or in asymmetric systems such as p+A collisions where flow may form smoke-ring structures with vanishing global polarization (Lisa, 2021). The results are consistent across simulation frameworks, having been verified with both UrQMD and SMASH transport codes.
