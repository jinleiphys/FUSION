# Local nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions within chiral effective field theory

> To obtain an understanding of the structure and reactions of nuclear systems from first principles has been a long-standing goal of nuclear physics. In this respect, few- and many-body systems provide a unique laboratory for studying nuclear interactions. During the past decades, the development of accurate representations of the nuclear force has undergone substantial progress. Particular emphasis has been devoted to chiral effective field theory (EFT), a low-energy effective representation of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Within chiral EFT, many studies have been carried out dealing with the construction of both the nucleon-nucleon ($N\!N$) and three-nucleon ($3N$) interactions. The aim of the present article is to give a detailed overview of the chiral interaction models that are local in configuration space, and show recent results for nuclear systems obtained by employing these local chiral forces.

## Key claim

This review argues that local chiral effective field theory interactions, which depend only on momentum transfer and thus Fourier-transform to functions of relative distance, are necessary for use in quantum Monte Carlo methods, and that such local interactions can be systematically constructed within both delta-less and delta-full chiral EFT frameworks while maintaining the connection to QCD symmetries.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical derivation of local nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions within chiral EFT, distinguishing between delta-less and delta-full approaches. They describe how interactions are separated into long-range pion-exchange contributions and short-range contact terms, expanded order by order in the chiral expansion (LO, NLO, N2LO, N3LO), and regularized with cutoff functions to remove ultraviolet divergences when solving the Schrodinger equation. The review then presents selected results from quantum Monte Carlo calculations using these local chiral interactions for light and medium-mass nuclei and neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass is approximately 140 MeV, and the rho meson mass is approximately 770 MeV, establishing the separation of scales used in chiral EFT
- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting is approximately 300 MeV, about 2 pion masses, motivating the delta-full approach
- Four-body interactions may contribute at the level of only about 100 keV in He-4 or pure neutron matter
- The u and d quark masses are approximately 2.4 MeV and 4.8 MeV respectively, with the ratio (m_d - m_u)/Lambda_QCD being very small
- Nucleon-nucleon potentials at N3LO and N4LO provide descriptions of NN data bases up to laboratory energies of 300 MeV with chi-squared per datum close to one
- Quantum Monte Carlo methods currently provide reliable solutions for up to A=12 nucleons, with Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo enabling studies up to A=16

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral EFT interactions pioneered by Weinberg (1990-1992) and Ordonez et al. (1995), and contrasts with phenomenological interactions such as Argonne v18 (Wiringa, 1994) and CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2000) that lack a direct connection to QCD. The local chiral interactions reviewed here were derived by Gezerlis et al. (2013-2014) for the delta-less approach and Piarulli et al. (2014-2016) for the delta-full approach, enabling their use in quantum Monte Carlo methods that require local potentials. This work enables systematic uncertainty quantification in ab-initio calculations of nuclear structure and neutron star properties, addressing limitations of phenomenological Hamiltonians like AV18+IL7 that do not provide sufficient repulsion for neutron stars above two solar masses.
