# Effective linear meson model

> The effective action of the linear meson model generates the mesonic n-point functions with all quantum effects included. Based on chiral symmetry and a systematic quark mass expansion we derive relations between meson masses and decay constants. The model ``predicts'' values for f_eta and f_eta' which are compatible with observation. This involves a large momentum dependent eta-eta' mixing angle which is different for the on--shell decays of the eta and the eta'. We also present relations for the masses of the 0^{++} octet. The parameters of the linear meson model are computed and related to cubic and quartic couplings among pseudoscalar and scalar mesons. We also discuss extensions for vector and axialvector fields. In a good approximation the exchange of these fields is responsible for the important nonminimal kinetic terms and the eta-eta' mixing encountered in the linear meson model.

## Key claim

The paper derives relations between meson masses and decay constants from an effective linear meson model based on chiral symmetry and a systematic quark mass expansion, but the "predictions" are conditional on a momentum-dependent eta-eta' mixing angle that differs for on-shell decays of the eta and eta', and the convergence of the expansion depends significantly on whether mixing effects are present.

## Method

The authors construct the most general effective action for the linear meson model (linear sigma model) including a complex 3x3 matrix field Phi transforming under SU_L(3) x SU_R(3), with all invariants consistent with chiral symmetry up to two derivatives. They expand around the vacuum expectation value of Phi in powers of the quark mass parameters Delta_u, Delta_d, Delta_s (which measure deviations of scalar expectation values from their zero-quark-mass values), and combine this with a derivative expansion of the inverse propagators. The analysis uses phenomenological input from M_pi+, M_K+, M_K0, M_eta', f_pi and f_K+ to compute decay constants f_eta and f_eta' and scalar meson masses.

## Key numbers

* f_pi = 92.4 MeV (experimental input for pion decay constant)
* f_K+ = 113 MeV (experimental input for kaon decay constant)
* M_eta = 547.5 MeV (experimental value used to fix one parameter)
* Predicted f_eta/f_pi = 1.23 (compared to experimental 1.06 +/- 0.05)
* Predicted f_eta'/f_pi = 0.91 (compared to experimental 0.81 +/- 0.02)
* Predicted f_pi0/f_pi = 1.00 (compared to experimental 1.00 +/- 0.04)
* (Z_m/Z_h)^(1/2) v = 13.7 MeV (estimate from f_K - f_pi difference)
* Scalar partner of eta predicted at mass (1300-1400) MeV, associated with f_0(1300) resonance

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory (Weinberg 1979, Gasser and Leutwyler 1982) from the nonlinear sigma model for the pseudoscalar octet to a linear meson model that includes the eta', the scalar 0++ octet, and a scalar singlet. It goes beyond earlier investigations (Pisarski and Wilczek 1984, among others) by including chiral symmetry breaking effects in the kinetic terms, which were previously neglected. The results provide a basis for future nonperturbative flow equation calculations of the three-flavor meson model parameters (Jungnickel and Wetterich 1995) and for studies of the temperature dependence of meson masses relevant for heavy ion collisions and the QCD phase transition.
