# Triple nuclear collisions - a new method to explore the matter properties under new extreme conditions

> We suggest to explore an entirely new method to experimentally and theoretically study the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter based on the triple nuclear collisions (TNC). We simulated the TNC using the UrQMD 3.4 model at the beam center-of-mass collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. It is found that in the most central and simultaneous TNC the initial baryonic charge density is about 3 times higher than the one achieved in the usual binary nuclear collisions at the same energies. As a consequence, the production of protons and $Λ$-hyperons is increased by a factor of 2 and 1.5, respectively. Using the MIT Bag model equation we study the evolution of the central cell in TNC and demonstrate that for the top RHIC energy of collision the baryonic chemical potential is 2-2.5 times larger than the one achieved in the binary nuclear collision at the same type of reaction. Based on these estimates, we show that TNC offers an entirely new possibility to study the QCD phase diagram at very high baryonic charge densities.

## Key claim

Triple nuclear collisions (TNC) can produce initial baryonic charge densities about 3 times higher than binary nuclear collisions at the same beam energies, offering a new experimental method to probe the QCD phase diagram at very high baryonic charge densities. The authors hedge that these results are based on UrQMD 3.4 simulations and MIT Bag model estimates, not experimental data.

## Method

The authors simulated Pb+Pb+Pb triple nuclear collisions using the UrQMD 3.4 model at center-of-mass energies of 200 GeV and 2.76 TeV, comparing results to binary Pb+Pb collisions at the same energies. They studied the time evolution of baryonic charge density in a central cell of size 3x3x3 fm³ and used the MIT Bag Model equation of state (with 3 colors, 3 massless quark flavors, and bag constant B_vac^(1/4) = 206 MeV) to extract temperature and baryonic chemical potential from the simulated energy and baryon densities.

## Key numbers

- Initial baryonic charge density in TNC at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: 0.913 fm⁻³, compared to 0.321 fm⁻³ in binary collisions (factor of about 3 higher)
- Initial baryonic charge density in TNC at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: 0.567 fm⁻³, compared to 0.146 fm⁻³ in binary collisions (factor of about 4 higher)
- Proton yield enhancement factor in TNC vs binary collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: factor of 2
- Lambda-hyperon yield enhancement factor in TNC vs binary collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: factor of 1.5
- Baryonic chemical potential in TNC at top RHIC energy: 2-2.5 times larger than in binary collisions at the same energy

## Context

This work extends the study of strongly interacting matter phase diagrams beyond what has been achievable in three decades of binary nuclear (A+A) collisions, where chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement phase transitions are expected at lower collision energies (thresholds around 4-5 GeV and 9-10 GeV respectively, as cited from earlier work by Bugaev and colleagues). The TNC concept, previously introduced by the same group (Vitiuk et al., 2021), enables future experimental searches for phase transformations at high baryonic densities, with possible experimental implementations including super-thin targets, jet targets of metallic droplets, or a third ring perpendicular to two colliding beams.
