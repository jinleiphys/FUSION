# Soft gluon multiplicity distribution revisited

> In this paper the soft gluon radiation from partonic interaction of the type: $2 \to 2$ + gluon has been revisited and a correction term to the widely used Gunion-Bertsch (GB) formula is obtained.

# Soft gluon multiplicity distribution revisited

## Key claim

The widely used Gunion-Bertsch (GB) formula for soft gluon radiation from partonic 2 → 2 + gluon processes requires a correction term that becomes non-negligible in the low temperature domain, contrary to the assumption that the original GB formula is sufficient across all relevant temperatures.

## Method

The authors derive the full invariant amplitude for the process gg → ggg in a thermal medium using Feynman diagram techniques, then isolate the correction term to the squared matrix element beyond the standard Gunion-Bertsch result. They compute the soft gluon multiplicity distribution including this correction, and evaluate the ratio R_c of the corrected distribution to the GB distribution as a function of temperature using s = 18T², m_D = √(4πα_s(T)) T, and q_⊥² obtained from the differential cross section integrated between m_D² and s/4.

## Key numbers

- The correction term to the squared matrix element is: 16g⁶N_c³/(N_c²-1) × q_⊥²/[k_⊥²[(k_⊥-q_⊥)²+m_D²]]
- The corrected soft gluon multiplicity distribution is: dn_g/(dη dk_⊥²) = [dn_g/(dη dk_⊥²)]_GB × (1 + (q_⊥²+m_D²)²/s²)
- The ratio R_c = 1 + (q_⊥²+m_D²)²/s², evaluated with s = 18T² and m_D = √(4πα_s(T)) T
- The lower limit of the q_⊥² integration is m_D² and the upper limit is s/4

## Context

This work extends the Gunion-Bertsch formula (1979) and the subsequent medium-modified version by Wong (1996) for soft gluon radiation in partonic interactions. The correction term derived here is relevant for calculating radiative energy loss of high-energy partons propagating through the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The results can be applied to processes qg → qgg and qq → qqg, and can be extended to heavy quarks by including a dead cone factor F² = k_⊥²/(ω²θ₀²+k_⊥²) as introduced by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001).
