# Nuclear spin features relevant to ab initio nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering

> Background: Effective interactions for elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering from first principles require the use of the same nucleon-nucleon interaction in the structure and reaction calculations, as well as a consistent treatment of the relevant operators at each order. Purpose: Previous work using these interactions has shown good agreement with available data. Here, we study the physical relevance of one of these operators, which involves the spin of the struck nucleon, and examine the interpretation of this quantity in a nuclear structure context. Methods: Using the framework of the spectator expansion and the underlying framework of the no-core shell model, we calculate and examine spin-projected, one-body momentum distributions required for effective nucleon-nucleus interactions in $J=0$ nuclear states. Results: The calculated spin-projected, one-body momentum distributions for $^4$He, $^6$He, and $^8$He display characteristic behavior based on the occupation of protons and neutrons in single particle levels, with more nucleons of one type yielding momentum distributions with larger values. Additionally, we find this quantity is strongly correlated to the magnetic moment of the $2^+$ excited state in the ground state rotational band for each nucleus considered. Conclusions: We find that spin-projected, one-body momentum distributions can probe the spin content of a $J=0$ wave function. This feature may allow future \textit{ab initio} nucleon-nucleus scattering studies to inform spin properties of the underlying nucleon-nucleon interactions. The observed correlation to the magnetic moment of excited states illustrates a previously unknown connection between reaction observables such as the analyzing power and structure observables like the magnetic moment.

## Key claim

The spin-projected, one-body momentum distribution \(S_n(q)\) for \(J=0\) nuclear states is strongly correlated with the magnetic moment of the \(2^+\) excited state in the ground state rotational band, suggesting that spin information normally accessible only through excited-state observables is already encoded in the ground-state wave function and can be probed by reaction calculations.

## Method

The authors used the spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory combined with no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations to compute the spin-projected, one-body momentum distribution \(S_n(\bm{q},\bm{\mathcal{K}})\) for the \(J=0\) ground states of \(^4\)He, \(^6\)He, and \(^8\)He. They integrated this distribution over the average momentum \(\mathcal{K}\) to obtain \(S_n(q)\) as a function of momentum transfer, and further integrated over \(q\) to obtain the scalar quantity \(S_n\). Toy-model calculations with frozen harmonic-oscillator shells were used to interpret the shell-structure dependence, and correlation coefficients between \(S_n\) and magnetic moments of the \(2^+\) states were computed using the impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

* For \(^8\)C, the correlation coefficient between the magnetic moment of the \(2^+\) state and \(S_n\) is \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{C}}_{\mu,S_n}=0.990\).
* For \(^{12}\)C, the correlation coefficient between the magnetic moment of the \(2^+\) state and \(S_n\) is \(\zeta^{^{12}\mathrm{C}}_{\mu,S_n}=0.994\).
* For \(^8\)He, the correlation coefficient between the magnetic moment of the \(2^+\) state and \(S_n\) is \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{He}}_{\mu,S_n}=-0.983\).
* When considering only the spin component of the magnetic moment, the correlation coefficients become \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{C}}_{\mu_s, S_n}=0.996\), \(\zeta^{^{12}\mathrm{C}}_{\mu_s, S_n}=0.995\), and \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{He}}_{\mu_s, S_n}=-0.993\).
* For \(^8\)He, the neutron-spin contribution to the correlation is \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{He}}_{\mu^{\mathrm{neu}}_s, S^{\mathrm{neu}}_n}=0.992\), while the proton-spin contribution is \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{He}}_{\mu^{\mathrm{pro}}_s, S^{\mathrm{pro}}_n}=-0.791\).
* For \(^8\)C, the proton-spin contribution to the correlation is \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{C}}_{\mu^{\mathrm{pro}}_s, S^{\mathrm{pro}}_n}=0.994\), while the neutron-spin contribution is \(\zeta^{^8\mathrm{C}}_{\mu^{\mathrm{neu}}_s, S^{\mathrm{neu}}_n}=-0.803\).
* For \(^{12}\)C, both proton and neutron spin contributions are strongly correlated: \(\zeta^{^{12}\mathrm{C}}_{\mu^{\mathrm{pro}}_s, S^{\mathrm{pro}}_n}=0.995\) and \(\zeta^{^{12}\mathrm{C}}_{\mu^{\mathrm{neu}}_s, S^{\mathrm{neu}}_n}=0.994\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Burrows (2020) for including the spin of the struck target nucleon in leading-order effective nucleon-nucleus interactions, where previous calculations showed that the analyzing power \(A_y\) and spin-rotation parameter \(Q\) for proton elastic scattering on \(^6\)He and \(^8\)He exhibited larger deviations than for closed-shell nuclei like \(^4\)He and \(^{16}\)O. The observed correlation between \(S_n\) and the magnetic moment of the \(2^+\) rotational-band state establishes a previously unknown connection between reaction observables (such as analyzing power) and structure observables (such as magnetic moments), and suggests that future ab initio nucleon-nucleus scattering studies could be sensitive to spin properties of the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction.
