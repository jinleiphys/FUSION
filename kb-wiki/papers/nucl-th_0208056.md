# Multi-Quark Hadrons and S=-2 Hypernuclei

> The general character of 4-quark (mesonic) and strange 6-quark (baryonic) quark systems is very briefly reviewed a la Jaffe, i.e. in the MIT bag, and so far still possibly viable candidates are indicated. Concentration is on S=-2 systems. Traditionally, one employs the (K^-,K^+) reaction on a relatively light target and hopes to retain two units of strangeness on a single final state fragment. Alternatively, heavy ion reactions can be used to produce Lambda-hyperons copiously and one seeks to observe coalescence of two of these particles into the lightest S=-2 nucleus, the H-dibaryon. The complications arising from the presence of a repulsive core in the baryon-baryon interaction on the production of the H are discussed. Also considered is the possible presence in the data from the AGS experiment E906, of slightly heavier S=-2 nuclei, in particular_{Lambda Lambda}^{4}H.

# Multi-Quark Hadrons and S=-2 Hypernuclei

## Key claim

The H-dibaryon may not have been observed because repulsive short-range forces in the baryon-baryon interaction prevent two Lambda hyperons from penetrating close enough to form a six-quark bag state, but within a finite nucleus two Lambdas held together for ~100 picoseconds might overcome this barrier and produce a hybrid H containing both dibaryon and six-quark components.

## Method

The authors use the MIT bag model with Jaffe's formalism to compute multi-quark state energies, diagonalizing antisymmetric wave functions in color, spin, and flavor and minimizing with respect to bag radius. For H-dibaryon production in heavy ion collisions, they model coalescence of two Lambda hyperons into a bag as a two-step process: formation of a deuteron-like state followed by barrier penetration through a repulsive core, using a potential derived from the Bonn model with omega and sigma meson exchange. They analyze data from AGS experiment E906, which uses the (K^-,K^+) reaction on a ^9Be target to produce tagged Xi^- beams that convert to Lambda pairs, searching for correlated two-pion decays from S=-2 hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The bag model parameters used are: gluon exchange coupling alpha_c = 0.55, bag constant B^(1/4) = 146 MeV, vacuum energy z_0/R = 1.84, and strange quark mass m_s = 279 MeV
- Jaffe's original estimate placed the H-dibaryon some 80 MeV bound
- Mass range for (q q-bar)^2 states is from 1460 MeV (S=0) to 2140 MeV (S=4)
- The pi_1(1400) candidate has mass 1370 +/- 10 (+50/-30) MeV and width 385 +/- 40 (+65/-105) MeV
- Previous calculation suggested ~0.07 H-dibaryons per central Au + Au collision
- For baryon center separations of 0.25 to 0.35 fermis, tracking sensitivity of 10^-4 to 10^-2 relative to background would be required
- The E906 experiment recorded 1040 events in the total two-prong sample, with ~70 above background in the first feature
- A lower limit of 40+20 _Lambda Lambda^4 H events were produced in two decay modes
- The Lambda-Lambda pairing energy Delta B_LambdaLambda is estimated at ~1-2 MeV from one projection and ~0.5-1.0 MeV from another
- The Xi^- beam from 1.8 GeV K^- has kinetic energy ~140 MeV

## Context

This work extends Jaffe's (1977) original MIT bag model prediction of the H-dibaryon and addresses the puzzle of its non-observation in searches such as BNL experiment E896 (Crawford). The authors introduce a repulsive core mechanism previously overlooked in production rate estimates by Kahana and Dover and others. The analysis of E906 data builds on earlier emulsion experiments by Aoki (KEK), Prowse, and Danysz et al. that found candidate double hypernuclei with pairing energies ~4.5 MeV (Millener), which the present work suggests may be lower (~1 MeV). The results enable future searches at the AGS or Japanese Hadron Facility, and more recent emulsion experiments have since definitively identified _Lambda Lambda^6 He with Delta B_LambdaLambda less than 1 MeV.
