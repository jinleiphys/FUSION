# Forward-backward rapidity correlations in relativistic heavy ion collisions : torqued fireball

> Statistical fluctuations in the transverse distribution of sources in relativistic heavy ion collisions and an asymmetric emission profile associated with the wounded nucleons lead to rapidity dependence of the reaction plane. The size of this effect is estimated for the gold-gold collisions at the highest RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 \mathrm{GeV}$) in the Glauber model using GLISSANDO. The hydrodynamical evolution of the resulting torqued fireball is carried out in 3+1 dimensions considering a perfect fluid. Hadronization is simulated using THERMINATOR including non-flow contribution coming from resonance decays. Some experimental measures that can be used to detect the torque effect are proposed.

## Key claim

Statistical fluctuations in the transverse distribution of sources and an asymmetric emission profile associated with forward- and backward-moving wounded nucleons cause the reaction plane to depend on rapidity, producing a "torqued fireball" whose principal axes twist in opposite directions at forward and backward rapidities. The effect is estimated to be of order 20 degrees for central collisions and 10 degrees for mid-peripheral collisions at RHIC, but it is significantly washed away during hadronization.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO Monte-Carlo generator within the Glauber mixed model to simulate the initial energy density of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, with forward- and backward-moving wounded nucleons having asymmetric emission profiles in rapidity. They then performed 3+1 dimensional perfect-fluid hydrodynamical evolution of the resulting torqued fireball, followed by hadronization simulation with THERMINATOR including non-flow contributions from resonance decays. Finally, they proposed experimental measures based on two- and four-particle cumulants using particles from different rapidity bins to detect the torque effect.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the expected size of the forward-backward angle difference Delta_FB for elliptic deformation is 20 degrees for central collisions and 10 degrees for mid-peripheral collisions.
- In the hydrodynamical simulation for centrality c=20-25%, the initial rotation angle applied to the density of forward- and backward-moving wounded nucleons was 5 degrees in opposite directions.
- The estimate of the difference between the fireball angle and the event angle was computed for multiplicities n=600, 100, and 20, with flow coefficients v_k=5% and v_k=2%.
- The cutoff in transverse momentum for the THERMINATOR simulation including resonance decays was 450 MeV < p_T < 3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the wounded nucleon model approach of Bialas (1976, 2008) and the torqued fireball picture introduced by Bozek (2010) by providing quantitative estimates of the torque effect and proposing experimental observables. The hydrodynamical simulation follows the method of Bozek (2009), and the hadronization uses the THERMINATOR framework from Broniowski (2007). The results suggest that the torque effect should be detectable in mid-central or mid-peripheral collisions at RHIC using forward and backward TPCs, which would support the initial assumptions about fluctuations and asymmetric emission functions in the early stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions.
