# Microscopic Approach to Nucleon Spectra in Hypernuclear Non-Mesonic Weak Decay

> A consistent microscopic diagrammatic approach is applied for the first time to the calculation of the nucleon emission spectra in the non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei. We adopt a nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, a one--meson exchange weak transition potential and a Bonn nucleon-nucleon strong potential. Ground state correlations and final state interactions, at second order in the nucleon--nucleon interaction, are introduced on the same footing for all the isospin channels of one- and two-nucleon induced decays. Single and double--coincidence nucleon spectra are predicted for 12_Lambda^C and compared with recent KEK and FINUDA data. The key role played by quantum interference terms allows us to improve the predictions obtained with intranuclear cascade codes. Discrepancies with data remain for proton emission.

## Key claim

A fully quantum-mechanical microscopic diagrammatic approach, including ground state correlations and final state interactions at second order in the nucleon-nucleon interaction, is applied for the first time to nucleon emission spectra in hypernuclear non-mesonic weak decay; the key role of quantum interference terms allows improvement over intranuclear cascade codes, but discrepancies with data remain for proton emission.

## Method

The authors adopt a nuclear matter formalism extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, using a one-meson exchange weak transition potential (with pseudoscalar and vector mesons from the Nijmegen soft-core interaction NSC97f) and a Bonn nucleon-nucleon strong potential. Ground state correlations and final state interactions are introduced on the same footing at second order in the nucleon-nucleon interaction for all isospin channels of one- and two-nucleon induced decays, with the many-body contributions represented by in-medium Lambda self-energy Feynman diagrams. Single and double-coincidence nucleon spectra are predicted for 12_Lambda^C and compared with KEK and FINUDA data.

## Key numbers

- Total non-mesonic decay width for 12_Lambda^C: Gamma_NM = 0.99 (in units of the free decay rate), compared to KEK-E508 value 0.95 +/- 0.04
- One-nucleon induced decay width: Gamma_1 = 0.62, compared to KEK-E508 value 0.68 +/- 0.13
- Two-nucleon induced decay width: Gamma_2 = 0.36, compared to KEK-E508 value 0.27 +/- 0.13 and FINUDA value 0.24 +/- 0.10
- Ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.33, compared to KEK-E508 value 0.51 +/- 0.13 +/- 0.05
- Ratio Gamma_2/Gamma_NM = 0.37, compared to KEK-E508 value 0.29 +/- 0.13 and FINUDA value 0.24 +/- 0.10
- Predicted two-nucleon decay isospin fractions: Gamma_np/Gamma_2 = 0.84, Gamma_pp/Gamma_2 = 0.12, Gamma_nn/Gamma_2 = 0.04
- Coincidence numbers for 12_Lambda^C with 30 MeV threshold: N_nn = 0.11 (back-to-back) and 0.13 (non-back-to-back), N_np = 0.35 and 0.31, N_pp = 0.04 and 0.05; compared to KEK-E508 values N_nn = 0.083 +/- 0.014 and 0.083 +/- 0.020, N_np = 0.138 +/- 0.014 and 0.060 +/- 0.018, N_pp = 0.005 +/- 0.002

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations by Bauer and collaborators (2007, 2010) that demonstrated negligible effects of ring and RPA contributions on nucleon spectra, and by Garbarino and collaborators (2010) that solved long-standing puzzles on Gamma_n/Gamma_p and the asymmetry parameter. The fully quantum-mechanical approach replaces the semi-classical nucleon rescattering used in intranuclear cascade models such as those by Ramos and collaborators (1997) and Garbarino and collaborators (2003), producing more reliable results for final state interactions especially at low nucleon kinetic energies. The predictions enable future coincidence experiments at J-PARC to measure nucleon spectra and determine Gamma_n, Gamma_p, and Gamma_2 for 12_Lambda^C with improved accuracy, and the microscopic framework is suitable for including new decay mechanisms from fermion antisymmetrization and the Delta(1232) resonance.
