# Physics of Dark Matter in the Light of Dark Atoms

> Direct searches for dark matter lead to serious problems for simple models with stable neutral Weakly Interacting Massive Particles (WIMPs) as candidates for dark matter. A possibility is discussed that new stable quarks and charged leptons exist and are hidden from detection, being bound in neutral dark atoms of composite dark matter. Stable -2 charged particles $O^{--}$ are bound with primordial helium in O-helium (OHe) atoms, being specific nuclear interacting form of composite Warmer than Cold dark matter. Slowed down in the terrestrial matter, OHe is elusive for direct methods of underground dark matter detection based on the search for effects of nuclear recoil in WIMP-nucleus collisions. The positive results of DAMA experiments can be explained as annual modulation of radiative capture of O-helium by nuclei. In the framework of this approach test of DAMA results in detectors with other chemical content becomes a nontrivial task, while the experimental search of stable charged particles at LHC or in cosmic rays acquires a meaning of direct test for composite dark matter scenario.

## Key claim

Direct searches for dark matter face serious problems for simple WIMP models, but the positive results of DAMA experiments can be explained as annual modulation of radiative capture of O-helium (OHe) by nuclei, provided that the OHe-nucleus binding energy for sodium lies in the range 2-4 keV and that no such binding occurs for iodine and thallium.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of composite dark matter where stable -2 charged particles (denoted O−−) bind with primordial helium-4 to form neutral OHe atoms. It analyzes the OHe-nucleus interaction potential using a simplified model treating the nucleus as a uniformly charged sphere and solving the Schrödinger equation with a rectangular well approximation. The rate of radiative capture of OHe by nuclei is calculated by analogy with neutron-proton radiative capture, accounting for isospin nonconservation and suppression of E1 transitions.

## Key numbers

* OHe Bohr orbit radius: approximately 2 × 10⁻¹³ cm
* OHe-nucleus elastic scattering cross section: σ₀ ∼ πr₀² ≈ 10⁻²⁵ cm²
* OHe decoupling temperature from baryonic matter: T_od ≈ 1 S₃²/³ eV, where S₃ = m_o/(1 TeV)
* OHe mass within cosmological horizon at decoupling: M_od ≈ 2 × 10⁴⁴ S₃⁻² g ≈ 10¹¹ S₃⁻² solar masses
* Drift velocity of OHe in terrestrial matter near Earth's surface: V ≈ 80 S₃ A_med¹/² cm/s, with A_med ∼ 30
* Local density of O-helium dark matter: n₀ = 3 × 10⁻⁴ S₃⁻¹ cm⁻³
* Binding energy of Na-OHe system required to explain DAMA results: E_Na = 3 keV
* Fraction of OHe disrupted by cosmic rays in the Galaxy: approximately 10⁻⁶ of total OHe
* Concentration of free O−− near Solar system from OHe disruption: n_O = 3 × 10⁻¹⁰ S₃⁻¹ cm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the composite dark matter scenario originally proposed by Glashow (tera-helium) and addresses the fatal problem identified by Fargion (2005) that stable -1 charged particles lead to anomalous isotope overproduction. The paper builds on prior work by the author and collaborators (Khlopov 2006, 2008) developing the OHe model from various particle physics frameworks including fourth generation quarks, almost-commutative geometry leptons, and walking technicolor. The approach offers a resolution to the apparent contradiction between DAMA/NaI and DAMA/LIBRA positive results and null results from other experiments like XENON100, CDMS, and CoGeNT, by predicting that OHe binds differently with different nuclei depending on their atomic number. The framework enables testable predictions for cosmic ray experiments (PAMELA, AMS02) searching for stable charged particles and for LHC searches for stable -2 charged particles.
