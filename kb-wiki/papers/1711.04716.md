# Solution of n-$^4$He elastic scattering problem using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations

> The first ever numerical solution of five-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations is presented in this work. Modern realistic Nucleon-Nucleon Hamiltonians have been tested when describing low energy elastic neutron scattering on $^4$He nucleus. Obtained results have been compared with those available in the literature and based on solution of the Schrödinger equation.

## Key claim

This work presents the first-ever numerical solution of five-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space, applied to low-energy elastic neutron scattering on \(^4\)He. The results are compared with those from Schrödinger-equation-based methods, but the authors note that approaches based on the Schrödinger equation have "much brighter prospects" for systems with more than five particles, and that the Faddeev-Yakubovsky formalism remains a reference due to its mathematical rigor.

## Method

The five-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations were solved in configuration space using a partial-wave expansion with Lagrange-Laguerre mesh basis functions. Three realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction models (AV18, INOY04, and I-N3LO) were employed, and the K-matrix elements for n-\(^4\)He elastic scattering were extracted using both the Kohn variational principle and the asymptote of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky components. The calculations assumed equal neutron and proton masses with \(\hbar^2/m = 41.471\) MeV fm\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- \(^4\)He ground-state binding energies calculated with the basis used in this work: \(-29.09\) MeV (INOY04), \(-25.24\) MeV (I-N3LO), \(-24.08\) MeV (AV18); these deviate from fully converged literature values by at most 150 keV.
- For I-N3LO potential at \(E_{\text{cm}} = 0.5\) MeV, the \(J^\pi = \frac{1}{2}^+\) phase shift is \(-22.0^\circ\) (Kohn method) and \(-21.3^\circ\) (FY method); for \(J^\pi = \frac{3}{2}^-\) it is \(9.10^\circ\) (Kohn) and \(9.34^\circ\) (FY).
- At \(E_{\text{cm}} = 5.0\) MeV, the \(J^\pi = \frac{1}{2}^+\) phase shift is \(-61.6^\circ\) (Kohn) and \(-62.1^\circ\) (FY); for \(J^\pi = \frac{3}{2}^-\) it is \(109.3^\circ\) (Kohn) and \(111.8^\circ\) (FY).
- The difference between phase shifts extracted via the two methods does not exceed 2%.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-Yakubovsky formalism, previously limited to A=3 and A=4 systems (Glockle, 1996; Lazauskas, 2017), to the five-body case for the first time. It provides a benchmark for comparison with Schrödinger-equation-based approaches such as those by Navratil (2010) and the Pisa group (Viviani, 2010; Barletta, 2009; Kievsky, 2010). The results enable future tests of three-nucleon forces and extension to more complex scattering systems, though the factorial scaling of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky approach limits its applicability beyond five particles.
