## Key claim

The independent-particle shell model (IPSM), despite its simplicity, can reproduce the experimental coincidence spectra for the nonmesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei, suggesting that final-state interactions and two-nucleon induced processes may not be very important for the decay of these hypernuclei, contrary to what some more complex models assume.

## Method

The authors employed two versions of the Independent-Particle Shell-Model (IPSM) to describe the nuclear structure of the final residual nuclei after nonmesonic weak decay: IPSM-a, which includes no correlations beyond the Pauli principle, and IPSM-b, where highly excited hole states are treated as quasi-stationary and described by Breit-Wigner distributions with widths estimated from experimental data. The transition rates were calculated using the one-meson-exchange potential within the Fermi Golden Rule, and the coincidence spectra were evaluated as a function of the sum of kinetic energies E_nN = E_n + E_N for the emitted nucleon pair.

## Key numbers

* The mass difference between the Lambda hyperon and nucleon is Delta M = 176 MeV, which is the energy liberated in the nonmesonic weak decay.
* The free Lambda hyperon decay rate is Gamma^0 = 2.50 x 10^{-6} eV, corresponding to a lifetime tau^0 = 2.63 x 10^{-10} seconds.
* The Q-value for the mesonic weak decay of free Lambda is Q_0 = 37 MeV, while for nonmesonic decay the Q-value is Q_NM ~ 120-135 MeV.
* The pion-nucleon strong coupling constant used is g_NNpi = 13.4.
* The soft vertex-form-factor cutoffs used in the pi+K exchange potential are Lambda_pi = 0.7 GeV and Lambda_K = 0.9 GeV.
* The single-particle energy of the 0s_{1/2} Lambda state ranges from -11.7 MeV for ^{13}_Lambda C to -26.5 MeV for ^{208}_Lambda Pb.
* For ^{12}_Lambda C, the mesonic decay rates relative to the free decay rate are Gamma_pi0/Gamma^0 = 0.217 +/- 0.084 and Gamma_pi-/Gamma^0 = 0.052^{+0.063}_{-0.035}.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to resolve the Gamma_n/Gamma_p puzzle in nonmesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei, which had persisted for nearly two decades with theoretical estimates for the ratio varying between 0.09 and 0.70 for ^{5}_Lambda He and between 0.08 and 0.50 for ^{12}_Lambda C. The paper interprets the recent Brookhaven National Laboratory experiment E788 on ^{4}_Lambda He within the IPSM framework, and shows that the pi+K exchange potential with soft cutoffs can simultaneously account for experimental data on Gamma_p and Gamma_n for ^{4}_Lambda H, ^{4}_Lambda He, and ^{5}_Lambda He. This enables future calculations to use the simpler IPSM approach rather than more complicated models that include final-state interactions and two-nucleon induced processes.
