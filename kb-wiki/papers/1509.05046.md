# Hydrodynamics, resurgence and trans-asymptotics

> The second-order hydrodynamical description of a homogeneous conformal plasma that undergoes a boost- invariant expansion is given by a single nonlinear ordinary differential equation, whose resurgent asymptotic properties we study, developing further the recent work of Heller and Spalinski [Phys. Rev. Lett. 115, 072501 (2015)]. Resurgence clearly identifies the non-hydrodynamic modes that are exponentially suppressed at late times, analogous to the quasi-normal-modes in gravitational language, organizing these modes in terms of a trans-series expansion. These modes are analogs of instantons in semi-classical expansions, where the damping rate plays the role of the instanton action. We show that this system displays the generic features of resurgence, with explicit quantitative relations between the fluctuations about different orders of these non-hydrodynamic modes. The imaginary part of the trans-series parameter is identified with the Stokes constant, and the real part with the freedom associated with initial conditions.

# Hydrodynamics, resurgence and trans-asymptotics

## Key claim

The second-order hydrodynamic gradient expansion for a conformal, boost-invariant plasma is a divergent asymptotic series whose resurgent structure encodes quantitative information about non-hydrodynamic modes (exponentially damped at late times), and the authors show that the full trans-series expansion of the nonlinear ordinary differential equation governing the system displays explicit resurgence relations between fluctuations about different orders of these non-hydrodynamic modes, with the imaginary part of the trans-series parameter identified as the Stokes constant and the real part as the freedom associated with initial conditions.

## Method

The authors reduce the second-order conformal hydrodynamic equations for boost-invariant (Bjorken) flow in d=4 spacetime dimensions to a single first-order nonlinear ordinary differential equation for the dimensionless function f(w), where w = τ T(τ) is the proper time measured in units of local temperature. They construct a trans-series ansatz for f(w) as a sum over exponentially damped terms (non-hydrodynamic modes) each multiplied by a formal asymptotic series in inverse powers of w, and derive recursion relations that determine all coefficients of these series. They then verify the resurgent relations between the hydrodynamic series coefficients and the first non-hydrodynamic series coefficients by generating many terms numerically and comparing with the predicted large-order growth formula.

## Key numbers

* The damping rate (instanton action) for the non-hydrodynamic modes is S = 3/(2 C_τ), which for N=4 super Yang-Mills parameters gives S = 3π/(2 - log 2) ≈ 7.21181
* The exponent β associated with the non-hydrodynamic modes is β = (C_η - 2C_λ)/C_τ, which for N=4 SYM parameters gives β = -3/(4 - 2 log 2) ≈ -1.1478
* The Stokes constant S_1 extracted from the large-order growth of the hydrodynamic series coefficients is S_1 ≈ -0.040883 i for the N=4 SYM parameters (C_η = 1/(4π), C_τ = (2 - log 2)/(2π), C_λ = 1/(2π))
* The leading hydrodynamic expansion for f(w) is f^{(0)}_+(w) ∼ -1/3 + (4C_η/9)(1/w) - (8C_η(C_λ - C_τ)/27)(1/w^2) + ...
* The first non-hydrodynamic series begins as f^{(1)}(w) ∼ 1 + [2(C_η^2 - C_η(C_λ - 6C_τ) + 2C_λ(C_τ - C_λ))]/(3C_τ w) + ...

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Heller and Spalinski (2015), who studied the resummation of the gradient expansion in conformal hydrodynamics for Bjorken flow, by providing a detailed resurgent trans-series analysis of the underlying nonlinear differential equation. The results demonstrate that the divergent asymptotic hydrodynamic expansion encodes quantitative information about non-hydrodynamic modes (analogous to quasi-normal modes in gravitational systems), and the authors argue this resurgence structure should extend to more complicated systems such as those arising from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Policastro, 2002; Kovtun, 2005; Bhattacharyya, 2008; Janik, 2010). The work enables systematic incorporation of exponentially small corrections into hydrodynamic expansions and provides a framework for understanding the relation between the gradient expansion and non-hydrodynamic modes in phenomenological models of heavy ion collisions.
