# Photon-Photon Scattering, Pion Polarizability and Chiral Symmetry

> Recent attempts to detect the pion polarizability via analysis of $γγ\rightarrowππ$ measurements are examined. The connection between calculations based on dispersion relations and on chiral perturbation theory is established by matching the low energy chiral amplitude with that given by a full dispersive treatment. Using the values for the polarizability required by chiral symmetry, predicted and experimental cross sections are shown to be in agreement.

## Key claim

The paper resolves the apparent discrepancy between chiral perturbation theory and dispersive treatments of $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi^0\pi^0$ by showing that the two descriptions are fully consistent when matching is performed, and that the large corrections to the one-loop chiral prediction near threshold arise primarily from final-state rescattering effects (the Omnes function) rather than from new physics or a modified pion polarizability.

## Method

The authors match a twice-subtracted dispersion relation for the helicity-conserving S-wave amplitude to the one-loop chiral perturbation theory result, using the Omnes function to incorporate $\pi\pi$ final-state interactions and fixing subtraction constants via known chiral low-energy constants ($L_9^r+L_{10}^r$). They then refine the calculation by replacing the Padé approximation for the $I=0$ Omnes function with a numerical evaluation from experimental phase shifts, and by adding contributions from $\rho$, $\omega$, and $A1$ meson exchanges to the $\gamma\pi\rightarrow\gamma\pi$ Compton amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Chiral low-energy constant combination: $L_9^r+L_{10}^r = (1.43 \pm 0.27) \times 10^{-3}$, determined from radiative pion decay.
- Chiral symmetry prediction for charged pion polarizability: $\bar{\alpha}_E^{\pi^+} = 2.7 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- Chiral symmetry prediction for neutral pion polarizability: $\bar{\alpha}_E^{\pi^0} = -0.5 \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- Experimental values for charged pion polarizability from different processes: $(6.8 \pm 1.4 \pm 1.2) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$ (radiative pion-nucleon scattering), $(20 \pm 12) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$ (pion photoproduction), $(2.2 \pm 1.6) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$ ($\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$).
- Experimental values for neutral pion polarizability from $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$: $|\bar{\alpha}_E^{\pi^0}| = (0.69 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.04) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$ and $(0.8 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$.
- Coupling constants for vector meson exchange: $R_\omega = 1.35$ GeV$^{-2}$, $R_\rho = 0.12$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Padé approximation constants for Omnes functions: $k_0 \cong 1/(25 m_\pi^2)$, $k_2 = -1/(30 m_\pi^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive approach of Morgan and Pennington (1990) by explicitly matching to chiral perturbation theory, and corrects the one-loop chiral analysis of Babusci et al. (1992) which claimed a 40% deviation from the chiral polarizability prediction for neutral pions. The authors show that the full dispersive calculation, including final-state rescattering via the Omnes function and vector meson exchanges, brings both charged and neutral pion cross sections into agreement with data using the chiral symmetry values for the polarizability. This enables a consistent framework for extracting pion polarizabilities from $\gamma\gamma\rightarrow\pi\pi$ data and highlights the need for remeasurement of the radiative pion scattering process, which currently disagrees with the chiral prediction at the three-sigma level.
