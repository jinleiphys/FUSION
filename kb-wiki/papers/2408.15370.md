# Infrared properties of the quark-gluon vertex in general kinematics

> In the present work we determine the eight form factors of the transversely-projected quark-gluon vertex in general kinematics, in the context of Landau-gauge QCD with two degenerate light dynamical quarks. The study is based on the set of Schwinger-Dyson equations that govern the vertex form factors, derived within the formalism of the three-particle-irreducible (3PI) effective action. The analysis is performed by employing lattice data for the main ingredients, such as gluon and quark propagators, and three-gluon vertex. The numerical treatment is simplified by decoupling the system of integral equations: the classical form factor is determined from a single non-linear equation involving only itself, while the remaining ones are subsequently computed through simple integrations. The form factors are obtained for arbitrary values of space-like momenta, and their angular dependence is examined in detail. A clear hierarchy is established at the level of the corresponding dimensionless effective couplings, in agreement with results of earlier studies. Furthermore, the classical form factor is found to be in excellent agreement with recent unquenched lattice data in the soft-gluon configuration, while the two non-classical dressings depart substantially from the lattice results. Finally, the accurate implementation of multiplicative renormalizability is confirmed, and the transition from Minkoswski to Euclidean space is elucidated.

## Key claim

The eight form factors of the transversely-projected quark-gluon vertex are computed for arbitrary space-like momenta using a decoupled system of Schwinger-Dyson equations, where the classical form factor is determined from a single non-linear equation and the remaining seven are obtained through simple integrations, with the caveat that the two non-classical dressings depart substantially from lattice results even though the classical form factor shows excellent agreement.

## Method

The authors employed the three-particle-irreducible (3PI) effective action formalism at three-loop order to derive the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the eight form factors of the transversely-projected quark-gluon vertex in Landau-gauge QCD with two degenerate light dynamical quarks. They decoupled the system by retaining only the classical form factor in the kernels of the integral equations, using lattice data for gluon and quark propagators and the three-gluon vertex as inputs. The classical form factor was solved from a single non-linear integral equation, then substituted into the kernels to compute the remaining seven form factors through simple integrations.

## Key numbers

- The classical form factor in the soft-gluon limit shows a 7% departure from lattice data of Kizilersu (2021) in the deep infrared, which can be further reduced through minimal adjustments of the three-gluon vertex dressing
- The analysis uses lattice inputs for N_f=2 gluon propagators from Ayala (2012) and Binosi (2016), and N_f=2 quark propagators from Oliveira (2018) and Kizilersu (2021)
- The three-gluon vertex input uses minor variations around the best fit to N_f=2+1 lattice data from Aguilar (2019)
- The renormalization condition sets lambda_1 at the subtraction point mu^2 equal to 1, with A_R(mu^2)=1 and Delta^{-1}_R(mu^2)=mu^2

## Context

This work extends the continuum nonperturbative study of the quark-gluon vertex using the 3PI effective action approach, building on earlier studies by Mitter (2014), Cyrol (2017), and Gao (2021) that established hierarchies among form factors. It incorporates recent developments in understanding the infrared structure of the three-gluon vertex, particularly the planar degeneracy property validated by Eichmann (2014), Pinto-Gomez (2022), Ferreira (2023), and Aguilar (2023). The results enable improved modeling of the quark-gluon vertex for studies of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, constituent quark masses, and bound state formation in QCD.
