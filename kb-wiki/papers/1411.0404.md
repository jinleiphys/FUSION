# Equation of state of nuclear matter from empirical constraints

> From empirically determined values of some of the characteristic constants associated with homogeneous nuclear matter at saturation and sub-saturation densities, within the framework of a Skyrme-inspired energy density functional, we construct an equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter.This EoS is then used to predict values of density slope parameters of symmetry energy $L(ρ)$, isoscalar incompressibility $K(ρ)$ and a few related quantities. The close consonance of our predicted values with the currently available ones for the density dependence of symmetry energy and incompressibility gleaned from diverse approaches offers the possibility that our method may help in settling their values in tighter bounds. Extrapolation of our EoS at supranormal densities shows that it is in good harmony with the one extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

Using only five empirically constrained bulk parameters of nuclear matter (saturation density, energy per nucleon, incompressibility, and symmetry energy at two densities) within a Skyrme-inspired energy density functional, the authors construct an equation of state that predicts the density slope parameter of symmetry energy L0 = 65.4 ± 13.5 MeV and the symmetry incompressibility Kτ = −321.6 ± 34.4 MeV, with the hedge that these predictions depend on the choice of the Skyrme functional form and the assumption of quadratic isospin asymmetry dependence.

## Method

The authors employ a Skyrme-type energy density functional with six unknown parameters and fix them using five empirical constraints: the saturation density ρ0, energy per nucleon e0, and incompressibility K0 for symmetric nuclear matter, plus the symmetry energy esym at ρ0 and at ρ = 0.1 fm−3. The sixth parameter is determined by imposing that the symmetry energy defined via the curvature of the energy with respect to isospin asymmetry equals the symmetry energy defined as the difference between pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter, which selects a specific value of the symmetry slope parameter L0.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using empirical nuclear bulk parameters to constrain the equation of state, building on prior analyses of nuclear masses and neutron skin thickness by Agrawal (2012, 2013) and on constraints from giant monopole and dipole resonances by Trippa (2008) and Khan (2012). The predicted L0 agrees with values from pygmy dipole resonance analysis by Colo (2014) and from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana (2009), while the predicted Kτ is consistent with the value of −370 ± 120 MeV extracted from isospin diffusion in heavy ion collisions. The method enables tighter constraints on the density dependence of symmetry energy and incompressibility, and the resulting equation of state can be used to predict neutron star properties and neutron skin thicknesses for comparison with future experimental measurements such as the PREX experiment.
