# Pairing and continuum effects in nuclei close to the drip line

> The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate representation are solved exactly, i.e., with correct asymptotic boundary conditions for the continuous spectrum. The calculations are preformed with effective Skyrme interactions. The exact HFB solutions are compared with HFB calculations based on box boundary conditions and with resonant continuum Hartree-Fock-BCS (HF-BCS) results. The comparison is done for the neutron-rich Ni isotopes. It is shown that close to the drip line the amount of pairing correlations depends on how the continuum coupling is treated. On the other hand, the resonant continuum HF-BCS results are generally close to those of HFB even in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that close to the neutron drip line, the amount of pairing correlations depends on how the continuum coupling is treated, with box-discretized HFB calculations overestimating pairing correlations compared to exact continuum HFB; however, the resonant continuum HF-BCS results are generally close to those of exact HFB even in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate representation with correct asymptotic boundary conditions for the continuous spectrum, using the Skyrme interaction SIII for the mean field and a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction. For neutrons, the continuum was treated exactly by matching the inner radial wave functions to asymptotic scattering solutions (spherical Bessel and Neumann functions for the upper component, decaying Hankel functions for the lower component), while for protons the continuum was treated by box discretization. The exact HFB solutions were compared with HFB calculations using box boundary conditions (box radius 22.5 fm) and with resonant continuum HF-BCS calculations that included only the first three low-lying single-particle resonances (d3/2, g7/2, h11/2) with their widths.

## Key numbers

- For the isotope 84Ni, the chemical potential after self-consistent convergence was lambda = -1.104 MeV.
- For the quasiparticle resonance corresponding to the bound state 2p1/2 in 84Ni, the resonance energy was E = 7.965 MeV and the width was Gamma = 338 keV.
- In the schematic square-well model (V0 = 45.35 MeV, Vso = 0.5 MeV, a = 5.2 fm, Delta = 1 MeV, lambda = -2.0 MeV), the p1/2 quasiparticle resonances had energies E1 = 30.889 MeV (width 0.40 keV) and E2 = 8.735 MeV (width 24.38 keV).
- In box HFB calculations for 84Ni, the pairing energy was about twice that of continuum HFB.
- For 86Ni, continuum HFB predicted zero pairing correlation energy while box HFB still gave a positive value.
- The two-neutron drip line was between 88Ni and 90Ni in both continuum and box HFB calculations.
- In 84Ni, the occupancy of the g7/2 single-particle resonance (at 3.6 MeV, width about 25 keV) in an energy interval 3.2 to 4 MeV was about 2% higher in box calculations than in continuum calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier HFB calculations in coordinate representation that used box boundary conditions (Dobaczewski et al., various years) and resonant continuum HF-BCS calculations (Sandulescu et al., various years; Kruppa et al., various years) by providing exact continuum HFB solutions for spherical systems with Skyrme forces. The paper shows that box HFB overestimates pairing correlations near the drip line because the discretization artificially increases the occupancy of resonant states, while the resonant continuum HF-BCS approach with only a few low-lying resonances gives pairing correlation energies close to exact HFB. The results enable future work to include additional continuum contributions in HF-BCS calculations to better reproduce particle densities and radii near the drip line.
