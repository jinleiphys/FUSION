# Systematic study of complete fusion suppression in reactions involving weakly bound nuclei at energies above the Coulomb barrier

> Complete fusion excitation functions of reactions involving breakup are studied by using the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model with breakup effects considered. An exponential function with two parameters is adopted to describe the prompt-breakup probability in the ECC model. These two parameters are fixed by fitting the measured prompt-breakup probability or the complete fusion cross sections. The suppression of complete fusion at energies above the Coulomb barrier is studied by comparing the data with the predictions from the ECC model without the breakup channel considered. The results show that the suppression of complete fusion are roughly independent of the target for the reactions involving the same projectile.

## Key claim

The suppression of complete fusion cross sections at energies above the Coulomb barrier is roughly independent of the target for reactions involving the same weakly bound projectile, based on calculations using the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model with breakup effects included via an exponential prompt-breakup probability function.

## Method

The authors used the empirical coupled-channel (ECC) model, which accounts for couplings to inelastic excitations and neutron transfer channels via a barrier distribution, and extended it to include breakup effects (ECCBU model). The prompt-breakup probability was described by an exponential function \(P_{\rm BU} = \exp[\nu + \mu R_{\rm min}]\) with two parameters \(\nu\) and \(\mu\); for \({}^{9}\)Be-induced reactions, these were extracted from measured sub-barrier prompt-breakup probabilities, and for other projectiles (\({}^{6,7}\)Li, \({}^{10,11}\)B), \(\mu\) was fitted to complete fusion data while assuming \(\nu = a - \mu R_0\) with \(a=0.557\). Complete fusion cross sections were then calculated by multiplying the penetration probability from the ECC model by the survival probability \((1-P_{\rm BU})\).

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{9}\)Be-induced reactions, the slope parameter \(\mu = -0.884 \pm 0.011\) fm\(^{-1}\) was extracted from measured prompt-breakup probabilities, and the suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.68\) was found to be independent of the target.
* For \({}^{6}\)Li-induced reactions, the mean slope parameter \(\bar\mu = -0.798\) fm\(^{-1}\) was obtained, with a suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.60\).
* For \({}^{7}\)Li-induced reactions, the mean slope parameter \(\bar\mu = -0.964\) fm\(^{-1}\) was obtained, with a suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.67\).
* For \({}^{10}\)B-induced reactions, the mean slope parameter \(\bar\mu = -1.415\) fm\(^{-1}\) was obtained, with a suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.80\).
* For \({}^{11}\)B-induced reactions, the mean slope parameter \(\bar\mu = -1.900\) fm\(^{-1}\) was obtained, with a suppression factor \(F_{\rm B.U.} = 0.91\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of complete fusion suppression in reactions with weakly bound nuclei, building on the universal fusion function (UFF) prescription of Canto et al. (2009) and the empirical coupled-channel model of Wang et al. (2015). It confirms the target-independence of suppression found by Wang et al. (2014) for \({}^{9}\)Be-induced reactions and extends the analysis to \({}^{6,7}\)Li and \({}^{10,11}\)B projectiles. The results enable future theoretical work to separate prompt and delayed breakup contributions in continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations, as suggested by Otomar et al. (2013), and provide input parameters for modeling breakup effects in fusion reactions.
