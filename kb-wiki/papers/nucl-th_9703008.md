# Binding and Off-Mass-Shell Effects in Deep Inelastic Scattering on Deuteron

> The role of relativistic off-mass-shell kinematics in the deep inelastic scattering on deuteron is analyzed. It is shown that the relativistic impulse approximation reproduces effects from the binding, Fermi motion, and two-nucleon contribution. The nonrelativistic limit of the deuteron structure function is in agreement with the nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim
The relativistic impulse approximation, when expanded near the nucleon mass-shell, reproduces the binding and off-mass-shell effects that give rise to the EMC-like suppression of the deuteron structure function at intermediate x, without requiring explicit mesonic exchange currents. The authors show that the nonrelativistic parametrization of binding effects can be understood as a consequence of relativistic off-mass-shell kinematics.

## Method
The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for the deuteron and write the forward Compton amplitude in terms of the BS vertex function and irreducible two-nucleon Green functions. They isolate the relativistic impulse approximation term and expand the deuteron hadron tensor around the nucleon mass-shell by taking the residue at the pole of the nucleon propagator, keeping first-order derivatives in the relative energy k0. The resulting expression for the deuteron structure function F2^D is then taken to the nonrelativistic limit for comparison with standard nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of deuteron to isoscalar nucleon structure functions equals 1 at small x (x ≃ 0) when two-nucleon contributions and mesonic exchange currents are neglected.
- At intermediate x (0.1 ≤ x ≤ 0.6), the deuteron structure function is suppressed relative to the nucleon one, in qualitative agreement with calculations including binding via mesonic corrections.
- At large x (x ≥ 0.6), the relativistic Fermi motion produces a rapid growth of the ratio, but the authors' result gives a softer growth than the comparison calculation.
- The contribution of P, S−−, and D−− waves is suppressed as a second power of the nucleon-nucleon potential compared to the S++ and D++ wave contribution.

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter based approach to deep inelastic scattering on the deuteron previously developed by the authors (Burov and Molochkov, 1995) and addresses the long-standing problem of reconciling the baryon sum rule with the presence of off-mass-shell effects. The paper shows that the EMC-like behavior of the deuteron structure function ratio, which in nonrelativistic models is attributed to mesonic exchange currents (Kopen, 1991), can instead be reproduced within a purely relativistic impulse approximation through off-mass-shell kinematics. This enables a consistent framework for extracting the neutron structure function from deuteron data without introducing ad hoc binding corrections.
