# Photodisintegration of the triton with realistic potentials

> The process $γ+ t \to n + d$ is treated by means of three-body integral equations employing in their kernel the W-Matrix representation of the subsystem amplitudes. As compared to the plane wave (Born) approximation the full solution of the integral equations, which takes into account the final state interaction, shows at low energies a 24% enhancement. The calculations are based on the semirealistic Malfliet-Tjon and the realistic Paris and Bonn B potentials. For comparison with earlier calculations we also present results for the Yamaguchi potential. In the low-energy region a remarkable potential dependence is observed, which vanishes at higher energies.

## Key claim

The full solution of the three-body integral equations, which includes final-state interaction, yields a 24% enhancement of the triton photodisintegration cross section at low energies compared to the plane-wave (Born) approximation, but this enhancement is specifically observed at the peak of the cross section and is based on calculations using the Paris potential.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for the process γ + t → n + d, using the W-Matrix representation to generate separable approximations of the two-body T-matrices. The triton bound state was obtained from the homogeneous version of the same equations, and the electromagnetic operator was treated both with and without meson-exchange currents via Siegert's theorem. Differential cross sections at 90° were calculated from threshold up to 40 MeV for the Yamaguchi, Malfliet-Tjon, Paris, and Bonn B potentials.

## Key numbers

- Full solution of the integral equations shows a 24% enhancement at the peak compared to the plane-wave (Born) approximation for the Paris potential.
- Inclusion of subsystem p-waves reduces the peak cross section by 8–10%.
- Triton binding energy for the symmetric Tabakin Yamaguchi potential: -9.72 MeV (compared to -9.33 MeV from Gibson and Lehman (1975)).
- Triton binding energy for the charge-dependent Yamaguchi potential (n-p triplet I, n-p singlet I, n-n singlet): -10.34 MeV.
- Triton binding energy for the present calculation with Yamaguchi n-p triplet III and n-n singlet III: -9.968 MeV.
- Calculations cover an energy region from threshold up to 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Barbour and Phillips (1970) and Gibson and Lehman (1975), which used only simple s-wave Yamaguchi interactions, by incorporating realistic potentials (Paris, Bonn B) and higher partial waves. The strong potential dependence observed in the low-energy peak region vanishes at higher energies, and the authors suggest that the reaction provides a sensitive test of two-nucleon potentials, calling for higher-accuracy low-energy measurements. The results also enable comparisons with calculations using the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler method (PEST, BEST, NEST potentials) planned for a subsequent publication.
