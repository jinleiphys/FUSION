# Study of Pentaquark and $Λ(1405)$

> We perform a five-body calculation for pentaquark $(q^4\bar{q})$ state of $Λ(1405)$ as well as the three-body calculations for the ground state baryons and the $Λ(1405)$, and two-body calculations for mesons. The hamiltonian, which reproduces reasonably well the energies of ground state baryons ($N,Λ,Σ,Ξ$ and $Δ$) and mesons ($π,K,ρ,K^\ast$), includes kinetic energy of semi-relativistic form, linear confinement potential, and the simplest form of color-magnetic interaction with Gaussian form factor. Flavor symmetry breaking ($m_s> m_{u,d}$) is taken into account. The energy calculated for $(q^4\bar{q})$ state of $Λ(1405)$ is lower than the energy for the $(q^3)$ state. The present result suggests that the $Λ(1405)$ is a pentaquark-dominated state if the color-magnetic potential plays a leading role of the quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions.

# Study of Pentaquark and Λ(1405)

## Key claim

The five-body calculation for the (q⁴q̄) pentaquark configuration of the Λ(1405) yields an energy lower than that of the conventional three-quark (q³) configuration, suggesting the Λ(1405) is a pentaquark-dominated state, but only if the color-magnetic potential plays the leading role in quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions.

## Method

The authors performed a five-body stochastic variational method (SVM) calculation for the pentaquark (q⁴q̄) state of the Λ(1405), alongside three-body calculations for ground-state baryons and the Λ(1405) itself, and two-body calculations for mesons. The Hamiltonian includes semi-relativistic kinetic energy, a linear confinement potential, and a color-magnetic interaction with a Gaussian form factor (replacing the delta function), with flavor symmetry breaking (m_s > m_u,d) taken into account. The trial wavefunction uses correlated Gaussians multiplied by orbital angular momentum parts, with all possible spin, isospin, and color configurations included and parameters optimized stochastically.

## Key numbers

- Calculated mass of the (q⁴q̄) pentaquark state for Λ(1405): 1292 MeV, compared to the (q³) state at 1405 MeV (experimental Λ(1405) mass: 1406 ± 4 MeV)
- Calculated π+Σ threshold: 1348 MeV; calculated K̄+N threshold: 1492 MeV; the (q⁴q̄) state at 1292 MeV lies below both thresholds
- Quark masses used: m_u,d = 0.34 GeV, m_s = 0.5508 GeV; confinement parameters: V₀ = 0.4534 GeV, C = 0.08265 (GeV)²; color-magnetic coupling α = 1.08, range Λ = 0.204 fm
- Calculated ground-state baryon energies (MeV): N = 949 (expt. 939), Δ = 1266 (expt. 1232), Λ = 1116 (expt. 1116), Σ = 1208 (expt. 1193), Ξ = 1336 (expt. 1318)
- Calculated meson energies (MeV): π = 141 (expt. 137), K = 543 (expt. 496), ρ = 771 (expt. 776), K* = 907 (expt. 892)

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark-model studies by Brau and Helminen (no year given in text) that suggested (q⁴q̄) configurations could contribute significantly to the Λ(1405). The authors note that under exact flavor SU(3) symmetry, two specific (q⁴q̄) states give strongly attractive color-magnetic interactions, and their five-body calculation confirms this attraction persists even with explicit flavor symmetry breaking. The result enables future work incorporating effective meson-exchange forces or coupling potentials between the (q³) and (q⁴q̄) configurations, with an initial attempt along these lines already reported by Nakamoto (no year given in text).
