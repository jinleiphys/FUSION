# Geometric scaling violations in the central rapidity region of d+Au collisions at RHIC

> We show that geometric scaling is satisfied to good accuracy in the forward region of d+Au collisions at RHIC. Scaling violations do show up, however, at mid-rapidity, and the anomalous dimension of the small-x gluon distribution evolves to near its DGLAP limit for transverse momenta of a few GeV. This represents a first consistency check of RHIC deuteron-nucleus and HERA DIS phenomenology, and of the universality of the underlying Color Glass Condensate (CGC) theory, which describes both phenomena. It also reconciles successful leading-twist LO and NLO perturbative QCD computations of mid-rapidity particle production with small-x evolution. Finally, we introduce a new parameterization for the anomalous dimension of the small-x gluon distribution which properly reproduces known theoretical limits at large rapidity, at large virtuality, and on the saturation boundary, and still fits the available data from d+Au collisions at RHIC. We find indications that sub-asymptotic terms in the rapidity-evolution of the anomalous dimension are large.

## Key claim

Geometric scaling violations are present in the mid-rapidity region of d+Au collisions at RHIC, contrary to the forward region where scaling holds to good accuracy; the anomalous dimension of the small-x gluon distribution evolves from approximately 0.6 near the saturation scale to near its DGLAP limit of 1 for transverse momenta of a few GeV, but this transition is only observable at mid-rapidity because at forward rapidity the scaling window is much broader.

## Method

The authors compute single-inclusive hadron production cross sections using a CGC-based formula that includes exact DGLAP evolution of the projectile parton distributions and fragmentation functions, with the target described by dipole scattering profiles. They compare the KKT dipole parameterization (which has weak scaling violations) and a new parameterization of the anomalous dimension they introduce, fitting to BRAHMS and STAR charged hadron data at mid-rapidity and forward rapidity for minimum-bias d+Au collisions at RHIC energy sqrt(s)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale at mid-rapidity is Q_s ~ 1 GeV for transverse momenta ~ 1 GeV, with Q_0 = 1 GeV and x_0 = 3e-4.
- The anomalous dimension at p_t ~ 1 GeV is close to 0.6, marking the onset of saturation dynamics.
- At p_t = 4 GeV, the anomalous dimension required to fit the data is approximately 0.9, nearly equal to the DGLAP limit of 1.
- The new parameterization uses d = 1.2 for the sub-asymptotic sqrt(Y) term; the subleading term dominates until Y ~ (d/lambda)^2 = 16.
- The LO calculation requires a K-factor of K ~ 3 at mid-rapidity, compared to K ~ 2 at y=3 and K ~ 1 at y=4, for the same scale Q_f = p_t.
- The boundary between extended scaling and dilute DGLAP regimes occurs at parton transverse momentum q_t ~ 5 GeV (hadron p_t ~ 3.5 GeV), estimated from Q_gs ~ Q_s^2 / Lambda with Lambda = 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous forward-rapidity study (Dumitru, Hayashigaki, Jalilian-Marian, 2005) to mid-rapidity, and reconciles leading-twist LO and NLO pQCD computations (Werner, 2005) with small-x evolution. It provides a consistency check between RHIC deuteron-nucleus phenomenology and HERA DIS phenomenology (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998; Stasto, Golec-Biernat, Kwiecinski, 2000; Iancu, Itakura, Munier, 2004), supporting the universality of the Color Glass Condensate theory. The new parameterization corrects deficiencies of the IIM dipole (Iancu, Itakura, Munier, 2004) at large Q^2 and the KKT dipole (Kharzeev, Kovchegov, Tuchin, 2003) at mid-rapidity, and enables future NLO calculations of particle production in pA collisions.
