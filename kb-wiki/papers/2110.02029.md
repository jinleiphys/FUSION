# Jet-temperature anisotropy revealed through high-$p_\perp$ data

> We explore to what extent, and how, high-$p_\perp$ data and predictions reflect the shape and anisotropy of the QCD medium formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. To this end, we use our recently developed DREENA-A framework, which can accommodate any temperature profile within the dynamical energy loss formalism. We show that the ratio of high-$p_\perp$ $v_2$ and $(1-R_{AA})$ predictions reaches a well-defined saturation value, which is directly proportional to the time-averaged anisotropy of the evolving QGP, as seen by the jets.

## Key claim

The ratio of high-$p_\perp$ elliptic flow $v_2$ and $(1-R_{AA})$ saturates at high transverse momentum and is directly proportional to a newly defined "average jet-temperature anisotropy" $\langle jT_2\rangle$, but this proportionality holds only after saturation (above $p_\perp \approx 30$ GeV for charged hadrons) and the relation $v_2/(1-R_{AA}) = \langle jT_2\rangle$ is approximate, with a fitted slope of $1.1 \pm 0.1$ across all models and centralities.

## Method

The authors used the DREENA-A framework, which implements the dynamical energy loss formalism with no free parameters in the energy loss, to compute $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ for charged hadrons, D mesons, and B mesons. They input five different fluid-dynamical temperature profiles (Glauber, Glauber + free streaming, EKRT, IP-Glasma, and T$_\mathrm{R}$ENTo), each tuned to reproduce low-$p_\perp$ data from Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV, and compared the resulting $v_2/(1-R_{AA})$ ratios to ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS data for $p_\perp > 10$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $v_2/(1-R_{AA})$ saturates above $p_\perp \approx 30$ GeV for charged hadrons, above $p_\perp > 20$ GeV for B mesons, and above $p_\perp > 80$ GeV for D mesons.
- The linear fit of $v_2/(1-R_{AA})$ versus the path-length anisotropy $\Delta L/\langle L \rangle$ at $p_\perp = 100$ GeV yields a slope of approximately 1.
- The ratio of $v_2/(1-R_{AA})$ to the average jet-temperature anisotropy $\langle jT_2\rangle$, averaged over all models and centralities for $p_\perp > 80$ GeV, is $1.1 \pm 0.1$.
- The critical temperature used to define the medium boundary is $T_c = 160$ MeV, within the lattice QCD uncertainty of $154 \pm 9$ MeV.
- Experimental constraints on $\langle jT_2\rangle$ were obtained by fitting $v_2/(1-R_{AA})$ data for $p_\perp > 20$ GeV using MINUIT.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analytic argument (Djordjevic et al., 2019) that $v_2/(1-R_{AA})$ saturates and reflects geometry, which was based on a simple 1D Bjorken expansion, to full 3+1D fluid-dynamical calculations with transverse expansion. It introduces the jet-temperature anisotropy $\langle jT_2\rangle$ as a new bulk-medium observable that can be extracted from high-$p_\perp$ data and used to constrain fluid-dynamical simulations. The finding that none of the tested evolution scenarios (Glauber, Glauber + free streaming, EKRT, IP-Glasma, T$_\mathrm{R}$ENTo) agrees well with the experimentally inferred $\langle jT_2\rangle$ provides a new constraint for future bulk-medium tuning, particularly relevant for the high-luminosity LHC Run 3.
