# Dispersion relation of the $Žü$ meson in hot/dense nuclear matter

> The dispersion relation of $Žü$ meson in both timelike and spacelike regimes in hot and dense nuclear medium is analyzed and compared with $Žā$ meson based on the quantum hadrodynamics model. The pole and screening masses of $Žü$ and $Žā$ are discussed. The behavior of screening mass of $Žü$ is different from that of $Žā$ due to different Dirac- and Fermi-sea contributions at finite temperature and density.

## Key claim

The dispersion relations of the rho meson in both timelike and spacelike regimes are analyzed using quantum hadrodynamics, and the screening mass of the rho meson behaves differently from that of the sigma meson due to distinct Dirac-sea and Fermi-sea contributions at finite temperature and density, with the caveat that the analysis is performed within the random phase approximation (RPA) and the relativistic Hartree approximation.

## Method

The authors employed the quantum hadrodynamics model (QHD-I) to calculate the in-medium nucleon effective mass and effective chemical potential via self-consistent equations in the relativistic Hartree approximation. The rho meson polarization tensor was computed in the random phase approximation (RPA) using the rho-NN interaction Lagrangian with both vector and tensor couplings, separating the contributions into Dirac-sea (vacuum) and Fermi-sea (particle-hole) parts. The sigma meson self-energy was similarly computed in RPA, and the pole masses and screening (Debye) masses for both mesons were determined from the pole positions of their full propagators in timelike and spacelike regimes.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density used: rho_0 = 0.1484 fm^{-3}
- QHD-I parameters: g_sigma^2 = 54.289, g_omega^2 = 102.770, m_sigma = 458 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, g_rhoNN = 2.63, kappa_rho = 6.1, m_rho = 770 MeV, M_N = 939 MeV
- Temperature for numerical results: T = 100 MeV
- The effective nucleon mass M_N^* decreases monotonously with increasing density at fixed T = 100 MeV
- The rho meson pole mass decreases at first and then becomes saturated with increasing density
- The sigma meson pole mass decreases in the low density region
- The longitudinal and transverse screening (Debye) masses of rho decrease with increasing density
- The screening mass of sigma increases with increasing density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium meson properties, particularly the analysis of rho meson pole masses by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) and Chen et al. (2002) which found that Dirac-sea contributions dominate over Fermi-sea contributions. It also builds on the discussion of sigma meson screening mass at zero temperature by Chanfray et al. (2001) using one-loop NN^{-1} excitation with free nucleon gas. The results enable better understanding of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions and isospin fluctuations as potential signatures of quark-gluon plasma formation, as discussed by Eletsky and Ioffe (1993).
