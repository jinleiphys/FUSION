# Entanglement in Four-Dimensional SU(3) Gauge Theory

> We investigate the quantum entanglement entropy for the four-dimensional Euclidean SU(3) gauge theory. We present the first non-perturbative calculation of the entropic $c$-function ($C(l)$) of SU(3) gauge theory in lattice Monte Carlo simulation using the replica method. For $0 \leqslant l \leqslant 0.7$~fm, where $l$ is the length of the subspace, the entropic $c$-function is almost constant, indicating conformally invariant dynamics. The value of the constant agrees with that perturbatively obtained from free gluons, with 20 % discrepancy. When $l$ is close to the Hadronic scale, the entropic $c$-function decreases smoothly, and it is consistent with zero within error bars at $l \gtrsim 0.9$ fm.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first non-perturbative calculation of the entropic c-function for SU(3) gauge theory using lattice Monte Carlo simulation, finding that the c-function is nearly constant at short distances (indicating conformal dynamics) and decreases smoothly at hadronic scales, but the constant value agrees with free-gluon perturbation theory only within a 20% discrepancy.

## Method
The authors used the replica method on a four-dimensional Euclidean lattice with volume 16^4 and replica number n=2, employing the standard Wilson plaquette action. They generated gauge configurations using the pseudo-heatbath algorithm with an interpolating action to compute the derivative of the entanglement entropy with respect to subsystem length l, then extracted the entropic c-function C(l) = (l^3/|∂A|)(∂S_A/∂l). The calculation involved 11 values of the interpolation parameter α between 0 and 1, with numerical integration using cubic polynomial functions, and results were obtained at multiple lattice spacings to check discretization effects.

## Key numbers
- Best-fit constant value of the entropic c-function in the region 0 ≤ l ≤ 0.7 fm: C = 0.206 ± 0.007 (statistical error at 1σ), with chi-square per degree of freedom 0.88.
- Systematic uncertainty from fit range variation: C = 0.208(8) for 0 ≤ l ≤ 0.6 fm and C = 0.202(7) for 0 ≤ l ≤ 0.8 fm.
- The c-function becomes consistent with zero within error bars at l ≳ 0.9 fm.
- Free-gluon estimate for SU(3) using the Ryu-Takayanagi coefficient (K ~ 0.0049 for free real scalar, scaled by 2 scalars per gauge boson and 8 color degrees of freedom): C(l)_free ~ 0.1568.
- Scaling the SU(3) result to SU(2) by (N_c^2 - 1) gives C_SU(2) ≈ 0.077, compared to SU(2) data in the range C = 0.07–0.08 at l ≤ 0.3 fm.
- Lattice spacings used range from a = 0.1707 fm (β = 5.700) to a = 0.1182 fm (β = 5.870), with the Sommer scale r_0 = 0.5 fm assumed for conversion.
- Critical temperature of pure SU(3) Yang-Mills: T_c = 280 MeV, corresponding to 1/T_c = 0.714 fm.
- Λ_MSbar scale from lattice: r_0 Λ_MSbar = 0.602(48) and 0.613(2)(25), giving Λ_MSbar^{-1} ~ 0.831 fm and ~ 0.816 fm respectively.

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of entanglement entropy in SU(2) gauge theory by Buividovich et al. (2008), which showed a clear discontinuity and enhancement in the c-function around 0.5 fm, qualitatively different from the smooth decrease seen here for SU(3). The results are compared with holographic models by Nishioka et al. (2006) and Klebanov et al. (2007) that predict phase transitions in large-N_c Yang-Mills theories, and with the static quark-antiquark potential which shows analogous smooth crossover between Coulomb and linear regimes. The method enables future studies of the entropic c-function in QCD with dynamical fermions and at finite temperature, where it could provide a quantum correlation length for the quark-gluon plasma phase.
