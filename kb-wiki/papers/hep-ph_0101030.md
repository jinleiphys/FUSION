# Complete analysis of pion-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory to third order

> We consider pion-nucleon scattering in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order. All electromagnetic corrections appearing to this order are included. We thus have a consistent description of strong and electromagnetic effects, which allows us to isolate the strong part of the interaction in an unambiguous way. We give pion-nucleon phase shifts up to pion laboratory momenta of 100 MeV and find sizeable differences for the S-waves of the elastic channels compared to previous phase shift analyses. The precise description of the scattering process also allows us to address the question of isospin violation in the strong interaction. For the usually employed triangle relation we find an isospin breaking effect of -0.7% in the S-wave, whereas the P-waves show an effect of -1.5% and -4 to -2.5%, respectively, for pion laboratory momenta between 25 and 100 MeV.

# Complete analysis of pion-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory to third order

## Key claim

The authors claim that a consistent treatment of both strong and electromagnetic interactions in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order yields sizeable differences in the S-wave phase shifts of elastic pion-nucleon channels compared to previous phase shift analyses, and that isospin breaking in the strong interaction is much smaller (around -0.7% to -4%) than the 6-7% reported in earlier works by Gibbs (1995) and Matsinos (1997), provided one includes all virtual-photon loop diagrams rather than only the standard one-photon and two-photon exchange diagrams.

## Method

The authors constructed the full pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to third chiral order, including all electromagnetic corrections (Coulomb potential, virtual-photon loops, Bremsstrahlung, and electromagnetic counterterms) with pions and photons treated relativistically and nucleons in the heavy-baryon expansion. They performed a combined fit of the 14 unknown low-energy constants (with some fixed from nucleon magnetic moments, charge radii, and the nucleon mass difference) to cross section data for elastic π⁺p, elastic π⁻p, and charge exchange π⁻p → π⁰n reactions up to pion laboratory momenta of 100 MeV, using data from Frank et al. (1983), Brack et al. (1986), Joram et al. (1995), Isenhower et al. (1996), Salomon et al. (1984), and Frlez et al. (1998). The strong amplitude was then isolated by switching off all electromagnetic interactions while retaining the hadronic mass differences of pions and nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The fit yields a χ²/dof value of 1.50 for the combined fit to all three scattering channels.
- The isospin breaking triangle ratio R in the S-wave projection is -0.7% for pion laboratory momenta between 25 and 100 MeV.
- The isospin breaking triangle ratio R in the P-wave G-projection is -1.5% over the same momentum range.
- The isospin breaking triangle ratio R in the P-wave H-projection ranges from -4% to -2.5% for pion laboratory momenta between 25 and 100 MeV.
- The dynamical (vertex-only) isospin breaking in the S-wave, with hadronic masses set equal, is -0.75%.
- The analysis covers pion laboratory momenta up to 100 MeV, corresponding to pion kinetic energies Tπ ≤ 32.2 MeV.
- The hadronic pion mass difference is taken as Mπ⁺ʰ = Mπ⁰ʰ + 0.1 MeV, with the absolute hadronic neutral pion mass uncertain to within 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous isospin-symmetric analysis (Fettes, Meißner, and Steininger, 1998) and their earlier studies including strong isospin breaking (Fettes and Meißner, 2000) by adding the full electromagnetic sector, completing the third-order chiral perturbation theory description of pion-nucleon scattering. The results contradict the larger isospin breaking effects (up to 6%) reported by Gibbs (1995) and Matsinos (1997), which the authors attribute to those analyses using electromagnetic corrections incompatible with the strong interaction description and omitting non-linear photon-pion-nucleon couplings. The work enables a clean separation of dynamical from static isospin breaking and provides strong phase shifts that can be compared with the analyses of KA85 (Koch, 1985), SP98 (Said, 1998), and EM98/EM00 (Matsinos, 1998, 2000).
