# EPS09 - a New Generation of NLO and LO Nuclear Parton Distribution Functions

> We present a next-to-leading order (NLO) global DGLAP analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) and their uncertainties. Carrying out an NLO nPDF analysis for the first time with three different types of experimental input -- deep inelastic $\ell$+A scattering, Drell-Yan dilepton production in p+$A$ collisions, and inclusive pion production in d+Au and p+p collisions at RHIC -- we find that these data can well be described in a conventional collinear factorization framework. Although the pion production has not been traditionally included in the global analyses, we find that the shape of the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm dAu}$ of the pion $p_T$-spectrum at midrapidity retains sensitivity to the gluon distributions, providing evidence for shadowing and EMC-effect in the nuclear gluons. We use the Hessian method to quantify the nPDF uncertainties which originate from the uncertainties in the data. In this method the sensitivity of $χ^2$ to the variations of the fitting parameters is mapped out to orthogonal error sets which provide a user-friendly way to calculate how the nPDF uncertainties propagate to any factorizable nuclear cross-section. The obtained NLO and LO nPDFs and the corresponding error sets are collected in our new release called {\ttfamily EPS09}. These results should find applications in precision analyses of the signatures and properties of QCD matter at the LHC and RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first next-to-leading order (NLO) global DGLAP analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) that includes inclusive pion production data from RHIC alongside deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data, finding that these data can be described within conventional collinear factorization, but the gluon distributions at small and large x retain large uncertainties that are only partially constrained by the pion data shape.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of nuclear modification factors \(R_i^A(x,Q^2)\) for valence quarks, sea quarks, and gluons, parametrized at the initial scale \(Q_0^2 = 1.69\) GeV\(^2\) and evolved via NLO DGLAP equations. They minimized a weighted \(\chi^2\) function against 929 data points from deep inelastic \(\ell\)+A scattering, Drell-Yan dilepton production in p+A collisions, and inclusive \(\pi^0\) production in d+Au and p+p collisions at RHIC, using the Hessian method with \(\Delta\chi^2 = 50\) to construct 30 orthogonal error sets for uncertainty propagation.

## Key numbers

- Total of 929 data points included in the fit, with a minimum \(\chi^2\) of 731.3 at NLO and 738.6 at LO
- Initial scale for parametrization: \(Q_0^2 = m_c^2 = 1.69\) GeV\(^2\), with charm quark mass \(m_c = 1.3\) GeV and bottom quark mass \(m_b = 4.5\) GeV
- Strong coupling constant fixed at \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.118\) (2-loop) for NLO, with 1-loop \(\alpha_s\) for LO
- Kinematical cuts: \(Q^2, M^2 \ge 1.69\) GeV\(^2\) for DIS and DY; \(p_T \ge 1.7\) GeV for pion production
- 15 free parameters in the error analysis, producing 30 error sets plus one central set (31 total nPDF sets in the EPS09 release)
- For lead at \(Q^2 = 100\) GeV\(^2\), the gluon nuclear modification \(R_G\) shows a best-fit value of 0.625 at the EMC minimum (\(y_e\) parameter) with sizable uncertainty bands at both small and large x

## Context

This work extends earlier leading-order nPDF analyses by the same group (Eskola et al., 1998-2008) and previous NLO nPDF analyses by Hirai et al. (2007) and de Florian et al. (2003) that were restricted to DIS and Drell-Yan data only. The inclusion of RHIC pion production data provides new constraints on gluon modifications, particularly evidence for shadowing and EMC-effect in nuclear gluons. The EPS09 release enables precision analyses of hard processes in nuclear collisions at the LHC and RHIC, allowing external users to propagate nPDF uncertainties to any factorizable nuclear cross-section using the provided error sets.
