# From chiral vibration to static chirality in ^{135}Nd

> Electromagnetic transition probabilities have been measured for the intra- and inter-band transitions in the two sequences in the nucleus ^{135}Nd that were previously identified as a composite chiral pair of rotational bands. The measurements are in good agreement with results of a new combination of TAC and RPA calculations. The chiral character of the bands is affirmed and it is shown that their behavior is associated with a transition from a vibrational into a static chiral regime.

## Key claim
The electromagnetic transition probabilities in the two candidate chiral bands of ^{135}Nd are nearly identical, affirming their chiral character and showing a transition from chiral vibration at low spin to static chirality at higher spin, with the caveat that the RPA calculation breaks down near the transition point.

## Method
Lifetimes of states in both chiral bands (Band A: spins 29/2^- to 43/2^-; Band B: spins 31/2^- to 39/2^-) were measured using the Doppler-Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM) with the Gammasphere array at ATLAS. The ^{135}Nd nuclei were produced via the ^{100}Mo(^{40}Ar,5n) reaction at 175 MeV using a thick target backed by Pb. The data were analyzed with the LINESHAPE codes using 5000 Monte Carlo simulations per state, and reduced transition probabilities B(M1) and B(E2) were derived from the extracted lifetimes. The experimental results were compared with a new combination of tilted-axis-cranking (TAC) and random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for the {πh^2_{11/2}, νh_{11/2}} configuration.

## Key numbers
- Lifetimes in Band A range from 1.00(5) ps at spin 29/2^- to 0.16(2) ps at spin 43/2^-
- Lifetimes in Band B range from 1.46(2) ps at spin 31/2^- to 0.24(4) ps at spin 39/2^-
- Intra-band B(E2) values in Band A are 0.32(2) e^2b^2 at spin 29/2^- and remain near 0.32 e^2b^2 through spin 37/2^-, then drop to 0.13(3) e^2b^2 at spin 39/2^-
- Intra-band B(E2) values in Band B are 0.28(3) e^2b^2 at spin 31/2^- and remain near 0.28 e^2b^2 through spin 37/2^-, then drop to 0.11(3) e^2b^2 at spin 39/2^-
- Intra-band B(M1) values in Band A range from 3.2(2) μ_n^2 at spin 29/2^- to 2.0(3) μ_n^2 at spin 43/2^-
- Intra-band B(M1) values in Band B range from 2.7(3) μ_n^2 at spin 31/2^- to 1.9(3) μ_n^2 at spin 39/2^-
- The angular momentum where the RPA energy goes to zero in the TAC calculations is one unit higher than the point where the two bands come closest in the data

## Context
This work extends the search for nuclear chirality from the two-quasiparticle cases in ^{128}Cs (Grodner et al.) and ^{134}Pr (Tonev et al.; Petrache et al.) to the three-quasiparticle configuration in ^{135}Nd, where the additional h_{11/2} quasiproton delays the onset of anharmonicity. The TAC+RPA approach provides the first microscopic calculation of the energy splitting between chiral partner bands, going beyond the 2-particle-plus-rotor model (Frauendorf and Meng) and core-quasiparticle coupling model (Starosta et al.) that could not be applied to three-quasiparticle configurations. The results enable future studies of the anharmonic regime near the transition to static chirality, where the B(E2) values may differ substantially between the two bands.
