# Toward a First-Principles Calculation of Electroweak Box Diagrams

> We derive a Feynman-Hellmann theorem relating the second-order nucleon energy shift resulting from the introduction of periodic source terms of electromagnetic and isovector axial currents to the parity-odd nucleon structure function $F_3^N$. It is a crucial ingredient in the theoretical study of the $γW$ and $γZ$ box diagrams that are known to suffer from large hadronic uncertainties. We demonstrate that for a given $Q^2$, one only needs to compute a small number of energy shifts in order to obtain the required inputs for the box diagrams. Future lattice calculations based on this approach may shed new light on various topics in precision physics including the refined determination of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements and the weak mixing angle.

# Toward a First-Principles Calculation of Electroweak Box Diagrams

## Key claim
The authors derive a Feynman-Hellmann theorem that relates the second-order nucleon energy shift from periodic electromagnetic and isovector axial current source terms to the parity-odd structure function \(F_3^N\), but only under the off-shell condition \(|\omega| < 1\) and for momentum transfers where the nucleon energy can be extracted on the lattice. This approach avoids the operator product expansion and is therefore not restricted to large \(Q^2\), unlike most lattice QCD methods for parton distribution functions.

## Method
The authors introduce two periodic source terms for the electromagnetic current \(J_{em}^2\) and the isovector axial current \(J_A^3\) into the QCD Hamiltonian, with a momentum transfer \(\vec{q}\). They then derive a Feynman-Hellmann theorem in Euclidean path integral formalism that expresses the mixed second derivative of the nucleon energy with respect to the source strengths in terms of the forward Compton amplitude \(T_3^N(\omega, Q^2)\) below the elastic threshold. This amplitude is related via a dispersion relation to the parity-odd structure function \(F_3^N(x, Q^2)\), and the first Nachtmann moment of \(F_3^N\), the quantity needed for electroweak box diagram calculations, can be approximated by a linear combination of energy shifts computed at a small number of discrete \(\omega\) values accessible on the lattice.

## Key numbers
- For a typical lattice configuration (ETM Collaboration cA2.09.48, spatial lattice size \(48 \times 0.0931\ \mathrm{fm} \approx 4.47\ \mathrm{fm}\)), the choice \(\vec{q} = (2\pi/L)(2,1,0)\) gives \(Q^2 \approx 0.38\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and restricts allowed \(|\omega|\) values to three: 0, 2/5, and 4/5.
- At \(Q^2 \approx 0.38\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), a linear combination of \(\Lambda(x,\omega)\) at \(\omega = 0, 2/5, 4/5\) with coefficients \(a = 7.82446\), \(b = -7.58605\), and \(c = 0.734787\) reproduces the first Nachtmann moment kernel \(\Pi(x, Q^2)\) to good agreement for \(x < 0.9\).
- A lattice calculation of the second-order nucleon energy shift at a few \(\omega\) points with a precision level of \(15\%\) would match the precision of existing Regge-exchange model estimates for the \(\gamma W\) box contribution, which is \((\alpha/\pi) \times (0.48 \pm 0.07)\) from multiparticle intermediate states at \(Q^2 \sim 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context
This work extends the dispersion relation approach to \(\gamma W\) and \(\gamma Z\) box diagrams developed by Seng (2018) and Gorchtein (2018), which reduced the problem to parity-odd structure functions but still relied on model-dependent inputs at hadronic scales. It also builds on the Feynman-Hellmann theorem applications by Chambers (2017) for electromagnetic form factors and by Agadjanov (2018) for Compton scattering amplitudes. The method enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of the first Nachtmann moment of \(F_3^N\) at \(Q^2 \lesssim 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), which could resolve the hadronic uncertainties in the extraction of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element \(V_{ud}\) and the weak mixing angle \(\sin^2\theta_W\) for experiments such as the upcoming P2 experiment at MESA.
