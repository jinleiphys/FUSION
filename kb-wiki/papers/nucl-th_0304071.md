# Low-Energy Direct Capture in the 8Li(n,gamma)9Li and 8B(p,gamma)9C Reactions

> The cross sections of the 8Li(n,gamma)9Li and 8B(p,gamma)9C capture reactions have been analyzed using the direct capture model. At low energies which is the astrophysically relevant region the capture process is dominated by E1 transitions from incoming s-waves to bound p-states. The cross sections of both mirror reactions can be described simultaneously with consistent potential parameters, whereas previous calculations have overestimated the capture cross sections significantly. However, the parameters of the potential have to be chosen very carefully because the calculated cross section of the 8Li(n,gamma)9Li reaction depends sensitively on the potential strength.

# Low-Energy Direct Capture in the 8Li(n,gamma)9Li and 8B(p,gamma)9C Reactions

## Key claim

The cross sections of both mirror reactions can be described simultaneously with consistent potential parameters using a direct capture model, but only if the scattering potential strength parameter is chosen very carefully (λ_scatt ≈ 0.55) because the calculated cross section of the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction depends sensitively on the potential strength; previous calculations that used standard potentials overestimated the capture cross sections significantly.

## Method

The direct capture model was applied using a real folding potential V_F(r) calculated from an approximate density for the A=8 nuclei and an M3Y-type effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, adjusted by a strength parameter λ. The bound state wave function strength λ_bound was fixed to reproduce the binding energies of a 1p3/2 neutron (proton) in 9Li (9C), while the scattering wave function strength λ_scatt was varied to study the cross section dependence. The cross sections for E1 transitions from incoming s-waves to bound p-states were calculated at low energies (E ≤ 1 MeV) using a spectroscopic factor C²S = 1.0.

## Key numbers

- For the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction at E = 25 keV, the experimental upper limit is σ ≤ 5.88 μb (derived from Coulomb breakup data of Koba03 using a standard 1/v energy dependence)
- For the 8B(p,γ)9C reaction, the astrophysical S-factor at zero energy is S_18(0) = 45.5 ± 5.5 eV·b (weighted average from Beau01 and Tra02)
- The bound state potential strength parameter λ_bound = 1.065 for 9Li and 1.045 for 9C
- The scattering potential strength parameter λ_scatt ≈ 0.55 simultaneously reproduces both experimental results
- The Q-value of the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction is 4064 keV; the Q-value of the 8B(p,γ)9C reaction is 1296 keV
- For the 8B(p,γ)9C reaction with λ_scatt = 1.50, a resonance appears at E = 360 keV with a width Γ = 50 keV
- The ratio r = σ(25 keV)/S_18(25 keV) has an experimental upper limit of r ≤ 0.129 MeV⁻¹

## Context

This work extends previous direct capture calculations for the 8Li(n,γ)9Li and 8B(p,γ)9C reactions, which had overestimated experimentally determined values by factors of 3 to 50 (Rau94, Bert99, Desc99, Mal89, Mao91). The analysis resolves the discrepancy by showing that standard potentials (λ ≈ 1) produce a low-lying 2s resonance whose tail artificially enhances the cross section, whereas a shallow potential (λ ≈ 0.55) correctly places the proton 2s1/2 orbit at higher energies. This enables reliable astrophysical reaction rates for inhomogeneous big bang models (where the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction competes with 8Li(α,n)11B) and for the hot pp-chain breakout to the rp-process via the 9C(α,p)12N reaction.
