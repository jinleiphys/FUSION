# Transport coefficients and quantum fields

> Various aspects of transport coefficients in quantum field theory are reviewed. We describe recent progress in the calculation of transport coefficients in hot gauge theories using Kubo formulas, paying attention to the fulfillment of Ward identities. We comment on why the color conductivity in hot QCD is much simpler to compute than the electrical conductivity. The nonperturbative extraction of transport coefficients from lattice QCD calculations is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent progress in calculating transport coefficients in hot gauge theories using diagrammatic methods in the imaginary-time formalism, emphasizing that the Ward identity must be satisfied by including both self-energy and vertex corrections, but that the additional vertex diagram required by the Ward identity does not contribute to the electrical conductivity at leading-logarithmic order.

## Method

The authors describe the diagrammatic calculation of the electrical conductivity in hot QED using Kubo formulas, where the retarded polarization tensor is evaluated by summing an infinite series of ladder diagrams with hard nearly on-shell fermions dressed with a thermal width and soft (HTL resummed) photons as rungs. The summation is performed by introducing an effective electron-photon vertex defined by an integral equation, which is solved after expanding in powers of the soft momentum over the hard momentum. The color conductivity in hot QCD is computed analogously but simplifies to an algebraic equation because the leading-order thermal width is sufficient.

## Key numbers

- The electrical conductivity at leading-log order is given by \(\sigma = C \frac{T}{e^{2} \ln(1/e)}\) with \(C = 15.6964\) (exact result) and \(C_{\rm approx} = 15.7424\) from an approximate solution.
- The soft-fermion contribution to the thermal width of a hard on-shell fermion is \(\Gamma^{\rm(sf,lo)}_{\pv} = \frac{\alpha\,m_f^2\ln(1/e)}{p}\left[1+2n_{B}(p)\right]\) with \(m_f = eT/\sqrt{8}\).
- The soft-photon contribution to the thermal width has a leading-log term \(\Gamma_\pv^{\rm (sp,lo)} = 2\al T\ln(m_D/\lmin)\) with Debye mass \(m_D = eT/\sqrt{3}\).
- The color conductivity in hot QCD is \(\sigma_c = \frac{g^2T^2}{9}\frac{N_f}{2}\frac{\cD_{\rm q}}{\Gamma^{\rm q}} + \frac{g^2T^2}{9}N_c\frac{\cD_{\rm g}}{\Gamma^{\rm g}}\) with \(\cD_{\rm q} = 1 - 1/N_c^2\) and \(\cD_{\rm g} = 2\).
- The leading-order thermal widths for quarks and gluons are \(\tw_{\pv}^{\rm q} = \frac{N_{c}^{2}-1}{2N_{c}}\,2\al_{s}T\ln(m_{D}/m_{g})\) and \(\tw_{\pv}^{\rm g} = N_{c}\,2\al_{s}T\ln(m_{D}/m_{g})\) with magnetic mass \(m_{g}\sim g^{2}T\).

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic approach of Jeon (1995) for shear viscosity in scalar field theory to gauge theories, building on the ladder summation method of Valle Basagoiti (2002) while correcting it to satisfy the Ward identity. The results for the electrical conductivity at leading-log order match those obtained earlier by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) using effective kinetic theory. The paper also discusses the prospects of extracting transport coefficients nonperturbatively from lattice QCD using the Maximal Entropy Method, noting that Euclidean correlators are remarkably insensitive to the small-energy spectral weight that determines transport coefficients in weakly coupled theories.
