# Kaon Interferometry: A Sensitive Probe of the QCD Equation of State?

> We calculate the kaon HBT radius parameters for high energy heavy ion collisions, assuming a first order phase transition from a thermalized Quark-Gluon-Plasma to a gas of hadrons. At high transverse momenta K_T ~ 1 GeV/c direct emission from the phase boundary becomes important, the emission duration signal, i.e., the R_out/R_side ratio, and its sensitivity to T_c (and thus to the latent heat of the phase transition) are enlarged. Moreover, the QGP+hadronic rescattering transport model calculations do not yield unusual large radii (R_i<9fm). Finite momentum resolution effects have a strong impact on the extracted HBT parameters (R_i and lambda) as well as on the ratio R_out/R_side.

## Key claim

Kaon interferometry at high transverse momenta (K_T ~ 1 GeV/c) can probe the QCD equation of state and the properties of the phase transition, but only if finite momentum resolution effects are properly accounted for; the sensitivity to the critical temperature T_c and latent heat is enlarged at high K_T, yet the bulk of the correlation signal is still dominated by the long-lived hadronic rescattering phase.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model combining ideal hydrodynamics with a bag-model equation of state (first-order phase transition) for the quark-gluon plasma phase, matched to a microscopic hadronic transport model (UrQMD) that treats resonance formation/decay and 2-to-n scattering explicitly. Kaon freeze-out distributions were computed for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV), and the three-dimensional correlation functions were calculated using the correlation-afterburner code CRAB, with Gaussian fits to extract HBT radius parameters (R_out, R_side, R_long) and the lambda intercept parameter.

## Key numbers

- For T_c ~ 160 MeV, direct emission from the phase boundary increases approximately linearly with K_T, reaching 30% at K_T = 1 GeV/c.
- For T_c ~ 200 MeV, R_out is only approximately 1 fm larger than in the T_c ~ 160 MeV case.
- The R_out/R_side ratio for kaons reaches a value of 1.5 at K_T approximately equal to the kaon mass (m_K ~ 494 MeV).
- With finite momentum resolution of about 2% of the center of each K_T bin (STAR detector value), the R_out/R_side ratio for T_c ~ 200 MeV is reduced from 1.8 to 1.35.
- All calculated radii satisfy R_i < 9 fm.
- Resonance contribution to kaon emission decreases with K_T from 70% to 50% for T_c ~ 160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pion interferometry study (Soff et al., 2001) to kaons, motivated by kaons being less contaminated by resonance decays than pions and having lower multiplicities that reduce higher-order correlation effects. The calculations contradict earlier predictions (Pratt, 1986; Rischke and Gyulassy, 1996) that a first-order phase transition would produce unusually large HBT radii; instead, the hadronic rescattering phase dominates the radii. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC using kaon correlations at high K_T to probe the space-time dynamics near the phase boundary, provided experiments properly correct for finite momentum resolution effects.
