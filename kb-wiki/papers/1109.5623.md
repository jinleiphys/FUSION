# The Nuclear Shell Model Toward the Drip Lines

> We describe the "islands of inversion" that occur when approaching the neutron drip line around the magic numbers N=20, N=28 and N=40 in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model in very large valence spaces. We explain these configuration inversions (and the associated shape transitions) as the result of the competition between the spherical mean field (monopole) which favors magicity and the correlations (multipole) which favor deformed intruder states. We also show that the N=20 and N=28 islands are in reallity a single one, which for the Magnesium isotopes is limited by N=18 and N=32.

# The Nuclear Shell Model Toward the Drip Lines

## Key claim
The islands of inversion at N=20 and N=28 are actually a single continuous region of deformation, bounded for Magnesium isotopes by N=18 and N=32, rather than two separate islands as previously thought. This unification is demonstrated through large-scale shell model calculations that reproduce experimental data across the full isotopic chains from the proton to the neutron drip line.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale Interacting Shell Model calculations using the SDPF-U effective interaction in a valence space comprising the full sd-shell (0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2) and pf-shell (0f7/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2, 0f5/2), allowing up to 6p-6h excitations from the sd-shell to the pf-shell. For the N=40 region, the valence space included the full pf-shell for protons and the 0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2, 0g9/2, and 1d5/2 orbits for neutrons. The calculations were compared with experimental 2+ excitation energies and B(E2) transition strengths for isotopic chains of Magnesium, Neon, Silicon, and N=40 isotones.

## Key numbers
- 42Si has a first 2+ state at 770 keV, identified as an oblate, well-deformed rotor
- 40Mg is predicted to have a 2+ state at approximately 720 keV, described as a very collective prolate rotor
- 34Si is identified as a doubly magic nucleus at Z=14, N=20, with a "majestic peak" in 2+ energy at N=20 in the Silicon isotopes
- 46Ar is non-collective despite four valence protons, while 44S shows the onset of quadrupole collectivity with the N=28 closure breaking
- The unified island of inversion for Magnesium isotopes is bounded by N=18 and N=32

## Context
This work extends the early shell model calculations of Poves and Retamosa (1987) and Warburton, Becker, and Brown (1990), which first identified deformed intruder configurations (2p-2h neutron excitations from sd to pf-shell) in the N=20 region around 31Na. The authors build on the monopole correction approach of Pasquini and Zuker (1976), who showed that the Kuo-Brown interaction required modifications to produce magic 48Ca and 56Ni, and connect to Otsuka et al. (2010) who attributed monopole anomalies to three-body forces. The results enable a unified description of nuclei between Oxygen and Calcium covering isotopes from the proton to neutron drip lines, and predict a new region of deformation centered at 64Cr for the N=40 case.
