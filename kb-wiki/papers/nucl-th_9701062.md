# J/ψ-dissociation by a color electric flux tube

> We adress the question of how a $c-\bar{c}$-state (a $J/ψ$) can be dissociated by the strong color electric fields when moving through a color electric flux tube. The color electric flux tube and the dissociation of the heavy quarkonia state are both described within the Friedberg-Lee color dielectric model. We speculate on the importance of such an effect with respect to the observed $J/ψ$-suppression in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

A bound $c\bar{c}$-state (a $J/\psi$) moving through a color electric flux tube is dissociated "rather immediately" (on a timescale of less than about 1 fm/c) by the strong color electric field, ending up in $D$- or $\Lambda_c$-like states; the authors speculate this effect may contribute to the observed $J/\psi$-suppression in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, but note that their numerical simulation is limited to cylindrically symmetric configurations and they cannot yet simulate a fully 3-dimensional situation with non-zero transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors use the Friedberg-Lee color dielectric model, solved via a semiclassical transport algorithm (Vlasov equations for quark distributions, a classical equation of motion for the scalar soliton field, and a Poisson-like equation for the confined color electric field). They first generate a bound $c\bar{c}$-state (mimicking a $J/\psi$) by adiabatically increasing the quark mass from 10 MeV to 1.56 GeV within a light meson state. They then prepare a stationary chromoelectric flux tube by pulling the opposite charge distributions of a light groundstate meson apart to a final spatial extent of about 8 fm, insert the $c\bar{c}$-state into the center of the tube, and solve the full set of dynamical equations.

## Key numbers

- The volume-averaged electric field in the flux tube is $\langle E_z \rangle = 1.6$ fm$^{-2}$, with $E_{z,max} = 2.2$ fm$^{-2}$ along the z-axis; the string radius is approximately 0.8 fm.
- The effective string constant is $\tau = 1$ GeV/fm.
- The generated $c\bar{c}$-state has a mass $m_{c\bar{c}} = 4.10$ GeV and a radius $r_{cms} \approx 0.25$ fm; the generated $c\bar{q}$-state has a mass $m_{c\bar{q}} = 2.25$ GeV and a radius $r_{cms} \approx 0.40$ fm.
- The dissociation timescale for the $c\bar{c}$-state inside the flux tube is less than about 1 fm/c.
- The time needed for a $J/\psi$ to pass through a flux tube is estimated as $\Delta t \approx 2$ to 4 fm/c for transverse momenta from 1 to 5 GeV/c.
- The gain in longitudinal momentum for the heavy quarks is estimated as $|\Delta p_z| \approx 1.6$ GeV/fm $\times \Delta t \approx 3$ to 6 GeV/c.
- The increase in longitudinal kinetic energy is estimated as $\Delta W \approx 3$ to 6 GeV, compared to the energy needed to break up the $c\bar{c}$-state into two D-meson-like states of about 0.68 GeV.
- The vector coupling is $\alpha_s \equiv g_v^2/4\pi = 1.92$.
- The bare charm quark mass used is $m_c = 1.56$ GeV.
- In a central S+U reaction at 200 AGeV, about 58 strings are produced in a transverse area of about 40 fm$^2$, corresponding to a mean area of 0.7 fm$^2$/string.

## Context

This work extends the Friedberg-Lee color dielectric model (Friedberg and Lee, 1977) to study $J/\psi$ dissociation by color electric flux tubes, which are visualized as the strings produced in microscopic models of heavy ion collisions (e.g., FRITIOF, RQMD, VENUS, HSD). The paper addresses the observed $J/\psi$-suppression in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, originally proposed as a quark-gluon plasma signal by Matsui and Satz (1986), and later seen in NA38 and NA50 data. The authors suggest that dissociation by flux tubes could provide an alternative or additional mechanism to absorption on nucleons (Gerschel and Hüfner, 1992) and absorption by comovers (Gavin and Vogt, 1996; Cassing and Ko, 1996). The work enables future incorporation of this dissociation mechanism into microscopic transport algorithms to test its quantitative impact on $J/\psi$ suppression.
