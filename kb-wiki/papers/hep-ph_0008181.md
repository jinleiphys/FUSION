# Towards an understanding of isospin violation in pion-nucleon scattering

> We investigate isospin breaking in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. This work extends the systematic analysis of [1] to the energy range above threshold. Various relations, which identically vanish in the limit of isospin symmetry, are used to quantify isospin breaking effects. We study the energy dependence of the S- and P-wave projections of these ratios and find dramatic effects in the S-waves of those two relations which are given in terms of isoscalar quantities only. This effect drops rather quickly with growing center-of-mass energy.

## Key claim

This work extends the systematic analysis of isospin violation in pion-nucleon scattering from threshold to energies above threshold using chiral perturbation theory, but only includes the leading isospin breaking effects from pion and nucleon mass differences while omitting explicit virtual-photon effects, which are left for future work.

## Method

The authors calculate the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude to third order in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including strong isospin breaking from the light quark mass difference and electromagnetic contributions to the pion and nucleon masses. They define six ratios \(R_1\) through \(R_6\) that vanish in the isospin-symmetric limit and study the energy dependence of their S-wave and P-wave projections from threshold up to about 50 MeV above threshold in the center-of-mass system.

## Key numbers

- The charged-to-neutral pion mass difference is of order 5 MeV, while the nucleon mass splitting is about 1 MeV.
- The low-energy constant \(C\) is fixed to \(C = 5.9 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^4\) from the pion mass difference.
- The strong contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is \((-2.05 \pm 0.30)\) MeV, and the electromagnetic contribution is \((0.76 \mp 0.30)\) MeV, fixing \(f_2 = (-0.45 \pm 0.19)\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The purely isoscalar ratios \(R_1\) and \(R_6\) show large isospin violation in S-waves, with the effect dropping by a factor of two over the first 50 MeV above threshold.
- Ratios \(R_2\) through \(R_5\) show isospin breaking effects of order 1–2% in S-waves.
- In the P\(_1\)-wave projection, \(R_2\) and \(R_3\) show effects as large as 30% about 30 MeV above threshold, but this arises from very small denominators in the isospin-conserving part.
- In the \(\mathcal{H}\)-projection of P-waves, \(R_2\) indicates isospin breaking of \(-6\%\) at threshold.

## Context

This work extends the threshold analysis of Fettes and Meißner (2000) to energies above threshold, building on the isospin-symmetric chiral perturbation theory calculations of Fettes, Meißner, and Steininger (1998) and the effective field theory framework for including virtual photons developed by Meißner, Müller, and Steininger (1997). The results enable future inclusion of full virtual-photon effects, which will allow direct fitting to experimental cross section and polarization data without relying on separate electromagnetic correction codes. The analysis contradicts earlier phenomenological studies by Gibbs (1987) and Matsinos (1997) that reported isospin violation as large as 7% in the triangle relation, though direct comparison is difficult due to different methods of separating electromagnetic effects.
