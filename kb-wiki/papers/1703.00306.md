# Repulsive interactions and their effects on the thermodynamics of hadron gas

> We compare two approaches in modeling repulsive interactions among hadrons: the excluded volume approximation and the S-matrix formalism. These are applied to study the thermodynamics of the $πN Δ$ system. It is shown that the introduction of an extraneous repulsion between pions and nucleons via the excluded volume approach, in addition to the interaction that generates the $Δ$-resonance, is incompatible with the analysis based on the physical phase shift of pion-nucleon scattering in the $P_{33}$ channel. This finding suggests that the repulsive force between hadrons is interaction-channel dependent and is hence unlikely to be captured by a single phenomenological parameter. The S-matrix approach employed here can be used, however, to provide useful estimates of the magnitude of the effective eigenvolume.

# Repulsive interactions and their effects on the thermodynamics of hadron gas

## Key claim

The introduction of an extraneous repulsive force between pions and nucleons via the excluded volume approach, in addition to the interaction that generates the Delta-resonance, is incompatible with the analysis based on the physical phase shift of pion-nucleon scattering in the P33 channel. This finding suggests that the repulsive force between hadrons is interaction-channel dependent and is unlikely to be captured by a single phenomenological parameter.

## Method

The authors compared two approaches for modeling repulsive interactions among hadrons: the excluded volume approximation (using a thermodynamically self-consistent formulation with a constant eigenvolume) and the S-matrix formalism (using the relativistic virial expansion of Dashen et al. with phase shifts). They applied both methods to study the thermodynamics of the pi N Delta system at finite temperature and zero chemical potential, computing the thermodynamic pressure and baryon number susceptibility. For the S-matrix approach, they used empirical P33 phase shift data from pion-nucleon scattering, parameterized by a phenomenological formula reproducing the P-wave scattering length, and also considered a modified version with an additional hard-core P-wave phase shift.

## Key numbers

- The P-wave scattering length for the P33 channel is a^{3/2}_{3/2} = 0.21 m_pi^{-3}, compatible with the experimental value of 0.21(1) m_pi^{-3}
- The excluded volume model used a constant eigenvolume v0 = (16/3) pi r0^3 with r0 = 0.3 fm
- The hard-core phase shift in the modified S-matrix scheme used a scattering length a_S = 2 r0 = 0.6 fm
- The Delta resonance parameters used in the phase shift fit: m0 = 1.2325 GeV, alpha0 = 45.37, c1 = 16.7 GeV^{-2}, c2 = 65.6 GeV^{-2}
- The empirical phase shift data were used in the elastic region M less than or approximately equal to 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model of Braun-Munzinger (2003) by addressing the treatment of repulsive interactions among hadrons. It builds on prior studies of excluded volume effects by Hagedorn (1980, 1982), Gorenstein (1981), Rischke, Kapusta, Albright (2015), Andronic (2012), and Zalewski (2015), as well as the S-matrix approach of Dashen et al. and Weinhold (1997). The paper also relates to studies of pi pi and pi K scattering by Broniowski and collaborators, where strong cancellation between different isospin channels in the S-wave was demonstrated. The finding that repulsive forces are channel-dependent enables future work using the S-matrix formalism with channel-dependent scattering lengths, as supported by phenomenology in pi pi and pi K scattering phase shifts where Ishida found a strong isospin dependence of the hard-core radius parameter.
