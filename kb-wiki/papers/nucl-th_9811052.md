# Carbon Isotopes Near Drip Lines in the Relativistic Mean-Field Theory

> We have investigated the ground-state properties of carbon isotopes in the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. RMF calculations have been performed with the non-linear scalar self-coupling of the $σ$ meson using an axially symmetric deformed configuration. We have also introduced the vector self-coupling of the $ω$ meson for the deformed mean-field calculations. The results show that the RMF predictions on radii and deformations are in good agreement with the available experimental data. It is shown that several carbon isotopes possess a highly deformed shape akin to a superdeformation. The single-particle structure of nuclei away from the stability line has been discussed with a view to understand the properties near the neutron drip line. Predictions of properties of carbon isotopes away from the stability line are made.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, using both scalar self-coupling of the σ meson and vector self-coupling of the ω meson, provides a good description of ground-state properties of carbon isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line, but the authors hedge that the RMF theory overestimates the charge radius of ¹²C slightly and that the predicted neutron radii for heavier isotopes do not show a neutron halo even near the drip line.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric deformed RMF calculations using the oscillator expansion method with 12 shells for fermionic and bosonic wavefunctions, employing three different RMF forces: NL-SH (scalar self-coupling only), TM1 (scalar and vector self-coupling), and NL-SV1 (newly developed scalar-vector force). Pairing was included using the BCS formalism with constant pairing gaps obtained from the Möller and Nix prescription, and the centre-of-mass correction was included using the harmonic oscillator zero-point energy.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for ¹²C: NL-SH gives 92.2 MeV, TM1 gives 92.1 MeV, NL-SV1 gives 91.5 MeV (experimental value 92.2 MeV from Audi and Wapstra 1993)
- Quadrupole deformation β₂ for ¹⁰C: NL-SH predicts highly prolate β₂ ~ 0.54; TM1 predicts oblate β₂ ~ -0.29; NL-SV1 predicts oblate β₂ ~ -0.21
- Quadrupole deformation β₂ for ¹²C: NL-SH gives β₂ ~ -0.29; TM1 gives β₂ ~ -0.39; NL-SV1 gives β₂ ~ -0.33 (experimental estimates range from β₂ ~ -0.29 to β₂ = -0.41)
- Charge radius of ¹²C: NL-SH predicts 2.66 fm, TM1 and NL-SV1 predict about 2.7 fm (experimental value 2.47 ± 0.02 fm)
- Charge radius of ¹⁴C: experimental value 2.56 ± 0.05 fm
- Neutron radius increase from ¹⁴C to ¹⁶C: approximately 0.5 fm for all forces
- Pairing gaps: Δₙ = 4.8/N¹/³ and Δₚ = 4.8/Z¹/³
- Secondary minimum for ¹⁰C with TM1: β₂ ~ 0.64 (superdeformed); with NL-SV1: β₂ ~ 0.58
- Quadrupole deformation for ²⁰C with TM1: β₂ = -0.44 (oblate, close to 2:3 axis ratio)
- Energy gap between 1s1/2 level and next shell for prolate ¹⁰C: about 17.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of nuclear structure across the periodic table, particularly following the success of the NL-SH force developed by Sharma et al. (1993) for describing isotope shifts and binding energies. The paper compares its RMF+BCS results with spherical Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations by Posc. (1997) using the NL3 force, finding good agreement for spherical nuclei. The study enables predictions for carbon isotopes near the neutron drip line, showing that while several isotopes exhibit large oblate deformations (¹⁸C, ²⁰C, ²²C), no neutron halo is predicted even for ²⁴C near the drip line, contrary to expectations from halo phenomena observed in lighter systems like ¹¹Li.
