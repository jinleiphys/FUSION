# Screening Effects on $^1S_0$ Pairing in Neutron Matter

> The $^1S_0$ superfluidity of neutron matter is studied in the framework of the generalized Gorkov equation. The vertex corrections to the pairing interaction and the self-energy corrections are introduced and approximated on the same footing in the gap equation. A suppression of the pairing gap by more than 50% with respect to the BCS prediction is found, which deeply changes the scenario for the dynamical and thermal evolution of neutron stars.

## Key claim
The inclusion of both self-energy corrections and vertex corrections (screening) on the same footing in the gap equation suppresses the \(^1S_0\) pairing gap in neutron matter by more than 50% relative to the BCS prediction, but the vertex corrections cause a further reduction that is smaller than the reduction from self-energy corrections alone.

## Method
The authors solved the generalized Gorkov gap equation for homogeneous neutron matter, truncating the expansion of the interaction block and self-energy to second order in the interaction (lowest-order particle-hole bubble insertions). They replaced the bare NN interaction with the phenomenological Gogny force D1 at all coupling vertices and used the pole approximation for the anomalous propagator, retaining the quasi-particle strength factor \(Z_k\) from the dynamical self-energy. The gap equation was solved for the \(^1S_0\) channel as a function of Fermi momentum \(k_F\).

## Key numbers
- The peak pairing gap at the Fermi surface is about 1.4 MeV at \(k_F = 0.6\) fm\(^{-1}\) when both self-energy and vertex corrections are included.
- The inclusion of vertex corrections (screening) produces a further reduction of the gap beyond the reduction from self-energy corrections alone, leading to a total suppression of more than 50% compared to the BCS prediction.
- The screening potential is reported at three densities: \(k_F = 0.5\), 0.8, and 1.1 fm\(^{-1}\), with strength concentrated near the Fermi energy (\(\omega = 0\)) and a peak at \(k = k' = k_F\).
- The second-order self-energy contributions \(\Sigma^{(2)}_{hh}\) show a pronounced maximum near the Fermi energy, in agreement with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the \(G\)-matrix (Zuo, 1999).

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Zuo (1999) on self-energy effects in neutron matter pairing by consistently adding vertex corrections (screening) on the same footing. It confirms the strong reduction of the \(^1S_0\) gap found in correlated basis function calculations by Clark et al. (1976), which predicted a suppression by a factor of 4, and is consistent with polarization potential approaches from Wambach et al. (1993) and Chen et al. (1993) using induced interaction theory. The results imply that the strong suppression of pairing due to polarization effects in neutron matter may not directly apply to nuclear matter or finite nuclei, where different quantum numbers and surface effects could alter the outcome.
