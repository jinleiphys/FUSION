# Magnetic moment and radius of the nucleon in a nonlocal model of meson-nucleon interaction

> Magnetic moment and radius of the nucleon are calculated in a nonlocal extension of the chiral linear σ-model. Properties of the nonlocal model under the vector and axial transformations are considered. The conserved electromagnetic and vector currents, and partially conserved axial vector current are obtained. In the calculation of the nucleon electromagnetic vertex the π- and σ-loop diagrams are included. Contribution from vector mesons is added in the vector meson dominance model with a gauge-invariant photon-meson coupling. The nonlocality parameter associated with the πN interaction is fixed from the experimental magnetic moment of the neutron. Other parameters (nonlocality parameter for the σN interaction and the mass of the σ-meson) are constrained by the magnetic moment of the proton. The calculated electric and magnetic mean-square radii of the proton and neutron are in satisfactory agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a nonlocal extension of the chiral linear σ-model, with the nonlocality parameter for πN interaction fixed from the experimental neutron magnetic moment and the σN nonlocality parameter and σ-meson mass constrained by the proton magnetic moment, can simultaneously describe the anomalous magnetic moments of both nucleons, whereas the local linear σ-model at one-loop level cannot.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonlocal extension of the chiral linear σ-model by introducing form functions h_π and h_σ for the πN and σN interactions, with a monopole form factor λ²/(λ² − k²) in momentum space. They calculated the γNN vertex in covariant perturbation theory at one-loop order, including π-loop and σ-loop diagrams, and verified gauge invariance through the Ward-Takahashi identity. Vector meson contributions (ρ and ω) were added using the vector meson dominance model with a gauge-invariant photon-meson coupling to improve the description of mean-square radii.

## Key numbers

- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment κ_n = −1.913 (experimental value used as reference); the nonlocality parameter λ_π was fixed to reproduce this value
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment κ_p = 1.793 (experimental value used as reference); the nonlocality parameter λ_σ and σ-meson mass m_σ were constrained to reproduce this value
- Prior local σ-model calculation gave κ_p = 0.51 and κ_n = −3.7 (Naus and Koch, 1987) for the pion-loop approximation with pseudo-scalar coupling
- Prior VMD model with pion-loop gave κ_p = 1.9, κ_n = −2.26 for pseudo-scalar coupling and κ_p = 1.74, κ_n = −2.08 for pseudo-vector coupling (Tiemeijer and Tjon, 1990)
- Prior dynamical model with Δ-resonance and VMD gave κ_p = 1.45 and κ_n = −1.65 (Schoch, 1995)
- The σ-meson mass used in the local model was m_σ ≈ 800 MeV (Davies et al., 1993)

## Context

This work extends the chiral linear σ-model approach of Davies et al. (1993), which could reproduce the proton anomalous magnetic moment with a heavy σ-meson but failed for the neutron. It also builds on the pion-loop and vector meson dominance calculations of Naus and Koch (1987) and Tiemeijer and Tjon (1990), which achieved partial success but did not simultaneously fit both nucleon magnetic moments. The nonlocal model enables a consistent one-loop description of both proton and neutron electromagnetic properties, and the inclusion of vector mesons via the VMD model of Schoch (1995) improves the electric and magnetic mean-square radii.
