# Relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with explicit intermediate negative energy states

> In a relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculation we include explicit negative-energy states in the two-body propagator. This is achieved by using the Gross spectator-equation, modified by medium effects. Qualitatively our results compare well with other RBHF calculations. In some details significant differences occur, e.g, our equation of state is stiffer and the momentum dependence of the self-energy components is stronger than found in a reference calculation without intermediate negative energy states.

## Key claim

Including explicit intermediate negative-energy states in the two-body propagator via the Gross spectator equation yields a stiffer equation of state and stronger momentum dependence of the self-energy components compared to standard relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations that omit such states, though the results depend significantly on how the off-shell nucleon formfactor is treated in the medium.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations using the Gross spectator equation, a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation that retains both positive- and negative-energy intermediate states in the two-body propagator. They employed the IA model of Gross et al. (1992) for the one-boson-exchange interaction, which fits nucleon-nucleon scattering data, and uniquely decomposed the nucleon self-energy into scalar, time-like vector, and space-like vector components by calculating all quadratic forms involving both positive- and negative-energy effective spinors. The self-consistent calculation iterated the self-energy evaluated at the Fermi surface, with medium effects incorporated through Pauli-blocking operators and a rescaled nucleonic formfactor depending on an adjustable parameter alpha.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility at saturation: 250 MeV for the spectator equation with alpha = 0.95, compared to 180 MeV for the Bonn-C potential (Thompson reduction).
- At normal nuclear matter density (p_f = 0.27 GeV/c), the isospin I=1 channel contributes Sigma^s = -237 MeV and Sigma^0 = -197 MeV; the I=0 channel contributes Sigma^s = -49 MeV and Sigma^0 = -10 MeV.
- The omega-meson coupling constant in the spectator-equation OBE model is g_omega^2/4pi = 9.85, compared to 20 in the Bonn-C potential.
- For the spectator equation at normal density, Sigma^s varies from -344 MeV at small momenta to -286 MeV at the Fermi surface and -186 MeV at the largest momentum; for Bonn-C, Sigma^s varies from -320 MeV deep in the Fermi sea to -297 MeV at p_f and -234 MeV at the highest momentum.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of Anastasio et al. (1983), Horowitz and Serot (1987), ter Haar and Malfliet (1987), and Machleidt (1989) by including negative-energy intermediate states that are absent in standard reductions like the Thompson and Blankenbecler-Sugar equations. The results enable future studies of medium effects on off-shell nucleon formfactors and call for investigations of negative-energy propagation in other three-dimensional reduction schemes, with the Utrecht group (Fleischer and Tjon, 1974-1982) and Gross et al. (1992) providing the only existing vacuum models that include such states.
