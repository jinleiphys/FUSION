# A Study on Shape of Te Isotopes in Mean Field Formalism

> The systematic investigation of ground state shape evolution from gamma unstable O(6) to spherical U(5) for Te isotopes has been presented by using the quadrupole moment constrained Hartree Fock Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the Skyrme force SLy4. 124Te has been pointed out as to be the possible critical point nucleus with E(5) symmetry.

## Key claim
The paper identifies ¹²⁴Te as a possible critical-point nucleus with E(5) symmetry, marking the shape phase transition between spherical U(5) and γ-unstable O(6) symmetries. However, the authors explicitly note that this conclusion is based only on potential energy curves with respect to the β degree of freedom, and that a quantitative identification of E(5) symmetry requires going beyond mean field.

## Method
The authors used the quadrupole moment constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method with the Skyrme force SLy4 to calculate ground-state properties of even-even ¹¹²⁻¹³⁴Te isotopes. They solved the HFB equations by expanding quasiparticle wave functions on a harmonic oscillator basis with 16 oscillator shells, employing zero-range pairing interaction with the Lipkin-Nogami method. Potential energy curves (PECs) were obtained by constraining the mass quadrupole moment and converting to the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂.

## Key numbers
- Total binding energies for ¹¹²⁻¹³⁴Te isotopes were reproduced with deviations at most 0.3% from experimental data (Audi 2003)
- Mean differences between experimental data and predictions: 2.532 MeV for the HFB method with SLy4, and 1.688 MeV for the RMF model with NL3 interaction (Lalazissis 1999)
- For ¹²⁴Te, the variation of binding energies in the PEC is less than 0.4 MeV over the β₂ range from -0.2 to 0.25
- Binding energy differences between spherical-state and ground-state for ¹¹²⁻¹³⁴Te range from 0 to 2.572 MeV
- Experimental ratio R₄/₂ = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) for ¹²⁴Te is 2.07, compared to the E(5) symmetry prediction of 2.20
- Experimental ratio R₀/₂ = E(0₂⁺)/E(2₁⁺) for ¹²⁴Te is 2.75, compared to the E(5) symmetry prediction of 3.03

## Context
This work extends previous mean-field studies of shape phase transitions in nuclei, following similar investigations of Ti isotopes using the HFB method (Bayram 2012) and RMF model (Guo 2008), as well as studies of Sm isotopes (Meng 2005) and Ce isotopes (Yu 2006) that identified critical-point nuclei with X(5) symmetry. The paper specifically builds on earlier experimental work by Subber (1986) that suggested ¹²⁴Te as a possible γ-soft nucleus, and on the theoretical framework of critical-point symmetries proposed by Iachello (2000, 2001). The results enable future quantitative studies using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with configuration mixing of projected wave functions, as applied in works by Niksic (2007) and Bender (2008), to confirm the E(5) symmetry assignment beyond the mean-field level.
