# On general ultraviolet properties of a class of confining propagators

> We study the ultraviolet properties of theories whose fundamental fields display a confining, Gribov-type, propagator. These are propagators that exhibit complex poles and violate positivity, thus precluding a physical propagating particle interpretation. We show that the properties of this type of confining propagators do not change the ultraviolet behavior of the theory, in the sense that no new ultraviolet primitive divergences are generated, thus securing the renormalizability of these confining theories. We illustrate these properties by studying a variety of models, including bosonic and fermionic confined degrees of freedom. The more intricate case of Super Yang-Mills with ${\cal N} =1$ supersymmetries in the Wess-Zumino gauge is taken as example in order to prove these statements to all orders by means of the algebraic renormalization set up.

## Key claim

The authors show that confining propagators of the Gribov type, which exhibit complex poles and violate positivity, do not alter the ultraviolet behavior of the theory: no new ultraviolet primitive divergences are generated, and the renormalizability of the original theory is preserved, provided the confining parameters are determined dynamically and do not renormalize independently.

## Method

The authors analyze the ultraviolet properties of theories with Gribov-type confining propagators by expanding the propagators into a standard massive term plus a highly ultraviolet-suppressed confining correction (e.g., \(\sim 1/k^4\) or \(1/k^6\) in four dimensions). They examine the primitive divergent diagrams at one-loop and two-loop order for scalar \(\phi^4\) theory, for a Yukawa theory with confined scalars and fermions, and for \(\mathcal{N}=1\) Super Yang-Mills in the Wess-Zumino gauge. For the supersymmetric case, they employ the algebraic renormalization formalism to prove the absence of new ultraviolet divergences to all orders.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger framework (Gribov 1978, Zwanziger 1989) and its refined version (Dudal et al. 2008, 2011) by proving that the ultraviolet renormalizability holds generally for theories with Gribov-type confining propagators, including scalar, fermionic, and supersymmetric cases. The results enable the consistent use of confining propagators in non-perturbative studies of gauge theories, such as glueball mass estimates (Dudal et al. 2010, 2013) and meson spectroscopy (Dudal et al. 2013), without introducing new ultraviolet divergences. The analysis of \(\mathcal{N}=1\) Super Yang-Mills builds on the earlier construction by Capri et al. (2014) and shows that the confining parameters are dynamically determined by the horizon condition and the vanishing of the vacuum energy.
