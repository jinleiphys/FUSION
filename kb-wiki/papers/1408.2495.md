# Repulsive aspects of pairing correlation in nuclear fusion reaction

> Numerical simulation on nuclear collisions are performed using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) in the three-dimensional coordinate space. Comparing results of the Cb-TDHFB and the conventional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations, we study effects of the pairing correlation on fusion reaction of $^{22}$O+$^{22}$O, $^{52}$Ca+$^{52}$Ca, and $^{22}$O+$^{52}$Ca, using the Skyrme SkM$^*$ functional and a contact-type pairing energy functional. Although current results are yet preliminary, they may suggest that the pairing correlation could hinder the fusion probability at energies in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier height. We also perform a calculation for heavier nuclei, $^{96}$Zn+$^{124}$Sn, which seems to suggest a similar hindrance effect.

# Repulsive aspects of pairing correlation in nuclear fusion reaction

## Key claim
The pairing correlation may hinder fusion probability at energies near the Coulomb barrier height, based on preliminary Cb-TDHFB simulations that show smaller fusion cross sections than TDHF calculations for several nuclear systems. The authors explicitly state that the results are preliminary and require further investigation of the initial gauge angle dependence.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of nuclear collisions using the canonical-basis time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Cb-TDHFB) in three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate space with the Skyrme SkM* functional and a contact-type pairing energy functional. They compared these results with conventional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations for symmetric collisions (22O+22O, 52Ca+52Ca), an asymmetric collision (22O+52Ca), and a heavier system (96Zn+124Sn), using boosted HF+BCS ground states as initial conditions.

## Key numbers
- 22O+22O: Coulomb barrier height 9 MeV; collision energy E_cm=10 MeV; average neutron gap energy 2.06 MeV; fusion cross section in TDHF larger than 11.79 pi fm^2, in Cb-TDHFB less than 11.79 pi fm^2; difference of 1.33 pi fm^2 between TDHF (11.79) and Cb-TDHFB (10.46)
- 52Ca+52Ca: Coulomb barrier height 49 MeV; collision energy E_cm=51.5 MeV; average neutron gap energy 1.86 MeV; difference of 0.28 pi fm^2 between TDHF (6.78) and Cb-TDHFB (6.50)
- 22O+52Ca: Coulomb barrier height 21 MeV; collision energy E_cm=25 MeV; impact parameter range 3.0 to 4.5 fm
- 96Zn+124Sn: charge product Z_P Z_T=2000; head-on collision (b=0) at E_cm=227.7 MeV; average neutron gaps 2.73 MeV for 96Zr and 2.42 MeV for 124Sn (about twice experimental values); in Cb-TDHFB the nuclei separate at approximately 1000 fm/c versus approximately 2000 fm/c in TDHF

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous development of the Cb-TDHFB method (Ebata and Nakatsukasa, 2010) and applies it to heavy-ion collisions for the first time with a modern effective interaction in three-dimensional space. The study contradicts the expectation that pairing correlation might enhance fusion, instead suggesting a repulsive hindrance effect. This enables future investigation of pairing effects in fusion reactions, particularly for heavier systems where fusion hindrance is known to occur when Z_P Z_T exceeds 1600, and builds upon earlier TDHF studies of heavy-ion collisions (Negele, 1982) and recent efforts toward TDHFB simulations (Bulgac and Luo, 2010; Hashimoto, 2013).
