# Coupled-channel study of gamma p --> K+ Lambda

> A coupled-channel (CC) approach has been developed to investigate kaon photoproduction on the nucleon. In addition to direct K+ Lambda production, our CC approach accounts for strangeness production including K+ Lambda final state interactions with both pi0 p and pi+ n intermediate states. Calculations for the gamma p --> K+ Lambda reaction have been performed, and compared with the recent data from SAPHIR, with emphasis on the CC effects. We show that the CC effects are significant at the level of inducing 20% changes on total cross sections; thereby, demonstrating the need to include pi N channels to correctly describe the gamma p --> K+ Lambda reaction.

## Key claim
Coupled-channel (CC) effects from intermediate πN states induce changes of up to 20% on total cross sections for γ p → K+ Λ, demonstrating that one-step isobar models ignoring these final-state interactions cannot correctly describe kaon photoproduction. The authors do not aim for an accurate reproduction of the SAPHIR data but rather to delineate the role of the CC mechanism.

## Method
The authors extended the dynamical model of Sato and Lee (1996) for pion photoproduction to include K+ Λ and πN channels. They adopted the Williams-Ji-Cotanch (WJC) isobar model for the direct γ p → K+ Λ amplitude and used VPI partial-wave amplitudes for γ N → π N and π N → π N. The nonresonant meson-baryon transition operators for KY → KY and π N → KY were derived from effective Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method, and the coupled-channel equations were solved numerically with a single cutoff parameter Λc = 680 MeV/c fixed by fitting π− p → K0 Λ total cross section data.

## Key numbers
- CC effects induce changes of up to 20% on total cross sections for γ p → K+ Λ.
- The cutoff parameter Λc was set to 680 MeV/c by fitting π− p → K0 Λ total cross section data.
- The kaon photoproduction threshold is at √s = 1.61 GeV; calculations extend to about 2.5 GeV.
- The readjusted coupling for Σ0 is gKΣN/√(4π) = 0.404 (WJC value was 0.222).
- The readjusted coupling for N*(1650) is GN4/√(4π) = -0.025 (WJC value was -0.044).
- The readjusted coupling for Λ*(1810) is GL5/√(4π) = 0.125 (WJC value was 0).
- The readjusted coupling for N*(1535) is GN3/√(4π) = 0.030 (WJC value was 0).

## Context
This work extends the dynamical coupled-channel approach of Sato and Lee (1996) for pion photoproduction to kaon photoproduction, addressing a gap left by isobar models such as Williams, Ji and Cotanch (1992) which ignore πN intermediate states. It differs from the chiral SU(3) dynamics approach of Kaiser et al. (1997) by including higher partial waves applicable at all energies, and from the K-matrix method of Feuster and Mosel (1998) by accounting for off-shell dynamical effects. The results enable future inclusion of KΣ channels, ηN, πΔ, and ρN channels for a complete coupled-channel calculation of kaon electromagnetic production.
