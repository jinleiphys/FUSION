# Fox's H-Functions: A Gentle Introduction Through Astrophysical Thermonuclear Functions

> Needed for cosmological and stellar nucleosynthesis, we are studying the closed-form analytic evaluation of thermonuclear reaction rates. In this context, we undertake a comprehensive analysis of three distinct velocity distributions, namely the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, the pathway distribution, and the Mittag-Leffer distribution. We emphasize the utilization of Meijer G-function and Fox H-function which are special functions of mathematical physics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that three distinct velocity distributions (Maxwell-Boltzmann, pathway, and Mittag-Leffler) used for computing thermonuclear reaction rates in stellar and cosmological nucleosynthesis can be expressed in closed analytic form using Meijer G-functions and Fox H-functions, with the Mittag-Leffler distribution being the most general, covering the Maxwell-Boltzmann case as a limit when the parameter beta equals 1.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for non-resonant thermonuclear reaction rate integrals under three velocity distribution models: the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, the pathway distribution (which generalizes Tsallis non-extensive statistics), and the Mittag-Leffler distribution (which generalizes the exponential function). For each case, they evaluate the reaction rate integral ⟨σv⟩ by expanding the cross-section in a power series in energy and representing the resulting integrals as Meijer G-functions or Fox H-functions via Mellin-Barnes contour integration. They also treat a cut-off version of the Mittag-Leffler case where the integral is truncated at a finite upper limit d.

## Key numbers

- The Maxwell-Boltzmann reaction rate is expressed as a Meijer G-function of the form G_{0,3}^{3,0} with argument x²/4, where x = 2π(μ/2)^{1/2} z_i z_j e²/ħ
- The pathway distribution parameter α satisfies α > 1 and 1/(α-1) > 3/2; the Maxwell-Boltzmann case is recovered as α → 1
- The Mittag-Leffler parameter β satisfies 0 < β ≤ 1; the Maxwell-Boltzmann case is recovered when β = 1
- Figures show velocity distributions computed for reduced mass μ = 1, with temperatures T = 100, 200, 300, 400, 500 for Maxwell-Boltzmann; T = 300 with α = 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5 for pathway; T = 500 and T = 700 with β = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, 1 for Mittag-Leffler

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatment of non-resonant thermonuclear reaction rates by Haubold and Kumar (2008), who first applied the pathway model to reaction rate integrals and represented them in terms of Fox H-functions. The paper also builds on the Mittag-Leffler Gaussian distribution model introduced by Agahi and Alipour. By expressing all three velocity distribution cases in closed form using special functions, the work enables efficient computation of reaction rates for scenarios where the plasma deviates from hydrostatic equilibrium, including cut-off, depleted, and screened cases relevant to stellar and cosmological nucleosynthesis modeling.
