# Polarization effects in $e^+ +e^-\to \bar d+d$ and determination of time like deuteron form factors

> Polarization effects in the reaction $e^++e^-\to \bar d+d$ have been investigated for the case of longitudinally polarized electron beam and arbitrary polarization of the produced deuteron, with the aim of a determination of the time-like complex deuteron electromagnetic form factors. General expressions of polarization observables are derived and numerical estimations have been carried out by means of various models of deuteron electromagnetic form factors, for kinematical conditions near threshold.

## Key claim

Polarization observables in the reaction \(e^+ + e^- \to \bar d + d\) can, in principle, fully determine the three complex time-like deuteron electromagnetic form factors, but the numerical predictions are highly model-dependent because existing parametrizations are constrained only by space-like data and their analytic continuation to the time-like region yields very different results, especially for polarization observables that depend on the imaginary parts of the form factors.

## Method

General expressions for the differential cross section and for the vector and tensor polarization observables of the produced deuteron were derived in the one-photon-exchange approximation, assuming a longitudinally polarized electron beam and arbitrary deuteron polarization. Numerical estimates were then carried out for a center-of-mass energy of 1.9 GeV (near the reaction threshold) using three different models of the deuteron electromagnetic form factors: a phenomenological sum-of-inverse-polynomials parametrization (Parametrization I), a vector-meson-dominance model (Iachello-type model), and a model based on unitarity and analyticity.

## Key numbers

* The total cross section for \(e^+e^- \to \bar d d\) is given by \(\sigma_{tot} = \frac{\pi\alpha^2\beta^3}{3q^2}\left[3|G_C|^2 + 4\tau(|G_M|^2 + \frac{2}{3}\tau|G_Q|^2)\right]\), where \(\beta = \sqrt{1-4M^2/q^2}\) is the deuteron velocity in the center-of-mass system and \(\tau = q^2/(4M^2)\).
* The center-of-mass threshold energy for the reaction is \(E_T = 2M \simeq 3.75\) GeV, corresponding to \(q^2 \simeq 14\) GeV\(^2\).
* The angular asymmetry \(R\) (defined from the unpolarized differential cross section) is predicted to be over 90% in absolute value and negative for all considered models near threshold.
* The imaginary parts of the form factors from the Iachello-type model are an order of magnitude smaller than the real parts for \(q^2 > 0.08\) GeV\(^2\); the model based on unitarity and analyticity has an imaginary part starting at the deuteron anomalous threshold \(q^2 = 1.73 m_\pi^2 \simeq 0.02\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the polarization method previously applied to elastic electron-proton scattering (Re68; JG00) to the time-like region for a spin-1 particle, enabling a complete determination of the three complex deuteron electromagnetic form factors. The formalism is model-independent and applies also to other spin-1 final states such as \(\rho^+\rho^-\) or \(\omega^+\omega^-\). The numerical predictions rely on extrapolations of space-like parametrizations (e.g., the VMD model of Iachello (1973) and the analyticity-based model of Dubnička (1991, 2006)) into the time-like region, where no experimental data exist; the results show that polarization effects either vanish or are large and measurable, but the models differ by orders of magnitude in the predicted cross section.
