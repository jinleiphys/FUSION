# The evolving distribution of hard partons traversing a hot strongly interacting plasma

> We present results for the evolution of the momentum distribution of a hard parton traversing a brick of quark-gluon plasma, considering both bremsstrahlung and collisional energy loss. The complete leading order transition rates are included. We find a significant modification of the evolving momentum distribution compared to results obtained by employing an approximate implementation of the collisional energy loss that uses the mean energy loss, together with momentum diffusion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the full leading-order perturbative treatment of collisional energy loss, beyond the commonly used diffusion approximation (mean energy loss plus momentum diffusion), leads to a significantly different shape of the evolving hard parton momentum distribution, even though the mean energy loss is only slightly larger.

## Method

The authors solve a Fokker-Planck type rate equation for the momentum distribution of a hard parton traversing a static "brick" of quark-gluon plasma at constant temperature. They compute the full leading-order transition rates for both elastic (collisional) and radiative (bremsstrahlung) energy loss, using two independent methods: method A separates soft and hard momentum transfers with a cutoff q*, while method B uses the effective thermal gluon propagator over the entire momentum range. These results are compared to the diffusion approximation used in prior work by Wicks (2005) and Qin (2007), where elastic collisions were approximated by a mean energy loss plus a diffusion term.

## Key numbers

* For a 10 GeV quark jet at T=200 MeV after 5 fm of plasma, the difference in mean energy loss between the full calculation and the diffusion method is approximately 30% for purely collisional energy loss.
* The calculations use a strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.3, with N_f = 3 quark flavors, at plasma temperatures of T=200 MeV and T=300 MeV.
* Results are shown for medium path lengths of 2 fm and 5 fm, for initial parton energies of E=10 GeV (with E=100 GeV noted as qualitatively similar).
* The thermal gluon mass is defined as m_g^2 = 1/2 (1 + N_f/6) g^2 T^2, which sets the characteristic soft scale of the system.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Qin (2007) and Wicks (2005), who first computed jet momentum distributions including both radiative and collisional energy loss but approximated elastic collisions by a mean energy loss plus momentum diffusion. The paper directly tests the validity of that diffusion approximation and finds it inadequate for describing the full shape of the distribution. The results enable more precise comparisons between theoretical energy loss formalisms and experimental jet quenching measurements at RHIC and LHC, particularly for observables sensitive to the shape of the momentum distribution rather than just the mean energy loss.
