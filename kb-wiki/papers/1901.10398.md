# Comment on "How (not) to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory"

> I critically discuss two of the potential inconsistencies pointed out in the recent manuscript by Epelbaum, Gasparyan, Gegelia and Meissner, published in Eur. Phys.J. A54, 186 (2018). The potential inconsistencies are: (i) a possible mismatch between the $\hbar$ expansion of the scattering amplitude and renormalization, (ii) an impossibility of "non-perturbative renormalization" to deal with repulsive singular interactions. The conclusion is that these inconsistencies do not happen.

# Comment on "How (not) to renormalize integral equations with singular potentials in effective field theory"

## Key claim
The two potential inconsistencies claimed by Epelbaum, Gasparyan, Gegelia and Meissner (2018), a mismatch between the ℏ expansion of the scattering amplitude and renormalization, and an impossibility of non-perturbative renormalization for repulsive singular interactions, do not actually occur when the renormalization procedure is properly understood and applied.

## Method
The author analyzes the ℏ-expansion inconsistency by introducing the concept of redundant counterterms (RCs), which cancel residual cutoff dependence without carrying physical information, and demonstrates that including RCs removes the apparent divergence in T[1]. For the repulsive singular potential toy model, the author performs explicit phase-shift calculations using a mixed perturbative and non-perturbative renormalization scheme, where the long-range repulsive singular potential is treated non-perturbatively at leading order while contact interactions are included according to a power counting that demotes them by one order relative to naive dimensional analysis.

## Key numbers
- Phase shifts are computed for the toy model of Epelbaum et al. (2018) at cutoffs Rc = 0.3, 0.6, 1.2, and 1.8 fm, up to order Q7 (N8LO) in the EFT expansion.
- The contact couplings C2n are determined by fitting to toy model phase shifts in the 20–80 MeV range for ν = 1,3 and the 80–200 MeV range for ν = 5,7.
- The minimum cutoff used is Rc ≥ 0.3 fm, below which numerical limitations prevent calculations.
- In the power counting used, C0 δ(r) enters at order Q1, C2 ∇2 δ(r) at order Q3, C4 ∇4 δ(r) at order Q5, and C6 ∇6 δ(r) at order Q7.

## Context
This comment directly addresses the critique by Epelbaum, Gasparyan, Gegelia and Meissner (2018) regarding non-perturbative renormalization in effective field theory, and extends the mixed renormalization approach previously advocated by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005). The work builds on the redundant counterterm concept from Valderrama (2016) and adapts power-counting ideas from Valderrama (2014) to handle repulsive singular interactions. The calculations follow methods analogous to Valderrama (2011) but extended to higher orders, and the results contradict the claim by Epelbaum et al. (2018) that a purely non-perturbative approach with judiciously chosen cutoff is necessary. The work enables future EFT treatments of singular potentials without strong restrictions on the cutoff scale.
