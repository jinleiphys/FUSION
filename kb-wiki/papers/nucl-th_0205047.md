# On the cascade approach to the quantum multiscattering problem

> The multiscattering problem is studied in the matrix density formalism. We study how to isolate the quasi-classical degrees of freedom in order to connect with a cascade approach. The different problems that arise, as well as their possible solutions, are discussed and exemplified with a pion-nucleus model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a cascade approach to quantum multiscattering can be systematically derived from exact quantum-mechanical equations using the Wigner transformation, provided that quasi-classical degrees of freedom are properly isolated and virtual states are integrated out. The author qualifies that the resulting "probability densities" in phase space are not necessarily positive definite, and that positivity is only required after integration to obtain cross sections.

## Method

The author uses the Wigner transformation of the density matrix to derive a quantum transport equation in phase space, then expands in powers of \(\hbar\) to obtain the classical limit. For the many-body problem, a full Wigner transform in both space and time is employed to handle inelastic channels, and the optical potential formalism is used to separate elastic from non-elastic degrees of freedom. The approach is exemplified with a pion-nucleus model where the pion self-energy is computed from Feynman graphs, and the imaginary part is interpreted as a reaction width that can be separated into different channels using Cutkosky rules.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty principle relations given are \(|\Delta t| \approx \hbar / |E - E_{\text{on-shell}}(\mathbf{p})|\) and \(|\Delta \mathbf{x}| \approx \hbar / |\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{p}_{\text{on-shell}}(E)|\) (Eqs. 1.1 and 1.2).
- For large \(\Delta t, \Delta \mathbf{x}\), the ratio \(|\Delta \mathbf{x} / \Delta t| \approx |dE/d\mathbf{p}|_{\text{on-shell}}\) (Eq. 1.3), which is the classical Hamilton's equation.
- The optical potential is required to satisfy \(\hat V_I = \mathcal{O}(\hbar)\) for the classical limit of \(\partial_t \rho\) to exist (Eq. 4.14).

## Context

This work extends the program of connecting quantum transport theory with cascade models, building on earlier applications of the Wigner transformation to scattering by Remler (1975, 1979, 1981) and Gyulassy (1983). The author's motivation is to justify and improve the approach used in Salcedo (1988) and Vicente Vacas (1994), where pion-nucleus inclusive reactions were computed using a Monte Carlo simulation with microscopically computed reaction probabilities. The paper aims to provide a systematic framework for including quantum corrections in cascade methods, which have been widely used in heavy ion collisions and pion-nucleus reactions by authors such as Bertsch (1984), Kruse (1985), and Wolf (1993).
