# Accuracy of fission dynamics within the time dependent superfluid local density approximation

> We discuss properties of the method based on time dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) within an application to induced fission of 240Pu and surrounding nuclei. Various issues related to accuracy of time evolution and the determination of the properties of fission fragments are discussed.

## Key claim

The time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) can describe induced fission of Pu isotopes with total energy conservation within 0.03% to 0.2% and total kinetic energy (TKE) accuracy within 2% of experimental data, but only when all Bogoliubov states are evolved and a cubic momentum cutoff renormalization is used; lower energy cutoffs are insufficient for the long time evolution required in fission.

## Method

The authors solved the TDSLDA equations on a spatial lattice with spacing 1.25 fm for induced fission of 240Pu, 238Pu, and 242Pu compound nuclei, using the Skyrme SLy4 nuclear energy density functional. Initial states were prepared by solving static DFT equations with a quadrupole constraint at deformations slightly beyond the outer barrier, then released adiabatically. Time evolution used a fifth-order Adams-Bashforth-Milne predictor-modifier-corrector method with time step 0.03 fm/c, evolving all states within the lattice space (approximately 4 times the number of lattice points) to ensure energy conservation over the 300,000 to 500,000 time steps required for saddle-to-scission dynamics.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 1.25 fm sets cubic momentum cutoff p_c = hbar k_c approximately 860 MeV/c
- For 240Pu, 238Pu, and 242Pu, the simulation box is (25 fm)^2 x 50 fm
- Time step Delta t = 0.03 fm/c (approximately 10^-25 s) is sufficient for stable solutions; total energy difference between trajectories with Delta t = 0.03 fm/c and 0.024 fm/c is within 1 eV
- Initial excitation energy for 240Pu is 8.08 MeV above ground state, corresponding to neutron incident energy E_n = 1.54 MeV; for 238Pu the excitation is 4.64 MeV
- Precision of total energy conservation during time evolution varies from 0.5 MeV to 3.5 MeV, which is 0.03% to 0.2% of total energy
- Typical accuracy of TKE determination is about 0.5 MeV (0.3%)
- For 239Pu(n,f) at E_n = 1.54 MeV: calculated TKE = 173.8 MeV (experimental 177.3 MeV)
- For 239Pu(n,f) at E_n = 5.63 MeV: calculated TKE = 176.1 MeV (experimental 175.8 MeV)
- Agreement of TKE with experimental data for 239Pu(n,f) at E_n < 6 MeV is within 3.5 MeV (2% error)

## Context

This work extends the TDSLDA method previously applied to ultracold atomic gases (Bulgar 2009), low-energy nuclear physics (Ion 2011, 2016), and neutron star crust physics (Gandolfi 2016) to the specific challenge of induced fission in Pu isotopes. The approach contrasts with methods based on adiabatic assumptions or semiclassical approximations such as Langevin-type equations, as TDSLDA provides a fully microscopic description that can directly extract fragment total kinetic energy, total excitation energy, and energy sharing between fragments. The results enable direct comparison with experimental fission data and can be improved by employing higher-order integration methods and smaller lattice constants.
