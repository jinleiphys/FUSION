# Multiplicity density at mid-rapidity in $AA$ collisions: effect of meson cloud

> We study the influence of the meson cloud of the nucleon on predictions of the Monte Carlo Glauber model for the charged particle multiplicity density at mid-rapidity in $AA$ collisions. We find that for central $AA$ collisions the meson cloud can increase the multiplicity density by $\sim 16-18$\%. The meson-baryon Fock component reduces the required fraction of the binary collisions by a factor of $\sim 2$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=0.2$ TeV and $\sim 1.5$ for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV.

# Multiplicity density at mid-rapidity in AA collisions: effect of meson cloud

## Key claim
The meson cloud of the nucleon can increase the charged particle multiplicity density at mid-rapidity in central AA collisions by approximately 16-18%, and including the meson-baryon Fock components reduces the required fraction of binary collisions by a factor of about 2 for Au+Au at 0.2 TeV and about 1.5 for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, but only under the assumption that all wounded bare particles produce the same amount of entropy per unit pseudorapidity.

## Method
The author incorporates the meson-baryon Fock components (primarily pi N, pi Delta, rho N, rho Delta) of the nucleon wave function into a Monte Carlo Glauber (MCG) model, treating each physical nucleon as interacting either as a bare nucleon (with probability 1-n_MB) or as a two-body meson-baryon system (with probability n_MB = 0.4). The inelastic cross sections for bare states follow constituent quark counting rules, and the parameters of a Gamma distribution for multiplicity fluctuations are fitted to pp data, then the binary collision fraction alpha is fitted to reproduce the centrality dependence of dN_ch/deta in Au+Au at 0.2 TeV and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers
- For central AA collisions, the meson cloud increases the multiplicity density by approximately 16-18%.
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV, the required fraction of binary collisions alpha is approximately 0.06 with meson cloud and approximately 0.135 without meson cloud (a reduction factor of about 2).
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV, the required alpha is approximately 0.09 with meson cloud and approximately 0.14 without meson cloud (a reduction factor of about 1.5).
- The total weight of the meson-baryon Fock components in the nucleon is taken as n_MB = 0.4.
- The mean squared transverse radius of the MB component is approximately 0.87 fm.
- The fitted inelastic NN cross sections are sigma_in_NN = 26.15 mb at sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV and 38.4 mb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV.
- The mean multiplicity per source for the scenario with meson cloud is <n> = 2.39 at sqrt(s) = 0.2 TeV and 4.13 at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV.
- The Gamma distribution shape parameter k is approximately 0.506 at 0.2 TeV and 0.52 at 2.76 TeV for the meson cloud scenario.

## Context
This work extends the two-component wounded nucleon Glauber model (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001) and the Monte Carlo Glauber approach (PHOBOS Collaboration, Miller et al.) by incorporating the meson cloud structure of the nucleon, which had been previously studied in the context of nucleon structure functions (Speth and Thomas, 1997). The results show that the meson cloud can partially replace the need for a large binary collision contribution, addressing tensions between the standard Glauber model and data on elliptic flow in U+U collisions (Voloshin et al., 2010; Bass et al., 2015). The approach is complementary to quark-level wounded nucleon models (Bozek, 2016; Loizides, 2016) and suggests that the meson cloud effect should be present in any model of initial conditions for AA collisions.
