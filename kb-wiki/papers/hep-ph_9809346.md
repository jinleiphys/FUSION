# Dynamical renormalization group resummation of finite temperature infrared divergences

> We introduce the method of dynamical renormalization group to study relaxation and damping out of equilibrium directly in real time and applied it to the study of infrared divergences in scalar QED. This method allows a consistent resummation of infrared effects associated with the exchange of quasistatic transverse photons and leads to anomalous logarithmic relaxation of the form $e^{-αT t \ln[t/t_0]}$ which prevents a quasiparticle interpretation of charged collective excitations at finite temperature. The hard thermal loop resummation program is incorporated consistently into the dynamical renormalization group yielding a picture of relaxation and damping phenomena in a plasma in real time that trascends the conceptual limitations of the quasiparticle picture and other type of resummation schemes. We derive a simple criterion for establishing the validity of the quasiparticle picture to lowest order.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the dynamical renormalization group as a real-time method to resum infrared divergences in finite-temperature scalar QED, showing that charged collective excitations with hard momentum relax anomalously as \( e^{-\alpha T t \ln[t/t_0]} \) rather than exponentially, thereby invalidating the quasiparticle picture for such excitations.

## Method

The authors formulate the real-time evolution of expectation values as an initial value problem using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism, then solve the equation of motion for the charged scalar field perturbatively. They identify logarithmic secular terms in the perturbative solution and resum them non-perturbatively via the dynamical renormalization group, which is the real-time analogue of the Euclidean renormalization group. The hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation is incorporated consistently into this framework to handle screening corrections alongside the infrared secular terms.

## Key numbers

- The charged scalar field expectation value with hard momentum relaxes as \( e^{-\alpha T t \ln[t/t_0]} \) at asymptotically long times, where \(\alpha\) is the fine-structure constant, \(T\) is the temperature, and \(t_0\) is a reference time scale.
- At zero temperature, the Bloch-Nordsieck resummation yields an anomalous power-law relaxation \(\phi_k(t) \sim t^{-\lambda} \cos(\omega_k t + \pi\lambda/2)\) with \(\lambda = g^2/(4\pi^2 m_R^2)\).
- The perturbative expansion breaks down at time scales \(t_{\text{break}} \approx e^{1/\lambda}/\omega_k\) due to logarithmic secular terms of the form \(\lambda \ln(\omega_k t)\).

## Context

This work extends the Bloch-Nordsieck resummation approach of Iancu (1997) and the HTL resummation program of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) to a real-time framework that directly reveals relaxation dynamics without assuming a quasiparticle structure. It contradicts the quasiparticle picture for hard charged excitations in QED and scalar QED, where earlier studies by Kobes (1991) and Pilon (1993) had already questioned exponential relaxation. The method enables systematic study of damping and thermalization in ultrarelativistic plasmas, particularly for fermionic excitations in QCD, and provides a criterion for when the quasiparticle approximation is valid.
