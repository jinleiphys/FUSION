# Description of the Ground and Octupole Bands in the Symplectic Extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model

> In the framework of the symplectic extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) a good description of the first excited positive and negative parity bands of the nuclei in the rare earth and the actinide region is achieved. The bands investigated in the model are extended to very high angular momenta as a result of their consideration as "yrast" bands with respect to the symplectic classification of the basis states. The analysis of the eigenvalues of the model Hamiltonian reveals the presence of an interaction between these bands. Due to this iteraction the Delta(L)=1 staggering effect between the energies of the states of two bands is also reproduced including the "beat" patterns.

## Key claim
The symplectic extension of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) can describe both the ground band and the first negative-parity octupole band in deformed even-even nuclei up to very high angular momenta, using only four phenomenological parameters, by treating the bands as "yrast" bands with respect to the total boson number \(N\).

## Method
The authors use the symplectic group \(Sp(12,R)\) as the dynamical symmetry of the IVBM, with basis states built from two types of vector bosons (\(p\) and \(n\)). The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the rotational limit defined by the subgroup chain \(U(6) \supset SU(3) \otimes U(2) \supset SO(3) \otimes U(1)\), with the ground band assigned to the \(SU(3)\) multiplet \((0,\mu)\) (pseudospin \(T=0\)) and the octupole band to \((2,\mu-1)\) (pseudospin \(T=1\)). The yrast condition selects the minimum energy state for each angular momentum \(L\) by varying \(N\) (which changes in steps of \(\Delta N = 2\)), yielding a simple energy formula \(E(L) = \beta L(L+1) + (\gamma + \eta)L + \xi\).

## Key numbers
- For the ground band, the yrast condition gives \(N = 2L\); for the octupole band, \(N = 2L + 2\).
- The energy formula contains four free parameters: \(\beta\), \(\gamma\), \(\eta\), and \(\xi\), related to the original parameters by \(\beta = 4b + \beta_3\), \(\gamma = 2a - 4b\), \(\eta = 8b\), \(\xi = 2a + 4b + 2\alpha_3\).
- The model is applied to nuclei including \(^{224}\text{Ra}\), \(^{226}\text{Th}\), \(^{152}\text{Sm}\), and \(^{168}\text{Yb}\) (Figure 1), and to \(^{232}\text{Th}\), \(^{234}\text{U}\), \(^{236}\text{U}\), and \(^{238}\text{U}\) where the two bands are "almost parallel."
- The staggering function \(Stg(L) = 6\Delta E(L) - 4\Delta E(L-1) - 4\Delta E(L+1) + \Delta E(L+2) + \Delta E(L-2)\) is used to compare with experimental data (Figure 2).

## Context
This work extends the earlier IVBM (Ganev et al., 2003) and its rotational limit (IVBMrl) by introducing the symplectic dynamical symmetry \(Sp(12,R)\), which allows the boson number \(N\) to vary and enables description of bands up to very high spins. It contrasts with models that add an explicit \(f\) boson (Iachello and Arima, 1987) or use coherent state methods (Alonso et al., 1992; Raduta and Ionescu, 1998), and with point-symmetry-group models (NS1, NS2) that also reproduce odd-even staggering. The approach enables application to a larger class of nuclei and further study of other collective bands.
