# Dynamical description of heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies

> Descriptions of heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies require to take into account in-medium dissipation and phase-space fluctuations. The interplay of these correlations with the one-body collective behaviour determines the properties (kinematics and fragment production) and the variety of mechanisms (from fusion to neck formation and multifragmentation) of the exit channel. Starting from fundamental concepts tested on nuclear matter, we build up a microscopic description which addresses finite systems and applies to experimental observables.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a stochastic one-body approach (the Boltzmann-Langevin equation) can efficiently describe dissipative heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, but the abstract's claim of applying to experimental observables is hedged: the work presents only simulated collisions of a single symmetric system (130Xe+130Xe) and does not compare to experimental data, instead offering "hints" on fragment production and mechanism thresholds.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann-Langevin equation numerically using the BLOB (Boltzmann-Langevin One-Body) approach, which generates fluctuations continuously through the collision term in full phase space while respecting Pauli blocking. The one-body distribution function is propagated via the test-particle method with a Skyrme-like SKM* effective interaction (soft isoscalar equation of state with compressibility K=200 MeV and linear stiff density dependence of the symmetry energy). They simulate 130Xe+130Xe collisions at various incident energies (15-56 A MeV) and impact parameters (central to b=8 fm), analyzing density distributions and fragment multiplicities from about 3500 events.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility of the isoscalar equation of state: K = 200 MeV (soft)
- Incident energies studied: 15, 23, 32, 40, and 56 A MeV for central collisions; 15, 23, and 32 A MeV for semiperipheral collisions
- Semi-peripheral impact parameter: b ≈ 7 fm (with additional comparison at b = 8 fm)
- Fragment multiplicity extracted at "saturation time" when mean multiplicity of fragments with Z>4 stops growing
- Mean multiplicity ⟨M_frag(Z>4)⟩ mapped as function of impact parameter and incident energy from statistics of about 3500 events
- Fusion cross section fades above 20 A MeV in central collisions; symmetric break-up pattern persists till around 45 A MeV
- Neck formation occurs in the interval of about 5 < b < 9 fm
- Transition to multiple IMFs at mid-rapidity appears above about 30 A MeV and below about 8 fm

## Context

This work extends the Boltzmann-Langevin approach to finite systems, building on earlier stochastic mean-field (SMF) methods by Colonna (1998) and the BLOB model. It connects to studies of spinodal instabilities in nuclear matter (Chomaz 2004, Borderie 2008) and to isospin migration mechanisms in neck formation (Neck). The results provide a unified dynamical picture of the transitions between fusion, binary splits, neck fragmentation, and multifragmentation in the Fermi-energy domain, enabling future comparisons with experimental data for Sn and Xe isotopes and guiding further theoretical work on frustrated fragmentation and neck fragmentation patterns.
