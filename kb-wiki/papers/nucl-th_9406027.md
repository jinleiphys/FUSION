# Trapping of Projectiles in Fixed Scatterer Calculations

> We study multiple scattering off nuclei in the closure approximation. Instead of reducing the dynamics to one particle potential scattering, the scattering amplitude for fixed target configurations is averaged over the target groundstate density via stochastic integration. At low energies a strong coupling limit is found which can not be obtained in a first order optical potential approximation. As its physical explanation, we propose it to be caused by trapping of the projectile. We analyse this phenomenon in mean field and random potential approximations. (PACS: 24.10.-i)

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a fixed-scatterer (closure approximation) model of multiple scattering off nuclei, a strong coupling limit exists for low-energy projectiles which cannot be obtained in a first-order optical potential approximation; this limit is proposed to be caused by trapping of the projectile in the target system, and it appears only when the number of scattering centers is four or more.

## Method

The authors calculate elastic scattering amplitudes for fixed spatial configurations of nucleons (using s-wave projectile-nucleon interactions) and then average these amplitudes over the nuclear ground-state density via stochastic (Monte Carlo) integration with up to 4×10^6 configurations. They compare these results to first-order optical potential calculations and also analyze the eigenvalue spectrum of the coupling matrix G_ij = 1/|r_i - r_j| to understand the origin of the strong coupling limit. Mean-field and random potential approximations are examined as possible simplified models.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He, the forward scattering amplitude in the strong coupling limit saturates at approximately F(θ=0) ≈ -2.2 fm + i × 0.6 fm.
- For ¹⁶O, the ratio Im F / Re F reaches a phase-independent limit of approximately -0.55 in the fixed-scatterer calculation, whereas the optical potential result remains strongly phase-dependent and decreases.
- The harmonic oscillator density size parameters used are b = 1.41 fm for ⁴He and b = 1.71 fm for ¹⁶O.
- For the A-dependence study, a homogeneous density with R = 2.6 fm was used, giving R_0 ≈ 2.2 fm, with f_0 = 250.0 fm + i × 6.3 fm.
- For the inelastic cross section study with a unitary elementary amplitude (scattering length a = 10 fm), the total and inelastic cross sections for ⁴He rise like 1/k at small k, while for three scattering centers the total cross section becomes constant at small k.

## Context

This work extends the fixed-scatterer (frozen nucleus) approximation previously applied to pion scattering (Gibbs, Bethe) and contrasts it with the first-order optical potential approximation commonly used in nuclear physics (Foldy). The authors draw an analogy to Anderson localization in disordered lattices (Anderson, Souillard, Ziman), where backscattering correlations are essential, and note that coherent potential approximations (the tight-binding analog of the first-order optical potential) fail to describe the metal-insulator transition. The finding that trapping requires four or more centers suggests that improved optical potentials including only low-order backscattering (Keister) may still miss important physics. The paper enables future work on whether similar trapping phenomena could appear under more realistic nuclear physics conditions, and calls for further investigation of the relation to Anderson localization and classical irregular scattering (Smilansky).
