# Multiprocess imaging of nuclear modifications on parton distributions in proton-nucleus collisions

> Nuclear modifications to collinear parton distribution functions are conventionally quantified by the ratios $ r^{\textrm{A}}_i(x,Q^2) = f^\textrm{A,proton}_i(x,Q^2) / f^\textrm{proton}_i(x,Q^2) $. For a given nucleus $A$, these ratios generally depend on the parton momentum fraction $ x $, the probing scale $ Q^{2} $, and the parton species $ i $. Determining these dependencies relies on a global analysis of diverse experimental data. However, in realistic observables, these dependencies are intricately intertwined, making their extraction challenging. In this paper, we propose a novel approach to effectively image the nuclear modification factors $ r^{\textrm{A}}_i(x,Q^2) $ at the observable level in proton-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Specifically, through a combined study of $ Z $-boson production, $ Z $+jet production, and $ Z+c $-jet production, we separately enhance signals arising from light-quark, gluon, and heavy-flavor (charm) distributions in nuclei. This enables us to effectively image the $ r^{\textrm{A}}_i(x,Q^2) $ for specific parton species. The feasibility of this method is validated through perturbative calculations at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling constant, employing three sets of nuclear PDF parametrizations: EPPS21, nCTEQ15, and TUJU19. Future measurements of these observables are expected to provide more efficient constraints on nuclear PDFs and yield new insights into the detailed partonic structures of nuclei.

# Multiprocess imaging of nuclear modifications on parton distributions in proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The authors propose that by combining reorganized differential cross sections of Z-boson production, Z+jet production, and Z+c-jet production in proton-lead collisions at the LHC, one can separately image the nuclear modification factors r^A_i(x,Q^2) for light quarks, gluons, and charm quarks at the observable level, provided that isospin effects are corrected and specific kinematic cuts are applied. The method is validated at next-to-leading order but the authors acknowledge that the coefficients used to construct the combined observables depend on the theoretical setting and that perfect flavor purification is not achievable.

## Method

The authors reorganize differential cross sections for Z-boson, Z+jet, and Z+c-jet production in pPb collisions by introducing a variable X_B that equals the nuclear parton momentum fraction at leading order, along with invariant mass and transverse momentum variables to control the probing scale. They then construct two combined observables: C_g(X_B) by subtracting the Z-boson cross section from a rescaled Z+jet cross section to isolate gluon signals, and C_charm(X_B) by subtracting C_g from a rescaled Z+c-jet cross section to isolate charm signals. The nuclear modification factors R_pA for these observables are computed at NLO using the MCFM program with CT18 proton PDFs and three nuclear PDF parametrizations (EPPS21, nCTEQ15, TUJU19).

## Key numbers

- The Z-boson invariant mass is constrained to 66 < M_V < 116 GeV, centered around the Z-mass peak
- For Z+jet production, kinematic cuts of 66 < M_VJ < 150 GeV and 40 < p_T,avg < 60 GeV are applied
- For Z+c-jet production, kinematic cuts of 66 < M_VJ < 150 GeV and 20 < p_T,avg < 60 GeV are applied
- The probing scale for Z-boson production is set to mu_0 = M_Z/2 = 45 GeV
- The probing scale for Z+jet and Z+c-jet production is set to mu_0 = (M_VJ + p_T,avg)/4
- Scale uncertainties are evaluated by varying mu_0 between mu_0/2 and 2*mu_0, with the ratio between scales in the two combined processes varying by a factor of 16

## Context

This work extends the kinematic scan method previously applied to dijet production in pA collisions by Shen et al. (2021), which provided an overall image of nuclear modification factors but combined contributions from different parton species simultaneously. The approach is analogous to how nuclear modifications to structure functions in deep-inelastic scattering, measured by the European Muon Collaboration (1981) and others, served as effective images of r^A_i for quark distributions. The proposed observables are expected to provide more direct constraints on flavor-separated nuclear modifications for global nPDF fits and help study non-perturbative nuclear dynamics, building on work by Alekhin et al. (2022), Cloet et al. (2012), and the nCTEQ collaboration (2023).
