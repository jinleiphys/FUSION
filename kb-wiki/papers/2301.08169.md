# Spanning the full range of neutron star properties within a microscopic description

> The high-density behavior of nuclear matter is analyzed within a relativistic mean-field description with non-linear meson interactions. To assess the model parameters and their output, a Bayesian inference technique is used. The Bayesian setup is limited only by a few nuclear saturation properties, the neutron star maximum mass larger than 2 M$_\odot$, and the low-density pure neutron matter equation of state (EOS) produced by an accurate N$^3$LO calculation in chiral effective field theory. Depending on the strength of the non-linear scalar vector field contribution, we have found three distinct classes of EOSs, each one correlated to different star properties distributions. If the non-linear vector field contribution is absent, the gravitational maximum mass and the sound velocity at high densities are the greatest. However, it also gives the smallest speed of sound at densities below three times saturation density. On the other hand, models with the strongest non-linear vector field contribution predict the largest radii and tidal deformabilities for 1.4 M$_\odot$ stars, together with the smallest mass for the onset of the nucleonic direct Urca processes and the smallest central baryonic densities for the maximum mass configuration. These models have the largest speed of sound below three times saturation density, but the smallest at high densities, in particular, above four times saturation density the speed of sound decreases approaching approximately $\sqrt{0.4}c$ at the center of the maximum mass star. On the contrary, a weak non-linear vector contribution gives a monotonically increasing speed of sound. A 2.75 M$_\odot$ NS maximum mass was obtained in the tail of the posterior with a weak non-linear vector field interaction. We found that pQCD favors models with a large non-linear vector field contribution or hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) description with nonlinear meson interactions, the strength of the nonlinear vector field contribution (the \(\omega^4\) term, parameter \(\xi\)) defines three distinct classes of equations of state, each producing qualitatively different neutron star properties; the authors hedge that a 2.75 \(M_\odot\) neutron star maximum mass was obtained only in the tail of the posterior distribution for models with a weak nonlinear vector field interaction, suggesting that the secondary object in GW190814 could be a neutron star but only under specific model conditions.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference on a seven-parameter RMF model with constant couplings and nonlinear meson self-interactions and cross-interactions (including \(\sigma^3\), \(\sigma^4\), \(\omega^4\), and \(\Lambda_\omega\) terms). The fit data comprised four nuclear saturation properties (saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy), the pure neutron matter pressure at three low densities from a chiral effective field theory N\(^3\)LO calculation, and the condition that the neutron star maximum mass exceeds 2 \(M_\odot\). The posterior was sampled using nested sampling (PyMultinest), and three separate calculations were performed by restricting the prior on the \(\xi\) parameter to different ranges: \(\xi \in [0, 0.004]\) (Set 1), \(\xi \in [0.004, 0.015]\) (Set 2), and \(\xi \in [0.015, 0.04]\) (Set 3).

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass: Set 1 median 2.073 \(M_\odot\) (90% CI: 2.013–2.306 \(M_\odot\)), Set 2 median 2.064 \(M_\odot\) (90% CI: 2.011–2.244 \(M_\odot\)), Set 3 median 2.048 \(M_\odot\) (90% CI: 2.010–2.162 \(M_\odot\))
- A 2.75 \(M_\odot\) neutron star maximum mass was obtained in the tail of the posterior for models with a weak nonlinear vector field interaction
- Radius of 1.4 \(M_\odot\) star: Set 1 median 12.34 km (90% CI: 12.03–12.89 km), Set 2 median 12.50 km (90% CI: 12.17–13.05 km), Set 3 median 12.87 km (90% CI: 12.42–13.30 km)
- Dimensionless tidal deformability for 1.4 \(M_\odot\) star: Set 1 median 399 (90% CI: 338–545), Set 2 median 439 (90% CI: 366–587), Set 3 median 535 (90% CI: 420–672)
- Effective tidal deformability for GW170817 with mass ratio \(q=1\): Set 1 median 474 (90% CI: 402–639), Set 2 median 519 (90% CI: 434–688), Set 3 median 631 (90% CI: 497–787)
- Central speed of sound squared at maximum mass: Set 1 median 0.63 \(c^2\) (90% CI: 0.58–0.70 \(c^2\)), Set 2 median 0.52 \(c^2\) (90% CI: 0.46–0.58 \(c^2\)), Set 3 median 0.43 \(c^2\) (90% CI: 0.39–0.45 \(c^2\))
- For Set 3, above four times saturation density the speed of sound decreases approaching approximately \(\sqrt{0.4}c\) at the center of the maximum mass star
- Central baryonic density at maximum mass: Set 1 median 1.079 fm\(^{-3}\) (90% CI: 0.914–1.138 fm\(^{-3}\)), Set 2 median 1.036 fm\(^{-3}\) (90% CI: 0.899–1.099 fm\(^{-3}\)), Set 3 median 0.972 fm\(^{-3}\) (90% CI: 0.883–1.035 fm\(^{-3}\))

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian study of RMF models with density-dependent couplings (Malik 2022) by instead using constant couplings with nonlinear mesonic interactions, and it goes beyond the study by Traversi (2020) by including pure neutron matter constraints from chiral effective field theory and higher-order \(\omega^4\) and \(\Lambda_\omega\) terms. The results enable systematic classification of RMF models by their high-density behavior and show that perturbative QCD favors models with a large nonlinear vector field contribution or hyperons, providing a framework for interpreting current and future NICER and LIGO-Virgo observations.
