# Nuclear medium effects in $ν/\barν$-A DIS

> Nuclear medium effects in the weak structure functions $F_2(x,Q^2)$ and $F_3(x,Q^2)$ have been studied for deep inelastic neutrino/antineutrino reactions in iron nucleus by taking into account Fermi motion, binding, pion and rho meson cloud contributions, target mass correction, shadowing and anti-shadowing corrections. The calculations have been performed in a local density approximation using relativistic nuclear spectral functions which include nucleon correlations. Using these structure functions we have obtained the ratio $R_{F2,F3}^A(x,Q^2)= \frac{2F_{2,3}^A(x,Q^2)}{AF_{2,3}^D(x,Q^2)}$, the differential scattering cross section $\frac{1}{E}\frac{d^2σ}{dxdy}$ and the total scattering cross section $σ$. The results of our numerical calculations in $^{56}Fe$ are compared with the experimental results of NuTeV and CDHSW collaborations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear medium effects in neutrino/antineutrino deep inelastic scattering on iron can be described by a model combining Fermi motion, binding, meson cloud contributions, target mass corrections, and shadowing/anti-shadowing, with the net effect being a 4-5% reduction in total cross section relative to the free nucleon case, though the agreement with experimental data is qualified by the choice of duality cuts and QCD evolution order.

## Method

The authors computed weak structure functions \(F_2\) and \(F_3\) for iron using a local density approximation with relativistic nuclear spectral functions that include nucleon correlations. They incorporated Fermi motion and binding effects as a base calculation, then added pion and rho meson cloud contributions, target mass corrections, and shadowing/anti-shadowing corrections using the prescription of Kulagin. The structure functions were used to obtain the ratio \(R_{F2,F3}^A(x,Q^2) = 2F_{2,3}^A / (A F_{2,3}^D)\), the differential cross section \(\frac{1}{E} d^2\sigma/dxdy\), and the total cross section \(\sigma\), with results compared to NuTeV and CDHSW experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The net effect of all nuclear medium corrections reduces the total cross section by 4-5% from the free nucleon case for neutrino energies around 2-5 GeV.
* When Fermi motion and binding effects alone are considered, the cross section reduces by about 8-9% from the free case.
* Adding shadowing/anti-shadowing and meson cloud contributions increases the cross section by about 4-6% from the base (Fermi motion + binding) results.
* With a duality cut of W=1.4 GeV and \(Q^2 > 0.8\) GeV\(^2\), the cross section reduces by about 28% at 2 GeV and 1% at 4 GeV compared to calculations without the duality cut and \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\).
* With a duality cut of W=1.7 GeV and \(Q^2 > 0.8\) GeV\(^2\), the cross section reduces by about 50% at 2 GeV and 6% at 4 GeV compared to the same baseline.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of electromagnetic nuclear structure functions (Athar et al.) to the weak interaction case for neutrino/antineutrino scattering. It provides a microscopic calculation that goes beyond phenomenological parameterizations such as those of Eskola et al., Hirai et al., and Schienbein et al., and it enables more reliable predictions for neutrino experiments using nuclear targets, particularly in the few-GeV energy region where quasi-elastic, inelastic, and deep inelastic processes all contribute.
