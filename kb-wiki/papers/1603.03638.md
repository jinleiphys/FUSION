# Pion-nucleon scattering in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta resonances

> We present the results of a third order calculation of the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in a chiral effective field theory with pions, nucleons and delta resonances as explicit degrees of freedom. We work in a manifestly Lorentz invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory using dimensional regularization and the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme. In the delta resonance sector, the on mass-shell renormalization is realized as a complex-mass scheme. By fitting the low-energy constants of the effective Lagrangian to the $S$- and $P$-partial waves a satisfactory description of the phase shifts from the analysis of the Roy-Steiner equations is obtained. We predict the phase shifts for the $D$ and $F$ waves and compare them with the results of the analysis of the George Washington University group. The threshold parameters are calculated both in the delta-less and delta-full cases. Based on the determined low-energy constants, we discuss the pion-nucleon sigma term. Additionally, in order to determine the strangeness content of the nucleon, we calculate the octet baryon masses in the presence of decuplet resonances up to next-to-next-to-leading order in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory. The octet baryon sigma terms are predicted as a byproduct of this calculation.

## Key claim

The paper presents a third-order (leading one-loop) calculation of the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude in a manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory that includes explicit Delta(1232) resonances, using the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme; the description of S- and P-wave phase shifts is satisfactory only after fitting low-energy constants to the Roy-Steiner equation analysis, while D- and F-waves are predictions.

## Method

The authors computed the pion-nucleon scattering amplitude up to order O(p³) using covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory with pions, nucleons, and explicit Delta(1232) resonances, employing dimensional regularization and the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme; for the Delta sector, the on-mass-shell renormalization was realized as a complex-mass scheme. The low-energy constants (LECs) of the effective Lagrangian were determined by fitting the calculated S- and P-wave phase shifts to those from the Roy-Steiner equation analysis, and the resulting amplitude was used to predict D- and F-wave phase shifts for comparison with the George Washington University group analysis.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon sigma term σπN was discussed based on the determined LECs, though no explicit numerical value is given in the truncated text.
- The octet baryon masses were calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory with decuplet resonances, yielding octet baryon sigma terms as a byproduct.
- The threshold parameters were calculated for both the delta-less and delta-full cases, but specific numerical values are not provided in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends previous covariant BChPT calculations of πN scattering at O(p³) by Alarcon (2012) and O(p⁴) by Chen (2012), which used the EOMS scheme but did not include explicit Delta resonances; the authors note that the convergence of the chiral expansion in those works was questionable without explicit Deltas. The inclusion of explicit Delta(1232) resonances follows the approach of Hemmert (1997) and aims to improve the description of the P33 partial wave and extend the applicability range, while the results are compared to the Roy-Steiner equation analysis by Hoferichter (2015) and the GWU group analysis (SAID).
