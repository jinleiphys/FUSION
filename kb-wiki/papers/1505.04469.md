# Origin of Low-Lying Enhanced E1 Strength in Rare-Earth Nuclei

> The experimental $E1$ strength distribution below 4 MeV in rare-earth nuclei suggests a local breaking of isospin symmetry. In addition to the octupole states, additional $1^-$ states with enhanced E1 strength have been observed in rare-earth nuclei by means of ($γ,γ'$) experiments. By reproducing the experimental results, the spdf interacting boson model calculations provide further evidence for the formation of an $α$ cluster in medium-mass nuclei and might provide a new understanding of the origin of low-lying E1 strength.

## Key claim

The low-lying enhanced E1 strength observed in rare-earth nuclei below 4 MeV, previously attributed to quadrupole-octupole coupled states or pygmy dipole resonance, can be explained by the spdf interacting boson model as evidence for an alpha-clustering mode (the p-boson mode) that provides an alternative interpretation without requiring complex mixing with giant dipole resonance components.

## Method

The authors performed systematic spdf interacting boson model (IBM) calculations for the Nd isotopes (A=142-150) and extended them to Sm, Gd, and Dy isotopes, using a Hamiltonian that treats positive- and negative-parity states separately. The p- and f-boson energies were fixed to reproduce the energies of the 1^-_1 and 3^-_1 states, while the full one-body E1 operator with smoothly varied parameters was used to reproduce reduced transition strengths. The calculations were compared to experimental data from (γ,γ') experiments and inelastic scattering measurements.

## Key numbers

- In ¹⁴²Nd, the 1^-_1 state at 3.4 MeV has B(E1)↑ = 17(4) × 10⁻³ e²fm² (experiment), with spdf IBM giving 19 × 10⁻³ e²fm²
- In ¹⁴⁴Nd, the 1^-_1 state at 2.19 MeV has B(E1)↑ = 6.1(10) × 10⁻³ e²fm² (experiment), with spdf IBM giving 6.7 × 10⁻³ e²fm²
- In ¹⁴⁶Nd, the 1^-_1 state at 1.38 MeV has B(E1)↑ = 5(2) × 10⁻³ e²fm² (experiment), with spdf IBM giving 5 × 10⁻³ e²fm²
- In ¹⁴⁸Nd, the 1^-_1 state at 1.02 MeV has B(E1)↑ = 14(6) × 10⁻³ e²fm² (experiment), with spdf IBM giving 9 × 10⁻³ e²fm²
- In ¹⁵⁰Nd, the 1^-_1 state at 0.85 MeV has B(E1)↑ = 15(7) × 10⁻³ e²fm² (experiment), with spdf IBM giving 19 × 10⁻³ e²fm²
- The centroid energy of additional 1^- states (excluding the 1^-_1) remains stable around 3.2-3.5 MeV across all Nd isotopes studied
- In ¹⁴⁴Nd, the 1^-_1 state wave function is dominated by the |4 0 1 1⟩ configuration at 55%, corresponding to a two-phonon 1^- state
- In ¹⁴²Nd, the |5 1 0 0⟩ configuration accounts for 28% of the 1^-_1 wave function, with n_p/n_f = 0.8

## Context

This work extends the long-standing proposal by Iachello (1985) that local isospin symmetry breaking in nuclei could manifest through enhanced E1 transitions from alpha clustering. It builds on systematic (γ,γ') studies of rare-earth nuclei by Pitz (1989, 1990), Friedrichs (1992, 1994), and Eckert (1997) that observed enhanced E1 transitions below 4 MeV in Nd, Sm, Gd, and Dy isotopes. The spdf IBM calculations provide an alternative to earlier microscopic interpretations by Zamfir (1990), Zilges (1992), and others that invoked 1p-1h admixtures from the giant dipole resonance tail. The results suggest that the p-boson mode, corresponding to alpha clustering at the nuclear surface, may be a general feature in medium-mass and heavy nuclei, and call for further experimental studies using (d,⁶Li), (⁶Li,d), (γ,γ), and (e,e') reactions to probe alpha structures.
