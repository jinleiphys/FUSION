# An effective field theory approach to quarkonium at small transverse momentum

> In this work we apply effective field theory (EFT) to observables in quarkonium production and decay that are sensitive to soft gluon radiation, in particular measurements that are sensitive to small transverse momentum. Within the EFT framework we study $χ_Q$ decay to light quarks followed by the fragmentation of those quarks to light hadrons. We derive a factorization theorem that involves transverse momentum distribution (TMD) fragmentation functions and new quarkonium TMD shape functions. We derive renormalization group equations, both in rapidity and virtuality, which are used to evolve the different terms in the factorization theorem to resum large logarithms. This theoretical framework will provide a systematic treatment of quarkonium production and decay processes in TMD sensitive measurements.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a systematic effective field theory (EFT) framework for quarkonium production and decay at small transverse momentum, where standard NRQCD factorization breaks down. The key advance is that promoting long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) into TMD quarkonium shape functions, combined with soft Wilson lines in vNRQCD operators, is necessary for proper gauge invariance and for resumming large logarithms via renormalization group evolution in both rapidity and virtuality.

## Method
The authors combine soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) with velocity NRQCD (vNRQCD) to describe the decay of χ_Q to light quarks followed by fragmentation to light hadrons. They perform tree-level matching of QCD diagrams with arbitrary numbers of soft gluon emissions onto NRQCD operators with soft Wilson lines, for both S-wave and P-wave Q\bar{Q} states. They derive a factorization theorem involving TMD fragmentation functions (TMDFFs) and new quarkonium TMD shape functions, and derive renormalization group equations in rapidity and virtuality to resum large logarithms at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) order.

## Key numbers
- The soft scale for bottomonium is about 1.4 GeV and for charmonium about 800 MeV, based on the scaling mν where ν is the heavy quark relative velocity.
- The heavy quark momentum components scale as k^μ ∼ m(ν^2, ν, ν, ν) in the quarkonium rest frame, with ν ≪ 1.
- The SCET power-counting parameter λ scales collinear momenta as p_c^μ ∼ Q(λ^2, 1, λ)_n and soft momenta as p_s^μ ∼ Q(λ, λ, λ)_n.
- The paper considers two hierarchies: ν ∼ λ (where SCET and vNRQCD merge into SCET_Q) and ν ≪ λ (where matching from SCET_Q onto vNRQCD is needed).

## Context
This work extends the NRQCD factorization approach of Caswell (1985) and Bodwin (1994) to the small-transverse-momentum regime where soft gluon radiation cannot be ignored, building on earlier studies of quarkonium photo/lepto-production in the endpoint region by Beneke (1997) and Fleming (2003, 2006). It introduces TMD quarkonium shape functions analogous to those in Ref. Echevarria (2019) for η_c production, and uses the manifestly gauge-invariant formulation of vNRQCD from Rothstein (2018). The framework enables systematic treatment of quarkonium production and decay in TMD-sensitive measurements, including processes proposed to study gluon TMDs in collider experiments as discussed by Accardi (2012).
