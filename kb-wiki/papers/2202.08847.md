# Jet broadening in dense inhomogeneous matter

> In this work, we study the jet momentum broadening in an inhomogeneous dense QCD medium. The transverse profile of this nuclear matter is described within a gradient expansion, and we focus on the leading gradient contributions. The leading parton is allowed to interact multiple times with the background through the soft gluon exchanges. We derive the associated final particle distribution using both the GLV opacity series and the BDMPS-Z formalism. We further discuss the modified factorization of the initial and final state effects and its consequences for phenomenological applications in the context of heavy-ion collisions and deep inelastic scattering. Finally, we present the broadening probability (describing the final state effects) in several limiting regimes, and give its numerical estimates for phenomenologically motivated sets of parameters.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading gradient corrections to jet momentum broadening in an inhomogeneous dense QCD medium, showing that the transverse variation of medium properties (density and Debye mass) generates a non-trivial angular dependence in the final parton distribution, but only at sub-eikonal order and under the assumption that the medium parameters vary slowly enough to justify a gradient expansion.

## Method

The authors model the dense QCD medium using stochastic color fields from static sources with a Gyulassy-Wang potential, where the source density and Debye mass vary slowly in the transverse plane. They compute the medium-modified single parton transverse momentum distribution by resumming the GLV opacity series to all orders and independently re-deriving the result within the BDMPS-Z formalism using a dressed in-medium propagator, keeping only the leading (linear) gradient terms in the expansion of the medium parameters.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Sadofyev (2021) which first included medium evolution effects in the GLV opacity expansion framework, by deriving the leading gradient corrections to all orders in opacity rather than only at first order. The gradient expansion approach is analogous to that used in hydrodynamic descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma and in holographic models for strongly interacting plasmas (Lekaveckas 2013, Rajagopal 2015, Sadofyev 2015). The result enables future phenomenological applications in jet quenching models (Caucal 2019, Casalderrey-Solana 2014, Sievert 2019, Putschke 2019, He 2015) for heavy-ion collisions and deep inelastic scattering experiments.
