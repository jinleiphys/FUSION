# Hyperon Beta-Decay and Axial Charges of the Lambda in view of Strongly Distorted Baryon Wave-Functions

> Within the collective coordinate approach to chiral soliton models we suggest that breaking of SU(3) flavor symmetry mainly resides in the baryon wave-functions while the charge operators maintain a symmetric structure. Sizable symmetry breaking in the wave-functions is required to reproduce the observed spacing in the spectrum of the (1/2)^+ baryons. The matrix elements of the flavor symmetric charge operators nevertheless yield g_A/g_V ratios for hyperon beta-decay which agree with the empirical data approximately as well as the successful F&D parameterization of the Cabibbo scheme. Demanding the strangeness component in the nucleon to vanish in the two flavor limit of the model, determines the structure of the singlet axial charge operator and yields the various quark flavor components of the axial charge of the Λ-hyperon. The suggested picture gains support from calculations in a realistic model using pion and vector meson degrees of freedom to build up the soliton.

## Key claim

Within the collective coordinate approach to chiral soliton models, the breaking of SU(3) flavor symmetry can reside mainly in the baryon wave-functions while the charge operators maintain a symmetric structure; this picture yields g_A/g_V ratios for hyperon beta-decay that agree with empirical data approximately as well as the successful F&D parameterization of the Cabibbo scheme, provided that sizable symmetry breaking in the wave-functions is required to reproduce the observed spacing in the spectrum of the (1/2)^+ baryons.

## Method

The author employs the collective coordinate quantization of chiral soliton models, where baryon states are obtained by quantizing large amplitude fluctuations (zero modes) of the soliton. The flavor symmetry breaking is treated by solving the exact eigenvalue problem for the collective Hamiltonian, which yields baryon wave-functions that are not pure octet states but contain admixtures from higher-dimensional SU(3) representations. The axial charge operators are parameterized in terms of three constants (c1, c2, c3) for the symmetric part, with c2 fixed from the proton spin puzzle, and c1 and c3 determined by fitting the nucleon axial charge and the Lambda-to-proton decay. A realistic vector meson soliton model is also used to compute the coefficients and verify the picture.

## Key numbers

- The empirical g_A/g_V ratios for hyperon beta-decays: n→p = 1.258, Λ→p = 0.718±0.015, Σ→n = 0.340±0.017, Ξ→Λ = 0.25±0.05, Ξ→Σ = 1.287±0.158, Σ→Λ (g_A only) = 0.61±0.02
- The F&D parameterization yields: n→p = 1.258, Λ→p = 0.725±0.009, Σ→n = 0.339±0.026, Ξ→Λ = 0.19±0.02, Ξ→Σ = 1.258, Σ→Λ = 0.65±0.01
- The effective symmetry breaking parameter ω² in the range 5 ≤ ω² ≤ 8 is required to obtain reasonable agreement with empirical mass differences for the 1/2⁺ and 3/2⁺ baryons
- For ω²=6.0 and ω²=8.0, the mass ratios (M_Λ-M_N):(M_Σ-M_Λ):(M_Ξ-M_Σ) are 1:0.69:0.70 and 1:0.61:0.77, respectively
- Setting ΔΣ_N=0.2 yields c1=-1.97, c2=0.12, and c3=-1.38 at ω²_fix=6.0
- In the realistic vector meson model, the spin content of the Λ for the best fit set: ΔU=ΔD=-0.155, ΔS=0.567, ΔΣ=0.256; for the nucleon: ΔU=0.603, ΔD=-0.279, ΔS=-0.034, ΔΣ=0.291
- The non-strange quark polarization in the Λ from the SU(3) symmetric analysis: ΔU_Λ=ΔD_Λ≈-0.20 with ΔS_Λ≈0.60

## Context

This work extends the collective coordinate approach to chiral soliton models initiated by Yabu and Ando (1988) and builds on earlier calculations by Park and collaborators (1990) that omitted subleading 1/N_C contributions to the axial charge operator. The paper addresses the proton spin problem studied by Brodsky, Jaffe, and others (1988, 1990) and the suggestion by Jaffe (1996) that polarized Lambdas could provide information about proton spin structure. The results enable further experimental study of Lambda polarization and fragmentation functions, as the predicted sizable non-strange quark polarizations (ΔU_Λ≈-0.16) provide motivation to measure these fragmentation functions.
