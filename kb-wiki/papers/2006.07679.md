# Nuclear-Elastic Scattering of $^9$Be+$^4$He using the Proximity Potential

> A nuclear-elastic $^9$Be+$^4$He reaction is investigated. A tangential literature overview of the proximity potential is presented, necessitated by an approximate method for calculating $l\text{-values}$. The model is surveyed intensively, from classical conception (liquid drop model) to physical implication (fusion barrier height), leading to a second-part calculation of the $^9$Be+$^4$He angular distribution. The results obtained are limited by the exclusion of phase variation, but provide enough accuracy to give order-of-magnitude estimates for nuclei interacting peripherally, in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The proximity potential, applied to the light-nuclei system \(^9\)Be+\(^4\)He, yields angular distributions that agree with experiment only at the order-of-magnitude level, because the calculation excludes phase variation and uses an approximate method for determining \(l\)-values; the results are limited to providing estimates for peripheral interactions.

## Method

The total interaction potential for the \(^9\)Be+\(^4\)He system was constructed as the sum of the Coulomb potential, the orbital angular momentum potential, and the nuclear proximity potential (Proximity 1977). Suitable \(l\)-values were estimated semi-classically from the impact parameter and then refined by locating the top of the effective potential barrier. The total differential cross section was computed as the squared modulus of the sum of Coulomb and nuclear scattering amplitudes, using spherical harmonics with \(m=0\) and a complex normalization constant \(N_r\), and compared with the experimental data of Taylor et al. (1965).

## Key numbers

* Impact parameter \(b \approx 4.58\) fm for the \(^9\)Be+\(^4\)He system, using a nucleon radius \(r_0 \sim 1.25\) fm.
* For projectile energy \(E = 9.5\) MeV, the dominant angular momentum is \(l \sim 5\); for \(E = 20\) MeV, \(l \sim 7\).
* The surface energy coefficient from the Myers-Swiatecki formula is \(\gamma_0 \approx 0.9517\) MeV/fm\(^2\), with surface asymmetry constant \(k_s \approx 1.7826\) and asymmetry parameter \(A_s \approx 0.0769\) for the combined system.
* The nuclear component of the differential cross section dominates the Coulomb component for scattering angles beyond 160 degrees at both 9.5 MeV and 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the proximity potential formalism, originally developed by Blocki et al. (1977) for heavy-ion surface interactions, to the light-nuclei system \(^9\)Be+\(^4\)He. It directly compares its angular distribution results with the experimental data and optical-model calculations of Taylor et al. (1965), who found that the optical model without a spin-coupling term gives imprecise back-angle predictions. The paper identifies the need for a full Hamiltonian treatment including phase variation and the imaginary component of the Coulomb amplitude, and suggests that the distorted-wave Born approximation would be a more rigorous next step.
