# The Anapole Moment of the Deuteron with the Argonne v18 Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction Model

> We calculate the deuteron anapole moment with the wave functions obtained from the Argonne $v18$ nucleon-nucleon interaction model. The anapole moment operators are considered at the leading order. To minimize the uncertainty due to a lack of current conservation, we calculate the matrix element of the anapole moment from the original definition. In virtue of accurate wave functions, we can obtain a more precise value of the deuteron anapole moment which contains less uncertainty than the former works. We obtain a result reduced by more than 25% in the magnitude of the deuteron anapole moment. The reduction of individual nuclear contributions is much more important however, varying from a factor 2 for the spin part to a factor 4 for the convection and associated two-body currents.

## Key claim

The deuteron anapole moment calculated with the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon interaction model is reduced by more than 25% in magnitude compared to previous zero-range-approximation results, but the reduction of individual nuclear contributions is much larger, varying from a factor of 2 for the spin part to a factor of 4 for the convection and associated two-body currents.

## Method

The authors calculated the deuteron anapole moment using wave functions obtained from the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon interaction model, with anapole moment operators considered at leading order. To minimize uncertainty from lack of current conservation, they computed the matrix element from the original definition of the anapole moment rather than the simplified form that assumes current conservation. The calculation included one-body spin and convection currents, two-body pair and pion exchange currents, and the nucleonic anapole moment, evaluated with parity-admixed deuteron wave functions containing a P-wave component generated by the parity-non-conserving one-pion exchange potential.

## Key numbers

- Total deuteron anapole moment: -0.909 e I h_piNN (in fm^2 units)
- Spin current contribution: -0.531 e I h_piNN
- Convection current contribution: +0.038 e I h_piNN
- Pair current contribution: -0.026 e I h_piNN
- Pion current contribution: +0.027 e I h_piNN
- Nucleonic anapole contribution: -0.417 e I h_piNN (including D-state probability P_D)
- Central part of spin contribution: -0.724 e I h_piNN
- Tensor part of spin contribution: +0.193 e I h_piNN
- Savage and Springer (1998) total result for comparison: -1.31 e I h_piNN
- Khriplovich and Korkin (2000) total result for comparison: -1.17 e I h_piNN

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the deuteron anapole moment by Savage and Springer (1998) and Khriplovich and Korkin (2000), who used zero-range-approximated wave functions and estimated uncertainties of about 20%. The authors show that using the realistic Argonne v18 potential yields a reduction of more than 25% in the total magnitude, with individual contributions suppressed by factors of 2 to 4. The result enables future work on gauge-invariant higher-order corrections, which the authors note could significantly modify the estimate, and provides a more precise benchmark for comparing the deuteron anapole moment with radiative corrections in atomic parity-violation experiments.
