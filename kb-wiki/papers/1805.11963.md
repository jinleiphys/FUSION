# GW170817: constraining the nuclear matter equation of state from the neutron star tidal deformability

> Constraints set on key parameters of the nuclear matter equation of state (EoS) by the values of the tidal deformability, inferred from GW170817, are examined by using a diverse set of relativistic and non-relativistic mean field models. These models are consistent with bulk properties of finite nuclei as well as with the observed lower bound on the maximum mass of neutron star $\sim 2 ~ {\rm M}_\odot$. The tidal deformability shows a strong correlation with specific linear combinations of the isoscalar and isovector nuclear matter parameters associated with the EoS. Such correlations suggest that a precise value of the tidal deformability can put tight bounds on several EoS parameters, in particular, on the slope of the incompressibility and the curvature of the symmetry energy. The tidal deformability obtained from the GW170817 and its UV/optical/infrared counterpart sets the radius of a canonical $1.4~ {\rm M}_{\odot}$ neutron star to be $11.82\leqslant R_{1.4}\leqslant13.72$ km.

## Key claim

The tidal deformability inferred from GW170817 can constrain nuclear matter equation of state parameters, particularly the slope of the incompressibility and the curvature of the symmetry energy, but only when specific linear combinations of these parameters are considered rather than individual parameters alone.

## Method

The authors employed 18 relativistic mean field (RMF) models and 24 non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock (SHF) models, all consistent with bulk properties of finite nuclei and the observed lower bound on neutron star maximum mass of approximately 2 solar masses. They computed the tidal deformability and Love number for each model by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the tidal Love number differential equation, then quantified correlations between nuclear matter saturation parameters and neutron star properties using Pearson correlation coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The radius of a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star is constrained to 11.82 ≤ R₁.₄ ≤ 13.72 km from GW170817 and its electromagnetic counterpart
- The combined dimensionless tidal deformability for the low-spin prior is bounded as 400 < Λ̃ ≤ 800 (or Λ̃ ≤ 1000 from a reanalysis)
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the Love number k₂ ranges from 0.07 to 0.11 across the models
- The tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ shows correlation coefficients of 0.92 with M₀ + 13.68 L₀ and 0.95 with M₀ + 4.83 K_sym,0
- The lower bound Λ̃ > 400 sets Λ₁.₄ > 344; the upper bounds Λ̃ < 800 and Λ̃ < 1000 set Λ₁.₄ < 687 and Λ₁.₄ < 859 respectively
- The slope of incompressibility M₀ is constrained to values above 1500 MeV (or 1800 MeV depending on the L₀ constraints used)

## Context

This work extends earlier correlation studies by Fattoyev (2013) and Fattoyev (2017) that used only a few selected relativistic mean field models, by employing a more diverse set of 42 models including both relativistic and non-relativistic frameworks. The authors note that Alam (2015) and Fortin (2016) found that correlations between neutron star properties and nuclear matter parameters are significantly affected when diverse model sets are used. The results enable tighter constraints on the slope of incompressibility M₀ and the curvature of symmetry energy K_sym,0 from gravitational wave observations, and provide a framework for using future precise tidal deformability measurements to bound nuclear matter parameters.
