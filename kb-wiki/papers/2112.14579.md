# S-wave pion-pion scattering lengths from nucleon-meson fluctuations

> We present calculations of the $S$-wave isospin-zero and isospin-two pion-pion scattering lengths within a nucleon-meson model with parity doubling. Both scattering lengths are computed in various approximations, ranging from a mean-field (MF) calculation towards the inclusion of loop corrections by means of the functional renormalization group (FRG). The bosonic part of the investigated nucleon-meson model is formulated in terms of stereographic projections as a "natural" set of coordinates on the respective vacuum manifold. We thereby elucidate subtleties concerning the truncation of the effective action w.r.t. higher-derivative pion interactions and the "successful" computation of the scattering lengths. As the main result, we find simultaneous agreement for the isospin-zero and isospin-two scattering lengths with experimental data within the $\mathrm{LPA}^{\prime}$-truncation of the FRG, together with chiral symmetry breaking (roughly) occurring at the characteristic scale of $4πf_π$. The isoscalar $σ$-mass is dynamically generated by the FRG integration of momentum modes, and is a prediction of the model. It ends being of the order of $500\ \mathrm{MeV}$, i.e., much lower than the value ($> 1\ \mathrm{GeV}$) found in MF or one-loop treatment of this or related models. Finally, the convergence of the corresponding low-energy expansion of the quantum effective action in terms of pion momenta is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find simultaneous agreement for both the isospin-zero and isospin-two S-wave pion-pion scattering lengths with experimental data, but only within the LPA'-truncation of the functional renormalization group (FRG), not in mean-field or one-loop approximations; this success is conditional on chiral symmetry breaking occurring roughly at the characteristic scale of 4πfπ and on the dynamically generated sigma mass ending up near 500 MeV rather than above 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors compute the S-wave isospin-zero and isospin-two pion-pion scattering lengths within a nucleon-meson model with parity doubling, using the functional renormalization group (FRG) to integrate quantum fluctuations. The bosonic part of the model is formulated using stereographic projections as coordinates on the S3 vacuum manifold, and the effective action is truncated at the LPA' level (including scale-dependent wave-function renormalizations and a Taylor-expanded potential up to order Nα = 6). The scattering lengths are extracted from the IR limit of the renormalization-group-invariant vertex functions, and the results are compared with mean-field and one-loop calculations as well as with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Isospin-zero scattering length: a0^0 = 0.2198 ± 0.0126 (experimental value from the paper, used as reference)
- Isospin-two scattering length: a0^2 = -0.0445 ± 0.0023 (experimental value from the paper, used as reference)
- Weinberg limits (tree-level ChPT): a0^0 = 7 Mπ^2 / (32π fπ^2) ≈ 0.1565; a0^2 = -Mπ^2 / (16π fπ^2) ≈ -0.0447
- Dynamically generated sigma mass from FRG: Mσ ≈ 500 MeV, much lower than the value > 1 GeV found in mean-field or one-loop treatments
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λχ ≈ 4π fπ ≈ 1.2 GeV (used as input parameter)
- Pion decay constant: fπ = (130.2 ± 1.2)/√2 MeV (convention used in the work)
- Pion mass: Mπ ≈ 138 MeV (averaged over π0 and π±)
- Chiral-invariant mass parameter m0: chosen as 800 MeV (from prior FRG studies of the same model)

## Context

This work extends prior linear sigma model calculations of pion-pion scattering lengths, which often failed to simultaneously reproduce a0^0 and a0^2 (e.g., Parganlija 2010, Divotgey 2016, Lakaschus 2018) due to favoring a heavy sigma mass above 1 GeV. The FRG approach dynamically generates a lighter sigma mass (∼500 MeV), enabling agreement with experiment, and identifies the sigma with the f0(500) resonance rather than the f0(1370). The use of stereographic coordinates follows earlier work by Eser (2018, 2019, 2020) and Divotgey (2019, 2020) on quark-meson models. The results enable future studies of low-energy hadronic observables within linear sigma models using nonperturbative renormalization group methods, and they clarify the role of fermionic fluctuations and parity doubling in reproducing chiral low-energy constants.
