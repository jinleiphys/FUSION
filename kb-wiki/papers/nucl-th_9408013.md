# Electromagnetic Form Factor of the Nucleon in the Time-like Region

> We explore the possibility to verify vector meson dominance for the nucleon by measuring the half-off-shell form factor in the vector-meson mass region. Cross sections for the process $γp \rightarrow p + e^+e^-$ be presented.

# Electromagnetic Form Factor of the Nucleon in the Time-like Region

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the half-off-shell electromagnetic form factor of the nucleon in the time-like region via the purely electromagnetic reaction γp → p e⁺e⁻ could provide a more conclusive test of vector meson dominance for the nucleon than analytic continuation from space-like data, provided the lepton detection angle is larger than 90 degrees where Bethe-Heitler background is negligible.

## Method

The authors extend a resonance-plus-Born amplitude description of real Compton scattering, previously fitted to data up to 1.2 GeV by Wada et al., to the case of virtual time-like photons by replacing the real photon vertex with a dilepton current and including longitudinal polarization contributions. They multiply the half-off-shell form factor F(q²,W²) by a vector meson dominance factor F_VMD(q²) = m_V²/(m_V² − q²) with a decay width to avoid the singularity, and compute five-fold differential cross sections for symmetric kinematics where the Bethe-Heitler contribution is suppressed at large opening angles.

## Key numbers

- The time-like region accessible via p p̅ annihilation opens only for q² > 4m_p² ≈ 3.6 GeV², leaving the vector-meson mass region near m_v ≈ 0.78 GeV inaccessible in on-shell processes.
- The dipole fit to space-like form factors uses m_v² ≈ 0.71 GeV².
- The analysis includes all nucleon resonances up to a mass of about 2 GeV, with parameters extracted from Compton scattering data up to 1.2 GeV.
- For angles larger than 90 degrees, the resonance contribution is larger than the nucleonic contribution by roughly a factor of two.
- For dilepton invariant masses above 400 MeV, the Bethe-Heitler contribution dominates the spectrum at angles less than 90 degrees when F_VMD = 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract the time-like nucleon form factor from dilepton production in p+p and p+d reactions by the DLS group at the BEVALAC (Roche), as well as from p+π → p+e⁺e⁻ experiments, which suffered from ambiguities due to strong-interaction vertices. The authors build on the Compton scattering amplitude decomposition of Ishii and Wada, and on the general half-off-shell vertex formalism of Tiemeijer and Tjon and Naus and Koch. The approach enables experimental access to the time-like form factor in the vector-meson mass region (0.78 GeV) that is otherwise unphysical for on-shell processes, potentially testing whether the nucleon form factor exhibits the resonance structure predicted by vector meson dominance, as already established for the pion.
