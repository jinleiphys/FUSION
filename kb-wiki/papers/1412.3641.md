# Numerical solution of the Boltzmann equation for trapped Fermi gases with in-medium effects

> Using the test-particle method, we solve numerically the Boltzmann equation for an ultra-cold gas of trapped fermions with realistic particle number and trap geometry in the normal phase. We include a mean-field potential and in-medium modifications of the cross-section obtained within a T matrix formalism. After some tests showing the reliability of our procedure, we apply the method to realistic cases of practical interest, namely the anisotropic expansion of the cloud and the radial quadrupole mode oscillation. Our results are in good agreement with experimental data. Although the in-medium effects significantly increase the collision rate, we find that they have only a moderate effect on the anisotropic expansion and on frequency and damping rate of the quadrupole mode.

## Key claim

The authors find that although in-medium effects (mean-field potential and in-medium cross-section) significantly increase the collision rate in trapped Fermi gases, they have only a moderate effect on the anisotropic expansion and on the frequency and damping rate of the radial quadrupole mode, with the mean field and in-medium cross-section effects practically canceling each other in expansion simulations when using the same initial temperature.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation numerically using the test-particle method for a two-component ultracold Fermi gas in the normal phase, including a mean-field potential and in-medium modifications of the cross-section obtained within a T-matrix formalism. They simulate the anisotropic expansion of the cloud after trap switch-off and the radial quadrupole mode oscillation, comparing results with experimental data from the Duke group (Cao 2011, Elliott 2014) and the Innsbruck group (Riedl 2008).

## Key numbers

- For the quadrupole mode experiment (Riedl 2008): trap frequencies of omega_r/2pi = 1800 Hz and omega_z/2pi = 32 Hz, with N = 6 x 10^5 atoms
- For the expansion experiment (Elliott 2014): trap frequencies omega_x/2pi = 2210 Hz, omega_y/2pi = 830 Hz, omega_z/2pi = 64.3 Hz, with N = 2.5 x 10^5 atoms and normalized energy tilde{E}/E_F = 1.46 (corresponding to T/T_F = 0.51)
- For the expansion experiments (Cao 2011): trap frequencies omega_x/2pi = 5283 Hz, omega_y/2pi = 2052 or 5052 Hz, omega_z/2pi = 182.7 Hz, with N ranging from 4 x 10^5 to 6 x 10^5 and T/T_F ranging from 0.79 to 1.54
- The Gaussian fit error for the column density during expansion does not exceed 2.5% of the central density
- Test-particle numbers used: 5 x 10^4 for quadrupole mode simulations and 1 x 10^5 for expansion simulations

## Context

This work extends the test-particle method developed by Lepers (2010) to include in-medium effects (mean-field potential and in-medium cross-section) for realistic trap geometries and particle numbers. It builds on previous T-matrix calculations by Chiacchiera (2009) and Bruun and Smith (2005) for in-medium correlations, and enables direct comparison with experimental expansion data from the Duke group (Cao 2011, Elliott 2014) and quadrupole mode data from the Innsbruck group (Riedl 2008), providing a Boltzmann-equation framework that can describe both hydrodynamic and collisionless regimes simultaneously.
