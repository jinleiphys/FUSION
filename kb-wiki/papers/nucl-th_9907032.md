# Residual correlation in two-proton interferometry from lambda-proton strong interactions

> We investigate the residual effect of lambda-proton strong interactions in pp correlations with one proton from lambda decays. It is found that the residual correlation is about 10% of the lambda-proton correlation strength, and has a broad distribution centered around q~40 MeV/c. The residual correlation cannot explain the observed structure on the tail of the recently measured pp correlation function in central Pb+Pb collisions by NA49 at the SPS.

## Key claim

The residual correlation in two-proton (pp) interferometry arising from lambda-proton strong interactions, where one proton comes from lambda decays, is about 10% of the lambda-proton correlation strength and has a broad distribution centered around q approximately 40 MeV/c; however, this residual correlation cannot explain the observed structure on the tail of the pp correlation function measured by NA49 in central Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS.

## Method

The author derived an analytic expression for the residual pp correlation function from lambda-proton strong interactions by assuming a Gaussian form for the lambda-proton correlation function, motivated by prior Urbana-type potential calculations. The lambda-proton correlation function was calculated for a Gaussian source size of Rg = 3.85 fm using thermal momentum distributions at temperature T = 3 MeV, and the resulting parameters (lambda = 0.62, k0 = 21 MeV/c) were used to compute the residual correlation. The effect on the measured pp correlation function was estimated by combining 56% direct proton pairs with 44% pairs containing one lambda decay proton, and the result was compared to NA49 data.

## Key numbers

- The residual correlation amplitude is approximately 0.05, representing a factor of about 10 reduction from the lambda-proton correlation strength
- The residual correlation peaks at q approximately 40 MeV/c
- The lambda decay momentum Q is approximately 100 MeV/c with a branching ratio of 64%
- The NA49 experiment extracted a Gaussian source size Rg = 3.85 +/- 0.15 (stat.) fm from pp correlations in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV
- 44% of proton pairs in the NA49 acceptance contained at least one weak decay proton
- The Gaussian fit to the lambda-proton correlation function yielded lambda = 0.62 and k0 = 21 MeV/c
- The observed bump structure in the NA49 pp correlation function was at q approximately 70 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the calculation of lambda-proton correlation functions using Urbana-type potentials by Wang and Pratt (1999) to investigate their residual effect on two-proton interferometry. The results contradict the possibility that the structure observed at q approximately 70 MeV/c in the NA49 pp correlation function arises from lambda decay proton contamination. The residual correlation most strongly affects the q approximately 40 MeV/c region of the pp correlation function, which has been shown by Pratt and Tsang (1987) to be sensitive to the elongation and lifetime of the proton source, potentially complicating the extraction of such information at high energies where lambda production is significant.
