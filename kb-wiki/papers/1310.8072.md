# Dynamics of strangeness and collective flows in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies

> Strangeness (K$^{0,+}$, $Λ$ and $Σ^{-,0,+}$) production in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies has been investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model. The kaon (anti-kaon)-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon potentials in dense nuclear matter are implemented in the model, which are computed from the chiral perturbation approach and the relativistic mean-field model, respectively. It is found that the in-medium potentials change the structure of transverse flow, and also affect the rapidity distributions and the inclusive spectra for strangeness production. The local temperature of the fire ball extracted from the kaon spectra of inclusive invariant cross sections is influenced by the kaon-nucleon potential. The stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy and the kaon-nucleon potential by distinguishing isospin effect play a significant role on the ratio of K$^{0}$/K$^{+}$, in particular at the subthreshold energies. The ratio of $Σ^{-}/Σ^{+}$ depends on the high-density symmetry energy, in which the $Σ$-nucleon potential has a neglectable contribution on the isospin ratio.

## Key claim

The in-medium kaon-nucleon (KN) and hyperon-nucleon (HN) potentials, implemented within the LQMD transport model, significantly modify the transverse flow, rapidity distributions, and inclusive spectra of strangeness (K⁰, K⁺, Λ, Σ) produced in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies; the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy and the isospin-dependent KN potential together influence the K⁰/K⁺ ratio, particularly at subthreshold energies, while the Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratio depends on the high-density symmetry energy with negligible contribution from the Σ-nucleon potential.

## Method

The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model was used to simulate heavy-ion collisions (⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni at 1.93A GeV and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at 1.5A GeV). The KN potential was computed from the chiral perturbation approach, and the hyperon-nucleon potential from the relativistic mean-field model (NL3 parameter set). Strangeness production channels included baryon-baryon (BB→BYK), pion-baryon (Bπ→YK), and associated production (BB→BBK¯K), with in-medium threshold corrections from effective masses. The symmetry energy stiffness was varied (soft, linear, hard, supersoft) to study its effect on isospin ratios.

## Key numbers

- The KN potential reduces the total kaon multiplicity by about 10% in ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions.
- The antikaon-nucleon potential enhances antikaon yields by about 200% due to attractive interaction.
- Inverse slope parameters (temperatures) without optical potentials: K⁺ 102±10 MeV, Λ 122±6 MeV, Σ⁰ 124±12 MeV, K⁻ 100±8 MeV.
- With KN potential and Lorentz force, kaon temperature rises to 138±8 MeV and 132±6 MeV, respectively; antikaon temperature drops to 94±10 MeV and 82±8 MeV.
- The hyperon-nucleon potential at saturation density is -32 MeV.
- The kaon-nucleon potential at saturation density is +27.8 MeV (repulsive); the antikaon-nucleon potential is -100.3 MeV (attractive).
- The effective mass ratio m*_K/m_K = 1.056 and m*_K̄/m_K̄ = 0.797 at normal baryon density in isospin symmetric matter.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is 31.5 MeV for all stiffness cases.
- The compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter is K = 230 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strangeness as a probe of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) and symmetry energy, building on the LQMD model developed by Feng (2010, 2011). It directly compares with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration (Becker 1997, Herrmann 1999, Ritman 1995) and KaoS collaboration (Menzel 2000) for kaon and hyperon production. The results enable future experimental constraints on the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy through precise measurements of subthreshold K⁰/K⁺ and Σ⁻/Σ⁺ ratios in neutron-rich collisions, and provide a framework for testing in-medium strangeness potentials against upcoming data.
