# Covariant Spin Kinetic Theory I: Collisionless Limit

> We develop a covariant kinetic theory for massive fermions in curved spacetime and external electromagnetic field based on quantum field theory. We derive four coupled semi-classical kinetic equations accurate at $O(\hbar)$, which describe the transports of particle number and spin degrees of freedom. The relation with the chiral kinetic theory is discussed. As an application, we study the spin polarization in the presence of finite Riemann curvature and electromagnetic field in both local and global equilibrium states.

## Key claim

The paper develops a covariant spin kinetic theory for massive fermions in curved spacetime and external electromagnetic fields, accurate to O(ℏ), but the derivation is restricted to the collisionless limit and does not include collision terms or higher-order quantum corrections.

## Method

The authors start from the quantum field theory definition of the covariant Wigner function for Dirac fermions in curved spacetime with a U(1) gauge field, then perform a semiclassical expansion in ℏ and decompose the Wigner function into Clifford algebra components. They derive a system of coupled equations for the Clifford coefficients, identify the vector current density Vμ and axial current density Aμ as independent variables, and obtain explicit solutions for the Wigner function components in terms of distribution functions f and fA and a spin quantization vector θμ.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic equations are derived accurate to O(ℏ), with Riemann curvature entering only at O(ℏ²) or higher.
- The chiral anomaly term A is given by A = -(ℏ/16π²) Fμν F̃μν f(0), where f(0) is the distribution function at zero momentum.
- The spin tensor for massive fermions is defined as ΣSμν = (1/2m) εμνρσ θρ pσ, with θμ satisfying θμθμ = -1 and pμθμ = 0.
- The vector current density solution includes a term ℏ εμνρσ pν Dρ (θσ fA) / (2m) at O(ℏ).

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory for massless fermions (Son 2012, Stephanov 2012, Gao 2012) to massive fermions, and differs from earlier spin kinetic theories (Gao 2019, Weickgenannt 2019, Hattori 2019, Wang 2019) by including curved spacetime geometry and electromagnetic fields simultaneously, and by showing that the frame-choosing vector can be eliminated for massive fermions. The formalism enables the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions beyond the simple thermal vorticity interpretation, and is applicable to astrophysical systems and condensed matter systems with thermal gradients or deformation.
