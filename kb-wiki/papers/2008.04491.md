# The possibility of the secondary object in GW190814 as a neutron star

> A compact object was observed with a mass $2.50-2.67~M_\odot$ by LIGO Scientific and Virgo collaborations (LVC) in GW190814, which provides a great challenge to the investigations into the supranuclear matter. To study this object, properties of neutron star are systematically calculated within the latest density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) parameterizations, which are determined by the ground state properties of spherical nuclei. The maximum masses of neutron star calculated by DD-MEX and DD-LZ1 sets can be around $2.55~M_\odot$ with quite stiff equations of state generated by their strong repulsive contributions from vector potentials at high densities. Their maximum speeds of sound $c_s/c$ are smaller than $\sqrt{0.8}$ at the center of neutron star and the dimensionless tidal deformabilities at $1.4~M_\odot$ are less than $800$. Furthermore, the radii of $1.4 ~M_\odot$ also satisfy the constraint from the observation of mass-radius simultaneous measurements (NICER). Therefore, we conclude that one cannot exclude the possibility of the secondary object in GW190814 as a neutron star composed of hadron matter from DDRMF models.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that one cannot exclude the possibility that the secondary object in GW190814 (mass 2.50–2.67 solar masses) is a neutron star composed of hadron matter, based on calculations using density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DDRMF) parameterizations. This conclusion is conditional on the specific DDRMF models DD-MEX and DD-LZ1, which produce maximum neutron star masses around 2.55 solar masses while still satisfying constraints from NICER observations and tidal deformability limits.

## Method

The authors systematically calculated neutron star properties using eight DDRMF parameterizations (DD-LZ1, DD2, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DD-MEX, DDV, DDVT, DDVTD) that were fitted to ground state properties of spherical nuclei. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations and computed tidal deformabilities and speeds of sound for neutron star matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- The secondary object in GW190814 has a mass of 2.50–2.67 solar masses, as reported by LIGO Scientific and Virgo collaborations
- Maximum masses of neutron star calculated by DD-MEX and DD-LZ1 sets can be around 2.55 solar masses
- Maximum speeds of sound at the center of neutron star are smaller than sqrt(0.8) times the speed of light
- Dimensionless tidal deformabilities at 1.4 solar masses are less than 800
- Radii of 1.4 solar mass neutron stars satisfy constraints from NICER mass-radius simultaneous measurements
- The slope of symmetry energy L from different DDRMF models ranges from approximately 40 to 70 MeV, satisfying the constraint L = 59.57 plus or minus 10.06 MeV from Zhang (2020)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of massive neutron stars using covariant density functional theory, following observations of PSR J1614-2230 (1.928 solar masses), PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses), and PSR J0740+6620 (2.14 solar masses). The authors compare their results to earlier DDRMF parameterizations studied by Sun et al. (2008) that generated neutron stars around 2.33–2.48 solar masses, and to the BigApple parameter set proposed by Fattoyev et al. (2020) which produced a 2.6 solar mass neutron star. The work enables further investigation into whether the GW190814 secondary object could be a neutron star rather than a black hole, and provides constraints on the equation of state of supranuclear matter at high densities.
