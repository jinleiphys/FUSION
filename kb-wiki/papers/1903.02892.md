# Bound states of relativistic nature

> Bethe-Salpeter equation, for massless exchange and large fine structure constant $α>π/4$, in addition to the Balmer series, provides another (abnormal) series of energy levels which are not given by the Schrödinger equation. So strong field can be created by a point-like charge $Z>107$. The nuclei with this charge, though available, they are far from to be point-like that weakens the field. Therefore, the abnormal states of this origin hardly exist. We analyze the more realistic case of exchange by a massive particle when the large value of coupling constant is typical for the strong interaction. It turns out that this interaction still generates a series of abnormal relativistic states. The properties of these solutions are studied. Their existence in nature seems possible.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation with massive ladder exchange, using coupling constants typical for strong interactions, still generates a series of abnormal relativistic bound states that are absent in the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation, making their existence in nature seem possible, unlike the massless-exchange case where the required coupling constant is too large to be realized with realistic point-like charges.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for two spinless particles interacting via massive scalar exchange (Yukawa-type kernel) using the Nakanishi integral representation for the BS amplitude. They derived and numerically solved the canonical integral equation for the Nakanishi weight function \(g(\gamma,z)\) using spline techniques, and studied the behavior of the coupling constant \(\alpha\) as a function of the speed of light \(c\) to distinguish normal from abnormal states by their non-relativistic limit.

## Key numbers

- For massless exchange, abnormal states exist only for fine structure constant \(\alpha > \pi/4 \approx 0.78\); this corresponds to a point-like charge \(Z > 107\) (bohrium).
- For massless exchange, the binding energies of abnormal states in the small-binding limit are \(B_{nk} \approx B_k = m \exp\left(-2\pi^{3/2}k / \sqrt{\alpha - \pi/4}\right)\).
- For massive exchange with parameters \(m=1\), \(\mu=0.15\), \(B=0.1\), the normal state required \(\alpha(c=1) \approx 1.45\) and the abnormal state required \(\alpha(c=1) \approx 10\).
- In the non-relativistic limit (\(c \to \infty\)) for the normal state with \(m=1\), \(\mu=0.15\), \(B=0.1\), the coupling constant tends to \(\alpha \approx 0.9\), matching the Yukawa potential value.

## Context

This work extends the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) of massless scalar exchange, where abnormal states were first discovered but required unrealistically large coupling constants for point-like charges. The authors show that abnormal states survive when the exchanged particle has mass, using coupling strengths typical of strong interactions, unlike the QED case where the required \(Z > 107\) is impractical due to finite nuclear size. This enables future searches for abnormal states in strongly interacting systems that are difficult to describe as ordinary bound states, and suggests calculating electromagnetic form factors and Fock-state decompositions to further characterize these states.
