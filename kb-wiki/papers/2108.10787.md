# Construction of energy density functional for arbitrary spin polarization using functional renormalization group

> We show an application of the functional-renormalization-group aided density functional theory to the homogeneous electron gas with arbitrary spin polarization, which gives the energy density functional in the local spin density approximation. The correlation energy per particle is calculated at arbitrary Wigner-Seitz radius $ r_{\rm s} $ and spin polarization $ ζ$. In the high-density region, our result shows good agreement with Monte Carlo (MC) data. The agreement with MC data is better in the case of small spin polarization, while the discrepancy increases as the spin polarization increases. The magnetic properties given by our numerical results are also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors extend the functional-renormalization-group aided density functional theory (FRG-DFT) to the homogeneous electron gas with arbitrary spin polarization, constructing the correlation energy per particle at any Wigner-Seitz radius and spin polarization within the local spin density approximation; the agreement with Monte Carlo data is good at high density and small spin polarization, but the discrepancy grows as spin polarization increases, leading to the absence of the magnetic phase transition predicted by quantum Monte Carlo calculations.

## Method

The authors formulate the FRG-DFT flow equations for the effective action and density correlation functions at arbitrary order in general spatial dimensions, using a vertex expansion truncated at second order. They apply this formalism to the homogeneous electron gas in three and two dimensions, solving the flow equations for the two-point density correlation function to obtain the correlation energy per particle as a function of Wigner-Seitz radius \( r_s \) and spin polarization \( \zeta \). The exchange part is treated analytically using the known Dirac exchange energy, and the correlation part is obtained by integrating the flow equation from the non-interacting system (\(\lambda=0\)) to the fully interacting system (\(\lambda=1\)).

## Key numbers

- Correlation energy per particle \( \epsilon_c(r_s, \zeta) \) is calculated at 65536 points in the Wigner-Seitz radii range \( r_s \in [10^{-6} \, \mathrm{a.u.}, 100 \, \mathrm{a.u.}) \) for the spin-unpolarized case, extended here to arbitrary spin polarization.
- The exchange energy per particle in three dimensions is given analytically as \( \epsilon_x = -\frac{3}{4} \left( \frac{9}{4\pi^2} \right)^{1/3} \frac{1}{r_s} \left[ 1 + (2^{1/3} - 1) f_x(\zeta) \right] \), where \( f_x(\zeta) = [(1+\zeta)^{4/3} + (1-\zeta)^{4/3} - 2] / (2^{4/3} - 2) \).
- In two dimensions, the exchange coefficient is \( a_2 = 4\sqrt{2}/(3\pi) \), and in three dimensions \( a_3 = 3[3/(16\pi)]^{2/3} \).
- The Coulomb interaction in momentum space is \( \tilde{U}(\bm{p}) = 2\pi/|\bm{p}| \) for \( D=2 \) and \( \tilde{U}(\bm{p}) = 4\pi/|\bm{p}|^2 \) for \( D=3 \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FRG-DFT calculations for the spin-unpolarized homogeneous electron gas (Yokota, 2021) to arbitrary spin polarization, enabling the construction of energy density functionals in the local spin density approximation for magnetic systems. The approach reproduces the exact high-density behavior given by the Gell-Mann–Brueckner resummation (Gell-Mann and Brueckner, 1957) and provides results at many more density points than available from quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Loo, 2016), which are limited to few values due to numerical cost. The method enables systematic improvement beyond the common approximation \( f_c(r_s, \zeta) \approx f_x(\zeta) \) used in standard LSDA parameterizations (von Barth and Hedin, 1972), and can be applied to nuclear matter systems with multiple components such as protons, neutrons, and hyperons.
