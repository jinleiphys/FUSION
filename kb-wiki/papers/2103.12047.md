# Perturbative QCD Analysis of Near Threshold Heavy Quarkonium Photoproduction at Large Momentum Transfer

> We apply perturbative QCD to investigate the near threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction at large momentum transfer. From an explicit calculation, we show that the conventional power counting method will be modified and the three quark Fock state with nonzero orbital angular momentum dominates the near threshold production. It carries a power behavior of $1/(-t)^5$ for the differential cross section. We further comment on the impact of our results on the interpretation of the experiment measurement in terms of the gluonic gravitational form factors of the proton.

## Key claim

The conventional power counting method for near-threshold heavy quarkonium photoproduction at large momentum transfer must be modified: the leading-twist (three-quark Fock state with zero orbital angular momentum) contribution is suppressed by a factor of \((1-\chi)\) near threshold, and the dominant contribution comes from the three-quark Fock state with nonzero orbital angular momentum (twist-4), which carries a \(1/(-t)^5\) power behavior for the differential cross section.

## Method

The authors apply perturbative QCD factorization, combining the nucleon form factor approach (using distribution amplitudes of the proton) with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for the heavy quarkonium. They compute the scattering amplitude for \(\gamma N \to J/\psi N\) in the heavy quark mass limit, expanding around the threshold parameter \(\chi = (M_V^2 + 2 M_p M_V)/(W_{\gamma p}^2 - M_p^2)\) which approaches 1 at threshold. They classify contributions by the orbital angular momentum of the three-quark Fock state of the nucleon and evaluate the power behavior in \((-t)\) for each twist level, including all permutations of gluon attachments.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for the leading twist-3 (zero orbital angular momentum) contribution scales as \(1/(-t)^4\) but is suppressed at threshold by a factor \((1-\chi)\).
- The dominant twist-4 (nonzero orbital angular momentum) contribution scales as \(1/(-t)^5\) for the differential cross section, with an additional factor \(\widetilde m_t^2 = M_p^2/(-t)\).
- For \(J/\psi\) production, the momentum transfer near threshold is \(-t \sim 2\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\); for \(\Upsilon\) production, \(-t \sim 10\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- Fitting the GlueX data (Ali 2019) with the twist-4 term yields \(\Lambda^2 = 1.41 \pm 0.20\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(N_0 = 51 \pm 22\ \mathrm{nb \cdot GeV^8}\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.} = 0.48\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD analysis of exclusive processes (Lepage & Brodsky 1980, Brodsky et al. 2000) to the near-threshold regime of heavy quarkonium photoproduction, which was originally proposed by Kharzeev (1995, 1998) as a probe of the trace anomaly contribution to the proton mass. The authors show that the conventional assumption of \(1/(-t)^4\) power behavior (used in phenomenological studies by Frankfurt 2002, Ali 2019, Kharzeev 2021) is suppressed at threshold, and that the three-gluon exchange diagrams (considered by Brodsky 2000) vanish due to C-parity conservation. The results challenge the direct interpretation of near-threshold photoproduction in terms of gluonic gravitational form factors (Tong 2021), but the authors note that a connection may still exist through a generalized parton distribution (GPD) formalism (Hatta 2021, Guo 2021). The analysis enables future precision tests at JLab-12GeV, the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and the EIC in China.
