# CPT Violation and the Nature of Neutrinos

> In order to accommodate the neutrino oscillation signals from the solar, atmospheric, and LSND data, a sterile fourth neutrino is generally invoked, though the fits to the data are becoming more and more constrained. However, it has recently been shown that the data can be explained with only three neutrinos, if one invokes CPT violation to allow different masses and mixing angles for neutrinos and antineutrinos. We explore the nature of neutrinos in such CPT-violating scenarios. Majorana neutrino masses are allowed, but in general, there are no longer Majorana neutrinos in the conventional sense. However, CPT-violating models still have interesting consequences for neutrinoless double beta decay. Compared to the usual case, while the larger mass scale (from LSND) may appear, a greater degree of suppression can also occur.

## Key claim

The solar, atmospheric, and LSND neutrino oscillation data can be explained with only three neutrinos if CPT violation is invoked to allow different masses and mixing angles for neutrinos and antineutrinos, but in such CPT-violating scenarios Majorana neutrino mass terms are still allowed even though the mass eigenstates are no longer Majorana particles in the conventional sense.

## Method

The authors construct a two-state mass matrix for a single neutrino flavor with a Dirac mass term μ, a CPT-violating term Δ (which allows different masses for ν and ν̄), and a Majorana mass term y (which mixes ν and ν̄). They diagonalize this matrix to obtain mass eigenstates and then examine the CPT transformation properties of these eigenstates. They extend the analysis to the leptonic amplitude for neutrinoless double beta decay, showing that the amplitude depends on the product of the neutrino and antineutrino mixing matrices U and Ū, which are no longer equal when CPT is violated.

## Key numbers

- The three mass-squared differences required by solar, atmospheric, and LSND data are δm² ≈ 10⁻⁵ eV², 10⁻³ eV², and 1 eV², respectively.
- In the CPT-violating scenario with LSND-scale masses, the effective mass for neutrinoless double beta decay satisfies 0 < mββ < 0.1 mLSND, where mLSND is approximately 1 eV.
- In the CPT-conserving case with solar mixing, the effective mass range is m₀ cos(2θ⊙) < mββ < m₀, where cos(2θ⊙) ≈ 0.4 for the best-fit solar mixing angle of about 30°.

## Context

This paper extends the CPT-violating neutrino framework proposed by Murayama (2000) and others (cpt1, cpt2) to examine the Majorana versus Dirac nature of neutrinos and the consequences for neutrinoless double beta decay. The work contrasts with the standard approach of introducing a sterile fourth neutrino to accommodate the three independent mass-squared differences from solar, atmospheric, and LSND data. The results show that neutrinoless double beta decay experiments can distinguish between CPT-conserving and CPT-violating scenarios, and that the observation of this decay would imply lepton number violation and Majorana mass terms, but not necessarily that neutrinos are self-conjugate Majorana particles.
