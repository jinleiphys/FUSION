# Two-photon decay of fully-charmed tetraquarks from light-by-light scattering at the LHC

> The LHC newly-discovered resonant structures around 7 GeV, such as the $X(6900)$, could be responsible for the observed excess in light-by-light scattering between 5 and 10 GeV. We show that the ATLAS data for light-by-light scattering may indeed be explained by such a state with the $γγ$ branching ratio of order of $10^{-4}$. This is much larger than the value inferred by the vector-meson dominance, but agrees quite well with the tetraquark expectation for the nature of this state. Further light-by-light scattering data in this region, obtained during the ongoing Run-3 and future Run-4 of the LHC, are required to pin down these states in $γγ$ channel.

## Key claim

The ATLAS light-by-light scattering excess between 5 and 10 GeV can be explained by the fully-charmed tetraquark candidate X(6900) with a diphoton branching ratio of order 10⁻⁴, which is much larger than vector-meson-dominance estimates but consistent with quark-model expectations for a tetraquark state.

## Method

The authors extended the Monte Carlo generator SuperChic v3.05, which previously included only the quark-loop contribution to light-by-light scattering, by adding the X(6900) resonance along with known bottomonium states (ηb(1S), ηb(2S), χb0(1P), χb0(2P)). They fitted the resonance parameters to the unfolded diphoton invariant mass spectrum from ATLAS ultra-peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, assuming the total width is dominated by the di-J/ψ decay, and explored both scalar and pseudoscalar quantum-number assignments.

## Key numbers

* Two-photon width of X(6900) from fit with interference: Γ = 67⁺¹⁵₋₁₉ keV
* Two-photon width of X(6900) from fit without interference: Γ = 45⁺¹¹₋₁₄ keV
* Branching ratio B[X(6900)→γγ] with interference: (4.0⁺⁰·⁹₋₁·₁) × 10⁻⁴
* Branching ratio B[X(6900)→γγ] without interference: (5.6⁺¹·³₋₁·⁶) × 10⁻⁴
* Vector-meson-dominance estimate for scalar X(6900): B_VMD = (2.77 ± 0.36) × 10⁻⁶
* Vector-meson-dominance estimate for pseudoscalar X(6900): B_VMD = (6.07 ± 0.80) × 10⁻⁶
* Quark-model estimate for two-photon width: approximately 10 keV, using |ψ(0)|² ≈ 2.85/4π GeV³ from Debastiani (2019)

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the ATLAS light-by-light scattering data (ATLAS Collaboration, 2020) by connecting it to the LHCb discovery of X(6900) in the di-J/ψ channel (LHCb Collaboration, 2020), which has been confirmed by ATLAS (2022) and CMS (2022). The extracted diphoton width exceeds vector-meson-dominance predictions (Barger, 1975; Redlich, 2000) by roughly two orders of magnitude but agrees with quark-model expectations for fully-charmed tetraquarks (Esposito, 2021; Debastiani, 2019). Future Run-3 and Run-4 LHC data with improved resolution in both the di-J/ψ and γγ channels are needed to resolve the apparent conflict between VMD and quark-model predictions and to enable quantum-number assignment.
