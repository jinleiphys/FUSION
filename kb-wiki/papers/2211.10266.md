# Two-photon decay of fully-charmed tetraquarks from light-by-light scattering at the LHC

> The LHC newly-discovered resonant structures around 7 GeV, such as the $X(6900)$, could be responsible for the observed excess in light-by-light scattering between 5 and 10 GeV. We show that the ATLAS data for light-by-light scattering may indeed be explained by such a state with the $ОіОі$ branching ratio of order of $10^{-4}$. This is much larger than the value inferred by the vector-meson dominance, but agrees quite well with the tetraquark expectation for the nature of this state. Further light-by-light scattering data in this region, obtained during the ongoing Run-3 and future Run-4 of the LHC, are required to pin down these states in $ОіОі$ channel.

The authors extended the Monte Carlo generator SuperChic v3.05, which previously included only the quark-loop contribution to light-by-light scattering, by adding the X(6900) resonance along with known bottomonium states (О·b(1S), О·b(2S), ПҮb0(1P), ПҮb0(2P)). ## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the ATLAS light-by-light scattering data (ATLAS Collaboration, 2020) by connecting it to the LHCb discovery of X(6900) in the di-J/ПҲ channel (LHCb Collaboration, 2020), which has been confirmed by ATLAS (2022) and CMS (2022). The extracted diphoton width exceeds vector-meson-dominance predictions (Barger, 1975; Redlich, 2000) by roughly two orders of magnitude but agrees with quark-model expectations for fully-charmed tetraquarks (Esposito, 2021; Debastiani, 2019). Future Run-3 and Run-4 LHC data with improved resolution in both the di-J/ПҲ and ОіОі channels are needed to resolve the apparent conflict between VMD and quark-model predictions and to enable quantum-number assignment.
