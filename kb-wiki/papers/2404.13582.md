# Light and hyper nuclei formation at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} =$ 3 GeV Au+Au collisions using Wigner coalescence approach

> The production of light nuclei and hyper-nuclei in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at high baryon density, is crucial for understanding the dynamical evolution of the collision system and exploring the internal state of nuclear matter of compacted stellar object. Despite being a topic of ongoing debate, an improved theoretical understanding is necessary. In this work, production of light nuclei ($d$, $t$, $^{3}$He, $^{4}$He) and hyper-nuclei ($^{3}_Λ$H, $^{4}_Λ$H) was investigated using the JAM microscopic transport model combined with an afterburner coalescence process at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} =$ 3 GeV Au+Au collisions. The formation of a specific nucleus during the coalescence process is determined by its Wigner function. The comparison of the calculations for $\mathrm{p_T}$ spectra, average $\mathrm{p_T}$, and rapidity distributions to the measurements from the STAR experiment was performed. We investigated the dynamic information carried by light nuclei and determined the averaged spatial distance $\langle ΔR \rangle$ and momentum difference $\langle ΔP \rangle$ of constituent nucleons ($Λ$) for each nucleus species.

## Key claim

The JAM transport model with mean-field mode, combined with a Wigner-function-based afterburner coalescence at a fixed evolution time of 20 fm/c, can qualitatively reproduce STAR measurements of pT spectra, average pT, and rapidity distributions for light nuclei and hyper-nuclei in 3 GeV Au+Au collisions, except that the 4He yield is underestimated by a factor of 6, possibly due to its large binding energy allowing earlier formation.

## Method

The phase-space distributions of nucleons and Λ hyperons were generated using the JAM microscopic transport model in mean-field mode at √sNN = 3 GeV Au+Au collisions, with positions and momenta recorded at a fixed evolution time of 20 fm/c. An afterburner coalescence process then formed light nuclei (d, t, 3He, 4He) and hyper-nuclei (3ΛH, 4ΛH) by evaluating the Wigner function overlap for all possible 2-body, 3-body, and 4-body combinations of constituent baryons boosted to their respective rest frames. The Wigner functions used harmonic-oscillator wave functions with rms radii taken from measured values, and for hyper-nuclei the coalescence was treated as a 2-body process between a Λ and a light-nucleus core.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron rms radius: 1.96 fm; binding energy: 2.225 MeV; spin factor: 3/4
- Triton rms radius: 1.59 fm; binding energy: 8.482 MeV; spin factor: 1/4
- 3He rms radius: 1.76 fm; binding energy: 7.718 MeV; spin factor: 1/4
- 4He rms radius: 1.45 fm; binding energy: 28.3 MeV; spin factor: 1/16
- 3ΛH rms radius: 4.9 fm; total binding energy: 2.354 MeV (core 2.225 MeV + Λ separation 0.13 MeV); spin factor: 1/4
- 4ΛH rms radius: 2.0 fm; total binding energy: 10.601 MeV (core 8.482 MeV + Λ separation 2.12 MeV); spin factor: 1/4
- 4He yield underestimated by a factor of 6 compared to STAR data
- Coalescence time fixed at 20 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the coalescence approach using Wigner functions, previously applied by Mattiello (1996), Sun (2015), and Zhang (2018), to the specific energy of √sNN = 3 GeV Au+Au collisions where high baryon density is created. The calculations are compared directly to recent STAR measurements from 2023. The extracted average spatial distances ⟨ΔR⟩ and momentum differences ⟨ΔP⟩ for each nucleus species provide new constraints on the intrinsic properties of light and hyper-nuclei formation, and the finding that 4He may form earlier due to its large binding energy (28.3 MeV) confirms a suggestion first made by Sun (2015). The work enables future studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions through hyper-nucleus production in dense nuclear matter.
