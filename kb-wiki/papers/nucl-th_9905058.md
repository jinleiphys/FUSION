# Symmetry conserving non-perturbative s-wave renormalization of the pion in hot and baryon dense medium

> A non-perturbative s-wave renormalization of the pion in a hot and baryon rich medium is presented. This approach proceeds via a mapping of the canonical pion into the axial Noether's charge. The mapping was made dynamical in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov random phase approximation (HFB-RPA). It is shown that this approach, while order mixing, is still symmetry conserving both in the baryon free and baryon rich sectors, at zero as well as finite temperature. The systematic character of this approach is emphasized and it is particularly argued that it may constitute an interesting alternative for the non-perturbative assessment of the nuclear matter saturation properties.

## Key claim

The authors present a non-perturbative s-wave renormalization of the pion in hot and baryon-dense medium that, while mixing orders in the coupling, preserves chiral symmetry (the lowest Ward identity) at zero and finite temperature in both baryon-free and baryon-rich sectors. The approach is argued to be a systematic alternative for assessing nuclear matter saturation properties, but the paper itself does not compute saturation properties numerically.

## Method

The authors start from the SU(2)×SU(2) linear sigma model with nucleons and perform a Bogoliubov transformation to a squeezed vacuum, then solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field gap equations variationally. On top of this HFB basis, they build a Random Phase Approximation (RPA) excitation operator that mimics the structure of the axial Noether charge, and solve the RPA secular equations via Rowe's equation-of-motion method in the quasi-boson approximation to obtain the renormalized pion spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The HFB quasi-pion mass at zero momentum is given by \( \mathcal{E}_{\pi}^2(0) = \mu^2 + \lambda^2 [5I_{\pi} + I_{\sigma} + \langle\hat{\sigma}\rangle^2] \), where \(I_{\pi}\), \(I_{\sigma}\) are one-loop tadpole integrals of the dressed quasi-pion and quasi-sigma propagators, and \(\langle\hat{\sigma}\rangle\) is the sigma condensate.
- The HFB quasi-sigma mass at zero momentum is \( \mathcal{E}_{\sigma}^2(0) = \mu^2 + 3\lambda^2 [I_{\pi} + I_{\sigma} + \langle\hat{\sigma}\rangle^2] \).
- The HFB quasi-fermion mass at zero momentum is \( \mathcal{E}_F(0) = -g\langle\hat{\sigma}\rangle \).
- The RPA eigenvalue equation for the renormalized pion frequency is \( \omega_{\nu}^2 = \mathcal{E}_{\pi}^2(0) + g^2 \Sigma_{F\bar{F}}(\omega_{\nu}^2) + \frac{4\lambda^4 \langle\hat{\sigma}\rangle^2 \Sigma_{\pi\sigma}(\omega_{\nu}^2)}{1 - 2\lambda^2 \Sigma_{\pi\sigma}(\omega_{\nu}^2)} \), where \(\Sigma_{F\bar{F}}\) and \(\Sigma_{\pi\sigma}\) are RPA bubble integrals defined in the text.
- In the chiral limit (\(c \to 0\)), the RPA spectrum contains a Goldstone mode (\(\omega_{\nu}=0\)) as shown by the identity \( \omega_{\nu}^2 = \frac{c}{\langle\hat{\sigma}\rangle} + g^2[\Sigma_{F\bar{F}}(\omega_{\nu}^2)-\Sigma_{F\bar{F}}(0)] + \frac{2\lambda^2[\mathcal{E}_{\sigma}^2(0)-\mathcal{E}_{\pi}^2(0)][\Sigma_{\pi\sigma}(\omega_{\nu}^2)-\Sigma_{\pi\sigma}(0)]}{1-2\lambda^2\Sigma_{\pi\sigma}(\omega_{\nu}^2)} \).

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-conserving dynamical mapping (SCDM) approach introduced by Aouissat (1998) from the baryon-free sector to finite temperature and finite baryon chemical potential within the SU(2)×SU(2) linear sigma model. It contrasts with standard coupling-constant perturbation theory and the 1/N expansion (which preserves Ward identities but treats the sigma mass perturbatively), and with classical mean-field approaches that violate the Goldstone theorem at the HFB level. The method enables a non-perturbative treatment of the pion that preserves chiral symmetry, and the authors state that higher approximations beyond HFB-RPA will be reported in a forthcoming paper (Aouissat, 1999).
