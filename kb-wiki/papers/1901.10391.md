# Examination of an isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution for the isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions

> Within a transport model using as the input nucleon momentum profiles from a parameterized isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution with a high momentum tail induced by short-range correlations, we employ the $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon to examine on one hand effects of the short-range correlations on the pion and flow observables in probing the nuclear symmetry energy, and on the other hand how reliable are this isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution as well as the corresponding parameter settings. Besides significant effects of the short-range correlations on the pion and flow observables are observed, we also find that the theoretical simulations of $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions with this momentum distribution using two sets of parameters extracted from the experimental analysis and the self-consistent Green's function prediction, respectively, can reproduce the neutron elliptic flows of the FOPI-LAND experiment and the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratios of the FOPI experiment under the symmetry energy setting in a certain range. Therefore, we conclude that this parameterized isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution is reliable for the isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, correspondingly, two sets of parameters extracted from both the experimental analysis and the self-consistent Green's function prediction can not be ruled out according to the available experimental information at present.

## Key claim

The parameterized isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution with a high-momentum tail induced by short-range correlations is reliable for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, but two sets of parameters (one from experimental analysis and one from self-consistent Green's function predictions) cannot be ruled out based on current experimental data, as both can reproduce the FOPI-LAND neutron elliptic flows and FOPI pion ratios under certain symmetry energy settings.

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central and semicentral 197Au + 197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. They initialized the colliding nuclei using a parameterized isospin-dependent single-nucleon momentum distribution with a high-momentum tail, employing two parameter sets (HMT-exp. and HMT-SCGF) that differ in the fraction of nucleons in the high-momentum tail. The nuclear interaction included a momentum-dependent mean field with parameters refitted to incorporate short-range correlation effects through the kinetic symmetry energy, along with pion potentials, Delta isovector potentials, and relativistic Lienard-Wiechert electromagnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The HMT-SCGF parameter set yields approximately 12.6% of total nucleons in the high-momentum tail in finite 197Au at saturation density rho0, with individual fractions of approximately 15% for protons and 11% for neutrons.
- The HMT-exp. parameter set yields approximately 25.2% of total nucleons in the high-momentum tail in finite 197Au at rho0, with individual fractions of approximately 30% for protons and 22% for neutrons.
- The self-consistent Green's function approach using the Av18 interaction predicts 11-13% of nucleons in the high-momentum tail for symmetric nuclear matter at rho0, and 4-5% for pure neutron matter at rho0.
- Experimental analyses at JLab suggest approximately 20% of nucleons are in the high-momentum tail in nuclei from 12C to 208Pb, and approximately 25% in symmetric nuclear matter at rho0.
- The nucleon emission rate before about 27 fm/c shows larger values in short-range correlation scenarios, but does not exceed 2.5% before or at 15 fm/c.
- Theoretical simulations using the HMT-SCGF parameter can reproduce the FOPI pion multiplicities with symmetry energy parameter x in the range from 0 to 2, and the pi-/pi+ ratio with x no less than 1.

## Context

This work extends studies of short-range correlations in nuclear matter by Cai and Li (2015) and Li et al. (2018), who proposed the parameterized isospin-dependent momentum distribution, and tests it against experimental data from the FOPI collaboration (pion ratios) and FOPI-LAND collaboration (neutron elliptic flows). The results contradict calculations without short-range correlations, which underestimate both pion multiplicities, and show that the HMT-exp. parameter set overestimates pion multiplicities. The findings enable future studies to use this momentum distribution in transport simulations while noting that the symmetry energy extraction from pion and flow observables depends on which parameter set is adopted, with the HMT-SCGF parameter favoring a mildly soft symmetry energy at high densities.
