# Application of Gaussian expansion method to nuclear mean-field calculations with deformation

> We extensively develop a method of implementing mean-field calculations for deformed nuclei, using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM). This GEM algorithm has the following advantages: (i) it can efficiently describe the energy-dependent asymptotics of the wave functions at large $r$, (ii) it is applicable to various effective interactions including those with finite ranges, and (iii) the basis parameters are insensitive to nuclide, thereby many nuclei in wide mass range can be handled by a single set of bases. Superposing the spherical GEM bases with feasible truncation for the orbital angular momentum of the single-particle bases, we obtain deformed single-particle wave-functions to reasonable precision. We apply the new algorithm to the Hartree-Fock and the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations of Mg nuclei with the Gogny interaction, by which neck structure of a deformed neutron halo is suggested for $^{40}$Mg.

## Key claim

The Gaussian expansion method (GEM) can be extended to deformed mean-field calculations by superposing spherical GEM bases with truncation on orbital angular momentum, and this approach suggests a neck structure of a deformed neutron halo in \(^{40}\)Mg, though the authors hedge that this feature is only "suggested" within the mean-field approximation and depends on the specific occupation of the \((\pi,m)=(-,1/2)\) orbit.

## Method

The authors represent deformed single-particle wave functions as superpositions of spherical Gaussian bases with complex range parameters, truncating the sum over orbital angular momentum \(\ell\) at \(\ell_{\text{max}}=7\). They apply this basis to Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations for Mg isotopes using the Gogny D1 and D1S interactions, storing the two-body interaction matrix elements (requiring 4.3 GB for \(\ell\leq7\)) and iteratively solving the mean-field equations until convergence.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^{40}\)Mg in HF with D1S: \(-260.7\) MeV, with rms matter radius \(\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle}=3.63\) fm and intrinsic quadrupole moment \(Q_0=227\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(^{24}\)Mg with D1 interaction: GEM gives \(-195.73\) MeV (GEM\(_1\), one-body c.m. subtracted) vs. \(-193.90\) MeV from HO bases and \(-188.33\) MeV from AMD; intrinsic quadrupole moment \(Q_0=114.39\) fm\(^2\).
- For \(^{34}\)Mg with D1S in HFB: GEM gives \(-254.02\) MeV vs. \(-252.82\) MeV from HO bases (experimental: \(-256.59\) MeV).
- The last two neutrons in \(^{40}\)Mg have single-particle energy \(\varepsilon_F=-1.14\) MeV and occupy a \((\pi,m)=(-,1/2)\) orbit with 68% \(\ell=1\) component; the ratio \(|\bar{c}^{(n_F)}_{1,+1/2}|^2/(|\bar{c}^{(n_F)}_{1,+1/2}|^2+|\bar{c}^{(n_F)}_{1,-1/2}|^2)\approx 0.95\).
- Basis parameters: \(\nu_0=(2.40\ \text{fm})^{-2}\), \(b=1.25\), with 12 bases per \((\ell,j)\) (6 real-range, 6 complex-range).

## Context

This work extends the spherical GEM mean-field method developed by Nakada (2002, 2006) to axially deformed nuclei, enabling treatment of finite-range interactions and energy-dependent asymptotics without nucleus-dependent basis optimization. The results improve upon prior HO-basis HF calculations by Flocard et al. (1978) for Mg isotopes, giving lower binding energies especially for neutron-rich nuclei (e.g., \(\sim10\) MeV gain for \(^{38}\)Mg), and are comparable to or better than HO-basis HFB results by Peru et al. (2000) for \(^{30-34}\)Mg. The method enables systematic studies of deformed drip-line nuclei, with the specific prediction of a neck-structured neutron halo in \(^{40}\)Mg that could be tested by future experiments or more advanced many-body calculations.
