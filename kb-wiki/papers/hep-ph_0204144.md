# Particle-Antiparticle Metamorphosis of Massive Majorana Neutrinos and Gauginos

> Recent results on neutrinoless double beta decay, as reported by Klapdor-Kleingrothaus et al., take us for the first time into the realm of Majorana spacetime structure. However, this structure has either been treated as an afterthought to the Dirac construct; or, when it has been attended to in its own right, its physical and mathematical content was never fully unearthed. In this Letter,we undertake to remedy the existing situation. We present a detailed formalism required for the description of the non-trivial spacetime structure underlying the "nu-nubar" metamorphosis - where "nu" generically represents a massive Majorana neutrino, or a massive gaugino.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the conventional Majorana construct, which grafts a Fock-space constraint onto Dirac spinors, is a "hybrid" that fails to capture the full spacetime structure of massive Majorana particles; it presents an *ab initio* formulation where the spinorial structure itself is of Majorana type, revealing that the particle/antiparticle interpretation of self/antiself conjugate spinors fails and that the $\nu \rightleftharpoons \overline{\nu}$ metamorphosis arises from the underlying spacetime structure rather than from a simple Fock-space identification.

## Method

The author constructs Majorana spinors as self/antiself conjugate eigenspinors of the charge conjugation operator in the $(1/2,0)\oplus(0,1/2)$ representation space, using Wigner's time reversal operator and boost generators. The wave equations for these spinors are derived and shown to be irreducible to the Dirac equation, and the Feynman-Dyson propagator for the Majorana field is computed in momentum space to explicitly exhibit the helicity-flipping mechanism of the $\nu \rightleftharpoons \overline{\nu}$ metamorphosis.

## Key numbers

- The bi-orthonormal norms of the self-conjugate Majorana spinors are $\overline{\lambda}^S_\uparrow(\p) \lambda^S_\downarrow (\p) = + 2 i m$ and $\overline{\lambda}^S_\downarrow(\p) \lambda^S_\uparrow (\p) = - 2 i m$, with diagonal norms vanishing.
- The orthonormality relation between $\lambda$ and $\rho$ spinors gives $\overline{\lambda}^S_\uparrow(\p) \rho^A_\uparrow(\p) = -2 m = \overline{\lambda}^A_\uparrow(\p) \rho^S_\uparrow (\p)$.
- The determinant of the wave equation matrix for $\lambda(\p)$ spinors is $\left(3 m^2 + 4E m-\p^2 + E^2 \right)^2 \left(m^2+\p^2-E^2\right)^2$, requiring the causal dispersion relation $m^2+\p^2-E^2=0$.
- In the massless limit, the $\lambda_\uparrow^S(\p)$ and $\lambda_\uparrow^A(\p)$ spinors "identically vanish," while $\lambda_\downarrow^S(\p)$ and $\lambda_\downarrow^A(\p)$ do not; similarly, $\rho^S_\downarrow(\p)$ and $\rho^A_\downarrow(\p)$ vanish in the massless limit.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for Majorana particles beyond the conventional hybrid approach used in neutrino physics, which treats the Majorana condition as a Fock-space afterthought to the Dirac construct (Majorana 1937; Furry 1939). The formalism is motivated by the Klapdor-Kleingrothaus et al. (2001) evidence for neutrinoless double beta decay, and it provides a spacetime picture of the $\nu \rightleftharpoons \overline{\nu}$ metamorphosis that is applicable to both massive Majorana neutrinos and massive gauginos in supersymmetric theories. The results enable a deeper understanding of parity violation in beta decay without invoking explicit projectors, and they open the door to treating Majorana spacetime structure on equal footing with the Dirac construct in left-right symmetric theories and supersymmetry.
