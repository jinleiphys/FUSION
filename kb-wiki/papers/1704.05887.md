# Simulating chiral magnetic effect and anomalous transport phenomena in the pre-equilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions

> We present a first principles approach to study the Chiral Magnetic Effect during the pre-equilibrium stage of a heavy-ion collision. We discuss the dynamics of the Chiral Magnetic Effect and Chiral Magnetic Wave based on real-time lattice simulations with dynamical (Wilson and Overlap) fermions simultaneously coupled to color and electromagnetic fields. While for light quarks we observe a dissipation-less transport of charges as in anomalous hydrodynamics, we demonstrate that for heavier quarks the effects of explicit chiral symmetry breaking lead to a significant reduction of the associated currents.

## Key claim

The authors present a first-principles real-time lattice simulation of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) during the pre-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions, showing that while light quarks exhibit dissipation-less charge transport as in anomalous hydrodynamics, explicit chiral symmetry breaking from finite quark mass significantly reduces the associated currents for heavier quarks.

## Method

The authors use classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations with dynamical Wilson and Overlap fermions coupled simultaneously to color and electromagnetic fields. They solve the operator Dirac equation numerically in the background of a single sphaleron (topological) transition, decomposing the fermion field operator into time-dependent complex wavefunctions. The simulations track the production of axial charge during the sphaleron transition and the subsequent transport of axial and vector charge densities via the CME and Chiral Separation Effect (CSE).

## Key numbers

* For light quarks with mass m * r_sph = 1.9 x 10^-2 and strong magnetic field q B * r_sph^-2 = 7.0, profiles of axial and vector densities are shown at times t/t_sph = 0.6, 0.9, 1.1, 1.3.
* The typical inverse size of the sphaleron is r_sph^-1 = 200-500 MeV.
* Anomalous transport essentially ceases to exist for m * r_sph >= 0.75, meaning when the quark mass becomes comparable to other relevant scales.
* Vector charge separation rises linearly with magnetic field for small fields and saturates to unity at large magnetic fields.

## Context

This work extends first-principles lattice techniques for studying anomalous transport phenomena during the non-equilibrium stage of heavy-ion collisions, building on the Color Glass Condensate framework (McLerran, 1993) and classical-statistical simulations (Berges, 2013; Gelis, 2013; Kurkela, 2012). The results can interface with macroscopic descriptions such as chiral kinetic theory (Son, 2012; Stephanov, 2012; Mueller, 2017) and anomalous hydrodynamics (Son, 2009; Hirono, 2014; Jiang, 2016), providing initial conditions for pre-equilibrium axial and vector charges and currents. The findings also enable future studies of the electromagnetic response of the dynamically created medium and the lifetime of the magnetic field by including fermionic back-coupling.
