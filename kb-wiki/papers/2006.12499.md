# Temperature dependence of $η/s$ of strongly interacting matter: effects of the equation of state and the parametric form of $(η/s)(T)$

> We investigate the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$ using a piecewise linear parametrization. To determine the optimal values of the parameters and the associated uncertainties, we perform a global Bayesian model-to-data comparison on Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $2.76$ TeV and $5.02$ TeV, using a 2+1D hydrodynamical model with the EKRT initial state. We provide three new parametrizations of the equation of state (EoS) based on contemporary lattice results and hadron resonance gas, and use them and the widely used $s95p$ parametrization to explore the uncertainty in the analysis due to the choice of the equation of state. We found that $η/s$ is most constrained in the temperature range $T\approx 150$--$220$ MeV, where, for all EoSs, $0.08 < η/s < 0.23$ when taking into account the 90% credible intervals. In this temperature range the EoS parametrization has only a small $\approx 10\%$ effect on the favored $η/s$ value, which is less than the $\approx 30\%$ uncertainty of the analysis using a single EoS parametrization. Our parametrization of $(η/s)(T)$ leads to a slightly larger minimum value of $η/s$ than the previously used parametrizations. When we constrain our parametrization to mimic the previously used parametrizations, our favored value is reduced, and the difference becomes statistically insignificant.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of strongly interacting matter is most constrained in the temperature range T ≈ 150–220 MeV, where for all equations of state studied, 0.08 < η/s < 0.23 at 90% credible intervals; the choice of equation of state parametrization has only a small ≈10% effect on the favored η/s value, which is less than the ≈30% uncertainty of the analysis using a single equation of state parametrization.

## Method

The authors performed a global Bayesian model-to-data comparison using a 2+1D hydrodynamical model with the EKRT initial state, comparing to Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV. They used a piecewise linear parametrization of (η/s)(T) with three parts: linearly decreasing and increasing regions at low and high temperatures connected by a constant-value plateau of variable range. They constructed three new equation of state parametrizations (s87h04, s88h18, s83s18) based on contemporary lattice QCD results and hadron resonance gas, and compared them to the widely used s95p parametrization, using Gaussian process emulators trained on 170 training points per equation of state to approximate the 90-dimensional output space reduced to 6 principal components.

## Key numbers

- η/s is constrained to 0.08 < η/s < 0.23 in the temperature range T ≈ 150–220 MeV, taking into account 90% credible intervals for all equations of state
- The equation of state parametrization has only a ≈10% effect on the favored η/s value, which is less than the ≈30% uncertainty of the analysis using a single equation of state parametrization
- Median values of η/s minimum (ηsmin) range from 0.15 (for s95p) to 0.18 (for s83s18), with 90% credible intervals of approximately ±0.03 to ±0.07
- Chemical freeze-out temperature Tchem median values are 153–155 MeV across all equations of state, with 90% credible intervals of ±2 to ±4 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature Tdec median values range from 126 MeV (s95p) to 132 MeV (s83s18 and s88h18), with 90% credible intervals of approximately ±10 to ±16 MeV
- The EKRT normalization parameter Ksat median values range from 0.43 (s95p) to 0.53 (s88h18), with 90% credible intervals of approximately ±0.09 to ±0.15

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of η/s temperature dependence (Bernhard 2016, Bass 2017, Bernhard 2019) by using a more flexible piecewise linear parametrization that does not impose monotonic increase above Tc, and by systematically comparing four different equations of state including the widely used s95p parametrization (Huovinen 2009) and three new parametrizations based on contemporary lattice QCD results from the HISQ (Bazavov 2014, 2017) and stout (Borsanyi 2013) discretization schemes. The results contradict earlier claims (Alba 2017, Schenke 2019) of a 30–60% increase in extracted η/s when switching from s95p to contemporary lattice-based equations of state, finding instead only a ≈10–20% effect within statistical uncertainties. This work enables more reliable extraction of transport properties from heavy-ion collision data by quantifying the systematic uncertainty from the equation of state choice.
