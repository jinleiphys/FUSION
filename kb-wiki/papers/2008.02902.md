# The emission of electromagnetic radiation from the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We estimate the production of electromagnetic radiation (real and virtual photons) from the early, pre-equilibrium, stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The parton dynamics are obtained as a solution of the Boltzmann equation in the Fokker-Planck diffusion limit. The photon and dilepton rates are integrated and the obtained yields are compared with those from standard sources and with available experimental data. Non-equilibrium photon spectra are predicted for Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV.

## Key claim
The paper estimates electromagnetic radiation (real and virtual photons) from the pre-equilibrium stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the results depend on the assumption that parton dynamics can be modeled via a Fokker-Planck diffusion limit of the Boltzmann equation, and the yields are compared with standard thermal sources rather than claimed as definitive predictions.

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in the Fokker-Planck diffusion approximation for gluon and quark phase-space distributions under Bjorken expansion, initialized with a gluon-only distribution inspired by the Color Glass Condensate. Photon and dilepton production rates are then integrated over the pre-equilibrium evolution from the initial time (τ ~ Q_s^{-1}) up to a switching time τ_hydro = 0.4 fm/c, after which hydrodynamic evolution takes over. The initial gluon occupation f_0 is fixed by matching entropy from hydrodynamical simulations to measured charged particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC), Q_s = 1 GeV, f_0 = 2.25 (ξ = 1.0) or 3.81 (ξ = 1.5).
- For Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (LHC), dS/dy = 13700, f_0 = 6.65 (Q_s = 1 GeV, ξ = 1.0) or 5.75 (Q_s = 2 GeV, ξ = 1.0).
- For Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (LHC), non-equilibrium photon spectra are predicted (specific numerical yields not given in truncated text).
- The switching time to hydrodynamics is τ_hydro = 0.4 fm/c.
- The effective strong coupling constant is α_s ~ 0.234, corresponding to 4πα_s²L ~ 1.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic probes from the quark-gluon plasma (Gale 2018) by focusing on the pre-equilibrium phase, which is often neglected in favor of thermal emission. It builds on the Fokker-Planck diffusion approach developed by Blaizot (2014) and Churchill (2020) for parton evolution, and uses initial conditions from the Color Glass Condensate framework (Gelis 2010). The results enable comparisons between pre-equilibrium and thermal photon yields, and can be tested against experimental data from RHIC and LHC, potentially constraining the early-time dynamics of heavy-ion collisions.
