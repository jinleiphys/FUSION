# Constraints on Neutrino Parameters from Neutral-Current Solar Neutrino Measurements

> We generalize the pull approach to define the $χ^2$ function to the analysis of the data with correlated statistical errors. We apply this method to the analysis of the Sudbury Neutrino Collaboration data obtained in the salt-phase. In the global analysis of all the solar neutrino and KamLAND data we find the best fit (minimum $χ^2$) values of neutrino parameters to be $\tan^2 θ_{12} \sim 0.42$ and $δm_{12}^2 \sim 7.1 \times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$. We confirm that the maximal mixing is strongly disfavored while the bounds on $δm_{12}^2$ are significantly strengthened.

# Constraints on Neutrino Parameters from Neutral-Current Solar Neutrino Measurements

## Key claim

The authors generalize the pull approach for chi-squared analysis to handle correlated statistical errors, and apply it to the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) salt-phase data. They find best-fit neutrino parameters of tan^2 theta_12 ~ 0.42 and delta m_12^2 ~ 7.1 x 10^-5 eV^2 from the global analysis including KamLAND data, confirming that maximal mixing is strongly disfavored while bounds on delta m_12^2 are significantly strengthened.

## Method

The authors generalized the pull approach to define the chi-squared function for data with correlated statistical errors, proving equivalence to the covariance approach. They performed a global analysis of all solar neutrino data (including gallium experiments SAGE, GALLEX, GNO; Homestake chlorine experiment; Super-Kamiokande 1496-day zenith-angle-spectrum; SNO 2002 day-night-spectrum; and the new SNO salt-phase NC, CC, and ES measurements) plus KamLAND reactor antineutrino data, using the Standard Solar Model fluxes and systematics from Bahcall (2000). Neutrino propagation in matter was calculated including Earth regeneration effects, and the SNO salt-phase data were incorporated with their provided statistical correlation matrix and systematic correlations.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit neutrino parameters from global solar + KamLAND analysis: tan^2 theta_12 ~ 0.42 and delta m_12^2 ~ 7.1 x 10^-5 eV^2
- Best-fit from solar-only analysis: tan^2 theta_12 ~ 0.42 and delta m_12^2 ~ 6.7 x 10^-5 eV^2, with minimum chi-squared of 70.5 for 83 data points and 2 parameters
- Best-fit CC/NC ratio from solar-only analysis: 0.33, compared to the SNO measured value of 0.306 +/- 0.026 (stat) +/- 0.024 (syst)
- Best-fit CC/NC ratio from SNO 2002 data (with T_e,th = 5 MeV): 0.35
- Best-fit shifted ^8B flux (when fixed at SSM value in solar + KamLAND analysis): 1.02 times the SSM value
- The LOW solution is excluded at the 99.78% confidence level

## Context

This work extends the global analysis previously performed by Balantekin and Yuksel (2003) using a covariance approach, now implementing the pull approach as formulated by Fogli et al. (2002). The new SNO salt-phase neutral-current measurements provide a model-independent determination of the total active solar neutrino flux, complementing the KamLAND reactor antineutrino results (Eguchi et al., 2002) which are more sensitive to delta m^2. This analysis enables tighter constraints on the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) region of neutrino parameter space, and the generalized pull method with correlated statistical errors can be applied to future precision neutrino measurements.
