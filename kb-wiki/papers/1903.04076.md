# Cluster structure of light nuclei

> We review recent studies of the cluster structure of light nuclei within the framework of the algebraic cluster model (ACM) for nuclei composed of k alpha-particles and within the framework of the cluster shell model (CSM) for nuclei composed of k alpha-particles plus x additional nucleons. The calculations, based on symmetry considerations and thus for the most part given in analytic form, are compared with experiments in light cluster nuclei. The comparison shows evidence for Z_2, D_{3h} and T_d symmetry in the even-even nuclei 8Be (k=2), 12C (k=3) and 16O (k=4), respectively, and for the associated double groups Z'_2 and D'_{3h} in the odd nuclei 9Be, 9B (k=2, x=1) and 13C (k=3, x=1), respectively.

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that the cluster structure of light nuclei composed of k alpha-particles and k alpha-particles plus x additional nucleons can be described by discrete geometric symmetries (Z2, D3h, Td and their double groups), with the caveat that the algebraic cluster model (ACM) is a phenomenological approach that does not attempt a microscopic description from nucleon-nucleon interactions, while the cluster shell model (CSM) provides a microscopic treatment using a symmetry-adapted basis.

## Method

The authors apply the algebraic cluster model (ACM), based on bosonic quantization of Jacobi coordinates using U(3k-2) Lie algebras, to describe rotations and vibrations of cluster configurations for k=2 (dumbbell, U(4)), k=3 (equilateral triangle, U(7)), and k=4 (tetrahedron, U(10)). For nuclei with additional nucleons (k alpha + x), they employ the cluster shell model (CSM), which places nucleons in single-particle orbitals of the cluster potential while fully accounting for the Pauli principle, extending the two-center shell model to three-center and four-center configurations.

## Key numbers

- For the dumbbell configuration (8Be, k=2), the energy formula in the large N limit gives E = E0'' + omega(v + 1/2) + B L(L+1), where only even L values are allowed due to Z2 symmetry.
- For the equilateral triangle configuration (12C, k=3), the semiclassical energy formula is E = E0 + omega1(v1 + 1/2) + omega2(v2 + 1) + kappa1 L(L+1) + kappa2 (K minus or plus 2*ell2)^2, with K = 3n for (v1, v2^{ell2=0}) and K = 3n+1, 3n+2 for (v1, v2^{ell2=1}).
- For the tetrahedral configuration (16O, k=4), the ground state (t=A) allows L^P = 0+, 3-, 4+, 6+-, ...; the E representation allows L^P = 2+-, 4+-, 5+-, 6+-; the F representation allows L^P = 1-, 2+, 3+-, 4+-, 5-+, 6++-.
- The CSM produces "magic" numbers for protons and neutrons at 4 for the dumbbell configuration, at 6 for the triangular configuration, and at 8 for the tetrahedral configuration.

## Context

This review extends the long history of alpha-cluster models dating back to Wheeler (1937), Hafstad and Teller (1938), and Brink (1965), who suggested specific geometric configurations for k-alpha nuclei. The ACM provides analytic formulas that serve as benchmarks for microscopic approaches such as antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), fermion molecular dynamics (FMD), lattice effective field theory (lattice EFT), and no-core shell model calculations. The CSM extends the two-center shell model of Von Oertzen to three-center and four-center configurations, enabling systematic studies of cluster structures in nuclei beyond the pure alpha-particle case, such as 9Be, 9B, and 13C.
