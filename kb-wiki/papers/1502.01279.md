# Unexpected Mesons X, Y, Z, ... (tetraquarks? hadron molecules? ...)

> This talk briefly discusses the set of meson resonances discovered in the latest decade. They are frequently treated in the literature as tetraquarks or hadron molecules. Our consideration (using the energy-time uncertainty relation) suggests, however, that the most reasonable description for each of them may be a two- (or more-) component Fock column, with one line being a conventional quark-antiquark pair, and the other line(s) corresponding to a charmed (or beauty) meson-antimeson pair near its threshold. Detailed investigation of decay properties might allow to reveal presence of several Fock components and separate their contributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the most reasonable description for each of the X, Y, Z meson resonances is a two- (or more-) component Fock column, with one line being a conventional quark-antiquark pair and the other line(s) corresponding to a charmed (or beauty) meson-antimeson pair near its threshold, rather than being pure tetraquarks or hadron molecules as frequently treated in the literature.

## Method

The author applies the energy-time uncertainty relation to quantum fluctuations in hadrons, arguing that when a resonance's Breit-Wigner peak overlaps a threshold for a pair of lighter hadrons, the corresponding fluctuation becomes effectively enhanced and "stabilized," making it a permanent component of the Fock column. The analysis focuses on the near-threshold S-wave state of hadron pairs as the dominant contribution to higher Fock components, and applies this framework to specific X and Z states.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) mass: 3871.7 MeV, width < 1.2 MeV, JPC = 1++; overlaps the D0D̄*0 threshold at 3871.8 MeV but not the D+D̄*- threshold at 3879.9 MeV
- Z±(4430) mass: about 4480 MeV, width about 200 MeV, JP = 1+; overlaps the threshold of D0*(0+)D̄1(1+)
- Λ(1520) branching ratio to Σ(1385)π is about 10%, unexpectedly large for a decay with formally no energy release
- f0(980) and a0(980) masses and widths are such that their Breit-Wigner peaks overlap the KK̄ threshold

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of tetraquark configurations, previously applied to scalar mesons a0(980) and f0(980) and later to excited D-mesons such as D(2400). The author argues that the proposed Fock-column picture differs from both the canonical tetraquark approach (which lacks a quark-antiquark component) and the hadron molecule approach (which treats the hadron pair as a bound molecule). The framework suggests that accurate experimental study of various decay modes could separate different Fock components, and that finding a meson with non-canonical flavor quantum numbers would be necessary to demonstrate existence of a genuine tetraquark.
