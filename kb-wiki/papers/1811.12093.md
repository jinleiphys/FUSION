# Jet tomography in high-energy nuclear collisions

> When an energetic parton traversing the QCD medium, it may suffer multiple scatterings and lose energy. This jet quenching phenomenon may lead to the suppression of leading hadron productions as well as medium modifications of full jet observables in heavy-ion collisions. In this talk we discuss the nuclear modification factors and yield ratios of identified meson such as $η$, $ρ^0$, $ϕ$, $ω$, and $K^0_{\rm S}$ as well as $π$ meson at large $p_T$ in A+A collisions at the next-to-leading order (NLO) with high-twist approach of parton energy loss. Then we discuss a newly developed formalism of combing NLO matrix elements and parton shower (PS) for initial hard production with parton energy loss in the QGP, and its application in investigating massive gauge boson ($Z^0$/$W^{\pm}$) tagged jet productions and $b\bar{b}$ dijet correlations in Pb+Pb at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that next-to-leading order (NLO) calculations combined with the high-twist approach of parton energy loss can simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factors of six identified meson species in heavy-ion collisions, and that the extracted jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0\) depends sensitively on the choice of hydrodynamic model for the QGP fireball evolution.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear modification factors and yield ratios for \(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), \(\rho^0\), \(\phi\), \(\omega\), and \(K^0_S\) mesons at large transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC using NLO perturbative QCD with medium-modified fragmentation functions from the higher-twist approach. For full jet observables (Z\(^0\)/W\(^\pm\)+jet and \(b\bar{b}\) dijets), they combine NLO matrix elements with parton shower (PS) from the Sherpa event generator and simulate parton energy loss in the QGP using the LBT (Linear Boltzmann Transport) model that includes both elastic and inelastic scattering processes.

## Key numbers

- Jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0 = 0.5\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) at RHIC \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\ \text{GeV}\) from global \(\chi^2\) fitting of six meson species using the OSU iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model
- Jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0 = 1.1-1.4\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) at LHC \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\ \text{TeV}\) from the same global fitting procedure
- The \(\eta/\pi^0\) ratio in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC overlaps the p+p ratio over a wide \(p_T\) range, with theoretical predictions showing decent agreement with ALICE data (Acharya 2018)
- For \(b\bar{b}\) dijets at the LHC, the momentum imbalance \(x_J = p_{T,2}/p_{T,1}\) distribution shifts to smaller values in Pb+Pb relative to p+p, with the depletion increasing with centrality
- For Z\(^0\)+jet production at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\ \text{TeV}\), the average number of jets per Z boson \(R_{jZ}\) is reduced in Pb+Pb compared to p+p due to parton energy loss

## Context

This work extends the high-twist approach of parton energy loss (Wang 2001, Zhang 2003) from leading hadron suppression to a systematic study of multiple identified meson species and full jet observables. It provides the first combined NLO+PS treatment of Z\(^0\)/W\(^\pm\)+jet and \(b\bar{b}\) dijet production with medium modification, enabling quantitative extraction of the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0\) from global fits to experimental data. The results enable future comparisons with other energy loss formalisms and provide benchmarks for interpreting LHC Run 2 and future heavy-ion data.
