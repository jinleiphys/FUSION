# Test of the hyperon-nucleon interaction within leading order covariant chiral effective field theory

> Motivated by the recent experimental measurements of differential cross sections of the $Σ^{-}p$ elastic scattering in the momentum range of $470$ to $850$ MeV$/c$ by the J-PARC E$40$ experiment, we extend our previous studies of $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon interactions to relatively higher energies up to $900$ MeV$/c$ for both the coupled-channel $Λp\rightarrow(Λp, Σ^{+}n, Σ^{0}p)$, $Σ^{-}p\rightarrow(Λn, Σ^{0}n, Σ^{-}p)$ and single-channel $Σ^{+}p\rightarrowΣ^{+}p$ reactions. We show that although the leading order covariant chiral effective field theory is only constrained by the low energy data, it can describe the high energy data reasonably well, in particular, the J-PARC E40 differential cross sections. The predicted cusp structure close to the $ΣN$ threshold in the $Λp\to Λp$ reaction agrees with the latest ALICE observation as well as with the results of the next-to-leading order heavy baryon chiral effective theory. On the other hand, the comparison with the latest CLAS data on the $Λp$ cross sections between 0.9 and 2.0 GeV$/c$ clearly indicates the need of higher order chiral potentials for such high momenta. This is also the case for the latest J-PARC data on the $Σp \rightarrow Λn$ differential cross sections. Nevertheless, even for these cases, the predictions are in qualitative agreement with the data, albeit with large uncertainties, implying that the predicted total and differential cross sections are of relevance for ongoing and planned experiments.

## Key claim

The leading order covariant chiral effective field theory, although fitted only to low-energy total cross section data (laboratory momenta below 350 MeV/c), can describe the J-PARC E40 differential cross sections for Sigma- p elastic scattering at momenta up to 850 MeV/c reasonably well, but it fails to reproduce quantitatively the J-PARC Sigma- p to Lambda n inelastic differential cross sections and the CLAS Lambda p cross sections at higher momenta, indicating the need for higher-order calculations.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled-channel Kadyshevsky equation for S=-1 hyperon-nucleon scattering using leading order covariant chiral effective field theory potentials, which consist of non-derivative four-baryon contact terms and one-meson exchanges. The 12 independent low-energy constants were determined by fitting to 36 low-energy total cross section data points (laboratory momenta below 350 MeV/c) and the Sigma N inelastic capture ratio at rest. Theoretical uncertainties were estimated using the chiral expansion parameter Q and the difference between leading order results and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy fit included 36 data points: 6 Lambda p to Lambda p points from Sechi-Zorn (1969) and 6 from Alexander (1969), 6 Sigma- p to Lambda n points from Engelmann (1966), 6 Sigma- p to Sigma0 n points from Engelmann (1966), 7 Sigma- p to Sigma- p points from Eisele (1971), and 4 Sigma+ p to Sigma+ p points from Eisele (1971)
- The Sigma N inelastic capture ratio at rest used in fitting was r_R = 0.468 plus or minus 0.010
- The optimal cutoff Lambda_F was set at 600 MeV/c
- The chiral expansion parameter Q is defined as Max{p/Lambda_b, m_pi/Lambda_b} with Lambda_b fixed at 600 MeV/c
- The predicted cusp structure in Lambda p to Lambda p total cross section near the Sigma N threshold reaches almost 50 mb
- The J-PARC E40 experiment measured Sigma- p elastic scattering differential cross sections in four momentum intervals: 470-550 MeV/c, 550-650 MeV/c, 650-750 MeV/c, and 750-850 MeV/c
- The CLAS data cover Lambda p cross sections in the incident Lambda momentum range of 0.9 to 2.0 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous covariant chiral EFT studies of S=-1 hyperon-nucleon interactions by Li (2016) and Song (2018) to higher energies up to 900 MeV/c, and provides the first test of these leading order predictions against the new J-PARC E40 differential cross section data from Miwa (2021) and the J-PARC E40 Sigma- p to Lambda n data from 2021. The results are compared with phenomenological models including the Julich 04 meson-exchange model from Haidenbauer (2005), the Nijmegen NSC97f potential from Rijken (1998), and the quark-cluster model fss2 from Fujiwara (2006), as well as with nonrelativistic heavy baryon chiral EFT results from Polinder (2006) and Haidenbauer (2013). The comparison shows that the leading order covariant approach produces a cusp structure at the Sigma N threshold comparable to the next-to-leading order heavy baryon result, but higher-order calculations are needed to describe the J-PARC inelastic channel and CLAS data, motivating future theoretical work and providing predictions for experiments at J-PARC, BEPC, LHC, and HIAF.
