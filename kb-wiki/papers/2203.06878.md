# Contribution of the Weinberg-type Operator to atomic and nuclear electric dipole moments

> The contribution of the CP violating three-gluon Weinberg operator, $\frac{1}{3!} w f^{abc} ε^{νραβ} G^a_{μν} G^b_{αβ} G^{c μ}_ρ$, to the atomic and nuclear EDMs is estimated using QCD sum rules. After calculating the transition matrix element between the pion and the vacuum through the Weinberg operator, we obtain the long-range CP-odd nuclear force by determining the isovector CP-odd pion-nucleon vertex, using chiral perturbation theory at NLO. The EDMs of $^{199}$Hg, $^{129}$Xe, $^{225}$Ra, $^2$H, and $^3$He are finally given including comprehensive uncertainty analysis. While the leading contribution of the $^{199}$Hg EDM is given by the intrinsic nucleon EDM, that of $^{129}$Xe atom may be dominated by the one-pion exchange CP-odd nuclear force generated by the Weinberg operator. From current experimental data of the $^{199}$Hg atomic EDM, we obtain an upper limit on the Weinberg operator magnitude of $\left|w \right| < 4 \times 10^{-10} {\rm GeV}^{-2}$ if we assume that it is the only source of CP violation at the scale $μ=1$ TeV.

## Key claim

The Weinberg operator's contribution to the isovector CP-odd pion-nucleon interaction, previously thought to be negligible due to chiral suppression, is potentially significant because it may be enhanced by the large pion-nucleon sigma term and by many-body effects in heavy atoms and nuclei. The authors obtain an upper limit on the Weinberg operator magnitude of \(|w| < 4 \times 10^{-10} \text{ GeV}^{-2}\) from current experimental data on the \(^{199}\)Hg atomic EDM, but only under the assumption that the Weinberg operator is the sole source of CP violation at the scale \(\mu = 1\) TeV.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules to compute the transition matrix element \(\langle \pi^0 | \mathcal{L}_W | 0 \rangle\) between the pion and the vacuum through the Weinberg operator, extracting the pion pole from the correlation function of two Weinberg operators. They then employed chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order (NLO) to determine the isovector CP-odd pion-nucleon vertex, which generates the long-range CP-odd nuclear force via one-pion exchange. The resulting CP-odd nuclear force was used to calculate the electric dipole moments (EDMs) of \(^{199}\)Hg, \(^{129}\)Xe, \(^{225}\)Ra, \(^2\)H, and \(^3\)He, including a comprehensive uncertainty analysis.

## Key numbers

- The pion-vacuum matrix element is \(|\langle 0 | \mathcal{L}_W | \pi^0 \rangle| \in w \cdot [2.3, 8.3] \times 10^{-6} \text{ GeV}^5\).
- The neutron EDM generated by the Weinberg operator is \(d_n = w (20 \pm 12) e \text{ MeV}\).
- The upper limit on the Weinberg operator magnitude from \(^{199}\)Hg data is \(|w| < 4 \times 10^{-10} \text{ GeV}^{-2}\) at the scale \(\mu = 1\) TeV.
- The NLO correction to the isovector CP-odd pion-nucleon coupling amounts to about 20% of the leading-order expression (assuming \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 60\) MeV).
- The Borel window for the sum rule analysis is defined with \(M^2_{\text{min}} = 0.25 \text{ GeV}^2\) and an upper threshold parameter \(s^{\text{max}}_{\text{th}} = 0.82 \text{ GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Weinberg operator's contribution to nucleon EDMs (Chemtob 1991, Demir 2002, Haisch 2019, Yamanaka 2020) by calculating its effect on the CP-odd nuclear force, which had not been seriously considered for atomic EDMs. The authors note that the quark EDM and chromo-EDM contributions from the Weinberg operator are small at the hadron level (Yamanaka 2018, Gupta 2018, Alexandrou 2019), while the CP-odd contact nucleon-nucleon interaction is suppressed by strong repulsion between nucleons (de Vries 2011, Dekens 2014, Yamanaka 2016). The results enable improved constraints on CP-violating new physics from atomic EDM experiments, particularly for \(^{129}\)Xe where the one-pion exchange CP-odd nuclear force may dominate over the intrinsic nucleon EDM contribution.
