# Limits on Lorentz violation from forbidden beta decays

> Forbidden (slow) beta decays offer new opportunities to test the invariance of the weak interaction under Lorentz transformations. Within a general effective field theory framework we analyze and reinterpret the only two relevant experiments, performed in the 1970s, dedicated to search for a preferred direction in space in first- and second-forbidden beta decays. We show that the results of these experiments put strong and unique limits on Lorentz violation, and in particular on the presence of several interactions in the modern Lorentz-violating Standard Model extension. We discuss prospects to improve on these limits.

# Limits on Lorentz violation from forbidden beta decays

## Key claim

Forbidden beta decays provide enhanced sensitivity to Lorentz violation in the weak interaction, with the Lorentz-violating parts of the decay rate scaled by a factor αZ/pR relative to Lorentz-symmetric contributions, but only in transitions with ΔI ≥ 2 (starting from unique first-forbidden transitions). The authors reinterpret two experiments from the 1970s to set limits on previously unconstrained parameters of the Standard Model Extension (SME), though the analysis assumes no cancellations between different SME parameters when deriving individual bounds.

## Method

The authors extended their earlier effective field theory framework for Lorentz violation in allowed beta decays to forbidden beta decays, using a multipole expansion of the Lorentz-violating Hamiltonian density with a complex tensor χ^μν that parametrizes deviations from the Minkowski metric in the current-current interaction. They reanalyzed the 1970s experiments on unique first-forbidden ⁹⁰Y decay (Newman 1976) and on ¹³⁷Cs and ⁹⁹Tc decays (Ullman 1978) by deriving theoretical expressions for the measured asymmetries in terms of the Lorentz-violating tensor components, then comparing these to the published experimental data to extract limits at 95% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- Upper limits from ⁹⁰Y experiment at 95% C.L.: |2X_r³⁰ − \~X_i³| < 2×10⁻⁸ (range −6×10⁻⁹ to 2×10⁻⁸); |3X_r³³ − X_r⁰⁰| < 3×10⁻⁶ (range −3×10⁻⁶ to 1×10⁻⁶); combined limit on (2X_r²⁰−\~X_i²) and (2X_r¹⁰−\~X_i¹) < 4×10⁻⁸; combined limits on (X_r¹³+X_r³¹) and (X_r²³+X_r³²) < 1×10⁻⁶; combined limits on (X_r¹²+X_r²¹) and (X_r²²−X_r¹¹) < 1×10⁻⁶
- From ⁹⁰Y experiment at 90% C.L.: dipole anisotropy |ε₁| < 1.6×10⁻⁷; quadrupole anisotropy |ε₂| < 2.0×10⁻⁶
- From ¹³⁷Cs and ⁹⁹Tc experiment at 95% C.L.: |3X_r³³−X_r⁰⁰| < 8×10⁻⁶ (weaker than the ⁹⁰Y bound of 3×10⁻⁶)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier framework for Lorentz violation in allowed beta decays (Noordmans 2013) to forbidden transitions, and reinterprets the only two dedicated experiments from the 1970s searching for preferred directions in space in forbidden beta decays (Newman 1976, Wieber 1972, Ullman 1978). The limits improve on recent bounds from pion decay (Altschul 2013) by three orders of magnitude for certain components of χ^μν, and provide the first direct bounds on several SME parameters in the electroweak sector. The results enable future experiments using forbidden beta decays with low endpoint energies, measurements of allowed beta decays of polarized nuclei (such as the KVI experiment on ²⁰Na by Wilschut 2013 and Muller 2013), or semileptonic decays of hadrons at high-energy facilities where Lorentz boost provides additional enhancement.
