# Non-Fermi liquid behavior of thermal relaxation time in degenerate electron plasma

> The thermal relaxation time ($τ_{κ_{ee}}$) for the degenerate electron plasma has been calculated by incorporating non-Fermi liquid (NFL) corrections both for the thermal conductivity and specific heat capacity. Perturbative results are presented by making expansion in $T/m_D$ with next to leading order corrections (NLO). We see that the NLO NFL corrections further reduce the decrease in relaxation time due to the leading order (LO) correction.

# Non-Fermi liquid behavior of thermal relaxation time in degenerate electron plasma

## Key claim

The thermal relaxation time in degenerate electron plasma deviates from the standard Fermi liquid scaling of 1/T^2 and instead scales approximately as 1/T when non-Fermi liquid corrections from the transverse (magnetic) interaction are included, but only in the perturbative regime where T/m_D is small and the expansion is truncated at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation for electron-electron scattering in degenerate electron plasma using the variational method with a trial function proportional to (epsilon - mu) v_z. They incorporate hard-dense-loop resummed photon propagators to handle screening, expand the polarization functions in the small-x limit (x = omega/q), and evaluate the thermal conductivity and specific heat capacity perturbatively in the small parameter lambda = T/m_D. The medium-modified quasiparticle dispersion relation is included through the derivative of the real part of the electron self-energy, which modifies the phase space factor.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order magnetic contribution to the thermal conductivity integral scales as 2 lambda^2 zeta(3), where lambda = T/m_D
- The electric contribution scales as (pi^5/15) lambda^3
- The longitudinal-transverse cross term scales as (2 pi)^(2/3)/3 lambda^(8/3) zeta(11/3) Gamma(14/3)
- The leading-order phase space correction gamma = e^2/(12 pi^2) log(4/(pi lambda))
- The next-to-leading order phase space correction gamma' = (2^(2/3) e^2 lambda^(2/3))/(9 sqrt(3) pi^(7/3)) - (40 x 2^(1/3) e^2 lambda^(4/3))/(27 sqrt(3) pi^(11/3))
- The specific heat capacity at leading order is c_v = mu^2 T/3, with NLO corrections containing terms proportional to m_D^2 T log(4/(pi lambda)) and fractional powers lambda^(5/3) and lambda^(7/3)

## Context

This work extends the calculations of thermal conductivity in degenerate electron plasma by Heiselberg (1993) and Shternin (2006), who showed that magnetic interaction increases electron-electron collision frequency at high density. It also builds on non-Fermi liquid studies of transport coefficients in ultradegenerate plasma by Gerhold (2005), Ipp (2004), and Sarkar (2011). The results are relevant for heat transport in neutron star crusts, where the temperature gradient between the hot crust and cold core drives thermal relaxation. The next step would be to incorporate these corrections into full neutron star cooling simulations to determine whether the modified relaxation time significantly alters the thermalization time scale compared to earlier estimates by Gnedin (2001) and Potekhin (1999).
