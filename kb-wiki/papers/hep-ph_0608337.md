# Dispersion analysis of the nucleon form factors including meson continua

> Dispersion relations provide a powerful tool to analyse the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon for all momentum transfers. Constraints from meson-nucleon scattering data, unitarity, and perturbative QCD can be included in a straightforward way. In particular, we include the 2pi, rho-pi, and KKbar continua as independent input in our analysis and provide an error band for our results. Moreover, we discuss two different methods to include the asymptotic constraints from perturbative QCD. We simultaneously analyze the world data for all four form factors in both the space-like and time-like regions and generally find good agreement with the data. We also extract the nucleon radii and the omega-NN coupling constants. For the radii, we generally find good agreement with other determinations with the exception of the electric charge radius of the proton which comes out smaller. The omega-NN vector coupling constant is determined relatively well by the fits, but for the tensor coupling constant even the sign can not be determined.

## Key claim

The authors perform a dispersion-relation analysis of all four nucleon electromagnetic form factors simultaneously in both space-like and time-like regions, including for the first time the $2\pi$, $\rho\pi$, and $K\bar{K}$ continua as independent input rather than fitting them to form factor data. The proton electric charge radius comes out smaller than other determinations, and while the $\omega NN$ vector coupling constant is determined relatively well, even the sign of the tensor coupling constant cannot be determined.

## Method

The spectral functions for the isovector and isoscalar form factors are constructed from unitarity constraints using the $2\pi$ continuum (from pion form factor data and $\pi\pi \to \bar N N$ P-wave amplitudes), the $K\bar{K}$ continuum (from analytic continuation of $KN$ scattering data), and the $\rho\pi$ continuum (from the Bonn-Jülich $NN$ model). Remaining spectral strength is parameterized by vector meson poles including the $\omega$, a residual $\phi$, and effective higher-mass poles, with asymptotic constraints from perturbative QCD enforced via either superconvergence relations or an explicit pQCD continuum term. The world data for all four form factors are fitted simultaneously using conjugate gradient algorithms with Monte-Carlo sampling for initial convergence stability.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric charge radius: comes out smaller than other determinations (no explicit numerical value given in the truncated text for this radius)
- $2\pi$ continuum parameterization constants: $a_1=1.10788$, $b_1=0.109364$, $c_1=0.36963$ GeV$^2$, $d_1=0.553034$ GeV$^2$ for $F_1$; $a_2=5.724253$, $b_2=1.111128$, $c_2=0.27175$ GeV$^2$, $d_2=0.611258$ GeV$^2$ for $F_2$; errors in these constants are of order 4% or less
- $K\bar{K}$ continuum pole residues: $a_1^{K\bar{K}}=0.1054$ GeV$^2$, $a_2^{K\bar{K}}=0.2284$ GeV$^2$ at the $\phi$ mass
- $\rho\pi$ continuum effective pole: mass $M_{\omega'} = 1.12$ GeV with residues $a_1^{\rho\pi}=-1.01$ GeV$^2$, $a_2^{\rho\pi}=-0.04$ GeV$^2$; $a_2^{\rho\pi}$ can vary between $-0.04$ and $-0.4$ without affecting the fit outcome
- Best fit in the superconvergence approach has 17 free parameters and a total $\chi^2$/dof of 1.8
- Effective pole masses in the superconvergence fit: isoscalar poles at $M_{s_1}\approx 1.1$ GeV and $M_{s_2}\approx 2.0$ GeV; isovector poles with masses ranging from 1 to 3 GeV; heaviest poles have width parameters from 5 GeV (isoscalar) to 19 GeV (isovector)
- Dipole mass parameter: $m_D^2=0.71$ GeV$^2$ for the normalization of $G_M^n$, $G_E^p$, $G_M^p$

## Context

This work extends the dispersion analysis of Hohler et al. (1976) and the updates by Mergell, Meißner, and Drechsel (1996) and Hammer and Meißner (2004) by including the $K\bar{K}$ and $\rho\pi$ continua as independent input alongside the $2\pi$ continuum, and by enforcing asymptotic pQCD constraints via two different methods. The analysis addresses the bump-dip structure in $G_E^n$ reported by Friedrich and Walcher (2003), finding that all neutron electric form factor data are described within the error band without such a pronounced structure. The results enable improved extraction of nucleon radii and vector meson coupling constants, and provide a framework for incorporating new time-like data from BES, CLEO, and BABAR as well as preliminary CLAS neutron magnetic form factor data.
