# A comment on the two definitions of the electric polarizability of a bound system in relativistic quantum theory

> In a paper published in A.J.P. by Coutinho, Nogami and Tomio, two definitions of the relativistic electric polarizability were introduced and discussed. The analyses and conclusions of the authors contain a fair number of serious mistakes. It is our purpose to correct any misunderstanding that might result from the errors appearing in their paper.

# A comment on the two definitions of the electric polarizability of a bound system in relativistic quantum theory

## Key claim

The paper by Coutinho, Nogami and Tomio (1999) contains serious mistakes in their analysis and conclusions regarding two definitions of relativistic electric polarizability, particularly in their claim that including the vacuum background yields a positive polarizability equivalent to the nonrelativistic one. The authors failed to explain why including the vacuum background is equivalent to setting the high energy limit regarding the scattering amplitude, and ignored essential references on bound-electron scattering and dispersion relations.

## Method

The paper critiques the two definitions of relativistic electric polarizability introduced by Coutinho, Nogami and Tomio (1999): α (obtained in quantum field theory with vacuum background included) and αQM (obtained in single-particle quantum mechanics without vacuum background). The author examines the expressions for second-order energy shifts WQM and WQFT, the relationship α = αQM + α'vac, and the hydrogen atom example used in the original paper. The critique draws on prior work by Sucher (on positivity of electric polarizability and high-energy assumptions), Erber (1959) (on coherent Compton amplitude and dispersion relations), and Goldberger and Low (1968) (on photon scattering from bound atomic systems and pair production cancellation).

## Key numbers

- The electric polarizabilities are defined via W = -1/2 α E², where E is the external electric field, with separate definitions for αQM and α.
- The second-order energy shift in single-particle quantum mechanics is given by WQM = Σ_{i≠1} |V_{i,1}|²/(E₁ - E_i) + Σ_j |V_{-j,1}|²/(E₁ - E_{-j}), where V = -q E·r.
- The second-order energy shift in quantum field theory is given by WQFT = Σ_{i≠1} |V_{i,1}|²/(E₁ - E_i) + Σ_{i≠1,j} |V_{i,-j}|²/(E_{-j} - E_i).
- The vacuum contribution is W'vac = Σ_{i,j} |V_{i,-j}|²/(E_{-j} - E_i), leading to WQFT = WQM + W'vac and α = αQM + α'vac.

## Context

This comment extends the critical discussion of relativistic electric polarizability definitions initiated by Coutinho, Nogami and Tomio (1999), correcting their misinterpretation of Sucher's work on positivity of electric polarizability and the high-energy assumption for forward scattering amplitudes. The author connects to prior work by Erber (1959) on coherent Compton amplitude and dispersion relations, and Goldberger and Low (1968) on bound-electron scattering and pair production cancellation, which were ignored in the original paper. This enables a proper understanding of how bound-electron scattering should be identified in relativistic calculations of electric polarizability, and highlights the importance of subtraction terms in dispersion relations for obtaining correct polarizability values.
