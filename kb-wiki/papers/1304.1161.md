# Towards a Renormalization Group Approach to Density Functional Theory - General Formalism and Case Studies -

> We discuss a two-point particle irreducible (2PPI) approach to many-body physics which relies on a renormalization group (RG) flow equation for the associated effective action. In particular, the general structure and properties of this RG flow equation are analyzed in detail. Moreover, we discuss how our 2PPI RG approach relates to Density Functional Theory and argue that it can in principle be used to study ground-state properties of non-relativistic many-body systems from microscopic interactions, such as (heavy) nuclei. For illustration purposes, we use our formalism to compute the ground-state properties of two toy models.

## Key claim

The authors present a two-point particle irreducible (2PPI) renormalization group approach to Density Functional Theory that can in principle compute ground-state properties of non-relativistic many-body systems from microscopic interactions, but they only demonstrate the formalism on two toy models with known analytic solutions, not on realistic nuclear systems.

## Method

The authors derive an exact renormalization group flow equation for the 2PPI effective action (which is directly related to the Hohenberg-Kohn energy density functional) by introducing a control parameter λ that continuously turns on the two-body interaction from zero at λ=0 to the full physical interaction at λ=1. The flow equation has a one-loop structure and contains a Hartree term plus a term involving the inverse of the second functional derivative of the effective action with respect to the density. The authors then expand the effective action about the ground-state density in a vertex expansion to obtain coupled flow equations for the ground-state energy, ground-state density, and n-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The flow equation parameter λ runs from 0 (non-interacting system) to 1 (fully interacting system at the physical point)
- The ground-state energy is obtained as E_gs = lim_{β→∞} (1/β) Γ[ρ_gs], where β is the inverse temperature
- The energy of the first excited state can be extracted from the two-point correlation function via (E_1 - E_gs) = -lim_{β→∞} (1/β) ln G_{σ,σ}(0,x;β,x')
- A dimensionless coupling parameter is defined as ū_0 = ℓ_V² u_0, where ℓ_V is a typical length scale associated with the background potential

## Context

This work extends the DFT-RG approach previously introduced by Schwenk (2004) and Braun (2011) by providing a detailed analysis of the general structure and properties of the 2PPI RG flow equation, including its connection to perturbation theory and the Hartree and Hartree-Fock approximations. The approach is complementary to microscopic nuclear energy density functional studies using density matrix expansions by Carlsson (2008) and Stoitsov (2010), and to ab-initio calculations of trapped neutron drops by Bogner (2011). The formalism enables future systematic computation of nuclear energy density functionals from chiral effective field theory interactions, though the present work only demonstrates the method on toy models.
