# Photoproduction of ${γp\to K^+Λ^*(1520)}$ and decay of $Λ^*(1520)\to K^- p$ in the Reggeized framework

> Photoproduction of the $Λ^*(1520)$ resonance of spin-parity ${3\over2}^-$ off the proton target is investigated within the Regge framework where the $t$-channel reggeization is applied for the $K(494)+K^*(892)+K_2^*(1430)$ exchanges in the Born amplitude. The present model is based on the two basic ingredients; the one is the minimal gauge prescription for the convergence of the reaction and the other is the role of the $K_2^*$ crucial to be consistent with high energy data. The cross sections for the total, differential and photon polarization asymmetry are reproduced without fit parameters and compared with existing data. The LAMP2 and LEPS measurements of the angular distribution of the $K^-$ in the $Λ^*(1520)\to K^-p$ decay are investigated and found to be dominated by the decay of $Λ^*$ with helicity $\pm3/2$ based on the analysis of the density matrix elements related. Detailed discussion on the density matrix elements is given to clarify the analysis of the observable. The reaction mechanism is featured by the dominance of the contact term with the $K$ and $K_2^*$ exchanges following in the low energy region. The $K^*$ exchange appears in minor role. At high energies beyond $E_γ\approx 5$ GeV the role of $K_2^*$ exchange leads over other exchanges in the reaction process.

# Photoproduction of γp → K⁺Λ*(1520) and decay of Λ*(1520) → K⁻ p in the Reggeized framework

## Key claim
The reaction γp → K⁺Λ*(1520) can be described from threshold to 11 GeV without fit parameters or cutoff functions, provided the minimal gauge prescription is applied for convergence and the tensor meson K₂*(1430) exchange is included. The model reproduces total and differential cross sections and the K⁻ decay angular distribution, showing that the Λ* decay is dominated by helicity ±3/2, though this finding contradicts the SAPHIR and CLAS electroproduction results.

## Method
The authors construct a Reggeized t-channel exchange amplitude for K(494), K*(892), and K₂*(1430) mesons in the Born amplitude for γp → K⁺Λ*(1520). Gauge invariance for the K exchange is enforced via the minimal gauge prescription, which includes only the pure charge term of the proton-pole (e_p·2p·ε) plus a contact term, rather than the full Dirac propagator. The K₂*NΛ* coupling is introduced for the first time, and its coupling constant is related to the K*NΛ* coupling by duality and vector dominance (f_{K₂*NΛ*}/m_{K₂*} = −3 f_{K*NΛ*}/m_{K*}). The K₂* coupling is fixed by fitting to the SLAC data point at E_γ = 11 GeV, and the K* coupling follows from the relation.

## Key numbers
- f_{KNΛ*} = 10.59 from the measured decay width Γ_{Λ*→N̄K} = 7.02 MeV (PDG); however, the model uses f_{KNΛ*}/√(4π) = 1.8 (set I for CLAS/LEPS) and 2.5 (set II for LAMP2/SLAC), smaller than the decay-width estimate by a factor of 0.84.
- g_{γK⁺K*⁺} = ±0.254 from Γ_{K*→γK±} = (0.05 ± 0.005) MeV; negative sign chosen.
- g_{γKK₂*} = ±0.276 from Γ_{K₂*→γK±} = (0.24 ± 0.05) MeV; negative sign chosen.
- f_{K*NΛ*} = 3.5 (set I and II), determined via the relation f_{K₂*NΛ*}/m_{K₂*} = −10.5/m_{K*}.
- At E_γ = 3.8 GeV and θ ≈ 0°, the density matrix elements are ρ₃₃ = 0.375 and ρ₁₁ = 0.125, satisfying ρ₃₃ + ρ₁₁ = 1/2.
- LEPS data (2009) give Σ ≈ −0.01 ± 0.07 at kaon angle θ < 60° in energy interval E_γ = 1.75–2.4 GeV.
- LEPS fit parameters for I(θ*): α ≈ 0.52, β ≈ 0.48 (forward, 1.9 < E_γ < 2.4 GeV, 0° < θ < 60°); α ≈ 0.63, β ≈ 0.37 (backward, 1.7 < E_γ < 2.4 GeV, 90° < θ < 180°).

## Context
This work extends the Reggeized framework previously applied to πΔ photoproduction (Yu, 2013) and KΣ*(1385) photoproduction (Yu, 2014) to the negative-parity spin-3/2 Λ*(1520). It addresses the long-standing uncertainty in the K*NΛ* coupling constant, which had been estimated as g_{K*NΛ*}/m_{K*} = 1.56/m_{K*} from coupled-channel approaches (Roca, 2006; Hyodo, 2006) versus ≈10 from quark models. By including the K₂* tensor meson, the authors resolve the ambiguity and find f_{K*NΛ*} = 3.5, closer to the coupled-channel result. The analysis contradicts the SAPHIR (Wieland, 2006) and CLAS electroproduction (Barrow, 2001) conclusions on the decay angular distribution, motivating future precise measurements of ρ₃₃ and ρ₁₁ in a specified Gottfried-Jackson frame. The model enables reexamination of N* resonance contributions without double-counting due to s-t channel duality.
