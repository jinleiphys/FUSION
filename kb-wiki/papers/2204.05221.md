# Hyperons, deconfinement and the speed of sound in neutron stars

> The effects of the presence of hyperons and a phase transition to deconfined quark matter on the speed of sound in neutron stars is investigated. For this purpose a composite description consisting of a model of the covariant field theory of hadrons and one for unbound quarks are used. A phase transition with continuous and monotonous variation of the equation of state is assumed. The predictions are contrasted with recent observational data on isolated neutron stars as well as on binary systems. Only one candidate is finally obtained from six different descriptions. According to the present calculations the onset of the hyperons causes the equilibrium speed of sound to exceed the conformal limit. Qualitative agreement with recent work about the influence of the speed of sound on the g-modes of oscillation in neutron stars is obtained.

# Hyperons, deconfinement and the speed of sound in neutron stars

## Key claim

The presence of hyperons in neutron star matter causes the equilibrium speed of sound to exceed the conformal limit of \(v_s/c = 1/\sqrt{3}\), but only one out of six different model descriptions (the M\(\sigma\delta\) hadronic model combined with the NJL quark model) simultaneously satisfies the observational constraints of maximum mass above \(2 M_\odot\) and tidal deformability limits from GW170817.

## Method

The author constructs composite equations of state by combining three hadronic covariant field theory models (GM1e, NL3e, and M\(\sigma\delta\)) with two quark matter descriptions (bag model and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model), using a Gibbs construction for a continuous phase transition with coexisting phases. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations are solved for nonrotating neutron stars, and the speed of sound is evaluated from the derivative of pressure with respect to energy density, with both equilibrium and adiabatic definitions considered for comparison with g-mode oscillation studies.

## Key numbers

- The conformal limit for the speed of sound is \(v_s/c = 1/\sqrt{3}\) (approximately 0.577)
- The M\(\sigma\delta\) model with NJL predicts a maximum neutron star mass of \(M/M_\odot = 2.003\) with radius \(R = 12.097\) km
- For the M\(\sigma\delta\) model, a star with mass \(M/M_\odot = 2.07\) (NH case) has radius \(R = 12.67\) km
- The M\(\sigma\delta\) model predicts a radius of \(R = 12.6\) km for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star, compatible with the range \(12.33^{+0.76}_{-0.81}\) km from Raajmakers (2021)
- The pressure at density \(n/n_0 = 2\) is constrained to \(P = 3.5^{+2.7}_{-1.7}\) dyn/cm\(^2\) from Abbott (2018); the NL3e model exceeds this by 3% with hyperons and 7% without
- The bag model requires \(B \geq 200\) MeV/fm\(^3\) to maintain pure hadronic matter below \(5 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\)
- The GM1e model with \(U_\Xi = 40\) MeV gives hyperon-scalar couplings \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N} = 0.617\), \(g_{\sigma\Sigma}/g_{\sigma N} = 0.404\), and \(g_{\sigma\Xi}/g_{\sigma N} = 0.113\)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the hyperon puzzle, where most relativistic field theory models fail to produce neutron stars with masses above \(2 M_\odot\) when hyperons are included, as noted by Glendenning (1985) and Weissenborn (2012). The study contrasts with approaches that artificially suppress hyperons, such as those reviewed by Lourenco (2021), and instead follows the line of Motta (2020) and Tan (2022) in including hyperonic degrees of freedom. The results enable future work on the detectability of g-mode oscillations in binary neutron star mergers, following the framework of Jaikumar (2021), where the difference between equilibrium and adiabatic speeds of sound determines the mode frequency.
