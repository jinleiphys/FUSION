# Reply by N. Dinh Dang to the Comment by A. Rabhi

> It is pointed out that the ``exact'' solutions given in the recent Comment by A. Rabhi on the paper [N. Dinh Dang ``Energies of the ground state and first excited $0^{+}$ state in an exactly solvable pairing model'', Eur. Phys. Jour. A 16 (2003) 18] do not correspond to the standard exact solution of the well-known two-level pairing model. Other issues raised in the Comment reiterate the discussions already published in the original paper by N. Dinh Dang.

## Key claim
The "exact" solutions presented in Rabhi's Comment do not correspond to the standard exact solution of the well-known two-level pairing model obtained using SU(2) algebra; the one-boson energy given by Eq. (53) of Dang (2003) is actually identical to the solution first obtained by Hogaasen-Feldman.

## Method
The author responds to three specific points raised in Rabhi's Comment: (A) the boson mapping in Sec. 4.1.1, (B) the approximations leading to the RPA matrices (58)–(61) and those from Hagino, and (C) the approximation (75). The Reply demonstrates that the boson formalism conserves total particle number even when individual level numbers are not conserved, using commutation relations between number operators and the additional and removal RPA operators.

## Key numbers
- The one-boson energy ω_RPA^(b) from Eq. (53) of Dang (2003) is exactly the same as the solution obtained by Hogaasen-Feldman using the space-variable technique.
- The difference between the matrices in Dang (2003) and those in Hagino is a factor of 2 in the denominator of the second term at the right-hand side of Eq. (58) in Dang (2003) and the corresponding term in Eq. (38) of Hagino.

## Context
This Reply addresses a Comment by Rabhi on Dang (2003, Eur. Phys. Jour. A 16, 181), which tested BCS, Lipkin-Nogami, RPA, QRPA, renormalized RPA, and renormalized QRPA approximations against the exactly solvable two-level pairing model. The Reply clarifies that Rabhi's claimed exact solutions differ from the standard SU(2) algebra solution used in prior work by Hogaasen-Feldman and others. The discussion of the q-term and scattering terms connects to numerical RPA calculations that neglect such terms, as in Soloviev, and to extended QRPA or modified QRPA approaches.
