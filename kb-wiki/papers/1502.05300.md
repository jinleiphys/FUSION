# Real-Time Dynamics of an Impurity in an Ideal Bose Gas in a Trap

> We investigate the behavior of a harmonically trapped system consisting of an impurity in a dilute ideal Bose gas after the boson-impurity interaction is suddenly switched on. As theoretical framework, we use a field theory approach in the space-time domain within the T-matrix approximation. We establish the form of the corresponding T-matrix and address the dynamical properties of the system. As a numerical application, we consider a simple system of a weakly interacting impurity in one dimension where the interaction leads to oscillations of the impurity density. Moreover, we show that the amplitude of the oscillations can be driven by periodically switching the interaction on and off.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that after suddenly switching on the impurity-boson interaction in a harmonically trapped ideal Bose gas, the impurity density exhibits oscillations whose amplitude can be driven by periodically switching the interaction on and off, but this result is derived within the T-matrix (ladder) approximation assuming a dilute gas where the condensate density is not appreciably changed by the impurity.

## Method

The authors use a field-theoretical approach in the space-time domain within the T-matrix (ladder) approximation, treating the impurity in a harmonically trapped ideal Bose gas at zero temperature. They derive the in-medium T-matrix for systems of different spatial dimensions by solving an integral equation for the function f(t) that describes the formation and decay of virtual dimers. As a numerical application, they consider a weakly-interacting one-dimensional system where the impurity-boson interaction is modeled by a Gaussian potential, and they compute the impurity density evolution by applying the infinitesimal time evolution operator repeatedly to the initial wave function.

## Key numbers

- Coupling strength used in numerical calculations: g N = 0.3 (in units where ℏ = m_I = Ω = 1)
- Time is measured in units of 1/Ω, distance in units of √(ℏ/(m_I Ω)), and density in units of √((m_I Ω)/ℏ)
- For the 1D case with short times (Ωt ≪ 1), the T-matrix in frequency domain is given by f^δ(ω) = g / [1 + (i g/ℏ) √(μ/(2ℏ)) (1/√ω)]
- For the 3D case with short times, the T-matrix in frequency domain is f^δ(ω) = 1 / [μ/(2πℏ² a) + i (μ/ℏ)^(3/2) √ω/(√2 π ℏ)]
- For the 2D case with short times, the T-matrix is f^δ(ω) = (2ℏ²π/μ) / [iπ - ln(a²_2D μ ω e^γ/ℏ)], where γ = 0.5772 is Euler's constant

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose polarons from homogeneous systems to trapped systems with real-time dynamics, building on the T-matrix approach used by Schmidt (2013) for homogeneous Bose-polaron problems. The paper connects to the broader literature on impurity dynamics in cold atomic gases, including experimental work with bosonic hosts by Widera (2012) and Oberthaler (2013), and theoretical studies using variational wave functions by Timmermans (2006) and Demler (2014). The results enable future investigations of non-equilibrium dynamics and relaxation processes in closed quantum systems following a quench of the coupling constant.
