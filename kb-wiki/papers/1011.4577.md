# Flavor unification, dark matter, proton decay and other observable predictions with low-scale $S_4$ symmetry

> We show how gauge coupling unification is successfully implemented through non-supersymmetric grand unified theory, $SO(10)\times G_f (~G_f=S_4, SO(3)_f, SU(3)_f)$, using low-scale flavor symmetric model of the type $SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y$ $ \times SU(3)_C \times S_4$ recently proposed by Hagedorn, Lindner, and Mohapatra, while assigning matter-parity discrete symmetry for the dark matter stability. For gauge coupling unification in the single-step breaking case, we show that a color-octet fermion and a hyperchargeless weak-triplet fermionic dark matter are the missing particles needed to complete its MSSM-equivalent degrees of freedom. When these are included the model automatically predicts the nonsupersymmetric grand unification with a scale identical to the minimal supersymmetric standard model/grand unified theory scale. We also find a two-step breaking model with Pati-Salam intermediate symmetry where the dark matter and a low-mass color-octet scalar or the fermion are signaled by grand unification. The proton-lifetime predictions are found to be accessible to ongoing or planned searches in a number of models. We discuss grand unified origin of the light fermionic triplet dark matter, the color-octet fermion, and their phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors show that gauge coupling unification in a non-supersymmetric $SO(10) \times G_f$ grand unified theory can be achieved by extending the low-scale $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y \times SU(3)_C \times S_4$ flavor model of Hagedorn, Lindner, and Mohapatra (HLM), but only if a color-octet fermion and a hyperchargeless weak-triplet fermionic dark matter particle are added at the TeV scale; without these additional particles, unification fails.

## Method

The authors performed two-loop renormalization group evolution of the three Standard Model gauge couplings from $M_Z$ to the Planck scale, using the Particle Data Group values for $\sin^2\theta_W(M_Z)$ and $\alpha^{-1}(M_Z)$ and a $3\sigma$ uncertainty on $\alpha_S(M_Z)$. They considered a single-step breaking scenario where $SO(10) \times G_f$ breaks directly to $G_{213} \times S_4$ at the GUT scale, with $S_4$ breaking to the Standard Model at a scale $M_S \sim 1-10$ TeV, and tested unification with and without additional scalar or fermionic multiplets. They also constructed a two-step breaking model with Pati-Salam intermediate symmetry and derived proton lifetime predictions including GUT-threshold and low-scale threshold effects.

## Key numbers

- The minimal $G_{213} \times S_4$ model with six electroweak doublets fails to unify gauge couplings; the three inverse fine-structure constants cross at three different points, with no overlapping region even when $\alpha_S(M_Z)$ is varied within $3\sigma$ ($\alpha_S(M_Z) = 0.1184 \pm 0.0007$).
- In the single-step breaking Model I with scalars, unification is achieved with $M_S = 10^{2.5}$ GeV, $M_X = M_{\sigma}(3,0,1) = 10^{3}$ GeV, $M_C(1,0,8) = 10^{3.5}$ GeV, $M_U = 10^{16}$ GeV, and $1/\alpha_G = 35.3$.
- The predicted proton lifetime for $p \rightarrow e^+ \pi^0$ in Model I is $\tau_p^0 = 2.48 \times 10^{35}$ years, which is about 25 times longer than the current experimental limit.
- Including threshold effects with superheavy component masses up to $10$ times heavier or lighter than the GUT scale and $M_S$-threshold parameter $|\eta'| = \ln(5)$, the proton lifetime is $\tau_p = 10^{35.245 \mp 0.428 \pm 0.2}$ years.
- The mass difference between the charged and neutral components of the weak-triplet fermionic dark matter is $m_{F^{\pm}_{\sigma}} - m_{F^0_{\sigma}} = 166$ MeV.
- The weak-triplet fermionic dark matter mass is estimated as $m_{F_{\sigma}} = 2.75 \pm 0.15$ TeV from WMAP relic density data with Sommerfeld resonance at $2.5$ TeV, and a more recent analysis gives $m_{F_{\sigma}} \simeq 4.5$ TeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-supersymmetric $G_{213} \times S_4$ flavor model proposed by Hagedorn, Lindner, and Mohapatra (HLM) by embedding it in $SO(10) \times G_f$ grand unification, addressing the absence of gauge coupling unification in the minimal HLM model. It contrasts with earlier non-supersymmetric $SO(10)$ studies by Frigerio and collaborators, which required a color-octet fermion with mass $7 \times 10^{10}$ GeV (inaccessible to accelerators), whereas the present model predicts both the triplet dark matter and the color-octet fermion at the TeV scale, making them testable at colliders. The results enable experimental searches for these particles at the LHC and next-generation proton decay experiments such as DUSEL.
