# Probing the Color Glass Condensate in $pp$ collisions at forward rapidities and very low transverse momenta

> The description of the hadron production at very forward rapidities and low transverse momentum is usually made using phenomenological models based on nonperturbative physics. However, at high energies and large rapidities the wave function of one of the projectiles is probed at very small Bjorken $x$, being characterized by a large number of gluons. In this kinematical regime, a new state of matter - the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) - is expected to be formed. One the main characteristics of such system is the presence of a new dynamical momentum scale, the saturation scale $Q_s$, which can assume values very larger than the QCD confinement scale $Λ_{QCD}$ and give the scale of the running coupling constant. In this paper we assume that in particular kinematical region probed by LHC forward (LHCf) experiment the saturation scale can be considered the hard momentum scale present in the process and calculate the forward neutral pion production at very low-$p_T$ using a perturbative approach. We demonstrate that the CGC formalism is able to successfully describe the LHCf data, which can be considered as a compelling indication of the presence of non-linear QCD effects at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism can successfully describe LHCf data for neutral pion production at very forward rapidities and very low transverse momentum, provided one assumes that the saturation scale \(Q_s\) becomes the dominant hard momentum scale in that kinematic region, making the coupling constant small enough for a perturbative approach.

## Method

The authors used the dilute-dense factorization formula of the CGC formalism, which convolves projectile parton distribution functions (CTEQ5), dipole-hadron scattering amplitudes, and parton fragmentation functions (KKP) into neutral pions. They employed two models for the dipole scattering amplitude: the solution of the improved Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling (rcBK) and the phenomenological BUW model. The factorization scale was set to the saturation scale \(Q_s\), and a constant \(\mathcal{K}\)-factor of 7 was used to mimic higher-order corrections, with the initial saturation scale \(Q_{0s}^2\) varied among 0.168, 0.1, and 0.05 GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- LHCf data were taken at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV for rapidities \(y > 8.9\) and transverse momentum \(p_T \le 0.6\) GeV.
- The initial saturation scale squared was taken as \(Q_{0s}^2 = 0.168\) GeV\(^2\) (with alternatives 0.1 and 0.05 GeV\(^2\)), with \(\lambda = 0.3\) and \(x_0 = 10^{-4}\) for the energy evolution.
- A \(\mathcal{K}\)-factor of 7 described the data for all rapidity bins, independent of the scattering amplitude model; using \(\mu = Q_s/2\) reduced the required \(\mathcal{K}\)-factor to 3.
- The rcBK parameters from a fit to \(F_2\) data were \(Q_{s0}^2 = 0.15\) GeV\(^2\), \(\gamma = 1.13\), \(\Lambda_{\rm{QCD}} = 0.241\) GeV, and \(x_0 = 0.01\).
- The BUW model parameters fitted to RHIC data were \(a = 2.82\) and \(b = 168\), with \(\gamma_s = 0.628\).

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism, previously applied to hadron production at \(p_T \ge 1\) GeV at RHIC and LHC (e.g., Dumitru et al. (2006), Albacete and Marquet (2010)), down to very low transverse momenta by treating the saturation scale as the hard scale. It directly confronts the LHCf data (2012), which had previously been described only by nonperturbative models. The success of the CGC description suggests that gluon saturation effects must be included in models of high-energy air showers for cosmic ray experiments, and it opens the possibility of using forward particle production at the LHC to constrain the saturation scale.
