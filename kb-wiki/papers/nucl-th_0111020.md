# Discerning the neutron density distribution of 208Pb from nucleon elastic scattering

> We seek a measure of the neutron density of 208Pb from analyses of intermediate energy nucleon elastic scattering. The pertinent model for such analyses is based on coordinate space nonlocal optical potentials obtained from model nuclear ground state densities. Those potentials give predictions of integral observables and of angular distributions which show sensitivity to the neutron density. When compared with experiment, and correlated with analyses of electron scattering data, the results suggest that 208Pb has a neutron skin thickness ~0.17 fm.

## Key claim

Analyses of intermediate-energy nucleon elastic scattering using coordinate-space nonlocal optical potentials from model nuclear ground state densities, when correlated with electron scattering data, suggest that 208Pb has a neutron skin thickness of approximately 0.17 fm; this result is conditional on the specific Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models used and the quality of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors generated coordinate-space nonlocal optical potentials by folding realistic effective nucleon-nucleon g-matrix interactions with ground-state density matrices from five different nuclear structure models for 208Pb (three Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models: SHF1, SHF2, SKM*; and two harmonic-oscillator models: HO1, HO2). They then compared predictions of differential cross sections and integral observables (total reaction and total neutron cross sections) from these potentials against experimental data for proton and neutron elastic scattering at 40, 65, and 200 MeV, using 40Ca scattering as a calibration test.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness S = 0.17 fm for 208Pb suggested by the SKM* model, consistent with the SHF1 (SKX) model value of S = 0.16 ± 0.02 fm
- SHF2 model gives S = 0.25 fm for 208Pb
- HO1 model: proton rms radius 5.45 fm, neutron rms radius 5.84 fm (S = 0.39 fm)
- HO2 model: neutron rms radius 5.61 fm, giving S = 0.16 fm
- SKM* model: incompressibility coefficient K = 217 MeV; SKX (SHF1): K = 270 MeV
- Previous analyses cited: proton scattering at 0.8 GeV gave S = 0.14 ± 0.04 fm; 650 MeV proton scattering gave S = 0.20 ± 0.04 fm; inelastic alpha scattering gave S = 0.19 ± 0.09 fm; pion reaction cross sections gave S = 0.0 ± 0.1 fm

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of neutron skin thickness in 208Pb that used phenomenological models of hadron scattering, which produced a range of results from 0.0 to 0.3 fm and were limited by systematic errors of about 0.2 fm. The authors use a g-folding approach with realistic effective interactions to discriminate between different Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models (Brown 2000) and harmonic-oscillator models, finding that the SKM* model (Bartel 1982) gives the best representation of the density. This enables a more precise determination of the neutron density distribution beyond just the rms radius, and suggests that the planned parity-violating electron scattering experiment at Jefferson Laboratory (2000) should be complemented by nucleon scattering analyses to obtain a complete picture of the neutron density.
