# Symmetry Energy Effects on Fusion Cross Sections

> We investigate the reaction path followed by Heavy Ion Collisions with exotic nuclear beams at low energies. We will focus on the interplay between reaction mechanisms, fusion vs. break-up (fast-fission, deep-inelastic), that in exotic systems is expected to be influenced by the symmetry energy term at densities around the normal value. The evolution of the system is described by a Stochastic Mean Field transport equation (SMF), where two parametrizations for the density dependence of symmetry energy (Asysoft and Asystiff) are implemented, allowing one to explore the sensitivity of the results to this ingredient of the nuclear interaction. The method described here, based on the event by event evolution of phase space quadrupole collective modes will nicely allow to extract the fusion probability at relatively early times, when the transport results are reliable. Fusion probabilities for reactions induced by 132Sn on 64,58Ni targets at 10 AMeV are evaluated. We obtain larger fusion cross sections for the more n-rich composite system, and, for a given reaction, in the Asysoft choice. Finally a collective charge equilibration mechanism (the Dynamical Dipole) is revealed in both fusion and break-up events, depending on the stiffness of the symmetry term just below saturation.

# Symmetry Energy Effects on Fusion Cross Sections

## Key claim

Fusion probabilities for neutron-rich systems are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy, with larger fusion cross sections obtained for the Asysoft parametrization (where the symmetry energy is nearly flat around saturation density) compared to the Asystiff parametrization (where it decreases quickly below normal density), but the total cross section differences are only 4-5% for the most neutron-rich system and even smaller for the less neutron-rich system, making the effect most visible in a specific angular momentum window (130 to 180 ℏ).

## Method

The authors performed Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport simulations for reactions of 132Sn on 64Ni and 58Ni targets at 10 AMeV beam energy, using two parametrizations of the symmetry energy (Asysoft and Asystiff). They extracted fusion probabilities from the event-by-event evolution of phase space quadrupole collective moments (coordinate-space quadrupole Q(t) and momentum-space quadrupole QK(t)) evaluated at early times (200-300 fm/c), classifying events as fusion or break-up based on the sign of Q' and QK. The fusion cross section was then obtained by integrating over impact parameters from 4.5 fm to 8.0 fm.

## Key numbers

- Total fusion cross section for 132Sn + 64Ni: 1128 mb (Asysoft) vs. 1078 mb (Asystiff), a difference of about 4-5%
- Total fusion cross section for 132Sn + 58Ni: 1020 mb (Asysoft) vs. 1009 mb (Asystiff)
- In the angular momentum window 130 ≤ l ≤ 180 ℏ, the fusion cross section difference reaches 10% for the more neutron-rich system
- The total fusion cross section for the more neutron-rich system is 10% larger in the Asysoft calculation and about 7% larger in the Asystiff calculation compared to the less neutron-rich system
- Prompt dipole gamma multiplicities for b=5.5 fm: 2.3×10⁻³ (Asysoft) vs. 1.6×10⁻³ (Asystiff); for b=6.0 fm: 1.9×10⁻³ (Asysoft) vs. 1.3×10⁻³ (Asystiff)
- Beam energy: 10 AMeV
- Impact parameter range studied: 4.5 fm to 8.0 fm, with Δb = 0.5 fm
- Normal nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ = 0.145 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A = -16 MeV, compressibility modulus around 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin dynamics in heavy ion collisions (Baran 2005, Bao 2008) to the low-energy regime just above the Coulomb barrier (5-20 AMeV), where fusion competes with deep-inelastic and fast-fission mechanisms. The method based on phase-space quadrupole fluctuations provides a way to extract fusion probabilities at early times when transport simulations are reliable, unlike previous approaches that required following the system to compound nucleus formation. The results show sensitivity to the symmetry energy that could be tested at future Radioactive Ion Beam facilities, and the observation of the Dynamical Dipole mode in both fusion and break-up events extends earlier work on charge equilibration (Bardip 2009) to deep-inelastic collisions with exotic beams. The authors note that their absolute fusion cross sections for the neutron-rich system agree with data from Liang (2007) at lower energy (around 5 AMeV) taken at ORNL.
