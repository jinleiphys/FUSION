# Fluctuating shapes of the fireballs in heavy-ion collisions

> We argue that energy and momentum deposition from hard partons into quark-gluon plasma induces an important contribution to the final state hadron anisotropies. We also advocate a novel method of Event Shape Sorting which allow to analyse the azimuthal anisotropies of the fireball dynamics in more detail. A use of the method in femtoscopy is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The authors argue that energy and momentum deposition from hard partons into the quark-gluon plasma during the fireball evolution, not just in the initial conditions, induces an important contribution to final state hadron anisotropies; this effect is relevant at LHC energies and must not be omitted in simulations aiming to extract matter properties.

## Method

The authors implemented source terms in a 3D ideal hydrodynamic simulation with an equation of state parametrized from lattice QCD results, using smooth initial conditions from the optical Glauber model with a flat rapidity profile. Hard partons were introduced as back-to-back pairs with fluctuating numbers and a power-law distribution of transverse momenta. Separately, the authors developed the Event Shape Sorting (ESS) method, which re-orders events based on Bayesian probabilities calculated from azimuthal angle histograms, without pre-defining a sorting observable, and applied it to event sets generated by DRAGON and AMPT.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow \(v_2\) in non-central collisions (centrality class 30-40%) is increased by about 50% in simulations with momentum deposition from hard partons compared to simulations with smooth initial conditions.
- The DRAGON event set contained \(1.5 \times 10^5\) events with anisotropy parameters set to \(a_2, \rho_2 \in [-0.1, 0.1]\) and \(a_3, \rho_3 \in [-0.03, 0.03]\).
- The AMPT event sets contained \(10^4\) events each for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter 7-10 fm at both \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (AMPT-RHIC) and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2760\) GeV (AMPT-LHC).

## Context

This work extends the standard paradigm that final state anisotropies result solely from the response of hot matter to initial state inhomogeneities, as used in hydrodynamic modeling. The authors specifically cite prior work by Schulc (2014) and Tachibana (2014) on momentum deposition from hard partons, and by Kopecna (2015) on the Event Shape Sorting method. The ESS method is contrasted with Event Shape Engineering (ESE) by Schukraft (2012), which requires a pre-defined sorting observable. The results enable more differential comparisons of theory to data by allowing simultaneous observation of second and third-order oscillations in hadron distributions and HBT correlation radii.
