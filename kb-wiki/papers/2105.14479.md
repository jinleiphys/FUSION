# Constraining the relativistic mean-field models from PREX-2 data: Effective forces revisited

> Based on the current measurement of the neutron distribution radius ($R_n$) of $^{208}$Pb through the PREX-2 data, we re-visited the recently developed G3 and IOPB-I force parameter by fine-tuning some of the specific couplings within the relativistic mean-field model. The $ω-ρ-$mesons coupling $Λ_ω$ and the $ρ-$meson coupling $g_ρ$ are refitted to reproduce the experimental neutron radius of $^{208}$Pb without compromising the bulk properties of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter observables. The modified parameter sets are applied to calculate the gross properties of finite nuclei for a few double closed-shell nuclei and further used to obtain the various infinite nuclear matter observables at saturation. In addition to these, the force parameters are adopted to calculate the properties of high isospin asymmetry dense system such as neutron star matter and tested for the validation for the constraint from GW170817 binary neutron star merger events. The tuned forces are predicting relatively good results for finite and infinite nuclear matter systems and the current limitation on neutron radius from PREX-2. A systematic analysis using these two refitted parameter sets over the nuclear chat will be communicated shortly.

## Key claim

By minimally retuning only the ω-ρ cross-coupling Λ_ω and the ρ-meson coupling g_ρ within the existing G3 and IOPB-I relativistic mean-field parameter sets, the authors reproduce the PREX-2 neutron radius of ⁵⁰⁸Pb without degrading the models' predictions for bulk properties of finite nuclei, infinite nuclear matter, or neutron star observables, including constraints from the GW170817 merger.

## Method

The authors start from the existing G3 and IOPB-I relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) parameter sets. They vary only two couplings, the ω-ρ cross-coupling Λ_ω and the ρ-meson coupling g_ρ, to fit the experimental binding energy and neutron distribution radius of ⁵⁰⁸Pb from PREX-2. The modified sets (G3(M) and IOPB-I(M)) are then used to compute binding energies and charge/neutron radii for several double closed-shell nuclei, infinite nuclear matter properties at saturation (symmetry energy J, slope L, incompressibility K_∞, etc.), and neutron star properties (mass-radius relation, tidal deformability) for comparison with observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- Neutron distribution radius of ⁵⁰⁸Pb: G3(M) gives R_n = 5.717 fm; IOPB-I(M) gives R_n = 5.721 fm; PREX-2 experimental value is R_n = 5.727 ± 0.071 fm.
- Symmetry energy J: G3(O) = 31.842 MeV → G3(M) = 33.063 MeV; IOPB-I(O) = 33.355 MeV → IOPB-I(M) = 34.831 MeV.
- Slope parameter L: G3(O) = 49.317 MeV → G3(M) = 65.530 MeV; IOPB-I(O) = 63.700 MeV → IOPB-I(M) = 78.947 MeV.
- Incompressibility K_∞: unchanged at 243.97 MeV (G3) and 222.33 MeV (IOPB-I); experimental range is 220–260 MeV.
- Maximum neutron star mass: G3(M) = 1.99 M_⊙; IOPB-I(M) = 2.14 M_⊙.
- Dimensionless tidal deformability Λ at 1.4 M_⊙: G3(M) = 498.23; IOPB-I(M) = 727.43; GW170817 constraint is 70–580.
- Binding energy of ⁵⁰⁸Pb: G3(M) = 1636.43 MeV; IOPB-I(M) = 1636.42 MeV; experimental value is 1636.43 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the calibration of relativistic mean-field models to incorporate the precise PREX-2 measurement of the neutron skin in ⁵⁰⁸Pb (Adhikari 2021). It directly responds to the analysis by Reed (2021), who used the PREX-2 result to constrain the symmetry energy slope L to 106 ± 37 MeV and symmetry energy J to 38.1 ± 4.7 MeV. The modified G3 and IOPB-I forces improve agreement with these constraints while preserving the successful predictions of the original parameter sets for finite nuclei (Kumar 2017, Kumar 2018) and neutron star properties, including the GW170817 tidal deformability limits (LIGO/Virgo 2017). The authors state that a systematic analysis over the nuclear chart will be communicated shortly.
