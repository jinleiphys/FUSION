# Probing the High Density Behaviour of Nuclear Symmetry Energy with High Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> High energy heavy-ion collisions are proposed as a novel means to constrain stringently the high density (HD) behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy. Within an isospin-dependent hadronic transport model, it is shown for the first time that the isospin asymmetry of the HD nuclear matter formed in high energy heavy-ion collisions is uniquely determined by the HD behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy. Experimental signatures in two sensitive probes, i.e., $π^-$ to $π^+$ ratio and neutron-proton differential collective flow, are also investigated.

# Probing the High Density Behaviour of Nuclear Symmetry Energy with High Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
High energy heavy-ion collisions can constrain the high density behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy, but the paper explores only the *upper bound* of symmetry energy effects on such collisions. The claim that the isospin asymmetry of high density nuclear matter is "uniquely determined" by the symmetry energy behaviour is demonstrated within a specific isospin-dependent hadronic transport model using two representative parameterizations of the symmetry energy.

## Method
The authors used an isospin-dependent hadronic transport model (IBUU) that solves the evolution of phase-space distribution functions for nucleons, Delta resonances, and pions with explicit isospin degrees of freedom, using the test-particle approach. The equation of state for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter was taken in the parabolic approximation, with the isoscalar part fixed by saturation properties and a compressibility of 201 MeV, while the symmetry energy was represented by two forms: a linearly increasing function and a function that crosses zero at a critical density. Isospin-dependent cross sections and Pauli blockings were included, and the reaction studied was 132Sn + 124Sn at 400 MeV/nucleon and an impact parameter of 1 fm.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is taken as E_sym(ρ₀) = 30 MeV.
- The compressibility of isospin symmetric nuclear matter is K_∞ = 201 MeV.
- The critical reduced density u_c for the decreasing symmetry energy form ranges from about 2.7 (Hartree-Fock with Skyrme interaction Sp) to 9 (variational many-body approach with UV14+UVII interaction).
- A variation of about 30% in the neutron-to-proton ratio at densities ρ/ρ₀ ≥ 1 due to different symmetry energies results in about 15% change in the final π⁻/π⁺ ratio, giving an appreciable response factor of about 0.5.
- For the neutron-proton differential collective flow, a striking difference of about a factor of 2 exists in reactions at beam energies E_beam ≥ 200 MeV/nucleon.
- With the linearly increasing symmetry energy, the direct URCA process can happen at densities higher than about 2.3ρ₀ in neutron stars.

## Context
This work extends the longstanding effort to determine the high density behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy, which had previously been studied with microscopic and phenomenological models (e.g., Hartree-Fock with Skyrme and Gogny interactions by Brown (year not given) and Maruyama (year not given)) that gave contradictory predictions. The paper proposes heavy-ion collisions as a terrestrial laboratory to constrain this behaviour, complementing astrophysical studies of neutron star composition and cooling (Lattimer et al., 1991; Sumiyoshi et al., 1994), kaon condensations (Lee, 1996; Kurtz, year not given), and mass-radius correlations (Prakash, 1988; Engvik, 1994). The results enable future experiments at the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) to provide the first direct terrestrial data on the high density symmetry energy.
