# Breaking of the number-of-constituent-quark scaling for identified-particle elliptic flow as a signal of phase change in low-energy data taken at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC)

> We argue that measurements of identified-particle elliptic flow in a wide energy range could shed light on the possible phase change in high-energy heavy ion collisions at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). When the hadronization process is dominated by quark coalescence, the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling for the identified-particle elliptic flow can serve as a probe for studying the strong interacting partonic matter. In the upcoming RHIC low-energy runs, the NCQ scaling behavior may be broken because of the change of the effective degrees of freedom of the hot dense matter, which corresponds to the transition from the dominant partonic phase to the dominant hadronic phase. A multiphase transport model is used to present the dependence of NCQ scaling behavior on the different hadronization mechanisms.

## Key claim

The presence or absence of number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling for identified-particle elliptic flow may serve as a signal for the onset of deconfinement in heavy ion collisions; the scaling should be retained when the system is dominated by partonic degrees of freedom at high energy, but may be broken at lower energies when the system is dominated by hadronic interactions.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model in two configurations: the "string melting" scenario, where initial strings are converted to soft partons and hadronization proceeds via quark coalescence, and the default version, where hadronization occurs via Lund string fragmentation without a partonic stage. They calculated elliptic flow \(v_2\) for identified particles in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 12.3\) GeV and examined whether the NCQ scaling (scaling \(v_2\) and transverse kinetic energy by the number of constituent quarks) held in each case.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, baryons (proton, Lambda, Xi, Omega) saturate at \(p_T \geq 3\) GeV/c with \(v_2 \sim 0.2\), while mesons saturate earlier at lower \(v_2\) values.
- The AMPT model with string melting at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 12.3\) GeV produces a "nice NCQ-scaling behavior" for identified particles; the default AMPT model without string melting at the same energy breaks the NCQ scaling.
- The lattice QCD prediction cited gives a transition or cross-over temperature \(T_c \approx 150\)–\(180\) MeV at vanishing baryon density.
- The SPS data show a turnover and decrease in \(\langle K^+ \rangle / \langle \pi^+ \rangle\) and related ratios around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7.6\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the observation of NCQ scaling at top RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV) to lower collision energies, proposing that the scaling behavior can diagnose the transition from partonic to hadronic matter. It builds on prior experimental results from the STAR Collaboration (2003–2006) showing NCQ scaling at 200 GeV, and on SPS data (2002) showing anomalous energy dependence around 7.6 GeV. The AMPT model simulations provide a testable prediction for the upcoming RHIC low-energy scan, which could distinguish between the coalescence-dominated (partonic) and fragmentation-dominated (hadronic) scenarios.
