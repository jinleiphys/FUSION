# Charmonium moving through a strongly coupled QCD plasma: a holographic perspective

> We study the properties of charmonium in a strongly coupled QCD-like plasma at finite momentum. As a basis for this study, a "bottom-up" holographic model is used which has been previously shown to reproduce charmonium phenomenology in vacuum and give a reasonable dissociation temperature at zero momentum. The finite momentum spectral functions are presented and found to be consistent with recent lattice results. The in-medium dispersion relation and momentum dependence of decay width of J/Psi have also been studied. We find no signature of a subluminal limiting velocity from the dispersion relation, while we note that the dissociation temperature decreases with momentum faster than previous holographic models. Based upon the dissociation temperature, a maximum momentum for J/Psi in medium is identified and its phenomenological implications on J/Psi suppression are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that the dissociation temperature of J/psi decreases with momentum faster than in previous holographic models, and they identify a maximum momentum for J/psi in medium based on this dissociation temperature, though they note that the specific dissociation criterion (peak height threshold H_D = 1) is not unique.

## Method

The authors use a "bottom-up" holographic model with a "shift and dip" potential that was previously fitted to reproduce vacuum charmonium masses and decay constants of J/psi and psi'. They extend this model to finite temperature by introducing an AdS black hole metric with h(z)=1-(z/z_h)^4, and solve the equations of motion for transverse and longitudinal gauge field fluctuations to obtain retarded Green's functions and spectral functions at finite momentum. Quasinormal modes are extracted numerically to determine the in-medium mass and width of J/psi.

## Key numbers

- Zero-momentum dissociation temperature: T_D(k=0) = 540 MeV
- Model parameters: a = 0.970 GeV, c = 2.781 GeV, alpha = 1.876 GeV, z_d^(-1) = 2.211 GeV
- Maximum limiting momentum for J/psi at T_c = 190 MeV: k_lim^max = 3.8 GeV (transverse mode), k_lim^max = 4.2 GeV (longitudinal mode)
- Temperature-horizon relation: z_h = (pi T)^(-1)

## Context

This work extends the "shift and dip" holographic model of Grigoryan (2010) from zero momentum to finite momentum charmonium in a strongly coupled plasma. It contrasts with earlier "top-down" holographic approaches by Liu (2006) and Mateos (2006) that did not reproduce vacuum charmonium phenomenology well, and with "bottom-up" models by Fujita (2009) that had only one scale for ground and excited state masses. The results are compared with lattice QCD results by Ding (2012) and with perturbative QCD calculations of gluon dissociation and quasi-free dissociation rates by Zhao (2007). The work enables future comparisons between holographic and lattice spectral functions at finite momentum, and provides a framework for understanding J/psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions.
