# Non-singlet structure function of the 3He-3H system and divergence of the Gottfried integral

> We study shadowing and antishadowing corrections to the flavor non-singlet structure function F_2(3He)-F_2(3H) and show that the difference between the one-particle density distributions of 3He and 3H plays an important role at very small x. We find that the flavor non-singlet structure function in these mirror nuclei is enhanced at small x by nuclear shadowing, which increases the nuclear Gottfried integral, integrated from 10^{-4} to 1, by 11-36 %. When integrated from zero, the Gottfried integral is divergent for these mirror nuclei. It seems likely that, as a consequence of charge symmetry breaking, this may also apply to the proton-neutron system.

## Key claim

The authors show that the flavor non-singlet structure function difference between 3He and 3H is enhanced at small x by nuclear shadowing, and that the Gottfried integral for this mirror-nucleus system diverges when integrated from zero because charge symmetry breaking (primarily from the Coulomb force) prevents cancellation of divergent terms, a divergence that may also apply to the proton-neutron system as a consequence of charge symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors used the Gribov-Glauber multiple scattering formalism in the target rest frame to calculate nuclear shadowing corrections to the structure functions F2 of 3He and 3H, modeling the effective hadronic state interacting with bound nucleons via two different effective cross sections (from Frankfurt and Strikman (1999) and from the two-phase model of Melnitchouk and Thomas). They modeled antishadowing by imposing conservation of valence up and down quark numbers in each nucleus, using Gaussian ground-state wave functions with different slope parameters for 3He and 3H to account for charge symmetry breaking, and combined their small-x results with the large-x calculations of Saito et al. (2000).

## Key numbers

- The nuclear Gottfried integral for the 3He-3H system, integrated from 10^-4 to 1, is increased by 11 to 36% due to nuclear shadowing, depending on the combination of wave function parameters and shadowing model.
- For case 3 at x = 10^-4 and Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, the ratio (F2(3He) - F2(3H))/(F2(3He) + F2(3H)) = 0.0085.
- The flavor singlet combination F2(3He) + F2(3H) is decreased by 4.5% (for the Frankfurt-Strikman model) or 6% (for the two-phase model) at x = 10^-4 relative to 3(F2(p) + F2(n)).
- The free proton-neutron Gottfried integral I_G^{p,n}(10^-4) = 0.24 using CTEQ5L, consistent with the NMC result 0.235 ± 0.026.
- Two pairs of matter radii were used: (R_m(3He), R_m(3H)) = (1.769, 1.524) fm and (1.658, 1.524) fm, corresponding to slope parameters (alpha, alpha') = (40.59, 30.06) GeV^-2 and (36.11, 30.06) GeV^-2.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the non-singlet structure function for bound nucleons initiated by Saito et al. (2000) for the A=3 mirror-nucleus system, incorporating nuclear shadowing and antishadowing corrections at small x that were not included in that earlier work. The finding that the Gottfried integral diverges for the 3He-3H system contrasts with the finite Gottfried integral for the free proton-neutron system measured by the NMC collaboration and raises the possibility that charge symmetry breaking may cause a similar divergence in the proton-neutron case. The predictions can be tested by future experiments at TJLAB, RIKEN, and RHIC (eRHIC).
