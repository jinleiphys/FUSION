# The chiral restored quark stars can exist in the universe

> In this paper, the equation of state (EOS) of deconfined quark stars is studied in the framework of the two-flavor NJL model, and the self-consistent mean field approximation is employed by introducing a parameter $α$ combining the original Lagrangian and the Fierz-transformed Lagrangian, $\mathcal{L}_R= (1-α)\mathcal{L}+α\mathcal{L}_F$, to measure the weights of different interaction channels. It is believed that the deconfinement of phase transition happens along with the chiral phase transition. Thus, due to the lack of description of confinement in the NJL model, the vacuum pressure is set to confine quarks at low densities, which is the pressure corresponding to the critical point of chiral phase transition. We find that deconfined quark stars can reach over two-solar-mass, and the bag constant therefore shifts from $(130 ~\mathrm{MeV})^4$ to $(150 ~\mathrm{MeV})^4$ as $α$ grows. In addition, the tidal deformability $Λ$ is yielded ranging from 253 to 482 along with the decrease of $~α$, which satisfies the astronomical constraint of $Λ<800$ for 1.4-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

Deconfined two-flavor quark stars can exist in the universe and reach over two solar masses, but only if the vector interaction channel is sufficiently strong (parameter α close to 1.0) and the vacuum pressure is redefined as the pressure at the chiral phase transition critical point, rather than using the conventional MIT bag constant.

## Method

The authors employ a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a self-consistent mean field approximation that introduces a weighting parameter α to interpolate between the original Lagrangian and its Fierz-transformed Lagrangian, thereby controlling the relative strength of scalar and vector interaction channels. Proper-time regularization is used instead of a three-momentum cutoff to handle the large chemical potentials (up to ~800 MeV) in massive quark star cores. The equation of state is computed under β-equilibrium and electric charge neutrality conditions, with the bag constant redefined as the integrated pressure up to the chiral phase transition critical chemical potential μ_c. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass reaches 2.06 solar masses for α = 1.0, with a corresponding radius of 9.9 km.
- For α = 0.5, maximum mass is 1.91 solar masses with radius 10.7 km.
- Bag constant B^{1/4} ranges from 132.918 MeV (α = 0.5) to 151.91 MeV (α = 1.0) under charge neutrality.
- Critical chemical potentials μ_c for chiral crossover under charge neutrality: 288, 291, 295, 302, 316, 359 MeV for α = 0.5, 0.6, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 1.0 respectively.
- Tidal deformability Λ for a 1.4-solar-mass quark star ranges from 252.886 (α = 1.0) to 481.812 (α = 0.5), all satisfying the GW170817 constraint Λ < 800.
- Baryon number density at μ = 800 MeV ranges from 0.54 fm^{-3} (α = 1.0) to 1.69 fm^{-3} (α = 0.5).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Su et al., 2020; Zhao et al., 2020) that used an improved NJL model to describe hybrid stars with hadronic surfaces and quark cores, by now considering pure quark stars where the surface consists of deconfined quark matter in the pseudo-Wigner phase. The approach contrasts with the MIT bag model (Witten, 1984) by redefining the bag constant in terms of the chiral phase transition pressure, motivated by lattice QCD results (PhysRevLett.110, 2013) suggesting that deconfinement and chiral transitions occur simultaneously. The results enable future observational tests: the predicted radii (~10 km for 1.4 solar masses) and tidal deformabilities (253–482) can be compared with data from gravitational wave events like GW170817 and X-ray observations such as those by Capano et al. (2020).
