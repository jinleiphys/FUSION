# Parameterization of Deformed Nuclei for Glauber Modeling in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The density distributions of large nuclei are typically modeled with a Woods-Saxon distribution characterized by a radius $R_{0}$ and skin depth $a$. Deformation parameters $β$ are then introduced to describe non-spherical nuclei using an expansion in spherical harmonics $R_{0}(1+β_2Y^0_2+β_4Y^0_4)$. But when a nucleus is non-spherical, the $R_{0}$ and $a$ inferred from electron scattering experiments that integrate over all nuclear orientations cannot be used directly as the parameters in the Woods-Saxon distribution. In addition, the $β_2$ values typically derived from the reduced electric quadrupole transition probability B(E2)$\uparrow$ are not directly related to the $β_2$ values used in the spherical harmonic expansion. B(E2)$\uparrow$ is more accurately related to the intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_{0}$ than to $β_2$. One can however calculate $Q_0$ for a given $β_2$ and then derive B(E2)$\uparrow$ from $Q_0$. In this paper we calculate and tabulate the $R_0$, $a$, and $β_2$ values that when used in a Woods-Saxon distribution, will give results consistent with electron scattering data. We then present calculations of the eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ with the new and old parameters. We demonstrate that $\varepsilon_3$ is particularly sensitive to $a$ and argue that using the incorrect value of $a$ has important implications for the extraction of $η/s$ from the QGP created in Heavy Ion collisions.

# Parameterization of Deformed Nuclei for Glauber Modeling in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The radius $R_0$ and skin depth $a$ inferred from electron scattering experiments that integrate over all nuclear orientations cannot be used directly as parameters in the Woods-Saxon distribution for non-spherical nuclei; the authors calculate corrected values that yield nuclei consistent with both electron scattering data and B(E2)$\uparrow$ measurements, and demonstrate that using the incorrect diffuseness parameter $a$ leads to an overestimate of the viscosity to entropy ratio $\eta/s$ extracted from model-to-data comparisons.

## Method

The authors take a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution with spherical harmonic expansion parameters $R_0$, $a$, $\beta_2$, and $\beta_4$, then rotate and average the density over all orientations to obtain an effective spherically-averaged density. They scan over combinations of $R_0$, $a$, and $\beta_2$, requiring that (1) the spherically-averaged charge distribution reproduces electron scattering data (minimizing the sum of squares of residuals, SSR) and (2) the intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_{20}$ calculated from the deformed density yields a B(E2)$\uparrow$ value matching experimental measurements. The finite size of the nucleon charge distribution is folded into the calculation using a dipole form factor for protons and a separate form factor for neutrons, though neutron contributions to $Q_{20}$ are found to be negligible.

## Key numbers

- For ${}^{238}$U: new parameters are $R_0 = 6.86$ fm, $a = 0.42$ fm, $\beta_2 = 0.265$; old parameters were $R_0 = 6.8054$ fm, $a = 0.605$ fm, $\beta_2 = 0.2863$; the B(E2)$\uparrow$ value used is $12.09 \pm 0.2$ $e^2b^2$
- For ${}^{208}$Pb: new parameters are $R_0 = 6.66$ fm, $a = 0.45$ fm, $\beta_2 = 0$; old parameters were $R_0 = 6.62$ fm, $a = 0.546$ fm
- For ${}^{197}$Au: new parameters are $R_0 = 6.42$ fm, $a = 0.41$ fm, $\beta_2 = -0.13$; old parameters were $R_0 = 6.38$ fm, $a = 0.535$ fm
- For mid-central Au+Au collisions with $N_{part} \approx 100$, the diffuseness amplifies $\varepsilon_3$ by a factor of nearly 2.1 for new parameters ($a=0.41$ fm) and 2.5 for old parameters ($a=0.535$ fm), compared to a hard-sphere nucleus ($a=0$ fm)

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber modeling framework used in relativistic heavy ion collisions, where nuclei have traditionally been described by Woods-Saxon parameters from electron scattering (exp_par_1) and deformation parameters from B(E2)$\uparrow$ measurements (Raman, 2001). The authors correct a mismatch between these parameter sets that arises because electron scattering averages over all orientations of deformed nuclei, while the deformed Woods-Saxon distribution (Filip, 2009) requires intrinsic parameters. The corrected parameters for ${}^{238}$U, ${}^{208}$Pb, and ${}^{197}$Au enable more accurate initial-state modeling for collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The finding that $\varepsilon_3$ is particularly sensitive to the diffuseness parameter $a$ has direct implications for extracting $\eta/s$ from comparisons of $v_3/v_2$ data to hydrodynamic models, suggesting that previous estimates using old parameter values (Schenke, 2014) may need revision.
