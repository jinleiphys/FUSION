# Parity Violation in Elastic Electron-Nucleon Scattering: Strangeness Content in the Nucleon

> Parity violation in elastic electron-nucleon scattering is studied with the basic goal of improving the understanding of electroweak hadronic structure with special emphasis on the strangeness content in the nucleon. Models for the parity-violating (PV) asymmetry are provided and compared with the world data measured at very different kinematics. The effects introduced in the PV asymmetry due to alternative descriptions of the hadronic structure are analyzed in detail. In particular, a wide selection of prescriptions for dealing with the electromagnetic and neutral current weak interaction nucleon form factors, including the most recent ones used in the literature, is considered.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering is sensitive to the strangeness content of the nucleon, but the uncertainty from the choice of electromagnetic nucleon form factors can be comparable to that from the axial-vector structure; the global analysis of world data is consistent with zero magnetic strangeness but shows consistency with positive electric strangeness, though present data do not allow selection of a specific strangeness content that works at all momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors computed the parity-violating (PV) asymmetry for elastic electron-proton scattering using the plane-wave Born approximation with single-boson exchange (photon and Z boson), incorporating electroweak radiative corrections and higher-order electromagnetic effects. They compared theoretical predictions evaluated with multiple recent parametrizations of electromagnetic and weak neutral current nucleon form factors against all available world data from the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, PVA4, G0, and Q-weak experiments. The analysis extracted 1-sigma and 2-sigma confidence ellipses in the magnetic-electric strangeness plane by fitting the strangeness form factor parameters to the full dataset.

## Key numbers

* The PV interference contribution Re(M_gamma* M_Z) is approximately 4-5 orders of magnitude smaller than the purely electromagnetic contribution |M_gamma|^2.
* The SAMPLE experiment used longitudinally polarized electrons with an energy of about 200 MeV at large scattering angles.
* The HAPPEX experiments used polarized electron beams with energy typically around 3 GeV at forward angles.
* The PVDIS experiment measured at |Q^2| = 1.1 and 1.9 (GeV/c)^2 for deep inelastic scattering from deuterium.
* The scale of parity-violating effects is given by A_0 = G_F |Q^2| / (2 sqrt(2) pi alpha), where G_F is the Fermi coupling and alpha is the fine structure constant.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Musolf and Donnelly (1992) where axial-vector and strangeness form factors were characterized by simple expressions with a few parameters to be varied. It provides a systematic comparison of multiple recent form factor parametrizations against all available PV electron-proton scattering data, including the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, PVA4, G0, and Q-weak experiments. The results enable improved constraints on the strange quark contributions to the nucleon's electric and magnetic form factors, and the methodology can be applied to future experiments at higher energies and to studies of nuclear structure effects in forthcoming work.
