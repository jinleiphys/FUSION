# Baryon sigma terms in SU(3) BChPT x 1/Nc

> ChPT and the $1/N_c$ expansion provide systematic frameworks for the strong interactions at low energy. A combined framework of both expansions has been developed and applied for baryons with three light-quark-flavors. The small scale expansion of the combined approach is identified as the $ξ$-expansion, in which the power counting of the expansions is linked according to $O(p)=O(1/N_c)=O(ξ)$. The physical baryon masses as well as lattice QCD baryon masses for different quark mass masses are analyzed to $O(ξ^3)$ in that framework. $σ$ terms are addressed using the Feynman Hellmann theorem. For the nucleon, a useful connection between the deviation of the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation and the $σ$ term $σ_{8N}$ associated with the scalar density $\bar u u+\bar d d-2\bar s s$ is identified. In particular, the deviation from the tree level relation $σ_{8N}=\frac 13(2 m_N-m_Σ-m_Ξ)$, which gives rise to the so called $σ$-term puzzle, is studied in the $ξ$-expansion. A large correction non-analytic in $ξ$ results for that relation, making plausible the resolution of the puzzle. Issues with the determination of the strangeness $σ$ terms are discussed, emphasizing the need for lattice calculations at smaller $m_s$ for better understanding the range of validity of the effective theory. The analysis presented here leads to $σ_{πN}=69(10)$~MeV and $σ_{πΔ}=60(10)$~MeV.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the long-standing "sigma-term puzzle", the discrepancy between the empirical value of σπN and the leading-order relation σπN ≈ σ̂, can be resolved by large non-analytic corrections to the tree-level relation σ8N = (1/3)(2mN − mΣ − mΞ) within the combined ξ-expansion of baryon chiral perturbation theory and the 1/Nc expansion, while the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation itself receives only small 1/Nc-suppressed corrections.

## Method
The authors construct a combined framework of SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory (BChPT) and the 1/Nc expansion, with a linked power counting O(p) = O(1/Nc) = O(ξ). They fit the effective Lagrangian at O(ξ³) to both physical baryon octet and decuplet masses and to lattice QCD baryon masses from Alexandrou et al. (2014) with Mπ ≲ 300 MeV, using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to extract sigma terms from the quark-mass dependence of baryon masses.

## Key numbers
- σπN = 69(10) MeV from the combined fit (Fit 2) using physical and lattice QCD masses
- σπΔ = 60(10) MeV from the same combined fit
- σ̂N = 70(7)(6) MeV from Fit 2, with a theoretical error of 6 MeV
- ΔGMO = 25.5(1.5) MeV from Fit 2, with electromagnetic corrections of about 3 MeV
- Δσ8N = −381(20) MeV from Fit 2, showing a large non-analytic correction
- The ratio Δσ8N/ΔGMO ≈ −14, which is nearly independent of gA and Fπ
- The effective theory is estimated to be reliable for MK ≲ 300–400 MeV

## Context
This work extends the combined BChPT × 1/Nc framework previously developed by Calle Cordon et al. (2012) and Fernando et al. (2017) for baryon masses and axial couplings. It addresses the sigma-term puzzle highlighted by Leutwyler (2015) by showing that the large corrections to σ8N arise naturally from non-analytic terms in the ξ-expansion, while the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation remains well-behaved. The results are consistent with the larger values of σπN from modern πN scattering analyses by Hoferichter et al. (2015, 2016) and Alarcon et al. (2011). The paper emphasizes the need for lattice QCD calculations at smaller ms to better determine the range of validity of the effective theory for strangeness sigma terms.
