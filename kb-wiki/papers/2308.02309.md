# Impact of intrinsic electromagnetic structure on the nuclear charge radius in relativistic density functional theory

> In this study, the effects of the nucleon's intrinsic electromagnetic (EM) structure on the nuclear charge radius have been explored within the framework of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory. It is found that the intrinsic EM structure corrections could make an effect for accurately describing the evolution of nuclear charge radius. After taking into account the intrinsic EM structure corrections, the descriptions of the evolution of charge radii for Pb, Sn, and Cd isotopes have been improved within relativistic density functional theory. Using the Pb isotopic chain as an example, the improvement in charge radii can be primarily attributed to the intrinsic neutron and neutron spin-orbit terms of the intrinsic EM structure. Additionally, nuclear charge densities and corresponding isotopic evolution in Pb isotopes have been discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including the full intrinsic electromagnetic structure of nucleons (intrinsic proton and neutron contributions plus spin-orbit contributions) improves the description of nuclear charge radii in relativistic density functional theory, but the improvement is conditional: the effect is most significant for reproducing the isotopic evolution of charge radii, particularly the kink at neutron number N=126, and the improvement is primarily attributed to the intrinsic neutron and neutron spin-orbit terms.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations with spherical symmetry imposed, using the PC-PK1 relativistic density functional for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. The nuclear charge density was constructed self-consistently from the RHB quasiparticle wave functions by including the full electromagnetic current operator, which yields charge form factors incorporating point-proton and neutron densities, proton and neutron spin-orbit densities, and single-nucleon charge densities. The resulting mean-square charge radius expression includes four correction terms beyond the conventional point-proton formula: intrinsic proton and neutron contributions, and proton and neutron spin-orbit contributions.

## Key numbers

* RMS deviation of charge radii for even-even Pb isotopes improved from 0.0175 fm (without pairing, using only point-proton formula) to 0.0106 fm (without pairing, with full EM structure corrections), and from 0.0145 fm (with pairing, point-proton formula) to 0.0055 fm (with pairing, with full EM structure corrections)
* The total intrinsic EM structure corrections (⟨r²⟩_c − ⟨r²⟩_p) range between 0.5 fm² and 0.6 fm² across the even-even Pb isotopic chain
* The intrinsic neutron contribution N/Z r²_n decreases linearly from −0.132 to −0.180 fm² with increasing neutron number along the Pb chain
* The intrinsic neutron and neutron spin-orbit contributions to δ⟨r²⟩_c^{100,126} are 0.035 fm² and 0.077 fm² respectively, improving the prediction from −1.477 fm² to −1.359 fm² (experimental value: −1.311(13) fm²)
* The proton charge radius used in the form factors is r_p = 0.8414 fm, and the neutron mean-square charge radius is r²_n = −0.11 fm²

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic density functional calculations of charge radii (e.g., the global RHB study by Xia et al. (2018) with RMS deviation 0.0358 fm) by incorporating the full intrinsic electromagnetic structure contributions that were neglected in conventional calculations using only the point-proton formula with a constant 0.64 fm² correction. The study builds on earlier work showing the importance of neutron spin-orbit density contributions to charge radii (Bertozzi et al. (1972), PhysRevC.62.054303) and recent implementations of intrinsic EM structure in relativistic and non-relativistic functionals (PhysRevC.107.054307). The results enable more precise theoretical predictions of charge radii for isotopic chains, particularly for understanding the kink at N=126 in Pb isotopes and the role of pairing correlations in modifying occupation of neutron orbitals 1i_{11/2} and 2g_{9/2}.
