# Thermal Fermionic Dispersion Relations in a Magnetic Field

> The thermal self-energy of an electron in a static uniform magnetic field $B$ is calculated to first order in the fine structure constant $α$ and to all orders in $eB$. We use two methods, one based on the Furry picture and another based on Schwinger's proper-time method. As external states we consider relativistic Landau levels with special emphasis on the lowest Landau level. In the high-temperature limit we derive self-consistent dispersion relations for particle and hole excitations, showing the chiral asymmetry caused by the external field. For weak fields, earlier results on the ground- state energy and the anomalous magnetic moment are discussed and compared with the present analysis. In the strong-field limit the appearance of a field-independent imaginary part of the self-energy, related to Landau damping in the $e^{+}e^{-}$ plasma, is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the thermal self-energy of an electron in a static uniform magnetic field to first order in the fine structure constant and to all orders in the magnetic field strength, deriving self-consistent dispersion relations for particle and hole excitations in the high-temperature limit that show chiral asymmetry caused by the external field, but only under the condition that the off-shell gauge dependence can be neglected in this limit.

## Method

The authors use two complementary methods: the Furry picture, which constructs the fermion propagator by explicit summation over solutions to the Dirac equation in the magnetic field, and Schwinger's proper-time method, which expresses the propagator directly in terms of operators without explicit representation of states. The thermal self-energy is computed to one-loop order in QED, separating contributions from vacuum, thermal electrons, and thermal photons, with the expectation value taken in relativistic Landau level states, focusing on the lowest Landau level.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum self-energy shift for the lowest Landau level is given by an integral expression: $\delta_{\rm vac} = \frac{m^2}{E_0} \frac{\alpha}{2\pi} \int_0^1 ds \int_0^\infty du \exp(-m^2 u s^2) \times \left\{ \frac{2eB [1+ s e^{-2eBus}]}{1+2eBu(1-s)-e^{-2eBus}} - \frac{1+s}{u} \right\}$, where $E_0 = \sqrt{m^2 + p_z^2}$ and $\alpha$ is the fine structure constant.

- The thermal electron contribution to the self-energy shift is expressed as $\delta_e = -i\frac{\alpha}{\pi^2}\frac{eB}{E_0} \int_{-\infty}^\infty dk_0 f_F(k_0) \int_{-\infty}^\infty ds \int_0^\infty du \int dk_z \exp\{-is[k_0^2-m^2-k_z^2]\} \times \exp\{iu[(k_0-E_0)^2-(k_z-p_z)^2+i\epsilon]\} \frac{m^2+(m^2-E_0k_0+p_z k_z)e^{i2eBs}}{1+2eB(\epsilon+iu)-e^{i2eBs}}$.

- The thermal photon contribution to the self-energy shift is $\delta_\gamma = i\frac{\alpha}{\pi^2}\frac{eB}{E_0} \int_{-\infty}^\infty dk_0 f_B(k_0) \int_{-\infty}^\infty ds \int_0^\infty du \int dk_z \exp\{-is[k_0^2-k_z^2]\} \times \exp\{iu[(k_0+E_0)^2-m^2-(k_z+p_z)^2+i\epsilon]\} \frac{m^2-(E_0k_0-p_z k_z)e^{-i2eBu}}{1+2eB(\epsilon-is)-e^{-i2eBu}}$.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electron dispersion relations that were done either in strong background fields without a heat bath (Tsai and Yildiz, 1973; Tsai, 1974; Schwinger and Tsai, 1974; Baier, Katkov, and Strakhovenko, 1974) or at high temperature without external fields (Klimov, 1982; Weldon, 1982; Braaten and Pisarski, 1990). The results enable further study of elementary processes in astrophysical and cosmological models where both strong magnetic fields and thermal plasmas are present, and provide a foundation for investigating the anomalous magnetic moment and Landau damping in electron-positron plasmas.
