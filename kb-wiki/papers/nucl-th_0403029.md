# CGC, Hydrodynamics, and the Parton Energy Loss

> Hadron spectra in Au+Au collisions at RHIC are calculated by hydrodynamics with initial conditions from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC). Minijet components with parton energy loss in medium are also taken into account by using parton density obtained from hydrodynamical simulations. We found that CGC provides a good initial condition for hydrodynamics in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) provides a good initial condition for hydrodynamics in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but only when combined with a minijet component that includes parton energy loss in the medium; the approach fails to reproduce data at peripheral collisions because neither CGC nor hydrodynamics can be applied in the low density region.

## Method

The authors use a \(k_T\) factorized formula following Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (KLN) to compute the gluon rapidity distribution from the CGC, then assume the system reaches local thermal equilibrium at a short time scale and use the gluon distribution as the initial condition for hydrodynamic evolution. Minijet components with parton energy loss in deconfined matter (T ≥ T_c) are added by using the parton density obtained from the hydrodynamic simulations, and the resulting hadron spectra are compared to PHENIX and PHOBOS data.

## Key numbers

- Initial transverse energy per particle from the CGC gluon distribution yields \(E_T/N_g \sim 1.6\) GeV at \(y=0\).
- After thermalization, the transverse energy per particle reduces to \(E_T/N_g \approx 1\) GeV within the authors' parameters.
- After the hydrodynamic afterburner, the transverse energy per particle becomes \((dE_T/dy)/(dN/dy)|_{y=0} = 0.54\) GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is integrated over \(p_T > 4.5\) GeV/c for \(|\eta| < 0.35\) and compared to PHENIX data.
- The scaled elliptic flow \(v_2/\varepsilon\) becomes almost constant around 0.2, as predicted by Kolb (2000).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic description of elliptic flow at low \(p_T\) (Huovinen 2003, Hirano 2001) and the parton energy loss interpretation of jet quenching (GVWZKW) by using CGC initial conditions from Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (KLN). It enables a unified dynamical approach that connects gluon saturation, thermalization, and hard parton energy loss, with a systematic study in progress. The authors note that the KLN calculation based on parton-hadron duality is a good approximation for rapidity distributions.
