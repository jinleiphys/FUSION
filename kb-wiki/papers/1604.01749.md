# Quantum Wigner molecules in semiconductor quantum dots and cold-atom optical traps and their mathematical symmetries

> Strong repelling interactions between a few fermions or bosons confined in two-dimensional circular traps lead to particle localization and formation of quantum Wigner molecules (QWMs) possessing definite point-group space symmetries. These point-group symmetries are "hidden" (or emergent), namely they cannot be traced in the circular single-particle densities (SPDs) associated with the exact many-body wave functions, but they are manifested as characteristic signatures in the ro-vibrational spectra. An example, among many, are the few-body QWM states under a high magnetic field or at fast rotation, which are precursor states for the fractional quantum Hall effect. The hidden geometric symmetries can be directly revealed by using spin-resolved conditional probability distributions, which are extracted from configuration-interaction (CI), exact-diagonalization wave functions. The hidden symmetries can also be revealed in the CI SPDs by reducing the symmetry of the trap (from circular to elliptic to quasi-linear). In addition the hidden symmetries are directly connected to the explicitly broken-symmetry (BS) solutions of mean-field approaches, such as unrestricted Hartree-Fock (UHF). A companion step of restoration of the broken symmetries via projection operators applied on the BS-UHF solutions produces wave functions directly comparable to the CI ones, and sheds further light into the role played by the emergence of hidden symmetries in the exact many-body wave functions. Illustrative examples of the importance of hidden symmetries in the many-body problem of few electrons in semiconductor quantum dots and of few ultracold atoms in optical traps (where unprecedented control of the interparticle interaction has been experimentally achieved recently) will be presented.

## Key claim

Strong repelling interactions between a few fermions or bosons confined in two-dimensional circular traps lead to particle localization and formation of quantum Wigner molecules (QWMs) possessing definite point-group space symmetries that are "hidden" (or emergent) in the circular single-particle densities of the exact many-body wave functions, but are manifested as characteristic signatures in the ro-vibrational spectra.

## Method

The authors use configuration-interaction (CI) exact-diagonalization wave functions to extract spin-resolved conditional probability distributions (CPDs) that directly reveal the hidden geometric symmetries. They also employ unrestricted Hartree-Fock (UHF) mean-field solutions that exhibit broken symmetry (BS), followed by restoration of broken symmetries via projection operators applied to the BS-UHF solutions, producing wave functions directly comparable to the CI ones. The analysis uses group theory to classify the point-group symmetries of the broken-symmetry orbitals and to derive sequences of magic angular momenta.

## Key numbers

- Wigner parameter for Coulomb repulsion: \(R_W = Z^2 e^2 / (\kappa l_0 \hbar \omega_0)\), where \(l_0 = \sqrt{\hbar/(m \omega_0)}\) is the oscillator length, \(\omega_0\) is the trap frequency, and \(\kappa\) is the dielectric constant.
- Wigner parameter for contact (Dirac-delta) repulsion: \(R_\delta = g m / (2\pi \hbar^2)\), where \(g\) is the contact interaction strength.
- For \(N=19\) electrons in a 2D parabolic quantum dot at \(R_W=5\) and zero magnetic field, the UHF electron density exhibits a (1,6,12) multi-ring structure with broken circular symmetry; parameters: \(\hbar \omega_0 = 5\) meV, effective mass \(m^* = 0.067 m_e\).
- For \(N=8\) neutral repelling bosons in a rotating 2D toroidal trap with reduced rotational frequency \(\Omega/\omega_0 = 0.2\) and \(R_\delta = 50\), the QWM ground-state angular momentum is \(L_z = 16\); the confining potential is centered at radius \(r_0 = 3 l_0\).
- For \(N=3\) fully spin-polarized electrons (\(S_z = 3/2\)) at \(R_W = 10\) and zero magnetic field, the UHF orbital energies are 44.801 meV (orbital a) and 46.546 meV (orbitals b and c, degenerate).
- Magic angular momenta sequences derived from group theory: for total spin \(s=1/2\), \(I = 3k + 1\) and \(I = 3k - 1\) (\(k = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots\)); for total spin \(s=3/2\) (fully polarized), \(I = 3k\) (\(k = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of Wigner molecules in quantum dots (Yannouleas and Landman, 1999, 2007) and rotating bosonic systems (Rontani et al., 2004, 2006) by providing a unified group-theoretical framework linking broken-symmetry mean-field solutions to exact CI wave functions. The analysis of magic angular momenta connects directly to the fractional quantum Hall effect, where the odd filling factors \(\nu = 1/(2q+1)\) arise from the point-group symmetries of the intrinsic wave function. The computational microscopy approach (Yannouleas and Landman, 2015) enables investigation of strongly correlated regimes in both ultracold fermionic and bosonic trapped atoms and confined electrons, with potential applications in quantum information and quantum computing.
