# Tricritical behavior of a relativistic field theory in one dimension

> The tricritical behavior in a class of one-dimensional (1D) field theories that exhibit spontaneous symmetry breaking at zero temperature and chemical potential is analyzed. In the Gross-Neveu (GN)-type models of massless fermions the discrete chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken. After doping, the symmetry is restored at a critical chemical potential. We investigate the temperature effects on this doped 1D system under an external constant Zeeman magnetic field $B_0$. We find that $B_0$ suppresses the gapless behavior present for certain values of chemical potential and is able to induce a gapless-gapped phase transition at a critical field strength. We also discuss about the consequences of the consideration of inhomogeneous condensates to the tricritical point, within the Ginzburg-Landau expansion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an external Zeeman magnetic field $B_0$ suppresses the gapless phase present at certain chemical potentials in the one-dimensional Gross-Neveu model and can induce a gapless-gapped phase transition at a critical field strength $B_{0,c}$, but this conclusion is derived within a high-temperature expansion of the effective potential truncated at order $(\Delta/k_BT)^6$ and $(\mu_{\uparrow,\downarrow}/k_BT)^4$, and the quantitative values for the tricritical point differ from full numerical results.

## Method

The authors analyze the Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions with $N$ flavors of massless fermions at finite temperature, chemical potential, and external Zeeman magnetic field. They compute the renormalized effective potential in the large-$N$ (mean-field) approximation, first for constant gap parameter $\Delta$ at zero and finite temperature, then perform a Ginzburg-Landau expansion of the effective potential up to order $\Delta^6$ and $\mu_{\uparrow,\downarrow}^4$ to determine second-order and first-order phase transition curves and the tricritical point. They also consider spatially inhomogeneous condensates $\Delta(x)$ and examine the averaged grand potential density.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential for symmetry restoration at zero temperature and zero magnetic field: $\mu_c = \Delta_0 / \sqrt{2}$, where $\Delta_0$ is the constant band-gap.
- Critical chemical potential asymmetry (Zeeman splitting) for gapless-gapped transition at zero temperature: $\delta\mu_c = 0.372 \Delta_0$, corresponding to critical magnetic field $B_{0,c} = 0.744 \, \Delta_0 / (g \mu_B)$.
- Tricritical point at zero magnetic field from high-temperature expansion: $T_{tc} = 0.6927 \, T_c(0) = 0.3927 \Delta_0$ and $\mu_{tc} = 0.9091 \, T_c(0) = 0.5154 \Delta_0$, where $T_c(0) = \Delta_0 e^{\gamma_E} / (\pi k_B)$ is the critical temperature at zero chemical potential and zero magnetic field.
- For comparison, full numerical results from Wolff give $T_{tc} = 0.318 \Delta_0$ and $\mu_{tc} = 0.608 \Delta_0$.
- Zero-temperature critical temperature at zero chemical potential: $T_c(0) = \Delta_0 e^{\gamma_E} / (\pi k_B)$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Gross-Neveu model phase diagram by Wolff, Chodos, and others, which established the first-order phase transition at $\mu_c = \Delta_0/\sqrt{2}$ and the second-order transition line with a tricritical point at zero magnetic field. The authors add an external Zeeman magnetic field, producing an imbalance between spin-up and spin-down chemical potentials, analogous to studies of metal-insulator transitions in 2D systems by Dent and others. The results enable predictions for transport and magnetic properties of one-dimensional organic conductors such as doped trans-polyacetylene, and the authors note that the critical field can be reduced by enhancement of the $g$-factor due to carrier doping, as studied by Xuan (2020).
