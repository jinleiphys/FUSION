# Heavy-flavour and quarkonium production in the LHC era: from proton-proton to heavy-ion collisions

> This report reviews the study of open heavy-flavour and quarkonium production in high-energy hadronic collisions, as tools to investigate fundamental aspects of Quantum Chromodynamics, from the proton and nucleus structure at high energy to deconfinement and the properties of the Quark-Gluon Plasma. Emphasis is given to the lessons learnt from LHC Run 1 results, which are reviewed in a global picture with the results from SPS and RHIC at lower energies, as well as to the questions to be addressed in the future. The report covers heavy flavour and quarkonium production in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. This includes discussion of the effects of hot and cold strongly interacting matter, quarkonium photo-production in nucleus-nucleus collisions and perspectives on the study of heavy flavour and quarkonium with upgrades of existing experiments and new experiments. The report results from the activity of the SaporeGravis network of the I3 Hadron Physics programme of the European Union 7th Framework Programme.

## Key claim

This report reviews the study of open heavy-flavour and quarkonium production in high-energy hadronic collisions, emphasizing that a quantitative understanding of cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects, measured through proton-nucleus collisions, is required before any reliable interpretation of hot QCD medium effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions can be made, with the caveat that disentangling the various CNM contributions (nuclear PDF modifications, parton saturation, multiple scattering, nuclear absorption, and comover interactions) remains an ongoing challenge.

## Method

The authors compiled and reviewed experimental data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC Run 1 for open heavy-flavour (D and B mesons) and quarkonium (J/ψ, ψ(2S), Υ) production in proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions. They compared these data with theoretical predictions from collinearly-factorized pQCD using nuclear-modified PDFs (nPDF) such as EPS09, the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) framework, and models of parton energy loss characterized by the transport coefficient q̂, as well as models of nuclear absorption and comover dissociation.

## Key numbers

- The LHC p–Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV provide coverage of the momentum fraction x₂ from 10⁻⁵ (for D mesons at forward rapidity) to 0.5 (for 10 GeV/c Υ at backward rapidity).
- For J/ψ production in p–Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the EPS09 NLO nPDF calculation gives RpPb ≈ 0.7–0.9 at low pT, rising to RpPb ≈ 1 at pT = 20 GeV/c.
- The coherence time for heavy-quark pair production is τc ∼ 1/m_QQbar ≲ 0.1 fm/c in the QQbar rest frame; the quarkonium formation time is τf ∼ (m₂S − m₁S)⁻¹ ∼ 0.3–0.4 fm/c.
- The charm quark mass and scale parameters used in the CEM calculations are mc = 1.27 ± 0.09 GeV/c², μF/mc = 2.10^{+2.55}_{-0.85}, and μR/mc = 1.60^{+0.11}_{-0.12}.
- The bottom quark mass and scale parameters used are mb = 4.65 ± 0.09 GeV/c², μF/mb = 1.4^{+0.77}_{-0.49}, and μR/mb = 1.1^{+0.22}_{-0.20}.

## Context

This review extends the work of earlier SPS and RHIC studies (e.g., NA50, NA60, PHENIX, STAR) by synthesizing the first LHC Run 1 results on heavy-flavour and quarkonium production across pp, p–Pb, and Pb–Pb collisions. It builds on the theoretical framework of nuclear PDFs (EPS09 by Eskola et al., 2009; nDS by de Florian et al., 2003) and the Colour Glass Condensate approach, and it identifies open questions such as the role of parton saturation at low x, the need for better-constrained gluon nPDFs, and the possible collective effects in high-multiplicity p–Pb collisions. The report enables future LHC Run 2 and 3 studies to disentangle initial-state CNM effects from final-state hot QCD medium effects with improved precision.
