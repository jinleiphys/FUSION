# Extraction of Electromagnetic Transition Form Factors for Nucleon Resonances within a Dynamical Coupled-Channels Model

> We explain the application of a recently developed analytic continuation method to extract the electromagnetic transition form factors for the nucleon resonances ($N^*$) within a dynamical coupled-channel model of meson-baryon reactions.Illustrative results of the obtained $N^*\rightarrow γN$ transition form factors, defined at the resonance pole positions on the complex energy plane, for the well isolated $P_{33}$ and $D_{13}$, and the complicated $P_{11}$ resonances are presented. A formula has been developed to give an unified representation of the effects due to the first two $P_{11}$ poles, which are near the $πΔ$ threshold, but are on different Riemann sheets. We also find that a simple formula, with its parameters determined in the Laurent expansions of $πN \rightarrow πN$ and $γN \rightarrowπN$ amplitudes, can reproduce to a very large extent the exact solutions of the considered model at energies near the real parts of the extracted resonance positions. We indicate the differences between our results and those extracted from the approaches using the Breit-Wigner parametrization of resonant amplitudes to fit the data.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic transition form factors for nucleon resonances, defined at the resonance pole positions on the complex energy plane, can be extracted via analytic continuation of the dynamical coupled-channels model amplitudes; the extracted form factors are complex numbers and differ from those obtained using Breit-Wigner parametrization, which yields real numbers.

## Method

The authors applied an analytic continuation method, previously developed for the EBAC-DCC model, to numerically continue the coupled-channels integral equations to the unphysical Riemann sheets where resonance poles lie. They identified resonance pole positions by finding zeros of the determinant of the dressed N* propagator, then performed Laurent expansions of the on-shell πN → πN and γN → πN amplitudes at those poles to extract the residues, which define the transition form factors. The integration contours were carefully chosen to avoid singularities from meson-exchange potentials and unstable-particle propagators, such as those in the πΔ channel.

## Key numbers

- P33 resonance pole at (1211, 50) MeV with elasticity 100% (EBAC-DCC), compared to PDG range (1209–1211, 49–51) MeV and 100% elasticity.
- D13 resonance pole at (1527, 58) MeV with elasticity 65% (EBAC-DCC), compared to PDG range (1505–1515, 52–60) MeV and 55–65% elasticity.
- P11 resonance poles at (1357, 76) MeV on sheet (upuupp) with elasticity 49%, and at (1364, 106) MeV on sheet (upuppp) with elasticity 60% (EBAC-DCC); PDG lists only one P11 near 1360 MeV with elasticity 55–75%.
- πN residue R for P33(1210): 52 with phase -46 degrees (EBAC-DCC), compared to 52 with -47 degrees (GWU-VPI) and 53 with -47 degrees (Cutkosky).
- πN residue R for D13(1521): 38 with phase 7 degrees (EBAC-DCC), compared to 38 with -5 degrees (GWU-VPI) and 35 with -12 degrees (Cutkosky).
- πN residue R for P11(1356): 37 with phase -111 degrees (EBAC-DCC), compared to 38 with -98 degrees (GWU-VPI) and 52 with -100 degrees (Cutkosky).

## Context

This work extends the analytic continuation method developed by Suzuki, Sato, and Lee (2009) for extracting N* pole positions from πN elastic scattering within the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels model (Matsuyama, Sato, Lee 2007; Julich-Moscow-Sao Paulo fit 2007). It enables the extraction of electromagnetic transition form factors at resonance poles for γN → πN and N(e,e'π)N reactions, providing a framework that contrasts with Breit-Wigner parametrization approaches used by groups such as GWU-VPI, MAID, and others. The results show that for well-isolated resonances like P33 and D13, the extracted parameters are robust, while for poles near thresholds like P11, the residues are sensitive to the dynamical content of the model.
