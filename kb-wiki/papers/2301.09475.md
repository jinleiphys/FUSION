# Dilepton production from hot and magnetized hadronic matter

> The rate of dilepton emission from a magnetized hot hadronic medium is calculated in the framework of real time formalism of finite temperature field theory. We evaluate the one loop self-energy of neutral rho-meson containing thermo-magnetic propagators for the charged pions in the loop. The in-medium thermo-magnetic spectral function of rho obtained by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation is shown to be proportional to the dilepton production rate. The study of the analytic structure of the neutral rho-meson spectral function in such a medium shows that in addition to the usual contribution coming from the Unitary cut beyond the two-pion threshold there is a non-trivial yield in the low invariant mass region originating due to the fact that the charged pions occupy different Landau levels before and after scattering with the neutral rho-meson and is purely a finite magnetic field effect.

## Key claim

The dilepton production rate from hot hadronic matter is calculated in the presence of a constant background magnetic field, showing that in addition to the standard Unitary cut contribution from the two-pion threshold, a non-trivial yield appears in the low invariant mass region purely due to charged pions occupying different Landau levels before and after scattering with the neutral rho-meson.

## Method

The authors calculate the one-loop self-energy of the neutral rho-meson using the real time formalism of finite temperature field theory, incorporating thermo-magnetic propagators for charged pions in the loop. The in-medium spectral function of the rho-meson is obtained by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation, and this spectral function is shown to be proportional to the dilepton production rate. The self-energy tensor is decomposed into independent covariants appropriate for the thermo-magnetic background, and the analytic structure of the spectral function is studied to identify the kinematic domains of the Unitary and Landau cuts.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 20.72\) GeV\(^{-2}\) is determined from the decay width \(\Gamma_{\rho\to\pi\pi} = 155.8\) MeV.
- The coupling \(F_\rho = 156\) MeV is obtained from the decay rate \(\Gamma_{\rho^0\to e^{+}e^{-}} = 7.0\) keV.
- The effective Landau level dependent pion mass is \(m_l = \sqrt{m_\pi^2 + (2l+1)eB}\).
- The Unitary-I cut contributes in the kinematic region \(\sqrt{q_z^2 + 4(m_\pi^2 + eB)} < q^0 < \infty\).
- The non-vanishing kinematic domain for the Landau cuts in the \(q_\perp=0\) case is \(|q^0| < \sqrt{q_z^2 + (\sqrt{m_\pi^2+eB} - \sqrt{m_\pi^2+3eB})^2}\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dilepton production from hot hadronic matter (Gale and Kapusta, 1988; Gale and Kapusta, 1990) by incorporating a constant background magnetic field, which is relevant for non-central heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC where magnetic fields of order \(10^{18}\) Gauss may be generated (Kharzeev, 2007; Skokov, 2009). The study also builds on prior work examining the modification of the dilepton production rate from the QGP medium in the presence of magnetic fields (Tuchin, 2012; Tuchin, 2013; Bandyopadhyay, 2016; Ghosh, 2018; Chaudhuri, 2021), but addresses the hadronic phase which dominates the low invariant mass region. The results enable future comparisons with experimental dilepton spectra from heavy ion collisions and may inform studies of magnetized matter in astrophysical contexts such as magnetars.
