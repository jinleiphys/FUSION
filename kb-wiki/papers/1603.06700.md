# Polarized-Deuteron Stripping Reaction at Intermediate Energies

> A general analytical expressions for the cross-section and the polarization of nucleons arising in the inclusive deuteron stripping reaction have been derived in the diffraction approximation. The nucleon-nucleus phases were calculated in the framework of Glauber formalism and making use of the double-folding potential. The tabulated distributions of the target nucleus density and the realistic deuteron wave function with correct asymptotic at large nucleon-nucleon distances were used. The calculated angular dependences for the cross-sections and the analyzing powers of the $(\vec{d},p)$ reaction are in good agreement with corresponding experimental data.

# Polarized-Deuteron Stripping Reaction at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim

The paper derives general analytical expressions for the cross-section and polarization of nucleons in inclusive deuteron stripping reactions within the diffraction approximation, but the applicability of this approach is confined to proton emission angles below approximately 30–35 degrees for the specific cases of 56 MeV deuterons on 24Mg and 40Ca targets.

## Method

The density matrix for the stripping reaction was calculated analytically by expanding the deuteron wave function and the nucleon-nucleus profile functions in series of Gaussian basis functions, reducing the multiple integrals to algebraic sums. The nucleon-nucleus scattering phases were computed using the Glauber formalism combined with a double-folding potential, employing tabulated target nucleus density distributions and a realistic deuteron wave function (Nijm I potential) with correct asymptotic behavior at large nucleon-nucleon distances. The cross-sections and analyzing powers were then obtained from the traces of the density matrix and compared with experimental data for the (d,p) reaction on 24Mg and 40Ca at 56 MeV, using a single fitting parameter (the normalization of the imaginary part of the double-folding potential).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energy: T = 56 MeV
- Spin-orbit parameters for 24Mg target: δ₁ = 0.12, γ₁ = 0.07 (for proton energy T₁ = 28 MeV)
- Spin-orbit parameters for 40Ca target: δ₁ = 0.13, γ₁ = 0.08 (for proton energy T₁ = 28 MeV)
- Deuteron beam polarization: P_d ≈ 0.5 (from Hat84) or 0.52 (from Uoz94)
- Normalization parameter for imaginary part of double-folding potential: N_W = 0.24 for 24Mg, N_W = 0.19 for 40Ca
- Number of Gaussian basis terms in expansions: N = 12
- Applicability region: emission angles θ < (30–35) degrees for the systems studied
- Parameter for model deuteron wave function (incorrect asymptotic): ξ = 0.049 fm⁻²

## Context

This work extends the author's previous approach (Kovalchuk 2015) from unpolarized to polarized deuteron stripping reactions, building on the foundational formalism of Serber (1947) and Akhiezer and Sitenko (1957, 1990). The results are compared with experimental data from Hat (1984) and Uoz (1994) for the (d,p) reaction on 24Mg and 40Ca at 56 MeV, and with calculations using the adiabatic distorted wave approximation from those same experimental works. The derived analytical expressions can be applied not only to deuteron stripping but also to deuteron, light-ion, and heavy-ion breakup processes, enabling calculation of both angular and energy distributions of emitted particles.
