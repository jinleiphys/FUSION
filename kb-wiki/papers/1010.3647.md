# Three body systems with strangeness and exotic systems

> We report on four $Σ$'s and three $Λ$'s, in the 1500 - 1800 MeV region, as two meson - one baryon S-wave $(1/2)^+$ resonances found by solving the Faddeev equations in the coupled channel approach, which can be associated to the existing $S$ = -1, $J^P= 1/2^+$ low lying baryon resonances. On the other hand we also report on a new, hidden strangeness $N^*$ state, mostly made of $K \bar{K} N$, with mass around 1920 MeV, which we think could be responsible for the peak seen in the $γp \to K^+ Λ$ around this energy. Finally we address a very novel topic in which we show how few body systems of several $ρ$ mesons can be produced, with their spins aligned up to J=6, and how these states found theoretically can be associated to several known mesons with spins J=2,3,4,5,6.

# Three body systems with strangeness and exotic systems

## Key claim
The authors report that all low-lying $J^P = 1/2^+$ $\Sigma$ and $\Lambda$ resonances up to 1800 MeV can be dynamically generated as two meson-one baryon states using chiral dynamics in the Faddeev equations, but the identification relies on associating theoretical peaks in the squared $T$-matrix with existing PDG resonances, with some states (e.g., $\Sigma(1560)$) having no previously assigned quantum numbers. The hidden strangeness $N^*$ state near 1920 MeV is suggested as the cause of the peak in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$, but this is a candidate explanation pending experimental confirmation via polarization measurements.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for three-body systems of two pseudoscalar mesons and one baryon, using chiral unitary dynamics to obtain the two-body $t$-matrices for meson-meson and meson-baryon interactions. They employed on-shell factorization of the chiral amplitudes, showing that off-shell contributions cancel in the SU(3) limit, and converted the coupled integral equations into algebraic equations by evaluating terms with two, three, and four interactions exactly. For the multirho states, they used the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, iteratively adding one $\rho$ meson at a time starting from the $\rho\rho$ interaction that generates the $f_2(1270)$.

## Key numbers
- Four $\Sigma$ states found in the $\pi \pi \Sigma$ amplitude with total isospin $I=1$: $\Sigma(1590)$ with $\Gamma = 70$ MeV, $\Sigma(1630)$ with $\Gamma = 39$ MeV, $\Sigma(1656)$ with $\Gamma = 30$ MeV, $\Sigma(1790)$ with $\Gamma = 24$ MeV.
- Two $\Lambda$ states found: $\Lambda(1568,1700)$ with $\Gamma = 60-136$ MeV, $\Lambda(1740)$ with $\Gamma = 20$ MeV.
- A hidden strangeness $N^*$ state with mass around 1924 MeV and width 20 MeV, mostly made of $K \bar{K} N$, found in the $N\pi\pi$, $N\pi\eta$, $NK\bar{K}$ coupled channels.
- Multirho states associated to known mesons: $\rho_3(1690)$ ($3^{--}$), $f_4(2050)$ ($4^{++}$), $\rho_5(2350)$ ($5^{--}$), $f_6(2510)$ ($6^{++}$), with masses matching experimental values from the PDG.

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously successful for $J^P = 1/2^-$ baryon resonances (Kaiser 1995, Jido 2003) to the $J^P = 1/2^+$ sector, which had been less understood theoretically and where quark models faced difficulties (Riska). The hidden strangeness $N^*$ state near 1920 MeV was first predicted using variational methods by Jido (2008) and is corroborated here; it is suggested as the explanation for the peak in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ seen by SAPHIR, Jefferson Lab, and Mizuki. The multirho states build on the finding by Molina (2008) that the $\rho\rho$ interaction in $I=0$, $J=2$ binds into the $f_2(1270)$, and the work enables experimental tests via polarization measurements at ELSA, MAMI B, or CEBAF, and via $K \bar{K}$ invariant mass distributions in $\gamma p \to K^+ K^- p$ at Spring8/Osaka.
