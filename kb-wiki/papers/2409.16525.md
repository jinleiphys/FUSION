# Spinodal decomposition in Bjorken flow

> The QCD first-order phase transition at large baryon densities is expected to proceed by spinodal decomposition. This spinodal phase is likely to leave its signatures on the experimental observables measured in heavy-ion collision experiments. Identifying these signatures requires phenomenological models integrating surface effects resulting from the phase transition into the hydrodynamical description of the expanding quark gluon plasma. We write the equations of relativistic hydrodynamics with spinodal decomposition and solve it in on a background of Bjorken flow relevant for heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic hydrodynamic framework incorporating spinodal decomposition for the QCD first-order phase transition, but the numerical solutions are restricted to a simplified Bjorken flow with a single sinusoidal perturbation in baryon density and a small gradient-energy coefficient \(K = 5\times10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-4}\), chosen to keep fourth-order derivatives tractable.

## Method

The authors extend a stable QCD equation of state into metastable and unstable coexistence regions by polynomial interpolation of pressure and chemical potential at fixed temperature, constrained by continuity at the phase boundaries. They write a covariant energy-momentum tensor and baryon current that include a gradient term \(K (\nabla n)^2\) in the free energy, following the Cahn-Hilliard approach. The equations are solved numerically for boost-invariant Bjorken flow starting at proper time \(\tau = 10\) fm, with the baryon density initialized as \(n(\tau_x) = n_0(\tau_x) + n_{fl} \sin(k \xi)\) where the amplitude is chosen so the lowest density just touches the coexistence region.

## Key numbers

- The evolution starts at proper time \(\tau_x = 10\) fm.
- The gradient-energy coefficient is set to \(K = 5\times10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-4}\).
- The energy density violation of boost invariance is verified to be less than 3\%.
- The system is evolved through proper times from 10 fm to 13.5 fm, as shown in the baryon density plots (five curves from \(\tau = 10\) fm, 10.5 fm, 11.5 fm, 12.5 fm, and 13.5 fm).

## Context

This work extends the Cahn-Hilliard theory of spinodal decomposition (Cahn and Hilliard, 1958, 1959) to relativistic hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions, building on the authors' own earlier interpolation scheme for the QCD equation of state (Kapusta, 2021; Kapusta, 2024). The formalism is intended to be incorporated into existing heavy-ion collision models to identify observable signatures of the QCD first-order phase transition, which is expected to be explored by the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC and future FAIR experiments.
