# Derivation of a 1/r^2 potential term for QQ-bar in a potential model

> We clarify the difference among potential models so far proposed to explain mass spectra of heavy-light mesons via transformations of the vacuum. Applying our idea to QQ-bar quarkonium, we obtain the extra term, 1/r^2 with positive coefficient, other than non-relativistic potential terms expected for quarkonium.

## Key claim

The paper derives an extra \(1/r^2\) potential term with a positive coefficient for \(Q\bar Q\) quarkonium, which appears in the lowest non-trivial order of the \(1/m\) expansion when applying the Foldy-Tani-Wouthuysen (FTW) transformation to both heavy quarks. This result is conditional on using a unitary FTW transformation (which respects the true vacuum in heavy quark effective theory) rather than the non-unitary free-fermion-field (FFF) transformation adopted by many other models.

## Method

The authors apply the Foldy-Tani-Wouthuysen (FTW) unitary transformation to both heavy quarks in a \(Q\bar Q\) system, combined with a charge conjugate transformation on the anti-heavy quark. They expand the transformed Hamiltonian, wave function, and energy in powers of \(1/m_1\) and \(1/m_2\), and obtain eigenvalue equations at the sum of zeroth and first order in \(1/m\) for the case \(m_1=m_2=m\). The resulting equations contain a term proportional to \(V^2\), where \(V\) is the vector potential, which yields a \(1/r^2\) contribution near the origin.

## Key numbers

- The observed masses of the \(D_{sJ}\) mesons are \(2.317\) GeV and \(2.460\) GeV.
- The FTW-based model of Matsuki et al. (2005) predicts \(D_{sJ}\) masses of \(2.325\) GeV and \(2.467\) GeV, close to the observed values.
- The FFF-based model of Matsuki et al. (1988) predicts \(D_{sJ}\) masses of \(2.525\) GeV and \(2.593\) GeV.
- The FFF-based model of Zeng et al. (1993) predicts \(D_{sJ}\) masses of \(2.38\) GeV and \(2.51\) GeV.
- The FFF-based model of Eichten et al. (1998) predicts \(D_{sJ}\) masses of \(2.463\) GeV and \(2.535\) GeV.
- The model of Godfrey and Kokoski (1991) predicts \(D_{sJ}\) masses of \(2.48\) GeV and \(2.55\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic potential model for heavy-light mesons (Matsuki and Morii, 1997; Matsuki et al., 2005) to the \(Q\bar Q\) quarkonium system. It clarifies that the choice of transformation (unitary FTW versus non-unitary FFF) determines whether the true vacuum of heavy quark effective theory is respected, and that this choice significantly affects predicted meson masses. The derived \(1/r^2\) term with positive coefficient contrasts with lattice results (Koma et al., 2006) where a negative coefficient would cause a critical value problem, and enables solving the eigenvalue equations without such difficulties.
