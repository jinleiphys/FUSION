# Sensitivity of one-neutron knockout observables of loosely- to more deeply-bound nuclei

> For the last few decades, one-nucleon knockout reactions on light composite targets -- $^9$Be or $^{12}$C -- have been extensively used to study the single-particle (s.p.) structure of nuclei far from stability. To determine which information can be accurately inferred from knockout cross sections, we conduct a sensitivity analysis of these observables considering various s.p. descriptions within the usual eikonal description of the reaction. This work shows that total one-neutron knockout cross sections are not sensitive to the short-range part of the s.p. wave function. Rather, they scale with the mean square radius of the overlap function. Using a perturbative expression of the cross section, we can easily explain our numerical predictions analytically. This analysis suggests that (i) spectroscopic factors extracted from knockout data suffer from sizeable model uncertainties associated with the choice of s.p. wave functions and (ii) knockout reactions constitute an excellent probe of the radius of the nucleus and therefore offer an alternative technique to infer the neutron-skin thickness of exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

Total one-neutron knockout cross sections are not sensitive to the short-range part of the single-particle wave function but instead scale linearly with the mean square radius of the overlap function; this holds across binding energies from loosely-bound (halo) to deeply-bound nuclei, though small deviations from linearity appear for deeply-bound cases due to higher-order effects.

## Method

The authors computed one-neutron knockout cross sections within the eikonal approximation for a model projectile (¹¹Be) with a valence neutron bound by either 0.5 MeV (halo) or 10 MeV (deeply-bound), using a Gaussian core-neutron potential and optical potentials for projectile-target interactions. They systematically applied a radial cutoff \(r_{\rm min}\) to the single-particle wave function to test sensitivity to the short-range interior, and derived perturbative expansions of the stripping and diffractive-breakup cross sections to second order to explain the observed scaling with mean square radius analytically.

## Key numbers

- For the loosely-bound projectile (\(S_{\rm n}=0.5\) MeV), knockout cross sections are unchanged for cutoffs up to \(r_{\rm min}\approx 2.5\) fm; for the deeply-bound projectile (\(S_{\rm n}=10\) MeV), this value reduces to \(r_{\rm min}\approx 1.5\) fm.
- The spectroscopic factor shows visible reduction when the wave function is set to zero below 0.5 fm, whereas cross sections are unaffected at that cutoff.
- The linear scaling of knockout cross sections with mean square radius \(\langle r^2 \rangle\) is verified numerically for both \(S_{\rm n}=0.5\) MeV and \(S_{\rm n}=10\) MeV, with small deviations from linearity observed for the deeply-bound case.
- The analysis covers neutron separation energies from \(S_{\rm n}\sim 0.5\)–20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous sensitivity studies on one-neutron halo nuclei (Hebborn and Capel, 2019, 2021) to more deeply-bound projectiles, showing that the peripherality of knockout observables persists but with a reduced radial range. The results directly address the long-standing puzzle of the asymmetry dependence of the quenching factor \(R_s\) observed in knockout experiments (Gade, 2008; Tostevin and Gade, 2021), which has been attributed either to reaction-model inaccuracies (e.g., Hebborn et al., 2022) or to missing correlations in shell-model descriptions. The finding that knockout cross sections scale with mean square radius rather than spectroscopic factor suggests that neutron radii and neutron-skin thicknesses of exotic nuclei could be extracted from knockout data, providing an alternative to methods like coherent pion photoproduction which carry significant uncertainty (Colonna, 2022).
