# Single particle spectra based of modern effective interactions

> The self-consistent Green's function method is applied to 16O using a G-matrix and Vucom as effective interactions, both derived from the Argonne v18 potential. The present calculations are performed in a larger model space than previously possible. The experimental single particle spectra obtained with the G-matrix are essentially independent of the oscillator length of the basis. The results shows that Vucom better reproduces spin-orbit splittings but tends to overestimate the gap at the Fermi energy.

## Key claim

The self-consistent Green's function method applied to 16O with a G-matrix and V_UCOM effective interactions (both derived from Argonne v18) shows that the G-matrix yields single-particle spectra nearly independent of oscillator length, while V_UCOM better reproduces spin-orbit splittings but overestimates the gap at the Fermi energy; however, the V_UCOM results are not yet fully converged with respect to oscillator length, and three-body forces appear necessary to reproduce the whole spectrum.

## Method

The self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) method was applied to 16O using a Faddeev expansion of the irreducible self-energy that couples quasiparticles to particle-hole and particle-particle/hole-hole collective excitations described by dressed RPA (DRPA) equations. Calculations were performed in a model space of eight major harmonic oscillator shells with orbital angular momentum l ≤ 4, using a G-matrix and V_UCOM effective interactions both derived from the Argonne v18 potential (with Coulomb and charge-independence-breaking terms neglected). The Dyson equation was solved iteratively until the single-particle energies near the Fermi level converged to within 200 keV.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splitting for 0p orbits with G-matrix: 3.1–3.2 MeV (b_HO = 1.9–2.1 fm)
- Spin-orbit splitting for 0p orbits with V_UCOM: 4.1–4.7 MeV (b_HO = 1.6–2.1 fm)
- Spin-orbit splitting for 0d orbits with G-matrix: 3.4–3.6 MeV (b_HO = 1.8–2.1 fm)
- Spin-orbit splitting for 0d orbits with V_UCOM: 3.3–4.9 MeV (b_HO = 1.6–2.1 fm)
- Energy gap at Fermi level (ε_d5/2 − ε_p1/2) with G-matrix: 13.0 MeV (b_HO = 1.9 fm)
- Energy gap at Fermi level (ε_d5/2 − ε_p1/2) with V_UCOM: 15.4 MeV (b_HO = 1.9 fm)
- Experimental energy gap at Fermi level: 11.5 MeV
- Root-mean-square radius with G-matrix: 2.63 fm
- Root-mean-square radius with V_UCOM: 2.45 fm
- Particle number conservation at self-consistency with V_UCOM: 15.99 < A < 16.02

## Context

This work extends earlier SCGF calculations on 16O (Barbieri and Dickhoff, 2002) by using a larger model space and avoiding phenomenological corrections to single-particle energies. The results are compared to coupled cluster calculations (Coupled Cluster, 2005) that reported larger spin-orbit splittings for the 0p and 0d orbits by about 1.5 and 0.5 MeV, respectively, and to unitary-model-operator approach calculations (Fujii, 2004) that showed stronger oscillator-length dependence. The study enables future inclusion of more complex collective modes, such as coupling to the first excited 0+ state in oxygen, and highlights the need for three-body forces to reproduce the full experimental spectrum.
