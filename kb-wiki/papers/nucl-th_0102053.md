# On the possibility of observation of $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing in the $pn \to da_0$ reaction

> It is shown that, if isospin is not conserved in $a^0_0$- and $f_0$-meson mixing, forward-backward asymmetry arises in the reaction $pn\to da^0_0$. This effect increases near the reaction threshold. The asymmetry is estimated within the framework of a model in which the $a^0_0$-$f_0$ mixing is due to the virtual $π^0\leftrightarrowη$ transition and the difference in masses of the charged and neutral kaons in decay channels. The angular asymmetry near the threshold of the $pn\to da^0_0$ reaction was found to be large, of the order of $8÷15$ %.

## Key claim

The paper claims that forward-backward asymmetry in the reaction $pn\to da^0_0$ arises from isospin-violating $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing, and that this asymmetry is large (8–15%) near the reaction threshold, provided the mixing is dominated by mechanisms such as the virtual $\pi^0\leftrightarrow\eta$ transition and the mass difference between charged and neutral kaons in decay channels.

## Method

The authors model the $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing via two mechanisms: the $\pi^0\leftrightarrow\eta$ transition (Fig. 1b) and the $K\bar K$ decay channel (Fig. 1c), where isospin violation arises from the mass difference between $K^\pm$ and $K^0$ mesons. They compute the mixing vertex $\Lambda_{af}$ as the sum of these contributions, then calculate the differential cross section for $pn\to d\pi^0\eta$ using amplitudes $M_1$, $M_2$, and $M_3$ (Eq. 7) that include the $p$-wave production of $a_0$ (isospin-conserving) and the $s$-wave production via $f_0$ (isospin-violating). The asymmetry $A$ is evaluated from the angular distribution of the $\pi^0\eta$ system near threshold, using a Hulthen deuteron wave function and impulse approximation for the production amplitudes $M_a$ and $M_f$.

## Key numbers

- The mixing vertex from the $\pi^0\leftrightarrow\eta$ mechanism is $\Lambda_{\pi\eta} \approx (118 - 186\, i)\, {\rm MeV}^2$ (Eq. 5), using $\lambda_{\pi\eta} \simeq -5000$ MeV$^2$ and assuming $\Gamma_0 = 50$ MeV/$c^2$ for both $a_0$ and $f_0$ mesons.
- The mixing angle from this mechanism is $\sin\theta_{af}\simeq |\Lambda_{\pi\eta}/{\bar m}\Gamma_0 |\simeq 0.0045$, with $\bar m = 980$ MeV/$c^2$.
- The mass difference between charged and neutral kaons is $2m_{K^0}-2m_{K^+}\approx 8$ MeV/$c^2$, leading to $|\Lambda_{K\bar K}|\gg |\Lambda_{\pi\eta}|$ near the $K\bar K$ thresholds.
- The predicted forward-backward asymmetry $A$ for the $pn\to da^0_0$ reaction near threshold is $8\div 15$ % (Fig. 5), depending on the mass window of the $\pi^0\eta$ system (parameter $C=1$ or $C=2$).

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing, notably the dynamical mechanism suggested by Achasov (around 1980) and the interpretation of WA102 collaboration data by Close (1999) as evidence for such mixing. The paper proposes the $pn\to da^0_0$ reaction as a clean experimental test, contrasting with the $pn\to d\pi^0$ reaction discussed by Niskanen (1999), where the asymmetry is suppressed because the isospin-conserving process already yields an $s$-wave final state. The results enable future experiments at facilities like ANKE (COSY) to search for the predicted asymmetry, and also suggest testing $a_0$-$f_0$ mixing in the isospin-forbidden reaction $dd\to a^0_0\,{^4}{\rm He}$.
