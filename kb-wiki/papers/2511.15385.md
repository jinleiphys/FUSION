# Resolving the PREX-CREX puzzle in covariant density functional theory

> The PREX-CREX puzzle, an apparent tension between parity-violating electron-scattering results for $^{208}$Pb and $^{48}$Ca revealed by modern density functional analyses, has posed a major challenge for nuclear theory. We show that this puzzle can be resolved within covariant density-dependent point-coupling density functional theory by introducing an enhanced isovector tensor coupling. A nonrelativistic reduction identifies the resulting strong isovector spin-orbit interaction as the key mechanism that reconciles the PREX and CREX measurements.

## Key claim
The PREX-CREX puzzle can be resolved within covariant density-dependent point-coupling density functional theory by introducing an enhanced isovector tensor coupling, which generates a strong isovector spin-orbit interaction that reconciles the PREX-II and CREX measurements within their 1σ uncertainties.

## Method
The authors extended a density-dependent point-coupling (DDPC) covariant energy density functional by treating the isovector-tensor coupling ατT as a free parameter, starting from the well-calibrated PCF-PK1 functional. They constructed three new parametrizations (ZH-1, ZH-2, ZH-3) by fitting to the experimentally determined charge-weak form-factor differences ΔFCW at the measured momentum transfers for 208Pb and 48Ca, along with binding energies, charge radii, spin-orbit splittings, symmetry energy constraints, and pure neutron matter equation of state from chiral effective field theory. A nonrelativistic reduction of the covariant potential energy density was performed to identify the connection between tensor couplings and spin-orbit interactions.

## Key numbers
- ΔFCW208(q=0.3977 fm−1) = 0.041 ± 0.013 from PREX-II for 208Pb
- ΔFCW48(q=0.8733 fm−1) = 0.0277 ± 0.0055 from CREX for 48Ca
- ZH-1 predicts ΔFCW48 = 0.0327 and ΔFCW208 = 0.0284; ZH-2 predicts ΔFCW48 = 0.0329 and ΔFCW208 = 0.0280; ZH-3 predicts ΔFCW48 = 0.0326 and ΔFCW208 = 0.0282
- The isovector-tensor coupling ατT is enhanced to 6.967 fm2 (ZH-1), 9.195 fm2 (ZH-2), and 7.114 fm2 (ZH-3), compared to 0.535 fm2 in PCF-PK1
- The density-averaged isovector spin-orbit strength ⟨bIV⟩ increases from 35.0–36.7 MeV·fm5 in PCF-PK1 to 213–238 MeV·fm5 in the ZH family
- The symmetry energy slope L(ρsat) varies from 19.4 MeV (ZH-3) to 66.7 MeV (ZH-1) across the three parametrizations
- The symmetry energy at two-thirds saturation density Esym(2ρsat/3) ranges from 25.4 to 26.9 MeV across the ZH family

## Context
This work extends the strong isovector spin-orbit mechanism proposed by Yue (2024) in nonrelativistic Skyrme energy density functionals to a covariant relativistic framework, addressing concerns raised by Kunjipurayil (2025) about level crossings and loss of magicity in relativistic mean-field models. The resolution enables future parity-violating electron scattering experiments to further constrain neutron distributions, symmetry energy, and the isovector spin-orbit interaction, while establishing a consistent relativistic framework for incorporating this mechanism.
