# Superfluidity of $Λ$ hyperons in neutron stars

> We study the $^1S_0$ superfluidity of $Λ$ hyperons in neutron star matter and neutron stars. We use the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory to calculate the properties of neutron star matter. In the RMF approach, the meson-hyperon couplings are constrained by reasonable hyperon potentials that include the updated information from recent developments in hypernuclear physics. To examine the $^1S_0$ pairing gap of $Λ$ hyperons, we employ several $ΛΛ$ interactions based on the Nijmegen models and used in double-$Λ$ hypernuclei studies. It is found that the maximal pairing gap obtained is a few tenths of a MeV. The magnitude and the density region of the pairing gap are dependent on the $ΛΛ$ interaction and the treatment of neutron star matter. We calculate neutron star properties and find that whether the $^1S_0 $ superfluidity of $Λ$ hyperons exists in the core of neutron stars mainly depends on the $ΛΛ$ interaction used.

## Key claim

The existence and magnitude of $^1S_0$ superfluidity of $\Lambda$ hyperons in neutron stars depend critically on the specific $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction used; with the weak attractive interactions suggested by the Nagara event, the pairing gap is at most of order $10^{-4}$ MeV or absent, making $\Lambda$ superfluidity unlikely in observed neutron stars.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the TM1 and NL3 parameter sets to compute the composition, effective masses, and Fermi momenta of neutron star matter under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They solved the $^1S_0$ gap equation for $\Lambda$ hyperons using several three-range Gaussian $\Lambda\Lambda$ potentials based on Nijmegen models (ESC00, ND1, ND2, NSC97b, NSC97e, NSC97f, NFs, NSC97s) and an Urbana-type potential, with meson-hyperon couplings constrained by hyperon potentials from hypernuclear data including the Nagara event.

## Key numbers

* Maximal pairing gap $\Delta_F^{\text{max}} \approx 0.8$ MeV with the ESC00 potential using the TM1 parameter set.
* With ND1 and ND2 potentials, pairing gaps are of order $0.1$–$0.2$ MeV.
* With NSC97e, NFs, NSC97s, and Urbana potentials, pairing gaps are of order $10^{-4}$ MeV (TM1) or absent (NL3); no pairing appears for NSC97b and NSC97f potentials.
* For TM1 (NL3) with ESC00: $\Lambda$ threshold density $\approx 0.31$ fm$^{-3}$ ($0.28$ fm$^{-3}$); maximal gap at $\rho_B \sim 0.34$ fm$^{-3}$ ($0.30$ fm$^{-3}$); gap vanishes at $\rho_B < 0.46$ fm$^{-3}$ ($0.38$ fm$^{-3}$).
* Maximum neutron star mass: $1.70 M_\odot$ (TM1) and $2.06 M_\odot$ (NL3).
* With ESC00 potential: $\Lambda$ superfluidity exists in stars with $1.37 M_\odot < M < 1.63 M_\odot$ (TM1) and $1.50 M_\odot < M < 1.82 M_\odot$ (NL3); above these masses, normal (nonsuperfluid) $\Lambda$ hyperons also appear in the core.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $\Lambda$ superfluidity by Balberg and Barnea (1998), Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1999, 2000), Tanigawa et al. (2003), and Takatsuka et al. (2006), which used nonrelativistic treatments or older $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions. The key advance is incorporating the weak $\Lambda\Lambda$ attraction implied by the Nagara event (2001) and using RMF theory with updated hyperon potentials. The results show that the weak interactions favored by the Nagara event suppress or eliminate $\Lambda$ superfluidity, which would significantly affect predictions for neutron star cooling via suppression of the direct Urca process.
