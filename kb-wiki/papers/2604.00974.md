# Electromagnetic form factors of heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons

> We report calculations of space-like electromagnetic form factors and charge radii of pseudoscalar mesons, covering both light and heavy-light flavour sectors within a flavour-dependent Bethe-Salpeter framework.

## Key claim

The paper reports calculations of space-like electromagnetic form factors and charge radii for pseudoscalar mesons from light to heavy-light sectors, using a flavour-dependent Bethe-Salpeter kernel that includes vertex contributions beyond the standard Taylor coupling; the results for heavy-light mesons are predictions that agree with other theoretical approaches but lack direct experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled system of Schwinger-Dyson equations for the quark propagator and quark-photon vertex, together with the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson bound-state amplitude, using a flavour-dependent interaction kernel that incorporates additional vertex contributions via the function \(\tilde\alpha_T(q^2)\). The electromagnetic form factor is computed in the impulse approximation, where the photon couples to valence quarks, with the momentum partition parameter \(\eta\) tuned per flavour combination to avoid propagator singularities. Input current-quark masses are fixed at \(\mu=4.3\) GeV: \(m_{u/d}=0.005\) GeV, \(m_s=0.094\) GeV, \(m_c=1.1\) GeV, \(m_b=3.5\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* Pion charge radius: \(0.656(5)\) fm, compared to experimental value \(0.659(4)\) fm
* Kaon charge radius: \(0.568(4)\) fm, compared to experimental value \(0.560(30)\) fm
* Neutral kaon squared charge radius: \(0.270\,i\) fm (negative squared radius), compared to experimental \(0.277(18)\,i\) fm
* D meson charge radius: \(0.428\) fm, compared to lattice QCD value \(0.450(24)\) fm
* D_s meson charge radius: \(0.368\) fm, compared to lattice QCD value \(0.465(57)\) fm
* B meson charge radius: \(0.631\) fm, compared to lattice QCD value \(0.692(21)\) fm
* B_c meson charge radius: \(0.213\) fm
* \(\eta_c\) single-quark charge radius (benchmark): \(0.267\) fm
* \(\eta_b\) single-quark charge radius (benchmark): \(0.082\) fm

## Context

This work extends the SDE/BSE approach previously applied to light mesons (Eichmann 2016, Sanchis-Alepuz 2017) into the heavy-light sector, where flavour asymmetry introduces additional complications. The results are compared to rainbow-ladder calculations (Xu 2024) and light-front model predictions (Xu 2025), as well as lattice QCD results (Wang 2020, Can 2012, Vujmilovic 2025). The calculations provide predictions for heavy-light meson form factors and charge radii that can be tested by future experimental facilities, and the \(\eta_c\) and \(\eta_b\) benchmarks offer reference points for heavy-heavy bound-state studies.
