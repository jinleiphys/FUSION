# $J/ψ$ near threshold in holographic QCD: A and D gravitational form factors

> The diffractive photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ on a nucleon, is mostly due to gluonic exchanges at all $\sqrt{s}$. In holographic QCD (large number of colors and strong $^\prime$t Hooft coupling), these exchanges are captured by gravitons near threshold, and their Reggeized Pomeron form asymptotically. We revisit our holographic analysis of the A and D gravitational form factors in light of the new lattice data, and use them to refine our predictions for the photoproduction of $J/Ψ$ near threshold, and the comparison to the GlueX data. We use these results to estimate the scalar and mass radii of the nucleon, and describe the gravitational pressure and shear across a nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors refine their previous holographic QCD predictions for the A and D gravitational form factors of the nucleon by fitting to newly released lattice QCD data from Pefkou (2021), and use these updated form factors to reanalyze the GlueX threshold photoproduction data for J/psi, finding that the D-term contribution is suppressed by the squared skewness of the process.

## Method

The authors compute the A and D gravitational form factors using holographic QCD in the soft wall model, where the A-term arises from spin-2 graviton exchange (dual to 2++ tensor glueballs) and the D-term from the difference between tensor and scalar glueball Regge trajectories. They fit the holographic parameters (kappa_T = 0.388 GeV fixed by the rho meson trajectory, A(0) = 0.430, and kappa_S varied between 0.217 GeV and 0.388 GeV) to the lattice QCD data of Pefkou (2021). The electroproduction amplitude for J/psi near threshold is then constructed from graviton exchange in the bulk, with the A and D form factors entering the transverse and longitudinal amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* A(0) = 0.430 from holographic fit to lattice data of Pefkou (2021), compared to A(0) = 0.429 from the lattice tripole fit
* D(0) = -1.275 from holographic QCD with kappa_S = 0.217 GeV, compared to D(0) = -1.930 from the lattice tripole fit of Pefkou (2021)
* kappa_T = 0.388 GeV (fixed by rho meson Regge trajectory), kappa_S = 0.217 GeV (finite N_c) or kappa_S = 0.388 GeV (large N_c limit with D(0)=0)
* Gluonic mass radius r_GM = 0.682 +/- 0.012 fm from holographic QCD, compared to r_GM = 0.747 +/- 0.033 fm from lattice tripole fit
* Gluonic scalar radius r_GS = 0.926 +/- 0.008 fm from holographic QCD, compared to r_GS = 1.073 +/- 0.033 fm from lattice tripole fit
* m_TT = 1.612 GeV (tripole approximation for A form factor), m_SS = 0.963 GeV (tripole approximation for D form factor) from holographic QCD

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic analysis (Mamo and Zahed, 2019; Mamo and Zahed, 2021) by incorporating new lattice QCD data from Pefkou (2021) for the gravitational form factors, improving the determination of A(0) and D(0). The holographic approach connects the A and D form factors through the difference between tensor and scalar glueball trajectories, which are degenerate at leading order in 1/N_c but split at finite N_c. The results enable extraction of gluonic pressure and shear distributions inside the proton, and provide a framework for interpreting threshold photoproduction data from the GlueX collaboration at JLAB. The work contrasts with earlier lattice fits by Shanahan (2018) which gave significantly different values (A(0)=0.580, D(0)=-10.000, r_GS=1.945^2 fm^2).
