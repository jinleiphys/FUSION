# Diquarks as Inspiration and as Objects

> It is plausible that several of the most profound aspects of low-energy QCD dynamics are connected to diquark correlations, including: paucity of exotics (which is the foundation of the quark model and of traditional nuclear physics), similarity of mesons and baryons, color superconductivity at high density, hyperfine splittings, $ΔI = 1/2$ rule, and some striking features of structure and fragmentation functions. After a brief overview of these issues, I discuss how diquarks can be studied in isolation, both phenomenologically and numerically, and present approximate mass differences for diquarks with different quantum numbers. The mass-loaded generalization of the Chew-Frautschi formula provides an essential tool.

# Diquarks as Inspiration and as Objects

## Key claim

Diquark correlations in the color-antitriplet, spin-0, flavor-antisymmetric ("good") channel provide a unifying framework for understanding several profound aspects of low-energy QCD dynamics, but the paper presents this as a plausible exploratory activity rather than a proven mechanism, noting that "the relevant calculations are not performed in a well-controlled approximation."

## Method

The paper derives a mass-loaded generalization of the Chew-Frautschi formula for a rotating relativistic string with massive endpoints, then applies it to baryon and meson spectroscopic data to extract effective quark and diquark mass differences. The author classifies nucleon and delta resonances according to whether they contain "good" diquarks [ud] (spin-0, isosinglet) or "bad" diquarks (ud) (spin-1, isotriplet), and uses the observed mass splittings between corresponding states to quantify diquark correlation energies.

## Key numbers

- The splitting between good and bad diquark states in non-strange baryons is about 200 MeV, visible from comparing the first column to the second and third columns of the resonance classification table.
- From the mass difference between N(1680) and Δ(1950), the quantity (ud)^{3/2} - [ud]^{3/2} = 0.28 GeV^{3/2}, implying (ud)-[ud] ranges from 360 to 240 MeV as [ud] ranges from 100 to 500 MeV.
- From the mass difference between Σ(2030) and Σ(1915), the quantity (us)^{3/2} - [us]^{3/2} = 0.12 GeV^{3/2}, implying (us)-[us] ranges from 150 to 100 MeV as [us] ranges from 200 to 600 MeV.
- The string tension σ ≈ 1.1 GeV², and the constant κ ≈ 1.15 GeV^{-1/2} in the mass-loaded Chew-Frautschi formula.
- The Σ-Λ mass difference is about 80 MeV, and the cleaner charm analogue Σ_c - Λ_c = 215 MeV.
- The Λ/Σ ratio from LEP data is about 10 at large z; total multiplicities in e⁺e⁻ annihilation give Λ_c:Σ_c = 0.100 ± 0.03 : 0.014 ± 0.007.

## Context

This work extends the diquark ideas previously applied to color superconductivity at high density and to the ΔI = 1/2 rule (as developed by Stech and Neubert) into a systematic spectroscopic analysis using Regge trajectories. It builds on Jaffe's earlier work on the H particle and on the bag model treatment of multiquark states. The paper enables quantitative extraction of diquark properties from existing data and makes specific predictions for missing resonances, including a spin-parity 5/2⁻ delta around 1700 MeV and a 7/2⁺ nucleon around 2000 MeV. The observed convergence between meson and baryon masses at large orbital angular momentum poses a challenge for Skyrme model or large-N approaches that treat mesons and baryons on vastly different footings.
