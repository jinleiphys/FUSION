# Exploring phases of dense QCD with compact stars

> I review a number of recent developments in the physics of compact stars containing deconfined quark matter, including (a)~their cooling with possible phase transition from a fully gapped to a gapless phase of QCD at low temperatures and large isospin, (b)~the transport coefficients of the 2SC phase and the role played by the Aharonov-Bohm interactions between flux-tubes and unpaired fermions; (c)~rapidly rotating compact stars and spin-down and spin-up induced phase transition between hadronic and QCD matter as well as between different phases of QCD.

## Key claim

The rapid cooling of the compact star in Cassiopeia A can be accounted for via a phase transition from the fully gapped 2SC phase to the gapless Fulde-Ferrell phase within the QCD phase diagram, but only after fine-tuning the phase-transition temperature to the specific value T* = 0.009385 MeV.

## Method

The author reviews models of hybrid compact stars containing a color-superconducting quark core surrounded by a nucleonic envelope, separated by a sharp interface. Cooling curves are computed for a stellar model with mass M/M_sun = 1.93, fitting the Cassiopeia A temperature data over a 10-year period from 2003 to 2013. Transport coefficients of the 2SC phase are computed using the Boltzmann equation in the transport relaxation approximation, with matrix elements computed to leading order in electromagnetic and strong coupling, assuming strongly degenerate matter with T << mu_q.

## Key numbers

- Pulsar masses in binary orbits with white dwarfs are inferred in the range 1.8 - 2.0 solar masses
- Pulsar radii are possibly in the range of 11 <= R <= 14 km
- The characteristic size of a baryon should be <= 0.84 fm (the measured charge radius of the proton)
- Cassiopeia A shows a decline in temperature of 2.9 +/- 0.9% over 10 years of observation
- The phase-transition temperature tuned to reproduce Cas A is T* = 0.009385 MeV
- For the 2SC phase, the Ginzburg-Landau parameter is kappa_2SC ≈ 11 * Delta/mu_q >= 1/sqrt(2)
- The critical field for formation of flux tubes is H_c1 ~ 10^17 G
- The thermal conductivity scaling for blue-d quarks is kappa_bd = 0.00617/(T/mu_q)
- The shear viscosity for electrons scales as eta_e ≈ 0.00231 (mu_q^4/5pi^2) alpha^{-5/3} T^{-1} (T/mu_q)^{-2}
- The electrical conductivity of electrons is sigma_e = 0.0433 (mu_q^2 e^2/pi^2) alpha^{-5/3} T^{-1} (T/mu_q)^{-2/3}
- The crossover from blue-d to electron domination for shear viscosity occurs at T/mu_q ~ 10^{-5}, corresponding to T ~ 10 keV
- The crossover for thermal conductivity occurs at T/mu_q ~ alpha/7.7 ~ 10^{-3}
- Spin frequencies considered range from 0 to 5.6 x 10^3 Hz

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier account (Sedrakian 2013) and discusses recent developments in compact star physics with deconfined quark matter. The cooling models build on prior work by Hess and Sedrakian (2011), Sedrakian (2013), and Sedrakian (2016), and compete with nucleonic models attributing rapid cooling to Cooper pair-breaking processes (Page 2011, Shternin 2011) or enhanced Urca rates via softened pionic modes (Schaab 1997). The transport calculations for the 2SC phase (Alford and Sedrakian 2014) and the Aharonov-Bohm scattering framework (Alford and Sedrakian 2010) provide a foundation for understanding how color-magnetic flux tubes affect transport coefficients. The spin-up and spin-down induced phase transitions extend earlier work on non-superconducting quark matter by Glendenning, Pei and Weber (1997) and subsequent studies, now incorporating color-superconducting phases (Alvarez and Sedrakian 2013).
