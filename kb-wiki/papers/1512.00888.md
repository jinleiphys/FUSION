# An analytic hydrodynamical model of rotating 3D expansion in heavy-ion collisions

> A new exact and analytic solution of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics is presented. It describes an expanding triaxial ellipsoid that rotates around one of its principal axes. The observables are calculated using simple analytic formulas. Azimuthal oscillation of the off-diagonal Bertsch-Pratt radii of Bose-Einstein correlations as well as rapidity dependent directed and third flow measurements provide means to determine the magnitude of the rotation of the fireball. Observing this rotation and its dependence on collision energy may lead to new information on the equation of state of the strongly interacting quark gluon plasma produced in high energy heavy ion collisions.

# An analytic hydrodynamical model of rotating 3D expansion in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
A new exact analytic solution of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics describes an expanding triaxial ellipsoid that rotates around one of its principal axes, with observables calculable via simple analytic formulas. The model claims that azimuthal oscillation of off-diagonal Bertsch-Pratt radii and rapidity-dependent directed and third flow measurements can determine the magnitude of rotation, which may provide information on the equation of state of the quark-gluon plasma, but only if the freeze-out parameters (such as tilt angle and cross-term slope parameters) are known from experimental data.

## Method
The authors solved the non-relativistic Euler equation, energy conservation, and particle number conservation equations for a perfect fluid with a linear, rotating Hubble-like velocity field in a co-rotating frame. They assumed a Gaussian density profile with spatially homogeneous temperature and a constant-time freeze-out condition, then analytically computed the single-particle spectrum and two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation function using a non-relativistic Boltzmann source function. The time evolution of the principal axes X, Y, Z and the rotation angle is governed by a Lagrangian for a point-like particle of mass m0.

## Key numbers
- The total angular momentum My of the flow is given by My = 2 M0 R0^2 omega0, where M0 = m0 (2pi)^(3/2) V0 n0
- The angular velocity omega(t) = omega0 * (R0/R)^2, where R = (X+Z)/2 and R0 = (X0+Z0)/2
- For the example calculation of v1, v2, v3: freeze-out parameters include theta_f = pi/5, beta'_xz = 5e-4 1/MeV, pT = 300 MeV/c, m = 140 MeV, Tx = 270 MeV, Ty = 170 MeV, Tz = 600 MeV
- For the HBT radii example: theta_f = pi/5, R'_x^2 = 25 fm^2, R'_y^2 = 16 fm^2, R'_z^2 = 36 fm^2, R'_xz^2 = 5 fm^2

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous exact solutions for non-rotating triaxial ellipsoids (Csorgo 2001) and rotating spheroids (Csorgo 2013, Csorgo 2015) to the more general case of rotating triaxial ellipsoidal expansion. It also builds on accelerating relativistic solutions (Csorgo 2006, Nagy 2007) and the first rotating relativistic exact solution (Nagy 2009). The model enables experimental determination of the rotation angle and angular velocity of the fireball through measurements of rapidity-dependent v1 and v3 anisotropy parameters and the 2pi-periodic oscillations of the R_ol and R_sl Bertsch-Pratt HBT radii, which could help locate the critical endpoint on the QCD phase diagram by studying rotation as a function of collision energy.
