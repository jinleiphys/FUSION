# Nuclear level densities and gamma-ray strength functions in samarium isotopes

> The gamma-strength functions and level densities in the quasi-continuum of 147;149Sm isotopes have been extracted from particle-coincidences using the Oslo method. The nuclei of interest were populated via (p,d) reactions on pure 148;150Sm targets and the reaction products were recorded by the Hyperion array. An upbend in the low-energy region of the gSF has been observed. The systematic analysis of the gSF for a range of Sm isotopes highlights the interplay between scissors mode and the upbend. Shell-model calculations show reasonable agreement with the experimental gSFs and confirm the correspondence between the upbend and scissors mode.

## Key claim

The low-energy upbend in the gamma-ray strength function is observed in the nearly spherical 147,149Sm isotopes, but the scissors mode is absent, consistent with the lack of deformation; the total M1 strength in the 0-5 MeV region remains fairly constant across the Sm isotopic chain, deviating by less than 13% from the average value.

## Method

The Oslo method was applied to particle-gamma coincidence data from (p,d) reactions on isotopically enriched 148,150Sm targets bombarded by a 28 MeV proton beam, with reaction products detected by the Hyperion array. First-generation gamma-ray matrices were constructed after unfolding the detector response using Geant4 simulations, and the level density and gamma-ray transmission coefficient were extracted via a chi-squared fitting procedure. The level densities were normalized to known discrete levels at low excitation energy and to the level density at the neutron separation energy estimated from s-wave neutron resonance spacings and systematics of neighboring odd-A Sm isotopes, while the transmission coefficients were normalized using the average total radiative width at the neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

- Level density at neutron separation energy for 147Sm: 0.31(5) x 10^6 MeV^-1 (estimated from systematics)
- Level density at neutron separation energy for 149Sm: 1.04(29) x 10^6 MeV^-1
- s-wave neutron resonance spacing D0 for 149Sm: 65(13) eV
- Constant temperature parameter T_CT for 147Sm: 0.58 MeV; for 149Sm: 0.48 MeV
- Upbend fit parameter C for 147Sm: 10(5) x 10^-7 MeV^-3; for 149Sm: 20(10) x 10^-7 MeV^-3
- Upbend fit parameter eta for 147Sm: 3.2(10) MeV^-1; for 149Sm: 5.0(10) MeV^-1
- Total B(M1) strength in 0-5 MeV region for 147Sm: 9.8(+16.7/-6.3) mu_N^2; for 149Sm: 7.2(+9.8/-4.2) mu_N^2
- Average total B(M1) strength across all four Sm isotopes: 8.27(+11.2/-4.7) mu_N^2

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of the low-energy upbend in the gamma-ray strength function across the Sm isotopic chain, following the previous measurements on 151,153Sm by Simon (2016). The results confirm the predictions of Schwengner (2018) that the total M1 strength remains fairly constant across the isotopic chain, and that the scissors mode appears only in deformed nuclei while the upbend persists even in nearly spherical isotopes. The findings enable better constraints on Hauser-Feshbach calculations for astrophysical neutron capture rates, particularly for extrapolating the gamma-ray strength function properties to neutron-rich Sm isotopes near the N=82 shell closure.
