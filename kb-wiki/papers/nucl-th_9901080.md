# Light-Front Quantum Chromodynamics

> Light-front quantum chromodynamics may lead to an accurate constituent approximation for the low-energy properties of hadrons. This requires a cutoff that violates explicit gauge invariance and Lorentz covariance, leading to the calculation of a renormalized QCD hamiltonian using a similarity renormalization group. Renormalization repairs broken symmetries, and in light-front field theory it moves dynamical effects that usually require large numbers of partons to few-body effective interactions. This has been shown to work in QED through dominant contributions to the Lamb shift. In QCD logarithmic confinement arises at second order, and initial bound state calculations produce reasonable results.

## Key claim

Light-front QCD may yield an accurate constituent approximation for low-energy hadron properties, but only if a cutoff is introduced that explicitly violates gauge invariance and Lorentz covariance, requiring a similarity renormalization group to compute a renormalized Hamiltonian. The constituent picture is presented as a "heretical conjecture" that guides calculations, and the approach is "completely fixed by QCD" so the constituent picture will fail if it is inadequate.

## Method

The author uses a similarity renormalization group transformation (Wegner's formulation) to run a cutoff on off-diagonal matrix elements of the light-front Hamiltonian, avoiding small energy denominators that plague traditional cutoffs. The renormalized Hamiltonian is computed as an expansion in powers of the canonical coupling to second order, with boundary conditions fixed by coupling coherence. Bound state problems are then solved using a nonrelativistic reduction of the effective Hamiltonian, treating the free part nonperturbatively and the remainder in bound state perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- In QED, the approach has produced "well-known results" through the Lamb shift (no explicit numerical value given for the shift itself).
- In QCD, "reasonable results for heavy quark bound states" have been obtained (no explicit binding energies or masses reported in the text).
- The Schwinger model (massless QED in 1+1 dimensions) is solved analytically and yields a single massive neutral scalar particle; in light-front coordinates the physical particle is a bound state of a single electron-positron pair with a wave function constant in the longitudinal fraction carried by either particle.

## Context

This work extends the light-front quantization program initiated by Dirac (1949) and developed by Brodsky and Lepage, applying renormalization group methods from Wilson (1965, 1970, 1974, 1975) and the similarity transformation of Glazek and Wilson (1993) and Wegner (1994). It directly contradicts the widely held view that the QCD vacuum has a complicated structure essential for confinement and chiral symmetry breaking; instead the author discards all zero-momentum modes and replaces their effects through renormalization. The approach enables first-principles bound state calculations in QCD without requiring the cutoff to approach infinity in the nonperturbative part, and glueball calculations are reported as being completed as the first fully relativistic QCD calculations in this framework.
