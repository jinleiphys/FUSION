# Bottomium suppression in PbPb collisions at LHC energies

> We substantially refine our previously developed model for the suppression of Upsilon mesons in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in 2.76 TeV PbPb collisions at the LHC. It accounts for gluodissociation of the six bottomium states Y(nS), chi_b(nP) with n = 1,2,3, collisional damping of these states as described in a complex potential, screening of the real part of the potential, and the subsequent decay cascade. In the hydrodynamical calculation of the expanding fireball, we take into account the effect of transverse expansion on the suppression factors, and finite transverse momenta of the heavy mesons in the medium. The running of the coupling is considered, resulting in larger gluodissociation decay widths. The initial central temperature is found to be 550 MeV at 0.1 fm/c. Our results are in good agreement with recent centrality-dependent CMS and ALICE data. The calculated suppression of the excited states relative to the ground state Y(1S) is not strong enough in peripheral collisions. Additional mechanisms are discussed. Predictions for 5.52 TeV are made.

# Bottomium suppression in PbPb collisions at LHC energies

## Key claim
The model for Upsilon meson suppression in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in 2.76 TeV PbPb collisions at the LHC achieves good agreement with centrality-dependent CMS and ALICE data, but the calculated suppression of excited states relative to the ground state Y(1S) is not strong enough in peripheral collisions, indicating that additional mechanisms beyond gluodissociation, collisional damping, and screening are needed.

## Method
The authors solve the temperature-dependent radial Schrodinger equation with a complex potential that includes color screening, collisional damping, and gluodissociation for all six bottomium states (Y(nS) and chi_b(nP) with n=1,2,3). They use an iterative procedure to account for the running of the strong coupling constant, which depends on the soft scale of each state. The fireball evolution is modeled with relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamics including transverse expansion, and the suppression factors are computed by integrating the total decay width (damping plus gluodissociation) along the bottomium trajectory through the medium, accounting for relativistic Doppler shifts and finite transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature: 550 MeV at 0.1 fm/c
- Bottom quark mass fixed from zero-temperature ground state: m_b = 4801 MeV
- Melting temperatures: Y(1S) at 655 MeV, chi_b(1P) at 273 MeV, Y(2S) at 320 MeV, chi_b(2P) at 206 MeV, Y(3S) at 228 MeV, chi_b(3P) at approximately 175 MeV
- Minimum bias R_AA for Y(1S) with formation time 0.5 fm/c: 0.57, compared to CMS value 0.56 ± 0.08 (stat) ± 0.07 (sys)
- Minimum bias R_AA for Y(2S) with formation time 0.1 fm/c: 0.10, compared to CMS value 0.12 ± 0.04 (stat) ± 0.02 (sys)
- Minimum bias R_AA for Y(3S) with formation time 0.1 fm/c: 0.04, compared to CMS value 0.03 ± 0.04 (stat) ± 0.01 (sys)
- Double ratio (Y(2S)/Y(1S))_PbPb/(Y(2S)/Y(1S))_pp with formation time 0.1 fm/c: 0.34, compared to CMS value 0.21 ± 0.07 (stat) ± 0.02 (sys)
- Predicted initial temperature at 5.52 TeV: 586 MeV (6.6% increase from 2.76 TeV)

## Context
This work substantially refines the authors' previous model (Nendzig and Wolschin, 2013) by including transverse expansion, finite transverse momentum effects, running coupling in wave function calculations, and the recently discovered chi_b(3P) state as proposed by Vaccaro et al. (2013). The complex potential combines results from Laine et al. (2007), Brambilla et al. (2008), Beraudo et al. (2008), and the non-perturbative ansatz of Karsch et al. (1988). The model enables predictions for bottomium suppression at 5.52 TeV PbPb collisions, where the ground state suppression is expected to increase by less than 10% compared to 2.76 TeV. The insufficient suppression of excited states in peripheral collisions points to the need for additional mechanisms beyond the three considered processes.
