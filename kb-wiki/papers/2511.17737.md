# Collinear spin correlations of final-state radiation in dense QCD matter

> Spin correlations are required to reproduce the correct azimuthal dependence of matrix elements for successive branchings at disparate angles in QCD jets. In this paper, we study modifications to this, $\cos(2ψ_{12})$, azimuthal pattern in the presence of a quark-gluon plasma. To that end, we consider a simplified setup in which a narrow and energetic QCD antenna is formed inside a medium of fixed length and radiates a collinear emission outside it. The calculation includes both light and heavy-quarks. Further, we do not include medium-induced spin-flip interactions since they are energy suppressed in our formalism. We show that the amplitude of the azimuthal modulation in the presence of a medium is always suppressed with respect to the vacuum baseline, with its magnitude depending on the medium properties and splitting kinematics. For a medium with a momentum space anisotropy, we find that the azimuthal modulation acquires a phase shift, i.e., $\cos(2ψ_{12}) \to \cos(2ψ_{12}+ϕ_{\rm med })$, where $ϕ_{\rm med}$ is a process-dependent function that again depends on the medium properties and splitting kinematics. This work provides theory guidance for implementing spin-driven interference effects in phenomenological studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The amplitude of the azimuthal modulation cos(2ψ₁₂) from spin correlations between successive QCD splittings is always suppressed in the presence of a quark-gluon plasma compared to the vacuum baseline, and for a medium with momentum-space anisotropy the modulation acquires a process-dependent phase shift cos(2ψ₁₂) → cos(2ψ₁₂ + φ_med), where φ_med depends on medium properties and splitting kinematics.

## Method

The authors consider a simplified setup in which a narrow, energetic QCD antenna is formed inside a medium of fixed length and radiates a collinear emission outside the medium, using the BDMPS-Z formalism with the medium modeled as a stochastic background color field. They compute the fully differential cross section for two successive collinear splittings (a → b g → b c d) in the strongly angular-ordered quasi-collinear limit, including both light and heavy quarks, while excluding medium-induced spin-flip interactions as they are energy-suppressed. The calculation is performed for both isotropic and anisotropic (momentum-space) media, with the anisotropy breaking rotational invariance while maintaining homogeneity.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum azimuthal modulation coefficient a is given by a ≡ S_{g→cd} × 4z₂(1−z₂)(1−z₁) / {z₁ [P_{a→bg}(z₁) + z₁ θ̃²_{m,1}] [P_{g→cd}(z₂) + θ̃²_{m,2}]}, where S_{g→gg} = +1 and S_{g→q\bar q} = −1
- For a massive g → Q\bar Q second splitting with θ̃_{m,2} = 0.1 (mass effects suppressed), spin correlations reach maximum around z₁ ≲ 0.1 and z₂ ∼ 0.5 for both q-initiated and g-initiated channels
- For θ̃_{m,2} = 2 (opening angle two times smaller than the dead-cone angle), spin correlations are depleted by almost a factor of 10 compared to the mass-suppressed case
- The medium-modified cross section takes the form (1+F_med)[1 + a_med cos(2ψ₁₂ + φ_med)], where in the vacuum limit 1+F_med → 1, a_med → a, and φ_med → 0

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-driven interference effects in QCD jets, previously explored in vacuum by Richardson (2018), Karlberg (2021), Hamilton (2021), and Höche (2025), to the case of jet fragmentation in a quark-gluon plasma, where such effects had not been previously investigated. Color interference effects in the antenna setup with two emissions have been studied by Mehtar-Tani (2010–2012), Casalderrey-Solana (2011–2012), and Barata (2021), among others, but the spin correlation case is new. The results provide theoretical guidance for implementing spin-driven interference effects in phenomenological studies of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, and the formalism for anisotropic media connects to work on glasma phase fields by Ipp (2020) and Carrington (2021), pre-equilibrium phase distributions by Boguslavski (2024), and matter gradients by Sadofyev (2021) and Barata (2022–2023).
