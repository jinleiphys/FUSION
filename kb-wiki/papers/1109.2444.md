# Effective Field Theories for Quarkonium and Dipole Transitions

> Effective field theories for quarkonium at zero and finite temperature provide an unifying description for a wide class of phenomena. As an example, we discuss physical effects induced by dipole transitions.

## Key claim
Effective field theories (EFTs) for quarkonium at zero and finite temperature provide a unifying description for a wide class of phenomena, but the paper focuses specifically on systems where the temperature is much lower than the typical momentum transfer in the bound state, and where the hierarchy \(Mv \gg T\) holds so that medium effects can be ignored when integrating out the scales \(M\) and \(Mv\).

## Method
The paper constructs non-relativistic effective field theories (NRQCD and pNRQCD) by systematically integrating out energy scales in the hierarchy \(M \gg p \sim Mv \gg E \sim Mv^2\) for quarkonium, and extends this to finite temperature by further integrating out thermal scales. The leading dipole interactions (chromoelectric, chromomagnetic, electric, and magnetic) are identified, and their effects on three observables are computed: the static quark-antiquark energy at zero temperature, the photon line shape in \(J/\psi \to X\,\gamma\) radiative decay, and the thermal width of \(\Upsilon(1S)\) in a medium at about twice the critical temperature.

## Key numbers
- Static quark-antiquark energy at N\(^3\)LL describes lattice data well up to distances of about 0.25 fm; extraction yields \(\Lambda^{n_f=0}_{\overline{\rm MS}} r_0 = 0.622^{+0.019}_{-0.015}\).
- Fitted \(\eta_c\) mass from \(J/\psi \to X\,\gamma\) line shape: \(M_{\eta_c} = 2985.9 \pm 0.6\) MeV (fit only, no theoretical errors).
- Fitted \(\eta_c\) width from \(J/\psi \to X\,\gamma\) line shape: \(\Gamma_{\eta_c} = 28.6 \pm 0.2\) MeV (fit only, no theoretical errors).
- Thermal width of \(\Upsilon(1S)\) up to \({\cal O}(m\alpha_s^5)\): \(\Gamma_{1S}^{(\mathrm{thermal})} = \frac{1156}{81}\alpha_s^3 T + \frac{7225}{162}E_1\alpha_s^3 + \frac{32}{9} \alpha_s T m_D^2 a_0^2 I_{1,0} - \left[\frac{4}{3} \alpha_s T m_D^2 \left(\ln\frac{E_1^2}{T^2}+ 2\gamma_E -3 -\ln 4- 2 \frac{\zeta^\prime(2)}{\zeta(2)} \right) + \frac{32\pi}{3} \alpha_s^2 T^3 \ln 2 \right] a_0^2\), where \(a_0 = \frac{3}{2M_b \alpha_s}\) and \(E_1 = -\frac{4 M_b \alpha_s^2}{9}\).
- Typical dissociation temperature for \(\Upsilon(1S)\) is about 450 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the systematic EFT framework for quarkonium developed by Brambilla, Pineda, Soto, and Vairo (2004) and others, and applies it to both zero-temperature and finite-temperature systems. It builds on NRQCD (Caswell 1985, Bodwin 1994) and pNRQCD (Pineda 1997, Brambilla 1999) to compute observables that can be compared with lattice data (Necco 2001) and experimental data (CLEO, Mitchell 2008; CMS, Silvestre 2011). The results enable precision studies of quarkonium ground states and provide a systematic understanding of thermal effects such as Landau damping and singlet-to-octet breakup, which are relevant for interpreting quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions.
