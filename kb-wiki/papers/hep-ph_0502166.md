# Pulsar kicks via spin-1 color superconductivity

> We propose a new neutrino propulsion mechanism for neutron stars which can lead to strong velocity kicks, needed to explain the observed bimodal velocity distribution of pulsars. The spatial asymmetry in the neutrino emission is naturally provided by a stellar core containing spin-1 color-superconducting quark matter in the A phase. The neutrino propulsion mechanism switches on when the stellar core temperature drops below the transition temperature of this phase.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that asymmetric neutrino emission from spin-1 color-superconducting quark matter in the transverse A phase inside hybrid neutron stars can generate strong velocity kicks (up to ~1000 km/s), potentially explaining the high-velocity component of the observed bimodal pulsar velocity distribution. The mechanism only activates after the stellar core temperature drops below the critical temperature of the A phase, meaning the kick occurs not immediately after the supernova but later during cooling.

## Method
The authors use the Kadanoff-Baym formalism to derive a general expression for neutrino emissivity in spin-1 color-superconducting phases, then specialize to the transverse A phase where quarks of the same flavor form Cooper pairs with total spin one. They calculate the imaginary part of the retarded W-boson polarization tensor from the quark propagator and vertices, obtaining both the total neutrino emissivity and the z-component of momentum carried away by neutrinos per unit volume. The velocity kick is then estimated using a model time dependence of the core temperature T(t) = T₀(t₀/t)^{1/4}, assuming the core cools through the transition temperature.

## Key numbers
- Maximum velocity kick expression: δv_max ≈ 0.033 α_s G_F² μ_e μ_u μ_d (4π/3) (R_c³ / 1.4 M_⊙) T₀⁴ T_c² t₀
- Input parameters used for numerical results: α_s = 1, T₀ = 100 keV at t₀ = 100 yr, μ_u = 400 MeV, μ_d = 500 MeV, μ_e = 100 MeV
- The function H(φ,φ) approximating the momentum asymmetry is given by a sum of five terms with coefficients h₁ = 0.3068, h₂ = -0.1977, h₃ = -0.7838, h₄ = 1.0286, h₅ = -0.3539, and r₀ = T_c/φ₀ ≈ 0.8125
- The shift in velocity curves at finite stellar age is about 10 keV at t = 10⁶ yr and about 30 keV at t = 10⁴ yr
- The star accumulates almost all of the maximum velocity kick in less than 10⁴ yr

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pulsar kick mechanisms (reviewed by Lai) and color-superconducting phases (reviewed in Ref. reviews), specifically building on the spin-1 color-superconducting A phase characterized by Schmitt (2004). It contrasts with mechanisms that produce kicks immediately after supernova explosions (Shklovsky) and proposes that the bimodal velocity distribution observed by Arzoumanian et al. (2002) and Brisken et al. (2003) results from an overlap of normal neutron stars and hybrid stars with A-phase quark cores. The mechanism predicts alignment of the kick velocity with the rotational axis, consistent with observational data from Romani (1996), and enables future tests of QCD at high density through pulsar observations.
