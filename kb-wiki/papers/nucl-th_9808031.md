# Gluon Field Dynamics in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions: Time evolution on a Gauge Lattice in 3+1 Dimensions

> We describe the dynamics of gluons and quarks in a relativistic nuclear collision, within the framework of classical mean-field transport theory, by the coupled equations for the classical Yang-Mills field and a collection of colored point particles. The particles represent the valence quarks in the colliding nuclei. We explore the time evolution of the gauge field in a numerical simulation of the collision of two Lorentz-boosted ``nuclei'' on a long three-dimensional gauge lattice. We report first results on soft gluon scattering and coherent gluon radiation.

## Key claim

The paper reports first numerical results on soft gluon scattering and coherent gluon radiation in a 3+1 dimensional gauge lattice simulation of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the initial conditions used to achieve stable propagating nuclei require unphysically high particle momenta (fictitious temperature of 100 GeV) and the simulation covers only 16 nucleons in a row rather than two full nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations coupled to colored point particles (representing valence quarks) on a 3-dimensional spatial lattice with continuous time, using SU(2) as the gauge group. The particles are evolved via Wong's equations, and the gauge fields are discretized using link variables in the temporal gauge, following the Kogut-Susskind formulation. Two Lorentz-boosted "nuclei" are prepared by randomly distributing color-charged test particles with Boltzmann-distributed momenta and evolving them to equilibrium before the collision, using a lattice of \(4^2 \times 12100\) points with spacing \(a = 0.3\) fm.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: \(a_j = 0.3\) fm in each spatial direction, giving a transverse cutoff momentum \(k_c = 2.0\) GeV.
- Kinetic energy per nucleon: 100 GeV/u, corresponding to Lorentz factor \(\gamma = 106.5\).
- At equilibrium for a single nucleus, the ratio of field energy to total energy ranges from \(W_{\rm f}/W_{\rm tot} = 0.45\) (for particle density \(n_b = 2\)) to \(W_{\rm f}/W_{\rm tot} = 0.14\) (for \(n_b = 10\)).
- After the collision, at time step 76000, about \(30.1\) GeV of the total transverse electric field energy \(W^{(E)}_{\rm t} = 490.7\) GeV (at time step 60000) is deposited in the spatial region between \(13.9\) fm and \(19.9\) fm.
- Within \(\Delta t = 0.5\) fm after the collision, about \(26\%\) (\(7.25\) GeV) of the total field energy (\(28.03\) GeV) left in the region between \(16.4\) fm and \(17.4\) fm is carried by longitudinal components of the E-field, with ratio \(w^{(E)}_{\rm l}/w^{(E)}_{\rm t} = 0.7\).
- The overlap time of the two nuclei is \(0.11\) fm.
- Soft gluon radiation into longitudinal directions amounts to about \(30\%\) of the intensity into transverse directions.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-dimensional studies of colliding Yang-Mills field wave packets by Hu and Mueller (1995) to three spatial dimensions, and is based on the non-Abelian Vlasov transport theory proposed by Heinz (1983) and Elze (1989). The approach enables first-principles simulation of soft gluon production in the non-perturbative regime of QCD, complementing perturbative calculations of gluon radiation from colliding quarks by Kovchegov (1997) and Matinyan (1997). Future work requires larger lattices to cover complete nuclei and inclusion of parton cascade effects for screening of soft gauge field components.
