# Comment on "Surprises in threshold antikaon-nucleon physics"

## Key claim

The chiral unitary approach fit (A₄⁺) proposed by Oller et al. (2005) to reconcile DEAR kaonic hydrogen data with K⁻p scattering data violates fundamental principles of scattering theory, specifically unitarity and causality, due to an unphysical isospin I=1 pole near the K⁻p threshold.

## Method

The authors reproduced the A₄⁺ fit of Oller et al. (2005) using an s-wave interaction kernel derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, next-to-leading order contact interactions, and direct and crossed Born terms. They then analytically continued the T-matrix into the complex √s-plane to examine pole positions, and checked the S-matrix modulus for the π⁰Λ → π⁰Λ channel below the first inelastic threshold. The phase shift variation with energy was compared against the Wigner causality bound.

## Key numbers

- Isospin I=1 pole found at √s = (1431 + 1.3i) MeV, right at the K⁻p threshold and above the physical region, with a width of about 3 MeV.
- The steep phase shift decrease in the π⁰Λ → π⁰Λ channel would require an interaction range of more than 300 fm to satisfy the Wigner condition, whereas the typical strong interaction range is roughly 1 fm.

## Context

This comment directly contradicts the claim of Oller et al. (2005) that DEAR kaonic hydrogen data and K⁻p scattering data could be reconciled in a chiral unitary approach. It extends the earlier analysis by Borasoy, Nissler, and Weise (2005) which had pointed to consistency questions between DEAR data and previous K⁻p scattering measurements. The work demonstrates that the unphysical subthreshold cuts arise from the on-shell formalism used in the coupled-channels approach, and that these artifacts would not appear in a full field-theoretical calculation. The finding enables future work to avoid such fine-tuned parameter combinations and to pursue complete field-theoretical treatments instead.
