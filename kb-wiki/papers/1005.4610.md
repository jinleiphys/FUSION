# Essence of the vacuum quark condensate

> We show that the chiral-limit vacuum quark condensate is qualitatively equivalent to the pseudoscalar meson leptonic decay constant in the sense that they are both obtained as the chiral-limit value of well-defined gauge-invariant hadron-to-vacuum transition amplitudes that possess a spectral representation in terms of the current-quark mass. Thus, whereas it might sometimes be convenient to imagine otherwise, neither is essentially a constant mass-scale that fills all spacetime. This means, in particular, that the quark condensate can be understood as a property of hadrons themselves, which is expressed, for example, in their Bethe-Salpeter or light-front wavefunctions.

## Key claim

The chiral-limit vacuum quark condensate is qualitatively equivalent to the pseudoscalar meson leptonic decay constant, in that both are obtained as the chiral-limit value of well-defined gauge-invariant hadron-to-vacuum transition amplitudes with a spectral representation in the current-quark mass; therefore, neither is essentially a constant mass-scale that fills all spacetime, and the condensate can be understood as a property of hadrons themselves.

## Method

The authors derive an exact QCD identity relating the pion mass, leptonic decay constant, and current-quark mass from the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. They then define an "in-pion condensate" as the product of the pion decay constant and the pseudoscalar projection of the pion's Bethe-Salpeter wavefunction onto the origin, and show via Goldberger-Treiman relations that the chiral limit of this in-pion condensate equals the conventional vacuum quark condensate. The analysis is performed using Dyson-Schwinger equations and the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for the pion bound state, with explicit consideration of the light-front formulation.

## Key numbers

- The measured pion leptonic decay constant is 92.4 MeV; chiral perturbation theory estimates the chiral-limit value \(f_\pi^0\) is about 5% below this value, while Dyson-Schwinger equation studies give a 3% chiral-limit reduction.
- The chiral-limit dressed-quark mass function at zero momentum is \(M(0) \sim 0.3\) GeV = 1/[0.66 fm].
- The dynamical gluon mass at zero momentum in quenched QCD is \(m_g^{\rm Q}(0) \approx 0.35\) GeV = 1/[0.55 fm]; with dynamical quarks it increases to \(m_g(0) \sim 0.45\) GeV.
- Both dynamical mass functions are similar at infrared momenta: \(M(0) \sim m_G(0) \approx 0.4\) GeV \(\approx\) 1/[0.5 fm].

## Context

This work extends the argument by Casher and Susskind (1974) that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking must be a property of hadron wavefunctions, not of the vacuum, and builds on the rigorous definition of an "in-hadron condensate" presented by Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997, 1998). It resolves the longstanding conundrum that a non-zero vacuum condensate appears incompatible with the light-front formulation of QCD, where the ground state is a structureless Fock space vacuum, and also avoids the cosmological constant problem that would arise from a spacetime-independent QCD condensate. The result enables a consistent light-front description of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking via higher Fock state contributions mediated by instantaneous quark propagators.
