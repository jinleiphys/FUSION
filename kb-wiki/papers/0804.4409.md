# Thermal conductivity and phase separation of the crust of accreting neutron stars

> Recently, crust cooling times have been measured for neutron stars after extended outbursts. These observations are very sensitive to the thermal conductivity $κ$ of the crust and strongly suggest that $κ$ is large. We perform molecular dynamics simulations of the structure of the crust of an accreting neutron star using a complex composition that includes many impurities. The composition comes from simulations of rapid proton capture nucleosynthesys followed by electron captures. We find that the thermal conductivity is reduced by impurity scattering. In addition, we find phase separation. Some impurities with low atomic number $Z$ are concentrated in a subregion of the simulation volume. For our composition, the solid crust must separate into regions of different compositions. This could lead to an asymmetric star with a quadrupole deformation that radiates gravitational waves. Observations of crust cooling can constrain impurity concentrations.

## Key claim
The thermal conductivity of the accreting neutron star crust is reduced by impurity scattering, but the reduction is larger than predicted by the standard impurity parameter formula because the impurities are not randomly distributed; instead, the solid crust phase-separates into regions of different compositions, which may invalidate the steady-state assumption that the crust composition equals the accreting material composition.

## Method
The authors performed classical molecular dynamics simulations of a 3456-ion multicomponent plasma using the composition from rapid proton capture nucleosynthesis followed by electron captures (Gupta et al. composition). They evolved the system at a reference density of 1e13 g/cm^3 over total simulation times up to 4e9 fm/c, calculated the static structure factor S(q) from the ion trajectories, and extracted the inelastic contribution S'(q) via a numerical filter to compute the Coulomb logarithm and thermal conductivity.

## Key numbers
- Observed crust cooling exponential time scales: 325 ± 100 days for KS 1731-260 and 505 ± 59 days for MXB 1659-29.
- Impurity parameter Q = 38.9 for the rp-process ash composition used.
- Coulomb logarithm Λ for rpcrust-05 at T=0.1 MeV: Λ = 0.173 (compared to Λ_OCPfit = 0.104 and Λ_OCP+imp = 0.146).
- Coulomb logarithm Λ for rpcrust-05 at T=0.2 MeV: Λ = 0.366 (compared to Λ_OCPfit = 0.232 and Λ_OCP+imp = 0.276).
- Coulomb logarithm Λ for rpcrust-05 at T=0.3 MeV: Λ = 0.530 (compared to Λ_OCPfit = 0.334 and Λ_OCP+imp = 0.377).
- Thermal conductivity κ at T=0.043 MeV (5e8 K) and density 7.91e11 g/cm^3: κ = 1.49e19 erg/K cm s (compared to κ_OCP = 2.48e19 erg/K cm s and κ_OCP+imp = 1.77e19 erg/K cm s).
- Thermal conductivity κ at T=0.043 MeV and density 9.89e10 g/cm^3: κ = 3.54e18 erg/K cm s (compared to κ_OCP = 5.58e18 erg/K cm s and κ_OCP+imp = 4.69e18 erg/K cm s).
- Thermal conductivity κ at T=0.043 MeV and density 2.92e10 g/cm^3: κ = 1.63e18 erg/K cm s (compared to κ_OCP = 2.58e18 erg/K cm s and κ_OCP+imp = 2.29e18 erg/K cm s).
- Simulation density: n = 7.18e-5 fm^{-3} (1e13 g/cm^3); one over plasma frequency 1/ω_p = 270 fm/c.
- OCP freezing at Γ=175; MCP freezing at Γ=247 (from prior work by the same authors).

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior molecular dynamics study of chemical separation during crust freezing (Horowitz et al. 2008) by now examining the solid crust structure and thermal conductivity. It contradicts the steady-state assumption that the crust composition equals the accreting material composition, and it shows that the impurity parameter formalism of Itoh and Kohyama (1993) underestimates the reduction in thermal conductivity because impurities are correlated rather than random. The results enable future work on gravitational wave emission from quadrupole deformations caused by phase separation, and they provide a framework for interpreting crust cooling observations from Wijnands et al. (2002) and Cackett et al. (2006) to constrain impurity concentrations.
