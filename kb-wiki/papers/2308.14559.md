# Renormalization of many-body effective field theory

> The renormalization of the effective field theories (EFTs) in many-body systems is the most pressing and challenging problem in modern nuclear ab initio calculation. For general non-relativistic EFTs, we prove that the renormalization group (RG) invariance can be achieved if and only if all single-particle momenta are regulated with a universal cutoff Λ. For a numerical demonstration, we construct a series of N^{2}LO chiral forces with Λvarying from 250 MeV to 400 MeV. With all low energy constants fixed in two- and three-nucleon systems, we reproduce the experimental binding energies of ^{4}He and ^{16}O nearly independently of Λ. In contrast, all recent nuclear EFT constructions regulate the relative momenta for Galilean invariance, thus inherently break the RG invariance. This explains the unpleasantly strong cutoff-dependences observed in recent ab initio calculations. Our method can also be used to build RG-invariant EFTs with non-perturbative interactions.

## Key claim

The authors prove that renormalization group (RG) invariance in non-relativistic many-body effective field theories can be achieved if and only if all single-particle momenta are regulated with a universal cutoff, explicitly breaking Galilean invariance; this contradicts the standard practice of regulating relative momenta, which the authors argue inherently breaks RG invariance and explains the strong cutoff-dependences observed in recent ab initio calculations.

## Method

The authors construct a series of N²LO chiral forces using a single-particle momentum regulator with a soft cutoff function \(g_\Lambda(p) = \exp(-p^6/2\Lambda^6)\), varying \(\Lambda\) from 250 MeV to 400 MeV. Low-energy constants are fitted to experimental neutron-proton scattering phase shifts below 200 MeV relative momentum and the triton binding energy. Ground-state energies of \(^3\)H, \(^4\)He, and \(^{16}\)O are computed using lattice EFT techniques with Lanczos algorithm and perturbative quantum Monte Carlo methods.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^3\)H fitted to experimental value \(-8.482\) MeV for all cutoffs
- \(^{16}\)O ground-state energy converges to experimental value with discrepancy smaller than \(2\) MeV for \(\Lambda \geq 300\) MeV
- Cutoff \(\Lambda\) varied from \(250\) MeV to \(400\) MeV in steps of \(25\) MeV
- Binding energies without three-nucleon force vary by about \(20\%\) over the considered cutoff interval
- Characteristic momentum in a typical nucleus estimated as \(Q \sim \sqrt{mE_B} \approx 100\) MeV, where \(E_B \approx 7\) MeV is the single-nucleon binding energy

## Context

This work extends the Wilsonian renormalization group framework to non-relativistic many-body systems, providing a theoretical explanation for the strong cutoff-dependence observed in recent chiral EFT calculations by Simonis (2017) and others. The authors demonstrate that the standard practice of regulating relative momenta for Galilean invariance, as used in constructions by Entem (2003), Epelbaum (2005), and Hebeler (2015), inherently breaks RG invariance. The method enables construction of RG-invariant EFTs with non-perturbative interactions and suggests that RG invariance may be realized at much lower orders and smaller cutoffs than previously expected.
