# Modeling Complex Nuclear Spectra - Regularity versus Chaos

> A statistical analysis of the spectrum of two particle - two hole doorway states in a finite nucleus is performed. On the unperturbed mean-field level sizable attractive correlations are present in such a spectrum. Including particle-hole rescattering effects via the residual interaction introduces repulsive dynamical correlations which generate the fluctuation properties characteristic of the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble. This signals that the underlying dynamics becomes chaotic. This feature turns out to be independent of the detailed form of the residual interaction and hence reflects the generic nature of the fluctuations studied.

# Modeling Complex Nuclear Spectra - Regularity versus Chaos

## Key claim

The spectrum of two particle–two hole (2p2h) doorway states in a finite nucleus, when including particle-hole rescattering effects via the residual interaction, exhibits fluctuation properties characteristic of the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), signaling chaotic dynamics, but this only occurs when the residual interaction is included; the unperturbed mean-field spectrum alone shows strong attractive correlations and clustering, not chaos.

## Method

The authors constructed a realistic mean field using a Woods-Saxon potential (diagonalized in a 16-shell harmonic oscillator basis) for the nucleus ⁴⁰Ca, and employed a zero-range Landau-Migdal residual interaction with empirical parameters. They generated the 2p2h basis for J^π = 0⁺ states (2696 states total) and computed the nearest-neighbor spacing (NNS) distribution after unfolding the spectrum with polynomials up to order 12, discarding the 50 lowest and 50 highest states. They compared the NNS distributions for three cases: unperturbed 2p2h energies, energies including pp and hh matrix elements only, and energies including ph rescattering matrix elements (diagram (b) in Fig. 1).

## Key numbers

- The maximum number of 2p2h states for any multipole in ⁴⁰Ca is 11720 (for J^π = 3⁻).
- A manageable number of 2696 2p2h states was found for J^π = 0⁺, which was used for the analysis.
- The Landau-Migdal parameters used were: f = -0.1, f' = 0.6, g = 0.15, g' = 0.7, with C₀ = 392 MeV fm³.
- Varying the interaction strength by factors between 1/5 and 2 left the NNS histogram essentially unchanged.
- A sequence of 400 states starting from the 51st state (covering excitation energies up to about 30 MeV) was selected for the Δ₃ statistics analysis.
- The amplitude of oscillations in the collision kernel was reduced by one order of magnitude when going from the unperturbed spectrum (Fig. 2a) to the GOE spectrum (Fig. 2c).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear dissipation as a consequence of chaotic motion, following the theoretical framework of Weidenmüller (Wei1) and the connection between dissipation and chaos established by both classical (SSD) and quantum (BRW) considerations. The fluctuation properties of compound nuclei were previously shown to be consistent with GOE (HPB; BFF), and the present paper quantitatively explores whether the 2p2h doorway state basis, central to extended RPA methods (DNS; Wam), can already support such GOE fluctuations. The results enable further investigation into whether nuclear collective motion is Markovian or non-Markovian (HSY; ADY), and suggest that the fine structure of resonances may be influenced by the chaotic nature of the 2p2h spectrum.
