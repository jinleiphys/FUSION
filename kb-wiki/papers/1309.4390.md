# A new family of exact and rotating solutions of fireball hydrodynamics

> A new class of analytic, exact, rotating, self-similar and surprisingly simple solutions of non-relativistic hydrodynamics are presented for a three-dimensionally expanding, spheroidally symmetric fireball. These results generalize earlier, non-rotating solutions for ellipsoidally symmetric fireballs with directional, three-dimensional Hubble flows. The solutions are presented for a general class of equations of state that includes the lattice QCD equations of state and may feature inhomogeneous temperature and corresponding density profiles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new class of exact, analytic, rotating solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics, but these solutions are limited to spheroidal symmetry (equal transverse axes) and non-relativistic kinematics, with the rotation axis fixed along the symmetry axis.

## Method

The authors solve the continuity, Euler, and energy equations for a non-relativistic perfect fluid by assuming a velocity field that combines a directional Hubble flow with a rigid rotation term having a time-dependent angular velocity. They consider two physical scenarios: one with a conserved particle number density (Case 1) and one with only entropy conservation and no conserved charge (Case 2), each further subdivided into constant or temperature-dependent ratio of energy density to pressure (kappa). The partial differential equations are reduced to ordinary differential equations for the time-dependent scale parameters R(t) and Z(t) using scaling variables that are comoving with the fluid.

## Key numbers

- Angular velocity follows omega = omega_0 * (R_0^2 / R^2), where R_0 is the initial transverse radius and omega_0 is the initial angular velocity.
- In Case 1A (conserved charge, constant kappa), the temperature evolves as T = T_0 * (V_0 / V)^(1/kappa), where V is the volume and V_0 its initial value.
- In Case 2A (no conserved charge, constant kappa), the acceleration term in the Euler equation is given by the constant 1/(1+kappa), independent of temperature.
- The total angular momentum M_z is proportional to m * omega_0 * R_0^2, establishing omega * R^2 = constant as a consequence of angular momentum conservation.

## Context

This work generalizes the non-rotating, ellipsoidally symmetric solutions of Csorgo et al. (2001) and Csorgo et al. (2001) to include rotation, and extends the approach of Csanad et al. (2012) for lattice QCD equations of state to rotating flows. The solutions enable explicit evaluation of rotation effects on observables in heavy-ion collisions with finite impact parameter, addressing a limitation of earlier exact solutions that lacked angular momentum. The authors note that these non-relativistic rotating solutions took 35 years after the first exact hydrodynamical solutions of Bondorf et al. (1978) to be discovered.
