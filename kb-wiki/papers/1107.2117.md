# Theory Uncertainties for Higgs and Other Searches Using Jet Bins

> Bounds on the Higgs mass from the Tevatron and LHC are determined using exclusive jet bins to maximize sensitivity. Scale variation in exclusive fixed-order predictions underestimates the perturbative uncertainty for these cross sections, due to cancellations between the perturbative corrections leading to large K factors and those that induce logarithmic sensitivity to the jet-bin boundary. To account for this, we propose that scale variation in the fixed-order calculations should be used to determine theory uncertainties for inclusive jet cross sections, whose differences yield exclusive jet cross sections. This yields a theory correlation matrix for the jet bins such that the additional uncertainty from large logarithms due to the jet boundary cancels when neighboring bins are added. This procedure is tested for H + 0, 1 jets, WW + 0 jets, and W + 0, 1, 2 jets, and found to be generally applicable. For a case where the higher-order resummation of the jet boundary corrections is known, we show that this procedure yields fixed-order uncertainties which are theoretically consistent with those obtained in the resummed calculation.

## Key claim

Standard scale variation applied directly to exclusive fixed-order jet-bin cross sections underestimates perturbative uncertainties because large K-factor corrections to the total cross section cancel against large logarithmic corrections from the jet-bin boundary; the authors propose instead to estimate uncertainties from scale variation on inclusive jet cross sections and propagate them to exclusive bins via quadrature, yielding a correlation matrix where boundary-induced uncertainties cancel when neighboring bins are summed.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of perturbative uncertainties for exclusive jet bins used in Tevatron Higgs analyses (Anastasiou et al., 2009) and LHC searches, where common scale variation produced 100% correlated uncertainties that underestimated the true uncertainty from jet-boundary logarithms. The proposed method provides a theory correlation matrix that enables consistent combination of jet-bin results in Higgs and new-physics searches, and is tested against resummed calculations at NNLL accuracy for gg‚ÜíH+0 jets, showing consistency. The approach is applicable to any process with large K factors and jet-veto logarithms, including WW+0 jets and W+0,1,2 jets.
