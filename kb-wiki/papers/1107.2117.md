# Theory Uncertainties for Higgs and Other Searches Using Jet Bins

> Bounds on the Higgs mass from the Tevatron and LHC are determined using exclusive jet bins to maximize sensitivity. Scale variation in exclusive fixed-order predictions underestimates the perturbative uncertainty for these cross sections, due to cancellations between the perturbative corrections leading to large K factors and those that induce logarithmic sensitivity to the jet-bin boundary. To account for this, we propose that scale variation in the fixed-order calculations should be used to determine theory uncertainties for inclusive jet cross sections, whose differences yield exclusive jet cross sections. This yields a theory correlation matrix for the jet bins such that the additional uncertainty from large logarithms due to the jet boundary cancels when neighboring bins are added. This procedure is tested for H + 0, 1 jets, WW + 0 jets, and W + 0, 1, 2 jets, and found to be generally applicable. For a case where the higher-order resummation of the jet boundary corrections is known, we show that this procedure yields fixed-order uncertainties which are theoretically consistent with those obtained in the resummed calculation.

## Key claim

Standard scale variation applied directly to exclusive fixed-order jet-bin cross sections underestimates perturbative uncertainties because large K-factor corrections to the total cross section cancel against large logarithmic corrections from the jet-bin boundary; the authors propose instead to estimate uncertainties from scale variation on inclusive jet cross sections and propagate them to exclusive bins via quadrature, yielding a correlation matrix where boundary-induced uncertainties cancel when neighboring bins are summed.

## Method

The authors treat the inclusive N-jet cross section σ≥N and the inclusive (N+1)-jet cross section σ≥N+1 as having independent perturbative series with uncorrelated scale uncertainties Δ≥N and Δ≥N+1, each obtained by varying renormalization and factorization scales by a factor of two around the central scale. The exclusive N-jet cross section is computed as σN = σ≥N − σ≥N+1, and its uncertainty is taken as ΔN² = Δ≥N² + Δ≥N+1²; the covariance matrix for neighboring bins then contains an anticorrelation −Δ≥N+1² that ensures the total uncertainty is recovered when bins are summed. The method is tested using NLO and NNLO fixed-order predictions from MCFM and FEHiP for gg→H, WW, and W production at the Tevatron and LHC.

## Key numbers

* For gg→H (mH=165 GeV) at the Tevatron with pTjet≥20 GeV and |ηjet|≤2.5: σtot = (0.386 ± 0.040) pb, σ≥1 = (0.132 ± 0.034) pb; combining gives σ0 = (0.254 ± 0.052) pb (20% uncertainty), whereas direct exclusive scale variation gives σ0 = (0.254 ± 0.006) pb.
* For gg→H (mH=165 GeV) at the LHC (7 TeV) with reference cuts: σtot = (8.70 ± 0.75) pb, σ≥1 = (3.08 ± 0.59) pb; combining gives σ0 = (5.63 ± 0.96) pb (17% uncertainty), versus direct exclusive variation giving σ0 = (5.63 ± 0.15) pb.
* For gg→H at the LHC with pT1jet≥30 GeV and pT2jet≥30 GeV: δ(σ0)=18%, δ(σ1)=32%, ρ(σ0,σ1)=−0.50, ρ(σ1,σ≥2)=−0.62; direct exclusive variation gives δ(σ0)=2.3% and δ(σ1)=5.5%.
* For gg→H at the LHC with pT1jet≥120 GeV and pT2jet≥60 GeV: the αs terms in σ1 cancel around pT2jet≥70 GeV.
* For gg→H at the LHC (7 TeV): σtot = (3.32 pb)[1 + 9.5αs + 35αs² + O(αs³)]; σ≥1(pTjet≥30 GeV, |ηjet|≤3.0) = (3.32 pb)[4.7αs + 26αs² + O(αs³)].
* For gg→H at the Tevatron: σtot = (0.15 pb)[1 + 9.0αs + 34αs² + O(αs³)]; σ≥1(pTjet≥20 GeV, |ηjet|≤2.5) = (0.15 pb)[4.1αs + 27αs² + O(αs³)].

## Context

This work extends the treatment of perturbative uncertainties for exclusive jet bins used in Tevatron Higgs analyses (Anastasiou et al., 2009) and LHC searches, where common scale variation produced 100% correlated uncertainties that underestimated the true uncertainty from jet-boundary logarithms. The proposed method provides a theory correlation matrix that enables consistent combination of jet-bin results in Higgs and new-physics searches, and is tested against resummed calculations at NNLL accuracy for gg→H+0 jets, showing consistency. The approach is applicable to any process with large K factors and jet-veto logarithms, including WW+0 jets and W+0,1,2 jets.
