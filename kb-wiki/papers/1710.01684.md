# Pinning down the linearly-polarised gluons inside unpolarised protons using quarkonium-pair production at the LHC

> We show that the production of J/psi or Upsilon pairs in unpolarised pp collisions is currently the best process to measure the momentum-distribution of linearly-polarised gluons inside unpolarised protons through the study of azimuthal asymmetries. Not only the short-distance coefficients for such reactions induce the largest possible cos 4 phi modulations, but analysed data are already available. Among the various final states previously studied in unpolarised pp collisions within the TMD factorisation approach, di-J/psi production exhibits by far the largest asymmetries, up to 50% in the region studied by the ATLAS and CMS experiments. In addition, we use the very recent LHCb data at 13 TeV to perform the first fit of the unpolarised transverse-momentum-dependent gluon distribution.

## Key claim
The production of J/psi or Upsilon pairs in unpolarised pp collisions is the best process to measure the momentum distribution of linearly-polarised gluons inside unpolarised protons, because the short-distance coefficients for these reactions induce the largest possible cos 4phi modulations. The claim is conditional on the TMD factorisation framework being applicable and on the dominance of colour-singlet transitions over colour-octet contributions, which the authors argue is satisfied in the kinematic regions of interest.

## Method
The authors computed the short-distance coefficients F_i for quarkonium-pair production within the TMD factorisation framework at leading order in alpha_s, using colour-singlet transitions for the quarkonia. They modelled the unpolarised gluon TMD f_1^g with a Gaussian dependence on transverse momentum and fitted its average transverse momentum squared to the normalised transverse-momentum spectrum of di-J/psi events from LHCb at 13 TeV, after subtracting double-parton-scattering contributions. They then used two models for the linearly-polarised gluon distribution h_1^{perp g} (a Gaussian model with r=2/3 and a model saturating the positivity bound) to predict the azimuthal asymmetries 2<cos 2phi_CS> and 2<cos 4phi_CS> as functions of the pair transverse momentum, invariant mass, and Collins-Soper angle.

## Key numbers
- The average gluon transverse momentum squared extracted from LHCb di-J/psi data at 13 TeV is <k_T^2> = 3.3 +/- 0.8 GeV^2, with a chi^2 of 1.08 for the fit.
- The 2<cos 4phi_CS> asymmetry reaches up to 50% in the q_T region probed by CMS and ATLAS for |cos theta_CS| < 0.25, using the model that saturates the positivity bound for h_1^{perp g}.
- The 2<cos 2phi_CS> asymmetry is below 5% for |cos theta_CS| < 0.25 but reaches nearly 15% in the bin 0.25 < |cos theta_CS| < 0.5.
- The ratio F_4/F_1 approaches 1 for M_{QQ} >> 2 M_Q and cos theta_CS -> 0, which is the key result enabling the large cos 4phi modulation.
- At threshold (M_{QQ} -> 2 M_Q), F_2/F_1 is close to 0.4%, making the influence of h_1^{perp g} on the transverse-momentum spectrum negligible.

## Context
This work extends the TMD factorisation approach for gluon-induced processes previously applied to gamma-gamma production (Qiu 2011), H^0+jet production (Boer 2014), and quarkonium+gamma production (Dunnen 2014) by showing that di-quarkonium production has uniquely favourable short-distance coefficients. It enables the first extraction of the linearly-polarised gluon distribution h_1^{perp g} from existing LHC data, and the authors performed the first fit of the unpolarised gluon TMD f_1^g from a gluon-induced process with a colourless final state. The analysis also opens the possibility of testing TMD universality by comparing with future measurements at an Electron-Ion Collider.
