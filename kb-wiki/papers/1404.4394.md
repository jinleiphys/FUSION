# Nuclear modification factor in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The transverse momentum dependent nuclear modification factors (NMF), namely $R_{CP}$, is investigated for protons produced in Au + Au at 1$A$ GeV within the framework of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. It is found that the radial collective motion during the expansion stage affects the NMF at low transverse momentum a lot. By fitting the transverse mass spectra of protons with the distribution function from the Blast-Wave model, the magnitude of radial flow can be extracted. After removing the contribution from radial flow, the $R_{CP}$ can be regarded as a thermal one and is found to keep unitary at transverse momentum lower than 0.6 GeV/c and enhance at higher transverse momentum, which can be attributed to Cronin effect.

## Key claim

The paper claims that after removing the contribution from radial flow, the nuclear modification factor \(R_{CP}\) for protons in Au+Au collisions at 1\(A\) GeV becomes unitary at transverse momentum lower than 0.6 GeV/c and enhances at higher transverse momentum, which can be attributed to the Cronin effect. This result is conditional on the use of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model and the Blast-Wave model to extract and subtract the radial flow contribution.

## Method

The authors simulated Au+Au collisions at 1\(A\) GeV using the IQMD transport model with both soft and hard equations of state including momentum-dependent interactions. They fitted the transverse mass spectra of protons with the Blast-Wave model to extract the radial flow velocity and freeze-out temperature. To obtain the thermal \(R_{CP}\), they subtracted the radial flow contribution from each particle's transverse momentum using the extracted flow parameters and a density criterion to define the freeze-out volume.

## Key numbers

- Average radial flow \(\langle\beta_r\rangle\) = 0.3309 \(\pm\) 0.0028 \(c\) for central collisions (0-10% centrality) from Blast-Wave fit of proton \(m_T\) spectra.
- Average radial flow \(\langle\beta_r\rangle\) = 0.2155 \(\pm\) 0.0056 \(c\) for peripheral collisions (60-80% centrality) from Blast-Wave fit.
- Freeze-out temperature \(T_f\) = 61.60 \(\pm\) 0.81 MeV for central collisions (0-10% centrality) from Blast-Wave fit.
- Slope temperature from Boltzmann fit: 93.06 \(\pm\) 0.91 MeV for central collisions (0-10% centrality) and 73.87 \(\pm\) 1.62 MeV for peripheral collisions (60-80% centrality).
- The thermal \(R_{CP}\) is unitary below \(p_T\) = 0.6 GeV/c and enhances above that value.
- Average collision numbers in IQMD: 242.5, 473, 795.5, 1136, and 1463 for five centralities from peripheral to central.
- Freeze-out time approximately 60 fm/c with freeze-out radius about 40 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear modification factors, previously investigated at relativistic energies by RHIC experiments, to intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions (1\(A\) GeV) for the first time. The authors compare their IQMD results with experimental data from the KaoS collaboration and the EOS collaboration (Lisa et al., 1995). The paper enables future studies of thermal medium properties in intermediate-energy collisions by providing a method to separate collective flow effects from thermal effects in the nuclear modification factor.
