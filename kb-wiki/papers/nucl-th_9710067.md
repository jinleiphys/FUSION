# Electromagnetic Corrections to the One-Pion-Exchange Potential

> Leading-order electromagnetic loop corrections to the one-pion-exchange potential are computed within the framework of chiral perturbation theory. These corrections generate an effective nucleon-nucleon potential, $V_{πγ}$, which supplements the sum of OPEP and the nucleon-nucleon Coulomb potential. This potential is charge dependent and its construction is demonstrated to be gauge invariant. The potential $V_{πγ}$ has been included in the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis of np data. A particular renormalization scheme is chosen that leads to a negligible change in the charged-piNN coupling constant and in the np 1S0 scattering length and effective range.

# Electromagnetic Corrections to the One-Pion-Exchange Potential

## Key claim
Leading-order electromagnetic loop corrections to the one-pion-exchange potential (OPEP) are computed within chiral perturbation theory, producing a charge-dependent πγ-exchange potential Vπγ. When incorporated into the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis using a specific renormalization scheme (γ̅ ≡ γE), the change in the charged-piNN coupling constant and the np ¹S₀ scattering length and effective range is negligible, but the potential would have larger effects if constructed explicitly for all distances rather than being partially subsumed into the phenomenological interior region.

## Method
The authors calculated 19 Feynman diagrams (including bubbles, vertex corrections, box diagrams, crossed-box diagrams, seagull diagrams, and a tadpole) in both Coulomb and Feynman gauges within the static approximation (MN → ∞) of chiral perturbation theory at leading order in 1/MQCD. The sum of diagrams was demonstrated to be gauge invariant, and a renormalization scheme was adopted that keeps only nonanalytic terms in divergent loops while subtracting the iteration of static OPEP and Coulomb potential. The resulting Vπγ was then incorporated into the tail (r > 1.4 fm) of the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis of 4107 np scattering data.

## Key numbers
- The nominal strength of the πγ-exchange force is (α/π) Vπ, where α ≈ 1/137.
- The renormalization scheme defines γ̅ ≡ γE = 0.577..., which makes the logarithmic term in I(z) vanish at r = 2 ħ / mπ c = 2.8 fm.
- The fitted charged-piNN coupling constant 10³ f²c = 74.96(34) without Vπγ, and 74.98(33) with Vπγ included and both f²c and the interior region refitted.
- The χ² of the fit changed from 4223.6 (without Vπγ) to 4222.8 (with Vπγ and full refit).
- A rough estimate using ad hoc form factors shows the magnitude of the np ¹S₀ scattering length decreases by approximately 0.67 fm when Vπγ is included explicitly for all r.
- The deuteron energy is raised by roughly 60 keV by the explicit Vπγ.
- Choosing γ̅ = 0 (no finite renormalization) lowers 1000 f²c by 0.18, slightly more than half the quoted uncertainty.

## Context
This work extends the systematic understanding of isospin violation in the nuclear force using chiral perturbation theory and dimensional power counting, as previously developed by van Kolck (1993) and others. The πγ-exchange potential was one of the few isospin-violating mechanisms not previously incorporated into the tail of the NN force in the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis of Stoks et al. (1993). The calculation provides complete analytic results for this mechanism, enabling future construction of NN potentials with explicit πγ components for all distances, which would show considerably larger effects than those seen when the short-range part is absorbed into phenomenological parameters.
