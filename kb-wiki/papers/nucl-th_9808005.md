# Radiative decays of decuplet hyperons

> We calculate the radiative decay widths of decuplet hyperons in a chiral constituent quark model including electromagnetic exchange currents between quarks. Exchange currents contribute significantly to the E2 transition amplitude, while they largely cancel for the M1 transition amplitude. Strangeness suppression of the radiative hyperon decays is found to be weakened by exchange currents. Differences and similarities between our results and other recent model predictions are discussed.

## Key claim

Exchange currents contribute significantly to the E2 transition amplitude in decuplet hyperon radiative decays while largely canceling for the M1 transition amplitude, and the strangeness suppression of these decays is weakened by exchange currents compared to impulse approximation predictions.

## Method

The authors calculate radiative decay widths of all decuplet hyperons using a chiral constituent quark model with three non-equal quark masses, including one-gluon exchange and pseudoscalar meson exchange (pion, kaon, eta) as residual interactions. Electromagnetic exchange currents are constructed via non-relativistic reduction of Feynman diagrams to satisfy the continuity equation with the exchange potentials, and spherical (0s)^3 oscillator states are used for baryon wave functions. The M1 and C2 transition form factors are computed at zero momentum transfer, and helicity amplitudes, E2/M1 ratios, and partial decay widths are derived from these form factors.

## Key numbers

- Transition magnetic moment for γn↔Δ⁰: μ_tot = 2.428 μ_N (with exchange currents), compared to impulse approximation value μ_imp = 2.828 μ_N
- Transition magnetic moment for γΞ⁰↔Ξ*⁰: μ_tot = 2.428 μ_N (with exchange currents), compared to impulse approximation value μ_imp = 2.404 μ_N
- Transition magnetic moments for negatively charged hyperons: approximately -0.4 μ_N, deviating from the SU_F(3) flavor-symmetric value of 0
- E2/M1 ratio for Σ*⁻→γΣ⁻ decay: -5.5%, almost three times larger than the Skyrme model result
- E2/M1 ratio for Σ⁰→γΣ*⁰ decay: -2.3%
- E2/M1 ratio for Λ→γΣ*⁰ decay: -2%, coming almost exclusively from the gluon exchange charge density
- Calculated values for Δ⁺→p decay: F_M1(0) = 2.533 μ_N, F_Q(0) = -0.089 fm², E2/M1 = -3.65%
- Experimental values for Δ⁺→p decay: μ_exp = 3.58(9) μ_N, helicity amplitudes A_{3/2} = -(257±8)×10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2} and A_{1/2} = -(141±5)×10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2}, decay width Γ_exp = 610-730 keV

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model calculations of Buchmann (1991, 1997) and Wagner (1995) to include exchange current effects on all decuplet hyperon radiative decays for the first time, complementing and improving upon earlier quark model calculations by Dar (1983) that used only impulse approximation. The results are compared with the SU_F(3) Skyrme model approach of Abaev (1996), Oh (1995), and Schat (1995), as well as quenched lattice calculations by Leinweber (1993). The calculations enable experimental discrimination between effective gluon versus pseudoscalar degrees of freedom in low-energy QCD through measurements of E2/M1 ratios, particularly for negatively charged hyperon decays where model predictions differ most strongly.
