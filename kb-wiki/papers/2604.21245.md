# Octupole correlation effects on two-neutron transfer intensity in rare-earth nuclei

> Impacts of octupole correlations on the low-lying $0^+$ states and two-neutron transfer intensities in rare-earth nuclei are investigated in terms of the interacting boson model that is based on the nuclear density functional theory. The octupole degrees of freedom are not only essential building blocks to describe properties of negative-parity states in the model, but also influence low-spin positive-parity states including excited $0^+$ states. The calculation produces a large number of low-energy $0^+$ states that contain significant amounts of octupole components, indicating important roles played by the octupole degrees freedom in this mass region. Octupole correlations are shown to make sizable contributions to the $(p,t)$ and $(t,p)$ transfer intensities and, in particular, to reproduce the discontinuous changes of these quantities near those nuclei with $N\approx88$ or 90, which are observed experimentally as a signature of the shape phase transition.

## Key claim

Octupole correlations, incorporated via the interacting boson model (IBM) with $s$, $d$, and $f$ bosons, make sizable contributions to $(p,t)$ and $(t,p)$ two-neutron transfer intensities in rare-earth nuclei and are necessary to reproduce the discontinuous changes of these quantities near $N\approx 88$ or 90, which the earlier $sd$-IBM mapping calculation (Nomura, 2019) failed to capture, producing only monotonous changes with neutron number.

## Method

The author performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1M energy density functional to obtain potential energy surfaces (PES) in terms of axially symmetric quadrupole $\beta_{20}$ and octupole $\beta_{30}$ deformations for even-even Nd, Sm, and Gd isotopes with $N=84-94$. These PES were then mapped onto the energy surface of an $sdf$-IBM Hamiltonian, whose parameters (including $d$- and $f$-boson energies, quadrupole-quadrupole, octupole-octupole, and rotational terms) were determined by the mapping. The resulting $sdf$-IBM wave functions were used to compute $(p,t)$ and $(t,p)$ transfer intensities via bosonic transfer operators that include both a leading-order $s$-boson term and a term proportional to the $f$-boson number operator $\hat n_f$, with the coefficient $c_2$ made dependent on the $\beta_{30}$ deformation.

## Key numbers

- The maximal octupole deformation from the Gogny-HFB PES is $\beta_{30}^{\rm min} \approx 0.12$ for $^{148}$Nd.
- The $f$-boson expectation value in the $0^+_2$ state is $\langle \hat n_f \rangle \approx 2$ for most cases, indicating large octupole contributions.
- The $E3$ transition matrix element $\langle 3^-_1 \| \hat T^{(E3)} \| 0^+_2 \rangle$ is of the same order as, or even larger than, the ground-state matrix element $\langle 3^-_1 \| \hat T^{(E3)} \| 0^+_1 \rangle$ for several nuclei, e.g., $^{152}$Sm and $^{148}$Nd.
- The effective boson charge $e_3^0$ is set to $0.105$ $e$b$^{3/2}$ for Gd and Sm isotopes and $0.12$ $e$b$^{3/2}$ for Nd isotopes.
- The parameter $c_2$ for the transfer operators takes a large negative value for the $N=88 \to 86$ $(p,t)$ and $N=86 \to 88$ $(t,p)$ transfers, varying appreciably at $N\approx 88$.

## Context

This work extends the mapped IBM approach of Nomura (2019), which used only $s$ and $d$ bosons and failed to reproduce the discontinuous changes in transfer intensities at the shape phase transition near $N=90$. By including octupole ($f$) bosons, the present study builds on earlier phenomenological $spdf$-IBM calculations for actinides (Levon, 2013; Spieker, 2013) and rare-earth nuclei (Bucurescu, 2019; Levon, 2020) that suggested the importance of negative-parity boson terms in transfer operators. The results enable future studies of configuration mixing in the IBM, which has been suggested to be important in the rare-earth region (Garrett, 2009; Heyde, 2011; Tsunoda, 2023), to further improve the description of excited $0^+$ states.
