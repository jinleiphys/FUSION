# Study of refractive structure in the inelastic 16O+16O scattering at the incident energies of 250 to 1120 MeV

> The data of inelastic 16O+16O scattering to the lowest 2+ and 3- excited states of 16O have been measured at Elab = 250, 350, 480, 704 and 1120 MeV and analyzed consistently in the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA), using the semi- microscopic optical potentials and inelastic form factors given by the folding model, to reveal possible refractive structure of the nuclear rainbow that was identified earlier in the elastic 16O+16O scattering channel at the same energies. Given the known transition strengths of the 2+ and 3- states of 16O well determined from the (e,e') data, the DWBA description of the inelastic data over the whole angular range was possible only if the absorption in the exit channels is significantly increased (especially, for the 16O+16O(2+) exit channel). Although the refractive pattern of the inelastic 16O+16O scattering was found to be less pronounced compared to that observed in the elastic scattering channel, a clear remnant of the main rainbow maximum could still be seen in the inelastic cross section at Elab = 350 - 704 MeV.

## Key claim

The refractive nuclear rainbow pattern observed in elastic 16O+16O scattering is also present in the inelastic scattering to the lowest 2+ and 3- excited states of 16O, but it is less pronounced; a clear remnant of the main rainbow maximum could still be seen in the inelastic cross section at laboratory energies from 350 to 704 MeV, but only after significantly increasing the absorption in the exit channels (especially for the 16O+16O(2+) exit channel).

## Method

The authors measured inelastic 16O+16O scattering data at five incident energies (250, 350, 480, 704, and 1120 MeV) using the Q3D magnetic spectrograph at HMI and the SPEG spectrograph at GANIL. They performed distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) analyses using semi-microscopic optical potentials and inelastic form factors calculated from the double-folding model with the CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, with deformation lengths fixed by known electric transition strengths from (e,e') data. The real folded potentials were supplemented by Woods-Saxon imaginary potentials, and the absorption in the exit channels was adjusted separately from the entrance channel to reproduce the measured angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Incident laboratory energies: 250, 350, 480, 704, and 1120 MeV
- Excitation energies of the 2+ and 3- states in 16O: 6.92 MeV and 6.13 MeV, respectively
- Deformation lengths used: delta_2 = 1.038 +/- 0.048 fm and delta_3 = 1.825 +/- 0.031 fm, derived from B(E2 up) = 40.6 +/- 3.8 e^2 fm^4 (Ram01) and B(E3 up) = 1480 +/- 50 e^2 fm^6 (Kib02)
- For the 2+ exit channel at 1120 MeV, the absorption strength (volume integral J_W) increased from 109.1 MeV fm^3 in the entrance channel to 152.7 MeV fm^3 in the exit channel (about 40% stronger)
- For the 3- exit channel at 1120 MeV, J_W increased from 109.1 to 119.0 MeV fm^3
- At 250 MeV, J_W for the 2+ exit channel was 119.4 MeV fm^3 versus 98.81 MeV fm^3 in the entrance channel
- The renormalization factor N_V for the real folded potential in the entrance channel ranged from 0.781 at 250 MeV to 0.905 at 1120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of refractive nuclear rainbow scattering in the 16O+16O system, which had identified clear rainbow patterns in elastic scattering (Khoa 2000) and one-neutron transfer reactions (Bohlen 2002). The enhanced absorption found in the inelastic exit channels (40-60% stronger than in the entrance channel) mirrors similar findings from the one-neutron transfer analysis, suggesting that the nuclear mean free path is shorter for excited, less-bound nuclear clusters. The results enable future studies of how nuclear transparency and refractive effects depend on the reaction channel and the structure of the excited states involved.
