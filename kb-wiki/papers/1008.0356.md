# Thermal neutron captures on $d$ and $^3$He

> We report on a study of the $nd$ and $n\,^3$He radiative captures at thermal neutron energies, using wave functions obtained from either chiral or conventional two- and three-nucleon realistic potentials with the hyperspherical harmonics method, and electromagnetic currents derived in chiral effective field theory up to one loop. The predicted $nd$ and $n\,^3$He cross sections are in good agreement with data, but exhibit a significant dependence on the input Hamiltonian. A comparison is also made between these and new results for the $nd$ and $n\,^3$He cross sections obtained in the conventional framework for both potentials and currents.

## Key claim

The predicted thermal neutron capture cross sections for nd and n³He, calculated with chiral effective field theory currents up to one loop, agree well with experimental data but exhibit a significant dependence on the input Hamiltonian (AV18/UIX versus N3LO/N2LO), with the n³He cross section underpredicted by about 15% in the AV18/UIX model at a cutoff of 600 MeV.

## Method

The authors solved the A=3 and A=4 Schrödinger equations using the hyperspherical harmonics method with wave functions from either the conventional AV18/UIX or chiral N3LO/N2LO potentials. They evaluated matrix elements of electromagnetic currents derived in chiral effective field theory up to N³LO, including one-body, one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, and contact currents, with the low-energy constants constrained by fits to the np capture cross section and the magnetic moments of the deuteron and trinucleons.

## Key numbers

- The nd thermal capture cross section from the AV18/UIX and N3LO/N2LO models is within about 2% of the experimental value from Jurney (1982)
- The n³He thermal capture cross section at Λ=600 MeV is well reproduced in the N3LO/N2LO calculation but underpredicted by about 15% in the AV18/UIX calculation relative to the experimental value from Wolfs (1989)
- The LO contribution alone gives nd and n³He cross sections less than half and a factor of five smaller than the measured values, respectively
- The SNPA* results (including relativistic corrections) for nd capture are within about 4% of experimental data and 2% of the chiral EFT predictions based on the AV18/UIX model

## Context

This work extends previous studies of radiative capture reactions by Marcucci et al. (2005) and Park et al. (1996, 2000), using the more recent chiral current derivations by Pastore et al. (2009) and Kolling et al. (2009). The results demonstrate that the N³LO low-energy constants are essential for bringing theory into agreement with experiment, and that the convergence of the chiral expansion is problematic for these processes due to the unnatural suppression of the leading-order contribution. The authors plan to pursue inclusion of explicit Δ degrees of freedom to improve convergence.
