# K* nucleon hyperon form factors and nucleon strangeness

> A crucial input for recent meson hyperon cloud model estimates of the nucleon matrix element of the strangeness current are the nucleon-hyperon-K* (NYK*) form factors which regularize some of the arising loops. Prompted by new and forthcoming information on these form factors from hyperon-nucleon potential models, we analyze the dependence of the loop model results for the strange-quark observables on the NYK* form factors and couplings. We find, in particular, that the now generally favored soft N-Lambda-K* form factors can reduce the magnitude of the K* contributions in such models by more than an order of magnitude, compared to previous results with hard form factors. We also discuss some general implications of our results for hadronic loop models.

# K* nucleon hyperon form factors and nucleon strangeness

## Key claim

The paper shows that the now generally favored soft N-Lambda-K* form factors, with cutoff parameters around 1.2-1.5 GeV rather than the previously used 2.2 GeV, can reduce the magnitude of the K* contributions to nucleon strangeness observables in hadronic loop models by more than an order of magnitude compared to earlier results with hard form factors. However, the authors emphasize that the very sensitive dependence of results on the K* cutoff reveals the limited and mostly qualitative character of hadron-loop model predictions.

## Method

The authors analyze the cutoff, coupling, and momentum dependence of K* contributions to the nucleon's vector strangeness content within a hadronic one-loop model containing K and K* mesons, using effective Lagrangians with pseudoscalar and vector-baryon couplings. They evaluate the loop integrals with monopole form factors at the hadronic KNY and K*NY vertices, varying the cutoff parameter Lambda_K* between 1.0 and 2.5 GeV and testing different coupling values from the Bonn-Julich and Nijmegen potential models, then compute the strange vector form factors at low momentum transfers up to Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- The Dirac strangeness radius from the K*K*Lambda contribution changes from 0.030 fm^2 at Lambda = 2.2 GeV to 0.0023 fm^2 at Lambda = 1.2 GeV
- The strangeness magnetic moment from the K*K*Lambda contribution changes from -4.149 at Lambda = 2.2 GeV to -0.180 at Lambda = 1.2 GeV
- The KK*Lambda contribution to the strangeness magnetic moment changes from 1.023 at Lambda = 2.2 GeV to 0.253 at Lambda = 1.2 GeV
- The kaon loop alone gives a Dirac strangeness radius of -0.007 fm^2 and a strangeness magnetic moment of -0.237
- For the MAMI A4 experiment at Q^2 = 0.23 GeV^2, the predicted form factor combination G_MAMI^(s) = -0.012 for Lambda = 0.9 GeV and -0.046 for Lambda = 1.2 GeV
- The K* cutoff from the Nijmegen potential is approximately 1.2 GeV, while the updated Bonn-Julich potential favors approximately 1.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hadronic one-loop model of Barz et al. (1998), which found anomalously large K* contributions using the Bonn-Julich potential form factors from Holzenkamp et al. (1989). The analysis was prompted by newer hyperon-nucleon potential models, specifically the Nijmegen potential of Rijken et al. (1999) and the forthcoming update of the Bonn-Julich potential by Haidenbauer et al. (1998), which favor substantially smaller K* cutoff parameters. The results enable more realistic comparisons with experimental data from the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, and MAMI A4 experiments, and suggest that kaon cloud contributions alone might be sufficient for rough estimates of nucleon strangeness content, contrary to the findings of Geiger and Isgur (1997) in the quark model.
