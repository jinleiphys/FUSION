# Matter induced charge symmetry violating NN potential

> We construct density dependent Class $III$ charge symmetry violating (CSV) potential due to mixing of $ρ$-$ω$ meson with off-shell corrections. Here in addition to the usual vacuum contribution, the matter induced mixing of $ρ$-$ω$ is also included. It is observed that the contribution of density dependent CSV potential is comparable to that of the vacuum contribution.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a density-dependent Class III charge symmetry violating (CSV) nucleon-nucleon potential that includes both the usual vacuum contribution from rho-omega meson mixing and a new matter-induced mixing contribution; the authors claim the density-dependent contribution is comparable to the vacuum contribution, but this result depends on the asymmetry parameter and nuclear matter density used.

## Method

The authors calculate the rho-omega mixing amplitude from nucleon-nucleon loop diagrams using effective Lagrangians for rho-NN and omega-NN interactions, including both vector and tensor couplings. The in-medium nucleon propagator is decomposed into a free vacuum part and a density-dependent part containing a Fermi distribution function, which generates the matter-induced mixing. The full CSV potential is derived in the non-relativistic limit, expressed in momentum space, and then Fourier transformed to coordinate space, with form factors introduced to remove short-range divergences.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter density used: rho_B = 0.148 fm^{-3}
* Asymmetry parameter used: alpha = 1/3 (where alpha = (rho_n - rho_p)/rho_B)
* The vacuum mixing amplitude is proportional to ln(M_p/M_n) times q^2, with coefficient A = (g_rho g_omega)/(12 pi^2) (2 + 3 C_rho)
* The density-dependent mixing amplitude contains a constant term Delta' proportional to (k_p^3/M_p - k_n^3/M_n) and a q^2 term with coefficient A' proportional to (k_p/M_p - k_n/M_n)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of rho-omega mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter by Abhee (1997), Broniowski (1998), and Roy (2008), which focused on dilepton spectra and meson dispersion relations, by constructing a two-body NN potential. It also builds on the off-shell momentum-dependent mixing amplitude calculations of Piekarewicz (1992), Goldman (1992), and Krein (1993), which showed that on-shell mixing amplitudes are insufficient. The authors note that Saito (2003) attempted a density-dependent CSV potential but only considered scalar mean field effects on nucleon mass, excluding matter-driven mixing. This work enables calculations of CSV observables such as binding energy differences of mirror nuclei (the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly) with both vacuum and medium contributions included.
