# Exclusive $c \to s,d$ semileptonic decays of spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 $cb$ baryons

> We present results for exclusive semileptonic decay widths of ground state spin-$1/2$ and spin-$3/2$ $cb$ baryons corresponding to a $c\to s, d$ transition at the quark level. The relevance of hyperfine mixing in spin-1/2 $cb$ baryons is shown. Our form factors are compatible with heavy quark spin symmetry constraints obtained in the infinite heavy quark mass limit.

## Key claim

The paper presents exclusive semileptonic decay widths for ground-state spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 doubly heavy \(cb\) baryons, showing that hyperfine mixing between \(S=0\) and \(S=1\) heavy-quark-subsystem states in spin-1/2 \(cb\) baryons significantly affects the computed decay widths, even though the mixing has little impact on the masses.

## Method

The authors used the AL1 quark potential of Semay (1994) to compute quark masses and wave functions variationally, then evaluated the hadronic matrix elements for \(c \to s,d\) semileptonic transitions. For spin-1/2 to spin-1/2 transitions they employed three vector and three axial form factors; for spin-1/2 to spin-3/2 or spin-3/2 to spin-1/2 they used four vector and four axial form factors; for spin-3/2 to spin-3/2 they computed the vector and axial matrix elements directly without a form-factor decomposition. The physical spin-1/2 states were constructed as admixtures of the pure \(S=0\) and \(S=1\) basis states, with mixing coefficients determined in earlier work by Albertus (2009).

## Key numbers

* \(\Xi_{cb}^{(1)}\) mass: 6967 MeV; \(\Xi_{cb}^{(2)}\) mass: 6919 MeV; \(\Omega_{cb}^{(1)}\) mass: 7046 MeV; \(\Omega_{cb}^{(2)}\) mass: 7005 MeV
* \(\Xi_{cb}^{(1)+} \to \Xi_b^0 e^+\nu_e\) width: \(3.74 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (with mixing); without mixing the value is \(3.45 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV
* \(\Xi_{cb}^{(1)+} \to \Xi_b^{\prime 0} e^+\nu_e\) width: \(3.88 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (with mixing); without mixing the value is \(1.66 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV
* \(\Omega_{cb}^{(1)0} \to \Omega_b^- e^+\nu_e\) width: \(7.21 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (with mixing); without mixing the value is \(3.12 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV
* \(\Omega_{cb}^{*(0)} \to \Omega_b^{*-} e^+\nu_e\) width: \(10.2 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (for \(c\to s\))
* \(\Xi_{cb}^{(1)+} \to \Lambda_b^0 e^+\nu_e\) width: \(0.219 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (with mixing); without mixing the value is \(0.196 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (for \(c\to d\))
* \(\Xi_{cbu}^{*(+)} \to \Sigma_b^{*0} e^+\nu_e\) width: \(0.246 \times 10^{-14}\) GeV (for \(c\to d\))

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Sanchis-Lozano (1994), Faessler (2001), and Kiselev (2001), which were limited to only a few decay channels, by providing a systematic study of all ground-state \(cb\) baryon semileptonic decays. The results demonstrate that hyperfine mixing in spin-1/2 \(cb\) baryons, previously studied by Albertus (2009) and Roberts (2008), is crucial for accurate decay-width predictions. The computed form factors approximately satisfy heavy quark spin symmetry constraints derived from the trace formalism of Falk (1990) and Flynn (2007), enabling future model-independent tests once experimental data on these doubly heavy baryons become available.
