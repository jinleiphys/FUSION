# Studying the $ω$ properties in pA collisions via the $ω{\to}π^0γ$ decay

> Within transport calculations we study the production and decay of $ω$-mesons in $pA$ reactions at COSY energies including elastic and inelastic $ωN$ rescattering, the $ω{\to}π^0γ$ Dalitz decay as well as $π^0 N$ rescattering. The resulting invariant $π^0 γ$ mass distributions indicate that in-medium modifications of the $ω$-meson may be observed experimentally.

## Key claim

Transport calculations of ω-meson production and decay in pA collisions at COSY energies show that in-medium modifications of the ω-meson may be observable via the ω→π⁰γ Dalitz decay, but the observability depends on target mass and the relative contributions of mass shift versus collisional broadening.

## Method

The authors performed transport model calculations for p+C, p+Cu, and p+Pb collisions at 2.4 GeV beam energy, including direct pN→ωpN and secondary πN→ωN production mechanisms, ωN elastic and inelastic rescattering, the ω→π⁰γ Dalitz decay, and π⁰N rescattering. The ω propagation was described by Hamilton's equations of motion, and in-medium modifications were introduced via a t-ρ approximation with a real potential shift (β = -0.16) and a collisional width (Γcoll = 20 or 50 MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³).

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 2.4 GeV for all pA collisions studied (p+C, p+Cu, p+Pb)
- Vacuum ω width ΓV = 8.4 MeV used for the survival probability in the rest frame
- For p+C target: 92% of ω-mesons decay in vacuum (ρ ≤ 0.05 fm⁻³); for p+Cu target: 19% decay inside the target nucleus
- Normal nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- ω-meson mass shift parameter β = -0.16 adopted from prior models
- Collisional width Γcoll at ρ₀ examined at two values: 20 MeV and 50 MeV
- Results shown for an assumed experimental mass resolution of 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of in-medium vector meson modifications, particularly the ρ⁰-meson studies via π⁺π⁻ decays in pA reactions (Sibirtsev et al., 1999), which found that final-state interactions of decay pions were too strong for invariant mass reconstruction. The ω→π⁰γ channel is advantageous because only the single pion can rescatter while the photon escapes without reinteraction. The calculations use ωN cross sections from earlier work (Fäldt and Wilkin; Kondratyuk et al.) and in-medium potential estimates from multiple theoretical models (Hatsuda and Lee; Klingl et al.; Rapp et al.). The proposed experiments could be carried out at COSY with neutral particle detectors, complementary to dilepton studies planned with the HADES detector at GSI.
