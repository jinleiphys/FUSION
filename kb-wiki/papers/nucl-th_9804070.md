# Strange Decays of Nonstrange Baryons

> The strong decays of excited nonstrange baryons into the final states Lambda K, Sigma K, and for the first time into Lambda(1405) K, Lambda(1520) K, Sigma(1385) K, Lambda K*, and Sigma K*, are examined in a relativized quark pair creation model. The wave functions and parameters of the model are fixed by previous calculations of N pi and N pi pi, etc., decays. Our results show that it should be possible to discover several new negative parity excited baryons and confirm the discovery of several others by analyzing these final states in kaon production experiments. We also establish clear predictions for the relative strengths of certain states to decay to Lambda(1405) K and Lambda(1520) K, which can be tested to determine if a three-quark model of the Lambda(1405) K is valid. Our results compare favorably with the results of partial wave analyses of the limited existing data for the Lambda K and Sigma K channels. We do not find large Sigma K decay amplitudes for a substantial group of predicted and weakly established negative-parity states, in contrast to the only previous work to consider decays of these states into the strange final states Lambda K and Sigma K.

## Key claim

The authors predict that several new negative-parity excited baryons (with wave functions predominantly in the N=3 oscillator band) should be discoverable through kaon production experiments analyzing strange final states, but they do not confirm the previous prediction by Forsyth and Cutkosky (1983) that many light negative-parity states between 2050 and 2300 MeV have large amplitudes to decay to Sigma K.

## Method

The authors used a relativized quark pair creation (3P0) model to calculate strong decay amplitudes of excited nonstrange baryons into the final states Lambda K, Sigma K, Lambda(1405) K, Lambda(1520) K, Sigma(1385) K, Lambda K*, and Sigma K*. Wave functions and model parameters were taken from previous calculations of N pi and N pi pi decays by Capstick and Roberts (1993, 1994) and Capstick and Isgur (1986), with no adjustments made for this work.

## Key numbers

- The model predicts that for N=3 band states where Forsyth and Cutkosky (1983) predicted large (up to 40 MeV) Sigma K widths, the authors find amplitudes at most 2.4 MeV^(1/2) in magnitude.
- The predicted mass range for the N=3 band negative-parity states with large Sigma K amplitudes in the Forsyth and Cutkosky (1983) model was 2050 to 2300 MeV.
- The authors predict that the Lambda(1405)K and Lambda(1520)K final states will be produced when sqrt(s) is roughly 2000 to 2300 MeV.
- The state N(1650)S11 = [N 1/2-]_2(1535) has a predicted Lambda K decay amplitude that agrees within errors with the amplitude extracted from analyses.

## Context

This work extends the relativized quark model calculations of Capstick and Roberts (1993, 1994) to strange final states, and provides the first predictions for decays into Lambda(1405)K, Lambda(1520)K, Sigma(1385)K, Lambda K*, and Sigma K*. It contrasts with the earlier work of Koniuk and Isgur (1980), who studied Lambda K and Sigma K decays of N=1 and N=2 band states using an elementary-meson emission model, and with Forsyth and Cutkosky (1983), who predicted large Sigma K widths for many N=3 band states. The results enable experimental tests of whether the Lambda(1405) has a conventional three-quark structure by comparing measured relative strengths of decays to Lambda(1405)K versus Lambda(1520)K with the clear predictions given here.
