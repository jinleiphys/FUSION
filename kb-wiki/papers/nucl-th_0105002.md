# Radiative corrections to polarization observables for elastic $e+A$-scattering. Part I: Virtual Compton Scattering

> We calculate polarization phenomena for virtual Compton scattering on protons, at relatively large momentum transfer 1 GeV$^2$ $\le -q^2\le$ 5 GeV$^2$ on the basis of a model for $γ^*+ p\to γ+p$ with two main contributions: $π^0$-exchange in $t-$channel and $Δ$-excitation in $s$-channel. This model applies from threshold to $Δ$ region. The parameters entering in this model, such as coupling constants and electromagnetic form factors are well known. The analyzing powers for $γ^*+\vec p\to γ+ p$ and the components of the final proton polarization in $γ^*+ p\to γ+ \vec p$ are large in absolute value and show strong sensitivity to $π\bigotimesΔ$ interference. These results can be applied to the calculation of radiative corrections to polarization phenomena in elastic $ep$-scattering.

## Key claim

The authors calculate T-even polarization observables for virtual Compton scattering (VCS) on protons using a model with only two contributions (pion exchange in the t-channel and Delta excitation in the s-channel), and claim these observables are large in absolute value and show strong sensitivity to the interference between the two mechanisms. However, they explicitly note that this is an oversimplified estimation that cannot be directly applied to experimental observables in elastic ep-scattering, and that the large polarization effects must be multiplied by the electromagnetic constant alpha after integration over experimental acceptance.

## Method

The authors construct a model for the process gamma* + p -> gamma + p using two Feynman diagrams: one-pion exchange in the t-channel and Delta-isobar excitation in the s-channel, with all coupling constants and electromagnetic form factors taken from existing experimental data. They calculate the four unpolarized structure functions W1-W4 and five polarized structure functions T1-T5 (for polarized target) and P1-P5 (for final proton polarization) analytically, then evaluate them numerically for momentum transfers 1 GeV^2 <= -q^2 <= 5 GeV^2 and invariant masses W from threshold to the Delta region (1.1 to 1.38 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range: 1 GeV^2 <= -q^2 <= 5 GeV^2 (space-like region)
- Invariant mass values used: W = 1.1, 1.232, and 1.38 GeV
- The reduced polarized structure functions T_i/W_1 and P_i/W_1 are in the range +/- 0.5
- The pion form factor parameter m_V is approximately 0.8 GeV
- The Delta transition magnetic moment squared: mu^2(Delta -> N + gamma) is approximately 22
- The pion decay constant squared: F_pi^2 = 0.0140 (from pi^0 -> 2gamma width)
- The Delta width parameter m_x^2 is approximately 6 GeV^2 for the magnetic form factor G(q^2)
- The ratio W_3/W_1 increases with -q^2 due to different q^2 behavior of the two form factors

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of radiative corrections to elastic ep-scattering, particularly motivated by the JLab experiment of Jones (2000) which showed strong deviation of G_Ep/G_Mp from unity. The authors build on the observation by Gunion and Stodolsky (1973), Franco (1973), Boitsov, Kondratyuk and Kopeliovich (1973), and Lev (1973) that two-photon exchange with equivalent sharing of momentum transfer must be considered, and on the soft-photon approximation work of Maximon and Tjon (2000) where polarization effects cancel exactly. The results enable estimation of VCS contributions to radiative corrections for polarization observables in elastic ep-scattering, complementing the standard QED calculations of Afanasiev, Akushevich and Merenkov (2001) which found small effects from Bethe-Heitler and infrared box mechanisms.
