# A hydrodynamic study of hyperon spin polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We perform a systematic study of the spin polarization of hyperons in heavy-ion collisions using the MUSIC hydrodynamic model with A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) pre-equilibrium dynamics. Our model calculations nicely describe the measured collision-energy, centrality, rapidity, and $p_T$ dependence of $Λ$ polarization. We also study and predict the global spin polarization of $Ξ^-$ and $Ω^-$ as a function of collision energy, which provides a baseline for the studies of the magnetic moment, spin, and mass dependence of the spin polarization. For the local spin polarization, we calculate the radial and azimuthal components of the transverse $Λ$ polarization and find specific modulating behavior which could reflect the circular vortical structure. However, our model fails to describe the azimuthal-angle dependence of the longitudinal and transverse $Λ$ polarization, which indicates that the hydrodynamic framework with the spin Cooper-Frye formula under the assumption of thermal equilibrium of spin degree of freedom needs to be improved.

## Key claim

The AMPT+MUSIC hydrodynamic model successfully describes the collision-energy, centrality, rapidity, and transverse momentum dependence of global Lambda hyperon polarization, but it fails to reproduce the azimuthal-angle dependence of both longitudinal and transverse Lambda polarization, indicating that the hydrodynamic framework with the spin Cooper-Frye formula under the assumption of thermal equilibrium of spin degrees of freedom needs to be improved.

## Method

The authors used the MUSIC 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model with initial conditions generated from the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which provides the full energy-momentum tensor and net baryon current for hydrodynamic evolution. The spin polarization of hyperons was calculated using the spin Cooper-Frye formula, which connects the spin polarization vector to the thermal vorticity on the freeze-out hypersurface, with results Lorentz-transformed to the particle rest frame for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Global Lambda polarization decreases with collision energy from 7.7 A GeV to 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions, fitting STAR data within error bars
- For 27 A GeV Au+Au collisions, global Lambda polarization as a function of centrality shows the same trend as experimental measurements
- The predicted global spin polarization ordering is P(Omega-) > P(Xi-) approximately equal to P(Lambda), with mass ratios m(Omega-):m(Xi-):m(Lambda) = 1.5:1.2:1 and spin ratios S(Omega-):S(Xi-):S(Lambda) = 3:1:1
- Initial time tau_0 for hydrodynamic evolution ranges from 0.4 fm/c for 200 A GeV to 2.0 fm/c for 7.7 A GeV Au+Au collisions
- Switching energy density E_sw ranges from 0.65 GeV/fm^3 for 200 A GeV to 0.35 GeV/fm^3 for 7.7 A GeV
- Specific shear viscosity eta/s is set to 0.08 for 200, 62.4, and 39 A GeV, increasing to 0.16 for 7.7 A GeV

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic calculations of Lambda polarization by Karpenko (2016), Xie (2017), and Ivanov (2019) that reproduced the energy dependence of global polarization but failed to reproduce the sign of azimuthal-angle dependence of local polarization. The study provides predictions for Xi- and Omega- polarization as a baseline for investigating magnetic moment, spin, and mass dependence, and enables future comparison with precise experimental measurements to probe magnetic fields at freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions.
