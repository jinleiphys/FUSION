# Thermal and Quantum Fluctuations in the Kaon Condensed Phase

> A new formulation is presented to treat thermal and quantum fluctuations around the kaon condensate on the basis of chiral symmetry; separating the zero-mode from the beginning and using the path-integral method, we can formulate the inclusion of fluctuations in a transparent way. Nucleons as well as kaons are treated in a self-consistent way to the one-loop order. The effects of the Goldstone mode, stemming from the breakdown of V- spin symmetry in the condensed phase, are figured out. A procedure is discussed to renormalize the divergent integrals properly up to the one-loop order. Consequently the thermodynamic potential is derived. It is pointed out that the zero-point fluctuation by nucleons gives a sizable effect, different from the kaonic one.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new formalism for treating thermal and quantum fluctuations around the kaon condensate, claiming that the zero-point fluctuation by nucleons gives a sizable effect on the ground-state property of the condensed phase, unlike the kaonic fluctuation which is small.

## Method

The authors use a path-integral formulation based on chiral symmetry, separating the zero-mode from the beginning by introducing fluctuations through successive chiral rotations on the SU(3) manifold. They treat nucleons and kaons self-consistently to one-loop order using the Hartree approximation, summing infinite series of bubble diagrams composed of one-loop diagrams by kaons and nucleons. The thermodynamic potential is derived and renormalized by introducing counterterms corresponding to nucleon and kaon masses, and the KN and KK interaction vertices.

## Key numbers

* The kaon condensation may occur at relatively low densities of (3-4)ρ₀, where ρ₀ is the nuclear density ≃ 0.16 fm⁻³ in neutron star matter.
* The effective nucleon mass is given by M*ᵢ = M − Σ_{Ki}/f² cos⟨θ⟩ n_K¹ − Σ_{Ki}(1−cos⟨θ⟩), where n_K¹ is the thermal kaon-loop contribution.
* The self-energy term b = f⁻² Tr[G_H⁻¹(3+τ₃)/8] = (4f²)⁻¹(ρ_n+2ρ_p) with nucleon number densities ρ_i (i=n,p).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field theory treatment of kaon condensation by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory approach of Thorsson and Ellis, who included only kaon zero-point fluctuations at T=0. The formalism enables consistent finite-temperature calculations for the kaon condensed phase, which is relevant for protoneutron star physics where temperatures rise to several tens of MeV, and enables study of relaxation processes from nonequilibrium states to the kaon condensed state in equilibrium.
