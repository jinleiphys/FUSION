# Third-order momentum correlation interferometry maps for entangled quantal states of three singly trapped massive ultracold fermions

> Analytic higher-order momentum correlation functions associated with the time-of-flight spectroscopy of three ultracold fermionic atoms singly-confined in a linear three-well optical trap are presented, corresponding to the W- and Greenberger-Horne-Zeilinger-type (GHZ) states that belong to characteristic classes of tripartite entanglement and represent the strong-interaction regime captured by a three-site Heisenberg Hamiltonian. The methodology introduced here contrasts with and goes beyond that based on the standard Wick's factorization scheme; it enables determination of both third-order and second-order spin-resolved and spin-unresolved momentum correlations, aiming at matter-wave interference investigations with trapped massive particles in analogy with, and having the potential for expanding the scope of, recent three-photon quantum-optics interferometry.

## Key claim
The paper presents analytic third-order and second-order momentum correlation functions for three ultracold fermionic atoms in a linear three-well trap, specifically for the W and GHZ entangled states that arise from the strong-interaction regime of a three-site Heisenberg Hamiltonian. The methodology goes beyond the standard Wick's factorization scheme, which is limited to determinantal spin-non-degenerate ground states and cannot treat the spin-degenerate entangled states studied here.

## Method
The authors solve the three-site Heisenberg Hamiltonian for three singly-trapped fermions, obtaining the W and GHZ eigenstates. They then construct many-body wave functions in first-quantization form using momentum-dependent Wannier-type spin-orbitals represented by displaced Gaussian functions. From these wave functions, they derive analytic expressions for both spin-resolved and spin-unresolved third-order and second-order momentum correlation functions, using the algebraic language MATHEMATICA to carry out the determinant calculations.

## Key numbers
- Gaussian width parameter: s = 0.5 μm; well separation: D = 3.8 μm (used for all plotted correlation maps)
- Three W-state energies: E₁ = -3J/2 (S=1/2), E₂ = -J/2 (S=1/2), E₃ = 0 (S=3/2), where J is the exchange coupling
- GHZ state energy: E_GHZ = 0 (S=3/2, ⟨S_z⟩=0)
- Third-order spin-unresolved correlation formula (Eq. g3un) contains coefficients A_i, B_i, C_i from Table I: for i=1 (E=-3J/2): A=1, B=-1, C=-1; for i=2 (E=-J/2): A=-1, B=1, C=-1; for i=3 (E=0): A=-2, B=-1, C=2

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of second-order momentum correlations for ultracold atoms (Bran et al. 2017, 2018; Yannouleas et al. 2018, 2019) to third-order correlations for entangled states. It contrasts with the standard Wick's factorization scheme used by Hodgman et al. (2011, 2013) and others for Gaussian-type ground states, which cannot treat spin-degenerate entangled states like W and GHZ. The results enable matter-wave interference studies with trapped massive particles in analogy with three-photon quantum-optics interferometry (Agne et al. 2017; Menssen et al. 2017), and open avenues for experimental time-of-flight measurements on entangled states relevant to quantum information theory.
