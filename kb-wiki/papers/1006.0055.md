# Heavy Quarkonium Melting in Large N Thermal QCD

> Large N QCD is mostly governed by planar diagrams and should show linear confinement when these diagrams are suitably summed. The linear confinement of quarks in a class of these theories using gravity duals that capture the logarithmic runnings of the coupling constants in the IR and strongly coupled asymptotic conformal behavior in the UV was studied in our previous work. We also extended the theories to high temperatures and argued the possibilities of meltings and suppressions of heavy quarkonium states. In this paper we give a formal proof of melting using very generic choices of UV completions, and point out some subtleties associated with meltings in generic large N theories. Our proof requires only the existence of well defined UV behaviors that are devoid of Landau poles and UV divergences of the Wilson loops, allowing degrees of freedom to increase monotonously with energy scale. We determine the melting temperatures of heavy quarkonium states, which could suggest the presence of deconfinement phase transitions in these theories.

# Heavy Quarkonium Melting in Large N Thermal QCD

## Key claim

The authors provide a formal proof that heavy quarkonium states melt in a class of large N thermal QCD theories with well-defined UV behaviors, but the melting does not correspond to a complete disappearance of the linear potential; rather, the linear potential ceases to have infinite range, and as temperature increases the range of the linear potential quickly becomes short. The proof requires only the existence of UV completions devoid of Landau poles and UV divergences of Wilson loops, with degrees of freedom that increase monotonically with energy scale.

## Method

The authors use gauge-gravity duality with a supergravity background constructed from D3, D5, and D7 branes and anti-branes, capturing logarithmic running of coupling constants in the IR and asymptotically conformal behavior in the UV. They analyze the Nambu-Goto action for a U-shaped string connecting a quark-antiquark pair in a black hole background, deriving constraint equations that determine when the interquark distance can become infinite (indicating linear confinement) versus when it reaches a finite maximum (indicating melting). Numerical analyses are performed for two specific choices of exponential warp factors with parameters (alpha, alpha-tilde, beta, beta-tilde) = (0.1, 3, 0.05, 3) and (0.1, 4, 0.05, 4).

## Key numbers

- For the cubic warp factor (alpha-tilde=3), the critical temperature is T_c^(1) ~ 0.28 (in units of 1/u_h)
- For the quartic warp factor (alpha-tilde=4), the critical temperature is T_c^(2) ~ 0.399 (in units of 1/u_h)
- The effective range of critical temperatures lies between 0.2 and 0.4 (in units of 1/u_h)
- After restoring units, the boundary melting temperature T_c lies in the range 0.91/L^2 to 1.06/L^2, where L is the asymptotic AdS throat radius
- The zero-temperature Coulomb potential is V_{QQ} = -0.236/d + O(d) (with AdS throat radius L and string tension T_0 set to 1)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous construction of a supergravity dual for large N QCD (Mia, Dasgupta, Gale, Jeon, 2009; 2010) that captures logarithmic running of couplings in the IR and conformal behavior in the UV. It provides a formal proof of melting that was only argued qualitatively in their earlier work on the heavy quarkonium potential. The numerical results suggest a deconfinement phase transition near T_c, with the screening length d_max behaving as C + exp(-gamma T) near the critical temperature. The approach contrasts with pure AdS or AdS with an IR cut-off duals, which cannot reproduce the additional constraint equation arising from the background RG flow. The work enables future studies of heavy quarkonium spectral densities and extensions to supersymmetric theories.
