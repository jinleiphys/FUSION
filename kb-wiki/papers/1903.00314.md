# Importance of initial and final state effects for azimuthal correlations in p+Pb collisions

> We study the influence and interplay of initial state and final state effects in the dynamics of small systems, focusing on azimuthal correlations at different multiplicities. To this end we introduce a new model, matching the classical Yang-Mills dynamics of pre-equilibrium gluon fields (IP-GLASMA) to a perturbative QCD based parton cascade for the final state evolution (BAMPS) on an event-by-event basis. Depending on multiplicity of the event, we see transverse momentum dependent signatures of the initial, but also the final state in azimuthal correlation observables, such as $v_2\left\lbrace 2PC\right\rbrace(p_T)$. In low-multiplicity events, initial state correlations dominate for transverse momenta $p_T>2~\mathrm{GeV}$, whereas in high-multiplicity events and at low momenta final state interactions dominate and initial state correlations strongly affect $v_2\left\lbrace 2PC\right\rbrace(p_T)$ for $p_T>2~\mathrm{GeV}$ as well as the $p_T$ integrated $v_2\left\lbrace 2PC\right\rbrace$. Nearly half of the final pT integrated $v_2\left\lbrace 2PC\right\rbrace$ is contributed by the initial state in low-multiplicity events, whereas in high-multiplicity the share is much less. Based on Ref. [1], we are now able to carry out a systematic multiplicity scan, probing the dynamics on the border of initial state dominated to final state dominated - but not yet hydrodynamic regime.

## Key claim

Initial state momentum correlations and final state interactions both contribute significantly to azimuthal correlations in p+Pb collisions, with their relative importance depending on event multiplicity and transverse momentum; in low-multiplicity events initial state correlations dominate for pT > 2 GeV, while in high-multiplicity events final state interactions dominate at low momenta but initial state correlations still strongly affect v2{2PC}(pT) for pT > 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors matched the classical Yang-Mills dynamics of pre-equilibrium gluon fields from the IP-GLASMA model to the perturbative QCD based parton cascade BAMPS on an event-by-event basis. They extracted gluon Wigner distributions from IP-GLASMA at τ0 = 0.2 fm/c, sampled individual gluons, and evolved them via the relativistic Boltzmann equation in BAMPS from 0.2 fm/c to 2.0 fm/c using elastic and radiative pQCD cross sections with fixed αs = 0.3. To isolate initial state effects, they performed control simulations where the azimuthal angle of each gluon's transverse momentum was randomized before the parton cascade evolution.

## Key numbers

- In low-multiplicity events (⟨dNg/dy⟩ = 6), nearly half (approximately 45%) of the final pT integrated v2{2PC} is contributed by the initial state
- In high-multiplicity events (⟨dNg/dy⟩ = 26), approximately 15% of the final pT integrated v2{2PC} is contributed by the initial state
- Average number of interactions in low-multiplicity events: Nscat = 4.5 ± 1.1; in high-multiplicity events: Nscat = 5.6 ± 1.1
- Average number of large angle scatterings in low-multiplicity events: Nscat^large-angle = 0.53 ± 0.14; in high-multiplicity events: Nscat^large-angle = 1.0 ± 0.18
- In the randomized azimuth scenario, final state interactions alone build up v2(pT) of approximately 4% at pT ∼ 2 GeV in high multiplicity events and less than 3% at pT ∼ 1 GeV in low multiplicity events

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether azimuthal correlations in small collision systems arise from initial state momentum correlations (as in the color glass condensate picture) or from final state hydrodynamic response to geometry. Previous studies by Dusling (2015) and Schlichting (2016) had treated either intrinsic momentum correlations or purely final state driven effects separately. This paper presents the first combined framework, building on the authors' earlier work (Greif 2017), enabling a systematic multiplicity scan that probes the transition from initial-state-dominated to final-state-dominated dynamics without requiring a hydrodynamic regime.
