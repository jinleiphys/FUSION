# Meson mass at real and imaginary chemical potentials

> The chemical-potential dependence of pi and sigma meson masses is analyzed at both real and imaginary chemical potentials, $μ_\mathrm{R}$ and $μ_\mathrm{I}$, by using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model that possesses both the extended ${\mathbb Z}_3$ symmetry and the chiral symmetry. In the $μ_\mathrm{I}$ region, the meson masses have the Roberge-Weiss periodicity. Assuming that the meson masses will be measured at finite $μ_\mathrm{I}$ by lattice QCD in future, we simulate how meson masses at finite $μ_\mathrm{R}$ are extracted from those at finite $μ_\mathrm{I}$, and propose a reliable extraction method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that meson masses (pi and sigma) at real chemical potential can be reliably extracted from lattice QCD data at imaginary chemical potential using the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, provided the lattice data are measured at temperatures near the deconfinement pseudo-critical temperature where the theta-dependence of meson masses becomes stronger.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor PNJL model in the mean field approximation with a Polyakov-loop potential fitted to pure-gauge lattice QCD data, calculating pi and sigma meson masses from the pole of the mesonic correlation function in the random phase approximation. They analyzed the chemical-potential dependence of meson masses at both real and imaginary chemical potentials, and tested two extrapolation methods (exponential and polynomial) from imaginary to real chemical potential by comparing them with direct PNJL calculations.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at T=mu=0: M_pi = 138 MeV, used as input to fix model parameters
- Pion decay constant at T=mu=0: f_pi = 93.3 MeV, used as input
- Pseudo-critical temperature of deconfinement at mu=0: T_c = 170 MeV in the PNJL model (compared to 173 +/- 8 MeV from full lattice QCD)
- At T=160 MeV, the normalized Fourier coefficients a_k(T)/a_0(T) for meson masses are about 0.1% for k=1 and negligibly small for k >= 2
- The PNJL result and the two extrapolation results coincide accurately up to mu_R/T approximately 1
- Two cases of bare quark mass are compared: m_0 = 5.5 MeV and m_0 = 80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Sakai, 2008) on the imaginary chemical-potential matching approach, where the PNJL model parameters are determined from lattice QCD data in the imaginary chemical potential region. The paper addresses the open problem of determining the vector-type four-quark interaction strength, which cannot be fixed at zero chemical potential and affects the location of the chiral phase transition critical endpoint. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations to determine model parameters from meson mass measurements at imaginary chemical potential, and show that the polynomial extrapolation method used by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2002) and D'Elia and Lombardo (2003) for thermodynamic quantities is essentially equivalent to the exponential extrapolation for meson masses.
