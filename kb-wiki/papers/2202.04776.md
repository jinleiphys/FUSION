# Coherent photoproduction of the neutral pion and eta meson on the deuteron at incident energies below 1.15 GeV

> Coherent photoproduction of the neutral pion and eta meson on the deuteron, $γ{d}${$\to$}$π^0η{d}$, has been experimentally studied at incident photon energies ranging from the reaction threshold to 1.15 GeV. The total cross section demonstrates a rapid rise below 1 GeV. The data are underestimated by the existing theoretical calculations based on quasi-free $π^0 η$ photoproduction on the nucleon followed by deuteron coalescence. At the same time, the data are rather well reproduced by the calculations taking into account the final-state interaction. We have also measured for the first time the differential cross sections: the $π^0 η$ invariant-mass distribution $dσ/dM_{πη}$, the $π^0 d$ invariant-mass distribution $dσ/dM_{πd}$, the $ηd$ invariant-mass distribution $dσ/dM_{ηd}$, and the distribution over the deuteron emission angle $dσ/dΩ_d$ in the overall center-of-mass frame. The measured cross section $dσ/dΩ_d$ does not exhibit strongly backward-peaking behavior predicted by the calculations. At all incident energies, an increase in $dσ/dM_{ηd}$ near the $ηd$ threshold is observed, which indicates a bound or virtual $ηd$ state resulting from a strong attraction between $η$ and a deuteron. The possibilities of using coherent $π^0η$ photoproduction on a nucleus to study the $η$-nuclear interaction are also discussed.

## Key claim

The total cross section for coherent $\gamma d \to \pi^0 \eta d$ rises rapidly below 1 GeV and is underestimated by quasi-free calculations, but is well reproduced when final-state interactions are included; the $\eta d$ invariant-mass distribution shows a near-threshold enhancement at all incident energies, suggesting a bound or virtual $\eta d$ state.

## Method

The experiment used a bremsstrahlung photon beam from a 1.20-GeV electron synchrotron at ELPH, incident on a liquid deuterium target (effective thickness 45.9 mm). Final-state particles ($\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma$, $\eta \to \gamma\gamma$, and deuteron) were detected with the FOREST electromagnetic calorimeter system covering 88% of the solid angle. Events were selected via a 6-constraint kinematic fit for the $\gamma d \to \pi^0\eta d$ hypothesis, with a $\chi^2$ probability cut above 0.2, and a separate kinematic fit for the quasi-free $\gamma p' \to \pi^0\eta p$ hypothesis was used to reject background (requiring $\chi^2$ probability below 0.01). Acceptance corrections were determined from Geant4 Monte Carlo simulations tuned to reproduce the measured distributions over $M_{\pi d}$, $M_{\eta d}$, and $\cos\theta_d$.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section $\sigma$ measured for incident photon energies from threshold to 1.15 GeV; data show a rapid rise below 1 GeV.
* Effective target thickness: 45.9 mm, corresponding to $\mathcal{N}_\tau = 0.237$ b$^{-1}$.
* Photon tagging energy range: 0.75 to 1.15 GeV; average photon transmittance (tagging efficiency) $\approx 53\%$.
* Energy resolutions of FOREST electromagnetic calorimeters for 1-GeV photons: forward (CsI) $\approx 3\%$, central (lead scintillating-fiber) $\approx 7\%$, backward (lead glass) $\approx 5\%$.
* Background contamination: quasi-free $\gamma p' \to \pi^0\eta p$ less than 0.5%; quasi-free $\gamma n' \to \pi^0\eta n$ much less than 0.1%; non-quasi-free $\gamma d \to \pi^0\pi^0 p n$ less than 0.2%.
* Systematic uncertainty from kinematic fit selection: 4% to 6% (standard deviation of accepted events when changing lower CKF probability limit from 0.1 to 0.5).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Ishikawa et al., 2021) with a systematic analysis of additional differential distributions. The data are compared to theoretical calculations by Egorov and Fix (2013) and by Egorov (2020), which include impulse approximation and final-state interaction (FSI) treatments. The measured $d\sigma/d\Omega_d$ does not show the strongly backward-peaking behavior predicted by calculations. The observed near-threshold enhancement in $d\sigma/dM_{\eta d}$ at all incident energies indicates a strong $\eta$-deuteron attraction, consistent with signatures seen in reactions such as $pd \to \eta\,{}^3$He (Mayer et al., 1996) and $\gamma\,{}^3$He $\to \eta\,{}^3$He (Pfeiffer et al., 2004; Pfeiffer et al., 2005; Pheron et al., 2012). The results enable further study of $\eta$-nuclear interactions using coherent $\pi^0\eta$ photoproduction on nuclei, which avoids complications from initial-state interactions present in hadronic processes.
