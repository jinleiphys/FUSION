# Effective field theory analysis of the Coulomb breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus 19C

> We analyse the Coulomb breakup of 19C measured at 67A MeV at RIKEN. We use the Coulomb-Corrected Eikonal (CCE) approximation to model the reaction and describe the one-neutron halo nucleus 19C within Halo Effective Field Theory (EFT). At leading order we obtain a fair reproduction of the measured cross section as a function of energy and angle. The description is insensitive to the choice of optical potential, as long as it accurately represents the size of 18C. It is also insensitive to the interior of the 19C wave function. Comparison between theory and experiment thus enables us to infer asymptotic properties of the ground state of 19C: these data put constraints on the one-neutron separation energy of this nucleus and, for a given binding energy, can be used to extract an asymptotic normalisation coefficient (ANC). These results are confirmed by CCE calculations employing next-to-leading order Halo EFT descriptions of 19C: at this order the results for the Coulomb breakup cross section are completely insensitive to the choice of the regulator. Accordingly, this reaction can be used to constrain the one-neutron separation energy and ANC of 19C.

## Key claim
The Coulomb breakup of 19C measured at 67A MeV at RIKEN can be reproduced fairly at leading order in Halo Effective Field Theory, and the description is insensitive to the choice of optical potential as long as it accurately represents the size of 18C and to the interior of the 19C wave function, enabling extraction of asymptotic properties of the ground state.

## Method
The authors used the Coulomb-Corrected Eikonal (CCE) approximation to model the reaction of 19C on a 208Pb target at 67A MeV. They described the one-neutron halo nucleus 19C within Halo Effective Field Theory using Gaussian potentials for the 18C-neutron interaction, adjusting the potential depth at leading order to reproduce a specific one-neutron separation energy and at next-to-leading order to also reproduce a specific asymptotic normalisation coefficient (ANC). The theoretical cross sections were folded with the experimental resolution from Nakamura et al. (1999) and compared to data.

## Key numbers
- One-neutron separation energy S_n = 0.58 ± 0.09 MeV (from Masses212, used as input for the potentials)
- Extracted ANC: C_{0s1/2} = 0.81 ± 0.02 fm^{-1/2} (from fitting scaled LO calculations to data in the forward-angle region θ < 2° and relative energy 0 ≤ E ≤ 0.5 MeV)
- Gaussian regulator widths σ considered: 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5 fm (for LO potentials)
- Core size of 18C: approximately 2.5 fm; halo size of 19C: estimated about 6.5 fm

## Context
This work extends the Halo EFT analysis of 19C photodissociation by Acharya and Phillips (2013), who used a rudimentary reaction theory and extracted S_n = 575 ± 55 (stat.) ± 20 (EFT) keV. By coupling Halo EFT to the more advanced CCE reaction model, the authors confirm the peripheral nature of the reaction and provide a more reliable extraction of the ANC. The approach follows the strategy successfully employed for 11Be and 15C reactions in Capel et al. (2018) and related works. This enables future studies to use Coulomb breakup as a systematic tool to constrain one-neutron separation energies and ANCs for one-neutron halo nuclei.
