# Multichannel oscillations and relations between LSND, KARMEN and MiniBooNE, with and without CP violation

> We show by examples that multichannel mixing can affect both the parameters extracted from neutrino oscillation experiments, and that more general conclusions derived by fitting the experimental data under the assumption that only two channels are involved in the mixing. Implications for MiniBooNE are noted and an example based on maximal CP violation displays profound implications for the two data sets (muon-neutrino and muon-antineutrino) of that experiment.

## Key claim

Multichannel mixing (involving more than two neutrino flavors or sterile states) can reconcile the apparent disagreement between the LSND and KARMEN antineutrino appearance results without requiring large disappearance rates at longer baselines, and the inclusion of CP violation further improves this agreement while making testable predictions for MiniBooNE.

## Method

The authors construct explicit analytic examples of antineutrino appearance probabilities using sums of sine-squared terms (for CP-conserving multichannel mixing) and additional sine terms (for CP-violating multichannel mixing), with parameters chosen to illustrate how a "rising baseline" from longer-wavelength oscillations modifies the simple two-channel oscillation pattern. They compare these multichannel probability functions to standard two-channel fits over the L/E ranges relevant to the KARMEN, LSND, and MiniBooNE experiments.

## Key numbers

- For the CP-conserving multichannel example "a": P = 0.005 sin²(0.7 L/E) + 0.001 sin²(0.3 L/E) + 0.0025 sin²(0.4 L/E), where the numerical coefficients in the sine arguments include the factor 1.27 and have units of eV².
- For the CP-conserving multichannel example "b": P = 0.004 sin²(0.7 L/E) + 0.005 sin²(0.2 L/E) + 0.002 sin²(0.5 L/E), with the same unit convention.
- For the CP-violating antineutrino appearance example: P = 0.0025 sin²(1.0 L/E) - 0.001 sin(2.0 L/E) + 0.0005 sin²(3.0 L/E) - 0.001 sin(6.0 L/E) + 0.001 sin²(4.0 L/E) + 0.001 sin(8.0 L/E) + 0.01 sin²(0.5 L/E), with the same unit convention.
- For the CP-violating neutrino appearance (CP conjugate): P = 0.0025 sin²(1.0 L/E) + 0.001 sin(2.0 L/E) + 0.0005 sin²(3.0 L/E) + 0.001 sin(6.0 L/E) + 0.001 sin²(4.0 L/E) - 0.001 sin(8.0 L/E) + 0.01 sin²(0.5 L/E).
- A normalization constant of 0.0026 is used for the very high Delta m² case, consistent with the scale of the LSND signal.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration (Goldman, Stephenson, McKellar, 2007) that a reduced-rank seesaw mechanism coupling multiple oscillations produces a "rising baseline" effect, analogous to the Fermi-Ulam problem of coupled oscillators. It directly addresses the tension between the LSND (Aguilar et al., 2001) and KARMEN (Eitel et al., 2000) antineutrino appearance results, which had been analyzed under the assumption of two-channel mixing. The paper enables future analyses of MiniBooNE data to test for multichannel effects and CP violation, and recommends that all oscillation experiments fit their data to functional forms including a rising baseline parameter T and a CP-violation parameter V, rather than reporting only two-channel fit parameters.
