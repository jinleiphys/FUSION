# Color coherence effects in the reaction $d(p,2p)n$

> The hard proton knock-out by the proton from the deuteron at relativistic energies is considered with a focus on the color transparency (CT) effect which influences the initial and final state interactions. The calculations are performed in the framework of the generalized eikonal approximation supplemented by the quantum diffusion model of CT. The main results of the previous calculations [1] at the beam momentum below 20 GeV/c are confirmed, including the dependence of the nuclear transparency on the transverse momentum of the spectator neutron, $p_{st}$, and on the relative azimuthal angle $ϕ$ between proton and neutron: absorption at $p_{st} < 0.2$ GeV/c and enhancement at $p_{st} > 0.3$ GeV/c due to rescattering on the neutron, the change of $ϕ$-dependence between these two regions, and the enhancement of CT effects with $p_{\rm lab}$. The study is then generalized to higher beam momenta, up to 75 GeV/c. It is shown that such behavior of the transparency is mainly preserved up to $p_{\rm lab}\simeq 50$ GeV/c, but changes significantly at higher beam momenta due to the interference of valence quark configurations of small and large sizes. As a result, the transparency at small $p_{st}$ exhibits oscillations as a function of the beam momentum (the nuclear filtering effect). The tensor analyzing power due to the longitudinal polarization of the deuteron is calculated. The event rate at NICA-SPD is estimated.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that color transparency effects in the reaction \(d(p,2p)n\) at beam momenta below 20 GeV/c produce absorption at low spectator neutron transverse momentum and enhancement at high transverse momentum, and extends this analysis to show that this behavior is preserved up to about 50 GeV/c but changes significantly at higher momenta due to interference between small- and large-size valence quark configurations, with the caveat that the coherence length parameter \(\Delta M^2\) remains uncertain within a range of 0.7 to 3 GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The calculations use the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) combined with the quantum diffusion model (QDM) to incorporate color transparency effects in the hard proton knock-out reaction \(d(p,2p)n\) at large center-of-mass angles. The hard elastic \(pp\) scattering amplitude is separated into a quark-counting (QC) contribution that forms point-like configurations and a Landshoff contribution that does not, with color transparency applied only to the QC part of the rescattering amplitudes. The soft elastic \(pn\) amplitudes are parameterized in the standard high-energy Glauber form, and the deuteron wave function from the Paris potential model is used.

## Key numbers

* The beam momentum range studied extends from 6 GeV/c up to 75 GeV/c.
* The transparency at small spectator neutron transverse momentum \(p_{st} < 0.2\) GeV/c shows absorption, while at \(p_{st} > 0.3\) GeV/c there is enhancement due to rescattering on the neutron.
* The behavior of the transparency is mainly preserved up to \(p_{\rm lab} \simeq 50\) GeV/c, but changes significantly at higher beam momenta.
* The maxima of the scaled \(pp\) elastic cross section at \(\Theta_{c.m.}=90^\circ\) are located at \(p_{\rm lab} = 1.7, 5.4, 14.8,\) and 42.6 GeV/c, while minima are at \(p_{\rm lab} = 3.2, 8.9, 24.8,\) and 75.1 GeV/c.
* The coherence length parameter \(\Delta M^2\) is set to 0.7 and 3 GeV\(^2\) to estimate the uncertainty range of the proton coherence length.
* The average transverse momentum of a quark in a proton is \(\sqrt{\langle k_t^2\rangle} = 0.35\) GeV/c.
* The slope parameter for soft \(pn\) scattering is given by \(B_{\rm p n} = 5.0 + 4s^{0.0808}\) (in GeV\(^{-2}\)) for high beam momenta.

## Context

This work extends the previous calculations of Frankfurt et al. (1996) for the \(d(p,2p)n\) reaction at beam momenta below 20 GeV/c to higher momenta up to 75 GeV/c, covering the energy range accessible at NICA-SPD. The study is related to earlier AGS experiments on \(A(p,pp)\) reactions on heavy targets (Aclander et al., 2004) which showed anomalous nuclear transparency behavior, and to the theoretical framework of Ralston and Pire (1988) and Jennings and Miller (1991-1993) that introduced interference of small- and large-size configurations. The results enable planning of new experiments at NICA-SPD for studying color transparency effects and generalized parton distributions in \(pd\) collisions.
