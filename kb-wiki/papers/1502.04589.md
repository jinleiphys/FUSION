# Recent progress in hard-thermal-loop QCD thermodynamics and collective excitations

> I review recent developments in QCD thermodynamics and collective excitations from the hard-thermal-loop effective theory. I begin by motivating the discussion with open questions from heavy-ion collisions. I then discuss a finite-temperature and -density calculation of QCD thermodynamics at NNLO from the hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory. Finally I discuss a recent exploration of generalizing the hard-thermal-loop framework to the (chromo)magnetic scale $g^2T$, from which a novel massless mode is uncovered.

## Key claim

The hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) framework, when extended to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) at finite temperature and chemical potential, yields thermodynamic functions (pressure, trace anomaly, speed of sound) and susceptibilities that agree well with lattice QCD data for temperatures above about 200–300 MeV, provided the central renormalization scale is used; the agreement near the pseudo-critical temperature \(T_c \sim 160\) MeV may be fortuitous because HTLpt does not incorporate the center symmetry \(Z(N_c)\).

## Method

The author reviews the NNLO thermodynamic potential for QCD at finite temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\), derived from the hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) reorganization scheme. The calculation involves a double expansion: first in the formal parameter \(\delta\) (set to 1 at the end) and then in the small ratios \(m_D/T\) and \(m_q/T\) (where \(m_D\) is the Debye mass and \(m_q\) the quark thermal mass), yielding an analytic expression accurate to order \(g^5\) in the weak-coupling limit. The author also summarizes a first exploration of collective excitations at the (chromo)magnetic scale \(g^2T\) by using the Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) action at finite temperature, which introduces a Gribov parameter \(\gamma_G\) that provides an infrared cutoff at the magnetic scale and modifies the gluon propagator.

## Key numbers

*   The NNLO HTLpt pressure for \(N_f=2+1\) at \(\mu_B=0\) agrees with lattice data for \(T \gtrsim 200\) MeV (central scale).
*   The NNLO HTLpt trace anomaly for \(N_f=2+1\) at \(\mu_B=0\) agrees with lattice data for \(T \gtrsim 220\) MeV (central scale).
*   The NNLO HTLpt speed of sound for \(N_f=2+1\) at \(\mu_B=0\) agrees with lattice data when the central scale is used.
*   The NNLO HTLpt 2nd-order baryon number susceptibility for \(N_f=2+1\) agrees with lattice data for \(T \gtrsim 300\) MeV.
*   The one-loop Gribov gap equation at asymptotically high temperature gives \(\gamma_G = \frac{D-1}{D} \frac{N_c}{4\sqrt{2}\pi} g^2 T\), providing an infrared cutoff at the magnetic scale \(g^2T\).
*   The renormalization scale \(\Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}}\) is fixed to 176 MeV by requiring \(\alpha_s(1.5\ \text{GeV}) = 0.326\) from lattice measurements (Bazavov 2012).

## Context

This work extends the HTLpt program from finite \(T\) and zero \(\mu\) (Andersen 2009, 2010, 2011) to finite \(T\) and \(\mu\), enabling comparisons with lattice data at non-zero baryon chemical potential (Borsanyi 2012). The exploration of the magnetic scale via the Gribov-Zwanziger action (Gribov 1977, Zwanziger 1989) addresses the Linde problem (Linde 1980, Gross 1980) and uncovers a novel massless mode, which is a step toward describing a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma in the phenomenologically relevant regime where \(g \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\).
