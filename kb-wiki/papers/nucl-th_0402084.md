# Chiral symmetry and resonances in QCD

> We study the formation of resonances in terms of the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. At leading order parameter-free predictions are obtained for the scattering of Goldstone bosons off any hadron once we insist on approximate crossing symmetry of the unitarized scattering amplitude. A wealth of empirically established resonances are recovered and so far unseen resonances are predicted. Here we review in some detail the properties of light axial-vector mesons.

# Chiral symmetry and resonances in QCD

## Key claim

The authors show that the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian, combined with a unitarization scheme that enforces approximate crossing symmetry, generates parameter-free predictions for the spectrum of light axial-vector meson resonances as poles in Goldstone-boson–vector-meson scattering amplitudes. The claim is hedged by the condition that the subtraction scales in the renormalization scheme must be chosen close to the heavy meson masses and that the approximate crossing symmetry is imposed by matching s-channel and u-channel unitarized amplitudes at subthreshold kinematics.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for the scattering of Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) off vector mesons (rho, omega, K*, phi) using the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction kernel from the chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. The on-shell part of the scattering amplitude is extracted via a decomposition into projectors carrying good total angular momentum J and parity P, and the loop functions are renormalized in a physical scheme where the subtraction scale is set to the mass of the rho(770) meson for channels with strangeness 0 or ±2, and to the mass of the K(892) meson for channels with strangeness ±1. For broad vector mesons (rho and K*), the two-particle propagator is folded with spectral functions describing their decays into two pions or a pion and a kaon, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used in the leading-order interaction is f ≃ 90 MeV.
- In the heavy SU(3) limit (m_π,K,η ≃ 500 MeV, m_ρ,ω,K*,φ ≃ 900 MeV), the resonance states become bound states forming two degenerate octets at 1367 MeV and one singlet at 1289 MeV.
- Varying the subtraction scale by ±20% shifts the octet bound-state mass from 1383 MeV (increase) to 1353 MeV (decrease).
- In the light SU(3) limit (m_π,K,η ≃ 140 MeV, m_ρ,ω,K*,φ ≃ 700 MeV), no bound states or resonance signals are observed.
- The K_1(1270) resonance is predicted with a width of about 90 MeV, decaying dominantly into the K ρ channel; a second broader state at about 1300 MeV has a width of about 175 MeV from decay into the π K* channel.
- The h_1(1380) resonance is predicted with a narrow width of about 80 MeV; the h_1(1170) resonance has a large width of about 360 MeV from π ρ decay.
- The f_1(1285) resonance appears as a narrow state at 1341 MeV with a width of about 10 MeV (factor two smaller than the empirical value).
- The b_1(1235) resonance is found at 1310 MeV with a width compatible with the empirical value of about 140 MeV.
- The a_1(1260) resonance appears as a broad peak at about 1300 MeV, consistent with an empirical width of 250–600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel approach previously applied to scalar mesons by Törnqvist (1980s) and Van Beveren (1986), and systematized using chiral Lagrangians by other groups (e.g., NVA02, NP02). It is directly analogous to the authors' earlier results on baryon resonances (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2002; Granada and Copenhagen collaborations), where chiral SU(3) symmetry was shown to generate parameter-free the J^P = 1/2^- and J^P = 3/2^- baryon resonance spectrum. The key finding that the h_1(1380), f_1(1285), and b_1(1235) resonances are dynamically generated by their coupling to the K K* channel mirrors the structure of the scalar f_0(980) resonance and its strong coupling to K K, emphasizing the role of chiral SU(3) symmetry even for non-strange resonances. The results enable future incorporation of chiral correction terms to improve the leading-order predictions.
