# Responses of the chiral-magnetic-effect-sensitive sine observable to resonance backgrounds in heavy-ion collisions

> A new sine observable, $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$, has been proposed to measure the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions; $ΔS = \left \langle \sin φ_+ \right \rangle - \left \langle \sin φ_- \right \rangle$, where $φ_\pm$ are azimuthal angles of positively and negatively charged particles relative to the reaction plane and averages are event-wise, and $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ is a normalized event probability distribution. Preliminary STAR data reveal concave $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ distributions in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions. Studies with a multiphase transport (AMPT) and anomalous-viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) models show concave $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ distributions for CME signals and convex ones for typical resonance backgrounds. A recent hydrodynamic study, however, indicates concave shapes for backgrounds as well. To better understand these results, we report a systematic study of the elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) and transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) dependences of resonance backgrounds with toy-model simulations and central limit theorem (CLT) calculations. It is found that the concavity or convexity of $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$ depends sensitively on the resonance $v_2$ (which yields different numbers of decay $π^+π^-$ pairs in the in-plane and out-of-plane directions) and $p_T$ (which affects the opening angle of the decay $π^+π^-$ pair). Qualitatively, low $p_{T}$ resonances decay into large opening-angle pairs and result in more `back-to-back' pairs out-of-plane, mimicking a CME signal, or a concave $R_{Ψ_2}(ΔS)$. Supplemental studies of $R_{Ψ_3}(ΔS)$ in terms of the triangular flow ($v_3$), where only backgrounds exist but any CME would average to zero, are also presented.

## Key claim

The concavity or convexity of the CME-sensitive sine observable \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)\) depends sensitively on the resonance elliptic flow (\(v_2\)) and transverse momentum (\(p_T\)), meaning that resonance backgrounds can produce either concave or convex distributions depending on resonance kinematics, contrary to earlier assumptions that backgrounds always yield convex shapes.

## Method

The authors performed toy-model Monte Carlo simulations of \(\rho\) meson decays into \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs, scanning the resonance elliptic flow \(v_{2,\rho}\) and transverse momentum \(p_{T,\rho}\) while fixing primordial pion properties from data. They also carried out central limit theorem (CLT) calculations to analytically understand the behavior of the \(C_{\Psi_2}\), \(C_{\Psi_2}^{\perp}\), and \(R_{\Psi_2}\) observables. Supplemental studies were conducted using the third-order harmonic plane \(\Psi_3\) with two different definitions of the sine observable.

## Key numbers

- The default \(v_{3,\rho}(p_T)\) was approximated as \(v_{3,\rho}(p_T) = \frac{1}{2}v_{2,\rho}(p_T)\) (half the magnitude of the default \(v_{2,\rho}(p_T)\) distribution).
- In the \(p_{T,\rho}\) scan, \(v_{2,\rho}\) was fixed to 0.06 to isolate the \(p_T\) dependence.
- The RMS (root mean square) of \(C_{\Psi_2}\) and \(C_{\Psi_2}^{\perp}\) was calculated as a function of \(v_{2,\rho}\) and \(p_{T,\rho}\) to quantify the shape changes.
- For the \(v_3\) studies, \(v_{3,\pi}\) was fixed to 0, and in one scan \(v_{3,\rho}\) was fixed to 0.03.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using AMPT and AVFD models (Magdy 2017) which showed concave \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)\) for CME signals and convex shapes for typical resonance backgrounds, and a hydrodynamic study (Bozek 2017) that indicated concave shapes for backgrounds as well. The results reconcile these conflicting findings by demonstrating that resonance backgrounds can produce either concave or convex \(R_{\Psi_2}(\Delta S)\) depending on resonance \(v_2\) and \(p_T\). This enables more careful interpretation of preliminary STAR data from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions (Roy 2017) and provides guidance for using the \(R_{\Psi_3}(\Delta S)\) observable as a background-only control.
