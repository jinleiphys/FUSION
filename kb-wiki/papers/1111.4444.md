# Fragmentation functions and parton distribution functions for the pion with the nonlocal interactions

> We study the unpolarized fragmentation functions and parton distribution functions of the pion employing the nonlocal chiral quark model. This model manifests the nonlocal interactions between the quarks and pseudoscalar mesons in the light-cone coordinate. It turns out that the nonlocal interactions result in substantial differences in comparison to typical models with only local couplings. We also perform the high Q^2-evolution for our results calculated at a relatively low renormalization scale Q^2 ~ 0.36 GeV}^2 to be compared with the experimental data. Our results after evolution are in qualitatively good agreement with those data.

## Key claim

The nonlocal chiral quark model (NLChQM), motivated by the instanton vacuum, predicts substantially larger elementary fragmentation functions for the pion than local models (NJL, PS, PV schemes) because the momentum-dependent effective quark mass already incorporates significant pion-cloud contributions; after renormalization and DGLAP evolution to high \(Q^2\), the results show only qualitative agreement with experimental data, with underestimates in the small-\(z\) and small-\(x\) regions.

## Method

The authors computed the elementary fragmentation function for the process \(u \to \pi^+ d\) using the nonlocal chiral quark model (NLChQM) in the light-cone coordinate, where the quark-pseudoscalar meson coupling is momentum-dependent and acts as a natural UV regulator. The integrated fragmentation function was obtained by integrating over the transverse momentum \(\bm{k}_T\), and the parton distribution function was extracted via the Drell-Levy-Yan (DLY) relation. Both functions were then evolved to high \(Q^2\) using DGLAP evolution with the code QCDNUM for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The initial renormalization scale is \(\mu \approx 0.6\) GeV, corresponding to \(Q^2 \approx 0.36\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\), set by the instanton parameters \(\bar{\rho} \approx 1/3\) fm and \(\bar{R} \approx 1\) fm.
- The first moment of the elementary fragmentation function (summed over pion isospin states) is \(\mathcal{N}^{\mathrm{present}}_{\pi/u} = 0.3685\), compared to \(\mathcal{N}^{\mathrm{NJL}}_{\pi/u} = 0.0257\), \(\mathcal{N}^{\mathrm{PS}}_{\pi/u} = 0.0843\), and \(\mathcal{N}^{\mathrm{PV}}_{\pi/u} = 0.1684\).
- The constituent quark mass is \(M_0 \approx 350\) MeV, determined self-consistently from the instanton parameters.
- The pion mass is taken as \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV and the current quark mass as \(m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV.
- DGLAP evolution was performed up to \(Q^2 = 4\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) for the minus-type (valence) quark distribution and up to \(Q^2 = 27\,\mathrm{GeV}^2\) for the fragmentation function.

## Context

This work extends the study of fragmentation functions and parton distribution functions from local chiral models (e.g., the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of Bentz (1999) and Ito (2009), and the pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling schemes of Bacchetta (2002) and Amrath (2005)) to a nonlocal interaction framework motivated by the instanton liquid model of Shuryak (1981) and Diakonov (1985). The nonlocal interactions produce larger pion-cloud effects than local models, but the remaining discrepancies at small \(z\) and small \(x\) indicate that quark-jet and resonance contributions, as studied by Ito (2009) and Matevosyan (2011), are needed for a full description. The results enable future work on incorporating quark-jet contributions into the nonlocal framework and testing the DLY relation with momentum-dependent couplings.
