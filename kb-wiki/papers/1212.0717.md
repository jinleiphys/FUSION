# Pion and nucleon in two flavour QCD with unimproved Wilson fermions

> We calculate pion mass, pion decay constant, PCAC quark mass and nucleon mass in two flavour lattice QCD with unimproved Wilson fermion and gauge actions. Simulations are performed using DD-HMC algorithm at two lattice spacings and two volumes for several values of the quark mass. The cutoff effects in pion mass and nucleon mass for the explored region of parameter space are found to be negligible. The chiral behaviours of pion mass, pion decay constant and quark condensate are found to be qualitatively consistent with NLO chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that unimproved Wilson fermions, despite their explicit chiral symmetry breaking, produce cutoff effects in pion and nucleon masses that are negligible for the explored lattice spacings (0.05–0.07 fm), and that the chiral behavior of pion mass, decay constant, and quark condensate is qualitatively consistent with next-to-leading-order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory, though the pion decay constant data show possible residual finite-volume and renormalization uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed two-flavor lattice QCD simulations using the DD-HMC algorithm with unimproved Wilson fermion and Wilson gauge actions at two gauge couplings (β = 5.6 and 5.8), corresponding to lattice spacings of approximately 0.072 fm and 0.057 fm, on volumes of 24³ × 48 and 32³ × 64. They computed pion masses, pion decay constants, PCAC quark masses, and nucleon masses from two-point correlation functions, using point sources for pions and wall sources for nucleons, with HYP smearing for gauge observables. The lattice scale was set using the Sommer parameter r₀ extracted from static quark potentials, and chiral extrapolations were performed using NLO chiral perturbation theory formulas.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass range studied: 290 ≲ m_π ≲ 750 MeV (from abstract and text)
- Lattice spacings determined: a = 0.072 fm at β = 5.6 and a = 0.057 fm at β = 5.8
- Sommer parameter in chiral limit: a/r₀ = 0.156(4) at β = 5.6 and a/r₀ = 0.12161(5) at β = 5.8
- From NLO χPT fit to pion mass squared: B = 2651(56) MeV and Λ₃ = 1120(73) MeV
- From NLO χPT fit to nucleon mass: M₀ = 0.81(4) GeV, c₁ = −1.04(5) GeV⁻¹, e₁ʳ = 1.2(1) GeV⁻³
- Pion-nucleon sigma term at physical point: σ = 0.052(4) GeV
- Chiral condensate from NLO fit at β = 5.6: Σ^{1/3} = 228(8) MeV
- Lattice data for am_π at β = 5.6 range from 0.2764(6) (κ = 0.1575) to 0.1322(15) (κ = 0.1583); at β = 5.8 from 0.1612(9) (κ = 0.1543) to 0.0833(22) (κ = 0.1547)
- Lattice data for am_N at β = 5.6 range from 0.5865(48) (κ = 0.1575) to 0.4024(268) (κ = 0.1583); at β = 5.8 from 0.3649(39) (κ = 0.1543) to 0.2854(185) (κ = 0.1547)

## Context

This work extends the authors' ongoing program (Chowdhury et al., 2011, 2012) studying chiral properties of unimproved Wilson lattice QCD, where they previously demonstrated suppression of topological susceptibility with decreasing quark mass. The results address the long-standing question of whether unimproved Wilson fermions belong to the same universality class as continuum QCD, which had been questioned due to large scaling violations in earlier quenched simulations at larger pion masses (Del Debbio et al., 2006). The findings enable future simulations using unimproved Wilson fermions in the chiral regime, though the authors note that more precise determinations of Z_A and simulations at smaller quark masses are needed to reach definitive conclusions about the pion decay constant and condensate.
