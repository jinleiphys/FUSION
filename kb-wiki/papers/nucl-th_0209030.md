# Light from Cascading Partons in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We calculate the production of high energy photons from Compton and annihilation processes as well as fragmentation off quarks in the parton cascade model. The multiple scattering of partons is seen to lead to a substantial production of high energy photons, which rises further when parton multiplication due to final state radiation is included. The photon yield is found to be proportional to the number of collisions among the cascading partons.

## Key claim

The photon yield from parton cascades in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is directly proportional to the number of hard parton-parton collisions, scaling as \(N_{\text{part}}^{4/3}\), but this result depends on neglecting medium modifications of matrix elements and fragmentation functions, and the LPM suppression of radiation (if treated with an upper-limit ansatz) reduces the photon emission at 2 GeV by a factor of 3.5, largely eliminating the enhancement from fragmentation.

## Method

The authors used the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) based on a relativistic Boltzmann equation, implementing all lowest-order QCD \(2 \to 2\) scattering processes and photon-producing processes (Compton \(qg \to q\gamma\), annihilation \(q\bar{q} \to g\gamma\), and bremsstrahlung \(q^\star \to q\gamma\)) for central collisions (\(b=0\)) of gold nuclei at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV per nucleon pair. They included final-state parton shower fragmentation in the leading logarithmic approximation with angular ordering for soft-gluon interference, and explored an upper-limit treatment of LPM suppression by forbidding fragmentation if the scattered parton re-scatters before its formation time.

## Key numbers

- Photon emission from QCD-induced fragmentations alone increases the yield by about a factor of 4 compared to binary collisions only, for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.
- The average squared transverse momentum of incoming rescattered partons at the photon vertex is \(\langle p_t^2 \rangle \approx 2.1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- The ratio \(\gamma/\text{partons}\) is about 0.04% when only multiple scatterings among partons are considered, rising to 0.15% when fragmentations are included.
- LPM suppression (upper-limit treatment) reduces photon emission at about 2 GeV by a factor of 3.5.
- Increasing the pQCD cut-off \((p_T^{\text{min}})^2\) to 1 GeV\(^2\) reduces the photon yield by a factor of about 1.5 beyond \(p_T = 1\) GeV near central rapidities.
- The integrated photon yield scales as \(N_{\text{part}}^{4/3}\) for both calculations with and without fragmentation processes.

## Context

This work extends the Parton Cascade Model approach of Geiger and Muller (1992) and corrects numerical issues in the earlier VNI implementation by Srivastava and Gale (1998). It provides a direct connection between the measurable photon yield and the unobservable number of hard parton-parton collisions during pre-equilibrium dynamics, enabling future studies of whether heavy quark production and high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression reflect similar scaling properties. The authors note that medium modifications of matrix elements and fragmentation functions are neglected, and that incorporating two-loop photon production rates (Aurenche et al., 1998; Arnold et al., 2001) into the PCM remains an open question.
