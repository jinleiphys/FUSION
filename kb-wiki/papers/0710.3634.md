# Neutrino Emission From Inhomogeneous Pion Condensed Quark Matter

> It is believed that quark matter can exist in neutron star interior if the baryon density is high enough. When there is a large isospin density, quark matter could be in a pion condensed phase. We compute neutrino emission from direct Urca processes in such a phase, particularly in the inhomogeneous Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) states. The neutrino emissivity and specific heat are obtained, from which the cooling rate is estimated.

## Key claim

Neutrino emissivity from direct Urca processes in the inhomogeneous Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) pion condensed phase of quark matter is slightly smaller than in normal quark matter, leading to a slightly slower cooling rate for neutron star cores; this result depends on the assumption that the gap parameter and LOFF momentum are small compared to quark chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors used a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to describe quark matter with a pion condensate carrying nonzero total momentum (LOFF state). They computed the neutrino emissivity from direct Urca processes (d → u + e⁻ + ν̄, u + e⁻ → d + ν) by evaluating the imaginary part of the W-boson polarization tensor and performing phase-space integrals at low temperature and high chemical potential, keeping only gapless positive-energy excitation modes. The specific heat was derived from the entropy density of the gapless quasiparticles, and the cooling rate was obtained by solving the time evolution equation dT/dt = -ε/c_V.

## Key numbers

- The four-fermion coupling constant used is G = G_F cos θ_C ≈ 1.13488 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻².
- The neutrino emissivity in normal quark matter is ε₀ ≈ (457/2520) π N_c G² μ_e μ_u μ_d [1 + μ_d/μ_u] κ T⁶, where κ = 2α_s/(3π) in perturbative QCD.
- Numerical evaluations used κ = 2/(3π), μ = 300 MeV, and -δμ = 125 MeV as typical parameters for the LOFF pion superfluid.
- The cooling rate equation is t - t₀ = -(210)/(457 π³ G² μ_e) ∫_{T₀}^{T} dT' / T'⁵ × G(Δ,l)/F(Δ,l), with initial conditions T₀ = 0.1 MeV at t₀ = 1 yr.
- The specific heat of normal two-flavor quark matter is c_V0 = γ T with γ = 2 N_c (μ_u² + μ_d²) T/6.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of neutrino emission in gapless pion condensed quark matter in the BEC region (Huang, 2007) to the inhomogeneous LOFF phase, which is stable when the BCS-type gapless phase is unstable. The results are similar to the case of inhomogeneous CFL quark matter studied by Anglani (2006). The calculations enable estimates of neutron star cooling rates in the LOFF pion superfluid phase, which can be compared to observations of neutron star surface temperatures.
