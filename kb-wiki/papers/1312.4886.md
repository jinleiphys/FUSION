# Nonlocal dispersive optical model ingredients for ${}^{40}$Ca

> A comprehensive description of all single-particle properties associated with the nucleus ${}^{40}$Ca has been generated by employing a nonlocal dispersive optical potential capable of simultaneously reproducing all relevant data above and below the Fermi energy. We gather all relevant functional forms and the numerical values of the parameters in this contribution.

# Nonlocal dispersive optical model ingredients for ${}^{40}$Ca

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a nonlocal dispersive optical model (DOM) potential can simultaneously reproduce all single-particle data above and below the Fermi energy for ${}^{40}$Ca, but only when nonlocal absorptive potentials are included; local absorption alone cannot generate the correct particle number of 20 or describe the charge density accurately while also describing positive-energy scattering data.

## Method
The authors parametrize the nucleon self-energy using a nonlocal Hartree-Fock term (with a Gaussian nonlocality form first suggested by Perey (1962)) and nonlocal volume and surface imaginary potentials, with separate parameters above and below the Fermi energy to account for asymmetry. They solve the Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in an angular-momentum basis, using dispersion relations to link the real and imaginary parts of the self-energy across all energies. The fit includes elastic-scattering cross sections up to 200 MeV for both protons and neutrons, charge density data from de Vries (1987), and high-missing-energy $(e,e'p)$ spectral function data from Jefferson Lab (Rohe, 2004), with particle number constrained to within 1% for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers
- Proton particle number: 19.88 from partial waves $\ell = 0$ to 5 (including $j = \ell \pm 1/2$); neutron particle number: 19.79; target is 20 for both, within the assigned 1% error.
- Hartree-Fock volume depth $V^0_{HF} = 100.06$ MeV with radius $r^{HF} = 1.10$ fm and diffuseness $a^{HF} = 0.68$ fm.
- Two nonlocality parameters for the HF volume term: $\beta_{vol_1} = 0.66$ fm and $\beta_{vol_2} = 1.56$ fm, with weight $x_1 = 0.48$.
- Wine-bottle shape potential depth $V^0_{wb} = 15.0$ MeV with range $\rho_{wb} = 2.06$ fm and nonlocality $\beta_{wb} = 1.10$ fm.
- Volume absorption nonlocality above Fermi energy: $\beta_{vol}^+ = 0.64$ fm; below Fermi energy: $\beta_{vol}^- = 0.81$ fm.
- Volume absorption energy-asymmetry parameter $E_a^- = 120$ MeV (extended domain below Fermi energy to accommodate Jefferson Lab data).
- Spin-orbit depth $V^{so}_0 = 6.03$ MeV with radius $r^{so} = 1.02$ fm and diffuseness $a^{so} = 0.66$ fm.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive optical model (DOM) approach of Mahaux and Sartor (1991) by introducing nonlocal potentials for both the real Hartree-Fock and imaginary absorptive parts, building on theoretical developments by Waldecker (2011) and Dussan (2011). It supersedes earlier local-potential DOM analyses of ${}^{40}$Ca by Charity (2006, 2007) and Mueller (2011), which could not simultaneously reproduce ground-state properties and scattering data. The nonlocal DOM enables future reanalysis of $(e,e'p)$ data from NIKHEF (Kramer, 1989) and transfer reactions (Nguyen, 2011), and the authors plan to extend the analysis to ${}^{48}$Ca and ${}^{208}$Pb.
