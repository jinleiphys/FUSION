# Evolution of giant monopole resonance with triaxial deformation

> Background: The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) splits into two peaks in prolately deformed nuclei. When a nucleus is triaxially deformed, a peak appears in the middle between the two peaks. Purpose: We investigate the mechanism of the appearance of the middle peak in the ISGMR in triaxial nuclei. Method: We perform the constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculation for arbitrary triaxial shapes in $^{100}$Mo. We calculate the strength functions of the isoscalar monopole (ISM) and IS quadrupole modes on the CHFB states. Furthermore, we investigate vibrations of matter distributions in $x$, $y$, and $z$ directions induced by the external ISM field, with the $z$ axis being the longest axis of the triaxial shape. Results: The middle peak in the ISM strength evolves from the triaxial degree $Îł=0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$. This is because the difference between the vibration in $x$ direction and that in $y$ direction is evident with an increase in $Îł$ and the quadrupole $K=2$ component of the induced density of the ISM at the middle peak increases as $Îł$ increases, where $K$ denotes the $z$ component of the angular momentum. This property is also obtained in the unperturbed ISM strength without the residual fields. Conclusions: The mixing between the monopole and quadrupole modes is primarily determined by the ground-state deformation. Therefore, the ISM strength of the middle peak becomes strong as the triaxial degree in the ground state increases.

## Key claim

The middle peak in the isoscalar monopole (ISM) strength in triaxially deformed nuclei arises primarily from the ground-state deformation itself, not from dynamical residual interactions, as demonstrated by the fact that the middle peak also appears in the unperturbed ISM strength without residual fields.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations for $^{100}$Mo at a fixed quadrupole deformation $\beta=0.28$ while varying the triaxiality parameter $\gamma$ from $0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$. They then carried out quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using the finite amplitude method (FAM) to obtain strength functions for the isoscalar monopole and isoscalar quadrupole modes. They analyzed the induced matter density distributions in the $x$, $y$, and $z$ directions and performed multipole expansion of the induced density to quantify the mixing of quadrupole components.

## Key numbers

- The ISM strength shows two prominent peaks at $\omega \approx 13$ MeV and $\omega \approx 18$ MeV for $\gamma=0^\circ$ in $^{100}$Mo.
- A middle peak emerges around $\omega=15$ MeV as $\gamma$ increases, positioned between the lower and higher peaks.
- The fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) value for the middle peak increases monotonically as $\gamma$ increases from $0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$.
- The CHFB calculation used a (13.2 fm)$^3$ box with 0.8 fm mesh spacing, 1400 neutron and 1120 proton HF-basis states, and pairing strengths of 240 MeV fm$^3$ (neutrons) and 285 MeV fm$^3$ (protons).
- The FAM-QRPA calculations were performed up to $\omega=40$ MeV with 0.25 MeV spacing and a smearing width of $\Gamma/2=0.5$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the recent time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) study by Shi and Stevenson (2023), who found that triaxial deformation ($\gamma=20^\circ$â€“$30^\circ$) in $^{100}$Mo produced an additional ISM peak between the two peaks seen in prolately deformed nuclei. The present study clarifies that the mixing between monopole and quadrupole modes is primarily determined by ground-state deformation rather than dynamical effects, consistent with earlier results from a triaxial harmonic oscillator potential model by Shimizu (1984). This enables systematic predictions of ISGMR splitting patterns as a tool for identifying static triaxial deformation in nuclei.
