# 3D structure of hadrons and energy-momentum tensor

> The three-dimensional spatial structure of hadrons is encoded in their form factors. Via appropriate Fourier transform, the latter describe how charge, energy, linear and angular momentum, but also pressure are distributed inside these systems. Electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon have been measured for a long time, but it is only recently that some gravitational form factors have been extracted from experimental data, creating a great deal of enthusiasm in the hadronic community. Here we summarize some of the recent developments on the interpretation of these form factors, and provide a quick overview of what we can learn about the nucleon mass, spin and internal pressure.

# 3D structure of hadrons and energy-momentum tensor

## Key claim

The paper argues that relativistic spatial distributions of hadrons become frame-dependent when recoil effects cannot be neglected, and that a phase-space approach with a quasi-probabilistic interpretation naturally interpolates between the Breit-frame (static target) and light-front (infinite-momentum) pictures, showing that distortions in light-front distributions are purely kinematic artifacts associated with spin rather than intrinsic properties of the hadron.

## Method

The author applies a phase-space formalism based on Wigner distributions to define elastic-frame (EF) distributions for hadron matrix elements, using Lorentz boosts and Wigner spin rotations to relate Breit-frame (BF) and light-front (LF) spatial distributions. The formalism is applied to both the electromagnetic current (parametrized by Dirac and Pauli form factors) and the energy-momentum tensor (parametrized by gravitational form factors A, D, J, S, and C-bar) for spin-1/2 targets, with explicit expressions given for the spin structure in different frames.

## Key numbers

- The total electric charge Q = integral d^2 b_perp J^0_EF(b_perp; P_z) is independent of the average longitudinal momentum P_z, confirming correct normalization of momentum-space amplitudes.
- The forward limits of gravitational form factors satisfy: A_q(0) + A_g(0) = 1, J_q(0) + J_g(0) = 1/2, and C-bar_q(Q^2) + C-bar_g(Q^2) = 0.
- The von Laue condition requires integral d^3 r p(r) = 0 for mechanical equilibrium, and stability arguments suggest D_q(0) + D_g(0) = M integral d^3 r r^2 p(r) should be negative.
- The Wigner rotation angle theta is given by cos(theta) = (P^0 + M(1+tau))/((P^0+M) sqrt(1+tau)) and sin(theta) = -sqrt(tau) P_z/((P^0+M) sqrt(1+tau)), where tau = Q^2/(4M^2).

## Context

This work extends the phase-space approach to relativistic spatial distributions developed by Lorce (2018, 2020) and contrasts with earlier attempts by Panteleeva (2021), Kim (2021), Freese (2021, 2022, 2023), and Epelbaum (2022) that maintained a strict probabilistic interpretation. The formalism clarifies why light-front distributions show features at odds with the expected picture of hadrons at rest, as noted by Miller (2007) and Carlson (2007). The first extraction of pressure distributions inside the proton from deeply virtual Compton scattering data was reported by Burkert (2018), with more conservative analyses by Kumericki (2019) and Dutrieux (2021); gluon gravitational form factors have also been extracted from J/psi photoproduction data by Duran (2022). The future Electron-Ion Collider physics program aims to determine the energy-momentum tensor matrix elements discussed here.
