# Effects of nuclear symmetry energy on eta meson production and its rare decay to the dark U-boson in heavy-ion reactions

> Using a relativistic transport model ART1.0, we explore effects of nuclear symmetry energy on eta meson production and its rare decay to the dark U-boson in heavy-ion reactions from 0.2 to 10 GeV/nucleon available at several current and future facilities. The yield of eta mesons at sub-threshold energies is found to be very sensitive to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy. Above a beam energy of about 5 GeV/nucleon in Au+Au reactions, the sensitivity to symmetry energy disappears. Using the branching ratio of the rare eta decay (eta->gamma U) available in the literature, we estimate the maximum cross section for the U-boson production in the energy range considered, providing a useful reference for future U-boson search using heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

The yield of eta mesons in heavy-ion reactions at sub-threshold energies is very sensitive to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, but this sensitivity disappears above a beam energy of about 5 GeV/nucleon in Au+Au reactions; the effect is strongest in the deeper sub-threshold region, where the eta multiplicity can vary by a factor of 5-10 depending on the symmetry energy stiffness.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model ART1.0, which includes baryons (N, Delta(1232), N*(1440), N*(1535), Lambda, Sigma) and mesons (pi, rho, omega, eta, K) with explicit isospin degrees of freedom. Eta meson production and absorption were modeled via the eta N to N*(1535) resonance channel, with the isovector (symmetry) potential implemented using a Skyrme-type parametrization where the parameter x controls the stiffness of the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy. The authors simulated inclusive Au+Au reactions at beam energies from 0.2 to 10 GeV/nucleon and compared results for soft (x=0) and stiff (x=-2) symmetry energies.

## Key numbers

- Eta meson mass: 547.853 MeV/c^2
- Threshold beam energy for eta production in free nucleon-nucleon collisions: E_th^eta = 1.2 GeV (compared to E_th^K = 1.6 GeV for kaons)
- Experimental eta/pi^0 ratio in most central Au+Au reactions at 1 GeV/nucleon: approximately 1.4 x 10^-2
- At a transverse momentum of about 200-300 MeV/c in Au+Au reactions at 0.2 GeV/nucleon, the effect of nuclear symmetry energy on eta yield reaches a factor of 5-10
- The maximum U-boson production cross section from eta rare decay in Au+Au reactions is in the range of 1-70 microbarns across beam energies from 0.2 to 10 GeV/nucleon
- The maximal U-boson-SM particle coupling constant used: epsilon^2 = 4.0 x 10^-6
- The predicted U-boson width is about 10 eV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy probes using pion ratios (Li et al., 2002; Xiao et al., 2009) and kaon ratios (Ferreira et al., 2005) by proposing eta mesons as a complementary probe that is sensitive to the number of p-n collisions rather than the ratio of n-n to p-p colliding pairs. The authors note that the FOPI pi^-/pi^+ data in Au+Au reactions at beam energies from about 0.4 to 1 GeV/nucleon favor the symmetry energy with x=1, while their eta calculations give a reasonable description of TAPS experimental data with x=0 and 1. The work also estimates U-boson production cross sections from eta rare decay, providing a reference for future searches at facilities such as CSR, HIAF, FAIR, NICA, and the RHIC BES program, building on the branching ratio estimates of Reece and Wang (2009) for meson decays to the dark U-boson.
