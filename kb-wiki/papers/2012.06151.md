# Dijet Acoplanarity in CUJET3 as a Probe of the Nonperturbative Color Structure of QCD Perfect Fluids

> Using the CUJET3=DGLV+VISHNU jet-medium interaction framework, we show that dijet azimuthal acoplanarity in high energy $A+A$ collisions is sensitive to possible non-perturbative enhancement of the jet transport coefficient, $\hat{q}(T,E)$, in the QCD crossover temperature $T\sim 150-300$ MeV range. With jet-medium couplings constrained by global RHIC\& LHC $χ^2$ fits to nuclear modification data on $R_{AA}$, we compare predictions of the medium induced dijet transverse momentum squared, $Q_s^2\sim \langle \hat{q} L \rangle \sim Δϕ^2 E^2$, in two models of the temperature, $T$, and jet energy $E$ dependence of the jet medium transport coefficient, $\hat{q}(T,E)$. In one model, wQGP, only perturbative quark and gluon dof are assumed. In the second model, sQGMP, nonperturbative three component semi-Quark-Gluon and Magnetic Monopole dof are asssumed. We show that the dijet path averaged medium induced azimuthal acoplanarity, $Δϕ^2$, in sQGMP is robustly a factor of $\sim 2$ larger than in perturbative wQGP while the radiative energy loss in both models is nearly identical as required to fit the same single jet nuclear modification $R_{AA}$ data. Future A+A dijet acoplanarity measurements correlated with $R_{AA}$ and azimuthal asymmetry $v_n$ measurements therefore appears to be a promising strategy to search for possible signatures of critical opalescence like phenomena in the QCD confinement temperature range.

## Key claim

The paper shows that dijet azimuthal acoplanarity in high-energy A+A collisions is sensitive to possible non-perturbative enhancement of the jet transport coefficient in the QCD crossover temperature range T ~ 150–300 MeV, and that the medium-induced acoplanarity in the sQGMP model (which includes magnetic monopole degrees of freedom) is robustly a factor of ~2 larger than in the perturbative wQGP model, even though both models produce nearly identical radiative energy loss as required to fit the same single-jet nuclear modification factor R_AA data.

## Method

The authors used the CUJET3 framework, which couples the DGLV jet quenching theory with VISHNU2+1 viscous hydrodynamic temperature and flow fields with Glauber initial conditions. They computed the medium-induced dijet transverse momentum squared Q_s^2 and the resulting azimuthal acoplanarity Δφ^2 for two models of the QCD fluid's color composition: wQGP (perturbative quark and gluon degrees of freedom) and sQGMP (semi-quark-gluon-magnetic monopole plasma with emergent color magnetic degrees of freedom). The jet-medium couplings in both models were constrained by global χ^2 fits to nuclear modification R_AA data at RHIC and LHC, with the two free parameters of CUJET3 fixed to α_c = 0.9 ± 0.1 and c_m ≈ 0.25 ± 0.03.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient in sQGMP is enhanced by a factor ~4 relative to wQGP near the freeze-out surface at T = 160 MeV and at late times in central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 ATeV.
- The medium-induced azimuthal acoplanarity Δφ^2 in sQGMP is robustly a factor of ~2 larger than in perturbative wQGP.
- The QCD crossover temperature range studied is T ~ 150–300 MeV.
- The maximum running coupling was constrained to α_c = 0.9 ± 0.1 and the ratio of magnetic to electric screening scales to c_m ≈ 0.25 ± 0.03 from global χ^2/dof < 2 fits.
- The study focuses on the jet energy range 20 < E_fin < 80 GeV as the "sweet spot" for resolving medium-induced acoplanarity from the dominant Sudakov vacuum contribution.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dijet acoplanarity as a probe of QGP properties (Appel, Baier 1996, D'Eramo 2018, Majumder 2008, Luo 2018, Gyulassy 2018) and addresses the degeneracy among current frameworks (Jaki, Shen 2014, Niemi 2015) that all fit existing R_AA and v_n data at the χ^2/dof < 2 level. The sQGMP model builds on proposals by Liao (2006, 2008) to solve the R_AA × v_2 puzzle by including emergent color magnetic monopole degrees of freedom. The results suggest that future correlated measurements of dijet acoplanarity together with R_AA and v_n could discriminate between different models of the nonperturbative color structure of QCD fluids and potentially reveal signatures of critical opalescence near the confinement temperature.
