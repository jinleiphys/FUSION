# Strongly Bound Dibaryon with Maximal Beauty Flavor from Lattice QCD

> We report the first lattice QCD study of the heavy dibaryons in which all six quarks have the bottom (beauty) flavor. Performing a state-of-the-art lattice QCD calculation we find clear evidence for a deeply bound $Ω_{bbb}$-$Ω_{bbb}$ dibaryon in the $^1S_0$ channel, as a pole singularity in the $S$-wave $Ω_{bbb}$-$Ω_{bbb}$ scattering amplitude with a binding energy $-81(_{-16}^{+14})$ MeV. With such a deep binding, Coulomb repulsion serves only as a perturbation on the ground state wave function of the parameterized strong potential and may shift the strong binding only by a few percent. Considering the scalar channel to be the most bound for single flavored dibaryons, we conclude this state is the heaviest possible most deeply bound dibaryon in the visible universe.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first lattice QCD evidence for a deeply bound $\Omega_{bbb}$-$\Omega_{bbb}$ dibaryon in the $^1S_0$ channel, with the caveat that the binding energy of $-81(_{-16}^{+14})$ MeV is extracted from a scattering amplitude analysis that includes systematic uncertainties from excited-state contamination, lattice cutoff effects, and fit-window choices, and that Coulomb repulsion may shift the strong binding only by a few percent.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using four ensembles with dynamical $u/d$, $s$, and $c$ quark fields generated by the MILC Collaboration, employing the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action. Bottom quarks were treated with a nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) Hamiltonian improved to $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s v^4)$. Two-point correlation functions for single $\Omega_{bbb}$ baryons and $\Omega_{bbb}$-$\Omega_{bbb}$ dibaryons were computed, and ground-state masses were extracted via single-exponential fits. The finite-volume energy levels were analyzed using Luscher's formalism to determine the $S$-wave scattering amplitude and identify the bound-state pole.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the $\mathcal{D}_{6b}$ dibaryon: $-81(^{+14}_{-16})(14)$ MeV relative to the $2M_{\Omega_{bbb}}$ threshold.
- Scattering length in the continuum limit: $a_0^{[0]} = 0.18(^{+0.02}_{-0.02})$ fm.
- Mass of the $\Omega_{bbb}$ baryon: $M_{\Omega_{bbb}}^{\mathrm{phys}} = 14366(7)(9)$ MeV.
- Mass of the $\mathcal{D}_{6b}$ dibaryon: $M^{\mathrm{phys}}_{\mathcal{D}_{6b}} = 28651(^{+16}_{-17})(15)$ MeV.
- Energy differences $\Delta E$ on individual ensembles ranged from $-61(11)$ MeV on the $24^3 \times 64$ lattice to $-71(7)$ MeV on the $48^3 \times 144$ lattice.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of dibaryons from the light and strange sectors, where interactions between two $\Omega$ baryons were found to be weak (Buchoff 2012, HALQCD 2015, Gongyo 2017), and from the charm sector, where a shallow $\Omega_{ccc}$-$\Omega_{ccc}$ bound state was reported (Lyu 2021). The deep binding observed here contrasts with those earlier results and aligns with recent findings that multihadron systems with multiple bottom quarks can have large binding energies (Junnarkar 2019, Junnarkar 2022). The results enable future experimental searches for heavy dibaryons and motivate further theoretical studies of the quark-mass dependence of baryon-baryon interactions.
