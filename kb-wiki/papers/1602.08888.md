# Effects of isovector scalar meson on hyperon star

> We study the effects of isovector-scalar ($δ$)-meson on neutron star. Influence of $δ$-meson on both static and rotating neutron star is discussed. Inclusion of $δ$-meson in a neutron star system consisting of proton, neutron and electron, make the equation of state stiffer in higher density and consequently increases the maximum mass of the star. But induction of $δ$-meson in the hyperon star decreases the maximum mass of the hyperon star. This is due to the early evolution of hyperons in presence of $δ-$meson.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the isovector-scalar (δ) meson in a hyperon star decreases the maximum mass of the star, contrary to its effect in a pure proton-neutron-electron star where it increases the maximum mass; this decrease is attributed to the earlier onset of hyperon production in the presence of the δ-meson.

## Method

The authors use an extended relativistic mean field (RMF) model based on the G2 parameter set of the effective field theory, to which the δ-meson interaction is added. The coupling constants g_ρ and g_δ are simultaneously adjusted to keep the symmetry energy fixed at E_s = 36.4 MeV. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved for static stars, and the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu (KEH) method is used for rotating stars, under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions including the full baryon octet.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy fixed at E_s = 36.4 MeV for all combinations of g_ρ and g_δ.
- For static proton-neutron stars without hyperons, the maximum mass obtained is approximately 2 M_⊙; for rotating stars without hyperons, the maximum mass is approximately 2.4 M_⊙.
- For static hyperon stars, the maximum mass obtained is approximately 1.4 M_⊙; for rotating hyperon stars, the maximum mass is approximately 1.6 M_⊙.
- The Σ⁻ hyperon appears at density ρ = 1.75 ρ₀ without the δ-meson, but at ρ = 1.67 ρ₀ when the δ-meson is included.
- The Ξ⁻ hyperon evolves at ρ_B = 6.5 ρ₀ without the δ-meson and at ρ_B ≈ 5.0 ρ₀ when the δ-meson is included.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF studies of neutron star structure by explicitly including the δ-meson, which is often neglected due to its small contribution at low densities. The authors compare their results with other parameter sets such as NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), NL-SH (Sharma 1993), FSU (Piekarewicz 2005), and FSU2, and note that the observed heavy masses of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97±0.04 M_⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (2.01±0.04 M_⊙) motivate a re-examination of the composition and interactions inside neutron stars. The finding that the δ-meson accelerates hyperon production and reduces the maximum mass of hyperon stars provides a constraint for future models of dense matter and for interpreting observations of compact star masses.
