# Relation between the $2νββ$ and $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements

> A formal relation between the GT part of the nuclear matrix elements $M^0ν}_{GT}$ of $0νββ$ decay and the closure matrix elements $M^{2ν}_{cl}$ of $2νββ$ decay is established. This relation is based on the integral representation of these quantities in terms of their dependence on the distance $r$ between the two nucleons undergoing transformation. We also discuss the difficulties in determining the correct values of the closure $2νββ$ decay matrix elements.

# Relation between the $2\nu\beta\beta$ and $0\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix elements

## Key claim
A formal relation exists between the Gamow-Teller part of the $0\nu\beta\beta$ nuclear matrix element $M^{0\nu}_{GT}$ and the closure matrix element $M^{2\nu}_{cl}$ of $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay, expressed as $C^{0\nu}_{GT}(r) = H(r,\bar{E}) \times C^{2\nu}_{cl}(r)$. However, the practical utility of this relation is limited because determining the correct values of $M^{2\nu}_{cl}$ is problematic: within QRPA these closure matrix elements are often negative, while experimental methods yield positive values, and the origin of negative contributions from high-lying $1^+$ states near the giant Gamow-Teller resonance remains unresolved.

## Method
The authors derived an integral representation of both $M^{0\nu}_{GT}$ and $M^{2\nu}_{cl}$ in terms of their dependence on the distance $r$ between the two transforming nucleons, defining functions $C^{0\nu}_{GT}(r)$ and $C^{2\nu}_{cl}(r)$. They then established that $C^{0\nu}_{GT}(r)$ equals the neutrino potential $H(r,\bar{E})$ multiplied by $C^{2\nu}_{cl}(r)$, valid for any shape of the neutrino potential. Using QRPA calculations, they examined the running sums of $M^{2\nu}_{cl}$ as a function of excitation energy in the intermediate odd-odd nucleus for several candidate nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The average distance between nucleons in a nucleus is approximately 1.2 fm, used to separate the $r$ range into a region $r \le 2-3$ fm governed by nucleon-nucleon correlations and a region $r \ge 2-3$ fm governed by nuclear many-body physics.
- Negative contributions to $M^{2\nu}_{cl}$ arise from excitation energies $E_{ex} \ge 10$ MeV, in the region of the giant Gamow-Teller resonance.
- The function $C^{2\nu}_{cl}(r)$ has a substantial negative tail past $r \sim 2-3$ fm, but these distances contribute very little to $C^{0\nu}_{GT}(r)$ due to the fast decrease of the neutrino potential $H(r,\bar{E})$ with increasing $r$.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Vogel and Simkovic, 2011) and builds on prior QRPA calculations (Vogel and collaborators, various years), nuclear shell model work (Menendez 2008, Caurier 2008), and the Interacting Boson Model (Barea 2009). The paper also references the Generator Coordinate Method (Gabriel) and Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov Method (PHFB) for $M^{0\nu}$ evaluation. The established formal relation between $0\nu\beta\beta$ and $2\nu\beta\beta$ matrix elements was intended to provide an independent observable to test calculated $0\nu\beta\beta$ matrix elements, but the authors conclude that unresolved discrepancies in $M^{2\nu}_{cl}$, particularly the sign and magnitude of contributions from high-lying $1^+$ states, prevent immediate practical application. The work challenges experimentalists studying charge-exchange reactions of the $(n,p)$ type and theorists using methods alternative to QRPA to determine whether noticeable $\beta^+$ strength exists near the giant Gamow-Teller resonance.
