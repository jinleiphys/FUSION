# Evolution of strangeness in equilibrating and expanding quark-gluon plasma

> We evaluate the strangeness production from equilibrating and transversely expanding quark gluon plasma which may be created in the wake of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We consider boost invariant longitudinal and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion of a gluon dominated partonic plasma, which is in local thermal equilibrium. Initial conditions obtained from the self screened parton cascade model are used. We empirically find that the final extent of the partonic equilibration rises almost linearly with the square of the initial energy density. This along with the corresponding variation with the number of participants may help us distinguish between various models of parton production.

# Evolution of strangeness in equilibrating and expanding quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Strangeness equilibration in a chemically evolving quark-gluon plasma depends sensitively on both the initial conditions (taken from the Self Screened Parton Cascade model) and the inclusion of transverse expansion; the final extent of partonic equilibration rises almost linearly with the square of the initial energy density, but the plasma is not fully equilibrated chemically at either RHIC or LHC energies by the time the temperature drops below 200 MeV.

## Method

The authors model a gluon-dominated partonic plasma undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion, assuming local thermal equilibrium. They solve the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for energy-momentum conservation coupled to master equations for chemical equilibration of gluons, light quarks, and strange quarks via the dominant reactions gg ↔ ggg and gg ↔ i i-bar. The initial conditions are taken from the Self Screened Parton Cascade (SSPC) model, and the temperature-dependent strong coupling constant is evaluated with a scale Q = 2πT and cutoff Λ₀ = 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Strange quark mass taken as 150 MeV.
- The temperature threshold for abundant strangeness production is T ≥ 200 MeV.
- The scale for the strong coupling is Q = 2πT, with cutoff Λ₀ = 300 MeV.
- For the case assuming λ_g = λ_u = λ_d = 1 and λ_s(τ₀) = 0.2, the strange quark fugacity λ_s rises to about 0.4 at RHIC energies and up to 0.72 at LHC energies for longitudinal expansion; with transverse expansion these numbers reduce to 0.38 at RHIC and 0.66 at LHC energies.
- The final fugacities for all partons increase almost linearly with the square of the energy density once the energy density exceeds about 20–40 GeV.
- The initial temperature in partonic models varies as A^{1/6}, implying strangeness equilibration scales as N_part^{4/3} if A is identified with N_part/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Levai et al. (2000) that used HIJING initial conditions and considered only longitudinal expansion, by incorporating transverse expansion and SSPC initial conditions. It contrasts with the approach of Rafelski and coworkers (e.g., Rafelski and Letessier, 1999), who assumed light quarks and gluons are already chemically equilibrated when strangeness evolution begins and used a Boltzmann approximation for s-quarks. The results enable distinguishing between minijet models and parton saturation models (Eskola et al., 1999) by comparing the scaling of strangeness equilibration with the number of participants, and are consistent with the centrality dependence of strangeness equilibration observed at CERN SPS energies in Pb+Pb collisions.
