# High-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics compared with tensor-optimized shell model for strong tensor correlation

> We treat the tensor correlation in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) including large-relative-momentum components among nucleon pairs for finite nuclei. The tensor correlation is described by using large imaginary centroid vectors of Gaussian wave packets for nucleon pairs with opposite directions, which makes a large relative momentum. We superpose the AMD basis states, in which one nucleon pair has various relative momenta for all directions; this new method is called "high-momentum AMD" (HM-AMD). We show the results for $^4$He using the effective interaction having a strong tensor force. It is found that HM-AMD provides a large tensor matrix element comparable to the case of the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM), in which the two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) excitations are fully included to describe the tensor correlation. The results of two methods agree with each other at the level of the Hamiltonian components of $^4$He. This indicates that in HM-AMD the high-momentum components described by the imaginary centroid vectors of the nucleon pair provide the equivalent effect of the 2p-2h excitations for the tensor correlation.

## Key claim

High-momentum AMD (HM-AMD), which introduces one nucleon pair with large imaginary centroid vectors in opposite directions, provides a tensor matrix element and Hamiltonian components for $^4$He that agree closely with those of the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM), indicating that the high-momentum pair in AMD is equivalent to the 2p-2h excitations used in TOSM to describe tensor correlation.

## Method

The authors constructed AMD basis states in which one nucleon pair (the "high-momentum pair") is given imaginary centroid positions $\vec Z_1 = i\vec D$ and $\vec Z_2 = -i\vec D$, with $\vec D$ real and varied from 1 fm to 11 fm in steps of 1 fm. They considered four spin-isospin combinations for the pair and two momentum directions (parallel and perpendicular to the intrinsic spin). These basis states were superposed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) to form the HM-AMD wave function for $^4$He, and the results were compared with TOSM calculations that fully include 2p-2h excitations using Gaussian expansion for particle states up to convergence in partial waves.

## Key numbers

* Total energy of $^4$He: $-64.66$ MeV in HM-AMD versus $-65.23$ MeV in TOSM.
* Tensor matrix element: $-66.28$ MeV in HM-AMD versus $-67.66$ MeV in TOSM.
* Kinetic energy: $83.58$ MeV in HM-AMD versus $84.78$ MeV in TOSM.
* Central matrix element: $-83.63$ MeV in HM-AMD versus $-84.13$ MeV in TOSM.
* Radius of $^4$He: $1.329$ fm in HM-AMD versus $1.320$ fm in TOSM.
* The energy minimum for a single p$_\uparrow$--n$_\uparrow$ pair with shift $D_z$ occurs at $D_z = 5$ fm, corresponding to a single-nucleon momentum of $2.5$ fm$^{-1}$, which is 1.8 times the empirical Fermi momentum $k_{\rm F}=1.4$ fm$^{-1}$.
* Convergence in TOSM required partial waves up to $L_{\rm max}=7$, corresponding to a momentum scale of $L_{\rm max}/b \sim 5$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kimura (2014) and Itagaki (2017), who introduced imaginary centroid values in AMD but obtained tensor matrix elements smaller than ab initio values. The present HM-AMD method achieves a tensor matrix element of $-66$ MeV for $^4$He, comparable to the ab initio value of Kamada (2001) and to the TOSM results of Myo (2005, 2007). The agreement between HM-AMD and TOSM establishes that one high-momentum pair in AMD captures the essential 2p-2h effect of the tensor force. This enables future combination of HM-AMD with the tensor-optimized AMD (TOAMD) framework to treat both short-range and tensor correlations simultaneously, and to apply the method to heavier nuclei and bare $NN$ interactions.
