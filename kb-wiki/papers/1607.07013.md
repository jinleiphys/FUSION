# Cold nuclear matter effects and a modified form of the proximity approach

> The influence of the cold nuclear matter effects on the Coulomb barriers and also on the fusion cross sections of 47 fusion reactions are systematically investigated within the framework of the proximity formalism. For this purpose, I modify the original version of this formalism (Prox. 77) using a new analytical form of the universal function which is formulated based on the double-folding model with three density-dependent versions of the M3Y-type interactions, namely DDM3Y1, CDM3Y3 and BDM3Y1. It is found that when the Prox. 77 potential is accompanied by each of the formulated universal functions, the agreement between the theoretical and experimental data of the barrier height and also the fusion cross section increase for our selected fusion systems. The present study also provides appropriate conditions to explore theoretically the variation effects of the NM incompressibility constant $K$ on the calculated results caused by the Prox. 77 model. It is shown that the accuracy of this potential model for reproducing the experimental fusion data enhances by increasing the strength of this constant. A discussion is also presented about the role of various physical effects in the theoretical results of the proximity approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that modifying the original Prox. 77 proximity formalism by incorporating a new universal function derived from the double-folding model with density-dependent M3Y-type interactions (DDM3Y1, CDM3Y3, BDM3Y1) improves agreement with experimental fusion barrier heights and cross sections for 47 selected reactions, with the improvement increasing as the nuclear matter incompressibility constant K increases from 176 MeV to 270 MeV.

## Method

The author calculated nuclear potentials using the double-folding model with three density-dependent versions of the M3Y-Paris effective interaction (DDM3Y1, CDM3Y3, BDM3Y1) and a two-parameter Fermi-Dirac density distribution for 47 symmetric and asymmetric fusion reactions with charge products between 48 and 2460. From these potentials, a new analytical fourth-order polynomial form of the universal function Phi(s) was parameterized for each interaction version, then inserted into the Prox. 77 proximity formalism to compute modified nuclear potentials. Fusion cross sections were calculated using the one-dimensional barrier penetration model with the WKB approximation for transmission coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The charge product range of the selected fusion reactions spans from Z1Z2 = 48 (for 12C+17O) to Z1Z2 = 2460 (for 70Zn+208Pb).
* The nuclear matter incompressibility constants for the three interaction versions are K = 176 MeV (DDM3Y1), K = 236 MeV (CDM3Y3), and K = 270 MeV (BDM3Y1).
* The surface energy coefficient gamma uses gamma_0 = 0.9517 MeV/fm^2 and k_s = 1.7826.
* The effective sharp radius formula is R_i = 1.28 A_i^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8 A_i^(-1/3) fm.
* The universal function for the original Prox. 77 uses a piecewise form: for xi <= 1.2511, Phi(xi) = -1/2 (xi-2.54)^2 - 0.0852 (xi-2.54)^3; for xi >= 1.2511, Phi(xi) = -3.437 exp(-xi/0.75).

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to improve the proximity formalism by incorporating physical effects such as thermal effects of the compound nucleus (RajKu, Rajetal, OR1, OR2, Salehi13), deformation effects (Def1, Def2), and surface tension effects (gamma1). The study is motivated by the observation that the original Prox. 77 potential overestimates barrier heights and underestimates fusion cross sections, as shown in systematic studies by OR4, Dutt, DP1, and DP2. The results are compared with other improved proximity models (IPM1 from GHS1, IPM2 from DP3, IPM3 from Dens), and the author concludes that the cold nuclear matter effects, particularly with the hard incompressibility (BDM3Y1), produce the most accurate predictions for both barrier heights and fusion cross sections among the considered modifications.
