# Baryon structure in a quark-confining non-local NJL model

> We study the nucleon and diquarks in a non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. For certain parameters the model exhibits quark confinement, in the form of a propagator without real poles. After truncation of the two-body channels to the scalar and axial-vector diquarks, a relativistic Faddeev equation for nucleon bound states is solved in the covariant diquark-quark picture. The dependence of the nucleon mass on diquark masses is studied in detail. We find parameters that lead to a simultaneous reasonable description of pions and nucleons. Both the diquarks contribute attractively to the nucleon mass. Axial-vector diquark correlations are seen to be important, especially in the confining phase of the model. We study the possible implications of quark confinement for the description of the diquarks and the nucleon. In particular, we find that it leads to a more compact nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors find that a non-local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a Gaussian form factor can simultaneously describe pions and nucleons, and that the presence of quark confinement (defined as a propagator with no real poles) leads to a more compact nucleon, but only for specific parameter sets that satisfy a particular inequality.

## Method

The authors solve a relativistic Faddeev equation for the nucleon in a covariant diquark-quark picture, truncating the two-body channels to scalar and axial-vector diquarks. The non-local interaction is taken as separable with a Gaussian form factor in Euclidean space, and the dressed quark propagator is obtained from a Schwinger-Dyson equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nucleon vertex function is then solved numerically, using an improved on-shell approximation for the diquark T-matrices that includes off-shell corrections.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass fixed to 139.6 MeV and pion decay constant to 92.4 MeV for both parameter sets.
* For the non-confining set A: dynamical quark mass at zero momentum m(0) = 297.9 MeV, current quark mass m_c = 7.9 MeV, cutoff Λ = 1046.8 MeV, coupling g_π = 31.6 GeV^{-2}.
* For the confining set B: m(0) = 351.6 MeV, m_c = 11.13 MeV, Λ = 847.8 MeV, g_π = 55.80 GeV^{-2}.
* Quark condensate in the chiral limit is -(207 MeV)^3 for set A and -(186 MeV)^3 for set B.
* First poles of the quark propagator: ±391 MeV (real) for set A; ±408 ± 238i MeV (complex) for set B.
* Scalar diquark mass equals the pion mass when the coupling ratio r_s = g_s/g_π = 1.
* The next pseudo-threshold from complex poles lies at 1.3 GeV for set A and 3 GeV for set B.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-local NJL models applied to mesons by Praschifka et al. (1987) and Plant and Birse (1998) to the baryon sector using the relativistic Faddeev approach developed by the Tübingen group (Buck et al., 1992; Oettel et al., 1998). It contrasts with the local NJL model, where bound axial-vector diquarks exist only for very strong couplings, and where the absence of confinement leads to unphysical quark-production thresholds. The results enable future studies of baryon structure and form factors in a framework that incorporates a form of quark confinement.
