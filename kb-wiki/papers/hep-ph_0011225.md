# Strangeness Content in the Nucleon

> I review recent studies of strangeness content in the nucleon pertaining to the flavor-singlet $g_A^0$, the $\bar{s}s$ matrix element and the strangeness electric and magnetic form factors $G_E^s(q^2)$ and $G_M^s(q^2)$, based on lattice QCD calculations. I shall also discuss the relevance of incorporating the strangeness content in nuclei in regard to strange baryon-antibaryon productions from proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations show that the small flavor-singlet axial coupling \(g_A^0\) and the large \(\pi N\) sigma term can be understood only when disconnected sea quark contributions are included, contradicting the valence quark model and the OZI rule which had assumed such contributions are negligible.

## Method

The author reviews quenched lattice QCD calculations of three observables: the flavor-singlet axial coupling \(g_A^0\), the scalar matrix element \(\langle N|\bar{s}s|N\rangle\), and the strangeness electric and magnetic form factors \(G_E^s(q^2)\) and \(G_M^s(q^2)\). For each, the three-point function is separated into a connected insertion (CI, containing valence and connected sea) and a disconnected insertion (DI, containing disconnected sea). The calculations use Wilson fermions in the quenched approximation, with results extrapolated to the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- Flavor-singlet axial coupling \(g_A^0 = 0.25 \pm 0.12\) from lattice (CI + DI), compared to experimental values \(0.28(16)\) (SMC 1997) and \(0.27(10)\) (E143 1995); the CI alone gives \(0.62 \pm 0.09\).
- Strange quark polarization \(\Delta s = -0.12 \pm 0.01\) from lattice, compared to experimental \(-0.10(5)\) (SMC 1997) and \(-0.10(4)\) (E143 1995).
- Ratio \(y = 2\langle N|\bar{s}s|N\rangle / \langle N|\bar{u}u + \bar{d}d|N\rangle = 0.36 \pm 0.03\) from lattice.
- \(\pi N\) sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 49.7 \pm 2.6\) MeV from lattice (CI + DI), compared to phenomenological 45 MeV (Gasser, Leutwyler, Sainio 1991); the CI alone gives \(17.8 \pm 0.5\) MeV.
- Neutron to proton magnetic moment ratio \(\mu_n/\mu_p\) from lattice with CI + DI approaches the experimental value \(-0.685\), while the CI alone deviates toward the valence prediction of \(-2/3\).

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of nucleon structure (Dong, Liu, et al. 1995; Fukugita et al. 1995; Gusken et al. 1999) and directly addresses the "proton spin crisis" discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (1988) and confirmed by E143 (1995) and SMC (1997). It also resolves the \(\pi N\) sigma term puzzle (Cheng 1976; Gasser, Leutwyler, Sainio 1991) by showing that the disconnected sea contributes about 1.8 times the connected insertion to the scalar matrix element. The results enable future dynamical fermion simulations and calculations with chiral fermions (Neuberger 1998) to reduce systematic errors, and they motivate inclusion of strangeness in models of strange baryon-antibaryon production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies.
