# Effective T-odd P-even hadronic interactions from quark models

> Tests of time reversal symmetry at low and medium energies may be analyzed in the framework of effective hadronic interactions. Here, we consider the quark structure of hadrons to make a connection to the more fundamental degrees of freedom. It turns out that for P-even T-odd interactions hadronic matrix elements evaluated in terms of quark models give rise to factors of 2 to 5. Also, it is possible to relate the strength of the anomalous part of the effective rho-type T-odd P-even tensor coupling to quark structure effects.

## Key claim

Hadronic matrix elements for P-even T-odd interactions, evaluated using constituent quark models, give rise to enhancement factors of 2 to 5 when relating quark-level operators to effective hadronic couplings, but these factors depend on the specific quark model used and the assumption that the hadronization mechanism is the same for both T-odd and T-even strong interactions.

## Method

The author evaluates matrix elements of T-odd P-even quark operators between nucleon states using the Fock space representation of hadrons in terms of constituent quarks, employing a factorization approximation where the six-quark matrix element separates into products of three-quark matrix elements. The calculation uses the Virginia potential framework, which assumes a quark pairing mechanism to generate effective meson-nucleon coupling constants, and is applied to three different quark models: the constituent quark model (CQM), the MIT bag model, and a relativistic model with linear confining potential. The internal quark degrees of freedom are integrated using three-quark wave functions in the Breit system, with relativistic effects introduced through explicit lower Dirac components.

## Key numbers

- For the constituent quark model with m_q = 0.33 GeV: g^T_{a1NN}/g^T_{a1qq} = 2.9; g^T_{rhoNN}/g^T_{rhoqq} = 1.5
- For the constituent quark model with m_q = 0.22 GeV: g^T_{a1NN}/g^T_{a1qq} = 4.9; g^T_{rhoNN}/g^T_{rhoqq} = 2.0
- For the MIT bag model: g^T_{a1NN}/g^T_{a1qq} = 3.5; g^T_{rhoNN}/g^T_{rhoqq} = 2.2
- For the linear confining model: g^T_{a1NN}/g^T_{a1qq} = 5.0; g^T_{rhoNN}/g^T_{rhoqq} = 3.2
- The anomalous coupling factor kappa for rho-type exchange is given by kappa = f/g, with values from the Virginia potential ranging from 1.53 (CQM) to 6.1 (Bonn potential)

## Context

This work extends the framework of effective T-odd P-even hadronic interactions parameterized by Simonius and Wyler (1977) for charged rho exchange and Sudarshan (1968) for axial vector exchange, providing a connection to quark-level degrees of freedom that was previously missing for the P-even case. The approach follows the Virginia potential method developed by Weber (1980) and Bozoian and Weber (1983) for strong interactions, and complements earlier work on P-odd T-odd interactions where PCAC and current algebra methods were applicable (Crewther et al., 1979). The results enable translation of experimental bounds on T-odd coupling strengths, such as those reviewed by Herczeg (1995), to more fundamental quark-level constraints, and suggest that the anomalous coupling factor kappa in the rho-type interaction can be related to nucleonic structure effects rather than being of T-violating origin.
