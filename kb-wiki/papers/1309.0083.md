# Chiral-odd pion generalized parton distributions beyond leading twist

> We define in a systematic way, based on the light-cone collinear factorization method, the chiral-odd generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of a pseudoscalar hadron (such as the pi0) up to twist 5. For that, we introduce the relevant matrix elements for 2-parton non-local operators, as well as matrix elements for 3-parton non-local correlators. Their detailed parametrization is fixed based on parity, charge conjugation and time reversal invariance. This leads to the introduction of 20 real GPDs, whose symmetry properties are explicitely given. The reduction of these GPDs to a minimal set is performed by the use of constraints provided by QCD equations of motion and rotation on the light-cone. We show that these 20 GPDs can be expressed through 8 GPDs which satisfy 4 integral sum rules. A surprising outcome of this analysis is the fact that, when assuming the vanishing of 3-parton correlators, as in the so-called Wandzura-Wilczek approximation, the whole set of GPDs vanishes.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic classification of chiral-odd generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for a pseudoscalar hadron (such as the pi0) up to twist 5, but the authors note that phenomenological applications are limited; a surprising result is that in the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation (where 3-parton correlators vanish), the entire set of 2-parton GPDs vanishes.

## Method

The authors use the light-cone collinear factorization (LCCF) method in momentum space, generalizing the Ellis-Furmanski-Petronzio approach to exclusive processes. They introduce matrix elements for 2-parton non-local operators and 3-parton non-local correlators, parametrizing them based on parity, charge conjugation, and time reversal invariance. The reduction to a minimal set of GPDs is performed using constraints from QCD equations of motion and the invariance under rotation and dilatation of the light-cone vector n.

## Key numbers

* 20 real chiral-odd GPDs are introduced for the pi0 from 2-parton and 3-parton correlators, covering twists 2 through 5.
* These 20 GPDs are reduced to a minimal set of 8 independent GPDs.
* The 8 independent GPDs satisfy 4 integral sum rules.
* In the Wandzura-Wilczek approximation (vanishing 3-parton correlators), the entire set of 2-parton GPDs vanishes.

## Context

This work extends the LCCF method previously applied to deeply virtual Compton scattering and meson production (Ji 1998, Collins 1998, Collins 1996) to the case of chiral-odd GPDs beyond leading twist. It builds on earlier analyses of chiral-odd distribution amplitudes for the rho meson (Ball 1996, Ball 1998) and studies of pi0 production (Goloskokov 2009, Goldstein 2010). The authors note that this classification for the pion is a preparatory step toward the more phenomenologically relevant case of chiral-odd nucleon GPDs, which will be addressed in forthcoming papers.
