# Non-perturbative definition of the QCD energy-momentum tensor on the lattice

> We present a strategy to define non-perturbatively the energy-momentum tensor in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) which satisfies the appropriate Ward identities and has the right trace anomaly. The tensor is defined by regularizing the theory on a lattice, and by fixing its renormalization constants non-perturbatively by suitable Ward identities associated to the Poincare' invariance of the continuum theory. The latter are derived in thermal QCD with a non-zero imaginary chemical potential formulated in a moving reference frame. A renormalization group analysis leads to simple renormalization-group-invariant definitions of the gluonic and fermionic contributions to either the singlet or the non-singlet components of the tensor, and therefore of their form factors among physical states. The lattice discussion focuses on the Wilson discretization of quark fields but the strategy is general. Specific to that case, we also carry out the analysis for the on-shell O(a)-improvement of the energy-momentum tensor. The renormalization and improvement programs profit from the fact that, as shown here, the thermal theory enjoys de-facto automatic O(a)-improvement at finite temperature. The validity of the proposal is scrutinized analytically by a study to 1-loop order in lattice perturbation theory with shifted and twisted (for quarks only) boundary conditions. The latter provides also additional useful insight for a precise non-perturbative calculation of the renormalization constants. The strategy proposed here is accessible to Monte Carlo computations, and in this sense it provides a practical way to define non-perturbatively the energy-momentum tensor in QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a strategy to define the energy-momentum tensor in lattice QCD non-perturbatively, satisfying the correct Ward identities and trace anomaly, by fixing its renormalization constants through Ward identities derived in thermal QCD with an imaginary chemical potential in a moving reference frame, with the condition that the strategy is validated analytically at 1-loop order in lattice perturbation theory with shifted and twisted boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors regularize QCD on a lattice using the Wilson plaquette action for gluons and the O(a)-improved Wilson-Dirac operator for quarks, then derive Ward identities associated with Poincare invariance in the continuum theory applied to thermal QCD with a non-zero imaginary chemical potential formulated in a moving reference frame. They perform a renormalization group analysis to obtain renormalization-group-invariant definitions of the gluonic and fermionic contributions to the singlet and non-singlet components of the energy-momentum tensor, and carry out an on-shell O(a)-improvement analysis. The proposal is scrutinized analytically through a 1-loop perturbative computation on the lattice with shifted and twisted boundary conditions, including explicit expressions for propagators, vertices, free-energy density, and expectation values of the vector current and sextet components of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Context

This work extends the program of non-perturbative renormalization of composite operators in lattice QCD, building on the automatic O(a)-improvement results for Wilson fermions from Frezzotti (2003) and Sint (2010), and on the thermal theory analysis by Dalla Brida (2017). The strategy enables practical Monte Carlo computations of the energy-momentum tensor in lattice QCD, providing a way to define its renormalization constants non-perturbatively, which is essential for studying transport coefficients, equation of state, and other thermodynamic properties of QCD matter.
