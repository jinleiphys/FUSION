# Data driven isospin analysis of timelike octet baryons electromagnetic form factors and charmonium decay into baryon-anti-baryon

> Inspired by the recent precise data, we perform model-independently an isospin decomposition of the timelike octet baryons electromagnetic form factors. As noted in our previous work, the relative magnitude of isoscalar and isovector component is determined with the input of data on various isospin channels. Herein we further assert that {their relative phase can be constraint by the phase difference of oscillatory modulation of effective form factors} between isospin channels. The framework is extended to analyze the data of differential cross sections and applied to the form factors of nucleon and hyperons with detail and isospin non-conservation of charmonium decay into baryon-anti-baryon as well. We address that isospin analysis is meaningful when the isospin broken scale is compared to or smaller than the uncertainties of data.

## Key claim

The relative phase between isoscalar and isovector components of timelike octet baryon electromagnetic form factors can be constrained by the phase difference of the oscillatory modulation of effective form factors between isospin channels, but the isospin analysis is only meaningful when the isospin broken scale is comparable to or smaller than the data uncertainties.

## Method

The authors perform a model-independent isospin decomposition of timelike octet baryon electromagnetic form factors using data from electron-positron annihilation into baryon-antibaryon pairs. They decompose the measured effective form factors into smooth (dipole-like) and oscillatory components, where the oscillatory part is attributed to vector meson production and parameterized as an exponentially damped cosine function of the baryon momentum. The isoscalar and isovector amplitudes are extracted from the smooth components of proton and neutron form factors, and the framework is extended to hyperons (Sigma baryons) and charmonium decay into baryon-antibaryon.

## Key numbers

- For nucleons: the ratio of smooth-component cross sections sigma_n^D / (sigma_p^0 / C) = 0.40 ± 0.03, constant over a wide energy range
- Oscillation parameters for nucleons: A_p = 0.07 ± 0.01, A_n = 0.08 ± 0.03, B_N = 1.01 ± 0.24 GeV^{-1}, C_N = 5.28 ± 0.36 GeV^{-1}, with phases D_p = 0.31 ± 0.17 and D_n = -3.77 ± 0.55, giving relative phase Delta_phi = (125 ± 12) degrees
- Isospin relevant ratio for nucleons: R_I^eff = (|G_p^D|^2 - |G_n^D|^2)/(|G_p^D|^2 + |G_n^D|^2) = 0.43 ± 0.03
- For Sigma baryons: cross section ratio of Sigma^+ to Sigma^- is 9.7 ± 1.3 from BESIII data
- Isospin relevant ratio for Sigma: R_I^eff = (|G_+|^2 - |G_-|^2)/(|G_+|^2 + |G_-|^2) = 0.81 ± 0.16
- Ratio for Sigma^0 channel: |G_0|^2/(|G_+|^2 + |G_-|^2) = 0.27 ± 0.04
- Dipole parameters for proton: A_p = 7.7, m_a^2 = 14.8 GeV^2; for neutron: A_n = 4.87 ± 0.09 with same pole mass
- Form factor ratio parameters: r_p = 3 ± 2 GeV^2, r_n = 6 ± 21 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous isospin formalism (Cao 2021) for nucleon electromagnetic form factors to the full octet of baryons, including hyperons. It builds on precise BESIII measurements of timelike nucleon form factors (BESIII 2021) and hyperon data from BESIII, BaBar (BaBar 2007), and Belle (Belle 2022). The analysis addresses the long-standing confusion about oscillatory structures in nucleon form factors previously interpreted as effects from nucleon internal structure (Bianconi 2015), vector meson excitations (de Melo 2008), threshold openings (Lorenz 2015), or final state interactions (Yang 2022). The framework enables future cross-checks of isospin amplitudes and quantification of isospin symmetry violation when more precise data become available, and can be applied to charmonium decay into baryon-antibaryon.
