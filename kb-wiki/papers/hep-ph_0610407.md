# Exploring backward pion electroproduction in the scaling regime

> We use general relations between the Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), entering the description of the p -> pi0 transition, and the proton Distribution Amplitudes (DAs) in the soft-pion limit to estimate the size of the amplitude for backward electroproduction of pi0 at large Q2.

## Key claim

The authors use the soft-pion limit to derive general relations between the proton-to-pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) and the proton Distribution Amplitudes (DAs), enabling an estimate of the amplitude for backward pion electroproduction at large \(Q^2\). The hedge is that this estimate relies on extrapolating the \(\xi \to 1\) soft-pion limit to the ERBL region, and the quantitative results are promised for future publication.

## Method

The authors define leading-twist TDAs for the \(p \to \pi^0\) transition at zero transverse momentum transfer (\(\Delta_T=0\)) from the Fourier transform of a three-quark matrix element. They apply the soft-pion theorem from current algebra to relate these TDAs to the proton DAs in the limit \(\xi \to 1\), obtaining explicit relations: \(V^{p\pi^0}_1 \to V^p(x_i/2)\), \(A^{p\pi^0}_1 \to A^p(x_i/2)\), and \(T^{p\pi^0}_1 \to 3 T^p(x_i/2)\). They then write the leading-order amplitude for \(\gamma^\star P \to P' \pi^0\) in terms of these TDAs and compare it to the baryonic form factor amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 133\) MeV is used in the TDA decomposition.
- The proton DA normalization constant \(f_N\) is estimated via QCD sum rules as \(5.2 \times 10^{-3}\) GeV\(^2\) (from Chernyak and Zhitnitsky, 1984).
- The asymptotic form of the proton DAs is \(120 x_1 x_2 x_3\), which gives a vanishing result for the proton form factor \(F_1^p\) or \(G_M^p\) but a nonzero result for the transition form factor \(F^{p\pi^0}\) due to the factor 3 in the \(T_1\) relation.

## Context

This work extends the factorisation approach for exclusive processes, previously applied to \(\pi^-\pi^+ \to \gamma^*\gamma\) and advocated for backward virtual Compton scattering (Lansberg, Pire, Szymanowski, 2006) and backward pion electroproduction, to the specific case of the \(p \to \pi^0\) transition. It uses the soft-pion limit to connect TDAs to the well-studied proton DAs of Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1984), enabling estimates that suggest the perturbative contribution may dominate at lower \(Q^2\) than for the proton form factor. The results are intended for comparison with measurements by Laveissiere et al. (2003) and will be presented in a forthcoming publication.
