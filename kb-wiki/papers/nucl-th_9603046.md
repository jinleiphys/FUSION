# Structure of Low-Energy Collective $0^{-}$-States in Doubly Magic Nuclei and Matrix Elements of the P-odd and P- and T-odd Weak Interaction

> The structure of the collective low-energy $J^π=0^{-}$ (T=0 and T=1) modes is studied for a doubly magic nucleus in a schematic analytic model of RPA. The $0^{-}$ phonon states ($T= 0,1$) lie at energies $E_{T=0}(0^{-}) \alt ω$ and $E_{T=1}(0^{-}) > ω$, where $ω$ is the oscillator frequency. The matrix elements of P-odd and P- and T-odd weak one-body potentials connecting the ground state to these $0^{-}$-states, $W_{coll}$, are enhanced by the factor $\sim 2 (\fracω{E})^{1/2}A^{1/3} \sim 10$ as compared to the single-particle value $w_{sp}$ what can result in values $|W_{coll}| \sim 20-30 eV$ if standard values of DDH parameters are used for $w_{sp}$. Similar enhancement arises in the P- and T-odd case.

# Structure of Low-Energy Collective 0⁻-States in Doubly Magic Nuclei and Matrix Elements of the P-odd and P- and T-odd Weak Interaction

## Key claim

The collective enhancement factor for matrix elements of P-odd and P- and T-odd weak one-body potentials connecting the ground state to low-energy 0⁻ phonon states in doubly magic nuclei is approximately 2(ω/E)^(1/2)A^(1/3) ~ 10 relative to the single-particle value, but this enhancement depends on the specific choice of residual interaction parameters and can become significantly larger (rising from 10-20 to considerably larger values) as the isoscalar 0⁻ mode energy approaches zero, with the isoscalar mode potentially becoming unstable for certain choices of the ρ-meson coupling constant.

## Method

The authors employed a schematic analytic random phase approximation (RPA) model for a doubly magic nucleus, using a harmonic oscillator single-particle basis and a residual strong interaction parametrized in the Landau-Migdal form with both velocity-independent (V₀) and velocity-dependent (V₁) components. They derived closed-form RPA equations for collective 0⁻ phonon operators by assuming a collective ansatz based on the operators (σ·r) and (σ·p), obtaining analytic expressions for the isoscalar (T=0) and isovector (T=1) phonon energies and the matrix elements of weak symmetry-violating operators. The calculations were also performed using explicit π- and ρ-meson exchange interactions as an alternative to the Landau-Migdal velocity-dependent term.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar 0⁻ phonon energy: E(T=0) = 1.03 ω, where ω is the oscillator frequency, using standard Landau-Migdal parameters (g=0.575, g'=0.725, g₁=-0.5, g'₁=-0.26)
- Isovector 0⁻ phonon energy: E(T=1) = 1.20 ω, using the same Landau-Migdal parameters
- Collective enhancement factor for P-odd matrix elements: F^P ~ 10-15 for the above Landau-Migdal parameter values
- For the π- and ρ-meson exchange interaction, the isoscalar 0⁻ mode energy ranges from approximately 0.2 ω to 0.4 ω for ρ-meson coupling constants f²_ρ between 1.86 (weak ρ-meson) and 4.86 (strong ρ-meson)
- The collective P-odd matrix element for ²⁰⁶Pb is estimated as |W^P_coll| ~ 20-30 eV, using weak interaction strengths g_p ≃ 4 and g_n ≃ 1 consistent with standard DDH parameters
- The universal Migdal constant used: C = 300 MeV·fm³
- The phonon normalization constant for N=Z: N_coll = 3A

## Context

This work extends earlier doorway state approaches to parity violation in compound nuclei by Auerbach (1990) and Auerbach and Bowman (1990), which identified the 0⁻ component of the spin-dipole state as an important mediator of parity mixing. The authors provide an analytic RPA confirmation of the collective enhancement of weak matrix elements that was assumed in those earlier works. The results are consistent with numerical Hartree-Fock+RPA calculations of the 0⁻ (T=1) states by Auerbach and Klein (1983) and with earlier studies of spin-dipole modes by Traini, Cazzola, and Zanon (1984) and Auerbach and Zamick (1984). The work enables quantitative predictions for observable parity-violating effects in electromagnetic transitions (multipole mixing) in doubly magic nuclei, and provides a framework for calculating the enhancement of both P-odd and P- and T-odd weak interaction matrix elements in nuclear structure.
