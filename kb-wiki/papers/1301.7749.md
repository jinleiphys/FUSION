# Longitudinal thermalization via the chromo-Weibel instability

> Non-Abelian plasma instabilities play an important role in the non-equilibrium dynamics of a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma. Using the discretized hard loop framework we calculate the time evolution of soft gluonic fields in a longitudinally free streaming background. Extrapolating our results to energies probed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions we find a pressure anisotropy that persists for a few fm/c. However, the chromofields quickly develop a Boltzmann longitudi- nal energy spectrum, suggesting fast longitudinal thermalization of the quark gluon plasma even though it remains momentum-space anisotropic. In this proceedings contribution we review our recent numerical results, present new results for the scaling of the isotropization time with the initial current fluctuation amplitude, and present tests of the gauge invariance of the extracted longitudinal spectra.

## Key claim
The chromo-Weibel instability in a longitudinally free-streaming quark-gluon plasma produces a Boltzmann (exponential) longitudinal energy spectrum, suggesting fast longitudinal thermalization even while the plasma remains momentum-space anisotropic; however, the inferred isotropization times should be taken as a lower bound because the hard-loop framework does not fully incorporate back-reaction at late times when field energy becomes comparable to particle energy.

## Method
The authors used the hard expanding loop (HEL) framework, solving coupled Vlasov-type equations for color perturbations and non-Abelian Yang-Mills equations for soft gauge fields in a longitudinally free-streaming background, discretized on a lattice with temporal gauge. They extracted isotropization times from the ratio of longitudinal to transverse total pressure (particle plus field) and decomposed chromo-electromagnetic fields into longitudinal wave number ν to obtain energy spectra, fitting them to a massless Boltzmann distribution integrated over transverse momenta.

## Key numbers
- Isotropization time extrapolated to initial current fluctuation amplitude Δ = 1.6 yields τ̃_ISO ∼ 5 (in units where one unit corresponds to approximately 1 fm/c at LHC and 2 fm/c at RHIC).
- The isotropization time scales as τ̃_ISO ∼ (log Δ⁻¹)² for Δ values in {0.1, 0.15, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8}.
- Lattice parameters: N_T² × N_η × N_u × N_φ = 40² × 128 × 128 × 32, with transverse lattice spacing a = Q_s⁻¹ and longitudinal lattice spacing a_η = 0.025.
- Random gauge transformations were restricted to |ξ|_r.m.s. ≤ 0.2 to keep total energy violations below 2% (occasional deviations up to 2%).
- At RHIC energies, isotropization occurs at ∼10 fm/c; at LHC energies, at ∼5 fm/c.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous HEL simulations (Attems 2012) by presenting new scaling results for isotropization time with initial fluctuation amplitude and testing gauge invariance of longitudinal spectra. It contrasts with earlier hard-loop simulations that found saturation of instability growth and turbulent power-law spectra; instead, the authors observe exponential (Boltzmann) spectra. The results provide a lower bound on isotropization times for heavy-ion collisions and suggest that longitudinal thermalization via chromo-Weibel instabilities may occur faster than previously expected, enabling further studies of thermalization mechanisms in weakly coupled QGP.
