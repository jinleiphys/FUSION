# Microscopic description of Cf-252 cold fission yields

> We investigate the cold fission of 252Cf within the two center shell model to compute the potential energy surface. The fission yields are estimated by using the semiclassical penetration approach. It turns out that the inner cold valley of the total potential energy is strongly connected with Z=50 magic number. The agreement with experimental values is very much improved only by considering mass and charge asymmetry degrees of freedom. Thus, indeed cold fission of 252Cf is a Sn-like radioactivity, related the other two "magic radioactivities", namely alpha-decay and heavy-cluster decay, called also Pb-like radioactivity. This calculation provides the necessary theoretical confidence to estimate the penetration cross section in producing superheavy nuclei, by using the inverse fusion process.

# Microscopic description of Cf-252 cold fission yields

## Key claim

The cold fission of 252Cf is shown to be a Sn-like radioactivity, strongly connected with the Z=50 magic number, but this agreement with experimental yields is achieved **only** by assuming that the fission path proceeds through an isomeric configuration in the second well rather than by direct barrier penetration.

## Method

The authors used the two-center shell model with a Woods-Saxon potential to compute the potential energy surface for 252Cf binary partitions. Fission yields were estimated via the semiclassical WKB penetration approach, using a five-dimensional nuclear shape parametrization (elongation, necking, mass asymmetry, and two fragment deformations) with the macroscopic energy from the Yukawa-plus-exponential model and microscopic shell corrections from Strutinsky prescriptions. The inertial parameter was computed in the cranking approximation, and the fission trajectory was taken as a linear variation of generalized coordinates from ground-state values to final fragment values.

## Key numbers

- Maximum experimental yield is located at light fragment mass A2 = 107; maximum theoretical transmission from the two-step (isomeric) path is at A2 = 106.
- The isomeric state in the second well is located at A2 = 110 and elongation R ≈ 8 fm.
- A sudden drop in theoretical penetrability occurs for channels with A2 > 110, corresponding to the transition from prolate to oblate ground-state fragment deformations.
- The liquid-drop model alone gave a maximum theoretical transmission at A2 = 104, failing to reproduce the experimental peak at A2 = 107.
- An enhancement of fission probability was obtained for the partition 120Ag + 132Sb (Z=50 in the heavy fragment).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of 252Cf cold fission using the double folding potential method (Sandulescu 1996, 1998) and macroscopic models (Gonnen 1991) by introducing a fully microscopic two-center shell model approach. The authors show that direct barrier transmission fails to reproduce experimental yields, but a two-step path through an isomeric configuration in the second well yields good agreement for A2 < 110. This connects cold fission to the same "magic radioactivity" family as alpha-decay and heavy-cluster (Pb-like) decay, and the calculation provides theoretical confidence for estimating penetration cross sections in producing superheavy nuclei via inverse fusion.
