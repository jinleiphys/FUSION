# Neutron-neutral particle mixing and its observable consequences

> In this work, we explore the mixing between neutron ($n$) and elementary neutral particle ($η$), which violates both the baryon number ($\mathcal{B}$) and the lepton number ($\mathcal{L}$) by one unit but conserves their difference $(\mathcal{B}-\mathcal{L})$. Such mixing may give rise to non-trivial effects that are different from the Standard Model predictions. We organize our discussions based on two scenarios, roughly depending on whether an interference between oscillation and decay occurs, or whether the new-physics effects associated with the $n$-$η$ mixing contribute to the absorptive mixing amplitude. If an oscillation process is not accompanied by an interference between oscillation and decay, or the new-physics interactions do not contribute to the absorptive mixing amplitude, such a process can be classified as pure oscillation. Otherwise, it can be classified as impure oscillation. In the scenario of pure oscillation, CP-violation arising from the Majorana phase can manifest itself through the $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation process and may lead to observable effects. In the scenario of impure oscillation, we analyze the testable implications on the masses and lifetimes of the mass eigenstates formed as a result of the $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation mediated by $η$. In this scenario, we also suggest a unified interpretation of the neutron lifetime anomaly and the $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation measurements based on the $n$-$η$ mixing. In both scenarios, we present the lower bounds imposed by the experimental searches for $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillations on the masses of the color multiplet bosons and point out that they could be within the reach of a direct detection at the LHC or future high-energy experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that mixing between the neutron ($n$) and an elementary neutral particle ($\eta$), which violates baryon number and lepton number by one unit each while conserving their difference, can produce observable effects in neutron-antineutron ($n$-$\bar{n}$) oscillation, with the specific phenomenology depending on whether the new-physics interactions contribute to the absorptive mixing amplitude (impure oscillation) or not (pure oscillation).

## Method

The authors construct effective field theory operators for $n$-$\eta$ mixing mediated by color-multiplet scalar bosons (diquarks) transforming as $(3,1,2/3)$ or $(3,1,-4/3)$ under the Standard Model gauge group, with coupling constants $\lambda_{11}$ and $\mu_{11}$. They derive the effective mass matrix for the $n$-$\eta$ system including magnetic field effects, compute mixing angles and oscillation probabilities, and classify the resulting $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation into two scenarios based on whether the $n$-$\eta$ mixing contributes to the absorptive mixing amplitude. Lower bounds on the color multiplet boson masses are extracted from experimental $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation limits.

## Key numbers

* Lower limit on $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation time from the ILL experiment in field-free vacuum: approximately $0.86 \times 10^{8}$ s.
* Most stringent constraint on $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation time from Super-Kamiokande (converted to field-free vacuum values): $4.7 \times 10^{8}$ s.
* Neutron lifetime from trap experiment: $\tau_n = 877.75^{+0.28}_{-0.28} \text{(stat.)}^{+0.22}_{-0.16}\text{(syst.)}$ s.
* Neutron lifetime from beam experiment: $\tau_n = 887.7^{+1.2}_{-1.2} \text{(stat.)}^{+1.9}_{-1.9}\text{(syst.)}$ s, giving approximately $4\sigma$ deviation between the two methods.
* Lattice QCD value for the quark overlap factor: $|\psi_q(0)|^2 = 0.0144 (3)(21)$ GeV$^3$.
* Neutron g-factor: $g_n \simeq -3.826$; nuclear magneton: $\mu_N \simeq 3.152 \times 10^{-8}$ eV$\cdot$T$^{-1}$.
* Magnetic field in ILL neutron propagation region: $B \lesssim 1 \times 10^{-8}$ T.
* Typical values chosen for the product of coupling constants: $|\lambda_{11} \mu_{11}| \simeq 10^{-1}$, $10^{-2}$, and $10^{-3}$.
* Mass range for color multiplet bosons accessible to LHC or future experiments: from several TeV to several $10$ TeV.
* Upper bounds on $|\lambda_{ij}\mu_{ij}|$ from FCNC constraints: from order $10^{-4}$ to order $1$.
* Restriction on $\eta$ mass for kinetic allowance of neutron decay: $m_{\eta} \lesssim m_n$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of neutron-mirror neutron mixing studied by Berezhiani (2004-2021) and Mohapatra (2005-2009) by replacing the composite mirror neutron with an elementary neutral particle $\eta$, which allows description via dimension-6 operators rather than the more suppressed dimension-9 operators. The paper connects to the neutron lifetime anomaly (approximately $4\sigma$ discrepancy between trap and beam experiments reported by Gonzalez (2021) and Yue (2013)) and proposes a unified interpretation with $n$-$\bar{n}$ oscillation measurements. The analysis builds on the partial compositeness model of Kaplan (1991) and the classification of neutral particle oscillations into pure and impure types, analogous to neutrino oscillation and meson-antimeson oscillation respectively. The results enable experimental searches at the LHC and future high-energy facilities for the color multiplet bosons mediating the mixing.
