# Non-Universal Behavior of the k-Body Embedded Gaussian Unitary Ensemble of Random Matrices

> Using a novel approach, we investigate the shape of the average spectrum and the spectral fluctuations of the $k$-body embedded unitary ensemble in the limit of large matrix dimension. We identify the transition point between semicircle and Gaussian shape. The transition also affects the spectral fluctuations which deviate from Wigner-Dyson form and become Poissonian in the limit $k << m << l$. Here $m$ is the number of Fermions and $l$ the number of degenerate single-particle states.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for the k-body embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble (EGUE(k)), the spectral fluctuations deviate from universal Wigner-Dyson form and become Poissonian in the limit \(k \ll m \ll l\), contrary to previous numerical simulations that had suggested universal behavior due to small matrix dimensions.

## Method

The authors used three complementary methods: the supersymmetry approach, the binary correlation approximation of Mon and French (1975), and the construction of two limiting ensembles (EGUE_min(k) and EGUE_max(k)). They analyzed the second moment of the ensemble via an eigenvector expansion and a duality relation connecting k-body and (m-k)-body interactions, then computed the kurtosis and loop corrections to determine the transition point between semicircle and Gaussian spectral shapes.

## Key numbers

- The transition point between semicircle and Gaussian average spectrum occurs at \(2k = m\), where \(m\) is the number of fermions and \(k\) the body rank of the interaction.
- For \(l \to \infty\) with fixed \(k\) and \(m\), the kurtosis parameter \(Q(k,m,l)\) tends to \({m-k \choose k}/{m \choose k}\) when \(2k \leq m\), and to 0 when \(2k > m\).
- The number of links per vertex \(M = \sum_{s=1}^k {m \choose s} {l-m \choose s}\); for \(k < m\), \(M < N-1\) while for \(k=m\), \(M = N-1\).
- The loop correction for the two-point function vanishes like \(N^{-2}\) for \(k=m\) but only like \((\ln N)^{-2k}\) for fixed \(k\) and filling factor \(f\).
- For \(k \ll m \ll l\), terms with \(s\) pairs of \(V_k\)'s not on the same Green function are smaller by at least a factor \(l^{-sk}\) than the terms with \(s=0\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of the embedded Gaussian ensemble introduced by Mon and French (1975), for which previous results had been limited to numerical simulations with small matrix dimensions. The paper contradicts the earlier expectation that EGUE(k) always yields Wigner-Dyson spectral fluctuations, as found in numerical work by French and collaborators (1971). The results enable future study of the transition regime between universal and Poissonian statistics, and the extension to the orthogonal case EGOE(k) is given in Benet, Rupp, and Weidenmueller (2000).
