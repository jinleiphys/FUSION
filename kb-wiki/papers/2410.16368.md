# New Angles on Energy Correlators

> Energy correlators have recently come to the forefront of jet substructure studies at colliders due to their remarkable properties: they naturally separate physics at different scales, are robust to contamination from soft radiation, and offer a direct connection with quantum field theory. The current parametrization used for energy correlators, however, is based on redundant pairwise angles with complex phase space restrictions. In this Letter, we introduce a new parametrization of energy correlators that features a simpler phase space structure and preserves information about the orientation of jet constituents. Further, our parametrization drastically reduces the computational cost to compute energy correlators on experimental data; whereas the time to compute a traditional projected $N$-point energy correlator scales as $M^N/N!$ on a jet with $M$ particles, our new parametrization achieves a scaling of $M^2 \log M$, remarkably independently of N. Even for N=3, this improved scaling is particularly important for studies of heavy ion collisions, and higher values of $N$ will enable new qualitative understanding of gauge theories. Theoretical calculations for our new energy correlators differ from those of traditional parametrizations only at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy and beyond, and we expect that our simpler phase space structure will simplify those calculations. We also discuss how to extend our parametrization to resolved $N$-point energy correlators that encode angular distances between greater numbers of particles, yielding intuitive visualizations of jet substructure that are qualitatively different for different jet samples. We propose two possible generalizations for probing multi-prong jets and testing jet scaling behavior.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a new parametrization of energy correlators that replaces the traditional redundant pairwise angles with a simpler phase space structure based on oriented polar coordinates centered on a special particle, achieving computational scaling of \(M^2 \log M\) independent of \(N\) (compared to \(M^N/N!\) for traditional projected \(N\)-point correlators). The new and old parametrizations differ theoretically only at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy and beyond.

## Method
The authors define a new projected \(N\)-point energy correlator (PENC) using a variable \(R_1\) that represents the maximum distance from a single "special" particle \(s\) to the other \(N-1\) particles, averaged over all choices of \(s\). For resolved energy correlators (RENCs), they employ \(2N-3\) non-redundant polar coordinates: radial distances \(R_j\) ordered by size and oriented azimuthal angles \(\phi_j\) measured relative to the previous resolved emission. The cumulative distribution is computed by sorting particles by distance to each special particle and summing momentum fractions within disks of radius \(R_1\).

## Key numbers
- Computational scaling for traditional projected \(N\)-point energy correlator: \(M^N/N!\) on a jet with \(M\) particles; new parametrization achieves \(M^2 \log M\), independent of \(N\).
- For \(N=3\), the improved scaling is particularly important for heavy ion collision studies.
- The new parametrization uses \(2N-3\) variables to completely characterize particle positions, compared to the \(\binom{N}{2}\) redundant pairwise distances in the traditional approach.
- The new PENC and traditional PENC satisfy \(R_L/2 \leq R_1 \leq R_L\) by the triangle inequality.
- Jet function differences between new and old parametrizations appear at \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) (NNLL).
- Data used: CMS 2011A Jet Primary Dataset, anti-\(k_t\) jets with \(p_T^{\text{jet}} \in [500, 550]\) GeV and \(|\eta^{\text{jet}}| < 1.9\).

## Context
This work extends the energy correlator formalism developed by Chen et al. (2020) for projected \(N\)-point correlators and by Dixon et al. (2019) for higher-point calculations. It addresses computational bottlenecks that have limited applications in heavy ion collisions (Bossi et al., 2024) and non-integer \(N\) studies (Budhraja et al., 2024). The new parametrization enables simpler theoretical calculations for the jet function, which previously required dedicated calculations for each \(N\) value (Dixon et al., 2019; Chen et al., 2023). The approach also connects to the "jets-without-jets" formalism of Bertolini et al. (2013, 2015) through a moment relation with the differential jet rate.
