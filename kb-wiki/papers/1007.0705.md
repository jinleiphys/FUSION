# A relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic description for systems including particle number changing processes

> Relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equations are extended by taking into account particle number changing processes in a gluon system, which expands in one dimension boost-invariantly. Chemical equilibration is treated by a rate equation for the particle number density based on Boltzmann equation and Grad's ansatz for the off-equilibrium particle phase space distribution. We find that not only the particle production, but also the temperature and the momentum spectra of the gluon system, obtained from the hydrodynamic calculations, are sensitive to the rates of particle number changing processes. Comparisons of the hydrodynamic calculations with the transport ones employing the parton cascade BAMPS show the inaccuracy of the rate equation at large shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. To improve the rate equation, the Grad's ansatz has to be modified beyond the second moments in momentum.

# A relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic description for systems including particle number changing processes

## Key claim
The paper extends relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics to include particle number changing processes via a rate equation derived from the Boltzmann equation using Grad's ansatz, but finds that this rate equation becomes inaccurate at large shear viscosity to entropy density ratios, requiring modification of Grad's ansatz beyond second moments in momentum.

## Method
The authors couple second-order Israel-Stewart and extended third-order hydrodynamic equations with a rate equation for particle number density in a one-dimensional boost-invariant expanding gluon system. They compare hydrodynamic solutions to transport calculations from the parton cascade BAMPS, using elastic binary 2-to-2 and inelastic 2-to-3 processes with isotropically distributed collision angles and a constant eta/s value.

## Key numbers
- Temperature decreases by 20% when fugacity increases from 0.5 to 1, for fixed energy density (since e ~ lambda T^4)
- Between the limits of sigma_23=0 (particle number conservation) and lambda=1 (instantaneous chemical equilibrium), there is a 50% difference in temperature at final time tau=10 fm/c
- The third-order correction term alpha = -8/9 in the shear tensor evolution equation
- Initial conditions: tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c, T(tau_0) = 0.5 GeV, lambda(tau_0) = 1 (or 0.2 for undersaturated case)
- Constant eta/s = 0.35 used for the main comparison calculations
- Deviations of the Grad's approximation from BAMPS results are on the level of 10%-20% at later times tau ~ 1-2 fm/c
- Third-order hydrodynamic results agree with BAMPS even at eta/s = 0.75, while Israel-Stewart results deviate by 10%-20% for eta/s = 0.35-0.75

## Context
This work extends previous studies of chemical equilibration in QGP that used either ideal hydrodynamics (Biro 1993, Srivastava 1996) or first-order hydrodynamics (Dutta 1999), by applying second-order Israel-Stewart and third-order equations. It builds on the authors' prior work (El 2009, third-order hydrodynamics) and comparisons with BAMPS (Xu 2004). The approach enables more realistic modeling of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC, particularly for dilepton and photon yields, and for hadronization using recombination models. The paper identifies the need for improved off-equilibrium distribution functions beyond Grad's ansatz as a direction for future work.
