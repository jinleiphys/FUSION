# Pion charge form factor and constraints from space-time translations

> The role of Poincaré covariant space-time translations is investigated in the case of a relativistic quantum mechanics approach to the pion charge form factor. It is shown that the related constraints are generally inconsistent with the assumption of a single-particle current, which is most often referred to. The only exception is the front-form approach with $q^+=0$. How accounting for the related constraints, as well as restoring the equivalence of different RQM approaches in estimating form factors, is discussed. Some extensions of this work and, in particular, the relationship with a dispersion-relation approach, are presented. Conclusions relative to the underlying dynamics are given.

# Pion charge form factor and constraints from space-time translations

## Key claim

The assumption of a single-particle current in relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) calculations of the pion charge form factor is generally inconsistent with constraints from Poincaré covariant space-time translations, except in the front-form approach with the kinematic condition q^+ = 0; accounting for these constraints restores the equivalence of different RQM approaches.

## Method

The authors test the consistency of single-particle current operators against constraints derived from the commutator of the space-time translation operator P^μ with the current, following relations proposed by Lev (1993). They modify the momentum transfer factor in the current by a coefficient α to enforce equality between the squared momentum transferred to the system (q^2) and to the constituents, then compute the pion charge form factor using a wave function from a mass operator with confinement and instantaneous one-gluon exchange interactions.

## Key numbers

- The calculated pion decay constant f_π overestimates the measured value: 106 MeV versus 93 MeV experimentally.
- The squared charge radius r_π^2 is related to f_π by the relation r_π^2 = 3/(4π^2 f_π^2).
- At low Q^2, the discrepancy between different RQM approaches exhibits in some cases a dependence of the squared radius on the inverse of the squared pion mass.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Desplanques (2004, 2008) on scalar constituent systems to the physical pion with spin-1/2 constituents, and builds on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction of the Poincaré algebra (Bakamjian, 1953) and its generalizations by Keister. The results contradict the approach of Krutov (2001) by a factor (s_i + s_f + Q^2)/(2√(s_i s_f)) while agreeing with Melikhov (2001). The work enables a path to restore equivalence among instant, front, and point forms of RQM, and connects the common expression to a dispersion-relation approach that is explicitly Lorentz invariant.
