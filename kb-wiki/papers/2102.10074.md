# Astrophysical Constraints on the Symmetry Energy and the Neutron Skin of $^{208}$Pb with Minimal Modeling Assumptions

> The symmetry energy and its density dependence are crucial inputs for many nuclear physics and astrophysics applications, as they determine properties ranging from the neutron-skin thickness of nuclei to the crust thickness and the radius of neutron stars. Recently, PREX-II reported a value of $0.283 \pm 0.071$ fm for the neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb, implying a slope parameter $L = 106 \pm 37$ MeV, larger than most ranges obtained from microscopic calculations and other nuclear experiments. We use a nonparametric equation of state representation based on Gaussian processes to constrain the symmetry energy $S_0$, $L$, and $R_\mathrm{skin}^{^{208}\mathrm{Pb}}$ directly from observations of neutron stars with minimal modeling assumptions. The resulting astrophysical constraints from heavy pulsar masses, LIGO/Virgo, and NICER clearly favor smaller values of the neutron skin and $L$, as well as negative symmetry incompressibilities. Combining astrophysical data with PREX-II and chiral effective field theory constraints yields $S_0 = 33.0^{+2.0}_{-1.8}$ MeV, $L=53^{+14}_{-15}$ MeV, and $R_\mathrm{skin}^{^{208}\mathrm{Pb}}=0.17^{+0.04}_{-0.04}$ fm.

## Key claim

The paper uses a nonparametric Gaussian process representation of the nuclear equation of state to constrain the symmetry energy parameters and neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb directly from neutron star observations, finding that astrophysical data favor smaller values of the slope parameter L and neutron-skin thickness than reported by the PREX-II experiment, though the tension is described as "mild" given PREX-II's broad uncertainties.

## Method

The authors employ a nonparametric Gaussian process representation for the nuclear equation of state (EOS) that models uncertainty in sound-speed correlations without specifying a fixed functional form, minimizing model dependence compared to parametric approaches. They generate EOS realizations via Monte Carlo sampling, then weight them against astrophysical observations including pulsar mass measurements (PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0348+0432), gravitational-wave data from GW170817, and NICER X-ray pulse-profile measurements of PSR J0030+0451. The symmetry energy parameters S0, L, and Ksym are extracted from each EOS realization by reconstructing the proton fraction in beta-equilibrium around nuclear saturation density using a quadratic expansion in asymmetry, with the neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb obtained from an empirical linear correlation with L based on nonrelativistic Skyrme and relativistic mean-field density functionals from Mondal (2016).

## Key numbers

* PREX-II reported neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, implying L = 106 ± 37 MeV (from Reed 2021)
* Combined astrophysical data with PREX-II and chiral effective field theory constraints yield: S0 = 33.0 (+2.0/-1.8) MeV, L = 53 (+14/-15) MeV, R_skin(208Pb) = 0.17 (+0.04/-0.04) fm
* Astrophysical-only constraints (nonparametric): L = 55-65 MeV (peak range from posterior)
* The correlation between L and R_skin(208Pb) from Mondal (2016): mu_R [fm] = 0.072 + 0.00194 × (L [MeV]), with sigma_R = 0.0143 fm
* Reference saturation density: n0 = 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends the nonparametric EOS inference framework developed by Landry, Essick, and Holz (2019-2020) to directly constrain nuclear symmetry energy parameters from astrophysical observations, contrasting with parametric approaches such as those by Alam and Agrawal (2016) or Carson and Steiner (2019). The results are in tension with the high L value inferred from PREX-II by Reed (2021) but agree well with dipole polarizability constraints from Roca Maza (2015) and with chiral effective field theory calculations from Hebeler (2013), Tews (2012), and Drischler (2017). The analysis enables future joint constraints from nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations, and suggests that if high L values from PREX-II are confirmed with smaller uncertainty, this may indicate a phase transition or peak in sound speed around saturation density.
