# Cosmological and Particle Physics Constraints on a New Non-Abelian SU(3) Gauge Model for Ordinary/Dark Matter Interaction

> We propose a mirror model for ordinary and dark matter that assumes a new SU(3) gauge group of transformations, as a natural extension of the Standard Model (SM). A close study of big bang nucleosynthesis, baryon asymmetries, cosmic microwave background bounds, galaxy dynamics, together with the Standard Model assumptions, help us to set a limit on the mass and width of the new gauge boson. The cross section for the elastic scattering of a dark proton by an ordinary proton is estimated and compare to the WIMP--nucleon experimental upper bounds. It is observed that all experimental bounds for the various cross sections can be accommodated consistently within the gauge model. We also suggest a way for direct detection of the new gauge boson via one example of a SM forbidden process: $e^+ + p \rightarrow μ^+ + X$, where $X = Λ$ or $Λ_c$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a mirror dark matter model based on a new SU(3) gauge symmetry that can accommodate all experimental bounds on cross sections, but only if the number of dark sectors is exactly five to explain the observed ratio of dark matter to baryonic matter, and if the dark sectors are colder than the ordinary matter sector with a temperature ratio T'/T < 0.52 for five sectors.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian with a new SU(3) gauge group where Standard Model matter fields are grouped into four multiplets by electric charge, and dark matter sectors are copies of this structure. A new massive gauge boson (WIMG) couples ordinary and dark sectors, acquiring mass through a scalar condensate mechanism rather than the Higgs mechanism. The model is constrained using Big Bang Nucleosynthesis bounds on extra relativistic degrees of freedom, baryon asymmetry requirements, and particle physics constraints from lepton anomalous magnetic moments, muon decay, and heavy meson leptonic decays.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of dark matter to baryonic matter: Omega_DM / Omega_b = 4.94 ± 0.66, requiring N_DM = 5 dark sectors
- BBN bound on temperature ratio for N_DM = 5: T'/T < 0.52
- Lower bound on WIMG mass from muon decay: M ≥ g_M × 28 TeV; for g_M ≈ 0.30, M ≥ 9 TeV
- Bound on coupling from muon anomalous magnetic moment (non-chiral theory): g^2_M / M^2 ≤ 8.14 × 10^{-13} MeV^{-2}, giving M ≥ g_M × 1.1 TeV
- Bound on coupling from muon anomalous magnetic moment (chiral theory): g^2_M / M^2 ≤ 4.33 × 10^{-11} MeV^{-2}, giving M ≥ g_M × 0.152 TeV
- Predicted branching ratio upper bound: Br(D^0 → μ^- e^+) < 8.7 × 10^{-13} (experimental limit: < 2.6 × 10^{-7})
- Predicted branching ratio upper bound: Br(B^0 → τ^- e^+) < 2.3 × 10^{-9} (experimental limit: < 2.8 × 10^{-5})
- Predicted branching ratio upper bound: Br(B^0_s → τ^- μ^+) < 2.7 × 10^{-9}

## Context

This work extends the mirror dark matter framework of Foot, Akhmedov, and Berezhiani (1996) and asymmetric dark matter models (ADM1-ADM4) by introducing a new SU(3) gauge symmetry rather than the usual U(1) or SU(2) mirror symmetries. The model is distinguished from prior approaches by requiring only two new parameters (g_M and M) and by using a scalar condensate mechanism for mass generation instead of the Higgs mechanism. The paper enables experimental searches for the WIMG through lepton flavor violating decays of D and B mesons, and through the SM-forbidden process e^+ + p → μ^+ + X (X = Λ or Λ_c), with predicted branching ratios that are orders of magnitude below current experimental limits.
