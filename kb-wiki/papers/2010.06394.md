# Evolution of $β$-decay half-lives at finite-temperatures

> $β$-decay properties of nuclei are investigated within the relativistic nuclear energy density functional framework by varying the temperature and density, conditions relevant to the final stages of stellar evolution. Both thermal and nuclear pairing effects are taken into account in the description of nuclear properties and in the finite temperature proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FT-PNRQRPA) to calculate the relevant allowed and first-forbidden transitions in the $β$-decay. The temperature and density effects are studied on the $β$-decay half-lives between temperatures $T = 0-1.5$ MeV, and at densities $ρY_e = 10^7$ g/cm${}^3$ and $10^9$ g/cm${}^3$. The relevant Gamow-Teller transitions are also investigated for Ti, Fe, Cd, and Sn isotopic chains at finite temperatures. We find that the $β$-decay half-lives increase with increasing density $ρY_e$, whereas half-lives generally decrease with increasing temperature. It is shown that the temperature effects decrease the half-lives considerably in nuclei with longer half-lives at zero temperature, while only slight changes for nuclei with short half-lives are obtained. We also show the importance of including the de-excitation transitions in the calculation of the $β$-decay half-lives at finite temperatures. Comparing the FT-PNQRPA results with the shell-model calculations for $pf-$shell nuclei, a reasonable agreement is obtained for the temperature dependence of $β$-decay rates. Finally, large-scale calculations of $β$-decay half-lives are performed at temperatures $T_9(\text{K}) = 5$ and $T_9(\text{K}) = 10$ and densities $ρY_e = 10^7$ g/cm${}^3$ and $10^9$ g/cm${}^3$ for even-even nuclei in the range $8 \leq Z \leq 82$, relevant for astrophysical nucleosynthesis mechanisms.

# Evolution of $\beta$-decay half-lives at finite-temperatures

## Key claim
The authors show that $\beta$-decay half-lives generally decrease with increasing temperature (up to T = 1.5 MeV) and increase with increasing density, but this temperature effect is considerably larger for nuclei with long half-lives at zero temperature while only slight changes occur for short-lived nuclei; the inclusion of de-excitation transitions (negative energy transitions) is essential for correctly capturing the temperature dependence.

## Method
The finite-temperature proton-neutron relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (FT-PNRQRPA) was applied on top of the finite-temperature Hartree-BCS theory, using the relativistic nuclear energy density functional with the D3C* parametrization (and DD-ME2 for comparison). Both allowed Gamow-Teller (GT) and first-forbidden (FF) transitions were included in the calculation of $\beta$-decay half-lives, and the physical strength function at finite temperature was obtained from the FT-PNRQRPA response function using a detailed balance relation that incorporates both positive-energy transitions and de-excitation (negative-energy) transitions.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: T = 0 to 1.5 MeV
- Stellar densities considered: $\rho Y_e = 10^7$ g/cm$^3$ and $10^9$ g/cm$^3$
- Large-scale calculations performed for even-even nuclei with $8 \leq Z \leq 82$ at temperatures $T_9 = 5$ K and $T_9 = 10$ K
- The neutron-proton mass difference used: $\Delta_{np} = 1.293$ MeV
- The superallowed $\beta$-decay constant: $K = 6144 \pm 2$ s
- Axial-vector coupling constant quenched to $g_A = -1.0$ (from free-nucleon value $g_A = -1.26$)
- Landau-Migdal term strength: $g^\prime = 0.76$ for D3C* interaction (adjusted to reproduce GT resonance in $^{208}$Pb); for Sn isotopes $g^\prime = 0.5$ was used
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 138.0$ MeV; pion-nucleon coupling: $f_\pi^2/(4\pi) = 0.08$
- De-excitation transitions become significant starting at T $\approx$ 0.3 MeV
- For $^{132}$Sn, FT-PNRQRPA predicts first significant temperature effect at T $\approx$ 0.4 MeV, while FT-RTBA calculations show visible effects from T $\approx$ 0.5 MeV

## Context
This work extends the finite-temperature QRPA approach previously applied to $N=82$ isotones using Skyrme-HF+BCS (PhysRevC.80.065808), which included only GT transitions and found half-life increases for some open-shell nuclei above T > 0.6 MeV. The present study is the first to perform large-scale calculations of $\beta$-decay half-lives at finite temperature in stellar environments using a self-consistent relativistic framework that includes both allowed and first-forbidden transitions. The results are compared with shell-model calculations for $pf$-shell nuclei (Langanke, 2000; Langanke, 2001) and with finite-temperature relativistic time-blocking approximation results for $^{132}$Sn (Litvinova, 2020). This work enables more realistic modeling of $\beta$-decay rates for astrophysical nucleosynthesis simulations in core-collapse supernovae and $r$-process environments where temperatures reach T = 1-10 GK and densities $\rho Y_e \geq 10^7$ g/cm$^3$.
