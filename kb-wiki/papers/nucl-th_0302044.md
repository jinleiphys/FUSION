# Nuclear absorption of Charmoniums in pA and AA collisions

> We have analysed the latest NA50 data on $J/ψ$ production in pA and AA collisions. The $J/ψ$ production is assumed to be a two step process, (i) formation of $c\bar{c}$ pairs, perturbatively calculable, and (ii) formation of $J/ψ$ from the pair, a non-perturbative process, which is conviniently parametrized. In a nuclear medium, as the $c\bar{c}$ pair passes through the nuclear medium, it gain relative square momentum and some of the pairs can gain enough square momentum to cross the threshold for open charm meson, leading to suppression in nuclear medium. Few parameters of the model were fixed from the latest high statistics NA50 pA and NA38 SU total $J/ψ$ cross sectional data. The model then reproduces the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ over Drell-Yan ration in 200 GeV/c S+U and 158 GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions. We also discuss the centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression at RHIC energy.

# Nuclear absorption of Charmoniums in pA and AA collisions

## Key claim
The QCD-based nuclear absorption model, where $J/\psi$ suppression arises from $c\bar{c}$ pairs gaining relative square momentum in the nuclear medium and crossing the open charm threshold, can consistently describe the centrality dependence of $J/\psi$ suppression in both S+U and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, provided that transverse energy fluctuations are included for the most central Pb+Pb events. The model parameters were re-fitted to the latest high-statistics NA50 pA data, yielding a 20% lower square momentum gain factor than earlier fits, indicating less absorption than previously thought.

## Method
The $J/\psi$ production cross section in AB collisions is factorized into a perturbatively calculable partonic subprocess for $c\bar{c}$ pair production and a non-perturbative transition probability $F_{c\bar{c} \rightarrow J/\psi}(q^2)$ parameterized as a function of the pair's relative square momentum $q^2$. In nuclear matter, the relative square momentum is increased by $\varepsilon^2 L$, where $\varepsilon^2$ is the square momentum gain per unit length and $L$ is the path length through the medium; pairs that gain enough square momentum cross the open charm threshold and are lost from the $J/\psi$ yield. The parameters $N_{norm} = K N_{J/\psi} / \sigma^{DY}_{NN}$ and $\varepsilon^2$ were fixed by fitting the total $J/\psi$ to Drell-Yan cross section ratio as a function of nuclear length from NA50 pA and NA38 SU data, with $\alpha_F$ fixed to 1.0. The centrality dependence of the $J/\psi$/DY ratio was then computed by averaging over the nuclear geometry and, for Pb+Pb, incorporating $E_T$ fluctuations via the replacement $L(b,s) \rightarrow L(b,s) E_T / \langle E_T \rangle(b)$.

## Key numbers
- Square momentum gain factor per unit length: $\varepsilon^2 = 0.1875$ GeV$^2$/fm, obtained from a fit to the latest NA50 pA and NA38 SU total $J/\psi$ cross section data; this is 20% lower than the earlier value of 0.225 GeV$^2$/fm from Chaudhuri (2002).
- Normalization parameter $N_{norm} = 10.18$ at 200 GeV/c and $N_{norm} = 4.43$ at 450 GeV/c; their ratio of 2.29 is consistent with the Craigie parameterization of Drell-Yan cross sections giving a ratio of 2.1–3.1.
- The $J/\psi$-nucleon absorption cross section from the latest NA50 450 GeV pA data is $\sigma^{J/\psi N}_{abs} = 4.4 \pm 1.0$ mb, compared to the earlier value of $6.4 \pm 0.8$ mb from NA50 (1997).
- A common $\sigma^{J/\psi N}_{abs} = 4.4 \pm 0.5$ mb was estimated from combined pA and NA38 200 GeV/c S+U data.

## Context
This work extends the QCD-based nuclear absorption model of Qiu, Vary and Zhang (2002) and the author's own earlier analysis (Chaudhuri, 2002) to the latest NA50 data from the 2000 Pb+Pb run and the high-statistics 450 GeV pA data presented at Quark Matter 2002. The earlier Glauber model fits to S+U data required a larger absorption cross section (7.1 mb) than the pA data now indicate (4.4 mb), creating an inconsistency that the QCD-based model resolves. The model enables a consistent description of $J/\psi$ suppression across pA, S+U and Pb+Pb systems at SPS energies without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, though the author notes that the centrality dependence in Pb+Pb remains anomalous in the Glauber framework. The analysis sets the stage for predictions of $J/\psi$ suppression at RHIC energies, which are discussed qualitatively.
