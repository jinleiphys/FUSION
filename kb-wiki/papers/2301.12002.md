# New isomeric transition in $^{36}$Mg: Bridging the N=20 and N=28 islands of inversion

> We observed a new isomeric gamma transition at 168 keV in $^{36}$Mg, with a half-life of T$_{1/2}$=[130-500]$(\pm40)(^{+800}_{-20})_{sys}$ ns. We propose that the observed transition de-excites a new 0$^+$ isomeric state and populates the previously known first 2$^+$ state. The existence of this isomer is consistent with the predictions of the large-scale shell model calculations of $^{36}$Mg using the sdpf-u-mix interaction. The observed excitation energy of the second 0$^+$ state is caused by the small energy separation between two prolate-deformed configurations where the intruder configuration corresponds to two neutron excitations from the {\it sd} to the {\it pf} shell. Within this interpretation, $^{36}$Mg becomes the crossing point between nuclei in which ground state deformed/superdeformed configurations are caused by the dominance of N=20 intruders ($^{32,34}$Mg) and nuclei where deformed configurations are associated with N=28 intruders ($^{38}$Mg and beyond). We found the lack of three-body monopole corrections in other effective interactions results in a predominance of N=20 intruder configurations past $^{38}$Mg incompatible with our observation. We conclude that $^{36}$Mg bridges the N=20 and N=28 islands of inversion, forming the so-called Big Island of Deformation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a new isomeric 0⁺ state in ³⁶Mg, decaying via a 168 keV gamma transition, bridges the N=20 and N=28 islands of deformation, forming a "Big Island of Deformation." The claim is hedged by the fact that the spin-parity assignment of 0⁺ is inferred indirectly (not directly measured) and relies on the absence of faster decay modes and the observed half-life.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (NSCL) using a ¹⁴⁰ MeV/u ⁴⁸Ca beam fragmented on a Be target. The resulting cocktail beam was implanted in a YSO detector, and delayed gamma-ray events between 40 ns and 500 ns after implantation were correlated with particle identification (time-of-flight and energy loss) to identify isomeric transitions in ³⁶Mg. The half-life was extracted via a log-likelihood fit of the time distribution of 168 keV gamma events, accounting for background from the implantation flash and systematic uncertainties from the transit time in the A1900 separator.

## Key numbers
- New isomeric gamma transition at 168 keV in ³⁶Mg.
- Half-life of the isomer: T₁/₂ = 90(⁺⁴¹⁰₋₅₀)ₛₜₐₜ (±40)ₜᵣₐₙ (⁺⁸⁰⁰₋₇₀)ₛᵧₛ ns.
- Proposed excitation energy of the new 0⁺ state: 833 keV, decaying to the known 2⁺ state at 665 keV.
- Reduced transition strength: B(E2) = 70(⁺⁸⁰₋₆₀)ₛₜₐₜ (⁺³⁰₋₄₀)ₜᵣₐₙ (⁺²¹⁰₋₆₀)ₛᵧₛ e²fm⁴.
- Shell model calculation (sdpf-u-mix) predicts the 0⁺₂ state at 1.02 MeV, with a B(E2) of 5 e²fm⁴ to the 2⁺₁ state.

## Context
This work extends the study of shape isomers in neutron-rich magnesium isotopes, following the observation of a 0⁺ isomer in ³²Mg by Wimmer (2010) and Elder (2019). It contradicts predictions from the EEdf1 interaction (Tsunoda, 2020), which places the 0⁺₂ state at 2.32 MeV with a negligible B(E2), incompatible with the observed isomer. The result supports the sdpf-u-mix interaction (Caurier, 2014) and its three-body monopole correction, which restores the N=20 shell closure beyond ³⁶Mg. The finding enables future searches for isomers in ³⁴Mg and ³⁸Mg at FRIB or RIKEN to test the predicted crossing point between the N=20 and N=28 islands of inversion.
