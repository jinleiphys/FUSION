# Unified Description of Nuclear Stopping in Central Heavy-ion Collision from 10$A$ MeV to 1.2$A$ GeV

> The detailed analysis of wide excitation function of nuclear stopping has been studied within a transport model, Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (IQMD) and an overall good agreement with the INDRA and FOPI experimental data has been achieved. It is found that mean value of isotropy in central Heavy-Ion Collision (HIC) reaches a minimum near Fermi energy and approaches a maximum around 300 - 400$A$ MeV. This suggests that, in statistical average, the equilibration is far from being reached even in central HIC especially near Fermi energy. A hierarchy in stopping of fragments, which favors heavy fragments to penetrate, provides a robust restriction on the global trend of nuclear stopping and could serve as a probe for nuclear equation of state.

# Unified Description of Nuclear Stopping in Central Heavy-ion Collision from 10$A$ MeV to 1.2$A$ GeV

## Key claim

The authors achieve an overall good agreement with INDRA and FOPI experimental data for nuclear stopping across a wide energy range using the IQMD transport model, but only after accounting for two previously overlooked ingredients: the difference between nucleon phase space and fragment phase space, and the mixing of impact parameters due to centrality selection criteria. The mean isotropy ratio reaches a minimum near Fermi energy and a maximum around 300-400$A$ MeV, indicating that equilibration is far from being reached even in central collisions, especially near Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors performed IQMD (Isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics) simulations for central collisions of $^{129}$Xe + $^{120}$Sn and $^{58}$Ni + $^{58}$Ni systems across incident energies from 10$A$ MeV to 1.2$A$ GeV. They calculated nuclear stopping using the energy-based isotropy ratio $R_E$ and the momentum-based ratio $R_p$, comparing results from nucleon phase space versus fragment phase space, and applying impact parameter mixing to match experimental centrality selection criteria. The stopping was evaluated for four different equation of state (EOS) parameter sets: soft (S), hard (H), soft with momentum-dependent interaction (SM), and hard with momentum-dependent interaction (HM).

## Key numbers

- Minimum stopping value of 0.70 at about 55$A$ MeV in IQMD fragment phase space, compared with experimental value of 0.68 at 40$A$ MeV for the Xe+Sn system
- Mean stopping value $R_E$ around 0.6-0.7 for exact central collision ($b_{red}=0$) at 50$A$ MeV, dropping to about 0.56 with width 0.42 when centrality cut $N_{ch}=44$ is applied (comparable to experimental mean 0.56 with width 0.47)
- Soft EOS (compressibility $\kappa$ = 200 MeV) and hard EOS ($\kappa$ = 380 MeV) parameter sets used, with Skyrme parameters: S ($\alpha$=-356 MeV, $\beta$=303 MeV, $\gamma$=1.17), H ($\alpha$=-124 MeV, $\beta$=71 MeV, $\gamma$=2.00)
- Momentum-dependent interaction parameters: $\delta$ = 1.57 MeV, $\epsilon$ = 500 (GeV/c)$^{-2}$
- Fragment recognition criteria: $\Delta r$ = 3.5 fm and $\Delta p$ = 300 MeV/c between nucleons
- Gaussian wave-packet width $L$ = 2.16 fm$^2$ for Xe+Sn system
- Symmetry potential parameter $t_6$ = 100 MeV, nuclear saturation density $\rho_0$ = 0.16 fm$^{-3}$
- Freeze-out time adopted as 200 fm/c, extended to 300 fm/c for lower energies

## Context

This work extends the earlier IQMD simulations by Liu et al. (2001), which overpredicted nuclear stopping at low energies by using nucleon phase space rather than fragment phase space. It directly addresses the discrepancy noted by the INDRA and ALADIN Collaborations (Lehaut et al., 2010), who observed a striking minimal stopping at 40$A$ MeV for the Xe+Sn system. The results are compared with experimental data from INDRA (Lehaut et al., 2010) at lower energies and FOPI (Reisdorf et al., 2004, 2010; Andronic et al., 2006) at higher energies. The finding that soft EOS is favored near Fermi energy is consistent with conclusions from kaon spectrum analysis at higher energy by Hartnack et al. (2006) and Sturm et al. (2001). The atomic-number-dependent stopping hierarchy provides a new probe for constraining the nuclear equation of state and could enable future experimental measurements near Fermi energy to further discriminate between EOS parameter sets.
