# Strong coupling series for QCD at finite temperature and density

> We discuss the use of strong coupling expansions for Yang-Mills theory and QCD at finite temperature and density. In particular we consider the onset of temperature effects for the free energy and screening masses, derive the hadron resonance gas model from first principles and compute the weakening of the deconfinement transition with chemical potential.

## Key claim
Strong coupling expansions on the lattice provide a first-principles analytic derivation of the hadron resonance gas model for QCD at finite temperature and density, and show that the deconfinement transition weakens with real chemical potential, but only within the radius of convergence of the series which is bounded by the critical quark mass where the first-order transition ends.

## Method
The authors perform a character expansion of the SU(N) Wilson action and a hopping-parameter expansion for Wilson fermions, then evaluate the resulting cluster and polymer graphs on lattices with compactified temporal extent N_t. The free energy density is obtained by subtracting the infinite-volume vacuum contribution, and screening masses are extracted from spatial correlation functions of plaquettes. The Polyakov loop susceptibility is expanded as a double series in the fundamental character coefficient u and the hopping parameter h, and critical parameters are extracted via Padé approximants to the Dlog of the series.

## Key numbers
- Leading-order pressure for Yang-Mills: P^{LO}(N_t,u) = (6/N_t) u^{4N_t}
- Leading-order screening mass shift for Yang-Mills: Δm^{LO}(N_t,u) = -(2/3) N_t u^{4N_t-6}
- Critical quark mass for N_f=1 on N_t=1 lattice: m_c/T = 2.08(7)
- Critical quark mass for N_f=2 on N_t=1 lattice: m_c/T = 2.78(7)
- Critical quark mass for N_f=3 on N_t=1 lattice: m_c/T = 3.17(10)
- Critical exponent from Polyakov loop susceptibility: λ = 1.03, consistent with 3d Ising universality (γ = 1.237)

## Context
This work extends the strong coupling expansion techniques previously applied to SU(2) and SU(3) pure gauge theory (Langelage, Philipsen 2010; sc3) to include dynamical quarks via the hopping expansion. It derives the hadron resonance gas model from QCD first principles, showing it emerges as an effective theory in the strong coupling regime, analogous to the glueball gas representation of Yang-Mills theory (sc1, sc3). The result that the deconfinement transition weakens with real chemical potential agrees with numerical findings from the Potts model (potts). The approach enables analytic study of the confined phase at finite baryon density and provides a systematic framework to connect QCD with effective models used for nuclear matter (scl1, scl2).
