# Observation of a dynamic magneto-chiral instability in photoexcited tellurium

> In a system of charged chiral fermions driven out of equilibrium, an electric current parallel to the magnetic field can generate a dynamic instability by which electromagnetic waves become amplified. Whether a similar instability can occur in chiral solid-state systems remains an open question. Using time-domain terahertz (THz) emission spectroscopy, we detect signatures of what we dub a ``dynamic magneto-chiral instability" in elemental tellurium, a structurally chiral crystal. Upon transient photoexcitation in a moderate external magnetic field, tellurium emits THz radiation consisting of coherent modes that amplify over time. An explanation for this amplification is proposed using a theoretical model based on a dynamic instability of electromagnetic waves interacting with infrared-active oscillators of impurity acceptor states in tellurium to form an amplifying polariton. Our work not only uncovers the presence of a magneto-chiral instability but also highlights its promise for THz-wave amplification in chiral materials.

## Key claim

The paper reports signatures of a "dynamic magneto-chiral instability" in photoexcited tellurium, where coherent terahertz (THz) modes amplify over time rather than decay. The amplification is attributed to a polaritonic coupling between THz light and infrared-active oscillators of impurity acceptor states, mediated by a far-from-equilibrium magneto-chiral current, rather than to the chiral magnetic effect from Weyl fermions.

## Method

Time-domain THz emission spectroscopy was performed on single-crystal elemental tellurium (chiral space group P3₁21) after photoexcitation with a 1.2 eV near-infrared pulse, in external magnetic fields up to ±6 T and at temperatures from 7 K to 17 K. The emitted THz electric field polarized parallel to the magnetic field was measured via electro-optic sampling, and the magnetic-field-antisymmetrized signal was analyzed using a non-iterative linear prediction algorithm that decomposes the time-domain data into sums of harmonic oscillators with exponential growth or decay factors. A theoretical model was constructed by inserting a frequency-dependent magneto-chiral conductivity and an IR-active oscillator response into Maxwell's equations to compute the polariton dispersion relations.

## Key numbers

- Pump photon energy: 1.2 eV; tellurium band gap: 0.32 eV
- External magnetic fields: ±6 T (with field dependence studied from 0 T to 6 T)
- Temperature range: 7 K to 17 K
- Observed amplified mode frequencies: 0.37 THz (for B₀ ∥ c), 0.32 THz and 0.39 THz (for B₀ ⟂ c)
- Amplification rates β extracted from fits: on the order of 10⁻² ps⁻¹, consistent with the model's Im[ω(k)] ≈ 10⁻² ps⁻¹ for γ = 0
- Model parameters: σ_E0 = 50 Ω⁻¹ m⁻¹, ω₀ = 1 ps⁻¹, n = 10¹⁶ cm⁻³, μ_DC = 0.5 eV, μ_AC = −0.5 eV, τ = 1 ps, ε_r = 30
- Laser penetration depth in tellurium: 300 nm
- The radiative instability vanishes when γ exceeds 3 × 10⁻² ps⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the concept of the chiral plasma instability, previously studied in quark-gluon plasmas (Fukushima, 2008; Akamatsu and Yamamoto, 2013) and predicted in Weyl semimetals (Hosur and Qi, 2015), to structurally chiral crystals without requiring Weyl fermions. The observed amplification is distinct from conventional photo-Dember effects (Dekorsy, 1993) and from Hall-current or surface-dipole rotation mechanisms (Johnston, 2002; Shan, 2001), which would only produce emission perpendicular to the magnetic field. The results open the possibility of THz-wave amplification in chiral materials and call for future time-resolved ARPES and DFT studies to clarify the microscopic origin of the non-equilibrium carrier chirality.
