# Anomalous transport effects and possible environmental symmetry "violation" in heavy ion collisions

> The heavy ion collision provides a unique many-body environment where local domains of strongly interacting chiral medium may occur and in a sense allow environmental symmetry "violation" phenomena. For example certain anomalous transport processes, forbidden in usual medium, become possible in such domains. We briefly review recent progress in both the theoretical understanding and experimental search of various anomalous transport effects (such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect, Chiral Separation Effect, Chiral Electric Separation Effect, Chiral Electric/Magnetic Waves, etc) in the hot QCD fluid created by such collisions.

# Anomalous transport effects and possible environmental symmetry "violation" in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Heavy ion collisions may create local domains of hot QCD matter with net chirality, where anomalous transport processes (such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect, Chiral Separation Effect, and Chiral Electric Separation Effect) that are forbidden in normal matter become possible, but these effects are inherently environmental and fluctuate on an event-by-event basis rather than representing fundamental symmetry violation.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical developments in anomalous transport effects in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, focusing on linear response of the chiral medium to external electromagnetic fields. It discusses the derivation of anomalous transport coefficients from the axial anomaly, the construction of coupled current-response matrices, and the identification of collective modes (chiral magnetic waves and chiral electric waves) from linearized evolution equations for vector and axial density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The topological winding number $Q_w$ takes integer values (e.g., $+1$ for one instanton, $-1$ for one anti-instanton) and is defined via $Q_w = \frac{g^2}{32\pi^2} \int d^4 x \, F^{a}_{\mu \nu} \tilde{F}_{a}^{\mu \nu}$
- The neutron electric dipole moment measurement suggests an upper limit $|\theta| < 10^{-11}$ for the QCD theta parameter
- The chiral conductivity $\sigma_5 \equiv N_c e / (2\pi^2)$ appears universally in both the Chiral Magnetic Effect ($\vec j_V = \sigma_5 \mu_A \vec B$) and the Chiral Separation Effect ($\vec j_A = \sigma_5 \mu_V \vec B$)
- The CESE conductivity for thermal QED plasma is $\sigma_{\chi e}^{QED} \approx 20.499 (\mu_V \mu_A / T^2) (T / [e^3 \ln(1/e)])$
- The CESE conductivity for QGP with $u,d$ light flavors at leading-log accuracy is $\sigma_{\chi e}^{QGP} = 14.5163 \, T \, (\text{Tr}_f[Q_e Q_A] / [g^4 \ln(1/g)]) (\mu_V \mu_A / T^2)$
- The strong-coupling holographic result gives $\sigma_{\chi e} \simeq T \times 0.025 \times (8 N_c \lambda / 81) (\mu_V \mu_A / T^2)$
- The chiral magnetic wave speed is $v_\chi = \sigma_5 \sqrt{\alpha_V \alpha_A} B$, and the chiral electric wave speed is $v_e = \alpha_A n_A \sqrt{2\sigma_2 \chi_e \alpha_V \alpha_A} E$
- The electromagnetic field strengths in heavy ion collisions are estimated as $|e\vec E|, |e\vec B| \sim \alpha_{EM} \gamma Z / R_A^2 \sim m_\pi^2 \sim 10^{17}$ Gauss
- For perfectly central collisions at $b=0$, the average magnetic field strength is $\langle (eB)^2 \rangle \approx 4 m_\pi^4$

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework developed by Kharzeev (2004) on the Chiral Magnetic Effect and subsequent work by Son and Zhitnitsky (2004) on the Chiral Separation Effect, as well as the Chiral Magnetic Wave proposed by Kharzeev and Yee (2010). It incorporates the recently discovered Chiral Electric Separation Effect by Huang and Liao (2013) and completes the transport response matrix for chiral media. The paper enables experimental searches for anomalous transport signatures in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, particularly through charge-dependent azimuthal correlations and elliptic flow measurements, and connects to broader questions about topological objects in QCD, the strong CP problem, and axion physics.
