# Momentum Sum Rule Is Violated in The Operator Product Expansion in QCD At The High Energy Colliders

> To prove the momentum sum rule in the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD at high energy colliders it is assumed that $<P| {\hat T}^{++}(0)|P>=2(P^+)^2$ where $|P>$ is the momentum eigenstate of the hadron $H$ with momentum $P^μ$ and ${\hat T}^{++}(0)$ is the $++$ component of the gauge invariant color singlet energy-momentum tensor density operator ${\hat T}^{μν}(0)$ of all the quarks plus antiquarks plus gluons inside the hadron $H$. However, in this paper, we show that this relation $<P| {\hat T}^{++}(0)|P>=2(P^+)^2$ is correct if ${\hat T}^{μν}(0)$ is the energy-momentum tensor density operator of the hadron but this relation $<P| {\hat T}^{++}(0)|P>=2(P^+)^2$ is not correct if ${\hat T}^{μν}(0)$ is the gauge invariant color singlet energy-momentum tensor density operator of all the quarks plus antiquarks plus gluons inside the hadron. Hence we find that the momentum sum rule is violated in the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD at high energy colliders.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard proof of the momentum sum rule in the operator product expansion (OPE) in QCD relies on the assumption that the hadronic matrix element of the ++ component of the gauge-invariant color-singlet energy-momentum tensor density operator of all partons equals \(2(P^+)^2\); the author claims this equality is incorrect when the operator is the partonic one, leading to violation of the sum rule.

## Method

The author compares the hadronic matrix element of the energy-momentum tensor density operator for the hadron itself with that for the gauge-invariant color-singlet operator of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons separately. Using the gauge-invariant Noether theorem in QCD, the author derives that the partonic momentum operator is not conserved due to a non-vanishing boundary surface term at large distance, which arises from confinement. This non-conservation is then used to show that the matrix element \(\langle P | \hat{T}^{++}(0) | P \rangle\) for the partonic operator does not equal \(2(P^+)^2\), contradicting the assumption needed for the momentum sum rule in OPE.

## Key numbers

* The momentum sum rule in OPE would require \(\sum_i \int_0^1 d\xi \, \xi \, f_{i/H}(\xi) = 1\), but the author finds \(\sum_i \int_0^1 d\xi \, \xi \, f_{i/H}(\xi) = \frac{1}{2(P^+)^2} \langle P | T^{++}(0) | P \rangle \neq 1\), where \(f_{i/H}(\xi)\) is the parton distribution function and \(\xi\) the longitudinal momentum fraction.
* The hadronic matrix element for the hadron operator satisfies \(\langle P | \hat{T}^{++}(0) | P \rangle = 2(P^+)^2\) (using the normalization \(\langle P' | P \rangle = 2P^+ \delta(P'^+ - P^+) \delta^{(2)}(\mathbf{P}'_T - \mathbf{P}_T)\)), but for the partonic operator the author argues \(\langle P | \hat{T}^{++}(0) | P \rangle \neq 2(P^+)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior finding (Nayak, 2018, cited as nkms) that the momentum sum rule in QCD is violated due to a non-vanishing boundary surface term at large distance from confinement, contradicting the standard proof in the operator product expansion given by Collins and Soper (1982, cited as jnc). The result challenges the foundation of parton distribution function extractions used in sets such as CTEQ, GRV, and MRST, which rely on the momentum sum rule to determine gluon distributions from measured quark distributions. The paper suggests that the correct momentum sum rule is given by equation (5) of Nayak (2018, nkms), and that the OPE-based proof is an assumption rather than a first-principles derivation.
