# Heavy mesons in a hadronic medium: interaction and transport coefficients

> We review the recent results of heavy meson diffusion in thermal hadronic matter. The interactions of D and B-bar mesons with other hadrons (light mesons and baryons) are extracted from effective field theories based on chiral and heavy-quark symmetries. When these guiding principles are combined with exact unitarity, physical values of the cross sections are obtained. These cross sections (which contain resonant contributions) are used to calculate the drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy mesons immersed in a thermal and dense medium. The transport coefficients are computed using a Fokker-Planck reduction of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the authors' own recent calculations of heavy meson transport coefficients in hadronic matter, claiming that the hadronic interaction contributes to the increase of the elliptic flow \(v_2\) and brings the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) closer to experimental results from RHIC, but only under the condition that the effective field theory interactions are combined with exact unitarization to produce physical cross sections containing resonant contributions.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians for \(D\) and \(\bar{B}\) mesons interacting with light mesons (\(\pi, K, \bar{K}, \eta\)) and baryons (\(N, \Delta\)) using chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries, with meson-meson interactions expanded to next-to-leading order in chiral counting and meson-baryon interactions kept at leading order in a Weinberg-Tomozawa contact vertex. They then unitarize the scattering amplitudes by solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation in a coupled-channel basis, and use the resulting cross sections (which contain resonances such as the \(D_0(2400)\) and bound states such as the \(D_{s0}^{*}(2317)\)) to compute drag and diffusion coefficients via a Fokker-Planck reduction of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time \(\tau_R = 1/F\) for \(\bar{B}\) mesons is approximately \(m_B/m_D \sim 3\) times larger than that for \(D\) mesons, based on the inverse-mass scaling of the drag force.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient \(D_x\) is approximately equal for \(D\) and \(\bar{B}\) mesons, as it does not carry an explicit dependence on the heavy-meson mass.
- Transport coefficients are computed for heavy-meson momentum of \(100\) MeV as functions of temperature and entropy per baryon.
- In a dynamical quasiparticle model simulation, the hadronic interaction increases \(v_2\) up to a factor of 2 and shifts the peak of \(R_{AA}\) to higher momenta.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of heavy-meson transport coefficients (Tolos 2013, Torres-Rincon 2014) by reviewing the full framework and comparing with results from the dynamical quasiparticle model for quark-gluon plasma (Berrehrah 2014) and lattice-QCD calculations around the transition temperature. The results have been applied to 2+1D Monte Carlo simulations of an expanding plasma (Ozvenchuk 2014) and dynamical quasiparticle model simulations (Song 2015), showing that hadronic interactions produce nonzero effects on \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) that bring them closer to experimental data from RHIC, and are expected to be more important for lower-energy collisions at future facilities such as FAIR and NICA.
