# Revisiting the extraction of charge radii of $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb with muonic atom spectroscopy

> The extractions of nuclear charge radii from muonic atom spectroscopy for $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb are revisited to analyze the model dependencies induced by employing a Fermi-type charge distribution. For that, the charge densities, together with the corresponding muonic transition energies, calculated by the covariant density functional theory are used as a benchmark. The root-mean-square deviation of transition energies is calculated to quantitatively investigate the sensitivities of transition energies to the details of the two-parameter Fermi distribution. It is found that the second and fourth moments of the charge distribution can be extracted accurately from the muonic atom spectroscopy without much model dependencies, whereas the obtained two-parameter Fermi distributions cannot reproduce the details of the benchmarking charge densities and, in particular, its surface-diffuseness parameter cannot be determined accurately with the present experimental uncertainties on the muonic transition energies.

## Key claim

The extraction of nuclear charge radii from muonic atom spectroscopy using a two-parameter Fermi (2pf) distribution is largely free of model dependency for the second and fourth moments of the charge distribution, but the surface-diffuseness parameter of the 2pf distribution cannot be determined accurately given current experimental uncertainties on muonic transition energies.

## Method

The authors used charge densities from relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory with the PC-PK1 density functional as a benchmark for 40Ca and 208Pb. They fitted 2pf charge distributions to the RCHB densities under various constraints (with and without fixing the second and fourth moments), then solved the Dirac equation for muonic atoms using the generalized pseudospectral method to compute transition energies. The root-mean-square deviation (RMSD) between transition energies from the 2pf distributions and those from the RCHB benchmark was used to quantify model dependencies and to study how uncertainties in transition energies propagate to uncertainties in extracted charge radii.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca, the best-fit 2pf distribution (minimizing RMSD) gave t = 2.2950 fm, c = 3.7214 fm, with minimum RMSD delta = 2.0 x 10^-5 keV
- For 208Pb, the best-fit 2pf distribution gave t = 2.1821 fm, c = 6.6977 fm, with minimum RMSD delta = 0.0031 keV
- The relative deviation in extracted charge radii between the best-fit 2pf and the RCHB benchmark was approximately 0.01% for 40Ca and 0.02% for 208Pb
- To achieve an uncertainty of +/- 0.01 fm in charge radius, the total uncertainty in transition energies would need to be less than 0.0017 keV for 40Ca and less than 1 keV for 208Pb
- The relative deviations of the fourth moment at the minimum RMSD were around 0.2% for 40Ca and 0.3% for 208Pb

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to extract nuclear charge radii from muonic atom X-ray spectroscopy, which began with Fitch and Rainwater (1953). It addresses the model dependency concern raised by earlier studies (e.g., PhysRevC.37.2821) regarding the use of Fermi-type charge distributions. The authors use state-of-the-art covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations as a benchmark, building on the RCHB method developed by Meng and Ring (1996). The results enable more reliable extraction of not only charge radii but also the fourth moment of the charge distribution from muonic atom data, which has applications for studying nuclear surface properties and neutron-skin thickness as explored by Reinhard and Nazarewicz (2021) and others.
