# Test of $T$-invariance in scattering of polarized protons on tensor-polarized deuterons at energies of the NICA SPD

> The effect of violation of $T$-invariance, provided that $P$-parity is preserved, is given by the total cross section of the interaction of a vector-polarized particle with a tensor-polarized target. A formalism for calculating this effect developed previously and based on the spin-dependent Glauber theory of elastic $pd$ scattering is used here to calculate the effect under discussion in the range of collision energies corresponding to the invariant mass of the $pN$ system $\sqrt{s_{pN}}=5$--$30$ GeV. The spin-dependent amplitudes of elastic $pN$ scattering required for this calculation are taken from existing phenomenological models for $pN$ scattering in the energy region considered.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the energy dependence of the time-reversal-invariance-violating parity-conserving (TVPC) signal in polarized proton–deuteron scattering, finding that the magnitude of the effect decreases with increasing collision energy, but the rate of decrease depends significantly on which phenomenological model of spin-dependent proton–nucleon amplitudes is used.

## Method

The authors use the spin-dependent Glauber theory of elastic proton–deuteron scattering, previously developed by the same group, to compute the TVPC signal given by the total cross section of a vector-polarized proton on a tensor-polarized deuteron. The required spin-dependent amplitudes for elastic proton–nucleon scattering are taken from two existing phenomenological models: a Regge parametrization (Sibirtsev 2010) covering laboratory momenta 3–50 GeV/c, and the HEGS Regge-eikonal model (Selyugin 2021) covering center-of-mass energies from 3.6 to 10 TeV. The TVPC amplitude is dominated by double-scattering mechanisms in the Glauber framework, and the calculation uses the deuteron form factors including S- and D-wave components.

## Key numbers

* The TVPC signal is calculated for invariant masses of the proton–nucleon system in the range \(\sqrt{s_{pN}} = 5\)–30 GeV, corresponding to the NICA SPD experiment.
* Using the SAID database at the maximum kinetic energy for proton–neutron scattering of 1.2 GeV (\(\sqrt{s_{pn}} = 2.4\) GeV), the TVPC signal in proton–deuteron scattering is about 7 times higher than its maximum value at \(\sqrt{s_{pp}} = 3\) GeV from the Sibirtsev (2010) parametrization.
* The Regge parametrization (Sibirtsev 2010) is valid for laboratory proton momenta \(p_l = 3\)–50 GeV/c, corresponding to \(\sqrt{s_{pp}} = 2.8\)–10 GeV.
* The HEGS model describes data for \(\sqrt{s}\) from 3.6 GeV to 10 TeV.
* The \(h_1(1170)\) meson parameters used are: mass \(m_h = 1.17\) GeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 1\) GeV, and coupling constant \(G_h = 4\pi \times 1.56\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism for TVPC signals in proton–deuteron scattering (Uzikov 2015, 2016, 2017) to the energy range of the planned NICA SPD experiment at Dubna. The TVPC interaction, which preserves P-parity, is a less-studied source of CP violation that could help explain the observed baryon asymmetry of the universe, complementary to electric dipole moment searches (Vergeles 2022) and CP violation in the kaon and B-meson systems (Riotto 1999, Bernreuther 2002). The results enable experimental planning for the NICA SPD facility and show that the energy dependence of the signal is model-dependent, motivating further measurements to constrain the underlying spin-dependent amplitudes.
