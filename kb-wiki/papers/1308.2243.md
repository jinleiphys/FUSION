# Restoration of Chiral Symmetry from a Boundary

> The imposition of Dirichlet boundary conditions in lattice computations obstructs the formation of a chiral condensate. We use chiral perturbation theory and meson models to address the effect of a Dirichlet boundary on chiral symmetry breaking. While pions are the longest-range modes in QCD, the restoration of chiral symmetry due to a boundary is shown not to depend upon the pion Compton wavelength but rather on that of the sigma meson. Power-law finite size corrections are exposed, and require prohibitively large lattices to overcome. We further speculate on the frustration of the chiral condensate for the case of confinement to the surface of a sphere.

# Restoration of Chiral Symmetry from a Boundary

## Key claim

The restoration of chiral symmetry induced by a Dirichlet boundary is controlled not by the pion Compton wavelength (the longest-range mode in QCD) but by the sigma meson Compton wavelength, and the resulting finite-size corrections to the chiral condensate are power-law in nature rather than exponential, requiring prohibitively large lattices to overcome.

## Method

The author uses chiral perturbation theory and the linear sigma model to study the effect of Dirichlet boundary conditions on the chiral condensate in QCD with two light quark flavors. The vacuum expectation value of the sigma field is determined by minimizing the Euclidean action subject to fixed endpoints where the sigma field vanishes at the boundaries, and the resulting condensate profile is solved analytically using elliptic integrals. For the spherical bag geometry, the problem is mapped onto a classical dynamics equation and solved numerically.

## Key numbers

- The maximum value of the condensate at the midpoint of the lattice approaches the infinite volume value as: ⟨ψ̅ψ(L/2)⟩ / ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ = 1 − 4 exp(−½ m_σ L)
- The volume-averaged condensate approaches infinite volume as a power law: ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ / ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ = 1 − (4 log 2)/(m_σ L)
- A non-zero condensate can form in the bulk provided L ≳ 2 fm
- The sigma meson mass is given by m_σ² = 8Λv², where Λ is the quartic coupling and v is the chiral-limit value of the pion decay constant
- The pion mass satisfies the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation: m_π² = λ m_q / v²

## Context

This work extends the treatment of chiral symmetry restoration on compact manifolds without boundaries, as developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1986) for finite temperature and finite volume with periodic boundary conditions. The study is motivated by practical lattice QCD applications including temporal Dirichlet boundary conditions for propagator inversion (Edwards, 2005), magnetic field simulations (Fiebig, 1988), rotating frames (Yamamoto, 2013), and the Schrödinger functional scheme (Sint, 1993). The results enable quantitative estimates of finite-size effects for lattice calculations using Dirichlet boundary conditions and suggest that bag-model confinement and chiral symmetry breaking may be incompatible within the sigma model.
