# New Elementary Operator for Kaon Photoproduction on the Nucleon and Nuclei

> A new elementary operator for kaon photoproduction on the nucleon and nuclei has been developed within a Feynman diagrammatic framework. By fitting the unknown coupling strengths at the electromagnetic and hadronic vertices of the baryon resonances to all available experimental data across the six isospin channels, the model achieves excellent agreement with the data. The operator includes 26 nucleon resonances in the $KΛ$ channels and 17 additional $Δ$ resonances in the $KΣ$ channels. For applications to nuclear reactions, such as hypernuclear photoproduction, the operator is formulated in Pauli space, allowing a straightforward implementation of the nonrelativistic approximation. Several alternative forms for expressing the operator output are proposed. In one of them, the spin operators and photon polarization vectors are separated from the operator, since both are frame dependent, thereby enhancing its versatility in nuclear applications.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new elementary operator for kaon photoproduction that achieves excellent agreement with experimental data across all six isospin channels, but the authors acknowledge that the model's validity is energy-dependent and varies by channel: it is reliable from threshold up to approximately 2.8 GeV for the K+Λ, K0Λ, and K+Σ0 channels, but only up to 2.1 GeV for K0Σ+, 2.2 GeV for K+Σ-, and 1.9 GeV for K0Σ0.

## Method

The authors constructed a Feynman diagrammatic isobar model including Born terms and resonance contributions, with gauge invariance restored via the Haberzettl prescription and higher-spin resonances treated using the Pascalutsa consistent interaction formalism. The unknown coupling strengths at electromagnetic and hadronic vertices were determined by fitting to all available experimental data across the six isospin channels, with the operator then reformulated in Pauli space for nuclear applications. The final operator is expressed in multiple alternative forms, including a 4×3 matrix representation that separates spin operators and photon polarization vectors to enhance versatility in nuclear calculations.

## Key numbers

- The operator includes 26 nucleon resonances in the KΛ channels and 17 additional Δ resonances in the KΣ channels
- Model A for KΛ channels used 23 nucleon resonances fitted to 9364 experimental data points, yielding χ²/N_dof = 1.46
- Model B for KΛ channels used 21 nucleon resonances with the same database, yielding χ²/N_dof = 1.52
- Model C for KΣ channels used the same nucleon resonances as Model A plus 17 Δ resonances, fitted to approximately 8000 data points, yielding χ²/N_dof = 1.18
- Model D for KΣ channels used 21 nucleon and 14 Δ resonances, yielding χ²/N_dof = 1.22
- Threshold energies range from 911.1 MeV (γp → K+Λ) to 1052.1 MeV (γn → K+Σ-) in photon laboratory energy
- Two distinct peaks are consistently observed in the γp → K+Λ differential cross section at W ≈ 1.650 GeV and 1.900 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Kaon-Maid model (Mart 1999, Bennhold 1999) by incorporating significantly more resonances and fitting to a much larger experimental database, achieving substantial improvements over the earlier model which was purely predictive for neutron channels. The operator enables hypernuclear photoproduction calculations on deuteron, 3He, and heavier nuclei, following earlier approaches such as Mart (1996) for hypertriton photoproduction and Yamamura (1999) for threshold phenomena in kaon photoproduction on the deuteron.
