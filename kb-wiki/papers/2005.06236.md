# Renormalization group and scattering-equivalent Hamiltonians on a coarse momentum grid

> We consider the $ππ$-scattering problem in the context of the Kadyshevsky equation. In this scheme, we introduce a momentum grid and provide an isospectral definition of the phase-shift based on the spectral shift of a Chebyshev angle. We address the problem of the unnatural high momentum tails present in the fitted interactions which reaches energies far beyond the maximal center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s}=1.4$ GeV. It turns out that these tails can be integrated out by using a block-diagonal generator of the SRG.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the unnatural high-momentum tails of separable potentials fitted to ππ scattering data (reaching up to 30 GeV, far beyond the physical region of √s ≤ 1.4 GeV) can be integrated out using a block-diagonal SRG generator, while preserving the scattering phase shifts via a new spectral-shift method based on the Chebyshev angle.

## Method

The authors solve the Kadyshevsky equation for ππ scattering using a Hamiltonian formulation discretized on a Gauss-Chebyshev momentum grid with N points up to a UV cutoff Λ_num. They define phase shifts from the spectral shift of the Chebyshev angle (the "φ-shift method"), relating the displacement of grid points after introducing the interaction to the scattering phase shifts. They then apply the similarity renormalization group (SRG) with a block-diagonal generator G = PHP + QHQ, using projectors P = θ(Λ - p) and Q = θ(p - Λ) to decouple the high-momentum region above a cutoff Λ ≈ 1.4 GeV from the physical low-momentum block.

## Key numbers

- The separable model potentials from Mathelitsch (1986) exhibit long momentum tails reaching up to 30 GeV, far beyond the maximal center-of-mass energy of √s = 1.4 GeV.
- Phase shifts calculated with the φ-shift method using only N = 5 grid points start to differ slightly from the exact solution; N = 4 and N = 7 points are also shown.
- The initial Hamiltonian matrix is 50 × 50; after SRG evolution, the reduced block below the cutoff is 11 × 11, yielding identical phase shifts with much lower computational cost.
- The cutoff Λ is chosen at approximately 1400 MeV (1.4 GeV) to separate the experimental energy region from the unphysical high-momentum tails.

## Context

This work extends the spectral-shift methods of DeWitt (1956) and Fukuda and Newton (1956), originally formulated for equidistant energy and momentum grids, to a Gauss-Chebyshev grid via the new φ-shift method introduced by Gómez-Rocha (2019). It applies the SRG approach of Glazek (1993, 1994) to remove the long-momentum tails that plague separable potentials fitted to ππ data, as in the model of Mathelitsch (1986). The combination enables efficient calculation of phase shifts in the Kadyshevsky framework, which is straightforward to extend to the three-body problem (e.g., ω or A₁ decays involving πππ interactions).
