# On convergence of the HFF expoansion for meson production in NN collisions

> We consider the application of heavy fermion formalism based ChPT to meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions. It is shown that to each lower chiral order irreducible diagram there corresponds an infinite sequence of loop diagrams which are of the same momentum power order. This destroys the one-to-one correspondence between the loop and small momentum expansion and thus rules out the application of any finite order HFF ChPT yo the NN-> NNpion reactions.

# On convergence of the HFF expansion for meson production in NN collisions

## Key claim

The one-to-one correspondence between loop expansion and small momentum expansion, which is believed to hold in the heavy fermion formalism (HFF) of chiral perturbation theory (χPT), is badly destroyed for processes with sufficiently large momentum transfer such as $NN \rightarrow NN\pi$; for each low chiral order irreducible diagram there exists an infinite sequence of higher-order loop diagrams that have the same low momentum power as the original diagram, ruling out any finite-order HFF χPT calculation for these reactions.

## Method

The authors apply modified Weinberg power counting rules tailored for meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, assigning momentum-power factors to vertices, propagators, and loop integrations. They then construct explicit examples: starting from the lowest-order impulse diagram (chiral order D=1), they add two zero-order πNN vertices, two nucleon propagators, one pion propagator, and a loop integral to produce a one-loop diagram, and repeat this procedure to generate an infinite sequence of n-loop diagrams. Using the power counting rules with characteristic momenta $Q \approx \sqrt{M m_\pi}$ and $v \cdot Q \approx m_\pi$, they show that all diagrams in the sequence have the same momentum power order as the original tree diagram.

## Key numbers

- Characteristic four-momentum transfer at threshold: $Q \approx (-m_\pi/2, \sqrt{M m_\pi})$, where $M$ is nucleon mass and $m_\pi$ is pion mass.
- Momentum power of the impulse diagram (graph 1a): $\Theta_0 \sim F^{-3}(m_\pi/M)^{1/2}$, where $F$ is the radiative pion decay constant.
- For the one-loop diagram (graph 1b), the additional factor relative to the impulse diagram is $Q^4 / [(4\pi F)^2 (v \cdot Q)^2]$, and with $4\pi F \sim M$ and $v \cdot Q \sim m_\pi$, this factor equals 1, so $\Theta_3 = \Theta_1$.
- For an n-loop diagram of chiral order $2n+1$, the momentum power is $\Theta_{2n+1} = \Theta_1 [Q^4 / ((4\pi F)^2 (v \cdot Q)^2)]^n = \Theta_1$.

## Context

This work extends the critique of HFF-based χPT for pion production in NN collisions, following earlier calculations by Park et al. (1996), Cohen et al. (1996), Sato et al. (1997), Gedalin et al. (1998), and others that showed one-loop contributions are sizably larger than lowest-order impulse and rescattering terms. The authors build on the observation by Bernard et al. (1998) and Gedalin et al. (1998) that the HFF power series expansion of the nucleon propagator is on the border of its convergence circle. The result demonstrates that the $NN \rightarrow NN\pi$ production process falls outside the HFF validity domain, and that alternative approaches beyond finite-order HFF χPT are needed for such large-momentum-transfer reactions.
