# The $μ^-d$ capture rate in effective field theory

> Muon capture on the deuteron is studied in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). It is found that by far the dominant contribution to $μd$ capture comes from a region of the final three-body phase-space in which the energy of the two neutrons is sufficiently small for HBChPT to be applicable. The single unknown low-energy constant having been fixed from the tritium beta decay rate, our calculation contains no free parameter. Our estimate of the $μd$ capture rate is consistent with the existing data. The relation between $μd$ capture and the $νd$ reactions, which are important for the SNO experiments, is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the muon capture rate on the deuteron using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) and find that the dominant contribution comes from a region of the final three-body phase space where the two-neutron relative energy is small enough for HBChPT to be applicable, meaning the "dangerous" high-momentum region contributes negligibly. The calculation contains no free parameter because the single unknown low-energy constant is fixed from the tritium beta decay rate.

## Method

The authors employ the Cut-off Weinberg scheme (CWS) of effective field theory, deriving transition operators from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) and sandwiching them between initial deuteron and final two-neutron wave functions obtained from standard nuclear physics. The single unknown low-energy constant, denoted d^R, is fixed using the experimental tritium beta decay rate, and the L=0 capture rate is computed as a function of a Gaussian cutoff parameter Lambda, with contributions from L>=1 states taken from the prior work of Tatara et al. (TKK).

## Key numbers

- The L=0 capture rate is 245 s^-1 (for Lambda=600 MeV and d^R=1.78), compared to TKK's result of 259 s^-1.
- The total capture rate, adding L>=1 contributions of 141 s^-1 from TKK, is 386 s^-1, compared to TKK's result of 397-400 s^-1.
- The variation of Gamma_mu_d^(L=0) over the cutoff range Lambda=500-800 MeV is less than 0.7 s^-1 after renormalizing d^R from tritium beta decay.
- Without the d^R term, Gamma_mu_d^(L=0) would change by as much as 16 s^-1 over the same Lambda range.
- The experimental values quoted are 409 +/- 40 s^-1 (Cargnelli et al. 1989) and 470 +/- 29 s^-1 (Bardin et al. 1986).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to muon capture on the deuteron, following the prior EFT calculation of Butler, Chen, and Kong (BCK) which contained one adjustable parameter, and the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) calculation of Tatara et al. (TKK). By fixing the low-energy constant from tritium beta decay, the authors provide a parameter-free prediction that enables future high-precision comparisons with the planned PSI measurement and supports the calculation of neutrino-deuteron cross sections needed for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) experiments.
