# Inelastic Processes in the Collision of Relativistic Highly Charged Ions with Atoms

> A general expression for the cross sections of inelastic collisions of fast (including relativistic) multicharged ions with atoms which is based on the genelazition of the eikonal approximation is derived. This expression is applicable for wide range of collision energy and has the standard nonrelativistic limit and in the ultrarelativistic limit coincides with the Baltz's exact solution ~\cite{art13} of the Dirac equation. As an application of the obtained result the following processes are calculated: the excitation and ionization cross sections of hydrogenlike atom; the single and double excitation and ionization of heliumlike atom; the multiply ionization of neon and argon atoms; the probability and cross section of K-vacancy production in the relativistic $U^{92+} - U^{91+}$ collision. The simple analytic formulae for the cross sections of inelastic collisions and the recurrence relations between the ionization cross sections of different multiplicities are also obtained. Comparison of our results with the experimental data and the results of other calculations are given.

# Inelastic Processes in the Collision of Relativistic Highly Charged Ions with Atoms

## Key claim
The authors derive a general expression for inelastic collision cross sections of fast (including relativistic) multicharged ions with atoms, based on a generalization of the eikonal approximation, which is applicable for a wide range of collision energies and has the standard nonrelativistic limit while coinciding with Baltz's exact solution of the Dirac equation in the ultrarelativistic limit. The approach avoids the breakdown of perturbation theory for large projectile charges (Z ≥ 75) where the Born approximation leads to ionization probabilities exceeding unity for small impact parameters.

## Method
The authors generalize the Glauber (eikonal) approximation for relativistic ion-atom collisions by accounting for atomic electrons described by the Dirac equation and using Lorentz transformations to relate the ion rest frame to the atom rest frame. They obtain a transition amplitude and cross section expressed as an integral over impact parameter of a matrix element involving the eikonal phase shift, and then match the eikonal result at small impact parameters with the Bethe asymptotic (Born) result at large impact parameters to obtain finite cross sections. For multiple ionization of complex atoms, they employ an independent electron approximation with averaged one-electron ionization probabilities and effective nuclear charges, using the Laplace method to evaluate cross sections for high ionization multiplicities.

## Context
This work extends the eikonal/Glauber approximation approach previously applied to nonrelativistic ion-atom collisions (Glauber, 1959) to the relativistic regime, addressing the breakdown of perturbation theory for highly charged projectiles (Z ≥ 75) noted by earlier authors. It provides an alternative to the finite-difference Dirac equation solutions of Becker et al. (1990) for U⁹²⁺-U⁹¹⁺ collisions at 1 GeV/u, and generalizes the exact ultrarelativistic solution of Baltz (1997) to arbitrary relativistic energies. The results enable analytical calculations of excitation, ionization, and multiple ionization cross sections for hydrogenlike, heliumlike, and complex atoms (neon up to eightfold, argon up to eighteenfold ionization) without the extensive numerical computations required by the Glauber approximation as implemented by previous authors.
