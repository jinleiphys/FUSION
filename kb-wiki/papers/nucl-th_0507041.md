# Polarized Neutron $β$-Decay: the Proton Asymmetry and Recoil-Order Currents

> We present an analytic, recoil-order calculation of the proton asymmetry from polarized neutron $β$-decay. The differential decay rate in terms of electron energy and proton direction follows, parametrized in terms of the most general Lorentz-invariant hadron current coupled to a left-handed lepton current. Implications for experimental efforts to measure recoil-order currents are discussed.

# Polarized Neutron β-Decay: the Proton Asymmetry and Recoil-Order Currents

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic, recoil-order calculation of the proton asymmetry from polarized neutron β-decay, but the principal result was later found to contain bookkeeping errors that render it incorrect; the erratum replaces the key equation (Equation 11) and provides corrected expressions for the proton asymmetry in two electron-energy regimes separated by a critical energy E_e^c ≈ 0.578 E_0.

## Method

The differential decay rate for polarized neutron β-decay was calculated analytically in the neutron rest frame by integrating over neutrino momentum and the electron-proton angle, using the most general Lorentz-invariant V-A hadron current parametrized by six dimensionless form factors (three vector f_i and three axial vector g_i). The calculation was performed to first order in the recoil parameter R ≡ E_0/m_n ≈ 0.0014, with separate expressions derived for electron energies below and above the critical energy E_e^c where the electron and neutrino momenta are equal. The proton asymmetry A_p was extracted from the angular distribution d²Γ/(dE_e d(cosθ_p)) = f(x)(1 + A_p cosθ_p).

## Key numbers

- Recoil parameter R ≡ E_0/m_n ≈ 0.0014 (dimensionless, defined as maximum electron energy divided by neutron mass)
- Critical electron energy x^c ≡ E_e^c/E_0 ≈ 0.578 (dimensionless, separating low- and high-energy regimes)
- λ = -1.2695 ± 0.0029 (world average ratio of axial vector to vector coupling)
- Beta asymmetry A = -0.1173 ± 0.0013 (world average experimental value)
- e-ν correlation a = -0.103 ± 0.004 (world average experimental value)
- The f_2 contribution to the proton asymmetry shifts the zero-crossing energy by 1.896 keV (specific numerical shift from CVC hypothesis value to f_2=0)
- The ratio Λ_p/Λ_e shows sensitivity to f_2 and λ at the 1% level
- Second-class current g_2 could contribute to Λ_p at approximately 5% of the level of f_2, requiring accuracy better than one part in ten thousand to extract

## Context

This work extends recoil-order calculations of neutron decay asymmetries previously performed by Harrington (1960) for polarized weak hadron decays, Holstein (1974) for general effective field theory treatment of composite nuclei and hadrons, and Gardner and Zhang (2001) for the β-asymmetry and eν correlation specifically in neutrons. The author notes agreement with Gardner and Zhang (2001) for calculations of a and A, but found differences with Harrington (1960) in the hadronic matrix element evaluation. The analytic proton asymmetry result fills a gap in the literature, as Glück and Toth (1992) had previously performed numerical calculations including the recoil asymmetry but no analytic expression existed. The ratio Λ_p/Λ_e, being independent of neutron polarization, offers a systematic handle for experiments such as abBA, UCNA, and SerA that are making precision measurements of A and the recoil asymmetry.
