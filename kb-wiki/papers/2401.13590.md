# Effects of dilute neutron matter on the neutron star crust equation of state

> We develop a compressible liquid-drop model to describe the crust of neutron stars for which the role of the nuclear clusters, the neutron gas, and the electrons are clearly identified. The novelty relies on the contribution of the neutron gas, which is qualitatively adjusted to reproduce 'ab initio' predictions in dilute neutron matter. We relate the properties of dilute neutron matter to the ones of neutron stars crust and we compare the mean-field approximation to an improved approach that better describes dilute neutron matter. The latter is quite sensitive to the unitary limit, a universal feature of Fermi systems having a large value of the scattering length and a small interaction range. While the impact of the accurate description of dilute neutron matter is important in uniform matter (up to 30\% corrections with respect to a mean-field calculations), we find a reduction of this impact in the context of the crust of neutron stars due to the additional matter components (nuclear clusters and electrons). In agreement with our previous works, dilute neutron matter is however a necessary ingredient for accurate predictions of the properties of the crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors develop a compressible liquid-drop model for the neutron star crust where the neutron gas component is adjusted to reproduce 'ab initio' predictions in dilute neutron matter; while corrections from beyond-mean-field correlations in uniform dilute neutron matter can reach up to 30%, the impact on the crust equation of state is reduced to less than 10% due to the additional contributions from nuclear clusters and electrons.

## Method

The authors construct a compressible liquid-drop model (CLDM) with Coulomb, surface, and curvature terms, treating the Wigner-Seitz cell as containing a nuclear cluster, a uniform neutron gas, and a uniform electron gas. The neutron gas energy density is decomposed into a mean-field part (from a meta-model calibrated to chiral EFT predictions) plus a correlation term parameterized by a field Δ_n(n_n) that is adjusted to reproduce several 'ab initio' predictions for dilute neutron matter (quantum Monte Carlo, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, and many-body perturbation theory results). The coupled CLDM equations (virial theorem, mechanical equilibrium, chemical equilibrium, beta-equilibrium) are solved for five extensions (ext1 to ext5) that gradually depart from the mean-field reference.

## Key numbers

- The Bertsch parameter for cold atoms is measured as 0.376 ± 0.004 (Ku 2012) and 0.41 ± 0.01 (Navon 2010).
- The ratio E_NM/E_FFG in dilute neutron matter is about 0.6 for densities in the range 10⁻⁴ to about 10⁻² fm⁻³, instead of the unitary limit value.
- Corrections from beyond-mean-field correlations in uniform dilute neutron matter reach up to 30% for the internal symmetry energy at densities n < 10⁻³ fm⁻³.
- The electron fraction in homogeneous beta-equilibrated matter is modified by up to about 25% for n < 10⁻³ fm⁻³.
- In the neutron star crust, the relative correction to the Wigner-Seitz cell radius R_WS is at most 2-3%.
- The overall impact of the improved dilute neutron matter description on crust properties is less than 10%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Grams 2022b) that showed the importance of the neutron gas component for neutron star crust properties by comparing chiral EFT and Skyrme Hamiltonians at the mean-field level. The present paper adds correlations beyond mean field by calibrating to 'ab initio' predictions from Gezerlis (2008, 2010), Vidana (2021), Gandolfi (2022), and Palaniappan (2023). The results enable more accurate predictions of neutron star crust properties by incorporating the universal features of dilute Fermi systems near the unitary limit, though the effect is moderated by the presence of nuclear clusters and electrons in the crust. A recent connection between dilute neutron matter and the crust using a phase coexistence model was performed by Gupta (2023).
