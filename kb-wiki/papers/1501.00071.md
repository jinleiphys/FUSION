# Investigation of $^{23}$N in a three-body model

> The neutron-drip-line nucleus $^{23}$N is investigated in a three-body model consisting of a $^{21}$N core and two valence neutrons. By solving the Faddeev equations with the realistic neutron-neutron potentials and the neutron-core potentials, we calculate the ground state properties of $^{23}$N and also find that there is a new excited state with two-neutron separation energy at about 0.18 MeV. The properties, such as the two-neutron separation energies, are obtained with a good agreement with experiments. By calculating the root-mean-square matter radii, the average distances between the valence neutrons, and the average distances between the core and the center-of-mass of the neutron pair, we show that the excited state of $^{23}$N has a clear halo structure. The correlation density distributions of the three-body system are also calculated to analyze its geometric configuration. At last, we find that the excited state of $^{23}$N has a very small binding energy, a large radius and distribution, and a triangular shape similar with the ground state. These roperties of the excited state indicate that it can be an Efimov state.

## Key claim
The paper predicts a new excited state of the neutron-drip-line nucleus $^{23}$N with a two-neutron separation energy of about 0.18 MeV, and argues that this state may be an Efimov state based on the geometric similarity and discrete scaling symmetry between the ground and excited states. The claim is hedged by noting that further theoretical or experimental investigations are needed to confirm the excited state.

## Method
The authors model $^{23}$N as a three-body system consisting of an inert $^{21}$N core and two valence neutrons, solving the Faddeev equations using the hyperspherical harmonic method. The neutron-neutron interaction is described by the GPT potential (central, tensor, and spin-orbit parts, omitting the spin-spin term), while the neutron-core interaction uses a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters fitted to reproduce the one-neutron separation energies of $^{22}$N (1.28 MeV) and $^{21}$N (4.59 MeV). Three parameter sets (A, B, C) are generated by fitting the upper bound, mean value, and lower bound of the $^{22}$N separation energy, and the Pauli principle is enforced via a supersymmetric transformation to eliminate forbidden bound states.

## Key numbers
- Two-neutron separation energy of the $^{23}$N ground state: $S_{2n} = 3.6 \pm 0.5$ MeV (theoretical, from Set B), compared to the experimental value $S_{2n} = 3.07 \pm 0.31$ MeV.
- Two-neutron separation energy of the excited state: $S_{2n}^{*}$ ranges from 0.069 to 0.315 MeV across parameter sets, with a mean value of 0.185 MeV.
- Root-mean-square matter radius of the ground state: $r_m \approx 3.0$ fm (Set B: 2.985 fm).
- Root-mean-square matter radius of the excited state: $r_m^{*} \approx 4.3$ fm (Set B: 4.358 fm).
- Average distance between the two valence neutrons in the ground state: $r_{nn} = 6.681$ fm (Set B).
- Average distance from the core to the center-of-mass of the valence-neutron pair in the ground state: $r_{C(nn)} = 3.496$ fm (Set B).
- Average distance between the two valence neutrons in the excited state: $r_{nn}^{*} = 16.326$ fm (Set B).
- Average distance from the core to the center-of-mass of the valence-neutron pair in the excited state: $r_{C(nn)}^{*} = 8.543$ fm (Set B).
- Ratio $r_{nn} / r_{C(nn)} \approx r_{nn}^{*} / r_{C(nn)}^{*} \approx 1.9$ for both states.
- Occupancies of the two valence neutrons in the ground state: 95% in $(1s)^2$ and 5% in $(0d)^2$.
- Occupancies of the two valence neutrons in the excited state: 77% in $(1s)^2$ and 23% in $(0d)^2$.

## Context
This work extends the Faddeev formalism, previously applied to halo nuclei such as $^{6,8}$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{12,14}$Be, $^{17}$B, and $^{22}$C (Thompson 2004, Nunes 1996, and others), to the neutron-drip-line nucleus $^{23}$N, which had rarely been studied since its first production in 1998. The prediction of a possible Efimov state in a nuclear system builds on the concept of discrete scaling symmetry in Efimov physics (Nielsen 1998) and earlier work on Efimov states in halo nuclei (Ren 1994, Ren 1996). The results motivate experimental searches for the predicted excited state and further theoretical studies of three-body effects near the neutron drip line.
