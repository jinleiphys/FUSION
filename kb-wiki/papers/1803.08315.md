# Spin polarized phases in strongly interacting matter: interplay between axial-vector and tensor mean fields

> The spontaneous spin polarization of strongly interacting matter due to axial-vector and tensor type interactions is studied at zero temperature and high baryon-number densities. We start with the mean-field Lagrangian for the axial-vector and tensor interaction channels, and find in the chiral limit that the spin polarization due to the tensor mean field ($U$) takes place first as the density increases for sufficiently strong coupling constants, and then that due to the axial-vector mean field ($A$) emerges in the region of finite tensor mean field. This can be understood that making the axial-vector mean field finite requires a broken chiral symmetry somehow, which is achieved by the finite tensor mean field in the present case. It is also found from symmetry argument that there appear the type I (II) Nambu-Goldstone modes with a linear (quadratic) dispersion in the spin polarized phase with $U\neq0$ and $A=0$ ($U\neq0$ and $A\neq0$), although these two phases exhibit the same symmetry breaking pattern.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the chiral limit, spontaneous spin polarization due to the tensor mean field (U) occurs first as density increases for sufficiently strong coupling constants, and the axial-vector mean field (A) can only become finite in the region where the tensor mean field is already finite, because the axial-vector mean field requires broken chiral symmetry which is provided by the tensor mean field.

## Method

The authors constructed a mean-field Lagrangian including axial-vector and tensor interaction channels for quark matter at zero temperature and finite baryon-number chemical potential. They derived the thermodynamic potential in the chiral limit (quark mass m=0), searched for its minima in the space of the two mean fields (A and U) as functions of the coupling constants g_A and g_U, and classified the resulting phase structure. They also applied the formalism to an extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to demonstrate the density dependence of the mean fields.

## Key numbers

- The critical tensor coupling for the onset of the tensor mean field is g_Ucrit = 6.57974 / μ^2 (where μ is the quark chemical potential).
- The threshold coupling for the axial-vector mean field to become possible is g_A1 = 12.4077 / μ^2.
- For the extended NJL model with g_A = 73.70 GeV^{-2} and g_U = 45.35 GeV^{-2}, at μ = 0.42 GeV the baryon-number density reaches ρ_B = 5.54 ρ_0 (with ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} the normal nuclear density).
- The estimated magnetic moment density from the tensor mean field at that point yields a magnetic field of approximately 1.1 × 10^{18} Gauss for quark mass m_q = 5 MeV/C^2 and spin average ⟨ψ̄ σ_{12} ψ⟩ / ⟨ψ̄ γ_0 ψ⟩ = 0.22.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Maedan (2006), Maruyama (2000, 2017), and others who separately investigated axial-vector or tensor spin polarization in dense matter, often within NJL-type models. The paper is the first to treat both interaction channels simultaneously and map the phase diagram in the coupling-constant plane. It enables future studies of the interplay between spin polarization and other phases such as chiral condensation and color superconductivity, and provides a framework for estimating magnetic fields in magnetar cores.
