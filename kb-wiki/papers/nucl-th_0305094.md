# Modified Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov at finite temperature

> The modified HFB (MHFB) theory at finite temperature is derived, which conserves the unitarity relation of the particle-density matrix. This is achieved by constructing a modified quasiparticle-density matrix, where the fluctuation of the quasiparticle number is microscopically built in. This matrix can be directly obtained from the usual quasiparticle-density matrix by applying the secondary Bogoliubov transformation, which includes the quasiparticle occupation number. It is shown that, in the limit of constant pairing parameter, the MHFB theory yields the previously obtained modified BCS (MBCS) equations. It is also proved that the modified quasiparticle RPA, which is based on the MBCS quasiparticle excitations, conserves the Ikeda sum rule. The numerical calculations of the pairing gap, heat capacity, level density, and level density parameter within the MBCS theory are carried out for $^{120}$Sn. The results show that the superfluid - normal phase transition is completely washed out. The applicability of the MBCS up to a temperature as high as $T\sim$ 5 MeV is analyzed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper derives a modified Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (MHFB) theory at finite temperature that restores the unitarity relation of the particle-density matrix, which is violated in standard finite-temperature HFB theory due to the omission of quasiparticle-number fluctuations. The authors show that the superfluid-normal phase transition is completely washed out when these statistical fluctuations are included, and the modified BCS (MBCS) limit is applicable up to temperatures as high as T ~ 5 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a modified quasiparticle-density matrix that includes the quasiparticle-number fluctuation microscopically, then apply a secondary Bogoliubov transformation involving the quasiparticle occupation number to obtain the MHFB equations. The MHFB equations are derived by minimizing the grand potential with respect to variations of the U, V, and modified occupation number matrices, yielding equations that formally resemble the standard FT-HFB equations but with modified single-particle and pairing potentials. Numerical calculations of the pairing gap, heat capacity, level density, and level density parameter are performed for the nucleus $^{120}$Sn using the MBCS limit of the theory.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap, heat capacity, level density, and level density parameter are calculated for $^{120}$Sn as functions of temperature.
- The MBCS theory is shown to be applicable up to temperatures as high as T ~ 5 MeV.
- The critical temperature in standard FT-BCS theory is approximately T_c ≈ (1/2)Δ(T=0), where Δ(T=0) is the zero-temperature pairing gap.
- The Ikeda sum rule difference S^{-} - S^{+} = (2J+1)(N-Z) is conserved in the modified QRPA based on MBCS quasiparticles, where N and Z are neutron and proton numbers respectively and J is the angular momentum of the transitions.

## Context

This work extends the previously developed modified BCS (MBCS) theory (Dang and Arima, 2002) to the full Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, and generalizes the secondary Bogoliubov transformation approach from Refs. [MRPA, MBCS]. The paper addresses the violation of the unitarity relation in finite-temperature HFB theory identified by Goodman (1981) and the violation of the Ikeda sum rule in renormalized QRPA noted by Civitarese et al. The MHFB theory enables consistent treatment of pairing correlations at high temperatures without the artificial phase transition seen in standard FT-HFB and FT-BCS calculations, and provides a foundation for modified QRPA calculations that conserve the Ikeda sum rule.
