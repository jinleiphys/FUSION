# Analysis of nature of phi -> gamma pi0 eta and phi -> gamma pi0 pi0 decays

> We study interference patterns in the phi -> gamma a0 + pi0 rho -> gamma pi eta and phi -> gamma f0 + pi0 rho -> gamma pi0 pi0 reactions. Taking into account the interference, we fit the experimental data and show that the background reaction does not distort the pi0eta spectrum in the decay phi -> gamma pi eta everywhere over the energy region and does not distort the pi0 pi0 spectrum in the decay phi -> gamma pi0 pi0 when the invariant mass m_{pi0 pi0} > 670 MeV. We discuss the details of the scalar meson production in the radiative decays and note that there are conclusive arguments in favor of the one-loop mechanism phi -> K+K- -> gamma a0 (or gamma f0). We discuss also distinctions between the four-quark, molecular, and two-quark models and argue that the establishment of the scalar meson production mechanism in the phi radiative decays gives new strong evidence in favor of the four-quark nature of the scalar a0(980) and f0(980) mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the interference between the signal decays (phi -> gamma a0/f0) and the dominant background (phi -> pi0 rho -> gamma pi eta / gamma pi0 pi0) is negligible across most of the phase space, and that the observed production mechanism (via a kaon loop, phi -> K+ K- -> gamma a0/f0) provides strong evidence for the four-quark (q^2 qbar^2) nature of the scalar a0(980) and f0(980) mesons, rather than a two-quark or molecular K Kbar state.

## Method

The author performed fits of experimental data from the SND detector at Novosibirsk for the decays phi -> gamma pi eta and phi -> gamma pi0 pi0, explicitly including the interference between the signal (phi -> gamma a0/f0) and the background (phi -> pi0 rho -> gamma pi eta / gamma pi0 pi0). The analysis compared the resulting mass spectra and branching ratios with predictions from the one-loop kaon model (phi -> K+ K- -> gamma a0/f0) and contrasted these with expectations from two-quark and molecular K Kbar models for the scalar mesons.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for phi -> gamma pi eta (signal plus background, with interference): BR = (0.79 +/- 0.2) x 10^{-4}
- Branching ratio for the signal phi -> gamma a0 -> gamma pi eta: BR = (0.75 +/- 0.2) x 10^{-4}
- Branching ratio for the background phi -> rho0 pi0 -> gamma pi0 eta: BR = 3.43 x 10^{-6}
- Branching ratio for phi -> gamma pi0 pi0 (signal plus background, with interference): BR = (1.26 +/- 0.29) x 10^{-4}
- Branching ratio for the signal phi -> gamma f0 -> gamma pi0 pi0: BR = (1.01 +/- 0.23) x 10^{-4}
- Branching ratio for the background phi -> rho0 pi0 -> gamma pi0 pi0: BR = 0.18 x 10^{-4}
- The background does not distort the pi0 pi0 spectrum when the invariant mass m_{pi0 pi0} > 670 MeV, corresponding to a photon energy omega < 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Achasov and Ivanchenko (1989) and Achasov and Gubin (1997, 2001) on the radiative decays of the phi meson as a probe of scalar meson structure. It directly fits experimental data from the SND and CMD-2 detectors at Novosibirsk and compares with results from DAΦNE. The paper argues that the kaon-loop mechanism is established at a physical level of proof, and that this mechanism, combined with the observed branching ratios and spectral shapes, rules out the two-quark model (which would be OZI suppressed) and the molecular K Kbar model (which would produce a much smaller real part of the amplitude and a narrower spectrum), thereby providing new strong evidence for the four-quark nature of the a0(980) and f0(980) mesons.
