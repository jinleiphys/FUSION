# The role of pion exchange in $η$ meson photo production on the deuteron

> Incoherent $η$ photoproduction on the deuteron is studied with the main emphasis on the role of final state interactions. In addition to the previously studied mechanisms of $NN$ and $ηN$ rescatterings, the role of an intermediate pion exchange is considered in detail, where first a pion is photoproduced on one nucleon and then rescatters into an $η$ meson on the other, the spectator nucleon. It is found, that the role of this pion mediated contribution is comparable in size to that of $ηN$ rescattering. Results for total and semi-inclusive differential cross sections and associated polarization observables are presented. In particular polarization observables show a significant sensititvity to final state interactions.

# The role of pion exchange in $\eta$ meson photo production on the deuteron

## Key claim
The pion exchange mechanism ($\gamma N \to \pi N \to \eta N$) contributes comparably to $\eta N$ rescattering in incoherent $\eta$ photoproduction on the deuteron, but only at photon lab energies above about 720 MeV and in kinematic regions where the two-nucleon response is important (e.g., backward $\eta$ emission angles); near threshold its effect is only about a 3% decrease in the total cross section.

## Method
The authors compute the $T$-matrix for $\gamma d \to \eta np$ as a sum of four contributions: impulse approximation (IA), $NN$ rescattering, $\eta N$ rescattering, and the pion exchange mechanism ($\pi N$). The elementary $\eta$ photoproduction operator is taken from the isobar model EtaMAID (including resonances up to $W=2$ GeV), the pion photoproduction amplitudes from MAID2007, and the $\pi N \to \eta N$ and $\eta N \to \eta N$ amplitudes are described by an isobar ansatz driven by the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance with parameters fitted to reproduce an $\eta N$ scattering length of $a_{\eta N} = (0.5 + i0.32)$ fm. The $NN$ scattering amplitude and deuteron wave function use a separable representation of the Paris potential (partial waves up to $J=2$), with checks against CD-Bonn and Nijmegen potentials showing less than 10% deviation near threshold.

## Key numbers
- The $\eta N$ scattering length used is $a_{\eta N} = (0.5 + i0.32)$ fm.
- At $E_\gamma^{lab} = 650$ MeV, inclusion of pion exchange decreases the total unpolarized cross section by about 3%.
- Near threshold (around $E_\gamma^{lab} = 640$ MeV), the total FSI enhancement factor is about 5 relative to the impulse approximation.
- The characteristic momentum of the intermediate pion when approximately on-shell is about 400 MeV/c, corresponding to internucleon distances of about 0.5 fm.
- The two-pion decay width parameter is $\gamma_{\pi\pi} = 4.3$ MeV.
- The calculation covers photon lab energies from threshold up to $E_\gamma^{lab} = 800$ MeV.
- At $E_\gamma^{lab} \geq 720$ MeV and backward angles ($\cos\theta_\eta \leq -0.5$), the pion exchange contribution becomes comparable in size to $\eta N$ rescattering.
- The $NN$ potential dependence is less than 10% deviation near threshold, decreasing rapidly with energy.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of $\eta$ photoproduction on the deuteron by Fix, Arenhövel, and collaborators (e.g., Fix and Arenhövel, 1997; 2000; 2004) that considered $NN$ and $\eta N$ rescattering but treated pion exchange only with crude approximations (e.g., including only the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance in the pion photoproduction operator, or using simplified kinematics). The present calculation uses full MAID2007 amplitudes for pion photoproduction and a more rigorous treatment of the pion propagator. The results show that pion exchange is not negligible at higher energies, contrary to earlier assumptions based on its suppression at short internucleon distances. The work enables future three-body $\eta NN$–$\pi NN$ calculations that could improve agreement with the experimental data of Krusche et al. (1995) in the near-threshold region, where the present perturbative approach still underestimates the measured total cross section.
