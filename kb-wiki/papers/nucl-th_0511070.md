# Modelization of the EOS

> This article summarizes theoretical predictions for the density and isospin dependence of the nuclear mean field and the corresponding nuclear equation of state. We compare predictions from microscopic and phenomenological approaches. An application to heavy ion reactions requires to incorporate these forces into the framework of dynamical transport models. Constraints on the nuclear equation of state derived from finite nuclei and from heavy ion reactions are discussed.

## Key claim
This article summarizes theoretical predictions for the density and isospin dependence of the nuclear mean field and equation of state, but notes that phenomenological models and effective field theory approaches contain parameters fixed around or below saturation density, making extrapolation to supra-normal densities questionable, while ab initio calculations remain too involved for systematic application to finite nuclei.

## Method
The authors compare three classes of models for the nuclear equation of state: phenomenological density functionals (Skyrme forces, Gogny forces, relativistic mean field models), effective field theory approaches (density functional theory and chiral perturbation theory), and ab initio many-body calculations (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, variational calculations, Green's function Monte-Carlo). They discuss how these forces are incorporated into dynamical transport models for heavy ion reactions and review constraints from finite nuclei and heavy ion data.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: approximately 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: approximately -16 MeV
- In QHD-I (Walecka model), the incompressibility K = 540 MeV, which is too stiff compared to empirical values
- Heavy ion reactions in the low and intermediate energy range (E_lab ~ 0.1 to 1 AGeV) access nuclear densities between 2 to 3 times saturation density
- The highest baryon densities (approximately 8 times saturation density) will likely be reached in the energy range of 20 to 30 AGeV at the future FAIR facility
- Phenomenological density functionals reproduce binding energies and rms-radii with an average relative error of about 1 to 5 percent
- The Dirac mass m*_D in relativistic models absorbs the scalar part of the mean field via m*_D = M - Gamma_S * rho_S
- The single particle potential U in relativistic dynamics is of the order of -50 MeV, resulting from cancellation of scalar and vector fields each of the order of several hundred MeV

## Context
This review extends earlier work on the nuclear equation of state from the Bevalac era (Gustafsson et al., 1984) and compares microscopic approaches such as Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Brueckner and Gammel, 1958; Zuo et al., 2002), variational calculations (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998), and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990; Boelting, Fuchs, and Faessler, 1999) with phenomenological Skyrme and relativistic mean field models. The work enables future constraints on the isospin-dependent EOS from radioactive beam facilities at FAIR/GSI, SPIRAL2/GANIL, and RIA, and provides a framework for interpreting heavy ion observables such as collective flow and kaon production in terms of nuclear matter properties.
