# Neutron Structure Function and A=3 Mirror Nuclei

> We investigate deep inelastic scattering from He-3 and H-3 within a conventional convolution treatment of binding and Fermi motion effects. Using realistic Faddeev wave functions together with a nucleon spectral function, we demonstrate that the free neutron structure function can be extracted in deep-inelastic scattering from A=3 mirror nuclei, with nuclear effects canceling to within 2% for x < 0.85.

## Key claim

The free neutron structure function can be extracted from deep inelastic scattering on A=3 mirror nuclei (He-3 and H-3) with nuclear effects canceling to within 2% for x < 0.85, provided one uses the ratio of EMC effects in the two nuclei rather than their absolute values.

## Method

The authors performed a conventional convolution treatment of binding and Fermi motion effects, using realistic Faddeev wave functions together with a nucleon spectral function to calculate nuclear structure functions for He-3 and H-3. They computed the ratio R of the EMC-type ratios for the two mirror nuclei using several different nucleon-nucleon potentials (PEST separable approximation to the Paris potential, unitary pole approximation to the Reid Soft Core potential, and Yamaguchi potential) and various parton distribution parameterizations (CTEQ, GRV, Donnachie-Landshoff, and BBS) at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects cancel to within 2% for x < 0.85 when extracting the free neutron structure function from A=3 mirror nuclei
- The ratio R of EMC ratios for He-3 and H-3 deviates from a central value of approximately 1.01 by less than +/- 1% for x < 0.85, with very weak dependence on the nuclear wave function
- The dependence of R on the input nucleon structure function parameterization is less than 0.5% for x < 0.6, and less than +/- 1% for 0.6 < x < 0.85
- Higher twist corrections to R are negligible for x < 0.8, increasing to approximately 1.5% at x ~ 0.85
- Modifying the PEST wave function to account for binding energy differences between He-3 and H-3 produces a shift of approximately 0.5-1% in R
- The proposed Jefferson Lab experiment would achieve statistical uncertainties of less than +/- 0.25% over the range 0.10 <= x <= 0.83, with total experimental error in the cross section ratio of H-3 to He-3 of less than 1.0%

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to extract the neutron structure function from nuclear data, which previously relied on deuteron measurements where nuclear binding corrections can introduce errors of up to 50% in F2^n/F2^p at x ~ 0.75 (Melnitchouk and Thomas, 1996; Whitlow et al., 1992). The method exploits the mirror symmetry of A=3 nuclei to achieve near-complete cancellation of nuclear effects, in contrast to earlier approaches using deuterium targets. The proposed measurements at Jefferson Lab with its 12 GeV energy upgrade would enable the first definitive determination of the neutron to proton structure function ratio at large x, and would also allow determination of the absolute EMC effect in A=3 nuclei, complementing the existing HERMES data on He-3 at small x and Q^2.
