# Renormalization of chiral nuclear forces with multiple subtractions in peripheral channels

> We analyse the renormalization of the of two-nucleon interaction with multiple subtractions in peripheral waves considering two chiral forces at N3LO. Phase shifts at low energies are then computed with several subtraction points below μ= 10 / fm. We show that for most peripheral waves the phase shifts have nearly no dependence on the renormalization scale. In two cases the phase shifts converge slowly as the renormalization scale approaches μ= 1 / fm and in one case the phase shifts presented oscillations with respect to the subtraction point μ.

## Key claim
For most peripheral waves (angular momentum J between 3 and 6), the phase shifts computed from N3LO chiral forces using a subtractive renormalization scheme show nearly no dependence on the renormalization scale μ, but in the ³H₄ and ³I₅ waves convergence is slow as μ approaches 1 fm⁻¹, and in the ³F₃ wave the phase shifts oscillate with respect to μ when using the Entem-Machleidt (EM) potential.

## Method
The authors applied a subtractive renormalization method with five subtractions to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-nucleon system, using bare (non-regulated) N3LO chiral potentials from Entem and Machleidt (EM) and from Epelbaum, Glöckle and Meissner (EGM). They computed neutron-proton phase shifts in peripheral partial waves with angular momentum J = 3 to 6 for several renormalization scales μ up to 10 fm⁻¹, with momentum integrations limited at Λ = 30 fm⁻¹ (effectively infinite cutoff). The K-matrix was solved using the principal value prescription, and results were compared to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers
- Phase shifts were computed for renormalization scales μ up to 10 fm⁻¹ for the EM potential and up to 2 fm⁻¹ for the EGM potential.
- The momentum integration cutoff was set to Λ = 30 fm⁻¹, described as "extremely large" and effectively infinite.
- For the EGM potential, a spectral function regularization cutoff of Λ̃ = 4 mπ (550 MeV) was used.
- The ³G₅ channel shows agreement with partial wave analysis only up to approximately 50 MeV laboratory energy.
- The ³F₃ wave with the EM potential shows oscillations when μ approaches 10 fm⁻¹.

## Context
This work extends previous subtractive renormalization studies of chiral nuclear forces, which had been applied at N2LO with four subtractions (Timóteo et al., 2012), to N3LO with five subtractions in peripheral channels. It contrasts with the standard cutoff regularization approach used by Epelbaum, Glöckle and Meissner (2000) and Entem and Machleidt (2003), where the potential is modified by a regulator function. The results enable a parameter-free description of peripheral waves (where only pion exchanges contribute) and provide a non-relativistic renormalization group flow equation for the driving terms, with the cutoff losing physical significance.
