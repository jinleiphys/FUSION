# Neutron and proton electric dipole moments from $N_f=2+1$ domain-wall fermion lattice QCD

> We present a lattice calculation of the neutron and proton electric dipole moments (EDM's) with $N_f=2+1$ flavors of domain-wall fermions. The neutron and proton EDM form factors are extracted from three-point functions at the next-to-leading order in the $θ$ vacuum of QCD. In this computation, we use pion masses 0.33 and 0.42 GeV and 2.7 fm$^3$ lattices with Iwasaki gauge action and a 0.17 GeV pion and 4.6 fm$^3$ lattice with I-DSDR gauge action, all generated by the RBC and UKQCD collaborations. The all-mode-averaging technique enables an efficient and high statistics calculation. Chiral behavior of lattice EDM's is discussed in the context of baryon chiral perturbation theory. In addition, we also show numerical evidence on relationship of three- and two-point correlation function with local topological distribution.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first-principles lattice QCD calculation of neutron and proton electric dipole moments (EDMs) from the QCD theta term, but the results are a feasibility study with relatively large statistical errors, particularly at the near-physical pion mass of 170 MeV where no clear EDM signal was observed.

## Method

The authors extracted the CP-odd EDM form factor F3 from three-point correlation functions of the electromagnetic current with nucleon interpolating operators, expanded to next-to-leading order in the theta vacuum. They used Nf=2+1 domain-wall fermion gauge ensembles generated by the RBC and UKQCD collaborations at three pion masses (330, 420, and 170 MeV), employing the all-mode-averaging technique to improve statistics. The CP-odd mixing angle alpha_N was determined from the parity-odd part of the nucleon two-point function, and the EDM form factor was obtained by subtracting the alpha_N-weighted CP-even form factors from the CP-odd three-point function.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 0.33 GeV and 0.42 GeV on 2.7 fm³ lattices (Iwasaki gauge action), and 0.17 GeV on 4.6 fm³ lattice (I-DSDR gauge action)
- Lattice spacings: a⁻¹ = 1.7848(6) GeV for Iwasaki 24³, a⁻¹ = 1.3784(68) GeV for I-DSDR 32³
- Topological susceptibility: chi_Q = 3.1(2)×10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ (330 MeV pion), 4.4(2)×10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ (420 MeV pion), 0.9(2)×10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ (170 MeV pion)
- CP-odd mixing angle alpha_N at zero momentum: -0.356(22) for 330 MeV pion, -0.370(22) for 420 MeV pion, -0.333(128) for 170 MeV pion
- EDM form factors on Iwasaki 24³ ensembles have roughly 25-30% statistical errors at best, growing to more than 100% at worst depending on nucleon and momenta
- Source-sink separations: approximately 1.3 fm (12 time-slices for 24³, 10 for 32³) and 0.9 fm (8 time-slices for 24³)

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of nucleon EDMs using strategy (2) (direct form factor extraction) with domain-wall fermions, which maintain chiral symmetry and avoid O(a) artifacts that affected previous Wilson-clover fermion calculations by Shintani et al. (2006, 2008) and Aoki et al. (2008). The results are compared to phenomenological estimates from baryon chiral perturbation theory and sum rules by Pospelov (1999), Hisano (2012), and others. The calculation enables future inclusion of higher-dimensional CP-odd operators from beyond-the-Standard-Model theories, such as quark electric and chromoelectric dipole moments, and provides a first-principles check of the low-energy constants appearing in effective field theory approaches.
