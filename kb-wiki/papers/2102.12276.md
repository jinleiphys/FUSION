# Hadron-quark mixed phase in the quark-meson coupling model

> We explore the possibility of a structured hadron-quark mixed phase forming in the interior of neutron stars. The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which explicitly incorporates the internal quark structure of the nucleon, is employed to describe the hadronic phase, while the quark phase is described by the same bag model as the one used in the QMC framework, so as to keep consistency between the two coexisting phases. We analyze the effect of the appearance of hadron-quark pasta phases on the neutron-star properties. We also discuss the influence of nuclear symmetry energy and the bag constant B in quark matter on the deconfinement phase transition. For the treatment of the hadron-quark mixed phase, we use the energy minimization method and compare it with the Gibbs construction. The finite-size effects like surface and Coulomb energies are taken into account in the energy minimization method; they play crucial roles in determining the pasta configuration during the hadron-quark phase transition. It is found that the finite-size effects can significantly reduce the region of the mixed phase relative to that of the Gibbs construction. Using a consistent value of B in the QMC model and quark matter, we find that hadron-quark pasta phases are formed in the interior of massive stars, but no pure quark matter can exist.

## Key claim

Using a consistent bag constant in both the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model and the MIT bag model for quark matter, structured hadron-quark pasta phases can form in the interior of massive neutron stars, but no pure quark matter can exist; the finite-size effects (surface and Coulomb energies) significantly reduce the density range of the mixed phase compared to the Gibbs construction.

## Method

The authors employed the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for the hadronic phase, where nucleons are treated as static MIT bags containing three confined quarks interacting via scalar and vector meson exchange in the mean-field approximation. The quark phase was described by the same MIT bag model with three-flavor quarks (u, d, s) and leptons in beta equilibrium. For the hadron-quark mixed phase, the energy minimization (EM) method was used within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, incorporating finite-size effects (surface and Coulomb energies) and allowing for geometric pasta configurations (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble), with the surface tension calculated self-consistently using the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) method.

## Key numbers

- The onset of quark droplet formation occurs at baryon density \(0.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for \(B^{1/4}=210.854\) MeV, compared to \(0.6\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) in the Gibbs construction.
- The mixed phase ends at \(1.47\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) for \(B^{1/4}=210.854\) MeV, where pure quark matter becomes energetically favored.
- Using a smaller bag constant \(B^{1/4}=180\) MeV leads to an earlier onset of the structured mixed phase at \(0.43\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and pure quark phase at \(0.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The surface tension at the hadron-quark interface lies in the range of \(43-48\) MeV/fm\(^2\) for the case with \(B^{1/4}=210.854\) MeV.
- Muons appear at baryon density \(0.11\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) in the QMC model.

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron-quark phase transitions in neutron stars by Panda et al. (2004, 2010, 2012), who used the QMC model with the Gibbs construction without finite-size effects. The authors apply the energy minimization method, previously used for nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions at subnuclear densities by Baym et al. (1971), Lattimer et al. (1985, 1991), and Bao et al. (2014), to the hadron-quark mixed phase. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors with structured mixed phases, and the approach can be used to interpret constraints from gravitational-wave events such as GW170817 and GW190425, as well as NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451.
