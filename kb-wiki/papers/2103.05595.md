# Determine the neutron skin type by relativistic isobaric collisions

> The effects of neutron skin on the multiplicity ($N_{\rm ch}$) and eccentricity($ε_2$) in relativistic $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV are investigated with the Trento model. It is found that the Ru+Ru/Zr+Zr ratios of the $N_{\rm ch}$ distributions and $ε_{2}$ in mid-central collisions are exquisitely sensitive to the neutron skin type (skin vs.~halo). The state-of-the-art calculations by energy density functional theory (DFT) favor the halo-type neutron skin and can soon be confronted by experimental data. It is demonstrated that the halo-type density can serve as a good surrogate for the DFT density, and thus can be efficiently employed to probe nuclear deformities by using elliptic flow data in central collisions. We provide hereby a proof-of-principle venue to simultaneously determine the neutron skin type, thickness, and nuclear deformity.

## Key claim

The Ru+Ru/Zr+Zr ratios of charged hadron multiplicity distributions and eccentricity in mid-central collisions can distinguish between skin-type and halo-type neutron skins, with the state-of-the-art energy density functional theory (DFT) calculations favoring the halo-type; the authors provide a proof-of-principle that these ratios can simultaneously determine neutron skin type, thickness, and nuclear deformity, but note that the halo-type WS density can serve as a good surrogate for DFT density only for comparative studies of these observables.

## Method

The authors used the Trento model to simulate relativistic isobaric collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy 200 GeV, with parameter p=0 (entropy proportional to sqrt(T_A T_B)), gamma fluctuation parameter k=1.4, and Gaussian nucleon size 0.6 fm. They computed the charged hadron multiplicity (N_ch) distributions and the root-mean-square eccentricity epsilon_2{2} from the initial geometry, and took the ratio of Ru+Ru to Zr+Zr results to cancel systematic uncertainties. Woods-Saxon (WS) parameterizations of neutron densities were constructed in two extreme cases: skin-type (larger radius parameter R_n, same diffuseness a_n as protons) and halo-type (same R_n as protons, larger a_n), and compared to DFT-calculated densities using the SLy4 Skyrme parameter set.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of Zr taken as Delta r_np = 0.12 fm for the WS skin-type and halo-type comparisons, with Ru having zero skin thickness
- WS charge density radius parameters: R = 5.085 fm for Ru, R = 5.021 fm for Zr; diffuseness a = 0.523 fm for both
- For halo-type neutron density of Zr: R = 5.021 fm, a = 0.592 fm (compared to proton a = 0.523 fm)
- For skin-type neutron density of Zr: R = 5.194 fm, a = 0.523 fm (compared to proton R = 5.021 fm)
- DFT-calculated neutron skin thickness of Zr is Delta r_np = 0.16 fm, larger than the 0.12 fm used in the WS comparison
- The Ru+Ru/Zr+Zr ratio of eccentricity in mid-central collisions reaches as large as 2% on the top for the halo-type density
- 50 million events were generated for each collision species in each density case; STAR experiment collected over 2 billion events for each isobar system

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the AMPT model (Li 2018) and Glauber calculations (Xu 2017) that found DFT densities predict a higher multiplicity in central Ru+Ru than Zr+Zr collisions, contrary to predictions using charge densities alone (Deng 2016). The paper builds on low-energy nuclear experiments (Trzcinska 2001, Jastrzebski 2004) suggesting Zr has a halo-type neutron skin of thickness 0.12 +/- 0.03 fm. The results enable experimental discrimination between skin-type and halo-type neutron skins using the soon-to-be-available isobar collision data from RHIC, and provide a method to simultaneously determine neutron skin type, thickness, and nuclear deformity that bypasses challenging DFT calculations of deformed nuclei.
