# Theory at Quark Matter '02

> I summarize theory at Quark Matter 2002, stressing the continuing inability of a single model to describe all notable features of the data from $\sqrt{s}/A: 55 \to 200$ GeV.

## Key claim

No single model can describe all notable features of the heavy-ion collision data across the energy range from SPS to RHIC (√s/A: 55 → 200 GeV), despite precise measurements that now rule out many theories.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical results presented at Quark Matter 2002, comparing lattice QCD calculations, statistical thermal models, hydrodynamic simulations, parton energy-loss models, and saturation approaches against experimental data from SPS (√s/A: 5 → 17 GeV) and RHIC (√s/A: 55, 130, and 200 GeV) for collisions of nuclei with atomic number A ≈ 200.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD pure gauge transition temperature: T_c ≈ 270 MeV, with errors ≈ ±5%, taking string tension as (400 MeV)²
- Nearly critical region about T_c: within ≈ ±10% of T_c for three colors; Polyakov loop potentials change very rapidly within ≈ ±2.5% of T_c
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC: T ≈ 165 MeV, with chemical potential μ ≈ 14 MeV
- Radial velocities of local rest frame at RHIC: up to ≈ 2/3 c, averaged over radius ≈ 1/2 c
- Elliptic flow linear in p_t for pions up to p_t ≈ 1.5 GeV; flat for p_t: 2 → 6 GeV
- HBT ratio R_out/R_side: decreases with increasing p_t, about one by p_t ≈ 400 MeV, with range 0.85 ≥ R_out/R_side ≥ 1.15
- High-p_t suppression factor: R_AA ≈ 1/3 → 1/4 for charged hadrons, R_AA ≈ 1/5 → 1/6 for pions, for p_t: 4 → 9 GeV
- SPS Cronin effect: R_AA up to ≈ 2.5 by p_t ≈ 3 GeV
- Initial time required in hydrodynamic calculations: ≈ 0.6 fm/c
- Average p_t at RHIC: ≈ 550 MeV; changes by at most 1% between √s/A = 130 and 200 GeV
- Multiplicity change between √s/A = 130 and 200 GeV: at least 15%
- HBT radii growth from SPS to RHIC: ≈ 50%, same as multiplicity increase
- Lattice with dynamical quarks: T_c ≈ 175 MeV; at μ ≈ 200 MeV, T_c decreases to ≈ 160 MeV
- Saturation momentum scale: p_sat ≈ 1 GeV, giving natural time scale ≈ 0.2 fm/c

## Context

This conference summary extends the theoretical discussion from Quark Matter 2001, where first RHIC Run I results were presented, and contrasts RHIC findings with SPS results on J/Ψ suppression (Ramello, Wessels, Wambach) and dilepton excess. The paper identifies four key RHIC observations, high-p_t suppression, elliptic flow, HBT radii, and jet absorption, that collectively challenge existing models. Prior approaches such as Bjorken's energy-loss picture (Baier, Muller, Wang), hydrodynamic models (Huovinen, Heinz, Pratt), and saturation models (Kharzeev, Iancu, Krasnitz) each explain some features but fail to describe all data simultaneously. The work enables future efforts to develop a unified framework incorporating non-equilibrium dynamics near T_c, as suggested by Dumitru and Shuryak, and motivates further lattice studies with physical quark masses and nonzero chemical potential.
