# Strange quark effects in electron and neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering

> The role of the sea quarks to ground state nucleon properties with electroweak probes is discussed. A relativistic Green's function approach to parity violating electron scattering and a distorted-wave impulse-approximation applied to charged- and neutral-current neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering are presented in view of the possible determination of the strangeness content of the nucleon.

# Strange quark effects in electron and neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering

## Key claim

Parity-violating electron scattering and neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering can provide complementary information on the strange quark content of the nucleon, but the extraction of the strange axial form factor from parity-violating electron data alone is prevented by the need for radiative corrections, while neutrino-induced reactions are primarily sensitive to the axial-vector form factor and require nuclear structure effects to be understood for reliable interpretation.

## Method

The authors apply a relativistic Green's function approach to inclusive parity-violating electron scattering on nuclei, using a single-particle optical-model Green's function based on a bi-orthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate. For neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering, they use a relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) where the transition amplitude is computed from single-particle matrix elements of the weak current operator, treating final state interactions via a relativistic optical potential with real and imaginary parts.

## Key numbers

- The scale factor for the parity-violating asymmetry is given as $A_0 \simeq 1.799 \times 10^{-4} Q^2$ (GeV/$c)^{-2}.
- The Weinberg angle is taken as $\sin^2{\theta_{\mathrm W}} \simeq 0.2313$.
- The vector form factor dipole mass is $M_{\mathrm V} = 0.843$ GeV.
- The axial mass is taken as $M_{\mathrm A} = (1.026 \pm 0.021)$ GeV from Bernard (2002).
- For $^{12}$C at $q = 400$ MeV/c, differences between calculations with different bound states and optical potentials are less than 10% at low and high energy transfers.
- For $^{12}$C at $q = 500$ MeV/c, $\omega = 120$ MeV, and $\vartheta = 30^\circ$, the parity-violating asymmetry reduces in absolute value up to $\simeq 40\%$ as $\rho^{\mathrm s}$ varies in the range $-3 \leq \rho^{\mathrm s} \leq +3$, and changes up to $\simeq 15\%$ for $-1 \leq \mu^{\mathrm s} \leq +1$.
- The proton-to-neutron ratio for quasi-elastic neutrino scattering on $^{12}$C is enhanced by a factor $\simeq 20$-$30\%$ when $g^{\mathrm s}_{\mathrm A}$ is included and by $\simeq 50\%$ when both $g^{\mathrm s}_{\mathrm A}$ and $\mu^{\mathrm s}$ are included.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Green's function approach previously applied to inclusive parity-conserving electron scattering (Meucci et al., 2003) and to inclusive quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (Meucci et al., 2004). It builds on parity-violating electron scattering measurements from the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, A4, and G0 collaborations, and on the BNL neutrino-proton elastic scattering experiment. The calculations aim to support the FINeSSE experiment at Fermilab, which plans to measure the ratio of neutral-current to charged-current neutrino-nucleus processes on carbon to determine strange form factors with uncertainties of $\simeq \pm 0.02$ per point in the range $0.25 \leq Q^2 \leq 0.75$ (GeV/$c)^2$, as proposed by Pate (2005).
