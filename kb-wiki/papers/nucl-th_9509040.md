# The Maximum Lifetime of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The width $ΔT$ of the deconfinement transition region is shown to influence strongly the flow structure in the (Landau-) hydrodynamical expansion of a quark-gluon plasma. For a sharp first order transition ($ΔT=0$) the mixed phase is rather long-lived, with a lifetime that has a maximum when the initial energy density is at the phase boundary between mixed and pure quark-gluon matter. For increasing $ΔT$, however, the lifetime decreases rapidly. Hadronic matter, however, remains long-lived as a consequence of the rapid change in the degrees of freedom in the transition region and the corresponding ``softening'' of the equation of state.

# The Maximum Lifetime of the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The width ΔT of the deconfinement transition region strongly influences the lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma in one-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion, with the lifetime of matter near the critical temperature decreasing rapidly as ΔT increases, contrary to earlier suggestions that the uncertainty in ΔT would have little effect on observables.

## Method

The authors solved the (1+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a finite slab of matter using the Harten-Lax-van Leer-Einfeldt (HLLE) algorithm, with an equation of state where the entropy density is parameterized by a hyperbolic tangent function that interpolates between hadronic and quark-gluon degrees of freedom. The width ΔT of the transition region was varied between 0 and 0.1 Tc, and the expansion solutions were computed for initial energy densities ranging from the hadron-mixed phase boundary to well above the quark-gluon phase boundary.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of degrees of freedom between quark-gluon and hadron phases is r = 37/3, corresponding to an ideal massless pion gas for hadrons and non-interacting massless u and d quarks and gluons for the plasma.
- The critical width separating thermodynamically anomalous from normal behavior is ΔT* ≃ 0.07676 Tc.
- For ΔT = 0 with initial energy density at the softest point (ε0 = 1.875 Tc sc), the lifetime of matter at T = Tc at the center is tlife ≃ 43 R, where R is half the initial slab size.
- For ΔT = 0 with initial energy density ε0 = 1.125 Tc sc (between hadron and quark-gluon phase boundaries), the mixed phase lifetime at the center is tlife ≃ 26 R.
- The upper boundary of the thermodynamically anomalous region in energy density is εΣ ≃ 0.18275 Tc sc, which is far below the quark-gluon phase boundary at εQ = 1.8125 Tc sc.
- For ΔT = 0.1 Tc with initial energy density ε0 = 0.1125 Tc sc, it takes about 4 R for the center to cool below T = 0.7 Tc and about 6 R to cool below T = 0.5 Tc, compared to 3 R and 5 R respectively for ΔT = 0.

## Context

This work extends the study of Blaizot and Ollitrault (1990) on the sensitivity of hydrodynamic flow to the width of the deconfinement transition, who found that structural differences in the flow had little effect on final observables for their chosen initial conditions. The paper also builds on the work of Hung and Shuryak (1995) who identified the "softest point" in the equation of state as leading to a maximum in the lifetime of mixed phase matter, and on the work of Rischke, Purschke, and Schäfer (1995) who demonstrated that a phase transition causes a minimum in the excitation function of directed transverse flow. The results show that while the lifetime of matter near Tc decreases rapidly with increasing ΔT, the lifetime of cooler hadronic matter remains long or even increases, suggesting that electromagnetic probes may be sensitive to ΔT while flow and correlation observables remain fairly insensitive.
