# Towards a solution of the Gn/Gp puzzle in the weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei

> In this contribution we discuss an evaluation of double-nucleon coincidence spectra for the non-mesonic weak decay of Lambda-hypernuclei. The results have been obtained by combining a one-meson-exchange model for the Lambda N -> nN transition in finite nuclei with a Monte Carlo code accounting for the nucleon final state interactions. A weak-decay-model independent analysis of KEK coincidence data allows us to extract Gn/Gp values around 0.3-0.4 for 5_Lambda-He and 12_Lambda-C, thus representing an important progress towards the solution of the Gn/Gp puzzle. Preliminary results for the angular asymmetries in the non-mesonic weak decay of polarized hypernuclei are also presented.

## Key claim

By combining a one-meson-exchange model with a Monte Carlo simulation of nucleon final state interactions, the authors extract values of the neutron-to-proton ratio \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) around 0.3–0.4 for \(^5_\Lambda\)He and \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C from KEK coincidence data, but these values depend on the assumed ratio of two-body induced decay width \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1\) and are substantially smaller than those obtained from single-nucleon spectra.

## Method

The authors combine a one-meson-exchange model (using NSC97a and NSC97f potentials) for the \(\Lambda N \to nN\) transition in finite nuclei with an intranuclear cascade Monte Carlo code that accounts for nucleon final state interactions (FSI). They also include the two-body induced decay mechanism \(\Lambda NN \to nNN\) via a local density approximation. A weak-decay-model independent analysis of KEK coincidence data for \(^5_\Lambda\)He and \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C is performed using six model-independent quantities to extract \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) and preliminary results for angular asymmetries in polarized hypernuclei are presented.

## Key numbers

- For \(^5_\Lambda\)He, the extracted \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.39 \pm 0.11\) if \(\Gamma_2 = 0\), and \(0.26 \pm 0.11\) if \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1 = 0.2\).
- For \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C, the extracted \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p = 0.38 \pm 0.14\) if \(\Gamma_2 = 0\), and \(0.29 \pm 0.14\) if \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1 = 0.25\).
- The experimental \(N_{nn}/N_{np}\) ratio for \(^5_\Lambda\)He is \(0.44 \pm 0.11\) and for \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C is \(0.40 \pm 0.09\) (KEK-E462 and KEK-E508 preliminary data).
- The OMEf model predicts \(N_{nn}/N_{np} = 0.61\) for \(^5_\Lambda\)He and \(0.43\) for \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C (back-to-back kinematics, nucleon kinetic energies \(\geq 30\) MeV).
- For the observable asymmetry \(a^\text{M}_\Lambda\) in \(^5_\Lambda\vec{\text{He}}\) with FSI and \(T^\text{th}_p = 0\), the value is \(-0.30\); for \(^{12}_\Lambda\vec{\text{C}}\) it is \(-0.16\).
- The ratio \(a_\Lambda / a^\text{M}_\Lambda \simeq 2\) for \(^5_\Lambda\vec{\text{He}}\) and \(\simeq 4\) for \(^{12}_\Lambda\vec{\text{C}}\) when \(T^\text{th}_p = 0\).

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) puzzle, where one-pion-exchange models (OPE) give very small ratios (\(\simeq 0.05\)–\(0.20\)) while earlier single-nucleon analyses gave larger experimental values. The authors extend prior work by Alberico et al. (2002) and Ramos et al. (1997) by using coincidence observables rather than single-nucleon spectra, and by including a weak-decay-model independent analysis of KEK data. The results enable future comparisons with forthcoming data from KEK and FINUDA to achieve better determinations of \(\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p\) and first constraints on \(\Gamma_2/\Gamma_1\), while the asymmetry puzzle remains open and requires new experiments on s- and p-shell hypernuclei.
