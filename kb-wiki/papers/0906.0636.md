# Non-local mean field effect on nuclei near Z=64 sub-shell

> Evolutions of single-particle energies and Z=64 sub-shell along the isotonic chain of N=82 are investigated in the density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory in comparison with other commonly used mean field models such as Skyrme HF, Gogny HFB and density dependent relativistic Hartree model (DDRMF). The pairing is treated in the BCS scheme, except for Gogny HFB. It is pointed out that DDRHF reproduces well characteristic features of experimental $Z$-dependence of both spin orbital and pseudo-spin orbital splittings around the sub-shell closure Z=64. Non-local exchange terms of the isoscalar $σ$ and $ω$ couplings play dominant roles in the enhancements of the spin-orbit splitting of proton 2d states, which is the key ingredient to give the Z=64 sub-shell closure properly. On the other hand, the $π$ and $ρ$ tensor contributions for the spin-orbit splitting cancel each other and the net effect becomes rather small. The enhancement of the sub-shell gaps towards Z=64 is studied by the DDRHF, for which the local terms of the scalar and vector meson couplings are found to be important.

## Key claim

The density dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory reproduces the experimental enhancement of the Z=64 sub-shell gap along the N=82 isotonic chain, but only when non-local exchange terms from isoscalar sigma and omega meson couplings are included; local mean-field models (DDRMF, Skyrme HF) fail to reproduce this feature.

## Method

Proton single-particle energies and sub-shell gaps were calculated along the N=82 isotonic chain using DDRHF with the PKA1 interaction, and compared with Gogny HFB (D1S), Skyrme HF (SLy4), and DDRMF (PKDD). Pairing was treated with BCS approximation (except Gogny HFB which used Bogoliubov transformation), and the spherical Dirac Hartree-Fock equation was solved to decompose contributions from local (direct) and non-local (exchange) terms of the sigma, omega, rho, and pion couplings.

## Key numbers

- The experimental spin-orbit splitting of proton 2d states shows an enhancement of approximately 1 MeV towards Z=65; DDRHF reproduces an enhancement of approximately 0.6 MeV, while Gogny and DDRMF give approximately 0.2 MeV.
- The experimental sub-shell gap (energy difference between pi2d5/2 and pi1h11/2) increases from 2.0 MeV at Z=61 to 2.4 MeV at Z=65 along the N=83 isotonic chain.
- DDRHF reproduces a sub-shell gap enhancement of approximately 0.5 MeV towards Z=64, compared to the experimental enhancement of approximately 1 MeV.
- Pseudo-spin orbital splittings for the pi2p-tilde doublet are less than 1 MeV energy difference across all models; DDRMF produces a violation of pseudo-spin symmetry of approximately 3 MeV for the pi1f-tilde doublet.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies showing that the relativistic mean field (RMF) model fails to describe the Z=64 sub-shell closure, producing instead an unphysical shell closure at Z=58 (Geng 2006, Long 2007). The DDRHF model, which includes Fock terms, was previously shown to eliminate these unphysical shell closures (Long 2007, Long 2008). The results enable systematic study of how non-local exchange terms, particularly from isoscalar sigma and omega couplings, govern spin-orbit splitting evolution, while showing that pion and rho tensor contributions largely cancel each other.
