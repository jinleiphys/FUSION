# Vortex rings in fragmentation regions in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39 GeV

> Vorticity generated in heavy-ion collisions at energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39 GeV is studied. Simulations are performed within a model of the three-fluid dynamics. A peculiar structure consisting of two vortex rings is found: one ring in the target fragmentation region and another one in the projectile fragmentation region. These rings are also formed in central collisions. The matter rotation is opposite in this two rings. These vortex rings are already formed at the early stage of the collision together with primordial fragmentation regions. The average vorticity, responsible for the global polarization of the observed $Λ$ and $\barΛ$, is also studied. In the semi-central collisions the average vorticity in the midrapidity region turns out to be more than an order of magnitude lower than the total one. The total vorticity is dominated by the contributions of the fragmentation regions and is produced because of asymmetry of the vortex rings in noncentral collisions. This suggests that in semi-central collisions the global polarization in the fragmentation regions should be at least an order of magnitude higher than that observed by the STAR collaboration in the midrapidity. This polarization should be asymmetrical in the reaction plain and correlate with the corresponding directed flow.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in semi-central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV, the average vorticity in the midrapidity region is more than an order of magnitude lower than the total vorticity, because the total vorticity is dominated by contributions from the fragmentation regions where two vortex rings form; the authors hedge that this suggests global polarization in the fragmentation regions should be at least an order of magnitude higher than that observed by STAR in the midrapidity, but this is a prediction rather than a measurement.

## Method

The authors performed simulations within the model of three-fluid dynamics (3FD), which treats the projectile and target baryon-rich fluids and a fireball fluid of newly produced particles, each governed by conventional hydrodynamic equations coupled by friction terms. They analyzed the time evolution of the relativistic kinematic vorticity and thermal vorticity in the reaction plane for semi-central (b = 6 fm) and central (b = 2 fm) Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV, using both a first-order-phase-transition equation of state and a crossover equation of state. The vorticity was weighted by proper energy density and averaged over the whole system or over a central region to compare fragmentation and midrapidity contributions.

## Key numbers

- The total angular momentum is conserved with an accuracy of 1% in the numerical scheme.
- At t > 2 fm/c, the average vorticity in the central region is more than an order of magnitude lower than the total vorticity.
- The proper baryon density in the central region at t = 1 fm/c is approximately nB/n0 ≈ 10, where n0 = 0.15 fm−3 is the normal nuclear density.
- The proper energy density in the central region at t = 1 fm/c is approximately ε ≈ 10 GeV/fm3.
- The angular momentum of the newly produced fireball fluid is almost two orders of magnitude lower than that of the overlapped baryon-rich fluids at the final stage of the collision.
- The evolution time of the fragmentation regions in the c.m. frame lasts approximately 30 fm/c for the first-order-transition scenario and approximately 25 fm/c for the crossover scenario.
- The local baryon-fireball relative velocity is vfB ≲ 0.2 at t ≳ 1 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions by Ivanov (2017) and Baznat (2013, 2015), which found that vorticity is predominantly localized at the boundary between participants and spectators and that a toroidal "femto-vortex sheet" forms at lower energies. At √sNN = 39 GeV, this sheet splits into two distinct vortex rings, one in each fragmentation region, which are also formed in central collisions. The results suggest that experimental measurements of Λ and Λ̄ polarization in the fragmentation regions, potentially accessible in fixed-target mode at the LHC as proposed by Brodsky (2012), should show polarization at least an order of magnitude higher than the midrapidity values observed by STAR (2017), with asymmetry in the reaction plane correlating with directed flow.
