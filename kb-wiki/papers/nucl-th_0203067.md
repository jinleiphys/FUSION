# Direct photons from relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS and at RHIC

> Assuming QGP as the initial state, we have analyzed the direct photon data, obtained by the WA98 collaboration, in 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS. It was shown, that for small thermalisation time, two loop rate contribute substantially to high $p_T$ photons. We argue that for extremely short thermalisation time scale, the higher loop contribution should not be neglected. For thermalisation time 0.4 fm or greater, when higher loop contribution are not substantial, the initial temperature of the QGP is not large and the system does not produce enough hard $p_T$ photons to fit the WA98 experiment. For initial time in the ranges of 0.4-1.0 fm, WA98 data could be fitted only if the fluid has initial radial velocity in the range of 0.3-0.5c. The model was applied to predict photon spectrum at RHIC energy.

# Direct photons from relativistic heavy ion collisions at CERN SPS and at RHIC

## Key claim
The WA98 single photon data from 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions can be fitted by a QGP initial state only if the thermalisation time is at least 0.4 fm and the fluid possesses an initial radial velocity in the range of 0.3-0.5c; for thermalisation times below 0.4 fm, neglected higher-loop contributions to the photon production rate become substantial and the results cannot be relied upon.

## Method
The authors solved relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a baryon-free, boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric QGP fluid with an equation of state using a bag constant obtained from the Gibbs condition at a critical temperature of 180 MeV and a freeze-out temperature of 100 MeV. Photon production from the QGP was computed using one-loop and two-loop rates from Kapusta et al. (1991) and Aurenche et al. (1998), with the two-loop Bremsstrahlung rate corrected by a factor of 4; hadronic photon contributions from processes such as pi pi -> rho gamma and pi rho -> pi gamma were included. The initial temperature was related to the observed pion multiplicity via isentropic expansion, and three different initial radial velocity profiles (Woods-Saxon, linearly increasing, and surface peaked) were tested against the WA98 data.

## Key numbers
- For initial time tau_i = 0.2 fm, the initial QGP temperature is 341 MeV; for tau_i = 0.4 fm, T_i = 271 MeV; for tau_i = 0.6 fm, T_i = 237 MeV; for tau_i = 0.8 fm, T_i = 215 MeV; for tau_i = 1.0 fm, T_i = 200 MeV (rapidity density dN/dY = 750, transverse radius 6.4 fm).
- For tau_i = 0.2 fm, the two-loop rate contributes more than 70% to high p_T photons; for tau_i >= 0.4 fm, the two-loop contribution is 25% or less.
- Without initial radial velocity, the model underestimates the WA98 high p_T data by a factor of 5 or more.
- For tau_i = 0.4 fm (T_i = 271 MeV), a surface-peaked initial radial velocity in the range 0.3-0.4c fits the WA98 data.
- For tau_i = 1.0 fm (T_i = 200 MeV), a surface-peaked initial radial velocity in the range 0.4-0.5c fits the data.
- At RHIC energy (Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 130 GeV), with tau_i = 0.5 fm and T_i = 300 MeV, thermal photons dominate for p_T < 2 GeV, hard QCD photons dominate for p_T > 4 GeV, and both contribute comparably in the intermediate range 2-4 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the WA98 collaboration (Aggarwal et al., 2000) single photon data, confirming the finding of Peressounko and Pokrovsky (2000) that an initial radial velocity is required to fit the data in a QGP scenario. It contrasts with the conclusions of Gallmeister et al. (2000) and Dumitru et al. (2001), who argued that prompt QCD photons alone could explain the high p_T data, and with Srivastava and Sinha (2000) and Alam et al. (2001), who found that direct QCD photons describe the data only within a factor of 3-8. The paper provides predictions for RHIC energy Au+Au collisions and emphasizes the need for pp collision data at RHIC to disentangle thermal from hard QCD contributions.
