# Probing neutron star superfluidity with gravitational-wave data

> We discuss the possibility that future gravitational-wave detectors may be able to detect various modes of oscillation of old, cold neutron stars. We argue that such detections would provide unique insights into the superfluid nature of neutron star cores, and could also lead to a much improved understanding of pulsar glitches. Our estimates are based on a detector configuration with several narrowbanded (cryogenic) interferometers operating as a "xylophone" which could lead to high sensitivity at high frequencies. We also draw on recent advances in our understanding of the dynamics of pulsating superfluid neutron star cores.

## Key claim

Future gravitational-wave detectors with sensitivity beyond advanced LIGO, specifically a "xylophone" configuration of narrowbanded cryogenic interferometers, could detect oscillation modes of old, cold neutron stars and thereby constrain the parameters of neutron star superfluidity (such as the effective proton mass ratio) and shed light on the pulsar glitch mechanism, but only if the mode excitation energy following a glitch is comparable to the spin-up energy release.

## Method

The authors performed fully relativistic calculations of the frequency and gravitational-wave damping rate of oscillation modes in a two-fluid superfluid neutron star core model, using a non-rotating stellar model with a simple two-polytrope equation of state. They then estimated the gravitational-wave signal-to-noise ratio for these modes by assuming that a glitch event deposits energy comparable to the spin-up energy into core oscillations, and convolving the resulting damped sinusoidal waveforms with the noise curves of a proposed advanced EURO detector in two configurations (with and without photon shot-noise limitation).

## Key numbers

- Model star mass: 1.36 solar masses, radius 7.9 km
- f-mode frequency: 3.29 kHz, damping time 0.092 s
- p1-mode frequency: 7.34 kHz, damping time 1.01 s
- s0 superfluid mode frequency: 3.76 kHz, damping time 15.75 s
- s1 superfluid mode frequency: 8.49 kHz, damping time 1.29 s
- Signal-to-noise ratio for f-mode with EURO xylophone configuration: 300 to 4.7 x 10^3 (Vela to Crab glitch scaling)
- Signal-to-noise ratio for s0 mode with EURO xylophone: 350 to 5.4 x 10^3
- Crab pulsar: period 33 ms, distance 2 kpc, fractional spin-up 10^-8, energy release 2 x 10^-13 solar masses c^2
- Vela pulsar: period 89 ms, distance 0.5 kpc, fractional spin-up 10^-6, energy release 3 x 10^-12 solar masses c^2
- Estimated effective proton mass ratio range: 0.3 to 0.8

## Context

This work extends the program of gravitational-wave asteroseismology (Andersson and Kokkotas, 1996; Kokkotas, Apostolatos and Andersson, 2001) by incorporating the two-fluid superfluid dynamics of neutron star cores, building on the relativistic calculations of Comer, Langlois and Lin (1999). It contrasts with the standard glitch model (Alpar et al., 1981) which relies on vortex pinning in the inner crust, and with cooling-based probes of superfluidity (Page et al., 2000). The results enable a future observational test of superfluid parameters (especially the entrainment-dependent effective proton mass) and could distinguish between normal-fluid and superfluid mode families, while also constraining the glitch trigger mechanism through the multipolar structure of excited modes.
