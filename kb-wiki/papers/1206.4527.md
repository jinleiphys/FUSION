# Nucleon Scalar and Tensor Charges from Lattice QCD with Light Wilson Quarks

> We present 2+1 flavor Lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon scalar and tensor charges. Using the BMW clover-improved Wilson action with pion masses between 150 and 350 MeV and three source-sink separations between 0.9 and 1.4 fm, we achieve good control over excited-state contamination and extrapolation to the physical pion mass. As a consistency check, we also present results from calculations using unitary domain wall fermions with pion masses between 300 and 400 MeV, and using domain wall valence quarks and staggered sea quarks with pion masses between 300 and 600 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon isovector scalar charge \(g_S\) and tensor charge \(g_T\) with good control over excited-state contamination and chiral extrapolation, but the authors caution that future more statistically accurate calculations may yet necessitate more detailed investigations of volume and lattice spacing effects, and that systematic errors may still be appreciable.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon forward matrix elements using the ratio-plateau method on three sets of lattice ensembles: Wilson-clover fermions (pion masses 149-356 MeV), domain wall fermions (297-403 MeV), and mixed-action (domain wall valence on staggered sea, 293-597 MeV). Operators are renormalized nonperturbatively using the Rome-Southampton method for the Wilson and domain wall ensembles, and with one-loop perturbation theory for the mixed-action ensembles. For the primary Wilson-clover data, chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass is performed using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for \(g_S\) and a formula including the leading non-analytic term for \(g_T\), with the crucial 149 MeV ensemble nearly eliminating extrapolation uncertainty.

## Key numbers

*   \(g_S = 1.08 \pm 0.28 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.16 \text{ (syst)}\) at \(\overline{MS}(2\text{ GeV})\) from the Wilson-clover data.
*   \(g_T = 1.038 \pm 0.011 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.012 \text{ (syst)}\) at \(\overline{MS}(2\text{ GeV})\) from the Wilson-clover data.
*   The lowest pion mass used in the primary analysis is 149 MeV (ensemble W2), with \(m_\pi L_x a = 4.2\).
*   Three source-sink separations were used on Wilson-clover ensembles: 0.93 fm, 1.16 fm, and 1.39 fm (for \(a=0.116\) fm: \(T/a = 8, 10, 12\)).
*   The systematic error from excited-state contamination was estimated as the average of absolute differences between the middle separation and the other two separations on the 149 MeV ensemble.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure, such as those by Bratt et al. (2010) and Syritsyn et al. (2009), by using a significantly lower pion mass (149 MeV) and multiple source-sink separations to control excited-state contamination. The results are directly relevant to searches for new scalar and tensor couplings beyond the Standard Model in neutron decay, as discussed by Bhattacharya et al. (2011), and to calculations of the CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar{g}_{\pi NN}\) relevant for the neutron electric dipole moment. The scalar charge results also contribute to understanding the nucleon sigma term and dark matter searches.
