# Thermodynamic Geometry of the Quark-Meson Model

> We study the thermodynamic geometry of the Quark-Meson model, focusing on the curvature, $R$, around the chiral crossover at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential. We find a peculiar behavior of $R$ in the crossover region, in which the sign changes and a local maximum develops; in particular, the height of the peak of $R$ in the crossover region becomes large in proximity of the critical endpoint and diverges at the critical endpoint. The appearance of a pronounced peak of $R$ close to the critical endpoint supports the idea that $R$ grows with the correlation volume around the phase transition. We also analyze the mixed fluctuations of energy and baryon number, $\langleΔUΔN\rangle$, which grow up substantially in proximity of the critical endpoint: in the language of thermodynamic geometry these fluctuations are responsible for the vanishing of the determinant of the metric, which results in thermodynamic instability and are thus related to the appearance of the second order phase transition at the critical endpoint.

## Key claim

The thermodynamic curvature $R$ of the Quark-Meson model changes sign and develops a local maximum in the chiral crossover region; the height of this peak grows as the chemical potential increases and diverges at the critical endpoint, supporting the idea that $|R|$ measures the correlation volume around the phase transition, though the authors note that the physical meaning of the sign change is unclear because the interaction is always attractive.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic curvature $R$ from the grand-canonical partition function of the renormalized Quark-Meson model at finite temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $\mu$, using the metric tensor $g_{ij} = \partial^2 \phi / \partial \beta^i \partial \beta^j$ where $\phi = \beta P$ and $(\beta^1, \beta^2) = (1/T, -\mu/T)$. The constituent quark mass $M(T,\mu)$ is obtained self-consistently by minimizing the mean-field effective potential, and $R$ is evaluated from second and third order cumulants of energy and baryon number fluctuations along the chiral crossover line.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass at $T=0$ and $\mu=0$: $M = 335$ MeV (for parameter set with $M_\sigma=700$ MeV, $F_\pi=93$ MeV, $M_\pi=138$ MeV, $G=3.6$)
* Critical endpoint location: $(T_E, \mu_E) \approx (30\ \mathrm{MeV}, 360\ \mathrm{MeV})$
* Pion decay constant: $F_\pi \approx 93$ MeV
* Pion mass: $M_\pi = 138$ MeV
* Sigma meson mass: $M_\sigma = 700$ MeV
* Coupling constant: $\lambda = M_\sigma^2 / 2F_\pi^2 = 28.3$
* Explicit symmetry breaking parameter: $h = M_\pi^2 F_\pi = 1.78 \times 10^6\ \mathrm{MeV}^3$
* The two critical temperatures $T_{c1}$ (from peak of $|dM/dT|$) and $T_{c2}$ (from positive peak of $R$) differ by "few percent at most" along the crossover line

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic geometry analysis previously applied to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Castorina (2019) to the renormalizable Quark-Meson model, which removes the dependence on an ultraviolet cutoff present in NJL calculations. The results support the general expectation from Ruppeiner's framework that $|R| \propto \xi^3$ near a second-order phase transition, and show that the mixed energy-baryon number fluctuation $\langle \Delta U \Delta N \rangle$ is responsible for the vanishing of the metric determinant at the critical endpoint. The study enables future investigations of how repulsive vector interactions affect the sign of $R$, and provides a quantitative comparison of model-dependent predictions for the QCD phase diagram.
