# Determination of electric dipole transitions in heavy quarkonia using potential non-relativistic QCD

> The electric dipole transitions $χ_{bJ}(1P)\to γΥ(1S)$ with $J=0,1,2$ and $h_{b}(1P)\to γη_{b}(1S)$ are computed using the weak-coupling version of a low-energy effective field theory named potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD). In order to improve convergence and thus give firm predictions for the studied reactions, the full static potential is incorporated into the leading order Hamiltonian; moreover, we must handle properly renormalon effects and re-summation of large logarithms. The precision we reach is $k_γ^{3}/(mv)^{2} \times \mathcal{O}(v^{2})$, where $k_γ$ is the photon energy, $m$ is the mass of the heavy quark and $v$ its velocity. Our analysis separates those relativistic contributions that account for the electromagnetic interaction terms in the pNRQCD Lagrangian which are $v^{2}$ suppressed and those that account for wave function corrections of relative order $v^{2}$. Among the last ones, corrections from $1/m$ and $1/m^2$ potentials are computed, but not those coming from higher Fock states since they demand non-perturbative input and are $Λ_{\text{QCD}}^{2}/(mv)^{2}$ or $Λ_{\text{QCD}}^{3}/(m^{3}v^{4})$ suppressed, at least, in the strict weak coupling regime. ## Key claim

The paper computes electric dipole transitions in bottomonium using weak-coupling pNRQCD, achieving precision at order \(k_{\gamma}^{3}/(mv)^{2} \times \mathcal{O}(v^{2})\), but explicitly excludes corrections from higher Fock states because they require non-perturbative input and are suppressed by \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^{2}/(mv)^{2}\) or \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^{3}/(m^{3}v^{4})\) in the strict weak-coupling regime.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrodinger equation with a leading-order Hamiltonian that includes the full static potential up to NNNLO (\(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^4)\)), incorporating renormalon cancellation and renormalization-group-improved (RGI) short-distance behavior. They compute decay rates using expressions from Brambilla et al. (2012) that separate relativistic corrections from higher-order electromagnetic operators (suppressed by \(v^2\)) from wave-function corrections due to \(1/m\) and \(1/m^2\) potentials, while omitting higher Fock state contributions.

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma(\chi_{b0}(1P)\to \gamma\Upsilon(1S)) = 28^{+2}_{-1}~\text{keV}\)
- \(\Gamma(\chi_{b1}(1P)\to \gamma\Upsilon(1S)) = 37^{+2}_{-1}~\text{keV}\)
- \(\Gamma(\chi_{b2}(1P)\to \gamma\Upsilon(1S)) = 45^{+1}_{-1}~\text{keV}\)
- \(\Gamma(h_b(1P)\to \gamma\eta_b(1S)) = 63^{+1}_{-1}~\text{keV}\)
- Predicted total widths: \(\Gamma(\chi_{b0}(1P)) = 1.6^{+0.3}_{-0.3}~\text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma(\chi_{b1}(1P)) = 110^{+9}_{-8}~\text{keV}\), \(\Gamma(\chi_{b2}(1P)) = 234^{+15}_{-16}~\text{keV}\), \(\Gamma(h_b(1P)) = 121^{+14}_{-12}~\text{keV}\)
- The leading-order decay width for \(\chi_{b1}(1P)\to \gamma\Upsilon(1S)\) varies from \(\sim33~\text{keV}\) at \(\nu=1~\text{GeV}\) to \(\sim39~\text{keV}\) at \(\nu=3~\text{GeV}\), with final result \(\sim37~\text{keV}\) and \(\sim8\%\) relative uncertainty from scale dependence.

## Context

This work extends the pNRQCD approach previously applied to magnetic dipole (M1) transitions in Pineda (2013) to the technically more complicated case of electric dipole (E1) transitions, closing a gap left by partial calculations in Pietrulewicz (2013) and Martinez (2016). The results cannot be directly compared to experimental branching fractions from the Particle Data Group (2016) because the total widths of the \(\chi_{bJ}\) and \(h_b(1P)\) mesons are unknown, but the predicted total width for \(\chi_{b0}(1P)\) is consistent with the Belle collaboration's upper limit of \(<2.4~\text{MeV}\) at 90% confidence level.
