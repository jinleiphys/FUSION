## Key claim

The paper presents a coupled-channel model that predicts cross sections for $J/\Psi$ production on nucleon and deuteron targets, with the key condition that the $J/\Psi$-$N$ interaction is parameterized as a Yukawa potential whose strength must be determined by future experiments, as the model's predictions depend strongly on the chosen potential strength parameter $\alpha$.

## Method

A coupled-channel model with $\pi N$, $\rho N$, and $J/\Psi N$ channels was constructed, where the $J/\Psi$-$N$ interaction was parameterized as a Yukawa potential $v_{J/\Psi N, J/\Psi N}(r) = -\alpha e^{-\mu_0 r}/r$ with $\mu_0 = 0.60$ GeV. The transition potentials $v_{\pi N, J/\Psi N}$ and $v_{\rho N, J/\Psi N}$ were calculated using one-$\rho$ and one-$\pi$ exchange mechanisms, respectively, with the coupling constant $g_{J/\Psi,\pi\rho}=0.032$ determined from the $J/\Psi \rightarrow \rho\pi$ decay width. The phenomenological potentials for $\pi N$ and $\rho N$ channels were constrained by fitting total cross section data of $\pi N$ reactions and $\gamma p \rightarrow \rho^0 p$ data via vector meson dominance. For deuteron targets, the calculations included impulse terms and one-loop calculations of final $NN$ and $J/\Psi N$ re-scattering effects, with $NN$ amplitudes from the Bonn potential.

## Key numbers

- $g_{J/\Psi,\pi\rho}=0.032$: the coupling constant determined from the $J/\Psi \rightarrow \rho\pi$ decay width
- $\mu_0 = 0.60$ GeV: the range parameter for the Yukawa potential $v_{J/\Psi N, J/\Psi N}(r) = -\alpha e^{-\mu_0 r}/r$
- $c=0.8$ fm and $t=0.4$ fm: the parameters for the Woods-Saxon form factor $f_{i,j}(r)=1/(1+e^{(r-c)/t})$ used for phenomenological $\pi N$ and $\rho N$ potentials
- $f_{J/\Psi}=11.2$ and $f_\rho=5.33$: the vector meson decay constants used in the vector meson dominance model for photo-production
- $\alpha_0=1.25$ and $\alpha'_P=0.25$ GeV$^{-1}$: the Regge trajectory parameters for the Pomeron exchange amplitude in $\gamma + N \rightarrow J/\Psi + N$
- $\beta_c=0.84$ GeV$^{-1}$: the coupling constant in the Pomeron exchange amplitude

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of $J/\Psi$-$N$ interactions previously studied using effective field theory (Peskin 1979, Luke et al. 1992, Brodsky et al.) and Lattice QCD (LQCD), as well as a Pomeron-quark coupling model (Brodsky et al. 1990) that found strong attraction. The authors build upon their own earlier work (Wu and Lee) to develop a more complete coupled-channel framework that includes $\pi N$, $\rho N$, and $J/\Psi N$ channels, enabling predictions for multiple reactions ($\pi^- + p \rightarrow J/\Psi + n$, $\gamma + d \rightarrow J/\Psi + n + p$, $\pi^+ + d \rightarrow J/\Psi + p + p$) whose experimental measurement could determine the $J/\Psi$-$N$ interaction strength. The results enable future experimental tests at facilities such as Jefferson Lab (JLab) to distinguish between different theoretical predictions for the $J/\Psi$-$N$ potential, which is a necessary step toward searching for possible nuclear bound states with hidden charm.
