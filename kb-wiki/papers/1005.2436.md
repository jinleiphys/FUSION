# Comment on "Precise half-life values for two-neutrino double beta decay"

> The results by A.S. Barabash [Phys. Rev. {\bf C 81}, 035501 (2010)] are shown to be incomplete. Tellurium data sets were reanalyzed using the best practices of ENSDF evaluations. Present analysis indicates much higher value for the 2$ν$-mode half-life time in $^{128}$Te and the corresponding ratio of $^{128,130}$Te half-lives. These values imply the $T^{2 ν}_{1/2}$ $\sim$ $\frac{1}{E^{8}}$ $ββ$-decay transition energy trend that is consistent with the two-nucleon mechanism of $ββ$(2$ν$)-decay.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the recommended half-life for the two-neutrino double-beta decay mode of \(^{128}\)Te derived by Barabash (2010) is underestimated because the analysis selectively rejected legitimate geochemical data sets, contradicting standard nuclear structure evaluation practices. The corrected analysis yields a higher half-life and a ratio of \(^{128,130}\)Te half-lives that follows a \(T^{2\nu}_{1/2} \sim 1/E^{8}\) trend, consistent with the two-nucleon mechanism.

## Method
The author reanalyzed the tellurium double-beta decay data sets using the evaluation procedures of the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF), including all available experimental results (both direct and geochemical) without selective rejection of discrepant data sets. The analysis applied standard statistical procedures (e.g., the LWEIGHT code) to deduce evaluated half-lives, and compared the resulting \(^{128,130}\)Te half-life ratio to the Primakoff and Rosen (1969) formalism.

## Key numbers
- NNDC \(^{128}\)Te half-life: \((3.5 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{24}\) y, compared to Barabash (2010) value of \((1.9 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{24}\) y.
- Ratio of \(^{128,130}\)Te half-lives: \(T_{1/2}^{2\nu}(^{128}\text{Te}) / T_{1/2}^{2\nu}(^{130}\text{Te}) \approx 5.7 \times 10^{3}\), consistent with a \(\sim 1/E^{8}\) dependence on transition energy.
- The observed energy dependence agrees with the Primakoff and Rosen (1969) prediction of \(\sim 1/E^{8.4}\).

## Context
This comment extends the critique of Barabash (2010) by applying ENSDF evaluation best practices, which require inclusion of all discrepant data sets rather than selective rejection. The work builds on the NNDC double-beta decay database (Pritychenko, 2008) and the Primakoff and Rosen (1969) formalism for two-nucleon double-beta decay. The corrected tellurium half-lives enable a more reliable test of the two-nucleon mechanism and the energy dependence of two-neutrino double-beta decay.
