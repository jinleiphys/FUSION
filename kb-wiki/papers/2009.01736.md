# Jet quenching in the hadron gas: An exploratory study

> In most calculations of hard particle suppression in heavy-ion reactions the hadronic stage has been neglected due to formation time arguments. Most of the hard particle shower exits the hot and dense medium before the system enters the hadronic evolution. In this contribution, a first assessment within the hadronic transport approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) of rescattering effects on hard particles is presented. In particular, it is shown that the hadronic energy loss depends on the particle species as well as the energy of the probe. A parametrization for the $\langle \tilde{q} \rangle$ parameter as a function of temperature and particle energy is given for pions. Overall, major effects of the hadronic stage are expected in the transverse momentum range from 2-10 GeV and therefore jet sub-structure analysis and (hard-soft) correlation observables might be affected.

# Jet quenching in the hadron gas: An exploratory study

## Key claim
Hadronic rescattering effects on hard particles, previously neglected in most jet quenching calculations due to formation time arguments, can produce significant energy loss in the transverse momentum range of 2-10 GeV, with the magnitude depending on particle species and probe energy. The extracted hadronic transport coefficient \(\tilde{q}\) is on the order of 20-30% of typical \(\hat{q}\) values reported from partonic calculations.

## Method
The hadronic transport approach SMASH-1.6 was used to simulate hard particles (pions, protons, kaons) propagating through an expanding sphere of hadronic matter and through infinite matter in a box with periodic boundary conditions at fixed temperatures. The mean free path and average transverse momentum transfer from the first interaction were extracted from the simulations, and the quantity \(\tilde{q} = q_\perp^2 / \lambda_{\rm{mfp}}\) was computed as a function of temperature and probe energy for pions.

## Key numbers
- For a pion with \(p_T = 10\) GeV in an expanding sphere, significant broadening of the angular distribution is observed already for a small cross-section of \(\sigma = 10\) mb.
- A pion gas with fixed cross-section of \(\sigma = 100\) mb corresponds roughly to a full hadron gas at the same temperature of \(T = 150\) MeV.
- The extracted values of \(\tilde{q}\) are on the order of 20-30% of the typical values for \(\hat{q}\) reported from partonic calculations.
- Major effects of the hadronic stage are expected in the transverse momentum range from 2-10 GeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies using hadronic transport approaches HSD and UrQMD (Cassing, 2003; da Silva, 2020) that indicated significant modification of the transverse momentum spectrum in the range \(p_T = 2-10\) GeV by hadronic scattering. It provides a first assessment within the SMASH framework and offers a parametrization of \(\tilde{q}\) as a function of temperature and particle energy for pions, ready to be used within a probabilistic energy loss approach in the hadronic stage when described by hydrodynamics. The results enable future investigations of jet substructure and hard-soft correlation observables, and raise the open question of how to address interactions of partons with hadrons in a hadronic environment, relevant for future EIC studies.
