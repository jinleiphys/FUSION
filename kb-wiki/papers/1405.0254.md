# Finite Amplitude Method for Charge-Changing Transitions in Axially-Deformed Nuclei

> We describe and apply a version of the finite amplitude method for obtaining the charge-changing nuclear response in the quasiparticle random phase approximation. The method is suitable for calculating strength functions and beta-decay rates, both allowed and forbidden, in axially-deformed open-shell nuclei. We demonstrate the speed and versatility of the code through a preliminary examination of the effects of tensor terms in Skyrme functionals on beta decay in a set of spherical and deformed open-shell nuclei. Like the isoscalar pairing interaction, the tensor terms systematically increase allowed beta-decay rates. This finding generalizes previous work in semimagic nuclei and points to the need for a comprehensive study of time-odd terms in nuclear density functionals.

## Key claim

The finite amplitude method (FAM) can be extended to charge-changing QRPA transitions in axially-deformed nuclei, enabling efficient calculation of beta-decay strength functions and half-lives without constructing the full QRPA matrix. The tensor terms in Skyrme functionals systematically increase allowed beta-decay rates in both spherical and deformed open-shell nuclei, but the effect is more complicated in deformed systems where half-lives may become too short.

## Method

The authors implemented a proton-neutron finite amplitude method (pnFAM) that solves the linearized time-dependent HFB equations in the complex-frequency plane, avoiding construction of the large QRPA A and B matrices. Starting from an axially-deformed HFB calculation using the hfbtho code, they compute the charge-changing response by numerically evaluating functional derivatives of the Skyrme energy density functional with respect to the perturbation. Beta-decay half-lives are obtained through contour integration of the FAM response functions, using polynomial approximations to the phase-space integrals and including rotational energy corrections via the Belyaev formula.

## Key numbers

- The constant for superallowed decay: κ = 6147.0 ± 2.4 s
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: ρ_c = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Neutron-hydrogen mass difference: ΔM_n-H = 0.78227 MeV
- Tensor interaction parameters used: t_e = 184.567 MeV fm⁵, t_o = -108.567 MeV fm⁵
- Axial-vector coupling constant quenched to g_A = 1.0 (rather than 1.27)
- 16 harmonic oscillator shells sufficient for convergence of low-energy strength functions
- 10th-order polynomial approximation sufficient for allowed-decay Fermi integrals
- Time-odd coupling constant C^s_1 set to 159 MeV fm³ to reproduce Gamow-Teller resonance energy in ²⁰⁸Pb
- Isoscalar pairing strength approximately 60% of isovector interaction strength used with SkO′ functional

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method, originally proposed by Nakatsukasa (2007) for RPA and generalized to QRPA by Avogadro (2011), to charge-changing transitions in deformed nuclei. It builds on previous matrix pnQRPA work by Mustonen (2013) and Terasaki (2011), which was computationally prohibitive for deformed systems. The study generalizes findings by Minato and Bai (2013) on tensor interaction effects in semi-magic nuclei to open-shell and deformed systems, and enables future r-process studies in the rare-earth region, evaluation of neutrino-capture rates, and better determination of time-odd parts of nuclear density functionals.
