# Spin-polarized phases of $^3P_2$ superfluids in neutron stars

> The interior of a neutron star is expected to be occupied by a neutron $^3P_2$ superfluid, which is the condensate of spin-triplet $p$-wave Cooper pairs of neutrons with total angular momentum $J=2$. Here we investigate the thermodynamic stability of $^3P_2$ superfluids in a neutron-star interior under a strong magnetic field. Using the theory incorporating the finite size correction of neutron Fermi surface, we show that the spin-polarized phases of $^3P_2$ superfluids, the magnetized biaxial nematic phase and the ferromagnetic phase, appear in high temperatures and high magnetic fields. These phases were missed in the previous studies using the quasiclassical approximation in which dispersions of neutrons are linearized around the Fermi surface. In particular, the ferromagnetic phase, which is the condensation of Cooper-paired neutrons with fully polarized spins, appears between the normal phase and the biaxial nematic phase and enlarge the thermodynamic stability of $^3P_2$ superfluids under strong magnetic fields. Furthermore, we present the augmented Ginzburg-Landau theory that incorporates the thermodynamic stability of spin-polarized $^3P_2$ superfluid phases.

## Key claim

Using a theory that incorporates the finite-size correction of the neutron Fermi surface (beyond the quasiclassical approximation which linearizes dispersions around the Fermi surface), the authors show that spin-polarized phases of \(^3P_2\) superfluids, the magnetized biaxial nematic phase and the ferromagnetic phase, appear at high temperatures and high magnetic fields, phases that were missed in previous studies using the quasiclassical approximation.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of the Luttinger-Ward thermodynamic potential for neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids without the quasiclassical approximation, incorporating the finite-size correction of the neutron Fermi surface. They solved the self-consistent equations for the gap, particle density, and magnetization density to determine the equilibrium state, using a \(3\times 3\) traceless symmetric tensor order parameter \(\mathcal{A}_{\mu i}\) with three real variational parameters \((\Delta, r, \kappa)\) to describe the order parameter under a magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis. The thermodynamic stability of each phase was determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential \(\Omega(T, \mu, B)\).

## Key numbers

* The ferromagnetic phase appears between the normal phase and the biaxial nematic phase at high temperatures and strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars, where the magnetic field is \(B = 10^{15}\)–\(10^{18}~\text{G}\).
* The superfluid critical temperature is about \(T_{\text{c}} \sim 10^{9}~\text{K}\).
* The Zeeman splitting of the neutron Fermi surface amounts to the critical field of the Pauli depairing of \(^1S_0\) Cooper pairs (\(\sim k_{\text{B}} T_{\text{c}}\)) or more.
* The gyromagnetic ratio for a neutron is \(\gamma_{\text{n}} = 1.2 \times 10^{-13}~\text{MeV/T}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(^3P_2\) superfluids in neutron stars that employed the quasiclassical approximation (Mizushima 2016, Masuda 2015, Masuda 2016) and Ginzburg-Landau theory (Yasui 2018, Yasui 2019), which could not properly incorporate the Zeeman splitting of the neutron Fermi surface. The results show that the ferromagnetic phase, a condensation of Cooper-paired neutrons with fully polarized spins, enlarges the thermodynamic stability of \(^3P_2\) superfluids under strong magnetic fields, which is relevant for understanding magnetars. The augmented Ginzburg-Landau theory presented here enables future studies of topological excitations, such as Abelian and non-Abelian fractional vortices, in these spin-polarized phases.
