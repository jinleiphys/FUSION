# Towards a new quark-nuclear matter EoS for applications in astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions

> The aim of our work is to develop a unified equation of state (EoS) for nuclear and quark matter for a wide range in temperature, density and isospin so that it becomes applicable for heavy-ion collisions as well as for the astrophysics of neutron stars, their mergers and supernova explosions. As a first step, we use improved EoS for the hadronic and quark matter phases and join them via Maxwell construction. We discuss the limitations of a 2-phase description and outline steps beyond it, towards the formulation of a unified quark-nuclear matter EoS on a more fundamental level by a cluster virial expansion.

# Towards a new quark-nuclear matter EoS for applications in astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The authors present a hybrid equation of state (EoS) for nuclear and quark matter constructed by joining a hadronic EoS (DD2) with a higher-order Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (hNJL) quark model EoS via Maxwell construction, but they acknowledge that this 2-phase approach suffers from an unphysical mismatch between the chiral transition (which occurs within the quark model) and the deconfinement transition (which is imposed by the construction), particularly at finite temperatures above 55 MeV where no hadronic matter exists in the model.

## Method
The hadronic phase is described using the density-dependent relativistic meanfield theory parametrisation DD2, which reproduces nuclear matter behavior near saturation density. The quark phase uses the higher-order NJL (hNJL) model, extended to finite temperature via the grand potential density with an effective potential and renormalized chemical potential. A bag pressure B is introduced to cure the absence of confinement in the hNJL model, and the two phases are joined via Maxwell construction (Gibbs conditions of phase equilibrium) to produce a hybrid EoS.

## Key numbers
- The hadronic DD2 EoS is used for temperatures up to 50 MeV, above which additional hadrons (pions, kaons) should be included.
- The hNJL model parameters are set to eta_02 = 0.08 and eta_04 = 5.0 for the isospin symmetric case.
- Bag pressures of B = 0, B = 160 MeV/fm^3, and B = 200 MeV/fm^3 are tested, with B = 160 MeV/fm^3 and B = 200 MeV/fm^3 resulting in critical temperatures at mu_b = 0 in the range 175-180 MeV.
- At finite temperature, above T = 55 MeV no hadronic matter exists in the construction without bag pressure.
- The deconfinement phase transition occurs at much lower densities compared to the EoS used by Ivanov in three-fluid hydrodynamic simulations (Ivanov 2010, Ivanov 2015).

## Context
This work extends the 2-phase description approach previously used by Barz (1989) for the QCD phase diagram, employing modern inputs: the DD2 hadronic EoS by Typel (1999, 2009) and the hNJL model by Benic (2014) for zero temperature hybrid compact stars. The authors identify deficiencies in the 2-phase construction, particularly the mismatch between chiral and deconfinement transitions, and outline a path toward a unified quark-nuclear matter EoS based on a cluster virial expansion and the Phi-derivable approach of Baym (1961), similar in spirit to cluster developments for nuclear matter by Roepke (2012). This unified approach would address the quarkyonic phase concept of McLerran (2007) and the triple point scenario of Andronic (2009), and could be applied to heavy-ion collisions at NICA and FAIR energies as well as neutron star astrophysics.
