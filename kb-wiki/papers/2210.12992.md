# Further understanding the nature of $a_0(1710)$ in the $D^+_s \to ¤Ç^0 K^+ K^0_S$ decay

> Based on our previous work about the role of $a_0(1710)$ in the $D_s^+\to¤Ç^+K_S^0K_S^0$ decay [Phy. Rev. D 105, 116010 (2022)], we perform a further theoretical study of $a_0(1710)^+$ in the process $D^+_s \to ¤Ç^0 a_0(1710)^+ \to ¤Ç^0 K^+ K^0_S$. In addition to $a_0(1710)$, the contributions of $K^*$ and $a_0(980)$ are also taken into account. Firstly, we consider the contributions from the tree diagrams of $K^{*+} \to K^+¤Ç^0$ and $\bar{K}^{*0} \to ¤Ç^0 \bar{K}^0$. Secondly, we describe the final state interaction of $K\bar{K}$ in the chiral unitary approach to study the contribution of $a_0(980)$, while the $a_0(1710)$ state is dynamically generated from the $K^*\bar{K}^*$ interaction, and then decays into $K^+\bar{K}^0$. Since the final $K^+ K_S^0$ state is in pure isospin $I=1$, the $D_s^+\to¤Ç^0K^+K_s^0$ decay is an ideal process to study the $a_0(1710)^+$ and $a_0(980)^+$ resonances. Based on our theoretical calculations, it is found that the recent experimental measurements on the $K^+K^0_S$, $¤Ç^0K^+$, and $¤Ç^0 K_S^0$ invariant mass distributions can be well reproduced, which supports the molecular $K^*\bar{K}^*$ nature of the scalar $a_0(1710)$ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the BESIII experimental data on the $D^+_s \to \pi^0 K^+ K^0_S$ decay can be well reproduced by a model where the $a_0(1710)$ resonance is dynamically generated from $K^*\bar{K}^*$ interactions, supporting its molecular nature, but only after adjusting the production strength parameter $V_{\pi^0K^{*+}\bar{K}^{*0}}$ from the value used in their previous work on the $D^+_s \to \pi^+ K^0_S K^0_S$ decay.

## Method

The authors model the $D^+_s \to \pi^0 K^+ K^0_S$ decay by including tree-level contributions from intermediate $K^{*+}$ and $\bar{K}^{*0}$ vector mesons, the $a_0(980)$ resonance generated via $S$-wave $K\bar{K}$ final-state interactions in the chiral unitary approach, and the $a_0(1710)$ resonance dynamically generated from $S$-wave $K^*\bar{K}^*$ interactions. The total decay amplitude is the sum of these four contributions, and the double differential decay width is integrated to obtain invariant mass distributions for $K^+K^0_S$, $\pi^0K^+$, and $\pi^0K^0_S$, which are compared to BESIII data.

## Key numbers

* The BESIII fitted Breit-Wigner mass of $a_0(1710)$ is $1817 \pm 8 \pm 20$ MeV, with width $97 \pm 22 \pm 15$ MeV.
* The maximum possible $K^+\bar{K}^0$ invariant mass in this decay is $1833.36$ MeV, given $M_{D^+_s} = 1968.34$ MeV and $m_{\pi^0} = 134.98$ MeV.
* The coupling constant for $K^{*+} \to K^+\pi^0$ is $g_{K^{*+}K^{+}\pi^0} = 3.23 \pm 0.03$, determined from a partial decay width of $16.9 \pm 0.3$ MeV.
* The coupling constant for $\bar{K}^{*0} \to \bar{K}^0\pi^0$ is $g_{\bar{K}^{*0}\bar{K}^0\pi^0} = 3.13 \pm 0.02$, determined from a partial decay width of $15.8 \pm 0.2$ MeV.
* The branching ratio for $D^+_s \to \bar{K}^0 K^{*+}$ is $(5.4 \pm 1.2)\%$, and the ratio $\frac{\mathrm{Br}(D_s^+\to \bar{K}^{\ast0} K^+)}{\mathrm{Br}(D_s^+\to \bar{K}^0K^{\ast+})} = 2.35^{+0.42}_{-0.23_{\text{stat.}}} \pm 0.10_{\text{syst.}}$ from BESIII.
* The cut-off used to regularize the $K\bar{K}$ loop function is $|\vec{q}_{\rm max}| = 903$ MeV.
* The pion decay constant used in the chiral unitary approach is $f = 93$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $a_0(1710)$ in the $D_s^+ \to \pi^+ K_S^0 K_S^0$ decay (Zhu et al., 2022, Phys. Rev. D 105, 116010) to the charged channel $D^+_s \to \pi^0 K^+ K^0_S$, following the BESIII observation of $a_0(1710)^+$ in this reaction (BESIII, 2022). It supports the molecular $K^*\bar{K}^*$ interpretation of $a_0(1710)$ over alternative descriptions such as a conventional $s\bar{s}$ state (Close, 2005) or a scalar glueball (Gui, 2012; Janowski, 2014; Fariborz, 2015). The results enable further refinement of the nature of isovector scalar mesons and can be compared with the Regge trajectory analysis of Guo (2022), which suggests $a_0(980)$, $a_0(1450)$, and $a_0(1710)$ (denoted $a_0(1817)$) form a family.
