# State-dependent Jastrow correlation functions for 4He nuclei

> We calculate the ground-state energy for the nucleus 4He with V4 nucleon interactions, making use of a Jastrow description of the corresponding wavefunction with state-dependent correlation factors. The effect related to the state dependence of the correlation is quite important, lowering the upper bound for the ground-state energy by some 2 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that introducing state-dependent (spin- and isospin-dependent) Jastrow correlation functions lowers the variational upper bound for the ground-state energy of 4He by approximately 1-10% compared to state-independent correlations, depending on the nucleon-nucleon interaction used, with the precise shift being around 2 MeV for some potentials.

## Method

The authors construct a trial wavefunction for 4He as a symmetrized product of two-body Jastrow correlation factors (with V4 operator structure) acting on an antisymmetric harmonic-oscillator shell-model reference state. They evaluate the ground-state energy expectation value using a multiplicative cluster expansion up to fourth order, with the correlation functions parametrized as linear combinations of Gaussians with state-dependent coefficients, and minimize the energy with respect to the harmonic-oscillator constant, correlation lengths, and correlation depths.

## Key numbers

- For the Afnan-Tang S3 potential with one Gaussian correlation component: state-independent energy -24.4042 MeV, state-dependent energy -25.3598 MeV (shift of -0.9556 MeV)
- For the Afnan-Tang S3 potential with two Gaussian components: state-independent energy -27.2136 MeV, state-dependent energy -29.9378 MeV (shift of -2.7242 MeV)
- For the Malfliet-Tjon MT I/III potential with one Gaussian component: state-independent energy -29.0604 MeV, state-dependent energy -29.3460 MeV (shift of -0.2856 MeV)
- For the Malfliet-Tjon MT I/III potential with two Gaussian components: state-independent energy -30.8752 MeV, state-dependent energy -32.0107 MeV (shift of -1.1355 MeV)
- For the Malfliet-Tjon MT V potential (Wigner type): state-independent and state-dependent energies are identical at -29.0604 MeV (one beta) and -30.8752 MeV (two betas)
- The two-beta state-dependent result for MT V (-30.8752 MeV) is compared to Green function Monte Carlo value of (-31.3 ± 0.2) MeV and diffusion Monte Carlo energy of (-31.32 ± 0.02) MeV
- The two-beta state-dependent result for MT I/III (-32.0107 MeV) is compared to the coupled cluster eigenvalue estimate of (-33.4 ± 0.1) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Jastrow calculations for light nuclei that used state-independent correlation functions (Guardiola 1979; Boscá, Buendía, Guardiola 1987) by introducing spin- and isospin-dependent correlation factors. The results are comparable to those from the translationally invariant coupled cluster (TICI2) method (Guardiola et al. 1996) and Monte Carlo approaches (Zabolitzky and Kalos 1981; Bishop et al. 1992), with the two-beta state-dependent results lying lower than the TICI2 energies by more than 1 MeV. The multiplicative cluster expansion tested here provides a framework for extending such calculations to heavier nuclei where full evaluation becomes computationally prohibitive.
