# Electrical conductivity of Hot and Dense QCD matter at RHIC BES energies: A Color String Percolation Approach

> Recently, transport coefficients viz. shear viscosity, electrical conductivity etc. of strongly interacting matter produced in heavy-ion collisions have drawn considerable interest. We study the normalised electrical conductivity ($σ_{\rm el}$/T) of hot QCD matter as a function of temperature (T) using the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM). We also study the temperature dependence of shear viscosity and its ratio with electrical conductivity for the QCD matter. We compare CSPM estimations with various existing results and lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (lQCD) predictions with (2+1) dynamical flavours. We find that $σ_{\rm el}$/T in CSPM has a very weak dependence on the temperature. We compare CSPM results with those obtained in Boltzmann Approach to Multi-Parton Scatterings (BAMPS) model. A good agreement is found between CSPM results and predictions of BAMPS with fixed strong coupling constant.

# Electrical conductivity of Hot and Dense QCD matter at RHIC BES energies: A Color String Percolation Approach

## Key claim
The normalized electrical conductivity sigma_el/T in the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) is almost independent of temperature and matches the results of the BAMPS model with fixed strong coupling constant, but lies well above lattice QCD predictions for all temperatures studied.

## Method
The authors derive electrical conductivity using the Drude formula within the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation, applied to the Color String Percolation Model. The mean free path is obtained from the effective number of color string sources per unit volume, where strings fragment into u-ubar pairs only. The percolation density parameter xi for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies is calculated using parameterizations from pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The normalized electrical conductivity sigma_el/T is calculated for u-quark and antiquark contributions using the fractional quark charge squared prefactor 4/9, with e^2 = 4*pi*alpha where alpha = 1/137
- The longitudinal extension of the string L is taken as approximately 1 fm
- The fitting parameters for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV are p0 = 1.982 and alpha = 12.877
- The ratio eta/s forms a dip at T/T_c = 1 in CSPM

## Context
This work extends the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM) to calculate electrical conductivity for the first time, building on prior CSPM studies of deconfinement phase transition and transport properties at RHIC BES energies by Sahoo et al. (2018). The results are compared with lattice QCD predictions with (2+1) dynamical flavors, the BAMPS transport model (Greif et al., 2014), the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics model (Cassing et al., 2013), and quasi-particle model calculations (Puglisi et al., 2014; Thakur et al., 2017). The work enables further study of the ratio (eta/s)/(sigma_el/T) to quantify the relative contributions of quark and gluon scatterings at different temperatures.
