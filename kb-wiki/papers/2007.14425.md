# QCD resummation on single hadron transverse momentum distribution with the thrust axis

> We derive the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization and resummation formula of the unpolarized transverse momentum distribution ($j_T$) for the single hadron production with the thrust axis in electron-positron collision. Two different kinematic regions are considered, including small transverse momentum limit $j_T \ll Q$, and joint transverse momentum and threshold limit $j_T \ll Q(1-z_h) \ll Q$, where $Q$ and $z_h$ are the hard scattering energy and the observed hadron momentum fraction. Using effective theory methods, we resum logarithms $\ln(Q/j_T)$ and $\ln(1-z_h)$ to all orders. In the end we present the differential cross sections and Gaussian widths calculated for the inclusive charged pion production and find that our results are consistent with the measurements reported by the Belle collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper derives a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization and resummation formula for the single hadron transverse momentum distribution with respect to the thrust axis in electron-positron collisions, finding that the theoretical predictions for Gaussian widths of charged pion production are consistent with Belle collaboration measurements, but only after including both small transverse momentum resummation and threshold resummation in the \(z_h \to 1\) limit.

## Method

The authors use Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to derive TMD factorization formulas for the process \(e^+e^- \to hX\) where the hadron transverse momentum \(j_T\) is measured relative to the thrust axis. They consider two kinematic regions: the small transverse momentum limit \(j_T \ll Q\) and the joint transverse momentum and threshold limit \(j_T \ll Q(1-z_h) \ll Q\). They resum logarithms \(\ln(Q/j_T)\) and \(\ln(1-z_h)\) to next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy using renormalization group evolution techniques, including non-global logarithms (NGLs) via the BMS equation. The factorization involves both quark and gluon TMD fragmentation functions, with the soft function being exactly half of the standard soft function for back-to-back hadron pair production due to the hemisphere constraint.

## Key numbers

- The hard scattering energy \(Q\) is the center-of-mass energy of the \(e^+e^-\) collisions, with \(Q^2 = s\)
- The hadron momentum fraction is defined as \(z_h = 2E_h/Q\)
- The leading-order cross section is \(\sigma_0 = 4\pi\alpha_{\rm em}^2/(3Q^2)\)
- The \(b_*\)-prescription uses \(b_{\rm max} = 1.5\ {\rm GeV}^{-1}\)
- The non-perturbative function parameters are \(Q_0^2 = 2.4\ {\rm GeV}^2\), \(g_2 = 0.84\), and \(g_h = 0.042\)
- The cusp anomalous dimension coefficients are \(\Gamma_0 = 4C_F\) and \(\Gamma_1 = (268/9 - 4\pi^2/3)C_F C_A - (40/9)C_F^2 n_f\)
- The non-cusp anomalous dimension coefficient is \(\gamma_0^{D_q} = 3C_F\)

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization formalism developed by Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1984) and the SCET-based approaches by Becher (2010) and Chiu (2011) to the non-global observable of single hadron production with the thrust axis. It provides a theoretical foundation for the Belle collaboration measurements reported by Seidl (2019), which showed Gaussian \(j_T\) distributions with a \(z_h\)-dependent width that rises then decreases as \(z_h \to 1\). The formalism enables future extractions of TMD fragmentation functions from this new observable, complementing existing extractions from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and back-to-back hadron pair production in \(e^+e^-\) collisions.
