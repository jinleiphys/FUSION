# Towards a unified description of the electroweak nuclear response

> We briefly review the growing efforts to set up a unified framework for the description of neutrino interactions with atomic nuclei and nuclear matter, applicable in the broad kinematical region corresponding to neutrino energies ranging between few MeV and few GeV. The emerging picture suggests that the formalism of nuclear many-body theory can be exploited to obtain the neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for both the interpretation of oscillation signals and simulation of neutrino transport in compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nuclear many-body theory (NMBT) can provide a unified framework for neutrino-nucleus cross sections across the broad energy range from few MeV to few GeV, but the formalism is only fully consistent in the nonrelativistic regime where momentum transfer \(|{\bf q}|/m \ll 1\), while the high-momentum-transfer region requires combining NMBT ground-state descriptions with relativistic treatments of final states and currents.

## Method

The authors review the application of nuclear many-body theory, using realistic Hamiltonians with two- and three-nucleon potentials (Argonne \(v_{18}\) and Fujita-Miyazawa-type three-body forces), to compute neutrino-nucleus cross sections. For low momentum transfer (\(|{\bf q}| \lesssim 500\) MeV), they employ nonrelativistic Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and Correlated Basis Function (CBF) methods to calculate response functions and their Laplace transforms (Euclidean responses). For high momentum transfer, they outline the impulse approximation (IA) scheme combined with the spectral function formalism.

## Key numbers

- The nonrelativistic regime is defined as \(|{\bf q}| \lesssim 500\) MeV, where the approximation is applicable.
- The high-momentum-transfer regime is defined as \(|{\bf q}| \gsim 500\) MeV, where impulse approximation can be applied.
- GFMC calculations have been performed for \(^{12}\)C and systems of 16 neutrons, corresponding to 540,672 and 65,536 spin-isospin states, respectively.
- The transverse electromagnetic Euclidean response of \(^{12}\)C is computed at \(|{\bf q}| = 570\) MeV and compared to experimental data from Jourdan (1996).
- The transverse electromagnetic response of \(^{4}\)He is computed at \(|{\bf q}| = 500\) MeV and compared to experimental data from Carlson (2002).
- Two-body current contributions account for approximately 30% of the strength in the transverse electromagnetic channel.

## Context

This review extends the formalism of nuclear many-body theory, previously applied to electron scattering studies using the spectral function approach (Benhar, 2005), to neutrino interactions. It builds on GFMC calculations of electromagnetic sum rules (Lovato, 2013) and neutral-weak response functions (Lovato, 2015) for light nuclei. The work enables improved neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for oscillation experiments like DUNE and for simulations of neutrino transport in compact stars, while also identifying the need to extend these methods to heavier nuclei beyond \(A \leq 12\).
