# Structure of Quark Star: A Comparative Analysis of Bayesian Inference and Neural Network Based Modeling

> In this work, we compare two powerful parameter estimation methods namely Bayesian inference and Neural Network based learning to study the quark matter equation of state with constant speed of sound parametrization and the structure of the quark stars within the two-family scenario. We use the mass and radius estimations from several X-ray sources and also the mass and tidal deformability measurements from gravitational wave events to constrain the parameters of our model. The results found from the two methods are consistent. The predicted speed of sound is compatible with the conformal limit.

## Key claim

The Bayesian inference and neural network methods produce consistent parameter estimates for the quark matter equation of state, but the neural network approach does not capture correlations between parameters and yields larger uncertainties, unlike the Bayesian method which reveals two distinct solution classes.

## Method

The authors parameterize the quark star equation of state using a constant-speed-of-sound model with two parameters: the energy density at zero pressure (e₀) and the squared speed of sound (cₛ²). They use mass-radius posteriors from six X-ray sources and mass-tidal deformability posteriors from two gravitational wave events (GW170817 and GW190425) to constrain these parameters. Bayesian inference is performed using Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling, while the neural network uses a three-hidden-layer architecture with 50 nodes per layer, trained on synthetic data generated from the same EOS parameter space with observational errors injected via Gaussian shifts.

## Key numbers

- Most probable e₀ from Bayesian one-parameter analysis: 191.84 MeV fm⁻³, with 68% CI; neural network prediction: 191.04 MeV fm⁻³ with RMSE = 10.03 MeV fm⁻³
- Most probable e₀ and cₛ² from Bayesian two-parameter analysis: e₀ = 183.48 MeV fm⁻³, cₛ² = 0.306; neural network prediction: e₀ = 191.29 MeV fm⁻³, cₛ² = 0.38
- Maximum mass from most probable one-parameter EOS: 2.18 M⊙ (Bayesian) and 2.19 M⊙ (neural network)
- Radius of 1.6 M⊙ star from most probable one-parameter EOS: 12.10 km (Bayesian) and 12.12 km (neural network)
- Tidal deformability of 1.6 M⊙ star from most probable one-parameter EOS: 368 (Bayesian) and 373 (neural network)
- Neural network RMSE on test set for two-parameter case: 15.5 MeV fm⁻³ for e₀ and 0.13 for cₛ²
- Lower bound on cₛ² from maximum mass constraint: approximately 0.26

## Context

This work extends the two-families scenario of Drago et al. (2013, 2014, 2015) by applying both Bayesian inference and neural network methods to constrain the quark star equation of state, following the Bayesian framework of Steiner (2010) and Ozel (2015) and the neural network methodology of Fujimoto (2017, 2019). The results enable future comparisons between the two-families scenario and the twin-stars model of Montana (2018) and Christian (2019), and motivate development of neural network methods that can capture parameter correlations and incorporate full observational distributions rather than marginalized Gaussians.
