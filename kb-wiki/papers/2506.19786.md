# Perfect spinfluid: A divergence-type approach

> We present a new formulation of non-dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics that incorporates spin degrees of freedom into the divergence-type theory framework. Due to the divergence-type structure, it is straightforward to enforce non-linear causality and symmetric hyperbolicity of the equations of motion, ensuring local well-posedness of the initial-value problem and stability of the theory. Furthermore, in a specific realization based on spin kinetic theory, we prove that the equations of motion remain non-linearly causal and symmetric-hyperbolic to all orders in the spin potential, provided a specific thermodynamic constraint is satisfied. This framework can be applied for numerical simulations to study the dynamics of spin-polarized fluids, such as the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new formulation of non-dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics within the divergence-type theory framework, proving that the equations of motion are non-linearly causal and symmetric-hyperbolic to all orders in the spin potential, provided a specific thermodynamic constraint (Eq. 4.1 in the full text) is satisfied.

## Method
The authors incorporate spin degrees of freedom into the divergence-type theory by generalizing the generating function to spin-phase-space, introducing the spin potential as a Lagrange multiplier corresponding to spin angular momentum. They work with a specific generalized equation of state derived from a kinetic theory of weakly coupled massive spin-1/2 particles, extending the Maxwell-Jüttner distribution function to obtain the currents for particle number, energy-momentum, and spin. The conservation laws for particle number, energy-momentum, and spin are collected into a single expression, and the system is recast in symmetric hyperbolic form using the Massieu current and its Legendre transform.

## Key numbers
- The system comprises 1+4+6 = 11 independent conservation equations and 1+4+6 = 11 independent components of the fields ζ_A = {α, β_μ, Ω_{μν}/2}.
- The spin potential Ω_{μν} has 6 independent components (antisymmetric tensor), parameterized by 5 independent fields φ^5 through φ^{10} in the chosen coordinate representation.
- The constraint for causality and symmetric hyperbolicity requires that the vector M^{λAB} Z_A Z_B be future-directed and non-spacelike for any non-zero Z_A = {Z, Z_μ, Z_{[μν]}}.

## Context
This work extends the divergence-type theory framework (Liu 1986, Geroch and Lindblom 1990) to include spin degrees of freedom, building on prior formulations of relativistic spin hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2017, Hattori 2019, Hongo 2021, Becattini 2023). The framework enables numerical simulations of spin-polarized fluids such as the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, with particular relevance at low collision energies measured by the STAR collaboration (2021, 2023). The approach reproduces previously known results when truncated to first order in spin potential and provides a foundation for studying spin dynamics across the entire range of collision energies.
