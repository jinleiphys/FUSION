# Study of Light Scalars, the learned Lessons

> Attention is paid to the production mechanisms of the light scalars that reveal their nature. In the linear sigma model it is revealed the chiral shielding of the σ(600) meson and shown that the σfield is described by its four-quark component. The ππscattering amplitude is constructed taking into account the σ(600) and f_0(980) mesons, the chiral shielding of σ(600), the σ(600)-f_0(980) mixing, and results, obtained on the base of the chiral expansion and the Roy equations. The data agree with the four-quark nature of σ(600) and f_0(980). It is shown, that the kaon loop mechanism of the $ϕ$ radiative decays into the light scalar mesons, which is ratified by experiment, is the four-quark transition and points to the four-quark nature of the light scalars. It is shown also, that the light scalars are produced in the two photon collisions via four-quark transitions in contrast to the classic P wave tensor q\bar q mesons, which are produced via two-quark transitions γγ\to q\bar q, that points to the four-quark nature of the light scalar mesons, too. A programme of further investigations is laid down.

# Study of Light Scalars, the learned Lessons

## Key claim

The light scalar mesons σ(600), κ(800), f₀(980), and a₀(980) have a four-quark (q²q̄²) structure rather than a conventional P-wave qq̄ structure, as demonstrated by their production mechanisms in ππ scattering, φ radiative decays, and two-photon collisions, but the analysis relies on a specific treatment of chiral shielding and the kaon-loop model, and the author cautions that the σ(600) pole position cannot be interpreted as a physical state due to its huge width and large imaginary residue.

## Method

The author constructs the ππ scattering amplitude in the linear sigma model framework, incorporating the chiral shielding of σ(600), σ(600)-f₀(980) mixing, and constraints from chiral expansion and Roy equations. The kaon-loop model (φ → K⁺K⁻ → γR) is used to describe φ radiative decays into a₀(980) and f₀(980), and the two-photon decays of light scalars are analyzed via rescattering mechanisms (σ → π⁺π⁻ → γγ, f₀ → K⁺K⁻ → γγ, a₀ → K⁺K⁻ + π⁰η → γγ). The σ meson propagator is computed with self-energy from intermediate ππ states, and pole positions are extracted on different Riemann sheets of the complex s-plane.

## Key numbers

- σ meson Breit-Wigner mass m_σ = 0.93 GeV, with a shifted resonance mass M_res = 0.43 GeV and Γ^{renorm}_{res}(M²_res) = 0.53 GeV
- σ pole in ππ scattering: √s_σ = (0.52 − i0.25) GeV, with residue g²_π = (0.12 + i0.21) GeV²
- Phenomenological fit results: m_σ = 507 MeV, Γ_σ(m_σ) = 353 MeV; m_{f₀} = 987 MeV, Γ_{f₀}(m_{f₀}) = 130 MeV; scattering length a⁰₀ = 0.226 m⁻¹_{π⁺}
- Coupling constants: g²_{σπ⁺π⁻}/4π = 0.57 GeV², g²_{σK⁺K⁻}/4π = 0.048 GeV², g²_{f₀π⁺π⁻}/4π = 0.36 GeV², g²_{f₀K⁺K⁻}/4π = 2 GeV²
- σ(600) pole on the most complete sheet: (581.6 − i411.0) MeV; f₀(980) pole on the most complete sheet: (1495.6 − i1057.7) MeV
- Predicted two-photon widths in the q²q̄² model: Γ(a₀→γγ) ∼ Γ(f₀→γγ) ∼ 0.27 keV
- Experimental two-photon widths: Γ(a₀→γγ) = (0.19 ± 0.07^{+0.1}_{-0.07})/B(a₀→πη) keV (Crystal Ball); Γ(f₀→γγ) = (0.31 ± 0.14 ± 0.09) keV (Crystal Ball)
- Averaged two-photon widths over resonance mass distributions: ⟨Γ_{f₀→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.19 keV, ⟨Γ_{a₀→γγ}⟩_{πη} ≈ 0.3 keV, ⟨Γ_{σ→γγ}⟩_{ππ} ≈ 0.45 keV

## Context

This work extends the chiral shielding idea first proposed by Achasov and Shestakov (1994) in the linear sigma model, which showed that a negative background phase hides the σ meson. It builds on the four-quark model of Jaffe (1977) from the MIT bag model, and the kaon-loop mechanism for φ radiative decays proposed by Achasov and Ivanchenko (1987, 1989). The analysis contrasts with the Roy equation approach of Caprini, Colangelo, and Leutwyler (2006), which the author argues is approximate because it only considers the ππ channel. The results enable further investigations including γγ → K⁺K⁻ near threshold, J/ψ decays to scalar mesons, a₀(980)-f₀(980) mixing searches, and new precise experiments on ππ → K̄K.
