# Isospin Distillation with Radial Flow: a Test of the Nuclear Symmetry Energy

> We discuss mechanisms related to isospin transport in central collisions between neutron-rich systems at Fermi energies. A fully consistent study of the isospin distillation and expansion dynamics in two-component systems is presented in the framework of a stochastic transport theory. We analyze correlations between fragment observables, focusing on the study of the average N/Z of fragments, as a function of their kinetic energy. We identify an EOS-dependent relation between these observables, allowing to better characterize the fragmentation path and to access new information on the low density behavior of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper identifies an equation-of-state-dependent correlation between the average N/Z of intermediate mass fragments and their kinetic energy in central heavy-ion collisions, proposing that this correlation can serve as a probe of the low-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy. The authors hedge that the sensitivity of the standard double N/Z ratio to the symmetry energy is rather small (less than 5 percent), and therefore introduce a "shifted" double ratio to enhance the sensitivity.

## Method

The authors performed stochastic mean-field (SMF) simulations using a Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport code (TWINGO) for central collisions (b = 2 fm) of symmetric tin systems (112Sn, 124Sn, 132Sn) at 50 MeV/u beam energy. They employed two parameterizations of the symmetry energy: a stiff form (roughly proportional to density squared) and a soft form (SKM*, saturating above normal density), with 1200 events run for each reaction and each symmetry energy. Primary fragments were identified via a coalescence procedure on matter with density above one-fifth of saturation density, and the average N/Z of intermediate mass fragments (charge 3 to 10) was analyzed as a function of fragment kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 50 MeV/u for all reactions.
- Impact parameter: b = 2 fm for central collisions.
- Compressibility modulus of the soft EOS: K = 200 MeV.
- Density cutoff for fragment identification: rho_cut = 1/5 rho_0.
- Kinetic energy bin width for fragment analysis: 1.5 MeV/u.
- Average fragment multiplicity: approximately 6 for the reactions considered.
- Fragment charge cutoff for analysis: fragments with charge larger than 10 were excluded.
- Sensitivity of the standard double N/Z ratio to the iso-EOS: less than 5 percent.
- Initial N/Z values of the three systems: 1.24 (112Sn), 1.48 (124Sn), 1.64 (132Sn).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isospin distillation in liquid-gas phase transitions of asymmetric nuclear matter (Mueller and Serot 1995; Bao-An Li and Ko 1997; Chomaz, Colonna, and Randrup 2004; Baran et al. 2005) and pre-equilibrium emission studies (Famiano et al. 2006; Li et al. 2006) by proposing fragment-based observables. The analysis is complementary to pre-equilibrium studies and enables a cross-check of model predictions against experimental data, with the shifted double N/Z ratio proposed as a more robust observable that is less affected by secondary decay effects.
