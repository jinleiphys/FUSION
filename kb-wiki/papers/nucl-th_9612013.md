# Meson Photoproductions of Nucleon In the Quark Model

> The meson photoproductions off nucleons in the chiral quark model are described. The role of the S-wave resonances in the second resonance region is discussed, and it is particularly important for the Kaon, $η$ and $η'$ photoproductions.

# Meson Photoproductions of Nucleon In the Quark Model

## Key claim
The chiral quark model approach, combining low-energy QCD Lagrangian with quark model wavefunctions, can describe meson photoproduction data with far fewer parameters than phenomenological isobar models, but the success depends critically on the assumption that the relative strength and phases of s-, u-, and t-channel contributions are determined by exact SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, and that the S-wave resonances in the second resonance region play a particularly important role for kaon, η, and η' photoproductions.

## Method
The photoproduction matrix element is decomposed into contact, t-channel, u-channel, and s-channel contributions based on the low-energy QCD Lagrangian. The s-channel resonances below 2 GeV are treated individually using quark model transition amplitudes translated into standard CGLN amplitudes, while resonances above 2 GeV are treated as degenerate contributions from each harmonic oscillator shell. The calculation uses quark model wavefunctions with exact SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, known resonance masses and widths from the Particle Data Group, and leaves only one free parameter, the meson-nucleon coupling constant, to be determined by fitting to data.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0.34 GeV, m_s = 0.55 GeV; harmonic oscillator parameter α² = 0.16 GeV²
- For the S₁₁(1535) resonance from fitting to MAMI η photoproduction data: total width Γ_T = 198 MeV, coefficient C_{S₁₁(1535)} = 1.608, coupling constant α_η = 0.435
- Model-independent quantity ξ for S₁₁(1535) from η photoproduction: ξ = 0.220 GeV⁻¹
- Branching ratios for S₁₁(1535) from coupled channel analysis: χ_{ηN} = 0.63, χ_{πN} = 0.31
- Helicity amplitude A^p_{1/2} = 98.9 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (derived using the branching ratios above)
- Coupling constant for η' photoproduction: α_{η'NN} = 0.35
- Evidence for a third S₁₁ resonance with mass 1.712 GeV and width Γ_{S₁₁} = 0.184 GeV

## Context
This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously applied to pion photoproduction (Copley, Karl and Obryk 1969; Feynman, Kisslinger and Ravndal 1971) to kaon and η photoproductions, showing dramatic improvement over isobar model calculations (Mart 1995). The approach enables direct confrontation of quark model predictions with photoproduction data, and highlights the role of S-wave resonances in the second resonance region. The results enable future tests at CEBAF experiments for η' photoproduction, and suggest that kaon production experiments (πN → KY and γN → KY) will be important for establishing the existence of a proposed third S₁₁ resonance that cannot be accommodated by the quark model alone.
