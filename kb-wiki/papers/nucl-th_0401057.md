# Surface tension in a compressible liquid-drop model: Effects on nuclear density and neutron skin thickness

> We examine whether or not the surface tension acts to increase the nucleon density in the nuclear interior within a compressible liquid-drop model. We find that it depends on the density dependence of the surface tension, which may in turn be deduced from the neutron skin thickness of stable nuclei.

# Surface tension in a compressible liquid-drop model: Effects on nuclear density and neutron skin thickness

## Key claim
Whether surface tension increases or decreases the nucleon density in the nuclear interior relative to the bulk saturation density depends on the density dependence of the surface tension, characterized by the parameter χ. This parameter can in principle be deduced from measurements of the neutron skin thickness of stable nuclei, but its extraction is coupled with uncertainties in the nuclear matter incompressibility K₀ and the density symmetry coefficient L.

## Method
The authors construct a compressible liquid-drop model in which the binding energy is written as a sum of volume, surface, and Coulomb terms, with the surface tension σ(n_in, δ_in) expanded linearly around the saturation density n₀ and symmetric matter. They derive the pressure equilibrium condition from optimization of the binding energy with respect to size, and then combine this with a thermodynamic description of the nuclear surface following Pethick and Ravenhall (1996) to obtain an expression for the neutron skin thickness Δr_np that includes the density-dependence parameter χ, the surface symmetry coefficient C_sym, and the bulk parameters K₀ and L.

## Key numbers
- The saturation density n₀ typically ranges from 0.14 to 0.17 fm⁻³, the saturation energy w₀ is −16 ± 1 MeV, and the symmetry energy coefficient S₀ ranges from 25 to 40 MeV.
- The incompressibility K₀ ranges from 180 to 360 MeV and the density symmetry coefficient L ranges from 0 to 200 MeV, based on fits to empirical masses and radii using a simplified Thomas-Fermi model.
- The surface tension at saturation σ₀ is approximately 1 MeV fm⁻², and the surface symmetry coefficient C_sym ranges from 1.5 to 2.5.
- The deviation of the interior density from the bulk saturation density is estimated as n_in − n_s ≈ 0.016 (230 MeV / K₀) (σ₀ / 1 MeV fm⁻²) (5 fm / R_p) × (1 − 3χ/2 − 3Z²e² / 40π R_p³ σ₀) fm⁻³.
- The ratio of Coulomb pressure to surface pressure, 3Z²e² / 40π R_p³ σ₀, is typically 0.2 to 0.6.
- For χ = 0 and χ = 4/3, the surface pressure can induce about 10% change in n_in in opposite directions.
- The neutron skin thickness expression includes a parameter C = (2σ₀ / S₀ n₀) (C_sym + 3Lχ / K₀).
- At N = Z, the interior neutron excess δ_in ≈ Ze² / 20 R_p S₀, which is typically about 0.02.
- The polarization reduction of Δr_np amounts to √(3/5) Ze² / 70 S₀, typically 0.01 to 0.04 fm.

## Context
This work extends the compressible liquid-drop approach of Myers and Swiatecki (1969, 1974), who set χ = 0, and the thermodynamic surface description of Pethick and Ravenhall (1996), who considered the incompressible limit. The authors show that the term 3Lχ/K₀ in the neutron skin expression can be comparable to C_sym and is absent in the incompressible limit, meaning that previous attempts to relate neutron skin thickness to the nuclear equation of state (Oyamatsu et al., 1998; Stringari and Lipparini, 1982; Danielewicz, 2003) did not fully account for uncertainties in χ, L, and K₀. The results enable future systematic analyses of proton and electron elastic scattering data from stable nuclei to simultaneously constrain the density dependence of surface tension and the bulk saturation density.
