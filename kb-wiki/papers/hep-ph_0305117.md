# Open charm production in binary reactions within the Regge theory

> We analyze the open charm production in the binary $πp \to \bar{D} Λ_c$ and $π(ρ) J/ψ$ reactions within the Regge theory including the absorption corrections. The calculations show that the total cross section is about a few $μ$b for the first processes and a few mb for the second reactions at energy close to the threshold. Then it decreases with increasing energy according to the true Regge asymptotics.

## Key claim

The total cross sections for open charm production in binary reactions like $\pi p \to \bar{D} \Lambda_c$ and $\pi(\rho) J/\psi$ are calculated within Regge theory including absorption corrections, and contrary to Lagrangian model predictions, these cross sections decrease with increasing energy according to true Regge asymptotics, with the absorption corrections being sizable only near threshold.

## Method

The authors apply a Regge-pole exchange amplitude using the $D^*$ Regge trajectory, with intercept $\alpha_{D^*}(0) = -0.86$ and slope $\alpha_{D^*}^\prime(0) = 0.5~\text{GeV}^{-2}$, to describe the binary reactions. Absorption corrections from elastic and inelastic rescattering of final charmed hadrons are included using the quasieikonal approximation with a one-Pomeron exchange graph, parameterized by a total cross section $\sigma_{D\bar{D}^*}^\text{tot} \simeq 10 \div 12~\text{mb}$ and an enhancement factor $C \simeq 1.5$. The differential cross section is then integrated over $t$ to obtain the total cross section.

## Key numbers

* Total cross section for $\pi p \to \bar{D} \Lambda_c$ reactions is about a few $\mu$b at energies close to threshold.
* Total cross section for $\pi(\rho) J/\psi$ reactions is about a few mb at energies close to threshold.
* The $D^*$ Regge trajectory intercept is $\alpha_{D^*}(0) = -0.86$ and its slope is $\alpha_{D^*}^\prime(0) = 0.5~\text{GeV}^{-2}$.
* The universal coupling constant is $g_0^2/4\pi = 2.7$, determined from the $\rho$-meson width.
* The flavor dependent scale factor is $s_0 = 4.0~\text{GeV}^2$.
* The total cross section for $D\bar{D}^*$ scattering is $\sigma_{D\bar{D}^*}^\text{tot} \simeq 10 \div 12~\text{mb}$.
* The slope of the Pomeron trajectory is $\alpha_\mathcal{P}^\prime(0) \simeq 0.2~\text{(GeV/}c\text{)}^{-2}$.
* The enhancement factor for absorption corrections is $C \simeq 1.5$.

## Context

This work extends the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) approach of Boreskov (1983) and Rzjanin (2001) for $\pi N \to \bar{D} \Lambda_c$ reactions to also cover $\pi(\rho) J/\psi$ processes, and includes absorption corrections following Kaidalov (1994). The results contradict the constant or slowly increasing energy dependence predicted by chiral Lagrangian approaches (Haglin 2000, Lin 2001, Oh 2000), and instead show a decreasing cross section with energy consistent with Regge asymptotics. This enables more reliable estimates of $J/\psi$ breakup cross sections by mesons, which are crucial for interpreting charmonium suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.
