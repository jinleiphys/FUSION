# Consequences of simultaneous chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement for the isospin symmetric phase diagram

> The thermodynamic bag model (tdBag) has been applied widely to model quark matter properties in both heavy-ion and astrophysics communities. Several fundamental physics aspects are missing in tdBag, e.g., dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (D$χ$SB) and repulsions due to the vector interaction are both included explicitly in the novel vBag quark matter model of Klähn and Fischer (2015) (Astrophys. J. 810, 134 (2015)). An important feature of vBag is the simultaneous D$χ$SB and deconfinement, where the latter links vBag to a given hadronic model for the construction of the phase transition. In this article we discuss the extension to finite temperatures and the resulting phase diagram for the isospin symmetric medium.

## Key claim

The vBag quark matter model, extended to finite temperatures, enforces simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement by adding a temperature-dependent deconfinement bag constant \(B_{\rm dc}(T)\) derived from the hadronic equation of state, which shifts the phase transition to lower densities compared to standard approaches where chiral and deconfinement transitions occur at different chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors extended the zero-temperature vBag model of Klähn and Fischer (2015) to finite temperatures by including temperature in the Fermi-gas expressions for pressure, energy density, and entropy, while keeping the chiral bag constant \(B_\chi\) medium-independent. They constructed a first-order phase transition between the hadronic equation of state HS(DD2) from Hempel (2009) and the vBag quark matter model by requiring simultaneous chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement at a common critical baryochemical potential \(\mu_{\rm B,\chi}\), achieved by adding a deconfinement bag constant \(B_{\rm dc}(T) = P^\mathcal{H}(T,\mu_{\rm B,\chi})\) to the quark pressure. Thermodynamic consistency then required additional contributions to entropy and energy density from the temperature derivative of \(B_{\rm dc}\).

## Key numbers

* Chiral bag constant: \(B_\chi^{1/4} = 152.7\) MeV, chosen to reproduce vacuum pion mass and form factors.
* Critical baryochemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration at \(T = 100\) MeV: \(\mu_{\rm B,\chi} = 772\) MeV for vBag.
* Deconfinement transition chemical potential for the 'standard' NJL approach (no deconfinement bag constant) at \(T = 100\) MeV: \(\mu_{\rm B,dc}^{\rm NJL} = 834\) MeV.
* Lattice QCD crossover temperature at vanishing chemical potential: \(154 \pm 9\) MeV (cited from Katz (2012), Laermann (2012), and Katz (2014)).

## Context

This work extends the vBag model of Klähn and Fischer (2015) from zero temperature to finite temperatures, providing a quark matter equation of state that enforces simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement, a feature suggested by Dyson-Schwinger studies (Qin (2010), Fischer (2014)). It contrasts with the thermodynamic bag model (tdBag) of Farhi (1984) and standard NJL approaches (Buballa (2003)) where chiral and deconfinement transitions occur at different chemical potentials, and with approaches that allow a quarkyonic phase (McLerran (2007), Blaschke (2010)). The resulting phase diagram is relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at NICA and FAIR, though the model cannot address the existence of a critical point and neglects thermal pions and gluons at high temperatures.
