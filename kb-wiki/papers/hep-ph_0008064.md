# Role of the Delta (1232) in DIS on polarized $^3$He and extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_{1}^{n}(x,Q^2)$

> We consider the effect of the transitions $n \to Δ^{0}$ and $p \to Δ^{+}$ in deep inelastic scattering on polarized $^3$He on the extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_{1}^{n}(x,Q^2)$. Making the natural assumption that these transitions are the dominant non-nucleonic contributions to the renormalization of the axial vector coupling constant in the A=3 system, we find that the effect of $Δ$ increases $g_{1}^{n}(x,Q^2)$ by $10 ÷40$% in the range $0.05 \le x \le 0.6$, where our considerations are applicable and most of the data for $g_{1}^{n}(x,Q^2)$ exist.

# Role of the Delta (1232) in DIS on polarized $^3$He and extraction of the neutron spin structure function $g_{1}^{n}(x,Q^2)$

## Key claim

The transitions $n \to \Delta^{0}$ and $p \to \Delta^{+}$ in deep inelastic scattering on polarized $^3$He increase the extracted neutron spin structure function $g_{1}^{n}(x,Q^2)$ by 10 to 40% in the range $0.05 \le x \le 0.6$, under the assumption that these transitions are the dominant non-nucleonic contributions to the renormalization of the axial vector coupling constant in the A=3 system. The correction is derived from requiring consistency between the Bjorken sum rule generalized to the tri-nucleon system and the measured axial coupling constant of triton.

## Method

The authors derived a master equation for $g_{1}^{^3He}(x,Q^2)$ that includes contributions from nucleon effective polarizations ($P_n = 86 \pm 0.8\%$, $P_p = -2.8 \pm 0.15\%$) and from interference terms $n \to \Delta^{0}$ and $p \to \Delta^{+}$. The interference spin structure functions were related to $g_{1}^{p}$ and $g_{1}^{n}$ using SU(6) valence quark model relations, and the effective polarizations of the interference terms were fixed by requiring that the theoretical prediction for the ratio of Bjorken sum rules for the tri-nucleon system match the experimental value derived from triton beta decay. The resulting correction was applied to re-analyze the E154 data at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$ and the HERMES data at correlated $x$ and $Q^2$.

## Key numbers

- The effective polarization of the neutron in $^3$He is $P_n = 86 \pm 0.8\%$; the effective polarization of each proton is $P_p = -2.8 \pm 0.15\%$.
- The ratio of axial coupling constants $\eta \equiv g_{A}|_{triton} / g_{A} = 0.956 \pm 0.004$, while the nucleon-only calculation gives $0.916 \pm 0.009$, a discrepancy of about 4%.
- The combined effective polarization of the interference terms is $2(P_{n \to \Delta^{0}} + 2P_{p \to \Delta^{+}}) = -0.027$.
- For the E154 data at $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$, the $\Delta$ correction increases $g_{1}^{n}$ by 12 to 20% in the range $0.05 \le x \le 0.34$, and by 20 to 40% for $x > 0.34$.
- For the HERMES data, the correction increases $g_{1}^{n}$ by 10 to 25%.
- The first moment of $g_{1}^{n}$ increases by 8% (from -0.0621 to -0.0574 at $Q_0^2=0.23$ GeV$^2$) and by 6% (from -0.0574 to -0.0540 at $Q_0^2=4$ GeV$^2$) for the two parton parameterizations considered.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of nuclear effects in polarized deep inelastic scattering beyond the well-established spin depolarization mechanism (Fry and collaborators, 1990s; the effective polarizations $P_n=86\%$, $P_p=-2.8\%$ are taken from the literature). The authors draw a direct analogy between two-body exchange currents involving the $\Delta$ isobar in triton beta decay (Saito and collaborators, 1990s) and interference diagrams in DIS on $^3$He. The paper provides a correction formula that can be applied to re-analyze existing data from the E154 and HERMES collaborations, and it enables more precise extraction of $g_{1}^{n}$ from future high-precision measurements planned at TJNAF in the $0.33 \le x \le 0.63$ region.
