# Microscopic theory of the γ-decay of nuclear giant resonances

> In the past decades, the γ-decay of giant resonances has been studied using phenomenological models. In keeping with possible future studies performed with exotic beams, microscopically-based frameworks should be envisaged. In the present paper, we introduce a model which is entirely based on Skyrme effective interactions, and treats the ground-state decay within the fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and the decay to low-lying states at the lowest order beyond RPA. The model is applied to 208Pb and 90Zr, and the results are compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a fully microscopic model for the gamma-decay of giant resonances based on Skyrme effective interactions, treating ground-state decay within self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and decay to low-lying states at the lowest order beyond RPA, but the results for the decay to low-lying states show strong dependence on the chosen Skyrme interaction and do not uniformly reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve Hartree-Fock (HF) equations on a radial mesh, then perform fully self-consistent RPA calculations to obtain vibrational states and their amplitudes. For decay to low-lying states, they evaluate all lowest-order perturbative diagrams from Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) involving particle-vibration coupling vertices, using the same Skyrme interaction consistently throughout. The model is applied to the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) in 208Pb and 90Zr, using four different Skyrme forces: SLy5, SGII, SkP, and LNS.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb ground-state decay of ISGQR: SLy5 gives 231.54 eV (bare), SGII gives 163.22 eV, SkP gives 119.18 eV, LNS gives 176.57 eV; experimental value is 130 ± 40 eV
- After rescaling ISGQR energy to experimental value (10.6 MeV), renormalized widths for 208Pb are: SLy5 127.58 eV, SGII 113.57 eV, SkP 159.72 eV, LNS 104.74 eV
- For 90Zr ground-state decay of ISGQR: SLy5 gives 211.77 eV at 15.33 MeV, SGII gives 182.03 eV at 14.90 MeV, SkP gives 107.27 eV at 13.09 MeV, LNS gives 182.71 eV at 15.48 MeV
- For decay to low-lying 3- state in 208Pb: SLy5 gives 3.39 eV at transition energy 8.66 MeV, SGII gives 29.18 eV at 8.58 MeV, SkP gives 8.34 eV at 6.99 MeV, LNS gives 39.87 eV at 8.90 MeV
- For decay to low-lying 3- state in 90Zr: SLy5 gives 5.81 eV at 12.51 MeV, SGII gives 50.58 eV at 12.16 MeV, SkP gives 5.14 eV at 10.42 MeV, LNS gives 16.95 eV at 12.72 MeV
- The spreading width from coupling to phonons for ISGQR in 208Pb using SLy5 is approximately 2 MeV, compared to experimental value 2.4 ± 0.4 MeV
- The ISGQR energy centroid shifts from RPA value 12.28 MeV to 10.9 MeV after including phonon coupling

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological studies of gamma-decay of giant resonances that used Nuclear Field Theory (Bortignon 1985) or the Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (Speth 1984), by implementing a fully microscopic framework based on Skyrme effective interactions. The approach enables future studies with exotic beams where phenomenological models may be inadequate. The results show that ground-state decay widths can be reproduced by scaling to experimental energies, but the decay to low-lying states shows strong interaction dependence, indicating the need for further refinement of the particle-vibration coupling vertices.
