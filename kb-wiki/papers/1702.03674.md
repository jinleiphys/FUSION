# Photoproduction of $ОіN\to K^+ ОЈ^*(1385)$ in the Reggeized framework

> Photoproduction of $ KОЈ^{*}(1385)$ on the nucleon is investigated within the Regge framework and the reaction mechanism is analyzed based on the data existing in the channels $Оіp\to K^+ОЈ^{*0}$ and $Оіn\to K^+ОЈ^{*-}$. The Reggeization of the $t$-channel meson exchanges $K(494)+K^*(892)+K_2^*(1430)$ is employed to construct the photoproduction amplitude. The Rarita-Schwinger formalism is applied for the spin-3/2$^+$ strangeness-baryon $ОЈ^*$ with a special gauge prescription utilized for the convergence of these reaction processes. Within a set of coupling constants determined from the symmetry arguement for the $K$ and $K^*$ and from the duality and vector dominance for the $K_2^*$, the data of the both processes are reproduced to a good degree. The production mechanism of these processes are featured by the dominance of the contact term plus the $K$ exchange with the role of the $K_2^*$ following rather than the $K^*$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the photoproduction of \(K\Sigma^*(1385)\) on the nucleon can be described within a Reggeized framework without fit parameters, using coupling constants determined from SU(3) symmetry and duality/vector dominance, but the description of the beam polarization asymmetry for the \(\gamma n\) process remains unresolved and requires further analysis.

## Method

The authors Reggeize the \(t\)-channel exchanges of \(K(494)\), \(K^*(892)\), and \(K_2^*(1430)\) mesons to construct the photoproduction amplitude for \(\gamma p\to K^+\Sigma^{*0}\) and \(\gamma n\to K^+\Sigma^{*-}\). The Rarita-Schwinger formalism is applied for the spin-3/2 \(\Sigma^*\) baryon, with a minimal gauge prescription used to ensure convergence at high energies. Coupling constants are fixed from SU(3) symmetry relations linking \(f_{KN\Sigma^*}\) to the empirically known \(f_{\pi N\Delta}\), and from duality and vector dominance for the \(K_2^*\) coupling.

## Key numbers

* Coupling constant \(f_{KN\Sigma^{*}} = -2.2\) used for both \(\gamma p\) and \(\gamma n\) processes, estimated from the SU(3) relation with \(f_{\pi^- p \Delta^{++}}\) in the range 1.7 to 2.
* Radiative decay constants: \(g_{\gamma KK^*} = -0.254\) (from \(\Gamma_{K^*\to K\gamma}=50\) keV) and \(g_{\gamma KK_2^*} = 0.276\) (from \(\Gamma_{K_2^*\to K\gamma}=0.24\pm0.05\) MeV).
* Trajectories: \(\alpha_K(t)=0.7\,(t-m_K^2)\), \(\alpha_{K^*}(t)=0.83\,t+0.25\), \(\alpha_{K_2^*}(t)=0.83\,(t-m_{K_2^*}^2)+2\).
* Total cross section for \(\gamma p\to K^+\Sigma^{*0}\) peaks at approximately 1 \(\mu\)b, compared to about 10 \(\mu\)b for \(\gamma p\to \pi^+\Delta^{0}\), consistent with the ratio \(|f_{KN\Sigma^*}/f_{\pi N\Delta}|^2\).
* For the neutral process \(\gamma p\to K^0\Sigma^{*+}\), measured cross sections are \(\sigma=0.68\pm0.48\) \(\mu\)b at \(E_\gamma=1.42\sim2\) GeV and \(\sigma=0.13\pm0.09\) \(\mu\)b at \(E_\gamma=2\sim5.8\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Reggeized model previously applied to \(\gamma N\to\pi^\pm\Delta\) by Yu and collaborators (2010) to the strangeness sector, specifically \(K\Sigma^*\) photoproduction. It contrasts with earlier effective Lagrangian approaches by Y. Oh et al. (2008) that included baryon resonances up to spin-5/2, and with hybrid Regge-plus-resonance models by Jun He and Wang (2010) that used mixed propagators and cutoff functions. The results enable future study of \(N^*\) resonances in neutral channels such as \(\gamma p\to K^0\Sigma^{*+}\), where only \(K^*\) and \(K_2^*\) exchanges contribute and baryon resonance signals may be more visible.
