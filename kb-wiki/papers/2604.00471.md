# Exact Construction and Uniqueness of the Coupled-Channel Green's Function

> We present a rigorous construction and uniqueness proof of the matrix Green's function for coupled radial Schrödinger equations with symmetric coupling potentials. The Green's matrix $g_{γγ'}(R,R')$ is built from two fundamental sets of $N$ linearly independent solutions, regular and outgoing, of the coupled radial equations. We prove that the associated Wronskian matrix is diagonal with elements $W_n = -k_n$ and independent of the radial coordinate, and demonstrate through the symplectic structure of the $2N$-dimensional phase space that the resulting construction is the unique Green's matrix satisfying the defining equation with correct boundary conditions, continuity at the source point, and the prescribed derivative discontinuity. The construction applies to any system of coupled radial Schrödinger equations with symmetric coupling potentials and open channels, including coupled-channels problems arising in nuclear, atomic, and molecular scattering. As an illustrative application, we show how the Green's matrix enters the nonlocal dynamical polarization potential (DPP) within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework, where retaining the off-diagonal elements captures multistep excitation pathways beyond the weak-coupling approximation.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first rigorous derivation and uniqueness proof of the coupled-channel Green's matrix for radial Schrödinger equations with symmetric coupling potentials, showing that the bilinear construction in terms of regular and outgoing solution matrices is the unique solution, not merely a valid construction as previously established by Levine and Soven (1984). The proof relies on three conditions: symmetric coupling potentials, all channels open (so that all channel wave numbers \(k_\gamma > 0\)), and well-defined regular and outgoing boundary conditions.

## Method

The authors construct the Green's matrix \(g_{\gamma\gamma'}(R,R')\) from two fundamental sets of \(N\) linearly independent solution vectors, regular solutions \(u^n\) (vanishing as \(R^{L_\gamma+1}\) at the origin) and outgoing solutions \(h^n\) (asymptotically behaving as Coulomb–Hankel functions \(H^{(+)}_{L_\gamma}(k_\gamma R)\) at infinity), organized into \(N \times N\) solution matrices \(\mathbf{U}\) and \(\mathbf{H}\). They derive the coefficient matrices \(\mathbf{A}\) and \(\mathbf{B}\) in the ansatz \(G(R,R') = \mathbf{U}(R)\mathbf{A}(R')\) for \(R < R'\) and \(G(R,R') = \mathbf{H}(R)\mathbf{B}(R')\) for \(R > R'\) by imposing continuity at the source point and the derivative jump condition from the defining equation, using the self-Wronskian identities \(\mathcal{W}[\mathbf{U},\mathbf{U}] = 0\) and \(\mathcal{W}[\mathbf{H},\mathbf{H}] = 0\) to eliminate cross-terms. Uniqueness is then proven through the symplectic structure of the \(2N\)-dimensional phase space, showing that the completeness relation \(\boldsymbol{\Phi}\boldsymbol{\Phi}^{-1} = \mathbf{I}_{2N}\) for the phase-space matrix \(\boldsymbol{\Phi}\) yields the continuity and jump conditions as necessary consequences.

## Key numbers

- The Wronskian matrix is diagonal with elements \(W_n = -k_n\), where \(k_n = \sqrt{2\mu E_n}/\hbar\) is the channel wave number for channel \(n\).
- The Green's matrix elements take the form \(g_{\gamma\gamma'}(R,R') = \frac{2\mu}{\hbar^2} \sum_n \frac{u_\gamma^n(R) h_{\gamma'}^n(R')}{W_n}\) for \(R < R'\) and \(g_{\gamma\gamma'}(R,R') = \frac{2\mu}{\hbar^2} \sum_n \frac{h_\gamma^n(R) u_{\gamma'}^n(R')}{W_n}\) for \(R > R'\), where the sum runs over \(N\) solution indices.
- The off-diagonal Wronskian elements vanish (\(W_{nm} = 0\) for \(n \neq m\)) due to the asymptotic boundary condition \(h_\gamma^m \to \delta_{\gamma m} H^{(+)}_{L_\gamma}(k_\gamma R)\) and the vanishing self-Wronskian of the Hankel function.

## Context

This work extends the construction of the coupled-channel Green's function originally developed by Levine and Soven (1984) and further discussed by Levine (1984), which verified that a bilinear formula satisfies the correct Wronskian relations and boundary conditions but did not address uniqueness. The present paper closes that gap by deriving the formula directly from the defining equation and proving uniqueness through the symplectic structure of the \(2N\)-dimensional phase space, analogous to the symplectic character of transfer matrices in the surface Green function matching framework of Rodriguez-Coppola et al. (1990) and Perez-Alvarez and Garcia-Moliner (2004). The construction enables the exact evaluation of the nonlocal dynamical polarization potential within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework, retaining all continuum-continuum couplings among discretized bins, removing the weak-coupling approximation that neglects off-diagonal elements and has been used in prior work by Rao (1973), Keeley et al. (2009), and Potel et al. (2025). The numerical implementation and application to deuteron-induced reactions on \(^{58}\)Ni are presented in a companion paper by Liu et al. (2025).
