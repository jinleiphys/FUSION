# Thermodynamics of strong coupling 2-color QCD with chiral and diquark condensates

> 2-color QCD (quantum chromodynamics with N_c=2) at finite temperature T and chemical potential μis revisited in the strong coupling limit on the lattice with staggered fermions. The phase structure in the space of T, μ, and the quark mass m is elucidated with the use of the mean field approximation and the 1/d expansion (d being the number of spatial dimensions). We put special emphasis on the interplay among the chiral condensate <\bar{q}q>, the diquark condensate <qq>, and the quark density <q^{\dagger}q> in the T-μ-m space. Simple analytic formulae are also derived without assuming μnor m being small. Qualitative comparisons are made between our results and those of recent Monte-Carlo simulations in 2-color QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the strong coupling limit of 2-color QCD, the phase structure in the (T, μ, m) space can be fully elucidated using mean field approximation and 1/d expansion, with the caveat that the chiral condensate vanishes identically at any T and μ in the chiral limit (m=0), a feature specific to 2-color QCD that contrasts with 3-color QCD.

## Method

The authors start from the lattice action with staggered fermions in the strong coupling limit (g→∞), then employ a 1/d expansion in spatial directions to integrate out spatial link variables. They introduce auxiliary fields σ and Δ for chiral and diquark condensates, adopt a mean field approximation with spatially uniform condensates, and exactly integrate over the temporal link variable U₀ and the fermion fields to derive an effective free energy Feff[σ,Δ]. The free energy is minimized to determine the condensates and quark number density as functions of T, μ, and m.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: Tc(μ=0) = 6/5 (in lattice units, for d=3)
- Upper-critical chemical potential at zero temperature: μc(T=0) = (1/2) arcsinh d ≈ 0.91 (for d=3)
- Quark number density at saturation: ρ = 2 (maximum number of quarks per lattice site due to Pauli exclusion)
- For small quark mass m at T=0: lower-critical chemical potential μc^low = m^(1/2) · {[(1+d²)^(1/2) - 1]/2}^(1/4)
- For small quark mass m at T=0: upper-critical chemical potential μc^up = (1/2) arcsinh d
- For sufficiently large m: μc^low ≈ μc^up ≈ arccosh m

## Context

This work extends the earlier strong coupling studies of 2-color QCD by DKM84, DMW87, and KM90, providing a more complete phase diagram in the T-μ-m space. The results enable qualitative comparisons with Monte-Carlo simulations of 2-color QCD by han99, kog02, and nonaka03, and provide physical insight into the Bose liquid of diquarks and the crossover from Bose-Einstein condensate to BCS-type condensate discussed by Itakura (2002). The analytic formulae for critical chemical potentials at finite m go beyond chiral perturbation theory approaches (ChPT0, ChPT) by not assuming small m.
