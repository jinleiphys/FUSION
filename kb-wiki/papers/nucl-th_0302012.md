# A schematic model for QCD II: finite temperature regime

> A schematic model for QCD, developed in a previous paper, is applied to calculate meson properties in the high temperature (up to 0.5 GeV) regime. It is a Lipkin model for quark-antiquark pairs coupled to gluon pairs of spin zero. The partition function is constructed with the obtained meson spectrum and several thermodynamical observables are calculated, like: the energy density, heat capacity, as well as relative production rates of mesons and absolute production rates for pions and kaons. The model predictions show a qualitative agreement with data. Based on these results we advocate the use of the model as a toy model for QCD.

## Key claim

The model predicts a qualitative, not quantitative, agreement with experimental data on meson production rates and thermodynamic observables in the high-temperature regime up to 0.5 GeV, and the authors explicitly advocate its use only as a toy model for QCD, not as a precise description.

## Method

The authors apply a Lipkin-type schematic model for quark-antiquark pairs coupled to gluon pairs of spin zero, previously fitted to 13 known meson states at zero temperature. They construct the grand canonical partition function from the model's meson spectrum and calculate thermodynamic observables (energy density, heat capacity) and particle production rates, introducing an elementary volume of hadronic size (radius 1 fm) and imposing confinement by hand below a critical temperature. The transition from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) to the hadron gas is studied by comparing calculations that allow all color states (above T_c) with those that restrict to color-singlet states only (below T_c).

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the QGP-to-hadron transition is found to be T_c = 0.170 GeV for zero baryon and strange chemical potentials.
* The bag pressure extracted at T_c = 0.170 GeV, using an elementary volume radius r_el = 1 fm, gives p^(1/4) ≈ 0.17 GeV.
* For the Au+Au collision at 10 GeV/A (SIS-GSI experiment), the model yields a total π⁺ production rate of approximately 180 at T = 0.13 GeV and μ_B = 0.55 GeV, compared to an experimental value of about 160.
* The K⁻/K⁺ production ratio at √s = 130 A GeV is reproduced with μ_s = 0.012 GeV and μ_B = 0.044 GeV.
* The QGP phase volume before the transition is about 2 × 10³ fm³ (radius ≈ 8 fm), expanding to about 3.4 × 10⁴ fm³ (radius ≈ 20 fm) after the transition to the hadron gas.
* The gluon pair energy level is fixed at 1.6 GeV, and the fermion level energy ω_f is fixed at 0.33 GeV (constituent quark effective mass).

## Context

This work extends the schematic model developed in the authors' previous paper (Jesgarz et al., 2003, referred to as Paper I) from zero-temperature meson spectroscopy to the finite-temperature regime, including the QGP phase. The model belongs to the class of algebraic, exactly solvable models related to the Lipkin model (Lipkin, 1965) and to earlier work by Schutte (treating nucleons coupled to pions) and Pittel (describing quarks with particle-conserving interactions). The results enable qualitative tests of thermodynamic descriptions of the QGP-to-hadron-gas transition, such as those in the ALCOR model, and provide a non-perturbative benchmark for many-body techniques applied to real QCD (e.g., the work of Swan and Bonn).
