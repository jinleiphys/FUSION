# Semiclassical expansions up to $\hbar^4$-order in relativistic nuclear physics

> We present the first calculation of the $\hbar^4$-Wigner--Kirkwood corrections to a relativistic system of fermions in the presence of external scalar and vector potentials. The method we propose allows to compute efficiently semiclassical corrections to one body operators such as mean energies and local densities. It also preserves gauge invariance and produces explicitly convergent results despite some apparent divergencies at the classical turning points. As a byproduct we obtain the $\hbar^4$ corrections stemming from the polarization of the Dirac sea. We apply our results to the relativistic $σ$-$ω$ Lagrangian in the Hartree valence approximation. We compare the semiclassical expansion with the exact result and with the Strutinsky average whenever it can be obtained. We find that the $\hbar^4 $ corrections are much smaller than typical shell effects. Our results provide convincing arguments to neglect higher than second order $\hbar$ effects in the Wigner--Kirkwood scheme to relativistic nuclear physics in the mean field approximation.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of $\hbar^4$-order Wigner-Kirkwood semiclassical corrections for a relativistic system of fermions with external scalar and vector potentials, and finds that these $\hbar^4$ corrections are much smaller than typical shell effects, providing arguments to neglect higher than second order $\hbar$ effects in the Wigner-Kirkwood scheme for relativistic nuclear physics in the mean field approximation.

## Method

The authors develop a method based on the cumulative number of states formalism, where the cumulative number of states is expressed as the phase of the determinant of the operator (H - E + i epsilon). They apply a semiclassical expansion using the Chan method extended to relativistic systems, computing the Wigner-Kirkwood expansion up to $\hbar^4$ order for the Dirac equation with external scalar and vector potentials. The method is applied to the relativistic sigma-omega Lagrangian in the Hartree valence approximation, and the semiclassical expansion is compared with exact results and Strutinsky averages where available.

## Key numbers

- The $\hbar^4$ corrections to the cumulative number of states for the non-relativistic three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator are given by $N_4(E) = \frac{17\nu}{1920} \, \hbar \omega \, \delta(E)$, where $\nu$ is the spin degeneracy factor
- The $\hbar^4$ corrections to the total energy for the non-relativistic three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator are $E_4(\mu) = -\frac{17\nu}{1920} \, \hbar \omega \, \Theta(\mu)$
- The $\hbar^2$ correction to the cumulative number of states for the non-relativistic three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator is $N_2(E) = -\frac{\nu}{8} \left( \frac{E}{\hbar \omega}\right) \Theta(E)$
- The leading order (Thomas-Fermi) cumulative number of states for the non-relativistic three-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator is $N_0(E) = \frac{\nu}{6} \left( \frac{E}{\hbar \omega}\right)^3 \Theta(E)$

## Context

This work extends previous semiclassical calculations in relativistic nuclear systems, where only $\hbar^2$ corrections had been computed (Ce90, We91), and in atomic systems where $\hbar^4$ corrections were known (Po91). In the non-relativistic case, $\hbar^4$ and $\hbar^6$ corrections had been known for a long time (Ho73, Mu81), with $\hbar^8$ corrections determined more recently (Sh92). The method enables efficient computation of semiclassical corrections to one-body operators such as mean energies and local densities, and can be applied to more realistic extensions of the sigma-omega model including Coulomb repulsion and tensor forces.
