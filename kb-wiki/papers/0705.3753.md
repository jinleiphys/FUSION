# Strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interactions using chiral effective field theory

> We derive the leading order strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interactions in chiral effective field theory. The potential consists of contact terms without derivatives and of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. The contact terms and the couplings of the pseudoscalar mesons to the baryons are related via SU(3) flavor symmetry to the S=-1 hyperon-nucleon channels. We show that the chiral effective field theory predictions with natural values for the low-energy constants agree with the experimental information in the S=-2 sector.

## Key claim
The leading-order chiral effective field theory predictions for strangeness S=-2 baryon-baryon interactions, with natural values for the low-energy constants, are consistent with the limited experimental information available, but the data are insufficient to quantitatively determine the one additional contact term that appears in the isospin-zero doubly strange channels.

## Method
The authors constructed a leading-order chiral effective field theory potential for the S=-2 baryon-baryon sector, consisting of four-baryon contact terms without derivatives and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. Five of the six contact terms were taken from a previous fit to S=-1 hyperon-nucleon data (Polinder 2006), with the sixth determined by SU(3) flavor symmetry relations. The coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved in momentum space with an exponential regulator function and cut-off values between 550 and 700 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The additional contact term C^{ΛΛ,ΛΛ}_{1S0} was varied in the range 2.0 to -0.05 times the natural value of 4π/f_π^2.
- The resulting ΛΛ scattering length ranged from a^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = -1.38 fm to -1.83 fm.
- For the reference calculation with C^{ΛΛ,ΛΛ}_{1S0}=0 and cut-off Λ=600 MeV, the ΛΛ scattering length was a^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = -1.52 fm with effective range r^{ΛΛ}_{1S0} = 0.59 fm.
- The Ξ^-p → ΛΛ cross section at p_lab = 500 MeV was reported experimentally as 4.3^{+6.3}_{-2.7} mb, with an upper limit of 24 mb at 90% confidence level for elastic Ξ^-p scattering.
- The Ξ^0p singlet scattering length ranged from a^{Ξ^0p}_{1S0} = 0.21 fm (Λ=550 MeV) to 0.13 fm (Λ=700 MeV).
- The Σ^+Σ^+ singlet scattering length ranged from a^{Σ^+Σ^+}_{1S0} = -6.23 fm (Λ=550 MeV) to -9.27 fm (Λ=700 MeV).

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to the S=-1 hyperon-nucleon interaction (Polinder 2006) into the S=-2 sector, using SU(3) flavor symmetry to relate the doubly strange channels to the singly strange ones. It provides a framework that can be systematically improved to higher orders, in contrast to earlier meson-exchange models (Nijmegen ESC04 model, Rijken 2006) and constituent quark models (Fujiwara 2006). The results enable future analysis of upcoming data from J-PARC and FAIR facilities, and the ΛΛ scattering lengths predicted are comparable to the Nijmegen ESC04d value of -1.32 fm but differ from the quark model value of -0.81 fm (Fujiwara 2006).
