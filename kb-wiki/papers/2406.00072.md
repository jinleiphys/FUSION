# Methodology for Analyzing Proton Multiplicity Fluctuations with Azimuthal Partitions in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> A primary objective in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is to investigate the phase transition between confined and deconfined color matter. Complementary to the cumulants of conserved charges integrated over the full azimuth, we introduce a novel experimental approach to explore particle fluctuations in azimuthal partitions, which are potentially sensitive to the first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collisions. We evaluate proton multiplicity ($N_w$) fluctuations in azimuthal partitions of width $w$ to quantitatively estimate the clustering tendency among these protons. The $Δσ^2$ observable is defined as the normalized difference between the variance of the $N_w$ distribution and the binomial baseline. We demonstrate the feasibility and characteristics of this observable through simulations using the AMPT and MUSIC+FIST models. We also use a Gaussian correlation model to illustrate that the dependence of $Δσ^2$ on $w$ can be parameterized to accurately extract the strength and the range of the input interaction among protons.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a novel experimental observable, Δσ², defined as the normalized difference between the variance of proton multiplicity in azimuthal partitions and a binomial baseline, which is potentially sensitive to the first-order phase transition in heavy-ion collisions through the detection of proton clustering, though the authors note that the signal size may be significantly reduced when mapping from coordinate space to momentum space.

## Method

The authors analyze event-by-event distributions of proton multiplicity (N_w) within randomly placed azimuthal partitions of width w, then compute Δσ² as the variance of N_w minus the binomial variance, normalized by N(N-1). They apply this method to simulated data from the AMPT model (string melting version with improved quark coalescence) and the MUSIC+FIST hydrodynamic model, using 72 partition samples per event, with corrections for elliptic flow and event-mixing procedures. A Gaussian correlation model is also developed where each proton's azimuthal probability distribution depends on previously generated protons through a Gaussian term with strength parameter A and range parameter σ.

## Key numbers

- For 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 39 GeV from AMPT simulations, Δσ²(N_120) is significantly negative, indicating repulsive interactions among protons
- In the MUSIC+FIST model with excluded-volume effect (baryon exclusion radius r = 1 fm), ⟨Δσ²⟩ values are markedly more negative than in the default MUSIC+FIST version
- The AMPT results show Δσ² values that become less negative with increasing reference multiplicity, following a universal curve across beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, and 62.4 GeV)
- The w-dependence of ⟨Δσ²⟩ is parameterized with a quadratic function: ⟨Δσ²⟩ = b[1 - ((w - 180)/z)²], where b quantifies the general scale and z quantifies the inverse curvature
- In the Gaussian correlation model with σ = 1, input parameters A = ±0.002, ±0.006, and ±0.01 produce Δσ² values that are positive for attractive (positive A) and negative for repulsive (negative A) correlations

## Context

This work extends the study of conserved charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which has traditionally focused on cumulants of net-proton distributions integrated over full azimuth (as measured by STAR in the Beam Energy Scan program), by introducing an azimuthally differential observable that may be more sensitive to spatial clustering from a first-order phase transition. The method enables future experimental analyses at RHIC to extract the strength and range of proton correlations through the w-dependence of Δσ², and the authors note that the observed negative Δσ² in AMPT is primarily driven by global momentum conservation, which is absent in the MUSIC+FIST model due to its lack of momentum conservation.
