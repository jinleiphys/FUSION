# Incoherent diffractive dijet production and gluon Bose enhancement in the nuclear wave function

> We investigate the effect of gluon Bose enhancement in the nuclear wave function on the dijet production in incoherent diffractive processes in DIS and ultraperipheral collisions. We demonstrate that Bose enhancement leads to an enhancement of diffractive dijet production cross section when the transverse momenta of the two jets are aligned at zero relative angle. This enhancement is maximal when the magnitude of the transverse momenta of the two jets are equal, and disappears rather quickly as a function of the ratio of the two momenta. We study both the dilute limit and fully nonlinear dense regime where the nuclear wave function is evolved with the leading order JIMWLK equation. In both cases we observe a visible effect, with it being enhanced by the evolution due to the dynamical generation of the color neutralization scale.

## Key claim

Bose enhancement in the nuclear wave function produces a measurable enhancement of the incoherent diffractive dijet production cross section when the two jets have aligned transverse momenta at zero relative angle, but this effect is maximal only when the magnitudes of the two transverse momenta are equal and disappears when their ratio reaches about 1.5-2.

## Method

The authors computed the incoherent diffractive dijet cross section in deep inelastic scattering using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, first in the dilute (low-density) limit by expanding Wilson lines to fourth order in the color charge density, and then in the fully nonlinear dense regime where the nuclear wave function was evolved using the leading-order JIMWLK equation. The target averaging was performed using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, with an infrared regulator enforcing color neutralization within a finite transverse distance 1/m, and the results were compared between the MV model and JIMWLK-evolved configurations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of Bose-Einstein correlations in trijet production (Kovner 2021, Kovner 2023) to the experimentally simpler dijet channel, building on the CGC framework for dijet production developed by Dominguez (2011) and others. The diffractive configuration offers the advantage that momentum imbalance is not affected by Sudakov radiation (Sudakov 1954, Mueller 2013), unlike the back-to-back dijets studied by Marquet (2007), Dominguez (2011), and others. The results enable experimental searches for gluon Bose enhancement at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider using a two-jet observable rather than the more challenging three-jet measurement previously proposed.
