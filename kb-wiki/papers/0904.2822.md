# Lambda(1405)-induced non-mesonic decay in kaonic nuclei

> Non-mesonic decay of kaonic nuclei is investigated under a Lambda(1405) doorway picture where the Kbar absorptions in nuclei take place through the Lambda(1405) resonance. Calculating Lambda(1405) N to YN transitions in one-meson exchange, we find that the non-mesonic decay ratio Gamma_{Lambda N} / Gamma_{Sigma^{0} N} depends strongly on the ratio of the couplings Lambda(1405)-Kbar N and Lambda(1405)-pi Sigma. Especially a larger Lambda(1405)-Kbar N coupling leads to enhancement of the decay to Lambda N. Using the chiral unitary approach for description of the Kbar N amplitudes, we obtain Gamma_{Lambda N} / Gamma_{Sigma ^{0} N} \approx 1.2 almost independently of the nucleon density, and find the total two-nucleon absorption of the Kbar in uniform nuclear matter to be 22 MeV at the normal density.

## Key claim

The non-mesonic decay ratio Γ_ΛN / Γ_Σ^0 N in kaonic nuclei is approximately 1.2, almost independent of nucleon density, but this value depends strongly on the ratio of the Λ(1405) couplings to K̅N and πΣ, with a larger Λ(1405)-K̅N coupling enhancing the ΛN decay channel.

## Method

The authors calculated Λ(1405)N → YN transition rates using a one-meson exchange model with s-wave Λ(1405) couplings to meson-baryon channels and p-wave meson-baryon-baryon interactions from flavor SU(3) symmetry. They fixed the Λ(1405) coupling ratios using K̅N scattering amplitudes from the chiral unitary approach, then evaluated the non-mesonic decay widths in uniform nuclear matter under the free Fermi gas approximation by summing transition rates over nucleon momentum states up to the Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- Γ_ΛN / Γ_Σ^0 N ≈ 1.2, almost independent of nucleon density
- Total non-mesonic decay width = 22 MeV at normal nuclear density (ρ_B = 0.17 fm⁻³)
- At normal density: Γ_Λp + Γ_Λn = 7 MeV, Γ_Σ^0 p + Γ_Σ^0 n = 5 MeV, Γ_Σ^+ n + Γ_Σ^- p = 10 MeV
- Ratio of Λ(1405) couplings from chiral unitary approach: |G_K̅N / G_πΣ| = 2.5
- Λ(1405) mesonic decay width to πΣ = 40 MeV (used for normalization)
- Λ(1405) mass taken as 1420 MeV in the K̅N channel

## Context

This work extends the Λ(1405) doorway picture for K̅ absorption in nuclei, building on prior studies of kaonic nuclei by Akaishi (2002) and Kanada-En'yo (2008) that showed strong K̅N correlations in few-body systems. The one-meson exchange approach was previously applied to Λ hypernuclei by Jido (2001), reproducing experimental Γ_n/Γ_p ratios. The results enable predictions for experimental observation of kaonic nuclei through non-mesonic decay channels, where the decay ratio's insensitivity to production details and nucleon density makes it a robust observable.
