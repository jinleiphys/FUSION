# RHIC Physics with the Parton Cascade Model

> We present an analysis of the net-baryon number rapidity distribution and of direct photon emission in the framework of the Parton Cascade Model.

# RHIC Physics with the Parton Cascade Model

## Key claim
The Parton Cascade Model (PCM) can reproduce the net-baryon excess observed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC in the central rapidity region, but only when multiple parton scatterings and fragmentation processes are included; primary-primary scatterings alone produce a net baryon density at mid-rapidity that is roughly 75% lower than the full calculation.

## Method
The authors used the VNI/BMS implementation of the Parton Cascade Model, which solves a relativistic Boltzmann equation for one-body parton distribution functions including all lowest-order QCD scattering processes between massless quarks and gluons, with a low momentum transfer cut-off pT^min to regularize infrared divergences. Parton fragmentation processes (q → qg, q → qγ, g → gg, g → qq̅) were included, with branchings terminated when virtuality drops below μ0 = 1 GeV. The GRV-HO parametrization was used for initial parton distribution functions sampled at an initialization scale Q0^2.

## Key numbers
- Net baryon density at mid-rapidity (y_cm = 0) for √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions: 5.0 from primary-primary scatterings only, increasing to approximately 17 when full parton rescattering and fragmentation are included
- The average "liberation factor" for partons in the PCM is 0.4, meaning the scaled initial state rapidity distribution matches the primary-primary scattering result when multiplied by this factor
- Multiple parton collisions increase the net baryon density at mid-rapidity by roughly 75% compared to primary-primary scatterings alone
- Average rapidity shift per collision for a quark after its first hard scattering is approximately 0.65 units
- The photon yield scales as N_part^(4/3) and is directly proportional to the number of hard parton-parton collisions

## Context
This work extends the Parton Cascade Model originally developed by Geiger and Müller (1992) to study pre-equilibrium dynamics at RHIC energies. The results for net baryon distributions are compared to experimental estimates from the BRAHMS collaboration and other RHIC experiments (2003-2004). The photon production analysis builds on earlier PCM photon studies by Bass, Müller, and Srivastava (2003). The finding that multiple scattering and fragmentation significantly enhance high-energy photon production provides a potential probe of pre-equilibrium reaction dynamics, while the net baryon results demonstrate that valence quark distributions combined with multiple scattering can explain the observed baryon excess without requiring full thermalization.
