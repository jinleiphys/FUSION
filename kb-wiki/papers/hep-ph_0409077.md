# Time Evolution of Hadronization and Grey Tracks in DIS off Nuclei

> The analysis of the grey tracks produced in Deep Inelastic Scattering (DIS) off nuclei provides important information on the space-time development of hadronization in nuclear medium. This method is complementary to the measurement of nuclear attenuation of leading inclusive hadrons. While the latter is focused on the hadronization dynamics for the quite rare process of leading hadrons production, the former covers the main bulk of inelastic events, and its Q^2 dependence is a very sensitive tool to discriminate between different models of hadronization. Employing the model of perturbative hadronization developed earlier, we calculate the Q^2 and x_{Bj} dependences of the number of collisions and relate it to the mean number of grey tracks, using an empirical relation obtained from the analysis of data from the Fermilab E665 experiment on DIS of muons off the Xe nucleus. We found the number of grey tracks to rise steeply with Q^2 in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The number of grey tracks in DIS off nuclei rises steeply with \(Q^2\), and this \(Q^2\) dependence serves as a sensitive tool to discriminate between different models of hadronization, with the authors' perturbative hadronization model successfully reproducing the experimental data from the Fermilab E665 experiment without readjustment of parameters.

## Method

The authors calculated the average number of collisions \(\langle \nu_c \rangle\) occurring during the evolution of a jet produced in DIS and propagating through nuclear matter, using a time-dependent effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}(t)\) derived from their hadronization model. This model combines soft hadronization dynamics described by the string model with hard perturbative QCD gluon radiation, where the pre-hadron multiplicity rises with time from both string decays and gluon radiation. The mean number of grey tracks \(\langle n_{gr} \rangle\) was then obtained from \(\langle \nu_c \rangle\) using the empirical relation \(\langle \nu_c \rangle = (2.08 \pm 0.13) + (3.72 \pm 0.14) \langle n_{gr} \rangle\) derived from the E665 experiment on DIS of muons off the Xe nucleus at 490 GeV beam energy.

## Key numbers

- The empirical relation between number of collisions and grey tracks: \(\langle \nu_c \rangle = (2.08 \pm 0.13) + (3.72 \pm 0.14) \langle n_{gr} \rangle\), derived from E665 data.
- The inelastic meson-nucleon cross section used: \(\sigma^{MN}_{in} = 17.7\) mb.
- The mass scale controlling transverse quark-gluon separation: \(\lambda = 0.65\) GeV.
- The time scale for string decay: \(\Delta t = 1\) fm, based on pair creation probability \(w \approx 2\) fm\(^{-2}\).
- The time parameter controlling gluon radiation: \(t_0 = (m_N x_{Bj})^{-1} = 0.2\) fm\(/x_{Bj}\).
- Fixed mean Bjorken \(x\) value used in calculations: \(\langle x_{Bj} \rangle = 0.068\).
- Parameters for \(Q^2\)-\(x_{Bj}\) correlation: \(Q^2 = A + B x_{Bj}\) with \(A = 2.2\) GeV\(^2\) and \(B = 178\) GeV\(^2\).
- Mean \(Q^2\) value: \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 14.3\) GeV\(^2\).
- Grey tracks are associated with protons in the momentum interval \(200-600\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative hadronization model developed earlier by the authors (Ciofi degli Atti and Kopeliovich, 2004) and used in prior studies of nuclear attenuation of leading hadrons (Kopeliovich et al., 1999; Kopeliovich et al., 2000). It is complementary to the measurement of nuclear attenuation of leading inclusive hadrons, which focuses on rare events, while grey tracks cover the main bulk of inelastic DIS events. The approach enables discrimination between different hadronization models, such as the induced energy loss scenario of Wang and Wang (2002) and color string models (Amelin et al., 2001; Giessen group; Aichelin et al., 2000), by providing a sensitive \(Q^2\)-dependent observable that can be tested against future experiments.
