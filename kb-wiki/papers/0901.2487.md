# Variational wavefunction for multi-species spinful fermionic superfluids and superconductors

> We introduce a new fermionic variational wavefunction, generalizing the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) wavefunction, which is suitable for interacting multi-species spinful systems and sustaining superfluidity. Applications range from quark matter to the high temperature superconductors. A wide class of Hamiltonians, comprising interactions and hybridization of arbitrary momentum dependence between different fermion species, can be treated in a comprehensive manner. This is the case, as both the intra-species and the inter-species interactions are treated on equally rigorous footing, which is accomplished via the introduction of a new quantum index attached to the fermions. The index is consistent with known fermionic physics, and allows for heretofore unaccounted for fermion-fermion correlations. We have derived the finite temperature version of the theory, thus obtaining the renormalized quasiparticle dispersion relations, and we discuss the appearance of charge and spin density wave order. We present numerical solutions for two electron species in 2 dimensions. Based on these solutions, we show that, for equivalent spin up and down fermions, the Fermi occupation factor (per spin) equals 1/2 deep in the Fermi sea. This constitutes a unique experimental prediction of the theory, both for the normal and superfluid states. Interestingly, this result, obtained in the thermodynamic limit, is consistent with Fermi occupation factor (in-)equalities for finite systems of electrons, derived (in a different context) by Borland and Dennis, J. Phys. B {\bf 5}, 7 (1972) and by Altunbulak and Klyachko, Commun. Math. Phys. {\bf 282}, 287 (2008).

## Key claim

The paper introduces a variational wavefunction that generalizes the BCS wavefunction to multi-species spinful fermionic systems, claiming that for equivalent spin up and down fermions the Fermi occupation factor (per spin) equals 1/2 deep in the Fermi sea, both in the normal and superfluid states, provided the inter-species potential is not very strong.

## Method

The authors introduce a new quantum index attached to fermions, allowing fermion operators to be expressed as superpositions of indexed operators, and construct a variational wavefunction from commuting bosonic pair-creation operators for two fermion species. They evaluate the expectation value of a general Hamiltonian with arbitrary momentum-dependent intra-species and inter-species potentials, then minimize the energy with respect to variational angles to obtain ground states, solving the resulting equations numerically for a two-dimensional two-band system with realistic tight-binding dispersions.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies for two electron species in 2D range from -3.711 to -10.841 (in units of t₁) depending on interaction strengths V₁=V₂=5 or 10 and V₀ between 0.5 and 4
- The numerical solutions use an N×N discretization of the Brillouin zone with N=120, yielding 43,200 total variational variables (2 species × 6 angles × N²/4 due to C₄ symmetry)
- Filling factors are n₁=0.91 and n₂=0.81 for the two species, with different chemical potentials calculated self-consistently
- The superconducting gaps exhibit d_{x²-y²}-wave symmetry for moderate V₀ values, with one species showing a d-wave gap and the other showing no gap in the solutions presented

## Context

This work extends the BCS wavefunction (Bardeen, Cooper, Schrieffer, 1957) and its two-species generalizations by Moskalenko and by Suhl, Matthias, and Walker, which could only treat strictly BCS-type interactions. The new wavefunction enables comprehensive treatment of both intra-species and inter-species interactions with arbitrary momentum dependence, relevant to systems ranging from quark matter to high-temperature superconductors. The predicted Fermi occupation factor of 1/2 deep in the Fermi sea is consistent with constraints derived for finite electron systems by Borland and Dennis (1972) and by Altunbulak and Klyachko (2008), though derived in a different context.
