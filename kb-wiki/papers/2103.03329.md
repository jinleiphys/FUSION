# Smooth matching of $\hat{q}$ from hadronic to quark and gluon degrees of freedom

> One of the key signatures of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) is the energy loss of high momentum particles as they traverse the strongly interacting medium. The energy loss of these particles is governed by the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}/T^3$, wherein it has been thought that there is a large jump as the system transitions between the hadron gas and Quark Gluon Plasma phases. Here we calculate $\hat{q}/T^3$ within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model with the particle list PDG16+ and find that, if one incorporates the experimental error in the hadronic calculation of $\hat{q}/T^3$ and assumes a higher pseudo-critical temperature, then a smooth transition from the hadron gas phase into the Quark Gluon Plasma phase is possible. We also find a significant enhancement in $\hat{q}/T^3$ at finite baryon chemical potential and find issues with the relationship between the shear viscosity and the jet transport coefficient within a hadron gas phase.

# Smooth matching of $\hat{q}$ from hadronic to quark and gluon degrees of freedom

## Key claim

If one incorporates the experimental error in the hadronic calculation of $\hat{q}/T^3$ and assumes a higher pseudo-critical temperature (around 200 MeV), then a smooth transition from the hadron gas phase into the Quark Gluon Plasma phase is possible, contrary to the previously assumed large jump in $\hat{q}/T^3$ across the phase transition.

## Method

The authors calculate the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}/T^3$ within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model using the particle list PDG16+, applying Eq. (9) from the JET Collaboration paper (Burke 2013) which relates $\hat{q}_{HRG}$ to meson and baryon densities weighted by the nucleon density and the jet transport parameter for a quark at the center of a nucleus. They compare ideal HRG results to an excluded volume approach solved via a Lambert W function, and extend the calculation to finite baryon chemical potential along isentropes from a BSQ equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The prefactor $\hat{q}_N/\rho_N$ lies in the range 2.4 to 5.8, derived from $\hat{q}_N = (0.024 \pm 0.008)$ GeV$^2$/fm and $\rho_N = (0.15-0.17)$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The JET collaboration extracted RHIC value is $\hat{q}/T^3 = 4.6 \pm 1.2$.
- At $\mu_B = 500$ MeV, $\hat{q}_{HRG}/T^3$ is approximately double its value at $\mu_B = 0$.
- The excluded volume approach uses a maximum radius of $r = 0.25$ fm for the PDG16+ list.
- The extracted $\eta T/w$ from $\hat{q}$ is approximately 0.2 for an ideal HRG and approximately 0.3 for the excluded volume approach at $\mu_B = 0$, assuming $T_{pc} = 200$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the JET Collaboration summary (Burke 2013) which found a large jump in $\hat{q}/T^3$ between the hadron gas phase ($\hat{q}/T^3 \lesssim 1$) and the QGP phase ($\hat{q}/T^3 \sim 4.5$). The authors use a holography calculation (Rougemont 2017) that predicts a pseudo-critical temperature for $\hat{q}/T^3$ around 195 MeV, motivating the higher transition temperature. The results enable systematic studies of energy loss models with smoother decoupling temperatures, and the authors note that the JETSCAPE collaboration has released new $\hat{q}/T^3$ results (Cao 2021) that would also smoothly match to these hadronic results. The paper also tests the quasi-empirical relationship $\eta T/w \sim T^3/\hat{q}$ from Majumder (2007) and finds it does not hold consistently in the hadronic phase.
