# Nuclear Structure, Random Interactions and Mesoscopic Physics

> Standard concepts of nuclear physics explaining the systematics of ground state spins in nuclei by the presence of specific coherent terms in the nucleon-nucleon interaction were put in doubt by the observation that these systematics can be reproduced with high probability by randomly chosen rotationally invariant interactions. We review the recent development in this area, along with new original results of the authors. The self-organizing role of geometry in a finite mesoscopic system explains the main observed features in terms of the created mean field and correlations that are considered in analogy to the random phase approximation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed systematics of nuclear ground state spins (even-even nuclei having J0=0 and lowest isospin) can be reproduced with high probability by randomly chosen rotationally invariant interactions, casting doubt on the necessity of specific coherent terms in the nucleon-nucleon interaction; the authors propose that the self-organizing role of geometry in a finite mesoscopic system, rather than particular interaction details, explains the main observed features.

## Method

The authors review the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) approach, where two-body interaction matrix elements are treated as uncorrelated normally distributed random variables under the constraints of rotational and isospin invariance, and compare this to realistic nuclear shell-model calculations in the sd shell. They analyze the statistical properties of eigenstates using information entropy and the distribution of ground state spins across many random Hamiltonian realizations, and develop a geometric interpretation based on random vector coupling of angular momenta (geometric chaoticity) that explains the predominance of J=0 ground states without invoking specific pairing forces.

## Key numbers

- In the sd shell model with 12 valence fermions, there are 839 states in the class with lowest quantum numbers JT=00, while the largest class (JT=31) has d_JT=6706 states.
- The variance of the total projection M in the random walk model is given by (ΔM)² = N⟨m²⟩, where N is the number of active fermions.
- The maximum of the multiplicity of states with given spin J occurs near J = ΔM - 1/2, and the multiplicity grows proportional to √N.
- The GOE limit for information entropy is I_GOE = ln(0.48 d), where d is the space dimension.
- The realistic distributions of many-body matrix elements in nuclei are generically close to exponential rather than Gaussian.

## Context

This work extends the unexpected observation by Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (2000) that random two-body interactions statistically favor J=0 ground states, which challenged the conventional understanding that pairing forces (as formulated by Racah, and developed by Bohr, Mottelson and Pines, and Belyaev) are responsible for nuclear ground state spin systematics. The paper connects nuclear structure to mesoscopic physics and many-body quantum chaos, building on prior work by Brody et al. on random matrix theory and by Flambaum, Izrailev and Casati on equilibration in the TBRE framework. The geometric chaoticity concept enables a statistical understanding of ground state properties without invoking specific interaction details, and opens the door to studying correlations between states of different angular momentum classes in chaotic many-body systems.
