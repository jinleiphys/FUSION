# Equation of state of dense nuclear matter and neutron star structure from nuclear chiral interactions

> We report a new microscopic equation of state (EOS) of dense symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and asymmetric and $β$-stable nuclear matter at zero temperature using recent realistic two-body and three-body nuclear interactions derived in the framework of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and including the $Δ(1232)$ isobar intermediate state. This EOS is provided in tabular form and in parametrized form ready for use in numerical general relativity simulations of binary neutron star merging. Here we use our new EOS for $β$-stable nuclear matter to compute various structural properties of non-rotating neutron stars.The EOS is derived using the Brueckner--Bethe--Goldstone quantum many-body theory in the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approximation. Neutron star properties are next computed solving numerically the Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkov structure equations. Our EOS models are able to reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter, the symmetry energy $E_{sym}$, and its slope parameter $L$ at the empirical saturation density $n_{0}$. In addition, our EOS models are compatible with experimental data from collisions between heavy nuclei at energies ranging from a few tens of MeV up to several hundreds of MeV per nucleon. These experiments provide a selective test for constraining the nuclear EOS up to $\sim 4 n_0$. Our EOS models are consistent with present measured neutron star masses and particularly with the mass $M = 2.01 \pm 0.04 \, M_{\odot}$ of the neutron stars in PSR~J0348+0432.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new microscopic equation of state for dense nuclear matter derived from chiral nuclear interactions that include the Δ(1232) isobar, which achieves numerical convergence in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock scheme up to densities typical of neutron star maximum mass configurations, unlike Δ-less chiral interactions that fail to converge above approximately 0.5 fm⁻³.

## Method
The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation within the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, using the local N3LO chiral two-nucleon potential including Δ isobar excitations (model b from Maria et al.) supplemented with density-dependent effective two-body forces derived from N2LO chiral three-nucleon forces with Δ isobar excitations. They then computed neutron star properties by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov structure equations numerically.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: n₀ = 0.171 fm⁻³ (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1 model) and 0.176 fm⁻³ (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2 model)
- Energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = -15.23 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1) and -15.09 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 35.39 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1) and 36.00 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2)
- Symmetry energy slope parameter: L = 76.0 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1) and 79.8 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2)
- Incompressibility: K_∞ = 190 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ1) and 176 MeV (N3LOΔ+N2LOΔ2)
- Energy difference between the two TNF models at n = 1.0 fm⁻³: approximately 41.5 MeV
- Relative change in BHF potential energy per nucleon at n = 1.0 fm⁻³ when including partial waves up to J_max = 10 versus J_max = 8: 0.0035

## Context
This work extends previous BHF calculations using chiral nuclear interactions by Logoteta et al. (2015, 2016a, 2016b) to higher baryon densities (above 2.5 n₀) relevant for neutron star astrophysics. The results are compatible with experimental elliptic flow data from heavy-ion collisions by Danielewicz et al. (2002) that constrain the nuclear equation of state up to approximately 4 n₀, and with the measured mass M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙ of the neutron star in PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013). The equation of state is provided in tabular and parametrized forms for use in numerical general relativity simulations of binary neutron star mergers.
