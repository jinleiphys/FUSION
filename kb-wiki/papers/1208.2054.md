# Phenomenology of Axial-Vector Mesons from an Extended Linear Sigma Model

> We discuss the phenomenology of the axial-vector mesons within a three-flavour Linear Sigma Model containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) can accommodate the $a_1(1260)$ meson as a predominantly $\bar q q$ state, but a correct description of the strange axial-vector states $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ requires the inclusion of mixing between an axial-vector and a pseudovector nonet, which the current model does not implement.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-flavour Linear Sigma Model with chiral $U(3)_L \times U(3)_R$ symmetry containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector degrees of freedom (the eLSM). A global fit of 11 model parameters was performed against 21 observables including meson masses and decay widths, with experimental errors artificially increased to 5% where smaller to account for neglected isospin-breaking effects. The fit used data from the Particle Data Group (PDG) for quantities such as $f_\pi$, $f_K$, $m_\pi$, $m_K$, $m_\rho$, $m_{a_1}$, $\Gamma_{\rho \to \pi\pi}$, and $\Gamma_{a_1 \to \rho\pi}$.

## Key numbers

- $m_{a_1} = 1186 \pm 6$ MeV from the fit, compared to the experimental $1230 \pm 62$ MeV
- $\Gamma_{a_1 \to \rho\pi} = 549 \pm 43$ MeV from the fit, compared to the experimental $425 \pm 175$ MeV
- $\Gamma_{a_1 \to \pi\gamma} = 0.66 \pm 0.01$ MeV from the fit, compared to the experimental $0.64 \pm 0.25$ MeV
- $m_{K_1} = 1282$ MeV predicted for the strange axial-vector state, close to the PDG value $m_{K_1(1270)} = 1272 \pm 7$ MeV
- Predicted total decay width of $K_1$ is 264 MeV, while PDG reports $\Gamma_{K_1(1270)} = 90 \pm 20$ MeV and $\Gamma_{K_1(1400)} = 174 \pm 13$ MeV
- Fit yields $\chi^2 = 12.33$, corresponding to $\chi^2/(21\ \text{observables} - 11\ \text{parameters}) = 1.23$

## Context

This work extends the three-flavour Linear Sigma Model approach previously developed by the same group (Parganlija et al., 2012; Kovacs et al., 2012) to include axial-vector mesons and perform a global fit. It addresses the longstanding ambiguity in the $a_1(1260)$ resonance, whose decay width ranges from 250 to 600 MeV in PDG listings, and tests whether it can be described as a $\bar q q$ state rather than a meson-meson molecule as suggested by Oset (2008). The results enable further study of mesons at finite temperature and density, particularly the expected restoration of chiral symmetry where vector and axial-vector mesons should become degenerate.
