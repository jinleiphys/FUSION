# Semiempirical Shell Model Masses with Magic Number Z = 126 for Superheavy Elements

> A semiempirical shell model mass equation applicable to superheavy elements up to Z = 126 is presented and shown to have a high predictive power. The equation is applied to the recently discovered superheavy nuclei Z = 118, A = 293 and Z = 114, A = 289 and their decay products.

# Semiempirical Shell Model Masses with Magic Number Z = 126 for Superheavy Elements

## Key claim

A semiempirical shell-model mass equation (SSME) using Z = 126 as the next spherical proton magic number after lead, rather than Z = 114, yields mass predictions for superheavy nuclei in region B (126 ≤ N ≤ 184, Z ≥ 82) that do not show increasing deviations as Z approaches 114, but the authors caution that these results are not a proof of superior magicity of Z = 126 compared to other recently proposed predictions because no comparative mass studies of this kind were made.

## Method

The authors take an early version of the SSME that was adjusted separately in shell regions using Z = 126 as a shell region boundary, and apply it to region B (126 ≤ N ≤ 184, Z ≥ 82) with the total nuclear energy written as a sum of pairing, deformation, and Coulomb energies. The deformation term includes particle-hole-symmetric configuration interaction terms with polynomial functions of N and Z, and the adjustable parameters were determined by a least-squares adjustment to 211 experimental masses from data available up to Spring 1973. The predictions are then compared to 56 new masses that were not used in the original adjustments, and applied to α-decay chains from recent superheavy element experiments.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation from the original 211 mass data for ΔM is 126 keV for the complete equation, compared to 1076 keV for the pairing-plus-Coulomb part alone without deformation terms.
- For the 56 new masses not used in the adjustments, the rms deviation for ΔM is 236 keV, with an average deviation of 53 keV.
- For the 49 nuclei with N ≥ 129 extending into the interior of the shell region, the deviations are smaller than the unbracketed values except for Qα.
- For the α-decay chain of ²⁹³118 and its decay products, the average deviation of predicted Qα values from data is −197 keV and the rms deviation is 308 keV.
- For the α-decay chain of ²⁸⁹114 and its decay products, the average deviation is 847 keV and the rms deviation is 905 keV.
- For the conceivable parent ²⁸⁸112, the average deviation is −181 keV and rms deviation is 366 keV; for ²⁹¹113, the average deviation is −242 keV and rms deviation is 417 keV.

## Context

This work extends the semiempirical shell-model mass equation approach of Liran, Marinov, and Zeldes (1970s) which originally assumed Z = 114 as the next proton magic number, and responds to recent experimental evidence from Ninov et al. (1999) showing a smooth α-decay chain from ²⁹³118 that seems to preclude Z = 114 as a magic proton number. The study is motivated by phenomenological BE(2) systematics from Zamfir et al. (1995) and Wigner term studies from Zeldes (1998) indicating Z = 126 as the next spherical proton magic number, and it provides a predictive tool for superheavy element research in region B until a new SSME adjustment to data in both regions A and B is undertaken. The results are compared with predictions from macroscopic-microscopic models such as Möller et al. (1995) and Möller, Nix, and Kratz (1997), and with self-consistent and relativistic mean field calculations from Cwiok et al. (1996), Rutz et al. (1997), Bender et al. (1999), and Kruppa et al. (2000) which variously predict proton magicities for Z = 114, 120, 124, and 126.
