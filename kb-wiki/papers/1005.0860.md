# Phase Fluctuations near the Chiral Critical Point

> The Helmholtz free energy density is parametrized as a function of temperature and baryon density near the chiral critical point of QCD. The parametrization incorporates the expected critical exponents and amplitudes. An expansion away from equilibrium states is achieved with Landau theory. This is used to calculate the probability that the system is found at a density other than the equilibrium one. Such fluctuations are predicted to be very large in heavy ion collisions.

# Phase Fluctuations near the Chiral Critical Point

## Key claim
The Helmholtz free energy density parametrization near the QCD chiral critical point, incorporating 3D Ising model critical exponents and amplitudes, predicts that density fluctuations in heavy ion collisions are very large, with the probability to find the system at a density anywhere between ±25% of the critical density exceeding 50% for a volume of 400 fm³. This result depends on the choice of critical exponents (particularly δ = 4.815, much larger than mean field values) and the specific parametrization of the free energy.

## Method
The Helmholtz free energy density f(n,T) is parametrized as a function of baryon density n and temperature T near the chiral critical point using a modified Landau expansion with a non-integer power σ = δ + 1 = 5.815. The coefficient functions f₀(t), f₁(t), f₂(t), and f_σ(t) are constructed to reproduce the critical exponents of the 3D Ising model (α = 0.11, β = 0.325, γ = 1.24, δ = 4.815) and the universal amplitude ratios (κ₊/κ₋ ≈ 5, c₊/c₋ ≈ 0.5), while maintaining sensible limits as T → 0 and μ → 0. The probability of density fluctuations away from equilibrium is then calculated using Landau theory for a finite volume system.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature T_c = 140 MeV and critical chemical potential μ_c derived from the quadratic critical curve with T₀ = 180 MeV and μ₀ = 1230 MeV
- Critical exponents used: α = 0.11, β = 0.325, γ = 1.24, δ = 4.815 (3D Ising model values)
- The non-integer power in the free energy expansion: σ = δ + 1 = 5.815
- Constant coefficient f_σ = 5P_c ≈ 512 MeV/fm³
- Volume used for fluctuation calculations: V = 400 fm³
- Estimated critical density: about 5n₀ ≈ 0.75 baryons/fm³, implying about 300 baryons participate in the fluctuation
- Probability to find the system with density between ±25% of the critical density is greater than 50%

## Context
This work extends prior parametrizations of the QCD equation of state near the chiral critical point, particularly the approach blending a 3D Ising model equation of state into quark and hadron equations of state (attract). It differs from mean field treatments (which give α = 0, β = 1/2, γ = 1, δ = 3) by using the 3D Ising model exponents. The parametrization is designed to be usable in dynamical simulations of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and FAIR, and raises open questions about whether the transition proceeds via nucleation (nucleate), spinodal decomposition (Randrup), or other mechanisms.
