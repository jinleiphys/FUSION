# Polarization observables in exclusive kaon photoproduction on the deuteron

> Single and double polarization observables for kaon photoproduction on the deuteron are studied theoretically with modern hyperon-nucleon forces. The kinematical region of the kaon scattered forward with large momentum is thoroughly investigated where either the quasifree scattering leading to the kaon and hyperon or the final-state interaction between hyperon and nucleon are expected. The quasifree scatterings show characteristic peaks in the inclusive cross sections. The final-state interaction effects are significant, especially around the Lambda-N and Sigma-N thresholds. The double polarization C_z is found to be sensitive to the final-state interaction effects. Precise data would help to clarify the property of the Lambda-N--Sigma-N interaction and also help to extract the information on the elementary amplitude from the quasifree scattering region.

## Key claim

The double polarization observable \(C_z\) is found to be sensitive to hyperon-nucleon final-state interaction effects, especially near the \(\Lambda N\) and \(\Sigma N\) thresholds, and precise data would help clarify the \(\Lambda N\)-\(\Sigma N\) interaction and extract information on the elementary amplitude from the quasifree scattering region.

## Method

The authors calculated single and double polarization observables for exclusive kaon photoproduction on the deuteron (\(\gamma d \to K^+ Y N\)) using modern hyperon-nucleon (YN) forces (NSC89 and NSC97f) and a kaon photoproduction operator based on an effective Lagrangian approach with nucleon and \(\Delta\) resonances. The nuclear matrix element was evaluated in the deuteron rest frame (lab frame) using nonrelativistic two-baryon wave functions, and the exclusive cross section was obtained by integrating over the final-state phase space. The observables studied include the hyperon recoil polarization \(P_y\), beam polarization asymmetry \(\Sigma\), and double polarizations \(C_x\) and \(C_z\), with the kaon scattered forward at angles less than \(20^\circ\) and an incoming photon energy of 1.3 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive cross section peaks at kaon lab momentum \(p_K \approx 950\) MeV/\(c\) and \(p_K \approx 810\) MeV/\(c\) for \(\theta_K = 1^\circ\) and \(E_\gamma = 1.3\) GeV, corresponding to quasifree scattering conditions.
- The \(\Sigma N\) threshold cusp structure in the inclusive cross section is prominent for NSC97f but shows only a little deviation from PWIA for NSC89.
- For the exclusive \(^2{\rm H}(\gamma, K^+ \Lambda)n\) process at \(p_K = 870\) MeV/\(c\) and \(\theta_K = 1^\circ\) (near the \(\Sigma N\) threshold), the double polarization \(C_z\) with FSI deviates from PWIA even at \(\theta_\Lambda' = 0^\circ\) and drastically above \(\theta_\Lambda' = 20^\circ\).
- For the exclusive \(^2{\rm H}(\gamma, K^+ \Sigma^-)p\) process at \(p_K = 860\) MeV/\(c\) and \(\theta_K = 1^\circ\), the available \(\Sigma\) lab angles are limited to less than \(\theta_\Sigma' = 21^\circ\).
- For the exclusive \(^2{\rm H}(\gamma, K^+ \Lambda)n\) process at \(p_K = 944\) MeV/\(c\) and \(\theta_K = 1^\circ\) (near the \(\Lambda\) QFS peak), PWIA dominates up to \(\theta_\Lambda' = 30^\circ\) in the differential cross section.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inclusive and exclusive kaon photoproduction on the deuteron by Yamamura et al. (1999) using modern hyperon-nucleon forces, and it provides a more detailed theoretical investigation of polarization observables that are experimentally accessible at facilities like JLab, ELSA, GRAAL, and SPRING8. The results are compared with the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and two YN interaction models (NSC89 and NSC97f), which both reproduce the hypertriton binding energy but differ in their \(\Lambda N\)-\(\Sigma N\) partial wave structures; the analysis aims to supply additional information on the YN interaction via the continuum, as rigorous four-body calculations by Nogga et al. have shown that neither model reproduces the bound states of \(^4_\Lambda\)H and \(^4_\Lambda\)He.
