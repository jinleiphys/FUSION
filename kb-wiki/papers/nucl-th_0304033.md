# Everything you always wanted to know about SUSY, but were afraid to ask

> New experimental tests of nuclear supersymmetry are suggested. They involve the measurement of one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions between nuclei that belong to the same supermultiplet. These reactions provide a direct test of the `fermionic' sector, i.e. of the operators that change a boson into a fermion or vice versa. We present some theoretical predictions for the supersymmetric quartet of nuclei: 194Pt, 195Pt, 195Au and 196Au.

## Key claim
New experimental tests of nuclear supersymmetry are suggested, specifically involving one- and two-nucleon transfer reactions between nuclei in the same supermultiplet. The authors hedge that while existing spectroscopic data for the quartet ¹⁹⁴Pt, ¹⁹⁵Pt, ¹⁹⁵Au, and ¹⁹⁶Au can be described to a good approximation by the U(6/12)ν ⊗ U(6/4)π supersymmetry, the crucial fermionic sector (operators that change a boson into a fermion) has not been directly tested, and it remains to be seen whether the predicted correlations are verified by experiments.

## Method
The authors derive one-proton transfer operators as tensor operators under the Spin(6), Spin(5), and Spin(3) subgroups of the U(6/12)ν ⊗ U(6/4)π supersymmetry chain, obtaining closed-form expressions for spectroscopic factors and selection rules. They apply these operators to predict the relative intensities of one-proton transfer from the ground state of ¹⁹⁴Pt to ¹⁹⁵Au and from ¹⁹⁵Pt to ¹⁹⁶Au, using the same Hamiltonian coefficients previously fitted to excitation energies of the quartet. The predictions are compared to existing data from (α,t) and (³He,d) reactions on ¹⁹⁴Pt, and new predictions are made for the unmeasured ¹⁹⁵Pt → ¹⁹⁶Au transfer.

## Key numbers
- For the one-proton transfer ¹⁹⁴Pt → ¹⁹⁵Au, the ratio of excited-state to ground-state intensity for operator T₂ is R₂ = 1.12 (with N = 5 bosons).
- For the same reaction, the ground state of ¹⁹⁵Au (J = 3/2) is excited with spectroscopic factor C²S = 0.175, while the first excited J = 3/2 state has C²S = 0.019, from experimental data on ¹⁹⁴Pt(α,t)¹⁹⁵Au and ¹⁹⁴Pt(³He,d)¹⁹⁵Au (Munger, year not given).
- The ratio R₂ for both ¹⁹⁴Pt → ¹⁹⁵Au and ¹⁹⁵Pt → ¹⁹⁶Au is given by the formula 9(N+1)(N+5) / [4(N+6)²], which equals 1.12 for N = 5.

## Context
This work extends the dynamical supersymmetry framework introduced by Iachello (1980) in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and its extension to odd-mass nuclei via the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM). It builds on prior classifications of the Pt-Au quartet by Bijker, Barea, and Frank (previous papers) and on high-resolution transfer experiments at TU/LMU München and PSI/Bonn that refined the level schemes of ¹⁹⁵Pt and ¹⁹⁶Au. The paper enables future experimental tests of the fermionic generators of supersymmetry, which have been less studied than the bosonic sector (energies and electromagnetic transitions), and suggests that two-nucleon transfer reactions such as ¹⁹⁴Pt(α,d)¹⁹⁶Au could provide even more sensitive tests of the wave functions.
