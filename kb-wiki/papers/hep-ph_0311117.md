# Meson-Baryon Couplings from QCD Sum Rules

> Coupling constants of the pseudoscalar mesons to the octet baryons are calculated in the QCD sum rule approach. Two-point correlation function of the baryons are evaluated in a single meson state and the vacuum, which yields the designated coupling. The emphasis is on the flavor SU(3) structure of the coupling constants and reliability in extracting the coupling constants from the two-point correlation functions. We first calculate the baryon-diagonal couplings and study the reliability of the sum rule. The F/D ratio of the coupling is determined in the SU(3) limit. We further formulate the baryon-off-diagonal couplings using the projected correlation functions and the vertex functions, so that the unwanted excited states do not contaminate the sum rule. As an example, the (π- Λ- Σ) coupling constant is calculated and the flavor SU(3) breaking effect is studied. We find that the effect of SU(3) breaking on the (π- Λ- Σ) coupling constant is small.

## Key claim

The authors determine the F/D ratio for pseudoscalar meson-octet baryon couplings directly from QCD in the SU(3) symmetric limit using the tensor (T) Dirac structure of the two-point correlation function, finding that the ratio is close to the SU(6) quark model value of 2/3 and consistent with the axialvector current ratio from hyperon semileptonic decays, but only when the baryon interpolating field parameter t is chosen outside the region near t ~ -0.6 where the OPE convergence is poor.

## Method

The authors evaluate two-point correlation functions of octet baryon interpolating fields in a single pseudoscalar meson state, expand the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 7 for the tensor (T) structure and dimension 8 for the pseudoscalar (PS) structure, and match to a phenomenological double-pole term representing the ground-state meson-baryon coupling after Borel transformation. They compare three Dirac structures (PV, PS, T) and select the T structure as most reliable based on its insensitivity to the continuum threshold, its weak dependence on the baryon interpolating field parameter t, and its independence from the choice of effective interaction Lagrangian. For the diagonal couplings (πNN, ηNN, πΣΣ, ηΣΣ, πΞΞ, ηΞΞ) they extract the coupling constants by linear fitting of the Borel-transformed sum rule to eliminate single-pole contamination, and then determine the F/D ratio from the SU(3) relations among the couplings.

## Key numbers

- F/D ratio = 0.65 ± 0.10, determined from the T sum rule in the SU(3) limit with Borel window 0.65 ≤ M² ≤ 1.24 GeV² and continuum threshold s_th = 2.07 GeV²
- The F/D ratio is insensitive to the continuum threshold: changing s_th from 2.07 GeV² to 2.57 GeV² changes the result by less than the quoted uncertainty
- The T sum rule results for g_mB λ_B²(t) show zeros at t = 1 and near t ~ -0.6, with an extremum near t ~ 0.2
- The PV sum rule result changes by about 15% at M² = 1 GeV² when the continuum threshold is varied from s_th = 2.07 GeV² to 2.57 GeV², while the PS and T structures change by only 2-3%
- The appropriate region for extracting F/D is defined by cosθ ≤ 0.61 (t ≥ 1.3) giving F/D ≤ 0.75, and cosθ ≥ -0.75 (t ≤ -0.9) giving F/D ≥ 0.55

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to meson-baryon couplings pioneered by Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1985) and improved by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) and Kim, Lee and Oka (1998), who studied the πNN coupling and identified the tensor structure as most reliable. The authors generalize the method to the full SU(3) octet of baryons and mesons, determining the F/D ratio directly from QCD without relying on the Goldberger-Treiman relation or semileptonic decay data. The result enables construction of hyperon-nucleon potentials from QCD-based coupling constants rather than treating the F/D ratio as a free parameter as done in the Nijmegen potential models, and provides a baseline for studying SU(3) breaking effects in off-diagonal couplings such as πΛΣ.
