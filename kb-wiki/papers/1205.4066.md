# Medium modification of the charged current neutrino opacity and its implications

> Previous work on neutrino emission from proto-neutron stars which employed full solutions of the Boltzmann equation showed that the average energies of emitted electron neutrinos and antineutrinos are closer to one another than predicted by older, more approximate work. This in turn implied that the neutrino driven wind is proton rich during its entire life, precluding $r$-process nucleosynthesis and the synthesis of Sr, Y, and Zr. This work relied on charged current neutrino interaction rates that are appropriate for a free nucleon gas. Here, it is shown in detail that the inclusion of the nucleon potential energies and collisional broadening of the response significantly alters this conclusion. Iso-vector interactions, which give rise to the nuclear symmetry energy, produce a difference between the neutron and proton single-particle energies $ΔU=U_n-U_p$ and alter the kinematics of the charged current reactions. In neutron-rich matter, and for a given neutrino/antineutrino energy, the rate for $ν_e+n\rightarrow e^-+p$ is enhanced while $ \barν_e+p\rightarrow n+e^+$ is suppressed because the $Q$ value for these reactions is altered by $\pmΔU$, respectively. In the neutrino decoupling region, collisional broadening acts to enhance both $ν_e$ and $\barν_e$ cross-sections and RPA corrections decrease the $ν_e$ cross-section and increase the $\bar ν_e$ cross-section, but mean field shifts have a larger effect. Therefore, electron neutrinos decouple at lower temperature than when the nucleons are assumed to be free and have lower average energies. The change is large enough to allow for a reasonable period of time when the neutrino driven wind is predicted to be neutron rich. It is also shown that the electron fraction in the wind is influenced by the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The inclusion of nucleon potential energies (mean field shifts) and collisional broadening in charged current neutrino interaction rates significantly alters previous conclusions about neutrino-driven winds: the change is large enough to allow for a reasonable period of time when the neutrino-driven wind is predicted to be neutron rich, potentially enabling r-process nucleosynthesis, whereas earlier work using free-nucleon rates had predicted a persistently proton-rich wind that precluded such nucleosynthesis.

## Method

The authors calculated charged current neutrino absorption rates for \(\nu_e + n \rightarrow e^- + p\) and \(\bar{\nu}_e + p \rightarrow n + e^+\) in dense nuclear matter by incorporating mean field energy shifts \(\Delta U = U_n - U_p\) (arising from the nuclear symmetry energy) into the nucleon dispersion relations and reaction kinematics. They also included collisional broadening by replacing the energy-conserving delta function in the polarization function with a Lorentzian of width \(\Gamma = 1/\tau\), using spin relaxation times from Bacca (2011), and studied Random Phase Approximation (RPA) correlations with constant particle-hole interactions. The resulting opacities were applied to proto-neutron star conditions to assess their impact on neutrino decoupling and the electron fraction of the neutrino-driven wind.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon potential difference is given by \(\Delta U \approx 40 \times (n_n - n_p)/n_0\) MeV, where \(n_0 = 0.16\) nucleons/fm\(^3\) is saturation density.
- At \(n_B = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(T = 8\) MeV, and \(Y_e = 0.027\), the inverse mean free path for 12 MeV \(\nu_e\) increases from \(5.9 \times 10^{-4}\) m\(^{-1}\) (no mean field) to \(5.2 \times 10^{-3}\) m\(^{-1}\) (with mean field, \(\Gamma = 0\)).
- At the same conditions, the inverse mean free path for 12 MeV \(\bar{\nu}_e\) decreases from \(3.5 \times 10^{-4}\) m\(^{-1}\) (no mean field) to \(2.7 \times 10^{-5}\) m\(^{-1}\) (with mean field, \(\Gamma = 0\)).
- Collisional broadening with \(\Gamma = 4\) MeV increases the axial portion of the \(\nu_e\) absorption cross-section by approximately 80% relative to the \(\Gamma = 0\) case at the same ambient conditions.
- At \(n_B = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\), including both mean fields and density-dependent \(\Gamma\) (from Bacca 2011) gives \(1/\lambda_{\nu_e} = 7.5 \times 10^{-3}\) m\(^{-1}\) and \(1/\lambda_{\bar{\nu}_e} = 4.5 \times 10^{-5}\) m\(^{-1}\) for 12 MeV neutrinos.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Reddy (1998) for charged current neutrino opacities in interacting nuclear matter and corrects the treatment of mean field effects in earlier work by Bruenn (1985) and Burrows (1999), which neglected the potential energy difference between incoming and outgoing nucleons in asymmetric matter. The results contradict the conclusions of Roberts (2012) and Fischer (2010, 2012), which used free-nucleon rates and found persistently proton-rich winds; the present work shows that including mean field shifts and collisional broadening can produce neutron-rich conditions suitable for r-process nucleosynthesis. The findings also demonstrate that the electron fraction in the neutrino-driven wind is influenced by the nuclear symmetry energy, connecting supernova neutrino emission to the equation of state of dense matter.
