# Wigner functional of fermionic fields

> The Wigner function, which provides a phase-space description of quantum systems, has various applications in quantum mechanics, quantum kinetic theory, quantum optics, radiation transport and others. The concept of Wigner function has been extended to quantum fields, scalar and electromagnetic. Then, one deals with the Wigner functional which gives a distribution of field and its conjugate momentum. We introduce here the Wigner functional of fermionic fields of the values in a Grassmann algebra. Properties of the functional are discussed and its equation of motion, which is of the Liouville's form, is derived.

# Wigner functional of fermionic fields

## Key claim

The Wigner functional for fermionic fields, defined via Berezin (Grassmann) integrals, satisfies a Liouville-type equation of motion without quantum corrections, in contrast to the bosonic case where the Liouville form holds only for free fields or when quantum corrections are neglected. This occurs because the Dirac equation is linear in the fermion field, so the fermionic Wigner functional remains "classical" except for effects related to the Pauli principle.

## Method

The author defines the Wigner functional for fermionic fields by analogy with the bosonic case, replacing the ordinary functional integral with a functional Berezin integral over Grassmann-valued fields. The definition is tested on a Gaussian density matrix, yielding an expected exponential form. The author then proves that the functional correctly reproduces operator expectation values: for operators depending only on the field or only on the conjugate momentum the correspondence is direct, while for mixed products the Wigner functional gives the antisymmetrized combination. The equation of motion is derived from the von Neumann equation for the density operator, splitting the Dirac Hamiltonian into gradient, mass, and interaction terms, and computing each contribution using Grassmann calculus and functional derivatives.

## Key numbers

- The Gaussian Wigner functional is computed as \( W[\Psi,\Pi] = C \, e^{-\int d^3x [\Psi({\bf x}) A\Psi({\bf x}) - \Pi({\bf x}) A^{-1}\Pi({\bf x})]} \), where \(A\) is an antisymmetric matrix and \(C\) is a constant.
- The anticommutation relation for the Dirac field operators is \( \{ \hat{\Psi}_\alpha (t,{\bf x}), \hat{\Pi}_\beta (t,{\bf x'}) \} = \delta^{\alpha \beta} \delta^{(3)}({\bf x} - {\bf x'}) \) with spinor indices \(\alpha, \beta = 1, 2, 3, 4\).
- The mass term in the equation of motion yields a pathological term proportional to \( {\rm Tr}[\gamma^0] \, \delta^{(3)}({\bf x}=0) \), which vanishes because \( {\rm Tr}[\gamma^\mu]=0 \), and is exactly canceled by a similar term from the functional derivative manipulation.

## Context

This work extends the Wigner functional formalism previously developed for real scalar fields by Mrowczynski (1994) and for electromagnetic fields by Bialynicki-Birula (2000) to fermionic fields valued in a Grassmann algebra. It builds on earlier studies of phase-space densities for systems with Grassmann variables by Berezin (1976) and Cahill (1999). The result enables a phase-space description of fermionic quantum fields, with the Liouville equation providing a classical-like evolution that may be useful for nonperturbative studies of gauge fields and quantum gravity, complementing the Schrodinger functional approach developed by Cornwall (1974), Barnes (1978), and Floreanini (1987).
