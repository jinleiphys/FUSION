# Tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distribution functions in a spin-one hadron

> To understand orbital-angular-momentum contributions is becoming crucial for clarifying nucleon-spin issue in the parton level. Twist-two structure functions b_1 and b_2 for spin-one hadrons could probe orbital-angular-momentum effects, which reflect a different aspect from current studies for the spin-1/2 nucleon, since they should vanish if internal constituents are in the S state. These structure functions are related to tensor structure in spin-one hadrons. Studies of such tensor structure will open a new field of high-energy spin physics. The structure functions b_1 and b_2 are described by tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distributions delta_T-q and delta_T-qbar. Using HERMES data on the b_1 structure function for the deuteron, we made an analysis of extracting the distributions delta_T-q and delta_T-qbar in a simple x-dependent functional form. Optimum distributions are proposed for the tensor-polarized valence and antiquark distribution functions from the analysis. A finite tensor polarization is obtained for antiquarks if we impose a constraint that the first moments of tensor-polarized valence-quark distributions vanish. It is interesting to investigate a physics mechanism to create a finite tensor-polarized antiquark distribution.

# Tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distribution functions in a spin-one hadron

## Key claim
The twist-two structure functions b1 and b2 for spin-one hadrons can probe orbital-angular-momentum effects because they vanish if internal constituents are in the S state. Using HERMES data on the b1 structure function for the deuteron, the analysis extracts tensor-polarized quark and antiquark distributions in a simple x-dependent functional form, finding that a finite tensor polarization is obtained for antiquarks if the first moments of tensor-polarized valence-quark distributions are constrained to vanish.

## Method
The author analyzed HERMES experimental data on the b1 structure function for the deuteron at leading order in the running coupling constant alpha_s, with Q^2 fixed at 2.5 GeV^2 (the average Q^2 of the HERMES measurements). Tensor-polarized distributions were parametrized as unpolarized PDFs in the deuteron multiplied by a common weight function delta_T w(x) = a x^b (1-x)^c (x_0 - x), with an additional constant alpha_qbar for antiquark distributions. Two analyses were performed: Set 1 with tensor-polarized antiquark distributions terminated (alpha_qbar = 0) and Set 2 allowing finite tensor-polarized antiquark distributions (alpha_qbar as a free parameter). The MSTW LO parametrization was used for unpolarized PDFs, and isospin symmetry was assumed to relate neutron PDFs to proton PDFs.

## Key numbers
- Set 1 (no antiquark tensor polarization): chi^2/d.o.f. = 2.83, a = 0.378 +/- 0.212, b = 0.706 +/- 0.324, c = 1.0 (fixed), x_0 = 0.229, alpha_qbar = 0.0 (fixed)
- Set 2 (finite antiquark tensor polarization): chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.57, a = 0.221 +/- 0.174, b = 0.648 +/- 0.342, c = 1.0 (fixed), x_0 = 0.221, alpha_qbar = 3.20 +/- 2.75
- The integral of b1(x) from the analysis with finite antiquark polarization gives: integral dx b1(x) = 0.0058
- HERMES experimental results for comparison: integral from 0.002 to 0.85 of b1(x) = [1.05 +/- 0.34 (stat) +/- 0.35 (sys)] x 10^{-2} and integral from 0.02 to 0.85 of b1(x) = [0.35 +/- 0.10 (stat) +/- 0.18 (sys)] x 10^{-2} in the restricted range with Q^2 > 1 GeV^2

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of spin-one hadron structure functions originally introduced by Frankel and Szwed (1983) and Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989), and follows the first measurement of b1 by the HERMES collaboration in 2005. The analysis provides the first parametrization of tensor-polarized valence-quark and antiquark distributions, enabling comparisons with theoretical model calculations such as the D-state admixture model of Frankel and Szwed (1983) and Kumano and Hama (1991), pion contributions by Miller (2007), and shadowing effects. The results motivate future measurements of b1 at JLab and Drell-Yan processes with polarized deuteron at J-PARC and GSI-FAIR, as well as theoretical investigation of the physics mechanism creating finite tensor-polarized antiquark distributions.
