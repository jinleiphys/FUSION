# Saturation of low-energy antiproton annihilation on nuclei

> Recent measurements of very low-energy ($p_{L}<100$ MeV/c) $\bar p$ annihilation on light nuclei reveal apparent suppression of annihilation upon increasing the atomic charge $Z$ and mass number $A$. Using $\bar p$-nucleus optical potentials $V_{\rm opt}$, fitted to $\bar p$-atom energy-shifts and -widths, we resolve this suppression as due to the strong effective repulsion produced by the very absorptive $V_{\rm opt}$. The low-energy $\bar p$-nucleus wavefunction is kept substantially outside the nuclear surface and the resulting reaction cross section saturates as function of the strength of Im $V_{\rm opt}$. This feature, for $E >0$, parallels the recent prediction, for $E < 0$, that the level widths of $\bar p$ atoms saturate and, hence, that $\bar p$ deeply bound atomic states are relatively narrow. Antiproton annihilation cross sections are calculated at $p_{L}=57$ MeV/c across the periodic table, and their dependence on $Z$ and $A$ is classified and discussed with respect to the Coulomb focussing effect at very low energies.

# Saturation of low-energy antiproton annihilation on nuclei

## Key claim
The apparent suppression of antiproton annihilation cross sections on light nuclei at very low momenta (p_L < 100 MeV/c) is resolved not as a genuine reduction in annihilation strength, but as a consequence of strong effective repulsion from a highly absorptive optical potential, which keeps the antiproton wavefunction outside the nuclear surface and causes the reaction cross section to saturate as a function of the imaginary potential strength.

## Method
The authors constructed antiproton-nucleus optical potentials of the t-rho form, fitted to antiprotonic atom energy shifts and widths for 3He and 4He (2p states), and separately to a comprehensive set of atomic data across the periodic table. They then used these potentials in Schrodinger-type equations to calculate total reaction (annihilation) cross sections at positive energies, particularly at p_L = 57 MeV/c, and compared the results to experimental data for 4He and Ne.

## Key numbers
- Calculated annihilation cross section for 57 MeV/c antiprotons on 4He: 901 mb, in excellent agreement with the interpolated experimental value of 915 ± 39 mb (Bianconi et al., 2000).
- Calculated cross sections for 4He at 47.0 and 70.4 MeV/c: 1060 and 771 mb, respectively, compared to experimental values of 979 ± 145 and 827 ± 38 mb (Zenoni et al., 1999).
- The Ne/4He ratio of sigma_R changes from about 15 in the perturbative regime of very weak absorptivity to about 3 in the strong-absorption regime.
- The asymptotic scaling value sigma_R / S approaches 4 pi r_0 / a_B^(p) ≈ 0.273, with r_0 = 1.25 fm and a_B^(p) = 57.6 fm.
- For Pb at p_L = 57 MeV/c, l_max is about 6 for antiprotons but less than 2 for antineutrons.

## Context
This work extends the optical model approach previously successful for hadronic atoms (Batty, Friedman, Gal, 1997) and antiprotonic atoms (Batty, Friedman, Gal, 1995) to the positive-energy regime of antiproton annihilation on nuclei. It parallels the recent prediction (Friedman, Gal, 1999) that level widths of antiproton atoms saturate, implying relatively narrow deeply bound antiproton atomic states. The results contradict the simple ZA^{1/3} scaling expected from Coulomb focussing (Bianconi et al., 2000) at 57 MeV/c, showing that this scaling is only approached asymptotically for unrealistically high A values. The work enables predictions of antiproton annihilation cross sections across the periodic table and connects antiproton atom data to low-energy annihilation measurements.
