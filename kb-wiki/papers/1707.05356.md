# Proton emission with a screened electrostatic barrier

> Half-lives of proton emission for Z$\ge$51 nuclei are calculated within a simple analytical model based on the WKB approximation for the barrier penetration probability which includes the centrifugal and overlapping effects besides the electrostatic repulsion. The model has a single free parameter associated to a Hulthen potential which emulates a Coulomb electrostatic interaction only at short distance. The agreement with experimental data is very good for most of the considered nuclei. Theoretical predictions are made for few cases with uncertain emitting state configuration or incomplete decay information. The model's assignment of the proton orbital momentum is in agreement with the differentiation of the experimental data by orbital momentum values realized with a newly introduced correlation formula.

# Proton emission with a screened electrostatic barrier

## Key claim

The authors claim that a simple analytical WKB model with a single free parameter, the screening parameter of a Hulthen potential used to emulate the Coulomb electrostatic interaction only at short distance, can reproduce experimental proton-emission half-lives for Z≥51 nuclei with a standard deviation of σ=0.418, comparable to a four-parameter universal decay law fit (σ=0.395). The hedge is that the model's agreement is "very good for most of the considered nuclei," with notable discrepancies for ¹³¹Eu, ¹³⁵Tb, and ¹⁴¹Ho, where theoretical log₁₀(T₁/₂) values overestimate experiment by approximately 0.9.

## Method

The authors calculate proton-emission half-lives using the WKB approximation for barrier penetration, with the total potential separated into an inner preformation region (parametrized as a quadratic polynomial matched at the touching configuration) and an outer region consisting of a centrifugal term (with Langer correction) plus a Hulthen electrostatic potential. The Hulthen potential, characterized by a single screening parameter a, replaces the usual Coulomb potential to account for finite-size nuclear effects and short-range deviations. The screening parameter is fixed by minimizing the standard deviation of log₁₀(T_th/T_exp) over 41 measured ground-state and isomeric proton emitters with Z>50.

## Key numbers

- Screening parameter a = 1.299×10⁻³ fm⁻¹, obtained by fitting 41 experimental data points.
- Standard deviation of the fit: σ = 0.418.
- For comparison, the four-parameter universal decay law (UDLP) fit to the same data gives σ = 0.395, with parameters A=0.374, B=−0.472, C=−17.828, D=2.463.
- The outer turning-point radius for the Hulthen barrier is shortened by several percent relative to the pure Coulomb barrier; for the considered nuclei, the Coulomb turning-point radius ranges between 70 and 115 fm.
- Example half-life predictions: ¹⁰⁵Sb (l=2, Qp=0.4830 MeV): theoretical 59 s vs. experimental 122(11) s; ¹⁰⁹I (l=2, Qp=0.8195 MeV): theoretical 85.3 μs vs. experimental 92.8(8) μs; ¹⁴¹Ho* (l=0, Qp=1.2430 MeV): theoretical 2.2 μs vs. experimental 7.3(3) μs; ¹⁴⁵Tm (l=5, Qp=1.7360 MeV): theoretical 10.37 μs vs. experimental 3.17(20) μs.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical WKB approach to proton radioactivity, previously applied by Medeiros (2006), Qi (2012), Ni (2008), and Zdeb (2013), by introducing a Hulthen potential to screen the Coulomb barrier, a modification not present in those earlier treatments. The model's single-parameter fit is compared directly to the four-parameter universal decay law for proton emission (UDLP) of Qi (2012). The authors also propose a new empirical Brown-type correlation formula, log T₁/₂ = α Z₁^β / √Qp + γ, differentiated by orbital momentum, which can serve as a tool for assigning angular momentum to proton-emitting states. The results enable predictions for nuclei with uncertain decay information (e.g., ¹¹⁷La, ¹²¹Pr, ¹⁷²Au) and support the assignment of l=3 for the ground-state proton emission from ¹¹⁷La and ¹²¹Pr, consistent with the calculations of Delion (2013).
