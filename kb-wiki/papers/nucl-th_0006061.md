# The Neutron Halo in Heavy Nuclei Calculated with the Gogny Force

> The proton and neutron density distributions, one- and two-neutron separation energies and radii of nuclei for which neutron halos are experimentally observed, are calculated using the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the effective interaction of Gogny. Halo factors are evaluated assuming hydrogen-like antiproton wave functions. The factors agree well with experimental data. They are close to those obtained with Skyrme forces and with the relativistic mean field approach.

## Key claim

The Gogny force D1S, used in spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, reproduces the variation of experimental halo factors across nineteen halo nuclei, but systematically underestimates their magnitude compared to the Skyrme SLy4 interaction, likely because deformation and correlations beyond the mean field are neglected.

## Method

Self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in spherical symmetry were performed for nineteen halo nuclei using the Gogny effective interaction with the D1S parameter set. Harmonic oscillator bases were carefully optimized for each nucleus by minimizing the HFB binding energy with respect to oscillator frequency and increasing the number of shells until convergence was reached. Neutron and proton density distributions, one- and two-neutron separation energies, and halo factors (computed from hydrogen-like antiproton wave functions) were extracted and compared to experimental data and to Hartree-Fock results with the Skyrme SLy4 force.

## Key numbers

- One- and two-neutron separation energies from the Gogny force agree with experiment within ±1 MeV in most spherical nuclei studied.
- In deformed nuclei, the spherical assumption leads to overestimation of S_n and underestimation of S_{2n} by up to 3 MeV.
- For $^{58}$Ni, convergence of the HFB binding energy was obtained with N_MAX=16 harmonic oscillator shells.
- The neutron density $\rho_n$ of $^{58}$Ni is sensitive to the chosen $\hbar\omega$ only in the peripheral region $r > 8$ fm, where $\rho_n$ is smaller than $10^{-10}$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Pairing correlations contribute about 10 MeV to the binding energy but have negligible effect on halo factors.
- The Gogny force D1S gives smaller halo factors than the Skyrme SLy4 interaction, which agrees better with experimental data overall.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron halos using the asymptotic density model (Wycech, 1993), relativistic mean field theory (Bao, 1995), and Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme forces (Lubowich, 1998) by applying the Gogny effective interaction for the first time in a systematic study of nineteen halo nuclei. The results enable future tests of the influence of nuclear deformation and ground-state correlations beyond the mean field on halo structure, particularly for the five heavier deformed nuclei and the two Te isotopes where discrepancies remain.
