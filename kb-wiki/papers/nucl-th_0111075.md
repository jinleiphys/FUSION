# The Shears Mechanism in 142Gd in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Method with the Tilted-Axis Cranking

> We report on the first Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with the tilted-axis cranking in the context of magnetic rotation. The mean field symmetries, differences between phenomenological and self-consistent methods and the generation of shears-like structures in the mean field are discussed. Significant role of the time-odd spin-spin effective interaction is pointed out. We reproduce the shears mechanism, but quantitative agreement with experiment is rather poor. It may have to do with too large core polarization, lack of pairing correlations or properties of the Skyrme force.

## Key claim

The first Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with tilted-axis cranking reproduce the shears mechanism in 142Gd, but quantitative agreement with experiment is rather poor, possibly due to too large core polarization, lack of pairing correlations, or properties of the Skyrme force.

## Method

The authors used the code HFODD (v1.91) to solve the nuclear Hartree-Fock equations with the Skyrme effective interaction (SLy4 parametrization) for 142Gd, applying tilted-axis cranking with the cranking-frequency vector allowed to take any direction. The time-odd coupling constants corresponding to the spin-spin terms (s^2, s·Δs, and s·T) were set to zero to avoid divergences, while the terms j^2 and s·(∇×j) were kept at their original SLy4 values to conserve local gauge invariance. Pairing correlations were not included.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for all four bands in 142Gd: β ≈ 0.19 and γ = 40°, which do not change significantly with rotational frequency.
- At the bandhead of the πh11/2^2 νh11/2^-2 magnetic dipole band, the two proton and two neutron holes in h11/2 each carry spin of 10 units, oriented perpendicularly along the shortest and longest axes respectively.
- The magnetic dipole band is observed experimentally up to spin 22, which is 2 units more than the two blades of spin 10 can account for.
- At rotational frequency ω = 0.6 MeV/ħ, the calculated total spin I ≈ 26, exceeding the experimental maximum of spin 22.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological tilted-axis cranking calculations of magnetic rotation bands (previously applied to light lead isotopes and 197Pb by Cooper (2001)) to a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework, with the only prior self-consistent tilted-cranking calculation being a relativistic mean field study of 84Rb by Madokoro (2000). The results enable future investigations into the role of pairing correlations and the correct treatment of time-odd terms in Skyrme forces for describing shears bands, particularly in transitional nuclei like 142Gd where the interplay between the shears mechanism and standard collective rotation can be studied.
