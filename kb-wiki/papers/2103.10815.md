# Using local nuclear scaling of initial condition parameters to improve the system size dependence of transport model descriptions of nuclear collisions

> We extensively study the system size dependence of nuclear collisions with a multi-phase transport model. Previously certain key parameters for the initial condition needed significantly different values for $pp$ and central $AA$ collisions for the model to reasonably describe the yields and transverse momentum spectra of the bulk matter in those collision systems. Here we scale two key parameters, the Lund string fragmentation parameter $b_L$ and the minijet transverse momentum cutoff $p_0$, with local nuclear thickness functions from the two colliding nuclei. This allows the model to use the parameter values for $pp$ collisions with the local nuclear scaling to describe the system size and centrality dependences of nuclear collisions self consistently. In addition to providing good descriptions of $pp$ collisions from 23.6 GeV to 13 TeV and reasonable descriptions of the centrality dependence of charged particle yields for Au+Au collisions from $7.7A$ GeV to $200A$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, the improved model can now well describe the centrality dependence of the mean transverse momentum of charged particles below $p_{\rm T} \lesssim 2$ GeV. It works similarly well for smaller systems including $p$Pb, Cu+Cu and Xe+Xe collisions.

## Key claim

By scaling the Lund string fragmentation parameter \(b_L\) and the minijet transverse momentum cutoff \(p_0\) with local nuclear thickness functions rather than using constant values, the AMPT model can self-consistently describe the system size and centrality dependence of nuclear collisions from \(pp\) to central \(AA\) using the same \(pp\) parameter values, provided the exponent functions \(\beta(s)\) and \(\alpha(s)\) are fitted to data at each energy.

## Method

The authors modified the initial condition of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model by making the Lund string fragmentation parameter \(b_L\) and the minijet transverse momentum cutoff \(p_0\) local variables that depend on the nuclear thickness functions \(T_A(s_A)\) and \(T_B(s_B)\) of the two colliding nuclei at each transverse position. The local scaling forms are \(b_L(s_A,s_B,s) = b_L^{pp} / [\sqrt{T_A(s_A) T_B(s_B)}/T_p]^{\beta(s)}\) and \(p_0(s_A,s_B,s) = p_0^{pp}(s) \times [\sqrt{T_A(s_A) T_B(s_B)}/T_p]^{\alpha(s)}\), where the exponent functions \(\beta(s)\) and \(\alpha(s)\) were determined by fitting to charged particle mean transverse momentum in central Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions and charged particle yields in central Pb+Pb collisions, respectively. The model was then applied to \(pp\), \(p\)Pb, Cu+Cu, Xe+Xe, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions over a wide energy range.

## Key numbers

- \(b_L^{pp} = 0.7\) GeV\(^{-2}\) was chosen as the constant value for \(pp\) and \(p\bar{p}\) collisions at all energies, determined from fitting \(\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) data from 23.6 GeV to 13 TeV.
- The exponent \(\beta(s)\) is parametrized as \(\beta(s) = 0.620 + 0.112 \ln(\sqrt{s}/E_0) \Theta(\sqrt{s}-E_0)\) with \(E_0 = 200\) GeV, giving \(\beta \approx 0.62\) at RHIC energies and larger values at LHC energies.
- The exponent \(\alpha(s)\) is parametrized as \(\alpha(s) = 0.0918 \ln(\sqrt{s}/E_0) - 0.00602 \ln^2(\sqrt{s}/E_0) + 0.000134 \ln^3(\sqrt{s}/E_0)\) for \(\sqrt{s} \geq E_0\), with \(\alpha(s)=0\) for \(\sqrt{s} < E_0\).
- The \(p_0^{pp}(s)\) function for \(pp\) collisions is given by \(p_0^{pp}(s) = -1.92 + 1.77\ln(\sqrt{s}) - 0.274\ln^2(\sqrt{s}) + 0.0176\ln^3(\sqrt{s})\) with \(\sqrt{s}\) in GeV.
- The average \(b_L\) in central Pb+Pb collisions at \(5.02A\) TeV is lower than at RHIC energies due to the larger \(\beta(s)\); the average \(p_0\) at \(5.02A\) TeV is larger than at \(2.76A\) TeV due to the larger \(\alpha(s)\).
- The improved model describes the centrality dependence of \(\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) for Au+Au collisions from \(7.7A\) GeV to \(200A\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies within approximately 3% of data.
- The model describes \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\) for \(pp\) collisions from 23.6 GeV to 13 TeV and for Au+Au collisions from \(7.7A\) GeV to \(200A\) GeV, though it underestimates peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model (Lin 2004) by replacing the previous approach where key parameters like \(b_L\) and \(p_0\) required very different constant values for \(pp\) and central \(AA\) collisions (Zhang 2019, Zheng 2019). The local nuclear scaling method resolves the previously observed incorrect centrality dependence of charged particle mean transverse momentum in heavy ion collisions (Ma 2016). The improved model now enables self-consistent descriptions of system size dependence across \(pp\), \(p\)Pb, Cu+Cu, Xe+Xe, Au+Au, and Pb+Pb collisions, and can be used to study the onset of collectivity and the applicability of hydrodynamics in small systems.
