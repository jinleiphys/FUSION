# Q^2 evolution studies of nuclear structure function F_2 at HERA

> Q**2 dependence of nuclear structure functions is important for understanding perturbative QCD in nuclear environment. However, current experimental data are not sufficient for studying nuclear dependence in the Q**2 evolution because the data are limited in the small Q**2 region at small x. The future HERA program can study the evolution in the large Q**2 region, and it provides us crucial information on recombination effects and on higher-order alpha_s effects in the nuclear structure functions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that future HERA nuclear data in the large \(Q^2\) region at small \(x\) could test parton recombination (PR) effects and higher-order \(\alpha_s\) contributions to the \(Q^2\) evolution of nuclear structure functions, but current NMC data are insufficient to distinguish between DGLAP and PR evolution because the PR results depend on an unknown higher-twist parameter \(K_{HT}\).

## Method

The authors employ the DGLAP evolution equations at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), and also a version extended with parton recombination (PR) terms proportional to \(\alpha_s A^{1/3}/Q^2\) as formulated by Mueller and Qiu. They use a hybrid parton model with \(Q^2\) rescaling and recombination to generate nuclear input parton distributions at \(Q_0^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\), tuned to reproduce NMC ratios \(F_2^{Ca}/F_2^D\) at small \(x\). They then evolve these distributions and compute the ratio \(F_2^{Ca}/F_2^D\) as a function of \(Q^2\) at \(x=0.0085\), and the derivative \(\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2]\) at \(Q^2=5\) GeV\(^2\), comparing with NMC data.

## Key numbers

- The initial distributions are set at \(Q_0^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\).
- The ratio \(F_2^{Ca}/F_2^D\) is evaluated at \(x = 0.0085\).
- The derivative \(\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2]\) is computed at \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The QCD scale \(\Lambda = 0.2\) GeV and number of flavors \(N_f = 3\) are used in the evolution.
- The recombination terms are proportional to \(\alpha_s A^{1/3} / Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the EMC effect (discovered in 1983) by focusing on the \(Q^2\) dependence rather than the usual \(x\) dependence of nuclear structure function ratios. It directly addresses the NMC finding (NMC, 1995) that \(\partial [F_2^{Sn}/F_2^C] / \partial [\ln Q^2] \ne 0\), which was the first indication of nuclear effects on \(Q^2\) evolution. The paper uses the parton recombination formalism of Mueller and Qiu and the hybrid model of Kumano, Miyama, and collaborators (Kumano, 1995) to show that while ordinary DGLAP evolution can roughly explain the NMC data, the HERA program could test whether large higher-twist recombination effects exist at smaller \(x\) values around \(10^{-4}\).
