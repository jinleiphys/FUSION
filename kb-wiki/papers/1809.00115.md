# Theoretical perspective for the future experiments on parton densities

> I explain the current status of parton-distribution-function (PDF) studies and future experimental prospects on their determinations. First, unpolarized PDFs of the nucleon are introduced as a field of precision QCD physics including higher-order $α_s$ corrections. Second, nuclear PDFs and polarized nucleonic PDFs are discussed. Third, the determination of fragmentation functions is explained. Forth, the three-dimensional (3D) structure functions are discussed in connection with the origin of the nucleon spin and gravitational form factors of hadrons. By the 3D structure functions, gravitational sources and the origin of nucleon mass could be clarified in the microscopic level of quarks and gluons. Furthermore, 3D structure studies of hadrons could be used for clarifying internal structure of exotic hadron candidates, nuclear composition of ultra-high-energy cosmic rays, and color-entanglement phenomena. The PDF field will be developed further along with progress in other fields of science.

## Key claim

The paper argues that future experimental facilities (EIC, LHeC, JLab 12 GeV, and others) will significantly improve the precision of parton distribution functions (PDFs) and three-dimensional structure functions, but notes that current determinations still have large uncertainties in several key areas, particularly for gluon polarization, nuclear PDFs at small x, and tensor-polarized PDFs of the deuteron.

## Method

The author reviews the current status of unpolarized nucleonic PDFs, nuclear PDFs, polarized PDFs, fragmentation functions, and three-dimensional structure functions (GPDs, TMDs, GDAs) by summarizing results from global chi-squared analyses of high-energy hadron reaction data. The paper discusses how these quantities are determined through DGLAP evolution equations with higher-order alpha_s corrections, and examines future experimental prospects at facilities including EIC, LHeC, JLab, COMPASS, RHIC, and J-PARC.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear corrections to PDFs are of order 10-30% for medium and large nuclei
- The strange-quark distribution is roughly 40% of the light antiquark distributions: s ~ 0.4 (u_bar + d_bar)/2 at small Q^2
- The q\bar{q} propagation length lambda = 0.2 fm/x becomes larger than 2 fm at x < 0.1, enabling multiple scattering in nuclei
- Nuclear modifications in F2^C/F2^D show 5-10% shadowing at x < 0.05 for carbon
- The first IceCube neutrino cross sections were reported up to the 10^15 eV range
- The Belle and BaBar collaborations published hadron production data at center-of-mass energy of about 10 GeV
- The average transverse-momentum squared <k_perp^2> varies with x, being small at large x where valence quarks dominate and larger at small x in the sea-quark region

## Context

This review extends the line of work on parton distribution functions from the unpolarized nucleonic PDFs established by groups such as NNPDF, CTEQ, and MSTW, and discusses nuclear PDFs following the EMC discovery of nuclear modifications. The paper connects to ongoing efforts by the nCTEQ collaboration on nuclear PDFs, the JAM collaboration on polarized PDFs and fragmentation functions, and the HERMES measurements of tensor-polarized structure functions. It enables future experimental programs at EIC, LHeC, JLab 12 GeV, and J-PARC to determine three-dimensional structure functions and clarify the origins of nucleon spin and mass through GPD and GDA measurements.
