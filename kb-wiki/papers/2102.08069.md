# On the QED corrections to elastic electron scattering at high momentum transfer

> Estimates of QED and dispersion effects on the cross section for elastic electron scattering from a 12C nucleus are provided for collision energies in the range of 120-450 MeV. While in general such corrections are smoothly varying with energy or scattering angle, they show structures in the vicinity of diffraction minima which are very sensitive to details of the theoretical models. This casts doubt on the assertion that the discrepancy between QED background-corrected experimental data and theory in these minima originates solely from dispersion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that QED corrections to elastic electron scattering from ¹²C show strong, model-dependent structures near diffraction minima, casting doubt on the claim that the discrepancy between QED-background-corrected experimental data and theory in these minima originates solely from dispersion effects.

## Method

The authors calculate QED corrections (vacuum polarization, vertex and self-energy correction, and soft bremsstrahlung) and dispersion corrections for elastic electron scattering from ¹²C, using both the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA) and a phase-shift analysis that accounts for Coulomb distortion. The dispersion correction is evaluated using the second-order Born theory with a closure approximation, setting the mean excitation energy to 15 MeV, and the correlation function is based on the harmonic oscillator model of Friar and Rosen (1974). The leading-order cross section is replaced by the exact phase-shift result, and Coulomb distortion is also incorporated into the next-to-leading-order QED terms by replacing the Born amplitude with the phase-shift amplitude throughout.

## Key numbers

- The vsb (vertex, self-energy, and soft bremsstrahlung) correction may amount up to 20% in first order for collision energies around 100 MeV.
- Dispersion corrections are of the order of 5–10% at collision energies between 300–500 MeV, increasing with energy.
- At 431.4 MeV, the deviation of the dispersion-corrected theory from experimental data at the diffraction minimum (49.86°) is 5.6%.
- At 300.5 MeV, the deviation between dispersion models and QED-corrected data exceeds 10% in the maximum.
- The root-mean-square charge radius of ¹²C is 2.47 fm.
- The effective momentum transfer formula uses the factor 3Z/(2c) × (ħc)/√(5/3 ⟨r²⟩) × 1/E_{i,kin}, with ħc = 197.4 MeV·fm.
- The experimental accuracy of the data points is about 2%.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of QED corrections beyond the standard PWBA-based approach used in nuclear charge distribution studies (e.g., Reuter et al., 1982; Offermann et al., 1991) by incorporating Coulomb distortion into the QED correction terms, following a suggestion by Maximon (1969). It contradicts the assumption that higher-order QED effects can be disregarded in diffraction minima because the first-order Born amplitude vanishes there. The paper enables improved data reduction for future experiments, including parity violation studies at Jefferson Lab (Jef20), and highlights the need for a full account of the electron-target field in QED corrections, which has not yet been accomplished.
