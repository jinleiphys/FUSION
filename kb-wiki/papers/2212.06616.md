# Quark-antiquark states of the lightest scalar mesons within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with flavor-dependent coupling constants

> The quark antiquark components of the U(3) lightest scalar meson nonet are investigated by considering the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with flavor-dependent coupling constants that were derived by considering (non-perturbative) gluon exchange.The strange quark effective mass ($M_s^*$) is varied such that the strangeness content of these scalar mesons can be analyzed further. The neutral states $S_0$, $S_8$ and $S_3$, are adopted as the most relevant quark-antiquark components respectively of the $σ(500)$, $f_0(980)$ and $A_0^0(980)$ mesons. As a result, the mass hierarchy between states $S_0$ and $S_8$, and $K^*_0(700) - A_0(980)$ mesons, can be respectively inverted and corrected for quite low values of the strange quark constituent mass. Besides that, for some particular values of $M_s^*$, the masses of $σ(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ can also be obtained by considering the mixing coupling constant $G_{08}$. However, the masses of all the nine mesons are not described simultaneously in a self-consistent procedure and this goes along with the need of non-quark-antiquark states to completely describe their masses. A neutral-meson mixing matrix is defined and the leading mixing angle is found by fitting a correct value of the $σ(500)$-$f_0(980)$ mass difference. Two different estimates for the strengths of the mixings $A_0^0 \to f_0$ and $f_0 \to A_0^0$ are proposed, leading to somewhat different behaviors. Firstly, by assuming leading transitions to intermediary flavor eigenstates and secondly by considering a dynamical evolution. Results may be in good agreement with experimental values from BESS-III depending on the value of the strange quark effective mass.

## Key claim

The quark-antiquark components of the lightest scalar mesons within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with flavor-dependent coupling constants can reproduce the mass hierarchy inversion between the sigma(500) and f0(980) states and correct the K*0(700)-A0(980) mass ordering for sufficiently low values of the strange quark effective mass, but the masses of all nine mesons cannot be described simultaneously in a self-consistent procedure, indicating the need for non-quark-antiquark states.

## Method

The authors employed the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with flavor-dependent coupling constants derived from non-perturbative gluon exchange, using the auxiliary field method to describe scalar quark-antiquark states S_i (i=0,1...8). They solved the gap equations for quark effective masses and Bethe-Salpeter equations at the Born level for the scalar meson bound states, varying the strange quark effective mass M_s* both self-consistently and freely while keeping up and down masses fixed. A neutral-meson mixing matrix was defined for the S_0, S_3, and S_8 states, with the leading mixing angle found by fitting the sigma(500)-f0(980) mass difference, and two estimates for the A0^0-to-f0 and f0-to-A0^0 mixing strengths were proposed.

## Key numbers

- The reference NJL coupling constant used was G_0 = 10 GeV^{-2}
- An infrared cutoff of Lambda_IR ~ 120 MeV was employed to avoid unphysical decay of meson bound states
- The mixing angle between flavor eigenstates from prior work (Klempt) was theta_s = 14 +/- 4 degrees, cited for comparison
- The sigma(500) decay width is approximately Gamma_sigma ~ 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the flavor-dependent NJL model developed in Braghin (PRD-2021, JPG-2022) to investigate scalar mesons, where previously only pseudoscalar mesons were studied. It addresses the long-standing problem that the standard NJL model and constituent quark models fail to reproduce the correct mass hierarchy of the lightest scalar mesons, particularly the inversion between sigma(500) and f0(980) and the ordering of K*0(700) and A0(980). The results support the conclusion from Pelaez (review) and others that non-quark-antiquark components (tetraquarks, meson molecules, or glueballs) are necessary for a complete description of these mesons, and the mixing analysis provides a framework for interpreting BES-III experimental data on A0^0-f0 mixing.
