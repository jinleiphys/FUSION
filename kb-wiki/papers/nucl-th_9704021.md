# State-by-state calculations for all channels of the exotic $(μ^-,e^-)$ conversion process

> The coherent and incoherent channels of the neutrinoless muon to electron conversion in nuclei, $μ^- (A,Z) \to e^- (A,Z)^*$, are studied throughout the periodic table. The relevant nuclear matrix elements are computed by explicitly constructing all possible final nuclear states in the context of the quasi-particle RPA. The obtained results are discussed in view of the existing at PSI and TRIUMF experimental data for $^{48}Ti$ and $^{208}Pb$ and compared with results obtained by: (i) shell model sum-rule techniques (ii) nuclear matter mapped into nuclei via a local density approximation and (iii) earlier similar calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the coherent (ground-state-to-ground-state) channel dominates the neutrinoless muon-to-electron conversion process throughout the periodic table, but this dominance is conditional on properly including the muon binding energy in the momentum transfer calculation; when this is done, the coherent fraction becomes larger than previously estimated, especially for heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) to construct all possible final nuclear states for six isotopes (48Ti, 60Ni, 72Ge, 112Cd, 162Yb, 208Pb), computing the nuclear matrix elements for both the vector and axial-vector components of the effective Hamiltonian. They calculated the coherent rate from ground-state-to-ground-state transitions using proton and neutron form factors, and the incoherent rate by summing state-by-state over all excited final states up to 6- and 6+ multipoles, removing spurious center-of-mass contributions from the 1- states. The G-matrix elements of the Bonn one-boson exchange potential were used, with pairing parameters determined from experimental pairing gaps.

## Key numbers

- The best experimental upper limit on the branching ratio for 48Ti is R_Ti_mue < 4.3 x 10^-12 (SINDRUM II at PSI), with a previous limit of R_Ti_mue < 4.6 x 10^-12 (TRIUMF)
- For 208Pb, the experimental upper limit is R_Pb_mue < 4.6 x 10^-11 (PSI), improved from the previous limit R_Pb_mue < 4.9 x 10^-10 (TRIUMF)
- The muon binding energy varies from about 1.3 MeV for 48Ti to about 10.5 MeV for 208Pb, causing the elastic momentum transfer to range from about 0.529 fm^-1 to about 0.482 fm^-1 respectively (compared to the bare muon mass value of about 0.535 fm^-1)
- For 208Pb, the QRPA form factors including binding energy are about 30% larger than those neglecting it
- The deviation between calculated and experimental form factors is less than 5% for most nuclei, except for 112Cd and 208Pb where it is about 10%
- The spurious center-of-mass contribution is less than 30% of the incoherent matrix elements, corresponding to 1.0-1.5% of the total conversion rate
- For 48Ti, ground state correlations reduce the matrix elements by about 30% but do not affect the coherent fraction eta
- The muon conversion electron energy for lead-region nuclei is about 95 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier QRPA calculations for 48Ti (Kosmas et al., 1994) to a full set of six nuclei across the periodic table, and directly compares with shell-model sum-rule calculations (Kosmas and Vergados, 1990) that used a mean excitation energy of about 20 MeV, which the authors find is too large. The results also contrast with local density approximation calculations (Chiang et al., 1993) that found the coherent contribution dominant for all nuclei but with a maximum in the lead region. The paper enables more reliable predictions for ongoing experiments at PSI (SINDRUM II) and TRIUMF that aim to improve sensitivity to branching ratios of 10^-14 or better, and supports the use of heavy nuclear targets like 208Pb for lepton flavor violation searches.
