# Masses and semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons in a nonrelativistic quark model

> We evaluate masses and semileptonic decay widths for the ground state of doubly heavy $Ξ$ and $Ω$ baryons in the framework of a nonrelativistic quark model. We solve the three-body problem by means of a variational ansatz made possible by heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints. Our masses are comparable to the ones obtained in relativistic calculations and we get one of the best agreements with lattice data. Our simple wave functions are used to evaluate semileptonic decays of doubly heavy $Ξ, Ξ'(J=1/2)$ and $Ω, Ω'(J=1/2)$ baryons. Our results for the decay widths are in reasonable agreement with calculations done in a relativistic calculation in the quark-diquark approximation. We also check that our wave functions comply with what it is expected in the infinite heavy quark mass limit.

# Masses and semileptonic decays of doubly heavy baryons in a nonrelativistic quark model

## Key claim
The authors show that a nonrelativistic quark model with a variational ansatz constrained by heavy-quark spin symmetry can reproduce lattice QCD masses for doubly heavy baryons to within about 100 MeV, and that the resulting simple wave functions yield semileptonic decay widths in reasonable agreement with relativistic quark-diquark calculations, though the results depend on which phenomenological quark-quark interaction is chosen.

## Method
The three-body problem for doubly heavy Ξ and Ω baryons (containing two heavy quarks and one light quark) was solved using a variational ansatz that assumes the total spin of the two-heavy-quark subsystem is well defined, as justified by heavy-quark spin symmetry in the infinite-mass limit. Several phenomenological quark-quark interactions (AL1 potential from Silvestre-Brac and Semay, and the potential from Bhaduri, Cohler, and Nogami) were employed, with parameters fixed from meson sector fits to avoid three-body ambiguities. The resulting wave functions were then used to evaluate form factors and semileptonic decay widths for b→c transitions between doubly heavy baryons with total spin J=1/2.

## Key numbers
- Mass of Ξ_cc: 3612^{+17} MeV (this work), compared to lattice values 3605±23 MeV (Khan 2000) and 3549±95 MeV (Flynn 2003), and the SELEX experimental value 3519±1 MeV (Mattson 2002)
- Mass of Ξ_bb: 10197^{+10}_{-17} MeV (this work), compared to lattice value 10314±47 MeV (Khan 2000)
- Mass splitting Ξ*_cc − Ξ_cc: 94^{+5}_{-11} MeV (this work), compared to lattice values 80±11 MeV (Lewis 2001) and 87±19 MeV (Flynn 2003)
- Mass splitting Ξ*_bb − Ξ_bb: 39^{+1}_{-6} MeV (this work), compared to lattice value 20±6 MeV (Khan 2000)
- Semileptonic decay width Γ(Ξ_bb → Ξ_bc l ν̅_l): 3.84^{+0.49}_{-0.10} × 10^{-14} GeV (this work), compared to 3.26 × 10^{-14} GeV from Ebert et al. (2004) and 28.5 × 10^{-14} GeV from Guo et al. (1998)
- Semileptonic decay width Γ(Ξ_bc → Ξ_cc l ν̅_l): 5.13^{+0.51}_{-0.05} × 10^{-14} GeV (this work), compared to 4.59 × 10^{-14} GeV from Ebert et al. (2004) and 8.93 × 10^{-14} GeV from Guo et al. (1998)
- Semileptonic decay width Γ(Ω_bb → Ω_bc l ν̅_l): 4.28^{+0.39}_{-0.03} × 10^{-14} GeV (this work), compared to 3.40 × 10^{-14} GeV from Ebert et al. (2004) and 28.8 × 10^{-14} GeV from Guo et al. (1998)
- All semileptonic widths computed using |V_cb| = 0.0413

## Context
This work extends the nonrelativistic quark model approach previously applied to singly heavy baryons by Albertus et al. (2004) to the doubly heavy sector, using heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints from Jenkins (1993) to simplify the three-body problem. The masses are compared to lattice QCD results from Khan (2000), Lewis (2001), and Flynn (2003), and the decay widths are compared to the relativistic quark-diquark model of Ebert et al. (2004) and the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Guo et al. (1998). The results enable further studies of doubly heavy baryon properties using simple variational wave functions that are shown to reproduce the correct infinite heavy quark mass limit, where the system reduces to a light quark bound to a pointlike heavy diquark.
