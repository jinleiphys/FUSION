# Particle Production in pA Collisions and QCD Saturation

> The forthcoming LHC measurements in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions at forward rapidities can discriminate between the color glass condensate (CGC) and alternative approaches including standard collinear factorization one. We report some of our recent predictions based on gluon saturation/CGC formalism for pA collisions at the LHC including the charged hadron multiplicity distribution, the nuclear modification factor for single inclusive hadron and prompt photon production, and the azimuthal angle correlation of the semi-inclusive hadron-photon production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that LHC measurements in proton-nucleus collisions at forward rapidities can discriminate between the color glass condensate formalism and alternative approaches including standard collinear factorization, but notes that current CGC predictions for the nuclear modification factor at midrapidity may be less reliable than results for very forward rapidity collisions due to large sensitivity to higher-order corrections.

## Method

The author employs the leading-log kt-factorization formalism within the color glass condensate framework, using several saturation models (MCrcBK, b-CGC, IP-Sat, and KLN) to compute particle production observables. The running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation is applied with initial saturation scales extracted from fits to small-x experimental data from HERA, RHIC, and the LHC. Predictions are compared with collinear factorization results from the EPS09 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity scaled with number of participants: minimum bias pA collisions at sqrt(s)=4.4 TeV use N_par=7.6
- Nuclear modification factor R^h_pA calculations use N_coll=3.6 at sqrt(s)=0.2 TeV, N_coll=6.5 at sqrt(s)=4.4 TeV, and N_coll=7.4 at sqrt(s)=8.8 TeV
- Initial nuclear saturation scales assumed: Q_0A^2 = 0.5 and 0.67 GeV^2, with fixed proton saturation scale Q_0p^2 = 0.168 GeV^2
- Theoretical uncertainties of about 10-20% are implicit in the pseudo-rapidity distribution curves

## Context

This work extends the CGC formalism previously applied successfully to pp and AA collisions at RHIC and the LHC (Rezaeian 2012, Jalilian-Marian 2012) to make quantitative predictions for pA collisions at the LHC. The results can discriminate between CGC and alternative approaches that combine nuclear shadowing, transverse momentum broadening, and cold matter energy loss. The paper enables experimental tests of gluon saturation through measurements of single inclusive hadron and prompt photon production at forward rapidities, as well as azimuthal angle correlations of semi-inclusive hadron-photon production.
