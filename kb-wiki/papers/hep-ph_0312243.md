# Eikonal representation in the momentum-transfer space

> By means of empirical fits to the differential cross section data on pp and p(bar)p elastic scattering, above 10 GeV (center-of-mass energy), we determine the eikonal in the momentum - transfer space (q^2- space). We make use of a numerical method and a novel semi-analytical method, through which the uncertainties from the fit parameters can be propagated up to the eikonal in the $q^2$- space. A systematic study of the effect of the experimental information at large values of the momentum transfer is developed and discussed in detail. We present statistical evidence that the imaginary part of the eikonal changes sign in the q^2- space and that the position of the zero decreases as the energy increases; after the position of the zero, the eikonal presents a minimum and then goes to zero through negative values. We discuss the applicability of our results in the phenomenological context, outlining some connections with nonperturbative QCD. A short review and a critical discussion on the main results concerning "model-independent" analyses are also presented.

## Key claim

The authors present statistical evidence that the imaginary part of the eikonal in the momentum-transfer space changes sign (has a zero) for both pp and pbarp elastic scattering above 10 GeV center-of-mass energy, and that the position of this zero decreases as energy increases; after the zero, the eikonal exhibits a minimum and then approaches zero through negative values.

## Method

The authors performed empirical fits to differential cross section data for pp and pbarp elastic scattering using a sum-of-exponentials parametrization of the scattering amplitude in q²-space, with free parameters determined via the CERN-MINUIT routine. They developed both a numerical method and a novel semi-analytical method to propagate uncertainties from the fit parameters to the eikonal in q²-space, and systematically investigated the effect of including experimental data at large momentum transfer (up to q² = 14.2 GeV²) by defining two ensembles of data.

## Key numbers

- For pp scattering at sqrt(s) = 52.8 GeV, the fit to ensemble A gave chi²/DOF = 323/196 = 1.65, with data covering 0.01 < q² ≤ 9.8 GeV²
- For pp scattering at sqrt(s) = 27.5 GeV, the data cover the region 5.5 ≤ q² ≤ 14.2 GeV²
- The pp data at sqrt(s) = 13.8 GeV were found incompatible with the data at 27.5 GeV (ensemble B)
- For pbarp scattering, none of the data sets were compatible with the pp data at 27.5 GeV
- The maximum momentum transfer for pbarp data ranges from q²_max = 0.626 GeV² (at sqrt(s) = 1800 GeV) to q²_max = 4.45 GeV² (at sqrt(s) = 19 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Carvalho and Menon, 1997) which found evidence for eikonal zeros in pp scattering at ISR energies (23.5-62.5 GeV) with an estimated zero position at q² = 7 ± 2 GeV², but could not infer energy dependence. The present analysis includes lower energy pp data (13.8, 19.5 GeV) and pbarp data from 14 GeV to 1.8 TeV, and addresses limitations of prior model-independent analyses by Amaldi and Schubert (which imposed geometrical scaling) and Sanielevici and Valin (which did not propagate uncertainties). The results enable improved input selection for eikonal models and suggest connections with nonperturbative QCD and the hadronic form factor of the proton.
