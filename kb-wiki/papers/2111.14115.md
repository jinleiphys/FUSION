# Systematic study of two-proton radioactivity half-lives based on a modified Gamow-like model

> In the present work, we systematically study the two-proton (2p) radioactivity half-lives of nuclei close to the proton drip line within a modified Gamow-like model. Using this model, the calculated 2p radioactivity half-lives can well reproduce the experimental data. Moreover, we use this model to predict the 2p radioactivity half-lives of 22 candidates whose 2p radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantied in evaluated nuclear properties table NUBASE2016. The predicted results are in good agreement with the ones obtained by using Gamow-like model, effective liquid drop model (ELDM), generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) as well as a four-parameter formula.

# Systematic study of two-proton radioactivity half-lives based on a modified Gamow-like model

## Key claim
The modified Gamow-like model, which replaces the pure Coulomb potential with an exponential Hulthén potential to account for electrostatic shielding effects, reproduces experimental two-proton radioactivity half-lives with a standard deviation of σ = 0.825, which is slightly better than the unmodified Gamow-like model (σ = 0.847) but worse than the effective liquid drop model (σ = 0.531). The model's predictive power is demonstrated for 22 candidates whose 2p radioactivity is energetically allowed or observed but not yet quantified in NUBASE2016.

## Method
The authors modify the Gamow-like model by replacing the pure Coulomb potential in the outer barrier region with the Hulthén potential V_H(r) = a Z_2p Z_d e^2 / (e^{ar} - 1), where a = 1.808 × 10^{-3} fm^{-1} is the screening parameter fitted to experimental data. The centrifugal potential V_l(r) = (l+1/2)^2 ħ^2 / (2μ r^2) is also included. The two-proton preformation probability uses a factor χ² = 0.0143 fitted to experimental data of ⁴⁵Fe, ¹⁹Mg, ⁴⁸Ni, and ⁵⁴Zn. The WKB approximation calculates the penetrability through the barrier, with the effective nuclear radius parameter r₀ = 1.14 fm taken from proton radioactivity studies.

## Key numbers
- Screening parameter a = 1.808 × 10^{-3} fm^{-1}, determined by fitting experimental data
- Effective nuclear radius parameter r₀ = 1.14 fm (unified with value for proton radioactivity)
- Two-proton preformation factor χ² = 0.0143, obtained by fitting experimental data of ⁴⁵Fe, ¹⁹Mg, ⁴⁸Ni, and ⁵⁴Zn
- Standard deviation σ = 0.825 for modified Gamow-like model, compared to σ = 0.847 for Gamow-like model, σ = 0.531 for ELDM, σ = 0.852 for GLDM, and σ = 1.222 for Sreeja formula
- For ¹⁹Mg (Q_2p = 0.750 MeV, l=0): calculated log₁₀ T_{1/2} = -11.39 s (experimental -11.40 s)
- For ⁴⁵Fe (Q_2p = 1.100 MeV, l=0): calculated log₁₀ T_{1/2} = -2.28 s (experimental -2.40 s)
- For ⁴⁸Ni (Q_2p = 1.290 MeV, l=0): calculated log₁₀ T_{1/2} = -2.69 s (experimental -2.52 s)
- For ⁵⁴Zn (Q_2p = 1.280 MeV, l=0): calculated log₁₀ T_{1/2} = -1.12 s (experimental -2.76 s)
- For ⁶⁷Kr (Q_2p = 1.690 MeV, l=0): calculated log₁₀ T_{1/2} = -0.84 s (experimental -1.70 s)
- Predicted half-lives for 22 candidates range from log₁₀ T_{1/2} = -17.16 s (¹⁰N, l=1) to +12.37 s (⁶²As, l=2)

## Context
This work extends the modified Gamow-like model previously applied to α decay and proton radioactivity by Cheng (2019) and Chen (2019) to the case of two-proton radioactivity. The electrostatic shielding effect, represented by the Hulthén potential, was previously introduced by Budaca (2017) for proton radioactivity and later extended to α decay and 2p radioactivity by Budaca (2020) and Zou (2021). The results are compared with the Gamow-like model by Liu (2021), the effective liquid drop model (ELDM) by Goncalves (2017), the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) by Cui (2020), and the Sreeja formula by Sreeja (2019). The predicted half-lives for 22 candidates from NUBASE2016 provide theoretical guidance for future experimental searches for new 2p radioactivity emitters.
