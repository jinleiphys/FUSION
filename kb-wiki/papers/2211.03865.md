# Gravitational chiral anomaly and anomalous transport for fields with spin 3/2

> In a fluid with vorticity and acceleration, an axial current arises in the third order of gradient expansion, called the kinematical vortical effect (KVE). While existing in the absence of gravitational fields, it is nevertheless associated with effects in curved space-time, namely with the gravitational chiral quantum anomaly. In this paper, the KVE transport coefficients were found using the Zubarev quantum-statistical density operator for the Rarita-Schwinger-Adler theory, which includes fields with spins 3/2 and 1/2. A prediction is made about the possible form of the transport coefficients for massless fields with arbitrary spin.

## Key claim

The kinematical vortical effect (KVE) transport coefficients for spin-3/2 fields in the Rarita-Schwinger-Adler theory satisfy the relation lambda_1 - lambda_2 = 32 N, where N is the gravitational chiral anomaly coefficient, confirming that the gravitational anomaly is encoded in third-order gradient transport even though the KVE exists in flat spacetime without gravitational fields.

## Method

The authors computed the KVE transport coefficients lambda_1 and lambda_2 for the Rarita-Schwinger-Adler theory using the Zubarev quantum-statistical density operator in the limit m to infinity. They evaluated four-point correlation functions of the stress-energy tensor and axial current operators at finite temperature using perturbation theory and finite-temperature field theory, summing over Matsubara frequencies and performing the necessary momentum integrals analytically with Wolfram Mathematica.

## Key numbers

- For the Rarita-Schwinger-Adler theory, lambda_1 = -53/(24 pi^2) and lambda_2 = -5/(8 pi^2), giving the KVE current j^mu_A,KVE = (-53/(24 pi^2) omega^2 - 5/(8 pi^2) a^2) omega^mu
- The difference lambda_1 - lambda_2 = -53/(24 pi^2) + 5/(8 pi^2) = -32 * 19/(384 pi^2), confirming the relation lambda_1 - lambda_2 = 32 N with N = -19/(384 pi^2) for the RSA theory
- For comparison, the Dirac field (spin 1/2) gives lambda_1 - lambda_2 = -1/(24 pi^2) + 1/(8 pi^2) = 32/(384 pi^2), consistent with N = 1/(384 pi^2)

## Context

This work extends the derivation of the KVE and its connection to the gravitational chiral anomaly, previously established for spin-1/2 Dirac fields by Prokhorov, Teryaev, and Zakharov (2022), to the more complex case of spin-3/2 fields described by the Rarita-Schwinger-Adler theory developed by Adler (2017). The result confirms the general spin-independent relation lambda_1 - lambda_2 = 32 N derived in that earlier work, and demonstrates that the gravitational anomaly coefficient -19 from the RSA theory appears in hydrodynamic transport, which could serve as a clear marker of gravitational anomaly effects in heavy ion collision experiments where hadrons with spin 3/2 are now being measured by the STAR collaboration (2020).
