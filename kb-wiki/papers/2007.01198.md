# Peaks within peaks and the possible two-peak structure of the Pc(4457): the effective field theory perspective

> The LHCb pentaquarks -- the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ -- have been theorized to be $Σ_c \bar{D}$ and $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ S-wave molecules. Here we explore the possibility that two of these pentaquarks -- the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ -- contain in addition a $Λ_c(2595) \bar{D}$ component in P-wave. We will analyze the effects of this extra channel within two effective field theories: the first one will be a standard contact-range effective field theory and the second one will include the non-diagonal pion dynamics connecting the $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ and $Λ_c(2595) \bar{D}$ channels, which happens to be unusually long-ranged. The impact of the coupled-channel dynamics between the $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ and $Λ_c(2595) \bar{D}$ components is modest at best for the $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$, which will remain to be predominantly $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ molecules. However, if the quantum numbers of the $P_c(4457)$ are $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-$, the coupled-channel dynamics is likely to induce the binding of a $Λ_c(2595) \bar{D}$ S-wave molecule (coupled to $Σ_c \bar{D}^*$ in P-wave) with $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$ and a mass similar to the $P_c(4457)$. If this is the case, the $P_c(4457)$ could actually be a double peak containing two different pentaquark states.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether the LHCb pentaquarks Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) contain a P-wave Lambda_c(2595) anti-D component in addition to their dominant Sigma_c anti-D* S-wave molecular structure, finding that if the Pc(4457) has quantum numbers J^P = 1/2^- then a partner state with J^P = 1/2^+ and similar mass is likely, potentially making the Pc(4457) a double peak containing two distinct pentaquark states.

## Method

The authors formulate two effective field theories for the coupled-channel dynamics between the Sigma_c anti-D* and Lambda_c(2595) anti-D systems: a pionless contact-range EFT and a "half-pionful" EFT that includes the unusually long-ranged pion-exchange transition potential (which behaves as a 1/r^2 potential) but not the shorter-range pion dynamics within the Sigma_c anti-D* system alone. They solve the reduced Schrodinger equation in coordinate space and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space, using Gaussian and delta-shell regulators, to determine bound-state masses for the Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) with different quantum number assignments.

## Key numbers

- The Sigma_c anti-D* and Lambda_c(2595) anti-D thresholds are located at 4462.2 MeV and 4459.5 MeV respectively in the isospin-symmetric limit
- The characteristic length scale of the long-ranged pion dynamics in the anti-D* Sigma_c to anti-D Lambda_c(2595) transition is between 10 and 20 fm
- The pion couplings used are g1 = 0.59 (from D* to D pi and D* to D gamma decays) and h2 = 0.63 (from Lambda_c(2595) to Sigma_c pi decay)
- The infrared cutoff for the half-pionful theory is chosen within the window Lambda_IR = 10-20 MeV, corresponding to an infrared range R_IR = 10-20 fm

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the LHCb pentaquarks (Aaij 2019) as Sigma_c anti-D and Sigma_c anti-D* bound states, following earlier theoretical predictions (Wu 2010, Karliner 2015). It builds on the proposal by Burns (2019) that the Lambda_c(2595) anti-D component might be important for binding molecular pentaquarks, and on the realization by Geng (2017) that the pion-exchange dynamics mediating the anti-D* Sigma_c to anti-D Lambda_c(2595) transition produces an unusually long-ranged 1/r^2 potential. The results enable future experimental searches for a possible double-peak structure in the Pc(4457) region, analogous to the original Pc(4450) pentaquark discovered in 2015 (Aaij 2015).
