# Nuclear multipole responses from chiral effective field theory interaction

> We probe nuclear multipole resonances in the framework of the random-phase approximation by using the interaction obtained from the chiral effective field theory. The three-nucleon force is included in a form of the in-medium two-nucleon interaction which was derived from the chiral three-nucleon force. The isoscalar monopole, isoscalar dipole, isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole resonances of the closed-shell $^{56,68,78}$Ni have been investigated. The calculations reasonably reproduce the experimental multipole resonances of $^{56,68}$Ni, and well describe the pygmy dipole resonance and dipole polarizability measured in $^{68}$Ni. The multipole resonances of $^{78}$Ni, including pygmy dipole resonance and dipole polarizability, are predicted. The detailed effects of the tensor force and three-body force are analyzed by dissecting the chiral interaction. We find that in general the tensor force effect on electric giant resonances is not as significant as the effect from the three-body force, although the tensor force provides more than half of the binding energy. The effect from three-body force is strong in light nuclei. Particularly, three-body force is crucial for the formation of the pygmy resonance in calculations.

## Key claim

The chiral effective field theory interaction N³LO(NN)+N²LO(3NF_eff), used within the Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation (HF-RPA), reasonably reproduces experimental multipole resonances in ⁵⁶,⁶⁸Ni and predicts those in ⁷⁸Ni, but the three-body force is crucial for the formation of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in calculations, while the tensor force effect on electric giant resonances is not as significant as the three-body force effect, even though the tensor force provides more than half of the binding energy.

## Method

The authors performed HF-RPA calculations for the isoscalar monopole, isoscalar dipole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole resonances of the closed-shell nuclei ⁵⁶,⁶⁸,⁷⁸Ni. The chiral EFT two-body interaction N³LO (Entem and Machleidt, 2003) was used, and the three-nucleon force was included via an in-medium effective two-nucleon interaction (3NF_eff) derived from the chiral N²LO 3NF by integrating one nucleon over the Fermi sea. The tensor force and three-body force effects were dissected by using a spin-tensor decomposition method to remove tensor terms or exclude the 3NF.

## Key numbers

- In ⁵⁶Ni, the experimental centroid energies of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) are 19.3±0.5 MeV and 16.2±0.5 MeV, respectively, measured via the ⁵⁶Ni(d,d′) reaction.
- In ⁶⁸Ni, the experimental isovector PDR and isovector GDR peaks are located at 9.55(17) MeV and 17.1(2) MeV, respectively (Rossi et al., 2013).
- The calculated dipole polarizability α_D for ⁶⁸Ni is 3.40 fm³, matching the experimental value of 3.40(23) fm³.
- In ⁶⁸Ni, the calculated strength below 12 MeV exhausts 5.2% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) energy-weighted sum rule, compared with experimentally extracted values of about 5% or 2.8(5)%.
- For ⁷⁸Ni, the predicted isovector PDR is peaked at 11.03 MeV, the isovector GDR energy is 17.17 MeV, and the dipole polarizability α_D is 3.76 fm³.
- The present HF-RPA calculation with 3NF gives 2.90 MeV for the 2₁⁺ excited state in ⁷⁸Ni, consistent with ab-initio coupled cluster and in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous HF-RPA calculations using the chiral NNLO_sat interaction for lighter closed-shell nuclei (⁴He, ¹⁶,²²,²⁴O, ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca) to the heavier nickel isotopes ⁵⁶,⁶⁸,⁷⁸Ni, with particular focus on the roles of the tensor force and three-body force. The results are compared with experimental data from Monrozeau et al. (2008) for ⁵⁶Ni and Rossi et al. (2013) for ⁶⁸Ni, as well as with coupled-cluster calculations using NNLO_sat (PhysRevC.94.034317) and density functional theory calculations with various Skyrme forces. The predictions for ⁷⁸Ni, including the PDR and dipole polarizability, provide testable quantities for future experiments and constrain the equation of state of neutron-rich matter.
