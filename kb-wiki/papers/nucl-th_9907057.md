# Coherent pion electroproduction on the deuteron in the $Δ(1232)$ resonance region

> Coherent pion electroproduction on the deuteron is studied in the $Δ(1232)$ resonance region in the impulse approximation, i.e., neglecting pion rescattering and two-body effects. The elementary reaction on the nucleon is described in the framework of an effective Lagrangian approach including the dominant $P_{33}(1232)$ resonance and the usual background terms of the Born contributions for $π^0$ production. We have studied the influence of these different contributions on the various structure functions which determine the unpolarized exclusive differential cross section in a variety of kinematic regions.

## Key claim

Coherent pion electroproduction on the deuteron in the Delta(1232) resonance region is studied in the impulse approximation, meaning that pion rescattering and two-body effects are neglected; the authors state that this is a first step and that a more refined treatment including those effects is deferred to later work.

## Method

The elementary reaction on the nucleon was described using an effective Lagrangian approach including the dominant P33(1232) resonance (with only the M1 transition, neglecting C2 and E2 parts) and Born terms from direct and crossed nucleon pole diagrams for neutral pion production. The production operator was implemented in the impulse approximation on the deuteron using the Bonn r-space potential deuteron wave function, with the spectator-on-shell prescription for assigning the invariant mass of the photon-nucleon subsystem. The four structure functions (longitudinal f_L, transverse f_T, longitudinal-transverse interference f_LT, and transverse-transverse interference f_TT) were evaluated numerically along various cuts in the energy-momentum transfer plane.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant was chosen as f_pi^2/(4 pi) = 0.08.
- The Delta mass was taken as m_Delta = 1232 MeV, with a bare mass m_Delta^0 = 1315 MeV.
- The hadronic form factor cutoff parameter was Lambda_Delta = 287.9 MeV.
- The NDelta coupling constant was f_Delta^2/(4 pi) = 1.393.
- The electromagnetic form factor used a dipole parametrization with scale 0.71 (GeV)^2.
- The gamma NDelta M1 coupling parameters from fitting the M_{1+}^{3/2} multipole were mu_0 = 4.16, mu_2 = 0.542, mu_4 = -0.0757, a_1 = 0.185 fm^{-3}, a_2 = 4.94 fm^{-1}.
- Structure functions were computed at fixed three-momentum transfers k^2 = 2, 5, and 10 fm^{-2} and at fixed energy transfer omega = 300 MeV.
- For sets B and C (k^2 = 5 and 10 fm^{-2}), the invariant masses W_{gamma*d} were 2057, 2137, 2217, and 2297 MeV.
- The transverse form factor F_T was found to be about one to two orders of magnitude larger than the longitudinal form factor F_L.
- Comparing sets B and C, the form factors decreased by a factor of about 5 due to higher four-momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of coherent photoproduction on the deuteron (Wilhelm and Arenhoevel, 1995, 1996) to the electroproduction case, adding charge and longitudinal contributions and electromagnetic form factors at the photon vertices. It provides a systematic study of the d(e,e' pi^0)d reaction from threshold through the Delta(1232) resonance, which had been missing in the literature; earlier work by Blaazer et al. (1993) considered only target asymmetries for two kinematic settings, and Jeschonnek and Schoch (1989) investigated exchange contributions near threshold. The results enable future inclusion of pion rescattering and two-body effects, as well as use of a more refined elementary production operator for the threshold region.
