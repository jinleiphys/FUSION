# Isospin and density dependences of nuclear matter symmetry energy coefficients II

> Symmetry energy coefficients of explicitly isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at variable densities (from .5$ρ_0$ up to 2 $ρ_0$) are studied as generalized screening functions. An extended stability condition for asymmetric nuclear matter is proposed. We find the possibility of obtaining stable asymmetric nuclear matter even in some cases for which the symmetric nuclear matter limit is unstable. Skyrme-type forces are extensively used in analytical expressions of the symmetry energy coefficients derived as generalized screening functions in the four channels of the particle hole interaction producing alternative behaviors at different $ρ$ and $b$ (respectively the density and the asymmetry coefficient). The spin and spin-isospin coefficients, with corrections to the usual Landau Migdal parameters, indicate the possibility of occurring instabilities with common features depending on the nuclear density and n-p asymmetry. Possible relevance for high energy heavy ions collisions and astrophysical objects is discussed.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficients of asymmetric nuclear matter, treated as generalized screening functions, can yield stable asymmetric nuclear matter even in cases where the symmetric nuclear matter limit is unstable, depending on the density and neutron-proton asymmetry.

## Method

The author derives analytical expressions for the static polarizabilities (inverse symmetry energy coefficients) in the four particle-hole channels (scalar, isovector, spin, and spin-isovector) using Skyrme-type effective forces within a non-relativistic linear response framework. The calculation extends previous work by incorporating explicit isospin asymmetry dependence through a prescription relating density fluctuations to the neutron-proton asymmetry parameter b, and by evaluating the coefficients at densities from 0.5 ρ₀ to 2 ρ₀ for several Skyrme parametrizations (SLyb, SkSC4, SkSC6, SkSC10).

## Key numbers

- The isovector symmetry energy coefficient A₀,₁ for symmetric nuclear matter is taken as aτ ≈ 30 MeV.
- For b = 2 (neutron density three times proton density), the isovector coefficient becomes A = 1.5 A_sym; in the limit of neutron matter (b → ∞), A → 2 A_sym.
- The spin-isospin coefficient A₁,₁ becomes negative (indicating possible pion condensation) between ρ ≈ 1.8 ρ₀ and 2.1 ρ₀ for all forces studied.
- The spin coefficient A₁,₀ is already negative at low densities for SkSC4, SkSC6, and SkSC10, and decreases further with increasing density.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Braghin 1999, 2003) on symmetry energy coefficients and their dependence on neutron-proton asymmetry, using Skyrme forces to explore behaviors that were previously only seen in relativistic calculations (e.g., the increasing symmetry energy with density from SkSC10). The results enable comparisons with microscopic calculations of neutron matter spin susceptibility (Fantoni, Sarsa, and Schmidt 2001) and with variational calculations of the symmetry energy (Baldo and Bomburg), and suggest that different Skyrme parametrizations may be appropriate for different density and asymmetry regimes relevant to neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions.
