# How well do we know the electromagnetic form factors of the proton?

> Several experiments have extracted proton electromagnetic form factors from elastic cross section measurements using the Rosenbluth technique. Global analyses of these measurements indicate approximate scaling of the electric and magnetic form factors (mu_p*G_E/G_M = 1), in contrast to recent polarization transfer measurements from Jefferson Lab. We present here a global reanalysis of the cross section data aimed at understanding the disagreement between the Rosenbluth extraction and the polarization transfer data. We find that the individual cross section measurements are self-consistent, and that the new global analysis yields results that are still inconsistent with polarization measurements. This discrepancy indicates a fundamental problem in one of the two techniques, or a significant error in polarization transfer or cross section measurements. An error in the polarization data would imply a large error in the extracted electric form factor, while an error in the cross sections implies an uncertainty in the extracted form factors, even if the form factor ratiois measured exactly.

## Key claim

The global reanalysis of Rosenbluth cross section data yields results that remain inconsistent with polarization transfer measurements from Jefferson Lab, indicating a fundamental problem in one of the two techniques or a significant error in either the polarization transfer or cross section measurements.

## Method

The author performed a global reanalysis of elastic electron-proton scattering cross section measurements using the Rosenbluth technique, updating radiative corrections for older experiments, subdividing experiments into independent data sets with separate normalization factors, and excluding small-angle data from Walker (1994) that had an identified correction error. The form factors were parameterized using a polynomial expansion in q = sqrt(Q^2) with N=5 parameters, and normalization factors for each of the 20 data sets from 16 experiments were fit simultaneously by minimizing chi-squared.

## Key numbers

- The polarization transfer data yield a fit of mu_p*G_E/G_M = 1 - 0.13(Q^2 - 0.04) with Q^2 in GeV^2, with total chi^2 = 34.9 for 28 points (statistical errors only)
- A refit allowing systematic offsets yields alpha = 0.135 +/- 0.008, Q_0^2 = 0.24 +/- 0.08 GeV^2, and chi^2 = 28.1 for 26 degrees of freedom
- Single experiment extractions from five data sets give chi^2 = 18.2 for 25 degrees of freedom when compared to the global Rosenbluth analysis
- The sum of chi^2 for single experiment extractions versus the global Rosenbluth analysis is 19.1 (79% CL), versus 62.0 (0.006% CL) when compared to the polarization transfer parameterization
- At Q^2 = 5 GeV^2, a 1% normalization difference between high-epsilon and low-epsilon measurements changes the ratio mu_p*G_E/G_M by 12%; at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, by 23%

## Context

This work extends the global analyses of Walker (1994) and Bosted (1994) by incorporating updated cross section data and improved radiative corrections, and directly addresses the discrepancy between Rosenbluth extractions and polarization transfer measurements from Jefferson Lab (Jones 1998, Gayou 2001, Gayou 2002). The analysis demonstrates that the cross section data are internally consistent and cannot be reconciled with the polarization results through exclusion of individual data sets or reasonable normalization adjustments, motivating further experimental tests to resolve the disagreement between the two techniques.
