# QCD sum rules for magnetically induced mixing between $η_c$ and $J/ψ$

> We investigate the properties of charmonia in strong magnetic fields by using QCD sum rules. We show how to implement the mixing effects between $η_c$ and $J/ψ$ on the basis of field-theoretical approaches, and then show that the sum rules are saturated by the mixing effects with phenomenologically determined parameters. Consequently, we find that the mixing effects are the dominant contribution to the mass shifts of the charmonia in strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim
The mixing effects between η_c and J/ψ induced by a strong magnetic field are the dominant contribution to the mass shifts of static charmonia, but the analysis is limited to the regime where |eB| ≪ 4m_c² + Q², and the sum rules are saturated by mixing effects with phenomenologically determined parameters.

## Method
The authors apply QCD sum rules to charmonium correlation functions in external magnetic fields, computing the operator product expansion (OPE) to second order in eB using Wilson coefficients for dimension-4 gluon condensates with appropriate color factor corrections. They construct the phenomenological side by including mixing between the pseudoscalar η_c and the longitudinal J/ψ via an effective Lagrangian vertex J/ψ → η_c + γ, with the coupling constant g_pv = 2.095 determined from the measured radiative decay width Γ_exp[J/ψ → γ η_c]. The sum rule is then evaluated using the standard Borel transformation method.

## Key numbers
- The phenomenological coupling constant g_pv = 2.095, fitted to the measured radiative decay width Γ_exp[J/ψ → γ η_c] = 1.58 keV.
- The direct-coupling strength f_dir is smaller than the mixing-induced contribution by a factor f_dir / (f_0 γ²/M_-⁴) ∼ 0.0003, justifying neglect of direct couplings.
- The Bohr radius a_0 = 0.811 GeV⁻¹, chosen to fit the root-mean-square radius of J/ψ from the Cornell potential model.
- The condition for validity of the OPE expansion is |eB| ≲ 10 m_π², expected for magnetic fields up to Large Hadron Collider energies.

## Context
This work extends QCD sum rule methods previously applied to charmonia at finite temperature by Morita and Lee (2008, 2010) and Gubler, Morita, and Oka (2011) to the case of external magnetic fields. It provides a field-theoretical framework for implementing mixing effects that complements the potential-model approach of Alford and Strickland (2013), who also found level repulsion between η_c and J/ψ. The treatment of mixing effects is intended to serve as a general guideline for other systems, such as B mesons in strong magnetic fields studied by Machado et al. (2014).
