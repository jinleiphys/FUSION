# $β$ decay of odd-A nuclei with the interacting boson-fermion model based on the Gogny energy density functional

> The low-energy excitations and $β$ decays of odd-A nuclei are studied within the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM), based on the Gogny-D1M nuclear energy density functional (EDF). The constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation is employed to compute potential energy surfaces in terms of triaxial quadrupole degrees of freedom for even-even Xe and Ba nuclei in the mass $A\approx 130$ region. The mean field approximation also provides spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the neighboring odd-A nuclei. Those quantities represent a microscopic input for spectroscopic calculations in odd-A Xe and Ba, Cs and La isotopes. The Gamow-Teller (GT) and Fermi (F) transition matrix elements, needed to compute $β$-decay $\log{ft}$ values are obtained without any phenomenological fitting. It is shown that both the low-lying states and $β$ decays of the studied odd-A systems are described reasonably well within the employed theoretical framework.

## Key claim
The interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional describes both low-lying states and beta-decay log ft values in odd-A nuclei of the A ≈ 130 region reasonably well without any phenomenological fitting of the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators, though the computed ft-values systematically underestimate experimental ones, especially for Cs→Xe decays.

## Method
Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1M functional were performed to obtain potential energy surfaces for even-even Xe and Ba cores, from which IBM-2 Hamiltonian parameters were fitted. Spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities from the same mean-field calculation provided microscopic input for the IBFM Hamiltonian, whose three boson-fermion coupling strengths (Γρ, Λρ, Aρ) were adjusted separately for positive and negative parity states to reproduce experimental low-energy levels in odd-A Xe, Ba, Cs, and La isotopes. Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition matrix elements were then computed from the IBFM wave functions using one-fermion transfer operators with no free parameters.

## Key numbers
- IBM-2 parameters for even-even cores: ε ranges from 0.28 MeV (¹²⁶Ba) to 1.00 MeV (¹³⁶Ba); κ ranges from −0.190 MeV (¹³⁴Xe) to −0.336 MeV (¹²⁴Xe)
- Boson-fermion coupling strengths Γρ range from 0.30 MeV (¹³³Xe, ¹³⁵Ba) to 3.20 MeV (¹²³Xe); Λρ range from 0.20 MeV (¹²³Xe) to 2.20 MeV (¹²⁹Xe); Aρ range from −2.7 MeV (¹²⁷La) to 0.0 MeV (¹²⁵Ba, ¹³⁷La)
- Effective charges used: eBν = eBπ = 0.108 e b; eFν = 0.5 e b; eFπ = 1.5 e b
- Boson g-factors: gBν = 0 μN, gBπ = 0.8 μN; free nucleon gA = 1.2701(25) used without quenching
- Computed log ft for ¹²⁵Cs→¹²⁵Xe (1/2⁺₁→1/2⁺₁): 3.725 (experiment ≈5.60)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous EDF-based IBFM approach (Nomura 2016, 2017) from spectroscopy to beta-decay properties in the A≈130 region, where phenomenological IBFM studies of beta decay had been performed earlier by Zuffi (2003) and Mardones (2016). The method provides a microscopic alternative to quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations (Sarriguren 2001, Simkovic 2013) and large-scale shell model approaches (Langanke 2003, Caurier 2005), with the advantage that no phenomenological quenching or normalization of the transition operators is required. The results enable systematic studies of beta-decay properties across isotopic chains and can be extended to odd-odd nuclei and double-beta decay calculations.
