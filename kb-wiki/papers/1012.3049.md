# Relativistic description of 3He(e,e'p)2H

> The Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation is used to describe the $^3$He($e,e^\prime p$)$^2$H process. We describe the $^3$He nucleus within the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. The overlap between the $^3$He and the deuteron wave functions can be accurately computed from a three-body calculation. The nucleons are described by solutions of the Dirac equation with scalar and vector (S-V) potentials. The wave function of the outgoing proton is obtained by solving the Dirac equation with a S-V optical potential fitted to elastic proton scattering data on the residual nucleus. Within this theoretical framework, we compute the cross section of the reaction and other observables like the transverse-longitudinal asymmetry, and compare them with the available experimental data measured at JLab.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (RDWIA) reproduces the JLab experimental data for the 3He(e,e'p)2H reaction very well across most of the missing momentum range, except near p_m = 0, and the calculation is completely parameter-free with no adjustable scale factor.

## Method

The 3He wave function was obtained by solving Faddeev equations with the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction and a structureless three-body potential, and alternatively using a variational correlated hyperspherical harmonic technique with AV18 plus URIX three-nucleon interactions. The overlap between the 3He and deuteron wave functions was computed from these three-body calculations, and the outgoing proton wave function was obtained by solving the Dirac equation with scalar-vector optical potentials fitted to elastic proton scattering data on the residual deuteron. The cross section and transverse-longitudinal asymmetry (A_TL) were then computed within the RDWIA framework and compared to JLab experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The three-body potential in the adiabatic expansion method was fitted to three different binding energies: -6.72 MeV (labeled "a"), -7.72 MeV ("b"), and -8.79 MeV ("c"); the Pisa variational calculation gave a binding energy of -7.74 MeV.
- The experimental data were measured at JLab with "unprecedent accuracy and detail" (no specific numerical precision given in the text).
- The theoretical results are "free from any scale factor" and the agreement with experiment is "comparable or better than the one of non-relativistic calculations."

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic approaches to the 3He(e,e'p)2H reaction by implementing a fully relativistic treatment within the RDWIA framework, using the same overlap integral employed in earlier analyses by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (2008) and Schiavilla et al. (2005). The good agreement between theory and experiment, comparable to non-relativistic calculations that used different frameworks for final-state interactions, indicates that the reaction is mostly sensitive to the overlap integral while other ingredients introduce small uncertainty. This enables future refinement of three-body nuclear structure descriptions by using the experimental data to discriminate between different overlap calculations, as demonstrated by the sensitivity at low missing momentum where the Pisa variational calculation best matches the data.
