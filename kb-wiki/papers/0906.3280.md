# Jet energy loss, photon production, and photon-hadron correlations at RHIC

> Jet energy loss, photon production and photon-hadron correlations are studied together at high transverse momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies. The modification of hard jets traversing a hot and dense nuclear medium is evaluated by consistently taking into account induced gluon radiation and elastic collisions. The production of high transverse momentum photons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is calculated by incorporating a complete set of photon-production channels. Comparison with experimental photon production and photon-hadron correlation data is performed, using a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic description of the thermalized medium created in these collisions. Our results demonstrate that the interaction between the hard jets and the soft medium is important for the study of photon production and of photon-hadron correlation at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent treatment of both radiative and collisional energy loss for hard jets, combined with a complete set of photon-production channels (direct, fragmentation, and jet-medium), is necessary to describe photon production and photon-hadron correlations at RHIC; however, the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for photons shows sensitivity to the precise p+p baseline, indicating that a quantitative understanding of photon data in p+p collisions is needed before firm conclusions about medium effects can be drawn.

## Method

The authors model hard jet evolution in a hot and dense nuclear medium using Fokker-Planck type rate equations that incorporate both induced gluon radiation (with LPM effect) and elastic collisions, following the AMY formalism. The thermalized medium is described by a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic ideal hydrodynamic simulation. High-\(p_T\) photon production is calculated including direct photons from hard partonic scatterings, fragmentation photons from surviving jets, and jet-medium photons (bremsstrahlung and conversion), with the medium-modified fragmentation functions obtained by solving the evolution equations for parton momentum distributions along the jet path.

## Key numbers

- Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.27\) is used throughout the calculation, fixed by describing the experimental \(R_{AA}\) in most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) AGeV.
- The \(K_{\rm jet}\)-factor for jet production is found to be \(2.8\) when renormalization and factorization scales are set to the transverse momentum of initial jets.
- For p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the total photon cross section is fit by a power-law: \(d\sigma/d^2p_Tdy = 1.027 / (1 + p_T/0.793\,{\rm GeV})^{6.873} \,{\rm GeV}^{-2}\,{\rm mb}\).
- In central Au+Au collisions (\(b=2.4\) fm), direct photons dominate at high \(p_T \geq 6\) GeV, while jet-medium interactions contribute significantly in the intermediate \(p_T\) range of approximately 3–5 GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for photons in central Au+Au collisions (\(b=2.4\) fm) shows a sharp rise at low \(p_T\) partly due to the p+p baseline being below data points.
- Photon-triggered fragmentation functions \(D_{pp}(z_T)\) and \(D_{AA}(z_T)\) are presented for associated hadron \(p_T\) ranges 3–5 GeV, 5–7 GeV, and 7–9 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous consistent calculation of radiative and collisional energy loss (Qin 2007) from the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) of neutral pions to photon production and photon-hadron correlations. It improves upon earlier photon calculations that used simpler (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken evolution or (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamics (Turbide 2005, Turbide 2007) by employing a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic medium and a complete set of photon sources. The study addresses the limitation that \(R_{AA}\) in central collisions cannot discriminate between different energy loss models (Renk 2006), and shows that photon-triggered correlations offer stronger tomographic constraints, as previously suggested by Renk (2006) and others. The results enable future quantitative extraction of medium properties by combining isolation and centrality cuts to isolate the predicted negative \(v_2\) contribution from jet-medium photons.
