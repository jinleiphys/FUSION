# The pion-three-nucleon problem with two-cluster connected-kernel equations

> It is found that the coupled piNNN-NNN system breaks into fragments in a nontrivial way. Assuming the particles as distinguishable, there are indeed four modes of fragmentation into two clusters, while in the standard three-body problem there are three possible two-cluster partitions and conversely the four-body problem has seven different possibilities. It is shown how to formulate the pion-three-nucleon collision problem through the integral-equation approach by taking into account the proper fragmentation of the system. The final result does not depend on the assumption of separability of the two-body t-matrices. Then, the quasiparticle method a' la Grassberger-Sandhas is applied and effective two-cluster connected-kernel equations are obtained. The corresponding bound-state problem is also formulated, and the resulting homogeneous equation provides a new approach which generalizes the commonly used techniques to describe the three-nucleon bound-state problem, where the meson degrees of freedom are usually suppressed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the coupled πNNN-NNN system can be formulated with connected-kernel integral equations using only four two-cluster partitions (rather than the seven partitions of the standard four-body problem), provided the Fock space is truncated to states with at most one dynamical pion and the formalism is restricted below the threshold for second-pion production.

## Method

The author starts from the Afnan-Blankleider equations for the coupled πNN-NN system and reformulates them as a matrix Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the πNNN-NNN system. The key step is identifying the correct four two-cluster partitions of the system (Tab. 3), then introducing subsystem equations for each partition and a sum rule for the generalized potential. The quasiparticle method à la Grassberger-Sandhas is applied to obtain effective two-cluster connected-kernel equations, without assuming separability of the two-body t-matrices.

## Key numbers

- Four modes of fragmentation into two clusters are identified for the πNNN-NNN system, compared to three in the standard three-body problem and seven in the four-body problem
- For each partition with s ≠ 0, there are five possible sequential break-ups in the four-body sector and one in the three-nucleon sector, giving six components for each channel interaction
- The total corresponds to 18 Yakubovskii components necessary for the complete dynamical description of four-body states

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev-Yakubovskii-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas formalism, originally developed for fixed numbers of particles, to the pion-multinucleon dynamics where particle number is not conserved. It corrects earlier attempts by Avishai and Mizutani (1993) and Ueda (1993) that either failed to produce connected-kernel equations or could not be linked to the underlying few-body dynamics. The author's own previous approach (Canton and collaborators, 1994) was shown to have disconnected kernels and ambiguous amplitudes (Canton and collaborators, 1997). The new formulation enables non-perturbative treatment of pion dynamics in three-nucleon bound-state calculations, generalizing techniques that typically suppress meson degrees of freedom, and provides a framework for consistently separating two- and three-body forces in the three-nucleon system.
