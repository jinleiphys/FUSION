# Quantized electrical conductivity in binary neutron star mergers

> We examine nature of longitudinal electrical conductivity in magnetized electron-ion plasma in the context of binary neutron star mergers. In presence of strong magnetic field, high density and temperature, quantum oscillatory behaviour for electrons emerge due to breakdown of the classical description. For pronounced thermodynamic effects, we consider zeroth Landau level population of electrons for electrical conductivity. We solve Boltzmann equation in presence of magnetic field to obtain the dissipative component of the conductivity. The conductivity is formulated considering dynamically scattering centres in the medium with magnetically modified screening. Numerical estimations show that the effect of magnetically modified screening mass on electrical conductivity is less. On the other hand, we observe that frequency dependent screening reduces electrical conductivity leading to a reduction in the Ohmic decay time scale to become of the order of the characteristic timescale of the merger process in the low density regime. This indicates the relevance of dissipative process for the merger simulation in the above mentioned domain.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when frequency-dependent (dynamical) screening is included in the calculation of quantized longitudinal electrical conductivity in magnetized electron-ion plasma relevant to binary neutron star mergers, the Ohmic decay time scale becomes comparable to the characteristic timescale of the merger process in the low density regime, indicating that dissipative processes cannot be neglected in merger simulations under those conditions.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in the presence of a strong magnetic field to obtain the dissipative (longitudinal) component of the electrical conductivity tensor, considering only the zeroth Landau level population of electrons for maximum quantum effect. The electron-ion scattering rate is computed using Hard-Dense-Loop (HDL) resummed photon propagators that include both longitudinal and transverse contributions with magnetically modified screening, treating ions as dynamical scattering centers rather than static ones. The resulting conductivity is then used to estimate the Ohmic decay time scale of the magnetic field via the induction equation.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic field strength considered: up to \(10^{17}\) G, with the critical magnetic field \(B_{cr}=4.413\times 10^{13}\) G used to define the dimensionless parameter \(b=B/B_{cr}\).
* Density regime examined: up to \(10^{12}\) gm/cm\(^3\) (low density regime for the merger context).
* Temperature regime examined: up to \(\sim 15\) MeV (with a typical value of \(\sim 12\) MeV stated).
* The condition for strong Landau quantization is given by \(\rho < \rho_B\) and \(T \ll T_{ce}\), where \(\rho_B = 7.045\times 10^3 (A/Z) (B_{10^{12}})^{3/2}\) g cm\(^{-3}\) and \(T_{ce} \approx 1.343\times 10^8 B_{10^{12}}\) K.
* The Ohmic decay time scale is found to become of the order of the characteristic timescale of the merger process (few milliseconds) in the low density regime when frequency-dependent screening is included.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electrical conductivity in neutron star crusts that used static screening or HTL-modified propagators without Landau quantization (Harutyunyan 2016) and earlier quantized conductivity calculations assuming static ions (Yakovlev 1980, Hernquist 1984, Potekhin 1996). It also builds on studies showing that Hall decay may be relevant for the survival time of the merged object (Harutyunyan 2018) and that dissipative effects could matter in merger simulations (Alford 2017). The paper enables more realistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of binary neutron star mergers by providing quantized conductivity values that include dynamical screening, showing that ideal MHD may break down in the low-density, high-magnetic-field regime.
