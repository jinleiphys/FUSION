# Squeezed particle-antiparticle correlations

> A novel type of correlation involving particle-antiparticle pairs was found out in the 1990's. Currently known as Squeezed or Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC), they should be present if the hadronic masses are modified in the hot and dense medium formed in high energy heavy ion collisions. Although well-established theoretically, such hadronic correlations have not yet been observed experimentally. In this phenomenological study we suggest a promising way to search for the BBC signal, by looking into the squeezed correlation function of phi phi and K^+ K^- pairs at RHIC energies, as function of the pair average momentum, K_{12}=(k_1+k_2)/2. The effects of in-medium mass-shift on the identical particle correlations (Hanbury-Brown & Twiss effect) are also discussed.

# Squeezed particle-antiparticle correlations

## Key claim

The paper suggests that squeezed (back-to-back) correlations between particle-antiparticle pairs, such as φφ and K⁺K⁻, could be observable at RHIC energies if hadronic masses are modified in the hot and dense medium, but only if the correlation function is analyzed as a function of the pair average momentum K₁₂ rather than the unobserved in-medium mass shift.

## Method

The authors compute the squeezed correlation function Cₛ(k₁,k₂) for φφ and K⁺K⁻ pairs using a non-relativistic treatment with a flow-independent squeezing parameter, derived from a Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation relating asymptotic and in-medium creation/annihilation operators. The correlation function is evaluated for a finite-size, expanding system with moderate radial flow (⟨u⟩=0.5) and finite emission time (Δt=2 fm/c), and plotted as a function of the pair average momentum 2K = (k₁+k₂) for various values of the relative momentum q = (k₁−k₂). The effects of in-medium mass shift on the identical particle (HBT) correlation function are also examined.

## Key numbers

- For φφ pairs with R=7 fm, radial flow ⟨u⟩=0.5, and finite emission time Δt=2 fm/c, the squeezed correlation signal is stronger over all investigated |q| regions compared to the no-flow case.
- For φφ pairs, the squeezed correlation is studied for two source radii: R=7 fm and R=3 fm, with fixed |q₁₂|=2.0 GeV/c.
- For K⁺K⁻ pairs, the squeezed correlation is analyzed for momenta within |K₁₂| ≤ 10 MeV/c, with a shifted mass of m* ≈ 650 MeV corresponding to one of the maxima.
- The finite emission time reduction factor is given by a Lorentzian: F(Δt) = [1 + (ω₁+ω₂)² Δt²]⁻¹, with Δt=2 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical formulation of squeezed (back-to-back) correlations originally developed for static infinite media by Padula et al. (1999) and later generalized to finite-size expanding systems by Padula et al. (2005). The paper provides a practical experimental search strategy by suggesting analysis in terms of the pair average momentum K rather than the unobserved in-medium mass, which had previously hindered experimental searches. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC and later LHC energies, and also predict that squeezing effects would distort the HBT correlation function, opposing the broadening effects of flow.
