# R-matrix analysis of elastic scattering, phase shift and radiative capture reaction cross sections in the $α+ α$ system

> The unstable nucleus $^8$Be, with its two $α$-cluster configuration, is the doorway to the formation of heavier $α$-cluster nuclei. Most importantly, its the precursor of the production of $^{12}$C through the Hoyle state, a resonance state of three $α$ clusters, in the helium burning phase of a massive star. The nucleus exhibits a ground state band of rotational states established through $α-α$ scattering experiments. A subsequent precision particle-$γ$ coincidence measurement of the electromagnetic transition between the 4$^+\rightarrow$ 2$^+$ excited states also corroborated the evidence for a highly deformed dumb-bell shaped structure of $^8$Be. A simultaneous phenomenological R-matrix analysis of the measured capture reaction cross sections along with the elastic excitation function and phase shift data has been performed. The resulting reduced transition strength of 21.96$\pm$3.86 $e^2 fm^4$ compares well with the estimated experimental value of 21.0$\pm$2.3 e$^2$ fm$^4$. The R-matrix yield of the B($E2$) value is closer to the prediction of cluster model but about 19$\%$ less than the {\it ab initio} result.

# R-matrix analysis of elastic scattering, phase shift and radiative capture reaction cross sections in the α+α system

## Key claim
A simultaneous phenomenological R-matrix analysis of elastic scattering, phase shift, and radiative capture data for the α+α system yields a reduced E2 transition strength B(E2)=21.96±3.86 e²fm⁴ for the 4⁺→2⁺ transition in ⁸Be, which is within error bars of the experimental value but about 19% lower than the ab initio prediction, and the fit required sacrificing the quality of the g-wave phase shift description in the 12–17 MeV energy window to maintain consistency with the capture data.

## Method
The authors performed a simultaneous phenomenological R-matrix analysis using the AZURE2 code, fitting three categories of data: elastic excitation functions at four center-of-mass angles (30.6°, 40.0°, 54.8°, and 90.0°) from 2 to 6 MeV, phase shift data for l=0, 2, 4 partial waves from 0.45 to 25 MeV, and radiative capture cross sections for the α(α,γ)⁸Be reaction around the 4⁺ resonance. Eight resonances in ⁸Be were considered, including the ground state band (0⁺, 2⁺, 4⁺) and higher-lying states, with four background poles introduced for Jπ=0⁺, 2⁺, and 4⁺. A channel radius of 3.73 fm was chosen after a grid search over 3.40–4.00 fm.

## Key numbers
- Reduced E2 transition strength B(E2) for 4⁺→2⁺: 21.96±3.86 e²fm⁴, compared to experimental value 21.0±2.3 e²fm⁴ and ab initio value 27.5±0.15 e²fm⁴
- Gamma partial width for 4⁺→2⁺ transition: Γγ = 0.502±0.084 eV, corresponding to branching ratio (1.88±0.32)×10⁻⁷
- Best-fit resonance energy of first 4⁺ state: Ex = 11.62±0.04 MeV, with particle width Γα = 2.67±0.04 MeV
- Best-fit resonance energy of first 2⁺ state: Ex = 3.04±0.001 MeV, with particle width Γα = 1.569±0.004 MeV
- Ground state 0⁺ particle width: Γα = (4.665±0.006)×10⁻⁶ MeV
- Peak cross section for 2⁺→0⁺ E2 capture: 10.3 nb (compared to 14 nb estimated in Langanke (1985))
- Channel radius chosen: 3.73 fm, with insensitivity range 3.40–4.00 fm

## Context
This work extends the R-matrix methodology applied to α-cluster nuclei, following the precision particle-γ coincidence measurement by Datar et al. (2013, 2015) that provided the first electromagnetic signature of the deformed dumbbell shape of ⁸Be. The analysis simultaneously constrains resonance parameters from scattering data (Tombrello et al., Heydenburg and Temmer, Nilson et al.) and capture data, resolving discrepancies between the cluster model prediction of 18.06 e²fm⁴ (Langanke and Rolfs) and the ab initio Green's Function Monte Carlo result of 27.5 e²fm⁴ (Datar et al., 2015). The results enable improved constraints on the structure of the ground state rotational band in ⁸Be and motivate future measurements of capture cross sections around the first 2⁺ resonance, which would test model predictions for the 2⁺→0⁺ reduced transition probability.
