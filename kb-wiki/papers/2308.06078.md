# Characterizing nuclear modification effects in high-energy O-O collisions at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider: A transport model perspective

> The present work focuses on Oxygen-Oxygen (O-O) collisions, which are planned at the CERN Large Hadron Collider. Oxygen, being a doubly magic number nucleus, has some very unique features. This study attempts to probe the exotic state of QCD matter in O-O collisions. Additionally, the role of different nuclear density profiles in governing the final state dynamics in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions is also explored. Using a multi-phase transport model, we obtain the nuclear modification factor ($\textit R_{\textit {AA}}$) for all charged hadrons and identified particles for O-O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}}$ = 7 TeV. Furthermore, we investigate the behavior of $\textit R_{\textit {AA}}$ as a function of transverse momentum ($\textit{p}_{\rm{T}}$) for three centralities (most central, mid-central, and peripheral) considering both $α$-cluster and Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles. We also extend this work to study the rapidity dependence of $\textit R_{\textit {AA}}$ for all charged hadrons. To better understand our findings of O-O collisions, the results are confronted with the available data of $\textit R_{\textit {AA}}$ for Pb-Pb collisions. The present study sheds light on particle production mechanisms, emphasizing factors influencing particle yield from pre-collision to post-collision stages in the context of O-O collisions.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT model with string melting, the authors find that the nuclear modification factor R_AA in central (0-5%) O-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV is comparable to that in (50-60%) Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, but with less suppression, suggesting that the larger geometric radius of Pb-Pb collisions leads to greater parton energy loss even at similar final-state multiplicities.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model (version 2.26t9b) in string melting mode with Lund fragmentation parameters a = 0.3, b = 0.15 GeV^-2 and parton cross-section sigma_gg = 3 mb for O-O and Pb-Pb collisions. The oxygen nucleus is modeled with both a Woods-Saxon density profile (r_0 = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051) and an alpha-clustered structure where four ^4He nuclei are placed at tetrahedron vertices with side length 3.42 fm. The nuclear modification factor R_AA is computed for charged hadrons and identified particles (pions, kaons, protons) as a function of transverse momentum for three centrality classes (0-5%, 30-40%, 60-70%), using pp reference spectra from both ALICE experimental data and AMPT simulations.

## Key numbers

- O-O collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV: average charged-particle multiplicity density <dN_ch/deta> = 187.54 +/- 0.14 for (0-5%) centrality with alpha-clustered density profile, and 161.07 +/- 0.15 for Woods-Saxon profile
- Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: <dN_ch/deta> = 183.00 +/- 8.00 (ALICE) for (50-60%) centrality, compared to 235.25 +/- 0.56 from AMPT simulation
- Number of binary collisions <N_coll> for O-O (0-5%): 55.12 +/- 8.90 (alpha-cluster) and 48.33 +/- 9.43 (Woods-Saxon); for Pb-Pb (50-60%): 90.88 +/- 33.00
- Number of participating nucleons <N_part> for O-O (0-5%): 30.73 +/- 2.06 (alpha-cluster) and 29.26 +/- 1.99 (Woods-Saxon) from AMPT
- Root-mean-square radius of alpha-clustered oxygen nucleus: 2.6999 fm; RMS radius of ^4He: 1.676 fm
- Tetrahedron side length for alpha-clustered oxygen: 3.42 fm

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of O-O collisions at LHC energies by Lim (2018), Rybczynski (2019), and Huss (2020), and builds on the authors' prior study of alpha-clustered oxygen structure (Behera, 2021). The comparison of O-O and Pb-Pb collisions at similar multiplicities follows the approach suggested by Brewer (2021) and Sievert (2019), who noted that Pb-Pb collisions have a 60% larger radius than O-O collisions at the same multiplicity. The finding that suppression is stronger in Pb-Pb than O-O at comparable multiplicity is consistent with the system-size dependence observed in ALICE (2018) comparisons of Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb collisions. This work enables future experimental studies of the short O-O run planned at the LHC, particularly for disentangling initial-state nuclear structure effects from final-state medium effects in small collision systems.
