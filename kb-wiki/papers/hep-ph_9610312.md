# QED Penguin Contributions To Isospin Splittings of Heavy-Light Quark Systems

> Recent experiments show that the isospin-violating mass splitting of the B mesons is very small, but the best fits with a QCD sum rule analysis give a splitting of at least 1.0 MeV. The isospin-violating mass splittings of the charmed mesons, on the other hand, are in agreement with experiment. In this letter we show that the inclusion of 2$^{nd}$ kind QED penguin diagrams can account for this discrepancy within the errors in the QCD sum rule method.

## Key claim

Inclusion of second-kind QED penguin diagrams can resolve the discrepancy between QCD sum rule predictions (which give a B meson isospin splitting of at least 1.0 MeV) and experimental measurements (which give 0.35 ± 0.29 MeV), while leaving the already-agreeing D meson splittings unchanged.

## Method

The authors compute isospin-violating mass splittings for heavy-light pseudoscalar and vector mesons using QCD sum rules, incorporating a quark-gluon vertex modification from second-kind QED penguin diagrams. They separate the contributions into nonperturbative (dimension-5 mixed condensate terms) and perturbative parts, using Borel-transformed sum rules to extract mass shifts. The penguin vertex modification V_q is taken from Goldman, Morley, and Stephenson (1992) in the limit of characteristic momenta larger than Λ_QCD.

## Key numbers

- Nonperturbative QED penguin contribution to D meson isospin splitting: 0.07 MeV
- Nonperturbative QED penguin contribution to B meson isospin splitting: 0.5 MeV
- Perturbative QED penguin contribution to D meson isospin splitting: 0.1–0.2 MeV (for threshold parameter s_0 = 1.8–2.1 GeV²)
- Perturbative QED penguin contribution to B meson isospin splitting: 0.7–1.5 MeV (for threshold parameter s_0 = 5.0–6.3 GeV²)
- Net B meson isospin splitting after including penguin contributions: 0.0–0.8 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.35 ± 0.29 MeV from CLEO (1995)
- Previous non-penguin QCD sum rule result for B⁺–B⁰ mass difference: –1.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the gauge-invariant QCD sum rule method for isospin splittings developed by Kisslinger, Li, and Miller (1995), which successfully described D and D* mesons but gave a B meson splitting inconsistent with experiment. The authors apply the second-kind electromagnetic penguin mechanism proposed by Goldman, Morley, and Stephenson (1992), which modifies the quark-gluon vertex and becomes more important for heavier quarks. The results reconcile the QCD sum rule approach with CLEO (1995) experimental data and suggest that the same framework can account for heavy-light meson isospin splittings without modifying existing light-quark parameters.
