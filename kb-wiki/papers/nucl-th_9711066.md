# Thermal Model Analysis of Particle Ratios at GSI Ni-Ni Experiments Using Exact Strangeness Conservation

> The production of hadrons in Ni-Ni at the GSI is considered in a hadronic gas model with chemical equilibrium. Special attention is given to the abundance of strange particles which are treated using the exact conservation of strangeness. It is found that all the data can be described using a temperature T = 70 pm 10 MeV and a baryon chemical potential mu_B = 720 pm 20 MeV.

# Thermal Model Analysis of Particle Ratios at GSI Ni-Ni Experiments Using Exact Strangeness Conservation

## Key claim
All measured hadronic ratios from Ni-Ni collisions at GSI, including strange particles, can be described by a hadronic gas model in chemical equilibrium using three parameters (temperature T ≈ 70 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ_B ≈ 720 MeV, and system radius R ≈ 4 fm), provided that exact strangeness conservation is imposed rather than a strangeness suppression factor γ_S. The authors note that this does not explain why K^- mesons are produced so easily below threshold, and that momentum spectra deviate strongly from a simple thermal distribution.

## Method
The authors used a hadronic gas model with chemical equilibrium, imposing exact (canonical) strangeness conservation by projecting the grand canonical partition function onto the state with strangeness S=0 via an integral over a phase angle φ. Particle abundances were calculated including all hadrons listed by the Particle Data Group (1994), with resonance widths treated via Breit-Wigner integration and isospin asymmetry accounted for using the condition n_B = 2(B/2Q)n_Q with B/2Q = 1.04 for Ni-Ni. The model contains all particles with strangeness 0, ±1, and the authors verified that cascade particles (strangeness ±2, ±3) do not modify the results at GSI energies.

## Key numbers
- Temperature: T = 70 ± 10 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential: μ_B = 720 ± 20 MeV
- System radius: R ≈ 4 fm (with additional calculations shown for R = 3.7 fm)
- Isospin asymmetry parameter for Ni-Ni: B/2Q = 1.04 (4% deviation from isospin symmetric case)
- Experimental ratio used: K^+/K^- = 26 ± 9
- Resulting Λ/K^+ ratio: 0.96 ± 0.02 (neglecting isospin asymmetry); 0.99 ± 0.03 (including charge chemical potential)
- Λ/K^0 ratio: 0.94 ± 0.03
- Charge chemical potential range extracted: μ_Q between -2 MeV and -6 MeV

## Context
This work extends the hadronic gas model analysis previously applied to non-strange particle ratios at GSI (Braun-Munzinger, private communication; TAPS Collaboration, 1997) by treating strange particles with exact strangeness conservation. The results occupy a distinct region in the (T, μ_B) plane compared to higher-energy experiments: GSI gives T ≈ 60-100 MeV and μ_B ≈ 700 MeV, while BNL gives T ≈ 100-140 MeV and μ_B ≈ 450-600 MeV, and CERN gives T ≈ 160-200 MeV and μ_B ≈ 180-350 MeV (Sollfrank, 1997; Becattini et al., 1997). The paper shows that the suppression of strange particles at GSI energies can be fully accounted for by canonical strangeness conservation without introducing a strangeness suppression factor γ_S, unlike analyses at higher CERN energies. The results enable mapping of freeze-out conditions across different collision energies and provide constraints for models of in-medium kaon mass modifications (Schaffner et al., 1996; Brown et al., 1997; Waas et al., 1997).
