# Predicting Energies of Small Clusters from the Inhomogeneous Unitary Fermi Gas

> We investigate the inhomogeneous unitary Fermi gas and use the long-wavelength properties to predict the energies of small clusters of unitary fermions trapped in harmonic potentials. The large pairing gap and scale invariance place severe restrictions on the form of the density functional. We determine the relevant universal constants needed to constrain the functional from calculations of the bulk in oscillating external potentials. Comparing with exact Quantum Monte Carlo calculations, we find that the same functional correctly predicts the lack of shell closures for small clusters of fermions trapped in harmonic wells as well as their absolute energies. A rapid convergence to the bulk limit in three dimensions, where the surface to volume ratio is quite large, is demonstrated. The resulting functional can be tested experimentally, and is a key ingredient in predicting possible polarized superfluid phases and the properties of the unitary Fermi gas in optical lattices.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple density functional with only two parameters beyond the local density approximation, constrained by the scale invariance and large pairing gap of the unitary Fermi gas, can accurately predict the energies of even very small trapped clusters of fermions, including the lack of shell closures. The hedge is that this functional is derived from bulk calculations in periodic potentials and validated against Quantum Monte Carlo results, but the functional's success depends on the specific extracted coefficients and the perturbative treatment of higher-order terms.

## Method

The authors performed Diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) and Auxiliary Field Monte Carlo (AFMC) calculations of the inhomogeneous unitary Fermi gas in three dimensions, using both a simple quadratic dispersion and an improved lattice action. They extracted the static structure factor and the static density susceptibility from calculations in a one-dimensional periodic external potential, and used these to determine the coefficients \(c_2\) and \(c_4\) in a gradient-expanded density functional. The functional was then applied to predict the ground-state energies of small clusters of fermions trapped in a harmonic potential, and these predictions were compared to independent DMC and AFMC calculations for those trapped systems.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the unitary Fermi gas energy to the free Fermi gas energy is \(\xi = E/E_{FG} \approx 0.37\).
- The pairing gap relative to the Fermi energy is \(\Delta / E_F \approx 0.45(5)\).
- The coefficient of the first gradient-squared correction in the density functional is \(c_2 = 0.30(5)\), extracted from DMC and AFMC results.
- For the static structure factor at \(q/k_F = 0.5\), the ratio \(S^1(q)/(k_F^2 S^0(q)) = 0.402(5)\).
- The finite-size energy correction for the \(q^2\) dispersion in the harmonic trap is \(\delta E(N)/E_{TF} \approx 0.0388\ \omega^{1/2} N^{1/6}\), which is approximately a 2% lowering of the QMC energy.
- The \(q^4\) propagator correction yields an approximately 1% increase in the energy for trapped systems.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the homogeneous unitary Fermi gas (Giorgini 2008, Randeria 2013) and recent experiments on small clusters of cold fermions at unitarity (Zurn 2013, Wenz 2013). It contradicts the expectation of significant shell closures for fermions in harmonic traps, as seen in the BCS limit or in atomic nuclei, and instead shows behavior analogous to Bose gases (Forbes 2012). The extracted density functional coefficients can be used to predict properties of the unitary Fermi gas in optical lattices and to constrain more sophisticated functionals such as the superfluid local density approximation (Bulgac 2007, Forbes 2012), enabling studies of polarized superfluid phases and inhomogeneous superconductors.
