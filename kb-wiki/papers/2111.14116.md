# Far from equilibrium Chiral Magnetic Effect in Strong Magnetic Fields from Holography

> We study the real time evolution of the chiral magnetic effect out-of-equilibrium in strongly coupled anomalous field theories. We match the parameters of our model to QCD parameters and draw lessons of possible relevance for the realization of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions. In particular, we find an equilibration time of about $\sim0.35$ fm/c in presence of the chiral anomaly for plasma temperatures of order $T\sim300-400$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors find that the chiral magnetic effect (CME) current builds up fast enough to be observable in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC but not at LHC, based on holographic simulations of strongly coupled plasma with an equilibration time of about 0.35 fm/c for plasma temperatures of order 300-400 MeV. This conclusion depends on the specific matching of the holographic model parameters to QCD and on the assumption that the magnetic field lifetime follows the formula tau_B ~ 115 GeV fm/c / sqrt(s).

## Method

The authors model the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma using a holographic (AdS/CFT) setup with a U(1)_A x U(1)_V gauge symmetry and a Chern-Simons term implementing the chiral anomaly. They solve the full backreacted Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations of motion in infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, starting from an initial state with zero CME current and zero dynamical pressure anisotropy but finite axial charge density and vector magnetic field. The time evolution of the vector current and pressure anisotropy is extracted from the asymptotic expansions of the bulk fields, and the model parameters are matched to three-flavor QCD entropy and anomaly coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time for the CME current: about 0.35 fm/c for plasma temperatures T ~ 300-400 MeV (abstract and conclusions).
- For RHIC parameters (T ~ 300 MeV, B = m_pi^2, mu_5 = 10 MeV): peak of vector current at v_peak ~ 0.54 fm/c; equilibration time for current v_eq^<J> = 0.380 fm/c across all initial pressure anisotropies studied (Table 1).
- For LHC parameters (T ~ 1000 MeV, B = 15 m_pi^2, mu_5 = 10 MeV): peak of vector current at v_peak ~ 0.14 fm/c; equilibration time for current v_eq^<J> = 0.114 fm/c across all initial pressure anisotropies studied (Table 2).
- Magnetic field lifetime estimates: tau_B^RHIC ~ 0.6 fm/c at sqrt(s) ~ 200 GeV; tau_B^LHC ~ 0.02 fm/c at sqrt(s) ~ 5000 GeV (from Guo (2019)).
- Chern-Simons coupling matched to QCD: alpha ~ 0.316; Newton constant matched to entropy: kappa^2 ~ 12.5.
- Axial chemical potential values used: mu_5 = 10 MeV (primary) and mu_5 = 100 MeV (check); magnetic field values: B = m_pi^2 and B = 0.1 m_pi^2 for RHIC; B = 15 m_pi^2 and B = 1.5 m_pi^2 for LHC.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of out-of-equilibrium chiral transport by Lin (2013), Ammon (2016), Grieninger (2017), Haack (2018), Cartwright (2020), Kharzeev (2020), Landsteiner (2017), Fernandez-Pendas (2019), and Morales-Tejera (2020) by incorporating the full backreacted magnetic field for the first time. The results enable a direct comparison with heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, suggesting that the CME should be observable at RHIC but not at LHC due to the shorter magnetic field lifetime at higher collision energies. The equilibration time found (~0.35 fm/c) is shorter than the ~0.5 fm/c estimate of Chesler (2008) and consistent with the ~0.3 fm/c estimate of Heinz (2004) for similar plasma temperatures.
