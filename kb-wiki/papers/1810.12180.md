# Core-polarization effects and effective charges in O and Ni isotopes from chiral interactions

> Most nuclear structure calculations, even for full configuration interaction approaches, are performed within truncated model spaces. These require consistent transformations of the Hamiltonian and operators to account for the missing physics beyond the active space, so that several recent efforts have been devoted to find compatible derivations of the effective operators. The effective charges employed in the shell-model calculations, and fitted to reproduce experimental data, can be seen as the phenomenological counterpart of such renormalization for electromagnetic operators. Here, we make a first step to lay the bases for their microscopic derivation in the context of the Self-Consistent Green's Function approach. We compute electric quadrupole (E2) effective charges from microscopic theory by coupling the single-nucleon propagators to core-polarization phonons, derived consistently from a realistic nuclear interaction. The polarization effects are included by evaluating the Feynman diagrams that couple the internal multi-nucleon configurations to the single-particle transitions induced by the electromagnetic operator. The effective charges for E2 static moments and transitions are computed for selected isotopes in the Oxygen ($^{14}\text{O}$, $^{16}$O, $^{22}$O and $^{24}$O) and Nickel ($^{48}$Ni, $^{56}$Ni, $^{68}$Ni and $^{78}$Ni) chains. The values found are orbital dependent especially for the neutron effective charges, which show also a characteristic decreasing trend along each isotopic chain. In general, the values are compatible with the phenomenological ones commonly used for shell-model studies in the $0p\, 1s\, 0d$ and $1p\,0f\,0g_{\frac{9}{2}}$ valence spaces.The phenomenological shell-model effective charges can be explained through \textit{ab initio} approaches, where the sole experimental input comes from the fitting of the realistic nuclear interaction.

## Key claim

This work provides a first-principles derivation of orbital-dependent effective charges for E2 transitions in Oxygen and Nickel isotopes using the Self-Consistent Green's Function (SCGF) approach, showing that the phenomenological shell-model effective charges can be explained through ab initio methods where the only experimental input is the fitting of the realistic nuclear interaction. The effective charges are computed by coupling single-nucleon propagators to core-polarization phonons derived consistently from chiral interactions, with the caveat that the values are orbital dependent and show a decreasing trend for neutron effective charges along each isotopic chain.

## Method

The authors computed electric quadrupole (E2) effective charges by evaluating Feynman diagrams that couple internal multi-nucleon configurations to single-particle transitions induced by the electromagnetic operator, using the third-order Algebraic Diagrammatic Construction (ADC(3)) and Faddeev Random-Phase Approximation (FRPA) methods. The effective charge between two orbits was defined as the ratio of the correlated matrix element (including core-polarization effects) to the bare matrix element in the valence space, with intermediate state configurations belonging to the valence space explicitly excluded to avoid double counting. The calculations used the Optimized Reference State (OpRS) propagator as the mean-field basis, and the self-energy was decomposed into static and dynamic parts, with the dynamic part incorporating all-order resummations of particle-particle, hole-hole, and particle-hole phonons.

## Key numbers

- Effective charges were computed for Oxygen isotopes: 14O, 16O, 22O, and 24O, and Nickel isotopes: 48Ni, 56Ni, 68Ni, and 78Ni
- Standard phenomenological effective charges for sd-shell nuclei are e_pi ~ 1.4 and e_nu ~ 0.5
- Standard phenomenological effective charges for pf-shell nuclei are e_pi ~ 1.5 and e_nu ~ 0.5
- The bare charges are e_nu = 0 for neutrons and e_pi = 1 for protons

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic derivations of effective charges using particle-vibration coupling models based on phenomenological interactions (Sagawa 1984, Hamamoto 1996, Sagawa 2004) by employing chiral nuclear interactions with no adjustable parameters beyond the interaction fit. It also builds on recent progress in deriving effective Hamiltonians from first principles using SCGF methods (Barbieri 2009) and in-medium similarity renormalization group approaches (PhysRevC.96.034324, 2017). The results enable future shell-model calculations with orbital-dependent effective charges tailored to specific valence spaces and numbers of active nucleons, potentially improving predictions for neutron-rich isotopes where standard effective charges have been questioned (Yuan 2012, PhysRevC.79.054303, PhysRevC.79.011302).
