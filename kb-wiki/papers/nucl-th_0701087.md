# Charmonium-Nucleon Dissociation Cross Sections in the Quark Model

> Charmonium dissociation cross sections due to flavor-exchange charmonium-baryon scattering are computed in the constituent quark model. We present results for inelastic $J/ψN$ and $η_c N$ scattering amplitudes and cross sections into 46 final channels, including final states composed of various combinations of $D$, $D^*$, $Σ_c$, and $Λ_c$. These results are relevant to experimental searches for the deconfined phase of quark matter, and may be useful in identifying the contribution of initial $c\bar c$ production to the open-charm final states observed at RHIC through the characteristic flavor ratios of certain channels. These results are also of interest to possible charmonium-nucleon bound states.

# Charmonium-Nucleon Dissociation Cross Sections in the Quark Model

## Key claim

The constituent quark model predicts that charmonium-nucleon dissociation cross sections rise rapidly at threshold and are then strongly damped above a hadron momentum scale set by the hadronic wavefunctions, in disagreement with meson exchange models that find slowly increasing total cross sections; the most important process, J/ψ p → D̅⁰ Λc⁺, has a peak cross section of approximately 7 mb, but the authors caution that this computation deals with free charmonium scattering rather than the "pre"-charmonium expected in RHIC collisions, and substantial density dependence may be present.

## Method

The authors computed Born-order quark interchange scattering amplitudes for charmonium-nucleon inelastic scattering using a constituent quark model interaction Hamiltonian containing color Coulomb, linear confinement, and regularized contact spin-spin hyperfine terms with T·T color structure. They evaluated 46 exclusive final channels involving various combinations of D, D*, Σc, and Λc mesons and baryons, using simple harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and summing over four quark-interchange diagrams. Cross sections were computed from the amplitudes using the standard two-body scattering formula with final-state phase space factors.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ p → D̅⁰ Λc⁺: peak cross section of 6.815 mb at center-of-mass energy 4.184 GeV (S=1/2 channel)
- J/ψ p → D̅⁰* Λc⁺: maximum cross section of 1.712 mb at 4.324 GeV (S=3/2 channel)
- ηc p → D̅⁰ Λc⁺: peak cross section of 1.190 mb at 4.196 GeV
- J/ψ p → D⁻ Σc⁺⁺: peak cross section of 0.246 mb at 4.355 GeV (S=1/2 channel)
- J/ψ p → D⁻* Σc⁺⁺: peak cross section of 0.354 mb at 4.501 GeV (S=1/2 channel)
- J/ψ p → D̅⁰ Σc⁺: peak cross section of 0.126 mb at 4.350 GeV (S=1/2 channel)
- J/ψ p → D̅⁰* Σc⁺: peak cross section of 0.180 mb at 4.498 GeV (S=1/2 channel)
- Total J/ψ N dissociation cross section peaks near 7 mb at threshold and falls to approximately 0.1 mb by 5 GeV center-of-mass energy
- Total ηc N dissociation cross section peaks near 1.5 mb at threshold and falls to approximately 0.1 mb by 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model approach to charmonium dissociation previously applied to meson-charmonium scattering by Martin et al. (1992) and Wong et al. (2000), now computing the first results for charmonium dissociation due to scattering from nucleons. The results disagree with meson exchange models such as those by Liu, Ko, and Lin (2001) and Sibirtsev, Tsushima, and Thomas (2001), which find larger cross sections for D*Λc channels than DΛc channels and predict slowly increasing total cross sections. The strong near-threshold behavior suggests that hydrodynamic simulations of charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions should be revisited, and the amplitudes are also relevant to the time-reversed process of charmonium regeneration and to possible charmonium-nucleon bound states as suggested by Brodsky et al. (1990).
