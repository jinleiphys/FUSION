# Study of relativistic bound states in a scalar model using diagonalization/Monte Carlo methods

> We use a recently proposed diagonalization/Monte Carlo computational scheme to study relativistic two-body and three-body bound states in 1+1 dimensional phi^6-phi^4 theory. We find that the approach is well-suited for calculating bound state energies and wavefunctions.

## Key claim
The diagonalization/Monte Carlo method is well-suited for calculating relativistic bound state energies and wavefunctions in scalar field theory, but the dominant error comes from higher-order corrections in the stochastic series expansion rather than from Monte Carlo statistics or ultraviolet cutoff dependence.

## Method
The authors studied relativistic two-body and three-body bound states in (φ⁶-φ⁴)₁₊₁ theory using a two-step computational scheme. First, they performed quasi-sparse eigenvector (QSE) diagonalization of the Hamiltonian in a Fock basis with momentum cutoff Λ=4μ, keeping 400 basis vectors per iteration and selecting 50 new vectors each iteration. Second, they applied first-order stochastic error correction via a series expansion method to sample contributions from basis states not included in the QSE subspace.

## Key numbers
- Box length L = 2.5π μ⁻¹, momentum cutoff N_max = 10 corresponding to Λ = 4μ
- For λ/μ² = 0.1: m₁/μ = 0.995(1), m₂/m₁ = 1.970(2), m₃/m₁ = 2.931(2)
- For λ/μ² = 0.6: m₁/μ = 0.61(4), m₂/m₁ = 1.71(10), m₃/m₁ = 2.62(15)
- Two-particle projection norm N₂ = 0.9977(1) at λ/μ² = 0.1, decreasing to N₂ = 0.79(2) at λ/μ² = 0.6
- Three-particle projection norm N₃ = 0.9966(2) at λ/μ² = 0.1, decreasing to N₃ = 0.66(4) at λ/μ² = 0.6
- First-order phase transition near λ = 0.73 μ², where ⟨φ⟩ = 1.02(2)
- Non-relativistic infinite-L binding energy for two-body: E = -9λ²/(4μ³)
- Non-relativistic infinite-L binding energy for three-body: E = -9λ²/μ³

## Context
This work extends the diagonalization/Monte Carlo method previously introduced by the authors (Lee, 2000) to relativistic bound states, providing a numerically stable alternative to standard lattice Monte Carlo approaches that struggle with states near continuum thresholds. The results are compared with non-relativistic Schrödinger approximations, the lattice diagonalization study of Barnes (BD), Gaussian effective potential results from Darewych and Stevenson (GEP), and truncated Fock space approximations. The method enables detailed wavefunction calculations and can be extended to study competition between two-body and three-body forces, with relevance to triton systems as noted by Bedaque (2000).
