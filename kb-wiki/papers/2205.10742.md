# Effects of pairing gap and band gap on superfluid density in the inner crust of neutron stars

> Calculations of the superfluid density in the inner crust of neutron stars by different approaches are in strong disagreement, which causes a debate on the accountability of pulsar glitches based on superfluidity. Taking a simple unified model, we study the dependence on approximation of the superfluid density in a periodic potential. In comparison with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HF-Bogoliubov) theory which treats the effects of the band gap and the pairing gap on equal footing, we examine the HF-BCS-type approximation in which the former is incorporated in priority, and another approximation in which the latter is incorporated in priority. We find that, when the pairing gap and the band gap are comparable as in the inner crust of neutron stars, they need to be treated on equal footing, and the HF-BCS approximation can considerably underestimate the superfluid density even if the pairing gap is much smaller than the Fermi energy. Our result suggests that the validity of the HF-BCS approximation for evaluating the superfluid density in neutron star crusts is questionable.

## Key claim

When the pairing gap and the band gap are comparable, as they are in the inner crust of neutron stars, the Hartree-Fock-BCS (HF-BCS) approximation can considerably underestimate the superfluid density even if the pairing gap is much smaller than the Fermi energy; the two gaps must be treated on equal footing via the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HF-Bogoliubov) theory.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional minimal model of a fermionic superfluid under a sinusoidal periodic potential, restricted to three Bloch bands. They compared three methods: (i) directly solving the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations with the band mixing strength V and the pairing gap Δ on equal footing (the HF-Bogoliubov theory); (ii) first incorporating only the band mixing by V, then including the pairing gap Δ (the HF-BCS approximation); and (iii) first incorporating only the pairing gap Δ, then including the band mixing by V. The superfluid density was computed as the second derivative of the energy density with respect to the superfluid momentum Q at Q=0.

## Key numbers

* At V/Δ = 1, Δ/E_F = 0.01, and K/k_F = 2, the superfluid density ratios are: n^s_BdG/n = 0.27 (Method i, HF-Bogoliubov), n^s_VΔ/n = 0.089 (Method ii, HF-BCS), and n^s_ΔV/n = 0.50 (Method iii).
* For Δ/E_F = 0.01, the ratio n^s_VΔ/n^s_BdG reaches a minimum of 0.29 at V/Δ = 1.6, and n^s_ΔV/n^s_BdG reaches a maximum of 8.6 at V/Δ = 4.0.
* The pairing gap of superfluid neutrons in the inner crust is typically of order 1% of the neutron Fermi energy (Δ/E_F ~ 0.01).
* The magnitude of the lattice potential Fourier component for the primitive reciprocal lattice vector is around 1–2 MeV, and the ¹S₀ pairing gap in the inner crust density region is typically ~1 MeV.
* The Fermi energy of dripped neutrons in the inner crust is about 15 MeV, and the distance between neighboring nuclei is 40–50 fm, giving K/k_F ~ 10⁻¹.

## Context

This work addresses a serious disagreement between Chamel (2012), who used the HF approximation and found a superfluid density about one tenth of the believed value, and Watanabe (2017), who used the HF-Bogoliubov theory and found a superfluid density around 70 percent of the believed value. Chamel (2018) later reported that the superfluid density remained low even when pairing was included via the BCS approximation. The present study uses a simple minimal model to demonstrate that the HF-BCS approximation (Method ii) systematically underestimates the superfluid density when the pairing gap and band gap are comparable, explaining the discrepancy. The results imply that the equal-footing HF-Bogoliubov treatment is essential for reliable calculations of the superfluid density in neutron star crusts, and that the HF-BCS approximation used by Chamel (2018) is questionable for this application.
