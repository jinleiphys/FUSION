# Enhanced Tensor-Force Contribution in Collision Dynamics

> The tensor and spin-orbit forces contribute essentially to the formation of the spin mean field, and give rise to the same dynamical effect, namely spin polarization. In this paper, based on time-dependent density functional calculations, we show that the tensor force, which usually acts like a small correction to the spin-orbit force, becomes more important in heavy-ion reactions and the effect increases with the mass of the system.

## Key claim

The tensor force, which usually acts as a small correction to the spin-orbit force, becomes significantly more important in heavy-ion reactions, and its contribution increases with the mass of the colliding system; however, the tensor force contribution remains opposite in sign and smaller in magnitude than the spin-orbit force contribution throughout the collision.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional time-dependent density functional calculations (TDDFT) with explicitly implemented tensor force using the Skyrme-force parameter set SV-tls for the tensor part combined with SkM* or SLy4d for the remainder. They simulated central collisions between identical N=Z nuclei (16O+16O, 40Ca+40Ca, and 56Ni+56Ni) at a relative velocity of 10% of the speed of light, and compared the x-components of the form factors of the spin mean field from tensor and spin-orbit forces.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca+40Ca at time = 6.0 × 10⁻²² s, the tensor-force contribution amounts to less than 10 percent of the spin-orbit force contribution.
- The enhancement of the tensor-force contribution for 40Ca+40Ca (ratio of tensor/spin-orbit at t=6.5×10⁻²² s to that at t=1.5×10⁻²² s) is 5.94 for protons and 6.23 for neutrons using SkM*+SV-tls, and 6.54 for protons and 7.11 for neutrons using SLy4d+SV-tls.
- For the heaviest system (56Ni+56Ni) with SkM*+SV-tls, the tensor force contribution reaches 20 percent of the spin-orbit contribution.
- The growth of spin polarization (normalized to 16O+16O) decreases with mass: for SkM*+SV-tls, values are 1.000 (16O+16O), 0.396 (40Ca+40Ca), and 0.260 (56Ni+56Ni); for SLy4d+SV-tls, values are 1.000 (16O+16O), 0.571 (40Ca+40Ca), and 0.085 (56Ni+56Ni).
- The tensor force parameters used are α = 71.102 MeV fm⁻⁵ and β = 35.142 MeV fm⁻⁵ for SV-tls.

## Context

This work extends earlier time-dependent Skyrme energy density functional calculations by Maruhn (2006) that demonstrated spontaneous spin polarization in central collisions arises from the time-odd part of the spin-orbit force. The study builds on recent investigations of the tensor force in nuclear structure by Otsuka (2005, 2006, 2010), Brown, Lesinski, Colo, and Bender, and applies these concepts to collision dynamics for the first time. The results enable future studies of the tensor force role in heavy-ion reactions involving exotic nuclei, where the different contributions from neutron and proton spin-orbit densities may become significant.
