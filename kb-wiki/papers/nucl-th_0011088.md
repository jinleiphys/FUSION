# Confinement in the Big Bang and Deconfinement in the Little Bangs at CERN-SPS

> The evolution of strongly interacting matter during the cosmological confinement transition is reviewed. Despite of many proposed relics no specific signal from the rearrangement of quarks and gluons into hadrons has been identified by observations. In contrast to this, several observables in heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS energies point to the creation of a matter state near or slightly above deconfinement. We focus here on the analysis of dileptons and direct photons. Similarities and differences of the Big Bang and the Little Bang confinement dynamics are elaborated.

## Key claim

The analysis of dilepton and photon spectra from CERN-SPS heavy-ion collisions points to a matter state with temperatures near or slightly above the deconfinement temperature of approximately 170 MeV, but the authors acknowledge that the effect of finite baryon density and the assumption of thermalization need further consideration.

## Method

The authors used relativistic hydrodynamics to model the evolution of strongly interacting matter in both cosmological (Big Bang) and heavy-ion collision (Little Bang) contexts. For the heavy-ion data, they parameterized thermal dilepton and photon emission using Eqs. (2) and (3), which replace the time evolution of fireball volume and temperature with averages, and adjusted the two parameters \(T_{\rm eff}\) and \(N_{\rm eff}\) to fit experimental spectra from the CERES, NA50, WA98, NA38, HELIOS/3, and WA80 collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement temperature \(T_c\) is in the order of 170 MeV or slightly larger, based on QCD lattice calculations.
- The dynamical time scale for the Big Bang at \(T \sim 200\) MeV is approximately \(10^{19}\) fm/c.
- The dynamical time scale for the Little Bang (heavy-ion collisions) is approximately 1 fm/c.
- The thermal contribution to dilepton and photon spectra is characterized by \(T_{\rm eff} = 170\) MeV and \(N_{\rm eff} = 3.3 \times 10^4\) fm\(^4\).
- The unique outcome of the studies is \(T_{\rm eff} = 160 \cdots 170\) MeV.
- With a dynamical scenario, the maximum temperature found is approximately 200 MeV or slightly above.
- For a first-order cosmic confinement transition described by a bag model, the transition begins at world age \(t_1 \sim 6\) \(\mu\)sec and ends at \(t_2 \sim 12\) \(\mu\)sec.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of electromagnetic signals from heavy-ion collisions at CERN-SPS, building on prior lattice QCD results from the Bielefeld group (Karsch, 1999) and quasi-particle models (Peshier, 1996). The paper contrasts the cosmological confinement transition, which left no identifiable relics despite many proposed candidates (e.g., isothermal baryon fluctuations, strangelets, black holes), with the Little Bangs at CERN-SPS where dilepton and photon data indicate temperatures near deconfinement. This enables future work at RHIC and LHC aimed at achieving matter states with temperatures clearly above \(T_c\).
