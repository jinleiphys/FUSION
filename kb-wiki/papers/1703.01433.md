# Quantum Nuclear Pasta and Nuclear Symmetry Energy

> Complex and exotic nuclear geometries are expected to appear naturally in dense nuclear matter found in the crust of neutron stars and supernovae environment collectively referred to as nuclear pasta. The pasta geometries depend on the average baryon density, proton fraction and temperature and are critically important in the determination of many transport properties of matter in supernovae and the crust of neutron stars. Using a set of self-consistent microscopic nuclear energy density functionals we present the first results of large scale quantum simulations of pasta phases at baryon densities $0.03 \leq ρ\leq 0.10$ fm$^{-3}$, proton fractions $0.05 \leq Y_p \leq 0.40$, and zero temperature. The full quantum simulations, in particular, allow us to thoroughly investigate the role and impact of the nuclear symmetry energy on pasta configurations. We use the Sky3D code that solves the Skyrme Hartree-Fock equations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid. For the nuclear interaction we use the state of the art UNEDF1 parametrization, which was introduced to study largely deformed nuclei, hence is suitable for studies of the nuclear pasta. Density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy is simulated by tuning two purely isovector observables that are insensitive to the current available experimental data. We find that a minimum total number of nucleons $A=2000$ is necessary to prevent the results from containing spurious shell effects and to minimize finite size effects. We find that a variety of nuclear pasta geometries are present in the neutron star crust and the result strongly depends on the nuclear symmetry energy. The impact of the nuclear symmetry energy is less pronounced as the proton fractions increase. Quantum nuclear pasta calculations at $T=0$ MeV are shown to get easily trapped in meta-stable states, and possible remedies to avoid meta-stable solutions are discussed.

# Quantum Nuclear Pasta and Nuclear Symmetry Energy

## Key claim

Using large-scale quantum Skyrme Hartree-Fock simulations with no pre-assumed pasta geometries, the authors demonstrate that nuclear pasta configurations in neutron star crusts depend strongly on the nuclear symmetry energy, but this dependence becomes less pronounced at higher proton fractions; however, the simulations at zero temperature are easily trapped in meta-stable states, requiring careful convergence criteria and sufficiently large simulation volumes to minimize finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors used the Sky3D code to solve static Skyrme Hartree-Fock equations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with periodic boundary conditions and a homogeneous negative electron background (jellium approximation). They employed the UNEDF1 parametrization of the Skyrme energy density functional and generated a second interaction with a stiff symmetry energy (L=80 MeV) by tuning two purely isovector parameters (x0 and x3), while keeping isoscalar properties nearly identical. Simulations were performed at zero temperature for baryon densities between 0.03 and 0.10 fm^-3 and proton fractions between 0.05 and 0.40, with a minimum of 2000 nucleons required to avoid spurious shell effects and minimize finite-size effects.

## Key numbers

- Minimum total number of nucleons required: A=2000, to prevent spurious shell effects and minimize finite-size effects
- Baryon density range studied: 0.03 ≤ ρ ≤ 0.10 fm^-3
- Proton fraction range studied: 0.05 ≤ Y_p ≤ 0.40
- Two symmetry energy slopes compared: L=40 MeV (soft, original UNEDF1) and L=80 MeV (stiff, modified UNEDF1)
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J=28.99 MeV for UNEDF1 (L=40 MeV) and J=32.87 MeV for UNEDF1* (L=80 MeV)
- Grid spacing up to Δx=1.50 fm maintains binding energy accuracy within 0.06% for A=800 nucleon systems at ρ=0.0512 fm^-3 and Y_p=0.4
- Convergence criterion: Δε_tot = ε_tot^(m) - ε_tot^(n) < -10^-4 MeV, where m = n + 10,000 iterations
- Binding energy per nucleon for A=2000 system at ρ=0.05 fm^-3 and Y_p=0.40: -11.8520 MeV
- Maximum local total density for A=2000 system: 0.1529 fm^-3; maximum local proton density: 0.0645 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous quantum simulations of nuclear pasta by Newton (2009), Schuetrumpf (2012), Pais (2012), and Sagert (2015), which were limited to smaller simulation volumes or assumed spatial symmetries, and complements classical molecular dynamics simulations by Horowitz (2004, 2005, 2014) and Schneider (2013, 2014) that used larger volumes but simpler nuclear interactions. The study confirms the existence of the nuclear waffle phase (perforated plates) previously identified by Schneider (2014) and Schuetrumpf (2012), and demonstrates that the density dependence of the symmetry energy, particularly the slope parameter L, critically determines which pasta geometries appear, consistent with the earlier Thomas-Fermi predictions by Oyamatsu and Iida (2006) that pasta formation may not be universal and depends on L being less than approximately 100 MeV. The results enable future finite-temperature quantum simulations and provide benchmarks for constraining the symmetry energy through neutron star observables.
