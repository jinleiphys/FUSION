# Transition form factors: $γ^\ast + p \to Δ(1232)$, $Δ(1600)$

> Electroproduction form factors describing the $γ^\ast p \to Δ^+(1232), Δ^+(1600)$ transitions are computed using a fully-dynamical diquark-quark approximation to the Poincaré-covariant three-body bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory. In this approach, the $Δ(1600)$ is an analogue of the Roper resonance in the nucleon sector, appearing as the simplest radial excitation of the $Δ(1232)$. Precise measurements of the $γ^\ast p \to Δ^+(1232)$ transition already exist on $0 \leq Q^2 \lesssim 8\,$GeV$^2$ and the calculated results compare favourably with the data outside the meson-cloud domain. The predictions for the $γ^\ast p \to Δ^+(1600)$ magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole transition form factors are consistent with the empirical values at the real photon point, and extend to $Q^2 \approx 6 m_p^2$, enabling a meaningful direct comparison with experiment once analysis of existing data is completed. In both cases, the electric quadrupole form factor is particularly sensitive to deformation of the $Δ$-baryons. Interestingly, whilst the $γ^\ast p \to Δ^+(1232)$ transition form factors are larger in magnitude than those for $γ^\ast p \to Δ^+(1600)$ in some neighbourhood of the real photon point, this ordering is reversed on $Q^2 \gtrsim 2 m_p^2$, suggesting that the $γ^\ast p \to Δ^+(1600)$ transition is more localised in configuration space.

## Key claim

The paper computes transition form factors for both the γ*p → Δ(1232) and γ*p → Δ(1600) reactions using a diquark-quark approximation, finding that while the Δ(1232) transition form factors are larger near the real photon point, the ordering reverses for Q² ≳ 2 m_p², suggesting the Δ(1600) transition is more localized in configuration space. The predictions for the Δ(1600) are consistent with empirical values only at the real photon point, and the authors note that meson-baryon final-state interactions must be incorporated to achieve full agreement with data at low Q².

## Method

The authors solve a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation using a fully-dynamical diquark-quark approximation to the three-body bound-state problem, employing dressed-quark and diquark propagators with diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The transition current is computed via six diagrams involving the Faddeev amplitudes of the nucleon and Δ-baryons, and the magnetic dipole (G_M*), electric quadrupole (G_E*), and Coulomb quadrupole (G_C*) form factors are extracted using projection operations. The Δ(1600) is treated as the first radial excitation of the Δ(1232), analogous to the Roper resonance in the nucleon sector.

## Key numbers

- Computed masses of the dressed-quark cores: m_p = 1.19 GeV, m_Δ(1232) = 1.35 GeV, m_Δ(1600) = 1.79 GeV (empirical pole locations are 0.939, 1.21 - i0.05, and 1.51 - i0.14 GeV respectively)
- For γ*p → Δ(1232): magnetic dipole form factor G_M* = 1.93 at Q²=0 (interpolation coefficient a₀^M); the result agrees with data for Q² ≳ 0.5 m_p²
- For γ*p → Δ(1600): magnetic dipole form factor G_M* = 0.32 at Q²=0 (interpolation coefficient a₀^M); electric quadrupole G_E* = -0.022 at Q²=0
- Δ(1600) elastic electric form factor has a zero at Q² ≈ 1.8 m_p²; Δ(1232) elastic electric form factor zero lies at Q² ≈ 2.7 m_p²
- Static properties: Δ(1232) charge radius r_E = 1.23 r_p, magnetic dipole moment G_M1(0) = 2.86; Δ(1600) r_E = 1.68 r_p, G_M1(0) = 1.50 (r_p = 0.61 fm)
- The γ*p → Δ(1232) data exist on 0 ≤ Q² ≲ 8 GeV²; predictions for Δ(1600) extend to Q² ≈ 6 m_p²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of baryon structure using the diquark-quark Faddeev equation approach (Segovia 2014, Chen 2019), which successfully described octet and decuplet baryons and their positive-parity excitations. The calculation provides a unified treatment of the N→Δ(1232) and N→Δ(1600) transitions, with the Δ(1600) identified as the radial excitation of the Δ(1232) analogous to the Roper resonance in the nucleon sector. The predictions enable direct comparison with existing CLAS data on Δ(1600) electroproduction (Trivedi 2018, Burkert 2019) once analysis is completed, and the results outside the meson-cloud domain (Q² ≳ 0.5 m_p²) can test the dressed-quark core contributions before meson-baryon final-state interactions are included.
