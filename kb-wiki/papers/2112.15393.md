# Nuclear states projected from a pair condensate

> Atomic nuclei exhibit deformation, pairing correlations, and rotational symmetries. To meet these competing demands in a computationally tractable formalism, we revisit the use of general pair condensates with good particle number as a trial wave function for even-even nuclei. After minimizing the energy of the condensate, we project out states with good angular momentum with a fast projection technique, allowing for general triaxial deformations. To show applicability, we present example calculations from pair condensates in several model spaces, and compare against projected Hartree-Fock and full configuration-interaction shell model calculations. This approach successfully generates spherical, vibrational and rotational spectra, demonstrating potential for modeling medium- to heavy-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The pair condensate ansatz, after energy minimization and subsequent angular momentum projection (projection after variation, or PVPC), can generate spherical, vibrational, and rotational spectra for even-even nuclei across multiple valence spaces, with ground state energies typically within 1–2 MeV of full configuration-interaction shell model results for rotational nuclei and within 0.5 MeV for semi-magic nuclei, though the approach is limited to a single reference condensate and may require additional reference states for higher-lying or yrare bands.

## Method

The authors construct a trial wave function as a direct product of proton and neutron pair condensates (antisymmetrized geminal power states) with good particle number, then minimize the energy expectation value using conjugate gradient methods (GNU Scientific Library) with up to 992 free parameters for the pair structure coefficients in the largest model space. After variation, they project out states of good angular momentum using the linear algebraic projection (LAP) technique, which is reported to be 10 times faster than standard quadrature, and also perform parity projection for the heaviest nuclei. The projected states are then used to solve the Hill-Wheeler equation for energies and to compute electromagnetic transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- In the $1s0d$, $1p0f$, and $2s1d0g_{7/2}0h_{11/2}$ major shells, the numbers of free parameters in variation of one pair condensate are 132, 380, and 992, respectively.
- For $^{132}$Dy in the $2s1d0g_{7/2}0h_{11/2}$ shell, the conjugate gradient descent converges after approximately 200 steps with 992 free parameters.
- For most $1s0d$-shell even-even nuclei, the projected Hartree-Fock (PHF) ground state energy is about 2 MeV higher than the exact shell model result; for three nuclei (e.g., $^{32}$S) the discrepancy exceeds 5 MeV.
- The PVPC ground state energy discrepancy for semi-magic nuclei in the $1s0d$ shell is typically less than 0.5 MeV; for rotational nuclei it is typically 1–2 MeV.
- For $^{26}$Si, the PVPC $B(E2, 4^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ value is about 60.31 $e^2$fm$^4$, compared to the shell model value of 11.29 $e^2$fm$^4$ (a factor of approximately 5.3 larger).
- The LAP technique is reported to be more than 10 times faster than quadrature for angular momentum projection, with the same accuracy for low-lying states.

## Context

This work extends the use of pair condensates (antisymmetrized geminal power wave functions) from quantum chemistry into nuclear structure, building on prior formulations for general $M$-scheme nucleon-pair bases by Lei (2021) and on the linear algebraic projection technique developed by Johnson (2017, 2019). It contrasts with the nucleon-pair approximation (NPA), which is limited to about 8 valence pairs due to computational burden, and with the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), which requires nontrivial mapping of fermion pairs to bosons. The PVPC approach enables calculations in the $2s1d0g_{7/2}0h_{11/2}$ valence space (nuclei with $N,Z$ between 50 and 82) where full configuration-interaction shell model calculations become intractable, and it can be extended in the future by including multiple reference condensates as in the generator coordinate method.
