# Exploring the neutron skin by hyperon-antihyperon production in antiproton-nucleus interactions

> In this work we propose a new method to measure the evolution of the neutron skin thickness between different isotopes. We consider antiproton-nucleus interactions close to the production threshold of $Λ\overlineΛ$ and $Σ\overlineΛ$ pairs. At low energies, $Λ\overlineΛ$ pairs are produced in p + p collisions, while $Σ\overlineΛ$ pairs can only be produced in p + n interactions. Measuring these cross sections provides information on the neutron skin thickness

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the double ratio of $\Sigma^-\overline{\Lambda}$ to $\Lambda\overline{\Lambda}$ production yields in antiproton-nucleus interactions for two different isotopes can provide a measure of the difference in neutron skin thickness between those isotopes, but the method relies on a simplified geometrical picture and the assumption that the proton distribution remains identical between isotopes, which the authors acknowledge is not realistic.

## Method

The authors derive an analytical expression for the double ratio DR (Eq. 8) based on a geometrical model where antiproton absorption in an additional neutron layer modifies production probabilities. They then perform microscopic transport simulations using the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) model for antiproton-xenon interactions at an incident momentum of 2.4 GeV/c, generating about 28 million events per isotope for the mass range A=129 to A=136.

## Key numbers

- Incident antiproton momentum: 2.4 GeV/c
- Xenon isotope mass range studied: A=129 to A=136
- About 28 million events generated per isotope in the GiBUU simulations
- Computational time: about one day on the MOGON2 high performance computing cluster at the University of Mainz

## Context

This work extends methods for measuring neutron skin thickness, which is traditionally studied in neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei such as $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{208}$Pb (as noted in the introduction). The proposed method aims to measure the evolution of neutron skin thickness along isotope chains, which the authors argue provides important information for understanding nuclear structure across the nuclear chart. The GiBUU transport model, which describes antiproton-nucleus interactions in the FAIR energy range (Buss 2012), is used to test the analytical predictions. The authors plan to apply this method to $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca in future work.
