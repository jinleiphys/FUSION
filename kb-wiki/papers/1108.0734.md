# Shear-Viscosity to Entropy-Density Ratio from Giant Dipole Resonances in Hot Nuclei

> The Green-Kubo relation and fluctuation-dissipation theorem are employed to calculate the shear viscosity $η$ of a finite hot nucleus directly from the width and energy of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) of this nucleus. The ratio $η/s$ of shear viscosity $η$ to entropy density $s$ is extracted from the experimental systematics of the GDR in copper, tin and lead isotopes at finite temperature $T$. These empirical results are then compared with the predictions by several independent models, as well as with almost model-independent estimations. Based on these results, it is concluded that the ratio $η/s$ in medium and heavy nuclei decreases with increasing temperature $T$ to reach $(1.3 - 4)\times\hbar/(4πk_{B})$ at $T=$ 5 MeV.

## Key claim

The ratio η/s in medium and heavy nuclei decreases with increasing temperature T to reach (1.3–4) × ħ/(4πkB) at T = 5 MeV, but this conclusion depends on the choice of η(0) = 1u (with lower and upper bounds 0.6u and 1.2u) adopted from fitting ground-state GDR data, and the result is sensitive to which theoretical model for the GDR width temperature dependence is used.

## Method

The shear viscosity η(T) was calculated from the GDR width Γ(T) and energy EGDR(T) using the Green-Kubo relation and fluctuation-dissipation theorem, which yields η(T) = η(0) × [Γ(T)/Γ(0)] × [EGDR(0)² + (Γ(0)/2)²] / [EGDR(T)² + (Γ(T)/2)²]. The entropy density s(T) was obtained from the Fermi gas formula with a temperature-dependent level density parameter. The empirical η/s values were extracted using experimental GDR systematics for copper, tin, and lead isotopes, and compared with predictions from four models: the phonon damping model (PDM), the adiabatic model (AM), the phenomenological thermal shape fluctuation model (pTSFM), and the Fermi liquid drop model (FLDM).

## Key numbers

- η(0) = 1u ≈ 0.016 TP (terapoise) adopted as the central value, with lower bound 0.6u and upper bound 1.2u from fission data analysis
- η/s at T = 5 MeV reaches (1.3–4) × ħ/(4πkB), where ħ/(4πkB) ≈ 5.24 × 10⁻²³ MeV·s is the KSS bound
- For the FLDM, the constraint η(T)/η(0) ≤ 3.41 is required to avoid imaginary GDR widths, using Γ(0) ≈ 0.3 EGDR(0)
- The pTSFM parametrization for GDR width uses Γ(T,A) = Γ₀(A) + c(A) ln(1 + T/T₀) with c(A) = 6.45 − A/100 and T₀ = 1 MeV
- The FLDM uses Fermi energy εF = 40 MeV and nuclear density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with parameter α between 4.6 and 9.2

## Context

This work extends the earlier estimation by Auerbach and Shlomo (2010) who obtained η/s = (4–19) KSS units for heavy nuclei and (2.5–12.5) KSS units for light nuclei using the Fermi liquid drop model, but whose results used a temperature-independent level density parameter and predicted GDR widths that increase monotonically with T, contradicting experimental systematics showing width saturation above T ≈ 3–4 MeV. The present paper enables direct comparison of η/s in finite nuclei with the KSS bound from Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005), and provides empirical constraints for hydrodynamic descriptions of nuclear systems, particularly relevant to understanding the nearly perfect fluid behavior observed in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC.
