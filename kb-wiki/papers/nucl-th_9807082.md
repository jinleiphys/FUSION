# Soft-core hyperon-nucleon potentials

> A new Nijmegen soft-core OBE potential model is presented for the low-energy YN interactions. Besides the results for the fit to the scattering data, which largely defines the model, we also present some applications to hypernuclear systems using the G-matrix method. An important innovation with respect to the original soft-core potential is the assignment of the cut-off masses for the baryon-baryon-meson (BBM) vertices in accordance with broken SU(3)$_F$, which serves to connect the NN and the YN channels. As a novel feature, we allow for medium strong breaking of the coupling constants, using the $^3P_0$ model with a Gell-Mann--Okubo hypercharge breaking for the BBM coupling. We present six hyperon-nucleon potentials which describe the available YN cross section data equally well, but which exhibit some differences on a more detailed level. The differences are constructed such that the models encompass a range of scattering lengths in the $ΣN$ and $ΛN$ channels. For the scalar-meson mixing angle we obtained values $θ_S=37$ to 40 degrees, which points to almost ideal mixing angles for the scalar $q\bar{q}$ states. The G-matrix results indicate that the remarkably different spin-spin terms of the six potentials appear specifically in the energy spectra of $Λ$ hypernuclei.

# Soft-core hyperon-nucleon potentials

## Key claim

The paper presents six new Nijmegen soft-core one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential models (NSC97) for low-energy hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions that all describe the available YN cross section data equally well (with χ²/Ndata ≈ 0.55), but which exhibit differences in their spin-spin terms that appear specifically in the energy spectra of Λ hypernuclei. The models encompass a range of scattering lengths in the ΣN and ΛN channels, allowing future hypernuclear studies to select the successful spin-spin interaction.

## Method

The potentials are generated by exchange of nonets of pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar mesons, plus J=0 contributions from tensor f₂, f'₂, a₂ and pomeron Regge trajectories, regularized with Gaussian form factors. The authors assign cut-off masses for baryon-baryon-meson (BBM) vertices according to broken SU(3)F symmetry (rather than per baryon-baryon SU(3)F-irrep as in earlier models), and introduce medium-strong breaking of coupling constants using the ³P₀ model with Gell-Mann–Okubo hypercharge breaking. The multichannel Schrödinger equation is solved on the physical particle basis, including Coulomb interaction and charge-symmetry breaking from Λ-Σ⁰ mixing.

## Key numbers

- χ²/Ndata ≈ 0.55 for 35 YN data points across all six potentials
- Inelastic capture ratio at rest fitted to rR = 0.468 ± 0.010
- Scalar-meson mixing angle θS = 37° to 40°, pointing to almost ideal mixing for scalar qq̄ states
- Pseudoscalar F/(F+D) ratio αPV = 0.355, fixed from semileptonic weak decays
- Vector meson φ–ω mixing angle θV = 37.5°
- η–η' mixing angle θPV = −23.0° from Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula
- Λ-Σ⁰ mixing gives fΛΛπ = −0.0283 × fΛΣπ
- Threshold laboratory momenta: Λp → Σ⁺n at 633.4 MeV/c, Λp → Σ⁰p at 642.0 MeV/c; Λn → Σ⁰n at 641.7 MeV/c, Λn → Σ⁻p at 657.9 MeV/c; average ΛN → ΣN threshold at 643.8 MeV/c
- Effective kaon mass in ΣN → NΣ drops from 495.8 MeV/c² to 425.8 MeV/c² in the static approximation

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core OBE models from earlier papers by Nagels et al. (1978) and Maessen et al. (1989), which provided simultaneous descriptions of NN and YN scattering but required corrections to the spin-spin interaction in ΛN channels for hypernuclear applications. The new NSC97 models improve upon these predecessors by assigning cut-off parameters per BBM vertex (allowing extension to ΛΛ and ΞN channels without extra free parameters) and by treating the magnetic F/(F+D) ratio αV^m as a free input to encompass a range of scattering lengths. The G-matrix results enable future selection of the correct spin-spin interaction by comparing model predictions with hypernuclear energy spectra.
