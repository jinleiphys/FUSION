# Spectra and Electromagnetic Properties of the Neutron-Excess Nucleus $^{132}$In

> Spectra of levels and transition rates are calculated for the odd--odd particle--hole neutron excess nucleus $^{132}$In in the framework of the RPA approach.

## Key claim

The paper presents RPA calculations of level spectra and electromagnetic transition rates for the odd-odd particle-hole neutron-excess nucleus 132In, finding that most low-lying states are rather pure and can be characterized by the quantum numbers of a leading configuration, with the lowest levels belonging to the configuration {anti-pi1g9/2, nu2f7/2} and showing good agreement with experimental data from Yungclaus (2016).

## Method

The author solved the RPA equations for the particle-hole Green function to obtain level spectra and amplitudes for 132In, using experimental single-particle energies from neighboring nuclei and two-body interactions with two parameter sets (RPA1 and RPA2). Electromagnetic transition rates were calculated using effective multipole operators with empirically determined effective charges and gyromagnetic ratios, with results tabulated for levels below 2 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of 132In is 7-1 (negative parity) with magnetic moment 4.78 mu_N and quadrupole moment 13.3 |e|·fm^2 (RPA2 calculation)
- Lowest excited state is 5-1 at 0.040 MeV (RPA1) or 0.041 MeV (RPA2), with magnetic moment 4.04 mu_N and quadrupole moment 15.3 |e|·fm^2
- 4-1 level at 0.105 MeV (RPA1) or 0.113 MeV (RPA2) with magnetic moment 3.84 mu_N and quadrupole moment 16.7 |e|·fm^2
- 3-1 level at 0.171 MeV (RPA1) or 0.167 MeV (RPA2) with magnetic moment 3.68 mu_N and quadrupole moment 15.3 |e|·fm^2
- For the lowest negative-parity multiplet {anti-pi1g9/2, nu2f7/2}, E2 transitions with Delta J=1 are enhanced (e.g., 6-1 to 5-1: B(E2)=189 e^2·fm^4) while Delta J=2 transitions are retarded (e.g., 7-1 to 5-1: B(E2)=0.243 e^2·fm^4)
- Potential parameters used: V0=-51.55 MeV, V_ls=32.4 MeV·fm^2, a(p)=0.63 fm, a(n)=0.66 fm, beta=1.31, beta_ls=-0.6, r0=1.27 fm, rc=1.25 fm
- Effective charges: e^p_lambda=2(eff)=1.6|e|, e^n_lambda=2(eff)=0.9|e|

## Context

This work extends the author's previous RPA studies of long chains of isotones with N=82 and N=50 (Erokhina, 2001; Isakov, 2016) and isotopes with Z=28 and Z=50 (Isakov, 2010; Isakov, 2013) to the specific odd-odd particle-hole nucleus 132In, which is close to doubly-magical 132Sn. The calculations were motivated by recent experimental data from Yungclaus (2016) on low-lying levels in 132In, and the results show opposite behavior for E2 transitions compared to the lowest proton-neutron multiplet in 134Sb studied by Isakov (2012), where Delta J=1 transitions were retarded and Delta J=2 transitions were enhanced. The tabulated B(E2) and B(M1) values together with the signs of reduced matrix element ratios enable calculation of the mixing parameter delta for angular correlation experiments.
