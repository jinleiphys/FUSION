# Scalar-glueball-mediated scale-anomaly dominance of the confining pressure of the pion in holographic QCD

> In this work, we analyze the energy density and the stress distribution inside the pion derived from gravitational form factors in top-down holographic QCD. In particular, we show that the dominance of the scale anomaly in the confining pressure, previously observed in the instant form for the nucleon, also holds for the pion in the light-front form. Furthermore, we find that in large-$N_c$ QCD described by this approach, the scalar glueball plays a mediating role in transmitting the confining pressure. These findings support the universal role of the scale anomaly in the stability of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the scale anomaly dominates the confining pressure inside the pion, with the scalar glueball acting as the mediator of this confining pressure in large-\(N_c\) QCD, extending the previously observed anomaly dominance from the nucleon (in instant form) to the pion (in light-front form).

## Method

The authors compute the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the pion in the chiral limit using the top-down holographic QCD model based on the D4–D8–\(\overline{\text{D8}}\) configuration (Sakai–Sugimoto model). They solve the linearized Einstein equations in the eleven-dimensional bulk spacetime sourced by the pion field, expand the metric fluctuations in terms of glueball eigenmodes (the \(\text{T}_4(2^{++})\), \(\text{T}_4(0^{++})\), and \(\text{S}_4(0^{++})\) modes), and extract the boundary EMT matrix elements via holographic renormalization. The two-dimensional transverse energy and stress distributions on constant light-front time hypersurfaces are then obtained by Fourier transforming the GFFs, and the pressure is decomposed into trace-anomaly and traceless parts.

## Key numbers

- The mass radius of the pion is \(\langle x_\perp^2 \rangle_{\text{mass}} = 4 \left. dA(t)/dt \right|_{t=0}\), defined from the two-dimensional transverse energy distribution.
- The mechanical radius is \(\langle x_\perp^2 \rangle_{\text{mech}} = 4D(0) / \int_{-\infty}^{0} dt D(t)\), defined from the two-dimensional transverse stress distribution.
- The form factor \(A(t)\) is entirely saturated by the tensor glueball (\(\text{T}_4(2^{++})\)) contribution, while \(D(t)\) receives contributions from all three glueball modes: \(\text{T}_4(2^{++})\), \(\text{T}_4(0^{++})\), and \(\text{S}_4(0^{++})\).
- The glueball masses \(m_n^{\text{T/S}}\) are the eigenvalues of the Sturm–Liouville problems in Eqs. (EVeq), with the eigenfunctions satisfying orthonormality relations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of the nucleon in instant form (Fujii, 2025) to the pion in light-front form, using the same top-down holographic QCD framework (Sakai–Sugimoto model, 2005) that successfully reproduced hadron spectra and form factors. The results support the universal role of the scale anomaly in hadron stability, as previously argued for the nucleon by the same group. The light-front formalism resolves ambiguities in defining spatial densities for relativistic systems like the pion, following the wave-packet approach of Miller (2018) and Jaffe (2020). The findings provide guidance for future theoretical and experimental studies of hadronic internal dynamics, including the ongoing experimental program at Jefferson Lab.
