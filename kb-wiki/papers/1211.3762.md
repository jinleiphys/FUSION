# Nuclear electric dipole moment of three-body system

> Nuclear electric dipole moments of $^{3}He$ and $^{3}H$ are calculated using Time Reversal Invariance Violating (TRIV) potentials based on the meson exchange theory, as well as the ones derived by using pionless and pionful effective field theories, with nuclear wave functions obtained by solving Faddeev equations in configuration space for the complete Hamiltonians comprising both TRIV and realistic strong interactions. The obtained results are compared with the previous calculations of $^{3}He$ EDM and with time reversal invariance violating effects in neutron-deuteron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear electric dipole moments (EDMs) of $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and $^{3}\mathrm{H}$ using three different classes of time-reversal invariance violating (TRIV) potentials (meson exchange, pionless EFT, and pionful EFT) with Faddeev wave functions, finding that results are systematically different from prior no-core shell model calculations by roughly a factor of 2 for isospin rank-0 and rank-1 operators and a factor of 5 for isospin rank-2 operators, suggesting a possible systematic error in one of the approaches.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations in configuration space for the three-nucleon bound states, including both realistic strong interaction potentials (AV18, Reid93, NijmII, INOY, and AV18 with Urbana IX three-nucleon force) and TRIV potentials. The TRIV potentials were constructed from meson exchange theory (with $\pi$, $\eta$, $\rho$, $\omega$ exchanges) and from pionless and pionful effective field theories, all sharing the same operator structure. The negative-parity components of the wave functions induced by the TRIV interaction were obtained by solving an inhomogeneous differential equation, and the nuclear EDM was computed as the sum of intrinsic nucleon EDM contributions and polarization contributions from the TRIV potential.

## Key numbers

- For the AV18 strong potential, the intrinsic nucleon EDM contribution to $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ EDM is $(-0.0468 d_p + 0.877 d_n)$ and to $^{3}\mathrm{H}$ EDM is $(0.877 d_p - 0.0480 d_n)$.
- For the INOY potential, the intrinsic nucleon EDM contribution to $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ is $(-0.0229 d_p + 0.927 d_n)$, showing a 5-6% difference from the AV18 result.
- The polarization contribution from operator 5 (isospin rank-1) with $\Lambda = m_\pi$ for AV18 is $19.4 \times 10^{-3}$ e-fm for $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and $19.6 \times 10^{-3}$ e-fm for $^{3}\mathrm{H}$.
- The polarization contribution from operator 1 with $\Lambda = m_\pi$ for AV18 is $(-5.32) \times 10^{-3}$ e-fm for $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and $5.28 \times 10^{-3}$ e-fm for $^{3}\mathrm{H}$.
- The deuteron polarization EDM calculated with AV18 gives $d_d^{(pol)} = 18.95 \times 10^{-2} \bar{g}_\pi^1 + 3.52 \times 10^{-3} \bar{g}_\eta^1 + 17.13 \times 10^{-4} \bar{g}_\rho^1 - 49.09 \times 10^{-4} \bar{g}_\omega^1$, in excellent agreement with Liu (2004).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $^{3}\mathrm{He}$ EDM by Stetcu (2008) and deVries (2011), who used no-core shell model and EFT approaches respectively, but finds systematic discrepancies with Stetcu (2008) by factors of roughly 2 for isospin rank-0 and rank-1 operators and factor 5 for rank-2 operators. The results enable improved interpretation of proposed storage-ring EDM measurements for light nuclei (Khriplovich 1998, Farley 2003, Semertzidis 2009, Lehrach 2012) and provide a benchmark for testing TRIV effects in neutron-deuteron scattering through spin-dependent cross section differences and neutron spin rotation.
