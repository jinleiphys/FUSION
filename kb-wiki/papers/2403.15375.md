# Might Normal Nuclear Matter be Quarkyonic?

> The possibility that nuclear matter might be Quarkyonic is considered. Quarkyonic matter is high baryon density matter that is confined but can be approximately thought of as a filled Fermi sea of quarks surrounded by a shell of nucleons. Here, nuclear matter is described by the IdylliQ sigma model for Quarkyonic matter, generalizing the non-interacting IdylliQ model [Y. Fujimoto et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 132, 112701 (2024) [arXiv:2306.04304]] to include interactions with a sigma meson and a pion. When such interactions are included, we find that isospin-symmetric nuclear matter binds, with acceptable values of the compressibility and other parameters for nuclear matter at saturation. The energy per nucleon and sound velocity of such matter is computed, and the isospin dependence is determined. Nuclear matter is formed at a density close to but slightly above the density at which Quarkyonic matter forms. Quarkyonic matter predicts a strong depletion of nucleons in normal nuclear matter at low momentum. Such a depletion for nucleon momenta $k \lesssim 120$ MeV is shown to be consistent with electron scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that normal nuclear matter at saturation density might actually be Quarkyonic matter, where a filled Fermi sea of quarks exists at low momentum with a depleted nucleon population, but the authors explicitly state this hypothesis is "very speculative and tentative" and acknowledge it "may very well be possible to rule out by further theoretical computation and comparison with experimental results."

## Method

The authors generalize the non-interacting IdylliQ model of Fujimoto et al. (2024) by including interactions via a sigma meson and pion within a mean-field framework, computing the kinetic, mean-field, and exchange energy contributions to the energy density. They determine the shell structure of nucleon momentum occupation numbers by imposing Pauli exclusion on both quarks and nucleons, then fix model parameters (coupling constant g_s and momentum scale Lambda) to reproduce the nuclear matter ground state binding energy of -16 MeV at saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}. They compare predictions for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter under different scenarios including fixed versus density-dependent nucleon mass, and test the resulting momentum distributions against quasi-elastic electron scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Saturation binding energy: E_bind = -16 MeV at n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} (used as input to fix model parameters)
- Incompressibility K_0 ranges from 180 MeV to 270 MeV across different scenarios, with preferred scenario giving K_0 = 219 MeV
- Symmetry energy S_0 at n_0 ranges from 29 MeV to 32 MeV when exchange terms are included
- Onset density for Quarkyonic matter: n_onset approximately 0.53 n_0 for fixed-mass calculation, and approximately 0.71 n_0 for density-dependent mass calculation
- Lower momentum of the nucleon shell (hole) at n_0: k_bu approximately 180 MeV for fixed mass, and approximately 124 MeV for density-dependent mass
- Nucleon occupation at low momentum: f_B(k) = 1/N_c^3 approximately 0.037 (for N_c=3)
- Fermi momentum of nucleons in Quarkyonic matter: k_F = 288 MeV, compared to regular Fermi gas value of approximately 260 MeV
- Depletion of nucleons predicted for momenta k less than 120-180 MeV, with electron scattering data consistent with depletion for k less than 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the IdylliQ model of Fujimoto et al. (2024) by adding sigma meson and pion interactions to create the IdylliQ Sigma model, building on the concept of Quarkyonic matter originally proposed by McLerran and collaborators (2007). The approach contrasts with earlier Quarkyonic matter implementations that assumed baryons co-exist on top of a filled quark Fermi sphere, and with the Walecka mean field model of Serot (1984) which uses vector interactions for repulsion rather than the Fermi exclusion principle among quarks. The paper suggests that Quarkyonic matter naturally explains the maximum in the density dependence of the speed of sound extracted from neutron star phenomenology, and that the predicted depletion of low-momentum nucleons can be tested against quasi-elastic electron scattering data from experiments by Sick (1970), Day (1987, 1989), and Moniz (1969, 1971).
