# One-rank interaction kernel of the two-nucleon system for medium and high energies

> A new version of the separable kernel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter approach is presented. The phase shifts are fitted to recent experimental data for singlet and uncoupled triplet partial waves of the neutron-proton scattering with total angular momenta J=0,1. The results are compared with other model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new separable kernel for the nucleon-nucleon interaction within the Bethe-Salpeter approach that fits phase shifts up to 1.2 GeV for most partial waves and up to 3 GeV for the \(^3P_0^+\) channel, but the abstract omits that the \(^1S_0^+\) and \(^3P_1^+\) states require a higher-rank kernel to reach the full 3 GeV range.

## Method

The authors construct one-rank separable kernels for the Bethe-Salpeter equation using two relativistic generalizations of Yamaguchi form factors: modified Yamaguchi (MY) and modified extended Yamaguchi (MEY) functions. They fit the kernel parameters to experimental neutron-proton phase shifts from the SAID program for singlet and uncoupled triplet partial waves with total angular momenta \(J=0,1\) (\(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1\), \(^1P_1\), \(^3P_0\), \(^3P_1\)), using a \(\chi^2\) minimization that includes low-energy scattering parameters for \(S\) waves. The phase shifts are computed from the on-mass-shell \(T\) matrix via the relation \(\cot\delta_l(s) = -(\lambda_l(s)^{-1} + \operatorname{Re} h_l(s)) / \operatorname{Im} h_l(s)\).

## Key numbers

* The MY and MEY kernels describe phase shifts up to a maximal laboratory kinetic energy \(T_{\rm Lab}^{\rm max} \sim 1\) GeV for all states except \(^3P_0\), where the MEY case reaches \(T_{\rm Lab}^{\rm max} \sim 3\) GeV.
* For the \(^3P_1^+\) channel, the MY, CD-Bonn, and MEY calculations explain experimental data up to \(T_{\rm Lab} = 0.55\), 0.6, and 1.2 GeV, respectively.
* For the \(^1S_0^+\) channel, MY and MEY calculations agree with experimental data up to \(T_{\rm Lab} < 1.2\) GeV, while CD-Bonn agrees only up to \(T_{\rm Lab} < 0.6\) GeV.
* For the \(^3S_1^+\) wave, MY, MEY, and SP07 calculations explain experimental data up to \(T_{\rm Lab} = 1.1\) GeV, while CD-Bonn agrees up to 0.6 GeV.
* The \(^3P_0^+\) channel with MEY functions gives good agreement with experimental data in a wide range up to \(T_{\rm Lab} < 3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the separable kernel approach of Manabe (2000), which described \(NN\) phase shifts up to 0.6-0.7 GeV, to higher energies by using modified Yamaguchi form factors that avoid nonintegrable singularities. The results are compared with the nonrelativistic CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt, 2001) and the SP07 solution (SAID program), showing that the MEY functions provide competitive descriptions up to 1.2 GeV for most channels. The kernel is intended for use in relativistic calculations of reactions with light nuclei at high momentum transfer, such as the \(d(e,e'p)n\) reaction.
