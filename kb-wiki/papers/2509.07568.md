# $K^*(892)$ Resonance Suppression in Ar+Sc Collisions at SPS Energies

> We investigate the production and suppression of short-lived $K^*(892)$ resonances in p+p and Ar+Sc collisions at CERN-SPS energies ($\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = $ 8.8, 11.9, and 16.8~GeV) using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. We present multiplicities, rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, and analyze the $K^*/K$ yield ratios as a function of energy and centrality. We further estimate the time interval between chemical and kinetic freeze-out using the experimental method. A detailed comparison with recent NA61/SHINE data demonstrates that the UrQMD model captures the essential features of resonance dynamics, although the very strong resonance suppression in central collisions observed in the data cannot be quantitatively reproduced.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model captures the essential features of K*(892) resonance dynamics in Ar+Sc collisions at SPS energies, but the very strong resonance suppression observed in central collisions by NA61/SHINE cannot be quantitatively reproduced, potentially indicating an additional suppression mechanism such as an extended hadronic lifetime from crossing a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v3.6) transport model to simulate p+p and Ar+Sc collisions at center-of-mass energies of 8.8, 11.9, and 16.8 GeV. They tracked all decay products of K*(892) resonances through the system evolution and considered a resonance as reconstructable only if none of its decay daughters underwent rescattering, matching the experimental method of invariant mass analysis. Centrality was estimated using impact parameter ranges (0-10% central events used b=0-2.67 fm) and the number of participants was calculated using the Glauber approximation.

## Key numbers

- Three center-of-mass energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8, 11.9, and 16.8 GeV for both p+p and Ar+Sc collisions
- K*(892) vacuum lifetime of approximately 4 fm/c used in lifetime estimates
- Mean transverse momenta of K* on the order of 600-650 MeV, depending on collision energy and centrality, used for Lorentz-gamma factor in lifetime calculations
- For the improved lifetime estimate using rate equations, a damping rate Gamma = 0.02 fm^{-1} was used, giving T = 1/Gamma = 50 fm/c, which is essentially energy independent for nucleus-nucleus collisions from sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 to 39 GeV
- The K*/K ratios decrease with increasing N_part, with the suppression effect captured by UrQMD but the experimental data showing a surprisingly strong suppression at mid and highest energy for the most central reactions in the K*/K- ratio

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by the same group on STAR@RHIC data for Au+Au reactions (Chabane 2024, Neidig 2025) to intermediate-mass Ar+Sc systems at SPS energies. It directly compares with recent NA61/SHINE measurements (Kozlowski 2024) and complements existing STAR measurements (STAR 2022) at similar energies. The findings enable further study of system size dependence of fireball lifetime and may provide evidence for a softened equation of state if the longer experimental lifetimes in central collisions are confirmed, potentially indicating a critical point or first-order phase transition.
