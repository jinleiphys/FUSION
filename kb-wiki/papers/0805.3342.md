# Baryons and Vector Dominance in Holographic Dual QCD

> The infinite tower of vector mesons encoded in holographic dual QCD bring drastic changes to the soliton structure of the nucleon. The nucleon is given by a point-like instanton in 5D surrounded by a vector meson cloud with the vector dominance restored by the infinite tower. I discuss the possible relevance of this structure in hot and dense hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The infinite tower of vector mesons in holographic dual QCD (hQCD) restores vector dominance for baryon form factors, which is violated when only the lowest vector meson is considered; this suggests that the "vector manifestation" (VM) fixed point and associated "hadronic freedom" picture derived from a single-rho effective theory (HLS₁) may not be a correct feature of QCD.

## Method

The author analyzes the Sakai-Sugimoto (SS) model, a top-down holographic QCD construction using D4-D8/D\(\bar{8}\) branes, which yields a 5D Yang-Mills action with a position-dependent coupling. By Kaluza-Klein decomposition along the fifth coordinate, the theory reduces to a 4D hidden local symmetry with an infinite tower of flavor gauge fields (HLS\(_\infty\)). Baryons emerge as instantons in 5D, and the author examines how the photon coupling to these instantons is modified by the infinite tower, comparing the resulting form factors to those from the truncated HLS₁ theory.

## Key numbers

- The instanton size scales as \(R \sim 1/(M_{KK}\sqrt{\lambda})\), making it pointlike in the large \(\lambda\) limit.
- For \(N_c=3\), the parameters \(\lambda \approx 10\) and \(M_{KK} \approx 0.94\) GeV are fixed by meson phenomenology, leaving no free parameters for the baryon sector.
- The nucleon form factors from the infinite-tower model agree within \(\sim 10\%\) accuracy for momentum transfers \(Q^2 \leq 1/2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The lowest four isovector mesons nearly saturate the sum rules \(F_1^\pi(0)=1\) and \(F_1^N(0)=1\); the lowest \(\rho\) overshoots by \(\sim 30\%\), compensated mostly by the next \(\rho'\).
- The physical size of the nucleon is almost entirely given by the vector-meson cloud, leaving only \(\sim 0.1\) fm for the intrinsic degree of freedom.
- In HLS₁, the flash point temperature is \(T_{flash} \sim 120\) MeV; the flash point density is estimated as \(n_{flash} \sim 2-3 n_0\).

## Context

This work extends the author's prior series with Hong, Yee, and Yi (HRYY, HRYY-VD) on baryons in holographic QCD, and contrasts with the HLS₁ effective theory developed by Harada and Yamawaki (HY:PR), which predicts vector manifestation (VM) with vanishing \(\rho\) mass and violation of vector dominance near chiral restoration. The infinite-tower result restores vector dominance for baryons, contradicting the HLS₁ prediction that vector dominance is violated in medium. The author notes that the quark condensate, which drives the VM fixed point, is sensitive to \(1/N_c\) corrections not captured in the large-\(N_c\) limit of hQCD, leaving open the question of how to compute these corrections to resolve the discrepancy.
