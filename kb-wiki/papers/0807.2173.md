# Symmetry interaction and many-body correlations

> It is shown how many-body correlations involving the symmetry potential naturally arise in the molecular dynamics CoMD-II model. The effect of these correlations on the collision dynamics at the Fermi energies is discussed. Small level of correlations for systems of moderate asymmetry are able to produce large effects also in simple observables like the charge distributions. The comparison with predictions based on Equation Of State static calculations is also discussed.

## Key claim

Many-body correlations involving the symmetry potential, which naturally arise in the CoMD-II molecular dynamics model, can produce large effects on observables like charge distributions even at small levels of correlation for systems of moderate asymmetry, contrary to the standard mean-field approximation that assumes a simple \(\beta^{2}\) dependence of the symmetry interaction.

## Method

The authors used the Constrained Molecular Dynamics Model-II (CoMD-II) to simulate the collision of \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) at 40 MeV/nucleon, implementing a symmetry interaction derived from microscopic two-body forces with explicit treatment of neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton overlap integrals. They compared three form-factor parameterizations (Stiff1, Stiff2, Soft) for the density dependence of the symmetry potential, and contrasted the full correlated results with calculations imposing the Isovectorial Mean Field Approximation (I.M.F.A.), which assumes equal average distances between all nucleon pairs.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(a_0 = (\Upsilon^1 - \Upsilon^0)/2 = 72\) MeV sets the strength of the microscopic symmetry interaction.
- In the I.M.F.A. limit, the symmetry potential density corresponds to \(e_a = 27\) MeV, giving an \(S_0\) value of about 40 MeV.
- For the \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) system at b=4 fm, the maximum average overlap integral \(s_{max}\) for the Stiff1 case is about 1.25 times the ground state value \(s_{g.s.}\).
- The correlation coefficient \(\alpha \simeq 0.15\) quantifies the enhancement of neutron-proton overlap relative to the average of neutron-neutron and proton-proton overlaps.
- The average symmetry potential per nucleon at t=0 fm/c (normal density) is of the order of -1.5 MeV/nucleon for the \(^{40}\text{Cl}\) ground state.
- The limiting asymmetry for which \(\beta_M\) becomes positive is \(|\beta_c| > 0.22\) for systems with mass around 68 units.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of the symmetry energy in semiclassical transport models beyond the standard \(\beta^2\) dependence used in BUU calculations and EOS static calculations (e.g., Bao0, Lattimer, Wiringa). The authors show that the CoMD-II model naturally generates many-body correlations that produce a negative \(\beta_M\) at moderate asymmetries, leading to an attractive average symmetry potential for stiff parameterizations, contrary to the repulsive behavior predicted by the I.M.F.A. This enables future experimental tests of the stiffness of the symmetry interaction through measurements of isospin observables in heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, and suggests that static EOS calculations should investigate variational parameters with charge/mass asymmetry dependence, similar to studies of pairing forces (Shuck, EDF).
