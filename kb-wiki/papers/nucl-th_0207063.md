# Isoscalar dipole strength in ^{208}_{82}Pb_{126}: the spurious mode and the strength in the continuum

> Isoscalar dipole (compression) mode is studied first using schematic harmonic-oscillator model and, then, the self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and random phase approximation (RPA) solved in coordinate space. Taking ^{208}Pb and the SkM* interaction as a numerical example, the spurious component and the strength in the continuum are carefully examined using the sum rules. It is pointed out that in the continuum calculation one has to use an extremely fine radial mesh in HF and RPA in order to separate, with good accuracy, the spurious component from intrinsic excitations.

# Isoscalar dipole strength in 208Pb: the spurious mode and the strength in the continuum

## Key claim

The isoscalar dipole strength in 208Pb contains an appreciable amount of strength in the energy region 7–17 MeV, well below the conventional isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) peak near 23 MeV, but the reliability of this low-energy strength depends critically on whether the spurious center-of-mass component has been fully removed; the authors find that even with a fine radial mesh of 0.1 fm in both HF and RPA, the spurious state appears slightly below zero energy and consumes only a major (not complete) portion of the expected energy-weighted sum rule.

## Method

The authors perform self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) and random phase approximation (RPA) calculations in coordinate space using the Green's function method with the Skyrme SkM* interaction for 208Pb, employing a radial mesh of 0.1 fm in both HF and RPA. They compare results using the full dipole operator r^3 Y_{1μ} with those using the subtracted operator r^3 Y_{1μ} − η r Y_{1μ}, and examine the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) contributions from the spurious state and intrinsic excitations. A schematic harmonic-oscillator model with separable residual interactions is also used to study the distribution of strength among ΔN=1 and ΔN=3 excitations.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb with SkM*: ⟨r²⟩ = 30.85 fm², ⟨r⁴⟩ = 1218.2 fm⁴
- Total EWSR for operator r³Y_{1μ}: S = 13.79 × 10⁶ fm⁶ MeV
- Intrinsic EWSR for operator r³Y_{1μ}: S_intr = 5.632 × 10⁶ fm⁶ MeV
- Spurious state contributes 8.16 × 10⁶ fm⁶ MeV to the EWSR, which is 59.2% of the total EWSR
- In the continuum calculation with Δr = 0.1 fm, the EWSR for E_x > 2 MeV is 114% of the expected value for operator r³Y_{1μ} and 96% for the subtracted operator
- In the discrete-basis calculation (n_max^HO = 12, N_max^HF = 24), only 92% of the correct EWSR portion is consumed by the calculated spurious state
- The ratio of EWSR in the region E_x < 17 MeV to that for E_x > 17 MeV is 1:4.9 for the subtracted operator
- In the harmonic-oscillator model, the ratio of unperturbed ΔN=1 to ΔN=3 transition strength is 1:6, and the ratio of unperturbed EWSR is 1:18

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous continuum RPA calculation (Hamamoto, Sagawa, Zhang 1998) by using a finer radial mesh (0.1 fm instead of 0.3 fm in RPA) and carefully checking sum rules, addressing criticism by Shlomo and Sanzhur (2002) that the earlier subtraction procedure oversubtracted strength. The results are compared with discrete-basis RPA calculations by Colo et al. (2000) and relativistic RPA by Vretenar et al. (2000), which also found low-energy IS dipole strength. The paper demonstrates that continuum calculations require extremely fine radial meshes to separate the spurious component, and that discrete-basis calculations converge surprisingly slowly for the spurious state properties, raising questions about their reliability for IS dipole strength estimates.
