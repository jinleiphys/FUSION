# Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis Reactions Catalyzed by a Long-Lived Negatively Charged Leptonic Particle

> An accurate quantum three-body calculation is performed for the new type of big-bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) reactions that are catalyzed by a long-lived negatively-charged, massive leptonic particle (called X^-) such as the supersymmetric (SUSY) particle stau. The reactions studied here includes, i) 4He-transfer reactions such as (4He X)+d --> 6Li+X, ii) radiative capture reactions such as (7Be X)+ p --> (8B X) + gamma, iii) three-body breakup reactions such as (7Li X)+ p --> 4He+4He+X, iv) charge-exchange reactions such as (p X)+4He -->(4He X) +p, and v) neutron induced reactions such as (8Be X)+ n -->9Be+X, where (A X) denotes a Coulombic bound state of a nucleus A and X^-. In recent papers it has been claimed that some of the catalyzed BBN reactions have significantly large cross sections so as to markedly change the abundances of some elements, not only giving a solution to the 6Li-7Li problem (calculated underproduction of 6Li by a factor of 1000 and overproduction of 7Li+7Be by a factor of nearly 3) but also imposing strong restrictions on the lifetime and the primordial abundance of X^-. However, most of the calculations of these reaction cross sections in the literature were performed assuming too naive models or approximations that are unsuitable for the complicated low-energy nuclear reactions. We use a high-accuracy few-body calculational method developed by the authors, and provide precise cross sections and rates of these catalyzed BBN reactions for use in the BBN network calculation.

## Key claim

The paper provides precise cross sections and reaction rates for X⁻-catalyzed big-bang nucleosynthesis reactions using a high-accuracy quantum three-body calculation method, correcting earlier estimates that used naive scaling relations or unsuitable approximations; the authors find that the enhancement of the catalyzed α-transfer reaction producing ⁶Li is about one order of magnitude smaller than previously claimed by Pospelov (2007).

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for the A + a + X⁻ system using all three sets of Jacobi coordinates, with the total wave function expanded to include two open channels (entrance channel (AX⁻)+a and exit channel B+X⁻) plus a closed-channel term expanded in Gaussian basis functions. The nuclear potentials between α and d, t, or ³He were constructed to reproduce observed binding energies, charge radii, and low-energy scattering phase shifts, while the Coulomb potential between nuclei and X⁻ used a Gaussian charge distribution. The integro-differential equations were solved by both direct finite-difference and Kohn-type variational methods to confirm accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The astrophysical S-factor for the catalyzed reaction (αX⁻)+d → ⁶Li+X⁻ at the Gamow peak energy is 38 keV·b, giving an enhancement over the standard BBN radiative capture reaction of S_X/S_γ ∼ 10⁷ (nearly one order of magnitude smaller than the ∼10⁸ claimed by Pospelov (2007) using a scaling relation)
- The binding energy of the (αX⁻)₁ₛ state is −337.3 keV for m_X = 100 GeV (and −347.6 keV for m_X → ∞), with an rms radius of 6.84 fm (6.69 fm for infinite mass)
- The binding energies of (⁷Be X⁻) and (αX⁻) are ∼1.3 MeV and ∼0.34 MeV, with rms radii ∼3.6 fm and ∼7 fm, respectively
- The standard BBN S-factors for α+t → ⁷Li+γ and α+³He → ⁷Be+γ at astrophysical energies are ∼0.1 keV·b and ∼0.5 keV·b, respectively, which are 5 orders of magnitude larger than the E1-hindered α+d → ⁶Li+γ reaction (S_γ ∼ 2×10⁻⁶ keV·b)
- The X⁻ particle is assumed to have mass m_X ≳ 100 GeV and lifetime τ_X ≳ 10³ s

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Hamaguchi et al. (2007) on ⁶Li production via (αX⁻)+d → ⁶Li+X⁻, which was the only previous quantum three-body calculation of these catalyzed reactions, and corrects the scaling-relation estimates of Pospelov (2007) and Cyburt et al. (2006) that overestimated cross sections for α-transfer reactions producing ⁶Li, ⁷Li, and ⁷Be. The precise reaction rates provided enable BBN network calculations to determine whether X⁻-catalyzed reactions can solve the ⁶Li-⁷Li problem (underproduction of ⁶Li by a factor of ∼1000 and overproduction of ⁷Li+⁷Be by a factor of ∼3 in standard BBN) and to constrain the lifetime and primordial abundance of the X⁻ particle.
