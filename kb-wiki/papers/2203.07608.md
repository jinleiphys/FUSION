# Model for collective motion

> Collective motion is a manifestation of emergent phenomena in medium-heavy and heavy nuclei. A relatively large number of constituent nucleons contribute coherently to nuclear excitations (vibrations, rotations) that are characterized by large electromagnetic moments and transition rates. Basic features of collective excitations are reviewed, and a simple model introduced that describes large-amplitude quadrupole and octupole shape dynamics, as well as the dynamics of induced fission. Modern implementations of the collective Hamiltonian model are based on the microscopic framework of energy density functionals, that provide an accurate global description of nuclear ground states and collective excitations. Results of illustrative calculations are discussed in comparison with available data.

## Key claim

This chapter reviews the geometric collective model of nuclear excitations and introduces a simple collective Hamiltonian that describes large-amplitude quadrupole and octupole shape dynamics, as well as induced fission, but the authors note that a description of collective phenomena starting from single-nucleon degrees of freedom still presents a considerable challenge, and the modern implementations rely on the microscopic framework of energy density functionals.

## Method

The authors present the classical and quantum theory of nuclear surface oscillations using the Bohr Hamiltonian, parameterizing the nuclear surface in spherical harmonics with shape parameters αλμ(t) and introducing the Bohr deformation parameters β and γ for quadrupole deformations. They derive the collective Hamiltonian from the liquid drop model, quantize it using the Pauli prescription, and separate the wave function into β, γ, and rotational components. The rotation-vibration model is then developed for well-deformed nuclei with small oscillations around an axially deformed minimum, and a microscopic derivation of the collective Hamiltonian is outlined using the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA).

## Key numbers

- The total energy of the five-dimensional quadrupole oscillator is given by E = (2n + l + 5/2)ħω = (N + 5/2)ħω, where N = 0, 1, ...
- For the rotation-vibration model, the total energy is E = ħωβ(nβ + 1/2) + ħωγ(2nγ + 1/2|K| + 1) + (ħ²/2J)[I(I+1) - K²], with ωβ = √(C₀/B) and ωγ = √(C₂/B).
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment is defined as Q₀ = (3ZeR₀²/√(5π)) β₀.
- The stretched B(E2) values in a rotational band for K = 0 bands are B(E2, I+2 → I) = Q₀² (5/16π) (3/2) (I+1)(I+2)/((2I+3)(2I+5)).

## Context

This work extends the geometric collective model originally introduced by Bohr (1952) and Bohr and Mottelson (1953), and further elaborated by Faessler and Greiner (1962). The microscopic derivation using the GCM with GOA follows approaches by Haff (1973), Banerjee (1973), and Onishi (1975), and the validity of these approximations is demonstrated by comparison with a full GCM calculation for ⁷⁶Kr, a nucleus characterized by shape coexistence (Yao 2014). The chapter enables future applications of the microscopic collective Hamiltonian based on covariant density functional theory to describe low-energy collective excitations and fission dynamics across the nuclear chart.
