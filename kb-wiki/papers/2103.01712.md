# Triangle singularity mechanism for the $pp \to π^+ d$ fusion reaction

> We develop a model for the $pp \to π^+ d$ reaction based on the $pp \to Δ(1232) N$ transition followed by $Δ(1232) \to πN'$ decay and posterior fusion of $N N'$ to give the deuteron. We show that the triangle diagram depicting this process develops a triangle singularity leading to a large cross section of this reaction compared to ordinary fusion reactions. The results of the calculation also show that the process is largely dominated by the $pp$ system in $L=2, S=0$, which transfers $J=2$ to the final $π^+ d$ system. This feature is shown to be well suited to provide $L=2,S=1$, $J^\mathrm{tot}=3$ for $np$ in the $np(I=0) \to π^- pp$ followed by $pp \to π^+ d$ reaction, which has been proposed recently, as a means of describing the so far assumed dibaryon $d^*(2380)$ peak.

## Key claim

The large cross section of the $pp \to \pi^+ d$ reaction (abnormally large for a fusion reaction) is explained by a triangle singularity mechanism, where the intermediate $\Delta(1232)$, neutron, and proton can all be placed nearly on shell and collinear; the singularity is not exact because the deuteron is bound by 2.2 MeV, but the amplitude develops a large strength by continuity, producing a broad peak around $\sqrt{s} = 2165$ MeV rather than the singular point at 2179 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a field-theoretical model for $pp \to \pi^+ d$ via $\pi$ exchange, where the $pp$ system excites a $\Delta(1232)$ and a nucleon, the $\Delta$ decays into $\pi N'$, and the two nucleons $N N'$ fuse into the deuteron. The triangle diagram is evaluated with full pion propagators (no static approximation), including $\rho$-exchange, short-range correlation corrections (Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$), and an impulse approximation term; the deuteron vertex is treated via a momentum-space cutoff form factor linked to the Bonn wave function. The free parameters ($M_\Delta$, $\Gamma_\Delta$, $\Lambda_\pi$, $\Lambda_\rho$) are fitted to data from the SAID database for $pp \to \pi^+ d$ and the time-reversed $\pi^+ d \to pp$ reaction.

## Key numbers

* The triangle singularity would appear at $\sqrt{s} = 2179$ MeV if the deuteron were unbound; the experimental peak is seen around $\sqrt{s} = 2165$ MeV.
* The $pp \to \pi^+ d$ cross section is of the order of $3-4$ mb, which is abnormally large for a fusion reaction.
* The $np \to \pi^+ \pi^- d$ peak strength is about $0.5$ mb, made possible by the size of the $pp \to \pi^+ d$ cross section.
* The best fits to data in the range $K_p^{\rm lab} \in [525:700]$ MeV give a fitted $\Delta$ mass of about 1215 MeV and a width of 150 MeV (fit d) or 117 MeV (fit e).
* The coupling constants used are $f = 1.00$ for $\pi NN$, $f^* = 2.13$ for $\pi N\Delta$, $f_\rho = 7.96$, and $f^*_\rho = 13.53$.
* The short-range correlation parameter is $g' \simeq 0.6$.

## Context

This work extends the quantum-mechanical treatments of the time-reversed reaction $\pi^+ d \to pp$ by Riska (1980s), Green (1970s), and Weise (1970s), which used deuteron wave functions and showed the cross section blowing up in the limit of zero $\Delta$ width, a characteristic of the triangle singularity that was not identified at the time. The model also supports a recent proposal by the same group (Ikeno, Molina, Oset, 2020) that the $d^*(2380)$ dibaryon peak observed by Clement and collaborators in $np \to \pi^0 \pi^0 d$ and $np \to \pi^+ \pi^- d$ can be explained by a two-step process $np \to \pi^- pp$ followed by $pp \to \pi^+ d$, without invoking a genuine dibaryon resonance. The results enable further studies of triangle singularities in few-nucleon fusion reactions and their role in simulating resonant structures.
