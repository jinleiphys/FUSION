# Universal Parametrization of Thermal Photon Rates in Hadronic Matter

> Electromagnetic (EM) radiation off strongly interacting matter created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions (HICs) encodes information on the high-temperature phases of nuclear matter. Microscopic calculations of thermal EM emission rates are usually rather involved and not readily accessible to broad applications in models of the fireball evolution which are required to compare to experimental data. An accurate and universal parametrization of the microscopic calculations is thus key to honing the theory behind the EM spectra. Here we provide such a parametrization for photon emission rates from hadronic matter, including the contributions from in-medium rho mesons (which incorporate effects from anti-/baryons), as well as Bremsstrahlung from pi-pi scattering. Individual parametrizations for each contribution are numerically determined through nested fitting functions for photon energies from 0.2 to 5 GeV in chemically equilibrated matter of temperatures 100-180 MeV and baryon chemical potentials 0-400 MeV. Special care is taken to extent the parameterizations to chemical off-equilibrium as encountered in HICs after chemical freezeout. This provides a functional description of thermal photon rates within a 20% variation of the microscopically calculated values.

## Key claim
The paper provides a universal parametrization of thermal photon emission rates from hadronic matter that reproduces microscopic calculations within 20% variation, covering photon energies from 0.2 to 5 GeV, temperatures from 100 to 180 MeV, and baryon chemical potentials from 0 to 400 MeV, with an extension to chemical off-equilibrium conditions encountered in heavy-ion collisions after chemical freezeout.

## Method
The authors constructed nested fitting functions for two main contributions: in-medium rho meson decays (including baryon and anti-baryon effects) and pi-pi Bremsstrahlung. For the rho meson contribution, they first parametrized rates at zero baryon chemical potential for nine temperatures, then added a factorized function to account for finite baryon chemical potential effects using polynomial expansions in mu_B with temperature-dependent coefficients. For pi-pi Bremsstrahlung, they extended previous calculations to higher photon energies and parametrized the rates using a similar exponential ansatz. Chemical off-equilibrium effects were incorporated through fugacity factors and an effective baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Photon energy range: 0.2 to 5 GeV
- Temperature range: 100 to 180 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential range: 0 to 400 MeV
- Parametrization reproduces calculated rates within 20% variation (mostly within 10%)
- Pi-pi Bremsstrahlung rates fitted within approximately 5% for T=100-180 MeV and q0=1-5 GeV
- At T=150 MeV, pi-pi Bremsstrahlung exceeds in-medium rho decays for q0 less than 0.4 GeV, but drops below by about an order of magnitude for q0 greater than or equal to 1 GeV
- Pi-K Bremsstrahlung amounts to about 20% of the pi-pi contribution

## Context
This work extends earlier photon rate parametrizations from Turbide (2003) by including baryon chemical potential as an additional variable and revisiting pi-pi Bremsstrahlung calculations from Liu (2007) to higher energies. The parametrizations are based on in-medium rho spectral function calculations developed by Rapp (1997, 1999) and Urban (1999) that have been successful in describing experimental dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions. The results enable broad application of microscopic thermal photon rates in fireball evolution models, which is needed to interpret direct-photon spectra and elliptic flow measurements from experiments at RHIC and LHC.
