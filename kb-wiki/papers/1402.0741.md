# Electromagnetic transition form factors of the Roper resonance in effective field theory

> We analyze the form factors of the electromagnetic nucleon-to-Roper-resonance transition in the framework of low-energy effective field theory. A systematic power-counting procedure is generated by applying the complex-mass scheme. Within this power counting we calculate the form factors to next-to-next-to-leading order and fit the results to empirical data.

# Electromagnetic transition form factors of the Roper resonance in effective field theory

## Key claim

The electromagnetic nucleon-to-Roper-resonance transition form factors can be calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order using low-energy effective field theory with the complex-mass scheme, but the perturbative expansion converges only up to approximately Q² = 0.4 GeV², and fits were extended to 0.58 GeV² only due to the lack of low-Q² empirical data.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian including pions, nucleons, the Roper resonance, and the rho meson as explicit degrees of freedom, with other resonances parametrized in low-energy coupling constants. They applied the complex-mass scheme to obtain a systematic power counting, calculated the transition form factors to next-to-next-to-leading order (O(q³)) from four tree diagrams and eighteen loop diagrams, and fitted the six remaining low-energy constants to empirical helicity amplitude data from single-pion and two-pion electroproduction analyses.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance pole mass used in the calculation is z = 1.365 − i/2 × 0.190 GeV, with the rho meson pole mass M_ρ = 0.775 − i/2 × 0.149 GeV
- The pion decay constant is F_π = 92.2 MeV, the pion mass M_π = 0.140 GeV, and the nucleon mass m_N = 0.938 GeV
- The axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.27
- The πNR coupling g_NR was fixed at 0.35 for the primary fit and 0.175 for an alternative fit
- The fitted isovector rho-meson coupling k_3 = −4.9 GeV⁻¹ (fit 2) or −5.5 GeV⁻¹ (fit 1)
- The fitted proton Dirac-type coupling c_p* = 0.29 GeV⁻¹ (fit 2) and neutron c_n* = −0.18 GeV⁻¹ (fit 2)
- The fitted proton slope parameter d_p* = 0.04 GeV⁻³ and neutron d_n* = 0.1 GeV⁻³ (fit 2)
- The fits cover data up to Q² = 0.58 GeV², with the authors cautioning that a one-loop calculation should be treated with care beyond Q² = 0.4 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the complex-mass scheme approach previously applied to the rho meson (Djukanovic 2009) and the Roper resonance magnetic moments (Bauer 2012) to the electromagnetic transition form factors. It addresses the failure of relativistic quark models (Cardarelli 1996, Ramalho 2010) and lattice QCD (Lin 2008) to correctly predict the low-Q² behavior of the nucleon-to-Roper helicity amplitudes. The results provide a framework for systematic extrapolations of lattice QCD simulations to the physical pion mass, and the transition vertex can serve as a building block for a full calculation of pion electroproduction in the Roper resonance region.
