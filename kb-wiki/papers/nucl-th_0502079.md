# Kaon Condensation and Lambda-Nucleon Loop in the Relativistic Mean-Field Approach

> The possibility of kaon condensation in high-density symmetric nuclear matter is investigated including both s- and p-wave kaon-baryon interactions within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory. Above a certain density, we have a collective ${\bar K}_s$ state carrying the same quantum numbers as the antikaon. The appearance of the ${\bar K}_s$ state is caused by the time component of the axial-vector interaction between kaons and baryons. It is shown that the system becomes unstable with respect to condensation of $K$-${\bar K}_s$ pairs. We consider how the effective baryon masses affect the kaon self-energy coming from the time component of the axial-vector interaction. Also, the role of the spatial component of the axial-vector interaction on the possible existence of the collective kaonic states is discussed in connection with $Λ$-mixing effects in the ground state of high-density matter. Implications of $K{\bar K}_s$ condensation for high-energy heavy-ion collisions are briefly mentioned.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in high-density symmetric nuclear matter, a collective $\bar K_s$ state (carrying antikaon quantum numbers) appears due to the time component of the axial-vector interaction between kaons and baryons, and that the system becomes unstable with respect to condensation of $K$-$\bar K_s$ pairs, but only when the $\Lambda$ particle-nucleon hole ($\Lambda N^{-1}$) loop contribution is included in the kaon self-energy, and the $K_s$ branch is found to be unphysical upon complex-plane analysis.

## Method

The authors extend the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism to include nucleons, $\Lambda$ hyperons, and kaons, incorporating both $s$-wave kaon-baryon interactions (via the $KN$ sigma term and Tomozawa-Weinberg vector interaction) and $p$-wave axial-vector coupling. They compute the kaon self-energy from the $\Lambda N^{-1}$ loop and solve the dispersion equation ${\rm Re} D_K^{-1}(q_0;{\bf q}) = 0$ for the kaon propagator, analyzing pole energies at zero and finite momentum using two parameter sets (PM1-L1 and PM1-L2) for the $\Lambda$ coupling to mean fields.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for appearance of the $\bar K_s$ collective mode ($\rho_c^{(1)}$): 4.0 $\rho_0$ for PM1-L1 and 3.0 $\rho_0$ for PM1-L2, where $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Critical density for $K$-$\bar K_s$ pair condensation ($\rho_c^{(2)}$): 7.2 $\rho_0$ for PM1-L1 and 4.9 $\rho_0$ for PM1-L2.
- Critical density for $K^+ (K^0)$ condensation (without $\Lambda N^{-1}$ loop): about 16 $\rho_0$ for PM1-L1 and 6 $\rho_0$ for PM1-L2.
- The $KN$ sigma term is taken as $\Sigma_{KN} = 400$ MeV.
- The $KN\Lambda$ coupling constant $\widetilde f = (D+3F)/(2\sqrt{3}f) \simeq 0.61/f$, with $f \simeq 93$ MeV, $D=0.81$, $F=0.44$.

## Context

This work extends the RMF treatment of strange hadronic matter by Maruyama and Tatsumi (2004) to include the $\Lambda N^{-1}$ loop in the kaon self-energy, which was previously omitted. It contrasts with the $K^+K^-$ pair condensation scenario proposed by Nelson and Kaplan (1987) for heavy-ion collisions, which the authors argue is unlikely because the $K^+$ excitation energy acquires repulsion rather than attraction in medium. The mechanism of $K\bar K_s$ condensation is analogous to $p$-wave charged pion condensation in neutron-star matter (Baym and Campbell, 1979; Ericson and Weise, 1988). The results enable further study of kaon condensation in neutron-star matter with hyperon mixing, and may have implications for strangeness production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
