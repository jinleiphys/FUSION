# Light-front Ward-Takahashi Identity for Two-Fermion Systems

> We propose a three-dimensional electromagnetic current operator within light-front dynamics that satisfies a light-front Ward-Takahashi identity for two-fermion systems. The light-front current operator is obtained by a quasi-potential reduction of the four-dimensional current operator and acts on the light-front valence component of bound or scattering states. A relation between the light-front valence wave function and the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter amplitude both for bound or scattering states is also derived, such that the matrix elements of the four-dimensional current operator can be fully recovered from the corresponding light-front ones. The light-front current operator can be perturbatively calculated through a quasi-potential expansion, and the divergence of the proposed current satisfies a Ward-Takahashi identity at any given order of the expansion. In the quasi-potential expansion the instantaneous terms of the fermion propagator are accounted for by the effective interaction and two-body currents. We exemplify our theoretical construction in the Yukawa model in the ladder approximation, investigating in detail the current operator at the lowest nontrivial order of the quasi-potential expansion of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The explicit realization of the light-front form of the Ward-Takahashi identity is verified. We also show the relevance of instantaneous terms and of the pair contribution to the two-body current and the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a three-dimensional electromagnetic current operator within light-front dynamics that satisfies a light-front Ward-Takahashi identity for two-fermion systems, but only when the instantaneous terms of the fermion propagator and the pair contribution to the two-body current are properly accounted for in the quasi-potential expansion.

## Method

The authors perform a quasi-potential reduction of the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-fermion systems, projecting onto the light-front hypersurface to obtain a three-dimensional valence wave function. They construct a light-front current operator from the four-dimensional electromagnetic current using reverse projection operators, and verify that its divergence satisfies a Ward-Takahashi identity at any given order of the quasi-potential expansion. The construction is exemplified in the Yukawa model with chargeless boson exchange in the ladder approximation, examining the current operator at the lowest nontrivial order of the quasi-potential expansion.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program for solving the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation using quasi-potential expansion and light-front projection, which was developed for two-boson systems in Sales et al. (2000) and for two-fermion systems in Sales et al. (2001). The paper generalizes the light-front current construction from the bosonic case (Adnei et al., 2007) to fermionic systems, where instantaneous fermion propagation makes the treatment fundamentally different. The approach is related to the gauging techniques developed by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (2003) for two-boson systems, and enables systematic calculation of electromagnetic properties of two-fermion bound and scattering states while preserving current conservation at any truncation order.
