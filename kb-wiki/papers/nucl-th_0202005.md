# Chiral dynamics of nuclear matter at finite temperature

> We extend a recent three-loop calculation of nuclear matter in the systematic framework of chiral perturbation theory to finite temperatures T. The contributions from one- and two-pion exchange diagrams which cause nuclear binding and saturation at T=0 are included for T>0 in the density and temperature dependent free energy per particle, $\bar F(rho,T)$. The so-called anomalous 2pi-exchange contribution $\bar A(rho,T)$ (with no counterpart in the ground state energy density at T=0) is consistently included. The calculated pressure isotherms display the familiar first-order liquid-gas phase transition of isospin symmetric nuclear matter with a critical point at T_c = 25.5 MeV and rho_c = 0.09 fm^{-3}. The too high value of the critical temperature originates from the strong momentum dependence of the underlying single-particle potential U(p,k_{f0}) near the Fermi-surface. We also consider pure neutron matter at T>0 in the same framework and find fair agreement with sophisticated many-body calculations for neutron densities rho_n < 0.2 fm^{-3}.

## Key claim

The chiral three-loop calculation of nuclear matter, which reproduces saturation properties at T=0, is extended to finite temperatures and yields a first-order liquid-gas phase transition, but the predicted critical temperature of 25.5 MeV is too high compared to the empirical range of 16-18 MeV because the underlying single-particle potential has a strong momentum dependence near the Fermi surface that leads to an effective nucleon mass of about 3M.

## Method

The free energy per particle of isospin symmetric nuclear matter was calculated at finite temperature using chiral perturbation theory at three-loop order, including one-pion exchange, iterated one-pion exchange, and irreducible two-pion exchange diagrams, plus an anomalous two-pion exchange contribution that vanishes at T=0. The temperature enters only through the nucleons' thermal occupation probabilities given by a Fermi-Dirac distribution, and all thermodynamic quantities are derived from the free energy density via standard relations. The calculation was then adapted to pure neutron matter by changing the isospin factors of the pion-exchange diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of isospin symmetric nuclear matter: T_c = 25.5 MeV
- Critical density: rho_c = 0.09 fm^{-3} (approximately 0.5 rho_0, with rho_0 = 0.178 fm^{-3})
- Critical pressure: P(rho_c, T_c) = 0.69 MeV fm^{-3}
- Cut-off scale: Lambda = 646.3 MeV (fine-tuned to binding energy per particle of 15.26 MeV at T=0)
- Effective nucleon mass at the Fermi surface: M*(k_{f0}) approximately 3M
- For pure neutron matter, fair agreement with sophisticated many-body calculations for neutron densities rho_n < 0.2 fm^{-3}
- In the chiral limit (m_pi=0) with reduced cut-off Lambda = 555.8 MeV, the critical temperature remains nearly unchanged at T_c approximately 27 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation of nuclear matter at T=0 (Fritsch, Kaiser, Weise, 2002) to finite temperatures, and also builds on their calculation of the complex single-particle potential (Fritsch, Kaiser, Weise, 2002). The predicted critical temperature of 25.5 MeV is higher than the empirical range of 16-18 MeV from heavy-ion collision data (Durand, 1990) and the value of about 18 MeV from sophisticated many-body calculations of the Urbana group using the V14 effective NN-interaction plus three-nucleon force. The too-high critical temperature is attributed to the large effective nucleon mass near the Fermi surface, and the authors note that more elaborate calculations in effective chiral field theory are needed to resolve this problem.
