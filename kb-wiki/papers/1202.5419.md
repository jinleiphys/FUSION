# Strangeness production at finite temperature and baryon density in an effective relativistic mean field model

> We study the strangeness production in hot and dense nuclear medium, by requiring the conservation of the baryon density, electric charge fraction and zero net strangeness. The hadronic equation of state is investigated by means of an effective relativistic mean field model, with the inclusion of the full octet of baryons and kaon mesons. Kaons are considered taking into account of an effective chemical potential depending on the self-consistent interaction between baryons. The obtained results are compared with a minimal coupling scheme, calculated for different values of the anti-kaon optical potential and with non-interacting kaon particles. In this context, we also consider the possible onset of the kaon condensation for a wide range of temperatures and baryon densities.

## Key claim
The paper finds that, under conditions of zero net strangeness and finite isospin density relevant to relativistic heavy ion collisions, kaon condensation does not occur at any temperature or baryon density explored, contrary to the situation in neutron stars where condensation may take place. This result holds for both the effective relativistic mean field model and the minimal coupling scheme, with the caveat that the two models agree closely only for a moderate anti-kaon optical potential of approximately -50 MeV.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic mean field (RMF) model including the full baryon octet and kaon mesons, with the GM3 parameter set for meson-nucleon couplings and hyperon couplings fixed to hypernuclear potential depths. Kaons are treated in two ways: (1) a minimal coupling scheme where kaons couple directly to sigma, omega, and rho meson fields via coupling constants derived from the anti-kaon optical potential, and (2) an effective RMF model where kaons form a quasi-ideal Bose gas with an effective chemical potential derived from self-consistent baryon interactions. The system is constrained by conservation of baryon number, electric charge fraction (Z/A = 0.4), and zero net strangeness, with three independent chemical potentials (mu_B, mu_C, mu_S) solved self-consistently.

## Key numbers
- Anti-kaon optical potential values used: U_K- = -50 MeV, -100 MeV, and -160 MeV at saturation nuclear density.
- Corresponding kaon optical potentials at saturation density: U_K+ = 47 MeV (for U_K- = -50 MeV), -3 MeV (for U_K- = -100 MeV), and -63 MeV (for U_K- = -160 MeV).
- Hyperon potential depths used to fix scalar couplings: U_Lambda^N = -28 MeV, U_Sigma^N = 30 MeV, U_Xi^N = -18 MeV.
- Scalar coupling ratios for GM3: x_sigma_Lambda = 0.606, x_sigma_Sigma = 0.328, x_sigma_Xi = 0.322.
- Strangeness production becomes significant above T = 80 MeV, with negligible strangeness below T = 60 MeV at saturation density.
- Maximum baryon density explored: rho_B = 4 rho_0 (four times nuclear saturation density).
- Temperature for condensation analysis: T = 80 MeV (lower bound for strange particle production).

## Context
This work extends the study of strangeness production in hot and dense nuclear matter, comparing an effective RMF model (previously introduced by Andrea et al.) with the standard minimal coupling scheme used by Banik, Glendenning, Pons, and Cavagnoli. The results are relevant for the future CBM experiment at FAIR, which will probe high baryon density and finite temperature conditions. The finding that kaon condensation does not occur under zero net strangeness conditions agrees with predictions from modern transport models by Hartnack et al., while contrasting with neutron star scenarios where kaon condensation is possible (as studied by Zakout, Pal, Thorsson, Kaplan, and Banik). The strong sensitivity of the K+/K- ratio to the anti-kaon optical potential depth is identified as a key observable for determining the real part of the anti-kaon optical potential in future heavy ion collision experiments.
