# QCD Equation of State at nonzero baryon density in external magnetic field

> This paper is devoted to the study of QCD equation of state in external magnetic field and nonzero baryon density. Our study is carried out by means of lattice simulation with 2+1 dynamical staggered quarks at the physical masses. The simulation is conducted at imaginary baryon chemical potential what allowed us to overcome the sign problem. We expand the pressure in the baryon imaginary chemical potential and study three leading nonzero coefficients in this expansion. These coefficients were calculated for the following values of magnetic field: $eB=0.3$, $0.6$, $1.2$ GeV$^2$ with the lattice sizes $8\times32^3$, $10\times40^3$, $12\times48^3$. Using these data we take continuum limit for the coefficients. Our results indicate considerable enhancement of the expansion coefficients by the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the expansion coefficients \(c_2, c_4, c_6\) of the QCD pressure in baryon chemical potential are considerably enhanced by an external magnetic field, with the enhancement growing with field strength; however, the results are obtained via analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential and are limited to \(\mu_B/\pi T \leq 0.9\) where the continuation is stable.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 dynamical staggered quarks at physical masses, using imaginary baryon chemical potential to avoid the sign problem. They measured the imaginary baryon density \(n_I/T^3\) as a function of \(\theta = i\mu_B/T\) on three lattice sizes (\(8\times32^3\), \(10\times40^3\), \(12\times48^3\)) at magnetic fields \(eB = 0.3, 0.6, 1.2\) GeV\(^2\). By fitting the \(\theta\)-dependence of \(n_I\) to a polynomial in \(\theta\), they extracted the coefficients \(c_2, c_4, c_6\) of the pressure expansion and performed a continuum extrapolation using a quadratic ansatz in lattice spacing.

## Key numbers

*   At \(eB = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\) and \(T = 143\) MeV, the continuum-extrapolated coefficient \(c_2\) (multiplied by 10) is \(4.448(127)\); at the same field and \(T = 152\) MeV, \(10\cdot c_2 = 4.237(143)\).
*   At \(eB = 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) and \(T = 152\) MeV, the continuum-extrapolated coefficient \(100\cdot c_4 = 0.643(152)\); at \(eB = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\) and \(T = 131\) MeV, \(100\cdot c_4 = 2.679(285)\).
*   At \(eB = 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) and \(T = 160\) MeV, the continuum-extrapolated coefficient \(1000\cdot c_6 = -0.742(199)\); at \(eB = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\) and \(T = 152\) MeV, \(1000\cdot c_6 = -1.499(186)\).
*   The \(c_2\) coefficient at the largest field \(eB = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\) is "by an order of magnitude larger than that at zero magnetic field" (no explicit zero-field number given in the text).
*   The pressure excess \(\Delta p = p(\mu_B) - p(\mu_B=0)\) was reconstructed for \(\mu_B/\pi T\) values up to 0.90; results using only \(c_2, c_4\) versus all three coefficients agree except at \(\mu_B/\pi T = 0.90\).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the equation of state at nonzero baryon density (Borsanyi 2012, Guenther 2017, Bazavov 2017, D'Elia 2016) to include an external magnetic field, and complements earlier lattice studies of the EoS at zero density in a magnetic field (Bonati 2013, Levkova 2013, Bali 2014). The results for \(c_2\) agree at high temperatures with the second-order fluctuation calculations of Ding (2021) at similar magnetic fields, but differ at \(T \leq 140\) MeV, which the authors attribute to the higher pion mass (\(m_\pi \approx 220\) MeV) used in that work. The observed shift of peaks in \(c_2, c_4, c_6\) to lower temperatures with increasing field is interpreted as consistent with the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon. The data enable reconstruction of the pressure at nonzero baryon density in a magnetic field for use in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
