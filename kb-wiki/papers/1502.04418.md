# Multi-dimensional fission model with a complex absorbing potential

> We study the dynamics of multi-dimensional quantum tunneling by introducing a complex absorbing potential to a two-dimensional model for spontaneous fission. We fist diagonalize the Hamiltonian with the complex potential to determine a resonance state as well as its life-time. We then solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with such basis in order to investigate the tunneling path. We compare this method with the semi-classical method for multi-dimensional tunneling with imaginary time. A good agreement is found both for the life-time and for the tunneling path.

# Multi-dimensional fission model with a complex absorbing potential

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully quantum mechanical method using a complex absorbing potential (CAP) to solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation yields fission lifetimes and tunneling paths that agree with semi-classical approximations to within about 20%, validating the semi-classical approach for this two-dimensional fission model while also providing a quantum mechanical alternative that captures fluctuations around the classical path.

## Method

The authors introduce a complex absorbing potential at the boundary of a finite spatial box to simulate outgoing wave boundary conditions, then diagonalize the resulting non-Hermitian Hamiltonian to obtain bi-orthogonal eigenfunctions. They identify the resonance state as the eigenstate with the smallest imaginary part of the eigenenergy, from which the decay width and lifetime are extracted, and also solve the time-dependent Schrödinger equation by expanding the wave function in this bi-orthogonal basis to follow the time evolution of the tunneling process. The method is applied to a two-dimensional fission model with an elongation coordinate R and an intrinsic degree of freedom ξ coupled linearly, using parameters that mimic symmetric fission of ²³⁴U with coupling to beta vibration.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters are ℏΩ = ℏω = 0.97 MeV, with coupling strength g² = MmΩ²/(16ℏ²), M = 234M_N/4, and m = 3A M_N R₀²/(8π) where A = 234 and R₀ = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.
- For the one-dimensional problem with R_b = 11 fm, the quantum mechanical decay rate is 1/τ = 5.76 × 10⁻⁹ (1/year), while the semi-classical approximation yields 1/τ = 5.69 × 10⁻⁹ (1/year).
- The numerical precision of the method allows calculation of lifetimes up to about 34 decimal digits, enabling treatment of lifetimes on the order of billions of years when the characteristic system time is about 10⁻²² s.
- The lattice mesh size used is ΔR = 0.25 fm with a 9-point finite difference formula for the second derivative in the kinetic energy operator.
- For the two-dimensional problem, the lattice spans R ∈ [-4.75 fm : 16 fm] and ξ ∈ [-7.25 : 5.5] with mesh sizes ΔR = 0.25 fm and Δξ = 0.25.
- The complex absorbing potential parameters are W₀ = -0.387 MeV and R_a = 14 fm for the one-dimensional case, and W₀ = -0.1 MeV and R_a = 11 fm for the two-dimensional case.
- The maximum deviation between quantum mechanical and semi-classical lifetimes across the range of R_b values studied is about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the complex absorbing potential method, previously developed for computing resonance states in atomic physics (Riss and Meyer 1993; Feuerstein and Thumm 2003; Santra and Cederbaum 2001) and nuclear physics (Masui et al. 2002; Ito and Yabana 2005; Ota and Ito 2014), to multi-dimensional tunneling problems in fission. The method is applied to a two-dimensional fission model originally considered by Brink et al. (1983) and Takada (1995), and the results are compared with the semi-classical method using imaginary-time evolution (Schmidt 1986; Koonin and Ikeda 1989; Ikeda 1994). The approach enables future application to more realistic systems using constrained mean-field theory for the potential energy surface or the time-dependent generator coordinate method (Goutte et al. 2005; Goutte et al. 2011), particularly for cases where bifurcation of tunneling paths or competition between fission modes is important.
