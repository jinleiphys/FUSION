# Extracting $\hat{q}$ from single inclusive data at RHIC and at the LHC for different centralities: a new puzzle?

> We present here an extraction of the jet transport coefficient, $\hat{q}$, using RHIC and LHC single-inclusive high-$p_T$ data for different centralities. We fit a $K$-factor that determines the deviation of this coefficient from an ideal estimate, $K \equiv \hat{q}/(2ε^{3/4})$, where $ε$ is given by hydrodynamic simulations. As obtained already in previous studies, this $K$-factor is found to be larger at RHIC than at the LHC. However it is, unexpectedly, basically no-dependent on the centrality of the collision. Taken at face value this result, the $K$-factor would not depend on local properties of the QGP as temperature, but on global collision variables such as the center of mass energy.

## Key claim

The jet transport coefficient extracted from single-inclusive high-\(p_T\) data, expressed as a \(K\)-factor (\(K \equiv \hat{q}/(2\epsilon^{3/4})\)), is found to be larger at RHIC than at the LHC but, unexpectedly, almost independent of collision centrality. This suggests the \(K\)-factor may depend on global collision variables such as center-of-mass energy rather than on local properties of the QGP like temperature.

## Method

The authors fit a constant \(K\)-factor to the nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) for single-inclusive hadron production at high transverse momentum, using data from PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at the LHC for different centrality classes. The energy loss is implemented via the ASW quenching weights in the multiple soft scattering (harmonic oscillator) approximation, with the medium described by three different hydrodynamic models (Hirano, Glauber, and fKLN) and three different extrapolations for the transport coefficient before thermalization. The \(K\)-factor is defined by \(\hat q(\xi)=K\cdot 2\epsilon^{3/4}(\xi)\), where \(\epsilon\) is the local energy density from hydrodynamics, and the best-fit \(K\) is obtained via \(\chi^2\) minimization.

## Key numbers

- The \(K\)-factor is found to be \(\sim 2\)–\(3\) times larger at RHIC than at the LHC.
- At the LHC, the fitted \(K\)-factor is close to unity.
- For the LHC at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV, the \(K\)-factor would be \(\sim 10\%\) smaller than the corresponding value at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV.
- The analysis uses PHENIX \(R_{\rm AA}\) data (Adare 2008) and ALICE \(R_{\rm AA}\) data (Abelev 2012) for multiple centrality classes.

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of \(\hat{q}\) (e.g., Burke 2013) by studying the centrality dependence at both RHIC and LHC energies simultaneously. The finding that the \(K\)-factor is independent of centrality but differs between RHIC and LHC creates a puzzle, as it contradicts the expectation that the \(K\)-factor should scale with local medium properties like temperature. The results motivate further theoretical work to understand the role of global collision variables and to test the formalism with new LHC data at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV from CMS.
