# On the stability of strange dwarf hybrid stars

> We investigate the stability of stars with a density discontinuity between a high-density core and a very low density mantle. Previous work on "strange dwarfs" suggested that such a discontinuity could stabilize stars that would have been classified as unstable by the conventional criteria based on extrema in the mass-radius relation. We investigate the stability of such stars by numerically solving the Sturm-Liouville equations for the lowest-energy modes of the star. We find that the conventional criteria are correct, and strange dwarfs are not stable.

## Key claim

The authors find that the conventional Bardeen-Thorne-Meltzer (BTM) mass-radius stability criteria correctly determine stellar stability even in the presence of a sharp first-order phase transition with a large density discontinuity, contradicting the claim by Glendenning and Weber that "strange dwarf" configurations in the unstable branch of the mass-radius curve could be stable.

## Method

The authors construct an equation of state with a sharp first-order phase transition at neutron drip pressure (3742 MeV⁴) from the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) degenerate matter equation of state to a constant-sound-speed (CSS) phase modeling strange quark matter, with a density jump of order 10³. They regulate the discontinuity into a smooth crossover of variable width δP and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to generate stellar configurations, then numerically solve the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue problem for the radial oscillation modes to determine stability, studying the limit δP → 0.

## Key numbers

- Critical transition pressure: P_crit = 3742 MeV⁴ (neutron drip pressure in BPS equation of state)
- Energy density at neutron drip: ε_drip = 4 × 10¹¹ g·cm⁻³ = 1.6 × 10⁶ MeV⁴
- CSS equation of state parameters: k = 3, B^(1/4) = 145 MeV
- Regulator widths studied: δP = 10 to 100 MeV⁴
- Example central pressure in strange dwarf region: 7 × 10⁶ MeV⁴, with phase transition radius at r = 2.4 km

## Context

This work directly tests the claim by Glendenning and Weber (1990s) that "strange dwarf" stars with a strange quark matter core and white-dwarf mantle could be stable despite lying in a region of the mass-radius curve that the BTM criteria (Bardeen, Thorne, Meltzer, 1966) classify as unstable. The authors find that the lowest radial eigenmode (ω₀²) remains negative for all configurations between extrema c and d on the mass-radius curve, confirming the BTM criteria and ruling out stable strange dwarfs. This resolves a long-standing controversy and reaffirms the standard stability analysis for hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions.
