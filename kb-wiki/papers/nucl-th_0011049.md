# Baryon-Rich Quark-Gluon Plasma in Nuclear Collision

> The maximum achievable temperature (energy density) and minimum kinetic energy required for the formation of a baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma formed at central rapidity in small impact parameter nuclear collisions is estimated. A possible mechanism leading to the pile-up of matter is introduced. Plasma formation is expected to appear at about 15 GeV/Nucleon uranium beam energy on a stationary target or 2.7 GeV/Nucleon in colliding beams.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that a baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma can form at central rapidity in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies as low as about 15 GeV/nucleon for uranium on a stationary target or 2.7 GeV/nucleon in colliding beams, but only if a "plasma seed", a local density fluctuation of two to three times normal nuclear density, is present early in the collision; without such a seed, the plasma will not form at these energies.

## Method

The authors develop a kinetic model balancing energy gain from nucleons absorbed into a plasma seed against energy loss via pion radiation from the seed surface, using a threshold function for pion emission based on normal momentum exceeding about 1/4 GeV/c. They compute the probability of finding a density fluctuation of N nucleons within a small volume in the nuclear ground state using a non-interacting gas model, and derive the minimum seed radius required for net energy growth as a function of beam energy and ignition temperature.

## Key numbers

- Plasma formation expected at about 15 GeV/nucleon uranium beam energy on a stationary target, or 2.7 GeV/nucleon in colliding beams (from abstract).
- Normal nuclear density taken as ρ₀ = 1/6 fm⁻³ (Section 2).
- For a numerical example: spherical plasma droplet of radius R = 4 fm, surface temperature T = 180 MeV, μ_q/T = 1 gives energy density 2.1 GeV/fm³, baryon density ~0.5/fm³ (about 3 ρ₀), total available energy ~400 GeV (Section 3).
- Energy loss rate for that example: dE^R/dt = A × 0.5 GeV/fm² × 10²³ s⁻¹, and over the first 10⁻²³ s the energy loss is 100 GeV (Section 3).
- For A ≈ 200, probability > 10⁻² per colliding nucleus to find a nine-quark cluster (N = 3) at substantial compression (Section 4).
- Mean free path of a quark in the seed: ℓ ~ 0.4 fm, using σ_q_seed ≈ 30 mb and ρ_seed ≈ two to three times 3ρ₀ (Section 4).
- For plasma at 2 GeV/fm³ energy density (~6 particles/fm³), mean free path λ < 0.1 fm (Section 4).
- At T_I ~ 150–160 MeV, beam energy of 10 to 20 GeV/nucleon suffices for plasma ignition (Section 5).
- Required temperature gradient at the surface of the numerical example: ∂T/∂r = 10–15 MeV/fm, leading to a center-to-surface temperature differential of ~50 MeV for a 4 fm radius (Section 5).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Busza and Goldhaber (1984) on nuclear stopping power in p-nucleus collisions at 100 GeV, which found that heavy nuclei lose about 2.8 units of rapidity rather than one, contradicting the baryon-free central region predicted by hadronic cascade calculations. The paper introduces a "plasma seed" mechanism to reconcile nuclear transparency with the opacity needed for plasma formation, and provides a framework for estimating the minimum beam energy and seed size required for baryon-rich plasma formation, enabling future experimental searches at moderate energies.
