# Baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The quark rearrangement model for baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction ($B\bar B\leftrightarrow 3M$) - incorporated in the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach - is extended to the strangeness sector. A derivation of the transition probabilities for the three-body processes is presented and a strangeness suppression factor for the invariant matrix element squared is introduced to account for the higher mass of the strange quark compared to the light up and down quarks. In simulations of the baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reformation in a box with periodic boundary conditions we demonstrate that our numerical implementation fulfills detailed balance on a channel-by-channel basis for more than 2000 individual $2 \leftrightarrow 3$ channels. Furthermore, we study central Pb+Pb collisions within PHSD from 11.7$A$ GeV to 158$A$ GeV and investigate the impact of the additionally implemented reaction channels in the strangeness sector. We find that the new reaction channels have a visible impact essentially only on the rapidity spectra of antibaryons. The spectra with the additional channels in the strangeness sector are closer to the experimental data than without for all antihyperons. Due to the chemical redistribution between baryons/antibaryons and mesons we find a slightly larger production of antiprotons thus moderately overestimating the available experimental data. We additionally address the question if the antibaryon spectra (with strangeness) from central heavy-ion reactions at these energies provide further information on the issue of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement. However, by comparing transport results with/without partonic phase as well as including/excluding effects from chiral symmetry restoration we find no convincing signals in the strange antibaryon sector for either transition due to the strong final-state interactions.

## Key claim

The quark rearrangement model for baryon-antibaryon annihilation and reproduction ($B\bar B \leftrightarrow 3M$) is extended to the strangeness sector, but the new reaction channels have a visible impact essentially only on the rapidity spectra of antibaryons, and the authors find no convincing signals in the strange antibaryon sector for either chiral symmetry restoration or deconfinement due to strong final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors extended the quark rearrangement model (QRM) for baryon-antibaryon annihilation and recreation to include strangeness, introducing a strangeness suppression factor $\lambda = 0.5$ for the invariant matrix element squared to account for the higher strange quark mass. They implemented the $2 \leftrightarrow 3$ reactions in the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, using an in-cell method for the three-meson fusion processes, and validated the implementation by checking detailed balance on a channel-by-channel basis in box simulations with periodic boundary conditions. They then simulated central Pb+Pb collisions from 11.7$A$ GeV to 158$A$ GeV and compared the results with and without the strangeness sector channels, as well as with and without partonic phase and chiral symmetry restoration effects.

## Key numbers

- The strangeness suppression factor for the transition matrix element squared is set to $\lambda = 0.5$, motivated by PYTHIA simulations.
- Box simulations with volume ~18000 fm$^3$, energy density $\epsilon = 0.4$ GeV/fm$^3$, net baryon density $\rho_B \approx 0.2$ fm$^{-3}$, and baryon-to-antibaryon ratio 2:1 were used for validation.
- Detailed balance is fulfilled better than 97% on a channel-by-channel basis for the 100 most dominant channels across 100 investigated systems.
- The critical energy density for the hadron-parton transition is $\epsilon_c \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$, as provided by lattice QCD calculations.
- Central Pb+Pb collisions were simulated from 11.7$A$ GeV to 158$A$ GeV.
- The three-body phase-space integrals $R_3$ were fitted using the functional form $R_3(t,m_1,m_2,m_3)=a_1 t^{a_2}\left( 1-\frac{1}{a_3 t+1+a_4}\right)$ with $t=\sqrt s -m_1-m_2-m_3$.
- The in-cell method used time step $dt = 4$ fm/c and volume element $dV = 40$ fm$^3$ for the box simulations.

## Context

This work extends the quark rearrangement model for baryon-antibaryon annihilation and recreation previously developed by Cassing (2001) for nonstrange particles to the full strangeness sector (SU(3)). It builds on the PHSD transport approach, which incorporates a partonic phase via the Dynamical-Quasi-Particle-Model (DQPM) fitted to lattice QCD results, dynamical hadronization, and aspects of chiral symmetry restoration in the hadronic phase as developed by Ales and collaborators. The study addresses whether strange antibaryon spectra from heavy-ion collisions can provide signals for chiral symmetry restoration or deconfinement, but finds that strong final-state interactions obscure such signals, enabling future work to focus on other observables or higher energies where partonic effects may be more distinguishable.
