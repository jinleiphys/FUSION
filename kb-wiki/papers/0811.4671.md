# New supersymmetric quartet of nuclei in the A=190 mass region

> We present evidence for a new supersymmetric quartet in the A=190 region of the nuclear mass table. New experimental information on transfer and neutron capture reactions to the odd-odd nucleaus 194 Ir strongly suggests the existence of a new supersymmetric quartet, consisting of the 192,193 Os and 193,194 Ir nuclei. We make explicit predictions for the odd-neutron nucleus 193 Os, and suggest that its spectroscopic properties be measured in dedicated experiments.

## Key claim

New experimental data on the odd-odd nucleus 194Ir from transfer and neutron capture reactions strongly suggests the existence of a new supersymmetric quartet in the A=190 mass region, consisting of 192,193Os and 193,194Ir, with the caveat that the odd-neutron nucleus 193Os has not yet been measured in sufficient detail and its predicted spectroscopic properties require dedicated experimental verification.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous fit of the energy formula for U(6/12)nu ⊗ U(6/4)pi supersymmetry to the known energy levels of 192Os, 193Ir, and 194Ir, using a single set of six parameters (A, B, B', C, D, E). The fit incorporated new experimental information from polarized (d,alpha) transfer reactions and neutron capture on 194Ir, which led to revised level assignments compared to previous work by Jolie and Garrett. The resulting parameter set was then used to predict the energy spectrum of the unmeasured odd-neutron nucleus 193Os, with level assignments guided by preliminary data from the 192Os(d,p)193Os one-neutron transfer reaction.

## Key numbers

- The simultaneous fit to 192Os, 193Ir, and 194Ir yields parameters: A = 41.0 keV, B = -6.0 keV, B' = -29.0 keV, C = 38.0 keV, D = 6.3 keV, E = 4.5 keV
- The ratio R for the stripping reaction 192Os → 193Os is calculated as R = 37.8, indicating most of the one-neutron transfer strength goes to the [7,1], (7,1,0) band doublet
- The J^P = 3/2- ground state of 193Os is populated very weakly in the (d,p) reaction, while the first excited state at 41 keV is not seen at all
- The j=1/2 strength in 193Os goes to the state at 234 keV, while j=3/2 and j=5/2 transfers go predominantly to states at 103 keV and 73 keV, respectively
- For the previously studied pair 192Os-193Ir, the rotational levels were described by C = 40 keV and D+E = 10 keV (Bars, Balantekin, and Iachello, 1981)
- For 194Ir, the rotational levels were described by C = 35.1 keV, D = 6.3 keV, and E = 4.5 keV, with vibrational terms A+B = 35 keV and B' = -33.6 keV

## Context

This work extends the nuclear supersymmetry framework previously established for the Pt-Au quartet (194,195Pt and 195,196Au), which was theoretically predicted by the quartet formalism and experimentally verified in 1999 by Metz, Groger, and Wirth. The new Os-Ir quartet shares the same U(6/12)nu ⊗ U(6/4)pi supersymmetry as the Pt-Au quartet, with the key difference that the odd proton occupies the 2d3/2 orbit of the 50-82 shell and the odd neutron occupies the 3p1/2, 3p3/2, and 2f5/2 orbits of the 82-126 shell. The parameter values obtained are closer to those of the Pt-Au quartet than previous fits by Jolie and Garrett, suggesting systematic behavior across this region of the nuclear chart. The predictions for 193Os enable future experimental tests via dedicated one-neutron transfer reactions and provide a foundation for studying two-proton transfer experiments between nuclei in the two quartets.
