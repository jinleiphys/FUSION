# Light-Heavy Ion Collisions: A window into pre-equilibrium QCD dynamics?

> Relativistic collisions of light on heavy ions (p+Au at sqrt(s)=7.7 GeV, p+Au, d+Au,3He+Au at sqrt(s)=62.4 GeV and 200 GeV and p+Pb, 3He+Pb at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV) are simulated using "superSONIC", a model that includes pre-equilibrium flow, viscous hydrodynamics and a hadronic cascade afterburner. Even though these systems have strong gradients and only consist of at most a few tens of charged particles per unit rapidity, one finds evidence that a hydrodynamic description applies to these systems. Based on these simulations, the presence of a triangular flow component in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is predicted to be similar in magnitude to that found in 3He+Au collisions. Furthermore, the v3(p_T) ratio of 3He+Au to d+Au is found to be sensitive to the presence of pre-equilibrium flow. This would imply an experimentally accessible window into pre-equilibrium QCD dynamics using light-heavy ion collisions.

# Light-Heavy Ion Collisions: A Window into Pre-equilibrium QCD Dynamics?

## Key claim

Viscous hydrodynamics may still be applicable to describe collective flow in light-heavy ion collisions even for systems as small as p+Au at sqrt(s)=7.7 GeV with only about 4.5 charged particles per unit rapidity, but the applicability is a gradual process rather than a yes/no question. The ratio of triangular flow v3 in 3He+Au to d+Au collisions is predicted to be sensitive to the presence of pre-equilibrium flow, offering an experimentally accessible probe of pre-equilibrium QCD dynamics.

## Method

The author used the "superSONIC" simulation package, which combines pre-equilibrium flow (modeled from strong-coupling N=4 SYM results), event-by-event Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, 2+1d relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (VH2+1 code), and a hadronic cascade afterburner (B3D). For each collision system, 100 hydrodynamic events were generated with 100,000 B3D events per hydrodynamic event, and the systematic uncertainty from higher-order gradient corrections was estimated by varying the second-order transport coefficient C_eta by 50 percent around the reference value of 3.

## Key numbers

- p+Au at sqrt(s)=7.7 GeV: predicted charged particle multiplicity dN_ch/deta = 4.5, mean pT = 0.550(6) GeV with pre-equilibrium flow
- d+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: simulated dN_ch/deta = 20, matching experimental value 20.3 +/- 1.7 (0-5% centrality)
- 3He+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: simulated dN_ch/deta = 27 +/- 1, mean pT = 0.567(2) GeV with pre-equilibrium flow
- p+Pb at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV: simulated dN_ch/deta = 39 +/- 1, mean pT = 0.716(6) GeV with eta/s=0.16 and pre-equilibrium flow
- 3He+Pb at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV: simulated dN_ch/deta = 74, mean pT = 0.676 GeV
- p+Au at sqrt(s)=7.7 GeV: predicted v2(2.5 GeV pT) approximately 5-8 percent
- p+Al at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: simulated dN_ch/deta = 5.4, mean pT = 0.575(6) GeV with pre-equilibrium flow
- Hydrodynamic starting time tau_0 = 0.25 fm/c, switching temperature T_SW = 0.17 GeV, Gaussian width w = 0.4 fm for participant nucleons
- Maximum temperature probed: p+Pb at 5.02 TeV reaches T less than 0.53 GeV (mean T = 0.4 GeV), p+Au at 7.7 GeV only reaches T less than 0.33 GeV (mean T = 0.25 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the SONIC model of Nagle et al. (2013) by adding event-by-event capability and pre-equilibrium flow, and builds on the earlier hydrodynamic studies of p+Pb and d+Pb collisions by Bozek (2011). It contrasts with the IP-Glasma approach of Schenke and Venugopalan (2014), which found little flow for p+Pb collisions in disagreement with experiment, and with the work of Kozlov et al. (2014) who used 3+1d hydrodynamics without pre-equilibrium flow or hadronic afterburner. The study enables experimental tests of pre-equilibrium QCD dynamics through v3 measurements in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, which had not been measured at the time of publication, and suggests that light-heavy ion collisions can probe QCD transport properties near the phase transition temperature.
