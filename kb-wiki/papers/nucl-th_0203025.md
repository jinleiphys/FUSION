# Nuclear re-interaction effects in quasi-elastic neutrino nucleus scattering

> The quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross section has been calculated by using a Fermi gas model corrected to consider the re-scattering between the emitted nucleon and the rest nucleus. As an example of the relevance of this effect we show results for the muon production cross section on 16O target.

# Nuclear re-interaction effects in quasi-elastic neutrino nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The commonly used Fermi gas model for quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering neglects final state interactions (FSI) between the emitted nucleon and the residual nucleus, and including these effects produces significant modifications to the double differential cross section at neutrino energies below 5.0 GeV, though the effect becomes negligible at 5.0 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections using a Fermi gas model based on the Smith and Moniz approach, then incorporated final state interactions via a folding integral where the response without FSI is convolved with a Lorentzian function whose width and shift are derived from optical potentials that reproduce elastic nucleon-nucleus scattering data. Results were calculated for muon production on an 16O target at neutrino energies of 0.2, 0.5, 1.0, and 5.0 GeV, with the muon emission angle fixed at 30 degrees for the double differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum value of 216 MeV/c used for the Fermi gas model, obtained from the average momentum prescription of Amaro et al. (1994)
- For E_nu = 5.0 GeV, FSI effects are negligible, with only a small shift in peak position due to effective nucleon mass
- At E_nu = 0.5 GeV, the Fermi gas response shows a linear behavior at high muon energies (small nuclear excitation energies) due to Pauli blocking of some particle-hole transitions
- For E_nu = 0.2 GeV, the cross sections integrated over muon energy as a function of emission angle are remarkably different between calculations with and without FSI
- The quasi-elastic region is identified with nuclear excitation energies from 50 to 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Smith and Moniz (1972) Fermi gas model by addressing its neglect of nucleon re-interaction with the residual nucleus, an approximation that had never been thoroughly tested. The authors build on comparisons between Fermi gas and Continuum Shell model results by Amaro et al. (1994), which showed that an appropriate Fermi momentum value yields good agreement between the two models for electron scattering. The FSI model itself follows the approach of Co' et al. (1988) and uses the spreading width evaluated by Drozdz et al. (1987). The results enable more realistic neutrino-nucleus cross section calculations for detector simulations, particularly for neutrino energies below 5 GeV, and the authors note that the FSI effects are independent of the excitation probe, applying also to electron and tau neutrino reactions on any nuclear system.
