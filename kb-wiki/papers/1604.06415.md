# Energy, centrality and momentum dependence of dielectron production at collider energies in a coarse-grained transport approach

> Dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at collider energies - i.e., for the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) - is studied within an approach that uses coarse-grained transport simulations to calculate thermal dilepton emission applying in-medium spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory and partonic production rates based on lattice calculations. The microscopic output from the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model is hereby put on a grid of space-time cells which allows to extract the local temperature and chemical potential in each cell via an equation of state. The resulting dilepton spectra are in good agreement with the experimental results for the range of RHIC energies, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6 - 200$ GeV. The comparison of data and model outcome shows that the newest measurements by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations are consistent and that the low-mass spectra can be described by a cocktail of hadronic decay contributions together with thermal emission from broadened vector-meson spectral functions and from the Quark-Gluon Plasma phase. Predictions for dilepton results at LHC energies show no significant change of the spectra as compared to RHIC, but a higher fraction of thermal contribution and harder slopes of the transverse momentum distributions due to the higher temperatures and flow obtained.

## Key claim

The coarse-grained transport approach, which uses purely hadronic UrQMD dynamics combined with a lattice equation of state for high temperatures, can reproduce experimental dilepton spectra at RHIC energies (√sNN = 19.6–200 GeV) despite lacking a microscopic description of the partonic phase, but the model's fireball evolution cannot reflect effects from Quark-Gluon Plasma creation on the microscopic level.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD hadronic transport model to simulate heavy-ion collisions, then coarse-grained the microscopic output onto a space-time grid to extract local energy density and net-baryon density in each cell. They applied a hadron gas equation of state for temperatures below 170 MeV and a lattice QCD equation of state for higher temperatures to obtain local temperature and baryochemical potential, then calculated thermal dilepton emission using in-medium vector-meson spectral functions from hadronic many-body theory for the low-mass region and lattice-based partonic rates for the high-mass region. Non-thermal contributions from π0, η, and φ meson decays were added separately.

## Key numbers

- RHIC energy range studied: √sNN = 19.6–200 GeV
- LHC energy studied: √sNN = 2.76 TeV (Pb+Pb) and up to 5500 GeV
- Critical temperature used for phase transition: Tc = 170 MeV
- Hadronic rates applied for invariant masses M < 1 GeV/c²
- UrQMD model includes all meson and baryon resonances up to mass 2.2 GeV/c²
- At top RHIC energy (√sNN = 200 GeV), the transport model reaches only roughly 60% of the measured elliptic flow v2 value
- The shear viscosity to entropy ratio from UrQMD box calculations yields η/s > 0.6

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach previously applied at SIS 18, FAIR, and SPS energies (Endres 2014, Endres 2015) to collider energies at RHIC and LHC. It provides a compromise between purely hydrodynamic models (Vujanovic 2013, Rapp 2013) and fully microscopic transport descriptions (Schmidt 2008, Bratkovskaya 2013), enabling systematic comparison of thermal dilepton emission from hadronic and partonic sources across a wide energy range. The results serve as a baseline for future studies of charm contributions and medium modifications of D mesons at collider energies.
