# Phase transition amplification of proton number fluctuations in nuclear collisions from a transport model approach

> The time evolution of particle number fluctuations in nuclear collisions at intermediate energies ($E_{\rm lab} = 1.23-10A$ GeV) is studied by means of the UrQMD-3.5 transport model. The transport description incorporates baryonic interactions through a density-dependent potential. This allows for an implementation of a first order phase transition including a mechanically unstable region at large baryon density. The scaled variance of the baryon and proton number distributions is calculated in the central cubic spatial volume of the collisions at different times. A significant enhancement of fluctuations associated with the unstable region is observed. This enhancement persists to late times reflecting a memory effect for the fluctuations. The presence of the phase transition has a much smaller influence on the observable event-by-event fluctuations of protons in momentum space.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a first-order phase transition in nuclear collisions at intermediate energies (E_lab = 1.23–10A GeV) produces a significant enhancement of baryon number fluctuations in coordinate space, and this enhancement persists to late times as a memory effect; however, the abstract glosses over the key qualifier that this enhancement is almost completely washed out when fluctuations are analyzed in momentum space, making the signal undetectable in experimental observables.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD-3.5 transport model with a density-dependent mean-field potential to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 1.23 to 10A GeV. Two equations of state were compared: the standard CMF (Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field) model and a modified version (PT) that introduces an additional first-order phase transition by truncating the mean-field potential at n_B^cut = 2.6 n_0 and interpolating with a polynomial to create a mechanically unstable spinodal region. The scaled variance of baryon and proton number distributions was calculated inside a central cubic spatial volume of 27 fm^3 as a function of collision time, and final-state proton cumulants were computed as functions of rapidity acceptance interval Δy.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear matter saturation density is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}; the PT model truncates the potential at n_B^cut = 2.6 n_0.
- The scaled variance of baryon number at E_lab = 2A GeV is enhanced by a factor of 2 at 2 n_0 in the PT scenario compared to the CMF scenario.
- At n_B = 1.2 n_0 (late stages), a noticeable difference in baryon number distributions persists between PT and CMF due to the memory effect.
- The scaled variance of final-state protons ω[p] at E_lab = 2A GeV ranges from approximately 1 to 1.04 at small Δy ≈ 0.2 and decreases monotonically at larger Δy, with no significant difference between PT and CMF cases.
- For the CMF EoS, the nuclear incompressibility is K_0 = 267 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous fluid-dynamical predictions of baryon number fluctuation amplification due to a first-order phase transition (Steinheimer, 2012; Steinheimer, 2013) by using a transport model (UrQMD) that treats dynamical evolution of fluctuations in phase space consistently. The memory effect for fluctuations was previously studied in hydrodynamics with explicit fluctuation evolution (Pradeep, 2022) or cumulant evolution equations (Mukherjee, 2015). The paper finds that the large baryon number fluctuations reported by the HADES Collaboration (2020) in Au+Au collisions at E_lab = 1.23A GeV are unlikely to result from spinodal amplification due to a phase transition, and suggests an alternative explanation was given in Savchuk (2022). The results enable future studies incorporating light nuclei and nuclear fragment production, which are not included in the current UrQMD simulations.
