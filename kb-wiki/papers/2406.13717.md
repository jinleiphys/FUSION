# Beyond modified Urca: the nucleon width approximation for flavor-changing processes in dense matter

> Flavor-changing charged current ("Urca") processes are of central importance in the astrophysics of neutron stars. Standard calculations approximate the Urca rate as the sum of two contributions, direct Urca and modified Urca. Attempts to make modified Urca calculations more accurate have been impeded by an unphysical divergence at the direct Urca threshold density. In this paper we describe a systematically improvable approach where, in the simplest approximation, instead of modified Urca we include an imaginary part of the nucleon mass (nucleon width). The total Urca rate is then obtained via a straightforward generalization of the direct Urca calculation, yielding results that agree with both direct and modified Urca at the densities where those approximations are valid. At low densities, we observe an enhancement of the rate by more than an order of magnitude, with important ramifications for neutron star cooling and other transport properties.

## Key claim

The nucleon width approximation (NWA) provides a systematically improvable method for calculating total Urca rates that avoids the unphysical divergence at the direct Urca threshold density that plagues improved modified Urca calculations, while agreeing with both direct and modified Urca results where those approximations are valid.

## Method

The authors replace the standard approach of summing separate direct Urca and modified Urca rates with a single calculation where the nucleon propagators include an imaginary contribution to the mass (nucleon width). The total Urca rate is obtained by "smearing" the direct Urca rate over a range of nucleon masses using Breit-Wigner spectral functions, with the nucleon widths taken from Brueckner theory calculations. The calculation uses the IUF equation of state and evaluates the full 4D phase space integral for the direct Urca rate with relativistic matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- At low densities (below the direct Urca threshold), the NWA predicts an enhancement of the Urca rate by "more than an order of magnitude" compared to standard modified Urca calculations
- The nucleon widths used are of order T²/(5 MeV), and for T ≲ 10 MeV are no larger than the intrinsic violation of isospin symmetry
- The NWA calculation at n_B = n_sat (0.16 fm⁻³) and n_B = 6 n_sat shows the expected T⁵ power-law behavior for direct Urca and T⁷ behavior for modified Urca at low temperatures
- The improved modified Urca calculation (with internal nucleon propagator) diverges at the direct Urca threshold density, while NWA smoothly matches to direct Urca

## Context

This work extends the standard Friman and Maxwell (1979) modified Urca calculation, which used the Fermi surface approximation and neglected neutrino momentum. It addresses the unphysical divergence identified by Shternin (2018) when using a more accurate internal nucleon propagator, which required a phenomenological infrared cutoff in the work of Suleiman et al. (2023). The NWA enables consistent calculations of total Urca rates at finite temperature, for non-equilibrium neutrino distributions, strong magnetic fields, and other scenarios beyond the scope of standard modified Urca calculations, with applications to neutron star cooling and transport in neutron star mergers.
