# Soft gluon effects in the extraction of higher twists at large Bjorken x

> Existing data on the (unpolarised) transverse structure function of the proton are analyzed for large values of the Bjorken variable x. The leading-twist and a phenomenological higher-twist contributions are simultaneously determined from a power correction analysis of the Nachtmann moments for values of the squared four-momentum transfer between ~ 1 and 20 (GeV/c)**2. The results obtained adopting the next-to-leading order approximation and those including the effects of soft gluon resummation are compared. The sensitivity of the extraction of large-x higher twists to high-order radiative corrections as well as to the value of alphas(MZ) is illustrated.

## Key claim

The extraction of higher-twist contributions from the proton's transverse structure function at large Bjorken x is highly sensitive to both the inclusion of soft gluon resummation effects and the value of \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2)\); the next-to-leading order (NLO) approximation for the leading twist leads to a strong overestimate of the extracted higher twists at large x, whereas including soft gluon resummation and using the updated PDG value \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.118\) reduces the total higher twist contribution to no more than about 30% of the leading twist for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 2\) (GeV/c)^2.

## Method

The author performed power correction analyses of the Nachtmann moments \(M_n(Q^2)\) of the unpolarised transverse proton structure function, constructed from world data in DIS kinematics and the nucleon-resonance production region for \(Q^2\) between ~1 and 20 (GeV/c)^2. The leading-twist contribution was computed both at NLO and including soft gluon resummation (SGR) via exponentiation in moment space, and the twist-4 and twist-6 parameters were simultaneously determined from a \(\chi^2\)-minimization fit to the twist expansion. The analyses were carried out for two values of \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2)\), namely 0.113 and 0.118, to assess the sensitivity of the higher-twist extraction.

## Key numbers

- The Nachtmann moments were analyzed for \(Q^2\) between ~1 and 20 (GeV/c)^2, using data up to \(x \simeq 0.98\) including the elastic peak contribution.
- For the SGR approach with \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.118\), the total higher twist contribution does not exceed ~30% of the leading twist for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 2\) (GeV/c)^2.
- Within the NLO approximation, the extracted higher twists for moment order \(n \geq 6\) can exceed 50% of the leading twist at \(Q^2 \sim\) few (GeV/c)^2 for both \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.113\) and \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.118\).
- The analysis used a reference scale \(Q_0^2 = 10\) (GeV/c)^2 for the twist expansion.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ricco (1999) by comparing NLO and soft gluon resummation treatments of the leading twist, and by testing the sensitivity to the value of \(\alpha_s(M_Z^2)\) (0.113 from Virchaux versus 0.118 from the PDG summary). The results show that previous NLO-based extractions of higher twists at large x, such as those by Kataev, Alekhin, and Virchaux, are not reliable because they overestimate the higher-twist contribution. The findings enable more accurate determinations of multiparton correlations in the nucleon and of the d- to u-quark ratio near \(x \simeq 1\), and they highlight the need for future systematic measurements to confirm the \(Q^2\)-behaviour of the Nachtmann moments.
