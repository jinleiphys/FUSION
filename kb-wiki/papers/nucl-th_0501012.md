# IMF isotopic properties in semi-peripheral collisions at Fermi energies

> We study the neutron and proton dynamical behavior along the fragmentation path in semi-peripheral collisions: 58Fe+58Fe (charge asymmetric, N/Z = 1.23) and 58Ni+58Ni (charge symmetric, N/Z = 1.07), at 47 AMeV. We observe that isospin dynamics processes take place also in the charge-symmetric system 58Ni+58Ni, that may produce more asymmetric fragments. A neutron enrichment of the neck fragments is observed, resulting from the interplay between pre-equilibrium emission and the phenomenon of "isospin-migration". Both effects depend on the EoS (Equation of State) symmetry term. This point is illustrated by comparing the results obtained with two different choices of the symmetry energy density dependence. New correlation observables are suggested, to study the reaction mechanism and the isospin dynamics.

## Key claim

Isospin dynamics processes, including neutron enrichment of neck fragments, occur even in charge-symmetric systems like 58Ni+58Ni (N/Z = 1.07) that initially have no isospin gradients, due to density gradients that develop during the reaction and the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed stochastic Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport simulations of 58Fe+58Fe (N/Z = 1.23) and 58Ni+58Ni (N/Z = 1.07) collisions at 47 AMeV with reduced impact parameter b_r = 0.5. They compared results using two different parameterizations of the symmetry energy density dependence: an "asy-stiff" EoS (linearly increasing with density) and an "asy-soft" EoS (saturating near normal density). Fragment properties were analyzed at freeze-out time, including N/Z ratios, masses, velocities, and emission angles.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 47 AMeV for both reactions
- Reduced impact parameter: b_r = 0.5 (semi-peripheral)
- Initial N/Z: 1.23 for 58Fe+58Fe, 1.07 for 58Ni+58Ni
- For the asy-stiff EoS, 40% of events produce at least one IMF in the neck region (ternary events)
- In 58Fe+58Fe, pre-equilibrium emission removes 14 neutrons and 11 protons (by t = 100 fm/c), changing the di-nuclear asymmetry from 1.23 to 1.22
- In 58Ni+58Ni, pre-equilibrium emission removes 13 protons and 12 neutrons, changing the di-nuclear asymmetry from 1.07 to 1.12
- For 58Fe+58Fe ternary events, residual PLF/TLF N/Z changes from 1.22 (at t = 100 fm/c) to 1.19 (at t = 200 fm/c)
- For 58Ni+58Ni ternary events, residual PLF/TLF N/Z changes from 1.12 (at t = 100 fm/c) to 1.125 (at t = 200 fm/c)
- For 58Ni+58Ni binary events, residual N/Z reaches 1.17 at t = 200 fm/c

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin dynamics in nuclear fragmentation, which previously focused on central collisions and spinodal decomposition (see the review by Chomaz, Colonna, and Randrup). It contrasts with isospin diffusion and N/Z equilibration processes in peripheral collisions investigated by Tsang (2004) and Souliotis (2004), which are driven by isospin gradients rather than density gradients. The results suggest new correlation observables between IMF mass, neutron content, and alignment relative to the PLF-TLF axis that can probe the density dependence of the symmetry energy, and the authors note that some evidence for these effects has been found in experimental data from Milazzo (2002), Gingras (2002), and Moustabchir (2003) on 58Ni-induced fragmentation.
