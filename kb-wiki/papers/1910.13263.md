# New evaluation of axial nucleon form factor from electron- and neutrino-scattering data and impact on neutrino-nucleus cross-section

> A joint fit to neutrino-nucleon scattering and pion electroproduction data is performed to evaluate the nucleon axial form factor in the two-component model consisting of a three-quark intrinsic structure surrounded by a meson cloud. Further constrains on the model are obtained by re-evaluating the electromagnetic form factor using electron scattering data. The results of the axial form factor show sizable differences with respect to the widely used dipole model. The impact of such changes on the Charged-Current Quasi-Elastic neutrino-nucleus cross-section is evaluated in the SuSAv2 nuclear model, based on the Relativistic Mean Field and including the contribution of two-body currents. How the different parametrizations of the axial form factor affect the cross-section prediction is assessed in full details and comparisons to recent T2K and MINERvA data are presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the two-component model (three-quark core plus meson cloud) for the nucleon axial form factor, fitted jointly to neutrino-nucleon scattering and pion electroproduction data, yields a form factor that differs significantly from the standard dipole model, up to 15-30% around 3 GeV², but the exact result depends on which theoretical interpretation of the pion electroproduction data (e.g., Soft Pion, PCAC, Dombey-Read) is adopted, introducing a systematic uncertainty larger than the statistical one.

## Method

The authors performed a joint fit of the axial form factor in the two-component model (Iachello-Jackson-Lande model) to neutrino-deuterium scattering data and pion electroproduction data, using a likelihood that includes penalty terms to enforce constraints from a re-evaluated fit of the electromagnetic form factors to electron scattering data and from chiral perturbation theory loop corrections. The resulting axial form factor parametrizations were then used as input to the SuSAv2 nuclear model (based on Relativistic Mean Field and including two-body meson-exchange currents) to compute charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross-sections on carbon, which were compared to T2K and MINERvA data.

## Key numbers

* The axial meson mass from (anti)neutrino scattering alone is M_A = (1.026 ± 0.021) GeV, while from electroproduction experiments it is M_A = (1.069 ± 0.018) GeV, leading to an axial radius difference of about 5%.
* The axial radius from the two-component model is √⟨r_A²⟩ = 0.60 fm for the original Iachello-Jackson-Lande model and 0.62 fm for the Bijker (2004) update, compared to 0.64 fm (M_A = 1.069 GeV) from electroproduction and 0.67 fm (M_A = 1.026 GeV) from neutrino scattering.
* The γ parameter from the electromagnetic fit is γ = 0.529 ± 0.004 GeV⁻² (statistical) and ±0.093 GeV⁻² after error inflation.
* For the joint fit using Dombey-Read pion data, the neutrino-scattering parameters are α_ν = 0.95 ± 0.04 and γ_ν = 0.53 ± 0.01 GeV⁻², with χ²/NDOF = 1.79 (46 degrees of freedom).
* The two-component model gives a larger axial form factor than the dipole below about 1 GeV² (up to 5% difference) and a smaller form factor above 1 GeV², with differences of 15-30% around 3 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the two-component model of Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973) for electromagnetic form factors to the axial sector, updating the electromagnetic fit of Bijker (2004) with additional data and QCD logarithmic corrections. It addresses the "M_A puzzle" identified by MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo, 2010) by quantifying the axial form factor uncertainty separately from nuclear effects, which were shown by Martini (2009), Amaro (2010), and Nieves (2011) to be the main source of the discrepancy. The results enable more precise predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, MINERvA, and DUNE by providing a form factor parametrization with quantified systematic uncertainties from different theoretical interpretations of pion electroproduction data.
