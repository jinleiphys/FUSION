# Collision Thermalization of Nucleons in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We consider a possible mechanism of thermalization of nucleons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Our model belongs, to a certain degree, to the transport ones; we investigate the evolution of the system created in nucleus-nucleus collision, but we parametrize this development by the number of collisions of every particle during evolution rather than by the time variable. We based on the assumption that the nucleon momentum transfer after several nucleon-nucleon (-hadron) elastic and inelastic collisions becomes a random quantity driven by a proper distribution. This randomization results in a smearing of the nucleon momenta about their initial values and, as a consequence, in their partial isotropization and thermalization. The trial evaluation is made in the framework of a toy model. We show that the proposed scheme can be used for extraction of the physical information from experimental data on nucleon rapidity distribution.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that thermalization of nucleons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be described by parametrizing the evolution of the system by the number of collisions each particle experiences rather than by time, and that this scheme can be used to extract physical information from experimental rapidity distributions, but the evaluation is made only in the framework of a toy model and is limited to AGS and low SPS energies where the number of created nucleon-antinucleon pairs is much less than the number of net nucleons.

## Method

The authors separate final-state nucleons into two groups: those that underwent only hadron reactions and those created during hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). They partition the hadron-reaction nucleons into ensembles according to the number of effective collisions M each nucleon experienced before freeze-out, where the effective collision number is derived from the total sum of random degrees of freedom gained in all physical collisions divided by three. The momentum transfer after each effective collision is treated as a random quantity, and the single-particle spectrum for each M-th ensemble is constructed using a multi-scattering form-factor obtained from the Fourier transform of the single-collision momentum-transfer form-factor, with the total nucleon spectrum given by a sum over ensembles plus a thermal QGP contribution.

## Key numbers

* The mean value of the momentum transfer squared from UrQMD simulations at AGS energies yields an estimate of q_max approximately equal to 800 MeV from the relation ⟨q²⟩ = (3/5) q_max².
* In the non-relativistic limit, the effective temperature is given by T_eff = 1/(β + β_coll(M)) where β_coll(M) = 3m/(M⟨q²⟩), and the collective velocity is v_h = [1/(1 + β/β_coll(M))] (k_0/m).
* The number of random degrees of freedom per particle in an inelastic collision with N_π pions created is d_p = 3 - 4/(2 + N_π), reaching a maximum of d_p = 3 when N_π is large.

## Context

This work extends transport model approaches by replacing the time variable with the number of collisions per particle, building on the framework of relativistic hydrodynamics initiated by Landau (1953) and the discovery at Brookhaven National Laboratory that the created matter behaves as a perfect fluid (Gyulassy 2004, Kapusta 2007). The model enables extraction of the "nucleon power" of the created QGP (P_QGP^(N) = N_QGP/N_total) from experimental rapidity distributions, and the authors demonstrate a fitting procedure using maximum entropy method with data from the E802 Collaboration (1999) at AGS energies.
