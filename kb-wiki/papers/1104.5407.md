# Correlations between the nuclear breathing mode energy and properties of asymmetric nuclear matter

> Based on microscopic Hartree-Fock + random phase approximation calculations with Skyrme interactions, we study the correlations between the nuclear breathing mode energy $E_{ISGMR}$ and properties of asymmetric nuclear matter with a recently developed analysis method. Our results indicate that the $E_{ISGMR}$ of $^{208}$Pb exhibits moderate correlations with the density slope $L$ of the symmetry energy and the isoscalar nucleon effective mass $m_{s,0}^{\ast}$ besides a strong dependence on the incompressibility $K_{0}$ of symmetric nuclear matter. Using the present empirical values of $L=60\pm 30$ MeV and $m_{s,0}^{\ast}=(0.8\pm 0.1)m$, we obtain a theoretical uncertainty of about $\pm 16$ MeV for the extraction of $K_{0}$ from the $E_{ISGMR}$ of $^{208}$Pb. Furthermore, we find the $E_{ISGMR}$ difference between $^{100}$Sn and $^{132}$Sn strongly correlates with $L$ and thus provides a potentially useful probe of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim
The extraction of the incompressibility \(K_0\) of symmetric nuclear matter from the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) centroid energy of \(^{208}\)Pb carries a theoretical uncertainty of about \(\pm 16\) MeV when the present empirical uncertainties of the density slope \(L\) of the symmetry energy and the isoscalar nucleon effective mass \(m_{s,0}^{\ast}\) are taken into account, assuming these two uncertainties are independent.

## Method
The authors performed microscopic Hartree-Fock (HF) plus continuum random phase approximation (RPA) calculations using Skyrme interactions for the spherical doubly-magic nuclei \(^{208}\)Pb, \(^{100}\)Sn, and \(^{132}\)Sn. Instead of varying the nine Skyrme parameters directly, they expressed them analytically in terms of nine macroscopic quantities (e.g., saturation density \(\rho_0\), incompressibility \(K_0\), symmetry energy slope \(L\), isoscalar effective mass \(m_{s,0}^{\ast}\)) and varied each macroscopic quantity individually within its empirical range to study correlations with the ISGMR centroid energy.

## Key numbers
- The ISGMR centroid energy \(E_{ave}\) of \(^{208}\)Pb obtained with the default MSL0 parameter set is 14.453 MeV, compared to the experimental value of \(14.17 \pm 0.28\) MeV from Youngblood et al. (1999).
- Using \(L = 60 \pm 30\) MeV gives an uncertainty of about \(\pm 0.281\) MeV for the ISGMR centroid energy in \(^{208}\)Pb.
- Using \(m_{s,0}^{\ast} = (0.8 \pm 0.1)m\) gives an uncertainty of about \(\pm 0.382\) MeV for the ISGMR centroid energy in \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The combined uncertainty from \(L\) and \(m_{s,0}^{\ast}\) on the ISGMR centroid energy in \(^{208}\)Pb is about \(\pm 0.474\) MeV, leading to an uncertainty of about \(\pm 16\) MeV for \(K_0 = 230\) MeV.
- Including all nine macroscopic quantities with their empirical uncertainties yields a total uncertainty of about \(\pm 0.647\) MeV for the ISGMR centroid energy in \(^{208}\)Pb, corresponding to about \(\pm 21\) MeV for \(K_0 = 230\) MeV.
- Varying \(K_0\) from 200 MeV to 270 MeV shifts the ISGMR peak in \(^{208}\)Pb from about 13.3 MeV to 15.4 MeV; varying \(L\) from 30 MeV to 90 MeV shifts the peak from about 14.6 MeV to 13.9 MeV; varying \(m_{s,0}^{\ast}\) from \(0.6m\) to \(0.9m\) shifts the peak from about 15.0 MeV to 13.9 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the analysis method of Chen et al. (2010) to study correlations between the ISGMR energy and asymmetric nuclear matter properties, addressing the long-standing puzzle that non-relativistic models predict \(K_0 = 210-230\) MeV while relativistic models predict \(K_0 \approx 270\) MeV from the ISGMR in \(^{208}\)Pb, as first noted by Piekarewicz (2002). The results show that the ISGMR centroid energy difference between \(^{100}\)Sn and \(^{132}\)Sn strongly correlates with the density slope \(L\) of the symmetry energy, providing a potentially useful probe of the symmetry energy that could help narrow uncertainties in future experiments.
