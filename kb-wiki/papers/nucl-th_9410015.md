# Relativistic models for quasielastic $(e,e')$\ at large momentum transfers

> Inclusive quasielastic response functions are calculated for electron scattering in a relativistic model including momentum dependent scalar and vector mean fields. The momentum dependence of the mean fields is taken from Dirac optical fits to proton nucleus scattering and is important in describing data at momentum transfers of 1 GeV/c or larger. Our simple model is applicable for quasielastic scattering over a large range of momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper argues that relativistic mean field models with momentum-independent scalar and vector potentials predict too large a binding energy shift for the quasielastic peak at momentum transfers near 1 GeV/c or larger, and that including momentum-dependent potentials from Dirac optical fits corrects this overprediction while remaining applicable over a broad range of momentum transfers.

## Method

The authors calculate inclusive quasielastic response functions for electron scattering using a relativistic mean field model where the scalar and vector self-energies are taken as momentum-dependent functions from a Dirac optical model fit to p-40Ca elastic scattering. The polarization tensor is computed in a local density approximation using nuclear matter with a Fermi momentum of 230 MeV/c, and a minimal vertex correction is added to satisfy current conservation. Results are compared for three cases: a free Fermi gas, constant mean field theory (MFT) potentials, and the full momentum-dependent potentials.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum used in calculations: k_F = 230 MeV/c, representing an average over nuclear volume scaled by factor α = (230/257)^3
- Constant MFT potentials: V = 354 MeV, S = -431 MeV (from Walecka model)
- Longitudinal response calculated for 56Fe at momentum transfer |q| = 550 MeV/c and |q| = 1.14 GeV/c
- Transverse response calculated for 40Ca at |q| = 550 MeV/c
- Dirac optical fit reproduces p-40Ca elastic scattering data from 21 MeV up to laboratory kinetic energy 1.04 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic mean field and RPA calculations of quasielastic scattering (Kurasaw and Suzuki 1988, Wehrberger and Beck 1988, Horowitz and Piekarewicz 1989) which assumed momentum-independent self-energies and failed at high momentum transfers. The momentum-dependent potentials are taken from Dirac optical fits to proton-nucleus elastic scattering (Cooper, Hama, Clark and Mercer 1993) and are consistent with relativistic Brueckner calculations (Horowitz and Serot 1987, Ansatasio et al. 1983). The model enables a simple relativistic impulse approximation applicable over a large range of momentum transfers, but future work should repeat calculations in a full finite nucleus formalism and calculate vertex corrections in a microscopic model such as relativistic Hartree-Fock.
