# Exotic meson decays in the environment with chiral imbalance

> An emergence of Local Parity Breaking (LPB) in central heavy-ion collisions (HIC) at high energies is discussed. LPB in the fireball can be produced by a difference between the number densities of right- and left-handed chiral fermions (Chiral Imbalance) which is implemented by a chiral (axial) chemical potential. The effective meson lagrangian induced by QCD is extended to the medium with Chiral Imbalance and the properties of light scalar and pseudoscalar mesons ($π, a_0$) are analyzed. It is shown that exotic decays of scalar mesons arise as a result of mixing of $ π$ and $ a_0 $ vacuum states in the presence of chiral imbalance. The pion electromagnetic formfactor obtains an unusual parity-odd supplement which generates a photon polarization asymmetry in pion polarizability. We hope that the above pointed indications of LPB can be identified in experiments on LHC, RHIC, CBM FAIR and NICA accelerators.

## Key claim

Local Parity Breaking (LPB) in the hadron fireball produced by heavy-ion collisions can be described by introducing a chiral (axial) chemical potential, which leads to mixing of pion and scalar meson states and enables exotic parity-forbidden decays; the authors hope these effects can be identified in experiments at LHC, RHIC, CBM FAIR, and NICA, but note that existing theoretical arguments for CP violation in finite volumes are not sufficient to calculate production rates.

## Method

The authors extend a QCD-inspired generalized sigma model for light scalar and pseudoscalar mesons (π, a₀, σ) by including a background 4-vector of axial chemical potential b_μ that implements chiral imbalance. They derive the mass gap equation for the scalar condensate, compute the meson mass spectrum after diagonalizing the mixed π-a₀ states, and calculate decay widths for the resulting exotic processes using the Wess-Zumino-Witten effective action modified for the chirally imbalanced medium.

## Key numbers

- Input parameters: m_π = 139 MeV, m_a = 980 MeV, m_σ = 500 MeV, m = 5.5 MeV, μ₅ = 0, M = 300 MeV, v = 92 MeV
- Model parameters derived: λ₁ = 1.64850×10, λ₂ = -1.31313×10, c = -4.46874×10⁴ MeV², B = 1.61594×10⁵ MeV²
- Decay width calculations shown for μ₅ = 100 MeV (Fig. 4, Fig. 5)
- Fireball size scale L = 5-10 fm used for topological charge considerations
- Chiral symmetry restoration occurs at |b²| = ½(M² + c) in the chiral vector imbalance region

## Context

This work extends the study of Local Parity Breaking in heavy-ion collisions, building on the Chiral Magnetic Effect for peripheral collisions (Kharzeev et al.) and addressing anomalous dilepton excess observed in CERES, PHENIX, STAR, NA60, and ALICE experiments. The authors previously proposed a vector meson dominance model with chiral imbalance (Andrianov et al., 2014) and here extend the analysis to scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. The results enable experimental searches for LPB through photon polarization asymmetry in pion polarizability, as proposed by Harada et al., and through resonance-enhanced decays of mixed π-a₀ states.
