# Electrical Conductivity of Quark-Gluon Plasma in Strong Magnetic Fields

> We compute the electrical conductivity of quark-gluon plasma in a strong magnetic field $B$ with quantum field theory at finite temperature using the lowest Landau level approximation. We provide the one-loop result arising from 1-to-2 scattering processes whose kinematics are satisfied by the (1+1) dimensional fermion dispersion relation. Due to the chirality conservation, the conductivity diverges in the massless limit, and is sensitive to the value of the current quark mass. As a result, we find that the conductivity along the direction of the magnetic field is quite large compared with the value at $B=0$, mainly because of the small value of the current quark mass. We show that the resummation of the ladder diagrams for the current-current correlator gives rise to only sub-leading contributions beyond the leading-log order, and thus verify our one-loop result at the leading-log accuracy. We also discuss possible implications for the relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The electrical conductivity of quark-gluon plasma along a strong magnetic field is quite large compared to the zero-field value, but this result depends critically on the current quark mass because the conductivity diverges in the massless limit due to chirality conservation; the one-loop result is verified at leading-log accuracy by showing that ladder diagram resummation gives only sub-leading contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the electrical conductivity using the Kubo formula in the real-time formalism of finite-temperature quantum field theory, working in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation valid when the magnetic field is much larger than the temperature. They evaluate the one-loop current-current correlator with resummed quark propagators containing the damping rate, and compute the quark damping rate from 1-to-2 scattering processes (pair annihilation and its inverse) using the LLL-projected quark-gluon vertex and the gluon spectral function. The calculation is performed for two regimes: current quark mass much larger than the gluon screening mass, and the opposite case.

## Key numbers

- The conductivity along the magnetic field direction is proportional to the inverse of the quark damping rate, which scales as \(\xi_k \propto g^2 C_F m_f^2 \ln(T/m_f)\) for the case \(m_f \gg M\) and \(\xi_k \propto g^2 C_F m_f^2 \ln(T/M)\) for the case \(m_f \ll M\), where \(m_f\) is the current quark mass and \(M\) is the gluon screening mass given by \(M^2 = (g^2/2\pi) \sum_f |e q_f B|/(2\pi)\).

- The hierarchy of scales assumed is \(m_f, M \ll T \ll \sqrt{eB}\), with the magnetic field \(\sqrt{eB}\) being the largest energy scale.

- The gluon screening mass from the quark loop in the LLL approximation is \(M^2 = (1/2) \cdot (g^2/\pi) \sum_f |e q_f B|/(2\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electrical conductivity in weak magnetic fields using lattice QCD (Buividovich 2010), AdS/CFT correspondence (Pu 2014), and the Boltzmann equation (Satow 2014, Gorbar 2016, Harutyunyan 2016, Kerbikov 2014) into the strong-field regime where the LLL approximation applies. The result enables improved dynamical modeling of magnetohydrodynamics and the lifetime of magnetic fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and contrasts with zero-field calculations (Jeon 1994, Arnold 2000) where the current quark mass could be safely neglected at high temperature.
