# Data-driven exploration of the neutron $^3\text{P}_2$ pairing gap using Cassiopeia A neutron star observational data: Direct $χ^2$ minimization

> The rapid cooling observed in the Cassiopeia~A neutron star (Cas~A NS) is one of the most stringent tests for neutron-star cooling theory. While Cooper-pair breaking and formation (PBF) neutrino emission is a leading candidate, uncertainties remain regarding the PBF efficiency factor $q$ and the neutron ${}^{3}\mathrm{P}_{2}$ pairing gap. This work explores in a data-driven manner how the optimized gap shape responds to variations of the PBF emissivity parameter $q$ within a fixed cooling setup. We introduce a novel gap parametrization, in which each parameter carries direct physical meaning and controls the gap amplitude, peak location, width, and asymmetry. Using a Fortran-based cooling code and the BSk24 equation of state, we perform parameter-space exploration guided by the Cas~A NS data. Global optimization is carried out with Optuna's tree-structured Parzen estimator, followed by local refinement using the Nelder--Mead method. The optimized solutions yield physically reasonable gaps with peak amplitudes $Δ_{\max}\approx0.5$--$0.6~\mathrm{MeV}$. Although the multi-objective formulation explores the parameter space more broadly, the single-objective $χ^{2}$-only optimization achieves the lowest $χ^{2}$. For $M_{\mathrm{NS}}=1.4\,M_{\odot}$, increasing $q$ drives the optimized gap and critical-temperature profiles toward smoother and more localized shapes, improving consistency with the observed trend. Models with $q\gtrsim0.4$ reproduce the decline rate within the $1σ$ confidence interval, whereas the baseline case $q\simeq0.19$ lies near the $3σ$ level. Our results suggest larger effective PBF emissivities than the baseline estimate, although robust constraints on $q$ require future Bayesian inference including uncertainties in mass, envelope composition, equation of state, pairing microphysics, and age offset. ## Key claim

Within a fixed cooling setup using the BSk24 equation of state and a canonical neutron star mass of 1.4 solar masses, increasing the phenomenological PBF efficiency factor q drives the optimized neutron 3P2 pairing gap and critical-temperature profiles toward smoother and more localized shapes, with models requiring q greater than or approximately 0.4 to reproduce the observed Cas A decline rate within the 1 sigma confidence interval, while the baseline theoretical value q approximately 0.19 lies near the 3 sigma level.

## Method

The authors introduced a novel six-parameter parametrization of the neutron 3P2 pairing gap where each parameter carries direct physical meaning, controlling the gap amplitude, peak location, width, and asymmetry. They performed systematic parameter-space exploration using a Fortran-based neutron star cooling code with the BSk24 equation of state, employing global optimization via Optuna's tree-structured Parzen estimator followed by local refinement using the Nelder-Mead simplex method, with both single-objective chi-squared minimization and multi-objective formulations examined under identical conditions.

## Key numbers

- Optimized solutions yield peak amplitudes Delta_max approximately 0.5 to 0.6 MeV for the neutron 3P2 pairing gap
- Models with q greater than or approximately 0.4 reproduce the Cas A NS decline rate within the 1 sigma confidence interval
- The baseline case q approximately 0.19 lies near the 3 sigma level within the present setup
- The direct-Urca threshold for the BSk24 equation of state is 1.595 solar masses, and the adopted canonical mass is 1.4 solar masses
- The maximum critical temperature of neutron 3P2 pairing has been constrained to the range T_Cn_max = (4 to 9.5) times 10^8 K in prior work by Shternin (2022)

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Ho (2021) and Shternin (2022) that found the PBF efficiency factor must be q greater than approximately 0.4 at 90 percent confidence, and by Shternin (2022) that constrained q to the range 0.4 to 2.1 at 68 percent confidence, revealing a discrepancy with the theoretical prediction of q approximately 0.19 from Leinson (2010). The physically interpretable gap parametrization and hybrid optimization strategy developed here provide a foundation for future Bayesian inference studies that will simultaneously account for uncertainties in neutron star mass, envelope composition, equation of state, pairing microphysics, and the age offset of Cas A NS.
