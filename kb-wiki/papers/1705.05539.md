# Description of odd-mass nuclei within the interacting boson-fermion model based on the Gogny energy density functional

> Spectroscopic properties of odd-mass nuclei are studied within the framework of the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) with parameters based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation. The parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional (EDF) has been used at the mean-field level to obtain the deformation energy surfaces for the considered nuclei in terms of the quadrupole deformations ($β,γ$). In addition to the energy surfaces, both single particle energies and occupation probabilities have been used as a microscopic input for building the IBFM Hamiltonian. Only three strength parameters for the particle-boson-core coupling are fitted to experimental spectra. The IBFM Hamiltonian is then used to compute the energy spectra and electromagnetic transition rates for selected odd-mass Eu and Sm nuclei as well as for $^{195}$Pt and $^{195}$Au. A reasonable agreement with the available experimental data is obtained for the considered odd-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) based on the Gogny D1M energy density functional describes low-lying states in odd-mass nuclei at the same level of accuracy as earlier studies using a relativistic EDF, but only three strength parameters for the particle-boson-core coupling are fitted to experimental spectra; the method's validity depends on the mapping procedure and the specific choice of single-particle energies and occupation probabilities from the mean-field calculation.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1M energy density functional to obtain deformation energy surfaces in terms of quadrupole deformations (β,γ) for even-even core nuclei, along with spherical single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the odd nucleon. These microscopic inputs were used to determine the parameters of the IBFM Hamiltonian, with only the three boson-fermion coupling strengths (Γ₀, Λ₀, A₀) fitted to reproduce the lowest few experimental energy levels for each nucleus. The Hamiltonian was then diagonalized in a spherical basis to compute energy spectra, B(E2) and B(M1) transition rates, and spectroscopic quadrupole and magnetic moments for selected odd-mass Eu and Sm isotopes as well as ¹⁹⁵Pt and ¹⁹⁵Au.

## Key numbers

- For the even-even core ¹⁹⁴Pt, the energy ratio R₄/₂ = E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) = 2.57, characteristic of γ-softness or O(6) symmetry.
- The fitted boson-fermion coupling parameters for positive-parity states in ¹⁴⁹Eu: Γ₀⁺ = 0.05 MeV, Λ₀⁺ = 2.5 MeV, A₀⁺ = -0.13 MeV.
- The fitted boson-fermion coupling parameters for positive-parity states in ¹⁵⁵Sm: Γ₀⁺ = 1.6 MeV, Λ₀⁺ = 22.5 MeV, A₀⁺ = -0.28 MeV.
- The energy gap between the 1g₇/₂ and 2d₅/₂ orbitals for odd-mass Eu isotopes is less than 1 MeV in the Gogny-D1M calculations (e.g., for ¹⁴⁹Eu: 1g₇/₂ at 0.000 MeV, 2d₅/₂ at 0.868 MeV).
- The occupation probability v² for the 1i₁₃/₂ orbital in ¹⁴⁹Sm is 0.022, and in ¹⁵⁵Sm is 0.067.
- The boson Hamiltonian parameter κ for ¹⁴⁸Sm is -0.079 MeV, and for ¹⁵⁴Sm is -0.074 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the method of Nomura (2016) which combined the IBFM with a relativistic EDF (DD-PC1) to describe odd-mass Eu isotopes, and applies it instead with the non-relativistic Gogny D1M EDF to the same Eu and Sm isotopes plus the γ-soft nuclei ¹⁹⁵Pt and ¹⁹⁵Au. The results demonstrate that the mapping procedure is robust across different EDF classes (non-relativistic vs. relativistic), and the addition of γ-soft nuclei confirms the approach's applicability beyond axially-deformed systems. The study enables future systematic calculations of odd-mass nuclei across the nuclide chart using the computationally efficient IBFM framework with microscopically derived parameters.
