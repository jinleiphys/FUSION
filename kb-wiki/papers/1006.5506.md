# Exotic atoms in two dimensions

> We study the behavior of energy levels in two dimensions for exotic atoms, i.e., when a long-range attractive potential is supplemented by a short-range interaction, and compare the results with these of the one- and three-dimensional cases. The energy shifts are well reproduced by a scattering length formula $ δ{E}= A_0^2/\ln (a/R)$, where $a$ is the scattering length in the short-range potential, $A_0^2/(2\,π)$ the square of the wave function at the origin in the external potential, and $R$ is related to the derivative with respect to the energy of the solution that is regular at large distances.

## Key claim

The energy shifts of exotic atoms in two dimensions are well reproduced by a scattering length formula $\delta{E}= A_0^2/\ln (a/R)$, where the denominator includes a scale $R$ derived from the derivative with respect to energy of the solution regular at large distances, making the formula accurate only when the short-range potential is sufficiently short-ranged and the energy shift remains small.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for a two-component potential $V_1 + \lambda V_2$, where $V_1$ is a long-range attractive potential and $V_2$ is a short-range interaction, in two spatial dimensions for isotropic ($m=0$) states. They match the short-range behavior of the solution regular at large distances to the asymptotic solution emerging from the short-range potential, using the scattering length $a$ in $V_2$ alone. The method is tested on three model systems: a double delta-shell potential, a double exponential well, and a harmonic oscillator with an added short-range exponential potential.

## Key numbers

- For the double delta-shell example with $g_1=1/2$, $R_2=0.04$, and $g_2=0.1$, the exact energy shift is $\delta E = -0.000833975$, while the improved scattering length formula gives $\delta E'' = -0.0008340$, showing near-perfect agreement.
- For the harmonic oscillator with an added exponential potential of range $R_2=0.02$ and strength $\lambda=-80$, the exact shift is $\delta E = -0.06999$, the improved formula gives $\delta E'' = -0.07020$, and the formula from Farrell and van Zyl (2010) gives $\tilde\delta E = -0.07017$.
- The ratio of $A_0^2$ to $|\phi(0)|^2$ (the square of the wave function at the origin) is $2\pi$ for $d=2$, compared to $2$ for $d=1$ and $4\pi$ for $d=3$.

## Context

This work extends the study of exotic atoms from the well-known three-dimensional case, where the Deser-Trueman formula (Deser 1954, Trueman 1961) applies, and the one-dimensional case (Combescure 2007), to the more delicate two-dimensional case. The results connect to the statistical physics of bosons through the pseudo-potential approach, where deriving the pseudo-potential as a function of scattering length in two dimensions is notoriously delicate (Yamaguchi 2008, Lee 2002, Olshanii 2002, Farrell and van Zyl 2010). The derived formula enables accurate calculation of energy shifts for hadronic atoms in two-dimensional geometries and provides a framework for understanding level rearrangement phenomena in reduced dimensions.
