# Impact of magnetic field on giant dipole resonance of $^{40}$Ca using the EQMD model

> By taking into account the magnetic field in the extended quantum molecular dynamics model (EQMD), we analyzed its effects on giant dipole resonance (GDR) by studying the responses and strengths of the dipole moments. The selected system is the $^{40}$Ca nucleus which is excited through the Coulomb interaction by $^{16}$O. Particle acceleration term in Liénard-Wiechert potential is discussed which, however, has small impact on magnetic field. The peak energy, strength and width of GDR, temperature, and angular momentum of $^{40}$Ca as a function of beam energy are investigated. It is found that the magnetic field enhances the peak energy, strength and width of GDR which is not only due to the temperature effects but also due to the enhancement of the angular momentum of nucleus. At beam energy {E} $>$ 200 MeV/nucleon, magnetic field maintains a constant value for the strength of GDR. The work sheds light on examining important roles of the magnetic field on nuclear structure in low-intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The magnetic field enhances the peak energy, strength, and width of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) in excited $^{40}$Ca, and this enhancement is not solely due to temperature effects but also arises from the magnetic field's enhancement of the nucleus's angular momentum; at beam energies above 200 MeV/nucleon, the magnetic field maintains a constant value for the GDR strength.

## Method

The authors incorporated the magnetic field from the Liénard-Wiechert retarded potential into the extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model. They simulated ultra-peripheral Coulomb excitation of $^{40}$Ca by $^{16}$O at an impact parameter of 7.13 fm for beam energies from 50 to 500 MeV/nucleon, then extracted the GDR spectrum via Fourier transform of the dipole moment time evolution and fitted it with a Gaussian to obtain peak energy, strength, and width.

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field strength at the central point (0,0,0) reaches $2.75 \times 10^{15}$ Gauss at approximately 60 fm/c for a 100 MeV/nucleon $^{16}$O + $^{40}$Ca collision.
- The magnetic field lifetime extends to approximately 120 fm/c at 100 MeV/nucleon.
- The magnetic field integral over time shows an almost linear correlation with beam energy from 50 to 500 MeV/nucleon.
- The GDR peak energy increases with incident energy and exceeds experimental data when beam energy is above 200 MeV/nucleon.
- The GDR width decreases with increasing incident energy below 200 MeV/nucleon but remains stable as incident energy further increases.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field effects on nuclear structure from ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Skokov 2009, Voronyuk 2011) into the low-intermediate energy domain, where the magnetic field is weaker but has a longer lifetime. It builds on prior EQMD model applications to GDR (WBH 2014, WBH 2016) and on the established understanding that GDR width increases with angular momentum (Alhassid 1999). The results enable future exploration of how magnetic fields influence angular momentum and nuclear shape evolution in heavy-ion collisions.
