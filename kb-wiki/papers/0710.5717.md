# Low-momentum ring diagrams of neutron matter at and near the unitary limit

> We study neutron matter at and near the unitary limit using a low-momentum ring diagram approach. By slightly tuning the meson-exchange CD-Bonn potential, neutron-neutron potentials with various $^1S_0$ scattering lengths such as $a_s=-12070fm$ and $+21fm$ are constructed. Such potentials are renormalized with rigorous procedures to give the corresponding $a_s$-equivalent low-momentum potentials $V_{low-k}$, with which the low-momentum particle-particle hole-hole ring diagrams are summed up to all orders, giving the ground state energy $E_0$ of neutron matter for various scattering lengths. At the limit of $a_s\to \pm \infty$, our calculated ratio of $E_0$ to that of the non-interacting case is found remarkably close to a constant of 0.44 over a wide range of Fermi-momenta. This result reveals an universality that is well consistent with the recent experimental and Monte-Carlo computational study on low-density cold Fermi gas at the unitary limit. The overall behavior of this ratio obtained with various scattering lengths is presented and discussed. Ring-diagram results obtained with $V_{low-k}$ and those with $G$-matrix interactions are compared.

## Key claim

By tuning the CD-Bonn potential to produce neutron-neutron scattering lengths approaching infinity, the ratio of the ground-state energy of neutron matter to that of the non-interacting gas is found to be a constant of approximately 0.44 over a wide range of Fermi momenta, confirming universality at the unitary limit. The authors note that this result is "remarkably close" to Monte Carlo results but carries an uncertainty of about 0.006 from their own calculations.

## Method

The authors first tuned the sigma-meson mass in the CD-Bonn potential to construct neutron-neutron potentials with various $^1S_0$ scattering lengths, including values near negative and positive infinity. These bare potentials were then renormalized using a T-matrix equivalence procedure to produce low-momentum potentials $V_{low-k}$ that preserve the scattering lengths. The ground-state energy of neutron matter was calculated by summing particle-particle hole-hole ring diagrams to all orders within a low-momentum model space using these $V_{low-k}$ interactions, with a fixed-point criterion used to determine the decimation scale $\Lambda$.

## Key numbers

- For the CD-Bonn-$\infty$ potential ($a_s = -12070$ fm), the ratio $E_0/E_0^{free}$ is $0.443 \pm 0.006$ over the range of Fermi momenta studied.
- The fixed-point decimation scale $\Lambda$ for CD-Bonn-$\infty$ is $2.3$ fm$^{-1}$; for the original CD-Bonn ($a_s = -18.97$ fm) it is $2.25$ fm$^{-1}$; for $a_s = -9.8$ fm it is $2.15$ fm$^{-1}$; and for $a_s = +21.01$ fm it is $2.4$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The potential energy per particle for CD-Bonn-$\infty$ follows $PE/A = (\hbar^2/m)(-0.1649 k_F^2 - 0.0035)$ with rms deviation less than 0.0013.
- The original CD-Bonn potential has a $^1S_0$ scattering length of $-18.97$ fm and effective range $2.82$ fm; the tuned CD-Bonn-$\infty$ has effective range $2.54$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of cold Fermi gases at the unitary limit, a problem originally formulated by Bertsch (1999), to neutron matter using realistic nuclear potentials. The result $\xi \approx 0.44$ agrees with Quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson et al. (2003) who obtained $0.44(1)$ and by Astrakharchik et al. (2004) who obtained $0.42(1)$, as well as with experimental measurements on trapped cold alkali gases. The authors compare their $V_{low-k}$ ring-diagram method with the earlier $G$-matrix ring-diagram method of Song et al. (1987) and find the two approaches fully consistent, while noting that the $V_{low-k}$ method is simpler because it avoids the energy-dependent self-consistency required by the $G$-matrix approach.
