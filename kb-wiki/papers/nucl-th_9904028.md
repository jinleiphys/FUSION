# Theoretical aspects of the CEBAF 89-009 experiment on inclusive scattering of 4.05 GeV electrons from nuclei

> We compare recent CEBAF data on inclusive electron scattering on nuclei with predictions, based on a relation between structure functions (SF) of a nucleus, a nucleon and a nucleus of point-nucleons. The latter contains nuclear dynamics, e.g. binary collision contributions in addition to the asymptotic limit. The agreement with the data is good, except in low-intensity regions. Computed ternary collsion contributions appear too small for an explanation. We perform scaling analyses in Gurvitz's scaling variable and found that for $y_G\gtrless 0$, ratios of scaling functions for pairs of nuclei differ by less than 15-20% from 1. Scaling functions for $<y_G>0$ are, for increasing $Q^2$, shown to approach a plateau from above. We observe only weak $Q^2$-dependence in FSI, which in the relevant kinematic region is ascribed to the diffractive nature of the NN amplitudes appearing in FSI. This renders it difficult to separate asymptotic from FSI parts and seriously hampers the extraction of $n(p)$ from scaling analyses in a model-independnent fashion.

# Theoretical aspects of the CEBAF 89-009 experiment on inclusive scattering of 4.05 GeV electrons from nuclei

## Key claim

The relation between nuclear, nucleon, and point-nucleon structure functions (SF) provides good agreement with CEBAF inclusive electron scattering data except in low-intensity regions, but the weak Q^2-dependence of final state interactions (FSI) in the relevant kinematic regime makes it difficult to separate asymptotic from FSI contributions, seriously hampering model-independent extraction of the single-nucleon momentum distribution n(p) from scaling analyses.

## Method

The authors compare CEBAF data on inclusive electron scattering of 4.05 GeV electrons from nuclear targets (notably Fe) with predictions based on a relation connecting nuclear structure functions F_k^A to nucleon structure functions F_k^N via a point-nucleon structure function f^PN that contains nuclear dynamics. The calculations employ a relativistic extension of the non-relativistic Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) series in 1/q, which includes an asymptotic limit related to the single-nucleon momentum distribution n(p) and final state interactions dominated by binary collisions between the knocked-on nucleon and a core nucleon. Scaling analyses are performed using Gurvitz's scaling variable y_G.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of the NA3 SLAC experiment (Day, 1993) and related experiments (Fillipone, 1992; Arrington, 1996) to the higher-energy CEBAF data, using the GRS series approach previously developed by Rinat and Taragin (1997). The results are compared with Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) analyses by Benhar et al. (1991) and Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1994), and with scaling analyses by Arrington et al. (1999). The finding that weakly Q^2-dependent FSI contaminate the plateau region challenges the conventional interpretation that plateaus in scaling functions directly yield n(p), suggesting that future extractions of nucleon momentum distributions from scaling analyses will require better understanding of FSI dynamics.
