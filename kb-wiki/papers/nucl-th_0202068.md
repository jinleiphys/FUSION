# Comment on "Forward K+ production in subthreshold pA collisions at 1.0 GeV"

## Key claim

The interpretation by Koptev et al. (2001) that K+ production in subthreshold pA collisions at 1.0 GeV requires collectivity among 6–7 bound nucleons lacks sufficient dynamical content; a microscopic calculation shows that the effective number of collectively cooperating nucleons is typically 4 at low K+ momenta and 2–3 at high K+ momenta, not 6–7.

## Method

The author evaluates the full transition amplitude for p(1.0 GeV/c) + 12C → K+ X with N collectively cooperating nucleons in 12C and M nucleons in the continuum, modeling 12C as s- and p-shell nucleons in a harmonic oscillator potential. The NN rescattering amplitude is modeled via exchange of effective (ππ) σ or ρ mesons including N* resonances in the mass range 1650–1800 MeV, with couplings from the Bonn potential and partial widths. The calculation compares a zero-range approximation and a Gaussian parametrization of the NN interaction fitted to NN → NN(ππ) data, with multi-nucleon phase space included in a nonrelativistic approximation.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic momentum transfer in the reaction is q ∼ √3 M ∼ 1500 MeV/c (where M is the nucleon mass).
- At low K+ momenta around 200 MeV/c, 4 collectively interacting nucleons dominate, with typically 3 nucleons emitted into the continuum.
- At K+ momenta around 500 MeV/c, 2–3 bound nucleons are involved, with knockout of a single nucleon.
- The nucleon momenta exchanged at high K+ momenta are close to the peak of the p-shell momentum density at around 200 MeV/c.
- The data cover K+ momenta from 150 MeV/c to above 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This Comment responds to the experimental letter by Koptev et al. (2001) on forward K+ production in subthreshold pA collisions, arguing that the original estimate of 6–7 collectively cooperating nucleons overstates the degree of collectivity. The work extends a microscopic picture from other near-exclusive high-momentum-transfer processes, where the interplay of NN rescattering amplitude strength, phase space, and momentum sharing constraints determines the effective collectivity. The author calls for further data on the transition to nearly exclusive K+ production in p-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and for covariant model calculations including full retardation to better understand energy sharing in collective phenomena.
