# Structure Factors of The Unitary Gas Under Supernova Conditions

> We compute with lattice field theory the vector and axial static structure factors of the unitary gas for arbitrary temperature above the superfluid transition and for fugacities 0.1 < z < 1.0. Using the lattice formulation, we calculate beyond the validity of the virial expansion, a commonly used technique in many-body physics. We find qualitative differences in the behavior of the structure factors at high fugacity compared to the predictions of the virial expansion. Due to the large scattering length of neutrons, we expect the unitary gas structure factors to approximate the structure factors of hot neutron gases, and we therefore expect our calculations to be useful in supernova simulations, where neutron gas structure factors are needed to compute in-medium neutrino-neutron scattering rates.

## Key claim

The authors compute the vector and axial static structure factors of the unitary gas for fugacities between 0.1 and 1.0, finding that the virial expansion (a commonly used perturbative technique) gives qualitatively incorrect predictions at high fugacity (z=1.0), specifically that the dramatic bump in the vector structure factor at small momentum predicted by the virial expansion is not present in the lattice calculation.

## Method

The authors use a lattice field theory formulation with a non-local hopping term that reproduces the continuum dispersion relation, combined with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to convert the four-fermion interaction into a coupling to auxiliary fields. They perform Hybrid Monte Carlo simulations on cubic lattices with up to 9^3 spatial sites, tuning the coupling to the unitarity point (infinite scattering length) by demanding a zero-energy two-body bound state. The structure factors are extracted from equal-time correlation functions of density and spin density operators computed via path integral expressions involving fermion matrix determinants.

## Key numbers

- Fugacity range explored: 0.1 < z < 1.0, corresponding to temperatures 0.6 T_F < T < 6 T_F
- Temperature used in simulations: T = 4.13 MeV (with M = 1 = 938 MeV, Δx = 1 = 5 fm, Δt = 0.01 = 1.19 fm)
- Systematic uncertainty assigned to vector structure factors at highest z: 5%
- Axial structure factor at q→0: S_A(q→0)/S_0 = 0.39(1) at T/T_F = 0.371(5), compared to Jensen et al. value of 0.403(1) at T/T_F = 0.353
- Coupling at unitarity: G ≈ 5.14435 Δx/M

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of unitary gas properties (thermodynamics by Jensen (2018) and others, contact by multiple groups, zero-temperature vector structure factor by PhysRevLett.110.055305) into the higher-temperature regime relevant for supernova neutrinospheres. The authors provide the first non-perturbative calculation of both vector and axial structure factors beyond the virial expansion regime, which was previously used perturbatively by PhysRevC.98.015802. The results are intended for use in supernova simulation codes where in-medium neutrino-neutron scattering rates depend on these structure factors, and where small changes in scattering rates can determine whether a supernova explodes.
