# Tetra-quark Systems in Heavy Mesons -- D_{s0}^+(2317), X(3872) and related --

> Typical candidates of open- and hidden-charm tetra-quark mesons are studied through their decays and productions, and are compared with conventional mesons. In addition, it is proposed how to confirm experimentally that they are tetra-quark mesons.

# Tetra-quark Systems in Heavy Mesons -- D_{s0}^+(2317), X(3872) and related --

## Key claim
The paper argues that the open-charm scalar D_{s0}^+(2317) and the hidden-charm X(3872) are most naturally interpreted as tetra-quark mesons rather than conventional quark-antiquark mesons or meson-meson molecules, based on comparisons of their decay ratios and production mechanisms with experimental constraints, though the author acknowledges that a small mixing of the charmonium chi'_c1 with X(3872) may be needed to fully understand the measured radiative decay ratio.

## Method
The author compares measured decay branching ratios of D_{s0}^+(2317) and X(3872) with predictions from tetra-quark, conventional charmonium, and meson-molecule assignments, using the vector meson dominance (VMD) model and the phenomenologically known omega-rho mixing parameter to estimate isospin-nonconserving decay rates. For the open-charm sector, the flavor SU_f(4) relation for strong vertices is applied with a 20-30% deviation of spatial wavefunction overlap from unity, using the well-known light scalar K_0^* as input to estimate widths of conventional scalar mesons D_0^* and D_{s0}^{*+}.

## Key numbers
- CLEO constraint on D_{s0}^+(2317): R(D_{s0}^+(2317)) = Gamma(D_{s0}^+(2317) -> D_s^{*+} gamma) / Gamma(D_{s0}^+(2317) -> D_s^+ pi^0) < 0.059
- For D_{s0}^+(2317) assigned to iso-triplet tetra-quark hat F_I^+: R ~ (4-5) x 10^{-3} << 0.059
- For D_{s0}^+(2317) assigned to conventional scalar D_{s0}^{*+}: R ~ 70 >> 0.059
- For D_{s0}^+(2317) assigned to iso-singlet tetra-quark hat F_0^+: R ~ 3 >> 0.059
- Measured ratio for X(3872): R = Br(X(3872) -> pi^+ pi^- pi^0 psi) / Br(X(3872) -> pi^+ pi^- psi) = 1.0 +/- 0.4 +/- 0.3
- Measured radiative ratio for X(3872) from Belle: R^gamma_X = Br(X(3872) -> gamma psi) / Br(X(3872) -> pi^+ pi^- psi) = 0.14 +/- 0.05
- Measured radiative ratio for X(3872) from Babar: R^gamma_X = 0.33 +/- 0.12
- Measured ratio for X(3872) radiative decays to psi' vs psi from Babar: Gamma(X -> gamma psi') / Gamma(X -> gamma psi) = 3.4 +/- 1.4
- Measured mass of D_0 from Babar: m_{D_0} = 2297 +/- 32 MeV, width Gamma(D_0) = 273 +/- 74 MeV
- Estimated widths for conventional scalar mesons: Gamma(D_0^*) ~ 50-60 MeV, Gamma(D_{s0}^{*+}) ~ 40-50 MeV
- Omega-rho mixing parameter: |g_{omega rho}| ~ 3.4 x 10^{-3} GeV^2
- Branching fraction for B -> bar D tilde D_{s0}^+(2317)[D_s^+ pi^0] from Belle: (8.5^{+2.1}_{-1.9} +/- 2.6) x 10^{-4}
- Branching fraction for B -> bar D tilde D_{s0}^+(2317)[D_s^{*+} gamma] from Belle: (2.5^{+2.0}_{-1.8} (< 7.5)) x 10^{-4}

## Context
This work extends the tetra-quark interpretation of light scalar mesons a_0(980) and f_0(980) by Jaffe (1977) to the charm sector, proposing that D_{s0}^+(2317) and X(3872) follow the same pattern. The analysis contradicts the conventional charmonium assignment for X(3872) and the simple quark-antiquark interpretation of D_{s0}^+(2317), and it addresses the puzzle of X(3872) decaying to final states with opposite G-parities by invoking the phenomenologically known omega-rho mixing mechanism. The paper enables experimental searches for the predicted charged partners of D_{s0}^+(2317) (hat F_I^0 and hat F_I^{++}) in B decays, and for the isospin partner {[cn](bar c bar n) - (cn)[bar c bar n]}_{I=0} of X(3872) in the pi^0 pi^0 psi channel.
