# Enhanced formation of a dense Kbar nuclear cluster K^- pp in pp collisions - Lambda*-p doorway dominance

> We have found theoretically that the elementary process, p + p to K+ + Lambda(1405) + p, which occurs in a short impact parameter (around 0.2 fm) and with a large momentum transfer (Q ~ 1.6 GeV/c), leads to unusually large self-trapping of Lambda(1405) by the projectile proton, when a Lambda* -p system exists as a dense bound state (size ~ 1.0 fm) propagating to K^-pp. The seed, called "Lambda*-p doorway", is expected to play an important role in the (p, K*) type reactions and heavy-ion collisions to produce various Kbar nuclear clusters.

# Enhanced formation of a dense Kbar nuclear cluster K^- pp in pp collisions - Lambda*-p doorway dominance

## Key claim

The authors theoretically predict that the elementary process p + p → K⁺ + Λ(1405) + p, despite having a large momentum transfer (Q ~ 1.6 GeV/c) that would normally suppress bound-state formation, instead produces an unusually large self-trapping of Λ(1405) by the projectile proton when the Λ*-p system exists as a dense bound state propagating to K⁻pp. This "Λ*-p doorway" dominance occurs specifically because the short collision range (~0.3 fm) matches the compact size of the predicted K⁻pp cluster (rms distance ~1.05 fm).

## Method

The authors calculate the spectral function for the p + p → K⁺ + K⁻pp reaction using a Λ* doorway model, where Λ(1405) is treated as a quasi-bound state of K⁻p. They model the elementary process with a Yukawa-type short-range NN interaction (using the ρ meson mass, m_B = 770 MeV/c²) and a large momentum transfer Q = 1.6 GeV/c, then solve the Schrödinger equation for the Λ*-p system using a complex optical potential (V₀ = -295 MeV, W₀ = -42 MeV, b = 1.0 fm). The absolute cross section scale is normalized to the empirical DISTO cross section of σ(pp → K⁺ + Λ* + p) = 20 μb at T_p = 2.85 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Predicted binding energy of K⁻pp: B_K = 86 MeV (revised theoretical value)
- Predicted width of K⁻pp: Γ = 58 MeV (revised theoretical value)
- Predicted rms distance of the Λ*-p system: R(Λ*p) = 1.05 fm
- Momentum transfer in the pp reaction: Q ~ 1.6 GeV/c
- Impact parameter for the elementary process: ~0.2 fm
- Boson mass used for NN collision range: m_B = m_ρ = 770 MeV/c²
- Bound/QF ratio for the realistic case: 4.6 (meaning bound-state peak dominates over quasi-free component)
- Empirical cross section for pp → K⁺ + Λ* + p at T_p = 2.85 GeV: 20 μb (from DISTO experiment)
- Incident proton energy for calculations: T_p = 4 GeV
- Predicted mass of K⁻pp: M(K⁻pp) ~ 2280 MeV/c²
- Average nucleon density of predicted Kbar nuclear clusters: ρ_ave ~ 0.5 fm⁻³ (about 3 times normal nuclear density ρ₀ ~ 0.17 fm⁻³)

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior predictions of Kbar nuclear clusters (Akaishi 2002, Yamazaki 2002) and the Λ* doorway model (Yamazaki 2002) to proton-induced reactions, where conventional wisdom from hypernuclear production (Shinmura) suggested cross sections as low as 10⁻⁶ of elementary Λ production. The paper contradicts the expectation that large momentum transfer (Q ~ 1.6 GeV/c) would suppress cluster formation, showing instead that the dense character of K⁻pp (size ~1.0 fm) enables dominant sticking. This enables experimental searches at GSI using the FOPI detector at T_p = 3.5 GeV, and suggests enhanced Kbar cluster formation in heavy-ion collisions through primary NN collisions and subsequent processes.
