# Mechanism of r-mode stability in young rapidly rotating pulsars

> We demonstrate that stability of r-modes in young rapidly rotating pulsars might be explained if one takes into account strong medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon interaction because of the softening of pionic degrees of freedom in dense nucleon matter. Presence of the efficient direct Urca processes is not required. Within our model the most rapidly rotating observed young pulsar PSR J0537-6910 should have the mass $\geq 1.8M_{\odot}$.

# Mechanism of r-mode stability in young rapidly rotating pulsars

## Key claim

The stability of r-modes in young rapidly rotating pulsars can be explained by strong medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon interaction due to softening of pionic degrees of freedom in dense nucleon matter, without requiring the presence of efficient direct Urca processes. Within this model, the most rapidly rotating observed young pulsar PSR J0537-6910 should have a mass of at least 1.8 solar masses.

## Method

The authors calculated the critical rotation frequency for r-mode stability by computing shear and bulk viscosities from all relevant dissipation processes, incorporating in-medium polarization effects including Coulomb screening in the electron-muon plasma and softening of pionic degrees of freedom with increasing nucleon density. They used the HDD equation of state matched to the Friedman-Pandharipande-Skyrme EoS at low densities, and included contributions from medium modified Urca (MMU) processes, pion Urca (PU) processes on a charged pion condensate, and radiative bulk viscosity, as well as lepton and neutrino shear viscosity terms. Nucleon superfluidity was included using pairing gaps from the nuclear medium cooling scenario.

## Key numbers

- The fastest young pulsar observed, PSR J0537-6910, has rotation frequency ν_max^young = 62 Hz and age 5 kyr
- The gravitational radiation time scale for the most unstable r-mode is τ_G^{-1} = 6.4 × 10^{-2} Hz × R_6^7 × Ω_4^6 × (ρ_cen/ρ_0), where ρ_0 = 2.63 × 10^{14} g/cm^3 is nuclear saturation density
- The shear viscosity damping time is τ_η^{-1} = 6.0 × 10^{-5} Hz × ⟨η_20⟩_4 × R_6^{-2} × (ρ_0/ρ_cen)
- The bulk viscosity damping time is τ_ζ^{-1} = 2.2 × 10^{-7} Hz × R_6^4 × Ω_4^4 × ⟨ζ_20[1+0.86(r/R)^2]⟩_8 × (M_⊙/M)^2 × (ρ_0/ρ_cen)
- The HDD equation of state produces maximum mass M_max ≈ 2.05 M_⊙
- The direct Urca processes start contributing only for densities n > 5n_0, corresponding to stars with masses M > M_c^DU ≈ 1.9 M_⊙
- The bulk viscosity from MMU reactions increases by three to four orders of magnitude for densities n ∼ 3n_0 compared to standard modified Urca calculations
- The opacity temperature for neutrino trapping with MU processes is T_opac ≈ 22 × 10^9 K, which decreases when MMU reactions are included
- PSR J0537-6910 stability is explained for M > 1.80 M_⊙ if MMU and PU processes are included, or for M > 1.84 M_⊙ if pion condensate is not formed
- Within the minimal cooling scenario without in-medium effects, ν_c,min exceeds ν_max^young only for masses M > 2.03 M_⊙

## Context

This work extends the nuclear medium cooling scenario developed by Blaschke (2013) and Voskresensky (earlier works), which successfully describes neutron star surface temperatures including Cassiopeia A cooling data, by applying the same in-medium polarization effects to r-mode dissipation mechanisms. It contradicts the conclusion from Andersson and Kokkotas (2000) that standard dissipation mechanisms cannot explain the observed rotation frequencies of young pulsars, and addresses the problem raised by Shternin (2008) that lepton shear viscosity is strongly suppressed by in-medium polarization effects compared to earlier calculations by Flowers and Itoh. The results enable predictions for the mass of PSR J0537-6910 and provide a framework for understanding r-mode stability without requiring direct Urca processes, while noting that old pulsars in LMXB sources with higher frequencies are accelerated by accretion and require separate treatment.
