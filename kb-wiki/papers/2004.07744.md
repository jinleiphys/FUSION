# Direct Astrophysical Tests of Chiral Effective Field Theory at Supranuclear Densities

> Recent observations of neutron stars with gravitational waves and X-ray timing provide unprecedented access to the equation of state (EoS) of cold dense matter at densities difficult to realize in terrestrial experiments. At the same time, predictions for the EoS with reliable uncertainty estimates from chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) bound our theoretical ignorance. In this work, we analyze astrophysical data using a nonparametric representation of the neutron-star EoS conditioned on $χ$EFT to directly constrain the underlying physical properties of the compact objects. We discuss how the data alone constrain the EoS at high densities when we condition on $χ$EFT at low densities. We also demonstrate how to exploit astrophysical data to directly test the predictions of $χ$EFT for the EoS up to twice nuclear saturation density, and estimate the density at which these predictions might break down. We find that the existence of massive pulsars, gravitational waves from GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 favor $χ$EFT predictions for the EoS up to nuclear saturation density over a more agnostic analysis by as much as a factor of 7 for the quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) calculations used in this work. While $χ$EFT predictions using QMC are fully consistent with gravitational-wave data up to twice nuclear saturation density, NICER observations suggest that the EoS stiffens relative to these predictions at nuclear saturation density. Additionally, we marginalize over the uncertainty in the density at which $χ$EFT begins to break down, constraining the radius of a $1.4\,M_\odot$ neutron star to $R_{1.4}=11.40^{+1.38}_{-1.04}$ ($12.54^{+0.71}_{-0.63}$) km and the pressure at twice nuclear saturation density to $p(2n_\mathrm{sat})=14.2^{+18.1}_{-8.4}$ ($28.7^{+15.3}_{-15.0}$) MeV/fm$^3$ with massive pulsar and gravitational-wave (and NICER) data.

## Key claim

The paper directly tests the range of validity of chiral effective field theory (χEFT) predictions for the neutron-star equation of state by allowing astrophysical data to determine the density at which χEFT predictions break down, rather than asserting a fixed breakdown density a priori; the authors find that data favor χEFT predictions up to nuclear saturation density by a factor of up to 7 over agnostic priors, but that NICER observations suggest the equation of state stiffens relative to χEFT predictions at or slightly above saturation density.

## Method

The authors use a nonparametric Gaussian process representation of the neutron-star equation of state, conditioned on χEFT predictions from quantum Monte Carlo calculations with local chiral interactions up to a variable maximum density, and extend the equation of state to higher densities with an agnostic prior. They perform Bayesian inference using data from massive pulsar mass measurements, gravitational-wave observations of GW170817, and NICER X-ray timing of PSR J0030+0451, computing Bayes factors to compare models that trust χEFT up to different densities and marginalizing over the breakdown density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that incorporated χEFT constraints as fixed priors up to a specific density (Tews 2012, Hebeler 2013, Annala 2017, Most 2018) by instead allowing the data to determine the breakdown density. It also improves upon parametric equation-of-state representations used in earlier work (Tews 2018, Greif 2018, Capano 2019, Raaijmakers 2019) by using a nonparametric approach that avoids modeling systematics. The results enable future direct comparisons between microscopic nuclear theory and astrophysical observations to identify the density where χEFT predictions become inconsistent with data.
