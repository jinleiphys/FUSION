# Comment on "Observation of a Pinning Mode in a Wigner Solid with nu=1/3 Fractional Quantum Hall Excitations"

> A recent letter [H. Zhu et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 105, 126803 (2010)] reports the first measurements of a pinned mode in a Wigner solid at the (electronic) nu = 1/3 fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE) filling. The authors suggest that an interpretation of their findings may be found within the interacting composite fermion (ICF) theory. However, to date there is no record reporting crystalline correlations for nu ~ 1/3 obtained from ICF theory, or related theories. We argue that an appropriate interpretation of these findings follows naturally from the rotating-Wigner-molecule approach, which (with inclusion of the rovibrational spectrum of the molecule) encompasses both the crystalline and liquid aspects in the FQHE regime (in particular for nu=1/3, as well as in its neighborhood created by deviations of nu from 1/3, as revealed experimentally).

## Key claim

The authors argue that the pinned mode observed by Zhu et al. (2010) in a Wigner solid at filling factor ν = 1/3 is naturally explained by the rotating-Wigner-molecule (RWM) approach, not by interacting composite fermion (ICF) theory, because no ICF calculation has ever reported crystalline correlations at ν = 1/3.

## Method

The authors computed conditional probability distributions (CPDs) for N = 7 electrons at ν = 1/3 using three theoretical descriptions: a variational rotating Wigner molecule (RWM) wave function, exact diagonalization (EXD) in the lowest Landau level, and the variational Laughlin wave function (which coincides with the mean-field composite fermion description). They compared the CPDs to assess which approach reproduces the crystalline correlations seen in EXD.

## Key numbers

- For N = 7 electrons at ν = 1/3, the total angular momentum is L = 63, with ν = N(N-1)/2L.
- The EXD calculation shows Wigner crystalline correlations at ν = 1/3, while the Laughlin wave function (mean-field composite fermion) fails to reproduce them.
- The variational RWM reproduces the EXD crystalline correlations qualitatively but overestimates electron localization relative to each other.

## Context

This comment extends the rotating-Wigner-molecule approach of Yannouleas and Landman (2004, 2010) to explain the pinned-mode measurements of Zhu et al. (2010) at ν = 1/3. It contradicts the suggestion by Zhu et al. that interacting composite fermion theory (Jain, 2006) could interpret the findings, noting that no ICF calculation has produced crystalline correlations at ν = 1/3. The RWM approach, with inclusion of the ro-vibrational spectrum, provides a unified picture of crystalline and liquid aspects in the fractional quantum Hall regime, enabling interpretation of experiments at ν = 1/3 and nearby fillings.
