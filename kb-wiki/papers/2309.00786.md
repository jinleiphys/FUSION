# Predication of novel effects in rotational nuclei at high speed

> The study of high-speed rotating matter is a crucial research topic in physics due to the emergence of novel phenomena. In this paper, we combined cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with a similar renormalization group approach to decompose the Hamiltonian from the cranking CDFT into different Hermit components, including the non-relativistic term, the dynamical term, the spin-orbit coupling, and the Darwin term. Especially, we obtained the rotational term, the term relating to Zeeman effect-like, and the spin-rotation coupling due to consideration of rotation and spatial component of vector potential. By exploring these operators, we aim to identify novel phenomena that may occur in rotating nuclei. Signature splitting, Zeeman effect-like, spin-rotation coupling, and spin current are among the potential novelties that may arise in rotating nuclei. Additionally, we investigated the observability of these phenomena and their dependence on various factors such as nuclear deformation, rotational angular velocity, and strength of magnetic field.

## Key claim

By combining cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with a similarity renormalization group approach, the author decomposes the cranking Dirac Hamiltonian to reveal three additional operators (rotational term, Zeeman effect-like term, and spin-rotation coupling) that may produce observable novel phenomena in high-speed rotating nuclei, but the predictions are based on a simplified model using Woods-Saxon potentials rather than full self-consistent CDFT calculations.

## Method

The author applied the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method to decompose the cranking Dirac Hamiltonian from CDFT into Hermitian components: non-relativistic term, dynamical term, spin-orbit coupling, Darwin term, rotational term, Zeeman effect-like term, and spin-rotation coupling. The resulting Hamiltonian was solved via basis expansion for the nucleus ⁶⁴Ge, using Woods-Saxon parameterizations for the scalar and vector potentials with axial quadrupole deformation, while replacing the canonical momentum \(\vec{\pi}\) with the kinetic momentum \(\vec{p}\) for computational simplicity.

## Key numbers

- The scalar potential depth \(S_{\text{WS}}\) was set to 350 MeV and the vector potential depth \(V_{\text{WS}}\) to -405 MeV, with diffuseness \(a = 0.67\) fm and radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.27\) fm for ⁶⁴Ge.
- For \(\beta_2 = 0.3\) and \(\Omega_x = 2.0\) MeV, when \(B_x = 20\) MeV, the Zeeman effect-like contribution to signature splitting changes energy by about 3.0 MeV for the \(1f_{7/2,1/2}\) and \(1g_{9/2,1/2}\) states.
- The spin-rotation coupling contribution to signature splitting is smaller than the Zeeman effect-like contribution by about 3 orders of magnitude.
- Assuming \(\Omega_x = 2\) MeV, \(B_x = 20\) MeV, and \(r_{yz} = 5\) fm, the estimated spin current magnitude is \(|J_s| \approx 5.41 \times 10^{-4}\) MeV = 0.541 keV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous application of SRG to decompose the Dirac Hamiltonian in spherical and deformed nuclei (Guo 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015) by now including rotation and the spatial component of the vector potential. The predictions of signature splitting, Zeeman effect-like, and spin current in rotating nuclei are new, and the author suggests that with advances in laser control technology, it may become possible to regulate nucleon spin and spin current in the near future.
