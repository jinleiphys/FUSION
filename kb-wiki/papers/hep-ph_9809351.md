# The Mechanism of Quark Confinement

> 20 pages, LaTeX with worldsci.sty and 10 epsf Figures, Talk given at 3rd International Conference on Quark Confinement and the Hadron Spectrum (Confinement III), Newport News, VA, 7-12 Jun 1998

## Key claim

Lattice simulations in the maximally Abelian projection (MAP) provide strong numerical evidence that the dual superconductor picture of confinement is realized in QCD, but the agreement is not perfect: the Abelian projected theory reproduces approximately 92% of the SU(2) string tension, and the flux tube width in the projected theory is much narrower than in full SU(2), indicating that off-diagonal gluon fields affect infrared observables even though the string tension is preserved.

## Method

The author reviews lattice gauge theory results in which SU(N_c) gauge fields are gauge-fixed to the maximally Abelian projection (MAP) by maximizing a functional that makes link variables as diagonal as possible, then decomposed into diagonal "photon" fields u_{x,mu} in the U(1)^{N_c-1} Cartan subgroup and off-diagonal "matter" fields C_{x,mu}. Magnetic monopole currents are identified from the singular part of the Abelian plaquette via the decomposition dθ = overline{θ}_□ + 2π m, and the photon and monopole contributions to observables are separated by solving the lattice Coulomb equation for the singular part θ^{sing} induced by monopoles.

## Key numbers

- The APSU(2) string tension accounts for (92 ± 4)% of the SU(2) string tension (lattice spacing a ≈ 0.081 fm).
- The monopole contribution to the Abelian string tension is (95 ± 1)% of the APSU(2) string tension.
- The 1/e radius of the flux tube in APSU(2) becomes consistent with a constant 2δ_{1/e} ≈ 0.36 fm for source separations r > 0.5 fm, while in full SU(2) the width saturates at 2δ_{1/e} ≈ 0.9 fm around r ≈ 1.2 fm (scale set by σ^{-1/2} ≈ 0.45 fm).
- The adjoint string breaks at a distance slightly smaller than 1.5 fm in SU(2) gauge theory.
- Lattice spacing a ≈ 0.175 fm and lattice extent La ≈ 2.8 fm were used for the hadron spectrum study in SU(3) with quark masses giving pion masses varied as shown on the horizontal axis.

## Context

This talk reviews lattice evidence for the dual superconductor mechanism of confinement originally proposed by 't Hooft (1981) and Mandelstam, in which magnetic monopoles condense and squeeze electric flux into Abrikosov-Nielsen-Oleson vortices. The results extend earlier work by Suzuki and collaborators on Abelian and monopole dominance, and by Di Giacomo and collaborators on monopole condensation as an order parameter. The puzzles identified, particularly the narrow flux tube width in the projected theory and the need to include off-diagonal gluons to describe adjoint string breaking, point toward the necessity of going beyond the simple Abelian Higgs model to construct a complete effective low-energy theory of QCD confinement.
