# Effective three-body interactions in nuclei

> It is shown that the three-body forces in the $1f_{7/2}$ shell, for which recently evidence was found on the basis of spectroscopic properties of the Ca isotopes and $N=28$ isotones, can be most naturally explained as an effective interaction due to excluded higher-lying shells, in particular the $2p_{3/2}$ orbit.}

# Effective three-body interactions in nuclei

## Key claim
The paper argues that three-body forces recently claimed in the $1f_{7/2}$ shell based on spectroscopic data for Ca isotopes and $N=28$ isotones are not genuine three-nucleon interactions, but can be most naturally explained as effective interactions arising from second-order perturbation due to excluded higher-lying shells, particularly the $2p_{3/2}$ orbit. The authors maintain that claims of evidence for genuine three-nucleon interactions should be treated with skepticism when based on calculations in a shell-model space that is too restricted.

## Method
The authors derive analytic expressions for effective two-body and three-body interactions in a pure $j^n$ configuration using second-order perturbation theory, where the intermediate states are configurations of the type $j^{n-1}j'$ (differing by one nucleon). They apply the formalism to the $1f_{7/2}$ shell using existing p3f7 interactions from Engeland (1966), Federman (1966), Auerbach (1967), and Lips (1970) that explicitly include the $2p_{3/2}$ orbit, as well as the more recent gxpf1a interaction from Honma (2004), to calculate the effective three-body matrix elements induced when the model space is restricted to $1f_{7/2}$.

## Key numbers
- The $3/2^-$ level in $^{41}$Ca associated with the $2p_{3/2}$ orbit lies only about 2 MeV above the $7/2^-$ ground state.
- Single-particle energies adopted in the gxpf1a interaction: $\epsilon_{1f_{7/2}} = -8.6240$ MeV, $\epsilon_{2p_{3/2}} = -5.6793$ MeV, $\epsilon_{2p_{1/2}} = -4.1370$ MeV, $\epsilon_{1f_{5/2}} = -1.3829$ MeV.
- For the $Z=20$ case, the effective three-body matrix element for $J=3/2$ is $-412$ keV (p3f7 from Engeland 1966) and $-173$ keV (p3f7 from Federman 1966), compared to Volya's fitted value of $-559 \pm 273$ keV.
- For the $N=28$ case, the effective three-body matrix element for $J=3/2$ is $-53$ keV (p3f7 from Auerbach 1967) and $-115$ keV (p3f7 from Lips 1970), compared to Volya's fitted value of $-128 \pm 88$ keV.
- The gxpf1a interaction yields a $J=3/2$ three-body matrix element of $-41$ keV for the $N=28$ case.
- For $J=7/2$, the effective three-body matrix elements are $+138$ keV ($Z=20$, Engeland 1966), $+76$ keV ($Z=20$, Federman 1966), $+42$ keV ($N=28$, Auerbach 1967), $+37$ keV ($N=28$, Lips 1970), and $+45$ keV (gxpf1a), compared to Volya's $+53 \pm 70$ keV ($Z=20$) and $+55 \pm 28$ keV ($N=28$).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of configuration mixing in calcium isotopes by Engeland (1966) and Federman (1966) and in $N=28$ isotones by Auerbach (1967) and Lips (1970), which showed that two-body interactions with configuration mixing could reproduce experimental data. It directly addresses the recent claim by Volya (2009) of three-body forces in $1f_{7/2}$-shell nuclei, and earlier work by Eisenstein (1973) and Quesne (1970) on three-body effective interactions. The paper enables a more cautious interpretation of three-body force evidence in nuclear structure, suggesting that renormalization effects from low-lying configurations rather than genuine three-nucleon interactions may explain the observed spectroscopic properties.
