# Possible evidence of quark matter in neutron star X-ray binaries

> We study the spin evolution of X-ray neutron stars in binary systems, which are being spun up by mass transfer from accretion disks. Our investigation reveals that a quark phase transition resulting from the changing central density induced by the changing spin, can lead to a pronounced peak in the frequency distribution of X-ray neutron stars. This finding provides one of several possible explanations available in the literature, or at least a contributor to part of the observed anomalous frequency distribution of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs), which lie in a narrow band centered at about 300 Hz, as found by the Rossi Explorer (RXTE).

## Key claim

The observed anomalous frequency distribution of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries (LMXBs), which shows a narrow band centered at about 300 Hz, may be partially explained by a quark phase transition in the stellar interior; the authors present this as one of several possible explanations, not a unique or definitive cause.

## Method

The authors computed the spin evolution of accreting neutron stars by solving the angular momentum evolution equation, incorporating a changing moment of inertia derived from general relativistic rotating stellar models. They compared two stellar models: one with only nucleons, hyperons, and leptons (no phase transition), and one allowing phase equilibrium between confined hadronic matter and deconfined quark matter described by the MIT bag model. The frequency distribution of X-ray stars was then constructed by assuming a constant birth rate and averaging over donor masses from 0.1 to 0.4 solar masses.

## Key numbers

- The observed anomalous frequency distribution of LMXBs is centered at about 300 Hz, as found by the Rossi X-ray Timing Explorer (RXTE).
- The calculated spike in the frequency distribution from the quark phase transition model is centered around 200 Hz, about 100 Hz lower than the observed anomaly.
- The epoch of spin saturation (during which the quark-to-hadron conversion occurs) lasts between about 10^7 and 10^9 years, depending on accretion rate and magnetic field parameters.
- Accretion rates assumed for the models range from Mdot_{-10} = 1 to 100, where Mdot_{-10} is the accretion rate in units of 10^{-10} solar masses per year; observed Z sources accrete at Mdot_{-10} ~ 200 and Atoll sources at Mdot_{-10} ~ 2.
- The initial neutron star mass in the calculations is 1.42 solar masses, and up to 0.4 solar masses is accreted.
- Neutron stars without quark matter are spun up to equilibrium frequencies between about 600 Hz and 850 Hz, depending on accretion rate and magnetic field.
- The magnetic field is assumed to decay from an initial value of 10^12 Gauss with a decay time of 10^6 years to an asymptotic value.
- Several millisecond pulsars have observed frequencies as high as about 650 Hz.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of spin-down in isolated millisecond pulsars (Glendenning 1997) to the case of accreting neutron stars in binary systems, where the changing spin can induce a quark-to-hadron phase transition. The mechanism is analogous to the "backbending" phenomenon in nuclear physics predicted by Mottelson and Valatin (1982) and discovered by Johnson (1972) and Stephens (1972). The paper provides an alternative or contributing explanation to other proposed mechanisms for the frequency spike, such as those discussed by Bildsten, Andersson, and Levin, and directly addresses the observational data compiled by van der Klis (2000) from RXTE observations.
