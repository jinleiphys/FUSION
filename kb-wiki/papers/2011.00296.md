# Effect of quantizing magnetic field on the inner crusts of hot Neutron Stars

> In the present work we study the effects of strongly quantizing magnetic fields and finite temperature on the properties of inner crusts of hot neutron stars. The inner crust of a neutron star contains neutron-rich nuclei arranged in a lattice and embedded in gases of free neutrons and electrons. We describe the system within the Wigner-Seitz (WS) cell approximation. The nuclear energy is calculated using Skyrme model with SkM* interaction. To isolate the properties of nuclei we follow the subtraction procedure presented by Bonche, Levit and Vautherin, within the Thomas-Fermi approximation. We obtain the equilibrium properties of inner crust for various density, temperatures and magnetic fields by minimizing the free energy of the WS cell satisfying the charge neutrality and $β-$equilibrium conditions. We infer that at a fixed baryon density and temperature, strong quantizing magnetic field reduces the cell radii, neutron and proton numbers in the cell compared with the field free case. However, the nucleon number in the nucleus increases in presence of magnetic field. The free energy per nucleon also decreases in magnetized inner crust. On the other hand, we find that finite temperature tends to smear out the effects of magnetic field. Our results can be important in the context of $r-$process nucleosynthesis in the binary neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strongly quantizing magnetic field (B* = 10^4, corresponding to B ~ 4.4 × 10^17 G) reduces the Wigner-Seitz cell radius, neutron and proton numbers in the cell, and free energy per nucleon compared to the field-free case, while increasing the nucleon number in the nucleus; however, finite temperature (up to 4 MeV) tends to smear out these magnetic effects, and at T = 4 MeV the matter becomes completely uniform at low densities.

## Method

The authors model the inner crust of a neutron star using the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation, with nuclear energy calculated via the Skyrme energy density functional with SkM* interaction. They employ the subtraction procedure of Bonche, Levit, and Vautherin within the finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi approximation to isolate nuclear properties from the surrounding neutron and electron gases. Equilibrium properties are obtained by minimizing the free energy of the cell under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, with electrons treated as Landau-quantized in a uniform magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength considered: B* = 10, 100, 10^3, and 10^4, where B* = B/B_c and B_c = 4.414 × 10^13 G; results shown only for B* = 10^4 (B ~ 4.4 × 10^17 G) where only the zeroth Landau level is populated at T = 0.
- Temperature range considered: 0–4 MeV.
- At ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.001 fm^-3, T = 1 MeV, and B* = 0: equilibrium cell radius R_c = 35 fm, proton fraction Y_p = 0.102, electron density ρ_e = 1.02 × 10^-4 fm^-3, electron chemical potential μ_e = 28 MeV.
- At same ⟨ρ⟩, T, and R_c but with B* = 10^4: μ_e reduces from 28 to 6 MeV; to restore beta-equilibrium, Y_p increases to 0.321 and ρ_e to 3.21 × 10^-4 fm^-3.
- At T = 2 MeV, B* = 0, ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.001 fm^-3: free energy minimum for nucleus+gas state is -3.029 MeV per nucleon, while the complete gas state has -2.780 MeV per nucleon.
- At T = 3 MeV, B* = 0, ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.001 fm^-3: free energy minimum for nucleus+gas state is -6.057 MeV per nucleon, while the complete gas state has -6.038 MeV per nucleon.
- At T = 3 MeV, B* = 10^4, ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.001 fm^-3: equilibrium nucleus has A = 98 at R_c = 35 fm.
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition at ⟨ρ⟩ = 0.001 fm^-3 lies between T = 3 and 4 MeV for B* = 0.
- For T = 4 MeV, B* = 0, matter becomes completely uniform at ⟨ρ⟩ ≲ 0.002 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature magnetized inner crust calculations of Nandi (2011) to include finite temperature effects, and builds on the subtraction procedure formalism developed by Bonche, Levit, and Vautherin (1984, 1985) and Suraud (1987). The results are relevant for understanding the composition of hot magnetars and binary neutron star merger ejecta, where crustal matter can be heated and magnetic fields amplified during the merging process as discussed by Ciolfi (2020). The authors note that at high temperatures a single-nucleus description may be inadequate, and future work should consider mixtures of different nuclei as in Lattimer (1985) and Hempel (2009).
