# Fixed particle number constraint in a simple model of a thermal expanding system and $pp$ collisions at the LHC

> Two-boson momentum correlations at fixed particle number constraint are studied in a simple analytically solvable model of a thermal expanding system. We show that the increase of expansion rate, as well as increase of particle multiplicity, enhances the ground-state contribution to particle momentum spectra and leads to suppression of the Bose-Einstein momentum correlations. The relations of these findings to the multiplicity-dependent measurements of the Bose-Einstein momentum correlations in high-multiplicity $p+p$ collision events at the LHC are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a simple analytically solvable model of a thermal expanding system with fixed particle number, increasing the expansion rate or particle multiplicity enhances the ground-state contribution to momentum spectra and suppresses Bose-Einstein momentum correlations; the authors suggest this effect may explain the low correlation strength parameter and saturation of HBT radii observed in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC, but note that a systematic quantitative analysis is still absent.

## Method

The authors construct a quasiequilibrium statistical operator for a nonrelativistic boson field with fixed particle number, using a harmonic trap chemical potential and a linear isotropic velocity profile u(r) = κr. They diagonalize the operator in an oscillator basis, apply a canonical projection to enforce fixed particle number N, and analytically compute one- and two-particle momentum spectra and the two-boson correlation function C_N(k,q) using recursive partition function formulas from Akkelin (2020). The model parameters are chosen to roughly correspond to pion mass (139.57 MeV), temperature (150 MeV), and system size R = 1.5 fm at breakup in LHC p+p collisions.

## Key numbers

- Particle mass m = 139.57 MeV (charged pion mass)
- Temperature T = 150 MeV
- System size parameter R = 1.5 fm
- Expansion parameter κ values used: 0.0, 0.1, and 0.2 fm^{-1}
- Correlation functions evaluated at average pair momentum k = 0.25 GeV/c
- The intercept λ(k,N,κ) decreases with increasing κ at fixed N and k
- The HBT radius R_HBT decreases with increasing κ according to R_HBT(k,N,κ) = ξ R_HBT(ξk,N,0) where ξ = 1/√(1+m²κ²b⁴) ≤ 1
- The ground-state fraction n_N⁰(p,κ)/n_N(p,κ) increases with κ at fixed momentum p

## Context

This work extends the fixed-particle-number canonical ensemble model of Akkelin (2020) by incorporating system expansion through a velocity field, bringing the model closer to LHC p+p collision conditions. The results contradict expectations from standard hydrodynamic expansion models that would predict different multiplicity dependence of HBT radii and correlation strength; the authors note that ATLAS (2017) and CMS (2011) data show saturation of interferometry radii and low λ values at high multiplicities that are at variance with thermalized hydrodynamic behavior. The model enables interpretation of these features as signatures of ground-state condensate formation in the canonical ensemble, and provides analytical relations (Eqs. 42-44) connecting expanding and non-expanding correlation functions that can be tested against experimental data.
