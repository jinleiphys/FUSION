# Multiplicity fluctuations in high energy hadronic and nuclear collisions

> The showers of cosmic rays entering the Earth's atmosphere are main sources of information on cosmic rays and are also believed to provide information on elementary interactions at energies not accessible to accelerators. In this context we would like first to remind the role of inelasticity K and elementary cross section $σ$ and then argue that similar in importance are fluctuations of different observables. The later will be illustrated by multiplicity fluctuations in hadronic and nuclear collisions.

# Multiplicity fluctuations in high energy hadronic and nuclear collisions

## Key claim
Multiplicity fluctuations in hadronic and nuclear collisions, quantified by the nonextensivity parameter q from Tsallis entropy, are argued to be as important as inelasticity K and elementary cross section σ for understanding extensive air showers, but the connection between multiplicity fluctuations and cross section fluctuations is not yet established by explicit formulas.

## Method
The authors apply a nonextensive information theory approach based on Tsallis q-entropy to rapidity distributions from pp and p̅p collisions, using mean multiplicity ⟨n⟩ and mean transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ as inputs to extract the inelasticity K and the nonextensivity parameter q as functions of energy. They further relate q to the parameter 1/k of the Negative Binomial multiplicity distribution by assuming that the normalized variance of mean multiplicity fluctuations D(n̅) equals q-1.

## Key numbers
- The long-flying component data correspond to fluctuations given by q = 1.3.
- For pp and p̅p collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the inelasticity K = 0.52; at √s = 900 GeV, K = 0.38.
- The nonextensivity parameter q is limited by q < 3/2, which corresponds to k < 2 in the Negative Binomial distribution; this saturation limit would appear at energies around 33 TeV (or E_LAB = 0.5 × 10^18 eV).
- For nuclear collisions with equation of state parameter c = p/ε = 1/3 and the limiting value q = 1.5, the variance relation is Var(n) = (q-1)·⟨n⟩²/(1+c)² ≈ 0.28·⟨n⟩².

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by the same group on the role of inelasticity K and cross section σ in extensive air showers (SWWW, 1994) and on the long-flying component explained by cross section fluctuations (Fluc, 1999). The nonextensive approach using Tsallis entropy (neq, 2001) is applied to multiplicity fluctuations, connecting them to the parameter 1/k of the Negative Binomial distribution (Shih, 1980). The paper enables future work on linking multiplicity fluctuations to cross section fluctuations, noting that nuclear collision data (Nonmon, 2004) show nonmonotonic behavior of fluctuations as a function of participating nucleons, complicating this connection.
