# Recent progress in anisotropic hydrodynamics

> The quark-gluon plasma created in a relativistic heavy-ion collisions possesses a sizable pressure anisotropy in the local rest frame at very early times after the initial nuclear impact and this anisotropy only slowly relaxes as the system evolves. In a kinetic theory picture, this translates into the existence of sizable momentum-space anisotropies in the underlying partonic distribution functions, <p_L^2> << <p_T^2>. In such cases, it is better to reorganize the hydrodynamical expansion by taking into account momentum-space anisotropies at leading-order in the expansion instead of as a perturbative correction to an isotropic distribution. The resulting anisotropic hydrodynamics framework has been shown to more accurately describe the dynamics of rapidly expanding systems such as the quark-gluon plasma. In this proceedings contribution, I review the basic ideas of anisotropic hydrodynamics, recent progress, and present a few preliminary phenomenological predictions for identified particle spectra and elliptic flow.

# Recent progress in anisotropic hydrodynamics

## Key claim
Anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) reorganizes the hydrodynamic expansion by treating momentum-space anisotropies at leading order rather than as perturbative corrections, and this framework most accurately reproduces all known exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation even in the limit of very large shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) and/or initial momentum-space anisotropy, though the "standard method" for implementing a realistic equation of state significantly underestimates low-pT hadron yields.

## Method
The authors solve 3+1 dimensional aHydro equations on a lattice with spacing a⊥ = 0.2 fm and aζ = 0.2 using 4th-order Runge-Kutta temporal updates, starting from optical Glauber initial conditions with no initial transverse flow and Bjorken longitudinal flow. The distribution function is taken as a Boltzmann form with an anisotropy tensor parameterized by three momentum-space ellipticities αi, and the equations of motion are obtained from the first and second moments of the Boltzmann equation with a relaxation time approximation collision kernel. Freeze-out is performed on a constant energy density hypersurface using a modified version of THERMINATOR 2 that accounts for ellipsoidal distribution functions.

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature T0 ≃ 560 MeV and freeze-out temperature TFO ≃ 130 MeV gave the best fit to ALICE data for LHC 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions
- Lattice spacing used: a⊥ = 0.2 fm and aζ = 0.2 with Nx = Ny = Nς = 128 grid points
- Temporal step size: ε = 0.01 fm/c
- Mixing factor κ = 0.145 and inelastic cross-section σin = 62 mb for LHC initial conditions
- Longitudinal profile parameters: Δς = 2.5 and σς = 1.4
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb: nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 0.17 fm⁻³, nuclear radius R = 6.48 fm, surface diffuseness a = 0.535 fm
- Best fit corresponded to 4πη/s = 3
- System evolved until maximum effective temperature below 120 MeV

## Context
This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework originally developed by Florkowski (2010) and Martinez (2010), which was shown to reproduce both ideal and free-streaming limits of 0+1d boost-invariant systems and reduce to Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamics in the small-anisotropy limit. The paper presents preliminary phenomenological comparisons with ALICE collaboration data for LHC 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, finding good agreement for identified particle v2 but significant deviations in low-pT spectra attributed to the "standard method" for implementing the equation of state. The next step is to optimize the quasiparticle aHydro approach for 3+1d simulations, which the authors note will be critical for exploring fluctuating initial conditions and extending to pA and pp collisions.
