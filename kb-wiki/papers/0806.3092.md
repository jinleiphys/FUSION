# 0th Order Heavy Quark Photon Bremsstrahlung

> We calculate the 0th order in opacity number distribution of massive photons (gluons) for heavy quark production radiation including interference from the away-side jet. While consistent with the soft photon (gluon) approximation, we find that approximating 1-x with 1, as done in previous calculations, strongly affects the magnitude of energy loss. Restoring gauge invariance by including the radiation associated with the away-side jet fills in the "dead cone," but is a relatively small effect. The Ter-Mikayelian reduction from vacuum energy loss is 10-40% for 5-25 GeV charm and bottom quarks.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the 0th order in opacity number distribution of massive photons (gluons) for heavy quark production radiation including interference from the away-side jet, finding that approximating 1-x with 1, as done in previous calculations, strongly affects the magnitude of energy loss, while restoring gauge invariance by including the away-side jet radiation fills in the "dead cone" but is a relatively small effect.

## Method

The author computed the number distribution of emitted photons by plugging massive 4-vectors into a standard classical electromagnetic calculation, then compared the result to the previously published pQCD result of Djordjevic (2003). The calculation was extended by evaluating the Feynman diagrams for 0th order in opacity production radiation, including the away-side jet diagram that is usually ignored in pQCD calculations, and enforcing the Ward identity. The energy loss was then computed numerically by integrating over x and transverse momentum with physical cutoffs requiring the emitted photon energy to be at least the gluon mass and the jet energy to be at least the quark mass.

## Key numbers

* The Ter-Mikayelian reduction from vacuum energy loss is 10-40% for 5-25 GeV charm and bottom quarks.
* Including the overall prefactor of (1-x)^2 changes the fractional energy loss by 50-150% for charm and bottom quarks.
* Filling in the "dead cone" by including the away-side jet radiation changes the energy lost by only 5-20%.
* The fixed coupling constant used in the numerical calculations was alpha = 0.5, and the Debye screening mass was mu = 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the pQCD calculations of Djordjevic (2003) on the Ter-Mikayelian effect for heavy quarks, correcting the previously published soft gluon number distribution by restoring factors of (1-x) and including the away-side jet radiation required for gauge invariance. The results are motivated by the need for theoretical predictions of photon bremsstrahlung in heavy ion collisions to compare with nascent gamma-hadron correlation data from p+p collisions (Hanks, 2007) and to distinguish between pQCD, AdS/CFT, and other energy loss mechanisms for heavy quarks. The author notes that the neglected (1-x)^2 prefactor may significantly alter R_AA calculations, especially at smaller momenta, and that these results will be necessary for comparison to AdS/CFT heavy quark drag results.
