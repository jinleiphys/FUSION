# Separate mass scaling of the widths of the rapidity distributions for mesons and baryons at energies available at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion research

> Evolution of the width of the rapidity distribution on beam rapidity has been studied for a number of produced particles with UrQMD-3.3p1 generated events at various FAIR (Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research) energies. The results for the width of the rapidity distribution with beam rapidity, obtained with UrQMD generated events, are compared with the existing experimental data (E802, E877, E896, E917, NA49). For both UrQMD and experimental data, the width of the rapidity distribution is found to bear scaling behavior with beam rapidity for all the hadrons. Such scaling behavior is found to follow separate mass ordering for the studied mesons and and baryons.

## Key claim
The width of the rapidity distribution for hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions scales with beam rapidity, but this scaling follows a separate mass ordering for mesons and baryons; the mass ordering is violated if mesons and baryons are considered together, which the authors attribute to the influence of net-baryon density on baryon rapidity distributions.

## Method
The authors used the UrQMD-3.3p1 event generator (without the hydrodynamic component) to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 10, 20, 30, and 40 A GeV. The rapidity distributions of π⁻, K⁻, φ, Λ, Ξ⁻, and Ω⁻ were parametrized by a sum of two Gaussian functions displaced symmetrically around midrapidity, and the root-mean-square (RMS) width was extracted. These UrQMD results were compared with experimental data from the AGS experiments (E802, E877, E896, E917) at 10 A GeV and the NA49 experiment at SPS energies (20-40 A GeV).

## Key numbers
- RMS width of π⁻ rapidity distribution from UrQMD: 0.959 ± 0.00004 at 10 A GeV, 1.050 ± 0.00005 at 20 A GeV, 1.111 ± 0.00003 at 30 A GeV, 1.149 ± 0.00007 at 40 A GeV
- RMS width of K⁻ rapidity distribution from UrQMD: 0.716 ± 0.0002 at 10 A GeV, 0.803 ± 0.0001 at 20 A GeV, 0.854 ± 0.0001 at 30 A GeV, 0.887 ± 0.0002 at 40 A GeV
- RMS width of φ rapidity distribution from UrQMD: 0.560 ± 0.0006 at 10 A GeV, 0.664 ± 0.0005 at 20 A GeV, 0.724 ± 0.0003 at 30 A GeV, 0.761 ± 0.0006 at 40 A GeV
- RMS width of Λ rapidity distribution from UrQMD: 0.630 ± 0.00007 at 10 A GeV, 0.741 ± 0.0003 at 20 A GeV, 0.814 ± 0.0006 at 30 A GeV, 0.864 ± 0.0006 at 40 A GeV
- RMS width of Ξ⁻ rapidity distribution from UrQMD: 0.519 ± 0.0008 at 10 A GeV, 0.605 ± 0.0008 at 20 A GeV, 0.661 ± 0.0002 at 30 A GeV, 0.705 ± 0.0063 at 40 A GeV
- RMS width of Ω⁻ rapidity distribution from UrQMD: 0.450 ± 0.0015 at 10 A GeV, 0.536 ± 0.0017 at 20 A GeV, 0.590 ± 0.0034 at 30 A GeV, 0.619 ± 0.004 at 40 A GeV
- Experimental RMS width of π⁻ from NA49: 0.991 ± 0.01 at 20 A GeV, 1.068 ± 0.01 at 30 A GeV, 1.123 ± 0.01 at 40 A GeV
- Experimental RMS width of φ from NA49: 0.582 ± 0.031 at 20 A GeV, 0.769 ± 0.030 at 30 A GeV, 0.852 ± 0.015 at 40 A GeV
- Experimental RMS width of Λ from NA49: 0.70 ± 0.01 at 20 A GeV, 0.89 ± 0.02 at 30 A GeV, 1.11 ± 0.08 at 40 A GeV

## Context
This work extends the scaling behavior of rapidity distribution widths reported by Friese et al. (NA49 collaboration) for Pb+Pb collisions to the Au+Au system at FAIR energies, and is the first study to include the Λ baryon alongside other hadrons. The authors build on earlier observations by Bleicher and by Steinheimer and Bleicher regarding the dependence of rapidity distribution widths on the initial up- and down-quark content of hadrons. The results enable future investigations at the CBM experiment at FAIR into how net-baryon density affects particle production mechanisms, and suggest that collective flow differences between mesons and baryons may need further study.
