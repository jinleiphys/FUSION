# Fluctuation of the initial condition from Glauber models

> We analyze measures of the azimuthal asymmetry, in particular the participant harmonic moments, epsilon*, in a variety of Glauber-like models for the early stage of collisions at RHIC. Quantitative comparisons indicate substantial model dependence for epsilon*, reflecting different effective number of sources, while the dependence of the scaled standard deviation sigma(epsilon*)/epsilon* on the particular Glauber model is weak. For all the considered models the values of sigma(epsilon*)/epsilon* range from ~0.5 for the central collisions to ~0.3-0.4 for peripheral collisions. These values, dominated by statistics, change only by 10-15% from model to model. For central collisions and in the absence of correlations between the location of sources we obtain through the use of the central limit theorem the simple analytic formula sigma(epsilon*)/epsilon*(b=0) ~ \sqrt{4/pi-1} ~ 0.52$, independent on the collision energy, mass number, or the number of sources. We investigate the shape-fluctuation effects for jet quenching and find they are important only for very central events. Finally, we list some remarks and predictions from smooth hydrodynamics on higher flow coefficients and their fluctuations, in particular sigma(v_4)/v_4=2 sigma(v_2)/v_2.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the scaled standard deviation of the participant eccentricity, σ(ε*)/ε*, is largely insensitive to the choice of Glauber model, changing by only 10–15% between models, and for central collisions approaches a universal value of approximately 0.52 that is independent of collision energy, mass number, or number of sources, provided there are no correlations between source locations.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of gold-gold collisions at RHIC using four Glauber-like models: the wounded-nucleon model, a mixed model with wounded nucleons and binary collisions, a hot-spot model with rare semi-hard collisions, and a hot-spot model with a superimposed gamma distribution for energy deposition. They computed the fixed-axes eccentricity ε and the participant (variable-axes) eccentricity ε* as functions of impact parameter, along with their scaled standard deviations, and compared these results to analytic formulas derived from the central limit theorem for a toy one-dimensional model.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions (b=0), the analytic formula gives σ(ε*)/ε* ≈ √(4/π − 1) ≈ 0.52, independent of collision energy, mass number, or number of sources.
- For all considered Glauber models, σ(ε*)/ε* ranges from approximately 0.5 for central collisions to approximately 0.3–0.4 for peripheral collisions.
- The scaled standard deviation changes by only 10–15% from model to model at intermediate impact parameters.
- At b=0, the hot-spot+Γ model yields a value of ε* three times larger than the wounded-nucleon model.
- For the toy model with ε=0, the scaled standard deviation is σ(f₂*)/f₂* = √(4/π − 1) ≈ 0.523.
- The mixed model uses α = 0.145 at √s_NN = 200 GeV, based on fits to particle multiplicities from Back (2004).
- The hot-spot model uses σ_hot-spot = 0.5 mb for semi-hard binary collisions.
- The hot-spot+Γ model uses κ = 0.5, giving var(w) = 5.

## Context

This work extends earlier event-by-event hydrodynamic studies by Aguiar (2000, 2001) that showed fluctuations of the initial shape lead to quantitatively relevant effects for azimuthal asymmetry signatures. It provides quantitative comparisons across multiple Glauber-like models, complementing color-glass condensate calculations by Hirano (2005) and Drescher (2006). The results enable predictions for elliptic flow fluctuations measured by Alver (2006, 2007) and Sorensen (2006), and suggest that measuring v₄* fluctuations could test smooth hydrodynamic evolution assumptions, with the specific prediction σ(v₄)/v₄ = 2σ(v₂)/v₂.
