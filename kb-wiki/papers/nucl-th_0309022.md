# Effect of color superconductivity on the mass and radius of a quark star

> We compare quark stars made of color-superconducting quark matter to normal-conducting quark stars. We focus on the most simple color-superconducting system, a two-flavor color superconductor, and employ the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the gap parameter and the equation of state. By varying the strength of the four-fermion coupling of the NJL model, we study the mass and the radius of the quark star as a function of the value of the gap parameter. If the coupling constant exceeds a critical value, the gap parameter does not vanish even at zero density. For coupling constants below this critical value, mass and radius of a color-superconducting quark star change at most by ca. 20% compared to a star consisting of normal-conducting quark matter. For coupling constants above the critical value mass and radius may change by factors of two or more.

## Key claim

For coupling constants below a critical value, the mass and radius of a color-superconducting quark star change by at most about 20% compared to a normal-conducting quark star; only when the coupling constant exceeds this critical value (so that the gap parameter persists even at zero density) can the mass and radius change by factors of two or more.

## Method

The authors model two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a point-like four-fermion interaction, deriving the gap equation and equation of state via the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism. They impose electric and color charge neutrality conditions, include electrons and non-interacting strange quarks as needed, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to compute mass-radius relations for pure quark stars, varying the four-fermion coupling strength to study its effect on the gap parameter and stellar properties.

## Key numbers

* The critical coupling constant separating the two regimes is not given a numerical value, but below it the mass and radius change by at most ~20% relative to normal-conducting quark matter.
* Above the critical coupling, mass and radius may change by factors of two or more compared to normal-conducting quark stars.
* The model parameters used for numerical results are: strange quark mass \(m_s = 0.1407\ \mathrm{GeV}\), coupling \(g^2/\Lambda^2 = 45.1467\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), cutoff \(\kappa = 0.6533\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and MIT bag constant \(B^{1/4} = 0.17\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of color-superconducting quark stars (Prakash, Baldo, Steiner, Buballa and Oertel, Lugones and Horvath, Alford and Reddy, Grigorian, Banik, Blaschke, Shovkovy) that typically found ~20% changes in mass and radius for standard parameter choices. By systematically varying the coupling strength, the authors identify a threshold beyond which color superconductivity produces much larger effects, and they focus on pure quark stars (rather than hybrid stars) to isolate the influence of the gap parameter from hadronic transition effects. This enables future observational tests: if quark stars exist, their measured masses and radii could distinguish whether the interior is in the weak- or strong-gap regime.
