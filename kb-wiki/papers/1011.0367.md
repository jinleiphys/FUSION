# Structure of p-shell hypernuclei

> Shell-model calculations that include both Lambda and Sigma configurations with p-shell cores are used to interpret gamma-ray transitions in Lambda-7Li, -9Be, -10B, -11B, -12C, -15N, and -16O observed with the Hyperball array of Ge detectors. It is shown that the data puts strong constraints on the spin dependence of the Lambda-N effective interaction and that the Lambda-Sigma coupling plays an important role.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations that include both Λ and Σ configurations with p-shell cores can reproduce the measured γ-ray transitions in p-shell hypernuclei, but a larger value of the spin-spin matrix element Δ is required near the beginning of the shell (most certainly for ⁷ΛLi) than for heavier hypernuclei, and the nuclear-spin-dependent spin-orbit term S_N does not provide a large enough contribution for mid-shell hypernuclei.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed using a Hamiltonian consisting of an empirical p-shell core Hamiltonian, a single-particle hyperon Hamiltonian supplying the ~80 MeV Λ-Σ mass difference, and a YN interaction parametrized in terms of five two-body matrix elements (V̄, Δ, S_Λ, S_N, T) for the direct ΛN interaction plus corresponding parameters for the Λ-Σ coupling interaction. The basis states used a weak-coupling form |(p^nα_cJ_cT_c, j_Yt_Y)JT⟩, with core energies taken from experiment where possible, and one-body density-matrix elements from p-shell wave functions were used to couple Λ and Σ configurations.

## Key numbers

- The ground-state doublet spacing in ⁷ΛLi is 692 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 693 keV from the parameter set Δ=0.430, S_Λ=-0.015, S_N=-0.390, T=0.030 (all in MeV)
- The excited-state doublet spacing in ⁷ΛLi is 471 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 494 keV
- The ⁹ΛBe doublet spacing is 43 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 44 keV
- The ¹¹ΛB ground-state doublet spacing is 264 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 267 keV
- The ¹¹ΛB excited-state doublet spacing is 505 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 475 keV
- The ¹²ΛC ground-state doublet spacing is 161 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 153 keV
- The ¹⁶ΛO ground-state doublet spacing is 26 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 23 keV
- The ¹⁶ΛO excited-state doublet spacing is 224 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 248 keV
- The ¹⁵ΛN excited-state doublet spacing is 481 keV (experimental), with a calculated value of 507 keV
- The Λ-Σ coupling parameters used are V̄'=1.45, Δ'=3.04, S_Λ'=S_N'=-0.09, T'=0.16 (all in MeV), based on G-matrix calculations for the nsc97e,f interactions

## Context

This work extends the pioneering shell-model calculations of Gal, Soper, and Dalitz (1971) and the early γ-ray era calculations of Millener (1985) and Fetisov (1991), which used one or more of the three Cohen and Kurath interactions (1965). The paper uses the 22 γ-rays observed with the Hyperball and Hyperball-2 Ge-detector arrays from experiments at KEK and BNL (1998-2005) to constrain the spin dependence of the ΛN effective interaction. The results enable future experimental tests with the new Hyperball-J detector at J-PARC and theoretical extensions that expand the nuclear core basis and explicitly include ΛNN interactions.
