# Dynamic Magnetic Response of Quark-Gluon Plasma to Electromagnetic Fields

> We investigate the electromagnetic response of a viscous quark-gluon plasma in the framework of the relativistic Boltzmann equation with current conserving collision term. Our formalism incorporates dissipative effects at all orders in linear response to the electromagnetic field while accounting for the full space- and time-dependence of the perturbing fields. As an example, we consider the collision of two nuclei in a stationary, homogeneous quark-gluon plasma. We show that for large collision energies the induced magnetic fields are governed by the response of quark-gluon plasma along the light-cone. In this limit we derive an analytic expression for the magnetic field along the beam axis between the receding nuclei and show that its strength varies only weakly with collision energy for $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \ge 30\,$GeV.

## Key claim

The induced magnetic field in a quark-gluon plasma at high collision energies is governed by the plasma response along the light-cone, and its strength varies only weakly with collision energy for \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \ge 30\) GeV, but this result holds only for the idealized case of a stationary, homogeneous plasma with a baryon-symmetric composition.

## Method

The authors solved Maxwell's equations in a homogeneous, stationary polarizable medium using the full frequency- and wave-vector-dependent relativistic polarization tensor derived from the Vlasov-Boltzmann equation with a current-conserving BGK collision term. They modeled the external current from two colliding nuclei as moving Gaussian charge distributions and computed the magnetic field at the collision center by Fourier transforming the self-consistent potential that includes the back-reaction of the induced current to all orders in the Debye screening mass. The calculation was performed for Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200\) GeV with impact parameter \(b = 6.4\) fm and QGP temperature \(T = 300\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Electromagnetic Debye mass at \(T = 300\) MeV: \(m_D = 74\) MeV.
- Quark damping rate at \(T = 300\) MeV: \(\kappa = 4.86\, m_D\).
- Static conductivity: \(\sigma_0 = 5.01\) MeV.
- Collision time for Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200\) GeV: \(t_\text{coll} = 0.086\) fm/c (using Gaussian radius \(R = 4.33\) fm).
- Relaxation time: \(\tau_\text{rel} = 0.36\) fm/c.
- Decay time: \(t_\sigma = 59\) fm/c.
- The magnetic field strength varies only weakly with collision energy for \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} \ge 30\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier analytic calculations that assumed a static conductivity (Tuchin 2013) by incorporating the full frequency and wave-vector dependence of the QGP response, building on the polarization tensor derived by Formanek (2021). It also contrasts with numerical magneto-hydrodynamic approaches (Inghirami 2016, 2019) and Vlasov-Boltzmann-Maxwell solutions (Yan 2021, Wang 2021) by providing analytic parametric predictions. The results enable improved modeling of the magnetic field evolution relevant to the chiral magnetic effect and hyperon polarization in heavy-ion collisions.
