# Quarkonium states in a complex-valued potential

> We calculate quarkonium binding energies using a realistic complex-valued potential for both an isotropic and anisotropic quark-gluon plasma. We determine the disassociation temperatures of the ground and first excited states considering both the real and imaginary parts of the binding energy. We show that the effect of momentum-space anisotropy is smaller on the imaginary part of the binding energy than on the real part of the binding energy. In the case that one assumes an isotropic plasma, we find disassociation temperatures for the J/psi, Upsilon and chi_b of 1.6 T_c, 2.8 T_c, and 1.5 T_c, respectively. We find that a finite oblate momentum-space anisotropy increases the disassociation temperature for all states considered and results in a splitting of the p-wave states associated with the chi_b first excited state of bottomonium.

## Key claim
The paper calculates quarkonium binding energies using a complex-valued potential that includes both real and imaginary parts, finding that momentum-space anisotropy has a smaller effect on the imaginary part of the binding energy than on the real part. The dissociation temperatures quoted in the abstract assume an isotropic plasma and depend on the specific definition of dissociation as the point where the real and imaginary parts of the binding energy become comparable.

## Method
The authors solved the three-dimensional time-independent Schrodinger equation on a lattice using the finite difference time domain method, evolving an initial random wavefunction in imaginary time to extract ground and excited state eigenvalues. The potential combined a real part based on the Karsch-Mehr-Satz model with entropy modifications and an imaginary part from leading-order perturbative calculations of Landau damping, both extended to include momentum-space anisotropy via a distribution function parameterized by the anisotropy parameter xi.

## Key numbers
- Dissociation temperature for J/psi in isotropic plasma: 1.6 T_c (where T_c = 192 MeV)
- Dissociation temperature for Upsilon in isotropic plasma: 2.8 T_c
- Dissociation temperature for chi_b in isotropic plasma: 1.5 T_c
- For J/psi with xi=0, imaginary part slope: 4.9 x 10^-2 GeV in the range 1-3 T_c
- For J/psi with xi=1, imaginary part slope: 6.4 x 10^-2 GeV in the range 1-3 T_c
- For Upsilon with xi=0, imaginary part slope: 2.8 x 10^-2 GeV in the range 1-4 T_c
- For Upsilon with xi=1, imaginary part slope: 4.2 x 10^-2 GeV in the range 1-4 T_c
- Splitting between L_z = 0 and L_z = +/- 1 states of chi_b: approximately 60 MeV
- String tension fixed at sigma = 0.223 GeV; strong coupling alpha = 0.385
- Charm quark mass: 1.3 GeV; bottom quark mass: 4.7 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Dumitru (2007) that considered only the real part of the quarkonium potential in an anisotropic plasma, and by Burnier (2009) and Dumitru (2009) who included the imaginary part. The authors compare their numerical results to the analytic estimate of Dumitru (2010) and find that the imaginary part of the binding energy is approximately linear in temperature near the phase transition, but with a smaller slope than the analytic prediction, which they attribute to the use of Coulombic wavefunctions in the analytic estimate versus the more realistic potential used here. The results enable more accurate predictions of quarkonium suppression patterns in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, where initial momentum-space anisotropies can persist for 1-10 fm/c.
