# Universality of multiplicity distribution in proton-proton and electron-positron collisions

> It is argued that the multiplicity distribution in proton-proton ($pp$) collisions, which is often parameterized by the negative binomial distribution, may result from the multiplicity distribution measured in electron-positron ($e^{+}e^{-}$) collisions, once the fluctuating energy carried by two leading protons in $pp$ is taken into account.

# Universality of multiplicity distribution in proton-proton and electron-positron collisions

## Key claim
The broad multiplicity distributions observed in proton-proton collisions, typically parameterized by the negative binomial distribution, may arise from the narrower multiplicity distributions measured in electron-positron collisions once the event-by-event fluctuation of energy carried away by two leading protons is taken into account. The author states this as a demonstration rather than a proof, noting the analysis is of "qualitative character" and the leading proton x-distribution is an "educated guess."

## Method
The author relates the proton-proton multiplicity distribution to the electron-positron multiplicity distribution via an integral over the beta-distributed momentum fractions of two leading protons, using the effective energy available for particle production. The electron-positron multiplicity distribution is assumed to follow a negative binomial distribution with mean parameterized as a power law in center-of-mass energy and inverse-k parameterized as linear in log energy. The leading proton x-distribution parameters are constrained by requiring the model to reproduce the known empirical relation between mean multiplicities in the two systems.

## Key numbers
- At sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the model gives k=5.8 for proton-proton collisions (full phase-space), compared to experimental data k=4.8±0.4.
- At sqrt(s)=30.4 GeV, the model gives k=12.7 for proton-proton collisions, compared to experimental data k=9.2±0.9.
- At sqrt(s)=1800 GeV, the model gives k=3.8 for proton-proton collisions, compared to experimental data k=3.1±0.1.
- The mean number of charged particles in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is 21.1 in the model, compared to experimental data 21.4±0.6.
- The coefficient of inelasticity K=0.35 in the relation N_pp(sqrt(s)) = N_ee(K*sqrt(s)) + 2 is found to be approximately energy independent from sqrt(s)=30 to 1800 GeV.
- The exponent alpha in the mean multiplicity parameterization N_ee(sqrt(s)) = a + b*s^alpha is approximately 0.17.
- The leading proton x-distribution parameters are lambda ≈ -0.8 and mu = -0.7, with mean x = 0.4.

## Context
This work extends the empirical relation for mean multiplicities between proton-proton and electron-positron collisions (Grosse-Oetringhaus 2009) to the full multiplicity distribution, building on earlier observations by Basile (1980, 1982) that differences between the two systems can be understood via the leading proton effect. The author notes that the coefficient K=0.35 is often interpreted as a manifestation of the three-quark structure of the nucleon (Chliapnikov 1990, Hoang 1994, Sarkisyan 2010, 2016). The approach enables future investigation of full multiplicity distributions in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the author suggests it would be interesting to test whether similar universality holds in those systems.
