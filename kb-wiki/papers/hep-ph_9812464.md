# A simple Dirac wave function for a Coulomb potential with linear confinement

> A simple analytical solution is found to the Dirac equation for the combination of a Coulomb potential with a linear confining potential. An appropriate linear combination of Lorentz scalar and vector linear potentials, with the scalar part dominating, can be chosen to give a simple Dirac wave function. The binding energy depends only on the Coulomb strength, and is not affected by the linear potential. The method works for the ground state, or for the lowest state with $l=j-{1/2}$, for any $j$.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution to the Dirac equation for a Coulomb potential combined with a linear confining potential, but only for the ground state or the lowest state with \(l=j-1/2\) for any \(j\), and only when the scalar part of the linear potential dominates over the vector part and the two confining constants satisfy a specific constraint.

## Method

The Dirac equation is solved with a potential consisting of a Coulomb term \(-\lambda/r\), a Lorentz scalar linear term \(\beta\mu r\), and a Lorentz vector linear term \(\nu r\). A trial wave function is chosen for the upper spinor \(u\) of the form \(u = r^{b-1} e^{-ar} e^{-\frac{1}{2}\alpha^2 r^2}\chi\), which is the exact Coulomb solution multiplied by a Gaussian factor. By matching terms in the coupled Dirac equations, the parameters \(a\), \(b\), \(E\), and \(\alpha^2\) are determined, and the method is extended to the lowest excited state with \(l=j-1/2\) by substituting \(\lambda \rightarrow \lambda/\kappa\) where \(\kappa = j+1/2\).

## Key numbers

- The binding energy is \(E = m\sqrt{1-\lambda^2}\), identical to the pure Coulomb case, independent of the linear confining potential.
- The parameter \(b = \sqrt{1-\lambda^2}\) for the ground state, and \(b = \sqrt{\kappa^2 - \lambda^2}\) for the lowest excited state with \(l=j-1/2\).
- The Gaussian constant is \(\alpha^2 = \lambda\mu\), where \(\mu\) is the scalar linear confining strength.
- The vector and scalar linear confining constants must satisfy \(\nu = -\mu\sqrt{1-\lambda^2}\), so the vector part is smaller in magnitude than the scalar part.

## Context

This work provides a simple relativistic wave function for quark model calculations, extending the exact Coulomb-Dirac solution to include linear confinement, which is suggested by QCD. The approach contrasts with more complex numerical or non-relativistic treatments of confined systems, and the author notes that radially excited states cannot be written as simply and would have energies depending on the confinement constants. The method enables parameterization of physical quantities in quark models using a simple but physically reasonable relativistic wave function.
