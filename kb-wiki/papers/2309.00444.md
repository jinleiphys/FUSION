# Correlation function for the $T_{bb}$ state: Determination of the binding, scattering lengths, effective ranges and molecular probabilities

> We perform a study of the $B^{*+}B^0,B^{*0}B^+$ correlation functions using an extension of the local hidden gauge approach which provides the interaction from the exchange of light vector mesons and gives rise to a bound state of these components in $I=0$ with a binding energy of about $21$~MeV. After that, we face the inverse problem of determining the low energy observables, scattering length and effective range for each channel, the possible existence of a bound state, and, if found, the couplings of such a state to each $B^{*+}B^0,B^{*0}B^+$ component as well as the molecular probabilities of each of the channels. We use the bootstrap method to determine these magnitudes and find that, with errors in the correlation function typical of present experiments, we can determine all these magnitudes with acceptable precision. In addition, the size of the source function of the experiment from where the correlation functions are measured can be also determined with a high precision.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that from smooth, structureless correlation functions of the $B^{*+}B^0$ and $B^{*0}B^+$ pairs, one can extract the existence of a bound $T_{bb}$ state with a binding energy of about 22 MeV, along with scattering lengths, effective ranges, molecular probabilities, and the source size, with the caveat that the sign of the off-diagonal potential $V_{12}$ cannot be determined from correlation functions alone, requiring external input from heavy quark flavor symmetry to assign the correct isospin.

## Method

The authors first compute the $B^{*+}B^0$ and $B^{*0}B^+$ correlation functions using the local hidden gauge approach with vector-meson exchange, obtaining the $T$-matrix from a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation regularized with a cutoff $q_{\rm max}=420$ MeV. They then generate synthetic correlation function data with 31 points per channel and 10% errors, and perform the inverse problem by fitting a general isospin-symmetric potential (allowing for non-molecular contributions via energy-dependent terms) using the bootstrap method with 50 resamplings to extract observables and their uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy from best fit: 20.62 MeV ($R^{\rm input}=1$ fm) and 18.48 MeV ($R^{\rm input}=5$ fm), compared to the input 21 MeV from Dai (2022)
- Bootstrap binding energy: $B = -22 \pm 15$ MeV ($R^{\rm input}=1$ fm) and $B = -22 \pm 21$ MeV ($R^{\rm input}=5$ fm)
- Scattering length for channel 1 ($B^{*+}B^0$): $a_1 = 0.85 \pm 0.18$ fm ($R^{\rm input}=1$ fm) and $0.85 \pm 0.19$ fm ($R^{\rm input}=5$ fm)
- Scattering length for channel 2 ($B^{*0}B^+$): $a_2 = (0.81 \pm 0.13) - i(0.03 \pm 0.03)$ fm ($R^{\rm input}=1$ fm) and $(0.77 \pm 0.13) - i(0.05 \pm 0.06)$ fm ($R^{\rm input}=5$ fm)
- Molecular probabilities: $P_1 = 0.44 \pm 0.06$, $P_2 = 0.43 \pm 0.05$ ($R^{\rm input}=1$ fm); $P_1 = 0.41 \pm 0.11$, $P_2 = 0.39 \pm 0.11$ ($R^{\rm input}=5$ fm)
- Non-molecular probability: $Z = 0.13 \pm 0.11$ ($R^{\rm input}=1$ fm) and $0.19 \pm 0.22$ ($R^{\rm input}=5$ fm)
- Recovered source size: $R = 0.974 \pm 0.024$ fm (input 1 fm) and $R = 5.052 \pm 0.614$ fm (input 5 fm)
- Threshold masses: 10604.37 MeV for $B^{*+}B^0$ and 10604.96 MeV for $B^{*0}B^+$

## Context

This work extends the inverse-problem methodology previously applied to the $D_{s0}(2317)$ state by Ikeno (2023) and to the $T_{cc}$ state by Albaladejo (2023), now targeting the bottom-sector analogue $T_{bb}$ predicted by Dai (2022). The results show that correlation function measurements, even with current experimental precision, can determine binding energies, scattering lengths, effective ranges, and molecular probabilities for the $T_{bb}$ state, and can distinguish its $I=0$ nature from an $I=1$ alternative using heavy quark flavor symmetry arguments. The formalism is also extendable to related $BB_s^*$ and $B^*B^*$ systems predicted in the same framework.
