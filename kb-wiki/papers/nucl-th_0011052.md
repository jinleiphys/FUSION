# Ground state correlations and anharmonicity of vibrations

> A consistent treatment of the ground state correlations beyond the random phase approximation including their influence on the pairing and phonon-phonon coupling in nuclei is presented. A new general system of nonlinear equations for the quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) is derived. It is shown that this system contains as a particular case all equations derived for the QPM early. New additional Pauli principle corrections resulting in the anharmonical shifts of energies of the two-phonon configurations are found. A correspondence between the generalized QPM equations and the nuclear field theory is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new general system of nonlinear equations for the quasiparticle phonon model (QPM) that consistently includes ground state correlations beyond the random phase approximation (RPA) and their influence on pairing and phonon-phonon coupling, finding that additional Pauli principle corrections beyond those considered in prior extended QPM work produce anharmonic shifts in the energies of two-phonon configurations.

## Method

The authors start from the QPM Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon mean field, pairing, and separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual forces, then apply the equation of motion approach to derive a self-consistent system of equations for the extended RPA (ERPA) phonon vacuum, including quasiparticle distributions in the ground state. Using these ERPA phonons as a basis, they construct wave functions for excited states as superpositions of one- and two-phonon configurations and derive a generalized secular equation for the energies and amplitudes, keeping terms containing the first power of the Pauli correction coefficients K and K_i and products KK, while neglecting products K_iK_i. The resulting matrix elements include anharmonic shifts of two-phonon configuration energies arising from the fermion structure of the phonon operators, which are expressed as sums of three contributions (Delta omega_1, Delta omega_2, Delta omega_3).

## Key numbers

* The anharmonic shift Delta omega_1 is shown to be much larger than Delta omega_2 and Delta omega_3 in the case where the forward-going phonon amplitudes psi are much larger than the backward-going amplitudes phi (psi >> phi).
* The shifts Delta omega_2 and Delta omega_3 become zero when the backward-going amplitudes phi = 0.
* The matrix element W_1 (Eq. r2.1) differs from the ERPA phonon energy due to corrections that include phonon amplitudes of the next orders, and these corrections become zero if phi = 0.
* The system of equations (EQ) reduces to the usual QPM equations when q_j = 0, X_1 = 0, and K_i = 0, and to equations describing coupling of one- and two-RPA phonons without Pauli principle when q_j = 0, K = 0, and K_i = 0.

## Context

This work extends the extended QPM equations developed in earlier papers by KVCGS98, SVK99, and VKCS00, which already included ground state correlations and some Pauli principle corrections but used the so-called quasidiagonal approximation for those corrections. The present paper removes that approximation and finds additional Pauli principle corrections that produce anharmonic shifts of two-phonon configuration energies. The authors discuss the correspondence between their generalized QPM equations and the Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and Bertsch (1977), showing that for the simplified case of only isoscalar particle-hole forces with q_j = 0, the anharmonic shifts correspond to NFT diagrams. The formalism enables more accurate calculations of nuclear vibrational states, particularly for low-lying states and double giant dipole resonances observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
