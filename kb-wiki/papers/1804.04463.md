# Large proton cumulants from the superposition of ordinary multiplicity distributions

> We construct a multiplicity distribution characterized by large factorial cumulants (integrated correlation functions) from a simple combination of two ordinary multiplicity distributions characterized by small factorial cumulants. We find that such a model, which could be interpreted as representing two event classes, reproduces the preliminary data for the proton cumulants measured by the STAR collaboration at $7.7$ GeV very well. This model then predicts very large values for the fifth and sixth order factorial cumulants, which can be tested in experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple superposition of two ordinary multiplicity distributions (each with small factorial cumulants), interpreted as representing two distinct event classes, can reproduce the unexpectedly large proton factorial cumulants observed by STAR at 7.7 GeV, and predicts that the fifth and sixth order factorial cumulants will be very large if this interpretation is correct.

## Method

The authors construct a two-component model where the proton multiplicity distribution is a weighted sum of two independent distributions: a binomial distribution (with B=350) to capture baryon number conservation for the dominant event class, and a Poisson distribution for a rare second event class with probability α. They fit the mean proton number and the third and fourth order factorial cumulants from preliminary STAR data to extract the model parameters α, the mean difference between the two classes, and the binomial parameter p.

## Key numbers

- At √sNN = 7.7 GeV, the preliminary STAR data give ⟨N⟩ ≈ 40, C2 ≈ -2, C3 ≈ -10, C4 ≈ 170
- The fit yields α ≈ 0.0033, the mean difference between classes ≈ 14.7, and p ≈ 0.114, giving ⟨N(a)⟩ ≈ 40 and ⟨N(b)⟩ ≈ 25.3
- The model predicts C5 ≈ -2645 and C6 ≈ 40900 for the factorial cumulants
- The predicted cumulant ratios are K5/K2 ≈ -34 and K6/K2 ≈ 312
- The ratio C4/C3 ≈ -17 ± 6 from the data, and the model predicts C6/C5 ≈ C5/C4 ≈ C4/C3

## Context

This work addresses the unexpectedly large four-particle correlations (factorial cumulants) observed in preliminary STAR data at 7.7 GeV, which are about three orders of magnitude larger than predictions from a basic Glauber model with baryon number conservation (Bzdak 2016). The two-event-class model naturally explains the observed scaling Cn ∼ ⟨N⟩n, consistent with the large correlation length in rapidity and transverse momentum reported in earlier analyses (Bzdak 2016, Bzdak 2017). The predictions for C5 and C6 can be tested in the next phase of the RHIC beam energy scan, and the authors suggest experimental checks such as measuring elliptic flow in low-proton-number events to distinguish between physical phase transition signals and experimental artifacts like misidentified peripheral events.
