# Gravitational form factors of pions, kaons and nucleons from dispersion relations

> The gravitational form factors of pions, kaons and the nucleons are investigated by employing modern dispersive techniques and chiral perturbation theory. We determine the gravitational form factors of pions and kaons, extending our analysis to explore the pion mass dependence of these form factors at several unphysical pion masses up to 391 MeV, for which lattice results exist for the meson-meson scattering phase shifts. We also review our analysis on the nucleon gravitational form factors at the physical pion mass, and then systematically calculate various three-dimensional spatial and two-dimensional transverse density distributions for the nucleons. These results provide new insights into the mass distribution inside nucleons. As a by-product, we match our dispersion relation results and those obtained from chiral perturbation theory with external gravitational source at the next-to-next-to-leading order, yielding values for the low-energy constants $c_8=-4.28_{-0.38}^{+0.37} ~\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$ and $c_9=-0.68_{-0.05}^{+0.06} ~\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$. These results offer a robust benchmark for future experimental and theoretical studies.

## Key claim

The authors use dispersion relations combined with chiral perturbation theory to determine the gravitational form factors of pions, kaons, and nucleons in a model-independent way, with the caveat that the results for pions and kaons rely on the dominance of the $\pi\pi$ and $K\bar{K}$ intermediate states in the low-energy region and on matching to next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory at low momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors employ the Källén-Lehmann spectral representation and elastic unitarity relations to compute the spectral functions of the gravitational form factors, using $\pi\pi$ and $K\bar{K}$ intermediate states for pions and kaons, and extending the dispersive framework to nucleons. The pion and kaon gravitational form factors are matched to next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory at low momentum transfer, and the nucleon gravitational form factors are analyzed at the physical pion mass to compute three-dimensional spatial and two-dimensional transverse density distributions.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy constants extracted from matching dispersion relations to chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-leading order are $c_8 = -4.28_{-0.38}^{+0.37}~\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$ and $c_9 = -0.68_{-0.05}^{+0.06}~\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$.
- The pion mass dependence of the gravitational form factors is explored at unphysical pion masses up to 391 MeV, for which lattice results exist for meson-meson scattering phase shifts.
- The nucleon $D$-term from the dispersive analysis in the authors' prior work (Cao 2024) is $D = -3.38_{-0.35}^{+0.34}$.
- The chiral perturbation theory results for the $D$-terms at physical masses are $D^\pi = -0.97 \pm 0.01$, $D^K = -0.76 \pm 0.09$, and $D^\eta = -0.65 \pm 0.12$, using the physical pion decay constant $F_\pi = 92.1~\mathrm{MeV}$.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of pion gravitational form factors pioneered by Donoghue (1990) and improved by Broniowski (2024) by incorporating coupled-channel $\pi\pi$-$K\bar{K}$ effects and matching to chiral perturbation theory. It also builds on the authors' prior framework (Cao 2024) for nucleon gravitational form factors, providing new insights into mass distributions inside nucleons and offering a benchmark for future lattice QCD calculations such as those by Hackett (2023) at $m_\pi = 170$ MeV.
