# A Universality Emerging in a Universality: Derivation of the Ericson Transition in Stochastic Quantum Scattering and Experimental Validation

> At lower energies, the resonances in scattering experiments are often isolated. In quantum chaotic many-body, disordered or generically stochastic systems, the resonances overlap at larger energies. Eventually, the Ericson regime is reached in which the cross section behaves like a random function. The scattering matrix elements then follow a universal Gaussian distribution. For more than sixty years, the emergence of this robust additional universal behavior on top of the universal system stochasticity awaits a concise analytical treatment. We derive the transition to the Ericson regime in the universal Heidelberg approach and prove the universal Gaussian distribution by a proper asymptotic expansion. We also obtain explicit formulae for the moments of the distributions. We compare with microwave experiments and numerical simulations.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytically, for the first time, the transition to the Ericson regime in stochastic quantum scattering, proving that the off-diagonal scattering matrix elements become Gaussian distributed in the limit of large resonance overlap, with explicit asymptotic corrections that capture the full transition.

## Method

The authors use the Heidelberg approach, modeling the Hamiltonian as a random matrix from the Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE, β=2) and employing supersymmetry techniques to obtain exact integral expressions for the characteristic function of the scattering matrix elements. They perform an asymptotic expansion in powers of 1/Ξ (where Ξ = Γ/D is the ratio of average resonance width to mean level spacing) by splitting the integration domain into disconnected and connected parts, applying Watson's lemma, and expanding the channel factor. The analytical results are validated against microwave network experiments and Monte Carlo simulations of random matrices.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless Ericson parameter is defined as Ξ = Γ/D = (1/2π) Σ_c T_c, where T_c are transmission coefficients (Weisskopf estimate).
- For the experimental validation: T_1 = T_2 = 0.967, absorption parameter τ_abs = 7.013, leading to Ξ ≈ 1.424 with M = 52 channels (50 fictitious absorption channels plus 2 strong channels).
- For deep Ericson regime validation: T_c = 1 for M = 60 channels, corresponding to Ξ ≈ 9.55.
- The leading-order Gaussian distribution for rescaled variables ξ_s = √Ξ x_s has variance determined by (g_a^+ + 1)(g_b^+ + 1)π/2, where g_c^+ = 2/T_c - 1.
- The subleading correction to the distribution at ξ_s = 0 is P_s^(sl)(0) = 3(g_a^+ g_b^+ - g_a^+ - g_b^+ - 3) / [8πΞ √(2(g_a^+ + 1)(g_b^+ + 1))].
- The leading-order cross-section distribution is exponential: p^(l)(σ̃_ab) = exp(-σ̃_ab), with mean ⟨σ_ab⟩ = 2/(πΞ(g_a^+ + 1)(g_b^+ + 1)) + O(Ξ^{-2}).

## Context

This work solves a sixty-year-old problem originating from Ericson's 1960s studies of overlapping resonances in nuclear scattering (Ericson, 1960). It extends the Heidelberg approach (Verbaarschot, 1985; Weidenmüller, 1995) by providing the first analytical derivation of the Gaussian distribution for off-diagonal S-matrix elements in the Ericson regime, which was previously only understood phenomenologically (Agassi, 1975). The results enable quantitative predictions for the transition from isolated to overlapping resonances in quantum chaotic systems, microwave cavities, electronic transport, and ultracold atomic-molecular systems, with the subleading corrections already sufficient to describe experimental data even at moderate Ξ values.
