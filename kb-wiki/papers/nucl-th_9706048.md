# Baryon Number Transport in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Recent SPS data on the rapidity distribution of protons in p+S, p+Au and S+S collisions at 200 AGeV and preliminary Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV are compared to HIJING and VENUS calculations as well as to predictions based on the Multi-Chain Model (MCM). The preliminary Pb data suggest that a linear dependence of the proton rapidity shift as a function of the nuclear thickness, as first observed in p+A reactions, may apply up to Pb+Pb reactions. The observed rapidity dependence of produced hyperons in both p+A and A+A reactions however cannot be explained in terms of such models without introducing additional non-linear effects.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the linear dependence of the proton rapidity shift on nuclear thickness, first observed in p+A reactions, may apply up to Pb+Pb reactions at SPS energies, but this conclusion is subject to caveats from preliminary data and does not extend to hyperon production, which requires additional non-linear dynamical mechanisms.

## Method

The authors compare preliminary SPS data on proton and hyperon rapidity distributions from p+S, p+Au, S+S (200 AGeV) and Pb+Pb (160 AGeV) collisions to three models: the Multi-Chain Model (MCM), HIJING1.3, and VENUS4.12. They use Glauber theory to separate nuclear geometry from multiple collision dynamics, with MCM parameterizing baryon transport via a single parameter α that controls the mean rapidity shift per inelastic collision. The analysis tests whether the linear extrapolation of baryon stopping power from p+A to A+A collisions holds, and examines the correlation between baryon number transport and energy degradation using veto calorimeter data.

## Key numbers

- The MCM parameter α = 3 ± 1, obtained from fits to Barton et al. (1983) p+A → p+X data at 100 GeV, gives a mean rapidity shift ⟨y⟩_n = 1 + (n-1)/α per collision number n.
- The fraction of incident protons that remain protons after fragmentation is r_p = 0.53, away from diffractive peaks.
- For p+S collisions at 200 AGeV, the mean rapidity shift in all three models is Δy ≈ 1.3; for p+Au, MCM gives Δy ≈ 2 while data suggest Δy ≈ 2.5.
- For central Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV, the average number of collisions per wounded nucleon is ⟨ν⟩ ≈ 4.2.
- The estimated central baryon density at RHIC is ρ_B ≈ ρ_0 (0.7 fm/τ), where ρ_0 ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The inelastic pp cross section used is σ_in ≈ 32 mb.
- The preliminary Pb+Pb Λ distribution peak is dN(Λ)/dy = 23 ± 3, giving Λ/p ≈ 0.7 if confirmed.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of baryon stopping power from Busza and Goldhaber (1984) and the Multi-Chain Model of Date et al., applying it to new SPS heavy-ion data from NA35, NA49, and NA44. The paper contradicts the suggestion in Kharzeev (1996) that conventional baryon trajectory exchange cannot reproduce nuclear stopping in nucleus-nucleus reactions, showing that MCM with α=3 fits the central baryon data. The anomalous hyperon production, already evident in p+S reactions, points to the need for non-linear mechanisms such as the double string hypothesis in VENUS, and the results enable future tests at RHIC where the rapidity gap opens to over 10 units.
