# Calculating Fluctuations and Self-Correlations Numerically for Causal Charge Diffusion in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We study the propagation and diffusion of electric charge fluctuations in the Bjorken hydrodynamic model with both white and Catteneo noise using purely numerical methods. We show that a global lattice of noise fluctuations is required to fully calculate the two-point correlators of charge. We solve the stochastic differential equations that arise from the charge conservation equation on the lattice. We explicitly identify the self-correlation term in the case of Catteneo noise and provide a physical interpretation. We provide a numerical recipe to remove this contribution from the full two-point correlators. Finally, we calculate the balance functions for charged hadrons. By limiting the speed of signal propagation, we observe the expected narrowing of the balance functions after removing the self-correlations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a global lattice of noise fluctuations is required to fully calculate two-point correlators of charge in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and that for Cattaneo (colored) noise the self-correlation term is non-trivial and must be explicitly identified and subtracted from the full two-point correlators to obtain physically meaningful balance functions.

## Method

The authors solve stochastic differential equations arising from charge conservation on a 1+1 dimensional spacetime lattice in the Bjorken hydrodynamic model, using finite difference methods with approximately 1-10 million random events. For white noise, the noise function is sampled from a normal distribution with zero mean and variance determined by the fluctuation-dissipation theorem; for colored Cattaneo noise, an additional Langevin equation is solved to generate noise with exponential time correlations characterized by a relaxation time tau_Q. The self-correlation is computed by solving a modified differential equation where the Green's function G is replaced by G/(ik), and the resulting charge-density-noise correlator is related to the self-correlation via a factor s(tau_f) tau_f / D_Q.

## Key numbers

* Diffusion constant D_Q = 0.162 fm, taken as an average over the temperature interval from 150 to 350 MeV from Ref. Aarts
* Initial proper time tau_i = 0.5 fm/c, final proper time tau_f = 6.352 fm/c
* Initial temperature T_i = 350 MeV, final temperature T_f = 150 MeV
* Lattice spacings: Delta xi = 0.09, Delta tau = 10^{-4} fm/c
* The combination (chi(tau_f) T_f)/(tau_f Delta xi) = 0.5122 MeV^3 fm^{-3}
* Electric charge susceptibility chi_Q = (2/3) T^2 (including up, down, and strange quarks)
* For white noise, the width of the Gaussian correlation is w^2 = 8 D_Q (1/tau_i - 1/tau_f)
* For colored noise, the maximum mode velocity is v_Q^2 = D_Q / tau_Q, with numerical results shown for v_Q^2 = 0.16

## Context

This work extends the analytic calculations of charge fluctuations and balance functions in the 1+1 dimensional Bjorken model by Plumberg and Kapusta (2020) to a fully numerical framework, preparing for extensions to 3+1 dimensional, non-boost-invariant hydrodynamic models where analytic solutions are impossible. The numerical method for solving stochastic differential equations with colored noise and identifying self-correlations enables future calculations of balance functions that can be compared with experimental data from the ALICE and STAR collaborations, and can be applied to study dynamic critical phenomena near a possible QCD critical point as investigated by Teaney, Stephanov, Nahrgang, and Bluhm.
