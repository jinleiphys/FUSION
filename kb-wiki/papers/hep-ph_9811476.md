# Quark deconfinement and meson properties at finite temperature

> A simple confining separable interaction Ansatz for the rainbow-ladder truncated QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations is used to study quark deconfinement and meson states at finite temperature. The model is fixed at T=0 to implement quark confinement while preserving the Goldstone mechanism for the $π$. Within the Matsubara formalism, a very slow temperature dependence is found for the $π$ and $ρ$ meson masses $M_H(T)$ until near the deconfinement temperature $T_c=143$ MeV. Related to rapid decrease of the dynamically-generated quark mass function for $T>T_c$, this model produces $π$ and $ρ$ masses that rise significantly and are better interpreted as spatial screening masses. The $T$-dependent screening mass defect $ΔM_H = 2πT-M_H(T)$ is compared to results of lattice gauge theory simulations and also to those of an infrared dominant analytic model.

## Key claim

Using a simple confining separable interaction Ansatz for rainbow-ladder truncated QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations, the π and ρ meson masses show very slow temperature dependence until near the deconfinement temperature Tc = 143 MeV, after which they rise significantly and are better interpreted as spatial screening masses rather than pole masses.

## Method

The authors employ a rank-1 separable interaction Ansatz with a Gaussian form factor for the rainbow-ladder truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) at finite temperature, using the Matsubara formalism. Meson bound states are described via the ladder-approximated Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the π described by a γ5 covariant and the ρ by a transverse γμ covariant. The model is extended to finite temperature by transcribing Euclidean quark momenta to discrete Matsubara frequencies, and meson masses are identified as spatial screening masses from the lowest Matsubara mode.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement temperature: Tc = 143 MeV
- Model parameters at T=0: m0/Λ0 = 0.0096, D0Λ0² = 128, Λ0 = 0.687 GeV
- At T=0: Mπ = 0.14 GeV, Mρ = Mω = 0.783 GeV, fπ = 0.104 GeV
- Quark condensate at T=0: ⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3} = -0.248 GeV
- ρ-γ coupling constant: gρ = 5.04
- For the infrared dominant (ID) model comparison: η = 1.107 GeV to produce the same Mω

## Context

This work extends the confining separable interaction Ansatz of Blaschke et al. (1997) to finite temperature, building on the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach at finite T and μ developed by Bender et al. (1996), Maris et al. (1997), and Blaschke and Roberts (1998). The results are compared to lattice gauge theory simulations by Gocksch (1991) and to the infrared dominant analytic model of Munczek and Nemirovsky (1983). The approach enables a simple, transparent continuum modeling of quark deconfinement and meson properties that can be extended to finite chemical potential and to the rank-2 version of the separable model for further investigation of screening mass behavior.
