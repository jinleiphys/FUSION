# Coulomb bridge mechanism for peripheral polarization of weakly bound projectiles

> We identify the matrix elements that carry peripheral polarization of weakly bound projectiles through the Feshbach dynamical polarization potential (DPP) within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework. Splitting the two P-Q bridge couplings into nuclear and Coulomb parts, while keeping a single Q-space propagator common to every term, decomposes the DPP into a nuclear, a Coulomb, and an interference component, $ΔU_{\rm DPP}=ΔU_N+ΔU_C+ΔU_{NC}$. Applied to $d+{}^{58}$Ni, ${}^{6}$Li$+{}^{208}$Pb, ${}^{11}$Be$+{}^{64}$Zn, and ${}^{8}$B$+{}^{64}$Zn, the decomposition reveals a controlled hierarchy: a nuclear bridge in the light system, a mixed bridge with strong destructive interference in the heavy stable case, and a Coulomb-dominated bridge in both halo systems, with the proton halo showing constructive nuclear-Coulomb interference. For the halo reactions, peripheral partial waves ($L\gtrsim 35$) satisfy $σ_R^L\simeqσ_{\rm DPP}^L\simeqσ_{\rm BU}^L$, with the high-$L$ DPP tail dominated by $σ_C^L$. Two diagnostic calculations isolate the responsible matrix elements: removing the off-diagonal Coulomb propagation inside Q leaves the pattern essentially intact, whereas removing the Coulomb part of the P-Q bridge collapses both DPP-induced absorption and breakup. The peripheral polarization of halo reactions is therefore a Coulomb-bridge effect, and the high-$L$ elastic-breakup yield serves as its observable signature.

# Coulomb bridge mechanism for peripheral polarization of weakly bound projectiles

## Key claim

The peripheral polarization of weakly bound halo projectiles is carried predominantly by the Coulomb part of the $P\leftrightarrow Q$ bridge couplings ($U^{(C)}_{0\gamma}$ and $U^{(C)}_{\gamma'0}$) rather than by Coulomb propagation inside the $Q$ space, and this mechanism produces a characteristic high-$L$ elastic-breakup yield that serves as its observable signature.

## Method

The authors split the $P\leftrightarrow Q$ bridge couplings in the Feshbach dynamical polarization potential (DPP) into nuclear and Coulomb components while keeping a single common $Q$-space coupled-channel propagator, yielding a decomposition $\Delta U_{\rm DPP} = \Delta U_N + \Delta U_C + \Delta U_{NC}$. They apply this decomposition within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework to four reactions: $d+{}^{58}{\rm Ni}$, ${}^{6}{\rm Li}+{}^{208}{\rm Pb}$, ${}^{11}{\rm Be}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$, and ${}^{8}{\rm B}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$. Two diagnostic calculations isolate the responsible matrix elements: removing off-diagonal Coulomb propagation inside $Q$ versus removing the Coulomb part of the $P\leftrightarrow Q$ bridge.

## Key numbers

- For ${}^{11}{\rm Be}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$ at $E_{\rm lab}=28.7$ MeV: peripheral partial waves with $L\gtrsim 35$ satisfy $\sigma_R^L \simeq \sigma_{\rm DPP}^L \simeq \sigma_{\rm BU}^L$, with the high-$L$ DPP tail dominated by $\sigma_C^L$.
- For ${}^{8}{\rm B}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$ at $E_{\rm lab}=36.11$ MeV: the Coulomb component $\sigma_C^L$ closely follows the total $\sigma_{\rm DPP}^L$ over almost the whole range of partial waves and determines the main peak near $L\simeq 25$.
- Neutron separation energy of ${}^{11}{\rm Be}$: $S_n = 504$ keV.
- Proton separation energy of ${}^{8}{\rm B}$: $S_p = 137$ keV.
- ${}^{11}{\rm Be}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$ continuum discretized up to $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=12$ MeV with $N_{\rm bin}=6$ bins per partial wave for $\ell \leq 5$.
- ${}^{8}{\rm B}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$ continuum discretized up to $\varepsilon_{\rm max}=12$ MeV with $N_{\rm bin}=6$ bins per partial wave for $\ell \leq 3$.
- ${}^{6}{\rm Li}+{}^{208}{\rm Pb}$ continuum discretized up to $\alpha$-$d$ relative kinetic energy of $12$ MeV with six bin states per partial wave for $\ell \leq 2$.
- Leading dipole effective charges: $\simeq 0.36\,e$ for ${}^{11}{\rm Be}$ and $\simeq 0.38\,e$ for ${}^{8}{\rm B}$.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative picture developed by Di Pietro et al. (2010, 2012) for ${}^{11}{\rm Be}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$ and by Spartà et al. (2021) for ${}^{8}{\rm B}+{}^{64}{\rm Zn}$, who showed by switch-off calculations that Coulomb couplings are essential to reproduce elastic scattering data. The present operator-level decomposition goes beyond sensitivity studies by identifying which specific matrix elements carry the peripheral polarization, using the Feshbach DPP formalism within CDCC previously formulated by the same authors (Liu et al., 2025, 2026). The decomposition enables future extensions to refractive observables sensitive to the real part of the DPP and to reactions on other halo nuclei such as ${}^{6}{\rm He}$.
