# Heavy Quark and Quarkonium Transport in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The strong interaction between heavy quarks and the quark gluon plasma makes the open and hidden charm hadrons be sensitive probes of the deconfinement phase transition in high energy nuclear collisions. Both the cold and hot nuclear matter effects change with the colliding energy and significantly influence the heavy quark and charmonium yield and their transverse momentum distributions. The ratio of averaged quarkonium transverse momentum square and the elliptic flow reveal the nature of the QCD medium created in heavy ion collisions at SPS, RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The ratio of averaged quarkonium transverse momentum square ($r_{AA}$) and the elliptic flow ($v_2$) reveal the nature of the QCD medium created in heavy ion collisions, but the simultaneous description of heavy quark $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$, and the partial thermalization of charmonia in small systems, remain puzzles beyond current expectations.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework for heavy quark and quarkonium transport in high energy nuclear collisions, covering the Fokker-Planck/Langevin approach for heavy quark Brownian motion in the quark-gluon plasma, the heavy quark potential from lattice QCD and effective field theories, and a transport equation for quarkonium evolution that includes both suppression (color screening, gluon dissociation, quasi-free dissociation) and regeneration (recombination of uncorrelated heavy quarks). The ratio $r_{AA} = \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{AA} / \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{pp}$ is constructed from the second moment of the transverse momentum distribution to study production and suppression mechanisms.

## Key numbers

* $J/\psi$ dissociation temperature in the limit $V=F$ (free energy) is $T_{J/\psi} \simeq 1.2 T_c$, while in the limit $V=U$ (internal energy) it is $T_{J/\psi} \simeq 2.1 T_c$, with $T_c$ the critical temperature of deconfinement.
* From lattice QCD spectral function analysis, the $J/\psi$ melting point is located at $T_{J/\psi} \simeq 1.5 T_c$.
* From a generalized Gauss law parameterization of the potential, the dissociation temperature for $J/\psi$ is $T_{J/\psi} \simeq 1.37 T_c$.
* Relativistic corrections enhance the $J/\psi$ dissociation temperature by $7\% - 13\%$ depending on the choice of potential.
* The predicted $r_{AA}$ for heavy ion collisions at LHC is less than unity and decreases toward more central collisions, confirmed by experimental results at forward rapidity.
* At SPS energy, $r_{AA}$ is always above unity and increases monotonically versus collision centrality.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on quarkonium suppression by Matsui and Satz (1986) and the sequential melting scenario supported by experimental data from STAR at RHIC and CMS at LHC. It discusses the cold nuclear matter effects (nuclear absorption, Cronin effect, nuclear shadowing) that must be fixed through p+A collisions, and the hot medium effects including color screening, gluon dissociation, quasi-free dissociation, and quarkonium regeneration. The paper enables future work on open quantum system formalisms (Akamatsu, 2015) and Hamiltonian-based approaches (Blaizot, 2016) that go beyond the adiabatic approximation for rapidly cooling QGP systems.
