# Chemical potential differentials in the QCD phase diagram from heavy-ion isobar collisions

> Temperature and baryon, charge, and strangeness chemical potentials characterize QCD matter under extreme conditions. Differences between these chemical potentials and their ratios probe conserved-charge correlations and the system's response in the multidimensional QCD phase diagram. We extract these quantities from STAR Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr isobar collisions using a Bayesian thermal analysis of hadron yields, which substantially reduces systematic uncertainties, and compare them with Taylor-expanded lattice-QCD and Chiral Mean Field model predictions. Isobar collisions thus emerge as a precision probe of four-dimensional QCD thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that small differences in the charge fraction between isobar collision systems (Ru+Ru vs. Zr+Zr) generate measurable shifts in chemical freeze-out conditions, enabling a precision probe of correlations among conserved charges in the four-dimensional QCD phase diagram. The key hedge is that this extraction relies on a Bayesian thermal analysis that fits both systems simultaneously using identified-hadron yields and net-charge yield differences, rather than fitting Ru and Zr independently, which substantially reduces systematic uncertainties but still requires theoretical input for interpretation.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian thermal analysis of hadron yields from STAR Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr isobar collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using the statistical model THERMUS with a Gaussian-process emulator as a surrogate model. Posterior distributions for freeze-out parameters (temperature T_Chem, chemical potentials mu_B, mu_Q, mu_S, fireball radius R, and strangeness-suppression factor gamma_s) were obtained via Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling, with uniform priors within physically motivated ranges and a Gaussian likelihood accounting for both emulator and experimental uncertainties. The analysis exploited a novel method isolating the net charge of the system through yield differences between isobar species, using double ratios of particle yields to cancel systematic uncertainties. Theoretical predictions were computed from the Taylor-expanded lattice-QCD equation of state (BQS) and the Chiral Mean Field model with improved meson description (mCMF), imposing strangeness neutrality and fixed charge fraction constraints.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T_Chem = 158.0_{-3.9}^{+3.9} MeV for both Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr (0-10% centrality)
- Baryon chemical potential mu_B = 20.31_{-4.0}^{+3.8} MeV for Ru+Ru; mu_B = 20.45_{-4.00}^{+3.80} MeV for Zr+Zr
- Delta mu_B (Zr minus Ru) = 0.156_{-0.012}^{+0.011} MeV from experiment
- Delta mu_Q (Zr minus Ru) = -0.161_{-0.012}^{+0.013} MeV from experiment
- Delta mu_S (Zr minus Ru) = 0.124_{-0.01}^{+0.01} MeV from experiment
- Experimental ratio Delta mu_B / Delta mu_Q = -0.968_{-0.012}^{+0.011}
- Experimental ratio Delta mu_S / Delta mu_Q = -0.769_{-0.026}^{+0.021}
- Experimental ratio Delta mu_B / Delta mu_S = 1.259_{-0.032}^{+0.028}
- Charge fraction difference between isobars: Y_Q = 44/96 approx 0.46 for Ru, Y_Q = 40/96 approx 0.42 for Zr, a difference of about 9%
- At mu_B=0, BQS gives d mu_B/d mu_Q approx -12.25, d mu_S/d mu_Q approx -3.33, d mu_B/d mu_S approx 3.67
- At mu_B=0, mCMF gives d mu_B/d mu_Q approx -16, d mu_S/d mu_Q approx -3, d mu_B/d mu_S approx 5.35

## Context

This work extends previous thermal model analyses of heavy-ion collisions (STAR 2005, Andronic 2017) that typically imposed simplifying assumptions such as mu_Q=0 or fixed charge fraction, leaving the interdependence among chemical potentials largely unexplored. It builds on the isobar collision method proposed by Lewis (2022) and the STAR measurement of baryon junction effects (STAR 2024) that motivated inclusion of baryon stopping via factor alpha=1/1.84. The results enable direct comparison between experimental extractions and first-principles lattice-QCD calculations (Borsanyi 2021, Noronha-Hostler 2019) and effective models (Kumar 2025), establishing a framework connecting heavy-ion phenomenology with neutron-star constraints and paving the way toward unified insights across the QCD phase diagram.
