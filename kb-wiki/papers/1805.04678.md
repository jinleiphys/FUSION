# Quark mean-field model for nuclear matter with or without bag

> We propose the new quark mean-field bag (QMFB) model by incorporating the bag confinement mechanism in the original quark mean-field model. Nuclear matter and neutron star properties are studied with the QMFB model. For the study of the bag effect, we newly fit 12 parameter sets by reproducing the empirical saturation properties of nuclear matter. Quark confinement is found to be mainly demonstrated by the bag after it is included in the model, instead of the confining potential. For nuclear matter, the bag decreases the binding energy and increases the symmetry energy. For neutron star, the bag affects significantly the radius $R$ of a $1.4M_\odot$ star, with the maximum mass only slightly modified. The bag also has a large suppression effect on the well-accepted $R$ vs $L$ dependence, with $L$ the symmetry energy slope at the saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that incorporating a bag confinement mechanism into the quark mean-field (QMF) model shifts the dominant confinement mechanism from the harmonic oscillator potential to the bag, and that this bag significantly suppresses the well-known correlation between neutron star radius and the symmetry energy slope L, a result that is conditional on the specific parameter sets fitted to empirical saturation properties.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid quark mean-field bag (QMFB) model by adding a spherical bag boundary condition to the existing QMF model, which previously confined constituent quarks (mass ~300 MeV) using only a harmonic oscillator potential. They solve the Dirac equation for quarks inside the bag, include center-of-mass, pionic, and gluonic corrections to the nucleon mass, and then couple the nucleons to sigma, omega, and rho mesons in a mean-field Lagrangian. Twelve new parameter sets (six with bag, six without) are fitted by reproducing empirical saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (density, binding energy, symmetry energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy slope, and effective mass), and the resulting equations of state are used to compute neutron star structure via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

*   Nucleon mass in free space: 939 MeV; nucleon radius in free space: 0.87 fm.
*   Bag constant B = 64.5136659 MeV/fm^3; zero-point energy parameter Z0 = 4.5445260.
*   For the QMFB model with L = 40 MeV and K = 240 MeV, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is approximately 12.2 km; for L = 80 MeV, it is approximately 12.7 km (a 3.9% increase).
*   For the QMF model without bag with L = 40 MeV and K = 240 MeV, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is approximately 11.7 km; for L = 80 MeV, it is approximately 13.7 km (a 14% increase).
*   The bag effect on the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is significant, approximately 0.7 km.
*   The maximum density at which the bag stiffens the equation of state is below 0.53 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the quark mean-field (QMF) model of Toki and collaborators (2007) and the quark meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) by merging their confinement mechanisms. It directly compares the new QMFB model to the original QMF model, showing that the bag suppresses the R vs L correlation previously established in studies such as Zhu (2018). The results enable future studies of finite nuclei and hypernuclei with the QMFB model and provide new parameter sets for neutron star equation of state calculations that are more consistent with radius constraints from the gravitational wave event GW170817.
