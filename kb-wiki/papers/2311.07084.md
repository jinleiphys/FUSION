# Single-Spin Asymmetry of Neutrons in Polarized pA Collisions

> Absorptive corrections, which are known to suppress proton-neutron transitions with a large fractional momentum z -> 1 in pp collisions, become dramatically strong on a nuclear target, and they push the partial cross sections of leading neutron production to the very periphery of the nucleus. The mechanism of the pion and axial vector a1-meson interference, which successfully explains the observed single-spin asymmetry in a polarized pp -> nX, is extended to the collisions of polarized protons with nuclei. When corrected for nuclear effects, it explains the observed single-spin azimuthal asymmetry of neutrons that is produced in inelastic events, which is where the nucleus violently breaks up. This single-spin asymmetry is found to be negative and nearly A-independent.

## Key claim

The single-spin asymmetry of neutrons produced in polarized proton-nucleus collisions is negative and nearly independent of the nuclear mass number A, provided one restricts to inelastic events where the nucleus breaks up violently; the mechanism of pion and axial-vector a1-meson interference, previously successful for pp collisions, can be extended to nuclear targets only after accounting for strong absorptive corrections that push leading-neutron production to the nuclear periphery.

## Method

The authors extend the Regge-based model of pion and a1-meson exchange, which describes the single-spin asymmetry in polarized pp -> nX, to collisions of polarized protons with nuclear targets. Absorptive corrections (Glauber-type multiple scattering) are applied to the proton-neutron transition amplitude, suppressing contributions from large fractional momentum z near 1 and confining the production to the nuclear surface. The resulting spin-dependent cross sections are computed for inelastic (dissociative) events, and the azimuthal asymmetry is evaluated as a function of nuclear mass number A.

## Key numbers

- The single-spin asymmetry for leading neutrons in polarized pA collisions is found to be negative and approximately A-independent, with a magnitude similar to that in pp collisions (the pp asymmetry is referenced as known from prior data).
- Absorptive corrections suppress the cross section for z -> 1 by a factor that grows with A, pushing the effective production radius to the nuclear periphery (quantified via the nuclear density profile, though no explicit numerical suppression factor is given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the pion-a1 interference mechanism that Kopeliovich and collaborators previously applied to polarized pp collisions (Kopeliovich et al., earlier works) to nuclear targets, where absorptive corrections are dramatically stronger. It explains the observed single-spin asymmetry in pA collisions measured at RHIC and other facilities, and it enables future studies of nuclear medium effects on spin-dependent hadron production, particularly the role of peripheral interactions in disentangling the isovector structure of the nucleon.
