# A new three-ranged Gogny interaction in touch with pion exchange

> We present a new Gogny-type finite-range effective interaction including a third Gaussian in the central term. Based on simple arguments valid for an arbitrary radial form factor, the three ranges are fixed from physical grounds, relating them to one-boson exchange interactions. Moreover, some parameters of the longest range are fixed through the G-matrix elements of the One Pion Exchange Potential. On top of giving a fairly good description of atomic nuclei properties, the resulting interaction leads to a remarkable improvement of some infinite matter properties that are relevant for astrophysical calculations.

## Key claim

The authors present a new Gogny-type effective interaction (D3G3) that adds a third Gaussian range to the central term, with the three ranges fixed by relating them to the Compton wavelengths of pi, rho, and sigma mesons from one-boson exchange, and with some parameters of the longest range fixed through G-matrix elements of the One Pion Exchange Potential; the resulting interaction improves infinite matter properties relevant for astrophysics while maintaining a fairly good description of atomic nuclei, though the global root mean square deviation for binding energies of approximately 2000 nuclei is about 7 MeV, comparable to D2 but far from the precision of D1M.

## Method

The authors fixed the three Gaussian ranges by equating the Fock/Hartree ratio for a Gaussian form factor to that of a Yukawa form factor for the pi, rho, and sigma meson masses at a Fermi momentum of 1.33 fm^-1, then allowed the ranges to vary within 10% during a chi-squared minimization. They fitted the interaction parameters using a protocol similar to Chabanat et al. (1997), constraining three of the four long-range parameters to OPEP values from Bertsch et al. (1977), and included constraints from the spin-isospin decomposition of the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state up to k_F = 1.33 fm^-1, plus binding energies of three double-magic nuclei (48Ca, 56Ni, 208Pb).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.165 fm^-3
- Energy per particle at saturation: E/A = -16.05 MeV
- Effective mass at saturation: m*/m = 0.68
- Nuclear incompressibility: K_infinity = 227 MeV
- Gaussian ranges (final fitted values): mu_1 = 0.470 fm, mu_2 = 0.749 fm, mu_3 = 1.967 fm
- Maximum neutron star mass: 2.04 solar masses
- Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R = 11.06 km
- Global RMS deviation for binding energies of ~2000 nuclei: sigma_RMS ~ 7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the standard two-range Gogny interaction (D1, D1M) by adding a third Gaussian range, motivated by the finding in Davesne et al. (2017) that two ranges do not provide enough degrees of freedom to reproduce infinite matter constraints from microscopic calculations. The approach builds on the M3Y interaction of Bertsch et al. (1977), which used three Yukawa terms with ranges fixed by exchanged boson masses, and on the work of Nakada (2003) who generalized such an interaction for nuclear structure. The resulting D3G3 interaction enables unified calculations of both finite nuclei and neutron star properties, addressing the need identified by Douchin and Haensel (2001), Chabanat et al. (2008), and Fantina et al. (2018) for a single interaction usable in both nuclear structure and astrophysical contexts.
