# Skyrmions up to Baryon Number 108

> The Skyrme crystal is built up of repeating units similar to the cubic Skyrmion of baryon number 4. Using this as guide, we construct new Skyrmion solutions in the massive pion case, with various baryon numbers up to 108. Most of our solutions resemble chunks of the Skyrme crystal. They are constructed using a multi-layer version of the rational map ansatz to create initial configurations, which are then relaxed numerically to find the energy minima. The coefficients of the rational maps are found by a geometrical construction related to the Skyrme crystal structure. We find some further solutions by numerical relaxation of clusters composed of baryon number 4 Skyrmions.

## Key claim

The authors construct new Skyrmion solutions in the massive pion case (pion mass parameter m=1) with baryon numbers up to 108, most of which resemble chunks of the Skyrme crystal, but the method does not yet produce solutions for every baryon number up to 300 as would be needed to model all nuclei.

## Method

The authors use a multi-layer rational map ansatz to create initial field configurations, where the coefficients of the rational maps are determined by a geometrical construction based on the locations of half-Skyrmions in the Skyrme crystal lattice. These initial configurations are then relaxed numerically to find energy minima. Some additional solutions are obtained by numerical relaxation of clusters composed of baryon number 4 Skyrmions glued together using the product ansatz.

## Key numbers

* Baryon number 32 Skyrmion constructed using a double-layer rational map with inner degree 4 map and outer degree 28 map, derived from a 4x4x4 cubic grid of half-Skyrmion locations.
* Baryon number 108 Skyrmion constructed using a triple-layer rational map with inner degree 4, middle degree 28, and outer degree 76 maps, derived from a 6x6x6 cubic grid.
* Corner cutting procedure generates Skyrmions with baryon numbers from 24 to 31 from the B=32 parent, and from 100 to 107 from the B=108 parent.
* The B=24 Skyrmion obtained by cutting all eight corners from B=32 consists of six B=4 cubes at the vertices of the dual octahedron rather than eight B=3 tetrahedra.
* Two new B=20 solutions have T_d and D_{2h} symmetry respectively, consisting of four B=4 Skyrmions with four B=1 Skyrmions between them.

## Context

This work extends the study of Skyrmions with massive pions (m=1) previously carried out for baryon numbers up to 32 by Battye, Sutcliffe, and collaborators (2011, 2010). The geometrical method for constructing rational maps from the Skyrme crystal structure was proposed by Manton and Sutcliffe (2004). The new solutions enable the study of larger nuclear clusters modeled as chunks of the Skyrme crystal, analogous to the alpha-particle model of nuclei, and the B=20 solutions resemble the 4-alpha-plus-4-neutron cluster structure recently suggested for oxygen-20 by Furutachi et al. (2011).
