# Dispersive analysis of the pion vector form factor without zeros

> We perform an updated analysis of $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-$ cross-section data using a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor. We show that the available data are compatible with the assumption that the form factor is free of complex zeros and that under this assumption the largest systematic uncertainty in a previous analysis can be eliminated. We investigate both a constrained Omnès representation as well as a hybrid phase-modulus representation and we quantify the discrepancies in the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon based on different $e^+e^-$ data sets. We find that the dispersive constraints exacerbate these discrepancies. Together with the assumption of the absence of zeros, the pion charge radius becomes a useful observable to discriminate between the different data sets. This provides an opportunity for future improved lattice-QCD determinations to probe the discrepancies independently of full computations of hadronic vacuum polarization. We also reevaluate the two-pion contribution to Euclidean windows and we observe that systematic discrepancies between the data sets persist even at very long distances.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the available $e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-$ cross-section data are compatible with the assumption that the pion vector form factor (VFF) has no complex zeros, and that imposing this assumption eliminates the largest systematic uncertainty in previous dispersive analyses, thereby stabilizing the fits and exacerbating discrepancies between different experimental data sets.

## Method
The authors perform dispersive fits of the pion VFF to high-statistics $e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-$ cross-section data from SND, CMD-2, BaBar, KLOE, BESIII, and CMD-3, using both a constrained Omnes representation and a hybrid phase-modulus representation. The absence of zeros is enforced either through an explicit zero-free parametrization of the inelastic factor or via a sum-rule penalty term, and the inelasticity parameter $\iota_1$ is treated with a Bayesian posterior to improve systematic uncertainty estimates.

## Key numbers
- The combined fit (excluding SND20 and CMD-3) yields $a_\mu^{\pi\pi}|_{\leq1\text{GeV}}^\text{comb} = 495.7(1.4)_{\text{stat}}(0.8)_{\text{syst}}(3.3)_{\text{BK}}\times10^{-10} = 495.7(3.7)\times10^{-10}$.
- The constrained fit to CMD-3 yields $a_\mu^{\pi\pi}|_{\leq1\text{GeV}}^\text{CMD-3} = 513.0(0.9)_{\text{stat}}(0.8)_{\text{syst}}\times10^{-10} = 513.0(1.2)\times10^{-10}$.
- The discrepancy between KLOE and CMD-3 reaches $9\sigma$ in the contribution to $a_\mu$, and almost $5\sigma$ in the pion charge radius.
- The omega width is taken as $\Gamma_\omega = 8.71(3)\text{ MeV}$ from an analysis of the three-pion channel (Hoferichter 2023).
- The phi meson mass and width are $M_\phi = 1019.21(2)\text{ MeV}$ and $\Gamma_\phi = 4.249(13)\text{ MeV}$.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive analyses of Colangelo (2018, 2020, 2022) and Stoffer (2023) by identifying that complex zeros in the VFF, which appear when using higher-order conformal polynomials, are the source of the dominant systematic uncertainty. The authors build on arguments by Leutwyler (2002), Ananthanarayan (2011), and Ruiz Arriola (2024) that the VFF should be zero-free, and show that imposing this condition stabilizes fits and sharpens tensions between CMD-3 and other experiments. The results enable future lattice-QCD determinations of the pion charge radius to independently probe these discrepancies without requiring full computations of hadronic vacuum polarization.
