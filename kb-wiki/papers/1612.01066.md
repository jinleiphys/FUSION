# Systematic study of symmetric cumulants at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV in Au+Au collision using transport approach

> Measurement of 4-particle symmetric cumulants has been considered to be a good tool to study the correlations between amplitudes of different orders of anisotropic flow harmonics in the heavy-ion collision. These new observables not yet been measured at RHIC. Using A Multi-Phase Transport model, a set of predictions for the centrality dependence of the normalized 4-particle symmetric cumulants in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV has been given. In addition, the effects of shear viscosity and hadronic rescattering on the magnitude of symmetric cumulants are discussed using AMPT model at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. It is shown that $sc(2,3)$ is found to be more sensitive to hadronic rescattering, whereas $sc(2,4)$ is more sensitive to the shear viscosity. Rapidity dependence of symmetric cumulants is also shown. A relation between symmetric cumulant and event plane correlation is investigated using AMPT model.

## Key claim

Using the AMPT transport model, the paper provides predictions for normalized 4-particle symmetric cumulants in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV, noting that these observables have not yet been measured at RHIC. The study shows that \(sc(2,3)\) is more sensitive to hadronic rescattering, whereas \(sc(2,4)\) is more sensitive to shear viscosity, but these conclusions are model-dependent and based on the specific AMPT configurations used.

## Method

The study uses the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model in both its default (hadronic) and string melting (partonic) configurations to generate approximately 5 million Au+Au events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV. The normalized symmetric cumulants \(sc(n,m)\) are calculated using multi-particle cumulant methods with charged hadrons (\(\pi\), \(K\), \(p\)) within \(0.2 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/c and \(|\eta| < 1.0\), applying a pseudorapidity gap of \(|\Delta\eta| > 1.0\) for 2-particle correlations to suppress non-flow biases. The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (\(\eta_s/s\)) is varied by adjusting the parton-parton scattering cross-section, and the hadronic cascade termination time is varied from 0.6 to 30 fm/c to study the effect of hadronic rescattering.

## Key numbers

- The AMPT model with parton-parton cross-sections between 1.5 mb and 3 mb describes the transverse momentum dependence of \(v_2\), \(v_3\), and \(v_4\) measured by PHENIX, with event plane resolutions comparable within 10%.
- Three values of shear viscosity to entropy density ratio are studied: \(\eta_s/s = 0.08\), 0.18, and 0.35, with \(\alpha_s = 0.47\) fixed.
- The hadronic cascade termination time is varied from 0.6 fm/c to 30 fm/c to study hadronic rescattering effects.
- The ratio between \(sc(2,4)\) calculated via the direct 4-particle cumulant method and via the event-plane correlation relation is approximately 20% in AMPT-SM and 40% in AMPT-Def.

## Context

This work extends the study of symmetric cumulants, previously measured by the ALICE collaboration at LHC energies (ALICE, 2015), to RHIC energies using a transport model approach. It provides baseline predictions for upcoming RHIC measurements and tests the validity of a relation between symmetric cumulants and event-plane correlations (derived by Jaki et al., 2015) beyond hydrodynamic frameworks. The study enables future comparisons between experimental data at RHIC and the AMPT model, which has been successful in describing flow harmonics at these energies.
