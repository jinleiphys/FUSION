# Multipole responses in fissioning nuclei and their uncertainties

> Electromagnetic multipole responses are key inputs to model the structure, decay and reaction of atomic nuclei. With the introduction of the finite amplitude method (FAM), large-scale calculations of the nuclear linear response in heavy deformed nuclei have become possible. This work provides a detailed study of multipole responses in actinide nuclei with Skyrme energy density functionals. We quantify both systematic and statistical uncertainties induced by the functional parameterization in FAM calculations. We also extend the FAM formalism to perform blocking calculations with the equal filling approximation for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, and analyze the impact of blocking configurations on the response. By examining the entire plutonium isotopic chain from the proton to the neutron dripline, we find a large variability of the response with the neutron number and study how it correlates with the deformation of the nuclear ground state.

# Multipole responses in fissioning nuclei and their uncertainties

## Key claim
The finite amplitude method (FAM) can be extended to odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei using the equal filling approximation, and the resulting multipole responses in actinide nuclei show large variability with neutron number that correlates with ground-state deformation; however, the systematic uncertainties from different Skyrme energy density functionals are often larger than the statistical uncertainties from parameter variations within a single functional, particularly for non-collective features like the shoulder structure in $^{208}$Pb and the $|K|=1$ peaks in $^{240}$Pu.

## Method
The authors developed the code *gfam* to perform axially-deformed FAM calculations of electromagnetic multipole responses using Skyrme energy density functionals, building on the charge-changing FAM code *pnfam*. They extended the FAM formalism to treat odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei via the equal filling approximation (EFA), treating the EFA-HFB state as a special case of finite-temperature HFB with quasiparticle occupancies of 1/2 for the blocked orbitals. Static HFB solutions were obtained with the *hfbtho* code using a deformed harmonic-oscillator basis up to $N_0 = 20$ shells, and the FAM equations were solved iteratively over a frequency grid with 0.1 MeV spacing and a standard smearing width of $\Gamma = 1$ MeV (or 2 MeV for comparison with data).

## Key numbers
- GDR energy of $^{90}$Zr: experimental value 16.8 MeV; systematic mean from five functionals 16.4 $\pm$ 0.8 MeV; statistical mean from HFB1 posterior samples 17.0 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV.
- GDR energy of $^{208}$Pb: experimental value 13.4 MeV; systematic mean 12.6 $\pm$ 0.3 MeV; statistical mean 12.5 $\pm$ 0.3 MeV.
- ISGQR energy of $^{208}$Pb: experimental value 10.9 MeV; systematic mean 12.0 $\pm$ 0.9 MeV; statistical mean 12.0 $\pm$ 0.1 MeV.
- IVGQR energy of $^{208}$Pb: experimental value 22.7 MeV; systematic mean 22.3 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV; statistical mean 21.9 $\pm$ 0.4 MeV.
- Systematic uncertainty of the HFB energy of $^{240}$Pu: 4.6 MeV; statistical uncertainty: 12.3 MeV.
- Pairing cutoff energy $E_{\rm cut} = 60$ MeV used in the particle-particle channel.
- FAM calculations over 500 $\omega$ points take less than one hour on a node with 56 CPU cores.

## Context
This work extends previous FAM studies of multipole responses in heavy deformed nuclei (Kortelainen 2015, Oishi 2016) by providing a systematic uncertainty analysis across five Skyrme functionals (SLy4, SLy5, SkM*, SkI3, HFB1) and by implementing the EFA for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, building on the *pnfam* code used for beta-decay calculations (Mustonen 2014, Shafer 2016, Ney 2020). The results enable future large-scale calculations of gamma-strength functions across the entire nuclear mass table, and the uncertainty quantification framework can be applied to constrain nuclear reaction models used in nucleosynthesis and fission technology applications.
