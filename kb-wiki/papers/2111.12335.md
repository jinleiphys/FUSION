# Detecting Entrainment in Fermi-Bose Mixtures

> We propose an experimental protocol to directly detect the Andreev-Bashkin effect (entrainment) in the bulk mixture of a bosonic and fermionic superfluid using a ring geometry. Our protocol involves the interferometric detection of the entrainment-induced phase gradient across a superfluid due to the flow of another in which it is immersed. The choice of ring geometry eliminates variations in the stronger mean-field interaction which can thwart the detection of entrainment in other geometries. A significant enhancement of the entrainment phase shift signal is possible, if the dimer-boson scattering length turns out to be large, which can be measured by tuning the interaction to the limit of miscibility of the two superfluids. With suggested improvements and careful design implementation, one may achieve $\approx 67$% shift in the interferometer fringes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an experimental protocol to directly detect the Andreev-Bashkin effect (entrainment) in a bulk mixture of bosonic and fermionic superfluids using a ring geometry, claiming that with careful design one may achieve approximately 67% shift in interferometer fringes, but this enhancement depends on the dimer-boson scattering length being large and on tuning interactions to the limit of miscibility.

## Method

The authors model a mixture of superfluid ⁶Li (fermionic) and ¹⁷⁴Yb (bosonic) using a coupled Gross-Pitaevskii-like formalism that includes an entrainment term coupling the phase gradients of the two superfluids. They propose generating circular flow in the fermionic superfluid via optical stirring in a ring trap, inserting a species-selective barrier to prevent bosonic flow, then measuring the entrainment-induced phase gradient in the stationary bosons through interferometric detection after expansion.

## Key numbers

- The calculated dipole oscillation frequency shift due to entrainment in a harmonic trap is approximately 0.02%, two orders of magnitude smaller than the observed mean-field shift
- The proposed protocol may achieve approximately 67% shift in the interferometer fringes with suggested improvements
- The dimer-boson scattering length is estimated as a_Db approximately 3.87 a_fb from mean-field effects for the mass ratio m_Yb/m_Li approximately 29
- The dipole oscillation frequency of the Yb BEC in the presence of the Li cloud was measured as 2π × 381.3(4) Hz, reproduced in simulations as 2π × 381.4(9) Hz
- The measured and calculated decay constants (ω_d τ) are 250 and 210 respectively

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions of the Andreev-Bashkin effect (Andreev and Bashkin, 1975) to a cold-atom system of ⁶Li and ¹⁷⁴Yb, building on prior proposals by Shevchenko (2007) for entrainment detection and by Nespolo (2017) for dimensional reduction enhancement. The protocol enables direct observation of bulk entrainment in 3D, which has not been achieved in ⁴He-³He mixtures (Pickett, 2002) due to phase separation constraints, and could inform models of neutron star glitch dynamics where entrainment between superfluid neutrons and superconducting protons plays a significant role.
