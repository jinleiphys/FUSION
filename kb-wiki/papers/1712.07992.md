# Dynamically generated hadronic states in the $\bar{K}N$ and $ηN$ coupled-channels interactions

> We compare and review the theoretical predictions for the $\bar{K}N$ and $ηN$ elastic amplitudes where sizable variations are found among the considered approaches, especially at subthreshold energies relevant for studies of kaonic atoms and meson-nuclear quasi-bound states. Conditions for an appearance of dynamically generated states in meson-baryon multi-channel interactions are established and discussed for the $\bar{K}N$ and $ηN$ systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that current chiral coupled-channels models, while all reproducing the same near-threshold experimental data for the $\bar{K}N$ and $\eta N$ systems, produce widely varying predictions for the elastic amplitudes at subthreshold energies, with the variations being much larger than the uncertainty bounds derived from kaonic hydrogen data alone.

## Method

The author compares several up-to-date chiral coupled-channels approaches (Kyoto-Munich, Prague, Murcia, and Bonn models for the $\bar{K}N$ system; CS, KSW, M2, IOV, and GW models for the $\eta N$ system) that solve Bethe-Salpeter or Lippmann-Schwinger equations with potentials derived from chiral Lagrangians. The origin of dynamically generated resonances is traced by switching off inter-channel couplings to reach the zero coupling limit (ZCL) and following the movement of poles as couplings are gradually restored to their physical values.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance lies about 30 MeV below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold
- The $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance is found about 50 MeV above the $\eta N$ elastic channel threshold
- The meson-baryon channels in the $S=-1$ sector have thresholds spread from 1250 to 1810 MeV
- For the $\eta N$ scattering length, the real part ranges from $\Re a_{\eta N} = 0.26$ fm up to $0.96$ fm across models, while the imaginary part is almost model independent at $\Im a_{\eta N} \approx 0.20 - 0.26$ fm
- A sufficiently large value of $\Re a_{\eta N} \gsim 0.7$ fm is a prerequisite for forming quasi-bound $\eta$-nuclear states as light as $\eta ^{4}$He
- The CS model fit to low energy $S_{11}$ and $S_{31}$ partial waves achieved $\chi^{2}/dof \approx 1.5$

## Context

This work extends the comparative analysis of Cieply (2016) for the $\bar{K}N$ system and Cieply (2013) for the $\eta N$ system, systematically examining the pole structure of dynamically generated resonances across multiple chiral approaches. The findings are relevant for studies of kaonic atoms and the possible formation of $\bar{K}$-nuclear and $\eta$-nuclear quasi-bound states, as previously investigated by Cieply (2011), Cieply (2013), Friedman (2016), and Hrtankova (2017). The paper shows that only the Kyoto-Munich and Prague models produce $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes consistent with kaonic atom data, while the Murcia and Bonn approaches (characterized by large NLO contributions) fail this in-medium test.
