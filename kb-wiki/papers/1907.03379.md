# Theory of elastic neutrino-electron scattering

> Theoretical predictions for elastic neutrino-electron scattering have no hadronic or nuclear uncertainties at leading order making this process an important tool for normalizing neutrino flux. However, the process is subject to large radiative corrections that differ according to experimental conditions. In this paper, we collect new and existing results for total and differential cross sections accompanied by radiation of one photon, $νe \to νe (γ)$. We perform calculations within the Fermi effective theory and provide analytic expressions for the electron energy spectrum and for the total electromagnetic energy spectrum as well as for double- and triple-differential cross sections with respect to electron energy, electron angle, photon energy, and photon angle. We discuss illustrative applications to accelerator-based neutrino experiments and provide the most precise up-to-date values of neutrino-electron scattering cross sections. We present an analysis of theoretical error, which is dominated by the $\sim 0.2 - 0.4\%$ uncertainty of the hadronic correction. We also discuss how searches for new physics can be affected by radiative corrections.

## Key claim

Radiative corrections to elastic neutrino-electron scattering are large and experiment-dependent, with the dominant theoretical uncertainty coming from hadronic loop corrections at the 0.2–0.4% level, rather than from the process itself which has no hadronic or nuclear uncertainties at leading order.

## Method

The authors performed calculations within the Fermi effective theory of four-fermion interactions, evaluating both virtual QED corrections (vertex corrections and closed fermion loops) and real photon radiation for the process νe → νe(γ). They used the MS renormalization scheme with n_f=4 active quarks at renormalization scale μ = 2 GeV, and exploited the analytical phase-space integration technique of Ram (1967) to obtain analytic expressions for differential cross sections with respect to electron energy, electron angle, photon energy, and photon angle.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic correction uncertainty: ∼0.2–0.4% (dominates the theoretical error budget)
- Effective couplings at μ = 2 GeV (in units 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²): c_L^{ν_ℓ ℓ'} (ℓ=ℓ') = 2.39818(33), c_L^{ν_ℓ ℓ'} (ℓ≠ℓ') = -0.90084(32), c_R = 0.76911(60)
- Threshold energy for muon production in neutrino-electron scattering: ω ≥ 10.9 GeV (for m' = m_μ)
- Threshold energy for tau production: ω ≥ 3089 GeV (for m' = m_τ)

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Lee (1964) who first calculated radiative corrections of order α with only soft-photon bremsstrahlung, and Ram (1967) who developed analytical phase-space integration for hard-photon bremsstrahlung. It reproduces and generalizes results from Sarantakos (1982), Bahcall (1995), and Passera (2000), while providing a new treatment of hadronic loop diagrams that impacts other neutral current neutrino processes such as coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering (Akimov 2017). The results enable more precise flux normalization for accelerator-based neutrino experiments including DUNE, MINERvA, NOvA, and T2K, and provide analytic expressions retaining full electron mass dependence useful for future reactor and solar neutrino experiments.
