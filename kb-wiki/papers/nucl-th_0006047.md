# Enhanced Event-by-Event Fluctuations in Pion Multiplicity as a Signal of Disoriented Chiral Condensates at RHIC

> The factorial moments of the pion multiplicity distributions are calculated with HIJING and UrQMD and found to be independent of the pT range included, in contrast to recent simulations with the linear sigma model which leads to large enhancements for pions with transverse kinetic energies below 200 MeV. This supports the use of the ratio of the factorial moments of low and high pT pions as a signal of ``new'' physics at low momentum scales, such as the formation of disoriented chiral condensates.

## Key claim

The ratio of reduced factorial moments of soft (transverse kinetic energy below 200 MeV) to hard pions may serve as a robust signal of disoriented chiral condensate formation at RHIC, but the linear sigma model results are only qualitative because the semi-classical treatment underestimates the enhancement (by a factor of two or more) and applies only to baryon-free systems with schematic Bjorken rod geometries.

## Method

The authors calculated reduced factorial moments of pion multiplicity distributions using the HIJING and UrQMD event generators for central Au+Au collisions (impact parameter b ≤ 3 fm) at 100 GeV per nucleon beam energy. They compared these results to earlier linear sigma model simulations of idealized Bjorken rods with a bulk temperature of 240 MeV and radius 6-10 fm, where the classical field equation was solved numerically on a cartesian lattice starting from τ₀ = 1 fm/c. The factorial moments were computed separately for soft pions (transverse momentum below 200 MeV/c) and hard pions, and the ratio between soft and hard reduced factorial moments was examined.

## Key numbers

- Soft pions (p_T ≤ 200 MeV/c) constitute a relatively small part of total pions; the calculated mean p_T of midrapidity pions ranges from 330 MeV (UrQMD) to 370 MeV (HIJING)
- The linear sigma model simulations used a bulk temperature T₀ of 240 MeV and rod radius R₀ of 6-10 fm, starting from proper time τ₀ = 1 fm/c
- HIJING and UrQMD both produce reduced factorial moments F_m that increase gently with order m, with no distinction between soft and hard pions, while the linear sigma model yields F_m close to unity for hard pions but rapidly increasing for soft pions
- The semi-classical treatment of the linear sigma model is expected to underestimate the enhancement by a factor of two or more

## Context

This work extends simulations by Randrup (1999) using the linear sigma model that suggested enhanced fluctuations in soft pion multiplicity from disoriented chiral condensates, and contrasts these with predictions from the standard event generators HIJING and UrQMD. The paper enables experimental searches for DCC formation at RHIC as a Year-One task, noting that early DCC experiments at CERN and Fermilab were unable to discern a signal from the neutral pion fraction distribution. The authors caution that the observed intermittency signal associated with very small rapidity bins (Δy ≪ 1) from KLM cannot be reproduced satisfactorily by the models employed, though for rapidity bins of width Δy ≈ 1 the data can be reproduced within experimental error bars.
