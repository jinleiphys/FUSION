# Annihilating States in Close-Coupling Method for Collisions between Hadronic and Ordinary Atoms

> Traditional close-coupling methods suppose an expansion of the total wave function in terms of inner stationary states of colliding subsystems. In the case of hadronic atoms, a similar expansion has to involve, \emph{inter alia}, low angular momentum states (ns, np) with large annihilation or nuclear absorbtion widths. The life times of these states are small as compared with the collision time and mean time between subsequent collisions, therefore the close-coupling approach has to be modified for the similar problems. In this paper we propose a generalization of the close-coupling method with annihilating states included in the basis. The correct asymptotic behaviour of the wave function in the annihilating channels suppose that the annihilating states can not be presented in the incoming channels whereas the corresponding components of the wave function of relative motion in outgoing channels have to damp out at large distances. The S-matrix of the transitions in the subspace of all other (non-annihilating) states is not unitary, because the hamiltonian of the problem is non-hermitian. The unitary defect gives the cross section of induced annihilation. The scheme of the numerical solving of the generalized close-coupling equations is proposed. It includes the calculations of two types of solution, from the origin to an intermediate sewing point R=a and from a large R to the point a.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of the close-coupling method for collisions involving hadronic atoms, where short-lived (annihilating) states with large annihilation or nuclear absorption widths must be included in the basis, but only under the condition that these states cannot appear in incoming channels and their wave function components must damp out at large distances.

## Method

The authors divide the channel space into two subspaces: α (stationary states with negligible widths) and β (annihilating states with ν_nl ≳ 1). They formulate modified boundary conditions where annihilating channels have zero incoming components and exponentially damped outgoing waves at large R. The numerical solution involves computing two types of solutions: regular solutions from the origin to an intermediate sewing point R=a, and solutions from large R inward to the same point, then sewing them together to obtain the S-matrix for transitions in the non-annihilating subspace.

## Key numbers

- The life times of annihilating states are given by τ_nl = ħ/Γ_nl, where Γ_nl is the annihilation or nuclear absorption width
- The parameter ν_nl = τ_coll/τ_nl ∼ Γ_nl R_0/ħv gives a criterion for state stationarity during collisions; states with ν_nl ≳ 1 are considered annihilating
- For (p̄p)_nl + H collisions, the semiclassical approximation holds for ka ≫ 1, or E ≳ 1 eV
- The atomic unit of energy E_a = 27.2 eV is used as an upper bound for the collision energy (E ≪ E_a) in the simplified model
- The paper references a separate application to antiprotonic helium ion (p̄He²⁺)* collisions with ordinary He atoms at very low energy (∼ 10 Kelvin)

## Context

This work extends the standard close-coupling method, which assumes stationary basis states with lifetimes much longer than collision times, to the case of hadronic atoms where low angular momentum states (ns, np) violate this condition. It corrects an earlier attempt by other authors (ref3 in the paper) who artificially turned off absorption widths at a distance R₀ = 5a₀, which the present authors note contradicts physical reality. The approach enables quantum-mechanical calculations of induced annihilation cross sections in collisions between hadronic atoms and ordinary atoms, with a specific application to antiprotonic helium ion collisions with helium atoms at cryogenic temperatures (∼10 K) presented in a separate paper (ref4).
