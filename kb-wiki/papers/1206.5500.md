# Light scalars in semi-leptonic decays of heavy quarkonia

> We study the mechanism of production of the light scalar mesons in the $D_s^+\toπ^+π^- e^+ν$ decays: $D_s^+\to s\bar s e^+ν\to [σ(600)+f_0(980)] e^+ν\toπ^+π^- e^+ν$, and compare it with the mechanism of production of the light pseudoscalar mesons in the $D_s^+\to (η/η') e^+ν$ decays: $D_s^+\to s\bar s e^+ν\to (η/η')e^+ν$. We show that the $s\bar s\toσ(600)$ transition is negligibly small in comparison with the $s\bar s\to f_0(980)$ one. As for the the $f_0(980)$ meson, the intensity of the $s\bar s\to f_0(980)$ transition makes near thirty percent from the intensity of the $s\bar s\toη_s$ ($η_s=s\bar s$) transition. So, the $D_s^+\toπ^+π^- e^+ν$ decay supports the previous conclusions about a dominant role of the four-quark components in the $σ(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $s\bar s \to \sigma(600)$ transition is negligibly small compared to the $s\bar s \to f_0(980)$ transition, and that the intensity of the $s\bar s \to f_0(980)$ transition is about thirty percent of the intensity of the $s\bar s \to \eta_s$ transition. This supports the conclusion that four-quark components dominate the $\sigma(600)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons, but the result is conditional on the specific model (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type) and the parameters taken from fits to $\pi\pi$ scattering and $\phi$ radiative decay data.

## Method

The authors study the semileptonic decay $D_s^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^- e^+ \nu$ via the intermediate process $D_s^+ \to s\bar s e^+ \nu \to [\sigma(600) + f_0(980)] e^+ \nu \to \pi^+ \pi^- e^+ \nu$, and compare it with the decays $D_s^+ \to (\eta/\eta') e^+ \nu$. They use a vector dominance model for the form factors and a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio type model to relate coupling constants, fitting the ratio $f_+^{\sigma}(0)/f_+^{f_0}(0)$ to the CLEO data (44 events) on the $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio $Br(D_s^+ \to f_0 e^+ \nu \to \pi^+ \pi^- e^+ \nu) = 0.17\%$.
- The ratio $(F_\sigma g_{s\bar s\sigma})/(F_{f_0} g_{s\bar s f_0}) = 0.039$ for the best fit (Fit 1), giving $(f_+^{\sigma}(0)/f_+^{f_0}(0))^2 \leq 0.003$, showing the $\sigma(600)$ production intensity is much less than that of $f_0(980)$.
- The ratio $g^2_{s\bar s f_0}/g^2_{s\bar s\eta_s} \approx 0.3$ (ranging from 0.24 to 0.32 depending on the $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle $\theta_P$), indicating the $s\bar s$ component in $f_0(980)$ is about 30%.
- The ratio $g_{f_0 \pi^+ \pi^-}^2 / g_{f_0 K^+ K^-}^2 = 0.154$ (from Fit 1), showing suppression of the $f_0(980)$ coupling to $\pi\pi$.
- The ratio $g_{\sigma K^+ K^-}^2 / g_{\sigma \pi^+ \pi^-}^2 = 0.04$ (from Fit 1), showing decoupling of $\sigma(600)$ from $K\bar K$ states.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light scalar mesons in $\phi$ radiative decays (Achasov 1989, 2003), photon-photon collisions (Achasov and Shestakov), and $\pi\pi$ scattering (Achasov and Kiselev 2004), which indicated four-quark structures for $\sigma(600)$, $f_0(980)$, and $a_0(980)$. It directly analyzes CLEO data (2009) on $D_s^+ \to \pi^+ \pi^- e^+ \nu$ decays, providing new evidence for the decoupling of $\sigma(600)$ from the $s\bar s$ pair and supporting the four-quark interpretation. The results enable future experimental searches for $D^0 \to a_0^-(980) e^+ \nu$ and $D^+ \to a_0^0(980) e^+ \nu$ decays to probe the quark content of the $a_0(980)$ meson.
