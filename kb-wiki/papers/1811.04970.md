# Second-Order Leptonic Radiative Corrections for Lepton-Proton Scattering

> The interpretation of high-precision lepton-nucleon scattering experiments requires the knowledge of higher-order radiative corrections. We present a calculation of the cross section for unpolarized lepton-proton scattering including leptonic radiative corrections up to second order, including one- and two-loop corrections, radiation of one and two photons and one-loop corrections for one-photon radiation. Numerical results are given for the planned P2 experiment at the MESA facility in Mainz, and some results are also discussed for Qweak and the suggested MUSE experiment.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of second-order leptonic radiative corrections for unpolarized lepton-proton scattering, but explicitly restricts itself to purely leptonic corrections, excluding two- or three-photon exchange (box graphs) and radiation from the proton, which are model-dependent and require additional assumptions.

## Method

The authors re-derive first-order radiative corrections and then compute second-order corrections including two-loop vertex diagrams, two-photon bremsstrahlung, and one-loop corrections to one-photon radiation. They use the phase-space slicing method with a soft-photon cut-off parameter Δ to separate infrared-divergent soft-photon contributions from hard-photon contributions, and implement the calculations in a Monte Carlo simulation program using the Cuba integration package and LoopTools for scalar integrals.

## Key numbers

- The soft-photon cut-off parameter Δ is chosen small, below the detection threshold for photon observation in the detector.
- The proton form factors are implemented using a simple dipole parametrization with Λ = 0.71 GeV², where G_E = (1 + Q²/Λ²)⁻² and G_M = κ_p G_E with κ_p = 2.7928.
- The hadronic vacuum polarization contribution is shown to be non-negligible for Q² values above a few times 10⁻² GeV².
- The two-loop correction for electron scattering, valid for Q²_ℓ ≫ m²_ℓ, is extracted from Hill (2016) and given by δ⁽²⁾_{2-loop} = (F₁^{ℓ(1)})² + 2 F₁^{ℓ(2)}.

## Context

This work extends the classical radiative correction framework of Mo and Tsai (1968) and subsequent improvements by de Calan (1990), Maximon (2000), and Akushevich (2015) by including second-order leptonic effects beyond the soft-photon and peaking approximations. The calculation is specifically tailored for the planned P2 experiment at the MESA facility in Mainz, with additional results for Qweak and the proposed MUSE experiment, and enables the required theoretical precision for interpreting high-luminosity lepton-proton scattering measurements.
