# Axial Anomaly, entanglement and polarization

> The (pion) decays controlled by axial anomaly imply the specific entanglement between photons having also the counterparts for classical electromagnetic waves. This is also a specific case of Eisnstein-Podolsky-Rosen-Bohm-Aharonov effect. The absence of causality and non-locality in (angular) momentum conservation is manifested, being especially clear for the generalization to the case of time rather than space separation corresponds to the polarization of dileptons described by time-like pion transition formfactors which may be studied experimentally. The similar decays in external magnetic field manifest the interplay with vacuum conductivity in external magnetic field and longitudinal polarization of vector mesons observed in heavy-ion collisions.

# Axial Anomaly, entanglement and polarization

## Key claim
The axial anomaly in pion decays implies a specific entanglement between photons that has classical electromagnetic wave counterparts, and this entanglement manifests the absence of causality and non-locality in angular momentum conservation, especially when generalized to time-separated (rather than space-separated) measurements via crossing-related scattering processes.

## Method
The author analyzes the VVP (vector-vector-pseudoscalar) correlator amplitude for anomalous pion decay to two photons, expressing the squared amplitude in terms of photon density matrices and Stokes parameters. The analysis is extended to the crossing-related t-channel process of photon scattering on a space-like pion, and to the decay of a pseudoscalar meson to a lepton pair in an external magnetic field, connecting the resulting longitudinal polarization to lattice QCD results on vacuum conductivity.

## Key numbers
- The squared decay amplitude for pion to two photons is proportional to N(1 - |(e1* e2)|^2), where e1, e2 are polarization vectors.
- The normalized density matrix for two-photon scattering is given by (1/2)(δ_ij δ_kl - δ_il δ_kj).
- Lambda polarization in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 5 GeV was estimated at the order of 1% using the kinetic Quark-Gluon String Model.
- The longitudinal conductivity in external magnetic field was confirmed by lattice calculations and found to grow with magnetic field strength.

## Context
This work extends the connection between axial anomaly (Adler 1969, Bell and Jackiw 1969) and the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen-Bohm-Aharonov effect, noting that J.S. Bell contributed to both areas. The analysis of vector meson tensor polarization builds on the fusion model of Efremov and Teryaev (1982) and relates to recent ALICE data on spin alignment (Acharya et al. 2019). The magnetic field effects connect to lattice studies of vacuum conductivity by Buividovich et al. (2010) and Astrakhantsev et al. (2019), and to the dynamical Chiral Magnetic Effect. The work enables experimental study of time-like pion transition form factors and polarization phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.
