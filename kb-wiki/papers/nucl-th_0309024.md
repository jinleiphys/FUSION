# Rescattering contributions to final state interactions in (e,e'p) reactions

> A semiclassical model is employed to study the effects of rescattering on (e,e'p) cross sections. We consider a two-step process with the propagation of an intermediate nucleon and use Glauber theory to account for the effects of N--N scattering. This calculation has relevance for the analysis of data at high missing energies. Of particular interest is the E97-006 experiment done at JLab. It is found that rescattering is strongly reduced in parallel kinematics and that the excitation of nucleon resonances is likely to give important contributions to the final-state interactions in the correlated region.

## Key claim

Rescattering contributions to final-state interactions in (e,e'p) reactions are strongly reduced in parallel kinematics, but the excitation of nucleon resonances (particularly the Delta resonance) likely gives important contributions to final-state interactions in the correlated region at high missing energies, which are not yet included in the semiclassical model.

## Method

The authors employed a semiclassical model that considers a two-step process: a direct knockout of a nucleon via the electromagnetic current, followed by propagation of an intermediate nucleon and a subsequent nucleon-nucleon scattering event. Glauber theory was used to account for the effects of N-N scattering, with in-medium total cross sections computed up to 300 MeV and extended above that energy to incorporate pion emission effects. The calculation was applied to 12C(e,e'p) reactions using both parallel kinematics (angle between momentum transfer and final proton approximately 5 degrees) and perpendicular kinematics (approximately 30 degrees).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear transparency T = 0.62 for 12C with an outgoing proton energy E_f approximately 1.8 GeV
- For parallel kinematics: angle between momentum transfer and final proton theta_qf approximately 5 degrees, final proton energy E_f approximately 1.6 GeV, four-momentum transfer Q^2 approximately 0.40 GeV^2
- For perpendicular kinematics: theta_qf approximately 30 degrees, E_f approximately 1 GeV, Q^2 approximately 0.40 GeV^2
- At missing energies E_m greater than 250 MeV, experimental results exceed calculated direct plus rescattering contributions by about an order of magnitude
- Short-range correlations remove about 10-15% of spectral strength from shell model orbitals to high missing energies and momenta

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical model of rescattering developed by V. Pandharipande and collaborators (VjPi) and applied by M. Vanhalst and collaborators (Marcel) at lower missing energies, now targeting the kinematics relevant for studying short-range correlations. The calculation is directly relevant to the E97-006 experiment at Jefferson Lab (Rohe, 2003). The results confirm the suggestion in the E97-006 proposal that parallel kinematics reduce rescattering contributions, enabling cleaner extraction of short-range correlation strength from experimental data, though the model must be extended to include Delta resonance excitation channels to fully describe the data at high missing energies.
