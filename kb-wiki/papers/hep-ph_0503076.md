# Comment on soft-pion emission in DVCS

> The soft-pion theorem for pion production in deeply virtual Compton scattering, derived by Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaegen, is shown to be consistent with chiral perturbation theory. Chiral symmetry requires that the nonsinglet operators corresponding to spin-independent and spin-dependent parton distributions have the same anomalous dimensions in cases where those operators are related by chiral transformations. In chiral perturbation theory, their scale-dependences can thus be absorbed in the coefficents of the corresponding effective operators, without affecting their chiral structures.

## Key claim

The soft-pion theorem for pion production in deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS), derived by Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaegen, is consistent with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), contrary to criticisms by Chen and Savage (2005), provided one considers only tree-level amplitudes at order \(m_\pi^0\) where pion-loop complications do not arise.

## Method

The author examines the twist-2 nonsinglet operators corresponding to moments of spin-independent and spin-dependent quark distributions, and shows that their chiral structures in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) are related by chiral transformations. By constructing right- and left-handed quark operators and using the fact that their anomalous dimensions are identical (proved to second order in perturbative QCD by Mertig and van Neerven (1996) and Vogelsang (1996)), the scale dependences can be absorbed into coefficients of effective operators without altering the chiral structure, thereby reconciling the soft-pion theorem with ChPT.

## Key numbers

- The soft-pion theorem is shown to be valid for tree-level amplitudes at order \(m_\pi^0\), where the difference between the divergence of the axial current and the pion field is of order \(m_\pi^2\) and thus irrelevant.
- The equality of anomalous dimensions for the operators \(\theta^{(n)a}_{V,\mu_1\dots\mu_n}\) and \(\theta^{(n)a}_{A,\mu_1\dots\mu_n}\) (or equivalently for the parton distributions \(q^{-}(x)\) and \(\Delta q^{+}(x)\)) has been proved to second order in perturbative QCD.

## Context

This comment addresses a debate between Guichon, Mosse and Vanderhaegen (2003) and Chen and Savage (2005) regarding the consistency of soft-pion theorems for \(\gamma^*+p\rightarrow \gamma+N+\pi\) with chiral perturbation theory. It supports the position of Kivel, Polyakov and Stratman (2005) that the soft-pion results are correct, and clarifies that the evolution of nonsinglet spin-dependent and spin-independent parton distributions is governed by the same splitting functions due to chiral symmetry, enabling future use of ChPT to describe pion production in DVCS without conflict with QCD evolution.
