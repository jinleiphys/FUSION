# Transverse Momentum Spectra of Pions at LHC Energies

> In order to characterize the transverse momentum spectra of positive pions measured in the ALICE experiment, two thermal approaches are utilized; one is based on degeneracy of non-perfect Bose-Einstein gas and the other imposes an {\it ad-hoc} finite pion-chemical potential. The inclusion of missing haron states and the out-of-chemical equilibrium greatly contribute to the excellent characterization of pion production. The excellent reproduction of the experimental data can be understood as a manifestation of not-yet-regarded anomalous pion-production, which likely contribute to the long-standing debate on the {\it "anomalous"} proton-to-pion ratios at top RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the transverse momentum spectra of positive pions from central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies can be excellently reproduced by two thermal approaches that assume out-of-chemical equilibrium (finite pion chemical potential), specifically at a value of mu_pi = 0.12 GeV, rather than by equilibrium models or non-extensive Tsallis statistics.

## Method

The author applies two thermal models to ALICE data for positive pions from 0-5% central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.67 TeV. The first model is based on Bogolubov's theory of superfluidity as a non-perfect Bose-Einstein gas, implemented via an out-of-chemical-equilibrium thermal distribution with finite pion chemical potential. The second model is the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, extended by imposing an ad-hoc finite pion chemical potential and including scalar sigma states from the Particle Data Group (PDG) 2018, with and without van der Waals excluded-volume corrections using a hard-core radius of 0.2 fm.

## Key numbers

*   The ALICE data used are from the most central (0-5%) Pb+Pb collisions at 2.67 TeV.
*   The best fit to the pion p_T spectra is achieved at a finite pion chemical potential of mu_pi = 0.12 GeV.
*   With inclusion of scalar sigma states and a hard-core radius r_h = 0.2 fm, the chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch is approximately 151 MeV.
*   With inclusion of scalar sigma states and r_h = 0.4 fm, T_ch is approximately 167 MeV.
*   Without scalar sigma states and with r_h = 0.2 fm, T_ch is approximately 159 MeV.
*   Without scalar sigma states and with r_h = 0.3 fm, T_ch is approximately 164 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas model by incorporating out-of-chemical-equilibrium conditions for pions, contrasting with the standard equilibrium HRG approach (Karsch 2003, Redlich 2004, Tawfik 2004). The author argues that the non-extensive Tsallis statistics (Tawfik 2016, 2017, 2018) is incomplete for describing bulk matter at LHC energies, and that the observed power-laws may instead stem from statistical cluster decay. The results suggest that anomalous pion production, possibly related to scalar sigma states, contributes to the long-standing debate on the "anomalous" proton-to-pion ratios at top RHIC and LHC energies, and the work enables future studies of p_T spectra for other hadrons and bosons under similar out-of-chemical-equilibrium conditions.
