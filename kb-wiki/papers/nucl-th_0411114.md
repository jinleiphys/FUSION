# $Nd\to{^3H}γ$ with Effective Field Theory

> The cross section of neutron-deuteron radiative capture $n d\to {^3H}γ$ is calculated at energies relevant to Big-Bang nucleosynthesis ($ 20 \leq E \leq 200 $ Kev) with pionless Effective Field Theory. At these energies, magnetic transition M1 gives the dominant contribution. The M1 amplitude is calculated up to next-to-next-to leading order(N$^2$LO) with insertion of three-body force. Results are in good agreement within few percent theoretical uncertainty in comparison with available calculated data below E=200 Kev.

## Key claim

The cross section of neutron-deuteron radiative capture is calculated using pionless Effective Field Theory up to next-to-next-to leading order (N$^2$LO) with a three-body force, and the results agree within a few percent theoretical uncertainty with evaluated data from the ENDF/B-VI database for center-of-mass energies between 20 and 200 KeV, but only when the three-body parameters are fixed using the measured doublet scattering length and triton binding energy.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev integral equation for nucleon-deuteron scattering in the doublet S-wave channel using pionless EFT, expanding the kernel perturbatively to N$^2$LO and iterating it numerically with a cutoff regulator. The M1 magnetic dipole amplitude for the radiative capture process was computed by folding the renormalized scattering amplitude with the electromagnetic interaction kernel order by order, including contributions from nucleon magnetic moments and a dibaryon-photon coupling with an unknown coefficient $L_1$. Three-body forces $H_0$ and $H_2$ were determined by requiring cutoff independence and fixing them to the measured doublet scattering length $a_d = 0.65$ fm and triton binding energy $B_3 = 8.48$ MeV, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy used as input: $B_3 = 8.48$ MeV
- Doublet scattering length used as input: $a_d = (0.65 \pm 0.04)$ fm
- At $E = 60$ KeV, the N$^2$LO cross section is $1.50(1)$ microbarn with 0.1% error compared to the ENDF value of $1.50(0)$ microbarn
- At $E = 100$ KeV, the N$^2$LO cross section is $1.93(3)$ microbarn with 0.5% error compared to the ENDF value of $1.94(0)$ microbarn
- At $E = 140$ KeV, the N$^2$LO cross section is $2.30(3)$ microbarn with 0.1% error compared to the ENDF value of $2.22(9)$ microbarn
- At $E = 40$ KeV, the N$^2$LO cross section is $1.25(0)$ microbarn with 1.5% error compared to the ENDF value of $1.27(0)$ microbarn
- The LO cross section at $E = 40$ KeV is $1.64(6)$ microbarn with 30% error

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless EFT from two-nucleon processes like $np \rightarrow \gamma d$ (Rupak, 1999) to the three-nucleon radiative capture reaction $nd \rightarrow {^3H}\gamma$, which is relevant for Big-Bang nucleosynthesis. It builds upon prior Faddeev calculations by Friar et al. (1990) and variational hyperspherical calculations by Viviani et al., which used realistic Hamiltonian models with two- and three-nucleon interactions and pion exchange currents, but the EFT approach provides a systematic power-counting framework with controlled theoretical uncertainties. The results enable model-independent predictions for the $nd$ capture cross section at BBN energies and can be extended to the $pd \rightarrow {^3He}\gamma$ process.
