# Terrestrial and Astrophysical Superfluidity: Cold Atoms and Neutron Matter

> After a brief historical overview of superfluidity in connection with neutron matter and cold fermionic atoms, we discuss the commonalities between these two systems as well as their relevance to the physics of neutron star crusts. We then present the methodological tools we use to attack the many-body problem, comparing Quantum Monte Carlo to mean-field theory and to the analytic expectations at weak coupling. We review recent results on the equation of state and the pairing gap of cold atoms and neutron matter and contrast them with a variety of calculations by other groups. We conclude by giving a few directions of possible future inquiry.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutron matter in neutron-star crusts and ultracold atomic Fermi gases are both "high-temperature superfluids" with comparably large ratios of critical temperature to Fermi temperature (around 0.1-0.2), despite being separated by 17 orders of magnitude in absolute temperature, and that cold-atom experiments can therefore help constrain nuclear theory for neutron matter, which is beyond direct experimental reach.

## Method

The authors employ fixed-node Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) simulations with Jastrow-BCS trial wave functions to compute the ground-state energy and pairing gap of both cold fermionic atoms and dilute neutron matter. For cold atoms, they use a modified Poschl-Teller potential tuned to reproduce the desired scattering length and effective range; for neutron matter, they use the Argonne v18 potential (or its AV4 simplification) and compare results to BCS mean-field theory and analytic weak-coupling expansions. The simulations are performed in periodic cubic boxes with approximately 66 particles, after verifying that this system size is close to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- Cold atoms at unitarity (infinite scattering length): BCS limiting value of the pairing gap is 0.69 times the Fermi energy \(E_F\)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: \(a = -18.5\) fm; effective range: \(r_e = 2.7\) fm
- Neutron matter critical temperature: approximately \(10^{10}\) K; ratio \(T_c/T_F \approx 0.1\)
- Atomic Fermi gas critical temperature: approximately 200 nK; ratio \(T_c/T_F \approx 0.2\)
- For cold atoms at unitarity, the pairing gap extracted from MIT experiments is approximately half of the Fermi energy \(E_F\), in agreement with QMC calculations
- The Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov polarization correction reduces the mean-field BCS pairing gap at weak coupling by a factor of \(1/(4e)^{1/3} \approx 0.45\)
- For neutron matter, \(k_F r_e\) ranges from 0 to 1.5, and \(k_F a\) ranges from 0 to -10

## Context

This work extends the long line of research on superfluidity in neutron matter that began with Migdal (1959) and connects it to the modern cold-atom experiments first demonstrating fermionic superfluidity at MIT in 2005. The authors contrast their Quantum Monte Carlo results with earlier BCS mean-field calculations and with the analytic weak-coupling results of Lee and Yang (1957) and Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961). The paper enables future work using cold-atom measurements to constrain the equation of state and pairing gap of neutron matter, which in turn informs models of neutron-star crust cooling and pulsar glitch dynamics, as developed by Baym (1969) and Page (2009).
