# New CMB constraints on the cosmic matter budget: trouble for nucleosynthesis?

> We compute the joint constraints on ten cosmological parameters from the latest CMB measurements. The lack of a significant second acoustic peak in the latest Boomerang and Maxima data favors models with more baryons than Big Bang nucleosynthesis predicts, almost independently of what prior information is included. The simplest flat inflation models with purely scalar scale-invariant fluctuations prefer a baryon density 0.022 <h^2 Omega_b < 0.040 and a total nonbaryonic (hot + cold) dark matter density 0.14 < h^2 Omega_dm < 0.32 at 95% confidence, and allow reionization no earlier than z~30.

## Key claim

The lack of a prominent second acoustic peak in the Boomerang and Maxima CMB data favors models with a baryon density higher than the standard Big Bang nucleosynthesis prediction, but this conclusion depends on the assumption of simple flat inflation models with purely scalar scale-invariant fluctuations; the tension is not a statistically rock-solid discrepancy, as the best-fit inflation model with low baryon density still yields an acceptable chi-squared value.

## Method

The authors performed a joint 10-parameter likelihood analysis using 87 CMB band-power measurements from COBE, Boomerang, and Maxima, employing a precomputed grid of theoretical power spectra from CMBfast with cubic interpolation to marginalize over parameters. They examined the effects of various priors including a Hubble parameter constraint (0.5 < h < 0.8), a tensor-free inflation prior (r=0, Omega_k=0, n_s=1), and repeated the analysis excluding Maxima data or using only COBE plus Boomerang to test robustness.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density under inflation prior: 0.022 < h^2 Omega_b < 0.040 at 95% confidence, with best-fit value 0.03
- Total nonbaryonic dark matter density under inflation prior: 0.14 < h^2 Omega_dm < 0.32 at 95% confidence, with best-fit value 0.20
- Reionization optical depth upper limit: tau < 0.28 under inflation prior at 95% confidence, corresponding to reionization redshift z < 28 for h = Omega_lambda = 0.7
- Best-fit inflation model with Omega_b = 0.02 gives chi-squared approximately 81 for 87 degrees of freedom, dropping to 71 for Omega_b = 0.03
- Nucleosynthesis constraint: Omega_b = 0.019 +/- 0.0024 from Burles and Tytler (1999)

## Context

This work extends the 10-parameter analysis of Tegmark and Zaldarriaga (2000) by incorporating the new Boomerang and Maxima data, which provided the first accurate measurements of the CMB power spectrum out to multipole l ~ 600-800. The results contradict the standard Big Bang nucleosynthesis baryon density from Burles and Tytler (1998) and Burles et al. (1999), while supporting the simplest inflation models of Steinhardt (1995) and Turner (1997) except for the baryon excess. The analysis enables future searches for a third acoustic peak to distinguish between high baryon density and alternative explanations such as delayed recombination or time-varying constants.
