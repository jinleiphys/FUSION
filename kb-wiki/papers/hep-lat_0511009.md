# Nuclear models on a lattice

> We present the first results of a quantum field approach to nuclear models obtained by lattice techniques. Renormalization effects for fermion mass and coupling constant in case of scalar and pseudoscalar interaction lagrangian densities are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first results of a lattice-based quantum field theory approach to nuclear one-boson exchange models, but the work is limited to the quenched approximation (neglecting virtual nucleon-antinucleon pairs) and focuses only on renormalization effects for fermion mass and coupling constant in scalar and pseudoscalar interaction Lagrangians.

## Method

The authors solved scalar and pseudoscalar Yukawa-type models on a discrete Euclidean space-time lattice of volume \(V=L^4\) using the quenched approximation, where virtual nucleon-antinucleon pairs are neglected. They used Monte Carlo techniques to generate meson field configurations and computed the fermion propagator by inverting the Dirac-Wilson operator. The renormalized fermion mass \(M_R\) was extracted from time-slice correlators, and the renormalized coupling constant \(g_R\) was determined using the MOM renormalization scheme at a scale \(\mu\).

## Key numbers

- The critical hopping parameter for the free (\(g=0\)) case is \(\kappa_c^0 = 1/8\).
- For scalar coupling, the critical lattice coupling constant is \(g_s^c \lesssim 1\), with negative eigenvalues of the Dirac-Wilson operator appearing for \(g \simeq 0.6\).
- For scalar coupling, the renormalized coupling constant equals the bare value \(g_R = g_0\) in the accessible range \(g_0 \lesssim 3\).
- The scalar coupling momentum dependence shows no structure up to \(\mu/m_0 \sim 12\), corresponding to \(p \sim 6\) GeV.
- The bare meson mass used in the calculations is \(m_0 = 0.25\) (in lattice units).

## Context

This work extends the lattice approach previously applied to a purely scalar \(\phi^2\chi\) model by NT (1996) to the more realistic case of fermion-boson interactions relevant for nuclear physics. It aims to go beyond the ladder approximation used in traditional one-boson exchange potential models such as those by NIJ (1993), AV18 (1995), and M (2001), as well as chiral-inspired NN models by W (1991), ORVK (1996), and EGM (2000). The results enable future calculations of binding energies in multifermion systems using the full quantum field theory treatment rather than potential approximations.
