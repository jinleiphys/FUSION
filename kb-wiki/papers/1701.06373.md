# (No) neutron star maximum mass constraint from hypernuclei

> (Abridged) The recent measurement of the mass of two $2\, M_\odot$ pulsars has raised the question whether such large masses allow for the existence of exotic degrees of freedom, such as hyperons, inside neutron stars. In the present work we will investigate how the existing hypernuclei properties may constrain the neutron star equation of state and confront the neutron star maximum masses obtained with equations of state calibrated to hypernuclei properties with the astrophysical $2\,M_\odot$ constraint. The study is performed using a relativistic mean field approach to describe both the hypernuclei and the neutron star equations of state. A set of five models consistent with $2\,M_\odot$ for a purely nucleonic composition are employed. The $Λ$-meson couplings are determined for all the models considered. Hyperonic stars with the complete baryonic octet are studied, restricting the coupling of the $Σ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons to the $ω-$, $ρ-$ and $σ-$mesons due to the lack of experimental data, and maximum star masses calculated for unified equations of state. We conclude that the currently available hypernuclei experimental data and the lack of constraints on the asymmetric equation of state of nuclear matter at high densities do not allow to further constrain the neutron star matter equation of state using the recent $2\, M_\odot$ observations. It is also shown that the $Λ$ potential in symmetric nuclear matter takes a value $\sim 30-32$ MeV at saturation for the $g_{ωΛ}$ coupling given by the SU(6) symmetry, close to values generally used in the literature. However, the $Λ$ potential in $Λ$ matter varies between -16 and -8 MeV taking for vector mesons couplings the SU(6) values, at variance with generally employed values between $-1$ and $-5$ MeV.

## Key claim
The currently available experimental data on hypernuclei and the lack of constraints on the asymmetric equation of state of nuclear matter at high densities do not allow to further constrain the neutron star matter equation of state using the recent 2 solar mass observations, contrary to the expectation that hypernuclear data might rule out or confirm the presence of hyperons in massive neutron stars.

## Method
The study uses a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach to describe both hypernuclei and neutron star equations of state. Five RMF models (TM1, TM2ωρ, NL3, NL3ωρ, and DDME2) that predict 2 solar mass purely nucleonic stars are employed. The Λ-meson couplings are determined from single and double Λ-hypernuclei binding energies, while Σ and Ξ hyperon couplings to the ω-, ρ-, and σ-mesons are restricted due to lack of experimental data. Hyperonic stars with the complete baryonic octet are studied, and maximum star masses are calculated for unified equations of state.

## Key numbers
- The Λ potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation takes a value of 30-32 MeV for the gωΛ coupling given by SU(6) symmetry (gωΛ = 2gωN/3).
- The Λ potential in Λ matter varies between -16 and -8 MeV when using SU(6) values for vector meson couplings, at variance with generally employed values between -1 and -5 MeV.
- When the SU(6) constraint is relaxed and vector meson couplings to hyperons are kept to values not larger than to nucleons, the Λ potential in Λ matter ranges between -17 and +9 MeV.
- The five RMF models predict purely nucleonic maximum masses ranging from 2.18 M⊙ (TM1) to 2.77 M⊙ (NL3).

## Context
This work extends the line of research on the "hyperon puzzle" raised by the measurements of 2 solar mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010; Fonseca 2016) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013). It follows a similar study by van Dalen, Colucci, and Sedrakian (2014) that used symmetry arguments to fix vector meson couplings to hyperons and single Λ-hypernuclei binding energies to constrain the σ-meson coupling to the Λ. The present work additionally incorporates double Λ-hypernuclei data and considers a wider range of coupling values. The results enable future work to focus on obtaining better experimental constraints on hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, particularly for Σ and Ξ hyperons, and on improving constraints on the asymmetric equation of state at high densities.
