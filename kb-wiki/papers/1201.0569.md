# Dilepton to photon ratio, a viscometer of QGP

> In the Israel-Stewart's 2nd order hydrodynamics, a viscous effect on dilepton emission from a QGP medium is investigated. Dileptons are strongly affected by QGP viscosity. Large invariant mass dileptons, due to their lower velocity, are less affected by viscosity than the low invariant mass dileptons. We also show that the ratio of photon to dilepton is sensitive to the viscosity and can serve as a viscometer for QGP.

# Dilepton to photon ratio, a viscometer of QGP

## Key claim

The ratio of dilepton to photon production in heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and can serve as a viscometer, provided that the dileptons have large invariant mass (e.g., 600–900 MeV) and the measurement is taken in a transverse momentum range where viscous hydrodynamics remains applicable (e.g., \(p_T \lesssim 1.5\) GeV for \(\eta/s = 0.12\)).

## Method

The authors solved Israel-Stewart's second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (using the code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA) for 20–40% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, assuming boost-invariance and an equation of state based on the Wuppertal-Budapest lattice simulation (pseudo-critical temperature \(T_c \approx 170\) MeV). The initial energy density was distributed via a Glauber model with a hard-scattering fraction \(f_{hard} = 0.13\), thermalisation time \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm, and central temperature \(T_0 = 350\) MeV. Dilepton production rates from quark-antiquark annihilation were computed with a non-equilibrium correction to the distribution function \(\delta f_{neq} = \frac{1}{2(\varepsilon+p)T^2} q_\mu q_\nu \pi^{\mu\nu}\), and the ratio of dilepton to photon yields was evaluated for several fixed values of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s\).

## Key numbers

- For \(\eta/s = 0.04\), 0.08, and 0.12, the non-equilibrium correction to dilepton production equals the equilibrium contribution at \(p_T \approx 2.9\), 1.9, and 1.5 GeV respectively for invariant mass \(M = 300\) MeV; at \(p_T \approx 3.0\), 2.1, and 1.7 GeV for \(M = 600\) MeV; and at \(p_T \approx 3.2\), 2.4, and 2.0 GeV for \(M = 900\) MeV.
- For dileptons of invariant mass \(M = 600\) MeV, if the dilepton-to-photon ratio is measured within 10% accuracy at \(p_T \approx 1.5\) GeV, the viscosity to entropy ratio \(\eta/s\) can be estimated within \(\sim 5\%\) accuracy; for \(M = 900\) MeV, the sensitivity improves to \(\sim 2\%\).
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) for dileptons of invariant mass \(M = 600\) MeV in ideal QGP reaches a maximum of \(\approx 0.04\) at \(p_T \approx 2.5\) GeV; with increasing viscosity, the flow is reduced and becomes negative at \(\eta/s = 0.12\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of viscous effects on electromagnetic probes, particularly the authors' own investigation of viscous effects on direct photon production (Chaudhuri, 2011) and the work of Dusling (2008, 2009) on viscous corrections to photon and dilepton signals. The paper builds on the idea that the dilepton-to-photon ratio, originally proposed as a clear signal of QGP formation (Sinha, 1983; Sinha, 1985), can also serve as a quantitative viscometer because large invariant mass dileptons move more slowly than photons and thus experience less viscous drag. The authors argue that this ratio largely cancels uncertainties from initial conditions, which in hadronic analyses can induce uncertainties of \(\sim 175\%\) or more in \(\eta/s\) (Chaudhuri, 2009). The results enable future experimental measurements (e.g., with the ALICE detector) to extract the QGP viscosity with improved precision.
