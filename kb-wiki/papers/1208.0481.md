# Shear Viscosity of a strongly interacting system: Green-Kubo vs. Chapman-Enskog and Relaxation Time Approximation

> The shear viscosity $η$ has been calculated by using the Green-Kubo relation in the framework of a partonic transport approach solved at cascade level. We compare the numerical results for $η$ obtained from the Green-Kubo correlator with the analytical formulas in both the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) and the Chapman-Enskog approximation (CE). We investigate and emphasize the differences between the isotropic and anisotropic cross sections and between the massless and massive particles. We show that in the range of temperature explored in a Heavy Ion collision and for pQCD-like cross section the RTA significantly underestimates the viscosity by about a factor of 2-3, while a good agreement is found between the CE approximation and Gree-Kubo relation already at first order of approximation. The agreement with the CE approximation supplies an analytical formula that allows to develop kinetic transport theory at fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$. This open the possibility to explore dissipative non-equilibrium evolution of the distribution functions vs T-dependent $η/s$ and particle momenta in the dynamics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Chapman-Enskog (CE) approximation at first order agrees well with the exact Green-Kubo calculation of shear viscosity for a partonic system, while the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) underestimates the viscosity by a factor of 2-3 for pQCD-like cross sections in the temperature range relevant to heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann transport simulations in a static box with periodic boundary conditions, using a Monte Carlo cascade method with test particles to solve the collision integral for elastic two-body collisions. They extracted the shear viscosity numerically from the Green-Kubo relation by computing the time correlation function of the shear component of the energy-momentum tensor, fitting it to an exponential decay to obtain the relaxation time. They compared these numerical results with analytical formulas from the Relaxation Time Approximation and the Chapman-Enskog method at first order, for both isotropic and anisotropic cross sections and for massless and massive particles.

## Key numbers

- For massless particles with isotropic cross section, the CE approximation at 16th order gives η = 1.267 T/σ_tot, while the first-order CE gives η = 1.2 T/σ_tot (a 6% difference); the RTA gives η = 0.8 T/σ_tot, which is about 40% smaller than the first-order CE value.
- The Green-Kubo results agree with the CE 16th-order prediction to within about 2% for isotropic cross sections over the temperature range T = 0.2 to 0.6 GeV and cross sections from 1 to 30 mb.
- For the massive case, the function f(z) = η σ_tot/T varies from 1.2 in the ultra-relativistic limit (z = m/T → 0) to values determined by the formula f(z) = (15/16) z^4 K_3^2(z) / [(15z^2+2)K_2(2z)+(3z^3+49z)K_3(2z)].
- The simulations used a time step Δt = 0.01 fm/c, box volume V_box = 27 fm^3, cell volume ΔV_cell = (0.1)^3 fm^3, and N_test = 1000 test particles, with T_max = 100 τ for convergence.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shear viscosity in relativistic plasmas using the Green-Kubo method, such as those by Demir and Bass (2009) and Muronga (2004) for hadronic systems, and by Fuini (2011) and Wesp (2011) for QCD with pQCD matrix elements. The key advance is the systematic comparison of the Green-Kubo numerical results with the CE and RTA analytical approximations, showing that the CE at first order provides an accurate analytical formula for η in terms of temperature, cross section, density, and mass. This enables the construction of kinetic transport theory at a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s, allowing exploration of dissipative non-equilibrium evolution in quark-gluon plasma dynamics with temperature-dependent η/s and particle momenta.
