# Coupled-channels effects in elastic scattering and near-barrier fusion induced by weakly bound nuclei and exotic halo nuclei

> The influence on fusion of coupling to the breakup process is investigated for reactions where at least one of the colliding nuclei has a sufficiently low binding energy for breakup to become an important process. Elastic scattering, excitation functions for sub-and near-barrier fusion cross sections, and breakup yields are analyzed for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co. Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations describe well the data at and above the barrier. Elastic scattering with $^{6}$Li (as compared to $^{7}$Li) indicates the significant role of breakup for weakly bound projectiles. A study of $^{4,6}$He induced fusion reactions with a three-body CDCC method for the $^6$He halo nucleus is presented. The relative importance of breakup and bound-state structure effects on total fusion is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether breakup (BU) couplings enhance or suppress fusion for weakly bound and halo nuclei, finding that Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations describe elastic scattering and fusion data well at and above the barrier, but the relative importance of breakup versus bound-state structure effects on total fusion remains ambiguous, particularly for $^6$He where the calculated total fusion cross sections exceed experimental values by 20-30%.

## Method

Standard three-body CDCC calculations were performed for $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co elastic scattering and breakup, assuming $\alpha+d$ and $\alpha+t$ cluster structures, with the continuum discretized into momentum bins up to $k_{\mathrm{max}} = 1.0$ fm$^{-1}$ and couplings up to multipolarity $\lambda = 3$ included. For $^6$He-induced fusion on $^{59}$Co, $^{63}$Cu, and $^{65}$Cu targets, a simplified two-body $^4$He + $^2n$ di-neutron model of $^6$He was employed with the CDCC fusion model, including couplings to the $2^+$ resonant state and non-resonant continuum states up to a maximum continuum energy of 8 MeV. Optical model (OM) fits to elastic scattering data were also performed to extract total reaction cross sections for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For $^7$Li+$^{59}$Co at 30 MeV: total reaction cross section from CDCC = 1610 mb, integrated BU cross section = 16.6 mb (about 1.0% of total reaction)
- For $^6$Li+$^{59}$Co at 30 MeV: total reaction cross section from CDCC = 1581 mb, integrated BU cross section = 61.4 mb (about 3.9% of total reaction)
- For $^6$Li+$^{59}$Co at 12 MeV: total reaction cross section from CDCC = 116.0 mb, integrated BU cross section = 7.46 mb (about 6.4% of total reaction)
- For $^7$Li+$^{59}$Co at 12 MeV: total reaction cross section from CDCC = 83.5 mb, integrated BU cross section = 0.31 mb (about 0.4% of total reaction)
- Measured total fusion cross sections for $^6$Li+$^{59}$Co: 988 $\pm$ 199 mb at 26 MeV, 467 $\pm$ 94 mb at 18 MeV, 57 $\pm$ 12 mb at 12 MeV
- Measured total fusion cross sections for $^7$Li+$^{59}$Co: 1014 $\pm$ 204 mb at 26 MeV, 547 $\pm$ 110 mb at 18 MeV, 38 $\pm$ 8 mb at 12 MeV
- For $^6$He+$^{65}$Cu at 30 MeV: CDCC total fusion with continuum couplings = 1846 mb, experimental total residue cross section = 1334 mb (CDCC overestimates by about 38%)
- For $^6$He+$^{63}$Cu at 30 MeV: CDCC total fusion with continuum couplings = 1830 mb, experimental total residue cross section = 1400 mb
- The $\alpha+d$ binding potential for $^6$Li yielded a $B(E2)$ of 24.0 e$^2$fm$^4$, compared to the measured value of $25.6 \pm 2.0$ e$^2$fm$^4$
- The $^6$He $2^+$ resonant state energy was set to 0.826 MeV with a width of 0.075 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier CDCC studies of weakly bound nuclei by Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002) and Keeley et al. (2002, 2003) to medium-mass targets like $^{59}$Co, where Coulomb breakup effects are smaller than for heavy targets such as $^{209}$Bi. The results contradict earlier suggestions by Zagrebaev (2003) that neutron transfer from $^6$He enhances fusion, instead finding that CDCC calculations overpredict fusion for $^6$He on copper targets compared to experimental data from Navin et al. (2004). The paper enables future four-body CDCC calculations for Borromean nuclei like $^6$He and highlights the need for more exclusive measurements to disentangle breakup, transfer, and fusion components.
