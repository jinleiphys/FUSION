# Large Nc Continuum Reduction and the Thermodynamics of QCD

> It is noted that if large Nc continuum reduction applies to an observable, then that observable is independent of temperature for all temperatures below some critical value. This fact, plus the fact that mesons and glueballs are weakly interacting at large Nc is used as the basis for a derivation of large Nc continuum reduction for the chiral condensate. The structure of this derivation is quite general and can be extended to a wide class of observables.

## Key claim

If large \(N_c\) continuum reduction applies to an observable, then that observable is independent of temperature for all temperatures below some critical temperature \(T_c\), up to corrections of order \(1/N_c\); this temperature independence is used as the basis for deriving that continuum reduction itself holds for the chiral condensate and a wide class of other operators.

## Method

The author uses standard 't Hooft large-\(N_c\) counting to show that mesons and glueballs are weakly interacting at large \(N_c\), with interaction strengths scaling as \(N_c^{-1}\) or \(N_c^{-2}\). From this, the finite-temperature free energy density is expressed as that of a non-interacting gas of mesons and glueballs, plus corrections vanishing at large \(N_c\). The chiral condensate is then obtained by differentiating the free energy with respect to quark mass, and the resulting temperature shift is shown to be suppressed by \(1/N_c\) relative to the zero-temperature condensate. The same logic is extended to boxes of finite spatial size, and by relabeling Euclidean time directions the author argues that the condensate becomes independent of all box dimensions above a critical size, establishing continuum reduction.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate at finite temperature \(T\) satisfies \(\frac{\langle \overline{q}q \rangle_T}{\langle \overline{q}q \rangle_{T=0}} = 1 + \mathcal{O}(T^8)\) in the large-\(N_c\) limit, based on chiral perturbation theory up to order \(T^6\) and the scaling \(f_\pi \sim N_c^{1/2}\).
- The shift in the renormalization-group-invariant quantity \(m\langle \overline{q}q\rangle\) from its zero-temperature value scales as \(\frac{m\langle \overline{q}q\rangle_T - m\langle \overline{q}q\rangle_{T=0}}{m\langle \overline{q}q\rangle_{T=0}} \sim \mathcal{O}(N_c^{-1})\) for temperatures below the critical temperature \(T_c\).
- The sigma commutator \(\sigma_s\) scales as \(N_c^0\) for mesons and as \(N_c^{-1}\) for glueballs; the zero-temperature condensate \(m\langle \overline{q}q\rangle_{T=0}\) scales as \(N_c^1\).

## Context

This work extends the Eguchi-Kawai (EK) reduction idea (Eguchi and Kawai, 1982) and the more recent large-\(N_c\) continuum reduction proposals (references cited as lncr1–lncr4, 2004) by providing a derivation based on well-known large-\(N_c\) hadronic phenomenology rather than relying on random matrix theory (RMT) as in the conjecture of reference cc (2004). The derivation shows that the chiral condensate is temperature-independent below a Hagedorn-like transition temperature \(T_c\), and this property is used to prove continuum reduction for a finite box. The result enables reliable large-\(N_c\) lattice simulations on relatively small boxes, potentially yielding converged results for a "colorful world" in the near term, and it opens the question of whether the same logic applies to a wider class of local Lorentz scalar operators.
