# Light Front Theory Of Nuclear Matter

> A relativistic light front formulation of nuclear dynamics is applied to infinite nuclear matter. A hadronic meson-baryon Lagrangian, consistent with chiral symmetry, leads to a nuclear eigenvalue problem which is solved, including nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations, in the one-boson-exchange approximation for the NN potential. The nuclear matter saturation properties are reasonably well reproduced, with a compression modulus of 180 MeV. We find that there are about 0.05 excess pions per nucleon.

# Light Front Theory Of Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

A relativistic light-front formulation of nuclear dynamics, using a one-boson-exchange approximation for the NN potential derived from a chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian, can reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties reasonably well, but only when medium-modified single-particle energies are used in the evaluation of the retardation term, which reduces the compression modulus from the earlier relativistic value of 250 MeV to 180 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a light-front Hamiltonian from a chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian, eliminate dependent degrees of freedom, and derive a one-boson-exchange NN potential that is essentially identical to the Bonn OBEP potentials. They then solve a light-front version of the Brueckner G-matrix equation for infinite nuclear matter, using a self-consistent procedure that determines the scalar and vector mean fields (U_S and U_V^-) from the G-matrix via a Hartree-Fock approximation, and compute the binding energy per nucleon as a function of Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A = -14.71 MeV
- Saturation Fermi momentum: k_F = 1.37 fm^{-1}
- Compression modulus (incompressibility): K = 180 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: M*(k_F) = 718 MeV (with a spread; also 744 MeV yields good saturation)
- Vector mean field at saturation: U_V = 164.4 MeV
- Excess pions per nucleon: approximately 0.05 (5.0% per nucleon)
- Nucleons carry 81% of the nuclear plus momentum (compared to 65% in mean field theory)

## Context

This work extends the light-front mean-field calculation of Miller (1997) by including nucleon-nucleon correlations via a Brueckner G-matrix approach, and it contrasts with non-relativistic Brueckner theory (Grangé and Preston, 1973; Saraceno et al., 1988), which predicts too high a saturation density. The compression modulus of 180 MeV is reduced from the earlier relativistic value of 250 MeV obtained by Brockmann and Machleidt (1984, 1990), and the computed 5% pionic enhancement is consistent within errors with the Drell-Yan data of Alde et al. (1990) and with (p,n) reaction data, unlike the 18% pion excess found by Friman, Pandharipande, and Wiringa (1983). The results enable improved calculations of nuclear deep inelastic scattering structure functions, increasing the computed F2A/F2N ratio by a factor of twenty toward the data extrapolated by Sick and Day (1992).
