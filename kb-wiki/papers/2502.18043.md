# Microscopic study of halo nuclei through (p,t) reactions

> We analyze (p,t) two-neutron transfer reactions in a semi-microscopic model. The overlap integrals of the target nucleus are calculated in a microscopic cluster model. The Resonating Group Method (RGM) assumes a cluster structure of the nucleus, and is well adapted to halo nuclei since the long-range part of the wave function is accurately described. We focus on (p,t) reactions involving 6He and 11Li, which are well known core+n+n halo nuclei. The RGM is based on a nucleon-nucleon interaction, and therefore does not involve any fitting procedure. It also provides overlap integrals of excited states of the core nucleus. We present overlap integrals and spectroscopic factors of 6He and 11Li. We compute the 6He(p,t)alpha and 11Li(p,t)9Li cross sections at the DWBA, and compare them with experiments. For 11Li we also determine the 11Li(p,t)9Li* cross section which involves the first excited states of 9Li. A fair agreement with experiment is obtained, considering that no parameter is adjusted.

# Microscopic study of halo nuclei through (p,t) reactions

## Key claim
The Resonating Group Method (RGM) provides overlap integrals for (p,t) two-neutron transfer reactions on halo nuclei without any fitting parameter, and achieves fair agreement with experimental cross sections for 6He and 11Li, though the model overestimates data at small angles for 6He and at large angles for 11Li.

## Method
The author calculates overlap integrals for 6He and 11Li using the Resonating Group Method (RGM), a microscopic cluster model that treats the target nucleus as a core plus two neutrons and includes core excited states via shell-model wave functions. These overlap integrals are then used as input to compute (p,t) transfer cross sections within the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), where the scattering wave functions in the entrance and exit channels are obtained from optical potentials.

## Key numbers
- Spectroscopic factor for 6He: S_c = 1.41 (core ground state 0+), in agreement with literature values of 1.39 (Varga et al., 1994) and 1.3957 (Broglia and Nathan, 2010)
- Spectroscopic factors for 11Li: S_c = 0.78 (S12=0) and 0.13 (S12=1) for the 9Li ground state (3/2-); S_c = 0.054 (S12=0) and 0.050 (S12=1) for the 9Li first excited state (1/2-)
- 6He(p,t)4He reaction measured at Elab = 150 MeV (Wolski et al., 1999; Giot et al., 2005)
- 11Li(p,t)9Li reaction measured at Elab = 33 MeV (Tanihata et al., 2008)
- For 6He, the maximum cross section at theta=0 degrees is predicted near Ecm ≈ 8 MeV, corresponding to Elab ≈ 56 MeV
- The 6He overlap integral contains a 16.1% S12=1 component due to the spin-orbit force

## Context
This work extends the three-body model calculations for 6He(p,t)4He by Ozhgibesov et al. (1999) and for 11Li(p,t)9Li by Descouvemont (2021) by replacing phenomenological overlap integrals with fully microscopic RGM overlap integrals that include core excitations. The method enables spectroscopic study of halo nuclei through two-neutron transfer without adjustable parameters, and the author suggests that measurements at other energies and on other halo nuclei such as 14Be would provide valuable tests of the model.
