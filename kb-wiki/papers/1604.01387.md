# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of two neutrons in finite volume

> Ab initio calculations provide direct access to the properties of pure neutron systems that are challenging to study experimentally. In addition to their importance for fundamental physics, their properties are required as input for effective field theories of the strong interaction. In this work, we perform auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of the ground and first excited state of two neutrons in a finite box, considering a simple contact potential as well as chiral effective field theory interactions. We compare the results against exact diagonalizations and present a detailed analysis of the finite-volume effects, whose understanding is crucial for determining observables from the calculated energies. Using the Lüscher formula, we extract the low-energy S-wave scattering parameters from ground- and excited-state energies for different box sizes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of two neutrons in a finite volume can reliably reproduce the low-energy S-wave scattering parameters (scattering length and effective range) via the Luscher formula, but only when the fit is restricted to momenta within the strict radius of convergence of pionless effective field theory, \(|p| < m_\pi/2\), to avoid complications from t-channel pion exchange cuts.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of the ground and first excited state of two neutrons in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using both a smeared contact potential and chiral effective field theory interactions at different orders. They compared the finite-volume energy eigenvalues against exact diagonalizations and against solutions of the Luscher formula combined with the effective-range expansion, extracting scattering parameters via a chi-squared minimization procedure. For the excited state, they introduced a nodal surface in the trial wave function, determined iteratively by requiring equal energies in the subspaces on either side of the node, and later improved this with a nonspherical nodal surface informed by exact diagonalization.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state AFDMC results for the contact potential with physical nn scattering length \(a = -18.9\) fm gave \(q^2 = -0.1001(3)\) at \(L = 5\) fm, \(-0.0879(7)\) at \(L = 10\) fm, and \(-0.069(2)\) at \(L = 20\) fm, in excellent agreement with the Luscher formula.
* For the contact potential with a very large scattering length \(a = -101.7\) fm, a two-parameter fit to ground-state AFDMC data yielded \(a = -98(4)\) fm and effective range \(r_e = 1.066(7)\) fm, consistent with the infinite-volume values.
* A three-parameter fit to combined ground- and excited-state AFDMC data (with improved nodal surface) for the contact potential with physical scattering length gave \(a = -19.0(1)\) fm and \(r_e = 1.081(5)\) fm, agreeing with the infinite-volume values.
* Excited-state energies from exact diagonalization agreed with the Luscher formula to within 0.01% for \(L = 20, 30, 40\) fm, with deviations of 1.6% at \(L = 5\) fm and 0.2% at \(L = 10\) fm due to the finite range of the regulator.

## Context

This work extends the application of AFDMC, previously used for neutron matter and nuclei (Carlson 2015, Gandolfi 2015), to finite-volume two-neutron calculations with chiral EFT interactions, benchmarking against the Luscher formalism (Luscher 1986, 1991). The results establish AFDMC as a reliable tool for extracting infinite-volume scattering parameters from finite-volume energies, which is a necessary step toward matching lattice QCD calculations to chiral EFT interactions (Barnoski 2013, Kirscher 2015). This enables future work where low-energy constants of chiral EFT could be determined directly from lattice QCD data rather than from scarce experimental data, particularly for systems like three neutrons.
