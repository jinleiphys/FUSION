# Evolution of the transverse-momentum dependent gluon distribution at small $x$

> Using the colour dipole picture for photon-nucleus interactions at small $x$ together with the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, we demonstrate that the next-to-leading (NLO) order corrections to the cross-section for the inclusive production of a pair of hard jets encode not only the JIMWLK evolution with decreasing $x$, but also the DGLAP evolution of the gluon distribution function and the CSS evolution of the gluon transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distribution. The emergent CSS equation takes the form of a rate equation describing the evolution of the dijet distribution in the transverse momentum imbalance $K_\perp$ when increasing the dijet relative momentum $P_\perp$. All three types of evolution become important when both $P_\perp$ and $K_\perp$ are much larger than the nuclear saturation momentum $Q_s(x)$ and we propose a framework which encompasses all of them. The solution to the JIMWLK equation provides the source term for the DGLAP evolution with increasing $K_\perp$, which in turn generates the initial condition for the CSS evolution with increasing $P_\perp$.

## Key claim
The NLO corrections to dijet production in the dipole picture encode not only the JIMWLK evolution with decreasing $x$, but also the DGLAP evolution of the gluon PDF and the CSS evolution of the gluon TMD, provided one considers the regime where both the dijet relative momentum $P_\perp$ and the imbalance $K_\perp$ are much larger than the nuclear saturation momentum $Q_s(x)$.

## Method
The authors performed an alternative calculation of the NLO corrections to inclusive back-to-back dijet production in photon-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory. They applied a leading-twist approximation that exploits the separation of transverse momentum scales ($P_\perp \gg K_\perp \gg Q_s$) and expanded the NLO corrections to leading order in the small ratios $K_\perp/P_\perp$ and $Q_s/K_\perp$. By changing the longitudinal variables from the projectile picture ($z_g$) to the target picture ($x_g$) and imposing time-ordering constraints from collinearly-improved BK/JIMWLK evolution, they identified the DGLAP splitting function $P_{gg}(\xi)$ and the CSS evolution equation from the real and virtual gluon emission corrections.

## Key numbers
* The Sudakov double logarithm at NLO is of order $\alpha_s \ln^2(P_\perp^2/K_\perp^2)$, where $P_\perp$ is the dijet relative momentum and $K_\perp$ is the momentum imbalance.
* The single logarithmic NLO corrections are of order $\alpha_s \ln(P_\perp^2/Q_s^2)$, associated with DGLAP evolution.
* The kinematic condition for the gluon emission to contribute to the DGLAP and CSS evolutions is $z_g \lesssim K_\perp/P_\perp$, where $z_g$ is the gluon longitudinal momentum fraction relative to the photon.
* The LO gluon PDF is defined as $xG^{(0)}(x, P_\perp^2) = \pi \int^{P_\perp^2}_{\Lambda^2} d\ell_\perp^2 \mathcal{F}_g^{(0)}(x, \ell_\perp)$, where $\Lambda$ is an infrared cutoff of the order of the confinement scale.

## Context
This work extends the CGC approach to dijet production (Caucal 2021, Taels 2022, Iancu 2022) by demonstrating that the NLO corrections contain the DGLAP and CSS evolutions of the target gluon distribution, which had not been previously identified in this framework. It builds on the TMD factorisation at small $x$ established by Dominguez (2011) and the collinearly-improved BK/JIMWLK equations (Beuf 2014, Iancu 2015). The result enables a unified framework that simultaneously resums small-$x$, DGLAP, and Sudakov logarithms for dijet production at the EIC and in ultra-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
