# Symmetric cumulants and event-plane correlations in Pb+Pb collisions

> The ALICE Collaboration has recently measured the correlations between amplitudes of anisotropic flow in different Fourier harmonics, referred to as symmetric cumulants. We derive approximate relations between symmetric cumulants involving $v_4$ and $v_5$ and the event-plane correlations measured by ATLAS. The validity of these relations is tested using event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations. The corresponding results are in better agreement with ALICE data than existing hydrodynamic predictions. We make quantitative predictions for three symmetric cumulants which are not yet measured.

## Key claim
The paper derives approximate proportionality relations between symmetric cumulants (involving \(v_4\) and \(v_5\)) and event-plane correlations measured by ATLAS, under the assumption that linear and nonlinear contributions to \(V_4\) and \(V_5\) are independent. These relations allow data-driven predictions for three unmeasured symmetric cumulants without relying on specific hydrodynamic models of initial conditions or medium properties.

## Method
The authors decompose \(V_4\) and \(V_5\) into linear and nonlinear parts, defining Pearson correlation coefficients \(\cos\Phi_{24}\) and \(\cos\Phi_{235}\) that correspond to ATLAS event-plane correlations. Assuming the linear parts are statistically independent of \(V_2\) and \(V_3\), they derive exact relations expressing symmetric cumulants \(sc(4,2)\), \(sc(4,3)\), \(sc(5,2)\), and \(sc(5,3)\) in terms of these event-plane correlations and moments of \(v_2\) and \(v_3\). The relations are tested using event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, \(\eta/s=0.08\), and v-USPhydro, generating 1000 events per 5% centrality bin for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.

## Key numbers
- For \(sc(4,2)\): the right-hand side of the derived relation using ATLAS data agrees with ALICE direct measurements across all centralities, with the absolute difference between left and right sides of the exact relations typically a few \(10^{-2}\) in hydrodynamic tests.
- The hydrodynamic test shows the difference between the two sides of Eqs. (exactrelations) has statistical error bars in the range \(0.02-0.03\), smaller than the difference itself.
- For \(sc(5,2)\) and \(sc(5,3)\): approximate relations using \(\langle\cos(2\Phi_2+3\Phi_3-5\Phi_5)\rangle_w^2\) and moments of \(v_2\) or \(v_3\) from ATLAS data are valid for central collisions but become worse as centrality percentile increases.
- For \(sc(4,3)\): the approximate relation uses ALICE data for \(sc(3,2)\) (which satisfies \(|sc(3,2)|\ll 1\) for all centralities) and ATLAS data for moments of \(v_2\) and \(\cos\Phi_{24}\).

## Context
This work extends the flow phenomenology pioneered by ALICE (2016) measuring symmetric cumulants \(sc(3,2)\) and \(sc(4,2)\), and by ATLAS (2014) measuring event-plane correlations. It provides a data-driven bridge between these two sets of observables, circumventing the typical limitation of hydrodynamic predictions that depend on initial conditions or medium properties (Luzum 2008, Gale 2012, Qiu 2012, Heinz 2013, Bozek 2013, van der Schee 2013, Retinskaya 2013). The derived relations enable direct tests of hydrodynamic behavior using existing data and make quantitative predictions for \(sc(5,2)\), \(sc(5,3)\), and \(sc(4,3)\) that can be measured in future ALICE analyses.
