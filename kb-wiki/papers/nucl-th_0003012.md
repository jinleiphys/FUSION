# From meson-nucleon scattering to vector mesons in nuclear matter

> We employ meson-nucleon scattering data to deduce the properties of the low-mass vector mesons in nuclear matter, and present results for the $ρ$ and $ω$ in-medium spectral functions. The corresponding thermal emission rate for lepton pairs is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper uses meson-nucleon scattering data to compute the leading-order in-medium spectral functions of the ρ and ω mesons in nuclear matter, but the authors caution that the results are only qualitatively correct at normal nuclear matter density and that quantitative changes may occur when higher-order terms in the low-density expansion are included.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic, unitary coupled-channel approach to meson-nucleon scattering, fitting the parameters of an effective field theory to elastic πN scattering data and pion-induced production data for η, ω, ρ, KΛ, and KΣ channels in the energy window 1.4 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 1.9 GeV. The resulting vector-meson–nucleon s-wave scattering amplitudes are then used to construct the ρ and ω self energies in nuclear matter to leading order in density via the low-density theorem.

## Key numbers

- ρ-nucleon scattering length: a_ρN = (-0.1 + 0.6 i) fm
- ω-nucleon scattering length: a_ωN = (-0.5 + 0.2 i) fm
- Leading-order in-medium mass shifts at nuclear matter density: Δm_ρ ≈ 10 MeV, Δm_ω ≈ 50 MeV
- Leading-order in-medium width changes at nuclear matter density: ΔΓ_ρ ≈ 120 MeV, ΔΓ_ω ≈ 40 MeV
- ρ meson coupling to N*(1520): f_{N*Nρ} = 3.2
- ω meson coupling to N*(1520): f_{N*Nω} = 6.5
- At ρ = ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}, the lower peak of the ρ spectral function carries about 20% of the energy-weighted sum rule, and the center-of-gravity shifts down by about 10%
- At T = 140 MeV, the resonance-hole mode in the ρ channel produces enhancement in the invariant-mass region 400–600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the low-density expansion approach (Lutz 1997) to vector mesons in nuclear matter, providing a systematic framework where subleading terms can be computed to check convergence. The results are relevant for interpreting the low-mass lepton-pair enhancement observed by the CERES and HELIOS-3 experiments in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, which earlier models (Li, Ko, Brown 1995; Rapp, Chanfray, Wambach 1997) attributed to ρ meson modifications. The approach enables future self-consistent calculations that include the in-medium properties of baryon resonances, and the authors note that complementary experiments with photon and pion induced vector meson production off nuclei would further constrain the amplitudes.
