# Nuclear response beyond the Fermi gas model

> The Fermi gas model, while providing a reasonable qualitative description of the continuum nuclear response, does not include the effects of dynamical nucleon-nucleon correlations in the initial and final states, that have long been recognized to play a critical role in specific kinematical regions. We review a many-body approach in which these effects are consistently taken into account and discuss the results of a calculation of the quasielastic neutrino-oxygen cross section as an illustrative example.

## Key claim

The Fermi gas model fails to describe nuclear response in kinematical regions where scattering off nucleons in strongly correlated pairs dominates (e.g., large momentum transfer and low energy loss, corresponding to Bjorken scaling variable \(x_B \gg 1\)), and a many-body approach incorporating dynamical nucleon-nucleon correlations in both initial and final states is needed to reproduce measured cross sections.

## Method

The paper reviews a nonrelativistic nuclear many-body approach based on the impulse approximation (IA), in which the nuclear cross section is expressed as an incoherent sum over elementary lepton-nucleon scattering processes weighted by the nuclear spectral function \(P(\mathbf{p},E)\). The spectral function for nuclei with A > 4 is constructed using the Local Density Approximation (LDA), combining experimental single-nucleon knockout data for shell-model states with theoretical calculations of the correlation part from infinite nuclear matter at varying densities. Final state interactions (FSI) are treated within the eikonal and frozen approximations, leading to a folding function that redistributes the IA strength.

## Key numbers

* The LDA spectral function of \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) shows that shell-model states account for \(\sim 80\%\) of the strength, while the remaining \(\sim 20\%\) is located at high momentum and large removal energy.
* In the oxygen spectral function, \(\sim 50\%\) of the strength at momentum \(|\mathbf{p}| = 320\) MeV is located at removal energy \(E > 80\) MeV.
* The average removal energy for \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) calculated from the LDA spectral function is \(\langle E \rangle = 42\) MeV, compared to the Fermi gas binding energy \(\overline{\epsilon} = 25\) MeV.
* For a 1 GeV nucleon knocked out from oxygen, the finite-width component of the FSI folding function contributes only \(\sim 15\%\) of the normalization, yet produces a sizable redistribution of the cross section strength.
* The Fermi gas model parameters used for comparison are \(k_F = 220\) MeV and \(\overline{\epsilon} = 25\) MeV for the neutrino-oxygen cross section calculation.

## Context

This work extends the Fermi gas model of Moniz (1971) and its application to neutrino-nucleus scattering by Moniz (1973), which provided a qualitative description of the quasielastic peak but failed to account for data at large momentum transfer and low energy loss, as shown by Arrington (experimental data). The many-body approach reviewed here, based on the spectral function formalism of Benhar, Fabrocini, and Fantoni (1994) and the LDA construction of Benhar, Fabrocini, Fantoni, and Sick (1994), has been extensively applied to electron-nucleus scattering and enables consistent inclusion of FSI effects via the eikonal approximation of Benhar, Fabrocini, and Fantoni (1994). The paper identifies open questions regarding the applicability of the IA picture at lower neutrino energies (\(E_\nu \lesssim 700\) MeV) where Pauli blocking may become important, and notes that the approach can be generalized to include inelastic channels.
