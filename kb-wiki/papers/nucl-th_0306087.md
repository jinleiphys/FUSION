# Superdeformed bands in neutron-rich Sulfur isotopes suggested by cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations

## Key claim

The paper suggests that, in addition to the well-known candidate 32S, the neutron-rich nucleus 36S and the drip-line nuclei 48S and 50S are good candidates for finding superdeformed rotational bands in Sulfur isotopes, but this suggestion is conditional on the stability of the superdeformed minima against reflection-asymmetric deformations, which varies depending on the Skyrme interaction used (e.g., the superdeformed state in 48S is unstable for the SLy4 interaction).

## Method

The authors performed cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations in a three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate-mesh representation without imposing any symmetry restrictions, using the imaginary-time evolution technique. They also carried out symmetry-restricted calculations imposing reflection symmetries about the three coordinate planes for comparison. Deformation energy curves were obtained using constrained HF procedures with constraining operators, and the calculations were performed with three Skyrme interactions: SIII, SkM*, and SLy4.

## Key numbers

- Superdeformed minima appear at quadrupole deformation parameter beta2 approximately 0.6 for 32S, 36S, 48S, and 50S.
- The calculations used a mesh size of h = 1 fm inside a sphere with radius R = 10 fm, with a smaller mesh size of h = 1/3 fm for detailed density analysis.
- Rotational bands were calculated up to rotational frequencies of approximately 2.5 MeV/hbar for 32S and 36S, and 1.8 MeV/hbar for 50S.

## Context

This work extends the systematic theoretical search for superdeformed bands in the mass A=30-50 region previously conducted by Yamagami et al. (2000) and Inakura et al. (2002a) using the cranked SHF method. The study is motivated by recent experimental discoveries of superdeformed rotational bands in 36Ar, 40Ca, and 44Ti by Svensson et al. (2000, 2001a, 2001b), Ideguchi et al. (2001), and Chiara et al. (2003). The predicted superdeformed neutron skins in 48S and 50S enable future experimental searches for these exotic structures near the neutron drip line, and the calculated density distributions showing superdeformed neutron skins extend a similar theoretical result previously reported by Hamamoto (1995) for the very neutron-rich nucleus 208Dy.
