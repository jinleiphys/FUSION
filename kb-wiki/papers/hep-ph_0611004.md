# Study of exotic hadrons in S-wave scatterings induced by chiral interaction in the flavor symmetric limit

> We study s-wave bound states of a hadron and a light pseudoscalar meson induced by the Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) interaction in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit. The WT interaction is a driving force to generate quasibound states dynamically in the chiral unitary approaches. The strength and sign of the WT interaction are determined only by the group theoretical structure of the target hadrons, and we present a general expression of the strengths for the flavor SU(3) case. We show that, for the channels which are more exotic than the target, the interaction is repulsive in most cases, and the strength of the attractive interaction is universal for any possible target states. We demonstrate that the attractive coupling is not strong enough to generate an exotic state from the physically known masses of target hadrons. In addition, we also find a nontrivial Nc dependence of the coupling strengths. We show that the channels which are attractive at Nc=3 changes into repulsive ones for large Nc, and, therefore, no attractive interaction exists in exotic channels in the large-Nc limit.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction in exotic channels (those more exotic than the target hadron) is repulsive in most cases, and the only possible attractive strength is universal with value \(C_{\alpha,T}=1\), which is not strong enough to generate a bound state from physically known target hadron masses.

## Method

The authors study s-wave scattering of a light pseudoscalar meson (the Nambu-Goldstone boson octet) with a target hadron using the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the kernel in a chiral unitary approach. They work in the flavor SU(3) symmetric limit, where the coupled-channel equations reduce to independent single-channel problems, and derive the general expression for the coupling strength \(C_{\alpha,T}\) using group theory and Casimir invariants. Bound state formation is analyzed by examining the scattering amplitude pole structure in the complex energy plane with a regularization scheme that fixes the subtraction constant at the baryon mass scale.

## Key numbers

- The coupling strength for exotic channels that are more exotic than the target is universally \(C_{\alpha,T}=1\), the smallest possible attractive strength of the Weinberg-Tomozawa term.
- For the octet baryon target (\(T=\bm{8}\)), the exotic channel \(\bm{27}\) has coupling strength \(C_{\bm{27},\bm{8}}=-2\) (repulsive), while \(\overline{\bm{10}}\) and \(\bm{35}\) have \(C=0\) and \(C=-3\) respectively.
- For the decuplet baryon target (\(T=\bm{10}\)), the exotic channel \(\bm{27}\) has \(C_{\bm{27},\bm{10}}=1\) (attractive), while \(\bm{35}\) has \(C_{\bm{35},\bm{10}}=-3\).
- For heavy baryon target \(T=\bm{6}\), the exotic channel \(\overline{\bm{15}}\) has \(C_{\overline{\bm{15}},\bm{6}}=1\).
- For heavy meson target \(T=\bm{3}\), the exotic channel \(\overline{\bm{6}}\) has \(C_{\overline{\bm{6}},\bm{3}}=1\).
- The subtraction constant obtained from the regularization scheme with averaged octet baryon mass \(M_T=1151\) MeV gives \(a(630\text{ MeV}) \sim -1.98\), consistent with the value \(a(630\text{ MeV}) \simeq -2\) from threshold branching ratios.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to dynamically generate known resonances such as \(\Lambda(1405)\) (Jido et al., 2003), \(\Lambda(1520)\) (Kolomeitsev and Lutz, 2003; Sarkar et al., 2004), and charmed resonances like \(D_s(2317)\) (Kolomeitsev and Lutz, 2003) and \(\Lambda_c(2593)\) (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2003). The paper systematically analyzes the group theoretical structure of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction for arbitrary target representations, showing that exotic bound states cannot be generated from physically known masses. The results also demonstrate a nontrivial \(N_c\) dependence: channels attractive at \(N_c=3\) become repulsive for large \(N_c\), implying no attractive interaction exists in exotic channels in the large-\(N_c\) limit.
