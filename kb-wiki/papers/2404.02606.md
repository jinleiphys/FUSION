# Chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature in a model with manifest confinement

> Multiple lattice evidences support the existence of a confining but chirally symmetric regime of QCD above the chiral symmetry restoration crossover at Tch ~ 155 MeV. This regime is characterised by an approximate chiral spin symmetry of the partition function, which is a symmetry of the colour charge and the confining electric part of the QCD Lagrangian. It is traditionally believed that confinement should automatically induce spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry, which would preclude the existence of a confining but chirally symmetric regime of QCD at high temperatures. We employ a well-known solvable quark model for QCD in 3+1 dimensions that is chirally symmetric and manifestly confining and argue that while confinement indeed induces dynamical breaking of chiral symmetry at T=0, a chiral restoration phase transition takes place at some critical temperature Tch. Above this temperature, the spectrum of the model consists of chirally symmetric hadrons with approximate chiral spin symmetry.

## Key claim

Using a solvable chirally symmetric quark model with manifest confinement in 3+1 dimensions, the authors demonstrate that while confinement induces dynamical chiral symmetry breaking at T=0, a chiral restoration phase transition occurs at a critical temperature Tch, above which the spectrum consists of chirally symmetric hadrons with approximate chiral spin symmetry. The hedge is that this is shown in a simplified model, not in full QCD, and the critical temperature depends on the particular shape of the confining potential.

## Method

The authors employ a 3+1-dimensional confining chiral quark model (Eq. HGNJL) with an instantaneous confining kernel, derived from the QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge by omitting the magnetic part. They solve the thermal mass-gap equation (Eq. mgeT) for the chiral angle φp at finite temperature using the real-time formalism, where thermal Fermi-Dirac distribution functions for dressed quarks modify the self-energy integrals. The chiral condensate is computed as a function of temperature, and the critical temperature Tch is identified as the point where the condensate vanishes.

## Key numbers

* Chiral condensate at T=0: ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩₀ ≈ -0.0123 (√σ)³, where σ is the string tension.
* Critical temperature for chiral restoration: Tch ≈ 0.084 √σ.
* Model prediction relating condensate and critical temperature: |⟨ψ̄ψ⟩₀|^{1/3} ≈ 2.75 Tch.
* For a chiral condensate value of -(250 MeV)³, the model predicts Tch ≈ 90 MeV (compared to lattice chiral limit value around 130 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the line of research on the confining but chirally symmetric regime of QCD above the chiral restoration crossover at Tch ≈ 155 MeV, which has been supported by multiple lattice studies (Rohrhofer 2017, 2019; Chiu 2023) and is characterized by approximate chiral spin SU(2)CS and SU(4) symmetries. The paper directly addresses the traditional belief from Casher (1979) and the MIT bag model that confinement necessarily induces chiral symmetry breaking, showing this does not hold at finite temperature. The mechanism of chiral restoration via Pauli blocking of condensate-forming levels by thermal excitations is the same as in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Vogl 1991, Klevansky 1992, Hatsuda 1994). The work enables further studies of the "stringy fluid" regime between hadron gas and quark-gluon plasma, and connects to the large-Nc phase structure proposed by Cohen (2023).
