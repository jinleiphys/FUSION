# Tensor force effect on the neutron shell closure in super-heavy elements

> A systematic study of the effect of tensor force on the evolution of shell structure in even-even super-heavy nuclei in the region of proton numbers Z=114, 120 and 126 and in the region of neutron numbers 178 $\le$ N $\le$ 188 is presented. We use, in this investigation, the Hartree-Fock framework by means of different types of Skyrme functionals in two cases with and without tensor force. The Bardeen-Cooper-Schriefer (BCS) approximation has been used to treat the pairing correlations. By investigating structural and decay properties of nuclei under consideration, it is found that N=184 shell gap is more enhanced by the tensor interaction which depends on the isoscalar tensor coupling constant $C_0^J$ of the used Skyrme interactions. In the case without tensor interaction, this gap is significant only for T22, T24, T42 and SLy5. So, it disappears with T46, T64 and T66, and is too weak for T26, T44 and T62. Without exception, the shell gap at N=184 becomes more pronounced when the tensor part is taken into account.

## Key claim

The tensor force enhances the N=184 neutron shell gap in super-heavy nuclei, but this enhancement depends strongly on the isoscalar tensor coupling constant \(C_0^J\) of the Skyrme interaction used; without the tensor force, the shell gap is significant only for some parametrizations (T22, T24, T42, SLy5) and disappears or is too weak for others (T46, T64, T66, T26, T44, T62).

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (HF+BCS) calculations using ten different Skyrme parametrizations (SLy5+T, T22, T24, T26, T42, T44, T46, T62, T64, T66) on even-even super-heavy nuclei with proton numbers Z=114, 120, and 126 and neutron numbers 178 \(\le\) N \(\le\) 188. For each parametrization, calculations were run both with and without the tensor force term, and the EV8 code was used with a box size of 26 fm\(^3\), a step size of 1.0 fm, and 500 iterations.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron shell gap \(\delta_{2n}\) at N=184 increased by about 5.5 MeV for T46, T64, and T66 when tensor force was included, across Z=114, 120, and 126.
* Two-neutron shell gap \(\delta_{2n}\) at N=184 increased by about 3.5 MeV for T26, T44, and T62 when tensor force was included.
* Two-neutron shell gap \(\delta_{2n}\) at N=184 increased by about 2 MeV for T24 and T42 when tensor force was included.
* For SLy5, the value of \(\delta_{2n}\) at N=184 was reduced by approximately 1.5 MeV when tensor force was included.
* The discrepancy in root-mean-square radii between different Skyrme sets without tensor force was up to 0.04 fm for neutrons and 0.05 fm for protons; this discrepancy was almost hidden when the tensor part was included.
* The pairing cutoff parameters were set to \(\mu_k = 0.5\) MeV and \(\Delta\epsilon_q = 5\) MeV for both neutrons and protons.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shell structure in super-heavy nuclei using density functional theory, specifically following the investigations of Suckling and Zhou on tensor force effects in the Hartree-Fock framework. The authors compare their results with predictions from the macroscopic-microscopic FRDM model (Moller 2016), which they find fails to describe the N=184 shell closure in Z=126 nuclei due to lack of self-consistency. The study enables more reliable predictions of the island of stability around N=184 by demonstrating that tensor force inclusion is essential for consistent shell gap predictions across different Skyrme parametrizations.
