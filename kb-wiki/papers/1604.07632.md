# Saturation of Nuclear Matter and Roles of Many-Body Forces: nuclear matter in neutron stars probed by nucleus-nucleus scattering

> Yoichiro Nambu put a great foot print in nuclear physics in the era of its fundamental developments including his pioneering insight into essential ingredients of repulsive core of nuclear force and its relation to the saturation of nuclear matter. The present review article focuses onto recent developments of the interaction models between colliding nuclei in terms of Brueckner's G-matrix theory staring from realistic nuclear forces and the saturation property of symmetric nuclear matter as well as neutron-star matter. A recently proposed unique scenario of extracting the saturation property of nuclear matter and stiffness of neutron stars through the analysis of nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering in laboratories is presented in some detail.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy per nucleon ≈ -16 MeV at density ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³) cannot be reproduced by non-relativistic Brueckner G-matrix calculations using only two-body nucleon-nucleon interactions; the introduction of repulsive three-body and four-body forces is necessary, and their unknown strength can be determined precisely through analyses of nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering experiments using the double-folding model with complex G-matrix interactions.

## Method

The author reviews the double-folding model (DFM) in which the complex G-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, derived from Brueckner's theory with the Bethe-Goldstone equation and Pauli-blocking operator, is folded over the nucleon density distributions of projectile and target nuclei using the frozen-density approximation. The direct and exchange parts of the nucleus-nucleus potential are calculated self-consistently with a local-energy approximation, where the G-matrix depends on the local density (sum of projectile and target densities) and the incident energy per nucleon. The resulting complex optical potential is then compared with elastic scattering data to constrain the strength of many-body forces, which in turn determines the stiffness of nuclear matter and neutron-star matter at high densities.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³
- Saturation energy (volume term of binding energy per nucleon): Ē₀ ≈ -16 MeV
- Observed binding energy per nucleon in finite nuclei: Ē ≈ 8 ± 0.5 MeV
- Observed masses of neutron stars: (1.97 ± 0.04) M⊙ for J1614-2230 and (2.01 ± 0.04) M⊙ for J0348-0432
- Incompressibility K_SNM from experiments: range from about 200 MeV to 300 MeV
- Incompressibility K_SNM from G-matrix calculations with only two-body force: ≤ 100 MeV
- Range of nuclear force: about 1.5 to 2.0 fm
- Short-range repulsive core region: distances less than about 0.5 fm

## Context

This review extends the long-standing effort to understand nuclear matter saturation, which began with the Coester line problem (Coester 1970) showing that two-body-only G-matrix calculations fail to reproduce the empirical saturation point. The work connects the microscopic double-folding model (developed by Brieva and Rook (1977, 1978), and later by Khoa et al. (1994, 2001)) to neutron-star physics, specifically addressing the maximum-mass problem raised by observations of J1614-2230 and J0348-0432. The proposed scenario enables direct laboratory tests of the stiffness of high-density nuclear matter (ρ > 2ρ₀ to 5ρ₀) relevant to neutron-star interiors, complementing astrophysical observations and giant monopole resonance measurements.
