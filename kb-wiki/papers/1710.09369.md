# Compton Scattering of Gamma-Ray Vortex with Laguerre Gaussian Wave Function

> In this work, we report calculation for Compton scattering of a gamma-ray vortex with Laguerre Gaussian wave function on an electron in the framework of the relativistic quantum mechanics. We have found the following unexpected feature. The momentum of scattered photon distributes outside of the reaction plane determined by the incident photon and the scattered electron, and hence the energy of the scattered photon also distributes. This novel result indicates that one can identify a gamma-ray vortex by measuring coincidentally the scattered angles of the electron and photon.

# Compton Scattering of Gamma-Ray Vortex with Laguerre Gaussian Wave Function

## Key claim

Compton scattering of a gamma-ray vortex with Laguerre Gaussian wave function produces scattered photon momenta that distribute outside the reaction plane defined by the incident photon and scattered electron, and the scattered photon energy also distributes, a feature that is exactly zero at the standard Compton scattering kinematics where the amplitude of the LG wave function vanishes for nonzero orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors computed the relativistic quantum mechanical Compton scattering amplitude for an incident gamma-ray vortex described by a Laguerre Gaussian wave function scattering on an electron at rest, using the standard Feynman propagator formalism. They assumed the final photon is a plane wave, averaged over initial electron spin and summed over final electron spin and final photon polarization, and derived the differential cross-section in terms of the Fourier transform of the LG wave function. The cross-section was then evaluated for coincidence measurements of the scattered electron and photon at fixed electron scattering angle, with the incident photon energy set to 500 keV and a waist radius of 25 pm.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energy: k = 500 keV, where Compton scattering dominates.
- Waist radius: w0 = 25 pm, approximately ten times the photon wavelength (2.48 pm).
- For L=1, p=0 at cos θe = 0.95, the peak with ΔE=0 is located at φy/π = 0.015.
- The cross-section is exactly zero at φy = 0 and ΔE = 0 for L ≠ 0, where standard Compton scattering would have its strength.

## Context

This work extends the study of photon vortices carrying orbital angular momentum, originally developed for optical wavelengths by Allen (1992), into the MeV gamma-ray regime where Compton scattering is the dominant interaction with matter. It provides a theoretical basis for identifying gamma-ray vortices, which have been proposed for generation via laser Compton scattering by Jentschura (2011) and Taira (2017), through coincidence measurements of scattered electron and photon angles and energies, enabling future experimental identification of the orbital angular momentum L and radial node p of gamma-ray vortices.
