# Ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions: where the spectators flow?

> In high energy heavy ion collisions, the directed flow of particles is conventionally measured with respect to that of the projectile spectators, which is defined as positive $x$ direction. But it is not known if the spectators deflect in the "outward" direction or "inward" -- toward the center line of the collision. In this Letter we discuss how the measurements of the directed flow at mid-rapidity, especially in asymmetric collision such as Cu+Au, can be used to answer this question. We show that the existing data strongly favor the case that the spectators, in the ultrarelativistic collisions, on average deflect outwards.

# Ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions: where the spectators flow?

## Key claim
The authors argue that existing directed flow measurements at mid-rapidity in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC and Cu+Au collisions at RHIC strongly favor the case that spectator nucleons in ultra-relativistic collisions deflect outward from the collision center-line, but note that this conclusion depends on the assumption that the direction of the highest density gradient can be determined unambiguously from the initial nuclear geometry.

## Method
The authors use a Monte-Carlo model with Woods-Saxon density distributions and standard nuclear parameters to calculate participant eccentricities and the correlation between the spectator flow direction (Ψ_sp) and the third-order participant plane angle (Ψ_13) that determines directed flow at mid-rapidity. They compare these model calculations to existing experimental measurements of the rapidity-even component of directed flow in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (ALICE) and Cu+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (PHENIX and STAR), using the sign of the correlation to infer whether spectators deflect inward or outward.

## Key numbers
- In Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used in the model is 64 mb; for Cu+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the cross section is 42 mb.
- The model calculations for Pb+Pb collisions use an impact parameter range of 2 < b < 3 fm; Cu+Au calculations also use 2 < b < 3 fm.
- The experimental mean v_1 in Cu+Au collisions is about an order of magnitude larger than the values of the even v_1 component in Pb+Pb collisions.
- The odd v_1 component at LHC is only about 3 times smaller than that at top RHIC energies.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of spectator deflection from low-energy measurements by Lemmon (1999) and Krofcheck (1989) and Ogilvie (1990), which showed a change from inward to outward deflection around E/A ~ 100 MeV. It addresses a question raised by Alvioli (2010) about the dependence of spectator deflection on nucleon transverse momentum. The results enable more reliable interpretation of anisotropic flow measurements, particularly for determining the direction of the magnetic field created in the collision and the system orbital momentum needed for global polarization measurements (Liang 2004, Voloshin 2004, Abelev 2007).
