# Electroweak Currents from Chiral Effective Field Theory

> Since the pioneering work of Weinberg, Chiral Effective Field Theory ($χ$EFT) has been widely and successfully utilized in nuclear physics to study many-nucleon interactions and associated electroweak currents. Nuclear $χ$EFT has now developed into an intense field of research and is applied to study light to medium mass nuclei. In this contribution, we focus on the development of electroweak currents from $χ$EFT and present applications to selected nuclear electroweak observables.

## Key claim

Chiral Effective Field Theory (χEFT) provides a systematic, improvable framework for constructing electroweak currents in nuclei, and the inclusion of two-body currents derived from χEFT resolves the long-standing "gA problem" in beta decay matrix elements, though the authors note that the two main derivations (Pisa-JLab and Bochum-Bonn groups) disagree on certain loop contributions to the axial current operator, with the numerical impact of this difference being minor but still under investigation.

## Method

The authors review the chiral expansion of nuclear electroweak current and charge operators as sums of one-body and many-body contributions, with the leading-order single-nucleon operator corresponding to the Gamow-Teller operator and two-body corrections entering at N2LO and N3LO. The calculations employ Green's Function Monte Carlo methods and the No-core Shell Model to compute electromagnetic form factors, magnetic moments, and Gamow-Teller beta decay matrix elements in nuclei from A=3 to A=100, using χEFT currents with pions, nucleons, and delta excitations as degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron magnetic form factor calculations agree with experimental data for momentum transfers q ≤ 3 fm⁻¹
- Two-body electromagnetic currents provide a 40% correction to the measured ⁹C magnetic moment, resolving a long-standing under-prediction
- Gamow-Teller matrix elements calculated in A=6-10 nuclei using χEFT axial currents up to N3LO show excellent agreement with experimental data
- χEFT predictions for beta decay matrix elements agree with experimental data from A=3 to ¹⁰⁰Sn

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work of Weinberg (1990-1992) on χEFT and the early derivations of electroweak currents by Park, Min and Rho (1993-1998), as well as the more recent formulations by the Pisa-JLab group (Pastore et al., 2008-2012) and the Bochum-Bonn group (Kölling et al., 2009-2011). The work enables precision calculations of electromagnetic reactions and beta decays across light to medium-mass nuclei, resolving the "gA problem" that had persisted since Chou et al. (1993), and establishes χEFT as entering a precision era for nuclear theory.
