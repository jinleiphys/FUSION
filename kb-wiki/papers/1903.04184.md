# Localized rainbows in the QCD phase diagram

> We study the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter with light quarks using a recently proposed, small parameter approach to infrared QCD in the Landau gauge. This is based on an expansion with respect to both the inverse number of colors and the pure Yang-Mills coupling in the presence of a Curci-Ferrari mass term. At leading order, this leads to the well-known rainbow equation for the quark propagator with a massive gluon propagator. We solve the latter at nonzero temperature and chemical potential using a simple semi-analytic approximation known to capture the essence of chiral symmetry breaking in the vacuum. In the chiral limit, we find a tricritical point which becomes a critical endpoint in the presence of a nonzero bare quark mass, in agreement with the results of nonperturbative functional methods and model calculations. This supports the view that the present approach allows for a systematic study of the QCD phase diagram in a controlled expansion scheme.

## Key claim

The authors find that the Curci-Ferrari model, when treated with a double expansion in inverse number of colors and pure Yang-Mills coupling, produces a QCD phase diagram with a tricritical point in the chiral limit that becomes a critical endpoint for nonzero bare quark mass, but only under the condition that the gluon mass parameter falls within a specific range relative to the vacuum quark mass.

## Method

The authors solve the leading-order rainbow equation for the quark propagator at nonzero temperature and chemical potential, using a massive gluon propagator from the Curci-Ferrari model. They employ a semi-analytic "localization" approximation that reduces the full integral equation to a simpler gap equation, testing two variants (Euclidean and physical localization) to check robustness. The bare quark-gluon coupling is traded for the vacuum dynamical quark mass B0, and the phase boundaries are determined from the resulting effective potential.

## Key numbers

- Tricritical point located at (μ, T) ≈ (237 MeV, 69 MeV) for B0 = 300 MeV and m = 500 MeV in the chiral limit
- Critical temperature at zero chemical potential: Tc(μ=0) ≈ 141 MeV
- Lower spinodal meets T=0 axis at μ ≈ 268 MeV; upper spinodal at μ ≈ 305 MeV
- First-order transition at T=0 occurs at μ ≈ 287 MeV (for the choice f+=1)
- Tricritical point exists only for gluon mass ratio x = m/B0 in the range [0.294, 3.398], corresponding to y = μ/B0 in [0.340, 1.157]
- Approach to tricriticality follows mean field exponents: ωT = ωμ = 2/5 and ωB = 1/5

## Context

This work extends the Curci-Ferrari model approach, previously successful for pure Yang-Mills and heavy quark QCD (Reinosa 2014, Reinosa 2015, Maelger 2017), to light quarks at finite temperature and chemical potential. The results agree qualitatively with nonperturbative functional methods (Fischer 2012, Fischer 2013) and NJL-type model calculations (Jakovac 2003, Schaefer 2007, Fukushima 2008), but with the advantage of a controlled expansion scheme. The approach enables systematic inclusion of corrections beyond the rainbow approximation and could be extended to study the interplay between chiral and center symmetry.
