# Measurement of global spin alignment of $K^{*0}$and $ϕ$ vector mesons using the STAR detector at RHIC

> We report the transverse momentum ($p_{\mathrm{T}}$) and centrality dependence of global spin alignment ($ρ_{00}$) of $K^{*0}$ vector meson at midrapidity ($|y|<0.5$) in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 54.4 and 200~GeV with the STAR experiment at RHIC. The $K^{*0}$ results are compared to that of $ϕ$ meson. At low-$p_{\mathrm{T}}$ region and midcentral collisions, the $K^{*0}$ $ρ_{00}$ is found to be smaller than 1/3 with about 4$σ$ significance, while that of $ϕ$ meson is observed to be larger than 1/3 with about 3$σ$ significance. The $ρ_{00}$ results are compared between RHIC and LHC energies. The physics implication of our results is also discussed.

# Measurement of global spin alignment of \(K^{*0}\) and \(\phi\) vector mesons using the STAR detector at RHIC

## Key claim

The paper reports that at low transverse momentum (\(p_{\mathrm{T}} < 2.0\) GeV/c) and in midcentral Au+Au collisions, the spin density matrix element \(\rho_{00}\) for \(K^{*0}\) mesons is smaller than 1/3 with about \(4\sigma\) significance, while for \(\phi\) mesons it is larger than 1/3 with about \(3\sigma\) significance; however, no current theoretical model can simultaneously explain the opposite trends observed for the two vector meson species.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured \(\rho_{00}\) for \(K^{*0}\) mesons at midrapidity (\(|y| < 0.5\)) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 54.4\) and 200 GeV, using the \(2^{\text{nd}}\)-order event plane as the polarization axis. The \(K^{*0}\) mesons were reconstructed via the hadronic decay channel \(K^{*0} \rightarrow K^{+}\pi^{-}\) (branching ratio 66%), with combinatorial background subtracted using a pair rotation technique; the observed \(\rho_{00}\) was extracted by fitting the yield versus \(\cos\theta^{*}\) distribution and then corrected for event plane resolution.

## Key numbers

- \(K^{*0}\) \(\rho_{00}\) at low \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) (\(< 2.0\) GeV/c) and midcentral (10–60%) Au+Au collisions is smaller than 1/3 with about \(4\sigma\) significance at both \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 54.4\) and 200 GeV.
- \(\phi\) meson \(\rho_{00}\) in midcentral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV for \(p_{\mathrm{T}} = 1.0\)–2.0 GeV/c is larger than 1/3 with about \(3\sigma\) significance.
- \(\phi\) meson \(\rho_{00}\) at LHC energies (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV) is smaller than 1/3 with about \(2\sigma\) significance, opposite to the trend at RHIC energies.
- For \(K^{*0}\) \(\rho_{00}\) in the range \(1.0 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 1.5\) GeV/c, a clear centrality dependence is observed with maximum deviation from 1/3 in midcentral collisions; peripheral and most central collisions yield \(\rho_{00}\) consistent with 1/3.

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of \(\Lambda\) hyperon polarization (STAR Collaboration, 2017) and earlier vector meson spin alignment results at lower RHIC energies (Zhou, 2018) and at LHC energies (ALICE Collaboration). The opposite behavior of \(K^{*0}\) and \(\phi\) meson \(\rho_{00}\), one smaller and one larger than 1/3 at RHIC, challenges existing models based on vorticity, magnetic fields, and hadronization scenarios such as quark coalescence and fragmentation (Liang and Wang, 2005). The results motivate further theoretical work to understand the role of mesonic fields and the different lifetimes of these vector mesons in probing the QGP medium at different time scales.
