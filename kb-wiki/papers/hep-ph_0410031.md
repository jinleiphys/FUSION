# The pion electromagnetic form-factor in a QCD-inspired model

> We present detailed numerical results for the pion space-like electromagnetic form factor obtained within a recently proposed model of the pion electromagnetic current in a confining light-front QCD-inspired model. The model incorporates the vector meson dominance mechanism at the quark level, where the dressed photon with $q^+>0$ decay in an interacting quark-antiquark pair,wich absorbs the initial pion and produces the pion in the final state.

## Key claim

The model reproduces the experimental pion space-like electromagnetic form factor data satisfactorily for \(-q^2 < 10\ (GeV/c)^2\), but the agreement with time-like data is only qualitative, not quantitative.

## Method

The pion electromagnetic current matrix elements were calculated using the Mandelstam formula within light-front dynamics, with the quark-photon vertex constructed via vector meson dominance (VMD) as a sum over vector meson vertex functions weighted by decay constants. The momentum dependence of the pion and vector meson vertex functions came from eigenstates of a QCD-inspired mass operator, and the integration over \(k^-\) was performed in a frame with \(q^+ \neq 0\) and \(\vec q_{\perp} = \vec 0_{\perp}\), assuming a massless pion.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass \(m = 0.265\ GeV\) and oscillator strength \(\omega = 1.39\ GeV^2\), constrained by the meson spectrum.
- A single vector meson width of \(0.15\ GeV\) was used for all vector mesons with mass \(M_n \ge 2.150\ GeV\); experimental widths were used for lower-mass mesons.
- The model was compared to experimental data in the space-like region for \(-q^2 < 10\ (GeV/c)^2\) and in the time-like region up to \(10\ (GeV/c)^2\).
- The ratio \(R_\pi(q^2) = F_\pi(q^2) / (1 - q^2/m_\rho^2)\) was computed and shown to tend asymptotically to a constant, consistent with QCD expectations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (de Melo et al., 2004) of pion form factors in both space-like and time-like regimes, using a QCD-inspired model from Pauli (2001) that describes meson spectra in light- and heavy-quark sectors. The model incorporates vector meson dominance at the quark level, following the light-front dynamics framework of Dirac (1949) and Brodsky (1998). The next step is to improve the time-like description by introducing isospin mixing and possible coupling of the photon to \(^3D_1\) mesons.
