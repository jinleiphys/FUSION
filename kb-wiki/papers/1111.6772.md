# Schwinger mechanism enhanced by the Nielsen--Olesen instability

> We discuss gluon production by the Schwinger mechanism in collinear color-electric and magnetic fields which may be realized in pre-equilibrium stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Fluctuations of non-Abelian gauge fields around a purely color-magnetic field contain exponentially growing unstable modes in a longitudinally soft momentum region, which is known as the Nielsen--Olesen instability. With a color-electric field imposed parallelly to the color-magnetic field, we can formulate this instability as the Schwinger mechanism. This is because soft unstable modes are accelerated by the electric fields to escape from the instability condition. Effects of instability remain in the transverse spectrum of particle modes, leading to an anomalously intense Schwinger particle production.

## Key claim

The Schwinger mechanism for gluon production in collinear color-electric and color-magnetic fields is anomalously enhanced because the Nielsen-Olesen instability, which normally prevents canonical quantization in a pure magnetic field, is regularized by the electric field: particles accelerated by the electric field escape the unstable low-momentum region, allowing the amplified fluctuations to be interpreted as real particle production.

## Method

The authors consider SU(N) pure Yang-Mills theory with a covariantly constant background field that yields collinear color-electric and color-magnetic fields. They perform a Cartan decomposition of the gauge group, expand the quantum fluctuations around the background, and solve the linearized equations of motion for the off-diagonal gluon and ghost modes. Using the asymptotic WKB criterion to define in- and out-solutions, they compute Bogoliubov transformations and the momentum distribution of produced gluons.

## Key numbers

- The pair creation rate per unit volume per unit time is \( w = \frac{N}{2} \frac{g^2 EB}{(2\pi)^2} \sum_{\sigma=\pm} \sum_{n=0}^\infty \ln\left[ 1 + e^{-\pi (2n+1-2\sigma) \frac{B}{E}} \right] \).
- For the lowest Landau level (\(n=0\), \(\sigma=+\)), the in-vacuum expectation of the out-number operator is \( \langle 0,\text{in}| a_{p_z,n=0}^{(+)\alpha\,\text{out}\dagger} a_{p_z,n=0}^{(+)\alpha\,\text{out}} |0,\text{in}\rangle = e^{+\pi \frac{B}{E}} \frac{V}{(2\pi)^3} \), showing exponential enhancement.
- The instability growth rate is \( \sqrt{eB} \), and the time scale for stabilization by the electric field is \( t_{\text{ele}} \sim \sqrt{eB}/eE \).
- For the pure electric field case, the momentum distribution of physical modes is \( \exp\left( -\frac{\pi p_\perp^2}{e_\alpha E} \right) \frac{V}{(2\pi)^3} \).

## Context

This work extends the canonical quantization approach to the Schwinger mechanism in non-Abelian gauge fields, previously studied for pure electric fields by Ambjorn and Hughes (1982) and for pure magnetic fields by Nielsen and Olesen (1978). It bridges these two limiting cases by showing that the Nielsen-Olesen instability in a constant magnetic field can be regularized by a parallel electric field, enabling particle interpretation. The results are directly relevant to the glasma flux-tube configuration expected in the pre-equilibrium stage of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as described by Lappi (2006), and suggest that the anomalously enhanced gluon production leads to rapid decay of coherent fields into particles, potentially followed by quark-gluon plasma formation.
