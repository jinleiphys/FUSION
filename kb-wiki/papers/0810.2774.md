# Two-pion-exchange and other higher-order contributions to the $pp\to ppπ^0$ reaction

> Much effort has been invested on effective-field-theoretical studies of the near-threshold NN \to NNπreactions and, in order to deal with the rather large three-momentum transfers involved, the momentum counting scheme was proposed as an alternative to the usual Weinberg counting scheme. To examine the practical utility of the momentum counting scheme, we study here the contributions of higher-order terms to the pp\to ppπ^0 reaction. We discuss the consequences of the different treatments of the nucleon propagators in these two counting schemes. We also investigate certain higher chiral-order pion-rescattering terms and the five-point counter-term, which turn out to give important contributions.

## Key claim

The momentum counting scheme (MCS) is superior to the Weinberg scheme for identifying dominant higher-order contributions to the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction, but the individual terms in the MCS expansion exhibit more complicated behavior than straightforward power counting in $\tilde{\epsilon}$ would indicate, and the practical utility of MCS remains to be fully established.

## Method

The authors calculated the transition amplitudes for the $pp\to pp\pi^0$ reaction at threshold using plane-wave approximation and frozen kinematics approximation (FKA), comparing results obtained with the Weinberg-scheme nucleon propagator and the MCS nucleon propagator. They evaluated the contributions from two-pion-exchange diagrams (I through IV), vertex-correction diagrams (V and VI), the recoil-correction diagram (VII), and the counter-term diagram, decomposing the two-pion-exchange amplitudes into leading terms of order $\tilde{\epsilon}^2$ and sub-leading terms of order $\tilde{\epsilon}^3$ to test the convergence of MCS.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter in MCS is $\tilde{\epsilon} \equiv p/m_N \simeq (m_\pi/m_N)^{1/2} \simeq 1/2.6$, compared to $\epsilon \simeq m_\pi/m_N \simeq 1/6.7$ in the Weinberg scheme.
- For $T_{lab} = 281$ MeV, the sub-leading two-pion-exchange amplitudes $J[t^\star;{\rm MCS}] \times 10^2$ are: diagram I = -1.4, diagram II = -13.2, diagram III = -23.1, diagram IV = 12.5, with sum = -25.2; the rescattering amplitude $J[t]_{\rm Resc} \times 10^2 = 8$.
- The leading $t_1$ term amplitudes $J[t_1] \times 10^2$ are: diagram I = 0, diagram II = -46.7, diagram III = -23.3, diagram IV = 70.0, with sum = 0.
- For diagrams V, VI, and VII at $T_{lab}=281$ MeV, $J[t;{\rm MCS}] \times 10^2$ values are: V = 3.4, VI = -2.3, VII = 20.8, compared to $J[t]_{\rm Resc} \times 10^2 = 8$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study KSMK1 (Kim et al., 2007) by using the proper MCS nucleon propagator instead of the standard heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory propagator, and by examining higher chiral-order pion-rescattering terms and the five-point counter-term. The results are relevant to the program initiated by Cohen, Friar, Miller and van Kolck (1996) who proposed MCS as an alternative to the Weinberg counting scheme for $NN\to NN\pi$ reactions, and to the formal discussions of MCS by Hanhart (2004). The study enables further investigation of whether MCS can serve as a valid effective-field-theory expansion scheme for nuclear processes involving large momentum transfers, beyond the specific context of pion production.
