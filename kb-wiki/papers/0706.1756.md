# IBM-1 description of the fission products $^{108,110,112}$Ru

> IBM-1} calculations for the fission products $^{108,110,112}$Ru have been carried out. The even-even isotopes of Ru can be described as transitional nuclei situated between the U(5) (spherical vibrator) and SO(6) ($γ$-unstable rotor) symmetries of the Interacting Boson Model. At first, a Hamiltonian with only one- and two-body terms has been used. Excitation energies and $B$(E2) ratios of gamma transitions have been calculated. A satisfactory agreement has been obtained, with the exception of the odd-even staggering in the quasi-$γ$ bands of $^{110,112}$Ru. The observed pattern is rather similar to the one for a rigid triaxial rotor. A calculation based on a Hamiltonian with three-body terms was able to remove this discrepancy. The relation between the IBM and the triaxial rotor model was also examined.

## Key claim

The even-even Ru isotopes $^{108,110,112}$Ru can be described as transitional nuclei between the U(5) (spherical vibrator) and SO(6) (gamma-unstable rotor) symmetries of the Interacting Boson Model, but the standard IBM-1 Hamiltonian with only one- and two-body terms fails to reproduce the odd-even staggering in the quasi-gamma bands of $^{110,112}$Ru; this discrepancy is removed only by adding a three-body term to the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors performed IBM-1 calculations for $^{108,110,112}$Ru using both a standard Hamiltonian with one- and two-body terms (diagonalized with the program PHINT) and an extended Hamiltonian that includes a cubic three-body term with J=3 (diagonalized with the code ibm1). Parameters were fitted to experimental excitation energies and E2 branching ratios from the recent work of Zhu et al. (2006), and the relation between the IBM and the Rigid Triaxial Rotor Model was examined via effective gamma values derived from quadrupole invariants.

## Key numbers

- For $^{108}$Ru, the standard IBM-1 fit used boson number N=10 with parameters: epsilon=0.804 MeV, chi=-0.128, kappa=-0.0485 MeV, lambda=0.014 MeV, beta=-0.106 MeV.
- For $^{110}$Ru, the standard fit used N=11 with epsilon=0.713 MeV, chi=-0.077, kappa=-0.0466 MeV, lambda=0.015 MeV, beta=-0.091 MeV.
- For $^{112}$Ru, the standard fit used N=10 with epsilon=0.509 MeV, chi=-0.060, kappa=-0.0439 MeV, lambda=0.016 MeV, beta=-0.089 MeV.
- The extended IBM-1 fit for $^{110}$Ru used a cubic term strength v3=-0.030 MeV, yielding rms deviation for the ground-state band sigma_g=0.030 MeV and for the quasi-gamma band sigma_gamma=0.034 MeV, compared to sigma_g=0.086 MeV and sigma_gamma=0.098 MeV with the standard Hamiltonian.
- For $^{112}$Ru, the extended fit used v3=-0.035 MeV, yielding sigma_g=0.025 MeV and sigma_gamma=0.030 MeV, compared to sigma_g=0.095 MeV and sigma_gamma=0.102 MeV with the standard Hamiltonian.
- Experimental E2 branching ratio for $^{110}$Ru: B(E2;2_2+ to 2_1+)/B(E2;2_2+ to 0_1+) = 14.9(2), compared to 12.1 (standard IBM) and 9.3 (extended IBM).
- Experimental E2 branching ratio for $^{112}$Ru: B(E2;2_2+ to 2_1+)/B(E2;2_2+ to 0_1+) = 22.2(3), compared to 10.6 (standard IBM) and 16.7 (extended IBM).
- The 0_2+ state in $^{108}$Ru is observed at 975 keV; the standard IBM-1 predicts it at 896 keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier IBM-1 description of Ru isotopes by Stachel et al. (1982), who used a schematic Hamiltonian for the U(5) to SO(6) transition, and the more recent study by Frank et al. (2001) on phase transitions in these nuclei. The paper also builds on experimental data from Zhu et al. (2006) and earlier work by the Gammasphere collaboration. The successful inclusion of a three-body term to reproduce triaxial-like staggering in the quasi-gamma bands of $^{110,112}$Ru establishes a connection between the IBM and the Rigid Triaxial Rotor Model, and enables future systematic studies of three-body interactions in other mass regions where similar staggering patterns are observed, such as the Xe and Ba isotopes around A=130 studied by Casten et al. (1985).
