# Effective field theory of boson-fermion mixtures and bound fermion states on a vortex of boson superfluid

> We construct a Galilean invariant low-energy effective field theory of boson-fermion mixtures and study bound fermion states on a vortex of boson superfluid. We derive a simple criterion to determine for which values of the fermion angular momentum l there exist an infinite number of bound energy levels. We apply our formalism to two boson-fermion mixed systems: the dilute solution of He3 in He4 superfluid and the cold polarized Fermi gas on the BEC side of the "splitting point". For the He3-He4 mixture, we determine parameters of the effective theory from experimental data as functions of pressure. We predict that infinitely many bound He3 states on a superfluid vortex with l=-2,-1,0 are realized in a whole range of pressure, 0\sim20 atm, where experimental data are available. As for the cold polarized Fermi gas, while only S-wave (l=0) and P-wave (l=\pm1) bound fermion states are possible in the BEC limit, those with higher negative angular momentum become available as one moves away from the BEC limit.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a Galilean-invariant low-energy effective field theory for boson-fermion mixtures and derives a criterion for the existence of infinitely many bound fermion states on a vortex of a boson superfluid, but only when the fermion dispersion minimum sits at zero momentum (opposite to the BCS regime) and the binding energy is small enough that the effective theory is valid far from the vortex core.

## Method

The authors build an effective Lagrangian for a boson superfluid (phonon field) coupled to dilute fermions, enforcing Galilean invariance via the identity \(T_{0i}=mJ_i\). They derive the radial Schrödinger equation for a fermion in the vortex background, solve it using modified Bessel functions in the region far from the vortex core (\(r \geq r_0\)), and obtain a quantization condition for bound energy levels. The free parameters (effective fermion mass \(m_3^*\) and the derivative of the fermion energy gap with respect to chemical potential \(\partial\Delta/\partial\mu_4\)) are determined from experimental data for the \(^3\)He-\(^4\)He mixture and from microscopic theory for the cold Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

* For the \(^3\)He-\(^4\)He mixture at \(P=0\) atm: \(m_3^*/m_3 = 2.18\), \(\alpha_0 = 0.288\), giving \(l_- = -2.34\) and \(l_+ = 0.566\); bound states exist for integer \(l\) satisfying \(l_- < l < l_+\), i.e., \(l = 0, -1, -2\).
* Asymptotic ratio of bound energy levels for \(l=0\) at \(P=0\) atm: \(E_\infty/E_{\infty-1} = 4.24 \times 10^{-3}\); at \(P=20\) atm: \(7.42 \times 10^{-4}\).
* For the cold polarized Fermi gas in the BEC limit, the coupling ratio \(\gamma = 1.47\) yields bound \(S\)-wave (\(l=0\)) energy levels with asymptotic ratio \(\epsilon_n/\epsilon_{n-1} = 5.65 \times 10^{-3}\) for \(n \to \infty\).
* The criterion for infinite bound states is \(\kappa^2 > 0\) where \(\kappa^2 = g_3 (m_3^*/m_4) \lv^2 - 2g_1 (m_3^*/m_4) \lv l - l^2\); for the \(^3\)He-\(^4\)He mixture with \(\lv=1\), this holds for \(l = 0, -1, -2\) across the pressure range \(0\)–\(20\) atm.

## Context

This work extends the Caroli–de Gennes–Matricon formalism (Caroli 1964) for bound states on vortices, which works well in the BCS regime but not in the BEC regime or for \(^3\)He-\(^4\)He mixtures where the fermion dispersion minimum is at zero momentum. The effective field theory approach is universal and can be applied to any dilute fermion excitation in a boson superfluid, including the \(^3\)He-\(^4\)He mixture studied experimentally by Hatakeyama (2003) and Boghosian (1967), and the cold polarized Fermi gas on the BEC side of the splitting point studied by Son (2005). The results enable experimental searches for bound fermion states on vortices in both systems, with measurable asymptotic energy ratios.
