# The decay of the omega meson at finite temperature

> The decay width of the $ω$ meson at finite temperature is calculated using the Gell-Mann Sharp Wagner model of $ρ$ pole dominance. Effective masses of the $ρ$ and $ω$ are determined within the framework of real-time formalism of finite temperature field theory. It is shown that even though the mass of the $ω$-meson decreases with temperature, its decay width increases because of an interesting interplay between the phase space factors, the transition matrix element and Bose enhancement.

## Key claim

The decay width of the ω meson increases with temperature even though its mass decreases, because the downward shift of the ρ meson pole increases the transition matrix element and Bose enhancement from the hot pion gas further amplifies the width, contrary to the assumption in Li, Ko and Brown (1996) that the ω decay width is proportional to its mass.

## Method

The authors calculate the ω → 3π decay width using the Gell-Mann Sharp Wagner model of ρ pole dominance, with effective thermal masses of the ρ and ω mesons determined from the poles of their dressed propagators at finite temperature. The thermal masses are generated by nucleon-antinucleon loop contributions to the vector meson self-energies, computed in the real-time formalism of finite temperature field theory, using an effective nucleon mass that decreases with temperature due to the σ meson mean field. The decay width integral includes a Bose enhancement factor accounting for stimulated emission of pions in the thermal bath.

## Key numbers

- The ω meson mass decreases with temperature, while its decay width increases; the specific numerical values are presented in Figures 1 and 2 (exact numbers not given in the text).
- The integration limits for the decay width are given: the maximum energy of a single pion is \(E_{+ (max)} = \frac{m_\omega^{\ast 2} - 3m_\pi^2}{2m_\omega^\ast}\) and the minimum is \(E_{+ (min)} = m_\pi\).
- The coupling constant relation used is \(g^2_{\omega\rho\pi} = \frac{9g^2_{\rho\pi\pi}}{8\pi^2 f_\pi^2}\), with \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\) determined by fitting the ρ → ππ decay width.

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature treatment of vector mesons by Song (1995) and Gale and Kapusta (1991), who studied ρ and ω masses using different formalisms, and directly contradicts the assumption in Li, Ko and Brown (1996) that the ω decay width scales proportionally to its mass. The results have implications for interpreting dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions at SPS, CERN, as the increased ω width at finite temperature could affect the observed low-mass dilepton yield.
