# Weinberg operator contribution to the CP-odd nuclear force in the quark model

> The contribution of the CP violating three-gluon interaction, proposed by Weinberg, to the short-range CP-odd nuclear force is evaluated in the nonrelativistic quark model. We first show that the naive leading contribution generated by the quark exchange process vanishes at sufficiently short distance within the resonating group method, by considering the one-loop level gluon exchange CP-odd interquark potential induced by the Weinberg operator with massive quarks and gluons. We then estimate the true leading contribution by evaluating the gluonic correction to the CP-odd interquark potential in the closure approximation. It is found that the resulting irreducible CP-odd nuclear force is comparable to that generated by the chiral rotation of the CP-even short-range nuclear force, where the CP-odd mass calculated with QCD sum rules is used as input. The explicit calculation of the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the $^3$He nucleus yields $d_{^3{\rm He}}^{\rm (irr)} (w) = - 1.5 \, w\, e $ MeV. The total $^3$He EDM, accounting for the intrinsic nucleon EDM, the pion-exchange and the short-range CP-odd nuclear force, is $d_{^3{\rm He}}^{\rm (tot)} (w) = 20_{-11}^{+14} \, w\, e $ MeV, with the dominant effect coming from the intrinsic nucleon EDM.

## Key claim

The naive leading contribution to the short-range CP-odd nuclear force from the Weinberg operator, generated by quark exchange processes, vanishes at sufficiently short distance within the resonating group method; the true leading contribution comes from gluonic corrections to the CP-odd interquark potential evaluated in the closure approximation, and is comparable to the contribution from chiral rotation of the CP-even short-range nuclear force.

## Method

The authors used the nonrelativistic quark model with the resonating group method (RGM) to derive the CP-odd nuclear force induced by the Weinberg operator. They first constructed the one-loop level CP-odd interquark potential with massive quarks and gluons, then showed that the quark exchange contribution vanishes by analyzing the color-spin matrix elements. The leading contribution was estimated by evaluating the gluonic correction to the CP-odd interquark potential in the closure approximation, using the box diagram as the representative process. The resulting irreducible CP-odd nuclear force was then applied to calculate the electric dipole moment (EDM) of the ³He nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The irreducible contribution to the ³He EDM is \(d_{^3{\rm He}}^{\rm (irr)} (w) = -1.5 \, w\, e\) MeV.
- The total ³He EDM, including intrinsic nucleon EDM, pion-exchange, and short-range CP-odd nuclear force, is \(d_{^3{\rm He}}^{\rm (tot)} (w) = 20_{-11}^{+14} \, w\, e\) MeV.
- The reducible short-range CP-odd nuclear force contribution to the ³He EDM is \(d_{^3{\rm He}}^{\rm (red)} (w) = 2_{-1}^{+8} w \, e\) MeV.
- The valence nucleon EDM contribution to the ³He EDM is \(d_{^3{\rm He}}^{\rm (val)} (w) = (19\pm 11) \, w \, e\) MeV.
- The CP-odd nucleon mass used in the chiral rotation calculation is \(m_{CP} = -m_N w \frac{3 g_s m_0^2}{32 \pi^2} \ln \left( \frac{M^2}{\mu_{\rm IR}^2} \right)\), with \(m_0^2 = (0.8 \pm 0.2)\) GeV² and \(\frac{M}{\mu_{\rm IR}} = \sqrt{2} (1.5 \pm 0.5)\).
- The gluon mass used is \(m_g \approx 350\) MeV, and the strong coupling is \(\alpha_s = 0.5\).
- The harmonic oscillator parameter for the quark model is \(b = 0.5\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the evaluation of the Weinberg operator contribution to hadronic CP violation beyond the nucleon EDM calculations of Yamanaka (2020) and the QCD sum rules analyses of Demir (2002) and Haisch (2019). It addresses the previously unevaluated irreducible CP-odd nuclear force, which introduces systematic uncertainty in nuclear and atomic EDM analyses. The results enable more reliable predictions for the ³He EDM, which is targeted by storage ring experiments with projected sensitivity of \(O(10^{-29}) e\) cm, and complement the chiral effective field theory framework where the short-range CP-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction is described by a contact term.
