# Single-inclusive jet production in electron-nucleon collisions through next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD

> We compute the ${\cal O}(α^2α_s^2)$ perturbative corrections to inclusive jet production in electron-nucleon collisions. This process is of particular interest to the physics program of a future Electron Ion Collider (EIC). We include all relevant partonic processes, including deep-inelastic scattering contributions, photon-initiated corrections, and parton-parton scattering terms that first appear at this order. Upon integration over the final-state hadronic phase space we validate our results for the deep-inelastic corrections against the known next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) structure functions. Our calculation uses the $N$-jettiness subtraction scheme for performing higher-order computations, and allows for a completely differential description of the deep-inelastic scattering process. We describe the application of this method to inclusive jet production in detail, and present phenomenological results for the proposed EIC. The NNLO corrections have a non-trivial dependence on the jet kinematics and arise from an intricate interplay between all contributing partonic channels.

## Key claim

The authors compute the full ${\cal O}(\alpha^2\alpha_s^2)$ perturbative corrections to single-inclusive jet production in electron-nucleon collisions, including all relevant partonic channels, but note that the NNLO corrections have a non-trivial dependence on jet kinematics and arise from an intricate interplay between all contributing partonic channels, meaning the corrections are not simply a uniform scaling of lower-order predictions.

## Method

The calculation uses the $N$-jettiness subtraction scheme to handle infrared divergences in the quark-lepton and gluon-lepton scattering channels, partitioning phase space into regions above and below a cutoff on the $\tau_1$ event shape variable. The below-cut region is obtained from an all-orders resummation formula expanded to ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$, while the above-cut region is computed from an NLO calculation of two-jet production using antennae subtraction. New partonic channels first appearing at NNLO, such as $q\bar{q} \to q\bar{q}$ and $qg \to qg$, are included via a quark-in-lepton distribution function derived from solving the DGLAP equation.

## Key numbers

- The NNLO corrections are ${\cal O}(\alpha^2\alpha_s^2)$ in the perturbative expansion, where $\alpha$ is the electromagnetic coupling and $\alpha_s$ is the strong coupling constant.
- The below-cut cross section contains terms of the form $\ln^n(\tau_1^{\text{cut}})$ where $n$ ranges from 0 to 4 at NNLO.
- The photon distribution function is $f_{\gamma/l}(\xi) = \frac{\alpha}{2\pi} P_{\gamma l}(\xi) \left[ \ln\left(\frac{\mu^2}{\xi^2 m_l^2} \right)-1\right] + {\cal O}(\alpha^2)$ with splitting function $P_{\gamma l}(\xi) = \frac{1+(1-\xi)^2}{\xi}$.
- The quark-in-lepton distribution is proportional to $(\alpha/2\pi)^2$ and contains terms up to $\log^2(\mu^2/m_l^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the NLO ${\cal O}(\alpha^2\alpha_s)$ calculation of Hinderer et al. (2015) for unpolarized $lN \to jX$ scattering to NNLO precision, and applies the $N$-jettiness subtraction scheme previously developed for hadronic collisions by Boughezal et al. (2015) and Gaunt et al. (2015) to electron-nucleon collisions. The results enable precision phenomenology for the proposed Electron Ion Collider (EIC), where NNLO predictions will be needed to match expected experimental precision, and follow recent NNLO calculations for similar processes such as massive charm-quark production in DIS by Berger et al. (2016) and dijet production by Currie et al. (2016).
