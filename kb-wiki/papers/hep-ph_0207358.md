# Baryon Charge Radii and Quadrupole Moments in the 1/N_c Expansion: The 3-Flavor Case

> We develop a straightforward method to compute charge radii and quadrupole moments for baryons both with and without strangeness, when the number of QCD color charges is N_c. The minimal assumption of the single-photon exchange ansatz implies that only two operators are required to describe these baryon observables. Our results are presented so that SU(3) flavor and isospin symmetry breaking can be introduced according to any desired specification, although we also present results obtained from two patterns suggested by the quark model with gluon exchange interactions. The method also permits to extract a number of model-independent relations; a sample is r^2_Lambda / r_n^2 = 3/(N_c+3), independent of SU(3) symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to compute baryon charge radii and quadrupole moments for arbitrary numbers of QCD color charges \(N_c\) in the 3-flavor case, under the minimal assumption of single-photon exchange. The authors claim that only two operators (one at the 2-body level and one at the 3-body level) are required to describe these observables, and that results can be obtained for arbitrary SU(3) flavor and isospin symmetry breaking, though the specific numerical examples use patterns suggested by the quark model with gluon exchange interactions.

## Method

The authors construct baryon states with strangeness using SU(2) \(U\)- and \(V\)-spin subgroups of SU(3), avoiding the need for SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. They compute 12 fundamental matrix elements of spin operators \(\langle \alpha \beta \rangle^{(0)}\) and \(\langle \alpha \beta \rangle^{(2)}\) for all flavor pairs \((u,u)\), \((u,d)\), \((u,s)\), \((d,d)\), \((d,s)\), and \((s,s)\) using the Wigner-Eckart theorem and \(6j\) symbols. These primitive matrix elements are then combined with flavor-dependent coefficients to obtain charge radii and quadrupole moments for any baryon in the ground-state multiplet, with SU(3) symmetry breaking introduced via quark mass ratios \(r = m/m_s\).

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius: \(r^2_n = -0.113(3)(4) \ \text{fm}^2\) (from Ref. [rn2])
- Proton charge radius: \(r^2_p = 0.779(25) \ \text{fm}^2\) (from reanalysis of Ref. [Rose])
- \(\Sigma^-\) charge radius: \(r^2_{\Sigma^-} = 0.61(12)(9) \ \text{fm}^2\) (from SELEX experiment)
- Predicted \(\Lambda\) charge radius: \(r^2_\Lambda = -0.057(3) \ \text{fm}^2\) (using \(N_c=3\) and the relation \(r^2_\Lambda = 3/(N_c+3) \ r_n^2\))
- Predicted \(\Xi^-\) charge radius: \(r^2_{\Xi^-} = 0.64(8) \ \text{fm}^2\) (from relation using measured \(p\), \(n\), and \(\Sigma^-\) radii)
- Model-independent relation: \(r^2_\Lambda / r_n^2 = 3/(N_c+3)\), independent of SU(3) symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of baryon charge radii (Buchmann and Lebed, 2001) and quadrupole moments (Buchmann, Hester, and Lebed, 2002) from the nonstrange sector to baryons with strangeness, using the \(1/N_c\) expansion method. The approach builds on the operator method developed for baryon observables in large-\(N_c\) QCD (see reviews by Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar, 1994) and generalizes the \(N_c=3\) calculation of quadrupole moments by Buchmann and Hester (2002). The results enable predictions for charge radii of strange baryons such as \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma^+\), \(\Xi^-\), and \(\Xi^0\) that may become measurable with improved experimental techniques, and provide model-independent relations that can test the \(1/N_c\) expansion against future data.
