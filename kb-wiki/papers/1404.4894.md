# Causal Baryon Diffusion and Colored Noise

> We construct a model of baryon diffusion which has the desired properties of causality and analyticity. The model also has the desired property of colored noise, meaning that the noise correlation function is not a Dirac delta function in space and time; rather, it depends on multiple time and length constants. The model can readily be incorporated in 3+1 dimensional second order viscous hydro-dynamical models of heavy ion collisions, which is particularly important at beam energies where the baryon density is large.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a model of baryon diffusion that achieves both causality (finite signal speed) and analyticity, while also producing "colored noise" (noise correlations that are smeared in both space and time, not Dirac delta functions), which is necessary for consistent numerical implementation in second-order viscous hydrodynamics at high baryon densities.

## Method

The authors generalize the baryon diffusion equation by successively adding higher-order space and time derivatives, moving from the ordinary diffusion equation to the Cattaneo (telegraph) equation and finally to the Gurtin-Pipkin equation. They derive the response function, baryon density autocorrelation function, and noise autocorrelation function for each model in Fourier space, then transform to coordinate space to examine causality and the spatial/temporal structure of correlations. The noise correlator is obtained from the fluctuation-dissipation theorem via the relation between the dissipative current and the noisy current.

## Key numbers

* For the Cattaneo equation, the characteristic length scale is \(\sqrt{\tau_D D}\) and the characteristic time scale is \(\tau_D\), related by \(\sqrt{\tau_D D} = v_0 \tau_D\) where \(v_0 = \sqrt{D/\tau_D}\) is the asymptotic wave speed.
* For the Cattaneo equation, the critical wavenumber separating oscillatory and exponential behavior is \(k_c = 1/(2\sqrt{\tau_D D})\).
* For the Gurtin-Pipkin equation, the asymptotic wave speed for baryon density fluctuations is \(v_0 = \sqrt{\tau_3' D/\tau_2^2}\).
* For the Gurtin-Pipkin equation with \(\tau_4 = 0\) and \(x = \tau_1/\tau_2 = 2\), the noise correlator in coordinate space is \(\langle I^i I^j (\mathbf{x},t) \rangle = (\sigma T)/(4\pi v_0 \tau_2^2 r) \delta(r - v_0 t) \delta_{ij}\), a pure pulse with no wake.
* For the ordinary diffusion equation, the noise correlator is \(\langle I^i I^j (t,\mathbf{x}) \rangle = 2\sigma T \delta(\mathbf{x}) \delta(t) \delta_{ij}\), a four-dimensional Dirac delta function.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of thermal noise in relativistic hydrodynamics from Kapusta (2011) and Young (2013), which identified singularities from white-noise correlations in second-order viscous hydrodynamics. The Gurtin-Pipkin approach provides a noise correlator that is smeared in both space and time, unlike the Cattaneo equation which still retains a spatial Dirac delta function. This model can be incorporated into 3+1 dimensional second-order viscous hydrodynamic codes such as MUSIC (Schenke, 2010) for simulations of heavy ion collisions at beam energy scan energies at RHIC, FAIR, SHINE, and NICA, where baryon density is large.
