# Quark matter in neutron stars within the Nambu - Jona-Lasinio model and confinement

> The quark matter equation of state (EOS) derived from the standard Nambu - Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is soft enough to render neutron stars (NS) unstable at the onset of the deconfined phase, and no pure quark matter can be actually present in its interior. Since this is a peculiarity of the NJL model, we have studied a modified NJL model with a momentum cut-off which depends on the density. This procedure, which improves the agreement between QCD and NJL model at large density, modifies the standard NJL equation of state, and then it is potentially relevant for the stability analysis of neutron stars. We show that also within this approach, the NS instability still persists, and that the vacuum pressure, as a signal of quark confinement, has a fundamental role for the NS stability. In this respect, our conclusions point to a relationship between confinement and NS stability.

## Key claim

The authors show that even after modifying the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a density-dependent momentum cutoff to improve agreement with QCD at high density, neutron stars still become unstable at the onset of deconfined quark matter; this instability persists because the vacuum pressure, which acts as a signal of quark confinement, is essential for neutron star stability.

## Method

The authors replaced the standard NJL model's constant momentum cutoff with several smooth, density-dependent cutoff functions Λ(μ) that grow with chemical potential, following the procedure of Casa et al. (2004). They computed the quark matter equation of state in the mean-field approximation for each cutoff choice, then matched it to a nucleonic equation of state derived from the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone method with the Argonne v18 two-body potential and Urbana three-body force, using the Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and stability curves.

## Key numbers

- The standard NJL cutoff is Λ₀ ≈ 600 MeV; the density-dependent cutoff Λ(μ) is set to (c+1)μ for μ > 435 MeV with c = 0.35.
- Nuclear saturation density is ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- The effective bag constant B_NJL extracted from the NJL model is close to 140 MeV fm⁻³.
- The transition from constant to density-dependent cutoff occurs at μ ≈ 435 MeV for the linear prescription, and at similar values (397–403 MeV) for the smooth interpolations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Buballa et al. (2003) and Schertler et al. (2000) which found that the standard NJL model produces neutron stars unstable at quark matter onset, unlike the MIT bag model or color-dielectric model. The authors show that the density-dependent cutoff procedure of Casa et al. (2004), which improves the NJL model's high-density behavior, does not remove this instability. The results point to confinement as the missing ingredient, consistent with the work of Thomas et al. (2005) who introduced a confining potential into the NJL model and found stable quark cores. This enables future work to focus on incorporating confinement explicitly into effective quark models for neutron star applications.
