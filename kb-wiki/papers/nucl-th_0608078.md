# Chiral phase transition in an extended NJL model with higher-order multi-quark interactions

> The chiral phase transition is studied in an extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with eight-quark interactions. Equations for scalar and vector quark densities, derived in the mean field approximation, are nonlinear and mutually coupled. The scalar-type nonlinear term hastens the restoration of chiral symmetry, while the scalar-vector mixing term makes the transition sharper. The scalar-type nonlinear term shifts the critical endpoint toward the values predicted by lattice QCD simulations and the QCD-like theory.

## Key claim

The scalar-type eight-quark interaction (sigma^4) shifts the critical endpoint of the chiral phase transition toward the values predicted by lattice QCD simulations and QCD-like theories, while the scalar-vector mixing term (sigma^2 omega^2) can make the transition sharper in the high chemical potential and low temperature region, partially canceling the effect of the repulsive vector-type four-quark interaction (omega^2).

## Method

The authors extended the standard two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by including eight-quark interactions: a scalar-type nonlinear term (sigma^4), a vector-type term (omega^2), and a scalar-vector mixing term (sigma^2 omega^2). They derived the thermodynamic potential and the coupled equations for scalar and vector quark densities in the mean field approximation, then solved for the effective quark mass and phase structure as functions of temperature and chemical potential. Parameters were fixed to reproduce the empirical pion decay constant (93.3 MeV), pion mass (138 MeV), and sigma meson mass (650 MeV) at zero temperature and density.

## Key numbers

* The scalar-type nonlinear interaction (sigma^4) reduced the transition temperature at zero chemical potential from T_c ~ 190 MeV (original NJL) to T_c ~ 180 MeV, closer to the lattice QCD value of T_c ~ 170 MeV.
* The critical endpoint in the NJL+sigma^4 model was located at (mu_e, T_e) = (308, 54) MeV, compared to (330, 47) MeV in the original NJL model.
* With an alternative parameter set reproducing M_sigma = 600 MeV, the NJL+sigma^4 model gave a critical endpoint at (276, 62) MeV.
* The effective coupling G_sigma (scalar) at zero temperature and density was 7.331 GeV^{-2} in models without sigma^4 and 7.922 GeV^{-2} in models with sigma^4.
* The three-dimensional momentum cutoff was set to Lambda = 0.6315 GeV, with the effective quark mass M = 0.3379 GeV and scalar density sigma_0 = -0.03023 GeV^3 at T = mu = 0.

## Context

This work extends the standard NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) by incorporating higher-order multi-quark interactions motivated by the nonperturbative renormalization group method (Aoki et al., 2000). The scalar-type eight-quark interaction shifts the critical endpoint toward values from lattice QCD simulations (Kogut et al., 2004; Zantow et al., 2002) and QCD-like theories (Kashiwa et al., 2005; Hatsuda et al., 2006), addressing a known shortcoming of the original NJL model. The vector-scalar mixing term provides a mechanism to suppress the vector interaction at high density while preserving it at normal nuclear density, analogous to nonlinear meson-nucleon couplings in relativistic nuclear theory (Toki et al., 1992; Kouno et al., 1995). This enables future studies of color superconductivity and its interplay with the chiral phase transition.
