# Time dependence of occupation numbers and thermalization time in closed chaotic many-body systems

> We study the time evolution of occupation numbers for interacting Fermi-particles in the situation when exact compound states are "chaotic". This situation is generic for highly excited many-particles states in heavy nuclei, complex atoms, quantum dots, spin systems and quantum computer models. Numerical data show perfect agreement with a simple theory for the onset of thermalization in close systems of interacting particles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in closed chaotic many-body systems, the time evolution of occupation numbers can be described by a simple interpolation formula, and that thermalization of these occupation numbers occurs on a shorter time scale than the onset of full statistical equilibrium, specifically on the time scale of population of the first class of basis states directly coupled by the two-body interaction.

## Method

The authors study the time evolution of an initially excited basis state in a system of interacting Fermi-particles, using the representation of exact eigenstates as chaotic superpositions of unperturbed shell-model basis states. They derive an analytical expression for the asymptotic (infinite-time) occupation numbers in terms of the F-function, which characterizes the shape of eigenstates in the unperturbed basis, and propose a simple formula for the time-dependent occupation numbers based on the survival probability of the initial state. Numerical simulations are performed using a model with random two-body interaction for 6 Fermi-particles occupying 12 single-particle levels, and the results are compared with the analytical predictions for both the Breit-Wigner regime (weak interaction) and the Gaussian regime (stronger interaction).

## Key numbers

- For the Breit-Wigner regime (weak interaction): interaction strength parameter \(\eta \equiv \langle V^2/D_0^2 \rangle \approx 0.003\), spreading width \(\Gamma \approx 0.50\), energy band width \(\Delta_E \approx 1.16\) (where \(D_0\) is the mean spacing between single-particle energies).
- For the Gaussian regime (stronger interaction): \(\eta \equiv \langle V^2/D_0^2 \rangle \approx 0.083\), \(\Gamma \approx 10.5\), \(\Delta_E \approx 5.8\).
- The total number of many-body states in the numerical model is 924 levels, with \(n=6\) particles and \(m=12\) single-particle levels.
- The asymptotic occupation numbers for the initial state in the middle of the spectrum are \(n_\alpha(\infty) = n/m = 1/2\), corresponding to infinite temperature.

## Context

This work extends the quantum chaos approach developed by the same authors and others (Flambaum and Izrailev, 2000; Flambaum and Izrailev, 1997) to the time-dependent problem of thermalization in closed many-body systems. It builds on prior studies of the F-function and chaotic eigenstates in complex atoms (Ce atom studies by Ponomarev), nuclei, and the two-body random interaction model. The results enable predictions for thermalization times in applications such as complex atoms, heavy nuclei, atomic clusters, and quantum dots, and suggest that occupation numbers thermalize faster than the system reaches full statistical equilibrium.
