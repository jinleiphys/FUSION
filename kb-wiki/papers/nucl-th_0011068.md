# A Possible Boson Realization of Generalized Lipkin Model for Many-Fermion System

> On the basis of the formalism proposed by three of the present authors (A.K., J.P.and M.Y.), generalized Lipkin model consisting of (M+1) single-particle levels is investigated. This model is essentially a kind of the su(M+1)-algebraic model and, in contrast to the conventional treatment, the case, where fermions are partially occupied in each level, is discussed. The scheme for obtaining the orthogonal set for the irreducible representation is presented.

# A Possible Boson Realization of Generalized Lipkin Model for Many-Fermion System

## Key claim

The paper presents a boson realization of the generalized Lipkin model in a non-symmetric representation that can handle partially occupied single-particle levels, in contrast to conventional treatments which are restricted to closed-shell systems where one level is fully occupied. The key condition is that the total fermion number may differ from the degeneracy of each level, and the formalism requires the specific case N=M for the su(M+1) and su(M,1) algebras.

## Method

The authors construct a Schwinger boson representation for the su(M+1) algebra using (M+1)(N+1) kinds of boson operators, and independently construct an su(N,1) algebra in the same boson space. They then define an intrinsic state in the boson space using determinants of boson creation operators (B_r* operators) that satisfy the conditions S_i|m⟩=0, S_i^j|m⟩=0 (j>i), T_p|m⟩=0, and T_q^p|m⟩=0 (q>p). The correspondence between the fermion intrinsic state and the boson intrinsic state is established through the eigenvalues of the S_i^i and T_p^p operators.

## Key numbers

- The boson space is constructed from (M+1)(N+1) kinds of boson operators, with the specific case N=M treated in the paper
- The intrinsic state in the boson space is given by |m⟩ = (B_1*)^(ν_M)(B_2*)^(ν_{M-1})...(B_M*)^(ν_1)(b*)^ν|0⟩, where all ν_i must be positive
- The eigenvalues of S_i^i on the intrinsic state are s_i = Σ_{k=1}^i ν_k + ν, with the ordering 0 ≤ s_1 ≤ s_2 ≤ ... ≤ s_M
- The eigenvalue of the operator S (analogous to fermion number) is S = Σ_{i=1}^M (ν-s_i) + ν, with the constraint -Σ_{i=1}^M s_i ≤ S ≤ (M+1)s_1 - Σ_{i=1}^M s_i
- For the su(2) case (M=1), the conventional Lipkin model has total fermion number N = Ω (the degeneracy), while the present work allows N ≠ Ω

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by three of the authors (Kuriyama, da Providencia, and Yamamura) in a previous paper (referred to as (I)) for the Schwinger boson representation of su(M+1) algebra. It generalizes the Lipkin model (Lipkin, Meshkov, Glick 1965) from the conventional su(2) case with fully occupied levels to the su(M+1) case with partially occupied levels, enabling the study of open shell systems. The Holstein-Primakoff (1940) and Schwinger (1952) boson representations are the standard approaches being generalized, and the present non-symmetric representation allows investigation of high-lying states and open shell systems that were inaccessible with the symmetric representation used in prior work by Klein and Marshalek (1991).
