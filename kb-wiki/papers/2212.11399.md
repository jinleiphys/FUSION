# Gluon PDF for the proton using the twisted mass formulation of lattice QCD

> We present results of the x-dependence of the unpolarized gluon PDF for the proton. We use an $N_f=2+1+1$ ensemble of maximally twisted mass fermions with clover improvement and the Iwasaki improved gluon action. The quark masses are tuned so that the pion mass is 260 MeV. We use a $32^3\times64$ lattice size with a lattice spacing $a=0.093$ fm giving a spatial extent of 3 fm. We employ the pseudo-distribution approach and obtain the light-cone Ioffe time distribution (ITD) combining data for nucleon momentum boosts up to 1.67 GeV and Wilson line lengths, $z$, up to 0.56 fm. We explore systematic effects such as the dependence on the maximum value of $z$ entering the fits to obtain the gluon PDF.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the unpolarized gluon PDF for the proton using the pseudo-distribution approach with twisted mass fermions, finding results consistent with both the HadStruc collaboration lattice calculation and the JAM20 global analysis, but the comparison is only qualitative because the lattice results are obtained on a single ensemble with a pion mass of 260 MeV rather than the physical value.

## Method

The calculation uses an \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensemble of maximally twisted mass clover-improved fermions with Iwasaki improved gluon action on a \(32^3\times64\) lattice with spacing \(a=0.093\) fm. The pseudo-distribution approach is employed: matrix elements of a non-local gluon operator with two field-strength tensors connected by Wilson lines are computed for nucleon momentum boosts up to 1.67 GeV, then formed into a double ratio to obtain the reduced Ioffe-time distribution (pseudo-ITD), which is evolved to a common scale and converted to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme at 2 GeV using one-loop matching. The gluon PDF is reconstructed by fitting the light-cone ITD with an ansatz \(x g(x) = N x^a (1-x)^b\), normalized using the gluon momentum fraction \(\langle x \rangle_g = 0.427(92)\) from a separate lattice calculation.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 260 MeV (approximately twice the physical value)
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.093\) fm, spatial extent: 3 fm
- Nucleon momentum boosts: 0.42, 0.83, 1.25, and 1.67 GeV
- Maximum Wilson line length: \(z = 6a \sim 0.56\) fm
- Maximum Ioffe time: \(\nu_{\text{max}} = 4.71\)
- Total measurements: 1,360,800 (1,134 configurations \(\times\) 200 source positions \(\times\) 6 directions)
- Gluon momentum fraction used for normalization: \(\langle x \rangle^{\overline{\text{MS}}}_g(\mu=2\ \text{GeV}) = 0.427(92)\) from Alexandrou (2020)
- Relative error at \(z=0\): \(\sim 6\%\) for \(P=0\), \(\sim 9\%\) for \(P=1.67\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-distribution approach, previously applied to quark PDFs, to the gluon PDF of the proton, addressing the additional challenges of disconnected diagrams and mixing with the quark singlet PDF. The results are compared with the HadStruc collaboration lattice calculation (HadStruc, 2021) which used a similar volume and lattice spacing but a higher pion mass of 358 MeV and the distillation method, and with the JAM20 global analysis (Moffat, 2021). The calculation enables future studies with physical pion masses, larger volumes, and higher statistics to provide lattice constraints on global analyses of gluon PDFs.
