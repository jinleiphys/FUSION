# When the Transition Temperature in Color Superconductors is Not Like in BCS Theory

> We study color superconductivity with $N_f=1,2,$ and 3 massless flavors of quarks. We present a general formalism to derive and solve the gap equations for condensation in the even-parity channel. This formalism shows that the leading-order contribution to the gap equation is unique for all color superconductors studied here, and that differences arise solely at the subleading order. We discuss a simple method to compute subleading contributions from the integration over gluon momenta in the gap equation. Subleading contributions enter the prefactor of the color-superconducting gap parameter. In the case of color-flavor and color-spin locking we identify further corrections to this prefactor arising from the two-gap structure of the quasiparticle excitations. Computing the transition temperature, $T_c$, where the color-superconducting condensate melts, we find that these contributions lead to deviations from the BCS behavior $T_c\simeq 0.57 ϕ_0$, where $ϕ_0$ is the magnitude of the zero-temperature gap at the Fermi surface.

## Key claim

The transition temperature $T_c$ in color superconductors deviates from the BCS relation $T_c \simeq 0.57 \phi_0$ when there are two distinct gapped quasiparticle excitation branches, as occurs in the color-flavor locked (CFL) and color-spin locked (CSL) phases, while the BCS relation holds for cases with only one gapped branch.

## Method

The authors derived and solved the QCD gap equation for six different color-superconducting systems with $N_f = 1, 2,$ and $3$ massless quark flavors, using a general formalism that separates leading-order contributions (from almost static magnetic gluon exchange) from subleading contributions (from static electric and non-static magnetic gluon exchange). They computed the eigenvalues $\lambda_s$ and coefficients $a_s$ characterizing the quasiparticle excitation branches for each system, extracted the subleading constant $d$ from the integration over gluon momenta, and solved the gap equation at zero temperature and at the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- For the 2SC phase (two-flavor spin-zero): $\phi_0^{\rm 2SC} = 2 \tilde{b} b_0' \mu \exp(-\pi/(2\bar{g}))$, with $\bar{g} \equiv g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)$, $\tilde{b} \equiv 256\pi^4 (2/(N_f g^2))^{5/2}$, $b_0' \equiv \exp(-(\pi^2+4)/8)$
- For the CFL phase (three-flavor): $\phi_0/\phi_0^{\rm 2SC} = 2^{-1/3}$, with $d = 0$ and $T_c/(e^\gamma \phi_0/\pi) = 2^{1/3}$
- For the CSL phase with transverse and longitudinal gaps: $\phi_0/\phi_0^{\rm 2SC} = 2^{-2/3} e^{-d}$ with $d = 5$, and $T_c/(e^\gamma \phi_0/\pi) = 2^{2/3}$
- For the CSL phase with longitudinal gap only: $d = 6$, $T_c/(e^\gamma \phi_0/\pi) = 1$
- For the CSL phase with transverse gap only: $d = 9/2$, $T_c/(e^\gamma \phi_0/\pi) = 1$ (when using the true gap $\sqrt{2}\phi_0$)
- For the polar phase: $d = \frac{3}{2}(3 + \cos^2\vartheta)$, $T_c/(e^\gamma \phi_0/\pi) = 1$
- The BCS relation $T_c \simeq 0.57 \phi_0$ corresponds to $T_c/\phi_0 = e^\gamma/\pi$ with $\gamma \simeq 0.577$

## Context

This work extends the QCD gap equation formalism developed by Son (1999), Rischke (2000), and Wang (2001) for two-flavor color superconductors to systems with one and three flavors, including the color-flavor locked phase of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the color-spin locked phase of Schaefer (2000). The paper demonstrates that the leading-order contribution to the gap equation is universal across all these systems, with differences only at subleading order, and shows that the two-gap structure in CFL and CSL phases produces deviations from the standard BCS relation between the zero-temperature gap and the critical temperature. This enables systematic computation of subleading corrections to the gap parameter and transition temperature in various color-superconducting phases relevant for dense quark matter in neutron stars.
