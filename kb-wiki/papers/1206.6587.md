# Locally anisotropic momentum distributions of hadrons at freeze-out in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A spheroidal anisotropic local momentum distribution is implemented in the statistical model of hadron production. We show that this form leads to exactly the same ratios of hadronic abundances as the equilibrium distributions, if the temperature is identified with a characteristic transverse-momentum scale. Moreover, to a very good approximation the transverse-momentum spectra of hadrons are the same for isotropic and anisotropic systems, provided the size of the system at freeze-out is appropriately adjusted. We further show that this invariance may be used to improve the agreement between the model and experimental HBT results.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using a spheroidal anisotropic local momentum distribution (Romatschke-Strickland form) in the statistical model of hadron production yields exactly the same ratios of hadronic abundances as equilibrium distributions, provided the temperature is identified with a characteristic transverse-momentum scale; furthermore, transverse-momentum spectra remain approximately invariant if the system size at freeze-out is appropriately adjusted, and this invariance can be exploited to improve agreement with experimental HBT results.

## Method

The authors implement the covariant Romatschke-Strickland form (RSF) for the phase-space distribution function, which introduces an anisotropy parameter ξ between longitudinal and transverse momenta, into the Cooper-Frye formalism for hadron emission from a freeze-out hypersurface. They compute hadronic multiplicities, transverse-momentum spectra, and pion HBT radii using two geometric models (the Cracow model and the blast-wave model) implemented in the Monte-Carlo generator THERMINATOR 2, comparing results to LHC data for Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- For the Cracow model with ξ = 2.5 and ξ = −0.5, the transverse-momentum spectra of positive pions and kaons for centrality 0%–5% are shown to be nearly identical to equilibrium results after rescaling geometric parameters by (1+ξ)^{1/6}.
- The equilibrium freeze-out temperature T₀ = 165.6 MeV is used as the scale Λ in anisotropic calculations.
- For the Cracow model equilibrium parameters: τ⁰₃f = 9.0 fm and r⁰_max = 11.4 fm for centrality 0%–5%; τ⁰₃f = 7.4 fm and r⁰_max = 9.6 fm for 10%–20%; τ⁰₃f = 5.9 fm and r⁰_max = 7.25 fm for 30%–40%.
- In the blast-wave model with A = −0.5, the HBT radius R_side grows with increasing ξ, while R_long is practically independent of ξ; the best agreement with ALICE data is obtained for ξ = 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the single-freeze-out thermal model previously applied to RHIC and LHC data by Broniowski, Florkowski, and collaborators (2001–2004), and specifically addresses the failure of the equilibrium model to reproduce proton spectra at LHC energies noted by Rybczyński (2012). The results qualitatively agree with the approximate invariance of hadronic abundance ratios demonstrated by Schenke (2003) under different assumptions. The paper shows that the thermal framework is robust against local momentum anisotropy, and that introducing a non-zero anisotropy parameter can improve the description of femtoscopic HBT radii, which were previously problematic in the equilibrium approach.
