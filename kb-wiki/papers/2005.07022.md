# A New Method to Extract Information of Near-Threshold Resonances: Uniformized Pole-Sum Representation of Green's Function and T-matrix

> We propose a new, simple model-independent method to extract information of near-threshold resonances, such as complex energies and residues. The method is based on the observation that the Green's function and the T-matrix can be represented as the sum of all poles, both bound and resonant poles, in the complex plane of a variable in which the Green's function and the T-matrix are single-valued functions. The symmetries of poles, which arise from the unitarity of the S-matrix, naturally impose the sum to obey the proper threshold behaviors. The imaginary part of Green's function and the T-matrix are directly related to observables such as scattering cross sections or invariant or missing mass distributions of hadron resonances. Thus we can determine their pole positions and residues by fitting their imaginary part to observables. We also test the new method by regarding the imaginary part of the $T$-matrix calculated exactly in a model theory as virtual experimental data. As a model theory, we take double-channel meson-baryon scatterings in the chiral unitary model with channels, $\overline{K}N (I=0)$, and $πΣ(I=0)$. By fitting the imaginary part of the $T$-matrix calculated in the model theory by that of the uniformized pole-sum, we obtain the pole positions and residues. Comparing the obtained results with those of the exact calculation in the model theory, we conclude that our new method works very well.

## Key claim

The authors propose a model-independent method to extract resonance parameters (complex energies and residues) from experimental observables by representing the Green's function or T-matrix as a sum of pole terms in a uniformized complex variable, which automatically incorporates proper threshold behaviors through the symmetry properties of the poles arising from unitarity of the S-matrix.

## Method

The method uniformizes the Riemann surface of the Green's function or T-matrix by introducing a kinetic variable (q for single-channel, z for double-channel systems) that unfolds the Riemann sheets, allowing the amplitude to be expressed as a sum of pole pairs. The imaginary part of this uniformized pole-sum representation, which is directly related to observables such as scattering cross sections or invariant mass distributions, is then fitted to experimental data to determine the complex pole positions and residues. The authors test the method by fitting the imaginary part of the T-matrix calculated exactly in a double-channel chiral unitary model for meson-baryon scatterings (channels: anti-K N (I=0) and pi Sigma (I=0)) and comparing the fitted pole parameters with the exact model results.

## Context

This work extends the analytic S-matrix approach to near-threshold resonances, addressing limitations of the Breit-Wigner form (Breit 1936) and the Flatte parameterization (Flatte 1976), which do not properly incorporate threshold behaviors. The method is tested against the chiral unitary model (Kaiser 1995, Oset 1997, Hyodo 2011, Morimatsu 2019) for the Lambda(1405) resonance region. The approach enables model-independent extraction of resonance parameters from experimental data, particularly for exotic hadron candidates near thresholds, and can be extended to systems with three or more channels by unfolding local regions of the Riemann surface.
