# Photon Intensity Interferometry for Expanding Sources

> Using Quantum Field Theory we derive a general formula for the double inclusive spectra of photons radiated by a system in local equilibrium. The derived expression differs significantly from the one mostly used up to now in photon intensity interferometry of heavy--ion collisions. We present a covariant expression for double inclusive spectra adapted for usage in numerical simulations. Application to a schematic model with a Bjørken type expansion gives strong evidence for the need of reinvestigating photon--photon correlations for expanding sources.

## Key claim

Using Quantum Field Theory, the authors derive a general formula for double inclusive photon spectra that differs significantly from the "pion-inspired" formulas previously used in photon intensity interferometry; the vector nature of photons and charge conservation reduce the correlation strength compared to scalar boson treatments, especially for expanding sources where the apparent source size may be overestimated by about 30% in rapidity.

## Method

The authors start from the S-matrix formalism in the interaction picture and express one- and two-photon spectra in terms of current-current correlators in the Heisenberg representation. They apply three physical assumptions (short-range hadronic correlations, mean free path much smaller than macroscopic variation scales, and hard photon condition) to simplify the four-point correlator into products of two-point correlators. The resulting expression for the double inclusive spectrum is then applied to a Bjorken-type hydrodynamic expansion model with an equation of state p = ε/3, using a parameterized photon production rate w(k₁,k₂|x) ∝ T(x)² exp(-(k̄·u(x))/T(x)).

## Key numbers

- For Bjorken hydrodynamics with initial temperature T_i = 200 MeV, final temperature T_f = 140 MeV, and initial proper time τ_i = 0.3 fm/c, the difference in apparent source size between the correct formula and the scalar approximation is about 30% in rapidity.
- The correlation function is plotted as a function of rapidity difference Δy at fixed transverse momenta k₁^⊥ = k₂^⊥ = 100 MeV.
- For a static source, the R-function reduces to R = 1 + (cosθ)², where cosθ = k₁·k₂/(|k₁||k₂|), showing that polarization overlap is less than 2 for non-zero angle between photon momenta.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of photon intensity interferometry beyond the static source calculation of Neuhauser (1986) and corrects the "pion-inspired" formulas used by Srivastava and Kapusta (1993, 1994) and Timmermann, Plum, Razumov, and Weinhold (1994), which assumed scalar boson behavior with a degeneracy factor of 3/2. The derived covariant expression is adapted for numerical simulations and provides a foundation for reinvestigating photon-photon correlations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for extracting source sizes from experimental data at GANIL (1994) and future facilities.
