# Fluctuation probes of quark deconfinement

> The magnitude of the local fluctuations of the net baryon number and electric charge differs widely between the confined and deconfined phases, and the difference is partially frozen at an early stage in relativistic heavy ion collisions. These fluctuations may thus be useful probes of the temporary formation of a deconfined state in such collisions.

## Key claim

The magnitude of local fluctuations of net baryon number and net electric charge differs between confined hadronic matter and deconfined quark-gluon plasma, and this difference is partially frozen at an early stage in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, provided the rapid longitudinal expansion prevents diffusive readjustment before freeze-out.

## Method

The authors compute the equilibrium fluctuations of net baryon number and net electric charge per unit entropy for an ideal hadron gas (using the Boltzmann approximation) and for an ideal quark-gluon plasma with two massless flavors, assuming matter that is meson-dominated (applicable at SPS energies and above). They then argue qualitatively, using a Bjorken boost-invariant flow profile and estimates of baryon diffusion lengths, that the short time between hadronization and freeze-out (about 9 fm/c at RHIC) prevents the readjustment of these fluctuations in rapidity bins of size Δη ≥ 1 for baryon number and Δη > 3 for electric charge.

## Key numbers

- For the quark-gluon plasma at high collision energies (μ/T → 0), the ratio of net baryon number fluctuations to entropy is (ΔN_b)²/S = 5/(37π²) ≈ 0.0137, with a μ-dependent correction of at most 5% at SPS energies.
- For a hadron gas, the net baryon number fluctuations are given by (ΔN_b)²_HG = 2 N_b(T) cosh(μ/T), where N_b(T) is the number of baryons.
- For net charge fluctuations in a hadron gas, (ΔQ)²_HG = N_ch, where N_ch is the total number of charged particles emitted from the subvolume.
- The ratio (ΔQ)²/S for the QGP is a factor 5/2 larger than the corresponding ratio for baryon number fluctuations.
- The fluctuations in the QGP are typically a factor 2 below those in the hadron gas, with a somewhat smaller reduction for baryon number than for charge fluctuations.
- At RHIC energies, the reaction zone takes about 9 fm/c to cool from hadronization temperature (≈ 170 MeV) to freeze-out, during which a rapidity interval Δη ≈ 1 expands from 5 fm to 14 fm in length.
- Baryons have an average thermal longitudinal velocity component v̄_z = 0.325 at T ≈ 170 MeV, allowing a baryon to travel only about 3 fm in the beam direction before freeze-out without rescattering.

## Context

This work extends the ideas of Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2000) and Jeon and Koch (2000), who first proposed using fluctuations of conserved quantities to probe the microscopic structure of dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions. It contrasts with earlier proposals by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998) that focused on temperature and pion multiplicity fluctuations as signals of a critical point. The paper enables experimental searches at SPS, RHIC, and LHC for event-by-event fluctuations of net baryon number and net electric charge as signatures of temporary quark-gluon plasma formation, and calls for future work on interaction effects (Jeon and Koch, 2000) and kinetic treatment of resonance decays.
