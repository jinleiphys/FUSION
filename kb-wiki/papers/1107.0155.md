# Holography at work for nuclear and hadron physics

> The purpose of this review is to provide basic ingredients of holographic QCD to non-experts in string theory and to summarize its interesting achievements in nuclear and hadron physics. We focus on results from a less stringy bottom-up approach and review a stringy top-down model with some calculational details.

## Key claim

This review argues that holographic QCD models, particularly the less-stringy bottom-up approach, can capture certain non-perturbative aspects of strongly coupled QCD, but the authors caution that what they mean by "QCD" might be mostly "QCD-like or a cousin of QCD" and that a simple tree-level analysis in the holographic dual model only captures the leading \(N_c\) contributions, with sub-leading corrections remaining a limitation.

## Method

The authors assemble results from bottom-up holographic QCD models (hard wall and soft wall) and review a top-down D4/D8/\(\overline{\rm D8}\) model, focusing on how to construct a 5D holographic dual using the AdS/CFT dictionary. They demonstrate the operator-field correspondence by mapping gauge-invariant local operators in QCD (such as \(\bar q \gamma^\mu t^a q\) and \(\bar q q\)) to bulk fields in the gravity side, with the 5D mass of the bulk field determined by the dimension and spin of the dual 4D operator. The review covers vacuum condensates, hadron spectroscopy and form factors, and QCD at finite temperature and density, using the hard wall model (with a sharp IR cutoff at \(z = z_m\)) and the soft wall model (with a smooth cutoff via a dilaton factor \(e^{-\Phi}\) where \(\Phi(z) = z^2\)).

## Key numbers

*   The 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\) is expressed in terms of the string length scale as \(\lambda = R^4 / l_s^4\), where \(R^2 = \sqrt{4\pi g_s N_c} \, l_s^2\).
*   In the soft wall model, the vector meson mass spectrum follows \(m_n^2 = 4c(n+1)\), where \(c\) restores the energy dimension and the \(\rho\) meson corresponds to \(n=0\).
*   The deconfinement temperature from the D4/D6 brane configuration is estimated as \(T_{deconf} = M_{KK}/(2\pi) \sim 160\) MeV, with \(M_{KK}\) around 1 GeV.
*   The five-dimensional gauge coupling in the hard wall model is given by \(g_5^2 = 12\pi^2 / N_c\).
*   The bulk mass of the scalar field \(X(x,z)\) dual to \(\bar q q\) is \(m_X^2 = -3\), derived from \(\Delta = 3\), \(p = 0\), and \(d = 4\) in the relation \(m_X^2 = (\Delta - p)(\Delta + p - d)\).

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Maldacena (1997) on the AdS/CFT correspondence and its application to QCD via holographic duals, building on the top-down D4/D8/\(\overline{\rm D8}\) model of Sakai and Sugimoto (2004, 2005) and the bottom-up hard wall and soft wall models of Erlich et al. (2005) and Da Rold and Pomarol (2005). It also references the light-front holography approach of Brodsky and de Teramond (2006) and the analysis of the relation between holographic QCD and QCD sum rules by Afonin et al. (2010). The review enables further work on applying holographic methods to dense baryonic matter, quark-gluon plasma, and hadron spectroscopy, while noting that open questions remain regarding sub-leading \(1/N_c\) corrections and the precise mapping between holographic models and real QCD.
