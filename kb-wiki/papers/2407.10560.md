# Confronting the low-scale seesaw and leptogenesis with neutrinoless double beta decay

> We revisit the impact of heavy neutrinos with masses in the MeV-GeV range on neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) in view of updated results for the lifetime of this process. Working in a minimal realistic extension of the Standard Model by two right-handed neutrino flavours, we show that the non-observation of $0νββ$ will impose strong bounds on the heavy neutrino properties that are complementary to the limits obtained from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and collider searches. For an inverted mass hierarchy of the light neutrinos we find that improved limits on $0νββ$ from next-generation experiments, assuming an improvement of two orders of magnitude on the current $0νββ$ limits will restrict the allowed parameter space for fixed mass splitting to narrow bands in the mass-mixing plane. Further combining this with the requirement to explain the baryon asymmetry of the universe via leptogenesis reduces these bands to windows in parameter space that are constrained in all directions and can be targeted by direct searches at accelerators, and restricts the mass splitting to values that can be resolved at experiments. For a normal mass hierarchy, restricting the allowed parameter will require even stronger $0νββ$ limits, and only parts of the parameter space can then be probed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that next-generation neutrinoless double beta decay experiments, assuming an improvement of two orders of magnitude on current limits, will restrict the allowed parameter space for heavy neutrinos in the MeV-GeV range to narrow bands in the mass-mixing plane, but only for an inverted mass hierarchy of the light neutrinos; for a normal mass hierarchy, even stronger limits are required and only parts of the parameter space can be probed.

## Method

The authors work in a minimal realistic extension of the Standard Model by two right-handed neutrino flavours (the 3+2 scenario), using the Casas-Ibarra parametrisation to fit neutrino oscillation data. They compute the neutrinoless double beta decay rate using a chiral effective field theory framework that includes both long-distance and short-distance contributions, and solve the quantum kinetic equations for leptogenesis numerically to determine the baryon asymmetry. The parameter space is scanned for heavy neutrino masses between 0.1 and 10 GeV with mass splittings up to 0.1, combining constraints from neutrinoless double beta decay, Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, collider searches, and the requirement to explain the observed baryon asymmetry.

## Key numbers

* Current best limit on the neutrinoless double beta decay half-life of 136Xe: 3.8 x 10^26 years (KamLAND-Zen 2024)
* Next-generation experiments project an improvement of about two orders of magnitude on current limits
* The 136Xe lifetime in the inverted hierarchy is roughly 10^27 years, and in the normal hierarchy roughly 10^29 years, up to an O(1) uncertainty
* The observed baryon-to-entropy ratio Y_B,obs = 8.7 x 10^-11 (Planck 2018)
* Phase-space factor G_01 = 1.4 x 10^-14 y^-1 for 136Xe and G_01 = 2.2 x 10^-15 y^-1 for 76Ge
* The weak sphaleron freeze-out temperature T_sph is approximately 130 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the interplay between neutrinoless double beta decay and low-scale leptogenesis by Drewes (2016), Hernandez (2016), and Asaka (2016), which used simplified formulae not accurate in the sub-GeV regime and did not perform a full parameter space scan. The paper applies the recently developed chiral effective field theory framework for neutrinoless double beta decay computations involving light heavy neutral leptons by Dekens (2023, 2024), which includes several new effects not considered in more traditional approaches. The results enable direct searches at accelerators to target specific windows in parameter space that are constrained in all directions, and restrict the mass splitting to values that can be resolved at experiments.
