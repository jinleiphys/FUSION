# Extracting Hypernuclear Properties from the $(e, e^\prime K^+)$ Cross Section

> Experimental studies of hypernuclear dynamics, besides being essential for the understanding of strong interactions in the strange sector, have important astrophysical implications. The observation of neutron stars with masses exceeding two solar masses poses a serious challenge to the models of hyperon dynamics in dense nuclear matter, many of which predict a maximum mass incompatible with the data. In this article, it is argued that valuable new insight may be gained extending the experimental studies of kaon electro production from nuclei to include the $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime K^+) \isotope[208][Λ]{\rm Tl}$ process. The connection with proton knockout reactions and the availability of accurate $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime p) \isotope[207][]{\rm Tl}$ data can be exploited to achieve a largely model-independent analysis of the measured cross section. A framework for the description of kaon electro production based on the formalism of nuclear many-body theory is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the forthcoming measurement of the $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime K^+) \isotope[208][\Lambda]{\rm Tl}$ cross section, combined with existing high-resolution $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime p) \isotope[207][]{\rm Tl}$ data, can yield a largely model-independent determination of $\Lambda$ binding energies in hypernuclei, provided the analysis uses a many-body theoretical framework that accounts for nuclear dynamics beyond the mean-field approximation.

## Method

The author derives a factorised expression for the $(e,e^\prime K^+)$ cross section within the impulse approximation, using the Green's function formalism of nuclear many-body theory. The nuclear transition amplitude is written as a product of two amplitudes: one describing proton removal from the target nucleus (as in $(e,e^\prime p)$ reactions) and one describing $\Lambda$ removal from the final hypernucleus. The connection between the missing energy measured in $(e,e^\prime K^+)$ and $(e,e^\prime p)$ experiments is exploited to express the $\Lambda$ binding energy as the difference of the two missing energies, $B_\Lambda = - (E^\Lambda_{\rm miss} - E^p_{\rm miss})$, allowing a model-independent extraction.

## Key numbers

* The average distance between nucleons in the target nucleus is $d_{NN} \sim 1.5\ {\rm fm}$, setting the scale for the impulse approximation regime where the virtual photon wavelength $\lambda \sim 1/|{\bf q}|$ must be short compared to this distance.
* The missing energy spectra from NIKHEF-K $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime p) \isotope[207][]{\rm Tl}$ measurements were obtained with a resolution better than 100 KeV and extend up to $\sim 30$ MeV.
* The discrepancy between measured peak energies and mean-field predictions for deeply bound proton states in $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}$ is as large as $\sim 3$ MeV.
* The smooth contribution to the nuclear spectral function from nucleon-nucleon correlations accounts for $\sim 20\%$ of the total strength.
* The potential energy per nucleon from three-nucleon interactions varies from $-0.23$ MeV in $\isotope[40][]{\rm Ca}$ to 2.78 MeV in nuclear matter at equilibrium density, showing a sign change with mass number.

## Context

This work extends the Green's function formalism previously applied to proton knockout reactions (Benhar, 2005; Benhar, 2008) to the case of kaon electroproduction, establishing a direct connection between $(e,e^\prime p)$ and $(e,e^\prime K^+)$ processes. The approach builds on high-resolution $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime p) \isotope[207][]{\rm Tl}$ measurements performed at NIKHEF-K (Quint, 1987; Quint, 1988; Bobeldijk, 1994; van den Brand, 1994) and theoretical calculations of $\Lambda$ spectral functions in hypernuclei (Vidana, 2015). The framework is designed to support the approved Jefferson Lab measurement of the $\isotope[208][]{\rm Pb}(e,e^\prime K^+) \isotope[208][\Lambda]{\rm Tl}$ cross section (Garibaldi, 2019), which aims to address the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star physics by providing constraints on hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon-nucleon interactions in a heavy neutron-rich nucleus that serves as a proxy for neutron star interior conditions.
