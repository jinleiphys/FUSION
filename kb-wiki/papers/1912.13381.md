# Boundary action and profile of effective bosonic strings

> The mean-square width of the energy profile of bosonic string is calculated considering two boundary terms in the effective action. The perturbative expansion of the Lorentz-invariant boundary terms at the second and the fourth order in the effective action is taken around the free Nambu-Goto string. The calculation are presented for open strings with Dirichlet boundary condition on cylinder.

## Key claim

The mean-square width of the bosonic string energy profile receives corrections from Lorentz-invariant boundary terms in the effective Luscher-Weisz action, calculated perturbatively around the free Nambu-Goto string for open strings with Dirichlet boundary conditions on a cylinder; these corrections are expected to account for deviations from free-string behavior observed in lattice data at intermediate distances and high temperatures, but the paper presents only the analytic derivation without numerical comparison to lattice data.

## Method

The authors start from the Luscher-Weisz effective string action and expand the Lorentz-invariant boundary terms at second order (coupling b2, four-derivative term) and fourth order (coupling b4, six-derivative term) around the free Nambu-Goto action. They compute the mean-square width of the string using perturbative expansion of the path integral, evaluating the expectation values of the quadratic field excitation with the boundary action using the free Green function propagator on a cylinder with Dirichlet boundary conditions at the ends and periodic boundary conditions in the compactified time direction. The divergent sums are regularized using zeta-function regularization.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order mean-square width of the free string is given by W²_lo(zeta1, tau) = (D-2)/(2*pi*sigma_0) * log(R/R_0(zeta1)) + (D-2)/(2*pi*sigma_0) * log|theta_2(pi*zeta1/R; tau)/theta_1'(0; tau)|, where D is spacetime dimension, sigma_0 is string tension, R is source separation, and tau = i*L_T/R with L_T the compactified time length.
* The next-to-leading order self-interaction correction to the width, W²_nlo, involves terms proportional to (D-2)*pi/(12*sigma²*R²) multiplied by combinations of Eisenstein series E_2(tau) and E_2(2tau).
* The boundary term correction at second order, W²_b2(R, L_T), is given by -pi³*b2*(D-2)/(16*R⁵*sigma²) * ( (1/3)*E_2(2*i*tau) + 2*(theta_1'/theta_1)(i*pi*tau/2, q)^(1) - 91/6 ), where b2 is the boundary coupling and q = exp(-pi*L_T/R).

## Context

This work extends the Luscher-Weisz effective string theory (Luscher, Symanzik, Weisz, 2002) by calculating the previously uncomputed contribution of Lorentz-invariant boundary terms to the mean-square width of the confining string, complementing prior studies of boundary corrections to the static quark potential (Caselle 2014, Brandt 2017, Bakry 2017-2020). The results are intended to explain deviations from free Nambu-Goto string behavior observed in lattice simulations of the flux-tube width profile at intermediate distances and high temperatures (Bakry 2010, Bicudo 2017), and to enable future confrontation with numerical lattice data for QCD flux-tubes.
