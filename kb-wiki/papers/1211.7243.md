# Shape evolution and the role of intruder configurations in Hg isotopes within the interacting boson model based on a Gogny energy density functional

> The interacting boson model with configuration mixing, with parameters derived from the self-consistent mean-field calculation employing the microscopic Gogny energy density functional, is applied to the systematic analysis of the low-lying structure in Hg isotopes. Excitation energies, electromagnetic transition rates, deformation properties, and ground-state properties of the $^{172-204}$Hg nuclei are obtained by mapping the microscopic deformation energy surface onto the equivalent IBM Hamiltonian in the boson condensate. These results point to the overall systematic trend of the transition from the near spherical vibrational state in lower-mass Hg nuclei close to $^{172}$Hg, onset of intruder prolate configuration as well as the manifest prolate-oblate shape coexistence around the mid-shell nucleus $^{184}$Hg, weakly oblate deformed structure beyond $^{190}$Hg up to the spherical vibrational structure toward the near semi-magic nucleus $^{204}$Hg, as observed experimentally. The quality of the present method in the description of the complex shape dynamics in Hg isotopes is examined.

## Key claim
The interacting boson model with configuration mixing, whose parameters are derived from microscopic Gogny-D1M energy density functional calculations, reproduces the systematic shape evolution from near-spherical vibrational states in neutron-deficient Hg isotopes through prolate-oblate shape coexistence around mid-shell to weakly oblate and spherical structures near the N=126 closed shell, with the caveat that the mapping procedure assumes approximate separation of coexisting mean-field minima and is valid only up to about 3 MeV excitation energy.

## Method
Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional were performed to obtain microscopic deformation energy surfaces for Hg isotopes with mass A=172-204. These surfaces were then mapped onto the equivalent IBM-2 Hamiltonian in the boson condensate using the coherent-state framework, with up to 2p-2h proton excitations included for nuclei exhibiting two minima. The IBM Hamiltonian parameters (single d-boson energy, quadrupole-quadrupole strength, cubic term, L·L term, mixing strength, and intruder offset) were determined by fitting the topology of the microscopic energy surface, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the enlarged boson space to obtain spectra and transition rates.

## Key numbers
- The R4/2 ratio for 172Hg is 2.34 and for 174Hg is 2.36, indicating vibrational-like behavior
- The prolate-oblate energy difference in the HFB energy surface reaches a minimum in 186Hg, signaling the most prominent shape coexistence
- The oblate minimum occurs at β ≈ 0.15 and the prolate minimum at β ≈ 0.25-0.3 across the isotopic chain
- The two minima in 176Hg lie within approximately 120 keV of each other
- The scale factors Cβ,1 ≈ 3 and Cβ,3 ≈ 5 for 176-190Hg
- The experimental R4/2 ratio for 172Hg is 2.26

## Context
This work extends the methodology previously applied to Lead isotopes (Nomura, 2012) to the systematic study of Hg isotopes, using the Gogny-D1M parametrization which has similar predictive power to the more conventional D1S set. The results are consistent with earlier mean-field calculations using Gogny-D1 and D1S parametrizations (Girod, 1982; Delaroche, 1994) and Skyrme-SLy4 (Moreno, 2006), but at variance with earlier Woods-Saxon potential studies (Bengtsson, 1989; Nazarewicz, 1993) that predicted oblate ground states for neutron-deficient Hg isotopes. The work enables systematic spectroscopic predictions for Hg isotopes that can be compared with experimental data from gamma-ray studies (Julin, 2001; Sandzelius, 2009; Elseviers, 2011).
