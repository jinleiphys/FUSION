# Polarizabilities from kaon Compton scattering

> The polarizabilities of light pseudoscalar mesons can be extracted from differential cross sections for Compton scattering near threshold. While this has been accomplished for charged pions employing Primakoff reactions, a corresponding measurement for kaons will be affected by the presence of the $K^*(892)$ resonance not too far from threshold. We propose a method to extend the energy range serviceable for this purpose by reconstructing the $K^*(892)$ contribution model-independently from its $Kπ$ intermediate state, using dispersion theory. We point out that, in contrast to the charged-pion analog, there is likely no strong hierarchy between sum and difference of electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities; we discuss the sensitivity to disentangling both by improved experimental angular coverage.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to extract charged-kaon electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities from Primakoff Compton scattering by using dispersion theory to reconstruct the $K^*(892)$ resonance contribution model-independently from its $K\pi$ intermediate state, thereby extending the energy range serviceable for polarizability extraction beyond the near-threshold region where the $K^*(892)$ resonance would otherwise limit the analysis.

## Method

The authors construct a representation of the kaon Compton scattering amplitude that combines Born terms, dipole polarizabilities as free parameters, dispersively reconstructed $K\pi$ $P$-waves (the $K^*(892)$ resonance) in the $s$- and $u$-channels, a dispersively reconstructed $\pi\pi$ $S$-wave (the $f_0(500)$ resonance) in the $t$-channel, and perturbative $t$-channel kaon loops from next-to-leading-order chiral perturbation theory. The $K^*$ contribution is reconstructed via twice-subtracted dispersion relations using $\gamma K \to K\pi$ amplitudes from Dax et al. (2020), with subtraction constants fixed by vector-meson-dominance estimates of the polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- The ratio $M_{K^*}/M_K \approx 1.8$, compared to $M_\rho/M_\pi \approx 5.5$, illustrating why the $K^*(892)$ resonance lies much closer to threshold for kaons than the $\rho$ resonance does for pions
- The chiral perturbation theory prediction for the charged-kaon polarizability difference is $(\alpha_1-\beta_1)^\text{ChPT}_{K^\pm} = 1.1(2) \times 10^{-4}~\text{fm}^3$
- The vector-meson-dominance estimate for the magnetic polarizability is $(\beta_1)^\text{VMD}_{K^\pm} = 0.36 \times 10^{-4}~\text{fm}^3$, which reduces the chiral prediction for $(\alpha_1-\beta_1)_{K^\pm}$ by roughly 30%
- The ratio $(\alpha_1+\beta_1)/(\alpha_1-\beta_1)$ for kaons is increased to almost 50% by vector-meson contributions, while it remains at around 3% for pions
- The maximum $|t|$ probed at fixed $s$ reaches $4M_\pi^2$ already at $\sqrt{s} \approx 0.65~\text{GeV}$

## Context

This work extends the methodology used for charged-pion polarizability extraction from Primakoff reactions (COMPASS, 2014) to the kaon case, where the $K^*(892)$ resonance lies much closer to threshold. The dispersive reconstruction of the $K^*$ resonance builds on the $\gamma K \to K\pi$ amplitudes calculated by Dax et al. (2020), and the $t$-channel $\pi\pi$ treatment follows the coupled-channel formalism of Garcia-Martin et al. (2010). The proposed method enables a simultaneous data analysis of charged-kaon polarizabilities, the chiral anomaly, and radiative couplings of the $K^*(892)$ resonance at the planned AMBER experiment at CERN.
