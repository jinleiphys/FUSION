# Proton-${}^3$H scattering calculation: Elastic and charge-exchange reactions up to 30 MeV

> Proton-${}^3$H elastic scattering and charge-exchange reaction ${}^3$H$(p,n){}^3$He in the energy regime above four-nucleon breakup threshold are described in the momentum-space transition operator framework. Fully converged results are obtained using realistic two-nucleon potentials and two-proton Coulomb force as dynamic input. Differential cross section, proton analyzing power, outgoing neutron polarization, and proton-to-neutron polarization transfer coefficients are calculated between 6 and 30 MeV proton beam energy. Good agreement with the experimental data is found for the differential cross section both in elastic and charge-exchange reactions; the latter shows a complicated energy and angular dependence. The most sizable discrepancies between predictions and data are found for the proton analyzing power and outgoing neutron polarization in the charge-exchange reaction, while the respective proton-to-neutron polarization transfer coefficients are well described by the calculations.

## Key claim

The authors present fully converged momentum-space calculations of proton-${}^3$H elastic scattering and the charge-exchange reaction ${}^3$H$(p,n){}^3$He between 6 and 30 MeV, finding good agreement with experimental differential cross sections but sizable discrepancies in proton analyzing power and outgoing neutron polarization for the charge-exchange reaction, while polarization transfer coefficients are well described.

## Method

The authors solve the momentum-space Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for symmetrized four-particle transition operators, using realistic two-nucleon potentials (INOY04, CD Bonn, and CD Bonn + Delta) with the two-proton Coulomb force included via screening and renormalization. To handle the complicated singularity structure of the kernel, the equations are solved numerically at several complex energies with finite positive imaginary parts, and the on-shell matrix elements are extrapolated to the physical limit. The calculations employ the isospin formalism and partial-wave decomposition.

## Key numbers

- The INOY04 potential predicts the ${}^3$He binding energy as 7.73 MeV and the ${}^3$H binding energy as 8.49 MeV, nearly reproducing the experimental values of 7.72 MeV and 8.48 MeV respectively
- The CD Bonn + Delta potential yields ${}^3$He and ${}^3$H binding energies of 7.53 MeV and 8.28 MeV; CD Bonn yields 7.26 MeV and 8.00 MeV
- At the minimum of the elastic differential cross section, predictions from CD Bonn and CD Bonn + Delta are below those of INOY04 by about 15%
- At 13.6 MeV proton energy and 0 degrees center-of-mass angle in the charge-exchange reaction, predictions of the three force models differ by almost 20%
- At 6 MeV proton energy, the predicted proton analyzing power and outgoing neutron polarization in the charge-exchange reaction are too small by a factor of 2 compared to data
- At 13.6 MeV, the discrepancy in proton analyzing power for the charge-exchange reaction remains about 25%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations on $n+{}^3$H scattering (Deltuva 2012), $p+{}^3$He scattering (Deltuva 2013), and $n+{}^3$He scattering (Deltuva 2014) to the $p+{}^3$H system, completing the set of four-nucleon scattering calculations with realistic two-nucleon forces. The discrepancies found in analyzing powers for the charge-exchange reaction are interpreted as a possible manifestation of the well-known $A_y$ puzzle seen in nucleon-deuteron and nucleon-trinucleon scattering (Cornelius 1990, Kievsky 2001, Viviani 2001, Viviani 2013, Deltuva 2007, Deltuva 2013). The calculations enable future inclusion of irreducible three-nucleon forces in momentum-space four-nucleon calculations and provide benchmarks for experimental data where inconsistencies exist between different data sets.
