# The CSM extension for the description of positive and negative parity bands in even-odd nuclei

> A particle-core Hamiltonian is used to describe the lowest parity partner bands $K^π=1/2^{\pm}$ in $^{237}$U and $^{239}$Pu. The quadrupole and octupole boson Hamiltonian associated to the core is identical to the one previously used for the description of four positive and four negative parity bands in the neighboring even-even isotopes. The single particle space for the odd nucleon consists of three spherical shell model states, two of positive and one of negative parity. The particle-core Hamiltonian consists of four terms: a quadrupole-quadrupole, an octupole-octupole, a spin-spin and a rotational $\hat{I}^2$ interaction, with $\hat {I}$ denoting the total angular momentum. The parameters involved in the particle-core coupling Hamiltonian were fixed by fitting four particular energies in the two bands. The calculated excitation energies are compared with the corresponding experimental data as well as with those obtained with other approaches. Also, we searched for some signatures for a static octupole deformation in the considered odd isotopes.

## Key claim

The coherent state model (CSM) extended to odd nuclei can describe the lowest parity partner bands \(K^\pi = 1/2^\pm\) in \(^{237}\)U and \(^{239}\)Pu, but the agreement is mixed: for \(^{239}\)Pu the present approach yields better agreement (r.m.s. 31.8 keV) than the earlier work of Bona (2000) (r.m.s. 60 keV), while for \(^{237}\)U the earlier work (r.m.s. 30 keV) outperforms the present approach (r.m.s. 48.97 keV).

## Method

A particle-core Hamiltonian is constructed from a spherical shell-model single-particle space (three spherical states: \(2g_{7/2}\), \(2g_{9/2}\), \(1h_{9/2}\)), a quadrupole-plus-octupole boson core Hamiltonian identical to that used previously for eight bands in even-even neighbors, and a coupling term containing quadrupole-quadrupole, octupole-octupole, spin-spin (\(\vec{j}\cdot\vec{J}\)), and rotational (\(\hat{I}^2\)) interactions. The core states are angular-momentum- and parity-projected from a product of quadrupole and octupole coherent states; the single-particle states are mixed via the mean-field Hamiltonian to break spherical and reflection symmetries. The four coupling strengths are fixed by fitting four specific excitation energies in the two bands, and the resulting energies for all states up to \(J=55/2\) are compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{237}\)U, the r.m.s. deviation of calculated excitation energies from experimental data is 48.97 keV.
- For \(^{239}\)Pu, the r.m.s. deviation is 31.8 keV.
- The fitted coupling parameters for \(^{237}\)U: \(X_2 b^2 = 1.080\) keV, \(X_3 b^3 = 2.227\) keV, \(X_{jJ} = -5.817\) keV, \(X_{I^2} = 4.634\) keV.
- The fitted coupling parameters for \(^{239}\)Pu: \(X_2 b^2 = -2.515\) keV, \(X_3 b^3 = 4.937\) keV, \(X_{jJ} = -3.985\) keV, \(X_{I^2} = 5.050\) keV.
- The mixing amplitudes for the single-particle states are \(|{\cal A}^{(+)}_{i,3}|^2 = 0.04\) and \(|{\cal A}^{(-)}_{i,3}|^2 = 0.06\) for \(i=1,2\).
- For comparison, the earlier approach of Bona (2000) gave r.m.s. deviations of 30 keV for \(^{237}\)U and 60 keV for \(^{239}\)Pu.

## Context

This work extends the coherent state model (CSM) of Raduta (1981, 1997, 2002, 2003, 2006) from even-even nuclei (where it described eight rotational bands) to odd-mass nuclei with both quadrupole and octupole deformation. It contrasts with earlier particle-core approaches (Raduta and Ceausescu; Lea) that restricted the single-particle space to a single \(j\) value, thereby eliminating the octupole-octupole interaction. The results are compared with those of Zubov (2000), Denisov (2000), and Bona (2000), who used a quadrupole-octupole Hamiltonian in intrinsic deformation variables \(\beta_2\) and \(\beta_3\). The formalism can be extended to describe six additional bands (three positive, three negative parity) and may enable studies of chiral symmetry in the three-component particle-core system.
