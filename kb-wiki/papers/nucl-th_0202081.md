# Predictions of total and total reaction cross sections for nucleon-nucleus scattering up to 300 MeV

> Total reaction cross sections are predicted for nucleons scattering from various nuclei. Projectile energies to 300 MeV are considered. So also are mass variations of those cross sections at selected energies. All predictions have been obtained from coordinate space optical potentials formed by full folding effective two-nucleon (NN) interactions with one body density matrix elements (OBDME) of the nuclear ground states. Good comparisons with data result when effective NN interactions defined by medium modification of free NN t matrices are used. Coupled with analyses of differential cross sections, these results are sensitive to details of the model ground states used to describe nuclei.

## Key claim

Total reaction cross sections for nucleon-nucleus scattering up to 300 MeV can be predicted successfully using coordinate-space optical potentials formed by folding effective two-nucleon interactions with one-body density matrix elements, but only when the effective interactions incorporate medium modifications (via Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone g-matrices) rather than free-space t-matrices; the predictions are sensitive to details of the nuclear ground state models used.

## Method

The authors constructed coordinate-space optical potentials by full-folding complex, energy- and density-dependent effective NN interactions (based on the Bonn-B potential) with one-body density matrix elements and single-particle wave functions from shell model or Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for targets ranging from 6Li to 238U. They solved the resulting nonlocal Schrodinger equations using the DWBA98 program to obtain S-matrices and phase shifts, from which total reaction cross sections were computed for projectile energies from 10 to 300 MeV. Two formulations were compared: "t-folding" using free-space NN t-matrices from Lippmann-Schwinger equations, and "g-folding" using medium-modified g-matrices from Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equations.

## Key numbers

- For 40.9A MeV 6He scattering from hydrogen, the reaction cross section varies from 350 mb (standard shell model with neutron skin) to 406 mb (extended halo distribution), compared to the measured value of 409 +/- 21 mb
- For 12C, t-folding overestimates reaction cross section data by 20-40% below 200 MeV, while g-folding gives very good agreement up to 300 MeV
- At 100 MeV, t-folding overestimates proton reaction cross sections by approximately 150 mb across the mass range, while g-folding predictions agree with measurements
- For 208Pb, increasing the oscillator length for the outer i13/2 neutron shell by 8% brings g-folding predictions into very good agreement with proton reaction data
- For 40Ca neutron total cross sections, the peak at 40 MeV is underpredicted by 10% using g-folding
- For 208Pb neutron total cross sections, g-folding with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock wave functions gives excellent agreement with data from 60 to 200 MeV, but underpredicts above 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the microscopic folding model approach reviewed by Amos (2000) to systematically predict total reaction cross sections for both protons and neutrons across a wide mass and energy range, demonstrating that medium-modified (g-matrix) interactions are essential for agreement with data. The results enable reliable predictions for applications including accelerator-driven systems, radiation therapy, and astrophysical nucleosynthesis, and show sensitivity to neutron density distributions that connects to neutron star properties as discussed by Brown (2000) and Horowitz (2001). The approach contrasts with phenomenological Woods-Saxon optical potentials, which the authors note are not truly predictive and suffer from parameter ambiguities, especially for 0p-shell nuclei.
