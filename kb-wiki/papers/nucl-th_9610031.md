# Effective kaon mass in dense baryonic matter: role of correlations

> We evaluate the effective kaon mass in dense nuclear matter. Pauli blocking and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations are incorporated. The effects of short-range correlations are shown to be moderate and figure importantly only at densities larger than 2 times normal nuclear density. We discuss the relations between the present results and the results obtained in next-to-next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory (${{\cal O} (Q^3)}$, where $Q$ is the characteristic small energy-momentum scale probed). We also discuss mean-field aspects, with some remarks on the relation between the short-range correlations and the four-Fermi contact terms in the chiral effective Lagrangian.

# Effective kaon mass in dense baryonic matter: role of correlations

## Key claim
Short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations produce a moderate repulsive effect on both K⁺ and K⁻ effective masses in dense nuclear matter, but this effect becomes sizeable only at densities larger than twice normal nuclear density (ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³). The authors show that the effective field method of Ericson and Ericson reproduces the full coupled-channels results for Pauli blocking, and that adding short-range correlations shifts the density threshold for possible kaon condensation to higher values.

## Method
The authors apply the Ericson-Ericson effective field method to kaon-nuclear s-wave interactions, incorporating Pauli blocking via a Fermi gas model and short-range NN correlations via a hard-core ansatz (r ≤ R) and a more realistic G-matrix parameterization using a spherical Bessel function with cutoff q_c = 3.93 fm⁻¹. The off-shell kaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes are taken from a coupled-channels chiral dynamics approach that describes low-energy kaon-nucleon data. The effective kaon mass is obtained by solving the dispersion relation (m_K^⋆)² − m_K² = 2 m_K^⋆ Re U_opt(m_K^⋆, k=0) with the generalized optical potential that includes charge exchange and two-body correlations to all orders.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Pseudoscalar decay constant: f ≃ 93 MeV
- Short-range correlation cutoff momentum: q_c = 3.93 fm⁻¹ (approximately the ω-meson mass)
- In symmetric nuclear matter, the K⁻ mass decreases by about 120 MeV in the present approach (approach A), compared to about 200 MeV attraction in the chiral perturbation approach of Lee et al. (approach B)
- The inverse correlation length at threshold in symmetric nuclear matter: [ξ₀₀ρ₀]_{ω=m_K}^{μ=0} = ⟨1/r⟩ = 3p_F/(2π)
- Pauli correlation parameters in symmetric nuclear matter: ξ ≡ ξ₀₀ = 3 ξ₁₁ = (9/p_F²) F(0, μ, p_F); ξ₁₀ = ξ₀₁ = 0
- Without short-range correlations, kaon condensation matching occurs around ρ ≈ 3 ρ₀; with short-range correlations this shifts to ρ > 3 ρ₀

## Context
This work extends the Ericson-Ericson effective field method (Ericson and Ericson, 1966) from pions to kaons, incorporating both Pauli blocking and short-range NN correlations. It builds on prior chiral SU(3) dynamics calculations by Brown et al., Thomas et al., and Waas et al. that included Pauli blocking but not short-range correlations. The results are compared with the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Lee et al. (1996) at O(Q³) and the mean-field approach of Brown and Rho (1996); the authors find that the additional attraction in the Lee et al. approach comes from four-Fermi interactions involving the Λ(1405) that are not included in the present treatment. The work enables more precise predictions for kaon condensation thresholds in neutron stars and provides a framework for interpreting ongoing GSI experiments (KaoS and FOPI collaborations) on kaon properties in dense nuclear matter.
