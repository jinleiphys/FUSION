# Isomerism in the "south-east" of $^{132}$Sn and a predicted neutron-decaying isomer in $^{129}$Pd

> Excited states in neutron-rich nuclei located south-east of $^{132}$Sn are investigated by shell-model calculations. A new shell-model Hamiltonian is constructed for the present study. The proton-proton and neutron-neutron interactions of the Hamiltonian are obtained through the existing CD-Bonn $G$ matrix results, while the proton-neutron interaction across two major shells is derived from the monopole based universal interaction plus the M3Y spin-orbit force. The present Hamiltonian can reproduce well the experimental data available in this region, including one-neutron separation energies, level energies and the experimental $B(E2)$ values of isomers in $^{134,136,138}$Sn, $^{130}$Cd, and $^{128}$Pd. New isomers are predicted in this region, $e.g.$ in $^{135}$Sn, $^{131}$Cd, $^{129}$Pd, $^{132,134}$In and $^{130}$Ag, in which almost no excited states are known experimentally yet. In the odd-odd $^{132,134}$In and $^{130}$Ag, the predicted very long $E2$ life-times of the low-lying $5^{-}$ states are discussed, demanding more information on the related proton-neutron interaction. The low-lying states of $^{132}$In are discussed in connection with the recently observed $γ$ rays. The predicted $19/2^{-}$ isomer in $^{129}$Pd could decay by both electromagnetic transitions and neutron emission with comparable partial life-times, making it a good candidate for neutron radioactivity, a decay mode which is yet to be discovered.

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations predict a new isomer in the neutron-rich nucleus $^{129}$Pd whose excitation energy lies above the neutron separation energy, making it a candidate for neutron radioactivity, a decay mode that has not yet been observed experimentally. The claim is hedged by the fact that the predicted neutron-decay lifetime (0.3–10 μs) is comparable to the electromagnetic decay lifetime, so the isomer could decay by either channel.

## Method
The authors constructed a new shell-model Hamiltonian (named $jj46$) for the model space consisting of four proton orbits ($\pi0f_{5/2}$, $\pi1p_{3/2}$, $\pi1p_{1/2}$, $\pi0g_{9/2}$) and six neutron orbits ($\nu1f_{7/2}$, $\nu2p_{3/2}$, $\nu2p_{1/2}$, $\nu0h_{9/2}$, $\nu1f_{5/2}$, $\nu0i_{13/2}$). Proton-proton and neutron-neutron interactions were taken from existing CD-Bonn $G$ matrix results, while the proton-neutron interaction across two major shells was derived from the monopole-based universal interaction (V$_{MU}$) plus the M3Y spin-orbit force. Shell-model calculations were performed using the OXBASH code, and neutron-emission lifetimes were computed using the two-potential approach with a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential.

## Key numbers
- One-neutron separation energy $S_n$ for $^{129}$Pd: 1.524 MeV (calculated, $jj46$)
- Excitation energy of the predicted $19/2^{-}$ isomer in $^{129}$Pd: 1.903 MeV
- Calculated $E2$ mean lifetime ($\tau_{E2}$) for the $19/2^{-}$ isomer in $^{129}$Pd: 0.955 μs
- Predicted neutron-emission lifetime from the $19/2^{-}$ isomer of $^{129}$Pd to the ground state of $^{128}$Pd: 0.3–10 μs (for decay energy $Q_n$ = 0.35–0.50 MeV)
- Calculated $B(E2)$ value for the $8^+ \rightarrow 6^+$ transition in $^{128}$Pd: 12.50 $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 8.43(0.25) $e^2$fm$^4$)
- Calculated $B(E2)$ value for the $8^+ \rightarrow 6^+$ transition in $^{130}$Cd: 59.07 $e^2$fm$^4$ (experimental: 66(13) or 50(10) $e^2$fm$^4$)
- Calculated $\tau_{E2}$ for the $5^-$ isomer in $^{132}$In: 345.7 μs (transition energy 0.067 MeV)
- Calculated $\tau_{E2}$ for the $5^-$ isomer in $^{134}$In: 13.28 μs (transition energy 0.074 MeV)
- Calculated $\tau_{E2}$ for the $5^-$ isomer in $^{130}$Ag: 22.53 μs (transition energy 0.072 MeV)

## Context
This work extends shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei around the doubly magic $^{132}$Sn, following experimental isomeric spectroscopy that confirmed the persistence of the $N=82$ shell gap down to $^{128}$Pd (Jungclaus 2007, Gorska 2009, Watanabe 2013) and the observation of seniority isomers in $^{134,136,138}$Sn (Simpson 2014). The predicted neutron-decaying isomer in $^{129}$Pd would provide the first example of neutron radioactivity, analogous to how the high-spin isomer in $^{53}$Co provided the first example of proton radioactivity (Jackson 1970). The results also connect to recent $\gamma$-ray observations in $^{132}$In from $\beta$-delayed neutron emission of $^{133}$Cd (Jungclaus 2016), and the authors call for more experimental data on the proton-neutron interaction in this region.
