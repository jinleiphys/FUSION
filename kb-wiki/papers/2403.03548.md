# Mass and decay width of $T_{ccs}$ from symmetries

> We analyze the mass and width of the doubly heavy tetraquark $T_{ccs}$ composed of a heavy diquark and a light-quark cloud with strangeness with assuming that a color antitriplet heavy diquark is a dominant component of the doubly charmed tetraquarks $T_{cc}$ and $T_{ccs}$. We construct an effective Lagrangian for masses of heavy hadrons based on the superflavor symmetry between the doubly heavy tetraquarks and the singly heavy baryons by including the terms that simultaneously break the heavy-quark and light-flavor symmetries, and predict the mass of $T_{ccs}$ as $M(T_{ccs}) = 4047\pm11$\,MeV. The comparison of this prediction with future experimental observation will give a clue to understand the color structure of the heavy diquark. We also predict the mass of $Ω_{cc}$ as $M(Ω_{cc}) = 3706^{+14}_{-15}\,$MeV. We next calculate the decay width of $T_{ccs}$, based on solely the light-flavor symmetry, as $Γ(T_{ccs}) = 42\pm 24$\,MeV.

## Key claim
The authors predict the mass of the doubly heavy tetraquark \(T_{ccs}\) as \(4047\pm11\) MeV and its decay width as \(42\pm 24\) MeV, under the assumption that a color antitriplet heavy diquark is the dominant component of the doubly charmed tetraquarks \(T_{cc}\) and \(T_{ccs}\). The prediction relies on an effective Lagrangian that includes terms breaking both heavy-quark and light-flavor symmetries, and the comparison with future experimental observation is intended to clarify the color structure of the heavy diquark.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for heavy hadron masses based on superflavor symmetry between doubly heavy tetraquarks and singly heavy baryons, including correction terms that break heavy-quark flavor symmetry (proportional to \(1/m_Q\)) and SU(3) light-flavor symmetry (via a spurion field for light-quark masses). They also incorporate a mixing term between color \(\bar{3}\) and color \(6\) diquark configurations for the \(T_{cc}\) system. The mass of \(T_{ccs}\) is then predicted by applying improved mass relations that use the experimental mass of \(T_{cc}^{+}\) as input, and the decay width is calculated using an effective Lagrangian approach based solely on light-flavor SU(3) symmetry.

## Key numbers
- Predicted mass of \(T_{ccs}\): \(M(T_{ccs}) = 4047 \pm 11\) MeV.
- Predicted mass of \(\Omega_{cc}\): \(M(\Omega_{cc}) = 3706^{+14}_{-15}\) MeV.
- Predicted decay width of \(T_{ccs}\): \(\Gamma(T_{ccs}) = 42 \pm 24\) MeV.
- Experimental mass of \(T_{cc}^{+}\) used as input: 3874.74 MeV (rounded to 3875 MeV in the analysis).
- Spin-averaged mass difference between \(D_s\) and \(D\) mesons: 103 MeV; between \(B_s\) and \(B\) mesons: 90 MeV.
- Mass difference between \(\Xi_c\) and \(\Lambda_c\): 183 MeV; between \(\Xi_b\) and \(\Lambda_b\): 175 MeV.
- Simple mass relation prediction for \(T_{cc}^{+}\) (without corrections): 3970 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 3875 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the application of superflavor symmetry, originally proposed by Georgi (1990) and Savage (1990), to doubly heavy tetraquarks, building on the diquark picture introduced by Jaffe (1976). It addresses discrepancies found in simple mass relations when compared to experimental data for \(T_{cc}^{+}\) from LHCb (2021), and improves upon earlier mass relations derived by Ma (2017). The predictions for \(T_{ccs}\) and \(\Omega_{cc}\) provide testable targets for future experiments, and the framework enables further study of the color configuration of heavy diquarks in exotic hadrons.
