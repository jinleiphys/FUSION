# From microphysics to dynamics of magnetars

> MeV-scale magnetic fields in the interiors of magnetars suppress the pairing of neutrons and protons in the $S$-wave state. In the case of a neutron condensate the suppression is the consequence of the Pauli-paramagnetism of the neutron gas, i.e., the alignment of the neutron spins along the magnetic field. The proton $S$-wave pairing is suppressed because of the Landau diamagnetic currents of protons induced by the field. The Ginzburg-Landau and BCS theories of the critical magnetic fields for unpairing are reviewed. The macrophysical implications of the suppression (unpairing) of the condensates are discussed for the rotational crust-core coupling in magnetars and the neutrino-dominated cooling era of their thermal evolution.

## Key claim

MeV-scale magnetic fields in the range of 10^16 to 10^17 Gauss can suppress S-wave nucleon pairing in magnetar interiors, with the suppression of neutron pairing driven by Pauli paramagnetic spin alignment and proton pairing driven by Landau diamagnetic currents, and this unpairing has observable consequences for rotational coupling and thermal evolution.

## Method

The authors review Ginzburg-Landau theory for proton-neutron superfluid mixtures to compute the critical field H_c2 for proton superconductivity, and solve BCS equations for spin-polarized neutron matter using a phase-shift-equivalent nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain the critical unpairing field for neutron S-wave condensates. They then compute the dynamical coupling time scale between the neutron superfluid and normal plasma by balancing the Magnus force against frictional scattering of unpaired protons off neutron vortex-core quasiparticles, using the relaxation time derived from the neutron-proton scattering cross section. The neutrino emissivity modifications are evaluated by analyzing the phase-space opening for the direct Urca process and single-nucleon bremsstrahlung under strong magnetic fields, and by considering the removal of pair-breaking-formation processes when condensates are destroyed.

## Key numbers

- The critical unpairing field for the neutron S-wave condensate is B > 2.6 × 10^17 G, above which the neutron fluid becomes non-superfluid
- The maximum of H_c2 for protons occurs at density n_b = 0.5 n_0, where n_0 is nuclear saturation density
- The dynamical coupling time scale ranges from several minutes at the crust-core boundary to a few seconds in the high-density core for a rotation frequency Ω = 1 Hz and temperatures T = 0.01 to 0.05 MeV
- The drag-to-lift ratio ζ is approximately 0.2 in the low-density outer core and 0.4 in the high-density inner core
- The relaxation time for proton scattering off neutron vortex-core quasiparticles scales as τ^{-1} ∝ T exp[-ε_{1/2}^0/T], where ε_{1/2}^0 = πΔ_n^2/(4ε_Fn)
- The neutrino emissivity via the Urca process is computed at temperatures T = 0.01 and 0.1 MeV and density n = n_0 for the case where emission is kinematically forbidden in zero field

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior work on magnetic field effects on nucleonic pairing (Sedrakian 2014, 2015, 2016) and its consequences for neutrino radiation (Sedrakian 2015) and crust-core coupling in magnetars (Sedrakian 2016). It builds on the magnetar interpretation of outburst activity from Thompson and Duncan (1995) and reviews of magnetar interiors by Turolla (2015) and Mereghetti (2015). The work enables future numerical simulations of magnetar thermal evolution that incorporate the combined effects of unpairing on neutrino emissivities, specific heats, and Ohmic dissipation time scales, and calls for more detailed studies of macroscopic dynamics including precession damping and rotational irregularities.
