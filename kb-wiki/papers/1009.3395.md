# The quark-antiquark spectrum from upside down

> We argue that the spectra of quark-antiquark systems should better be studied from higher radial excitations and, in particular, from configurations with well-defined quantum numbers, rather than from ground states and lower radial excitations, the most suitable system being charmonium.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the spectra of quark-antiquark systems, particularly charmonium, are better studied from higher radial excitations rather than from ground states and lower radial excitations, because meson loops cause large mass shifts in the lowest-lying states that obscure the underlying confinement spectrum.

## Method

The authors employ the Resonance-Spectrum Expansion (RSE) model, which expresses the meson-meson scattering amplitude in terms of the confinement spectrum \(E_{NL}\) and coupling constants \(g_{NL}\), without specifying the confinement mechanism. They analyze experimental data from electron-positron annihilation into vector charmonium states, using a harmonic oscillator (HO) confinement spectrum with a radial level spacing of 380 MeV, and compare the predicted pattern of dominantly \(S\)-wave and \(D\)-wave states to data from the BABAR and Belle Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Radial level splitting of about 380 MeV is consistent with light and heavy meson spectra, as found for radially excited \(f_2\) mesons (see Table 3 of Ref. EPJA31p468).
- For higher vector charmonium states, the mass shift of the lower pole (dominantly \(^{3}S_1\)) becomes of the order of 150–200 MeV, whereas the higher pole (mostly \(^{3}D_1\)) shifts only a few to at most about 50 MeV away from the HO spectrum.
- The well-established \(J^{PC}=1^{--}\) \(c\bar{c}\) spectrum anno 2010 consists of \(J/\psi\), \(\psi (2S,3S,4S)\), and \(\psi (1D,2D)\) only.
- Evidence was found for further charmonium states: \(\psi(5S,6S,7S,8S)\) and \(\psi(3D,4D,5D,6D)\) in data from the BABAR Collaboration.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the Resonance-Spectrum Expansion (van Beveren and Rupp, various years) and the model of Ref. PRD21p772, which accounts for meson loops. It contradicts the common approach of fitting confinement models to low-lying states, arguing that meson loops distort those states significantly. The findings enable a more reliable extraction of the confinement spectrum from higher radial excitations, particularly in the clean \(c\bar{c}\) vector system produced in \(e^{+}e^{-}\) annihilation, and suggest that a simple harmonic oscillator spectrum with 380 MeV spacing works well for both light and heavy mesons.
