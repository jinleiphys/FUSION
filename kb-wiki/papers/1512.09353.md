# Thermalization of mini-jets in a quark-gluon plasma

> We present the complete physical picture for the evolution of a high-energy jet propagating through a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by analytical and numerical investigation of thermalization of the soft components of the jet. Our results support the following physical picture: the leading particle emits a significant number of mini-jets which promptly evolve via multiple branching and thus degrade into a myriad of soft gluons, with energies of the order of the medium temperature $T$. Via elastic collisions with the medium constituents, these soft gluons relax to local thermal equilibrium with the plasma over a time scale which is considerably shorter than the typical lifetime of the mini-jet. The thermalized gluons form a tail which lags behind the hard components of the jet. Together with the background QGP, they behave hydrodynamically.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the soft gluons produced by a high-energy jet's multiple branching in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) relax to local thermal equilibrium via elastic collisions over a time scale considerably shorter than the typical lifetime of the mini-jet, but this thermalization picture is explicitly derived and demonstrated for the case where the branching time is much larger than the relaxation time (t_br >> t_rel), and the full quenching of the jet (complete disappearance of the leading particle) only occurs at times t > t_br, which is not reached in LHC conditions.

## Method

The authors study the medium-induced evolution of a high-energy jet by solving a kinetic equation for the longitudinal gluon distribution f_l(t,z,p), derived from the full phase-space Boltzmann equation after integrating out transverse phase-space. The equation includes an elastic collision term in the diffusion approximation with jet quenching parameter q_hat and an inelastic collision integral for medium-induced gluon branching in the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) regime. They first analyze a simplified problem with a steady source of soft gluons at momentum p_0 ~ T, obtaining analytical solutions, and then solve the full kinetic equation numerically for a jet with initial energy E = 90 T.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time for a gluon with momentum p ~ T is t_rel = 4T^2/q_hat ~ 1/(alpha_s^2 T ln(1/alpha_s))
- The branching time for a gluon with momentum p is t_br(p) = (1/alpha_s) sqrt(p/q_hat)
- For the numerical example with E = 90 T, the branching time is t_br(E) = 9.5 t_rel
- At early time t = 0.1 t_br(E), most energy is still carried by the leading particle, with a pronounced peak at p/T = 90 and z = t
- At time t = 0.3 t_br(E) ~ 3 t_rel, a thermalized tail develops at z less than or equal to t - t_rel
- For quantitative comparison with LHC phenomenology, the authors suggest T = 0.5 GeV and t_rel = 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical picture of in-medium jet evolution developed by Baier (2000), Blaizot (2012, 2013), Fister (2014), Kurkela (2014), and Apolinario (2014), who showed that jet energy is efficiently transmitted to soft gluons via multiple quasi-democratic branchings. The paper specifically builds on the authors' own prior work (Iancu 2015) which first presented the longitudinal phase-space picture of this evolution. The results enable a qualitative comparison with di-jet asymmetry observations at the LHC (Aad 2010, Chatrchyan 2011) and suggest that the thermalized gluon tail can be described by hydrodynamics, as also studied by He (2015).
