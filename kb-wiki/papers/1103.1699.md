# Role of high-spin hyperon resonances in the reaction of $γp \to K^+ K^+ Ξ^-$

> The recent data taken by the CLAS Collaboration at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility for the reaction of $γp \to K^+ K^+ Ξ^-$ are reanalyzed within a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. The present model is an extension of the one developed in an earlier work by Nakayama, Oh, and Haberzettl [Phys. Rev. C 74, 035205 (2006)]. In particular, the role of the spin-5/2 and -7/2 hyperon resonances, which were not included in the previous model, is investigated in the present study. It is shown that the contribution of the $Σ(2030)$ hyperon having spin-7/2 and positive parity has a key role to bring the model predictions into a fair agreement with the measured data for the $K^+Ξ^-$ invariant mass distribution.

## Key claim

The spin-7/2 positive-parity hyperon resonance Σ(2030) is essential to eliminate the double-bump structure in the predicted K⁺Ξ⁻ invariant mass distribution that appeared in the earlier model of Nakayama, Oh, and Haberzettl (2006), bringing the model into fair agreement with CLAS data; however, the fitted coupling constants should be regarded as rough estimates because many hyperon resonances can contribute.

## Method

The authors extended a relativistic meson-exchange model at tree level (Nakayama, Oh, and Haberzettl, 2006) for the reaction γp → K⁺K⁺Ξ⁻ by adding high-spin hyperon resonances (spin-5/2 and -7/2) not included previously. Using effective Lagrangians and Rarita-Schwinger formalism for the higher-spin fields, they analyzed the on-shell production amplitude to identify which spin-parity combinations contribute significantly, then included the Σ(2030) (Jᴾ = 7/2⁺) as the only viable candidate near 2 GeV. The model parameters were adjusted to reproduce the CLAS data on invariant mass and differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The Σ(2030) hyperon has spin 7/2, positive parity, mass ≈ 2030 MeV, and decay width ≈ 180 MeV (four-star PDG rating).
- The product of coupling constants for Σ(2030) was set to g_{NΣK} g_{ΞΣK} = −4.2.
- The baryonic form factor cutoff for spin-7/2 hyperons was set to Λ_B = 800 MeV.
- The model of Nakayama, Oh, and Haberzettl (2006) predicted a double-bump structure in the K⁺Ξ⁻ invariant mass distribution near 2 GeV that was not present in the CLAS data; the Σ(2030) contribution fills the valley between the bumps.
- The Σ(2030) contribution becomes prominent at incident photon energies above 3.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the model of Nakayama, Oh, and Haberzettl (2006) for Ξ photoproduction, which had included only spin-1/2 and -3/2 hyperon resonances and predicted a double-bump structure in the K⁺Ξ⁻ invariant mass distribution that disagreed with CLAS data (CLAS Collaboration, 2007). The present study shows that the Σ(2030) hyperon resonance, with spin 7/2⁺, resolves this discrepancy. The formalism developed for high-spin resonances can be applied to analyze other reaction processes, and the results motivate more precise experimental data and more sophisticated models to extract reliable resonance parameters.
