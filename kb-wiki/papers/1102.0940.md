# High-Performance Algorithm for Calculating Non-Spurious Spin- and Parity-Dependent Nuclear Level Densities

> A new high-performance algorithm for calculating the spin- and parity-dependent shell model nuclear level densities using methods of statistical spectroscopy in the proton-neutron formalism was recently proposed. When used in valence spaces that cover more than one major harmonic oscillator shell, this algorithm mixes the genuine intrinsic states with spurious center-of-mass excitations. In this paper we present an advanced algorithm, based on the recently proposed statistical moments method, that eliminates the spurious states. Results for unnatural parity states of several sd-shell nuclei are presented and compared with those of exact shell model calculations and experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper presents an advanced algorithm that eliminates spurious center-of-mass excitations from spin- and parity-dependent nuclear level densities calculated via statistical spectroscopy, but the method relies on splitting states into harmonic oscillator \(N\hbar\omega\) subspaces, which is an approximation that depends on the auxiliary harmonic oscillator frequency parameter.

## Method
The algorithm calculates configuration centroids and widths of the nuclear Hamiltonian in restricted \(N\hbar\omega\) subspaces using traces of the Hamiltonian and its square, with intermediate-state sums restricted by excitation quanta. These partial level densities for each \(N\hbar\omega\) subspace are then combined via a recursive subtraction formula to remove spurious center-of-mass contributions, yielding non-spurious spin- and parity-dependent level densities. The method was implemented for \(1\hbar\omega\) subspaces of unnatural parity states in several \(sd\)-shell nuclei using the Warburton-Brown (WBT) interaction.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{20}\)Ne in the \(s-p-sd-pf\) model space, ground state energy \(E_{\text{g.s.}} = -184.2\) MeV (calculated with shell model, WBT interaction, \(0\hbar\omega\) subspace) and cut-off parameter \(\eta = 2.8\).
- For \(^{22}\)Na, ground state energy \(E_{\text{g.s.}} = -201.2\) MeV; for \(^{22}\)Mg, \(E_{\text{g.s.}} = -202.3\) MeV (both with WBT interaction, \(\eta = 2.8\)).
- For \(^{26}\)Al, ground state energy \(E_{\text{g.s.}} = -250.3\) MeV; for \(^{28}\)Si, \(E_{\text{g.s.}} = -285.0\) MeV (both with WBT interaction, \(0\hbar\omega\) subspace).
- The Lawson method used \(\beta = 5\) to shift spurious states; for \(A=22\) this corresponds to an energy shift of 110 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the statistical spectroscopy moments method previously developed by the authors (Sen'kov, Horoi, Zelevinsky, 2010) and others (Alhassid, 2000; Langanke, 1998) for calculating shell model level densities. It directly addresses the problem of spurious center-of-mass excitations that arise when valence spaces span more than one major harmonic oscillator shell, which was not handled by earlier algorithms. The technique enables reliable prediction of spin- and parity-dependent nuclear level densities for unstable nuclei relevant to the \(rp\)-process, and can be extended to realistic mean-field potentials and continuum effects in future work.
