# Multi-reference many-body perturbation theory for nuclei II -- Ab initio study of neon isotopes via PGCM and IM-NCSM calculations

> The neon isotopic chain displays a rich phenomenology, ranging from clustering in the ground-state of the self-conjugate doubly open-shell stable $^{20}$Ne isotope to the physics of the island of inversion around the neutron-rich $^{30}$Ne isotope. This second (i.e. Paper II) of the present series proposes an extensive ab initio study of neon isotopes based on two complementary many-body methods, i.e. the quasi-exact in-medium no-core shell model (IM-NCSM) and the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) that is ideally suited to capturing strong static correlations associated with shape deformation and fluctuations. Calculations employ a state-of-the-art generation of chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians and evaluate the associated systematic uncertainties. In spite of missing so-called dynamical correlations, which can be added via the multi-reference perturbation theory proposed in the first paper (i.e. Paper I) of the present series, the PGCM is shown to be a suitable method to tackle the low-lying spectroscopy of complex nuclei. Still, describing the physics of the island of inversion constitutes a challenge that seems to require the inclusion of dynamical correlations. This is addressed in the third paper (i.e. Paper III) of the present series.

## Key claim
The projected generator coordinate method (PGCM), even without the dynamical correlations that will be added via multi-reference perturbation theory in Paper III, is shown to be a suitable method for the low-lying spectroscopy of complex nuclei like neon isotopes; however, describing the physics of the island of inversion around neutron-rich ³⁰Ne constitutes a challenge that appears to require the inclusion of those dynamical correlations.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio calculations for even-even neon isotopes using two complementary many-body methods: the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) and the quasi-exact in-medium no-core shell model (IM-NCSM). The PGCM mixes Bogoliubov vacua generated by constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations, with subsequent restoration of neutron number, proton number, angular momentum, and parity symmetries, using axial quadrupole, non-axial quadrupole, and axial octupole moments as generator coordinates. The IM-NCSM pre-processes the Hamiltonian via a nucleus-dependent MR-IMSRG transformation to incorporate dynamical correlations, followed by a converged NCSM diagonalization. Both methods employed chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians at NLO, N²LO, and N³LO orders with a 500 MeV cutoff, evolved via vacuum SRG to a resolution scale of 1.88 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers
- The HFB energy minimum for ²⁰Ne is found at deformation parameters (β₂ = 0.57, β₃ = 0), corresponding to a reflection-symmetric prolate shape.
- After projection, the minimum of the 0⁺ PHFB total energy surface for ²⁰Ne shifts to (β₂ = 0.75, β₃ = 0.53).
- For ²⁸Ne, the model-space uncertainty on the nominal energy of the first 0⁺ state is 830 keV (0.7%), and on the first 2⁺ state is 810 keV (0.7%); the uncertainty on the ground-state charge radius is 0.02 fm (0.7%).
- The PGCM error on the ground-state charge radius of ²⁰Ne (³⁰Ne) due to the normal-ordered two-body approximation for three-nucleon interactions is 0.7% (2.5%), and on low-lying excitation energies is 1.5% (2.6%).
- For ²⁸Ne, the energy of the first 0⁺ (2⁺) changes by 70 keV (72 keV) when increasing the basis from e_max=10 to e_max=12.

## Context
This work extends the ab initio PGCM framework, previously applied with realistic Hamiltonians pre-processed via SRG and IMSRG transformations (Frosini 2021, Yao 2019), by benchmarking it against the quasi-exact IM-NCSM approach (Gebrerufael 2016) for neon isotopes. The results are qualitatively and quantitatively close to those from relativistic multi-reference energy density functional calculations (Zhou 2016, Marević 2018), though the present ab initio calculation produces more rigid total energy surfaces, especially on the oblate side. This paper (Paper II) sets the stage for Paper III, which will present the first PGCM-PT calculations beyond zeroth order to include dynamical correlations, and for future applications to the island of inversion around ³⁰Ne.
