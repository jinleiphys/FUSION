# Pauli blocking effects and Cooper triples in three-component Fermi gases

> We investigate the effect of Pauli blocking on universal two- and three-body states in the medium. Their corresponding energies are extracted from the poles of two- and three-body in-medium scattering amplitudes. Compared to the vacuum, the binding of dimer and trimer states is reduced by the medium effects. In two-body scattering, the well-known physics of Cooper pairs is recovered. In the three-body sector, we find a new class of positive energy poles which can be interpreted as Cooper triples.

# Pauli blocking effects and Cooper triples in three-component Fermi gases

## Key claim

The authors find that in a three-component Fermi gas at zero temperature, Pauli blocking from the Fermi sea reduces the binding of both dimer and trimer states compared to vacuum, and they identify a new class of positive-energy three-body poles in the in-medium scattering amplitude that can be interpreted as "Cooper triples", three-particle bound-like states analogous to Cooper pairs, but only when the total momentum of the three particles is constrained to zero and the three-body interaction is set to zero in the calculation.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory with auxiliary dimer fields to compute two- and three-body in-medium scattering amplitudes at zero temperature, extracting pole energies from the propagators. For the two-body sector, they sum the bubble diagrams for the dimer propagator with Pauli-blocking theta functions that prevent intermediate particles from scattering into occupied Fermi-sea states. For the three-body sector, they solve an integral equation for the S-wave fermion-dimer scattering amplitude at zero total momentum, expanding in partial waves and implementing the Pauli-blocking boundary conditions numerically with a momentum cutoff.

## Key numbers

* For two-body poles at zero total momentum and Fermi momentum \(k_F = 0.7/l_0\), the positive-energy pole in the negative scattering length region asymptotically approaches \(E = 0.49/l_0^2\) in the limit \(1/a_k \to -\infty\); for \(k_F = 1/l_0\) it approaches \(E = 1/l_0^2\).
* The maximum energy of the two-body positive-energy pole is \(k_F^2\) (e.g., \(0.49/l_0^2\) for \(k_F = 0.7/l_0\)), corresponding to the maximum kinetic energy of two particles inside the Fermi sphere.
* For three-body poles with zero total momentum and all three scattering lengths negative, the positive-energy poles disappear when the energy reaches \(1.5\,k_F^2\), which is the maximum kinetic energy of three particles inside the Fermi sphere.
* In the three-body spectrum for equal scattering lengths \(a_k = l_0\) and cutoff \(\Lambda = 250/l_0\), the less bound state disappears through threshold as \(k_F l_0\) increases, while the more deeply bound state loses only about 5% of its binding energy as \(k_F l_0\) increases from 0 to 1.

## Context

This work extends studies of Pauli blocking on Efimov trimers in three-component Fermi gases, going beyond the calculation of Nygaard (2011) which considered one fermion embedded in a Fermi sea, by treating all three fermions as embedded in the Fermi sphere. The two-body results recover the standard BCS physics of Cooper pairs (Fetter, 1971), and the three-body analysis reveals a new class of positive-energy poles interpreted as Cooper triples. The results enable future investigation of the crossover from BCS-like pairing to BEC of dimers and trimers in three-component Fermi systems, and open the question of whether Cooper triples persist when the total momentum is nonzero or when particle-hole excitations beyond Pauli blocking are included.
