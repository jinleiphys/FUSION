# The Delta-Hole model at Finite Temperature

> The spectral function of pions interacting with a gas of nucleons and Delta-33-resonances is investigated using the formalism of Thermo Field Dynamics. After a discussion of the zero Delta-width approximation at finite temperature, we take into account a constant width of the resonance. Apart from a full numerical calculation, we give analytical approximations to the pionic spectral function including such a width. They are found to be different from previous approximations, and require an increase of the effective Delta-width in hot compressed nuclear matter. The results are summarized in an effective dispersion relation for interacting pions.

## Key claim

The authors show that the quasistatic zero-width approximation for the Δ-hole model at finite temperature must be corrected by an increased effective Δ-width in hot compressed nuclear matter, and that previous analytical approximations for the pionic spectral function including a constant Δ-width are incorrect.

## Method

The pion spectral function is calculated using Thermo Field Dynamics (TFD) with a one-loop polarization function for pions coupled to nucleons and Δ₃₃-resonances, first in the quasistatic zero-width approximation and then with a constant Lorentz-type spectral function for the Δ resonance. An asymptotic expansion of the Δ spectral function to first order in its width Γ is performed, and the resulting analytical approximations are compared to full numerical calculations of the dispersion integral.

## Key numbers

- Δ₃₃-resonance vacuum width: Γ = 0.12 GeV
- Bare masses used: m_π = 0.14 GeV, M_N = 0.938 GeV, M_Δ = 1.232 GeV
- Coupling constant: f^π_{NΔ} = 2, with g′ = 0.5
- At temperatures above 0.1 GeV, the Δ-hole like excitation dissolves in the medium and cannot be considered a degree of freedom
- The crossover momentum k_⊥ where pion and Δ-hole residues are equal is given by the equation E²_π(k_⊥) = E²_{NΔ}(k_⊥)
- At 1.69 nuclear density, the quasistatic zero-width approximation yields eigenmodes ω_± that correspond well to peak locations in the full calculation

## Context

This work extends the Δ-hole model, previously used by Weise and others, to finite temperature using Thermo Field Dynamics rather than the Closed-Time-Path method. The authors correct earlier treatments by EWC (1993), KXS (1989), and XKK (1993) which used baryon density instead of the factor (ρ⁰_N − ¼ρ⁰_Δ) in the polarization function, a difference that becomes crucial at higher temperatures where 50-60% of baryon density consists of Δ's. The results enable improved effective dispersion relations for pions in hot compressed nuclear matter, relevant for heavy-ion collision theory.
