# Electromagnetic radiation at extreme angular velocity

> We consider a system rotating at extremely high angular velocity, so that its matter is found mostly at the light-cylinder. We posit that it can be described by quantum fields confined to the two-dimensional cylindrical surface rotating about its symmetry axis. We apply this model to study the electromagnetic radiation. In particular, we compute the photon spectrum emitted by the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper posits that a system rotating at extremely high angular velocity (where the light-cylinder radius \(R=1/\Omega\) is much smaller than the mean-free-path \(\ell\)) can be described by quantum fields confined to a two-dimensional cylindrical surface of radius \(R\); this model is then applied to compute the photon spectrum emitted by the quark-gluon plasma, with the caveat that the plasma is not yet in the extremely fast rotation regime and the calculation is only a back-of-the-envelope estimate.

## Method

The authors model charged scalar particles moving freely on a thin cylindrical sheet of radius \(R=1/\Omega\) rotating with angular velocity \(\Omega\) about its symmetry axis. They compute the electromagnetic radiation intensity for a single particle using scalar QED, deriving the S-matrix element and transition current for photon emission, then sum over final magnetic quantum numbers and integrate over phase space. For a thermal system, they assume an ideal Maxwell-Boltzmann gas and compute the photon energy spectrum and the differential photon yield in heavy-ion collisions, using the delta-function constraint to integrate over longitudinal momentum.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic frequency for photon emission from a state with magnetic quantum number \(m>0\) is \(\omega_0 \approx 2m\Omega\) in the limit \(\Omega \gg M\) (mass \(M=1\) in natural units).
- For a single particle with \(m>0\) at rest (\(p_z=0\)), the radiation intensity in the \(\Omega\gg M\) approximation is \(W = \frac{e^2\Omega^2}{8\pi}\left[ S_1(m) + \frac{1}{4}S_2(m) + m^2 J_m^2(2m)\log\frac{4m\Omega}{M} \right]\).
- For a single particle with \(m=0\), the intensity is \(W^B = \frac{e^2 M^2}{32\pi}\).
- For a single particle with \(m<0\), the intensity is \(W^C = \frac{e^2 M^4}{192\pi \Omega^2}\frac{(2|m|+1)^2}{|m|^3(|m|+1)^3}\).
- The photon spectrum has an infrared threshold at \(\omega_\text{min} = \Omega/2\).
- The photon spectrum peaks at \(\omega \sim \Omega\).
- For the heavy-ion collision estimate, the authors use \(\Omega = 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\Delta t = 10\) fm/\(c\), \(L = 10\) fm, and \(M = 150\) MeV, with degeneracy factor \(g=18\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of electromagnetic radiation from rotating systems (Buzzegoli and Tuchin 2022, 2023), which considered "relatively slowly" rotating systems in magnetic fields, and their fast rotation regime (Buzzegoli and Tuchin 2023). The paper introduces a new regime of "extremely fast rotation" where the light-cylinder radius is much smaller than the mean-free-path, building on the MIT bag model approach for boundary conditions. The results are compared to experimental data from PHENIX (2014) on photon production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that rotation must be accounted for in comprehensive phenomenological models and may help resolve the puzzle of photon excess at low momenta.
