# Out-of-equilibrium electromagnetic radiation

> We derive general formulas for photon and dilepton production rates from an arbitrary non-equilibrated medium from first principles in quantum field theory. At lowest order in the electromagnetic coupling constant, these relate the rates to the unequal-time in-medium photon polarization tensor and generalize the corresponding expressions for a system in thermodynamic equilibrium. We formulate the question of electromagnetic radiation in real time as an initial value problem and consistently describe the virtual electromagnetic dressing of the initial state. In the limit of slowly evolving systems, we recover known expressions for the emission rates and work out the first correction to the static formulas in a systematic gradient expansion. Finally, we discuss the possible application of recently developed techniques in non-equilibrium quantum field theory to the problem of electromagnetic radiation. We argue, in particular, that the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action formalism provides a powerful resummation scheme for the description of multiple scattering effects, such as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression recently discussed in the context of equilibrium QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives general formulas for photon and dilepton production rates from an arbitrarily non-equilibrated medium, generalizing equilibrium expressions by relating the rates to the unequal-time in-medium photon polarization tensor, but the derivation assumes the relative energy loss from electromagnetic emission is negligible and treats electromagnetic interactions at lowest order in perturbation theory.

## Method

The author formulates electromagnetic radiation as an initial value problem in real-time quantum field theory using Schwinger's closed time-path formalism, treating the emitting system's initial state as an arbitrary density matrix. The virtual electromagnetic dressing of the initial state is handled by introducing an adiabatic switching-on of interactions from the infinite past, which is formally equivalent to extending the Keldysh contour to negative times. At lowest order in the electromagnetic coupling constant, the photon and dilepton production rates are expressed in terms of the non-equilibrium in-medium photon polarization tensor, which is the exact unequal-time current-current correlator of the emitting system.

## Context

This work extends the real-time formulation of Boyanovsky et al. (2003) for electromagnetic radiation from equilibrium systems to arbitrarily far-from-equilibrium systems, addressing the problem of virtual radiation that plagued earlier approaches by consistently including the electromagnetic dressing of the initial state. The formalism recovers known equilibrium formulas from Feinberg (1976), Gale (1990), and McLerran (1984) in the appropriate limit, and derives first corrections to static formulas via gradient expansion for slowly evolving systems. The paper proposes that the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action formalism of Berges (2000) provides a resummation scheme for multiple scattering effects such as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression, which was recently discussed in equilibrium QCD by Arnold (2001) and Aurenche (2002).
