# Ab initio nuclear structure simulations: the speculative $^{14}$F nucleus

> We present results from ab initio No-Core Full Configuration simulations of the exotic proton-rich nucleus $^{14}$F whose first experimental observation is expected soon. The calculations with JISP16 NN interaction are performed up to the $N_{\max}=8$ basis space. The binding energy is evaluated using an extrapolation technique. This technique is generalized to excitation energies, verified in calculations of $^6$Li and applied to $^{14}$B, the $^{14}$F mirror, for which some data are available.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first ab initio prediction for the binding energy and excitation spectrum of the yet-unobserved proton-rich nucleus $^{14}$F, using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction and extrapolation techniques; the authors hedge that significant differences between their predictions and future experimental data would indicate deficiencies in the JISP16 interaction or the role of neglected three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors performed No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations with the bare JISP16 NN interaction in harmonic oscillator basis spaces up to $N_{\max}=8$, containing 1,990,061,078 basis states for $^{14}$F. They applied the No-Core Full Configuration (NCFC) approach, extrapolating results from successive finite basis spaces to the infinite basis space limit using two methods: a global extrapolation (method A) over four basis spaces and five oscillator parameter values, and a fixed-$\hbar\Omega$ extrapolation (method B) over three successive basis spaces. The same extrapolation technique was generalized to excitation energies by extrapolating total energies of excited states independently and taking differences with the ground state.

## Key numbers

- $^{14}$F binding energy: $72 \pm 4$ MeV from combining both extrapolation methods, agreeing with AME03 extrapolation of $73.3 \pm 0.4$ MeV
- $^{14}$F ground state energy from extrapolation A: $-70.9 \pm 3.6$ MeV; from extrapolation B: $-73.1 \pm 3.7$ MeV
- $^{14}$B binding energy: $86 \pm 4$ MeV from NCFC, compared to experimental value of $85.423$ MeV
- $^{13}$O binding energy: $77 \pm 3$ MeV from NCFC, compared to experimental value of $75.556$ MeV
- Predicted energy difference between $^{14}$F and $^{13}$O ground states: $4.6$ MeV
- Predicted energy difference between $^{14}$F and $^{14}$B ground states: $13.5$ MeV
- Largest NCSM calculation for $^{14}$F used $N_{\max}=8$ basis with 1,990,061,078 states, requiring 2-3 hours on 7,626 quad-core nodes at Jaguar supercomputer

## Context

This work extends the NCFC approach previously introduced by Maris et al. (2008) and Bogner et al. (2007) to excitation energies, validated on $^{6}$Li where calculations up to $N_{\max}=16$ were performed. The results provide baseline ab initio predictions for $^{14}$F, which is expected to be observed soon at the Cyclotron Institute at Texas A&M University. The study enables direct comparison with future experimental data on the $^{13}$O + p reaction that produces $^{14}$F, and any discrepancies would inform the role of three-nucleon forces and shell structure evolution at extreme isospin.
