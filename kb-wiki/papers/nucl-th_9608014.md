# Pbar Annihilation in Au+Au at AGS Energies

> Antinucleon production in heavy ion collisions is potentially an excellent signal for unusual phenomena in hot and dense matter. However, at the low energies available at the AGS the annihilation process must be handled with care. In this Comment, we consider the case of Au + Au collisions at approximately 11 GeV/c, applying the ARC treatment of pbar production and annihilation to the analysis of experiment E878. It is apparent that classical screening introduced for Si + Au is crucial in the understanding of data obtained with the more massive projectile. Unfortunately, there seems no necessity for invoking unusual behaviour in the Au + Au system.

## Key claim

Classical screening of the annihilation cross-section, previously introduced for Si+Au collisions, is essential for understanding antiproton yields in Au+Au at AGS energies; there is no need to invoke unusual phenomena in the Au+Au system.

## Method

The authors used the ARC (A Relativistic Cascade) model to simulate antiproton production and annihilation in Au+Au collisions at 11 GeV/c, applying a time-delay screening mechanism that permits annihilation only after complete overlap of the nucleon and antinucleon. They compared two screening implementations: the original "old" complete-overlap screening and a new volume-overlap screening where annihilation probability is proportional to the overlap volume. Calculations were performed for impact parameter b < 5 fm to approximate the trigger used in experiment E878.

## Key numbers

- Normalization factor of approximately 30 between antiproton yields for pure production (with rescattering) and production plus unadulterated annihilation without screening
- Variance between "old" complete-overlap screening and new volume-overlap screening is about 35%, taken as an extreme limit for theoretical uncertainty
- Free-space annihilation cross sections allow annihilation at separations in excess of 3 fm, motivating the screening modification

## Context

This Comment responds to the E878 experiment (Beavis et al., 1995) which reported antiproton yields in Au+Au collisions and compared them to RQMD and ARC calculations. The work extends the screening approach developed for Si+Au collisions (Kahana et al., 1993; Pang et al., 1993) to the heavier Au+Au system. The results suggest that antiprotons cannot serve as a clean signal for unusual behavior at these low energies, and the authors recommend measurements of the pp → pbar cross section and more comprehensive data from AGS experiments E864 and E866 to further exploit the modeling successes.
