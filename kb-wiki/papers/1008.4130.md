# Effective 3-Body Interaction for Mean-Field and Density-Functional Theory

> Density functionals for nuclei usually include an effective 3-body interaction that depends on a fractional power of the density. Using insights from the many-body theory of the low-density two-component Fermi gas, we consider a new, nonlocal, form for the energy functional that is consistent with the Fock space representation of interaction operators. In particular, there is a unique spatially nonlocal generalization of the contact form of the interaction that preserves the density-to-the-seven-thirds dependence required by the many-body theory. We calculate the ground state energies for particles in a harmonic trap using the nonlocal induced 3-body interaction, and compare them to numerically accurate Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations. Using no free parameters, we find that a nonlocality in the space domain provides a better description of the weak-coupling regime than the local-density approximation.

## Key claim

A nonlocal, finite-range effective 3-body interaction, derived from the many-body theory of the low-density two-component Fermi gas, provides a better description of the weak-coupling regime in finite systems than the local-density approximation, using no free parameters.

## Method

The authors derived a spatially nonlocal 3-body interaction operator from the second-order Lee-Yang expansion of the ground-state energy of a low-density Fermi gas, matching its uniform-matter limit to the known \(\rho^{7/3}\) density dependence. They then applied this operator in a variational density-functional calculation for 8 fermions in a harmonic trap, comparing the resulting ground-state energies to numerically accurate Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) simulations.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless coefficient \(C\) in the nonlocal 3-body interaction is \(C = \frac{64 \pi (11 - 2\ln2)}{3 (92 - 27\ln3)} \approx 10.336\).
- For a system of 8 trapped fermions at scattering length \(a = 0.2082 \sqrt{\hbar/m \omega}\), the ground-state energy from the new nonlocal interaction (DFT with \(H_3\) NLO) is \(19.465 \, \hbar\omega\), compared to the GFMC result of \(19.485(1) \, \hbar\omega\).
- The local-density approximation using the Lee-Yang term (DFT LY LO + NLO) gives \(19.436 \, \hbar\omega\) for the same system.

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang (1957) expansion for the low-density Fermi gas to a nonlocal form suitable for density-functional theory, addressing the problem of constructing functionals from first principles as formulated at a 2005 INT workshop. It provides a microscopically derived alternative to the commonly used local-density approximation in Skyrme and Gogny functionals, enabling more accurate treatment of finite systems without free parameters, though at higher computational cost (order \(N^3 N_r^2\) versus \(N N_r\) for local functionals).
