# Path-integral formula for local thermal equilibrium

> We develop a complete path-integral formulation of relativistic quantum fields in local thermal equilibrium, which brings about the emergence of thermally induced curved spacetime. The resulting action is shown to have full diffeomorphism invariance and gauge invariance in thermal spacetime with imaginary-time independent backgrounds. This leads to the notable symmetry properties of emergent thermal spacetime: Kaluza-Klein gauge symmetry, spatial diffeomorphism symmetry, and gauge symmetry. A thermodynamic potential in local thermal equilibrium, or the so-called Masseiu-Planck functional, is identified as a generating functional for conserved currents such as the energy-momentum tensor and the electric current.

## Key claim

The paper develops a complete path-integral formulation of relativistic quantum fields in local thermal equilibrium using the local Gibbs distribution, showing that the resulting Massieu-Planck functional can be written as a quantum field theory in an emergent curved spacetime whose structure is completely determined by the local thermodynamic variables, with the caveat that the formalism is restricted to imaginary-time independent backgrounds.

## Method

The author constructs the local Gibbs distribution by maximizing information entropy under constraints on conserved charge densities (energy-momentum density and charge density) on a spacelike hypersurface, using the Arnowitt-Deser-Misner (ADM) decomposition of spacetime to maintain manifest covariance. The Massieu-Planck functional is then expressed as a path integral in an emergent curved spacetime geometry, and its variational derivatives with respect to background metric and gauge fields are shown to yield expectation values of the energy-momentum tensor and electric current. The symmetry properties of the resulting action are analyzed for scalar fields, Dirac fields, and gauge fields.

## Key numbers

- The inverse temperature parameter is defined as $\beta^\mu(x) = \beta(x) u^\mu(x)$ with $u^\mu u_\mu = -1$, where $u^\mu$ is the fluid four-velocity
- The chemical potential parameter is $\nu(x) = \beta(x) \mu(x)$
- The ADM metric takes the form $g_{\mu\nu} = \begin{pmatrix} -N^2 + N_i N^i & N_j \\ N_i & \gamma_{ij} \end{pmatrix}$ with lapse function $N(x) > 0$ and shift vector $N^\mu(x)$
- The hypersurface volume element is $d\Sigma_\pt = d^{d-1} \bm{x} \sqrt{\gamma}$ where $\gamma = \det \gamma_{ij}$
- The spacetime volume element is $\int d^d x \sqrt{-g} = \int d^d x N \sqrt{\gamma}$

## Context

This work extends the imaginary-time (Matsubara) formalism for global thermal equilibrium to local thermal equilibrium, providing a microscopic justification for the hydrostatic generating functional method developed by Banerjee (2012) and Jensen (2012). The path-integral formulation enables calculation of thermodynamic and transport properties of locally thermalized matter from underlying quantum field theories, and provides a starting point to derive hydrodynamic equations from microscopic theories, complementing the hydrodynamic description of quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collision experiments as reviewed by Kolb (2003), Romatschke (2009), Teaney (2009), and Gale (2013).
