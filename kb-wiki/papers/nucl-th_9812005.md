# Modeling Nonperturbative QCD for Mesons and Couplings

> We discuss aspects of a covariant QCD modeling of meson physics by illustrating applications to several coupling constants and form factors. In particular, we cover the $ρππ$ and $π^0 γγ$ interactions, the $ρ$ contribution to the pion charge radius, and $πNN$ coupling.

## Key claim

A covariant QCD modeling approach based on Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, with parameters fixed by soft chiral observables, can capture dominant infrared physics for meson couplings and form factors without further adjustment; however, the pseudovector component of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is found to be important at the level of about 25% and above for quantities such as \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\) and \(g_{\pi NN}\), and its omission would lead to significant overestimates.

## Method

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) is solved using a phenomenological infrared-enhanced effective gluon propagator and dressed quark-gluon vertex, producing a confined quark propagator with no mass-shell pole. Meson bound states are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in ladder/rainbow approximation with a separable ansatz for the kernel, preserving the axial Ward-Takahashi identity and Goldstone's theorem. The resulting Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the pion and rho meson are then used in skeleton graph expansions to compute the \(\rho\pi\pi\) vertex, the \(\pi^0\gamma\gamma\) transition, the rho contribution to the pion charge form factor, and the \(\pi NN\) coupling via a mean-field chiral quark-meson model of the nucleon.

## Key numbers

* \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 6.28\) (predicted) compared to experimental value 6.05 from \(\rho\rightarrow\pi\pi\) decay width.
* In the \(\rho\pi\pi\) calculation, the pseudoscalar (\(\gamma_5\)) pion covariant contributes 171% and the pseudovector (\(\gamma_5\gamma\cdot Q\)) contributes -71%; the \(\gamma_\mu\) rho covariant contributes 94.5%.
* The rho pole contribution to the pion charge radius squared is \((r_\pi^{\text{pole}})^2 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^2\), obtained with \(f_{\rho\pi\pi}(0) \approx 0.5\) and \(f_{\rho\gamma}(0) \approx 0.65\).
* The total pion charge radius from the present approach is \(0.47\ \text{fm}^2\) (non-resonant impulse contribution \(0.31\ \text{fm}^2\) plus rho pole \(0.16\ \text{fm}^2\)), compared to the experimental value \(0.44\ \text{fm}^2\).
* For the \(\pi NN\) coupling, including both pseudoscalar and pseudovector pion components gives \(g_{\pi NN} \approx 11\); using only the pseudoscalar component gives \(g_{\pi NN} \approx 17\), compared to the empirical value 13.4.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to nonperturbative QCD modeling, previously applied to pion and kaon observables by Maris and Roberts (1997) and to the \(\rho\pi\pi\) coupling by Mitchell and Tandy (1997) and others. It demonstrates that the pseudovector component of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, often neglected in earlier studies, produces corrections of 20-30% for quantities such as \(m_\pi\), \(f_\pi\), \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\), and \(g_{\pi NN}\). The results enable future calculations of the large-momentum behavior of form factors such as \(\gamma\pi\pi\) and \(\pi\gamma\gamma\), which will require inclusion of both types of pseudovector amplitudes evident in the full pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.
