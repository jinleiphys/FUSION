# Dynamical fluctuations in critical regime and across the 1st order phase transition

> In this proceeding, we study the dynamical evolution of the sigma field within the framework of Langevin dynamics. We find that, as the system evolves in the critical regime, the magnitudes and signs of the cumulants of sigma field, $C_{3}$ and $C_{4}$, can be dramatically different from the equilibrated ones due to the memory effects near $T_c$. For the dynamical evolution across the 1st order phase transition boundary, the supercooling effect leads the sigma field to be widely distributed in the thermodynamical potential, which largely enhances the cumulants $C_3, \ C_4$, correspondingly.

## Key claim
The dynamical evolution of the sigma field near the QCD critical point can produce cumulants C3 and C4 whose signs and magnitudes differ dramatically from equilibrium predictions due to memory effects, while supercooling across the first-order phase transition boundary enhances cumulants C2-C4 beyond their equilibrium maxima.

## Method
The authors solved the Langevin equation for the sigma field using an effective potential from the linear sigma model with constituent quarks, treating the damping coefficient eta as a free parameter with white noise input. The heat bath was evolved along trajectories of constant chemical potential (200 MeV for crossover near the critical point, 240 MeV for first-order transition) with a Hubble-like temperature decrease T(t)/T0 = (t/t0)^(-0.45). Initial sigma field configurations were constructed from the probability distribution P[sigma] ~ exp(-epsilon(sigma)/T), and cumulants were computed from the moments of the spatially-averaged sigma field over many events.

## Key numbers
- At t = 12 fm/c along the crossover trajectory (mu = 200 MeV), both C3 and C4 show a positive sign, opposite to the sign of the equilibrium values at that time.
- The temperature evolution follows T(t)/T0 = (t/t0)^(-0.45) for the Hubble-like cooling.
- The effective quark mass at T=0 is approximately 310 MeV, corresponding to a proton mass in vacuum of about 930 MeV, obtained with coupling g = 3.3.
- The damping coefficient eta was treated as a free parameter with three constant values shown in the figures.

## Context
This work extends the non-equilibrium studies of Mukherjee (2015) on cumulants of the sigma field using the Fokker-Planck equation, but moves beyond their zero-mode approximation by solving the full Langevin equation with spatial information. The authors previously calculated equilibrium critical fluctuations of net protons (Jiang 2015) that over-predicted C2 and C3 data; the dynamical approach presented here has the potential to resolve that discrepancy. The results enable future coupling of the full space-time sigma field evolution to hydrodynamic freeze-out surfaces for direct comparison with STAR collaboration data on net-proton cumulants from Au+Au collisions at 7.7 to 200 GeV.
