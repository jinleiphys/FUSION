# Alpha-particle condensation in 16O via a full four-body OCM calculation

> In order to explore the 4 α-particle condensate state in 16O, we solve a full four-body equation of motion based on the 4 αOCM (Orthogonality Condition Model) in a large 4 αmodel space spanned by Gaussian basis functions. A full spectrum up to the 0_6^+ state is reproduced consistently with the lowest six 0^+ states of the experimental spectrum. The 0^+_6 state is obtained at about 2 MeV above the 4 αbreakup threshold and has a dilute density structure, with a radius of about 5 fm. The state has an appreciably large αcondensate fraction of 61 %, and a large component of α+12C(0_2^+) configuration, both features being reliable evidence for this state to be of 4 αcondensate nature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $0_6^+$ state in $^{16}$O, located about 2 MeV above the 4-alpha breakup threshold, is the 4-alpha condensate state, but this identification is tentative and based on the state having a dilute density structure, a large alpha condensate fraction of 61%, and a large component of the alpha + $^{12}$C($0_2^+$) configuration.

## Method

The authors solved a full four-body equation of motion using the Orthogonality Condition Model (OCM) for 4 alpha particles, with the model space spanned by 5120 Gaussian basis functions via the Gaussian expansion method. The Hamiltonian included two-body, Coulomb, three-body, and four-body effective alpha-alpha interactions, with the Pauli exclusion operator eliminating forbidden states between two alpha particles. The wave function was expanded in Jacobi coordinates and the coefficients were determined by the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle.

## Key numbers

- The $0_6^+$ state is obtained at about 2 MeV above the 4-alpha breakup threshold.
- The $0_6^+$ state has a radius of about 5 fm (calculated rms radius of 5.0 fm with SW force, 5.6 fm with MHN force).
- The $0_6^+$ state has an alpha condensate fraction of 61%.
- The ground state ($0_1^+$) has a calculated rms radius of 2.7 fm, consistent with the experimental value of $2.71 \pm 0.02$ fm.
- The $0_2^+$ state has a calculated monopole transition matrix element $M(E0)$ of 4.1 fm$^2$ (SW) or 3.9 fm$^2$ (MHN), compared to the experimental value of $3.55 \pm 0.21$ fm$^2$.
- The total alpha decay width of the $0_6^+$ state is calculated as 50 keV (experimental value: 166 keV).
- The $0_4^+$ state has a calculated width of about 150 keV (experimental: 600 keV), and the $0_5^+$ state about 50 keV (experimental: 185 keV).

## Context

This work extends previous OCM calculations for $^{16}$O that used harmonic oscillator basis sets (Suzuki 1976, Kato 1992) or the alpha + $^{12}$C Generator Coordinate Method (Baye 1977), which could not simultaneously describe both alpha + $^{12}$C cluster configurations and 4-alpha gas-like configurations. It also builds on the prediction of a 4-alpha condensate state by Tohsaki et al. (2001) using a microscopic condensate wave function that could not reproduce the alpha + $^{12}$C structures. The present calculation enables the identification of the $0_6^+$ state as the 4-alpha condensate analog of the Hoyle state in $^{12}$C, and calls for further experimental confirmation and independent theoretical calculations.
