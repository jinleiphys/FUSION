# Multiple rescattering effects in the hard knockout reaction $\mathbf{^2\mbox{H}(p,2p)n}$

> The interaction of a proton with a deuteron is the simplest nuclear reaction. However, it allows the study of precursors of nuclear medium effects such as initial-state/final-state interactions (ISI/FSI). In case of hard proton knockout, the deviation of ISI/FSI from the 'standard' values may carry a signal of color transparency. In this regard, it is important to define the 'standard' as precisely as possible. This work continues previous studies within the framework of the Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA). The focus is on processes where the participating protons experience multiple soft rescattering on the spectator neutron. It is shown that correct treatment of deviations of the trajectories of outgoing protons from the longitudinal direction leads to a significant modification of partial amplitudes with soft rescattering of two outgoing protons and non-vanishing amplitudes with rescattering of incoming and outgoing protons. The new treatment of multiple rescattering is important in kinematics with a forward spectator neutron.

## Key claim

The paper shows that correctly accounting for deviations of outgoing proton trajectories from the longitudinal direction significantly modifies the partial amplitudes for double soft rescattering of two outgoing protons and produces non-vanishing amplitudes for rescattering of incoming and outgoing protons, which is important for kinematics with a forward spectator neutron.

## Method

The author rederives the multiple soft rescattering amplitudes within the Generalized Eikonal Approximation (GEA) by treating the soft momentum transfers relative to the actual directions of the asymptotic particle momenta, rather than relative to the beam direction. The longitudinal momentum transfers (Δ factors) are calculated iteratively using the average transverse momentum transfer squared from the slope parameter of the pn elastic amplitude, and the infinite series of ladder rescattering diagrams for two outgoing protons is summed to produce renormalized double rescattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum range considered: p_lab = 6–75 GeV/c
- Transverse momentum of spectator neutron up to about 0.4 GeV/c where deuteron wave function uncertainties do not sensitively change the nuclear transparency ratio
- The slope parameter B_pn of the pn elastic amplitude is used to compute the average transverse momentum transfer squared as ⟨k_t²⟩ = 1/B_pn(|p_j|)
- The renormalization factor for the double rescattering amplitude is given by ℱ = (1 − Γ₃(̃b)/2) / (1 − Γ₃(̃b)Γ₄(̃̃b)/4)

## Context

This work extends previous GEA-based studies of the d(p,2p)n reaction by Frankfurt (1996) and Larionov (2022), which treated soft momentum transfers relative to the beam direction or used the old method for double rescattering. The new treatment resolves the disappearance of partial amplitudes with rescattering of incoming and outgoing protons that occurred in the earlier approach. The results enable more precise definition of the "standard" initial-state/final-state interactions against which color transparency signals can be identified in hard knockout reactions.
