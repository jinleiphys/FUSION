# Exactly Solvable Pairing Models

> Some results for two distinct but complementary exactly solvable algebraic models for pairing in atomic nuclei are presented: 1) binding energy predictions for isotopic chains of nuclei based on an extended pairing model that includes multi-pair excitations; and 2) fine structure effects among excited $0^+$ states in $N \approx Z$ nuclei that track with the proton-neutron ($pn$) and like-particle isovector pairing interactions as realized within an algebraic $sp(4)$ shell model. The results show that these models can be used to reproduce significant ranges of known experimental data, and in so doing, confirm their power to predict pairing-dominated phenomena in domains where data is unavailable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two exactly solvable algebraic pairing models can reproduce known experimental data and predict pairing-dominated phenomena in regions where data is unavailable, but the extended pairing model's predictive power for binding energies relies on an assumed quadratic form for the pairing strength parameter log(G(A)) fitted to data from the upper and lower parts of the shell.

## Method

The authors applied an extended pairing model that includes multi-pair excitations to calculate binding energies of Yb isotopes, using Nilsson single-particle energies and solving for the pairing strength G(A) that exactly reproduces experimental binding energies. They also employed an sp(4) algebraic shell model to describe isobaric analog 0+ states in medium-mass nuclei (32 ≤ A ≤ 100), fitting interaction strength parameters to experimental energies of 149 nuclei and then predicting higher-lying states and binding energies for unmeasured nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the Yb isotopes (154-181), log(G(A)) was fit to quadratic polynomials: log(G(A)) = 662.2247 - 7.7912 A + 0.0226 A^2 for even A and log(G(A)) = 716.3279 - 8.4049 A + 0.0244 A^2 for odd A
- The sp(4) model interaction strengths: G/Ω = 23.9 ± 1.1 / A, E/2Ω = -52 ± 5 / A, D = -37 ± 5 / A + (-0.24 ± 0.09), C = (32 ± 1 / A)^(1.7 ± 0.2)
- χ-statistics for sp(4) model fit to experimental energies: χ = 0.496 in the 1d3/2 shell, χ = 0.732 in the 1f7/2 shell, χ = 1.787 in the 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 major shell
- Predicted binding energy for 48Ni: E0 = 348.19 MeV, which is 0.07% greater than the Moller and Nix semi-empirical estimate
- Predicted binding energies for odd-odd nuclei: 358.62 MeV (44V), 359.34 MeV (46Mn), 357.49 MeV (48Co), 394.20 MeV (50Co)
- For even-even nuclei in the 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 shell, predicted binding energies are on average 0.05% less than the Moller and Nix semi-empirical approximation

## Context

This work extends the Richardson-Bethe ansatz approach to pairing (Richardson, 1963) by providing an exactly solvable extended pairing model applicable to well-deformed nuclei, where earlier Bethe ansatz methods required extensive numerical work for large systems. The sp(4) model builds on prior algebraic approaches (Helmers, 1961; Engel et al., 1996) and provides a microscopic framework for describing pairing in N ≈ Z nuclei, which is essential for understanding the rp-process in nucleosynthesis (Langanke, 1998). The results enable predictions of binding energies for proton-rich and neutron-rich nuclei far from stability and for higher-lying 0+ states in medium-mass nuclei where experimental data are incomplete.
