# Event-by-Event Fluctuations in Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Motivated by forthcoming experiments at RHIC and LHC, we study event-by-event fluctuations in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions in participant nucleon as well as thermal models. The calculated physical observables, including multiplicity, kaon to pion ratios, and transverse momenta agree well with recent NA49 data at the SPS, and indicate that such studies do not yet reveal the presence of new physics. Finally, we present a simple model of how a first order phase transition can be signaled by very large fluctuations.

# Event-by-Event Fluctuations in Ultrarelativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Event-by-event fluctuations in multiplicity, kaon-to-pion ratios, and transverse momentum measured by NA49 at the SPS can be explained by straightforward statistical fluctuations from participant nucleon models and thermal models, without requiring new physics; however, a first-order phase transition could produce fluctuations more than two orders of magnitude larger than those currently observed.

## Method
The authors calculate event-by-event fluctuations using two limiting models: the wounded nucleon model (WNM), where particle production occurs from individual participant nucleons without rescattering of secondaries, and the thermal model, where rescattering brings the system into local thermal equilibrium. They compare these calculations to NA49 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon, analyzing multiplicity fluctuations, kaon-to-pion ratio fluctuations, and transverse momentum fluctuations, and then present a simple droplet model for how a first-order phase transition would produce large fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- NA49 measures average charged particle multiplicity <N> ≈ 270 in the rapidity region 4 < y < 5.5 for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon
- Average number of participants <N_p> ≈ 350 estimated from Glauber theory for central collisions with impact parameter b < 3.5 fm
- Multiplicity fluctuation ω_N ≈ 2.0 calculated from the WNM, in good agreement with the experimental value ω_M^exp ≈ 2.01
- Thermal model including Bose-Einstein statistics and resonances gives ω_N^(BE+R) ≈ 1.3, and with volume fluctuations ω_N ≈ 2.1, consistent with NA49 data
- Average kaon-to-pion ratio <K/π> = 0.18 with average pion multiplicity <π> ≈ 220, yielding D ≈ 0.20 compared to the experimental value D = 0.23
- Average transverse momentum <p_t> = 377 MeV with <N> = 270, giving (σ(P_t/N)^(1/2)/<p_t> ≈ 4.3%, accounting for most of the measured fluctuation of 4.65%
- The Φ(p_t) quantity is 5 MeV in NA49 data, compared to 4.5 MeV in the Fritiof model (WNM) and approximately 6.5 MeV from HBT correlations in the thermal limit
- In the droplet model for a first-order phase transition, ω_N ≈ <N>, which is more than two orders of magnitude larger than the expected value of order unity from NA49

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior proposals for event-by-event fluctuation analyses (Baym and Heiselberg, 1998) and earlier transverse energy fluctuation studies (Baym et al., 1994) that extracted evidence for color fluctuations in the binary collision picture. It directly compares with NA49 data (1999) and addresses theoretical work on thermalization signals (Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski, 1992) and critical fluctuations at the QCD phase transition (Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak, 1998, 1999). The paper establishes baseline statistical fluctuations that must be understood before searching for phase transition signals at RHIC and LHC, and provides a simple droplet model framework for detecting first-order phase transitions through enhanced event-by-event fluctuations.
