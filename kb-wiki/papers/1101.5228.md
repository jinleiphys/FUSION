# QCD phase diagram using PNJL model with eight-quark interactions

> We present the phase diagram and the fluctuations of different conserved charges like quark number, charge and strangeness at vanishing chemical potential for the 2+1 flavor Polyakov Loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with eight-quark interaction terms using three-momentum cutoff regularisation. The main effect of the higher order interaction term is to shift the critical end point to the lower value of the chemical potential and higher value of the temperature. The fluctuations show good qualitative agreement with the lattice data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of eight-quark interaction terms in the 2+1 flavor Polyakov Loop extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model shifts the critical end point (CEP) to lower chemical potential and higher temperature, making the ratio mu_C/T_C consistent with the lattice constraint of less than or equal to 2.5, which the six-quark model alone cannot satisfy.

## Method

The authors studied the phase diagram and fluctuations of conserved charges (quark number, charge, strangeness) at vanishing chemical potential using the 2+1 flavor PNJL model with both six-quark and eight-quark interaction terms, regularized via a three-momentum cutoff. They computed the location of the critical end point for both NJL and PNJL models with each interaction type, and calculated second and fourth order cumulants (c_2, c_4) and their ratios (kurtosis) for quark number and strangeness, comparing with lattice QCD data from Cheng et al. and Gupta et al.

## Key numbers

- CEP location for PNJL8 (set1): (mu_C, T_C) = (260 MeV, 118.5 MeV)
- CEP location for PNJL8 (set2): (mu_C, T_C) = (237 MeV, 122.05 MeV)
- CEP location for PNJL6 (set1): (mu_C, T_C) = (313 MeV, 92.85 MeV)
- CEP location for NJL8 (set1): (mu_C, T_C) = (263 MeV, 61.2 MeV)
- Softest point of equation of state: (p/epsilon)_min approximately 0.06 for PNJL8, compared to approximately 0.07 for PNJL6 and approximately 0.05 from lattice data of Cheng et al.
- High temperature value of quark number susceptibility (c_2^q) reaches almost 99% of the ideal gas value

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the 2+1 flavor PNJL model with four-quark and six-quark interactions by Deb et al. and addresses the vacuum instability problem of six-quark interactions by introducing eight-quark terms, following the approach of Osipov et al. The results enable better comparison with lattice QCD constraints on the CEP location from Ejiri et al. and improve agreement with lattice data for thermodynamic quantities such as the softest point of the equation of state, though discrepancies remain in the behavior of moments at intermediate temperatures compared to lattice results from Gupta et al.
