# A new angle on an old problem: Helicity approach to neutron beta decay in the Standard Model

> We elaborate on the dichotomy between the description of the semileptonic decays of heavy hadrons on the one hand and the semileptonic decays of light hadrons such as neutron $β$ decays on the other hand. For example, almost without exception the semileptonic decays of heavy baryons are described in cascade fashion as a sequence of two two-body decays $B_1 \to B_2 + W_{\rm off-shell}$ and $W_{\rm off-shell} \to \ell + ν_\ell$ whereas neutron $β$ decays are analyzed as true three-body decays $n \to p + e^- +\bar ν_e$. Within the cascade approach it is possible to define a set of seven angular observables for polarized neutron $β$ decays as well as the longitudinal, transverse and normal polarization of the decay electron. We determine the dependence of the observables on the usual vector and axial vector form factors. In order to be able to assess the importance of recoil corrections we expand the rate and the $q^2$ averages of the observables up to NLO and NNLO in the recoil parameter $δ=(M_n-M_p)/(M_n+M_p)= 0.689\cdot 10^{-3}$. Remarkably, we find that the rate and three of the four parity conserving polarization observables that we analyze are protected from NLO recoil corrections when the second class current contributions are set to zero.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutron beta decay can be analyzed using a cascade approach (as a sequence of two two-body decays \(n \to p + W_{\rm off-shell}\) and \(W_{\rm off-shell} \to e^- + \bar\nu_e\)) rather than the traditional three-body decay treatment, enabling the definition of a larger set of angular observables; however, the physical content of both approaches is equivalent and can be converted into one another by appropriate boosts.

## Method

The authors apply the helicity formalism to the quasi-two-body decay \(n \to p + W_{\rm off-shell}\), constructing the double spin density matrix for the hadronic transition and combining it with the leptonic decay of the off-shell \(W\) boson via Wigner \(d\)-functions. They expand the total rate and the \(q^2\)-averaged observables up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the recoil parameter \(\delta = (M_n - M_p)/(M_n + M_p)\), using the standard set of six vector and axial-vector form factors \(F_i^{V/A}\) with the conserved vector current (CVC), partial conservation of the axial vector current (PCAC), and second-class-current constraints.

## Key numbers

* Recoil parameter \(\delta = (M_n - M_p)/(M_n + M_p) = 0.689 \times 10^{-3}\)
* CVC-determined weak magnetism form factor \(F_2^V(0) = \frac12(\kappa_p - \kappa_n) = 1.853\)
* Induced pseudoscalar form factor from PCAC: \(F_3^A(0) = 2 M_n^2 / m_\pi^2 \times F_1^A(0) = 118.02\) (Marshak 1969); alternative lattice-based estimate \(F_3^A(0) = 175\) (Gonzalez-Alonso 2013)
* Axial form factor from PDG: \(F_1^A(0) = 1.2724(23)\) (Tanabashi 2018)
* Lattice results for \(F_1^A(0)\): \(1.278(21)(26)\) (Berkowitz 2017), \(1.271(13)\) (Chang 2017, 2018), \(1.251(24)\) (Ottnad 2018)
* At zero recoil, the rate and three of the four parity-conserving polarization observables are protected from NLO recoil corrections when second-class currents are set to zero

## Context

This work bridges the gap between the heavy-baryon semileptonic decay formalism (traditionally treated as cascade decays, as in Kadeer (2005) and Korner (1987, 1989)) and the traditional three-body treatment of neutron beta decay (Jackson 1957, Wilkinson 1982, Abele 2008, Dubbers 2011). The cascade approach enables up to 15 independent observables compared to the three normalized observables available in the three-body approach, providing new tools for independent measurements of the axial form factor \(F_1^A\) that do not depend on the value of \(V_{ud}\), which is timely given the \(4\sigma\) discrepancy between beam and trap lifetime measurements and recent claims of underestimated radiative corrections (Seng 2018).
