# Deep Learning Exotic Hadrons

> We perform the first model independent analysis of experimental data using Deep Neural Networks to determine the nature of an exotic hadron. Specifically, we study the line shape of the $P_c(4312)$ signal reported by the LHCb collaboration and we find that its most likely interpretation is that of a virtual state. This method can be applied to other near-threshold resonance candidates.

## Key claim
The paper performs the first model-independent amplitude analysis of experimental data using Deep Neural Networks to determine the nature of an exotic hadron. The analysis finds that the most likely interpretation of the P_c(4312) signal reported by LHCb is that of a virtual state, but this conclusion is conditional on the validity of an effective range expansion of the amplitude near the Sigma_c+ anti-D0 threshold and on the assumption that no other nearby singularities significantly affect the line shape.

## Method
The authors parametrize the J/psi p invariant mass distribution reported by LHCb using an effective range expansion of the amplitude near the Sigma_c+ anti-D0 threshold, which maps onto four Riemann sheets with poles corresponding to bound or virtual states on different sheets. They generate a training dataset of 10^5 synthetic line shapes with 5% Gaussian noise, label each by the pole type (bound/virtual and sheet number), and train a deep neural network with two hidden layers (400 and 200 nodes) as a classifier using the PyTorch framework. The trained DNN is then applied to three LHCb datasets (the original dataset and two with background suppression cuts) to predict the classification probabilities, with uncertainty quantified via bootstrap and dropout Monte Carlo methods and interpretability provided by SHAP value analysis.

## Key numbers
- The training dataset consists of 10^5 line shapes generated from the amplitude parametrization.
- Line shapes are evaluated in the invariant mass range [4.251, 4.379] GeV with 2 MeV bin spacing, yielding 65 intensity values per line shape.
- 5% Gaussian noise is added to the signal to match statistical uncertainties reported by LHCb.
- For the cos(theta_Pc)-weighted LHCb dataset, the DNN assigns 98.3% probability to the virtual state on sheet IV (v|4), 1.1% to v|2, 0.6% to b|2, and less than 0.01% to b|4.
- For the m_Kp > 1.9 GeV dataset, the probabilities are 97.0% (v|4), 1.6% (v|2), 1.4% (b|2), and less than 0.1% (b|4).
- For the full m_Kp dataset, the probabilities are 73.6% (v|4), 21.0% (v|2), 5.4% (b|2), and less than 0.1% (b|4).

## Context
This work extends the bottom-up amplitude analysis of the P_c(4312) signal performed by Fernandez-Ramirez et al. (2019), which favored a virtual state interpretation using standard chi-squared fitting, by introducing deep neural networks as a model classifier that can determine the probability of each physical interpretation directly. The method is benchmarked against the known nucleon-nucleon bound state analysis by Sombillo et al. (2020) and pion-nucleon resonance studies by Sombillo et al. (2021). The approach enables future application to other near-threshold resonance candidates and opens the possibility of transfer learning to obtain general resonance classifiers across scattering channels.
