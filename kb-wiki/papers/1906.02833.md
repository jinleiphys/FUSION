# Progress and Simulations for Intranuclear Neutron--Antineutron Transformations in ${}^{40}_{18} Ar$

> With the imminent construction of the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) and Hyper-Kamiokande, nucleon decay searches as a means to constrain beyond Standard Model (BSM) extensions are once again at the forefront of fundamental physics. Abundant neutrons within these large experimental volumes, along with future high-intensity neutron beams such as the European Spallation Source, offer a powerful, high-precision portal onto this physics through searches for $\mathcal{B}$ and $\mathcal{B}-\mathcal{L}$ violating processes such as neutron--antineutron transformations ($n\rightarrow\bar{n}$), a key prediction of compelling theories of baryogenesis. With this in mind, this paper discusses a novel and self-consistent intranuclear simulation of this process within ${}^{40}_{18} Ar$, which plays the role of both detector and target within DUNE's gigantic liquid argon time projection chambers. An accurate and independent simulation of the resulting intranuclear annihilation respecting important physical correlations and cascade dynamics for this large nucleus is necessary to understand the viability of such rare searches when contrasted against background sources such as atmospheric neutrinos. Recent theoretical improvements to our model, including the first calculation of ${}^{40}_{18} Ar$'s $\bar{n}$-intranuclear suppression factor, and Monte Carlo simulation comparisons to another publicly available $n\rightarrow\bar{n}$ generator within GENIE, are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel intranuclear simulation of neutron-antineutron transformations in argon-40, finding that the intranuclear suppression factor for argon is approximately 12% smaller than previous coarse estimates taken from iron-56, which could affect the sensitivity of future searches at DUNE.

## Method

The authors solved the Sternheimer equation for each neutron shell of argon-40 using a realistic antineutron-nucleus optical potential, deriving shell-by-shell radial annihilation probability distributions. They then implemented these distributions into a Monte Carlo simulation that includes a full intranuclear cascade model with proper handling of annihilation pair dynamics, mass defects, and momentum modification, and compared their results to the GENIE event generator's default n→n̅ module.

## Key numbers

* The intranuclear suppression factor for argon-40 is T_R^Ar ∼ 5.6 × 10^22 s^{-1}, which is about 12% smaller than previous coarse estimates taken from iron-56.
* For the deuteron, the reduced lifetime is estimated to be about T_R^D ∼ 3 × 10^22 s^{-1}.
* The deuteron binding energy used in the calculation is E_0 = -0.0022 GeV.
* The Hulthen wave function parameters for the deuteron are λ_1 = 0.2316 ℏc and λ_2 = 5.98 λ_1.
* Super-Kamiokande's bound n→n̅ search set a limit on the intranuclear neutron lifetime of 1.9 × 10^32 years, corresponding to a free transformation time of ≥ 2.7 × 10^8 s.
* The annihilation releases approximately 2 GeV of rest-mass energy, from which about 4-5 mesons are emitted on average.
* The intranuclear cascade model is assumed to hold at energies above approximately 30-40 MeV.
* Modifying the real and imaginary parts of the antineutron-nucleus potential by ±20% changes the width by less than 10%.
* For the most external shell of argon-40, the approximate width formula overestimates the width by a factor of about 3.5.

## Context

This work extends the Sternheimer equation approach previously applied by Dover, Gal, and Richard (1982, 1985, 1994) and Friedman and Gal (2008) to oxygen-16 and iron-56, now calculating the first intranuclear suppression factor specifically for argon-40 as needed for DUNE. The authors compare their simulation to the GENIE event generator's n→n̅ module developed by Hewes (2017) and the DUNE Nucleon Decay Working Group, noting several physics approximations in that model including incorrect treatment of the surface dominance of annihilation, limited annihilation channels, and lack of a true cascade model. The results enable more accurate estimates of signal efficiency and background rejection for future n→n̅ searches at DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, and the deuteron calculation shows consistency with the recent work by Haidenbauer et al. (2019) while disagreeing with Oosterhof et al. (2019).
