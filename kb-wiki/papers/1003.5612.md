# Properties of the Lambda(1520) Resonance from High-Precision Electroproduction Data

> High-resolution spectrometer measurements of the reaction H(e,e'K+)X at small Q2 are used to extract the mass and width of the Lambda(1520). We investigate the influence of various assumptions used in the extraction. The width appears to be more sensitive to the assumptions than the mass. To reach a width uncertainty about 1 MeV or better, one needs to know the structure of the non-resonant background. Based on the new Jefferson Lab Hall A data, our final values for the Breit-Wigner parameters are M = 1520.4 +- 0.6 (stat) +- 1.5 (syst) MeV, Gamma = 18.6 +- 1.9 (stat) +- 1 (syst) MeV. For the first time, we also estimate the pole position for this resonance and find that both the pole mass and width seem to be smaller than the Breit-Wigner values.

## Key claim

The extracted Breit-Wigner mass and width of the Lambda(1520) depend not only on experimental data but also on the way the data are treated; the width is more sensitive to assumptions about the non-resonant background and fitting procedure than the mass, and achieving a width uncertainty of about 1 MeV or better requires knowing the structure of that background.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed high-resolution spectrometer data from Jefferson Lab Hall A for the reaction H(e,e'K+)X at small Q^2 (average ~0.1 (GeV/c)^2) with a 5.09 GeV electron beam. They increased the missing-mass bin size from 1 MeV to 4 MeV to reduce statistical fluctuations, then fitted the missing-mass spectrum from 1.45 to 1.65 GeV using a Breit-Wigner term (relativistic form with energy-dependent width) plus a background composed of a linear term and a tail from the Lambda(1405) resonance. They compared least-squares (min-chi^2) and log-likelihood fitting methods and varied the background description to assess systematic uncertainties, and also solved for the S-matrix pole position.

## Key numbers

*   Breit-Wigner mass M = 1520.4 ± 0.6 (stat) ± 1.5 (syst) MeV from the min-chi^2 fit with relativistic energy-dependent width and background including Lambda(1405).
*   Breit-Wigner width Gamma = 18.6 ± 1.9 (stat) ± 1 (syst) MeV from the same fit.
*   Log-likelihood fit gave M = 1520.3 ± 0.6 MeV and Gamma = 17.8 ± 1.9 MeV.
*   Estimated pole position: pole mass M_p = 1518.8 MeV, pole width Gamma_p = 17.2 MeV.
*   The missing-mass resolution of the spectrometers was sigma = 1.5 MeV, determined from calibration peaks of the neutron, Lambda(1116), and Sigma0(1193).
*   The fit used 13,070 detected events in the mass interval 1.45–1.65 GeV.

## Context

This work provides new, high-precision Breit-Wigner parameters for the Lambda(1520) resonance, whose last experimental inputs in the Review of Particle Physics (PDG) dated before 1980, and whose earlier values came from bubble-chamber experiments (e.g., Ca77, Co75) and partial-wave analyses (e.g., Al78, Go77). The authors also present the first estimate of the pole position for this hyperon resonance, finding both pole mass and width smaller than the Breit-Wigner values, a result that may be general for resonances with thresholds nearby. The analysis demonstrates that future extractions of the Lambda(1520) width with uncertainties of 1–2 MeV will require a detailed understanding of the non-resonant background, and it enables more refined studies of hyperon spectroscopy and the possible molecular nature of the resonance.
