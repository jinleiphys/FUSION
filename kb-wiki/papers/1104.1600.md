# Moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions from lattice QCD

> We present results on the lower moments of the nucleon generalized parton distri butions within lattice QCD using two dynamical flavors of degenerate twisted mass fermions. Our simulations are performed on lattices with three different values of the lattice spacings, namely $a=0.089$ fm, $a=0.070$ fm and $a=0.056$ fm, allowing the investigation of cut-off effects. The volume dependence is examined using simulations on two lattices of spatial length $L=2.1$ fm and $L=2.8$ fm. The simulations span pion masses in the range of 260-470 MeV. Our results are renormalized non-perturbatively and the values are given in the $\bar{\rm MS}$ scheme at a scale $ μ=2$ GeV. They are chirally extrapolated to the physical point in order to compare with experiment. The consequences of these results on the spin carried by the quarks in the nucleon are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD results for the lower moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) using two dynamical flavors of degenerate twisted mass fermions, finding that the isovector moments ⟨x⟩_{u-d} and ⟨x⟩_{Δu-Δd} show no significant finite volume effects for lattices satisfying L m_π > 3.3 and no visible cut-off effects within statistical errors, though the lattice values remain higher than the phenomenological values even after chiral extrapolation to the physical point.

## Method

The authors compute the n=2 Mellin moments of nucleon GPDs by evaluating matrix elements of one-derivative local operators on Euclidean lattices with three lattice spacings (a=0.089 fm, 0.070 fm, 0.056 fm) and two spatial volumes (L=2.1 fm and L=2.8 fm), using pion masses ranging from 260-470 MeV. The generalized form factors (GFFs) A_{20}, B_{20}, C_{20}, \tilde{A}_{20}, and \tilde{B}_{20} are extracted from ratios of two-point and three-point correlation functions with sequential inversions through the sink, and the results are renormalized non-perturbatively in the RI'-MOM scheme and converted to the \bar{MS} scheme at μ=2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: a=0.089(1)(5) fm at β=3.9, a=0.070(1)(4) fm at β=4.05, a=0.056(2)(3) fm at β=4.2
- Pion mass range: 260-470 MeV
- Spatial lattice extents: L=2.1 fm and L=2.8 fm
- Renormalization constants for vector operators with μ=ν: Z_V=0.970(26) at β=3.9, 1.013(14) at β=4.05, 1.097(6) at β=4.2
- Renormalization constants for vector operators with μ≠ν: Z_V=1.061(29) at β=3.9, 1.131(18) at β=4.05, 1.122(10) at β=4.2
- Renormalization constants for axial-vector operators with μ≠ν: Z_A=1.076(1) at β=3.9, 1.136(0) at β=4.05, 1.165(10) at β=4.2
- The GFF C_{20} is consistent with zero within error bars for all momentum transfers
- Phenomenological value for comparison: ⟨x_{u-d}⟩ ∼ 0.16

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure using twisted mass fermions, following previous studies of nucleon form factors by Alexandrou et al. (2010, 2011) and complements results from other collaborations including LHPC using a hybrid action (Bratt et al., 2010), RBC-UKQCD using domain wall fermions (Aoki et al., 2010), and QCDSF using clover fermions (Pleiter et al., 2011, preliminary). The results enable the determination of the quark contribution to the nucleon spin via Ji's sum rule J^q = 1/2[A_{20}^q(0) + B_{20}^q(0)] and the separation into quark helicity and orbital angular momentum, though the isoscalar quantities neglect disconnected contributions and require further investigation.
