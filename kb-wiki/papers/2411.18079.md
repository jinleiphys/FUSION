# Exploring the nuclear momentum anisotropy based on intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We simulate ultra-central collisions of prolate uranium-uranium nuclei at intermediate energies using the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model to investigate the impact of momentum anisotropy on spatial geometric effects. By defining the quadrupole deformation parameter in momentum space $β_\text{p}$, we establish an ellipsoidal Fermi surface, aligning its rotational symmetry axis with the one in coordinate space. It is found that oblate momentum density enhances elliptic flow $v_2$, while prolate momentum density has the opposite effect, particularly pronounced in the outer, high transverse momentum $p_\text{t}$ region. Momentum anisotropy also causes differences in the initial momentum mean projection along the beam direction, with larger projections producing more pion mesons. Additionally, significant effects on mean square elliptic flow are observed in non-polarized collisions. We further examine the relationship between the $v_2$-$p_\text{t}$ slope and $β_\text{p}$, eliminating systematic errors through the two-system ratio. These findings provide important references for experimentalists in heavy-ion collisions and valuable feedback to theorists regarding nuclear structure.

# Exploring the nuclear momentum anisotropy based on intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that momentum-space quadrupole deformation (βₚ) in prolate uranium nuclei can be extracted from elliptic flow and pion observables in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, but only at beam energies below approximately 700 MeV/nucleon; at higher energies the momentum anisotropy effects become negligible, making relativistic collisions more reliable for extracting coordinate-space deformation alone.

## Method

The authors simulate ultra-central body-body and tip-tip collisions of prolate ²³⁸U + ²³⁸U at beam energies of 300, 500, and 700 MeV/nucleon using the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent mean-field single nucleon potential. They introduce a quadrupole deformation parameter βₚ in momentum space to define an ellipsoidal Fermi surface, aligned with the coordinate-space symmetry axis, and compare cases with βₚ = −0.29 (oblate), 0 (spherical), and +0.29 (prolate) while fixing the coordinate-space deformation at βᵣ = 0.29 (or 0 for comparison). The elliptic flow v₂, pion multiplicities, and π⁻/π⁺ ratios are computed as functions of transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Coordinate-space quadrupole deformation parameter for ²³⁸U: βᵣ = 0.29 (experimental value from nudat database)
- Momentum-space deformation parameters tested: βₚ = −0.29, 0, and +0.29
- Beam energies: 300, 500, and 700 MeV/nucleon
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.168 fm⁻³; diffuseness a = 0.54 fm; R₀ = 6.81 fm
- Momentum tail diffuseness coefficient: aₚ = 0.01 (10% of nucleons above Fermi surface)
- Number of test particles: N = 50
- Impact parameter for ultra-central collisions: b ∈ [0, 1] fm
- Mean square elliptic flow ⟨v₂²⟩ (×10⁻⁵) at 500 MeV/nucleon for βᵣ = 0.29: 25.071 ± 0.237 (βₚ = −0.29), 13.588 ± 0.194 (βₚ = 0), 6.120 ± 0.253 (βₚ = 0.29)
- Mean square elliptic flow ⟨v₂²⟩ (×10⁻⁵) at 500 MeV/nucleon for βᵣ = 0: 2.636 ± 0.134 (βₚ = −0.29), 1.391 ± 0.094 (βₚ = 0), 3.293 ± 0.154 (βₚ = 0.29)
- Slope k₅₀₀ of v₂(pₜ) extracted from pₜ = 0.15 to 0.6 GeV/c at 500 MeV/nucleon
- Slope ratio k₃₀₀/k₇₀₀ computed between 300 and 700 MeV/nucleon
- Reaction duration in tip-tip collisions is approximately 15% longer than in body-body collisions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear deformation in heavy-ion collisions from ultra-relativistic energies (several hundred GeV/nucleon, as in Zhang (2022) and Jia (2022)) down to the intermediate-energy regime (300–700 MeV/nucleon), following earlier theoretical work by Fan (2023) and Yang (2024) on geometric effects in U+U collisions. The paper introduces momentum-space anisotropy as a new degree of freedom, motivated by short-range correlation experiments (2018 Nature 560) showing similar high-momentum tails for protons and neutrons, and by the need for momentum correction factors (Fan, 2022) and momentum gaps (Yong, 2018) to interpret np-dominated SRCs. The results enable experimental extraction of momentum-space deformation parameters at intermediate energies and provide feedback for nuclear structure models, particularly for low-energy fusion reactions relevant to synthesizing elements 119 and 120.
