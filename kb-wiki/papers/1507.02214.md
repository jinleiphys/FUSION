# Nucleon Properties in the Quantized Linear Sigma Model at Finite Temperature and Chemical Potential

> The linear sigma model at finite temperature and chemical potential is systematically studied using the coherent-pair approximation, in which fully taking quantum of fields are included. The expectation value of the chiral Hamiltonian density is minimized and the resulting equations for the nucleon are solved. The qualitative features of the quantized sigma and pion fields are strong sensitive to the change of temperature and baryonic chemical potential and are in agreement with the mean-field approximation calculations. It is noticed that the nucleon mass increases with increasing coherence parameter. In addition, the nucleon mass increases with increasing temperature and the baryonic chemical potential and then it decreases at higher values of the temperature and baryonic chemical potential. The obtained results show that the mean-square radius of the proton and the neutron increase with increasing temperature or the baryonic chemical potential and that the pion-nucleon coupling constant decreases with the temperature or chemical potential. We conclude that the coherent-pair approximation successfully give better description of the nucleon properties at finite temperature and baryonic chemical potential

## Key claim

The coherent-pair approximation (CPA), which includes both thermal and quantum fluctuations, successfully describes nucleon properties at finite temperature and baryonic chemical potential, with the nucleon mass increasing with temperature and chemical potential up to a point and then decreasing at higher values, and with the mean-square radii of the proton and neutron increasing while the pion-nucleon coupling constant decreases.

## Method

The authors applied the coherent-pair approximation to the linear sigma model at finite temperature and chemical potential, minimizing the expectation value of the chiral Hamiltonian density to obtain four nonlinear coupled differential equations for the quark fields (u and v), the sigma field, and the pion field. These equations were solved self-consistently using the modified COLSYS code for fixed values of the coherence parameter x and Fock-space parameters (alpha, beta, gamma), with initial parameters at zero temperature and chemical potential used as starting points. The nucleon mass and other properties were then calculated from the resulting field configurations.

## Context

This work extends the application of the coherent-pair approximation in the linear sigma model, previously applied only at finite temperature by the same group (Ref. [14] in the paper), to include finite baryonic chemical potential. It contrasts with earlier mean-field approximation studies by Christov et al. (Ref. [12]) and Dominguez et al. (Ref. [13]) that ignored quantum fluctuations, and with work by Bilic and Nikolic (Ref. [15]) that studied chiral transition in mean-field approximation. The results enable further investigation of nucleon properties in hot and dense matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron star physics.
