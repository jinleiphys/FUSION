# The Effect of the Pairing Interaction on the Energies of Isobar Analog Resonances in $^{112-124}$Sb and Isospin Admixture in $^{100-124}$Sn Isotopes

> In the present study, the effect of the pairing interaction and the isovector correlation between nucleons on the properties of the isobar analog resonances (IAR) in $^{112-124}$Sb isotopes and the isospin admixture in $^{100-124}$Sn isotopes is investigated within the framework of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). The form of the interaction strength parameter is related to the shell model potential by restoring the isotopic invariance of the nuclear part of the total Hamiltonian. In this respect, the isospin admixtures in the $^{100-124}$Sn isotopes are calculated, and the dependence of the differential cross section and the volume integral $J_{F}$ for the Sn($^{3}$He,t)Sb reactions at E($^{3}$He)$=200$ MeV occurring by the excitation of IAR on mass number A is examined. Our results show that the calculated value for the isospin mixing in the $^{100}$Sn isotope is in good agreement with Colo et al.'s estimates $(4-5%)$, and the obtained values for the volume integral change within the error range of the value reported by Fujiwara et al. (53$\pm$5 MeV fm$^{3}$). Moreover, it is concluded that although the differential cross section of the isobar analog resonance for the ($^{3}$He,t) reactions is not sensitive to pairing correlations between nucleons, a considerable effect on the isospin admixtures in $N\approx Z$ isotopes can be seen with the presence of these correlations.

## Key claim

The pairing interaction between nucleons has a considerable effect on the isospin admixture in neutron-deficient Sn isotopes near N=Z, shifting the admixture values downward, but the differential cross section of the isobar analog resonance for the (3He,t) reaction is not sensitive to these pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors used the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with a Woods-Saxon potential (Chepurnov parametrization) and pairing forces to calculate isospin admixtures in the ground states of 100-124Sn isotopes and the properties of isobar analog resonances (IAR) in 112-124Sb isotopes. The effective interaction strength was determined self-consistently from the shell model potential by restoring isotopic invariance of the nuclear Hamiltonian, making the model free of adjustable parameters. The differential cross sections for the Sn(3He,t)Sb reactions at E(3He)=200 MeV were computed using the volume integral of the effective interaction and distortion factors.

## Key numbers

* Isospin admixture b2 in 100Sn: 4.100% (this work), compared to 4.067% from Ref. [13] and 1.885% from the hydrodynamic model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975).
* Isospin admixture b2 in 124Sn: 0.248% (this work), compared to 0.168% from Ref. [13] and 0.167% from the hydrodynamic model.
* IAR energy in 112Sb: 5.994 MeV (this work), with experimental value 6.16±0.03 MeV.
* IAR energy in 124Sb: 12.087 MeV (this work), with experimental value 12.19±0.03 MeV.
* Energy shift due to pairing forces for 104-122Sb isotopes: ranges from 529 keV to 53 keV.
* Volume integral JF: calculated values for A=112-124 change within the range 53±5 MeV fm3, consistent with the value reported by Fujiwara et al. (1982).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of isospin mixing in proton-rich nuclei around A=80-100, which had been performed using Hartree-Fock and RPA methods without pairing correlations (Refs. [10,13]). The authors specifically compare their results to the hydrodynamic model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and to the shell-model-based estimates of Colo et al. (Ref. [13]), finding that pairing correlations reduce the isospin admixture values for N≈Z isotopes. The study enables more accurate predictions for Fermi beta-decay matrix elements and the effective vector coupling constant, and provides a parameter-free framework for calculating IAR properties in medium-mass nuclei.
