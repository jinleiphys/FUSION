# Decay Pattern of Pygmy States Observed in Neutron-Rich 26 Ne

> Coulomb excitation of the exotic neutron-rich nucleus Ne26 on a Pb208 target was measured at 58 MeV/u in order to search for low-lying E1 strength above the neutron emission threshold. This radioactive beam experiment was carried out at the RIKEN Accelerator Research Facility. Using the invariant mass method in the Ne25+n channel, we observe a sizable amount of E1 strength between 6 and 10 MeV excitation energy. By performing a multipole decomposition of the differential cross section, a reduced dipole transition probability of B(E1)=0.49+-0.16e2fm2 is deduced, corresponding to 4.9+-1.6% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule. For the first time, the decay pattern of low-lying strength in a neutron-rich nucleus is measured. The extracted decay pattern is not consistent with several mean-field theory descriptions of the pygmy states.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of the decay pattern of low-lying dipole (pygmy) strength in a neutron-rich nucleus, finding that the observed decay pattern is not consistent with several mean-field theory descriptions of pygmy states, particularly those predicting dominant neutron excitations from the Fermi surface.

## Method

The experiment used Coulomb excitation of a radioactive \({}^{26}\)Ne beam at 58 MeV/u on a \({}^{208}\)Pb target at the RIKEN facility. The invariant mass method was applied to reconstruct the excitation energy from the \({}^{26}\)Ne → \({}^{25}\)Ne + n decay channel, and a multipole decomposition of the differential cross section was performed to extract the E1 component. Neutron branching ratios to bound states of \({}^{25}\)Ne were extracted and compared with statistical decay calculations and theoretical predictions.

## Key numbers

- Reduced dipole transition probability B(E1) = 0.49 ± 0.16 e²fm², corresponding to 4.9 ± 1.6% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule at around 9 MeV excitation energy
- The E1 cross section after subtraction of E2 contribution: σ_Pb^(L=1) = 48.5 ± 4.8 mb
- Excitation energy resolution estimated at 0.8 MeV at E* = 8 MeV
- Neutron energy resolution of 2.5 MeV (FWHM) for neutrons of interest
- Beam intensity of approximately 5 × 10³ particles per second with 80% purity

## Context

This work extends previous observations of low-lying dipole strength in neutron-rich nuclei such as oxygen isotopes studied by Leistenschneider et al. and tin isotopes studied by Adrich et al., where approximately 5% of the TRK sum rule was measured in \({}^{130,132}\)Sn. The measurement of decay patterns provides a new observable that tests microscopic models; the results contradict predictions from relativistic QRPA calculations by Cao and Ma and from non-relativistic QRPA calculations using Gogny forces by Peru et al. and Skyrme forces by Yoshida et al., which predicted dominant 2s₁/₂ → 2p₃/₂ or 2s₁/₂ → 2p₁/₂ neutron transitions. The data show better qualitative agreement with calculations by Arteaga et al. predicting a dominant K^π = 1^- state with mixed configurations.
