# Giant dipole resonance and shape evolution in Nd isotopes within TDHF method

> The isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in even-even Nd isotopes from A=124 to A=160 is studied in the framework of time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) with Skyrme forces SkI3, SVbas, SLy5 and SLy6. The dipole strength is calculated and compared with the experimental data on photon absorption cross section $σ_γ$. An overall agreement between them is obtained. The dipole strengths in $ ^{124-140}\text{Nd}$ and $^{152-160}\text{Nd} $ are predicted. In addition, the correlation between the quadrupole deformation parameter $β_{2}$ and the splitting $ΔE/ \bar{E}_{m}$ of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) spectra is studied. The results confirm that $ΔE/ \bar{E}_{m}$ is proportional to $β_{2}$. Shape phase transition in Nd isotopes is also investigated in the light of IVGDR.

## Key claim
The TDHF calculations with four Skyrme forces (SkI3, SVbas, SLy5, SLy6) reproduce the experimental GDR spectra of Nd isotopes with an overall agreement, but with a systematic down-shift of about 0.5 MeV for SLy5, SLy6, and SkI3 in weakly deformed isotopes (142-148Nd) and a slight up-shift of about 0.5 MeV for SVbas, rather than the "overall agreement" stated in the abstract without these quantitative caveats.

## Method
The authors performed static Hartree-Fock plus BCS calculations to obtain ground-state properties (deformation parameters β2 and γ) for even-even Nd isotopes from A=124 to A=160 using the SKY3D code with four Skyrme forces (SkI3, SVbas, SLy5, SLy6) on a 24x24x24 fm³ Cartesian grid with 1 fm spacing. They then applied an instantaneous isovector dipole boost operator to the ground-state wavefunction and evolved the system via TDHF for 5000 time steps of 0.2 fm/c (total 1000 fm/c), recording the dipole moment D(t) and obtaining the GDR strength distribution through Fourier transform with cosine filtering.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 142Nd: 0.000 (spherical) for all four Skyrme forces, compared to experimental value 0.091 from Raman (2001)
- Maximum deviation between calculated and experimental β2 values: approximately 0.090 for 142Nd
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 for 160Nd: 0.377 (SkI3), 0.364 (SVbas), 0.371 (SLy6), 0.367 (SLy5), saturating near β2 ≈ 0.365 for neutron numbers above N=82
- GDR centroid energy shift relative to experiment: approximately 0.5 MeV down-shift for SLy5, SLy6, and SkI3 in weakly deformed 142-148Nd isotopes; approximately 0.5 MeV up-shift for SVbas
- Simulation parameters: 24 grid points per direction, 1 fm grid spacing, 5000 time steps with dt = 0.2 fm/c, final time Tfin = 1000 fm/c
- Convergence criterion for static HF+BCS: single-particle energy fluctuations less than 10⁻⁵

## Context
This work extends earlier TDHF studies by Maruhn et al. (2005) who examined GDR in 142-150Nd with SkI3 and SLy6 forces, finding a down-shift of about 1 MeV relative to experiment. The present study covers a wider isotopic range (A=124 to A=160) and includes two additional Skyrme forces (SVbas and SLy5). The results are compared with experimental photoabsorption data from Carlos et al. (1971) and with Separable Random Phase Approximation (SRPA) calculations by Nesterenko et al. (2008). The work enables systematic predictions of GDR properties for Nd isotopes where experimental data are not yet available (124-140Nd and 152-160Nd) and confirms the proportionality between the GDR splitting ΔE/Ēm and the quadrupole deformation parameter β2.
