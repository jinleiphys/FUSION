# Emergent symmetries of relativistic fluid dynamics from local ergodicity

> We show that volume-preserving diffomorphisms and the chemical shift symmetry defining relativistic lagrangian ideal fluid dynamics can be derived as an emerging symmetry when ergodicity is assumed to apply locally in a way that is invariant under smooth spacetime foliations. This can be used as a way to derive the ideal hydrodynamic limit in a strongly coupled but strongly fluctuating medium. We comment on the connection with thermalization in small systems, the Eigenstate thermalization hypothesis and deviations from the ideal limit.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the local symmetries of ideal relativistic Lagrangian hydrodynamics (volume-preserving diffeomorphisms and chemical shift symmetry) can be derived as emergent properties from the assumption that ergodicity applies locally in a way that is invariant under smooth spacetime foliations, rather than from the usual thermodynamic or coarse-graining limits.

## Method
The author starts from the ergodic hypothesis formulated as a local condition on phase-space degrees of freedom within a spacetime cell, then imposes foliation-independence by requiring that this local ergodicity holds for any causal spacetime foliation. By analyzing how the phase-space volume and conservation laws transform under changes of foliation, the author derives that the effective Lagrangian must be invariant under volume-preserving diffeomorphisms of the fluid coordinates and under arbitrary shifts of the internal phase variable, which are exactly the symmetries defining ideal Lagrangian hydrodynamics as developed by Jackiw, Nicolis, and Son.

## Key numbers
- The microscopic scale is defined as the averaging interval Δt, which sets the maximum allowed radius of curvature of the foliations.
- The entropy current is identified as s^μ = b u^μ, where b is related to the microscopic temperature via T_0 = b dL_eff/db.
- The hierarchy for the approach is L_diss = 0, L_micro ≡ Δ ≪ L_macro, in contrast to the usual hierarchy L_micro ≪ L_diss ≪ L_macro.

## Context
This work connects the Lagrangian formulation of ideal hydrodynamics (Jackiw, Nicolis, Son) with the statistical-mechanical foundations of hydrodynamics, extending the approach of Khinchin (1949) on ergodicity and Jaynes on statistical mechanics. It provides a framework for understanding hydrodynamic behavior in small systems (Zajc, ultracold atoms, Brazilian nut effect) where the number of degrees of freedom per cell is small, and it suggests a connection to the Eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (ETH) and to the Zubarev formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics. The approach enables a non-perturbative treatment of fluctuations and may help resolve conceptual issues about the onset of hydrodynamics in small systems.
