# A systematic study of the superdeformation of Pb isotopes with relativistic mean field theory

> The microscopically constrained relativistic mean field theory is used to investigate the superdeformation for Pb isotopes. The calculations show that there exists a clear superdeformed minimum in the potential energy surfaces with four different interactions NL3, PK1, TM1 and NLSH. The excitation energy, deformation and depth of well in the superdeformed minimum are comparable for four different interactions. Furthermore the trend for the change of the superdeformed excitation energy with neutron number is correctly reproduced. The calculated two-neutron separation energy in the ground state and superdeformed minimum together with their differences are in agreement with the data available. The larger energy difference appearing in superdeformed minimum reflects a lower average level density at superdeformations for Pb isotopes.

# A systematic study of the superdeformation of Pb isotopes with relativistic mean field theory

## Key claim

The microscopically constrained relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with four different interactions (NL3, PK1, TM1, NLSH) reproduces the trend of superdeformed excitation energies with neutron number for Pb isotopes, but the agreement with experimental data is interaction-dependent; the NL3 interaction gives the best overall agreement, with a maximum deviation of less than 0.34 MeV, while the NLSH interaction fails to reproduce a superdeformed minimum for 192,194Pb.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic quadrupole-constrained relativistic mean field calculations for 190-204Pb isotopes, expanding nucleon wavefunctions in a deformed axially symmetric harmonic-oscillator basis. Pairing correlations were treated using the BCS method with a constant pairing gap of 12/√A for even-number nucleons. Potential energy surfaces were obtained by constraining the quadrupole moment ⟨Q2⟩ to different values, and the calculations were repeated with four different effective interactions: NL3, PK1, TM1, and NLSH.

## Key numbers

- Maximum deviation between calculated and experimental excitation energies for the NL3 interaction: less than 0.34 MeV
- Quadrupole deformation β2 in superdeformed minima lies systematically between 0.5 and 0.7 for all four interactions across 190-204Pb
- Depth of the superdeformed well V for 190Pb with NL3: 0.46439 MeV; for 204Pb with NL3: 3.51464 MeV
- Depth of the superdeformed well V for 190Pb with PK1: 0.42181 MeV; for 204Pb with PK1: 2.24769 MeV
- Depth of the superdeformed well V for 190Pb with TM1: 0.22936 MeV; for 204Pb with TM1: 1.49029 MeV
- Depth of the superdeformed well V for 196Pb with NLSH: 0.46892 MeV; for 204Pb with NLSH: 1.96709 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy in the ground state S2n,ND for 190Pb (N=110) with NL3: 17.862 MeV; experimental value: 18.400 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy in the superdeformed minimum S2n,SD for 192Pb (N=112) with NL3: 17.031 MeV; experimental value: 17.16(4) MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy difference in the superdeformed minimum ΔS2n,SD for 192Pb (N=112) with NL3: 1.063 MeV; experimental value: 0.85(8) MeV
- The RMF theory predicts barrier heights lower than 1 MeV for 190,192Pb and higher than 1 MeV for 196-214Pb in all interactions

## Context

This work extends previous RMF calculations that had been applied to individual nuclei such as 194Hg and 194Pb (Lalazissis 1998) to a systematic study across the entire Pb isotope chain from 190Pb to 204Pb. It directly addresses the experimental data from Wilson (2005) on excitation energies of superdeformed bands in 196Pb, 194Pb, and 192Pb. The authors compare their results with nonrelativistic approaches including the Strutinsky method with a Woods-Saxon potential (Satula), the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method (Krieger, Heenen, Libert), and the cluster model (Adamian), noting that those models reproduce only the gross trend of decreasing energy with decreasing neutron number but fail to consistently reproduce absolute energies and their differences. The RMF results enable a more reliable prediction of superdeformed properties for Pb isotopes and suggest that the larger two-neutron separation energy differences in the superdeformed minimum reflect a lower average level density at superdeformations.
