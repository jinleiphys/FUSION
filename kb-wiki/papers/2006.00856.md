# $d^\ast (2380)$ dibaryon from lattice QCD

> The $ΔΔ$ dibaryon resonance $d^\ast (2380)$ with $(J^P, I)=(3^+, 0)$ is studied theoretically on the basis of the 3-flavor lattice QCD simulation with heavy pion masses ($m_π=679, 841$ and $1018$ MeV). By using the HAL QCD method, the central $Δ$-$Δ$ potential in the ${}^7S_3$ channel is obtained from the lattice data with the lattice spacing $a\simeq 0.121$ fm and the lattice size $L\simeq 3.87$ fm. The resultant potential shows a strong short-range attraction, so that a quasi-bound state corresponding to $d^\ast (2380)$ is formed with the binding energy $25$-$40$ MeV below the $ΔΔ$ threshold for the heavy pion masses. The tensor part of the transition potential from $ΔΔ$ to $NN$ is also extracted to investigate the coupling strength between the $S$-wave $ΔΔ$ system with $J^P=3^+$ and the $D$-wave $NN$ system. Although the transition potential is strong at short distances, the decay width of $d^\ast (2380)$ to $NN$ in the $D$-wave is kinematically suppressed, which justifies our single-channel analysis at the range of the pion mass explored in this study.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with heavy pion masses (679–1018 MeV), the HAL QCD Collaboration finds a strong short-range attraction in the ΔΔ (³⁺,0) channel that produces a quasi-bound state corresponding to the d*(2380) dibaryon, with the caveat that at these heavy pion masses the Δ baryon is stable and the decay to NN is kinematically suppressed, justifying a single-channel analysis.

## Method

The HAL QCD method was used to extract the central Δ-Δ potential in the ⁷S₃ channel from the equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function computed on 32³×32 lattices with lattice spacing a ≈ 0.121 fm and volume (3.87 fm)³. The potential was obtained from the time-dependent HAL QCD equation at leading order in the derivative expansion, using 3-flavor full QCD gauge configurations in the flavor-SU(3) limit with non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson quarks at three values of the hopping parameter. The tensor part of the transition potential from ΔΔ to NN was also extracted using Misner's projection method to separate partial-wave components.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: mπ = 679.0(2) MeV, 840.6(2) MeV, and 1017.5(2) MeV
- Δ baryon masses: mΔ = 1676.9(8) MeV, 1940.3(6) MeV, and 2213.6(7) MeV at the three pion masses
- Binding energy of the ΔΔ quasi-bound state: BΔΔ = 29.8(3.4)(+6.7/-5.0) MeV at mπ = 679 MeV; 33.6(3.7)(+1.8/-1.7) MeV at mπ = 841 MeV; 37.4(3.3)(+1.2/-0.4) MeV at mπ = 1018 MeV
- Root-mean-square distance of the quasi-bound state: 0.8–1 fm
- Estimated decay width to NN (D-wave): Γ = 6.4(1.8) MeV at mπ = 679 MeV; 5.6(1.7) MeV at mπ = 841 MeV; 1.6(6) MeV at mπ = 1018 MeV (using free NN wave functions)
- Fitted potential parameters at mπ = 679 MeV and t/a=10: b1 = -457(29) MeV, b2 = 0.090(4) fm, b3 = -121(31) MeV, b4 = 0.15(2) fm, b5 = -1924(533) MeV·fm, b6 = 0.98(16) fm

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous studies of dibaryons, particularly the di-Omega in the spin-0 channel (Gongyo 2017), to the ΔΔ system in the spin-3, isospin-0 channel where the experimentally observed d*(2380) resonance lies. The results confirm the quark Pauli principle prediction that the anti-symmetric 10* flavor representation supports S-wave dibaryons (Dyson 1964), and the extracted potentials provide input for effective field theory approaches toward the physical point (Haidenbauer 2017). The study also implies that other members of the 10* representation, such as ΔΣ* and ΔΞ*, may have dibaryons, though these systems are more intricate due to additional decay channels.
