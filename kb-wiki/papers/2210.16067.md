# Islands of shape coexistence from single-particle spectra in covariant density functional theory

> Using covariant density functional theory with the DDME2 functional and labeling single-particle energy orbitals by Nilsson quantum numbers, a search for particle-hole (p-h) excitations connected to the appearance of shape coexistence is performed for Z=38 to 84. Islands of shape coexistence are found near the magic numbers Z=82 and Z=50, restricted in regions around the relevant neutron midshells N=104 and N=66 respectively, in accordance to the well accepted p-h interpretation of shape coexistence in these regions, which we call neutron-induced shape coexistence, since the neutrons act as elevators creating holes in the proton orbitals. Similar but smaller islands of shape coexistence are found near N=90 and N=60, restricted in regions around the relevant proton midshells Z=66 and Z=39 respectively, related to p-h excitations across the 3-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator (3D-HO) magic numbers N=112 and N=70, which correspond to the beginning of the participation of the opposite parity orbitals 1i13/2 and 1h11/2 respectively to the onset of deformation. We call this case proton-induced shape coexistence, since the protons act as elevators creating holes in the neutron orbitals, thus offering a possible microscopic mechanism for the appearance of shape coexistence in these regions. In the region around N=40, Z=40, an island is located on which both neutron p-h excitations and proton p-h excitations are present.

## Key claim

Using covariant density functional theory with the DDME2 functional, the authors identify islands of shape coexistence by tracking particle-hole excitations in single-particle spectra, proposing that shape coexistence near N=90 and N=60 arises from proton-induced neutron excitations across 3D harmonic oscillator magic numbers rather than across standard shell-model gaps.

## Method

The authors solved relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations using the DDME2 functional for the long-range particle-hole interaction and the Gogny-like TMR force for pairing, with the axial DIRHB code expanding single-nucleon wavefunctions in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis. They tracked the evolution of single-particle energies and occupation probabilities across isotopic and isotonic chains, identifying regions where orbitals change from particle to hole status relative to the Fermi surface, which they interpret as signatures of shape coexistence.

## Key numbers

- Particle-hole excitations in the Z=82 region occur for N=98-110, involving the 11/2[505] orbital of 1h11/2, the 1/2[400] orbital of 3s1/2, and the 3/2[402] orbital of 2d3/2 as holes, with particles from the 1/2[541] and 3/2[532] orbitals of 1h9/2
- In the Z=50 region, 2p-2h proton excitations are seen for N=64-68, forming a small island expected from 116,118,120Te
- In the Z=40 region, 6p-6h proton excitations occur for N=38-40 in Zr, and 4p-4h excitations for N=38-40 in Sr, predicting an island from 78Sr, 78Zr, and 80Zr
- In the N=90 region, p-h excitations across the N=112 3D-HO magic number occur in 150Nd, 152Sm, 154Gd (N=90) and 152Nd, 154Sm, 156Gd (N=92), involving particles in 1/2[660] or 3/2[651] orbitals of 1i13/2 and holes in the 5/2[523] orbital of 2f7/2
- In the N=60 region, 4p-4h proton excitations across the N=70 3D-HO magic number are seen for Z=40-42, predicting shape coexistence in 98Zr, 100Zr, and 102Mo

## Context

This work extends the particle-hole interpretation of shape coexistence previously established for the Z=82 and Z=50 regions (Wood, Heyde) to the N=90, Z=64 and N=60, Z=40 regions, where the microscopic mechanism had remained an open question (Garrett). The results narrow down the broader shape coexistence stripes predicted by the proxy-SU(3) model (Bonatsos, EPJASC) into specific islands, and are corroborated by interacting boson model calculations with configuration mixing (Nomura 2011, 2012, 2013; Ramos 2014, 2015) and by relativistic energy-density functional studies (ZPLi2009). The findings enable more specific experimental guidance for searching shape coexistence and suggest a connection between the proposed particle-hole mechanism and shape phase transitions.
