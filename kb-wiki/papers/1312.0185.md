# Baryon-anti-baryon Flavor Correlation in Quark Combination Models in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We extend the study of hadron yield correlation in combination models in a recent publication to baryon-anti-baryon flavor correlations in heavy ion collisions. We show that the universal behaviors of the anti-baryon to baryon yield ratios as functions of that of $K^-$ to $K^+$ are naturally explained. We study also the "mixed ratios" and propose other measurements that might be sensitive to the hadronization mechanism.

# Baryon-anti-baryon Flavor Correlation in Quark Combination Models in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The universal behaviors of anti-baryon to baryon yield ratios as functions of the K- to K+ yield ratio are naturally explained by the quark combination mechanism under the assumptions of flavor-momentum factorization and independent flavor production, provided that the net-quark contribution parameter ╬│_net is the controlling variable. The relationships are claimed to be independent of collision energy, nuclei, centrality, and kinematic region as long as the combination mechanism is at work.

## Method
The authors apply a previously developed quark combination formalism (Wang, Shao, and Liang, 2012) that assumes factorization of flavor and momentum dependence in the combination kernel and independent flavor production with a strangeness suppression factor ╬╗. They derive analytic expressions for anti-baryon to baryon yield ratios (for p, ╬¢, ╬₧, ╬⌐) and for the K- to K+ ratio as functions of ╬│_net = Γƒ¿N_q^netΓƒ⌐/Γƒ¿N_qΓƒ⌐, the fraction of net quarks from incident nuclei. They also compute "mixed ratios" (ratios of anti-baryon/baryon ratios) and baryon-anti-baryon flavor correlation factors C_{B_j \bar B_k}.

## Context
This work extends the hadron yield correlation study of Wang, Shao, and Liang (2012) to baryon-anti-baryon flavor correlations in heavy ion collisions. It explains the universal scaling behavior observed by STAR, NA49, NA44, and BRAHMS collaborations for anti-baryon to baryon ratios versus K- to K+ ratios across multiple collision energies. The paper proposes that deviations from the predicted mixed ratios at lower energies (such as ΓêÜs_NN = 7.7 GeV and E_beam = 20 AGeV) may indicate the failure of the quark combination mechanism and possibly the absence of deconfined matter at those energies. The correlation factor predictions provide testable quantities for future experiments to distinguish hadronization mechanisms.
