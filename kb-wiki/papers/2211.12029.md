# Pseudoscalar and Scalar Meson Photoproduction Interpreted by Regge Phenomenology

> We have evaluated pseudoscalar and scalar neutral pion photoproduction in $\vecγp\toπ^0p$ and $\vecγp\to a_0^0p$ above the resonance region and within Regge phenomenology. Our fit, including GlueX $Σ$ pseudoscalar photoproduction data, shows that previous SLAC $Σ$ measurements for $\vecγp \to π^0p$ above $E_γ= 4~\mathrm{GeV}$ are at variance with SLAC data with more recent measurements made by GlueX in vicinity of $E_γ= 9~\mathrm{GeV}$. The Regge model predicts that the beam polarization asymmetry $Σ$ of the scalar meson is opposite to that of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction, however, the cross sections are similar. While the vector natural parity meson exchange is dominant in both cases, the contribution of the pseudovector unnatural parity meson exchange is very small. Using Regge phenomenology, we predicted high energy behavior for double polarized observables $\mathbb{E}$, $\mathbb{F}$, $\mathbb{G}$, and $\mathbb{H}$ for the reactions $γp\to π^0p$ and $γp\to a_0^0p$.

## Key claim

The Regge model predicts that the beam polarization asymmetry Σ for scalar meson photoproduction (γp → a₀⁰p) is opposite in sign to that for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction (γp → π⁰p), while the unpolarized cross sections are similar; however, the authors qualify that previous SLAC Σ measurements for π⁰ photoproduction above Eγ = 4 GeV are at variance with more recent GlueX measurements at Eγ = 9 GeV and may be unreliable due to background contamination from Compton scattering.

## Method

The authors performed three Regge-model fits to available unpolarized and polarized data for γp → π⁰p above Eγ = 3 GeV, using the framework of Kashevarov (2017). The fits compared differential cross sections from SLAC and DESY with beam asymmetry Σ data from SLAC and GlueX, target asymmetry T data from Daresbury and DESY, and recoil polarization P data from CEA, to evaluate the discrepancy between the older SLAC and newer GlueX Σ measurements. The model was then extended to predict double-polarized observables E, F, G, and H for both π⁰ and a₀⁰ photoproduction at Eγ = 8.7 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The dip in Σ beam asymmetry for γp → π⁰p at |t| ∼ 0.5 GeV², reported by SLAC at Eγ = 4, 6, and 10 GeV, is almost absent (or within uncertainties) in GlueX measurements at Eγ = 8.7 GeV.
- At Eγ = 15 GeV and t = −0.5 (GeV/c)², two-thirds of the observed yield in the SLAC data was due to Compton scattering background.
- Predictions for double-polarized observables E, F, G, and H are given at Eγ = 8.7 GeV (W = 4.15 GeV) for both γp → π⁰p and γp → a₀⁰p.
- The differential cross section for π⁰ photoproduction shows a dip at |t| ∼ 0.5 GeV², visible in measurements from SLAC, DESY, and CLAS at Eγ = 3 to 15 GeV.

## Context

This work extends Regge phenomenology for light meson photoproduction, building on prior approaches by Goldstein (1973), Donnachie (2015), Yu (2011), Mathieu (2015), and Kashevarov (2017). It directly addresses the discrepancy between SLAC Σ measurements (Anderson 1971) and GlueX Σ data (GlueX 2017) for π⁰ photoproduction, suggesting the SLAC data may be unreliable due to Compton scattering contamination. The predictions for double-polarized observables E, F, G, and H provide testable targets for future experiments at JLab, ELSA, and SPring-8, and the analysis of GlueX Σ data for a₀⁰ photoproduction is noted as in progress (Fegan 2020).
