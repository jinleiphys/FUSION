# Net-proton fluctuations influenced by baryon stopping and quark deconfinement

> Preliminary data from the Beam-Energy Scan II measurements by the STAR Collaboration at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider suggest a dip in the fourth-to-second-order cumulant ratio when plotted vs. beam energy. At the same energy range where the structure appears, a transition from hadrons to quarks is expected, the deconfinement transition. In this paper, the role of quark deconfinement in establishing fluctuaitions in the early stages of the collision is considered. Two models are compared: one with stopping occurring on a baryon-by-baryon basis, and a second where stopping proceeds through quark degrees of freedom. In the latter model, the fluctuation of baryon number is significantly reduced and this signal is found to survive recombination into hadrons and the subsequent diffusion. The transformation from baryon to quark stopping thus produces a dip in the fourth-to-second-order cumulant ratio when plotted vs. beam energy, consistent with observations.

# Net-proton fluctuations influenced by baryon stopping and quark deconfinement

## Key claim
The dip in the fourth-to-second-order cumulant ratio (kurtosis) of net-proton distributions observed by STAR at beam energies around 7.7–20 GeV can be explained by a transition from baryon-by-baryon stopping to quark-level stopping, without invoking the QCD critical point. The signal from quark degrees of freedom survives recombination into hadrons and subsequent diffusion, but only in higher-order cumulants.

## Method
Two models for baryon number stopping in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions are compared: one where stopped baryon charge is carried by independent baryons, and another where it is carried by independent quarks that later recombine into hadrons. For the quark-stopping scenario, a binomial distribution for quarks in rapidity is combined with a transfer matrix describing hadron movement after recombination, parameterized by a single equilibration parameter α₁₁. Pair production contributions are added identically in both models, assuming independent pair creation with binomial acceptance. The acceptance parameter α is extracted from STAR data as the ratio of net-proton yield in acceptance to the number of participating nucleons.

## Key numbers
- The acceptance parameter α ranges from approximately 0.02 at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 0.12 at √sNN = 200 GeV (Fig. 3).
- For the quark-stopping model, the recombination parameter α₁₁ = 0.75 is used, independent of collision energy.
- The number of stopped baryons Bs = 337 is used for initial quark redistributions in the recombination calculation.
- The transition from baryon to quark stopping is observed around √sNN ≈ 14–20 GeV across all fluctuation measures.
- At √sNN = 200 GeV, both models give similar predictions because pair production dominates over stopped charge.

## Context
This work extends the global conservation baseline calculations of Vovchenko (2021) and Braun-Munzinger (2020) by introducing a microscopic mechanism for the observed deviations. It contrasts with critical-point explanations (Stephanov, 1998; 1999) by attributing the kurtosis dip to the onset of deconfinement, previously studied by the NA61 collaboration (Gazdzicki, 2004; 2011). The recombination model builds on the quark recombination formalism of Fries (2003; 2004) and Muller (2022). The results enable using higher-order cumulants as a probe of the deconfinement transition in the Beam Energy Scan II data, and suggest that scaled variance and skewness are insensitive to the nature of charge carriers due to rapid equilibration.
