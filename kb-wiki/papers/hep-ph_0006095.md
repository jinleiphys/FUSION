# Tritium Beta Decay, Neutrino Mass Matrices and Interactions Beyond the Standard Model

> The interference of charge-changing interactions, weaker than the V-A Standard Model (SM) interaction and having a different Lorentz structure, with that SM interaction, can, in principle, produce effects near the end point of the Tritium beta decay spectrum which are of a different character from those produced by the purely kinematic effect of neutrino mass expected in the simplest extension of the SM. We show that the existence of more than one mass eigenstate can lead to interference effects at the end point that are stronger than those occurring over the entire spectrum. We discuss these effects both for the special case of Dirac neutrinos and the more general case of Majorana neutrinos and show that, for the present precision of the experiments, one formula should suffice to express the interference effects in all cases. Implications for "sterile" neutrinos are noted.

## Key claim

Interference between the Standard Model V-A interaction and weaker non-SM currents with different Lorentz structure can produce excess counts near the tritium beta decay endpoint that mimic a negative neutrino mass squared in analyses that assume only the Standard Model; these interference effects are stronger near the endpoint than over the full spectrum when multiple mass eigenstates exist.

## Method

The authors derive the differential and integral tritium beta decay spectra including interference terms between the Standard Model left-chiral vector current and right-chiral vector, right-chiral scalar, and left-chiral scalar currents, for both Dirac and Majorana neutrinos. They express the spectrum as a sum over neutrino mass eigenstates with mixing angles for each current type, then approximate the near-endpoint form to obtain an analytic expression for the integral spectrum that depends on effective parameters ε_k and φ_k encoding the interference strengths.

## Key numbers

- Limits on relative strengths of additional currents from other experiments: ρ_R ≤ 0.07, ρ_{S_R} ≤ 0.1, ρ_{S_L} ≤ 0.01 (assuming unit value for the cosine of the angle between current eigenstates)
- The ratio m_e/E_β varies by less than 2 parts in 10^3 as the β kinetic energy varies from 17.5 keV to 18.5 keV
- The scalar interaction interference term is proportional to G_V^2/(G_V^2 + 3G_A^2), making scalar interactions less efficient than right-chiral vector interactions for the same relative strength

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions by Koefed-Hansen (1950s) and Enz (1960s) on how the Lorentz structure of weak currents affects neutrino mass extraction from beta decay, and by Jackson, Treiman and Wyld (1950s) on interference effects including time reversal violation. The authors build on their own recent work (Stephenson, Goldman 1999) showing that interference between SM and non-SM currents can be exacerbated by lepton mixing. The paper enables experimentalists to use a functional form for data analysis that avoids theoretical prejudice toward the Standard Model alone, and notes implications for sterile neutrinos in the context of see-saw models (Gell-Mann, Ramond, Slansky 1979) and pseudo-Dirac neutrinos (Wolfenstein 1981, Schechter and Valle 1981, Bilenky and Petcov 1987).
