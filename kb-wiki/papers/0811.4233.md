# Constraints on nuclear matter parameters of an Effective Chiral Model

> Within an effective non-linear chiral model, we evaluate nuclear matter parameters exploiting the uncertainties in the nuclear saturation properties. The model is sternly constrained with minimal free parameters, which display the interlink between nuclear incompressibility ($K$), the nucleon effective mass ($m^{\star}$), the pion decay constant ($f_π$) and the $σ-$meson mass ($m_σ$). The best fit among the various parameter set is then extracted and employed to study the resulting Equation of state (EOS). Further, we also discuss the consequences of imposing constraints on nuclear EOS from Heavy-Ion collision and other phenomenological model predictions.

## Key claim

Within an effective non-linear chiral model, the authors evaluate nuclear matter parameters by exploiting uncertainties in nuclear saturation properties, finding that the best parameter sets must simultaneously satisfy constraints from heavy-ion collision flow data, the experimental sigma meson mass (513 ± 32 MeV), and the pion decay constant (130 ± 5 MeV), with the most consistent parameter sets corresponding to nucleon effective mass ratios m*/m between 0.84 and 0.86.

## Method

The authors use a chiral sigma-omega model with dynamically generated vector meson mass and higher-order scalar field interactions (up to quartic order) in the mean-field approximation. They fix the five model parameters (three meson-nucleon coupling constants Cσ, Cω, Cρ and two higher-order scalar field constants b and c) by solving the field equations self-consistently at saturation density ρ0 = 0.153 fm⁻³, with binding energy per nucleon fixed at -16.3 MeV, while varying the nucleon effective mass ratio Y = m*/m from 0.75 to 0.90. The resulting equation of state is then compared with experimental heavy-ion collision flow data and other relativistic mean-field models.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon: B/A - m = -16.3 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass ratio range explored: Y = m*/m = 0.75 to 0.90
- Calculated sigma meson mass range: 340 to 700 MeV across the parameter sets
- Calculated pion decay constant range: 110.185 to 193.437 MeV across the parameter sets
- Calculated nuclear incompressibility range: 163 to 1098 MeV across the parameter sets
- Best fit parameter set 9 (Y = 0.83): mσ = 562.500 MeV, fπ = 129.465 MeV, K = 391 MeV
- Best fit parameter set 11 (Y = 0.85): mσ = 509.644 MeV, fπ = 139.710 MeV, K = 303 MeV
- Best fit parameter set 13 (Y = 0.87): mσ = 444.614 MeV, fπ = 153.984 MeV, K = 231 MeV
- Experimental sigma meson mass constraint: mσ = 513 ± 32 MeV (from Murakami et al.)
- Experimental pion decay constant: fπ ≈ 131 MeV (from Particle Data Group)
- Heavy-ion collision incompressibility bound: K = 167 to 380 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral model studies by Jha and Mishra (2006, 2008) that included hyperons and neutron star applications, but now systematically constrains parameters using vacuum properties of chiral symmetry. The authors compare their results with the relativistic mean-field NL3 parameterization (which has K = 271.6 MeV and m*/m = 0.60) and non-relativistic DBHF calculations, finding that their chiral model produces softer equations of state at high density despite having comparable incompressibility values. The study enables future applications to neutron star structure at densities of 2-5 ρ0, where the softer behavior at high density may have astrophysical consequences.
