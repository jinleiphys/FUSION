# Momentum-dependence of charmonium spectral functions from lattice QCD

> We compute correlators and spectral functions for J/psi and eta_c mesons at nonzero momentum on anisotropic lattices with Nf=2. We find no evidence of significant momentum dependence at the current level of precision. In the pseudoscalar channel, the ground state appears to survive up to T~450MeV or 2.1T_c. In the vector channel, medium modifications may occur at lower temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper finds no evidence of significant momentum dependence in charmonium spectral functions at the current level of precision, but notes that the reconstruction of spectral functions becomes progressively more uncertain with increasing momentum, so some additional momentum dependence cannot be ruled out.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate flavors of O(a)-improved Wilson fermions on anisotropic lattices with spatial lattice spacing a_s = 0.162 fm and anisotropy xi = 6. They computed correlators and spectral functions for J/psi and eta_c mesons at momenta p = 0, 0.66, 0.93, 1.14, and 1.32 GeV using the maximum entropy method, with all-to-all propagators and dilution in time, color, and varying levels in space and spin.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing a_s = 0.162 fm and anisotropy xi = a_s/a_tau = 6
- Inverse temporal lattice spacing a_tau^{-1} = 7.35(3) GeV
- Momentum values: p = 0, 0.66, 0.93, 1.14, 1.32 GeV (corresponding to n^2 = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4)
- Temperatures studied: 92 MeV (0.42 T_c), 230 MeV (1.05 T_c), 263 MeV (1.20 T_c), 306 MeV (1.40 T_c), 368 MeV (1.68 T_c), 408 MeV (1.86 T_c), 459 MeV (2.09 T_c)
- In the pseudoscalar channel, the ground state appears to survive up to T ~ 450 MeV or 2.1 T_c
- In the vector channel, the J/psi appears to dissolve for T > 370 MeV or 1.7 T_c
- The renormalised anisotropy on the 12^3 x 80 lattice is 5.829 +/- 0.05, giving a 3% systematic error

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of charmonium spectral functions at zero momentum (Aarts 2007) to nonzero momentum, providing the first results in this direction. The results are relevant for interpreting J/psi suppression patterns in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where different scenarios (sequential suppression and charmonium regeneration) predict different transverse momentum dependence. The authors note that finer lattices are needed to provide greater temporal resolution and more reliable spectral function determinations, and that computing correlators of the conserved vector current will permit quantitative determination of the charm quark diffusion rate.
