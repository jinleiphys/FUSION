# Evidence of strong dynamic core excitation in $^{19}$C resonant break-up

> The resonant break-up of $^{19}$C on protons measured at RIKEN [Phys. Lett. B 660, 320 (2008)] is analyzed in terms of a valence-core model for $^{19}$C including possible core excitations. The analysis of the angular distribution of a prominent peak appearing in the relative-energy spectrum could be well described with this model and is consistent with the previous assignment of $5/2^{+}$ for this state. Inclusion of core-excitation effects are found to be essential to give the correct magnitude of the cross section for this state. By contrast, the calculation assuming an inert $^{18}$C core is found to largely underestimate the data.

## Key claim

The resonant break-up cross section of \(^{19}\)C on protons at 70 MeV/nucleon is dominated almost entirely by dynamic core excitation of the \(^{18}\)C core, overwhelming the role of the halo neutron; this effect is much larger than in the previously studied case of \(^{11}\)Be, and an inert-core calculation assuming a pure \(2s_{1/2}\rightarrow 1d_{5/2}\) single-particle transition underestimates the data by a large margin.

## Method

The authors analyzed the resonant break-up of \(^{19}\)C on protons at 70 MeV/nucleon measured at RIKEN (Satou et al., 2008) using a valence-core model for \(^{19}\)C that includes core excitations of \(^{18}\)C. They performed extended distorted-wave Born approximation (XDWBA) and extended continuum-discretized coupled-channels (XCDCC) calculations that incorporate both static and dynamic core excitations, using a semi-microscopic particle-core model for \(^{19}\)C based on antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) densities and the JLM nucleon-nucleon interaction for the core-target potential.

## Key numbers

- The prominent peak in the break-up cross section appears at an excitation energy \(E_x = 1.46 \pm 0.10\) MeV, consistent with a \(5/2^+\) resonance assignment.
- The neutron separation energy of \(^{19}\)C is \(\epsilon_B = 0.589\) MeV.
- The first excited \(2^+\) state of the core (\(^{18}\)C) lies at 1.6 MeV.
- The incident beam energy was 70 MeV/nucleon.
- The first \(5/2^+\) resonance has a weight of 0.721 in the \(|2^+ \otimes s_{1/2}\rangle\) configuration and 0.276 in the \(|0^+ \otimes (\ell s)j\rangle\) configuration; the second \(5/2^+\) resonance has a weight of 0.657 in the \(|2^+ \otimes d_{5/2}\rangle\) configuration and 0.200 in the \(|0^+ \otimes (\ell s)j\rangle\) configuration.
- The JLM folding potential used renormalization factors of 1.2 for the real part and 0.8 for the imaginary part, with a range parameter \(t = 1.4\) fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dynamic core excitation in halo nuclei, particularly the case of \(^{11}\)Be (Mor12a; Che16), where valence and core excitations were found to be of similar magnitude. The present analysis of \(^{19}\)C shows a much stronger dominance of core excitation, attributed to the lower excitation energy (1.6 MeV) and larger deformation of \(^{18}\)C compared to \(^{10}\)Be. The results contradict the naive inert-core picture of halo nuclei and suggest that similar effects will be important for heavier halo candidates such as \(^{31}\)Ne and \(^{37}\)Mg. The analysis also resolves a discrepancy noted in earlier inert-core AGS-Faddeev calculations (Cre11b) that could not explain the data magnitude.
