# Complex Langevin dynamics and other approaches at finite chemical potential

> I review the presence of the sign problem in lattice QCD at nonzero baryon density and its relation with the overlap and Silver Blaze problems. I then discuss progress in some cases where the sign problem can be handled, either because the sign problem is absent or because it is milder than in full QCD. Some time is spent on effective three-dimensional models, which can be treated with a variety of methods. I conclude with a discussion of the applicability of complex Langevin dynamics at nonzero density.

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics can solve severe sign and Silver Blaze problems in certain theories at nonzero chemical potential, but there is no formal proof of correctness for complex actions, and "disasters of various degrees" were encountered in early applications in the mid-1980s.

## Method

The paper reviews the sign problem in lattice QCD at nonzero baryon density, its relation to the overlap and Silver Blaze problems, and then surveys cases where simulations are possible because the sign problem is absent (e.g., two-colour QCD and G₂ gauge theory), milder (e.g., effective three-dimensional SU(3) spin models and strong-coupling Polyakov loop models), or can be handled via complex Langevin dynamics, which complexifies the degrees of freedom (e.g., SU(N) gauge fields become SL(N, C) variables) and seeks a real positive distribution in the complexified space via a stochastic process.

## Key numbers

- In the SU(3) spin model, the effective parameters map to four-dimensional lattice QCD as β ∼ (β₄D/18)^(N_τ) and h = (2κ)^(N_τ), where κ is the Wilson hopping parameter and μ corresponds to μ/T in four dimensions.
- For the pure SU(3) gauge theory effective model with N_τ = 4, the nearest-neighbour coupling is λ₁(u, N_τ=4) = u⁴ exp[4(4u⁴ + 12u⁵ − 14u⁶ + ... + (1035317/5120)u¹⁰ + ...)] and the next-to-nearest neighbour coupling is λ₂(u, N_τ=4) = u⁸[12u² + 26u⁴ + 364u⁶ + ...], where u(β) = β/18 + ...
- The critical temperature from four-dimensional lattice simulations of pure SU(3) gauge theory is 270 MeV, compared with the value obtained from the one-coupling effective three-dimensional theory.
- In the relativistic Bose gas at nonzero chemical potential, the onset occurs at μ_c ∼ 1.15 (in lattice units with am = λ = 1), and the average phase factor in the phase-quenched theory behaves as ⟨e^(iθ)⟩_pq = Z/Z_pq = e^(−ΩΔf) with Ω = N_s³N_τ, dropping exponentially with volume.
- In one-dimensional QCD, the phase-quenched theory has a transition at μ = μ_c = arcsin m (with m the quark mass), while the full theory has no transition when the gauge group is U(N_c); the chiral condensate is μ-independent (Silver Blaze).

## Context

This review extends the discussion of the sign problem in lattice QCD at nonzero baryon density, building on earlier work by de Forcrand (2010) and Levkova (2012) on standard methods at small μ/T, and by Lombardo (2012) on the phase diagram at vanishing and small chemical potential. It connects the sign problem to the overlap problem (where the phase-quenched ensemble differs from the full theory) and the Silver Blaze problem (where μ-dependence must cancel exactly in thermodynamic observables at zero temperature), as formulated by Cohen (2003) and analysed via the OSV mechanism by Osborn (2005, 2008). The paper surveys recent progress in effective three-dimensional models (e.g., the SU(3) spin model studied by Banks (1983), and systematic strong-coupling expansions by Fromm (2011, 2012)) and complex Langevin dynamics (pioneered by Parisi (1984) and Klauder (1983), with early difficulties reported by Ambjørn (1985, 1986)), and discusses the Lefschetz thimble approach proposed by Cristoforetti (2012) as an alternative.
