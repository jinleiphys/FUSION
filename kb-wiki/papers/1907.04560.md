# Allowed $β$-decay spectrum with numerical electron wave functions

> Using numerical electron wave functions and state-of-the-art nuclear many-body methods, I evaluate the $β$-decay spectra for typical decay channels of spherical nuclei. I check errors brought by various approximations used for deriving the analytical shape factors (the so-called Fermi Function) of allowed decay. I estimate the errors brought by different electric charge distributions and give a way of estimation of $β$-spectra with available decay data of specific nuclei. I found that the traditional ways of approximating the electron wave functions by Fermi Function could be a very severe source of error for spectra simulation.

## Key claim

Using numerical electron wave functions instead of the traditional analytical Fermi Function approximation, the author finds that the Fermi Function can introduce errors of 1% to 10% in beta-decay spectra depending on the nucleus and decay energy, with heavier nuclei and larger Q-values showing the largest deviations.

## Method

The author solves the Dirac equation numerically using the RADIAL subroutine to obtain electron wave functions for point-charge and three finite-size nuclear charge distributions (uniform, Fermi, and modified Gaussian). These numerical wave functions are combined with nuclear many-body calculations, shell model with GXPF1a Hamiltonians for Ca isotopes and spherical QRPA with CD-Bonn potentials for Cd isotopes, to compute differential decay widths and beta spectra for four allowed Gamow-Teller decay branches of spherical nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For decay branch IV (132Cd, Q=8.650 MeV), the deviation of the Fermi Function from numerical point-charge wave functions approaches about 10% at high electron energies
- For decay branch I (50Ca, Q=3.621 MeV), the deviation of the Fermi Function from numerical point-charge wave functions is less than 1%
- For decay branch II (58Ca, Q=11.461 MeV), the deviation of the Fermi Function from numerical point-charge wave functions is about 4%
- For decay branch III (120Cd, Q=2.282 MeV), the deviation of the Fermi Function from numerical point-charge wave functions is about 2%
- The inclusion of exact neutrino wave functions beyond the long-wavelength approximation modifies decay widths by less than 1% for all branches
- The relative correction from finite nuclear charge size is smaller than 2% for all branches
- The absolute reduction of decay width from using finite-size charge distributions relative to the Fermi Function varies from several percent to nearly ten percent, reaching as high as 20% for decay branch IV at electron energies above 7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analytical beta-decay formalism of Fermi (1934) and the finite-nuclear-size corrections of Buehler (1963) and Wilkinson (1990) by using numerical electron wave functions combined with modern nuclear many-body methods. The results are relevant to the reactor antineutrino anomaly (Mention et al., 2011), where discrepancies between predicted and measured reactor neutrino spectra have been attributed to possible sterile neutrinos; the author shows that traditional approximations in beta-spectra calculations can introduce errors large enough to affect such interpretations. The work enables more accurate neutrino spectra simulations for reactor experiments by providing quantified error estimates and a method to improve spectra using available decay data.
