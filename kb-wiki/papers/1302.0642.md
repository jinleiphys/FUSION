# Chiral and U_A(1) symmetry in correlation functions in medium

> In this review, we will discuss how the chiral symmetry and U_A(1) breaking effects are reflected in the correlation functions. Using the Banks-Casher formula, one can identify the density of zero eigenvalues to be the common ingredient that governs the chiral symmetry breaking in correlation functions between currents composed of light quarks with or without a heavy quark. Similarly the presence of the U_A(1) breaking effect is determined through the contribution of the topologically non trivial configurations that depends on the number of flavors. We also discuss how the symmetry breaking effects are reflected in the gluon correlation functions. Finally, we review the Witten Veneziano formula for the eta' mass in medium.

## Key claim

This review argues that chiral symmetry breaking in all correlation functions, including those involving light quarks, heavy quarks, and baryons, is governed by a common ingredient: the density of zero eigenvalues of the Dirac operator, as identified through the Banks-Casher formula. The U_A(1) breaking effect, however, arises from topologically nontrivial gauge configurations and is in principle independent of chiral symmetry breaking, though its manifestation in n-point functions depends on the number of flavors.

## Method

The authors use the Banks-Casher formula to relate the chiral condensate to the density of zero eigenvalues of the Dirac operator, then demonstrate analytically that differences between correlation functions of chiral partners (scalar-pseudoscalar, vector-axial vector, and baryon parity partners) vanish when the zero-mode current density matrix vanishes. For U_A(1) effects, they separate the QCD partition function by topological charge and show that configurations with nonzero topological charge contribute to correlation functions only when the number of external operators saturates the zero-mode integrals. They also derive low-energy theorems for gluonic correlation functions using scaling arguments and the renormalization group, and generalize the Witten-Veneziano formula to finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- The Witten-Veneziano formula yields m_η' ≈ 464 MeV using f_π = 130 MeV, ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩ = (0.35 GeV)^4, and b = 11 for pure glue theory.
- For the charm sector, using f_D = 206.7 MeV, m_D = 1865 MeV, m_D0 = 2318 MeV, m_c = 1.26 GeV, and ⟨q̄q⟩ = (0.225 GeV)^3, the sum rule gives f_D0 = 182 MeV.
- The low-energy theorems for gluonic correlators at zero momentum give S(q=0) = (18/b) ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩ and P_0(q=0) = -(8/b) ⟨(α_s/π)G²⟩ in pure gauge theory.

## Context

This review extends the Banks-Casher framework (Banks and Casher, 1980) to show that the zero-mode density is the unifying factor for chiral symmetry breaking across all correlation functions, building on work by Cohen (1996). For U_A(1) effects, it builds on the analysis of Lee (1996) and Evans (1996) regarding topological configurations and flavor dependence. The generalized Witten-Veneziano formula for the η' mass in medium extends the original work of Witten (1979) and Veneziano (1979), and the finite-temperature low-energy theorems follow the approach of Lee (2000). The results enable predictions for how the η' mass behaves near chiral symmetry restoration, which can be tested in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and nuclear target experiments.
