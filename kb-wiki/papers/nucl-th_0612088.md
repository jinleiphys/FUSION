# Proton Threshold States in the 22Na(p,gamma)23Mg Reaction and Astrophysical Implications

> Proton threshold states in 23Mg are important for the astrophysically relevant proton capture reaction 22Na(p,gamma)23Mg. In the indirect determination of the resonance strength of the lowest states, which were not accessible by direct methods, some of the spin-parity assignments remained experimentally uncertain. We have investigated these states with Shell Model, Coulomb displacement, and Thomas-Ehrman shift calculations. From the comparison of calculated and observed properties we relate the lowest relevant resonance state at E=7643 keV to an excited 3/2+ state in accordance with a recent experimental determination by Jenkins et al.. From this we deduce significantly improved values for the 22Na(p,gamma)23Mg reaction rate at stellar temperatures below T_9=0.1K.

# Proton Threshold States in the 22Na(p,γ)23Mg Reaction and Astrophysical Implications

## Key claim

The 7643 keV resonance state in 23Mg, which is critical for the 22Na(p,γ)23Mg reaction rate at stellar temperatures below T₉ = 0.1 K, is assigned spin-parity 3/2⁺ based on shell model calculations combined with Coulomb displacement and Thomas-Ehrman shift analyses, consistent with the experimental determination by Jenkins et al. (2004, 2006). This assignment eliminates the possibility of an l=0 orbital angular momentum transfer, thereby significantly reducing the previously large uncertainty (more than two orders of magnitude) in the resonance strength.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations using the OXBASH code in the sd configuration space with Wildenthal matrix elements to compute positive parity states in the mirror nuclei ²³Na and ²³Mg. They applied Coulomb shifts from a charge-dependent isospin-nonconserving interaction and Thomas-Ehrman shifts calculated from Woods-Saxon single particle potential models to relate the experimentally known states in ²³Na to their isobaric analogues in ²³Mg, using the mean deviation of ±60 keV from low-lying bound states to establish an expected accuracy of ±120 keV for the threshold region.

## Key numbers

- The proton capture threshold in ²³Mg is Qp = 7579 keV (Audi 1995)
- The first resonance state at Ex = 7583 keV has spin 5/2⁺ and negligible contribution to the reaction rate due to barrier penetration
- The resonance strength for the 7643 keV state was previously constrained to 1.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ ≤ ωγ ≤ 3.1 × 10⁻⁸ meV (Schmidt 1995); the present work gives an upper limit of ωγ = 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁰ meV using the 3/2⁺ assignment
- The 7622 keV state resonance strength from the present work is ωγ = 1.0 × 10⁻¹³ meV (high limit)
- The mean deviation of calculated versus experimental energies for low-lying bound states is about ±60 keV, with the largest deviation being 138 keV
- The experimental B(GT) value for the 8166 keV, 5/2⁺ state in ²³Mg is 0.290 ± 0.015, matching the shell model prediction of 0.312 for the 5/2₁₀⁺ state

## Context

This work extends the approach of Champagne et al. (1993) and Iliadis et al. (1996) in applying shell model calculations to proton threshold states of astrophysical interest in the Ne-Na cycle, specifically addressing the uncertain spin assignments that remained after the indirect transfer reaction study of Schmidt et al. (1995). The results confirm the experimental spin assignment of Jenkins et al. (2004, 2006) from a purely theoretical perspective, and enable improved reaction rate calculations for the ²²Na(p,γ)²³Mg reaction at stellar temperatures below T₉ = 0.1 K, which is relevant for understanding ²²Na production and destruction in explosive hydrogen burning environments such as novae.
