# Infinite-cutoff renormalization of the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N3LO

> Naively, the "best" method of renormalization is the one where a momentum cutoff is taken to infinity while maintaining stable results due to a cutoff-dependent adjustment of counterterms. We have applied this renormalization method in the non-perturbative calculation of phase-shifts for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering using chiral NN potentials up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO). For lower partial waves, we find that there is either no convergence with increasing order or, if convergence occurs, the results do not always converge to the empirical values. For higher partial waves, we always observe convergence to the empirical phase shifts (except for the 3G5 state). Furthermore, no matter what the order is, one can use only one or no counterterm per partial wave, creating a rather erratic scheme of power counting that does not allow for a systematic order-by-order improvement of the predictions. The conclusion is that infinite-cutoff renormalization is inappropriate for chiral NN interactions, which should not come as a surprise, since the chiral effective field theory, these interactions are based upon, is designed for momenta below the chiral-symmetry breaking scale of about 1 GeV. Therefore, this value for the hard scale should also be perceived as the appropriate upper limit for the momentum cutoff.

## Key claim

Infinite-cutoff renormalization of chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to N3LO fails to produce systematic order-by-order convergence to empirical phase shifts for lower partial waves, and the scheme requires at most one counterterm per partial wave regardless of chiral order, which contradicts the expectations of Weinberg counting and prevents systematic improvement of predictions.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Blankenbecler-Sugar equation (reduced to a nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation) for neutron-proton scattering using chiral NN potentials at LO, NLO, NNLO, and N3LO, with a Gaussian regulator function. They varied the momentum cutoff Lambda from 0.5 GeV to 10 GeV, fitting at most one counterterm per partial wave to empirical scattering lengths (for S-waves) or to phase shifts at 50, 100, or 200 MeV laboratory energy (for higher partial waves), and examined convergence of phase shifts as Lambda increased.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff Lambda varied from 0.5 GeV to 10 GeV (with n=2 for LO, NLO, NNLO and n=3 for N3LO)
- S-wave scattering lengths fitted: a_s = -23.740 fm (1S0) and a_t = 5.417 fm (3S1)
- For partial waves with J <= 2, counterterms were fitted to the phase shift at T_lab = 50 MeV from the Nijmegen multi-energy np phase-shift analysis
- For J >= 3, counterterms were fitted at T_lab = 100 MeV or 200 MeV
- At N3LO, convergence with increasing cutoff was achieved for all phase parameters with J <= 6, but cutoff-converged results showed large discrepancies with empirical phase shifts in 1S0, 3S1, 3D1, 3D2, and epsilon_2

## Context

This work extends the infinite-cutoff renormalization studies of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) at LO and of Valderrama and Arriola (2005, 2007) at NNLO, providing the first full analysis through N3LO for all partial waves with J <= 6. The results contradict the expectation of Weinberg counting (Weinberg, 1990, 1991) that the number of counterterms should increase with chiral order, and instead show that at most one counterterm per partial wave is needed at any order. The authors conclude that infinite-cutoff renormalization is inappropriate for chiral NN interactions because chiral EFT is designed for momenta below the chiral-symmetry breaking scale of about 1 GeV, which should be the upper limit for the momentum cutoff.
