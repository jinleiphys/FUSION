# "RHIC serves the perfect fluid" -- Hydrodynamic flow of the QGP

> The bulk of the hot and dense matter created at RHIC behaves like an almost ideal fluid. I present the evidence for this and also discuss what we can learn about the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) from the gradual breakdown of ideal fluid dynamic behavior at large transverse momenta, lower beam energies, larger impact parameters, and forward rapidities.

## Key claim

The bulk of the matter created in Au+Au collisions at RHIC behaves like an almost ideal (perfect) fluid, but this conclusion is qualified by the gradual breakdown of ideal fluid dynamic behavior at large transverse momenta (above about 1.5 GeV/c for mesons and 2.2 GeV/c for baryons), lower beam energies, larger impact parameters, and forward rapidities, where viscous effects, particularly from the hadronic stage, become important.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic equations for ideal hydrodynamics under the assumption of boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using initial conditions fixed by fitting pion and proton spectra at midrapidity in central collisions and the centrality dependence of total charged multiplicity. Initial entropy density profiles for all impact parameters were calculated from collision geometry using the Glauber model, and all hadron spectra for non-central collisions (including elliptic flow) were then parameter-free predictions. The freeze-out was implemented via the Cooper-Frye algorithm, with chemical freeze-out at the critical temperature and kinetic decoupling at lower temperatures, including feeddown from unstable hadron resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the QCD phase transition: Tc = 173 ± 15 MeV from lattice QCD
- Critical energy density: ec ≈ 0.7 GeV/fm³
- Initial thermalization time upper limit: τ₀ ≤ 0.6 fm/c
- Initial peak energy density in central Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV: e₀ ≈ 30 GeV/fm³
- Initial central fireball temperature: T₀ ≈ 360 MeV ≈ 2 Tc
- Initial entropy density in fireball center: s₀ = 110 fm⁻³
- Freeze-out energy density: e_dec = 0.075 GeV/fm³
- Speed of sound above 2 Tc: c_s² ≈ 1/3; near Tc drops to c_s² ≈ 1/20 (the "softest point"); in hadron resonance gas phase: c_s² ≈ 0.15
- Elliptic flow data agree with ideal hydrodynamics for p⊥ ≲ 1.5 GeV/c and impact parameters b ≤ 7 fm
- Hydrodynamic prediction breaks down above p⊥ ≳ 1.5 GeV/c for mesons and above p⊥ ≳ 2.2 GeV/c for baryons
- Spatial eccentricity of time-integrated hydrodynamic pion emission function at b = 7 fm: ⟨ε_x⟩ = 0.14 (56% of initial value ε_x(τ₀) = 0.25); STAR measurement: ⟨ε_x⟩ = 0.11 ± 0.035 (45 ± 15% of initial deformation)
- Normalized sound attenuation length at RHIC: Γ_s/τ cannot be much larger than about 0.1, implying η/s close to the conjectured lower limit of η/s = ħ/(4π)

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling approach pioneered by Kolb, Sorge, and others (Sorge 1997; Kolb, Sorge, and Heinz 2000) to interpret RHIC data as evidence for a nearly perfect fluid, contradicting earlier expectations that lattice QCD results supported a weakly interacting perturbative QGP. The paper enables future studies of QGP transport properties through viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, and identifies the need for hybrid hydro+cascade approaches (Teaney 2001) to handle the highly viscous hadronic stage. It also motivates experimental programs at the LHC and with uranium-uranium collisions at RHIC (Heinz and Kuhlman 2005) to confirm non-viscous fluid behavior at higher energy densities.
