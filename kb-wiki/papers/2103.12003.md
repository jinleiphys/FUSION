# Correlating Non-Resonant Di-Electron Searches at the LHC to the Cabibbo-Angle Anomaly and Lepton Flavour Universality Violation

> In addition to the existing strong indications for lepton flavour university violation (LFUV) in low energy precision experiments, CMS recently released an analysis of non-resonant di-lepton pairs which could constitute the first sign of LFUV in high-energy LHC searches. In this article we show that the Cabibbo angle anomaly, an (apparent) violation of first row and column CKM unitarity with $\approx3\,σ$ significance, and the CMS result can be correlated and commonly explained in a model independent way by the operator $[Q_{\ell q}^{(3)}]_{1111} = (\bar{\ell}_1γ^μσ^I\ell_1)(\bar{q}_1γ_μσ^Iq_1)$. This is possible without violating the bounds from the non-resonant di-lepton search of ATLAS (which interestingly also observed slightly more events than expected in the electron channel) nor from $R(π)=π\toμν/π\to e ν$. We find a combined preference for the new physics hypothesis of $4.5\,σ$ and predict $1.0004<R(π)<1.0009$ (95\%~CL) which can be tested in the near future with the forthcoming results of the PEN experiment.

## Key claim

The Cabibbo angle anomaly (a roughly 3-sigma deficit in first-row CKM unitarity) and a recent CMS excess in non-resonant di-electron production can be correlated and commonly explained by a single Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) operator, without violating bounds from ATLAS di-lepton searches or charged pion decay measurements. The combined preference for this new physics hypothesis is 4.5 sigma.

## Method

The authors perform a model-independent effective field theory analysis using the operator [Q_lq^(3)]_1111, which generates both a tree-level contribution to beta decays (addressing the Cabibbo angle anomaly) and a contribution to non-resonant di-electron production at the LHC. They recast the ATLAS di-lepton search limits for this specific operator structure (which has opposite sign couplings to up and down quarks compared to the operator ATLAS originally constrained) and compute the CMS di-muon to di-electron double ratio using parton distribution functions and tree-level cross sections. A combined chi-squared fit is performed including the CKM unitarity constraint, the CMS di-lepton ratio, and the R(pi) measurement.

## Key numbers

* The deficit in first-row CKM unitarity is |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 0.9985(5), corresponding to approximately 3 sigma significance.
* The best-fit Wilson coefficient from the Cabibbo angle anomaly alone is [C_lq^(3)]_1111 = 1.22(4)/(10 TeV)^2.
* The CMS di-lepton data gives a best-fit Wilson coefficient of approximately 1.0/(10 TeV)^2 with Delta chi^2 approximately -10 relative to the Standard Model.
* The ATLAS 95% CL upper limit on the Wilson coefficient is [C_lq^(3)]_1111 less than approximately 1.4/(10 TeV)^2.
* The combined fit yields a minimum at [C_lq^(3)]_1111 approximately 1.1/(10 TeV)^2 with Delta chi^2 approximately -20, corresponding to a 4.5 sigma preference over the Standard Model.
* The 95% CL allowed range from the combined fit is 0.6/(10 TeV)^2 less than [C_lq^(3)]_1111 less than 1.4/(10 TeV)^2.
* The predicted range for R(pi) = pi to mu nu / pi to e nu at 95% CL is 1.0004 less than R(pi) less than 1.0009.

## Context

This work connects two previously separate anomalies: the Cabibbo angle anomaly (deficit in CKM unitarity first observed in precision beta decay and kaon decay measurements) and a recent CMS observation of an excess in di-electrons over di-muons at high invariant mass (Sirunyan 2021). The authors extend the approach of Crivellin (2020) and Coutinho (2019), who showed the Cabibbo angle anomaly could be explained by lepton flavour universality violation, by demonstrating that the same operator also predicts a signal in LHC di-electron searches consistent with the CMS data. The results enable future tests: the PEN and PiENu experiments will measure R(pi) with improved precision, and LHC Run 3 data can further scrutinize the di-electron excess.
