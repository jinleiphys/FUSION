# The Rise and Fall of the Ridge in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Recent data from heavy ion collisions at RHIC show unexpectedly large near-angle correlations that broaden longitudinally with increasing centrality. The amplitude of this ridge-like correlation rises rapidly, reaches a maximum, and then falls in the most central collisions. In this letter we explain how this behavior can be explained as initial-state coordinate-space anisotropies converted into final-state momentum-space correlations. We propose $v_n^2/ε_{n,\mathrm{part}}^{2}$ as a useful way to study length scales and provide a prediction for the ridge in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=$ 2.76 TeV.

# The Rise and Fall of the Ridge in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The centrality dependence of the near-side ridge amplitude in heavy ion collisions, its rapid rise, maximum, and subsequent fall in the most central collisions, can be explained by initial-state coordinate-space anisotropies (eccentricity fluctuations) converted into final-state momentum-space correlations, with the rise and fall arising from the interplay between the almond-shaped overlap geometry and statistical fluctuations in the number of participants.

## Method
The authors used a Monte-Carlo Glauber model to calculate participant eccentricities ε_{n,part} for multiple harmonics n, then estimated the ridge amplitude A₁ from the difference between ε_{2,part}² and the standard eccentricity ε_{std}² (for n=2) and from ε_{3,part}² alone (for n=3). They combined these with a conversion efficiency c_n extracted from the measured ratio v₂{2}/ε_{2,part} as a function of particle density (1/S)dN/dy, using a fit function adapted from Knudsen (2008), and with the single-particle rapidity density ρ₀ from data.

## Key numbers
- The conversion efficiency fit parameters for v₂{2}/ε_{2,part} = A/(1 + B/((1/S)dN/dy)) are A = 0.412 and B = 15.175, based on data at √s_{NN} = 0.2 and 2.76 TeV.
- For the n=2 harmonic, the relation between the Fourier component and the Gaussian amplitude is (1/2π)∫(Δρ/√ρ_ref)cos(2Δφ)dΔφ = 0.11 A₁; for n=3, the corresponding factor is 0.039 A₁.
- The azimuthal width of the near-side Gaussian is taken as σ_{Δφ} = 0.65, with weak centrality dependence.
- The n=3 conversion efficiency is estimated as c₃ = c₂/2.6, consistent with AMPT results from Ref. [v3].
- The ridge amplitude A₁ for 200 GeV Au+Au reaches a maximum of 0.7 near ν = 2N_bin/N_part ≈ 5.
- The Gaussian width σ_n(r_part) from fits to ⟨ε_{n,part}²⟩ follows σ_n(r_part) = 7.22 r_part^{-0.916} for participant smearing radii r_part from 0 to 4 fm.

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of the ridge phenomenon first observed at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2008; PHOBOS data) by providing a quantitative explanation for its centrality dependence, which had previously been noted but not explained. The authors build on the proposal by Voloshin (2003) and McLerran (2008) that flux-tube-like structures in the initial state could produce long-range correlations, and on earlier work by Sorensen (2008) linking eccentricity fluctuations to the ridge. The calculation enables experimental extraction of the conversion efficiency c_n = v_n{2}²/ε_{n,part}{2}² as a function of harmonic n, centrality, and particle kinematics, which will probe length scales such as the mean-free-path and acoustic horizon in the quark-gluon plasma. The prediction for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_{NN} = 2.76 TeV provides a testable benchmark for LHC experiments.
