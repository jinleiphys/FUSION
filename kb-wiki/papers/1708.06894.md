# The equation of state for dense nucleonic matter from a metamodeling. I. Foundational aspects

> A metamodeling for the nucleonic equation of state (EOS), inspired from a Taylor expansion around the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, is proposed and parameterized in terms of the empirical parameters. The present knowledge of nuclear empirical parameters is first reviewed in order to estimate their average values and associated uncertainties, and thus defining the parameter space of the metamodeling. They are divided into isoscalar and isovector type, and ordered according to their power in the density expansion. The goodness of the metamodeling is analyzed against the predictions of the original models. In addition, since no correlation among the empirical parameters is assumed a priori, all arbitrary density dependences can be explored, which might not be accessible in existing functionals. Spurious correlations due to the assumed functional form are also removed. This meta-EOS allows direct relations between the uncertainties on the empirical parameters and the density dependence of the nuclear equation of state and its derivatives, and the mapping between the two can be done with standard Bayesian techniques. A sensitivity analysis shows that the more influential empirical parameters are the isovector parameters $L_{sym}$ and $K_{sym}$, and that laboratory constraints at super-saturation densities are essential to reduce the present uncertainties. The present metamodeling for the EOS for nuclear matter is proposed for further applications in neutron stars and supernova matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a metamodel for the nucleonic equation of state (EOS) based on a Taylor expansion around saturation density, parameterized by empirical parameters, which can reproduce a wide range of existing nuclear models while removing spurious correlations imposed by specific functional forms; however, the convergence of the expansion is only demonstrated up to about 4 times saturation density, and the authors note that the expansion is not guaranteed to converge in general.

## Method

The authors construct a metamodel by expanding the energy per nucleon of nuclear matter in a Taylor series in the parameter x = (n0 - nsat)/(3 nsat) around the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter, separating the expansion into isoscalar and isovector channels. They compile predictions from 50 existing nuclear interactions (Skyrme, relativistic mean-field, and relativistic Hartree-Fock models) to extract average values and uncertainties for the empirical parameters, then test the metamodel's ability to reproduce the original EOS predictions across a density range up to 4 nsat.

## Key numbers

- The total average saturation energy Esat from 50 models is -16.03 MeV with a total standard deviation of 0.20 MeV
- The total average symmetry energy Esym is 33.30 MeV with a total standard deviation of 2.65 MeV
- The total average slope parameter Lsym is 76.6 MeV with a total standard deviation of 29.2 MeV
- The total average incompressibility Ksat is 251 MeV with a total standard deviation of 29 MeV
- The total average isovector incompressibility Ksym is -3 MeV with a total standard deviation of 132 MeV
- The total average isoscalar skewness Qsat is 13 MeV with a total standard deviation of 431 MeV
- The total average isovector skewness Qsym is 388 MeV with a total standard deviation of 289 MeV
- The total average isoscalar kurtosis Zsat is 3925 MeV with a total standard deviation of 2270 MeV
- The total average isovector kurtosis Zsym is -5268 MeV with a total standard deviation of 4282 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Taylor-expanding the nuclear EOS around saturation density, previously used by Steiner (2010) and others, by systematically quantifying uncertainties from a large ensemble of 50 existing nuclear models. The metamodel enables direct Bayesian mapping between uncertainties in empirical parameters and the density dependence of the EOS, and is proposed for applications to neutron star structure and supernova matter in a companion paper by Margueron (2017). The sensitivity analysis identifies the isovector parameters Lsym and Ksym as the most influential, highlighting the need for experimental constraints at supersaturation densities from heavy ion collisions.
