# Strangeness in the Scalar Form Factor of the Nucleon

> The scalar form factor of the nucleon and related physical quantities are investigated in the framework of the semibosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio soliton model. We take into account the rotational $1/N_c$ corrections and linear $m_s$ corrections. The strangeness content of the nucleon in the scalar form factor is discussed in detail. In particular, it is found that the $m_s$ corrections play an essential role of reducing the $\langle N | \bar{s} s | N \rangle$ arising from the leading order and rotational $1/N_c$ contributions. We obtain the $σ_{πN} (0)=40.80\;\mbox{MeV}$, $Δσ= σ_{πN} (2m^{2}_π)-σ_{πN} (0) = 18.18\;\mbox{MeV}$ and $\langle r^2\rangle^{S}_{N} = 1.50\;\mbox{fm}^2$. The results are in a remarkable agreement with empirical data analyzed by Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio~\cite{gls}.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the strange quark mass corrections play an essential role in reducing the strangeness content of the nucleon in the scalar form factor, bringing it into agreement with empirical data; without these corrections, the strangeness content is roughly twice as large.

## Method

The authors compute the scalar form factor of the nucleon in the semibosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio soliton model (chiral quark soliton model), treating the nucleon as N_c valence quarks coupled to a polarized Dirac sea bound by a chiral mean field. They include rotational 1/N_c corrections and linear m_s corrections, using a proper-time regularization scheme with the Kahana-Ripka discretized basis, and evaluate the scalar form factor from the Cheng-Dashen point to the space-like region.

## Key numbers

- sigma_piN(0) = 40.80 MeV for constituent quark mass M = 420 MeV with m_s corrections included
- Delta sigma = sigma_piN(2 m_pi^2) - sigma_piN(0) = 18.18 MeV for M = 420 MeV
- Scalar radius squared <r^2>^S_N = 1.50 fm^2 for M = 420 MeV
- y = 2 <N|bar s s|N> / <N|bar u u + bar d d|N> = 0.27 with m_s corrections, compared to 0.48 without m_s corrections (for M = 420 MeV)
- sigma_0 = 46.24 MeV for M = 420 MeV with m_s corrections
- R_s = <N|bar s s|N> / <N|bar u u + bar d d + bar s s|N> = 0.12 for M = 420 MeV with m_s corrections

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio soliton model calculations of baryon properties to the scalar form factor, addressing the long-standing pion-nucleon sigma term discrepancy first highlighted by Cheng and later analyzed by Donoghue and Nappi using the Skyrme model and bag model. The results agree with the empirical analysis of Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1991), who found sigma = 45 +/- 8 MeV and y approximately 0.2, and contradict the claims of Bass and of Ball, Forte, and Tigg that no strangeness contribution is needed. The large scalar radius (1.5 fm^2 versus the electric radius 0.74 fm^2) indicates a long-ranged tail from mesonic clouds, enabling future studies of strangeness in nucleon structure.
