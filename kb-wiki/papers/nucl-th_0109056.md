# Effect of particle-phonon coupling on pairing correlations in nuclei

> The influence of particle-phonon coupling on pairing correlations in nuclei is studied by solving the Dyson equation including the anomalous (pairing) Green function. We develop the formalism for solving the equation with the minimum of approximations. The solution of the Dyson equation is compared with the diagonalization of particle-phonon coupled Hamiltonian in a small space. This comparison reveals that the effect of many-phonon states is incorporated in the Dyson equation. We calculate the pairing gap of the neutron in 120Sn. We compare analytically the present method with a simpler treatment based on Bloch-Horowitz perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The particle-phonon coupling significantly affects pairing correlations in nuclei beyond the mean-field BCS approximation, and the Dyson equation approach with anomalous Green functions incorporates the effect of many-phonon states that are not captured by simpler diagonalization in a limited particle-phonon space, though the two methods yield nearly identical results for occupation probabilities and correlation energies in a small test space.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson equation for the 2x2 matrix Green function (including normal and anomalous pairing Green functions) with a self-energy constructed from the particle-phonon coupling vertex and the phonon Green function, iterating until convergence. They compared this field-theoretical solution with a direct diagonalization of the particle-phonon coupled Hamiltonian in a space of two particles coupled to 0-2 phonons with J=0, using three single-particle orbits (g9/2, d5/2, j15/2) above the N=126 shell gap and the lowest 2+ and 3- phonons from a QRPA calculation for 208Pb. The pairing gap of neutrons in 120Sn was calculated using the Dyson equation method.

## Key numbers

- Occupation probability v² for g9/2: 0.178 (Dyson) vs 0.179 (diagonalization), with η = η_D = 1 keV and ε_F = -4.7908 MeV
- Occupation probability v² for j15/2: 0.013 (both methods)
- Occupation probability v² for d5/2: 0.001 (both methods)
- Correlation energy E_cor: -1.62 MeV (Dyson) vs -1.53 MeV (diagonalization)
- Unperturbed single-particle energies used: g9/2 at -4.314 MeV, j15/2 at -2.490 MeV, d5/2 at -1.992 MeV
- Phonon energies: 2+ at 4.10 MeV, 3- at 2.10 MeV
- Vertex matrix elements: g9/2-g9/2 with 2+ phonon: 1.36 MeV; j15/2-j15/2 with 2+ phonon: 2.24 MeV; j15/2-g9/2 with 3- phonon: 4.02 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier BCS calculation with phonon-induced interaction by Barranco et al. (1999) and the linear-approximation Dyson equation approach by Avdeenkov and Kamerdzhiev (1999), providing a nonperturbative treatment that includes fragmentation of single-particle strength beyond the mean-field and mean-pair-field approximations. The formalism builds on the field-theoretical framework introduced by Belyaev et al. (1961, 1965, 1966) and Migdal et al. (1967, 1968) for treating particle-phonon coupling with anomalous Green functions, analogous to the Eliashberg equation in condensed matter physics. The method enables future calculations of pairing gaps in open-shell nuclei with proper treatment of the interplay between particle-phonon coupling and pairing correlations, including effects of many-phonon states that are not accessible in perturbative or small-space diagonalization approaches.
