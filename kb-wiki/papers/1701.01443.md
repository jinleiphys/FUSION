# Neutrinoless double beta decay and chiral $SU(3)$

> TeV-scale lepton number violation can affect neutrinoless double beta decay through dimension-9 $ΔL= ΔI = 2$ operators involving two electrons and four quarks. Since the dominant effects within a nucleus are expected to arise from pion exchange, the $ π^- \to π^+ e e$ matrix elements of the dimension-9 operators are a key hadronic input. In this letter we provide estimates for the $π^- \to π^+ $ matrix elements of all Lorentz scalar $ΔI = 2$ four-quark operators relevant to the study of TeV-scale lepton number violation. The analysis is based on chiral $SU(3)$ symmetry, which relates the $π^- \to π^+$ matrix elements of the $ΔI = 2$ operators to the $K^0 \to \bar{K}^0$ and $K \to ππ$ matrix elements of their $ΔS = 2$ and $ΔS = 1$ chiral partners, for which lattice QCD input is available. The inclusion of next-to-leading order chiral loop corrections to all symmetry relations used in the analysis makes our results robust at the $30\%$ level or better, depending on the operator.

## Key claim

The paper provides estimates for the $\pi^- \to \pi^+$ matrix elements of all Lorentz scalar $\Delta I = 2$ four-quark operators relevant to TeV-scale lepton number violation in neutrinoless double beta decay, with the results robust at the 30% level or better depending on the operator, based on chiral $SU(3)$ symmetry relations that include next-to-leading order chiral loop corrections.

## Method

The authors use chiral $SU(3)$ symmetry to relate the $\pi^- \to \pi^+$ matrix elements of $\Delta I = 2$ operators to the $K^0 \to \bar{K}^0$ and $K \to \pi\pi$ matrix elements of their $\Delta S = 2$ and $\Delta S = 1$ chiral partners, for which lattice QCD input is available. They compute next-to-leading order chiral loop corrections to all symmetry relations, and extract the low-energy constants $g_{6 \times \bar{6}}$ and $g_{8 \times 8}$ from lattice QCD results for kaon matrix elements, then translate these into the pionic matrix elements of interest.

## Key numbers

* $\langle \pi^+ | O_1 | \pi^- \rangle = (1.0 \pm 0.1 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-4} \text{ GeV}^4$ in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* $\langle \pi^+ | O_2 | \pi^- \rangle = -(2.7 \pm 0.3 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* $\langle \pi^+ | O_3 | \pi^- \rangle = (0.9 \pm 0.1 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* $\langle \pi^+ | O_4 | \pi^- \rangle = -(2.6 \pm 0.8 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* $\langle \pi^+ | O_5 | \pi^- \rangle = -(11 \pm 2 \pm 3) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4$ in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at $\mu = 3$ GeV
* The total chiral correction factor $R_{8 \times 8} = 0.72(21)$ (approximately 30% uncertainty) and $R_{6 \times \bar{6}} = 0.76(14)$ (approximately 20% uncertainty) for the symmetry relations connecting $\pi^+\to\pi^-$ and $K^0\to\bar{K}^0$ matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry analysis of Savage (1998) from a single operator to all Lorentz scalar $\Delta I = 2$ four-quark operators relevant for neutrinoless double beta decay. Previous estimates relied on vacuum saturation or naive dimensional analysis, while this paper provides controlled hadronic input using lattice QCD results for $K^0$-$\bar{K}^0$ mixing from multiple groups including Carrasco (2015), Bertone (2012), Jang (2015), Boyle (2012), and Garron (2016). The results enable more reliable interpretation of neutrinoless double beta decay experiments in terms of TeV-scale lepton number violation mechanisms, and can be further improved as lattice QCD precision on kaon matrix elements increases.
