# Complex Langevin dynamics and zeroes of the fermion determinant

> QCD at nonzero baryon chemical potential suffers from the sign problem, due to the complex quark determinant. Complex Langevin dynamics can provide a solution, provided certain conditions are met. One of these conditions, holomorphicity of the Langevin drift, is absent in QCD since zeroes of the determinant result in a meromorphic drift. We first derive how poles in the drift affect the formal justification of the approach and then explore the various possibilities in simple models. The lessons from these are subsequently applied to both heavy dense QCD and full QCD, and we find that the results obtained show a consistent picture. We conclude that with careful monitoring, the method can be justified a posteriori, even in the presence of meromorphicity.

## Key claim

Complex Langevin dynamics can be justified a posteriori for QCD at nonzero baryon chemical potential even when the drift is meromorphic (due to zeroes of the fermion determinant), provided careful monitoring is performed; the formal justification of the approach is affected by poles in the drift, but the method can still produce correct results under appropriate conditions.

## Method

The authors first derive how poles in the Langevin drift affect the formal justification of complex Langevin dynamics, then explore various possibilities in simple one-pole and U(1) one-link models using fixed and dynamic Taylor expansions to approximate the meromorphic drift by holomorphic polynomials. The lessons from these models are subsequently applied to heavy dense QCD (HDQCD) and full QCD, where the structure of zeroes of the fermion determinant is analyzed for gauge groups SU(3) and SL(3,C).

## Key numbers

- For the U(1) one-link model at $\beta=0.3$, $n_p=1$, $\mu=1$, $\kappa=2$, Taylor expansions to order 10 and 20 were tested, with results comparable to those obtained by restricting the process to the region $G_+$ where $\Re D > 0$
- In the solvable second-order pole model with action $S(z) = \frac{\omega z^2}{2} - \ln(z^n)$, the ground state of the Langevin operator $L$ is a constant, and the ground state of its dual $L^T$ is $\rho(x) = c x^2 \exp(-\omega x^2/4)$
- For the second-order pole model, the expectation value $\langle x^2 \rangle = 3/\omega$ is obtained as $t \to \infty$, with convergence rate $e^{-2\omega t}$
- For the U(1) one-link model with $n_p=1$, $\kappa=2$, $\mu=0$, $\beta=0.5$, the cured complex Langevin procedure using a positive density $\sigma = \rho + c$ shows agreement with exact results when the parameter $\alpha = c e^{-\beta}/\kappa$ is large enough to make $\sigma$ nonnegative

## Context

This work extends the formal justification of complex Langevin dynamics developed by Aarts, Seiler, and Stamatescu (2010) and addresses the problem of meromorphic drift arising from zeroes of the fermion determinant, which was identified as a key obstacle in earlier studies. The paper builds on prior work on the U(1) one-link model and heavy dense QCD by Aarts, James, Pawlowski, Seiler, Sexty, and Stamatescu (2013), and enables further application of complex Langevin methods to full QCD at nonzero baryon density, provided appropriate monitoring and potential cures (such as fixed or dynamic expansions) are employed.
