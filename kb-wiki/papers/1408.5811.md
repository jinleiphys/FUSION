# Non-extensive Statistics and a Systematic Study of Meson-Spectra at LHC Energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> The transverse momentum spectra of secondary pions and kaons, produced in $P+P$ and various central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at LHC, have been analyzed systematically with an approach based on Tsallis non-extensive statistics. The analytical results have been utilized to determine some of the very important thermodynamical parameters bearing characteristic signatures of the partonic medium produced in such collisions.

## Key claim

The Tsallis non-extensive statistics approach successfully reproduces pion and kaon transverse momentum spectra in both P+P and central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, but the extracted parameters indicate that the produced fireball retains strong correlations and does not approach the ideal gas limit, even at this high energy.

## Method

The transverse momentum spectra of pions and kaons from P+P and various central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV were fitted using a Tsallis non-extensive distribution function with constraints linking effective temperature, non-extensivity parameter q, and average multiplicity. From the fitted parameters, the partition function was computed numerically using a hadronic mass spectrum parametrization, and thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, entropy density, trace anomaly, specific heat, and speed of sound were derived.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature T0 from pion spectra: average value 0.144 ± 0.002 GeV, consistent with T0 = 0.131 GeV from e+e- collisions (Wilk, 2012)
- Hagedorn temperature T0 from kaon spectra: average value 0.222 ± 0.001 GeV, in the range 0.170-0.200 GeV predicted by Lattice-QCD calculations (Ejiri, 2008; Miura, 2009)
- Participant nucleon numbers range from Npart = 382.8 for 0-5% central to Npart = 7.5 for 80-90% central Pb+Pb collisions
- The χ²/ndf values indicate satisfactory fits except for pion production at 80-90% centrality

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of hadron spectra using Tsallis statistics at RHIC energies (De, 2012; De, 2013) and at LHC for P+P collisions (Cleymans, 2011; Deppman, 2012). The results are compared with Lattice-QCD thermodynamics calculations (Borsanyi, 2010) at zero chemical potential, showing moderate agreement at high temperature. The finding that the trace anomaly and speed of sound remain far from ideal gas limits suggests the need to incorporate collective transverse flow in future analyses of heavier hadron spectra.
