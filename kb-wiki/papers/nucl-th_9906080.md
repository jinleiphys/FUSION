# Scaling of particle production with number of participants in high-energy A+A collisions in the parton-cascade model

> In view of the recent WA98 data of $π^0$ spectra from central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS, we analyze the production of neutral pions for A+A collisions across the periodic table at $\sqrt{s}=17$ AGeV and 200 AGeV within the framework of the parton-cascade model for relativistic heavy ion collisions. The multiplicity of the pions (having $p_T > 0.5$ GeV) in the central rapidity region, is seen to scale as $\sim (N_{part})^α$, where $N_{part}$ is the number of participating nucleons, which we have approximated as 2A for central collisions of identical nuclei. We argue that the deviation of $α$ ($\simeq 1.2$) from unity may have its origin in the multiple scattering suffered by the partons. We also find that the constant of proportionality in the above scaling relation increases substantially in going from SPS to RHIC energies. This would imply that the (semi)hard partonic activity becomes a much cleaner signal above the soft particle production at the higher energy of RHIC, and thus much less dependent on the (lack of) understanding of the underlying soft physics background.

## Key claim

The multiplicity of neutral pions (with \(p_T > 0.5\) GeV) in central rapidity scales as \((N_{part})^\alpha\) with \(\alpha \simeq 1.2\), where the deviation from unity is argued to originate from multiple parton scattering; the constant of proportionality in this scaling increases substantially from SPS to RHIC energies, implying that (semi)hard partonic activity becomes a cleaner signal above soft particle production at the higher energy.

## Method

The authors used the event generator VNI, which implements the parton-cascade model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, to simulate central \(A+A\) collisions across the periodic table at \(\sqrt{s}=17\) A GeV and 200 A GeV. They calculated the yield of \(\pi^0\) mesons in the central rapidity region (\(-0.5 < y < 0.5\)) with transverse momentum \(p_T \ge 0.5\) GeV/c, and fitted the results as a function of the number of participating nucleons \(N_{part} = 2A\). The simulations were compared to WA98 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s}=17\) A GeV (CERN SPS), the scaling exponent \(\alpha = 1.16\) for \(\pi^0\) multiplicity with \(N_{part}\).
- At \(\sqrt{s}=200\) A GeV (BNL RHIC), the scaling exponent \(\alpha = 1.23\) for \(\pi^0\) multiplicity with \(N_{part}\).
- The WA98 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV incident momentum (corresponding to \(\sqrt{s}\simeq 17\) A GeV) measured \(\pi^0\) spectra up to \(p_\perp \simeq 4\) GeV/c.
- The simulations for central Pb+Pb at SPS used impact parameters \(0 < b < 4.5\) fm, corresponding to 10% of minimum-bias cross-section.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of WA98 data (1999) on \(\pi^0\) production in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS, using the parton-cascade model of Geiger (VNI) to interpret the observed scaling with participant number. The authors argue that the exponent \(\alpha \approx 1.2\) lies between linear scaling (single-collision scenario) and the Glauber-type multiple-collision scaling \(\sim (N_{part})^{1.42}\), indicating that partons undergo multiple scatterings but are limited by deflection and finite collision time. The results enable predictions for RHIC energies, where the increased constant of proportionality suggests that semi-hard partonic signals become more distinguishable from soft physics backgrounds.
