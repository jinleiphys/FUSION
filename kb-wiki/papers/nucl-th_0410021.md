# Spin-asymmetry energy of nuclear matter

> We calculate the density-dependent spin-asymmetry energy $S(k_f)$ of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter in the three-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. The interaction contributions to $S(k_f)$ originate from one-pion exchange, iterated one-pion exchange, and (irreducible) two-pion exchange with no, single, and double virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation. We find that the truncation to $1π$-exchange and iterated $1π$-exchange terms (which leads already to a good nuclear matter equation of state) is spin-unstable, since $S(k_{f0})<0$. The inclusion of the chiral $πNΔ$-dynamics guarantees the spin-stability of nuclear matter. The corresponding spin-asymmetry energy $S(k_f)$ stays positive within a wide range of an undetermined short-range parameter $S_5$ (which we also estimate from realistic NN-potentials). Our results reemphasize the important role played by two-pion exchange with virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation for the nuclear matter many-body problem. Its explicit inclusion is essential in order to obtain good bulk and single-particle properties.

# Spin-asymmetry energy of nuclear matter

## Key claim

The spin-asymmetry energy of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, calculated in chiral perturbation theory to three-loop order, becomes positive (ensuring spin-stability) only when two-pion exchange with virtual Δ-isobar excitation is included; truncation to one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange alone yields a negative spin-asymmetry energy at saturation density, indicating spin-instability, regardless of regularization scheme if contact terms are consistent with empirical bulk properties.

## Method

The spin-asymmetry energy S(k_f) was computed by expanding the energy per particle of spin-polarized nuclear matter around the spin-saturation line to second order in the polarization parameter η, using the three-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. Contributions were evaluated from one-pion exchange (Fock diagram), iterated one-pion exchange (Hartree and Fock diagrams), irreducible two-pion exchange with nucleon intermediate states, and two-pion exchange with single and double virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation, including three-body diagrams arising from Pauli blocking. Short-range contributions were parameterized via a subtraction constant S₅, estimated from the CD-Bonn, Nijm-II, and AV-18 high-precision NN-potentials.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.173 fm⁻³, k_f0 = 2m_π = 270 MeV), the spin-asymmetry energy from 1π-exchange and iterated 1π-exchange alone is S(k_f0) = −20.1 MeV, indicating spin-instability.
- With inclusion of chiral πNΔ-dynamics and S₅ = 0, the spin-asymmetry energy at saturation density becomes S(2m_π) = 67.3 MeV.
- The term linear in density contributes −17.0 MeV at saturation density (reduced by a factor of 2.1 compared to the fourth-order calculation).
- Two-body Fock and three-body Hartree contributions from Δ-excitation sum to 68.3 MeV at saturation density (54.6 MeV + 13.7 MeV), with 16.2 MeV from the three-body contact interaction (ζ = −3/4).
- Estimates of the short-range parameter S₅ from realistic NN-potentials yield S₅ = −7.10 (CD-Bonn), −7.98 (Nijm-II), and −7.27 (AV-18), with average S₅ = −7.45.
- Even with S₅ = −30 (four times the estimated value), the spin-asymmetry energy remains positive, preserving spin-stability.
- The isospin-asymmetry energy at saturation density in the fourth-order calculation is A(k_f0) = 38.9 MeV, compared to an empirical value of (34 ± 2) MeV.
- The nuclear matter compressibility from the fourth-order calculation is K = 252 MeV, consistent with an empirical extrapolation of (260 ± 10) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear matter previously developed by Lutz (1999) and Kaiser (2002), which computed the energy per particle up to three-loop order, and by Kaiser (2003) which included Δ-isobar effects. The finding that truncation to one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange leads to spin-instability parallels earlier observations that spin-stability criteria required modifications of phenomenologically successful Skyrme forces, as noted by Chang (1975), Bäckman (1975), and Krewald (1976). The results enable further applications of the chiral framework to spin-polarized nuclear systems and confirm that the improved nuclear matter properties reported in Kaiser (2003) are based on a spin-stable ground state.
