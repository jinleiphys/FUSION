# Nuclear Structure Functions at a Future Electron-Ion Collider

> The quantitative knowledge of heavy nuclei's partonic structure is currently limited to rather large values of momentum fraction $x$ -- robust experimental constraints below $x \sim 10^{-2}$ at low resolution scale $Q^2$ are particularly scarce. This is in sharp contrast to the free proton's structure which has been probed in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) measurements down to $x \sim 10^{-5}$ at perturbative resolution scales. The construction of an Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) with a possibility to operate with a wide variety of nuclei, will allow one to explore the low-$x$ region in much greater detail. In the present paper we simulate the extraction of the nuclear structure functions from measurements of inclusive and charm reduced cross sections at an EIC. The potential constraints are studied by analyzing simulated data directly in a next-to-leading order global fit of nuclear parton distribution functions based on the recent EPPS16 analysis. A special emphasis is placed on studying the impact an EIC would have on extracting the nuclear gluon PDF, the partonic component most prone to non-linear effects at low $Q^2$. In comparison to the current knowledge, we find that the gluon PDF can be measured at an EIC with significantly reduced uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper simulates the extraction of nuclear structure functions from inclusive and charm reduced cross-section measurements at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), finding that the gluon parton distribution function (PDF) can be measured with significantly reduced uncertainties compared to current knowledge, particularly at low momentum fraction \(x\) and low resolution scale \(Q^2\), but the quark sector analysis is limited by the inability to use flexible parametrizations for all quark flavors simultaneously.

## Method

The authors generated Monte Carlo pseudodata for inclusive and charm-tagged reduced cross sections in electron-gold collisions using PYTHIA 6.4 with EPS09 nuclear PDFs, assuming center-of-mass energies of 31.6, 44.7, and 89.4 GeV with a combined integrated luminosity of 10 fb\(^{-1}\). They then performed a next-to-leading order global fit of nuclear parton distribution functions based on the EPPS16 analysis framework, using an extended small-\(x\) parametrization for the gluon density, and studied the impact of the simulated EIC data on the extracted nPDFs. For charm production, events were selected by tagging kaons from \(D\) meson decays with a displaced vertex between 0.01 and 3 cm, with additional particle identification requirements across different pseudorapidity ranges.

## Key numbers

- The combined integrated luminosity assumed for the EIC pseudodata is 10 fb\(^{-1}\), distributed as 2 fb\(^{-1}\) at \(\sqrt{s}=31.6\) GeV and 4 fb\(^{-1}\) at each of \(\sqrt{s}=44.7\) and 89.4 GeV.
- The bin-by-bin systematic uncertainty assumed for inclusive reduced cross-section measurements is 1.6%, with an additional overall 1.4% systematic uncertainty from the luminosity measurement.
- For charm-tagged reduced cross-section measurements, the bin-by-bin systematic uncertainty is 3.5%, with the same overall 1.4% luminosity uncertainty.
- The overall charm selection efficiency was estimated to be approximately 30%, with no significant center-of-mass energy dependence.
- The background-over-signal ratio for charm events was estimated as 0.95% at \(\sqrt{s}=31.6\) GeV, 0.98% at \(\sqrt{s}=44.7\) GeV, and 1.16% at \(\sqrt{s}=89.4\) GeV, never significantly exceeding 2% as a function of \(x\) and \(Q^2\).
- At low resolution scale \(Q^2=1.69\) GeV\(^2\), the gluon distribution can be determined well down to \(x\sim 10^{-2}\).
- The longitudinal structure function \(F_L\) can be measured with high precision down to \(x \sim 7 \times 10^{-4}\) at low \(Q^2\) using the highest center-of-mass energies.
- The proposed EIC designs plan for per-nucleon center-of-mass energies ranging from 20 GeV to 90 GeV for large nuclei, and up to 145 GeV for polarized electron-proton collisions, with peak luminosities larger than \(10^{34}\) cm\(^{-2}\) s\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the EPPS16 global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (Eskola 2016) by simulating the impact of future EIC measurements, which would probe the low-\(x\) region below \(10^{-2}\) that is currently inaccessible to fixed-target deep inelastic scattering experiments. The study addresses the need for cleaner probes of nuclear partonic structure compared to proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC and RHIC, where low-transverse-momentum observables carry significant theoretical uncertainties. The results enable future comparisons between linear and non-linear QCD evolution (saturation) at low \(x\) and low \(Q^2\), and provide a benchmark for detector requirements and systematic uncertainty targets for the proposed EIC designs at Jefferson Lab (JLEIC) and Brookhaven National Laboratory (eRHIC).
