# Large-space cluster model calculations for the He3(He3,2p)He4 and H3(H3,2n)He4 reactions

> The He3(He3,2p)He4 and H3(H3,2n)He4 reactions are studied in a microscopic cluster model. We search for resonances in the He3+He3 and He4+p+p channels using methods that treat the two- and three-body resonance asymptotics correctly. Our results show that the existence of a low-energy resonance or virtual state, which could influence the Be-7 and B-8 solar neutrino fluxes, is rather unlikely. Our calculated He3(He3,2p)He4 and H3(H3,2n)He4 cross sections are in a good general agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence for a low-energy resonance or virtual state in either the He3+He3 or He4+p+p channels that could affect the He3(He3,2p)He4 reaction cross section, but they note that their He3+He3 model may be too simple because it neglects triplet forces that could arise from a small D-state admixture in He3.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic cluster model calculations using a resonating group model (RGM) wave function for the coupled He4+p+p and He3+He3 systems, with the Minnesota effective interaction. They searched for resonances in the He4+p+p channel using the complex scaling method, and in the He3+He3 channel using analytic continuation of the S matrix to complex energies. The reaction cross sections were calculated using a continuum-discretized coupled channel (CDCC) approximation with a finite square-integrable basis.

## Key numbers

- The He3+He3 and He3+H3 thresholds in the model are at 17.11 MeV and 15.57 MeV, respectively, relative to the He4+N+N threshold (experimental values are 12.86 MeV and 11.33 MeV)
- The adiabatic screening potential for He3+He3 is U_e = 240 eV
- The LUNA collaboration measured the He3(He3,2p)He4 cross section down to E_cm = 20.76 keV
- The absolute normalization of the calculated cross section curves depends 10-20% on the chosen continuum discretization pattern

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster model studies of these reactions by Typel et al. (1991) and Descouvemont (1994) using significantly larger model spaces (roughly 5-10 times bigger). The results contradict the suggestion by Fowler (1972) of a hypothetical low-energy resonance that could affect solar neutrino fluxes. The calculations enable improved subtraction of electron screening effects from the LUNA experimental data, and the authors note that further studies using a (d+p)+(d+p) four-cluster model or full six-body dynamical model would be interesting for the He3+He3 channel.
