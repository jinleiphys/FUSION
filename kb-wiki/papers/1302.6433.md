# The initial state of heavy-ion collisions

> We present a brief review of recent theoretical developments and related phenomenological approaches for understanding the initial state of heavy-ion collisions, with emphasis on the Color Glass Condensate formalism.

## Key claim
The Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism provides a framework for determining the initial state of heavy-ion collisions, but the detailed dynamics of thermalization are often skipped and the pT-integrated distribution of produced particles in the transverse plane is used directly to initialize hydrodynamic simulations, which appears reasonable only over length scales larger than the thermalization time.

## Method
The authors review the CGC effective theory, which separates degrees of freedom into soft small-x gluons treated as dynamical gauge fields and high-x static valence sources. They describe how the B-JIMWLK renormalization group equations account for quantum corrections and non-linear terms that lead to a saturation scale Qs, and how the BK equation in the large-Nc limit provides a closed evolution equation for the dipole scattering amplitude. The review compares CGC approaches to alternative models including the wounded nucleon (Glauber) model, collinear factorization with nuclear PDFs, and Monte Carlo event generators like HIJING and HYDJET.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale for the proton from the GBW model: Qs,N^2(x) = (x0/x)^0.288 GeV^2 with x0 = 3 x 10^-4
- The AAMQS fits use initial conditions at x0 = 0.01 with parameters including Qs0,proton^2 values of 0.2, 0.168, and 0.157 GeV^2 and gamma values of 1, 1.119, and 1.101
- The thermalization time scale from hydrodynamical simulations is tau_therm ~ 0.5 to 1 fm/c
- The ratio (1/N_part) dN/deta or (1/N_part) dE_T/deta increases by about a factor of two from peripheral to central Pb+Pb collisions
- The AAMQS fits use Lambda = 0.241 GeV as an infrared parameter

## Context
This review extends the line of work on the Color Glass Condensate formalism for heavy-ion collisions, building on the GBW model (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) and the AAMQS global fits (Albacete et al., 2009, 2010) to HERA data. It contrasts CGC approaches with the wounded nucleon model and with nuclear PDF parametrizations like EPS (Eskola et al., 2009), and discusses how the IP-Glasma model (Schenke et al., 2012) bypasses B-JIMWLK evolution by using the IP-Sat model fit to HERA data. The work enables future studies of higher eccentricity harmonics and the nature of fundamental QCD fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, with open questions about the thermalization process and the need for full NLO corrections in phenomenological applications.
