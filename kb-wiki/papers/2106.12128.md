# Quadrupole moments and proton-neutron structure in p-shell mirror nuclei

> Electric quadrupole (E2) matrix elements provide a measure of nuclear deformation and related collective structure. Ground-state quadrupole moments in particular are known to high precision in many p-shell nuclei. While the experimental electric quadrupole moment only measures the proton distribution, both proton and neutron quadrupole moments are needed to probe proton-neutron asymmetry in the nuclear deformation. We seek insight into the relation between these moments through the ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI), or no-core shell model (NCSM), approach. Converged ab initio calculations for quadrupole moments are particularly challenging, due to sensitivity to long-range behavior of the wave functions. We therefore study more robustly-converged ratios of quadrupole moments: across mirror nuclides, or of proton and neutron quadrupole moments within the same nuclide. In calculations for mirror pairs in the p shell, we explore how well the predictions for mirror quadrupole moments agree with experiment and how well isospin (mirror) symmetry holds for quadrupole moments across a mirror pair.

# Quadrupole moments and proton-neutron structure in p-shell mirror nuclei

## Key claim
Meaningful predictions of electric quadrupole moment ratios can be made in ab initio NCCI calculations even when the individual moments themselves are not converged, provided the incomplete convergence is a systematic effect of basis truncation that cancels between structurally similar states. However, robust predictions are not always possible if the moments are exquisitely sensitive to fine details such as delicate mixing of competing low-lying configurations.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for ground-state quadrupole moments across all particle-bound mirror pairs in the p-shell where angular momentum selection rules permit nonzero quadrupole moments (A=7, 8, 9, 9', 11, 12, and 13'). They used three different internucleon interactions (Daejeon16, JISP16, and LENPIC) and systematically varied the basis truncation parameter Nmax from 4 to 14 and the oscillator energy hw to assess convergence. Rather than focusing on individual quadrupole moments, the study analyzed ratios of quadrupole moments across mirror pairs and ratios of neutron to proton quadrupole moments within single nuclides.

## Key numbers
- For the A=7 mirror pair (7Li/7Be), the ratio of quadrupole moments (7Be to 7Li) was calculated with the Daejeon16 interaction at Nmax=14, showing convergence behavior across hw values from approximately 10 MeV to 25 MeV.
- The naive homogeneous liquid-drop estimate gives Q_n/Q_p = N/Z, yielding values such as 4/3 ≈ 1.33 for 7Li, 5/3 ≈ 1.67 for 8Li, and 8/5 ≈ 1.6 for 13B.
- A schematic alpha-triton cluster model for 7Li yields Q_n/Q_p ≈ 1.47 (50/34).
- The Elliott SU(3) model estimate for 7Li gives Q_n/Q_p = 2, while for 9Be the Elliott-Wilsdon rotational model estimate gives Q_n/Q_p ≈ 0.68.
- The ab initio predictions for 7Li give Q_n/Q_p in the range approximately 1.6-1.7, about 10-15% larger than the schematic cluster estimate.
- The 9B ground state has a width of only ~0.5 keV despite being unstable to proton emission.

## Context
This work extends prior ab initio NCCI studies of rotational bands (Maris 2015, Caprio 2020) and mirror transitions (Henderson 2019) where ratios of E2 matrix elements showed improved convergence. It directly compares with Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations by Pastore (2013) for A≤9 and with experimental quadrupole moments compiled by Stone (2016). The results enable testing of cluster model interpretations (Freer 2007, Kanada-Enyo 1997-2012) and Elliott SU(3) symmetry descriptions (Dytrych 2013, Launey 2016, McCoy 2018-2020) for understanding proton-neutron asymmetry in nuclear deformation. The approach also provides a framework for predicting unknown quadrupole moments where only one member of a mirror pair is experimentally measured.
