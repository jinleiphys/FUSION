# Variation after parity projection calculation with Skyrme interaction for light nuclei

> A self-consistent calculation with variation after parity projection is proposed to study both ground and excited states of light nuclei. This procedure provides description of the ground state incorporating some correlation effects, and self-consistent solutions for the excited states of negative parity. For flexible description of nuclear shapes, single particle orbitals are represented on a uniform grid in the three-dimensional Cartesian coordinates. The angular momentum projection is performed after variation to calculate rotational spectra. To demonstrate the usefulness of the method, results are shown for two N=Z nuclei, 20Ne and 12C, for which clustering correlations are known to be important. In the 20Ne nucleus, both cluster-like and shell-model-like states are described simultaneously in the present framework. For 12C nucleus, the appearance of three-alpha clustering correlation in the ground state is investigated in relation to the strength of the two-body spin-orbit interaction.

# Variation after parity projection calculation with Skyrme interaction for light nuclei

## Key claim

The variation after parity projection (VAPP) method with Skyrme interactions can simultaneously describe ground states (with correlation effects beyond mean-field) and negative-parity excited states in light N=Z nuclei, but the Skyrme interaction requires restrictions (time-reversal invariance and omission of certain time-odd energy functionals) to avoid unphysical solutions, and the spin-orbit strength must be artificially weakened for 12C to reproduce clustering.

## Method

The authors perform variation after parity projection (VAPP) on a Slater determinant, representing single-particle orbitals on a uniform 3D Cartesian grid (0.8 fm spacing, 7.2 fm radius sphere). They use the Skyrme SGII interaction with enforced time-reversal invariance and omission of time-odd densities (rho, j, T) in transition densities. After obtaining self-consistent solutions, they perform three-dimensional angular momentum projection (AMP) to calculate rotational spectra, discretizing Euler angles into 20 steps for alpha and gamma and 400 steps for beta.

## Key numbers

- 20Ne ground state binding energy (PPSHF before AMP): -168.232 MeV (experimental: -160.645 MeV)
- 20Ne ground state binding energy (PPSHF after AMP): -171.333 MeV
- 20Ne Kπ=2- excitation energy before AMP: 5.47 MeV; after AMP: 4.91 MeV (experimental 2-: 4.97 MeV)
- 20Ne Kπ=0- excitation energy before AMP: 7.96 MeV; after AMP: 6.42 MeV (experimental 1-: 5.78 MeV)
- 20Ne ground state quadrupole deformation β2: 0.535 (PPSHF) vs 0.728 (SHF)
- 20Ne ground state octupole deformation β30: 0.314 (PPSHF) vs 0.0 (SHF)
- 12C ground state binding energy (PPSHF after AMP at xLS=0.8): -96.499 MeV (experimental: -92.162 MeV)
- 12C B(E2: 2+ → 0+): 5.14 e2fm4 (experimental: 7.8 ± 0.4 e2fm4)
- 12C ground state β2: 0.271, β33: 0.327 (PPSHF at xLS=0.8)
- Grid spacing: 0.8 fm; grid sphere radius: 7.2 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous VAPP calculations with the simple BKN interaction (Takami 1996) to realistic Skyrme interactions, and connects to the AMD method of Kanada-En'yo and Horiuchi (2001) which uses Gaussian wave packets as an approximation. The approach enables systematic study of clustering correlations (alpha-16O in 20Ne, 3-alpha in 12C) within a self-consistent mean-field framework, and is intended for future application to neutron-rich unstable nuclei. The method reveals that the Skyrme interaction, parameterized for single Slater determinants, requires modifications for parity-projected calculations, and that the moment of inertia discrepancies resemble those in AMD calculations (Kanada-En'yo 2003) and HFBCS+GCM+AMP calculations (Bender 2003).
