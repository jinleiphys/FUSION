# Shielding of an external oscillating electric field inside atoms

> According to the Schiff theorem an external electric field vanishes at atomic nucleus in a neutral atom, i.e. it is completely shielded by electrons. This makes a nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) unobservable. In this paper an extension of the Schiff theorem to an oscillating electric field is considered. Such field can reach the nucleus and interact with the nuclear EDM. The enhancement effect appears if the field is in resonance with atomic or molecular transition. The shielding by electrons strongly affects low-energy nuclear electric dipole transition amplitudes in different nuclear reactions including radiative transitions, radiative nucleon capture, photo or electro-excitation of nuclei and laser-induced or laser-enhanced nuclear reactions.

# Shielding of an external oscillating electric field inside atoms

## Key claim
The Schiff theorem, which states that a static external electric field is completely shielded by electrons and cannot reach the atomic nucleus, does not hold for oscillating electric fields; such fields can penetrate to the nucleus and interact with a nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM), with the shielding becoming incomplete at finite oscillation frequencies and potentially producing large fields at the nucleus when the field frequency is resonant with an atomic or molecular transition.

## Method
The author derives an analytic expression for the total electric field amplitude at the nucleus in a neutral atom or ion subjected to an external oscillating electric field, using time-dependent perturbation theory applied to the Schrödinger or Dirac Hamiltonian for atomic electrons. The derivation employs commutator relations between the total electron momentum operator and the dipole operator, and expresses the induced electron field at the nucleus in terms of the atomic dynamical polarizability. The result is extended to the resonant case using a two-level system with Rabi oscillations.

## Key numbers
- For lanthanum (Z=57) with a static scalar polarizability of 213.7 atomic units, the shielding factor in a low-frequency laser field at 1 eV (tilde epsilon = 1/27.2) is 0.005.
- At the lanthanum atomic resonance at 1.64 eV (tilde epsilon = 0.0604), the field at the nucleus is estimated as approximately 10^5 V/cm, using a rough estimate of the dipole matrix element tilde D_z ~ 0.3.
- For a light nucleus with Z ~ 1 and a very optimistic frequency of 658 GHz (tilde epsilon = 10^-4), the resonant field at the nucleus is approximately 50 V/cm.
- The scale of the electron field at the nucleus is given by e/a_B^2 = 5.14 × 10^9 V/cm.
- For the 0.734 eV p-wave resonance in ^139La (Z=70), a 7% parity violating effect has been observed.

## Context
This work extends the Schiff theorem (Schiff, 1963) from static electric fields to oscillating fields, addressing a gap left by prior treatments that considered only static shielding or finite nuclear size effects (Sandars, 1965; Hinds, 1975; Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich, 1984; Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov, 1986). The author notes that previous theoretical predictions for laser-induced neutron capture experiments (Zaretski and Lomonosov, 1981; Zaretski and Lomonosov, 1983) failed to account for electron shielding, which may explain why subsequent experiments (exp1, exp2, exp3) did not observe the predicted effects. The results enable re-evaluation of laser-induced nuclear reactions including radiative nucleon capture, photo-excitation, and laser-enhanced nuclear reactions, and suggest that atomic resonances could produce strong enough fields at the nucleus to make such experiments feasible.
