# Extraction of neutron density distributions from high-statistics coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data

> Forthcoming fixed-target coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments aim at measurements with $\cal{O}(\text{tonne})$-scale detectors and substantially reduced systematic and statistical uncertainties. With such high quality data, the extraction of point-neutron distributions mean-square radii requires a better understanding of possible theoretical uncertainties. We quantify the impact of single-nucleon electromagnetic mean-square radii on the weak-charge form factor and compare results from weak-charge form factor parametrizations and weak-charge form factor decompositions in terms of elastic vector proton and neutron form factors, including nucleon form factors $Q$-dependent terms up to order $Q^2$. We assess as well the differences arising from results derived using weak-charge form factor decompositions in terms of elastic vector proton and neutron form factors and a model-independent approach based solely on the assumption of spherically symmetric nuclear ground state. We demonstrate the impact of the main effects by assuming pseudo-data from a one-tonne LAr detector and find that, among the effects and under the assumptions considered in this paper, weak-charge form factor parametrizations and weak-charge form factor decompositions in terms of elastic vector proton and neutron form factors enable the extraction of the $^{40}\text{Ar}$ point-neutron distribution mean-square radius with a $\sim 15\%$ accuracy. With a substantial reduction of the beam-related neutron and steady-state backgrounds a $\sim 1\%$ precision extraction seems feasible, using either of the two approaches.

## Key claim

Under the assumptions considered in this paper, weak-charge form factor parametrizations and weak-charge form factor decompositions in terms of elastic vector proton and neutron form factors enable extraction of the 40Ar point-neutron distribution mean-square radius with approximately 15% accuracy; with substantial reduction of beam-related neutron and steady-state backgrounds, approximately 1% precision extraction seems feasible using either approach.

## Method

The author quantified theoretical uncertainties in the weak-charge form factor by comparing leading-order (LO) calculations, where the point-proton distribution mean-square radius is set equal to the nuclear charge radius and nucleon form factors are taken as Q-independent, with calculations including single-nucleon electromagnetic mean-square radii and nucleon form factor Q-dependent terms up to order Q^2. The analysis used the Helm, Klein-Nystrand, and Fourier transform of the symmetrized Fermi distribution parametrizations for the elastic vector proton and neutron form factors, and compared these parametrization-based approaches with a model-independent series expansion method. Pseudo-data from a one-tonne liquid argon (LAr) detector were assumed to assess the precision of point-neutron distribution mean-square radius extraction.

## Key numbers

- For argon, assuming R_n = R_p + 0.1 fm, the percentage difference in the weak-charge form factor between LO approximations and the full calculation (including nucleon form factor Q^2 terms and corrected point-proton radius) reaches up to approximately 3% over the relevant Q range.
- For cesium, assuming R_n = R_p + 0.2 fm, the same comparison yields percentage differences up to approximately 3%.
- The percentage difference between using a Helm parametrization for the weak-charge form factor (with R_W^2) and using Eq. (EW-FF-approx) reaches up to approximately 9% for argon and approximately 18% for cesium.
- Percentage differences due to form factor parametrization choice (Helm vs. Klein-Nystrand vs. Fermi) are approximately 1% or below for argon and approximately 2% or below for cesium.
- The 40Ar point-neutron distribution mean-square radius can be extracted with approximately 15% accuracy using pseudo-data from a one-tonne LAr detector; with substantial background reduction, approximately 1% precision seems feasible.

## Context

This work extends previous CEνNS analyses (e.g., Cadeddu 2017, Papoulias 2019, Miranda 2020, Coloma 2020, Aristizabal Sierra 2021) that considered only leading-order effects, where the nuclear charge radius was equated to the point-proton distribution radius and only Q-independent nucleon form factor terms were used. The paper quantifies the theoretical uncertainties from these approximations and from form factor parametrization choices, showing that for forthcoming high-statistics experiments with tonne-scale detectors, nucleon form factor Q-dependent terms must be included. The results enable more precise extraction of neutron distribution radii from future COHERENT data at the second target station or from the Coherent Captain-Mills Experiment.
