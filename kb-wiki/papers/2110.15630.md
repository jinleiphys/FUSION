# Anomalous spin polarization from turbulent color fields

> We study the important, yet widely overlooked, role of gluons for spin transport with a connection to local parity violation in quark gluon plasmas. We employ the formalism of quantum kinetic theory to quarks in weakly coupled quantum chromodynamics to derive the source terms for quark spin polarization. These source terms involve parity-odd correlators of dynamically generated color fields in near-equilibrium quark gluon plasmas and give rise to locally fluctuating axial charge currents. Our results provide a possible explanation for the spin alignment of vector mesons measured in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that parity-odd correlators of dynamically generated color fields in a near-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma can generate local quark spin polarization and axial charge currents, providing a possible explanation for the spin alignment of vector mesons observed in high-energy nuclear collisions; however, this mechanism yields a vanishing contribution to the global polarization of Lambda hyperons because the sign of the polarization fluctuates event-by-event.

## Method

The authors employ the formalism of quantum kinetic theory for quarks in weakly coupled quantum chromodynamics, extending the collisionless axial kinetic theory to include gluonic effects. They derive the Kadanoff-Baym equation for the Wigner function up to order O(hbar), decompose the resulting scalar and axial-vector kinetic equations into color-singlet and color-octet components, and solve for the color-singlet axial-vector distribution function in terms of parity-odd color-field correlators under near-equilibrium assumptions with a Gaussian correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The source terms from color-field correlators are at O(g^2), where g denotes the QCD coupling, which dominate over the expected collision term at O(g^4 ln g) at weak coupling.
- The axial charge current in the medium rest frame is given by J5^mu = -(hbar u^mu / (8 pi^2)) sqrt(pi) tau_c <E^a dot B^a> I, where tau_c is the correlation time and I is an integral over the Fermi distribution.
- The spin polarization generated by the source term is proportional to (t - t0), giving a secular growth, while the explicit source term in the axial-charge density current is limited by the correlation time tau_c of gauge field fluctuations.
- For massless quarks, the axial charge current takes the same form with I = 1.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of spin transport in heavy ion collisions that focused almost exclusively on quarks, such as the thermal vorticity approach of Becattini (2013) and the chiral kinetic theory of Hidaka (2017), by incorporating the previously overlooked role of gluons. The mechanism connects to the conjectured phenomenon of local parity violation in QCD, where axial charge density could be produced by sphaleron transitions (McLerran 1990) or overpopulated gluons (Mace 2016), and may induce turbulent color fields via the chiral plasma instability (Joyce 1997, Akamatsu 2013). The results provide a qualitative explanation for the spin alignment of K*0 and phi mesons measured by the ALICE collaboration (2019) and suggest that the mechanism can be tested in numerical simulations coupling the kinetic equations to the Yang-Mills equation.
