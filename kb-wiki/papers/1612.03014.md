# Improved WKB approximation for quantum tunneling: Application to heavy ion fusion

> In this paper we revisit the one-dimensional tunneling problem. We consider Kemble's approximation for the transmission coefficient. We show how this approximation can be extended to above-barrier energies by performing the analytical continuation of the radial coordinate to the complex plane. We investigate the validity of this approximation by comparing their predictions for the cross section and for the barrier distribution with the corresponding quantum mechanical results. We find that the extended Kemble's approximation reproduces the results of quantum mechanics with great accuracy.

# Improved WKB approximation for quantum tunneling: Application to heavy ion fusion

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Kemble's improved WKB approximation for the transmission coefficient can be extended to above-barrier energies by analytically continuing the radial coordinate into the complex plane, and that this extended approximation reproduces exact quantum mechanical results for fusion cross sections and barrier distributions with great accuracy, provided the integration contour is chosen along a curve where the complex potential is real.

## Method

The authors analytically continue the radial coordinate to the complex plane and identify a contour (approximately an ellipse) on which the effective potential (Coulomb plus nuclear plus centrifugal) is real. They evaluate the WKB integral between complex turning points along this contour to obtain the Kemble transmission coefficient at energies above the barrier top. They then compute fusion cross sections via partial-wave summation and barrier distributions via the second derivative of the energy-weighted cross section, comparing these to exact quantum mechanical results and to the standard Hill-Wheeler/Wong parabolic approximation for the light heavy-ion system $^6$Li + $^{12}$C.

## Key numbers

- The barrier height of the bare potential for $^6$Li + $^{12}$C is $V_{\scr B} = 3.25$ MeV.
- The maximum of the quantum mechanical barrier distribution (and of Kemble's approximation) occurs at $E_{\rm max} = 2.95$ MeV, about 0.3 MeV below the bare barrier height.
- At the lowest energies shown ($E \sim 1$ MeV), the Wong formula overestimates the quantum mechanical cross section by more than three orders of magnitude.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for the Akyüz-Winther nuclear potential in the $^6$Li + $^{12}$C system are: $V_0 = -31.35$ MeV, $r_0 = 1.16$ fm, and $a_0 = 0.56$ fm.
- The Coulomb radius parameter is $r_{0{\scr C}} \simeq 1$ fm.

## Context

This work extends Kemble's (1935) improved WKB connection formula, which gives the correct transmission coefficient of 1/2 at the barrier top but fails at above-barrier energies, by using complex-plane integration techniques previously applied to WKB phase shifts by Brink and Takigawa (1977) and others. The extended Kemble approximation provides a more accurate alternative to the Hill-Wheeler parabolic approximation (1953) and the Wong formula (1973), which overestimate sub-barrier fusion cross sections for light systems like $^6$Li + $^{12}$C. This enables reliable single-channel calculations of fusion cross sections and barrier distributions without solving the full Schrödinger equation, particularly useful for light systems where the parabolic approximation breaks down.
