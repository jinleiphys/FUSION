# Role of two-flavor color superconductor pairing in a three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with axial anomaly

> The phase diagram of strongly interacting matter is studied within a three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, which contains the coupling between chiral and diquark condensates through the axial anomaly. Our results show that it is essential to include the 2SC phase in the analysis. While this is expected for realistic strange quark masses, we find that even for equal up, down, and strange bare quark masses, 2SC pairing can be favored due to spontaneous flavor-symmetry breaking by the axial anomaly. This can lead to a rich phase structure, including BCS- and BEC-like 2SC and CFL phases and new endpoints. On the other hand, the low-temperature critical endpoint, which was found earlier in the same model without 2SC pairing, is almost removed from the phase diagram and cannot be reached from the low-density chirally broken phase without crossing a preceding first-order phase boundary. For physical quark masses no additional critical endpoint is found.

## Key claim

The authors find that even for equal up, down, and strange bare quark masses, the two-flavor color superconductor (2SC) phase can be favored over the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase due to spontaneous flavor-symmetry breaking induced by the axial anomaly, contradicting the earlier assumption that only CFL pairing is relevant for equal masses. For physical quark masses, no additional critical endpoint is found in the phase diagram.

## Method

The authors employ a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in mean-field approximation, extended with a six-point interaction term that couples chiral and diquark condensates through the axial anomaly. They solve the gap equations for independent chiral condensates (phi_i) and diquark condensates (s_i) for each flavor, allowing both 2SC and CFL pairing patterns, and construct the phase diagram by selecting the solution with the lowest thermodynamic potential at each point in the mu-T plane.

## Key numbers

* For equal bare quark masses (m_u = m_d = m_s = 5.5 MeV) and coupling Kt = 4.2 K, the 2SC phase replaces a large part of the CFL phase in the phase diagram, with a first-order 2SC-CFL transition line ending on a second-order phase boundary to the normal quark phase.
* At T = 0 and mu = 310 MeV, the ratio of the 2SC gap parameter Delta_3 to the common CFL gap parameter Delta rises from about 1.25 at Kt = 0 to 2.0 at Kt = 4 K for equal bare quark masses.
* For realistic strange quark mass (m_s = 140.7 MeV) and Kt = 4.2 K, the CFL phase is pushed to higher chemical potentials and the small CFL region between the BEC-like and BCS-like 2SC phases disappears already at m_s approximately 7.5 MeV.
* The first-order phase transition between the BEC-like and BCS-like 2SC phases ends at m_s approximately 105 MeV for Kt = 4.2 K.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Abuki et al. (2010), who studied the same NJL model with equal quark masses but only allowed a single common diquark condensate and a single common chiral condensate, finding a low-temperature critical endpoint. The present results show that when independent condensates are allowed, the 2SC phase is favored and the low-temperature critical endpoint is almost removed from the phase diagram. The findings also relate to the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of Hatsuda et al. (2006), Yamamoto et al. (2007), and Baym et al. (2008), which predicted such an endpoint but did not consider the 2SC pairing possibility for equal masses. The results enable future studies of the phase diagram with charge neutrality constraints relevant for compact stars.
