# Deformed quasiparticle-random-phase approximation for neutron-rich nuclei using the Skyrme energy density functional

> We develop a new framework of the deformed quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) where the Skyrme density functional and the density-dependent pairing functional are consistently treated. Numerical applications are carried out for the isovector dipole and the isoscalar quadrupole modes in the spherical $^{20}$O and in the deformed $^{26}$Ne nuclei, and the effect of the momentum dependent terms of the Skyrme effective interaction for the energy-weighted sum rule is discussed. As a further application, we present for the first time the moments of inertia of $^{34}$Mg and $^{36}$Mg using the Thouless-Valatin procedure based on the self-consistent deformed QRPA, and show the applicability of our new calculation scheme not only for the vibrational modes but also for the rotational modes in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully self-consistent deformed quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (QRPA) framework using the Skyrme energy density functional, including the full momentum dependence of the particle-hole residual interaction, and demonstrate that this explicit treatment is crucial for accurately satisfying the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in both spherical and deformed neutron-rich nuclei, whereas the Landau-Migdal approximation underestimates or overestimates the EWSR by 10-13%.

## Method

The authors solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in coordinate space using cylindrical coordinates with axial and reflection symmetries, employing the SkM* Skyrme interaction for the mean field and a density-dependent contact interaction for pairing. The QRPA equation is solved in matrix formulation using the self-consistent quasiparticle basis, with the residual particle-hole interaction derived from the full Skyrme density functional including momentum-dependent terms, and the residual particle-particle interaction derived from the pairing functional. The residual interactions are renormalized by overall factors to place spurious modes (center-of-mass motion, rotational motion, and pairing rotation) at zero energy.

## Key numbers

- For $^{20}$O: total binding energy 157.7 MeV, average neutron pairing gap $\langle \Delta \rangle_{\nu}=1.92$ MeV; the energy-weighted sum up to 60 MeV reaches 99.5% of the EWSR value for the isovector dipole mode (with enhancement factor $\kappa=0.32$), while the Landau-Migdal approximation underestimates by 13%
- For $^{20}$O isoscalar quadrupole mode: the calculated sum satisfies 98.9% of the EWSR value; the Landau-Migdal approximation overestimates by 9.3%
- For $^{26}$Ne: the energy-weighted sum up to 60 MeV overestimates by only 1.6% the EWSR value (with $\kappa=0.32$), compared to an underestimation of about 10% in the Landau-Migdal approximation
- For $^{34}$Mg and $^{36}$Mg: the energy-weighted sums up to 60 MeV for the $K^{\pi}=0^{+}$ excitation amount to 99.0% of the EWSR; the Landau-Migdal approximation overestimates by 11.5% and 11.0%, respectively
- For $^{34}$Mg: neutron pairing gap $\langle \Delta_{\nu}\rangle=1.71$ MeV, proton pairing gap 0.0 MeV; intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_0=62.2$ $e\cdot$fm$^2$, giving $B(E2;0^{+}\to 2_1^{+})=385$ $e^{2}\cdot$fm$^{4}$; low-lying $K^{\pi}=0^{+}$ mode at 2.65 MeV with intrinsic isoscalar quadrupole transition strength of about 30 Weisskopf units, enhanced by 10.6 times relative to unperturbed strength
- For $^{36}$Mg: intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_0=60.1$ $e\cdot$fm$^2$, giving $B(E2;0^{+}\to 2_1^{+})=359$ $e^{2}\cdot$fm$^{4}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deformed QRPA calculations (Yoshida 2006, 2008) that used either a simplified Skyrme interaction neglecting momentum dependence or the Landau-Migdal approximation, which did not satisfy the EWSR accurately. It also builds on continuum QRPA studies for spherical systems (Mizuyama 2007) and complements fully consistent HFB+QRPA calculations with the Gogny effective interaction for deformed nuclei (Peru 2008), which use a harmonic oscillator basis that is less suitable for describing neutron drip-line nuclei. The new method enables simultaneous treatment of deformation, pairing correlations including unbound quasiparticle states, and full momentum dependence of the Skyrme interaction, allowing quantitative description of both vibrational modes and rotational modes (moments of inertia via the Thouless-Valatin procedure) in neutron-rich nuclei.
