# Nuclear enhancement of universal dynamics of high parton densities

> We show that the enhancement of the saturation scale in large nuclei relative to the proton is significantly influenced by the effects of quantum evolution and the impact parameter dependence of dipole cross sections in high energy QCD. We demonstrate that there is a strong A dependence in diffractive deeply inelastic scattering and discuss its sensitivity to the measurement of the recoil nucleus.

## Key claim

The nuclear enhancement of the saturation scale in large nuclei relative to the proton is significantly larger than the simple geometric expectation of approximately \(0.26 A^{1/3}\) when one includes the effects of QCD evolution and the realistic impact-parameter dependence of dipole cross sections; the enhancement depends on whether one uses a DGLAP-based (IPsat) or a geometric-scaling (bCGC) model of quantum evolution.

## Method

The authors generalize the dipole formalism for deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) from a proton to a nucleus by introducing the coordinates of individual nucleons distributed according to the Woods-Saxon profile. They compute the nuclear dipole cross section in two models: the IPsat model, which incorporates DGLAP evolution of the gluon distribution, and the bCGC model, which implements geometric scaling from nonlinear small-\(x\) evolution. The saturation scale \(Q_s\) is defined as the solution of \(\mathrm{d}\sigma/\mathrm{d}^2 b (r^2 = 1/Q_s^2) = 2(1-e^{-1/4})\), and its \(A\)- and \(x\)-dependence is evaluated at both \(b=0\) and the median impact parameter \(b_\mathrm{med.}\) for the total cross section.

## Key numbers

- The simple geometric estimate gives \(Q_{s,A}^2 / Q_{s,p}^2 \approx 0.26 A^{1/3}\) for a nucleus with radius \(R_A \approx 1.1 A^{1/3}\,\mathrm{fm}\) and \(2\pi R_p^2 \approx 20\,\mathrm{mb}\).
- In gold nuclei at \(x=0.001\), the saturation scale at the median impact parameter is about 70% of the value at \(b=0\); for the proton, \(Q_s^2(b_\mathrm{med.})\) is only about 35% of the value at \(b=0\).
- To obtain the same \(Q_{s,A}^2(b_\mathrm{med.})(x)\) as in an e+Au collider, an e+p collider would require a center-of-mass energy roughly 14 times larger.
- For large nuclei, the fraction of diffractive \(q\bar{q}\) events relative to the inclusive cross section can be enhanced by up to 100% compared to the fraction for a proton.

## Context

This work extends the impact-parameter saturation model (IPsat) of Kowalski and Teaney (2003) and the bCGC model of Iancu, Itakura, and Munier (2003) from proton targets to nuclear targets, comparing predictions with nuclear DIS data from the NMC collaboration (Amaudruz et al., 1995). The results show that the nuclear saturation scale grows faster than \(A^{1/3}\) due to DGLAP scaling violations in the IPsat model, while the bCGC model shows a weaker enhancement; this difference can be tested at a future electron-ion collider. The analysis also demonstrates that the impact-parameter dependence of the dipole cross section is crucial for understanding the \(t\)-dependence of diffractive scattering and the distinction between coherent (nucleus intact) and incoherent (nucleus breaks up) diffraction.
