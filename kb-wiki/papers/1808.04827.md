# Anyonic particle-vortex statistics and the nature of dense quark matter

> We show that $\mathbb{Z}_3$-valued particle-vortex braiding phases are present in high density quark matter. Certain mesonic and baryonic excitations, in the presence of a superfluid vortex, have orbital angular momentum quantized in units of $\hbar/3$. Such non-local topological features can distinguish phases whose realizations of global symmetries, as probed by local order parameters, are identical. If $\mathbb{Z}_3$ braiding phases and angular momentum fractionalization are absent in lower density hadronic matter, as is widely expected, then the quark matter and hadronic matter regimes of dense QCD must be separated by at least one phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that high-density quark matter exhibits $\mathbb{Z}_3$-valued particle-vortex braiding phases and angular momentum fractionalization in units of $\hbar/3$, and argues that if these topological features are absent in lower-density hadronic matter (as widely expected), then the quark matter and hadronic matter regimes of dense QCD must be separated by at least one phase transition, contradicting the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schafer and Wilczek (1998).

## Method

The authors analyze color holonomies (Wilson loops) around superfluid vortices in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense QCD with $SU(3)$ flavor symmetry, using an effective Lagrangian for the diquark condensate field $\phi$ and examining the holonomy effective potential on $\mathbb{R}^2 \times S^1$. They compute the $SU(3)$ holonomy around a minimal superfluid vortex using the explicit vortex field ansatz of Balachandran et al. (2005), and construct a 5D topological field theory (BF theory) that encodes the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ braiding phases in the low-energy effective description.

## Key numbers

- Quark pairing gap $\Delta \sim \mu \, g(\mu)^{-5} e^{-3\pi^2/(g(\mu)\sqrt{2})}$ in the CFL phase
- Holonomy effective potential energy difference between local minima and global minimum: $\Delta V_{\rm eff} = 4\pi^2 \kappa\Delta^2 v_s^2 / (3L^2)$ on $\mathbb{R}^2 \times S^1$
- Superfluid velocity along compactified direction: $u_z = \pi/(\mu_B L) \, (k_1+k_2+k_3)$
- Orbital angular momentum shift for excitations in presence of minimal vortex: $\Delta L_z = \hbar/3$
- The $SU(3)$ holonomy around a minimal vortex gives phase $\gamma = \exp(2\pi i /3 \cdot \ell(C,P))$ where $\ell(C,P)$ is the linking number

## Context

This work extends the analysis of topological order in condensed matter systems (Wen 1990, Hansson 2004) to dense QCD, and directly addresses the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schafer and Wilczek (1998). The results contradict the assertion of Alford et al. (2018) that color holonomies around vortices must be trivial due to color screening. The paper enables future investigation of whether these topological features persist when flavor symmetry is broken toward physical quark masses, and establishes that the conventional low-energy effective action for the $U(1)_B$ Nambu-Goldstone boson is incomplete, requiring a 5D topological term to capture the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ braiding phases.
