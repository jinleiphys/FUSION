# Three-alpha-cluster structure of the 0^+ states in ^{12}C and the effective alpha-alpha interactions

> The $0^{+}$ states of $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ are considered within the framework of the microscopic three-$α$-cluster model. The main attention is paid to accurate calculation of the width of the extremely narrow near-threshold $0^+_2$ state which plays a key role in stellar nucleosynthesis. It is shown that the $0^{+}_2$-state decays by means of the sequential mechanism ${^{12}\mathrm{C}} \to α+{^8\mathrm{Be}} \to 3α$. Calculations are performed for a number of effective $α- α$ potentials which are chosen to reproduce both energy and width of $^8\mathrm{Be}$. The parameters of the additional three-body potential are chosen to fix both the ground and excited state energies at the experimental values. The dependence of the width on the parameters of the effective $α- α$ potential is studied in order to impose restrictions on the potentials.

## Key claim

The width of the extremely narrow near-threshold \(0^+_2\) state of \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) is calculated using a microscopic three-alpha-cluster model, and the result depends strongly on the chosen effective alpha-alpha potential; for all potentials considered the calculated width overestimates the experimental value of \(8.5 \pm 1\) eV by a factor of 1.2 to 3.1, although the difference for potential 3 is within theoretical and experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The three-alpha system is described by a Schrodinger equation with an effective two-body alpha-alpha potential (a modified Ali-Bodmer form) plus an additional three-body Gaussian potential. The total wave function is expanded in eigenfunctions on a hypersphere (Macek 1968), and the resulting hyper-radial equations are solved for both the bound ground state and the scattering problem for the excited resonance. The two-body potentials are chosen to reproduce the experimental energy and width of the \(^8\mathrm{Be}\) ground state, while the three-body potential parameters are adjusted to fix both the ground and excited state energies of \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) at their experimental values.

## Key numbers

* The width \(\Gamma\) of the \(0^+_2\) state of \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) is calculated for four different alpha-alpha potentials; the values range from 10 eV (potential 3, with \(N_1=3\) HRE) to 26 eV (potential 2, with \(N_1=3\) HRE), compared to the experimental value \(8.5 \pm 1.0\) eV.
* The two-body alpha-alpha resonance (ground state of \(^8\mathrm{Be}\)) widths \(\gamma\) used are 6.80 eV (potential 1), 8.53 eV (potential 2), 5.11 eV (potential 3), and 5.10 eV (potential 4); the experimental value is \(91.89\) keV for the energy, with width uncertainty \(\pm 1.7\) eV.
* The ground-state RMS radius \(R^{(1)}\) of \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) is calculated as 2.55 fm (potential 1, \(N_1=3\)), 2.77 fm (potential 2), 2.24 fm (potential 3), and 2.49 fm (potential 4); the experimental value is 2.47 fm.
* The monopole transition matrix element \(M_{12}\) is calculated as 9.03 fm\(^2\) (potential 1), 8.85 fm\(^2\) (potential 2), 8.14 fm\(^2\) (potential 3), and 8.21 fm\(^2\) (potential 4); the experimental value is 5.7 fm\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous three-alpha-cluster calculations by Fedorov (1996) and Filikhin (2000), achieving significantly better accuracy for the resonance width. The study shows that the width of the Hoyle state (\(0^+_2\)) depends sensitively on the width of the two-body alpha-alpha resonance in \(^8\mathrm{Be}\), and that the decay proceeds sequentially via \(^{12}\mathrm{C} \to \alpha + ^8\mathrm{Be} \to 3\alpha\). The results enable more precise constraints on effective alpha-alpha interactions and provide a necessary step toward a unified treatment of the low-energy triple-alpha reaction, including the non-resonant regime relevant for accretion onto white dwarfs and neutron stars.
