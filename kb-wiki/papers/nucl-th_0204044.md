# On the spin, parity and nature of the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance

> Using a unitary extension of chiral perturbation theory with a lowest-order s-wave SU(3) chiral Lagrangian we study low-energy meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness $S=-2$ sector. A scattering-matrix pole is found around 1606 MeV which corresponds to an s-wave $Ξ$ resonance with $J^P=1/2^-$. We identify this resonance with the $Ξ(1620)$ state, quoted in the Particle Data Book with $I=1/2$ but with unknown spin and parity. The addition of the $S=-2$ state to the recently computed $Λ(1670)$, $Σ(1620)$ and N(1535) states completes the octet of $J^P=1/2^-$ resonances dynamically generated in this chiral unitary approach.

## Key claim

Using a unitary extension of chiral perturbation theory, the authors identify a dynamically generated s-wave Ξ resonance with J^P = 1/2^- around 1606 MeV as the Ξ(1620) state, but the identification is conditional: the pole mass is stable near 1600 MeV, not at 1690 MeV, and the apparent width observed experimentally is much smaller than the pole width due to a threshold (Flatté) effect from the nearby \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) channel opening at 1611 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S = -2 sector, using the lowest-order s-wave SU(3) chiral Lagrangian as the driving kernel V. The loop integrals G are regularized with subtraction constants a_l, and poles are searched for in the second Riemann sheet of the elastic πΞ → πΞ amplitude for isospin I = 1/2. The subtraction constants are varied within a natural range (around -2) to study the stability of the pole position and width.

## Key numbers

- Pole mass found at 1607 MeV with half-width Γ/2 = 140 MeV for all subtraction constants set to -2.0 (Set 1).
- With a_πΞ = -3.1 and a_\(\bar{K}\Lambda\) = -1.0 (Set 5), the pole mass is 1605 MeV and half-width Γ/2 = 66 MeV.
- The \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) threshold opens at 1611 MeV, causing a Flatté effect that reduces the apparent Breit-Wigner width to about 50 MeV for Set 5.
- The squared coupling to πΞ is |g_πΞ|^2 = 8.7 (Set 1) and 5.9 (Set 5); to \(\bar{K}\Lambda\) it is 5.5 (Set 1) and 7.0 (Set 5); couplings to \(\bar{K}\Sigma\) and ηΞ are weak (|g|^2 < 1 in all sets).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the S = -1 sector, where the Λ(1670) and Σ(1620) resonances were identified by the same group (Ref. "cornelius" in the text), and to the S = 0 sector for the N*(1535) by Siegel (1995), Kaiser (1995, 1997), and others. The addition of the Ξ(1620) completes the J^P = 1/2^- octet of dynamically generated s-wave resonances, alongside the Λ(1405) which is largely the SU(3) singlet. The results enable experimental confirmation of the spin and parity assignment for the 1-star Ξ(1620) and motivate renewed measurements of its decay properties.
