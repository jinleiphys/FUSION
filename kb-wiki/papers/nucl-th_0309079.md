# Pairing correlations and transitions in nuclear systems

> We discuss several pairing-related phenomena in nuclear systems, ranging from superfluidity in neutron stars to the gradual breaking of pairs in finite nuclei. We describe recent experimental evidence that points to a relation between pairing and phase transitions (or transformations) in finite nuclear systems. A simple pairing interaction model is used in order to study and classify an eventual pairing phase transition in finite fermionic systems such as nuclei. We show that systems with as few as 10-16 fermions can exhibit clear features reminiscent of a phase transition.

# Pairing correlations and transitions in nuclear systems

## Key claim
Systems with as few as 10-16 fermions can exhibit clear features reminiscent of a phase transition, but the transition from a paired to an unpaired phase in such finite systems is not a phase transition in the ordinary thermodynamical sense; instead, the system develops several intermediate steps where different numbers of broken pairs can coexist, and the analysis indicates a second-order rather than first-order transition.

## Method
The authors diagonalized a simple pairing Hamiltonian with equidistant single-particle levels and a fixed pairing strength G=0.2 MeV for systems of up to 16 particles in 16 doubly degenerate levels at half-filling. They computed the exact density of states and used a free-energy histogram method, following Lee and Kosterlitz (1990) and Ferrenberg and Swendsen (1988), to analyze the development of free-energy minima as a function of temperature and system size. Experimental heat capacities for 161,162Dy and 171,172Yb were extracted from level densities measured using 45 MeV 3He projectiles at the University of Oslo cyclotron, and compared with shell model Monte Carlo results from Liu and Alhassid (1999) for iron isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The 1S0 neutron pairing gap in neutron star matter peaks at about 3 MeV at a Fermi momentum near kF ≈ 0.8 fm−1
- The 1S0 proton gap in β-stable matter is ≤ 1 MeV, and could be further reduced by a factor 2-3 by polarization effects
- The 3P2 gap in β-stable matter is of order ∼ 0.1 MeV
- The critical temperature for pairing in nuclei is Tc ≈ 0.5-1 MeV
- The entropy of even and odd 161,162Dy systems merges at T ≈ 0.5 MeV
- For the pairing model with d/G = 0.5 and 16 particles, the free-energy difference per particle ΔF/N at T = 0.85 MeV is approximately 0.5 MeV, remaining nearly constant (0.531, 0.505, 0.501, 0.495 MeV for N = 10, 12, 14, 16 respectively)
- For d/G > 1.5, the free energy develops only one minimum for all temperatures, indicating no sign of a phase transition

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of pairing phase transitions from infinite systems (where the BCS transition is well established as second order) to finite fermionic systems such as nuclei, building on earlier work by Anderson (1959) on the breakdown of superconductivity in small systems and by Tinkham et al. (1998) on ultrasmall aluminum grains. The experimental heat capacity measurements for rare-earth nuclei follow the work of Schiller et al. (2000) and are compared with shell model Monte Carlo calculations by Liu and Alhassid (1999) for iron isotopes. The free-energy histogram method was adapted from Lee and Kosterlitz (1990) and Ferrenberg and Swendsen (1988). The results enable future work with more realistic interactions and extensive shell-model Monte Carlo simulations to test whether such calculations support the experimentally observed level densities, and leave open questions about the role of polarization terms and hyperon pairing in neutron star superfluidity.
