# Feshbach Resonance due to Coherent Lambda-Sigma Coupling in Lambda_He^7

> Coherent Lambda-Sigma coupling effect in Lambda_He^7 is analyzed within three-body framework of two coupled channels, Lambda-t-t and Sigma-tau-t, where tau represents trinucleon which is either H^3 or He^3. The hyperon-trinucleon (Y tau) and trinucleon-trinucleon (tau tau) interactions are derived by folding G-matrices of YN and NN interactions with trinucleon density distributions. It is found that the binding energy of Lambda_He^7 is 4.04 MeV below the Lambda+t+t threshold without Lambda-Sigma coupling and the binding energy is increased to 4.46 MeV when the coupling effect is included. This state is 7.85 MeV above the He^6+Lambda threshold and it may have a chance to be observed as a Feshbach resonance in Li^7 (e,e'K^+)Lambda_He^7 experiment done at Jefferson Lab.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Feshbach resonance state in \(^{7}_{\Lambda}\)He exists at 7.85 MeV above the \(^{6}\)He+\(\Lambda\) threshold due to coherent \(\Lambda\)-\(\Sigma\) coupling, but notes that this state may only be observable if future electro-production experiments on \(^{7}\)Li with high statistics confirm the peak structure seen in earlier JLab data, as a more recent experimental spectrum shows only a bound-state peak below the threshold.

## Method

The authors performed a three-body coupled-channel calculation for \(^{7}_{\Lambda}\)He using the \(\Lambda\)-\(t\)-\(t\), \(\Sigma^{0}\)-\(t\)-\(t\), and \(\Sigma^{-}\)-\(h\)-\(t\) channels, where \(t\) is a triton and \(h\) is \(^{3}\)He. Hyperon-trinucleon (\(Y\tau\)) and trinucleon-trinucleon (\(\tau\tau\)) interactions were derived by folding \(G\)-matrices of the Nijmegen model-D \(YN\) potential and Tamagaki's OPEG \(NN\) potential with trinucleon density distributions, with range and strength parameters adjusted to reproduce empirical \(\Lambda\) binding energies of \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)H states. The three-body Schrödinger equation was solved using Kamimura's coupled rearrangement-channel method with Gaussian basis expansions, and Pauli exclusion between the two tritons was treated via the orthogonality-condition model.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^{7}_{\Lambda}\)He below the \(\Lambda\)+\(t\)+\(t\) threshold: 4.04 MeV without \(\Lambda\)-\(\Sigma\) coupling, increased to 4.46 MeV when the coupling effect is included.
- The resonance state lies 7.85 MeV above the \(^{6}\)He+\(\Lambda\) threshold.
- The missing-mass spectrum was calculated with a 3 MeV width for the \(tt\Lambda\) resonance.
- The hyperon-trinucleon interaction strengths for \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)H(0\(^{+}\)) and \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)H(1\(^{+}\)) are given in a table with five-range Gaussian parameters; for example, the \(V^{S=0}_{k}(\Lambda t\)-\(\Lambda t)\) strengths range from 1.7284 MeV (range \(\mu_{1}=1.00\) fm) to -1.1202 MeV (range \(\mu_{5}=3.50\) fm).

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent \(\Lambda\)-\(\Sigma\) coupling effects previously applied to s-shell hypernuclei by Harada (2000) and Akaishi et al. (2000), where the coupling contribution was found to be on the order of 1 MeV in \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)H and \(^{4}_{\Lambda}\)He ground states. The analysis uses a three-body cluster model similar to that employed by Kamimura (1981) for light nuclei, and the \(YN\) interaction is based on the Nijmegen model-D potential from Maessen et al. (1989). The predicted Feshbach resonance could be tested by future high-statistics electro-production experiments on \(^{7}\)Li at Jefferson Lab, building on the earlier JLab data from L. Yuan et al. (2006) that showed a peak near 7 MeV above the \(^{6}\)He+\(\Lambda\) threshold, though a more recent experiment by O. Hashimoto et al. (2010) found only a bound-state peak.
