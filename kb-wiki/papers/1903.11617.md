# Patterns of CP violation from mirror symmetry breaking in the $η\toπ^+π^-π^0$ Dalitz plot

> A violation of mirror symmetry in the $η\toπ^+π^-π^0$ Dalitz plot has long been recognized as a signal of C and CP violation. Here we show how the isospin of the underlying C- and CP-violating structures can be reconstructed from their kinematic representation in the Dalitz plot. Our analysis of the most recent experimental data reveals, for the first time, that the C- and CP-violating amplitude with total isospin $I=2$ is much more severely suppressed than that with total isospin $I=0$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the isospin of C- and CP-violating structures in η→π⁺π⁻π⁰ decay can be reconstructed from their kinematic representation in the Dalitz plot, and analysis of the most recent experimental data reveals that the C- and CP-violating amplitude with total isospin I=2 is much more severely suppressed than that with total isospin I=0, though the authors note their results depend on using next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory as an approximation.

## Method

The authors decompose the η→π⁺π⁻π⁰ decay amplitude into C-conserving (I=1) and C-violating (I=0 and I=2) components, parameterizing the latter with unknown complex constants α and β. They extract α and β by fitting the C- and CP-violating interference term to the odd-X polynomial coefficients (c, e, h, l) from KLOE-2 experimental data, using both a Taylor expansion method and a global χ² fit over a discretized Dalitz plot mesh, employing NLO chiral perturbation theory amplitudes from Gasser-Leutwyler and Bijnens-Ghorbani.

## Key numbers

- The left-right asymmetry from the global fit using the Gasser-Leutwyler amplitude: A_LR = (−7.18 ± 4.51) × 10⁻⁴
- The left-right asymmetry from the global fit using the Bijnens-Ghorbani amplitude: A_LR = (−7.20 ± 4.52) × 10⁻⁴
- Global fit result for Re(α) using Gasser-Leutwyler: −0.65 ± 0.80
- Global fit result for Re(β) using Gasser-Leutwyler: (−6.3 ± 14.7) × 10⁻⁴
- Global fit result for Im(α) using Gasser-Leutwyler: 0.44 ± 0.74
- Global fit result for Im(β) using Gasser-Leutwyler: (2.2 ± 2.0) × 10⁻³
- Quadrant asymmetry from Gasser-Leutwyler fit: A_Q = (2.85 ± 3.72) × 10⁻⁴
- Sextant asymmetry from Gasser-Leutwyler fit: A_S = (3.87 ± 4.04) × 10⁻⁴
- KLOE-2 unbinned results for comparison: A_Q = (1.8 ± 4.5 ⁺⁴·⁸₋₂·₃) × 10⁻⁴, A_S = (−0.4 ± 4.5 ⁺³·¹₋₃·₅) × 10⁻⁴

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Lee (1965) and Nauenberg (1965), who first identified charge asymmetries in η→π⁺π⁻π⁰ as signals of C and CP violation, and applies it to modern high-statistics data from the KLOE-2 collaboration (Anastasi, 2016). The method enables future experiments such as those planned at Jefferson Lab (Gan, 2017) and other facilities to discriminate between I=0 and I=2 C-violating new physics contributions through their distinct kinematic patterns in the Dalitz plot, rather than relying solely on integrated asymmetries like A_Q and A_S which hide the underlying strong amplitude structure.
