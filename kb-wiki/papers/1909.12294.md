# Non-equilibrium photons from the bottom-up thermalization scenario

> In this work, I calculate the $p_\perp$ resolved spectra for the three stages of the \textit{bottom-up} scenario, which are comparable to the thermal contribution, particularly at higher values of the saturation scale $Q_S^2$. Analytical solutions are obtained by including a parametrization of scaling solutions from far-from-equilibrium classical statistical lattice simulations into a small angle kinetic rate. Furthermore, a theoretically motivated ansatz is used to account for near-collinear enhancement of the low-$p_\perp$ radiation. The system is phenomenologically constrained using the charge hadron multiplicities from LHC and RHIC as in previous parametric estimates and fair agreement with the data available for photons was found. I find that for this realistic set of parameters, the contribution from the thermalizing glasma dominates the excess photons.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium photon contribution from the thermalizing glasma in the bottom-up scenario is comparable to the thermal contribution, and for realistic parameters constrained by LHC and RHIC charged hadron multiplicities, the glasma contribution dominates the excess photons, particularly at higher values of the saturation scale \(Q_S^2\).

## Method

The author calculates \(p_\perp\)-resolved photon spectra for the three stages of the Baier-Mueller-Schiff-Son (BMSS) bottom-up thermalization scenario using a \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) kinetic photon rate simplified via a small-angle approximation. The non-equilibrium quark distribution is sampled via the hard \(g\rightarrow q\bar{q}\) approximation \(f_q \sim \alpha_s f_g\), where the gluon distribution is taken from a parametrization of scaling solutions obtained from far-from-equilibrium classical statistical lattice simulations. A theoretically motivated ansatz replaces the temperature in the leading-order thermal bremsstrahlung and annihilation functions with the characteristic glasma scale \(Q_s\) to account for near-collinear enhancement of low-\(p_\perp\) radiation.

## Key numbers

* For the first glasma stage, the photon invariant yield per unit transverse area is \(\frac{1}{S_\perp}\frac{\mathrm{d}N_\gamma}{\mathrm{d}^2 p_\perp\mathrm{d}y} = \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2}}\frac{20}{9\pi^4} \alpha_e\,\kappa_g \,f_0\, \frac{\sigma_0}{Q_s} \,\log\left(\frac{\tau_1}{\tau_0}\right) \times \frac{Q^2_s}{p^2_\perp}\, W_r[p_\perp-Q_s]\), with \(\tau_0 = c_0 Q_s^{-1}\) and \(\tau_1 = c_1 \alpha_s^{-3/2} Q_s^{-1}\).
* For the second glasma stage, the photon invariant yield is \(\frac{1}{S_\perp}\frac{\mathrm{d}N^{(ii)}_\gamma}{\mathrm{d}^2 p_\perp\mathrm{d}y} = \sqrt{2\pi}\frac{20}{9\pi^4} \alpha_e\,\kappa_g\,f_0\, \frac{\sigma_0}{Q_s} \, \frac{Q^2_s}{p^2_\perp}\, W_r[p_\perp-Q_s] \,\left(\sqrt{\frac{c_2}{c_1}}\alpha_s^{-1/2}-1\right)\).
* The total yield from stage (i) for \(p_\perp<Q_s\) is \(\frac{1}{S_\perp}\frac{\mathrm{d}N_\gamma}{\mathrm{d}y} = \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2}}\frac{20}{9\pi^3} \alpha_e\,\kappa_g\,f_0\, \sigma_0\, Q_s \,\log(\alpha_s^{-3/2}) \log\left(\frac{Q_s^2}{\sigma_0^2}\right)\).
* The total yield from stage (ii) for \(p_\perp<Q_s\) is \(\frac{1}{S_\perp Q^2_S}\frac{\mathrm{d}N_\gamma}{\mathrm{d}y} = \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2}}\frac{40}{9\pi^3} \alpha_e\,\mathcal{L}\, \kappa_g\, \kappa_q \,\left(\alpha_s^{-1/2}-1\right)\).
* For RHIC at 200 GeV, the saturation scale for 0-5% centrality is \(Q_s^2 = 2.00 \pm 0.04\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) with \(N_{part}=353.0 \pm 10.0\) and transverse area \(S_\perp = 143.3 \pm 2.7\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
* For LHC at 2.76 TeV, the saturation scale for 0-5% centrality is \(Q_s^2 = 3.60 \pm 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) with \(N_{part}=383.5 \pm 1.9\) and transverse area \(S_\perp = 155.8 \pm 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
* The thermalization time and temperature are \(\tau_{th} \sim c_{eq}\,\alpha^{-13/5} Q_s^{-1}\) and \(T_{th} \sim c_{T}\,c_{eq}\,\alpha^{2/5} Q_s\), with the combined coefficient \(c_{eq} c_T^{3/4}\) determined phenomenologically; for LHC 0-5% centrality this coefficient is \(0.278 \pm 0.004\).

## Context

This work extends the parametric estimates of Berges et al. (2017), who found comparable total yields from thermal and glasma photons in the BMSS bottom-up thermalization scenario, by calculating the full \(p_\perp\)-resolved spectra. The calculation uses the scaling solutions from classical statistical lattice simulations by Berges et al. (2013, 2014) and the BMSS thermalization scenario by Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2000). The results address the "direct photon puzzle" by showing that pre-equilibrium photons, traditionally omitted in hydrodynamical models, can dominate the excess at low \(p_\perp\), enabling future phenomenological comparisons with ALICE and PHENIX data.
