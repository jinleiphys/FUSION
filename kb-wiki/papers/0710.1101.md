# Deuteron spin structure functions in the resonance and DIS regions

> We derive relations between spin-dependent nuclear and nucleon g_1 and g_2 structure functions within the nuclear impulse approximation, which are valid at all Q^2, and in both the resonance and deep inelastic regions. We apply the formalism to the specific case of the deuteron, which is often used as a source of neutron structure information, and compare the size of the nuclear corrections calculated using exact kinematics and using approximations applicable at large Q^2.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact relations between spin-dependent nuclear and nucleon \(g_1\) and \(g_2\) structure functions that are valid at all \(Q^2\) and in both the resonance and deep inelastic regions, but the derivation relies on the weak binding limit (WBL) in which nuclear matrix elements are expanded in powers of nucleon momentum \(\bm{p}/M\) and the nucleon off-shell structure functions are approximated by their on-shell values.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic nuclear impulse approximation for the hadronic tensor, then perform a systematic expansion in the nucleon momentum and separation energy (weak binding limit) to obtain a generalized convolution formula for nuclear \(g_1^A\) and \(g_2^A\) in terms of nucleon \(g_1^\tau\) and \(g_2^\tau\) and spin-dependent nucleon momentum distributions \(D_{ab}\). They specialize to the deuteron by expressing the spectral functions \(f_0, f_1, f_2\) in terms of the deuteron \(S\)- and \(D\)-state wave functions from the Paris and Bonn potentials, and evaluate the structure functions using the MAID unitary isobar model, Simula et al., and EG1 parameterizations for the nucleon inputs.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy \(\epsilon_D = -2.2\) MeV.
- Paris potential deuteron \(D\)-state probability \(P_D = 5.8\%\) and \(S\)-\(D\) interference \(P_{SD} = 9.4\%\); Bonn potential gives \(P_D = 4.3\%\) and \(P_{SD} = 10.1\%\).
- At \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) and the \(\Delta\) resonance peak (\(x \approx 0.75\)), the deuteron \(g_1^d\) magnitude is about half that of the nucleon \(g_1^N\).
- At intermediate \(x\) (second and third resonance regions), the full finite-\(Q^2\) results are 10–15% smaller in magnitude than the Bjorken limit approximation; at the \(\Delta\) peak the full results are 25–30% smaller.
- For leading twist structure functions at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\), the ratio of deuteron to nucleon first moments is \(\Gamma_1^d/\Gamma_1^N = 0.90\) with full kinematics and 0.91 with Bjorken limit kinematics; for \(\Gamma_2\) the same ratios hold.
- The depolarization factor for the deuteron is \((1 - \frac32 P_D) \approx 0.91\) for the Paris wave function.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nuclear effects in spin-dependent structure functions for the deuteron and \(^3\)He (e.g., SS, Italy, ScopD, ScopHe3) that were restricted to the Bjorken limit, by providing a formalism valid at finite \(Q^2\) and in the resonance region. It enables reliable extraction of neutron spin structure from deuteron data in the resonance region, where recent Jefferson Lab experiments (EG1b, EG4, RSS) have taken high-precision data, and quantifies the additional smearing from finite-\(Q^2\) kinematics that is missed by the Bjorken-limit convolution approximations used in prior work.
