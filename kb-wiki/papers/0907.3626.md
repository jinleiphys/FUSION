# J/psi production at mid and forward rapidity at RHIC

> We calculate the rapidity dependence of $J/ψ$ nuclear modification factor and averaged transverse momentum square in heavy ion collisions at RHIC in a 3-dimensional transport approach with regeneration mechanism.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed stronger suppression of J/ψ at forward rapidity compared to midrapidity at RHIC can be explained only when continuous regeneration from thermalized charm quarks is included, because models with only initial production predict the opposite trend.

## Method

The authors solved a 3-dimensional transport equation for charmonium distribution in phase space, including both initial production from nucleon-nucleon collisions and continuous regeneration via gluon dissociation and its inverse process. The QGP evolution was described by ideal hydrodynamics, and the charm quark distribution was taken as a thermalized Fermi distribution with a Gaussian rapidity dependence. The calculation was performed for Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV at both midrapidity (|y| < 0.35) and forward rapidity (1.2 < y < 2.2).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of charm production cross section at forward to midrapidity was taken as (dσ_pp^{c\bar{c}}/dη|_{η=1.7}) / (dσ_pp^{c\bar{c}}/dη|_{η=0}) = 1/3, used to determine the Gaussian width η_0.
- The charm production cross section at midrapidity was set to dσ_pp^{c\bar{c}}/dη|_{η=0} = 120 μb, consistent with experimental data.
- The critical temperature for deconfinement was taken as T_c = 165 MeV, with dissociation temperatures T_d^{J/ψ}/T_c = 1.9 and T_d^{χ_c}/T_c = T_d^{ψ'}/T_c = 1.
- The initial J/ψ transverse momentum squared at midrapidity was ⟨p_t²⟩(0) = 4.1 (GeV/c)², with a rapidity dependence ⟨p_t²⟩(y) = ⟨p_t²⟩(0)(1 − y²/y_max²).
- The ratio of initial contributions from J/ψ, χ_c, and ψ' was taken as 6:3:1.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport models by Zhu (2008) and Yan (2006) to include rapidity dependence, addressing the surprising experimental finding from PHENIX (2007) that J/ψ suppression is stronger at forward rapidity than at midrapidity, which contradicted models with only initial production. The results enable predictions for LHC energies where regeneration is expected to become the dominant J/ψ production mechanism. The approach contrasts with the statistical model of Braun-Munzinger (2003) and the kinetic models of Thews (2001) and Grandchamp (2004).
