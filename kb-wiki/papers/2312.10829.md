# Systematic Improvement of $x$-dependent Unpolarized Nucleon Generalized Parton Distribution in Lattice-QCD Calculation

> We present a first study of the effects of renormalization-group resummation (RGR) and leading-renormalon resummation (LRR) on the systematic errors of the unpolarized isovector nucleon generalized parton distribution in the framework of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). This work is done using lattice gauge ensembles generated by the MILC collaboration, consisting of 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks with a physical pion mass at lattice spacing $a\approx 0.09$ fm and a box width $L\approx 5.76$ fm. We present results for the nucleon $H$ and $E$ GPDs with average boost momentum $P_z\approx 2$ GeV at momentum transfers $Q^2=[0, 0.97]$ GeV$^2$ at skewness $ξ=0$ as well as $Q^2\in 0.23$ GeV$^2$ at $ξ=0.1$, renormalized in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at scale $μ=2.0$ GeV, with two- and one-loop matching, respectively. We demonstrate that the simultaneous application of RGR and LRR significantly reduces the systematic errors in renormalized matrix elements and distributions for both the zero and nonzero skewness GPDs, and that it is necessary to include both RGR and LRR at higher orders in the matching and renormalization processes.

## Key claim
The simultaneous application of renormalization-group resummation (RGR) and leading-renormalon resummation (LRR) significantly reduces systematic errors in renormalized matrix elements and distributions for both zero and nonzero skewness generalized parton distributions, and the authors demonstrate that including both RGR and LRR is necessary at higher orders in the matching and renormalization processes, with the caveat that the DGLAP evolution used for RGR matching is only applicable to zero-skewness GPDs and a more sophisticated technique is required for the ERBL region at nonzero skewness.

## Method
The authors used lattice gauge ensembles from the MILC collaboration with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks at physical pion mass and lattice spacing a≈0.09 fm, with clover valence fermions. Bare nucleon matrix elements with average boost momentum Pz≈2.2 GeV were renormalized in the hybrid-ratio scheme, then Fourier transformed to obtain quasi-GPDs, and finally matched to lightcone GPDs using perturbative matching kernels at NLO and NNLO, with RGR and LRR applied to both the Wilson coefficients in renormalization and the matching kernels.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a≈0.09 fm and box width L≈5.76 fm, with physical pion mass mπ≈130 MeV
- Average boost momentum Pz=10×(2π/L)≈2.2 GeV, from 501,760 measurements across 1960 lattice configurations
- Momentum transfers Q²=[0, 0.97] GeV² at skewness ξ=0, and Q²=0.23 GeV² at ξ=0.1
- Renormalization scale μ=2.0 GeV in the MSbar scheme
- Linear divergence δm=0.668(10) GeV determined from fitting zero-momentum pion matrix elements in the interval z=[0.54, 1.53] fm
- Systematic error reduction from NLO to (NLO+LRR)×RGR of approximately 70% to 90% in renormalized matrix elements
- Systematic error reduction from NNLO to (NNLO+LRR)×RGR of 30% to 70% at Q²=0.39 GeV² and 70% to 90% from NNLO to (NNLO+LRR)×RGR

## Context
This work extends the application of RGR and LRR, previously demonstrated for the pion PDF by Zhang (2023) and for the nucleon transversity PDF by Gao (2023), to the unpolarized nucleon generalized parton distribution for the first time. The calculation uses the same lattice ensembles and bare matrix elements as the earlier GPD study by Lin (2020), and the results enable more precise lattice-QCD calculations of nucleon tomography for comparison with future experimental programs at the Electron-Ion Collider.
