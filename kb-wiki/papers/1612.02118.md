# Fission barriers of two odd-neutron actinide nuclei taking into account the time-reversal symmetry breaking at the mean-field level

> Background: Fission barriers of actinide nuclei have been mostly and for long been microscopi- cally calculated for even-even fissioning systems. Calculations in the case of odd nuclei have been performed merely within a so-called equal-filling approximation (EFA) as opposed to an approach taking explicitly into account the time reversal breaking properties at the mean field level- and for only one single-particle configuration. Purpose: We study the dependence of the fission barriers on various relevant configurations (e.g. to evaluate the so-called specialization energy). Besides, we want to assess the relevance as a func- tion of the deformation of the EFA approach which has been already found out at ground state deformation. Methods: Calculations within the Hartree-Fock plus BCS with self-consistent particle blocking have been performed using the SkM* Skyrme effective interaction in the particle-hole channel and a seniority force in the particle-particle channel. Axial symmetry has been imposed throughout the whole fission path while the intrinsic parity symmetry has been allowed to be broken in the outer fission barrier region. Results: Potential energy curves have been determined for six different configurations in U-235 and four in Pu-239. Inner and outer fission barriers have been calculated along with some spectroscopic properties in the fission isomeric well. These results have been compared with available data. The influence of time-reversal breaking mean fields on the solutions has been investigated. Conclusions: A sizeable configuration dependence of the fission barrier (width and height) has been demonstrated. A reasonable agreement with available systematic evaluations of fission barrier heights has been found. The EFA approach has been validated at the large elongations occurring at the outer barrier region.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fission barriers in odd-neutron actinide nuclei depend significantly on the specific single-particle configuration of the unpaired neutron, and validates the equal-filling approximation (EFA) for describing the outer barrier region, though the EFA had previously only been validated at ground-state deformation.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-plus-BCS calculations with self-consistent particle blocking using the SkM* Skyrme effective interaction for the particle-hole channel and a seniority force for the particle-particle channel. Axial symmetry was imposed throughout the fission path, while intrinsic parity symmetry was allowed to break in the outer barrier region. Potential energy curves were computed as functions of the quadrupole moment Q20 for six configurations in 235U and four in 239Pu, with the rotational energy correction treated via three different approaches to the moment of inertia (Inglis-Belyaev, Inglis-Belyaev plus 32% Thouless-Valatin correction, and a 50% reduction from the Intrinsic Vorticity Model).

## Key numbers

- For 239Pu, calculations were performed for four configurations with quantum numbers Ωπ = 1/2+, 5/2+, 7/2−, and 7/2+
- For 235U, calculations were performed for six configurations with quantum numbers Ωπ = 1/2+, 3/2+, 5/2+, 5/2−, 7/2+, and 7/2−
- The basis size parameter N0 = 14 was used, corresponding to 15 spherical major shells
- The pairing strengths obtained with SkM* were Gn = Gp = 16.0 MeV
- The valence space for pairing included all single-particle states up to λq + 6 MeV
- At ground-state deformation of 234U, the spurious rotational energy was 2.371 MeV (IB) versus 1.232 MeV (IVM), giving Iav = 12.988
- At ground-state deformation of 240Pu, the spurious rotational energy was 2.408 MeV (IB) versus 1.232 MeV (IVM), giving Iav = 13.143
- The moment of inertia scaling factor α = 0.32 was used for the Thouless-Valatin correction following Libert, Girod, and Delaroche (1999)

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic fission-barrier calculations, which had been performed almost exclusively for even-even nuclei (e.g., Nikolov 2011, McDonnell 2013, Staszczak 2013), into the odd-mass regime. It builds on earlier studies of odd-mass actinides by Goriely et al. (2009) using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach and by Robledo et al. (2009) and Iglesia et al. (2009) using the equal-filling approximation for 235U and 239Pu. The results enable more reliable calculations of neutron-induced fission cross-sections and provide a benchmark for assessing the validity of the EFA approach across the full deformation range of the fission path.
