# Chiral effective model of cold and dense two-color QCD: The linear sigma model approach

> This review is devoted to summarizing recent developments of the linear sigma model (LSM) in cold and dense two-color QCD (QC$_2$D), in which lattice simulations are straightforwardly applicable thanks to the disappearance of the sign problem. In QC$_2$D, both theoretical and numerical studies derive the presence of the so-called baryon superfluid phase at sufficiently large chemical potential ($μ_q$), where diquark condensates govern the ground state. The hadron mass spectrum simulated in this phase shows that the mass of an iso-singlet ($I=0$) and $0^-$ state is remarkably reduced, but such a mode cannot be described by the chiral perturbation theory. Motivated by this fact, I invent the LSM constructed upon the linear representation of chiral symmetry, or more precisely the Pauli-Gürsey symmetry. Then, it is shown that my LSM successfully reproduces the low-lying hadron mass spectrum in a broad range of $μ_q$ simulated on the lattice. As applications of the LSM, topological susceptibility and sound velocity in cold and dense QC$_2$D are evaluated to compare with lattice results. Besides, generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation and hardon mass spectrum in the presence of a diquark source are analyzed. I also introduce an extended version of the LSM incorporating spin-$1$ hadrons.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model (LSM) constructed upon the linear representation of the Pauli-Gürsey SU(4) symmetry successfully reproduces the low-lying hadron mass spectrum in cold and dense two-color QCD across a broad range of quark chemical potential, including the iso-singlet (I=0) and 0^- state that cannot be described by chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The author constructs a linear sigma model based on the linear representation of the Pauli-Gürsey SU(4) symmetry for two-flavor two-color QCD, which is spontaneously broken to Sp(4). The model incorporates both Nambu-Goldstone bosons (pions and scalar diquarks) and their parity partners (scalar mesons and 0^- diquarks) as explicit degrees of freedom. The effective model is matched to underlying QC2D via spurion fields and Ward-Takahashi identities derived from the symmetry properties of the path-integral formulation. The model predictions are compared with lattice simulation results for hadron masses, topological susceptibility, and sound velocity.

## Key numbers

- The number of Nambu-Goldstone bosons associated with SU(2N_f) to Sp(2N_f) breaking is 2N_f^2 - N_f - 1, which for N_f=2 gives 5 NG bosons: N_f^2-1 = 3 pions and N_f^2-N_f = 2 flavor-antisymmetric scalar diquarks
- The symmetry breaking pattern is SU(4) to Sp(4) for two-flavor QC2D with identical quark masses
- The SU(4) group has 15 generators, of which 10 belong to the Sp(4) algebra and 5 belong to the coset SU(4)/Sp(4)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Kogut (1999, 2000) for two-color QCD, which successfully describes hadron masses and sound velocity near the phase transition but cannot describe the iso-singlet 0^- mode observed in lattice simulations by Murakami (2022). The linear sigma model approach enables description of both NG bosons and their parity partners, with validity at higher chemical potential where chiral symmetry is restored. The model enables comparison with lattice results for topological susceptibility and sound velocity, and provides a framework for analyzing generalized Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relations and hadron mass spectra in the presence of a diquark source.
