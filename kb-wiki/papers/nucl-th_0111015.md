# Electromagnetic structure of the deuteron

> Recent measurements of the deuteron electromagnetic structure functions A, B, and $T_{20}$ extracted from high energy elastic $ed$ scattering, and the cross sections and asymmetries extracted from high energy photodisintegration $γ+d\to n+p$, are reviewed and compared to theory. The theoretical calculations range from nonrelativistic and relativistic models using the traditional meson and baryon degrees of freedom, to effective field theories, to models based on the underlying quark and gluon degrees of freedom of QCD, including nonperturbative quark cluster models and perturbative QCD. We review what has been learned from these experiments, and discuss why elastic $ed$ scattering and photodisintegration seem to require very different theoretical approaches, even though they are closely related experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical progress on deuteron electromagnetic structure, arguing that elastic electron-deuteron scattering and photodisintegration require very different theoretical approaches even though they are closely related experimentally: elastic scattering can be described by relativistic meson-baryon models with minor modifications to nucleon currents, while photodisintegration at high energies (4 GeV photons) seems to require explicit quark degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors survey experimental measurements of the deuteron electromagnetic structure functions A, B, and T20 from elastic electron-deuteron scattering, and cross sections and asymmetries from high-energy photodisintegration (gamma + d → n + p), comparing these data to theoretical calculations ranging from nonrelativistic and relativistic meson-baryon models to effective field theories, quark cluster models, and perturbative QCD. They analyze the world data set of 269 cross sections from 19 references dating from 1960 to the present, plus 20 published T20 data points and 19 points for other polarization observables.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy epsilon = 2.224 MeV, used with nucleon mass m = 940 MeV in scaling functions
- The D-state probability P_D is a model-dependent quantity with no unique measurable value
- At Q^2 = 0, the form factors give: G_C(0) = 1 (in units of e), G_Q(0) = Q_d (in units of e/m_d^2), G_M(0) = mu_d (in units of e/2m_d)
- The minimum of T20 ≈ -√2 is reached when y = 2ηG_Q/3G_C = 1
- At the node in the charge form factor G_C = 0, T20 = -1/√2
- Beam energies needed for B measurements at 180°: E = 0.65, 1.02, 1.35, 1.67, and 1.97 GeV correspond to Q^2 = 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 GeV^2 respectively
- Energy or angle offsets of a few times 10^{-3} can cause Q^2 offsets up to 0.5%, leading to A and B offsets reaching about 2% at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 and 4% at Q^2 = 6 GeV^2
- The five wave function models shown are almost identical (variations less than 10%) for momenta below about 400 MeV, varying by less than a factor of 2 as momenta reach 1 GeV
- The Hall A experiment achieved luminosity of approximately 5 × 10^{38}/cm^2/s with >100 μA beams on a 15 cm cryogenic LD2 target
- Hall C data are systematically larger than Hall A data by just over 2σ, slightly over 10%, with uncertainties in each experiment dominated by systematics of approximately 5-6% and statistical precisions near 1%

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Garcon and Van Orden (2001) and Sick (2001), which covered static deuteron properties and nucleon form factors. The paper synthesizes results from multiple JLab experiments (E91-026, E94-018, and photodisintegration experiments 89-012, 89-019, 96-003) alongside earlier measurements from SLAC E101, Bonn, CEA, Saclay ALS, Mainz, and Monterey. The work enables future experiments at JLab (including proposals 00-103, 00-007, and 00-107) and highlights the need for new high-precision measurements in the Q^2 range where Mainz and Saclay ALS data disagree.
