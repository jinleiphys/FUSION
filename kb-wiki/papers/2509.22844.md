# Regularized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula for higher partial waves in femtoscopy

> Femtoscopy is one of the promising experimental approaches to put constraints on interactions between various species of hadrons from the momentum correlation functions measured in high-energy nuclear collision experiments. The Koonin-Pratt and Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formulae provide useful expressions of the correlation functions and have been widely used to analyze the experimental data based on the assumption that the effect of higher partial waves is negligible. Those formulae can be generalized for higher partial waves, but the generalized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula produces wrong results due to a singular behavior of the asymptotic wave function at the origin. In this study, we attempt to solve the problem by regularizing the generalized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula with a cutoff and validate it using the Koonin-Pratt formula as a reference. We also show the relationship between the cutoff in the regularized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz and the effective-range correction in the original Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formula. Using the obtained formula, we investigate the source-size dependence, the validity of the effective range expansion, and the cutoff dependence of the correlation function. We also discuss the interaction dependence using the heatmap as a function of the scattering-length parameter $1/a_l$ and the momentum $q$.

## Key claim

The generalized Lednicky-Lyuboshitz (LL) formula for higher partial waves ($l\ge1$) produces unphysical results due to a singular behavior of the asymptotic wave function at the origin; this work proposes a regularization with a cutoff that resolves the singularity and shows that the regularization effect is equivalent to the effective-range correction in the original $s$-wave LL formula.

## Method

The authors regularize the generalized LL formula by introducing a cutoff radius $r_c$ and switching between two asymptotic forms of the partial-wave function $R_l(r)$ at $r=r_c$: a regular form proportional to $j_l(\kappa r)$ for $r<r_c$ and the standard asymptotic form $j_l(qr)+iqf_l(q)h_l(qr)$ for $r>r_c$. Three regularization schemes are tested: \rLLc{} (plane-wave interior, discontinuous at $r_c$), \rLLd{} (continuous wave function with delta-shell potential), and \rLLw{} (continuous wave function and derivative with square-well potential). The regularized formulae are validated against the exact Koonin-Pratt (KP) formula using Gaussian central potentials with reduced mass $\mu = 469\ \mathrm{MeV}$.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass $\mu = 469\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (approximately $m_N/2$) used for all test calculations.
- Source size fixed to $R = 2.0\ \mathrm{fm}$ in the demonstration of partial-wave contributions (Figure 3).
- The two-range Gaussian potential $V_\mathrm{2G}(r)$ has a shallow $s$-wave bound state with binding energy $2.193\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (deuteron-like).
- The attractive one-range Gaussian potential $V_\mathrm{1GA}(r)$ has an $s$-wave bound state with binding energy $26.87\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- The cutoff $r_c$ is related to the effective range $r_0$ by $r_c = r_0/2$ in the low-momentum limit, derived from equating the regularization correction to the effective-range correction term.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous generalization of the Koonin-Pratt and Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formulae to higher partial waves (Murase 2024), which suffered from singular behavior at the origin for $l\ge1$. The regularization approach is analogous to the ultraviolet regulator used by Albaladejo (2024) for the $s$-wave case. The regularized LL formula enables model-independent extraction of scattering parameters (scattering length $a_l$, effective range $r_l$) from femtoscopic correlation functions for higher partial waves, which is relevant for analyzing resonances such as $\Lambda(1520)$ ($d$-wave) in $K^-p$ correlations and $\Xi(1820)$ in $\Lambda K^-$ correlations measured by ALICE.
