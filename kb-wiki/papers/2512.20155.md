# Internal structure of near-threshold states using compositeness

> Understanding the internal structure of near-threshold states is essential for revealing the nature of exotic hadrons. Motivated by this challenge, we discuss the clustering structures of near-threshold $s$-wave eigenstates using the compositeness, which characterizes the clustering nature of the states. We show that shallow bound states usually possess cluster-dominant structures, while near-threshold narrow resonances are non-cluster-dominant. Through this study, we establish a theoretical foundation for the threshold energy rule, which has been known empirically.

## Key claim

Shallow bound states below threshold usually possess cluster-dominant structures, while near-threshold narrow resonances above threshold are non-cluster-dominant; this provides a theoretical foundation for the empirically known threshold energy rule, but the result depends on the model parameter range and an interpretation scheme for complex compositeness.

## Method

The authors employed a nonrelativistic effective field theory (EFT) model in which a bare state with zero compositeness (pure elementary state) is coupled to free s-wave scattering states, with a cutoff momentum Λ specifying the typical scale. For bound states, the compositeness X was expressed as a function of model parameters and binding energy, and its dependence on the bare-state energy ν₀ was examined at fixed binding energy B = 0.01 E_typ. For resonances, the compositeness was derived from the effective range expansion (ERE) via the weak-binding relation, and an interpretation scheme was introduced to convert the complex compositeness into three probabilistic quantities (𝒳, 𝒴, 𝒵) using a parameter α ≈ 1.1318 chosen to exclude broad resonances (those with ℜE ≤ Γ) from interpretation.

## Key numbers

- For a shallow bound state with B = 0.01 E_typ, the compositeness satisfies X > 0.5 in 88% of the allowed parameter region (colored region in Fig. 1).
- The remaining 12% of the parameter region allows non-cluster-dominant shallow bound states, but this requires fine-tuning.
- The interpretation parameter α is set to α = (√5 − 1 + √(10 − 4√5))/2 ≈ 1.1318.
- For near-threshold narrow resonances in the interpretable region, the elementarity 𝒵 satisfies 𝒵 ≳ 0.8, indicating non-cluster-dominant structure.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy universality framework of Braaten (2004) and Naidon (2016) and the compositeness formalism of Weinberg (1965) and Hyodo (2013, 2014) to provide a theoretical basis for the threshold energy rule, which was empirically known from Ikeda (1968) and the Ikeda diagram for α-cluster states in nuclear physics. The results enable quantitative predictions about whether a near-threshold exotic hadron is likely to be a hadronic molecule (cluster-dominant) or a compact multiquark state (non-cluster-dominant), and show that above-threshold narrow resonances require specific dynamics rather than universal mechanisms.
