# Model-Independent Inference of Neutron Star Radii from Moment of Inertia Measurements

> A precise moment of inertia measurement for PSR J0737-3039A in the double pulsar system is expected within the next five years. We present here a new method of mapping the anticipated measurement of the moment of inertia directly into the neutron star structure. We determine the maximum and minimum values possible for the moment of inertia of a neutron star of a given radius based on physical stability arguments, assuming knowledge of the equation of state only at densities below the nuclear saturation density. If the equation of state is trusted up to the nuclear saturation density, we find that a measurement of the moment of inertia will place absolute bounds on the radius of PSR J0737-3039A to within $\pm$1 km. The resulting combination of moment of inertia, mass, and radius measurements for a single source will allow for new, stringent constraints on the dense-matter equation of state.

# Model-Independent Inference of Neutron Star Radii from Moment of Inertia Measurements

## Key claim
If the equation of state is trusted only up to nuclear saturation density, a future measurement of the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A will place absolute bounds on its radius to within ±1 km, independent of assumptions about the behavior of matter at higher densities.

## Method
The authors numerically integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for stellar structure simultaneously with a relativistic differential equation for the moment of inertia, assuming an equation of state only at densities below a fiducial density (either nuclear saturation density or half that value). For the interior region above that density, they adopt one of two constant-density configurations to either maximize or minimize the resulting moment of inertia, subject to physical stability conditions for a perfect fluid. They repeat this calculation for a fixed neutron star mass of 1.338 solar masses (the mass of Pulsar A) across a range of stellar radii, using the AP4 equation of state as a representative low-density EoS and then verifying the weak dependence on that choice by testing other EoS.

## Key numbers
- The mass of Pulsar A is fixed at 1.338 solar masses.
- The nuclear saturation density is approximately 2.7 × 10^14 g cm^-3.
- The moments of inertia predicted by 41 different equations of state for a 1.338 solar mass neutron star vary by more than a factor of approximately 3, corresponding to radii varying by nearly 10 km.
- A measurement of the moment of inertia with 10% accuracy will constrain the radius of PSR J0737-3039A to within ±1 km.
- The observation of a 2 solar mass neutron star already places a lower limit on the radius of 8.3 km.
- For a sample measurement of I = 1.3 × 10^45 g cm^2 with 10% uncertainty, the inferred pressure at nuclear saturation density is approximately 2.6 MeV fm^-3, and the symmetry energy parameter L is approximately 48 ± 10 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Morrison (2004) and Bejger (2005), which calculated moments of inertia for specific classes of equations of state but were limited by sampling only a restricted range of physical possibilities. The new method provides model-independent bounds that do not require assuming any particular high-density EoS, enabling direct comparison of moment-of-inertia measurements to other astrophysical radius measurements from spectroscopic methods such as those reviewed by Ozel (2015). This framework will allow quantitative constraints on the symmetry energy parameters at nuclear saturation density and can be combined with the existence of 2 solar mass neutron stars to further tighten radius limits.
