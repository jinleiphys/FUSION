# The role of the $Δ(1232)$-resonance in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We stress, on theoretical and phenomenological grounds, the importance of the $Δ(1232)$-resonance in a chiral effective field theory approach applied to the study of $πN$ scattering. We show how its inclusion as a dynamical degree of freedom allow us to obtain reliably valuable information from $πN$ scattering data.

## Key claim

The explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance as a dynamical degree of freedom in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, combined with the extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme, is necessary to achieve reliable convergence of the chiral series and to extract trustworthy information from πN scattering data, a result that is not obtained in the infrared regularization or heavy baryon formulations.

## Method

The author employs the consistent formulation of chiral Lagrangians for the Rarita-Schwinger field representing the Δ(1232) together with the δ-counting that accounts for the mass splitting δ = mΔ − mN. The extended-on-mass-shell renormalization scheme is used to preserve explicit Lorentz invariance while maintaining the standard chiral counting. The resulting covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory amplitude is then applied to fit modern πN scattering data to extract the pion-nucleon sigma term and the strangeness content of the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- σπN = 59(7) MeV, extracted from modern πN scattering data using covariant BChPT including the Δ(1232) resonance.
- σ0 = 58(8) MeV, obtained from a covariant BChPT calculation including the decuplet contribution, updating the earlier value of 35(5) MeV from Gasser (1980) and 36(7) MeV from Borasoy (1996).
- σs = 16(80) MeV, derived from σπN = 59(7) MeV and σ0 = 58(8) MeV, in contrast to the classical value σπN = 45(8) MeV from Gasser (1990) which yields σs = −150(80) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the line of baryon chiral perturbation theory applied to πN scattering that began in the early 1990s with Jenkins (1991), and addresses the convergence problems identified in the infrared regularization scheme by Becher (2001). The inclusion of the Δ(1232) in the covariant EOMS scheme resolves previous disagreements with dispersive methods and enables reliable extraction of the pion-nucleon sigma term and strangeness content from experimental data alone, with results compatible with modern LQCD determinations by Alvarez-Ruso (2013), Ohki (2008), and Junnarkar (2013).
