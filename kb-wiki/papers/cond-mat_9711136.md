# On Charged Mesoscopic Metallic Bubbles

> We investigate the existence of stable charged metallic bubbles using the shell correction method. We find that for a given mesoscopic system of n atoms of a given metal and q less n (positive) elementary charges, a metallic bubble turns out to have a lower total energy than a compact spherical cluster, whenever the charge number q is larger than acritical charge number q_c. For a magic number (n-q) of free electrons, the spherical metallic bubble may become stable against fission.

# On Charged Mesoscopic Metallic Bubbles

## Key claim

For a mesoscopic system of n atoms of a given metal with q positive elementary charges (q ≪ n), a spherical metallic bubble has lower total energy than a compact spherical cluster whenever the charge number q exceeds a critical value q_c, but only if the number (n−q) of free electrons corresponds to a magic number can the bubble become stable against fission.

## Method

The authors applied the Strutinsky shell correction method, decomposing the total energy into a liquid-drop (LD) part (volume, surface, and Coulomb terms) and a shell correction energy from single-particle states in an infinite spherical square-well potential. The LD energy was evaluated for spherical bubble geometries parameterized by the hole fraction f = R₂³/R₁³, and the shell correction was computed from eigenvalues of the Schrödinger equation in the bubble potential. Stability against fission was assessed by treating small deformations via a scaling transformation to a spheroidal bubble, with the deformation-dependent Hamiltonian treated perturbatively or by diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- For Na clusters: Wigner-Seitz radius r_s = 2.070 Å, surface tension σ = 0.01894 eV, volume energy coefficient τ = 0.03017 eV.
- For Sb clusters: r_s = 1.130 Å, σ = 0.02474 eV, τ = 0.4552 eV.
- The critical fissility parameter above which the LD energy favors a bubble is X₀^cr = 3.4.
- For Sb bubble clusters with hole fraction f = 0.7, the shell correction energy reaches −3 eV for n = 648 valence electrons.
- For the magic bubble cluster ₂₁₀Sb⁺¹⁰, the shell energy at the spherical configuration is −2.7 eV, producing a fission barrier of comparable magnitude.
- The average energy gain per atom for Na bubbles is approximately 0.3 eV; for Sb bubbles it can reach 1 eV per atom.
- The charge number for promising Sb bubble candidates is a fraction of 0.04 to 0.15 of the atom number.
- New magic numbers for bubble clusters are listed: 2, 8, 18, 32, 50, 72, 98, 128, 162, 200, 242, 288, 338, 392, 450, 512, 578, 648, etc.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear bubbles, previously studied by Myers and Swiatecki (1966) and by Dietrich and Pomorski (1997) for nuclear systems, to charged mesoscopic metallic clusters. It contradicts the assumption in all prior theoretical studies (e.g., Garcias et al., 1995; Gross and Hervieux, 1995) that the ground state of a charged metal cluster is always a compact spherical or deformed shape. The results enable experimental searches for bubble-shaped metal clusters, particularly in antimony, and suggest that epitaxial fabrication of layered metallic shells could exhibit similar shell effects.
