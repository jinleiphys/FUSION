# 0+ states and collective bands in 228Th studied by the (p,t) reaction

> The excitation spectra in the deformed nucleus 228Th have been studied by means of the (p,t)-reaction, using the Q3D spectrograph facility at the Munich Tandem accelerator. The angular distributions of tritons were measured for about 110 excitations seen in the triton spectra up to 2.5 MeV. Firm 0+ assignments are made for 17 excited states by comparison of experimental angular distributions with the calculated ones using the CHUCK3 code. Assignments up to spin 6+ are made for other states. Sequences of states are selected which can be treated as rotational bands and as multiplets of excitations. Moments of inertia have been derived from these sequences, whose values may be considered as evidence of the two-phonon nature of most 0+ excitations. Experimental data are compared with interacting boson model and quasiparticle-phonon model calculations and with experimental data for 229Pa.

## Key claim

The paper claims that 17 excited 0+ states in 228Th can be firmly assigned via the (p,t) reaction, and that the moments of inertia derived from rotational bands built on these states provide evidence for a two-phonon octupole nature of most 0+ excitations, though the authors hedge that this interpretation is suggested rather than proven definitively.

## Method

The 230Th(p,t)228Th reaction was measured at 25 MeV proton energy using the Q3D magnetic spectrograph at the Munich Tandem accelerator, with triton angular distributions recorded at 10 laboratory angles from 5° to 40° for excitations up to 1.8 MeV and at 5 angles from 7.5° to 30° for excitations from 1.8 to 2.5 MeV. Spin assignments were made by fitting experimental angular distributions with DWBA calculations using the CHUCK3 code, employing one-step direct transfer for most states and multi-step coupled-channel calculations for about 20% of states where one-step transfer was insufficient.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of 0+ excitations in actinide nuclei via the (p,t) reaction, following earlier studies on 230Th by Levon (2009) and preliminary results on 228Th, 230Th, and 232U by Wirth (2004). The paper provides a more complete analysis than the preliminary work of Wirth (2004), which concentrated only on energies and transfer strengths of excited 0+ states, by now including spin assignments for many other states and constructing rotational bands. The results enable comparison with interacting boson model (IBM) and quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) calculations, and with experimental data for the odd nucleus 229Pa studied by Levon (1994), where 228Th corresponds to 229Pa minus a proton. The paper also builds on the earlier suggestion by Dalmasso (1987) that the first excited 0+ band in 228Th might have an octupole two-phonon structure, and proposes a two-phonon octupole quadruplet with Kπ = 0+, 2+, 4+, 6+ states.
