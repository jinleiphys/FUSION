# Global Properties of fp-Shell Interactions in Many-nucleon Systems

> Spectral distribution theory, which can be used to compare microscopic interactions over a broad range of nuclei, is applied in an analysis of two modern effective interactions based on the realistic CD-Bonn potential for $0\hbarΩ$ no-core shell model calculations in the fp shell, as well as in a comparison of these with the realistic shell-model GXPF1 interaction. In particular, we explore the ability of these interaction to account for the development of isovector pairing correlations and collective rotational motion in the fp shell. Our findings expose the similarities of these two-body interactions, especially as this relates to their pairing and rotational characteristics. Further, the GXPF1 interaction is used to determine the strength parameter of a quadrupole term that can be used to augment an isovector-pairing model interaction with Sp(4) dynamical symmetry, which in turn is shown to yield reasonable agreement with the low-lying energy spectra of $^{58}$Ni and $^{58}$Cu.

# Global Properties of fp-Shell Interactions in Many-nucleon Systems

## Key claim

Spectral distribution theory reveals that the GXPF1 interaction shows well-developed collective rotational features and isovector pairing correlations in the upper fp-shell region, while the CD-Bonn and CD-Bonn+3terms interactions derived from the realistic CD-Bonn potential for 0ħΩ no-core shell model calculations are unlikely to fully reflect the rotational properties of these nuclei, despite the abstract's emphasis on similarities among the interactions.

## Method

Spectral distribution theory was applied to compute correlation coefficients between the pure two-body parts of three fp-shell interactions (CD-Bonn, CD-Bonn+3terms, and GXPF1) and a pairing-plus-quadrupole model Hamiltonian with Sp(4) dynamical symmetry for isovector pairing and SU(3) symmetry for collective rotation. The analysis was performed for both scalar (all states) and isospin-scalar (states of fixed isospin T) distributions across the upper fp-shell domain (1f5/2, 2p1/2, 2p3/2 orbits), with propagation of two-nucleon correlation measures to many-nucleon systems. The quadrupole strength parameter χ in the model Hamiltonian was varied to maximize correlation with each effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- Scalar distribution correlation coefficient between pure two-body GXPF1 and the pairing+quadrupole model HM: 0.83
- Scalar distribution correlation coefficient between pure two-body CD-Bonn and HM: 0.57
- Scalar distribution correlation coefficient between pure two-body CD-Bonn+3terms and HM: 0.54
- Scalar distribution correlation coefficient between pure two-body GXPF1 and the full quadrupole-quadrupole interaction HQ(2): 0.67
- Scalar distribution correlation coefficient between pure two-body CD-Bonn and HQ(2): 0.28
- Scalar distribution correlation coefficient between pure two-body CD-Bonn+3terms and HQ(2): 0.33
- RSp(4) value (percentage of HM that is Sp(4) symmetric) for GXPF1: 61.3%
- RSp(4) value for CD-Bonn: 93.1%
- RSp(4) value for CD-Bonn+3terms: 85.7%
- Scalar correlation between pure two-body CD-Bonn and CD-Bonn+3terms: 0.90
- Scalar correlation between pure two-body CD-Bonn and GXPF1: 0.56
- Scalar correlation between pure two-body CD-Bonn+3terms and GXPF1: 0.53
- Optimal quadrupole strength parameter χ used in model Hamiltonian for energy spectra: 0.027

## Context

This work extends the spectral distribution analysis previously performed by Sviratcheva, Draayer, and Vary (2006) for the 1f7/2 orbit to the full upper fp-shell domain, comparing the CD-Bonn-based interactions from Popescu et al. (2005) with the empirically adjusted GXPF1 interaction from Honma, Otsuka, Brown, and Mizusaki (2004). The findings enable the use of the simpler Sp(4)-symmetric pairing-plus-quadrupole model as an approximation to the GXPF1 interaction, demonstrated through reasonable reproduction of low-lying energy spectra for 58Ni and 58Cu without parameter adjustment. The results also suggest that larger no-core shell model spaces may be necessary for the CD-Bonn interactions to better reproduce pairing and rotational spectral features in the upper fp-shell region.
