# Ground state energy fluctuations in the Nuclear Shell Model

> Statistical fluctuations of the nuclear ground state energies are estimated using shell model calculations in which particles in the valence shells interact through well defined forces, and are coupled to an upper shell governed by random 2-body interactions. Induced ground-state energy fluctuations are found to be one order of magnitude smaller than those previously associated with chaotic components, in close agreement with independent perturbative estimates based on the spreading widths of excited states.

## Key claim
Ground-state energy fluctuations induced by random interactions in the upper major shell are approximately one order of magnitude smaller than those previously attributed to chaotic components in nuclear masses, with the specific condition that the random interactions are confined to the upper shell while lower-shell interactions remain realistic.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed for four representative nuclei (⁸Be, ²⁰Ne, ⁴⁴Ti, ⁸⁴Mo) with two protons and two neutrons outside closed shells, using a valence space of two major shells for protons and two for neutrons. Single-particle energies and interactions in the lower shells were fixed using realistic forces (Cohen-Kurath, Brown-Wildenthal, KB3, BonnC, KLS), while two-body interactions in the upper shell were drawn randomly from a Gaussian distribution whose width was determined from a statistical analysis of realistic interactions. Ground-state energy fluctuations from these calculations were then scaled to mid-shell nuclei using a factor derived from earlier single-shell random-matrix studies.

## Key numbers
- Interaction widths follow σ_int(A) = 12.54 A^{-0.72} MeV, fitted from realistic interactions in the p, sd, pf, gds, and hf shells.
- Ground-state energy fluctuations for 2p-2n systems: σ_{2p-2n}(A) = 2.92 A^{-1.34} MeV, from calculations on ⁸Be, ²⁰Ne, ⁴⁴Ti, and ⁸⁴Mo.
- Final estimated chaotic fluctuations for general nuclei: σ(A) ≈ 20 A^{-1.34} MeV, yielding approximately 40 keV for A=100.
- The A-dependence is close to A^{-4/3}, matching the perturbative result σ_pert = 19 A^{-4/3} MeV from Abe (2005).
- Average mass error in large-scale shell model calculations for N=126 Po-Pu isotones is smaller than 50 keV (Caurier 2003).
- Average mass error in FRDM is of order 0.7 MeV (Moller 1995), an order of magnitude larger than the present estimates.

## Context
This work extends the study of chaotic fluctuations in nuclear masses initiated by Bohigas (2002), who interpreted FRDM mass errors as evidence of quantum chaos, and by Velazquez (2003), who studied fluctuations with random interactions in single shells. The present results contradict the interpretation of FRDM errors as arising from neutron-resonance chaos, since the induced fluctuations are an order of magnitude smaller and follow a different A-dependence. The findings agree closely with the perturbative estimates of Abe (2005) and Molinari-Weidenmuller (2004), and with local mass errors from Garvey-Kelson relations (Barea 2004), suggesting that the large chaotic signal in FRDM errors is an artifact of the mean-field approximation rather than a physical phenomenon.
