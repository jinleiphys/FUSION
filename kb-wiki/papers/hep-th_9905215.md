# Effective Theory of QCD

> We derive a generalized Skyrme-Faddeev action as the effective action of QCD in the low energy limit. Our result demonstrates the existence of a mass gap in QCD which triggers the confinement of color.

# Effective Theory of QCD

## Key claim

The authors derive a generalized Skyrme-Faddeev action as the effective action of QCD in the low-energy (infra-red) limit, but only in a one-loop approximation and with the caveat that the resulting effective Lagrangian contains extra interactions not quadratic in time-derivative, unlike the standard Skyrme-Faddeev action. The existence of a mass gap is demonstrated through the appearance of a kinetic term for the topological field **n**, which the authors interpret as evidence for confinement via the dual Meissner effect.

## Method

The authors decompose the SU(2) gauge connection into an Abelian projection (containing the topological degrees via a unit vector field **n**) and a covariant vector field **X**_μ representing valence gluons. They then integrate out the six dynamical degrees of freedom (the transverse components of A_μ and **X**_μ) in two steps using functional integration in one-loop approximation with dimensional regularization, first integrating out **X**_μ to obtain an effective restricted QCD, then integrating out A_μ to obtain the final effective action expressed solely in terms of the topological field **n**.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop renormalization factor for the restricted QCD effective Lagrangian is z₁ = 1 − (22g²)/(3(4π)²) · (1/ε), where ε is the dimensional regularization parameter.
- The Faddeev-Popov determinant from the A_μ integration yields a term −(μ₀²)/(4π) · (1/ε) (∂_μ **n**)², introducing a mass scale μ₀.
- The final effective Lagrangian contains a kinetic term −(μ²)/2 (∂_μ **n**)², where μ is a renormalized coupling constant with dimensions of mass.
- The coefficient of the H_μν² term in the effective Lagrangian is (1 − (8g²)/((4π)²) · (1/ε)).

## Context

This work extends the conjecture by Faddeev and Niemi (late 1990s) that the Skyrme-Faddeev Lagrangian could be an effective Lagrangian for QCD in the low-energy limit, and provides a first-principles derivation that had been missing. It builds on earlier work by Cho (1990s) on the decomposition of non-Abelian gauge fields and the restricted gauge theory, as well as on the dual Meissner effect mechanism for confinement proposed by Nambu and Cho. The result enables explicit testing of whether knot solitons of the Faddeev-Niemi type could describe glueball states in QCD, and provides a framework for generalizing the analysis to arbitrary gauge groups such as SU(3).
