# Role of non-gaussian quantum fluctuations in neutrino entanglement

> The flavor evolution of neutrinos in environments with large neutrino number densities is an open problem at the nexus of astrophysics and neutrino flavor physics. Among the many unanswered questions pertaining to this problem, it remains to be determined whether neutrino-neutrino coherent scattering can give rise to nontrivial quantum entanglement among neutrinos, and whether this can affect the flavor evolution in a meaningful way. To gain further insight into this question, here we study a simple system of two interacting neutrino beams, and obtain the exact phase-space explored by this system using the Husimi quasi-probability distribution. We observe that the entanglement induced by the coupling leads to strong delocalization in phase-space with largely non-Gaussian quantum fluctuations. The link between the neutrino entanglement and quantum fluctuations is illustrated using the one- and two-neutrino entropy. In addition, we propose an approximate phase-space method to describe the interacting neutrinos problem, where the exact evolution is replaced by a set of independent mean-field evolutions with a statistical sampling of the initial conditions. The phase-space approach provides a simple and accurate method to describe the gross features of the neutrino entanglement problem. Applications are shown using time-independent and time-dependent Hamiltonians in the non-adiabatic regime.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutrino-neutrino coherent scattering in a two-beam system generates non-Gaussian quantum fluctuations in phase space that are directly linked to quantum entanglement between neutrinos, and that these fluctuations cannot be captured by mean-field theory. The authors propose that an approximate phase-space method using statistical sampling of initial conditions can reproduce the gross features of the exact entanglement dynamics, even in the non-adiabatic regime with time-dependent Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors study a system of two interacting neutrino beams (subsystems A and B) with N_A and N_B neutrinos respectively, described by a Hamiltonian with a coupling term proportional to the dot product of the total quasi-spin operators of the two beams. They obtain the exact time-dependent wavefunction by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in blocks of constant total magnetic quantum number M = m_A + m_B, using the method of Xiao et al. (2021) and Martin et al. (2021). The Husimi quasi-probability distribution for subsystem A is constructed by projecting the reduced density matrix onto SU(2) coherent states. The authors also implement an approximate phase-space method where the exact evolution is replaced by a set of independent mean-field evolutions with statistical sampling of initial conditions, following the approach of Ayik et al. (2008) and Lacroix et al. (2014).

## Key numbers

* The system uses N_A = N_B = 50 neutrinos in each beam for the bipolar symmetric case, with total particle number N = 100
* The bipolar mode uses coupling parameter Omega = 0.5, with initial angles theta_A = pi - 0.2, phi_A = pi, theta_B = 0.2, phi_B = 0
* The precession mode uses Omega = 1.2, with theta_A = 0.5978067, phi_A = 0, theta_B = 0.2175694, phi_B = 0
* The ratio of two-neutrino entropy to one-neutrino entropy is approximately xi_{2/1} = (ln 3)/(ln 2) ≈ 1.585, observed when both neutrinos are taken from the same beam
* The approximate phase-space method uses 10^5 events for the Monte Carlo sampling of initial conditions

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino many-body systems beyond the mean-field approximation, which has been the standard approach for collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae and neutron star mergers (Duan et al., 2006; Balantekin et al., 2007). The paper builds on exact solutions for small neutrino systems using Bethe ansatz techniques (Pehlivan et al., 2011; Birol et al., 2018) and tensor network approaches (Rrapaj et al., 2019; Roggero et al., 2021; Cervia et al., 2022), but introduces a phase-space perspective that can potentially scale to larger systems and time-dependent Hamiltonians. The Husimi distribution analysis and the proposed stochastic mean-field method enable future studies of neutrino entanglement in more realistic astrophysical environments where the Hamiltonian is time-dependent and the adiabatic approximation breaks down.
