# Coulomb Effects and Wigner-SU(4) Symmetry in He-3 Charge and Magnetic Properties

> This work studies the non-perturbative Coulomb corrections to the He-3 binding energy, magnetic moment, and charge and magnetic radii in leading-order (LO) Pionless Effective Field Theory (Pionless EFT). The splitting between He-3 and H-3 binding energy is found to be 0.85(3) MeV. The Coulomb corrections to the He-3 point charge radius and full magnetic radius are found to be 0.043(2) fm and 0.036(2) fm, respectively. These corrections are 4% of the LO predictions without Coulomb and should be taken into account at next-to-next-to-leading order or beyond in Pionless EFT to achieve the desired EFT accuracy. The Coulomb correction to the He-3 magnetic moment is found to be -0.0041(1)$μ_N$, only 0.2% of the LO prediction without Coulomb. The impact of Wigner-SU(4) symmetry in the presence of the non-perturbative Coulomb interaction is also discussed and used to help explain the hierarchy of Coulomb effects in He-3 observables.

## Key claim

The non-perturbative Coulomb corrections to He-3 static properties are found to be approximately 4% of the leading-order predictions for charge and magnetic radii, and only 0.2% for the magnetic moment, meaning these corrections must be included at next-to-next-to-leading order or beyond in Pionless EFT to achieve the desired accuracy, but the hierarchy of corrections can be understood through Wigner-SU(4) symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author solved the integral equation for the He-3 vertex function in leading-order Pionless EFT with the Coulomb interaction treated non-perturbatively, using a three-channel cluster configuration space formalism with spin-triplet, spin-singlet, and proton-proton dibaryon fields. The He-3 wavefunction was constructed from the vertex function, and form factors for charge, magnetic, and matter distributions were computed using partial-wave projected matrix elements of single-nucleon operators. The three-body force was fit to the triton binding energy of 8.48 MeV at each cutoff, and the He-3 binding energy was then obtained by solving the singularity condition for the dressed He-3 propagator.

## Key numbers

* He-3 and H-3 binding energy splitting: 0.85(3) MeV
* Coulomb correction to He-3 point charge radius: 0.043(2) fm
* Coulomb correction to He-3 full magnetic radius: 0.036(2) fm
* Coulomb correction to He-3 magnetic moment: -0.0041(1) nuclear magnetons
* Coulomb corrections to radii are approximately 4% of the leading-order predictions without Coulomb
* Coulomb correction to magnetic moment is approximately 0.2% of the leading-order prediction without Coulomb

## Context

This work extends prior Pionless EFT studies of He-3 static properties by Vanasse (2015, 2017) that omitted Coulomb effects even at next-to-next-to-leading order, and complements earlier treatments of Coulomb corrections to the He-3 binding energy by Ando (2010), Vanasse (2014), and Konig (2014, 2015, 2016). The results enable future EFT calculations of proton-deuteron radiative capture into He-3, an important reaction in nuclear astrophysics where recent experimental results by Stockel (2024) show tension with potential-model calculations.
