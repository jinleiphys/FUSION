# The quark-meson coupling model and chiral symmetry

# The quark-meson coupling model and chiral symmetry

## Key claim
The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model can be extended to incorporate chiral symmetry (the chiral QMC or CQMC model), and the resulting mean-central potential in nuclear matter is modified by the internal structure change of the nucleon, specifically through the scalar polarizability and the density-dependent reduction of the axial coupling constant g_A.

## Method
The author starts from the volume-coupling version of the cloudy bag model (CBM) with gluon fields, including one-gluon exchange (OGE) and pion-quark interactions. The Lagrangian is extended by adding sigma and omega meson fields in mean-field approximation to describe nuclear matter, and the effective nucleon mass is calculated as a function of the sigma mean field, yielding a scalar polarizability term. The resulting hadron-level Lagrangian is then expanded to show its equivalence to a chiral effective Lagrangian with higher-order many-body force terms.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV; pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV
- Free nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV, with bag radius R_N = 0.8 fm
- Delta mass M_Delta = 1232 MeV; Omega mass M_Omega = 1672 MeV
- u,d quark mass m_0 = 5 MeV
- N-Delta mass difference of about 300 MeV is reproduced mainly by OGE (about 240 MeV) with pion-cloud contribution about 60 MeV
- Normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Nuclear saturation condition: E_tot - M_N = -15.7 MeV at rho_0
- Sigma meson mass m_sigma = 550 MeV; omega meson mass m_omega = 783 MeV
- The quantity (C_s^2/f_pi^2) a (N-bar N) is about 0.3 at normal nuclear density
- The CQMC predicts g_A(rho_B)/g_A(0) ~ 1 - b (rho_B/rho_0) with b ~ 0.14

## Context
This work extends the original QMC model of Guichon by incorporating chiral symmetry through the cloudy bag model framework of Thomas and collaborators. It relates the CQMC model to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as developed by Weinberg and Machleidt, showing that the scalar mean-field from two-pion exchange can be connected to the QMC description. The reduction of the central force in matter due to nucleon internal structure is noted as potentially resolving the overbinding problem seen in N^3LO EFT calculations at high density.
