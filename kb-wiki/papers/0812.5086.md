# Cluster radioactivity in very heavy nuclei: a new perspective

> Exotic cluster decay of very heavy nuclei is studied using the microscopic nuclear potentials obtained by folding density dependent M3Y effective interaction with the densities of the cluster and the daughter nuclei. The microscopic nuclear potential, Coulomb interaction and the centrifugal barrier arising out of spin-parity conservation are used to obtain the potential between the cluster and the daughter nuclei. Half life values are calculated in the WKB framework and the preformation factors are extracted. The latter values are seen to have only a very weak dependence on the mass of the emitted cluster.

## Key claim

The paper argues that cluster preformation probabilities extracted from microscopic DDM3Y folding potentials show only a very weak dependence on the mass of the emitted cluster, contrary to previous expectations; however, the authors acknowledge that the theoretical half lives systematically underestimate experimental values by factors of 10⁻² to 10⁻³ when assuming unit preformation probability, and that a renormalisation factor of 0.7 for the nuclear potentials is needed to bring calculations into reasonable agreement with data spanning about fifteen orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors calculate microscopic nuclear potentials by double folding the density distributions of the emitted cluster and daughter nucleus with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The total interaction potential includes the nuclear potential, Coulomb interaction, and centrifugal barrier from spin-parity conservation; half lives are computed using the WKB framework for barrier penetration, and cluster preformation factors are extracted as the ratio of calculated to experimental half lives.

## Key numbers

- For α decay of ²¹²Po (Q = 8.954 MeV), the theoretical half life with preformation factor 1.00 gives log₁₀(T₁/₂) = -7.01, compared to experimental log₁₀(T₁/₂) = -6.52.
- For ²²²Ra → ¹⁴C (Q = 33.050 MeV), the theoretical half life assuming unit preformation gives log₁₀(T₁/₂) = 8.71, while the experimental value is log₁₀(T₁/₂) = 11.00, yielding -log₁₀(P₀) = 2.29.
- For ²³⁰U → ²²Ne (Q = 61.400 MeV), the theoretical half life with unit preformation gives log₁₀(T₁/₂) = 18.23, experimental log₁₀(T₁/₂) = 19.57, and -log₁₀(P₀) = 1.34.
- For ²³⁸Pu → ³²Si (Q = 91.190 MeV), theoretical log₁₀(T₁/₂) = 23.30, experimental log₁₀(T₁/₂) = 25.30, and -log₁₀(P₀) = 2.00.
- The extracted preformation factors for clusters with 6 ≤ Z_e ≤ 14 are approximately 10⁻² to 10⁻³.
- Fitting -log₁₀(P₀) for even-even systems yields c₁ = -0.38 × 10⁻³, c₂ = 2.56 for a linear fit in Z_d Z_e, and c₃ = 0.028, c₄ = 2.10 for a linear fit in A_e.

## Context

This work extends the use of density-dependent M3Y effective interactions, previously applied successfully to proton scattering (Gupta 2005, 2006), proton radioactivity (Basu 2005), and α radioactivity (Basu 2006, 2007), to cluster radioactivity in very heavy nuclei. It contrasts with the analytical superasymmetric fission model (ASAFM) of Poenaru (1986, 1991) and the preformed cluster model (PCM) of Malik and collaborators (1993), which used phenomenological potentials; the present microscopic approach avoids renormalisation of the nuclear potential for α decay but requires a 0.7 renormalisation factor for heavier clusters. The results challenge earlier findings by Zhang (2004) using renormalised M3Y without density dependence, and suggest that core polarisation effects from the emitted cluster may influence which excited states of the daughter nucleus are populated, offering a challenge to more microscopic models and experimentalists.
