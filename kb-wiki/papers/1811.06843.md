# Nuclear symmetry energy and hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars

> We study the hadron-quark mixed phase, which may occur in the interior of neutron stars. The relativistic mean-field model is employed to describe the hadronic phase, while the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model is used for the quark phase. We examine the effects of nuclear symmetry energy in the hadronic phase and repulsive vector interaction in the quark phase. For the treatment of hadron-quark mixed phase, we describe and compare four methods: (1) energy minimization method; (2) coexisting phases method; (3) Gibbs construction; and (4) Maxwell construction. The finite-size effects like surface and Coulomb energies are taken into account in the energy minimization and coexisting phases methods, which play a key role in determining the pasta configuration during the hadron-quark phase transition. It is found that massive neutron stars may contain hadron-quark pasta phases, but pure quark matter is unlikely to occur in the interior of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Massive neutron stars may contain hadron-quark pasta phases, but pure quark matter is unlikely to occur in their interiors; this conclusion depends on the treatment of finite-size effects (surface and Coulomb energies) and on the values of the surface tension and the vector coupling in the NJL model.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model (TM1 and IUFSU parametrizations) for hadronic matter and the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with repulsive vector interactions for quark matter. They compared four treatments of the hadron-quark mixed phase: energy minimization (EM) method, coexisting phases (CP) method, Gibbs construction, and Maxwell construction, with the EM and CP methods including finite-size effects via the Wigner-Seitz approximation.

## Key numbers

- The original TM1 model has symmetry energy slope L = 110.8 MeV and symmetry energy at saturation E_sym(n0) = 36.9 MeV; the original IUFSU model has L = 47.2 MeV and E_sym(n0) = 31.3 MeV.
- For the TM1 model, generated models cover L from 50 to 110.8 MeV, with E_sym(n0) ranging from 32.39 MeV (L=50 MeV) to 36.89 MeV (L=110.8 MeV).
- For the IUFSU model, generated models cover L from 47.2 to 110 MeV, with E_sym(n0) ranging from 31.30 MeV (L=47.2 MeV) to 37.27 MeV (L=110 MeV).
- Using the TM1 model for the hadronic phase, a pure quark phase does not appear at densities below 20 times nuclear saturation density for G_V/G_S > 0.45.
- The surface tension at the hadron-quark interface is treated as a free parameter; literature values range from approximately 2 to 20 MeV/fm^2 (MRE method) up to 145-165 MeV/fm^2 (NJL model with color superconductivity).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadron-quark phase transitions in neutron stars (Glen92, Sche99, Sche00, Latt00, Burg02, Mene03, Shar07, Yang08, Xu10, Chen13, Orsa14) by systematically comparing four treatment methods and examining the role of nuclear symmetry energy slope L. The authors use generated RMF models based on Bao14b to vary L while keeping other saturation properties fixed, enabling a focused study of symmetry energy effects on pasta structures. The results enable future work to constrain the surface tension and vector coupling by comparing predicted pasta-phase properties with neutron-star observations.
