# Entanglement Enabled Intensity Interferometry in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral nuclear collisions

> An important tool in studying the sub-femtoscale spacetime structure of matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry of identical particles in the final state of such collisions. We show here that a variant of an entanglement enabled intensity interferometry ($E^2 I^2$) proposed by Cotler and Wilczek provides a powerful alternative to HBT interferometry in extracting fundamental nonperturbative features of QCD at high energies. In particular, we show that the spatial distributions of color singlet (pomeron) configurations in nuclei can be obtained from exclusive resonant decays of $ρ$-mesons into $π^\pm$-pairs in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral nuclear collisions (UPCs) at RHIC and the LHC. The $E^2 I^2$ framework developed here is quite general. It can be employed to extract information on the spin structure of pomeron couplings as well as enhance the discovery potential for rare odderon configurations from exclusive vector meson decays into few-particle final states both in UPCs and at the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that entanglement enabled intensity interferometry (E²I²), a variant of the Cotler-Wilczek framework, can extract model-independent spatial distributions of color singlet (pomeron) configurations in nuclei from exclusive resonant decays of ρ-mesons into π±-pairs in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral nuclear collisions (UPCs), though a preliminary analysis suggests that careful treatment of the interplay between E²I² in the vector meson exclusive decay and the incoherent cross-section for exclusive vector meson production will be required for a model-independent extraction.

## Method

The authors compute the exclusive production of π±-pairs from ρ-meson decay in UPCs using a formalism that treats the ρ-meson decay as an entangled state of π+π−, incorporating the phase information from the entanglement of vector meson wavefunctions with their daughter particles. They express the production amplitude as a product of the γ + pomeron → ρ amplitude and the ρ → π+π− decay amplitude, employing the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory to describe the pomeron as a color singlet configuration of gluons localized in patches of size ∼ 1/ΛQCD within the nucleus, and then compute the average number of correlated pairs for both coherent and total exclusive cross-sections.

## Key numbers

* The observed cos(2φ) modulation in exclusive ρ0-meson decays into π± pairs peaks with a value of ∼40% at |q⊥| ≈ 20 MeV/c, as measured by the STAR collaboration.
* The cos(2φ) modulation shows a wave interference structure exhibiting a minimum and second maximum around |q⊥| ≈ 120 MeV/c.
* The cos(4φ) modulation is observed for |q⊥| ≈ 120 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement enabled intensity interferometry (E²I²) framework originally proposed by Cotler and Wilczek (2015) and developed further by Cotler, Wilczek and Borish (2021), applying it to ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions as an alternative to traditional Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry. The formalism builds on prior models that explain the cos(2φ) modulation through linear polarization of Weizsäcker-Williams photons and interference between amplitudes from the two nuclei, as studied by Klein and Nystrand (1999) and later by Goncalves (2017), Bendova (2020), and others, but aims to provide a more complete description that persists at larger transverse momenta corresponding to strong interaction scales. The framework can be applied to extract information on the spin structure of pomeron couplings and enhance the discovery potential for rare odderon configurations at both UPC experiments and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
