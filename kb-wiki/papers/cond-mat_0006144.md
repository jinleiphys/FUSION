# Spectral Statistics of the Two-Body Random Ensemble Revisited

> Using longer spectra we re-analyze spectral properties of the two-body random ensemble studied thirty years ago. At the center of the spectra the old results are largely confirmed, and we show that the non-ergodicity is essentially due to the variance of the lowest moments of the spectra. The longer spectra allow to test and reach the limits of validity of French's correction for the number variance. At the edge of the spectra we discuss the problems of unfolding in more detail. With a Gaussian unfolding of each spectrum the nearest neighbour spacing distribution between ground state and first exited state is shown to be stable. Using such an unfolding the distribution tends toward a semi-Poisson distribution for longer spectra. For comparison with the nuclear table ensemble we could use such unfolding obtaining similar results as in the early papers, but an ensemble with realistic splitting gives reasonable results if we just normalize the spacings in accordance with the procedure used for the data.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the non-ergodicity of the two-body random ensemble (TBRE) is essentially due to the variance of the lowest moments (centers and widths) of the spectra, and that re-centering and normalizing the widths of each spectrum individually removes this non-ergodicity at the center of the spectrum. At the edge of the spectrum, the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution between ground state and first excited state tends toward a semi-Poisson distribution when a Gaussian unfolding is applied to each spectrum individually.

## Method

The authors constructed TBRE Hamiltonians for eight spin-1/2 particles in the 2s-1d nuclear shell, using Gaussian-distributed two-body interaction matrices with degenerate single-particle states. They generated ensembles of matrices for three sets of quantum numbers (J=0,T=0; J=0,T=2; J=2,T=0) with dimensions ranging from 287 to 1206 and ensemble sizes of 500 or 708 matrices. For the realistic two-body random ensemble (RTBRE), they lifted the degeneracy condition by adding realistic single-particle energies. Spectral fluctuations were analyzed using the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(s) and the number variance Σ²(L), with unfolding performed either by a polynomial fit to the ensemble-averaged level density or by individual polynomial or Gaussian unfolding of each spectrum.

- For the TBRE (J=0,T=0): standard deviation of the center distribution σ_c = 20.1, of the width distribution σ_v = 4.5, and of the total level density σ_tot = 39.7; ratios σ_c/σ_tot = 0.51 and σ_v/σ_tot = 0.11
- For the TBRE (J=0,T=2): σ_c = 22.1, σ_v = 4.3, σ_tot = 37.1; ratios σ_c/σ_tot = 0.59 and σ_v/σ_tot = 0.12
- For the TBRE (J=2,T=0): σ_c = 20.3, σ_v = 4.2, σ_tot = 38.2; ratios σ_c/σ_tot = 0.53 and σ_v/σ_tot = 0.11
- For the RTBRE (J=0,T=0): σ_c = 5.0, σ_v = 1.96, σ_tot = 12.3; ratios σ_c/σ_tot = 0.41 and σ_v/σ_tot = 0.16
- For the GOE: σ_c = 0.015, σ_v = 3.17×10⁻³, σ_tot = 1.0; ratios σ_c/σ_tot = 1.5×10⁻² and σ_v/σ_tot = 3.1×10⁻³
- Spacings between ground and first excited states for TBRE (J=0,T=0) were observed up to 8 times the average spacing; for (J=2,T=0) up to 6 times the average
- For the central region analysis, 115 states were used on average per spectrum with a polynomial of degree 15 for ensemble unfolding; for individual unfolding, 85 states were discarded at each edge

## Context

This work revisits and extends the numerical studies of the TBRE originally performed by French (1970), Bohigas and Flores (1971), and others in the early 1970s, which were limited by small matrix dimensions due to limited computer facilities. The paper confirms the earlier findings that the TBRE is non-ergodic and that GOE statistics are recovered after individual unfolding, but shows that French's correction formula (1973) for the number variance works well only for short-range correlations (up to L≈5) and fails at larger distances. The results enable future investigation of non-ergodicity effects on wave function properties and suggest that the semi-Poisson distribution, previously discussed by Shklovskii (1993) in other contexts, may be relevant for edge-of-spectrum statistics in the TBRE.
