# Pion-Nucleon Physics and the Polarizabilities of the Nucleon

> I discuss recent results regarding the influence of pion-nucleon and and nucleon-resonance physics on the (spin-) polarizabilities of the nucleon as measured in (polarized) Compton scattering.

# Pion-Nucleon Physics and the Polarizabilities of the Nucleon

## Key claim
The six polarizabilities of the nucleon, calculated to O(epsilon^3) in the "small scale expansion" of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with explicit pion, nucleon, and delta degrees of freedom, modify the simple picture of polarizabilities as just a pion-nucleon loop effect; however, the O(epsilon^3) results for the spin-independent polarizabilities do not reproduce experimental values, and O(epsilon^4) calculations are needed to judge convergence of the perturbation series.

## Method
The author performs a low-energy expansion of the six structure-dependent functions A_i(omega, theta) for Compton scattering off the proton, extracting the electric polarizability alpha_E, magnetic polarizability beta_M, and four spin-polarizabilities gamma_1 through gamma_4. The calculation uses heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) at O(p^3) and O(p^4), and a newer "small scale expansion" formalism at O(epsilon^3) that includes explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom via pion-nucleon loops, Delta-pole graphs, pion-Delta loops, and neutral pion exchange through the anomalous pi^0 gamma gamma vertex.

## Key numbers
- Electric polarizability of the proton from recent fits: alpha_E^(p) = (12.1 +/- 0.8 +/- 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton from recent fits: beta_M^(p) = (2.1 -/+ 0.8 -/+ 0.5) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- HBChPT O(p^4) prediction: alpha_E^(p) = (10.5 +/- 2.0) x 10^(-4) fm^3, beta_M^(p) = (3.5 +/- 3.6) x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Small scale expansion O(epsilon^3) result for alpha_E^(p): [12.2 (Npi-loop) + 0 (Delta-pole) + 4.2 (Deltapi-loop) + 0 (anom.)] x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Small scale expansion O(epsilon^3) result for beta_M^(p): [1.2 (Npi-loop) + 7.2 (Delta-pole) + 0.7 (Deltapi-loop) + 0 (anom.)] x 10^(-4) fm^3
- Experimental combination from unpolarized data: gamma_0^(p) = gamma_1 - gamma_2 - 2 gamma_4 ≈ -1.34 x 10^(-4) fm^4
- Experimental combination from unpolarized data: gamma_pi^(p) = gamma_1 + gamma_2 + 2 gamma_4 ≈ -(28.0 +/- 2.8 +/- 2.5) x 10^(-4) fm^4
- Small scale expansion O(epsilon^3) prediction: gamma_0^(p) = +2.0 x 10^(-4) fm^4, gamma_pi^(p) = -37.2 x 10^(-4) fm^4

## Context
This work extends earlier HBChPT calculations of nucleon polarizabilities by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1990s) at O(p^3) and O(p^4), which treated the nucleon's structure as dominated by its "pion cloud" in contrast to dispersion relation analyses by L'vov (1990s). The inclusion of explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom via the "small scale expansion" formalism addresses the need to systematically incorporate the first nucleon resonance, which in HBChPT could only be included via counterterms. The results call for future O(epsilon^4) calculations to resolve discrepancies with experiment, particularly for beta_M and alpha_E, and motivate planned double-polarization Compton scattering experiments at Mainz to determine the poorly known spin-polarizabilities.
