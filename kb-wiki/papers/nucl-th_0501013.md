# Nuclear Pairing: Surface or Bulk ?

> We analyse how the spatial localisation properties of pairing correlations are changing in a major neutron shell of heavy nuclei. It is shown that the radial distribution of the pairing density depends strongly on whether the chemical potential is close to a low or a high angular momentum level and has very little sensitivity to whether the pairing force acts in the surface or in the bulk. The averaged pairing density over one major shell is however rather flat, practically independent of the pairing force. Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for the isotopic chain $^{100-132}$Sn are presented for demonstration purposes.

## Key claim

The radial distribution of pairing correlations (pairing density) in a major neutron shell depends strongly on whether the chemical potential is near a low or high angular momentum level, and has very little sensitivity to whether the pairing force acts in the surface or in the bulk; the averaged pairing density over one major shell is rather flat and practically independent of the pairing force.

## Method

Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations were performed for the isotopic chain 100-132Sn under spherical symmetry, using a Skyrme-type force (SLy4) in the particle-hole channel and two zero-range pairing forces in the particle-particle channel: a pure delta-force and a density-dependent delta (DDD) force. The DDD force parameters (V0 = -430 MeV fm-3, eta = 0.45, alpha = 0.47) were fitted to reproduce the pairing gap from the Gogny D1S force in nuclear matter, with a 60 MeV cut-off in the quasiparticle spectrum; the pure delta-force used V0 = -220 MeV fm-3 with the same cut-off.

## Key numbers

* Averaged pairing gap <Delta> for Sn isotopes with DDD-force: 1.4 MeV (A=104), 1.77 MeV (A=108), 1.91 MeV (A=114), 1.88 MeV (A=116), 1.6 MeV (A=124), 1.3 MeV (A=128).
* Chemical potential lambda for Sn isotopes with DDD-force: -10.9 MeV (A=104), -10.0 MeV (A=108), -8.97 MeV (A=114), -8.64 MeV (A=116), -7.3 MeV (A=124), -6.7 MeV (A=128).
* Occupation probabilities for the 3s1/2 state in Sn isotopes with DDD-force: 0.097 (A=104), 0.16 (A=108), 0.43 (A=114), 0.54 (A=116), 0.89 (A=124), 0.95 (A=128).
* Occupation probabilities for the 1h11/2 state in Sn isotopes with DDD-force: 0.03 (A=104), 0.05 (A=108), 0.11 (A=114), 0.14 (A=116), 0.44 (A=124), 0.7 (A=128).
* DDD-force parameters: V0 = -430 MeV fm-3, eta = 0.45, alpha = 0.47; pure delta-force strength: V0 = -220 MeV fm-3; cut-off energy: 60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing density in finite nuclei by Dobaczewski (year not given) and in half-infinite nuclear matter by Farine and Schuck (year not given), which showed only moderate surface peaking. The results contradict the common belief from Local Density Approximation (LDA) arguments, as used by Garrido (year not given) and others, that pairing is strongly surface-peaked; instead, the pairing density profile is dominated by the single-particle structure of the valence shell. The findings enable experimental tests via pair transfer reactions and clarify that quantities like odd-even mass differences probe the density dependence of the pairing force rather than Cooper pair localisation.
