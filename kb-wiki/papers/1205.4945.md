# Open heavy flavor in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV within a transport model

> The space-time evolution of open heavy flavor is studied in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV using the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS). An updated version of BAMPS is presented which allows interactions among all partons: gluons, light quarks, and heavy quarks. Heavy quarks, in particular, interact with the rest of the medium via binary scatterings with a running coupling and a Debye screening which is matched by comparing to hard thermal loop calculations. The lack of radiative processes in the heavy flavor sector is accounted for by scaling the binary cross section with a phenomenological factor K=3.5, which describes well the elliptic flow v_2 and nuclear modification factor R_AA at RHIC. Within this framework we calculate in a comprehensive study the v_2 and R_AA of all interesting open heavy flavor particles at the LHC: electrons, muons, D mesons, and non-prompt J/psi from B mesons. We compare to experimental data, where it is already available, or make predictions. To do this accurately next-to-leading order initial heavy quark distributions are employed which agree well with proton-proton data of heavy flavor at sqrt(s)=7 TeV.

## Key claim
The paper claims that elastic heavy quark scatterings alone, with binary cross sections scaled by a phenomenological factor K=3.5 to account for missing radiative processes, can simultaneously describe the elliptic flow v2 and nuclear modification factor RAA of open heavy flavor at RHIC, and that applying the same prescription at LHC energy yields calculations that are consistent with or slightly underestimate the experimental data.

## Method
The authors used the 3+1 dimensional partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings) to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV. Heavy quarks interact with the medium via binary scatterings with a running coupling and Debye screening matched to hard thermal loop calculations, with the binary cross section scaled by K=3.5. Initial heavy quark distributions were generated using the next-to-leading order event generator MC@NLO, and heavy quarks were fragmented to D or B mesons using the Peterson fragmentation function.

## Key numbers
- K=3.5: the phenomenological factor scaling the binary cross section for heavy quarks, determined from fitting RHIC heavy flavor electron data
- Debye mass prefactor kappa = 0.184 (approximately 0.2) from comparison of HTL energy loss to leading order pQCD cross section
- Charm quark mass Mc = 1.3 GeV, bottom quark mass Mb = 4.6 GeV
- Fragmentation parameter epsilon_Q = 0.05 for charm quarks, 0.005 for bottom quarks
- Energy density threshold for fragmentation: 0.6 GeV/fm^3
- Lambda = 200 MeV for the running coupling
- Impact parameter b = 9.7 fm for D meson v2 calculations at LHC
- Impact parameter b = 3.6 fm for electron RAA calculations at LHC

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous BAMPS calculations (Uphoff 2011) that included only gluons and heavy quarks (n_f=0+2) to now include light quarks (n_f=3+2). It builds on earlier heavy flavor transport studies at RHIC and provides the first comprehensive BAMPS predictions for open heavy flavor at LHC energies. The results enable comparison with ALICE D meson data, CMS non-prompt J/psi data, and heavy flavor lepton measurements, while motivating future inclusion of explicit radiative processes for heavy quarks to replace the phenomenological K factor.
