# Axially Deformed Proton-Neutron Relativistic Quasiparticle Finite Amplitude Method for Charge-Exchange Transitions

> The quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) is extended to describe charge-exchange transitions based on the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model, adopting the point-coupling energy density functional DD-PC1 and a finite-range separable pairing force. After validation through comparison with relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) results in spherical nuclei, the deformation effects on isobaric analog resonances (IAR) and Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions in Zn isotopes are investigated. The GT strength exhibits significant fragmentation in deformed nuclei. The analysis of summed strengths and centroid energies in GT resonance region between the $K=0$ and $K=1$ components reveals that prolate configurations exhibit stronger $K=1$ strength and lower $K=1$ centroid energy, while oblate shapes show an opposite behavior, with stronger $K=0$ strength and lower $K=0$ energy. The effects of isoscalar pairing on GT strength distributions for different shape configurations are also examined.

# Axially Deformed Proton-Neutron Relativistic Quasiparticle Finite Amplitude Method for Charge-Exchange Transitions

## Key claim

The relativistic quasiparticle finite amplitude method (QFAM) is extended for the first time to charge-exchange transitions in axially deformed nuclei, enabling efficient calculation of Fermi and Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions without matrix diagonalization. The method reveals systematic trends in the K-splitting of GT resonances for prolate versus oblate configurations, with the caveat that quasiparticle-vibration coupling effects are not included and a fixed smearing width of 0.25 MeV is used to resolve individual peaks.

## Method

The authors formulate the proton-neutron QFAM (pnQFAM) within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model using the DD-PC1 point-coupling energy density functional for the particle-hole channel and a finite-range separable pairing force for the particle-particle channel, including both isovector and isoscalar pairing. The linear response equations are solved iteratively in the axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, avoiding explicit construction and diagonalization of the QRPA matrix. The method is validated by comparison with relativistic QRPA results in spherical nuclei, then applied to study deformation effects on isobaric analog resonances and GT transitions in Zn isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The isovector-pseudovector coupling constant is set to α_TPV = -0.734 fm², adopted from Vale (2021).
- A fixed imaginary smearing width of γ = 0.25 MeV is used to enhance resolution of individual peaks in strength distributions.
- The Ikeda sum rule (N-Z) is used as the normalization scale for comparing GT strength differences between different harmonic oscillator basis truncations.
- Basis truncations of N_HO = 16, 18, and 20 are tested against a reference case of N_HO_max = 22 for convergence in ⁷⁴Zn.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic QFAM framework, previously applied to non-charge-exchange transitions by Niksic (2013), Sun (2017), and Bjelcic (2020), to charge-exchange transitions for the first time within relativistic density functional theory. It parallels the non-relativistic Skyrme-based pnQFAM developments by Mustonen (2014) and Shafer (2016), while incorporating the advantages of relativistic DFT including natural spin-orbit coupling and Lorentz invariance. The systematic investigation of K-splitting in GT resonances for prolate versus oblate nuclei builds on prior studies of isovector dipole giant resonance systematics by Danos (1958), Xu (2021), and Martini (2016), but addresses the previously unexplored case of GT transitions. This work enables future incorporation of quasiparticle-vibration coupling effects in deformed charge-exchange calculations within the relativistic framework.
