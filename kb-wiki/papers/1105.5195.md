# Systematic description of 6Li(n, n')6Li* $\to$ d + $α$ reactions with the microscopic coupled-channels method

> We investigate $^6$Li($n$, $n'$)$^6$Li$^*$ $\to$ $d$ + $α$ reactions by using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with the complex Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. In this study, the $^6$Li nucleus is described as a $d$ + $α$ cluster model. The calculated elastic cross sections for incident energies between 7.47 and 24.0 MeV are good agreement with experimental data. Furthermore, we show the neutron spectra to $^6$Li breakup states measured at selected angular points and incident energies can be also reproduced systematically.

## Key claim

The continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method with the complex Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction can systematically reproduce elastic cross sections, inelastic cross sections to the 3+ resonance state, and neutron breakup spectra for the 6Li(n, n')6Li* -> d + alpha reaction at low incident neutron energies, but only after optimizing a single normalization parameter for the imaginary part of the interaction (lambda_w = 0.1). The method underestimates experimental data in the low neutron energy region of the breakup spectra, which the authors attribute to the missing four-body breakup channel 6Li(n, nnp)alpha.

## Method

The authors describe the n + 6Li system as a three-body n + d + alpha model and solve the Schrodinger equation using the CDCC method, where the 6Li nucleus is treated as a d + alpha cluster. The internal wave functions of 6Li are obtained by diagonalizing the d-alpha Hamiltonian using complex-range Gaussian basis functions for relative angular momenta l = 0 and l = 2 (S-wave and D-wave states), and the diagonal and coupling potentials between n and 6Li are calculated via a single folding model using the complex JLM effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The normalization factor for the imaginary part of the JLM interaction, lambda_w, is optimized to reproduce experimental elastic cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of 6Li relative to the d-alpha breakup threshold is 1.47 MeV.
- Elastic cross sections are calculated for incident neutron energies between 7.47 and 24.0 MeV and show good agreement with experimental data.
- The normalization parameter for the imaginary part of the JLM interaction is set to lambda_w = 0.1 for all incident energies between 7.47 and 24.0 MeV.
- Inelastic cross sections to the 3+ resonance state (0.71 MeV above the d-alpha threshold) are calculated for incident neutron energies of 11.5, 14.1, and 18.0 MeV.
- For the total cross section at 150 MeV, the required normalization factor is lambda_w = 1.5.
- The JLM interaction is applicable up to 160 MeV, the maximum energy suggested in the original paper.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, previously applied by Sakuragi et al. to 6Li scattering on various targets, to the specific case of neutron-induced breakup of 6Li into d and alpha, where experimental data for continuum breakup processes above 20 MeV are extremely rare (Chiba, Hogue, Hansen). The authors note that the statistical model used for medium-to-heavy nuclei cannot be applied to 6Li(n, n') reactions, and that mechanisms leading to three- or four-body final states (n + d + alpha and n + n + p + alpha) are important (Chiba, Fukahori). The present work enables future four-body CDCC calculations for higher incident energies and suggests analyzing p + 6Li elastic scattering to resolve the normalization problem at higher energies.
