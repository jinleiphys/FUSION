# Magnetized strange quark matter and magnetized strange quark stars

> Strange quark matter could be found in the core of neutron stars or forming strange quark stars. As is well known, these astrophysical objects are endowed with strong magnetic fields which affect the microscopic properties of matter and modify the macroscopic properties of the system. In this paper we study the role of a strong magnetic field in the thermodynamical properties of a magnetized degenerate strange quark gas, taking into account beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. Quarks and electrons interact with the magnetic field via their electric charges and anomalous magnetic moments. In contrast to the magnetic field value of 10^19 G, obtained when anomalous magnetic moments are not taken into account, we find the upper bound B < 8.6 x 10^17 G, for the stability of the system. A phase transition could be hidden for fields greater than this value.

## Key claim

The paper finds that when the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of quarks and electrons are included in the thermodynamic description of strange quark matter (SQM) under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, the maximum stable magnetic field is bounded by \(B \lesssim 8.6 \times 10^{17}\) G, rather than the \(10^{19}\) G limit obtained when AMM are neglected. Above this bound, the thermodynamic quantities become complex and a phase transition is expected.

## Method

The authors model SQM as a degenerate, non-interacting Fermi gas of up, down, and strange quarks plus electrons, using the MIT bag model with a constant uniform magnetic field. The Dirac-Pauli equation is solved for each particle to obtain energy spectra that include both Landau quantization (from electric charge) and the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) contribution. The system is constrained by beta-equilibrium (\(\mu_u + \mu_e = \mu_d = \mu_s\)), charge neutrality, and fixed baryon density \(n_B = 0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\); the chemical potentials and all thermodynamic quantities (density, magnetization, susceptibility, pressures, energy per baryon) are computed numerically as functions of magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on magnetic field for stable SQM: \(B \lesssim 8.6 \times 10^{17}\) G, set by the \(u\)-quark saturation field \(B_u^s\).
- Individual particle saturation fields: \(B_e^s = 7.6 \times 10^{16}\) G, \(B_u^s = 8.6 \times 10^{17}\) G, \(B_d^s = 1.6 \times 10^{18}\) G, \(B_s^s = 8.2 \times 10^{19}\) G.
- Critical fields (cyclotron energy equals rest mass): \(B_e^c = 4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G, \(B_u^c = 6.3 \times 10^{16}\) G, \(B_d^c = 1.3 \times 10^{16}\) G, \(B_s^c = 1.1 \times 10^{19}\) G.
- Quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 5\) MeV, \(m_s = 150\) MeV.
- Baryon density fixed at \(n_B = 2.5\, n_0 = 0.4\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Without AMM, total spin polarization is reached at \(B \simeq 2 \times 10^{19}\) G; with AMM, total polarization is not reached before the bound.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetized quark matter by Chakrabarty (1996) and Perez Martinez et al. (2005, 2007) by including the anomalous magnetic moments of all SQM constituents in a fully relativistic treatment, rather than treating Pauli paramagnetism classically. The results contradict the earlier classical bound of \(10^{19}\) G and show that the AMM introduces a stricter upper limit on the magnetic field, which has direct implications for the existence and properties of strange quark stars. The finding enables future work on whether a ferromagnetic phase transition occurs at the saturation field, and on the role of AMM in quark droplet nucleation and Bose-Einstein condensation of di-quarks.
