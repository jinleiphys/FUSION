# Origin of fine structure of the giant dipole resonance in sd-shell nuclei

> A set of high resolution zero-degree inelastic proton scattering data on 24Mg, 28Si, 32S, and 40Ca provides new insight into the long-standing puzzle of the origin of fragmentation of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) in sd-shell nuclei. Understanding is provided by state-of-the-art theoretical Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculatios for deformed nuclei using for the first time a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential with the unitary correlation operator method and supplemented by a phenomenological three-nucleon contact interaction. A wavelet analysis allows to extract significant scales both in the data and calculations characterizing the fine structure of the GDR. The fair agreement supports that the fine structure arises from ground-state deformation driven by alpha clustering.

## Key claim

The fine structure of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) in sd-shell nuclei at scales of a few hundred keV arises primarily from ground-state deformation driven by alpha clustering, rather than from coupling to surface vibrations as in heavier nuclei. This conclusion is supported by fair agreement between experimental data and deformed-basis RPA calculations that include deformation but do not yet incorporate coupling to the continuum or to more complex configurations.

## Method

High-resolution zero-degree inelastic proton scattering data were taken at RCNP, Osaka using 295 MeV proton beams with energy resolution of 20-30 keV FWHM, covering excitation energies from roughly 5-25 MeV. Theoretical B(E1) strength distributions were calculated using Random Phase Approximation (RPA) on axially deformed Hartree-Fock ground states, employing a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential via the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) supplemented by a phenomenological three-nucleon contact interaction. A wavelet analysis using the complex Morlet wavelet was applied to both experimental spectra and theoretical strength distributions to extract characteristic energy scales of the fine structure.

## Key numbers

- Experimental energy resolution: 20-30 keV FWHM for the (p,p') reaction at 295 MeV and mean scattering angle 0.4 degrees
- In 28Si, a Class I scale at approximately 80 keV is observed in all experimental spectra, attributed to Ericson fluctuations
- Class II scales in 28Si from (p,p') data: 0.23, 0.36, and 0.59 MeV
- Class III scales in 28Si from (p,p') data: 1.0 and 2.9 MeV
- The RPA calculation for 28Si produces Class II scales at 0.23 and 0.44 MeV, and a Class III scale at 2.1 MeV
- The QRPA calculation for 28Si produces Class II scales at 0.42 and 0.77 MeV, and a Class III scale at 1.6 MeV
- Nuclear background contribution in 40Ca: about 2 mb/(sr MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of GDR fine structure in lighter nuclei (Usman et al., 2011; Usman et al., 2016) and contrasts with the mechanism identified for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) in heavier nuclei, where coupling of 1p1h states to low-lying surface vibrations is the driver (Shevchenko et al., 2004; Shevchenko et al., 2009). The results enable future investigation of whether triaxial deformations, as indicated by cluster models (Kimura, 2012) and density functional approaches (Yao et al., 2010) for 24Mg, can explain remaining discrepancies between theory and experiment.
