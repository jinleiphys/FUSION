# Nuclear equation of state at finite $μ_B$ using deep learning assisted quasi-parton model

> To accurately determine the nuclear equation of state (EoS) at finite baryon chemical potential ($μ_B$) remains a challenging yet essential goal in the study of QCD matter under extreme conditions. In this study, we develop a deep learning assisted quasi-parton model, which utilizes three deep neural networks, to reconstruct the QCD EoS at zero $μ_B$ and predict the EoS and transport coefficient $η/s$ at finite $μ_B$. The EoS derived from our quasi-parton model shows excellent agreement with lattice QCD results obtained using Taylor expansion techniques. The minimum value of $η/s$ is found to be approximately 175 MeV and decreases with increasing chemical potential within the confidence interval. This model not only provides a robust framework for understanding the properties of the QCD EoS at finite $μ_B$ but also offers critical input for relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of nuclear matter produced in heavy-ion collisions by the RHIC beam energy scan program.

## Key claim
The authors develop a deep learning assisted quasi-parton model that uses three neural networks to reconstruct the QCD equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential and predict it at finite baryon chemical potential, with the hedge that predictions beyond the range of 0 ≤ μ_B/T ≤ 3.5 require additional physical constraints.

## Method
The authors model strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma as a system of non-interacting quasi-partons whose masses depend on temperature and baryon chemical potential. Three residual neural networks (each with 8 hidden layers of 32 neurons) learn the mass functions for u/d quarks, s quarks, and gluons by minimizing a loss function that includes entropy density, trace anomaly, generalized susceptibilities χ₂^B and χ₄^B, and high-temperature hard thermal loop mass constraints. The partition function is computed using ideal gas statistical formulae with numerical momentum integration via 50-point Gaussian quadrature, and thermodynamic quantities are derived through automatic differentiation.

## Key numbers
- The minimum value of η/s is approximately 175 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential.
- The mass constraint uses a high-temperature cutoff T_cut = 0.150 GeV.
- Current quark masses used in constraints: m̄_s ≈ 95 MeV and m̄_{u/d} ≈ 5 MeV.
- The learning rate used in training is α ∼ 10⁻⁵.
- The neural network architecture uses 8 hidden layers with 32 neurons each.
- Predictions are validated within the range 0 ≤ μ_B/T ≤ 3.5.

## Context
This work extends prior quasi-parton model approaches (Liu 2021, Soloveva 2021, Soloveva 2023) that relied on parameterized mass functions, which the authors note can introduce biases. The model reproduces lattice QCD results from the HotQCD collaboration (2014, 2018) and Bazavov (2017) at zero chemical potential, and uses Taylor expansion techniques from Bollweg (2022) and Borsanyi (2021) for comparison at finite chemical potential. The results provide equation of state inputs for relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions in the RHIC beam energy scan program, and the authors propose extending the approach to include finite charge and strangeness chemical potentials and the QCD critical point.
