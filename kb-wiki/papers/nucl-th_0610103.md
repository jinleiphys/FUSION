# Particle number conserving approach to correlations

> In the present work the so-called Higher Tamm-Dancoff Apporximation method is presented for the generalized case of isovector and isoscalar residual interactions treated simultaneously. The role of different particle-hole excitations and of proton-neutron pairing correlations in the ground state of the self-conjugate 64Ge nucleus is discussed.

## Key claim

The Higher Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (HTDA) method, which explicitly conserves particle number, can be extended to simultaneously treat isovector and isoscalar residual interactions, and it yields a much smoother decrease of correlations than Bogoliubov-type approaches in weak pairing regimes, avoiding the sharp transition between like-particle and T=0 pairing modes seen in BCS calculations for self-conjugate nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a many-body basis of particle-hole excitations (0p0h, 1p1h, 2p2h, etc.) over a Hartree-Fock vacuum obtained from a Skyrme force (SIII) and diagonalize the full Hamiltonian including a zero-range δ-force residual interaction. For the self-conjugate nucleus ⁶⁴Ge, they first treat the limiting case of only proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing, then generalize to include proton-neutron pairs in both T=0 and T=1 channels, using the Lanczos algorithm for diagonalization and adjusting the δ-force strength to reproduce experimental odd-even mass differences.

## Key numbers

- Experimental three-point gaps for ⁶⁴Ge: Δ_n = 1.48 MeV, Δ_p = 1.14 MeV (evaluated from experimental masses of Audi (2003)).
- In the pp/nn-only case with all 1p1h and 2p2h excitations: the 0p0h vacuum contributes 69.6% (neutrons) and 70.6% (protons); one-pair excitations contribute 29.6% (neutrons) and 29.3% (protons); 1p1h components are below 0.01%.
- Correlation energy for neutrons with only one-pair excitations: -1.16 MeV; with one and two pairs: -1.43 MeV; with one, two, and three pairs: -1.45 MeV.
- Correlation energy for protons with only one-pair excitations: -1.18 MeV; with one and two pairs: -1.48 MeV; with one, two, and three pairs: -1.50 MeV.
- The probability of T_z=0 (proton-neutron) pair excitations becomes significant relative to pp or nn pair excitations when the ratio x = V_0(T=0)/V_0(T=1) exceeds 2.

## Context

This work extends the HTDA method, previously applied to heavier nuclei and high-spin states by Pillet (2002) and Quentin (2004), to include proton-neutron pairing correlations. It addresses the known deficiency of BCS-type approaches (Goswami 1964, Camiz 1965, Goodman 1968) that violate particle number conservation and produce sharp phase transitions between pairing modes in N=Z nuclei, a problem previously noted by Satula (2000) and Simkovic (2003). The method enables future studies of ground-state correlations in self-conjugate nuclei without the artifacts of number-nonconserving treatments, and the authors note that the gain in correlation energy from pn coupling is similar in magnitude to that from BCS calculations with Lipkin-Nogami projection (Sieja 2006), but occurs even for parameter ranges where no pn pairing solution exists in that approach.
