# Theoretical Evaluation of the Reaction Rates for 26Al(n,p)26Mg and 26Al(n,a)23Na

> The reactions that destroy 26Al in massive stars have significance in a number of astrophysical contexts. We evaluate the reaction rates of 26Al(n,p)26Mg and 26Al(n,a)23Na using cross sections obtained from the codes EMPIRE and TALYS. These have been compared to the published rates obtained from the non-smoker code and to some experimental data. We show that the results obtained from EMPIRE and TALYS are comparable to those from non-smoker. We also show how the theoretical results vary with respect to changes in the input parameters. Finally, we present recommended rates for these reactions using the available experimental data and our new theoretical results.

## Key claim

The reaction rates for 26Al(n,p)26Mg and 26Al(n,alpha)23Na calculated using the EMPIRE and TALYS codes are comparable to those from the NON-SMOKER code, but the authors caution that the available experimental data are sparse and inconsistent, and that their evaluated rates are "no substitute for future measurements."

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for 26Al(n,p)26Mg and 26Al(n,alpha)23Na using the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model codes EMPIRE and TALYS, which include direct, pre-equilibrium, and compound reaction mechanisms. They systematically varied the input parameters, specifically the nuclear level density models and optical model potentials, to assess the sensitivity of the calculated cross sections and reaction rates. The resulting theoretical rates were compared to experimental data from Koehler et al., Trautvetter et al., De Smet et al., and Skelton et al., and recommended rates were derived by combining experimental data at low temperatures with the theoretical calculations at higher temperatures.

## Key numbers

- For 26Al(n,p)26Mg, the three theoretical calculations (EMPIRE, TALYS, NON-SMOKER) are consistent with each other above 100 MK to within a factor of 1.7.
- For 26Al(n,p)26Mg, the theoretical results agree with the experimental rates of Koehler et al. at temperatures between 0.04 and 0.4 GK within a factor of 2.
- For 26Al(n,p)26Mg, the theoretical results are higher than those of Trautvetter et al. by more than a factor of 2.5 on average.
- For 26Al(n,alpha)23Na, the theoretical results are in agreement above 100 MK within a factor of 1.7.
- For 26Al(n,alpha)23Na, the theoretical rates agree with the experimental rate of De Smet et al. at T approximately 0.1 to 0.3 GK.
- Varying the optical model potentials in EMPIRE gave rate differences up to a factor of 3 for 26Al(n,p)26Mg and up to a factor of 3.5 for 26Al(n,alpha)23Na.
- Varying the optical model potentials in TALYS gave a ratio of maximum to minimum rates within a factor of 2 for both reactions.
- The recommended median stellar rate for 26Al(n,p)26Mg at T = 1.000 GK is 5.02 x 10^7 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1, with a low rate of 1.92 x 10^7 and a high rate of 1.31 x 10^8 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1.
- The recommended median stellar rate for 26Al(n,alpha)23Na at T = 1.000 GK is 1.58 x 10^7 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1, with a low rate of 1.02 x 10^7 and a high rate of 2.44 x 10^7 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical evaluations using the NON-SMOKER code (Rauscher and Thielemann) by employing the more flexible EMPIRE and TALYS codes to systematically explore parameter sensitivities. The study was motivated by a large-scale sensitivity analysis of 26Al synthesis in massive stars by Iliadis et al., and the present recommended rates supersede those used in that earlier work. The results enable more reliable modeling of 26Al production and destruction in massive stars, Wolf-Rayet stars, and core-collapse supernovae, but the authors emphasize that future direct measurements are needed to resolve inconsistencies among existing experimental data from Koehler et al., Trautvetter et al., and De Smet et al.
