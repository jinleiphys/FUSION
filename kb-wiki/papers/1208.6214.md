# Efimov effect in quantum magnets

> Physics is said to be universal when it emerges regardless of the underlying microscopic details. A prominent example is the Efimov effect, which predicts the emergence of an infinite tower of three-body bound states obeying discrete scale invariance when the particles interact resonantly. Because of its universality and peculiarity, the Efimov effect has been the subject of extensive research in chemical, atomic, nuclear and particle physics for decades. Here we employ an anisotropic Heisenberg model to show that collective excitations in quantum magnets (magnons) also exhibit the Efimov effect. We locate anisotropy-induced two-magnon resonances, compute binding energies of three magnons and find that they fit into the universal scaling law. We propose several approaches to experimentally realize the Efimov effect in quantum magnets, where the emergent Efimov states of magnons can be observed with commonly used spectroscopic measurements. Our study thus opens up new avenues for universal few-body physics in condensed matter systems.

## Key claim
Collective excitations in quantum magnets (magnons) can exhibit the Efimov effect when an easy-axis exchange or single-ion anisotropy is tuned to induce a two-magnon scattering resonance, with the three-magnon binding energies fitting the universal discrete scaling law. The claim is conditional on the system being at or near the critical anisotropy where the s-wave scattering length diverges.

## Method
The authors employed an anisotropic Heisenberg model on a simple cubic lattice with exchange anisotropy (J_z ≠ J) and single-ion anisotropy (D ≠ 0). They solved the two-magnon Schrödinger equation via the Lippmann–Schwinger equation to locate the anisotropy-induced two-magnon resonances (where the s-wave scattering length diverges), then solved the three-magnon bound-state problem at zero center-of-mass momentum in the s-wave channel using coupled integral equations discretized with Gaussian quadrature.

## Key numbers
- For S=1/2, the two-magnon resonance occurs at exchange anisotropy J_z/J = 2.93654.
- For S=1 with D=0, the two-magnon resonance occurs at J_z/J = 4.87307.
- For S=1 with J_z=J, the two-magnon resonance occurs at single-ion anisotropy D/J = 4.76874.
- For S=1 with J_z=-J, the two-magnon resonance occurs at D/J = 5.12703.
- At the two-magnon resonance for S=1/2, the three-magnon binding energies are: n=0: E_0/J = -2.09×10^{-1}; n=1: E_1/J = -4.15×10^{-4}; n=2: E_2/J = -8.08×10^{-7}. The ratio sqrt(E_{n-1}/E_n) approaches the universal value 22.6944.
- The universal scale factor λ = 22.6944.
- The Efimov parameter κ_* for S=1/2 is κ_*a ≃ 0.463; for S=1 with J_z=J, κ_*a ≃ 0.173; for S=1 with J_z=-J, κ_*a ≃ 0.0478.

## Context
This work extends the Efimov effect, previously studied in atomic, nuclear, and particle physics (Efimov 1970; Braaten 2006), to condensed matter systems by showing that magnons in quantum magnets can form universal three-body bound states. It enables experimental realization in organic magnets with tunable single-ion anisotropy (e.g., Ni²⁺ molecular squares with D/J ≃ 3.0(5) from Koch 2003) or in spatially anisotropic S=1/2 ferromagnets, and suggests that other universal few-body phenomena (e.g., four-body resonances from Hammer 2007 and Stecher 2009) should also apply to magnon systems.
