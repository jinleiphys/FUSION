# Topological Strings in SU(3) Gauge Theory at Finite Temperature

> We investigate string configurations in the deconfined phase of SU(3) gauge theory, which arise from the spontaneous breaking of the $Z_3$ center symmetry. These configurations form at the junctions of domain walls of the theory. The complex phase of the Polyakov loop changes by multiples of $2π$ on large spatial loops around the string, rendering them topologically stable. Using the Monte Carlo simulations of the partition function, we compute the free energy associated with these configurations. The simulations are performed on lattices with spatial dimensions $N_{x,y}=60, N_z=4$, and temporal extent $N_τ=2$. Our results show that the free energy of the $Z_3-$strings is dominated by the domain walls. Further near the transition point, thermal fluctuations cause the decay of domain walls as well as the $Z_3$ strings into confined-deconfined interfaces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates through first-principles lattice simulations that topological Z3 string configurations exist in the deconfined phase of SU(3) gauge theory, with the free energy dominated by the attached domain walls rather than the string core itself, and that near the critical temperature thermal fluctuations cause both domain walls and strings to decay into confined-deconfined interfaces.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the SU(3) gauge theory using the standard Wilson action on lattices with spatial dimensions Nx=Ny=60, Nz=4, and temporal extents Nτ=2 and 4. They imposed boundary conditions on the temporal links at the spatial boundary to induce a Z3 string configuration with three attached domain walls, then computed the action difference between configurations with and without the string, integrating this difference over the gauge coupling β to obtain the free energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Z3 domain walls in pure SU(3) gauge theory by Kajantie (1990), Aoki (1993), and Holland (1999) for Nτ=2, and by Huang (1990) for Nτ=4, as well as interface tension calculations by deForcrand (2004, 2005). The string configurations were previously studied using effective Polyakov loop potentials by Layek (2005), but this work provides first-principles lattice validation. The results enable future investigations of how these topological strings might affect quark-gluon plasma dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, particularly above the temperature Tm where metastable Z3 states exist, as studied in the PNJL model by Biswal (2019).
