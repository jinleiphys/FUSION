# Possible three-nucleon force effects in D-P scattering at low energies

> We present measurements of the analyzing powers Ay and iT11 for proton-deuteron scattering at Ecm=432 keV. Calculations using a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential (Argonne V18) are found to underpredict both analyzing powers by 40. The inclusion of the Urbana three-nucleon interaction does not significantly modify the calculated analyzing powers. Due to its short range, it is difficult for this three-nucleon interaction to affect Ay and iT11 at this low energy. The origin of the discrepancy remains an open question.

## Key claim

Measurements of the analyzing powers \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) for proton-deuteron scattering at \(E_{c.m.}=432\) keV are underpredicted by approximately 40% by calculations using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential, and the inclusion of the Urbana three-nucleon interaction does not significantly modify the calculated values, leaving the origin of the discrepancy an open question.

## Method

The authors measured the vector analyzing powers \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) for p-d elastic scattering at \(E_{c.m.}=432\) keV using polarized proton and deuteron beams from an atomic beam polarized ion source at Triangle Universities Nuclear Laboratory. Scattered deuterons and protons were detected in coincidence using silicon surface barrier detectors, with beam polarization determined via polarimeter reactions. The experimental results were compared to theoretical calculations using the Pair-Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic basis and the Kohn variational principle with the AV18 potential alone and with the AV18 plus the Urbana three-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- The measurements were performed at \(E_{c.m.}=432\pm 1\) keV, with beam energy loss of approximately 10 keV in the thin carbon targets.
- Both analyzing powers \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) are underpredicted by the AV18 calculations by approximately 40%.
- The change in calculated \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) from including the Urbana three-nucleon interaction is too small by an order of magnitude to explain the discrepancy.
- Agreement with the data could be optimized by reducing the \(^4P_{1/2}\) phase shift from its calculated value to \(5.22^\circ\) (a 1.6% reduction in absolute value) and increasing the absolute value of the \(\varepsilon_{3/2}^-\) mixing parameter to \(-1.02^\circ\) (a 15% increase).
- These phase-shift parameter changes affect the cross section by less than 0.15% and the other analyzing powers by less than 0.0012.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the "\(A_y(\theta)\) puzzle" in n-d scattering (Witała, 1994) and p-d scattering (Kievsky, 1995; Kievsky, 1996) to lower energies, where the influence of higher partial waves is strongly reduced and the connection between observables and underlying interactions is simplified. The results contradict the possibility that two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces are responsible for the analyzing power discrepancies, as the short-range nature of such forces cannot significantly affect \(A_y\) and \(iT_{11}\) at this low energy. The findings motivate the consideration of new types of three-nucleon interactions, such as spin-orbit three-nucleon forces, or the possibility that inadequacies in the nucleon-nucleon interaction are responsible for the discrepancy.
