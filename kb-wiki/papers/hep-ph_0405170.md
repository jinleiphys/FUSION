# Phase diagram of dense neutral three-flavor quark matter

> We study the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter as a function of the strange quark mass, the quark chemical potential, and the temperature, employing a general nine-parameter ansatz for the gap matrix. At zero temperature and small values of the strange quark mass, the ground state of matter corresponds to the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. At some critical value of the strange quark mass, this is replaced by the recently proposed gapless CFL (gCFL) phase. We also find several other phases, for instance, a metallic CFL (mCFL) phase, a so-called uSC phase where all colors of up quarks are paired, as well as the standard two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase and the gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase.

# Phase diagram of dense neutral three-flavor quark matter

## Key claim

The ground state of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter transitions from the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase to the gapless CFL (gCFL) phase at a critical strange quark mass, but at nonzero temperature there is no sharp phase transition between CFL and gCFL; instead, an insulator-metal crossover occurs from CFL to a metallic CFL (mCFL) phase, followed by a separate transition to the gCFL phase when gapless modes appear.

## Method

The authors employed a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type model with a point-like current-current interaction approximating gluon exchange, using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism to derive the thermodynamic potential. A general nine-parameter ansatz for the gap matrix was used, incorporating pairing in both the antisymmetric and symmetric color-flavor channels, and the gap equations were solved numerically together with three neutrality conditions (electric, color-3, and color-8) at fixed quark chemical potential mu = 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The diquark coupling strength was set to g^2 / Lambda^2 = 45.1467 GeV^{-2} with a three-momentum cutoff kappa = 0.6533 GeV.
- The critical value for the CFL-to-gCFL transition at zero temperature occurs at m_s^2 / mu approximately 2 Delta, about 190 MeV.
- At fixed strange quark mass m_s = 300 MeV, the CFL phase is the ground state for mu greater than about 475 MeV, while the gCFL phase appears at lower chemical potentials.
- In the CFL phase, the color chemical potential mu_8 satisfies mu_8 = -m_s^2 / (sqrt(3) mu).
- The electron mass was taken as m_e = 0.511 MeV and the muon mass as m_mu = 105.66 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the results of the gapless CFL (gCFL) phase proposed by Alford et al. (2004) by using a more general nine-parameter gap ansatz that includes pairing in the symmetric sextet-sextet channel, which was neglected in earlier studies. The paper also builds on the gapless 2SC (g2SC) phase work by Shovkovy and Huang (2003) and the finite-temperature phase structure of three-flavor quark matter studied by Iida et al. (2004). The results enable more realistic modeling of quark matter in compact star interiors, where local charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium are required, and identify the mCFL phase as a distinct crossover regime at nonzero temperature.
