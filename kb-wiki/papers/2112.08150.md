# On the Photoproduction Reactions $γd\toπNN$

> A review of our works providing a theoretical description of incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron is presented. The existing $γd\toπNN$ data are analysed, especially those obtained more recently by the CLAS Collaboration at JLab, the A2 Collaboration at MAMI at Mainz, and the PION@MAX-lab Collaboration at Lund. A procedure, which accounts for the final state interactions (FSI), is applied to extract $γn\toπN$ differential cross sections from the deuteron data. The role of FSI is discussed. We also comment on the results of other works in connection with some discrepancies seen in comparing the model with experiment. The electromagnetic properties of baryon $N^\ast$ resonances, improved using the extracted $γn\toπN$ differential cross sections, are presented. A model description of the cross section data from charged-pion photoproduction reactions $γd\toπ^{\pm}NN$ near threshold is also given. The $γd\toπ^-pp$ data are used to extract the $E_{0+}$ multipole and total crsoss sections of the reaction $γn\toπ^-p$ near threshold.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the authors' theoretical framework for extracting neutron-target pion photoproduction cross sections from deuteron data, emphasizing that the extraction requires model-dependent nuclear corrections (final-state interactions and Fermi motion) and that the correction factor R deviates significantly from unity only at small pion angles, narrowing with increasing photon energy.

## Method

The authors model the γd → πNN amplitude using impulse approximation plus NN and πN final-state interaction (FSI) terms, with elementary γN → πN amplitudes from the SAID partial-wave analysis, NN scattering amplitudes from the GW NN solution, and the Bonn deuteron wave function. They compute the differential cross section for γd → π⁻pp and define a correction factor R that relates the deuteron cross section to the quasi-free neutron cross section, then apply this factor to extract γn → π⁻p differential cross sections from CLAS deuteron data using an iterative self-consistency procedure.

## Key numbers

- The correction factor R is computed for photon energies in the range 1000–2700 MeV, with a kinematical cut |p₂| < 200 MeV/c < |p₁| on the final proton momenta.
- The off-shell correction to the ¹S₀ partial amplitude of pp-scattering uses a form factor with β = 1.2 fm⁻¹.
- The Glauber approximation estimate yields R ≈ 0.95 for large angles, using typical values σᵗ_{NN} ≈ 45 mb and σᵗ_{πN} (value not explicitly given in the truncated text).
- The effective number of neutrons c(p_max) approaches 1 as p_max → ∞.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior series of papers (Tarasov 2011, Chen 2012, Briscoe 2012, Tarasov 2015, Strandberg 2018, Briscoe 2020) on incoherent pion photoproduction from deuterium, and contrasts with the extraction procedures of Nakamura (2017, 2018) who used a different model with impulse approximation plus FSI terms. The analysis uses data from CLAS at JLab, A2 at MAMI (Mainz), and PION@MAX-lab (Lund), and enables improved determination of γn → πN differential cross sections and the electromagnetic couplings of N* and Δ* resonances, which previously were largely restricted to charged states from proton-target data.
