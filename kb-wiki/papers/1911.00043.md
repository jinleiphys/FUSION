# Radius of convergence in lattice QCD at finite $μ_B$ with rooted staggered fermions

> In typical statistical mechanical systems the grand canonical partition function at finite volume is proportional to a polynomial of the fugacity $e^{μ/T}$. The zero of this Lee-Yang polynomial closest to the origin determines the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of the pressure around $μ=0$. The computationally cheapest formulation of lattice QCD, rooted staggered fermions, with the usual definition of the rooted determinant, does not admit such a Lee-Yang polynomial. We argue that the radius of convergence is then bounded by the spectral gap of the reduced matrix of the unrooted staggered operator. This is a cutoff effect that potentially affects all estimates of the radius of convergence with the standard staggered rooting. We suggest a new definition of the rooted staggered determinant at finite chemical potential that allows for a definition of a Lee-Yang polynomial, and, therefore of the numerical study of Lee-Yang zeros. We also describe an algorithm to determine the Lee-Yang zeros and apply it to configurations generated with the 2-stout improved staggered action at $N_t = 4$. We perform a finite-volume scaling study of the leading Lee-Yang zeros and estimate the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion extrapolated to an infinite volume. We show that the limiting singularity is not on the real line, thus giving a lower bound on the location of any possible phase transitions at this lattice spacing. In the vicinity of the crossover temperature at zero chemical potential, the radius of convergence turns out to be $μ_B/T \approx 2$ and roughly temperature independent. Our simulations are performed at strange quark chemical potential $μ_s=0$, but the method can be straightforwardly extended to strangeness chemical potential $μ_S=0$ or strangeness neutrality.

## Key claim

The radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion of the pressure in lattice QCD at finite baryon chemical potential, when using the standard rooted staggered fermion formulation, is bounded by the spectral gap of the reduced matrix of the unrooted staggered operator rather than by a genuine Lee-Yang zero, which is a cutoff effect that potentially affects all previous estimates; the authors propose a new definition of the rooted staggered determinant that restores the polynomial character of the partition function and allows a direct determination of Lee-Yang zeros.

## Method

The authors compute the eigenvalues of the reduced matrix \(P\) for the staggered Dirac operator on gauge configurations generated with the 2-stout improved staggered action at \(N_t = 4\). To circumvent the analyticity problems of standard rooting, they identify doublets of eigenvalues by minimizing the total sum of distances within each pair using the Blossom algorithm, replace each pair by their geometric mean with a global phase correction to preserve the determinant properties, and thereby define a rooted determinant that is a polynomial in the fugacity. They then compute the Lee-Yang zeros of the resulting polynomial partition function and perform a finite-volume scaling study to extrapolate the leading zero to infinite volume.

## Key numbers

- The radius of convergence in the vicinity of the crossover temperature at zero chemical potential is \(\mu_B/T \approx 2\) and roughly temperature independent.
- The simulations are performed on lattices with temporal extent \(N_t = 4\) and spatial volume \(V = N_s^3\) where \(N_s\) is even; the reduced matrix has \(6V\) eigenvalues for unrooted fermions and \(3V\) eigenvalues after pairing for \(N_f = 2\).
- The partition function polynomial after geometric matching has degree \(3V\) (for \(N_f = 2\)), yielding exactly \(3V\) Lee-Yang zeros.
- The limiting singularity from the finite-volume scaling study is not on the real line, giving a lower bound on the location of any possible phase transitions at this lattice spacing.

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang zero approach to rooted staggered fermions, which is the computationally cheapest formulation of lattice QCD, addressing the analyticity problems identified in prior work by Giordano (2019) on the relation between Taylor coefficients and Lee-Yang zeros. The method builds on the reduced matrix formulation of Hasenfratz (1991) and the suggestion by Golterman (2006) to average taste multiplets for improved rooting. The results provide a direct determination of the radius of convergence at finite lattice spacing, which serves as input for heavy-ion collision phenomenology and constrains the location of the conjectured critical end point, complementing Taylor expansion methods (Gavai, 2003; Borsanyi, 2011) and analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential (de Forcrand, 2002; D'Elia, 2002).
