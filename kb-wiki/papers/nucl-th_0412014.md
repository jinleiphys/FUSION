# Lattice gas models derived from effective field theory

> We start from a low-energy effective field theory for interacting fermions on the lattice and expand in the hopping parameter to derive the nearest-neighbor interactions for a lattice gas model. In this model the renormalization of couplings for different lattice spacings is inherited from the effective field theory, systematic errors can be estimated a priori, and the breakdown of the lattice gas model description at low temperatures can be understood quantitatively. We apply the lattice gas method to neutron matter and compare with results from a recent quantum simulation.

## Key claim
The authors derive a lattice gas model from a low-energy effective field theory for interacting fermions by expanding in the spatial hopping parameter, but the approximation is only quantitatively reliable when the dimensionless hopping parameter \(\beta/(2m)\) is less than about 0.4; at larger values the lattice gas model fails to capture quantum phase transitions such as superfluidity.

## Method
Starting from a lattice effective field theory with single-site interactions and nearest-neighbor hopping, the authors perform a spatial hopping parameter expansion to second order in \(\beta/(2m)\). They sum over all configurations of fermion states on each lattice site to obtain an approximate grand canonical partition function that resembles a \(2^{n_f}\)-state Ising model. The interaction coefficient \(C\) for neutron matter is fixed by matching the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude on the lattice to the physical \(^1S_0\) scattering length using Lüscher's formula.

## Key numbers
- For neutron matter at \(T = 8\) MeV, the hopping parameter \(\beta/(2m)\) ranges from 0.17 (for \(a^{-1} = 50\) MeV) to 0.43 (for \(a^{-1} = 80\) MeV).
- For neutron matter at \(T = 4\) MeV, the hopping parameter \(\beta/(2m)\) ranges from 0.33 (for \(a^{-1} = 50\) MeV) to 0.85 (for \(a^{-1} = 80\) MeV).
- The interaction coefficient \(C\) for neutron matter is \(-8.01 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-2}\) at \(a^{-1} = 50\) MeV and \(-5.10 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-2}\) at \(a^{-1} = 80\) MeV.
- The lattice gas model agrees well with full quantum simulations when the spatial hopping parameter is less than about 0.4.
- The computational cost of the lattice gas model simulation is several hundred times less than the quantum simulation.

## Context
This work extends phenomenological lattice gas models used in nuclear physics for multifragmentation and nuclear matter thermodynamics (Pan 1995, Das Gupta 1996, Kuo 1996) by placing them on a firmer effective field theory footing. It builds on recent quantum lattice simulations for neutron matter (Lee 2004) and provides a computationally cheaper alternative that can describe classical liquid-gas phase transitions but not quantum phase transitions such as superfluidity. The approach enables systematic error estimation and renormalization-group-consistent simulations at different lattice spacings, and is applicable to other fermion systems such as trapped Fermi gases near a Feshbach resonance.
