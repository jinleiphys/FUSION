# Cranking mass parameters for fission

> A formalism for semi-adiabatic cranking mass parameters is presented. For the fission process of 234U, the time-dependent pairing equations of motion were used to calculate the excitation energy and to extract values of the cranking inertia. A fission barrier is determined by minimizing the action trajectory in a five dimensional configuration space spanned by elongation, necking, deformations of fragments and mass-asymmetry. The deformation energy is computed in the the frame of the microscopic-macroscopic model. The two center shell model with Woods-Saxon potentials is used in this context. Values of the inertia for excited fissioning systems are reported. A dependence between the cranking mass parameters and the intrinsic excitation energy is evidenced.

# Cranking mass parameters for fission

## Key claim

The paper presents a semi-adiabatic cranking formalism that incorporates intrinsic excitation energy into the calculation of mass parameters for fission, showing that the cranking inertia decreases significantly when the fissioning nucleus is internally excited, but only under the assumption that the system deforms slowly enough that seniority-two configurations can be neglected and the system remains in a seniority-zero state during fission.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent pairing equations of motion (time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations) for the fission of ²³⁴U along a least-action trajectory in a five-dimensional configuration space spanned by elongation, necking, fragment deformations, and mass asymmetry. The deformation energy is computed using the microscopic-macroscopic model with a Woods-Saxon two-center shell model, and the cranking mass parameters are extracted from the time-dependent solutions for three different internuclear velocities. The inertia is calculated using a newly derived semi-adiabatic cranking formula that depends on the time derivatives of the pairing moment components and single-particle densities obtained from the time-dependent pairing equations.

## Key numbers

- Three internuclear velocities were used: 1×10⁴, 1×10⁵, and 1×10⁶ fm/fs, corresponding to barrier penetration times between 10⁻¹⁸ and 10⁻²⁰ s
- The inertia along the minimal action trajectory has the largest values for the lowest velocity (1×10⁴ fm/fs), which produces practically negligible intrinsic excitation energy
- For higher velocities (1×10⁵ and 1×10⁶ fm/fs), where excitation energy becomes important, the magnitude of the inertia is considerably lower than the adiabatic cranking values
- The semi-adiabatic inertia in the first well has larger values than those obtained within the adiabatic cranking model
- Asymptotically after scission, the inertia approaches approximately the reduced mass μ

## Context

This work extends previous cranking model calculations for fission by incorporating intrinsic excitation energy effects, building on the temperature-dependent cranking model of Schneider (no year given) which showed that increasing temperature smoothens and lowers the mass parameter. The formalism contrasts with the adiabatic cranking model of Funny (no year given) and the generator coordinate method with Gaussian Overlap Approximation of Brink (no year given), which typically gives mass parameters about 2/3 smaller than adiabatic cranking. The results enable improved dynamical calculations of fission barriers and fragment kinetic energies by providing excitation-energy-dependent inertia that can be used in action minimization procedures.
