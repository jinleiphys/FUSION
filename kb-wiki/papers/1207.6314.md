# Comparative study of neutron and nuclear matter with simplified Argonne nucleon-nucleon potentials

> We present calculations of the energy per particle of pure neutron and symmetric nuclear matter with simplified Argonne nucleon-nucleon potentials for different many-body theories. We compare critically the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results to other formalisms, such as the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone expansion up to third order, Self-Consistent Green's Functions, Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo, and Fermi Hyper Netted Chain. We evaluate the importance of spin-orbit and tensor correlations in the equation of state and find these to be important in a wide range of densities.

# Comparative study of neutron and nuclear matter with simplified Argonne nucleon-nucleon potentials

## Key claim
The simplified Argonne V'_8 potential, despite reproducing the phase shifts and deuteron properties of the full V_18 potential almost exactly, predicts a substantially more attractive equation of state for both symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach, while the less realistic V'_6 potential yields results closer to V_18 due to an intricate series of cancellations between partial wave contributions.

## Method
The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of the energy per particle for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the simplified Argonne V'_8, V'_6, and V'_4 potentials, and compared these results to the full Argonne V_18 potential. They also compared BHF results with other many-body formalisms including Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone expansion up to third order, Self-Consistent Green's Functions, Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo, and Fermi Hyper Netted Chain, analyzing the partial wave decomposition of the binding energy at a density of 0.3 fm^{-3}.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric nuclear matter at saturation within BHF: V_18 gives -16.4 MeV at 0.20 fm^{-3}; V'_8 gives -20.0 MeV at 0.20 fm^{-3}; V'_6 gives -16.2 MeV at 0.20 fm^{-3}; V'_4 gives -29.7 MeV at 0.20 fm^{-3}
- For neutron matter at 0.30 fm^{-3} within BHF: V_18 gives 25.3 MeV; V'_8 gives 19.4 MeV; V'_6 gives 28.9 MeV; V'_4 gives 31.5 MeV
- The deuteron D-state probability is 5.78% for both V_18 and V'_8, 5.33% for V'_6, and 0.00% for V'_4
- The deuteron binding energy is -2.24 MeV for all refitted potentials (V_18, V'_8, V'_6, V'_4)
- At 0.3 fm^{-3} in symmetric nuclear matter, the ^3SD_1 partial wave contributes -20.4 MeV for V_18, -25.2 MeV for V'_8, -23.9 MeV for V'_6, and -43.5 MeV for V'_4

## Context
This work extends previous investigations by Baldo (2004) and Bombaci (2005) on the equation of state of nuclear matter using different many-body approaches. The study complements benchmark calculations within the few-body community (Kamada, 2001) by providing systematic comparisons for infinite matter. The results demonstrate that simplified potentials like V'_8, while phase-shift equivalent to V_18 in low partial waves, produce significantly different saturation properties, and that the V'_6 potential's closer agreement with V_18 is misleading due to cancellations between partial wave contributions. This enables quantification of theoretical uncertainties in equation of state predictions and highlights the need for careful treatment of spin-orbit and tensor correlations in many-body calculations.
