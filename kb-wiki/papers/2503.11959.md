# Tomography of the Rho meson in the QCD Instanton Vacuum: Transverse Momentum Dependent Parton Distribution Functions

> We analyse the rho meson unpolarized and polarized transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) in the instanton liquid model (ILM). The corresponding TMDs in ILM are approximated by a constituent quark beam function in the leading Fock state multiplied by a rapidity-dependent factor resulting from the staple-shaped Wilson lines, for fixed longitudinal momentum, transverse separation, and rapidity. At the resolution of the ILM, all of the rho meson TMDs are symmetric in parton x for fixed transverse momentum, and Gaussian-like in the transverse momentum for fixed parton x. The latter is a direct consequence of the profiling of the quark zero modes in the ILM. The evolved TMDs at higher rapidity using the Collins-Soper kernel, and higher resolution using the renormalization group (RG), show substantial skewness towards low parton x.

## Key claim
The rho meson transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) are computed in the instanton liquid model (ILM) at low resolution, where they are symmetric in parton longitudinal momentum fraction x for fixed transverse momentum and Gaussian-like in transverse momentum for fixed x; after evolution to higher rapidity using the Collins-Soper kernel and higher resolution using the renormalization group, the TMDs develop substantial skewness towards low x.

## Method
The authors compute the rho meson TMDs using light-front wave functions (LFWFs) in the leading Fock state, multiplied by a rapidity-dependent factor from staple-shaped Wilson lines, within the instanton liquid model of the QCD vacuum. The TMDs are derived from the Lorentz decomposition of quark correlation functions for a spin-1 vector meson, yielding nine leading-twist T-even TMDs including three tensor-polarized functions specific to spin-1 targets. The soft subtraction scheme is implemented, and double evolution in scale and rapidity is carried out using the Collins-Soper kernel and renormalization group equations.

## Key numbers
* The instanton pseudoparticle size is set to ρ = 0.313 fm, with a constituent quark mass M = 398.17 MeV, yielding a rho meson mass m_ρ = 791.0 MeV.
* The rho LFWF normalization constant is C_ρ = 1.9888, with the parameter λ_ρ = 1.260 fixed by the rho electroweak decay constant f_ρ.
* The mean transverse momentum ⟨k_⊥⟩ for the unpolarized TMD f_1 in the ILM is 0.0810 GeV, compared to 0.32 GeV in the NJL model, 0.238 GeV in LFHM, 0.328 GeV in LFQM, and 0.399 GeV in BSE approaches.
* The mean transverse momentum for g_1 is 0.0751 GeV, for g_1T is 0.0785 GeV, for h_1 is 0.0785 GeV, for h_1L is 0.0751 GeV, and for f_1TT is 0.0734 GeV, all in the ILM.
* The width of the TMDs in transverse momentum is approximately √⟨k_⊥²⟩ ≈ 0.1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' recent study of spin-0 TMDs for the pion and kaon in the ILM (Liu and Zahed, 2025) to the spin-1 rho meson. It provides nonperturbative predictions for rho meson TMDs that can be compared with results from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Ninomiya et al., 2017), light-front holographic and light-front quark models (Kaur et al., 2020), and Bethe-Salpeter equation approaches (Shi et al., 2022). The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations using LaMET and with empirical extractions from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering experiments at COMPASS, JLAB, LHC, and the future EIC.
