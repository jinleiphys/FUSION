# Neutrino-induced pion production from nuclei at medium energies

> We present a fully relativistic formalism for describing neutrino-induced $Δ$-mediated single-pion production from nuclei. We assess the ambiguities stemming from the $Δ$ interactions. Variations in the cross sections of over 10% are observed, depending on whether or not magnetic-dipole dominance is assumed to extract the vector form factors. These uncertainties have a direct impact on the accuracy with which the axial-vector form factors can be extracted. Different predictions for $C_5^A(Q^2)$ induce up to 40-50% effects on the $Δ$-production cross sections. To describe the nucleus, we turn to a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) using realistic bound-state wave functions derived in the Hartree approximation to the $σ$-$ω$ Walecka model. For neutrino energies larger than 1 GeV, we show that a relativistic Fermi-gas model with appropriate binding-energy correction produces comparable results as the RPWIA which naturally includes Fermi motion, nuclear-binding effects and the Pauli exclusion principle. Including $Δ$ medium modifications yields a 20 to 25% reduction of the RPWIA cross section. The model presented in this work can be naturally extended to include the effect of final-state interactions in a relativistic and quantum-mechanical way. Guided by recent neutrino-oscillation experiments, such as MiniBooNE and K2K, and future efforts like MINER$ν$A, we present $Q^2$, $W$, and various semi-inclusive distributions, both for a free nucleon and carbon, oxygen and iron targets.

# Neutrino-induced pion production from nuclei at medium energies

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully relativistic formalism for neutrino-induced Δ-mediated single-pion production from nuclei, but the abstract's claim of "fully relativistic" is qualified by the fact that final-state interactions are explicitly excluded from the present work. The model is intended as a starting ground for implementing final-state interactions in a relativistic and quantum-mechanical way, but this implementation falls beyond the scope of the paper.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) using realistic bound-state wave functions derived in the Hartree approximation to the σ-ω Walecka model, with the W1 parameterization. They compute charged-current neutrino-induced Δ-mediated single-pion production cross sections for free nucleons and for carbon, oxygen, and iron targets, comparing RPWIA results with relativistic Fermi-gas (RFG) model calculations. Medium modifications of the Δ particle are included by shifting the Δ mass by +30 MeV and the width by +40 MeV, using an average nuclear density of ρ = 0.75 ρ₀.

## Key numbers

- Variations in cross sections of over 10% are observed depending on whether magnetic-dipole dominance is assumed to extract the vector form factors
- Different predictions for C₅ᴬ(Q²) induce up to 40-50% effects on the Δ-production cross sections
- Including Δ medium modifications yields a 20 to 25% reduction of the RPWIA cross section
- For neutrino energies larger than 1 GeV, the RFG model with appropriate binding-energy correction produces comparable results to RPWIA
- The free Δ decay width is 120 MeV, with coupling constant f_{πNΔ} = 2.21
- The Δ mass is M_Δ = 1.232 GeV
- The axial mass parameter M_A = 1.05 GeV is used in the standard parameterization
- Near Q² = 0, cross sections using χCQ and QCD model results for C₅ᴬ are about 40% lower than the Δ-dominance fit calculation
- The χCQ result for C₅ᴬ halves the integrated cross section relative to the Δ-dominance fit
- Differences between the traditional ΔπN coupling and the Pascalutsa coupling do not exceed the 2% level
- The elementary cross section is halved near threshold for nuclear targets; this effect dwindles to 20% at E_ν = 800 MeV and 8% at E_ν = 2 GeV
- The iron curve exceeds the carbon and RFG results by roughly 15% just beyond threshold
- Binding-energy effects fall to a 1% correction level at E_ν = 1 GeV
- For RFG calculations, k_F = 225 MeV and average binding energy E_B = 20 MeV are adopted

## Context

This work extends the relativistic formalism previously applied in quasi-elastic nucleon-knockout studies (Martinez, Lava, Praet, Jachowicz) to the Δ-mediated single-pion production channel. It addresses ambiguities in the vector and axial-vector form factors that have been problematic since early bubble-chamber neutrino data analyses (Hernandez, Kuzmin), and compares with alternative form-factor calculations from chiral constituent-quark models (Barq) and lattice QCD (Alexandrou). The model is designed to support upcoming neutrino-oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE, K2K, and MINERνA by providing detailed Q², W, and semi-inclusive distributions for carbon, oxygen, and iron targets.
