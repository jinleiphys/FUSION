# Can the energy dependence of elliptic flow reveal the QGP phase transition?

> Ideal hydrodynamic simulations are performed to compute the evolution with collision energy of hadron spectra and elliptic flow between AGS and LHC energies. We argue that viscous effects should decrease with increasing energy, improving the applicability of ideal fluid dynamics at higher energies. We show that the increasing radial flow at higher energies pushes the elliptic flow to larger transverse momenta, leading to a peaking and subsequent decrease of the elliptic flow at fixed p_T with increasing collision energy, independent of whether or not there is a phase transition in the equation of state.

## Key claim
The non-monotonic energy dependence of elliptic flow at fixed transverse momentum is a generic consequence of increasing radial flow with collision energy, independent of whether a quark-hadron phase transition exists in the equation of state, and therefore cannot be used as unambiguous evidence for the phase transition or a critical end point.

## Method
Ideal relativistic fluid dynamic simulations were performed to compute hadron spectra and elliptic flow from low AGS to LHC energies, using entropy conservation to relate initial temperature and thermalization time to final charged multiplicity. The initial conditions for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC were taken as a baseline, with the initial thermalization time scaling as τ₀ ~ (dN_ch/dy)^(-1/2) and peak entropy density scaling as s₀ ~ (dN_ch/dy)^(3/2) for extrapolation to other energies.

## Key numbers
- Central 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC correspond to s₀ = 117 fm⁻³ and dN_ch/dy = 685
- The explored range in the study was s₀ ≤ 270 fm⁻³ (dN_ch/dy ≤ 1200)
- The differential elliptic flow v₂(p_T) increases from s₀ = 8.3 fm⁻³ (√s ≈ 4 GeV) to s₀ = 23.4 fm⁻³ (√s ≈ 10 GeV), then decreases monotonically for √s ≥ 10 GeV at all p_T ≤ 1 GeV
- In ideal relativistic fluid dynamics, the p_T-integrated elliptic flow of pions and charged hadrons increases about 10-15% from RHIC to LHC energies; accounting for viscous effects, the corresponding increase is about 25%

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Kolb (1999) on the non-monotonic energy dependence of integrated elliptic flow predicted by ideal hydrodynamics, which was contradicted by the monotonic increase observed by the NA49 and STAR collaborations (Alt 2003). The study shows that the saturation of differential elliptic flow at fixed p_T observed by PHENIX, previously interpreted by Lacey (2006) as a possible phase transition signature, is instead a generic radial flow effect. The results enable future searches for QCD phase transition signatures to focus on observables other than fixed-p_T elliptic flow, such as fluctuations or correlations.
