# Quasiparticle resonance in decay spectrum of unbound nuclei near neutron drip-line

> Background: The pairing correlation in weakly bound nuclei causes a mixing among bound and unbound configurations. A remarkable consequence is emergence of the quasiparticle resonance, which has been predicted with the coordinate space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, but not yet observed experimentally. Purpose: We discuss possible observation of quasiparticle resonances in decay spectrum of unbound nuclei near the neutron drip-line. We deal with an example of unbound nucleus ${}^{21}$C which disintegrates to ${}^{20}$C and a neutron. Method: We describe a scattering state consisting of ${}^{20}$C and a neutron in the framework of the HFB formalism. We assume that a nucleon knockout reaction produces a doorway state of the decay, and we evaluate the decay spectrum by taking an overlap of the doorway state and the scattering state of ${}^{20}\mathrm{C}+n$. A numerical calculation was performed with the Woods-Saxon potential and a density-dependent effective pairing interaction. Results: We show that the quasiparticle resonance appears as low-lying peaks in the decay spectrum of ${}^{21}$C. They originate from the weakly bound single-neutron orbits $2s_{1/2}$ and $1d_{5/2}$, but emerge as unbound resonant quasiparticle states under the influence of the neutron pairing correlation. The resonance energy and the width of the calculated quasiparticle resonances are consistent with an experimental observation whereas they are sensitive to the neutron pairing correlation. Conclusion: The results suggest that nucleon knockout reactions populating the unbound nucleus ${}^{21}\mathrm{C}$ provide realistic opportunity of experimentally observing the quasiparticle resonance and of disclosing the pairing correlation in neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle resonance, predicted by coordinate space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory but not yet experimentally observed, may appear as low-lying peaks in the decay spectrum of the unbound nucleus 21C produced via nucleon knockout reactions, provided the neutron pairing correlation is sufficiently strong.

## Method

The authors describe the scattering state of 20C + n within the HFB formalism, assuming a nucleon knockout reaction produces a doorway state of the decay. They evaluate the decay spectrum by taking an overlap of the doorway state and the scattering state of 20C + n, using a Woods-Saxon potential and a density-dependent effective pairing interaction. Numerical calculations are performed with the coordinate space HFB equation solved via the Runge-Kutta method with a box boundary condition at 30 fm.

## Key numbers

* Resonance energy for the s1/2-wave quasiparticle resonance in 21C: eR = 0.846 MeV, with width Γ = 0.628 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental values of 0.8 ± 0.15 MeV and 0.9 ± 0.9 MeV from Leblond (2015).
* Resonance energy for the d5/2-wave quasiparticle resonance in 21C: eR = 1.629 MeV, with width Γ = 0.227 MeV (calculated), compared to experimental values of 1.5 ± 0.1 MeV and 0.2+0.9-0.2 MeV from Leblond (2015).
* Neutron Fermi energy in 22C: λ = -0.112 MeV, giving an estimated two-neutron separation energy S2n ≈ 0.224 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -0.14 ± 0.46 MeV from Gaudefroy (2012).
* Neutron Fermi energy in 20C: λ = -1.098 MeV, giving S2n ≈ 2.195 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 3.56 ± 0.23 MeV.
* Matter r.m.s. radius of 20C: 3.019 fm (calculated), compared to 2.97+0.03-0.05 fm from Togano (2016).
* Matter r.m.s. radius of 22C: 3.184 fm (calculated), compared to 3.44 ± 0.08 fm from Togano (2016).
* Pairing force strength used for the representative result: v0 = -320.0 MeV·fm3, giving average neutron pair gaps Δ ≈ 2.3 - 3.0 MeV for both 20C and 22C.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB studies of quasiparticle resonances in elastic neutron scattering (Kobayashi 2016, Kobayashi 2020) to the experimentally accessible decay spectrum of unbound nuclei produced in knockout reactions. The results suggest that low-lying peak structures observed in SAMURAI experiments at RIKEN RIBF (Leblond 2015, Orr 2016) could be signatures of quasiparticle resonances, offering a realistic opportunity to experimentally observe this predicted phenomenon and to probe the pairing correlation in neutron-rich nuclei. The approach uses an approximation adopted by Hagino and Sagawa (2016) for evaluating the decay spectrum from the overlap between a doorway state and the scattering final state.
