# One-meson-loop NJL model: Effect of collective and noncollective excitations on the quark condensate at finite temperature

> We explore the effect of including quantum fluctuations in the two flavor Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature. This is accomplished, in a symmetry preserving way, by including collective and noncollective modes in the one-meson-loop gap equation which originate from poles and branch cuts in the complex plane, respectively. The inclusion of a boson cutoff, $Λ_b$, is necessary to regularize the meson-loop momenta. This new parameter is used to study the influence of going beyond the usual mean field approximation in the quark condensate. As the temperature increases, chiral symmetry tends to get restored, the collective modes melt and only noncollective modes contribute to the quark condensate. With the inclusion of such modes, the quark condensate at finite temperature has a different behavior than at mean field level that will be explored.

## Key claim

The paper extends the symmetry-conserving one-meson-loop approximation of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to finite temperature by including both collective (pole) and noncollective (branch cut) contributions from meson fluctuations, showing that the quark condensate at finite temperature behaves differently than at mean field level, with the collective modes melting as chiral symmetry is restored and only noncollective modes contributing at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors derive the one-meson-loop gap equation for the two-flavor NJL model using the effective action formalism, then separate the meson-loop contributions into collective modes (arising from poles in the complex plane) and noncollective modes (arising from branch cuts). The meson-loop momenta are regularized with a boson cutoff Λ_b, while quark loops are regularized with a fermion cutoff Λ_f; the ratio α = Λ_b/Λ_f is constrained by requiring the maximum momentum scale P_max = (1+α)Λ_f to be of order 1 GeV. The gap equation is solved at finite temperature using Matsubara sum techniques transformed into contour integrations around the real axis.

## Key numbers

- The maximum momentum scale in the system is P_max = Λ_f + Λ_b = (1+α)Λ_f, constrained to be of order 1 GeV
- The one-meson-loop corrections Σ_σ and Σ_π are of order O(N_c^0), suppressed by an overall 1/N_c factor relative to the leading-order quark loop term of order O(N_c)
- The collective mode dispersion relation is E_M^2 = q^2 + 4S^2 δ_{Mσ} for meson channel M
- The region in-between branch cuts for the function F(S,q,ω) is given by [√((Λ_f+q)^2 + S^2) - √(Λ_f^2 + S^2), E_σ]

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-conserving one-meson-loop formalism developed by Nikolov et al. (1996) and Oertel et al. (2000) from vacuum calculations to finite temperature, building on the low-temperature approximation of Florkowski et al. (1996) that only considered the lowest-lying pion pole. The separation into collective and noncollective modes follows the approach of Yamazaki et al. (2013, 2014). The calculation enables future studies of the NJL phase diagram beyond mean field approximation, particularly near the critical region where long-range correlations are important, and complements functional renormalization group approaches applied to the NJL model by Braun et al. (2011) and Aoki et al. (2014).
