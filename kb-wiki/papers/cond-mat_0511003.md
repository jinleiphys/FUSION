# A possibility for precise Weinberg angle measurement in centrosymmetric crystals with axis

> We demonstrate that parity nonconserving interaction due to the nuclear weak charge Q_W leads to nonlinear magnetoelectric effect in centrosymmetric paramagnetic crystals. It is shown that the effect exists only in crystals with special symmetry axis k. Kinematically, the correlation (correction to energy) has the form H_PNC ~ Q_W (E,[B,k])(B,k), where B and E are the external magnetic and electric fields. This gives rise to magnetic induction M_PNC ~ Q_W {k(B,[k,E]) + [k,E](B,k)}. To be specific we consider rare-earth trifluorides and, in particular, dysprosium trifluoride which looks the most suitable for experiment. We estimate the optimal temperature for the experiment to be of a few kelvin. For the magnetic field B = 1 T and the electric field E = 10 kV/cm, the expected magnetic induction is 4 πM_PNC = 0.5 * 10^-11 G, six orders of magnitude larger than the best sensitivity currently under discussion. Dysprosium has several stable isotopes, and so, comparison of the effects for different isotopes provides possibility for precise measurement of the Weinberg angle.

## Key claim

The parity-nonconserving (PNC) weak interaction due to the nuclear weak charge \(Q_W\) produces a nonlinear magnetoelectric effect in centrosymmetric paramagnetic crystals with a special symmetry axis, but the theoretical uncertainty (estimated at 20-50%) prevents direct extraction of \(Q_W\) itself; instead, comparing the effect across different isotopes of dysprosium in dysprosium trifluoride (DyF\(_3\)) could allow a precise determination of the Weinberg angle \(\sin^2\theta_W\).

## Method

The authors derived a single-ion effective PNC Hamiltonian for rare-earth ions in the crystal lattice by rescaling previous calculations for the nuclear anapole moment (Mukhamedjanov and Sushkov 2005), using the ratio \(\langle s_{1/2}|H_W|p_{1/2}\rangle / \langle s_{1/2}|H_a|p_{1/2}\rangle \sim Q_W/\kappa_a\). They then averaged this Hamiltonian over the ground state of the \(4f^n\) configuration for each rare-earth ion, incorporated the crystal field environment of rare-earth trifluorides (RF\(_3\)) using parameters from Morrison and Leavitt (1979), and computed the macroscopic PNC magnetization by taking the derivative of the energy density with respect to the magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* For DyF\(_3\) at \(B = 1\ \text{T}\) and \(E = 10\ \text{kV/cm}\), the predicted PNC magnetic induction is \(4\pi M \sim 0.5 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{G}\).
* For HoF\(_3\) under the same conditions, \(4\pi M \sim 0.1 \times 10^{-11}\ \text{G}\).
* The estimated uncertainty in the Weinberg angle from the isotope comparison is \(\delta s^2 \sim 5 \times 10^{-4}\), based on the neutron skin uncertainty \(\delta R_n \approx 0.058\ \text{fm}\) from the empirical fit of Trzcinska et al. (2001).
* The optimal temperature for the experiment is a few kelvin; the calculation assumes \(T = 1\ \text{K}\) for Kramers ions.
* The number density of rare-earth ions in the compound is \(n \approx 1.8 \times 10^{22}\ \text{cm}^{-3}\), and the dielectric constant is \(\epsilon \approx 14\).

## Context

This work extends the idea of using solids for fundamental symmetry violation searches, originally proposed by Shapiro (1968) for the electron electric dipole moment and later revived by Lamoreaux (2002) for garnet materials. It is similar in spirit to the atomic PNC measurement proposals for dysprosium by Dzuba and Flambaum (1986) and for ytterbium by DeMille (1995), but uses a solid-state magnetoelectric effect in centrosymmetric crystals (rare-earth trifluorides) rather than atomic beams. The predicted PNC magnetization is six orders of magnitude larger than the best sensitivity currently under discussion (Lamoreaux 2002), enabling a potential measurement of the Weinberg angle via isotope comparisons, though the theoretical accuracy is limited to 20-50% for absolute values.
