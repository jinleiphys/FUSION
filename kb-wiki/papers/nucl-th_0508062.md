# Quark Matter 2005 -- Theoretical Summary

> This is a review of the latest developments in the theory of superdense nuclear matter, formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions or in the core of collapsed stars, as they were reported and discussed at the Quark Matter 2005 conference in Budapest (Hungary).

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes theoretical developments presented at the Quark Matter 2005 conference, noting that while experiments have produced an "amazing wealth of new and exciting data," progress in theoretical understanding of quark matter properties has been "modest" and that "definitive theoretical results will require very large investments in time, manpower, and computing resources."

## Method

The author surveys theoretical talks and posters presented at the Quark Matter 2005 conference in Budapest, organizing the discussion into new ideas (thermalization via plasma instabilities, Unruh phenomenon analogies, jet-induced Mach cones, DWBA for pion correlations, soft-hard recombination), established approaches (lattice QCD, fluid dynamics, parton transport, parton saturation, strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma), and the status of heavy ion phenomenology.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature from new lattice calculations: T_c = 186 (3) (3) MeV (N_t = 4) and T_c = 193 (6) (3) MeV (N_t = 6), compared to earlier values of T_c^(N_f=2) = 173 ± 8 MeV and T_c^(N_f=3) = 154 ± 8 MeV
- Entropy per unit of rapidity: dS/dy = 5100 ± 400, corresponding to s = (33 ± 3) fm^{-3} at τ = 1 fm/c
- Diffusion coefficient for charm quarks: D = (3...6)/(2π T), consistent with shear viscosity η/s = (1...2)/(4π T)
- Asymptotic shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory: η/s = (1/4π)[1 + 135 ζ(3)/(8g^2 N_c)^{3/2} + ...]
- Transition energy density from lattice QCD: approximately 1 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to temperature T_c ≈ 170 MeV

## Context

This theoretical summary reviews the state of the field following the RHIC experiments' announcement of a "perfect fluid" with extremely low shear viscosity to entropy ratio. It extends discussions from previous Quark Matter conferences and prior work by Karsch (2002) on lattice QCD transition temperatures, by Arnold, Rebhan, and Dumitru on plasma instabilities, and by Starinets on the conjectured lower bound of η/s. The review identifies open questions including whether the new higher lattice QCD critical temperature values will be confirmed, whether plasma instabilities can explain rapid thermalization, and whether the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma contains long-lived quasiparticles.
