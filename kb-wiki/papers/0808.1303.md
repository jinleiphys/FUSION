# The Density Matrix Renormalization Group and the Nuclear Shell Model

> We summarize recent efforts to develop an angular-momentum-conserving variant of the Density Matrix Renormalization Group method into a practical truncation strategy for large-scale shell model calculations of atomic nuclei. Following a brief description of the key elements of the method, we report the results of test calculations for $^{48}$Cr and $^{56}$Ni. In both cases we consider nucleons limited to the 2p-1f shell and interacting via the KB3 interaction. Both calculations produce a high level of agreement with the exact shell-model results. Furthermore, and most importantly, the fraction of the complete space required to achieve this high level of agreement goes down rapidly as the size of the full space grows.

## Key claim

The angular-momentum-conserving variant of the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (JDMRG) can accurately reproduce exact shell-model results for both \(^{48}\)Cr and \(^{56}\)Ni, but the fraction of the complete space required to achieve this high level of agreement goes down rapidly as the size of the full space grows, suggesting the method may become a practical truncation strategy for larger nuclei.

## Method

The authors implemented a three-block JDMRG strategy that preserves angular momentum throughout the iterative growth, truncation, and renormalization stages, using reduced matrix elements of all sub-operators of the Hamiltonian. They tested the method on \(^{48}\)Cr and \(^{56}\)Ni, assuming a doubly-magic \(^{40}\)Ca core with valence nucleons limited to the 2p-1f shell and interacting via the KB3 interaction, and compared results to exact shell-model diagonalizations.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{48}\)Cr: exact ground-state energy is \(-32.953\) MeV; JDMRG with \(m=180\) yields \(-32.945\) MeV (maximum superblock dimension 11,062 out of 41,355 \(0^+\) states, i.e., about 27% of the full \(0^+\) space).
- For \(^{48}\)Cr: exact \(2^+_1\) energy is \(-32.148\) MeV; JDMRG with \(m=180\) yields \(-32.127\) MeV (dimension 50,030 out of 182,421 \(2^+\) states).
- For \(^{56}\)Ni: exact ground-state energy is \(-78.46\) MeV; JDMRG with \(m=180\) yields \(-78.393\) MeV (maximum superblock dimension 162,019 out of 15,443,684 \(0^+\) states, i.e., about 1% of the full \(0^+\) space).
- For \(^{56}\)Ni: the full m-scheme space contains 1,087,455,228 states; the full \(0^+\) space contains 15,443,684 states.

## Context

This work extends the JDMRG method previously applied to \(^{48}\)Cr by Pittel and Sandulescu (2007) and to the Gamow Shell Model by Ploszajczak and collaborators. It contrasts with the traditional m-scheme DMRG applied to \(^{56}\)Ni by Papenbrock and Dean (2005), which could only achieve ground-state energy accuracy to within 400 keV. The results suggest the JDMRG may enable practical shell-model calculations for nuclei beyond the reach of exact diagonalization, though the authors note that only two data points (\(^{48}\)Cr and \(^{56}\)Ni) exist and more are needed to establish scaling behavior.
