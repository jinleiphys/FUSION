# The proton-neutron resonance states by solving Schrodinger equation

> The proton-neutron interaction is investigated by solving the Schrodinger equation, where a Yukawa type of potential with one pion exchanging between the proton and the neutron is assumed. Since the deutron is the unique bound state of the proton-neutron system, the coupling constant is fixed according to the binding energy of the deutron. The scattering process of the proton and the neutron is studied when the outgoing wave condition is taken into account, and two proton-neutron resonance states are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation, which lie at $1905-i13$MeV and $2150-i342$MeV on the complex energy plane, respectively. It is no doubt that the calculation results would give some hints on the experimental research on the proton-neutron interaction in future.

# The proton-neutron resonance states by solving Schrodinger equation

## Key claim
By solving the Schrodinger equation with a Yukawa-type one-pion-exchange potential and imposing an outgoing wave boundary condition, two proton-neutron resonance states emerge on the complex energy plane; however, these resonances are obtained purely from the zero-points of the second-kind Hankel function, and the paper offers no experimental confirmation or comparison to measured scattering data.

## Method
The authors solve the radial Schrodinger equation in the S-wave approximation for the proton-neutron system, assuming a Yukawa potential with a one-pion-exchange form. The coupling constant is fixed by requiring that the first nonzero zero-point of the Bessel function reproduces the deuteron binding energy. For the scattering problem, the outgoing wave condition is imposed by setting the second-kind Hankel function to zero, and the complex energy poles of the scattering amplitude are identified as resonance states.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.224575 ± 0.000009 MeV, used to fix the coupling constant.
- Order of the Bessel function for the deuteron bound state: ρ = 0.6547.
- First nonzero zero-point of the Bessel function: α = 3.3622.
- Coupling constant in the Yukawa potential: g²/ħc = 0.4203.
- Two proton-neutron resonance states on the complex energy plane: 1905 − i13 MeV and 2150 − i342 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the non-Hermitian quantum mechanics approach of Moiseyev (2011), who studied scattering in a square well using different boundary conditions, to the proton-neutron system with a realistic one-pion-exchange potential. The method builds on the deuteron bound-state treatment of Zhang (2003) and applies the same Hankel-function formalism previously used by Sun et al. (2015) for meson-meson scattering to generate hadronic resonances dynamically. The results suggest an intrinsic relation between bound states and resonance states in the same interaction, but the lack of experimental data on pn resonances means the predictions await future verification.
