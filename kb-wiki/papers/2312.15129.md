# Hyperorder net baryon number fluctuations in nuclear matter at low temperature

> We calculate the density fluctuations of net baryon number up to sixth order induced by the interactions of nuclear matter, and explore their relationship with the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT), including the stable and metastable phase as well as the region far from the phase transition. The results show that dramatic density fluctuations exist in the vicinity of LGPT, and the hyperorder density fluctuations are more sensitive than the lower order ones to the interactions and structural properties of nuclear matter. The study also indicates that, even far away from the critical region of LGPT, the hadronic interactions can still lead to significant hyperorder density fluctuations. In combination with the chemical freeze-out line fitted from the experimental data, the derived results can be referred to investigate the chiral phase transition, nuclear LGPT, as well as the analysis of related experimental signals.

# Hyperorder net baryon number fluctuations in nuclear matter at low temperature

## Key claim
The nonlinear Walecka model predicts that hyperorder (fifth and sixth order) net baryon number fluctuations are more sensitive than lower-order fluctuations to hadronic interactions and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT), and that significant hyperorder fluctuations persist even far from the critical region. The authors note that the ratios of net proton number fluctuations measured by STAR at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV with centrality $0\%-40\%$ are close to their model results at $T=28$ MeV and $\mu_B=864$ MeV, but these conditions differ from the chemical freeze-out conditions extracted for that collision energy ($T \approx 70$ MeV, $\mu_B \approx 750$ MeV), leaving the interpretation open.

## Method
The authors used the nonlinear Walecka model in the mean-field approximation to compute the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter, including $\sigma$, $\omega$, and $\rho$ meson fields with cubic and quartic self-interactions of the $\sigma$ field. From the thermodynamic potential, they calculated the generalized susceptibilities $\chi_n^B$ of net baryon number up to sixth order by taking derivatives of the pressure with respect to $\mu_B/T$, and presented the ratios $\chi_3^B/\chi_1^B$, $\chi_4^B/\chi_2^B$, $\chi_5^B/\chi_1^B$, and $\chi_6^B/\chi_2^B$ as functions of chemical potential, baryon density, and temperature.

## Key numbers
- Critical endpoint of nuclear LGPT: $T_c = 15.9$ MeV, $\mu_c = 909.8$ MeV
- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- Model parameters fitted to: compression modulus $K = 240$ MeV, symmetry energy $a_{sym} = 31.3$ MeV, effective nucleon mass $m^*_N = 0.75 m_N$, binding energy $B/A = -16.0$ MeV at saturation density
- Chemical freeze-out line fit parameters: $a = 0.166$ GeV, $b = 0.139$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c = 0.053$ GeV$^{-3}$ in $T(\mu_B) = a - b\mu_B^2 - c\mu_B^4$
- At $T=28$ MeV and $\mu_B=864$ MeV, the model yields $\chi_4^B/\chi_2^B$, $\chi_5^B/\chi_1^B$, and $\chi_6^B/\chi_2^B$ close to STAR net proton data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3$ GeV

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of density fluctuations up to fourth order in nuclear matter (Poberezhnyuk 2019, Shao 2020, Xu 2023) to hyperorder (fifth and sixth order) fluctuations, motivated by recent STAR measurements of net proton fluctuations up to sixth order at RHIC (Aboona 2023). The study complements van der Waals model calculations of higher-order fluctuations (Vovchenko 2017, Poberezhnyuk 2021) and the double parity model study of second-order susceptibilities near chiral and liquid-gas transitions (Marczenko 2023). The results provide a baseline for interpreting future HADES measurements of higher-order net proton and net charge fluctuations in Au+Au reactions at $0.2A$ to $1.0A$ GeV (Bluhm 2020) and for distinguishing between scenarios for the QCD critical endpoint.
