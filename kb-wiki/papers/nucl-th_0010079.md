# Toy Model for Pion Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions

> We develop a toy model of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions that reproduces some of the features of the chiral Lagrangian calculations. We calculate the production amplitude and examine some common approximations.

# Toy Model for Pion Production in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions

## Key claim
The toy model shows that for final-state interactions, the “fixed kinematics approximation” (using $q_0 = m_\pi/2$ in both the pion propagator and the seagull vertex) is reasonable, but for initial-state interactions the common approximations fail because they miss the important $\pi NN$ three-body branch cut. The authors caution that chiral symmetry is expected to suppress this cut in the real world, so the toy model’s findings are qualitative rather than quantitative.

## Method
The authors construct a solvable toy model with a scalar “pion” field coupled via Yukawa coupling to one nucleon field, a scalar sigma meson to mock up nuclear interactions, and a seagull vertex inspired by chiral $\pi N$ interactions. They evaluate the exact time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) amplitude for pion production at threshold, including sigma exchange as either a final-state or initial-state interaction, and compare the exact result to two common approximations: the “fixed kinematics approximation” (using $q_0 = m_\pi/2$ and a Klein-Gordon propagator) and the “$(E-E’)$ approximation” (using $q_0$ as the difference of nucleon energies).

## Key numbers
- The initial relative momentum between the two nucleons at threshold is $p_i = \sqrt{m_\pi M_N}$, with $m_\pi = 139$ MeV and $M_N = 939$ MeV.
- For final-state interaction with a sigma mass of 550 MeV, the stretched box contributions never exceed 6% of the DWBA piece, for cutoffs ranging from $3m_\pi$ to infinity.
- The fixed kinematics approximation for the final-state interaction deviates from the exact result by no more than 25% across the same cutoff range.
- For initial-state interaction with cutoff $\Lambda = 10 m_\pi$, the $\pi NN$ cut contribution $M_{\pi NN}$ is about 5 times larger than the $NN$ cut contribution $M_{NN}$.
- For initial-state interaction with cutoff $\Lambda = 10 m_\pi$, the stretched boxes are about 30% of the box contributions.

## Context
This work addresses technical ambiguities in chiral effective field theory calculations of near-threshold pion production, specifically the choice of the pion energy variable $q_0$ and the form of the pion propagator in distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations. It extends earlier tree-level calculations by Cohen et al. (1996), Sato et al. (1997), and Hanhart et al. (1998), and loop calculations by Dmitrasinovic et al. (1999) and Ando et al. (2000). The toy model clarifies that the $(E-E’)$ prescription used by Sato et al. (1997) and by Hernandez et al. (1999) is inadequate for initial-state interactions, while the fixed kinematics approximation is reasonable for final-state interactions. The findings motivate further study of the $\pi NN$ three-body cut in realistic calculations with chiral symmetry, and suggest that the unitary transformation method of van Kolck et al. (1998) may provide a proper framework for isolating the different singularities.
