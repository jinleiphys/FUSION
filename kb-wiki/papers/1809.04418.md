# Two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian for chiral and wobbling modes II: Electromagnetic transitions

> The intraband electromagnetic transitions in the framework of collective Hamiltonian for chiral and wobbling modes are calculated. By going beyond the mean field approximation on the orientations of rotational axis, the collective Hamiltonian provides the descriptions on both yrast band and collective excitation bands. For a system with one $h_{11/2}$ proton particle and one $h_{11/2}$ neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rotor ($γ=-30^\circ$), the intraband electromagnetic transitions given by the one-dimensional and two-dimensional collective Hamiltonian are compared to the results by the tilted axis cranking approach and particle rotor model. Compared with the tilted axis cranking approach, the electromagnetic transitions given by the collective Hamiltonian have a better agreement with those by the particle rotor model, due to the consideration of the quantum fluctuations.

## Key claim

The collective Hamiltonian method, by including quantum fluctuations of the orientation angles, yields intraband electromagnetic transition probabilities for chiral and wobbling bands that agree better with the particle rotor model than the tilted axis cranking approach does, but the odd-even staggering of B(M1) values seen in the particle rotor model is not reproduced because angular momentum is not a good quantum number in the collective Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors construct one-dimensional (1DCH) and two-dimensional (2DCH) collective Hamiltonians using the azimuth angle φ (1DCH) or both polar angle θ and azimuth angle φ (2DCH) as collective coordinates, derived from tilted axis cranking (TAC) solutions. They compute intraband M1 and E2 transition probabilities by integrating the semiclassical TAC transition formulas weighted by the squared collective wavefunctions over the angular coordinates. The system studied is a single j-shell model with one h11/2 proton particle and one h11/2 neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rotor with γ = -30°, using the same parameters as in prior work by Frauendorf (1997) and Chen (2013, 2016).

## Key numbers

- The coupling coefficients are Cπ = 0.25 MeV for the proton particle and Cν = -0.25 MeV for the neutron hole.
- The moment of inertia for irrotational flow is J0 = 40 ħ²/MeV.
- The effective g-factors are set to gp - gR = 1 and gn - gR = -1.
- The electric quadrupole moment is Q0 = 1.0 eb.
- The B(M1) values in the TAC drop rapidly to zero around I = 37 ħ, while the 1DCH and 2DCH give non-vanishing B(M1) at high spin due to quantum fluctuations.
- At low spin (I ≤ 10 ħ), the TAC azimuth angle φ is zero (planar solution), but the 2DCH effective azimuth angle φeff is non-zero due to quantum fluctuations.

## Context

This work extends the collective Hamiltonian framework previously developed by Chen (2013, 2014, 2016) for chiral and wobbling modes, which had been applied only to energy spectra, to now include electromagnetic transition probabilities. It compares results from the 1DCH and 2DCH with the tilted axis cranking approach of Frauendorf (1997) and the particle rotor model of Frauendorf (1997) and Koike (2004). The successful description of both energy spectra and EM transitions paves the way for building a collective Hamiltonian based on microscopic tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory as in Zhao (2017).
