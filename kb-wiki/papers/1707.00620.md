# Smoothed square well potential

> The classical square well potential is smoothed with a finite range smoothing function in order to get a new simple strictly finite range form for the phenomenological nuclear potential. The smoothed square well form becomes exactly zero smoothly at a finite distance, in contrast to the Woods-Saxon form. If the smoothing range is four times the diffuseness of the Woods-Saxon shape both the central and the spin-orbit terms of the Woods-Saxon shape are reproduced reasonably well. The bound single particle energies in a Woods-Saxon potential can be well reproduced with those in the smoothed square well potential. The same is true for the complex energies of the narrow resonances.

# Smoothed square well potential

## Key claim

The smoothed square well (SSQW) potential reproduces bound single-particle energies and narrow resonance energies of the Woods-Saxon (WS) potential reasonably well, but only if the smoothing range parameter γ is set to four times the diffuseness a of the WS potential; the SSQW potential has the advantage of being strictly zero at a finite distance with continuous derivatives of all orders, unlike the cut-off Woods-Saxon (CWS) potential which has a discontinuous derivative at the cut-off radius.

## Method

The authors start from the classical square well potential and convolve it with a finite-range weight function w(x) (Eq. 4) that is itself smooth with compact support, producing the SSQW radial shape f^{ssq}(r) via the convolution integral in Eq. (6). The smoothing range γ is set to 4a, where a is the WS diffuseness, so that the SSQW range becomes R_max = R + γ. Bound and resonant single-particle energies are then computed for both the CWS and SSQW potentials using the GAMOW code, with the same central depth V_0, radius R, and spin-orbit strength V_{so} as the WS potential, and compared for an A=50 nucleus and for 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- For an A=50 nucleus with WS parameters V_0 = 50 MeV, R = 4.6 fm, a = 0.65 fm, the smoothing parameter is γ ≈ 2.6 fm (= 4a), giving SSQW range R_max = 7.2 fm.
- For bound states in the A=50 nucleus, the average deviation of SSQW single-particle energies from WS energies is about 220 keV, compared to 330 keV for the earlier SV potential.
- For 208Pb, the minimum of the sum of squared deviations Δ(V_{so}) occurs at V_{so} = 16.5 MeV, which is exactly the spin-orbit strength used in the WS potential.
- The CWS potential typically requires R_max larger than R + 6a, while the SSQW range is R + γ = R + 4a, reducing the integration domain.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier introduction of the SV potential (Salamon and Vertse, 2008), which was also a strictly finite-range form but required fitting its parameters to the WS potential and had a spin-orbit shape that differed considerably from the WS shape. The SSQW potential avoids these drawbacks: its parameters (V_0, R, γ) are in direct relation to WS parameters with no fitting needed, and its spin-orbit strength is the same as the WS value. The paper also references the work of Nándori (2013) who used a similar compactly supported smooth regulator function in quantum electrodynamics. The SSQW form enables unambiguous definition of the nuclear interaction range and eliminates the dependence of broad resonance energies on the arbitrary cut-off radius that plagues the CWS potential, as previously noted by Salamon and Vertse (2014).
