# Joint transverse momentum and threshold resummation beyond NLL

> To describe the transverse momentum spectrum of heavy color-singlet production, the joint resummation of threshold and transverse momentum logarithms is investigated. We obtain factorization theorems for various kinematic regimes valid to all orders in the strong coupling, using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory. We discuss how these enable resummation and how to combine regimes. The new ingredients in the factorization theorems are calculated at next-to-leading order, and a range of consistency checks is performed. Our framework goes beyond the current next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy (NLL).

## Key claim

The paper develops a framework for the joint resummation of threshold and transverse momentum logarithms in heavy color-singlet production using Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), going beyond the current next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, but the framework requires assuming a relative power counting between the threshold parameter \(1-z\) and transverse momentum \(p_T \gg \Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}\) to derive factorization theorems for three distinct kinematic regimes.

## Method

The authors derive factorization theorems for three kinematic regimes depending on the relative size of the threshold parameter \(1-z\) and transverse momentum \(p_T\): regime 1 where \(1 \sim 1-z \gg p_T/Q\) (transverse momentum factorization), regime 2 where \(1 \gg 1-z \gg p_T/Q\) (intermediate regime requiring collinear-soft degrees of freedom in SCET+), and regime 3 where \(1 \gg 1-z \sim p_T/Q\) (threshold factorization). The new ingredients in the factorization theorems, including the collinear-soft functions and the double-differential soft function, are calculated at next-to-leading order (NLO), and consistency checks between regimes are performed using renormalization group equations and anomalous dimensions.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop TMD soft function contains terms proportional to \(\frac{\alpha_s C_i}{\pi^2} \left[ -\frac{1}{\mu^2} \mathcal{L}_1\left(\frac{p_T^2}{\mu^2}\right) + \frac{2}{\mu^2} \mathcal{L}_0\left(\frac{p_T^2}{\mu^2}\right) \ln\frac{\nu}{\mu} - \frac{\pi^2}{12} \delta(p_T^2) \right]\)
- The one-loop collinear-soft function is \(\frac{\alpha_s C_i}{\pi^2} \left[ \frac{1}{\mu} \mathcal{L}_0\left(\frac{p^-}{\mu}\right) \frac{1}{\mu^2} \mathcal{L}_0\left(\frac{p_T^2}{\mu^2}\right) - \frac{1}{\mu^2} \mathcal{L}_0\left(\frac{p_T^2}{\mu^2}\right) \ln\frac{\nu}{\mu} \delta(p^-) \right]\)
- The double-differential soft function at one-loop contains terms with \(\ln^2(2p^0/\mu)\) and \(\mathrm{Li}_2(-e^{-2a})\) where \(a = \mathrm{arccosh}(p_0/p_T)\)
- The non-cusp anomalous dimension of the collinear-soft function \(\gamma_{\mathcal{S}}^i(\alpha_s)\) vanishes up to two-loop order

## Context

This work extends the joint resummation formalism developed by Laenen, Sterman, and Vogelsang (2000) which achieved NLL accuracy using Mellin moments and impact parameters, and has been applied to prompt-photon, electroweak, Higgs boson, heavy-quark, slepton pair, and gaugino pair production. The SCET-based framework enables going beyond NLL by using collinear-soft (csoft) degrees of freedom in SCET+, as recently employed for joint resummations by Bauer, Fleming, and others (2011), Procura, Waalewijn, and Zeune (2014), and Larkoski, Marzani, and Thaler (2015). The results enable NNLL joint resummation for color-singlet production at hadron colliders, with the factorization theorems providing an all-order relation between the anomalous dimensions of the thrust soft function and threshold soft function.
