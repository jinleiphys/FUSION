# Scrutiny of the new class of three-nucleon forces

> In a recent publication, Cirigliano {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. Lett. 135, 022501 (2025)] argue that three-nucleon forces (3NFs) involving short-range operators that couple two pions with two nucleons are enhanced beyond what is expected in chiral effective field theory based on naive dimensional analysis. Here, we scrutinize the arguments and conclusions of that paper by taking into account renormalization scheme dependence of the corresponding low-energy constants. We gain further insights into the expected impact of these 3NFs by comparing them with contributions of similar type, induced by pion-exchange diagrams at lower orders in the chiral expansion. We also estimate the impact of these 3NFs on properties of nuclear matter. After removal of scheme-dependent short-distance components in pion loops, the 3NFs considered by Cirigliano {\it et al.} are shown to yield reasonably small contributions to the equation of state of neutron and symmetric nuclear matter in agreement with expectations based on Weinberg's power counting.

## Key claim

After removing scheme-dependent short-distance components from pion loops, the three-nucleon forces (3NFs) involving short-range operators that couple two pions with two nucleons, claimed by Cirigliano et al. (2025) to be enhanced by a factor of \(Q^{-2}\) beyond naive dimensional analysis, yield reasonably small contributions to the equation of state of neutron and symmetric nuclear matter, consistent with expectations based on Weinberg's power counting, provided one adopts renormalization conditions appropriate to the Weinberg scheme rather than the Kaplan-Savage-Wise scheme.

## Method

The authors critically reexamine the renormalization group (RG) arguments of Cirigliano et al. (2025) by analyzing the scheme dependence of the low-energy constant \(D_2\) that accompanies the quark-mass-dependent derivative-less NN contact interaction. They compare the scaling of \(D_2\) in the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) scheme (where \(\mu \sim p\)) and the Weinberg scheme (where \(\mu \sim \Lambda_b\)), and estimate its magnitude using RG running supplemented with numerical values from state-of-the-art chiral NN potentials (Reinert et al., 2017; Epelbaum et al., 2022). They then compare the novel 3NFs with parameter-free N\(^3\)LO and N\(^4\)LO contributions of similar type induced by three-pion exchange diagrams, and estimate the impact on the equation of state of pure neutron and symmetric nuclear matter using regularized \(t\)-channel dispersion relations to remove short-range components from pion loops.

## Key numbers

*   In the Weinberg scheme with renormalization scale \(\mu \sim \Lambda = 450\) MeV, the estimated magnitude of the low-energy constant \(D_2\) is \(|D_2| \sim 0.4\) fm\(^4\).
*   Using the semilocal momentum-space regularized (SMS) chiral NN potentials of Reinert et al. (2017) with cutoff \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV, the extracted value of \(C_0 = (4\pi)^{-1} \tilde C_{1S0}^{\rm np} = 0.3\) fm\(^2\) leads to an estimated \(|D_2| \sim 0.03\) fm\(^4\); for \(\Lambda = 500\) MeV, \(C_0 = 1.7\) fm\(^2\) gives \(|D_2| \sim 0.8\) fm\(^4\).
*   The authors adopt \(|D_2| \sim 1\) fm\(^4\) as a realistic upper bound for this LEC in calculations using Weinberg's power counting.
*   In the KSW scheme, the expected magnitude is \(|D_2| \sim 3.9\) fm\(^4\), close to the range \(|D_2| \lesssim 4\) fm\(^4\) employed by Cirigliano et al. (2025) in their numerical estimations.

## Context

This work directly challenges the claim by Cirigliano et al. (2025) that certain two-pion-exchange-contact 3NFs are enhanced to N\(^3\)LO rather than N\(^5\)LO, which would pressure the mainstream application of chiral effective field theory to nuclear systems (reviewed by Epelbaum et al., 2008; Machleidt, 2011). The authors show that the apparent enhancement arises from adopting the KSW renormalization scheme (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998), whereas in the Weinberg scheme (Weinberg, 1990, 1991) with \(\mu \sim \Lambda_b\), the LEC \(D_2\) scales naturally and the 3NFs remain small. This enables continued use of the standard NDA-based hierarchy for nuclear forces and clarifies that the convergence pattern of chiral EFT for three-nucleon forces is consistent with expectations when proper renormalization conditions are applied.
