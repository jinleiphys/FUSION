# Di-lepton production from a single photon in strong magnetic fields: Vacuum dichroism

> We study di-lepton production from a single photon in the presence of a strong constant magnetic field. By the use of the Ritus-basis formalism, we analytically evaluate the photon--to--di-lepton conversion vertex with fully taking into account the non-perturbative interactions between the produced fermions and the strong magnetic field. We show that the di-lepton spectrum becomes anisotropic with respect to the magnetic-field direction and depends on the photon polarization as a manifestation of the vacuum dichroism in a strong magnetic field. According to the energy conservation in the presence of the Landau quantization, not only the transverse momentum of the produced fermions but also the longitudinal momentum is discretized, and the di-lepton spectrum exhibits spike structures as functions of the incident photon energy and the magnetic field strength. We also show that the di-lepton production is strictly prohibited for massless fermions in the lowest Landau levels as an analogue of the so-called helicity suppression.

## Key claim

The paper shows that di-lepton production from a single photon in a strong constant magnetic field is possible only because the magnetic field non-perturbatively modifies the fermion dispersion relation via Landau quantization, and that the resulting di-lepton spectrum is anisotropic and exhibits spike structures as functions of the incident photon energy and magnetic field strength, with the production strictly prohibited for massless fermions in the lowest Landau levels.

## Method

The authors used the Ritus-basis formalism, which maps the Dirac equation in an external magnetic field into a free Dirac equation, to analytically evaluate the photon-to-di-lepton conversion vertex with all-order Landau levels. They treated the interaction with the external magnetic field non-perturbatively while treating the dynamical photon field perturbatively at leading order, and evaluated the lepton tensor and squared amplitude in the Landau gauge, verifying gauge invariance via the Ward identity.

## Key numbers

- The energy level of a charged fermion in the magnetic field is given by \(\epsilon_n \equiv \sqrt{ m^2 + 2 n |qB| + p_z^2 }\), where \(n=0,1,2,\ldots\) is the Landau level quantum number and \(B\) is the magnetic field strength.
- The scalar form factor \(\Gamma_{n,n'}\) contains the factor \(e^{- \frac{1}{2} |\bar{\bm k}_\perp|^2}\) where \(|\bar{\bm k}_\perp|^2 \equiv |{\bm k}_\perp|^2 / (2|qB|)\).
- The squared amplitude includes a factor \((2\pi)^3 \delta^{(2)}( k_\parallel - p_\parallel - p_\parallel') \delta(k_y - p_y + p'_y)\) enforcing energy-momentum conservation in the longitudinal and \(y\)-directions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Hattori and collaborators (2012) that showed the Landau-level structure appearing in the complex refractive index, providing the most general analytic form of the photon-to-di-lepton conversion rate with all-order Landau levels. The differential cross section computed here gives more information than the imaginary part of the refractive index (which corresponds to the integrated cross section) and opens a new avenue to study energy and momentum distributions of di-leptons as signatures of vacuum dichroism, which is complementary to vacuum birefringence. The results are relevant for experimental detection in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as in astronomical systems such as neutron stars and magnetars.
