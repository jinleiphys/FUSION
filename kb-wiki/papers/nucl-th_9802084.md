# The Status of the Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constant

> A review is given of the various determinations of the different piNN coupling constants in analyses of the low-energy pp, np, pbarp, and pi-p scattering data. The most accurate determinations are in the energy-dependent partial-wave analyses of the NN data. The recommended value is f^2 = 0.075 . A recent determination of f^2 by the Uppsala group from backward np cross sections is shown to be model dependent and inaccurate, and therefore completely uninteresting. We also argue that an accurate determination of f^2 using pp forward dispersion relations is not a realistic option.

## Key claim
The recommended charge-independent value of the pion-nucleon coupling constant is $f^2 = 0.075$, derived from energy-dependent partial-wave analyses of nucleon-nucleon (NN) data. The authors argue that a recent determination by the Uppsala group using backward np cross sections is model dependent and inaccurate, and that an accurate determination using pp forward dispersion relations is not a realistic option.

## Method
The authors review determinations of the $\pi NN$ coupling constants from energy-dependent partial-wave analyses (PWA) of low-energy $pp$, $np$, $\overline{p}p$, and $\pi p$ scattering data, primarily performed by the Nijmegen group. They compare these results with older determinations from dispersion relations and single-energy PWA, and critically examine the Chew extrapolation method applied to backward $np$ scattering data.

## Key numbers
- Recommended charge-independent value: $f^2 = 0.075$ (or $g^2 = 13.5$).
- From the 1997 Nijmegen $pp$ PWA (1955 data, 20 parameters): $f_p^2 = 0.0753(5)$.
- From the 1993 Nijmegen $pp$ and $np$ PWA (4301 data, 39 parameters): $f^2 = 0.0749(4)$.
- From the 1993 Nijmegen $np$ PWA alone (2512 data): $f_c^2 = 0.0748(3)$.
- From the 1997 Nijmegen $\overline{p}p$ PWA (3847 data, 36 parameters): $f_c^2 = 0.0736(10)$.
- From the Los Alamos-Groningen $\pi N$ PWA (1092 $\pi^+p$ data, 14 parameters): $f_c^2 = 0.0743(8)$.
- The Uppsala group's backward $np$ data (31 data) have a $\chi^2 = 291.6$ with respect to standard PWA.

## Context
This review by de Swart, Rentmeester, and Timmermans (1998) summarizes the work of the Nijmegen group, which consistently found a lower value for the coupling constant ($f^2 \approx 0.075$) than the previously standard value of $f^2 = 0.079(1)$ from Bugg et al. (1973) and Koch and Pietarinen (1980). The paper argues that energy-dependent PWA of thousands of data points are vastly superior to single-energy PWA or extrapolation methods like the Chew method proposed by Chew (1958). The results enable future checks of charge-independence breaking in the coupling constants, as predicted by models such as the QCD sum rules of Meissner and Henley (1996).
