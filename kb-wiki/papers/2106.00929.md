# Effect of the pion field on the distributions of pressure and shear in the proton

> In light of recent experimental progress in determining the pressure and shear distributions in the proton, these quantities are calculated in a model with confined quarks supplemented by the pion field required by chiral symmetry. The incorporation of the pion contributions is shown to account for the long-range distributions, in general agreement with the experimentally extracted quark contributions. The results of the model are also compared with lattice QCD results at unphysically large quark mass.

## Key claim

The pion cloud contribution to the proton's pressure and shear distributions, calculated within the cloudy bag model (CBM), accounts for the long-range behavior and reproduces the qualitative features of experimental data, but the comparison with lattice QCD at unphysically large quark mass (mπ = 450 MeV) shows some discrepancy for radii beyond 0.8 fm, suggesting further work is needed to determine whether this difference arises from the input quark and gluon distributions in the pion or the nucleon calculations themselves.

## Method

The authors calculate the pressure and shear distributions in the proton using the cloudy bag model, where the nucleon is treated as a three-quark MIT bag surrounded by a perturbative pion cloud. The valence quark contribution from the bare bag is computed using the static spherical cavity approximation, while the pionic contribution is obtained as a convolution of the distribution of pions in the nucleon (Dπ/N) with the quark and gluon D-terms of the pion (Dπq,g) taken from lattice QCD calculations. The bag EMT is evolved to μ² = 1.4 GeV² and μ² = 4 GeV² for comparison with experimental data and lattice QCD results, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The pion cloud contribution to the D-term of the nucleon is computed using the product form DπNq,g(t) = Dπ/N(t) Dπq,g(t), where Dπ/N is calculated from the CBM vertex function with bag radius R = 0.8 fm, axial charge gA = 1.27, and pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV.
- The Nπ and Δπ components of the nucleon wave function have probabilities of order 20-25% and 10%, respectively.
- The mechanical radius ⟨r²mech⟩ calculated in the model at mπ = 450 MeV is 0.42 fm², compared to lattice QCD results of 0.51 fm² (z-expansion) and 0.57 fm² (tripole) at the same pion mass.
- The quark D-term form factor of the proton was extracted from DVCS experiment at JLab at scale μ² = 1.39 GeV².
- The lattice QCD calculations of gluon GFFs were performed at mπ = 0.45 GeV and scale μ² = 4 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the MIT bag model calculations of Neubelt et al. (2019) by adding the pion cloud required by chiral symmetry, and provides the first chiral calculation of pressure and shear in the nucleon using the cloudy bag model. The results are compared with the experimental extraction of quark pressure and shear by Burkert et al. (2018, 2021) from JLab DVCS data, and with lattice QCD results at unphysically large quark mass from Shanahan et al. (2018). The model enables future comparisons with more precise experimental data from JLab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and can be improved by generating approximate eigenstates of momentum to smooth the discontinuity at the bag radius.
