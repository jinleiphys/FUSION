# Jet modification in three dimensional fluid dynamics at next-to-leading twist

> The modification of the single inclusive spectrum of high transverse momentum ($p_T $) pions emanating from an ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision is investigated. The deconfined sector is modelled using a full three dimensional (3-D) ideal fluid dynamics simulation. Energy loss of high $p_T$ partons and the ensuing modification of their fragmentation is calculated within perturbative QCD at next-to-leading twist, where the magnitude of the higher twist contribution is modulated by the entropy density extracted from the 3-D fluid dynamics simulation. The nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) for pions with a $p_T \geq 8$ GeV as a function of centrality as well as with respect to the reaction plane is calculated. The magnitude of contributions to the differential $R_{AA}$ within small angular ranges, from various depths in the dense matter is extracted from the calculation and demonstrate the correlation of the length integrated density and the $R_{AA}$ from a given depth. The significance of the mixed and hadronic phase to the overall magnitude of energy loss are explored.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a next-to-leading twist perturbative QCD energy loss calculation, when combined with a realistic three-dimensional ideal fluid dynamics simulation of the medium, can reproduce the centrality and transverse momentum dependence of the nuclear modification factor for pions with pT >= 8 GeV in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, but only after tuning a free parameter (the maximum transport coefficient q̂0) to fit a single data point.

## Method
The authors model the deconfined medium using a full three-dimensional ideal relativistic fluid dynamics simulation with an equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition, starting at τ0 = 0.6 fm. The energy loss of high-pT partons and the modification of their fragmentation functions are computed within the higher-twist formalism of perturbative QCD, where the transport coefficient q̂ is assumed proportional to T3 times a factor accounting for the hadronic phase (cHG). The single free parameter q̂0 is tuned so that the RAA at pT = 10 GeV in 0-5% centrality equals 0.2, and all other results are predictions.

## Key numbers
- The maximum temperature in the fluid dynamics simulation is T0 = 0.405 GeV.
- The fitted value of q̂0 is approximately 1.3 GeV2/fm for gluon jets (with cHG = 1).
- The RAA for central Au-Au collisions (0-5% centrality) at pT = 10 GeV is set to 0.2 by construction.
- The RAA for 0-5% centrality at pT >= 8 GeV shows a reduction of almost a factor of 5 compared to binary-scaled p-p collisions.
- The entropy density ratio between QGP and hadronic phases near the phase transition is approximately sQGP ~ 5 sHG.
- The RAA is computed in angular bins of 15 degrees with respect to the reaction plane for 20-30% centrality.
- The transverse plane is divided into longitudinal strips of width 1 fm perpendicular to the impact parameter for depth-dependent analysis.

## Context
This work extends earlier jet-quenching calculations that used simplified functional forms for the medium density (e.g., ρ ~ ρ0(τ0/τ)) or one-dimensional hydrodynamics (Renk, 2006) by incorporating a full three-dimensional ideal fluid dynamics simulation (Nonaka, 2006) that was previously fitted to soft-sector data. It contrasts with the approach of Hirano (2002) which used a realistic medium but treated energy loss in a simplified manner, and with the quenching-weights approach of Salgado (2003) applied to 3-D media by Renk (2006) which reformulated the medium in terms of an effective length. The results enable future studies to place bounds on the ratio of transport coefficients between hadronic and partonic phases, and to use differential observables like RAA versus reaction plane to constrain the initial density profile of the quark-gluon plasma.
