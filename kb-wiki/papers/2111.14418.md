# On the $QQ\bar q\bar q$-Quark Potential in String Models

> We propose a string theory construction for the system of two heavy quarks and two light antiquarks. The potential of the system is a function of separation between the quarks. We define a critical separation distance below which the system can be thought of mainly as a compact tetraquark. The results show the universality of the string tension and factorization at small separations expected from heavy quark-diquark symmetry. Our estimate of the screening length is in the range of lattice QCD. We also make a comparison with the potential of the $QQq$ system. The potentials look very similar at small quark separations but at larger separations they differ. The reason for this is that the flattening of the potentials happens at two well-separated scales as follows from the two different mechanisms: string breaking by light quarks for $QQq$ and string junction annihilation for $QQ\bar q\bar q$. Moreover, a similar construction can also be applied to the $\bar Q\bar Q qq$ system.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a string theory construction for the \(QQ\bar q\bar q\) system and defines a critical separation distance below which the system behaves as a compact tetraquark; the claim is hedged by the fact that the construction relies on a specific AdS/QCD model with parameters fitted to lattice data, and the analysis is limited to the case of two light quarks of equal mass.

## Method

The author constructs static string configurations in a five-dimensional deformed AdS geometry (soft-wall model) using Nambu-Goto strings, baryon/antibaryon vertices with action \(S_{\text{v}} = \tau_v \int dt \, e^{-2\s r^2}/r\), and light (anti)quarks represented by a constant tachyon field with action \(S_{\text{q}} = \text{T}_0 R \int dt \, e^{\frac{\s}{2} r^2}/r\). The potential is obtained by minimizing the total action over the positions of vertices and light antiquarks, yielding parametric expressions for the energy \(E_{QQ\bar q\bar q}\) as a function of heavy-quark separation \(\ell\). The analysis considers four regimes of the connected configuration (small, slightly larger, intermediate, and large \(\ell\)) and compares with disconnected configurations representing decay products; the final potential is defined as the minimum of the connected energy and twice the meson energy, with a two-state mixing Hamiltonian used to interpolate between them.

## Key numbers

* String tension parameter \(\s = 0.450\,\text{GeV}^2\) fixed from the Regge trajectory of \(\rho(n)\) mesons.
* Fitted parameter \(\g = 0.176\) from matching the string tension \(\sigma\) to lattice QCD data in Bulava (2019).
* Tachyon parameter \(\n = 3.057\) adjusted to reproduce the string breaking distance \(\boldsymbol{\ell}_{Q\bar Q} = 1.22\,\text{fm}\) for \(u,d\) quarks from Bulava (2019).
* Baryon vertex parameter \(\k\) is set to the upper bound \(\k = \km = -\frac{1}{4} e^{1/4}\) (approximately \(-0.284\)), the closest allowed value to the phenomenologically motivated \(\k = -0.102\).
* The energy difference \(E_{\qqq} - E_{\QQq} + E_{\qQb} \approx 1.445\,\text{GeV}\) makes the disconnected configuration \(QQq + \bar q\bar q\bar q\) irrelevant for the ground state.
* The energy difference \(E_{\Qqqq} - E_{\Qqb} \approx 1.24\,\text{GeV}\) makes the disconnected configuration \(Qq\bar q\bar q + Q\bar q\) irrelevant.
* The discrepancy between \(E_{QQ\bar q\bar q}(\ell)\) and \(E_{\QQq}(\ell) + E_{\Qqq} - E_{\qQb}\) does not exceed \(42\,\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the string theory approach to tetraquarks previously applied to the \(QQq\) system by Andreev (2021) and to the three-quark potential by Andreev (2008), using the same soft-wall AdS/QCD model with metric \(ds^2 = e^{\s r^2} (R^2/r^2)(dt^2 + d\vec x^2 + dr^2)\). The construction follows the hadro-quarkonium picture of Voloshin (2008) and is motivated by the LHCb discovery of the double-charm tetraquark \(T_{cc}^+\) in 2021. The results enable future potential-model studies of doubly heavy tetraquark bound states and can be extended to the \(\bar Q\bar Q qq\) system.
