# Emission source functions in heavy ion collisions

> The three-dimensional pion and kaon emission source functions are extracted from the HKM model simulations of the central Au+Au collisions at the top RHIC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The model describes well the experimental data, previously obtained by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations using the imaging technique. In particular, the HKM reproduces the non-Gaussian heavy tails of the source function in the pair transverse momentum (out) and beam (long) directions, observed in the pion case and practically absent for kaons. The role of the rescatterings and long-lived resonances decays in forming of the mentioned long range tails is investigated. The particle rescatterings contribution to the out tail seems to be dominating. The model calculations also show the substantial relative emission times between pions (with mean value 14.5 fm/c in LCMS), including those coming from resonance decays and rescatterings. The prediction is made for the source functions in the LHC Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, which are still not extracted from the measured correlation functions.

## Key claim

The hydrokinetic model (HKM) reproduces the non-Gaussian heavy tails observed in pion source functions from PHENIX and STAR data, and the particle rescatterings contribution to the out-direction tail appears to be dominating over long-lived resonance decays, contrary to some earlier interpretations that emphasized resonance halo effects.

## Method

The hybrid hydrokinetic model (hHKM) simulates central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV by combining ideal hydrodynamics with a lattice-QCD inspired equation of state, followed by a gradual decoupling stage that switches to the UrQMD hadronic cascade at the chemical freeze-out isotherm Tch = 165 MeV. The three-dimensional pion and kaon source functions are extracted directly from the event-generator output by computing pair separation distributions in the pair rest frame, using the Bertsch-Pratt coordinate system with out, side, and long directions. The model parameters (maximal initial energy density ε0 = 430 GeV/fm³ and initial transverse flow coefficient α = 0.45 fm) are fitted to reproduce experimental mean charged particle multiplicity and one-particle spectra from the 20% most central collisions.

## Key numbers

- Mean relative emission time between pions in LCMS: 14.5 fm/c, including contributions from resonance decays and rescatterings
- Maximal initial energy density for Au+Au at √sNN = 200 GeV: ε0 = 430 GeV/fm³
- Initial transverse flow coefficient α = 0.45 fm, giving maximal initial transverse velocity at the periphery of 0.05
- Chemical freeze-out isotherm temperature for switching to UrQMD cascade: Tch = 165 MeV
- Early thermalization assumed at proper time τ = 0.1 fm/c
- Predictions made for 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV at LHC

## Context

This work extends the source imaging analyses performed by PHENIX (2005) and STAR (2009) collaborations, which extracted pion and kaon source functions from experimental correlation data but could not isolate the physical mechanisms producing the non-Gaussian tails. The HKM approach improves upon simpler event generators such as Therminator (which uses Blast-Wave parametrization without rescatterings) and Hadronic Rescattering Calculations (HRC, which includes only 8 resonance species and assumes evolution from t=0). The results enable more reliable interpretation of femtoscopic data and provide model source functions that can be used in Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical models for extracting strong interaction parameters between exotic particles. The predictions for LHC energies await experimental verification from correlation function measurements.
