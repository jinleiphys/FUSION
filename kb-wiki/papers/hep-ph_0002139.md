# Numerical Solution of the Spinless Salpeter Equation by a Semianalytical Matrix Method (a Mathematica 4.0 routine)

> In quantum theory, the so-called "spinless Salpeter equation," the relativistic generalization of the nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation, is used to describe both bound states of scalar particles and the spin-averaged spectra of bound states of fermions. A numerical procedure solves the spinless Salpeter equation by approximating this eigenvalue equation by a matrix eigenvalue problem with explicitly known matrices.

## Key claim

The paper presents a semianalytical matrix method for solving the spinless Salpeter equation that avoids numerical integration by using explicitly known matrices, but the method is demonstrated only for spherically symmetric potentials of generalized power-law form and requires separate calculation of kinetic-energy matrices for each value of orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The spinless Salpeter equation in configuration space is converted into a finite-dimensional matrix eigenvalue problem by expanding the reduced radial wave function in a basis of Laguerre-polynomial functions. The kinetic-energy matrix elements are computed analytically for orbital angular momenta ℓ=0 and ℓ=1 using explicit formulas involving sums over combinatorial factors and double factorials, while the potential matrix elements for power-law potentials become linear combinations of analytically known "power matrices." The resulting matrix equation is solved numerically using a Mathematica 4.0 routine called Salpeter.nb.

## Key numbers

- For a harmonic-oscillator potential V(r)=a r² with m=1 GeV and a=0.5 GeV³, the 1S ground-state eigenvalue converges to 3.82493 GeV (exact value) as matrix size increases; the matrix method with d=25 gives 3.82494 GeV.
- The 2S eigenvalue converges to 5.79102 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 5.79112 GeV.
- The 3S eigenvalue converges to 7.48208 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 7.48323 GeV.
- The 4S eigenvalue converges to 9.00749 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 9.01617 GeV.
- For ℓ=1, the 1P eigenvalue converges to 4.90145 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 4.90149 GeV.
- The 2P eigenvalue converges to 6.69305 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 6.69298 GeV.
- The 3P eigenvalue converges to 8.28464 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 8.28585 GeV.
- The 4P eigenvalue converges to 9.74276 GeV (exact); the matrix method with d=25 gives 9.74304 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the semianalytical matrix approach previously developed by Lucha (1992) for solving the spinless Salpeter equation, which itself is a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation used in quark potential models for hadron spectroscopy (Lucha, 1991). The method enables efficient computation of bound-state energies and wave functions for relativistic two-body systems with power-law or exponentially damped power-law potentials, and the provided Mathematica routine allows practitioners to apply it without performing numerical integrations.
