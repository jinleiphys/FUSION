# Momentum Shell in Quarkyonic Matter from Explicit Duality: A Dual Model for Cold, Dense QCD

> We present a model of cold QCD matter that bridges nuclear and quark matter through the duality relation between quarks and baryons. The baryon number and energy densities are expressed as functionals of either the baryon momentum distribution, $f_{\rm B}$, or the quark distribution, $f_{\rm Q}$, which are subject to the constraints on fermions, $0 \le f_{\rm B,Q} \le 1$. The theory is ideal in the sense that the confinement of quarks into baryons is reflected in the duality relation between $f_{\rm Q}$ and $f_{\rm B}$, while other possible interactions among quarks and baryons are all neglected. The variational problem with the duality constraints is formulated and we explicitly construct analytic solutions, finding two distinct regimes: A nuclear matter regime at low density and a Quarkyonic regime at high density. In the Quarkyonic regime, baryons underoccupy states at low momenta but form a momentum shell with $f_{\rm B}=1$ on top of a quark Fermi sea. Such a theory describes a rapid transition from a soft nuclear equation of state to a stiff Quarkyonic equation of state. At this transition, there is a rapid increase in the pressure.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an analytically solvable "IdylliQ" (Ideal Dual Quarkyonic) model of cold, dense QCD matter that bridges nuclear and quark matter through a duality relation between baryon and quark momentum distributions, finding that at high density baryons underoccupy low-momentum states but form a fully occupied momentum shell on top of a quark Fermi sea. The model describes a rapid transition from a soft nuclear equation of state to a stiff Quarkyonic equation of state, but the authors note that the continuity of thermodynamic quantities holds only up to the first derivative, with the baryon susceptibility and sound speed exhibiting a discontinuity at saturation.

## Method

The authors formulate a variational problem to minimize the energy density functional for a given baryon density, using the duality relation between baryon and quark momentum distributions as a constraint. They choose a specific single-quark momentum distribution in a baryon (an exponential form that is the inverse of a linear differential operator) to reduce the global constraint problem to one determining a few global constants. The optimized distributions are found by patching together local solutions from variational calculations and boundary conditions (0 ≤ f ≤ 1), requiring continuity up to the first derivative at junction points.

## Key numbers

- The saturation of quark states first occurs at q=0 when k_F ≈ √(2/N_c) Λ, or n_B/n_0 = 2.58 × (Λ/0.4 GeV)^3 for N_c=3, where n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3} is normal nuclear density.
- For Λ = 0.4 GeV, the transition from the ideal baryon gas regime to the post-saturation regime occurs at n_B/n_0 ≈ 2.6.
- At large momentum (k_sh > Λ), the baryon momentum shell thickness approaches Δ_B ≈ Λ/N_c^2 + Λ^2/(N_c k_sh).
- The singular part of the sound speed near saturation scales as v_s^2 ~ (1/N_c^3) √(k_sat/δk), where δk = k_sh - k_sat.
- For causal sound speed, the required smearing width of the baryon Fermi surface is k_dif ≳ N_c^{-3} k_sat.

## Context

This work extends the Quarkyonic matter concept originally developed in the large-N_c limit of QCD by McLerran (2007) and later by McLerran and Reddy (2018), providing a dynamical description that makes full use of the duality between baryons and quarks previously explored by Kojo (2021). The model produces an equation of state that rapidly stiffens from low to high density, consistent with observed neutron star properties including a rapid rise in sound speed and vanishing trace anomaly as discussed by Masuda (2012), Kojo (2014), Fujimoto (2017-2021), and others. The IdylliQ model provides a baseline for theories of Quarkyonic matter that can be perturbed by interaction functionals, and the authors note that extensions for multi-flavors and multi-baryon species will be discussed in forthcoming papers.
