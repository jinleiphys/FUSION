# Charmonium correlation and spectral functions at finite temperature

> We study the properties of charmonium states at finite temperature in quenched QCD on isotropic lattices. We measured charmonium correlators using non-perturbatively $\cO(a)$ improved clover fermions on fine ($a=0.01$ fm) lattices with a relatively large size of $128^{3}\times 96$, $128^3\times48$, $128^3\times32$ and $128^3\times24$ at $0.73~T_c$, $1.46~T_c$, $2.20~T_c$ and $2.93~T_c$, respectively. Our analysis suggests that $\Jpsi$ is melted already at $1.46~T_c$ and $η_c$ starts to dissolve at $1.46~T_c$ and does not exist at higher temperatures. We also identify the heavy quark transport contribution at the spectral function level for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the J/psi is already melted at 1.46 Tc and the eta_c starts to dissolve at 1.46 Tc and does not exist at higher temperatures, but the analysis of correlator ratios alone leaves ambiguity: the vector channel ratio after removing zero-mode contributions is near unity at 1.46 Tc, which could indicate survival up to that temperature, while the spectral function analysis favors melting already at 1.46 Tc.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using the standard Wilson plaquette action for gauge fields and non-perturbatively O(a) improved clover fermions for charm quarks on isotropic lattices with lattice spacing a = 0.01 fm. They measured charmonium correlation functions in the pseudoscalar (PS) and vector (V_ii) channels on 128^3 x N_tau lattices at four temperatures (0.73, 1.46, 2.20, 2.93 Tc) and extracted spectral functions using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) with a modified kernel to explore low-energy behavior.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a = 0.01 fm, inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 18.974 GeV
* Lattice volumes: 128^3 x 96 at 0.73 Tc, 128^3 x 48 at 1.46 Tc, 128^3 x 32 at 2.20 Tc, 128^3 x 24 at 2.93 Tc
* In the PS channel at the largest distance, the ratio G/G_rec deviates from unity by about 5% at 1.46 Tc, 8% at 2.20 Tc, and 12% at 2.93 Tc
* In the V_ii channel at the largest distance, the ratio G/G_rec deviates from unity by about 16% at 1.46 Tc, comparable to deviations at higher temperatures
* After removing zero-mode contributions, the vector channel ratio at 1.46 Tc is approximately unity at all distances
* The heavy quark diffusion constant DT is roughly 0.28 at 1.46 Tc (precise determination deferred to further study)

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of charmonium at finite temperature (Ding 2009) and addresses the long-standing question of charmonium dissolution as a QGP signal proposed by Satz (1986). The authors introduce a new exact relation (generalizing Meyer 2010) that allows direct reconstruction of correlators at one temperature from data at another temperature without needing to determine the spectral function first. The results contradict potential model approaches (Mocsy 2009) that suggest higher dissociation temperatures, and provide first-principles lattice input for understanding heavy quark transport, enabling future calculations of the heavy quark diffusion constant.
