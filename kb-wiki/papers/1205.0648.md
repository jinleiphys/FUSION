# Transverse mass spectra and scaling of hadrons at RHIC and LHC energies

> We present a systematic study of transverse mass spectra ($m_T$) of mesons and baryons at RHIC and LHC energies. In an earlier study, it was shown that all the mesons produced in p+p and d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV follow $m_T$ scaling while in Au+Au collisions at same energy the mesons with strange and charm quark contents do not follow $m_T$ scaling which can be attributed to medium modifications. We extend this study for baryons produced in all above colliding systems at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Although all available baryon spectra behave differently from mesons but are found to scale with protons for both p+p and d+Au collisions. In case of Au+Au collisions, the strange baryons behave differently from protons. This study has also been performed on the meson spectra in p+p collisions at 62.4 GeV which lies in between the highest RHIC energy and SPS energy where the $m_T$ scaling was first observed and we arrive at same conclusions as those at 200 GeV. We test the $m_T$ scaling at LHC with the first meson spectra measured in p+p collisions at 900 GeV and find that the $m_T$ spectra of kaons and $ϕ$ do not scale with pions. Thus $m_T$ scaling which was working well up to p+p collisions at 200 GeV seems to be broken. Here we use all data measured in the mid rapidity region. Baryon to meson ratio is studied as a function of $m_T$, at different energies and for different systems and is found that this ratio for p+p at 900 GeV is quite different from that in p+p collisions at RHIC energies.

# Transverse mass spectra and scaling of hadrons at RHIC and LHC energies

## Key claim

The authors find that m_T scaling, which held for mesons in p+p collisions up to 200 GeV, appears broken at LHC energies: in p+p collisions at 900 GeV, the m_T spectra of kaons and phi mesons do not scale with pions. For baryons at 200 GeV, all available baryon spectra scale with protons in p+p and d+Au collisions, but in Au+Au collisions the strange baryons behave differently from protons.

## Method

The authors fit measured pion and proton transverse mass spectra using a modified Hagedorn function (exponential at low p_T, power law at high p_T). They then apply m_T scaling by using the pion fit function to predict meson spectra (kaons, phi, K_S^0) and the proton fit function to predict baryon spectra (Lambda, Xi, Omega, Delta^{++}), with a relative normalization factor S obtained by fitting the measured hadron spectra. The analysis uses published data from PHENIX, STAR, and ALICE collaborations for p+p collisions at 62.4, 200, and 900 GeV, d+Au collisions at 200 GeV, and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV for four centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- For p+p at 200 GeV, the relative normalization S for Lambda/proton is 0.785 +/- 0.021; for Xi/proton it is 0.140 +/- 0.030; for Omega/proton it is 0.060 +/- 0.022
- For p+p at 900 GeV, the relative normalization S for Lambda/proton is 0.93 +/- 0.2; for Xi/proton it is 0.2 +/- 0.02
- For p+p at 900 GeV, the relative normalization S for K/pi is 0.424 +/- 0.02; for K_S^0/pi it is 0.44 +/- 0.02; for phi/pi it is 0.23 +/- 0.02
- For p+p at 62.4 GeV, the relative normalization S for K/pi is 0.38 +/- 0.02
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV (10-20% centrality), the relative normalization S for Omega/proton is 0.16 +/- 0.008
- The proton to pion ratio for p+p at 900 GeV is found to be quite low compared to that in p+p collisions at RHIC energies

## Context

This work extends a prior study by the same group (Khandai et al., 2011) which showed that mesons follow m_T scaling in p+p and d+Au collisions at 200 GeV but not in Au+Au collisions. The present study adds baryon scaling analysis at 200 GeV and tests m_T scaling at LHC energies using the first ALICE data from p+p collisions at 900 GeV. The m_T scaling concept was first observed by the WA80 collaboration in S+S collisions at 200 AGeV. The results enable systematic comparisons of hadron production mechanisms across energies from SPS through RHIC to LHC, and the authors note that similar studies at 2.76 TeV and 7 TeV would be valuable once data tables become available.
