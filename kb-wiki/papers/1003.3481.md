# Production of exotic atoms at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

> We study in details the space-time dependence of the production of muonic, pionic, and other exotic atoms by the coherent photon exchange between nuclei at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN. We show that a multipole expansion of the electromagnetic interaction yields an useful insight of the bound-free production mechanism which has not been explored in the literature. Predictions for the spatial, temporal, and angular distribution, as well as the total cross sections, for the production of exotic atoms are also included.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a multipole expansion of the electromagnetic interaction provides new insight into the bound-free production mechanism of exotic atoms in ultra-peripheral collisions, and that the electric dipole (E1) multipolarity alone dominates the production cross section at LHC energies due to the very large Lorentz factor, with contributions from E2 and M1 multipolarities being much smaller.

## Method

The authors use first-order perturbation theory with the Lienard-Wiechert potential to calculate the production amplitude for bound-free pair production, where a negative particle (muon, pion, antiproton, etc.) is captured into a K-shell orbit around a proton or lead nucleus. They separate the electromagnetic interaction into distant collisions (using a multipole expansion of the potential) and close collisions (using a separate potential form), and compute the production probability as a function of impact parameter, time, and the energy and angle of the emitted positive particle. The cross sections are obtained by integrating over impact parameters and using distorted hydrogenic wavefunctions for the captured particle and plane waves with Coulomb corrections for the free positive particle.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at the LHC, the effective Lorentz factor is approximately Γ ≈ 10^8; for Pb-Pb collisions, Γ ≈ 10^7.
- The Bohr radius for a muonic atom is a_muon = 255 fm.
- The production probability at zero impact parameter is 5 orders of magnitude smaller than at b = a_muon.
- The impact parameter region b > a_muon contributes at least a factor 100 more to the cross section than the region b < a_muon.
- Cross sections for exotic atom production in pp collisions: anti-hydrogen 63.4 pb, muonic 44.8 × 10^(-4) pb, pionic 21.3 × 10^(-4) pb, kaonic 1.3 × 10^(-4) pb, ρ-atom 0.51 × 10^(-4) pb, protonium 0.09 × 10^(-4) pb.
- Cross sections for exotic atom production in Pb-Pb collisions: anti-hydrogen 132 b, muonic 0.16 mb, pionic 0.09 mb, kaonic 4.3 μb, ρ-atom 1.3 μb, protonium 0.3 μb.
- The maximum impact parameter for muon and pion capture is b_max ≈ 10^8 fm; for proton-antiproton capture, b_max ≈ 10^7 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of bound-free pair production used to create anti-hydrogen at CERN (Oerter 1996) and Fermilab (Blanford 1998), and builds on the theoretical framework of Bertulani and Baur (1998) for electron-positron pair production with capture. The paper provides the first detailed space-time and angular distribution analysis for exotic atom production at LHC energies, showing that while pp collisions yield negligible rates, Pb-Pb collisions should produce abundant exotic atoms, enabling experimental studies of muonic atoms, pionic atoms, and protonium to test quantum electrodynamics and quantum chromodynamics.
