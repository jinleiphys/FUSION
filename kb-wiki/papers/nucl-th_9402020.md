# Microscopic multicluster description of the neutron-rich helium isotopes

> The neutron halo structure of $^{6}$He and of $^{8}$He is studied in a microscopic three-body and five-body model, respectively. Various cluster arrangements are included to embody a variety of correlations between the clusters. The intercluster wave function is determined with the stochastic variational method. The $^{6}$He and $^{8}$He energies are reproduced with the same effective force very well. The radii and densities are compared with the results of empirical analyses. The calculated difference of the neutron and proton radii exceeds 0.8 fm, confirming the thick neutron skin. The two-neutron removal spectroscopic amplitudes are calculated and used for evaluating the momentum distribution and the $β$ decay spectrum, which are sensitive to the halo structure. A fair agreement with experiment is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully microscopic multicluster model, using a simple central nucleon-nucleon interaction with no free parameters for $^{8}$He after fixing the $\alpha$-particle radius and the interaction strength $u$ to reproduce $^{6}$He, can reproduce the ground-state energies, radii, and neutron-skin thickness of $^{6}$He and $^{8}$He, but the model overbinds $^{8}$He by about 200 keV and gives a smaller matter radius for $^{8}$He than for $^{6}$He, contrary to empirical estimates.

## Method

The authors describe $^{6}$He and $^{8}$He as a microscopic three-body ($\alpha$ + n + n) and five-body ($\alpha$ + n + n + n + n) system, respectively, using the stochastic variational method to select basis states from randomly generated sets of harmonic oscillator width parameters. The trial wave function is a superposition of multiple cluster arrangements (different Jacobi coordinate sets) and partial waves, with the intercluster antisymmetrizer applied, and the central effective interaction of Thompson, LeMere and Tang is used with the Coulomb force neglected.

## Key numbers

- $^{6}$He ground-state energy: theoretical -0.994 MeV, experimental -0.975 MeV (overbound by 20 keV).
- $^{8}$He ground-state energy: theoretical -3.301 MeV, experimental -3.112 MeV (overbound by about 200 keV).
- $^{4}$He ground-state energy: theoretical -25.243 MeV, experimental -28.30 MeV.
- Calculated rms matter radius of $^{6}$He: 2.46 fm; empirical estimates: 2.33 fm (Ref. [4]) and 2.48 fm (Ref. [15]).
- Calculated rms matter radius of $^{8}$He: 2.40 fm; empirical estimates: 2.49 fm (Ref. [4]) and 2.52 fm (Ref. [15]).
- Calculated difference of neutron and proton rms radii: 0.87 fm for $^{6}$He and 0.82 fm for $^{8}$He; empirical estimates from Ref. [skin] are 0.87 fm for $^{6}$He and 0.93 fm for $^{8}$He.
- Two-neutron removal spectroscopic factor norm: 1.39 for $^{6}$He and 1.36 for $^{8}$He.
- Two-neutron removal reaction cross sections on carbon target: 189 +/- 14 mb for $^{6}$He and 202 +/- 17 mb for $^{8}$He.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic multicluster study (Varga, Suzuki, Ohbayasi, previous paper) by adjusting the $\alpha$-particle size parameter to a realistic value and including higher partial waves and additional cluster arrangements. It contrasts with the cluster-orbital shell model (COSM) and three-body models (e.g., Zhukov, 1993) that use a passive-core-plus-outer-nucleons approximation, and with earlier microscopic cluster models that treat at most three clusters. The results enable further tests of halo wave functions through comparison with momentum distributions from two-neutron removal reactions and $\beta$-decay spectra, and they highlight the need for more sophisticated effective interactions or a reanalysis of radius extraction from interaction cross section data.
