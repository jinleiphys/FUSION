# Critical view of the claimed $Θ^+$ pentaquark

> We use a theoretical model of the $γ~d \to ~K^+ K^- ~n ~p $ reaction adapted to the experiment done at LEPS where a peak was observed and associated to the $Θ^{+}(1540)$ pentaquark. The study shows that the method used in the experiment to associate momenta to the undetected proton and neutron, together with the chosen cuts, necessarily creates an artificial broad peak in the assumed $K^+ n$ invariant mass in the region of the claimed $Θ^{+}(1540)$. It is shown that the LEPS fit to the data, used to make the claim of the $Θ^{+}(1540)$, grossly distorts the background. An alternative fit, assuming a background plus a fluctuation, returns a background practically equal to the theoretical one and a fluctuation identical to the one seen in the experimental $K^- p$ spectrum of 2$σ$ significance.

## Key claim

The peak in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass distribution observed by the LEPS experiment and attributed to the $\Theta^+(1540)$ pentaquark is an artifact created by the combination of the minimum momentum spectator approximation (MMSA) used to assign momenta to undetected nucleons and the specific kinematic cuts applied, rather than evidence for a genuine resonance.

## Method

The authors constructed a theoretical model of the $\gamma d \to K^+ K^- n p$ reaction that includes $\phi$ production on the proton and neutron, $\Lambda(1520)$ production on the proton, and kaon rescattering. They simulated the LEPS experimental setup by generating events at twenty random photon energies between 2 GeV and 2.4 GeV, imposing the LEPS angular cut (kaon angles no larger than 20 degrees relative to the incident photon), the MMSA prescription for undetected nucleon momenta, and the same mass cuts used to remove $\phi$ production events. The resulting $K^+ n$ invariant mass distribution was then compared to the LEPS data and to the LEPS collaboration's own fits.

## Key numbers

- The LEPS experiment observed a peak around 1526 MeV in the $K^+ n$ invariant mass distribution.
- The accumulated strength of the three bins constituting the claimed $\Theta^+$ peak over the theoretical "exact" curve is about 35 events.
- A similar peak-and-dip structure appears in the $K^- p$ mass distribution around 1650 MeV with an accumulated strength of about 33 events, which the LEPS collaboration dismissed as a statistical fluctuation of about $2\sigma$ significance.
- The LEPS experiment considered events with effective photon energy $E^{eff}_{\gamma}$ between 2000 MeV and 2500 MeV, and imposed a cut requiring $|p_{min}| < 100$ MeV for the spectator nucleon momentum.

## Context

This work extends the critical re-examination of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark claim following the initial observation by Nakano et al. (2003) in the $\gamma\, ^{12}C \to K^+ K^- X$ reaction. The LEPS experiment on a deuteron target (Nakano et al., 2009) reported a peak around 1526 MeV, but a higher-statistics experiment with complete kinematics by McKinnon et al. (2006) found no evidence for the $\Theta^+$. The present study demonstrates that the MMSA prescription combined with the LEPS cuts necessarily produces an artificial broad peak, and that the claimed signal has the same statistical significance (about $2\sigma$) as a fluctuation in the $K^- p$ spectrum that the LEPS collaboration themselves dismissed as not physical. This enables a reinterpretation of the LEPS data as consistent with no pentaquark signal.
