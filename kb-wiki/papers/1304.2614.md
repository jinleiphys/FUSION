# One-pion production in neutrino-nucleus collisions

> We use our model for neutrino pion production on the nucleon to study pion production on a nucleus. The model is conveniently modified to include in-medium corrections and its validity is extended up to 2 GeV neutrino energies by the inclusion of new resonant contributions in the production process. Our results are compared with recent MiniBooNE data measured in mineral oil. Our total cross sections are below data for neutrino energies above 1 GeV. As with other theoretical calculations, the agreement with data improves if we neglect pion final state interaction. This is also the case for differential cross sections convoluted over the neutrino flux.

## Key claim

The model's total cross sections for one-pion production in neutrino-nucleus collisions fall below MiniBooNE data for neutrino energies above approximately 1 GeV, and agreement with data improves systematically if pion final state interaction (FSI) is neglected, a pattern also seen in other theoretical calculations.

## Method

The authors extend their existing nucleon-level model for neutrino pion production, originally developed for the Delta resonance region, up to 2 GeV neutrino energies by adding direct and crossed D13(1520) resonance pole terms. In-medium corrections are incorporated via a local density approximation, including Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, and a modified Delta propagator with a density-dependent self-energy parameterized from Oset (1987). Pion final state interactions are simulated using the code of Salcedo (1987), which accounts for P- and S-wave absorption and quasielastic scattering, with probabilities evaluated in nuclear matter and applied to finite nuclei via the local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for CC pi+ production on mineral oil fall below MiniBooNE data for neutrino energies above approximately 0.9 GeV.
- Differential cross section dsigma/dT_pi for CC pi+ production is underestimated for pion kinetic energies above 0.15 GeV when FSI is included.
- Differential cross section dsigma/dp_pi for CC pi0 production shows better agreement with data without FSI for pion momenta above 0.2 GeV/c, but agreement improves below 0.2 GeV/c when FSI is included.
- For NC pi0 production, the model without FSI shows good agreement with data, while the full model shows a depletion in the momentum region 0.25-0.5 GeV/c.
- The nuclear saturation density used is rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-level model of Hernandez (2007, 2010) to nuclear targets, adding the D13 resonance contribution following Leitner (2008). The results are compared to MiniBooNE data from Aguilar-Arevalo (2010) and are consistent with the findings of Sobczyk (2012) and Lalakulich (2012), who also find that neglecting FSI improves agreement with data. The authors note that the same FSI model gives a fair reproduction of pion photoproduction data, leaving the source of disagreement in neutrino-induced reactions as an open question. The introduction of a formation time/zone, as done by Golan (2012), is suggested as a possible way to reduce the impact of FSI and improve agreement.
