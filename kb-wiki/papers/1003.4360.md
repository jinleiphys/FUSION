# Qualitative aspects of the models of $n\bar{n}$ transition in nuclei

## Key claim

The two alternative models for neutron-antineutron ($n\bar{n}$) transitions in nuclei give radically different results because the process is extremely sensitive to the description of the neutron state and the antineutron self-energy; the paper argues that model 1 (diagram technique with propagators) is inapplicable to the problem, while model 2 (using wave functions) is more rigorous but yields results that span many orders of magnitude depending on how the antineutron self-energy is treated.

## Method

The paper compares two diagrammatic models for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclei followed by annihilation. Model 1 uses a neutron propagator in the $n\bar{n}$ transition vertex and describes the process in momentum representation, leading to a probability proportional to $\Gamma t$. Model 2 uses neutron wave functions (plane wave or bound state) in the initial state and a hermitian Hamiltonian for $\bar{n}$-medium interaction; depending on whether the antineutron self-energy $\Sigma$ is included in the propagator, the probability is either proportional to $t^2$ (infrared-divergent case) or to $\Gamma t / \Sigma^2$ (non-singular case).

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time from model 1: $\tau^1_{\text{min}} = (0.7-2.5) \times 10^{8}$ s.
- Lower limit from model 2 with antineutron self-energy included: $\tau^b_{\text{min}} = (3.5-7.5) \times 10^{8}$ s.
- Lower limit from model 2 without antineutron self-energy (infrared-divergent case): $\tau^a_{\text{min}} = 10^{16}$ yr.
- The realistic limit $\tau_{\text{min}}$ is stated to lie in the range $\tau^a_{\text{min}} > \tau_{\text{min}} > \tau^b_{\text{min}}$.
- Model 1 probability: $W_1(t) = \epsilon^2 / (6 E_n^2) \, \Gamma t$, where $\epsilon = 1/\tau_{n\bar{n}}$ and $\Gamma$ is the $\bar{n}B$ annihilation width.
- Model 2 probability (infrared-free approach): $W_a(t) \approx \epsilon^2 t^2$, equal to the free-space $n\bar{n}$ transition probability.
- Model 2 probability (with self-energy): $W_b(t) \approx \epsilon^2 / \Sigma^2 \, \Gamma t$.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate on $n\bar{n}$ transition calculations in nuclear matter, which began with potential-model treatments by earlier authors (references 1-10 in the paper). The author previously proposed model 1 in Nazaruk (2009, reference 11) and it was repeated for deuteron by another group (reference 12), but was later criticized by the same author in Nazaruk (2010, reference 13) as unsuitable because the neutron line should be a wave function, not a propagator. The paper contrasts with the recent manuscript (reference 16) that attempted to repeat the earlier calculations; the author argues that model 1 is infrared-free for any process and therefore cannot reproduce the infrared singularity that exists for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in both medium and vacuum.
