# Space- and time-like electromagnetic form factors of the $\mathbfΩ$ baryon

> We present predictions for the elastic electromagnetic form factors of the $Ω$ baryon in both space-like and time-like regions, computed within a confining, symmetry-preserving framework based on a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact interaction. The calculation is performed in the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, combined with a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the three-quark bound state. The $Ω$ baryon, composed solely of strange quarks, provides a particularly clean environment in which to investigate the role of quark mass and SU(3)-flavor symmetry in shaping baryon structure. Within this approach, the electromagnetic current is constructed consistently with the Ward-Takahashi identity, yielding four independent form factors associated with the electric monopole, magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole, and magnetic octupole moments. We compute these form factors over a broad kinematic domain and analyze their behavior in both space-like and time-like regions. The resulting static electromagnetic moments and multipole form factors of the $Ω$ baryon exhibit a visible sensitivity to the dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment, particularly in the magnetic and higher-order multipole sectors. The time-like form factors are obtained through asymptotic analytic continuation of the corresponding space-like solutions, allowing the construction of the effective form factor and its comparison with available experimental data and recent phenomenological analyses.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for the elastic electromagnetic form factors of the Ω baryon in both space-like and time-like regions, computed within a symmetry-preserving contact-interaction framework. The results exhibit a visible sensitivity to the dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment, particularly in the magnetic and higher-order multipole sectors, but the time-like form factors are obtained through a model-dependent asymptotic analytic continuation that does not capture the complex phase structure from intermediate hadronic states.

## Method

The calculation employs a vector⊗vector contact interaction within the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, combined with a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the three-quark bound state. The Ω baryon is treated in a quark-diquark picture dominated by axial-vector diquark correlations with flavor content {ss}1+, and the electromagnetic current is constructed consistently with the Ward-Takahashi identity, yielding four independent form factors associated with the electric monopole, magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole, and magnetic octupole moments. Time-like form factors are obtained through asymptotic analytic continuation of the corresponding space-like solutions using a shift of 2mΩ² in the arguments.

## Key numbers

- Ω baryon mass: mΩ = 1.76 GeV (slightly higher than the empirical mass of 1.67 GeV)
- Constituent strange-quark mass: M = 0.53 GeV, obtained with current quark mass mq = 0.17 GeV
- Axial-vector diquark mass: m{ss}1+ = 1.26 GeV
- Effective coupling for quark exchange: gΩ = 1.56
- Charge radius: rΩ = 0.555 ± 0.026 fm (consistent with lattice-QCD determinations)
- Magnetic dipole moment: μ̂Ω = (−2.742 ± 0.359) μN (in nuclear magnetons)
- Electric quadrupole moment: Q̂Ω = (+0.0211 ± 0.0039) fm²
- Magnetic octupole moment: ÔΩ = (−0.344 ± 0.052) ON (in units of the nucleon octupole moment)
- The quark-photon dressing function PT(Q²) exhibits a pole at Q² = −1.262 GeV², consistent with the φ-meson pole at mφSCI = 1.13 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier contact-interaction investigations of baryon structure by Wilson (2011), Segovia (2014), Raya (2021), and Albino (2025) to the Ω baryon, providing a consistent description of elastic form factors across both space-like and time-like kinematic domains. The results are compared with lattice-QCD calculations by Boinepalli (2009) and Alexandrou (2010) in the space-like region, as well as continuum three-body Bethe-Salpeter analyses by Sanchis-Alepuz (2013) and phenomenological analyses by Ramalho (2020) and Fu (2025) in the time-like region. The framework enables future studies of baryon structure within a symmetry-preserving continuum approach, though the time-like predictions remain model-dependent and would benefit from explicit treatment of meson-cloud effects and unitarity constraints.
