# Generalized Jaynes-Cummings Model with Intensity-Dependent and Non-resonant Coupling

> We study the intensity-dependent and nonresonant Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian when the field is described by an arbitrary shape-invariant system. We determine the eigenstates, eigenvalues, time evolution matrix and the population inversion matrix factor.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact eigenstates, eigenvalues, time evolution matrix, and population inversion factor for a generalized Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian where the field is described by any shape-invariant system (not just a harmonic oscillator) and the coupling is both intensity-dependent and non-resonant; the authors note that the intensity-dependent and intensity-independent generalized Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonians share the same eigenstates in the resonant limit.

## Method

The authors start from a generalized Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian with intensity-dependent coupling and detuning, expressed in terms of operators that satisfy the shape invariance condition from supersymmetric quantum mechanics. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian by finding common eigenstates of the free and interaction parts, using the B-operator formalism and the algebraic approach to supersymmetric quantum mechanics developed in their prior work (Aleixo, Balantekin, Ribeiro, preceding paper). They then solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation to obtain the evolution matrix and derive the population inversion matrix factor, verifying two limiting cases: the intensity-dependent resonant limit and the standard harmonic-oscillator intensity-dependent Jaynes-Cummings limit.

## Key numbers

- Eigenvalues of the generalized Hamiltonian: \(E_m^{(\pm)} = {\cal E}_{m+1} \pm \sqrt{\alpha^2 {\cal E}_{m+1}^2 + \hbar^2\Delta^2}\), where \({\cal E}_{m+1}\) are the eigenvalues of the shape-invariant system, \(\alpha\) is the coupling constant, and \(\Delta\) is the detuning parameter.
- In the resonant limit (\(\Delta = 0\)): \(E_m^{(\pm)} = (1 \pm \alpha)\,{\cal E}_{m+1}\).
- In the standard harmonic oscillator limit: \(E_m^{(\pm)} = (m+1)\hbar\omega \pm \hbar\sqrt{\alpha^2\omega^2 (m+1)^2 + (\omega-\omega_o)^2}\), where \(\omega\) is the field frequency and \(\omega_o\) is the atomic transition frequency.
- The coefficients in the standard limit satisfy \(C_{m+1}^{(\pm)} = \gamma_m^{(\pm)}C_m^{(\pm)} = C_m^{(\mp)} = 1/\sqrt{1 + (\gamma_m^{(\mp)})^2}\), with \(\gamma_m^{(\pm)} = \sqrt{1 + \delta_m^2} \mp \delta_m\) and \(\delta_m = (\omega-\omega_o)/[\alpha\omega (m+1)]\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior generalization of the Jaynes-Cummings model (Aleixo, Balantekin, Ribeiro, preceding paper) from intensity-independent to intensity-dependent coupling, while also incorporating non-resonant effects. The standard Jaynes-Cummings model (Jaynes and Cummings, 1963) and its intensity-dependent variants (Buck and Sukumar, 1981; Singh, 1982; Buzek, 1989; Buzek and Jex, 1990) are recovered as special cases when the field is a harmonic oscillator. The results provide exactly solvable coupled-channel problems that can be used to test approximate methods for coupled-channel calculations in nuclear physics.
