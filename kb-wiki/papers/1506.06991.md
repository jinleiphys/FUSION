# The concept of nuclear cluster forbiddenness

> In nuclear cluster systems, a rigorous structural forbiddenness of virtual nuclear division into unexcited fragments is obtained. We re-analyze the concept of forbiddenness, introduced in Ref. 1 for the understanding of structural effects in nuclear cluster physics. We show that the concept is more involved than the one presented previously, where some errors were committed. Due to its importance, it is reanalyzed here. In the present contribution a simple way for the determination of forbiddenness is given, which may easily be extended to any number of clusters, though in this contribution we discuss only two-cluster systems, for illustrative reasons. A simple rule is obtained for the minimization of the forbiddenness, namely to start from a cluster system with a large SU(3) irrep (lambda,mu), but minimizing (lambda-mu), i.e. the system has to be oblate. The rule can be easily implemented in structural studies, done up to now with an oversimplified definition of forbiddenness. The new method is applied to various systems of light clusters and to some decay channels of 236U and 252Cf.

# The concept of nuclear cluster forbiddenness

## Key claim
The paper reanalyzes the concept of "forbiddenness" in nuclear cluster systems and claims that the original definition by Smirnov contained errors, specifically that the forbiddenness for the ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca → ⁸⁰Zr system is 40 rather than the previously reported value of 20. The authors provide a corrected analytical formula for computing forbiddenness that depends on the compactness and oblateness of the two-cluster system before adding relative motion quanta.

## Method
The authors derive a new analytical expression for the forbiddenness n_c (the minimal number of oscillator quanta needed to excite the cluster system) using SU(3) group theory and Young diagram multiplication rules. They apply the method to two-cluster systems by coupling the excited cluster irrep (λ_c+λ_0, μ_c+μ_0) with the relative motion irrep (n_r,0) to obtain the parent nucleus irrep (λ,μ), then minimize n_c = λ_0+2μ_0 subject to constraints from the Wildermuth condition. The method is applied to light cluster systems and to fission channels of ²³⁶U and ²⁵²Cf using the pseudo-SU(3) model for heavy nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca → ⁸⁰Zr: forbiddenness n_c = 40 (correcting the previously reported value of 20 from Smirnov)
- For ²⁰Ne+¹⁶O → ³⁶Ar: forbiddenness n_c = 8
- For ¹²C+⁸Be → ²⁰Ne: forbiddenness n_c = 0
- For ²³⁶U decay channels: forbiddenness ranges from n_c = 0 (⁴He+²³²Th) to n_c = 36 (¹³²Sn+¹⁰⁴Mo)
- For ²⁵²Cf decay channels: forbiddenness ranges from n_c = 0 (⁴He+²⁴⁸Cm and ¹⁶O+²³⁶Th) to n_c = 28 (multiple channels including ¹⁰⁰Zr+¹⁵²Ce and ¹⁰⁴Mo+¹⁴⁸Ba)

## Context
This work extends and corrects the concept of cluster forbiddenness originally introduced by Smirnov for understanding structural effects in nuclear cluster physics. The corrected formalism builds on the Semimicroscopic Algebraic Cluster Model (SACM) developed by the authors and collaborators, and provides a tool for understanding suppression and enhancement of cluster distributions in fission and fusion processes. The results are qualitatively consistent with cluster radioactivity theories by Sandulescu, Poenaru, and Gupta, showing that forbiddenness increases with the mass of the lightest cluster. The method can be applied to heavy nuclei using pseudo-SU(3) extensions and has implications for spectroscopic factor calculations in algebraic models as developed by Serban and Draayer.
