# Modification of $Z^0$ leptonic invariant mass in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions as a measure of the electromagnetic field

> An extraordinary strong magnetic field, $eB_0 \approx 10^{18}$ Gauss, is expected to be generated in non-central ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions and it is envisaged to induce several effects on hot QCD matter including the possibility of local parity and local parity and charge conjugation symmetry violations. A direct signature of such e.m. fields and a first quantitative measurement of its strength and lifetime are still missing. We point out that both the mean value of leptonic invariant mass of $Z^0$ boson, reconstructed by its decaying lepton pairs, and the relative width are modified in relativistic heavy ion collisions due to the presence of strong initial e.m. fields. We propose a measurement of the leptonic invariant mass of $Z^0$ as a novel probe of the strength of the $B_y$. Both shifts could be up to about few hundred MeV and are found to depend on the integral of $B_y$ over the time duration quadratically (approximate). Hence it provides a novel and clear probe of electromagnetic fields, which can be tested experimentally.

## Key claim

The mean value of the leptonic invariant mass of the $Z^0$ boson decreases and its width increases in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions due to the presence of strong initial electromagnetic fields, with both shifts reaching up to about a few hundred MeV. The shifts depend approximately quadratically on the time integral of the magnetic field component \(B_y\), providing a novel quantitative probe of the electromagnetic field strength and lifetime.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo method to generate \(Z^0\) bosons with a Breit-Wigner invariant mass distribution (mass \(M_0 = 91.1876\) GeV, width \(\Gamma = 2.4952\) GeV) and momentum distributions fitted to LHC data for 5.02 TeV PbPb collisions at 20-30% centrality. The decay lepton pairs were propagated through parameterized electromagnetic fields (\(B_y\) and \(E_x\)) derived from a Gaussian transverse profile and a power-law time decay \(B(\tau) = eB_0 / (1 + (\tau/\tau_B)^a)\), with parameters varied over a wide range. The reconstructed invariant mass distribution of the \(Z^0\) from the lepton pairs was then compared to the vacuum distribution to compute the shift in mean mass \(\Delta \langle M \rangle\) and the change in width \(\Delta \sigma\).

## Key numbers

- The initial magnetic field strength is \(eB_0 = 73\, m_\pi^2\) (equivalent to \(eB_0 \approx 10^{18}\) Gauss) for 5.02 TeV PbPb collisions at impact parameter \(b = 7.5\) fm.
- For the reference field parameters (\(eB_0 = 73 m_\pi^2\), \(\tau_B = 0.4\) fm/\(c\), \(a = 1\)), the mean invariant mass decreases by \(\Delta \langle M \rangle \approx -250\) MeV and the width increases by \(\Delta \sigma \approx 300\) MeV.
- Varying \(eB_0\) from \(73 m_\pi^2/5\) to \(73 m_\pi^2\) (with \(\tau_B = 0.4\) fm/\(c\), \(a = 1\)) gives \(\Delta \langle M \rangle\) from \(-9.9\) MeV to \(-246\) MeV, and \(\Delta \sigma\) from 12.6 MeV to 305 MeV.
- Varying \(\tau_B\) from 0.05 fm/\(c\) to 0.4 fm/\(c\) (with \(eB_0 = 73 m_\pi^2\), \(a = 1\)) gives \(\Delta \langle M \rangle\) from \(-7.46\) MeV to \(-246\) MeV, and \(\Delta \sigma\) from 10.2 MeV to 305 MeV.
- Varying \(a\) from 1 to 3 (with \(eB_0 = 73 m_\pi^2\), \(\tau_B = 0.4\) fm/\(c\)) gives \(\Delta \langle M \rangle\) from \(-246\) MeV to \(-27.9\) MeV, and \(\Delta \sigma\) from 305 MeV to 44.1 MeV.
- The width shift \(\Delta \sigma\) follows \(\Delta \sigma = k_\sigma (\int_{\tau_0}^{\tau_1} d\tau eB(\tau))^2\) with \(k_\sigma = 6.44 \times 10^{-3}\) for all configurations explored.
- The mean mass shift follows \(\Delta \langle M \rangle = k_M (\int_{\tau_0}^{\tau_1} d\tau eB(\tau))^n\) with \(n = 2.16 \pm 0.16\) and \(k_M\) varying between \(-2.69 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(-5.17 \times 10^{-3}\).
- The effect of lepton-quark scattering in QGP gives \(\Delta \langle M \rangle = -1.9\) MeV and \(\Delta \sigma \le 0.2\) MeV, negligible compared to the electromagnetic field effect.
- Centrality dependence: \(\Delta \langle M \rangle\) increases from \(-64\) MeV to \(-340\) MeV and \(\Delta \sigma\) from 79 MeV to 423 MeV as centrality increases from 5% to 45%.

## Context

This work extends the search for direct probes of the strong electromagnetic fields generated in non-central heavy ion collisions, which have been studied via directed flow splitting of charged hadrons (Gursoy 2014, Gursoy 2018), heavy meson pairs such as \(D^0\) and \(\overline{D}^0\) (Das 2016, Chatterjee 2018, Sun 2020), and leptons from \(Z^0\) decay (Sun 2020). The proposed measurement of the \(Z^0\) leptonic invariant mass distribution is significantly easier than measuring the directed flow splitting of its decay leptons. The results enable experimental tests at the LHC and could help constrain the time evolution of the magnetic field, which is crucial for understanding the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2007, Fukushima 2008) and related phenomena such as the splitting of \(\Lambda\) polarization (Becattini 2016, Han 2017, Guo 2019).
