# General classification and analysis of neutron beta-decay experiments

> A method for the general analysis of the sensitivities of neutron beta-decay experiments to manifestations of possible deviations from the Standard model is proposed. In a consistent fashion, we take into account all known (radiative and recoil) corrections which are incorporated within the Standard Model to provide a description of angular correlations in neutron decay in the first order of approximation, or down to the level of $\sim 10^{-5}$. The contributions from models beyond the Standard model are, for low energy neutron decay, parameterized in terms of vector, axial-vector, scalar and tensor coupling constants and in terms of parameters related to specific models. For the present analysis we derive the exact expressions for the neutron beta decay probability which includes all possible manifestations models beyond the Standard Model down to level of $\sim 10^{-5}$ without time-reversal violation. Based on the general expressions for manifestation of the deviations from the standard model, we present analysis of the sensitivities for selected neutron decay experiments.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general framework for analyzing the sensitivity of neutron beta-decay experiments to new physics beyond the Standard Model, but only for time-reversal conserving interactions; the analysis is valid down to a theoretical accuracy of about 10^{-5}, which is well below the anticipated experimental accuracy of 10^{-3} to 10^{-4} in next-generation experiments.

## Method

The authors derive exact expressions for the neutron beta-decay probability that include all Standard Model radiative and recoil corrections (using effective field theory) plus contributions from non-standard scalar, tensor, vector, and axial-vector couplings parameterized by low-energy constants C_i. They then compute the statistical sensitivity (minimum variance bound estimator) of experiments measuring the angular correlation coefficients a, A, and B to each type of new-physics coupling, assuming all non-standard couplings have the same value of 1 x 10^{-3}. The analysis is extended to specific model parameters (a_{jl}, alpha_{jl}, A_{jl}) from left-right symmetric models, leptoquarks, supersymmetry, and other extensions.

## Key numbers

* Theoretical accuracy of the Standard Model description: ~10^{-5} (level of radiative and recoil corrections included)
* Anticipated experimental accuracy for next-generation neutron decay experiments: ~10^{-3} to 10^{-4}
* Relative statistical error K for the a-coefficient experiment to tensor interactions (C_T): 2.59 (for C_i = 1 x 10^{-3})
* Relative statistical error K for the A-coefficient experiment to scalar interactions (C_S): 26.70 (for C_i = 1 x 10^{-3})
* Relative statistical error K for the B-coefficient experiment to scalar interactions (C_S): 1.46 (for C_i = 1 x 10^{-3})
* Relative statistical error K for the a-coefficient experiment to a_{LR} parameter: 0.25 (for a_{ij} = 1 x 10^{-3})
* Relative statistical error K for the B-coefficient experiment to a_{RL} parameter: 89 (for a_{ij} = 1 x 10^{-3})
* Expected value of a_{RL} in left-right symmetric models: 0.067
* Expected value of a_{RR} in left-right symmetric models: 0.075
* Expected value of (A_{LL}+A_{LR}) in SUSY models: 7.5 x 10^{-4}
* Expected value of (A_{LL}+A_{LR}) in Higgs models: 3 x 10^{-6}
* Value of the inner radiative correction parameter (alpha/(2pi) e_V^R): 0.02 (estimated)
* Change of results by about 1% when the inner radiative correction parameter is varied by a factor of two

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory description of neutron beta-decay from Gudkov, Greene, and Calarco (2004) by including the most general non-standard beta-decay interactions parameterized by scalar, tensor, vector, and axial-vector coupling constants. It provides a framework for comparing different neutron decay experiments in terms of their discovery potential for new physics, complementing high-energy experiments. The analysis enables optimization of next-generation precision experiments and shows that the usual tree-level parametrization in terms of coefficients a, A, and B is inadequate when experimental sensitivities reach the 10^{-3} to 10^{-4} level, requiring instead the full expression in terms of coupling constants.
