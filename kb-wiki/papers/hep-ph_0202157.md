# Hadron 2001 Conference Summary: Theory

> This contribution reviews some of the theoretical issues and predictions that were discussed at HADRON2001. The topics are divided into principle areas, 1) exotics, 2) vectors, 3) scalars, and 4) higher-mass states. The current status of theoretical predictions for each area are summarized, together with a brief description of experiment. New and detailed experimental results are presented in the companion Experimental Summary by Klempt.

## Key claim

This conference summary reviews theoretical issues and predictions discussed at HADRON2001, covering exotics, vectors, scalars, and higher-mass states, but explicitly notes that the theoretical predictions are for an unfamiliar system in the quenched approximation and that mass shifts due to couplings to virtual meson loops are currently unclear and may be rather large.

## Method

The author surveys theoretical approaches presented at the conference, including quark potential models, lattice gauge theory (LGT) in the quenched approximation, the flux tube model, and the $^3$P$_0$ decay model. The review organizes results by hadron category (exotics, vectors, scalars, higher-mass states) and compares theoretical predictions with experimental results from E852 at BNL, Crystal Barrel at LEAR, VES, and other facilities.

## Key numbers

- LGT predicts $b\bar b$-hybrid $1^{-+}$ mass at approximately 10.9-11.0 GeV and $c\bar c$-hybrid $1^{-+}$ mass at approximately 4.3 GeV
- LGT predicts $n\bar n$-hybrid $1^{-+}$ mass at approximately 1.9-2.1 GeV
- The $\pi_1(1600)$ exotic candidate has a fitted total width of $\Gamma_{tot} = 340 \pm 40 \pm 50$ MeV from the $\eta'\pi$ final state
- The $b_1\to \omega\pi$ decay shows a D/S ratio of approximately +0.28, supporting the $^3$P$_0$ model
- Quark potential model predicts $n\bar n$ $2^3$S$_1$ radial excitations near 1.5 GeV, L=2 $^3$D$_1$ states near 1.7 GeV, and 3S radial excitation near 2.1 GeV
- $K$ and $\phi$ analogues of the 2S states are expected about 0.12 and 0.25 GeV higher in mass than their non-strange counterparts
- Relative flavor cross section ratios for $\rho^\circ :\omega : \phi$ in $e^+e^-$ annihilation are $9:1:2$

## Context

This review summarizes the theoretical state of hadron spectroscopy at the HADRON2001 conference, extending the flux tube model calculations of Isgur, Kokoski and Paton (1990s) and LGT glueball spectrum calculations of Morningstar and Peardon. The paper contrasts the $\pi_1(1400)$ candidate (which VES now finds no clear preference for as a resonance) with the $\pi_1(1600)$ candidate seen by E852, and notes that LGT results from Drummond et al. find a level ordering of $2^{-+} > 1^{--} > 1^{-+} > 0^{-+}$ for hybrids that matches bag model predictions rather than flux tube model degeneracy predictions. The work enables future studies of vector mesons at upgraded $e^+e^-$ facilities at Novosibirsk and Frascati, and of $c\bar c$ spectroscopy at BES and CLEO-c.
