# PNJL model with a Van der Monde term

> We extend the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for two degenerate flavours by including the effect of the SU(3) measure with a Van der Monde (VdM) term. This ensures that the Polyakov loop always remains in the domain [0,1]. The pressure, energy density, specific heat, speed of sound and conformal measure show small or negligible effects from this term. However various quark number and isospin susceptibilities are all found to approach their respective ideal gas limits around 2 $T_c$. We compare our methods with other similar approaches in PNJL model and also present a quantitative comparison with Lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

The authors extend the PNJL model by including a Vandermonde (VdM) term as a Jacobian factor in the path integral measure, which constrains the Polyakov loop to remain in the domain [0,1]; however, the pressure, energy density, specific heat, speed of sound, and conformal measure show only small or negligible effects from this term, while quark number and isospin susceptibilities approach their ideal gas limits around 2 T_c.

## Method

The authors introduce the VdM term as a logarithmic correction to the thermodynamic potential, with a dimensionless parameter kappa set to 0.2, and minimize the modified potential to obtain mean-field values of the Polyakov loop and chiral condensates. They compute thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, specific heat, speed of sound, and conformal measure, and evaluate Taylor expansion coefficients of pressure in quark number and isospin chemical potentials up to sixth order. The results are compared to lattice QCD data from Ref. sixx and to earlier PNJL calculations without the VdM term.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature T_c = 230 MeV, taken as the average position of peaks in dPhi/dT and dsigma/dT which differ by 5 MeV
- Optimized VdM coupling constant kappa = 0.2 (constant value)
- For pure gauge theory, T_0 = 0.27 GeV and T_c = 0.270 GeV
- All susceptibilities approach their respective ideal gas limits around 2 T_c
- The second-order quark number susceptibility c_2 compares well with lattice data up to about 1.2 T_c, then rises toward the Stefan-Boltzmann limit while lattice values saturate at about 80% of this limit
- The fourth-order coefficient c_4 matches lattice data for the full temperature range, in contrast to earlier PNJL results without VdM term which matched only up to T ~ 1.1 T_c

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model of pnjl2, pnjl3, pnjl4 by adding a Vandermonde term to cure the unphysical behavior where the Polyakov loop exceeds 1 at temperatures above 2 T_c. The approach differs from that of Ratti et al. (2006) who included the VdM term directly in the pressure calculation; here the VdM term only affects the mean fields and not the pressure itself. The results enable improved comparison with lattice QCD data for susceptibilities, particularly fixing the discrepancy in the second-order flavor off-diagonal susceptibility c_2^{ud} that remained non-zero up to 2 T_c in earlier PNJL calculations.
