# Electromagnetic interaction of low transverse momentum electrons and positrons with heavy nuclei in ultra-peripheral ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The photon-photon interaction in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision is a source of the $(e^+,e^-)$ pairs. The photon-photon fusion leads the lepton creation in the broad configuration space around "collision" point. Those created close to heavy nuclei may undergo strong interaction with them. The impact parameter space distribution of electrons and positrons are calculated within the b-space EPA model of such collisions. The evolution due to the electromagnetic interaction of $(e^+,e^-)$ with nuclei is studied, and the distortion of rapidity and transverse momentum distributions are shown. We show first exploratory results for the reaction Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 17.3 GeV (SPS) and 200 GeV (RHIC) energies. We provide results for selected creation points and when integrating over their position as dictated by the $b$-space EPA model. We observe strong effects at low transverse momenta, so far not measured regions of the phase space. In particular, we predict a possible sizeable accumulation of electrons with rapidities close to the beam rapidity. The EM effects lead to asymmetry in the production of electrons and positrons.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic final-state interaction (FSI) between electrons/positrons and fast-moving heavy nuclei in ultra-peripheral collisions can produce strong distortions of lepton rapidity and transverse momentum distributions at low transverse momenta (below about 0.1 GeV), a phase-space region not yet measured experimentally; the effect is exploratory and the authors use a classical trajectory approach rather than a full quantum treatment of multi-photon exchanges.

## Method

The authors first calculate the initial production of electron-positron pairs via photon-photon fusion using the impact-parameter-space equivalent photon approximation (b-space EPA), which provides the differential cross section as a function of the lepton's creation point in the transverse plane, its rapidity, and its transverse momentum. They then evolve the leptons in the electromagnetic fields of the two fast-moving lead nuclei by solving the relativistic classical equation of motion, treating the nuclei as point charges moving along straight-line trajectories at ultra-relativistic velocities. The calculation is performed for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV (SPS) and 200 GeV (RHIC), for a fixed impact parameter of 14 ± 0.05 fm (approximately twice the nuclear radius) and also for selected individual creation points.

## Key numbers

- The analysis is performed for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV (SPS) and sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC).
- The impact parameter is fixed at b = 14 ± 0.05 fm, approximately twice the radius of the lead nucleus; results are also shown for b = 50 ± 0.05 fm.
- The cross section for electron/positron production at the CM point (b_x=0, b_y=0) is about three orders of magnitude larger than at (b_x=40 fm, b_y=0).
- The differential cross section in the (y, p_T) plane is shown for the rapidity range (-5, 5) and transverse momentum range (0, 0.1 GeV).
- The distribution of the cross section in impact parameter b is shown for sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3, 50, and 200 GeV (from bottom to top in the figure).
- The two-dimensional cross section as a function of b_x and b_y is shown for b = 14 ± 0.05 fm and b = 50 ± 0.05 fm.

## Context

This work extends the classical electromagnetic FSI formalism previously applied to charged pions in heavy-ion collisions by Rybicki (2006) and Ozvenchuk (2019) to the case of electrons and positrons produced via photon-photon fusion in ultra-peripheral collisions. It contrasts with the earlier b-space EPA calculations for dilepton production by Klusek-Gawenda (2010, 2016) and with the Wigner-function approach of the same group, which are needed for precise low-p_T distributions but are not yet combined with FSI. The paper notes that no ready quantum formalism exists for calculating Coulomb modifications of differential lepton distributions in this regime, and that the classical approach used here is a practical alternative. The results are exploratory and intended to motivate future measurements at low transverse momenta, possibly with the ALICE3 detector.
