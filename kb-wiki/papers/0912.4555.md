# Nonlocal chiral quark models with Polyakov loop at finite temperature and chemical potential

> We analyze the chiral restoration and deconfinement transitions in the framework of a non-local chiral quark model which includes terms leading to the quark wave function renormalization, and takes care of the effect of gauge interactions by coupling the quarks with the Polyakov loop. Non-local interactions are described by considering both a set of exponential form factors, and a set of form factors obtained from a fit to the mass and renormalization functions obtained in lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The non-local chiral quark model with Polyakov loop, including quark wave function renormalization, provides a simultaneous description of chiral restoration and deconfinement transitions, but the position of the critical end point (CEP) depends crucially on the choice of form factor parametrization, with the lattice-motivated parametrization yielding lower T_CEP and higher μ_CEP than the exponential parametrization.

## Method

The authors construct a non-local SU(2) chiral quark model coupled to the Polyakov loop, with non-local currents that generate momentum-dependent quark mass and wave function renormalization. They work in the mean-field approximation, bosonize the theory, and extend it to finite temperature and chemical potential using the Matsubara formalism, then solve the coupled gap equations for the mean fields σ₁, σ₂, and Φ. Two form factor sets are used: exponential form factors (parametrization S1) and lattice-motivated form factors fitted to mass and renormalization functions from lattice QCD (parametrization S2), with a third parametrization (S3) omitting wave function renormalization for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Current quark mass m_c = 5.7 MeV (S1), 2.37 MeV (S2), 5.78 MeV (S3)
- Chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩^{1/3} = 240 MeV (fixed for S1)
- Pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV and pion mass m_π = 139 MeV (reproduced for all parametrizations)
- Quark wave function renormalization at zero momentum Z(0) = 0.7 (fixed for S1)
- Cutoff parameters: Λ₀ = 814.42 MeV (S1), 850.00 MeV (S2), 752.20 MeV (S3); Λ₁ = 1034.5 MeV (S1), 1400.0 MeV (S2)
- Chemical potential at T=0 for chirally restored phase: μ_c(T=0) ≈ 310 MeV
- Critical end point (CEP) location: shifts to lower T and higher μ when wave function renormalization is included, with S2 giving even lower T_CEP and higher μ_CEP than S1

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models of Meisinger (1995), Fukushima (2003), Megias (2004), Ratti (2005), and Roessner (2006) by introducing non-local interactions and quark wave function renormalization. It builds on the vacuum and meson sector analysis of Noguera (2008) at T=μ=0, and uses the Polyakov loop effective potential fitted to lattice QCD results from Roessner (2006). The model enables extrapolation into regions of the QCD phase diagram not accessible by lattice calculations, and predicts a quarkyonic phase where the system remains confined (Φ < 0.3) even after chiral symmetry restoration.
