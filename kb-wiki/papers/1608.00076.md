# Dirac Coupled-channel Analyses of Polarized Proton Scatterings to the 2$^+$ Gamma Vibrational Band in $^{24}$Mg and $^{26}$Mg

> Dirac coupled channel calculations are performed phenomenologically for the high-lying excited states that belong to the 2$^+$ gamma vibrational band at the 800-MeV polarized proton inelastic scatterings from the s-d shell nuclei, $^{24}$Mg and $^{26}$Mg. Optical potential model is used and scalar and time-like vector potentials are considered as direct potentials. First-order vibrational collective models are used to obtain the transition optical potentials in order to accommodate the high-lying excited vibrational collective states. The complicated Dirac coupled channel equations are solved phenomenologically to reproduce the differential cross section and analyzing power data by varying the optical potential and deformation parameters. It is found that the relativistic Dirac coupled channel calculation could describe the high-lying excited states of the 2$^+$ gamma vibrational band at the 800-MeV polarized proton inelastic scatterings from s-d shell nuclei $^{24}$Mg and $^{26}$Mg reasonably well, showing better agreement with the experimental data compared to the results obtained from the nonrelativistic calculations. Calculated deformation parameters for the excited states are analyzed and compared with those of nonrelativistic calculations.

## Key claim

The relativistic Dirac coupled-channel calculation, using a first-order vibrational collective model, describes the high-lying excited states of the 2⁺ gamma vibrational band in ²⁴Mg and ²⁶Mg for 800-MeV polarized proton inelastic scattering "reasonably well," showing better agreement with experimental data than nonrelativistic calculations, but the theoretical lines are slightly out of phase with the data at the position of the second minimum of the diffraction pattern for the 2₂⁺ state.

## Method

Dirac coupled-channel equations were solved phenomenologically using the ECIS code, employing scalar and time-like vector optical potentials of Woods-Saxon shape as direct potentials. Transition potentials for the excited states were obtained via the first-order vibrational collective model, assuming the deformed potentials follow the shape of the nuclear density and are proportional to the first-order derivatives of the diagonal potentials. The 12 parameters of the diagonal potentials were first fitted to elastic scattering data for ²⁴Mg, then six-parameter and eight-parameter searches were performed including the 2₂⁺ and 4₂⁺ states of the gamma vibrational band, varying deformation parameters (β_S, β_V) and potential strengths.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁴Mg, the scalar real potential strength is -310.3 MeV (radius 2.661 fm, diffusiveness 0.8014 fm); scalar imaginary strength is 21.70 MeV (radius 2.299 fm, diffusiveness 0.7990 fm); vector real strength is 118.2 MeV (radius 2.783 fm, diffusiveness 0.8192 fm); vector imaginary strength is -82.28 MeV (radius 2.631 fm, diffusiveness 0.6437 fm).
- Deformation parameters for the 2₂⁺ state in ²⁴Mg (4.24 MeV): β_S = 0.204, β_V = 0.201; nonrelativistic values are 0.163 and 0.187.
- Deformation parameters for the 2₂⁺ state in ²⁶Mg (2.94 MeV): β_S = 0.174, β_V = 0.191; nonrelativistic values are 0.142 and 0.141.
- Deformation parameters for the 4₂⁺ state in ²⁴Mg (6.01 MeV): β_S = 0.347, β_V = 0.274; nonrelativistic values are 0.334 and 0.333.
- Deformation parameters for the 4₂⁺ state in ²⁶Mg (4.90 MeV): β_S = 0.102, β_V = 0.162; nonrelativistic values are 0.165 and 0.206.
- In the three-state coupled case for ²⁴Mg, potential strengths changed to -322.8 MeV (scalar real), 19.68 MeV (scalar imaginary), 123.9 MeV (vector real), and -80.75 MeV (vector imaginary).
- In the three-state coupled case for ²⁶Mg, potential strengths changed to -314.1 MeV (scalar real), -21.99 MeV (scalar imaginary), 190.0 MeV (vector real), and -81.16 MeV (vector imaginary).

## Context

This work extends relativistic Dirac phenomenology, previously successful for elastic scattering from spherical and deformed nuclei (references 3-6), to inelastic scattering exciting the 2⁺ gamma vibrational band in s-d shell nuclei. It directly compares with nonrelativistic calculations by Tanaka et al. (1997) and others (references 17-19), finding that the Dirac approach yields better agreement with experimental differential cross section and analyzing power data. The results enable further study of collective excitations in light nuclei using relativistic frameworks, though the multistep channel-coupling effect between the 2₂⁺ and 4₂⁺ states was found to be insignificant for these cases.
