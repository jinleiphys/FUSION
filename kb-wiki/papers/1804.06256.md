# Nuclear Equation of State from ground and collective excited state properties of nuclei

> This contribution reviews the present status on the available constraints to the nuclear equation of state (EoS) around saturation density from nuclear structure calculations on ground and collective excited state properties of atomic nuclei. It concentrates on predictions based on self-consistent mean-field calculations, which can be considered as an approximate realization of an exact energy density functional (EDF). EDFs are derived from effective interactions commonly fitted to nuclear masses, charge radii and, in many cases, also to pseudo-data such as nuclear matter properties. Although in a model dependent way, EDFs constitute nowadays a unique tool to reliably and consistently access bulk ground state and collective excited state properties of atomic nuclei along the nuclear chart as well as the EoS. For comparison, some emphasis is also given to the results obtained with the so called {\it ab initio} approaches that aim at describing the nuclear EoS based on interactions fitted to few-body data only. Bridging the existent gap between these two frameworks will be essential since it may allow to improve our understanding on the diverse phenomenology observed in nuclei. Examples on observations from astrophysical objects and processes sensitive to the nuclear EoS are also briefly discussed. As the main conclusion, the isospin dependence of the nuclear EoS around saturation density and, to a lesser extent, the nuclear matter incompressibility remain to be accurately determined. Experimental and theoretical efforts in finding and measuring observables specially sensitive to the EoS properties are of paramount importance, not only for low-energy nuclear physics but also for nuclear astrophysics applications.

## Key claim

This review concludes that the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state (EoS) around saturation density and, to a lesser extent, the nuclear matter incompressibility remain to be accurately determined, despite extensive constraints from ground state and collective excited state properties of nuclei.

## Method

The authors review constraints on the nuclear EoS derived from self-consistent mean-field calculations (energy density functionals, EDFs) fitted to nuclear masses, charge radii, and collective excited state properties such as isoscalar giant monopole resonances (ISGMR), isovector giant dipole resonances (IVGDR), dipole polarizability, isobaric analog states (IAS), and anti-analog giant dipole resonances (AGDR). They compare these EDF-based constraints with results from macroscopic-microscopic models, ab initio approaches, and experimental data on neutron densities in 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density rho_0: 0.154-0.159 fm^-3, from most accurate EDFs on masses and charge radii
- Saturation energy e_0: -16.2 to -15.6 MeV, from most accurate EDFs on masses and charge radii
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K_0: 220-245 MeV from EDFs on masses; 220-260 MeV from ISGMR in closed shell nuclei; 250-315 MeV from Blaizot's formula; approximately 200 MeV from EDF describing open shell nuclei
- Symmetry energy at saturation J: 29-35.6 MeV from EDFs on masses and charge radii; compilations give 29.0-32.7 MeV (Lattimer 2013), 30.7-32.5 MeV (Li 2013), and 28.5-34.9 MeV (Oertel 2017)
- Slope parameter L: 27-113 MeV from EDFs on masses and charge radii; compilations give 40.5-61.9 MeV (Lattimer 2013), 42.4-75.4 MeV (Li 2013), and 30.6-86.8 MeV (Oertel 2017)
- K_tau parameter: -620 to -400 MeV from antiprotonic atoms (EDF analysis); -650 to -450 MeV from ISGMR in Sn isotopes; -630 to -480 MeV from ISGMR in Cd isotopes; -840 to -350 MeV from Blaizot's formula

## Context

This review extends and synthesizes constraints from multiple prior compilations including Lattimer (2013), Li (2013), and Oertel (2017), as well as individual experimental analyses of neutron densities in 208Pb from Zenihiro (2010) and Tarbert (2014). It contrasts EDF-based approaches with ab initio methods (e.g., Gandolfi 2012, Hebeler 2010) and macroscopic-microscopic models. The work enables future efforts to bridge the gap between phenomenological EDFs and ab initio frameworks, and to identify observables that can more precisely determine the isospin dependence of the EoS for nuclear astrophysics applications.
