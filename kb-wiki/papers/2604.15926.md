# Exact expectation values in a boost-invariant fluid of Dirac fermions with finite spin density

> We study a boost-invariant, out-of-equilibrium fluid of non-interacting Dirac fermions with a finite canonical spin potential. After solving the Dirac equation in Milne coordinates, we exactly diagonalize the non-equilibrium density operator and compute the partition function and expectation values of relevant observables, including spin polarization, energy density, longitudinal and transverse pressures, spin density, and \emph{spin torque}, i.e. the source of spin non-conservation. We find an analytic expression for the partition function at finite spin potential, and show numerically that thermodynamic relations connecting it to thermodynamic functions hold in the system under consideration. We show that, in a boost-invariant system, both shear-induced polarization and the spin Hall effect are absent, and that a non-vanishing polarization can only arise from a finite spin potential in a free theory. We obtain an analytic expression for the spin polarization as a function of the spin potential in some particular cases, and otherwise compute numerically its exact expectation value at finite spin potential. Our results are discussed in the context of relativistic spin hydrodynamics and quark--gluon plasma phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a boost-invariant fluid of non-interacting Dirac fermions, a non-vanishing spin polarization can only arise from a finite spin potential, not from thermal shear or a non-constant chemical potential, contradicting naive expectations from linear-response theory.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation in Milne coordinates and exactly diagonalize the non-equilibrium density operator for a boost-invariant system with finite canonical spin potential. They compute the partition function and expectation values of the energy-momentum tensor, spin tensor, and spin polarization vector in both the Belinfante and canonical pseudogauges, using creation and annihilation operators of Milne modes.

## Key numbers

* The spin potential is defined as \(\SP(\tau) \equiv \Omega(\tau)/T(\tau)\), where \(\Omega\) is the proper spin potential with dimensions of energy in natural units.
* The thermal shear tensor is \(\xi_{\mu\nu}(\tau)={\rm diag}\left(-\dot{T}(\tau)/T^2(\tau),0,0,-\tau/T(\tau)\right)\), while the thermal vorticity \(\varpi_{\mu\nu}(\tau)=0\).
* The energy-momentum conservation yields \(\dot{\mathcal{E}}(\tau)+(\mathcal{E}(\tau)+\mathcal{P}_{\rm L}(\tau))/\tau=0\).
* The spin tensor conservation yields \(\dot{\mathcal{S}}(\tau)+\mathcal{S}(\tau)/\tau-\mathcal{T}(\tau)=0\), where \(\mathcal{T}(\tau)\) is the spin torque density.

## Context

This work extends the exact calculation approach previously applied to scalar fields in boost-invariant settings (Akkelin 2018, Rindori 2021) to Dirac fermions with finite spin density. It provides the first exact expression for spin polarization at local thermodynamic equilibrium with a finite spin potential, serving as a benchmark for linear-response formulas used in spin hydrodynamics. The results are relevant to resolving the polarization sign puzzle in quark-gluon plasma phenomenology and test the framework of spin hydrodynamics (Becattini 2018, Florkowski 2017).
