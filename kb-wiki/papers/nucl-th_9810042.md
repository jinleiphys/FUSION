# Finite Temperature Nuclear Response in Extended Random-Phase Approximation

> The nuclear collective response at finite temperature is investigated for the first time in the quantum framework of the small amplitude limit of the extended TDHF approach, including a non-Markovian collision term. It is shown that the collision width satisfies a secular equation. By employing a Skyrme force, the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole excitations in $^{40}Ca$ are calculated and important quantum features are pointed out. The collisional damping due to decay into incoherent 2p-2h states is small at low temperatures but increases rapidly at higher temperatures.

# Finite Temperature Nuclear Response in Extended Random-Phase Approximation

## Key claim

The paper presents the first quantum (as opposed to semi-classical) investigation of nuclear collective response at finite temperature using the small-amplitude limit of extended TDHF theory, showing that the collisional damping width satisfies a secular equation that must be solved self-consistently. The collisional damping due to decay into incoherent 2p-2h states is small at low temperatures but increases rapidly at higher temperatures, though the temperature dependence is more complex than the quadratic increase predicted by semi-classical calculations.

## Method

The authors linearize the extended TDHF transport equation around a finite-temperature Hartree-Fock equilibrium state, including a non-Markovian collision term. They expand the density fluctuation in finite-temperature RPA modes and derive a secular equation for the collisional damping width by substituting a Lorentzian energy-conserving factor that includes the damping width itself. Using the Skyrme SGII interaction, they compute the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole strength distributions in $^{40}Ca$ at temperatures from 0 to 4 MeV, solving the Hartree-Fock problem in coordinate representation and generating particle states by diagonalizing in a 12-shell harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- Monopole average energy at T=0 MeV: E = 21.5 MeV, with Landau spreading over 16-28 MeV
- Dipole average energy at T=0 MeV: main peak near E = 16.5 MeV, with strength concentrated in 16-23 MeV range
- Quadrupole peak energy at T=0 MeV: E = 17.5 MeV, agreeing with experimental average energy of 17 MeV
- Temperatures investigated: T = 0, 2, 4 MeV for strength distributions; T = 0, 2, 3, 4, 5 MeV for damping widths
- Landau spreading width (FWHM) at T=0 MeV: approximately 4 MeV for both monopole and dipole
- Quadrupole collisional damping width saturates above T = 3-4 MeV
- Dipole collisional damping at T=0 MeV: zero in $^{40}Ca$ due to absence of odd-parity 2p-2h states at the collective energy

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical Thomas-Fermi calculations of collisional damping at finite temperature by Ayik, Yilmaz, and collaborators (1990s) into a fully quantum framework, incorporating shell effects that are absent in the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) approach. The authors compare their results to experimental data on giant resonances in $^{40}Ca$ from Youngblood (monopole), Berman (dipole), and the quadrupole calculations of Sagawa and Bertsch. The paper enables future systematic studies of effective interactions for damping properties and suggests that quantum effects persist even at relatively high excitations, with the possibility of extending such calculations to heavier nuclei like $^{120}Sn$ and $^{208}Pb$.
