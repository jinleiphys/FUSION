# Effective nucleon mass in relativistic mean field theory

> In the $σ$-$ω$-$ρ$ model of the relativistic mean field theory with nonlinear $σ$-meson self-interaction, the effective nucleon mass $M^*$ is discussed with relation to the symmetry incompressibility $K_s$ of nuclear matter, based on the model parameters fitted to nuclear matter properties. It is shown that $M^*$ is larger than $0.73M$, if $K_s$ is assumed to be negative and the nuclear matter incompressibility $K_0$ is kept less than $300 MeV$. Furthermore, the field system is shown to be stable, as the $σ$-meson self-interaction energy is lower bounded in this parameter region.

## Key claim

The effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) in the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) relativistic mean field model with nonlinear \(\sigma\)-meson self-interaction can be larger than \(0.73M\) (rather than the typical value around \(0.6M\)), provided the symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) is taken to be negative and the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_0\) is kept below 300 MeV; under these conditions the field system is also stable.

## Method

The authors fit the five model parameters (\(C_\sigma^2\), \(C_\omega^2\), \(C_\rho^2\), \(b\), \(c\)) of the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) Lagrangian to five nuclear matter properties: the nuclear radius constant \(r_0\), volume energy \(a_1\), incompressibility \(K_0\), symmetry energy \(J\), and symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\). Using the standard density \(\rho_0 = 1/(4\pi r_0^3/3)\) and the equilibrium condition \(p(\rho_0,0)=0\), they derive algebraic relations for the parameters and then scan over values of the reduced effective mass \(\xi = M^*/M\) and \(C_\sigma^2\) to map the resulting \(K_0\) and \(K_s\). They also test parameter sets taken from nonrelativistic models (Myers-Swiatecki, Skyrme SIII, Ska, SkM, SkM\(^*\), RATP, and Tondeur) as input.

## Key numbers

- The effective nucleon mass \(M^*\) is larger than \(0.73M\) when \(K_s\) is negative and \(K_0 \le 300\) MeV.
- For the nonrelativistic-model input sets, the resulting effective nucleon mass is around \(0.89M\).
- Input values used: nuclear radius constant \(r_0 \approx 1.14\) fm, volume energy \(a_1 \approx 16\) MeV.
- The symmetry energy \(J = 30\) MeV is used in the calculation of \(C_\rho^2\) and \(L\).
- The symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) is negative for all tested nonrelativistic interactions (Myers-Swiatecki, SIII, Ska, SkM, SkM\(^*\), RATP, Tondeur).
- Experimentally extracted \(K_s\) from isoscalar giant monopole resonance data ranges from \(-566 \pm 1350\) to \(34 \pm 159\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) relativistic mean field model (Serot, 1997) by addressing the controversy that standard parameter sets yield a positive symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) (opposite to nonrelativistic model predictions) and an uncomfortably low effective mass \(M^* \approx 0.6M\). The authors show that by fitting to nuclear matter properties extracted from nonrelativistic models such as the Myers-Swiatecki interaction (Myers, 1996), Skyrme interactions (Brack), and the Tondeur interaction (Tondeur), one obtains a negative \(K_s\) and a larger \(M^*\) around \(0.89M\), while also ensuring the field system is stable (positive coefficient \(c\) for the \(\phi^4\) term). This enables consistent relativistic descriptions of nuclear matter that align with nonrelativistic results and experimental constraints on \(K_s\).
