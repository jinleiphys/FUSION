# Dimer-atom-atom recombination in the universal four-boson system

> The dimer-atom-atom recombination process in the system of four identical bosons with resonant interactions is studied. The description uses the exact Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas equations for the four-particle transition operators that are solved in the momentum-space framework. The dimer-dimer and atom-trimer channel contributions to the ultracold dimer-atom-atom recombination rate are calculated. The dimer-atom-atom recombination rate greatly exceeds the three-atom recombination rate.

# Dimer-atom-atom recombination in the universal four-boson system

## Key claim

The dimer-atom-atom recombination rate in a system of four identical bosons with resonant interactions greatly exceeds the three-atom recombination rate, but this result holds specifically at ultracold temperatures where initial particle momenta are negligible and the system energy is at the dimer-atom-atom threshold. The dominance of the dimer-atom-atom channel over three-atom recombination has important consequences for the lifetime of trapped atomic gases with a small admixture of dimers.

## Method

The author solved the exact Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum space, using a separable two-particle potential to reduce the equations to two continuous variables (Jacobi momenta). The dimer-atom-atom recombination amplitude was derived from the AGS operators via time-reversal symmetry, relating the three-cluster recombination to the three-cluster breakup of the initial two-cluster state. Calculations were performed at the dimer-atom-atom threshold energy with zero initial momenta, where only the total angular momentum \(\mathcal{J}=0\) partial wave contributes.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor between successive Efimov trimer states is \(a_{n+1}^d / a_n^d \approx 22.694\), defining the regime \(1 < a/a_n^d < 22.694\) for universal behavior.
- The \(n\)-th atom-trimer and dimer-dimer thresholds intersect at \(a/a_n^d \approx 6.789\).
- The \(n\)-th atom-trimer contribution \(K_{211}^n\) exceeds the dimer-dimer contribution \(K_{211}^{dd}\) for \(a/a_n^d < 13.5\).
- The three-body dimer-atom-atom model reproduces \(K_{211}^n\) with 10% or better accuracy for \(a/a_n^d < 4\).
- Calculations require \(n \ge 3\) (four or more atom-trimer channels) for negligible non-universal short-range corrections.

## Context

This work extends prior four-boson studies of elastic and inelastic atom-trimer scattering (Deltuva 2010, 2011), dimer-dimer scattering (Deltuva 2011), and four-atom recombination (Deltuva 2012) by addressing the previously unstudied three-cluster recombination process. The results enable quantitative predictions for the lifetime and stability of ultracold atomic gases containing both atoms and dimers, complementing experimental observations near the universal regime (Ferlaino 2008, 2009; Pollack 2009; Zaccanti 2009). The author also tested a simplified three-body model treating the dimer as point-like (Deltuva 2012) and found it inadequate except for the \(n\)-th atom-trimer channel at small \(a/a_n^d\).
