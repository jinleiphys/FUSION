# Investigating the Role of Quantum Entanglement in Heavy Ion Collisions through Elliptic Flow

> This paper investigates the relationship between initial spatial anisotropy and final state momentum anisotropy in heavy ion collisions through the analysis of elliptic flow ($v_2$) as a function of transverse momentum ($p_T$). Building upon previous studies on thermalization in heavy ion collisions using transverse momentum distributions, we extend the analysis to the $p_T$ dependence of $v_2$ in Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collisions. By employing a two-component model to extract the thermal and hard scattering contributions to the elliptic flow, we aim to gain further insights into the role of quantum entanglement in the rapid thermalization and collective behavior of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP).

## Key claim

The paper claims that a two-component model combining thermal and hard scattering contributions can successfully describe elliptic flow ($v_2$) as a function of transverse momentum ($p_T$) in Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe collisions, and that the observed thermal dominance at low $p_T$ may be consistent with quantum entanglement between the participant and spectator regions of the collision, but the authors hedge that establishing this connection requires further theoretical development.

## Method

The authors analyzed elliptic flow ($v_2$) data as a function of transverse momentum ($p_T$) from Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.44$ TeV (ALICE) and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV (ATLAS) and 2.76 TeV (ALICE) across 20-30%, 30-40%, and 40-50% centrality classes. They employed a two-component model where the thermal contribution is described by an exponential function of transverse mass and the hard scattering contribution by a power-law function, fitting the sum to the $v_2(p_T)$ data using Python's SciPy package and assessing fit quality with $R^2$ and $\chi^2$ values.

## Key numbers

* Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.44$ TeV: hard scattering integral to total integral ratio is $0.56 \pm 0.01$ for 20-30% centrality, $0.56 \pm 0.01$ for 30-40% centrality, and $0.44 \pm 0.01$ for 40-50% centrality (pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 0.8$)
* Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV: hard scattering integral to total integral ratio is $0.44 \pm 0.01$ for 20-30% centrality, $0.60 \pm 0.01$ for 30-40% centrality, and $0.50 \pm 0.01$ for 40-50% centrality (pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 2.5$)
* Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV: hard scattering integral to total integral ratio is $0.49 \pm 0.01$ for 20-30% centrality, $0.51 \pm 0.01$ for 30-40% centrality, and $0.61 \pm 0.01$ for 40-50% centrality (pseudorapidity $|\eta| < 0.8$)
* The thermal component dominates at low $p_T \lesssim 2$ GeV/c across all systems and centralities

## Context

This work extends previous studies on thermalization in heavy ion collisions using transverse momentum distributions (Baker 2018, Ho 2015) by analyzing the $p_T$ dependence of elliptic flow specifically. The two-component model builds on approaches by Zhang (2021) and Baker (2018). The results show non-monotonic centrality dependence of the hard scattering ratio in Xe-Xe at 5.44 TeV and Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV, contrasting with monotonic increase in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, suggesting sensitivity to collision conditions. The findings enable future studies incorporating more sophisticated models of quantum effects, such as those based on AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena 1999, Gubser 2008) or the color glass condensate framework (Gelis 2010), and motivate extending the analysis to proton-nucleus collisions, higher-order flow harmonics (Yan 2014), and event-by-event fluctuations (Giacalone 2017).
