# Low-energy Compton scattering on the nucleon and sum rules

> The Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and Baldin-Lapidus sum rules are evaluated in the dressed K-matrix model for photon-induced reactions on the nucleon. For the first time the sum $α+β$ of the electric and magnetic polarisabilities and the forward spin polarisability $γ_0$ are explicitly calculated in two alternative ways -- from the sum rules and from the low-energy expansion of the real Compton scattering amplitude -- within the {\em same} framework. The two methods yield compatible values for $α+β$ but differ somewhat for $γ_0$. Consistency between the two ways of determining the polarisabilities is a measure of the extent to which basic symmetries of the model are obeyed.

## Key claim

For the first time, the sum of electric and magnetic polarisabilities (α+β) and the forward spin polarisability (γ₀) are calculated both from sum rules and from the low-energy expansion of the Compton amplitude within the same dressed K-matrix model; the two methods yield compatible values for α+β but differ somewhat for γ₀, and this discrepancy is a measure of the extent to which basic symmetries of the model are obeyed.

## Method

The authors evaluate the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH), Baldin-Lapidus (BL), and forward spin-polarisability sum rules using total photoabsorption cross sections computed in the dressed K-matrix model, which incorporates unitarity, crossing symmetry, gauge invariance, and analyticity via dressing of propagators and vertices with meson loops. They then extract the same polarisabilities from a low-energy expansion of the real Compton scattering amplitude within the same model, and compare the two sets of results. The dressing procedure is progressively increased from a bare (tree-level) calculation to a fully dressed calculation that includes a dressed 4-point γγNN contact term.

## Key numbers

* The sum α+β from the low-energy expansion in the fully dressed model is 14.5 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³), while from the BL sum rule at ≈1.5 GeV it is also 14.5 (same units).
* The forward spin polarisability γ₀ from the low-energy expansion in the fully dressed model is 2.4 (in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴), while from the sum rule at ≈1 GeV it is -0.8 (same units).
* The GDH sum rule integral at ≈1 GeV is consistent with the proton anomalous magnetic moment κ=1.79.
* In an exploratory calculation where the scalar part of the Δ self-energy BΔ(p²) was increased from ≈1.28 to ≈1.35 below the nucleon mass, the low-energy and sum-rule values of γ₀ were matched.

## Context

This work extends previous evaluations of the GDH and BL sum rules in meson-exchange models (Drechsel et al., 2001) and compares them with chiral perturbation theory calculations of polarisabilities (Bernard et al., 1994; Kumar et al., 2000; Ji et al., 2000). The dressed K-matrix model was previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering, pion photoproduction, and Compton scattering (Kondratyuk and Scholten, 2001). The discrepancy found for γ₀ points to the need for improved treatment of the Δ resonance self-energy and vertex corrections beyond the one-loop approximation, which could enable a fully consistent description of nucleon polarisabilities within a single dynamical framework.
