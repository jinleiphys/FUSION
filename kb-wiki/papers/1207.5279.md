# Comprehensive application of a coupled-channel complex scaling method to the KbarN-piY system

> We have applied the coupled-channel complex scaling method (ccCSM) to K^{bar}N-πY system. One advantage of ccCSM is that resonant states as well as scattering states can be treated in the same framework. For the interactions in the system, we have constructed a meson-baryon potential-matrix by basing on the chiral SU(3) theory and respecting the K^{bar}N scattering length obtained in the Martin's analysis. For future purpose to apply it more complicated system such as K^{bar}NN, we adopt a local Gaussian form in the r-space. We have investigated both the non-relativistic (NR) and the semi-relativistic (SR) kinematics. In the SR case, two types of the potentials are obtained. To test the constructed potentials, we have calculated scattering amplitudes and searched resonances. One resonance pole, corresponding to Λ(1405), is found in isospin I=0 system around (1419, -20) MeV ((1425, -25) or (1419, -13) MeV) on complex-energy plane with the NR (SR) kinematics. Mean distance between meson and baryon in the resonant state is 1.3 - i0.3 fm (1.2 - i0.5 fm) for NR (SR), in which the states are treated as Gamow states. In addition, we have observed a signature of another pole in lower-energy region involving large decay width, although they are unstable against the change of scaling angle θ. This may correspond to the lower pole of the double-pole of Λ(1405) discussed in literature to date.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the coupled-channel complex scaling method (ccCSM) can be successfully applied to the $\bar{K}N$-$\pi Y$ system to simultaneously treat resonant and scattering states, but the signature of a second, lower-energy pole of the $\Lambda(1405)$ is unstable against changes of the scaling angle $\theta$ and therefore not conclusively established.

## Method

The authors constructed a meson-baryon potential matrix based on the leading Weinberg-Tomozawa term of chiral SU(3) theory, using a local Gaussian form in coordinate space and constraining it to reproduce the $\bar{K}N$ scattering lengths from Martin's analysis. They applied the coupled-channel complex scaling method (ccCSM) to the $s$-wave $\bar{K}N$-$\pi Y$ system, investigating both non-relativistic (NR) and semi-relativistic (SR) kinematics, and calculated scattering amplitudes and resonance poles using the "CS-WF" method with Gaussian basis functions.

## Key numbers

* $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance pole found at $(1419, -20)$ MeV on the complex-energy plane for non-relativistic kinematics, and at $(1425, -25)$ MeV or $(1419, -13)$ MeV for semi-relativistic kinematics (two potential types)
* Mean distance between meson and baryon in the resonant state: $1.3 - i0.3$ fm for NR kinematics and $1.2 - i0.5$ fm for SR kinematics, treating the states as Gamow states
* Scattering length for the phenomenological potential test: $-1.77 + i0.47$ fm in the CS-WF calculation, compared to $-1.76 + i0.46$ fm in the original work by AY (2002)
* Self-consistency for the energy-dependent potential achieved within five iterations

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method, previously successful in nuclear physics for studying resonant states of unstable nuclei (Myo, various years), to the meson-baryon system relevant to kaonic nuclei. It provides a foundation for future application to the three-body $\bar{K}NN$ system, where the ccCSM approach can overcome limitations of variational studies (AY, 2002; DHW) that eliminate $\pi Y$ channels, and Faddeev-AGS studies (Ikeda; Shevchenko) that assume separable potentials and do not explicitly obtain wave functions. The double-pole nature of $\Lambda(1405)$ discussed in the chiral unitary model (Oset and Ramos; Jido; Hyodo and Weise) remains an open question, as the lower pole signature observed here is unstable against scaling angle variation.
