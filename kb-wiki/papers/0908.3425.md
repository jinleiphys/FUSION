# Chiral Perturbation Theory: Introduction and Recent Results in the One-Nucleon Sector

> We provide an introduction to the basic concepts of chiral perturbation theory and discuss some recent developments in the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of the one-nucleon sector.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) provides a systematic effective field theory description of the strong interactions at low energies, but extending the manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation to the one-nucleon sector has historically suffered from an apparent loss of power counting because higher-loop diagrams can contribute to terms as low as \( \mathcal{O}(q^2) \); however, new developments in renormalization schemes over the last decade have restored a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of the QCD Lagrangian in the chiral limit, the spontaneous breakdown of chiral \(\mathrm{SU(3)}_L \times \mathrm{SU(3)}_R\) symmetry to \(\mathrm{SU(3)}_V\), and the identification of the pseudoscalar octet as Goldstone bosons. It then introduces the generating functional of QCD coupled to external vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar fields, from which all chiral Ward identities can be derived. For the one-nucleon sector, the power-counting problem is illustrated and solutions are presented in terms of suitable renormalization conditions that restore a systematic expansion in \(q/\Lambda_\chi\) within a manifestly Lorentz-invariant framework.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi = \mathcal{O}(1\ \mathrm{GeV})\), which sets the expansion parameter \(q/\Lambda_\chi\) for momenta and masses of the pseudoscalar octet.
* The masses of the light quarks are given as: \(m_u = 1.5 - 3.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_d = 3.5 - 6.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(m_s = 70 - 130\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The masses of the heavy quarks are: \(m_c = 1.27^{+0.07}_{-0.11}\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(m_b = 4.20^{+0.17}_{-0.07}\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and \(m_t = 171.2 \pm 2.1\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This review extends the foundational mesonic ChPT framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) to the one-nucleon sector, addressing the power-counting problem first identified by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988). It enables systematic calculations of nucleon properties such as electromagnetic form factors, axial form factors, Compton scattering, and pion electroproduction within a manifestly Lorentz-invariant approach, complementing heavy-baryon ChPT methods developed by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) and others.
