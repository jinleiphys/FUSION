# Production of hypertritons in heavy ion collisions around the threshold of strangeness production

> We use the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics approach supplemented with a phase space coalescence to study the properties of the production of hypertritons. We see strong influences of the hyperon rescattering on the yields. The hypertritons show up to be quite aligned to the properties of nuclear matter underlining the necessity of rescattering to transport the hyperons to the spectator matter.

# Production of hypertritons in heavy ion collisions around the threshold of strangeness production

## Key claim

The formation of hypertritons in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV is strongly dependent on hyperon-nucleon rescattering to transport hyperons from midrapidity into the spectator matter region, with full rescattering yielding nearly three times more hypertritons than calculations without rescattering; the authors note that their calculated hyperon temperatures are somewhat lower than experimental values, and that the hypertriton yields are also influenced by the nuclear equation of state and the kaon optical potential.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) transport model, which propagates nucleons via Skyrme, Yukawa, and Coulomb interactions with momentum-dependent and asymmetry potentials, supplemented by a phase-space coalescence approach to form hypertritons. Hyperons were treated with Skyrme interactions at two-thirds the nucleon potential strength, and the model was applied to Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV incident energy, comparing results with and without hyperon rescattering and with different rescattering cross-section parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- Full rescattering yields nearly 3 times more hypertritons than calculations disabling rescattering (ratio hypertriton/hyperon as function of impact parameter).
- Reducing rescattering cross sections to half their value still yields nearly double the hypertriton yield compared to calculations without rescattering.
- Hyperons bound in hypertritons freeze out at densities well below normal matter density, around one third of ground state density.
- Hyperons in hypertritons have undergone more than 7 collisions on average, compared to almost 3 collisions for unclustered hyperons.
- The formation time for hypertritons is about 2-3 times the passing time (roughly 9 fm/c), with larger hyperclusters staying in contact for more than 40 fm/c.
- Maximum density experienced by hyperons is around twice nuclear matter density.

## Context

This work extends transport-model studies of hypernucleus production by combining IQMD with phase-space coalescence, following earlier theoretical approaches such as Gaitanos, Steinheimer, and Botvina (various years) that combined transport and fragmentation models. The results are compared to the FRIGA fragmentation model of Arnaud (2015), which uses a minimum binding energy approach and reproduces FOPI data on hypertritons as well as HypHI data on hypertritons and ⁴_ΛH. The study enables future detailed comparisons to experimental data from FOPI and other collaborations, with the authors noting that further analysis is needed for quantitative comparison.
