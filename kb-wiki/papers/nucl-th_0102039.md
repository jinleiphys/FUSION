# Directed and Elliptic Flow in $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn Collisions below 100 MeV/nucleon

> The directed and elliptic flow in collisions of $^{112}Sn$ + $^{112}Sn$ at energies from 35 to 90 MeV/nucleon were studied in an isospin-dependent quantum molecule dynamics model (IQMD). With increasing incident energy, the directed flow rises from the negative flow to the positive flow. Its magnitude depends on the nuclear equation of state (EOS). However, the elliptic flow shows decrease with increasing incident energy and its magnitude is not very sensible to EOS. Systematic studies of the impact parameter dependence and cluster mass dependence were also performed. The study of directed flow at intermediate energies thus provides a means to extract the information on the nuclear equation of state.

# Directed and Elliptic Flow in $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn Collisions below 100 MeV/nucleon

## Key claim
Using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, the directed flow in $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn collisions transitions from negative to positive with increasing incident energy and shows strong sensitivity to the nuclear equation of state (EOS), while the elliptic flow is not very sensitive to the EOS in this energy range. The balance energy (energy of disappearance of directed flow) depends on both the EOS and the impact parameter.

## Method
The authors employed an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model, which includes isospin-dependent Coulomb potential, symmetry potential, nucleon-nucleon cross sections, and Pauli blocking. Clusters were constructed using a coalescence model with parameters $R_0 = 2.4$ fm and $P_0 = 200$ MeV/c. For each impact parameter, 200 events were calculated, and results were averaged over five time intervals from 120 to 200 fm/c to yield statistical averages of 1000 "events." The directed flow parameter $F$ was defined as the slope of in-plane transverse momentum at mid-rapidity, and the elliptic flow $V_2$ was defined as the average difference between the squares of the $x$ and $y$ components of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- Balance energy for soft EOS (compressibility K = 220 MeV) at impact parameter 3.0–4.5 fm: near 85 MeV; for stiff EOS (K = 380 MeV): near 70 MeV.
- Balance energy for soft EOS at impact parameter 0–1.5 fm: near 70 MeV; for stiff EOS: near 60 MeV.
- Incident energy range studied: 35 to 90 MeV/nucleon.
- Coalescence parameters: $R_0 = 2.4$ fm, $P_0 = 200$ MeV/c.
- Compressibility values: soft EOS with K = 220 MeV, stiff EOS with K = 380 MeV.
- Symmetry potential parameter $c$ = 32 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of collective flow using the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) model by Li et al. and Zheng et al., which could not describe fragment flow due to being one-body transport models. The IQMD model used here includes many-body correlations and allows study of fragment flow. The results show that directed flow is a more sensitive probe of the nuclear equation of state than elliptic flow in this energy range, and the findings enable future comparisons with experimental data to extract EOS information.
