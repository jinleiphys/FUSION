# Collective Expansion at the LHC: selected ALICE anisotropic flow measurements

> The collective expansion of matter created in collisions of heavy-ions, ranging from collision energies of tens of MeV to a few TeV per nucleon pair, proved to be one of the best probes to study the detailed properties of these unknown states of matter. Collective expansion originates from the initial pressure gradients in the created hot and dense matter. These pressure gradients transform the initial spatial deformations and inhomogeneities of the created matter into momentum anisotropies of the final state particle production, which we call anisotropic flow. These momentum anisotropies are experimentally characterised by so-called flow harmonics. In this paper I review ALICE measurements of the flow harmonics at the CERN Large Hadron Collider and discuss some of the open questions.

## Key claim

The paper reviews ALICE measurements of anisotropic flow harmonics at the LHC, showing that the hot and dense matter created in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair still behaves like a fluid with almost zero friction, but the precise determination of the shear viscosity over entropy ratio (η/s) remains complicated by uncertainties in initial conditions, the relative contributions from hadronic and partonic phases, and the unknown temperature dependence of η/s.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of anisotropic flow coefficients (v_n) using Fourier decomposition of azimuthal particle distributions, with flow estimates obtained from two-particle correlations and multi-particle cumulants (using Q-cumulant methods) to suppress nonflow contributions from resonance decays, jet fragmentation, and Bose-Einstein correlations. The measurements are compared to viscous hydrodynamic model calculations (including the VISHNU hybrid model coupling hydrodynamics to the UrQMD hadron cascade) and to initial-state models (MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, IP-Glasma) to constrain the initial conditions and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

- Integrated elliptic flow (v_2) of charged particles at LHC (2.76 TeV) increases by about 30% compared to the highest RHIC energy (0.2 TeV) for the 20-30% centrality class
- The v_2(p_T) for charged particles is the same within uncertainties at low p_T between RHIC and LHC energies, despite beam energies differing by more than one order of magnitude
- Elliptic flow remains large above 15 GeV/c in transverse momentum for the 30-40% centrality class
- For typical Pb-Pb collisions at LHC with M_c = 500 (number of independent particle clusters), nonflow contributions can be reduced by more than an order of magnitude using higher order cumulants
- The φ-meson v_2 follows the mass hierarchy below p_T = 2.5 GeV/c, with an indication that φ-meson v_2 is larger than proton v_2 at the lowest p_T bin

## Context

This review extends the anisotropic flow measurements from RHIC (Ackermann 2000, Adler 2003) to LHC energies, building on the discovery that the QGP behaves as a strongly coupled liquid rather than a weakly interacting gas. The measurements provide constraints on the temperature dependence of η/s and discriminate between initial-state models (MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, IP-Glasma) as shown in Heinz (2013). The comparison with VISHNU hybrid model calculations (Song 2013) tests the role of hadronic interactions in breaking the mass hierarchy of v_2, while the mass ordering at intermediate p_T (2-3 GeV/c) where baryons and mesons separate is not expected in ideal hydrodynamics and points to the need for improved theoretical descriptions of particle production mechanisms.
