# Traveling Through the Universe: Back in Time to the Quark-Gluon Plasma Era

> We survey the early history of the discovery of quark gluon plasma and the early history of the Universe, beginning with the present day and reaching deep into QGP and almost beyond. We introduce cosmological Universe dynamics and connect the different Universe epochs with one another. We describe some of the many remaining open questions that emerge.

## Key claim

The paper surveys the early history of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) discovery and connects it to the thermal history of the early Universe, but it is a review-style lecture summary that presents no new experimental or numerical results; the authors explicitly note that many open questions remain and that the QGP discovery was a paradigm shift rather than a single experimental event, with the discovery being "rediscovered" at each new energy scale (SPS, RHIC, LHC).

## Method

The authors integrate the Friedmann-Lemaitre-Robertson-Walker (FLRW) cosmological equations forward in time from the electroweak era through the QGP era, using an equation of state that includes a bag constant to model the QGP phase transition. They then connect three eras of Universe evolution (present day to BBN, BBN to hadron era, and QGP era to electroweak era) by solving the Hubble equation and entropy conservation relations, using the Planck 2013 cosmological parameters (31% matter, 69% dark energy) and assuming three neutrino flavors with two having mass 30 meV.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for QGP hadronization: \(T_c \simeq 155 \pm 10\) MeV, based on QCD lattice studies (Philipsen 2012)
- Lifetime of the quark Universe: 10–15 \(\mu\)s, from the end of the electroweak era to QGP hadronization
- Number of effective bosonic degrees of freedom in the Standard Model above the top quark mass scale: \(g_{\rm SM} = 106.75\) (28 bosonic + 90 fermionic \(\times\) 7/8)
- Ratio of Universe inflation factor beyond thermal redshift from 100 GeV to today: \(r_{100{\rm GeV}}/r_{\rm now} = 27\), computed from \(g_{\rm SM} / g_{\rm now} = 106.75 / [2 + (7/8)\times 6 \times (4/11)]\)
- Neutrino-to-photon temperature ratio today: \(T_\nu/T_\gamma \approx (4/11)^{1/3}\), from entropy conservation after \(e^+e^-\) annihilation
- Deceleration parameter values: \(q \simeq 1/2\) for matter-dominated Universe, \(q \simeq 1\) for radiation-dominated Universe, \(q = -1\) for dark-energy-dominated Universe

## Context

This paper extends the line of work connecting heavy-ion collision QGP physics to early-Universe cosmology, complementing the authors' own prior studies (Fromerth and Rafelski 2012, Rafelski 2013) on the QGP era. It reviews the historical development from Hagedorn's bootstrap model (Hagedorn 1984) through the CERN QGP announcement in 2000 to the modern strongly-coupled liquid QGP picture described by Jacak and Muller (2012). The work enables future detailed studies of QGP hadronization inhomogeneities, baryon-antibaryon annihilation entropy production, and neutrino decoupling kinetics (Birrell 2013), as well as addressing open questions about the origin of baryon asymmetry (Canetti 2012).
