# Hyperon-Nucleon Interaction from Lattice QCD at ${(m_π,m_K)\approx(146,525)}$ MeV

> Comprehensive study of generalized baryon-baryon ($BB$) interaction including strangeness is one of the important subject of nuclear physics. In order to obtain a complete set of isospin-base baryon interactions, we perform a large scale lattice QCD calculation with almost physical quark masses corresponding to $(m_π,m_K)\approx(146,525)$ MeV and large volume $(La)^4=(96a)^4\approx$ (8.1 fm)$^4$. A large number of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlation functions from nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) to $ΞΞ$ are calculated simultaneously. In this contribution, we focus on the strangeness $S=-1$ channels of the hyperon interactions by means of HAL QCD method. Three potentials ((i) the $^1S_0$ central, (ii) the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ central, and (iii) $^3S_1-^3D_1$ tensor potentials) are presented for four isospin components; (1) the $ΣN - ΣN$ (the isospin $I=3/2$) diagonal, (2) the $ΛN - ΛN$ diagonal, (3) the $ΛN \rightarrow ΣN$ transition, and (4) the $ΣN - ΣN$ ($I=1/2$) diagonal. Scattering phase shifts for $ΣN$ $(I=3/2)$ system are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD results for hyperon-nucleon interactions in the strangeness S=-1 sector at nearly physical quark masses, but the potentials and phase shifts shown are preliminary, obtained from time slices t-t0 = 5-14 where the single-baryon effective mass plateaux only begin around t-t0 ≈ 14, meaning the results are not yet at the fully saturated regime.

## Method

The HAL QCD Collaboration computed Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) correlation functions for 52 baryon-baryon channels from NN to ΞΞ simultaneously on a 96^4 lattice with 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations at (mπ,mK)≈(146,525) MeV and volume (8.1 fm)^4. From the NBS wave functions, they extracted leading-order (LO) central and tensor potentials using the velocity expansion, then parametrized the potentials with analytic functional forms to solve the Schrödinger equation for scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: (mπ,mK)≈(146,525) MeV, volume (La)^4=(96a)^4≈(8.1 fm)^4, lattice spacing a≈0.085 fm
- 414 gauge configurations used, with 96 wall sources, at every 5 trajectories; statistical errors estimated by jackknife with bin size 46
- For ΣN (I=3/2) system: the ^1S_0 central potential shows short-range repulsive core and medium-to-long-range attractive well, similar to NN ^1S_0; the ^3S_1-^3D_1 central potential shows a stronger repulsive core with range r≲1 fm
- For ΣN (I=3/2) ^1S_0 channel: the scattering phase shift indicates the interaction is attractive on average, though fluctuations are large at time slices t-t0=10,11
- For ΣN (I=3/2) ^3S_1-^3D_1 channels: the bar-phase shift δ̄0 at time slices t-t0=9-11 shows repulsive interaction, while δ̄2 behaves around almost zero degree
- For ΛN-ΛN diagonal: both ^1S_0 and ^3S_1-^3D_1 central potentials show repulsive cores at short distance and attractive wells at medium to long range; the tensor potential is relatively weak
- For ΛN→ΣN transition: the ^3S_1-^3D_1 central potential is short ranged; the tensor potential shows sizable strength though not as strong as the NN tensor potential
- For ΣN-ΣN (I=1/2) diagonal: very strong repulsive core is seen in the ^1S_0 central potential

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method previously applied to NN interactions (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009) and various baryonic systems (Inoue 2011, Aoki 2011, Aoki 2012, Inoue 2013, Murano 2013, Inoue 2014, Etminan 2014, Sasaki 2015, Yamada 2015, Iritani 2016, Iritani 2017, Gongyo 2017) to the strangeness S=-1 hyperon-nucleon sector at nearly physical quark masses. A very preliminary study with small statistics of only the ΛN-ΛN single channel was reported at HYP2015 (Nemura 2016). The present results enable future coupled-channel analyses of ΛN-ΣN scattering and provide input for understanding hypernuclear structure and the equation of state of dense nuclear matter relevant to neutron star observations.
