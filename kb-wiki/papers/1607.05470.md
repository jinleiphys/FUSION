# Electron-positron pair production in ion collisions at low velocity beyond Born approximation

> We derive the spectrum and the total cross section of electromagnetic $e^{+}e^{-}$ pair production in the collisions of two nuclei at low relative velocity $β$. Both free-free and bound-free $e^{+}e^{-}$ pair production is considered. The parameters $η_{A,B}=Z_{A,B}α$ are assumed to be small compared to unity but arbitrary compared to $β$ ($Z_{A,B}$ are the charge numbers of the nuclei and $α$ is the fine structure constant). Due to a suppression of the Born term by high power of $β$, the first Coulomb correction to the amplitude appears to be important at $η_{A,B}\gtrsim β$. The effect of a finite nuclear mass is discussed. In contrast to the result obtained in the infinite nuclear mass limit, the terms $\propto M^{-2}$ are not suppressed by the high power of $β$ and may easily dominate at sufficiently small velocities.

## Key claim

The Born term for electron-positron pair production in low-velocity ion collisions is suppressed by a high power of β (β⁸), so the first Coulomb correction becomes important when the parameters η_A,B = Z_A,B α are comparable to or larger than β, contrary to the usual expectation that higher-order terms in η are negligible at small coupling.

## Method

The authors derived the spectrum and total cross section for both free-free and bound-free e⁺e⁻ pair production in collisions of two nuclei at low relative velocity β, using eigenfunctions of angular momentum as a basis and the small-r asymptotics of Coulomb wave functions. They assumed η_A,B ≪ 1 but arbitrary compared to β, and worked in the rest frame of one nucleus, integrating over momentum transfers and summing over discrete quantum numbers. The effect of finite nuclear mass was then treated separately by considering the bremsstrahlung mechanism of pair production via virtual photons emitted during the nuclear scattering.

## Key numbers

- Free-free total cross section (infinite nuclear mass, η_A ∼ η_B): σ_ff = (η_A² η_B² β⁶)/(1050π m²) {π²(η_A+η_B)² + (592/105)β²}
- Bound-free total cross section (infinite nuclear mass, η_A ∼ η_B): σ_bf = (16(η_A² η_B⁵ + η_B² η_A⁵) β⁶)/(15015 m²) ζ₃ {π²(η_A+η_B)² + (976/153)β²}
- Free-free cross section for scalar particles: σ_ff⁽⁰⁾ = (4 η_A² η_B² β⁴)/(135π m²)
- Bremsstrahlung mechanism contribution (finite nuclear mass, leading logarithmic approximation): σ_ff^BS = (8 η_A² η_B²)/(27π β²) (Z_A/M_A − Z_B/M_B)² ln³(ω₀/m), where ω₀ = M_r β³/(Z_A Z_B α)
- Constraint for neglecting nuclear trajectory curvature: β ≫ (m Z_A Z_B α / M_r)^{1/3} ∼ (m η_max / M_p)^{1/3}

## Context

This work extends the exact Born calculation of free-free pair production by Lee and Mingulov (2016) to include the first Coulomb corrections, which become important at low velocity due to the accidental suppression of the Born term. It contradicts the results of Khriplovich (2014, 2016), which the authors argue overestimate the cross sections and have incorrect β-dependence. The results enable future studies of pair production in the supercritical regime (Z_A+Z_B > 173) and at velocities where finite nuclear mass effects dominate, and they clarify the relationship between free-free and bound-free production for subcritical charges.
