# Two species $k$-body embedded Gaussian unitary ensembles: $q$-normal form of the eigenvalue density

> Eigenvalue density generated by embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble with $k$-body interactions for two species (say $\mathbfπ$ and $\mathbfν$) fermion systems is investigated by deriving formulas for the lowest six moments. Assumed in constructing this ensemble, called EGUE($k:\mathbfπ \mathbfν$), is that the $\mathbfπ$ fermions ($m_1$ in number) occupy $N_1$ number of degenerate single particle (sp) states and similarly $\mathbfν$ fermions ($m_2$ in number) in $N_2$ number of degenerate sp states. The Hamiltonian is assumed to be $k$-body preserving $(m_1,m_2)$. Formulas with finite $(N_1,N_2)$ corrections and asymptotic limit formulas both show that the eigenvalue density takes $q$-normal form with the $q$ parameter defined by the fourth moment. The EGUE($k:\mathbfπ \mathbfν$) formalism and results are extended to two species boson systems. Results in this work show that the $q$-normal form of the eigenvalue density established only recently for identical fermion and boson systems extends to two species fermion and boson systems.

## Key claim

The eigenvalue density generated by the embedded Gaussian unitary ensemble with $k$-body interactions for two species fermion and boson systems takes a $q$-normal form, extending a result previously established only for identical fermion and boson systems, with the $q$ parameter defined by the fourth moment and verified by the sixth moment to within less than 1% deviation.

## Method

The authors derive formulas for the lowest six moments (second, fourth, and sixth) of the eigenvalue density for the EGUE($k:\mathbf{\pi}\mathbf{\nu}$) ensemble using $U(N)$ Wigner-Racah algebra and the binary correlation approximation, both with finite $(N_1,N_2)$ corrections and in the asymptotic (dilute) limit where $N_1,N_2 \rightarrow \infty$, $m_1/N_1, m_2/N_2 \rightarrow 0$. They then compute the reduced fourth moment $\mu_4$ to extract the $q$ parameter and compare the reduced sixth moment $\mu_6$ from their formulas to the prediction of the $q$-normal distribution, performing numerical calculations for various $(N_1,m_1),(N_2,m_2)$ values and interaction ranks $k$, including cases with non-uniform interaction strengths $v^2(i,j)$ and small $m_1$.

## Key numbers

* For EGUE($k:\mathbf{\pi}\mathbf{\nu}$) with $(N_1,m_1)=(30,6)$ and $(N_2,m_2)=(40,8)$, the $q$ parameter varies from approximately 0.9 for $k=1$ to near 0 for $k \sim \min(m_1,m_2)/2$ in the asymptotic limit.
* The difference between $\mu_6(m_1,m_2)$ computed from finite-$N$ formulas and the $q$-normal prediction is in general less than 1% across all tested parameter sets.
* For the asymptotic limit, the second moment formula is $\overline{\langle H^2 \rangle^{m_1,m_2}} = \sum_{i+j=k} v^2(i,j) \binom{m_1}{i} \binom{N_1}{i} \binom{m_2}{j} \binom{N_2}{j}$.
* The $q$-normal distribution has reduced fourth moment $\mu_4 = 2+q$ and reduced sixth moment $\mu_6 = 5+6q+3q^2+q^3$, with $q=1$ giving a Gaussian and $q=0$ giving a semi-circle.

## Context

This work extends the $q$-normal form of the eigenvalue density, recently established for identical spinless fermion and boson systems by Vyas and Kota (2021) for EGUE($k$) and by Chen and collaborators for bosonic systems, to two-species fermion and boson systems (EGUE($k:\mathbf{\pi}\mathbf{\nu}$) and BEGUE($k:\mathbf{\pi}\mathbf{\nu}$)). The ensemble was discussed earlier by Kota and Mani (2015) where the eigenvalue density was assumed to be Gaussian; the present results show it is actually $q$-normal. The findings enable application of statistical nuclear spectroscopy based on $q$-normal forms, as developed in Vyas and Kota (2023) and Kota and Mani (2015), to two-species systems such as proton-neutron mixtures in atomic nuclei and two-component Bose-Einstein condensates.
