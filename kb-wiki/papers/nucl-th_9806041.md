# Three nucleons at very low energies

> We discuss the effective field theory approach to nuclear phenomena with typical momentum below the pion mass. We particularly focus on the three body problem. In the $J=3/2$ channel,effective field theory is extremelly predictive and we are able to describe the nucleon-deuteron scattering amplitude at the percent level using only two parameters obtained from nucleon-nucleon scattering. We briefly comment onthe $J=1/2$ channel and the issues related to the non-perturbative renormalization of the three body force.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the $J=3/2$ channel, effective field theory with pions integrated out can describe nucleon-deuteron scattering at the percent level using only two parameters from nucleon-nucleon scattering, but this high predictive power relies on the strong suppression of three-body forces in that channel (suppressed by $(r_0/a)^6$), whereas the $J=1/2$ channel faces a non-perturbative renormalization problem where three-body forces may appear at leading order.

## Method

The author constructs a pionless effective field theory for momenta $p \sim 1/a \ll m_\pi$, introducing a dibaryon field to resum the geometric series of two-nucleon interactions in the $^3S_1$ channel. For neutron-deuteron scattering in the $J=3/2$ channel, the leading-order diagrams (an infinite series of dibaryon-exchange graphs) are summed by solving an integral equation that reduces to an effective two-body problem with a separable potential. The equation is projected to s-wave and solved numerically using a K-matrix formulation to handle the $i\epsilon$ prescription, with results compared to experimental phase shifts and the zero-energy scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon scattering length: $a = 5.42$ fm; effective range: $r_0 = 1.75$ fm (from Nijmegen data)
- Dibaryon coupling constant: $g^2 = 1.6 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$; dibaryon mass parameter: $\Delta = 8.7$ MeV
- Theoretical quartet scattering length: $^4a_{\text{th}} = 6.33 \pm 0.10$ fm, compared to experimental $^4a = 6.35 \pm 0.02$ fm from Dilg (1975)
- Leading-order calculation (setting $\eta=0$, i.e., $r_0=0$) reproduces the Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosyan result
- Expected theoretical errors are of order $(r_0/a)^3$ and $(k r_0)^3$, smaller than experimental uncertainty at finite energy
- In the $J=1/2$ channel, the three-body force suppression is only $(r_0/a)^4$; the Efimov parameter $s_0 \simeq 1.006$ appears from solving $s \cos(s\pi/2) = (8/\sqrt{3})\sin(s\pi/6)$

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory approach (which at two-body level reproduces the effective range expansion) to the three-nucleon system, building on the power-counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) and Martin (1997) where pion exchanges are treated perturbatively. The $J=3/2$ channel results match the phase-shift analysis of van Oers (1978) and the zero-energy measurement of Dilg (1975). The $J=1/2$ channel analysis reveals the Thomas effect (Thomas, 1935) and Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) in a field-theoretic context, showing that three-body forces may be required at leading order, which challenges the predictive power of the effective theory unless a single counterterm suffices to renormalize the ultraviolet divergences.
