# On the chiral expansion of vector meson masses

> We study the chiral expansion of meson masses and decay constants using a chiral Lagrangian that was constructed previously based on the hadrogenesis conjecture. The one-loop self energies of the Goldstone bosons and vector mesons are evaluated. It is illustrated that a renormalizeable effective field theory arises once specific conditions on the low-energy constants are imposed. For the case where the hadrogenesis mass gap scale Lambda_{HG} is substantially larger than the chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi a partial summation scheme is required. All terms proportional to (M/Λ_χ)^n can be summed by a suitable renormalization, where M is the chiral and large-N_c limit of the vector meson masses in QCD. The size of loop effects from vector meson degrees of freedom is illustrated for physical quarks masses. Naturally sized effects are observed that have significant impact on the chiral structure of low-energy QCD with three light flavours.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a renormalizable effective field theory for vector mesons can be constructed based on the hadrogenesis conjecture, provided that specific conditions on the low-energy constants are imposed and that the hadrogenesis mass gap scale \(\Lambda_{\rm HG}\) is substantially larger than the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_\chi\). The claim is conditional: the consistency of the renormalization program requires that three terms initially proposed at order \(Q^2\) in the Lagrangian must instead contribute at order \(Q^4\) only.

## Method

The authors evaluate one-loop self energies of Goldstone bosons and vector mesons using a chiral Lagrangian previously constructed based on the hadrogenesis conjecture, with vector mesons described in the antisymmetric tensor representation. They compute tadpole and bubble contributions to the vector meson self energies, and derive the scale dependence of the low-energy constants. A partial summation scheme is introduced for the case where \(M/\Lambda_{\rm HG} \sim Q\), allowing all terms proportional to \((M/\Lambda_\chi)^n\) to be summed by suitable renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level estimate for the parameter \(h_1 = f_V h_P/f \simeq 2.5 \pm 0.25\), with \(f \simeq 90\) MeV as the chiral limit value of the pion or kaon decay constant
- The tree-level estimate for \(h_2 = h_A = 2.33 \pm 0.03\)
- The tree-level estimate for \(h_3 = f h_V/f_V \simeq 0.05\)
- The chiral limit decay width of vector mesons is estimated as \(\Gamma_\chi = h_1^2 M_\chi^3 / (2\pi (16 f)^2) \simeq 0.24\) GeV, obtained with \(M_\chi = 0.8\) GeV and \(f = 92\) MeV
- At renormalization scale \(\mu = M = 0.80\) GeV, the chiral limit vector meson mass correction is \(M^2_\chi - M^2 \simeq e_1 M^4 - 0.118 M^2\), which does not depend on the unknown parameters \(g_n\)

## Context

This work extends the hadrogenesis conjecture approach introduced by Terschlusen (2012) and Lutz (2008), which proposes that meson resonances not included as explicit degrees of freedom may be dynamically generated by coupled-channel dynamics. The paper addresses the open issue of whether the chiral Lagrangian with vector mesons can be renormalized convincingly, a question raised by Terschlusen (2016). The results support the dimensional counting scheme and enable future systematic studies of vector meson properties in low-energy QCD with three light flavours, while noting that for parameters \(c_i\) and \(g_i\) no reliable numerical estimates yet exist.
