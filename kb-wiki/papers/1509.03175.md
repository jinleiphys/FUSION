# In-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections determined from nucleon-induced reaction cross sections data

> Within the framework of the improved quantum molecular dynamics model, the medium modifications on the free nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections are investigated. By using various in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections in the model, the nucleon-induced reactions on various targets are simulated, and the excitation functions of reaction cross sections in the energy range from 25 MeV to 1 GeV are calculated. By comparing the calculations with the experimental data, an isospin, density, and momentum-dependence medium correction factor on free nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections is determined.

## Key claim

The authors determine an isospin-, density-, and momentum-dependent medium correction factor for free nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections by comparing improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model calculations of nucleon-induced reaction cross sections with experimental data, finding that the in-medium elastic cross sections must be suppressed below 500 MeV but approach the free values above that energy.

## Method

The authors used the ImQMD05 model to simulate proton- and neutron-induced reactions on targets from carbon to lead, with incident energies from 25 MeV to 1 GeV. They tested various parameterizations of a medium correction factor applied to the free isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections from Cugnon (1996), and compared the calculated excitation functions of reaction cross sections to experimental data to determine the best-fit parameters for the correction factor.

## Key numbers

- The energy range studied is from 25 MeV to 1 GeV for nucleon-induced reactions.
- For the density-dependent correction factor \(F_u\), the best-fit parameter set FU4 uses \(\lambda = 1/5\) and \(\zeta = 0.45\).
- For the momentum-dependent correction factor \(F_{u,\delta}\), the best-fit parameter set FP6 uses \(f_0 = 1\), \(p_0 = 0.725\) GeV/c, and \(\kappa = 10\).
- The momentum-dependent correction transitions at \(p_{\rm{NN}} = 1\) GeV/c, above which the correction factor becomes constant at \(f_0 = 1\).
- The isospin-dependent correction uses \(A(u) = 0.85/(1+3.25u)\) for the asymmetry term.

## Context

This work extends prior parameterizations of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections by Li (2006, 2011) and Wang (2014), which used similar functional forms but with different parameter sets. The authors show that nucleon-induced reactions, with their simpler reaction mechanism than heavy-ion collisions, provide a cleaner probe of in-medium cross sections below saturation density. The resulting parameterization (FU4FP6) successfully reproduces reaction cross sections for both proton- and neutron-induced reactions on targets from carbon to lead, including the neutron-rich nucleus \({}^{48}\)Ca, and can be used in transport models for nuclear reactions.
