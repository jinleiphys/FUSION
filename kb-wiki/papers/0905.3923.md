# Proof of Factorization Using Background Field Method of QCD

> Factorization theorem plays the central role at high energy colliders to study standard model and beyond standard model physics. The proof of factorization theorem is given by Collins, Soper and Sterman to all orders in perturbation theory by using diagrammatic approach. One might wonder if one can obtain the proof of factorization theorem through symmetry considerations at the lagrangian level. In this paper we provide such a proof.

## Key claim

The paper claims to provide a proof of the QCD factorization theorem through symmetry considerations at the Lagrangian level using the background field method, but the proof relies on a specific gauge-fixing identity derived in prior work (Nayak 2009) and is valid only when the background field is in an abelian-like pure gauge configuration.

## Method

The author uses the background field method of QCD to represent the soft and collinear gluon cloud as a classical background field in an abelian-like pure gauge. A gauge-fixing identity relating the standard QCD generating functional to the background field QCD generating functional is applied. By taking functional derivatives and appropriate color traces, the correlation functions in QCD are shown to factorize into Wilson line exponentials (which encode soft and collinear divergences) multiplied by correlation functions computed without the background field.

## Key numbers

- The Wilson line phase factor is given by \( V = g\,\omega(k) = i g \frac{h \cdot \mathcal{A}(k)}{h \cdot k} \), which reproduces the eikonal Feynman vertex for soft and collinear divergences.
- The lightlike vector \( n^\mu = (1,0,0) \) or \( n^\mu = (0,1,0) \) is used to reproduce soft gluon divergences; a non-lightlike vector \( n_B^\mu = (n_B^+, n_B^-, 0) \) reproduces collinear divergences.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic proof of factorization by Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1980s) by providing an alternative Lagrangian-level derivation. It addresses an incomplete attempt by Tucci (1985) to prove factorization using the background field method, which was successful for QED (Nayak 2008) but incomplete for QCD due to the gauge-fixing term's dependence on the background field. The result enables a symmetry-based understanding of factorization and may simplify future proofs for more complex processes.
