# Open questions in QCD at high parton density

> I present the state of our understanding of the QCD dynamics at play in the parton saturation regime of nuclear wave functions. I explain what are the biggest open questions in the field, their intrinsic interest, but also why is it important to answer them from the quark-gluon-plasma physicists' perspective. Focusing on those aspects that proton-nucleus collisions cannot investigate to a satisfactory degree, I show that future high-energy electron-ion colliders have the potential to address these questions, providing thorough answers in most cases, and exploratory measurements otherwise.

## Key claim

Parton saturation is likely already relevant at today's colliders, but the author acknowledges that the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory is not yet widely accepted because its applicability can be questioned when saturation scales drop below 1 GeV, and because alternative explanations exist for each observable.

## Method

The paper reviews the current understanding of QCD dynamics in the parton saturation regime of nuclear wave functions, focusing on the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. It identifies open questions in the field and evaluates which measurements at future electron-ion colliders (EIC and LHeC) could provide smoking-gun evidence for saturation effects, comparing these to what can be achieved with proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale scales roughly as \(Q_s \sim A^{1/3}\) for nuclear targets
- At HERA, the NLO DGLAP description of diffractive structure functions breaks down below \(Q^2 \sim 8\) GeV\(^2\)
- The ratio \(\sigma_{diff}/\sigma_{tot}\) is predicted to be a factor two larger in e+Au than in e+p collisions in the low \(M_X\) range
- A proton in its rest frame hit by a 25 TeV electron remains intact 10% of the time in diffractive DIS

## Context

This paper extends the work on parton saturation and the Color Glass Condensate framework reviewed by Albacete (year not given in text) and addresses open questions identified in the e+A chapter of the INT report on the EIC physics case (Boer et al., 2011), the EIC white paper (Deshpande et al., 2012), and the LHeC Conceptual Design Report (Abelleira Fernandez et al., 2012). The paper argues that electron-ion collisions are uniquely positioned to resolve uncertainties in initial-state physics that currently limit the extraction of quark-gluon plasma properties like \(\eta/s\) from heavy-ion collision data, and that measurements such as diffractive DIS and di-hadron correlations can provide unambiguous signals of saturation that proton-nucleus collisions cannot deliver.
