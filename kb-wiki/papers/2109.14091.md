# $g$-modes of neutron stars with hadron-to-quark crossover transitions

> We perform the first study of the principal core $g$-mode oscillation in hybrid stars containing quark matter, utilizing a crossover model for the hadron-to-quark transition inspired by lattice QCD. The ensuing results are compared with our recent findings of $g$-mode frequencies in hybrid stars with a first-order phase transition using Gibbs constructions. We find that models using Gibbs construction yield $g$-mode amplitudes and the associated gravitational energy radiated that dominate over those of the chosen crossover model owing to the distinct behaviors of the equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds in the various models. Based on our results, we conclude that were $g$-modes to be detected in upgraded LIGO and Virgo detectors it would indicate a first-order phase transition akin to a Gibbs construction.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first study of core g-mode oscillations in hybrid stars with a hadron-to-quark crossover transition, finding that g-mode amplitudes and gravitational wave emission from crossover models are significantly smaller than those from first-order phase transitions using Gibbs constructions; the authors conclude that detection of g-modes by upgraded LIGO and Virgo would indicate a first-order phase transition rather than a crossover.

## Method

The authors extended the Kapusta-Welle (KW) crossover model to include beta-equilibrium, a crust, and vector interactions among quarks, combining a Zhao-Lattimer (ZL) hadronic EOS with a vMIT bag model for quark matter using a chemical-potential-dependent switching function. They calculated the equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds for both the crossover model and a Gibbs-constructed first-order transition, then computed the Brunt-Vaisala frequency and the resulting g-mode oscillation frequencies for hybrid neutron stars. The calculations were performed at zero temperature without superfluidity effects in either nucleons or quarks.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses supported by the crossover EOS: 2.11 Msolar (XOA), 2.04 Msolar (XOB), and 2.13 Msolar (XOC)
- Radius of a 1.4 Msolar neutron star: 12.4 km (XOA and XOB), 13.8 km (XOC)
- Tidal deformability at 1.4 Msolar: 428.9 (XOA), 426.7 (XOB), 841.3 (XOC)
- Central squared equilibrium sound speed at 1.4 Msolar: 0.398 c^2 (XOA), 0.331 c^2 (XOB), 0.294 c^2 (XOC)
- Central squared adiabatic sound speed at 1.4 Msolar: 0.429 c^2 (XOA), 0.331 c^2 (XOB), 0.497 c^2 (XOC)
- Crossover scale parameter mu_0: 1.8 GeV for all three parameter sets
- Quark vector interaction parameter a: 0.20 fm^2 (XOA), 0.23 fm^2 (XOB), 0.15 fm^2 (XOC)
- Bag constant B^{1/4}: 180.0 MeV for all three parameter sets

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Jaikumar 2021, referred to as Paper I) of g-mode frequencies in hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions using Gibbs constructions, now comparing with the crossover model of Kapusta and Welle (2021). The results show that Gibbs-constructed models produce g-mode amplitudes and gravitational energy that dominate over crossover models due to distinct behaviors of equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds, enabling future gravitational wave observations to distinguish between first-order and crossover transitions in neutron star interiors. The paper notes that quarkyonic shell models of McLerran and Reddy (2018) and Zhao and Lattimer (2020) are unsuitable for g-mode calculations in their current form due to superluminal adiabatic sound speeds.
