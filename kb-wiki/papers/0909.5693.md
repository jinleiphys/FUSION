# Nuclear Astrophysics with Radioactive Beams

> The quest to comprehend how nuclear processes influence astrophysical phenomena is driving experimental and theoretical research programs worldwide. One of the main goals in nuclear astrophysics is to understand how energy is generated in stars, how elements are synthesized in stellar events and what the nature of neutron stars is. New experimental capabilities, the availability of radioactive beams and increased computational power paired with new astronomical observations have advanced the present knowledge. This review summarizes the progress in the field of nuclear astrophysics with a focus on the role of indirect methods and reactions involving beams of rare isotopes.

## Key claim

This review summarizes progress in nuclear astrophysics with a focus on indirect methods and reactions involving beams of rare isotopes, but notes that many reaction rates for explosive burning processes such as the rp process in X-ray bursts are still based exclusively on theory, and that a site capable of producing r-process elements self-consistently has not been identified.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism for thermonuclear reaction rates, including charged-particle reactions, neutron-induced reactions, photodisintegration, electron and positron capture, and beta-decay, with the reaction network equations expressed in terms of nuclear abundances. It then surveys theoretical reaction models used to predict cross sections when direct measurements are impossible, including potential models, the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), microscopic models such as the Resonating Group Method (RGM) and Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) method for peripheral reactions.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow peak location for charged-particle reactions is given by \(E_0 = 1.22 (Z_j^2 Z_k^2 m_{jk} T_6^2)^{1/3}\) keV, with effective width \(\Delta = 0.749 (Z_j^2 Z_k^2 m_{jk} T_6^5)^{1/6}\) keV, where \(T_6\) is temperature in \(10^6\) K.
- For s-wave neutron-induced reactions, the effective energy window is \(E_0 \approx 0.172 T_9 (l + 1/2)\) MeV with width \(\Delta \approx 0.194 T_9 (l + 1/2)^{1/2}\) MeV, where \(T_9\) is temperature in \(10^9\) K.
- Early ANC method calculations obtained \(S_{17}(0) = 15.5\) eV b for the \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B reaction using \(0\hbar\omega\) oscillator wave functions and the M3Y(E) effective NN potential.

## Context

This review extends the work of prior reviews on nuclear structure input to nuclear astrophysics (Grawe 2007) and on X-ray burst reaction sequences (Schatz and Rehm 2006, Fisker 2008), as well as earlier treatments of the r process (Cowan 1991, Qian 2003). It enables future work by surveying the theoretical and experimental toolkit available at rare-isotope beam facilities, identifying key open questions such as the unknown site of the r process and the need for improved measurements of reactions like \(^7\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^8\)B and \(^{12}\)C(\(\alpha\),\(\gamma\))\(^{16}\)O.
