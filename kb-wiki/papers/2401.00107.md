# Radius extrapolations for two-body bound states in finite volume

> Simulations of quantum systems in finite volume have proven to be a useful tool for calculating physical observables. Such studies to date have focused primarily on understanding the volume dependence of binding energies, from which it is possible to extract asymptotic properties of the corresponding bound state, as well as on extracting scattering information. For bound states, all properties depend on the size of the finite volume, and for precision studies it is important to understand such effects. In this work, we therefore derive the volume dependence of the mean squared radius of a two-body bound state, using a technique that can be generalized to other static properties in the future. We test our results with explicit numerical examples and demonstrate that we can robustly extract infinite-volume radii from finite-volume simulations in cubic boxes with periodic boundary conditions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading-order finite-volume dependence of the mean squared radius for two-body bound states in cubic boxes with periodic boundary conditions, showing that the radius shift can be expressed in terms of the known energy shift plus additional terms that depend on the asymptotic wave function; the derivation assumes that the correction term in the interior wave function is independent of box size to leading order, which the authors verify explicitly only for S-wave and P-wave states.

## Method

The authors construct an ansatz for the finite-volume wave function by combining an asymptotic solution (expanded in the energy shift) with an interior solution, using translation operators to enforce periodic boundary conditions. They then expand the expectation value of the squared radius operator in powers of the energy shift and the asymptotic wave function, keeping only terms of leading exponential order. The resulting expression is simplified by exploiting cubic symmetry and applying rotation operators to reduce the sum over nearest-neighbor lattice vectors to a single direction.

## Key numbers

* The energy shift scales as \(\Eshift = \order{e^{-\kappa L}}\) where \(\kappa^2 = 2\mu \Einfp\) and \(\Einfp\) is the infinite-volume binding energy.
* Corrections beyond leading order are of order \(\order{e^{-\sqrt{2}\kappa L}}\).
* The finite-volume radius shift \(\Delta\!\expval{r^2}(L)\) is given by Eq. (??) as \(\alpha \Eshift\) plus a sum over nearest-neighbor lattice vectors of matrix elements involving the asymptotic wave function and factors of \(L^2 - 2\vecr\cdot\vecn L\).
* The asymptotic wave function for a state with orbital angular momentum \(\ell\) is \(\psiinfa(\vecr) = -i^\ell \gamma h_\ell^{(1)}(i\kappa r)Y_\ell^m(\vecr/r)\) with ANC \(\gamma\).

## Context

This work extends the well-known finite-volume formalism for binding energies (Luscher 1985, Konig 2011, Konig 2017) to static observables beyond the energy, specifically the mean squared radius. The derivation generalizes techniques used for energy shifts in Refs. (More 2013, Furnstahl 2012, Furnstahl 2013) based on the "linear energy method" of Djajaputra (2000). The results are directly applicable to Lattice QCD studies of the deuteron radius and to Lattice EFT simulations of atomic nuclei, and the formalism paves the way for deriving analogous relations for other static observables and for bound states with more than two particles.
