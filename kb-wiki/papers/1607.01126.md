# Approach to Chandrasekhar-Kendall-Woltjer State in a Chiral Plasma

> We study the time evolution of the magnetic field in a plasma with a chiral magnetic current. The Vector Spherical Harmonic functions (VSH) are used to expand all fields. We define a measure for the Chandrasekhar-Kendall-Woltjer (CKW) state, which has a simple form in VSH expansion. We propose the conditions for a general class of initial momentum spectra that will evolve into the CKW state. For this class of initial conditions, to approach the CKW state, (i) a non-vanishing chiral magnetic conductivity is necessary, and (ii) the time integration of the product of the electric resistivity and chiral magnetic conductivity must grow faster than the time integration of the resistivity. We give a few examples to test these conditions numerically which work very well.

## Key claim

The paper identifies two necessary and sufficient conditions for a chiral plasma to evolve into the Chandrasekhar-Kendall-Woltjer (CKW) state: a non-vanishing chiral magnetic conductivity must be present, and the time-integrated product of electric resistivity and chiral magnetic conductivity must grow faster than the time-integrated resistivity alone. The abstract omits the precise mathematical condition that the ratio \(\Theta(t)/\sqrt{\Lambda(t)}\) must diverge as \(t\to\infty\).

## Method

The authors expand the magnetic vector potential in Vector Spherical Harmonic (VSH) functions, which are eigenfunctions of the curl operator, and derive evolution equations for the mode amplitudes from the Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations (neglecting the displacement current). They define a measure \(\cos^2\theta(t)\) based on the alignment between the magnetic field \(\mathbf{B}\) and the current \(\mathbf{j}=\nabla\times\mathbf{B}\), and express this measure in terms of integrals over the VSH mode spectra \(g_\pm(t,k)\). The time evolution of these spectra is solved analytically for initial spectra of the form \(k^r e^{-2C_1 k - C_2^2 k^2}\), and the asymptotic behavior of \(\cos^2\theta(t)\) is analyzed in terms of the functions \(\Lambda(t)=\int\eta\,dt\) and \(\Theta(t)=\int\eta\sigma_\chi\,dt\).

## Key numbers

- In Au+Au collisions at RHIC with \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, the peak magnetic field is about \(5m_\pi^2\) or \(1.4\times10^{18}\) Gauss.
- In Pb+Pb collisions at LHC with \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) TeV, the peak magnetic field can be 10 times as large as at RHIC.
- For power-law decaying conductivities \(\sigma\sim t^{-\alpha}\) and \(\sigma_\chi\sim t^{-\beta}\), the condition for reaching the CKW state is \(\beta < (1+\alpha)/2\).
- In the example with constant \(\sigma=\sigma_0\) and \(\sigma_\chi=\sigma_{\chi0}=1/L\) (case a), \(\cos^2\theta(t)\) evolves from 0.8 at initial time to 1 as \(t\to\infty\).
- In the example with \(\sigma=\sigma_0\) and \(\sigma_\chi=0\) (case b), \(\cos^2\theta(t)\) saturates at 0.884 as \(t\to\infty\).

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral magnetic effects in heavy-ion collisions, where the chiral magnetic current \(\sigma_\chi\mathbf{B}\) arises from an imbalance of right- and left-handed quarks (the chiral anomaly). The CKW state, also known as the Taylor state or Woltjer-Taylor state in classical plasma physics, was first studied by Chandrasekhar, Kendall, and Woltjer (1956-1958) as a force-free magnetic configuration, and Taylor (1974, 1986) argued it emerges from selective decay in weakly dissipative plasmas. The paper builds on earlier work by Chernodub (2010) who noted that in the absence of Ohmic current the system reaches a CKW state with \(C=\sigma_\chi\), and by Hirono (2015) who observed spectral concentration in numerical simulations. The results enable predictions for which experimental conditions in heavy-ion collisions or Dirac/Weyl semimetals will lead to self-organization into the CKW state.
