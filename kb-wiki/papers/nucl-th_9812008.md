# Isospin structure of one- and two-phonon GDR excitations

> Isospin is included in the description of Coulomb excitation of multiple giant isovector dipole resonances. In the excitation of even-even nuclei, a relevant portion of the excitation strength is shown to be associated with 1+ two-phonon states, which tends to be hindered or completely supressed in calculations in which the isospin degree of freedom is not considered. We find that the excitation cross sections is strongly dependent on the ground state isospin.

# Isospin structure of one- and two-phonon GDR excitations

## Key claim

Including isospin in the description of Coulomb excitation of double giant isovector dipole resonances (DGDR) in even-even nuclei reveals that a relevant portion of the excitation strength is associated with 1+ two-phonon states, which are hindered or completely suppressed when isospin is neglected; the effect is strongly dependent on the ground state isospin of the target nucleus, becoming more pronounced for nuclei with lower isospin.

## Method

The authors extended the isospin splitting formalism of Fallieros (1971) to two-phonon states and performed semiclassical coupled-channels calculations using the RELEX code, solving a time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the intrinsic nuclear degrees of freedom with the projectile-target motion approximated by a straight-line classical trajectory at relativistic energies. The electromagnetic matrix elements were assumed to be isospin independent, with the isospin dependence entering through 3J and 6J coefficients in isospin space and through the energy splitting of states with different total isospin. Calculations were performed for 208Pb (T=22) and 48Ca (T=4) targets bombarded by 208Pb projectiles at 640 MeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Isospin splitting of the one-phonon GDR isospin doublet in 208Pb: Δ(1)T+1 = 6.63 MeV (T=22 ground state)
- Isospin splitting of the one-phonon GDR isospin doublet in 48Ca: Δ(1)T+1 = 6.25 MeV (T=4 ground state)
- Isospin splitting of the two-phonon T+2 state in 208Pb: Δ(2)T+2 = 13.85 MeV
- Isospin splitting of the two-phonon T+2 state in 48Ca: Δ(2)T+2 = 15 MeV
- GDR cross section for 208Pb: 2240 mb for the T=22 state at 13.5 MeV; 59.86 mb for the T=23 state at 20.1 MeV (about 3% of total GDR cross section)
- GDR cross section for 48Ca: 318.5 mb for the T=4 state at 19.2 MeV; 33.11 mb for the T=5 state at 25.4 MeV (over 10% of GDR cross section)
- DGDR cross section for 208Pb: 8.373 mb for the T=23, Jπ=1+ state at 33.6 MeV (about 6% of total DGDR cross section)
- DGDR cross section for 48Ca: 1.275 mb for the T=5, Jπ=1+ state at 44.6 MeV (32% of the dominant T=4 DGDR cross section)
- Reduced matrix element for GS to GDR transition in 208Pb: 7.16 fm·e (T=22) compared to 7.39 fm·e without isospin
- Reduced matrix element for GS to GDR transition in 48Ca: 2.66 fm·e (T=4) compared to 3.00 fm·e without isospin

## Context

This work extends the pioneering isospin splitting analysis of the isovector giant dipole resonance by Fallieros (1971) to the double giant dipole resonance, addressing the longstanding discrepancy where coupled-channels Coulomb excitation theory underestimated DGDR data by a factor of 2-3 for 136Xe and 197Au (Schmidt 1993, Aumann 1993). Previous attempts to resolve this discrepancy considered anharmonicities (Volpe 1995, Bortignon 1997) and the Brink-Axel mechanism (Carlson 1998). The present results show that isospin effects redistribute electromagnetic strength and enhance 1+ DGDR states, with the effect being much stronger for 48Ca (low isospin) than for 208Pb (high isospin), suggesting that isospin splitting should contribute to broadening the DGDR, particularly for nuclei with low ground state isospin. This formalism is expected to be especially relevant for studying excitation cross sections across isotopic chains from the proton to the neutron dripline.
