# LHCb $P_c^+$ Resonances as Molecular States

> Recent experiments at the LHCb detector have once again raised the possibility that quark bags may exist containing more than three quarks. Specifically the LHCb collaboration points to evidence for hadronic resonances decaying into J/$ψ$ and proton: the $P_c(4450)^+$ and $P_C(4380)^+$. Here we put forth a case that a reasonable description of these states is possible as molecular resonances of a $p$ and a J/$ψ$. Our model seems to accommodate the observed states and their measured widths, both the lower lying, broader, negative parity state and the higher lying, narrow positive parity state. If these resonances do indeed exist one might envision a rich spectroscopy of such pentaquark states waiting to be discovered, though this would deepen the mystery of their absence in earlier hadron spectroscopy. Perhaps the presence of the heavy charmed quarks, as well as the lighter u,d and s quarks, is the determining factor.

# LHCb $P_c^+$ Resonances as Molecular States

## Key claim
The two pentaquark candidates observed by LHCb, $P_c(4450)^+$ and $P_c(4380)^+$, can be described as molecular resonances of a proton and a J/$\psi$ bound by a surface potential, with the higher-mass state assigned orbital angular momentum $l=4$ and the lower-mass state $l=3$, naturally explaining their opposite parities and differing widths. The authors hedge that this description is "reasonable" and "seems to accommodate" the data, but note that the existence of such states would deepen the mystery of why pentaquarks were absent in earlier hadron spectroscopy.

## Method
The authors employed a non-relativistic potential model using the GAMOW code to find resonant states of a proton and J/$\psi$ system. They explored both volume (Woods-Saxon) and surface forms of the inter-hadronic potential, ultimately presenting results for a surface potential of the form $V_S(l)=V_s x^{\alpha-1}(1-x)^{\beta-1}$ with $\alpha=9$, $\beta=5$, and scale parameter $r_0=0.85$ fm. The potential well depth was adjusted to reproduce the observed excitation energies, yielding predicted widths as outputs of the calculation.

## Key numbers
- $P_c(4450)^+$: excitation energy $E=410$ MeV, total mass $M=4450$ MeV, assigned $l=4$, predicted width $\Gamma \simeq 50$ MeV, potential depth $V_S \simeq 2360$ MeV
- $P_c(4380)^+$: excitation energy $E=340$ MeV, assigned $l=3$, predicted width $\Gamma \simeq 142$ MeV, potential depth $V_S=1410$ MeV
- Ratio of potential depths $V_S(3)/V_S(4) \sim 1/2$, consistent with the $l$-dependence from the Hasenfratz-Kuti surface potential formula
- The atomic components (J/$\psi$ and proton) have radii of approximately $0.3$ fm and $0.8$ fm respectively, fitting comfortably inside the molecular state

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier molecular description of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark candidate (Kahana and Kahana, prior work), which was later disconfirmed, to the higher-quality LHCb data. The approach uses the Hasenfratz-Kuti model (Hasenfratz and Kuti, prior work) with deformable bag surfaces and surface oscillations. The authors contrast their molecular picture with purely bag-like or soliton-like pentaquark models, which they argue produce low angular momentum states with very large widths (Capstick, prior work). If confirmed, this work enables searches for related states with $b\bar{b}$ replacing $c\bar{c}$ in the J/$\psi$, or with higher charmonium states such as $\psi'$, and suggests a possible $l=2$ resonance at lower excitation energy ($E\sim 100-200$ MeV) that might be observable.
