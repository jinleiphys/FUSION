# Two-photon exchange effect on deuteron electromagnetic form factors

> Corrections of two-photon exchange to proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors are employed to study the effect of two-photon exchange on the deuteron electromagnetic form factors. Numerical results of the effect are given. It is suggested to test the effect in the measurement of $P_z$ in a small angle limit.

## Key claim

Two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections to the deuteron electromagnetic form factors are estimated by incorporating TPE corrections to the proton and neutron form factors from the work of Blunden, Melnitchouk and Tjon (2005), under the assumption that the dominant TPE contribution comes from direct coupling of the two photons to one of the nucleons inside the deuteron. The authors suggest that the TPE effect can be tested in the measurement of the polarization observable \(P_z\) in the small angle limit, where the one-photon-exchange contribution to \(P_z\) vanishes while the TPE contribution remains non-vanishing.

## Method

The authors compute the matrix element of the electromagnetic current for elastic electron-deuteron scattering including two-photon exchange, using an effective Lagrangian approach where the deuteron is treated as a weakly bound proton-neutron system with a Gaussian correlation function characterized by a size parameter \(\Lambda_D\). The TPE corrections to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors (including the new form factor \(\tilde F_3^{'N}\)) from Blunden, Melnitchouk and Tjon (2005) are inserted into the deuteron current via the diagrams where both photons couple to the same nucleon. The coupling constant \(g_D\) is fixed by the compositeness condition (vanishing wave function renormalization), and the resulting TPE contributions to the deuteron charge, magnetic, and quadrupole form factors (\(G_C, G_M, G_Q\)) and to a new form factor \(G_6'\) are evaluated numerically for three scattering angles (\(\theta = \pi/6, \pi/2, 5\pi/6\)).

## Key numbers

- The size parameter for the deuteron is chosen as \(\Lambda_D = 0.30\) GeV, based on the constraint \(\langle r^{-2} \rangle < 0.02\) GeV\(^2\) from Mathiot (1984).
- The typical magnitudes of the ratios \(R_C = G_C^{(2)}/G_C'\) and \(R_M = G_M^{(2)}/G_M'\) due to TPE corrections are always less than 10\%.
- The ratio \(R(P_x) = P_x^{(2)}/P_x\) from the new form factor \(G_6'\) is less than 1\% in the range \(0.5 \leq Q^2 \leq 3\) GeV\(^2\).
- The deuteron charge form factor \(G_C'\) has a crossing point at \(Q^2 \sim 0.7\) GeV\(^2\) and the magnetic form factor \(G_M'\) at \(Q^2 \sim 2.0\) GeV\(^2\), based on the parametrization of Garcon and Van Orden (2001).

## Context

This work extends the study of two-photon exchange effects from elastic electron-proton scattering (where TPE was invoked to resolve the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the proton form factor ratio) to the deuteron. It builds on the TPE formalism for nucleons by Blunden, Melnitchouk and Tjon (2005) and on previous effective Lagrangian calculations of deuteron form factors by Dong and collaborators (2008). The results suggest that the polarization observable \(P_z\) (or \(T_{10}\)) in the small angle limit is a clean probe of TPE effects, and the authors note that additional TPE sources (e.g., photons coupling to the deuteron contact points) remain to be included in future work.
