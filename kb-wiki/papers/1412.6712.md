# The equation of state in two-, three-, and four-color QCD at non-zero temperature and density

> We calculate the equation of state at non-zero temperature and density from first principles in two-, three- and four-color QCD with two fermion flavors in the fundamental and two-index, antisymmetric representation. By matching low-energy results (from a `hadron resonance gas') to high-energy results from (resummed) perturbative QCD, we obtain results for the pressure and trace anomaly that are in quantitative agreement with full lattice-QCD studies for three colors at zero chemical potential. Our results for non-zero chemical potential at zero temperature constitute predictions for the equation of state in QCD-like theories that can be tested by traditional lattice studies for two-color QCD with two fundamental fermions and four-color QCD with two two-index, antisymmetric fermions. We find that the speed of sound squared at zero temperature can exceed one third, which may be relevant for the phenomenology of high-mass neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the equation of state for two-, three-, and four-color QCD with two fermion flavors by matching a hadron resonance gas (low energy) to resummed perturbative QCD (high energy), finding that the speed of sound squared at zero temperature can exceed one third. The quantitative agreement with lattice QCD for three colors at zero chemical potential is used to argue that the same matching procedure yields predictions for QCD-like theories that can be tested by future lattice studies, but the four-color antisymmetric case with one specific scale choice could not be matched at zero chemical potential in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors compute the high-temperature and high-density equation of state using resummed perturbative QCD (pQCD) at three-loop order, following the methods of Kajantie et al. (2002, 2003) and Vuorinen (2003) with resummation modifications from Blaizot et al. (2003). The low-temperature and low-density equation of state is computed using a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, where the hadron spectrum for two- and four-color theories is determined from group-theoretic arguments, Fermi statistics, and large-N expansions, with the scale set by the string tension and the relation from Lucini et al. (2008). The two asymptotic equations of state are matched by requiring equal pressures and equal first derivatives at a transition point, with a bag constant added to the pQCD pressure, and the renormalization scale is varied by a factor of two to produce uncertainty bands.

## Key numbers

- For three-color QCD with three fundamental quarks, the lattice data from the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal Collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2013) and the HotQCD Collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2014) agree reasonably well with the HRG+pQCD band for both pressure and trace anomaly at zero chemical potential.
- The ratio Lambda_MSbar / sqrt(sigma) for two-color QCD with two fundamental fermions is 1.032, and for four-color QCD with two fundamental fermions it is 0.723, obtained by scaling pure-gauge values from Lucini et al. (2008) using the ratio from Fritzsch et al. (2012).
- For the four-color antisymmetric theory, two values of Lambda_MSbar / sqrt(sigma) are used: 0.527 (pure-glue value from Lucini et al., 2008) and 0.723 (same as the four-color fundamental value), with the expectation that the true value lies in this range.
- The critical temperature T_c for all theories is within 20 percent of each other when scaled by sqrt(sigma), while the critical chemical potential mu_c spans a much broader range.
- For the four-color antisymmetric case with Lambda_MSbar / sqrt(sigma) = 0.723, the matching procedure at zero chemical potential in the chiral limit could not be carried out because the HRG pressure rose too sharply and never intersected the pQCD pressure band.

## Context

This work extends the HRG+pQCD matching approach previously applied to three-color QCD by Laine (2006), Huovinen et al. (2009), and Kurkela et al. (2009) to QCD-like theories with two and four colors, which do not suffer from the sign problem and are thus amenable to direct lattice simulations. The results provide predictions for the equation of state at non-zero chemical potential that can be tested by traditional lattice studies, enabling indirect validation of non-traditional methods for QCD at large densities relevant to neutron star phenomenology. The authors note that the speed of sound squared exceeding one third at zero temperature may be relevant for high-mass neutron stars, and the similar behavior of all theories when scaled by T_c or mu_c extends the known phenomenon from pure-gauge theories (Panero, 2009).
