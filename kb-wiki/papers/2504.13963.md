# Rapidity scan with DCCI at LHC energy

> We extend the dynamical core-corona initialization (DCCI2) model to include the baryon number evolution in the entire system created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Introducing the source term for the baryon number, we describe the early-stage equilibration and later-stage hydrodynamic evolution of the baryon number throughout the system from midrapidity to forward rapidity. Through numerical simulations with this extended model, we show that extremely large baryon chemical potentials are realized in forward rapidity regions at the LHC energy and are comparable to those of the BES energies. Moreover, we show that fluctuations of baryon chemical potentials are large and, consequently, negative baryon chemical potential regions appear at midrapidity.

## Key claim
The extended DCCI2 model shows that extremely large baryon chemical potentials are realized in forward rapidity regions at LHC energy, comparable to those at RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) energies, while large fluctuations of baryon chemical potentials at midrapidity can produce negative baryon chemical potential regions even there.

## Method
The authors extended the dynamical core-corona initialization (DCCI2) model by adding a baryon number conservation equation with a source term, where baryon number from dead partons is dynamically deposited into a locally equilibrated QGP fluid. Initial partons were generated using Pythia8 and Pythia8 Angantyr, and the fluid evolution used the NEOS-BQS equation of state incorporating finite baryon, charge, and strangeness chemical potentials consistent with lattice QCD. Particlization was performed on a hypersurface at constant energy density of 0.547 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers
- Pb+Pb collision at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV with impact parameter b = 2.46 fm
- Particlization hypersurface at constant energy density e(T,mu_B) = 0.547 GeV/fm^3
- In midrapidity region (-1 <= eta_s <= 1), some hypersurface elements reach mu_B ≈ -0.4 GeV
- In forward rapidity region (6 <= eta_s <= 8), some elements reach mu_B = 0.6 GeV
- Averaged mu_B in the range 7 <= |eta_s| <= 8 is comparable to that of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV
- Beam rapidity y_beam ≈ 8 for this collision energy

## Context
This work extends the DCCI2 model of Kanakubo (2020) to include baryon number evolution, building on earlier suggestions by Li (2017) that high baryon density exists in forward rapidity regions at top RHIC and LHC energies. The results enable rapidity scan analysis as a complementary approach to RHIC-BES for exploring the high baryon chemical potential region of the QCD phase diagram, and highlight the need for careful treatment of negative baryon chemical potential fluctuations in dynamical models.
