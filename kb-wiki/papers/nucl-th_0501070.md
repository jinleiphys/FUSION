# Number of Spin $I$ States of Identical Particles

> In this paper we study the enumeration of number (denoted as ${D_I}$) of spin $I$ states for fermions in a single-$j$ shell and bosons with spin $l$. We show that $D_I$ can be enumerated by the reduction from SU$(n+1)$ to SO(3). New regularities of $D_I$ are discerned.

# Number of Spin \(I\) States of Identical Particles

## Key claim

The number \(D_I\) of spin \(I\) states for fermions in a single-\(j\) shell or bosons with spin \(l\) can be enumerated via reduction from SU\((n+1)\) to SO(3), and for even particle number \(n\) there is an exact identity between the boson and fermion state counts when \(2l = 2j+1-n\); for odd \(n\) the method requires fictitious bosons with half-integer spin and the correspondence does not hold.

## Method

The authors propose an alternative counting procedure (Process B) based on partitions of an integer \(I_0\) with constraints on the parts, which is equivalent to the standard combinatorial subtraction of angular-momentum projection counts. They then show that Process B for \(\bar{n}\) bosons of spin \(L = n/2\) (with \(\bar{n}=2l\) for bosons or \(\bar{n}=2j+1-n\) for fermions) reproduces the same \(D_I\) as the original system, exploiting the SU\((n+1)\) symmetry that is independent of \(j\) or \(l\). For the specific case \(n=4\), they derive closed-form analytic expressions for \(D_I\) by applying the known reduction rules of SU(5) to SO(3) for \(d\) bosons, using the interacting-boson-model classification in terms of seniority \(\nu\), \(n_\Delta\), and \(\lambda\).

## Key numbers

* For four bosons with spin \(l\) (or four fermions in a single-\(j\) shell with \(2l=2j-3\)), the number of even-spin states \(D_{I=2I_0}\) for \(I_0 \le l\) is given by a formula involving \(k = \lfloor 2l/6 \rfloor\) and \(\kappa = 2l \bmod 6\); for \(\kappa=0\): \(D_{I=2I_0} = (I_0+1)k - (9K^2 - K + 3K\mathcal{K} + (2\mathcal{K}-5)\theta(2\mathcal{K}-5)) + \delta_{\mathcal{K}0}\), where \(K = \lfloor I_0/6 \rfloor\) and \(\mathcal{K} = I_0 \bmod 6\).
* For odd \(I\) with \(I \le 2l\), the relation \(D_{I=2I_0} - D_{I=2I_0+3} = \lfloor I_0/2 \rfloor + 1\) holds.
* For \(I \ge 2l\) (even or odd), \(D_{I_{\text{max}} - 2I_0} = D_{I_{\text{max}} - 2I_0-3} = 3\lfloor I_0/6 \rfloor(\lfloor I_0/6 \rfloor+1) - \lfloor I_0/6 \rfloor + (\lfloor I_0/6 \rfloor+1)((I_0 \bmod 6)+1) + \delta_{(I_0 \bmod 6),0} - 1\).

## Context

This work extends the classic enumeration methods of Racah (seniority scheme) and the generating-function approach of Katriel et al. and Sunko et al., providing a new algebraic route via SU\((n+1)\) reduction that yields analytic formulas where previously only empirical constructions existed (e.g., Zhao for \(n=3,4,5\)). The results enable systematic tabulation of spin-state counts for larger \(j\) or \(l\) without tedious combinatorial enumeration, and the boson-fermion identity for even \(n\) connects the single-\(j\) shell problem to the interacting boson model of Arima and Iachello. For odd \(n\), the method remains valid but requires further study of the fictitious half-integer-spin bosons.
