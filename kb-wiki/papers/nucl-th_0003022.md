# Tilted Pion Sources from Azimuthally Sensitive HBT Interferometry

> Intensity interferometry in noncentral heavy ion collisions provides access to novel information on the geometry of the effective pion-emitting source. We demonstrate analytically that, even for vanishing pair momentum, the cross terms $R_{ol}^2$ and $R_{sl}^2$ of the HBT correlation function in general show a strong first harmonic in their azimuthal dependence. The strength of this oscillation characterizes the tilt of the major axis of the spatial emission ellipsoid away from the direction of the beam. Event generator studies indicate that this tilt can be large (>20 degrees) at AGS energies which makes it by far the most significant azimuthally sensitive HBT signal at these energies. Moreover, transport models suggest that for pions this spatial tilt is directed opposite to the tilt of the directed flow ellipsoid in momentum space. A measurement of the azimuthal dependence of the HBT cross terms $R_{ol}^2$ and $R_{sl}^2$ thus probes directly the physical origin of directed pion flow.

## Key claim

The cross terms \(R_{ol}^2\) and \(R_{sl}^2\) of the HBT correlation function show a strong first harmonic in their azimuthal dependence even for vanishing pair momentum, and the strength of this oscillation characterizes the tilt of the major axis of the spatial emission ellipsoid away from the beam direction; this tilt can be large (\(>20^\circ\)) at AGS energies, making it the most significant azimuthally sensitive HBT signal at those energies.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the azimuthal (\(\Phi\)) dependence of all six HBT radius parameters \(R_{ij}^2\) in terms of the spatial correlation tensor \(S_{\mu\nu}\) (the Gaussian widths of the emission function in space-time). They then applied symmetry arguments at midrapidity for noncentral collisions of equal-mass nuclei to show that five off-diagonal components of \(S_{\mu\nu}\) vanish, leaving a single off-diagonal component \(S_{13} \equiv \langle \tilde x \tilde z \rangle\) that generates first-harmonic oscillations in \(R_{ol}^2\) and \(R_{sl}^2\). The tilt angle \(\theta_s\) of the source ellipsoid in the reaction plane was defined in terms of \(S_{13}\), \(S_{11}\), and \(S_{33}\). The analysis was validated with a toy Gaussian source model (with and without longitudinal flow) and with simulations using the RQMD (v2.3) transport model for semiperipheral Au+Au collisions at 2 \(A\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The tilt angle \(\theta_s\) extracted from a global fit to the toy model (with \(\Theta = 25^\circ\), \(\sigma_t = 5\) fm/\(c\), \(\sigma_x = 4\) fm, \(\sigma_y = 5\) fm, \(\sigma_z = 7\) fm, and thermal temperature 20 MeV) was \(24.6^\circ \pm 0.6^\circ\), in good agreement with the input value of \(25^\circ\).
- The diagonal homogeneity lengths from the same fit were: \(\sigma_x = 4.05 \pm 0.05\) fm, \(\sigma_y = 4.97 \pm 0.04\) fm, \(\sigma_z = 6.97 \pm 0.04\) fm, and \(\sigma_t = 4.78 \pm 0.45\) fm.
- Adding a boost-invariant longitudinal flow to the toy model increased the tilt angle \(\theta_s\) from \(25^\circ\) to \(33^\circ\) and reduced \(S'_{33}\) from 49 fm\(^2\) to 31 fm\(^2\).
- In the RQMD simulation of Au+Au at 2 \(A\) GeV and impact parameter \(b = 3-7\) fm, the magnitude of the collective pion motion in the reaction plane was \(\sim 10\) MeV/\(c\), compared to a typical \(p_T\) scale of \(\sim 200\) MeV/\(c\).
- A typical reaction-plane uncertainty of \(30^\circ\) reduces the first and second harmonics in the HBT radii by \(\sim 15\%\) and \(\sim 45\%\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of azimuthally sensitive HBT interferometry developed by Wiedemann (1998) and Wiedemann and Heinz (1999), which showed that all 10 components of the spatial correlation tensor are accessible in noncentral collisions. Prior studies had focused on the second-harmonic oscillations in \(R_s^2\), \(R_o^2\), and \(R_{os}^2\) (Wiedemann 1998; Voloshin and Cleymans 1996; Heinz 1999), but the first-harmonic signals in \(R_{ol}^2\) and \(R_{sl}^2\) had received less attention. The paper enables a direct experimental probe of the physical origin of directed pion flow: a positive spatial tilt \(\theta_s\) (opposite to the tilt in momentum space) would support a reflection-dominated mechanism for pion flow, while a negative tilt would indicate absorption-dominated flow, as discussed in transport model studies by Bass and collaborators.
