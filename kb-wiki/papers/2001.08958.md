# Modeling neutrino-nucleus interaction at intermediate energies

> We present the current status of the research activities of the Ghent group on neutrino-nucleus interactions. These consist in the modeling of some of the relevant neutrino-nucleus reaction channels at intermediate energies: low-energy nuclear excitations, quasielastic scattering, two-nucleon knockout processes and single-pion production. The low-energy nuclear excitations and the quasielastic peak are described using a Hartree-Fock-CRPA (continuum random phase approximation) model that takes into account nuclear long-range correlations as well as the distortion of the outgoing nucleon wave function. We include two-body current mechanisms through short-range correlations and meson-exchange currents. Their influence on one- and two-nucleon knockout responses is computed. Bound and outgoing nucleons are treated within the same mean-field framework. Finally, for modeling of the neutrino-induced single-pion production, we use a low-energy model that contains resonances and the background contributions required by chiral symmetry. This low-energy model is combined with a Regge approach into a Hybrid model, which allows us to make predictions beyond the resonance region.

## Key claim

The Ghent group's suite of models provides a unified description of multiple neutrino-nucleus reaction channels at intermediate energies (1-5 GeV), but the current precision of neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections in this energy region is known to not exceed 20%, and several model components (delta currents in MEC, final-state interactions in pion production) remain incomplete.

## Method

The nuclear ground state is described via Hartree-Fock with a Skyrme effective interaction, and long-range correlations are added through a continuum random phase approximation (CRPA) using the same Skyrme parameterization. Two-body current mechanisms are included via short-range correlations (SRC) and meson-exchange currents (MEC), with bound and outgoing nucleons treated consistently in the same mean-field framework. For single-pion production, a low-energy model containing resonances and chiral-symmetry background contributions is combined with a Regge approach into a Hybrid model, implemented within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) description for bound nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections in the 1-5 GeV energy region are known with a precision not exceeding 20%.
- For forward scattering angles in CCQE neutrino-40Ar scattering folded with the MicroBooNE flux, the strength from energy transfers below 50 MeV contributes approximately 30-50% of the total cross section.
- The CRPA model predictions for inclusive 12C(e,e') data show that long-range correlations notably improve agreement with data at low excitation energies, while inducing only small corrections at pure quasielastic kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the Ghent group's ongoing program of modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions, building on prior developments by Pandey (2014, 2015), Martini (2016), and Van Cuyck (2016, 2017). The CRPA approach is benchmarked against electron scattering data and compared with the relativistic global Fermi gas model of Amaro (2005) and Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014), which is widely used in Monte Carlo neutrino event generators. The single-pion production Hybrid model extends the low-energy model of Hernandez (2007) by incorporating Regge phenomenology to cure pathological high-energy behavior. The results enable improved predictions for current and future neutrino oscillation experiments (MiniBooNE, T2K, MicroBooNE, MINERvA, DUNE), with work in progress to complete the MEC calculation by including delta currents and to implement final-state interactions in the pion-production model.
