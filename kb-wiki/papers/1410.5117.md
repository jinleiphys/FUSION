# Universality of Hard-Loop Action

> The effective actions of gauge bosons, fermions and scalars, which are obtained within the hard-loop approximation, are shown to have unique forms for a whole class of gauge theories including QED, scalar QED, super QED, pure Yang-Mills, QCD, super Yang-Mills. The universality occurs irrespective of a field content of each theory and of variety of specific interactions. Consequently, the long-wavelength or semiclassical features of plasma systems governed by these theories such as collective excitations are almost identical. An origin of the universality, which holds within the limits of applicability of the hard-loop approach, is discussed.

## Key claim

The hard-loop effective actions for gauge bosons, fermions, and scalars are shown to have identical functional forms across a wide class of gauge theories (QED, scalar QED, super QED, pure Yang-Mills, QCD, and super Yang-Mills), but this universality holds only within the domain of validity of the hard-loop approximation, which requires the external momentum \(k\) to be much smaller than the internal momentum \(p\) of plasma constituents (the soft scale \(k \sim gT\)) and breaks down at the magnetic scale \(k \le g^2 T\).

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop self-energies (polarization tensors, fermion self-energies, and scalar self-energies) for each theory diagrammatically using the Keldysh-Schwinger real-time formalism, assuming homogeneous, locally colorless, and unpolarized plasmas with arbitrary momentum distributions. They then applied the hard-loop approximation (\(k \ll p\)) to each self-energy, showing that all contributions reduce to universal forms parameterized only by a coupling constant factor and an effective distribution function. The effective action was reconstructed by integrating the self-energies over the respective fields and then promoting ordinary derivatives to covariant derivatives to enforce gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The hard-loop polarization tensor for all theories takes the form \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}(k) = C_{\Pi} \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{f_{\Pi}({\bf p})}{E_p} \frac{k^2 p^\mu p^\nu - (k^\mu p^\nu + p^\mu k^\nu - g^{\mu\nu}(k\cdot p))(k\cdot p)}{(k\cdot p + i0^+)^2}\), with \(C_{\Pi}\) and \(f_{\Pi}\) given in Table II for each system.
- The hard-loop fermion self-energy for all theories is \(\Sigma(k) = C_{\Sigma} \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{f_{\Sigma}({\bf p})}{E_p} \frac{p\!\!/}{k\cdot p + i0^+}\), with \(C_{\Sigma}\) and \(f_{\Sigma}\) given in Table IV.
- The hard-loop scalar self-energy for all theories is \(P(k) = -C_P \int \frac{d^3p}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{f_P({\bf p})}{E_p}\), which is real, negative, and independent of the wave vector \(k\), with \(C_P\) and \(f_P\) given in Table VI.
- The hard-loop approximation is valid for external momenta \(k \sim gT\) (the soft scale) and breaks down for \(k \le g^2 T\) (the magnetic scale), where the fermion self-energy becomes of order \(\mathcal{O}(g^0)\) and is not perturbatively small.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior systematic comparisons of supersymmetric and non-supersymmetric plasmas (Czajka 2010, Czajka 2011, Czajka 2012) by collecting all results into a unified framework. It generalizes the long-known result that QED and QCD gauge boson self-energies have the same structure in the long-wavelength limit (Weldon 1982) to include fermion and scalar self-energies across six distinct gauge theories. The universality implies that collective excitations and instabilities at the soft scale are qualitatively identical for all these plasmas, but the authors note that recent efforts to extend hard-loop methods to the ultrasoft scale (Maas 2011, Gao 2014, Hidaka 2011, Blaizot 2014, Su 2014) explicitly show the limitations of this universality.
