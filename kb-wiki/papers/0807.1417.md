# Vector-axialvector mixing from a chiral effective field theory at finite temperature

> We study the vector-axialvector mixing in a hot medium and its evolution toward the chiral phase transition using different symmetry restoration scenarios based on the generalized hidden local symmetry framework. We show that the presence of the $a_1$ meson reduces the vector spectral function around $ρ$ meson mass and enhances it around $a_1$ meson mass. The coupling strength of $a_1$ to $ρ$ and $π$ vanishes at the critical temperature due to the degenerate $ρ$-$a_1$ masses. This feature holds rigorously in the chiral limit and still stays intact to good approximation for the physical pion mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the vector-axialvector (V-A) mixing in hot matter causes the coupling strength of the \(a_1\) meson to \(\rho\) and \(\pi\) to vanish at the critical temperature \(T_c\) due to degenerate \(\rho\)-\(a_1\) masses, a feature that holds rigorously in the chiral limit and remains a good approximation for the physical pion mass. The authors qualify this by noting that the analysis assumes several possible symmetry restoration scenarios (dropping or non-dropping \(\rho\) mass) and lacks explicit scalar \(\bar{q}q\) modes that may modify the correlators near \(T_c\).

## Method

The study uses the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) framework to construct an effective Lagrangian that includes pions, vector mesons (\(\rho\)), and axialvector mesons (\(a_1\)) consistent with chiral symmetry. The vector spectral function is computed at finite temperature using one-loop two-point functions in the Matsubara formalism, with temperature dependence introduced through parameterized scaling of the \(a_1\) mass (Option A) or the gauge coupling and masses (Option B) to model chiral symmetry restoration. The analysis examines three restoration patterns: non-dropping \(\rho\) mass with dropping \(a_1\) mass, and two types of dropping \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) masses (type I: \(\rho\)-\(a_1\) chiral partners; type II: \(\rho\)-\(\pi\) chiral partners).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature \(T_c = 200\) MeV is used for numerical calculations, with flash temperature \(T_f = 0.7\,T_c = 140\) MeV.
- Vacuum masses: \(M_\rho = 0.775\) GeV, \(M_{a_1} = 1.26\) GeV, \(m_\pi = 0.140\) GeV, \(F_\pi = 0.0924\) GeV.
- At \(T = T_c\) for Option A with physical pion mass, the mass ratio \(M_\rho^2 / M_{a_1}^2 \to (a g^2 F^2 + h_V m_\pi^2) / (a g^2 F^2 + h_A m_\pi^2) \simeq 1\), and the \(a_1\)-\(\rho\)-\(\pi\) coupling \(g_{a_1\rho\pi} \sim \sqrt{h_A - h_V}\, m_\pi \sim 0.06\, m_\pi\).
- For Option B (type I and II) with finite pion mass, the mass ratio near \(T_c\) becomes \(M_\rho^2 / M_{a_1}^2 \to h_V / h_A \simeq 1\), with \(h_V = 0.869\) and \(h_A = 0.927\).

## Context

This work extends earlier model-independent low-energy theorems for V-A mixing at temperatures \(T \ll 2f_\pi\) (e.g., the mixing theorem) to higher temperatures near the chiral phase transition, using the GHLS framework developed by Harada, Sasaki, and Weise. It builds on prior studies of chiral symmetry restoration patterns, including Brown-Rho scaling (dropping masses) and the Weinberg sum rules (Weinberg, 1967), and enables future calculations of dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where many-body effects from baryons are reduced. The authors note that explicit scalar \(\bar{q}q\) modes, which become important near \(T_c\) in the Ginzburg-Landau picture, are not included and will be addressed in forthcoming work (Harada, Sasaki, and Weise, in progress).
