# Classical Skyrmions -- Static Solutions and Dynamics

> Skyrmions with a realistic value of the pion mass parameter are expected to be quite compact structures, but beyond baryon number B=8 only a few examples are known. The largest of these is the cubically symmetric B=32 Skyrmion which is a truncated piece of the Skyrme crystal. Here it is proposed that many more such Skyrmions could be found, without any restriction on the baryon number, as pieces of the Skyrme crystal. Particular attention is given to the possibility of reducing B by 1 by chopping a corner off a cubic crystal chunk. Nuclei are modelled by Skyrmions with quantised spin and isospin. Here it is argued that these quantised states can be approximated by classically spinning Skyrmions. The orientations of the spinning Skyrmions corresponding to polarised protons, neutrons and deuterons are identified. Nuclear collisions and the nuclear spin-orbit force are discussed in terms of classically spinning Skyrmions. Going beyond the rigid collective motions of Skyrmions, there are spatially modulated collective motions, which are oscillatory in time. They are argued to describe not just vibrational excitations of nuclei, but also giant resonance states. A speculative proposal for identifying quarks inside Skyrmions is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Many more Skyrmions with realistic pion mass could be found as pieces of the Skyrme crystal without any restriction on baryon number, including a novel cubically symmetric Skyrmion with baryon number 14 that may model carbon-14; however, no substantial computations have been carried out to confirm these proposals.

## Method

The paper collects qualitative arguments and geometric reasoning about how truncated pieces of the Skyrme crystal, bounded by surfaces where the sigma field vanishes, can be smoothly connected to the vacuum at infinity to produce approximate Skyrmion solutions. The author also considers classical spinning Skyrmions as approximations to quantised nuclear states, identifying specific orientations for polarised protons, neutrons, and deuterons, and discusses spatially modulated collective motions that could describe giant resonance states.

## Key numbers

- The largest known Skyrmion with realistic pion mass has baryon number B=32 and is cubically symmetric, constructed as a truncated piece of the Skyrme crystal.
- The Skyrme crystal has energy per baryon E/B = 1.036 × 12π² at the optimal lattice spacing L.
- Cubic crystal chunks of the second type have baryon numbers B = 4n³, giving the sequence 4, 32, 108, 256, etc.
- Cubic crystal chunks of the first type have baryon numbers B = ((2n-1)³ + 1)/2 = 4n³ - 6n² + 3n, giving the sequence 1, 14, 63, 172.
- The dimensionless pion mass parameter m is calibrated to values of approximately 0.5, 1.0, or 1.125 depending on the calibration method.

## Context

This work extends the known set of Skyrmion solutions beyond B=8 for massive pions, where only B=8, 12, 16, and 32 had been previously constructed by Battye and Sutcliffe (2006) and Baskerville (1997). The proposal builds on the Skyrme crystal structure discovered by Castillejo et al. (1989) and Kugler and Shtrikman (1988), and on the layered crystal analysis of Silva Lobo and Ward (2009). The paper suggests that the B=14 Skyrmion could enable modelling of carbon-14 as a doubly magic nucleus, complementing the existing alpha-particle model of nuclei by Brink et al. (1970).
