# Weighted-residual methods for the solution of two-particle Lippmann-Schwinger equation without partial-wave decomposition

> Recently there has been a growing interest in computational methods for quantum scattering equations that avoid the traditional decomposition of wave functions and scattering amplitudes into partial waves.The aim of the present work is to show that the weighted-residual approach in combination with local basis functions give rise to convenient computational schemes for the solution of the multi-variable integral equations without the partial wave expansion.The weighted-residual approach provides a unifying framework for various variational and degenerate-kernel methods for integral equations of scattering theory. Using a direct-product basis of localized quadratic interpolation polynomials,Galerkin, collocation and Schwinger variational realizations of the weighted-residual approach have been implemented for a model potential. It is demonstrated that, for a given expansion basis, Schwinger variational method exhibits better convergence with basis size than Galerkin and collocation methods. A novel hybrid-collocation method is implemented with promising results as well.

# Weighted-residual methods for the solution of two-particle Lippmann-Schwinger equation without partial-wave decomposition

## Key claim

The weighted-residual approach (Petrov-Galerkin method) provides a unifying framework for solving the two-particle Lippmann-Schwinger equation without partial-wave decomposition, and for the specific model potential studied, the Schwinger variational method exhibits better convergence with basis size than Galerkin and collocation methods, though the paper does not claim this superiority holds universally for all potentials or energy regimes.

## Method

The three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-particle T-matrix is reduced to a two-dimensional integral equation by integrating out the azimuthal angle, exploiting the central-potential dependence on only q, q', and the cosine of the angle between them. A direct-product basis of localized quadratic interpolation polynomials (finite-element type) is constructed over a grid in momentum magnitude q and angular variable x = cos θ, and the weighted-residual approach is implemented in Galerkin, collocation, Schwinger variational, and a novel hybrid-collocation realizations. The resulting T-matrix is obtained as a separable expansion of rank K = N × M, where N and M are the numbers of basis functions in q and x, respectively.

## Key numbers

- On-shell momentum values used in calculations: q₀ = 0.5 and q₀ = 2.0 (atomic units)
- The computational q-domain is divided into two intervals: [0, 2q₀] and [2q₀, ∞), with the second interval mapped via u = (q − 2q₀ − f)/(q − 2q₀ + f) where f is a scale factor
- Momentum cutoff q_max ran into several thousand atomic units depending on the values of u_max and scale factor f
- The number of finite elements covering [0, q_max] is I = I₁ + I₂, with the choice I₂ = 3I₁ (hence I = 4I₁) found adequate after experimentation
- The total number of grid points in q is N = 2I + 1; the total number of grid points in x is M = 2J + 1, where J is the number of subintervals partitioning [-1, 1]
- The matrix dimension of the three-dimensional Nystrom method would be N_q × N_θ × N_φ if a direct-product quadrature scheme is used

## Context

This work extends the growing interest in computational methods for quantum scattering that avoid partial-wave decomposition, motivated by the slow convergence of partial-wave expansions at intermediate and high energies (where ion-atom collisions may require several thousand partial waves as noted in Refs. [6, 10]) and by the need for efficient generation of off-shell two-particle T-matrices as input to three- and four-particle Faddeev calculations (Refs. [2, 13, 14]). The weighted-residual framework unifies various variational and degenerate-kernel methods previously applied to scattering equations, and the separable expansion form of the T-matrix offers a computationally economical alternative to the Nystrom method for generating off-shell matrix elements at many different energies and momenta. The approach enables direct solution of few-particle dynamics in terms of vector momenta without angular momentum algebra, and the hybrid-collocation method introduced here combines advantages of collocation and Schwinger variational methods for future applications.
