# The Non-perturbative Interaction of the Pseudovector Coupling for the Pion-nucleon Scattering

## Key claim
The non-perturbative term arising from the pseudovector coupling interaction, when combined with the exact form of the nucleon self-energy, causes the elastic pion-nucleon scattering cross section to approach a constant value at high laboratory momentum (p_L → ∞), but the exact self-energy form introduces unphysical poles that restrict its applicability to the high-energy region; the approximate lowest-order self-energy form is required to describe intermediate-energy data.

## Method
The author modifies the standard pseudovector coupling vertex for the pion-nucleon-nucleon three-point vertex by adding a non-perturbative term Γ₁(p,q) = G(p)⁻¹ γ₅ + γ₅ G(q)⁻¹, where G(p)⁻¹ contains the nucleon self-energy Σ(p). The scattering amplitude for π⁺-proton elastic scattering is computed by expanding the fermion line into four terms (α₁ through α₄), with the exact nucleon propagator approximated by the free propagator in the α₁ term. The self-energy Σ(p) is expressed in terms of coefficients c₁(p²) and c₂(p²), and the resulting amplitude is parameterized by functions C(x) and D(x) which encode the self-energy effects. The dispersion relation for the forward scattering amplitude is then applied to compute total cross sections, using a linear approximation for Re T(ω) with coefficients b₀ and b₁ that depend on the treatment of the self-energy.

## Key numbers
- The experimental high-energy limit of the π⁻-proton elastic cross section is σ_∞ ∼ 3.3 mb at laboratory beam momentum 100 GeV/c.
- Using the relation f_π² = (m_π²/M) √(2π σ_∞), the extracted pseudovector coupling constant is f_π ∼ 0.4, compared to the standard value f_π ∼ 1.
- The resonance energy of the π-proton scattering used to determine the parameter α is α ∼ 0.3 GeV.
- The ratio of total cross sections around the resonance energy ω ∼ 0.3 GeV is σ₊/σ₋ ∼ 3 from experiment.
- The coefficients (b₀, b₁) for four models are: no Γ₁: (0, 1/2); no Σ: (1, 0); lowest Σ: (3, 2); Σ_∞: (−1/2, 0).
- The second-order approximation yields coefficients (b₀, b₁) = (1, −1).
- The appropriate pseudovector coupling constant for the second-order model is around f_π ∼ 0.7, with values 0.6, 0.7, and 0.8 tested.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Kinpara) of the nucleon self-energy's role in the electromagnetic form factor of the proton to the pion-nucleon scattering process. The pseudovector coupling is favored over pseudoscalar coupling based on empirical success in finite nuclei and nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering via the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The non-perturbative term relates the two coupling types through chiral symmetry breaking. The dispersion relation approach follows the standard method of Bjorken and Drell (1965) for pion-nucleon scattering, and the low-energy coupling constant is compared to the value extracted from dispersion relations and phase shifts by Haber-Schaim. The results enable future inclusion of self-energy corrections in the intermediate-state nucleon propagator and extension to phenomena with weaker off-shell properties.
