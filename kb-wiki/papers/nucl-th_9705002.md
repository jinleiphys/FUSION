# Halo Excitation of $^6$He in Inelastic and Charge-Exchange Reactions

> Four-body distorted wave theory appropriate for nucleon-nucleus reactions leading to 3-body continuum excitations of two-neutron Borromean halo nuclei is developed. The peculiarities of the halo bound state and 3-body continuum are fully taken into account by using the method of hyperspherical harmonics. The procedure is applied for A=6 test-bench nuclei; thus we report detailed studies of inclusive cross sections for inelastic $^6$He(p,p')$^6$He$^*$ and charge-exchange $^6$Li(n,p)$^6$He$^*$ reactions at nucleon energy 50 MeV. The theoretical low-energy spectra exhibit two resonance-like structures. The first (narrow) is the excitation of the well-known $2^+$ three-body resonance. The second (broad) bump is a composition of overlapping soft modes of multipolarities $1^-, 2^+, 1^+, 0^+$ whose relative weights depend on transferred momentum and reaction type. Inelastic scattering is the most selective tool for studying the soft dipole excitation mode.

# Halo Excitation of $^6$He in Inelastic and Charge-Exchange Reactions

## Key claim

The low-energy continuum of $^6$He contains not only the well-known $2^+$ three-body resonance but also a broad bump composed of overlapping soft modes of multipolarities $1^-$, $2^+$, $1^+$, and $0^+$, whose relative weights depend on transferred momentum and reaction type; inelastic scattering is the most selective tool for studying the soft dipole excitation mode, though the dipole mode is a "3-body virtual excitation" rather than a true three-body resonance.

## Method

The authors developed a four-body distorted wave theory for nucleon-nucleus reactions leading to three-body continuum excitations of two-neutron Borromean halo nuclei, using the method of hyperspherical harmonics (HH) to describe both the bound state and the three-body continuum of $^6$He. They applied this formalism to calculate inclusive cross sections for inelastic $^6$He(p,p')$^6$He$^*$ and charge-exchange $^6$Li(n,p)$^6$He$^*$ reactions at a nucleon energy of 50 MeV, using a free nucleon-nucleon $t$-matrix parametrization (Love and Franey) for the effective interaction and phenomenological optical potentials for the distorted waves.

## Key numbers

- The $2^+_1$ resonance in $^6$He is predicted at 1.72 MeV excitation energy with a width of 0.04 MeV (HH method), compared to the experimental value of 1.8 MeV with width 0.113 MeV.
- A second $2^+_2$ resonance is predicted at 4.0 MeV with width 1.2 MeV.
- A $1^+$ resonance is predicted at 4.4 MeV with width 1.8 MeV.
- A $0^+_2$ resonance is predicted at 6.0 MeV with width 6.0 MeV.
- The calculations were performed at a nucleon energy of 50 MeV for both reactions.
- The soft dipole mode ($1^-$) was not found as a resonance by any method (HH, Complex Scaling Method, or experiment).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of $^6$He continuum structure (Ershov et al., 1997; Danilin et al., 1997) by incorporating reaction dynamics into the analysis of continuum excitations, moving beyond earlier response function calculations. The paper builds on the hyperspherical harmonics method previously applied to bound states and weak/electromagnetic properties of $A=6$ nuclei (Danilin et al., 1993; Danilin et al., 1995), and addresses the need for theoretical methods that account for the peculiarities of weakly bound halo systems in reactions. The results enable experimental interpretation of inclusive spectra from facilities such as GANIL, Kurchatov Institute, NSCL, RIKEN, JINR, and GSI, and provide predictions that can distinguish between overlapping resonances and soft modes in the 3-6 MeV excitation energy region.
