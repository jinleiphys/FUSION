# Comparison of Chiral Perturbation Theory and QCD Sum Rule Results for Pseudoscalar Isoscalar-Isovector Mixing

> The forms of the neutral, non-strange pseudoscalar propagator matrix and mixed axial current correlator, $\lngle0|T(A_μ^3 A_ν^8)|0\rangle$, are discussed at next-to-leading (one-loop) order in chiral perturbation theory, and the results compared to those obtained using QCD sum rules. This comparison provides a check of the truncations employed in the sum rule treatment of the current correlator. Values for the slope of the correlator with $q^2$ in the two approaches are found to differ by more than an order of magnitude and the source of this discrepancy is shown to be the incorrect chiral behavior of the sum rule result.

# Comparison of Chiral Perturbation Theory and QCD Sum Rule Results for Pseudoscalar Isoscalar-Isovector Mixing

## Key claim
The QCD sum rule treatment of the mixed axial current correlator fails at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion because the truncation scheme (dropping terms of O(m_q^2) and truncating the operator product expansion at dimension 6) removes the leading chiral behavior of the slope with respect to q^2, producing a numerical result that differs from the one-loop chiral perturbation theory expression by more than an order of magnitude. Additionally, the mixing parameter theta(q^2) of the pseudoscalar propagator matrix cannot be extracted from the mixed axial correlator beyond leading order in the chiral expansion, contrary to the claim made in the sum rule analysis.

## Method
The author computed the neutral non-strange pseudoscalar propagator matrix and mixed axial current correlator at next-to-leading (one-loop) order in chiral perturbation theory using the low-energy effective chiral Lagrangian of Gasser and Leutwyler (1985). The one-loop chiral perturbation theory results for the isospin-breaking parameters epsilon_1 and epsilon_2, the decay constants f_pi and f_eta, and the form factor Pi_1^{38} were compared to the corresponding quantities extracted from the QCD sum rule analysis of Chan, Henley, and Meissner (1994). The chiral expansions of the physical meson masses were used to expose the quark mass dependence of the sum rule result for the slope g_eta - g_pi.

## Key numbers
- The one-loop chiral perturbation theory prediction for the slope parameter g_eta - g_pi is 53 MeV^2, using input values epsilon_1 = 1.67 x 10^{-2}, epsilon_2 = 1.35 x 10^{-2}, f_pi = 93 MeV, and f_eta/f_pi = 1.3.
- The QCD sum rule result for g_eta - g_pi, rescaled for violations of Dashen's theorem, is 3.9 MeV^2, more than an order of magnitude smaller than the chiral perturbation theory value.
- The individual pole residues from chiral perturbation theory are g_eta = 197 MeV^2 and g_pi = 144 MeV^2, compared to the sum rule values g_eta = 143 MeV^2 and g_pi = 139 MeV^2 (both rescaled by a factor of 1.22 for Dashen's theorem violations).
- The leading-order mixing angle is given by theta_0 = sqrt(3)(m_d - m_u) / [4(m_s - m_hat)], where m_hat = (m_u + m_d)/2.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate about the q^2-dependence of meson mixing parameters in charge symmetry breaking, following earlier studies by Goldman, Henderson, and Thomas (1992), Maltman (1993), and Hatsuda, Henley, Meissner, and Krein (1994). The paper directly contradicts the claim of Chan, Henley, and Meissner (1994) that the q^2-dependence of pi-eta mixing could be extracted from QCD sum rules for the axial current correlator. The author shows that the sum rule truncation scheme removes the leading chiral contribution to the slope, analogous to problems encountered in sum rule analyses of the nucleon mass where the chiral behavior was only restored by more careful treatment of continuum contributions, as discussed by Griegel and Cohen (1994) and Lee, Choe, Cohen, and Griegel (1994). The result enables future work to use chiral perturbation theory constraints to improve sum rule applications to few-body systems.
