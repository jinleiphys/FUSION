# Thermal Production of Charmonia at the LHC Energies

> This work studies the thermal production of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ with Boltzmann transport model in the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) produced in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions. $J/ψ$ nuclear modification factors are studied in details with the mechanisms of primordial production and the recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks in the thermal medium. $ψ(2S)$ binding energy is much smaller in the hot medium compared with the ground state, so $ψ(2S)$ with middle and low $p_T$ can be mainly thermally regenerated in the later stage of QGP expansions which enables $ψ(2S)$ inherit larger collective flows from the bulk medium. We quantitatively study both nuclear modification factors of $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ in different centralities and transverse momentum bins in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the sequential thermal regeneration of $J/\psi$ and $\psi(2S)$ in different stages of QGP expansion, driven by their different binding energies, can explain the observed nuclear modification factors and their ratio at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions, but the author notes that the experimental data for the $\psi(2S)/J/\psi$ ratio carry large error bars which may prevent any solid conclusions.

## Method

The author employs a two-component Boltzmann transport model for charmonium evolution in the QGP, coupled with (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the bulk medium expansion. The decay rate of charmonia in the medium is calculated from gluon dissociation cross sections, with the $\psi(2S)$ decay rate extracted from the $J/\psi$ rate via a geometric scaling by the ratio of mean squared radii ($\langle r\rangle^2_{\psi(2S)}/\langle r\rangle^2_{J/\psi}$). The regeneration source term includes the recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks, whose spatial density is evolved via a conservation equation coupled to the hydrodynamic flow.

## Key numbers

- Initial maximum QGP temperature: $T_0({\bf x}_T=0, \tau_0)= 510$ MeV in central rapidity $|y|<2.4$ and $450$ MeV in forward rapidity $2.5<|y|<4$, with $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c and QGP lifetime $\sim 10$ fm/c in most central collisions.
- Charm pair cross section in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV: $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/dy=0.86$ mb in central rapidity $|y|<0.9$ and $0.56$ mb in forward rapidity $2.5<|y|<4$.
- $J/\psi$ cross section in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV: $d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy=5.0\ \mu b$ in central rapidity $|y|<1$ and $3.25\ \mu b$ in forward rapidity $2.5<|y|<4$.
- Mean transverse momentum square for $J/\psi$ in pp collisions: $\langle p_T^2\rangle_{pp}|_{y=0}=12.5\ \mathrm{(GeV/c)^2}$ with parameter $n=3.2$.
- Mean radii of charmonia in vacuum: $\langle r\rangle_{J/\psi}=0.5$ fm, $\langle r\rangle_{\chi_c}=0.72$ fm, $\langle r\rangle_{\psi(2S)}=0.9$ fm.
- Critical temperature for phase transition: $T_c=170$ MeV.
- Non-prompt fraction from B-hadron decays: $f_B=0.04+0.023p_T/(GeV/c)$, with quench factor $R_Q=0.4$ for bottom quarks.
- Shadowing suppression factor from EPS09 NLO model: roughly $\sim 0.8$ depending on impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Chen (2013) from 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions to 5.02 TeV, using an improved decay rate that replaces the earlier approximation of a survival temperature $T_d$ above which no charmonia survive. The model builds on the Boltzmann transport approach developed by the Tsinghua Group (Zhu 2004, Chen 2012, Shi 2017) and is compared with the quasifree dissociation approach of the TAMU Group (Grandchamp 2001, Du 2015). The results enable future studies of how open charm quark diffusion and QGP expansion histories are encoded in the sequential regeneration patterns of different charmonium states.
