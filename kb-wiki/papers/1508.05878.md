# A viscous blast-wave model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Using a viscosity-based survival scale for geometrical perturbations formed in the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, we model the radial flow velocity during freeze-out. Subsequently, we employ the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription, with first-order viscous corrections to the distribution function, to obtain the transverse momentum distribution of particle yields and flow harmonics. For initial eccentricities, we use the results of Monte Carlo Glauber model. We fix the blast-wave model parameters by fitting the transverse momentum spectra of identified particles at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and demonstrate that this leads to a fairly good agreement with transverse momentum distribution of elliptic and triangular flow for various centralities. Within this viscous blast-wave model, we estimate the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s\simeq 0.24$ at the LHC.

# A viscous blast-wave model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors claim that a blast-wave model incorporating viscous damping of geometrical anisotropies via a viscosity-based survival scale can reproduce transverse momentum spectra and flow harmonics at the LHC, yielding an estimate of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of approximately 0.24. However, this estimate depends on the specific choice of the conversion efficiency parameter alpha_0 = 0.4 and requires different freeze-out temperatures for protons (135 MeV) versus pions and kaons (120 MeV) to achieve agreement with data.

## Method

The authors generalize the blast-wave model by introducing a viscosity-based survival scale for geometrical perturbations formed in the early stages of collisions, which determines the conversion efficiency of initial eccentricities into radial flow velocity anisotropies. They use the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription with first-order viscous corrections to the distribution function (Grad's 14-moment approximation) and employ Monte Carlo Glauber model results for initial eccentricities. The model parameters are fixed by fitting transverse momentum spectra of identified particles (pions, kaons, protons) at LHC energies, and the freeze-out time for non-central collisions is determined from the centrality dependence of initial energy density using Bjorken scaling.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s approximately 0.24 for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Freeze-out temperature for pions and kaons: T_f = 120 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature for protons: T_f = 135 MeV
- Freeze-out time for 0-5% most central collisions: tau_f0 = 8 fm
- Conversion efficiency parameter: alpha_0 = 0.4
- Initial radius of each nucleus: R_0 = 1.25 A^(1/3) fm

## Context

This work extends the traditional blast-wave model of Schnedermann et al. (1993) and the anisotropic generalization by Huovinen et al. (2001) by incorporating viscous effects through a damping mechanism based on the dispersion relation for sound in a viscous medium, as introduced by Staig and Shuryak (2010). The model differs from the approach of Shuryak and Zahed (2013), who applied acoustic damping directly to the ratio v_n/epsilon_n, by instead applying the damping to the hydrodynamic flow velocity anisotropies, which allows for studying the p_T dependence of anisotropic flow. The model enables estimation of eta/s without performing full viscous hydrodynamic evolution and can be extended to event-by-event analysis.
