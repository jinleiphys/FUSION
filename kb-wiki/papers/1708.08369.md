# Observables for possible QGP signatures in central pp collisions

> Proton-proton (pp) data show collective effects, such as long-range azimuthal correlations and strangeness enhancement, which are similar to phenomenology observed in heavy ion collisions. Using simulations with and without explicit existing models of collective effects, we explore new ways to probe pp collisions at high multiplicity, in order to suggest measurements that could help identify the similarities and differences between large- and small-scale collective effects. In particular, we focus on the properties of jets produced in ultra-central pp collisions in association with a Z boson. We consider observables such as jet energy loss and jet shapes, which could point to the possible existence of an underlying quark-gluon plasma, or other new dynamical effects related to the presence of large hadronic densities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that jet observables in ultra-central proton-proton (pp) collisions with a Z boson, such as jet energy loss and jet shapes, could distinguish between a genuine quark-gluon plasma (QGP) interpretation of collective effects and alternative models based on modified hadronization, but the simulations show that existing non-QGP models (rope hadronization, color reconnection, thermal models) do not predict jet quenching, meaning that observing quenching would be a strong signal for QGP while its absence would not rule it out.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of Z+jet events in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV using Pythia 8.226 with several modifications: rope hadronization, two color reconnection models, and a thermal hadronization model. They compared these against default Pythia hadronization, studying event properties as a function of three multiplicity definitions (total track multiplicity, Z-side track multiplicity, and forward multiplicity) and examining strangeness enhancement, jet energy balance via the ratio pT,J/pT,Z, and jet substructure observables including soft drop mass and momentum sharing zg.

## Key numbers

- The median total track multiplicity (TTM) for Z+jet events is 44, median Z-side track multiplicity (ZTM) is 22, and median forward multiplicity (FM) is 36
- With 5 fb^-1 at sqrt(s)=7 TeV, there are many hundreds of Z->mu+mu- events with a single jet in the >99% percentile of the multiplicity distribution
- The standard deviation of the xZJ = pT,J/pT,Z ratio distribution is about 20%
- With approximately 500 jets in the 1% percentile category, the statistical precision in determining xZJ will be less than 1%
- A 1 GeV energy loss for a 20 GeV jet corresponds to a 5% effect on the mean xZJ
- The Rope hadronization model predicts a multiplicity-dependent difference in the zg distribution of about 10% compared to default Pythia

## Context

This work extends the study of collective effects in small systems, following observations of strangeness enhancement by ALICE (2017) and long-range azimuthal correlations (the "ridge") by ATLAS (2015) and CMS (2010, 2015) in high-multiplicity pp collisions. The paper directly compares several existing models: rope hadronization (Bierlich 2016), color reconnection models (Ortiz 2013, Christiansen 2015), and thermal hadronization (Fischer 2016), which all reproduce strangeness enhancement without invoking a QGP. The proposed Z+jet measurements could enable experimental discrimination between these interpretations, complementing ongoing studies in pPb collisions by ATLAS, CMS, ALICE, and LHCb.
