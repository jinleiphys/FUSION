# Non-Abelian Energy Loss at Finite Opacity

> A systematic expansion in opacity, $L/λ$, is used to clarify the non-linear behavior of induced gluon radiation in quark-gluon plasmas. The inclusive differential gluon distribution is calculated up to second order in opacity and compared to the zeroth order (factorization) limit. The opacity expansion makes it possible to take finite kinematic constraints into account that suppress jet quenching in nuclear collisions below RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV) energies.

# Non-Abelian Energy Loss at Finite Opacity

## Key claim

The opacity expansion in mean number of scatterings $\bar{n}=L/\lambda$ converges rapidly even for realistic nuclear opacities ($L/\lambda_g\sim 5$) because the effective expansion parameter is actually the product of opacity and the gluon formation probability $L\mu^2/xE$, not the opacity alone. This means the first-order term already captures the leading quadratic dependence of energy loss on nuclear thickness, contrary to the expectation that the thick-plasma ($\bar{n}\gg 1$) limit would be required.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma as $N$ well-separated color-screened Yukawa potentials and compute the inclusive differential gluon distribution induced by a finite medium up to second order in opacity ($L/\lambda$). They sum direct (real) and double Born (virtual) amplitudes for gluon emission from a hard quark jet, averaging over longitudinal target profiles and momentum transfers via the color Yukawa potential. The calculation extends the tagged-target approach of Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev (2000) by including virtual corrections necessary for inclusive jet quenching.

## Key numbers

- For a 50 GeV quark jet in a medium with $\lambda_g=1$ fm, screening scale $\mu=0.5$ GeV, and $\alpha_s=0.3$, the first-order induced energy loss is $\Delta E^{(1)} = \frac{C_R\alpha_s}{N(E)} \frac{L^2\mu^2}{\lambda_g} \log\frac{E}{\mu}$, where $N(E)=7.3,\ 10.1,\ 24.4$ for $E=500,\ 50,\ 5$ GeV respectively (accounting for finite kinematic constraints).
- At SPS energies (5 GeV jet), the total energy loss for opacity $L/\lambda_g\sim 2$ is only $\sim 100$ MeV, too small to be observable.
- At RHIC energies ($\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV), the expected rapidity density of gluons is $dN/dy\sim 1000$ for $A=200$, with elastic cross section $\sigma_{el}\sim 2$ mb and plasma formation time $\sim 0.5$ fm/c, giving $\bar{n}\sim 4$.
- The second-order contribution to integrated energy loss remains small up to opacities $L/\lambda_g\sim 5$, except at low SPS energies.

## Context

This work extends the tagged-target opacity expansion of Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev (2000) by including virtual corrections to compute inclusive rates, providing a complementary analytic approach to the thick-plasma results of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff (1997) and Zakharov (1996). The finite kinematic constraints naturally explain the absence of jet quenching observed at SPS energies by Wang (1998) and Gyulassy, Levai (1998), which had been a puzzle. The results enable improved Monte Carlo event simulations of jet quenching and predict observable nonlinear suppression of high-$p_\perp$ hadrons at RHIC energies relative to scaled $pp$ data.
