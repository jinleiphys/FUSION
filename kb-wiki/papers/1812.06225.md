# Centrality dependence of deuteron production in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV via hydrodynamics and hadronic afterburner

> The deuteron binding energy is only 2.2 MeV. At the same time, its yield in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = $2.76 TeV corresponds to a thermal yield at the temperature around 155 MeV, which is too hot to keep deuterons bound. This puzzle is not completely resolved yet. In general, the mechanism of light nuclei production in ultra-high energy heavy ion collisions remains under debate. In a previous work [1] we suggest a microscopic explanation of the deuteron production in central ultra-relativistic Pb+Pb collisions, the main mechanism being $πpn \leftrightarrow πd$ reactions in the hadronic phase of the collision. We use a state-of-the-art hybrid approach, combining relativistic hydrodynamics for the hot and dense stage and hadronic transport for a later, more dilute stage. Deuteron rescattering in the hadronic stage is implemented explicitly, using its experimentally measured vacuum cross-sections. In these proceedings we extend our previous work to non-central collisions, keeping exactly the same methodology and parameters. We find that our approach leads to a good description of the measured deuteron transverse momentum spectra at centralities up to 40%, and underestimates the amount of deuterons at low transverse momentum at higher centralities. Nevertheless, the coalescence parameter $B_2$, measured by ALICE collaboration, is reproduced well in our approach even for peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously proposed mechanism for deuteron production in central Pb+Pb collisions (via pi p n <-> pi d reactions in the hadronic phase) to non-central collisions, finding that the approach describes measured deuteron transverse momentum spectra for centralities up to 40% but underestimates deuteron yields at low transverse momentum for higher centralities, while the coalescence parameter B2 is reproduced well even for peripheral collisions.

## Method

The study uses a hybrid approach combining relativistic hydrodynamics for the hot, dense stage of the collision with hadronic transport for the later, more dilute stage. Deuteron rescattering in the hadronic stage is implemented explicitly using experimentally measured vacuum cross-sections for pi p n <-> pi d reactions. The same methodology and parameters from the authors' previous work on central collisions are applied to non-central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy is 2.2 MeV, while the thermal yield temperature in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV is around 155 MeV
- The approach describes measured deuteron transverse momentum spectra at centralities up to 40%
- The coalescence parameter B2, measured by the ALICE collaboration, is reproduced well even for peripheral collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic explanation of deuteron production in central ultra-relativistic Pb+Pb collisions (Oliinychenko et al., 2018) to non-central collisions, addressing the long-standing puzzle that deuteron yields correspond to thermal temperatures around 155 MeV despite the deuteron binding energy being only 2.2 MeV. The approach enables further testing of the hadronic reaction mechanism for light nuclei production across different collision geometries and could be applied to other light nuclei such as tritium and helium-3.
