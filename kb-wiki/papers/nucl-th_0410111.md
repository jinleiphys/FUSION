# Forward Production in d+Au Collisions by Parton Recombination

> Hadron production at forward rapidities in d+Au collisions is studied in the framework of parton recombination in the final state. Multiple scattering and gluon saturation in the initial state are not explicitly considered. The recombination of soft and shower partons is found to be important. The soft parton densities are determined by extrapolation from the parametrization fixed at η=0 with no unconstrained adjustments. The suppression of R_{CP} observed at high ηis understood as the simple consequence of the reduction of the soft parton density on the deuteron side compared to that on the gold side. The effect of momentum degradation responsible for baryon stopping is also considered. The asymmetry of backward-to-forward spectra can be reproduced within the same framework without any change in the basic physics.

## Key claim

The suppression of $R_{CP}$ observed at high pseudorapidity in d+Au collisions can be understood as a simple consequence of the reduction of the soft parton density on the deuteron side compared to that on the gold side, without invoking new physics such as gluon saturation or multiple scattering in the initial state, provided that a mild dependence of the inverse slope parameter $T$ on centrality and pseudorapidity and the effect of momentum degradation are included.

## Method

The authors extended their parton recombination formalism, previously successful at midrapidity for Au+Au and d+Au collisions, to forward rapidities ($\eta > 0$) by modifying the soft parton density $C(\beta,\eta)$ using PHOBOS charged-hadron multiplicity data via $C(\beta,\eta) = C(\beta,0) [dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta(\beta) / dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta|_{\eta=0}(\beta)]^{1/2}$, and by parametrizing hard parton distributions $f_i(k_T,y)$ from leading-order minijet calculations with CTEQ5 parton distributions and EKS98 nuclear shadowing, including a kinematic dampening factor $(1 - k_T/k_0)^{m_i}$ for $y > 1$. The pion spectra were computed from three recombination components (soft-soft, soft-shower, and shower-shower) using Eq. (2), with the inverse slope $T$ either fixed at 0.208 GeV or allowed a weak centrality- and rapidity-dependent variation $T(\beta,\eta) = T_0(1 - \varepsilon\beta\eta)$ with $\varepsilon = 0.0205$, and momentum degradation applied multiplicatively to the shower distribution via $\zeta(\beta,\eta) = \exp[-\kappa(N_c - 1)\eta]$ with $\kappa \approx 0.01$.

## Key numbers

* Soft parton density parameter $C(\beta,0)$ at $\eta=0$: 12.0, 9.0, and 6.55 (GeV/$c)^{-1}$ for centrality cuts $\beta =$ 0-20%, 30-50%, and 60-80%, respectively (interpolated from values in Ref. hy2).
* Inverse slope $T = 0.208$ GeV for the soft parton exponential distribution, fixed at $\eta=0$ from Ref. hy2.
* $C(\beta,\eta)$ at $\eta=3.2$: 7.05, 6.0, and 5.1 (GeV)$^{-1}$ for $\beta =$ 0-20%, 30-50%, and 60-80%, respectively (Table I).
* Kinematic limit $k_0(\eta) = \sqrt{s}/(2\cosh y)$: 21.9 GeV/$c$ at $y=2.2$ and 8.13 GeV/$c$ at $y=3.2$.
* Momentum degradation parameter $\kappa \approx 0.01$, determined from matching the valon model at $\eta=3.2$ with $\langle p_T \rangle = 2$ GeV/$c$ and $\langle x_F \rangle = 0.25$.
* $T(\beta,\eta)$ variation parameter $\varepsilon = 0.0205$, giving less than 4.6% decrease even at the highest $\beta=0.7$ and $\eta=3.2$.

## Context

This work extends the parton recombination approach of Hwa, Yang, and Fries (2004) from midrapidity to forward rapidities in d+Au collisions, where previous explanations invoked color glass condensate (CGC) physics (Iancu, McLerran, 2001; Kharzeev, Levin, 2001) or multiple scattering in the initial state for the Cronin effect. The authors show that the same hadronization mechanism that explains the Cronin enhancement at $\eta=0$ and the $p/\pi$ ratio (Hwa, Yang, 2003; Fries, Muller, 2004) also reproduces the suppression of $R_{CP}$ at high $\eta$ and the backward-forward asymmetry observed by BRAHMS and STAR, without requiring a transition between different initial-state physics regimes. This enables a unified description of particle production across all pseudorapidities and centralities in d+Au collisions.
