# Transition from the Seniority to the Anharmonic Vibrator Regime in Nuclei

> A recent analysis of experimental energy systematics suggests that all collective nuclei fall into one of three classes -- seniority, anharmonic vibrational, or rotational -- with sharp phase transitions between them. We investigate the transition from the seniority to the anharmonic vibrator regime within a shell model framework involving a single large j-orbit. The calculations qualitatively reproduce the observed transitional behavior, both for U(5) like and O(6) like nuclei. They also confirm the preeminent role played by the neutron-proton interaction in producing the phase transition.

# Transition from the Seniority to the Anharmonic Vibrator Regime in Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper presents a schematic shell-model study that qualitatively reproduces the observed rapid transition from seniority-type nuclei to anharmonic vibrational nuclei, showing that the same mechanism, the neutron-proton quadrupole interaction, drives the transition for both U(5)-like and O(6)-like nuclei, but the calculations should not be directly interpreted as representing any real nuclear region due to the simplifying assumption of a single active j-orbit.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations assuming neutrons and protons each occupy a single large j=15/2 orbital, using a Hamiltonian with surface delta interactions (SDI) and quadrupole interactions for proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and neutron-proton terms. They calculated the energies of the lowest 2+ and 4+ states for systems with 0 to 6 active protons combined with either 0 to 6 active neutrons or 0 to 6 neutron holes, and analyzed the seniority structure of the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For anharmonic vibrator nuclei in the Z=38-82 region, the relation E(4_1+) = 2E(2_1+) + epsilon_4 holds with epsilon_4 approximately 156 keV; in the actinides, epsilon_4 is approximately 67 keV
- For the semi-magic system with Z=4, N=0, the calculated ratio R = E(4_1+)/E(2_1+) is 1.25, with E(2_1+) = 1.41 MeV and E(4_1+) = 1.77 MeV
- Adding two particles to the semi-magic system (Z=4, N=2) raises the ratio to R = 2.14, with E(2_1+) = 0.40 MeV and E(4_1+) = 0.85 MeV
- For the particle-hole case with Z=4, N=-2, the ratio rises to R = 1.72, with E(2_1+) = 0.65 MeV and E(4_1+) = 1.11 MeV
- The largest calculated ratio is R = 2.51 for Z=4, N=4, with E(2_1+) = 0.23 MeV and E(4_1+) = 0.59 MeV
- The Hamiltonian parameters include: proton SDI strength G_pp = 0.24 MeV, neutron SDI strength G_nn = 0.22 MeV (particle-like) or 0.20 MeV (hole-like), and neutron-proton quadrupole strength chi_np = 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the empirical observations of Casten, Zamfir, and Brenner (1993, 1994), who identified three distinct classes of collective nuclei (seniority, anharmonic vibrational, rotational) with sharp transitions between them. The calculations confirm the preeminent role of the neutron-proton interaction, building on earlier work by Federman and Pittel (1979), and provide a microscopic mechanism for the emergence of S-D pair dominance that underlies the Interacting Boson Model of Iachello and Arima (1987). The results enable further study of how seniority mixing drives the onset of collectivity in nuclei.
