# Construction of Basis Vectors For Symmetric Irreducible Representations of O(5) supset O(3)

> A recursive method for construction of symmetric irreducible representations of O(2l+1) in the O(2l + 1) supset O(3) basis for identical boson systems is proposed. The formalism is realized based on the group chain U(2l + 1) supset U(2l- 1) x U(2), of which the symmetric irreducible representations are simply reducible. The basis vectors of the O(2l+1) supset O(2l-1) x U(1) can easily be constructed from those of U(2l + 1) supset U(2l-1) x U(2) supset O(2l-1) x U(1) with no boson pairs, from which one can construct symmetric irreducible representations of O(2l+1) in the O(2l-1) x U(1) basis when all symmetric irreducible representations of O(2l-1) are known. As a starting point, basis vectors of symmetric irreducible representations of O(5) are constructed in the O1(3) x U(1) basis. Matrix representations of O(5) supset O1(3) x U(1), together with the elementary Wigner coefficients, are presented. After the angular momentum projection, a three-term relation in determining the expansion coefficients of the O(5) supset O(3) basis vectors in terms of those of the O1(3) x U(1) is derived. The eigenvectors of the projection matrix with zero eigenvalues constructed according to the three-term relation completely determine the basis vectors of O(5) supset O(3). Formulae for evaluating the elementary Wigner coefficients of O(5) supset O(3) are derived explicitly. Analytical expressions of some elementary Wigner coefficients of O(5) supset O(3) for the coupling (tau, 0) x (1,0) with resultant angular momentum quantum number L = 2 tau+ 2 - k for k = 0, 2, 3,...,6 with a multiplicity 2 case for k = 6 are presented.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a recursive method for constructing basis vectors of symmetric irreducible representations of O(2l+1) in the O(2l+1) ⊃ O(3) basis for identical boson systems, using the group chain U(2l+1) ⊃ U(2l-1) ⊗ U(2) as a starting point. The method is explicitly demonstrated for the O(5) ⊃ O(3) case (l=2), where the basis vectors are expanded in terms of those of the O(5) ⊃ O₁(3) ⊗ U(1) chain, and the expansion coefficients are determined by solving a three-term recurrence relation derived from angular momentum projection.

## Method

The authors first construct orthonormal basis vectors of O(5) ⊃ O₁(3) ⊗ U(1) from those of U(5) ⊃ U(3) ⊗ U(2) ⊃ O₁(3) ⊗ U(1) using the boson pairing operator and normalization conditions. They then derive matrix elements of single-boson creation operators in this basis and use the Racah factorization lemma to obtain elementary Wigner coefficients (isoscalar factors) for O(5) ⊃ O₁(3) ⊗ U(1). Finally, they expand the desired O(5) ⊃ O(3) basis vectors in terms of the O₁(3) ⊗ U(1) basis and impose the condition L₊|ψ⟩ = 0, which yields a three-term recurrence relation for the expansion coefficients; the eigenvectors of the resulting projection matrix with zero eigenvalue provide the coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For the coupling (τ,0) ⊗ (1,0) with resultant angular momentum L = 2τ + 2 - k, analytical expressions of elementary Wigner coefficients are presented for k = 0, 2, 3, ..., 6, with a multiplicity 2 case for k = 6.
- The dimension of the O(5) irrep (τ,0) is given by dim(τ,0) = (τ+1)(τ+2)(2τ+3)/6.
- The O(5)-reduced matrix element satisfies ⟨(τ+1,0)||T^(10)||(τ,0)⟩ = √(τ+1).
- For k=0, the unique highest weight state has c₀,₀(τ,0)=1 with L=2τ.
- For k=2, the normalized expansion coefficients are c₀,₀(τ,2)=√(3/(4τ-1)) and c₁,₀(τ,2)=-√(4(τ-1)/(4τ-1)) for τ>1.
- For k=6, the multiplicity is 2 for τ>5, with explicit polynomial expressions for the two sets of expansion coefficients given in terms of τ.

## Context

This work extends the vector-coherent-state (VCS) representations of O(5) ⊃ O(3) developed by Rowe, Hecht, and others (Rowe 1994, Rowe and Hecht, Turner) and the O(5) spherical harmonics of Rowe and Turner. It provides an alternative recursive construction method that starts from the simpler O(5) ⊃ O₁(3) ⊗ U(1) basis and uses angular momentum projection, complementing existing approaches such as those of Sun and Caprio. The method enables systematic construction of O(5) ⊃ O(3) basis vectors for identical d-boson systems, which are useful in the nuclear collective model (Bohr, Rowe) and the interacting boson model (Iachello).
