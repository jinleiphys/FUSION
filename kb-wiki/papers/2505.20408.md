# Quantum computation of hadron scattering in a lattice gauge theory

> We present a digital quantum computation of two-hadron scattering in a $Z_2$ lattice gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions. We prepare well-separated single-particle wave packets with desired momentum-space wavefunctions, and simulate their collision through digitized time evolution. Multiple hadronic wave packets can be produced using the efficient, systematically improvable algorithm of this work, achieving high fidelity with the target initial state. Specifically, employing a trapped-ion quantum computer (IonQ Forte), we prepare up to three meson wave packets using 11 and 27 system qubits, and simulate collision dynamics of two meson wave packets for the smaller system. Results for local observables are consistent with numerical simulations at early times, but decoherence effects limit evolution into long times. We demonstrate the critical role of high-fidelity initial states for precision measurements of state-sensitive observables, such as $S$-matrix elements. Our work establishes the potential of quantum computers in simulating hadron-scattering processes in strongly interacting gauge theories.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate a digital quantum computation of two-hadron scattering in a Z2 lattice gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions, but decoherence effects on the trapped-ion hardware (IonQ Forte) limit the time evolution to early times, and the results for local observables are consistent with numerical simulations only at those early times.

## Method

The authors use a minimal gauge-link formulation of a Z2 lattice gauge theory with dynamical staggered fermions on a periodic spatial lattice, mapping the Hilbert space to N+1 qubits via Jordan-Wigner transformation. They prepare well-separated single-particle wave packets using a systematically improvable ansatz for meson creation operators that exploits translation symmetry and the confinement scale, then simulate collisions through digitized time evolution using Trotter product expansion. The state preparation and time evolution are implemented on the IonQ Forte trapped-ion quantum computer and compared against noiseless emulator results.

## Key numbers

- Up to three meson wave packets were prepared using 11 and 27 system qubits on the IonQ Forte quantum computer
- Two-meson collision dynamics were simulated for the smaller system (11 qubits)
- The ansatz for the meson creation operator uses a polynomial number of parameters in the system size, with cost scaling polynomially with the correlation length rather than with system size

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid classical-quantum variational algorithm for wave-packet preparation in confining gauge theories (Davoudi 2024) by introducing a more efficient position-space parameterization. It builds on the wave-packet preparation method proposed by Rigobello (2021) for tensor-network calculations in U(1) lattice gauge theories, and on the digital quantum simulation of scattering originally proposed by Jordan, Lee, and Preskill (2012) for scalar field theory. The results establish the potential of near-term quantum computers for simulating hadron scattering in strongly interacting gauge theories, while highlighting the critical role of high-fidelity initial states for precision measurements of S-matrix elements.
