# A systematic comparison of jet quenching in different fluid-dynamical models

> Comparing four different (ideal and viscous) hydrodynamic models for the evolution of the medium created in 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions, combined with two different models for the path length dependence of parton energy loss, we study the effects of jet quenching on the emission-angle dependence of the nuclear suppression factor R_AA(phi) and the away-side per trigger yield I_AA(phi). Each hydrodynamic model was tuned to provide a reasonable description of the single-particle transverse momentum spectra for all collision centralities, and the energy loss models were adjusted to yield the same pion nuclear suppression factor in central Au-Au collisions. We find that the experimentally measured in-plane vs. out-of-plane spread in R_AA(phi) is better reproduced by models that shift the weight of the parton energy loss to later times along its path. Among the models studied here, this is best achieved by energy loss models that suppress energy loss at early times, combined with hydrodynamic models that delay the dilution of the medium density due to hydrodynamic expansion by viscous heating. We were unable to identify a clear tomographic benefit of a measurement of I_AA(phi) over that of R_AA(phi).

## Key claim
The experimentally measured in-plane vs. out-of-plane spread in the nuclear suppression factor R_AA(phi) is better reproduced by models that shift the weight of parton energy loss to later times along its path, which is best achieved by energy loss models that suppress energy loss at early times combined with hydrodynamic models that delay the dilution of medium density through viscous heating.

## Method
The authors compared four different ideal and viscous hydrodynamic models for the medium created in 200 AGeV Au-Au collisions, combined with two different parton energy loss models (the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann radiative model and a hybrid AdS/CFT strong-coupling model). Each hydrodynamic model was tuned to describe single-particle transverse momentum spectra for all collision centralities, and the energy loss models were adjusted to yield the same pion nuclear suppression factor in central collisions. The directional dependence of R_AA(phi) and the away-side per trigger yield I_AA(phi) were then computed and compared across model combinations.

## Key numbers
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c for the (3+1)-d ideal hydrodynamic model, 0.17 fm/c for the (2+1)-d ideal model, and 0.4 fm/c for the viscous model
- Freeze-out temperature T_dec = 130 MeV for the (3+1)-d ideal and viscous models, 160 MeV for the (2+1)-d ideal model
- Chemical decoupling temperature T_chem = 165 MeV used in the viscous model with s95p-PCE equation of state
- Specific shear viscosity fixed at eta/s = 0.2, independent of temperature
- Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV for the bag model equation of state with first order phase transition used in the (3+1)-d ideal model
- All three (2+1)-d models complete freeze-out at approximately 7.2-7.5 fm/c for the 20-30% centrality class (b = 7.49 fm)

## Context
This work extends systematic comparisons of jet quenching models by studying how different hydrodynamic medium evolutions affect high-p_T observables, building on earlier work by SysJet1 and SysJet2 that compared parton-medium interaction models using the same fluid-dynamical background. The study finds that R_AA(phi) is more discriminating than I_AA(phi) for constraining the combination of medium evolution and energy loss models, and that no clear tomographic benefit of measuring I_AA(phi) over R_AA(phi) was identified. The results enable future work to constrain both the medium evolution and parton-medium interaction models simultaneously.
