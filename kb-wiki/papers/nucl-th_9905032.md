# Relativistic Faddeev approach to the NJL model at finite density

> We study the nucleon solution of the relativistic Faddeev equation as a function of density in the framework of a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We truncate the interacting two-body channels to the scalar diquark channel, the coupling constant of which is treated as a parameter. A 3-momentum cut-off is used to regularize the model. The Faddeev equation is solved numerically using the methods developed by Tjon and others. At zero density the nucleon is bound only for unrealistically large values of the scalar coupling and this binding energy decreases quickly with increasing density.

## Key claim
At zero density, the nucleon is bound only for unrealistically large values of the scalar diquark coupling (g_s/g_pi >= 0.8), and this binding energy decreases quickly with increasing density, disappearing well before nuclear matter density.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic Faddeev equation for the nucleon as a function of density in a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, truncating the interacting two-body channels to the scalar diquark channel. A 3-momentum cutoff is used to regularize the model, and the Faddeev equation is solved numerically using methods developed by Huang and Tjon (1994). Density effects are incorporated by restricting the quark 3-momentum to values larger than the Fermi momentum k_F.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV and constituent quark mass M = 400 MeV are used to fit the model parameters g_pi = 7.01 GeV^{-2} and cutoff Lambda = 0.593 GeV.
- Chiral restoration occurs at k_F/Lambda = 0.58, corresponding to about 2.1 times nuclear matter density.
- The nucleon is bound at zero density only for scalar coupling ratio g_s/g_pi >= 0.8.
- For g_s/g_pi = 0.83, the nucleon binding disappears well before nuclear matter density (k_F/Lambda ~ 0.45).
- The diquark binding energy increases with density to reach more than half a GeV for g_s/g_pi = 0.83.

## Context
This work extends studies of baryons at zero density in the NJL model by groups such as Ishii et al. (1995) and Huang and Tjon (1994). It addresses the question of whether the quark Fermi sea is unstable with respect to 3-quark clustering, in contrast to the colour superconductivity studies by Alford et al. (1998) and Rapp et al. (1998) which focused on diquark pairing. The results suggest that in the region characteristic of colour superconductivity, the Fermi sea is unstable only with respect to diquark formation, not 3-quark clusters, but the authors note that including the axial-vector qq interaction (which gives an important contribution to nucleon binding at zero density according to Oettel et al. (1998)) could significantly modify these results.
