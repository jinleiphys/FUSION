# Intermittency analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Local density fluctuations near the QCD critical point can be probed by an intermittency analysis of power-law behavior on scaled factorial moments in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We study the second-order scaled factorial moment in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 7.7-200 GeV from the UrQMD model. Since the background subtraction and efficiency correction are two important aspects in this measurement, we propose a cumulative variable method to remove background contribution and a cell-by-cell method for efficiency correction in the intermittency analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the second-order intermittency index extracted from directly calculated scaled factorial moments in UrQMD model events is small but nonzero, even though the model does not include critical density fluctuations; this indicates the presence of non-critical background effects that must be removed.

## Method

The authors study the second-order scaled factorial moment \(F_2(M)\) of proton density in transverse momentum space (\(p_x-p_y\)) for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 7.7-200\) GeV using the UrQMD model. They propose a cumulative variable method to remove background contributions and a cell-by-cell efficiency correction method based on a binomial response model to recover the true scaled factorial moments from detector-affected measurements.

## Key numbers

- The intermittency index \(\phi_2\) from directly calculated SFMs is small but nonzero at all studied energies (7.7, 19.6, 39, and 200 GeV).
- After applying the cumulative variable method, the corrected index \(\phi_2^c\) is much smaller than \(\phi_2\), demonstrating effective background removal.
- The cell-by-cell efficiency correction method recovers the original \(F_2(M)\) values, with corrected moments almost overlapping the true ones at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the intermittency analysis approach previously applied by the NA49 experiment (2006) in Si+Si collisions at 158A GeV and by preliminary STAR experiment results showing a minimum in the scaling exponent around \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 20-30\) GeV. The paper provides a non-critical baseline from the UrQMD model and offers practical methods for background subtraction and efficiency correction that can guide future experimental intermittency analyses at RHIC and other facilities.
