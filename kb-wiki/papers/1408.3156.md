# Centrality dependence of high energy jets in p+Pb collisions at the LHC

> The recently measured centrality dependence of high energy jets in proton-lead collisions at the LHC is investigated. We hypothesize that events with jets of very high energy (a few hundred GeV) are characterized by a suppressed number of soft particles, thus shifting these events into more peripheral bins. This naturally results in the suppression (enhancement) of the nuclear modification factor, $R_{pA}$, in central (peripheral) collisions. Our calculations suggest that a moderate suppression of the order of $20\%$, for $10^{3}$ GeV jets, can quantitatively reproduce the experimental data. We further extract the suppression factor as a function of jet energy and test our conjecture using available $R_{pA}$ data for various centralities.

## Key claim

The observed centrality dependence of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) for high-energy jets in p+Pb collisions can be explained by an anti-correlation between soft particle production and very hard jet production, where events with jets of a few hundred GeV have a suppressed number of soft particles, shifting them into more peripheral centrality bins; a moderate suppression of about 20% for 1000 GeV jets is sufficient to quantitatively reproduce the ATLAS data.

## Method

The authors constructed a Glauber model of p+Pb collisions, using a Woods-Saxon distribution for the lead nucleus and a nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section of 70 mb. They modeled soft particle production per participant using a negative binomial distribution (with parameters \(\langle n_{pp}\rangle = 5\) and \(k_{pp} = 1.1\) from p+p data) and introduced a suppression factor \(s\) that reduces the mean number of soft particles per participant in events containing a high-energy jet. Centrality classes were defined from the minimum-bias multiplicity distribution with \(s=0\), and the nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}\) was computed as a function of \(s\) for each centrality bin.

## Key numbers

*   A suppression factor of \(s \approx 0.2\) (20%) for jets of energy \(E_{\rm jet} \approx 1000\) GeV quantitatively reproduces the ATLAS data on \(R_{cp}\) (the ratio of central 0-10% to peripheral 60-90% \(R_{pA}\)).
*   The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section used in the Glauber model is 70 mb.
*   The negative binomial distribution parameters for p+p collisions are \(\langle n_{pp}\rangle = 5\) and \(k_{pp} = 1.1\).
*   The model results are practically unchanged for values of \(\epsilon\) (the probability to produce a high-energy jet in a single nucleon-nucleon interaction) ranging from \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work addresses the surprising ATLAS (2014) observation that \(R_{pA}\) for high-energy jets in p+Pb collisions is less than unity in central collisions and greater than unity in peripheral collisions, contrary to the expectation from perturbative QCD that \(R_{pA}\) should be near unity. The proposed mechanism of soft-particle suppression extends prior work on multiplicity bias from hard triggers by PHENIX (Adare 2013) and on color fluctuations by Alvioli (2013, 2014), and it provides a baseline for interpreting jet quenching measurements in heavy-ion collisions. The model enables a quantitative extraction of the suppression factor as a function of jet energy, which can be tested against future data from the LHC.
