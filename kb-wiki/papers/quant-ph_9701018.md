# Robertson Intelligent States

> Diagonalization of uncertainty matrix and minimization of Robertson inequality for n observables are considered. It is proved that for even n this relation is minimized in states which are eigenstates of n/2 independent complex linear combinations of the observables. In case of canonical observables this eigenvalue condition is also necessary. Such minimizing states are called Robertson intelligent states (RIS). The group related coherent states (CS) with maximal symmetry (for semisimple Lie groups) are particular case of RIS for the quadratures of Weyl generators. Explicit constructions of RIS are considered for operators of su(1,1), su(2), h_N and sp(N,R) algebras. Unlike the group related CS, RIS can exhibit strong squeezing of group generators. Multimode squared amplitude squeezed states are naturally introduced as sp(N,R) RIS. It is shown that the uncertainty matrices for quadratures of q-deformed boson operators a_{q,j} (q > 0) and of any k power of a_j = a_{1,j} are positive definite and can be diagonalized by symplectic linear transformations. PACS numbers: 03.65.Fd, 42.50.Dv

## Key claim

The paper proves that for an even number of observables, the Robertson uncertainty inequality is minimized in states that are eigenstates of n/2 independent complex linear combinations of those observables, and that for canonical observables this eigenvalue condition is also necessary; these minimizing states are called Robertson intelligent states (RIS), and they generalize the concept of group-related coherent states.

## Method

The author diagonalizes the uncertainty matrix (dispersion matrix) for n observables using linear transformations of the operators, then derives the condition for minimization of the Robertson inequality det σ ≥ det C. For 2N observables, the author constructs N independent nonhermitean operators A'_j = X'_j + iX'_{N+j} as complex linear combinations of the original observables and proves that eigenstates of all A'_j satisfy the equality in the Robertson relation. Explicit constructions are carried out for the algebras su(1,1), su(2), h_N, and sp(N,R) by solving eigenvalue equations for general elements of the complexified algebras.

## Key numbers

- For canonical variables (p_j, q_j) with N modes, the trace inequality Tr[iσ(Q,ρ)J]^(2k) ≥ N/2^(2k-1) is established, recovering the Schrodinger relation for N=1 and k=1.
- For su(1,1) representations with Bargman index k = 1/4, 3/4, and discrete series k = 1/2, 1, 3/2, ..., the eigenvalue equation (uK_- + vK_+ + wK_3)|ψ⟩ = z|ψ⟩ is solved with parameters a = k + z/√(w²-4uv), b = 2k, and normalizability conditions requiring (1/(2|u|))|w - √(w²-4uv)| < 1 or (1/(2|u|))|w + √(w²-4uv)| < 1.
- For quadrature components of a^k (k-th power of boson annihilation operator), defined as X_j^(k) = (a_j^k + a_j^(k†))/√(2k) and X_(N+j)^(k) = -i(a_j^k - a_j^(k†))/√(2k), the commutator -i[X_j^(k), Y_j^(k)] = (1/k)[a^k, a^(k†)] is positive definite.

## Context

This work extends the concept of intelligent states from the two-observable Schrodinger uncertainty relation (previously studied by Trifonov (1994) and by Aragone (1974) for spin states) to the n-observable Robertson inequality. The paper connects three generalizations of coherent states: as eigenstates of destruction operators, as group orbits (following Klauder and Skagerstam), and as minimum uncertainty states. It shows that group-related coherent states with maximal symmetry for semisimple Lie groups are a subset of RIS for the quadratures of Weyl generators, while RIS can exhibit stronger squeezing than those group-related coherent states. The work enables the introduction of multimode squared amplitude squeezed states as sp(N,R) RIS and establishes that uncertainty matrices for q-deformed boson operators (q > 0) and for powers of boson operators are positive definite and diagonalizable by symplectic transformations.
