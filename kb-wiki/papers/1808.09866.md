# Probing the gluon TMDs with quarkonia

## Key claim

Quarkonium hadroproduction can be used to access both the unpolarised gluon TMD $f_1^{g}(x,\kT^2)$ and the distribution of linearly-polarised gluons $h_1^{\perp\,g}(x,\kT^2)$, but the applicability of TMD factorisation is restricted to final-state transverse momenta $P_{fT}$ smaller than half the invariant mass of the observed colourless system, which severely limits the kinematic range for single-quarkonium production.

## Method

The authors review the TMD factorisation formalism for gluon-fusion processes producing colourless final states, expressing the differential cross section as a convolution of two gluon TMD correlators with a partonic short-distance amplitude calculated in perturbative QCD. They analyse three classes of quarkonium-production processes: single pseudoscalar quarkonium ($\eta_c$, $\eta_b$), associated production of a vector quarkonium with a photon or $Z$ boson ($J/\psi+\gamma$, $\Upsilon+\gamma$), and vector-quarkonium-pair production ($J/\psi+J/\psi$, $\Upsilon+\Upsilon$). For each process, they compute the hard-scattering coefficients $F_i$ that determine the relative size of the azimuthal modulations $\cos 2\phi_{\CS}$ and $\cos 4\phi_{\CS}$ in the Collins-Soper frame.

## Key numbers

- For di-$J/\psi$ production, the first extraction of the unpolarised gluon TMD $f_1^{g}$ using LHCb data yields $\langle \kT^2 \rangle = 3.3 \pm 0.8$ GeV$^2$, assuming a Gaussian $\kT$ dependence, at a scale on the order of 8 GeV.
- In the large invariant-mass limit $M_{\Q\Q} \gg M_{\Q}$ for di-quarkonium production, the hard coefficient $F_4$ approaches $F_1$, making this process the most sensitive probe of $h_1^{\perp g}$ among all gluon-TMD-sensitive processes.
- For pseudoscalar-quarkonium production, the TMD factorisation domain requires $P_{\eta_c T}$ below 1 to 1.5 GeV, while existing LHCb data for $\eta_c$ production are at $P_{\eta_cT}$ larger than 6 GeV.
- For $J/\psi+\gamma$ production, $F_2$ vanishes, $F_3$ is power suppressed in $M_f$, and $F_4$ scales like $F_1$, offering opportunities to extract $h_1^{\perp g}$ via $\cos 4\phi_{\CS}$ modulations.
- For $M_f$ close to $2M_{\Q}$ in di-quarkonium production, $F_2 \to 3/787 \times F_1$ and $F_{3,4} \to 0$.

## Context

This work extends the TMD-factorisation approach to quarkonium production pioneered by Boer and Pisano (2012) for pseudoscalar quarkonia, and by the same authors for $J/\psi+\gamma$ and $\Upsilon+\gamma$ production (2014). It builds on the general TMD formalism for gluon-induced processes with unpolarised protons developed by Lansberg, Pisano, Scarpa, and Schlegel (2017). The most promising case, di-$J/\psi$ production, enables the first extraction of $f_1^{g}$ from existing LHCb data (2016) and predicts large $\cos 4\phi_{\CS}$ modulations that can be tested with upcoming LHC measurements. These studies complement future target-spin asymmetry measurements to access the gluon Sivers function and enable complete gluon tomography of the proton.
