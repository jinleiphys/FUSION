# Neutron Capture and Neutron Halos

> The connection between the neutron halo observed in light neutron rich nuclei and the neutron radiative capture process is outlined. We show how nuclear structure information such as spectroscopic factors and external components of the radial wave function of loosely bound states can be derived from the neutron capture cross section. The link between the direct radiative capture and the Coulomb dissociation process is elucidated.

# Neutron Capture and Neutron Halos

## Key claim
The neutron capture cross section can be used to derive spectroscopic factors and the radial wave function of loosely bound halo states, but only when the capture proceeds through direct radiative capture (DRC) without compound-nucleus formation, and when the final bound orbit has a strong single-particle s-wave component and small binding energy.

## Method
The authors apply the direct radiative capture (DRC) model for electric dipole (E1) transitions, decomposing the matrix element into a spectroscopic factor, an angular-momentum factor, and a radial overlap integral. They show that for s-wave bound states and p-wave continuum states, the radial overlap becomes a Fourier-Bessel transform of the bound-state wave function, which can be inverted to reconstruct the radial wave function. The same matrix elements are shown to relate neutron capture cross sections to Coulomb dissociation B(E1) strength distributions, enabling studies of unstable nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The ground state of 11Be is bound by only 505 keV and is dominated by the |10Be(0+) ⊗ (2s1/2)ν configuration.
- For a bound s-orbit at 500 keV in a Woods-Saxon potential (radius R = 2.83 fm, depth V0 = 57.8 MeV, diffuseness d = 0.62 fm), the overlap integral differs by less than 5% among three different continuum potentials for neutron energies up to 5 MeV.
- For 19C, the E1 strength distribution for the |18C(0+) ⊗ (2s1/2)ν configuration differs from the |18C(0+) ⊗ (1d5/2)ν and |10C(0+) ⊗ (1d3/2)ν configurations by more than a factor of 40.
- The radial wave function of the 11Be ground state could be reconstructed down to approximately 7 fm using the Fourier-Bessel inversion technique.

## Context
This work extends the DRC model previously applied to proton capture measurements into the neutron capture regime for halo nuclei, building on prior calculations by Otsuka et al. (1994) and Kratz et al. (1996). The authors connect their approach to Coulomb dissociation experiments on 11Be by Nakamura et al. (1994) and propose that the structure of 19C, suggested as a halo state by Baumann et al. (1995), can be deduced from B(E1) measurements. The technique enables nuclear structure studies of unstable neutron-rich nuclei using radioactive beam facilities, with applications to astrophysical reaction rates for nucleosynthesis in the inhomogeneous big-bang scenario as discussed by Kajino (1995).
