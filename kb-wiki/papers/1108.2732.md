# Nuclear effects in charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> After a short review of the recent developments in studies of neutrino-nucleus interactions, the predictions for double-differential and integrated charged current-induced quasielastic cross sections are presented within two different relativistic approaches: one is the so-called SuSA method, based on the superscaling behavior exhibited by electron scattering data; the other is a microscopic model based on relativistic mean field theory, and incorporating final-state interactions. The role played by the meson-exchange currents in the two-particle two-hole sector is explored and the results are compared with the recent MiniBooNE data.

## Key claim

The MiniBooNE CCQE data are substantially underestimated by the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, but this discrepancy can be attributed to incomplete nuclear modeling rather than requiring a larger axial mass; the paper shows that two relativistic models (SuSA and RMF) that incorporate more realistic nuclear dynamics still under-predict the data, and that including 2p-2h meson-exchange currents improves agreement but does not fully resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The author presents predictions for double-differential and integrated charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) cross sections for muon neutrinos on carbon-12 using two relativistic approaches: the Superscaling (SuSA) model, which uses a phenomenological scaling function extracted from inclusive electron scattering data, and the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model, which uses self-consistent Dirac-Hartree bound-state wave functions and includes final-state interactions via scalar and vector potentials. The contribution of two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) excitations from meson-exchange currents (MEC) is then added to the SuSA model, and all results are flux-averaged over the MiniBooNE neutrino energy spectrum and compared to the MiniBooNE data.

## Key numbers

- The standard axial mass value used in all calculations is \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV/c\(^2\), compared to the world average of \(M_A \simeq 1.026 \pm 0.021\) GeV/c\(^2\) extracted from (anti)neutrino data.
- A best fit of the RMF and SuSA results to the MiniBooNE data gives an effective axial mass \(M_A^{\rm eff} \simeq 1.5\) GeV/c\(^2\), with values in the range \(1.35 < M_A^{\rm eff} < 1.65\) GeV/c\(^2\) yielding results compatible with the data within experimental errors.
- The MiniBooNE neutrino flux spans energies from 0 to 3 GeV with an average value of about 0.8 GeV.
- At the most forward scattering angles, 30-40% of the cross section corresponds to very low energy transfers (below 50 MeV), where collective effects dominate.
- The RMF model provides longitudinal and transverse scaling functions that differ by typically 20%, with the transverse one being larger.
- The RFG model gives a symmetric superscaling function with a maximum value 20-30% higher than the electron scattering data.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of electron scattering data (Day 1990, Jourdan 1996, Donnelly 1998, Maieron 2001) to neutrino-nucleus interactions, and compares the SuSA phenomenological approach with the microscopic RMF model (Caballero 2006, 2007). The results are contrasted with the MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010) and with other recent calculations including spectral function models (Benhar 2005, Ankowski 2010), relativistic Green function approaches (Meucci 2003, 2011), and models including multinucleon knockout (Martini 2009, Nieves 2011). The inclusion of 2p-2h MEC contributions is based on the relativistic calculations of De Pace (2003) and Amaro (2010). The work enables future studies of the axial mass extraction by showing that nuclear effects can mimic a larger axial mass, and it identifies the need to include correlation diagrams alongside MEC to achieve gauge-invariant two-body currents.
