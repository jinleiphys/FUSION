# Test of CDF dijet anomaly within the standard model

> Dijet anomaly reported by the CDF (Collider Detector at Fermilab) collaboration in 1.96 TeV p-pbar collisions is investigated within the standard model by considering effects of parton distribution functions on various processes: W+dijet, Z+dijet, WW, ZW, and top production. Since the anomalous peak exists in the dijet-mass region of 140 GeV with the p-pbar center-of-mass energy sqrt{s}=1.96 TeV, a relevant momentum fraction x of partons is roughly 0.1. In this x region, recent HERMES semi-inclusive charged-lepton scattering experiment indicated that the strange-quark distribution could be very different from a conventional one, which has been used for many years, based on opposite-sign dimuon measurements in neutrino-induced deep inelastic scattering. We investigated effects of such variations in the strange-quark distribution s(x) on the anomaly. We found that distributions of W+dijets and other process are affected by the strange-quark modifications in wide dijet-mass regions including the 140 GeV one. Since the CDF anomaly was observed in the shoulder region of the dijet-mass distribution, a slight modification of the distribution shape could explain at least partially the CDF excess. Therefore, it is important to consider such effects within the standard model for judging whether the CDF anomaly indicates new physics beyond the standard model. We also show modification effects of the strange-quark distribution in the LHC (Large Hadron Collider) kinematics, where cross sections are sensitive to a smaller-x region of s(x).

## Key claim

The CDF dijet anomaly at 140 GeV could be at least partially explained within the standard model by modifying the strange-quark parton distribution function (PDF) in the momentum fraction region around x ~ 0.1, rather than requiring new physics beyond the standard model.

## Method

The authors used the GR@PPA event generator to calculate cross sections for W+dijet, Z+dijet, WW, ZW, and top production processes in 1.96 TeV p-pbar collisions, applying the same kinematical cuts as the CDF analysis. They modified the strange-quark distribution s(x) from the CTEQ6L1 PDF set using two weight functions: one based on HERMES semi-inclusive charged-lepton scattering data at Q²=2.5 GeV², and another trial function that enhances the large-x strange distribution toward the light-antiquark level. The modified distributions were evolved via DGLAP equations to the hard scale Q²=2M_W² appropriate for the CDF dijet analysis.

## Key numbers

- The CDF anomaly appears in the dijet-mass region of 140 GeV at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s)=1.96 TeV, corresponding to a parton momentum fraction x ~ 0.1
- The HERMES data indicate the strange-quark distribution at Q²=2.5 GeV² is two times larger than the conventional distribution at x=0.03 and much smaller at x=0.2
- The weight function w₁ parameters at Q₀²=2.5 GeV² are x₀=0.0796 and Δx=0.0253
- The strange-quark-moment ratio κ = ∫dx x(s+bar s) / ∫dx x(bar u+bar d) from neutrino dimuon measurements is in the range 0.3 ≤ κ ≤ 0.5
- At the CDF-jet scale Q²=2M_W², the weight function w₁ has parameters x₀=0.0768 and Δx=0.0408
- At the neutrino DIS scale Q²=20 GeV², the strange to light-antiquark ratio is 0.23 ≤ (s+bar s)/(bar u+bar d) ≤ 0.68

## Context

This work extends the approach used to resolve the 1996 CDF jet excess, which was explained by modifying the gluon distribution at large x (CTEQ, 1996), to the 2011 CDF dijet anomaly by instead considering modifications to the strange-quark distribution. The authors challenge the conventional strange-quark PDF derived from opposite-sign dimuon measurements in neutrino deep inelastic scattering (CCFR, NuTeV) by incorporating recent HERMES (2008) semi-inclusive kaon production data. This study enables a standard-model explanation of the anomaly before invoking beyond-standard-model theories such as Z' bosons, supersymmetry, or Higgs boson interpretations, and also shows that LHC cross sections are sensitive to the smaller-x region of s(x).
