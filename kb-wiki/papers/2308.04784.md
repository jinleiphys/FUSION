# Beyond-mean-field description of octupolarity in dysprosium isotopes with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

> The emergence and stability of (static) octupole deformation effects in Dy isotopes from dripline to dripline ($72 \le N \le 142$) is analyzed in this paper using mean-field and beyond-mean-field techniques often used for this purpose. We find static octupole deformations at the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) level with the Gogny D1M force for $N \approx 134$ isotopes, while nuclei with $N \approx 88$ exhibit reflection-symmetric ground states. It is shown that, given the softness found in the mean-field and parity-projected potential energy surfaces along the octupole direction, neither of these two levels of approximation is suficcient to extract conclusions about the (permanent and/or vibrational) nature of octupole dynamic in Dy isotopes. From the analysis of the collective wave functions as well as the excitation energies of the first negative-parity states and $B(E3)$ strengths, obtained within the framework of a two-dimensional symmetry-conserving generator coordinate method (2D-GCM), it is concluded that the increased octupole collectivity in Dy isotopes with $N \approx 88$ and $N \approx 134$ is a vibrational-like effect that is not directly related to permanent mean-field octupole deformation in the considered nuclei. A pronounced suppression of the $B(E1)$ strengths is predicted for isotopes with $N \approx 82$ and $N \approx 126$. The comparison of results obtained with other parametrizations, show the robustness of the predicted trends with respect to the underlying Gogny energy density functional.

# Beyond-mean-field description of octupolarity in dysprosium isotopes with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

## Key claim

While Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1M force predict static octupole deformations for very neutron-rich Dy isotopes around N=134, the authors conclude that the increased octupole collectivity in Dy isotopes with N=88 and N=134 is a vibrational-like effect, not directly related to permanent mean-field octupole deformation, once beyond-mean-field correlations are included via a two-dimensional symmetry-conserving generator coordinate method (2D-GCM).

## Method

The authors performed constrained HFB calculations with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional for even-even Dy isotopes from N=72 to N=142, using a deformed axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis with 15 major shells and constraining on both quadrupole (Q20) and octupole (Q30) operators. Beyond-mean-field correlations were incorporated first through parity projection of the intrinsic HFB states, and then through a 2D-GCM approach that simultaneously restores reflection symmetry and mixes quadrupole-octupole configurations. The collective wave functions, excitation energies of the first negative-parity states, and B(E1) and B(E3) transition strengths were computed from the 2D-GCM solutions.

## Key numbers

- Static octupole deformations at the HFB level are predicted only for 198-202Dy (N=134 region), with ground state octupole deformations in the range 2.25 b^(3/2) to 2.75 b^(3/2) (beta_3 = 0.10 to 0.12)
- The HFB energy gained by breaking reflection symmetry for 198-202Dy is small: 188 keV (198Dy), 266 keV (200Dy), and 70 keV (202Dy)
- Ground state quadrupole deformations across the isotopic chain range from -6 b to 12 b (beta_2 from -0.19 to 0.34)
- For A=150, a quadrupole deformation Q20=5 b is equivalent to beta_2=0.217; for A=200, an octupole deformation Q30=2.5 b^(3/2) is equivalent to beta_3=0.113
- The 2D-GCM ground state average octupole moments (Q30_bar) for all considered nuclei are within 0.25 b^(3/2) to 0.93 b^(3/2), less than half of the static HFB ground state octupole deformations for N=134 isotopes
- A pronounced suppression of B(E1) strengths is predicted for isotopes with N=82 and N=126

## Context

This work extends previous Gogny-EDF studies of quadrupole-octupole coupling in Sm and Gd isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman, 2010), actinide nuclei with N=134 (Rodriguez-Guzman, 2012), and Rn, Ra, Th isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman, 2015) using the same 2D-GCM framework. The results contradict recent relativistic mean-field calculations (microscopic-22) that concluded Dy isotopes with N=88 and N=134 exhibit permanent octupole deformation based on plain mean-field results. The findings agree with macroscopic-microscopic calculations (MM-6) that found no static octupole deformation in the N=88 region. The work enables future studies of octupole correlations in other isotopic chains and highlights the necessity of beyond-mean-field methods for drawing conclusions about the nature of octupole deformation.
