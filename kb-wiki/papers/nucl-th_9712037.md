# Off-shell effects in the electromagnetic production of strangeness

> Previous approaches to the photo- and electro-production of strangeness off the proton, based upon effective hadronic Lagrangians, are extended here to incorporate the so called off-shell effects inherent to the fermions with spin >= 3/2. A formalism for intermediate-state, spin 3/2, nucleonic and hyperonic resonances is presented and applied to the processes $γ+ p ---> K^{+} + Λ$, for $E_γ^{lab}$ <= 2.5 GeV, $e + p ---> e' + K^+ + Λ$, as well as the branching ratio for the crossed channel reaction $K^- + p ---> γ+ Λ$, with stopped kaons. The sensitivity, from moderate to significant, of various observables to such effects are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Saclay-Lyon (SL) effective-Lagrangian model for photo- and electroproduction of strangeness by incorporating off-shell effects for spin-3/2 baryonic resonances, which were previously omitted or handled inconsistently between s-channel and u-channel exchanges. The authors show that the off-shell parameters provide a controlled way to eliminate unphysical growth in the non-pole parts of the amplitudes without resorting to ad hoc modifications that break the propagator's mathematical consistency.

## Method

The authors construct a gauge-invariant effective-Lagrangian formalism for spin-3/2 nucleonic and hyperonic resonances, introducing off-shell parameters (X, Y, Z) in the interaction vertices that preserve the point-transformation symmetry of the free Rarita-Schwinger field. They derive the invariant amplitudes for s-channel and u-channel resonance exchanges, including both γ⁵ and derivative electromagnetic couplings, and apply the formalism to γp → K⁺Λ, ep → e'K⁺Λ, and the crossed-channel reaction K⁻p → γΛ with stopped kaons. The u-channel amplitudes are obtained via a substitution rule derived from charge-conjugation properties, ensuring a consistent treatment of both channels.

## Key numbers

- Photoproduction cross sections are calculated for incident photon energies up to E_γ^(lab) ≤ 2.5 GeV
- The spin-3/2 propagator is given in its simplest form with the parameter A = -1, yielding the explicit expression P_μν(q) = (q̸ + M_R)/[3(q² − M_R²)] × [3g_μν − γ_μγ_ν − 2q_μq_ν/M_R² − (q_νγ_μ − q_μγ_ν)/M_R]
- The off-shell parameters appear in the vertices via the combinations X̃ ≡ X + 1/2, Ỹ ≡ 2Y + 1, Z̃ ≡ Z + 1/2
- The pole contributions to the invariant amplitudes are completely independent of the off-shell parameters X, Y, Z
- For negative-parity resonances, the invariant amplitudes obey the parity rule E_ij^{(-)}(M_R) = (−1)^{i+1} E_ij^{(+)}(−M_R) for i=1,2 and j=1,...,6

## Context

This work extends the Saclay-Lyon (SL) model (1997) for strangeness electromagnetic production, which had previously avoided spin-3/2 hyperonic resonances in the u-channel due to singular propagator behavior. It builds upon the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) group's treatment of spin-3/2 particles in pion photoproduction (Benmerrouche et al., 1989; Davidson et al., 1991) and η photoproduction (Benmerrouche et al., 1992, 1995), applying their off-shell parameter formalism to strangeness production. The approach enables a consistent simultaneous description of s-channel and u-channel spin-3/2 resonance exchanges, which will be necessary for interpreting data from new facilities such as JLAB, ELSA, ESRF, and SPring-8.
