# Hybrid star phenomenology from the properties of the special point

> We study the properties of hybrid stars containing a color superconducting quark matter phase in their cores, which is described by the chirally symmetric formulation of the confining relativistic density functional approach. It is shown that depending on the dimensionless vector and diquark couplings of quark matter, the characteristics of the deconfinement phase transition are varied, allowing us to study the relation between those characteristics and mass-radius relations of hybrid stars. Moreover, we show that the quark matter equation of state (EoS) can be nicely fitted by the Alford-Braby-Paris-Reddy model that gives a simple functional dependence between the most important parameters of the EoS and microscopic parameters of the initial Lagrangian. Based on it, we analyze the special points of the mass-radius diagram in which several mass-radius curves intersect. Using the found empirical relation between the mass of the special point, the maximum mass of the mass-radius curve, and the onset mass of quark deconfinement, we constrain the range of values of the vector and diquark couplings of the quark matter model. With this constraint, we construct a family of mass-radius curves, which allow us to describe the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 with a mass of $2.35\pm 0.17~\rm M_\odot$ as a hybrid star with a color superconducting quark matter core.

## Key claim

The authors show that the mass of the "special point" (where multiple mass-radius curves intersect) in hybrid star phenomenology can be used to constrain the vector and diquark couplings of a confining relativistic density functional approach to quark matter, enabling the description of the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 as a hybrid star with a color superconducting quark matter core, but only within the specific parameter ranges where the special point relation holds.

## Method

The authors model quark matter using a chirally symmetric confining relativistic density functional (RDF) approach with density-dependent vector and diquark couplings, fitted to reproduce vacuum QCD phenomenology (pion mass, decay constant, scalar meson mass, chiral condensate, and pseudocritical temperature). They then fit the resulting quark matter equation of state to the Alford-Braby-Paris-Reddy (ABPR) model, obtaining analytical parameterizations of the ABPR parameters \(A_4\), \(\Delta\), and \(B\) as functions of the dimensionless vector coupling \(\eta_V\) and diquark coupling \(\eta_D\). Hybrid star configurations are constructed by matching the quark matter EoS to the hadronic DD2npY-T EoS via Maxwell construction and solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

* The black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607 has a mass of \(2.35 \pm 0.17~M_\odot\) and is described as a hybrid star with a color superconducting quark matter core.
* The vacuum scalar coupling is \(G_{S0} = 18.1~\mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), and the maximum diquark coupling before vacuum color superconductivity occurs is \(\eta_D^{\mathrm{max}} = 0.78\).
* The non-perturbative gluon mass is fixed to \(M_g = 600~\mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with the Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov expansion.
* The ABPR parameter \(A_4\) ranges from 0.281 to 0.898 across the analyzed parameter space (\(\eta_V \in [0.1, 0.4]\), \(\eta_D \in [0.4, 0.8]\)), while the diquark gap \(\Delta\) ranges from 162.6 to 275.1 MeV and the bag pressure \(B\) ranges from 95.0 to 210.1 MeV/fm\(^3\).
* The fit parameters for the ABPR representation are given in Table 3, with coefficients such as \(a_1 = 0.757\), \(b_1 = -1.955\), \(c_1 = 1.799\), \(d_1 = -0.063\), and \(e_1 = 0.046\) for \(A_4\).

## Context

This work extends the confining RDF approach developed by Ivanytskyi et al. (2022) and Kaltenborn et al. (2017) by providing an analytical ABPR parameterization that bridges microscopic quark matter couplings to phenomenological hybrid star observables. It builds on the concept of special points in the mass-radius diagram introduced by Yudin et al. (2014) and further studied by Cierniak et al. (2020, 2021) and Blaschke et al. (2020), which show weak sensitivity to details of the hadronic EoS. The results enable future numerical-relativity simulations and observational tests using the special point relation to constrain quark matter properties from neutron star mass measurements.
