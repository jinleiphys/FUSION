# Neutrons from multiplicity-selected La-La and Nb-Nb collisions at 400A MeV and La-La collisions at 250A MeV

> Triple-differential cross sections for neutrons from high-multiplicity La-La collisions at 250 and 400 MeV per nucleon and Nb-Nb collisions at 400 MeV per nucleon were measured at several polar angles as a function of the azimuthal angle with respect to the reaction plane of the collision. The reaction plane was determined by a transverse-velocity method with the capability of identifying charged-particles with Z=1, Z=2, and Z > 2. The flow of neutrons was extracted from the slope at mid-rapidity of the curve of the average in-plane momentum vs the center-of-mass rapidity. The squeeze-out of the participant neutrons was observed in a direction normal to the reaction plane in the normalized momentum coordinates in the center-of-mass system. Experimental results of the neutron squeeze-out were compared with BUU calculations. The polar-angle dependence of the maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio $r(θ)$ was found to be insensitive to the mass of the colliding nuclei and the beam energy. Comparison of the observed polar-angle dependence of the maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio $r(θ)$ with BUU calculations for free neutrons revealed that $r(θ)$ is insensitive also to the incompressibility modulus in the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

The maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio \(r(\theta)\) of neutrons emitted in high-multiplicity heavy-ion collisions is insensitive to the mass of the colliding nuclei, the beam energy (between 250 and 400A MeV), and the incompressibility modulus \(K\) in the nuclear equation of state, based on comparisons with Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations for free neutrons.

## Method

Triple-differential cross sections for neutrons were measured at several polar angles as a function of azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane for multiplicity-selected La-La collisions at 250 and 400A MeV and Nb-Nb collisions at 400A MeV. The reaction plane was determined using a transverse-velocity method with charged-particle identification for Z=1, Z=2, and Z>2. Neutron flow was extracted from the slope at mid-rapidity of the average in-plane momentum versus center-of-mass rapidity, and neutron squeeze-out was observed in normalized momentum coordinates after rotating events onto the flow axis.

## Key numbers

- Neutron flow for La-La collisions at 400A MeV: \(145 \pm 11\) MeV/c
- Neutron flow for La-La collisions at 250A MeV: \(104 \pm 13\) MeV/c
- Dispersion angles for reaction plane determination: \(31.3^{\circ} \pm 2.4^{\circ}\) (La-La 400A MeV), \(33.8^{\circ} \pm 2.3^{\circ}\) (La-La 250A MeV), and \(39.8^{\circ} \pm 3.0^{\circ}\) (Nb-Nb 400A MeV)
- Charged-particle multiplicity cuts: 34 (La-La 400A MeV), 30 (La-La 250A MeV), and 25 (Nb-Nb 400A MeV)
- Maximum normalized impact parameter for multiplicity cuts: \(b_{max}/(R_p+R_t) \approx 0.5\)
- Critical coalescence distance \(d_c\) for BUU free-neutron calculations: 2.8 fm (Nb-Nb and La-La at 400A MeV) and 3.0 fm (La-La at 250A MeV)
- BUU calculations tested with incompressibility moduli \(K = 150, 215, 380\) MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron collective flow and azimuthal anisotropy by Zhang et al. (1993) for Au-Au collisions and Elaasar et al. (1994) for Nb-Nb collisions, and by Madey et al. (1993) who found that the maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio does not depend on the mass of the colliding nuclei for Au-Au and Nb-Nb at 400A MeV. The present results confirm that this ratio is also insensitive to beam energy and to the incompressibility modulus in the nuclear equation of state, but the observed neutron squeeze-out is significantly stronger than BUU predictions, suggesting that further theoretical work is needed to understand the in-medium aspects of nuclear transport.
