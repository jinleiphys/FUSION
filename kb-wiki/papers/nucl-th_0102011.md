# Event-by-event analysis of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions in smoothed particle hydrodynamics

> The method of smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) is applied for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The SPH method has several advantages in studying event-by-event fluctuations, which attract much attention in looking for quark gluon plasma (QGP) formation, because it gives a rather simple scheme for solving hydrodynamical equations. Using initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy produced by NeXus event generator, we solve the hydrodynamical equation in event-by-event basis and study the fluctuations of hadronic observables such as dN/dy due to the initial conditions. In particular, fluctuations of elliptic flow coefficient v2 is investigated for both the cases, with and without QGP formation. This can be used as an additional test of QGP formation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that event-by-event fluctuations of the elliptic flow coefficient v2 in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions depend on the equation of state used, with the dispersion δv2 being about 20-30% smaller when a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) equation of state is assumed compared to a resonance gas (RG) equation of state, even though the average v2 values differ by only a few percent.

## Method

The authors applied smoothed particle hydrodynamics (SPH) using entropy as the conserved quantity carried by each SPH particle, formulated in (τ, x, y, η) coordinates. Initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at √s = 130A GeV were generated by the NeXus event generator, with primary hadrons aligned on the τ₀ = 1.0 fm hypersurface. The hydrodynamical evolution was solved event-by-event using two equations of state: a resonance gas (RG) with constant P/ε = 0.20, and a QGP equation of state featuring a first-order phase transition with bag constant B = 0.32 GeV/fm³ and critical temperature Tc = 160 MeV. Single-particle spectra were computed via the Cooper-Frye formula at freeze-out temperature Tf = 140 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at √s = 130A GeV with impact parameter b = 7.0 fm, the fluctuation δv₂ in QGP is about 20-30% smaller than in RG, while the difference in average v₂ between RG and QGP is only a few percent.
- The RG equation of state uses P/ε = 0.20 constant for all energy densities.
- The QGP equation of state has three regimes: P/ε = 0.20 for 0.00 < ε < 0.28 GeV/fm³ (ε₁), P/ε = 0.056/ε for 0.28 < ε < 1.45 GeV/fm³ (ε₂), and P/ε = 1/3 - 4B/3ε for ε > 1.45 GeV/fm³.
- The bag constant B = 0.32 GeV/fm³ gives critical temperature Tc = 160 MeV.
- Initial conditions use τ₀ = 1.0 fm and smoothing scale parameter h = 1 fm.
- Freeze-out temperature Tf = 140 MeV.
- The slope parameter T̃ and its fluctuation δT̃ are systematically smaller for QGP than for RG.
- Multiplicity fluctuations δn/⟨n⟩ are slightly larger for QGP than for RG.

## Context

This work extends the application of smoothed particle hydrodynamics, originally developed for astrophysics (SPH-as), to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on prior hydrodynamical descriptions dating back to Landau. The authors use initial conditions from the NeXus event generator (Werner and Drescher) and compare with experimental data from the STAR Collaboration (STAR00). The study enables event-by-event analysis of fluctuations as a tool to infer the equation of state and detect QGP formation, complementing earlier work on elliptic flow by Voloshin (1996) and event-by-event analysis by Heiselberg (2000). The SPH formulation follows the variational approach of Elze (1999) and the relativistic SPH framework (SPH-nu).
