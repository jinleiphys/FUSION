# Some insights on the partonic collectivity in heavy-ion collisions

> Elliptic flow and Number of Constituent Quarks scaling provide crucial insights into the underlying dynamics and degrees of freedom in heavy-ion collisions. This article provides some insights on the transition from hadronic to partonic collectivity, revealing possible key signatures of medium evolution. At low energies around ($\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \leq 4.0$ GeV), $v_2$ may be negative and NCQ scaling might break down. We expect that as the collision energy rises beyond 4.0 GeV, $v_2$ may become positive and NCQ scaling gradually restores. The transition in $v_2$ behavior, coupled with the breakdown and restoration of NCQ scaling, highlights the increasing significance of partonic interactions at higher energies, marking the onset of partonic collectivity and the emergence of quark-gluon plasma (QGP)-like properties.

# Some insights on the partonic collectivity in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The transition from hadronic to partonic collectivity in heavy-ion collisions is expected to be signaled by a change in elliptic flow (v2) from negative to positive values and the breakdown and subsequent restoration of Number of Constituent Quarks (NCQ) scaling as collision energy increases beyond approximately 4.0 GeV, though these expectations remain theoretical conjectures subject to experimental verification.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental results from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) program across collision energies from 3 to 62.4 GeV, comparing measured elliptic flow (v2) and NCQ scaling patterns at different energies. The analysis examines v2 as a function of transverse momentum (pT) and the scaling of v2 with the number of constituent quarks for various hadron species, including pions, kaons, protons, and phi mesons, to identify the energy threshold where partonic collectivity emerges.

## Key numbers
- At low collision energies around sqrt(s_NN) ≤ 4.0 GeV, v2 may be negative and NCQ scaling might break down
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, STAR data show a clear breakdown of NCQ scaling among pi+, K+, and proton v2
- NCQ scaling was relatively well maintained at sqrt(s_NN) ≥ 7.7 GeV during RHIC BES-I
- Deviations from NCQ scaling for phi-meson v2 were observed at approximately 2 sigma significance at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 and 11.5 GeV
- RHIC BES-II covers collision energies from sqrt(s_NN) = 3 to 19.6 GeV
- The BES program spans sqrt(s_NN) = 3 to 62.4 GeV

## Context
This work extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, which systematically reduces collision energy to search for signatures of a QCD first-order phase boundary and critical point (Fukushima 2010, Bzdak 2019, Luo 2020). The paper builds on experimental results from STAR (2021) showing NCQ scaling breakdown at 3 GeV and earlier findings from PHENIX and STAR collaborations that NCQ scaling holds above 7.7 GeV. The expected transition signatures would enable identification of the energy threshold for quark-gluon plasma formation and help distinguish between hadronic transport models (such as AMPT-HC) and partonic descriptions of the medium.
