# Light-nuclei spectra from chiral dynamics

> A major goal of nuclear theory is to explain the spectra and stability of nuclei in terms of effective many-body interactions amongst the nucleus' constituents-the nucleons, i.e., protons and neutrons. Such an approach, referred to below as the basic model of nuclear theory, is formulated in terms of point-like nucleons, which emerge as effective degrees of freedom, at sufficiently low energy, as a result of a decimation process, starting from the fundamental quarks and gluons, described by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). A systematic way to account for the constraints imposed by the symmetries of QCD, in particular chiral symmetry, is provided by chiral effective field theory, in the framework of a low-energy expansion. Here we show, in quantum Monte Carlo calculations accurate to $\leq\!2\%$ of the binding energy, that two- and three-body chiral interactions fitted {\sl only} to bound- and scattering-state observables in, respectively, the two- and three-nucleon sectors, lead to predictions for the energy levels and level ordering of nuclei in the mass range $A\,$=$\,$4-12 in very satisfactory agreement with experimental data. Our findings provide strong support for the fundamental assumptions of the basic model, and pave the way to its systematic application to the electroweak structure and response of these systems as well as to more complex nuclei.

## Key claim
Quantum Monte Carlo calculations using two- and three-body chiral interactions fitted only to two- and three-nucleon bound- and scattering-state observables (not to any data in heavier systems) predict energy levels and level ordering for nuclei with mass number A=4-12 in very satisfactory agreement with experiment, with the caveat that the three-body interaction parameters c_D and c_E are determined by simultaneously reproducing the experimental triton ground-state energy and the neutron-deuteron doublet scattering length.

## Method
The authors constructed local, configuration-space chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (Norfolk or NV2 models) at N2LO in the long-range part and up to N3LO in the short-range part, fitted to the Granada NN database up to 125 or 200 MeV laboratory energy. They then added a leading three-nucleon (3N) chiral interaction including Δ-isobar intermediate states, with two low-energy constants c_D and c_E fitted to the triton binding energy and nd doublet scattering length via hyperspherical-harmonics (HH) calculations. The full NV2+3 Hamiltonians were used in Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations for nuclei up to A=12, with the fermion sign problem requiring constrained path propagation even for A=4.

## Key numbers
- The NV2+3-Ia model gives GFMC ground-state energies: E0(³H) = -8.463(9) MeV, E0(³He) = -7.705(9) MeV, E0(⁴He) = -28.24(3) MeV, with Monte Carlo statistical errors in parentheses
- The rms energy deviation from experiment for 37 nuclear states in A=4-12 is 0.72 MeV for NV2+3-Ia, compared to 0.80 MeV for the phenomenological AV18+IL7 Hamiltonian
- The ³H and ⁴He ground-state energies without the 3N interaction range from -7.606 to -7.956 MeV and -23.99 to -25.80 MeV respectively, depending on the NV2 model variant
- The nd doublet scattering length without 3N interactions ranges from 0.993 to 1.284 fm (experimental value: 0.645 ± 0.010 fm)
- The ⁴He ground-state energy with 3N interactions differs by only 140 keV between models Ib and IIb, and by a few keV between cutoff variants a and b
- The final ¹²C ground state calculation required 650,000 cpu-hours on the Theta supercomputer

## Context
This work extends the chiral effective field theory (χEFT) approach to nuclear interactions by constructing local, configuration-space potentials suitable for quantum Monte Carlo methods, in contrast to earlier momentum-space chiral interactions that were non-local and unsuitable for such calculations (Gezerlis 2013, Piarulli 2016). It builds on the phenomenological AV18+IL7 Hamiltonian (Wiringa 1995, Pieper 2008) which achieved similar agreement with experiment but lacked the direct connection to QCD symmetries that χEFT provides. The results enable systematic application of the basic model to electroweak structure and response of light nuclei and more complex systems, while the persistent "Ay puzzle" in pd scattering at 3 MeV indicates that subleading contact terms in the 3N interaction (formally derived by Girlanda 2011) still need to be implemented.
