# Quantum analysis of fluctuations of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions

> We perform quantum calculations of fluctuations of the electromagnetic fields in $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The analysis is based on the fluctuation-dissipation theorem. We find that in the quantum picture the field fluctuations are very small. They turn out to be much smaller than the predictions of the classical Monte-Carlo simulation with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density.

## Key claim

Quantum calculations of electromagnetic field fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, based on the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, show that these fluctuations are very small and much smaller than predictions from classical Monte-Carlo simulations with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density, contradicting earlier claims that quantum diffusion of protons might be important.

## Method

The analysis uses the fluctuation-dissipation theorem (FDT) in the zero-temperature limit to express field fluctuations in AA collisions via the nuclear dipole polarizability. The dipole polarizability is parametrized by a single giant dipole resonance (GDR) state, with parameters fitted to experimental photoabsorption cross section data for 197Au and 208Pb nuclei. The field fluctuations are computed in the proper time region τ ∼ 0.2–1 fm, where the QGP may already be present and the magnetic field is still significant.

## Key numbers

- For 197Au: ω10 ≈ 13.6 MeV, Γ ≈ 4.38 MeV, c ≈ 18.2 GeV⁻² from fitting photoabsorption data
- For 208Pb: ω10 ≈ 13.3 MeV, Γ ≈ 3.72 MeV, c ≈ 18.93 GeV⁻² from fitting photoabsorption data
- Quantum dipole moment squared ⟨0|d²|0⟩ ≈ 1.91 fm² for 197Au and ≈ 2.02 fm² for 208Pb
- Classical Monte-Carlo with Woods-Saxon density gives ⟨d²⟩ ≈ 9.89 fm² for 197Au and ≈ 10.39 fm² for 208Pb (classical overestimates by factor ∼5)
- Ratio ⟨δBx²⟩¹/²/⟨By⟩ from quantum calculations is smaller than classical by factor ∼5–8 for RHIC (√s = 0.2 TeV) and by factor ∼13–27 for LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV)

## Context

This work extends the classical Monte-Carlo simulations of electromagnetic field fluctuations by Skokov, Deng, and Liao (all around 2010–2013) by providing a quantum treatment based on the fluctuation-dissipation theorem from Landau and Lifshitz. The results contradict the analysis by Tuchin (2015) which argued that quantum diffusion of protons may be very important, but that analysis was performed for free particles and is inapplicable to nuclei in the ground state. The work enables future joint analyses of field fluctuations and fluctuations of initial entropy deposition for event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of AA collisions.
