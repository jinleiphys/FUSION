# Description of the chiral bands in $^{188,190}Os$

> To a phenomenological core described by the Generalized Coherent State Model a set of interacting particles are coupled. Among the particle-core states one identifies a finite set which have the property that the angular momenta carried by the proton and neutron quadrupole bosons and the particles respectively, are mutually orthogonal. The magnetic properties of such states are studied. All terms of the model Hamiltonian satisfy the chiral symmetry except for the spin-spin interaction. There are four bands of two quasiparticle-core dipole states type, which exhibit properties which are specific for magnetic twin bands. Application is made for the isotopes $^{188, 190}$Os.

## Key claim

The paper claims that four magnetic twin bands (two of which are degenerate) with chiral properties exist in the even-even isotopes $^{188,190}$Os, arising from a particle-core coupling where the spin-spin interaction breaks the chiral symmetry of the otherwise chirally symmetric Hamiltonian, producing distinct bands related by chiral transformations of the angular momenta.

## Method

The authors couple two quasiparticles (protons in a single-j $h_{11/2}$ orbital) to a phenomenological core described by the Generalized Coherent State Model (GCSM), which uses separate proton and neutron quadrupole bosons. The particle-core Hamiltonian includes a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and a spin-spin force; the spin-spin term breaks the chiral symmetry. The model parameters are fixed by fitting the ground, beta, and gamma band excitation energies of each isotope via a least-square procedure, and the magnetic dipole transition probabilities are calculated using gyromagnetic factors derived consistently from the collective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* For $^{188}$Os: deformation parameter $\rho = 2.2$ (dimensionless); structure coefficients $A_1 = 438.7$ keV, $A_2 = -93.8$ keV, $A_3 = -70.5$ keV, $A_4 = 9.1$ keV; quadrupole-quadrupole strength $X'_{pc} = 1.02$ keV; spin-spin strength $X_{sS} = 3.0$ keV; gyromagnetic factors $g_p = 0.828 \mu_N$, $g_n = -0.028 \mu_N$, $g_F = 1.289 \mu_N$; root-mean-square deviation of fitted energies = 16.93 keV.
* For $^{190}$Os: deformation parameter $\rho = 2.0$; structure coefficients $A_1 = 366.1$ keV, $A_2 = 92.6$ keV, $A_3 = 24.0$ keV, $A_4 = 12.2$ keV; $X'_{pc} = 1.66$ keV; $X_{sS} = 2.0$ keV; $g_p = 0.7915 \mu_N$, $g_n = 0.0086 \mu_N$, $g_F = 1.289 \mu_N$; root-mean-square deviation = 18.63 keV.
* The signature for triaxial shape, $E_{2^+_g}+E_{2^+_{\gamma}}=E_{3^{+}_{\gamma}}$, is obeyed with a deviation of 2 keV for $^{188}$Os and 11 keV for $^{190}$Os.
* Calculated B(M1) values for the $1^+ \to 0^+_g$ and $1^+ \to 2^+_g$ transitions: $0.2772 \mu^2_N$ and $0.0139 \mu^2_N$ for $^{188}$Os; $0.1752 \mu^2_N$ and $0.0085 \mu^2_N$ for $^{190}$Os.
* The two-quasiparticle energy for the proton $h_{11/2}$ orbital was taken as 1.947 MeV for $^{188}$Os and 2.110 MeV for $^{190}$Os.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the GCSM to $^{192}$Pt (Raduta et al., 2014) and differs from prior chiral-band studies that focused mainly on odd-odd nuclei (e.g., Petrache et al., 1996; Simon et al., 2005; Vaman et al., 2004; Petrache et al., 2006) or even-odd and even-even systems (Mukhopadhyay et al., 2007; Luo et al., 2009). While earlier formalisms typically identified two magnetic bands related by a chiral transformation, the present approach yields four bands (with two degenerate) in even-even nuclei, and the gyromagnetic factors are calculated consistently from the collective Hamiltonian rather than taken as fixed ratios. The results enable future experimental searches for chiral bands in even-even nuclei and systematic studies of chiral features in this mass region.
