# Study of the hidden charm $D\bar{D}^*$ interactions in chiral effective field theory

> We study the chiral interactions of the hidden charm $D\bar{D}^*$ system within chiral effective field theory. Chiral Lagrangians are constructed by incorporating the chiral symmetry, heavy quark symmetry as well as proper charge conjugation properties of the heavy mesons. The interacting potentials of the $S$-wave $D\bar{D}^*$ are calculated up to second chiral order at 1-loop level, where complete two-pion exchange interactions are included. We further investigate the behaviors of the potentials in coordinate space, as well as their bound state properties. Our studies indicate that there exists a interacting strength ordering among considered four channels: $\text{str.}[0^+(1^{++})]>\text{str.}[0^-(1^{+-})] > \text{str.}[1^+(1^{+-})] > \text{str.}[1^-(1^{++})]$ where str. stands for the strength of the $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction. Moreover, we find that $X(3872)$ can be treated as a good candidate of $0^+(1^{++})$ molecular state. There also tends to form $0^-(1^{+-})$ and $1^+(1^{+-})$ molecular states and we expect the experiments to search for the predicted multi-structures around the $D\bar{D}^*$ mass region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the hidden charm \(D\bar{D}^*\) system, studied within chiral effective field theory up to second chiral order at one-loop level, exhibits a specific ordering of interaction strengths across four channels, and that the \(X(3872)\) is a good candidate for a \(0^+(1^{++})\) molecular state, with additional molecular states predicted in the \(0^-(1^{+-})\) and \(1^+(1^{+-})\) channels. The hedge is that these conclusions depend on the chiral effective field theory framework and the specific power-counting scheme adopted, and the existence of the predicted states requires experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors constructed chiral Lagrangians for the \(D\bar{D}^*\) system incorporating chiral symmetry, heavy quark symmetry, and charge conjugation properties. They calculated the \(S\)-wave \(D\bar{D}^*\) interacting potentials up to second chiral order \(O(\epsilon^2)\) at one-loop level, including contact, one-pion exchange (OPE), and complete two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions. The potentials were then iterated in the Schrödinger equation to investigate bound state properties in four channels defined by isospin \(I=0,1\) and \(C\)-parity \(C=\pm1\).

## Key numbers

* The \(X(3872)\) is identified as a good candidate for the \(0^+(1^{++})\) molecular state, with its mass extremely close to the \(D^0\bar{D}^{*0}\) threshold (the mass difference for the analogous \(T_{cc}\) state is cited as \(-273\pm61\pm5^{+11}_{-14}\) keV).
* The interaction strength ordering among the four channels is: \(\text{str.}[0^+(1^{++})] > \text{str.}[0^-(1^{+-})] > \text{str.}[1^+(1^{+-})] > \text{str.}[1^-(1^{++})]\).
* The pion decay constant \(f\) and the axial coupling \(g=0.59\) are used as input parameters in the chiral Lagrangians.

## Context

This work extends the heavy meson chiral effective field theory (HMChEFT) framework, previously applied to double-heavy systems such as \(\bar{B}\bar{B}\) (Liu 2012) and \(DD^*\) (Xu 2017), to the heavy-antiheavy \(D\bar{D}^*\) system. It provides a systematic, chiral-symmetry-based calculation that includes complete two-pion exchange, contrasting with earlier phenomenological one-boson-exchange model studies of the same system (Liu 2008, Li 2012, Wang 2021). The results enable future experimental searches for predicted multi-structures around the \(D\bar{D}^*\) mass region and offer a comparison point for other theoretical approaches to \(XYZ\) states.
