# Excited States of the Uniform Electron Gas

> The uniform electron gas (UEG) is a cornerstone of density-functional theory (DFT) and the foundation of the local-density approximation (LDA), one of the most successful approximations in DFT. In this work, we extend the concept of UEG by introducing excited-state UEGs, systems characterized by a gap at the Fermi surface created by the excitation of electrons near the Fermi level. We report closed-form expressions of the reduced kinetic and exchange energies of these excited-state UEGs as functions of the density and the gap. Additionally, we derive the leading term of the correlation energy in the high-density limit. By incorporating an additional variable representing the degree of excitation into the UEG paradigm, the present work introduces a new framework for constructing local and semi-local state-specific functionals for excited states.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a generalization of the uniform electron gas (UEG) for excited states by creating a gap at the Fermi surface through excitation of electrons near the Fermi level, and reports closed-form expressions for kinetic and exchange energies as functions of density and gap, plus the leading correlation term in the high-density limit. The work is presented as a pure-state formalism (not ensemble-based) and the author acknowledges that the mapping from a global UEG property to a local molecular property "requires further investigation" and "remains to be thoroughly validated."

## Method

The author constructs excited-state UEGs by opening a gap of magnitude k_Fσ Δ_σ at the Fermi level for each spin channel, where electrons in the range k_Fσ(1-Δ_σ) to k_Fσ are excited to occupy levels from k_Fσ to k_Fσ(1+κ_σ Δ_σ), with κ_σ determined by requiring the spin density to match the ground-state value. Closed-form expressions for the reduced kinetic and exchange energies are derived analytically as functions of the gap parameter Δ, and the leading-order (logarithmic) term of the correlation energy in the high-density limit is obtained by identifying six divergent contributions in Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory.

- Excited-state kinetic scaling function limit at Δ=1: Ξ_s(1) = 2^(5/3) - 1 ≈ 2.1748
- Excited-state exchange scaling function limit at Δ=1: Ξ_x(1) ≈ 0.944717

- κ parameter limit at Δ=1: κ(1) = 2^(1/3) - 1 ≈ 0.259921

## Context

This work extends the ground-state UEG paradigm (ParrBook, VignaleBook, LoosGil-WIREs-16) to excited states, building on the "jellium with a gap" model (Cal-PR-59, ReySav-IJQC-98) and the recent ensemble-based cofe UEG approach of Gould and Pittalis (2024). The author moves beyond the ensemble framework to a pure-state formalism, providing a foundation for constructing local and semi-local state-specific functionals for excited states in DFT, analogous to how the ground-state UEG underpins the LDA. The work also relates to earlier efforts by Harbola and collaborators (SamHar-JPB-05, HemHar-JMS-10) who constructed LDA exchange functionals for excited states, though the author notes their approach "clearly lacks generality and is restricted to exchange."
