# Towards symmetry-unrestricted Skyrme-HFB in coordinate-space representation: the example of rotational bands of the octupole-deformed nucleus $^{222}$Th

> We report on cranked Skyrme-HFB calculations of rotational bands of the octupole-deformed nucleus $^{222}$Th. A sudden change in configuration is observed, with the shape of the yrast state jumping from large octupole deformation at low spin to small octupole deformation at high spin.

# Towards symmetry-unrestricted Skyrme-HFB in coordinate-space representation: the example of rotational bands of the octupole-deformed nucleus $^{222}$Th

## Key claim
Cranked Skyrme-HFB calculations predict that the yrast states of $^{222}$Th undergo a sudden change from shapes with large octupole deformation ($\beta_{30} \approx 0.12$) to almost symmetric ones ($\beta_{30} \approx 0.05$) between angular momentum $\langle \hat{\mathcal{J}}_x \rangle = 31$ and $32$, but this transition has not been observed experimentally and might occur at higher spins than those populated in past experiments by Smith (1995).

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SLy5s1 parameterisation of the Skyrme energy density functional with a surface-type contact pairing interaction and the Lipkin-Nogami procedure. Rotational bands were described by cranking about the $x$-axis perpendicular to the symmetry axis, lifting time-reversal, parity, and signature symmetries while retaining only the $y$-time simplex symmetry, using the new 3D coordinate-space code MOCCa.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state minimum of $^{222}$Th located at $(\beta_{20}, \beta_{30}) \approx (0.15, 0.12)$, about 1 MeV lower than the reflection-symmetric saddle point at $(0.14, 0)$
- Abrupt structural change occurs between $\langle \hat{\mathcal{J}}_x \rangle = 31$ and $32$, where octupole deformation drops from $\beta_{30} \approx 0.12$ to $\beta_{30} \approx 0.05$
- Static octupole deformation sets in for $^{220}$Th with SLy5s1, while with the D1M Gogny parameterisation it sets in with $^{222}$Th (Robledo, 2011)
- Macroscopic-microscopic models (Nazarewicz, 1984; Nazarewicz, 1987) predicted a similar transition but already at angular momentum of about $24$

## Context
This work extends cranked mean-field studies of octupole-deformed rotational bands (Garrote, 1997; Garrote, 1998; Tsvetkov, 2002) by demonstrating an abrupt shape change at high spin that had not been put into evidence before. The results differ from relativistic mean-field calculations with DD-PC1 and NL3 parameterisations (Nomura, 2013; Agbemava, 2016), which find octupole minima only for heavier Thorium isotopes. The calculations enable predictions for future experiments on $^{222}$Th and neighbouring odd-mass nuclei such as $^{223}$Th (Maquart, 2017).
