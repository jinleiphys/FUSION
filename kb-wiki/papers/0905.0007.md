# Quantum three-body calculation of the nonresonant triple-αreaction rate at low temperatures

> The triple-αreaction rate is re-evaluated by directly solving the three-body Schrödinger equation. The resonant and nonresonant processes are treated on the same footing using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method for three-body scattering. Accurate description of the α-αnonresonant states significantly quenches the Coulomb barrier between the two-α's and the third αparticle. Consequently, the α-αnonresonant continuum states below the resonance at 92.04 keV, i.e., the ground state of 8Be, give markedly larger contribution at low temperatures than in foregoing studies. We show that Nomoto's method for three-body nonresonant capture processes, which is adopted in the NACRE compilation and many other studies, is a crude approximation of the accurate quantum three-body model calculation. We find about 20 orders-of-magnitude enhancement of the triple-αreaction rate around 10^7 K compared to the rate of NACRE.

## Key claim

The nonresonant triple-alpha reaction rate at low temperatures (around 10^7 K) is enhanced by about 20 orders of magnitude compared to the NACRE compilation rate, because the accurate quantum three-body calculation reveals that alpha-alpha nonresonant continuum states below the 92.04 keV resonance significantly quench the Coulomb barrier between the two-alpha system and the third alpha particle, an effect that Nomoto's method (used in NACRE) cannot capture.

## Method

The authors directly solved the three-body Schrödinger equation for the three-alpha system using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), which treats resonant and nonresonant processes on the same footing. They discretized the alpha-alpha continuum from 0.668 keV to 176 keV into 122 momentum bins, including the 86th bin corresponding to the alpha-alpha resonance at 92.0 keV, and solved coupled-channel equations for the relative motion between the alpha-alpha subsystem and the third alpha particle. The triple-alpha reaction rate was then obtained by averaging the E2 transition probability to the 2_1^+ state of 12C over the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution for temperatures from 10^7 K to 10^9 K.

## Key numbers

- At T = 10^7 K, the CDCC triple-alpha reaction rate is 1.08 × 10^{-44} cm^6 s^{-1} mol^{-2}, which is a factor of 3.7 × 10^{26} larger than the NACRE rate.
- At T = 1.5 × 10^8 K, the CDCC rate is 3.42 × 10^{-38} cm^6 s^{-1} mol^{-2}, a factor of 5.4 × 10^{23} larger than NACRE.
- At T = 2.5 × 10^8 K, the ratio to NACRE is 1.6 × 10^{20}, and at T = 10^9 K the ratio is unity after renormalization.
- The alpha-alpha resonance energy is 92.04 keV with a width of 5.57 eV (experimental values); the potential used gives 92.0 keV with width 4.8 eV.
- The Hoyle resonance energy is 379.5 keV above the three-alpha threshold.
- The discretization covered alpha-alpha relative momenta from 0.008 fm^{-1} (epsilon_12 = 0.668 keV) to 0.130 fm^{-1} (epsilon_12 = 176 keV) with bin width 0.001 fm^{-1}, giving i_max = 122.
- The renormalization factor applied to match the NACRE rate at 10^9 K was 1.54, with an estimated uncertainty of about 50%.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic three-alpha calculations of Kamimura and Fukushima (using the resonating group method) and Descouvemont and Baye (using the generator coordinate method), which included only a few discretized nonresonant states and completely missed alpha-alpha continuum states below the 92.04 keV resonance. The present CDCC calculation shows that Nomoto's method, adopted in the NACRE compilation and many other studies, is a crude approximation because it cannot account for the quenching of the Coulomb barrier when the alpha-alpha subsystem is in nonresonant states. The newly evaluated reaction rate will affect studies of helium burning in accreting white dwarfs and neutron stars, and has already been reported to cause inconsistency with observations of red giant branches when used in stellar evolution models.
