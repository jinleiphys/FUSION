# Rho resonance parameters from lattice QCD

> We perform a high-precision calculation of the phase shifts for $π$-$π$ scattering in the I = 1, J = 1 channel in the elastic region using elongated lattices with two mass-degenerate quark favors ($N_f = 2$). We extract the $ρ$ resonance parameters using a Breit-Wigner fit at two different quark masses, corresponding to $m_π = 226$MeV and $m_π = 315$MeV, and perform an extrapolation to the physical point. The extrapolation is based on a unitarized chiral perturbation theory model that describes well the phase-shifts around the resonance for both quark masses. We find that the extrapolated value, $m_ρ = 720(1)(15)$MeV, is significantly lower that the physical rho mass and we argue that this shift could be due to the absence of the strange quark in our calculation.

## Key claim

The extrapolated rho meson mass from two-flavor lattice QCD, \(m_\rho = 720(1)(15)\ \mathrm{MeV}\), is significantly lower than the physical rho mass; the authors argue this shift could be due to the absence of the strange quark in their calculation, which uses only two mass-degenerate quark flavors (\(N_f = 2\)).

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of \(\pi\pi\) scattering phase shifts in the \(I=1\), \(J=1\) channel using elongated boxes with two mass-degenerate dynamical quark flavors. They extracted the lowest three or four energy levels from a variational basis of four quark-antiquark and two or three pion-pion interpolators, using the Laplacian-Heaviside (LapH) method to compute all-to-all quark propagators. Phase shifts were obtained via Luscher's formula extended to elongated boxes and boosted frames, and the rho resonance parameters were extracted using a Breit-Wigner fit at two pion masses (\(m_\pi = 226\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_\pi = 315\ \mathrm{MeV}\)), followed by a chiral extrapolation to the physical point using a unitarized chiral perturbation theory model.

## Key numbers

*   Extrapolated rho meson mass: \(m_\rho = 720(1)(15)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (first error statistical, second systematic), from a Breit-Wigner fit and chiral extrapolation to the physical point using \(N_f=2\) lattice QCD.
*   Two pion masses used in the calculation: \(m_\pi = 226\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(m_\pi = 315\ \mathrm{MeV}\), corresponding to the two different sea quark masses for the lattice ensembles.
*   The elastic region studied is below the \(4m_\pi\) threshold, which for these ensembles contains only the lowest three or four states.

## Context

This work extends a previous lattice QCD calculation by Pelissier et al. (2012) by adding a larger interpolator field basis and ensembles at lower pion masses, enabling a chiral extrapolation to the physical point. The results are significantly lower than the physical rho mass, and the authors argue this discrepancy is likely due to the absence of the strange quark in their \(N_f=2\) calculation, contrasting with \(N_f=2+1\) results such as those by Bolton et al. (2015). This study enables future investigations into the role of the strange quark in the rho resonance and tests of chiral perturbation theory models against lattice data.
