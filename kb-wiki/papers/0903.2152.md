# Antiproton-nucleus collisions simulation within a kinetic approach with relativistic mean fields

> The Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model with relativistic mean fields is used to simulate $\bar p$-nucleus collisions. Antiproton absorption cross sections and momentum distributions of annihilation products are calculated by varying the $\bar p$ coupling strength to the mean meson fields. Parameters of the antiproton-nucleus optical potential are extracted from the comparison of the model calculations with experimental data.

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model with relativistic mean fields can reproduce antiproton-nucleus absorption data only when the antiproton coupling to mean meson fields is reduced to about 21% of the G-parity value (ξ = 0.21 ± 0.03), contradicting the naive G-parity expectation of ξ = 1 that would give an extremely deep real optical potential of −661 MeV.

## Method

The Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model was used to simulate antiproton-nucleus collisions, solving semiclassical kinetic equations for baryons, antibaryons, and mesons coupled via collision terms and relativistic mean fields (σ, ω, ρ mesons). Antiproton absorption cross sections were calculated for ¹²C, ²⁷Al, and ⁶⁴Cu targets as a function of beam momentum by varying the scaling factor ξ that relates antinucleon-meson coupling constants to nucleon-meson coupling constants. The model was fitted to KEK data at beam momenta 470–880 MeV/c, and the resulting optical potential parameters were extracted by approximating the real and imaginary parts with Woods-Saxon forms.

## Key numbers

- Scaling factor ξ = 0.21 ± 0.03 from global fit to 18 data points for three nuclei (χ²/F = 2.0, F = 17)
- For ¹²C: ξ = 0.22 ± 0.03, real depth V₀ = 153 ± 21 MeV, imaginary depth W₀ = 110 MeV
- For ²⁷Al: ξ = 0.21 ± 0.04, real depth V₀ = 162 ± 37 MeV, imaginary depth W₀ = 108 MeV
- For ⁶⁴Cu: ξ = 0.21 ± 0.04, real depth V₀ = 153 ± 29 MeV, imaginary depth W₀ = 103 MeV
- At ξ = 1 (G-parity value), the antiproton vector potential in nuclear matter at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³ is V⁰ = −308 MeV, scalar potential S = −380 MeV, giving Re(V_opt) = −661 MeV
- The extracted real depth V₀ ≈ 150 ± 30 MeV is about 40% deeper than the value reported by Friedman (2005)
- Annihilation spectra favor even deeper real part V₀ ≈ 220 ± 70 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous attempts to determine the antiproton-nucleus optical potential, which had considerable ambiguity: elastic scattering data favored a shallow real part V₀ = 0–70 MeV (Nakamura 1984, Garreta 1984), while antiprotonic atom data from Friedman (2005) gave V₀ = 110 MeV. The present analysis uses absorption cross sections that probe the potential at about half-density radius, deeper than diffractive elastic scattering but not as deep as the extreme periphery probed by antiprotonic atoms. The results enable future studies of cold compression effects when antiprotons penetrate deeply into nuclear interiors, relevant for planned experiments at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR).
