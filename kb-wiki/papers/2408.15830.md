# The $g^6$ pressure of hot Yang-Mills theory: Canonical form of the integrand

> We present major progress towards the determination of the last missing piece for the pressure of a Yang-Mills plasma at high temperatures at order $g^6$ in the strong coupling constant. This order is of key importance due to its role in resolving the long-standing infrared problem of finite-temperature field theory within a dimensionally reduced effective field theory setup. By systematically applying linear transformations of integration variables, or momentum shifts, we resolve equivalences between different representations of Feynman sum-integrals. on the integrand level, transforming those into a canonical form. At the order $g^6$, this results in reducing a sum of O(100000) distinct sum-integrals which are produced from all four-loop vacuum diagrams down to merely 21. Furthermore, we succeed to map 11 of those onto known lower-loop structures. This leaves only 10 genuine 4-loop sum-integrals to be evaluated, thereby bringing the finalization of three decades of theoretical efforts within reach.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a dramatic reduction of the four-loop Yang-Mills pressure integrand at order $g^6$ from approximately 100,000 distinct sum-integrals down to only 21 canonical forms, with 11 of those mapped onto known lower-loop structures, leaving just 10 genuine four-loop sum-integrals to be evaluated; this progress brings within reach the completion of a three-decade theoretical effort to resolve the infrared problem of finite-temperature field theory, though the final numerical evaluation of those 10 integrals remains an open task.

## Method

The authors generate all four-loop vacuum Feynman diagrams for pure SU(Nc) Yang-Mills theory using Qgraf, then apply Feynman rules in general covariant gauges and perform color and Lorentz algebra with FORM to produce scalar sum-integrals expressed as lists of integer exponents on a fixed set of 10 momentum combinations. They systematically apply linear transformations of integration variables (momentum shifts) to resolve equivalences between different representations of Feynman sum-integrals at the integrand level, transforming them into a canonical form, and use graph polynomial comparisons to map integrals onto a standard momentum family.

## Key numbers

* The reduction compresses approximately 100,000 distinct sum-integrals (from 65 four-loop vacuum diagrams) down to 21 canonical forms.
* 11 of the 21 canonical sum-integrals are mapped onto known lower-loop structures, leaving 10 genuine four-loop sum-integrals to be evaluated.
* The gauge parameter xi appears up to xi^6 in the coefficient functions before canonicalization.
* The free theory term is p0 = (pi^2 T^4 / 45) * [(Nc^2 - 1) + (7/4) Nc Nf].
* Diagrams with gluon-3-vertices only contain 6 vertices and 9 propagators, leading to 6^6 * 2^9 ≈ 24 million terms each before combination.

## Context

This work extends the dimensionally reduced effective field theory roadmap laid out by Braaten and Nieto (1995) for resolving the infrared problem in hot QCD, which had been identified by Linde (1978, 1980) and Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981). The missing four-loop coefficient (beta_E1 in the notation of Kajantie et al., 2002) is the last perturbative piece needed to complete the high-temperature expansion of the Yang-Mills pressure; previous work by Kajantie et al. (2000, 2001, 2003), Hietanen et al. (2004), Giovannangeli and Karsch (2005), Schroder (2005), Laine and Schroder (2006), and Di Renzo et al. (2006, 2008) completed all other required steps. The present canonicalization enables the final evaluation of the remaining 10 integrals, which will allow comparison with non-perturbative lattice Monte Carlo simulations of pure Yang-Mills theory by Boyd et al. (1996), Beinlich et al. (1997), the CP-PACS collaboration (1999), and Borsanyi et al. (2012).
