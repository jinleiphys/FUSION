# The Entropy of a Jet

> Scattering processes often inevitably include the production of infrared states, which are highly correlated with the hard scattering event, and decohere the hard states. This can be described using the entropy of the hard reduced density matrix, which is obtained from tracing over infrared states. We determine this entropy for an asymptotically-free gauge theory by separating the Hilbert space into hard and infrared states, and calculate it in a leading-logarithmic approximation for jets. We find that the entropy increases when the resolution scales defining the hard radiation are lowered, that this entropy is related to the subjet multiplicity, and explore connections to using jet images for machine learning, and the forward-scattering density matrix of partons in a nucleon probed in deep-inelastic scattering.

## Key claim
The entropy of a jet's hard reduced density matrix, calculated at leading-logarithmic (LL) accuracy, increases as the resolution scales defining hard radiation are lowered and is asymptotically proportional to the subjet multiplicity; the calculation assumes strong angular ordering and the soft limit of splitting functions, and the result depends on an arbitrary scale Λ introduced to make the probability dimensionless.

## Method
The authors separate the Hilbert space of an asymptotically-free gauge theory into hard and infrared states, tracing over soft radiation below an energy scale E_c and collinear radiation below an angular scale R_c. They compute the von Neumann entropy of the resulting hard reduced density matrix at LL accuracy using angular-ordered factorization of splitting probabilities, where the diagonal matrix elements correspond to exclusive n-subjet cross sections. The entropy evolution equation is derived by taking the soft limit (z ≪ 1) of the splitting functions and solving analytically for a gluon jet with fixed coupling, then comparing to a Monte Carlo parton shower with both fixed and running coupling using the Cambridge/Aachen clustering algorithm.

## Key numbers
- Casimir factors: C_q = 4/3 for (anti-)quarks, C_g = 3 for gluons
- The entropy S_g(E,R) for a gluon jet at fixed coupling is expressed in terms of x ≡ 2[(2α_s C_A/π) ln(E/E_c) ln(R/R_c)]^{1/2}, with modified Bessel functions I_0 and I_1
- Asymptotically, the entropy grows as S_g ∝ e^x/√x, the same growth as the average subjet multiplicity
- The Monte Carlo result uses jet energy E = 1 TeV and jet radius R = 1 for the running coupling calculation shown in Fig. 1

## Context
This work extends perturbative QCD calculations of entropy production in parton cascades, contrasting with non-perturbative approaches such as Berges (2017) using the Lund string model. The authors compare their results to forward-scattering entropy calculations in deep-inelastic scattering by Kutak (2011), Kovner (2015), Kharzeev (2017), and Hagiwara (2017), noting that under a conformal mapping their entropy grows as e^{β√Y} (with Y rapidity) rather than proportional to Y (Kharzeev 2017) or exponential in Y (Hagiwara 2017). The entropy is connected to the λ-measure introduced by Andersson (1988, 1989) as a proxy for multiplicity, and the results have implications for machine learning on jet images, suggesting that coarse-grained representations can provide similar discrimination power because information is stochastically diluted at finer resolutions.
