# Chiral dynamics in the low-temperature phase of QCD

> We investigate the low-temperature phase of QCD and the crossover region with two light flavors of quarks. The chiral expansion around the point $(T, m_q = 0)$ in the temperature vs. quark-mass plane indicates that a sharp real-time excitation exists with the quantum numbers of the pion. We determine its dispersion relation and test the applicability of the chiral expansion. The time-dependent correlators are also analyzed using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), yielding consistent results. Finally, we test the predictions of ordinary chiral perturbation theory around the point $(T = 0, m_q = 0)$ for the temperature dependence of static observables. Around the crossover temperature, we find that all quantities considered depend only mildly on the quark mass in the considered range 8MeV $\leq \bar{m}^{\bar{\text{MS}}} \leq$ 15MeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a sharp, real-time pion-like excitation exists in the low-temperature phase of QCD with two light quark flavors, and that its dispersion relation follows the form predicted by chiral effective theory, but only up to temperatures around 190 MeV, above which the interpretation as a pion velocity becomes unreliable.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate dynamical quark flavors using Wilson fermions with a clover term for O(a) improvement, on lattices of size 16×32³. They computed Euclidean correlation functions of the pseudoscalar density and axial vector current, extracted the screening pion mass and decay constant from spatial correlators, and derived two independent lattice estimators (u_f and u_m) for the pion velocity u(T) based on chiral Ward identities. The spectral functions were also reconstructed using the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass range studied: 8 MeV ≤ m̅^MS̅ ≤ 15 MeV (renormalized in the MS̅ scheme at μ=2 GeV)
- Temperature range covered: 150 ≤ T ≤ 235 MeV
- Lattice size: 16 × 32³, with T = 1/(16a)
- Sommer scale: r₀ = 0.503(10) fm
- For the zero-temperature test ensemble A5 (pion mass 290 MeV): u_f = 0.96(2), u_m = 0.92(6), u_f/u_m = 1.04(4)
- At T ≈ 150 MeV with m̅^MS̅ = 15 MeV: u_f = 0.88(2)
- At T ≈ 150 MeV, the screening pion mass m_π and decay constant f_π changed by about 5% from their T=0 values
- Critical temperature at the chiral limit: T_c(0) ≈ 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective theory approach of Son and Stephanov (2002) for the pion dispersion relation at finite temperature, and tests the predictions of ordinary chiral perturbation theory from Leutwyler (1993) for static observables. The results enable future studies of the pion quasiparticle at nonzero momentum, finite-volume effects with larger aspect ratios (LT=3 and 4), and extension to physical quark masses with a dynamical strange quark.
