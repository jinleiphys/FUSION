# Puzzles in the hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment

> We summarize recent developments in the Standard-Model evaluation of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_╬╝$, both in the hadronic-light-by-light and hadronic-vacuum-polarization contributions. The current situation for the latter is puzzling as we are confronted with multiple discrepancies that are not yet understood. We present updated fits of a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor to the new CMD-3 data set and quantify the tensions with the other high-statistics $e^+e^-\toŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ experiments in the contribution to $a_╬╝$ in the energy range up to 1 GeV, as well as in the corresponding contribution to the intermediate Euclidean window.

## Key claim

The Standard-Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment is currently complicated by multiple unresolved discrepancies in the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution, including a new 5Žā tension between the CMD-3 e+e- ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ- data set and the combination of all other high-statistics experiments for the energy range below 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed updated fits of a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor, constructed using an Omn├©s function with elastic ŽĆŽĆ-scattering P-wave phase shifts, a factor for Žü-Žē interference, and a conformal polynomial for inelastic contributions, to the new CMD-3 data set and to other major e+e- ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ- experiments (BaBar, KLOE, BESIII, SND06, CMD-2, SND20). They quantified the tensions between experiments in the contribution to a╬╝ in the energy range up to 1 GeV and in the intermediate Euclidean window, using the fit results to compute the significance of discrepancies.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework for the pion vector form factor developed by Colangelo, Hoferichter, and Stoffer (2018) and applies it to the new CMD-3 data set (2023). The paper addresses the tension between the BMWc lattice-QCD result (2020) and the data-driven WP evaluation, as well as the newly emerged discrepancy between CMD-3 and other e+e- experiments including BaBar (2012), KLOE (2017), BESIII (2015), SND06 (2006), and SND20 (2020). The results enable future work on understanding the source of these discrepancies through ongoing e+e- ŌåÆ ŽĆ+ŽĆ- analyses, reinvestigation of radiative corrections, and further lattice-QCD computations.
