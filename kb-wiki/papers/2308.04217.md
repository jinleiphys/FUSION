# Puzzles in the hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment

> We summarize recent developments in the Standard-Model evaluation of the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $a_μ$, both in the hadronic-light-by-light and hadronic-vacuum-polarization contributions. The current situation for the latter is puzzling as we are confronted with multiple discrepancies that are not yet understood. We present updated fits of a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor to the new CMD-3 data set and quantify the tensions with the other high-statistics $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-$ experiments in the contribution to $a_μ$ in the energy range up to 1 GeV, as well as in the corresponding contribution to the intermediate Euclidean window.

## Key claim

The Standard-Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment is currently complicated by multiple unresolved discrepancies in the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution, including a new 5σ tension between the CMD-3 e+e- → π+π- data set and the combination of all other high-statistics experiments for the energy range below 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed updated fits of a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor, constructed using an Omnès function with elastic ππ-scattering P-wave phase shifts, a factor for ρ-ω interference, and a conformal polynomial for inelastic contributions, to the new CMD-3 data set and to other major e+e- → π+π- experiments (BaBar, KLOE, BESIII, SND06, CMD-2, SND20). They quantified the tensions between experiments in the contribution to aμ in the energy range up to 1 GeV and in the intermediate Euclidean window, using the fit results to compute the significance of discrepancies.

## Key numbers

- Experimental value of the muon anomalous magnetic moment: aμexp = 116 592 061(41) × 10-11 (0.35 ppm precision)
- 2020 White Paper Standard-Model prediction: aμWP = 116 591 810(43) × 10-11, giving a 4.2σ tension with experiment
- BMWc lattice HVP leading-order contribution: aμHVP, LO, BMWc = 7 075(55) × 10-11, in 2.1σ tension with the WP evaluation based on e+e- data
- HLbL WP value: aμHLbL, WP = 92(19) × 10-11
- Lattice HLbL results: 109.6(15.9) × 10-11 (Chao et al.) and 124.7(14.9) × 10-11 (Blum et al.)
- CMD-3 fit result for aμππ up to 1 GeV: 513.7(1.1)(4.0) × 10-11
- Combination fit result for aμππ up to 1 GeV: 494.8(1.5)(1.4)(3.4) × 10-11
- Discrepancy between CMD-3 and the combination for aμππ up to 1 GeV: 3.7σ (5.0σ excluding the BaBar-KLOE systematic effect)
- Discrepancy between CMD-3 and the combination for the intermediate window: 3.8σ (5.7σ excluding the BaBar-KLOE systematic effect)
- Discrepancy between CMD-3 and KLOE for aμππ in the [0.60,0.88] GeV range: 5.6σ
- ω width used in fits: Γω = 8.71(6) MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersive framework for the pion vector form factor developed by Colangelo, Hoferichter, and Stoffer (2018) and applies it to the new CMD-3 data set (2023). The paper addresses the tension between the BMWc lattice-QCD result (2020) and the data-driven WP evaluation, as well as the newly emerged discrepancy between CMD-3 and other e+e- experiments including BaBar (2012), KLOE (2017), BESIII (2015), SND06 (2006), and SND20 (2020). The results enable future work on understanding the source of these discrepancies through ongoing e+e- → π+π- analyses, reinvestigation of radiative corrections, and further lattice-QCD computations.
