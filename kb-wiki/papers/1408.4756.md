# Enhanced production of $ψ(2S)$ mesons in heavy ion collisions

> We study the production of a $ψ(2S)$ meson in heavy ion collisions. We evaluate Wigner functions for the $ψ(2S)$ meson using both Gaussian and Coulomb wave functions, and investigate the wave function dependence in the $ψ(2S)$ meson production by recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks. The enhanced transverse momentum distribution of $ψ(2S)$ mesons compared to that of $J/ψ$ mesons, originated from wave function distributions of the $ψ(2S)$ and $J/ψ$ meson in momentum space, provides a plausible explanation for the recent measurement of the nuclear modification factor ratio between the $ψ(2S)$ and $J/ψ$ meson.

# Enhanced production of ψ(2S) mesons in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The enhanced transverse momentum distribution of ψ(2S) mesons relative to J/ψ mesons, arising from differences in their momentum-space wave function distributions, provides a plausible explanation for the measured increase of the nuclear modification factor ratio R_AA^{ψ(2S)}/R_AA^{J/ψ} with centrality in heavy ion collisions, but only under the assumption that both charmonium states are produced by recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method
The author evaluates Wigner functions for the ψ(2S) meson using both Gaussian wave functions (based on a harmonic oscillator potential) and Coulomb wave functions (based on a color Coulomb potential), then applies these Wigner functions within a coalescence model to compute the transverse momentum distributions of charmonia produced by recombination of charm and anti-charm quarks. The calculation uses charm quark p_T spectra from RHIC at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and LHC at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, with hadronization volumes of 1000 fm^3 and 2700 fm^3 respectively, and determines oscillator frequencies and Bohr radii from known quark separation distances.

## Key numbers
- Quark separation distances used: r_0 = 0.50 fm for J/ψ, 0.72 fm for χ_{c1}, and 0.90 fm for ψ(2S) (from Satz 2005)
- Oscillator frequencies obtained: 311.4 MeV for J/ψ, 250.3 MeV for χ_{c1}, and 224.3 MeV for ψ(2S)
- Coulomb Bohr radii: a_0^{J/ψ} = 0.289 fm, a_0^{ψ(2S)} = 0.139 fm
- Hadronization volume: 1000 fm^3 for central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV (RHIC); 2700 fm^3 for central Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV (LHC)
- Charm quark p_T distribution for RHIC: d^2N_c/d^2p_T = 19.2(1+p_T^2/36)/(1+p_T/3.7)^{12}/(1+e^{0.9-2p_T})(0.8e^{-p_T/1.2}+0.6e^{-p_T/15}) (from Oh 2009)
- Charm quark p_T distribution for LHC: dN_c/dp_T = p_T/(1+0.379p_T^2)^{5.881} (from Lang 2013)
- Statistical factor for J/ψ formation: g_{J/ψ} = 3/36
- Feed-down fractions used: 0.348 from χ_{c1}, 0.198 from χ_{c2}, 0.603 from ψ(2S) (from Beringer 2012)

## Context
This work extends the recombination model previously applied to J/ψ production (Thews 2000, Braun-Munzinger 2000, Andronic 2007) and to the quark number scaling of elliptic flows (Molnar 2003, Greco 2003, Fries 2003) by specifically addressing the ψ(2S) excited state. The study provides a wave-function-dependent explanation for the CMS measurement (2013) of the double ratio R_AA^{ψ(2S)}/R_AA^{J/ψ} increasing with centrality at LHC, which had not been clearly understood. The results enable future experimental tests: precise measurements of the same ratio at midrapidity could confirm ψ(2S) production by recombination and help distinguish between Coulomb-like and harmonic-oscillator-like potentials between charm quarks.
