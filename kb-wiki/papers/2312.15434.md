# New quantification of symmetry energy from neutron skin thicknesses of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb

> Precise knowledge of the nuclear symmetry energy can be tentatively calibrated through multimessenger constraints. The neutron skin thickness of a heavy nucleus is one of the most sensitive indicators for probing the isovector components of effective interactions in asymmetric nuclear matter. Recent studies have suggested that the experimental data from the CREX and PREX2 Collaborations are not mutually compatible within existing nuclear models. In this study, we review the quantification of the slope parameter of symmetry energy $L$ from the neutron skin thicknesses of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. Skyrme energy density functionals classified by various isoscalar incompressibility coefficients $K$ are employed to evaluate the bulk properties of finite nuclei. The calculated results suggest that the slope parameter $L$ deduced from $^{208}$Pb is sensitive to the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter, but not that from $^{48}$Ca. The effective parameter sets classified by $K=220$ MeV can provide an almost overlaping range of $L$ from $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb.

# New quantification of symmetry energy from neutron skin thicknesses of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb

## Key claim

The slope parameter $L$ of the nuclear symmetry energy deduced from the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb is sensitive to the isoscalar incompressibility coefficient $K$ of symmetric nuclear matter, whereas the $L$ value deduced from $^{48}$Ca is not; the authors find that effective parameter sets classified by $K=220$ MeV can provide an almost overlapping range of $L$ from both nuclei, suggesting that the apparent incompatibility between CREX and PREX2 data may be partially resolved when the isoscalar compression modulus is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors employed Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) with parameter sets classified by three different isoscalar incompressibility coefficients ($K = 220$, 230, and 240 MeV) to self-consistently calculate the neutron and proton density distributions and neutron skin thicknesses of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. Linear fits were applied to the correlation between the slope parameter $L$ and the neutron skin thickness for each $K$ class, and the resulting $L$ ranges were compared with the experimental constraints from the CREX and PREX2 Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from PREX2: $\Delta R_{\mathrm{np}}^{208} = 0.212$ to $0.354$ fm.
- Neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca from CREX: $\Delta R_{\mathrm{np}}^{48} = 0.071$ to $0.171$ fm.
- For $K=220$ MeV, the slope parameter $L$ deduced from $^{48}$Ca ranges from $0$ to $60.96$ MeV (systematic uncertainty $3.08$ MeV), and from $^{208}$Pb ranges from $62.94$ to $136.65$ MeV (systematic uncertainty $1.70$ MeV).
- For $K=230$ MeV, $L$ from $^{48}$Ca ranges from $0$ to $57.64$ MeV ($2.87$ MeV), and from $^{208}$Pb ranges from $74.05$ to $155.99$ MeV ($1.64$ MeV).
- For $K=240$ MeV, $L$ from $^{48}$Ca ranges from $0$ to $52.78$ MeV ($2.54$ MeV), and from $^{208}$Pb ranges from $81.35$ to $168.01$ MeV ($1.33$ MeV).
- The linear correlation coefficient for the $L$ versus $\Delta R_{\mathrm{np}}^{208}$ fit at $K=220$ MeV is $R=0.99$.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter from experimental data on giant monopole resonances covers $230 \pm 10$ MeV.

## Context

This work addresses the well-known tension between the CREX and PREX2 experimental results, which previous studies (Tagami et al., 2022) found to yield non-overlapping $L$ ranges ($0$ to $50$ MeV from CREX and $76$ to $165$ MeV from PREX2) when using existing nuclear EDFs. The authors extend prior analyses by explicitly classifying Skyrme parameter sets according to the isoscalar incompressibility coefficient $K$, an approach that had been less emphasized in earlier work focused primarily on isovector components. The finding that $K=220$ MeV yields overlapping $L$ ranges suggests that future calibration of new Skyrme parameter sets should incorporate the isoscalar compression modulus as an auxiliary condition, and the authors recommend extending this analysis to relativistic EDFs.
