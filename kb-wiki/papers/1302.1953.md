# Virtual decuplet effects on octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We extend a previous analysis of the lowest-lying octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) by explicitly taking into account the contribution of the virtual decuplet baryons. Up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO), the effects of these heavier degrees of freedom are systematically studied. Their effects on the light-quark mass dependence of the octet baryon masses are shown to be relatively small and can be absorbed by the available low-energy constants up to N$^3$LO. Nevertheless, a better description of the finite-volume corrections of the lattice QCD data can be achieved, particularly those with small $M_ϕL$ ($<4$), which is demonstrated by a careful study of the NPLQCD and QCDSF-UKQCD small-volume data. Finally, we show that the predicted pion- and strangeness-baryon sigma terms are only slightly changed by the inclusion of the virtual decuplet baryons.

# Virtual decuplet effects on octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The explicit inclusion of virtual decuplet baryon contributions to octet baryon masses in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) yields effects that are relatively small and can be absorbed by the available low-energy constants, but provides a better description of finite-volume corrections in lattice QCD data, particularly those with small $M_\phi L$ (less than 4).

## Method

The authors extended the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) baryon ChPT framework to include virtual decuplet baryon contributions up to N$^3$LO, using the consistent coupling scheme for octet-decuplet-pseudoscalar vertices. They performed a simultaneous fit of publicly available $n_f=2+1$ lattice QCD data from the PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, QCDSF-UKQCD, and NPLQCD collaborations, with finite-volume corrections calculated self-consistently from both octet and decuplet loop diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The octet-decuplet mass splitting $\delta = m_D - m_0$ was fixed to 0.231 GeV, the average mass gap between octet and decuplet baryons
- The $\phi B D$ coupling constant $\mathcal{C}$ was fixed to 0.85, the SU(3)-average value among decuplet-to-octet pionic decay channels
- The effective decuplet mass parameter $m_D^{\rm eff} = m_D - t_0(2M_K^2 + M_\pi^2) = 1.215$ GeV
- The LEC $t_D = -0.326$ GeV$^{-1}$ was fixed by fitting to physical decuplet baryon masses
- The chiral limit octet baryon mass $m_0$ from the fit including decuplet contributions was 908(24) MeV (Fit-I), compared to 879(22) MeV without decuplet contributions
- The $\chi^2/\mathrm{d.o.f.}$ was 1.0 for both the octet-only and octet-plus-decuplet fits to lattice data Set-I ($M_\pi < 500$ MeV and $M_\pi L > 4$)
- For the NPLQCD data with $M_\phi L = 3.86$, the octet-plus-decuplet fit (Fit-12) yielded $\tilde{\chi}^2 = 0.7$ for the finite-volume dependence

## Context

This work extends the previous N$^3$LO EOMS baryon ChPT analysis of Ren et al. (2012), which did not explicitly include virtual decuplet baryons, by systematically incorporating their contributions. It builds on earlier studies of decuplet effects in heavy baryon ChPT by Jenkins et al. (1991) and Bernard et al. (1993), and on NNLO studies of finite-volume corrections by Geng et al. (2011). The results confirm that for static octet baryon properties, most virtual decuplet contributions are hidden in the low-energy constants, but their explicit inclusion improves the description of lattice data at small volumes, particularly for the NPLQCD and QCDSF-UKQCD data with $M_\phi L < 4$. This enables more reliable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results and better determinations of baryon sigma terms via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem.
