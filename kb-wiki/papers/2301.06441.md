# Production of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ in heavy ion collisions

> The yields of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ with its two possible configurations, i.e., the hadronic molecular state and tetraquark state, for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02~\rm{TeV}$ is studied. A volume effect is found from the centrality distribution of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$, which could help to distinguish the inner structure of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$. We also show the rapidity and the transverse momentum distributions of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ production as well as its elliptic flow coefficient as a function of the transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The production yield of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02~\rm{TeV}$ differs by two orders of magnitude between the hadronic molecular and tetraquark configurations, and the centrality dependence of this yield shows a strongly decreasing trend for the molecular state versus a mild change for the tetraquark state, which could serve as a probe of the internal structure. The abstract does not specify that this conclusion is based on simulations using the AMPT model with a modified $K$ factor and thermal-model composition estimates, nor that the qualitative result is robust against order-of-magnitude uncertainties in the charm and strange quark yields.

## Method

The authors introduced production mechanisms for both the hadronic molecular and tetraquark configurations of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ into the AMPT transport model (v1.26t9b) for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02~\rm{TeV}$. For the molecular picture, $D_s^+$ ($D_s^-$) and $D_s^{-*}$ ($D_s^{+*}$) mesons were coalesced if their relative distance was within $[5~\rm{fm},~7~\rm{fm}]$ and invariant mass within $[2M_{D_s^+},~2M_{D_s^{+*}}]$. For the tetraquark picture, diquarks $[cs]$ and antidiquarks $[\bar{c}\bar{s}]$ were formed by matching nearest (anti-)charm and (anti-)strange quarks, then coalesced if relative distance $<1~\rm{fm}$ and invariant mass within $[2M_{[cs]_1},~2M_{[cs]_0}]$; relative yields of spin states were estimated using a thermal model with freeze-out temperature $T_{\rm freezeout}\simeq160~\rm{MeV}$.

## Key numbers

* Inclusive yield of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ in the molecular picture: approximately 42000 per million minimum-bias events for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02~\rm{TeV}$.
* Inclusive yield of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ in the tetraquark picture: approximately 200 per million minimum-bias events for the same system and energy.
* Inclusive yield of $X(3872)$ in the molecular picture: approximately 171000 per million events (scaled by factor $1/4$ for wavefunction normalization).
* Inclusive yield of $X(3872)$ in the tetraquark picture: approximately 600 per million events.
* Ratio of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ yield to $X(3872)$ yield: approximately $1/4$.
* Ratio of molecular to tetraquark yield for $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$: a factor of approximately 200 (two orders of magnitude).
* Thermal-model composition fractions: $30\%$ $D_s^+$ and $70\%$ $D_s^{+*}$ for the molecular picture; $35\%$ spin-triplet diquark and $65\%$ spin-singlet diquark for the tetraquark picture, varied by $\pm10\%$ for uncertainty bands.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zhang (2020) for $X(3872)$ production in heavy ion collisions to the $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ system, where the light $u/d$ quarks are replaced by $s$ quarks. The study aims to distinguish between the hadronic molecular and compact tetraquark interpretations of the $X(4140)$, $X(4274)$, $X(4500)$, $X(4685)$, and $X(4700)$ states observed by LHCb, CDF, CMS, D0, and BaBar collaborations. The predicted strong centrality dependence and two-order-of-magnitude yield difference between the two configurations could be tested in future LHC measurements, and the authors suggest that a strangeness enhancement effect in the ratio of $X_{cs\bar{c}\bar{s}}$ to $X(3872)$ may provide further evidence for quark-gluon plasma formation.
