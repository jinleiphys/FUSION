# Implications of an increased $Λ$-separation energy of the hypertriton

> Stimulated by recent indications that the binding energy of the hypertriton could be significantly larger than so far assumed, requirements of a more strongly bound $^3_Λ{\rm H}$ state for the hyperon-nucleon interaction and consequences for the binding energies of $A=4,5$ and $7$ hypernuclei are investigated. As basis a $YN$ potential derived at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory is employed, Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations are solved to obtain the corresponding $3$- and $4$-body binding energies, respectively, and the Jacobi no-core shell model is used for $^5_Λ$He and $^7_Λ$Li. It is found that the spin-singlet $Λp$ interaction would have to be much more attractive which can be, however, accommodated within the bounds set by the available $Λp$ scattering data. The binding energies of the $^4_Λ{\rm He}$ hypernucleus are predicted to be closer to the empirical values than for $YN$ interactions that produce a more weakly bound $^3_Λ{\rm H}$. The quality of the description of the separation energy and excitation spectrum for $^7_Λ$Li remains essentially unchanged.

# Implications of an increased Λ-separation energy of the hypertriton

## Key claim
A significantly larger hypertriton binding energy (as suggested by the STAR collaboration) can be accommodated within existing Λp scattering data by increasing the spin-singlet Λp interaction strength, but only if one relaxes strict SU(3) symmetry for the contact interactions in the hyperon-nucleon (YN) forces, a symmetry breaking that is consistent with chiral effective field theory power counting.

## Method
The authors employed a YN potential derived at next-to-leading order (NLO) in chiral effective field theory (NLO19 and NLO13 parametrizations) and solved Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations to obtain three- and four-body binding energies, respectively. For A=5 and A=7 hypernuclei, they used the Jacobi no-core shell model (J-NCSM) with similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved interactions. They constructed three illustrative fits (A, B, C) with progressively larger spin-singlet Λp scattering lengths (a_s = -4.0, -4.5, and -5.0 fm) while reducing the triplet scattering length to maintain agreement with scattering data.

## Key numbers
- Hypertriton separation energy: NLO19 baseline gives 0.09–0.10 MeV; Fit A gives 0.28–0.32 MeV; Fit B gives 0.36–0.39 MeV; Fit C gives 0.44–0.47 MeV (depending on cutoff Λ = 500–650 MeV)
- STAR collaboration value for hypertriton: 0.41 ± 0.12 MeV (combined ³ΛH + ³Λ̄H̄)
- Benchmark value from Juric et al. (1973): 0.13 ± 0.05 MeV
- ⁴ΛHe(0⁺) separation energy: NLO19 gives 1.46–1.64 MeV; Fit C gives 1.92–2.24 MeV; experimental value: 2.39 ± 0.03 MeV
- ⁴ΛHe(1⁺) separation energy: NLO19 gives 0.92–1.24 MeV; Fit C gives 0.49–0.89 MeV; experimental value: 0.98 ± 0.03 MeV
- ⁴ΛHe spin splitting ΔE_Λ: NLO19 gives 0.30–0.61 MeV; Fit C gives 1.23–1.47 MeV; experimental value: 1.406 ± 0.002 ± 0.002 MeV
- Λp singlet scattering length a_s: NLO19 = -2.91 fm; Fit A = -4.00 fm; Fit B = -4.50 fm; Fit C = -5.00 fm; experimental constraint: -1.8 (+2.3, -4.2) fm
- Λp triplet scattering length a_t: NLO19 = -1.41 fm; Fit C = -1.09 fm; experimental constraint: -1.6 (+1.1, -0.8) fm
- ⁵ΛHe separation energy (used as SRG calibration): 3.10–3.16 MeV; experimental: 3.12 ± 0.02 MeV
- ⁷ΛLi separation energy: NLO19 gives 5.61–5.86 MeV; Fit C gives 6.31 ± 0.03 MeV; experimental: 5.58 ± 0.03 MeV (Davis 1992) or 5.85 ± 0.13 ± 0.10 MeV (Agnello 2009)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous chiral EFT YN potential development (Haidenbauer 2013, 2019) and few-body hypernuclear calculations using Faddeev and Yakubovsky methods (Nogga 2002). It directly addresses the STAR collaboration's 2019 measurement suggesting a larger hypertriton binding energy than the decades-old benchmark from Juric (1973). The study enables future discrimination between scenarios through improved hypertriton binding energy measurements (Achenbach 2018) and determination of the Λp spin-singlet scattering length via double-polarization experiments or Λp correlation functions from heavy-ion collisions (STAR, ALICE).
