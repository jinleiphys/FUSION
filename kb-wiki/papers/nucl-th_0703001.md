# Local versus nonlocal $αα$ interactions in $3α$ description of $^{12}$C

> Local $αα$ potentials fail to describe $^{12}$C as a $3α$ system. Nonlocal $αα$ potentials that renormalize the energy-dependent kernel of the resonating group method allow interpreting simultaneously the ground state and $0^+_2$ resonance of $^{12}$C as $3α$ states. A comparison with fully microscopic calculations provides a measure of the importance of three-cluster exchanges in those states.

## Key claim

Local alpha-alpha potentials, whether shallow or deep, fail to describe the ground state and the Hoyle resonance of 12C as a three-alpha system, but a nonlocal potential that renormalizes the energy-dependent resonating-group-method kernel can simultaneously reproduce both states, with the remaining 1.9 MeV discrepancy in the ground-state energy providing a measure of three-cluster exchange effects.

## Method

The authors start from the resonating-group-method (RGM) kernel for the alpha-alpha system, which is energy-dependent and contains Pauli-forbidden states. They construct an energy-independent nonlocal potential V^RGM by applying the transformation g = sqrt(N) chi to the relative motion function, where N is the norm kernel, and then solving for the renormalized potential. This V^RGM potential is expanded in harmonic-oscillator eigenfunctions of the norm kernel, excluding the Pauli-forbidden states, and is used in a macroscopic three-body calculation of 12C with the stochastic variational method.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 12C with the V^RGM nonlocal potential: -9.44 MeV (from the 3-alpha threshold), compared to the microscopic result of -11.37 MeV and the experimental value of -7.27 MeV.
- Energy of the 0+2 (Hoyle) resonance with V^RGM: 0.597 MeV (above the 3-alpha threshold), identical to the microscopic result of 0.597 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 0.38 MeV.
- Root-mean-square radius of the point-alpha distribution for the ground state with V^RGM: 1.62 fm, compared to the microscopic value of 1.64 fm.
- Ground-state energy with the energy-dependent V^epsilon K potential: -9.60 MeV, with an rms radius of 1.45 fm.
- Ground-state energy with the local ABd shallow potential: -1.52 MeV, with an rms radius of 2.34 fm.
- Ground-state energy with the local BFW deep potential (HO forbidden states): -20.62 MeV, with an rms radius of 1.29 fm.
- Ground-state energy with the local BFW deep potential (bound-state forbidden states): -0.66 MeV, with an rms radius of 2.31 fm.
- Preliminary ground-state energy for 9Be (alpha-alpha-n system) with V^RGM: -2.16 MeV, compared to the microscopic result of -2.61 MeV and the experimental value of -1.57 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to describe 12C as a three-alpha cluster system, which began with the microscopic RGM calculation by Kamimura (1981) and has been pursued with local potentials by various groups (Varga and Lovas, 1971; Smirnov, 1990; Tursunov, 2001; Tursunov, Baye, and Descouvemont, 2003). The authors show that the failure of local potentials, which produce either overbinding or underbinding depending on the treatment of forbidden states, is resolved by using the microscopically founded nonlocal V^RGM potential. This enables semi-microscopic calculations that are qualitatively close to fully microscopic results, with the remaining difference quantifying three-cluster exchange effects. The approach is being extended to 9Be and provides tractable wave functions for reaction and decay models.
