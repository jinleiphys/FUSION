# QCD sum rules for the neutron, $Σ$, and $Λ$ in neutron matter

> The nuclear density dependencies of the neutron and $Σ$ and $Λ$ hyperons are important inputs in the determination of the neutron star mass as the appearance of hyperons coming from strong attractions significantly changes the stiffness of the equation of state (EOS) at iso-spin asymmetric dense nuclear matter. In-medium spectral sum rules have been analyzed for the nucleon, $Σ$, and $Λ$ hyperon to investigate their properties up to slightly above the saturation nuclear matter density by using the linear density approximation for the condensates. The construction scheme of the interpolating fields without derivatives has been reviewed and used to construct a general interpolating field for each baryon with parameters specifying the strength of independent interpolating fields. Optimal choices for the interpolating fields were obtained by requiring the sum rules to be stable against variations of the parameters and the result to be consistent with known phenomenology. The optimized result shows that Ioffe's choice is not suitable for the $Λ$ hyperon sum rules. It is found that, for the $Λ$ hyperon interpolating field, the up and down quark combined into the scalar diquark structure $u^T C γ_5 d$ should be emphasized to ensure stable sum rules. The quasi-$Σ$ and -$Λ$ hyperon energies are always found to be higher than the quasineutron energy in the region $0.5 <ρ/ρ_0<1.5 $ where the linear density approximation in the sum-rule analysis is expected to be reliable.

## Key claim

The quasi-$\Sigma$ and -$\Lambda$ hyperon energies are always higher than the quasineutron energy in the density region $0.5 < \rho/\rho_0 < 1.5$, where the linear density approximation in the sum-rule analysis is expected to be reliable; however, this result depends on the optimal choice of interpolating fields, and Ioffe's choice is found to be unsuitable for the $\Lambda$ hyperon sum rules.

## Method

The authors performed in-medium QCD sum-rule analyses for the neutron, $\Sigma$, and $\Lambda$ baryons using the linear density approximation for condensates up to slightly above saturation nuclear matter density. They constructed general interpolating fields for each baryon with parameters specifying the strength of independent bases, then obtained optimal choices by requiring the sum rules to be stable against parameter variations and consistent with known phenomenology. The operator product expansion (OPE) was computed for the correlation functions, and Borel sum rules were applied to extract the quasibaryon self-energies and energies.

## Key numbers

- The quasibaryon energies were calculated in the density region $0.5 < \rho/\rho_0 < 1.5$, where $\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3} = (110\ \text{MeV})^3$ is the saturation nuclear matter density.
- The external three-momentum for the quasineutron was set at the Fermi momentum $|\vec{q}| = 270\ \text{MeV}$ at $\rho = \rho_0$.
- The external momentum for the quasihyperons was set to $|\vec{q}| = 0\ \text{MeV}$.
- The strange quark content parameter in the proton was taken as $y = 0.1$, constrained by chiral effective theory and lattice QCD studies to $y \leq 0.2$.
- The strange quark condensate in vacuum was taken as $\langle \bar{s}s \rangle_{\text{vac}} = (0.8)\langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\text{vac}}$.
- The twist-4 matrix element parameter for strange quarks was $A^s_2 = 0.050$.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum-rule studies of nucleons in nuclear matter (Drukarev 1988, Cohen 1991, Furnstahl 1992, Jin 1992-1993) and hyperons (Jin 1993-1994) by using generalized interpolating fields with updated condensate values from lattice QCD (Durr 2015, Yang 2015) and chiral effective theory (Alarcon 2012). The results are relevant for determining the equation of state of neutron star matter, where the appearance of hyperons can soften the equation of state and reduce the maximum neutron star mass below $2M_\odot$ (Glendenning 1984, Knorren 1995). The findings enable more reliable predictions for hyperon properties in dense matter and can be used as inputs for neutron star structure calculations.
