# Electromagnetic form factors of the Delta with D-waves

> The electromagnetic form factors of the Delta baryon are evaluated within the framework of a covariant spectator quark model, where S and D-states are included in the $Δ$ wave function. We predict all the four Delta multipole form factors: the electric charge GE0, the magnetic dipole GM1, the electric quadrupole GE2 and the magnetic octupole GM3. We compare our predictions with other theoretical calculations. Our results are compatible with the available experimental data and recent lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, extended to include D-wave components in the Delta wave function, predicts nonzero values for the electric quadrupole (G_E2) and magnetic octupole (G_M3) form factors, which are absent in a pure S-wave description; the D-state contributions to G_E2 and G_M3 are proportional to the admixture coefficients a and b and vanish at Q^2=0 due to orthogonality between S and D-states, but the form factors remain finite because the overlap integrals scale as tau ~ Q^2.

## Method

The Delta baryon is described as a system of three constituent quarks within the covariant spectator formalism, where one quark interacts with the electromagnetic field while the quark-pair (diquark) acts as an on-shell spectator. The Delta wave function is a mixture of an S-state and two D-states (D3 with quark core spin 3/2 and D1 with quark core spin 1/2), with admixture coefficients a and b determined by fitting to gamma N to Delta transition data. The electromagnetic current is computed in impulse approximation using dressed quark form factors parameterized with vector meson dominance, and the four multipole form factors (G_E0, G_M1, G_E2, G_M3) are derived from the current matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For the Spectator 1 model: D3-state admixture of 0.88% and D1-state admixture of 4.36% in the Delta wave function.
- For the Spectator 2 model: admixture of 0.72% for both D3 and D1 states.
- The Delta mean lifetime is 5.6 x 10^(-24) s.
- The quark current parameters include lambda = 1.22, c_+ = 4.16, c_- = 1.16, d_+ = d_- = -0.686, kappa_+ = 1.639, and kappa_- = 1.823, with m_v = m_rho (or m_omega) and M_h = 2 M_N.
- The charge radius predictions for Delta++ range from 0.35 fm^2 (Spectator 1) to values between 0.51 and 0.90 fm^2 from other models (Skyrme, QSM, CQM, GP/Large-N_c).
- For Delta+, the charge radius is 0.29 fm^2 (Spectator 1) compared to values from 0.328 +/- 0.016 fm^2 (chiPT) to 0.815 fm^2 (chiQSM SU(3)).

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to the nucleon and the gamma N to Delta transition (Ramalho, Pena, Gross, 2008-2010) by including D-wave components in the Delta wave function, following the formalism developed for the transition form factors. The predictions are compared with lattice QCD data from the MIT-Nicosia group (Alexandrou, 2007-2009) and the Adelaide group (Boinepalli, 2009), as well as with results from the chiral Quark-Soliton model (Ledwig, 2008) and chiral perturbation theory (Geng, 2009). The results enable future analysis of Delta deformation from both classical Breit-frame and transverse-density perspectives, and provide a framework to extrapolate lattice QCD results to the physical pion mass.
