# Update to the Bodek-Yang Unified Model for Electron- and Neutrino- Nucleon Scattering Cross Sections

> We construct a model for inelastic neutrino- and electron-nucleon scattering cross sections using effective leading order parton distribution functions with a new scaling variable $ξ_w$. Non-perturbative effects are well described using the $ξ_w$ scaling variable, in combination with multiplicative $K$ factors at low $Q^2$. Our model describes all inelastic charged lepton-nucleon scattering (including resonance) data (HERA/NMC/BCDMS/SLAC/JLab) ranging from very high $Q^2$ to very low $Q^2$ and down to the photo-production region. The model describes existing inelastic neutrino-nucleon scattering measurements, and is currently used in analyses of neutrino oscillation experiments in the few GeV region.

# Update to the Bodek-Yang Unified Model for Electron- and Neutrino-Nucleon Scattering Cross Sections

## Key claim
The authors construct a unified model for inelastic neutrino- and electron-nucleon scattering cross sections using effective leading order parton distribution functions with a new scaling variable ξ_w, claiming it describes all inelastic charged lepton-nucleon scattering data from very high Q² down to the photoproduction region. However, the model's parameters are fit exclusively to charged lepton data (no neutrino data are included in the fit), and the neutrino predictions are derived by applying separate vector and axial K factors with an additional correction H(x,Q²) to account for different scaling violations in F₂ and xF₃.

## Method
The authors replace the standard Bjorken scaling variable x with a new variable ξ_w that incorporates target mass corrections, initial quark transverse momentum, final state quark masses, and higher order QCD effects via fitted parameters A and B. They multiply GRV98 leading order parton distribution functions by vector K factors (separate forms for valence and sea quarks) to describe low Q² data down to the photoproduction limit, freeze PDF evolution below Q²=0.80 GeV², and fit the parameters to charged lepton scattering data on hydrogen and deuterium targets from SLAC, BCDMS, NMC, and H1. For neutrino scattering, they introduce separate axial K factors and a correction H(x,Q²) to xF₃, then apply nuclear corrections using iron-to-deuterium structure function ratios.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous Bodek-Yang model by introducing separate K factors for up and down valence and sea quarks, and by including photoproduction and resonance data in the fit. The model is currently used in analyses of neutrino oscillation experiments in the few GeV region, and its predictions agree with CCFR, CDHSW, and CHORUS neutrino and antineutrino differential cross section measurements. The approach contrasts with standard global PDF fits (e.g., GRV98) that are performed at high Q² where non-perturbative effects are negligible, and enables modeling of neutrino-nucleon scattering at low energies relevant for oscillation experiments.
