# Subleading contributions to $N$-boson systems inside the universal window

> We study bosonic systems in the regime in which the two-body system has a shallow bound state or, equivalently, a large value of the two-body scattering length. Using the effective field theory framework as a guide, we construct a series of potential terms which have decreasing importance in the description of the binding energy of the systems. The leading order potential terms consist of a two-body term, usually attractive, plus a three-body term, usually repulsive; this last term is required to prevent the collapse of systems with more than two particles. At this order, the parametrization of the two-body potential is done to obtain a correct description of the scattering length, which governs the dynamics in this regime, whereas the three-body term fixes a three-body datum. We investigate the role of the cut-off in the leading order description and we extend the exploration beyond the leading order by including the next-to-leading order terms in both, the two- and three-body potentials. We use the requirement of the stability of the N-body system, whose energy is variationally estimated, to introduce the three-body forces. The potential parametrization, as a function of the cut-off, is fixed to describe the energy of 4 He clusters up to seven particles within the expected accuracy. Finally, we also explore the possibility to describe at the same time the atom-dimer scattering length.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a next-to-leading order (NLO) effective field theory inspired potential, including both two-body and three-body forces, can describe the binding energies of bosonic clusters up to seven particles within the expected NLO accuracy of about 0.5%, but only for specific tuned values of the potential ranges; the LO three-body force alone is insufficient to stabilize the many-body system at NLO, and a subleading three-body force is required.

## Method

The authors construct a series of Gaussian potential terms ordered according to the power counting of pionless effective field theory, with the LO consisting of a two-body Gaussian plus a repulsive three-body Gaussian, and the NLO adding a derivative term to the two-body potential and a second term to the three-body potential. They fix the low-energy constants by reproducing the two-body scattering length and effective range of the LM2M2 helium potential, and the three-body ground-state energy, then variationally compute the ground-state energies of N=4,5,6,7 boson clusters using the hyperspherical harmonics method. The cutoff dependence is studied by varying the Gaussian range r0, and the atom-dimer scattering length is also explored.

## Key numbers

* The two-body scattering length of the reference LM2M2 potential is \(\bar{a}=189.415\,a_0\), the effective range is \(\bar{r}_e=13.845\,a_0\), and the small expansion parameter is \(\varepsilon=\bar{r}_e/\bar{a}\approx 7\%\).
* The LO Gaussian strength in the scaling limit (\(r_0/\bar{a}\rightarrow 0\)) converges to \(C_0=-2.68402(3)\).
* At the special LO point where all two-body LM2M2 data are reproduced, the Gaussian parameters are \(V_0=-1.22717064\,\text{K}\) and \(r_0=10.03018708\,a_0\).
* The NLO description achieves binding energies for N=4,5,6,7 inside a \(5\permil\) (0.5%) strip at two special points: \(r_0/\bar{a}\approx 0.042\) with \(\rho_0/r_0\approx 7/8\), and \(r_0/\bar{a}\approx 0.063\) with \(\rho_0/r_0\approx 7/12\).

## Context

This work extends the LO analysis of bosonic clusters in the universal window performed by Kievsky et al. (2017, 2018, 2020) and the perturbative NLO study by Bazak et al. (2019) which suggested a subleading four-body force is needed for N>4. The present paper treats the NLO terms non-perturbatively and shows that a NLO three-body force can stabilize systems up to N=7 within the expected accuracy, using the LM2M2 helium potential (Aziz, 1991) as the reference. This enables future studies of the many-body limit and the atom-dimer scattering length within a consistent EFT framework.
