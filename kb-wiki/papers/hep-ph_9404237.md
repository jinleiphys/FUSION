# Multiplicities for LHC Nuclear Collisions Using HERA Structure Functions

> We compute in QCD perturbation theory the transverse energy carried by gluons, quarks and antiquarks with $p_T\ge p_0\approx 2$ GeV in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5500$ $A$GeV by using structure functions compatible with the small-$x$ increase observed at HERA. This gives a perturbative estimate for the energy and entropy density of the bulk system at times $τ\sim 0.1$ fm. The predicted initial gluon entropy density gives a lower limit of about 2200...3400 for the final charged multiplicity. Sources of further entropy increase are discussed.

## Key claim

Using HERA-inspired gluon distribution functions that rise at small x, the authors compute a perturbative QCD estimate for the final charged multiplicity in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, obtaining a lower limit of 2200-3400 for the charged multiplicity per unit rapidity, but note that this estimate assumes a K factor of 2, neglects shadowing, and considers only the gluonic component under the assumption of immediate thermalization.

## Method

The authors compute the lowest-order perturbative QCD minijet cross section and transverse energy for partons with pT >= p0 = 2 GeV in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV, using the MRS D0', H, and D-' parton distribution functions that incorporate the small-x rise seen at HERA. They then scale these pp results to central Pb+Pb collisions using the nuclear overlap function T_PbPb(0) = 32/mb, assume the produced gluons thermalize at proper time tau_i = 1/p0 = 0.1 fm, and estimate the final charged multiplicity from the initial gluon entropy density under the assumption of adiabatic expansion.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5500 AGeV, the predicted lower limit for charged multiplicity per unit rapidity is dN_ch/dy > 2180 for the MRS H set (xg(x) ~ x^{-0.3}) and > 3360 for the MRS D-' set (xg(x) ~ x^{-0.5}), compared to > 914 for the older Duke-Owens set 1 (xg(x) ~ constant)
- The transverse energy in one unit of rapidity near y=0 for central Pb+Pb collisions is 8640 GeV for the H set and 15300 GeV for the D-' set, compared to 2700 GeV for DO1
- The average energy per gluon in the central region is 3.2 GeV for the H set and 2.9 GeV for the D-' set, compared to 4.1 GeV for DO1
- The initial formation time is tau_i = 0.1 fm, corresponding to 1/p0 with p0 = 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier minijet calculations by Eskola, Kajantie, and Lindfors (1989) by incorporating the new HERA data on the proton structure function F2 at small x from the H1 and ZEUS collaborations (1993), which show a significant rise in the gluon distribution at small x. The results provide updated predictions for the ALICE experiment planning at the LHC, where previous theoretical estimates ranged from 1500-8000 for charged multiplicity, and suggest that the higher multiplicities from HERA-based distributions increase the likelihood of observing quark-gluon plasma phenomena at LHC energies.
