# QED-SCF, MCSCF and Coupled-cluster Methods in Quantum Chemistry

> We investigate the method to combine the techniques of quantum chemisty with QED. In our theory, we treat the N-electron system and the Dirac sea on an equal footing; we regard both of them as the dynamical degrees of freedom of a many-body system. After the introduction of our QED-SCF method, the QED-SCF solutions are classified into several classes on the basis of group-theoretical operations such as time reversal, parity and O(3) rotational symmetry. The natural orbitals of general QED-SCF solutions are determined by diagonalizing the first order density matrix. Thus, we obtain the possibility to treat the system under strong QED effect by the methods of quantum chemistry, such as QED-MCSCF and QED-coupled-cluster approaches.

## Key claim

The authors propose a formal framework to combine quantum electrodynamics (QED) with quantum chemistry many-body techniques by treating the N-electron system and the Dirac sea as dynamical degrees of freedom on an equal footing, but the actual implementation remains a formal proposal with no numerical demonstration; the paper explicitly states that detailed formulations of the multi-reference coupled-cluster approach "will be discussed elsewhere."

## Method

The authors construct a QED Hamiltonian written in terms of creation and annihilation operators for electrons and positrons, using a relativistic Slater determinant in the Thouless form to derive time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations, HF conditions, and random phase approximation (RPA) equations within QED. They classify the 4x4 density matrix of QED-HF solutions into symmetry classes using group-theoretical operations (time reversal, parity, O(3) rotational symmetry) and expand the density matrix in the 16-dimensional basis of gamma matrices. They then outline how to obtain natural orbitals by diagonalizing the first-order density matrix and use these to select active spaces for QED-MCSCF and QED-coupled-cluster approaches.

## Key numbers

- The density matrix is a 4x4 matrix expanded into 16-dimensional complete set of gamma matrices (scalar, vector, 2-rank antisymmetric tensor, axial vector, pseudoscalar).
- The QED Hamiltonian includes 16 distinct types of interaction terms in the interaction part (H_I) involving combinations of electron and positron creation/annihilation operators.
- The excitation operator S_2 in the QED-CC approximation includes 10 distinct operator types (e.g., a†_i a_j a†_k a_l, a†_i a_j a†_k b†_l, etc.).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QED-SCF theory (Ohsaku, 2000) and builds on the nonrelativistic general spin orbital (GSO) HF and DFT methods developed by Yamanaka et al. (2000) and Yamaki et al. (2000). The paper contrasts its approach with the Dirac-Coulomb-Breit no-sea scheme (Sapirstein, 1998), which neglects the Dirac sea and works well for neutral heavy elements, and with standard QED perturbation theory (Mohr et al., 1998), which treats only one-particle QED corrections for highly ionized systems. The proposed method aims to fill the gap between these regimes for systems where both many-body effects and QED effects are significant, such as heavy elements at intermediate ionicity or uranium atom collisions.
