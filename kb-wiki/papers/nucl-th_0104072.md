# Study of $Λ$ hypernuclei in the quark mean field model

> We extend the quark mean field model to the study of $Λ$ hypernuclei. Without adjusting parameters, the properties of $Λ$ hypernuclei can be described reasonably well. The small spin-orbit splittings for $Λ$ in hypernuclei are achieved, while the $Λ$ single particle energies in the present model are slightly underestimated as compared with the experimental values. About 3% deviation from the quark model prediction for the $ω-Λ$ couplings is required in order to reproduce the experimental single particle energies.

# Study of Λ hypernuclei in the quark mean field model

## Key claim
The quark mean field (QMF) model, extended to flavor SU(3) without any adjustable parameters, describes Λ hypernuclei reasonably well but slightly underestimates Λ single particle energies compared to experiment; a 3% reduction in the ω–Λ coupling from the quark model value of Rω = 2/3 is required to reproduce the experimental single particle energies.

## Method
The authors describe nucleons and Λ hyperons as composites of three constituent quarks confined by either a scalar potential (χc = ½kr²) or a scalar-vector potential (χc = ½kr²(1+γ⁰)/2) with k = 700 MeV/fm², solving Dirac equations for the quarks in the presence of σ, ω, and ρ meson mean fields. The σ meson couples only to u and d quarks, reducing the effective masses of nucleons and Λ hyperons self-consistently; the ω meson couples to the Λ with gωΛ = 2gωq (Rω = 2/3) based on the quark model. The coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations for the hypernucleus are solved self-consistently, and spin-orbit splittings are added perturbatively via Thomas precession.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses: mq = 313 MeV, ms = 490 MeV; confinement strength k = 700 MeV/fm².
- Free-space masses: MN = 939 MeV, MΛ = 1116 MeV.
- Spin-orbit splitting for the 1f states (1f5/2 – 1f7/2) in ⁹¹ΛZr: 0.03 MeV with perturbative correction, compared to 1.5 MeV without it; experimental splitting in ⁸⁹ΛY is 1.6 ± 0.15 MeV.
- Spin-orbit splittings for the 1d states (1d3/2 – 1d5/2) in ⁴¹ΛCa: 0.02 MeV with correction (≈1.4 MeV without); in ²⁰⁹ΛPb: 0.01 MeV (≈0.5 MeV without).
- Scalar and vector potentials for the 1s1/2 Λ state in ⁴¹ΛCa and ²⁰⁹ΛPb: their difference at the center is about 20–25 MeV.
- With a scaled coupling 0.97 × gωΛ, the 1s1/2 single particle energy in ²⁰⁹ΛPb becomes −27.2 MeV (QMFI) and −26.0 MeV (QMFII).
- A 3% reduction in Rω provides about 6 MeV less repulsion, yielding a Λ potential near the empirical value UΛ ≈ 25–30 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the quark mean field model, previously applied to nuclear matter and finite nuclei (Shen and Toki, earlier work), to Λ hypernuclei, contrasting with the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Guichon et al.) which uses the MIT bag model and gives overbinding. The QMF model yields smaller Rσ values than the constant 2/3 used in relativistic mean field (RMF) models (e.g., Brockmann and Weise; Mares et al.), leading to underbinding rather than overbinding. The results enable future studies of medium modifications to confinement and spin correlations, and provide a parameter-free baseline for comparing with experimental Λ hypernuclear spectra.
