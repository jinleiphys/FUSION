# Neutron electric dipole moment from lattice QCD

> We carry out a feasibility study for the lattice QCD calculation of the neutron electric dipole moment (NEDM) in the presence of the $θ$ term. We develop the strategy to obtain the nucleon EDM from the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor $F_3$ at small $θ$, in which NEDM is given by $\lim_{q^2\to 0}θF_3(q^2)/(2m_N)$ where $q$ is the momentum transfer and $m_N$ is the nucleon mass. We first derive a formula which relates $F_3$, a matrix element of the electromagnetic current between nucleon states, with vacuum expectation values of nucleons and/or the current. In the expansion of $θ$, the parity-odd part of the nucleon-current-nucleon three-point function contains contributions not only from the parity-odd form factors but also from the parity-even form factors multiplied by the parity-odd part of the nucleon two-point function, and therefore the latter contribution must be subtracted to extract $F_3$. We then perform an explicit lattice calculation employing the domain-wall quark action with the RG improved gauge action in quenched QCD at $a^{-1}\simeq 2$ GeV on a $16^3\times 32\times 16$ lattice. At the quark mass $m_f a =0.03$, corresponding to $m_π/m_ρ\simeq 0.63$, we accumulate 730 configurations, which allow us to extract the parity-odd part in both two- and three-point functions. Employing two different Dirac $γ$ matrix projections, we show that a consistent value for $F_3$ cannot be obtained without the subtraction described above. We obtain $F_3(q^2\simeq 0.58 \textrm{GeV}^2)/(2m_N) =$ $-$0.024(5) $e\cdot$fm for the neutron and $F_3(q^2\simeq 0.58 \textrm{GeV}^2)/(2m_N) =$ 0.021(6) $e\cdot$fm for the proton.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a method to extract the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor \(F_3\) from lattice QCD at first order in the \(\theta\) parameter, but only after subtracting a previously overlooked contribution from parity-even form factors multiplied by the parity-odd part of the nucleon two-point function; without this subtraction, consistent values for \(F_3\) cannot be obtained.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions in quenched lattice QCD using domain-wall fermions and an RG-improved gauge action on a \(16^3 \times 32 \times 16\) lattice at \(a^{-1} \simeq 2\) GeV. They expand the correlation functions to first order in \(\theta\) by inserting the topological charge \(Q\) as an operator, and extract \(F_3\) from the parity-odd part of the three-point function after subtracting the contribution from parity-even form factors multiplied by the parity-odd part of the two-point function (parameterized by \(f_N^1\)). Two different Dirac \(\gamma\)-matrix projections are used to check consistency.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a^{-1} \simeq 2\) GeV (from \(\rho\) meson mass, \(a^{-1} = 1.875(56)\) GeV).
* Quark mass \(m_f a = 0.03\), corresponding to \(m_\pi/m_\rho \simeq 0.63\) (specifically \(m_{PS}/m_V = 0.629(8)\)).
* 730 gauge configurations accumulated.
* For the neutron: \(F_3(q^2 \simeq 0.58\ \text{GeV}^2)/(2m_N) = -0.024(5)\ e\cdot\text{fm}\).
* For the proton: \(F_3(q^2 \simeq 0.58\ \text{GeV}^2)/(2m_N) = 0.021(6)\ e\cdot\text{fm}\).
* Parity-odd parameter from two-point function: \(f_N^1 = -0.247(17)\) for \(|\vec{p}| = 0\) and \(-0.243(20)\) for \(|\vec{p}| = 1\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice attempt by Aoki (1990) which was later found to be a lattice artifact due to explicit chiral symmetry breaking, and addresses the omission in the recent calculation by Blum (2004) where the subtraction of parity-even form factor contributions was not performed. The method enables future lattice QCD calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment with controlled systematics, and is directly applicable to full QCD simulations with dynamical fermions and smaller quark masses.
