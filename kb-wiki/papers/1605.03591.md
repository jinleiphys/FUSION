# From Neutron Star Observables to the Equation of State. I. An Optimal Parametrization

> The increasing number and precision of measurements of neutron star masses, radii, and, in the near future, moments of inertia offer the possibility of precisely determining the neutron star equation of state. One way to facilitate the mapping of observables to the equation of state is through a parametrization of the latter. We present here a generic method for optimizing the parametrization of any physically allowed EoS. We use mock equations of state that incorporate physically diverse and extreme behavior to test how well our parametrization reproduces the global properties of the stars, by minimizing the errors in the observables mass, radius, and the moment of inertia. We find that using piecewise polytropes and sampling the EoS with five fiducial densities between ~1-8 times the nuclear saturation density results in optimal errors for the smallest number of parameters. Specifically, it recreates the radii of the assumed EoS to within less than 0.5 km for the extreme mock equations of state and to within less than 0.12 km for 95% of a sample of 42 proposed, physically-motivated equations of state. Such a parametrization is also able to reproduce the maximum mass to within 0.04 M_sun and the moment of inertia of a 1.338 M_sun neutron star to within less than 10% for 95% of the proposed sample of equations of state.

# From Neutron Star Observables to the Equation of State. I. An Optimal Parametrization

## Key claim
The authors claim that a piecewise-polytropic parametrization of the neutron star equation of state using five fiducial densities evenly spaced in log between 1 and 7.4 times nuclear saturation density is optimal for reproducing neutron star observables, but this conclusion is conditional on the parametrization starting at exactly nuclear saturation density and using logarithmic spacing; other choices (linear spacing or starting at 0.5 times saturation density) produce larger errors.

## Method
The authors generated 53,343 mock equations of state composed of 15 polytropic segments with extreme but physically allowed polytropic indices (Gamma = 1 or the minimum of 5 and the causal limit), starting at two different pressures at nuclear saturation density corresponding to the SLy and PS equations of state. They also generated a separate sample of ~175,000 mock equations of state that included a single first-order phase transition at arbitrary density and duration. For each mock equation of state, they solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass, radius, and moment of inertia, then compared these to the same quantities computed from piecewise-polytropic parametrizations using 3 to 12 fiducial densities above nuclear saturation density. They optimized the number of segments by requiring residuals in radius, maximum mass, and moment of inertia to fall below thresholds comparable to observational uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Five fiducial densities at (1.0, 1.4, 2.2, 3.3, 4.9, 7.4) times nuclear saturation density (rho_sat ~ 2.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3) are recommended as optimal.
- For the 53,343 extreme mock equations of state without phase transitions, using 5 fiducial densities gives radius residuals of 0.50, 0.44, and 0.48 km at masses of 1.4, 1.6, and 1.8 solar masses respectively for 95% of cases.
- For the ~175,000 mock equations of state with phase transitions, using 5 fiducial densities gives radius residuals of 0.48, 0.43, and 0.46 km at the same three masses for 95% of cases.
- For a sample of 42 physically-motivated proposed equations of state, using 5 fiducial densities reproduces radii to within 0.10, 0.12, and 0.09 km at 1.4, 1.6, and 1.8 solar masses for 95% of cases.
- Maximum mass is reproduced to within 0.04 solar masses for 95% of the 42 proposed equations of state.
- Moment of inertia for a 1.338 solar mass neutron star (Pulsar A in PSR J0737-3039) is reproduced to within 0.02 x 10^45 g cm^2 for 95% of the 42 proposed equations of state.

## Context
This work extends earlier parametrization studies such as Read (2009), Ozel (2009), Hebeler (2010), and Steiner (2016) by systematically optimizing the number of piecewise-polytropic segments and the density spacing, using a much larger and more extreme set of mock equations of state than previous work. The authors specifically note that earlier studies used existing theoretical equations of state as benchmarks and sought only to represent that subset, whereas this work tests against physically extreme behavior including first-order phase transitions. The optimized 5-segment parametrization is designed to enable model-independent inference of the equation of state from current and upcoming neutron star mass, radius, and moment of inertia measurements, particularly the anticipated 10% accuracy measurement of the moment of inertia of Pulsar A in the double pulsar system PSR J0737-3039 (Lyne 2004, Kramer 2009).
