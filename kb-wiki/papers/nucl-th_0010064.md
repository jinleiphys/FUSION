# J/psi absorption by pi and rho mesons in a meson exchange model with anomalous parity interactions

> We reanalyze the dissociation process of the $J/ψ$ by $π$ and $ρ$ mesons into $D + \bar{D}$, $D^* + \bar{D}$, $D + \bar{D}^*$, and $D^* + \bar{D}^*$ within a meson exchange model. In addition to the dissociation mechanisms considered in the literature, we consider anomalous parity interactions, whose couplings are constrained by heavy quark spin symmetry and phenomenology. This opens new dissociation channels and adds new diagrams in the previously considered processes. Compared to the previous results, we find that these new additions have only a minor effect on the $ρ+ J/ψ$ total inelastic cross section, but reduce the one for $π+ J/ψ$ by about 50 % near the threshold.

## Key claim

The inclusion of anomalous parity interactions (gauged Wess-Zumino terms) in the meson exchange model for J/ψ absorption opens new dissociation channels and adds new diagrams to previously considered processes; these additions have only a minor effect on the ρ + J/ψ total inelastic cross section, but reduce the one for π + J/ψ by about 50% near the threshold.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian for π, ρ, D, and D* mesons using a "bottom-up" approach starting from SU(4) symmetric chiral Lagrangian with vector mesons introduced via the massive Yang-Mills approach, then added anomalous parity terms from the gauged Wess-Zumino action. Coupling constants were determined from experimental data (D* → Dπ decay, vector meson dominance for radiative decays), heavy quark spin symmetry constraints, and SU(4) symmetry relations where direct data were unavailable. Cross sections for J/ψ absorption by π and ρ mesons into D + D̄, D* + D̄, D + D̄*, and D* + D̄* were calculated at tree level, both with and without monopole form factors.

## Key numbers

- g_{D* D π} = 8.8 (from QCD sum rule, constrained by experimental upper bound of 0.131 MeV for D* → Dπ decay width)
- g_{ψ DD} = 7.71, g_{DD ρ} = 2.52 (from vector meson dominance applied to γ D+ D- and γ D0 D̄0 couplings)
- g_{D* D* π} ≈ 9.08 GeV^{-1} (from heavy quark spin symmetry, using average D/D* mass)
- g_{ψ D* D} = 7.40 to 8.64 GeV^{-1}, g_{D* D ρ} = 2.82 GeV^{-1} (from vector meson dominance applied to D* → Dγ radiative decays, using relativistic potential model values g_V^± ≈ 1.12 GeV^{-1}, g_V^0 ≈ 0.96 GeV^{-1})
- g_{ψ DD π} ≈ 16.0 GeV^{-3} (from SU(4) relation with F_D ≈ 2.3 F_π)
- π + J/ψ → D + D̄ cross section is less than 0.1 mb over the whole energy region
- π + J/ψ total absorption cross section is less than 10 mb for √s ≤ 4 GeV (E_kin ≤ 0.8 GeV)
- Without form factors, π + J/ψ → D* + D̄* dominates at high energies; with form factors (Λ = 1.5 GeV), cross sections are reduced

## Context

This work extends the meson exchange model calculations of J/ψ absorption by π and ρ mesons previously performed by Matinyan and Muller (1998), Haglin (2000), and Lin and Ko (2000), which used only the minimal SU(4) Yang-Mills Lagrangian (normal parity interactions). The authors note that previous studies did not include anomalous parity interactions from the gauged Wess-Zumino action, which are required by heavy quark spin symmetry. The new anomalous terms open channels (π + J/ψ → D + D̄, π + J/ψ → D* + D̄*, ρ + J/ψ → D* + D̄) not previously considered in the effective Lagrangian approach, though some were studied in quark exchange models by Martins, Blaschke, and Quack (1995) and Wong, Swanson, and Barnes (2000). The results enable more reliable estimates of J/ψ absorption cross sections needed for interpreting J/ψ suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.
