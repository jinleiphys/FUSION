# High-Temperature Limit of Landau-Gauge Yang-Mills Theory

> The infrared properties of the high-temperature limit of Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory are investigated. In a first step the high-temperature limit of the Dyson-Schwinger equations is taken. The resulting equations are identical to the Dyson-Schwinger equations of the dimensionally reduced theory, a three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory coupled to an effective adjoint Higgs field. These equations are solved analytically in the infrared and ultraviolet, and numerically for all Euclidean momenta. We find infrared enhancement for the Faddeev-Popov ghosts, infrared suppression for transverse gluons and a mass for the Higgs. These results imply long-range interactions and over-screening in the chromomagnetic sector of high temperature Yang-Mills theory while in the chromoelectric sector only screening is observed.

## Key claim

The high-temperature limit of Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory is equivalent to a three-dimensional Yang-Mills theory coupled to an adjoint Higgs field, and the infrared behavior of this dimensionally reduced theory shows that chromomagnetic gluons remain confined even at infinitely high temperatures, while the Higgs (chromoelectric sector) acquires a mass and decouples in the infrared.

## Method

The authors took the infinite-temperature limit of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory using the Matsubara formalism, which yields a dimensionally reduced three-dimensional theory with an effective adjoint Higgs field. They solved the truncated system of DSEs for the ghost, gluon, and Higgs dressing functions analytically in the infrared and ultraviolet regimes, and numerically for all Euclidean momenta, using a bare ghost-gluon vertex and adjusting tadpole terms to cancel spurious divergences from gauge invariance violations.

## Key numbers

- Ghost infrared exponent \(g = 1/2\) and gluon infrared exponent \(t = -3/2\) for the solution independent of the projection parameter \(\zeta\).
- For transverse projection (\(\zeta = 1\)), the alternative solution gives ghost exponent \(g \approx 0.3976\) and gluon exponent \(t \approx -1.2952\).
- The allowed range for the ghost exponent \(g\) in three dimensions is \(1/4 < g \le 3/4\), with the Fourier transform condition restricting to \(g \le 1/2\).
- The allowed range for the projection parameter \(\zeta\) is \(1/4 \le \zeta < 3\).
- In the ghost-loop-only truncation at \(\zeta = 3\), the gluon propagator exhibits a maximum located at momentum \(k/g_3^2 \approx 0.25\).
- All dressing functions approach tree-level behavior in the ultraviolet as \(1 + c_i g_3^2 / \sqrt{q^2}\), with positive constants \(c_i\).

## Context

This work extends vacuum Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations for Yang-Mills theory (vonSmekal, 1997) and full QCD (Fischer, 2003) to the high-temperature limit, and builds on an earlier exploratory study (Maas, 2002). The results confirm the Zwanziger-Gribov confinement scenario (Zwanziger, 2003) and the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion (Kugo, 1995) at high temperature, showing that chromomagnetic gluons remain confined while chromoelectric gluons screen. The findings enable further studies of finite-temperature corrections and comparisons with lattice calculations (Cucchieri, 2001; Kajantie, 1995).
