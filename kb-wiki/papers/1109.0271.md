# Non-perturbative Jet Quenching from Geometric Data

> This contribution discusses the geometric tomography by highly energetic jets penetrating the hot QCD matter in heavy ion collisions from RHIC to LHC energies. In particular the geometric data on the azimuthal anisotropy of high $p_t$ hadrons discriminate different models and strongly hint at energy loss mechanism beyond those based on perturbation theory. Taking together the RHIC and LHC data, the comparison with models is in favor of the model with strong enhancement of jet quenching in near-$T_c$ matter.

## Key claim

Geometric data on the azimuthal anisotropy of high-\(p_t\) hadrons at both RHIC and LHC energies strongly favor jet quenching models that incorporate non-perturbative mechanisms, specifically a model with strong enhancement of jet quenching in near-\(T_c\) matter, over models based purely on perturbative energy loss.

## Method

The author compares three classes of geometric jet quenching models, an \(L^2\) model (square path-length dependence from LPM effect, constant quenching strength), a near-\(T_c\) enhancement model (same path-length dependence but with a Gaussian enhancement of quenching strength near the critical temperature), and an \(L^3\) model (cubic path-length dependence, constant quenching strength), by fixing each model's parameters to central collision data at RHIC 200 GeV and then comparing their predictions for the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and the hard azimuthal anisotropy \(V_2^{hard}\) as functions of centrality (\(N_{part}\)) against PHENIX data and preliminary LHC data from ATLAS and ALICE at Quark Matter 2011.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC 200 GeV, the near-\(T_c\) enhancement model uses a Gaussian enhancement factor \(\xi = 6\) with density scale \(s_c\) and width \(s_w\) to describe the strong jet quenching component near \(T_c\).
- The \(L^2\) model under-predicts the hard azimuthal anisotropy \(V_2^{hard}\) at RHIC, while both the near-\(T_c\) enhancement model and the \(L^3\) model can account for the sizeable anisotropy observed in PHENIX data.
- At LHC, the \(L^2\) and \(L^3\) models both somewhat over-quench the jets (i.e., under-predict \(R_{AA}\)), while the near-\(T_c\) model gives a fairly good description of \(R_{AA}\); the \(L^3\) model overshoots the \(V_2^{hard}\) data, while the \(L^2\) and near-\(T_c\) models are in reasonable agreement with preliminary LHC data.

## Context

This work extends the geometric tomography approach to jet quenching previously applied at RHIC (Shuryak 2001, Adler 2006) and addresses the long-standing failure of perturbative models to simultaneously describe \(R_{AA}\) and \(V_2^{hard}\). It builds on the insight of Liao (2008) that including a non-perturbative jet quenching component enhanced in near-\(T_c\) matter, motivated by the magnetic scenario for strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (Liao 2006), can explain the geometric data. The comparison with LHC data further distinguishes the near-\(T_c\) enhancement model from alternative models with cubic path-length dependence (Marquet 2009, Jia 2010) motivated by AdS/CFT calculations, and the results favor the near-\(T_c\) enhancement scenario.
