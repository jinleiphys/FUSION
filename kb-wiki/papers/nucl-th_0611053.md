# Model Independent Considerations on Two-Photon Exchange in Electron Proton Scattering and in the Crossed Processes

> The presence of two photon exchange in $ep$ elastic scattering and in the crossed processes ($\bar p +p\leftrightarrow \ell^+ +\ell^-$, $\ell=e$ or $μ$) is discussed in terms of three complex amplitudes which are functions of two kinematical variables, the momentum transfer squared $q^2$ and the polarization of the virtual photon, $ε$. Model independent expressions of the differential cross section and of polarization observables are derived. Particular attention is devoted to the extraction of hadron electromagnetic form factors which becomes much more difficult in presence of two photon exchange, and to the relevant experimental observables which could give evidence for such mechanism.

# Model Independent Considerations on Two-Photon Exchange in Electron Proton Scattering and in the Crossed Processes

## Key claim
Two-photon exchange contributions in elastic electron-proton scattering and the crossed annihilation processes can be described model-independently using three complex amplitudes, but the extraction of nucleon electromagnetic form factors becomes much more difficult because it requires six additional functions of two kinematical variables instead of two real functions of a single variable. The paper shows that the two-photon contribution cancels in the sum of electron and positron differential cross sections, enabling a generalized Rosenbluth separation, but only if measurements with both lepton charges are available.

## Method
The author parametrizes the matrix element for elastic electron-nucleon scattering using three complex invariant amplitudes A1, A2, A3 that are functions of the momentum transfer squared Q2 and the polarization parameter epsilon (or equivalently s and Q2). Using C-invariance, crossing symmetry, and lepton helicity conservation, model-independent expressions are derived for differential cross sections and polarization observables in both the space-like (elastic scattering) and time-like (annihilation) regions. The formalism introduces generalized form factors that separate the one-photon-exchange Born terms from the two-photon-exchange interference contributions.

## Key numbers
- The two-photon exchange contribution is expected to become important at Q2 approximately 10 GeV2 in electron-nucleon scattering, based on the faster decrease of form factors for heavier targets (d, 3He, 4He) where effects may appear starting from Q2 = 1 GeV2.
- In the time-like region, the differential cross section for e+ + e- -> N + anti-N is given by dsigma_un/dOmega = (alpha2 beta)/(4 q2) D, where D contains terms of order alpha from two-photon exchange compared to the dominant Born contribution.
- The total cross section for the annihilation channel is independent of two-photon exchange terms: sigma_t(q2) = (4pi/3)(alpha2 beta/q2)[|GM(q2)|2 + (1/(2tau))|GE(q2)|2].
- The polarization Py of the outgoing antinucleon in the annihilation channel, which is T-odd, vanishes at theta = 90 degrees in the Born approximation but becomes non-zero of order alpha when two-photon exchange is present.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous analysis with Rekalo (2004) on general properties of C-invariance and crossing symmetry in electron-nucleon scattering, and builds on earlier estimates by Rekalo (1999) of two-photon exchange effects from deuteron elastic scattering data at JLab up to Q2 = 6 GeV2. The paper addresses the discrepancy between proton form factor ratios extracted via the Rosenbluth method (Arrington 1975) versus the polarization transfer method (Jones 2000), which motivated renewed interest in two-photon exchange mechanisms. The results enable experimental strategies for extracting nucleon form factors using either positron beams (potentially at HERA) or measurements of multiple polarization observables with electron beams alone.
