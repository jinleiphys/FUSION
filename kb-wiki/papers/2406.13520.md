# Energy dependence of particle production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}}$ = 7.7-200 GeV using a multiphase transport model

> In this study, we employ a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to understand the production of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, $\overline{p}$, $K^{0}_{s}$, $Λ$, $\barΛ$, and $ϕ$ in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$, $27$, $39$, $62.4$, and $200$ GeV. We have studied the energy dependence of various bulk properties of the system such as transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra, particle yields ($dN/dy$), mean transverse mass ($\langle m_T \rangle$), and anti-particle to particle ratios. Model calculations using both default and string melting versions of the AMPT with three distinct sets of initial conditions are compared to the data from the STAR experiment. In the case of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, and $\overline{p}$, we observe that the string melting version shows better agreement with data at higher energies, while the default version performs better at lower collision energies. However, for $K^{0}_{s}$, $Λ$, and $ϕ$, it is observed that the default version is able to describe the data better at all energies. In addition, we have used the blast-wave model to extract the kinetic freeze-out properties, like the kinetic freeze-out temperature and the radial flow velocity. We observe that these parameters are comparable with the data.

## Key claim

The AMPT model's ability to describe STAR data depends on both the collision energy and the particle species: the string melting version agrees better with data for pions, kaons, and protons at higher energies, while the default version performs better at lower energies; for strange hadrons (K0s, Lambda, phi), the default version describes the data better at all studied energies.

## Method

The authors used both the default and string melting versions of the AMPT (a multiphase transport) model with three distinct sets of input parameters for Lund string fragmentation and parton scattering cross sections. They simulated Au+Au collisions at five center-of-mass energies (7.7, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) and compared the resulting transverse momentum spectra, particle yields, mean transverse mass, and antiparticle-to-particle ratios to STAR experimental data. They also extracted kinetic freeze-out temperature and radial flow velocity by fitting the pT spectra with a blast-wave model.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Three sets of input parameters were used: Set 1 (a=0.55, b=0.15 GeV^-2, sigma=3 mb), Set 2 (a=2.2, b=0.15 GeV^-2, sigma=3 mb), Set 3 (a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV^-2, sigma=1.5 mb)
- Parton scattering cross section values: 3 mb for Sets 1 and 2, 1.5 mb for Set 3
- Debye screening mass values: 2.265 fm^-1 for Sets 1 and 2, 3.2 fm^-1 for Set 3
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.33 for all three sets
- The critical energy density for string melting is approximately 1 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT model studies by Ye (2017), PhysRevC.102.024902, and Tiwari (2020) by systematically testing three parameter sets against STAR Beam Energy Scan data across a wide energy range. The results help constrain AMPT model parameters for future studies of the QCD phase diagram and the search for a first-order phase transition between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma, as suggested by Van Hove (1982). The study enables better interpretation of experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
