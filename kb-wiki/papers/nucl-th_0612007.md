# A possible Omega-pi molecular state

> The structure of Omega-pi state with isospin I=1 and spin-parity J^p=3/2^- are dynamically studied in both the chiral SU(3) quark model and the extended chiral SU(3) quark model by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The model parameters are taken from our previous work, which gave a satisfactory description of the energies of the baryon ground states, the binding energy of the deuteron, the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts, and the hyperon-nucleon (YN) cross sections. The calculated results show that the Omega-pi state has an attractive interaction, and in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model such an attraction can make for an Omega-pi quasi-bound state with the binding energy of about several MeV.

# A possible Omega-pi molecular state

## Key claim

The Omega-pi system with isospin I=1 and spin-parity J^p = 3/2^- may form a weakly bound quasi-bound molecular state, but only in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model; in the original chiral SU(3) quark model the system remains unbound. The binding is conditional on the specific choice of model parameters and the inclusion of vector-meson exchanges.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for the Omega-pi two-cluster system using two variants of the chiral SU(3) quark model: the original model (model I) and the extended model (models II and III) which includes vector-meson exchanges. Model parameters were taken from previous work that reproduced baryon ground-state energies, the deuteron binding energy, nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, and hyperon-nucleon cross sections. The quark-antiquark annihilation interaction was neglected because it acts only at very short range and does not contribute significantly to a molecular state.

## Key numbers

- In the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with f_chv/g_chv = 0 (model II), the Omega-pi binding energy is 2 MeV.
- In the extended chiral SU(3) quark model with f_chv/g_chv = 2/3 (model III), the Omega-pi binding energy is 0.5 MeV.
- The harmonic-oscillator width parameter b_u is 0.5 fm for the chiral SU(3) quark model and 0.45 fm for the extended chiral SU(3) quark model.
- The up (down) quark mass is 313 MeV and the strange quark mass is 470 MeV in all models.
- The sigma meson mass is 595 MeV in model I, 535 MeV in model II, and 547 MeV in model III.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dynamical study of the N-phi system (Huang et al., 2006), which found similar weakly bound states in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model. The Omega-pi system is particularly interesting because both Omega and pi have long lifetimes (on the order of 10^-10 and 10^-8 seconds, respectively), there is no coupling channel below the Omega-pi threshold, and the Omega-pi^- state carries two negative electric charges, making it potentially easy to distinguish experimentally. The results are similar to those found for the N-phi system because all these systems consist of two color-singlet hadrons with no common flavor quarks and no one-gluon exchange between them, with attraction dominantly provided by sigma exchange. The authors call for experimental searches for this state in e+e- collisions or heavy ion collisions.
