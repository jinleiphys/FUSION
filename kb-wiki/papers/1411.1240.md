# Effects of $α$-cluster breaking on 3$α$ cluster structures in $^{12}$C

> To clarify the effects of $α$-cluster breaking on 3$α$ cluster structures in $^{12}$C, we investigate $^{12}$C using a hybrid model that combines the Brink-Bloch cluster model with the $p_{3/2}$ subshell closure wave function. We have found that $α$-cluster breaking caused by spin-orbit force significantly changes cluster structures of excited $0^{+}$ states through orthogonality to lower states. Spatially developed cluster components of the $0^{+}_{2}$ state are reduced. The $0^{+}_{3}$ state changes from a vibration mode in the bending motion of three $α$ clusters to a chain-like 3$α$ structure having an open triangle configuration. As a result of these structure changes of $0^{+}$ states, the band assignment for the $2^{+}_{2}$ state is changed by the $α$-cluster breaking effect. Namely, in model calculations without the $α$-cluster breaking effect, the $0^{+}_{2}$ state is assigned to be the band-head of the $2^{+}_{2}$ state. However, when we incorporate $α$-cluster breaking caused by the spin-orbit force, the $0^{+}_{3}$ state is regarded as the band-head of the $2^{+}_{2}$ state.

## Key claim

The authors show that α-cluster breaking caused by the spin-orbit force, incorporated by mixing a single \(p_{3/2}\) subshell closure configuration into the 3α Brink-Bloch cluster model space, qualitatively changes the cluster structures of excited \(0^{+}\) states in \(^{12}\)C, altering the band assignment of the \(2^{+}_{2}\) state. The abstract's claim that the \(0^{+}_{3}\) state changes from a bending vibration to a chain-like structure is conditional on the spin-orbit strength being near the realistic value of \(u_{\rm ls} = 1600\) MeV; at weaker or stronger strengths the structure reverts to the pure cluster or pure shell-model limits, respectively.

## Method

The authors superpose 252 Brink-Bloch 3α cluster wave functions (parameterized by inter-cluster distances \(d_1, d_2 = 1\)–6 fm and bending angle \(\theta = n\pi/12\) with \(n=1,\dots,12\)) with a single \(p_{3/2}\) subshell closure wave function, using parity and angular momentum projection. The Hamiltonian includes the Volkov No. 2 central force (Majorana parameter \(M=0.60\)) and the G3RS spin-orbit force; the spin-orbit strength \(u_{\rm ls}\) is varied from 0 to 3200 MeV to control the degree of α-cluster breaking. The \(p_{3/2}\) subshell closure wave function contributes only to \(J^{\pi}=0^{+}\) states, so cluster breaking effects on \(2^{+}\) states enter only indirectly through the orthogonality of the \(0^{+}\) band-heads.

## Key numbers

- The \(0^{+}_{1}\) state at \(u_{\rm ls}=1600\) MeV has a squared overlap of 34.3% with the \(p_{3/2}\) subshell closure wave function, indicating significant α-cluster breaking; at \(u_{\rm ls}=0\) MeV this overlap is only 2.46%.
- The rms radius of the \(0^{+}_{2}\) state decreases from 3.44 fm (\(u_{\rm ls}=0\) MeV) to 2.99 fm (\(u_{\rm ls}=1600\) MeV), compared to the experimental value of \(2.89 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- The \(0^{+}_{1} \rightarrow 0^{+}_{2}\) monopole transition strength is 8.2 fm\(^{2}\) at \(u_{\rm ls}=0\) MeV and 8.1 fm\(^{2}\) at \(u_{\rm ls}=1600\) MeV, versus the experimental \(5.4 \pm 0.2\) fm\(^{2}\).
- The \(2^{+}_{2} \rightarrow 0^{+}_{3}\) \(E2\) transition strength is 64.4 \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\) at \(u_{\rm ls}=0\) MeV but rises to 166 \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\) at \(u_{\rm ls}=1600\) MeV, while the \(2^{+}_{2} \rightarrow 0^{+}_{2}\) strength drops from 183 to 76.5 \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\); the experimental \(2^{+}_{2} \rightarrow 0^{+}_{1}\) strength is \(1.57^{+0.14}_{-0.11}\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\).
- The \(2^{+}_{1} \rightarrow 0^{+}_{1}\) \(E2\) strength is 7.4 \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\) at \(u_{\rm ls}=1600\) MeV, in good agreement with the experimental \(7.6 \pm 0.4\) \(e^{2}\)fm\(^{4}\).

## Context

This work extends the 3α cluster model of Uegaki (1977) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) by adding a single \(p_{3/2}\) subshell closure configuration, mimicking the α-cluster breaking effects seen in more complex antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations by Kanada-En'yo (1998, 2007, 2012) and fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) by Neff (2004). The results contradict the pure 3α cluster model assignment of the \(0^{+}_{2}\) state as the band-head of the \(2^{+}_{2}\) state, and instead support the AMD/FMD picture where the \(0^{+}_{3}\) state is the band-head. The work enables future studies to disentangle cluster breaking from other correlations in \(^{12}\)C and to apply the same hybrid approach to other light nuclei where cluster and shell-model configurations compete.
