# Emergence of Coherent Long Wavelength Oscillations After a Quench: Application to QCD

> To model the dynamics of the chiral order parameter in a far from equilibrium phase transition, we consider quenching in the O(4) linear sigma model. We argue, and present numerical evidence, that in the period immediately following the quench long wavelength modes of the pion field are amplified. This results in large regions of coherent pion oscillations, and could lead to dramatic phenomenological consequences in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that after a sudden quench from high to low temperature in the O(4) linear sigma model, long wavelength modes of the pion field are amplified and oscillate coherently, even though the explicit symmetry breaking term (which gives pions a nonzero mass) might naively be expected to prevent such behavior. The effect occurs because the spatially averaged squared field oscillates below the vacuum expectation value squared, causing the effective mass squared for low-momentum pion modes to become temporarily negative.

## Method

The authors numerically simulate a quench in the classical O(4) linear sigma model (Gell-Mann–Levy Lagrangian) with an explicit symmetry breaking term Hσ. Initial field configurations are chosen randomly on a cubic lattice with ϕ and ϕ̇ drawn from gaussian distributions centered at zero, representing a disordered high-temperature state. The system is then evolved using the zero-temperature equations of motion via a finite-difference staggered leapfrog scheme, and the spatial Fourier transform is computed to obtain the angular-averaged power spectrum as a function of time.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass mπ = 135 MeV, sigma mass mσ = 600 MeV, pion decay constant fπ = 92.5 MeV (for lattice spacing a = (200 MeV)⁻¹)
- Critical temperature estimate Tc = 2v = 175 MeV from one-loop effective potential
- For quench from Ti = 1.2 Tc, lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 260 MeV and ϕmax = 1.87 v from the truncated gaussian distribution
- Longest wavelength pion modes (ka = 0.20) amplified by a factor of order 1000 relative to shortest wavelength modes in the optimistic simulation
- When total energy E ≳ 10 × (λ/4)v⁴, dramatic growth does not occur

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous equilibrium analysis (Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993) which found that explicit symmetry breaking (nonzero quark masses) prevents large misaligned vacuum regions near the critical temperature in equilibrium. The quench mechanism provides a nonequilibrium alternative to produce coherent pion oscillations, building on earlier proposals for misaligned vacuum and "pion lasers" by Anselm and Ryskin (1991), Blaizot and Krzywicki (1992), and Bjorken (1992). The numerical approach adapts methods used by Turok and Spergel (1991) for cosmological applications, but includes explicit symmetry breaking which prevents the scaling solution found in the symmetric case. The results suggest a possible mechanism for Centauro and anti-Centauro cosmic ray events (Lattes, Fujimoto, and Hasegawa, 1980; Lord and Iwai, 1992) and could enable experimental searches for clusters of pions with non-Gaussian charge distributions in heavy ion collisions.
