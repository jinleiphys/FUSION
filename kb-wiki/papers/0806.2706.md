# The axial anomaly and the phases of dense QCD

> The QCD axial anomaly, by coupling the chiral condensate and BCS pairing fields of quarks in dense matter, leads to a new critical point in the QCD phase diagram \cite{HTYB,chiral2}, which at sufficiently low temperature should terminate the line of phase transitions between chirally broken hadronic matter and color superconducting quark matter. The critical point indicates that matter at low temperature should cross over smoothly from the hadronic to the quark phase, as suggested earlier on the basis of symmetry. We review here the arguments, based on a general Ginzburg-Landau effective Lagrangian, for the existence of the new critical point, as well as discuss possible connections between the QCD phase structure and the BEC-BCS crossover in ultracold trapped atomic fermion systems at unitarity. and implications for the presence of quark matter in neutron stars.

# The axial anomaly and the phases of dense QCD

## Key claim

The QCD axial anomaly, through a six-quark 't Hooft interaction that couples the chiral condensate and diquark pairing fields, induces a new critical point (point A) in the QCD phase diagram at low temperature, which terminates the line of first-order phase transitions between chirally broken hadronic matter and color superconducting quark matter; this critical point exists only for three massless flavors (or realistic quark masses intermediate between two and three massless flavors) and is absent in the two-flavor case.

## Method

The authors construct a model-independent Ginzburg-Landau effective free energy expanded in powers of the chiral condensate σ (proportional to ⟨q̄q⟩) and the diquark condensate d (proportional to ⟨qq⟩), including all terms allowed by symmetry up to fourth order in the fields. For three massless flavors in the color-flavor locked phase, the free energy contains a cubic σ³ term and a γ d²σ coupling term, both arising from the axial anomaly; the phase structure is then mapped in the (a,α) plane, where a and α are the quadratic coefficients for the chiral and pairing fields respectively. For two massless flavors in the 2SC state, the cubic terms vanish identically, yielding a qualitatively different phase diagram without a critical point.

## Key numbers

- The critical point A occurs at positive a > 0 and negative α, with the double line terminating at this point in the (a,α) plane for three massless flavors.
- The parameter measuring the relative importance of three- and higher-body forces in nuclear matter is of order n/(2mπ)³ ∼ 0.4 n/fm⁻³, where mπ is the pion mass.
- Quark matter is estimated to first appear at baryon densities n of order 7-10 times nuclear matter density n₀, based on conventional nucleonic equations of state.
- Neutron star masses as large as 1.8 M⊙ (for Vela X-1 and Cyg X-2) would practically preclude quark matter cores under conventional approaches.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion of "hadron-quark continuity" by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) and builds on the authors' own prior Ginzburg-Landau analysis (Hatsuda, Tachibana, Yamamoto, and Baym, 2006; also "chiral2" 2007). The new critical point is distinct from the well-known high-temperature critical point (point C) reviewed by Stephanov (2004). The paper draws an analogy between the QCD coexistence region and the BEC-BCS crossover in ultracold atomic fermion systems at unitarity, as studied experimentally via Feshbach resonances. The results suggest that the transition from hadronic to quark matter in neutron stars may be a smooth crossover rather than a first-order transition, challenging conventional equations of state based on nucleon-nucleon interactions alone (e.g., Pandharipande and collaborators).
