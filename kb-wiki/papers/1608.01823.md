# Skewness of elliptic flow fluctuations

> Using event-by-event hydrodynamic calculations, we find that the fluctuations of the elliptic flow ($v_2$) in the reaction plane have a negative skew. We compare the skewness of $v_2$ fluctuations to that of initial eccentricity fluctuations. We show that skewness is the main effect lifting the degeneracy between higher-order cumulants, with negative skew corresponding to the hierarchy $v_2\{4\}>v_2\{6\}$ observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC. We describe how the skewness can be measured experimentally and show that hydrodynamics naturally reproduces its magnitude and centrality dependence.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the small splitting between higher-order cumulants of elliptic flow (specifically \(v_2\{4\} > v_2\{6\}\)) observed in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC is primarily due to the negative skewness of elliptic flow fluctuations in the reaction plane, and that this skewness is naturally reproduced by event-by-event hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic calculations of Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV using the Monte Carlo Glauber model for initial conditions and the V-USPHYDRO code with shear viscosity over entropy ratio \(\eta/s = 0.08\). They computed the distributions of the elliptic flow components \(v_x\) and \(v_y\) at freeze-out for pions in the transverse momentum range \(0.2 < p_t < 3\) GeV/\(c\), and compared the skewness of these distributions to the skewness of the initial eccentricity fluctuations. They derived an analytic relation connecting the standardized skewness \(\gamma_1\) to the experimentally accessible cumulants \(v_2\{2\}\), \(v_2\{4\}\), and \(v_2\{6\}\).

## Key numbers

* Standardized skewness \(\gamma_1\) of elliptic flow fluctuations is negative above 15% centrality and reaches approximately \(-0.5\) in peripheral collisions (from ATLAS data), with hydrodynamic calculations compatible within error bars.
* The splitting between \(v_2\{4\}\) and \(v_2\{6\}\) is at the percent level for most centralities in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (ATLAS data).
* The ratio \(v_2\{6\}/v_2\{4\}\) from hydrodynamic calculations is compatible with experimental data and coincides with the corresponding ratio for initial eccentricities \(\varepsilon_2\{6\}/\varepsilon_2\{4\}\) up to 60% centrality.
* The relation \(v_2\{6\} - v_2\{8\} = \frac{1}{11}(v_2\{4\} - v_2\{6\})\) is predicted but could not be tested with the available hydrodynamic statistics.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of non-Gaussian fluctuations of elliptic flow beyond the Bessel-Gaussian approximation (Voloshin, 2007) and the earlier study of eccentricity fluctuations by Yan (2014). It directly addresses the fine structure of higher-order cumulants observed by ATLAS (2014) and shows that the skewness of \(v_2\) fluctuations, rather than the asymmetry between \(\sigma_x\) and \(\sigma_y\), is the dominant effect lifting the degeneracy between \(v_2\{4\}\) and \(v_2\{6\}\). The result enables experimental extraction of the standardized skewness \(\gamma_1\) from cumulant measurements, providing a new constraint on the early dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma and models of initial conditions.
