# Decoherence as a signature of an excited state quantum phase transition

> We analyze the decoherence induced on a single qubit by the interaction with a two-level boson system with critical internal dynamics. We explore how the decoherence process is affected by the presence of quantum phase transitions in the environment. We conclude that the dynamics of the qubit changes dramatically when the environment passes through a continuous excited state quantum phase transition. If the system-environment coupling energy equals the energy at which the environment has a critical behavior, the decoherence induced on the qubit is maximal and the fidelity tends to zero with finite size scaling obeying a power-law.

## Key claim

The decoherence induced on a single qubit by a two-level boson environment becomes maximal when the system-environment coupling energy equals the critical energy of a continuous excited state quantum phase transition (ESQPT) in the environment, with the fidelity tending to zero in the thermodynamic limit. This singular behavior is conditional on the environment being in the broken-symmetry phase (control parameter α < 4/5) and the coupling strength λ taking the specific critical value λ_c = (4 - 5α)/2.

## Method

The authors model a single qubit coupled to a spin environment described by the Lipkin model, transformed into a two-level boson Hamiltonian with control parameter α. The system-environment coupling is chosen as H_coup = λ n_t, where n_t counts t-bosons, so that the effective Hamiltonian for the qubit state |1⟩ becomes H_1 = (α+λ)n_t - (1-α)(Q_t)^2/N. Starting the environment in its ground state, the decoherence factor |r(t)| = |⟨g_0|e^{-iH_1 t}|g_0⟩| is computed numerically for system sizes up to N=10000, and its second maximum r_max(λ) is analyzed as a function of λ for various α values.

## Key numbers

- The environment has a second-order quantum phase transition at α_c = 4/5 (0.8) in arbitrary energy units.
- The critical coupling strength for maximal decoherence is λ_c(α) = (4 - 5α)/2; for α=0 this gives λ_c = 2.
- For λ ≈ λ_c, the second maximum of the decoherence factor r_max(λ_c) scales with environment size N as a power law r_max(λ_c) ∝ N^{-γ} with critical exponent γ = 1/4.
- Finite-size scaling analysis was performed for N = 600, 2500, and 10000 at α values of 0, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.7.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Zanardi (2006), Paz and Zurek (2007), and Paz, Roncaglia, and Zurek (2008) that connected decoherence to ground-state quantum phase transitions, but found that the universal Gaussian decay regime is neither always Gaussian (Rossini et al., 2007) nor always related to an environmental quantum phase transition (Paz et al., 2008). The authors show that an excited state quantum phase transition (ESQPT), previously identified in the Lipkin model by Heiss (2005) and in the interacting boson model by Heinze (2006), produces a distinct signature in decoherence. The specific two-level boson Hamiltonian studied belongs to a broader class used in nuclear structure (Vidal, 2006; Bonatsos, 2008), molecular physics (Curro, 2008), and optical cavity QED (Morrison, 2008), suggesting experimental feasibility for detecting ESQPTs via decoherence measurements.
