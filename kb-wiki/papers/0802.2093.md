# Gross Theory Model for Neutrino-Nucleus Cross Section

> The nuclear gross theory, originally formulated by Takahashi and Yamada for the $β$-decays, is applied for the electronic-neutrino nucleus reactions, employing a more realistic description to the energetics of the Gamow-Teller resonances. The model parameters are gauged from the most recent experimental data, both for $β^-$ decay and electron-capture, separately for even-even, even-odd, odd-odd, odd-even nuclei. The numerical estimates for neutrino-nucleus cross sections agree fairly well with previous evaluations done within the framework of microscopic models. The formalism presented here can be extended to the heavy nuclei mass region, where weak processes are quite relevant, which is of astrophysical interest because of its applications in supernova explosive nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The nuclear gross theory, originally formulated for beta-decay by Takahashi and Yamada, can be applied to neutrino-nucleus reactions and yields cross sections that agree fairly well with microscopic model evaluations, provided the Gamow-Teller resonance energy is described by a more realistic analytic fit (Nakayama et al. 1982) rather than the earlier approximation that set it equal to the Fermi resonance energy.

## Method

The authors adapt the gross theory of beta-decay (GTBD) to compute neutrino-nucleus cross sections for the reaction ν_e + (Z,A) → (Z+1,A) + e^-, using a Gaussian single-particle strength function for the Fermi and Gamow-Teller transitions. The Gamow-Teller resonance energy is taken from an analytic fit to (p,n) reaction data (Nakayama et al. 1982) rather than the earlier Takahashi-Yamada approximation, and the width parameter σ_N is adjusted by minimizing a χ² function (with experimental errors) against recent beta-decay and electron-capture half-life data for nuclei with A < 70, separately for each parity family (even-even, even-odd, odd-even, odd-odd). The neutrino flux is modeled as a zero-chemical-potential Fermi-Dirac distribution with temperature T_ν.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi resonance energy for β^- decay is given by E_F = +(1.44 Z A^{-1/3} - 0.7825) MeV, with standard deviation σ_F = 0.157 Z A^{-1/3} MeV.
- The Gamow-Teller resonance energy shift relative to the Fermi resonance is δ = 26 A^{-1/3} - 18.5 (N - Z)/A MeV (Nakayama et al. 1982 fit).
- The adjustable width parameter σ_N (standard deviation from spin-dependent forces) is determined separately for each parity family; for example, for even-even nuclei using the χ²_B minimization and Eq. (13) for E_GT, σ_N = 2.8 MeV (from Table I, inferred from text).
- The mismatch factor η = 10^{√(χ²/N_0)} is reduced by the χ²_B minimization procedure, particularly for odd-odd nuclei.
- The best agreement between calculated and experimental half-lives occurs for Q_β A^{1/3} ≥ 40 MeV for even-even, even-odd, and odd-even families, and for Q_β A^{1/3} ≥ 45 MeV for odd-odd families.

## Context

This work extends the gross theory of beta-decay originally formulated by Takahashi and Yamada (1969) and later refined in the second-generation gross theory (GT2) and semi-gross theory (SGT) by Tachibana et al. (1990) and Nakata et al. (1997). It improves upon earlier gross-theory neutrino capture calculations by Itoh et al. (1977) by using a more realistic Gamow-Teller resonance energy from (p,n) reaction systematics (Nakayama et al. 1982) and by fitting the width parameter separately for each parity family using recent experimental data. The results are compared with microscopic approaches such as the extended Thomas-Fermi plus continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (ETFSI+CQRPA) by Borzov et al. (2000) and the ETFSI+GT2 method. The formalism is intended for extension to heavy nuclei relevant to supernova explosive nucleosynthesis and r-process calculations in neutrino-rich environments.
