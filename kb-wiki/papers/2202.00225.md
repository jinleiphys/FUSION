# Effective polarization in proton-induced $α$ knockout reactions

> The effective polarization of the residual nucleus in the proton-induced $α$ knockout reaction is investigated within the distorted wave impulse approximation framework. The strong absorption of the emitted $α$ particle results in strong selectivity on the reaction "position" depending on the third component of the single-particle orbital angular momentum of the $α$ particle inside a nucleus, hence on the spin direction of the reaction residue. This is caused by a mechanism that is similar to the Maris effect, the effective polarization in the proton-induced proton knockout reactions. However, as a distinct feature of the effective polarization in the $α$ knockout process, the spin degrees of freedom of the reacting particles play no role. The $α$ knockout process with complete kinematics can be a useful polarization technique for the residual nucleus, without actively controlling the spin of the proton.

## Key claim
The effective polarization of the residual nucleus in proton-induced alpha knockout reactions is realized through strong absorption of the emitted alpha particle, which selects specific magnetic quantum number components (\(m_y = \pm 2\)) depending on the recoil momentum direction. This mechanism requires no spin-dependent interactions or polarized beams, unlike the Maris effect in nucleon knockout reactions.

## Method
The authors employed the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) in its factorized form without spin degrees of freedom to calculate the triple differential cross section (TDX) for \(^{120}\)Sn\((p,p\alpha)^{116}\)Cd\(_{2^+}\) and \(^{20}\)Ne\((p,p\alpha)^{16}\)O\(_{2^+}\) reactions at 392 MeV. The TDX was decomposed into components of the magnetic quantum number \(m_y\) (projection of orbital angular momentum on the \(y\)-axis defined by \(\boldsymbol{K}_0 \times \boldsymbol{K}_1\)), and the recoil momentum \(p_\mathrm{R}\) was varied around the recoilless condition. The bound alpha wave function was obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential, and optical potentials included both phenomenological (Koning-Delaroche for protons, Avrigeanu for alphas) and microscopic (single-folding with Melbourne \(g\)-matrix) forms.

## Key numbers
- The recoilless condition for \(^{120}\)Sn\((p,p\alpha)^{116}\)Cd\(_{2^+}\) at 392 MeV is realized at \(T_1^\mathrm{L} = 328\) MeV, \(\theta_1^\mathrm{L} = 43.2^\circ\), \(\theta_2^\mathrm{L} = 61^\circ\).
- The recoilless condition for \(^{20}\)Ne\((p,p\alpha)^{16}\)O\(_{2^+}\) at 392 MeV is realized at \(T_1^\mathrm{L} = 341\) MeV, \(\theta_1^\mathrm{L} = 35^\circ\), \(\theta_2^\mathrm{L} = 65^\circ\).
- The Woods-Saxon bound-state potential parameters are \(R_0 = 1.27 \times 116^{1/3}\) fm and \(a = 0.67\) fm for the \(^{120}\)Sn target.
- The bound alpha wave function for \(^{120}\)Sn uses quantum numbers \(n = 7\) and \(l = 2\).

## Context
This work extends the concept of the Maris effect (Maris 1958, Jacob 1976, Krein 1995) from proton-induced nucleon knockout to alpha knockout reactions, where the spinless nature of the alpha particle eliminates spin-dependent mechanisms entirely. The reaction position selectivity (RPS) previously identified in \(^{120}\)Sn\((p,p\alpha)\) by Yoshida (2016) is shown to produce spin polarization of the residual nucleus through purely kinematic and absorptive effects. This enables a polarization technique for residual nuclei in both normal and inverse kinematics without requiring polarized beams, complementing the spin alignment mechanism discussed by Hansen (2003) for one-nucleon removal reactions.
