# The High-Density Symmetry Energy and Direct Urca

> The symmetry energy of nucleonic matter is usually assumed to be quadratic in the isospin density. While this may be justified at sub-saturation densities, there is no need to enforce this restriction at super-saturation densities. The presence of a quartic term can strongly modify the critical density for the direct Urca process which leads to faster cooling of neutron stars. Neutron star cooling predictions which lie below the observational data can, for some equations of state, be repaired with a quartic term which effectively turns off the direct Urca process.

## Key claim

The presence of a quartic term (fourth-order in isospin deviation) in the symmetry energy expansion can strongly modify the critical density for the direct Urca process in neutron stars, potentially turning off this fast cooling channel for some equations of state and thereby reconciling cooling predictions that lie below observational data.

## Method

The author parameterizes the quartic dependence using a parameter η, defined from the energy per baryon at different proton fractions, and modifies only the potential energy part of existing equations of state (APR, Skyrme SLy230a, MDI, and three relativistic mean-field models RMF, RMFlo, RMFhi) while leaving the kinetic part unchanged. The critical density for direct Urca is then computed from the condition that the neutron, proton, and electron Fermi momenta can form a triangle, equivalent to the proton fraction exceeding about 10 percent.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density is set to n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy E/A = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 230 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 34 MeV, and reduced Dirac effective mass M*/M = 0.8 for the RMF models.
- The allowed range for η is 3/7 < η < 5, derived from requiring that nuclear matter is always the most energetically favored configuration at fixed density.
- For the APR equation of state, varying η_pot can shift the direct Urca critical density from below 1.4 solar masses to above the central density of the maximum mass neutron star.
- The RMF, RMFlo, and RMFhi models have direct Urca critical densities of 0.306, 0.275, and 0.806 fm⁻³, respectively.
- The maximum mass and radius of the maximum mass neutron star are essentially unaffected by modification of η_pot.

## Context

This work extends the study of the symmetry energy beyond the usual quadratic approximation, which is justified at sub-saturation densities (Lagaris 1981, Prakash 1987, Wiringa 1988) but not necessarily at super-saturation densities. The paper directly addresses the tension noted by Gusakov (2005), who found that APR-based neutron stars above about 1.7 solar masses cool too rapidly via direct Urca to match observations; Steiner shows that a quartic term can turn off Urca and resolve this discrepancy. The work also clarifies that the correlation between Urca threshold and neutron skin thickness of lead (Steiner 2005) depends on the assumption that η_pot is near unity, and that quartic terms affect Urca but not neutron star radii, which is relevant to the radius-Urca correlation suggested by Horowitz (2002).
