# Heavy quark spin multiplet structure of $P_c$-like pentaquark as P-wave hadronic molecular state

> We study the heavy quark spin (HQS) multiplet structure of P-wave $Q\bar{Q}qqq$-type pentaquarks treated as molecules of a heavy meson and a heavy baryon. We define the light-cloud spin (LCS) basis decomposing the meson-baryon spin wavefunction into the LCS and HQS parts. Introducing the LCS basis, we find HQS multiplets classified by the LCS; five HQS singlets, two HQS doublets, and three HQS triplets. We construct the one-pion exchange potential respecting the heavy quark spin and chiral symmetries to demonstrate which HQS multiplets are realized as a bound state. By solving the coupled channel Schrödinger equations, we study the heavy meson-baryon systems with $I=1/2$ and $J^P=(1/2^+, 3/2^+, 5/2^+, 7/2^+)$. The bound states which have same LCS structure are degenerate at the heavy quark limit, and the degeneracy is resolved for finite mass. This HQS multiplet structure will be measured in the future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that P-wave $\bar{P}^{(*)}\Sigma_Q^{(*)}$ molecular states (candidate $P_c$-like pentaquarks) can be classified into heavy quark spin (HQS) multiplets using a light-cloud spin (LCS) basis, and that states sharing the same LCS structure are degenerate in the heavy quark limit, with degeneracy resolved for finite heavy quark masses.

## Method

The authors define a light-cloud spin (LCS) basis that separates the heavy quark spin from the light-cloud spin in the wavefunction of $\bar{P}^{(*)}\Sigma_Q^{(*)}$ molecular states. They construct one-pion exchange potentials (OPEP) respecting heavy quark spin symmetry and chiral symmetry, then solve coupled-channel Schrodinger equations using the Gaussian expansion method for systems with isospin $I=1/2$ and total angular momentum-parity $J^P = (1/2^+, 3/2^+, 5/2^+, 7/2^+)$.

## Key numbers

* The mass of $P_c(4380)$ is $4380 \pm 8 \pm 28$ MeV and its decay width is $205 \pm 18 \pm 86$ MeV; the mass of $P_c(4450)$ is $4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5$ MeV and its decay width is $39 \pm 5 \pm 19$ MeV (from LHCb, cited in the introduction).
* The threshold of $\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*$ is $4385.3$ MeV and $\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c$ is $4462.2$ MeV.
* The mass difference between $D$ and $D^*$ is about $140$ MeV; between $B$ and $B^*$ it is $45$ MeV.
* The mass difference between $\Sigma_c$ and $\Sigma_c^*$ is about $65$ MeV; between $\Sigma_b$ and $\Sigma_b^*$ it is about $20$ MeV.
* The heavy meson-pion coupling constant $|g| = 0.59$ is determined from $D^* \to D\pi$ decay; the heavy baryon-pion coupling constant $g_1 = 0.94$ is estimated from the quark model; the pion decay constant is $f_{\pi} = 92.4$ MeV.
* The cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ is set to $1000$ MeV and $1500$ MeV for the monopole form factor.

## Context

This work extends the HQS multiplet analysis of S-wave $\bar{P}^{(*)}\Sigma_Q^{(*)}$ molecular states from Shimizu, Yamaguchi, and Harada (2018) to P-wave states, enabling the study of positive-parity pentaquark candidates. It builds on the hadronic molecular picture of the $P_c$ pentaquarks observed by LHCb (Aaij et al., 2015) and uses the heavy quark spin symmetry framework developed by Isgur and Wise (1989, 1990, 1991) and Neubert (1994). The results provide predictions for the HQS multiplet structure that can be tested in future experiments, particularly for identifying which multiplets can form bound states under the one-pion exchange potential.
