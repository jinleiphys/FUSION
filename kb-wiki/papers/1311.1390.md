# Distribution amplitudes of light-quark mesons from lattice QCD

> We exploit a method introduced recently to determine parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) from minimal information in order to obtain light-quark pseudoscalar and vector meson PDAs from the limited number of moments produced by numerical simulations of lattice-regularised QCD. Within errors, the PDAs of pseudoscalar and vector mesons constituted from the same valence quarks are identical; they are concave functions, whose dilation expresses the strength of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking; and SU(3)-flavour symmetry is broken nonperturbatively at the level of 10%. Notably, the appearance of precision in the lattice moments is misleading. The moments also exhibit material dependence on lattice volume, especially for the pion. Improvements need therefore be made before an accurate, unified picture of the light-front structure of light-quark pseudoscalar and vector mesons is revealed.

## Key claim

The paper exploits a recently introduced method to reconstruct parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) from a limited number of lattice-QCD moments, finding that within errors the PDAs of pseudoscalar and vector mesons with the same valence quarks are identical and concave, but the authors caution that the apparent precision of the lattice moments is misleading and that the moments exhibit material dependence on lattice volume, especially for the pion.

## Method

The authors use a reconstruction method based on a parametrization of the PDA as a beta distribution, φ(x) = x^α (1-x)^β / B(α,β), which is determined from the first two nontrivial moments of the PDA obtained from lattice-QCD simulations with N_f = 2+1 domain-wall fermions at ζ = 2 GeV in the MS-bar scheme. They apply this to pseudoscalar and vector meson moments from two lattice volumes (16^3 × 32 and 24^3 × 64) to extract PDAs for π, ρ_∥, φ, K, and K*_∥ mesons, then evolve the resulting distributions to higher scales using leading-order ERBL evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- For equal-mass valence quarks (du mesons) on the 16^3 × 32 lattice: α_du = β_du = 0.50^{+0.20}_{-0.16}
- For equal-mass valence quarks on the 24^3 × 64 lattice: α_du = β_du = 0.29^{+0.15}_{-0.13}
- For s-u mesons on the 16^3 × 32 lattice: α_su = 0.56^{+0.21}_{-0.18}, β_su = 0.45^{+0.19}_{-0.16}
- For s-u mesons on the 24^3 × 64 lattice: α_su = 0.48^{+0.19}_{-0.16}, β_su = 0.38^{+0.17}_{-0.15}
- The second moment of the pion PDA: ⟨(x- x̄)^2⟩ = 0.25(1)(2) on 16^3 × 32 and 0.28(1)(2) on 24^3 × 64
- The first moment of the kaon PDA: ⟨(x- x̄)^1⟩ = 0.035(2)(2) on 16^3 × 32 and 0.036(1)(2) on 24^3 × 64
- The "1/x" moment w_du (defined as (1/3)⟨x^{-1}⟩) = 1.33^{+0.32}_{-0.19} on 16^3 × 32 and 1.83^{+1.00}_{-0.41} on 24^3 × 64
- The DSE prediction for the chiral-limit pion PDA gives w_du = 1.53
- The ratio F_K(ζ_2^2)/F_π(ζ_2^2) = 1.21^{+1.22}_{-0.62} from the 16^3 × 32 lattice and 0.74^{+1.20}_{-0.51} from the 24^3 × 64 lattice
- The s-quark momentum excess in the kaon remains above 50% of its ζ_2 value until energy scales exceeding 7.3 TeV

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Chang et al. (2013) and Cloet et al. (2013) for reconstructing PDAs from limited moments, applying it to lattice-QCD data from Arthur et al. (2010). The results are compared with DSE predictions from Chang et al. (2011) and QCD sum rules analyses by Bakulev et al. (2001), as well as models by Mikhailov et al. (1986) and Brodsky et al. (2006). The analysis reveals that current lattice moments have material volume dependence and that the 24^3 × 64 lattice produces a pion PDA that appears too broad compared to continuum predictions, highlighting the need for improved lattice calculations before an accurate unified picture of light-front meson structure emerges.
