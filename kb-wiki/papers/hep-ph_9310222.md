# Vacuum Misalignment in High Energy Collisions

> We study a recent proposal to observe the disoriented chiral condensate in high energy collisions. In order to produce a large fluctuation in pion probability distribution, a large size of the correlated region is essential. We study the role of the intrinsic symmetry breaking and suggest that a negative (mass)^2 of mesons arising from a misaligned vacuum may be a candidate for such a mechanism. We further point out a possibility to observe unsuppressed strong $CP$ violation in the disoriented vacuum phase.

# Vacuum Misalignment in High Energy Collisions

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a negative (mass)^2 for mesons can arise from a misaligned vacuum relative to the intrinsic symmetry breaking (ISB) direction, providing a mechanism to sustain large correlated regions and produce observable coherent pion emissions; however, this mechanism only operates when the vacuum orientation angle θ lies in the interval (π/2, 3π/2), and the energy density available for pion emission depends on whether the vacuum expectation value has reached its zero-temperature value.

## Method

The author studies the disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) scenario using the linear σ-model with an explicit symmetry breaking term cσ, analyzing the vacuum misalignment that occurs when the system undergoes a rapid quench from high temperature (T > T_c) to zero temperature. The vacuum orientation is treated as a relic of the high-temperature history, with the VEV specified as ⟨φ⟩_θ,ñ ≈ (F_π cosθ, F_π sinθ ñ), and the (mass)^2 of the would-be Goldstone bosons is computed from the second derivative of the vacuum energy density with respect to the misalignment angle. The analysis is extended to the U(1)_A phase using an extended linear σ-model with eight degrees of freedom, including an instanton-induced U(1)_A-breaking term V_K, to study CP-violating effects such as η → 2π decays.

## Key numbers

- The probability distribution for the neutral pion fraction r = n_π⁰/(n_π⁰ + n_π±) is given by dP(r) = (1/2)(1/√r) dr, which differs sharply from the ordinary Gaussian peak at 1/3.
- The (mass)^2 of pions in a misaligned vacuum is m̃_π² = m_π² cosθ, becoming negative when π/2 < θ < 3π/2.
- The instanton amplitude suppression factor at high temperature is approximately 10⁻⁷ for T = 2Λ_QCD, based on the expression K(T) ∝ Λ_QCD² (Λ_QCD/(πT))⁸ [9 ln(πT/Λ_QCD)]⁶.
- The η → 2π decay rate is estimated as Γ(η → 2π) ≈ 165 sin²φ cos²φ MeV, where φ is the U(1)_A misalignment angle.
- The total energy available in the interior (mod E₀) is E ≈ 28 (1 − cos 2φ) GeV for a correlated region radius R ≈ 3 fm, implying up to about 10 η-mesons per event.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) on disoriented chiral condensates formed via rapid quenching in high-energy collisions, where negative (mass)² modes arise from the hesitation of the VEV near zero. The author introduces an additional mechanism based on vacuum misalignment relative to intrinsic symmetry breaking, which is closer to the original proposal by Anselm (1988) and Taylor (1993) where the classical pion field amplitude equals the zero-temperature VEV. The paper also points toward the possibility of observing unsuppressed strong CP violation in the DCC phase, such as η → 2π decays, which would be absent in normal QCD due to CP conservation.
