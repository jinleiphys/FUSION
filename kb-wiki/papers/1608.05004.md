# Role of medium modifications for neutrino-pair processes from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung - Impact on the protoneutron star deleptonization

> In this article the neutrino-pair production from nucleon-nucleon (NN) bremsstrahlung is explored via medium-modifications of the strong interactions at the level of the one-pion exchange approximation. It governs the bulk part of the NN interaction at low densities relevant for the neutrino physics in core-collapse supernova studies. The resulting medium modified one-pion exchange rate for the neutrino-pair processes is implemented in simulations of core collapse supernovae in order to study the impact on the neutrino signal emitted from the deleptonization of the nascent proto-neutron star. Consequences for the nucleosynthesis of heavy elements of the material ejected from the PNS surface are discussed.

## Key claim

Medium modifications to the one-pion exchange approximation for nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, specifically the dressing of the local πNN-vertex (MVOPE), suppress the neutrino-pair emission/absorption rate compared to the unmodified free OPE (FOPE) approach, with the onset of reduction starting at densities of about 10^12 g cm^-3 and a suppression factor of about 5 at nuclear saturation density ρ0.

## Method

The author implements a density-dependent parametrization of the medium-modified πNN-vertex (γ) based on the Fermi-liquid theory of Migdal (1978, 1990) and Voskresensky (1987) and Voskresenskaya (2001), which scales the neutrino-pair emission kernel as (1 + 1/3 (ρ/ρ0)^(1/3))^(-6). This medium-modified rate (NN*) is incorporated into the Boltzmann neutrino transport module of the spherically symmetric, general relativistic core-collapse supernova model AGILE-BOLTZTRAN of Liebendoerfer (2004), using the HS(DD2) nuclear equation of state from Hempel (2009) and Typel (2009). The simulations are launched from an 18 M⊙ pre-collapse progenitor of Woosley (2002) and evolved through the PNS deleptonization phase up to 30 seconds post bounce.

## Key numbers

- Suppression of the neutrino-pair emission kernel at ρ0: about a factor of 5, in quantitative agreement with calculations of Bartl (2014) based on the T-matrix approach and chiral effective field theory.
- Onset of reduction of the neutrino-pair process starts at densities of about 10^12 g cm^-3.
- For the simulation based on NN*, the average energies of νμ/τ and ν̄e are increased by up to about 1 MeV compared to the reference case using unmodified FOPE.
- The PNS deleptonization timescale considered is up to 30 seconds post bounce, with the transition from neutrino opaque to transparent occurring at t > 40 seconds post bounce when temperatures drop below T ∼ 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium modifications for weak processes involving nucleon-nucleon interactions, following the Fermi-liquid approach of Migdal (1978, 1990) and Voskresensky (1987) and Voskresenskaya (2001), and provides a density-dependent parametrization for applications in core-collapse supernova simulations. It contrasts with the free OPE (FOPE) approximation of Hannestad (1997) and Friman (1979), which underestimates neutron star cooling, and shows quantitative agreement with the chiral effective field theory results of Bartl (2014) who found significant suppression of the neutrino-pair process at low densities. The implementation enables quantification of the role of NN-bremsstrahlung for the neutrino signal during PNS deleptonization, with consequences for the electron fraction Ye in the neutrino-driven wind and the nucleosynthesis of heavy elements ejected from the PNS surface, as studied by Woosley (1994), Otsuki (1999), and Wanajo (2006).
