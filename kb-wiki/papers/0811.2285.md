# Empirical transverse charge densities in the nucleon-to-P11(1440) transition

> Using recent experimental data, we analyze the electromagnetic transition from the nucleon to the P11(1440) resonance. From the resulting empirical transition form factors, we map out the quark transverse charge densities which induce the N to P11(1440) transition. It is found that the transition from the proton to its first radially excited state is dominated by up quarks in a central region of around 0.5 fm and by down quarks in an outer band which extends up to about 1 fm.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic transition from the proton to the P11(1440) resonance is dominated by up quarks in a central region of around 0.5 fm and by down quarks in an outer band extending up to about 1 fm, based on the MAID2007 parameterization of experimental helicity amplitudes.

## Method

The authors used the MAID2007 parameterization of the gamma* N to P11(1440) helicity amplitudes A1/2 and S1/2, which were fitted to recent JLab/CLAS pi0 and pi+ electroproduction data for the proton and older quasi-free pi- p data for the neutron. From these helicity amplitudes, they extracted the two independent transition form factors F1 and F2. They then performed Fourier transforms of these form factors to map out the quark transverse charge densities in the light-front frame, both for unpolarized and transversely polarized nucleon and resonance.

## Key numbers

- P11(1440) resonance mass: M* = 1.440 GeV
- Pauli-type form factor at the real photon point for the proton: F2^{p N*}(0) = -0.64 ± 0.04
- Transverse helicity amplitude at Q2 = 0 for the proton: A0_{1/2} = -61.4 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Longitudinal helicity amplitude at Q2 = 0 for the proton: S0_{1/2} = 4.2 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Transverse helicity amplitude at Q2 = 0 for the neutron: A0_{1/2} = 54.1 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- Longitudinal helicity amplitude at Q2 = 0 for the neutron: S0_{1/2} = -41.5 × 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}
- The proton helicity amplitude A1/2 changes sign from negative at Q2 = 0 to a broad positive maximum around Q2 ≈ 2 GeV^2
- The proton helicity amplitude S1/2 has a maximum around Q2 ≈ 0.6 GeV^2
- The proton transition form factor F2 changes sign around Q2 ≈ 1 GeV^2
- Experimental data for the proton extend up to Q2 ≈ 5 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the transverse charge density mapping approach previously applied to the nucleon by Miller (2007) and Carlson (2007), and to the N to Delta(1232) transition by Carlson (2007) using MAID2007 data. It provides the first empirical mapping of quark transverse charge densities for the N to P11(1440) transition, enabling comparison with lattice QCD calculations such as those by Lin (2008) and with baryon structure models. The authors propose future extension to the N to S11(1535) and N to D13(1520) transitions.
