# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors in twisted mass lattice QCD

> We present results on the nucleon electromagnetic form factors within lattice QCD using two flavors of degenerate twisted mass fermions. Volume effects are examined using simulations at two volumes of spatial length L=2.1 fm and L=2.8 fm. Cut-off effects are investigated using three different values of the lattice spacings, namely a=0.089 fm, a=0.070 and a=0.056 fm. The nucleon magnetic moment, Dirac and Pauli radii are obtained in the continuum limit and chirally extrapolated to the physical pion mass allowing for a comparison with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that lattice QCD results for nucleon isovector electromagnetic form factors, after continuum extrapolation and chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass, yield values for the magnetic moment and radii that can be compared with experiment, but the lattice data at finite pion masses show a weaker \(Q^2\)-dependence than experimental data, and the final comparison depends on the chiral extrapolation procedure.

## Method

The authors compute the isovector nucleon electromagnetic form factors \(G_E^{p-n}(Q^2)\) and \(G_M^{p-n}(Q^2)\) using two flavors of degenerate twisted mass fermions at maximal twist, which provides automatic \({\cal O}(a)\) improvement. They perform simulations at three lattice spacings (\(a = 0.089\) fm, \(0.070\) fm, \(0.056\) fm) and two spatial volumes (\(L = 2.1\) fm and \(L = 2.8\) fm), with pion masses ranging from 260 MeV to 470 MeV, and extract form factors from ratios of two- and three-point correlation functions using sequential inversions through the sink.

## Key numbers

*   Lattice spacings used: \(a = 0.089(1)(5)\) fm at \(\beta = 3.9\), \(a = 0.070(1)(4)\) fm at \(\beta = 4.05\), and \(a = 0.056(2)(3)\) fm at \(\beta = 4.2\), set using the nucleon mass.
*   Pion mass range: from 260.0(9) MeV (at \(\beta = 3.9\), \(a\mu = 0.003\), \(L = 2.8\) fm) to 469.8(18) MeV (at \(\beta = 4.2\), \(a\mu = 0.0065\), \(L = 2.39\) fm).
*   Spatial volumes: \(L = 2.1\) fm (\(24^3 \times 48\) lattice) and \(L = 2.8\) fm (\(32^3 \times 64\) lattice) at \(\beta = 3.9\).
*   Dipole fit parameters at \(\beta = 3.9\) for the lightest pion mass (260.0 MeV): electric dipole mass \(m_E = 1.03(6)\) GeV, magnetic dipole mass \(m_M = 1.13(13)\) GeV, and \(G_M(0) = 3.95(27)\), yielding a magnetic moment \(\mu_N = 2.14(12)\).
*   Dipole fit parameters at \(\beta = 3.9\) for the heaviest pion mass (467.5 MeV): \(m_E = 1.40(7)\) GeV, \(m_M = 1.62(17)\) GeV, \(G_M(0) = 4.02(18)\), yielding \(\mu_N = 2.57(22)\).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure by Alexandrou et al. (2008, 2009, 2010) using twisted mass fermions, and provides a systematic study of cut-off and volume effects for electromagnetic form factors. The results enable a comparison with experimental parametrizations such as Kelly (2004) and with other lattice discretizations, and they demonstrate that the \(\rho\)-meson mass from the same simulations provides a good description of the form factor \(Q^2\)-dependence via a dipole parametrization, supporting vector-meson dominance ideas.
