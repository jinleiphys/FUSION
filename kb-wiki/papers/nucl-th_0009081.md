# Nuclear fragmentation: sampling the instabilities of binary systems

> We derive stability conditions of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter ($ANM$) and discuss the relation to mechanical and chemical instabilities of general two-component systems. We show that the chemical instability may appear as an instability of the system against isoscalar-like rather than isovector-like fluctuations if the interaction between the two constituent species has an attractive character as in the case of $ANM$. This leads to a new kind of liquid-gas phase transition, of interest for fragmentation experiments with radioactive beams.

# Nuclear fragmentation: sampling the instabilities of binary systems

## Key claim
The authors show that in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM), the liquid-gas phase transition arises from instability against isoscalar-like fluctuations rather than isovector-like fluctuations, due to the attractive character of the proton-neutron interaction. This means that chemical instability can manifest through isoscalar-like modes, contradicting the usual association of chemical instability with isovector fluctuations in symmetric systems.

## Method
The authors derive generalized Pomeranchuk stability conditions for asymmetric binary systems using Fermi liquid theory, introducing a mixing angle that diagonalizes the free energy variation into independent isoscalar-like and isovector-like normal modes. They then apply this formalism to asymmetric nuclear matter using a Skyrme-like potential energy density with parameters adjusted to reproduce saturation properties and the symmetry energy coefficient, calculating spinodal lines for various proton fractions at finite temperatures.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Skyrme parameters: A = −356.8 MeV, B = 303.9 MeV, α = 1/6, C₁ = 125 MeV, C₂ = 93.5 MeV
- A high-density instability appears around 1.5 fm⁻³ where the interaction between components becomes repulsive and 1 + F₀ᵍᵃ < 0
- Calculations performed at T = 1 MeV for proton fractions y = 0.5, 0.4, 0.25, and 0.1

## Context
This work extends the stability analysis of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) by Baym and Pethick (1978) and earlier studies of ANM fragmentation by Serot (1992), Bao (1997), and Baran (1998). While previous work showed that a diffusive or chemical spinodal is significant in asymmetric systems, the detailed nature of the responsible fluctuations had not been clarified. The results explain the "isospin distillation" observed experimentally by Xu (2000) and provide a framework for interpreting fragmentation experiments at new Radioactive Beam facilities, enabling direct studies of the charge-dependent part of the nuclear interaction far from normal conditions.
