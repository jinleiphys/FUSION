# Non-flow effects in three-particle mixed-harmonic azimuthal correlations in small collision systems

> The Multi-particle technique has been used to unravel the nature of the long-range collectivity in small collision systems. A large three-particle mixed-harmonic correlation signal was recently observed by the ATLAS Collaboration, but the role of non-flow correlations is not yet studied. We estimate the influence of non-flow correlations to the three-particle correlators in $pp$ and $p$+Pb collisions using PYTHIA and HIJING models, and compare with the ATLAS results. The large non-flow effects from the jet and dijet production is found to be largely suppressed in $p$+Pb collisions using the subevent cumulant method by calculating the azimuthal correlation between two or more longitudinal pseudorapidity ranges. Depending on the experimental subevent method, however, the non-flow effects may still be significant in $pp$ collisions.

## Key claim

The three-subevent cumulant method can largely suppress non-flow correlations from jets and dijets in p+Pb collisions, but significant non-flow contributions may still remain in pp collisions depending on the experimental subevent method used.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA8 to generate pp events at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV and HIJING to generate p+Pb events at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, models which contain non-flow correlations from jets, dijets, and resonance decays but no genuine long-range collectivity. They calculated three-particle asymmetric cumulants ac_{2,2|4} and ac_{2,3|5} using standard, two-subevent, and three-subevent methods, correlating charged particles with 0.3 < pT < 3.0 GeV/c and |eta| < 2.5. The subevent methods group particles into two or three non-overlapping pseudorapidity intervals and correlate particles only between different intervals, with some calculations also including an additional 0.5 unit pseudorapidity gap between subevents.

## Key numbers

- For ac_{2,2|4} in pp collisions, values decrease sharply up to Nch ~ 60, then decrease very slowly for higher Nch
- In p+Pb collisions, ac_{2,2|4} values from HIJING are larger than ATLAS data for Nch < 80, but decrease to below the data for Nch > 80
- The estimated collective signal for ac_{2,3|5} is about 3 to 5 x 10^{-6}, based on v2 ~ 0.06 and v3 ~ 0.02 from ATLAS measurements and a non-linear response coefficient chi_{23,5} ~ 2-3 from Pb+Pb collisions
- The three-subevent method with an additional 0.5 unit pseudorapidity gap further suppresses ac_{2,2|4} values compared to the three-subevent method without the gap

## Context

This work extends the study of non-flow effects in small collision systems from four-particle cumulants c_n{4} and symmetric cumulants SC(n,m) (studied by Jia (2017), Huo (2017), and the ATLAS Collaboration (2017, 2018)) to three-particle mixed-harmonic correlators. The ATLAS Collaboration (2018) recently observed a large three-particle mixed-harmonic correlation signal ac_{2,2|4} in pp and p+Pb collisions, but the role of non-flow correlations had not been studied. This paper provides model-based estimates of non-flow contributions and demonstrates that the three-subevent method can effectively suppress them in p+Pb collisions, while also predicting that ac_{2,3|5} should be measurable at the LHC.
