# Two-Nucleon Solitary Wave Exchange Potentials (SWEPs)

## Key claim
The sine-Gordon solitary wave exchange potential (SG SWEP) can reproduce realistic singlet-even NN phase shifts and deuteron parameters using only two adjustable parameters (β and Ω), with the ¹S₀ state requiring only one parameter (β). This is presented as an example of a class of potentials derived from nonlinear quantum field theory that require fewer parameters than conventional one-boson-exchange potentials (OBEPs), though the current SG SWEP lacks a spin-orbit term needed for triplet states.

## Method
The authors derive a two-nucleon potential by replacing the linear Klein-Gordon propagator used in OBEPs with a solitary wave propagator constructed from exact particular solutions of the sine-Gordon equation, a nonlinear generalization of the Klein-Gordon equation. The momentum-space potential is obtained from lowest-order Feynman diagrams with mixed pseudoscalar-pseudovector πNN coupling, then Fourier transformed to coordinate space. Phase shifts up to 500 MeV are calculated using a least-squares method for solving the radial Schrödinger equation.

## Key numbers
- The SG SWEP uses only two parameters (β = 0.89, Ω = 1/4) with N = 4 terms in the propagator expansion
- The coupling constant G = 10.35 MeV, derived from the πNN coupling constant g²/(4π) = 13.55 (f² = 0.075)
- Pion mass m = 138.033 MeV and nucleon mass M = 938.926 MeV used in calculations
- The potential reproduces phase shifts up to 500 MeV laboratory energy
- The ¹S₀ state potential involves only one parameter (β), as it is unaffected by the PS-PV mixing parameter Ω

## Context
This work extends the program of deriving nucleon-nucleon potentials from nonlinear quantum field theory developed by Burt (1981), following earlier SWEP work by Sebhatu (1976, 1989, 1990). The SG SWEP is compared to the Reid soft-core potential (Reid, 1968) and is contrasted with OBEPs from the Bonn (Machleidt, 1987), Paris (Lacombe, 1980), and Nijmegen (Nagels, 1978) groups, which require about a dozen adjustable parameters. The authors note that the current SG SWEP lacks a spin-orbit term and propose incorporating ρ meson, two-pion, or scalar meson exchange contributions to address triplet NN states, with the long-term goal of connecting nonlinear meson field theory to QCD-inspired quark cluster models (Myhrer, 1988).
