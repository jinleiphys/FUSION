# Deformation and the Nuclear Matrix Elements of the Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> In this talk I will review the "state of the art" of the calculations of the nuclear matrix elements (NME) of the neutrinoless double beta decays for the nuclei 48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 124Sn, 128Te, 130Te and 136Xe in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model (ISM), and compare them with the NME's obtained using the Quasi-particle RPA approach (QRPA). I will also discuss the effect of the competition between the pairing and quadrupole correlations in the value of these NME's. In particular I will show that, as the difference in deformation between parent and grand daughter grows, the NME's of both the neutrinoless and the two neutrino modes decrease rapidly.

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for both neutrinoless and two-neutrino double beta decay decrease rapidly as the difference in deformation between the parent and grand-daughter nuclei grows, but this effect is conditional on the nuclei having strong quadrupole correlations; in transitions where both nuclei are equally deformed, the NME can remain constant or even increase significantly (up to 50%) in full major oscillator shells.

## Method

The authors performed Interacting Shell Model (ISM) calculations of the 0νββ and 2νββ nuclear matrix elements for seven candidate nuclei (48Ca, 76Ge, 82Se, 124Sn, 128Te, 130Te, 136Xe) and compared them with Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) results. They systematically varied the strength of an added quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (λQ·Q) and the maximum seniority truncation (s_m) in the wave functions, using 82Se→82Kr as a test case and also studying unphysical mirror transitions in A=26, 50, 66, and 110 to isolate deformation effects. Short-range correlations were treated with both UCOM and Jastrow prescriptions.

## Key numbers

- For 82Se→82Kr, the NME values range from 0.07 to 2.7 (a factor of 40 span) as the difference in mass quadrupole sum rule between parent and grand daughter varies; the λ=0 value is 2.18.
- At seniority truncation s_m=4 (roughly the implicit level in spherical QRPA), only about 70% of the full quadrupole correlations are incorporated in the 82Kr wave function for λ=0; this drops to 57% for λ=1 and 50% for λ=2.
- In the equally deformed mirror case for A=66, the NME in the purely quadrupole interaction limit (λ_qq→∞) is about half the value obtained with no additional quadrupole.
- For the A=26 and A=50 mirror transitions in full major oscillator shells, the NME increases up to 50% at large deformation, unlike the A=66 and A=110 cases.
- The only net effect of UCOM compared to Jastrow short-range correlations is an increase of the Jastrow results by about 20% for both ISM and QRPA.

## Context

This work extends the ISM calculations of Caurier et al. (2004) and Caurier et al. (2007) by systematically studying the interplay between pairing and quadrupole correlations, and it corrects a transformation error in the comparison with QRPA results from Kortelainen et al. (2007) and Suhonen (2008). The finding that seniority truncations become unreliable when quadrupole correlations are large explains why spherical-basis QRPA overestimates NMEs for strongly deformed nuclei like 76Ge, 82Se, 128Te, and 130Te, while both methods agree for weakly deformed cases like 124Sn and 136Xe. The results enable more reliable predictions for future experiments, particularly for the well-deformed candidate 150Nd decaying into the much less deformed 150Sm.
