# Smooth band termination in $^{115}$I

> The cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky formalism is employed to investigate the band structure of $^{115}$I from low to high spin. A set of eight rotational bands with well established spins and parities are analyzed. Five bands start at low spin and are assigned to the single-particle proton orbitals ($d_{5/2}$, $g_{7/2}$), $g_{9/2}$, and $h_{11/2}$, which are close to the Z=53 Fermi surface for a deformation of $\varepsilon_2=0.2$. The analysis of the observed level energies relative to a rotating liquid drop and spins versus $γ$-ray energies allow to track the evolution of the configurations with increasing spin. It is of special interest that one band in the valence space is observed to a smooth termination at $I_{max} = 67/2^+$.

# Smooth band termination in $^{115}$I

## Key claim

One band in the valence space of $^{115}$I is observed to undergo a smooth termination at a maximum spin of $I_{max} = 67/2^+$, which to the authors' knowledge represents the first time such a valence-space band has been observed up to its maximum spin value. The interpretation challenges the more standard explanation of band crossings as paired alignments, instead attributing the sharp crossings in bands 3-6 to a decrease in the number of particles in the high-$j$ $h_{11/2}$ shell.

## Method

The cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) formalism was employed to calculate configuration energies and deformation trajectories for eight rotational bands in $^{115}$I with well-established spins and parities. The analysis used plots of observed level energies relative to a rotating liquid drop ($E - E_{rld}$) versus spin $I$, and spin $I$ versus $\gamma$-ray energy $E_{\gamma}$, to track configuration evolution with increasing spin. Configurations were labeled by the number of particles in low-$j$ and high-$j$ orbitals relative to a $^{100}$Sn core, with deformation optimized in ($\varepsilon_2, \gamma, \varepsilon_4$) for each configuration at each spin value.

## Key numbers

- One band in the valence space terminates smoothly at $I_{max} = 67/2^+$ (band 6, positive parity, negative signature)
- Band 1 (negative parity, $\nu h_{11/2}$ configuration) is observed up to two transitions short of its calculated termination at $I = 39.5$
- Band 2 is observed for spin values $I = 21.5$ to $27.5$ and is assigned to the $[21_-;A2]$ configuration (where A=10 in hexadecimal)
- Band 6 starts at $I = 7/2^+$ at an excitation energy of 56 keV above the $5/2^+$ ground state
- Sharp crossings occur at $I \approx 10$ in bands 4, 5, and 6, and at $I \approx 20$ in bands 3 and 6
- Typical low-spin deformation in $^{115}$I is ($\varepsilon_2 = 0.22$, $\gamma = 0^\circ$)
- The terminating state of band 6 corresponds to $\pi(dg)^2_6(h_{11/2})^1_{5.5} \nu(dg)^{-5}_{10.5}(h_{11/2})^3_{13.5}$, with spin values specified in subscripts

## Context

This work extends the analysis of high-spin states in the $A=115$ mass region, following recent high-statistics experiments with the JUROGAM array that allowed extension of previously known bands and identification of several bands to spin values above $I=30$. The results are compared with the isotone $^{117}$Cs studied by Jodidar et al., where low-spin bands 1, 2, 3, 4 correspond one-to-one to bands 1, 3, 4, 8 of $^{115}$I. The smooth terminating band observed in the valence space is analogous to smooth terminating bands with holes in the proton core previously observed in this region by Ragnarsson (1995) and in the review by Ragnarsson and collaborators, but this is the first valence-space band observed up to its maximum spin value. The interpretation of crossings as a decrease in the number of $h_{11/2}$ particles rather than paired alignments follows the framework developed for $Z=66-70$ nuclei by Bengtsson and Ragnarsson (1985).
