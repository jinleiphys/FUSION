# Search for doubly charmed dibaryons in baryon-baryon scattering

> We perform a systematical investigation of the doubly charmed dibaryon system with quantum numbers $IJ=01$, and strangeness numbers $S=0$, $-2$ and $-4$ in the framework of the chiral quark model. Two resonance states with strangeness numbers $S=-2$ is obtained in the $ΛΩ_{cc}$ scattering channel, which are $Ξ_{cc}^{\ast}Ξ$ with resonance mass 5081 MeV and decay width 0.3 MeV, and the $Ξ_{c}Ξ_{c}^{\ast}$ state with the mass 5213 MeV and decay width 19.8 MeV, respectively. These two predicted charmed dibaryon candidates are worth searching for experimentally. Besides, we would like to emphasize that the multi-channel coupling calculation is important to confirm the existence of multiquark states. The coupling can shift the energy of the resonance, give the width to the resonance and even destroy the resonance. Therefore, to provide the necessary information for experiments to search for exotic hadron states, the coupling calculation between the bound channels and open channels is indispensable.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model predicts two resonance states in the doubly charmed dibaryon system with quantum numbers \(IJ=01\) and strangeness \(S=-2\), but only when multi-channel coupling between bound and open channels is included; without such coupling, the resonances may shift in energy, acquire width, or even disappear entirely.

## Method

The authors employed the chiral quark model extended to SU(4) flavor symmetry, incorporating one-gluon-exchange, confinement, scalar \(\sigma\) meson exchange (between u,d quarks), and Goldstone boson exchange potentials including \(\pi\), \(K\), \(\eta\), \(D\), \(D_s\), and \(\eta_c\) mesons. They used the resonating group method (RGM) to reduce the six-body problem to a two-body scattering problem, and solved for scattering phase shifts via the Kohn-Hulthén-Kato variational method in both single-channel and multi-channel coupling frameworks. Resonance states were identified by rapid phase-shift changes exceeding 90 degrees in the \(\Lambda\Omega_{cc}\) scattering channel.

## Key numbers

- \(\Xi_{cc}^{\ast}\Xi\) resonance: mass = 5081 MeV, decay width = 0.3 MeV, from the \(\Lambda\Omega_{cc}\) scattering channel with strangeness \(S=-2\).
- \(\Xi_{c}\Xi_{c}^{\ast}\) resonance: mass = 5213 MeV, decay width = 19.8 MeV, from the same \(\Lambda\Omega_{cc}\) scattering channel with strangeness \(S=-2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of singly charmed dibaryons (Cui 2024) to the doubly charmed sector, and builds on prior investigations of heavy dibaryons using the one-pion-exchange model (cc1, cc2, cc3), the quark delocalization color screening model (D), QCD sum rules (Wang 2021), and lattice QCD (Junnarkar 2022, Junnarkar 2024). The predicted resonances provide specific candidates for experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb, and the study emphasizes that multi-channel coupling calculations are indispensable for reliably predicting the existence and properties of multiquark states.
