# Methods for analyzing anisotropic flow in relativistic nuclear collisions

> The strategy and techniques for analyzing anisotropic flow (directed, elliptic, etc.) in relativistic nuclear collisions are presented. The emphasis is on the use of the Fourier expansion of azimuthal distributions. We present formulae relevant for this approach, and in particular, show how the event multiplicity enters into the event plane resolution. We also discuss the role of non-flow correlations and a method for introducing flow into a simulation.

# Methods for analyzing anisotropic flow in relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim
The paper presents a comprehensive Fourier-based methodology for extracting anisotropic flow coefficients (directed, elliptic, and higher harmonics) from azimuthal particle distributions in relativistic nuclear collisions, with the crucial caveat that the observed flow coefficients must be corrected by dividing by the event plane resolution, which depends on the event multiplicity and the harmonic used for plane determination.

## Method
The method first estimates the reaction plane by constructing an event flow vector Q_n from weighted sums of cos(nφ) and sin(nφ) over detected particles, yielding the event plane angle Ψ_n. Particle azimuthal distributions are then measured with respect to this event plane and expanded in Fourier series, where the coefficients v_n^obs = ⟨cos(n(φ-Ψ_m))⟩ are evaluated. These observed coefficients are corrected to the true reaction plane values by dividing by the event plane resolution ⟨cos(km(Ψ_m-Ψ_r))⟩, which is determined from correlations between independent sub-events and depends on the parameter χ_m = v_m/σ, where σ is inversely proportional to the square root of the event multiplicity. Non-flow correlations (from momentum conservation, resonance decays, jets) are addressed through analysis of correlations perpendicular to the reaction plane and through the parameter c characterizing the non-flow contribution to the Q-vector correlations.

## Key numbers
- The event plane resolution parameter σ² = (1/(2N)) × ⟨w²⟩/⟨w⟩², where N is the number of particles used for the event plane determination and w are weights.
- For weak flow (χ_m ≪ 1), the resolution ⟨cos(m(Ψ_m-Ψ_r))⟩ ≈ √(π/8) × χ_m, where χ_m = v_m√(2N) for number flow.
- For the correlation between sub-events of different harmonics: ⟨cos(2(Ψ₁ᵃ-Ψ₂ᵇ))⟩ ≈ ±(2/π) × ⟨cos(Ψ₁ᵃ-Ψ₁ᵇ)⟩ × √(⟨cos(2(Ψ₂ᵃ-Ψ₂ᵇ))⟩).
- The interpolation formula for k=1 resolution: ⟨cos(m(Ψ_m-Ψ_r))⟩ = 0.626657χ_m - 0.09694χ_m³ + 0.02754χ_m⁴ - 0.002283χ_m⁵, valid for χ_m < 3.
- The interpolation formula for k=2 resolution: ⟨cos(2m(Ψ_m-Ψ_r))⟩ = 0.25χ_m² - 0.011414χ_m³ - 0.034726χ_m⁴ + 0.006815χ_m⁵, valid for χ_m < 3.
- For zero flow, ⟨Q_n²⟩ = ⟨N⟩; with flow, ⟨Q_n²⟩ = ⟨N⟩ + v̄_n²⟨N²⟩.
- Non-flow parameter c ≈ 0.034 ± 0.025 from S+S and S+Au data at multiplicities of about 30-50, compared with v₂ ≈ 0.04-0.05.
- The fraction of events with m|Ψ_mᵃ-Ψ_mᵇ| > π/2 equals e^{-χ_m²/4}/2 for small χ_m.

## Context
This paper formalizes and extends the Fourier expansion method for flow analysis introduced by Voloshin and Zhang (1996) and the sub-event resolution technique from Danielewicz and Odyniec (1985), providing the complete mathematical framework that became the standard for anisotropic flow measurements at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. The work enables systematic extraction of directed (v₁) and elliptic (v₂) flow coefficients corrected for finite multiplicity resolution, and establishes the methodology for studying higher harmonics (n≥3) that became crucial for probing the quark-gluon plasma properties at RHIC and LHC. The paper also provides practical tools for introducing flow into Monte Carlo simulations and for estimating non-flow correlation contributions, which remain active areas of investigation in modern heavy-ion experiments.
