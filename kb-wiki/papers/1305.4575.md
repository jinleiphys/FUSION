# Multichannel parametrization of $\bar K N$ scattering amplitudes and extraction of resonance parameters

> We present results of a new multichannel partial-wave analysis for $\bar K N$ scattering in the c.m.\ energy range 1480 to 2100 MeV. Resonance parameters were extracted by fitting partial-wave amplitudes from all considered channels using a multichannel parametrization that is consistent with $S$-matrix unitarity. The resonance parameters are generally in good agreement with predictions of the Koniuk-Isgur quark model.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new multichannel partial-wave analysis for antikaon-nucleon scattering that extracts resonance parameters using a parametrization consistent with S-matrix unitarity, finding general agreement with the Koniuk-Isgur quark model predictions. The key hedge is that the analysis covers the center-of-mass energy range 1480 to 2100 MeV and includes seven coupled channels, but the reliability depends on the specific partial-wave data sets used from prior analyses.

## Method
The authors performed a multichannel energy-dependent fit using the KSU model, which parametrizes the partial-wave S-matrix as a product of unitary background matrices and a generalized multichannel Breit-Wigner matrix. The fit included amplitudes for seven channels: Kbar N, pi Lambda, pi Sigma, pi Lambda(1520), pi Sigma(1385), Kbar* N, and Kbar Delta, using single-energy amplitudes from the authors' own analyses for the first three channels and published solutions from Cameron (1977-1978) and Litchfield (1974) for the remaining channels. The fit covered the center-of-mass energy range from 1480 to 2170 MeV, with resonance parameters extracted as Breit-Wigner masses and signed square roots of partial widths, with uncertainties propagated using the full error matrix.

## Context
This work extends the KSU model previously used by Manley (2003) and Manoj (2012) for extracting N* and Delta* parameters, now applied to hyperon resonances (Lambda* and Sigma*). It addresses shortcomings of prior partial-wave analyses by Gopal (1977) and others (Armenteros 1969, Baillon 1975, Horn 1975) that used simple energy-dependent parametrizations violating unitarity, by employing a unitary multichannel parametrization that treats all channels equally and includes non-resonant backgrounds. The results enable improved determination of hyperon resonance parameters and can be compared with predictions from the Koniuk-Isgur quark model, potentially resolving the long-standing "missing resonance" problem in baryon spectroscopy.
