# Light-cone QCD predictions for elastic ed-scattering in the intermediate energy region

> The contributions of helicity-flip matrix elements to the deuteron form factors are discussed in the light-cone frame. Normalized $A(Q^2)$, $B(Q^2)$, $G_Q(Q^2)$ and $T_{20}$ are obtained in a simple QCD-inspired model. We find that $G_{+-}^+$ plays an important role in $G_Q(Q^2)$. Our numerical results are consistent with the data in the intermediate energy region.

## Key claim

The helicity-flip matrix elements \(G^+_{+0}\) and \(G^+_{+-}\), though dynamically suppressed by powers of \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/Q\), can contribute significantly to the magnetic and quadrupole form factors of the deuteron in the intermediate energy region because of kinematic enhancement factors \(1/\sqrt{2\eta}\) and \(1/\eta\); neglecting \(G^+_{+-}\) in particular leads to a contradiction with data and the conventional meson-nucleon picture at low \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors model the deuteron electromagnetic form factors in the light-cone frame by first extracting the normalized helicity-zero to zero matrix element \(G^+_{00}\) from a fit to the structure function \(A(Q^2)\) using a QCD-inspired model with a harmonic-oscillator body wave function, an effective vector boson mass \(M_b\), and an effective coupling \(\alpha_{\rm eff}\). They then introduce two parameters \(f\) and \(c\) to model the helicity-flip matrix elements as \(G^+_{+0} = (f/\sqrt{2\eta})\,G^+_{00}\) and \(G^+_{+-} = c\,(f^2/2\eta)\,G^+_{00}\), consistent with pQCD power counting, and determine \(f\) from the zero of \(B(Q^2)\) at \(Q_0^2 = 1.85\) GeV\(^2\). The resulting expressions for \(G_C\), \(G_M\), and \(G_Q\) are evaluated for various choices of \(c\), with a detailed demonstration for \(c=1\).

## Key numbers

* The zero of the structure function \(B(Q^2)\) occurs at \(Q_0^2 = 1.85\) GeV\(^2\) (corresponding to \(\eta_0 = 0.13\)), used to determine the parameter \(f\).
* The fitted model parameters for \(G^+_{00}\) are: effective vector boson mass \(M_b = 0.5\) GeV, oscillator parameter \(\alpha = 0.21\) GeV, and effective coupling \(\alpha_{\rm eff} = g^2_{\rm eff}/4\pi = 0.15\) (reduced to 0.11 when helicity-flip corrections are included).
* The charge form factor \(G_C\) has a node at \(Q^2 = 0.75\) GeV\(^2\), consistent with conventional meson-nucleon predictions.
* If the parameter \(c > 0.43\), the quadrupole form factor \(G_Q\) remains positive for all momentum transfers in the model.
* The parameter \(f\) is found to be around 0.2 from the fits to \(G_Q\) and \(T_{20}\) data.

## Context

This work extends the QCD-inspired model of Cao and Wu (1996) for the deuteron form factor by incorporating helicity-flip matrix elements, which were previously neglected or treated only partially. It addresses the failure of the simple ratio \(G_C : G_M : G_Q = (1 - \frac{2}{3}\eta) : 2 : -1\) predicted by Brodsky and Hiller (1992) under the assumption of \(G^+_{00}\) dominance, showing that kinematic enhancements require the inclusion of \(G^+_{+0}\) and \(G^+_{+-}\) even at moderate \(Q^2\). The results enable a smooth connection between the conventional meson-nucleon picture (Gari and Hyuga, 1976) at low \(Q^2\) and pQCD predictions at high \(Q^2\), and point to the need for future experiments at CEBAF to determine the parameter \(c\) reliably.
