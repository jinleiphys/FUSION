# Polyakov Loop Behavior in Non-Extensive SU(2) Lattice Gauge Theory

> In order to come closer to a realistic model of high-energy collisions, we simulate SU(2) lattice gauge theory under fluctuating temperature. The fluctuations are Euler-Gamma distributed, leading to a canonical state maximizing the Renyi and Tsallis entropy formulas. We test the random lattice spacing method numerically on the Polyakov Loop expectation value. The critical coupling and presumably also the critical deconfinement temperature shifts about 30 per cent to higher values with a realistic degree of fluctuations.

## Key claim

The critical coupling and deconfinement temperature for SU(2) lattice gauge theory shift about 30 per cent higher when temperature fluctuations are modeled with a realistic Euler-Gamma distribution (width parameter c=5.5) compared to the traditional fixed-temperature case, but this shift is a preliminary estimate based on assuming the traditional scaling between coupling and physical temperature.

## Method

The authors simulate SU(2) pure gauge theory on a 10^3 x 2 lattice using a superstatistical Monte Carlo method where the timelike to spacelike lattice spacing ratio theta = a_t/a_s is treated as a random variable drawn from an Euler-Gamma distribution with mean 1 and width 1/sqrt(c). A new theta value is chosen every 5 Metropolis sweeps over the lattice, and the Polyakov loop expectation value is measured as the order parameter for the confinement-deconfinement transition. The reference case c=1024 approximates the traditional fixed-temperature Gibbs distribution, while c=5.5 corresponds to a realistic width inferred from experimental particle spectra.

## Key numbers

- The width parameter c=5.5 corresponds to a relative width of 1/sqrt(c) approximately 0.43 (43 per cent) for the inverse temperature fluctuations.
- The critical inverse coupling shifts from 4/g_c^2 approximately 1.85 for c=1024.0 to 4/g_c^2 approximately 2.12 for c=5.5.
- The deconfinement temperature at c=5.5 is estimated as T_c(5.5) approximately 1.3 times T_c(c=infinity), i.e. about 30 per cent higher.
- The trivial statistical factor from averaging 1/theta gives exv{1/theta} = c/(c-1) approximately 1.22, which accounts for only part of the observed shift.
- The critical coupling shift of 15 per cent in 4/g^2_c leads to about a 30 per cent increase in T_c under the assumed scaling.

## Context

This work extends the superstatistical approach of Beck and Cohen (2003) and the non-extensive thermodynamics framework of Tsallis (1988) to lattice gauge theory simulations, moving beyond the fixed-temperature canonical Monte Carlo methods used in standard lattice QCD studies such as those by Fodor and Katz (2004) and by Petreczky and collaborators. The results suggest that experimental programs like FAIR at GSI should consider a deconfinement temperature about 30 per cent higher than traditional lattice predictions when interpreting high-energy collision data, and that the width parameter c can be extracted from event-by-event particle spectra.
