# Exact correlation functions of the BCS model in the canonical ensemble

> We evaluate correlation functions of the BCS model for finite number of particles. The integrability of the Hamiltonian relates it with the Gaudin algebra ${\cal G}[sl(2)]$. Therefore, a theorem that Sklyanin proved for the Gaudin model, can be applied. Several diagonal and off-diagonal correlators are calculated. The finite size scaling behavior of the pairing correlation function is studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims to provide exact expressions for diagonal and off-diagonal correlation functions of the BCS model in the canonical ensemble for a finite number of particles, using a generating function technique originally developed by Sklyanin for the Gaudin model. The hedge is that the method applies only because the BCS Hamiltonian shares the same underlying Gaudin algebra \( {\cal G}[sl(2)] \) as the Gaudin model, with the difference being a different constraint on the lowest-weight vector of that algebra (the Richardson-Sherman equations for BCS versus the \( g \to \infty \) limit for Gaudin).

## Method

The authors exploit the algebraic connection between the BCS model and the \( sl(2) \) Gaudin model, noting that the BCS integrals of motion \( \tau_j \) are related to the Gaudin Hamiltonians \( \Xi_j \). They extend a theorem by Sklyanin, originally proved for the Gaudin model, which expresses correlation functions of \( su(2) \) operators in terms of a generating function involving coordinated partitions of the sets of spectral parameters. The charge and pairing correlation functions are then obtained as residues of this generating function at the single-particle energies \( 2\varepsilon_j \), using the exact Bethe eigenstates of the BCS Hamiltonian constructed from the Gaudin algebra generators.

## Key numbers

*   The finite-size scaling analysis of the pairing correlation function \( \Psi \) (defined as \( \sum_j \sqrt{1/4 - \langle S^z_j \rangle^2} \)) for systems with 16 electrons (8 pairs) at half-filling reveals a second crossing point at \( g^* = 0.417 \) with scaling exponent \( \eta = 1.028 \), in addition to a previously known crossing point from Mastellone (2000).
*   The data collapse at \( g^* \) yields a scaling exponent \( 1/\nu = 0.15 \).
*   The numerical evaluation of the formulas is limited by the number of partitions, which depends on the number of pairs \( N \) and the order of the correlation function \( |{\cal H}| \), but not on the dimension of the Hilbert space \( \Omega \).

## Context

This work extends the exact results of Richardson (1963) for the diagonal pairing correlation function to both diagonal and off-diagonal correlation functions, filling a gap in the literature. It builds on the integrability of the BCS model established by Cambiaggio (1997) and the generating function technique for the Gaudin model developed by Sklyanin (2000). The results are directly relevant to the study of superconducting fluctuations in small metallic grains, where the canonical ensemble is required, and enable further work on asymptotics of correlation functions and thermal Green functions, as well as refinements using the scheme of Korepin (1990).
