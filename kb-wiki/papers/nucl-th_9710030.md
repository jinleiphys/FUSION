# Influence of pion-delta-hole configuration on the rho meson mass spectrum in dense hadronic matter

> We discuss medium modification of the rho meson originating from its coupling to in medium quasi-pions and delta-hole states. This medium effect leads to a marked structure of the rho meson strength function in the $500 MeV$ invariant mass region. We suggest that this effect may provide an explanation of the structure observed by the DLS collaboration in the $Ca+Ca/C+C$ ratio at $1 GeV/u$.

# Influence of pion-delta-hole configuration on the rho meson mass spectrum in dense hadronic matter

## Key claim
The structure observed by the DLS collaboration in the Ca+Ca/C+C ratio at 1 GeV/u in the 500-600 MeV invariant mass region may be explained, at least qualitatively, by the coupling of the rho meson to intermediate states made of collective pionic modes and transverse delta-hole states, without invoking QCD vacuum modification or partial chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method
The authors calculated the in-medium rho meson propagator by incorporating its self-energy from coupling to longitudinal collective modes (built from pions and delta-hole states) and transverse modes (delta-hole states and rho), including vertex corrections to ensure gauge invariance. The imaginary part of the self-energy was computed using a spectral representation, with the delta width corrected for Pauli blocking and extra 2p-2h contributions. The ratio of the rho meson strength function at two different densities was then compared to the DLS data for Ca+Ca and C+C collisions.

## Key numbers
- The structure in the rho meson strength function appears in the invariant mass region around 500 MeV, arising from the opening of the pion-transverse delta-hole channel at q0 >= m_pi + omega_Delta ~ 500 MeV.
- The short-range correlation parameter g' = 0.5 was used in the pion self-energy dominated by p-wave coupling to delta-hole states.
- The coupling constant g = 5 and form factor parameters were fitted on the pion electromagnetic form factor and I=J=1 pi-pi phase shifts.
- Expected nuclear compressions in heavy ion reactions at 1 GeV/u range from 1 to 2.5 rho_0 depending on system size.
- The ratio of strength functions was taken at two different densities typical of Ca+Ca and C+C reactions, multiplied by the ratio of A_p * A_t values.

## Context
This work extends the conventional many-body approach previously developed by Chanfray, Schuck, and collaborators (1990s) for comparison with earlier DLS data, and later adapted to the CERN/SPS regime by Rapp and collaborators (1990s). It contrasts with the interpretation by Li and collaborators (1990s) that the observed low-mass enhancement in rho meson strength indicates a downward mass shift from QCD vacuum modification and partial chiral symmetry restoration. The mechanism proposed here, coupling to pion-delta-hole states, was previously shown to explain part of the CERES data for S+Au at 200 GeV/u and Pb+Au at 158 GeV/u, and the present work suggests it may also account for the DLS structure in the 500-600 MeV region at lower beam energies. A realistic comparison would require a full dynamical calculation incorporating experimental acceptance.
