# Bayes goes fast: Uncertainty Quantification for a Covariant Energy Density Functional emulated by the Reduced Basis Method

> A covariant energy density functional is calibrated using a principled Bayesian statistical framework informed by experimental binding energies and charge radii of several magic and semi-magic nuclei. The Bayesian sampling required for the calibration is enabled by the emulation of the high-fidelity model through the implementation of a reduced basis method (RBM) - a set of dimensionality reduction techniques that can speed up demanding calculations involving partial differential equations by several orders of magnitude. The RBM emulator we build - using only 100 evaluations of the high-fidelity model - is able to accurately reproduce the model calculations in tens of milliseconds on a personal computer, an increase in speed of nearly a factor of 3,300 when compared to the original solver. Besides the analysis of the posterior distribution of parameters, we present predictions with properly estimated uncertainties for observables not included in the fit, specifically the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and 48Ca, as reported by PREX and CREX collaborations. The straightforward implementation and outstanding performance of the RBM makes it an ideal tool for assisting the nuclear theory community in providing reliable estimates with properly quantified uncertainties of physical observables. Such uncertainty quantification tools will become essential given the expected abundance of data from the recently inaugurated and future experimental and observational facilities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Reduced Basis Method (RBM) emulator, trained on only 100 high-fidelity model evaluations, can accelerate covariant density functional theory calculations by a factor of approximately 3,300 while maintaining sufficient accuracy for Bayesian uncertainty quantification, but this performance is contingent on the absence of level crossings in the single-particle spectrum for the parameter ranges studied.

## Method

The authors constructed a covariant energy density functional based on a relativistic mean-field Lagrangian with nucleons and mesons (sigma, omega, rho, and photon) as effective degrees of freedom. They calibrated the model's eight-dimensional parameter space by sampling bulk nuclear matter properties (saturation density, binding energy, effective mass, incompressibility coefficient, symmetry energy parameters) rather than the bare coupling constants, using Bayesian inference with experimental binding energies and charge radii of ten magic and semi-magic nuclei. The Bayesian sampling was enabled by an RBM emulator built via Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (singular value decomposition) of 50 high-fidelity solutions per nucleus, which projects the Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations onto low-dimensional subspaces for rapid evaluation.

## Key numbers

- The RBM emulator achieved a speed increase of nearly a factor of 3,300 compared to the original solver, computing observables in tens of milliseconds on a personal computer.
- Only 100 evaluations of the high-fidelity model were used to build the emulator across all nuclei studied.
- The high-fidelity solver used a spatial grid of L=20 fm with Δr=0.05 fm, yielding N=400 grid points per function.
- The training set consisted of 50 parameter samples obtained from a previous calibration by Chen (2014).
- The dataset for calibration included 18 experimental data points: 10 binding energies and 8 charge radii from the nuclei 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 68Ni, 90Zr, 100Sn, 116Sn, 132Sn, 144Sm, and 208Pb.
- The proton radius used for charge radius folding was r_p=0.84 fm, from the CODATA 2018 recommended values by Tiesinga (2021).

## Context

This work extends the application of reduced order modeling techniques, specifically the Reduced Basis Method previously demonstrated for nuclear DFT by Bonilla (2022), to enable full Bayesian calibration of a covariant energy density functional. The approach addresses a computational bottleneck identified by the nuclear theory community (Phillips 2021, Furnstahl 2020, Drischler 2021) where direct Bayesian inference is impractical due to the millions of sequential model evaluations required. The calibrated model provides uncertainty-quantified predictions for the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and 48Ca, which are directly comparable to results from the PREX and CREX experimental collaborations (Adhikari 2021, CREX 2022), and enables rapid re-calibration as new experimental data from facilities like FRIB become available.
