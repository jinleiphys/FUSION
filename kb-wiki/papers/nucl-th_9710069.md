# Deconfinement at finite chemical potential

> In a confining, renormalisable, Dyson-Schwinger equation model of two-flavour QCD we explore the chemical-potential dependence of the dressed-quark propagator, which provides a means of determining the behaviour of the chiral and deconfinement order parameters, and low-energy pion observables. We find coincident, first order deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions at μ_c = 375 MeV. f_πis insensitive to μuntil μ\approx μ_0 = 0.7 mu_c when it begins to increase rapidly. m_πis weakly dependent on μ, decreasing slowly with μand reaching a minimum 6% less than its μ=0 value at μ=μ_0. In a two-flavour free-quark gas at μ=μ_c the baryon number density would be approximately 3 ρ_0, where ρ_0=0.16 fm^{-3}; while in such a gas at μ_0 the density is ρ_0.

# Deconfinement at finite chemical potential

## Key claim
In a confining, renormalisable Dyson-Schwinger equation model of two-flavour QCD, the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions are coincident and first order at a critical chemical potential of μ_c = 375 MeV, with the caveat that the model uses a gluon propagator with no explicit μ-dependence, which may be inadequate near the critical point.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed-quark propagator at finite chemical potential using a rainbow truncation (bare vertex) and a Landau-gauge model gluon propagator that includes an infrared singular term to model confinement and a perturbative tail. The model parameters (m_t = 0.69 GeV, m_R = 1.1 MeV, d = 4/9) are fixed by fitting π-meson observables at T=0, μ=0, leaving no free parameters. The deconfinement order parameter κ is extracted from the first zero of the configuration-space Schwinger function, while the chiral order parameter χ is the real part of the scalar self-energy at zero momentum. The bag constant B(μ) is computed as the pressure difference between the Nambu-Goldstone and Wigner-Weyl phases to determine the transition order and critical chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential: μ_c = 375 MeV, where both deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occur via a first-order transition.
- Chiral order parameter at μ_c: χ(μ_c)/χ(0) ≈ 1.2, increasing with μ before dropping discontinuously to zero.
- Pion mass minimum: m_π(μ_0)/m_π(0) ≈ 0.94 at μ_0 = 0.7 μ_c ≈ 260 MeV, a 6% decrease from its μ=0 value.
- Pion decay constant at μ_c: f_π(μ_c⁻)/f_π(0) ≈ 1.25, insensitive to μ until μ ≈ μ_0 then increasing sharply.
- Bag constant at μ=0: B(0) = (0.104 GeV)⁴.
- Baryon number density gauge: a two-flavour free-quark gas at μ_c would have ρ_B ≈ 2.9 ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³; at μ_0 the density would be ρ_0.
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit for quark pressure is reached at μ ∼ 5 μ_c.

## Context
This work extends the finite-temperature Dyson-Schwinger equation study of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration in two-flavour QCD by Bender, Blaschke, Roberts, and Schmidt (1996), which found coincident second-order transitions at T_c ≈ 150 MeV with critical exponent β = 0.33 ± 0.03. At finite chemical potential the transition becomes first order, with μ_c = 375 MeV, approximately 30% larger than the value obtained in an earlier study by Bender, Roberts, and Schmidt (1996) that omitted the perturbative tail of the gluon propagator. The results enable calculation of pion properties in dense matter and provide a continuum QCD benchmark for lattice studies, which currently lack reliable finite-density algorithms. The anticorrelation between f_π and m_π as functions of μ contradicts the conjecture of Brown (1990).
