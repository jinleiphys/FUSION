# Type-I superconductivity and neutron star precession

> Type-I proton superconducting cores of neutron stars break up in a magnetic field into alternating domains of superconducting and normal fluids. We examine two channels of superfluid-normal fluid friction where (i) rotational vortices are decoupled from the non-superconducting domains and the interaction is due to the strong force between protons and neutrons; (ii) the non-superconducting domains are dynamically coupled to the vortices and the vortex motion generates transverse electric fields within them, causing electronic current flow and Ohmic dissipation. The obtained dissipation coefficients are consistent with the Eulerian precession.

# Type-I superconductivity and neutron star precession

## Key claim

The dissipation coefficients derived for two channels of superfluid-normal fluid friction in type-I proton superconducting cores of neutron stars are consistent with undamped Eulerian precession, provided the drag-to-lift ratio ζ satisfies the condition (I_S/I_N)ζ < 1, which holds for both mechanisms studied.

## Method

The paper examines two distinct models of friction between superfluid neutrons and normal fluid in a type-I superconducting proton core: (i) a "1+1" model where each rotational neutron vortex carries a single coaxial normal domain, with dissipation arising from Ohmic heating due to transverse electric fields generated by vortex motion through the domain; (ii) a "1+N" model where multiple neutron vortices are accommodated by a single normal domain, with dissipation due to scattering of normal protons off quasiparticles confined in neutron vortex cores. The friction coefficients are computed from the electrical conductivity of the electron Fermi-liquid in normal proton matter (1+1 model) and from neutron-proton scattering relaxation times (1+N model), then compared to the Sedrakian, Wasserman, and Cordes (SWC) no-go theorem for precession.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium domain size d ~ 3.2 × 10⁻³ cm, derived from core size L ≃ 5 × 10⁵ cm and coherence length ξ ≃ 200 fm
- Ratio of superfluid to normal domain sizes d_S/d_N = √(H_cm/B) ~ 10, where B ~ 10¹² G is average magnetic induction and H_cm ~ 10¹⁴ G is thermodynamic magnetic field
- Drag-to-lift ratio ζ ~ 10⁻⁴–10⁻³ for the 1+1 model at T = 10⁸ K (Table I: ζ = 0.39 × 10⁻⁴, 0.36 × 10⁻⁴, 0.88 × 10⁻⁴ for densities 7.91, 8.31, 8.56 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ respectively)
- Drag-to-lift ratio ζ ~ 10⁻² for the 1+N model at T = 10⁸ K (Table II: ζ = 7.95 × 10⁻², 1.86 × 10⁻², 1.42 × 10⁻² for densities 0.40, 0.60, 0.80 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ respectively), reduced by factor (d_S/d_N)² ≃ 100
- Lower bound on moment of inertia ratio I_S/I_N ≥ 0.1 from glitch observations

## Context

This work extends the SWC no-go theorem (Sedrakian, Wasserman, and Cordes, 1999) which states that Eulerian precession in a superfluid neutron star is impossible if (I_S/I_N)ζ > 1. It addresses the discrepancy raised by Link (2003) that long-term timing variations attributed to precession are incompatible with type-II superconductivity, for which type-I superconductivity was proposed as a resolution. The paper provides the first dynamical treatment of type-I superconducting domains in neutron star interiors, building on the equilibrium structure theory of Sedrakian, Shahabasyan, and Zhitnitsky (2003) and the domain size estimates of Buckley, Metlitski, and Zhitnitsky (2004). The results enable future work on whether Tkachenko oscillations of the vortex lattice could also explain observed long-term periodicities without being damped by mutual friction.
