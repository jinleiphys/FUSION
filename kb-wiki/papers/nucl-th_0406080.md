# Ab initio calculation of Li7 photodisintegration

> The Li7 total photoabsorption cross section is calculated microscopically. As nucleon-nucleon interaction the semi-realistic central AV4' potential with S- and P-wave forces is taken. The interaction of the final 7-nucleon system is fully taken into account via the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method. For the calculation of the LIT we use expansions in hyperspherical harmonics (HH) in conjunction with the HH effective interaction (EIHH) approach. The convergence of the LIT expansion is discussed in detail. The calculated cross section agrees quite well with the available experimental data, which cover an energy range from threshold up to 100 MeV.

## Key claim
The total photoabsorption cross section of ⁷Li, calculated microscopically using the semi-realistic central AV4' nucleon-nucleon potential and the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, agrees quite well with experimental data from threshold to 100 MeV, but the authors note that a new measurement with higher precision would be desirable to determine whether a more realistic nuclear force including a three-nucleon force is needed.

## Method
The authors computed the inclusive unpolarized response function for ⁷Li photodisintegration using the LIT method, which avoids explicit calculation of final-state wave functions by solving a bound-state-like problem. The ground state and channel wave functions were expanded in hyperspherical harmonics (HH) with the effective interaction HH (EIHH) approach, using the AV4' potential that includes S- and P-wave central forces. The LIT was evaluated via the Lanczos algorithm for the six dipole-allowed channels, and the total cross section was obtained by inverting the LIT and summing channel contributions.

## Key numbers
- Ground state binding energy of ⁷Li: 45.28 MeV with K⁰_max = 9; increasing to K⁰_max = 11 changes the binding energy by only 0.05 MeV.
- Ground state expectation value of the operator D†_z D_z: 1.877 fm² with K⁰_max = 9; the symmetry-truncated expansion up to K_max = 10 gives 1.871 fm², a difference of 0.3%.
- Estimated total uncertainty from HH expansion truncation: about 5%.
- Theoretical threshold for the T = 1/2 channel (⁷Li + γ → ⁴He + t): 4.70 MeV (experimental threshold is 2.47 MeV).
- Theoretical threshold for the T = 3/2 channel (⁷Li + γ → ⁶He + p): 12.41 MeV (experimental threshold is 9.975 MeV).
- For channel C=3 (the dominant T=1/2 channel) at K_max = 10, the number of HH basis states was reduced from 190,440 to 111,900 by symmetry truncation, with an estimated error below 0.5%.

## Context
This work extends the combined LIT and EIHH approach, previously applied to A=6 nuclei (Bacca et al., 2002, 2004), to the first microscopic calculation of a photoabsorption reaction on a nucleus with A=7. It contradicts the traditional limitation of such calculations to A ≤ 3 by demonstrating that the LIT method can handle the full final-state interaction for 7-nucleon systems. The results enable future tests of whether the semi-realistic AV4' potential is sufficient or whether more realistic forces including three-nucleon forces are needed, and they motivate new high-precision measurements of the ⁷Li total photoabsorption cross section.
