# Lensing of Dirac monopole in Berry's phase

> Berry's phase, which is associated with the slow cyclic motion with a finite period, looks like a Dirac monopole when seen from far away but smoothly changes to a dipole near the level crossing point in the parameter space in an exactly solvable model. This topology change of Berry's phase is visualized as a result of lensing effect; the monopole supposed to be located at the level crossing point appears at the displaced point when the variables of the model deviate from the precisely adiabatic movement. The effective magnetic field generated by Berry's phase is determined by a simple geometrical consideration of the magnetic flux coming from the displaced Dirac monopole.

## Key claim

Berry's phase, which appears as a Dirac monopole in the precise adiabatic limit, smoothly changes to a dipole near the level crossing point when the period of cyclic motion is finite; this topology change is mathematically equivalent to the Dirac monopole drifting away from the level crossing point by a distance \(\pi/\mu T\) in parameter space, an effect the authors call "lensing."

## Method

The authors analyze an exactly solvable model of a magnetic moment \(\mu\hbar\vec{\sigma}\) in a rotating magnetic field \(\vec{B}(t)=B(\sin\theta\cos\varphi(t),\sin\theta\sin\varphi(t),\cos\theta)\) with \(\varphi(t)=\omega t\). They derive the exact solution of the Schrödinger equation and compute the Berry connection and the effective magnetic field \(\mathcal{B}=\nabla\times\mathcal{A}\) in the parameter space \(\vec{B}\). They then show that this field is identical to the field of a Dirac monopole displaced from the origin by a distance \(B/\eta = \pi/\mu T\), where \(\eta = \mu BT/\pi\) and \(T=2\pi/\omega\) is the period.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(\eta = \mu BT/\pi\) controls the topology: \(\eta > 1\) corresponds to a monopole, \(\eta = 1\) to a half-monopole, and \(\eta < 1\) to a dipole.
- The Dirac string end point is located at \(B = \pi/\mu T\) and \(\theta = \pi\).
- The effective magnetic field in the parameter space is \(\mathcal{B} = \frac{e_M}{4\pi}\frac{\eta^2}{B^2}\frac{1}{(1+\eta^2+2\eta\cos\theta)^{3/2}}[(\eta+\cos\theta)\mathbf{e}_B - \sin\theta\,\mathbf{e}_\theta]\) with \(e_M = 2\pi\hbar\).
- In the adiabatic limit \(\eta \to \infty\), the field reduces to the Dirac monopole field \(\mathcal{B} = \frac{e_M}{4\pi}\frac{1}{B^2}\mathbf{e}_B\).
- At the origin \(B=0\) with fixed finite \(T\) (\(\eta \to 0\)), the field approaches a constant: \(\mathcal{B} = \frac{e_M}{4\pi}(\frac{\mu T}{\pi})^2[\cos\theta\,\mathbf{e}_B - \sin\theta\,\mathbf{e}_\theta]\).
- The total magnetic flux through a small circle around the Dirac string at \(\theta=\pi\) is \(\frac{e_M}{2}(1-\cos\Theta(\pi,\eta))\), which equals \(e_M = 2\pi\hbar\) for \(\eta>1\) and vanishes for \(\eta<1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis in DF1 (2020) of the topology change in Berry's phase from a monopole to a dipole in an exactly solvable model. It provides a more explicit representation of the magnetic field generated by Berry's phase and reveals a geometrical interpretation: the monopole appears displaced from the level crossing point by a distance \(\pi/\mu T\) when the precise adiabatic condition (Simon, 1983) is violated by finite period \(T\). This lensing picture connects to applications of Berry's phase in momentum space, such as the anomalous Hall effect (Jungwirth, 2002) and the spin Hall effect (Hirsch, 1999), where the effective Hamiltonian \(\hat{H} = -\mu \vec{\sigma}\cdot\vec{p}(t)\) is analogous to the model studied here.
