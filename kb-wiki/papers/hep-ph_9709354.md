# Pion--Nucleon Scattering in a New Approach to Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study pion--nucleon scattering with a chiral lagrangian of pions, nucleons, and $Δ$-isobars. The scattering amplitude is evaluated to one-loop $Q^3$ order, where $Q$ is a generic small momentum, using a new approach which is equivalent to heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We obtain a good fit to the experimental phase shifts for pion center-of-mass kinetic energies up to 100 MeV. A sigma term greater than 45 MeV is favored, but the value is not well determined.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a good fit to experimental pion-nucleon phase shifts up to 100 MeV center-of-mass kinetic energy using a one-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation at order Q^3 that includes explicit Delta-isobar degrees of freedom, but the pion-nucleon sigma term is only loosely constrained to be greater than 45 MeV rather than precisely determined.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian including pions, nucleons, and Delta-isobar fields organized by powers of momentum Q, then evaluate all one-loop diagrams contributing to pion-nucleon scattering at order Q^3. They separate loop contributions into "soft" parts (kept explicitly) and "hard" parts (absorbed into Lagrangian parameters), using a covariant Q/M expansion of baryon propagators that preserves power counting and yields results equivalent to heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The calculated phase shifts are fitted to experimental data for pion kinetic energies up to 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The fit to experimental phase shifts covers pion center-of-mass kinetic energies up to 100 MeV
- The pion decay constant used is f_pi approximately 93 MeV
- The pion-nucleon sigma term is favored to be greater than 45 MeV, but the value is not well determined
- The calculation is performed to one-loop order Q^3, where Q is a generic small momentum scale

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of Mojzis (1997), who computed the full pion-nucleon amplitude to order Q^3 using only pion and nucleon fields, by explicitly including the Delta-isobar as a dynamical degree of freedom to better describe scattering up to the Delta resonance region. The new approach for separating hard and soft contributions is equivalent to heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory as developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) and provides a systematic power counting scheme. The results enable improved tests of chiral perturbation theory against pion-nucleon phase shift data and further investigation of the pion-nucleon sigma term, though its precise value remains undetermined.
