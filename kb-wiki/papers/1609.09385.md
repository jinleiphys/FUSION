# Static critical fluctuations on the freeze-out surface

> In this proceeding, we summarize the main results of our recent paper, which introduces a freeze-out scheme to the dynamical models near the QCD critical point. Within such framework of static critical fluctuations, the Beam Energy Scan (BES) data of $C_4$ and $κσ^2$ for net protons within different $p_T$ ranges can be roughly described. Besides, the momentum acceptance dependence of higher cumulants at lower collision energies can also be qualitatively described. However, $C_2$ and $C_3$ are always over-predicted due to the positive static critical fluctuations.

# Static critical fluctuations on the freeze-out surface

## Key claim
The paper introduces a freeze-out scheme for dynamical models near the QCD critical point that can roughly describe Beam Energy Scan data for the fourth-order cumulant $C_4$ and the ratio $\kappa\sigma^2$ of net protons, but always over-predicts $C_2$ and $C_3$ because static critical fluctuations provide positive contributions to those lower-order cumulants.

## Method
The authors assume that protons emitted from the fireball near the critical point follow static statistics with a variable effective mass induced by coupling to the fluctuating $\sigma$ field. They expand the proton distribution function to leading order in the $\sigma$ field, compute correlators of the distribution function using the $\sigma$ field probability distribution with cubic and quartic terms, and integrate these correlators over the freeze-out surface obtained from the hydrodynamic code VISH2+1. The critical point is set close to the chemical freeze-out point at 19.6 GeV, with parameters $g$, $\xi$, $\lambda_3$, and $\lambda_4$ tuned according to effective model ranges and monotonicity.

## Key numbers
- $C_4$ data for net protons in 0-5% Au+Au collisions within $0.4 < p_T < 0.8$ GeV and $0.4 < p_T < 2$ GeV can be roughly described within error bars by the model calculations.
- $\kappa\sigma^2 = C_4/C_2$ data for net protons in 0-5% Au+Au collisions can be roughly described by the model, except $S\sigma = C_3/C_2$ at lower collision energies within $p_T$ range (0.4, 2) GeV.
- At collision energies higher than 30 GeV, critical fluctuations become approximately zero for different $p_T$ ranges.
- The critical fluctuations scale as $\left\langle(\delta N)^n\right\rangle_c \sim N^n$, leading to dramatic increases in cumulants as $p_T$ acceptance is enlarged at lower collision energies.

## Context
This work extends the framework of Stephanov (2009) for critical fluctuations near the QCD critical point by introducing a freeze-out scheme that had been lacking in earlier dynamical models such as those by Paech (2003). The approach enables comparison with Beam Energy Scan data from STAR (Aggarwal 2010, Adamczyk 2013, Luo 2015) for net proton cumulants, but the over-prediction of $C_2$ and $C_3$ indicates that a treatment of dynamical critical fluctuations, rather than static ones, is needed to simultaneously describe all cumulants.
