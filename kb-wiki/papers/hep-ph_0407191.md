# Vector meson dominance pion electromagnetic form factor with the sigma-model loop corrections

> A model is developed for electromagnetic form factor of the pion. One-loop corrections are included in the linear sigma-model. The rho-meson contribution is added in an extended VMD model. The form factor, calculated without fitting parameters, is in a good agreement with experiment for space-like and time-like photon momenta. Loop corrections to the two-pion hadronic contribution a^{(had, π)}_μto the muon anomalous magnetic moment are calculated. The optimal value of the sigma-meson mass appears to be close to the rho-meson mass.

# Vector meson dominance pion electromagnetic form factor with the sigma-model loop corrections

## Key claim

The pion electromagnetic form factor calculated in the linear sigma-model with one-loop corrections and an extended VMD rho-meson contribution, without any fitting parameters, agrees well with experimental data for both space-like and time-like photon momenta; however, the sigma-meson mass is a free parameter, and the optimal value that reproduces the experimental two-pion contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is close to the rho-meson mass (approximately 785 MeV).

## Method

The authors constructed a Lagrangian combining the linear sigma-model (with nucleon, pion, and sigma fields) and an extended VMD model for the rho-meson, where rho coupling to pions and nucleons is introduced via gauge-covariant derivatives and the direct gamma-rho coupling has gauge-invariant form. One-loop corrections from pion, sigma, and nucleon intermediate states were calculated using dimensional regularization and renormalized via counterterms, with the rho-meson self-energy and gamma-rho vertex also receiving loop corrections from pion and nucleon loops. The pion form factor was then computed as the sum of the sigma-model loop contribution and the rho-meson contribution, with all parameters fixed either from chiral relations (Goldberger-Treiman) or from experimental decay widths (rho to pi pi, rho to e+ e-, omega to e+ e-).

## Key numbers

- The rho-meson mass used is m_rho = 768.5 MeV, and the omega-meson mass is m_omega = 782.57 MeV (from PDG).
- The bare rho-meson mass (before self-energy corrections) is hat_m_rho = 795 MeV.
- The rho to pi pi decay width Gamma_rho_to_pi_pi = 150.7 MeV gives |g_rho(m_rho^2)| = 6.05; the real part is Re g_rho(m_rho^2) = 6.036 and the imaginary part is Im g_rho(m_rho^2) = 0.405.
- The rho to e+ e- decay width Gamma_rho_to_e+e- = 6.77 KeV gives |f_rho(m_rho^2)| approximately 5.03; the real part is Re f_rho(m_rho^2) = 4.96 and the imaginary part is Im f_rho(m_rho^2) = -0.82.
- The two-pion contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment a_mu^(had,pi) in the sigma-model+VMD calculation is 4763 x 10^{-11} (integration limit 0.8 GeV^2), compared to 4774 +/- 51 x 10^{-11} from the fit of Troconiz (2001).
- The sigma-model loop corrections increase the low-energy part of a_mu^(had,pi) by about 2 percent compared to sQED+VMD.
- The sigma-meson mass that reproduces the experimental integral lies in the interval 720 to 850 MeV, with central value 785 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the extended VMD model of Klingl (1996) by incorporating one-loop corrections from the linear sigma-model of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960), including nucleon loops which were not considered in that prior work. The calculation enables estimation of model-dependent uncertainties in the radiative correction process e+ e- to pi+ pi- gamma, which is relevant for the muon g-2 measurement from the Brookhaven E821 experiment. The authors find that the sigma-meson mass is degenerate with the rho-meson mass, supporting the hypothesis of Weinberg (1990). Future work is planned to include loops with intermediate rho-meson and axial-vector a1-meson in gauged sigma-model or chiral quantum hadrodynamics approaches.
