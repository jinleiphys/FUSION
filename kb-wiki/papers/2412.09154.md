# High-energy Coulomb scattering of spatially extended particles

> We analyze pure Coulomb high-energy elastic scattering of charged particles (hadrons or nuclei), discarding their strong interactions. We distinguish three scattering modes, determined by the magnitude of the momentum transfer, in which particles behave as point-like, structureless extended, and structured composite objects. The results are compared in the potential and QFT approaches of the eikonal model. In the case of proton Coulomb scattering at the LHC the difference between these two approaches is significant. This indicates the unsuitability of the potential approach. However, in the case of Coulomb scattering of heavy nuclei, the leading one is the optical approximation, which formally reproduces the prescription of the potential approach.

# High-energy Coulomb scattering of spatially extended particles

## Key claim

The paper argues that the potential-eikonal approach is unsuitable for describing Coulomb scattering of protons at LHC energies because it yields cross sections that differ from the QFT-based eikonal approach by up to 19% in the momentum-transfer region where Coulomb-nuclear interference is significant; however, for heavy nuclei such as lead, the optical approximation formally reproduces the potential approach and the two methods agree to within 1%.

## Method

The author compares two formulations of the eikonal model for pure Coulomb elastic scattering: the potential approach, where the eikonal phase is derived from a classical interaction potential and form factors enter directly into the phase, and the QFT approach, where the Born amplitude is defined by one-photon exchange and form factors appear as multiplicative factors in the full amplitude after averaging over initial and final state wave functions. For composite particles, the paper generalizes Glauber's multiple-scattering theory to Coulomb forces, deriving the scattering amplitude for nucleus-nucleus scattering by treating protons as independent scattering centers and applying the optical approximation twice with linearization in the fine-structure constant.

## Key numbers

- For proton-proton scattering at LHC energies, the discrepancy between the potential and QFT approaches reaches approximately 19% at the right end of the region |t| < 10^{-2} GeV^2, and is 6% at |t| = 0.002 GeV^2 where Coulomb contributions begin to dominate over strong interactions.
- For Pb-Pb Coulomb scattering in the region |t| less than or approximately 2 x 10^{-4} GeV^2, the difference between the two approaches is less than 1%.
- The proton form factor is parameterized as a dipole with Lambda = sqrt(0.71) GeV, and the transition to the structureless extended scattering mode for protons occurs at q^2 less than or approximately 10^{-2} GeV^2.
- For heavy nuclei, the optical approximation gives an accuracy of about 2-3% for the number of scattering centers from 10 or more.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on how to incorporate electromagnetic form factors in Coulomb scattering at high energies, referencing prior formulations by Bethe, Islam (1967), Cahn, and Petrov (2018, 2024) for the potential approach, and by Cheng and Wu and Levy and Sucher for the QFT eikonal method. The paper generalizes Glauber's (1959) multiple-scattering theory, originally developed for strong interactions of a point particle off a nucleus, to the case of Coulomb scattering of two composite objects, and shows that the optical approximation of Czyz and Formanek becomes the leading contribution for heavy nuclei due to the smallness of the fine-structure constant. The results enable improved treatment of Coulomb-nuclear interference in proton-proton scattering at the LHC and provide a consistent framework for describing Coulomb scattering of heavy ions.
