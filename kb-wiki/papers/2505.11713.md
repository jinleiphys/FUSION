# Probing Nuclear Geometry through Multi-Particle Azimuthal Correlations and Rapidity-Even Dipolar Flow in ${}^{16}$O+${}^{16}$O Collisions

> We study symmetric and asymmetric cumulants as well as rapidity-even dipolar flow in ${}^{16}$O+${}^{16}$O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$~GeV to explore $α$-clustering phenomena in light nuclei within the viscous relativistic hydrodynamics framework. Signatures of $α$-clustering manifest in the anisotropic flow coefficients and their correlations -- particularly in observables involving elliptic-triangular flow correlations. We show that final-state symmetric and asymmetric cumulants -- especially $\mathrm{NSC}(2,3)$ and $\mathrm{NAC}_{2,1}(2,3)$ -- are sensitive to the initial nuclear geometry. Additionally, we observe a significant difference in rapidity-even dipolar flow, $v_1^{\text{even}}$, between $α$-clustered and Woods--Saxon configurations in high-multiplicity events. These findings underscore the pivotal role of nuclear structure in heavy-ion collision dynamics and provide observables for distinguishing nuclear geometries, particularly in ultra-central collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(2,3) and normalized asymmetric cumulant NAC_{2,1}(2,3) can discriminate between Woods-Saxon and tetrahedral alpha-clustered configurations of oxygen-16 in ultra-central collisions, but this discriminatory power is lost in mid-central and peripheral collisions due to insufficient flow in small systems.

## Method

The authors used the TRENTo model to generate initial entropy density profiles for oxygen-16 collisions at 200 GeV, employing both a three-parameter Fermi (Woods-Saxon) distribution and three tetrahedral alpha-cluster configurations with varying cluster compactness (Cl. I, Cl. II, Cl. III) while keeping the root-mean-square radius fixed at 2.73 fm. The hydrodynamic evolution was performed using the MUSIC code with shear viscosity to entropy density ratios of 0.08 and 0.12, and a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity, starting at an initial time of 0.4 fm/c. They computed normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(2,3) and NSC(2,4), normalized asymmetric cumulants NAC_{2,1}(2,3), and rapidity-even dipolar flow v_1^{even} from the final-state particle distributions using pseudorapidity ranges of [-0.5, 0.5] and transverse momentum ranges of [0.01, 3.0] GeV.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square radius of oxygen-16 fixed to 2.73 fm, taken from nuclear structure experiments (DeVries 1987)
- For Cl. I (alpha cluster): side length l = 3.0 fm, cluster radius r_alpha = 2.0 fm, ratio r_alpha/l = 0.67
- For Cl. II (alpha cluster): side length l = 3.6 fm, cluster radius r_alpha = 1.6 fm, ratio r_alpha/l = 0.44
- For Cl. III (alpha cluster): side length l = 4.0 fm, cluster radius r_alpha = 1.2 fm, ratio r_alpha/l = 0.30
- Woods-Saxon parameters: R = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, omega = -0.051
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.4 fm/c
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon_f = 0.26 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to approximately 151 MeV
- In 0-0.1% centrality, NSC(2,3) for Cl. I, Cl. II, and Cl. III are enhanced by more than 100%, 300%, and 400% compared to Woods-Saxon in the initial state
- In 0-1% centrality, final-state NSC(2,3) for Cl. I, Cl. II, and Cl. III are enhanced by about 37%, 98%, and 123% compared to Woods-Saxon
- In 0-0.1% centrality, NAC_{2,1}(2,3) for Cl. I, Cl. II, and Cl. III are enhanced by around 80%, 180%, and 230% compared to Woods-Saxon

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetric and asymmetric cumulants in heavy-ion collisions such as Au+Au and Pb+Pb using hydrodynamic and transport models (Zhu 2016, Schenke 2019, Mordasini 2019, Taghavi 2020, Hirvonen 2022, Magdy 2022, Magdy 2024, Gardim 2016, Nasim 2016) and experimental measurements by STAR at RHIC and ALICE at LHC. The authors build on their earlier work (Shafi 2024) showing that NSC(2,3) is insensitive to hydrodynamic transport coefficients and late-stage hadronic interactions, making it an excellent probe for the initial state. The findings enable future experimental discrimination of nuclear geometries in oxygen-oxygen collisions at RHIC, particularly through the NAC_{2,1}(2,3) observable which shows even narrower systematic uncertainty bands than NSC(2,3).
