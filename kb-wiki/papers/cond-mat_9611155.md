# Quantum Electrodynamics of Internal Source X-Ray Holographies: Bremsstrahlung, Fluorescence, and Multiple Energy X-Ray Holography

> Quantum electrodynamics (qed) is used to derive the differential cross sections measured in the three new experimental internal source ensemble x-ray holographies: bremsstrahlung (BXH), fluorescence (XFH), and multiple-energy (MEXH) x-ray holography. The polarization dependence of the BXH cross section is also obtained. For BXH, we study analytically and numerically the possible effects of the virtual photons and electrons which enter qed calculations in summing over the intermediate states. For the low photon and electron energies used in the current experiments, we show that the virtual intermediate states produce only very small effects. This is because the uncertainty principle limits the distance that the virtual particles can propagate to be much shorter than the separation between the regions of high electron density in the adjacent atoms. We also find that using the asymptotic form of the scattering wave function causes about a 5-10% error for near forward scattering.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for real atoms in real solids, classical electrodynamics works very well for internal source x-ray holographies because the uncertainty principle limits the propagation distance of virtual photons and electrons to be much shorter than the interatomic spacing, but a full quantum electrodynamic derivation is still necessary to properly derive the equations for analyzing the holograms.

## Method

The authors used quantum electrodynamics (QED) to derive differential cross sections for bremsstrahlung x-ray holography (BXH), fluorescence x-ray holography (XFH), and multiple-energy x-ray holography (MEXH), starting from Feynman diagrams for the relevant processes. For BXH specifically, they computed the reference amplitude from Born bremsstrahlung terms and the object amplitude from Compton scattering terms, then evaluated the separated atom approximation by analyzing the virtuality of intermediate photons and the propagation of virtual particles between atoms.

## Key numbers

- The modulation of intensity in x-ray holograms is 1-5 × 10^{-3}, about 100 times weaker than the modulation in electron holograms (1-5 × 10^{-1})
- Using the asymptotic form of the scattering wave function causes about a 5-10% error for near forward scattering
- A conventional 400 watt x-ray source produces about 10^{13} short wavelength bremsstrahlung photons per second into 4π steradians
- The bremsstrahlung energies considered for experiments are 40-60 keV
- Neglecting certain spin-dependent terms in the bremsstrahlung amplitude produces an approximately constant 10% reduction in the cross section over the kinematic region of interest

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundation for internal source x-ray holographies beyond the simple wave picture that had been used in most prior work, such as Barton's (1988) original holographic inversion procedure based on Helmholtz-Kirchoff inversion for classical scalar waves. The authors connect their QED treatment to the separated scatterer approximation used in high energy hadron-nuclear scattering experiments (references cited as SSA1 through SSA4). The paper enables experimental implementation of bremsstrahlung x-ray holography by showing that classical electrodynamic expressions are valid for real solids, while providing the correct QED-derived equations needed for analyzing holograms.
