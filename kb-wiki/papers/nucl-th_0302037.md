# Effect of gluon-exchange pair-currents on the ratio G(E(P))/G(M(P))

> The effect of one-gluon-exchange (OGE) pair-currents on the ratio $μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ for the proton is investigated within a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (CQM) starting from $SU(6) \times O(3)$ nucleon wave functions, but with relativistic corrections. We found that the OGE pair-currents are important to reproduce well the ratio $μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p$. With the assumption that the OGE pair-currents are the driving mechanism for the violation of the scaling law we give a prediction for the ratio $μ_n G_E^n/G_M^n$ of the neutron.

## Key claim

Within a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) with relativistic boost corrections, one-gluon-exchange (OGE) pair-currents are necessary to reproduce the experimentally observed decrease of the proton ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p from unity, and the authors predict that this ratio will cross zero at Q^2 ~ 8.1 GeV^2, implying a negative value at the planned JLAB measurement at Q^2 ~ 9 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors start from SU(6) × O(3) nucleon wave functions in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a harmonic oscillator confinement potential and a residual OGE interaction. They compute the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M from the sum of one-body quark currents and two-body OGE pair-currents (derived from the qq potential by minimal substitution), then apply a semiclassical Lorentz boost prescription to obtain relativistic form factors in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- The ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p is calculated for quark mass m_q = 400 MeV and quark core radius b = 0.5 fm, with the strong coupling constant α_s varied; the best description of data is obtained with α_s = 0.4.
- The ratio μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p is predicted to cross zero at Q^2 ~ 8.1 GeV^2.
- For the γ q q̅ vertex, a dipole form factor with cutoff Λ_{γ q q̅} = 770 MeV (ρ-meson mass) is used as an alternative to pointlike interaction.
- Good description of the data can also be found for quark masses m_q ~ 313–400 MeV, bag radii b ~ 0.4–0.6 fm, and α_s ~ 0.2–0.6.
- An approximate analytic relation is given: μ_n G_E^n/G_M^n ≃ (2/3) (1 − μ_p G_E^p/G_M^p).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Grabmayr and Kaskulov on interaction currents in nucleon electromagnetic properties, and addresses the discrepancy between the scaling law G_E^p = G_M^p/μ_p (historically extracted from Rosenbluth separation) and the new polarization-transfer experiments from Milbrath (1999), Jones (2000), Gayou (2001), and Gayou (2002) up to Q^2 ~ 5 GeV^2. The authors contrast their approach with relativistic quark models (Miller and Frank, 2002; Cardarelli, 1995) that emphasize Melosh rotation effects, and with the cloudy bag model (Lu, 1998; Miller, 2002) where pion cloud and Poincaré invariance are key. The OGE pair-current mechanism provides a prediction for the neutron ratio G_E^n/G_M^n that can be tested in upcoming polarized-target experiments.
