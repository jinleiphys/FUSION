# Open charm mesons and Charmonia in magnetized strange hadronic matter

> We investigate the in-medium masses of open charm mesons ($D$($D^0$, $D^+$), $\bar{D}$($\bar{D^0}$, $D^-$), $D_s$(${D_{s}}^+$, ${D_{s}}^-$)) and charmonium states ($J/ψ$, $ψ(3686)$, $ψ(3770)$, $χ_{c0}$, $χ_{c2}$) in strongly magnetized isospin asymmetric strange hadronic matter using a chiral effective model. In the presence of the magnetic field, the number density and scalar density of charged baryons have contributions from Landau energy levels. The mass modifications of open charm mesons arise due to their interactions with nucleons, hyperons, and the scalar fields (the non-strange field $σ$, strange field $ζ$ and isovector field $δ$) in the presence of the magnetic field. The mass modifications of the charmonium states arise from the variation of dilaton field ($χ$) in the magnetized medium, which simulates the gluon condensates of QCD. The in-medium mass of open charm mesons and charmonia are observed to decrease with an increase in baryon density, whereas the charged $D^+$, $D^-$, ${D_{s}}^+$ and ${D_{s}}^-$ mesons have additional positive mass shifts due to Landau quantization in the presence of the magnetic field. The effects of strangeness fraction are found to be more dominant for the $\bar{D}$ mesons as compared to the $D$ mesons. The mass shifts of charmonia are observed to be larger in hyperonic medium compared to the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The in-medium masses of open charm mesons and charmonia decrease with increasing baryon density in magnetized strange hadronic matter, but charged mesons ($D^+$, $D^-$, ${D_{s}}^+$, ${D_{s}}^-$) receive additional positive mass shifts from Landau quantization in the magnetic field, and the mass shifts of charmonia are larger in hyperonic medium than in nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors used a chiral effective model based on SU(3)$_L \times$ SU(3)$_R$ symmetry extended to SU(4) to compute in-medium masses of open charm mesons and charmonia in isospin-asymmetric strange hadronic matter under a strong external magnetic field. The scalar fields ($\sigma$, $\zeta$, $\delta$) and dilaton field ($\chi$) were solved self-consistently from coupled equations of motion in the mean-field approximation, with charged baryon number and scalar densities including Landau level summations and anomalous magnetic moments. The open charm meson masses were obtained from dispersion relations incorporating Weinberg-Tomozawa, scalar exchange, and range terms, while charmonium mass shifts were derived from the medium change of the dilaton field simulating gluon condensates, using harmonic oscillator wave functions for the quarkonium states.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: $eB \sim 2 m_\pi^2 \sim 6 \times 10^{18}$ Gauss (RHIC) and $eB \sim 15 m_\pi^2 \sim 10^{19}$ Gauss (LHC)
- Coupling constants used: $g_{\sigma N} = 10.6$, $g_{\zeta N} = -0.47$, $g_{\omega N} = 13.3$, $g_{\rho p} = 5.5$, $g_4 = 79.7$, $g_{\delta p} = 2.5$
- Scalar meson masses: $m_\sigma = 466.5$ MeV, $m_\zeta = 1024.5$ MeV, $m_\delta = 899.5$ MeV
- Hyperon-scalar couplings: $g_{\sigma\Lambda} = 7.52$, $g_{\zeta\Lambda} = 5.8$, $g_{\delta\Lambda} = 0$, $g_{\sigma\Sigma} = 6.13$, $g_{\zeta\Sigma} = 5.8$, $g_{\delta\Sigma^+} = 6.79$
- Charged open charm meson effective mass including lowest Landau level: $m^{eff}_j = \sqrt{m_j^{*2} + |eB|}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy flavor mesons in nuclear matter using the chiral effective model (Mishra et al., 2009; Kumar and Mishra, 2010; Divakar and Mishra, 2014, 2015) by incorporating strong magnetic fields, isospin asymmetry, and strangeness fraction into the hadronic medium. It builds on QCD sum rule investigations of open charm mesons in magnetic fields (Gubler et al., 2016; Cho et al., 2015) and potential model studies of charmonia in magnetic fields (Alford et al., 2012; Bonati et al., 2014; Suzuki et al., 2016; Yoshida et al., 2016). The results enable predictions for experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, as well as for astrophysical compact objects like magnetars where interior magnetic fields may reach $10^{18}$ Gauss.
