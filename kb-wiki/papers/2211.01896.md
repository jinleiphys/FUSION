# $X(3872)$, $X(4014)$, and their bottom partners at finite temperature

> The properties of the $X(3872)$ and its spin partner, the $X(4014)$, are studied both in vacuum and at finite temperature. Using an effective hadron theory based on the hidden-gauge Lagrangian, the $X(3872)$ is dynamically generated from the $s$-wave rescattering of a pair of pseudoscalar and vector charm mesons. By incorporating the thermal spectral functions of open charm mesons, the calculation is extended to finite temperature. Similarly, the properties of the $X(4014)$ are obtained out of the scattering of charm vector mesons. By applying heavy-quark flavor symmetry, the properties of their bottom counterparts in the axial-vector and tensor channels are also predicted. All the dynamically generated states show a decreasing mass and acquire an increasing decay width with temperature, following the trend observed in their meson constituents. These results are relevant in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at high energies, in analyses of the collective medium formed after hadronization or in femtoscopic studies, and can be tested in lattice-QCD calculations exploring the melting of heavy mesons at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The X(3872) and X(4014) are dynamically generated as molecular-type bound states of charmed meson pairs, and their masses decrease while their decay widths increase with temperature; however, this behavior depends on the assumption that these states are purely hadronic molecules generated from the hidden-gauge Lagrangian, and the results for the bottom-sector partners rely on applying heavy-quark flavor symmetry to extrapolate from the charm sector.

## Method

The authors use the local hidden-gauge Lagrangian to construct s-wave scattering amplitudes for pseudoscalar-vector (PV) and vector-vector (VV) meson interactions in the charm sector, considering coupled channels of D meson pairs. They unitarize these amplitudes via a Bethe-Salpeter equation with a cutoff regularization, fixing the cutoff to reproduce the vacuum mass of the X(3872). To extend the calculation to finite temperature, they incorporate thermal spectral functions of the open charm mesons within the imaginary-time formalism, and then apply heavy-quark flavor symmetry to predict the properties of bottom-sector counterparts.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) vacuum mass is reproduced by fixing the cutoff; its vacuum width is given as Gamma_X = 1.19 +/- 0.21 MeV from experimental data.
- The X(4014) is observed by Belle with mass M = 4014.3 +/- 4 +/- 1.5 MeV and width Gamma = 4 +/- 11 +/- 6 MeV.
- The D D* threshold is at 3875.80 MeV; the D_s D_s* threshold is at 4080.54 MeV; the D* D* threshold is at 4017.11 MeV; the D_s* D_s* threshold is at 4224.40 MeV.
- The decay constants used are: sqrt(2) f_D = 212.6 MeV, sqrt(2) f_Ds = 249.9 MeV, sqrt(2) f_B = 190.0 MeV, sqrt(2) f_Bs = 230.0 MeV.
- The hadronic medium is considered at temperatures around T_c = 156 MeV, the critical temperature for the phase transition.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the X(3872) in hot matter by Cleven (2019), who studied the X(3872) in a finite-temperature pion bath, and by Albaladejo (2021), who studied the X(3872) in dense nuclear matter. It also builds on the heavy-quark spin symmetry analyses of Nieves (2012) and Guo (2013), who predicted the existence of molecular partners including the X(4014) as a 2++ D* D* state. The results are relevant for interpreting X(3872) production in Pb-Pb collisions at CMS and for future lattice-QCD calculations exploring the melting of heavy mesons at finite temperature.
