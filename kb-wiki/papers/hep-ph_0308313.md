# Photoproduction of Theta baryon from the neutron

> We study photoproduction reactions for Theta^+, which was recently observed as a candidate of an exotic five quark state uudd(bar s). We employ effective lagrangian interactions in the linear and non-linear formalism of chiral symmetry and investigate two cases of spin and parity of Theta^+, J^P = 1/2^+ and 1/2^-. We calculate cross sections and discuss possibilities to discriminate one from the other.

## Key claim

The photoproduction cross section for the exotic pentaquark Theta^+ from a neutron target can discriminate between positive and negative parity assignments, but the absolute magnitude depends strongly on the unknown anomalous magnetic moment of Theta^+ and on the choice of effective Lagrangian scheme, with the hybrid model providing the most realistic energy dependence.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level Feynman diagrams for the reaction gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ using effective Lagrangians of the linear sigma model (pseudoscalar coupling) and nonlinear sigma model (pseudovector coupling), plus a hybrid model that interpolates between them. They considered two parity cases for Theta^+ (J^P = 1/2^+ and 1/2^-) and varied the unknown anomalous magnetic moment of Theta^+ as a free parameter. Coupling constants were fixed by assuming a decay width Gamma(Theta^+ -> KN) = 15 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Theta^+ mass assumed: M_Theta = 1530 MeV (from chiral soliton model of Diakonov et al.)
- Decay width used to fix couplings: Gamma = 15 MeV
- Coupling constants for positive parity: g = 4.05 (PS), g*_A = 0.306 (PV); for negative parity: g = 0.542 (PS), g*_A = 0.171 (PV)
- Hybrid model total cross section (positive parity, averaged over E_CM up to 2.5 GeV): 1.34 microbarn (kappa_Theta = +1.79), 0.78 microbarn (kappa_Theta = 0), 0.54 microbarn (kappa_Theta = -1.79)
- Estimated cross sections after applying 18% suppression factor from form factor (positive parity): 240 nb (kappa_Theta = +1.79), 140 nb (kappa_Theta = 0), 100 nb (kappa_Theta = -1.79)
- Estimated cross sections after suppression (negative parity): 20 nb (kappa_Theta = +1.79), 16 nb (kappa_Theta = 0), 14 nb (kappa_Theta = -1.79)
- Negative parity total cross sections are smaller than positive parity by a factor of 1/10 to 1/5

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the pentaquark Theta^+, first reported experimentally by the LEPS group at SPring-8 (Nakano, 2003) and subsequently confirmed by DIANA, CLAS, and SAPHIR collaborations. The authors build on the chiral soliton model predictions of Diakonov et al. (1997) and contrast with the earlier study of Liu and Ko (2003) who considered photoproduction from a proton target assuming positive parity. The calculations provide predictions for the reaction gamma n -> K^- Theta^+ that can be tested at existing photon facilities, and suggest that the angular distribution (forward peak versus suppressed forward angle) may serve as a parity discriminator, though the absolute cross section depends sensitively on the unknown decay width and form factor effects.
