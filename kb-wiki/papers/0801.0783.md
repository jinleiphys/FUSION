# Finite-width effects on Delta baryons in QCD Sum Rules

> The quantum chromodynamics (QCD) sum rules for the Delta baryons are analyzed by taking into account the finite-width effects, through explicit utilization of the Breit-Wigner shape. We apply a Monte-Carlo based analysis to the 'traditional' and the parity-projected sum rules. The first Delta excitation state is also considered as a sub-continuum resonance and the widths are calculated using the mass values as input.

# Finite-width effects on Delta baryons in QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim

The finite-width effects on Delta baryon masses are minor (about 3% change) in traditional QCD sum rules but significant (about 10% change) in parity-projected sum rules, where including the Breit-Wigner shape yields mass values in better agreement with experiment than the zero-width approximation.

## Method

The authors replaced the conventional delta-function representation of resonance contributions in QCD sum rules with a Breit-Wigner shape, using a four-pulse approximation to evaluate the integrals numerically. They applied Monte-Carlo based analysis with 1000 Gaussianly distributed parameter sets to both traditional and parity-projected sum rules for the Delta baryons, treating the first excitation states as sub-continuum resonances.

## Key numbers

- Delta(1232) mass from traditional chiral-odd sum rule (OSR): 1.43 ± 0.12 GeV in zero-width limit; 1.48 ± 0.11 GeV with finite width Γ = 0.12 GeV (experimental mass: 1.232 GeV)
- Delta(1232) mass from traditional chiral-even sum rule (ESR): 1.21 ± 0.26 GeV in zero-width limit; 1.24 ± 0.21 GeV with finite width Γ = 0.12 GeV
- Delta(1232) width extracted from OSR with mass fixed at 1.48 GeV: Γ = 0.20 ± 0.08 GeV (experimental: 0.118 ± 0.002 GeV)
- Delta(1232) mass from parity-projected sum rule with w_+ = 1.5 GeV: 1.13 ± 0.09 GeV in zero-width; 1.23 ± 0.09 GeV with Γ = 0.12 GeV
- Delta(1600) width extracted from parity-projected sum rule with m_+ = 1.6 GeV: Γ'_+ = 0.22 ± 0.07 GeV (experimental: 0.35 ± 0.10 GeV)
- Delta(1700) mass from parity-projected sum rule with w_- = 2.4 GeV and Γ_- = 0.3 GeV: m_- = 1.57 ± 0.32 GeV (experimental: 1.710 ± 0.040 GeV)
- Delta(1940) width extracted from parity-projected sum rule: Γ'_- = 0.65 ± 0.29 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule analyses of Delta baryons by Lee (1997) and others, which used the zero-width approximation, and applies the finite-width method developed by Elias (1997) for the pion excitation state. The parity-projected sum rule technique follows the approach of Jido (1996) for separating positive- and negative-parity contributions. The Monte-Carlo based analysis follows the method introduced by Leinweber (1995) for systematic treatment of uncertainties. The results enable improved extraction of Delta resonance parameters from QCD sum rules and can be extended to all decuplet baryons and their excited states.
