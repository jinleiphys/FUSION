# On the role of the Vacuum Energy in the Thermodynamics of Neutron Matter

> The only way neutron matter can couple to the electromagnetic field is through an anomalous coupling, which plays an important role in the thermodynamics of pure neutron matter. Such theories are, however, perturbatively non-renormalisable, which presents a difficulty in terms of the unambiguous treatment of the divergencies. Here we show that despite this, an unambiguous expression can be obtained for the vacuum energy contribution to the grand canonical potential in the case of a constant magnetic field. We find that this contribution is quite small, which justifies the no-sea approximation usually made. We also discuss the density and temperature dependence of the full grand canonical potential.

# On the role of the Vacuum Energy in the Thermodynamics of Neutron Matter

## Key claim
Despite the theory being perturbatively non-renormalisable due to the anomalous coupling of neutrons to the electromagnetic field, an unambiguous expression for the vacuum energy contribution to the grand canonical potential can be obtained for a constant magnetic field. This contribution is found to be very small, justifying the no-sea approximation usually made in such calculations.

## Method
The authors compute the grand canonical potential for pure neutron matter in a constant magnetic field using a saddle-point approximation of the Euclidean path integral, truncating at tree level. They handle the divergent vacuum energy contribution by noting that the fifth and higher derivatives of the vacuum term with respect to the dimensionless parameter x = gB/m are finite, allowing them to integrate a finite differential equation and fix integration constants by requiring that the grand canonical potential reduces to the classical magnetic field energy density in the infinite-mass limit.

## Key numbers
- Neutron mass: m = 4.761 fm^{-1}; anomalous coupling strength: g = 0.031 fm
- Conversion factor: 1 fm^{-2} = 1.993 × 10^{18} G
- For realistic magnetic fields, the dimensionless parameter x = gB/m is of order 10^{-3}
- Saturation density: ρ_s = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The vacuum contribution to the grand canonical potential is too small to be discerned on graphs at typical values of x ≈ 10^{-3}
- At zero temperature and ρ = ρ_s and ρ = 2ρ_s, vacuum corrections are not discernable on plots of Ω and ε
- At β = 20 fm (corresponding to about 1% of m) and ρ = ρ_s and ρ = 2ρ_s, vacuum corrections remain not discernable
- The vacuum energy expression f_0(x) becomes singular at x = gB/m = ±1, corresponding to the threshold where spontaneous pair generation becomes possible

## Context
This work extends previous studies of magnetised nuclear matter that employed the no-sea approximation, including relativistic mean-field computations by Lattimer2 and others, and the analysis of anomalous coupling effects by Ferr3 which concluded the coupling is important for neutrons as their only coupling mechanism to magnetic fields. The paper improves upon the ad hoc momentum cut-off approach used by Ferr4 and the minimal subtraction scheme proposed by Diener2, providing a more rigorous method for computing the vacuum energy contribution. The results justify the no-sea approximation used in prior work and suggest that for pure neutron matter, vacuum energy effects are negligible for realistic magnetic field strengths, though the authors note that a full neutron star equation of state would require including protons, leptons, and mesons as done by Lattimer2 and Diener3.
