# Renormalization of proton-proton fusion in chiral effective field theory

> Renormalization of proton-proton fusion is studied in the framework of chiral effective field theory. Strict perturbative treatment of subleading corrections is applied in the analysis. Possible enhancement of two-nucleon contact axial current operators is the focus of the study. We find evidence that supports a previous proposal in the literature to promote one of the contact axial current operators.

# Renormalization of proton-proton fusion in chiral effective field theory

## Key claim
The authors find evidence that a two-body contact axial current operator, which naive dimensional analysis (NDA) would place at N³LO, must be promoted to N²LO to maintain renormalization-group (RG) invariance in chiral effective field theory calculations of proton-proton fusion. This conclusion is conditional on the strict perturbative treatment of subleading chiral nuclear forces and the use of Δ-less potentials with a breakdown scale of approximately 300 MeV.

## Method
The authors calculate the nuclear matrix element of the axial current for pp fusion up to N²LO in chiral effective field theory, using perturbative treatment of subleading chiral nuclear forces rather than summing them with the leading-order potential. The pp scattering wave functions are constructed by fully iterating the Coulomb potential at leading order, with strong phase shifts computed using an artificial infrared cutoff in coordinate space. The deuteron wave function is obtained from chiral potentials in the coupled ³S₁-³D₁ channel following the power counting of Long (2011). Contact LECs for the pp ¹S₀ interaction are fitted to empirical phase shifts from partial-wave analysis, and the cutoff dependence of the radial matrix element Λ_R is examined for cutoff values from 0.5 to 3.2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron binding momentum γ = 45.7 MeV, used as a low-energy scale for estimating EFT truncation errors.
- The inverse Bohr radius α m_N ≈ 7 MeV, setting the scale below which the Coulomb potential must be treated nonperturbatively.
- At NLO with Λ = 1 GeV, the radial matrix element Λ_R(0) = 2.65 ± 0.08, with the uncertainty corresponding to the expected NLO truncation error of (γ/δ)² ≈ 3%.
- The N²LO correction from the ³S₁-³D₁ potential shows up to 40% deviation from LO based on Λ_R values at Λ = 1.3 and 1.6 GeV.
- The N²LO correction from the ¹S₀ potential shows fluctuation of about 5% based on Λ_R values at Λ = 1.7 and 2.7 GeV.
- The acceptable N²LO truncation error is (γ/δ)³ ≈ 0.4%, which is much smaller than the observed cutoff variations.

## Context
This work extends the RG-based analysis of nuclear electroweak currents initiated by Shi and Long (2022) to the specific process of proton-proton fusion. It builds on the power counting schemes for nuclear forces developed by Long (2011, 2012) and the RG analysis of nuclear currents pioneered by Pavon Valderrama (2014), who assigned the contact axial current operator to N^(7/4)LO based on short-range wave function behavior. The results enable improved theoretical predictions of the pp fusion cross section with quantified uncertainties, though the promotion of the contact operator means that without a reliable determination of its low-energy constant d̂_R, the cross section can only be predicted up to NLO with a conservatively estimated 3% uncertainty. The work contrasts with NDA-based ChEFT calculations by Acharya (2016) and Marcucci (2013), which did not account for the RG-invariance requirement that drives the promotion of the contact operator.
