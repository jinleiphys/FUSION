# A coupled K-matrix description of the reactions pi+N -> pi+N, pi+N -> eta+N, gamma+N -> pi+N and gamma+N -> eta+N

> The coupled pi+N, eta+N, gamma+N systems are described by a K-matrix method. The parameters in this model are adjusted to get an optimal fit to pi+N -> pi+N, pi+N -> eta+N, gamma+N -> pi+N and gamma+N -> eta+N data in an energy range of about 100 MeV each side of the eta-threshold. The coupling of photons to the N(1535) state is extracted and also an alternative to the current S11(gamma+N -> pi+N) amplitudes suggested. Expansions are given for the eta-eta and gamma-eta amplitudes in terms of the eta-momentum. Effects of interference of this state with background potential interactions are discussed and experimental consequences are indicated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a coupled K-matrix model that simultaneously fits data for four reactions (\(\pi N \rightarrow \pi N\), \(\pi N \rightarrow \eta N\), \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi N\), and \(\gamma N \rightarrow \eta N\)) within about 100 MeV of the \(\eta\) threshold, but the authors note that the \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi N\) amplitudes used as input are not unique, so the model's S11 amplitudes for that reaction may be considered a prediction consistent with the other channels rather than a definitive extraction.

## Method

The authors construct a K-matrix formalism coupling the \(\pi N\), \(\eta N\), \(\pi\pi N\), and \(\gamma N\) channels, then eliminate the \(\pi\pi N\) channel as an optical potential to work with three separate 2x2 T-matrices: one for coupled \(\pi N\) and \(\eta N\), one for coupled \(\eta N\) and \(\gamma N\), and one for coupled \(\pi N\) and \(\gamma N\). The K-matrix elements are parametrized with energy-independent background terms plus poles for the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances, and the 13 free parameters are fitted via Minuit to 158 data points: 23 \(\pi N\) amplitudes, 11 \(\pi N \rightarrow \eta N\) cross sections, 53 \(\gamma N \rightarrow \eta N\) cross sections, and up to 48 S11(\(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi N\)) amplitudes in the center-of-mass energy range 1350–1650 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The best fit (case A) yields an overall \(\chi^2\)/dof of 0.89 when fitting data up to \(E_{c.m.} = 1550\) MeV, with the \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi N\) amplitude errors increased to \(\pm \sqrt{2}\).
* The extracted partial decay width for \(N(1535) \rightarrow \gamma N\) is \(\Gamma_\gamma(0) = 0.171\) MeV for case A and 0.157 MeV for case B.
* The \(\eta N\) scattering length from the effective range expansion is 15% larger (case A) and 40% larger (case B) than the value from the authors' prior work Green and Wycech (1997).
* At \(E_{c.m.} = 1538.6\) MeV (50 MeV above \(\eta\) threshold), the expansion of \(1/A_{\gamma\eta}\) gives \(8.4 - i20.0\) fm\(^{-1}\) compared to the exact value \(7.9 - i19.7\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* The \(\gamma N \rightarrow \eta N\) cross section predictions for cases A and D are larger than cases B and C by up to a factor of two at \(E_{c.m.} \approx 1650\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier two-channel K-matrix model (Green and Wycech, 1997) by explicitly including the \(\gamma N\) channel, replacing the earlier assumption that \(\sigma(\gamma N \rightarrow \eta N) \propto |T_{\eta\eta}|^2\) with a direct calculation of \(T_{\gamma\eta}\). The model provides updated \(\eta N\) scattering lengths and effective range parameters that are more favorable for the existence of \(\eta\)-nucleus quasi-bound states, as studied by Vldov (year not given) and Hayano (year not given), and the authors suggest that future \(\gamma N \rightarrow \eta N\) cross section data from the GRAAL collaboration up to \(E_{c.m.} \approx 1650\) MeV could distinguish between the different parameter sets and better constrain the \(\eta N\) interaction.
