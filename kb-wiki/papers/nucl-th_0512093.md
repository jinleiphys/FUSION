# Isovector Channel Role of Relativistic Mean Field Models in the Neutrino Mean Free Path

> An improvement in the treatment of the isovector channel of relativistic mean field (RMF) models based on effective field theory (E-RMF) is suggested, by adding an isovector scalar (delta) meson and using a similar procedure to the one used by Horowitz and Piekarewicz to adjust the isovector-vector channel in order to achieve a softer density dependent symmetry energy of the nuclear matter at high density. Their effects on the equation of state (EOS) at high density and on the neutrino mean free path (NMFP) in neutron stars are discussed.

# Isovector Channel Role of Relativistic Mean Field Models in the Neutrino Mean Free Path

## Key claim

Adding an isovector scalar (delta) meson to the E-RMF model and adjusting the isovector-vector channel parameters can produce a softer density-dependent symmetry energy at high density, which removes the anomalous behavior in the neutrino mean free path (NMFP) where the mean free path decreases with decreasing matter density. However, the presence of the delta meson actually increases the proton fraction at high density and makes the equation of state stiffer, contrary to the softening effect from isovector-vector adjustments alone.

## Method

The authors extended the E-RMF model (based on the G2 parameter set) by adding a delta meson term to the Lagrangian density and by adjusting the g_rho and eta_rho coupling constants in the isovector-vector channel, following the procedure of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001). They calculated the symmetry energy, proton fraction, equation of state, nucleon effective mass, and neutrino mean free path for neutron star matter at zero temperature using the relativistic mean field approximation without RPA correlations, comparing results across the G2, NL-3, NL-Z, and Z271 parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy fixed at E_sym = 24.1 MeV at k_F = 1.14 fm^{-1} for isovector-vector adjustments, and at E_sym = 26.57 MeV at k_F = 1.172 fm^{-1} for delta meson studies
- G2 parameter set yields M* approximately 0.6 M at saturation density, which is the demanding feature for finite nuclei
- NL-3 parameter set shows anomalous NMFP behavior with a stiff EOS and relatively small M* at high density
- For the delta meson case with eta_rho = 0.39, g_delta values of 5.026 and 7.540 were used; for eta_rho = 4.49, the same g_delta values were applied
- The delta meson reduces the PNM M* and makes the PNM EOS stiffer, with the reduction magnitude depending on the magnitude of g_delta
- In the region rho_B = (1.5-2) rho_0, the difference in symmetry energy between E-RMF with and without delta meson is not significant

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino mean free path in neutron star matter by Reddy et al. (1998), Niembro et al. (2001), and the authors' own prior work (Hutauruk et al., 2004). It addresses the known problem that standard RMF models like NL-3 predict too large proton fractions at high density, which correlates with the direct URCA cooling process (Lattimer et al., 1991). The authors show that the E-RMF model (Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1996) with its extra nonlinear and tensor terms can simultaneously satisfy finite nuclei constraints (small M* at saturation density) and neutron star constraints (large M* at high density), unlike the standard RMF models. The results enable future work to re-explore whether RMF models necessarily predict low threshold densities for the direct URCA process, pending experimental determination of the density-dependent symmetry energy at high density.
