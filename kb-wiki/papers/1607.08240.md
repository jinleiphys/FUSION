# IC at IC: IceCube can constrain the intrinsic charm of the proton

> The discovery of extraterrestrial neutrinos in the $\sim$ 30 TeV -- PeV energy range by IceCube provides new constraints on high energy astrophysics. An important background to the signal are the prompt neutrinos which originate from the decay of charm hadrons produced by high energy cosmic-ray particles interacting in the Earth's atmosphere. It is conventional to use the calculations of charm hadroproduction using gluon splitting $g \to c \bar c$ alone. However, QCD predicts an additional "intrinsic" component of the heavy quark distribution which arises from diagrams where heavy quarks are multiply connected to the proton's valence quarks. We estimate the prompt neutrino spectrum due to intrinsic charm. We find that the atmospheric prompt neutrino flux from intrinsic charm is comparable to those calculated using QCD computations not including intrinsic charm, once we normalize the intrinsic charm differential cross sections to the ISR and the LEBC-MPS collaboration data. In future, IceCube will constrain the intrinsic charm content of the proton and will contribute to one of the major questions in high energy physics phenomenology.

## Key claim

The atmospheric prompt neutrino flux from intrinsic charm can be comparable to those calculated using perturbative QCD computations that include only gluon splitting, once the intrinsic charm differential cross sections are normalized to the ISR and LEBC-MPS collaboration data; however, the normalization of intrinsic charm is not well known and the authors adopt three test cases (A, B, C) to bracket the uncertainty.

## Method

The authors solve coupled cascade equations for protons, charm hadrons, and leptons in the atmosphere, using the production moments formalism. They compute the intrinsic charm contribution to the differential cross section $d\sigma/dx_F$ for charm hadroproduction by normalizing to data from the ISR experiment for $\Lambda_c$ production and the LEBC-MPS collaboration for $D$ meson production at high $x_F$, adopting three different normalization scenarios. They then convert the charm hadron fluxes into neutrino fluxes via decay moments, using four different cosmic-ray flux models (H3P, H3A, H14A, H14B) and accounting for nuclear scaling and energy dependence of the cross section.

## Key numbers

- The prompt neutrino flux from intrinsic charm is comparable to the ERS w/G flux; the current IceCube upper limit on prompt neutrinos is 1.06 times the ERS w/G flux (Aartsen 2016).
- For Case A, the production cross sections at $\sqrt{s} \approx 39$ GeV are: $\sigma(D^\pm) = 3.88$ $\mu$b, $\sigma(D^0 + \bar{D}^0) = 3.88$ $\mu$b, $\sigma(D_s^\pm) = 2.29$ $\mu$b, and $\sigma(\Lambda_c^\pm) = 183.54$ $\mu$b.
- For Case B, the cross sections are: $\sigma(D^\pm) = 3.88$ $\mu$b, $\sigma(D^0 + \bar{D}^0) = 3.88$ $\mu$b, $\sigma(D_s^\pm) = 2.29$ $\mu$b, and $\sigma(\Lambda_c^\pm) = 4.99$ $\mu$b.
- For Case C, the cross sections are: $\sigma(D^\pm) = 1.09$ $\mu$b, $\sigma(D^0 + \bar{D}^0) = 1.09$ $\mu$b, $\sigma(D_s^\pm) = 0.64$ $\mu$b, and $\sigma(\Lambda_c^\pm) = 1.47$ $\mu$b.
- The intrinsic charm cross section scales with mass number $A$ as $A^{0.755 \pm 0.016}$, according to SELEX (BlancoCovarrubias 2009).
- The total atmospheric neutrino flux including intrinsic charm can dominate the flux within the pQCD framework at energies $\gtrsim$ 200 TeV for $\nu_e + \bar{\nu}_e$ and $\gtrsim$ 2 PeV for $\nu_\mu + \bar{\nu}_\mu$.
- A measurement of the atmospheric $\nu_e + \bar{\nu}_e$ flux at $\sim$ 200 TeV at $\sim$ 50% accuracy could distinguish between the pQCD contribution and intrinsic charm component.

## Context

This work extends earlier prompt neutrino calculations that considered only gluon splitting $g \to c\bar{c}$ (Enberg, Reno and Sarcevic 2008; Gaisser 2013; Bhattacharya 2015; Garzelli 2015; Gauld 2015) by adding the intrinsic charm component, which was predicted by Brodsky (1980) and has received less attention in the atmospheric neutrino context since the early works of Bugaev (1989) and Gondolo (1995). The authors normalize to the ISR data (Chauvat 1987) and LEBC-MPS collaboration data (Ammar 1988), which were not used in the earliest intrinsic charm calculations. The results show that IceCube can constrain the intrinsic charm content of the proton, a question that has been difficult to address in collider experiments for decades, and can complement collider searches (Rostami 2015; Bailas 2015; Boettcher 2015; Lipatov 2016) and global analyses (Dulat 2013; Ball 2016).
