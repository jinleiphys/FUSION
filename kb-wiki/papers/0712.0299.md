# Azimuthal asymmetry of direct photons in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions

> Hard photon emitted from energetic heavy ion collisions is of very interesting since it does not experience the late-stage nuclear interaction, therefore it is useful to explore the early-stage information of matter phase. In this work, we have presented a first calculation of azimuthal asymmetry, characterized by directed transverse flow parameter $F$ and elliptic asymmetry coefficient $v_2$, for proton-neutron bremsstrahlung hard photons in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions. The positive $F$ and negative $v_2$ of direct photons are illustrated and they seem to be anti-correlated to the corresponding free proton's flow.

# Azimuthal asymmetry of direct photons in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation showing that direct hard photons from proton-neutron bremsstrahlung in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions exhibit a non-zero directed transverse flow parameter (positive F) and elliptic asymmetry coefficient (negative v2), which are anti-correlated to the corresponding free proton flow parameters. This anti-correlation is explained by the preferential emission of hard photons perpendicular to the incident proton direction in the individual proton-neutron center-of-mass system.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a nuclear mean field parameterized to include symmetry energy terms, simulating the reaction of 40Ca + 40Ca collisions at 30 MeV/nucleon for semi-central events (40-60% centrality). Hard photon production was calculated from individual proton-neutron bremsstrahlung using a hard-sphere collision model with a double differential cross section formula, and photons were classified as direct or thermal based on their emission time relative to the first maximum expansion of the system at t = 80 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Directed transverse flow parameter F for direct photons: +3.7 MeV/c, extracted from the slope of mean in-plane transverse momentum versus center-of-mass rapidity over the mid-rapidity region (-0.5, 0.5)
- Directed transverse flow parameter F for free protons: -12.4 MeV/c, extracted from the same mid-rapidity region
- Elliptic asymmetry coefficient v2 for direct photons: -2.7%, indicating out-of-plane enhancement
- Elliptic asymmetry coefficient v2 for free protons: +7.2%, indicating preferential in-plane emission
- Reaction system: 40Ca + 40Ca at 30 MeV/nucleon, with nuclear equation of state compressibility K = 235 MeV (parameters a = -218 MeV, b = 164 MeV, sigma = 4/3)
- Symmetry energy strength C_sym = 32 MeV used in the nuclear mean field
- Hard photons defined as gamma-rays with energies above 30 MeV
- Direct photons defined as those emitted before t = 80 fm/c (first maximum expansion time)

## Context

This work extends the study of azimuthal asymmetry of hard photons from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where such asymmetry had been reported experimentally and theoretically by Aggarwal, Adams, Turbide, and Heinz, down to the intermediate energy regime where no previous experimental data or theoretical predictions existed. The results enable future systematic studies of how the equation of state, in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, impact parameter, and incident energy affect direct photon azimuthal asymmetry, and suggest that direct photons could serve as a probe sensitive specifically to the in-medium neutron-proton cross section, which is advantageous for isospin-dependent studies of nuclear matter properties.
