# Low-energy Pion-nucleon Scattering

> This paper contains the results of an analysis of recent low-energy pion-nucleon scattering experiments. Obtained are phase shifts, the pion-nucleon coupling constant and an estimate of the Sigma term.

# Low-energy Pion-nucleon Scattering

## Key claim

Contrary to most previous analyses, the scattering volumes for the P31 and P13 partial waves are equal within errors, corresponding to a symmetry found in the hamiltonian of many theories; however, this result depends on excluding the Bertin et al. data sets, which the authors find inconsistent with modern data.

## Method

The authors model pion-nucleon dynamics using a coupled-channel Klein-Gordon equation with a potential taken as the fourth component of a Lorentz four-vector, solving for each partial wave with sums of exponential potentials via the Jost representation. They fit the model to charged pion-proton elastic scattering data from multiple experiments (Brack, Frank, Auld, Ritchie, Wiedner, Joram I and II) at energies from 29.4 to 86.8 MeV, and also fit charge-exchange data directly. The subthreshold amplitudes are extrapolated using the same potential model to estimate the Sigma term, with a constraint that the isovector amplitude vanishes at the Cheng-Dashen point.

## Key numbers

- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: f² = 0.0764 ± 0.0007, obtained from scattering lengths and the GMO sum rule
- Isoscalar scattering length: b0 = 0.001 ± 0.003 μ⁻¹
- Isovector scattering length: b1 = -0.086 ± 0.003 μ⁻¹
- P31 and P13 scattering volumes: both -0.028 ± 0.003 μ⁻³ (equal within errors)
- P33 scattering volume: 0.191 μ⁻³ from Chew-Low theory prediction (using f² = 0.0765), in reasonable agreement with fit values near 0.178 μ⁻³
- Sigma term values from the full fit range from 45.14 to 53.59 MeV depending on data set inclusion and constraint strength
- Pion decay constant used: fπ = 93.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Gibbs, Ai, Kaufmann, earlier paper) on isospin breaking using the same Klein-Gordon technique with five potential forms, now focusing on exponential and Yukawa potentials. It provides an alternative to dispersion-relation determinations of the Sigma term by Hohler, Koch, Hite, and Sainio, and offers a potential-model perspective that relates subthreshold behavior directly to the shape of the interaction. The finding that P31 and P13 scattering volumes are equal supports predictions from the Skyrmion model (Mattis) and the large 1/Nc expansion of QCD (Dashen), and enables improved low-energy input for nuclear force calculations.
