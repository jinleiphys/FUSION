# Landau Potential Study of the Chiral Phase Transition in a QCD-Like Theory

> We investigate the chiral phase transition of a QCD-like theory, based the shape change of the effective potential near the critical point. The potential is constructed with the auxiliary field method, and a source term coupled to the field is introduced in order to compute the potential shape numerically. We also generalize the potential so as to have two independent order parameters, the quark scalar density and the number density. We find a tri-critical point locating at (T,mu)=(97, 203) MeV, and visualize it as the merging point of three potential minima.

## Key claim

The authors find a tri-critical point at (T, μ) = (97, 203) MeV in a QCD-like theory, but this result depends on a covariant-like ansatz for the mass function and the assumption that the frequency-dependent and momentum-dependent components of the self-energy vanish.

## Method

The effective potential (Landau potential) is constructed using the auxiliary field method in the renormalization-group improved ladder approximation of the Schwinger-Dyson equation for QCD. A source term coupled to the square of the self-energy is introduced to compute the potential shape away from the extremum, and the potential is generalized to have two independent order parameters: the quark condensate and the quark number density. The potential is evaluated numerically at finite temperature and chemical potential using the imaginary time formalism with a covariant-like ansatz for the mass function.

## Key numbers

- Tri-critical point located at (T, μ) = (97, 203) MeV
- At μ = 0 and μ = 100 MeV, a second-order transition occurs in the temperature range T = 100–120 MeV
- At μ = 300 MeV, a first-order transition occurs between T = 75 MeV and 80 MeV
- At T = 80 MeV, a first-order transition occurs when the chemical potential changes between μ = 280 MeV and 290 MeV

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-group improved ladder approximation for the Schwinger-Dyson equation of QCD, previously used by the same authors and others (Tsue, Fujii, Hashimoto, earlier works) to calculate the pion-nucleon sigma term and quark condensate at finite density. The method builds on the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) potential functional approach but uses a Landau potential with an auxiliary field method to study the potential shape away from the extremum. The results enable mapping the chiral phase transition order in the temperature-chemical potential plane and identifying the tri-critical point where first-order and second-order transition lines meet.
