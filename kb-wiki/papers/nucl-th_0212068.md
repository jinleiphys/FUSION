# Does the Charm Flow at RHIC?

> Recent PHENIX Au+Au -> e- + X data from open charm decay are shown to be consistent with two extreme opposite dynamical scenarios of ultra-relativistic nuclear reactions. Perturbative QCD without final state interactions was previously shown to be consistent with the data. However, we show that the data are also consistent with zero mean free path hydrodynamics characterized by a common transverse flow velocity field. The surprising coincidence of both D and B hydrodynamic flow spectra with pQCD up to p_T ~ 3 and 5 GeV, respectively, suggests that heavy quarks may be produced essentially at rest in the rapidly expanding gluon plasma. Possible implications and further tests of collective heavy quark dynamics are discussed.

## Key claim

The PHENIX single-electron data from open charm decay in Au+Au collisions at RHIC are consistent with both the extreme scenario of perturbative QCD without final-state interactions (infinite mean free path) and the opposite extreme of zero mean free path hydrodynamics with a common transverse flow velocity field, meaning the data alone cannot distinguish between these two fundamentally different dynamical pictures.

## Method

The authors compared PYTHIA pQCD calculations (with parameters tuned to lower-energy charm data) against a thermal hydrodynamic model using a transverse boosted Bjorken parametrization with a linear radial boost profile, freeze-out temperature T=128 MeV, and maximum boost β_max=0.65 fitted to PHENIX π,K,p spectra. They computed D and B meson transverse momentum distributions from both models, then calculated the resulting decay electron spectra and compared them to PHENIX single-electron data for 10% central and minimum-bias Au+Au collisions at √s=130 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T=128 MeV used in the hydrodynamic model, fitted to PHENIX π,K,p spectra
- Maximum transverse boost β_max=0.65 for central collisions, determined from fitting PHENIX π,K,p distributions
- The pQCD and hydrodynamic D meson spectra agree up to p_T ≈ 3 GeV; B meson spectra agree up to p_T ≈ 5 GeV
- For 10% central Au+Au collisions, nuclear geometry amplifies the p+p rate by N_binary ≈ 905 binary collisions, with T_AuAu(cent)=22.6 mb^{-1}
- For minimum-bias collisions, T_AA=6.2 mb^{-1} (N_binary=246)
- PHENIX measured dN/dy(π^+) = 276 ± 3 for central collisions, used to normalize the hydrodynamic π calculation

## Context

This work extends the puzzle of heavy quark dynamics at RHIC by showing that the single-electron data from PHENIX (Adcox et al., 2002) cannot differentiate between pQCD without final-state interactions and hydrodynamic collective flow. The hydrodynamic model that successfully describes light hadron spectra (Huovinen et al., 2001; Teaney et al., 2001) is shown to also reproduce the charm decay electron data, suggesting heavy quarks may be produced essentially at rest in the expanding gluon plasma. The authors propose that elliptic flow measurements of D mesons or J/ψ could serve as a smoking-gun signature to distinguish these scenarios, noting that current statistical methods for extracting charm via single electrons make v_2 measurements challenging.
