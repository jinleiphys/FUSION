# Chiral dynamics in the gamma p --> p pi0 reaction

> We investigate the neutral pion photoproduction on the proton near threshold in covariant chiral perturbation theory with the explicit inclusion of Delta degrees of freedom. This channel is specially sensitive to chiral dynamics and the advent of very precise data from the Mainz microtron has shown the limits of the convergence of the chiral series for both the heavy baryon and the covariant approaches. We show that the inclusion of the Delta resonance substantially improves the convergence leading to a good agreement with data for a wider range of energies.

## Key claim

Including the Delta(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in a covariant chiral perturbation theory calculation at order p^3 substantially improves the agreement with precise Mainz Microtron data for neutral pion photoproduction on the proton, over a wider energy range than previous heavy-baryon or covariant calculations without the Delta.

## Method

The authors performed a covariant chiral perturbation theory calculation of the gamma p -> p pi0 reaction up to chiral order p^3, using the Extended On Mass Shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme. They included all tree-level and loop diagrams with only pions, nucleons, and photons as degrees of freedom, and then added the Delta(1232) resonance contributions at tree level using consistent Rarita-Schwinger Lagrangians with couplings fixed from the Delta width and from fits to pion production at resonance energies. The free parameters were the axial coupling g_A and three combinations of low-energy constants (LECs): c6+c7, d8+d9, and 2d16-d18.

## Key numbers

- The Delta(1232) mass is approximately 300 MeV above the nucleon mass (delta = M_Delta - m ~ 300 MeV)
- The pion decay constant was fixed to F = 92.4 MeV
- The Delta couplings used were h_A = 2.85, g_M = 2.97, and g_E = -1.0, taken from Pascalutsa (2005)
- The best-fit value for the magnetic N-Delta coupling g_M was 3.1, very close to the literature value of 2.97
- The full model achieved a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.69, compared to 4.96 for the model without the Delta resonance
- The model without Delta overestimates low-energy data and underestimates higher-energy data; the Delta mechanisms provide a sharper energy slope that allows good fits over the full energy range

## Context

This work extends earlier heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard (2001) and covariant EOMS calculations by Hilt (2013), which both failed to describe the precise Mainz Microtron data from Hornidge (2012) beyond about 20 MeV above threshold. The inclusion of the Delta resonance was motivated by Hemmert (1996), who found moderate Delta effects in a static heavy-baryon approach, and by the observation that the threshold region is only about one pion mass away from the Delta resonance mass. The results enable future studies that could fit the Delta couplings directly from low-energy data and extend the analysis to other charge channels and a wider energy range.
