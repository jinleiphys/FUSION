# Parton-hadron matter in- and out-off equilibrium

> We study the shear and bulk viscosities of partonic and hadronic matter - as well as the electric conductivity - as functions of temperature $T$ within the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach. Dynamical hadronic and partonic systems in equilibrium are studied by the PHSD simulations in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. The ratio of the shear viscosity to entropy density $η(T)/s(T)$ from PHSD shows a minimum (with a value of about 0.1) close to the critical temperature $T_c$. For $T<T_c$, i.e. in the hadronic phase, the ratio $η/s$ rises fast with decreasing temperature due to a lower interaction rate of the hadronic system and a significantly smaller number of degrees-of-freedom. The bulk viscosity $ζ(T)$ -- evaluated in the relaxation time approach -- is found to strongly depend on the effects of mean fields (or potentials) in the partonic phase. We find a significant rise of the ratio $ζ(T)/s(T)$ in the vicinity of the critical temperature $T_c$, when consistently including the scalar mean-field from PHSD, which is also in agreement with that from lQCD calculations. Furthermore, we present the results for the ratio $(η+3ζ/4)/s$, which is found to depend non-trivially on temperature and to generally agree with the lQCD calculations as well. Within the PHSD calculations, the strong maximum of $ζ(T)/η(T)$ close to $T_c$ has to be attributed to mean-fields (or potential) effects that in PHSD are encoded in the temperature dependence of the quasiparticle masses, which is related to the infrared enhancement of the resummed (effective) coupling $g(T)$. We also find that the dimensionless ratio of the electric conductivity over temperature $σ_0/T$ rises above $T_c$ approximately linearly with $T$ up to $T=2.5 T_c$, but approaches a constant above $5 T_c$, as expected qualitatively from perturbative QCD (pQCD).

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s from PHSD shows a minimum with a value of about 0.1 close to the critical temperature Tc, but this result is obtained specifically from simulations of infinite matter in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions, not from actual heavy-ion collision simulations.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) off-shell transport approach to simulate infinite partonic and hadronic matter in a cubic box of 9³ fm³ with periodic boundary conditions. They extracted shear viscosity using both the Kubo formalism (from correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor) and the relaxation time approximation, while bulk viscosity was evaluated only in the relaxation time approach, including scalar mean-field effects from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM).

## Key numbers

- η/s minimum value of about 0.1 close to the critical temperature Tc
- Critical energy density εc ≈ 0.5 GeV/fm³ for the dynamical phase transition
- The ratio σ₀/T rises approximately linearly with T up to T = 2.5 Tc, then approaches a constant above 5 Tc
- Tc = 158 MeV (used for scaling the electric conductivity results)
- Box size fixed to 9³ fm³ for all simulations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of transport coefficients in strongly-interacting matter by extracting shear and bulk viscosities from the PHSD approach, which has already been applied to pp, p+A, and A+A collisions from √sNN = 5 GeV up to 2.76 TeV. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations for pure SUc(3) gauge theory from several groups (lQCDtransport1, lQCDtransport2, lQCDtransport3, lQCDtransport4) and with the virial expansion approach of Mattiello (2012). The findings enable improved viscous hydrodynamic calculations for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC by providing temperature-dependent transport coefficients that are compatible with experimental observations of single-particle spectra, collective flow, and dilepton data.
