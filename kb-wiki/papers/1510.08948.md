# Neutron capture and the antineutrino yield from nuclear reactors

> We identify a new, flux-dependent correction to the antineutrino spectrum as produced in nuclear reactors. The abundance of certain nuclides, whose decay chains produce antineutrinos above the threshold for inverse beta decay, has a nonlinear dependence on the neutron flux, unlike the vast majority of antineutrino producing nuclides, whose decay rate is directly related to the fission rate. We have identified four of these so-called nonlinear nuclides and determined that they result in an antineutrino excess at low-energies below 3.2MeV, dependent on the reactor thermal neutron flux. We develop an analytic model for the size of the correction and compare it to the results of detailed reactor simulations for various real existing reactors, spanning 3 orders of magnitude in neutron flux. In a typical pressurized water reactor the resulting correction can reach 0.9% of the low energy flux which is comparable in size to other, known low-energy corrections from spent nuclear fuel and the non-equilibrium correction. For naval reactors the nonlinear correction may reach the 10% level.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new, flux-dependent correction to the reactor antineutrino spectrum arising from nuclides whose production depends quadratically on neutron flux, rather than linearly. The correction is conditional on long irradiation times (many months to years) and high thermal neutron flux, and in typical pressurized water reactors it reaches about 0.9% of the low-energy flux, comparable to other known low-energy corrections, but for naval reactors it may reach the 10% level.

## Method

The authors identified four "nonlinear" nuclides (100Tc, 104Rh, 110Ag, 142Pr) whose production proceeds via neutron capture on a precursor rather than directly from fission, making their abundance proportional to φ² rather than φ. They solved the coupled Bateman equations analytically for these nuclides and compared the analytic results to full reactor simulations using the SCALE-6.1 code suite for nine different reactor configurations spanning three orders of magnitude in thermal neutron flux (1.6×10¹² to 2.5×10¹⁵ n/cm²/s). The antineutrino spectra from the nonlinear nuclides were computed following the beta-decay description of Huber (2011) and summed to obtain the total nonlinear correction.

## Key numbers

- The nonlinear correction reaches 0.92% of the low-energy flux for a 3-batch pressurized water reactor (PWR) with thermal neutron flux 4.4×10¹³ n/cm²/s and burn-up 1,890,000 MWd/t.
- For a 5 MWe natural uranium graphite-moderated reactor (φ = 1.6×10¹² n/cm²/s), the correction is 0.027%.
- For the High Flux Isotope Reactor (HFIR) at φ = 2.5×10¹⁵ n/cm²/s, the correction is 0.10%.
- For the ILL reactor measurements (φ = 3.3×10¹⁴ n/cm²/s, irradiation times 12–43 hours), the correction is at most 4.7×10⁻³%, confirming these measurements are uncontaminated.
- The four nonlinear nuclides have beta endpoints: 100Tc at 3.2 MeV, 104Rh at 2.4 MeV, 110Ag at 2.9 MeV, and 142Pr at 2.2 MeV.
- The precursor capture cross-sections are: 99Tc 17.0 b, 103Rh 127 b, 109Ag 80.9 b, and 141Pr 6.53 b.
- The analytic model predicts a 4% correction for a reactor with φ = 10¹⁵ n/cm²/s after 5 years of irradiation.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of low-energy corrections to reactor antineutrino spectra, which previously included the spent fuel correction (Zhou 2012) and the non-equilibrium correction (Mikaelyan 2002, Kopeikin 2001, Mueller 2011). The nonlinear correction is distinct because it depends on neutron flux and irradiation time, not just on fission rates. The results show that the widely-used cumulative beta spectra measurements from Schreckenbach (1985) and Hahn (1989) at the ILL reactor are unaffected by this correction due to their short irradiation times. The correction will be important for future experiments like JUNO (Han 2015) and for geoneutrino searches where reactor backgrounds must be subtracted.
