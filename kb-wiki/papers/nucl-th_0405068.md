# Centrality Dependence of Chemical Freeze-out in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> We will report centrality dependence of chemical freeze-out temperature ($T_{ch}$), light quark chemical potential ($μ_{q}$), strange quark chemical potential ($μ_{s}$), and strangeness saturation factor ($γ_{s}$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 130 and 200 GeV. A systematic study for combination of ratios for chemical freeze-out fit is studied and we have found small ombination dependences. The results show $γ_{s}$ increasing with centrality but the other parameters have less sensitivity to the centralities.

# Centrality Dependence of Chemical Freeze-out in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

## Key claim
The chemical freeze-out temperature, light quark chemical potential, and strange quark chemical potential show no significant centrality dependence in Au+Au collisions at both 130 and 200 GeV, but the strangeness saturation factor increases with centrality and reaches full equilibration only in central collisions, with the rise being rapid up to approximately 100-150 participants and then slowly increasing.

## Method
The authors applied a grand canonical statistical model to fit particle ratios measured at mid-rapidity by the BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR experiments at RHIC. They used the MINUIT package to perform chi-squared minimization for six different combinations of particle ratios (ranging from only pions, kaons, and protons up to including multi-strange hadrons like Omega) to extract the chemical freeze-out parameters T_ch, mu_q, mu_s, and gamma_s as functions of the average number of participants.

## Key numbers
- T_ch values range from approximately 143 to 169 MeV across all centralities and beam energies, with no systematic centrality dependence
- mu_q values range from approximately 6.4 to 13.2 MeV, showing small but finite net baryon density at mid-rapidity
- mu_s values are consistent with zero, ranging from approximately -3.0 to 3.5 MeV
- gamma_s increases from about 0.70 in peripheral collisions (N_part ~ 14-48) to about 1.03 in central collisions (N_part ~ 322-345) at 200 GeV
- At 130 GeV, gamma_s ranges from about 0.70 (N_part = 48) to 0.98 (N_part = 317) depending on the data set combination
- The variation in fitted parameters across different ratio combinations is about 10 MeV for T_ch, 4 MeV for mu_q, 1 MeV for mu_s, and 0.1 for gamma_s at 130 GeV; at 200 GeV the variation is smaller: about 5 MeV, 2 MeV, 2 MeV, and 0.06 respectively

## Context
This work extends the statistical model approach previously applied at GSI-SIS and CERN-SPS energies (as summarized by Cleymans and collaborators in the 1990s) to RHIC energies, using the first centrality-dependent measurements of multi-strange hadrons. The results confirm the universal chemical freeze-out temperature of 150-170 MeV seen in earlier work by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999) and Becattini et al. (1996), while demonstrating that the strangeness saturation factor gamma_s, which was 0.5-0.8 at SPS energies, reaches unity only in central RHIC collisions. The centrality dependence of gamma_s enables future studies of how the approach to strangeness equilibrium correlates with system size and energy density in heavy-ion collisions.
