# Effects of the ground state correlations on the structure of vibrational states

> A method to treat the ground state correlations beyond the RPA is presented. A set of nonlinear equations taking into account effects of the ground state correlations on the pairing and phonon-phonon coupling is derived. The influence of such correlations on properties of the vibrational states in spherical nuclei is studied.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to treat ground state correlations (GSC) beyond the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) by deriving a set of nonlinear equations that self-consistently incorporate the effects of GSC on both pairing and phonon-phonon coupling, and applies this to study vibrational states in spherical nuclei. The key hedge is that the method is demonstrated only for separable effective forces and for a single nucleus, \(^{68}\)Zn, with the claim that the most consistent approach (EQPMPP) gives "rather good description of experimental data in general" but with the caveat that three-phonon terms are omitted due to numerical complexity.

## Method

The authors start from the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM) Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon mean field, pairing, and separable multipole residual forces. They derive a system of nonlinear equations (9)–(14) that determine phonon energies, amplitudes, and quasiparticle distributions \(q_j\) in the ground state, extending the RPA by including blocking factors \((1 - q_{jj'})\) that account for Pauli principle corrections due to ground state correlations. Using these ERPA phonons as a basis, they generalize the QPM equations to include one- and two-phonon configurations, with the quasiparticle-phonon coupling matrix elements renormalized by the GSC, and solve the resulting secular equation (33) for the energies and wave functions of low-lying states in \(^{68}\)Zn.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole strength parameter for the ERPA calculation is \(\kappa^{(2)}_0 = 0.0259\) MeV/fm, compared to the RPA value of \(\kappa^{(2)}_0 = 0.0227\) MeV/fm and the critical RPA constant of 0.0242 MeV/fm.
- The octupole strength parameter is \(\kappa^{(3)}_0 = 0.0235\) MeV/fm for RPA and \(\kappa^{(3)}_0 = 0.0250\) MeV/fm for ERPA.
- Inclusion of two-phonon terms (QPM) reduces the interior bump of the charge transition density for the first \(2^+\) state by 17% relative to RPA.
- The ERPA alone suppresses interior oscillations by 9% compared to RPA.
- The full EQPM approach gives a 30% reduction of the interior charge transition density \(\rho_1^{(2)}(r)\) in the nuclear interior region.
- The proton two-quasiparticle configuration \(\{2p_{3/2}, 2p_{3/2}\}\) contributes about 40% to the norm of the first quadrupole phonon in \(^{68}\)Zn according to RPA calculations.

## Context

This work extends the Renormalized RPA (ERPA) approach previously developed by the same group (Karadjov et al., 1993, 1994) to include phonon-phonon coupling, generalizing the standard QPM equations of Soloviev (1992) to account for ground state correlations. It directly addresses the known problem that RPA overestimates the interior oscillations of charge transition densities in spherical nuclei, as noted in comparisons with experimental data from inelastic electron scattering (Heisenberg, 1984) and earlier RPA studies (Sansur, 1991; Kim, 1991). The method enables more realistic calculations of vibrational state properties, and the authors note that the finite rank separable approximation for Skyrme forces (Goriely et al., 1998) could allow extension to more realistic nuclear interactions beyond the separable forces used here.
