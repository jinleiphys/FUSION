# Implications of the first evidence for coherent elastic scattering of reactor neutrinos

> The recent evidence for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) in the NCC-1701 germanium detector using antineutrinos from the Dresden-II nuclear reactor is in good agreement with standard model expectations. However, we show that a $2σ$ improvement in the fit to the data can be achieved if the quenching factor is described by a modified Lindhard model. We also place constraints on the parameter space of a light vector or scalar mediator that couples to neutrinos and quarks, and on a neutrino magnetic moment. We demonstrate that the constraints are quite sensitive to the quenching factor at low recoil energies by comparing constraints for the standard Lindhard model with those by marginalizing over the two parameters of the modified Lindhard model.

## Key claim

The NCC-1701 germanium detector data are consistent with the standard model, but a modified Lindhard model for the quenching factor (with a negative parameter q) improves the fit by approximately 2σ compared to the standard Lindhard model; the constraints on new physics are highly sensitive to the choice of quenching factor model at low recoil energies.

## Method

The authors analyzed the residual event spectrum from the NCC-1701 3 kg p-type point contact germanium detector exposed to antineutrinos from the 2.96 GW Dresden-II reactor at ~10 m distance for 96.4 days (289.2 kg·day exposure). They performed χ² fits to 20 bins in measured energy from 0.2 to 0.4 keV_ee, comparing the standard Lindhard model and a modified Lindhard model with two free parameters (k and q) against the data, and then placed constraints on light vector Z' mediators, light scalar mediators, and neutrino magnetic moments under different quenching factor assumptions.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit modified Lindhard model: k = 0.167, q = -22.2 × 10⁻⁵, with χ²_min = 8.14 for 17 degrees of freedom
- Standard Lindhard model (k = 0.157, q = 0): χ²_min = 14.34 for 19 degrees of freedom
- For fixed k = 0.157, q < 0 is preferred at 2.5σ significance
- Best-fit light Z' mediator (standard Lindhard): m_Z' = 63.1 MeV, g_Z' = 1.4 × 10⁻⁴, χ²_min = 9.09/17 d.o.f.
- Best-fit light scalar mediator (standard Lindhard): m_φ = 25.1 MeV, g_φ = 1.6 × 10⁻⁵, χ²_min = 7.77/17 d.o.f.
- Best-fit neutrino magnetic moment (standard Lindhard): μ_ν = 2.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B, χ²_min = 11.71/18 d.o.f.
- 90% CL bound on neutrino magnetic moment from NCC-1701: μ_ν < 4.0 × 10⁻¹⁰ μ_B
- Reactor thermal power: P = 2.96 GW; distance: d = 10 m; exposure: t = 289.2 kg·day
- Detector parameters: σ_n = 68.5 eV (electronic noise), η = 2.96 eV (electron-hole pair creation energy), F ≈ 0.105 (Fano factor)

## Context

This work extends the first evidence for CEνNS from reactor antineutrinos reported by the NCC-1701 collaboration (Colaresi 2021) and builds on earlier quenching factor measurements by Collar (2021) that showed deviations from the Lindhard model below ~1 keV_nr, though Bonhomme (2022) found no such discrepancy. The analysis improves upon previous reactor CEνNS constraints from CONNIE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2019) and CONUS (Bonet 2020), and provides new bounds on light mediators that complement those from the COHERENT experiment (Akimov 2017, 2020). The results enable future reactor CEνNS experiments to better understand quenching factor systematics and to search for new physics at low energies.
