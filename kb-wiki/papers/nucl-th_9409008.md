# Thermal photon production in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We use a boost-invariant one-dimensional (cylindrically symmetric) fluid dynamics code to calculate thermal photon production in the central rapidity region of S+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy ($\sqrt{s}=20$ GeV/nucleon). We assume that the hot matter is in thermal equilibrium throughout the expansion, but consider deviations from chemical equilibrium in the high temperature (deconfined) phase. We use equations of state with a first-order phase transition between a massless pion gas and quark gluon plasma, with transition temperatures in the range $150 \leq T_c \leq 200$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including transverse expansion in the fluid-dynamical model destroys the previously suggested monotonic correlation between the apparent temperature of the photon spectrum and the QCD transition temperature, so that the transition temperature cannot be determined from the shape of the single photon spectrum alone; however, the total production rate may still be sensitive enough to determine the transition temperature when compared to data.

## Method

The authors use a boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric one-dimensional fluid dynamics code employing the Godunov method to simulate the space-time evolution of hot matter in S+Au and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy (20 GeV/nucleon). They assume thermal equilibrium throughout the expansion but allow deviations from chemical equilibrium in the quark-gluon plasma by scaling the quark and antiquark densities by a fixed fraction. The equations of state feature a first-order phase transition between a massless pion gas and a quark-gluon plasma, with transition temperatures between 150 and 200 MeV, and the photon production rate includes the effect of the a1 resonance.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature for S+Au collisions: T0 = 355 MeV for standard equilibration time tau0 = 0.2 fm/c.
* Initial temperature for Pb+Pb collisions: T0 = 441 MeV for tau0 = 0.2 fm/c and T0 = 258 MeV for tau0 = 1 fm/c.
* Fit temperature extracted from preliminary WA80 data for S+Au: Tfit = 213 MeV, using the fitting formula in the pT range 1 to 2 GeV.
* For the standard parameter set with x=0.9, Tfit is about 3 MeV higher than for x=1.
* The total four-volume of the mixed phase is about 25% larger for tau0 = 1 fm/c than for tau0 = 0.2 fm/c.
* Excluding the a1 resonance decreases the photon yield by about one-third in the transverse-momentum range 1 to 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions by Seibert (1993) that suggested a correlation between the apparent temperature of photon spectra and the QCD transition temperature, but finds that transverse expansion destroys this monotonic relationship. The calculation uses a fluid-dynamical approach similar to that of Srivastava and Sinha (1994) and incorporates the a1 resonance contribution as parametrized by Xiong, Shuryak, and Brown (1992). The results enable future comparisons with WA80 data and Pb+Pb collision data to constrain the equilibration time and transition temperature, though the authors note that a more realistic equation of state including pion masses may further improve agreement with data.
