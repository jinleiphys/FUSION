# Gravitational Wave from Phase Transition inside Neutron Stars

> In this work, we propose a new source for gravitational wave (GW) radiation associated with the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) phase transition in the inner cores of neutron stars. The mechanism is based on the bubble dynamics during the first-order phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter. We identify the characteristic frequency to be of order $ω_c\sim 10^6~{\rm rad/s}$ for this kind of sources and the strain magnitude ($h\sim 10^{-24}$ for a neutron star at a distance of $0.1~{\rm Mpc}$) reachable by future GW detectors. The GW spectra are shown to be useful to check the transition nature at high baryon chemical potential as well as to constrain the radius and density of the inner cores, which are still indistinct up to now.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that gravitational waves (GWs) can be generated by the first-order QCD phase transition from nuclear matter to quark matter inside neutron star cores, with the characteristic frequency and strain magnitude making them potentially detectable by future GW detectors, provided the inner core is sufficiently large and the source is sufficiently close.

## Method

The authors model the phase transition using a renormalizable quark-meson model at zero temperature and finite baryon chemical potential, computing the effective potential for the sigma field. They simulate bubble nucleation via quantum tunneling, using the thin-wall approximation to determine bubble properties, and then calculate the GW strain and energy spectrum from bubble collisions using the envelope approximation, treating the false vacuum boundary as an effective second bubble.

## Key numbers

* Critical baryon chemical potential for the first-order phase transition: \(\mu_B^c = 957\) MeV for the chosen model parameters.
* Characteristic GW angular frequency: \(\omega_c \sim 6\pi \times 10^5\) rad/s for a core radius \(R_c = 1\) km.
* GW strain magnitude: \(h \sim 10^{-24}\) for a neutron star at distance \(L = 0.1\) Mpc.
* Latent heat density in the quark-meson model: \(\varepsilon_v = 3.74 \times 10^7\) MeV\(^4\).
* Total GW radiation energy: \((10^{-13} - 10^{-11}) M_\odot\) for a source at \(L = 0.1\) Mpc.
* GW absorption rate by the outer nuclear matter core: \(\gamma \lesssim 0.03\) for \(\omega_c \sim 6\pi \times 10^5\) rad/s.
* Inner core radius range from theoretical studies: \(R_c = 0-3\) km.

## Context

This work extends the mechanism of GW generation from first-order phase transitions, previously studied in the context of the early universe by Turner (1990), Kosowsky (1991, 1992), and others, to the dense interior of neutron stars. It differs from the periodic quasi-normal modes of hybrid neutron stars studied by Sotani (2001) and Marranghello (2002). The proposed GW signal could constrain the radius and density of inner cores and test whether the QCD transition is first-order at high baryon chemical potential, complementing constraints from binary neutron star mergers detected by LIGO and Virgo.
