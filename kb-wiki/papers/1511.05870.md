# Lattice calculation of the HVP contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of muon

> We report our (HPQCD) progress on the calculation of the Hadronic Vacuum Polarisation contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of muon. In this article we discuss the calculations for the light (up/down) quark connected contribution using our method described in Phys.Rev. D89(2014) 11, 114501 and give an estimate for the disconnected contribution. Our calculation has been carried out on MILC Collaboration's $n_f = 2+1+1$ HISQ ensembles at multiple values of the lattice spacing, multiple volumes and multiple light sea quark masses (including physical pion mass configurations).

## Key claim

The HPQCD collaboration reports a preliminary lattice QCD result for the light (up/down) quark connected contribution to the hadronic vacuum polarisation (HVP) part of the muon anomalous magnetic moment, achieving a precision of approximately 2% after applying a rescaling procedure that removes dependence on the rho meson mass and corrects for pion-loop effects; the final total HVP contribution is not yet given at the same precision level because the disconnected contribution is only estimated as a systematic bound.

## Method

The calculation uses the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) discretisation on MILC collaboration's n_f = 2+1+1 gauge ensembles at three lattice spacings (approximately 0.15 fm, 0.12 fm, and 0.09 fm) with multiple light sea quark masses including the physical pion mass. The time-moments of local vector current correlators at zero spatial momentum are computed, and the vacuum polarisation function is reconstructed via a [2,2] Pade approximant; to improve signal-to-noise for the light quark case, the correlators are replaced by multi-exponential fits at large times beyond t* = 1.5 fm. The light quark results are rescaled by removing the pi-pi contribution using one-loop staggered finite-volume chiral perturbation theory and restoring it from continuum chiral perturbation theory, then scaling by powers of the rho meson mass to eliminate lattice artefacts.

## Key numbers

* Preliminary light quark connected contribution: a_mu^light = 598(11) x 10^(-10), including 1% QED and 1% isospin uncertainties added in quadrature.
* The light quark contribution is approximately 12 times larger than the strange quark contribution, partly due to a factor of 5 from electric charges.
* The disconnected contribution is estimated as a_mu,disc / a_mu,conn approximately -1.5(1.5)% using experimental values for the omega and rho meson masses and decay constants (m_rho = 0.775 GeV, f_rho = 0.21(1) GeV, m_omega = 0.783 GeV, f_omega = 0.20(1) GeV).
* The pi-pi contribution to the light quark HVP is approximately 70 x 10^(-10).
* The experimental discrepancy is a_mu^exp - a_mu^SM = 25(9) x 10^(-10), at approximately 3 sigma.

## Context

This work extends the HPQCD method previously developed for strange and charm quark contributions (Phys.Rev. D89 (2014) 11, 114501) to the more challenging light quark case, where the rho meson resonance dominates and the signal-to-noise ratio is poor. The rescaling procedure builds on an idea from the ETM Collaboration (Burger et al., 2013) to remove lattice artefacts by using the rho meson mass. The disconnected contribution estimate uses an approach comparing isoscalar (omega) and isovector (rho) correlators. The results aim to improve upon the current best determination from dispersion relations and e+e- to hadrons data, which has approximately 1% uncertainty, and to match the precision needed for upcoming Fermilab and J-PARC experiments that will reduce experimental uncertainty by a factor of 4.
