# Chiral spiral in the presence of chiral imbalance

> The phase diagram of the two-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (or chiral Gross-Neveu) model is characterized by an order parameter in the form of a chiral spiral. Its radius vanishes at a critical temperature, its period depends only on the chemical potential. We generalize these findings to chirally imbalanced systems by including a chiral chemical potential $μ_5$. The relationship between the present, static approach and a previous, time dependent one is traced back to a half-local symmetry which the NJL$_2$ model shares with massless Dirac fermions, but which has been neglected so far. The structure of chiral spiral matter is further elucidated by computing fermion and antifermion momentum distribution functions, using a Bogoliubov transformation.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of the two-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL2) model with a chiral chemical potential μ5 exhibits a single critical surface at a temperature Tc independent of both μ and μ5, meaning the chiral spiral order parameter's radius depends only on temperature and its pitch only on μ, with no phase transition as a function of μ or μ5.

## Method

The author solves the thermal Hartree-Fock equations for the NJL2 model in the 't Hooft limit (N → ∞, Ng2 constant) at finite temperature T, chemical potential μ, and chiral chemical potential μ5. A local chiral transformation is used to gauge away the chemical potentials, reducing the problem to the known zero-chemical-potential case with a temperature-dependent dynamical fermion mass m. The mean fields are then reconstructed as a chiral spiral S = m cos 2μx, P = -m sin 2μx, independent of μ5. Momentum distribution functions for quarks and antiquarks are computed via a Bogoliubov transformation relating chiral spiral spinors to vacuum spinors.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: Tc = m0 eγE / π, where m0 is the zero-temperature dynamical fermion mass and γE is the Euler constant; this value is independent of μ and μ5.
- At T = 0, the energy density difference from vacuum: (E(μ,μ5) - E(0,0))/N = μ2/(2π) + μ52/(2π).
- At T = 0, the axial charge density: ρ5/N = μ5/π.
- At T = 0, the momentum density: P/N = μ μ5 / π.
- The invariant mass squared of a chirally imbalanced chunk of matter of size L: M2 = N2 L2 ((μ2 - μ52)/(2π))2.

## Context

This work extends the known phase diagram of the NJL2 model at finite T and μ (Thies 2003, 2006) to include chiral imbalance via μ5, and clarifies the relationship between a static Hartree-Fock solution and a time-dependent boosted chiral spiral approach (Ref. [10] in the paper). The results agree with a variational calculation of an extended NJL2 model with Cooper pairing (Ref. [9]) but are shown to be a fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock solution. The paper also elucidates the physical structure of chiral spiral matter by computing quark and antiquark momentum distributions for the first time.
