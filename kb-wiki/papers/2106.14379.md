# Rise and fall of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ global polarization in semi-central heavy-ion collisions at HADES, NICA and RHIC energies from the core-corona model

> We compute the $Λ$ and $\overlineΛ$ global polarizations in semi-central heavy-ion collisions using the core-corona model where the source of $Λ$'s and $\overlineΛ$'s is taken as consisting of a high-density core and a less dense corona. We show that the overall properties of the polarization excitation functions can be linked to the relative abundance of $Λ$s coming from the core versus those coming from the corona. For low collision energies, the former are more abundant whereas for higher energies the latter become more abundant. The main consequence of this reversing of the relative abundance is that both polarizations peak at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\lesssim 10$ GeV. The exact positions and heights of these peaks depend not only on this reversal of relative abundances, but also on the centrality class, which is directly related to the QGP volume and lifetime, as well as on the relative abundances of $Λ$s and $\overlineΛ$s in the core and corona regions.The intrinsic polarizations are computed from a field theoretical approach that links the alignment of the strange quark spin with the thermal vorticity and modeling the QGP volume and lifetime using a Bjorken expansion scenario. We predict that the $Λ$ and $\overlineΛ$ global polarizations should peak at the energy range accessible to NICA and HADES.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that both Lambda and anti-Lambda global polarization peak at collision energies below about 10 GeV, with the exact peak positions and heights depending on the reversal of relative abundances of hyperons from the core versus corona regions, on centrality class, and on the relative abundances of Lambda and anti-Lambda in each region.

## Method

The authors model the source of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons in semi-central heavy-ion collisions as consisting of a high-density core (where quark-gluon plasma is formed) and a less dense corona (where particle production resembles p+p collisions). They compute the number of hyperons from each region using a critical participant density of 3.3 fm^-2, with core Lambda production scaling as the square of participants above this threshold and corona production using the p+p Lambda production cross-section. The intrinsic polarizations are calculated from a field-theoretical approach linking strange quark spin alignment with thermal vorticity, with the QGP volume and lifetime modeled via a Bjorken expansion scenario.

## Key numbers

- Critical participant density for QGP formation: n_c = 3.3 fm^-2
- Lambda production threshold energy: sqrt(s_th) approximately 2.55 GeV for p+p -> K+ + Lambda + p
- Average impact parameter used: b = 7.26 fm, corresponding to 10%-40% centrality class
- Strange quark mass used: m_s = 100 MeV
- Nuclear radius: R = 6.554 fm with skin depth a = 0.523 fm
- Scaling factor for core Lambda production: c = 0.0025
- Initial formation time range considered: tau_0 = 0.35 to 0.60 fm
- Fit parameter for low-energy w (Lambda/anti-Lambda ratio in corona): alpha = 0.0010 +/- 0.0003 for sqrt(s) < 15 GeV
- Fit parameters for high-energy w: A = -0.8603 +/- 0.0965, B = 0.4935 +/- 0.0314, C = -0.0324 +/- 0.0024 for sqrt(s) > 15 GeV

## Context

This work extends the core-corona model previously developed by Ayala et al. (2020) to predict Lambda and anti-Lambda global polarization at NICA and HADES energies, improving upon earlier work by accounting for a possible bias in s versus anti-s quark production at finite chemical potential. The results are compared with preliminary HADES data at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.42 GeV and STAR-BES data at sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, and the predicted peak location agrees with the three-Fluid Dynamics (3FD) model of Ivanov (2020), though the anti-Lambda trend differs. The model enables predictions for the energy range accessible to NICA and HADES experiments, where both polarizations are expected to peak before decreasing toward the Lambda production threshold.
