# Constraints on Phase Transitions in Neutron Star Matter

> Recent inference results of the sound velocity in the cores of neutron stars are summarized. Implications for the equation of state and the phase structure of highly compressed baryonic matter are discussed. In view of the strong constraints imposed by the heaviest known pulsars, the equation of state must be very stiff in order to ensure the stability of these extreme objects. This required stiffness limits the possible appearance of phase transitions in neutron star cores. For example, a Bayes factor analysis quantifies strong evidence for squared sound velocities $c_s^2 > 0.1$ in the cores of 2.1 solar-mass and lighter neutron stars. Only weak first-order phase transitions with a small phase coexistence density range $Δρ/ρ< 0.2$ (at the 68\% level) in a Maxwell construction still turn out to be possible within neutron stars. The central baryon densities in even the heaviest neutron stars do not exceed five times the density of normal nuclear matter. In view of these data-based constraints, much discussed issues such as the quest for a phase transition towards restored chiral symmetry, and the active degrees of freedom in cold and dense baryonic matter, are reexamined.

## Key claim

Recent sound velocity inferences from neutron star observations show that the equation of state must be very stiff to support the heaviest known pulsars, which strongly limits the possible appearance of phase transitions in neutron star cores. Only weak first-order phase transitions with a small phase coexistence density range (Delta rho/rho < 0.2 at the 68% level) in a Maxwell construction remain possible within neutron stars.

## Method

The authors summarize recent Bayesian inference results for the sound velocity in neutron star cores, using data from the heaviest known pulsars to constrain the equation of state. They perform a Bayes factor analysis to quantify evidence for lower bounds on the squared sound speed, and examine the allowed parameter space for first-order phase transitions using a Maxwell construction. The central baryon densities of even the heaviest neutron stars are estimated from these constraints.

## Key numbers

- Squared sound velocities c_s^2 > 0.1 are strongly supported by Bayes factor analysis in the cores of 2.1 solar-mass and lighter neutron stars.
- Only weak first-order phase transitions with a phase coexistence density range Delta rho/rho < 0.2 (at the 68% level) are possible within neutron stars.
- Central baryon densities in even the heaviest neutron stars do not exceed five times the density of normal nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends recent Bayesian inference results on the sound velocity in neutron star cores (e.g., work by the same authors and others in the field) to place quantitative constraints on phase transitions. The findings contradict scenarios that require strong first-order phase transitions or large density jumps in neutron star interiors, and they limit the possibility of a phase transition to restored chiral symmetry at the densities reached in neutron stars. The results enable more focused theoretical work on the equation of state and active degrees of freedom in cold dense baryonic matter, ruling out many proposed phase transition scenarios.
