# Production of $Y(4260)$ as a hadronic molecule state of $\bar{D}D_1 +c.c.$ in $e^+e^-$ annihilations

> We study the $Y(4260)$ production mechanism in $e^+e^-$ annihilations in the framework of hadronic molecules and investigate the consequence of such a picture in different decay channels. In the hadronic molecule picture the $Y(4260)$ is described as a mixture state composed of a long-ranged $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ molecule state and a compact $c\bar{c}$ component. We show that the compositeness relation can still provide a reasonable constraint on the wavefunction renormalization parameter due to the dominance of the molecular component. Such a mechanism can be regarded as a natural consequence of the heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) breaking. This study elaborates the molecular picture for the $Y(4260)$ in the $e^+e^-$ annihilations and affirms that the cross section lineshape of $e^+e^-\to \bar{D}D^*π+c.c.$ in the vicinity of the $Y(4260)$ should have a nontrivial behavior. In this framework we predict that the upper limit of the $Y(4260)$ leptonic decay width is about 500 eV. We also investigate the coupling for $D_1(2420)\to D^*π$ in the $^3P_0$ quark model and examine the possible HQSS breaking effects due to the deviation from the $|^1P_1\rangle$ and $|^3P_1\rangle$ ideal mixing. This will in turn provide a constraint on the HQSS breaking coupling for the $Y(4260)$ to $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ via its $c\bar{c}$ component.

## Key claim

The Y(4260) is predominantly a hadronic molecule of \(\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.\) with a small admixture of a compact \(c\bar{c}\) component, and its production in \(e^+e^-\) annihilations is possible only through heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) breaking effects, which allow a small \(S\)-wave coupling between the molecular and compact components.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) propagator for the Y(4260) as a mixture of a \(\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.\) molecule and a compact \(c\bar{c}\) component, using Weinberg's compositeness theorem to relate the wavefunction renormalization constant to the molecular probability. They fitted the BESIII data for the angular distribution of the recoiled pion and the \(\bar{D}D^*\) invariant mass spectrum, together with Belle data for the \(e^+e^-\to D^0D^{*-}\pi^+\) cross section, to extract the bare couplings and the renormalization constant. The HQSS breaking effects were independently examined using the \(^3P_0\) quark model to study the mixing between the \(|^1P_1\rangle\) and \(|^3P_1\rangle\) states of the \(D_1(2420)\).

## Key numbers

- The probability of the compact \(c\bar{c}\) component inside the Y(4260) is \(\alpha^2 = Z = 0.132 \pm 0.003\), and the probability of the \(\bar{D}D_1+c.c.\) molecular component is \(\beta^2 = 1-Z = 0.868 \pm 0.003\).
- The fitted pole mass of the Y(4260) is \(m_Y = 4.217 \pm 0.002\) GeV, and the non-\(\bar{D}D_1\) width is \(\Gamma^{\text{non}-\bar{D}D_1} = 0.056 \pm 0.003\) GeV.
- The predicted upper limit of the Y(4260) leptonic decay width is \(\Gamma(Y(4260)\to e^+e^-) \simeq 483\) eV.
- The total width of the Y(4260) extracted from the propagator at the pole position is \(\Gamma_{Y}^{\text{total}} = 73.0 \pm 3.5\) MeV.
- The partial width for the \(S\)-wave component of \(\bar{D}D^*\pi\) is \(56.54 \pm 4.24\) MeV, and for the \(D\)-wave component is \(8.37 \pm 0.95\) MeV.
- The bare coupling \(y^\text{bare}\) is \((10.88 \pm 0.10)\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\), and the effective coupling after renormalization is \(y^\text{eff} = (3.94 \pm 0.04)\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecule picture for the Y(4260) proposed by Ding (2008), Wang (2013), and Cleven (2013), and addresses the concern raised by Li (2013) that in the HQSS limit the \(\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.\) molecule should decouple from the \(S\)-wave \(c\bar{c}\) component, making production in \(e^+e^-\) annihilations problematic. The study shows that HQSS breaking effects, quantified via the \(^3P_0\) quark model and the mixing angle of the \(D_1\) states, are sufficient to account for the observed cross sections. The predicted leptonic decay width of about 500 eV is significantly larger than the 23 eV estimate of Dai (2012) and also larger than the LQCD hybrid scenario upper limit of 40 eV from Chen (2016), providing a clear observable to distinguish between molecular and hybrid interpretations. Future precise measurements of the cross section lineshape in \(\bar{D}D^*\pi\) at BESIII are identified as crucial for confirming the predicted nontrivial behavior.
