# Two-Body Electrodisintegration of $^3$He at High Momentum Transfer

> The $^3$He$(e,e^\prime p)$$d$ reaction is studied using an accurate three-nucleon bound state wave function, a model for the electromagnetic current operator including one- and two-body terms, and the Glauber approximation for the treatment of final state interactions. In contrast to earlier studies, the profile operator in the Glauber expansion is derived from a nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude, which retains its full spin and isospin dependence and is consistent with phase-shift analyses of two-nucleon scattering data. The amplitude is boosted from the center-of-mass frame, where parameterizations for it are available, to the frame where rescattering occurs. Exact Monte Carlo methods are used to evaluate the relevant matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator. The predicted cross section is found to be in quantitative agreement with the experimental data for values of the missing momentum $p_{\rm m}$ in the range (0--700) MeV/c, but underestimates the data at $p_{\rm m} \simeq 1$ GeV/c by about a factor of two. However, the longitudinal-transverse asymmetry, measured up to $p_{\rm m} \simeq$ 600 MeV/c, is well reproduced by theory. A critical comparison is carried out between the results obtained in the present work and those of earlier studies.

# Two-Body Electrodisintegration of $^3$He at High Momentum Transfer

## Key claim

The predicted cross section for $^3$He$(e,e^\prime p)d$ agrees quantitatively with experimental data for missing momenta $p_{\rm m}$ in the range (0–700) MeV/c, but underestimates the data at $p_{\rm m} \simeq 1$ GeV/c by about a factor of two; the longitudinal-transverse asymmetry, measured up to $p_{\rm m} \simeq 600$ MeV/c, is well reproduced by theory.

## Method

The authors used accurate three-nucleon bound state wave functions from the AV18/UIX Hamiltonian model solved with the correlated hyperspherical-harmonics method, combined with a model for the electromagnetic current operator including one- and two-body terms. Final state interactions were treated using the Glauber approximation, where the profile operator was derived from a nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude retaining full spin and isospin dependence, boosted from the center-of-mass frame to the rescattering frame. Exact Monte Carlo methods with Gaussian quadrature integration over radial and angular variables were used to evaluate matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator.

## Key numbers

- The predicted cross section agrees with experimental data for missing momenta $p_{\rm m}$ in the range (0–700) MeV/c, but underestimates data at $p_{\rm m} \simeq 1$ GeV/c by about a factor of two.
- The longitudinal-transverse asymmetry was measured up to $p_{\rm m} \simeq 600$ MeV/c and is well reproduced by theory.
- The momentum transfer $q = 1.50$ GeV/c and energy transfer $\omega = 0.84$ GeV for the reaction kinematics studied.
- The number of deuterons in $^3$He is calculated to be about 1.34, implying roughly 90% of $T=0$ nucleon pairs are in the deuteron state.
- The $N$$N$ scattering amplitude parameterizations are for $s$ values in the range (3.92–5.09) GeV, corresponding to lab kinetic energies (210–831) MeV.
- The $p$$p$ inelastic cross section at 0.5 GeV increases abruptly from about 2 mb to 30 mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier Glauber approximation studies of $A(e,e^\prime p)$ processes by Ciofi degli Atti (2005) and others, but differs by retaining the full spin and isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude and avoiding the factorization approximation. Previous calculations by Benhar (2000), Ryckebusch (2003), and Ciofi degli Atti (2005) typically included only the spin-independent term in the scattering amplitude. The results enable improved theoretical description of high-missing-momentum knockout reactions, relevant for experiments such as $^4$He$(\vec e,e^\prime \vec p)^3$H where proton polarizations are measured.
