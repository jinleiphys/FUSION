# Modification of $Δ(1232)$ in the neutrino nucleus reaction

> The remarkable observations in the pion induced and charge exchange reactions on a nucleus are the significant shift and broadening of the $Δ(1232)$-peak relative to free pion nucleon scattering. For the forward going massless leptons, the weak process $(ν_l,l)$ on a nucleon, according to Adler's partially conserved axial current theorem, is connected to the pion nucleon scattering. This mechanism is also applicable to the nucleus. Therefore, the modification of the $Δ$-peak in a nucleus can be seen in the $(ν_l,l)$ reaction since it is connected to the pion nucleus scattering. To investigate this issue quantitatively, the double differential cross sections of the forward going ejectile $l$ energy distribution in the $(ν_l,l)$ reaction are calculated in the $Δ$-excitation region for both proton and nucleus. The measured pion nucleon and pion nucleus scattering cross sections in the quoted energy region are used in these reactions as input. Since the $(ν_l,l)$ reaction is connected to the pion induced scattering, the features of the previous reaction is shown analogous to those of the later.

## Key claim

The modification (shift and broadening) of the Δ(1232) peak in nuclei, previously observed in pion-induced and charge-exchange reactions, can also be seen in the (ν_l,l) reaction on nuclei because Adler's PCAC theorem connects the weak process to pion-nucleus scattering, provided the outgoing lepton is forward-going and massless (or nearly massless).

## Method

The double differential cross section dσ/dE_l dΩ_l for the forward-going lepton energy distribution in the (ν_l,l) reaction on proton and nuclei was calculated in the Δ-excitation region using Adler's PCAC theorem. The measured total cross sections σ_t(πA) for π^+ proton and inclusive π^+ nucleus scattering from Refs. [barn] and [clou] were used as input in the formalism, thereby incorporating all reaction mechanisms. For exclusive and semi-exclusive channels on ^12C, the measured partial cross sections for elastic, inelastic, and absorption channels of pion-nucleus scattering from Ref. [ashe] were used separately.

## Key numbers

- The shift of the Δ-peak in ^12C nucleus relative to proton is significantly large, approximately 50–60 MeV, and increases with nuclear size.
- The Fermi coupling constant used is G_W ≈ 1.15 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}.
- The pion weak decay constant f_π = 0.13 GeV is used in the cross section formula.
- The electron mass correction factor C_lm is found very close to unity in the Δ-excitation region.
- Calculations are presented at a fixed neutrino beam energy of E_ν = 1 GeV for both (ν_e,e) and (ν_μ,μ) reactions.
- The MiniBooNE neutrino flux distribution (Fig. 27 in Ref. [agui]) is used for flux-averaged cross section calculations.

## Context

This work extends the study of Δ(1232) modification from pion-nucleus scattering (Bion (year), Clough (year)) and charge-exchange reactions like (^3He,t) (Contardo (year)) and (p,n) (Bjork (year)) to neutrino-nucleus reactions using Adler's PCAC theorem (Adler (year)). The results show that the Δ-peak shift in the inclusive (ν_l,l) reaction arises from exclusive and semi-exclusive channels (elastic pion production, inelastic pion production, and pion absorption) that occur in nuclei but not for free protons, analogous to the findings in hadron-induced reactions. This enables future studies of Δ dynamics in neutrino-nucleus scattering, with the calculated flux-averaged cross sections directly comparable to measurements from experiments like MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo (year)).
