# Quark-diquark string tension, excited baryonic resonances and thermal fluctuations

> We study the baryonic fluctuations from second to eighth order involving electric charge, baryon number and strangeness below the quark-gluon plasma crossover and numerically known from lattice QCD calculations. By considering a particular realization of the Hadron Resonance Gas model, we provide evidence on the dominant role of quark-diquark degrees of freedom to describe excited baryonic resonances. After proving by means of suitable Polyakov loop correlators that the quark-diquark and the quark-antiquark forces coincide, $V_{\bar q q } (r) = V_{\bar q D } (r) + \textrm{const}$, we find that the corresponding susceptibilities can be saturated with excited baryonic states in a quark-diquark model picture.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that excited baryonic resonances can be described by quark-diquark degrees of freedom, and that the quark-diquark potential coincides with the quark-antiquark potential up to an additive constant, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the baryonic spectrum effectively conforms to a two-body system interacting with a linearly growing potential, as suggested by the observed M^6 growth of the cumulative number of baryonic states rather than the expected M^12.

## Method

The authors used a quark-diquark model Hamiltonian with a linear-plus-Coulomb potential V_qD(r) = -tau/r + sigma r + mu, with tau = pi/12 and sigma = (0.42 GeV)^2, and solved it variationally using a 3-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator basis. They computed baryonic susceptibilities from second to eighth order within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model using the quark-diquark spectrum, and fitted the model parameters (constituent quark mass m_cons, strange current quark mass hat_m_s, and constant mu) to lattice QCD data for baryonic fluctuations from Bazavov (2012).

## Key numbers

- String tension: sigma = (0.42 GeV)^2
- Constituent quark mass: m_u,d = 0.3 GeV
- Strange current quark mass: hat_m_s = 0.10 GeV
- Non-strange scalar diquark mass: m_D,ns = 0.6 GeV
- Diquark mass difference: Delta m_D = 0.21 GeV
- Constant term in potential: mu = -0.459 GeV
- Harmonic oscillator basis parameter range: 0.55 fm less than or equal to b less than or equal to 0.65 fm
- Lowest-lying state for BB and BQ susceptibilities: M_0^BB = M_0^BQ = M_p (proton mass, 938 MeV)
- Lowest-lying state for BS susceptibility: M_0^BS = M_Lambda0 (Lambda baryon mass)
- Temperature range for fits: T less than or equal to 165 MeV
- Good description of lattice data achieved for T less than or equal to 160 MeV for higher-order susceptibilities

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas approach to baryonic fluctuations by incorporating a quark-diquark model for excited baryons, building on earlier quark-diquark studies by Santopinto (2004), Ferretti (2011), and Gutierrez (2014). The authors compare their results to lattice QCD calculations from Borsanyi (2011) and Bazavov (2012), and to the Relativized Quark Model spectrum of Capstick (1986). The finding that the quark-diquark potential equals the quark-antiquark potential agrees with recent lattice studies by Koma (2017). This work enables future studies of baryonic fluctuations at higher orders and provides a framework for identifying missing baryonic states in the Particle Data Group listings.
