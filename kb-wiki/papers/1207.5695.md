# Microscopic two-nucleon overlaps and knockout reactions from $^{12}$C

> The nuclear structure dependence of direct reactions that remove a pair of like or unlike nucleons from a fast $^{12}$C projectile beam are considered. Specifically, we study the differences in the two-nucleon correlations present and the predicted removal cross sections when using $p$-shell shell-model and multi-$\hbarω$ no-core shell-model (NCSM) descriptions of the two-nucleon overlaps for the transitions to the mass $A$=10 projectile residues. The NCSM calculations use modern chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon (NN+3N) interactions. The $np$-removal cross sections to low-lying $T$=0, $^{10}$B final states are enhanced when using the NCSM two-nucleon amplitudes. The calculated absolute and relative partial cross sections to the low energy $^{10}$B final states show a significant sensitivity to the interactions used, suggesting that assessments of the overlap functions for these transitions and confirmations of their structure could be made using final-state-exclusive measurements of the $np$-removal cross sections and the associated momentum distributions of the forward travelling projectile-like residues.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using multi-$\hbar\omega$ no-core shell-model (NCSM) descriptions of two-nucleon overlaps, rather than conventional $p$-shell shell-model descriptions, enhances the predicted $np$-removal cross sections to low-lying $T=0$, $^{10}$B final states; however, the calculated absolute and relative partial cross sections show significant sensitivity to the specific chiral NN+3N interactions used, suggesting that final-state-exclusive measurements could discriminate between different nuclear Hamiltonians.

## Method

The authors used the eikonal reaction model in the isospin formalism to calculate two-nucleon removal cross sections from a $^{12}$C projectile on a carbon target at 250, 1050, and 2100 MeV per nucleon. They constructed two-nucleon overlap functions using both conventional $p$-shell shell-model calculations (with WBP and PJT interactions) and ab-initio NCSM calculations with chiral N$^3$LO NN plus N$^2$LO 3N interactions (labeled ncsm1 and ncsm2) for model spaces up to $N_{max}=6$ major oscillator shells, and also performed calculations with the chiral 3N interaction switched off (ncsm3). The two-nucleon amplitudes (TNA) from these structure calculations were used to compute inclusive and exclusive cross sections and momentum distribution widths for transitions to $^{10}$B and $^{10}$C final states.

## Key numbers

- The experimental $\sigma_{-np}:\sigma_{-nn}$ ratio for a $^{12}$C beam at 2.1 GeV per nucleon was 8.54, compared to a simple pair-counting factor of 8/3.
- For $^{10}$B inclusive cross sections at 2100 MeV per nucleon: WBP calculation gave 19.02 mb, ncsm1 gave 25.00 mb, ncsm2 gave 26.01 mb, and the experimental value was 35.10 $\pm$ 3.40 mb.
- For $^{10}$C inclusive cross sections at 2100 MeV per nucleon: WBP gave 5.04 mb, ncsm1 gave 6.22 mb, ncsm2 gave 7.28 mb, and the experimental value was 4.11 $\pm$ 0.22 mb.
- The ratio of theoretical to experimental cross sections for ncsm1 was $R_s(2n)=0.66$ for $nn$-removal and $R_s(np)=1.40$ for $np$-removal; for ncsm2, $R_s(2n)=0.56$ and $R_s(np)=1.35$.
- The cross section ratio $\sigma_{-2N}^{WBP}/\sigma_{-2N}^{NCSM}$ for ncsm1 was 0.81 for the $nn$ channel and 0.76 for the $np$ channel; for ncsm2, 0.69 and 0.73 respectively.
- The $[p_{3/2}]^2$ two-nucleon amplitude for the $^{10}$B $3^+$ ground state was 1.976 (WBP), 1.913 (ncsm1), 2.213 (ncsm2), and 1.644 (ncsm3).

## Context

This work extends the earlier eikonal reaction model analysis of two-nucleon removal from $^{12}$C by Simpson et al. (previous), which used only $p$-shell shell-model calculations and found that experimental $np$-removal cross sections were factors of 1.45 to 2.2 larger than theoretical predictions. The present study introduces ab-initio NCSM overlap functions with chiral NN+3N interactions, showing that the larger model spaces enhance cross sections but still underpredict the $np$ channel. The results demonstrate that the $A=10-12$ mass region is ideal for testing details of nuclear Hamiltonians, particularly the role of three-nucleon interactions, and that final-state-exclusive measurements of $np$-removal cross sections and momentum distributions could provide constraints on the underlying nuclear interactions.
