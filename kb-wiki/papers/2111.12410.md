# Influence of triaxiality on the description of low-energy excitation spectrum of $^{96}$Zr

> The observed properties of the low-lying collective states of $^{96}$Zr are investigated within the geometrical collective model. The quadrupole-collective Bohr Hamiltonian with the potential having spherical and axially-symmetric deformed minima is applied. The role of triaxiality is investigated by rotating the potential in $γ$-degree of freedom so that the deformed minimum occurs at various axially asymmetric shapes. The change of excitation energies and reduced matrix elements of quadrupole transitions with increase of triaxiliaty is analyzed.

## Key claim
The paper investigates how introducing triaxiality (non-axial deformation) into the potential energy of the Bohr Hamiltonian affects the description of low-energy collective states in \(^{96}\)Zr, concluding that the deformed minimum must be axially symmetric or have only a slight degree of triaxiality to reproduce experimental data, particularly the \(B(E2; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) transition probability.

## Method
The authors used the geometrical collective model with a quadrupole-collective Bohr Hamiltonian, where the potential energy has both a spherical and a deformed minimum separated by a barrier. They rotated the potential in the \(\gamma\) degree of freedom to shift the deformed minimum to various axially asymmetric shapes (\(\gamma_0\) from \(0^\circ\) to \(30^\circ\)), then diagonalized the Hamiltonian to compute excitation energies and reduced electric quadrupole transition matrix elements as functions of the rotation angle.

## Key numbers
- The deformed minimum was fixed at \(\beta = 0.24\), based on the experimental value of \(B(E2; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_2)\).
- The parameter \(C_\gamma\) was fixed at 50 MeV to reproduce a \(\gamma\)-vibration frequency close to 1.5 MeV.
- For \(\gamma_0 = 0^\circ\) (axial symmetry), calculated energies: \(E(0^+_2) = 1.58\) MeV, \(E(2^+_1) = 1.72\) MeV, \(E(2^+_2) = 2.24\) MeV; experimental values are 1.58 MeV, 1.75 MeV, and 2.23 MeV respectively.
- For \(\gamma_0 = 30^\circ\) (maximal triaxiality), \(E(0^+_2) = 1.17\) MeV, \(E(2^+_1) = 1.51\) MeV, \(E(2^+_2) = 1.91\) MeV.
- The experimental value of \(B(E2; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1)\) is 2.8 W.u.; the calculation shows this transition probability decreases to zero as \(\gamma_0\) approaches \(30^\circ\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Mardyban, Sazonov, and colleagues (2020) which described \(^{96}\)Zr using a Bohr Hamiltonian with an axially symmetric deformed minimum. It tests the sensitivity of the shape-coexistence description to triaxiality, motivated by the abrupt shape change observed in Zr isotopes around A \(\approx\) 100 (Heyde, 2011; Garcia, 2016). The results enable future work to refine the potential for \(^{96}\)Zr and nearby nuclei by constraining the degree of triaxiality needed to match experimental transition probabilities.
