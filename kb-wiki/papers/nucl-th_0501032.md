# Antiprotonic hydrogen in static electric field

> Effects of the static electric field on the splitting and annihilation widths of the levels of antiprotonic hydrogen with a large principal quantum number (n=30) are studied. Non-trivial aspects of the consideration is related with instability of (p\bar{p})^*-atom in ns and np-states due to coupling of these states with the annihilation channels. Properties of the mixed nl-levels are investigated depending on the value of external static electric field. Specific resonance-like dependence of effective annihilation widths on the strength of the field is revealed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a static electric field of order 1000 V/cm induces annihilation widths of order 10^8 to 10^9 1/s in highly excited (n=30) antiprotonic hydrogen states by mixing np states with higher-l states, but the ns state contributes negligibly below 3 x 10^6 V/cm; a resonance-like dependence of widths and shifts on field strength appears for levels near the shift sign-change point.

## Method

The authors construct a non-hermitian Hamiltonian matrix for n=30 antiprotonic hydrogen states, including complex energy shifts (real parts: level shifts; imaginary parts: annihilation widths) for ns and np states from strong interaction, and a Stark interaction matrix element coupling states of different l at fixed n. They diagonalize this matrix for static electric fields up to 5000 V/cm to obtain the field-dependent complex energies of the mixed nl states, using hydrogen-like wave functions and input widths and shifts from prior work (Korenman and Yudin, citing Ref. 8).

## Key numbers

- Annihilation width of ns state at n=30: Gamma_ns/2 = 20.78 meV; shift epsilon_ns = 25.59 meV.
- Annihilation width of np state at n=30: Gamma_np/2 = 0.00671 meV; shift epsilon_np = 0.
- Critical field for mixing ns and np states at n=30: E_b ~ 3.3 x 10^6 V/cm.
- Critical field for mixing np and nd states at n=30: E_c ~ 2000 V/cm.
- Induced widths for states with l != 0 at E ~ 1000 V/cm: order 10^8 to 10^9 1/s.
- Shifts for most states at E up to 5000 V/cm: order 10^-5 eV.
- Radiative lifetime of circular orbit (n=30, l=29): tau_gamma = 2.09 microseconds.

## Context

This work extends the study of Stark mixing in exotic atoms from the well-known collisional (dynamical) Stark effect in hadronic hydrogen (discussed in Refs. 6 and 7) to the case of a static external electric field, which had not been previously treated. The results are relevant for the ASACUSA project at CERN's Antiproton Decelerator, which aims to directly observe antiprotonic hydrogen formation in rarefied gases; the calculated field-induced quenching rates must be accounted for in precision laser-spectroscopy experiments on long-lived (p-bar p) states, analogous to methods used for antiprotonic helium (Ref. 1).
