# Nuclear symmetry energy and its density slope at normal density extracted from global nucleon optical potentials

> Based on the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, it is shown that both the symmetry energy E$_{sym}(ρ)$ and its density slope $L(ρ)$ at normal density $ρ_0$ are completely determined by the global nucleon optical potentials that can be extracted directly from nucleon-nucleus scatterings, (p,n) charge exchange reactions and single-particle energy levels of bound states. Adopting a value of $m^*/m=0.7$ for the nucleon effective k-mass in symmetric nuclear matter at $ρ_0$ and averaging all phenomenological isovector nucleon potentials constrained by world data available in the literature since 1969, the best estimates of $E_{sym}(ρ_0)=31.3$ MeV and $L(ρ_0)=52.7$ MeV are simultaneously obtained. Uncertainties involved in the estimates are discussed.

# Nuclear symmetry energy and its density slope at normal density extracted from global nucleon optical potentials

## Key claim
The symmetry energy $E_{sym}(\rho_0)$ and its density slope $L(\rho_0)$ at normal nuclear density are completely determined by global nucleon optical potentials, but the best estimates of $E_{sym}(\rho_0)=31.3$ MeV and $L(\rho_0)=52.7$ MeV are obtained only under the assumption of a nucleon effective k-mass $m^*/m=0.7$ and by averaging all phenomenological isovector nucleon potentials constrained by world data since 1969, with uncertainties that cannot be rigorously quantified due to the diverse experiments and methods involved.

## Method
The authors applied the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem to derive analytical expressions for $E_{sym}(\rho)$ and $L(\rho)$ in terms of the isoscalar and isovector nucleon single-particle potentials and their momentum dependence. Using the effective k-mass approximation for the isoscalar potential and averaging all available energy-dependent phenomenological symmetry potentials from global optical model analyses of nucleon-nucleus scattering, (p,n) charge exchange reactions, and single-particle bound state energies, they evaluated these expressions at normal density $\rho_0$ with Fermi momentum $k_F=1.36$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers
- $E_{sym}(\rho_0) = 31.3$ MeV, obtained using $m^*/m = 0.7$ and $U_{sym}(\rho_0, k_F) = 26.11$ MeV
- $L(\rho_0) = 52.7$ MeV, obtained simultaneously from the same inputs
- The averaged symmetry potential is $U_{sym}(\rho_0, E) = 22.75 - 0.21E$ (MeV), with $E$ in MeV
- The uncertainty on $E_{sym}(\rho_0)$ is approximately $\pm 4.5$ MeV, from $\pm 1.0$ MeV (effective mass) and $\pm 3.5$ MeV (symmetry potential)
- The uncertainty on $L(\rho_0)$ is approximately $\pm 22.5$ MeV, from $\pm 2.0$ MeV ($L(1)$), $\pm 10.5$ MeV ($L(2)$), and $\pm 10$ MeV ($L(3)$)
- The symmetry potential at the Fermi momentum lies in the range $26.11 \pm 7.0$ MeV
- The slope parameter $b_{sym}$ has uncertainty $0.21 \pm 0.1$

## Context
This work extends the application of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem to directly connect the density slope of the symmetry energy to momentum-dependent nucleon optical potentials, a relationship derived analytically for the first time. Prior determinations of $L(\rho_0)$ from heavy-ion reactions (isospin diffusion, neutron/proton ratios, isoscaling), neutron-skin measurements, and giant dipole resonances had yielded values scattering between about 20 and 115 MeV, while $E_{sym}(\rho_0)$ was known to be around 28-34 MeV from liquid-drop model analyses of nuclear masses (Myers 1963, Pearson 1995, Pomorski 2003). The results provide a reference for calibrating the symmetry potential at abnormal densities predicted by many-body theories and for constraining neutron star properties such as the core-crust transition density and neutron-skin thickness.
