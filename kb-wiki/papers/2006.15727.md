# In medium properties of axion within a Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We estimate the axion properties i.e. its mass, topological susceptibility and the self-coupling within the framework of Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. PNJL model, where quarks couple simultaneously to the chiral condensate and to a background temporal quantum chromodynamics (QCD) gauge field, includes two important features of QCD phase transition, i.e. deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration. The Polyakov loop in PNJL model plays an important role near the critical temperature. We have shown significant difference in the axion properties calculated in PNJL model compared to the same obtained using Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. We find that both the mass of the axion and its self-coupling are correlated with the chiral transition as well as the confinement-deconfinement transition. We have also estimated the axion properties at finite chemical potential. Across the QCD transition temperature and/or quark chemical potential axion mass and its self-coupling also changes significantly. Since the PNJL model includes both the fermionic sector and the gauge fields, it can give reliable estimates of the axion properties, i.e. it's mass and the self-coupling in a hot and dense QCD medium. We also compare our results with the lattice QCD results whenever available.

## Key claim

The axion mass and self-coupling are correlated with both the chiral transition and the confinement-deconfinement transition, and the Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model predicts significantly different axion properties compared to the NJL model, particularly near the QCD transition temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential of the two-flavor PNJL model in the mean-field approximation, including a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction term that couples quarks to the axion field. They solve the coupled gap equations for the scalar condensate, pseudoscalar condensate, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, then evaluate the axion mass and self-coupling from the second and fourth derivatives of the effective axion potential at zero axion field.

## Key numbers

- Chiral transition temperature in the NJL model: \(T_c \sim 190\) MeV
- Chiral transition temperature in the PNJL model: \(T_c \sim 235\) MeV
- Confinement-deconfinement transition temperature in the PNJL model: \(T_c \sim 230\) MeV
- Pseudocritical temperature adopted for the PNJL model: \(T_c = 230\) MeV
- Cutoff parameter \(\Lambda = 590\) MeV, bare quark mass \(m = 6\) MeV, coupling \(g = 2.435/\Lambda^2\), with \(c = 0.2\) (so \(g_1 = (1-c)g\), \(g_2 = cg\))
- Pion mass \(m_\pi = 140.2\) MeV and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.6\) MeV used to fix parameters

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axion properties in the NJL model (Das and Mishra, 2020; Farias et al., 2019) by incorporating the Polyakov loop, which accounts for the confinement-deconfinement transition alongside chiral symmetry restoration. The PNJL model improves upon the NJL model by including a temporal background gluon field, addressing the missing gluonic degrees of freedom that become important near the QCD transition scale. The results enable more reliable estimates of axion mass and self-coupling in hot and dense QCD matter, relevant for axion dark matter abundance calculations and stellar cooling studies, and are compared with lattice QCD results where available.
