# Wee partons in large nuclei: from virtual dream to hard reality

> We construct a weak coupling, many body theory to compute parton distributions in large nuclei for $x\ll A^{-1/3}$. The wee partons are highly coherent, non--Abelian Weizsäcker--Williams fields. Radiative corrections to the classical results are discussed. The parton distributions for a single nucleus provide the initial conditions for the dynamical evolution of matter formed in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

For large nuclei, in the kinematic region \(x \ll A^{-1/3}\), the high density of partons introduces a scale \(\mu^2 \approx A^{1/3}\) fm\(^{-2}\) that allows a weak coupling expansion to compute quark and gluon distributions, provided the coupling constant \(\alpha_S(\mu^2)\) is small; the classical background field solution yields Weizsacker-Williams distributions scaled by \(\mu^2\) for transverse momenta \(k_t^2 \gg \alpha_S^2 \mu^2\), while for \(k_t^2 \ll \alpha_S^2 \mu^2\) the transverse momentum dependence changes and likely plateaus to a constant.

## Method

The authors formulate the low-\(x\) parton distribution problem as a many-body system in the presence of an external source: a sheet of static valence quarks localized in the longitudinal direction and uniform in the transverse direction. They write a partition function that includes a stochastic Gaussian average over the external color charge sources, introducing the parameter \(\mu^2\) as the average valence quark color charge squared per unit area. The classical Yang-Mills equations are solved in Light Cone gauge \(A_- = 0\) with a source \(J^\mu_a = \delta^{+\mu} \rho_a(x^+, \vec{x}_\perp) \delta(x^-)\), yielding a solution where the only nonzero fields are transverse vector fields that are zero ahead of the source and two-dimensional pure gauge fields behind it. Gluon distribution functions are then expressed as correlation functions of a two-dimensional Euclidean field theory, computed analytically in the weak coupling regime and via a Monte Carlo algorithm in the strong coupling regime.

## Key numbers

- The scale \(\mu^2\) is estimated as \(\mu^2 \approx 1.1 A^{1/3}\) fm\(^{-2}\), derived from \(\mu^2 = \rho_{\text{valence}} \langle Q^2 \rangle \equiv (3A / \pi R^2) (4/3) g^2\).
- For \(k_t^2 \gg \alpha_S^2 \mu^2\) (weak coupling regime), the gluon distribution per unit transverse area is \({1 \over \pi R^2} {dN \over dx d^2q_t} = {\alpha_S \mu^2 (N_c^2 - 1) \over \pi^2} {1 \over x q_t^2}\), the Weizsacker-Williams result scaled by \(\mu^2\).
- For \(k_t^2 \ll \alpha_S^2 \mu^2\) (strong coupling regime), the distribution is expected to behave as \({dN \over d^2 k_t} \sim {1 \over k_t^2 + \alpha_S \mu^2}\), plateauing to a constant at small momentum.
- The characteristic time scale for dissipation of nonlinearities in the time-dependent Weizsacker-Williams fields is \(\tau = 1/p_\perp \equiv 1/\alpha_S \mu\).

## Context

This work extends the low-\(x\) QCD problem to large nuclei, building on the BFKL approximation (which sums leading logs in \(x\) and gives a gluon distribution \(\sim x^{-\lambda}\) with \(\lambda = 4\alpha_S \log 2\)) and the DLLA approach (which sums leading logs in both \(x\) and \(k_t\)). It provides a first-principles weak-coupling framework for computing parton distributions in nuclei, enabling quantitative understanding of shadowing observed in fixed-target DIS experiments (E665, NMC) where \(F_2^A / A F_2^N\) shows depletion at \(x \leq 10^{-2}\) with an \(A^{0.9}\) dependence. The results also provide initial conditions for the dynamical evolution of matter formed in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC, potentially leading to higher temperatures, enhanced minijet production, and enhanced intrinsic strangeness and charm compared to partonic cascade models such as those by Geiger (1995).
