# The symmetric heavy-light ansatz

> The symmetric heavy-light ansatz is a method for finding the ground state of any dilute unpolarized system of attractive two-component fermions. Operationally it can be viewed as a generalization of the Kohn-Sham equations in density functional theory applied to N-body density correlations. While the original Hamiltonian has an exact Z_2 symmetry, the heavy-light ansatz breaks this symmetry by skewing the mass ratio of the two components. In the limit where one component is infinitely heavy, the many-body problem can be solved in terms of single-particle orbitals. The original Z_2 symmetry is recovered by enforcing Z_2 symmetry as a constraint on N-body density correlations for the two components. For the 1D, 2D, and 3D attractive Hubbard models the method is in very good agreement with exact Lanczos calculations for few-body systems at arbitrary coupling. For the 3D attractive Hubbard model there is very good agreement with lattice Monte Carlo results for many-body systems in the limit of infinite scattering length.

## Key claim

The symmetric heavy-light ansatz provides an exact relation for the ground state energy of any dilute unpolarized system of attractive two-component fermions by breaking the Z2 symmetry of the original Hamiltonian, solving the many-body problem in the limit where one component is infinitely heavy, and then enforcing Z2 symmetry as a constraint on N-body density correlations. The method is in very good agreement with exact Lanczos calculations for few-body systems at arbitrary coupling and with lattice Monte Carlo results for many-body systems in the limit of infinite scattering length, but the lowest filling approximation used in practice is an approximation that assumes most of the probability weight lies in the lowest N orbitals.

## Method

The method starts with the heavy-light Hamiltonian H_HL, which deletes the kinetic energy for the up spin while doubling the kinetic energy of the down spin, corresponding to masses m_up = infinity and m_down = m/2. For a fixed configuration of N infinitely heavy up spins at positions R, the down spins see static delta-function potentials and occupy single-particle orbitals; the ground state energy of the original Hamiltonian H is then expressed as a weighted average over all up-spin configurations and orbital fillings of the sum of orbital energies. The lowest filling approximation restricts the down spins to the N lowest orbitals for each up-spin configuration, and the Z2 symmetry constraint is imposed by requiring equality of the N-body up-spin and down-spin densities, yielding a fixed-point equation for the probability distribution |F_R|^2.

## Key numbers

* For the four-body system in three dimensions on a 4^3 lattice at unitarity, the ground state energy of H(theta) remains relatively constant for theta < 0.6, while at theta = 1 (the heavy-light Hamiltonian) there are 35 energy states with E_2,2 / E_2,2^0,free less than 0.25.
* The neutron scattering length is roughly -18 fm while the range of the interaction is comparable to the pion Compton wavelength, m_pi^-1 approximately 1.4 fm, so the unitarity point is approximately realized when the interparticle spacing is about 5 fm.
* The three-body system consisting of two up spins and one down spin collapses due to an attractive 1/r^2 potential when m_up is greater than or approximately equal to 13 m_down (Petrov 2002).

## Context

This work introduces a new theoretical method that generalizes the Kohn-Sham equations in density functional theory to N-body density correlations, rather than one-body densities. The method is tested against exact Lanczos calculations for the attractive Hubbard model in one, two, and three dimensions, and against lattice Monte Carlo results for the 3D attractive Hubbard model at unitarity (Burovski 2006, Lee 2004-2007). The approach enables predictions for much larger systems not yet accessible using alternate methods, and provides a heuristic picture of the competition between Fermi repulsion and attractive interactions.
