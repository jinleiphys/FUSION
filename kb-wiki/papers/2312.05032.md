# Off-lightcone Wilson-line operators in gradient flow

> Off-lightcone Wilson-line operators are constructed using local operators connected by time-like or space-like Wilson lines, which ensure gauge invariance. Off-lightcone Wilson-line operators have broad applications in various contexts. For instance, space-like Wilson-line operators play a crucial role in determining quasi-distribution functions (quasi-PDFs), while time-like Wilson-line operators are essential for understanding quarkonium decay and production within the potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) framework. In this work, we establish a systematic approach for calculating the matching from the gradient-flow scheme to the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme in the limit of small flow time for off-lightcone Wilson-line operators. By employing the one-dimensional auxiliary-field formalism, we simplify the matching procedure, reducing it to the matching of local current operators. We provide one-loop level matching coefficients for these local current operators. For the case of hadronic matrix element related to the quark quasi-PDFs, we show at one-loop level that the finite flow time effect is very small as long as the flow radius is smaller than the physical distance $z$, which is usually satisfied in lattice gradient flow computations. Applications include lattice gradient flow computations of quark/gluon quasi-PDFs, gluonic correlators related to quarkonium decay and production in pNRQCD, and spin-dependent potentials in terms of chromoelectric and chromomagnetic field insertions into a Wilson loop.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a systematic method for computing matching coefficients from the gradient-flow scheme to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme for off-lightcone Wilson-line operators in the limit of small flow time, using the one-dimensional auxiliary-field formalism to reduce the problem to matching of local current operators. The authors show at one-loop level that for hadronic matrix elements related to quark quasi-PDFs, the finite flow time effect is very small provided the flow radius is smaller than the physical distance \(z\), a condition usually satisfied in lattice gradient flow computations.

## Method

The authors employ the one-dimensional auxiliary-field formalism, which extends QCD by adding a Lagrangian term for a "heavy" auxiliary scalar field, to relate Wilson-line operators to products of local current operators. They then apply the gradient-flow formalism to these local current operators and perform a small flow-time expansion, computing one-loop matching coefficients between the gradient-flow scheme and the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme by setting external momenta to zero and subtracting infrared poles. The matching coefficients are decomposed into products of field matching coefficients (for the ringed fermion field, the "heavy" auxiliary field, and the flowed gluon field) and vertex matching coefficients, each calculated separately using Schwinger parametrization and dimensional regularization in Euclidean spacetime.

## Key numbers

* The one-loop "mass correction" for the "heavy" auxiliary field is \(\delta m = -\frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi}C_R\frac{\sqrt{2\pi}}{\sqrt{t}} + O(\alpha_s^2)\), where \(C_R = C_F\) for fundamental representation and \(C_R = C_A\) for adjoint representation, and \(t\) is the flow time.
* The one-loop matching coefficient for the "heavy" auxiliary field is \(\zeta_{h_v} = 1 - \frac{\alpha_s C_R}{4\pi}\log\left(2\mu^2 t e^{\gamma_E}\right) + O(\alpha_s^2)\).
* The one-loop matching coefficient for the ringed fermion field to the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) renormalized fermion field is \(\mathring{\zeta}_{\psi}= 1 + \frac{1}{2} \times\frac{\alpha_s}{4\pi}C_F\left[\log\left(2\mu^2 t e^{\gamma_E}\right)-\log(432)\right] + O(\alpha_s^2)\).
* The one-loop quark self-energy diagram \((c_2)\) contributes \(\frac{\alpha_s C_F}{4\pi}\left[\frac{1}{\epsilon_{\rm UV}}+1+\log\left(2\mu^2 t e^{\gamma_E}\right)\right]\).
* The one-loop quark self-energy diagram \((c_3)\) contributes \(-4\times\frac{\alpha_s C_F}{4\pi}\left[\frac{1}{\epsilon_{\rm UV}}+\frac{1}{2}+\log\left(2\mu^2 t e^{\gamma_E}\right)\right]\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative gradient-flow matching framework, previously applied to local operators, to non-local off-lightcone Wilson-line operators relevant for quasi-PDFs (Ji, 2013) and pseudo-PDFs (Radyushkin, 2017), as well as to gluonic correlators used in pNRQCD for quarkonium decay and production (Pineda, 1997; Brambilla, 1999, 2004, 2001, 2002, 2020, 2021, 2022). The approach builds on the one-dimensional auxiliary-field formalism for Wilson-line renormalization (Dorn, 1986) and extends earlier one-loop matching calculations for quasi-PDFs with gradient flow (Monahan, 2017) by providing a systematic method valid in the small flow-time limit. The results enable lattice gradient flow computations of quark and gluon quasi-PDFs, gluonic correlators for quarkonium physics in pNRQCD, and spin-dependent potentials from chromoelectric and chromomagnetic field insertions into Wilson loops.
