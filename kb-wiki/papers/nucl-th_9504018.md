# Relativistic Hydrodynamics for Heavy--Ion Collisions -- I. General Aspects and Expansion into Vacuum

> We present algorithms to solve relativistic hydrodynamics in 3+1--dimensional situations without apparent symmetry to simplify the solution. In simulations of heavy--ion collisions, these numerical schemes have to deal with the physical vacuum and with equations of state with a first order phase transition between hadron matter and a quark--gluon plasma. We investigate their performance for the one--dimensional expansion of baryon-free nuclear matter into the vacuum, which is an analytically solvable test problem that incorporates both the aspect of the vacuum as well as that of a phase transition in the equation of state. The dependence of the lifetime of the mixed phase on the initial energy density is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents numerical algorithms for solving relativistic hydrodynamics in 3+1 dimensions without simplifying symmetries, but tests them only for one-dimensional expansion of baryon-free nuclear matter into vacuum, where an analytical solution exists for comparison. The authors hedge that while ideal hydrodynamics may be applicable in the expansion stage of heavy-ion collisions, it is not justified for the initial stage at higher beam energies where non-equilibrium effects dominate.

## Method

The authors implemented two numerical algorithms: the SHASTA (Flux Corrected Transport) algorithm and the relativistic HLLE (Harten-Lax-van Leer-Einfeldt) Godunov-type scheme, solving the equations of ideal relativistic hydrodynamics for energy-momentum and charge conservation. They tested these algorithms against the analytically solvable problem of one-dimensional expansion of semi-infinite baryon-free nuclear matter into vacuum, using an equation of state with a first-order phase transition between hadron matter and quark-gluon plasma (MIT bag model matched to hadron matter EoS via Gibbs conditions). The performance was evaluated for both thermodynamically normal matter (where a simple rarefaction wave forms) and thermodynamically anomalous matter (where a rarefaction shock wave forms), and the lifetime of the mixed phase was studied as a function of initial energy density for finite expanding systems.

## Key numbers

- The Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy (CFL) criterion requires the ratio of time step width to spatial grid spacing to be less than unity, \(\Delta t / \Delta x \equiv \lambda < 1\); the SHASTA algorithm requires \(\lambda < 1/2\) to avoid excessive prediffusion.
- For the relativistic HLLE algorithm, the parameter \(\eta = 0.5\) in the Roe-averaged sound velocity formula was found to be a safe choice for all problems studied.
- The initial condition for the expansion problem sets energy density \(\epsilon(x,0) = \epsilon_0\) for \(x \leq 0\) and \(\epsilon(x,0) = 0\) for \(x > 0\), with velocity \(v(x,0) = 0\) for \(x \leq 0\) and \(v(x,0) = 1\) for \(x > 0\).
- The paper discusses BEVALAC energies (\(E_{Lab}^{kin} = 0.1 - 2\) AGeV), AGS energies (\(E_{Lab}^{kin} \simeq 11\) AGeV), CERN-SPS energies (\(E_{Lab}^{kin} \simeq 60 - 200\) AGeV), and RHIC/LHC energies (\(E_{CM} \geq 100\) AGeV) as contexts for heavy-ion collision simulations.

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies of relativistic hydrodynamics by Schneider et al. (1993) that tested algorithms on the Riemann problem for ideal gas equations of state, by now addressing two specific challenges for heavy-ion collision simulations: the presence of vacuum and first-order phase transitions in the equation of state. The paper builds on the Bjorken (1983) scaling solution for one-dimensional expansion and prior work on the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma using the MIT bag model, and it enables future full 3+1-dimensional simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies where initial conditions may be inhomogeneous and lack simplifying symmetries.
