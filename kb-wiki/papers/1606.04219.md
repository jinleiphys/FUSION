# Density dependence of the nuclear energy-density functional

> The explicit density (rho) dependence in the coupling coefficients of the non-relativistic nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) encodes effects of three-nucleon forces and dynamical correlations. The necessity for a coupling coefficient in the form of a small fractional power of rho is empirical and the power often chosen arbitrarily. Consequently, precision-oriented parameterisations risk overfitting and loss of predictive power. Observing that the Fermi momentum kF~rho^1/3 is a key variable in Fermi systems, we examine if a power hierarchy in kF can be inferred from the properties of homogeneous matter in a domain of densities which is relevant for nuclear structure and neutron stars. For later applications we want to determine an EDF that is of good quality but not overtrained. We fit polynomial and other functions of rho^1/3 to existing microscopic calculations of the energy of symmetric and pure neutron matter and analyze the fits. We select a form and parameter set which we found robust and examine the parameters' naturalness and the resulting extrapolations. A statistical analysis confirms that low-order terms like rho^1/3 and rho^2/3 are the most relevant ones. It also hints at a different power hierarchy for symmetric vs. pure neutron matter, supporting the need for more than one rho^a terms in non-relativistic EDFs. The EDF we propose accommodates adopted properties of nuclear matter near saturation. Importantly, its extrapolation to dilute or asymmetric matter reproduces a range of existing microscopic results, to which it has not been fitted. It also predicts neutron-star properties consistent with observations. The coefficients display naturalness. Once determined for homogeneous matter, EDFs of the present form can be mapped onto Skyrme-type ones for use in nuclei. The statistical analysis can be extended to higher orders and for different ab initio calculations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) can be written as a low-order polynomial expansion in the cubic root of the density (the Fermi momentum \(k_F \sim \rho^{1/3}\)), and that this expansion is physically motivated by effective field theory and Brueckner theory, but the authors explicitly state that this is an assumption they test numerically; they find that the expansion works well for symmetric nuclear matter but hints at a different power hierarchy for pure neutron matter.

## Method

The authors fit polynomial and other functions of \(\rho^{1/3}\) to existing microscopic variational calculations (the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) pseudodata) for the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM). They perform least-squares fits for all possible combinations of one to five parameters from the set \(\{c_0, c_1, c_2, c_3, c_{\ln}\}\) in the expansion \(\mathcal{E}/A = \text{kinetic energy} + \sum_{i=0}^3 c_i \rho^{1+i/3} + c_{\ln} \rho^2 \ln[\rho\,\text{fm}^3]\), using a weighted cost function that emphasizes the saturation region, and analyze the Hessian matrix eigenvalues to assess parameter sloppiness.

## Key numbers

* The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is taken as \(\varrho_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* For SNM with \(\beta=1\), the normalized cost function \(\chi^2_n\) for the two-parameter fit \(k=(0,1)\) is 0.001151, while for \(k=(0,2)\) it is 0.009435 and for \(k=(0,3)\) it is 0.035831.
* For PNM with \(\beta=1\), the two-parameter fit \(k=(0,2)\) gives \(\chi^2_n = 0.000719\), which is better than \(k=(0,1)\) with \(\chi^2_n = 0.001566\).
* The Hessian eigenvalue ratio (highest to lowest) for the three-parameter fit \(k=(0,1,2)\) in SNM is approximately \(10^3\), while for the five-parameter fit it exceeds \(10^5\), indicating overfitting.

## Context

This work extends earlier explorations of polynomial expansions in \(\rho^{1/3}\) for the nuclear EDF, such as those by Coc et al. (2004) and by Agrawal et al. (2006), but with the explicit goal of determining the relevant terms from homogeneous matter before applying to finite nuclei. The paper uses the APR variational results (Akmal, Pandharipande, Ravenhall, 1998) as pseudodata and compares its extrapolations to chiral EFT results from Drischler et al. (2014). The resulting functional can be mapped onto extended Skyrme-type functionals for use in finite nuclei, and the statistical analysis can be extended to higher orders and different ab initio calculations.
