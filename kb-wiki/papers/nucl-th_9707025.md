# Strangeness Photoproduction with the Saphir Detector

> Statistically improved data of total cross sections and of angular distributions for differential cross sections and hyperon recoil polarizations of the reactions γp --> K^+ Λand γp --> K^+ Σ^0 have been collected with the SAPHIR detector at photon energies between threshold and 2.0 GeV. Here total cross section data up to 1.5 GeV are presented. The opposite sign of Λand Σpolarization and the change of sign between forward and backward direction could be confirmed by higher statistics. A steep threshold behaviour of the K^+ Λtotal cross section is observed.

# Strangeness Photoproduction with the Saphir Detector

## Key claim
Statistically improved total cross sections and angular distributions for γp → K⁺Λ and γp → K⁺Σ⁰ are presented up to 1.5 GeV, but the analysis of data at higher energies (up to 2.0 GeV) is still in progress, so the results are preliminary for the full energy range.

## Method
Data were collected with the SAPHIR detector using a trigger on two charged particles in the final state. Events were reconstructed from three tracks, with a pπ⁻ subsample preselected by requiring the invariant mass of a positive-negative track pair to lie within the Λ mass range; the secondary Λ decay vertex was determined from the p and π⁻ tracks, while the remaining K⁺ track identified the primary vertex. The K⁺Λ and K⁺Σ⁰ channels were separated by kinematical fits, and background was removed via cuts on missing mass, pπ⁻ invariant mass, and vertex fit probabilities.

## Key numbers
- Total cross sections for γp → K⁺Λ and γp → K⁺Σ⁰ are presented for photon energies between threshold and 1.5 GeV.
- The K⁺Λ total cross section rises rapidly from threshold up to a pronounced maximum, with a steep threshold behavior observed.
- The Λ and Σ⁰ polarizations show opposite signs and a sign change between forward and backward directions, confirmed with higher statistics.
- The data are compared with chiral perturbation theory calculations (threshold region), isobar models (up to 1.5 GeV), Regge-based models (6–12 GeV), and quark models.

## Context
This work extends the earlier SAPHIR data of the same collaboration (Menze et al., prior thesis) by adding new data to improve statistics and reanalyzing previous results. It provides experimental constraints for chiral SU(3) dynamics calculations (which predict a cusp structure near the K⁺Σ⁰ threshold) and for isobar models that include s-, t-, and u-channel resonances. The data enable more stringent tests of theoretical approaches, though no existing model fully describes the polarization features in detail.
