# Polarized Deep Inelastic and Elastic Scattering From Gauge/String Duality

> In this paper, we investigate deep inelastic and elastic scattering on a polarized spin-1/2 hadron using gauge/string duality. This spin-1/2 hadron corresponds to a supergravity mode of the dilatino. The polarized deep inelastic structure functions are computed in supergravity approximation at large t' Hooft coupling $λ$ and finite $x$ with $λ^{-1/2}\ll x<1$. Furthermore, we discuss the moments of all structure functions, and propose an interesting sum rule $\int_{0}^{1} \textrm{d}x g_2(x, q^2) =0$ for $g_2$ structure function which is known as the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule in QCD. In the end, the elastic scattering is studied and elastic form factors of the spin-1/2 hadron are calculated within the same framework.

## Key claim

The authors compute polarized deep inelastic structure functions for a spin-1/2 hadron (modeled as a dilatino in the supergravity approximation) at large t'Hooft coupling \(\lambda\) and finite Bjorken-\(x\) with \(\lambda^{-1/2}\ll x<1\), finding that the parity-violating structure functions \(F_3, g_3, g_4, g_5\) are as large as \(F_2\) and that the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule \(\int_0^1 dx\, g_2(x,q^2)=0\) holds in this regime, but the first moment of \(g_1\) vanishes at large \(q^2\) because small-\(x\) contributions (axial vector Regge exchange) are not included in the supergravity calculation.

## Method

The authors use the hard wall model of AdS/CFT, where a spin-1/2 hadron is represented by a dilatino supergravity mode in \(AdS_5 \times S^5\) with a confinement scale \(\Lambda\). They compute the hadronic tensor \(W^{\mu\nu}\) for deep inelastic scattering of an \(\mathcal{R}\)-current off the dilatino by evaluating the matrix element of the current between initial and intermediate states, summing over radial excitations, and extracting the structure functions from the symmetric and antisymmetric parts of \(W^{\mu\nu}\). For elastic scattering, they set \(M_X^2=M^2\) and \(x=1\) and compute the Dirac and axial form factors from the same current matrix element.

## Key numbers

* The structure functions satisfy \(2F_1 = F_2 = F_3 = 2g_1 = g_3 = g_4 = g_5 = \pi A' \mathcal{Q}^2 (\Lambda^2/q^2)^{\tau-1} x^{\tau+1} (1-x)^{\tau-2}\) for \(\tau = \Delta - 1/2\).
* The \(g_2\) structure function is \(2g_2 = \left(\frac{1}{2x}\frac{\tau+1}{\tau-1} - \frac{\tau}{\tau-1}\right) \pi A' \mathcal{Q}^2 (\Lambda^2/q^2)^{\tau-1} x^{\tau+1} (1-x)^{\tau-2}\).
* The Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule \(\int_0^1 dx\, g_2(x,q^2) = 0\) is obtained from the \(n=1\) moment of \(g_2\).
* The first moment of \(g_1\) is \(\int_0^1 2g_1(x,q^2) dx = \pi A' \mathcal{Q}^2 (\Lambda^2/q^2)^{\tau-1} \frac{\Gamma(\tau-1)\Gamma(\tau+2)}{\Gamma(2\tau+1)}\), which vanishes as \(q^2 \to \infty\).
* The elastic form factors \(\mathcal{F}_1\) and \(\mathcal{F}_1^5\) are computed; the Pauli form factor \(\mathcal{F}_2\) is zero in this framework.

## Context

This work extends the gauge/string duality calculations of deep inelastic scattering by Polchinski and Strassler (2002), who computed the unpolarized structure functions \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) for spinless and spin-1/2 hadrons, to include all polarized structure functions (\(g_1, g_2, g_3, g_4, g_5\)) and the parity-violating \(F_3\). The results are obtained in the supergravity approximation valid for \(\lambda^{-1/2} \ll x < 1\); the authors note that small-\(x\) contributions from Pomeron exchange (for \(F_1, F_2\)) and axial vector Regge exchange (for \(g_1\)) are missing and will be needed to obtain finite moments at large \(q^2\). The elastic form factor calculation follows the same formalism and complements AdS/QCD studies of nucleon form factors by Hong, Brodsky, and Teramond (2007-2009).
