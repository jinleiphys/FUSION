# Lessons from fitting the lowest order energy independent chiral based $\bar{K}N$ potential to experimental data

> It is shown, that fitting parameters of a $\bar{K}N$ interaction model to different sets of experimental data can lead to physical conclusions which might provide a deeper insight into the physics of this multichannel system. The available experimental data are divided into three parts: the "classical" set consisting of the low-energy $K^-p$ cross sections and the threshold branching ratios, the SIDDHARTA $1s$ level shift in kaonic hydrogen and the CLAS photoproduction data. We have fitted the parameters of the potential to different combinations of these data. We found, that the two poles corresponding to the $I=0$ nuclear quasi-bound state ($\Lam$) and to the $K^-p$ $1s$ atomic level seem to resist to their simultaneous reproduction at the right place, though a more or less satisfactory compromise can be achieved. Potentials with the $\Lam$ pole pinned down close to the PDG value fail to reproduce the classical two-body data with an acceptable accuracy. We also added comments on two papers criticizing the potential used in the fits.

## Key claim
Fitting a simple, lowest-order energy-independent chiral-based $\bar{K}N$ potential to different combinations of experimental data reveals a fundamental tension: the pole corresponding to the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance and the $K^-p$ $1s$ atomic level shift cannot be simultaneously reproduced at their experimentally preferred values, though a workable compromise can be found.

## Method
The author used a five-channel separable potential based on the lowest-order Weinberg-Tomozawa term of chiral SU(3) meson-baryon interaction, with seven adjustable parameters (two meson decay constants $F_{\bar{K}}$ and $F_{\pi}$, and five range parameters $\beta_i$). The potential was fitted to different combinations of three data sets: the "classical" set (low-energy $K^-p$ cross sections and threshold branching ratios $\gamma$, $R_n$, $R_c$), the SIDDHARTA $1s$ level shift in kaonic hydrogen, and the CLAS photoproduction $\pi^0\Sigma^0$ missing mass spectrum. For the CLAS data, a two-step reaction model was used with the $t$-matrix from the fitted potentials, treating the production amplitude as a complex constant fitted to the data.

## Key numbers
- Fit A (classical data only): $\chi^2 = 0.915$; $\Lambda(1405)$ pole at $1422 - 20i$ MeV; $K^-p$ $1s$ level shift $\Delta E = 392 - 232i$ eV (experimental: $283 \pm 36 - (271 \pm 46)i$ eV).
- Fit B (classical data + SIDDHARTA $\Delta E$): $\chi^2 = 1.140$; $\Lambda(1405)$ pole at $1440 - 27i$ MeV (above $\bar{K}N$ threshold); $\Delta E = 277 - 329i$ eV.
- Fit C (classical data + $\Delta E$ + artificial $\Lambda(1405)$ pole at $1425 \pm 5 - (25 \pm 5)i$ MeV): $\chi^2 = 1.057$; $\Lambda(1405)$ pole at $1428 - 24i$ MeV; $\Delta E = 329 - 275i$ eV.
- Fit D (classical data + PDG $\Lambda(1405)$ at $1405 \pm 2 - (25 \pm 2)i$ MeV): $\chi^2 = 2.04$; $\Lambda(1405)$ pole at $1407 - 23i$ MeV; $\Delta E = 369 - 137i$ eV.
- Fit E (classical data + PDG $\Lambda(1405)$ + $\Delta E$): $\chi^2 = 2.78$; $\Lambda(1405)$ pole at $1408 - 25i$ MeV; $\Delta E = 352 - 143i$ eV.
- Experimental threshold branching ratios: $\gamma = 2.36 \pm 0.04$ (used with increased error $2.36 \pm 0.1$), $R_n = 0.189 \pm 0.015$, $R_c = 0.664 \pm 0.011$.
- Experimental SIDDHARTA $\Delta E$: $283 \pm 36 - (271 \pm 46)i$ eV (statistical and systematic errors added quadratically).
- PDG $\Lambda(1405)$ position: $1405^{+1.3}_{-1.0} - (25 \pm 1)i$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the line of chiral-based $\bar{K}N$ potentials designed for few-body calculations (class b) in the author's classification), following the author's own earlier work (Revai 2018a, 2018b). It directly confronts the tension between the $\Lambda(1405)$ pole position and the kaonic hydrogen level shift, previously noted by Ikeda (2012) who found that next-order chiral terms were needed to reproduce $\Delta E$. The paper also comments on criticisms by Mai and Meissner (2015) regarding the two-pole structure of $\Lambda(1405)$ and by Borasoy and Cieply (2017) regarding the chiral limit of the scattering length. The results enable a practical compromise potential (fit C) for use in calculations of few-body kaonic nuclear systems, while arguing against the strong $\bar{K}N$ binding implied by forcing the $\Lambda(1405)$ pole to the PDG value.
