# Effects of partial thermalization on HBT interferometry

> Hydrodynamical models have generally failed to describe interferometry radii measured at RHIC. In order to investigate this ``HBT puzzle'', we carry out a systematic study of HBT radii in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions within a two-dimensional transport model. We compute the transverse radii $R_o$ and $R_s$ as functions of $p_t$ for various values of the Knudsen number, which measures the degree of thermalization in the system. For realistic values of the Knudsen number estimated from $v_2$ data, we obtain $R_o/R_s \simeq 1.2$, much closer to data than standard hydrodynamical results. Femtoscopic observables vary little with the degree of thermalization. Azimuthal oscillations of the radii in non central collisions do not provide a good probe of thermalization.

# Effects of partial thermalization on HBT interferometry

## Key claim

For realistic values of the Knudsen number (K ≈ 0.3, estimated from elliptic flow data), the ratio R_o/R_s ≈ 1.2 is much closer to experimental data than standard ideal hydrodynamical results, showing that partial thermalization alone solves most of the HBT puzzle without requiring an early freeze-out or a soft equation of state.

## Method

The authors solved a 2+1 dimensional relativistic Boltzmann equation with massless particles and a constant isotropic partonic cross section, using a Gaussian initial density profile with σ_x = σ_y = 3 fm to mimic central Au-Au collisions at RHIC. They varied the Knudsen number K (ratio of mean free path to system size) to control the degree of thermalization from free-streaming (K → ∞) to ideal hydrodynamics (K → 0), and computed HBT radii from the rms widths of the space-time distribution of particles with the same outgoing momentum. Two initial momentum distributions were used: a locally thermal distribution and a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) parametrization from Krasnitz (2001) and Lappi (2003), both scaled to an average transverse momentum ⟨p_t⟩ = 420 MeV.

## Key numbers

- R_o/R_s ≈ 1.2 for K = 0.3 (the value obtained from fitting the centrality dependence of elliptic flow), compared to ideal hydrodynamical values of approximately 1.5
- Average transverse momentum per particle ⟨p_t⟩ = 420 MeV, corresponding to pions at top RHIC energy
- Gaussian initial density profile with σ_x = σ_y = 3 fm, corresponding to the rms width from an optical Glauber calculation
- Hydrodynamic calculations at t = 0.2 fm/c give the same radii as transport for high K; at t = 40 fm/c give nearly the same results as transport for small K
- For realistic K in the range [0.3, 0.5], azimuthal oscillation amplitude ratios deviate little from unity

## Context

This work addresses the "HBT puzzle" at RHIC, where ideal hydrodynamical models (e.g., those reviewed by the authors as Hydro, Hydro2, Hydro3) predicted R_o/R_s ≈ 1.5 while data from STAR (Adams, 2004) were compatible with 1. The study extends the transport model approach of Gombeaud (2007) to femtoscopic observables, showing that the same partial thermalization that explains the centrality dependence of elliptic flow also resolves most of the HBT puzzle. The results are consistent with the argument by Broniowski (2008) that early freeze-out in ideal hydrodynamics can explain the puzzle, and with Pratt's suggestion that multiple factors contribute, but demonstrate that partial thermalization alone is sufficient. The azimuthally sensitive HBT analysis shows that ratios of oscillation amplitudes are not good probes of thermalization, while the missing eccentricity reduction in data likely requires a soft QCD equation of state beyond this massless-particle model.
