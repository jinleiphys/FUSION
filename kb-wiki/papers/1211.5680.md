# Results on flow from the ALICE Collaboration

> This short overview includes recent results from the ALICE Collaboration on anisotropic flow of charged and identified particles in sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. We also discuss charge dependent and event plane dependent azimuthal correlations that are important in tests of the chiral magnetic effect, as well as understanding the dynamics of the system evolution and hadronization process. Lastly, we present ALICE results obtained with a new technique, the event shape engineering, which allows to perform a physical analysis on events with very large or small flow.

## Key claim

Anisotropic flow at LHC is large and in rough agreement with hydrodynamic model expectations, but new questions have arisen regarding initial geometry fluctuations, and the charge-dependent azimuthal correlations consistent with the chiral magnetic effect are also consistent with local charge conservation combined with large elliptic flow.

## Method

The ALICE Collaboration measured anisotropic flow of charged and identified particles in sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions using event plane methods from the VZERO detectors (separated from the TPC by two units of rapidity) and multiparticle cumulant methods (2-particle, 3-particle, and 4-particle). Nonflow effects were suppressed by using event planes from detectors separated in pseudorapidity, with remaining nonflow included in systematic errors. The event shape engineering technique selected events with large or small flow vector magnitude measured in one VZERO detector, with analysis performed in the TPC at different pseudorapidity.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow and triangular flow measured up to pt ≈ 20 GeV/c in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(sNN) = 2.76 TeV
- At pt > 10 GeV/c, elliptic flow results are well described by the WHDG model extrapolation to LHC energies
- Anisotropic flow is almost independent of pseudorapidity in the region |eta| < 2, decreasing at larger pseudorapidities
- Average elliptic and triangular flow measured up to eta = 5
- NCQ scaling holds at the level of about 10-15% in the range pt/n_q > 1 GeV/c
- Flow fluctuations are almost independent of pseudorapidity at all centralities and very similar at different transverse momenta up to pt ∼ 10 GeV/c
- Charge-dependent correlations relative to the second order reaction plane are remarkably similar in strength to those measured at RHIC
- The ratio of elliptic flow in events with large and small flow is approximately constant up to pt ≈ 10 GeV/c

## Context

This overview extends ALICE's earlier flow measurements published in Aamodt (2010) and ALICE (2011) to higher transverse momentum and larger pseudorapidity, and presents new results on charge-dependent correlations relevant to the chiral magnetic effect proposed by Kharzeev (2004). The measurements enable tests of the limiting fragmentation (longitudinal scaling) picture first observed at lower collision energies, and the event shape engineering technique introduced by Schukraft (2012) allows selection of events with unusually large or small eccentricities for unprecedented studies of system evolution and hadronization.
