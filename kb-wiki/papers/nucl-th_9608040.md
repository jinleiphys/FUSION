# Effects of in-medium vector meson masses on low-mass dileptons from SPS heavy-ion collisions

> Using a relativistic transport model to describe the expansion of the fire-cylinder formed in the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions at SPS/CERN energies, we study the production of dileptons with mass below about 1 GeV from these collisions. The initial hadron abundance and their momentum distributions in the fire-cylinder are determined by following the general features of the results from microscopic models based on the string dynamics and further requiring that the final proton and pion spectra and rapidity distributions are in agreement with available experimental data. ## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed enhancement of low-mass dileptons (invariant mass between 250 MeV and 1 GeV) in central S+Au and S+W collisions at SPS/CERN energies can be quantitatively explained by the decrease of vector meson masses in hot and dense hadronic matter due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry, but this explanation depends on the specific initial conditions of the fire-cylinder and the particular model used for in-medium mass modifications.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Li, Ko, and Brown, 1995) by including Œ∑, Œ∑‚Ä≤, œÜ, and a‚ÇÅ mesons omitted in the earlier Letter, and by using the original Walecka model instead of the nonlinear Walecka model to lower initial baryon density from unrealistically high values. The paper provides a quantitative explanation for the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES collaboration in central S+Au collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon and by the HELIOS-3 collaboration in central S+W collisions, which could not be explained by uncertainties in normalization procedures (Tserruya). The approach contrasts with lattice QCD simulations (Boyd et al., 1995) showing no significant change of rho-meson screening mass up to 0.92T_c, and with effective Lagrangian calculations (Song) showing rho meson mass unchanged at leading order in temperature, instead supporting the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis based on restoration of scale invariance. The results enable further experimental tests at SIS/GSI by the HADES collaboration and at higher energies, and provide a framework for studying the transition from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma through dilepton observables.
