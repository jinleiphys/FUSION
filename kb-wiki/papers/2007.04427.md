# The Equation of State of Nuclear Matter : from Finite Nuclei to Neutron Stars

> {\it Background.} We investigate possible correlations between neutron star observables and properties of atomic nuclei. Particularly, we explore how the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, $M_{1.4}$, and the neutron skin thickness of ${^{48}}$Ca and ${^{208}}$Pb are related to the stellar radius and the stiffness of the symmetry energy. {\it Methods.} We examine a large set of nuclear equations of state based on phenomenological models (Skyrme, NLWM, DDM) and {\it ab-initio} theoretical methods (BBG, Dirac-Brueckner, Variational, Quantum Monte Carlo). {\it Results.} We find strong correlations between tidal deformability and NS radius, whereas a weaker correlation does exist with the stiffness of the symmetry energy. Regarding the neutron skin thickness, weak correlations appear both with the stiffness of the symmetry energy, and the radius of a $M_{1.4}$. {\it Conclusion.} The tidal deformability of a $M_{1.4}$ and the neutron-skin thickness of atomic nuclei show some degree of correlation with nuclear and astrophysical observables, which however depends on the ensemble of adopted EoS.

## Key claim

The paper finds that correlations between neutron star observables (tidal deformability, radius) and nuclear properties (neutron skin thickness of 48Ca and 208Pb) exist but are ensemble-dependent, meaning the strength of these correlations depends on which set of nuclear equations of state is included in the analysis.

## Method

The authors examined a large set of nuclear equations of state (EoS) based on both phenomenological models (Skyrme, NLWM, DDM) and ab-initio theoretical methods (BBG, Dirac-Brueckner, Variational, Quantum Monte Carlo). They computed correlations between the tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the neutron skin thickness of 48Ca and 208Pb, the stellar radius, and the stiffness of the symmetry energy across this ensemble of EoS.

## Key numbers

- Tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star (M1.4) shows strong correlations with the neutron star radius.
- Tidal deformability of M1.4 shows weaker correlation with the stiffness of the symmetry energy.
- Neutron skin thickness of 48Ca and 208Pb shows weak correlations both with the stiffness of the symmetry energy and with the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing effort to connect laboratory measurements of nuclear properties (neutron skin thickness) to astrophysical observables (neutron star tidal deformability and radius). The study systematically compares multiple EoS families (phenomenological Skyrme, NLWM, DDM and ab-initio BBG, Dirac-Brueckner, Variational, Quantum Monte Carlo) to quantify how model-dependent these correlations are. The results enable future work to identify which specific EoS features drive the correlations and to guide experimental programs measuring neutron skins at facilities like Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
