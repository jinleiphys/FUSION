# The Structure of Light Nuclei and Its Effect on Precise Atomic Measurements

> This review consists of three parts: (a) what every atomic physicist needs to know about the physics of light nuclei; (b) what nuclear physicists can do for atomic physics; (c) what atomic physicists can do for nuclear physics. A brief qualitative overview of the nuclear force and calculational techniques for light nuclei will be presented, with an emphasis on debunking myths and on recent progress in the field. Nuclear quantities that affect precise atomic measurements will be discussed, together with their current theoretical and experimental status. The final topic will be a discussion of those atomic measurements that would be useful to nuclear physics, and nuclear calculations that would improve our understanding of existing atomic data.

# The Structure of Light Nuclei and Its Effect on Precise Atomic Measurements

## Key claim

This review argues that recent progress in understanding the nuclear force through chiral perturbation theory and Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations has made nuclear physics sufficiently reliable that nuclear quantities can now be calculated or measured with enough accuracy to meaningfully contribute to, and benefit from, precise atomic measurements, though nuclear uncertainties remain orders of magnitude larger than atomic ones.

## Method

The paper reviews the development of nuclear forces across three generations, from first-generation forces (pre-1993) through second-generation forces (beginning 1993) to third-generation forces incorporating chiral perturbation theory. It describes how Green's Function Monte Carlo techniques solve the Schrödinger equation for nuclei with up to ten nucleons, requiring solution of more than 200,000 coupled partial-differential equations in 27 continuous variables. The review then catalogs specific nuclear quantities that affect atomic measurements, charge radii, magnetic radii, quadrupole moments, and electric polarizabilities, and tabulates their current experimental and theoretical values for light nuclei from hydrogen through helium-4.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses extracted from nucleon-nucleon scattering data: m(π±) = 139.4(10) MeV and m(π0) = 135.6(13) MeV, agreeing with free pion masses (m(π±) = 139.57018(35) MeV, m(π0) = 134.9766(6) MeV)
- Proton charge radius: 0.880(15) fm from Coulomb scattering, 0.883(14) fm from two-loop analysis
- Deuteron charge radius: 2.130(10) fm; magnetic radius: 2.072(18) fm; quadrupole moment: 0.282(19) fm² (nuclear) and 0.2860(15) fm² (molecular)
- Tritium charge radius: 1.755(87) fm; helium-3 charge radius: 1.959(34) fm (nuclear) and 1.954(8) fm (atomic)
- Helium-4 charge radius: 1.676(8) fm
- Neutron charge radius squared: -0.1140(26) fm²; neutron magnetic radius: 0.873(11) fm
- Calculated electric polarizability of deuteron: 0.6328(17) fm³; experimental values: 0.61(4) fm³ and 0.70(5) fm³
- Calculated electric polarizability of tritium: 0.139 fm³; helium-3: 0.145 fm³; helium-4: 0.076 fm³
- Experimental electric polarizability of helium-4: 0.072(4) fm³
- Average virtual excitation energy for light nuclei: approximately 10 MeV, with deuteron giving about 6 MeV
- Nuclear length scale: R ~ 1 fm = 10⁻⁵ Å; atomic length scale: a₀ ~ 1 Å

## Context

This review extends the tradition of connecting nuclear structure to atomic precision measurements, building on the work of Ingo Sick (review of electron-nucleus scattering) and the Green's Function Monte Carlo developments by Joe Carlson and Steve Pieper. It addresses the needs of atomic physicists who require nuclear corrections for Lamb shift and hyperfine splitting calculations, as documented in the comprehensive review cited as "review" in the text. The paper enables future work by identifying specific nuclear quantities, particularly tritium radii, which have errors nearly an order of magnitude larger than deuterium, that would benefit from improved atomic measurements, and by establishing that three-nucleon forces with only three adjustable parameters can reproduce several dozen nuclear energy levels.
