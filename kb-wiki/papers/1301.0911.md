# An Outlook on Nuclear Physics

## Key claim
Low-energy nuclear physics has made significant progress in recent years, particularly in *ab initio* calculations of nuclear structure and reactions, but several open questions remain, especially regarding the role of three-nucleon forces, the origin of subbarrier fusion hindrance, and the site of r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Method
The paper reviews selected recent developments in nuclear structure theory, including *ab initio* calculations using lattice simulations, Green's function Monte Carlo techniques, and the no-core shell model with chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians. It also discusses subbarrier fusion reactions, rare isotope physics for nuclear astrophysics and fundamental symmetries, and connections between nuclear physics and quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at high energies.

## Key numbers
- The Hoyle state in $^{12}$C is a 7.65 MeV $0^+$ excited state, crucial for carbon formation in the Universe.
- $^{14}$C has a beta-decay half-life of approximately 5,730 years, enabling carbon-dating; three-nucleon forces reduce contributions from nucleon-nucleon interactions by an order of magnitude.
- The solar metallicity ratio was revised to $(Z/X)_{\odot} = 0.0178$ (Asplund 2009), compared to the previous value of $(Z/X)_{\odot} = 0.0229$ (Grevesse 1998).
- The viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio in strongly-coupled quark-gluon plasma approaches $\eta/s = \hbar/(4 \pi k_B)$, the conjectured lower limit from uncertainty principle arguments.

## Context
This conference summary extends the work of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences decadal study "Nuclear Physics: Exploring the Heart of Matter" (2012) by highlighting specific recent calculations such as Epelbaum et al. (2011) on the Hoyle state, Maris et al. (2011) on $^{14}$C, and Navratil et al. (2011) on the $^7$Be(p,$\gamma$)$^8$B reaction. It enables future work on neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements, r-process nucleosynthesis sites, and electric dipole moment searches in octupole-deformed nuclei like $^{225}$Ra.
