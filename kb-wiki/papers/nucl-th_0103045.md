# Coulomb Distortion in Quasielastic $(e,e')$ Scattering on Nuclei: Effective Momentum Approximation and Beyond

> The role of the effective momentum approximation to disentangle Coulomb distortion effects in quasielastic $(e,e')$ reactions is investigated. The separation of the cross section in longitudinal and transverse components is discussed including higher order DWBA corrections due to the focusing of the electron waves.The experimental studies performed, in the last few years, making use of different approximate treatments are shown to be sometime inconsistent. As a consequence some of the longitudinal and transverse responses, extracted from the inclusive cross sections cannot be considered reliable. A separation procedure based on the effective momentum approximation is discussed in connection with the recent experimental data on electron/positron quasielastic scattering on $^{12}$C and $^{208}$Pb.

# Coulomb Distortion in Quasielastic (e,e') Scattering on Nuclei: Effective Momentum Approximation and Beyond

## Key claim

The Effective Momentum Approximation (EMA) can reliably disentangle Coulomb distortion effects in quasielastic (e,e') reactions on heavy nuclei, provided the effective momentum transfer is determined experimentally rather than assumed from the potential at the origin; however, several recent experimental analyses that used different approximate treatments are inconsistent, and some extracted longitudinal and transverse responses cannot be considered reliable.

## Method

The author derives an analytic expansion of the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) cross section up to order (Zα)^2, building on the high-energy electron wave function formalism of Yennie, Boos and Ravenhall (1965) and Lenz and Rosenfelder (1971). The expansion separates leading-order focusing corrections (which preserve the classical Mott cross section) from higher-order phase-deformation effects, yielding a separable EMA form where the effective momentum transfer q_eff replaces the kinematic momentum q in the nuclear structure functions. The author then compares this EMA with the complete numerical DWBA calculations of Co' and Heisenberg (1987) and Kim et al. (1996), and reviews experimental data on 12C, 40Ca, 56Fe, 208Pb, and 238U.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the experimentally determined average Coulomb potential energy is |V_bar_C| = 18.9 ± 1.5 MeV, compared to the charge-density-weighted average of 20.1 MeV and the central value |V_C(0)| = 25.9 MeV (Gueye et al., 1999).
- Higher-order focusing corrections Δ_L and Δ_T remain within 0.5% in the quasielastic peak region, reaching 2–4% for high-ω forward angles or low-ω backward angles (model-dependent estimate).
- The longitudinal-transverse interference term S_int is negligible for nuclei as large as 208Pb, contributing less than 0.01% relative to S_L and S_T.
- For 208Pb, the Kim et al. (1996) LEMA approach introduces deviations of approximately +4% on the quasielastic peak and approximately -20% at higher energy transfer (when the cross section reaches 1/3 of its maximum) at θ = 140° and momentum transfer 400 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the analytic Coulomb correction framework of Czyz and Gottfried (1963), Yennie, Boos and Ravenhall (1965), and Rosenfelder (1980), and directly addresses the numerical DWBA calculations of Co' and Heisenberg (1987) and Kim et al. (1996). The paper demonstrates that the EMA, validated by the electron/positron scattering experiment of Gueye et al. (1999), provides a consistent separable form for extracting longitudinal and transverse responses. It calls for reanalysis of existing data on 238U (Blatchley et al., 1986), 208Pb (Zghiche et al., 1994), and 40Ca (Yates et al., 1993; Williamson et al., 1997), as well as Jourdan's (1995, 1996) analysis of 12C, 40Ca, and 56Fe, which the author argues contain inconsistent flux renormalization procedures.
