# Hadronic in-Medium Effects with Elementary Probes

> The sensitivity of dilepton production in elementary reactions of photons and pions with nuclei on in-medium changes of hadronic properties is studied. It is shown that this sensitivity is comparable to that encountered in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is also shown how a significant broadening of the mesons affects other photonuclear processes.

## Key claim

Pion- and photon-induced dilepton production on nuclei is as sensitive to possible in-medium changes of hadronic properties as ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, even though the baryon densities probed in such elementary reactions are always below normal nuclear matter density (rho_0), because the observed dilepton yield in heavy-ion collisions inherently integrates over the whole reaction history from low to high density and back.

## Method

The calculations use a new transport-theoretical treatment that transports a "spectral phase space density" F(x,p,mu,t) containing information about the spectral function of the resonance, rather than the usual phase-space density f(x,p,t). This method allows consistent transport of broad resonances and treats the widths of vector mesons consistently, with an ad-hoc potential that drives particles back on mass-shell when they leave the nucleus. Input cross sections are based on the Manley coupled channel analysis of pi N data.

## Key numbers

- The collision width for the rho meson at normal nuclear matter density reaches up to 500 MeV, and for the omega meson 70 MeV, both using realistic VN cross sections.
- The overall sensitivity to in-medium effects in pion-induced dilepton production is about a factor of 2, close to the expected systematic uncertainty of the transport calculations.
- In ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, about 60% of all dileptons are produced at densities below 2 rho_0.
- The N(1520) D13 resonance decays on resonance with a probability of 15-25% into N rho, even though this decay is energetically closed at the pole mass of the broad rho.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Effenberger et al. (1999) for transport-theoretical treatment of broad mesons, and addresses the same in-medium effects invoked to explain the CERES experiment's excess dilepton yield in the invariant mass region between 200 and 600 MeV. Prior approaches by Rapp (1999) and Koch (1999) attempted to explain these observations through rho-a1 mixing or standard hadronic sources, respectively. The paper enables future experiments with HADES using the pion beam at GSI and photon beam experiments with TAPS at MAMI to distinguish between different in-medium scenarios by comparing dilepton spectra from pion-induced and photon-induced reactions, which sample different density regions.
