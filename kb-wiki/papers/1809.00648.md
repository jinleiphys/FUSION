# Isolating dynamical net-charge fluctuations

> We modify the usual definitions of cumulants of net-charge fluctuations in a way that isolates dynamical fluctuations. The new observables, which we call dynamical cumulants, are robust with respect to trivial correlations induced by volume fluctuations and global charge conservation. We illustrate the potential of dynamical cumulants by carrying out Monte Carlo simulations where all correlations are trivial. The results of our simulations agree well with Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) data, and are used to illustrate that dynamical cumulants consistently isolate dynamical fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper introduces "dynamical cumulants" as modified observables for net-charge fluctuations that isolate genuine dynamical correlations by subtracting trivial contributions from volume fluctuations and global charge conservation, and demonstrates that existing RHIC data can be reproduced without any dynamical fluctuations when these non-dynamical effects are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors construct dynamical cumulants through a generating function that interpolates between ordinary cumulants (at acceptance fraction α=1) and factorial cumulants (at α→0), with explicit expressions derived up to order 4 for net-charge fluctuations. They perform Monte Carlo simulations where N+ and N− follow independent binomial (or Poisson) distributions at fixed impact parameter, with impact parameter fluctuations within centrality bins modeled using gamma distributions, and compare the resulting ordinary and dynamical cumulants to PHENIX and STAR data for Au+Au collisions at various energies.

## Key numbers

- For PHENIX data at √sNN = 62.4 GeV, the factorial cumulant f2 (equivalent to dynamical cumulant d2 since α≪1) is smaller than the ordinary cumulant κ2 by a factor of approximately 1000.
- For PHENIX data at √sNN = 19.6 GeV, the mean values of N+ and N− in central collisions are fixed to 11.5 and 8.25 respectively to match measured κ1 and κ2.
- For STAR data at √sNN = 19.6 GeV, the acceptance fraction is estimated as α ≈ 0.216, and the dynamical cumulant d2 is smaller than the ordinary cumulant κ2 by a factor of approximately 100.
- For STAR data at √sNN = 19.6 GeV, the mean values of N+ and N− in central collisions are chosen as 155 and 130 respectively to reproduce the magnitudes of κ1 and κ2.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of factorial cumulants (Mueller, 1971; Bzdak, 2012) by incorporating corrections for global charge conservation, and addresses the problem that ordinary cumulants of net-charge fluctuations measured at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2014; PHENIX Collaboration, 2015) are contaminated by volume fluctuations (Skokov, 2012; Braun-Munzinger, 2016) and global charge conservation (Bzdak, 2012). The dynamical cumulants enable cleaner comparisons with lattice QCD calculations (Bazavov, 2017) and ab-initio calculations of hadronic interactions, as they remove the ambiguity from volume fluctuations that complicates comparisons where the lattice volume is fixed.
