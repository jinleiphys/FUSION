# Harmonic-Oscillator-Based Effective Theory

> I describe harmonic-oscillator-based effective theory (HOBET) and explore the extent to which the effects of excluded higher-energy oscillator shells can be represented by a contact-gradient expansion in next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). I find the expansion can be very successful provided the energy dependence of the effective interaction, connected with missing long-wavelength physics associated with low-energy breakup channels, is taken into account. I discuss a modification that removes operator mixing from HOBET, simplifying the task of determining the parameters of an NNLO interaction.

## Key claim

Harmonic-oscillator-based effective theory (HOBET) can successfully represent the effects of excluded higher-energy oscillator shells by a contact-gradient expansion at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), but only if the energy dependence of the effective interaction, connected to missing long-wavelength physics from low-energy breakup channels, is properly taken into account by summing the kinetic energy operator to all orders before applying the expansion.

## Method

The Bloch-Horowitz equation was solved numerically for the av18 NN potential to generate exact effective Hamiltonians for the deuteron and 3He/3H in harmonic-oscillator model spaces. The Q-space rescattering contributions were then fit to a contact-gradient expansion in LO, NLO, and NNLO, using a redefinition of the gradient operators that removes operator mixing by acting on polynomials in r rather than plane waves. A reorganization of the Bloch-Horowitz equation was employed that sums the relative kinetic energy operator QT to all orders, isolating the short-range VGV term from the long-range physics associated with edge states at the P-Q boundary.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy reproduced as -2.21 MeV by the NNLO contact-gradient calculation in the 3S1-3D1 channel
- For the 3S1 channel, average error in NNLO matrix elements is about 100 keV (1% accuracy) when using the QT-summed reorganization
- For the 15 1S0 matrix elements, average error is about 10 keV (0.1% accuracy)
- The 0hw model space for 3He contains only 31.1% of the exact wave function probability; this grows to 85.5% at 8hw
- Sums to 140 hw were required for ~1 keV accuracy in deuteron binding energy with the standard Bloch-Horowitz equation
- Sums to 70 hw were required for ~10 keV accuracy in 3He/3H with the standard Bloch-Horowitz equation
- The standard BH formulation with P(T+V)P fails to bind the deuteron even with Lambda_P=10, while the reorganized BH equation reproduces correct binding for Lambda_P=6

## Context

This work extends the author's prior development of HOBET (Haxton, 2006) and addresses the combined ultraviolet/infrared problem in harmonic-oscillator bases previously identified by the same group. The approach contrasts with effective field theory methods that expand around k=0 (Weinberg, 1990; Savage, 1998) and with the Lee-Suzuki transformation used in no-core shell model calculations (Navratil et al., 2000). The results suggest that V-low-k potentials (Schwenk et al., 2003), derived in plane-wave bases where T is diagonal, would require similar renormalization when embedded into harmonic-oscillator shell-model spaces. The work enables a purely phenomenological determination of the shell-model effective interaction directly from data, without requiring knowledge of the bare Hamiltonian outside the model space.
