# Quasielastic Versus Inelastic and Deep Inelastic Lepton Scattering in Nuclei at x > 1

> We have made a thorough investigation of the nuclear structure function W_2A in the region of 0.8 < x < 1.5 and Q^2 < 20 GeV^2, separating the quasielastic and inelastic plus deep inelastic contributions. The agreement with present experimental data is good giving support to the results for both channels. Predictions are made in yet unexplored regions of x and Q^2 to assert the weight of the quasielastic or inelastic channels. We find that at Q^2 < 4 GeV^2 the structure function is dominated by the quasielastic contributions for x < 1.5, while for values of Q^2 > 15 GeV^2 and the range of x studied the inelastic channels are over one order of magnitude bigger than the quasielastic one. The potential of the structure function at x > 1 as a source of information on nuclear correlations is stressed once more.

## Key claim

The nuclear structure function W₂A in the region 0.8 < x < 1.5 and Q² < 20 GeV² is dominated by quasielastic contributions at Q² < 4 GeV² for x < 1.5, while at Q² > 15 GeV² the inelastic channels are over one order of magnitude larger than the quasielastic one; however, in the intermediate Q² region (4–15 GeV²) the results are subject to large uncertainties tied to the behavior of nucleon structure functions at x close to 1, preventing firm conclusions.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Feynman diagrammatic many-body framework with the local density approximation, incorporating nucleon spectral functions from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations and semiphenomenological self-energy evaluations. They separated the nuclear structure function W₂A into quasielastic contributions (evaluated from the imaginary part of the lepton self-energy using Sachs form factors) and inelastic plus deep inelastic contributions (using parametrizations of the nucleon structure function from Stein et al. (low Q²), MRS, and CTEQ). The calculations were performed for ⁵⁶Fe and compared with experimental data from SLAC at various beam energies and angles.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 1.27 GeV² (E = 3.595 GeV, θ = 20°), the quasielastic contribution dominates the structure function for x > 1, while at x = 0.8 the inelastic contribution is about 70% of the quasielastic one.
- At Q² = 3.1 GeV² (E = 3.595 GeV, θ = 39°), the quasielastic and inelastic contributions are about equal at x = 0.95.
- At Q² = 6.83 GeV² (E = 5.12 GeV, θ = 56.6°), the quasielastic and inelastic contributions are similar around x = 1.2, with inelastic dominating at lower x.
- At Q² = 20 GeV² and x = 1, results obtained with different nucleon structure function parametrizations differ by about a factor of 5.
- At Q² ≈ 1 GeV² and x = 1, results with different parametrizations differ by about a factor of 50.
- At Q² ≈ 85 GeV², results for F₂A from three different parametrizations (MRS, CTEQ, Duke and Owens) agree within 10% or less, and agree with experimental data within 30% for 0.8 < x < 1.3.
- The quasielastic contribution dominates for Q² < 3 GeV² at x = 1, while for Q² > 13 GeV² the inelastic contribution is an order of magnitude larger than the quasielastic one.
- For x < 1.5, the inelastic contribution dominates for Q² > 20 GeV² and the quasielastic contribution dominates for Q² < 4 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Marco and Oset (1996) for deep inelastic scattering at high Q² to the region of low and intermediate Q², including quasielastic contributions that were negligible at high Q². It complements the findings of Bodek et al. (1983) on the separation of quasielastic, inelastic, and deep inelastic regions, and confirms their qualitative conclusions about dominance regions while highlighting new uncertainties from nucleon structure function parametrizations. The results demonstrate that measurements of F₂A at x > 1 and high Q² (≈ 85 GeV²) are sensitive to nuclear correlations beyond the mean field approximation, as previously emphasized by Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1991), and that future experiments at TJNAF could help resolve discrepancies between different models for the nucleon structure function at x close to 1.
