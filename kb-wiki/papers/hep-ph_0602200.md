# Leading Chiral Corrections to the Nucleon Generalized Parton Distributions

> Using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory we study the leading chiral corrections to the complete set of nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs). We compute the leading quark mass and momentum transfer dependence of the moments of nucleon GPDs through the nucleon off-forward twist-2 matrix elements. These results are then applied to get insight on the GPDs and their impact parameter space distributions.

## Key claim

Using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, the authors compute the leading quark mass and momentum transfer dependence of the moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) through the nucleon off-forward twist-2 matrix elements, with the caveat that these results are valid only at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and apply to the low-energy regime below the chiral symmetry breaking scale (approximately 1 GeV).

## Method

The authors perform operator product expansions to convert the non-local quark bilinear operators defining GPDs into sums of local twist-2 operators, then match these to hadronic operators in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. They compute one-loop diagrams (including pion loop contributions and wave function renormalization) for the vector, axial vector, and tensor twist-2 matrix elements, extracting the non-analytic structures in the quark mass and momentum transfer dependence. The calculations are performed in the Breit frame using the standard heavy baryon expansion with expansion parameters ε = p/μχ, mπ/μχ, p/M, and mπ/M treated as the same order.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant Fπ = 93 MeV and the pion mass mπ ≃ 139 MeV are the relevant light scales in the calculation
- The axial-vector coupling constant gA = 1.26 is used in the chiral Lagrangian
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale μχ ∼ 4πFπ ∼ 1 GeV sets the upper validity bound of the theory
- For the isovector charge operator E1,0^3(0), the number of u quark minus d quark in a π+ meson is A1,0^π,3(0) = 2, ensuring this operator is not renormalized
- The isoscalar wave function renormalization contribution is C_M^0 = -3gA^2 mπ^2/(4πFπ)^2 ln(mπ^2/μ^2)
- The isovector wave function renormalization contributions are C_E^3 = C_C^3 = -(3gA^2+1)mπ^2/(4πFπ)^2 ln(mπ^2/μ^2)

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to hadronic twist-2 matrix elements previously developed by Arndt and Savage (2003) and Chen and Ji (2001) from forward parton distributions to the complete set of off-forward generalized parton distributions. The results provide model-independent extrapolation formulas for lattice QCD calculations of GPD moments (such as those from the QCDSF collaboration and the latest unquenched lattice results), enabling extraction of physical GPDs from lattice data computed at unphysical quark masses and large momentum transfers. The formalism also connects to earlier soft pion theorem results by Polyakov (1998) and Mankiewicz (1998), and enables future applications to the quark orbital angular momentum contribution to proton spin as formulated by Ji (1997).
