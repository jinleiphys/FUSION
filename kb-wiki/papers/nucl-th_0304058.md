# Comment on "Negative heat capacities and first order phase transitions in nuclei" by L.G. Moretto et al

> In a recent paper L.G. Moretto et al [nucl-th/0208024] claim that the negative heat capacities presented in our previously published paper \cite{PRL2000} are "artifacts" coming from the use of periodic boundary conditions in the Lattice-Gas calculations. We stress in this comment that this claim is wrong: in ref. \cite{PRL2000} we did not use periodic boundary conditions and anyhow the boundary conditions are irrelevant for the statistical ensemble used in \cite{PRL2000}. The second claim of [nucl-th/0208024] is that, because of the Coulomb repulsion, systems "with A>60 should present no anomalous negative heat capacities". We show that this conclusion is contradicted by exact Lattice-gas simulations including Coulomb forces which present negative heat capacities even for A>200.

# Comment on "Negative heat capacities and first order phase transitions in nuclei" by L.G. Moretto et al

## Key claim

The authors argue that the negative heat capacities reported in their earlier work (Gulminelli and Chomaz, 2000) are not artifacts of periodic boundary conditions, contrary to the claim of Moretto et al. They further assert that negative heat capacities persist even for systems with A>200 when Coulomb forces are included, contradicting Moretto et al.'s conclusion that such anomalies vanish above A>60.

## Method

The authors performed Lattice-Gas model simulations in the microcanonical isobar ensemble (constant pressure via Lagrange multiplier λ) without periodic boundary conditions, using a large enough lattice so that boundary conditions do not affect the results. They also conducted exact Lattice-gas simulations including Coulomb forces for a system of A=207 particles with charge Z=82, and compared results with and without periodic boundary conditions for a smaller test system of 216 particles in a 5832-site lattice.

## Key numbers

- For 216 particles in a 5832-site lattice at energy E=0.4ε with log λ=-8: temperature T=(0.687±0.004)ε, heat capacity C=-16.3±0.2, kinetic energy fluctuation σ_k²/T²=1.64±0.03 (without periodic boundary conditions)
- With periodic boundary conditions for the same system: T=(0.682±0.004)ε, C=-16.9±0.4, σ_k²/T²=1.63±0.07
- Temperature decrease in the phase transition region: ΔT=(7.6±0.4)×10⁻³ε (with periodic boundary conditions) and ΔT=(6.7±0.6)×10⁻³ε (without periodic boundary conditions)
- The charged Lattice-gas simulation used A=207 particles, Z=82, with coupling ε=5.5 MeV and log λ=-7

## Context

This comment responds to Moretto et al. (2002, nucl-th/0208024), who claimed that the negative heat capacities reported in Gulminelli and Chomaz (2000, Physical Review Letters) were artifacts of periodic boundary conditions. The authors clarify that their 2000 work used an isobar ensemble without periodic boundary conditions, while their earlier canonical ensemble work (Gulminelli and Chomaz, 1999) used periodic boundary conditions but reported only positive heat capacities. The results extend the debate on whether Coulomb repulsion suppresses negative heat capacity in nuclear systems, with the authors showing that multifragmentation channels (multiplicity m≥3) rather than single-cluster configurations are responsible for the observed anomalies, and that the simple model of Moretto et al. is insufficient for quantitative conclusions.
