# Photoproduction of phi mesons from nuclei

> We investigate the consequences of possible medium modifications of the phi meson at finite nuclear matter density on the K+K- mass distribution in photonuclear reactions. The inclusive cross sections for K+K- pair production are calculated within a semi--classical BUU transport model, which combines the initial state interaction of the incoming photon with the final state interactions of the produced particles. The effects of final state interactions on the invariant mass distribution of the observed K+K- pairs are discussed in detail. In addition we calculate the Coulomb correction and possible effects of hadronic kaon potentials on the K+K- mass spectrum. Due to the large cross sections for reactions of the final state particles with the surrounding nuclear medium and the influence of the Coulomb potential we find no measurable sensitivity of the observables to the phi properties at finite baryon density.

## Key claim

The authors find no measurable sensitivity of the K⁺K⁻ invariant mass distribution to possible medium modifications of the φ meson at finite baryon density, because final-state interactions and the Coulomb potential distort the observable spectrum beyond the level of any putative in-medium signal.

## Method

The authors use a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate inclusive photoproduction of φ mesons from a ⁴⁰Ca target at a photon energy of 2.4 GeV. The calculation combines initial-state interactions of the incoming photon (including nuclear shadowing) with explicit propagation of all produced particles through the nucleus, accounting for elastic and inelastic φN, KN, and K̄N collisions, as well as non-resonant K⁺K⁻ background production. The φ meson spectral function is parameterized as a relativistic Breit-Wigner, and in-medium modifications are introduced via a collision width that scales linearly with nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The free φ meson width is 4.4 MeV.
- At nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ and vanishing φ momentum, the φ collision width amounts to 24 MeV.
- For a ⁴⁰Ca nucleus (radius ≈ 4 fm), three momentum cutoffs are applied: Λ = 300 MeV (decay distance d = 12.8 fm), Λ = 150 MeV (d = 6.4 fm), and Λ = 100 MeV (d = 4.3 fm).
- Nuclear shadowing reduces the cross sections by about 15%.
- When φN cross sections are multiplied or divided by a factor of two, the low-momentum φ yield changes by up to 30%.
- The estimated mass resolution of the detector at Spring8/Osaka is 600 keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier many-body calculations by Oset et al. (2001), who predicted an observable enhancement of the φ width in the K⁺K⁻ invariant mass spectrum using a Glauber-type absorptive model for final-state interactions. The present study improves upon that approach by including inelastic φ photoproduction processes (absent in the earlier work) and a more realistic treatment of final-state interactions that accounts for regeneration, side-feeding, and inelastic scattering, not just absorption. The results indicate that the planned experiment at Spring8/Osaka will not be able to detect medium modifications of the φ meson, contrary to the earlier optimistic prediction.
