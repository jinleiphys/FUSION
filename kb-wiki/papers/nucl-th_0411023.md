# HFB calculations with a microscopic pairing interaction

> Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations making use of a recently proposed microscopic effective pairing interaction are presented. The interaction was shown to reproduce the pairing properties provided by the realistic $AV18$ force very accurately in infinite matter. Although finite-ranged and non-local, it makes 3D HFB calculations in coordinate space tractable. As a first application, basic pairing properties of calcium isotopes in their ground-state are studied. By comparing the results with those obtained using a standard Density-Dependent Delta Interaction, the crucial isovector character of the microscopic interaction is highlighted.

## Key claim

The microscopic effective pairing interaction, derived from the realistic AV18 force and applied in 3D HFB calculations of calcium isotopes, reproduces pairing properties without any adjustment to finite nuclei, but the results depend on using a p-h vertex with an effective mass consistent with a G-matrix supplemented by a three-body force.

## Method

3D HFB calculations with Lipkin-Nogami particle-number projection were performed for calcium ground-states from proton to neutron drip-lines, using the SLy4 Skyrme force in the particle-hole channel. Each calculation was repeated three times: with the microscopic finite-range force defined through its closed coordinate-space form, with its zero-range approximation, and with a standard surface-peaked Density-Dependent Delta Interaction (DDDI). Self-consistent blocking and time-reversal symmetry breaking were included for odd isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The finite-range microscopic force has range α = √0.52 fm and intensity λ = -840 MeV·fm³, with no further adjustment in finite nuclei.
- The DDDI predicts the two-neutron drip-line in Ca at N = 44, while the microscopic forces extend stability up to N = 50.
- The DDDI was adjusted with a phenomenological cut-off defining an active window of ±5 MeV around the Fermi energy.
- The SLy4 force used in the p-h channel predicts an isoscalar effective mass m*/m = 0.7.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic effective pairing interaction proposed by Duguet (2004) from infinite matter to finite nuclei, contrasting with phenomenological forces like the Gogny force (Decharge, 1980) and DDDI (Duguet, 2002; Dobaczewski, 2004) that lack a clear link to the bare NN interaction. The results enable systematic HFB calculations to identify the role of the bare NN force in building pairing in finite nuclei and provide a foundation for beyond-mean-field calculations including symmetry restorations and the Generator Coordinate Method.
