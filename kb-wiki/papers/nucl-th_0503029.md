# Study on the One-Proton Halo Structure in $^{23}$Al

> The Glauber theory has been used to investigate the reaction cross section of proton-rich nucleus $^{23}$Al. A core plus a proton structure is assumed for $^{23}$Al. HO-type density distribution is used for the core while the density distribution for the valence proton is calculated by solving the eigenvalue problem of Woods-Saxon potential. The transparency function in an analytical expression is obtained adopting multi-Gaussian expansion for the density distribution. Coulomb correction and finite-range interaction are introduced. This modified Glauber model is apt for halo nuclei. A dominate s-wave is suggested for the last proton in $^{23}$Al from our analysis which is possible in the RMF calculation.

## Key claim

The analysis suggests a dominant s-wave for the last proton in ²³Al, indicating a one-proton halo structure, but the authors note that the experimental error in the reaction cross section is comparable to the difference between s- and d-wave predictions, and even the s-wave calculation still underestimates the measured data.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber model in the optical limit approximation with a multi-Gaussian expansion of the density distributions to obtain an analytical transparency function. They assumed a core (²²Mg) plus one proton structure for ²³Al, using a Woods-Saxon potential to calculate the valence proton wavefunction for both s- and d-wave configurations, and introduced energy-dependent finite-range interaction and Coulomb corrections. The reaction cross sections were calculated for ²³Al on a ¹²C target and compared with experimental data to determine the s-wave spectroscopic factor.

## Key numbers

- The last proton separation energy in ²³Al is 0.125 MeV.
- The measured reaction cross section for ²²Mg at 33.4 A MeV is 1531 ± 125 mb.
- The measured reaction cross section for ²³Al at 35.9 A MeV is 1892 ± 145 mb.
- The lower limit of the s-wave spectroscopic factor is around 0.65.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were diffuseness a = 0.67 fm and radius parameter r₀ = 1.27 fm, with spin-orbit strength V_ls = 17 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proton halo candidates such as ⁸B and ¹⁷Ne, where discrepancies were found, and follows measurements by Cai et al. (2002) and Zhao et al. (2002) that showed enhanced reaction cross sections for ²³Al compared to its neighbors. The results contradict the shell model prediction that the last proton in ²³Al occupies the 1d₅/₂ orbit, but support the possibility of inversion of the 1s₁/₂ and 1d₅/₂ orbits suggested by relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations. The authors call for more precise measurements and momentum distribution studies of the residue after one-proton removal to obtain a more confirmative conclusion.
