# Entrainment in Superfluid Neutron Star Crusts: Hydrodynamic Description and Microscopic Origin

> In spite of the absence of viscous drag, the neutron superfluid permeating the inner crust of a neutron star cannot flow freely, and is entrained by the nuclear lattice similarly to laboratory superfluid atomic gases in optical lattices. The role of entrainment on the neutron superfluid dynamics is reviewed. For this purpose, a minimal hydrodynamical model of superfluidity in neutron-star crusts is presented. This model relies on a fully four-dimensionally covariant action principle. The equivalence of this formulation with the more traditional approach is demonstrated. In addition, the different treatments of entrainment in terms of dynamical effective masses or superfluid density are clarified. The nuclear energy density functional theory employed for the calculations of all the necessary microscopic inputs is also reviewed, focusing on superfluid properties. In particular, the microscopic origin of entrainment and the different methods to estimate its importance are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the neutron superfluid in the inner crust of a neutron star is entrained by the nuclear lattice, meaning it cannot flow freely even in the absence of viscous drag, and this entrainment is shown to be sufficiently strong that the crustal neutron superfluid alone cannot explain giant pulsar glitches, suggesting another superfluid reservoir in the stellar core must be involved.

## Method

The author presents a minimal hydrodynamical model of superfluidity in neutron-star crusts based on a fully four-dimensionally covariant action principle originally developed by Carter (1989), and demonstrates its equivalence with the more traditional heuristic approach of Pethick (2010) and Kobyakov (2013). The model describes the crust as two interpenetrating fluids (a neutron superfluid and a charge-neutral plasma of electrons and ions locked together) with entrainment coupling, and the microscopic inputs are calculated using nuclear energy density functional theory with Skyrme-type effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- The inner crust exists at densities between approximately 10^11 g cm^-3 and 10^14 g cm^-3
- The mean ion spacing is given by a_I = (3/(4 pi n_I))^(1/3) where n_I is the ion number density
- The speed of lattice vibrations in the absence of entrainment is v^0_ell = sqrt(tilde K / rho_I), where tilde K is the bulk modulus of the electron-ion system
- The speed of Bogoliubov-Anderson sound in a pure neutron superfluid is v^0_phi = sqrt((n^f_n / m)(partial mu^n / partial n^f_n))
- With entrainment, the lattice vibration speed becomes v_ell = v^0_ell sqrt(rho_I / (m(n_p + n^N_n))) which is less than or equal to v^0_ell
- With entrainment, the Bogoliubov-Anderson speed becomes v_phi = v^0_phi sqrt(n^S_n / n^f_n) which is greater than or equal to v^0_phi
- The stability condition for the static configuration requires bar_rho_np > - x_p (1 - x_p) rho, where x_p = n_p/(n_n + n_p) is the proton fraction

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic formalism of Carter (1989, 2004, 2005, 2006) and the microscopic calculations of Chamel (2005, 2006, 2012) to provide a unified framework for entrainment in neutron-star crusts. It clarifies the relationship between different formulations of entrainment in terms of dynamical effective masses and superfluid densities, and shows that the covariant variational approach is equivalent to the heuristic approach of Pethick (2010) and Kobyakov (2013). The results enable more realistic modeling of pulsar glitches, thermal relaxation of accreting neutron stars, and quasiperiodic oscillations in soft-gamma repeater flares, as discussed in the review by Chamel (2017).
