# Hypothesis about semi-weak interaction and experiments with solar neutrinos

> A new concept is proposed to solve the solar neutrino problem, that is based on a hypothesis about the existence of semi-weak interaction of electron neutrinos with nucleons mediated by massless pseudoscalar bosons. Owing to about 10 collisions of a solar neutrino with nucleons of the Sun, the fluxes of left- and right-handed solar neutrinos at the Earth surface are approximately equal, and their spectrum is changed in comparison with the one at the production moment. The postulated model with one free parameter provides a good agreement between the calculated and experimental characteristics of the processes with solar neutrinos: ${}^{37}{\rm Cl} \rightarrow {}^{37}{\rm Ar}$, ${}^{71}{\rm Ga} \rightarrow {}^{71}{\rm Ge}$, $ν_{e} e^{-}\rightarrow ν_{e} e^{-}$, and $ν_{e}D \rightarrow e^{-}pp$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the solar neutrino problem can be solved by a new "semi-weak" interaction of electron neutrinos with nucleons, mediated by massless pseudoscalar bosons, rather than by neutrino oscillations. The model uses a single free parameter (the effective number of neutrino-nucleon collisions in the Sun, taken as 10 or 11) and claims good agreement with experimental data across four different solar neutrino processes, though the author notes this agreement could theoretically be "a game of chance."

## Method

The author postulates a Lagrangian describing a massless pseudoscalar boson that couples to electron neutrinos and nucleons (with opposite signs for protons and neutrons) but not to electrons at tree level. Using the derived differential and total cross sections for elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering, the author calculates how the solar neutrino energy spectrum is modified by approximately 10 collisions during passage through the Sun, where each collision flips the neutrino handedness and reduces its energy. The modified spectra are then used to compute expected rates for four processes (chlorine capture, gallium capture, elastic scattering on electrons, and deuteron charged-current disintegration) and compared with experimental results from Homestake, GALLEX/GNO, Super-Kamiokande, and SNO.

## Key numbers

- The product of coupling constants is found to be \(g_{\nu_{e}ps}g_{Nps}/4\pi = (3.2 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-5}\), smaller by three orders than the electromagnetic constant \(\alpha\) and the weak constant \(g^{2}/4\pi\)
- For the chlorine experiment (\({}^{37}{\rm Cl} \rightarrow {}^{37}{\rm Ar}\)), the model with \(n_0 = 11\) gives a total rate of 2.72 SNU, compared to the experimental value of \(2.56 \pm 0.16 \pm 0.16\) SNU and the SSM prediction of 7.9 SNU
- For the gallium experiment (\({}^{71}{\rm Ga} \rightarrow {}^{71}{\rm Ge}\)), the model with \(n_0 = 11\) gives 62.6 SNU, compared to experimental values of \(65.4^{+3.1}_{-3.0}{}^{+2.6}_{-2.8}\) SNU and \(62.9^{+6.0}_{-5.9}\) SNU, and SSM predictions of 132 SNU
- The effective \({}^{8}{\rm B}\) neutrino flux from elastic \(\nu e\) scattering at Super-Kamiokande III (threshold 5.0 MeV) is calculated as \(2.27 \times 10^{6}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) for \(n_0 = 11\), compared to the experimental \(2.32 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05 \times 10^{6}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\)
- The effective \({}^{8}{\rm B}\) neutrino flux from the charged-current deuteron disintegration at SNO III (threshold 6.5 MeV) is calculated as \(1.67 \times 10^{6}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\) for \(n_0 = 11\), compared to the experimental \(1.67^{+0.05}_{-0.04}{}^{+0.07}_{-0.08} \times 10^{6}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\)
- The total cross section for elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering via the semi-weak interaction is of order \(10^{-36}\) cm\(^{2}\) at low energies
- The relative energy loss per collision is \(\Delta \omega/\omega \simeq \omega/M\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass

## Context

This work proposes an alternative to the neutrino oscillation hypothesis for solving the solar neutrino problem, which the author criticizes for relying on multiple free parameters and lacking a single-valued calculation procedure for all five observed solar neutrino processes. The model extends the author's earlier proposals involving massless axial photons (Slad, prior work) and is contrasted with the Wolfenstein-Mikheev-Smirnov mechanism and the standard three-flavor oscillation framework with parameters \(\theta_{12}\), \(\theta_{13}\), \(\theta_{23}\) and \(\Delta m_{21}\), \(\Delta m_{31}\). The paper enables future calculations of the neutral-current deuteron disintegration process \(\nu D \rightarrow \nu np\) in SNO, which the author notes would involve two non-interfering sub-processes from standard Z-boson exchange and the new pseudoscalar exchange.
