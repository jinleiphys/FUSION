# Coulomb corrections to electron scattering on the extended source and the proton charge radius

> It is shown that the account for the proton charge form factor in the Coulomb corrections to the electron-proton scattering cross section noticeably diminishes the difference between the value of the proton charge radius $r_{\mathrm{E}}$, extracted from the $ep$ scattering data, and that following from the muonic hydrogen data. For the electron energy much higher than the electron mass but much smaller than $r_{\mathrm{E}}^{-1}\approx 230$~MeV, the relative correction has the universal form $δr_{\mathrm{E}}/r_{\mathrm{E}}=-πα/2$, where $α$ is the fine structure constant.

# Coulomb corrections to electron scattering on the extended source and the proton charge radius

## Key claim
The authors show that including the proton charge form factor in the leading Coulomb corrections (two-photon exchange) to electron-proton scattering produces a shift in the extracted proton charge radius that is significantly larger than the model uncertainties claimed by Bernauer et al. (2010, 2013), and that this shift reduces the discrepancy between the radius from ep scattering and that from muonic hydrogen data. The relative correction has the universal form δr_E/r_E = -πα/2 only in the regime where the electron energy is much higher than the electron mass but much smaller than r_E^{-1} ≈ 230 MeV.

## Method
The authors compute the electron-proton scattering cross section in the external field approximation, including the one-photon and two-photon exchange amplitudes, with the proton charge form factor F(Q^2) incorporated into the external potential. They derive an expression for the shift δr_E^2 that arises from the finite proton charge radius in the Coulomb corrections, and evaluate this integral analytically in the low-energy limit (E ≪ r_E^{-1}) and numerically for the dipole and monopole form factors at energies relevant to the Bernauer et al. (2010) experiment.

## Key numbers
- The universal relative correction in the low-energy limit (E ≪ r_E^{-1} ≈ 230 MeV): δr_E/r_E = -πα/2 ≈ -0.0115, where α ≈ 1/137 is the fine structure constant.
- For the dipole form factor with Λ = 840 MeV, the relative correction at E = 180 MeV (the minimal energy in the Bernauer et al. (2010) experiment) is δr_E/r_E = -0.82 × 10^{-2}.
- The minimal momentum transfer in the Bernauer et al. (2010) experiment was Q = 60 MeV, and the lowest electron energy was E = 180 MeV.
- For the high-energy asymptotics (E ≫ r_E^{-1}) with the dipole form factor, the relative correction is δr_E/r_E = -35αΛ/(64E).

## Context
This work addresses the proton radius puzzle, specifically the 5-standard-deviation discrepancy between the proton charge radius extracted from ep scattering data (Bernauer 2010, 2013) and that from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013). The authors show that the standard practice of applying only the Feshbach correction δ_F (Tsai 1961, Feshbach 1948) for Coulomb corrections is insufficient at the claimed precision level. The calculation extends earlier work on radiative corrections to ep scattering (Lewis 1956, Tsai 1961, Bottino 1971) by explicitly including the proton form factor in the two-photon exchange amplitude. The result enables a re-evaluation of the proton radius extracted from scattering data and suggests that the discrepancy with muonic hydrogen results may be partially resolved by properly accounting for these Coulomb corrections.
