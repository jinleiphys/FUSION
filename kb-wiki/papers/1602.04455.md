# Combined analysis of the $π^-p\rightarrow K^0Λ$, $ηn$ reactions in a chiral quark model

> A combined analysis of the reactions $π^-p\rightarrow K^0Λ$ and $ηn$ is carried out with a chiral quark model. The data in the center-of-mass (c.m.) energy range from threshold up to $W\simeq 1.8$ GeV are reasonably described. For $π^-p\rightarrow K^0Λ$, it is found that $N(1535)S_{11}$ and $N(1650)S_{11}$ paly crucial roles near threshold. The $N(1650)S_{11}$ resonance contributes to the reaction through configuration mixing with $N(1535)S_{11}$. The constructive interference between $N(1535)S_{11}$ and $N(1650)S_{11}$ is responsible for the peak structure around threshold in the total cross section. The $n$-pole, $u$- and $t$-channel backgrounds provide significant contributions to the reaction as well. While, for the $π^-p\rightarrow ηn$ process, the "first peak" in the total cross section is dominant by $N(1535)S_{11}$, which has a sizeable destructive interference with $N(1650)S_{11}$. Around $P_π\simeq $ 1.0 GeV/c ($W\simeq 1.7$ GeV), there seems to be a small bump structure in the total cross section, which might be explained by the interference between the $u$-channel and $N(1650)S_{11}$. The $N(1520)D_{13}$ resonance notably affects the angle distributions of the cross sections, although less effects are seen in the total cross section. The role of $P$-wave state $N(1720)P_{13}$ should be further confirmed by future experiments. If $N(1720)P_{13}$ has a narrow width of $Γ\simeq 120$ MeV as found in our previous work by a study of the $π^0$ photoproduction processes, obvious evidence should be seen in the $π^-p\rightarrow K^0Λ$ and $ηn$ processes as well.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model can reasonably describe the combined data for both reactions from threshold up to about 1.8 GeV center-of-mass energy, but the extracted width for the N(1720)P13 resonance (about 400 MeV) is much broader than the narrow width of about 120 MeV previously found by the authors in neutral pion photoproduction, leaving the role of that resonance to be confirmed by future experiments.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of the π⁻p → K⁰Λ and π⁻p → ηn reactions using a chiral quark model with SU(6)⊗O(3) symmetry, where quark-pseudoscalar-meson couplings are described by an effective chiral Lagrangian. The s-channel resonance amplitudes were derived in the harmonic oscillator basis, with configuration mixing included for the S-wave states N(1535)S11 and N(1650)S11 via a mixing angle. The t-channel contributions from vector (K*) and scalar (κ, a₀(980)) meson exchanges were included, and all adjustable parameters were determined by globally fitting the measured differential cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The mixing angle between N(1535)S11 and N(1650)S11 was determined to be θ_S ≃ 26.9°
- The extracted coupling constants were g_KNΛ ≃ 6.87 and g_ηNN ≃ 2.50
- The πNN coupling constant was fixed at g_πNN = 13.48
- For N(1535)S11: mass 1524 MeV, width 124 MeV; partial width ratio Γ_ηN/Γ_πN = 1.57
- For N(1650)S11: mass 1670 MeV, width 119 MeV; partial width ratio Γ_ηN/Γ_πN = 0.23 and Γ_KΛ/Γ_πN = 0.20
- The reduced χ² per data point for the full model was 5.98 (χ²_η = 8.54 for the ηn reaction, χ²_K = 2.78 for the K⁰Λ reaction)
- The N(1720)P13 resonance required a width of about 400 MeV in the global fit, compared to about 120 MeV found in the authors' previous photoproduction work

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model studies of η and π⁰ photoproduction (Zhong 2011, Xiao 2015) to pion-induced reactions, and addresses conflicting interpretations in the literature: for example, Shrestha (2012) attributed the peak near W=1.7 GeV in π⁻p → K⁰Λ to N(1650)S11 and N(1710)P11, while Penner (2002) attributed it to N(1720)P13. The combined analysis clarifies that N(1535)S11 and N(1650)S11 with configuration mixing are the dominant contributors near threshold, and that the N(1720)P13 width remains uncertain. The results enable future experimental tests to distinguish between a narrow or broad N(1720)P13 state in these reactions.
