# Molecular $Ω_b$ states

> Motivated by the recent finding of five $Ω_c$ states by the LHCb collaboration, and the successful reproduction of three of them in a recent approach searching for molecular states of meson-baryon with the quantum numbers of $Ω_c$, we extend these ideas and make predictions for the interaction of meson-baryon in the beauty sector, searching for poles in the scattering matrix that correspond to physical states. We find several $Ω_b$ states: two states with masses 6405~MeV and 6465~MeV for $J^P= \frac{1}{2}^-$; two more states with masses 6427~MeV and 6665~MeV for $\frac{3}{2}^-$; and three states between 6500 and 6820~MeV, degenerate with $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-,\,\frac{3}{2}^-$, stemming from the interaction of vector-baryon in the beauty sector.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of several molecular $\Omega_b$ states by extending a formalism that successfully reproduced three of the five $\Omega_c$ states observed by LHCb, but the predictions rely on the assumption that the same cut-off regularization (650 MeV) used in the charm sector respects heavy quark symmetry in the beauty sector, and the authors note that the non-diagonal transition terms are suppressed by a factor of about 0.1 due to heavy vector meson exchange.

## Method

The authors use the local hidden gauge formalism extended to the beauty sector, constructing coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations for meson-baryon interactions in S-wave. They replace the charm quark by a bottom quark in the wave functions and transition potentials from their prior work on $\Omega_c$ states (Liang et al., 2017), using a cut-off regularization with $q_{max}=650$ MeV to preserve heavy quark symmetry. Poles in the scattering matrix are searched in the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane to identify bound states.

## Key numbers

- Two $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-$ states at 6405 MeV and 6465 MeV (pseudoscalar-baryon channels)
- Two $J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-$ states at 6427 MeV and 6665 MeV (pseudoscalar-baryon channels)
- Three states from vector-baryon channels, degenerate in $J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-$ and $\frac{3}{2}^-$, at 6508 MeV, 6676 MeV, and 6818 MeV
- The $\Xi_b \bar{K}$ threshold is 6289 MeV; the $\Xi'_b \bar{K}$ threshold is 6431 MeV; the $\Xi \bar B$ threshold is 6598 MeV
- The $\Xi^*_b \bar{K}$ threshold is 6451 MeV; the $\Xi^* \bar B$ threshold is 6813 MeV
- The $\Xi \bar{B}^*$ threshold is 6643 MeV; the $\Xi'_b \bar K^*$ threshold is 6829 MeV
- The estimated $\Omega^*_b$ mass is 6071 MeV, derived from a 25 MeV splitting from $\Omega_b$

## Context

This work extends the molecular approach of Liang et al. (2017) and Montana et al. (2017) from the charm sector to the beauty sector, where no molecular $\Omega_b$ predictions previously existed. The authors note that unlike in the charm sector, where the first $\frac{3}{2}^-$ state had higher energy than the two $\frac{1}{2}^-$ states, here the $\frac{3}{2}^-$ state at 6427 MeV lies between the two $\frac{1}{2}^-$ states, a qualitative difference that future experiments could test. The predictions are intended to guide searches at LHCb with increased luminosity, and the authors suggest that comparison with experiment will help understand hadron dynamics and the nature of hadronic states.
