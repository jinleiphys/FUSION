# Counting states and the Hadron Resonance Gas: Does X(3872) count?

> We analyze how the renowned X(3872), a weakly bound state right below the $D \bar D^*$ threshold, should effectively be included in a hadronic representation of the QCD partition function. This can be decided by analyzing the $D \bar D^*$ scattering phase-shifts in the $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ channel and their contribution to the level density in the continuum from which the abundance in a hot medium can be determined. We show that in a purely molecular picture the bound state contribution cancels the continuum providing a vanishing occupation number density at finite temperature and the $X(3872)$ does not count below the Quark-Gluon Plasma crossover happening at $T \sim 150$MeV. In contrast, within a coupled-channels approach, for a non vanishing $c \bar c$ content the cancellation does not occur due to the onset of the $X(3940)$ which effectively counts as an elementary particle for temperatures above $T \gtrsim 250$MeV. Thus, a direct inclusion of the $X(3872)$ in the Hadron Resonance Gas is not justified. We also estimate the role of this cancellation in X(3872) production in heavy-ion collision experiments in terms of the corresponding $p_T$ distribution due to a finite energy resolution.

## Key claim

The X(3872) should not be directly included in the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) because its contribution as a bound state cancels with the nearby D D* continuum in a purely molecular picture; only when a non-vanishing c cbar content is present does the cancellation fail, but this is due to the onset of the X(3940) resonance rather than the X(3872) itself.

## Method

The authors analyze D D* scattering phase shifts in the JPC=1++ channel using two approaches: a purely molecular contact interaction model and a coupled-channels constituent quark model that includes both D D* molecular states and c cbar confined states. They compute the cumulative number of states via the S-matrix formulation (Levinson's theorem) and the level density from the derivative of the phase shift, then evaluate the occupation number density at finite temperature and the pT distribution with finite energy resolution binning.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of X(3872): B_X = 0.01(18) MeV (PDG value)
- For the purely molecular picture: scattering length α0 = 3.14 fm, effective range r0 = 1.25 fm; the bound state pole is at k_X = i 0.43 fm^-1 corresponding to M_X = 3868 MeV
- For the coupled-channels model with γ=0.23 (21.50% c cbar probability): scattering length α0 = 2.73 fm, effective range r0 = 0.63 fm; X(3940) mass M = 3947.05 MeV and width Γ = 56.03 MeV
- PDG values for X(3940): M = 3942(9) MeV and Γ = 37+27-17 MeV
- Lattice results (Prelovsek 2013) at mπ=266 MeV: α0 = 1.7(4) fm, r0 = 0.5(1) fm, binding energy -11±7 MeV below D0 D0* threshold
- Quark-Gluon Plasma crossover temperature: T ~ 150 MeV
- Temperature above which X(3940) counts as elementary: T ≳ 250 MeV
- Experimental binning in pp collisions: Δm = 3 MeV (CMS) and Δm = 1.5 MeV (ATLAS), which are 10-15 times larger than the X(3872) binding energy

## Context

This work extends the Dashen-Kane (1974) argument about loosely bound states not counting in the hadron spectrum, previously applied to the deuteron, to the charmonium-like X(3872) state. It contradicts the naive assumption that all PDG-listed states should be directly included in the HRG, which has been used successfully for lattice QCD thermodynamics by the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations. The results enable more accurate modeling of X(3872) production in heavy-ion collisions and clarify that the X(3940) resonance, not the X(3872) itself, is responsible for any non-vanishing occupation number in the 1++ channel at temperatures above the QGP crossover.
