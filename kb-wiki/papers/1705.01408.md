# Toroidal high-spin isomers in the nucleus $^{304}{120}$

> Strongly deformed oblate superheavy nuclei form an intriguing region where the toroidal nuclear structures may bifurcate from the oblate spheroidal shape. The bifurcation may be facilitated when the nucleus is endowed with a large angular moment about the symmetry axis with $I=I_{z}$. The toroidal high-$K$ isomeric states at their local energy minima can be theoretically predicted using the cranked self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method. We use the cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method to predict the properties of the toroidal high-spin isomers in the superheavy nucleus $^{304}{120}_{184}$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that toroidal high-spin isomeric states can exist in the superheavy nucleus $^{304}{120}$ when it is endowed with large angular momenta aligned along the symmetry axis, even though the toroidal shape of this nucleus without spin is unstable. The authors hedge that such stabilization requires specific discrete angular momentum values arising from multi-particle-multi-hole excitations.

## Method

The authors used a three-step theoretical approach: first, deformation-constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations to search for toroidal nuclear density distributions; second, cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with an angular momentum constraint $I=I_z$ about the symmetry axis to map the energy landscape; and third, symmetry- and deformation-unconstrained cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations at candidate minima to confirm stable toroidal high-spin isomeric states in free convergence. The Skyrme SkM* force was used with a mixed pairing interaction, and calculations were performed with the symmetry-unrestricted code HFODD.

## Key numbers

- Two toroidal high-spin isomeric states were located in $^{304}{120}_{184}$: one with angular momentum $I=I_z=81\hbar$ (proton 2p-2h, neutron 4p-4h excitation) at quadrupole moment $Q_{20}=-297.7$ b and energy 79.2 MeV above the spherical ground state
- The second state has $I=I_z=208\hbar$ (proton 5p-5h, neutron 8p-8h excitation) at $Q_{20}=-300.8$ b and energy 101.6 MeV above the spherical ground state
- Both toroidal high-spin isomers have maximum nuclear density close to 0.16 fm$^{-3}$ and a torus major to minor radius aspect ratio $R/d=3.25$
- The toroidal density solutions appear for $Q_{20}\le -158$ b, with a sudden shape transition from biconcave disc to torus at $Q_{20}\approx -200$ b accompanied by an energy reduction of 10.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of toroidal high-spin isomers in light nuclei (Ichikawa 2012, 2014; Staszczak 2014, 2015a, 2015b, 2016) to the superheavy region, and builds on earlier predictions that toroidal energy minima may exist for superheavy nuclei with atomic numbers $Z\gtrsim 122$ (Staszczak 2008). The results demonstrate that aligned angular momenta can stabilize toroidal shapes in $^{304}{120}$, which is located near the center of the "island of stability," and suggest that future work should examine toroidal shells at matching proton and neutron numbers for enhanced stability.
