# Self-consistent description of vector-mesons in matter

> We study the influence of the virtual pion cloud in nuclear matter at finite densities and temperatures on the structure of the rho- and omega-mesons. The in-matter spectral function of the pion is obtained within a selfconsistent scheme of coupled Dyson equations where the coupling to the nucleon and the Delta(1232)-isobar resonance is taken into account. The selfenergies are determined using a two-particle irreducible (2PI) truncation scheme (Phi-derivable approximation) supplemented by Migdal's short range correlations for the particle-hole excitations. The so obtained spectral function of the pion is then used to calculate the in-medium changes of the vector-meson spectral functions. With increasing density and temperature a strong interplay of both vector-meson modes is observed. The four-transversality of the polarisation tensors of the vector-mesons is achieved by a projector technique. The resulting spectral functions of both vector-mesons and, through vector dominance, the implications of our results on the dilepton spectra are studied in their dependence on density and temperature.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the in-medium modification of the virtual pion cloud, calculated self-consistently through a 2PI truncation scheme with Migdal short-range correlations, leads to a strong interplay between the rho- and omega-meson modes in nuclear matter, with the caveat that the real parts of the vector-meson selfenergies are dropped and the results are therefore less precise for mass shifts.

## Method

The authors used a self-consistent scheme of coupled Dyson equations within a two-particle irreducible (2PI) truncation scheme (Phi-derivable approximation) to compute the in-medium pion spectral function, coupling the pion to nucleon and Delta(1232)-isobar degrees of freedom with p-wave couplings and a non-relativistic spinor approximation. Migdal short-range correlations were included via an RPA resummation to prevent pion condensation, and the resulting dressed pion propagator was then used to calculate the rho- and omega-meson selfenergies from pion loops, with four-transversality of the polarization tensors enforced by a projector technique.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is g_piNN = 0.007 MeV^-1, and the pion-Delta coupling constant is g_piNDelta = 0.02 MeV^-1.
- The formfactor cutoff parameter is Lambda = 440 MeV.
- The Migdal parameters used are g_11 = 0.5, g_22 = 0.6, and g_12 = 0.5.
- The numerical calculations were performed on a two-dimensional grid with 5 MeV by 5 MeV/c resolution.
- The calculations were performed at normal nuclear density rho = rho_0 and at temperatures T = 0 MeV, T = 60 MeV, and T = 120 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of vector-mesons in dense matter by Gale and Kapusta (1991), Korpa and Pratt (1991), and Herrmann (1993) by including a self-consistent treatment of the in-medium pion cloud, which previous work commonly neglected by using unperturbed free pion states. It also builds on the Migdal short-range correlation formalism (Migdal, 1978) and the 2PI effective action approach of Baym (1962). The results enable more realistic predictions for dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the low-mass region below the vector-meson peaks, and can be compared with experimental data from HADES, CERES, and HELIOS collaborations.
