# Backbending and $Gamma$-Vibrations

> We propose that the backbending phenomenon can be explained as a result of the disappearance of collective $gamma$-vibrational mode in the rotating frame. Using a cranking+random phase approximation approach for the modified Nilsson potential + monopole pairing forces, we show that this mechanism is responsible for the backbending in $^{156}$Dy, $^{158}$Er and obtain a good agreement between theoretical and experimental results.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the backbending phenomenon in rotating nuclei can be explained as a result of the disappearance of the collective γ-vibrational mode in the rotating frame, rather than solely by the rotational alignment of a nucleon pair in a high-j intruder orbital. The authors show that this mechanism is responsible for backbending in ¹⁵⁶Dy and ¹⁵⁸Er, but hedge that the two-quasiparticle alignment itself does not create the backbending; instead, the softening and vanishing of γ vibrations at a critical rotational frequency triggers the shape transition.

## Method

The authors used a cranking + random phase approximation (CRPA) approach based on the modified Nilsson potential with monopole pairing forces. They solved the Hartree-Bogoliubov (HB) equations for the rotating mean field using experimental deformation parameters as input, then diagonalized the CRPA Hamiltonian to obtain vibrational eigenfrequencies. The self-consistency was checked by ensuring that spurious modes (related to broken rotational invariance and particle number conservation) were properly separated from physical vibrational modes, and by verifying that the Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia matched the dynamical moment of inertia from the mean field.

## Key numbers

- The critical rotational frequency where the γ-vibrational frequency vanishes is ℏΩ_cr ≈ 0.324 MeV for ¹⁵⁶Dy and ≈ 0.33 MeV for ¹⁵⁸Er.
- The experimental critical frequency estimated from the γ-vibrational energy in the laboratory frame (ℏω̃_{K=2}) is ℏΩ_cr ≈ 0.345 MeV for ¹⁵⁶Dy (using ℏω̃_{K=2} = 0.691 MeV) and ≈ 0.306 MeV for ¹⁵⁸Er (using ℏω̃_{K=2} = 0.613 MeV).
- The pairing gaps at zero rotation are Δ_n(0) = 0.857 MeV, Δ_p(0) = 0.879 MeV for ¹⁵⁶Dy, and Δ_n(0) = 0.874 MeV, Δ_p(0) = 0.884 MeV for ¹⁵⁸Er.
- The band-crossing frequency used for the pairing gap parametrization is Ω_c = 0.32 MeV for ¹⁵⁶Dy and 0.33 MeV for ¹⁵⁸Er.
- The configuration space exhausted 97% of the energy-weighted sum rule for quadrupole transitions.
- The energy difference between collective and noncollective rotations near the critical frequency is about 15 keV for both nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the standard interpretation of backbending as a single-particle alignment effect (e.g., in cranking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Frauendorf and others) by including quantum fluctuations beyond the mean field via the RPA. It is the first self-consistent quantitative treatment of the relation between spontaneously broken symmetry and RPA spurious modes in rotating nuclei. The results enable a new understanding of backbending as a collective shape transition driven by the softening of γ vibrations, and suggest that similar analyses could be applied to other rotating nuclei near the N ≈ 90 region.
