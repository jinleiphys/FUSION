# Charmlets

> We discuss the possibility, that multiply charmed exotics are produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Production probabilities at RHIC are estimated and found to be large enough to allow for the possible detection of single and double charmed exotics. We demonstrate that charm matter is bound due to an attractive one-gluon exchange potential. Furthermore, we study the color magnetic and color electric potential separately, as well as the group structure of such exotics and estimate their masses.

## Key claim

Multiply charmed exotic hadrons ("charmlets") may be produced in detectable quantities at RHIC, provided the production probability per event exceeds approximately \(10^{-6}\), which the authors estimate is achievable for states satisfying \(2A + 2C < 9\) (where \(A\) is baryon number and \(C\) is charm number). The abstract's claim of "large enough" production probabilities is qualified by the need for a coalescence parameter \(p_0\) and the assumption that a quark-gluon plasma forms to enhance charm enrichment.

## Method

The authors estimate production probabilities for multiply charmed exotics in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV using a simple coalescence model, scaling the charm pair production cross section (\(\sigma_{c\bar{c}} \approx 300~\mu\)b) relative to the nucleon-nucleon cross section, and incorporating light quark density from the pion-to-nucleon ratio. They then model the masses and binding energies of charmlets using a modified MIT bag model, where the bag energy is minimized with \(\alpha_c = 0\) and color electric and magnetic corrections are added perturbatively, treating all quarks (including heavy charm) via the Dirac equation with flavor symmetry broken by explicit masses.

## Key numbers

- Charm pair production cross section at RHIC: \(\sigma_{c\bar{c}} \approx 300~\mu\)b, giving about 10 \(c\bar{c}\) pairs per central Au+Au collision.
- Estimated number of detectable events per year at RHIC: \(N_{\text{events}} \approx 10^6\), implying observables with likelihood \(>10^{-6}\) can be detected.
- Production probability scaling: \(P(C,A) \approx 10^{3-2C-2A}\), leading to detectability condition \(2A + 2C < 9\).
- Charm matter (bulk) binding energy: 55 MeV per baryon at charm fraction \(f_c = 1.7\) for \(\alpha_c = 0.3\).
- Bag model parameters fitted to light hadrons: \(B^{1/4} = 0.1684\) GeV, \(Z_0 = 1.585\), \(\alpha_c = 0.4764\), \(m_q = 0\), \(m_s = 0.342\) GeV.
- Charm quark mass fitted to \(\Lambda_c\): \(m_c = 1.788\) GeV.
- Estimated color magnetic binding for \(\{udsccc\}\) (the \(H_{ccc}\) dibaryon): approximately \(-110\) MeV.
- Estimated color magnetic binding for \(\{uudscc\}\) (the \(H_{cc}\) dibaryon): approximately \(-150\) MeV.
- Estimated matrix elements from bag model: \(M = 18\) MeV (magnetic), \(E_{qc} = 50\) MeV (electric between heavy and light quarks), \(E_{cc} = 70\) MeV (electric between heavy quarks).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of multiquark states with charm, such as the tetraquark (Carlson, 1988; Pepin, 1997), the pentaquark (Lipkin, 1987; Gignoux, 1987), and hexaquark/heptaquark states (Chow, 1995), by considering objects with more than two charm quarks ("charmlets") and their production in heavy-ion collisions. The authors use a coalescence model similar to that of Dover (1990) and compare with HIJING simulations (Lin, 1997). The paper enables future experimental searches at RHIC for multiply charmed exotics, particularly if a quark-gluon plasma forms and enriches charm via a distillation mechanism analogous to that proposed for strangelets (Greiner, 1988).
