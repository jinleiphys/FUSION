# On the reaction g d -> pi0 d near the threshold of eta production

> We consider the reaction g+d -> pi+d in a wide energy range around and above the eta-meson photoproduction threshold at backward CM angles of the outgoing pion. Our theoretical analysis is essentially motivated by the recent measurements of the CLAS Collaboration at Jefferson Lab, where this kinematical region of the reaction has been thoroughly studied for the first time and a cusps in the energy dependence of the differential cross section in the region of Eg = 600-800 MeV has been observed. Our preliminary and qualitative analysis, based on single- and double-scattering diagrams, shows that the observed structure can be explained by the contribution of the double-scattering diagram with intermediate production of the eta meson. The effect, to a considerable extent, is formed due to the contribution of N(1535) resonance to the amplitudes of subprocesses on the nucleons.

## Key claim

The observed cusp in the differential cross section of the reaction γ d → π⁰ d at photon energies Eγ = 600–800 MeV and backward pion angles can be explained by the contribution of a double-scattering diagram with intermediate η-meson production, primarily through the N(1535) resonance in the subprocesses on the nucleons, though the analysis is preliminary and qualitative rather than a detailed fit to the CLAS data.

## Method

The authors constructed the total reaction amplitude from single-scattering and double-scattering diagrams, using non-relativistic approximations for the elementary amplitudes on the nucleon. The elementary photoproduction amplitudes γ N → h N (h = π, η) were built from resonance terms (N(1535) and N(1440)) and vector-meson-exchange terms (ω and ρ exchanges), while the meson-nucleon amplitudes h N → π N were written through resonance contributions. The deuteron wave function from the Bonn potential was employed, and the double-scattering integrals were evaluated in the static approximation, with non-static corrections studied separately for the intermediate η-meson amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The cusp in the differential cross section is observed in the photon energy region Eγ = 600–800 MeV, near the η-meson photoproduction threshold.
- The analysis covers photon energies from 500 to 1000 MeV, far from the π d threshold and above the Δ(1232) region.
- The relative accuracy of the non-relativistic approximation near the η photoproduction threshold (√s ∼ m + mη) is of order q/2m ∼ 0.2–0.3.
- The ratio of ω-exchange to ρ-exchange amplitudes in γ N → π⁰ N is estimated as Mγωπ / Mγρπ ∼ 10, based on coupling constants from Garc (year not specified in text).
- The ρπγ vertex isoscalar coupling constant g1 is taken to dominate, with g2,3 ≪ g1, based on PDG (year not specified) ratios Γρ⁰→π⁰γ / Γρ±→π±γ ∼ 1.7–1.8.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of coherent π⁰ photoproduction on the deuteron, previously considered by Garc (year not specified) and Kamal (year not specified) without the intermediate η-meson effect, to backward angles where the CLAS Collaboration (2004) observed a cusp. It builds on the prediction by Kond (1970s) that intermediate particles in two-step processes produce cusps in backward π d elastic scattering, which was confirmed experimentally by pide (year not specified). The analysis enables future detailed fits to the CLAS data and motivates further study of the N(1535) resonance role in few-body photoproduction.
