# Kaon photoproduction with form factors in a gauge-invariant approach

> The general gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism given by Haberzettl is applied to kaon photoproduction off the nucleon at the tree level, with form factors describing composite nucleons. We demonstrate that, in contrast to Ohta's gauge-invariance prescription, this formalism allows electric current contributions to be multiplied by a form factor, i.e., they do not need to be treated like bare currents. Numerical results show that Haberzettl's gauge procedure, when compared to Ohta's, leads to much improved $χ^2$ values. Moreover, predictions for the new Bonn SAPHIR data for $p(γ,K^+)Λ$ are given.

# Kaon Photoproduction with Form Factors in a Gauge-invariant Approach

## Key claim
Haberzettl's gauge-invariant formalism for kaon photoproduction allows electric current contributions to be multiplied by hadronic form factors, unlike Ohta's prescription which forces them to be treated as bare currents. The resulting χ² values are improved by at least a factor of two compared to Ohta's method, and the predictions for new Bonn SAPHIR data favor Haberzettl's approach.

## Method
The authors applied the general gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism of Haberzettl (1997) to kaon photoproduction off the nucleon at tree level, using hadronic form factors to describe composite nucleons. They compared two prescriptions for restoring gauge invariance, Ohta's (1989) method, which fixes the undetermined function $\widehat{F}$ to a constant value of 1, and Haberzettl's method, which allows $\widehat{F}$ to be a linear combination of the s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel form factors with coefficients summing to unity. The underlying resonance model was taken from the work of Mart, Bennhold, and Hyde-Wright (1995).

## Key numbers
- χ² per data point for Haberzettl's method is better than Ohta's by at least a factor of two, across the range of the leading Born coupling constant $g_{K\Lambda N}/\sqrt{4\pi}$.
- The form factor cutoff parameter Λ decreases with increasing $g_{K\Lambda N}$ coupling constant in Haberzettl's method, leaving the effective coupling roughly constant; in Ohta's method the cutoff remains insensitive to the coupling constant.
- Differential cross sections for $p(\gamma, K^+)\Lambda$ are predicted for four energies using new, unpublished Bonn SAPHIR data.

## Context
This work extends the general gauge-invariant photoproduction formalism of Haberzettl (1997) to the specific case of kaon photoproduction, and directly contrasts it with the earlier gauge-invariance prescription of Ohta (1989). The results demonstrate that Ohta's approach is too restrictive to account for hadronic structure while maintaining gauge invariance, whereas Haberzettl's method can accommodate composite nucleon effects. The predictions for the new Bonn SAPHIR data for $p(\gamma, K^+)\Lambda$ provide a test that will distinguish between the two approaches when those data become publicly available.
