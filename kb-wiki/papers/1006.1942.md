# Nucleon-nucleon scattering within a multiple subtractive renormalization approach

> A methodology to renormalize the nucleon-nucleon interaction, using a recursive multiple subtraction approach to construct the kernel of the scattering equation, is presented. We solve the subtracted scattering equation with the next-leading-order (NLO) and next-to-next-leading-order (NNLO) interactions. The results are presented for all partial waves up to $j=2$, fitted to low-energy experimental data. In our renormalizaton group invariant method, when introducing the NLO and NNLO interactions, the subtraction energy emerges as a renormalization scale and the momentum associated with it comes to be about the QCD scale ($Λ_{QCD}$), irrespectively to the partial wave.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a multiple subtractive renormalization approach, which avoids any cutoff regularization, can produce finite and renormalization-group-invariant results for nucleon-nucleon scattering up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and partial waves up to \(j=2\), with the subtraction scale emerging naturally near the QCD scale (\(\Lambda_{QCD}\)) regardless of the partial wave.

## Method

The authors solve a subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space using a recursive multiple subtraction technique, where the number of subtractions (\(n\)) is chosen to render the kernel finite: \(n=1\) for leading order (LO), \(n=3\) for next-to-leading order (NLO), and \(n=4\) for NNLO. The driving term is constructed by evolving the potential through consecutive subtractions at a reference energy scale \(-\mu^2\), with the LO, NLO, and NNLO interactions (including one-pion exchange, two-pion exchange, and contact terms) inserted at the step where enough subtractions have been performed. The method is applied to all partial waves up to \(j=2\), and the contact strengths are fitted to low-energy experimental phase-shift data up to about 200 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

* The subtraction energy scale \(-\mu^2\) is found to lie between \(-50\) and \(-100\) MeV for all partial waves up to \(j=2\), corresponding to a momentum scale about the QCD scale (\(\Lambda_{QCD}\)).
* For the LO contact interactions, the strengths are \(\lambda_0^{^1S_0} = -0.0203\) fm and \(\lambda_0^{^3S_1} = -0.24142\) fm at the scale \(-\bar\mu^2\) with \(\bar\mu = 30\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* For the NLO fit, the \(^1S_0\) channel uses \(\lambda_2 = 2.2660\) fm\(^3\) and \(\lambda_3 = 2.0047\) fm\(^5\) at \(-\mu^2 = -50\) MeV; the \(^3P_0\) channel uses \(\lambda_1 = 0.25\) fm\(^3\) at \(-\mu^2 = -100\) MeV.
* The spectral regularization scale for the two-pion exchange is set to \(\tilde\Lambda = 600\) MeV (approximately four pion masses).

## Context

This work extends the multiple subtractive renormalization method previously applied to LO interactions (Timoteo et al., 1999; Frederico et al., 2000) to NLO and NNLO chiral potentials, going beyond earlier calculations that were limited to lower orders or partial waves. The approach differs from the coordinate-space renormalization of Valderrama and Arriola (2009) and the mixed scheme of Yang, Epelbaum, and collaborators (2008, 2009), which still rely on a cutoff; here, no cutoff is used, and unitarity is strictly preserved. The results enable systematic, RG-invariant calculations of NN scattering at higher chiral orders and provide a framework for incorporating subleading contact interactions without perturbative treatment.
