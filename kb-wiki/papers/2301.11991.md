# Real-time non-perturbative dynamics of jet production: quantum entanglement and vacuum modification

> The production of jets should allow testing the real-time response of the QCD vacuum disturbed by the propagation of high-momentum color charges. Addressing this problem theoretically requires a real-time, non-perturbative method. It is well known that the Schwinger model [QED in $(1+1)$ dimensions] shares many common properties with QCD, including confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, and the existence of vacuum fermion condensate. As a step in developing such an approach, we report here on fully quantum simulations of a massive Schwinger model coupled to external sources representing quark and antiquark jets as produced in $e^+e^-$ annihilation. We study, for the first time, the modification of the vacuum chiral condensate by the propagating jets and the quantum entanglement between the fragmenting jets. Our results indicate strong entanglement between the fragmentation products of the two jets at rapidity separations $Δη\leq 2$, which can potentially exist also in QCD and can be studied in experiments.

## Key claim

The paper reports fully quantum simulations of a massive Schwinger model coupled to external sources representing quark and antiquark jets, showing for the first time that the propagating jets strongly modify the vacuum chiral condensate and that the fragmentation products of the two jets exhibit strong quantum entanglement at rapidity separations Δη ≤ 2, which the authors suggest could potentially exist also in QCD and be studied in experiments.

## Method

The authors discretize the massive Schwinger model on a lattice of N sites with staggered fermions and non-compact U(1) gauge fields, then integrate out the gauge fields via Gauss law to reduce the Hilbert space to 2^N dimensions. They prepare the ground state of the massive Schwinger model, then time-evolve it under a Hamiltonian that includes external currents representing a quark-antiquark pair moving along the light cone, using exact diagonalization. They compute local charge density, electric field energy, chiral condensate, and entanglement entropy between left and right halves of the lattice, and compare two-point correlation functions of the scalar fermion density for correlated versus uncorrelated source configurations.

## Key numbers

- For parameters N=20, m=0.25/a, and g=0.5/a, the vacuum fermion condensate integrated over the lattice length is ν0 = -5.16, corresponding to a local vacuum condensate of ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ = -0.258/a.
- Strong entanglement between fragmentation products is observed at rapidity separations Δη ≤ 2.
- The boundary zone in open-boundary simulations extends over approximately 4-5 lattice sites, matching the naive estimate of the correlation length ξ ∼ 1/m = 4a.
- For the massless case (m=0, g=1/a, N=20), the electric field energy stays roughly constant after a short initial time, indicating strong screening.
- The correlation functions measured for the correlated state are an order of magnitude greater than those for the uncorrelated state.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Casher, Kogut, and Susskind (1974) to model quark-antiquark production in e+e- annihilation using the Schwinger model with external sources, and builds on analytical solutions for the massless case found by Loshaj (2011) and Kharzeev (2012). It goes beyond the classical-statistical approach of Hebenstreit (2014) by performing fully quantum simulations, and enables future quantum computing implementations of jet fragmentation. The authors suggest that the observed entanglement could be studied experimentally through correlations between pion pairs produced in jet fragmentation, as previously proposed by Efremov (1995).
