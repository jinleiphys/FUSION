# Shear and Bulk Viscosities of a Weakly Coupled Quark Gluon Plasma with Finite Chemical Potential and Temperature---Leading-Log Results

> We calculate the shear (eta) and bulk (zeta) viscosities of a weakly coupled quark gluon plasma at the leading-log order with finite temperature T and quark chemical potential mu. We find that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s increases monotonically with mu and eventually scales as (mu/T)^2 at large mu. In contrary, zeta/s is insensitive to mu. Both eta/s and zeta/s are monotonically decreasing functions of the quark flavor number N_f when N_f \geq 2. This property is also observed in pion gas systems. Our perturbative calculation suggests that QCD becomes the most perfect (i.e. with the smallest eta/s) at mu=0 and N_f = 16 (the maximum N_f with asymptotic freedom). It would be interesting to test whether the currently smallest eta/s computed close to the phase transition with mu=0 and N_f = 0 can be further reduced by increasing N_f.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) increases monotonically with quark chemical potential μ and scales as (μ/T)² at large μ, while the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio (ζ/s) is insensitive to μ; both ratios decrease monotonically with quark flavor number Nf for Nf ≥ 2, suggesting that QCD becomes the most perfect fluid at μ = 0 and Nf = 16, though this is a perturbative leading-log result that may not directly apply near the phase transition temperature.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma using the Chapman-Enskog expansion, considering two-particle scattering processes at leading-log order with finite temperature T and quark chemical potential μ. They computed shear viscosity η and bulk viscosity ζ from the linearized collision kernels using the Kubo formula, with thermal masses for gluons and quarks computed self-consistently. The numerical solutions were obtained by expanding the perturbation functions in orthogonal polynomials and solving the resulting linear equations systematically from below.

## Key numbers

- η/s scales as (μ/T)² at large μ (μ/T → ∞)
- ζ/s is insensitive to μ, with ζ ∝ μ²T in the T/μ → 0 limit
- η ∝ 1/T in the T/μ → 0 limit (inversely proportional to temperature)
- ζ ∝ T in the T/μ → 0 limit (proportional to temperature)
- The maximum ζ occurs at Nf = 5 or 6, depending on the value of μ/T
- The Taylor expansion coefficients aη (η at μ=0) agree with Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) to better than 1% for all Nf computed
- The Taylor expansion coefficients aζ (ζ at μ=0) agree with Arnold, Dogan, and Moore (2006) to better than 1% for all Nf computed
- η/s is monotonically decreasing with Nf for Nf ≥ 2 (but not for Nf = 0 and 1, with a crossing between Nf = 1 and 2 at μ²/T² ≈ 1.8)

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative QCD calculations of shear and bulk viscosities by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) and Arnold, Dogan, and Moore (2006) from zero chemical potential to finite quark chemical potential μ at leading-log order. The results are qualitatively consistent with pion gas calculations by Chen and Wang (2006, 2007) which showed similar Nf dependence of η/s and ζ/s in the hadronic phase. The calculation enables exploration of whether the minimal η/s near the phase transition temperature (currently computed for Nf = 0) could be further reduced by increasing the number of quark flavors, and suggests that the most perfect fluid in perturbative QCD occurs at μ = 0 and Nf = 16.
