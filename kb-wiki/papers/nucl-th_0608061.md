# Hadron Resonances and Phase Threshold in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We show that a measurement of the reaction energy dependence of relative hadron resonance yields in heavy ion collisions can be used to study the phase structure of the dense QCD matter created in these collisions, and investigate the origin of the trends observed in the excitation functions of certain soft hadronic observables. We show that presence of chemical nonequilibrium in light quark abundance imparts a characteristic signature on the energy dependence of resonance yields, that differs considerably from what is expected in the equilibrium picture.

## Key claim

The energy dependence of hadron resonance-to-ground-state yield ratios can discriminate between chemical equilibrium and chemical non-equilibrium freeze-out scenarios in heavy ion collisions, because the two models predict opposite trends: a monotonic rise versus a characteristic dip at a critical energy near 30 A GeV.

## Method

The authors used the statistical hadronization code SHARE to compute resonance-to-stable-particle ratios (e.g., K*/K, Δ/p, Σ*/Λ, Λ(1520)/Λ, Ξ*/Ξ) as functions of collision energy, employing two sets of freeze-out parameters: the equilibrium parametrization from Cleymans et al. (2005) with γq=γs=1, and the non-equilibrium parametrization from Becattini et al. (2004) with fitted γq and γs. They compared the resulting energy dependence of 12 different resonance ratios across AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies, and considered the potential influence of resonance rescattering and regeneration using results from the UrQMD model.

## Key numbers

- In the equilibrium model, freeze-out temperature T increases monotonically from T ~ 50 MeV at lowest SIS energies to T ~ 160-170 MeV at highest RHIC energies.
- In the non-equilibrium model, freeze-out temperature shows a minimum near 30 A GeV, where the system transitions from chemically under-saturated (γq < 1) to over-saturated (γq > 1) light quark phase space.
- The non-equilibrium model gives T ~ 170 MeV for 11.6 A GeV (highest AGS) and 20 A GeV (lowest SPS) reactions, dropping to T ~ 140 MeV at the supercooling point near 30 A GeV.
- The UrQMD calculation (figures 7 and 8 in the cited work) shows a smooth rise of resonance ratios with energy, qualitatively similar to the equilibrium model predictions.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model debate between the equilibrium approach of Cleymans et al. (2005) and the non-equilibrium approach of Becattini et al. (2004), which disagree on whether absolute chemical equilibrium holds for light quarks. The paper proposes resonance ratios as a new observable to resolve this disagreement, motivated by the "kink" in multiplicity per participant and the "horn" in K+/π+ ratios that the equilibrium model cannot reproduce. If confirmed, the predicted dip in resonance yields would provide evidence for supercooling from a high-entropy-density phase (QGP) and rule out rescattering/regeneration explanations, while a monotonic rise would support the equilibrium picture. The measurement is feasible with existing SPS (NA49) and RHIC experiments, as well as the planned CBM experiment.
