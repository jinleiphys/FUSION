# $NN \to NN π$ reaction near threshold in a covariant one-boson-exchange model

> We calculate the cross sections for the $p(p,nπ^{+})p$ and $p(p,pπ^{0})p$ reactions for proton beam energies near threshold in a covariant one-boson-exchange model, which incorporates the exchange of $π$, $ρ$, $σ$ and $ω$ mesons and treats both nucleon and delta isobar as intermediate states. The final state interaction effects have also been included. The $ω$ meson exchange is found to contribute significantly at these energies, which, along with other meson exchanges, provides an excellent agreement with the data. The cross sections at beam energies $\leq$ 300 MeV are almost free from the contributions of the $Δ$ isobar excitation.

# NN → NNπ reaction near threshold in a covariant one-boson-exchange model

## Key claim

The covariant effective one-boson-exchange model (CEOBEM), which includes π, ρ, σ, and ω meson exchanges and treats both nucleon and Δ isobar intermediate states, provides an excellent description of both p(p,nπ⁺)p and p(p,pπ⁰)p total cross sections and angular distributions near threshold, but only when final state interactions (FSI) among the outgoing nucleons are included. The ω meson exchange contributes significantly near threshold, and cross sections at beam energies ≤ 300 MeV are almost free from Δ isobar excitation contributions.

## Method

The authors calculate the transition amplitude using a fully covariant one-boson-exchange model (CEOBEM) that sums coherently contributions from π, ρ, σ, and ω meson exchange diagrams, with both nucleon and Δ isobar intermediate states. Final state interactions among the two outgoing nucleons are included via the Watson-Migdal theory, where the rescattering amplitude T_ff is approximated by the inverse of the Jost function for s-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering, using effective range parameters from the literature. The primary production amplitude T_fi is computed with coupling constants and cutoff masses previously determined by fitting to NN scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for p(p,nπ⁺)p and p(p,pπ⁰)p are calculated for proton beam energies from near threshold up to approximately 320 MeV and above, with excellent agreement to experimental data.
- At beam energies ≤ 300 MeV, the contribution of Δ isobar excitation to the cross section is negligible; at 320 MeV, Δ isobar excitation makes a visible contribution.
- Angular distributions for p(p,nπ⁺)p are shown at beam energies of 294 MeV, 300 MeV, and 320 MeV, with near isotropy observed at ≤ 300 MeV, indicating s-wave pion final state.
- The ω meson exchange contribution is found to be larger than the π exchange contribution very close to threshold, while the σ meson exchange contribution is smaller than that found by Horowitz et al. (1990).

## Context

This work extends the covariant one-boson-exchange model previously developed by the same authors (Shyam and Mosel, 1995) to include final state interactions, enabling a consistent description of both π⁺ and π⁰ production in pp collisions near threshold. It addresses the failure of earlier non-relativistic approaches such as those of Koltun and Reitan (1966), Lee and Riska (1993), and Horowitz et al. (1990) to reproduce the energy dependence of measured cross sections, and the inadequacy of the Schillaci-Silbar-Young (1969) and Lee-Matsuyama (1987) models for π⁺ production. The results enable future tests of the short-range axial-charge operator relevant to first-forbidden β transitions in heavy nuclei, and motivate measurements of polarization observables to further constrain the role of final state interactions and pion rescattering.
