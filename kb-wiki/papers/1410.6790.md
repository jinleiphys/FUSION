# Anisotropic hydrodynamics for conformal Gubser flow

> We derive the equations of motion for a system undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal and azimuthally-symmetric transverse "Gubser flow" using leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics. This is accomplished by assuming that the one-particle distribution function is ellipsoidally-symmetric in the momenta conjugate to the de Sitter coordinates used to parameterize the Gubser flow. We then demonstrate that the SO(3)_q symmetry in de Sitter space further constrains the anisotropy tensor to be of spheroidal form. The resulting system of two coupled ordinary differential equations for the de Sitter-space momentum scale and anisotropy parameter are solved numerically and compared to a recently obtained exact solution of the relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann equation subject to the same flow. We show that anisotropic hydrodynamics describes the spatio-temporal evolution of the system better than all currently known dissipative hydrodynamics approaches. In addition, we prove that anisotropic hydrodynamics gives the exact solution of the relaxation-time approximation Boltzmann equation in the ideal, eta/s -> 0, and free-streaming, eta/s -> infinity, limits.

## Key claim

Anisotropic hydrodynamics describes the spatio-temporal evolution of a system undergoing Gubser flow better than all currently known dissipative hydrodynamics approaches, and it gives the exact solution of the relaxation-time approximation Boltzmann equation in both the ideal (eta/s -> 0) and free-streaming (eta/s -> infinity) limits, but only for the specific case of conformal Gubser flow with SO(3)_q symmetry.

## Method

The authors assumed a one-particle distribution function that is ellipsoidally-symmetric in the momenta conjugate to de Sitter coordinates used to parameterize Gubser flow, then showed that SO(3)_q symmetry in de Sitter space constrains the anisotropy tensor to spheroidal form. They derived two coupled ordinary differential equations for the de Sitter-space momentum scale and anisotropy parameter by taking the first and second moments of the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation, and solved these equations numerically.

## Key numbers

- The de Sitter-space metric tensor is diag(-1, cosh^2 rho, cosh^2 rho sin^2 theta, 1), with determinant -cosh^4 rho sin^2 theta.
- The energy density in de Sitter space is given by hat_epsilon = (3 hat_alpha_theta^4 hat_lambda^4)/(2 pi^2) H_2(bar_y), where bar_y = sqrt((3 hat_alpha_varsigma^2 - 1)/2).
- The effective temperature matching condition is hat_T = (hat_alpha_varsigma/bar_y) (H_2(bar_y)/2)^(1/4) hat_lambda.
- The relaxation time is hat_tau_eq = 5 hat_bar_eta / hat_T, where hat_bar_eta = eta/s.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework, previously applied to boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous (0+1d) flow by Florkowski (2013) and Nopoush (2014), to the more realistic case of Gubser flow which includes both cylindrically-symmetric transverse and boost-invariant longitudinal (1+1d) expansion. The results are compared to exact solutions of the relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann equation obtained by Denicol (2014) and to Israel-Stewart second-order viscous hydrodynamics solutions from Marrochio (2013). This enables testing of anisotropic hydrodynamics in a setting more relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
