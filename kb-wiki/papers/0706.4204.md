# Investigation of Pygmy Dipole Resonances in the Tin Region

> The evolution of the low-energy electromagnetic dipole response with the neutron excess is investigated along the Sn isotopic chain within an approach incorporating Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov (HFB) and multi-phonon Quasiparticle-Phonon-Model (QPM) theory. General aspects of the relationship of nuclear skins and dipole sum rules are discussed. Neutron and proton transition densities serve to identify the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) as a generic mode of excitation. The PDR is distinct from the GDR by its own characteristic pattern given by a mixture of isoscalar and isovector components. Results for the $^{100}$Sn-$^{132}$Sn isotopes and the several N=82 isotones are presented. In the heavy Sn-isotopes the PDR excitations are closely related to the thickness of the neutron skin. Approaching $^{100}$Sn a gradual change from a neutron to a proton skin is found and the character of the PDR is changed correspondingly. A delicate balance between Coulomb and strong interaction effects is found. The fragmentation of the PDR strength in $^{124}$Sn is investigated by multi-phonon calculations. Recent measurements of the dipole response in $^{130,132}$Sn are well reproduced.

## Key claim

The Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) is identified as a generic excitation mode distinct from the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) by a mixture of isoscalar and isovector components, with its character changing from neutron-skin-related to proton-skin-related as the isotopic chain moves from neutron-rich \(^{132}\)Sn toward the proton-rich \(^{100}\)Sn.

## Method

The authors combine Hartree-Fock-Bogoljubov (HFB) mean-field theory with the multi-phonon Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) to calculate the low-energy dipole response along the Sn isotopic chain from \(^{100}\)Sn to \(^{132}\)Sn and for several N=82 isotones. Ground-state properties are obtained from a phenomenological density functional approach using Wood-Saxon potentials fitted to reproduce binding energies and charge radii, while excited states are described in a QRPA framework with transition densities used to distinguish PDR from GDR modes.

## Key numbers

- The pairing interaction strength is set to \(V_{ext} = -9280\ \text{MeV fm}^3\) to reproduce the singlet-even scattering length \(a_{SE} = -17.3\ \text{fm}\), and \(V_{int} = -0.721\ \text{MeV fm}^3\) to give a maximum pairing gap \(\Delta(\rho_c) = 2\ \text{MeV}\) at \(\rho_c = 1/3 \rho_0\) with \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Neutron skins in heavy Sn isotopes are predicted to exceed the proton distribution by up to about 1 fm.
- The density exponent in the pairing strength is \(\beta = 1/85 \approx 0.012\).
- The PDR fragmentation in \(^{124}\)Sn is investigated by multi-phonon calculations, and dipole response measurements in \(^{130,132}\)Sn are well reproduced.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier QPM studies of the PDR in \(^{208}\)Pb (Rye02, Tsoneva 2004a, 2004b) to the Sn isotopic chain, confirming the PDR as a generic mode related to neutron-skin thickness. The theoretical approach builds on the three-fluid hydrodynamical model of Mohan (1971) and addresses its failure for Ca isotopes noted by Chambers (1994). The results enable systematic predictions of PDR evolution toward the proton dripline and provide a framework for interpreting upcoming radioactive-beam experiments on unstable tin isotopes.
