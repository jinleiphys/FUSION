# Kaon production cross sections from baryon-baryon interactions

> In a one-pion plus one-kaon exchange model, we calculate the kaon production cross sections in nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions. We find that this model describes reasonably well the experimental data on kaon production in the proton-proton interaction. Near the kaon production threshold, the cross section obtained from this model is smaller than that from the linear parameterization of Randrup and Ko. For kaon production cross sections from the nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions, the cross sections are singular in free space, so we calculate them in a nuclear medium by including the (complex) pion self-energy. The results are compared with the scaling ansatz of Randrup and Ko. The theoretical cross sections are then used in a transport model to study kaon production from Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon.

# Kaon production cross sections from baryon-baryon interactions

## Key claim

In a one-pion plus one-kaon exchange model, the authors calculate kaon production cross sections for nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-delta, and delta-delta interactions, finding that near the kaon production threshold the cross section from this model is smaller than that from the linear parameterization of Randrup and Ko. For nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions, the cross sections are singular in free space and are therefore calculated in a nuclear medium by including the complex pion self-energy.

## Method

The authors use a one-pion plus one-kaon exchange model to compute kaon production cross sections in baryon-baryon interactions, with the pion and kaon exchange amplitudes treated incoherently. For nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions, the pion propagator is replaced by an in-medium propagator that includes a complex pion self-energy calculated from delta-hole polarization in the random-phase approximation, with the delta decay width and pion self-energy determined self-consistently. The theoretical cross sections are then used in a non-relativistic transport model (VUU) to study kaon production from Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The kaon production threshold in nucleon-nucleon interactions in free space is approximately 1.58 GeV.
- The linear parameterization of Randrup and Ko is given by σ_linear = 24 (p_max / m_K) μb, and the quartic parameterization of Zwermann by σ_quartic = 800 (p_max / m_K)^4 μb.
- Coupling constants used: f_πNN^2/(4π) = 0.08, f_πNΔ^2/(4π) = 0.37, f_KNΛ^2/(4π) = 0.97, f_KNΣ^2/(4π) = 0.07, f_KΔΣ^2/(4π) = 0.23.
- Cut-off parameters: Λ_π = 1.2 GeV, Λ_K = 0.9 GeV.
- In Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon, there are about 112 baryon-baryon collisions with available energies above kaon production thresholds, with an average energy of about 2.64 GeV.
- The nucleon-delta interaction accounts for about 65% of kaon production in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon.
- The theoretical kaon momentum spectrum overestimates experimental data from Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon by about 30%.
- The nuclear matter density used for pion self-energy calculations is 2ρ_0, with ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends the one-pion exchange model of Ko (1989) by adding one-kaon exchange contributions and by treating nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions, which had previously only been handled via the scaling ansatz of Randrup and Ko (1980). The results contradict the linear parameterization of Randrup and Ko (1980) near threshold, where the present model gives smaller cross sections, and instead agree more closely with the quartic parameterization of Zwermann (1987) at low energies. The calculated cross sections enable more accurate transport model studies of subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for Au+Au at 1 GeV/nucleon, and the authors plan future work using a relativistic transport model that includes consistent medium effects on both the elementary cross sections and collision dynamics.
