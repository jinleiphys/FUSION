# Pseudoscalar Meson Radial Excitations

> Goldstone modes are the only pseudoscalar mesons to possess a nonzero leptonic decay constant in the chiral limit when chiral symmetry is dynamically broken. The decay constants of their radial excitations vanish. These features and aspects of their impact on the meson spectrum are illustrated using a manifestly covariant and symmetry preserving model of the kernels in the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations.

## Key claim

Goldstone modes are the only pseudoscalar mesons with a nonzero leptonic decay constant in the chiral limit when chiral symmetry is dynamically broken; the decay constants of their radial excitations vanish identically in that limit, a necessary consequence of the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity combined with the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation.

## Method

The authors solve the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations using a renormalisation-group-improved model for the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, which combines a finite-width representation of a long-range confining interaction with a perturbative term that reproduces QCD’s ultraviolet behaviour. The gap equation is solved for the dressed-quark propagator, and the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation is then solved numerically to obtain the masses, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and leptonic decay constants of the ground-state pion and its first radial excitation for several values of the renormalised current-quark mass.

## Key numbers

- In the chiral limit (renormalised current-quark mass \(m(\zeta_0)=0\)), the ground-state pion mass \(m_{\pi_0}=0\) and its leptonic decay constant \(f_{\pi_0}=0.091\) GeV; the first radial excitation has mass \(m_{\pi_1}=1.08\) GeV and \(f_{\pi_1}=0\).
- For a renormalised current-quark mass \(m(\zeta_0)=0.0055\) GeV (physical pion), the calculated values are \(m_{\pi_0}=0.14\) GeV, \(f_{\pi_0}=0.093\) GeV, \(m_{\pi_1}=1.10\) GeV, and \(f_{\pi_1}=-0.002\) GeV.
- For \(m(\zeta_0)=0.125\) GeV (strange-quark mass region), the results are \(m_{\pi_0}=0.69\) GeV, \(f_{\pi_0}=0.130\) GeV, \(m_{\pi_1}=1.40\) GeV, and \(f_{\pi_1}=-0.023\) GeV.
- The pseudoscalar vertex residue for the first excited state is negative in the chiral limit: \(\rho_{\pi_1}(\zeta)=-(0.49)^2\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\zeta=19\) GeV.
- A 30% increase in the interaction range parameter \(r_a=1/\omega\) raises \(m_{\pi_0}\) by 3% but reduces \(m_{\pi_1}\) by 14%.
- The estimated charge radius of the first radial excitation is \(r_{\pi_1}=1.7\,r_{\pi_0}\).

## Context

This work extends the exact chiral-limit result that the leptonic decay constant of any radially excited pseudoscalar meson must vanish (previously noted by Dominguez (year) and Volkov (year)) and illustrates it within a manifestly covariant, symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework. The model used is the renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation introduced by Maris and Tandy (1999), which has been widely applied to meson properties. The results provide a constructive constraint on approaches to hadron spectroscopy and enable future quantitative studies of higher radial excitations, systems with unequal quark masses, and the interpretation of exotic hadron candidates.
