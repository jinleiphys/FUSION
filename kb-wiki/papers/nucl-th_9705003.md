# Neutrino-nucleus reactions on ^{12}C and ^{16}O

> Exclusive and inclusive $(ν_μ, μ^-), (ν_e, e^-)$ cross-sections and $μ^-$-capture rates are calculated for ^{12}C and ^{16}O using the consistent random phase approximation (RPA) and pairing model. After a pairing correction is introduced to the RPA results the flux-averaged theoretical $(ν_μ, μ^-), (ν_e, e^-)$ cross-sections and $μ^-$-capture rates in $^{12}$C are in good agreement with experiment. In particular when one takes into account the experimental error bars, the recently measured range of values for the $(ν_μ, μ^-)$ cross-section is in agreement with the present theoretical results. Predictions of $(ν_μ, μ^-)$ and $(ν_e, e^-)$ cross-sections in ^{16}O are also presented.

# Neutrino-nucleus reactions on ^{12}C and ^{16}O

## Key claim

After introducing a pairing correction to the consistent Hartree-Fock Random Phase Approximation (HF-RPA) results, the flux-averaged theoretical cross-sections and muon-capture rates for ^{12}C agree with experiment within error bars, but the agreement depends on using the same pairing parameter for all calculated quantities and the results are sensitive to the choice of the pairing parameter value.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock calculations using Skyrme forces SGII and SIII, then applied charge-exchange RPA to compute transition amplitudes for neutrino-nucleus reactions on ^{12}C and ^{16}O. For ^{12}C, which is not a good closed-shell nucleus, they introduced pairing correlations via Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation factors applied to the single-particle matrix elements from the RPA solutions. The energy-dependent cross-sections were folded with experimental neutrino flux spectra from the LSND and KARMEN experiments to obtain flux-averaged cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Inclusive ^{12}C(nu_mu, mu^-)X cross-section: 13.5 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (SGII) and 14.5 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (SIII), compared to experimental LSND value of (11.2 +/- 0.3 +/- 1.8) x 10^{-40} cm^2
- Inclusive ^{16}O(nu_mu, mu^-)X cross-section predictions: 27.8 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (SGII) and 27.1 x 10^{-40} cm^2 (SIII)
- Inclusive ^{16}O(nu_e, e^-)X cross-section predictions: 17.2 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (SGII) and 16.9 x 10^{-42} cm^2 (SIII)
- Calculated HF-RPA muon-capture rate for ^{16}O: 1.05 x 10^5 s^{-1} (SGII) and 1.00 x 10^5 s^{-1} (SIII), compared to experimental value of 0.98 x 10^5 s^{-1}
- Pairing correction reduced exclusive cross-sections to the ground state of the daughter nucleus by factors of 4-7 compared to RPA results
- Inclusive cross-sections to excited states were reduced by 25% due to pairing correction
- RPA reduced muon-capture rates by 30% in ^{12}C and 50% in ^{16}O compared to Hartree-Fock

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA calculations of muon-capture rates by Mekjian (1974) and addresses a factor-of-two disagreement between previous theoretical results by Kolbe et al. (1994) and the LSND experimental measurement for inclusive ^{12}C(nu_mu, mu^-)X. The authors show that including pairing correlations, which had previously been applied only to exclusive processes by Kolbe et al. (1994), is also necessary for inclusive cross-sections. The results enable improved theoretical predictions for neutrino-nucleus reactions relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments using carbon and oxygen targets, and provide predictions for ^{16}O where experimental neutrino data were lacking at the time.
