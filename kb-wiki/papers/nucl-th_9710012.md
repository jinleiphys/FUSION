# Quantum Kinetics of Deconfinement Transitions in Dense Nuclear Matter: Dissipation Effects at Low Temperatures

> Effects of energy dissipation on quantum nucleation of two-flavor quark matter in dense nuclear matter encountered in neutron star cores are examined at low temperatures. We find that low-energy excitations of nucleons and electrons reduce the nucleation rate exponentially via their collisions with the surface of a quark matter droplet.

## Key claim

Energy dissipation from ballistic collisions of low-energy nucleon and electron excitations with the surface of a quark matter droplet reduces the quantum nucleation rate exponentially at low temperatures, increasing the critical overpressure required for droplet formation compared to the dissipation-free case.

## Method

The author models the formation of a two-flavor quark matter droplet in β-stable nuclear matter via quantum tunneling using a Lagrangian for the droplet radius, with an effective mass derived from nucleon kinetic energy and a potential including bulk, electron, and surface terms. Dissipation is introduced as an Ohmic frictional force from momentum transfer when ballistic quasiparticles (mean free paths > 10² fm, much larger than the critical droplet radius of 1–10 fm) collide with the droplet surface, following the path-integral formalism of Caldeira and Leggett as applied by Burmistrov and Dubovskii. The nucleation time is computed from the WKB tunneling probability multiplied by a dissipation-induced reduction factor, and the crossover temperature between quantum tunneling and thermal activation is derived.

## Key numbers

- The static deconfinement transition pressure is P₀ ≅ 445 MeV fm⁻³, where the baryon density jumps from ~5n₀ to ~8n₀ (n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density).
- For σ = 10 MeV fm⁻², α = 1, and N = 10⁴⁸, dissipation changes the critical overpressure ΔP_c from ~7 MeV fm⁻³ to ~21 MeV fm⁻³ and the critical droplet radius R_c from ~4.1 fm to ~1.9 fm.
- The crossover temperature T₀ from quantum tunneling to thermal activation for the same parameters goes from ~1.7 MeV to ~0.4 MeV.
- With nucleon superfluidity (n_{nm,n} = n_{nm,p} = 0), for σ = 10 MeV fm⁻², α = 1, and N = 10⁴⁸, ΔP_c ~ 11 MeV fm⁻³, R_c ~ 3.2 fm, and T₀ ~ 1.0 MeV.
- The oscillation frequency ν₀ is of order 10²³ sec⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature quantum nucleation calculations of Iida and Sato (1997) by incorporating energy dissipation from low-energy excitations, which were previously ignored. It applies the dissipative quantum tunneling formalism of Caldeira and Leggett (1983) and Burmistrov and Dubovskii (1991) to the deconfinement transition in neutron star cores. The results enable estimates of whether the mixed phase of quark and nuclear matter proposed by Glendenning (1992) and Heiselberg et al. (1993) can actually form dynamically during neutron star spin-down or accretion, and indicate that quantum nucleation likely dominates over thermal activation for core temperatures ≲ 0.1 MeV.
