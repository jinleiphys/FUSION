# pbarp partial wave analysis and NbarN potentials

> Invited talk given at the NAN '93 Conference in Moscow, Russia, sept 1993. A review is given of the different ways to describe pbarp scattering. Next the Nijmegen partial wave analyses of the pbarp data as well as the corresponding Nijmegen NbarN database are discussed. These partial wave analyses are finally used as a tool to construct a better NbarN potential model and also to clarify questions raised in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a statistically successful energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of antiproton-proton scattering data is possible, despite the near absence of spin-transfer and spin-correlation data, because the long-range potential (dominated by pion, rho, omega, and epsilon exchange) is well known and dominates the partial-wave amplitudes; the authors hedge that their fit yields χ²_min/N_df = 1.085, which is about 3.5 standard deviations from the ideal expectation of 1.000 ± 0.024, but they note this is comparable to the quality of their pp PWA.

## Method

The authors performed an energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of all available elastic and charge-exchange pbarp scattering data in the laboratory momentum range 119 MeV/c < p_L < 923 MeV/c, using a hybrid boundary-condition and optical-potential model with a boundary radius b = 1.3 fm. The long-range potential (r > b) consisted of the charge-conjugated Nijmegen NN potential Nijm78 plus electromagnetic interactions, while the short-range interaction (r ≤ b) was parametrized via a square-well optical potential with 30 free parameters. The final dataset contained N_d = 3646 experimental data points, from which 744 data were rejected as contradictory or unreliable.

## Key numbers

- The final pbarp dataset contains N_d = 3646 experimental data points, with N_n = 113 normalizations and N_p = 30 parameters, giving N_df = 3503 degrees of freedom.
- The minimum chi-squared per degree of freedom is χ²_min/N_df = 1.085 for pbarp, compared to χ²_min/N_df = 1.108 for pp (NijmPWA93).
- The charged pion-nucleon coupling constant determined from the charge-exchange reaction pbarp → barnn is f_c² = 0.0732 ± 0.0011, which is 64 standard deviations from zero.
- The Lambda-N-K coupling constant determined from pbarp → barLambda Lambda is f²_{Lambda N K} = 0.071 ± 0.007, and the fitted kaon mass is m(K) = 480 ± 60 MeV (experimental value 493.646 MeV).
- The boundary radius is fixed at b = 1.3 fm, determined by the width of the diffraction peak.
- The coupled-channels model CC93 achieves χ²/N_d = 1.58 with 26 parameters, while the optical-potential model gave χ²_min = 6 × 10^9 for N_d = 3309 data.
- Only 22% of the final dataset comes from LEAR; 610 elastic and 189 charge-exchange data points are from LEAR, versus 2536 elastic and 217 charge-exchange from other sources.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen group's long-standing program of partial-wave analyses of NN scattering (NijmPWA93, Klo93) to the pbarp system, using the same methodology. It contradicts the common assumption that a pbarp PWA is nearly impossible due to lack of spin data, and it enables the construction of improved NbarN potential models, specifically the Nijmegen CC93 coupled-channels model. The paper also clarifies questions raised in the literature about one-pion exchange and the G-parity rule in the NbarN system, and it provides a tool for judging the quality of individual datasets, leading to the Nijmegen NbarN database (Tim93).
