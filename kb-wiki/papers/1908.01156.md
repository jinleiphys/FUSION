# Isospin splitting of pion elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the framework of an extended multiphase transport model with mean-field potentials in both the partonic phase and the hadronic phase, we explain the elliptic flow difference between $π^+$ and $π^-$ in the beam-energy scan program at the relativistic heavy-ion collider by incorporating the vector-isovector potential for quarks and antiquarks with different isospins. It is found that the isospin splitting of pion elliptic flow favors a strong vector-isovector interaction, and thus serves as a probe of the quark matter equation of state as well as the QCD phase structure at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials.

# Isospin splitting of pion elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The elliptic flow difference between π⁺ and π⁻ observed in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program can be explained by incorporating a vector-isovector potential for quarks and antiquarks, but only if the vector-isovector coupling strength is strong (greater than twice the scalar-isoscalar coupling strength); the effect saturates for reduced coupling strengths R_IV > 2.

## Method
The authors used an extended multiphase transport (AMPT) model with mean-field potentials from a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including isovector couplings, with parton scattering cross section fitted to reproduce charged particle elliptic flow. The partonic phase evolution was described by a relativistic Boltzmann transport approach with test-particle method, followed by an improved coalescence hadronization algorithm that selects parton combinations with the largest Wigner function, and finally a hadronic transport (ART) model with mean-field potentials for pions, kaons, and baryons.

## Key numbers
- Parton scattering cross section: 10 mb (isotropic), used to reproduce charged particle v₂ at all RHIC-BES energies.
- Vector-isoscalar coupling strength: G_V = 1.1 G_S, fixed throughout the calculation.
- Reduced vector-isovector coupling strength R_IV = G_IV/G_S: values tested include 0, 2, and 5; the experimentally observed v₂ difference favors R_IV > 2.
- NJL model parameters: m_u = m_d = 3.6 MeV, m_s = 87 MeV, G_SΛ² = 3.6, KΛ⁵ = 8.9, cutoff Λ = 750 MeV.
- Central energy density threshold for end of partonic phase: 0.8 GeV/fm³.
- Collision energies studied: √s_NN = 7.7 GeV (representative system for detailed analysis) and other RHIC-BES energies.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Xu et al., 2014, 2016) that attributed v₂ splitting between protons/antiprotons and K⁺/K⁻ to vector-isoscalar potentials, now addressing the π⁺/π⁻ splitting via vector-isovector interactions. It contradicts the chiral magnetic wave explanation proposed by Burnier et al. (2011), which is disfavored by transport simulations based on chiral kinetic equations (Sun et al., 2016; Han et al., 2019). The results enable constraints on the quark matter equation of state and QCD phase structure at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials, and connect to predictions about the quark matter symmetry energy and critical point location from Liu et al. (2016).
