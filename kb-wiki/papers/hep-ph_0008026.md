# Correspondence of consistent and inconsistent spin-3/2 couplings via the equivalence theorem

> The Rarita-Schwinger theory of free massive spin-3/2 field obeys the physical degrees of freedom counting, and one can distinguish ``consistent'' couplings which maintain this property and ``inconsistent'' couplings which destroy it. We show how one can obtain consistent couplings from inconsistent ones by a redefinition of the spin-3/2 field. The field redefinition gives rise to additional ``contact terms'' which, via the equivalence theorem, can be associated with the contribution of the extra lower-spin degrees of freedom involved by the inconsistent coupling.

## Key claim

Any "inconsistent" linear coupling of a massive spin-3/2 field (which introduces spurious lower-spin degrees of freedom) can be transformed into a "consistent" gauge-invariant coupling via a field redefinition, with the equivalence of the two descriptions at the S-matrix level guaranteed by the equivalence theorem provided the resulting contact terms are included.

## Method

The author starts with the Rarita-Schwinger Lagrangian for a free massive spin-3/2 field and considers a general linear coupling to other fields. A field redefinition of the spin-3/2 field is performed, choosing the transformation such that the new linear coupling satisfies the divergenceless condition (gauge invariance) that preserves the physical degrees of freedom. The transformation generates additional contact terms quadratic in the coupling constant, and the equivalence theorem ensures that the original inconsistent coupling and the transformed consistent coupling plus contact terms yield identical S-matrix elements. The method is explicitly demonstrated for the conventional pi N Delta coupling, showing how the spin-1/2 background contributions in Delta-exchange amplitudes are canceled by the contact terms.

## Key numbers

* The Rarita-Schwinger propagator for a massive spin-3/2 field is given by S^{mu nu}(p) = (slashed p + m)/(p^2 - m^2 + i epsilon) times the tensor [-eta^{mu nu} + (1/3) gamma^mu gamma^nu + (1/(3m))(gamma^mu p^nu - gamma^nu p^mu) + (2/(3m^2)) p^mu p^nu].
* The conventional pi N Delta coupling contains an off-shell parameter z, and only for neutral particles with z = -1 is the degrees-of-freedom counting correct.
* The consistent (gauge-invariant) pi N Delta coupling is given by Lagrangian L' = -(ig/(2m)) bar G_{mu nu} gamma^{mu nu lambda} T^a Psi partial_lambda phi^a + H.c., where G_{mu nu} = partial_mu psi_nu - partial_nu psi_mu.
* The contact term generated by the field transformation for the pi N Delta case is L_{pi pi NN} = -(g/m)^2 bar Psi O^{(x)}_{rho mu} (gamma^{mu nu alpha} i partial_alpha + m gamma^{mu nu}) O^{(x)}_{nu sigma} T^{dagger b} T^c Psi (partial^rho phi^{dagger b})(partial^sigma phi^c), with x = -(1/3)(1+z).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous gauge-symmetric construction of pi N Delta and gamma N Delta couplings (Pascalutsa, 1998; Pascalutsa and Timmermans, 1999) by establishing their formal equivalence to the conventional inconsistent couplings used in decades of Delta-isobar studies (e.g., Nath, Etemadi, and Kimel, 1971). The result provides a theoretical foundation for understanding the "spin-1/2 backgrounds" that appear in conventional treatments, showing they can be reabsorbed into contact interactions. This enables cleaner extraction of spin-3/2 resonance properties from experimental programs at CEBAF and MAMI, and confirms the redundancy of the off-shell parameter in chiral perturbation theory as argued by Tang and Ellis (1996).
