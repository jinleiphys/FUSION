# Conformal Window and Correlation Functions in Lattice Conformal QCD

> We discuss various aspects of Conformal Field Theories on the Lattice. We mainly investigate the SU(3) gauge theory with N_f degenerate fermions in the fundamental representation, employing the one-plaquette gauge action and the Wilson fermion action. First we make a brief review of our previous works and thereby clarify the reason why we conjecture that the conformal window is 7 le N_f le 16. Secondly, we introduce a new concept, "conformal theories with an IR cutoff" and point out that any numerical simulation on a lattice is bounded by an IR cutoff Lambda_{IR}. Then we make predictions that when N_f is within the conformal window, the propagator of a meson G(t) behaves at large t, as G(t) = c exp(-m_H t)/t^alpha, that is, a modified Yukawa-type decay form, instead of the usual exponential decay form in the small quark mass region. This holds on an any lattice for any coupling constant g, as far as g is between 0 and g^*, where g^* is the IR fixed point. We verify that numerical results really satisfy the predictions for the N_f=7 case and the N_f=16 case. Thirdly, we discuss small number of flavors (N_f=2 sim 6) QCD at finite temperatures. We point out theoretically and verify numerically that the correlation functions at T/T_c > 1 exhibit the characteristics of the conformal function with IR cutoff, an exponential decay with power correction. Further, we observe our data are consistent with the picture that the N_f=7 case and the N_f=2 at T sim 2 T_c case are close to the meson unparticle model, and we estimate gamma^* = 1.2(1). On the other hand, the N_f=16 case and the N_f=2 at T= 10^2 sim 10^5 Tc cases are close to a free state in the Z(3) twisted vacuum. The results give clues for long standing issues such as slow approach of the free energy to the Stefan-Boltzmann ideal gas limit.

# Conformal Window and Correlation Functions in Lattice Conformal QCD

## Key claim

The conformal window for SU(3) gauge theory with N_f degenerate fundamental fermions is conjectured to be 7 ≤ N_f ≤ 16, based on the reasoning that no confinement phase exists on the massless line for these flavor numbers; within this window, meson propagators on a finite lattice follow a modified Yukawa-type decay form G(t) = c exp(-m_H t)/t^α rather than the usual exponential decay, provided the hadron mass satisfies m_H ≤ c Λ_IR where Λ_IR is the infrared cutoff from finite lattice size.

## Method

The study employs lattice QCD simulations with the one-plaquette gauge action and Wilson fermion action for SU(3) gauge theory with N_f = 7 and N_f = 16 degenerate flavors, using blocked HMC and RHMC algorithms on lattices of sizes 8³×32, 16³×64, and 24³×96. Meson propagators are analyzed through a "local-analysis" method where the propagator is written as G(t) = c exp(-m(t) t)/t^{α(t)} to extract the t-dependent mass and power exponent, and results are compared with N_f = 2 simulations at high temperatures (T/T_c ~ 2, 100, and 10⁵).

## Key numbers

- Conformal window conjectured as 7 ≤ N_f ≤ 16 for SU(3) gauge theory
- For N_f = 7 at β = 6.0 on a 16³×64 lattice, the power exponent α = 0.8(1) from fits in the range t = [15:31] for K = 0.1452 and K = 0.1459
- Anomalous mass dimension γ* estimated as 1.2(1) for N_f = 7, derived from α = 2 - γ* using the plateau value α = 0.8(1)
- Critical constant c estimated as c ~ 2.16 for N_f = 7 and c ~ 1.94 for N_f = 16, using Λ_IR = 2π/(N³ × N_t)^{1/4}
- For N_f = 16 at β = 11.5, the shoulder in α(t) shows a value around 1.5 at t = 12-24 on the 16³×64 lattice
- For N_f = 2 at high temperature, simulations performed at β = 6.5 (T ~ 2 T_c), β = 10.0 (T ~ 100 T_c), and β = 15.0 (T ~ 10⁵ T_c)

## Context

This work extends the author's previous phase structure studies (Iwasaki 2004) that conjectured the conformal window boundaries for SU(3) and SU(2) gauge theories. The concept of "conformal theories with IR cutoff" is introduced as a new framework for understanding lattice simulations, where the finite lattice size provides a natural infrared cutoff Λ_IR. The results connect to the Banks-Zaks (1982) analysis of infrared fixed points and to the Pisarski-Wilczek analysis of chiral phase transition order in N_f = 2 QCD. The correspondence observed between large N_f theories and small N_f theories at high temperature suggests a mapping that could help resolve long-standing issues such as the slow approach of the free energy to the Stefan-Boltzmann ideal gas limit at high temperatures and the order of the chiral phase transition in N_f = 2 QCD.
