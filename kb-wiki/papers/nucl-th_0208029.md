# Lorentz Boosted NN Potential for Few-Body Systems: Application to the three-nucleon bound state

> A Lorentz boosted two-nucleon potential is introduced in the context of equal time relativistic quantum mechanics. The dynamical input for the boosted nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential is based on realistic NN potentials, which by a suitable scaling of the momenta are transformed into NN potentials belonging to a relativistic two-nucleon Schroedinger equation in the c.m. system. This resulting Lorentz boosted potential is consistent with a previously introduced boosted two-body t-matrix. It is applied in relativistic Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound state to calculate the ^3H binding energy. Like in previous calculations the boost effects for the two-body subsystems are repulsive and lower the binding energy.

## Key claim

The relativistic boost effects on the two-nucleon potential, when applied in relativistic Faddeev equations for the three-nucleon bound state, are repulsive and lower the triton binding energy by about 0.29–0.43 MeV for realistic potentials, but the authors note this result depends on the specific functional form of the potential and that the sign of relativistic contributions has been controversial in the literature.

## Method

The authors start from a nonrelativistic NN potential and apply a momentum scale transformation to obtain a phase-equivalent relativistic NN potential that obeys a relativistic two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation in the c.m. system. They then derive an explicit expression for the Lorentz-boosted NN potential (depending on the total two-nucleon momentum) using the bound state wave function and half-shell t-matrices from the c.m. system. This boosted potential is inserted into a relativistic three-body Faddeev equation (Glöckle 1986) to calculate the triton binding energy in a 5-channel partial wave calculation, using the Reid Soft Core, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I/II/93, AV18, and two Yamaguchi potentials.

## Key numbers

- The difference between relativistic and nonrelativistic triton binding energies for realistic potentials spans a range of about 0.29–0.43 MeV (Table 1).
- For the Yamaguchi potentials (purely attractive), the relativistic repulsive effect is weaker, about 0.2 MeV.
- The experimental triton binding energy is 8.48 MeV, while nonrelativistic calculations give values between 8.0 and 7.6 MeV.
- For the Reid Soft Core potential, the boost effect was studied at a total two-nucleon momentum of p = 20 fm⁻¹ to show visible effects; for momenta of about 5 fm⁻¹ the boost effects are much smaller.
- The normalization factor N gives a repulsive contribution in all cases (Table 1, last column).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hamiltonian approach of Glöckle (1986) and Carlson (1993) for three-nucleon bound states, where relativistic effects are found to be repulsive. This contrasts with the field-theoretical Bethe-Salpeter approach of Rupp and Tjon (1979) and Stadler (1997), which finds attractive relativistic corrections. The boosted NN potential developed here enables future relativistic three-nucleon scattering calculations, which are needed to analyze neutron-deuteron scattering data above 100 MeV where nonrelativistic Faddeev calculations with NN forces alone are insufficient (Witala 1999).
