# Magnetic field effect on nuclear matter from skyrmion crystal model

> We explore magnetic field effects on the nuclear matter based on the skrymion crystal approach for the first time. It is found that the magnetic effect plays the role of a catalyzer for the topological phase transition (topological deformation for the skyrmion crystal configuration from the skrymion phase to half-skyrmion phase). Furthermore, we observe that in the presence of the magnetic field, the inhomogeneous chiral condensate persists both in the skyrmion and half-skyrmion phases. Explicitly, as the strength of magnetic field gets larger, the inhomogeneous chiral condensate in the skyrmion phase tends to be drastically localized, while in the half-skyrmion phase the inhomogeneity configuration is hardly affected. It also turns out that a large magnetic effect in a low density region distorts the baryon shape to an elliptic form but the crystal structure is intact. However, in a high density region, the crystal structure is strongly effected by the strong magnetic field. A possible correlation between the chiral inhomogeneity and the deformation of the skrymion configuration is also addressed. The results obtained in this paper might be realized in the deep interior of compact stars.

## Key claim

The magnetic field acts as a catalyzer for the topological phase transition from the skyrmion phase to the half-skyrmion phase in the skyrmion crystal model of nuclear matter, but this conclusion is qualified by the observation that the magnetic effect on the per-baryon energy is only of order (eB)^2 because the linear terms vanish upon integration over the crystal volume due to symmetry.

## Method

The authors incorporated a constant magnetic field along the z-direction into the Skyrme model Lagrangian by replacing ordinary derivatives with gauge covariant derivatives using the symmetric gauge, then discretized the resulting expressions to maintain compatibility with the translational symmetry of the face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal lattice. They parameterized the chiral field U in terms of Fourier series with coefficients determined by minimizing the per-baryon energy for fixed crystal size L and magnetic field strength eB, using the pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV and Skyrme parameter g = 5.93.

## Key numbers

- Per-baryon energy increases monotonically with magnetic field for fixed crystal size L, with no minimum observed (unlike isolated skyrmion case); at low density (large L) the effect is quadratic in eB, while at high density (small L) the magnetic effect becomes negligible because the covariant derivative scales as eB L^2
- The critical crystal size for the topological phase transition (where the order parameter <phi_0> vanishes) shifts to smaller L (higher density) as magnetic field increases; at sqrt(eB)=0 the critical point agrees with Ma (2016)
- At L=2.0 fm (skyrmion phase), the inhomogeneous chiral condensate becomes drastically localized as sqrt(eB) increases from 0 to 800 MeV, while at L=1.0 fm (half-skyrmion phase) the inhomogeneity configuration is hardly affected by the same magnetic field range
- The normalized pion decay constant f_pi*/f_pi increases with magnetic field strength at fixed crystal size, indicating magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach of Klebanov (1985) and Lee (2003) to include magnetic fields for the first time, building on studies of magnetic effects in other effective models such as chiral perturbation theory (Shushpanov, 1997; Andersen, 2012) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Inagaki, 2003; Boomsma, 2009). The results suggest that strong magnetic fields in compact stars could modify the equation of state through the topological phase transition and deformation of baryon shapes, with the inhomogeneous chiral condensate providing a potential indirect observational probe.
