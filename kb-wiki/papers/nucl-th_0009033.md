# Liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear multifragmentation

> The equation of state of nuclear matter suggests that at suitable beam energies the disassembling hot system formed in heavy ion collisions will pass through a liquid-gas coexistence region. Searching for the signatures of the phase transition has been a very important focal point of experimental endeavours in heavy ion collisions, in the last fifteen years. Simultaneously theoretical models have been developed to provide information about the equation of state and reaction mechanisms consistent with the experimental observables. This article is a review of this endeavour.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter, predicted by mean-field theory with a critical temperature of 15.64 MeV, is expected to occur during the disassembly of hot nuclear systems formed in heavy-ion collisions, but the actual experimental signatures are weakened by finite particle number effects, Coulomb interactions, and the fact that mean-field theories overestimate the critical temperature by about 50 percent as seen in the Ising model.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of nuclear matter using the Skyrme parametrization for the equation of state, where the potential energy density is expressed as a function of density with attractive and repulsive terms, and isotherms are calculated using the finite-temperature Fermi-gas model for kinetic energy. They then survey experimental approaches including event selection for central and peripheral collisions using observables such as charged particle multiplicity, transverse kinetic energy, and Zbound, as well as intensity interferometry to extract space-time information about the emitting source.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ with binding energy ≈ 16 MeV/A
- Skyrme parameters used: a = -356.8 MeV, b = 303.9 MeV, σ = 7/6, giving compressibility k = 201 MeV
- Critical temperature from mean-field theory: 15.64 MeV
- Freeze-out density assumed in the model: ρfr = 0.04 fm⁻³, one-quarter of normal nuclear density
- In mean-field Thomas-Fermi theory including Coulomb, the peak in specific heat for ¹⁵⁰Sm is at 10 MeV; without Coulomb it is at 13 MeV
- For the system Kr+Nb, multifragmentation emission time saturates at ≈125 fm/c above 55 MeV per nucleon
- For hadron-induced multifragmentation, saturation time is <100 fm/c

## Context

This review extends earlier theoretical work by Jaqaman et al. on the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter and by Bertsch and Siemens on the expansion dynamics of hot nuclear systems. It surveys experimental results from the mid-1980s onward, including work by Bowman et al. (1991) on Xe+Au collisions showing that fragment multiplicities require expansion to less than 1/3 normal density, and by Bauge et al. (1993) on emission times from Kr+Nb. The paper enables future work by identifying the need for more realistic models that account for finite particle number, Coulomb effects, and collective flow, and by highlighting the open question of how to extract unambiguous phase transition signals from multifragmentation data.
