# Exact spectrum of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model in the thermodynamic limit and finite-size corrections

> The spectrum of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model is exactly derived in the thermodynamic limit by means of a spin coherent states formalism. In the first step, a classical analysis allows one to distinguish between four distinct regions in the parameter space according to the nature of the singularities arising in the classical energy surface; these correspond to spectral critical points. The eigenfunctions are then analyzed more precisely in terms of the associated roots of the Majorana polynomial, leading to exact expressions for the density of states in the thermodynamic limit. Finite-size effects are also analyzed, leading in particular to logarithmic corrections near the singularities occuring in the spectrum. Finally, we also compute expectation values of the spin operators in a semi-classical analysis in order to illustrate some subtle effects occuring in one region of the parameter space.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytic expressions for the density of states of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model in the thermodynamic limit, showing that the full spectrum contains four distinct parameter regions (rather than the two phases known from ground-state analysis) and that singularities in the density of states correspond to saddle points of the classical energy surface, not just to the quantum phase transition point.

## Method

The authors use a spin-coherent-state representation to convert the time-independent Schrodinger equation into a first-order nonlinear Riccati equation for the logarithmic derivative of the Majorana polynomial. They solve this equation in the thermodynamic limit by a 1/s expansion, computing the integrated density of states as a contour integral of the leading-order solution G0 around the branch cut C1, which accumulates the Majorana roots. Finite-size corrections are obtained by analyzing the next order in the 1/s expansion near the singular points.

## Key numbers

- The spectrum lies in the interval -h ≤ ε0 ≤ h for zone I (|γy| < γx < h), with the density of states being a smooth decreasing function of energy.
- Near the singularity at ε0 = -h in zone II, the density of states diverges as ρ0^II(ε0) ~ -log|ε0 + h| / [2π sqrt((γx - h)(h - γy))].
- Near the singularity at ε0 = +h in zone III, the density of states diverges as ρ0^III(ε0) ~ -log|ε0 - h| / [2π sqrt(-(γx + h)(h + γy))].
- The integrated density of states at the singularity ε0 = -h in zone II is given by N0^II(-h) = 1 + (2/(π sqrt(γx γy))) [a- arctan(a-/b+(h)) - a+ arctan(a+/b0(h))], where a± = h ± sqrt(γx γy) and b±, b0 are defined in terms of γx, γy, and h.
- The integrated density of states at the singularity ε0 = +h in zone III is N0^III(h) = (2/(π sqrt(γx γy))) [a+ arctan(a+/b0(-h)) - a- arctan(a-/b-(-h))].

## Context

This work extends the numerical observations of exceptional points by Heiss (2005, 2006) and the classical energy surface analysis by Castanos (2006) by providing exact analytic formulas for the full spectrum of the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model in the thermodynamic limit. The model was originally proposed by Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick (1965) to describe shape phase transitions in nuclei, and the results enable precise computation of finite-size logarithmic corrections near spectral singularities, which are relevant for understanding quench dynamics (Das, 2006) and entanglement properties studied by Vidal (2004, 2007) and others.
