# K^- nuclear potentials from in-medium chirally motivated models

> A self consistent scheme for constructing K^- nuclear optical potentials from subthreshold in-medium Kbar-N s-wave scattering amplitudes is presented and applied to analysis of kaonic atoms data and to calculations of K^- quasibound nuclear states. The amplitudes are taken from a chirally motivated meson-baryon coupled-channel model, both at the Tomozawa-Weinberg leading order and at the next to leading order. Typical kaonic atoms potentials are characterized by a real part -Re V(K^-;chiral)=(85+/-5) MeV at nuclear matter density, in contrast to half this depth obtained in some derivations based on in-medium Kbar-N threshold amplitudes. The moderate agreement with data is much improved by adding complex rho- and rho^2-dependent phenomenological terms, found to be dominated by rho^2 contributions that could represent Kbar-NN -> YN absorption and dispersion, outside the scope of meson-baryon chiral models. Depths of the real potentials are then near 180 MeV. The effects of p-wave interactions are studied and found secondary to those of the dominant s-wave contributions. The in-medium dynamics of the coupled-channel model is discussed and systematic studies of K^- quasibound nuclear states are presented.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that self-consistent treatment of subthreshold in-medium Kbar-N scattering amplitudes, rather than threshold amplitudes, yields K^- nuclear potentials with real depths of approximately 85 MeV at nuclear matter density, contradicting earlier shallow-potential derivations that obtained only half this depth from threshold-based calculations.

## Method
The authors constructed K^- nuclear optical potentials from chirally motivated coupled-channel meson-baryon models at leading order (TW1) and next-to-leading order (CS30), using a self-consistent scheme where the K^- potential appears as an argument in the expressions for the subthreshold Mandelstam variable sqrt(s) and relative momentum p^2. The in-medium scattering amplitudes incorporated Pauli blocking and hadron self energies, and were applied to solve the Klein-Gordon equation for kaonic atoms across 65 data points from Li to U, with phenomenological rho- and rho^2-dependent terms added to improve agreement with data.

## Key numbers
- Real potential depth -Re V_K^- = 85 +/- 5 MeV at nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3 for the chiral models without phenomenological additions
- Leading-order Tomozawa-Weinberg Born approximation gives V_K^- = -57 MeV at rho_0
- Best-fit phenomenological density-dependent potentials yield real depths near 180 MeV at nuclear center
- Chi-squared per point of about 10 for chiral models alone, improving to chi-squared values around 124-140 for 65 data points with added phenomenological terms
- TW1 model parameters: f_pi = 113 +/- 2 MeV, inverse range alpha = 701 +/- 20 MeV
- SIDDHARTA measured K^- hydrogen 1s shift Delta E_1s = 283 +/- 42 eV and width Gamma_1s = 541 +/- 111 eV

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent approach of Lutz (1998) and Cieply et al. (2001) by identifying the subthreshold energy domain required for constructing V_K^-, following earlier insights by Wycech (1971), Bardeen and Torigoe (1972), and Rook (1975) about the dominance of the Lambda(1405) resonance. The results contradict the shallow potentials (40-60 MeV) obtained by Ramos and Oset (2000) from threshold amplitudes, and instead support the deep potentials (150-200 MeV) found in global fits by Friedman, Gal, and collaborators (1993-2007). The work enables systematic calculations of K^- quasibound nuclear states and provides a framework for incorporating multi-nucleon absorption processes beyond the one-nucleon chiral model.
