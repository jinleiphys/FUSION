# Relativistic nuclear structure effects in quasielastic neutrino scattering

> Charged-current cross sections are calculated for quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering using a relativistic meson-nucleon model. We examine how nuclear-structure effects, such as relativistic random-phase-approximation (RPA) corrections and momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies, influence the extraction of the axial form factor of the nucleon. RPA corrections are important only at low-momentum transfers. In contrast, the momentum dependence of the relativistic self-energies changes appreciably the value of the axial-mass parameter, $M_A$, extracted from dipole fits to the axial form factor. Using Brookhaven's experimental neutrino spectrum we estimate the sensitivity of M$_A$ to various relativistic nuclear-structure effects.

## Key claim

The extraction of the axial-mass parameter $M_A$ from quasielastic neutrino scattering data is significantly affected by relativistic nuclear-structure effects, particularly momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies, while random-phase-approximation (RPA) corrections are important only at low momentum transfers below $Q^2 \approx 0.3$ GeV$^2$.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino cross sections using a relativistic meson-nucleon model, starting from a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) ground state and improving it with mean-field theory (MFT) where nucleons acquire an effective mass $M^*$ from scalar and vector mean fields. They incorporated relativistic RPA correlations using a $(\pi + \rho + g')$ residual isovector interaction with a Landau-Migdal parameter $g'$, and also introduced phenomenological momentum-dependent self-energies from optical potentials to replace the constant mean-field values. The cross sections were integrated over the BNL experimental neutrino spectrum to estimate the sensitivity of the extracted $M_A$ value to these nuclear-structure corrections.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass in mean-field theory: $M^* = 638$ MeV at a Fermi momentum $k_F = 225$ MeV, obtained from couplings reproducing nuclear matter saturation properties ($g_s^2(M^2/m_s^2)=267.1$)
- At $Q^2 = 0.2$ GeV$^2$, there is a 6 percent difference between the impulse with $M$ calculation and the $M^*$ and $M_{\bf p}^{*}$ calculations; at $Q^2 = 1.0$ GeV$^2$, this difference grows to 20-30 percent
- The BNL experiment extracted $M_A = 1.09 \pm 0.03$ (stat) $\pm 0.02$ (syst) GeV from antineutrino data in the range $Q^2 = 0.2-1$ GeV$^2$ using a Fermi gas formalism
- At $|{\bf q}| = 0.5$ GeV, the peak positions from $M^*$ and $M_{\bf p}^{*}$ calculations are shifted by less than 50 MeV relative to the Fermi-gas peak
- RPA correlations substantially reduce the cross section at $Q^2 \leq 0.3$ GeV$^2$ for both neutrinos and antineutrinos

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic RPA study of Singh and Oset (1993) by incorporating relativistic effects from quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), specifically the momentum-dependent nucleon self-energies that were not addressed in that earlier calculation. The authors show that while RPA corrections are only important at low momentum transfers (consistent with Singh and Oset's conclusion), the relativistic self-energy effects produce substantial changes to the extracted $M_A$ value at higher $Q^2$, which is critical for interpreting the BNL experiment's extraction of the strange-quark axial form factor $g_A^s$. The results enable more accurate extraction of nucleon form factors from neutrino scattering data and inform the design of future experiments combining parity-violating electron scattering with neutrino measurements to determine strange-quark matrix elements.
