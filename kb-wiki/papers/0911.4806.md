# Hadron Production in Ultra-relativistic Nuclear Collisions: Quarkyonic Matter and a Triple Point in the Phase Diagram of QCD

> We argue that features of hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions, mainly at CERN-SPS energies, may be explained by the existence of three forms of matter: Hadronic Matter, Quarkyonic Matter, and a Quark-Gluon Plasma. We suggest that these meet at a triple point in the QCD phase diagram. Some of the features explained, both qualitatively and semi-quantitatively, include the curve for the decoupling of chemical equilibrium, along with the non-monotonic behavior of strange particle multiplicity ratios at center of mass energies near 10 GeV. If the transition(s) between the three phases are merely crossover(s), the triple point is only approximate.

## Key claim

The paper argues that features of hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions, mainly at CERN-SPS energies, can be explained by the existence of three forms of matter (Hadronic Matter, Quarkyonic Matter, and Quark-Gluon Plasma) that meet at a triple point in the QCD phase diagram, but the authors concede that if the transitions between phases are merely crossovers, the triple point is only approximate rather than exact.

## Method

The authors analyze experimental data from heavy ion collisions across a range of center-of-mass energies, extracting temperature and baryon chemical potential values from Statistical Model fits to hadro-chemical abundances. They compare these freeze-out parameters to predictions from a Hagedorn model for mesonic and baryonic resonance spectra, and to percolation theory for hadronic matter, to identify phase boundaries. The observed non-monotonic behavior of strange particle ratios and the saturation of temperature at around 160 MeV are interpreted as signatures of a triple point where Hadronic Matter, Quarkyonic Matter, and Quark-Gluon Plasma coexist.

## Key numbers

- The triple point is estimated to occur at baryon chemical potential mu_B = 350-400 MeV and temperature T = 150-160 MeV.
- The temperature saturates at an asymptotic value of about 160 MeV for center-of-mass energies near 10 GeV.
- The Hagedorn temperature for mesons is approximately T_H = 190 MeV from the resonance gas model.
- The percolation temperature for mesons is T_p = 180 MeV, assuming hadron radius R_0 = 0.8 fm.
- The baryonic Hagedorn temperature is estimated as T_H^B = 250 MeV from the freeze-out curve.
- The parameter M^0_B, representing the mass scale at which a baryonic Hagedorn spectrum appears, is approximately 1 GeV.
- The limiting baryochemical potential at T=0 from hard core baryon percolation is mu_p = 1.12 GeV.
- The width of the Quarkyonic transition in baryon chemical potential is estimated as delta mu_B = 35 MeV for Lambda_QCD = 200 MeV and k_F = 0.263 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Quarkyonic Matter proposed by McLerran (2007) and Hidaka (2008) to explain experimental data from the CERN-SPS heavy ion program, particularly the non-monotonic behavior of strange particle ratios and the freeze-out curve. The paper builds on Statistical Model analyses by Braun-Munzinger (1994), Cleymans (1999), and Andronic (2008) that extracted temperature and chemical potential from hadron abundances. The proposed triple point provides an alternative interpretation to critical endpoint scenarios (Stephanov) and suggests that future experiments at FAIR and NICA can probe the Quarkyonic phase transition at high baryon density.
