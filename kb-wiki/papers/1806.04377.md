# Hybrid stars from the NJL model with a tensor interaction

> In order to obtain the equation of state and construct hybrid stars, we calculate the thermodynamic potential in the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks. In addition, we impose the beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions on the system. We show that the tensor condensate appears at large chemical potential, however, it is difficult to hold hybrid stars with two-solar mass by using the equation of state with the tensor interaction. Although we cannot obtain the stars with two-solar mass because of the absense of the repulsive interaction, the estimated magnetic moment density is very large. Therefore, we expect that the tensor interaction descrobes the magnetic fields of compact stars.

## Key claim

The tensor condensate appears at large chemical potential in the two-flavor NJL model with tensor interaction, but the resulting equation of state cannot support hybrid stars with two-solar mass because the model lacks a repulsive vector interaction; the authors therefore expect the tensor interaction to describe magnetic fields of compact stars rather than to enable massive hybrid stars.

## Method

The authors calculate the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation for the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks, imposing beta equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. They minimize the renormalized thermodynamic potential with respect to the chiral condensate M and the tensor condensate F, then use the resulting equation of state to construct hybrid stars by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation with a Maxwell construction that matches the quark EOS to the hadronic NL3ωρ model EOS.

## Key numbers

- The three-momentum cutoff parameter Λ = 0.631 GeV and scalar coupling G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2} are used for all models; tensor coupling G_T takes values 0, 11.0, 12.0, 13.0, and 14.0 GeV^{-2} for models GT0, GT11, GT12, GT13, and GT14 respectively.
- For model GT14, the phase transition from chiral condensed phase to tensor condensed phase occurs directly; for GT11, GT12, and GT13, the transition goes through a chiral symmetric phase (M = 0, F = 0) before the tensor condensate appears.
- The tensor condensate F becomes nonzero at baryon number densities above approximately ρ_B ∼ 0.6 fm^{-3}.
- The estimated magnetic moment density reaches M_mag ∼ 10^{19} G for the largest tensor couplings.
- The hybrid star mass-radius curves show bending points at (r/r_0, m/m_sun) ∼ (1.3, 1.3) for the onset of quark matter, and additional bends at (1.2, 1.7) for GT11 and (1.25, 1.5) for GT12 when the tensor condensate appears; model GT13 snaps off at (1.28, 1.2) and GT14 bends at (1.18, 0.7).
- No hybrid stars with two-solar mass are obtained for any model with G_T ≤ 14.0 GeV^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tensor condensates in the NJL model by Tsue et al. (2012, 2013, 2015) and Matsuoka et al. (2016, 2017), which investigated the competition between chiral condensate, tensor condensate, and color superconductivity at finite temperature and chemical potential. The authors apply these results to hybrid star construction, following the approach of Pereira et al. (2016) for the hadronic EOS, and discuss the possibility of twin stars as previously reported by Benic et al. (2015) and Alvarez et al. (2017). The work enables future studies that include vector repulsion to reach two-solar mass stars, extension to flavor SU(3) with strange quarks, and investigation of color superconducting phases at high density.
