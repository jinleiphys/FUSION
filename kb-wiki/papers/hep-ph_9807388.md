# Open Charm, Photon and Dilepton Production in an Increasingly Strongly Interacting Parton Plasma

> We examine the effects of the new equilibration scenario of the increasingly strongly interacting parton plasma and of the non-equilibrium environment have on the production of open charm, photon and dilepton at LHC and at RHIC energies. We show that an out-of-equilibrium effect, not shown before, changes significantly the relative yield of the two main partonic contributions to photon production in the higher $p_T$ range, and higher orders for electromagnetic emissions have increased significance in the new scenario especially at RHIC energies. We argue that the effects of the new scenario are not restricted just to the parton phase but will continue through to the later hadron gas phase with potentially positive implications for the detection of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the new equilibration scenario of the Increasingly Strongly Interacting Parton Plasma (ISIPP), where the strong coupling increases with time due to decreasing momentum transfers from longitudinal cooling and parton creation, significantly modifies particle production yields compared to the standard fixed-coupling scenario, but the effects depend on the production scale and are partially compensated by changes in parton densities and phase duration.

## Method

The author calculated open charm, photon, and dilepton production rates from a parton plasma using time-evolved parton distribution functions from a previous equilibration study, comparing results from the ISIPP scenario (with a time-dependent coupling alpha_s^v) against a standard scenario with fixed alpha_s = 0.3. For open charm, a fixed coupling of 0.3 was used in the production matrix elements because it is a hard process, while for photon and dilepton production the evolving coupling was used since the production scale matches the equilibration scale. The spacetime integration was performed over a cylindrical geometry with radius R_A and proper time tau, centered at zero spatial and particle rapidity.

## Key numbers

- At LHC, the total charm yield from ISIPP is about the same as from the standard plasma, with only a slight reduction visible in the quark-antiquark annihilation contribution
- At RHIC, there is a slight reduction in total charm yield from ISIPP compared to the standard plasma
- For dilepton production, ISIPP yields are down by an approximate factor of 1.6 at LHC and 1.9 at RHIC on average compared to the standard plasma
- At LHC, the out-of-equilibrium effect causes quark-antiquark annihilation to dominate over Compton scattering for photon production at p_T above approximately 1.6 GeV
- At RHIC, Compton scattering remains dominant over quark-antiquark annihilation for photon production up to p_T = 5.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous investigation on the ISIPP equilibration scenario (Wong 1997) and applies it to particle production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at LHC and RHIC energies. The study builds on prior calculations of electromagnetic probes by McLerran and Toimela (1985), Kajantie et al. (1986), and others, as well as open charm studies by Lin and Gyulassy (1995) and Levai et al. (1995). The results show that while the ISIPP scenario reduces dilepton yields, the usefulness of open charm as a probe of initial parton densities is retained, and the out-of-equilibrium effect in photon production could enlarge the window for observing photon emission from deconfined matter.
