# Possible scenario of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the interacting instanton liquid model

> We compute the vacuum energy density as a function of the quark condensate in the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) and examine the pattern of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking from its behavior around the origin. This evaluation is performed by using simulation results of the IILM. We find that chiral symmetry is broken in the U(1)_A anomaly assisted way in the IILM with three-flavor dynamical quarks. We call such a symmetry breaking the anomaly-driven breaking which is one of the scenarios of chiral symmetry breaking proposed in the context of the chiral effective theories. We also find that the instanton-quark interaction included in the IILM plays a crucial role for the anomaly-driven breaking by comparing the full and the quenched IILM calculations.

## Key claim

The authors propose that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) with three-flavor dynamical quarks can occur via an "anomaly-driven" mechanism, where the curvature of the vacuum energy density with respect to the quark condensate at the origin is positive, even though the four-quark coupling is below its critical value. This claim is conditional on the specific definition that the sign of the second derivative of the vacuum energy density at zero quark condensate determines the breaking pattern, rather than model-specific coupling constants.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) with 16 instantons and 16 anti-instantons in a four-dimensional volume, generating 5000 configurations after 1000 initial sweeps for each instanton density. They computed the free energy (vacuum energy density) as a function of instanton density using the thermodynamic integration method, and the quark condensate by averaging the trace of the quark zero-mode propagator over configurations and space-time coordinates. The free energy was then expressed as a function of the quark condensate by combining these two sets of results for three different current quark masses (0.1Λ, 0.15Λ, and 0.2Λ) in the SU(3) flavor symmetric limit.

## Key numbers

- The scale parameter Λ was determined as 373 MeV, 360 MeV, and 351 MeV for current quark masses of 37 MeV, 54 MeV, and 70 MeV, respectively, by fixing the vacuum instanton density to 1 fm⁻⁴.
- The average instanton size in the simulations was 0.35 fm to 0.37 fm, compared to 0.42 fm in prior work by Schafer (1996).
- The free energy at the vacuum instanton density was -149 MeV/fm³, -187 MeV/fm³, and -228 MeV/fm³ for current quark masses of 37 MeV, 54 MeV, and 70 MeV, respectively.
- The cubic root of the quark condensate at the vacuum instanton density was 188 MeV, 196 MeV, and 200 MeV for current quark masses of 37 MeV, 54 MeV, and 70 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the concept of anomaly-driven chiral symmetry breaking, previously proposed by Kono (2021) in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and linear sigma model, to the interacting instanton liquid model (IILM) developed by Schafer and Shuryak (1996, 1998). The authors generalize the definition of anomaly-driven breaking from model-specific coupling constants to the sign of the curvature of the vacuum energy density at zero quark condensate, enabling application beyond chiral effective theories. This enables future investigation of whether the anomaly-driven scenario universally describes chiral symmetry breaking in QCD and whether it predicts a sigma meson mass below 800 MeV, as suggested by Kono (2021).
