# Effect of initial-state nucleon shadowing on the elliptic flow of thermal photons

> Recently the effect of nucleon shadowing on the Monte-Carlo Glauber initial condition was studied and its role on the centrality dependence of elliptic flow ($v_2$) and fluctuations in initial eccentricity for different colliding nuclei were explored. It was found that the results with shadowing effects are closer to the QCD based dynamical model as well as to the experimental data. Inspired by this outcome, in this work we study the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC by incorporating the shadowing effects in deducing the initial energy density profile required to solve the relativistic hydrodynamical equations. We find that the thermal photon spectra remain almost unaltered, however, the elliptic flow of photon is found to be enhanced significantly due to shadowing effects.

## Key claim
The authors find that including nucleon shadowing in the Monte-Carlo Glauber initial condition significantly enhances the elliptic flow of thermal photons (by 32-50% depending on conditions), while leaving the transverse momentum spectra of thermal photons almost unchanged, when the initial conditions are constrained to reproduce the same charged hadron multiplicity as the conventional model.

## Method
The authors used a (2+1)-dimensional inviscid relativistic hydrodynamic model with lattice QCD equation of state to evolve the fireball. Initial entropy density profiles were constructed from Monte-Carlo Glauber models both with (shMCG) and without (MCG) nucleon shadowing, using a weight factor S(n,λ)=exp(-nλ) where λ=0.12 at RHIC and λ=0.08 at LHC. Thermal photon production rates from QGP (using results from Arnold et al.) and hadronic matter (using results from Turbet et al.) were integrated over the space-time evolution to obtain spectra and elliptic flow.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at RHIC (200A GeV, 20-40% centrality, wounded nucleon profile): peak v2 of thermal photons is about 32% larger with shMCG compared to MCG initial condition.
- For Au+Au at RHIC (200A GeV, 20-40% centrality, two-component model with ν=0.14 for MCG and ν=0.31 for shMCG): peak v2 of thermal photons is about 36% larger with shMCG compared to MCG.
- For the QGP contribution alone at RHIC (two-component model, pT ~1.5-2.0 GeV): v2 is about 50% larger with shMCG compared to MCG.
- For the hadronic matter contribution alone at RHIC (two-component model): v2 is about 30% larger with shMCG compared to MCG.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC (2.76A TeV, two-component model): peak v2 of thermal photons is about 50% larger with shMCG compared to MCG.
- For pions at RHIC (pT = 1.74 GeV): v2 is about 29% larger with shMCG compared to MCG.
- Shadowing parameter λ = 0.12 at RHIC and λ = 0.08 at LHC.
- Initial thermalization times: τ0 = 0.17 fm/c at RHIC and τ0 = 0.14 fm/c at LHC.
- Freeze-out temperature: 160 MeV; quark-hadron transition temperature: 170 MeV.
- σ (Gaussian smearing width) = 0.4 fm for both MCG and shMCG.

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon shadowing in Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, previously applied to hadronic observables by the same group (Dasgupta et al., 2016), to thermal photon observables. The conventional MC Glauber model (MCG) has been unable to simultaneously explain the photon spectra and elliptic flow data from PHENIX at RHIC and ALICE at LHC, a discrepancy known as the "direct photon puzzle". The authors show that shadowing effects enhance photon v2 significantly, suggesting that including this effect in event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions (planned for future work) could help resolve the puzzle. The results are compared with earlier hydrodynamic calculations using smooth MCG initial conditions (Chatterjee et al., 2011, 2012) and with event-by-event fluctuating initial condition studies (Chatterjee et al., 2016).
