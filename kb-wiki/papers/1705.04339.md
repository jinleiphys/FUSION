# Dispersion relations for $η'\toηππ$

> We present a dispersive analysis of the decay amplitude for $η'\toηππ$ that is based on the fundamental principles of analyticity and unitarity. In this framework, final-state interactions are fully taken into account. Our dispersive representation relies only on input for the $ππ$ and $πη$ scattering phase shifts. Isospin symmetry allows us to describe both the charged and neutral decay channel in terms of the same function. The dispersion relation contains subtraction constants that cannot be fixed by unitarity. We determine these parameters by a fit to Dalitz-plot data from the VES and BES-III experiments. We study the prediction of a low-energy theorem and compare the dispersive fit to variants of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a dispersive analysis of the decay amplitude for η′ → ηππ that fully accounts for final-state interactions via analyticity and unitarity, but the dispersion relation contains subtraction constants that cannot be fixed by unitarity and must instead be determined by fitting to Dalitz-plot data from the VES and BES-III experiments.

## Method

The authors set up Khuri–Treiman-type dispersion relations for the decay amplitude, decomposing it into single-variable functions of the Mandelstam variables s, t, and u that satisfy unitarity relations with ππ and πη S-wave rescattering. The integral equations are solved numerically by iteration, using as input the ππ S-wave phase shift from Roy-equation analyses and the πη S-wave phase shift from a coupled-channel T-matrix construction. The free subtraction constants are then determined by fitting the resulting amplitude to experimental Dalitz-plot distributions.

## Key numbers

- The ππ S-wave phase shift δ₀⁰(s) is taken from the Roy-solution analysis of Caprini et al. (2011), with low-energy uncertainty shown as a light gray band; the physical region of the decay lies between threshold s = 4Mπ² and s = (Mη′ − Mη)².
- The πη S-wave phase shift δ₀¹(t) is taken from Albaladejo et al. (2015), with a central parameter value δ₁₂ = 107.5° and an uncertainty band generated by varying δ₁₂ in the range 90° ≤ δ₁₂ ≤ 125°.
- The low-energy constants L₂ʳ and L₃ʳ used in the large-Nc χPT input for the inelastic ππ phase are L₂ʳ = 0.63(13) × 10⁻³ and L₃ʳ = −2.63(46) × 10⁻³, taken from the dispersive analysis of Kℓ4 decays by Colangelo et al. (2015).
- The DR₃ representation depends on three subtraction constants α, β, γ; the DR₄ representation depends on four subtraction constants α₀, β₀, γ₀, γ₁.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive Khuri–Treiman framework previously applied to η → 3π (Kambor 1995, Anisovich 1996, Kampf 2011, Lanz 2013, Guo 2015, Colangelo 2016, Albaladejo 2017) and to ω/φ → 3π (Niecknig 2012) to the decay η′ → ηππ. It addresses the long-standing question of whether large-Nc chiral perturbation theory alone can describe this decay, given that final-state interactions play a significant role (Escribano 2010, Riazuddin 1971). The results enable future extraction of πη scattering parameters and predictions for the neutral decay channel η′ → ηπ⁰π⁰, including a cusp effect at the charged-pion threshold predicted by Kubis (2009).
