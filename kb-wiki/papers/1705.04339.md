# Dispersion relations for $╬Ę'\to╬ĘŽĆŽĆ$

> We present a dispersive analysis of the decay amplitude for $╬Ę'\to╬ĘŽĆŽĆ$ that is based on the fundamental principles of analyticity and unitarity. In this framework, final-state interactions are fully taken into account. Our dispersive representation relies only on input for the $ŽĆŽĆ$ and $ŽĆ╬Ę$ scattering phase shifts. Isospin symmetry allows us to describe both the charged and neutral decay channel in terms of the same function. The dispersion relation contains subtraction constants that cannot be fixed by unitarity. We determine these parameters by a fit to Dalitz-plot data from the VES and BES-III experiments. We study the prediction of a low-energy theorem and compare the dispersive fit to variants of chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors set up KhuriŌĆōTreiman-type dispersion relations for the decay amplitude, decomposing it into single-variable functions of the Mandelstam variables s, t, and u that satisfy unitarity relations with ŽĆŽĆ and ŽĆ╬Ę S-wave rescattering. The integral equations are solved numerically by iteration, using as input the ŽĆŽĆ S-wave phase shift from Roy-equation analyses and the ŽĆ╬Ę S-wave phase shift from a coupled-channel T-matrix construction. The free subtraction constants are then determined by fitting the resulting amplitude to experimental Dalitz-plot distributions.
