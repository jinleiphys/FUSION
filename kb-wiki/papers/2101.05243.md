# Novel twist-three transverse-spin sum rule for the proton and related generalized parton distributions

> We derive a new twist-3 partonic sum rule for the transverse spin of the proton, which involves the well-know quark spin structure function $g_T(x)=g_1(x)+g_2(x)$, the less-studied but known transverse gluon polarization density $ΔG_T(x)$, and quark and gluon canonical orbital angular momentum densities associated with transverse polarization. This is the counter part of the sum rule for the longitudinal spin of the proton derived by Jaffe and Manohar previously. We relate the partonic canonical orbital angular momentum densities to a new class of twist-3 generalized parton distribution functions which are potentially measurable in deep-virtual exclusive processes. We also discuss in detail an important technicality related to the transverse polarization in the infinite momentum frame, i.e., separation of intrinsic contributions from the extrinsic ones. We apply our finding to the transverse-space distributions of partons, angular momentum, and magnetic moment, respectively, in a transversely polarized proton.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new twist-3 partonic sum rule for the transverse spin of the proton that involves the quark spin structure function g_T(x) = g_1(x) + g_2(x), the transverse gluon polarization density ΔG_T(x), and quark and gluon canonical orbital angular momentum densities associated with transverse polarization, but note that this sum rule is valid only after separating intrinsic contributions from extrinsic ones related to center-of-mass motion in the infinite momentum frame.

## Method

The authors parameterize a comprehensive set of twist-2 and twist-3 generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for both quarks and gluons, including bilinear and three-field (D-type and F-type) light-cone correlators. They then relate these GPDs to the energy-momentum tensor and angular momentum operators in QCD, following the approach of Ji (2020) to isolate intrinsic angular momentum contributions from those associated with center-of-mass motion. The transverse-spin sum rule is derived by evaluating the transverse angular momentum expectation value in a transversely polarized nucleon state in the infinite momentum frame.

## Key numbers

- The twist-2 sum rule for quark angular momentum is given by J_q = ∫_{-1}^{1} (x/2)(H_q(x,0,0) + E_q(x,0,0)) dx
- There are 28 distinct GPDs catalogued across 6 types of light-cone correlators: 2 twist-2 and 4 twist-3 GPDs for each of the 6 correlator types
- For the twist-3 quark GPDs with Γ = γ^⊥, there are 4 independent parameters: G_{q,1}, G_{q,2}, G_{q,3}, and G_{q,4}
- For the D-type three-field GPDs with α=+, β=⊥, there are 32 independent structures total, with 12 independent functions for the anti-symmetric part

## Context

This work extends the longitudinal spin sum rule derived by Jaffe and Manohar (1990) to the transverse spin case, and builds upon the covariant spin sum rule of Ji (1997) and the recent resolution of controversies surrounding transverse angular momentum by Ji (2020). The new sum rule enables experimental constraints on transverse spin structure through measurements of twist-3 GPDs in deeply virtual exclusive processes at facilities such as Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV program and the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
