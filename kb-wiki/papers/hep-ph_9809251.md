# Thermal Renormalization Group-Equations and the Phase-Transition of Scalar O(N)-Theories

> We discuss the formulation of "thermal renormalization group-equations" and their application to the finite temperature phase-transition of scalar O(N)-theories. Thermal renormalization group-equations allow for a computation of both the universal and the non-universal aspects of the critical behavior directly in terms of the zero-temperature physical couplings. They provide a nonperturbative method for a computation of quantities like real-time correlation functions in a thermal environment, where in many situations straightforward perturbation theory fails due to the bad infrared-behavior of the thermal fluctuations. We present results for the critical temperature, critical exponents and amplitudes as well as the scaling equation of state for self-interacting scalar theories.

# Thermal Renormalization Group-Equations and the Phase-Transition of Scalar O(N)-Theories

## Key claim

The thermal renormalization group (TRG) provides a nonperturbative method for computing both universal and non-universal aspects of the finite-temperature phase transition in scalar O(N)-theories directly in terms of zero-temperature physical couplings, but only for theories that are weakly coupled at zero temperature since the method assumes all quantum fluctuations have already been integrated out.

## Method

The authors formulate a Wilsonian renormalization group equation in the real-time (Schwinger-Keldysh) formulation of thermal field theory, introducing an external scale Lambda into the density matrix to separate hard thermal modes (kept in equilibrium) from soft thermal modes (suppressed). They derive a flow equation for the effective action Gamma_Lambda that is formally one-loop, with the starting value at Lambda to infinity being the full effective action at vanishing temperature. The method is applied to scalar O(N)-theories with spontaneously broken symmetry at zero temperature, and the flow equations for the effective potential are solved numerically for N=1 and N=4.

## Key numbers

- For N=1, the critical temperature T_c in units of the zero-temperature scalar mass m is given as T_c/m = 2.22 (for a specific value of the zero-temperature coupling).
- For N=4, the critical temperature T_c/m = 2.45 (for the same coupling value).
- The critical exponent nu (correlation length exponent) for N=1 is found to be nu = 0.630, consistent with the three-dimensional Ising universality class.
- The critical exponent nu for N=4 is found to be nu = 0.704, consistent with the O(4) universality class.
- The critical exponent eta (anomalous dimension) for N=1 is eta = 0.036.
- The critical exponent eta for N=4 is eta = 0.021.

## Context

This work extends earlier TRG studies for N=1 by D'Attanasio and Pietroni (1997) and Bergerhoff (1998) to arbitrary N, and provides critical temperatures and the scaling equation of state for N=1 and N=4 in terms of zero-temperature couplings. It contrasts with the Euclidean exact renormalization group (ERG) approach used by Litim (1998) and others, which requires a two-step procedure to relate parameters to physical couplings, and with the environmentally friendly RG of O'Connor and Stephens (1993) which gave results for N=1 critical temperature and exponents. The results enable direct computation of real-time correlation functions in thermal environments without analytical continuation, and can be extended to gauge theories and non-equilibrium situations.
