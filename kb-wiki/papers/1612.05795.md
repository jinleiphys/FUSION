# What is the magnetic field distribution for the equation of state of magnetized neutron stars?

> In this Letter, we report a realistic calculation of the magnetic field profile for the equation of state inside strongly magnetized neutron stars. Unlike previous estimates, which are widely used in the literature, we find that magnetic fields increase relatively slowly with increasing baryon chemical potential (or baryon density) of magnetized matter. More precisely, the increase is polynomial instead of exponential, as previously assumed. Through the analysis of several different realistic models for the microscopic description of stellar matter (including hadronic, hybrid and quark models) combined with general relativistic solutions endowed with a poloidal magnetic field obtained by solving Einstein-Maxwell's field equations in a self-consistent way, we generate a phenomenological fit for the magnetic field distribution in the stellar polar direction to be used as input in microscopic calculations.

## Key claim

The magnetic field inside strongly magnetized neutron stars increases polynomially (quadratically) with baryon chemical potential, not exponentially as previously assumed in about one hundred publications. This result is obtained from self-consistent solutions of Einstein-Maxwell equations and is largely model independent across hadronic, hybrid, and quark equations of state.

## Method

The authors combined three different microscopic equation-of-state models (hadronic G-model, hybrid D-model with hyperons and quarks, and quark NJL H-model) with general-relativistic solutions of Einstein-Maxwell field equations assuming a poloidal magnetic field configuration, using the LORENE numerical relativity library. For each model, they computed equilibrium stellar configurations with fixed dipole magnetic moments and extracted the magnetic field profile along the stellar polar direction as a function of baryon chemical potential, then averaged the results to produce a phenomenological quadratic fit.

## Key numbers

- For a 2.2 M_sun baryonic mass star with dipole moment mu = 3e32 Am^2, central magnetic fields range from 8.33e17 G (G-model) to 1.33e18 G (H-model); surface fields range from 4.14e17 G to 4.73e17 G.
- For a 2.2 M_sun star with mu = 1e30 Am^2, central fields range from 4.30e15 G (G-model) to 7.85e15 G (H-model); surface fields range from 1.34e15 G to 1.87e15 G.
- For a 1.6 M_sun baryonic mass star (approximately 1.4 M_sun gravitational) with mu = 2e32 Am^2, central fields range from 5.31e17 G (H-model) to 6.04e17 G (D-model); surface fields range from 1.03e17 G to 2.87e17 G.
- The quadratic fit coefficients for Eq. (1) for a 2.2 M_sun star: a = -7.69e-1 G^2/(A m^2), b = 1.20e-3 G^2/(A m^2 MeV), c = -3.46e-7 G^2/(A m^2 MeV^2).
- The quadratic fit coefficients for a 1.6 M_sun star: a = -1.02 G^2/(A m^2), b = 1.58e-3 G^2/(A m^2 MeV), c = -4.85e-7 G^2/(A m^2 MeV^2).

## Context

This work directly contradicts the widely used exponential magnetic field profile formula first suggested by Bandyopadhyay (1997) and subsequently used in about one hundred publications, including works by Menezes (2008, 2009), Barkovich (2004), Dexheimer (2011), and Rabhi (2009). The authors show that such ad hoc exponential profiles do not fulfill Gauss law, as previously pointed out by Menezes (2016). The provided quadratic fit allows any microscopic equation-of-state model to include a realistic magnetic field profile in the polar direction without solving Einstein-Maxwell equations, enabling future studies of magnetic field effects on stiffness, particle populations, phase transitions, transport properties, and thermal evolution of magnetized neutron stars.
