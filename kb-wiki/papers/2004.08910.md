# Anomalous Lorentz transformation and side jump of a massive fermion

> The side jump in the anomalous Lorentz transformation, arising from the spin-orbit interactions, plays important roles in various intriguing physics, such as chiral vortical effects and spin polarization. In this work, the side jump of the spin-half massive particles, which has rarely been discussed, is visualized and evaluated for the first time. A compact analytical expression describing such side jumps is derived, and found approaching the one describing the chiral fermions in the massless limit. It is further demonstrated that the covariance of the total angular momentum, which would be broken by a normal Lorentz transformation, is restored after the obtained side jumps are taken into account.

## Key claim

The authors derive, for the first time, a compact analytical expression for the side jump (anomalous displacement) of massive spin-half fermions under Lorentz boosts, showing that it reduces to the known massless result in the limit of zero mass and that the covariance of total angular momentum is restored only when this side jump is included.

## Method

The authors construct a Gaussian spinor wave-packet solution to the Dirac equation, polarized in a chosen direction, and compute the expectation value of its spatial coordinate in both the rest frame and a boosted frame. By comparing the world-lines in the two frames, they extract the spatial displacement (side jump) required to connect them via a Poincare transformation. They then verify the result by demanding covariance of the total angular momentum tensor, expressing the side jump in terms of the Pauli-Lubanski vector.

## Key numbers

- Side jump saturates for large boost rapidity (e.g., rapidity = 4 in the visualization), becoming independent of eta for arbitrarily chosen wave-packet amplitude.
- For a Gaussian wave-packet with mass m = 0.5 fm^-1 and smearing width sigma = 1 fm^-1, the side jump is visualized as a shift in the positive y-direction perpendicular to both the spin (z-direction) and boost (x-direction).
- Side jump decreases with mass; under a Gaussian amplitude choice, it is "almost proportional to m^{-1}" when the ratio sigma/m is fixed.
- In the massless limit (m = 0) with finite smearing width sigma, the side jump remains finite.
- Side jump decreases with increasing smearing width sigma.

## Context

This work extends the study of side jumps from massless chiral fermions, previously developed by Chen (2014, 2015), Duval (2014), Stone (2015), and Hidaka (2016), to massive fermions, which had rarely been investigated. The results are essential for realistic transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions involving massive partons and for understanding the local polarization of the Lambda baryon, which contains a massive strange quark. The derived side jump can be implemented in transport models that treat spin as a dynamical degree of freedom, enabling microscopic studies of spin polarization phenomena.
