# Lattice calculations for A=3,4,6,12 nuclei using chiral effective field theory

> We present lattice calculations for the ground state energies of tritium, helium-3, helium-4, lithium-6, and carbon-12 nuclei. Our results were previously summarized in a letter publication. This paper provides full details of the calculations. We include isospin-breaking, Coulomb effects, and interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors present the first lattice calculations for lithium-6 and carbon-12 using chiral effective field theory, including isospin-breaking and Coulomb effects, with interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). However, the calculations use a relatively large spatial lattice spacing of 1.97 fm and a low-cutoff expansion that reduces the NNLO two-pion exchange potential to contact interactions, meaning the results depend on this specific regularization scheme and will require verification at smaller lattice spacings.

## Method

The authors use a Euclidean-time transfer-matrix lattice formalism with spatial lattice spacing a = (100 MeV)^{-1} = 1.97 fm and temporal lattice spacing a_t = (150 MeV)^{-1} = 1.32 fm. They employ an improved leading-order (LO) action treated non-perturbatively, with next-to-leading order (NLO) and NNLO interactions included perturbatively; the NNLO two-pion exchange potential is expanded in powers of q^2/(4m_pi^2) and absorbed into contact interactions due to the low-cutoff scheme. Three-nucleon forces at NNLO (contact, one-pion exchange, and two-pion exchange) are included, along with isospin-breaking one-pion exchange and Coulomb interactions with fitted proton-proton and neutron-neutron contact counterterms. The unknown operator coefficients are fit using low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering data, and ground state energies are computed via auxiliary-field Monte Carlo projection.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing: a = (100 MeV)^{-1} = 1.97 fm
- Temporal lattice spacing: a_t = (150 MeV)^{-1} = 1.32 fm
- Pion mass used: m_pi = m_pi0 = 134.98 MeV
- Nucleon mass: m = 938.92 MeV
- Axial coupling: g_A = 1.29
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.2 MeV
- Low-energy constants from pion-nucleon scattering: c_1 = -0.81 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -4.7 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.4 GeV^{-1}
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Lambda_chi = 700 MeV
- Gaussian smearing parameter for contact interactions: b = 0.6

## Context

This work extends lattice effective field theory calculations from nuclei with A <= 4 (previously studied by Borasoy (2005, 2006) and Epelbaum (2009)) to lithium-6 and carbon-12 for the first time, and also presents the first lattice calculations including isospin-breaking and Coulomb effects. The approach builds on the framework of chiral effective field theory (reviewed by van Kolck (1999), Bedaque (2002), and Epelbaum (2005, 2008)) and the lattice methods reviewed by Lee (2008). The results enable future studies using smaller lattice spacings and higher-order interactions to systematically reduce systematic errors from missing operators, finite volume, and finite Euclidean-time extrapolation.
