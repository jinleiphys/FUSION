# Precise neural network predictions of energies and radii from the no-core shell model

> For light nuclei, ab initio many-body methods such as the no-core shell model are the tools of choice for predictive, high-precision nuclear structure calculations. The applicability and the level of precision of these methods, however, is limited by the model-space truncation that has to be employed to make such computations feasible. We present a universal framework based on artificial neural networks to predict the value of observables for an infinite model-space size based on finite-size no-core shell model data. Expanding upon our previous ansatz of training the neural networks to recognize the observable-specific convergence pattern with data from few-body nuclei, we improve the results obtained for ground-state energies and show a way to handle excitation energies within this framework. Furthermore, we extend the framework to the prediction of converged root-mean-square radii, which are more difficult due to the much less constrained convergence behavior. For all observables robust and statistically significant uncertainties are extracted via the sampling over a large number of network realizations and evaluation data samples.

## Key claim

The authors present a universal artificial neural network framework that, once trained on few-body nuclei (A ≤ 4), can predict converged no-core shell model observables for heavier p-shell nuclei up to ⁹Be, with the key condition that the networks are trained exclusively on data from light systems and then applied as a universal tool without retraining.

## Method

The authors train dense feedforward neural networks on Jacobi-NCSM calculations for ²H, ³H, and ⁴He using chiral NN+3N interactions at N²LO, N³LO, and N⁴LO' with cutoffs of 450, 500, and 550 MeV, both bare and SRG-evolved with flow parameters of 0.02, 0.04, and 0.08 fm⁴. The networks take as input sequences of observable values at three harmonic-oscillator frequencies and four consecutive Nmax values, and output a prediction for the fully converged observable. For evaluation, the authors generate all possible input samples from the evaluation data and evaluate them with 1000 independently trained networks, fitting a Gaussian to the resulting prediction distribution to extract a mean and 1σ uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- Training data consists of 756 converging sequences from Jacobi-NCSM calculations up to Nmax = 50 (²H), 40 (³H), and 24 (⁴He) for seven HO frequencies: 12, 14, 16, 20, 24, 28, and 32 MeV
- Training uses 1,000,000 samples for ABS and DIFF input modes and approximately 350,000 native samples for MINMAX mode
- Evaluation data are limited to Nmax ≤ 12 to emulate the situation in heavier p-shell nuclei
- For ⁴He ground-state energy at Nmaxmax = 8, the MINMAX mode gives predictions very close to the fully converged value, with uncertainties decreasing systematically as Nmaxmax increases
- For ⁴He mass rms radius at Nmaxmax = 8, the MINMAX mode is already extremely accurate, while ABS and DIFF modes give rather inaccurate results with large uncertainties

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Wolfgruber et al., 2023) from ground-state energies to excitation energies and root-mean-square radii, which are more challenging due to non-monotonous convergence patterns not protected by the variational principle. The method contrasts with alternative ANN approaches (Negoita et al., 2019; Jiang et al., 2019) that require retraining for each nucleus, interaction, and observable, and with traditional exponential extrapolation schemes (Roth, 2009; Maris et al., 2009) that work well for ground-state energies but lack widely established methods for radii. The framework enables precise predictions for p-shell nuclei up to ⁹Be using only NCSM data up to Nmax = 12, which would otherwise require computationally prohibitive larger model spaces.
