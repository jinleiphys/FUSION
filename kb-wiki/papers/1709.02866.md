# Time-Dependent Observables in Heavy Ion Collisions I: Setting up the Formalism

> We adapt the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism to study heavy-ion collisions in perturbative QCD. Employing the formalism, we calculate the two-point gluon correlation function $G_{22}^{aμ,bν}$ due to the lowest-order classical gluon fields in the McLerran-Venugopalan model of heavy ion collisions and observe an interesting transition from the classical fields to the quasi-particle picture at later times. Motivated by this observation, we push the formalism to higher orders in the coupling and calculate the contribution to $G_{22}^{aμ,bν}$ coming from the diagrams representing a single rescattering between two of the produced gluons. We assume that the two gluons go on mass shell both before and after the rescattering. The result of our calculation depends on the ordering between the proper time of the rescattering $τ_Z$ and the proper time $τ$ when the gluon distribution is measured. For (i) $τ_Z\gg 1/Q_s$ and $τ-τ_Z\gg 1/Q_s$ (with $Q_s$ the saturation scale) we obtain the same results as from the Boltzmann equation. For (ii) $τ-τ_Z\gg τ_Z\gg 1/Q_s$ we end up with a result very different from kinetic theory and consistent with a picture of "free-streaming" particles. Due to the approximations made, our calculation is too coarse to indicate whether the ordering (i) or (ii) is the correct one: to resolve this controversy, we shall present a detailed diagrammatic calculation of the rescattering correction in the $φ^4$ theory in the second paper of this duplex.

## Key claim

The Schwinger-Keldysh formalism is adapted to heavy-ion collisions in perturbative QCD, and the calculation of a single rescattering between two produced gluons yields results that depend on the ordering of the rescattering proper time τ_Z relative to the measurement proper time τ: for ordering (i) τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s and τ − τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s the result matches the Boltzmann equation, while for ordering (ii) τ − τ_Z ≫ τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s the result is consistent with free-streaming particles and differs from kinetic theory; however, the approximations made are too coarse to determine which ordering is correct.

## Method

The authors adapted the Schwinger-Keldysh (closed-time path) formalism to perturbative QCD in the light-cone gauge A⁺ = 0, doubling the field content into type-1 and type-2 fields. They calculated the two-point gluon correlation function G₂₂^{aμ,bν} at leading order using classical gluon fields from the McLerran-Venugopalan model, then extended the calculation to include a single 2 → 2 rescattering diagram between two produced gluons, assuming both gluons go on mass shell before and after the rescattering. The nuclear wave functions at t = −∞ were modeled as ensembles of valence quarks with the MV model Wigner distribution, and the averaging over initial states reduced to averaging over positions and colors of the valence quarks.

## Key numbers

- The classical-field contribution to G₂₂^{aμ,bν} at O(A^{2/3} g⁶) contains a factor (A/S_⟂)² and a logarithmic enhancement ln(k₁²/Λ²) in the transverse momentum k₁, with Λ the infrared cutoff.
- For ordering (i): τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s and τ − τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s, the diagrammatic result matches the Boltzmann equation.
- For ordering (ii): τ − τ_Z ≫ τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s, the result is consistent with free-streaming particles and very different from the Boltzmann equation.
- The saturation scale Q_s sets the scale for the proper-time conditions: τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s and τ − τ_Z ≫ 1/Q_s.

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, previously used in thermal field theory and for resumming leading ln(1/x) terms in high-energy nuclear physics (Gelis 2008, Jeon 2013), to a perturbative QCD description of heavy-ion collisions from early times onward. It builds on the classical Yang-Mills approach of McLerran and Venugopalan (1993, 1994) and Kovchegov (1996, 1997), and on the Boltzmann-equation studies of Baier (2000) and Kurkela (2015). The paper sets up a formalism to study the transition from classical fields to quasi-particles and to resolve whether the Boltzmann equation or a free-streaming picture applies at late times; the resolution of this ambiguity is deferred to a companion paper using φ⁴ theory.
