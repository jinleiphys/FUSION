# J/psi production in a gluon plasma produced in Au-Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Centrality dependence of J/psi production in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV nucleon-nucleon center of mass energy is studied using the Glauber model plus the kinetic formation model. Initial J/psi production and destruction by incoming nucleons are described by the Glauber model. J/psi-charm equilibration in the gluon plasma is described by the kinetic formation model. We explore the possibility of J/psi suppression suggested by the recent PHENIX data. We show that the J/psi yield monotonically decreases with increasing centrality in the kinetic formation model when the charm mass is smaller than a critical mass. The final J/psi yield is between the value after the Glauber suppression and the dynamical equilibrium value. This underscores the importance of both J/psi production from d-Au collisions, which is essential in determining the Glauber suppression, and high statistics Au-Au data, which can further constrain final state J/psi-charm equilibration.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the kinetic formation model, the J/psi yield in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV monotonically decreases with increasing centrality when the charm quark mass is smaller than a critical mass, contrary to earlier predictions of J/psi enhancement from charm recombination. The final J/psi yield lies between the value after Glauber suppression by incoming nucleons and the dynamical equilibrium value, underscoring that both d-Au data (to fix Glauber suppression) and high-statistics Au-Au data are needed to constrain final-state J/psi-charm equilibration.

## Method

The authors combine the Glauber model for initial J/psi production and destruction by incoming nucleons with a kinetic formation model for J/psi-charm equilibration in a gluon plasma. The plasma evolution is described by the Bjorken model with local temperatures, and the transverse plane is divided into cells (20x40) to account for spatial variation. The charm quark mass is varied between 1.3 GeV and 1.9 GeV, the J/psi-gluon dissociation cross section is varied between 1 mb and 6 mb (constant or dipole form), and the J/psi-nucleon dissociation cross section is set to 0, 4.4 mb, or 7.1 mb.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon rapidity density in central Au-Au collisions: dN_g/dy = 300 (varied to 500 in sensitivity checks)
- Initial charm rapidity density in central collisions: dN_c/dy = 2.5
- Initial J/psi rapidity density before Glauber suppression: dN_J/psi/dy = 0.033, normalized to PHENIX pp cross section at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Charm quark mass varied between 1.3 GeV and 1.9 GeV
- J/psi mass fixed at m_J/psi = 3.1 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 0.15 GeV (varied to 0.17 GeV)
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c
- J/psi-nucleon dissociation cross section sigma_J/psi N varied among 0, 4.4 mb, and 7.1 mb
- J/psi-gluon dissociation cross section sigma_J/psi g varied between 1 mb and 6 mb (constant) or using a dipole cross section with peak about 2 mb
- Typical plasma lifetime for dN_g/dy = 300 and T_f = 0.15 GeV: about 5 fm/c
- Typical initial temperature in central cell: about 500 MeV
- About 10 c-cbar pairs produced on average per Au-Au event, over rapidity range Delta y = 4
- For m_c = 1.9 GeV and sigma_J/psi g = 6 mb, the approximate solution overestimates the exact J/psi yield by about 4%

## Context

This work extends the kinetic formation model of Thews (2001) to Au-Au collisions at RHIC, following the release of PHENIX data (2003) that excluded strong J/psi enhancement. It contrasts with earlier predictions of J/psi enhancement from charm recombination in a Quark-Gluon Plasma, and instead explores the possibility of J/psi suppression. The study uses the Glauber model for nuclear effects, following approaches by Kharzeev (1997) and Gavin (1997) for J/psi-nucleon dissociation, and compares constant and dipole cross section forms for J/psi-gluon dissociation based on perturbative QCD calculations by Peskin (1979) and Bhanot (1979). The results enable future comparisons with d-Au data to fix Glauber suppression and with higher-statistics Au-Au data to constrain J/psi-charm equilibration.
