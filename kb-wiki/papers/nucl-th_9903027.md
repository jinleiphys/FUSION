# Systematic study of the effect of short range correlations on the form factors and densities of s-p and s-d shell nuclei

> Analytical expressions of the one- and two-body terms in the cluster expansion of the charge form factors and densities of the s-p and s-d shell nuclei with N=Z are derived. They depend on the harmonic oscillator parameter b and the parameter $β$ which originates from the Jastrow correlation function. These expressions are used for the systematic study of the effect of short range correlations on the form factors and densities and of the mass dependence of the parameters b and $β$. These parameters have been determined by fit to the experimental charge form factors. The inclusion of the correlations reproduces the experimental charge form factors at the high momentum transfers ($q\geq 2 1/fm$). It is found that while the parameter $β$ is almost constant for the closed shell nuclei, $^4$He, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca, its values are larger (less correlated systems) for the open shell nuclei, indicating a shell effect in the closed shell nuclei.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) improve the reproduction of experimental charge form factors at high momentum transfers (q ≥ 2 fm⁻¹) for s-p and s-d shell nuclei with N=Z, but the correlation parameter β is almost constant for closed shell nuclei (⁴He, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca) while taking larger values (indicating less correlated systems) for open shell nuclei, revealing a shell effect that the abstract mentions but does not fully contextualize as a systematic finding across the entire mass range studied.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the one-body and two-body terms in the cluster expansion of charge form factors and densities using the factor cluster expansion of Clark et al. and Jastrow correlation functions of the form f(r_ij)=1-exp[-β(r_i-r_j)²]. They applied these expressions to s-p and s-d shell nuclei with N=Z using harmonic oscillator wave functions, determining the oscillator parameter b and the correlation parameter β by least-squares fit to experimental charge form factors for each nucleus separately.

## Key numbers

- The correlation parameter β is approximately constant for closed shell nuclei: values for ⁴He, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca are not individually listed in the truncated text, but the paper states β is "almost constant" for these three nuclei
- For the open shell nucleus ³²S, the occupation probability η_{2s} was found to be 0.2945, with corresponding η_{1d} = 0.7411
- For ²⁴Mg, the occupation probability η_{2s} was 0.0355, with η_{1d} = 0.3929
- For ²⁸Si, the occupation probability η_{2s} was 0.0245, with η_{1d} = 0.5951
- The harmonic oscillator parameter b(HO) is systematically larger than b(SRC) for all nuclei studied, with the difference Δb being larger for closed shell nuclei than open shell nuclei
- For ³⁶Ar, the correlation parameter was taken as β = 2.2937 fm⁻² (the mean of ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca values), and the calculated RMS charge radius was ⟨r²⟩^{1/2} = 3.3343 fm, within the experimental error of 3.327 ± 15 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Massen (1988, 1989, 1990) by avoiding the approximation of expanding two-body terms in powers of the correlation parameter that was used for open shell nuclei, instead deriving general expressions applicable to both closed and open shell nuclei. The approach differs from earlier work by Ciofi Degli Atti (1968, 1970), Bohigas and Stringari (1980), and Dal Ri et al. (1982) by using expressions that are functionals of single-particle wave functions rather than relative motion wave functions, making extrapolation to open shell nuclei straightforward. The systematic determination of the mass dependence of both b and β enables predictions for other s-p and s-d shell nuclei where experimental data are limited, such as ³⁶Ar.
