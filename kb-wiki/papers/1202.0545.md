# Conformal symmetry breaking and degeneracy of high-lying unflavored mesons

> We show that though conformal symmetry can be broken by the dilaton, such can happen without breaking the conformal degeneracy patterns in the spectra. We departure from R^1XS^3 slicing of AdS_5 noticing that the inverse radius, R, of S^3 relates to the temperature of the deconfinement phase transition and has to satisfy, \hbar c/R >> Λ_{QCD}. We then focus on the eigenvalue problem of the S^3 conformal Laplacian, given by 1/R^2 (K^2+1), with K^2 standing for the Casimir invariant of the so(4) algebra. Such a spectrum is characterized by a (K+1)^2 fold degeneracy of its levels, with K\in [0,\infty). We then break the conformal S^3 metric as, d\tilde{s}^2=e^{-bχ} ((1+b^2/4) dχ^2 +\sin^2χ(dθ^2 +\sin^2θdφ^2)), and attribute the symmetry breaking scale, b\hbar^2c^2/R^2, to the dilaton. We show that such a metric deformation is equivalent to a breaking of the conformal curvature of S^3 by a term proportional to b\cot χ, and that the perturbed conformal Laplacian is equivalent to (\tilde{K}^2 +c_K), with c_K a representation constant, and \tilde{K}^2 being again an so(4) Casimir invariant, but this time in a representation unitarily inequivalent to the 4D rotational. In effect, the spectra before and after the symmetry breaking are determined each by eigenvalues of a Casimir invariant of an so(4) algebra, a reason for which the degeneracies remain unaltered though the conformal group symmetry breaks at the level of the representation of its algebra. We fit the S^3 radius and the \hbar^2c^2b/R^2 scale to the high-lying excitations in the spectra of the unflavored mesons, and observe the correct tendency of the \hbar c /R=373 MeV value to notably exceed Λ_{QCD}. The size of the symmetry breaking scale is calculated as \hbar c \sqrt{b}/R=673.7 MeV.

# Conformal symmetry breaking and degeneracy of high-lying unflavored mesons

## Key claim
Conformal symmetry can be broken by the dilaton without breaking the conformal degeneracy patterns in the spectra, because the symmetry breaking occurs at the level of the representation of the conformal algebra rather than at the level of the eigenvalue spectrum. The breaking is achieved through a metric deformation of the S^3 hypersphere by an exponential factor, which is equivalent to adding a cotangent potential to the conformal Laplacian.

## Method
The authors start from the R^1 × S^3 slicing of AdS_5 and consider the eigenvalue problem of the conformal Laplacian on S^3, which is given by (K^2 + 1)/R^2 where K^2 is the Casimir invariant of the so(4) algebra. They break the conformal S^3 metric by introducing an exponential deformation factor e^{-bχ} with a symmetry breaking scale bℏ^2c^2/R^2 attributed to the dilaton. They show that the perturbed conformal Laplacian with a cotangent potential -2B cot χ is equivalent to a Casimir invariant of so(4) in a unitarily inequivalent representation, preserving the (K+1)^2-fold degeneracy. The model parameters R and b are fitted to the high-lying unflavored meson spectra from the Crystal Barrel collaboration compilation.

## Key numbers
- ℏc/R = 373 MeV, the inverse S^3 radius, which notably exceeds Λ_QCD
- ℏc√b/R = 673.7 MeV, the calculated size of the symmetry breaking scale attributed to the dilaton
- b = 3.2793 ± 0.0697, the dimensionless parameter from the least square fit
- R values from fits: 0.5157 fm (I=0, J_min=0^-), 0.5294 fm (I=0, J_min=1^-), 0.5444 fm (I=1, J_min=0^-), 0.5269 fm (I=1, J_min=1^-)
- ℏ^2c^2b/R^2 values from fits: 0.4597 GeV^2 (I=0, J_min=0^-), 0.4255 GeV^2 (I=0, J_min=1^-), 0.5228 GeV^2 (I=1, J_min=0^-), 0.4118 GeV^2 (I=1, J_min=1^-)
- Fitted meson masses range from approximately 1349.60 MeV (K=1, I=0, J_min=0^-) to 2280.1 MeV (K=4, I=0, J_min=0^-)

## Context
This work extends the gauge-gravity duality approach to QCD, specifically the AdS_5/CFT correspondence of Maldacena (1998) and Witten (1998), by working on the compactified Minkowski space-time R^1 × S^3 rather than flat Minkowski space. The cotangent potential on S^3, first considered by Schrodinger (1940, 1941), is shown to be related to the Cornell potential from lattice QCD. The authors fit their model to the Crystal Barrel data compilation of Afonin (2007) on high-lying unflavored mesons. This work enables a description of conformal symmetry breaking that preserves spectral degeneracies, with the symmetry breaking scale attributed to the dilaton mass rather than Λ_QCD as commonly used in soft-wall models such as those of Colangelo (2008) and Kelley (2011).
