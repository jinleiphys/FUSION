# Systematics of dynamic moment of inertia in super-deformed bands in Mass ~150 region

> An empirical semi-classical model have been proposed to investigate the nature of dynamic moment-of-inertia , of the super-deformed (SD) bands in nuclei of mass 150 region. The model incorporates an additional frequency dependent distortion, to the dynamic moment-of-inertia term akin to a vibrational component to explain the extreme spin structure of these bands. Using this model two separate components to the dynamic moment of inertia, $\Im^{(2)}$ have been identified for the SD band structure for the mass 150 region. Three distinct nature of the moment-of-inertia, also have been identified using the two parameter model.

# Systematics of dynamic moment of inertia in super-deformed bands in Mass ~150 region

## Key claim

An empirical semi-classical two-parameter model, incorporating a frequency-dependent vibrational distortion term, can reproduce the dynamic moment of inertia (ℑ^(2)) of super-deformed bands in the mass 150 region, but only for bands that exhibit smooth behavior with no sudden alignment; the model identifies three distinct categories of ℑ^(2) behavior based on the magnitude and sign of the coupling between a constant core component and a variational component.

## Method

The author extracted experimental dynamic moment of inertia (ℑ^(2)) values for 25 super-deformed bands in the mass 150 region and performed least-square fitting to a two-parameter function: ℑ^(2) = ℑ_c^(2) ± ℑ_χ^(2) [(ω_max − ω)/ω_max]^2, where ℑ_c^(2) is a constant core component and ℑ_χ^(2) is a variational component describing deviation from a perfect rotor. The coupling sign (positive or negative) and the ratio of ℑ_c^(2) to ℑ_χ^(2) were used to classify the bands into three categories. The model builds on the semi-classical formalism of Swiatecki (1987) for moment of inertia and the two-parameter expression of Wu et al. (1987, 1992) derived from the Bohr Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- ℑ_c^(2) values for all 25 bands show a small spread: 72.3^(+15.2%)_(−9.36%) ℏ^2 MeV^(−1)
- ℑ_χ^(2) values span from 3.7 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1) to 88.7 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1)
- Category I (4 bands: ¹⁴³Eu band 1, ¹⁴³Gd yrast SD band, ¹⁴⁴Gd band 2, ¹⁴⁷Tb yrast SD band): average ℑ_c^(2) = 68.7 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1), average ℑ_χ^(2) = 8.8 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1), with negative coupling
- Category II (4 bands: ¹⁵⁰Gd SD band 12, ¹⁵¹Tb SD band 2, ¹⁵²Dy SD band 1, ¹⁵³Dy SD band 3): average ℑ_c^(2) = 82.98 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1), average ℑ_χ^(2) = 17.1 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1), with positive coupling
- Category III (13 bands): average ℑ_c^(2) = 69.3 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1), average ℑ_χ^(2) = 47 ℏ^2 MeV^(−1), with positive coupling

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical approach of Swiatecki (1987), who identified a stiff core component at high spin, and the two-parameter formalism of Wu et al. (1987, 1992) for describing SD band rotational spectra using the Bohr Hamiltonian. The model provides a systematic classification of ℑ^(2) behavior across 25 SD bands in the mass 150 region, suggesting a weak dependence on nucleonic configurations. The author proposes that the model can be extended to smooth SD bands in other mass regions and to calculate B(E2) transition rates by parametrizing charge asymmetry through the distortion effect.
