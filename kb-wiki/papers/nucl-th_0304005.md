# Color Ferromagnetism in Quark Matter

> We show a possibility that there exists a color ferromagnetic state in quark matter, in which a color magnetic field is spontaneously generated. The state arises between the hadronic state and the color superconducting state when the density of quarks is varied.Although the state (Savvidy state) has been known to involve unstablemodes of gluons, we show that the modes compose a quantum Hall state to stabilize the ferromagnetic state. Such a quantum Hall statecan arise only when quarks matter is present. We also show that the order of the phase transition between the state andthe quark gluon plasma is of the first order.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a color ferromagnetic state, in which a color magnetic field is spontaneously generated, can exist in quark matter between the hadronic and color superconducting phases, but only if the unstable gluon modes (known from the Savvidy state) form a quantum Hall state stabilized by the presence of quark matter.

## Method
The authors use a one-loop effective potential for the color magnetic field in SU(2) gauge theory with two-flavor massless quarks, comparing the energy density of quarks in a constant color magnetic field with that of quarks in the BCS (color superconducting) state. They then employ Chern-Simons gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions to show that the unstable gluon modes in the lowest Landau level can condense into a uniform quantum Hall state, which stabilizes the ferromagnetic phase.

## Key numbers
- The energy density of quarks in the magnetic field is lower than that of free quarks by 26.6%, 5.9%, 2.68%, and 0.28% for N = 1, 2, 3, and 10 respectively, where N = ε_f²/(2e'B)
- The phase boundary between the ferromagnetic and superconducting phases occurs around a chemical potential μ of approximately 500 MeV
- The minimum chemical potential needed for the ferromagnetic phase is approximately 180 MeV × (⟨eB⟩/0.04 GeV²)^(1/3) × (3 fm/L)^(1/3), where L is the radius of the quark matter
- The typical energy scale of the color magnetic field is ⟨eB⟩ ∼ 0.04 GeV²
- The ratio √(eB)/Λ takes values 1.3, 1.7, 1.9, 2.1, and 2.4 for μ/Λ = 1, 5, 10, 20, and 40 respectively
- The critical temperature T_c/Λ for the first-order transition to quark-gluon plasma is 0.8, 1.5, 1.9, 2.1, and 2.4 for μ/Λ = 1, 5, 10, 20, and 40 respectively
- The real magnetic field produced in the color ferromagnetic phase is estimated at 10^14 to 10^15 Gauss

## Context
This work extends the known Savvidy state (Savvidy 1977) and subsequent studies of unstable gluon modes (Nielsen and Olesen 1978) by showing that these modes can be stabilized in dense quark matter through the formation of a quantum Hall state, which had previously been studied in condensed matter systems. The paper connects to the broader program of understanding QCD phase structure, including color superconductivity (recent progress cited as "colors" in the text) and the dual superconductor picture of confinement (cited as "monopole" and "abelian" in the text). The result enables future work on the phase diagram of quark matter at intermediate densities, relevant for neutron star interiors and heavy ion collision experiments.
