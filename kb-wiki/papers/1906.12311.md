# Suppression of elliptic flow without viscosity

> We investigate fluid-to-particle conversion using the usual Cooper-Frye approach but with more general local equilibrium distributions than the Boltzmann or Bose/Fermi distributions typically used. Even though we study ideal fluids (i.e., shear stress and bulk pressure are zero everywhere), we find a suppression of elliptic flow (v2) at high transverse momenta (pT>1.5 GeV/c), relative to results obtained with the traditional Boltzmann distributions. The non-viscous suppression shows qualitatively similar features to the well-known shear viscous suppression of v2; for example, it increases with pT, and it is smaller for heavier species as seen in self-consistent kinetic theory calculations. Our results question whether all of the v2 suppression seen in the data can be attributed to viscous effects, and indicate that shear viscosities extracted from RHIC and LHC elliptic flow data might be overestimated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that using Tsallis distributions (instead of the usual Boltzmann distributions) for fluid-to-particle conversion in ideal hydrodynamics produces a suppression of elliptic flow at high transverse momenta, qualitatively similar to the suppression typically attributed to shear viscosity. This suggests that shear viscosities extracted from RHIC and LHC elliptic flow data might be overestimated, because not all of the observed suppression necessarily comes from viscous effects.

## Method

The authors first illustrate the effect using a simple four-source model with boosted thermal sources, replacing the Boltzmann factor with Tsallis distributions parameterized by an exponent α. They then perform 2+1D ideal hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using the AZHYDRO code with the s95-p1 equation of state, converting fluid to particles on constant-temperature Cooper-Frye hypersurfaces using Tsallis distributions instead of the standard Boltzmann distributions.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow suppression sets in at transverse momenta pT > 1.5 GeV/c (from abstract)
- Source parameters in the four-source model: vx = 0.5, vy = 0.45, T = 140 MeV, which roughly reproduce charged hadron v2 in Au+Au at RHIC at ~30% centrality
- Initial conditions: Bjorken proper time τ0 = 0.6 fm, peak entropy density s0 = 110 fm⁻³, conversion temperature Tconv = 140 MeV
- Three impact parameters simulated: b = 2.5, 7.3, 8.7 fm, corresponding to 0-5%, 20-30%, and 30-40% centrality classes
- Tsallis exponents α ~ 0.03-0.05 give marked improvement in describing power-law tails in pion spectra; α ~ 0.01-0.05 found in earlier blast-wave fits by Tang (2008), while α ~ 0.08 was extracted by Osada (2008) using hydrodynamics
- The non-viscous flow suppression for α ≈ 0.05 corresponds to an effective shear viscosity η/s ≈ 0.05 when compared to shear viscous flow suppression calculations

## Context

This work extends the four-source model of Huovinen (2001) and the Cooper-Frye particlization formalism by replacing Boltzmann distributions with Tsallis distributions, following earlier studies by Osada (2008) and Tang (2008) that focused on particle momentum spectra rather than elliptic flow. The results question the standard interpretation of viscous hydrodynamic studies such as Luzum (2008), which attribute all v2 suppression to shear viscosity. The paper enables future investigations into the interplay between non-Boltzmann equilibrium distributions and viscous dynamics, and suggests that kinetic theory models incorporating non-Boltzmann fixed-point distributions, as discussed by Osada (2008) and Biro and Molnar, could help resolve the ambiguity.
