# Effects of nuclear absorption on the antiLambda/antiproton ratio in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> An enhanced antiLambda/antiproton ratio in heavy-ion relative to p+p collisions has been proposed as one of the signatures for the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) formation. A significantly large (antiLambda+antiSigma0+1.1*antiSigma-)/antiproton ratio of 3.5 has been observed in the mid-rapidity and low transverse momentum region in central Au+Au collisions at the nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 4.9 GeV at the Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS). This is an order of magnitude larger than the values in peripheral Au+Au collisions and p+p collisions at the corresponding energy. By using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, we demonstrate that the observed large ratio can be explained by strong absorption of antiprotons (~99.9%) and antiLambdas (~99%) in dense nuclear matter created in central collisions. We find within the model that the initial antiLambda/antiproton ratio, mainly from string fragmentation, does not depend on the collision centrality, and is consistent with that observed in p+p collisions. This suggests that the observed large (antiLambda+antiSigma0+1.1*antiSigma-)/antiproton ratio at the AGS does not necessarily imply the formation of the QGP. We further study the excitation function of the ratio in UrQMD, which may help in the search and study of the QGP.

## Key claim

The observed large (antiLambda+antiSigma0+1.1*antiSigma-)/antiproton ratio of 3.5 in central Au+Au collisions at AGS energies can be explained by strong nuclear absorption of antiprotons (~99.9%) and antiLambdas (~99%) in dense nuclear matter, without requiring Quark-Gluon Plasma formation.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 4.9 GeV, recording both initial production abundances of antibaryons from string fragmentation and final freeze-out abundances. They compared the freeze-out (antiLambda+antiSigma0+1.1*antiSigma-)/antiproton ratio to experimental data from the E864 Collaboration as a function of impact parameter. The model uses measured antiproton-proton annihilation cross-sections and applies the Additive Quark Model to derive antiLambda-proton annihilation cross-sections, finding an effective reduction factor of approximately 0.67 relative to antiproton-proton annihilation.

## Key numbers

- (antiLambda+antiSigma0+1.1*antiSigma-)/antiproton ratio of 3.5 observed in central Au+Au collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy of 4.9 GeV at mid-rapidity and low transverse momentum
- Approximately 99.9% of antiprotons and 99% of antiLambdas produced at mid-rapidity and low transverse momentum are annihilated in central Au+Au collisions
- The effective antiLambda-proton annihilation cross-section is approximately 0.67 times the antiproton-proton annihilation cross-section at AGS energies
- The initial (antiLambda+antiSigma0+1.1*antiSigma-)/antiproton ratio from UrQMD is approximately 0.46, and the initial antiLambda/antiproton ratio is approximately 0.14, both independent of centrality
- The measured antiproton yield power factor of 0.74 with participant number would become about 1.46 after correcting for absorption

## Context

This work extends the debate on strangeness enhancement as a QGP signature, following earlier proposals by Rafelski, Lee, and Ko that the antiLambda/antiproton ratio should be enhanced in a baryon-dense QGP. The authors use UrQMD, which has no QGP state, to show that the AGS data from E864 and E917 can be explained by nuclear absorption alone, contradicting interpretations that require QGP formation. The results enable future beam energy scan measurements at RHIC to disentangle absorption effects from genuine QGP signatures by studying the excitation function of the ratio from AGS through SPS to RHIC energies.
