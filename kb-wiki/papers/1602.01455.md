# Electromagnetic radiation as a probe of the initial state and of viscous dynamics in relativistic nuclear collisions

> The penetrating nature of electromagnetic signals makes them suitable probes to explore the properties of the strongly-interacting medium created in relativistic nuclear collisions. We examine the effects of the initial conditions and shear relaxation time on the spectra and flow coefficients of electromagnetic probes, using an event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulation (MUSIC).

## Key claim

Electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) emitted from the quark-gluon plasma are sensitive to the shear relaxation time, a transport coefficient that has negligible effect on hadronic observables, but the net sensitivity of the total thermal emission depends on a nontrivial balance between QGP and hadronic contributions that can partially cancel.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions at RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) for 20%-40% centrality. They varied the shear relaxation time parameter b_pi (5, 10, 20) and the initial shear-stress tensor initialization parameter c (0 to 1), then computed thermal photon and dilepton spectra and flow coefficients using viscous-corrected emission rates for both the QGP and hadron gas phases.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio fixed at eta/s = 0.08
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.4 fm; freeze-out temperature T_FO = 145 MeV
- Shear relaxation time parameter b_pi varied as 5, 10, and 20, where tau_pi = b_pi * eta/(epsilon+P)
- Photon elliptic flow increased by more than 50% for p_T > 3 GeV when increasing b_pi from 5 to 20
- The transition between hadron gas and QGP dominance in dilepton invariant mass occurs at M approximately 1.1 GeV
- Two hundred hydrodynamic events were sampled for each parameter set, with impact parameters b_imp = 6.7 to 9.48 fm for 20%-40% centrality

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of electromagnetic probes (Chatterjee 2013, Dion 2011, Shen 2013) that under-predicted photon elliptic flow by factors of 2-4, and builds on recent calculations (vanHees 2014, Paquet 2015) that reduced tension with data. The results demonstrate that electromagnetic probes can complement hadronic probes in constraining non-equilibrium dynamics, particularly the shear relaxation time, which had been shown to have poor sensitivity in hadronic observables (Song 2009, Niemi 2011, Song 2008). The findings enable future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to constrain transport coefficients that are otherwise inaccessible through hadronic observables alone.
