# Neutron stars in the context of $f$($\mathbb{T}$,$\mathcal{T}$) gravity

> In this work, we investigate the existence of neutron stars (NS) in the framework of $f$($\mathbb{T}$,$\mathcal{T}$) gravity, where $\mathbb{T}$ is the torsion tensor and $\mathcal{T}$ is the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. The hydrostatic equilibrium equations are obtained, however, with $p$ and $ρ$ quantities passed on by effective quantities $\bar{p}$ and $\barρ$, whose mass-radius diagrams are obtained using modern equations of state (EoS) of nuclear matter derived from relativistic mean field models and compared with the ones computed by the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations. Substantial changes in the mass-radius profiles of NS are obtained even for small changes in the free parameter of this modified theory. The results indicate that the use of $f$($\mathbb{T}$,$\mathcal{T}$) gravity in the study of NS provides good results for the masses and radii of some important astrophysical objects, as for example, the low-mass X-ray binary (LMXB) NGC 6397 and the pulsar of millisecond PSR J0740+6620. In addition, radii results inferred from the Lead Radius EXperiment (PREX-2) can also be described for certain parameter values.

## Key claim

Neutron star mass-radius relations in $f(\mathbb{T},\mathcal{T})$ gravity can accommodate the PREX-2 radius constraint for the IU-FSU equation of state and nearly all observational constraints for the QMC equation of state, but no single parameter choice satisfies all constraints simultaneously.

## Method

The authors derived the hydrostatic equilibrium equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars in $f(\mathbb{T},\mathcal{T})$ gravity, where the function was chosen as $f(\mathbb{T},\mathcal{T}) = \overline{\omega} \, \mathbb{T}^n \mathcal{T} - 2\Lambda$ with $n=1$ and $\Lambda=0$. They solved the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using two realistic equations of state from relativistic mean field models (IU-FSU and quark-meson coupling QMC) with the BPS crust, varying the coupling parameter $\overline{\omega}$ between $-0.2$ and $0.2$.

## Key numbers

- For the IU-FSU equation of state, the radius of a canonical $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star is considerably affected by $\overline{\omega}$; values $\overline{\omega}=0.08$ and $\overline{\omega}=0.1$ produce mass and radius that agree with the PREX-2 constraint of $13.25 \text{ km} \lesssim R_{1.4} \lesssim 14.26 \text{ km}$.
- For the QMC equation of state, taking $\overline{\omega}=-0.01$ or $\overline{\omega}=-0.02$ yields solutions that accommodate almost all constraints with smaller radius than in general relativity; the PSR J0030+0451 constraint ($11.80 \text{ km} \leq R_{1.4} \leq 13.1 \text{ km}$) can only be described with $\overline{\omega}=-0.2$.
- The maximum mass changes very little with $\overline{\omega}$ for both equations of state; the case $\overline{\omega}=-0.2$ produces mass-radius curves typical of quark stars.
- The $\Lambda$ parameter has no significant effect on the mass-radius profiles and was set to $\Lambda=0$.

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron stars in modified gravity, specifically following the $f(\mathbb{T},\mathcal{T})$ gravity formalism introduced by Harko et al. (2014). It contrasts with previous neutron star studies in $f(R)$ gravity and $f(R,T)$ gravity by using a teleparallel framework where gravity is associated with torsion rather than curvature. The results enable future work testing different functional forms of $f(\mathbb{T},\mathcal{T})$ (e.g., higher powers in $\mathbb{T}$ and new couplings) and additional equations of state, as the current model could not simultaneously satisfy all observational constraints from NICER (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620), PREX-2, and massive pulsar measurements (PSR J1614+2230, PSR J0348+0432).
