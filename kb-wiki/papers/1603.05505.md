# Systematics of oscillatory behavior in hadronic masses and widths

## Key claim
Regular oscillations in hadron masses and widths, fitted by a simple cosine function, are observed when the difference between adjacent masses of each family is plotted versus the mean mass; the author notes that smaller periods than those reported may also reproduce the data, and the figures show the largest possible values.

## Method
The author extracted hadron masses and widths from the Review of Particle Physics (PDG), including data omitted from summary tables. For each hadron family with at least five known masses, the difference between successive masses was plotted against the mean of the two masses, and the resulting distributions were fitted with a cosine function ΔM = α₀ + α₁ cos((M − M₀)/M₁). The same procedure was applied to total widths versus mass for each family.

- Oscillation period for f₀ meson masses: P = 647 MeV (mass 475 MeV)
- Oscillation period for N* baryon masses: P = 146.4 MeV (mass 939 MeV)
- Oscillation period for Δ baryon masses: P = 282.7 MeV (mass 1232 MeV)
- Oscillation period for charmonium (c̄c) masses (all quantum numbers): P = 358 MeV (mass 2981.5 MeV)
- Oscillation period for bottomonium (b̄b) masses (all quantum numbers): P = 408 MeV (mass 9391 MeV)
- Oscillation period for bottomonium (b̄b) masses (0⁻⁺ only): P = 452 MeV (mass 9391 MeV)
- Oscillation period for charmonium widths: P = 308 MeV
- Oscillation period for bottomonium widths: P = 308 MeV
- Predicted next f₀ meson masses: approximately 2670 and 2760 MeV
- Predicted next Ψ meson masses: approximately 4805 and 5080 MeV
- Predicted next Υ meson masses: approximately 11330 and 11560 MeV
- Global period observed in mass oscillation periods versus lower mass: P = 829 MeV
- Global period observed in width oscillation periods versus lower mass: P = 415 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier observations of oscillatory behavior in hadronic properties, building on studies of mass ratios and fractal properties (Tatischeff, previous work) and structures in time-like proton form factors (Bianconi, year not specified). The analysis covers meson families (f₀, f₂, charmed mesons, charmonium, bottomonium) and baryon families (N*, Δ, Λ, Λc, Ξ, Ξc) using PDG data supplemented by LHCb data (Batra, year not specified) and BaBar data (Babar, year not specified). The observed regularities enable tentative predictions of unobserved hadron masses, while the theoretical explanation of the oscillating distributions and their amplitudes remains an open question.
