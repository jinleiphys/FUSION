# Infrared QCD

> We prove that Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is an exact description of infrared QCD deriving it from QCD Lagrangian. The model we obtain is renormalizable and confining but, taking very small momenta fixes completely all the parameters of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model through those of QCD. The choice of the infrared propagator is done consistently with recent numerical results from lattice and Dyson-Schwinger equations for Yang-Mills theory. The model we get coincides, once the ultraviolet contribution is removed, with the one proposed by Langfeld, Kettner and Reinhardt [Nucl. Phys. A {\bf 608}, 331 (1996)].

## Key claim
The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is an exact description of infrared QCD, but only in the limit of very small momenta where the gluon propagator can be approximated by a constant; the derivation holds only for momenta below a natural cutoff of about 527 MeV.

## Method
The author starts from the QCD Lagrangian and uses a small-time approximation to write the gluon field as a convolution of the gluon propagator with the quark current. The gluon propagator is taken as a sum of massive free propagators derived from an exact solution of classical Yang-Mills equations, which produces a mass gap. Substituting this propagator back into the QCD action yields a renormalizable Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that reduces to a contact interaction in the infrared limit.

## Key numbers
- The gluon propagator at zero momentum is G(0) ≈ 3.761402959 / σ, where √σ is the string tension.
- The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio coupling constant is G_NJL ≈ 3.76 × g²/σ.
- The natural cutoff for the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is Λ_NJL = (π / (2 K(i))) √σ ≈ 527 MeV, using √σ = 440 MeV from lattice computations.
- The constant K(i) = ∫₀^{π/2} dθ / √(1 + sin²θ) ≈ 1.3111028777.
- The gluon propagator masses are m_n = (n + 1/2) (π / K(i)) (N g² / 2)^{1/4} μ, with N the number of colors and μ an integration constant.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Langfeld, Kettner and Reinhardt (1996), who proposed a renormalizable Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model based on a plausible gluon propagator; the present paper derives that same model directly from QCD. The gluon propagator used is consistent with lattice computations by Cucchieri and Mendes (2007), Sternbeck et al. (2007), and Ilgenfritz et al. (2007), as well as with numerical solutions of Dyson-Schwinger equations by Alkofer et al. (2007). The result enables systematic computations of nuclear matter properties using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with parameters fixed by QCD, and provides a natural cutoff for the effective theory.
