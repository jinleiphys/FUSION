# Deuteron and Antideuteron Coalescence in Heavy-Ion Collisions: Energy Dependence of the Formation Geometry

> We investigate the collision energy dependence of deuteron and antideuteron emission in the RHIC-BES low- to mid-energy range $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.6-200$ GeV where the formation rate of antinuclei compared to nuclei is strongly suppressed. In the coalescence picture, this can be understood as bulk emission for nuclei in contrast to surface emission for antinuclei. By comparison with experimental data on the coalescence parameter $B_2$, we are able to extract the respective source geometries. This interpretation is further supported by results from the UrQMD transport model, and establishes the following picture: At low energies, nucleons freeze out over the total fireball volume, while antinucleons are annihilated inside the nucleon-rich fireball and can only freeze out on its surface. Towards higher energies, this annihilation effect becomes irrelevant (due to the decreasing baryochemical potential) and the system's freeze-out is driven by the mesons. Thus, the nucleon and antinucleon freeze-out distributions become similar with increasing energy.

# Deuteron and Antideuteron Coalescence in Heavy-Ion Collisions: Energy Dependence of the Formation Geometry

## Key claim
The suppression of antideuteron relative to deuteron production in heavy-ion collisions from sqrt(s_NN) = 4.6-200 GeV can be explained by a spatial coalescence model where nucleons freeze out over the entire fireball volume while antinucleons are annihilated in the baryon-rich interior and can only freeze out on the surface; this surface-suppression effect diminishes at higher collision energies as the baryochemical potential decreases and the fireball becomes pion-dominated.

## Method
The authors employed Mrowczynski's spatial coalescence model, parametrizing the nucleon source function as a Gaussian of width r_0 (volume emission) and the antinucleon source function as a difference of two Gaussians with an additional suppression radius r_* (surface emission). They fitted the coalescence parameter B_2 from experimental data (NA49, STAR) to extract r_0 from deuteron yields and r_* from antideuteron yields, then validated the extracted source geometries by fitting freeze-out coordinate distributions from the UrQMD transport model for central Au+Au collisions at eight beam energies.

## Key numbers
- Energy range studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 4.6-200 GeV (RHIC-BES low to mid energies)
- Maximum fireball radius r_0 = 5.35 fm reached around sqrt(s_NN) = 63 GeV
- Hulthén wave function parameters: alpha = 0.23 fm^{-1}, beta = 1.61 fm^{-1}
- UrQMD analysis performed at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV for central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions
- The r_*/r_0 ratio extracted from data shows a local maximum around sqrt(s_NN) approximately 27 GeV

## Context
This work extends the spatial coalescence approach of Mrowczynski (1993) and earlier work by Nagle (1994, 1996) and Bleicher (1995), who proposed that antinucleon emission is restricted to the fireball surface due to annihilation in the baryon-rich interior. The results are compared with experimental data from NA49 (Ambrosini 1998, VanBuren 1999) and STAR (Armstrong 2000, Adam 2019). The UrQMD validation supports the coalescence model assumptions and enables future investigation of whether the non-monotonous structure in r_*/r_0 near 27 GeV relates to a QCD critical point, pending more low-energy antideuteron statistics.
