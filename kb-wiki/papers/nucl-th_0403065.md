# Chiral symmetry, strangeness and resonances

> We review the important role played by the chiral SU(3) symmetry in predicting the properties of antikaons and hyperon resonances in cold nuclear matter. Objects of crucial importance are the meson-baryon scattering amplitudes obtained within the chiral coupled-channel effective field theory. The formation of baryon resonances as implied by chiral coupled-channel dynamics is discussed. Results for antikaon and hyperon-resonance spectral functions are presented for isospin symmetric and asymmetric matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral SU(3) coupled-channel dynamics, implemented via the \(\chi\)-BS(3) approach with natural subtraction points, can generate baryon resonances such as the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\) without fine-tuning parameters, but the authors hedge that the empirical data set for antikaon-nucleon scattering still leaves room for different theoretical interpretations, with subthreshold scattering amplitudes differing by up to a factor of two between analyses.

## Method

The authors apply the \(\chi\)-BS(3) approach, solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson-baryon scattering using the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction) with a systematic on-shell reduction. The scattering amplitudes are unitarized in both s- and u-channels, and the renormalization condition is fixed by natural subtraction points chosen to protect baryon-octet masses in p-wave amplitudes. Results for antikaon and hyperon-resonance spectral functions in cold nuclear matter are obtained through a self-consistent scheme that includes the feedback of the antikaon spectral function on the antikaon-nucleon scattering process.

## Key numbers

- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 4\pi f \simeq 1.13\) GeV, with \(f \simeq 90\) MeV determined by the pion decay process.
- In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit (meson masses \(m_\pi = m_K = m_\eta = 500\) MeV), the \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\) sector generates 72 bound states forming octet and decuplet representations of SU(3).
- The \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance is generated by coupled-channel dynamics at leading order without parameter adjustment; chiral dynamics predicts that with kaon mass of 300 MeV the \(\Lambda(1405)\) would not exist.
- Speed plots show resonance masses close to empirical values: \(\Xi(1690)\), \(\Lambda(1405)\), \(\Lambda(1670)\), \(N(1535)\) for \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^-\); and \(\Xi(1820)\), \(\Lambda(1520)\), \(\Sigma(1670)\), \(N(1520)\), \(\Delta(1700)\) for \(J^P = \frac{3}{2}^-\).
- A bound state in the \((0,-3)\) channel is predicted at mass 1950 MeV, associated with a decuplet resonance not yet observed.
- The \((1,-1)\) speed plot shows a state at 1920 MeV identified with the three-star \(\Xi(1940)\) resonance.

## Context

This work extends the \(\chi\)-BS(3) approach developed by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2002) and applies it to predict baryon resonance properties and antikaon in-medium spectral functions. It contrasts with phenomenological coupled-channel schemes by Oset and collaborators (2001, 2002) and Jido et al. (2003), which use subtraction scales as free parameters that the authors argue conflict with chiral power counting rules. The results enable future tests with unquenched lattice QCD simulations and provide a foundation for studying antikaon properties in nuclear matter up to twice saturation density, relevant for kaonic atom data and subthreshold antikaon production in heavy ion collisions at GSI.
