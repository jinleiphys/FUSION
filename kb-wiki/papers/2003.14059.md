# Study of $n+{}^3$H, $p+{}^3$He, $p+{}^3$H, and $n+{}^3$He scattering with the HH method

> The $n+{}^3$H, $p+{}^3$He, $p+{}^3$H, and $n+{}^3$He elastic and charge exchange reactions at low energies are studied by means of the hyperspherical harmonic method. The considered nuclear Hamiltonians include modern two- and three-nucleon interactions, in particular results are reported in case of chiral two-nucleon potentials, with and without the inclusion of chiral three-nucleon (3N) interactions. A detailed study of the convergence and numerical stability of the method is presented. We have found that the effect the 3N force is in general tiny except for $p+{}^3$H scattering below the opening of the $n+{}^3$He channel. In such a case, the effect of 3N forces is appreciable and a clear dependence on the cutoff used to regularize the high-momentum tail of the interactions is observed. Such a dependence is related to the presence of the poorly known sharp $0^+$ resonance, considered to be the first excited state of ${}^4$He.

## Key claim

The effect of chiral three-nucleon (3N) forces on low-energy four-nucleon scattering is generally tiny, except for \(p+{}^3\)H scattering below the opening of the \(n+{}^3\)He channel, where the 3N force effect is appreciable and shows a clear dependence on the cutoff used to regularize the high-momentum tail of the interactions, related to the poorly known sharp \(0^+\) resonance considered to be the first excited state of \({}^4\)He.

## Method

The four-body scattering problem was solved using the Kohn variational principle with the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method, expanding the core part of the wave function in HH functions and the hyperradial part in Laguerre polynomials. The nuclear Hamiltonians included chiral two-nucleon (NN) potentials at N3LO and N4LO with cutoffs \(\Lambda=450\), 500, 550, and 600 MeV, with and without chiral 3N interactions at N2LO, as well as the AV18 NN potential combined with Urbana IX (UIX) and Illinois 7 (IL7) 3N potentials. The asymptotic wave functions used regularized Coulomb functions, and the \(S\)-matrix elements were determined variationally for \(n+{}^3\)H, \(p+{}^3\)He, \(p+{}^3\)H, and \(n+{}^3\)He elastic and charge-exchange reactions at low energies.

## Key numbers

- For \(n+{}^3\)H elastic scattering, the total cross section at zero energy and in the peak region (\(E_n\approx 3.5\) MeV) shows sizable disagreement with experimental data when using NN interactions alone; the discrepancy is removed at low energy by including a 3N force fixed to reproduce the triton binding energy, but remains in the peak region.
- For \(p+{}^3\)He elastic scattering, the proton analyzing power \(A_{y0}\) shows a glaring discrepancy between theory and experiment, similar to the \(A_y\) puzzle in \(N+d\) scattering; inclusion of the chiral 3N interaction improves agreement with experimental data, particularly for the proton vector analyzing power.
- For \(p+{}^3\)H and \(n+{}^3\)He below the \(d+d\) threshold, the resonance energy and width of the first excited \(0^+\) state of \({}^4\)He were estimated directly from calculated phase shifts; the transition form factors calculated in prior work by Hiyama (2004) and Bacca (2013, 2014) differ by a factor of two.

## Context

This work extends prior four-nucleon scattering calculations using the Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equations solved in momentum space (DF07, DFS08, DF12, DF14, DF15, DF17) and in configuration space (Cie98, Lea05, Lazaus09, Lazaus12, LC20), as well as resonating group model (RGM) calculations (HH97, PHH01, HH03) and no-core shell model (NCSM) RGM calculations (Sofia08, Sofia10). The HH method results were benchmarked against FY results for \(n+{}^3\)H and \(p+{}^3\)He elastic scattering (bm11) and for \(p+{}^3\)H and \(n+{}^3\)He elastic scattering and charge-exchange reactions (bm16), showing good agreement. The study enables improved predictions for astrophysically important reactions such as \(p+{}^3\)H \(\rightarrow {}^4\)He + \(e^+\) + \(e^-\) and clarifies the role of 3N forces in the poorly understood \(0^+\) resonance of \({}^4\)He.
