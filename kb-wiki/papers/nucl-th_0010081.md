# Nonexistence of a $ηNN$ quasibound state

> We have solved the Faddeev equations for $ηd$ elastic scattering using realistic separable interactions for the $NN$ and coupled $ηN$-$πN$ subsystems. We found that including explicitly the pion channel in the integral equations drastically reduces the attraction that is present in the system. As a consequence, the existence of a $ηNN$ quasibound state is excluded by the modern $ηN$ amplitude analysis.

# Nonexistence of a ηNN quasibound state

## Key claim

The existence of a ηNN quasibound state is excluded by modern ηN amplitude analysis when the pion channel is included explicitly in the Faddeev equations for ηd elastic scattering, because the pion contribution drastically reduces the attraction in the system. The authors note that if the pion contribution were taken with a plus sign instead of the correct minus sign, all six models would give rise to a quasibound state.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations for ηd elastic scattering using realistic separable interactions for the NN and coupled ηN-πN subsystems, with six phenomenological models fitted to recent ηN amplitude analyses. They constructed separable potential models with real and energy-independent strengths, where the imaginary part of the ηN scattering length is generated by coupling to the πN channel, and compared calculations with and without explicit inclusion of the pion channel in the integral equations.

## Key numbers

- The ηN scattering lengths for the six models range from 0.72+i0.26 fm to 1.07+i0.26 fm
- The ηd scattering length from the full calculation with pion contribution ranges from 1.55+i0.49 fm to 3.17+i0.98 fm across the six models
- The ηd scattering length from the full calculation without pion contribution ranges from 2.46+i1.62 fm to 5.02+i3.14 fm
- The effect of separating the propagator equation into two equations produces changes in the ηd scattering length of less than 1%
- Ueda (1991) predicted a ηNN quasibound state with a mass of 2430 MeV and a width of 10-20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous truncated approximation (Garcilazo and Pena, 2000) by including the pion channel explicitly in the Faddeev equations, and contradicts earlier predictions by Ueda (1991) and subsequent calculations by Rakityansky et al. (1996), Green et al. (1996), Shevchenko et al. (1998), and Deloff (2000) which found a ηNN quasibound state depending on the real part of the ηN scattering length. The results enable a definitive conclusion that no ηNN quasibound state exists, and the authors note an unexpected cancellation pattern between π and η rescattering processes previously found in a one-loop calculation for the πd → ηNN reaction by Garcilazo and Pena (2000).
