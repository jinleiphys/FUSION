# Fluctuations and correlations of net baryon number, electric charge and strangeness in a background magnetic field

> We present results on the second-order fluctuations of and correlations among net baryon number, electric charge and strangeness in (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD in the presence of a background magnetic field. Simulations are performed using the tree-level improved gauge action and the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with a fixed scale approach ($a\simeq$ 0.117 fm). The light quark mass is set to be 1/10 of the physical strange quark mass and the corresponding pion mass is about 220 MeV at vanishing magnetic field. Simulations are performed on $32^3\times N_τ$ lattices with 9 values of $N_τ$ varying from 96 to 6 corresponding to temperatures ranging from zero up to 281 MeV. The magnetic field strength $eB$ is simulated with 15 different values up to $\sim$2.5 GeV$^2$ at each nonzero temperature. We find that quadratic fluctuations and correlations do not show any singular behavior at zero temperature in the current window of $eB$ while they develop peaked structures at nonzero temperatures as $eB$ grows. By comparing the electric charge-related fluctuations and correlations with hadron resonance gas model calculations and ideal gas limits we find that the changes in degrees of freedom start at lower temperatures in stronger magnetic fields. Significant effects induced by magnetic fields on the isospin symmetry and ratios of net baryon number and baryon-strangeness correlation to strangeness fluctuation are observed, which could be useful for probing the existence of a magnetic field in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first lattice QCD computation of quadratic fluctuations and correlations of net baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in a background magnetic field, finding that these quantities develop peaked structures at nonzero temperatures as the magnetic field grows, but show no singular behavior at zero temperature for the magnetic field strengths studied (up to approximately 2.5 GeV²).

## Method
The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations using the tree-level improved gauge action and the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action with a fixed scale approach at lattice spacing a ≈ 0.117 fm. They used 32³ × Nτ lattices with 9 values of Nτ from 96 to 6, corresponding to temperatures from near zero up to 281 MeV, and simulated 15 different magnetic field strengths eB up to approximately 2.5 GeV² at each nonzero temperature. The light quark mass was set to 1/10 of the physical strange quark mass, giving a pion mass of about 220 MeV at vanishing magnetic field.

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD studies of conserved charge fluctuations from zero magnetic field (Bazavov et al., 2012; Bazavov et al., 2020) to nonzero magnetic fields, where previous studies were limited to hadron resonance gas models (Fukushima, 2016; Ferreira, 2018; Bhattacharyya, 2015) and Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models (Fu, 2013). The results enable experimental searches for magnetic field signatures in heavy-ion collisions through isospin symmetry breaking effects and ratios of fluctuations, and provide first-principles input for understanding the QCD phase diagram in the T-eB plane, including the possible approach to a critical end point suggested by Endrodi (2015).
