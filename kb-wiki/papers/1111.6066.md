# Simplified solutions of the Cox-Thompson inverse scattering method at fixed energy

> Simplified solutions of the Cox-Thompson inverse quantum scattering method at fixed energy are derived if a finite number of partial waves with only even or odd angular momenta contribute to the scattering process. Based on new formulae various approximate methods are introduced which also prove applicable to the generic scattering events.

# Simplified solutions of the Cox-Thompson inverse scattering method at fixed energy

## Key claim
The authors derive simplified nonlinear equations for the Cox-Thompson inverse scattering method that avoid explicit matrix inversion of a matrix containing the unknown shifted angular momenta, but these simplifications are strictly valid only when the input phase shifts belong exclusively to either even or odd partial waves (as in identical boson or fermion scattering). For generic scattering involving both parities, the simplified equations can only be used as approximations.

## Method
The authors start from the Cox-Thompson (CT) inverse scattering formalism at fixed energy, which expresses the unknown transformation kernel as a sum over an artificial set of shifted angular momenta L. By differentiating the asymptotic expansion functions twice with respect to the radial variable, they obtain a simple periodic solution that allows separation of the even and odd partial wave sectors. This leads to new closed-form equations for the shifted angular momenta that involve only products and trigonometric functions, without requiring the inverse of the unknown-dependent matrix M_cos that appears in the generic CT equations. The authors then test these simplified equations on three examples: an effective Rb-Rb potential from ultracold bosonic collisions, a Gauss potential test case, and neutron-12C scattering at 10 MeV.

## Key numbers
- For the 87Rb + 87Rb system at E = 303 microK: input phase shifts (in rad) for l=0,2,4 are -1.287, 1.635, 0.005 respectively; the difference between original and recalculated phase shifts from the inverse potential is 0.014, 0.042, 0.004 for l=0,2,4
- For the Gauss potential V_G(r) = -2 exp(-5r^2) at E = 18 au (k = 6 au): the shifted angular momenta from the simplified even/odd equations for l=0 through l=10 range from -0.0809 (l=0) to 10.0001 (l=10), compared to the generic CT values from -0.0893 to 10.0001
- For n + 12C scattering at E_n^lab = 10 MeV (E_n^c.m. = 9.23 MeV, k = 0.638 fm^-1): the combined phase shifts (real parts) for l=0 through l=4 are 0.827, -0.562, -0.365, 0.057, 0.021 rad; elasticities range from 0.526 to 0.959; the CT method reproduces input phase shifts with differences Delta_l between 0.014 and 0.040 rad

## Context
This work extends the Cox-Thompson inverse scattering method (Cox 1970) by exploiting the analytical structure of the asymptotic expansion functions to derive simplified equations. The authors note that according to Ramm and Horvath, even or odd partial wave phase shifts alone would determine a unique potential if an infinite subset were used. The simplified equations enable easier solution of the inverse problem for identical boson/fermion scattering, and the authors introduce three approximation schemes (potential addition A, T-set unification, and one-term L approximation) for generic scattering cases. This enables application to experimental phase shift data where solving the full nonlinear CT equations with matrix inversion is numerically difficult, as demonstrated on the n-12C data from Chen and Thornow (2005) and the Rb-Rb data from Schumayer (2008).
