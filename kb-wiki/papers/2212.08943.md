# Consideration of memory of spin and parity in the fissioning compound nucleus by applying the Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay model to photonuclear reactions

> Prompt and $β$-delayed fission observables, such as the average number of prompt and delayed neutrons, the independent and cumulative fission product yields, and the prompt $γ$-ray energy spectra for the photonuclear reactions on $^{235,238}$U and $^{239}$Pu are calculated with the Hauser-Feshbach Fission Fragment Decay (HF$^3$D) model and compared with available experimental data. In the analysis of neutron-induced fission reactions to the case of photo-induced fission, an excellent reproduction of the delayed neutron yields supports a traditional assumption that the photo-fission might be similar to the neutron-induced fission at the same excitation energies regardless of the spin and parity of the fissioning systems.

## Key claim

The paper supports the traditional assumption that photo-fission can be approximated by neutron-induced fission at the same excitation energies, regardless of the spin and parity of the fissioning systems, based on the excellent reproduction of delayed neutron yields without fitting them to experimental data.

## Method

The authors applied the Hauser-Feshbach Fission Fragment Decay (HF\(^3\)D) model, implemented in the BeoH module of the CoH\(_3\) statistical reaction code, to calculate prompt and beta-delayed fission observables for photonuclear reactions on \(^{235,238}\)U and \(^{239}\)Pu. They used model parameters previously obtained for neutron-induced fission, with the total kinetic energy re-adjusted to reproduce the average number of prompt neutrons, and compared the calculated cumulative fission product yields, prompt gamma-ray spectra, and delayed neutron yields with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The total kinetic energy for photo-fission of \(^{235}\)U was parameterized as \(T = 171.5 - 0.10E_\gamma\) MeV, compared to the Viola systematics value of 170.4 MeV.
- The total kinetic energy for photo-fission of \(^{238}\)U was parameterized as \(T = 171.1 - 0.05E_\gamma\) MeV, compared to the Viola systematics value of 169.7 MeV.
- The total kinetic energy for photo-fission of \(^{239}\)Pu was parameterized as \(T = 182.0 - 0.42E_\gamma\) MeV, compared to the Viola systematics value of 176.6 MeV.
- The spin-cutoff scaling parameter \(f_J\) was 2.96 for \(^{235,238}\)U and 1.58 for \(^{239}\)Pu.
- The calculated cumulative fission product yields at \(E_\gamma = 13\) MeV were compared with 37 data points for \(\gamma + ^{235}\)U and 36 for \(\gamma + ^{238}\)U from Krishichayan (2019).
- The calculated average number of delayed neutrons showed notable structure near 11 and 17 MeV due to multi-chance fission effects.

## Context

This work extends the HF\(^3\)D model, previously applied to neutron-induced fission by Okumura (2022) and Lovell (2021), to photo-induced fission on major actinides. It directly compares the spin and parity populations in the compound nucleus formed by neutron-induced reactions (which populate a range of \(J^\Pi\) states) versus photo-induced reactions (which selectively populate \(1^\pm\) states via E1 and M1 transitions). The results enable future studies of whether spin and parity information is transmitted to fission fragments, and support the use of neutron-induced fission parameters for photo-fission calculations in nuclear data evaluations.
