# Comparison of the Near-Threshold Production of eta- and K-Mesons in Proton-Proton Collisions

> The pp -> pp eta and pp -> pLambda K^+ reactions near threshold are dominated by the first and second S_11 resonance respectively. It is shown that a one-pion-exchange model exciting these isobars reproduces well the ratio of the production cross sections. The consequences for this and other channels are discussed.

# Comparison of the Near-Threshold Production of eta- and K-Mesons in Proton-Proton Collisions

## Key claim
A one-pion-exchange model that excites the first S11 resonance (N*(1535)) for eta production and the second S11 resonance (N*(1650)) for K+ production reproduces the ratio of the pp → ppη and pp → pΛK+ cross sections near threshold, provided the overall normalization is fixed by the lower-energy eta data points.

## Method
The authors employed a zero-range one-pion-exchange model where the production operator reduces to a delta-function in the inter-baryon separation vector at high momentum transfers. They parametrized the S-wave pp and Λp systems using Bargmann potentials to obtain the scattering length and effective range parameters, then integrated the non-relativistic three-body phase space weighted with the final-state interaction energy dependence to compute cross sections for both reactions.

## Key numbers
- The squared pion-induced eta production amplitude at threshold: |f_{π0p→ηp}|² = (380 ± 50) μb/sr, deduced from π⁻p data (Binnie).
- The squared pion-induced K production amplitude at threshold: |f_{π0p→K+Λ}|² = (25 ± 5) μb/sr, deduced from π⁻p data (Saxon).
- The first measurement of pp → pΛK+ was reported at Q = 2 MeV (Jan).
- The N*(1535) resonance width is 150 MeV, compared to an excitation energy Q of 20 MeV for eta production.
- The deviation of the model from the pp → ppη data at high Q is about a factor of 1.6, compared to a factor of 30 variation in cross section with energy.
- The predicted production rates with a neutron target (pn → pΛK⁰/nΛK⁺) are likely larger by a factor of 5–10 compared to pp collisions.

## Context
This work extends the one-pion-exchange model previously applied by the authors (Faldt and Wilkin) to pp → ppπ⁰ and pp → ppη, now applied to the newly measured pp → pΛK⁺ reaction (Balewski and Oelert, 1996). The model treats eta and K production as tagging the first and second S11 resonances respectively, with identical production operators and spin-angular momentum algebra. The approach enables predictions for near-threshold ΛK⁺ production on light nuclei, which will be sensitive to the S-wave Λ-nucleus interaction, and suggests that future measurements at Q between 1 and 5 MeV will further test the model.
