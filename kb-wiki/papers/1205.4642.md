# Matrix elements of unstable states

> Using the language of non-relativistic effective Lagrangians, we formulate a systematic framework for the calculation of resonance matrix elements in lattice QCD. The generalization of the Lüscher-Lellouch formula for these matrix elements is derived. We further discuss in detail the procedure of the analytic continuation of the resonance matrix elements into the complex energy plane and investigate the infinite-volume limit.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic framework for calculating resonance matrix elements in lattice QCD by using non-relativistic effective field theory, deriving a generalization of the Lüscher-Lellouch formula that enables extraction of infinite-volume resonance matrix elements from finite-volume lattice data, provided the effective-range expansion is valid up to the resonance energy.

## Method

The authors formulate a covariant non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) in a finite volume, using the Lagrangian to compute two-point and three-point correlation functions in Euclidean space. They derive the finite-volume scattering equation (the Gottlieb-Rummukainen formula) and the finite-volume matrix element expression, then identify the piece whose infinite-volume limit coincides with the analytically continued resonance matrix element defined through the pole of the three-point Green function in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The resonance pole position is defined by \(P^2 = s_R\) on the second Riemann sheet, with \(\text{Re}\,s_R = m_R^2 - \Gamma_R^2/4\) and \(\text{Im}\,s_R = -m_R\Gamma_R\).
- The finite-volume spectrum is determined by \(\det(\delta_{ll'}\delta_{mm'} - \tan\delta_l(s) \mathcal{M}_{lm,l'm'}(s;\mathbf{P})) = 0\), where \(\mathcal{M}_{lm,l'm'}\) involves the Lüscher zeta-function \(Z^{\mathbf{d}}_{lm}(1;s)\) with \(\eta = |\mathbf{p}^*|L/(2\pi)\).
- The finite-volume matrix element for a scalar form factor is \(|\langle E_n(\mathbf{P})|j(0)|0\rangle| = L^{-3/2}|\bar{F}(t)| \frac{p^*|\cos\delta(s_n)|}{(2\pi\sqrt{s}E_n)^{1/2}} \frac{1}{|\delta'(s) + (\phi^{\mathbf{d}}(s_n))'|^{1/2}}\).
- The infinite-volume matrix element satisfies \(|F(t)| = |\bar{F}(t)\cos\delta(s)|\) via Watson's theorem.

## Context

This work extends the Lüscher-Lellouch formalism (Lellouch, 2001) for stable-particle matrix elements to the case of resonances, building on Lüscher's finite-volume framework (Lüscher, 1991) and the Gottlieb-Rummukainen moving-frame formula (Gottlieb, 1996). It enables lattice QCD calculations of resonance form factors (e.g., electromagnetic form factors of the \(\rho\)-meson, \(\Delta\)-resonance, and Roper resonance) at physical quark masses, where resonances become genuinely unstable, by providing a prescription to analytically continue finite-volume matrix elements to the complex energy plane.
