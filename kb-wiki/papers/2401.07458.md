# Weak magnetic effect in quark-gluon plasma and local spin polarization

> We propose the weak magnetic effect, which emerges in quark-gluon plasma close to local thermal equilibrium as the dissipative correction to the quark phase space distribution function, as a novel contribution to the observed Lambda hyperon local spin polarization. With a finite field strength, which is consistent with previous estimate of the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, one is able to explain the experimentally observed Lambda local spin polarization through all centrality classes. Moreover, the weak magnetic effect plays an unambiguous role in the ordering between the second-order and the third-order modulations of the Lambda local spin polarization in experiments.

## Key claim

The weak magnetic effect, a dissipative correction to the quark phase space distribution function in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) close to local thermal equilibrium, is proposed as a novel contribution to Lambda hyperon local spin polarization; with a finite field strength consistent with previous estimates of the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, it can explain the experimentally observed Lambda local spin polarization through all centrality classes, and it plays an unambiguous role in the ordering between the second-order and third-order modulations of the polarization.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation for quarks in QGP using the Chapman-Enskog method with a relaxation time approximation, obtaining a dissipative correction to the quark distribution function linear in the electromagnetic field strength tensor. They then incorporate this correction into the Cooper-Frye formula for spin polarization, replacing the equilibrium Fermi-Dirac distribution with the sum of the equilibrium distribution and the dissipative correction. Finally, they apply this modified formula to 3+1D MUSIC hydrodynamic simulations of isobar collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using a constant magnetic field with a space-time rapidity dependence determined from retarded potential solutions, and compare the resulting second-order and third-order sine modulations of Lambda local spin polarization to STAR collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength considered satisfies |eB| << m_pi^2, with m_pi the pion mass; the time-integrated value is less than or equal to 50 MeV.
- The electrical conductivity is taken as sigma_el/T = 1, within the pQCD expectation range sigma_el/T in [0.2, 2].
- The s-quark mass m_s is set to 0.8 GeV for the main results.
- The impact parameter for RuRu collisions is b = 7 fm.
- The centrality-dependent magnetic field extracted from Lambda local spin polarization data is shown in Fig. 2(b), with error bars stemming from experimental uncertainties and sigma_el/T in [0.2, 2].

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin generation in QGP beyond thermal vorticity, which failed to describe the sign of the local spin polarization (Wu 2019, Fu 2020, Xia 2018, Becattini 2017, STAR 2019, ALICE 2021). It introduces a dissipative effect from a weak magnetic field, distinct from the coupling via a finite magnetic moment of the hyperon (Becattini 2016). The results are compared with shear-induced polarization formulations by Liu and Yin (LY:SIP) and by Becattini, Bucciantini, and Palermo (BBP:SIP). The extracted field strengths are consistent with estimates from direct photon elliptic flow (Sun 2023). The work enables a unified understanding of the sign and the ordering of the second- and third-order modulations of Lambda local spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions.
