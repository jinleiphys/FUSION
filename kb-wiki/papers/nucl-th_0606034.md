# Isospin fractionation : equilibrium versus spinodal decomposition

> This paper focuses on the isospin properties of the asymmetric nuclear-matter liquid-gas phase transition analyzed in a mean-field approach, using Skyrme effective interactions. We compare two different mechanisms of phase separation for low-density matter: equilibrium and spinodal decomposition. The isospin properties of the phases are deduced from the free-energy curvature, which contains information both on the average isospin content and on the system fluctuations. Some implications on experimentally accessible isospin observables are presented.

## Key claim

The paper compares equilibrium (Gibbs) phase separation and out-of-equilibrium spinodal decomposition in asymmetric nuclear matter, finding that both mechanisms produce similar isospin fractionation but spinodal decomposition systematically yields a more pronounced fractionation (the liquid phase becomes closer to symmetric matter) than equilibrium, with the effect depending on the total density relative to the critical density.

## Method

The authors use a mean-field approach with Skyrme effective interactions (SLy230a, SGII, and SIII parameterizations) to compute the constrained entropy surface and free-energy curvature matrix for asymmetric nuclear matter at fixed temperature. They determine the equilibrium phase-separation direction from the concave envelope of the constrained entropy (Gibbs construction) and the spinodal decomposition direction from the eigenvector associated with the most negative eigenvalue of the free-energy curvature matrix, then compare the slopes δρ₃/δρ (degree of fractionation) as functions of system asymmetry ρ₃/ρ at different total densities.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric matter: T_c = 14.54 MeV (SLy230a), 14.46 MeV (SGII), 17.96 MeV (SIII)
- Phase diagram and fractionation slopes are computed at T = 10 MeV for all three parameterizations
- The slope δρ₃/δρ (fractionation measure) is compared at three total densities: below, at, and above the respective critical density ρ_c for each Skyrme force
- At the critical points, both equilibrium and instability directions become tangent to the coexistence and spinodal borders

## Context

This work extends the study of the nuclear-matter liquid-gas phase transition from symmetric matter (Das, Physics Reports) to asymmetric matter, specifically addressing the isospin fractionation phenomenon previously discussed by Xu and by Muller-Serot. The paper directly compares equilibrium phase separation (Gibbs construction, as used in neutron-star crust modeling by Glendenning) with spinodal decomposition (relevant for heavy-ion collisions and supernova cores, as reviewed by Chomaz, Physics Reports). The results enable qualitative estimates of how isospin fractionation may affect the extraction of the symmetry-energy coefficient C_sym from isoscaling data (Tsang, Ono, Shetty), suggesting that using the source proton fraction instead of the fragment proton fraction in the isoscaling formula may introduce systematic deviations.
