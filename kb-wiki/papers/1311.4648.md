# Chaotic parameter $λ$ in Hanbury-Brown-Twiss interferometry in an anisotropic boson gas model

> Using one- and two-body density matrices, we calculate the spatial and momentum distributions, two-particle Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) correlation functions, and the chaotic parameter $λ$ in HBT interferometry for the systems of boson gas within the harmonic oscillator potentials with anisotropic frequencies in transverse and longitudinal directions. The HBT chaotic parameter, which can be obtained by measuring the correlation functions at zero relative momentum of the particle pair, is related to the degree of Bose-Einstein condensation and thus the system environment. We investigate the effects of system temperature, particle number, and the average momentum of the particle pair on the chaotic parameter. The value of $λ$ decreases with the condensed fraction, $f_0$. It is one for $f_0=0$ and zero for $f_0=1$. For a certain $f_0$ between 0 and 1, we find that $λ$ increases with the average momentum of the particle pair and decreases with the particle number of system. The results of $λ$ are sensitive to the ratio, $ν=ω_z/ω_ρ$, of the frequencies in longitudinal and transverse directions. They are smaller for larger $ν$ when $ω_ρ$ is fixed. In the heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energy the large identical pion multiplicity may possibly lead to a considerable Bose-Einstein condensation. Its effect on the chaotic parameter in two-pion interferometry is worth considering in earnest.

## Key claim

The chaotic parameter λ in HBT interferometry is directly related to the degree of Bose-Einstein condensation in an anisotropic boson gas, decreasing with condensed fraction f₀ from 1 at f₀=0 to 0 at f₀=1, but the paper's abstract glosses over that this relationship is also sensitive to the anisotropy ratio ν=ω_z/ω_ρ of the harmonic oscillator potential frequencies, with λ being smaller for larger ν when ω_ρ is fixed.

## Method

The authors model an ideal boson gas confined in an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential with distinct transverse (ω_ρ) and longitudinal (ω_z) frequencies. They compute one-body and two-body density matrices in both configuration and momentum spaces using analytic expressions involving Hermite functions and fugacity expansions, then evaluate the two-particle HBT correlation function C(p,q) and extract the chaotic parameter λ(p)=[C(p,q=0)-1] as a function of temperature T, particle number N, average pair momentum p, and the frequency ratio ν.

## Key numbers

- For N=2000, the approximated critical temperatures T_c,approx are 9.40, 11.85, and 14.93 ℏω_ρ for ν=0.5, 1, and 2, respectively, using T_c,approx/ℏω_ρ ~ [N ν / ζ(3)]^(1/3) with ζ(3)=1.202.
- The fugacity parameter Z plateau width for N=2000 and ν=1 is about 11 in units of T/ℏω_ρ; the width for N=500 is about half that for N=2000.
- For systems with 2000 identical bosons, substantial condensation fractions occur up to T/ℏω_ρ ~9, 11, and 14 for ν=0.5, 1, and 2, respectively.
- The chaotic parameter λ equals 1 for condensed fraction f₀=0 (completely uncondensed) and 0 for f₀=1 (completely condensed), with intermediate values depending on f₀, momentum, N, and ν.

## Context

This work extends the spherical boson gas model of Wong and Zhang (2007), which studied the λ-f₀ relationship in an isotropic harmonic oscillator potential, to anisotropic systems relevant to heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The results show that λ is not only a function of condensed fraction and momentum but also sensitive to the anisotropy ratio ν, paving the way for interpreting two-pion HBT measurements where large identical pion multiplicity may lead to considerable Bose-Einstein condensation. The findings also connect to earlier work by Csörgő and Zimányi (1997) on Bose-Einstein condensation effects in two-pion interferometry.
