# Polarization Observables for Two-Pion Production off the Nucleon

> We develop polarization observables for the processes $γN\toππN$ and $πN\toππN$, using both a helicity and hybrid helicity-transversity basis. Such observables are crucial if processes that produce final states consisting of a spin-1/2 baryon and two pseudoscalar mesons are to be fully exploited for baryon spectroscopy. We derive relationships among the observables, as well as inequalities that they must satisfy. We also discuss the observables that must be measured in `complete' experiments, and briefly examine the prospects for measurement of some of these observables in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general formalism for polarization observables in two-pion photoproduction and pion-induced production off nucleons, going beyond the quasi-two-body approximations used in prior work. The authors derive that at least seven judiciously chosen measurements at each kinematic point are required for a "complete" experiment to extract the underlying amplitudes, though they note this number does not necessarily represent a minimal set.

## Method

The authors construct the most general 2x2 matrix amplitude for the processes γN→ππN and πN→ππN in terms of Pauli spinors, then express the observables in both helicity and hybrid helicity-transversity bases. They derive the 16 possible polarization observables for each process, establish relationships among them using the absolute magnitudes and phases of transversity amplitudes, and obtain inequalities that these observables must satisfy. The formalism is developed for the three-body final state (spin-1/2 baryon plus two pseudoscalar mesons) using five kinematic variables: s, t, sππ, and the solid angle of the dipion system in its rest frame.

## Context

This work extends the well-documented polarization formalism for single-pion production (πN→πN and γN→πN) to the more complex case of two-pion final states, which have been identified as crucial for finding "missing" baryon resonances (as discussed by the "missing resonances" community). Prior treatments by various authors (e.g., the Δπ or Nρ quasi-two-body approaches) had treated the Nππ final state as arising from intermediate resonances like Δ or ρ, but the authors argue this is insufficient for modern high-precision data from facilities like JLab. The formalism enables complete amplitude extraction for baryon spectroscopy from two-pion production data, analogous to what has been achieved for single-pion photoproduction as documented by Barker, Donnachie, and Storrow.
