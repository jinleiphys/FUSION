# Observing Spontaneous Strong Parity Violation in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We discuss the problem of observing spontaneous parity and CP violation in collision systems. We discuss and propose observables which may be used in heavy-ion collisions to observe such violations, as well as event-by-event methods to analyze the data. Finally, we discuss simple monte-carlo models of these CP violating effects which we have used to develop our techniques and from which we derive rough estimates of sensitivities to signals which may be seen at RHIC.

# Observing Spontaneous Strong Parity Violation in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Spontaneous parity and CP violation in hot hadronic matter, as proposed by Kharzeev et al., could produce metastable regions with nonzero chromoelectric-chromomagnetic field alignment in relativistic heavy-ion collisions; however, because the handedness of such regions changes randomly event-by-event, the average value of any parity-odd observable over many events must be zero, and only the widths of event-by-event distributions can reveal the violation.

## Method

The authors developed three simple Monte Carlo models (Models I, II, and III) that embed CP-violating "bubbles" with aligned electric and magnetic fields into heavy-ion collision events generated by HIJING or RHIC::EVENT. They simulated the momentum-space effects of these fields on charged pions and on baryons (via constituent quarks), then tested several parity- and CP-odd observables (including a tensor observable T_zz, a pseudoscalar J_c·k_-, and K_twist) using event-by-event analysis methods such as mixed-event reference distributions and the subevent method. The simulations assumed an acceptance of -1 to +1 in rapidity and 0.120 to 1 GeV/c in transverse momentum, similar to the STAR detector at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Nominal field strength chosen to give a 30 MeV/c momentum kick to a relativistic quark traversing the CP-violating region (field strengths expressed in units where this value equals one)
- Bubble radius set to 1/5 or 2/5 of the collision region radius, chosen so that fields affect approximately 100 pions per event, as suggested in Kharzeev and Pionic (2000)
- For Model I with nominal parameters, approximately a few × 10^5 central RHIC Au+Au events required for a 3σ signal using the mixed-event method with T_zz
- For strong evidence using the subevent method, a minimum of a few × 10^7 central RHIC events needed
- Signal strength in the subevent method scales as field strength to the fourth power
- Model II (spherically symmetric Boltzmann distribution with temperature 70 MeV and non-uniform longitudinal field) predicts considerably better sensitivity than Model I
- Model III (Landau expansion with transverse temperature 150 MeV and flat rapidity distribution over four units) gives results similar to Model I

## Context

This work extends the theoretical proposal by Kharzeev et al. (1998) that metastable CP-violating states could form in hot hadronic matter, and the subsequent suggestions for experimental signatures in heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev and Pionic (2000) and by Gyulassy and RBRC (2000). The paper addresses practical difficulties with the originally proposed J observable (which is forced nearly to zero by symmetry constraints in identical-particle collisions) and introduces alternative observables including T_zz, J_c·k_-, and K_twist. The results enable experimental searches at RHIC by providing quantitative sensitivity estimates and comparing analysis methods, though the authors caution that the transition from theory to their models requires several assumptions and that changing these assumptions could yield quite different event-number requirements.
