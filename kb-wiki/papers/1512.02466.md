# Comment on "Structure of the two-neutrino double-β decay matrix elements within perturbation theory" by Dušan Štefánik, Fedor Šimkovic, Amand Faessler, arXiv:1506.00835 [nucl-th], Phys.Rev.C91 (2015) 6, 064311

> We comment on a priority claim given by the authors of Phys.Rev.C91 (2015) 6, 064311.

# Comment on "Structure of the two-neutrino double-β decay matrix elements within perturbation theory"

## Key claim
The authors of Phys.Rev.C91 (2015) 6, 064311 made a false claim of priority by stating that double Fermi and Gamow-Teller sum rules associated with ΔZ = 2 nuclei were introduced in their own prior work (Štefánik et al., 2013), whereas these sum rules were actually first introduced 15 years earlier in Rumyantsev & Urin (1998).

## Method
The comment identifies that the sum rules in question first appeared in Eq.(5) of Rumyantsev & Urin (1998), where a model-independent identity transformation of the 2νββ decay amplitude M^(2ν) was introduced. This transformation partitions M^(2ν) into two terms sensitive to different parts of the nuclear Hamiltonian, with one term proportional to the sum rule. The authors note that the original 1998 work only considered a mean-field contribution and incorrectly concluded the sum rules must vanish, a shortcoming corrected in follow-up papers by Rodin et al. (2005, 2011) that evaluated the dominating particle-particle sector contribution.

## Key numbers
- The sum rules were first introduced 15 years prior to the publication of Štefánik et al. (2013), specifically in Rumyantsev & Urin (1998).
- The sum rules appear in Eq.(5) of Rumyantsev & Urin (1998), with explicit representations given in Eqs.(27),(28) of Štefánik et al. (2015).

## Context
This comment corrects a priority claim made by Štefánik et al. (2015) regarding the origin of double Fermi and Gamow-Teller sum rules for double-beta decay. The sum rules were originally introduced by Rumyantsev & Urin (1998), with subsequent work by Rodin et al. (2005, 2011) correcting the earlier conclusion that the sum rules must vanish and demonstrating the dominant role of the particle-particle interaction. The authors note that Štefánik et al. (2013, 2015) cited Rodin et al. (2005, 2011) in their references and one of their co-authors was also a co-author of those earlier papers, indicating awareness of the prior work.
