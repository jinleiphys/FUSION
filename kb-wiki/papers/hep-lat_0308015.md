# Lattice calculations of hadron properties

> Recent lattice studies of hadron properties, in particular of exotic states and charmonia are reviewed. Sea quark and quark mass effects are discussed as well as decays and mixing.

## Key claim

This review argues that lattice QCD simulations are approaching the boundary between qualitative tests of principle and quantitative predictions for exotic hadrons, charmonia, and mixing phenomena, but systematic uncertainties from the quenched approximation (neglecting sea quarks) and finite computational resources still limit precision, particularly for light scalar mesons and decay widths.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of hadron properties, focusing on glueballs, charmonia, hybrids, and light scalar mesons. It compares quenched (qQCD) and unquenched (n_f=2) simulations using various fermion discretizations (Wilson, clover, staggered) and anisotropic lattices, with scale setting via the Sommer parameter r_0 or spin-averaged 1S charmonium states. Results are compiled from multiple collaborations (CP-PACS, Columbia, UKQCD, SESAM, GF11, RBC, QCD-TARO) and compared to experimental data and potential model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Lightest glueball is scalar with mass between 1.4 and 1.8 GeV (quenched, scale uncertainty ~10%)
- Lightest spin-exotic glueball is 2^{+-} around 4 GeV
- Quenched charmonium spin-averaged level splittings have systematic errors up to 10% from omitting sea quarks; fine structure undershoots real world by up to 40%
- η_c' mass from Belle: 3630(8) MeV
- Lightest exotic hybrid (1^{-+}) mass: 1.9(2) GeV for light quarks, with strange quark exotic about 200 MeV heavier
- Quenched a_0 mass from Bardeen et al.: 1.33(5) GeV in units of ρ mass, about 200±100 MeV lighter than the glueball
- RBC results: light quark a_0 mass 1.04(7) GeV, I=0 f_0/σ mass 0.9(1) GeV
- Glueball-scalar mixing energies: ~300 MeV (GF11, qQCD) and ~500 MeV (UKQCD, n_f=2) on coarse lattices; continuum extrapolation by GF11 gives 61(45) MeV
- D_{sJ}^+(2317) lies about 40 MeV below DK threshold
- D_0^* mass from Belle: 2308(17)(15)(28) MeV; D_1' mass: 2427(26)(20)(15) MeV, both with widths ~300 MeV
- σ (f_0(600)) pole: real part 800(400) MeV, imaginary part 450(150) MeV

## Context

This review extends earlier lattice reviews (McNeile 2003, Michael 2003) and the author's own review (Bali 2003) on baryon spectrum and structure. It addresses the long-standing challenge of connecting lattice QCD to experimental spectroscopy of exotic states, particularly the scalar glueball mixing puzzle (Amsler and Close 1995, Close and Kirk 2001, Burakovsky 1998, Lee 1999) and the nature of the D_{sJ}^+(2317) discovered by BaBar (2003). The work enables future quantitative comparisons with experiments such as GlueX at JLAB and PANDA at GSI, and highlights the need for improved unquenched simulations at lighter quark masses and multiple lattice spacings to resolve the scalar meson puzzle and hybrid meson spectroscopy.
