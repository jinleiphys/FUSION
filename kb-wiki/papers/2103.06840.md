# Enstrophy without boost symmetry

> We construct an approximately conserved current for $2+1$ dimensional, Aristotelian (non boost invariant), fluid flow. When Aristotelian symmetry is enhanced to Galilean symmetry, this current matches the enstrophy current responsible for the inverse cascade in incompressible fluids. Other enhancements of Aristotelian symmetry discussed in this work include Lorentzian, Carrollian and Lifshitz scale symmetry.

## Key claim

The authors construct an approximately conserved enstrophy current for 2+1 dimensional Aristotelian (non-boost-invariant) fluid flow, but the conservation holds only under specific constraints on the equation of state or in the generalized incompressible limit; the current reduces to the known enstrophy current only when Aristotelian symmetry is enhanced to Galilean symmetry.

## Method

The authors identify a sufficient condition for enstrophy conservation: the existence of a closed two-form Ω that is orthogonal to the fluid velocity u^μ under the equations of motion. They solve this condition for Aristotelian fluids by constraining the equation of state, using an ansatz Ω = d(f v̄) + d(g n) where v̄ is the velocity one-form and n is the time-direction one-form, and requiring that Ω_μν v^ν = 0 under the equations of motion (E:EOM1). They also analyze the incompressible (low Mach number) limit as an alternative route that does not require a particular equation of state.

## Key numbers

- The enstrophy current is constructed in 2+1 spacetime dimensions (d=2), where the term proportional to Θ(1-2/d)Ω^2 in equation (E:OnuO) vanishes identically.
- For the Galilean invariant limit, the kinematic mass density ρ is related to the particle number density n via ρ = m n, where m is the mass.
- The solution for branch A yields f = ∂_{v^2}H/(sξ) and g = (v^2/(sξ))∂_{v^2}H - (1/2)∂_{sξ}H, with G = H + sη, where ξ = ξ(ñ) and η = η(ñ) are arbitrary functions.
- For the Galilean invariant solution, the pressure takes the barotropic form P(n) = -H_G(n) + n H_G'(n).

## Context

This work extends the study of enstrophy conservation from well-understood contexts (incompressible flow, barotropic flow, and relativistic fluids studied by Kraichnan (1967), Carrasco (2012), Westernacher-Schneider (2015, 2017), and Marjieh (2020)) to Aristotelian fluids with minimal symmetry. The authors build on the framework of Aristotelian hydrodynamics developed by deBoer (2017, 2020), Novak (2019), and Armas (2020), who showed that Lorentz, Galilean, Carrollian, and Lifshitz invariant fluids can all be obtained from Aristotelian fluids with enhanced symmetry. This work enables the study of enstrophy conservation in systems with varying degrees of symmetry, including potential connections to flocking dynamics as studied by Toner and Tu (1998).
