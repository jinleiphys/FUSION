# Exactly Soluble Model for Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition

> Thermodynamical properties of nuclear matter undergoing multifragmentation are studied within a simplified version of the statistical model. An exact analytical solution has been found for the grand canonical ensemble. Excluded volume effects are taken into account in the thermodynamically self-consistent way. In thermodynamic limit the model exhibits a first order liquid-gas phase transition with specific mixed phase properties. An extension of the model including the Fisher's term is also studied. The possibility of the second order phase transition at or above the critical point is demonstrated. The fragment mass distributions in the different regions of the phase diagram are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution of a simplified statistical multifragmentation model in the grand canonical ensemble, demonstrating that the model exhibits a first-order liquid-gas phase transition in the thermodynamic limit, but only when the surface tension parameter sigma(T) is positive (i.e., below T_c = 18 MeV) or when the Fisher exponent tau exceeds 5/2.

## Method

The authors formulate a simplified version of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) with excluded volume effects treated in a thermodynamically self-consistent van der Waals approximation. They introduce the isobaric partition function via Laplace transformation of the grand canonical partition function, which reduces the problem to analyzing the singularities of this function. The farthest-right singularity of the isobaric partition function determines the system pressure in the thermodynamic limit V -> infinity, with a simple pole corresponding to the gaseous phase and an essential singularity corresponding to the liquid phase (an infinite-mass fragment).

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: rho_o = 0.16 fm^{-3}, used as the inverse of the excluded volume parameter b = 1/rho_o
- Volume binding energy per nucleon: W_o = 16 MeV
- Fermi-gas level density parameter: epsilon_o = 16 MeV
- Surface tension parameter: sigma_o = 18 MeV
- Critical temperature for tau = 0: T_c = 18 MeV, at which sigma(T) vanishes
- Pressure isotherms shown for T = 4, 8, 10, 14, 18, and 22 MeV for tau = 0 (Fig. 3)
- For tau = 0, the critical point occurs at T = T_c = 18 MeV, rho = rho_o, with formally infinite pressure
- For tau > 5/2, the liquid-gas phase transition extends to all temperatures; for 5/2 < tau < 7/2, the transition becomes second order above T_c, with a tricritical point at T = T_c, rho = rho_o

## Context

This work extends the numerical canonical ensemble studies of the simplified SMM by Gulminelli and collaborators (1998, 1999) by providing an exact analytical solution in the thermodynamic limit using the grand canonical ensemble. The method of isobaric partition function singularities, previously applied to similar problems by Gorenstein (1981), allows the authors to rigorously establish the existence and nature of the liquid-gas phase transition. The results enable future extensions incorporating finite-size effects, Coulomb interactions, and symmetry energy to make the model more realistic for experimental comparisons.
