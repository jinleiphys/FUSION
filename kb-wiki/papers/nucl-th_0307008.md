# Mass Dependence of Disappearance of Transverse Flow

> A complete theoretical study is presented for the disappearance of flow, for the first time, by analyzing 15 reactions with masses between 47 and 476 units. We demonstrate that the effect of nucleon-nucleon cross-section reduces to insignificant level for heavier colliding nuclei in agreement with previous studies. A stiff equation of state with nucleon-nucleon cross-sections of 35-40 mb is able to explain all the measured balance energies within few percent. A power law is also given for the mass dependence of the disappearance of flow which is in excellent agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a stiff equation of state combined with a constant nucleon-nucleon cross-section of 35–40 mb can explain all measured balance energies within a few percent, but this agreement is conditional on the mass of the system: for heavier colliding nuclei, the effect of the nucleon-nucleon cross-section reduces to an insignificant level.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model to simulate 15 heavy-ion reactions with combined masses between 47 and 476 units, covering systems from 20Ne+27Al to 197Au+197Au. They employed a stiff equation of state (C=380 MeV) and varied the nucleon-nucleon cross-section (20, 40, 55 mb, plus energy-dependent and medium-dependent variants) to extract the balance energy (Ebal) for each system. The balance energy was determined by interpolating the incident energy at which the directed transverse momentum ⟨p_x^dir⟩ passes through zero.

## Key numbers

- The experimental balance energy for 197Au+197Au is 42±3±1 MeV/nucleon, while for 20Ne+27Al it is 111±10 MeV/nucleon.
- For 20Ne+27Al, reducing the cross-section from 55 mb to 20 mb changes Ebal from 89 MeV/nucleon to 244 MeV/nucleon (a change of 155 MeV/nucleon); for 197Au+197Au, the same reduction changes Ebal from 38 MeV/nucleon to 59 MeV/nucleon (a change of 21 MeV/nucleon).
- The power-law exponent τ for the mass dependence of Ebal from experimental data is τ_expt = -0.42079 ± 0.04594; with σ=40 mb the model gives τ_40 = -0.41540 ± 0.08166, and with σ=35 mb gives τ_35 = -0.43037 ± 0.08558.
- The power-law exponent for the charge dependence of Ebal from experimental data is τ = -0.46703 ± 0.04745; for heavier nuclei only, τ = -0.59316 ± 0.0622.

## Context

This work extends earlier mass-dependence studies of balance energy, which had considered only up to six systems (Westmeier et al. 1993; Magestro et al. 2000, 2001; Buta et al. 1995; Zhou et al. 1994), by analyzing 15 reactions across a wider mass range (47 to 476). The authors find that the power-law exponent τ = -0.42 differs from the earlier reported τ ≈ -1/3 for masses ≤200 (Westmeier et al. 1993) and τ = -0.46±0.06 for masses ≤400 (Magestro et al. 2001). The results enable a unified description of balance energy across different experimental setups and confirm that a constant cross-section of 35–40 mb with a stiff equation of state best reproduces the data, while also showing that for heavy systems the balance energy becomes nearly independent of the cross-section.
