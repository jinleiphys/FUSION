# Phenomenological analysis of associated production of $Z^0+b$ in the $b \rightarrow J/ψX$ decay channel at the LHC

> The ATLAS collaboration recently reported on the first observation of associated-production of a $Z^0$ boson with a $J/ψ$. We recently claimed that the corresponding yield of the {\it prompt} $J/ψ$ was dominated by double parton scatterings in the ATLAS acceptance with a somewhat small value of $σ_{\rm eff}$. We also found out that single parton scatterings were only dominant at large transverse momenta. We present here the first phenomenological analysis of another part of the ATLAS data sample, namely of a $Z^0$ boson plus a {\it non-prompt} $J/ψ$. Our study is performed at next-to-leading order in $α_s$ and includes parton-shower effects via the {\sc\small MadGraph5_aMC@NLO} framework. We find out that the data, unlike the case of prompt $J/ψ+Z^0$, do not hint at significant DPS contributions. Owing to the current experimental and theoretical uncertainties, there is still a room for these but with a lower limit of $σ_{\rm eff}$ close to 5 mb. We stress the importance of QCD corrections to account for the ATLAS data.

## Key claim
The authors find that, unlike the case of prompt J/psi + Z production, the ATLAS data for non-prompt J/psi + Z production do not show significant double parton scattering contributions; however, given current experimental and theoretical uncertainties, a double parton scattering contribution with a lower limit on sigma_eff close to 5 mb remains possible.

## Method
The authors performed next-to-leading order QCD calculations for pp -> Z + b + bbar + X in the 3-initial-quark-flavour scheme using the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework, matched to parton showers via the MC@NLO approach with Pythia 8.1. They compared their single parton scattering predictions to ATLAS data on Z + non-prompt J/psi production at sqrt(s) = 8 TeV, and extracted limits on the double parton scattering effective cross section sigma_eff using the pocket formula.

## Key numbers
- NLO SPS cross section for non-prompt J/psi + Z in ATLAS fiducial region: 1760 +240.9/-220.8 fb at sqrt(s) = 8 TeV
- NLO SPS cross section for non-prompt J/psi + Z in ATLAS inclusive region: 2922 +392.9/-361.1 fb at sqrt(s) = 8 TeV
- Experimental cross section ratio npR(J/psi+Z) in ATLAS fiducial region: (65.8 +/- 9.2 +/- 4.2) x 10^{-7}
- NLO SPS prediction for npR(J/psi+Z) in ATLAS fiducial region: (64.6 +8.8/-8.1) x 10^{-7}
- Lower limit on sigma_eff at 68% confidence level: 5.0 mb
- Lower limit on sigma_eff at 95% confidence level: 2.3 mb
- NLO QCD corrections increase the SPS yield by a factor of 1.46 relative to LO

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of prompt J/psi + Z production (Lansberg and Shao, 2016) to the non-prompt channel, filling a gap in the literature where no theory comparison existed for the ATLAS measurement (Aad et al., 2014). The study enables improved understanding of double parton scattering in processes involving heavy flavour, complementing prior extractions of sigma_eff from quarkonium pair production by D0 (Abazov et al., 2014, 2015) and from jet production. The results are relevant for controlling backgrounds to Higgs production via Z + H and for probing bottom-quark Yukawa coupling measurements.
