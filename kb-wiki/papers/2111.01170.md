# Positivity and renormalization of parton densities

> There have been recent debates about whether $\overline{\text{MS}}$ parton densities exactly obey positivity bounds (including the Soffer bound), and whether the bounds should be applied as a constraint on global fits to parton densities and on nonperturbative calculations. A recent paper (JHEP ${\bf \text{11}}$ (2020) 129) appears to provide a proof of positivity in contradiction with earlier work by other authors. We examine their derivation and find that its primary failure is in the apparently uncontroversial statement that bare pdfs are always positive. We show that under the conditions used in the derivation, that statement fails. We provide some elementary calculations in a model QFT that show how this situation can generically arise in reality. In addition, we observe that the methods used in the derivation are in common with much, but not all, other work where factorization is derived. Our examination pinpoints considerable difficulties with these methods that render them either wrong or highly problematic. The issue of positivity highlights that these methods can lead to wrong results of phenomenological importance. From our analysis we identify the restricted situations in which positivity can be violated.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the claim by Candido, Forte and Hekhorn (2020) that MSbar parton densities are always positive is false, because the proof relies on the incorrect assertion that bare parton distribution functions (pdfs) are always positive under the conditions used; the authors show that positivity can fail when dimensional regularization is used simultaneously for both ultraviolet and collinear divergences, because the state-space metric need not remain positive after analytic continuation between different spacetime dimensions.

## Method

The authors examine the derivation of positivity in Candido, Forte and Hekhorn (2020), identifying its reliance on what they call "track B" factorization, where a hadronic structure function is asserted to equal the convolution of an unsubtracted massless partonic structure function with a bare pdf. They contrast this with "track A" factorization, where pdfs are defined via light-cone operator matrix elements with explicit UV renormalization. The authors provide explicit calculations in a Yukawa field theory model to demonstrate how MSbar pdfs can become negative, and they analyze the divergence structure of both approaches to show where the positivity argument fails.

## Key numbers

- The renormalization factor Z^A in the MSbar scheme has the form: Z_{ij}^A(ξ) = δ(1-ξ)δ_{ij} + Σ_{n=1}^∞ C_{n,ij}(ξ,α_s) (S_ε/ε)^n, where S_ε ≡ (4π)^ε/Γ(1-ε) and spacetime dimension is n=4-2ε
- For massless partonic targets in dimensional regularization, the bare track-A pdf equals δ(ξ-1)δ_{ij} (exact to all orders, with no finite part)
- The renormalized pdf on a massless partonic target equals Z^A_{i/j} exactly (massless, dimensional regularization)
- In heavy quark treatments, the MSbar heavy quark pdf contains a factor ln(μ/m_h) and becomes negative when the MSbar scale μ is somewhat less than the heavy quark mass m_h
- A low initial scale example cited is μ_0 = 0.78 GeV from Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations (Bednar, 2018)

## Context

This paper extends the long-standing debate about whether MSbar parton densities obey positivity bounds, including the Soffer bound (Soffer, 1994). It directly contradicts the proof by Candido, Forte and Hekhorn (2020) that positivity is automatic, and supports earlier cautionary statements by Diehl (2019) and the fitting approach of Martin (2009) that allow for slightly negative pdfs at low scales. The work distinguishes between two historical approaches to factorization, "track A" originating from operator-product expansion methods (Wilson, 1969; Zimmermann, 1972) and "track B" from early parton model generalizations (Politzer, 1977; Ellis, 1978; Curci, 1980), and argues that track B methods can produce wrong results of phenomenological importance. The analysis enables more informed decisions about whether to impose positivity constraints in global fits at low initial scales and in nonperturbative calculations such as lattice QCD (Ji, 2013; Constantinou, 2020) and Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches.
