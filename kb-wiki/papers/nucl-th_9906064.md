# Omega- and phi-meson production in p n -> d M reactions near threshold and OZI-rule violation

> We investigate the reactions p n -> d omega and p n -> d phi close to the corresponding thresholds. The S-wave amplitudes are calculated within the framework of the two-step model which is described by a triangle graph with pi, rho and omega mesons in the intermediate state. The cross sections of the reactions p n -> d omega and p n -> d phi are predicted to be significantly larger than the cross sections of the corresponding reactions p p -> p p omega and p p -> p p phi at the same values of the c.m. excess energy Q. The ratio of the yields of phi to omega is found to be (30 +/- 7) x 10^-3.

## Key claim

The cross sections of the reactions p n -> d omega and p n -> d phi near threshold are predicted to be significantly larger than the corresponding p p -> p p omega and p p -> p p phi reactions at the same center-of-mass excess energy Q, with the phi-to-omega yield ratio found to be (30 +/- 7) x 10^-3; this ratio is lower than the ratio in pp collisions and in the reaction p d -> He-3 M, and any significant deviation from this two-step model prediction could be evidence for an intrinsic s-bar-s component in the nucleon wave function.

## Method

The S-wave amplitudes for the reactions p n -> d omega and p n -> d phi near threshold were calculated within the framework of a two-step model described by triangle graphs with pi, rho, and omega mesons in the intermediate state. The amplitudes include contributions from pi, rho, and omega exchanges, with the elementary pi N -> M N amplitudes taken from experimental S-wave cross sections and the rho and omega exchange contributions estimated using the vector-dominance model from photoproduction data. The cross sections were calculated as an incoherent sum of the pi, rho, and omega exchange contributions, multiplied by a normalization factor N = 0.68 to account for initial state interaction effects, as previously applied to the reaction p n -> d eta.

## Key numbers

*   The predicted phi-to-omega yield ratio for p n -> d M is R = (30 +/- 7) x 10^-3.
*   The cross section parameterization for p n -> d omega gives D_omega = (2.7 +/- 0.3) microbarn / MeV^(1/2) in the formula sigma ≈ D_M sqrt(Q).
*   The cross section parameterization for p n -> d phi gives D_phi = (0.09 +/- 0.02) microbarn / MeV^(1/2) in the formula sigma ≈ D_M sqrt(Q).
*   The elementary S-wave cross sections used are: sigma(pi- p -> omega n) = (8.3 +/- 0.07) p_cm^M microbarn and sigma(pi- p -> phi n) = (0.29 +/- 0.06) p_cm^M microbarn (p_cm^M in MeV/c).
*   The predicted cross section for p n -> d omega at Q = 15 MeV is about 10 times larger than the cross section for p p -> p p omega at the same Q (approximately 1 microbarn for the pp reaction).
*   For phi production at Q = 82 MeV, the predicted sigma(p n -> d phi) is approximately 0.6 - 1 microbarn, compared to the estimated sigma(p p -> p p phi) = 0.28 +/- 0.14 microbarn from DISTO data.

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to Pontecorvo reactions (Kondratyuk, earlier work) and to the reaction p n -> d eta (Grishina, 1999) to the production of omega and phi mesons. The predicted phi/omega ratio of (30 +/- 7) x 10^-3 is lower than the ratio of (49 +/- 26) x 10^-3 found in pp collisions by the SPES3 collaboration and significantly lower than the ratio of (63 +/- 5 +27/-8) x 10^-3 found in the reaction p d -> He-3 X at SATURNE II (Wurzinger, 1994; Wurzinger, 1995). If line-reverse invariance holds, the ratio could be as high as (250 +/- 60) x 10^-3 based on OBELIX data for anti-p d -> n M, providing a test for the presence of intrinsic strangeness in the nucleon wave function as proposed by Ellis (1995). The predicted cross sections are large enough that the reactions could be measured at COSY-Juelich using the missing mass method with forward deuteron detection.
