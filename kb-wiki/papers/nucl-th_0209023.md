# Analytical approach to electromagnetic processes in loosely bound nuclei: application to 8B

> In this paper we develop an analytical model in order to study electromagnetic processes involving loosely bound neutron--rich and proton--rich nuclei. We construct a model wave function, to describe loosely bound few--body systems, having the correct behaviour both at large and small distances. The continuum states are approximated by regular Coulomb functions. As a test case we consider the two--body Coulomb dissociation of 8B and, the inverse, radiative capture reaction. The difference between using a pure two--body model and the results obtained when incorporating many--body effects, is investigated. We conclude that the interpretation of experimental data is highly model dependent and stress the importance of measuring few--body channels.

## Key claim

The interpretation of experimental data from Coulomb dissociation and radiative capture reactions on loosely bound nuclei is highly model dependent; specifically, a pure two-body model of 8B fails to reproduce experimental data, while a model incorporating many-body (three-body) effects yields excellent agreement with the shape of the energy spectrum and the radiative capture cross section, although the Coulomb dissociation cross section remains about 20% larger than the experimental data from Davids et al. (2001).

## Method

The authors constructed an analytical model wave function for loosely bound two-body systems that has correct asymptotic behavior at both large and small intercluster distances, using a Whittaker function multiplied by a cutoff factor. Continuum states were approximated by regular Coulomb functions with zero nuclear phase shifts. The model was applied to 8B, first as a pure two-body (7Be + p) system and then using the two-body projection of a three-body wave function from Grigorenko et al. (1999), with the E1 strength function computed analytically via hypergeometric functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 8B: E0 = 137 keV (two-body system)
- RMS intercluster distance for 8B: r_rms = 4.57 fm (extracted from Grigorenko et al. 1999)
- Radiative capture cross section at 186 keV from three-body model: sigma_rc = 16.1 nb, compared to experimental value 16.7 ± 2.1 nb from Hammache et al. (2001)
- E2 contribution to radiative capture cross section: approximately 0.03%
- Three-body model Coulomb dissociation cross section about 20% larger than experimental data from Davids et al. (2001) at beam energy 82.7 MeV/A
- Analytical approximation of E1 strength function agrees with numerical "exact" model to within less than 2% in the region of interest
- Binding energy for the 7Be excited state component: E0 = 566 keV

## Context

This work extends analytical approaches previously applied to one-neutron halo nuclei (Bertsch and Esbensen 1988, Otsuka et al. 1994, Kaltchev et al. 1996) and two-neutron halo nuclei (Puschl et al. 1996, Forssen et al. 2002) to proton-rich systems like 8B. The model enables analytical study of electromagnetic processes relevant to solar neutrino production, specifically the 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, and demonstrates that many-body effects must be included to reproduce experimental data. The authors stress the importance of measuring few-body channels in complete kinematics, as demonstrated by Cortina-Gil et al. (2002) who observed the excited core component of the 8B wave function.
