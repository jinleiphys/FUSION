# Impact of muons on the bulk viscosity of neutron star matter metamodels

> Recent studies invoke a unified description of different neutron star observables using metamodels, which parametrize the Equation of State (EoS) of neutron star matter close to nuclear saturation density in terms of few nuclear parameters. In this light, the bulk viscosity in the neutrino-transparent regime of dense nuclear matter composed of neutrons, protons and electrons has been recently shown to be mostly sensitive to the value of the nuclear symmetry energy. As muons are also present at densities around nuclear saturation, we further analyse in this manuscript their impact on this transport coefficient as a function of the slope $L$ of the symmetry energy. We find that muons introduce both relevant qualitative and quantitative effects in the bulk viscous dissipation. Increasing $L$ by a factor two has an effect of several orders of magnitude on the (frequency-independent) bulk viscosity. We also find that for all values of $L$ the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity presents a double peak structure for some values of the density, absent without muons. This also represents changes in orders of magnitude of the viscosity in narrow windows of densities that could be attainable in a neutron star for enough high values of $L$. We present a systematic numerical analysis of both second-order transport coefficients, frequency-dependent bulk viscosity, and damping times of density oscillations as a function of the density and the slope, and find when these could be relevant for the dynamics of the merger of neutrons stars.

## Key claim

Muons introduce both qualitative and quantitative changes to the bulk viscosity of neutron star matter in the neutrino-transparent regime, including a double-peak structure in the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity that is absent when only electrons are considered; these effects depend strongly on the slope \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy, with changes of several orders of magnitude when \(L\) is varied by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors use a metamodel for the nuclear equation of state (EoS) expanded around saturation density in terms of isoscalar and isovector parameters (binding energy \(B_{\rm sat}\), incompressibility \(K\), symmetry energy \(J\), slope \(L\), and higher-order terms). They impose \(\beta\)-equilibrium and charge neutrality to compute particle fractions of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons as functions of baryon density. The bulk viscosity is then computed from the linearized evolution equations of chemical imbalances, using electroweak rates for direct-Urca and modified-Urca processes in the Fermi-surface approximation, and solving the resulting Burgers-type equation for the bulk-viscous pressure.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is taken as \(n_0 = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The symmetry energy at saturation is fixed to \(J = 32\ \text{MeV}\) and the incompressibility to \(K = 240\ \text{MeV}\); the slope \(L\) is varied among 50, 70, and 110 MeV.
- The muon mass is \(m_\mu \approx 105\ \text{MeV}\).
- The Fermi coupling constant is \(G_F = 1.166 \times 10^{-5}\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), the Cabbibo angle \(\Theta_C \approx 13.02^\circ\), and the axial-vector coupling \(g_A = 1.26\).
- The effective nucleon mass at saturation is \(m_N^* \approx (0.7 \pm 0.1) m_N\) with \(m_N \approx 940\ \text{MeV}\).
- The charged pion mass is \(m_\pi \approx 139.57\ \text{MeV}\).
- The direct-Urca rate coefficient scales as \(\propto T^4\) and the modified-Urca rate coefficient as \(\propto T^6\).
- The muon decay coefficient \(\bar{\alpha} \approx 0.0168\) is subdominant and neglected in the numerical results.

## Context

This work extends recent studies by Yang et al. (2025) and Harris et al. (2025) that showed the bulk viscosity of neutron-proton-electron matter is highly sensitive to the symmetry energy slope \(L\). It also builds on prior calculations of bulk viscosity with muons by Alford et al. (2022, 2023) in the neutrino-transparent regime, which used specific microscopic EoS models rather than a metamodel. The results enable more accurate modeling of damping times of density oscillations in neutron star mergers, where bulk viscosity may leave an imprint on gravitational wave signals, and motivate future inclusion of these transport coefficients in merger simulations.
