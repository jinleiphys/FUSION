# Validity of relativistic hydrodynamics beyond local equilibrium

> We examine the applicability of relativistic hydrodynamics far from equilibrium by constructing formal solutions of the Boltzmann moment equations in the relaxation time approximation. These solutions naturally decompose into a divergent gradient series and exponentially decaying non-perturbative modes that encode initial conditions. The non-perturbative contributions are essential for understanding causality, the divergence of the gradient series, and the unexpected effectiveness of relativistic hydrodynamics far from equilibrium. In the 0+1D Bjorken scenario, we demonstrate that the exact evolution of non-equilibrium terms shares the same structural form as the gradient expansion, differing only through modified transport coefficients that reflect both initial data and free-streaming dynamics. Extending to 3+1D, we find that hydrodynamics remains effective not because the system is close to equilibrium, but because it interpolates smoothly between free streaming and collective behavior. This perspective offers a natural explanation for the remarkable success of hydrodynamics in modeling quark-gluon plasma evolution.

# Validity of Relativistic Hydrodynamics Beyond Local Equilibrium

## Key claim

Relativistic hydrodynamics remains effective far from equilibrium not because the system is close to local equilibrium, but because it interpolates smoothly between free-streaming and collective behavior, with the exact evolution of non-equilibrium terms sharing the same structural form as the gradient expansion when transport coefficients are appropriately rescaled to reflect initial data and free-streaming dynamics.

## Method

The authors construct formal solutions of the Boltzmann moment equations in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a 0+1D Bjorken flow system, decomposing the exact solution into a gradient series and exponentially decaying non-perturbative modes that encode initial conditions. They derive evolution equations for the non-equilibrium moments and show that these equations have the same structural form as the gradient expansion, differing only through modified transport coefficients. The analysis is then extended to 3+1D relaxation-type equations to demonstrate the general applicability of the framework.

## Key numbers

- The free-streaming solution for moments at late times scales as g_{n,l}(τ >> τ₀) ∼ (τ₀/τ)^(2l+1), independent of the energy-scaling index n
- The damping factor ξ₀ = ∫_{τ₀}^{τ} (u·p/τ_R) dt' controls the transition between free-streaming (ξ₀ < 1, corresponding to τ < τ_R) and hydrodynamic (ξ₀ ≫ 1) regimes
- The non-perturbative corrections become relevant when the Knudsen number approaches unity (Kn ∼ 1), during early times when ξ₀ < 1

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hydrodynamic attractors (Romatschke 2017, Heller 2011, 2015, Buchel 2016) and the fixed-point perspective on rescaled transport coefficients (Blaizot 2017, 2019, 2020, 2021; Jaiswal 2022) by providing a fully analytic decomposition of the exact solution into gradient and non-perturbative contributions. The authors demonstrate that the failure of attractors to explain early-time universal behavior in non-conformal and 3+1D systems (Dash 2020, Chen 2021, Kamata 2022, Jaiswal 2021, Gangadharan 2023) can be resolved by recognizing that hydrodynamics with modified transport coefficients interpolates between free-streaming and equilibrium fixed points. This framework enables a systematic understanding of why viscous hydrodynamics successfully describes quark-gluon plasma evolution in heavy-ion collisions despite the system being created in a highly anisotropic, far-from-equilibrium state.
