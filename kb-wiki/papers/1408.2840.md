# Influence of angular momentum and Coulomb interaction of colliding nuclei on their multifragmentation

> Theoretical calculations are performed to investigate the angular momentum and Coulomb effects on fragmentation and multifragmentation in peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies. Inhomogeneous distributions of hot fragments in the freeze-out volume are taken into account by microcanonical Markov chain calculations within the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM). Including an angular momentum and a long-range Coulomb interaction between projectile and target residues leads to new features in the statistical fragmentation picture. In this case, one can obtain specific correlations of sizes of emitted fragments with their velocities and an emission in the reaction plane. In addition, one may see a significant influence of these effects on the isotope production both in the midrapidity and in the kinematic regions of the projectile/target. The relation of this approach to the simulations of such collisions with dynamical models is also discussed.

## Key claim

Including angular momentum and long-range Coulomb interactions between projectile and target residues in the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) produces new features in the statistical fragmentation picture, such as correlations of fragment sizes with velocities and preferential emission in the reaction plane, which were previously assumed to require dynamical models.

## Method

The authors performed microcanonical Markov chain calculations within the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) for peripheral $^{84}$Kr+$^{84}$Kr collisions at 35 MeV per nucleon. They simulated the break-up of a single $^{84}$Kr projectile source in the proximity of a $^{84}$Kr target source, generating fragment partitions and coordinate positions in the freeze-out volume via the Metropolis algorithm, with direct calculation of Coulomb interactions between non-overlapping fragments and inclusion of angular momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

* Angular momentum value of $L=80\hbar$ was selected as an upper estimate for the projectile-like source.
* The excitation energy transferred to the residues was approximately 5 MeV/nucleon.
* Calculations were performed at two freeze-out densities: $\rho=\rho_0/2$ and $\rho=\rho_0/6$, where $\rho_0=0.15$ fm$^{-3}$ is normal nuclear matter density.
* The relative velocity between projectile and target residues was estimated to decrease to $\sim$50 mm/ns from the initial 80 mm/ns.
* The target source was located at $R_Y=-10.6$ fm and $R_Z=10.6$ fm with respect to the projectile source.
* The neutron-to-proton ratio ($N/Z$) of the $^{84}$Kr source is 1.33.
* About $5\times10^5$ Monte Carlo events were generated for sufficient statistics.

## Context

This work extends previous statistical multifragmentation studies of single isolated sources (Botvina 2002, Ogul 2011, Buyukcizmeci 2005) to include the effects of angular momentum and external Coulomb fields from a second source, as suggested in earlier theoretical work (Botvina 1999, Botvina 2001). The approach enables analysis of peripheral heavy-ion collisions at Fermi energies, particularly for experiments such as FAZIA (2013), and provides a statistical alternative to dynamical models like AMD and QMD for describing midrapidity emission and neutron-rich fragment production.
