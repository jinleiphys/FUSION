# Examination of the Nature of the ABC Effect

> Recently it has been shown by exclusive and kinematically complete experiments that the appearance of a narrow resonance structure in double-pionic fusion reactions is strictly correlated with the appearance of the so-called ABC effect, which denotes a pronounced low-mass enhancement in the $ππ$-invariant mass spectrum. Whereas the resonance structure got its explanation by the $d^*(2380)$ dibaryonic resonance, a satisfactory explanation for the ABC effect is still pending. In this paper we discuss possible explanations of the ABC effect and their consequences for the internal structure of the $d^*$ dibaryon.

## Key claim

The ABC effect, a pronounced low-mass enhancement in the ππ-invariant mass spectrum observed in double-pionic fusion reactions, is strictly correlated with the appearance of the d*(2380) dibaryon resonance, but its satisfactory explanation remains pending; the paper examines multiple hypotheses, including a d* → ΔΔ vertex function, a ΔΔ final-state interaction, and alternative decay branches, confronting each with experimental data from the np → dπ⁰π⁰ and np → npπ⁰π⁰ reactions.

## Method

The authors calculate the s-channel resonance process pn → d*(2380) → ΔΔ → dπ⁰π⁰ using a Breit-Wigner amplitude with momentum-dependent partial widths, a deuteron wave function, and Monte-Carlo integration over three-body phase space. They compare the resulting π⁰π⁰-invariant mass distributions to data at √s = 2.38 GeV and 2.5 GeV, and also compute the np → npπ⁰π⁰ reaction with a four-body phase space and a Migdal-Watson final-state interaction for the unbound pn pair. Alternative explanations, a ΔΔ final-state interaction parameterized by a scattering length, the D₀₃ → D₁₂π decay branch proposed by Platonova and Kukulin, and a possible D-state between the two Δs, are each evaluated against the same datasets.

## Key numbers

- The d*(2380) resonance has mass m_R ≈ 2.38 GeV and width Γ_R ≈ 70 MeV (from total cross sections of pn → dπ⁰π⁰ and pn → dπ⁺π⁻).
- The resonance pole is at (2380 ± 10) − i(40 ± 5) MeV in the ³D₃–³G₃ coupled partial waves, based on SAID partial-wave analysis.
- For the d* → ΔΔ vertex function of monopole type FF ∼ Λ²/(Λ² + p_ΔΔ²), the fitted cutoff parameter is Λ ≈ 0.16 GeV/c, corresponding to a length scale r = ħ√6/Λ ≈ 2 fm.
- At √s = 2.5 GeV (above the d* region), the t-channel ΔΔ process reproduces the data with a dominant high-mass enhancement.
- For the ΔΔ final-state interaction model, a scattering length a_s ≈ 30 fm (with r₀ = 2 fm and R = 1.1 fm) gives a reasonable description of the M_π⁰π⁰² spectrum.
- The upper limit for the relative nucleon momentum q inside the deuteron used in numerical calculations is q_max = 800 MeV/c; extending to 1.5 GeV/c yields no significant changes.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (Bashkanov et al., 2009, 2011) that established the d*(2380) resonance and its correlation with the ABC effect, originally discovered by Abashian, Booth, and Crowe (1960). The paper reviews and tests multiple explanations: the vertex-function approach previously used in Bashkanov et al. (2011), the ΔΔ final-state interaction from earlier WASA analyses (Bashkanov, 2009), the D₀₃ → D₁₂π decay branch proposed by Platonova and Kukulin (2013), and a new hypothesis involving a D-state between the two Δs. The analysis enables future fundamental theoretical treatment of the ABC effect and its connection to the internal structure of the d* dibaryon, while identifying the np → npπ⁰π⁰ reaction as the most challenging case for any explanation.
