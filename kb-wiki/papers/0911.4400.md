# Eccentricity fluctuations make flow measurable in high multiplicity p-p collisions

> Elliptic flow is a hallmark of collectivity in hadronic collisions. Its measurement relies on analysis techniques which require high event multiplicity and could be applied so far to heavy ion collisions only. Here, we delineate the conditions under which elliptic flow becomes measurable in the samples of high-multiplicity ($dN_{\rm ch}/dy \geq 50$) p-p collisions, which will soon be collected at the LHC. We observe that fluctuations in the p-p interaction region can result in a sizable spatial eccentricity even for the most central p-p collisions. Under relatively mild assumptions on the nature of such fluctuations and on the eccentricity scaling of elliptic flow, we find that the resulting elliptic flow signal in high-multiplicity p-p collisions at the LHC becomes measurable with standard techniques.

## Key claim
Elliptic flow may become measurable in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC, provided that event-by-event fluctuations in the interaction region produce a sizable spatial eccentricity and that the eccentricity scaling of elliptic flow follows the same pattern observed in heavy ion collisions. The claim is conditional on the assumption that hadronic activity is localized in a small number of "hot spots" within the proton, rather than being smoothly distributed.

## Method
The authors model the proton-proton interaction region as a collection of $N_s$ randomly distributed "hot spots" of radius $r_0$, each with a Gaussian density profile. They calculate the participant eccentricity distribution for this class of models and then estimate the elliptic flow signal $v_2\{4\}$ from four-particle cumulants using an interpolation formula (the Knudsen number approach) that relates spatial eccentricity to momentum anisotropy, with parameters extracted from RHIC heavy ion data.

## Key numbers
- High-multiplicity p-p events at the LHC are expected to reach $dN_{\rm ch}/dy \geq 50$ (from Monte Carlo simulations).
- For a model with $N_s = 3$ hot spots in a zero impact parameter collision, the eccentricity $\epsilon\{4\}$ takes values of 0.69, 0.54, and 0.40 for spot radii $r_0 = 0.1$, 0.2, and 0.3 fm respectively.
- The minimal signal strength required to discriminate $v_2$ from non-flow effects using two-particle cumulants is $v_2 > 0.14$ for $n_{\rm mult}=50$, and using four-particle cumulants is $v_2 > 0.05$ for $n_{\rm mult}=50$.
- The hydrodynamic limit used is $(v_2/\epsilon)^{hydro}=0.3$ with $\bar \lambda /K_0=5.8 \,{\rm fm}^{-2}$ extracted from RHIC data.

## Context
This work extends the observation from lighter Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC (Alver et al., 2006; Alver et al., 2008) that event-by-event fluctuations of the density distribution can increase initial spatial eccentricity significantly above geometric estimates. Previous estimates of $v_2$ in p-p collisions based on smoothness assumptions (d'Enterria, 2009; Luzum, 2009) or other methods (Cunqueiro, 2008) reported small, non-measurable values. The paper enables a new experimental program at the LHC to search for collective flow in the smallest hadronic system, which would constrain models of multiple parton interactions and provide insight into equilibration and dissipation mechanisms in QCD. A contemporaneous study by Bozek (2009) using a two-flux-tube model arrived at similar conclusions.
