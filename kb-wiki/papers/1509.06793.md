# Application of supersymmetric quantum mechanics to study bound state properties of exotic hypernuclei

> Bound state properties of few single and double-$Λ$ hypernuclei is critically examined in the framework of core-$Λ$ and core+$Λ+Λ$ few-body model applying hyperspherical harmonics expansion method (HHEM). The $ΛΛ$ potential is chosen phenomenologically while the core-$Λ$ potential is obtained by folding a phenomenological $ΛN$ interaction into the density distribution of the core. The depth of the effective $ΛN$ potential is adjusted to reproduce the experimental data for the core-$Λ$ subsystem. The three-body Schrödinger equation is solved by hyperspherical adiabatic approximation (HAA) to get the ground state energy and wave function. The ground state wavefunction is used to construct the supersymmetric partner potential following prescription of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) algebra. The newly constructed supersymmetric partner potential is used to solve the three-body Schrödinger equation to get the energy and wavefunction for the first excited state of the original potential. The method is repeated to predict energy and wavefunction of the next higher excited states. The possible number of bound states is found to increase with the increase in mass of the core of the hypernuclei. The Root Mean Squared (RMS) matter radius and some other relevant geometrical observables are also predicted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) can be used to circumvent the slow convergence of the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method (HHEM) for excited states of double-Λ hypernuclei, by constructing a partner potential whose ground state corresponds to the first excited state of the original potential. This claim is conditional on the validity of the phenomenological ΛΛ and core-Λ potentials chosen, and on the truncation of the expansion basis at Kmax=20.

## Method

The author treats single-Λ hypernuclei as two-body core-Λ systems and double-Λ hypernuclei as three-body core+Λ+Λ systems, solving the Schrödinger equation using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method (HHEM) with hyperspherical adiabatic approximation (HAA). The core-Λ potential is obtained by folding a phenomenological ΛN Gaussian potential into a Fermi-type density distribution of the core, with the ΛN potential depth adjusted to reproduce experimental single-Λ binding energies. For excited states, the ground-state wavefunction is used to construct a supersymmetric partner potential via SSQM algebra, and the partner potential's ground state is solved to obtain the first excited state of the original system; this procedure is repeated iteratively for higher excited states.

## Key numbers

- For \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{6}\)He, the calculated two-Λ separation energy \(B_{\Lambda\Lambda}\) and ΛΛ bond energy \(\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda}\) at \(K_{max}=20\) agree with experimental values \(7.25 \pm 0.19\) MeV and \(1.01\) MeV respectively.
- The depth of the effective ΛN potential follows a logarithmic fit \(v_0(A_c) = \delta_1 \log_e(A_c) + \delta_2\) with \(\delta_1 = 1.55033\) MeV and \(\delta_2 = -43.8843\) MeV.
- The core density distribution uses parameters \(a = 0.65\) fm and \(r_0 = 1.7\) fm for the Fermi function half-density radius \(r_c = r_0 A_c^{1/3}\).
- The hyper angular momentum quantum number is truncated at \(K_{max}=20\) for all calculations.
- The core masses studied are \(A_c = 4, 6, 30, 40, 90\) corresponding to double-Λ hypernuclei with mass numbers \(A = 6, 8, 34, 42, 92\).

## Context

This work extends earlier variational and approximate few-body studies of hypernuclei (referenced as works [19-22] in the paper) by applying the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method, which is described as essentially exact. The use of SSQM to obtain excited states is a novel approach that avoids the slow convergence of HHEM for excited states. The results for \(_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{6}\)He are compared with experimental data from emulsion experiments (referenced as [7] in the paper) and with the author's previous calculation (referenced as [52] in the paper). The work enables predictions of excited-state energies and geometrical observables for double-Λ hypernuclei across a range of core masses, and provides an empirical formula for the effective ΛN potential depth as a function of core mass.
