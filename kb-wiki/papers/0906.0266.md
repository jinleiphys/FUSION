# On the electromagnetic form factors of hadrons in the time-like region near threshold

> Hadron electromagnetic form factor in the time-like region at the boundary of the physical region is considered. The energy behavior of the form factor is shown to be determined by the strong hadron-antihadron interaction. Imaginary parts of the scattering lengths for $p\bar{p}$, $Λ\barΛ$, $Λ\barΣ^0 (\barΛΣ^0)$ and $Σ^0\barΣ^0$ are estimated. Developed approach enables us to estimate imaginary part of the scattering volume from $D^*\bar{D^*}$ experimental data. The form factor energy behavior away from the threshold is obtained within a semiphenomenological model of hadron-antihadron interaction.

## Key claim

The energy behavior of hadron electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region near threshold is determined primarily by the strong hadron-antihadron final state interaction, and the form factor can be factorized into a constant factor from far singularities and a factor reflecting this final state interaction; the model's applicability is demonstrated for the proton form factor up to relative momenta of 400 MeV/c, where both scattering length and effective range approximations fail.

## Method

The authors develop a semiphenomenological model of hadron-antihadron interaction where the form factor near threshold is expressed in terms of the Jost function and scattering parameters. For the region not too close to threshold, they introduce a model with an exponential potential tail (with diffuseness parameter ρ) and an energy-independent boundary condition at a matching distance, parameterized by a complex phase Φ, to describe the transition from exponential decay near threshold to a 1/√(π k ρ) behavior away from threshold. They fit this model to experimental data on proton, Λ, Σ⁰, and D* form factors to extract imaginary parts of scattering lengths and volumes.

## Key numbers

- Imaginary part of the p̅p scattering length extracted from LEAR data: Im α = -(0.73 ± 0.05) fm, in agreement with the value Im α(³S₁) = -0.8 fm from protonium triplet s-level shifts and widths.
- Model parameters for p̅p: ρ = 0.3 fm, Φ = 0.01 + i1.02.
- Imaginary scattering lengths for baryon-antibaryon systems: ΛΛ̅: Im α = -(1.3 ± 0.6) fm, ρ = (0.4 ± 0.3) fm; ΛΣ̅⁰ (Λ̅Σ⁰): Im α = -(2.4 ± 0.7) fm, ρ = (0.7 ± 0.1) fm; Σ⁰Σ̅⁰: Im α = -(2.5 ± 0.8) fm, ρ = (0.6 ± 0.1) fm.
- For a possible ΛΛ̅ near-threshold resonance fit: ρ = 0.1 fm, Φ = 1.57 + i0.55, Im α = -0.71 fm.
- Imaginary part of the scattering volume for D*D̅*: Im α₁ = -0.61 fm³, with ρ = 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Dalkarov et al. (earlier works from the same group, cited as references 10-13) that treats final state interaction as the dominant physical reason for form factor energy behavior near baryon-antibaryon thresholds, which was itself a consequence of the quasi-nuclear model of low energy baryon-antibaryon interaction (references 14-18). The paper contrasts with vector dominance models (VDM) that successfully describe pion and space-like nucleon form factors but fail for the proton form factor in the time-like region; the authors note that VDM models predict neutron form factors five to ten times larger than the proton one, whereas their model predicts the neutron form factor does not exceed the proton one. The work enables extraction of hadron-antihadron scattering parameters from form factor measurements and suggests experiments to search for quasinuclear bound states in systems with hidden strangeness, charm, and beauty.
