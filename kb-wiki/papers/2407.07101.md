# Mean-field derived IBM-1 Hamiltonian with intrinsic triaxial deformation

> An interacting-boson-model-1 (IBM-1) Hamiltonian, derived from self-consistent mean-field calculations using a Skyrme energy density functional is employed for the study of energy spectra and $B(E2)$ transition strengths in the even-even $^{162-184}\mathrm{Hf}$ and $^{168-186}\mathrm{W}$. An intrinsic triaxial deformation, derived from fermionic proxy-SU(3) irreps, is incorporated into the IBM-1 potential energy curve, which is subsequently mapped to the fermionic one, in order to derive the parameters of the IBM-1 Hamiltonian. It is shown that the inclusion of the intrinsic triaxial deformation derived from the proxy-SU(3) irreps leads to a significantly improved agreement between the theoretical predictions and experimental data for the low-lying quadrupole bands in the examined isotopes, without the need of higher-order terms in the IBM-1 Hamiltonian. The calculated $B(E2)$ transition strengths are also improved, compared to the axially symmetric case. The recently suggested preponderance of triaxial deformation over extended regions of the nuclear chart is obtained as a by-product. Future potential improvements and extensions to this mapping approach are also discussed.

## Key claim

Including an intrinsic triaxial deformation derived from proxy-SU(3) shell-model irreps into the classical limit of the IBM-1 potential energy curve significantly improves agreement with experimental spectra and B(E2) strengths for low-lying quadrupole bands in even-even Hf and W isotopes, without requiring higher-order terms in the IBM-1 Hamiltonian. The improvement is demonstrated for the specific isotopic chains studied, but the authors note that the approach is parameter-free regarding the triaxial deformation and that further testing on Os and Pt isotopes is needed.

## Method

Potential energy curves (PECs) were calculated using the axial Hartree-Fock+BCS code SkyAx with a Skyrme energy density functional (SV-bas). The IBM-1 PEC in its classical limit was then fitted to the microscopic PEC to determine the IBM-1 Hamiltonian parameters. Two sets of calculations were performed: one assuming axial symmetry (gamma=0 degrees) and one incorporating an intrinsic triaxial deformation gamma_s obtained from proxy-SU(3) highest-weight irreps in a parameter-free way. Spectra and B(E2) transition rates were then computed using the IBAR code.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameter at the HF PEC minimum (beta_F^min) ranges from 0.208 (162Hf) to 0.328 (174Hf, 176Hf) for Hf isotopes, and from 0.236 (186W) to 0.313 (176W) for W isotopes.
- Mass coefficients for beta-bands (alpha_beta) range from 0.650 (180Hf) to 0.844 (176Hf) for Hf, and from 0.578 (170W) to 0.740 (180W) for W.
- Mass coefficients for gamma-bands (alpha_gamma) range from 0.795 (180Hf) to 1.616 (174Hf) for Hf, and from 0.553 (180W) to 1.058 (176W) for W.
- The SU(3) limit value for the chi parameter is -sqrt(7)/2 = -1.323, which the calculations approach for well-deformed nuclei when gamma_s is included.
- Maximum B(E2) values are obtained at N=102 for Hf and N=98 for W, rather than at the midshell N=104.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field to IBM mapping approach previously applied by Nomura et al. (2011) and Rudigier et al. (2015) for Hf isotopes using IBM-2 with Gogny EDFs, and by Nomura et al. (2010) for Xe and Ba isotopes. It differs by using IBM-1 (no proton-neutron distinction) with a Skyrme EDF and by incorporating triaxial deformation from proxy-SU(3) rather than from triaxial mean-field calculations. The results enable future application to Os and Pt isotopes, where a prolate-to-oblate shape/phase transition is expected near N=116, and suggest that triaxial mean-field calculations (e.g., Maruhn et al. 2014, Schuetrumpf et al. 2018) could provide an alternative microscopic input for the mapping.
