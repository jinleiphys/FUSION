# Formation and decay of hadronic resonances in the QGP

> Hadronic resonances can play a pivotal role in providing experimental evidence for partial chiral symmetry restoration in the deconfined quark-gluon phase produced at RHIC. Their lifetimes, which are comparable to the lifetime of the partonic plasma phase, make them an invaluable tool to study medium modifications to the resonant state due to the chiral transition. In this paper we show that the heavier, but still abundant, light and strange quark resonances K*, phi, Delta and Lambda* have large probability to be produced well within the plasma phase due to their short formation times. We demonstrate that, under particular kinematic conditions, these resonances can be formed and will decay inside the partonic state, but still carry sufficient momentum to not interact strongly with the hadronic medium after the QCD phase transition. Thus, K*, phi, Delta and Lambda* should exhibit the characteristic property modifications which can be attributed to chiral symmetry restoration, such as mass shifts, width broadening or branching ratio modifications.

## Key claim

Hadronic resonances such as K*, phi, Delta, and Lambda* can be formed and decay inside the quark-gluon plasma phase at RHIC and LHC, provided they carry sufficient momentum (p_T > 2 GeV/c) to avoid strong hadronic reinteraction after the QCD phase transition, thereby making them sensitive probes of partial chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

The authors calculate hadronic formation times using a lightcone-based independent fragmentation formalism, where the formation time depends on the hadron mass, parent parton momentum, and the momentum fraction z carried by the hadron. They evaluate mean formation times by convolving these formation times with probability distributions P(z) derived from known fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas, using these as proxies for the unknown resonance fragmentation functions. The QGP lifetime is estimated from a Bjorken expansion scenario using measured charged hadron rapidity densities and mean transverse momenta at RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (sqrt(s)=200 GeV Au+Au), the QGP proper formation time tau_0 = 0.44 fm/c, with initial temperature T(tau_0) = 435 MeV for b=3 fm and dN^g/dy = 1100
- At LHC (sqrt(s)=5.5 TeV Pb+Pb), tau_0 = 0.23 fm/c, with T(tau_0) = 713 MeV for dN^g/dy = 2800
- QGP lifetime under longitudinal Bjorken expansion: tau_QGP = 6.2 fm/c at RHIC and 14 fm/c at LHC, assuming critical temperature T_c = 180 MeV
- In-vacuum decay widths: Gamma(K*) = 50.8 MeV, Gamma(phi) = 4.65 MeV, Gamma(Delta) = 120 MeV, Gamma(Lambda*) = 15.6 MeV
- Proper decay lengths: K* (c*tau = 3.88 fm), Delta (c*tau = 1.64 fm), Lambda* (c*tau = 12.6 fm), phi (c*tau = 46.5 fm)
- At T=250 MeV, the phi meson decay width broadening can be as large as a factor of 10, implying Delta_Gamma_th = 50 MeV
- For experimental studies, resonances below 5 GeV/c at RHIC or 10 GeV/c at LHC are recommended for observing medium modifications

## Context

This work extends the space-time picture of hadronization developed by Adil and Vitev (2007) for heavy quarks to light and strange quark resonances, and builds upon earlier formation time studies by Bjorken (1976) and Kopeliovich (1984). The paper proposes a triggered jet correlation measurement using same-side and away-side quadrants relative to a high-p_T trigger particle, which provides a built-in reference from off-axis low momentum resonances. This method aims to resolve the question of resonance modification through partial chiral symmetry restoration, which had been previously studied in hadronic medium calculations by Holt (2004) and Filip (2001), but not specifically in the context of the partonic phase.
