# Role of colliding geometry on the balance energy of mass-asymmetric systems

> We study the role of colliding geometry on the balance energy (Ebal) of mass-asymmetric systems by varying the mass asymmetry (η = AT - Ap/AT + AP, where AT and AP are the masses of the target and projectile, respectively) from 0.1 to 0.7, over the mass range 40-240 and on the mass dependence of the balance energy. Our findings reveal that colliding geometry has a significant effect on the Ebal of asymmetric systems. We find that, as we go from central collisions to peripheral ones, the effect of mass asymmetry on Ebal increases throughout the mass range. Interestingly, we find that for every fixed system mass (Atot) the effect of the impact parameter variation is almost uniform throughout the mass-asymmetry range. For each η, Ebal follows a power-law behavior (\propto Aτ) at all colliding geometries

## Key claim

The effect of mass asymmetry on balance energy (Ebal) increases as collisions become more peripheral, but for every fixed total system mass (Atot), the percentage change in Ebal with impact parameter variation is almost uniform across the mass-asymmetry range (η = 0.1 to 0.7).

## Method

The author used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model with a momentum-dependent soft equation of state and standard energy-dependent Cugnon nucleon-nucleon cross section (labeled SMD) to simulate reactions of mass-asymmetric systems. Simulations were performed for total system masses Atot = 40, 80, 160, and 240, with mass asymmetry η ranging from 0.1 to 0.7, at three reduced impact parameters (b/bmax = 0.25, 0.5, and 0.75), over incident energies from 90 to 600 MeV/nucleon. The balance energy was extracted from the directed transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- For Atot = 40, the mean percentage change in Ebal with impact parameter (Coulomb ON) is 29.80% at b/bmax = 0.5 and 127.69% at b/bmax = 0.75, relative to b/bmax = 0.25.
- For Atot = 240, the mean percentage change in Ebal with impact parameter (Coulomb ON) is 16.88% at b/bmax = 0.5 and 56.47% at b/bmax = 0.75, relative to b/bmax = 0.25.
- The power-law exponent τ (where Ebal ∝ A^τ) averaged over η is -0.29 (Coulomb ON) at b/bmax = 0.25, -0.35 at b/bmax = 0.5, and -0.51 at b/bmax = 0.75.
- For η = 0.1, the power-law exponent τ (Coulomb ON) is -0.30 at b/bmax = 0.25, -0.34 at b/bmax = 0.5, and -0.52 at b/bmax = 0.75.
- For η = 0.7, the power-law exponent τ (Coulomb ON) is -0.28 at b/bmax = 0.25, -0.36 at b/bmax = 0.5, and -0.50 at b/bmax = 0.75.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Goyal, 2011) which examined mass asymmetry effects on Ebal only for central collisions (b/bmax = 0.25). It also builds on prior work showing that for symmetric systems, Ebal increases approximately linearly with impact parameter (Ref. 11a, 12 in the paper) and follows a power-law mass dependence (Ref. 9a). The results enable systematic understanding of how both colliding geometry and mass asymmetry jointly influence the disappearance of collective transverse flow, which is relevant for constraining the nuclear equation of state and nucleon-nucleon cross section.
