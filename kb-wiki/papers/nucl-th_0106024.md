# Comment on ``Structure of exotic nuclei and superheavy elements in a relativistic shell model''

> A recent paper [M. Rashdan, Phys. Rev. C 63, 044303 (2001)] introduces the new parameterization NL-RA1 of the relativistic mean-field model which is claimed to give a better description of nuclear properties than earlier ones. Using this model ^{298}114 is predicted to be a doubly-magic nucleus. As will be shown in this comment these findings are to be doubted as they are obtained with an unrealistic parameterization of the pairing interaction and neglecting ground-state deformation.

# Comment on "Structure of exotic nuclei and superheavy elements in a relativistic shell model"

## Key claim

The new NL-RA1 parameterization of the relativistic mean-field model and its prediction that ²⁹⁸114 is a doubly-magic nucleus are doubtful, because the results are obtained with an unrealistic parameterization of the pairing interaction and by neglecting ground-state deformation of nuclei known to be deformed.

## Method

The author recalculates properties of transactinide nuclei using a more realistic state-dependent delta pairing force with parameters adjusted following the strategy of Bender (2000), allowing for deformation, and compares the results to those obtained by Rashdan (2001) using the NL-RA1 parameterization with two constant-gap pairing models (ΔI and ΔII). The author examines the single-particle spectrum, pairing gaps, and two-proton gaps for N=184 isotones to identify the origin of the claimed shell closure at Z=114.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap for ²⁹⁸114 from model ΔII is Δp = 1.7 MeV, of the same order as the Z=114 gap in the single-particle spectrum (1.4 MeV)
- The missing deformation energy in the original calculations is on the order of 10 MeV or 0.5% of the binding energy
- The pairing gap for transactinides is overestimated by model ΔII by a factor of 3
- Corrections from blocking and mean-field contributions to calculated pairing gaps are usually smaller than 20% and decrease with A

## Context

This comment extends the critique of the NL-RA1 parameterization introduced by Rashdan (2001) and contradicts its claim of a doubly-magic ²⁹⁸114. The author compares with earlier studies by Bueervenich (1998) on superheavy nuclei and with Rutz (1997) who found no significance for a major shell closure at Z=114 using more realistic pairing models. The work enables proper assessment of shell closures in superheavy nuclei by demonstrating that the two-proton gap δ₂p cannot be used as a signature for shell closures when the pairing gap and its fluctuations are of similar size as the spacing of single-particle energies.
