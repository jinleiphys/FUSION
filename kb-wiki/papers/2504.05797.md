# Possible existence of pygmy dipole resonance built on excited states in a neutron-rich $^{80}$Ge nucleus

> The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) at the low-lying tail of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) is an interesting research subject as it carries important information about the nuclear surface with mixed isoscalar and isovector vibrations in $N > Z$ systems. The present paper investigates the possible existence of the PDR built on excited states in a neutron-rich $^{80}$Ge nucleus using the phonon damping model (PDM) with and without pairing correlation at zero and finite temperatures. The results obtained within the PDM with exact pairing (EP+PDM) reveal the appearance of two enhanced $γ$-transitions $E_γ= 7.25$ and 7.35 MeV at $T=0.6$ MeV, which carry the PDR nature and hence implying the possible existence of PDR built on excited states in this nucleus. These two $γ$-energies nicely match with a recent experimental observation, thus indicating the crucial role of the inclusion of exact pairing solution in the precise description of low-lying $γ$-transitions. The partition function-based analysis shows that this predicted PDR at $T=$ 0.6MeV is mainly contributed by the first $2^+$ excited state of the $^{80}$Ge nucleus. The isospin mixing at the nuclear surface is also observed in the investigated PDR at $T=0.6-0.7$ MeV. The primary mechanism underlying the emergence of the hot PDR is found due to the coupling of GDR phonon to non-collective particle-particle and hole-hole configurations at finite temperatures within the PDM framework, along with the shift in particle-hole excitation energies due to thermal pairing correlations

## Key claim

The paper claims the possible existence of a pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) built on excited states in the neutron-rich nucleus $^{80}$Ge, but only within a specific temperature range of 0.6-0.7 MeV, which implies a violation of the Brink-Axel hypothesis in the PDR region.

## Method

The authors employed the phonon damping model (PDM) with and without exact pairing (EP+PDM) at zero and finite temperatures to calculate the electric dipole (E1) strength distribution of $^{80}$Ge. The single-particle spectra and wave functions were obtained from Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field calculations using the SLy5 interaction, and the E1 strength functions and transition densities were computed to identify PDR signatures. The partition function was constructed to determine which excited states contribute to the thermal equilibrium background at finite temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Two enhanced gamma-transitions at E_gamma = 7.25 and 7.35 MeV were found at T = 0.6 MeV within the EP+PDM, carrying PDR nature
- The experimental gamma-transitions reported in Ref. [Ren] were 7181(53_stat + 18_sys) and 7337(53_stat + 19_sys) keV, matching the calculated values
- The GDR parameters from RIPL-3 database: E1_I ~ 15.9 MeV, E1_II ~ 18.2 MeV, Gamma1_I ~ 4.11 MeV, Gamma1_II ~ 6.40 MeV
- The quadrupole deformation parameter of $^{80}$Ge is beta_2 = 0.182
- At T = 0.6 MeV, the first 2+ excited state contributes approximately 50% to the thermal equilibrium background, indicating the PDR is mainly built on this state
- The PDR signatures appear in the temperature range T = 0.6-0.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the PDR at zero temperature using models such as Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation (HFRPA), quasiparticle phonon model (QPM), and relativistic QRPA, which only describe PDR built on the ground state. The results directly respond to a recent experiment by Ren (2024) that reported the first evidence of PDR built on excited states with non-1- spin-parity in $^{80}$Ge via beta-decay. The finding that the PDR appears only within a specific temperature range (0.6-0.7 MeV) suggests a violation of the Brink-Axel hypothesis in the PDR region, consistent with recent experimental studies on lighter nuclei such as $^{22-27}$Ne and heavier nuclei like $^{208}$Pb.
