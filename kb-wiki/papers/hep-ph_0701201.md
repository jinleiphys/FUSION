# String theory and quark-gluon plasma

> 12 pages, 12 figures, ReVTeX4; Based on invited talks presented at "Frontiers in the physics of quark-gluon plasma" (July 7-8 2006, RIKEN), "String theory and quantum field theory" (Sep. 12-16 2006, YITP), the Fall Meeting 2006 of the Physical Society of Japan (Sep. 20-22 2006, Nara Women's Univ.), "AdS/CFT and strongly coupled quark matter" (Nov. 21-22 2006, CCAST), YKIS2006 (Nov. 20-28 2006, YITP), and a string theory workshop at Rikkyo Univ. (Dec. 26-27 2006)

## Key claim

The AdS/CFT duality predicts a universal lower bound for the shear viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio of gauge theory plasmas at strong coupling, \(\eta/s = \hbar/(4\pi k_B)\), which is very close to the value inferred from RHIC experiments for the quark-gluon plasma, even though the duality applies to supersymmetric gauge theories rather than real QCD.

## Method

The author reviews how the AdS/CFT correspondence maps finite-temperature \({\cal N}=4\) super-Yang-Mills theory to type IIB string theory on a Schwarzschild-AdS\(_5\) black hole times \(S^5\). The shear viscosity is computed from the black hole absorption cross section for a graviton of specific polarization, using the Kubo formula and the relation between the cross section and the horizon area. The result \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) is then shown to be universal across many gravity duals, including nonconformal theories, charged black holes (finite chemical potential), and theories with fundamental matter.

## Key numbers

* \(\eta/s = \hbar/(4\pi k_B)\) for gauge theory plasmas with gravity duals at large 't Hooft coupling (strong coupling limit).
* For water under normal circumstances, \(\eta/s \approx 3 \times 10^3\) (in units of \(\hbar/k_B\)), compared to the AdS/CFT value.
* RHIC estimates give \(\eta/s \sim O(0.1) \times \hbar/k_B\), close to the AdS/CFT bound.
* The finite-coupling correction for \({\cal N}=4\) SYM at \(\lambda = 6\pi\) (or \(\alpha_{\rm SYM}=1/2\)) raises \(\eta/s\) by about 9%.

## Context

This review extends the AdS/CFT approach to quark-gluon plasma signatures originally discussed in heavy-ion experiments at RHIC, covering the low shear viscosity, jet quenching, and \(J/\psi\) suppression. The universality of \(\eta/s\) was first computed for the D3-brane by Policastro (2001) and later proven for nonconformal theories by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004) and others; the extension to finite chemical potential was checked by Mas (2006) and Maeda (2006). The work enables the use of black hole physics to compute strongly coupled plasma properties that perturbative QCD cannot reliably address at RHIC temperatures.
