# Cold asymmetrical fermion superfluids in nonperturbative renormalisation group

> The application of the nonperturbative renormalisation group approach to a system with two fermion species is studied. Assuming a simple ansatz for the effective action with effective bosons, describing pairing effects we derive a set of approximate flow equations for the effective coupling including boson and fermionic fluctuations. The case of two fermions with different masses but coinciding Fermi surfaces is considered. The phase transition to a phase with broken symmetry is found at a critical value of the running scale. The large mass difference is found to disfavour the formation of pairs. The mean-field results are recovered if the effects of boson loops are omitted. While the boson fluctuation effects were found to be negligible for large values of $p_{F} a$ they become increasingly important with decreasing $p_{F} a$ thus making the mean field description less accurate.

# Cold asymmetrical fermion superfluids in nonperturbative renormalisation group

## Key claim

The nonperturbative renormalisation group (NRG) approach shows that boson fluctuation corrections to the mean-field approximation for asymmetrical fermion superfluids are negligible at large values of the dimensionless interaction parameter \(p_F a\), but become significant (up to approximately 30%) when \(p_F a \sim 1\) and the mass asymmetry is large (\(M_b > 5 M_a\)), making the mean-field description inadequate in that regime.

## Method

The author constructs an effective average action for a nonrelativistic two-fermion system at zero temperature, introducing a boson field to describe pairing, and derives approximate flow equations for the effective coupling that include both fermionic and bosonic fluctuations. The evolution equations are solved numerically using a sharp cutoff regulator, with the running of the chemical potential, effective potential parameters, and boson wave-function renormalisation, while keeping the fermion densities fixed. The case of two fermion species with different masses but coinciding Fermi surfaces is considered, and the gap is computed as a function of mass asymmetry and the parameter \(p_F a\).

## Key numbers

- For the unitary limit (\(a = -\infty\)) with Fermi momentum \(p_F = 1.37 \, \text{fm}^{-1}\), boson loop corrections to the gap are only \(2-4\%\) in the broken phase, with even smaller corrections for the chemical potential.
- For maximal mass asymmetry \(M_b = 10 M_a\) and \(p_F a \sim 1\), boson loop corrections to the gap become significant at approximately \(30\%\).
- For \(M_b = 5 M_a\) and \(p_F a \sim 1\), boson fluctuation effects reach approximately \(10\%\).
- The critical scale for the phase transition to the gapped phase does not depend on the mass asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the NRG approach previously applied to the standard pairing problem with one type of fermion (Krippa, 2004; 2005) to the case of two fermion species with different masses. It builds on mean-field studies of asymmetric fermion systems, including the LOFF phase (Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1965), breached-pair superfluidity (Wilczek, 1990), and mixed phases (Bedaque, 2003), which were all performed within the mean-field approximation. The results enable future studies of systems with mismatched Fermi surfaces, including the stability of Sarma, mixed, and LOFF phases beyond mean field, and applications to trapped fermionic atoms and strongly interacting quark matter.
