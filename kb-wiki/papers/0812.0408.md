# Nuclear constraints on gravitational waves from rapidly rotating neutron stars

> Gravitational waves are tiny disturbances in space-time and are a fundamental, although not yet directly confirmed, prediction of General Relativity. Rapidly rotating neutron stars are one of the possible sources of gravitational radiation dependent upon pulsar's rotational frequency, details of the equation of state of stellar matter, and distance to detector. Applying an equation of state with symmetry energy constrained by recent nuclear laboratory data, we set an upper limit on the strain-amplitude of gravitational waves emitted by rapidly rotating neutron stars.

## Key claim

Applying an equation of state with symmetry energy constrained by recent nuclear laboratory data (specifically the MDI EOS with parameter x between -1 and 0, consistent with isospin-diffusion data and neutron skin measurements), the authors set an upper limit on the strain-amplitude of gravitational waves from rapidly rotating neutron stars, under the assumption that the observed spin-down is entirely due to gravitational radiation or that the ellipticity takes its maximum plausible value of 10^{-6}.

## Method

The authors used the RNS code to compute the principal moment of inertia for neutron star models rotating at 641, 716, and 1122 Hz, employing several nucleonic equations of state (MDI with x=0 and x=-1, APR, and DBHF+Bonn B). For PSR B1937+21 (which has a measured spin-down rate), the gravitational wave strain amplitude was calculated via the spin-down formula h_0^{sd} = (5/2)(G I_{zz} |\dot{\nu}| / c^3 r^2 \nu)^{1/2}. For PSR J1748-2446ad and XTE J1739-285 (which lack measured spin-down rates), the strain amplitude was calculated via h_0 = (16\pi^2 G / c^4)(\epsilon I_{zz} \nu^2 / r) assuming a fixed maximum ellipticity of \epsilon = 10^{-6}.

## Key numbers

- For PSR B1937+21 (641.93 Hz, distance 3.60 kpc), the upper limit on gravitational wave strain amplitude for 1.4 M_sun models is h_0^{sd} = [2.24 - 2.61] x 10^{-27}, with corresponding ellipticity \epsilon^{sd} = [2.68 - 3.12] x 10^{-9}.
- For PSR J1748-2446ad (716.35 Hz, distance 8.70 kpc), assuming \epsilon = 10^{-6} and mass ~1.4 M_sun, the strain amplitude is h_0 = [3.81 - 5.36] x 10^{-25}.
- For XTE J1739-285 (1122.00 Hz, distance 10.60 kpc), assuming \epsilon = 10^{-6} and mass in the range M = [1.69 - 2.07] M_sun, the absolute upper bound on strain amplitude is h_0 = [1.17 - 1.74] x 10^{-24}.
- The spin-down energy loss rate for PSR B1937+21 is \dot{E} ~ [0.6 - 3.1] x 10^{36} erg/s, depending on the equation of state.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on slowly rotating pulsars (Krastev, 2008) to rapidly rotating neutron stars, and applies the MDI equation of state previously constrained by nuclear laboratory data on isospin diffusion and neutron skin thickness (Li and Chen, 2005; Li and Steiner, 2005). The results provide the first direct nuclear constraint on gravitational waves from rapidly rotating neutron stars, and the predicted strain amplitudes for PSR J1748-2446ad and XTE J1739-285 (assuming maximum ellipticity) lie within the current detection range of LIGO, though the authors note that the actual ellipticity could be significantly lower. The work enables future comparisons with LIGO and VIRGO observations, and highlights the need for more realistic calculations of neutron star ellipticity and the inclusion of exotic matter phases.
