# Magnetic Dipole Moment of SGRs and AXPs Described as Massive and Magnetic White Dwarfs

> The Anomalous X-ray Pulsars (AXPs) and Soft Gamma-ray Repeaters (SGRs) are some of the most interesting groups of pulsars that have been intensively studied in the recent years. They are understood as neutron stars (NSs) with super strong magnetic fields, namely $B\gtrsim10^{14}$ G. However, in the last two years two SGRs with low magnetic fields $B\sim(10^{12}-10^{13})$ G have been detected. Moreover, three fast and very {\it magnetic} white dwarfs (WDs) have also been observed in the last years. Based on these new pulsar discoveries, we compare and contrast the magnetic fields, magnetic dipole moment, characteristic ages, and X-ray steady luminosities of these two SGRs (in the WD model) with three fast white dwarfs, to conclude that they show strong similarities corroborating an alternative description of several SGRs/AXPs as very massive and magnetic white dwarfs. The pulsar magnetic dipole moment $m$ depending only on the momentum of inertia $I$, and observational properties, such as the period $P$ and its first time derivative $\dot{P}$, can help to identify the scale of $I$ for SGRs/AXPs. We analyze the pulsar magnetic dipole moment $m$ of SGRs and AXPs when a model based on a massive fast rotating highly magnetized white dwarf is considered. We show that the values for $m$ obtained for several SGRs and AXPs are in agreement with the observed range $10^{34}{\rm emu}\leq m \leq10^{36}{\rm emu}$ of isolated and polar magnetic white dwarfs. This result together with the fact that for {\it magnetic} white dwarfs $B\sim(10^6-10^8)$ G their magnetic dipole moments are almost independent of the star rotation period ($10^{4}\lesssim P \lesssim10^{6} {\rm s}$) - a phenomenology not shared by neutron stars pulsars - suggests a possible {\it magnetic} white dwarf nature for some of SGRs/AXPs that have much smaller periods ($P\sim 10$ s).

# Magnetic Dipole Moment of SGRs and AXPs Described as Massive and Magnetic White Dwarfs

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole moments of several SGRs and AXPs, when calculated using a white dwarf model with a moment of inertia of 1.26 × 10^50 g cm^2, fall within the observed range 10^34 emu ≤ m ≤ 10^36 emu of isolated and polar magnetic white dwarfs, suggesting that some SGRs/AXPs may be very massive and magnetic white dwarfs rather than neutron stars. This claim is conditional on the assumption that these sources have radii of approximately 3000 km and masses of 1.4 solar masses, consistent with fast and very massive white dwarfs.

## Method

The authors applied the standard magnetic dipole model for rotation-powered pulsars, equating the rotational energy loss rate to the magnetic dipole radiation power, to derive the magnetic dipole moment m = (3c^3 I P dot{P} / 8π^2)^(1/2) for each SGR and AXP. They used observed periods P and period derivatives dot{P} from the McGill SGR/AXP Online Catalog, adopting a white dwarf fiducial model with I = 1.26 × 10^50 g cm^2, R = 3000 km, and M = 1.4 solar masses, and compared the resulting m values to those of 82 magnetic white dwarfs selected from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) sample for which both period and magnetic field are known.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic dipole moment range for polar and isolated magnetic white dwarfs is 10^34 emu ≤ m ≤ 10^36 emu, based on 82 WDs from the SDSS sample with magnetic field strengths from 10^4 to 10^9 G.
- For the white dwarf model of SGRs/AXPs, the magnetic dipole moment is m_WD = 1.14 × 10^40 (P dot{P})^(1/2) emu, and the surface magnetic field is B_WD = 4.21 × 10^14 (P dot{P})^(1/2) G, assuming I = 1.26 × 10^50 g cm^2 and R = 3000 km.
- For a canonical neutron star model (M = 1.4 solar masses, R = 10 km, I ~ 10^45 g cm^2), the magnetic dipole moment is m_NS = 3.2 × 10^37 (P dot{P})^(1/2) emu, and the surface magnetic field is B_NS = 3.2 × 10^19 (P dot{P})^(1/2) G.
- The ratio of X-ray luminosities L_X^(SGRs/AXPs) / L_X^(XDINs) ~ m_WD / m_NS ~ 10^3, explaining why SGRs/AXPs have larger steady luminosities than X-ray Dim isolated neutron stars (XDINs) at similar rotational periods (P ~ 10 s).
- The two low-magnetic-field SGRs (SGR 0418+5729 and Swift J1822.3-1606) have characteristic ages τ ~ (10^6 - 10^7) years, older than other SGRs/AXPs with τ ~ (10^3 - 10^4) years.
- The rotational periods of SGRs/AXPs are in the range P ~ (2 - 12) s, with spin-down rates dot{P} ~ (10^-13 - 10^-10) s/s.
- Magnetic white dwarfs have rotational periods in the range 10^4 s ≤ P ≤ 10^6 s and magnetic fields B ~ (10^6 - 10^8) G, with magnetic dipole moments almost independent of rotation period.

## Context

This work extends the alternative white dwarf model for SGRs/AXPs proposed by Malheiro, Rueda and Ruffini (2012), following earlier suggestions by Morini et al. (1988) and Paczynski (1990). It directly challenges the standard magnetar model developed by Duncan and Thompson, which interprets SGRs/AXPs as neutron stars with super-strong magnetic fields B ≥ 10^14 G. The paper systematically compares the magnetic dipole moments of SGRs/AXPs as white dwarfs to those of 82 magnetic white dwarfs from the SDSS catalog (Kawka), showing for the first time that SGRs/AXPs share the same magnetic dipole moment range as magnetic WDs despite having much shorter periods (P ~ 10 s versus 10^4 - 10^6 s). This result enables future observational tests to distinguish between the neutron star and white dwarf interpretations, particularly for the two low-magnetic-field SGRs (SGR 0418+5729 and Swift J1822.3-1606) that challenge the magnetar model.
