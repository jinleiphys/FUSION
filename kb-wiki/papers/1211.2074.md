# Unfolding of event-by-event net-charge distributions in heavy-ion collision

> We discuss a method to obtain the true event-by-event net-charge multiplicity distributions from a corresponding measured distribution which is subjected to detector effects such as finite particle counting efficiency. The approach is based on the Bayes method for unfolding of distributions. We are able to faithfully unfold back the measured distributions to match with their corresponding true distributions obtained for a widely varying underlying particle production mechanism, beam energy and collision centrality. Particularly the mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis, their products and ratios of net-charge distributions from the event generators are shown to be successfully unfolded from the measured distributions constructed to mimic a real experimental distribution. We demonstrate the necessity to account for detector effects before associating the higher moments of net-charge distributions with physical quantities or phenomena. The advantage of this approach being that one need not construct new observable to cancel out detector effects which loose their ability to be connected to physical quantities calculable in standard theories.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Bayes unfolding method can faithfully recover the true event-by-event net-charge multiplicity distributions from measured distributions that are distorted by detector inefficiencies, but only if the detector response can be satisfactorily modeled and the statistics are large enough.

## Method

The authors generated 5 million Au+Au collision events per centrality bin using HIJING at 19.6 GeV and THERMINATOR at 200 GeV, constructing true net-charge distributions within pseudorapidity -0.5 < eta < 0.5 and transverse momentum 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c. They simulated measured distributions by smearing the true positive and negative charge multiplicities event-by-event with a Gaussian function having mean corresponding to 65% efficiency and width 10% of the mean, then applied the iterative Bayes unfolding algorithm from the RooUnfold package with 4 iterations to recover the true distributions from the measured ones.

## Key numbers

- Average charged particle counting efficiency taken as 65%, following the pT-dependent efficiency for charged pions from STAR experiment (Abelev 2008)
- Gaussian smearing width for efficiency variation set to 10% of the mean (0.065 for mean 0.65)
- 5 million events generated per centrality bin for each event generator (HIJING at 19.6 GeV, THERMINATOR at 200 GeV)
- 2.5 million events used for training the response matrix, 2.5 million for unfolding
- Optimal regularization parameter (number of iterations) set to 4

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals relating higher moments of conserved quantity distributions to correlation length and susceptibilities (Stephanov 2008, Bazavov 2012, Cheng 2008), and addresses the problem that experimentally measured event-by-event distributions are typically presented without corrections for detector efficiency (Aggarwal 2010, Appelshauser 1999). The method provides an alternative to constructing factorial moments or other observables that cancel detector effects but become complicated functions of lower-order moments, making physical interpretation difficult (Mrowczynski 1999, Voloshin 1999, Pruneau 2002). The approach enables direct comparison of unfolded moments to theoretical calculations of susceptibilities and correlation lengths, though it requires realistic modeling of detector response and works best for high-multiplicity, high-statistics datasets.
