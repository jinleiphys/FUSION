# Short Distance Repulsion Among Baryons

> We review recent investigations on the short distance behaviors of potentials among baryons, which are formulated through the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function. After explaining the method to define the potentials, we analyze the short distance behavior of the NBS wave functions and the corresponding potentials by combining the operator product expansion and a renormalization group analysis in the perturbation theory of QCD. These analytic results are compared with numerical results obtained in lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that short-distance repulsion among baryons can be derived analytically from perturbative QCD using the operator product expansion (OPE) and renormalization group analysis, but the sign of the potential in certain channels (such as the 2-nucleon case) cannot be determined by perturbation theory alone and requires non-perturbative input or model-dependent reasoning.

## Method

The authors define baryon-baryon potentials through the equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function in the HAL QCD scheme, then analyze the short-distance behavior of these potentials by applying the operator product expansion to the product of two (or three) baryon interpolating operators. They compute the one-loop anomalous dimensions of local gauge-invariant 6-quark and 9-quark operators, diagonalize the mixing matrices, and determine which operators dominate the OPE at short distances. The analytic predictions are compared with numerical results from lattice QCD simulations using both conventional and time-dependent extraction methods.

## Key numbers

* For 2-nucleon potentials, the short-distance behavior scales as \( V(\bx) \sim R \, \bar{g}^{2(1-\beta')}(|\bx|) / \bx^2 \), where \(\beta' < 0\) corresponds to subleading operators and \(R\) is a non-perturbative ratio of matrix elements.
* In the 3-nucleon case, all operators satisfy \(\gamma_A < 3\gamma_N\), leading to a total local potential that behaves as \(\mathcal{V}_{3N}(\br,\brho) \sim \bar{g}^2(s)/s^2\) at short distances, with \(s = \sqrt{\br^2 + \brho^2}\).
* For 2-baryon interactions in the flavor SU(3) limit, lattice simulations at pseudo-scalar meson mass \(M_{\rm PS} = 469(1)\) MeV and lattice spacing \(a = 0.121(2)\) fm show that the central potential in the singlet representation exhibits attraction at short distance, while the \(8_s\) representation shows the strongest repulsion among all six irreducible representations.
* In 2+1 flavor QCD at \(m_\pi = 701\) MeV with lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.091\) fm on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice, the central potentials for both spin-singlet and spin-triplet sectors have repulsive cores, with the repulsion significantly enhanced compared to quenched QCD at \(m_\pi = 731\) MeV and \(a \simeq 0.137\) fm on a \(32^4\) lattice.

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii et al., 2006; Aoki et al., 2008, 2009) for defining nuclear potentials from QCD to include analytic predictions for short-distance behavior via OPE and renormalization group analysis. The results confirm the existence of repulsive cores in nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials, which are essential for explaining the stability of nuclei and the existence of neutron stars as heavy as two solar masses (Demorest et al., 2010). The analysis enables future lattice QCD studies to test whether the predicted divergent short-distance behavior emerges in the continuum limit, and provides a framework for understanding which baryon-baryon channels exhibit attraction versus repulsion based on the anomalous dimensions of the contributing operators.
