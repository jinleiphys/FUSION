# Testing T Invariance in the Interaction of Slow Neutrons with Aligned Nuclei

> The study of five-fold (P even, T odd) correlation in the interaction of slow polarized neutrons with aligned nuclei is a possible way of testing the time reversal invariance due to the expected enhancement of T violating effects in compound resonances. Possible nuclear targets are discussed which can be aligned both dynamically as well as by the "brute force" method at low temperature. A statistical estimation is performed of the five-fold correlation for low lying p wave compound resonances of the $^{121}$Sb, $^{123}$Sb and $^{127}$I nuclei. It is shown that a significant improvement can be achieved for the bound on the intensity of the fundamental parity conserving time violating (PCTV) interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that measuring the five-fold (P even, T odd) correlation in the interaction of slow polarized neutrons with aligned nuclei can improve the bound on the parity-conserving time-violating (PCTV) interaction, provided that the current bound of \(\lambda_T < 10^{-4}\) from direct searches is used rather than the stricter bound from electric dipole moment data, which depends on model assumptions ("Scenario A") that may not hold.

## Method

The authors perform a statistical estimation of the five-fold correlation \(\Delta\sigma_{PCTV}\) for low-lying p-wave compound resonances of \(^{121}\)Sb, \(^{123}\)Sb, and \(^{127}\)I, using a model where the PCTV interaction mixes p-wave resonances. They derive the root-mean-square value \(\bar p_T\) of the effect in a given resonance, assuming Gaussian random matrix elements for the PCTV interaction and independent Gaussian random neutron amplitudes for the p-wave partial widths. The estimation uses known resonance parameters (positions, neutron widths) from prior parity-violation studies and sums over all known p-wave resonances with appropriate spin-weight factors.

## Key numbers

* The current bound on the PCTV interaction from direct searches is \(\lambda_T < 10^{-4}\), while the bound from EDM data under "Scenario A" is \(\lambda_T < 10^{-8}\).
* The root-mean-square matrix element of the PCTV interaction between compound states, \(v_T\), should be less than 1 eV based on the bound on the single-particle matrix element; the paper suggests future experiments should focus on \(v_T \le 100\) meV.
* The root-mean-square deformation effect \(\bar p_D\) is calculated as a function of nuclear spin \(I\); for \(I = 3/2\) it is 0.296, for \(I = 5/2\) it is 0.395, and for \(I = 7/2\) it is 0.452.
* For the \(^{127}\)I nucleus, there are at least two pairs of p-wave resonances separated by about 1 eV: 52.20 eV and 53.82 eV, and 352.0 eV and 353.3 eV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bunakov and collaborators (1982, 1988, 1990) on using compound-nucleus enhancement to test time-reversal invariance, and directly builds on the list of p-wave resonances compiled from parity-violation studies reviewed by Mitchell et al. (2001). The paper proposes that the same experimental conditions used for measuring parity-violating asymmetries in p-wave resonances can be adapted to search for the five-fold correlation, with comparable statistical accuracy. It enables a new generation of direct T-invariance tests using slow neutrons, particularly at high-intensity spallation sources like the SNS (USA) and JSS (Japan), and notes that the bound on the PCTV interaction could be improved or the effect discovered.
