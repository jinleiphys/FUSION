# Measurement of directed flow of $D^{0}$ and $\bar{D^{0}}$ mesons in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC using the STAR detector

> Charm quarks, owing to their large mass, are produced predominantly in the initial hard scatterings in heavy-ion collisions, and therefore can be a valuable tool for studying the early time dynamics of these collisions. The rapidity-odd directed flow at mid-rapidity in heavy-ion collisions originates from a tilt in the reaction plane of the thermalized medium caused by the asymmetry between the number of participants from projectile and target nuclei as a function of rapidity. Recently, it has been predicted that the slope of the directed flow at mid-rapidity for $D^0$ mesons, arising from the transport of charm quarks in the tilted medium, can be several times larger than that of light flavor hadrons. The magnitude of the slope is expected to be sensitive to the magnitude of the tilt and the charm quark drag coefficient in the medium. It has also been predicted that the transient electromagnetic field generated at early time can induce a much larger directed flow for heavy quarks than for light quarks. In these proceedings, we will report on the first measurement of the directed flow for the $D^0$ and $\bar{D}^0$ mesons as a function of rapidity in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 200 GeV using high statistics data collected with the Heavy Flavor Tracker in 2014 and 2016 RHIC runs.

## Key claim
The directed flow slope of D⁰ mesons in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions is measured to be about 20 times larger than that of kaons, with a 3σ significance, indicating strong interaction of charm quarks with the initially tilted source; however, the current precision is insufficient to draw firm conclusions about the splitting between D⁰ and anti-D⁰ mesons induced by the initial electromagnetic field.

## Method
D⁰ and anti-D⁰ mesons were reconstructed via their hadronic decay channel D⁰ → K⁻π⁺ (and charge conjugate) using the STAR Time Projection Chamber, Heavy Flavor Tracker, and Time of Flight detector from approximately 2 billion minimum-bias events collected in 2014 and 2016. The first-order event plane was reconstructed using the Zero Degree Calorimeter Shower Maximum Detectors at |η| > 6.3 to reduce non-flow effects. The directed flow v₁ was calculated using the event plane method, with the D⁰ yield obtained by integrating the invariant mass distribution of unlike-sign Kπ pairs and subtracting combinatorial background, then fitting the yield as a function of φ−ψ₁.

## Key numbers
- The combined D⁰ and anti-D⁰ dv₁/dy slope is −0.08 ± 0.02 (stat.) ± 0.02 (syst.) in 10-80% central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV for pT > 1.5 GeV/c
- The D⁰ v₁ slope is approximately 20 times larger than that of charged kaons, with a 3σ level of significance
- The measured Δv₁ slope between D⁰ and anti-D⁰ is −0.041 ± 0.041 (stat.) ± 0.020 (syst.), consistent with zero
- The D⁰ decay channel has cτ ≈ 122.9 μm and branching ratio of 3.89 ± 0.04%

## Context
This work presents the first measurement of directed flow for D⁰ and anti-D⁰ mesons in heavy-ion collisions, extending prior studies of light-flavor hadron v₁ at RHIC energies. The results are compared to a hydrodynamic model incorporating Langevin dynamics for heavy quarks within a tilted bulk medium (Chatterjee et al.) and predictions for electromagnetic field effects (Das et al., Gursoy et al.). The measurement constrains model parameters such as the tilt magnitude and charm quark drag coefficients, and enables future studies of the early-time electromagnetic field in heavy-ion collisions with higher statistics.
