# Color superconductivity and charge neutrality in Yukawa theory

> It is generally believed that when Cooper pairing occurs between two different species of fermions, their Fermi surfaces become locked together so that the resultant state remains 'neutral', with equal number densities of the two species, even when subjected to a chemical potential that couples to the difference in number densities. This belief is based on mean-field calculations in models with a zero-range interaction, where the anomalous self-energy is independent of energy and momentum. Following up on an early report of a deviation from neutrality in a Dyson-Schwinger calculation of color-flavor-locked quark matter, we investigate the neutrality of a two-species condensate using a Yukawa model which has a finite-range interaction. In a mean field calculation we obtain the full energy-momentum dependence of the self energy and find that the energy dependence leads to a population imbalance in the Cooper-paired phase when it is stressed by a species-dependent chemical potential. This gives some support to the suggestion that the color-flavor-locked phase of quark matter might not be an insulator.

# Color superconductivity and charge neutrality in Yukawa theory

## Key claim

Cooper pairing between two fermion species does not guarantee equal number densities when the interaction has finite range, contrary to the long-held belief based on zero-range (NJL) models. The energy dependence of the anomalous self-energy in Yukawa theory leads to a nonzero charge imbalance susceptibility, meaning the paired phase can sustain a population imbalance under a species-dependent chemical potential.

## Method

The authors performed a mean-field calculation of a two-flavor, two-taste Yukawa model with a massive scalar boson mediator, obtaining the full energy-momentum dependent anomalous self-energy by solving the gap equation iteratively on a discretized grid. They then computed the charge imbalance susceptibility from the free energy derivative, identifying the key role of the factor \(k_4 \partial \Delta / \partial k_4\) in the susceptibility integral. The numerical solutions used Gauss-Legendre quadrature on grids up to 512×256 points, with cutoffs in the range \(10^4\) to \(10^{10}\) MeV, and were performed on a graphics processing unit.

## Key numbers

- Charge imbalance susceptibility \(\chi_\delta = dN_\delta/d\mu_\delta|_{\mu_\delta=0}\) ranges from 1919.7 to 3470.5 (dimensionless) across nine parameter sets, all positive, at fermion chemical potential \(\mu = 350\) MeV.
- Yukawa boson masses studied: \(m = 25, 50, 75\) MeV, with couplings \(g\) tuned to produce spectral gaps of approximately 50, 75, and 100 MeV.
- For the case \(m = 50\) MeV, \(g = 4.871645\), and spectral gap \(\approx 50\) MeV, the susceptibility is \(\chi_\delta = 2174.6\).
- At large \(k_4\), the anomalous self-energy \(\Delta \propto 1/k_4^2\); at large 3-momentum \(|\vec{k}|\), it drops roughly as \(1/|\vec{k}|^{1.7}\).
- The anomalous self-energy reaches a maximum at \(|\vec{k}| \approx \mu\) (350 MeV).

## Context

This work extends and clarifies the earlier Dyson-Schwinger study of color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter by Nickel (2008), which found electrons present in the CFL phase contrary to NJL-based predictions. It directly contradicts the standard result from Rajagopal and Wilczek (2000) that Fermi surfaces become rigidly locked in NJL-type models, where the anomalous self-energy is energy-independent. The result supports the suggestion that the CFL phase of quark matter may not be an electrical insulator, with major implications for neutron star phenomenology: a nonzero electron population would dominate specific heat, thermal conductivity, and magnetic field evolution. The work also relates to very recent calculations by Sedrakian et al. (2017) who studied energy dependence in a two-flavor model with sigma and pi mesons but did not compute charge imbalance.
