# Symmetry restoration in the mean-field description of proton-neutron pairing

> We show that the symmetry-restored paired mean-field states (quasiparticle vacua) properly account for isoscalar versus isovector nuclear pairing properties. Full particle-number, spin, and isospin symmetries are restored in a simple SO(8) proton-neutron pairing model, and prospects to implement a similar approach in a realistic setting are delineated. Our results show that, provided all symmetries are restored, the pictures based on pair-condensate and quartet-condensate wave functions represent equivalent ways of looking at the physics of nuclear proton-neutron pairing.

## Key claim

The authors show that symmetry-restored paired mean-field states (quasiparticle vacua) properly account for isoscalar versus isovector nuclear pairing properties, but only when full particle-number, spin, and isospin symmetries are restored simultaneously; without such restoration, the mean-field approach fails to describe the coexistence of both pairing types.

## Method

The authors implement a variation-after-projection (VAP) method within the SO(8) proton-neutron pairing model, performing a complete seven-dimensional integration to restore particle-number, spin, and isospin symmetries in Thouless states. The Thouless pair is parametrized by two angles controlling the relative amplitude and phase between isovector and isoscalar pairs, and the projected energies are minimized over these angles. Results are compared with exact solutions obtained by group-theory diagonalization of the SO(8) Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For the SO(8) model with Ω=12, projection on A=24 and T=S=0, the VAP energies at x=0 are precise up to 1.5% relative error.
- The VAP results for A=4 and A=6 are exact for all values of the mixing parameter x (precise up to numerical accuracy).
- The Hamiltonian mixing parameter x ranges from -1 (pure isovector) to +1 (pure isoscalar), with the interval -1<x<1 showing competition between pairing types.
- For the unprojected states at x=0, the HFB energy is entirely independent of the mixing angle α, with states of any isovector-isoscalar mixing exactly degenerate.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field description of proton-neutron pairing beyond earlier Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) studies (Goodman 1999, Satula 2001, Gloaguen 2004, Gezerlis 2011) that did not include full symmetry restoration. The authors show that previous conclusions about the inferiority of pair-condensate models compared to quartet-condensation models (Sandulescu 2015, 2018) were due to the lack of full VAP symmetry restoration, not the pair-condensate approximation itself. The results enable future implementation of full VAP calculations with realistic density functionals, building on existing particle-number restoration methods (Sheikh 2000, Stoitcheva 2007) and rotational/isospin restoration without pairing (Satula 2012).
