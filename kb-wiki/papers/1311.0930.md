# Limits on Lorentz and CPT violation from double beta decay

> Deviations from Lorentz and CPT invariance in the neutrino sector and their observable effects in double beta decay are studied. For two-neutrino double beta decay, a spectral distortion and its properties are characterized for different isotopes. Majorana couplings for Lorentz violation are studied and shown to trigger neutrinoless double beta decay even for negligible Majorana mass. Existing data are used to obtain first limits of $5 \times 10^{-9}$ for 18 individual coefficients and attainable sensitivities in current and future experiments are presented.

# Limits on Lorentz and CPT violation from double beta decay

## Key claim
Existing data from neutrinoless double beta decay experiments can be used to set first limits on 18 individual coefficients for Lorentz and CPT violation in the neutrino sector at the $5 \times 10^{-9}$ level, under the assumption that only one coefficient is nonzero at a time and using the lowest value of the corresponding nuclear matrix element.

## Method
The paper studies Lorentz-violating effects in both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double beta decay within the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework. For two-neutrino decay, the isotropic coefficient $\ring{a}_\text{of}^{(3)}$ produces a distortion of the electron sum spectrum characterized by a well-defined energy dependence. For neutrinoless decay, the Majorana-type coefficient $g^{(4)\lambda\rho\sigma}_{Ma'\bar{a}'}$ triggers the decay even with negligible Majorana mass, producing unique electron angular correlations that depend on sidereal time and laboratory location. Existing experimental half-life limits are reinterpreted as bounds on the effective coefficients $|\langle g^{\lambda\rho}\rangle|$.

## Key numbers
- First limits on 18 individual effective coefficients $|\langle g^{\lambda\rho K}_{\beta\beta}\rangle| < 5 \times 10^{-9}$ at 90% C.L., derived from the most stringent experimental bound (KamLAND-Zen on $^{136}$Xe)
- Best current estimated upper limit on $|\langle g^{\lambda\rho}\rangle|$: $3 \times 10^{-9}$ from KamLAND-Zen ($^{136}$Xe)
- Best current estimated upper limit on $|\langle g^{\lambda\rho}\rangle|$: $4 \times 10^{-9}$ from GERDA ($^{76}$Ge)
- Projected sensitivity for $^{76}$Ge: $4 \times 10^{-10}$ (GERDA Phase II and MAJORANA)
- Projected sensitivity for $^{136}$Xe: $4 \times 10^{-10}$ (nEXO)
- Projected sensitivity for $^{130}$Te: $8 \times 10^{-10}$ (SNO+)
- Energy of maximal Lorentz-violating modification $K_m$ for $^{48}$Ca: 1.98 MeV; for $^{76}$Ge: 0.81 MeV; for $^{136}$Xe: 1.02 MeV
- Parameter $k_\theta$ for angular correlations: ranges from 0.81 ($^{76}$Ge) to 0.93 ($^{48}$Ca)

## Context
This work extends the SME framework for Lorentz violation in the neutrino sector, previously applied to neutrino oscillations (Kostelecky and Mewes, 2004) and single beta decay (Diaz, Kostelecky, and Lehnert, 2013), to double beta decay experiments. It provides the first limits on countershaded SME coefficients that are inaccessible to neutrino oscillation experiments. The results enable future double beta decay experiments (GERDA Phase II, MAJORANA, nEXO, CUORE, SNO+) to improve sensitivity by at least one order of magnitude and, if neutrinoless double beta decay is observed, to distinguish Lorentz-violating from Majorana-mass mechanisms through the dependence on nuclear radius.
