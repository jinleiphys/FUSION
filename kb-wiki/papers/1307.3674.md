# Medium modification of photon-tagged and inclusive jets in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions

> We study modification of the photon-tagged and inclusive jets in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV due to mini-quark-gluon plasma which can be produced in high multiplicity events. We show that for underlying events with $dN_{ch}/dη\sim 20-60$ the medium effects lead to a considerable modification of the photon-tagged and inclusive jet fragmentation functions. For inclusive jets the magnitude of the effect is surprisingly large. The effect is quite strong even for typical underlying events. We find that at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV the spectrum of charged hadrons is suppressed by $\sim 35-40$ % at $p_{T}\sim 5-10$ GeV.

## Key claim

Assuming a mini-quark-gluon plasma (QGP) fireball can be created in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the medium effects lead to a considerable modification of both photon-tagged and inclusive jet fragmentation functions, with the effect being surprisingly large for inclusive jets and quite strong even for typical underlying events.

## Method

The author uses the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, treating parton energy loss within a scheme developed previously for AA collisions that accounts for both radiative and collisional energy loss. The QGP evolution is modeled using 1+1D Bjorken expansion with thermalization time τ0 = 0.5 fm, and the initial temperature T0 is fixed using the entropy/multiplicity ratio from the literature. The fireball radius Rf is taken from IP-Glasma model simulations, and averaging over the fast parton path length L in the QGP is performed using the quark distribution from the MIT bag model.

## Key numbers

- For underlying events with dNch/dη = 20-60, the medium effects lead to considerable modification of photon-tagged and inclusive jet fragmentation functions
- At √s = 7 TeV, the spectrum of charged hadrons is suppressed by ∼35-40% at pT ∼ 5-10 GeV
- For dNch/dη = 40, the initial temperature T0 = 325 MeV, close to that for central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV
- For dNch/dη = 60, T0 = 372 MeV; for dNch/dη = 20, T0 = 258 MeV; for dNch/dη = 6, T0 = 196 MeV; for dNch/dη = 3, T0 = 177 MeV
- The fireball radius Rf = 1.538 fm for dNch/dη = 20, 40, and 60; Rf = 1.27 fm for dNch/dη = 6; Rf = 1.046 fm for dNch/dη = 3
- For γ-tagged jets at ET ∼ 25-100 GeV and z ∼ 0.5-0.8, the suppression is ∼10-40% for dNch/dη ∼ 20-40
- For typical underlying events with dNch/dη ∼ 14, the suppression at moderate z is ∼20-30%
- The medium modification factor Rpp ∼ 0.6-0.65 at pT ∼ 5-10 GeV for √s = 7 TeV
- For minimum bias multiplicity dNch/dη = 6.01, the suppression effect is still significant

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching from heavy-ion collisions to high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions, where a mini-QGP may be produced. The approach builds on the author's previous successful framework for explaining RAA results in AA collisions (Zakharov, 2011-2013) and follows suggestions by Wang (2012) for using γ+jet events to observe medium effects. The results suggest that the mini-QGP could produce observable jet modification in pp collisions, which would affect the interpretation of nuclear modification factors RpA and RAA, and may be relevant to understanding the ridge correlation structure observed by CMS in high-multiplicity pp events.
