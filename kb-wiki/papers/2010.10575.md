# Early-Stage Shear Viscosity far from Equilibrium via Holography

> Shear viscosity is a crucial property of QCD matter which determines the collective behavior of the the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. Extending the near-equilibrium, high-precision investigations in theory and experiment, we take into account the fact that, in a collision, the QGP is generated far from equilibrium. We use the AdS/CFT correspondence to study a strongly coupled plasma and find a significant impact on the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, $η/s$. In particular, we investigate the initial heating phase and find a decrease reaching down to below 60% followed by an overshoot to 110% of the near-equilibrium value. This finding might be highly relevant for the extraction of transport coefficients from anisotropic flow measurements at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that during the early, far-from-equilibrium heating phase of a heavy-ion collision, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s deviates significantly from its near-equilibrium value of 1/4π, dropping below 60% and then overshooting to 110% of that value. This result is obtained via holography and is conditional on modeling the heating phase as a rapid homogeneous energy deposition onto a strongly coupled plasma.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a strongly coupled plasma as a Reissner-Nordström Vaidya black brane in an asymptotically Anti-de Sitter spacetime. A sudden homogeneous energy deposition is implemented as a rapid mass accretion onto the black brane, parameterized by a hyperbolic tangent function. The time-dependent shear viscosity is extracted from the retarded Green's function for the shear channel, obtained by solving the linearized Einstein equations for metric perturbations using a pseudospectral method in space and a fourth-order Runge-Kutta scheme in time, followed by a Wigner transformation and the time-dependent Kubo formula.

## Key numbers

- η/s drops to below 60% of the near-equilibrium value (1/4π) during the far-from-equilibrium heating phase.
- η/s overshoots to 110% of the near-equilibrium value (1/4π) during the same phase.
- The near-equilibrium asymptotic value of η/s is 1/4π in natural units.
- The temperature profile modeled for RHIC conditions increases to 310 MeV within 0.3 fm/c, typical for collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Context

This work extends near-equilibrium calculations of η/s from lattice QCD (Astrakhantsev 2017) and functional renormalization group methods (Christiansen 2014) into the far-from-equilibrium regime relevant for the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. It builds on the holographic prediction of a small constant η/s = 1/4π by Kovtun (2004) and on time-dependent holographic methods developed by Chesler (2013) and Banerjee (2016). The results enable improved hydrodynamic simulations and more accurate extraction of transport coefficients from anisotropic flow measurements at RHIC and LHC, and suggest that comparable corrections may apply to near-equilibrium results from FRG and lattice QCD.
