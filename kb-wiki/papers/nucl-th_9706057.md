# Kaons in dense matter, kaon production in heavy-ion collisions, and kaon condensation in neutron stars

> The recent past witnesses the growing interdependence between the physics of hadrons, the physics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the physics of compact objects in astrophysics. A notable example is the kaon which plays special roles in all the three fields. In this paper, we first review the various theoretical investigations of kaon properties in nuclear medium, focusing on possible uncertainties in each model. We then present a detailed transport model study of kaon production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies. We shall discuss especially the elementary kaon and antikaon production cross sections in hadron-hadron interactions, that represent one of the most serious uncertainties in the transport model study of particle production in heavy-ion collisions. The main purpose of such a study is to constrain kaon in-medium properties from the heavy-ion data. This can provide useful guidances for the development of theoretical models of the kaon in medium. In the last part of the paper, we apply the kaon in-medium properties extracted from heavy-ion data to the study of neutron star properties. Based on a conventional equation of state of nuclear matter that can be considered as one of the best constrained by available experimental data on finite nuclei, we find that the maximum mass of neutron stars is about 2$M_\odot$, which is reduced to about 1.5$M_\odot$ once kaon condensation as constrained by heavy-ion data is introduced.

## Key claim

The paper argues that kaon in-medium properties extracted from heavy-ion collision data can constrain the maximum mass of neutron stars, finding that the maximum mass is reduced from about 2 solar masses to about 1.5 solar masses once kaon condensation is introduced, but this result depends on the specific choice of the nuclear equation of state and on phenomenological parameters \(a_K\) and \(a_{\bar K}\) that are fitted to heavy-ion data rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors first review chiral perturbation theory and meson-exchange models for kaon properties in nuclear matter, introducing two free parameters \(a_K\) and \(a_{\bar K}\) that determine the scalar attraction for \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) respectively. They then perform a transport model study of kaon production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (1-2 AGeV), using parameterizations of elementary kaon and antikaon production cross sections in hadron-hadron interactions, including pion-baryon and baryon-baryon collisions. Finally, they apply the kaon in-medium properties constrained by the heavy-ion data to calculate neutron star masses using the nuclear equation of state of Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot.

## Key numbers

* The maximum neutron star mass without kaon condensation is about 2.0 \(M_\odot\), based on the equation of state of Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot.
* The maximum neutron star mass with kaon condensation as constrained by heavy-ion data is about 1.5 \(M_\odot\).
* At normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), the \(K^+\) feels a repulsive potential of about 20 MeV, using \(a_K = 0.22\) GeV\(^2\)fm\(^3\).
* The antikaon mass drops by about 22% at normal nuclear matter density, using \(a_{\bar K} = 0.45\) GeV\(^2\)fm\(^3\) fitted to heavy-ion data.
* The parameter \(b_K = 3/(8f_\pi^2) \approx 0.333\) GeV fm\(^3\).
* The kaon-nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_{KN} \approx 400\) MeV is implied by \(a_K = 0.22\) GeV\(^2\)fm\(^3\).
* The pion-nucleon sigma term \(\Sigma_{\pi N}\) is about 45 MeV.
* The \(K^-\) optical potential from kaonic atom data is about 200 \(\pm\) 20 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
* The cross section for \(pp \rightarrow p\Lambda K^+\) at 2 MeV above threshold is about 8 nb, measured by the COSY-11 collaboration.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on kaon condensation in dense matter initiated by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and connects it to neutron star astrophysics as discussed by Brown and Bethe. The paper uses the nuclear equation of state of Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot, which is considered one of the best constrained by experimental data on finite nuclei. The study enables future work to refine kaon in-medium properties by comparing transport model predictions with heavy-ion data from the KaoS collaboration at GSI, and to apply these constraints to neutron star structure calculations, potentially resolving the discrepancy between observed neutron star masses (all less than 1.5 \(M_\odot\) according to a compilation by Thorsett quoted by Brown) and theoretical predictions of maximum masses above 2 \(M_\odot\).
