# Optimization of basis functions for the multi-configuration mixing using the Replica Exchange Monte-Carlo method and its application to $^{12}$C

> To calculate excited states in quantum many-body systems, multi-configuration mixing has often been employed. However, it has been still unclear how to choose important Slater determinants from a huge model space. We propose a novel efficient method as the Replica Exchange Monte-Carlo (RXMC) method to sample important Slater determinants and optimize and analyze the obtained results. As an application, we apply it to the ground and excited states of $^{12}$C based on the Bloch-Brink $α$ cluster model and show the detailed structure of the obtained states. The RXMC method enables us to efficiently sample Slater determinants following the Boltzmann distribution on the multi-dimensional potential energy surface (PES) under a given model space. To analyze the obtained excited states, we embed sampled basis functions onto the PES calculated with the $β$-$γ$ constraint method and discuss the main component in the state. The RXMC method can efficiently perform the samplings with a temperature parameter of $T_L=2.5$ MeV in $^{12}$C. We obtain the gas-like state with a wide density distribution in the tail part in the second $0^+$ state. We also obtain the linear-chain-like states with the bending and stretching vibrational modes in the third and fourth $0^+$ states, respectively. In the fifth $0^+$ state, the main component of the basis functions contains expanded equilateral triangle configurations. The second $0^+$ gas-like state emerges at the local minimum in the PES, which is the beginning of the valley structure connected to the linear-chain breakup channel. The third and fourth linear-chain-like states emerge in this valley structure. We conclude that the RXMC method is a powerful method to calculate the excited states of nuclei, which would be applied to much complicated nuclear fission dynamics in heavier nuclei.

## Key claim

The Replica Exchange Monte-Carlo (RXMC) method can efficiently sample important Slater determinants for multi-configuration mixing in nuclear many-body calculations, but its success depends on choosing an appropriate temperature parameter (here \(T_L=2.5\) MeV for \(^{12}\)C) and the method does not guarantee that all physically relevant configurations are sampled if the temperature is too low.

## Method

The authors apply the Bloch-Brink \(\alpha\) cluster model for \(^{12}\)C, describing the nucleus as three antisymmetrized \(\alpha\)-cluster Gaussian wave packets. They perform independent Markov-chain Monte-Carlo samplings at 24 different temperatures (with common ratio \(b=1.5\)), exchanging configurations between neighboring temperatures with a probability \(P_{\rm ex}\) to escape local minima. After burn-in of at least 5,000 steps, 5,000 basis functions are sampled at the target temperature, from which 500 are randomly extracted and then optimized to 100 basis functions that minimize the energies of the first five \(0^+\) states (20 bases per state).

## Key numbers

- The lowest energy minimum on the potential energy surface is at \(\beta=0.421\) and \(\gamma=27.5^{\circ}\) with \(E=-85.98\) MeV.
- A shallow local minimum appears at \(\beta=1.75\) and \(\gamma=5.0^{\circ}\) with \(E=-75.80\) MeV; its depth is 0.14 MeV relative to the inner saddle point at \(\beta=1.58\) and \(\gamma=5.0^{\circ}\) with \(E=-75.66\) MeV.
- The lowest-energy saddle point (equilateral triangular configuration) is at \(\beta=2.75\) and \(\gamma=32.5^{\circ}\) with \(E=-71.71\) MeV; its height from the ground state minimum is \(B_f^{\rm(tri)}=14.27\) MeV.
- With \(T_L=2.5\) MeV, the obtained energies of the first five \(0^+\) states are \(-87.96\) MeV, \(-81.37\) MeV, \(-78.95\) MeV, \(-78.11\) MeV, and \(-76.80\) MeV, corresponding to excitation energies \(E_{\rm ex}=6.59\), 9.01, 9.85, and 11.16 MeV.
- The three-\(\alpha\) threshold energy is \(-82.71\) MeV.
- The RXMC sampling uses a maximum radius \(r_{\rm max}=6.0\) fm and a size parameter \(b=1.46\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic sampling approach for the Bloch-Brink \(\alpha\) cluster model previously developed by the same authors (Ichikawa and Itagaki, 2011, 2012), which suffered from contamination by unphysical spurious states and many identical Slater determinants under rotational symmetry. The RXMC method overcomes the defect that random sampling produces many less-contributing determinants, and it is contrasted with the \(\beta\)-\(\gamma\) constraint method of Suhara and Kanada-En'yo (2010) and the Monte-Carlo shell model (MCSM). The method enables systematic study of cluster states in \(^{12}\)C, including the Hoyle state (second \(0^+\) gas-like state) and linear-chain configurations, and is intended for future application to nuclear fission dynamics in heavier nuclei.
