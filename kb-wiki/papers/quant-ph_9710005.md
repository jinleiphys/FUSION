# Spectral Properties of the Two-Dimensional Laplacian with a Finite Number of Point Interactions

> We discuss spectral properties of the Laplacian with multiple ($N$) point interactions in two-dimensional bounded regions. A mathematically sound formulation for the problem is given within the framework of the self-adjoint extension of a symmetric (Hermitian) operator in functional analysis. The eigenvalues of this system are obtained as the poles of a transition matrix which has size $N$. Closely examining a generic behavior of the eigenvalues of the transition matrix as a function of the energy, we deduce the general condition under which point interactions have a substantial effect on statistical properties of the spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that point interactions in a two-dimensional quantum billiard substantially affect the statistical properties of the spectrum only when the coupling strength satisfies a specific logarithmic condition relative to the energy; outside this narrow window, the spectrum remains essentially Poissonian regardless of the presence of point interactions.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem using self-adjoint extension theory for the Laplacian with point interactions in a bounded two-dimensional region, deriving the Green's function via Krein's formula. They introduce an approximation that replaces the squared wavefunction at the interaction point by its spatial average (1/S), allowing them to analytically estimate the condition for strong coupling. For multiple interactions, they develop a perturbative diagonalization procedure that reduces the N-dimensional transition matrix to a sequence of rank-one updates.

## Key numbers

- The average level density of the empty billiard is \(\rho_{av} = \frac{MS}{2\pi}\), independent of energy in two dimensions.
- For a single point interaction, the width of the strong coupling region in the inverse coupling parameter is \(\delta_{\bar{v}^{-1}} \simeq \frac{\pi M}{2}\), where \(M\) is the particle mass.
- The condition for substantial spectral effect from a point interaction with coupling strength \(\bar{v}\) is \(\left| \bar{v}^{-1} - \frac{M}{2\pi} \ln \frac{\omega}{\Lambda} \right| \lesssim \frac{\pi M}{4} \simeq M\).
- The maximal coupling condition is \(\bar{v}^{-1} \simeq \frac{M}{2\pi} \ln \frac{\omega}{\Lambda}\), where \(\Lambda\) is an arbitrary mass scale.
- The derivative of the regularized Green's function at the inflection point is \(|\bar{G}'(\tilde{\omega})| \simeq \pi^2 \langle \varphi_n(\vec{x}_1)^2 \rangle \rho_{av}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum billiards with point impurities, building on the self-adjoint extension framework of Akhiezer and Glazman (1988) and the deficiency index analysis of von Neumann (1932) and Reed and Simon (1975). The authors address the divergence problems that arise in two-dimensional delta-function potentials, which are not mathematically well-defined without regularization. The results enable systematic classification of when point impurities can induce chaotic level statistics (GOE-type level repulsion) in otherwise integrable billiards, as previously observed numerically by Shigehara (1994) for rectangular billiards. The condition derived here provides a criterion for designing quantum dot systems where impurities significantly alter spectral properties.
