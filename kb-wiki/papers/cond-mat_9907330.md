# Phase Transitions in a Two-Component Site-Bond Percolation Model

> A method to treat a N-component percolation model as effective one component model is presented by introducing a scaled control variable $p_{+}$. In Monte Carlo simulations on $16^{3}$, $32^{3}$, $64^{3}$ and $128^{3}$ simple cubic lattices the percolation threshold in terms of $p_{+}$ is determined for N=2. Phase transitions are reported in two limits for the bond existence probabilities $p_{=}$ and $p_{\neq}$. In the same limits, empirical formulas for the percolation threshold $p_{+}^{c}$ as function of one component-concentration, $f_{b}$, are proposed. In the limit $p_{=} = 0$ a new site percolation threshold, $f_{b}^{c} \simeq 0.145$, is reported.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a scaled control variable $p_{+}$ that reduces an N-component percolation model to an effective one-component model, but the reduction yields two distinct universal branches for the percolation threshold depending on whether both bond types are active or one is zero; in the limits where either $p_{=}$ or $p_{\neq}$ goes to zero, a first-order phase transition in the infinite-cluster probability $p_{\infty}$ occurs, not captured by the one-component analogy.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations were performed on simple cubic lattices of sizes $16^{3}$, $32^{3}$, $64^{3}$, and $128^{3}$ for a two-component site-bond percolation model with symmetric bond probabilities $p_{=} \equiv p_{bb} = p_{rr}$ and a distinct cross-species bond probability $p_{\neq}$. The lattice was populated randomly according to a fixed fraction of blue sites $f_{b}$, bonds were activated with probabilities $p_{=}$ and $p_{\neq}$, and the resulting cluster structure was analyzed using a cluster-finding algorithm to record $p_{\infty}$, the probability that a site belongs to the infinite network. The scaled control parameter $p_{+} = \alpha_{\neq} p_{\neq} + \alpha_{=} p_{=}$ was computed, where $\alpha_{\neq} = 2 f_{b} (1-f_{b})$ and $\alpha_{=} = 1 - \alpha_{\neq}$.

## Key numbers

* For $f_{b}=0.5$ with both $p_{=}$ and $p_{\neq}$ non-zero, the critical scaled control parameter is $p_{+}^{c} = 0.251 \pm 0.002$, matching the one-component bond percolation threshold on a simple cubic lattice.
* For $f_{b}=0.5$ in the limits $p_{=} \to 0$ or $p_{\neq} \to 0$, the critical scaled control parameter shifts to $p_{+}^{c} = 0.280 \pm 0.002$.
* The critical exponent $\beta = 0.41$ from one-component bond percolation theory applies to both branches.
* In the limit $p_{=} = 0$, a new site percolation threshold for the concentration of blue sites is found: $f_{b}^{c} = 0.145 \pm 0.001$, obtained from a fit to the scaling relation $|p_{+}^{c}(L) - p_{+}^{c}| \propto L^{-1/\nu}$ with $\nu = 0.88$.
* The critical bond probability $p_{\neq}^{c}(f_{b})$ in the limit $p_{=}=0$ is fit by $\varphi = a \exp(-d f_{b}) + c$ with $a = 2.1 \pm 0.07$, $d = 10.9 \pm 0.2$, and $c = 0.547 \pm 0.002$.

## Context

This work extends the polychromatic percolation framework of Zallen (1977) and the two-component site-bond percolation model previously applied to nuclear liquid-gas phase transitions by Bauer and collaborators (1997). The method of reducing multi-component percolation to a single control parameter $p_{+}$ generalizes the approach of Kortemeyer, Bauer, and Kunde (1997) to N components. The results enable future studies of multi-component systems on higher-dimensional or higher-connectivity lattices, with potential applications to gelation phenomena, wetting, and stock-market simulations.
