# Linking partial dynamical symmetry to nuclear energy density functionals

> We use self-consistent mean-field methods in combination with the interacting boson model (IBM) of nuclei, to establish a linkage between universal energy density functionals (EDFs) and partial dynamical symmetry (PDS). An application to $^{168}$Er shows that IBM Hamiltonians derived microscopically from known nonrelativistic and relativistic EDFs in this region, conform with SU(3)-PDS.

# Linking partial dynamical symmetry to nuclear energy density functionals

## Key claim
IBM Hamiltonians derived microscopically from both nonrelativistic and relativistic energy density functionals for $^{168}$Er produce eigenstates whose SU(3) decomposition and spectral properties resemble those of SU(3)-partial dynamical symmetry, provided the derived parameters satisfy conditions ($h_0 \neq h_2$, $\beta_0 \approx \sqrt{2}$) that are approximately met for most EDF choices except the SkP parameterization with the largest pairing strength.

## Method
Self-consistent mean-field calculations (Hartree-Fock plus BCS for nonrelativistic Skyrme EDFs SLy4 and SkP; relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov for DD-PC1 and DD-ME2 functionals) were performed with constrained quadrupole moments to generate microscopic energy surfaces for $^{168}$Er. The parameters $\{h_0, h_2, \beta_0\}$ of an interacting boson model Hamiltonian of the form $\hat{H} = h_0 P_0^\dagger(\beta_0)P_0(\beta_0) + h_2 P_2^\dagger(\beta_0)\cdot\tilde{P}_2(\beta_0) + \rho \hat{L}\cdot\hat{L}$ were determined by mapping the microscopic energy surface onto the IBM energy surface near the global minimum, with the rotational parameter $\rho$ obtained from the Thouless-Valatin cranking moment of inertia.

## Key numbers
- For the SU(3)-PDS phenomenological fit to $^{168}$Er: $h_0 = 8.0$ keV, $h_2 = 4.0$ keV, $\rho = 13$ keV, $\beta_0 = \sqrt{2} \approx 1.41$, with $N = 16$ bosons
- For the SLy4 EDF with pairing strength $V_0 = 1250$ MeV fm$^3$: $h_0 = 10.4$ keV, $h_2 = 4.0$ keV, $\rho = 12.3$ keV, $\beta_0 = 1.39$, giving $E(2_2) = 809$ keV and $E(0_2) = 1334$ keV
- For the DD-ME2 EDF with $V_0 = 837$ MeV fm$^3$: $h_0 = 9.9$ keV, $h_2 = 4.2$ keV, $\rho = 11.73$ keV, $\beta_0 = 1.50$, giving $E(2_2) = 883$ keV and $E(0_2) = 1499$ keV
- Experimental bandhead energies for $^{168}$Er: $E(2_2) = 821$ keV, $E(0_2) = 1217$ keV, ratio $R = 1.48$
- For the SkP EDF with $V_0 = 1000$ MeV fm$^3$: $h_0/h_2 = 6.95$, $\beta_0 = 0.99$, producing large SU(3) fragmentation
- SU(3)-PDS $\beta$ band decomposition: $(2N-4,2)$ 87.5%, $(2N-6,0)$ 9.6%, $(2N-8,4)$ 2.9% for $N=16$
- For SLy4 ($V_0=1250$) and SkP ($V_0=800$): ground and $\gamma$ band SU(3) purity at 99.8%

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological SU(3)-PDS studies of Leviatan (1996) and Leviatan and Sinai (1999) by providing a microscopic foundation through the nuclear energy density functional framework. It connects the algebraic interacting boson model approach (Iachello and Arima, 1987) with self-consistent mean-field methods (Bender et al., 2003; Vretenar et al., 2005) using the mapping procedure developed by Nomura et al. (2008, 2010, 2011). The results enable using observed PDS patterns to constrain and optimize energy density functionals, and predict partial symmetries in exotic nuclei not yet experimentally accessible.
