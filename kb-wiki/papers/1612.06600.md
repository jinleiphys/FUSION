# Recent progress on dense nuclear matter in skyrmion approaches

> The Skyrme model provides a novel unified approach to nuclear physics. In this approach, single baryon, baryonic matter and medium-modified hadron properties are treated on the same footing. Intrinsic density dependence (IDD) reflecting the change of vacuum by compressed baryonic matter figures naturally in the approach. In this article, we review the recent progress on accessing dense nuclear matter by putting baryons treated as solitons, namely, skyrmions, on crystal lattice with accents on the implications in compact stars.

## Key claim

The skyrmion crystal approach predicts a robust topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density around \(n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0\), which is not visible in other approaches; this change is not a bona-fide phase transition with an order parameter but is essential for the equation of state of compressed baryonic matter relevant for massive compact stars.

## Method

The authors review the method of placing skyrmions (topological solitons representing baryons) onto a face-centered-cubic (FCC) crystal lattice, then squeezing the crystal size to simulate increasing baryon density. The skyrmion configuration is decomposed into normalized fields \((\phi_0, \vec{\phi})\) with Fourier expansions, and the minimum energy per particle at each crystal size is found by varying Fourier coefficients. The medium-modified pion decay constant is calculated by treating the skyrmion crystal solution as a background classical field and introducing fluctuating pion fields on top of it, then evaluating the axial-vector current correlator in the low-energy limit.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) (or \(0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) in one instance).
- Crystal size corresponding to normal nuclear density: \(L = 1.43\ \text{fm}\).
- The skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition density is estimated as \(n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0\).
- In the Skyrme model (pions only), the minimum energy per baryon occurs at \(L \sim 0.85\ \text{fm}\) with \(E/B \simeq 957\ \text{MeV}\).
- The half-skyrmion phase appears when the crystal size shrinks to \(L \sim 1.3\ \text{fm}\), where the space-averaged \(\langle \phi_0 \rangle\) vanishes.
- The medium-modified pion decay constant drops to \(f_\pi^* \sim 0.65 f_\pi\) at the half-skyrmion transition density and then stays constant for higher densities.
- Free-space pion decay constant: \(f_\pi \simeq 92.4\ \text{MeV}\) (or \(\simeq 93\ \text{MeV}\)).
- Skyrme parameter \(e = 5.93\) (taken from the hidden local symmetry gauge coupling).

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Klebanov (1985) on putting skyrmions on a crystal lattice and the subsequent FCC crystal structure proposed by Kugler and Shtrikman (1988, 1989). It contrasts the skyrmion approach with the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory initiated by Walecka (1974) and the energy-density functional approach, which can yield wildly different predictions for the symmetry energy at densities above \(n_0\) by adjusting parameters. The topology change to half-skyrmions enables a unified description of single baryons, baryonic matter, and medium-modified hadron properties, and is used to inform an effective field theory for dense matter relevant to compact stars, as discussed in Harada (2016).
