# Collective Excitation in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions -- In Memory of Professor Lianshou Liu

> We celebrate the legacies of our friend and mentor Professor Lianshou Liu who was one of the pioneers for the phenomenology of multi-particle interactions and initiated the physics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions in China. In this article, we discuss some of the recent exciting experimental observations on the collective phenomena including collectivity, chirality, criticality, strangeness production, and thermal equilibrium in high-energy nuclear collisions. Future directions, especially the physics at high baryon density, will be discussed with a focus on the first-order phase boundary and hyperon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim
This article is a commemorative review that surveys recent experimental observations of collective phenomena in high-energy nuclear collisions, arguing that these observations collectively point to the formation of a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma. The review hedges that while signatures such as collectivity, chirality, and criticality are observed, definitive confirmation of specific properties like the first-order phase boundary requires future experiments at high baryon density.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental results from relativistic heavy-ion colliders (RHIC and LHC) on several key observables: anisotropic flow coefficients (v2, v3) as measures of collective expansion; charge separation relative to the reaction plane as a signature of the chiral magnetic effect; and fluctuations in net-proton multiplicity as a probe of the QCD critical point. It also discusses strangeness enhancement as a signature of deconfinement and thermal equilibrium, and outlines future directions for the Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key numbers
*   Anisotropic flow (v2) measurements at RHIC and LHC show a robust collective expansion, with values reaching up to approximately 0.2 for central collisions at top RHIC energy.
*   The chiral magnetic effect is studied via charge-dependent azimuthal correlations; the signal magnitude is on the order of 10^-4 to 10^-3, requiring large statistics to isolate from background.
*   Net-proton fluctuation measurements (kappa sigma^2) from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan show a non-monotonic energy dependence, with a minimum near sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, consistent with a critical point search.

## Context
This review extends the legacy of Lianshou Liu, a pioneer in multi-particle interaction phenomenology who initiated the field of relativistic heavy-ion physics in China. It synthesizes results from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program and LHC experiments, building on prior work by the STAR and ALICE collaborations. The paper enables future searches for the QCD phase boundary and hyperon-nucleon interactions at upcoming facilities such as FAIR and NICA.
