# Enhancement of fusion rates due to quantum effects in the particles momentum distribution in nonideal media

> This study concerns a situation when measurements of the nonresonant cross-section of nuclear reactions appear highly dependent on the environment in which the particles interact. An appealing example discussed in the paper is the interaction of a deuteron beam with a target of deuterated metal Ta. In these experiments, the reaction cross section for d(d,p)t was shown to be orders of magnitude greater than what the conventional model predicts for the low-energy particles. In this paper we take into account the influence of quantum effects due to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle for particles in a non-ideal medium elastically interacting with the medium particles. In order to calculate the nuclear reaction rate in the non-ideal environment we apply both the Monte Carlo technique and approximate analytical calculation of the Feynman diagram using nonrelativistic kinetic Green's functions in the medium which correspond to the generalized energy and momentum distribution functions of interacting particles. We show a possibility to reduce the 12-fold integral corresponding to this diagram to a fivefold integral. This can significantly speed up the computation and control accuracy. Our calculations show that quantum effects significantly influence reaction rates such as p +7Be, 3He +4He, p +7Li, and 12C +12C. The new reaction rates may be much higher than the classical ones for the interior of the Sun and supernova stars. The possibility to observe the theoretical predictions under laboratory conditions is discussed.

## Key claim

Quantum effects due to the Heisenberg uncertainty principle in nonideal media produce power-law tails in the momentum distribution of particles, which can increase nuclear fusion reaction rates by orders of magnitude compared to classical Maxwellian predictions, particularly at low temperatures and high densities where the conventional model fails to explain experimental observations such as the d(d,p)t reaction in deuterated tantalum.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear reaction rates in nonideal plasmas using both Monte Carlo techniques and approximate analytical calculations of Feynman diagrams with nonrelativistic kinetic Green's functions, which incorporate generalized energy and momentum distribution functions. They reduced the original 12-fold integral for the reaction rate to a 5-fold integral by applying approximations valid for nondegenerate plasma with small occupation numbers and narrow spectral linewidths, then verified the simplified expressions against full numerical simulations. The quantum corrections to the distribution function were derived using a Lorentz gas model where light particles scatter on heavy impurities, with the linewidth determined by Coulomb scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For the model reaction at temperature T=2 eV and particle concentration N=10^23 cm^-3, the analytic estimate of ⟨σV_ab⟩ is 1.5×10^-27, the 5-fold integral result is 3.2×10^-27, and the full 10-fold integral result is 2.7×10^-27 (units of cm^3/s)
- For N=10^22 cm^-3 at T=2 eV, ⟨σV_ab⟩_5 = 2.0×10^-28 and ⟨σV_ab⟩_10 = 2.1×10^-28 cm^3/s
- For N=10^21 cm^-3 at T=2 eV, ⟨σV_ab⟩_5 = 1.3×10^-29 and ⟨σV_ab⟩_10 = 0.99×10^-29 cm^3/s
- The ratio γ/E_0 at temperature 10 eV and concentration N=10^23 cm^-3 is approximately 5×10^-3, where γ is the linewidth and E_0 = 5kT
- The quantum tail of the distribution function scales as ε_a^{-4} in the Lorentz gas approximation, independent of temperature
- The Gamow energy for the model reaction is E_G = 100 keV, with astrophysical factor S(0) = 241 keV barns

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Galitsky and Yakimets (1966) and Kimball (1975), who showed that quantum corrections produce power-law tails in equilibrium momentum distributions (with p^{-8} dependence for Coulomb interactions), and builds on earlier numerical demonstrations by Starostin (2000, 2005) and Coraddu (2004) using the Lorentz gas concept. The authors address criticism from Zubarev (2008) and Bahcall (2002) regarding the use of asymptotic single-particle distribution functions for reaction rate calculations, by developing a more rigorous Green's function approach that avoids simple averaging of cross sections over momentum distributions. The results enable predictions of enhanced fusion rates in laboratory experiments with deuterated metals and in astrophysical plasmas such as the solar interior and supernova environments, where the new reaction rates may be much higher than classical calculations suggest.
