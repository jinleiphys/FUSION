# Brief review of the theory of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift and the proton radius

> Recently the muonic hydrogen lamb shift has been measured with unprecedented accuracy, allowing for a precise determination of the proton radius. This determination is 5 sigma away from the previous CODATA value obtained from (mainly) the hydrogen lamb shift and the electron-proton scattering. Within an effective field theory formalism, I will define the proton radius and briefly review some aspects of the theoretical prediction for the muonic hydrogen lamb shift, studying both the pure QED-like computation and the hadronic effects.

## Key claim

The 5-sigma discrepancy between the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement and the CODATA proton radius value could be resolved either by errors in the theoretical prediction or by errors in previous radius determinations; the paper argues that the pure QED computation is solid but the hadronic corrections, particularly the polarizability correction, remain the major source of theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The author uses the effective field theory Potential Non-Relativistic QED (pNRQED) to organize the computation of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, integrating out degrees of freedom at scales from the proton mass down to the muonic hydrogen binding energy. The static potential is expanded in powers of the fine-structure constant alpha and the muon mass, with hadronic finite-size effects encoded in the matching coefficients of the NRQED Lagrangian, specifically the coefficients c3, d2, and cD that multiply delta-function potentials.

## Key numbers

- Experimental Lamb shift: E_exp = 206.2949(32) meV
- Proton radius from muonic hydrogen: r_p = 0.84184(67) fm
- CODATA proton radius: r_p = 0.8768(69) fm
- Discrepancy between experiment and theory: E_exp - E_th = 0.311 meV
- Leading-order vacuum polarization contribution: 205.0074 meV (O(m_r alpha^3))
- Two-loop static potential contribution: 1.5079 meV (O(m_r alpha^4))
- Iteration of one-loop potential: 0.151 meV (O(m_r alpha^4))
- Three-loop static potential (vacuum polarization): 0.0076 meV (O(m_r alpha^5))
- Light-by-light (Wichmann-Kroll and Delbruck) contribution: -0.0009 meV (O(m_r alpha^5))
- Tree-level relativistic corrections (1/m_mu^2 potential): 0.0575 meV (O(m alpha^4))
- One-loop vacuum polarization to relativistic potential: 0.0169 meV (O(m alpha^5))
- Ultrasoft photon interaction: -0.6677 meV (O(m alpha^5))
- Ultrasoft effects with m_mu/m_p ratio: -0.045 meV (O(m alpha^5 * m_mu/m_p))
- Hadronic vacuum polarization (d2_had): 0.011 meV
- Zemach correction from chiral perturbation theory: 0.019 meV (from <r_p^3> = 1.9 fm^3)
- Zemach correction from dispersion relations (Friar-Sick): 0.027-0.029 meV (from <r_p^3> = 2.71(13) or 2.85(8) fm^3)
- Polarizability correction from chiral perturbation theory (pions only): 0.018 meV
- Polarizability correction from dispersion relations (average used in Pohl 2010): 0.015 +/- 0.004 meV

## Context

This review addresses the proton radius puzzle that emerged from the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl (2010), which gave a radius 5 sigma below the CODATA value from Mohr (2008). The paper extends the effective field theory approach of Pineda (2004) and Nevado (2007) for muonic hydrogen, building on the pNRQED framework developed for heavy quarkonium. The chiral perturbation theory predictions for the Zemach and polarizability corrections provide model-independent checks that rule out the extremely large <r_p^3> value (~36.5 fm^3) proposed by DeRujula (2010), while the polarizability correction remains the dominant uncertainty and requires further study including Delta particle contributions.
