# Pygmy dipole mode in deformed neutron-rich Mg isotopes close to the drip line

> We investigate the microscopic structure of the low-lying isovector-dipole excitation mode in neutron-rich $^{36,38,40}$Mg close to the drip line by means of the deformed quasiparticle-random-phase-approximation employing the Skyrme and the local pairing energy-density functionals. It is found that the low-lying bump structure above the neutron emission-threshold energy develops when the drip line is approached, and that the isovector dipole strength at $E_{x} < 10$ MeV exhausts about $6.0 %$ of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule in $^{40}$Mg. We obtained the collective dipole modes at around 8-10 MeV in Mg isotopes, which consist of many two-quasiparticle excitations of neutron. The transition density clearly shows an oscillation of the neutron skin against the isoscalar core. We found significant coupling effects between the dipole and octupole excitation modes due to the nuclear deformation. It is also found that the responses for the compressional dipole and isoscalar octupole excitations are much enhanced in the lower energy region.

## Key claim

The low-lying isovector dipole strength below 10 MeV in neutron-rich Mg isotopes near the drip line is identified as a pygmy dipole resonance, but this identification is conditional on the deformed quasiparticle-random-phase-approximation (QRPA) framework using the SkM* Skyrme interaction and a mixed-type pairing functional, and the strength is found to be fragmented across many two-quasiparticle excitations rather than forming a single collective state.

## Method

The author performed deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in coordinate space using cylindrical coordinates with axial and reflection symmetries, employing the SkM* interaction for the mean field and a density-dependent contact interaction for pairing. On top of the HFB ground states, the QRPA equation was solved in matrix formalism for the isovector dipole, isoscalar octupole, and isoscalar compressional dipole excitations in the neutron-rich isotopes 36Mg, 38Mg, and 40Mg. The pairing strength was fitted to reproduce the experimental pairing gap of 34Mg (1.7 MeV), and the residual interactions were renormalized by factors f_ph = 1.06 and f_pp = 1.21 to restore self-consistency for the spurious mode.

## Key numbers

- In 40Mg, the isovector dipole strength at excitation energy Ex < 10 MeV exhausts about 6.0% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) dipole sum rule.
- In 36Mg and 38Mg, the summed transition strength up to 10 MeV exhausts about 3.6% and 4.8% of the TRK sum rule, respectively.
- The neutron-proton root-mean-square radius difference increases from 0.41 fm in 36Mg to 0.54 fm in 40Mg, indicating development of a neutron skin.
- The neutron pairing gap decreases from 1.71 MeV in 36Mg to 1.49 MeV in 40Mg.
- The summed octupole transition strengths up to 10 MeV exhaust about 26.6% (Kpi=0-) and 27.3% (Kpi=1-) of the energy-weighted sum rule value for isoscalar octupole excitations in 40Mg.
- The calculated energy-weighted sum of the isovector dipole strength up to 60 MeV reaches 98.5% of the EWSR value including the enhancement factor (kappa = 0.33) in 40Mg.

## Context

This work extends the deformed QRPA approach previously applied to neutron-rich Ne isotopes by Yoshida (2008) and to Sn isotopes by Pena Arteaga and Ring (2009) to the Mg isotopic chain approaching the drip line. The results contradict the assumption that pygmy dipole modes are restricted to spherical systems, showing that deformation leads to coupling between dipole and octupole excitation modes, as earlier suggested by Inakura et al. (2002). The findings enable future investigations of continuum effects on these low-lying resonances using frameworks such as the QRPA in Gamow basis, as pursued by Michel et al. (2009).
