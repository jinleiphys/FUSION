# Elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections of $^{6}$Li studied with a microscopic continuum discretized coupled channels model

> We present a systematic study of $^{6}$Li elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections at incident energies around the Coulomb barrier within the continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) framework, where $^{6}$Li is treated in an $α$+$d$ two-body model. Collisions with $^{27}$Al, $^{64}$Zn, $^{138}$Ba and $^{208}$Pa are analyzed. The microscopic optical potentials (MOP) based on Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interaction for $α$ and $d$ are adopted in CDCC calculations and satisfactory agreement with the experimental data is obtained without any adjustment on MOPs. For comparison, the $α$ and $d$ global phenomenological optical potentials (GOP) are also used in CDCC analysis and a reduction no less than 50$\%$ on the surface imaginary part of deuteron GOP is required for describing the data. In all cases, the $^6$Li breakup effect is significant and provides repulsive correction to the folding model potential. The reduction on the surface imaginary part of GOP of deuteron reveals a strong suppression of the reaction probability of deuteron as a component of $^{6}$Li as compared with that of a free deuteron. A further investigation is made by taking the $d$ breakup process into account equivalently within the dynamic polarization potential approach and it shows that $d$ behaves like a tightly bound nucleus in $^{6}$Li induced reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that microscopic optical potentials (MOPs) based on Skyrme nucleon-nucleon interactions can reproduce experimental data for 6Li elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections without any adjustment, but only when the breakup effect is explicitly included via continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculations; the abstract's claim of "satisfactory agreement" depends on this specific methodological condition.

## Method

The authors performed CDCC calculations treating 6Li as an alpha-plus-deuteron two-body system, using the Lagrange-mesh method to discretize continuum states up to 30 MeV and partial waves up to l=2. They constructed microscopic optical potentials for alpha and deuteron clusters by folding nucleon-nucleus MOPs (based on Skyrme SkMP interaction) with cluster density distributions, and compared these against calculations using global phenomenological optical potentials (GOPs) for alpha and deuteron. For the GOP calculations, the surface imaginary part of the deuteron optical potential was multiplied by a reduction factor lambda_d optimized for each target.

## Key numbers

- Breakup threshold energy of 6Li into alpha+d: 1.47 MeV
- Coulomb barriers in laboratory system: 8.0 MeV for 6Li+27Al, 14.5 MeV for 6Li+64Zn, 22.0 MeV for 6Li+138Ba, 30.6 MeV for 6Li+208Pb
- Reduction factor lambda_d on surface imaginary part of deuteron GOP: 0 for 6Li+27Al, 0.45 for 6Li+64Zn, 0.50 for 6Li+138Ba, 0.35 for 6Li+208Pb
- Convergence criteria: epsilon_max=30 MeV and l_max=2 ensure convergence of all CDCC calculations, with absolute values of R_1 and R_2 less than 2%
- For d breakup analysis: incident energy for d+208Pb system set to E_d=11.0 MeV (one-third of 6Li lab energy of 33.0 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the CDCC studies of Sakuragi (1986) for 6Li scattering at energies well above the Coulomb barrier to energies around the Coulomb barrier, using four target systems (27Al, 64Zn, 138Ba, 208Pb). It builds on the authors' previous development of nucleon-nucleus MOPs based on Skyrme interactions (Shen 2009) and their application to light ions (Guo 2009-2014, Chen 2020). The finding that deuteron behaves like a tightly bound nucleus within 6Li, requiring a reduction of at least 50% on the surface imaginary part of the deuteron GOP, resolves inconsistencies in earlier work (Lei 2015, 2017) where the required adjustment depended on the choice of deuteron optical potential. This enables future application of the microscopic CDCC approach to heavier weakly bound projectiles such as 11Li and 11Be.
