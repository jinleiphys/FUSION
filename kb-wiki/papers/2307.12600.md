# The baryon number fluctuation $κσ^2$ as a probe of nuclear matter phase transition at high baryon density

> Two critical end points (CEPs) of the chiral phase transition and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition show up at finite baryon chemical potential. The kurtosis $κσ^2$ of baryon number fluctuation on the $T-μ_B$ plane is positive on the first-order side and negative on the crossover side along the phase boundary. The freeze-out line extracted from the heavy ion collisions crosses between these two phase boundaries, one can observe a peak of $κσ^2$ around the collision energy $5 {\rm GeV}$ near the CEP of the chiral phase transition, and negative $κσ^2$ at low collision energies due to the CEP of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. This expalains the experimental measurement of $κσ^2$ at the collision energies of 2.4 GeV at HADES and 3 GeV and 7.7-200 GeV at STAR for most central collision. Thus we propose that the baryon number fluctuation $κσ^2$ can be used as a probe of nuclear matter phase structure at high baryon density.

## Key claim

The baryon number fluctuation $\kappa\sigma^2$ can serve as a probe of nuclear matter phase structure at high baryon density, but only if both the chiral critical end point (CEP) and the nuclear liquid-gas CEP are included in a hybrid model; the negative $\kappa\sigma^2$ observed at low collision energies (2.4 GeV at HADES, 3 GeV at STAR) is explained specifically by the freeze-out line crossing the crossover side of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, not by the chiral CEP alone.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid model combining the Polyakov-Loop Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model for the quark phase and the Walecka model for the hadronic phase, connected by a Gibbs condition that selects the dominant degree of freedom (quarks or baryons) based on which grand potential is lower at a given temperature and baryon chemical potential. They solved the gap equations for each model under mean-field approximation, computed the baryon number susceptibilities $\chi_2$ and $\chi_4$, and then evaluated $\kappa\sigma^2 = \chi_4/\chi_2$ along two freeze-out lines (one fitted to experiment, one modified by lattice QCD) as a function of collision energy $\sqrt{s}$.

## Key numbers

- Chiral CEP located at $T_{\chi}^{\rm CEP}=103\ {\rm MeV}$, $\mu_{B,\chi}^{\rm CEP}=679\ {\rm MeV}$.
- Nuclear liquid-gas CEP located at $T_{LG}^{\rm CEP}=11\ {\rm MeV}$, $\mu_{B,LG}^{\rm CEP}=927\ {\rm MeV}$.
- PNJL model pseudo-critical temperature at zero baryon chemical potential: $T_{pc}^{\rm PNJL}=162.5\ {\rm MeV}$; lattice QCD pseudo-critical temperature: $T_{pc}^{\rm LQCD}=156.5\pm 1.5\ {\rm MeV}$.
- A peak of $\kappa\sigma^2$ is predicted around collision energy $\sqrt{s}\approx 5\ {\rm GeV}$ near the chiral CEP, followed by a drop to negative values at lower energies.
- The transition between quark-dominated and baryon-dominated degrees of freedom occurs around $\sqrt{s}\approx 5\ {\rm GeV}$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of baryon number fluctuations as a probe of the QCD critical end point, notably the beam energy scan (BES) program at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2010-2022) and the prediction by Li et al. (2018) that a peak in $\kappa\sigma^2$ near $\sqrt{s}\sim 5$ GeV is related to the chiral CEP. It directly addresses the unexplained negative $\kappa\sigma^2$ measurements from HADES at 2.4 GeV (2020) and STAR at 3 GeV (2022) by incorporating the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, which had been studied separately by Vovchenko et al. (2016) and He et al. (2022). The hybrid model enables future tests of the existence of an extra CEP at zero temperature and high baryon density (Hatsuda, 2006) and the quarkyonic phase (McLerran, 2007), with implications for neutron star structure and mergers.
