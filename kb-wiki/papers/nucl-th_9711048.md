# K0-Sigma+ Photoproduction with SAPHIR

> Preliminary results of the analysis of the reaction p(gamma,K0)Sigma+ are presented. We show the first measurement of the differential cross section and much improved data for the total cross section than previous data. The data are compared with model predictions from different isobar and quark models that give a good description of p(gamma,K+)Lambda and p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 data in the same energy range. Results of ChPT describe the data adequately at threshold while isobar models that include hadronic form factors reproduce the data at intermediate energies.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first measurement of differential cross sections for the reaction p(gamma,K0)Sigma+, along with improved total cross section data; however, these are preliminary results, and the comparison with models shows that no single theoretical approach simultaneously describes the data across the full energy range.

## Method
Data were taken with the SAPHIR magnetic spectrometer at the ELSA electron accelerator in Bonn, using a tagged photon beam from an electron energy of 1.7 GeV, covering photon energies from threshold (1.047 GeV) to 1.6 GeV. Events were reconstructed from three charged tracks, requiring a K0 candidate from pi+ pi- invariant mass and a missing mass consistent with the Sigma+; the Sigma+ decay channel was identified via a kinematical fit. Differential and total cross sections were compared with predictions from isobar models (Mart et al., Saghai, Cotanch), a quark model (Li), and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT, Sven).

## Key numbers
- Photon energy range covered: from threshold at 1.047 GeV to 1.6 GeV, using an electron beam energy of 1.7 GeV.
- The tagging range of the photon beam was (0.55 - 0.95) times the electron beam energy.
- The isobar model with an overall monopole form factor used a cut-off around 700 MeV for fitting K+Lambda and K+Sigma0 data.
- The gauge-invariant form factor implementation (Ohta procedure) used cut-offs of about 1 GeV for s- and u-channel diagrams and 800 MeV for t-channel resonance diagrams.
- Total cross section data are reproduced up to 1.4 GeV in the isobar descriptions.

## Context
This work extends previous SAPHIR measurements of p(gamma,K+)Lambda and p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 (Lindemann, 1995) to the isospin-related channel p(gamma,K0)Sigma+, for which only poor-statistics total cross section data existed (ABBHHM). The results enable tests of isobar models (Mart et al., Saghai, Cotanch) and quark models (Li) that successfully describe the charged kaon channels, and show that a gauge-invariant form factor implementation (Ohta) improves agreement at intermediate energies. ChPT (Sven) describes the threshold region adequately but cannot be applied at higher energies, leaving open the need for a unified theoretical description across the full energy range.
