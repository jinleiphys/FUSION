# Warm dense matter and cooling of supernovae remnants

> We study the thermal effects on the nuclear matter (NM) properties such as binding energy, incompressibility, free symmetry energy and its coefficients using NL3, G3 and IU-FSU parameter sets of relativistic mean-field models. These models being consistent with the properties of cold NM, have also been used to study the effect of temperature by incorporating the Fermi function. The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in the symmetric NM is found to be 14.60, 15.37 and 14.50 MeV for NL3, G3 and IU-FSU parameter sets respectively, which is in excellent agreement with previous theoretical and experimental studies. We inspect that the properties related to second differential coefficient of the binding energy and free symmetry energy at saturation density ( i.e. K 0 (n, T ) and Q sym,0) exhibit the contrary effects for NL3 and G3 parameters as the temperature increases. We find that the prediction of saturated curvature parameter ( K sym,0 ) for G3 equation of state at finite temperature favour the combined analysis of K sym,0 for the existence of massive pulsars, gravitational waves from GW170817 and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451. Further, we investigate the cooling mechanism of newly born stars through neutrino emissivity controlled by direct Urca process and instate some interesting remarks about neutrino emissivity. We also deliberate the effect of temperature on the M-R profile of Proto-Neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the G3 equation of state at finite temperature predicts a saturated curvature parameter (K_sym,0) that is consistent with combined constraints from massive pulsars, gravitational wave event GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, but this agreement is conditional on the specific choice of parameter set and temperature range studied.

## Method

The authors employed a temperature-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with NL3, G3, and IU-FSU parameter sets, incorporating the Fermi distribution function to include thermal effects. They calculated nuclear matter properties including binding energy, incompressibility, free symmetry energy and its derivatives (L_sym, K_sym, Q_sym) for symmetric nuclear matter at temperatures from 0 to 20 MeV. The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition was determined from the inflection point of the pressure-density curve, and neutrino emissivity via the direct Urca process was evaluated for proto-neutron star cooling.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter: 14.60 MeV (NL3), 15.37 MeV (G3), and 14.50 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Saturation density at zero temperature: 0.148 fm^-3 for NL3 and G3, 0.154 fm^-3 for IU-FSU; increases linearly to 0.196 fm^-3 at 20 MeV for NL3 and G3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation and zero temperature: -16.29 MeV (NL3), -16.02 MeV (G3), -16.39 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Incompressibility at saturation (K_0) at zero temperature: 271.38 MeV (NL3), 243.96 MeV (G3), 231.31 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Free symmetry energy at saturation (F_sym,0) at zero temperature: 37.43 MeV (NL3), 31.84 MeV (G3), 32.71 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Slope parameter (L_sym,0) at zero temperature: 120.65 MeV (NL3), 49.31 MeV (G3), 49.26 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Curvature parameter (K_sym,0) at zero temperature: 101.34 MeV (NL3), -106.07 MeV (G3), 23.28 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Skewness parameter (Q_sym,0) at zero temperature: 177.90 MeV (NL3), 915.47 MeV (G3), 536.46 MeV (IU-FSU)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of cold nuclear matter properties using RMF models (Lalazissis 1997 for NL3; Kumar 2017 for G3; Carbone 2014 for IU-FSU) to finite temperatures relevant for supernova and proto-neutron star physics. The results for critical temperature agree with prior theoretical and experimental studies reporting values in the 10-20 MeV range (Subrata Pal, Kupper, Bo Nan, Moretto, Tatsuyuki). The paper enables improved modeling of proto-neutron star cooling through neutrino emissivity and mass-radius relations, and provides temperature-dependent constraints on symmetry energy parameters that can be tested against future heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as GANIL-SPIRAL2, CSR in China, and FRIB in the United States.
