# Big Bang nucleosynthesis revisited via Trojan Horse Method measurements

> Nuclear reaction rates are among the most important input for understanding the primordial nucleosynthesis and therefore for a quantitative description of the early Universe. An up-to-date compilation of direct cross sections of 2H(d,p)3H, 2H(d,n)3He, 7Li(p,alpha)4He and 3He(d,p)4He reactions is given. These are among the most uncertain cross sections used and input for Big Bang nucleosynthesis calculations. Their measurements through the Trojan Horse Method (THM) are also reviewed and compared with direct data. The reaction rates and the corresponding recommended errors in this work were used as input for primordial nucleosynthesis calculations to evaluate their impact on the 2H, 3,4He and 7Li primordial abundances, which are then compared with observations.

# Big Bang nucleosynthesis revisited via Trojan Horse Method measurements

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating Trojan Horse Method (THM) measurements for four key BBN reactions yields updated reaction rates that deviate by up to 20% from previous compilations, but that the longstanding discrepancy between calculated and observed primordial abundances of ³He and ⁷Li persists and "seems to not be due to nuclear reaction rates uncertainties."

## Method

The authors compiled direct cross-section data and THM measurements for four reactions: ²H(d,p)³H, ²H(d,n)³He, ³He(d,p)⁴He, and ⁷Li(p,α)⁴He. They performed R-matrix fits using the AZURE code to both direct and THM data simultaneously, then used the fitted S-factors to calculate thermonuclear reaction rates via Maxwellian averaging. These rates were input into a modified version of the standard BBN code derived from Wagoner, Fowler, and Hoyle (1967) and Kawano (1988, 1992) to compute primordial abundances.

## Key numbers

- For ²H(d,p)³H, the THM bare-nucleus S-factor was measured from 2.6 keV up to 1.5 MeV with a 5% experimental error
- For ²H(d,n)³He, the THM bare-nucleus S-factor was measured from 2.6 keV up to 1.5 MeV with a 5% experimental error
- The baryon-to-photon ratio adopted was η = 6.16 × 10⁻¹⁰, from Komatsu et al. (2011)
- The neutron lifetime used was τ_n = 878.5 s
- The calculated ⁴He mass fraction using all THM rates was Y_p = 0.2485⁺⁰·⁰⁰¹₋₀·₀₀₂, compared to the observed value of 0.256 ± 0.006 from Izotov and Thuan (2010)
- The calculated D/H ratio using all THM rates was (2.692⁺⁰·¹⁷⁷₋₀·₀⁷⁰) × 10⁻⁵, compared to the observed mean of (2.82 ± 0.26) × 10⁻⁵ from Meissner et al. (2006)
- The calculated ⁷Li/H ratio using all THM rates was (4.683⁺⁰·³³⁵₋₀·²⁹²) × 10⁻¹⁰, compared to the observed value of (1.58 ± 0.31) × 10⁻¹⁰ from Sbordone et al. (2010)
- The calculated ³He/H ratio using all THM rates was (9.441⁺⁰·⁵¹¹₋₀·⁴⁶⁶) × 10⁻⁶, compared to the observed lower limit of (11 ± 2) × 10⁻⁶ from Bania, Rood, and Balser (2002)

## Context

This work extends the experimental campaign applying the Trojan Horse Method to BBN reactions, following earlier THM studies by Spitaleri et al. (1999, 2001) and Tumino et al. (2011, 2014). The updated reaction rates are compared with the NACRE compilation by Angulo et al. (1999), the compilation by Cyburt (2004), and the Smith, Kawano, and Malaney compilation (1993). The results confirm that the "lithium problem", the factor-of-three discrepancy between SBBN predictions and observed ⁷Li abundances in halo stars, remains unresolved by improved nuclear reaction rates, pointing toward non-standard BBN models or stellar phenomena as possible explanations.
