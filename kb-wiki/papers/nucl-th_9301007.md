# A Quark Model of Antilambda-Lambda Production in Pbar-P Interactions

> A quark model which includes both scalar and vector contributions to the reaction mechanism is used in a DWBA calculation of total and differential cross-sections, polarizations, and spin correlation coefficients for the reaction $\bar p p \rightarrow \bar ΛΛ$ at laboratory momenta from threshold to 1.92 GeV/c. The free parameters of the calculation include the scalar and vector strengths, a quark cluster size parameter, and six parameters in the unknown $\bar ΛΛ$ interaction. Excellent agreement with experiment is found for a constructive interference of scalar and vector terms, and for a $\bar ΛΛ$ potential which differs from that suggested by several authors on the basis of SU(3) arguments. The fit to the data is better than that obtained by other quark models, which use only scalar or vector annihilation terms. The agreement with experiment is as good as that found in meson-exchange models, which use more parameters than the present calculation.

## Key claim

A quark model that includes both scalar and vector contributions to the reaction mechanism, rather than either term alone, can fit the experimental data for $\bar p p \rightarrow \bar \Lambda \Lambda$ as well as meson-exchange models that use more parameters, provided the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ potential is allowed to differ significantly from the SU(3)-based predictions of Kohno and Weise (1986, 1988).

## Method

The authors performed a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculation using a quark model with both scalar (``$^3P_0$") and vector (``$^3S_1$") operators for $\bar q q$ annihilation and creation. The initial $\bar p p$ interaction was described by the Kohno-Weise potential, and the final $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ interaction was parameterized with a real part (central, tensor, spin-orbit) and an imaginary Woods-Saxon annihilation term. A nine-parameter global fit (later reduced to eight by fixing the annihilation diffuseness) was made to 356 data points from the PS185 collaboration at laboratory momenta from 1436 to 1695 MeV/c, using Monte Carlo, simplex, gradient, and simulated annealing minimization techniques.

## Key numbers

- Global fit with combined vector+scalar mechanism achieved $\chi^2$/data = 3.2 for 356 data points, compared to 7.1 for vector alone and 6.4 for scalar alone.
- Best-fit vector strength $g_v = -17.4$ and scalar strength $g_s = 6.5$ (dimensionless coupling constants).
- Quark cluster size parameter $r_0 = 0.43$ fm in the combined fit.
- The $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ annihilation potential depth $W_{\bar \Lambda \Lambda}^{(0)} = -1956$ MeV, radius $r_w = 0.66$ fm, and diffuseness $a_w = 0.05$ fm (fixed).
- The $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ real central strength $V = 147$ MeV, tensor strength $V_T = -151$ MeV, and spin-orbit strength $V_{LS} = -315$ MeV.
- The Nijmegen group (Timmermans et al., 1992) achieved $\chi^2$/data = 1.2 for 157 data points (after excluding 7 points) using 10 parameters over the momentum range 1436-1546 MeV/c.
- Strange quark mass $m_s = 491$ MeV/c$^2$ and light quark mass $m = 313$ MeV/c$^2$ were used in the scalar operator.

## Context

This work extends the quark-model approach of Kohno and Weise (1986, 1988) and Furui and Faessler (1987) by including both scalar and vector terms in the reaction mechanism, rather than treating them separately. It directly competes with meson-exchange models by Tabakin and Eisenstein (1985), Timmermans et al. (1992), LaFrance et al. (1988, 1991), and Haidenbauer et al. (1992). The authors find that the $\bar \Lambda \Lambda$ potential required to fit the data differs from the SU(3)-based predictions of Kohno and Weise, with larger tensor and spin-orbit terms and a deeper, longer-range annihilation term. The work enables future tests at higher momenta and in the $\bar \Sigma \Lambda$ and $\bar \Sigma \Sigma$ production channels to distinguish between quark and meson-exchange models.
