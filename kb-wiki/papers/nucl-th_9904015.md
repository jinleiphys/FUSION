# Novel features of $J/Ψ$ dissociation in matter

> We make a detailed study of the effect that the recently predicted modification of the in-medium masses of charmed mesons would have on $J/Ψ$ dissociation on pion and $ρ$-meson comovers in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We find a substantial dependence of the $J/Ψ$ absorption rates on the density and temperature of the nuclear matter. This suggests that a quantitative analysis of $J/Ψ$ dissociation in nucleus nucleus collisions should include the effects of the modification of meson masses in dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the in-medium modification of charmed meson masses (predicted by the quark-meson coupling model) dramatically enhances the \(J/\Psi\) dissociation cross sections on pion and \(\rho\)-meson comovers, producing a strong density and temperature dependence that had not been considered before; this effect can explain the NA50 \(Pb+Pb\) data without invoking a constant phenomenological cross section.

## Method

The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to calculate the in-medium scalar and vector potentials for \(D\), \(D^*\), and \(\rho\) mesons in symmetric nuclear matter, solving the Dirac equations for light quarks in the meson bags with mean-field potentials. They then compute the \(\pi+J/\Psi \to D^*+\bar{D}, \bar{D}^*+D\) and \(\rho+J/\Psi \to D+\bar{D}\) dissociation cross sections as functions of invariant energy, including the in-medium mass shifts and potentials, and thermally average these cross sections over a Bose-distributed pion or \(\rho\)-meson gas at given temperature and chemical potential. Finally, they incorporate the density-dependent comover absorption cross section into a heavy-ion collision model (Capella et al.) to compare with NA50 data on the \(J/\Psi\) over Drell-Yan ratio as a function of transverse energy.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density \(\rho_B = 3\rho_0\) (\(\rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the \(D\)-meson feels an attractive scalar potential of about 120 MeV and an attractive vector potential of about 250 MeV.
- In vacuum, the thermally averaged \(\pi+J/\Psi\) dissociation cross section is less than about 0.3 mb.
- At \(\rho_B = 3\rho_0\) and a pion gas temperature of 120 MeV, the thermally averaged \(\pi+J/\Psi\) absorption cross section is about a factor of 7 larger than at \(\rho_B = 0\).
- At \(\rho_B = 3\rho_0\), the thermally averaged \(\rho+J/\Psi\) dissociation cross section becomes larger than 1 mb.
- The nuclear absorption cross section used in the heavy-ion model is taken as 4.5 mb.
- The calculations reproduce NA50 data up to transverse energy \(E_T = 100\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic dissociation scenario for \(J/\Psi\) suppression (Brodsky 1988, Capella 1997, Martins 1995, Mueller 1998) by incorporating for the first time the density-dependent in-medium potentials of charmed mesons predicted by the QMC model (Guichon 1988, Tsushima 1998). Prior calculations using vacuum meson properties found \(\pi,\rho+J/\Psi\) cross sections too small to explain the anomalous suppression observed by NA50 in \(Pb+Pb\) collisions (Qm97, Last), forcing models to treat the comover absorption cross section as a free parameter (Cassing 1998, Vogt 1998, Capella 1997). The present results enable a theoretically motivated, density-dependent description of \(J/\Psi\) absorption on comovers that agrees with data without adjustable parameters.
