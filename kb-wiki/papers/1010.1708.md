# Meson-exchange currents and quasielastic neutrino cross sections in the SuperScaling Approximation model

> We evaluate the quasielastic double differential neutrino cross sections obtained in a phenomenological model based on the superscaling behavior of electron scattering data. We compare our results with the recent experimental data for neutrinos of MiniBooNE and estimate the contribution of the vector meson-exchange currents in the 2p-2h sector.

## Key claim

The SuSA model, which uses a scaling function extracted from longitudinal electron scattering data, systematically underestimates MiniBooNE CCQE cross sections at large muon scattering angles and low muon energies; including 2p-2h meson-exchange currents improves agreement for angles up to about 50 degrees, but the model should not be trusted for very forward angles where low-energy nuclear excitations dominate.

## Method

The authors compute flux-integrated double differential CCQE cross sections for muon neutrinos on carbon-12 using the SuSA phenomenological scaling function, which is a fit to the longitudinal (e,e') reduced response function. They then add the contribution of 2p-2h meson-exchange currents taken from the fully relativistic model of De Pace (2003), which affects only the transverse vector response. Results are compared with MiniBooNE data across bins of muon kinetic energy and scattering angle, using an axial mass of 1.03 GeV/c^2 and the Hoehler parametrization of electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- The SuSA model uses an axial mass value of M_A = 1.03 GeV/c^2, in contrast to the ad hoc value of 1.35 GeV/c^2 required by the RFG model to match MiniBooNE data.
- Coulomb distortion effects on the outgoing muon are below 2% for muon kinetic energies T_mu >= 200 MeV, but reach about 10% for T_mu around 90-100 MeV.
- At the most forward angle bin (cos theta > 0.9), roughly 1/2 of the cross section arises from the first 50 MeV of excitation energy, as shown by applying a cut at omega = 50 MeV.
- The MiniBooNE data have an overall normalization error of delta N = 10.7%.

## Context

This work extends the SuSA phenomenological approach of Amaro (2004), which assumes universality of the scaling function for electromagnetic and weak interactions, to CCQE neutrino reactions. It contrasts with the Relativistic Fermi Gas model used in MiniBooNE's experimental analysis, which underestimates the total cross section unless an unusually large axial mass is used. The results enable future comparisons with relativistic mean field theory predictions and with the fully relativistic 2p-2h model of Amaro (2010) that includes correlation diagrams required by gauge invariance, which may resolve the remaining discrepancies at large angles.
