# Spin polarization from nucleon-nucleon scatterings in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a new mechanism of generating spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions dominated by nucleon degree of freedom. By incorporating the spin change in nucleon-nucleon scatterings based on the phase shift data together with the constraint of rigorous angular momentum conservation and Pauli blocking, we illustrate through a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model that appreciable spin polarization (about $1 \sim 2\%$) can be generated in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. This mechanism, together with the nuclear spin-orbit potential, may help to understand the spin polarization in few-GeV heavy-ion collisions dominated by nucleon degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The authors propose that nucleon-nucleon (NN) scatterings, when combined with rigorous angular momentum conservation and Pauli blocking, can generate appreciable spin polarization (about 1–2%) in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, even without the nuclear spin-orbit potential. The abstract's claim of "appreciable spin polarization" is hedged by the condition that this mechanism only produces measurable polarization when both angular momentum conservation and Pauli blocking are properly incorporated; without Pauli blocking the polarization is negligibly small.

## Method

The authors incorporate spin changes in elastic NN scatterings using phase shift data from proton-neutron (0–425 MeV) and proton-proton (1–500 MeV) scatterings, transforming the S-matrix from helicity to canonical spin states via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. They implement this in a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model for Au+Au collisions, with spin-dependent differential cross sections, rigorous angular momentum conservation enforced through iterative adjustment of final-state coordinates and momenta, and Pauli blocking. The initial nucleon spin expectation directions are randomly sampled in 4π solid angle, and free nucleons are defined as those with densities below 1/8 of saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Spin polarization of about 1–2% for free nucleons perpendicular to the reaction plane in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions
- For np scatterings at incident energy 100 MeV and in-plane scattering (φ′_p=0), the polarization P_y peaks around θ′_p=0.4π (Figure 1a)
- Phase shift data used: proton-neutron scatterings from 0 to 425 MeV, proton-proton scatterings from 1 to 500 MeV (from PhysRevC.15.1002)
- Beam energies studied: 100 AMeV and higher; impact parameter b = 8 fm for midcentral collisions
- Pauli blocking probability is largest for NN scatterings with L_y/L ~ 0, and smaller for L_y/L ~ 1 than for L_y/L ~ -1 (Figure 3a)
- Average incident energy ⟨E_inc⟩ is larger for L_y/L ~ 1 than for L_y/L ~ -1 (Figure 3a)

## Context

This work extends the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions from the relativistic regime (where vorticity in quark-gluon plasma produces hyperon polarization, as observed by STAR (2017) and ALICE (2019)) to intermediate energies dominated by nucleon degrees of freedom. It provides a more realistic treatment of spin change in NN scatterings compared to the previous work by PhysRevC.109.014615, and the magnitude of nucleon spin polarization is smaller but comparable to that from the nuclear spin-orbit potential alone (Xu (2025)). This mechanism, together with the nuclear spin-orbit potential, may help understand the measurable spin polarizations of Λ hyperons observed in few-GeV heavy-ion collisions (PhysRevC.104.L061901, 2022137506) and hypertritons (Sun (2025)).
