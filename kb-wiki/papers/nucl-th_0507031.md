# Time-Reversal-Violating Schiff Moment of 199Hg

> We calculate the Schiff moment of the nucleus 199Hg, created by pi-N-N vertices that are odd under parity (P) and time-reversal (T). Our approach, formulated in diagrammatic perturbation theory with important core-polarization diagrams summed to all orders, gives a close approximation to the expectation value of the Schiff operator in the odd-A Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov ground state generated by a Skyrme interaction and a weak P- and T-odd pion-exchange potential. To assess the uncertainty in the results, we carry out the calculation with several Skyrme interactions (the quality of which we test by checking predictions for the isoscalar-E1 strength distribution in 208Pb) and estimate most of the important diagrams we omit.

# Time-Reversal-Violating Schiff Moment of 199Hg

## Key claim

The Schiff moment of 199Hg depends on the three P- and T-odd pi-N-N coupling constants with coefficients that are significantly smaller than earlier estimates for the isoscalar and isotensor channels, but the isovector coefficient remains similar to the initial estimate of Flambaum (1986). The results carry substantial uncertainty from the choice of effective interaction, with the isoscalar coefficient varying by about a factor of 4 across five different Skyrme forces.

## Method

The authors calculate the Schiff moment using diagrammatic perturbation theory with a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean field generated by a Skyrme interaction, treating core polarization through the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) in the even-even nucleus 198Hg before adding a valence neutron in the 2p1/2 level. The P- and T-odd interaction is taken as pion exchange only, with the three coupling constants g-bar_0 (isoscalar), g-bar_1 (isovector), and g-bar_2 (isotensor). The calculation is performed with five different Skyrme interactions (SkO', SkM*, SkP, SIII, SLy4), and the quality of each is tested by comparing predicted isoscalar-E1 strength distributions in 208Pb against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Preferred result (SkO' interaction): S = 0.010 g g-bar_0 + 0.074 g g-bar_1 + 0.018 g g-bar_2 [e fm^3]
- Averaged result over five Skyrme interactions: S = 0.007 g g-bar_0 + 0.071 g g-bar_1 + 0.018 g g-bar_2 [e fm^3]
- The isoscalar coefficient a_0 ranges from 0.002 (SkP) to 0.010 (SIII, SkO') e fm^3 across the five interactions
- The isovector coefficient a_1 ranges from 0.057 (SIII) to 0.090 (SLy4) e fm^3
- The isotensor coefficient a_2 ranges from 0.011 (SkP) to 0.025 (SIII) e fm^3
- Experimental limit on the 199Hg atomic EDM: |d| < 2.1 x 10^{-28} e cm, yielding |S| < 7.5 x 10^{-12} e fm^3
- The isoscalar coefficient is smaller than the Flambaum (1986) estimate by a factor of 9 to 40; the isotensor coefficient is smaller by a factor of 7 to 16
- The high-energy isoscalar-E1 peak in 208Pb is predicted between 23.4 MeV (SkP) and 28.3 MeV (SIII), compared to experimental values of 22.4 +/- 0.5 MeV (Davis 1997) and 19.9 +/- 0.8 MeV (Clark 2001)

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of Flambaum, Khriplovich and Sushkov (1986), which used a simple Wood-Saxon potential and zero-range approximation for the P- and T-odd interaction, and improves upon the more recent RPA calculations of Dmitriev et al. (2003, 2005) by using self-consistent Skyrme mean fields with pairing, treating the full finite-range pion-exchange potential, and employing multiple interactions to assess uncertainty. The results enable tighter constraints on the QCD parameter theta-bar and other sources of CP violation from the experimental limit on the 199Hg atomic EDM, and the authors note that better measurements of low-lying dipole strength, particularly in the isoscalar channel, would help reduce the nuclear structure uncertainty.
