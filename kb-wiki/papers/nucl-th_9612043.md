# An Effective Lagrangian with Broken Scale and Chiral Symmetry III: Mesons at Finite Temperature

> We investigate the finite temperature behavior of the meson sector of an effective Lagrangian which describes nuclear matter. A method is developed for evaluating the logarithmic terms in the effective potential which involves expansion and resummation; the result is written in terms of the exponential integral. In the absence of explicit chiral symmetry breaking, a phase transition restores the symmetry at a temperature of 190 MeV; when the pion has a mass the transition is smooth. At a much higher temperature a first order phase transition restores scale symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper investigates finite-temperature behavior of the meson sector of an effective Lagrangian that describes nuclear matter, focusing on chiral and scale symmetry restoration. The abstract's claim of a phase transition at 190 MeV is hedged: the authors state they do not believe their approximation scheme is sufficiently accurate to determine the order of the transition, and the result depends on the prescription used to define the masses.

## Method

The authors study the mesonic part of their effective Lagrangian (containing glueball field φ, σ, and pion fields π) at finite temperature using mean field theory with thermal fluctuations. They develop a method to handle the logarithmic term ln(σ²+π²) in the effective potential by expanding in fluctuations, resumming the infinite series, and expressing the result in terms of exponential integrals E_n(y). The equations of motion and effective masses are solved self-consistently, with thermal averages of field fluctuations computed using Bose-Einstein distributions.

## Key numbers

- Chiral phase transition temperature T_c = 187 MeV for the case without explicit chiral symmetry breaking (ε₁' = 0)
- For the physical case with explicit chiral symmetry breaking (pion mass), no phase transition occurs; ν smoothly decreases to a small value
- Scale symmetry restoration first-order phase transition temperature ~550 MeV
- Vacuum glueball mass taken as approximately 1.6 GeV (based on QCD sum rule estimates of 1.5 GeV and lattice estimates of 1.7 GeV)
- Parameter B₀ = (334.9 MeV)⁴ for the case with explicit symmetry breaking, and (342.6 MeV)⁴ without
- σ₀ = 110 MeV, φ₀ = 140.9 MeV
- Expansion parameter 4ν²⟨Δν²⟩/(ν²+⟨ψ²⟩)² found to be < 0.05

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature studies (Carter, Ellis, Rudaz, papers I and II) of an effective Lagrangian incorporating broken scale and chiral symmetry, which successfully described nuclear matter and finite nuclei at the mean field level. The finite-temperature analysis goes beyond earlier studies by Kalogera and by Papa which included only thermal effects for nucleons. The chiral restoration temperature of 187 MeV is compared to the standard chiral model result of 132 MeV (using f_π = 93 MeV) and to Gerber and Leutwyler's estimate of 190 MeV from two-flavor QCD using an effective chiral Lagrangian to three-loop order. The results enable future work incorporating nucleon degrees of freedom to study how chiral restoration is affected.
