# Investigation of the short-range correlation and its induced high-momentum tail by photon emission from intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions

> The effect of high-momentum tail (HMT) in nucleon momentum distribution of neutron-rich nuclei has been investigated by employing proton-neutron bremsstrahlung photons in the reactions of $^{124}$Sn +$ ^{124}$Sn, $^{112}$Sn + $^{112}$Sn and $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au at intermediate energies based on the IBUU transport model. With the time evolution of photon multiplicity and the double differential probability of photon production, we show the emission of bremsstrahlung photons is sensitive to the high-momentum component in nucleon momentum distribution of neutron-rich nuclei, and the HMT can lead to an obvious increase of hard photon production from proton-neutron bremsstrahlung collisions. Finally, the ratio of double differential photon production probability is examined as possible probe of the HMT in nucleon momentum distribution of neutron-rich nuclei.

# Investigation of the short-range correlation and its induced high-momentum tail by photon emission from intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions

## Key claim

The high-momentum tail (HMT) in nucleon momentum distributions of neutron-rich nuclei leads to an obvious increase of hard photon production from proton-neutron bremsstrahlung collisions, and the ratio of double differential photon production probability can serve as a possible probe of the HMT, but the effect of HMT on bremsstrahlung photons becomes weaker at higher incident beam energy (e.g., approximately 37% growth at 50 MeV/nucleon versus about 21% growth at 140 MeV/nucleon).

## Method

The author employed the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate heavy-ion reactions of 124Sn + 124Sn, 112Sn + 112Sn, and 197Au + 197Au at beam energies of 50 and 140 MeV/nucleon. Two versions of initial single-nucleon momentum distributions were used: a free Fermi gas (FFG) step function and a parameterized HMT distribution with a C/k^4 tail containing about 25% of nucleons above the Fermi momentum, with isospin-dependent weighting. Photon production from proton-neutron bremsstrahlung was calculated perturbatively using the one boson exchange model formula, and the time evolution of photon multiplicity and double differential probabilities were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- In 124Sn + 124Sn central collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon, the photon multiplicity in the FFG case is 9.1 × 10^{-3}; the HMT produces approximately 37% growth in photon production.
- In 124Sn + 124Sn central collisions at 140 MeV/nucleon, the photon multiplicity in the FFG case is 3.06 × 10^{-2}; the HMT produces about 21% growth in photon production.
- For 124Sn + 124Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon, the multiplicity of hard photons within 50 MeV ≤ Eγ ≤ 125 MeV increases from 9.0 × 10^{-3} (FFG) to 1.2 × 10^{-2} (HMT), a 33% growth.
- For 124Sn + 124Sn at 140 MeV/nucleon, the multiplicity of hard photons with 140 MeV ≤ Eγ ≤ 215 MeV increases from 1.45 × 10^{-3} (FFG) to 3.0 × 10^{-3} (HMT).
- For 197Au + 197Au at 50 MeV/nucleon, the multiplicity of hard photons increases from 1.6 × 10^{-2} (FFG) to 2.23 × 10^{-2} (HMT).
- The ratio Rp of double differential photon production probabilities between 50 and 140 MeV/nucleon shows a difference between HMT and FFG of up to about 10.5% at high photon energies.
- The HMT parameterization uses a high-momentum cutoff λ ≈ 2.75 and yields about 25% of nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter (δ=0) involved in the HMT.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of HMT effects using bremsstrahlung photons in symmetric systems (e.g., 12C + 12C by the same author in 2023) to neutron-rich asymmetric nuclei such as 124Sn, 112Sn, and 197Au. It builds on experimental findings from Jefferson Lab (2019) showing that about 20% of nucleons in 12C are correlated, with 90% of correlated pairs being neutron-proton pairs, and that even in heavy isospin-rich nuclei the SRC pairs are predominantly n-p correlations. The ratio Rp is proposed to reduce uncertainties from the photon production probability and NN scattering cross section, following the approach of prior work (e.g., 2010) on differential photon production ratios. This enables future experimental tests using hard photon measurements to probe the high-momentum component of nucleon momentum distributions in neutron-rich nuclei.
