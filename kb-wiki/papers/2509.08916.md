# Microscopic calculation of two-particle-two-hole meson-exchange currents in $^{40}$Ar and asymmetric scaling properties for neutrino and electron scattering

> We present a microscopic calculation of two particle-two hole meson exchange current response functions in asymmetric nuclei, with particular emphasis on the $^{40}$Ar nucleus. Employing a relativistic mean-field and relativistic Fermi gas framework, we compute the nuclear response for $^{40}$Ar and compare it with that of the symmetric $^{40}$Ca nucleus, analyzing the role of proton-neutron imbalance. The model incorporates distinct proton and neutron Fermi momenta to accurately capture the nuclear dynamics of systems with $Z \neq N$. Our results indicate that using $^{40}$Ca as a proxy for $^{40}$Ar leads to a systematic error of approximately 10\%. Additionally, we propose an asymmetric scaling formula to obtain the 2p2h response for arbitrary nuclei from the $^{12}$C response, improving the description of asymmetric nuclei. Finally, we benchmark our predictions against inclusive electron scattering and neutrino cross sections.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) framework with distinct proton and neutron Fermi momenta, the authors find that using the symmetric nucleus $^{40}$Ca as a proxy for the asymmetric $^{40}$Ar introduces a systematic error of approximately 10% in the 2p2h response, and they propose an asymmetric scaling formula to obtain 2p2h responses for arbitrary nuclei from $^{12}$C reference calculations.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic calculations of two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) meson-exchange current (MEC) response functions for $^{40}$Ar, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{12}$C within both RMF and RFG frameworks, incorporating separate proton and neutron Fermi momenta to account for isospin asymmetry. They evaluated the full seven-dimensional integral for the hadronic tensor numerically on a high-performance computing cluster, resolving contributions by final-state isospin channels ($pp$, $np$, and $nn$), and then proposed asymmetric scaling formulas based on phase-space factors involving proton number, neutron number, and the cubic powers of Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- Using $^{40}$Ca as a proxy for $^{40}$Ar leads to a systematic error of approximately 10% in the 2p2h response (from abstract).
- In the RMF calculation at $q = 500$ MeV/$c$, the $pp$ responses of $^{40}$Ar are about 6% larger than those of $^{40}$Ca, while the $np$ responses of $^{40}$Ar exceed those of $^{40}$Ca by more than 30%.
- The per-nucleon ratio $R^T_{\nu}(^{40}\mathrm{Ar})/R^T_{\nu}(^{12}\mathrm{C})$ at the $\Delta$-peak maximum ranges from 1.04 (RMF, $q=500$ MeV/$c$) to 1.30 (RMF, $q=2000$ MeV/$c$) and from 1.33 (RFG, $q=500$ MeV/$c$) to 1.53 (RFG, $q=2000$ MeV/$c$) in the $pp+pn$ channel.
- The density-based scaling prescription of Ama17 underestimates the $^{40}$Ar response in the RFG case by up to approximately 38%.
- The RFG responses are typically larger than RMF responses, with the $\Delta$ peak narrower and shifted by approximately 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous 2p2h MEC calculations for symmetric nuclei like $^{12}$C (Nieves 2011, Megias 2016) to asymmetric nuclei by following the approach of Barbero (2018) for 1p-1h processes, assigning different Fermi momenta to protons and neutrons. The results enable improved neutrino interaction modeling for experiments such as DUNE and MicroBooNE that use argon targets, and provide a theoretically grounded alternative to the ad hoc scaling formulas currently implemented in Monte Carlo event generators like GENIE (Schmitz 2016, Dolan 2019).
