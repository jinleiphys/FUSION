# The hadronic nucleus-nucleus cross section and the nucleon size

> Even though the total hadronic nucleus-nucleus cross section is among the most fundamental observables, it has only recently been measured precisely for lead-lead collisions at the LHC. This measurement implies the nucleon width should be below 0.7 fm, which is in contradiction with all known state-of-the-art Bayesian estimates. We study the implications of the smaller nucleon width on quark-gluon plasma properties such as the bulk viscosity. The smaller nucleon width dramatically improves the description of several triple-differential observables.

## Key claim

The recently measured total hadronic lead-lead cross section at the LHC implies a nucleon width below approximately 0.7 fm, which contradicts all previous state-of-the-art Bayesian estimates that favored widths around 0.82–1.05 fm; the smaller width dramatically improves the description of triple-differential observables but requires reweighting of model-dependent observables in the Bayesian analysis to achieve consistency.

## Method

The authors used the Trajectum 1.3 framework with a 21-dimensional parameter space to perform a global Bayesian analysis of 653 experimental data points from PbPb and pPb collisions at LHC energies. They modeled the initial state using the TRENTo model with Gaussian nucleon overlap functions of variable width w, and computed the total hadronic nucleus-nucleus cross section from the condition that at least one nucleon-nucleon interaction occurs. They compared fits with and without including the measured PbPb cross section (7.67 ± 0.24 b at 5.02 TeV) and the pPb cross section (2.06 ± 0.08 b at 5.02 TeV), and introduced a weighting scheme that down-weights more model-dependent observables such as particle-identified and pT-differential spectra.

## Key numbers

- PbPb total hadronic cross section: 7.67 ± 0.24 b at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (ALICE measurement)
- pPb cross section: 2.06 ± 0.08 b at √sNN = 5.02 TeV (CMS measurement)
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section: 67.6 mb at 5.02 TeV and 61.2 mb at 2.76 TeV
- Previous Bayesian nucleon width estimates: 0.98 ± 0.18 fm (1808.02106), 0.96 ± 0.05 fm (Bernhard 2019), 0.94 ± 0.18 fm (2010.15130), 1.05 ± 0.13 fm (2011.01430), 0.82 ± 0.23 fm (2110.13153)
- Implied nucleon width from cross section: approximately 0.4–0.5 fm
- Proton charge radius: 0.841 fm (Pohl 2010)
- Black disk approximation radius from σNN = 68 mb: 0.74 fm
- Posterior PbPb cross section with weighting and σAA included: 8.02 ± 0.19 b (compared to ALICE 7.67 ± 0.24 b)
- Posterior pPb cross section with weighting and σAA included: 2.20 ± 0.06 b (compared to CMS 2.06 ± 0.08 b)
- IP-Glasma transverse two-gluon radius from J/ψ scattering: 0.50 ± 0.03 fm (Caldwell 2010)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian analysis framework of Nijs et al. (2020, 2021) and Bernhard et al. (2019) by incorporating the recently measured ALICE total hadronic cross section for PbPb collisions. The finding that the nucleon width must be below 0.7 fm contradicts all previous Bayesian estimates (Bernhard 2019, Nijs 2020, 2021) and aligns instead with the IP-Glasma model based on HERA data (Caldwell 2010) and the earlier Bayesian study of Moreland et al. (2016) that found w = 0.48 ± 0.1 fm. The smaller width enables improved description of triple-differential observables like ρ(vn², ⟨pT⟩) and implies larger bulk viscosity, but raises questions about the reliability of Bayesian analyses when systematic uncertainties and correlations are not fully captured.
