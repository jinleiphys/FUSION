# Examination of the $c\bar{c}+n+^{10}$Be bound-state problem within three cluster models based on QCD charmonium-nucleon interactions

> The possible bound state of the $c\bar{c}+n+^{10}$Be system, representing a hypothetical charmonium-nucleus configuration, is investigated. The analysis is conducted within a three-cluster framework, in which the binary subsystems are treated as $n+^{10}\textrm{Be}$, $^{10}\textrm{Be}+c\bar{c}$, and $c\bar{c}+n$. The hyperspherical harmonics method is employed to provide a convenient description of this three-cluster configuration. The calculations are performed using effective $^{10}\textrm{Be}\textrm{-}c\bar{c}$ potentials constructed via the single-folding procedure. These potentials have been derived recently on the basis of state-of-the-art lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD Collaboration, which provided interactions for the spin-$3/2$ $J/ψN$, spin-$1/2$ $J/ψN$, spin-$1/2$ $η_{c}N$, and spin-averaged $J/ψN$ channels, all obtained at nearly physical pion masses. The numerical results indicate that the central binding energies of the spin-$3/2$ $J/ψ+n+^{10}$Be, spin-$1/2$ $J/ψ+n+^{10}$Be, and spin-$1/2$ $η_{c}+n+^{10}$Be systems are 3.47, 3.55, and 1.91 MeV, respectively. The corresponding root-mean-square nuclear matter radii are predicted to be approximately 2.49, 2.48, and 2.60 fm.

# Examination of the $c\bar{c}+n+^{10}$Be bound-state problem within three cluster models based on QCD charmonium-nucleon interactions

## Key claim

The $c\bar{c}+n+^{10}$Be system can form bound states, with central binding energies ranging from approximately 1.91 to 4.28 MeV depending on the spin channel of the charmonium-nucleon interaction, but these results depend on the assumption that the three-cluster configuration dominates and that the $^{10}$Be core can be treated as inert.

## Method

The three-body system was treated using the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method within a Faddeev-equation framework, with the binary subsystems represented as $n+^{10}$Be, $^{10}$Be+$c\bar{c}$, and $c\bar{c}+n$. Effective $^{10}$Be-$c\bar{c}$ potentials were constructed via the single-folding procedure, integrating the nucleon density distribution of $^{10}$Be against lattice QCD-derived $c\bar{c}$-nucleon interactions from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Lyu et al., 2025) at nearly physical pion masses. The $n$-$^{10}$Be interaction employed a Woods-Saxon potential with central and spin-orbit terms, with Pauli-forbidden two-body states removed via a supersymmetric transformation.

## Key numbers

- Central binding energy of the spin-3/2 $J/\psi+n+^{10}$Be system: 3.47 MeV (spin-averaged case) or 3.12 MeV (spin-polarized case)
- Central binding energy of the spin-1/2 $J/\psi+n+^{10}$Be system: 3.55 MeV (spin-averaged case) or 4.28 MeV (spin-polarized case)
- Central binding energy of the spin-1/2 $\eta_c+n+^{10}$Be system: 1.91 MeV
- Root-mean-square nuclear matter radius for spin-3/2 $J/\psi+n+^{10}$Be: 2.49 fm
- Root-mean-square nuclear matter radius for spin-1/2 $J/\psi+n+^{10}$Be: 2.48 fm (spin-averaged) or 2.45 fm (spin-polarized)
- Root-mean-square nuclear matter radius for spin-1/2 $\eta_c+n+^{10}$Be: 2.60 fm
- Two-body binding energy of $^{10}$Be-$c\bar{c}$ for spin-3/2 $J/\psi N$: 2.18 MeV
- Two-body binding energy of $^{10}$Be-$c\bar{c}$ for spin-1/2 $J/\psi N$: 3.24 MeV
- Two-body binding energy of $^{10}$Be-$c\bar{c}$ for spin-1/2 $\eta_c N$: 1.11 MeV
- Two-body binding energy of $^{10}$Be-$c\bar{c}$ for spin-averaged $J/\psi N$: 2.52 MeV
- Lattice QCD pion mass used: $m_\pi = 146.4(4)$ MeV at lattice spacing $a = 0.0846$ fm

## Context

This work extends recent cluster-model studies of $c\bar{c}+\alpha+\alpha$ systems by Etminan (2025) and Zhou (2026) to a heavier core nucleus ($^{10}$Be), using the same hyperspherical harmonics methodology. The calculations rely on the HAL QCD Collaboration's lattice QCD results for $c\bar{c}$-nucleon interactions (Lyu et al., 2025), which supersede earlier phenomenological approaches such as the QCD van der Waals mechanism proposed by Brodsky (1990) and the folding-model estimates by Wasson (1991) that predicted binding energies around 30 MeV for heavier nuclei. The results enable predictions for experimental searches at Jefferson Lab, J-PARC, FAIR, RHIC, and the LHC, and complement femtoscopy proposals by Krein et al. (2020-2023) for extracting $J/\psi$-nucleon interactions from correlation functions.
