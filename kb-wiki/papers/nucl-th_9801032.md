# Observation of the Mott Effect in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Possibility of observing the Mott momentum in the distribution of the deuterons produced in the process $p + n \to d + γ$, in the first stage of a nuclear reaction is presented. The correlation of a hard photon with a deuteron allows to select those deuterons produced at the beginning of a reaction.

# Observation of the Mott Effect in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The correlation of a hard photon (Eγ > 30 MeV) with a deuteron produced in the process p + n → d + γ during the first stage of a heavy-ion collision can be used to observe the Mott momentum for deuterons, provided the incident energy lies in the range 70–140 MeV/A where the available phase space for bound deuterons is strongly reduced by the Mott effect.

## Method
The authors employ a first-chance collision model (FCCM) for the initial phase of a heavy-ion reaction, where the initial nucleon momentum distribution is given by two Fermi spheres. The condition for bound deuteron formation is implemented via a parametrization of the Pauli-blocking effect: the integral of the deuteron wave function squared weighted by the surrounding nucleon momentum distribution must be less than 0.2. The cross section for p + n → d + γ is obtained from experimental data on the inverse process γ + d → p + n, using a parameterization with two power-law forms for Eγ below and above 50 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The condition for deuteron existence at normal nuclear density is pd/2 > pMott/2 ≃ 1.2 pF, where pF is the Fermi momentum.
- The interesting energy range for studying the dependence of the Mott momentum on incident energy is 70–140 MeV/A; around Elab = 140 MeV/A the lower limit on deuteron momentum disappears, and around 50 MeV/A kinematics do not allow d–γ pair production with Eγ > 30 MeV.
- Calculations were performed for pF = 210 MeV/c.
- The cross section for γ + d → p + n is parameterized as σ [mb] = 32.3 / Eγ^1.39 for Eγ < 50 MeV and σ [mb] = 7.72 / Eγ^1.01 for Eγ > 50 MeV, where Eγ is in MeV.
- The probability of producing a d–γ pair per participant in medium-size nuclei at 90 MeV/A, after accounting for deuteron absorption, is 10^{-6}.
- Only about 20% of produced deuterons survive in reactions of medium-size nuclei, estimated using σdN^ine ≃ 2 σNN.

## Context
This work extends prior analyses of hard photon production in heavy-ion collisions by the TAPS collaboration, which emphasized the π + N → N + γ process, and introduces the previously neglected p + n → d + γ channel. The Mott effect for deuterons was discussed earlier by Bertsch and collaborators (Bertsch 1977) and by other authors (OgMott, OgMott1, BD) in the context of three-body deuteron formation. The proposed d–γ correlation provides a direct experimental test of the Mott mechanism in nuclear matter and enables the first measurement of the contribution of a specific channel to the total hard photon yield, which is believed to be important especially at energies above 140 MeV/A.
