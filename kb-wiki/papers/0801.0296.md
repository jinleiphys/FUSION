# Triaxial projected shell model study of gamma-vibrational bands in even-even Er isotopes

> We expand the triaxial projected shell model basis to include triaxially-deformed multi-quasiparticle states. This allows us to study the yrast and gamma-vibrational bands up to high spins for both gamma-soft and well-deformed nuclei. As the first application, a systematic study of the high-spin states in Er-isotopes is performed. The calculated yrast and gamma-bands are compared with the known experimental data, and it is shown that the agreement between theory and experiment is quite satisfactory. The calculation leads to predictions for bands based on one- and two-gamma phonon where current data are still sparse. It is observed that gamma-bands for neutron-deficient isotopes of 156Er and 158Er are close to the yrast band, and further these bands are predicted to be nearly degenerate for high-spin states.

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) basis is expanded to include triaxially-deformed multi-quasiparticle states (two-neutron, two-proton, and four-quasiparticle configurations), enabling the first systematic study of yrast and gamma-vibrational bands up to high spins (I ~ 24) in even-even Er isotopes from A=156 to 170, with the caveat that the axial deformation parameter epsilon is fixed from measured quadrupole deformations and the non-axial parameter epsilon' is chosen to reproduce the gamma-band head rather than obtained variationally.

## Method

The authors construct a triaxial Nilsson mean-field basis with given deformation parameters (epsilon, epsilon') and generate quasiparticle states via BCS pairing. They then perform exact three-dimensional angular momentum projection onto good angular momentum states from 0-quasiparticle, two-neutron, two-proton, and four-quasiparticle (two protons plus two neutrons) configurations. Finally, they diagonalize a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian in this projected basis to obtain configuration-mixed states for the yrast, gamma-, and two-gamma bands.

## Key numbers

- The monopole pairing strength is given by G_M = [21.24 -/+ 13.86(N-Z)/A]/A, with the minus sign for neutrons and plus for protons, approximately reproducing odd-even mass differences in the rare-earth region.
- The quadrupole pairing strength G_Q is taken as 0.18 times G_M.
- For 156Er and 158Er, the (2,0) gamma-band and (0,0) ground-state band become nearly degenerate for I >= 16, with even-spin states in the (2,0) band slightly lower than the (0,0) band.
- The (1,2n) neutron two-quasiparticle band crosses the (0,0) ground-state band at I=12 for 156Er, 158Er, and 160Er; at I=14 for 162Er; at I=16 for 164Er; and at I=18 for 166Er.
- For 166Er and 168Er, the calculated two-gamma bands agree with available experimental data from Fa96 and Ha98, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach of Sheikh, Sun, and collaborators (1999, 2000, 2001, 2002, 2007) from a restrictive 0-quasiparticle basis (limited to low-spin and low-excitation studies) to a multi-quasiparticle basis capable of describing high-spin states up to second bandcrossing. The method contrasts with the quasiparticle phonon nuclear model (QPNM), multi-phonon method (MPM), and dynamic deformation model (DDM), which do not produce wavefunctions that are eigenstates of angular momentum. The results enable predictions for one- and two-gamma phonon bands in neutron-deficient Er isotopes where experimental data are sparse, and demonstrate increasing anharmonicity of gamma vibrations with increasing neutron number.
